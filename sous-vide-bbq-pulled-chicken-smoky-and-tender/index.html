Sous Vide BBQ Pulled Chicken: Smoky and Tender


If you’re a fan of tender, juicy, and smoky BBQ pulled chicken, then you’re in for a treat! In this recipe, we’ll take your ordinary pulled chicken to the next level by using the sous vide cooking method. Sous vide is a French technique that involves cooking food in a precise temperature-controlled water bath, resulting in perfectly cooked and flavorful dishes every time.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 150°F (65°C).

  2. In a small bowl, mix together the smoked paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. This will be our dry rub for the chicken.

  3. Season the chicken breasts generously with the dry rub, making sure to coat all sides.

  4. Place the seasoned chicken breasts in a sous vide bag and seal it using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  5. Carefully place the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 2 hours. This will ensure that the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  6. After 2 hours, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully open it. Be cautious as the steam inside the bag can be hot.

  7. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, remove the chicken breasts from the bag and pat them dry using paper towels.

  8. Brush each side of the chicken breasts with your favorite BBQ sauce, ensuring they are well coated.

  9. Place the chicken breasts on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they develop a beautiful char and the BBQ sauce caramelizes.

  10. Once done, remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.

  11. Using two forks, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces.

  12. Serve the sous vide BBQ pulled chicken on a fresh bun or enjoy it on its own. You can also add additional BBQ sauce or your favorite toppings like coleslaw or pickles for extra flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have a sous vide machine, you can still make this recipe by poaching the seasoned chicken breasts in a pot of simmering water for about 20-25 minutes until they reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

  • Feel free to experiment with different flavors of BBQ sauce to suit your taste preferences. Whether it’s sweet and tangy, spicy, or smoky, the choice is yours.

  • If you prefer a smokier flavor, you can add a teaspoon of liquid smoke to the BBQ sauce before brushing it onto the chicken.

  • Leftover pulled chicken can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It also freezes well, so you can make a big batch and enjoy it whenever you’re in the mood for some delicious BBQ.

So there you have it - a mouthwatering recipe for sous vide BBQ pulled chicken that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. The sous vide cooking method ensures that the chicken is perfectly tender and infused with smoky flavors. Give it a try and impress your friends and family with this elevated twist on a classic BBQ favorite. Enjoy!
