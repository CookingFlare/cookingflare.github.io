How to Cook the Perfect Samosas: A Traditional Recipe


Samosas, the crispy, savory, and utterly delightful pastries, have been a staple in South Asian cuisine for centuries. These beautifully crafted appetizers are filled with a flavorful mixture of spices, potatoes, peas, and onions, then wrapped in a delicate dough wrapper. When cooked to perfection, the contrasting textures of the crunchy shells and the soft, spicy filling create a truly unforgettable culinary experience. In this recipe, we will guide you through the traditional process of crafting samosas by hand, a skill passed down through generations of home cooks in the subcontinent. Preparation time will be around 45 minutes, while cooking time will be approximately 30 minutes. Gathering the necessary ingredients and tools beforehand will ensure a seamless cooking experience. Ingredients

2 cups all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1/4 teaspoon ground coriander 1/4 teaspoon turmeric 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder 1/4 cup ghee or vegetable oil 3 cups lukewarm water For the filling: 2 large potatoes, boiled and diced 1 cup peas 1 large onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger paste 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder Salt, to taste Chopped cilantro, for garnish

Step 1: Make the Dough To make the traditional samosa dough, combine the flour, salt, cumin seeds, ground coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder in a large mixing bowl. This blend of spices is essential in creating the distinct flavor profile of samosas. Using your fingertips, work the ghee or oil into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually pour in the lukewarm water, and start kneading the dough on a floured surface. Continue kneading for about 10 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and pliable. Wrap the dough in a damp cloth, and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. Step 2: Prepare the Filling While the dough is resting, we'll prepare the filling. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute. Then, add the boiled and diced potatoes, peas, ginger paste, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well to combine the filling ingredients, and cook for 5 minutes, or until the potatoes are lightly browned. Step 3: Assemble the Samosas Once the dough has rested, pinch off a small portion, approximately the size of a small egg. Roll it out into a thin circle using a rolling pin. Brush the surface lightly with water using a clean brush or a piece of cloth. Place a tablespoon or two of the prepared filling in the center of the dough circle, leaving a small border around the edges. Fold the dough over the filling to form a cone shape, and press the edges together to seal the samosa. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling. Step 4: Fry the Samosas Heat enough oil in a deep frying pan to fry the samosas over medium heat. When the oil is hot, slide a few of the samosas gently into the oil, taking care not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the samosas until they turn golden brown, approximately 6-7 minutes on each side. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove the fried samosas from the oil, and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Step 5: Serve Serve the golden fried samosas hot, garnished with chopped cilantro, and accompanied by a side of mint chutney or raita, or a simple salad for a more substantial meal. Alternatively, bake the samosas in a preheated oven at 375°F for about 20 minutes, turning them halfway through the cooking time, for a lighter and crisper version. In preparing these samosas, you will be drawing on the rich culinary heritage of South Asia, where flavors, textures, and aromas come together in perfect harmony. As you fold a samosa with your own hands, remember that you are part of a long tradition of family cooks, small takeaways, and street vendors, all united in their passion for this beloved dish.
