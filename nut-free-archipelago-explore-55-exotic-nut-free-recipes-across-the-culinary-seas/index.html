Nut-Free Archipelago: Explore 55 Exotic Nut-Free Recipes Across the Culinary Seas


Welcome to the Nut-Free Archipelago, a creative culinary journey where land meets sea, and taste meets dietary restriction. As you set sail on this voyage through uncharted gastronomic waters, you’ll discover that stepping away from nuts doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor. Together, we’ll traverse five different “seas” each home to 11 unique recipes, giving you a deliciously diverse sampling of nut-free dishes to entice your taste buds. Let’s begin!

Sea of Summons

First, we find ourselves in the Sea of Summons, where flavors like a trumpet call to attention. _Zing, zing, zing, indeed!

1. Island of Herbes de Provence Flatbread

A symphony of flavors, this flatbread features herbes de Provence, sundried tomatoes, and kalamata olives, creating a Mediterranean masterpiece you won’t soon forget.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball pizza dough
  • 2 tablespoons herbes de Provence
  • 1/4 cup sundried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Olive oil for brushing *Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (232°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, stretch your pizza dough into an oval shape.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the herbes de Provence with the sundried tomatoes and olives.
  4. Spoon the mixture over the stretched dough, leaving a small border around the edge. Sprinkle the mozzarella on top and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Carefully transfer the flatbread to a baking sheet. Brush the edges with olive oil.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the crust is crisp and golden. Slice into squares and serve warm.

2. Caribbean Coconutter Coconut Shrimp

Transport yourself to a Caribbean paradise without the worry of nut allergies with this zesty coconut shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup finely grated unsweetened coconut
  • 1 tablespoon Old Bay seasoning
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika

Directions:

  1. In a shallow dish, place the flour. In a second dish, beat the eggs. In a third dish, mix together the coconut, Old Bay seasoning, and paprika.
  2. Lightly dredge each shrimp in the flour, then coat with the egg mixture, followed by the coconut mixture. Ensure each shrimp is evenly coated, shaking off any excess.
  3. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, add enough oil to reach a depth of 1/2 inch. Heat the oil to 350°F (177°C).
  4. Carefully lower the shrimp into the hot oil, working in batches to prevent overcrowding. Fry for 2-3 minutes per side or until golden brown.
  5. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the shrimp to a paper-towel-lined plate to drain. Serve with lemon wedges for squeezing over the top.

3. Moroccan Matrimony Couscous

Seasonal spices from the Moroccan market blend harmoniously in this couscous dish, bringing rich and aromatic flavors to your taste palette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 1/4 cups boiling water
  • 1/2 cup dried apricots, sliced
  • 1/2 cup golden raisins
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the couscous and boiling water. Cover and set aside for 5 minutes, or until the water is absorbed.
  2. Fluff the couscous with a fork, then fold in the apricots, raisins, and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet over medium heat, add the olive oil. Sauté the onion until it softens and becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the garlic, cumin, cinnamon, and ginger, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Combine the sautéed onion mixture and the couscous in a large mixing bowl. Drizzle with the lemon zest and juice, stirring well to ensure even distribution.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and sprinkle with the chopped parsley.
  7. Serve the Moroccan Matrimony Couscous with grilled chicken, lamb, or fish for a complete meal.

Sea of Seafood

Heading directly into the Sea of Seafood, we encounter the savory wonders of the deep.

4. Ocean of Avocado Seabass Ceviche

A twist on a classic, this bright, chunky ceviche showcases perfectly cooked seabass in a zesty marinade.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound seabass, skin-on fillets
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 3 limes
  • 2 tablespoons chopped jalapeño (remove seeds for less heat)

Directions:

  1. Cut the seabass fillets into bite-size pieces. Heat a skillet over medium heat and sear the fish until it’s cooked through, about 3 minutes per side. (Note: Avoid overcooking, as it will prevent the fish from “cooking” in the lime juice) Transfer the fish to a cutting board and let it cool before cutting into pieces.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the seared seabass, diced avocado, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, and jalapeño.
  3. Mix gently to combine, taking care not to break up the fish. Season with salt to taste.
  4. Serve with sturdy corn tortilla chips, allowing your guests to scoop up the ceviche with their preferred amount of chip.

5. Rhumb Line Red Curry Shrimp

A zippy, aromatic curry with a subtle kick - perfect to set the sails for an exotic adventure.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup red curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1/4 cup basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, add the vegetable oil and then the red curry paste. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  2. Add the coconut milk, fish sauce, and sugar, stirring well to ensure the sugar dissolves.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and allow it to cook for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the shrimp, kaffir lime leaves, and cook for 3-4 minutes or until the shrimp are cooked through.
  5. Sprinkle with the basil leaves and cilantro. Stir gently, releasing the essential oils of the basil and allowing the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve over rice with steamed vegetables for a well-rounded meal.

Sea of Sides

In the Sea of Sides, we find dishes to generously pair with our main courses - simple and scrumptious.

6. Mounded Monkfish Medallions

Medallions of monkfish sit pretty on a dense and creamy parsnip purée.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound monkfish, cut into 1-inch medallions
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound parsnips, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives

Directions:

  1. Season the monkfish medallions generously with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, add the olive oil. Once hot, add the monkfish medallions, cooking them for 3-4 minutes per side or until golden brown and cooked through.
  3. For the parsnip purée, place the chopped parsnips in a medium saucepan. Add the broth and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 15 to 20 minutes, or until the parsnips are tender.
  4. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the parsnips to a blender or food processor. Add the butter, and purée until smooth.
  5. Season the parsnip purée with salt and pepper to taste, and spoon it next to the monkfish medallions.
  6. Garnish with chopped chives before serving.

7. Journey of Jalapeno Corn Maque Choux

A Southern classic with a touch of heat, this corn side dish is perfect for those who don’t shy away from a little kick.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • 2 cups fresh corn kernels (about 4 ears)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet over medium heat, add the olive oil, then the onion. Cook until the onion softens and becomes translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in the garlic and jalapeño, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the corn kernels, basil, and chives, cooking the mixture for 5-7 minutes while stirring occasionally, as the corn reaches a tender-crisp consistency.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Add the butter, allowing it to melt and infuse into the mixture before removing from heat.
  5. Serve alongside your favorite entrée and prepare for a taste adventure.

8. Nautical New Potato and Green Bean Medley

Returning to the Sea of Sides, astute sailors lock in new friendships over this stellar potato and green bean pairing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound small new potatoes, halved
  • 1 pound green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small shallot, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the halved new potatoes and the green beans. Drizzle with the olive oil, sprinkle with salt, pepper, minced shallot, and chopped thyme.
  2. Spread the potato-green bean mixture in a single layer on a large baking sheet and roast in a preheated 400°F (204°C) oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and browned.
  3. Stir the vegetables half-way through roasting for even cooking and browning.
  4. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving.

Deep Sea Treats

As we voyage deeper, we discover the Deep Sea Treats - dishes center-stage showcasing decadent dessert indulgences.

9. Seabrook Sundae Supreme

A staggering dessert culmination hailing from Seabrook, where we’ll indulge in a hefty helping of this sundae supreme.

Ingredients:

  • 4 scoops vanilla ice cream
  • Maple walnut ice cream (optional)
  • Warm chocolate syrup
  • Hot fudge sauce
  • Real whipped cream
  • Chopped walnuts
  • Maraschino cherry
  • Fresh mint leaf

Directions:

  1. Scoop out the vanilla ice cream into tall sundae glasses, adding a few spoonfuls of the maple walnut ice cream for contrast if that suits your fancy.
  2. Drizzle with generous spoonfuls of warm chocolate syrup and hot fudge sauce, making sure to pour enough to highlight the vanilla ice cream shades.
  3. Add a dollop of real whipped cream to the top of the sundae, followed by a sprinkle of chopped walnuts. Place a maraschino cherry on top, then finish with a sprig of fresh mint leaf for garnish.
  4. Indulge as requested.

10. Sunkist Sorbet Swirl

A refreshing, citrus-infused sorbet pairing, it’s Sunkist Sorbet Swirl

Ingredients:

  • 1 quart tangerine sorbet
  • 1/2 quart lemon sorbet
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Edible flowers, for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. Scoop the tangerine sorbet into one bowl and the lemon sorbet into another.
  2. Using the back of a spoon or a small ice cream scoop, gently swirl the sorbets together, creating a dreamy, marbled effect.
  3. Spoon the Sunkist Sorbet Swirl into individual serving bowls, garnishing with fresh mint leaves and, if you desire, edible flowers.

11. Concocted Coconut Cream Pie

A creamy classic, allow our Concocted Coconut Cream Pie to serenade your senses.

Ingredients:

  • pie crust (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 cup coconut custard filling
  • Whipped cream topping

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and carefully fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Fold the edges back to create a fluted border. Prick the crust all over with a fork, then line the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans.
  3. Bake the crust for 15 minutes, then remove from the oven and carefully remove the parchment with the weights/beans. Return the crust to the oven and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, until lightly golden brown.
  4. Allow the crust to cool for a few minutes while you prepare the filling.
  5. In a medium saucepan over medium-high heat, combine the heavy cream, coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 minute. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  6. In a separate bowl, whisk the coconut custard filling with a whisk or hand mixer until smooth. Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the custard, constantly stirring to prevent lumps.
  7. Pour the coconut custard filling from the blender into the pre-baked pie crust. Sprinkle the shredded coconut evenly over the filling.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and lightly golden brown. Allow the pie to cool, then refrigerate for a minimum of 3 hours.
  9. Before serving, add a generous dollop of whipped cream to the top of the Concocted Coconut Cream Pie. Chant the words and serve with a flourish.
