South African Malva Pudding Recipe with Apricot and Caramel


If you’re looking to add a touch of South African flavor to your dessert repertoire, then the Malva Pudding with Apricot and Caramel is a must-try. This sweet and indulgent dessert has a rich history and a delightful combination of flavors that will surely tantalize your taste buds. So, let’s dive into the world of South African culinary delights and learn how to create this mouthwatering treat.

Ingredients

For the pudding:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon smooth apricot jam
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon apricot extract

For the sauce:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 teaspoon caramel extract

Instructions

Making the Pudding

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking soda, and salt.

  3. In another bowl, whisk the egg and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the apricot jam, vinegar, melted butter, milk, and apricot extract. Mix until well combined.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until the batter is smooth.

  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the pudding is golden and a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.

Making the Caramel Sauce

  1. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, sugar, and butter over medium heat. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is smooth.

  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the caramel extract.

Serving the Malva Pudding

  1. While the pudding is still warm, prick it all over with a skewer and pour half of the hot caramel sauce over the top, allowing it to soak in.

  2. Serve the pudding warm, drizzled with extra caramel sauce and a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

The Origin of Malva Pudding

Malva Pudding is a beloved South African dessert that has its roots in Dutch colonial cuisine. The pudding is named after the Malva flower, which is known for its sweet fragrance. Originally, this dessert was made with apricot jam and served with a warm custard sauce. Over the years, variations of this recipe have emerged, with the addition of caramel and other flavors to enhance its richness.

The combination of apricot and caramel in this Malva Pudding recipe adds a delightful fruity sweetness and a rich, buttery undertone that elevates the traditional dessert to a whole new level of decadence.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting dessert, this South African Malva Pudding with Apricot and Caramel is sure to impress. Its luscious texture and irresistible flavors make it a standout dish that will leave your guests asking for more. So, why not embark on a culinary adventure and bring a taste of South Africa to your table with this delightful and indulgent treat? Enjoy!
