Korean Steamed Bun Recipe (Jjinppang)


Korean steamed buns, also known as Jjinppang (찐빵), are a beloved snack and street food in South Korea. These soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet buns are filled with a variety of fillings, with red bean paste being one of the most popular. Jjinppang is enjoyed by people of all ages and is often served during cold weather or festive seasons, making it a cozy comfort food.

In this post, we will walk you through the entire process of making these delectable Korean steamed buns from scratch. From preparing the dough to selecting the perfect fillings, we’ll give you all the tips and tricks you need to create the perfect batch of Jjinppang in your own kitchen.

History of Jjinppang

Jjinppang has a long and rich history in Korean cuisine. Traditionally, these buns were served as a street food, especially in the colder months when the warmth of a steamed bun was especially comforting. The name “Jjinppang” comes from the Korean word “Jjin” (찐), which means “steamed,” and “ppang” (빵), which means “bread” or “bun.” Over time, these buns became popular at festivals, markets, and various social gatherings.

While red bean paste remains the most common filling, you can find variations of Jjinppang with other fillings such as pork, vegetables, or even sweetened chestnuts. Each family may have its own unique take on the bun, adding their personal flair to this delightful dish.

Ingredients for Korean Steamed Buns (Jjinppang)

Dough Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons of dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 3/4 cup of warm water (about 110°F/43°C)
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil (or melted butter)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder

Red Bean Paste Filling:

  • 1 cup of cooked adzuki beans (or canned adzuki beans)
  • 1/4 cup of sugar (adjust to your sweetness preference)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil (optional for added richness)

Additional Fillings (Optional):

  • 1/2 cup of chopped vegetables (e.g., mushrooms, cabbage, onions)
  • 1/2 cup of cooked pork or chicken, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, finely grated

For Steaming:

  • 2-3 cups of water (for steaming)
  • Parchment paper or cabbage leaves (to prevent sticking)

Equipment:

  • Mixing bowls
  • Steam basket or steamer pot
  • Rolling pin
  • A pot with a lid for steaming

Instructions for Making Korean Steamed Buns (Jjinppang)

1. Prepare the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and dry yeast. Stir gently to mix, then set it aside for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This step is essential to ensure that your buns rise properly.

While waiting for the yeast to activate, sift the all-purpose flour into a large mixing bowl. Add the salt and baking powder to the flour and mix well. Once the yeast mixture is ready, pour it into the flour mixture along with the vegetable oil (or melted butter). Mix everything together with a wooden spoon or your hands until a rough dough forms.

Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a small amount of flour at a time. Be careful not to add too much flour, as it can make the dough tough.

Once the dough is kneaded properly, shape it into a ball and place it in a lightly greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and allow the dough to rise for about 1-1.5 hours or until it doubles in size.

2. Prepare the Red Bean Paste Filling

While the dough is rising, it’s time to prepare the red bean paste filling. If you’re using canned adzuki beans, drain and rinse them to remove any excess liquid. If you’re using cooked adzuki beans, ensure they are soft and tender.

In a saucepan, combine the adzuki beans and sugar. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture becomes thick and smooth. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can mash the beans with a spoon or potato masher.

Once the filling has thickened, remove it from the heat and stir in the sesame oil for an extra layer of flavor (optional). Allow the red bean paste to cool completely before using it as a filling.

3. Shape the Dough and Add the Filling

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release the air. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead it briefly to remove any excess air.

Divide the dough into equal portions (about 12-15 pieces, depending on the size of your buns). Roll each portion into a small ball and flatten it into a circle, about 3-4 inches in diameter. You can use a rolling pin to make this process easier.

Place a generous spoonful of the red bean paste in the center of each dough circle. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pinching them together to seal the bun. You should now have a neat, round bun.

To ensure that the buns maintain their shape while steaming, you can place them on a small square of parchment paper or on a cabbage leaf. This will prevent the buns from sticking to the steamer basket.

4. Steam the Buns

Now it’s time to steam the buns. In a large steamer pot, bring 2-3 cups of water to a boil. Place the buns in a single layer in the steamer basket, leaving some space between each bun for expansion.

Cover the steamer with a lid and steam the buns over medium-high heat for about 15-20 minutes. The buns should puff up and become soft and fluffy. Be careful not to open the lid during steaming, as this can affect the texture of the buns.

5. Serve and Enjoy

Once the buns are steamed to perfection, remove them from the steamer and let them cool slightly. Serve your Jjinppang warm, and enjoy the soft, pillowy texture and sweet red bean filling.

These steamed buns are perfect on their own, but they also pair wonderfully with tea or other Korean snacks. You can also try different fillings to suit your taste, such as a savory pork filling, vegetable mix, or even a sweet chestnut paste.

Tips for Making Perfect Jjinppang

  1. Use warm, but not hot, water for activating the yeast. Water that is too hot can kill the yeast, while water that is too cold may not activate it properly.

  2. Knead the dough well to ensure it becomes smooth and elastic. This is important for achieving the light and fluffy texture of the buns.

  3. Let the dough rise properly. Don’t rush the rising process. If the dough doesn’t rise enough, your buns might be dense instead of light and fluffy.

  4. Adjust the sweetness of the filling to your preference. Some people like their red bean paste to be on the sweeter side, while others prefer it less sweet.

  5. Don’t overfill the buns. You want enough filling to enjoy the flavor, but not so much that the bun becomes difficult to seal or bursts during steaming.

  6. Use a steamer with a lid. A tight-fitting lid is crucial for keeping the steam contained, ensuring that the buns cook evenly.

  7. Experiment with different fillings. While red bean paste is the classic choice, feel free to explore other fillings like ground pork with vegetables, sweet potatoes, or chestnut paste for a unique twist on this traditional dish.

Variations of Jjinppang

Jjinppang can be made with various fillings, depending on your taste preferences. Here are a few creative variations you can try:

1. Savory Jjinppang (Pork or Chicken Filling)

Instead of the sweet red bean paste, you can fill the buns with a savory mixture of ground pork or chicken, onions, and mushrooms. Season it with soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger for a flavorful filling that is perfect for those who prefer savory dishes.

2. Vegetable Jjinppang

For a vegetarian version, try using a filling made from stir-fried vegetables such as mushrooms, cabbage, carrots, and onions. Add soy sauce and a touch of sesame oil for flavor. This is a healthy and delicious alternative to the traditional sweet filling.

3. Sweet Potato Jjinppang

For a comforting variation, use mashed sweet potatoes mixed with a little sugar and butter as the filling. The natural sweetness of the sweet potatoes makes for a perfect filling for those who prefer a sweeter alternative to red bean paste.

4. Chestnut Jjinppang

Chestnuts, especially when cooked and sweetened, make for a rich and flavorful filling. You can use boiled chestnuts mixed with sugar and a little butter for a luxurious, nutty filling.

5. Kimchi Jjinppang

For a bold and spicy version, use a filling made of chopped kimchi and ground pork. The fermented flavors of kimchi combined with savory pork will provide a tangy and spicy contrast to the soft, fluffy bun.

Final Thoughts

Making Korean steamed buns (Jjinppang) at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With a bit of time and patience, you can enjoy this traditional Korean snack in the comfort of your own kitchen. Whether you stick to the classic red bean filling or experiment with new fillings, these buns are sure to impress anyone who tries them. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start steaming! Happy cooking!
