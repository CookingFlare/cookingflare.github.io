The Ultimate Guide to Middle Eastern Flatbread: Markouk Bread


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve probably come across various types of delicious flatbreads. One such bread that stands out is the Markouk Bread. With its thin and elastic texture, this bread is perfect for wrapping around your favorite fillings or simply enjoying on its own. In this ultimate guide, we’ll explore the history, ingredients, and step-by-step process of making this Middle Eastern delight.

History of Markouk Bread

Markouk Bread, also known as Markook or Markouk, is a traditional Middle Eastern flatbread that has been enjoyed for centuries. It originated in the Levant region, which includes countries such as Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, and Palestine. The word “Markouk” translates to “thin” in Arabic, which perfectly describes the bread’s unique characteristic.

Ingredients

To make Markouk Bread, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup warm water

Step-by-Step Process

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making Markouk Bread:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Mix well to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  2. Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the olive oil and warm water. Using your hands or a wooden spoon, gradually mix the ingredients together until a soft dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes. The dough should become smooth and elastic.

  4. Divide the dough into small golf ball-sized portions and cover them with a clean kitchen towel. Let the dough rest for 30 minutes to allow it to relax and rise slightly.

  5. Preheat a large skillet or griddle over medium-high heat.

  6. Take one portion of the dough and roll it out into a thin circle, approximately 10-12 inches in diameter. If needed, lightly dust the dough and rolling pin with flour to prevent sticking.

  7. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough onto the hot skillet and cook for about 1-2 minutes on each side. The bread should develop golden-brown spots and puff slightly.

  8. Once cooked, remove the Markouk Bread from the skillet and place it on a clean kitchen towel. Fold the towel over the bread to keep it warm and pliable while cooking the remaining portions.

  9. Repeat the rolling and cooking process with the rest of the dough portions until all the bread is cooked.

Serving Suggestions

Markouk Bread can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Use it as a wrap for your favorite Middle Eastern fillings, such as falafel, shawarma, or grilled vegetables.
  • Tear it into pieces and dip it into flavorful dips like hummus, baba ganoush, or tzatziki.
  • Serve it alongside your favorite Middle Eastern stews or curries to scoop up the delicious flavors.

Storage and Reheating

To store Markouk Bread, allow it to cool completely and then place it in an airtight container or ziplock bag. It can be stored at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

When ready to reheat, you can either wrap the bread in foil and warm it in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for a few minutes or reheat it directly on a skillet over low heat.

Conclusion

Markouk Bread is a versatile and delicious Middle Eastern flatbread that adds a unique touch to any meal. With its thin and elastic texture, it’s perfect for wrapping around your favorite fillings or dipping into flavorful sauces. By following this ultimate guide, you’ll be able to make your own homemade Markouk Bread and impress your friends and family with your culinary skills. So, next time you’re craving Middle Eastern flavors, give Markouk Bread a try and enjoy a taste of the Levant region.
