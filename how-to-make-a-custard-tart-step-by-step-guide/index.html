How to Make a Custard Tart: Step-by-Step Guide


A custard tart is one of those timeless desserts that transcends generations. Its sweet, creamy filling combined with a crisp, buttery crust is the epitome of comfort food. Whether you’re preparing it for a family gathering, a special occasion, or just because you’re in the mood for something indulgent, making a homemade custard tart can be a rewarding experience. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of crafting this delicious treat, from the basics of the pastry dough to the silky-smooth custard filling.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before we dive into the process of making your custard tart, let’s take a look at the ingredients required for both the crust and the custard filling.

For the Pastry Crust:

  • 250g all-purpose flour: This will form the base of your crust, giving it structure and a tender crumb.
  • 125g unsalted butter: Butter adds richness and flavor to the pastry dough.
  • 75g powdered sugar: For sweetness, as well as a finer texture to the dough.
  • 1 large egg yolk: Helps bind the dough and provides richness.
  • Pinch of salt: Balances the sweetness and enhances the flavors of the dough.
  • 2-3 tablespoons cold water: To bring the dough together, as needed.

For the Custard Filling:

  • 500ml whole milk: Full-fat milk will give the custard a rich and creamy texture.
  • 200ml heavy cream: This adds extra richness to the custard filling.
  • 6 large eggs: Eggs are the base of the custard filling, providing structure and a silky consistency.
  • 100g granulated sugar: Sweetens the custard without overpowering the delicate flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: Adds a warm, aromatic flavor to the custard.
  • Pinch of salt: Enhances the flavor of the custard.
  • Freshly grated nutmeg (optional): A classic spice that pairs beautifully with custard.

Step 1: Preparing the Pastry Dough

Making the Dough:

The first step in making your custard tart is preparing the pastry dough. Start by placing the flour and powdered sugar into a large mixing bowl. Using a fine sieve will help ensure that there are no lumps in your dry ingredients. Add the pinch of salt and mix these ingredients together.

Next, cut the unsalted butter into small cubes and add them to the dry ingredients. Use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour mixture, creating a breadcrumb-like texture. This technique, known as “cutting in,” is essential for achieving that flaky texture we all love in pastry.

Once the butter has been incorporated into the flour mixture, make a well in the center and add the egg yolk. Gently mix it into the flour and butter mixture. Gradually add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing with your hands until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough as this can lead to a tough crust.

Resting the Dough:

Once the dough has come together, form it into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out and less likely to shrink when baked.

Step 2: Rolling Out the Dough

After your dough has chilled, take it out of the fridge and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes. This makes it easier to roll out. On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough into a circle, aiming for about 3mm thickness. Ensure that the dough is large enough to line the bottom and sides of your tart pan.

Carefully transfer the dough into the tart pan, pressing it gently into the corners and edges. Trim off any excess dough using a sharp knife or pastry cutter. You can use the excess dough to patch any holes or imperfections.

Next, lightly prick the base of the tart shell with a fork. This helps prevent the crust from puffing up while baking. Cover the tart with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans to prevent the crust from shrinking or bubbling during the baking process.

Step 3: Blind Baking the Crust

Blind baking is a crucial step to ensure that the pastry remains crisp and doesn’t become soggy once the custard filling is added. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

Place the tart shell in the oven and bake it for about 15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. After 15 minutes, remove the tart from the oven, take out the parchment paper and pie weights, and return the tart shell to the oven for an additional 5-10 minutes. The crust should be golden brown and fully cooked. Once done, remove the tart shell from the oven and let it cool completely while you prepare the custard filling.

Step 4: Preparing the Custard Filling

The key to a perfect custard tart is the custard filling. This velvety filling is what makes this dessert so irresistible, and it’s surprisingly easy to prepare.

Making the Custard:

In a large saucepan, combine the milk and heavy cream. Warm the mixture over medium heat until it’s just about to come to a simmer. Stir occasionally to prevent the milk and cream from burning. Once it’s steaming, remove it from the heat.

While the milk and cream are heating, whisk together the eggs, sugar, and a pinch of salt in a separate bowl. Use a whisk to combine these ingredients until they are well blended and slightly frothy.

Next, very slowly pour the warm milk and cream mixture into the egg mixture while whisking constantly. This gradual incorporation is important to avoid curdling the eggs. Once the liquid has been fully combined with the egg mixture, return the mixture to the saucepan.

Cooking the Custard:

Place the saucepan back on low heat and continue stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or heat-resistant silicone spatula. You want to cook the custard gently, making sure that it doesn’t come to a boil, as this can cause the eggs to scramble. Continue stirring until the custard thickens to the consistency of heavy cream or slightly thicker. To test, dip the back of a spoon into the custard; it should coat the spoon and leave a clear line when you run your finger through it.

Once the custard has thickened, remove it from the heat. Stir in the vanilla extract and, if desired, a pinch of freshly grated nutmeg. This adds an extra layer of warmth to the custard. Allow the custard to cool slightly before pouring it into the baked pastry crust.

Step 5: Assembling the Custard Tart

With the pastry crust fully baked and the custard mixture ready, carefully pour the custard filling into the cooled tart shell. Pour slowly to avoid disturbing the delicate pastry crust. Fill it just below the edge of the crust, as the custard will expand slightly as it sets.

Use a spatula to smooth the top of the custard and ensure that it’s evenly distributed. The custard should sit perfectly within the tart shell, with no air pockets or uneven spots.

Step 6: Baking the Custard Tart

Bake the assembled tart in a preheated oven at 160°C (325°F) for about 25-30 minutes. You’ll know the tart is done when the custard is set but still slightly wobbly in the center. The top should have a light golden hue, and the custard should not jiggle too much when you gently shake the pan.

Once the tart is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. This step is crucial as it allows the custard to fully set. For best results, let the tart cool completely in the pan before transferring it to a serving platter.

Step 7: Serving Your Custard Tart

After the tart has cooled, it’s time to serve! Before serving, you may want to lightly dust the top with powdered sugar or a touch of freshly grated nutmeg for a final flourish. Slice the tart into wedges, serve it on a nice plate, and enjoy the deliciously rich flavors of your homemade custard tart.

Optional Garnishes:

While a classic custard tart is often left plain, you can get creative with your presentation. Consider adding a dollop of whipped cream or a few fresh berries like raspberries or blueberries for a pop of color and a burst of fresh flavor. You could also add a drizzle of caramel or a sprinkle of cinnamon for extra depth.

Tips for Success

  • Chill the dough: This is essential for achieving a tender, flaky crust. Don’t skip the resting time in the refrigerator.
  • Don’t overbake the custard: The custard should be set, but it will continue to firm up as it cools. If it overbakes, it can become rubbery.
  • Use full-fat milk and cream: For the richest, creamiest custard, always use whole milk and heavy cream. Skim or low-fat versions won’t provide the same luxurious texture.
  • Make ahead: The tart can be made a day or two ahead of time. Just store it in the fridge and bring it to room temperature before serving.

Conclusion

Making a custard tart from scratch is a rewarding and satisfying experience. With a buttery, flaky crust and a rich, creamy filling, this dessert is sure to impress family and friends. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a beginner, following these steps will lead you to a delicious result. Don’t be afraid to experiment with flavors or garnishes to make this tart your own. Enjoy the process and, of course, the delicious outcome!
