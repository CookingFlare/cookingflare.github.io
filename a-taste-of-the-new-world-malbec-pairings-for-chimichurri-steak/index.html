A Taste of the New World: Malbec Pairings for Chimichurri Steak


Argentina’s rich cultural heritage is deeply rooted in its love for food, wine, and music. One of the most iconic Argentine dishes is the grilled steak, often served with a tangy and herby chimichurri sauce. But what makes this dish truly exceptional is the pairing with a glass of Malbec, a varietal that has gained worldwide recognition for its bold flavors and smooth tannins.

In this post, we’ll explore the art of pairing Malbec with chimichurri steak, delving into the world of Argentine cuisine and the characteristics of this iconic grape variety.

The Birthplace of Malbec

Malbec, a black grape variety, originated in southwestern France, where it was once a key component of the Bordeaux blend. However, it was in Argentina that Malbec found its true calling, where it thrived in the country’s fertile soil and favorable climate. The first Malbec vines were planted in the 19th century, and since then, Argentina has become synonymous with this varietal.

The Flavors of Malbec

Malbec is known for its deep, rich flavors, which are often described as plum, blackberry, and black cherry. The wine is also characterized by its smooth, velvety texture and moderate tannins, making it an excellent match for bold, savory flavors.

Chimichurri Steak

Chimichurri steak is a classic Argentine dish that consists of grilled steak served with a tangy and herby chimichurri sauce. The sauce is made with parsley, oregano, garlic, red pepper flakes, red wine vinegar, and olive oil, which gives it a bright, citrusy flavor.

Pairing Malbec with Chimichurri Steak

When pairing Malbec with chimichurri steak, the key is to find a balance between the bold flavors of the wine and the tangy, herby flavors of the sauce. A Malbec with moderate tannins and a smooth texture is ideal for this pairing, as it can stand up to the bold flavors of the steak and the sauce.

Here are a few tips for pairing Malbec with chimichurri steak:

  • Look for a Malbec with a moderate level of acidity, as this will help cut through the richness of the steak and the sauce.
  • Choose a Malbec with a smooth, velvety texture, as this will complement the bold flavors of the steak and the sauce.
  • Consider a Malbec with a touch of oak, as this will add complexity and depth to the pairing.

Some popular Malbecs that pair well with chimichurri steak include:

  • Catena Malbec: This Malbec is known for its bold flavors and smooth tannins, making it an excellent match for chimichurri steak.
  • Trapiche Malbec: This Malbec is characterized by its moderate acidity and smooth texture, making it an excellent choice for pairing with chimichurri steak.
  • Doña Paula Malbec: This Malbec is known for its bold flavors and moderate tannins, making it an excellent match for chimichurri steak.

The Art of Pairing

Pairing wine with food is an art that requires attention to detail and a deep understanding of the flavors and characteristics of both the wine and the food. When pairing Malbec with chimichurri steak, the key is to find a balance between the bold flavors of the wine and the tangy, herby flavors of the sauce.

Here are a few tips for pairing wine with food:

  • Consider the flavors and characteristics of the wine and the food, and look for a balance between the two.
  • Pay attention to the textures and temperatures of the wine and the food, and ensure that they complement each other.
  • Don’t be afraid to experiment and try new pairings, as this is the best way to develop your palate and find new favorite pairings.

In conclusion, pairing Malbec with chimichurri steak is a match made in heaven. The bold flavors of the wine complement the tangy, herby flavors of the sauce, while the smooth texture of the wine complements the bold flavors of the steak. Whether you’re a wine enthusiast or a foodie, this pairing is sure to delight. So the next time you’re cooking up a storm in the kitchen, be sure to grab a bottle of Malbec and experience the true taste of the new world.
