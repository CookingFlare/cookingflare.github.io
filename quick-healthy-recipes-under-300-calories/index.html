Quick Healthy Recipes Under 300 Calories


When you’re embarking on a journey towards healthier eating, finding recipes that are both quick to prepare and low in calories might seem like a challenge. However, a delicious, satisfying, and nutrient-packed meal doesn’t have to be time-consuming or calorie-heavy. Below, you’ll find a collection of wholesome recipes, all under 300 calories, that can fit into your busy schedule while supporting your health goals.

1. Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

This refreshing salad is perfect for a light lunch or as a side dish. Packed with plant-based protein, fiber, and vibrant flavors, it’s as nourishing as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup canned chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine chickpeas, cucumber, tomato, red onion, and parsley.
  2. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice over the salad. Toss gently to coat the ingredients evenly.
  3. Add salt and black pepper to taste.
  4. Serve immediately or chill in the fridge for a more refreshing flavor.

Calories per serving: ~280 calories

2. Spicy Garlic Shrimp and Zoodles

This dish is for those who crave something light yet full of zest. The zucchini noodles (zoodles) bring a low-carb twist, while the shrimp adds lean protein.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium zucchini, spiralized
  • 8 oz shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 lemon, juiced

Directions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and chili flakes, and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Add shrimp to the skillet, cooking for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque. Season with salt, black pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Remove shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the spiralized zucchini and toss for 1-2 minutes until slightly tender.
  4. Serve the shrimp over the zoodles for a flavor-packed, low-calorie meal.

Calories per serving: ~230 calories

3. Creamy Avocado and Greek Yogurt Dip with Veggie Sticks

This creamy dip doubles as a great healthy snack or appetizer. The combination of avocado and Greek yogurt is both rich and nourishing, paired perfectly with crunchy vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small avocado
  • 1/4 cup plain non-fat Greek yogurt
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Assorted veggie sticks (carrots, celery, cucumber, or bell peppers)

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, mash the avocado until smooth.
  2. Mix the Greek yogurt, garlic, lime juice, salt, and black pepper into the avocado until combined.
  3. Serve the dip alongside an assortment of fresh veggie sticks for dipping.

Calories per serving: ~150 calories

4. Herb-Crusted Baked Chicken Thighs

When you need a quick and satisfying protein-packed meal, these baked chicken thighs are an excellent option. The aromatic herbs enhance the flavor without adding extra calories.

Ingredients:

  • 2 small boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp dried basil
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rub the chicken thighs with olive oil, then sprinkle garlic powder, basil, oregano, salt, and black pepper over both sides.
  3. Place the chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until cooked through.
  4. Serve with a side of steamed veggies or a fresh salad.

Calories per serving: ~220 calories

5. Berry and Almond Protein Smoothie

A smoothie is a fantastic option for a quick breakfast or post-workout snack. This version is loaded with antioxidants, protein, and healthy fats to keep you energized.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup frozen mixed berries
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • 1 tsp almond butter
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup ice

Directions:

  1. Add all the ingredients to a blender and blend until smooth.
  2. Pour into a glass and enjoy immediately.

Calories per serving: ~190 calories

6. Cauliflower Fried Rice

Transform plain cauliflower into a flavorful, low-calorie alternative to fried rice. This quick meal satisfies cravings while keeping your calorie count in check.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cauliflower rice
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots blend
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tsp soy sauce (low sodium)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add garlic, cooking until fragrant.
  2. Add the cauliflower rice and frozen peas and carrots. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until tender.
  3. Push the veggies to the side of the skillet. Pour the beaten egg into the empty side, scramble it, and then mix it with the vegetables.
  4. Stir in soy sauce, adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, and serve.

Calories per serving: ~180 calories

7. Lemon Garlic Baked Cod

This tender and flaky baked cod is loaded with protein, omega-3 fatty acids, and a zesty lemon-garlic flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fillet cod (4-5 oz)
  • 1/2 lemon, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the cod fillet on a piece of parchment paper. Drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle garlic, salt, and black pepper on top.
  3. Arrange lemon slices over the fish and wrap the parchment paper to form a sealed packet.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.

Calories per serving: ~200 calories

8. Tomato and Basil Egg White Omelette

Start your day right with this nutrient-rich omelette. It’s light yet filling, making it perfect for breakfast or even a quick dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 medium tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp olive oil

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat.
  2. Pour the egg whites into the pan and cook until partially set.
  3. Add tomatoes and basil evenly over one half of the omelette. Season with black pepper.
  4. Fold the omelette in half, cook for another minute, and serve warm.

Calories per serving: ~150 calories

Eating healthy on a busy schedule doesn’t have to be bland or complicated. These quick and easy recipes prove that you can enjoy satisfying meals under 300 calories without sacrificing flavor or nutrition. Whip one of these up the next time you’re short on time but craving something delicious—and your body will thank you!
