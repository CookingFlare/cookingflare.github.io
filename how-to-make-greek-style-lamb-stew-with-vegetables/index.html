How to Make Greek Style Lamb Stew with Vegetables


Greek cuisine is celebrated for its vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and hearty meals that bring people together. One dish that embodies these qualities is the Greek-style lamb stew with vegetables. This comforting and aromatic stew is perfect for family gatherings or a cozy night in. In this blog post, we’ll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to make this delicious dish, ensuring you understand every aspect of the cooking process.

Ingredients

To create a flavorful Greek-style lamb stew, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Stew:

  • 2 lbs of lamb shoulder (cut into 1.5-inch pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 4 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 3 large carrots (sliced)
  • 2 medium potatoes (peeled and diced)
  • 1 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced)
  • 1 can (14 oz) of diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 2 cups of chicken or beef broth
  • 1 cup of red wine (optional, but adds depth of flavor)
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish)

For the Marinade (optional but recommended):

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Preparing the Lamb

Marinating the Lamb

While marinating is optional, it enhances the flavor of the lamb and makes it tender. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Combine the Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Coat the Lamb: Add the lamb pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight for maximum flavor.

Browning the Lamb

Once marinated, it’s time to brown the lamb:

  1. Heat Olive Oil: In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Brown the Lamb: Add the marinated lamb pieces in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Brown the meat on all sides for about 5-7 minutes, then remove it from the pot and set it aside.

Building the Stew

With the lamb browned and set aside, it’s time to build the flavors of your stew:

Sautéing the Aromatics

  1. Onions and Garlic: In the same pot, add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent (about 5 minutes). Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, ensuring it doesn’t burn.

Adding the Vegetables

  1. Carrots, Potatoes, and Bell Pepper: Add the sliced carrots, diced potatoes, and chopped bell pepper to the pot. Stir and cook for about 5 minutes to soften the vegetables slightly.
  2. Zucchini: Finally, add the sliced zucchini and give everything a good stir.

Incorporating the Lamb Back to the Pot

  1. Return the Lamb: Place the browned lamb back into the pot with the sautéed vegetables.
  2. Add Tomatoes and Broth: Pour in the can of diced tomatoes (with juice) and the broth. If you’re using red wine, now is the time to add it. This will deglaze the pot, lifting all those delicious browned bits from the bottom.

Seasoning the Stew

  1. Herbs and Spices: Stir in the dried oregano, ground cinnamon, salt, and black pepper. These spices are quintessential in Greek cooking, adding warmth and depth to the dish.
  2. Bring to a Simmer: Increase the heat until the stew begins to simmer, then reduce the heat to low.

Cooking the Stew

  1. Cover and Cook: Cover the pot with a lid and let the stew cook on low heat for about 1.5 to 2 hours. Stir occasionally and check to ensure it doesn’t stick to the bottom. The lamb should become tender and the flavors well integrated.
  2. Adjust Consistency: If the stew is too thick, add a bit more broth or water. If it’s too thin, leave the lid off and let it simmer for another 15-20 minutes.

Final Touches

After the cooking time is up, you’ll want to taste the stew and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add more salt, pepper, or herbs based on your preference.

Garnishing

When ready to serve, ladle the stew into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. This not only adds a pop of color but also enhances the dish’s fresh flavor profile.

Serving Suggestions

Greek-style lamb stew is a hearty meal on its own, but you can elevate your dining experience with a few simple sides:

  • Crusty Bread: Serve with a loaf of crusty bread to soak up the rich broth.
  • Rice or Couscous: A bed of rice or couscous can complement the stew beautifully.
  • Greek Salad: A refreshing Greek salad with feta, olives, and cucumbers provides a crunchy contrast to the warm stew.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers, this stew keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors deepen as it sits, making it even more delicious the next day. To reheat, simply warm it on the stove over low heat, adding a splash of broth or water if needed to loosen the consistency.

Conclusion

Making Greek-style lamb stew with vegetables is a rewarding cooking experience that yields a delicious and comforting dish. The combination of tender lamb, hearty vegetables, and aromatic spices creates a symphony of flavors that will transport you to the Mediterranean. Whether you’re preparing a special meal for loved ones or simply indulging in a weeknight feast, this stew is sure to impress. Enjoy the process, savor the flavors, and most importantly, share it with the people you love. Happy cooking!
