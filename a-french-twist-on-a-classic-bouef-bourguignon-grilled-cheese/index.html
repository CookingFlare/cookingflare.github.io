A French Twist on a Classic: Bouef Bourguignon Grilled Cheese


The humble grilled cheese sandwich. It’s a comfort food staple that never goes out of style. But why settle for the ordinary when you can elevate it to new heights with a French twist? Enter: Bouef Bourguignon Grilled Cheese.

For those unfamiliar, Bouef Bourguignon is a classic French dish originating from the Burgundy region. It’s a hearty beef stew cooked in red wine, onions, and mushrooms, resulting in tender, fall-apart meat and a rich, flavorful sauce. So, what happens when you take this iconic dish and turn it into a grilled cheese? Magic, that’s what.

The key to this recipe is using high-quality ingredients, particularly the cheese. You’ll want a blend that melts beautifully and has a rich, creamy texture. I recommend using a combination of Gruyère and Comté, two French cheeses that are perfect for melting. Add in some caramelized onions, sautéed mushrooms, and tender beef, and you’ve got a sandwich that’s sure to impress.

But what really sets this grilled cheese apart is the addition of the Bouef Bourguignon sauce. By using the same sauce from the original dish, you’re adding a depth of flavor that takes this sandwich from ordinary to extraordinary. The tangy, slightly sweet sauce complements the cheese and beef perfectly, creating a flavor profile that’s both familiar and innovative.

So, how do you make this culinary masterpiece? It’s surprisingly simple. Start by caramelizing some onions in butter until they’re sweet and golden. Add in some sautéed mushrooms and cook until they’re tender. Then, add in some cooked beef (you can use leftover or cook some specifically for this recipe) and stir to combine.

Next, butter two slices of bread and place them in a skillet over medium heat. Add a slice of cheese to each bread slice, followed by a spoonful of the beef and onion mixture. Top with another slice of cheese and place the other bread slice on top.

Cook for about 2-3 minutes on the first side, or until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melted. Flip the sandwich over and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the other side is also golden brown.

While the sandwich is cooking, warm up the Bouef Bourguignon sauce by simmering it on the stovetop or in the microwave. Once the sandwich is cooked, place it on a plate and spoon some of the warm sauce over the top.

And there you have it – a French twist on a classic that’s sure to become a new favorite. The combination of tender beef, caramelized onions, sautéed mushrooms, and melted cheese, all held together by a rich and tangy Bouef Bourguignon sauce, is a game-changer. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the magic of this French-inspired grilled cheese for yourself.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of bread
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 medium onion, caramelized
  • 2 cups of mushrooms, sautéed
  • 1 pound of cooked beef, sliced
  • 2 cups of grated Gruyère and Comté cheese
  • 1 cup of Bouef Bourguignon sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Caramelize the onions in butter until they’re sweet and golden.
  2. Add the sautéed mushrooms and cooked beef to the onions and stir to combine.
  3. Butter two slices of bread and place them in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add a slice of cheese to each bread slice, followed by a spoonful of the beef and onion mixture.
  5. Top with another slice of cheese and place the other bread slice on top.
  6. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on the first side, or until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  7. Flip the sandwich over and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the other side is also golden brown.
  8. Warm up the Bouef Bourguignon sauce and spoon some over the top of the sandwich.
  9. Serve immediately and enjoy!
