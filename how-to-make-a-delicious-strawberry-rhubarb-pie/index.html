How to Make a Delicious Strawberry Rhubarb Pie


As the seasons change and spring arrives, the sweet aroma of fresh strawberries and rhubarb fills the air, beckoning us to create a delectable pie that embodies the essence of the season. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of crafting a mouthwatering strawberry rhubarb pie that’s sure to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

For the Crust

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water

For the Filling

  • 3 cups sliced strawberries
  • 2 cups sliced rhubarb
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

Make the Crust

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish and trim the edges to fit.

Prepare the Filling

  1. In a separate large mixing bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and rhubarb. Add the granulated sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Toss gently until the fruit is evenly coated with the dry ingredients.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Pour the mixture over the fruit and toss until everything is well combined.

Assemble and Bake the Pie

  1. Pour the fruit filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly to the edges.
  2. Roll out the remaining dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a pastry cutter or a knife to cut the dough into strips for a lattice-top crust.
  3. Place the lattice strips on top of the filling, weaving them into a lattice pattern. Trim the edges of the strips and press the edges of the lattice to seal.
  4. Brush the lattice top with a little bit of milk or beaten egg for a golden glaze.
  5. Bake the pie for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  6. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • To ensure the crust doesn’t shrink during baking, make sure to chill it for at least 30 minutes before filling and baking.
  • If you prefer a more intense flavor, you can add a splash of Grand Marnier or Cognac to the filling.
  • Consider using a combination of sugar and honey to sweeten the filling for a unique flavor profile.
  • For a more rustic appearance, you can leave the edges of the lattice untrimmed and slightly rough.

Conclusion

With this recipe, you’ll be able to create a show-stopping strawberry rhubarb pie that’s sure to impress your family and friends. The combination of sweet and tangy flavors, along with the flaky crust, makes this pie a true masterpiece. So go ahead, get baking, and indulge in the sweet taste of spring!
