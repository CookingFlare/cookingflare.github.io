How to Make Korean Pajeon (Scallion Pancakes) from Scratch


If you’re craving something savory, crispy, and utterly satisfying, Korean pajeon—traditional scallion pancakes—are an excellent dish to add to your next cooking adventure. This beloved dish combines the earthy flavor of scallions, the subtle zest of freshly mixed batter, and the delightful crunch that makes it a household favorite in Korean cuisine. Whether it’s a rainy day comfort food or a party appetizer, pajeon offers versatility and a bold taste in every bite.

Let’s dive into the process of making authentic Korean pajeon entirely from scratch. With simple ingredients and easy-to-follow instructions, you’ll master this classic recipe in no time.

Ingredients

Before we begin, make sure you’ve gathered all the necessary ingredients for the batter, filling, and dipping sauce. Everything can be found at your local Asian grocery store or pantry.

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup rice flour (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • 1½ cups water
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Optional Add-ons:

  • 1 egg (creates a richer texture)

For the Filling:

  • 1 bunch scallions, trimmed and cut into 2-3 inch lengths
  • Vegetables (optional): julienned carrots, zucchini, or onion slices
  • Proteins (optional): shrimp, squid, or thin slices of pork belly

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • ½ teaspoon sesame oil
  • ½ teaspoon Korean chili flakes (gochugaru) (optional, for spice)
  • ½ teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds (optional)
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Batter

The base for any good pajeon begins with a reliable batter that’s smooth, light, and versatile. Start by mixing the all-purpose flour, rice flour, and salt in a large bowl. Gradually pour in the water while whisking the mixture until you achieve a lump-free consistency. If you prefer a richer pancake, crack in an egg and incorporate it into the batter—though this step is optional, it adds a slightly softer texture.

The finished batter should have a pourable consistency, similar to thick cream. Avoid overmixing, as gluten development can make the pancakes dense instead of airy.

2. Prep the Filling

Scallions are the star of the show when it comes to pajeon! Wash the scallions thoroughly and pat them dry with paper towels. Trim off the roots and wilted tips, leaving only the vibrant green and white stalks. Slice them into pieces about 2-3 inches long.

If you’re opting to include additional vegetables like carrots or zucchini, julienne them into thin, matchstick-sized pieces. Protein lovers can prepare shrimp, squid, or pork belly by slicing them thinly for quick cooking.

3. Make the Dipping Sauce

A flavorful dipping sauce elevates the pajeon experience. In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, chili flakes, sugar, and sesame seeds. Stir well to dissolve the sugar completely. Add finely chopped green onions for an extra kick and set it aside.

4. Heat Your Pan

To achieve crispy, golden pancakes, you’ll need the right cooking technique. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add 1-2 tablespoons of your preferred cooking oil (vegetable, canola, or sesame oil works well). Swirl the oil to coat the surface evenly.

5. Build and Cook the Pajeon

Once your skillet is hot, it’s time to assemble the pancake. Pour a thin layer of batter into the pan, just enough to cover the surface in a circular shape. Arrange the scallions and any optional add-ons evenly over the batter. Pour another layer of batter over the filling until everything is lightly coated.

Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy. Carefully flip the pancake using a wide spatula and cook for another 3-4 minutes on the other side. Adjust the heat as needed to avoid burning while ensuring a crunchy texture.

For thicker pancakes, lower the heat slightly and cook for an additional minute per side to ensure the filling is thoroughly cooked.

6. Serve and Enjoy

Once your pajeon is beautifully golden and crisp on both sides, remove it from the pan and transfer it to a cutting board. Slice the pancake into bite-sized pieces and serve it alongside the dipping sauce. The contrast between the crunchy pancake and the tangy sauce is simply irresistible.

Tips for Perfect Pajeon

  • Use cold water for the batter: Cold water slows gluten formation, resulting in a light and crispy texture for your pancake.
  • Don’t skimp on oil: The signature crunch of pajeon comes from frying in sufficient oil, so don’t hold back if you want perfectly crisp edges.
  • Experiment with fillings: While scallions are traditional, you can customize the filling with ingredients like kimchi, mushrooms, or even cheese for a fusion twist.
  • Cook in batches: If your pan isn’t large enough, cook smaller pancakes instead of overcrowding the skillet. This ensures even cooking and consistent results.

Pairing Suggestions

Pajeon is excellent on its own, but it becomes truly magical when paired with other Korean dishes. Serve your scallion pancakes with a bowl of hot kimchi jjigae (kimchi stew) or spicy tteokbokki (stir-fried rice cakes). Add a refreshing Korean radish salad or accompany it with makgeolli, Korea’s nostalgic rice wine, for the full experience.

Wrapping It Up

Making Korean pajeon from scratch is a joyous, hands-on culinary experience. The dish’s simplicity allows the fresh ingredients to shine, while the crunchy texture and flavorful dipping sauce create a harmony of tastes that’s hard to resist. Whether you’re introducing yourself to Korean cuisine or perfecting your skills, this recipe delivers every time.

So grab your ingredients, heat up your skillet, and treat yourself to the irresistible flavors of homemade Korean scallion pancakes.
