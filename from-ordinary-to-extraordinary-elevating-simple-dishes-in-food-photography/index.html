From Ordinary to Extraordinary: Elevating Simple Dishes in Food Photography


As food lovers, we are constantly bombarded with stunning images of beautifully plated dishes on social media platforms. It’s hard not to feel inspired and motivated to recreate those visually appealing meals in our own kitchens. However, achieving such extraordinary results can sometimes seem daunting, especially if you’re just starting out in food photography. But fear not! In this blog post, we will explore simple techniques to elevate your food photography and turn ordinary dishes into extraordinary masterpieces.

1. The Art of Plating

Plating is an essential aspect of food photography. It’s not just about arranging the food on a plate; it’s about creating a visually pleasing composition that tells a story. Here are some tips to take your plating skills to the next level:

  • Colorful Accents: Incorporate vibrant and contrasting colors to make your dish pop. Think about using fresh herbs, edible flowers, or colorful sauces to add that extra visual interest.

  • Textural Contrasts: Play with different textures to add depth to your dish. Consider adding crunchy elements, creamy sauces, or even a sprinkle of sea salt to create a sensory experience for the viewer.

  • Negative Space: Don’t overcrowd the plate. Embrace negative space to give your dish room to breathe. This will allow the focus to remain on the main components and create a sense of elegance.

2. Lighting for Impact

Lighting is a crucial factor in food photography, as it can make or break your shot. Here are some lighting techniques to enhance your food photography:

  • Natural Light: Whenever possible, utilize natural light to capture your dishes. Position your setup near a window or shoot outdoors during the golden hour for soft, flattering light that brings out the natural colors and textures of the food.

  • Diffuse Light: If natural light is limited, use diffusers or light modifiers to soften harsh lighting. This will help reduce shadows and create a more even illumination on your subject.

  • Backlighting: Experiment with backlighting to add depth and dimension to your images. By placing the light source behind your dish, you can create a beautiful glow that highlights the textures and contours of the food.

3. Composition and Props

Composition plays a vital role in food photography, helping to guide the viewer’s eye and create a visually appealing image. Here are some composition and prop tips to enhance your food photography:

  • Rule of Thirds: Divide your frame into a 3x3 grid and position your main subject on one of the intersecting points. This will create a visually balanced and pleasing composition.

  • Leading Lines: Utilize leading lines, such as utensils or table edges, to guide the viewer’s eye towards the main subject. This adds a sense of movement and depth to your image.

  • Props and Backgrounds: Choose props and backgrounds that complement the dish and tell a story. Consider using rustic wooden boards, vintage cutlery, or colorful textiles to add interest and create a visually captivating scene.

4. Post-Processing Magic

Post-processing is the final step in elevating your food photography. Here are some editing techniques to enhance your images:

  • Color Correction: Adjust the white balance and color tones to ensure the dish looks appetizing and true to life.

  • Contrast and Clarity: Enhance the contrast and clarity to make the dish stand out and add definition to the textures.

  • Selective Editing: Use selective editing tools to draw attention to specific areas of the dish. This can be done by adjusting exposure, saturation, or sharpness on certain elements.

In conclusion, food photography is an art form that allows us to elevate simple dishes and transform them into extraordinary visual experiences. By mastering the art of plating, understanding lighting techniques, utilizing composition and props effectively, and applying post-processing magic, you can create stunning food photographs that capture the essence and beauty of your culinary creations. So, grab your camera, experiment with these techniques, and let your creativity shine through your lens. Happy shooting!
