Delectable Thanksgiving Leftover Spring Roll Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for family, friends, and a delicious feast. After the holiday, you’re left with a mountain of leftovers, but don’t let them go to waste. Instead, get creative and turn them into a tasty spring roll dish that’s perfect for a post-Thanksgiving dinner or a quick snack.

Turkey and Stuffing Spring Rolls

These spring rolls are a twist on the classic turkey sandwich. By wrapping shredded turkey and stuffing in a crispy spring roll wrapper, you’ll create a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shredded turkey
  • 1/2 cup stuffing
  • 1/4 cup chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 package spring roll wrappers
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded turkey, stuffing, celery, scallions, and garlic.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and olive oil. Pour the mixture over the turkey mixture and toss until everything is well coated.
  3. Lay a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface. Place about 1/4 cup of the turkey mixture in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the bottom half of the wrapper over the filling, then fold in the sides and roll up the wrapper to form a tight cylinder. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  5. Heat about 1 inch of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add the spring rolls and cook until they’re golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes on each side. Drain on paper towels.

Mashed Potato and Cranberry Spring Rolls

These spring rolls are a sweet and savory twist on the classic mashed potato dish. By adding cranberries and a hint of cinnamon, you’ll create a flavor combination that’s perfect for the fall season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup cranberries, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 package spring roll wrappers
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, cranberries, scallions, and garlic.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and olive oil. Pour the mixture over the potato mixture and toss until everything is well coated.
  3. Lay a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface. Place about 1/4 cup of the potato mixture in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the bottom half of the wrapper over the filling, then fold in the sides and roll up the wrapper to form a tight cylinder. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  5. Heat about 1 inch of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add the spring rolls and cook until they’re golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes on each side. Drain on paper towels.

Green Bean and Cream Cheese Spring Rolls

These spring rolls are a creamy and crunchy twist on the classic green bean casserole. By wrapping blanched green beans and cream cheese in a crispy spring roll wrapper, you’ll create a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup blanched green beans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 package spring roll wrappers
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chopped green beans, cream cheese, scallions, and garlic.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and olive oil. Pour the mixture over the green bean mixture and toss until everything is well coated.
  3. Lay a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface. Place about 1/4 cup of the green bean mixture in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the bottom half of the wrapper over the filling, then fold in the sides and roll up the wrapper to form a tight cylinder. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  5. Heat about 1 inch of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add the spring rolls and cook until they’re golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes on each side. Drain on paper towels.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor to your spring rolls, try adding some chopped fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro.
  • If you want to make your spring rolls a little crunchier, try dipping them in some beaten eggs and then coating them in some breadcrumbs before frying.
  • You can also add some diced turkey or chicken to the filling for added protein.
  • If you want to make your spring rolls a little sweeter, try adding some chopped dried cranberries or cherries to the filling.

By following these recipes, you’ll be able to turn your Thanksgiving leftovers into a delicious and unique spring roll dish that’s perfect for a post-holiday dinner or a quick snack. So next time you’re facing a mountain of leftovers, don’t be afraid to get creative and try something new. Happy cooking!
