The Best Thai Stir-Fried Noodles: Recipes to Try


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and the perfect balance of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy. Among the many delightful dishes that Thai cooking has to offer, stir-fried noodles stand out as a favorite for many. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, making Thai stir-fried noodles at home can be a rewarding experience. In this post, we will explore the best Thai stir-fried noodle recipes, tips for perfecting your dish, and the essential ingredients that make these recipes shine.

Understanding Thai Stir-Fried Noodles

Before diving into the recipes, it’s essential to understand what makes Thai stir-fried noodles unique. The key components of these dishes include:

  • Noodles: The type of noodle used can vary, with popular choices being rice noodles, egg noodles, or even glass noodles. Each type brings its own texture and flavor to the dish.
  • Sauce: A well-balanced sauce is crucial. Common ingredients include soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, and tamarind paste, often combined with sugar and lime juice for that signature Thai taste.
  • Vegetables: Fresh vegetables add color, crunch, and nutrition. Common choices include bell peppers, carrots, bean sprouts, and green onions.
  • Protein: Chicken, shrimp, tofu, or beef are popular protein options that can be added to make the dish more filling.
  • Herbs and Spices: Fresh herbs like cilantro and basil, along with spices like chili flakes or fresh chilies, elevate the dish’s flavor profile.

Now that we have a basic understanding of what goes into Thai stir-fried noodles, let’s explore some of the best recipes you can try at home.

1. Pad Thai: The Classic Thai Stir-Fried Noodles

Ingredients

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined (or chicken/tofu)
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • Lime wedges for serving

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes or until they are soft. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix together the fish sauce, soy sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, and chili powder. Set aside.

  3. Stir-Fry: Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  4. Add Protein: Add the shrimp (or chicken/tofu) and cook until they are just cooked through, about 2-3 minutes.

  5. Scramble the Eggs: Push the shrimp to one side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs into the other side. Scramble until just set.

  6. Combine: Add the soaked noodles and sauce to the pan. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated and heated through.

  7. Finish with Vegetables: Stir in the bean sprouts and green onions, cooking for an additional minute.

  8. Serve: Plate the Pad Thai and top with crushed peanuts and lime wedges. Enjoy your homemade classic!

2. Drunken Noodles (Pad Kee Mao)

Ingredients

  • 8 oz wide rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken, sliced (or shrimp/tofu)
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced

  • 1/4 cup basil leaves

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili paste (adjust to taste)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the wide rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes until soft. Drain and set aside.
