Delicious Thanksgiving Veggie Dishes to Enjoy


Thanksgiving is a time of gratitude, gathering, and, of course, indulgence. While the turkey often takes the spotlight, the vegetable side dishes are equally deserving of attention. These veggie dishes are not only packed with flavor, but they also provide the perfect balance to a hearty Thanksgiving spread. Whether you are a vegetarian, looking to reduce your meat intake, or simply want to add more color and nutrition to your plate, these delicious Thanksgiving veggie dishes will surely delight your guests and elevate your festive meal.

1. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Garlic and Balsamic Glaze

Brussels sprouts are a classic fall vegetable that can easily become a Thanksgiving favorite. When roasted, they become crispy on the outside while staying tender on the inside. To make them even more irresistible, toss them with minced garlic and a drizzle of balsamic glaze. The sweetness of the balsamic complements the earthiness of the sprouts, creating a harmonious flavor that will have everyone reaching for more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are golden brown and crispy.
  5. In a small pan, sauté the garlic in a bit of olive oil until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  6. Drizzle the roasted Brussels sprouts with balsamic vinegar and the sautéed garlic.
  7. Toss to coat and serve hot.

This dish pairs perfectly with mashed potatoes or stuffing and adds a burst of flavor to your Thanksgiving table.

2. Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Topping

Sweet potatoes are a quintessential Thanksgiving side dish, and this sweet potato casserole with a crunchy pecan topping is sure to steal the show. The natural sweetness of the potatoes is complemented by the rich, buttery crunch of the pecans, making it a dish that offers both savory and sweet notes. This casserole is also a great way to include more vegetables in your meal while keeping things indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted (for topping)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in a large pot of water until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and mash them.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, brown sugar, melted butter, milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth.
  4. Transfer the sweet potato mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the pecans, flour, melted butter, and brown sugar. Sprinkle this topping evenly over the sweet potato mixture.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve warm.

This dish is a wonderful mix of flavors and textures, making it a perfect choice for Thanksgiving. Plus, the pecan topping gives it an extra touch of elegance.

3. Green Bean Almondine

Green beans are a classic vegetable side that pairs well with almost any Thanksgiving dish. Green bean almondine takes this simple veggie to the next level with the addition of toasted almonds and a touch of lemon. The crisp-tender green beans, nutty almonds, and bright lemon flavor create a dish that is both sophisticated and easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Steam the green beans for 5-7 minutes until they are tender but still crisp. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Add the sliced almonds to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the almonds are golden brown.
  4. Add the green beans to the skillet and toss to coat in the butter and almonds.
  5. Stir in the lemon juice and zest, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve warm.

Green bean almondine is a light and flavorful option that adds a refreshing contrast to richer dishes like mashed potatoes and gravy.

4. Stuffed Acorn Squash

Acorn squash is an excellent autumn vegetable, and when stuffed with a savory filling, it becomes an impressive and hearty side dish. This recipe combines quinoa, cranberries, and roasted vegetables for a dish that is both nutritious and delicious. It’s the perfect way to highlight the flavors of fall and make your Thanksgiving spread a bit more vibrant.

Ingredients:

  • 4 acorn squash, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender.
  3. While the squash is roasting, cook the quinoa according to package instructions using vegetable broth instead of water for extra flavor.
  4. In a large pan, sauté the onion, carrots, and celery in olive oil until softened, about 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, and chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Once the squash halves are cooked, stuff each one with the quinoa mixture.
  7. Return the stuffed squash to the oven for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until heated through.
  8. Serve warm.

The sweetness of the acorn squash and cranberries pairs beautifully with the savory quinoa filling, making this a perfect vegetarian Thanksgiving dish.

5. Maple-Glazed Carrots

Carrots are a simple and nutritious vegetable that can easily be dressed up for Thanksgiving. Maple-glazed carrots are sweet, tender, and savory all at once, thanks to the rich, caramel-like syrup that coats them. The natural sweetness of the carrots combined with the earthy flavor of thyme creates a balance that will complement any Thanksgiving entrée.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb baby carrots, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, boil the carrots in water until tender, about 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Add the maple syrup and thyme to the skillet, stirring to combine.
  4. Add the cooked carrots to the skillet and toss to coat them in the glaze.
  5. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the glaze has thickened and the carrots are nicely coated.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve.

These maple-glazed carrots are a simple yet elegant addition to your Thanksgiving meal. Their natural sweetness and vibrant color make them a showstopper on any holiday table.

6. Butternut Squash Soup

Nothing says fall quite like a warm bowl of butternut squash soup. This creamy soup is velvety smooth, lightly spiced, and incredibly comforting. Whether served as a starter or enjoyed as a main dish, butternut squash soup is a perfect choice for Thanksgiving. It’s also a great way to incorporate more vegetables into your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast on a baking sheet for 25-30 minutes, or until tender.
  3. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the roasted squash, vegetable broth, ginger, and cinnamon to the pot. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, or transfer it to a blender in batches.
  6. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve warm, garnished with a sprinkle of cinnamon or a drizzle of coconut milk.

This butternut squash soup is rich, creamy, and comforting. It’s a wonderful way to start your Thanksgiving meal and set the tone for the rest of the feast.

7. Cauliflower Gratin

For those who love a creamy, cheesy side dish, cauliflower gratin is a fantastic option. This dish takes cauliflower to the next level by baking it in a luscious cheese sauce and topping it with a crispy breadcrumb crust. It’s indulgent, comforting, and a great alternative to traditional mashed potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Steam the cauliflower florets until tender, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to make a roux.
  4. Slowly whisk in the milk and cook until the sauce thickens, about 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the shredded Gruyère cheese until melted and smooth.
  6. In a baking dish, combine the steamed cauliflower and cheese sauce. Top with breadcrumbs.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbling.
  8. Serve hot.

Cauliflower gratin is a rich, comforting dish that’s sure to be a hit with your Thanksgiving guests. It offers a cheesy, creamy alternative to traditional potato-based dishes.

Conclusion

Thanksgiving is a time to indulge in delicious food, and these veggie dishes are the perfect addition to any holiday table. From the earthy flavors of Brussels sprouts to the comforting sweetness of sweet potato casserole, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you’re looking for savory, sweet, or creamy options, these veggie dishes will complement your Thanksgiving feast and add a burst of flavor to the celebration.

Don’t forget to incorporate plenty of seasonal vegetables into your Thanksgiving meal, and experiment with different flavors and textures to keep things exciting. With these delightful veggie dishes, your Thanksgiving will be a feast to remember!
