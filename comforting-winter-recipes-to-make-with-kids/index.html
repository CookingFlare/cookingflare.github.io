Comforting Winter Recipes to Make with Kids


Winter is the perfect season for cozying up in the kitchen, and what better way to bond with your little ones than by whipping up some comforting recipes together? Cooking with children not only teaches them valuable skills, but it also creates lasting memories filled with laughter and delicious smells wafting through the house. In this post, we will explore a variety of comforting winter recipes that are fun and easy to make with kids. From hearty soups to sweet treats, these recipes will warm your hearts and bellies during the chilly months ahead.

1. Hearty Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups diced potatoes
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Ingredients: Let the kids help wash and peel the vegetables. They can learn to chop soft veggies like carrots and celery with a safe knife.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5 minutes until they start to soften.

  3. Add Garlic and Seasoning: Stir in the minced garlic and dried thyme. This is a great moment to teach kids about the aroma of herbs and spices!

  4. Combine Everything: Pour in the vegetable broth, diced potatoes, and diced tomatoes. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20 minutes.

  5. Add Green Beans: Stir in the green beans and season with salt and pepper. Continue to simmer for another 10 minutes until all the vegetables are tender.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Enjoy this hearty soup with some warm bread!

2. Classic Mac and Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Breadcrumbs for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Boil water in a large pot and add the macaroni. Let the kids help measure out the pasta and stir it occasionally. Cook according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In the same pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Allow your kids to help sprinkle in the flour and whisk it together until it forms a roux (about 2 minutes).

  3. Add Milk and Cheese: Gradually add the milk while whisking to avoid lumps. Once the mixture thickens, stir in the cheese until melted. This is a great time for kids to see the magic of cheese turning into a creamy sauce!

  4. Combine: Add the drained pasta to the cheese sauce, mixing well. Season with paprika, salt, and pepper.

  5. Optional Baking: If you want a baked version, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and transfer the mac and cheese to a baking dish. Top with breadcrumbs and bake for 20 minutes until bubbly and golden.

  6. Serve: Let the kids serve themselves and watch their faces light up at this classic comfort food!

3. Warm Cinnamon Apples

Ingredients:

  • 4 large apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • Vanilla ice cream for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Apples: Allow the kids to help peel and slice the apples. Discuss the different varieties of apples and their flavors.

  2. Cook the Apples: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced apples, brown sugar, and cinnamon. Stir to coat the apples evenly.

  3. Add Raisins: If using, add the raisins and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the apples are tender.

  4. Serve Warm: Spoon the warm cinnamon apples into bowls and top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra treat.

4. Chewy Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • Icing for decoration (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, combine the flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, baking soda, and salt. Let the kids use a whisk to mix everything together.

  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Kids can help with this using a hand mixer, but always supervise!

  3. Combine and Chill: Beat in the egg and molasses, then gradually add the dry ingredients. Once mixed, cover the dough and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  4. Roll and Cut: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface and use cookie cutters to make festive shapes. Kids will love choosing their favorite shapes!

  5. Bake: Place the cookies on a baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes. Allow them to cool before decorating with icing if desired.

5. Hot Chocolate Bar

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Toppings: whipped cream, marshmallows, chocolate shavings, peppermint sticks, crushed cookies

Instructions:

  1. Make the Hot Chocolate: In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the milk, heavy cream, cocoa powder, sugar, and vanilla extract. Let the kids help whisk everything together until smooth and heated through.

  2. Set Up the Hot Chocolate Bar: Pour the hot chocolate into a large serving bowl or individual mugs. Set out a variety of toppings in small bowls and let the kids create their own unique hot chocolate concoctions.

  3. Enjoy Together: Gather around and enjoy your delicious hot chocolate creations. This cozy activity is perfect for chilly winter afternoons!

Conclusion

Cooking with kids during the winter months is a delightful way to spend quality time together while creating comforting meals and treats. These recipes are not only simple but also allow for creativity and fun in the kitchen. Whether you’re making hearty vegetable soup, classic mac and cheese, or sweet gingerbread cookies, each dish is infused with warmth and love. So gather your little chefs, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy the magic of cooking together this winter!
