Instant Pot Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Peppers Recipe: A Creative and Delicious Option

As the weather starts to cool down, our taste buds crave warm and comforting dishes that can be easily prepared at home. One such recipe that fits the bill perfectly is Instant Pot Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Peppers. This creative and delicious option is a game-changer for those looking for a healthy and satisfying meal that can be prepared in no time. Ingredients 4 large bell peppers, any color 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into small pieces 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup fresh spinach leaves 1 cup cooked white rice 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional) 2 tablespoons olive oil 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional) Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Peppers Rinse the bell peppers and cut off the tops. Remove the seeds and membranes, and place them in the Instant Pot. Add 1 cup of water to the Instant Pot, and close the lid. Make sure the valve is set to “sealing”. Press the “manual” or “pressure cook” button and set the cooking time to 5 minutes at high pressure. Step 2: Cook the Chicken and Spinach In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic, spinach, paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the skillet. Cook until the spinach is wilted. Stir in the cooked rice, diced tomatoes, and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer. Step 3: Assemble the Stuffed Peppers Once the peppers are cooked, remove them from the Instant Pot and set them aside. Add the cooked chicken and spinach mixture to the peppers, dividing it evenly among the four peppers. Top each pepper with shredded cheddar cheese (if using). Step 4: Finish Cooking the Stuffed Peppers Close the Instant Pot and set the valve to “sealing”. Press the “manual” or “pressure cook” button and set the cooking time to 5 minutes at high pressure. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure. Serve the stuffed peppers hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley or cilantro (if desired). Tips and Variations Use any combination of bell pepper colors you like, or a mix of colors for a festive look. Substitute ground turkey or beef for the chicken if you prefer. Add some heat to the dish by using hot sauce or red pepper flakes. Serve the stuffed peppers with a side of steamed vegetables or a green salad for a well-rounded meal. Conclusion Instant Pot Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Peppers is a creative and delicious recipe that is perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. With its easy-to-follow instructions and minimal ingredients, it’s a great option for busy home cooks. Give it a try and enjoy the warm and comforting flavors of this unique recipe! ...

Quick and Easy Instant Pot Chicken Pot Pie Recipes

Are you looking for a comforting and delicious meal that can be whipped up in no time? Look no further than these quick and easy Instant Pot chicken pot pie recipes! Whether you’re a busy parent, a working professional, or just someone who loves a hearty and satisfying meal, these recipes are sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Classic Chicken Pot Pie Ingredients: 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cubed 1 cup diced carrots 1 cup frozen peas 1/2 cup diced onion 1/3 cup butter 1/3 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon celery seed 1 3/4 cups chicken broth 2/3 cup milk 2 unbaked pie crusts Instructions: Start by setting your Instant Pot to the sauté function and melt the butter. Add the chicken, carrots, peas, and onion to the pot, and cook until the chicken is no longer pink. Stir in the flour, salt, pepper, and celery seed until well combined. Gradually stir in the chicken broth and milk, ensuring there are no lumps. Close the lid of the Instant Pot, and set it to high pressure for 5 minutes. Once the cooking time is complete, do a quick pressure release, and carefully open the lid. Spoon the chicken mixture into a deep-dish pie plate, and cover it with the pie crust. Bake in a preheated oven at 425°F for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Creamy Chicken Pot Pie Ingredients: 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, chopped 1 cup diced potatoes 1 cup sliced mushrooms 1/2 cup chopped celery 1/4 cup butter 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1 1/2 cups chicken broth 1/2 cup heavy cream 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder Salt and pepper to taste Puff pastry sheets Instructions: Press the sauté button on the Instant Pot, and melt the butter. Add the chicken, potatoes, mushrooms, and celery, and cook until the chicken is browned. Stir in the flour, and cook for a minute to get rid of the raw flour taste. Pour in the chicken broth and heavy cream, and stir until well combined. Season with thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Close the lid, and cook on high pressure for 8 minutes. Once the cooking time is up, allow for a natural pressure release for 5 minutes, then do a quick release. Transfer the creamy chicken mixture into individual ramekins, and top with puff pastry sheets. Broil in the oven until the pastry is puffed and golden. Conclusion With these Instant Pot chicken pot pie recipes, you can enjoy all the flavors of a traditional pot pie without spending hours in the kitchen. The Instant Pot makes it possible to have a delicious and comforting meal on the table in a fraction of the time. Whether you opt for the classic version or the creamy twist, these recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings for a warm and hearty dish.
