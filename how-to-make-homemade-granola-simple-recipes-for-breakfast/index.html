How to Make Homemade Granola: Simple Recipes for Breakfast


If you’re looking to elevate your breakfast routine, homemade granola is a game-changer. It’s versatile, nutritious, and easy to customize to your taste preferences and dietary needs. Store-bought options may be convenient, but they often come packed with added sugars and preservatives. By making your own granola at home, you take full control of the ingredients, flavor profile, and texture. Plus, the aroma of freshly baked granola is absolutely irresistible. Ready to get started? Let’s walk through some simple recipes for creating granola that’s perfect for any breakfast table.

The Essentials of a Great Granola

Before diving into recipes, it’s helpful to understand the structure of granola. At its core, granola typically consists of a few key components:

  1. Base Ingredients:

    • Rolled oats are the foundation of most granola recipes. They provide the hearty, chewy texture that makes granola perfect for breakfast. Choose old-fashioned rolled oats instead of quick oats, as they hold their shape better during baking.

  2. Sweeteners:

    • Sweeteners help bind ingredients together and add flavor. Options like honey, maple syrup, agave nectar, or brown sugar work beautifully. Choose one that complements the flavors you’re building.

  3. Fats:

    • Adding fats like coconut oil, olive oil, or melted butter ensures your granola achieves a crisp, satisfying texture when baked.

  4. Mix-ins:

    • The fun part! Mix-ins include nuts, seeds, dried fruits, spices, and any other add-ins you love. These bring texture and flavor to your creation.

  5. Binding Agents (Optional):

    • Ingredients like peanut butter, almond butter, or mashed banana can serve as additional binding agents. They also add a creamy, indulgent twist to your granola.

  6. Seasonings:

    • A pinch of salt and spices such as cinnamon, nutmeg, or cardamom can elevate the flavor and balance the sweetness.

Once you master the basic framework, you can experiment to create endless variations. Now, let’s get started with some recipes!

Classic Homemade Granola Recipe

This classic recipe works as an excellent base. Whether you use it as-is or customize it with your favorite toppings, it won’t disappoint.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup nuts (such as almonds, pecans, or walnuts), roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened coconut flakes (optional)
  • 1/4 cup seeds (like sunflower seeds, chia seeds, or pumpkin seeds)
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil or olive oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup dried fruits (raisins, cranberries, or chopped apricots), added after baking

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the oats, nuts, seeds, coconut flakes, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the honey (or maple syrup), melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet mixture over the dry ingredients and mix until evenly coated.
  5. Spread the granola mixture evenly onto the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, stirring once halfway through to ensure even browning. Keep an eye on it to prevent burning.
  7. Remove from the oven and let the granola cool completely on the baking sheet. It will crisp up as it cools.
  8. Once cool, mix in dried fruits and transfer the granola to an airtight container for storage.

Chocolate and Almond Indulgence Granola

Need a little chocolatey goodness in your morning routine? This recipe pairs rich cocoa with the crunch of almonds for a decadent but wholesome breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup slivered almonds
  • 1/2 cup sunflower seeds
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips, added after baking

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, stir together oats, almonds, sunflower seeds, cocoa powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup and melted coconut oil.
  4. Drizzle the wet ingredients over the dry mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Spread the mixture evenly onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 25 minutes, stirring halfway through.
  6. Once the granola is out of the oven and has cooled completely, sprinkle in the chocolate chips for an extra treat.
  7. Store in an airtight container for up to two weeks.

Tropical Paradise Granola

Capture the tastes of a tropical getaway with this fruity, coconut-infused granola. It’s delightful on its own or served atop fresh Greek yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup macadamia nuts or cashews, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup shredded unsweetened coconut
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • Zest of 1 lime or lemon
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup dried pineapple, mango, or papaya, chopped (added after baking)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine the oats, macadamia nuts, coconut, chia seeds, salt, and lime zest in a large bowl.
  3. In a smaller bowl, mix the honey and melted coconut oil until smooth.
  4. Pour the wet mixture over the dry ingredients and stir until combined.
  5. Spread the mixture onto the baking sheet in an even layer.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through to ensure even toasting.
  7. Cool completely, then mix in the dried fruits. Store in an airtight container.

Savory Granola

Who says granola has to be sweet? This savory twist is perfect for pairing with yogurt, avocado toast, or even as a crunchy topping for soups and salads.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup tamari or soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine oats, pumpkin seeds, sesame seeds, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  3. Whisk the olive oil and tamari together in a separate bowl. Pour over the dry mixture and mix well.
  4. Spread the granola mixture evenly on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes, until golden brown and fragrant.
  6. Allow to cool completely before storing in an airtight container.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Granola

  • Monitor the Baking Process: Granola can burn quickly at the edges. Stirring it halfway through ensures even cooking.
  • Allow to Cool Fully: Granola will continue to solidify and crisp as it cools, so don’t panic if it feels soft coming out of the oven.
  • Experiment with Ratios: Like your granola clumpy? Add an extra binding agent, such as nut butter or mashed banana. For a finer consistency, press the mixture tightly on the baking sheet before baking.
  • Store Properly: Keep granola in an airtight container at room temperature to maintain freshness. Most recipes will last 2-3 weeks.

With these recipes, homemade granola may just become your go-to breakfast staple. Whether you prefer sweet, savory, or a combination of both, the possibilities are endless. Enjoy your granola as a cereal, mix it into yogurt parfaits, or snack on it right out of the jar. Once you’ve mastered these recipes, try creating your own signature blend—it’s all part of the fun!
