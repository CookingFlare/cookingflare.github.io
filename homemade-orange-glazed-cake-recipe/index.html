Homemade Orange Glazed Cake Recipe


Baking a cake from scratch can be a truly rewarding experience. There’s something magical about the combination of simple ingredients coming together to create a moist, flavorful dessert that fills your kitchen with an irresistible aroma. One such cake that stands out is the homemade orange glazed cake, a perfect balance of citrusy sweetness with a rich, tender crumb. Whether you’re looking for a showstopper for a special occasion or a delightful treat for an afternoon snack, this orange glazed cake recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients for the Orange Glazed Cake

Before diving into the recipe, it’s important to have all your ingredients ready. The cake itself requires a few basic pantry staples, along with fresh oranges to infuse the cake with a natural, zesty flavor. The glaze adds an extra layer of sweetness and an attractive finish to the cake.

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter (softened to room temperature)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest (freshly grated)
  • ½ cup fresh orange juice (about 2 medium oranges)
  • ½ cup sour cream (or Greek yogurt for a tangier flavor)

For the Orange Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar (sifted)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh orange juice
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest (freshly grated)

Optional for Decoration:

  • Fresh orange slices
  • A few mint leaves for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Cake Batter

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan (or you can use a bundt pan for a more decorative shape). Make sure the pan is well-prepared to prevent the cake from sticking.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set this aside for later. The combination of baking powder and baking soda will help the cake rise evenly, giving it a light and airy texture.

  3. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed until the mixture becomes light and fluffy. This usually takes about 3-4 minutes. The sugar and butter should be well-incorporated, and the mixture should appear pale in color.

  4. Add the Eggs: Add the eggs one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. This helps the batter emulsify and prevents it from curdling. Once the eggs are mixed in, add the vanilla extract and orange zest, continuing to beat for another minute until everything is well combined.

  5. Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients: Now, it’s time to add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Start by adding about a third of the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Then, add half of the orange juice and half of the sour cream (or Greek yogurt). Mix until smooth, and then repeat with the remaining dry ingredients, orange juice, and sour cream. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed to ensure even mixing.

  6. Final Mix: Once all ingredients are fully combined, give the batter one final mix to ensure it’s smooth and evenly distributed.

Baking the Cake

  1. Transfer the batter into your prepared cake pan and use a spatula to spread it evenly.

  2. Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes (if using a round cake pan), or 45-50 minutes (if using a bundt pan). Check the doneness of the cake by inserting a toothpick into the center. If it comes out clean or with just a few crumbs attached, your cake is ready.

  3. Cool the cake in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. The cooling process helps the cake firm up, making it easier to glaze.

Preparing the Orange Glaze

While the cake is cooling, you can prepare the orange glaze. The glaze is the key to bringing that citrusy sweetness to the cake, giving it a glossy finish.

  1. Mix the Glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together the sifted powdered sugar, orange juice, and orange zest. Start by adding the orange juice gradually to the powdered sugar to ensure you get the right consistency. The glaze should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon but thin enough to drizzle over the cake.

  2. Adjust Consistency: If the glaze is too thick, add a little more orange juice, a teaspoon at a time, until you achieve the perfect consistency. If it’s too thin, add a little more powdered sugar.

Glazing the Cake

Once the cake has completely cooled, it’s time to apply the glaze.

  1. Drizzle the Glaze: Using a spoon or a small icing spatula, drizzle the orange glaze over the top of the cake. If you’re using a bundt pan, you can pour the glaze over the center of the cake and allow it to drip down the sides for a beautiful, cascading effect.

  2. Let the Glaze Set: Allow the glaze to set for about 15-20 minutes before serving. This gives it time to harden slightly, creating a nice sugary finish on the cake.

Optional Garnishes

For an extra touch of elegance, you can garnish the cake with fresh orange slices on top. The bright citrus color will make the cake even more visually appealing. A few fresh mint leaves can also add a pop of green and enhance the presentation.

Tips for the Perfect Orange Glazed Cake

  • Use fresh oranges: Fresh orange juice and zest will give the cake a more vibrant flavor compared to bottled juice. Choose oranges that are firm and heavy for their size, as they will have the most juice.

  • Room temperature ingredients: To ensure the batter mixes well, make sure your butter, eggs, and sour cream (or yogurt) are at room temperature before you begin. Cold ingredients can lead to an uneven texture.

  • Don’t overmix: When combining the dry and wet ingredients, mix until just incorporated. Overmixing can lead to a dense, tough cake, which is not what we want.

  • Make it ahead of time: This cake can be made a day in advance. Simply wrap it tightly in plastic wrap once it’s cooled completely, and store it at room temperature. Add the glaze just before serving to keep it looking fresh.

Why You’ll Love This Cake

This homemade orange glazed cake is the perfect blend of sweet and citrusy, with a light, fluffy texture that melts in your mouth. The orange glaze adds a glossy, irresistible finish, making it a true crowd-pleaser. Whether it’s served as a dessert for a dinner party or as a sweet snack with your afternoon tea, this cake is sure to become a family favorite.

The citrusy notes make it a wonderful choice for spring and summer gatherings, though it’s delicious year-round. The orange flavor isn’t overpowering, making it a great option for those who prefer subtler, more refined dessert options.

Variations on the Recipe

If you’re looking to add a twist to this classic orange glazed cake, here are a few variations you can try:

  • Add Poppy Seeds: Adding 1-2 tablespoons of poppy seeds to the cake batter can create a delightful texture and a sophisticated look. The poppy seeds will also add a nice crunch that contrasts beautifully with the soft cake.

  • Incorporate Almond Flour: To make this cake slightly denser and add a nutty flavor, you can substitute about ½ cup of the all-purpose flour with almond flour. This will give the cake a slightly different texture and a richer taste.

  • Orange and Coconut: For a tropical twist, try adding shredded coconut to the cake batter. The sweetness of the coconut pairs wonderfully with the bright, zesty orange flavor.

  • Gluten-Free Option: You can easily make this cake gluten-free by replacing the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend. Be sure to check the specific instructions on your flour blend for best results.

Storing the Orange Glazed Cake

If you have leftovers, don’t worry! This cake stores well for a few days.

  • Room Temperature: Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. The glaze will remain intact, but the cake will become even more moist as it sits.

  • Refrigeration: If you live in a hot climate or if you’re storing the cake for an extended period, keep it in the fridge. The glaze may firm up a bit in the fridge, but the cake will remain fresh for up to a week.

  • Freezing: If you want to freeze the cake, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil. It can be frozen for up to 3 months. To serve, let it thaw in the fridge overnight and glaze it before serving.

Conclusion

This homemade orange glazed cake is not only incredibly delicious, but it’s also a delight to make. With its fresh citrusy flavor, tender crumb, and glossy glaze, it’s sure to become a favorite in your baking repertoire. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow and delivers a truly impressive result. So, grab your ingredients and bake up this delightful treat—you won’t regret it!
