Vegan and Vegetarian Burrito Recipes You Can Make in 30 Minutes


Burritos are a fantastic choice for a quick, satisfying, and delicious meal. Whether you’re a vegan, vegetarian, or someone simply looking to add more plant-based dishes to your menu, burritos are versatile enough to fit all diets. With a few basic ingredients and a little creativity, you can whip up a flavorful burrito in just 30 minutes. From hearty bean fillings to fresh vegetable combinations, these vegan and vegetarian burrito recipes are sure to become your go-to for busy days when you crave something tasty, filling, and nutritious.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of easy-to-make burrito recipes that are perfect for a quick lunch, dinner, or even meal prep. Get ready to discover the delicious possibilities that will leave you craving more!

Classic Vegan Black Bean Burrito

One of the most beloved burrito recipes is the classic black bean burrito. This simple, hearty, and protein-packed burrito will leave you feeling satisfied while still being light on the stomach. With only a handful of ingredients, you can create a wholesome meal in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 large avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 large whole-wheat or flour tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, bell pepper, and garlic, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are soft.
  2. Add the black beans, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well and cook for another 3-4 minutes, allowing the beans to heat through and absorb the spices.
  3. While the beans are cooking, warm the tortillas in a separate pan or microwave for 10-15 seconds.
  4. To assemble the burritos, place the warmed tortillas on a flat surface. Layer each tortilla with a spoonful of the bean mixture, sliced avocado, salsa, and cilantro.
  5. Roll up the burrito tightly, folding in the sides as you go. Serve immediately or wrap in foil for meal prep.

This vegan black bean burrito is a simple yet flavorful option that’s perfect for any meal of the day.

Sweet Potato and Black Bean Burrito

Sweet potatoes and black beans are a match made in heaven. The sweetness of the roasted sweet potatoes pairs beautifully with the savory, hearty black beans. This combination creates a well-balanced meal that’s full of flavor, fiber, and protein.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup vegan sour cream (optional)
  • 2 large tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the diced sweet potato on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat the sweet potatoes evenly and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  2. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, heat a small pan over medium heat and add the corn kernels. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes until they’re slightly charred and heated through.
  3. In a separate pan, warm the black beans over medium heat, stirring occasionally until they’re heated through.
  4. Once the sweet potatoes are done, remove them from the oven and set aside.
  5. To assemble the burritos, place the tortillas on a flat surface. Layer each tortilla with a scoop of roasted sweet potatoes, black beans, corn, red onion, and fresh cilantro. Add a dollop of vegan sour cream, if desired.
  6. Roll up the burritos, tucking in the sides as you go. Serve immediately or wrap them in foil for later.

These sweet potato and black bean burritos are comforting, hearty, and offer a wonderful balance of textures and flavors.

Chickpea and Avocado Burrito

Chickpeas are a fantastic plant-based protein source that can be used in various dishes. For this burrito, we pair chickpeas with creamy avocado for a nutritious and satisfying meal that’s ready in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 large avocado, mashed
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup spinach or arugula
  • 2 large tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chickpeas, turmeric, coriander, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well and sauté for about 5 minutes, mashing some of the chickpeas with the back of a spoon to create a chunky mixture.
  2. While the chickpeas are cooking, mash the avocado in a bowl and season with salt and pepper.
  3. To assemble the burritos, spread a layer of mashed avocado on each tortilla. Add a generous spoonful of the chickpea mixture, followed by diced tomatoes, red onion, and spinach.
  4. Roll up the burritos, folding in the sides as you go. Serve immediately, or wrap in foil for meal prep.

This chickpea and avocado burrito is packed with flavor and perfect for anyone looking for a quick and nutritious plant-based meal.

Spicy Tofu and Veggie Burrito

For those who enjoy a bit of heat, a spicy tofu and veggie burrito is the perfect choice. Tofu takes on the flavors of the spices you cook it with, making it a great filling for any burrito. Paired with sautéed vegetables and spicy seasonings, this burrito is a flavor-packed delight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and crumbled
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup spinach or kale
  • 1 tablespoon taco seasoning
  • 2 large tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Crumble the tofu into small pieces and toss it with soy sauce and sriracha. Set aside to marinate for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced bell pepper and zucchini and sauté for about 5 minutes until tender.
  3. Add the marinated tofu and taco seasoning to the skillet. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tofu is golden brown and slightly crispy.
  4. Add the spinach or kale to the skillet and cook for another 1-2 minutes, until wilted.
  5. To assemble the burritos, lay the tortillas on a flat surface. Add a generous portion of the tofu and veggie mixture to each tortilla.
  6. Roll up the burritos tightly, folding in the sides as you go. Serve immediately or wrap them in foil for later.

This spicy tofu and veggie burrito is perfect for those who love bold flavors and a little kick in their meal.

Tips for Making the Best Vegan and Vegetarian Burritos

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh vegetables and herbs make a world of difference in the flavor and texture of your burritos. Consider using freshly diced tomatoes, cilantro, or any veggies you have on hand.

  2. Get Creative with Fillings: Burritos are an excellent way to use up leftovers. Roasted vegetables, rice, quinoa, or any other grains can serve as great bases for your filling. Feel free to mix and match ingredients based on what you have available.

  3. Don’t Forget the Sauces: Salsa, vegan sour cream, guacamole, or hot sauce can elevate the flavors of your burritos. A simple homemade salsa made with tomatoes, onions, cilantro, and lime juice can add a fresh zing to your burrito.

  4. Warm the Tortillas: Heating your tortillas before assembling the burritos makes them more pliable and easier to roll without breaking. A quick 10-15 seconds in the microwave or on a hot pan will do the trick.

  5. Meal Prep: Burritos are perfect for meal prepping. Simply assemble your burritos ahead of time, wrap them tightly in foil, and refrigerate. When you’re ready to eat, reheat them in the oven or microwave.

Conclusion

Burritos are a delicious, customizable, and easy-to-make meal that’s perfect for any dietary preference. Whether you’re craving a simple black bean burrito or something more adventurous like a spicy tofu filling, these vegan and vegetarian burrito recipes are a great way to enjoy plant-based meals in no time. With minimal effort, you can create a wholesome, flavorful meal that satisfies your hunger and your taste buds.

Next time you’re in a rush and want something filling yet healthy, try one of these 30-minute burrito recipes, and you’ll have a delicious meal ready in no time!
