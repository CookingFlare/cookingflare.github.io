How to Make a Classic French Apple Tart


Few desserts embody the elegance and simplicity of French baking quite like the classic French apple tart, also known as Tarte aux Pommes. This timeless recipe showcases the beauty of fresh apples, balanced by a buttery, flaky crust and a glossy finish. Whether you’re preparing it for a refined dinner party or simply indulging yourself with a baking project, this tart never fails to impress. Follow these step-by-step instructions to create a dessert that’s as stunning as it is delicious.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Tart Crust (Pâte Sablée):

  • 1 ¼ cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup (50g) powdered sugar
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter (cold and cut into cubes)
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1–2 tablespoons ice-cold water
  • Pinch of salt

For the Apple Filling:

  • 4–5 medium apples (such as Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Golden Delicious)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (melted)
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Apricot Glaze:

  • 3 tablespoons apricot preserves
  • ½ tablespoon water

Preparing the Perfect Tart Crust

The crust, or pâte sablée, is the base of any French tart and provides the foundation for its light yet rich texture. This buttery shortcrust is tender and melts in your mouth, perfectly complementing the delicate apple filling.

1. Combine Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt to ensure an even distribution of flavors.

2. Add Butter

Work the cold butter cubes into the dry mixture using a pastry cutter or your fingertips. Continue until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Avoid overworking the butter to maintain a tender crust.

3. Incorporate the Egg Yolks

Add the egg yolk and mix gently to bring the dough together. If needed, add 1–2 tablespoons of ice-cold water, one at a time, until the dough holds together without being sticky.

4. Chill the Dough

Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling allows the butter to firm up, ensuring the crust won’t shrink or become tough during baking.

5. Roll Out the Dough

Once chilled, roll out the dough to about ⅛-inch thickness on a lightly floured surface. Carefully transfer it to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Press the dough gently into the edges and trim any excess. Prick the base lightly with a fork to prevent bubbling during baking.

6. Blind Bake the Crust

Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake it in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 15 minutes. Remove the weights and parchment, then bake for an additional 5–7 minutes, until just golden. Set aside to cool.

Crafting the Apple Filling

The beauty of a classic French apple tart lies in its thinly sliced apples, meticulously arranged into a decorative spiral. Their natural sweetness, enhanced with subtle spices, creates a delightful and aromatic dessert.

1. Prepare the Apples

Peel, core, and slice the apples into thin, uniform pieces (about ⅛ inch thick). Aim for consistency, as the final presentation depends on how neatly the slices are arranged.

2. Toss with Spices

In a large bowl, gently toss the apple slices with the melted butter, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and ground cinnamon. The lemon juice prevents the apples from browning, while the cinnamon adds just a hint of warmth.

3. Arrange the Apples

Starting from the outer edge of the tart crust, overlap the apple slices in a circular pattern, working your way toward the center. Take your time to create a visually appealing spiral that showcases the apples’ natural beauty.

4. Bake the Tart

Bake the assembled tart in the preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes. The apples should be tender, lightly golden, and slightly caramelized along the edges.

Adding the Finishing Touch: Apricot Glaze

A glossy apricot glaze is the hallmark of a French apple tart, giving it a polished and professional appearance.

1. Heat the Preserves

In a small saucepan, heat the apricot preserves and water over low heat, stirring until smooth.

2. Brush the Glaze

Using a pastry brush, lightly coat the surface of the tart and apples with the warm glaze. This adds a subtle sweetness and a vibrant shine, elevating the final presentation.

Serving Suggestions and Storage Tips

Serve your classic French apple tart slightly warm or at room temperature. Pair it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a lavish touch. This tart is best enjoyed fresh but can be stored in the refrigerator for up to two days. To refresh, gently rewarm it in the oven before serving.

Why This Recipe Stands the Test of Time

The French apple tart is more than just a dessert; it’s an ode to simplicity and artistry. Its delicate crust, fragrant apples, and shimmering glaze create a harmonious balance of flavor and texture. What makes this recipe timeless is its versatility—whether you use tart Granny Smith apples, mild Golden Delicious, or juicy Honeycrisp, the tart adapts to highlight the natural character of the fruit.

This dessert is also the perfect opportunity to hone your pastry skills. From mastering the buttery crust to arranging the apple slices into an elegant pattern, every step encourages creativity and care. The reward is a show-stopping tart that embodies the refined yet approachable nature of French cuisine.

Final Thoughts

Making a classic French apple tart may take a bit of patience and planning, but the results are undeniably worth it. The process of crafting this dessert is not just about following instructions; it’s about appreciating the beauty of simple ingredients prepared with care. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is your gateway to creating a taste of France in your own kitchen. Gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the magic happen—bon appétit!
