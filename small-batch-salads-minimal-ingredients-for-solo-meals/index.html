Small Batch Salads: Minimal Ingredients for Solo Meals


Life can get pretty lonely sometimes – especially for those who live alone or maintain busy schedules. While eating alone might be a bitter pill to swallow, who says that it has to be dreary and unexciting? Enter the small batch salad, a game-changer that will get you excited about cooking again. These delish dishes not only nourish your body but also your soul. Keep reading to discover our favorite small batch salad recipes guaranteed to make your lunch interesting and fresh.

1. Avocado and Quinoa Salad

Are you craving a satisfying meal with a mix of protein and healthy fats? Enter, the Avocado and Quinoa Salad. It has everything you need – nutrient-rich quinoa, fiber-packed avocado, and the health benefits of lemon. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1/2 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 ripe avocado
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Small handful of chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa, diced avocado, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and parsley.
  2. Squeeze the avocado with a little pressure to release the juices and allow it to blend well with the rest of the ingredients.
  3. Toss the ingredients and enjoy!

2. Greek Chickpea Salad

This Greek Chickpea Salad is perfect for those days when you can’t decide between a Greek salad and a hummus sandwich. It’s packed full of goodness with chickpeas, cucumber, tomato, and feta cheese to name a few. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1/2 cup canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh diced onion
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl.
  2. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice over the salad, season with salt and pepper, and mix well.
  3. Your Greek Chickpea Salad is ready to be served.

3. Mixed Greens with Smoked Salmon

This salmon salad recipe delivers a burst of flavor in every bite. It incorporates mixed greens, along with juicy cherry tomatoes and succulent smoked salmon. It’s a delightful and quick lunch idea that you’ll come back to. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, sliced in half
  • 1/4 cup smoked salmon
  • 1 tablespoon crème Fraiche or Greek Yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon capers
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine mixed greens and cherry tomatoes in a salad bowl.
  2. Top with smoked salmon and capers.
  3. Drizzle with crème fraîche or Greek yogurt and squeeze lemon juice over the salad.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, and you’ve got a refreshing meal to enjoy.

4. Spinach Apple Salad

This vibrant green and red mixture will not only tempt your taste buds but also spice up any lunchtime. It combines sweet apples, crunchy walnuts, and protein-packed spinach to keep you full and energized throughout the day. Here’s what you need:

  • 2 cups baby spinach leaves
  • Half of a red apple, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped walnuts
  • 1 tablespoon shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider or white wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine baby spinach leaves, apple slices, walnuts, and cheddar cheese in a salad bowl.
  2. Drizzle olive oil and apple cider or white wine vinegar over the salad.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and enjoy your spin on a classic salad.

5. Caprese Salad

A classic Caprese Salad is simple yet mouthwatering with its combination of ripe tomatoes, fresh basil, and mozzarella. Jump on the bandwagon and try this heart-healthy, and protein-packed single-serve salad. All you need:

  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 ounce fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • A few fresh basil leaves, torn
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Arrange cherry tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil on a plate.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and enjoy a taste of Italy.

Now that you have some fantastic ideas on delectable small batch salad recipes for solo meals, there’s no need to feel left out in your meal planning. Feel free to switch up your ingredients or add extras to suit your taste buds. Bon appétit!
