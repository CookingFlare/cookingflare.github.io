Mauritian Dholl Puri and Snacks


Mauritian cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors and cultures, influenced by Indian, Chinese, African, and European traditions. One dish that perfectly embodies this culinary diversity is the beloved Mauritian Dholl Puri. Dholl Puri is a thin, soft, and savory pancake made from lentils, filled with a flavorful split pea curry, and served with a variety of chutneys and pickles. In this blog post, we will explore the art of making Dholl Puri, along with some other mouthwatering Mauritian snacks.

Dholl Puri Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup split yellow peas (dholl)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for soaking and making the dough)
  • Oil (for cooking)
  • Butter (optional, for serving)

For the filling:

  • 2 cups split yellow peas (dholl)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil (for cooking)

Instructions:

  1. Start by soaking the split yellow peas (dholl) in water for at least 2 hours. Drain and rinse well.

  2. In a blender or food processor, grind the soaked dholl into a smooth paste. Transfer the paste to a large mixing bowl.

  3. To the dholl paste, add all-purpose flour, turmeric powder, cumin powder, chili powder, and salt. Mix well to form a soft dough. Gradually add water as needed to achieve the right consistency. The dough should be soft and pliable but not sticky.

  4. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball. Roll each ball into a thin circle, approximately 6-8 inches in diameter.

  5. Heat a non-stick pan or tawa over medium heat. Place one rolled-out dough circle on the pan and cook until bubbles start to appear on the surface. Flip the dholl puri and cook for another minute or until both sides are lightly golden brown. Repeat this process for the remaining dough balls.

  6. While the dholl puris are cooking, prepare the filling. In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until golden brown.

  7. Add the split yellow peas (dholl) to the pan and cook for a few minutes. Stir in the curry powder and salt. Cook until the peas are tender and the flavors have melded together.

  8. To assemble the dholl puri, place a spoonful of the filling in the center of a cooked puri. Fold the sides over the filling, creating a semi-circle shape. Serve hot with a dollop of butter, if desired.

Other Mauritian Snacks

Apart from Dholl Puri, Mauritian cuisine offers a wide range of delectable snacks that are perfect for any occasion. Here are a few favorites:

1. Gateau Piment (Chili Cakes):

Gateau Piment are small, deep-fried lentil cakes seasoned with chili and spices. These crispy and spicy snacks are often enjoyed with a cup of tea or as a side dish with a meal.

2. Samosas:

Samosas are triangular pastries filled with a savory mixture of potatoes, peas, and spices. They are deep-fried until golden brown and served with chutney or tamarind sauce. Samosas are a popular street food in Mauritius and are loved by locals and tourists alike.

3. Faratas:

Faratas are fluffy, flaky bread similar to Indian parathas. They are made with all-purpose flour, water, and a touch of oil. Faratas are usually served with curry dishes or used to wrap various fillings, making them a versatile snack option.

4. Gato Pima:

Gato Pima are small, fried dumplings made from split peas, flour, and spices. They have a crispy outer layer and a soft, savory interior. Gato Pima is often served with chutney or enjoyed on their own as a quick and tasty snack.

Conclusion

Mauritian cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors, and Dholl Puri and other snacks are just the tip of the iceberg. These recipes capture the essence of Mauritian culture, blending diverse influences into mouthwatering treats. Whether you’re exploring the streets of Mauritius or recreating these dishes in your own kitchen, prepare to embark on a culinary journey like no other. So go ahead, whip up some Dholl Puri and indulge in the delightful world of Mauritian snacks!
