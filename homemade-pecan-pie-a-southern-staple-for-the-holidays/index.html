Homemade Pecan Pie: A Southern Staple for the Holidays


There’s something undeniably comforting about the aroma of a freshly baked pecan pie wafting through the house during the holiday season. This classic Southern dessert has become a staple on dining tables across the country, and it’s not hard to see why. With its rich, buttery crust and sweet, gooey filling studded with crunchy pecans, homemade pecan pie is the perfect indulgence for those special occasions.

The Perfect Crust

Every great pecan pie starts with a great crust. For this recipe, we’ll be making a homemade crust from scratch. The key to a flaky and tender crust lies in using cold ingredients and handling the dough as little as possible. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ¼ - ½ cup ice water

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, tossing the mixture with a fork until it starts to come together.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times to bring it together. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour.

Once chilled, roll out one disc of dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Carefully transfer the dough to the dish, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges. Place the pie dish with the crust in the refrigerator while you prepare the filling.

The Irresistible Filling

Now it’s time to make the star of the show: the pecan filling. The combination of butter, sugar, and pecans creates a luscious, caramel-like filling that will have your taste buds dancing. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 cups pecan halves

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, corn syrup, melted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. Stir in the pecan halves, making sure they are evenly coated in the mixture.

Take the pie dish with the crust out of the refrigerator and pour the filling into the prepared crust. Use a spoon or spatula to spread the filling evenly.

Baking to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the pie dish on a baking sheet to catch any drips during baking.

Carefully transfer the pie to the preheated oven and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown. You may need to cover the pie with aluminum foil halfway through baking to prevent the crust from over-browning.

Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. The filling will continue to set as it cools, so be patient. Once cooled, slice and serve with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for the ultimate indulgence.

A Slice of Southern Heaven

Homemade pecan pie is a true labor of love, but the end result is more than worth it. With its buttery crust, sweet and nutty filling, and irresistible aroma, this Southern staple is sure to become a favorite at your holiday gatherings. So grab your apron, preheat your oven, and get ready to create a dessert that will have everyone asking for seconds. Happy baking!
