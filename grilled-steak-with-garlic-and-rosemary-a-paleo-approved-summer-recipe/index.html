Grilled Steak with Garlic and Rosemary: A Paleo-Approved Summer Recipe


Summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to fire up the grill and cook up some delicious, flavorful steaks. In this recipe, we’ll be combining the richness of grilled steak with the pungency of garlic and the herbal notes of rosemary to create a dish that’s sure to please even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

  • 1.5-2 pounds ribeye or strip steak
  • 4-6 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges (optional)

Instructions

To start, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, prepare your steak by seasoning it with salt and pepper on both sides. Make sure to coat the steak evenly, but don’t overdo it – you want to be able to taste the natural flavors of the meat.

Once the grill is hot, place the steak on the grates and sear for 3-4 minutes per side, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. For a medium-rare, cook for 5-6 minutes total. Use a thermometer to check the internal temperature of the steak – it should read at least 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare.

While the steak is cooking, prepare your garlic and rosemary mixture. In a small bowl, mix together the minced garlic and chopped rosemary leaves. You can adjust the amount of garlic and rosemary to your taste – some people prefer a stronger garlic flavor, while others like a more subtle hint of rosemary.

Once the steak is cooked to your liking, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. During this time, brush the garlic and rosemary mixture evenly over the top of the steak. You can use a spoon or your fingers to spread the mixture – just be gentle so you don’t press too hard and push the juices out of the meat.

To serve, slice the steak against the grain and serve with a squeeze of lemon juice, if desired. The acidity of the lemon will help cut through the richness of the steak and the bold flavors of the garlic and rosemary.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor to your steak, try marinating it in a mixture of olive oil, garlic, and rosemary for a few hours before grilling. This will help the flavors penetrate deeper into the meat and create a more complex taste profile.
  • If you prefer a more well-done steak, cook it for an additional 2-3 minutes per side. Just keep an eye on the temperature to make sure it doesn’t overcook.
  • To make this recipe more substantial, serve the grilled steak with a side of roasted vegetables or a hearty salad. The smoky flavor of the steak pairs well with a variety of ingredients, so feel free to get creative and experiment with different combinations.

Paleo-Friendly Notes

This recipe is perfectly Paleo-friendly, as it doesn’t include any grains, dairy, or processed ingredients. The only ingredients that might raise an eyebrow are the garlic and rosemary, which are technically plants. However, these ingredients are commonly used in Paleo cooking and are considered safe and healthy.

In conclusion, this grilled steak with garlic and rosemary recipe is a delicious and easy-to-make dish that’s perfect for a summer evening. With its bold flavors and tender texture, it’s sure to become a new favorite. So fire up the grill, get out your apron, and get ready to indulge in a culinary masterpiece.
