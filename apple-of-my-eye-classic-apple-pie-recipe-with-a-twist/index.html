Apple of My Eye: Classic Apple Pie Recipe with a Twist


Who doesn’t love the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the kitchen? It’s a classic dessert that never fails to delight, but what if we told you there’s a way to take this beloved recipe to the next level? Get ready to tantalize your taste buds with our unique twist on the classic apple pie. We’ve added a surprising combination of ingredients that will elevate this traditional dessert to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Ingredients:

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6 large apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Zest of one lemon

For the Twist:

  • ½ cup caramel sauce
  • ½ cup chopped pecans
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt

Instructions:

Making the Pie Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the chilled, cubed butter to the flour mixture.
  3. Using a pastry cutter or two forks, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together.
  5. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice and lemon zest to prevent browning.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Add the sugar mixture to the apples and toss to coat evenly.

Pie Assembly:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out one disc of the chilled pie dough on a floured surface and line a 9-inch pie dish with it.
  3. Drizzle the caramel sauce over the bottom of the pie crust.
  4. Spoon the apple filling into the pie crust, mounding it in the center.
  5. Sprinkle the chopped pecans over the apple filling and drizzle with a little more caramel sauce.
  6. Sprinkle the sea salt over the top of the filling for a surprising twist of flavor.
  7. Roll out the second disc of pie dough and place it over the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  8. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 50 to 60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  2. If the edges of the pie start to darken too quickly, cover them with aluminum foil halfway through baking.
  3. Once done, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 2 hours before slicing.

Serving Suggestion:

This apple pie is best enjoyed warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. The combination of sweet caramel, crunchy pecans, and a hint of sea salt adds a delightful contrast to the classic apple filling, making each bite a symphony of flavors.

Conclusion:

There you have it, a classic apple pie with a delightful twist that is sure to impress your family and friends. The added caramel, pecans, and sea salt bring a whole new dimension to this timeless dessert, making it a standout at any gathering. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to bake your way to apple pie perfection!
