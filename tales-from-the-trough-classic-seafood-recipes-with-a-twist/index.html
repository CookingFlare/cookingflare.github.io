üêö 1. Poached Salmon with Lemon-Dill Risotto

When it comes to seafood, few dishes are as iconic as the classic poached salmon. But let’s take it up a notch by pairing it with a zesty, creamy lemon-dill risotto. This luxurious blend brings out the delicate flavors of the salmon while providing a harmonious balance between tangy, savory, and sweet.

Here’s how to try this dish at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 large salmon fillet (skin-on)
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 lemon, juiced (plus extra lemon slices for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring chicken broth and water to a simmer.
  2. Season salmon with salt and place skin-side down in the simmering broth, covering it with a lid. Cook for 15 minutes or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  3. While the salmon is poaching, prepare the risotto. Begin by heating olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add the rice and stir well to coat. Pour in the wine and cook for 2 minutes or until the liquid has been absorbed.
  5. Gradually add the broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each ladle to be absorbed before adding the next. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and al dente, about 20-25 minutes.
  6. Stir in the dill, parsley, Parmesan, and lemon juice. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  7. Remove the salmon from the broth and flake it with a fork.
  8. Spoon the lemon-dill risotto onto plates, placing the poached salmon on top. Garnish with lemon slices and additional dill, if desired.

ü¶à 2. Seared Scallops with Mango-Avocado Salsa

Few things can rival the buttery, melt-in-your-mouth texture of seared scallops. Add a fruity, fresh, and vibrant mango-avocado salsa, and you’ll have a dish that’s not only pleasing to the palate but also visually stunning. It’s a symphony of flavors with a hint of sweet, reaching new culinary heights!

Ingredients:

  • 12 large scallops
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 mango, diced
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:

  1. Season the scallops with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. In a large, heavy skillet, heat the olive oil over high heat.
  3. Once the oil is shimmering, add the scallops, and sear for 1-2 minutes on each side or until golden brown. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine the mango, avocado, onion, cilantro, lime juice, and honey. Gently toss to combine.
  5. Arrange the seared scallops on a serving platter and top with the mango-avocado salsa. Serve immediately.

ü¶û 3. Lobster Mac and Cheese

If you’re looking for a twist on a comfort food classic, our lobster mac and cheese is the perfect blend of rich, creamy, and indulgent. There’s just something about adding succulent chunks of lobster meat that takes this dish to a new level of decadence.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pound cooked lobster meat, chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Cook macaroni according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until hot.
  4. In a separate large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour, whisking constantly to create a roux. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  5. Gradually add the hot milk to the roux, whisking constantly to combine.
  6. Simmer until the mixture thickens and becomes smooth, stirring frequently. Remove from heat.
  7. Stir in the cheddar cheese, Dijon mustard, paprika, salt, and pepper until the cheese has melted and the sauce is creamy.
  8. Add the cooked macaroni, lobster meat, and Parmesan cheese. Stir well to combine.
  9. Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish and sprinkle additional Parmesan cheese on top.
  10. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbly.
