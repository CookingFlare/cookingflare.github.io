Middle Eastern Wraps: A Delicious Recipe for Shawarma or Falafel


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and colorful dishes that transport your taste buds to the heart of the region. Among the most popular and beloved street foods are Middle Eastern wraps, particularly shawarma and falafel. These wraps are not just delicious but also versatile, catering to various tastes and dietary preferences. Whether you are a fan of juicy, tender meat or crave a flavorful vegetarian option, there’s a wrap for everyone.

In this post, we will dive deep into the delicious world of Middle Eastern wraps and provide a detailed recipe for both Shawarma and Falafel, two of the most iconic dishes in the region. We’ll explore the ingredients, preparation techniques, and serve options, ensuring that you can recreate these flavorful wraps in your own kitchen with ease.

What Makes Middle Eastern Wraps So Special?

Middle Eastern wraps are not just about the ingredients; they represent the essence of the region’s culinary history and culture. Traditionally, wraps are made using pita or flatbread, which serve as the perfect vessel for filling, flavorful ingredients. The combination of savory meats, fresh vegetables, and aromatic sauces creates an explosion of flavors in every bite.

The spices used in Middle Eastern wraps are an integral part of their distinct flavor profile. From cumin and coriander to paprika and turmeric, the spices are what give the wraps their irresistible taste. The use of fresh herbs like parsley, cilantro, and mint adds brightness and a refreshing contrast to the hearty fillings.

One of the most iconic wraps in the Middle Eastern culinary world is shawarma, which features tender, marinated meat cooked on a vertical rotisserie. Another popular option is falafel, a deep-fried vegetarian ball made of chickpeas or fava beans, combined with a variety of herbs and spices. Both of these dishes are incredibly versatile and can be served with a variety of toppings and sauces, making them perfect for customization.

Ingredients for Shawarma

To make an authentic shawarma at home, the most important aspect is marinating the meat properly to allow the flavors to penetrate. Here’s a list of ingredients for shawarma:

For the Shawarma Marinade:

  • 1 lb boneless chicken thighs (you can also use beef or lamb for a more traditional variation)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt (Greek yogurt works well)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper (optional for extra heat)

For the Shawarma Wrap:

  • Pita bread or flatbreads (whole wheat or regular)
  • Lettuce (shredded)
  • Tomatoes, sliced
  • Cucumber, thinly sliced
  • Red onions, thinly sliced
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley
  • Tahini sauce or garlic sauce

Ingredients for Falafel

Falafel is an excellent vegetarian option for those who prefer plant-based meals. These crunchy, savory balls are packed with flavor from a blend of chickpeas and herbs. Here’s what you’ll need to make falafel:

For the Falafel Mix:

  • 1 ½ cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight) or 2 cans of chickpeas, drained
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2-4 tablespoons flour (to help bind the falafel)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Falafel Wrap:

  • Pita bread or flatbreads
  • Lettuce, shredded
  • Tomatoes, sliced
  • Cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • Pickled turnips (optional, but adds a tangy crunch)
  • Tahini sauce or yogurt-based garlic sauce

How to Make Shawarma at Home

Making shawarma at home involves marinating the meat, cooking it to perfection, and assembling the wrap with fresh ingredients. Here’s how you can do it:

Step 1: Prepare the Shawarma Marinade

Begin by preparing the shawarma marinade. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, yogurt, garlic, lemon juice, cumin, paprika, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, allspice, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix everything together to create a smooth paste.

Add the chicken thighs to the marinade, making sure each piece is coated thoroughly. Cover the bowl and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, though overnight marination is ideal for the best flavor.

Step 2: Cook the Shawarma

Once the chicken is marinated, it’s time to cook it. If you have a grill or barbecue, preheat it to medium-high heat. Alternatively, you can cook the chicken in a skillet or oven.

If grilling, place the chicken on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through. If using a skillet, heat a little oil over medium-high heat, and cook the chicken for about 6-8 minutes on each side. Once cooked, let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing it thinly.

Step 3: Assemble the Shawarma Wrap

To assemble the shawarma wrap, lay a warm pita or flatbread on a plate. Add a few slices of the cooked chicken, and then top with fresh vegetables such as lettuce, tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion. Drizzle with tahini sauce or garlic sauce for added flavor. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley, then fold the sides of the flatbread and roll it up tightly.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Your homemade shawarma wrap is ready to enjoy! Serve it with a side of crispy fries or a fresh salad for a complete meal.

How to Make Falafel at Home

Falafel is a great vegetarian alternative that is just as delicious as shawarma. Here’s how you can make your own falafel from scratch:

Step 1: Prepare the Falafel Mix

Start by soaking the dried chickpeas in water overnight. If you’re using canned chickpeas, simply drain and rinse them. Place the soaked chickpeas in a food processor, along with the chopped onion, parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, cayenne pepper, baking soda, salt, and pepper. Pulse everything together until the mixture becomes coarse and crumbly.

If the mixture feels too wet, add a bit of flour, one tablespoon at a time, until it holds together when shaped into balls. Once ready, transfer the mixture to a bowl, cover it, and let it rest in the refrigerator for about an hour. This step is essential for the falafel to hold its shape when frying.

Step 2: Fry the Falafel

Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or pot over medium heat. Using your hands or a spoon, shape the falafel mixture into small balls, about the size of a walnut. Once the oil is hot, carefully drop the falafel balls into the oil, frying them in batches to avoid overcrowding.

Fry the falafel for about 3-4 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides. Remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Step 3: Assemble the Falafel Wrap

To assemble your falafel wrap, place a warm pita or flatbread on a plate. Add 2-3 falafel balls to the center of the bread, then top with fresh vegetables like lettuce, tomato, cucumber, and pickled turnips. Drizzle with tahini sauce or garlic yogurt sauce, and garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro. Roll up the wrap, and it’s ready to enjoy!

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Your homemade falafel wrap is a perfect vegetarian option for lunch or dinner. It’s packed with flavor and texture, making it a satisfying meal. Serve it with a side of roasted vegetables or a refreshing cucumber salad to complete the meal.

Tips for Perfect Middle Eastern Wraps

  • Marinate the meat well: The key to a flavorful shawarma is a good marinade. Let the meat marinate for several hours (or overnight) to allow the spices to fully infuse.
  • Use fresh, quality ingredients: Whether you’re making shawarma or falafel, using fresh vegetables and herbs will elevate the flavors in your wraps.
  • Customize your sauces: You can experiment with different sauces, such as garlic sauce, tahini, or even yogurt-based sauces, depending on your preference.
  • Fry falafel at the right temperature: Make sure the oil is hot enough (around 350°F/175°C) before frying the falafel, to achieve the perfect crispy exterior.
  • Experiment with fillings: While the traditional fillings for shawarma and falafel include lettuce, tomato, cucumber, and onions, don’t hesitate to add pickles, olives, or even roasted vegetables for extra flavor.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern wraps, whether it’s shawarma or falafel, are an irresistible combination of flavors and textures that are sure to satisfy any craving. By following these recipes and tips, you can easily recreate these beloved dishes in your own kitchen. Whether you choose the juicy, spiced chicken shawarma or the crispy, herb-packed falafel, you’re in for a delicious meal that brings the vibrant flavors of the Middle East to your table. Enjoy the process of making these wraps from scratch, and don’t forget to customize them to your taste with your favorite fillings and sauces. Happy cooking!
