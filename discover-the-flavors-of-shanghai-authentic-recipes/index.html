Discover the Flavors of Shanghai: Authentic Recipes


Shanghai, a vibrant city in China, is known not only for its stunning skyline and bustling streets but also for its incredible culinary scene. The flavors of Shanghai are diverse, with a combination of sweet, savory, and tangy tastes that will tantalize your taste buds. In this blog post, we will explore some authentic Shanghai recipes that will transport you to the streets of this vibrant city.

Steamed Shanghai Soup Dumplings (Xiao Long Bao)

Xiao Long Bao, also known as steamed Shanghai soup dumplings, are a popular street food in Shanghai. These delicate dumplings are filled with flavorful broth and minced pork, creating a burst of flavors in every bite. Here’s how you can recreate this authentic dish in your own kitchen:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/2 pound minced pork
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and warm water. Knead until a smooth dough forms. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the minced pork, chicken broth, soy sauce, sesame oil, grated ginger, green onion, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined.
  3. Roll out the dough into a thin sheet and cut out small circles using a round cookie cutter.
  4. Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the edges together and pinch to seal, creating a small dumpling.
  5. Arrange the dumplings in a steamer basket and steam for about 10 minutes, or until the dumplings are cooked through.
  6. Serve hot with soy sauce for dipping.

Shanghai Fried Rice

Shanghai fried rice is a classic dish that showcases the flavors of Shanghai. It is a simple yet satisfying meal that can be easily prepared at home. Here’s how you can make this delicious dish:

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice, chilled
  • 1/2 cup cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup diced chicken breast
  • 1/4 cup diced carrots
  • 1/4 cup frozen peas
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced chicken breast and cook until no longer pink. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the diced carrots and frozen peas. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs into the other side. Scramble the eggs until cooked through.
  5. Add the cooked rice, shrimp, and cooked chicken back to the pan. Stir-fry for a few minutes until everything is heated through.
  6. Stir in the soy sauce and oyster sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Continue to stir-fry for another minute, ensuring that all the ingredients are well combined.
  8. Remove from heat and serve hot.

Shanghai-Style Braised Pork Belly (Hong Shao Rou)

Hong Shao Rou, or Shanghai-style braised pork belly, is a rich and flavorful dish that is often served as a main course in Shanghai. The pork belly is slow-cooked in a savory sauce, resulting in tender and melt-in-your-mouth meat. Here’s how you can make this indulgent dish:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pork belly, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup dark soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice wine
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 slices ginger
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the pork belly pieces and cook until browned on all sides. Remove from the pot and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the minced garlic and ginger slices. Stir-fry for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice wine, brown sugar, and chicken broth to the pot. Stir until the sugar has dissolved.
  5. Return the pork belly to the pot and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork is tender.
  6. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
  7. Once the pork is tender, remove the lid and increase the heat to medium-high. Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  8. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

Conclusion

Shanghai’s cuisine is a testament to the city’s rich culinary heritage. From the delicate soup dumplings to the flavorful fried rice and indulgent braised pork belly, these authentic Shanghai recipes offer a glimpse into the vibrant flavors that make this city a food lover’s paradise. So, put on your apron, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to Shanghai right in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
