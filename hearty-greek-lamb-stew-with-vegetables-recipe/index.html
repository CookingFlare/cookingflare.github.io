Hearty Greek Lamb Stew with Vegetables Recipe


A classic dish that brings together the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean, the Hearty Greek Lamb Stew with Vegetables is a comforting meal that can satisfy your cravings for something rich, nourishing, and delicious. This stew is a perfect blend of tender lamb, fresh vegetables, and a medley of herbs and spices that transport you straight to the sun-drenched hills of Greece. Ideal for cozy dinners and family gatherings, it’s a dish that’s both hearty and wholesome.

The Origins of Greek Lamb Stew

Greek cuisine is known for its simple yet bold flavors, often making use of fresh ingredients that are abundant in the region. Lamb is a staple in many traditional Greek dishes, particularly stews, where it’s paired with vegetables, olive oil, and a variety of seasonings. The combination of lamb with vegetables like tomatoes, potatoes, and carrots is a favorite, providing both substance and flavor to the dish.

This recipe draws inspiration from the traditional Greek “kleftiko” lamb stew, which is slow-cooked to perfection. The long cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, creating a dish that’s bursting with aromatic, savory goodness.

Key Ingredients for Greek Lamb Stew

To make this Hearty Greek Lamb Stew with Vegetables, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Lamb:

  • 2 lbs (900g) bone-in lamb shoulder – Bone-in cuts are ideal for slow-cooking, as they impart a rich flavor to the stew.
  • Olive oil – For browning the lamb and sautéing the vegetables.
  • 1 medium onion, diced – A foundational ingredient that adds sweetness and depth to the stew.
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced – Garlic infuses the stew with its fragrant essence.
  • 1 cup dry white wine – This adds acidity and helps tenderize the lamb.
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes – A base for the sauce that brings juiciness and tanginess.
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste – For a more concentrated tomato flavor.
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar – Adds a slight tang to balance the richness of the lamb.
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano – Oregano is a classic herb in Greek cooking, adding a robust and earthy flavor.
  • 1 tsp ground cumin – This spice provides warmth and complexity to the stew.
  • 1 tsp cinnamon – A pinch of cinnamon gives the dish a touch of sweetness and warmth, characteristic of Greek stews.
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth – A flavorful base that helps create the stew’s savory liquid.

For the Vegetables:

  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks – Potatoes absorb the flavors of the stew, adding creaminess as they cook.
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced – Sweet carrots complement the savory lamb perfectly.
  • 1 zucchini, sliced – Adds a delicate texture to the stew and absorbs the rich broth.
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed – Green beans add crunch and freshness, balancing the tenderness of the lamb.
  • 1 bell pepper, diced – For a touch of color and sweetness.

Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped – Adds a fresh and herbaceous note when sprinkled on top.
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional) – For a creamy and tangy finish that enhances the Greek flavors of the stew.

How to Make Greek Lamb Stew with Vegetables

Step 1: Brown the Lamb

Start by heating the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the lamb shoulder pieces and brown them on all sides. This will take about 8-10 minutes. Browning the lamb helps to develop rich flavors and creates a caramelized crust that will infuse the stew with a deep, savory taste.

Once the lamb is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside. You may want to work in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté them over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

This step builds the foundation of flavor for the stew. The onion and garlic will soften and release their natural sweetness, which will blend perfectly with the lamb.

Step 3: Deglaze the Pot

Once the onion and garlic are softened, pour in the dry white wine. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. These little caramelized pieces are packed with flavor and will make your stew even more delicious.

Allow the wine to simmer for 2-3 minutes, letting the alcohol cook off while the wine adds depth to the sauce.

Step 4: Add the Tomatoes and Seasonings

Add the diced tomatoes (with their juices), tomato paste, red wine vinegar, oregano, cumin, and cinnamon to the pot. Stir to combine, making sure the tomato paste dissolves into the liquid. Let everything simmer for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 5: Return the Lamb to the Pot

Now, return the browned lamb pieces to the pot, nestling them into the tomato and herb mixture. Add the chicken or vegetable broth, making sure the lamb is mostly submerged. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot.

Let the stew simmer gently for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the lamb becomes fork-tender and easily pulls apart. The slow cooking process ensures the lamb absorbs all the wonderful flavors and becomes melt-in-your-mouth tender.

Step 6: Add the Vegetables

After the lamb has cooked, it’s time to add the vegetables. Start by adding the potatoes, carrots, zucchini, green beans, and bell pepper to the pot. Stir everything together, making sure the vegetables are evenly distributed.

Cover the pot again and let the stew cook for another 30-45 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the stew has thickened slightly. The vegetables will soak up the flavors from the broth, becoming perfectly tender and flavorful.

Step 7: Final Adjustments

Once the vegetables are done, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you prefer a bit more acidity, feel free to add another splash of red wine vinegar or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. If you like it spicier, a pinch of red pepper flakes can be added for a little kick.

Step 8: Serve and Garnish

Ladle the hearty lamb stew into bowls, making sure to include a good amount of vegetables with each serving. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley and, if desired, crumbled feta cheese for an extra layer of tanginess and richness.

Serve the stew hot with warm crusty bread on the side, perfect for dipping into the savory broth. You can also pair the dish with a light, crisp salad for a balanced meal.

Tips for the Best Greek Lamb Stew

  • Choose the right cut of lamb: A bone-in shoulder or shank is ideal for slow-cooked stews. These cuts become incredibly tender when simmered for a long time and offer a rich, deep flavor.
  • Let the stew rest: Like many stews, this dish often tastes even better the next day. The flavors continue to develop as it sits, making it an excellent option for leftovers.
  • Adjust the vegetables: You can switch out some of the vegetables depending on what’s in season or what you prefer. Leeks, parsnips, or even eggplant would work well in this stew.
  • Make it a one-pot meal: This stew is already loaded with protein, vegetables, and hearty flavor, so it’s a perfect stand-alone meal. If you prefer a lighter version, you can serve it alongside a simple side of rice or couscous.
