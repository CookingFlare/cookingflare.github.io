Fresh and Light: 4 Citrus-Infused Marinades for Grilled Shrimp


Are you looking for a delicious and refreshing way to elevate your grilled shrimp? Look no further! In this recipe blog post, we will explore four citrus-infused marinades that will add a burst of flavor and brightness to your grilled shrimp. These marinades are not only easy to make but also perfect for those who prefer fresh and light dishes. Let’s dive in!

1. Tangy Lemon and Herb Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the raw shrimp in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish and pour the marinade over the shrimp.
  3. Toss the shrimp to ensure they are evenly coated with the marinade.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight for maximum flavor.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the shrimp from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off.
  7. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side until they are cooked through and have a slight char.
  8. Serve immediately and enjoy the tangy and herbaceous flavors of this marinade.

2. Zesty Orange and Ginger Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine orange juice, soy sauce, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes if desired.
  2. Place the shrimp in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them.
  3. Gently toss the shrimp to ensure they are fully coated with the marinade.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the shrimp from the marinade, shaking off any excess.
  7. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side until they are pink and opaque.
  8. Plate the shrimp and garnish with some fresh cilantro or green onions if desired.
  9. Savor the vibrant and zesty flavors of this citrus-infused marinade.

3. Refreshing Lime and Coconut Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon grated lime zest
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine lime juice, coconut milk, honey, soy sauce, grated lime zest, minced garlic, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the shrimp to a ziplock bag or a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them.
  3. Toss the shrimp gently to ensure they are evenly coated.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to develop.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the shrimp from the marinade, discarding any excess.
  7. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side until they are opaque and nicely charred.
  8. Transfer the shrimp to a serving platter and sprinkle some fresh cilantro on top for an extra burst of freshness.
  9. Experience the delightful combination of tangy lime and creamy coconut in this marinade.

4. Citrusy Grapefruit and Chili Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed grapefruit juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together grapefruit juice, olive oil, honey, chili powder, ground cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the shrimp in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them.
  3. Toss the shrimp gently to ensure they are well-coated with the marinade.
  4. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the shrimp from the marinade, shaking off any excess.
  7. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side until they are pink and cooked through.
  8. Serve the shrimp with a squeeze of fresh grapefruit juice and a sprinkle of chopped cilantro for a burst of citrusy goodness.
  9. Enjoy the perfect balance of tangy grapefruit and smoky spices in this marinade.

Conclusion

Grilled shrimp is a versatile and delicious dish that can be enjoyed year-round. These four citrus-infused marinades provide a fresh and light twist to your traditional grilled shrimp. Whether you prefer tangy lemon, zesty orange, refreshing lime, or citrusy grapefruit, these marinades will elevate your shrimp to a whole new level of flavor. So fire up your grill, marinate your shrimp, and get ready to savor the vibrant taste of summer!
