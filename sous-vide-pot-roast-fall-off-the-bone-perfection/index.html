Sous Vide Pot Roast: Fall-Off-The-Bone Perfection


If you’re a fan of tender, succulent pot roast that practically melts in your mouth, then you’re in for a treat. Sous vide cooking is the secret to achieving the perfect pot roast that’s guaranteed to impress your family and friends. This cooking method ensures that the meat is cooked to precision, resulting in an unbelievably tender and flavorful dish. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps of preparing a sous vide pot roast that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pounds of beef chuck roast
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 1/2 cup of red wine
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Beef

  1. Begin by seasoning the chuck roast generously with salt and pepper. This will help to enhance the flavor of the meat.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, sear the chuck roast on all sides until it develops a rich, golden-brown crust. This step will create a beautiful depth of flavor in the final dish.

Step 2: Preparing the Vegetables and Aromatics

  1. Place the sliced onion, minced garlic, chopped carrots, and celery into a large resealable bag.
  2. Add the seared chuck roast to the bag, along with the fresh rosemary and thyme sprigs.
  3. Pour the beef broth and red wine into the bag, ensuring that the ingredients are evenly distributed.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Seal the bag using the water displacement method, making sure to remove as much air as possible before sealing.
  2. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare, or 145°F (63°C) for medium doneness.
  3. Submerge the bag in the water bath and cook for 24-48 hours, depending on your desired level of tenderness. The long cooking time will allow the collagen in the chuck roast to break down, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

  1. Once the chuck roast has finished cooking, carefully remove it from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels.
  2. Strain the cooking liquid and vegetables from the bag, and transfer the liquid to a saucepan.
  3. Simmer the liquid over medium heat until it reduces and thickens slightly, creating a rich and flavorful sauce to accompany the pot roast.

Step 5: Serving

  1. Slice the pot roast against the grain into thick, luscious pieces.
  2. Drizzle the reduced sauce over the slices of pot roast for an extra burst of flavor.
  3. Serve the sous vide pot roast alongside your favorite sides, such as creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or buttered dinner rolls.

Conclusion

Sous vide pot roast is a game-changer when it comes to achieving unparalleled tenderness and flavor in your favorite comfort food. This method allows you to create a truly unforgettable dining experience for yourself and your loved ones. So, fire up your sous vide machine, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the most delectable pot roast you’ve ever tasted. Happy cooking!
