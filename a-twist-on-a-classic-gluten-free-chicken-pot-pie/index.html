A Twist on a Classic: Gluten-Free Chicken Pot Pie


Are you a fan of classic comfort foods but struggle with gluten intolerance? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll show you how to put a delicious twist on a beloved classic: Gluten-Free Chicken Pot Pie. With a flaky and buttery crust and a creamy, flavorful filling, this dish is sure to become a new family favorite. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon xanthan gum
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/3 cup ice water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried sage
  • 2 cups cooked chicken breast, shredded
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, xanthan gum, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Slowly add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.

  5. Sprinkle the gluten-free all-purpose flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to cook off the raw flour taste.

  6. Gradually whisk in the chicken broth and almond milk, making sure there are no lumps. Add the dried thyme, dried rosemary, and dried sage. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until thickened, about 5 minutes.

  7. Stir in the shredded chicken and frozen peas. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat.

  8. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit your baking dish. Place the filling in the dish and cover it with the rolled-out dough, crimping the edges to seal.

  9. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking. Place the pot pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  10. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy this twist on a classic comfort food!

Conclusion

This gluten-free chicken pot pie recipe is a delicious twist on a classic dish that even those with gluten intolerance can enjoy. The flaky and buttery crust paired with the creamy and flavorful filling will have your taste buds dancing with joy. So why not give this recipe a try and see for yourself? Your family and friends will be impressed by your culinary skills and grateful for this scrumptious gluten-free option. Happy cooking!
