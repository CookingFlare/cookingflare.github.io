Top Tools You Need for Taco and Burrito Making


When it comes to tacos and burritos, the magic lies not only in the flavors but also in the tools you use to create them. From the perfect tortillas to stuffing your favorite fillings, having the right kitchen equipment makes the experience easier, faster, and much more fun. Whether you’re hosting taco night or whipping up burritos for meal prep, using proper tools will help you elevate your Mexican-inspired creations to the next level. Here’s everything you need to transform your kitchen into a taco and burrito-making haven.

1. Tortilla Press

A sturdy tortilla press helps create consistently thin and round tortillas, ensuring that they’re perfect for holding all your fillings. While store-bought tortillas are convenient, homemade tortillas bring authenticity and freshness that is unparalleled. Typically made from cast iron or aluminum, tortilla presses flatten the dough evenly, saving you time and energy compared to rolling out each piece by hand.

How to Use:

  1. Prepare your tortilla dough with your choice of masa harina or flour mix.
  2. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls.
  3. Place a ball between two pieces of parchment paper inside the tortilla press and press firmly.

Homemade tortillas can cook quickly on a griddle or frying pan and add a distinct, fresh taste to tacos and burritos.

2. Comal or Griddle

The comal is a traditional Mexican cooking tool used for heating tortillas, roasting vegetables, or toasting spices. This flat, smooth cooking surface evenly distributes heat, ensuring your tortillas—whether corn or flour—are warm, pliable, and ready for filling. If you don’t have a traditional comal, a heavy-duty griddle or cast iron skillet will work just as well.

Why You Need It:

Perfectly heated tortillas make all the difference. A cold tortilla will crack under the pressure of fillings, while a warm one stays sturdy and flexible. Additionally, you can use the comal to char your salsa ingredients like tomatoes, jalapeños, and onions.

3. Chef’s Knife

A high-quality chef’s knife is essential for preparing the ingredients that go into your tacos and burritos. From slicing succulent skirt steak for carne asada tacos to chopping up crisp vegetables for a fresh pico de gallo, your knife should be sharp, balanced, and reliable.

How to Choose:

  • Opt for a knife that feels comfortable in your hand and has a stainless steel blade.
  • Ensure it’s razor-sharp for precision cuts on delicate ingredients, like herbs.

A dull knife can ruin the texture and presentation of ingredients, so keeping it sharpened is just as important as choosing the right one.

4. Chopping Board

Pair your chef’s knife with a sturdy chopping board. A wooden or plastic cutting board provides a stable base to dice, slice, and chop your taco and burrito components, ensuring safety while prepping. Many home cooks prefer boards with a juice groove to catch liquids from marinated meats or chopped tomatoes.

Bonus Tip:

Consider dedicating one board for vegetables and another for proteins to avoid cross-contamination.

5. Mandoline or Vegetable Slicer

Shredding lettuce, slicing onions, and julienning veggies for tacos can be tedious without the right tools. A mandoline or vegetable slicer simplifies these tasks, producing uniform slices in seconds and saving you plenty of prep time. Uniformly sliced vegetables are easier to layer in tacos and burritos, creating a visually appealing dish every time.

Pro Tip:

Adjust the thickness setting to customize how thin or thick you want your veggies. Just be careful when handling the sharp blade; a safety guard can prevent accidents.

6. Pots and Pans for Cooking Fillings

A taco or burrito is only as good as its fillings. To cook meat fillings like carnitas, shredded chicken, or seasoned ground beef, you’ll need high-quality pots and pans. Cast iron pans are ideal for retaining heat and achieving crisp edges on proteins, while stainless steel pots are perfect for simmering beans or braising meats.

Versatility:

Use a large skillet for sautéing vegetables or frying carnitas, and a slow cooker or Instant Pot for preparing tender beef barbacoa or pork.

7. Mixing Bowls

Durable mixing bowls serve a multitude of purposes when making tacos and burritos. From tossing vegetables in lime juice for a zesty garnish to mixing homemade marinades for your proteins, mixing bowls are indispensable.

Tip for Choosing:

Invest in a variety of bowl sizes to handle all parts of your meal prep—from creating sauce to storing leftovers.

8. Citrus Juicer

Citrus is a cornerstone of Mexican flavors. Whether you’re squeezing fresh lime juice for guacamole, pico de gallo, marinade, or taco toppings, a citrus juicer can help you extract every drop of juice without the mess. Manual handheld juicers are compact and easy to use, while larger electric versions can save time if you’re preparing in bulk.

Why It’s Important:

Fresh lime juice enhances just about every component of your taco or burrito, bringing tangy brightness to each bite.

9. Mortar and Pestle (Molcajete)

A mortar and pestle, or molcajete, is the traditional tool for grinding spices, mashing avocado into guacamole, and preparing handmade salsas. This age-old kitchen tool gives you better control over textures compared to modern blenders, resulting in chunky guacamole or rustic salsa that bursts with flavor.

Usage:

  • Grind roasted garlic, chili peppers, and salt for homemade salsa roja.
  • Mash ripe avocados with lime and cilantro for guacamole.

10. Measuring Cups and Spoons

Precision is key when it comes to seasoning taco and burrito filling. Measuring tools ensure that your spices, marinades, and sauces are perfectly balanced. For instance, adding the right amount of cumin or chili powder to your beans will enhance their flavor without overpowering them.

Quick Tip:

Keep both dry and liquid measuring tools on hand—you’ll need them for everything from spice blends to mixing masa dough.

11. Toppings Containers or Bowls

Part of the joy of tacos and burritos lies in customizing them with a variety of toppings. Use small containers or bowls to organize and serve toppings like shredded cheese, chopped cilantro, diced tomatoes, onions, crema, and hot sauce. This not only makes it easier for guests to serve themselves but also keeps your kitchen clean and organized.

Presentation Matters:

Choose colorful or traditional Mexican-style bowls to make your taco night visually appealing.

12. Taco Holders

Tacos are amazing—until they spill over on the plate. Taco holders keep tacos upright, preventing fillings from spilling out so everyone can get the perfect bite. These handy tools come in multi-slot designs made from stainless steel, plastic, or ceramic.

Bonus Use:

Beyond tacos, taco holders can also be great for organizing burritos while serving or displaying beautifully rolled tortillas.

13. Foil or Parchment Paper

If burritos are the star of the night, foil or parchment paper is a practical tool you shouldn’t overlook. Wrapping burritos tightly in foil after assembling helps maintain their shape and keeps them warm longer. Parchment paper, meanwhile, can be used to wrap your burritos for easy meal prep or secure tacos for on-the-go eating.

For Meal Prep:

Use foil-wrapped burritos to store in the fridge or freeze for later meals. Just reheat and enjoy without losing flavor or texture!

14. Blender or Food Processor

For silky smooth quesadilla sauce or salsa verde, having a powerful blender or food processor comes in handy. Freshly blended sauces and dips always elevate tacos and burritos, delivering authentic Mexican flavors with minimal effort.

Recipes to Try:

  • Blend roasted tomatillos, garlic, and jalapeños for salsa verde.
  • Whip up a creamy chipotle sauce with yogurt or sour cream.

With these essential tools in your taco and burrito-making arsenal, you’ll bring the flavors of Mexico right into your kitchen with ease and authenticity. Whether you’re preparing fresh tortillas, crafting homemade salsa, or stuffing burritos with your favorite fillings, the right equipment will simplify the process and enhance the final result. Explore these tools, experiment with ingredients, and get ready to delight family and friends with your culinary creations!
