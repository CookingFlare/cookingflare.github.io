Grilled Fourth of July Vegetable Recipes for a Flavorful BBQ


The Fourth of July is a time for celebration, and what better way to celebrate than with a flavorful BBQ? While most BBQs focus on meats, we shouldn’t overlook the delicious and healthy option of grilled vegetables. Grilled veggies can add a burst of color, flavor, and nutrition to your Independence Day feast. Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or simply looking to add some variety to your BBQ spread, these grilled vegetable recipes will be a hit with everyone at your party.

Grilled Corn with Chili Lime Butter

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • Zest of 2 limes
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, lime zest, lime juice, chili powder, salt, and black pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Place the husked corn on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until the kernels are tender and slightly charred.
  4. Remove the corn from the grill and generously spread the chili lime butter over each ear.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the zesty, smoky flavors of this grilled corn.

Grilled Veggie Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 green zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 oz. mushrooms, cleaned
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak wooden skewers in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating colors and varieties.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, minced garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  5. Brush the vegetable skewers with the seasoned oil mixture.
  6. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  7. Serve the grilled veggie skewers as a colorful and flavorful side dish.

Grilled Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, any color
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  3. In a large bowl, mix together the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, cheddar cheese, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stuff the bell peppers with the quinoa and vegetable mixture.
  5. Place the stuffed bell peppers on the grill and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.
  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving.

These grilled vegetable recipes are not only delicious and nutritious but also easy to prepare, making them perfect for your Fourth of July BBQ. So fire up the grill, celebrate with friends and family, and enjoy the vibrant flavors of summer vegetables. Happy Fourth of July!
