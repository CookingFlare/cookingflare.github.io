Mixed Berry Muffins: A Burst of Fruity Goodness


There’s something undeniably delightful about biting into a warm, freshly baked mixed berry muffin. The juicy bursts of berries, the tender crumb, and the hint of sweetness - it’s a perfect treat for any time of day. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, these mixed berry muffins are a must-try. So, let’s dive into the recipe and discover the secrets to creating these fruity delights in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Wet Ingredients:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Mixed Berries:

  • 1 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen) - such as raspberries, blueberries, and chopped strawberries

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Batter

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well to ensure even distribution of the leavening agents.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients: yogurt, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Ensure that the mixture is smooth and well combined.
  4. Gently fold the mixed berries into the wet ingredients, being careful not to overmix. This will help prevent the berries from bleeding into the batter.

Step 2: Mixing and Baking

  1. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, and fold everything together until just combined. Be sure not to overmix, as this can result in tough muffins.
  2. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about two-thirds full.
  3. Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  4. Once baked, remove the muffins from the oven and allow them to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 3: Enjoying the Fruity Goodness

  1. Once the muffins have cooled slightly, it’s time to enjoy the burst of fruity goodness. These mixed berry muffins are best enjoyed warm, but they can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or frozen for longer storage.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle the muffin tops with a little coarse sugar before baking.
  • Feel free to customize the berry combination to suit your preferences. Blackberries, cranberries, or even a mix of tropical fruits can create delightful variations.
  • If using frozen berries, do not thaw them before adding to the batter to prevent excessive bleeding.

Final Thoughts

These mixed berry muffins are a celebration of the summer harvest, packed with the vibrant flavors of assorted berries. Whether enjoyed as a quick breakfast, a midday snack, or a sweet ending to a meal, these muffins are sure to bring a smile to your face. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the fruity goodness of these muffins brighten your day!
