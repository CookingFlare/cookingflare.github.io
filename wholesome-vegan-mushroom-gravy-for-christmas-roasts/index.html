Wholesome Vegan Mushroom Gravy for Christmas Roasts


As the holiday season approaches, the aroma of festive feasts fills the air, creating an atmosphere of warmth, joy, and togetherness. For those of us who embrace a plant-based lifestyle, the challenge often lies in finding that perfect sauce or gravy that complements our holiday roasts. Enter the Wholesome Vegan Mushroom Gravy—a rich, flavorful, and satisfying addition that will elevate your Christmas dinner to new heights.

This recipe not only offers a savory depth that is sure to please both vegans and non-vegans alike but also incorporates wholesome ingredients that are easy to find. Let’s delve into the world of umami flavors with this hearty mushroom gravy that pairs beautifully with roasted vegetables, lentil loafs, and nut roasts.

Ingredients

To create this delicious vegan mushroom gravy, you will need:

  • Mushrooms: 500g of mixed mushrooms such as cremini, shiitake, and button mushrooms, finely chopped. These varieties provide a wonderful depth of flavor.
  • Onion: 1 large onion, diced. The sweetness of the onion will enhance the overall taste of the gravy.
  • Garlic: 4 cloves of garlic, minced. Garlic adds a lovely aromatic quality.
  • Vegetable broth: 2 cups of low-sodium vegetable broth for a balanced flavor.
  • Soy sauce or tamari: 2 tablespoons for a salty, umami kick.
  • Nutritional yeast: 2 tablespoons, which lends a cheesy flavor without dairy.
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons for sautéing the vegetables.
  • All-purpose flour or cornstarch: 2 tablespoons, to thicken the gravy.
  • Thyme: 1 teaspoon of dried thyme or 2 teaspoons of fresh thyme for a herby aroma.
  • Black pepper: To taste.
  • Salt: To taste.

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Mushrooms

Start by cleaning your mushrooms. Wipe them with a damp cloth to remove any dirt. Avoid rinsing them under water, as mushrooms absorb moisture, which can make your gravy watery. Once cleaned, finely chop the mushrooms.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large skillet or saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes translucent. Next, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant. Stir in the chopped mushrooms, and let them cook for 7-10 minutes until they release their moisture and become golden brown.

Step 3: Create the Gravy Base

Once the mushrooms are beautifully browned, sprinkle the flour (or cornstarch) over the mushroom mixture and stir well to coat all the ingredients. This step is crucial as it helps to create a roux, which will thicken your gravy. Cook for another minute to eliminate the raw flour taste.

Step 4: Add Liquids and Seasonings

Gradually pour in the vegetable broth while continuously stirring to prevent lumps from forming. Add the soy sauce (or tamari), nutritional yeast, thyme, black pepper, and salt. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, allowing it to thicken for about 5-7 minutes. If the gravy becomes too thick, you can add more broth or water to reach your desired consistency.

Step 5: Taste and Adjust

Once the gravy has reached your desired thickness, taste it. Adjust the seasoning as necessary, adding more salt or pepper to suit your palate. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can blend the gravy using an immersion blender or transfer it to a regular blender and blend until smooth.

Step 6: Serve

Your Wholesome Vegan Mushroom Gravy is now ready to be served! Pour it generously over your favorite holiday roasts, mashed potatoes, or roasted vegetables. This gravy is not only delicious but also versatile, making it a great addition to any festive meal.

Why This Gravy Works for Christmas Roasts

The holiday season is all about indulgence, and this vegan mushroom gravy delivers that richness without compromising on health. The blend of mushrooms provides a depth of flavor comparable to traditional gravies, while the nutritional yeast adds a cheesy note that rounds out the taste perfectly.

Moreover, the use of fresh herbs like thyme brings a seasonal touch that complements many classic Christmas flavors. This gravy pairs wonderfully with lentil loaves and nut roasts, making it a fantastic centerpiece for your plant-based holiday feast.

Storage and Reheating

If you find yourself with leftover gravy, don’t worry! This mushroom gravy can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. To reheat, simply warm it on the stove over low heat, adding a splash of vegetable broth if it has thickened too much.

For longer storage, this gravy can be frozen for up to three months. Just make sure to cool it completely before transferring it to a freezer-safe container. When ready to use, thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and reheat as described above.

Pairing Suggestions

While this mushroom gravy is fantastic on its own, consider some delicious pairing options to create a complete holiday meal:

  • Roasted Brussels Sprouts: Their slight bitterness and crispy texture contrast beautifully with the rich gravy.
  • Garlic Mashed Potatoes: Creamy mashed potatoes drenched in mushroom gravy is a classic comfort food combination.
  • Savory Stuffing: Drizzle this gravy over your stuffing for added moisture and flavor.
  • Cranberry Sauce: For those who enjoy a sweet and savory blend, a spoonful of cranberry sauce alongside your gravy can create a delightful contrast.

Conclusion

This Wholesome Vegan Mushroom Gravy is the perfect companion for your Christmas roasts and festive meals. Its rich and savory profile is sure to satisfy everyone at the table, regardless of their dietary preferences. By using simple, wholesome ingredients, you can create a vegan gravy that is not only delicious but also nourishing.

This holiday season, embrace the spirit of togetherness and joy by sharing a meal that everyone can enjoy. With this mushroom gravy, you can celebrate the season with a dish that embodies the warmth and love of family gatherings. Enjoy every drop of this savory goodness, and let it become a cherished part of your holiday traditions for years to come!
