The Classic Combo: 4 Garlic and Herb Rubs for Steaks


There’s nothing quite like a perfectly seasoned steak, seared to perfection and bursting with flavor. And when it comes to enhancing the natural taste of a juicy steak, garlic and herb rubs are a classic choice. These aromatic blends not only infuse the meat with rich, savory flavors but also add a touch of freshness that elevates the overall dining experience. In this blog post, we’ll explore four different garlic and herb rubs that will take your steak to the next level. So, let’s dive right in!

1. The Classic Garlic and Rosemary Rub

This timeless combination of garlic and rosemary is a match made in culinary heaven. To prepare this rub, start by finely mincing three cloves of garlic. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic with two tablespoons of dried rosemary, one tablespoon of kosher salt, one teaspoon of black pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil. Mix everything together until well combined. Generously coat both sides of your steak with the rub, ensuring that it is evenly distributed. Allow the steak to marinate for at least thirty minutes before grilling or pan-searing to your desired level of doneness. The fragrant aroma of garlic and rosemary will make your taste buds dance with delight.

2. The Zesty Garlic and Lemon Rub

If you’re looking to add a tangy twist to your steak, this zesty garlic and lemon rub is the way to go. Start by zesting one lemon and mincing three cloves of garlic. In a mixing bowl, combine the lemon zest, minced garlic, two tablespoons of dried oregano, one tablespoon of kosher salt, one teaspoon of black pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined. Rub this delightful mixture onto your steak, ensuring that every inch is coated. Let the flavors meld together for at least thirty minutes before cooking. The combination of garlic, lemon, and herbs will create a burst of freshness with every bite.

3. The Spicy Garlic and Paprika Rub

For those who like a little kick in their steak, this spicy garlic and paprika rub will deliver the perfect amount of heat. Begin by mincing three cloves of garlic and combining it with two tablespoons of smoked paprika, one tablespoon of chili powder, one teaspoon of cumin, one teaspoon of brown sugar, one teaspoon of kosher salt, and a drizzle of olive oil. Mix until all the ingredients are blended together. Coat your steak generously with this spicy rub, ensuring that it is evenly distributed. Allow the flavors to mingle for thirty minutes before cooking. The combination of garlic, paprika, and spices will create a tantalizing heat that will leave your taste buds tingling.

4. The Herbaceous Garlic and Thyme Rub

If you’re a fan of aromatic herbs, this herbaceous garlic and thyme rub will be a delightful addition to your steak. Start by mincing three cloves of garlic and combining it with two tablespoons of dried thyme, one tablespoon of dried parsley, one teaspoon of dried basil, one teaspoon of kosher salt, one teaspoon of black pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil. Mix everything together until well combined. Coat your steak generously with this fragrant rub, making sure it is evenly coated. Allow the flavors to infuse for thirty minutes before cooking. The combination of garlic and a medley of herbs will transport you to a culinary paradise.

In conclusion, these four garlic and herb rubs are sure to elevate your steak game. Whether you prefer the classic combination of garlic and rosemary, the zesty twist of garlic and lemon, the spicy kick of garlic and paprika, or the herbaceous blend of garlic and thyme, each rub offers its own unique flavor profile. Experiment with these rubs to find your favorite, and enjoy the delectable results. So, fire up your grill or heat up your skillet, and get ready to savor the classic combo of garlic and herbs on your perfectly cooked steak. Happy cooking!
