Holiday Sweets: 10 Chocolate-Covered Christmas Treats


The holiday season brings with it an air of joy and celebration, where families and friends gather to share laughter, stories, and, of course, delicious treats. One of the most delightful ways to indulge during this festive time is with chocolate-covered sweets. These treats not only taste amazing but also make for perfect gifts, party favors, or simply an after-dinner delight. In this post, we will explore ten irresistible chocolate-covered Christmas treats that are sure to impress your loved ones and add a touch of sweetness to your holiday celebrations.

1. Chocolate-Covered Pretzels

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pretzel rods or twists
  • 12 oz dark or milk chocolate chips
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil (optional)
  • Holiday sprinkles or crushed peppermint for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Melt the Chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chocolate chips and coconut oil. Heat in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until smooth and fully melted.
  2. Dip the Pretzels: Dip each pretzel into the melted chocolate, allowing any excess to drip off. Lay them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Decorate: While the chocolate is still wet, sprinkle with holiday sprinkles or crushed peppermint.
  4. Set: Let the chocolate harden at room temperature or in the refrigerator for quicker results.

These chocolate-covered pretzels offer a wonderful combination of salty and sweet, making them a favorite among all ages.

2. Chocolate-Covered Oreos

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of Oreos
  • 12 oz white chocolate or milk chocolate
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Crushed candy canes or sprinkles for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Melt the Chocolate: Using the same method as above, melt the chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl.
  2. Dip the Oreos: Carefully dip each Oreo into the melted chocolate, ensuring it is fully coated. Use a fork to remove it and tap gently to remove excess chocolate.
  3. Decorate: Before the chocolate sets, sprinkle with crushed candy canes or colored sprinkles.
  4. Set: Place on a parchment-lined tray to cool and harden.

Chocolate-covered Oreos are a classic holiday treat that’s simple to make and always a hit at gatherings.

3. Chocolate-Covered Cherry Bombs

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar maraschino cherries, drained and dried
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 12 oz dark chocolate
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine the cream cheese and vanilla until smooth. Stuff each cherry with a small amount of the cream cheese mixture.
  2. Melt the Chocolate: Melt the dark chocolate as previously described.
  3. Dip the Cherries: Dip each stuffed cherry into the melted chocolate and place it on a parchment-lined baking tray.
  4. Chill: Refrigerate until the chocolate is set.

These cherry bombs are a delightful surprise with their creamy filling and chocolate coating, perfect for those who love a bit of fruit in their sweets.

4. Chocolate-Covered Marshmallows

Ingredients:

  • 1 bag of large marshmallows
  • 12 oz milk chocolate
  • 1 cup crushed nuts or sprinkles for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Melt the Chocolate: Melt the milk chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl.
  2. Dip the Marshmallows: Insert a lollipop stick into each marshmallow, then dip into the melted chocolate. Allow excess chocolate to drip off.
  3. Decorate: Roll in crushed nuts or sprinkles before the chocolate sets.
  4. Set: Place on a tray and let cool until the chocolate hardens.

Chocolate-covered marshmallows are whimsical and fun, making them an excellent choice for holiday parties or kids’ gatherings.

5. Chocolate-Covered Almonds

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw almonds
  • 12 oz dark chocolate
  • Sea salt for sprinkling (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Almonds: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the almonds on a baking sheet and roast for about 10 minutes, until slightly golden.
  2. Melt the Chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the dark chocolate.
  3. Coat the Almonds: Dip each almond into the chocolate, then place on parchment paper.
  4. Sprinkle with Salt: If desired, sprinkle a tiny pinch of sea salt on top before the chocolate sets.
  5. Set: Allow the almonds to cool until the chocolate hardens.

These chocolate-covered almonds are a delightful blend of crunch and rich chocolate flavor, perfect for snacking.

6. Chocolate-Covered Biscotti

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup almonds, chopped
  • 3 eggs
  • 8 oz dark chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Make the Biscotti Dough: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, mix flour, sugar, and almonds. Add eggs and mix until a dough forms.
  2. Bake the Biscotti: Shape the dough into logs and place on a baking sheet. Bake for 25 minutes. Let cool, then slice into pieces and bake again for an additional 10 minutes.
  3. Melt the Chocolate: In a bowl, melt the dark chocolate.
  4. Dip the Biscotti: Dip one end of each biscotti into the chocolate and place on parchment paper to set.

Chocolate-covered biscotti are perfect for dipping in coffee or tea, bringing a touch of elegance to your holiday table.

7. Chocolate-Covered Peanut Butter Balls

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 12 oz milk chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Make the Filling: In a bowl, mix peanut butter and powdered sugar until smooth. Roll into small balls and freeze for about 30 minutes.
  2. Melt the Chocolate: Melt the milk chocolate in a bowl.
  3. Dip the Balls: Dip each frozen peanut butter ball into the melted chocolate and place on parchment paper.
  4. Set: Refrigerate until chocolate is firm.

These chocolate-covered peanut butter balls are a rich and satisfying treat that will leave everyone wanting more.

8. Chocolate-Covered Fruit

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strawberries, washed and dried
  • 1 cup banana slices
  • 12 oz dark chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Melt the Chocolate: Melt the dark chocolate using the microwave method.
  2. Dip the Fruit: Dip strawberries and banana slices into the melted chocolate.
  3. Set: Place on a parchment-lined tray and allow to cool until the chocolate hardens.

Chocolate-covered fruit is a refreshing way to enjoy the sweetness of the season while still indulging in chocolate.

9. Chocolate-Covered Coconut Balls

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 12 oz dark chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Make the Coconut Mixture: In a bowl, mix shredded coconut and sweetened condensed milk until combined. Roll into small balls and place on a baking sheet.
  2. Chill: Refrigerate for 30 minutes to firm up.
  3. Melt the Chocolate: Melt the dark chocolate in a bowl.
  4. Dip the Coconut Balls: Dip each coconut ball into the melted chocolate and place on parchment paper.
  5. Set: Allow to cool until the chocolate is set.

These chocolate-covered coconut balls offer a tropical twist to your holiday treats, making them a unique addition to your dessert spread.

10. Chocolate-Covered Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 tbsp ginger
  • 1 tbsp cinnamon
  • 12 oz white chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Make the Gingerbread Dough: In a bowl, combine flour, brown sugar, molasses, ginger, and cinnamon to form a dough. Roll out and cut into shapes.
  2. Bake the Cookies: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 10-12 minutes. Let cool.
  3. Melt the Chocolate: Melt the white chocolate in a bowl.
  4. Dip the Cookies: Dip each cooled gingerbread cookie into the melted chocolate and let excess chocolate drip off.
  5. Set: Place on parchment paper to cool.

Chocolate-covered gingerbread cookies combine the classic flavors of the season with a decadent chocolate coating, making them a festive favorite.

Conclusion

These ten chocolate-covered Christmas treats are sure to enchant your family and friends this holiday season. Whether you’re preparing them for a party, gifting them, or simply indulging yourself, each treat offers a delightful combination of flavors and textures that capture the spirit of the season. Enjoy the process of making these sweets, and don’t forget to share the joy they bring with your loved ones. Happy holidays and happy baking!
