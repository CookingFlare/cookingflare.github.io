Delicious Mediterranean Vegetable Tagine


Are you looking for a flavorful and aromatic dish to add some excitement to your meal routine? Look no further than the Mediterranean Vegetable Tagine, a traditional North African stew originating from Morocco. This delightful recipe is a symphony of sweet and savory flavors, with a rich and exotic aroma that will transport you to the souks and markets of Marrakech. What is a Tagine? A tagine, by definition, is a slow-cooked stew made with a combination of aromatic spices, herbs, and vegetables. It's named after the clay pot in which it's cooked, with a distinctive conical lid that allows the flavors to meld together and the steam to condense, creating a rich and velvety sauce. In this recipe, we'll be using a pressure cooker to mimic the traditional cooking method, so don't worry if you don't have a traditional clay pot. The Flavor Profile The Mediterranean Vegetable Tagine is a reflection of the rich cultural heritage of the Mediterranean region. Aromas of cumin, coriander, and paprika mingle with the sweetness of carrots, zucchini, and bell peppers, all perfectly balanced by the tanginess of preserved lemons and olives. The sauce is made rich and creamy by adding a combination of yogurt and honey, giving it a silky texture and a hint of sweetness. Ingredients For this recipe, you'll need:

2 tablespoons olive oil 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon ground paprika 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon powder 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped 2 medium zucchinis, chopped 2 medium bell peppers, chopped 1 large eggplant, chopped 1 large can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes 2 cups vegetable broth 1/2 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons honey 2 preserved lemons, chopped 1 cup pitted green olives, chopped Fresh parsley, chopped (optional) Cooking oil or ghee for frying (optional)

Preparing the Spice Blend Before starting, prepare the spice blend by mixing together cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper in a small bowl. Set aside. Cooking the Vegetable Tagine In a large pressure cooker, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant. Next, add the spice blend and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. Add the chopped carrots, zucchinis, bell peppers, and eggplant. Cook for 5 minutes, until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Add the crushed tomatoes, vegetable broth, salt, and black pepper. Stir well to combine, then bring to a simmer. Adding the Sweet and Tangy Touch Reduce the heat to low and let the tagine simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the vegetables are tender. Stir in the yogurt and honey, then use an immersion blender to puree the mixture until smooth. Finishing Touches Stir in the chopped preserved lemons and olives. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Traditional Serving Suggestions Traditionally, the Mediterranean Vegetable Tagine is served with couscous or over rice. If you want to add some extra crunch, try garnishing with toasted pine nuts or chopped fresh parsley. Tips and Variations To enhance the flavor of your tagine, you can add other ingredients such as chopped dates, chopped fresh herbs, or a sprinkle of sumac. Experiment with different vegetables or spices to make the recipe your own! Nutritional Information This recipe makes 4-6 servings, and each serving has approximately:

Calories: 350-400 Fat: 15-20g Saturated Fat: 2-3g Cholesterol: 10-12mg Carbohydrates: 40-50g Fiber: 5-6g Protein: 10-12g

Conclusion The Mediterranean Vegetable Tagine is a rich and flavorful dish that's perfect for any meal. With its unique blend of spices and herbs, it's a true taste of the Mediterranean region. So, next time you're looking for a delicious and exotic dish, give this recipe a try and let the flavors of the Mediterranean transport you to a world of culinary delight! Cooking Time Preparation Time: 20 minutes Cooking Time: 25-30 minutes Total Time: 50 minutes Difficulty Level Easy to medium This recipe can be made in under an hour, with most of the cooking time taken up by the pressure cooker. It's a great option for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion.
