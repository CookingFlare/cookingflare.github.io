10 Delicious Easter Recipes to Celebrate Spring


Easter is the perfect time to gather with family and friends, celebrate the joys of spring, and, of course, enjoy delicious food. Whether you are hosting a festive brunch, a lively dinner, or preparing a picnic for a sunny day, these 10 mouthwatering Easter recipes will help you celebrate in style. From savory main dishes to sweet desserts, there is something for everyone. So, let’s dive into these Easter favorites that are sure to brighten your table this spring.

1. Honey Glazed Ham

A classic Easter main dish, honey glazed ham never fails to impress. The rich, smoky flavor of the ham combined with a sweet and tangy glaze makes for a show-stopping centerpiece.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Place the ham in a roasting pan and score the surface in a crisscross pattern.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine honey, Dijon mustard, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, cinnamon, cloves, and ginger. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently, until it thickens.
  4. Brush the glaze generously over the ham, making sure to coat the entire surface.
  5. Roast the ham in the preheated oven, basting every 20 minutes with more glaze, until the ham is heated through and caramelized, about 1.5 to 2 hours.
  6. Garnish with fresh rosemary and serve.

This honey glazed ham is tender, juicy, and full of flavor, making it the perfect centerpiece for your Easter meal.

2. Spring Vegetable Quiche

A savory quiche filled with fresh spring vegetables is a wonderful way to incorporate the season’s bounty into your Easter brunch. The light, flaky crust and creamy filling will be a hit with guests of all ages.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup broccoli florets, blanched
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers (red, yellow, or green)
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Blind bake the pie crust for about 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, milk, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  4. Add the blanched broccoli, diced bell peppers, green onions, and shredded cheddar cheese to the egg mixture. Stir gently to combine.
  5. Pour the mixture into the partially baked pie crust.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

This quiche is a flavorful and colorful addition to any Easter spread, with a perfect balance of creamy texture and crisp vegetables.

3. Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins

Lemon is the perfect fruit to usher in the freshness of spring, and these lemon poppy seed muffins are a delightful treat for Easter morning. Light, fluffy, and full of citrusy goodness, they pair beautifully with a cup of coffee or tea.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and poppy seeds.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together sugar, eggs, melted butter, buttermilk, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 full.
  6. Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the muffins cool for a few minutes before serving.

These lemon poppy seed muffins are bursting with spring flavor and will add a refreshing touch to your Easter brunch.

4. Roasted Baby Carrots with Dill and Honey

Carrots are in season during spring, and roasting them with honey and dill brings out their natural sweetness and flavor. This simple yet elegant side dish is a great way to complement your Easter meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound baby carrots, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the baby carrots with olive oil, honey, dill, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the carrots in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the carrots are tender and caramelized, stirring halfway through.
  5. Serve warm, garnished with additional fresh dill if desired.

These roasted baby carrots are a delicious and healthy side dish that pairs perfectly with ham, quiche, or any other main course.

5. Deviled Eggs with Fresh Herbs

Deviled eggs are a must-have at any Easter celebration. This classic dish gets a fresh twist with the addition of herbs like parsley, chives, and tarragon, adding depth of flavor and a touch of spring.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh tarragon, chopped
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Hard boil the eggs by placing them in a pot of water, bringing it to a boil, then reducing the heat and simmering for 10 minutes. Cool the eggs in ice water, then peel and slice in half.
  2. Scoop out the yolks and place them in a bowl. Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites.
  4. Sprinkle the eggs with fresh parsley, chives, tarragon, and a light dusting of paprika.
  5. Chill in the fridge until ready to serve.

These deviled eggs are creamy, tangy, and bursting with fresh herb flavors, making them a delightful appetizer for your Easter gathering.

6. Spring Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

A light, refreshing salad is a perfect way to balance out the heavier dishes on your Easter table. This spring salad combines fresh greens, radishes, and peas with a zesty lemon vinaigrette for a bright, flavorful dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, and baby lettuce)
  • 1/2 cup sugar snap peas, sliced
  • 1/2 cup radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley leaves
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, sugar snap peas, radishes, mint, and parsley.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper until emulsified.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately, or refrigerate until ready to serve.

This light and vibrant salad is the perfect accompaniment to your Easter meal, adding a burst of freshness and color to your table.

7. Strawberry Shortcake

For a sweet Easter treat, strawberry shortcake is a classic dessert that never goes out of style. The sweet, juicy strawberries paired with flaky biscuits and whipped cream make for a heavenly combination.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries with sugar and let them sit for 30 minutes to release their juices.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Add the heavy cream and vanilla extract, stirring until just combined.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and pat it into a 1-inch thick rectangle. Cut the dough into rounds using a biscuit cutter and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown.
  7. To assemble, slice the biscuits in half, top with macerated strawberries, and a dollop of whipped cream.

This strawberry shortcake is a perfect way to end your Easter meal on a sweet note, capturing the essence of spring in every bite.

8. Grilled Lamb Chops with Rosemary and Garlic

Lamb is a traditional Easter dish, and these grilled lamb chops with rosemary and garlic are an easy yet impressive way to prepare it. The savory marinade enhances the natural flavor of the meat, making it tender and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 8 lamb chops
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine garlic, rosemary, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Rub the marinade onto the lamb chops and let them marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours) in the fridge.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the lamb chops for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until desired doneness.
  5. Let the lamb chops rest for a few minutes before serving.

These grilled lamb chops are a flavorful and tender main dish that will be the star of your Easter dinner.

9. Hot Cross Buns

Hot cross buns are a traditional Easter treat, with a sweet, spiced dough topped with a simple icing cross. These soft and fragrant buns are perfect for breakfast or as a snack with your afternoon tea.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1/2 cup currants or raisins
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • Icing for cross (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. Add the yeast mixture, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Add the currants or raisins and knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl and let it rise for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.
  6. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  7. Punch down the dough, divide it into 12 equal portions, and shape into buns.
  8. Place the buns on a baking sheet and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  9. Brush the buns with an egg wash and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. If desired, pipe icing in the shape of a cross on top of the buns.

Hot cross buns are a fragrant, spiced treat that brings a sense of tradition to your Easter table.

10. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is a beloved Easter dessert, and this recipe is the perfect way to end your meal. Moist and flavorful, with a tangy cream cheese frosting, it’s the ultimate springtime indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

For the frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together oil, eggs, sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until combined.
  5. Fold in the grated carrots and walnuts (if using).
  6. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the cakes cool completely before frosting.
  8. For the frosting, beat together cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  9. Frost the cooled cakes, spreading a generous amount of frosting between the layers and on top.

Carrot cake is the ultimate Easter dessert, with its moist texture, spicy flavor, and creamy frosting. It’s a sweet and satisfying way to conclude your festive meal.

These 10 Easter recipes capture the essence of spring and offer a variety of flavors and textures to make your celebration extra special. Whether you’re cooking for a large group or a small gathering, these dishes will bring joy and deliciousness to your table. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
