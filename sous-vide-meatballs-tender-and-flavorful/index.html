Sous Vide Meatballs: Tender and Flavorful


If you’re a meatball enthusiast, you’re in for a treat! In this recipe, we’re going to explore the wonderful world of sous vide cooking and how it can transform your regular meatballs into tender, flavorful morsels of delight. Sous vide has gained popularity in recent years for its ability to consistently cook food to perfection, and meatballs are no exception. By immersing the meatballs in a precisely controlled water bath, you can achieve results that are difficult to replicate using traditional cooking methods.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 1/2 cups marinara sauce

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Meatball Mixture

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, milk, egg, minced garlic, salt, black pepper, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes (if using). Gently mix the ingredients until just combined, being careful not to overwork the mixture.

Step 2: Form the Meatballs

Using your hands, gently shape the meat mixture into meatballs, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Place the meatballs on a plate or a baking sheet, ensuring they are evenly spaced and not touching each other.

Step 3: Preheat the Water Bath

Fill a sous vide container with water and set the sous vide machine to 140°F (60°C).

Step 4: Vacuum Seal the Meatballs

Place the meatballs in a single layer in a vacuum sealing bag, ensuring they are not overcrowded. Vacuum seal the bag according to the manufacturer’s instructions, ensuring a tight seal to prevent water from entering the bag during cooking.

Step 5: Sous Vide Cooking

Carefully lower the sealed bag of meatballs into the preheated water bath, ensuring the meatballs are fully submerged. Cook the meatballs for 1 1/2 to 2 hours to ensure they are fully cooked and tender.

Step 6: Finish and Serve

Once the meatballs have finished cooking, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Gently open the bag and transfer the meatballs to a serving dish. Heat the marinara sauce in a saucepan, and spoon it over the meatballs before serving.

Why Sous Vide Meatballs?

Sous vide cooking offers several unique advantages when it comes to preparing meatballs. The precise temperature control ensures that the meatballs are cooked evenly from edge to edge, resulting in a consistent texture throughout. Additionally, the extended cooking time allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in meatballs that are incredibly tender and bursting with flavor. The vacuum-sealing process also helps to infuse the meatballs with the flavors of the seasonings and spices, resulting in a more intense and enjoyable eating experience.

In Conclusion

Sous vide meatballs are a game-changer for anyone who loves this classic dish. The tender, flavorful results are sure to impress even the most discerning meatball aficionados. Whether you’re serving them as a main course with pasta or as a delectable appetizer, sous vide meatballs are bound to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. So, grab your sous vide machine and get ready to elevate your meatball game to a whole new level!
