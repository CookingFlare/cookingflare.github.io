How to Sous Vide Steak to Perfection: A Step-by-Step Guide


Sous vide cooking has taken the culinary world by storm in recent years, offering a foolproof way to achieve steak cooked to perfection every single time. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a home cook looking to level up your skills, sous vide is a technique that guarantees precise control over temperature and texture, ensuring your steak turns out tender, juicy, and flavorful. In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know about cooking steak sous vide, from choosing the right cut to finishing it with a perfect sear.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, French for “under vacuum,” is a cooking method where food is sealed in a vacuum bag and cooked in a water bath at a precise, low temperature for an extended period of time. The beauty of sous vide cooking lies in its ability to maintain consistent temperatures throughout the entire cooking process. This allows you to achieve perfect doneness, with no risk of overcooking.

When it comes to steak, sous vide ensures the meat cooks evenly from edge to edge, allowing you to reach your desired level of doneness every time—whether you prefer it rare, medium-rare, or well-done. With this technique, you can rest assured that your steak will have the perfect texture, tenderness, and juiciness.

Why Sous Vide is the Best Way to Cook Steak

Sous vide cooking takes the guesswork out of steak preparation. Traditional methods such as pan-searing, grilling, or broiling rely on fluctuating temperatures, which can easily lead to overcooked edges or uneven cooking. With sous vide, however, the water bath is kept at a precise temperature, ensuring that the entire steak cooks evenly. This eliminates the risk of a dry or tough steak and allows the natural flavors of the meat to shine.

Moreover, sous vide cooking helps break down collagen and fat within the meat, resulting in a super-tender texture that’s hard to achieve through other methods. Plus, by using sous vide, the steak retains its juices and doesn’t lose moisture during the cooking process. The result? A steak that’s both incredibly tender and packed with flavor.

Choosing the Right Cut of Steak for Sous Vide

While sous vide can be used with nearly any cut of steak, some cuts are better suited for this technique than others. Here are a few popular choices:

1. Ribeye

The ribeye is one of the most popular cuts for sous vide because of its marbling and rich flavor. The fat in the ribeye breaks down beautifully during sous vide cooking, making it incredibly tender and juicy.

2. New York Strip

New York strip steaks are leaner than ribeyes but still offer a good balance of tenderness and flavor. They work well with sous vide cooking and are perfect for those who want a slightly less fatty steak.

3. Filet Mignon

Filet mignon is known for its tenderness and is a great choice if you’re looking for a melt-in-your-mouth experience. Though it lacks the fat marbling of other cuts, sous vide can help bring out the best in this tender steak.

4. Flank or Skirt Steak

While these cuts are more fibrous and leaner, sous vide cooking can help break down the connective tissue and create a surprisingly tender result. Just be sure to slice these steaks against the grain to enhance their tenderness.

5. Sirloin

Sirloin steaks are relatively affordable compared to other cuts, and sous vide can help tenderize them while maintaining their beefy flavor. These steaks are a great option if you’re looking to impress guests without breaking the bank.

Ultimately, the choice of cut depends on your preferences. Tender cuts like filet mignon are perfect for those seeking a buttery texture, while cuts like ribeye offer a more robust, juicy bite. Choose according to your flavor and texture preference!

Essential Equipment for Sous Vide Cooking

To get started with sous vide, you’ll need a few essential pieces of equipment:

1. Sous Vide Precision Cooker

A sous vide precision cooker is the heart of this cooking method. It controls the temperature of the water bath and ensures that your steak cooks to the exact temperature you desire. There are various models available, ranging from affordable immersion circulators to high-end units with advanced features. Popular brands include Anova, Joule, and Sansaire.

2. Vacuum Sealer or Zipper Lock Bags

You’ll need a vacuum sealer to seal the steak in a bag and remove air. Alternatively, if you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use resealable zipper lock bags and the water displacement method (more on this later).

3. Large Pot or Container

You’ll need a large pot or container to hold the water bath. The size of the container depends on how many steaks you plan to cook. Ensure that the container is large enough to fully submerge the steak without overcrowding.

4. Cast Iron Skillet or Grill (for Searing)

While sous vide cooks your steak to perfection, the final step is to sear the meat in a hot pan or on a grill. A cast-iron skillet is ideal for this task, as it retains heat well and creates a beautiful crust.

5. Thermometer (Optional)

While not strictly necessary, having a thermometer can help you double-check the water temperature and ensure that everything is on track during cooking.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Sous Vide Steak

Now that you have your equipment and ingredients ready, let’s walk through the process of cooking a steak sous vide. Whether you’re cooking ribeye, filet mignon, or another cut, the steps are largely the same.

Step 1: Season Your Steak

Before sealing your steak in a bag, it’s important to season it. You can keep it simple with just salt and pepper, or you can get creative with additional herbs and spices. Common seasonings for sous vide steaks include garlic, rosemary, thyme, and a dash of olive oil or butter.

To season your steak:

  • Sprinkle a generous amount of salt and pepper on both sides.
  • Add any optional seasonings like garlic powder, fresh herbs, or a drizzle of olive oil.
  • If using butter, you can add a small pat of butter into the bag, which will infuse the steak with rich flavor as it cooks.

Step 2: Vacuum Seal the Steak

Once seasoned, it’s time to vacuum seal your steak. Place the steak in a vacuum seal bag and use your vacuum sealer to remove as much air as possible. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a resealable zipper lock bag and the water displacement method.

To use the water displacement method:

  • Place your seasoned steak in the zipper lock bag.
  • Slowly lower the bag into a pot of water, making sure the water pressure pushes the air out of the bag.
  • Once the air is displaced, seal the bag completely.

Step 3: Prepare the Water Bath

Fill your large pot or container with water. Set your sous vide precision cooker to the desired temperature for your steak. For a medium-rare steak, aim for a water temperature of 130°F (54°C). For medium, set the temperature to 140°F (60°C), and for well-done, use 160°F (71°C).

Once the water has reached the target temperature, you’re ready to start cooking.

Step 4: Cook the Steak

Submerge the sealed bag with the steak into the preheated water bath. Ensure that the steak is fully submerged and that the bag is not touching the sides or bottom of the container. For best results, you may want to use a rack or clips to suspend the bag in the water, preventing it from floating.

Cook the steak for the recommended time based on its thickness:

  • 1-inch thick steak: 1 to 1.5 hours
  • 1.5-inch thick steak: 1.5 to 2 hours
  • 2-inch thick steak: 2 to 2.5 hours

While sous vide cooking is very forgiving, it’s important to stick to the recommended cooking times to achieve the best texture and doneness.

Step 5: Finish with a Sear

Once the steak has finished cooking, remove it from the bag. The steak will be fully cooked but lack the delicious sear we all love. To achieve that crispy, golden-brown crust, you’ll need to sear it quickly in a hot pan or on a grill.

Heat a cast-iron skillet over high heat, and add a small amount of oil with a high smoke point, like vegetable oil or grapeseed oil. Once the oil is shimmering, add the steak and sear each side for 1-2 minutes, until a deep brown crust forms. You can also add a pat of butter, garlic, and fresh herbs during the searing process for added flavor.

Step 6: Rest and Serve

After searing, let the steak rest for a couple of minutes to allow the juices to redistribute. Slice the steak against the grain, and serve immediately.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Steak Every Time

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Bag: If you’re cooking multiple steaks, make sure each steak has enough space in the bag for water to circulate around it. Overcrowding can result in uneven cooking.
  • Use a Meat Thermometer: If you’re unsure about the exact doneness, you can use a meat thermometer to check. For medium-rare, the internal temperature should be around 130°F (54°C), for medium, it should be around 140°F (60°C).
  • Experiment with Time: While sous vide cooking is largely about temperature, the time can influence the steak’s texture. If you want a more tender steak, you can cook it for a little longer, but avoid going too far past the recommended time to prevent overcooking.

Conclusion

Sous vide cooking offers an unparalleled method for making steak that’s perfectly tender, juicy, and flavorful every time. By following this simple, step-by-step guide, you can master the art of sous vide steak and impress your family and friends with restaurant-quality results right at home. So, whether you’re cooking a ribeye, filet mignon, or any other cut, sous vide guarantees that your steak will always be cooked to perfection. Enjoy your perfectly cooked steak, and happy cooking!
