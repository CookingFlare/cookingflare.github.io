Vegan Cherry Pie: A Sweet, Tangy Dessert


Cherry pie has always been a beloved classic dessert, with its perfectly flaky crust and its rich, tart filling. But what if you could enjoy all that deliciousness without the use of dairy or eggs? That’s right, vegan cherry pie is here, and it brings the same beloved flavors and textures while being entirely plant-based! Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or someone who’s just trying out plant-based desserts, this recipe will surely become a staple in your baking routine.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make a delectable vegan cherry pie that is sweet, tangy, and absolutely irresistible. We’ll cover everything from the perfect pie crust to creating the cherry filling and tips for assembling the pie to achieve that golden, flaky texture everyone loves.

Why Make Vegan Cherry Pie?

Vegan baking can sometimes seem intimidating, but it’s easier than you think once you understand how to substitute common ingredients. Traditional cherry pie recipes often call for butter, eggs, and sometimes dairy-based cream cheese for a creamy filling. By making a few simple swaps, you can create a vegan version that’s just as satisfying and scrumptious as the original.

Not only is this vegan cherry pie cruelty-free, but it’s also great for anyone with dairy or egg allergies, and it can be enjoyed by people with various dietary preferences. It’s a dessert that everyone can enjoy at the table, no matter their eating habits. Plus, the vibrant red color of the cherries is so inviting, making it perfect for holidays, parties, or just because you’re craving something sweet!

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour – This forms the base of your crust and gives it that signature texture.
  • 1 tsp salt – Adds flavor and balances the sweetness of the filling.
  • 1 cup coconut oil (or vegan butter) – The fat needed for a rich, flaky crust. You can use cold coconut oil for a firmer crust, or use softened vegan butter for a more traditional taste.
  • ¼ cup ice-cold water – The key to a flaky crust is keeping the dough cold.
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup (optional) – A touch of sweetness in the crust helps balance the tartness of the cherry filling.

For the Cherry Filling:

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen cherries – This is the star ingredient! Cherries offer the perfect balance of sweet and tart flavors. If you’re using frozen cherries, make sure to thaw and drain them.
  • ¾ cup organic sugar – Adjust the amount to taste, depending on the sweetness of your cherries.
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch – Helps thicken the cherry filling into a nice, gooey consistency.
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice – Adds a touch of tang and enhances the natural flavors of the cherries.
  • ½ tsp almond extract – This subtle flavor boost elevates the cherry filling and gives it a lovely depth.
  • ¼ tsp cinnamon (optional) – Adds warmth and complexity to the overall taste.
  • Pinch of salt – Balances the sweetness and acidity.

Optional:

  • 1 tbsp non-dairy milk – For brushing on top of the crust before baking to achieve a golden finish.
  • 1 tsp sugar – To sprinkle on top for a sweet, crunchy finish.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pie Crust

Start by making the dough for your pie crust. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and salt. Add the coconut oil (or softened vegan butter) to the flour mixture, and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the fat into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. You want some pea-sized pieces of fat in the dough to ensure a flaky crust once baked.

Slowly add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently as you go. You don’t want to overwork the dough, as this can make it tough. Keep adding water until the dough comes together into a shaggy ball. If the dough feels too dry, add a little more water, but be cautious not to make it too wet.

Once the dough is formed, divide it in half and shape each half into a disk. Wrap the disks in plastic wrap or parchment paper and refrigerate them for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough ensures the crust will be flaky when baked.

Step 2: Make the Cherry Filling

While the dough chills, it’s time to prepare the cherry filling. In a large saucepan, combine the fresh or frozen cherries with sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, almond extract, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the cherries begin to release their juices and the mixture starts to thicken. This should take about 10-12 minutes.

Once the filling has thickened and become a nice, jammy consistency, remove it from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes. The filling will continue to thicken as it cools.

Step 3: Roll Out the Pie Crust

Once the dough has chilled and the filling is ready, it’s time to roll out the pie crust. Lightly flour a clean surface and rolling pin to prevent sticking. Take one disk of dough and roll it out into a circle that’s slightly larger than your pie pan (about 12 inches in diameter). Transfer the rolled dough into your pie pan, pressing gently to fit the shape. Trim any excess dough, leaving about an inch around the edges for the top crust.

Step 4: Fill the Pie

Carefully spoon the cooled cherry filling into the prepared pie crust, making sure to spread it out evenly. Be sure to scrape the saucepan to get every last bit of that delicious filling!

Step 5: Add the Top Crust

Roll out the second disk of dough as you did with the first, and place it over the cherry filling. Trim the edges of the dough and pinch the top and bottom crusts together to seal. You can crimp the edges with your fingers or a fork for a decorative touch.

To allow steam to escape while baking, cut a few slits in the top crust. If you like, you can brush the top of the pie with non-dairy milk to give it a shiny, golden finish. Sprinkle a little sugar over the top for extra sweetness and a nice crunch.

Step 6: Bake the Pie

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any potential spills) and bake it in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling through the slits in the top.

Once the pie is done, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for at least 1-2 hours before serving. This gives the filling time to set, making it easier to slice and serve.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Cherry Pie

  • Chill the dough: Make sure to refrigerate your dough before rolling it out. This helps maintain its structure and ensures a flaky pie crust.
  • Use cold fat: Whether you use coconut oil or vegan butter, keeping it cold helps create a flaky texture in the crust. The cold fat forms pockets that expand during baking, leading to a light, tender crust.
  • Let the pie cool: As tempting as it might be to slice into that hot, fragrant pie immediately, let it cool for a bit. This allows the filling to firm up, preventing it from spilling out when you cut into it.
  • Use fresh cherries when possible: While frozen cherries work just fine, fresh cherries in season provide the best flavor and texture.
  • Experiment with flavors: Don’t be afraid to experiment with flavor add-ins like vanilla extract, a splash of orange zest, or a pinch of ginger in the filling for an extra kick of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Vegan cherry pie is a versatile dessert that can be served on its own or with a variety of accompaniments. Here are some ideas to elevate your pie experience:

  • Serve with dairy-free whipped cream – A dollop of vegan whipped cream on top of a warm slice of pie creates a perfect contrast to the tart cherries.
  • Pair with non-dairy ice cream – Vegan vanilla or coconut milk-based ice cream complements the tangy cherry filling beautifully.
  • Top with fresh fruit – Add a sprinkle of fresh berries on top for added texture and flavor.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This vegan cherry pie offers everything you love about a traditional cherry pie – a buttery, flaky crust with a sweet, tangy filling – without any animal products. The beauty of this recipe is that it’s easy to make, flexible, and can be enjoyed by anyone, whether they follow a plant-based lifestyle or not. The filling is naturally sweetened and thickened, with a perfect balance of tart cherries, cinnamon, and a touch of almond flavor.

Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion, a holiday meal, or just because, this vegan cherry pie is guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser. You won’t have to miss out on that sweet, tangy, fruity delight just because you’re avoiding animal products. It’s a dessert that everyone can enjoy!

So go ahead, preheat your oven, and get ready to bake up a slice of heaven with this vegan cherry pie. Enjoy the sweet and tangy flavors that will fill your home with the scent of fresh, warm pie. It’s time to indulge!
