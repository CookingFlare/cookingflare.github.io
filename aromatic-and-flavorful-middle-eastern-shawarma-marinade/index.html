Aromatic and Flavorful: Middle Eastern Shawarma Marinade


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve probably had the pleasure of indulging in a delicious shawarma wrap. The tender, marinated meat, wrapped in a warm pita bread and topped with tangy tahini sauce, is a flavor explosion in your mouth. But have you ever wondered how to recreate that mouthwatering shawarma flavor at home? Look no further – in this blog post, I’ll be sharing with you a recipe for an aromatic and flavorful Middle Eastern shawarma marinade that will take your homemade shawarma to the next level.

Ingredients

To make this delectable marinade, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for those who enjoy a bit of heat)

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the plain yogurt, minced garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil. Mix well until smooth and well combined.

  2. Next, add the ground cumin, ground coriander, ground paprika, ground turmeric, ground cinnamon, ground ginger, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the yogurt mixture. Stir well to ensure that all the spices are evenly distributed and incorporated into the marinade.

  3. Place your choice of meat – whether it’s chicken, beef, or lamb – into a large ziplock bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the meat, making sure it is fully coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  4. When you’re ready to cook your shawarma, preheat your grill or stovetop grill pan to medium-high heat. Remove the meat from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.

  5. Grill the marinated meat until it is cooked through and nicely charred on the outside. The cooking time will vary depending on the type and thickness of the meat, so keep an eye on it to avoid overcooking.

  6. Once the meat is cooked to perfection, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute and ensure a tender and juicy final result.

  7. Slice the grilled meat thinly, across the grain, to achieve that classic shawarma texture. Serve the slices of meat in warm pita bread, and top with your favorite accompaniments such as sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, pickles, and a generous drizzle of tahini sauce.

Now you have all the tools to create your own aromatic and flavorful Middle Eastern shawarma marinade. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply satisfying your cravings, this marinade will elevate your homemade shawarma to an entirely new level. Give it a try and prepare to be transported to the bustling streets of the Middle East with every bite. Enjoy!

Note: Remember to adjust the quantities of the marinade ingredients according to the amount of meat you are marinating.
