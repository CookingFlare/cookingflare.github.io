How to Make a Fun Unicorn Cake for Kids


Unicorn-themed parties are all the rage, and what better way to celebrate than with a magical unicorn cake? This vibrant, whimsical treat is perfect for birthdays, celebrations, or just a fun baking activity with the kids. The great thing about this cake is that it’s not just visually stunning, but also delicious and customizable to suit different tastes. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, you’ll find that creating this enchanting cake is easier than it looks. Let’s dive into the step-by-step guide on how to make a fun unicorn cake that will surely dazzle your little ones.

Ingredients for the Unicorn Cake

To create a unicorn cake, you’ll need a few basic cake ingredients and some fun decorations. Here’s a complete list to get you started:

For the Cake Layers:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • Food coloring (preferably gel colors for vibrant shades)

For the Buttercream Frosting:

  • 2 cups unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream or milk
  • Food coloring (pink, purple, blue, yellow, and green for the mane)

For the Unicorn Decorations:

  • Edible gold or silver glitter
  • Fondant (white, for the horn and ears)
  • Candy pearls or sprinkles
  • Lollipops (optional, for added decoration)
  • Edible flowers (optional, for extra magic)

With these ingredients ready, let’s start crafting your magical unicorn cake!

Step 1: Preparing the Cake Layers

  1. Preheat your oven: Set your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour three 8-inch round cake pans.

  2. Sift dry ingredients: In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. This ensures that the dry ingredients are evenly distributed and that your cake texture will be smooth.

  3. Cream the butter and sugar: Using a stand mixer or hand mixer, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. This typically takes around 4-5 minutes. Be sure to scrape down the sides of the bowl occasionally to ensure everything is incorporated.

  4. Add eggs: Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each one is fully incorporated before adding the next. Add the vanilla extract and mix well.

  5. Alternate wet and dry ingredients: With the mixer on low, add about a third of the dry ingredients, followed by half of the milk. Continue alternating between the dry ingredients and milk, ending with the dry ingredients. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make your cake dense.

  6. Divide and color the batter: Now comes the fun part! Divide your cake batter evenly into separate bowls. You’ll want enough batter for each color of the unicorn mane, so typically four or five bowls should suffice. Add food coloring to each bowl, starting with just a few drops and mixing until you achieve the desired shade. Gel food coloring works best for vibrant colors and doesn’t affect the consistency of the batter.

  7. Layer the colors in the pans: Spoon the colored batter into the prepared cake pans. Don’t worry about making it perfect – the more randomly you add the colors, the more unique your cake will look. You can use a skewer to swirl the colors together for a marbled effect.

  8. Bake: Place the cake pans in the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Once baked, let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring them to wire racks to cool completely.

Step 2: Making the Buttercream Frosting

While your cakes are cooling, it’s time to make the buttercream frosting.

  1. Cream the butter: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter on medium speed until it’s light and fluffy (about 3-4 minutes).

  2. Add powdered sugar: Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, mixing on low speed until each addition is fully incorporated.

  3. Add vanilla and cream: Once all the powdered sugar has been added, mix in the vanilla extract. Slowly add the heavy cream (or milk) until the frosting reaches a smooth and spreadable consistency. You can add more cream if it’s too thick or a little more powdered sugar if it’s too thin.

  4. Color the frosting: Divide the frosting into separate bowls for each color of the unicorn’s mane. Color each bowl with the desired gel food coloring, mixing until you achieve vibrant, pastel shades.

Step 3: Assembling the Unicorn Cake

Now that your cakes and frosting are ready, let’s begin assembling the unicorn cake.

  1. Trim the cakes: If your cake layers have domed in the center, use a serrated knife to trim the tops so that they are flat. This ensures your cake layers stack evenly.

  2. Layer the cakes: Place the first cake layer on a cake board or serving plate. Spread a thin layer of frosting on top to act as a crumb coat. This helps seal in any loose crumbs, making the final frosting layers smoother.

  3. Repeat the layers: Add the second layer of cake, frosting, and then the third layer. Apply another thin coat of frosting over the entire cake to seal in the crumbs.

  4. Chill the cake: Place the cake in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to set the crumb coat. This step is crucial for achieving a smooth and clean frosting finish.

  5. Frost the cake: Once the crumb coat is set, use an offset spatula to apply a generous layer of frosting over the entire cake. Smooth it out as best as you can to create a clean, polished look.

Step 4: Creating the Unicorn Horn and Ears

To give your cake that magical unicorn touch, you’ll need to make the horn and ears.

  1. Unicorn horn: Roll a small piece of white fondant into a cone shape to create the unicorn’s horn. You can make it as tall as you like, but a typical unicorn horn should be around 6-8 inches long. Twist the fondant as you roll it to get the signature spiral effect. Once rolled, set it aside to dry for 1-2 hours.

  2. Unicorn ears: Roll out small pieces of white fondant and cut two small triangles for the outer ears. For the inner ear, roll out smaller pink fondant triangles and attach them inside the larger white ones. Once formed, set the ears aside to harden along with the horn.

Step 5: Decorating the Unicorn Cake

With the cake, frosting, and decorations ready, it’s time to bring the unicorn to life!

  1. Position the horn and ears: Once the cake is fully frosted and set, gently press the fondant horn into the top center of the cake. Position the ears on either side of the horn, slightly tilted for a playful look.

  2. Add the unicorn mane: Using a piping bag fitted with a star tip, pipe the buttercream frosting around the top of the cake to create the unicorn’s flowing mane. Start from the front of the cake and work your way around, alternating colors to make it look like a magical, multi-colored mane. You can make the mane as wild and voluminous as you like!

  3. Final touches: For an extra sparkle, dust the unicorn horn with edible gold or silver glitter. You can also sprinkle candy pearls or edible flowers around the base of the cake for added magic. If you want, you can even stick lollipops into the cake for a fun, whimsical effect.

Step 6: Enjoying Your Unicorn Cake

Once your unicorn cake is decorated and looking absolutely magical, it’s time to slice and enjoy! Cut a generous slice and watch the delighted expressions on your kids’ faces as they dig into this colorful, whimsical treat. The combination of the light, fluffy cake layers and the sweet, creamy buttercream frosting will surely be a hit with both kids and adults alike.

Tips for Success

  • Use gel food coloring: Gel colors are more concentrated than liquid dyes, so they’ll give you vibrant hues without affecting the texture of your cake or frosting.
  • Chill the cake between frosting layers: This helps achieve a smoother finish and prevents the colors from bleeding into one another.
  • Customize the flavors: If you want to give your unicorn cake a unique flavor, consider adding lemon zest, almond extract, or even a swirl of fruit puree to the cake batter for extra flavor.
  • Get creative with decorations: You can personalize your unicorn cake by adding other fun details like edible glitter, unicorn-shaped cookies, or even mini unicorn figurines to enhance the theme.

Conclusion

Making a fun unicorn cake for kids doesn’t have to be complicated or intimidating. With a few simple ingredients, some creativity, and a little time, you can craft a cake that will become the centerpiece of any celebration. Whether you’re making it for a birthday party or just for fun, this unicorn cake is sure to bring a touch of magic to the table. Happy baking!
