The Best Homemade Cinnamon Rolls Recipe Ever


When it comes to homemade baked goods, few things can rival the warm, sugary bliss of freshly made cinnamon rolls. Soft, fluffy spirals filled with buttery cinnamon sugar and topped with a luscious glaze—it’s the kind of indulgence that turns an ordinary morning into something truly special. The best part? Making them at home isn’t as intimidating as you might think, and the results are far better than anything store-bought. This is the recipe to make if you’re looking to create the most pillowy, flavorful, and foolproof cinnamon rolls from scratch.

Let’s dive into all the details, tips, and tricks that will help you create the best homemade cinnamon rolls ever. Whether you’re serving them for a lazy weekend breakfast or a festive holiday treat, these cinnamon rolls are sure to become a go-to recipe in your kitchen.

Ingredients for the Perfect Cinnamon Rolls

The secret to making mouthwatering cinnamon rolls comes down to the perfect balance of soft dough, flavorful filling, and the right gooey topping. This recipe uses simple, pantry-friendly ingredients that, when combined, create a showstopping breakfast or brunch treat.

For the Dough:

  • ¾ cup whole milk (warm to about 110°F)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 2 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter (softened)
  • 3 ¾ cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Cinnamon Filling:

  • ⅔ cup light brown sugar (packed)
  • 2 ½ tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter (softened)

For the Cream Cheese Glaze:

  • 4 ounces cream cheese (softened)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk (adjust for consistency)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Cinnamon Roll Perfection

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by warming the milk to around 110°F—warm to the touch, but not hot. In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm milk and granulated sugar. Sprinkle the active dry yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until it looks foamy. This step ensures the yeast is active and ready to give your dough the rise it needs.

Step 2: Create the Dough

Once the yeast has activated, add the eggs and softened butter. Mix until combined. Gradually stir in the all-purpose flour and salt, about one cup at a time, until a soft dough forms. If you’re using a stand mixer, attach the dough hook and knead on medium speed for about 8 minutes. Alternatively, knead the dough by hand on a floured surface for 10 minutes. The dough should be smooth, elastic, and just slightly tacky to the touch.

Step 3: First Rise

Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it has doubled in size. To create a perfect rising environment, you can place the bowl in a slightly warm oven (preheat it to 200°F and then turn it off before placing the dough inside).

Step 4: Prepare the Filling

While the dough is rising, make the cinnamon filling. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, brown sugar, and cinnamon until you have a thick paste. This rich, buttery mixture is the star of the cinnamon rolls, so ensure the ingredients are well-blended.

Step 5: Roll, Spread, and Roll Again

Once the dough has doubled in size, punch it down gently to release excess air. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and roll it into a large rectangle, roughly 12x18 inches. Spread the cinnamon filling evenly over the surface of the dough using a spatula or the back of a spoon.

Starting on one long edge, roll the dough tightly into a cylinder, keeping the seam side down. Use a sharp knife or unflavored dental floss to slice the cylinder into 12 equal rolls.

Step 6: Second Rise

Arrange the cut rolls in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, leaving a little space between each roll. Cover the dish loosely and let the rolls rise for another 30–45 minutes, or until they’ve puffed up and are touching each other.

Step 7: Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 350°F. Once the rolls have risen, bake them in the center of the oven for 22–25 minutes, or until they’re golden brown and the center rolls are cooked through. If the tops are browning too quickly, tent the dish with aluminum foil during the last few minutes of baking.

Step 8: Prepare the Glaze

While the cinnamon rolls bake, make the cream cheese glaze. In a medium bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth and creamy. Add the vanilla extract and powdered sugar, mixing until combined. Gradually add milk until the glaze reaches your desired consistency.

Step 9: Frost and Serve

Once the cinnamon rolls are out of the oven, let them cool for about 5 minutes before spreading the cream cheese glaze generously over the top. Serve warm and enjoy the most irresistible homemade cinnamon rolls ever.

Tips for Success

  1. Use Room Temperature Ingredients: Let your eggs and butter sit at room temperature before starting. This ensures they incorporate smoothly into the dough, resulting in a softer and more pliable texture.
  2. Measure Flour Correctly: Too much flour can lead to tough, dense rolls, so spoon and level your flour rather than scooping it directly from the bag.
  3. Don’t Rush the Rising Time: Yeast needs adequate time to work its magic. Be patient and let the dough rise fully for the best texture.
  4. Dental Floss Cutting Method: Using dental floss instead of a knife prevents the rolls from being squished or misshapen during slicing. Simply slide the floss under the roll, cross the ends over the top, and pull through.

Customization Options

  • Add Nuts or Raisins: Sprinkle chopped pecans, walnuts, or raisins over the cinnamon filling for added flavor and texture.
  • Try a Different Glaze: Swap the cream cheese glaze for a simple vanilla icing by mixing powdered sugar, milk, and a splash of vanilla.
  • Pumpkin Spice Variation: Replace some of the cinnamon with pumpkin pie spice for a seasonal twist.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers (unlikely, but just in case), store the cinnamon rolls in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week. To reheat, warm individual rolls in the microwave for about 15 seconds to restore their gooey softness.

You can also freeze baked cinnamon rolls for longer storage. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and reheat before serving.

Why Make Cinnamon Rolls at Home?

Making cinnamon rolls from scratch is well worth the effort. The aroma of freshly baked rolls fills your kitchen with an irresistible fragrance, and the joy of pulling apart a soft, gooey roll fresh from the oven is unmatched by store-bought options. Plus, you can customize your rolls to suit your taste, ensuring they’re exactly how you like them every time.

Crafting the perfect cinnamon rolls takes a bit of practice, but it’s an incredibly rewarding experience. This recipe balances all the right elements—pillowy dough, a rich cinnamon-sugar swirl, and a creamy glaze—to deliver the ultimate comfort food. Give it a try, and prepare for everyone to ask you for the recipe. It really is the best homemade cinnamon rolls recipe ever!
