Christmas Side Dishes: 12 Recipes to Complement Your Feast


As the holiday season approaches, the scent of pine needles, cinnamon, and roasted meats fills the air. Christmas is a time for family gatherings, festive decorations, and, of course, mouth-watering feasts. While the centerpiece of your holiday table may be a glorious turkey, ham, or roast, it’s the side dishes that truly elevate the meal. From comforting casseroles to vibrant vegetable dishes, these Christmas side dishes will enchant your guests and complement your festive feast perfectly. Here are 12 delightful recipes to inspire your holiday cooking.

1. Creamy Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • ½ cup of heavy cream
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  2. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  3. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot. Mash until smooth.
  4. Stir in the garlic butter, heavy cream, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh chives.

These creamy garlic mashed potatoes are rich and velvety, making them a perfect companion for your holiday roast.

2. Honey-Glazed Carrots

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of baby carrots
  • ¼ cup of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the baby carrots and sauté for about 5 minutes.
  3. Drizzle in the honey, thyme, salt, and pepper, stirring to coat the carrots evenly.
  4. Cook for an additional 10 minutes, until the carrots are tender and glazed.

These honey-glazed carrots offer a sweet and savory flavor that pairs beautifully with savory main dishes.

3. Classic Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of stale bread, cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of dried sage
  • 1 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, melt the butter and sauté the onion and celery until soft.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the bread cubes, sautéed vegetables, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Gradually add the chicken broth until the mixture is moist but not soggy.
  5. Transfer to a greased baking dish and bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown.

This classic stuffing recipe is aromatic and flavorful, guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser at your holiday table.

4. Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • ¼ cup of balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a baking sheet, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, bacon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes until crispy and caramelized.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar before serving.

The combination of savory bacon and tangy balsamic glaze will have your guests coming back for seconds.

5. Sweet Potato Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ½ cup of brown sugar
  • ½ cup of milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of mini marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil sweet potatoes until tender, then mash in a bowl.
  3. Mix in brown sugar, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Pour into a greased baking dish and top with mini marshmallows.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes or until marshmallows are golden brown.

This sweet potato casserole is a delightful mix of sweet and creamy, sure to impress your guests.

6. Green Bean Almondine

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fresh green beans, trimmed
  • ½ cup of sliced almonds
  • 3 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the green beans in boiling water for 3-4 minutes, then plunge into ice water.
  2. In a skillet, melt the butter and toast the almonds until golden.
  3. Add the green beans to the skillet, squeezing in lemon juice and seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve warm.

This elegant dish is both nutritious and visually appealing, adding a fresh element to your holiday meal.

7. Cranberry Sauce with Orange Zest

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces of fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of water
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Juice of 1 orange

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water, bringing to a boil.
  2. Add the cranberries, orange zest, and juice, simmering until the cranberries burst, about 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and cool before serving.

This homemade cranberry sauce is a tangy, sweet addition that balances the richness of other dishes.

8. Roasted Garlic and Herb Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of baby potatoes, halved
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss the potatoes with olive oil, garlic, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 30-35 minutes, until golden and crispy.

These roasted garlic and herb potatoes are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them an irresistible side.

9. Spinach and Artichoke Dip

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 1 cup of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup of cream cheese
  • ½ cup of sour cream
  • ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine artichokes, spinach, cream cheese, sour cream, Parmesan, and garlic powder.
  3. Spread the mixture into a baking dish and bake for 25-30 minutes until bubbly and golden.

This creamy dip can be served warm with crackers or bread and makes a great appetizer or side dish.

10. Cornbread Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cornmeal
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of milk
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a bowl, mix cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together milk, melted butter, and eggs.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed, then fill muffin cups.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden.

These cornbread muffins are light and fluffy, perfect for soaking up gravy or sauce.

11. Winter Squash Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups of mixed greens
  • ½ cup of feta cheese, crumbled
  • ¼ cup of walnuts, toasted
  • 3 tablespoons of balsamic vinaigrette

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the diced squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender.
  3. In a large bowl, combine mixed greens, roasted squash, feta, and walnuts.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic vinaigrette before serving.

This refreshing winter squash salad adds a vibrant color and healthy crunch to your holiday spread.

12. Glazed Pearl Onions

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of pearl onions, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • ¼ cup of sugar
  • ½ cup of chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt the butter and add the pearl onions.
  2. Sprinkle with sugar and cook until caramelized, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add chicken broth, salt, and pepper, simmering until the liquid reduces and the onions are glazed.

These glazed pearl onions are sweet, savory, and a delightful addition to your Christmas table.

Conclusion

As you prepare for the festive season, remember that the magic of Christmas feasts lies not just in the main dish but also in the wonderful side dishes that accompany it.
