Classic Beef Stroganoff Recipe Made Simple


Beef Stroganoff is a classic dish that has delighted taste buds around the world for decades. This rich and creamy meal, typically served with egg noodles or rice, is a perfect comfort food for cozy dinners, family gatherings, or special occasions. If you’ve ever wondered how to make a hearty, delicious Beef Stroganoff from scratch, you’re in the right place. In this post, we’ll break down the steps to create this flavorful dish in a simple and accessible way.

Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this Beef Stroganoff recipe will ensure that you can enjoy the luxurious taste of tender beef and creamy sauce without complicated ingredients or techniques. Let’s dive in and learn how to make this delicious, timeless dish at home!

Ingredients for Beef Stroganoff

To make Beef Stroganoff, you’ll need a few basic ingredients. Most of these should already be staples in your pantry or fridge, making this dish quick and easy to prepare. Here’s a comprehensive list:

  • 1 lb beef (sirloin or tenderloin works best): The beef should be cut into thin strips or bite-sized pieces for easy cooking and a tender bite.
  • 2 tablespoons of butter: This helps create a rich, flavorful base for the sauce.
  • 1 onion (finely chopped): Adds a subtle sweetness and depth of flavor to the dish.
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced): Garlic gives the dish an aromatic kick.
  • 8 oz mushrooms (sliced): Mushrooms provide an earthy flavor that complements the beef and sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon flour: Used to thicken the sauce and give it the right consistency.
  • 1 cup beef broth: This enhances the flavor of the sauce, making it savory and hearty.
  • 1 cup sour cream: The creamy element that gives Beef Stroganoff its signature texture.
  • Salt and pepper: For seasoning, adjusting to your taste.
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard: Adds a tangy kick that balances the richness of the dish.
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce: A touch of this sauce adds a savory umami flavor.
  • Egg noodles or rice (for serving): Traditionally, Beef Stroganoff is served over egg noodles, but you can also opt for rice or mashed potatoes if preferred.

These ingredients combine to create a rich, creamy sauce that envelops the tender beef, making it an unforgettable meal.

How to Make Beef Stroganoff

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

To get started, it’s important to slice the beef into thin strips or bite-sized cubes. If you’re using tenderloin or sirloin, these cuts will remain tender when cooked quickly. Ensure that the beef is as uniform in size as possible to allow for even cooking.

Once sliced, season the beef generously with salt and pepper. Set it aside while you prepare the other ingredients.

Step 2: Sauté the Onions, Garlic, and Mushrooms

In a large skillet, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until softened and translucent, about 3-5 minutes. This will help bring out the natural sweetness of the onions.

Next, add the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms. Continue to sauté for another 5-7 minutes, or until the mushrooms are browned and have released their moisture. The mushrooms add an earthy depth to the dish that pairs beautifully with the beef.

Step 3: Cook the Beef

Push the mushroom and onion mixture to one side of the skillet and melt the remaining tablespoon of butter on the other side. Add the beef to the skillet in a single layer. Allow it to cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until browned. Don’t overcrowd the beef, as this can cause it to steam rather than sear. You may need to cook the beef in batches if your skillet isn’t large enough.

Once the beef is browned, return the mushrooms and onions to the pan and stir to combine. This is the perfect time to add the flour. Sprinkle the flour over the beef and vegetable mixture, and stir it in. The flour will help thicken the sauce later, so make sure it’s well incorporated.

Step 4: Create the Sauce

With the beef and mushrooms combined with the flour, it’s time to add the liquid ingredients that will form the sauce. Pour in the beef broth, stirring to incorporate. Scrape the bottom of the pan to release any flavorful browned bits (known as fond) that have stuck to the skillet. These bits add depth to the sauce.

Allow the broth to simmer for 5-7 minutes, letting the sauce thicken slightly. You’ll notice the sauce becoming rich and velvety as it reduces. This is when you want to add the Dijon mustard and Worcestershire sauce for extra flavor.

Step 5: Stir in the Sour Cream

Once the sauce has thickened, reduce the heat to low and stir in the sour cream. This will give the sauce its signature creamy texture. Stir until the sauce is smooth and well combined. Taste and adjust the seasoning with additional salt, pepper, or mustard, if necessary.

The sour cream provides that signature tangy creaminess that balances the richness of the beef and broth. The sauce should be silky and coat the back of a spoon when done.

Step 6: Serve the Beef Stroganoff

While the beef stroganoff is simmering, you can cook your egg noodles or rice. For egg noodles, cook according to the package instructions, then drain and toss with a little bit of butter or olive oil to keep them from sticking.

Once your noodles or rice are ready, spoon the Beef Stroganoff over the top. The creamy, beefy sauce will melt into the noodles, creating a deliciously comforting dish that’s sure to please everyone.

Step 7: Garnish and Enjoy!

To finish, garnish the Beef Stroganoff with a sprinkle of fresh parsley for color and freshness. You can also add a dash of paprika for a bit of smokiness, or even a squeeze of lemon juice for a burst of acidity to balance the richness.

Serve your Beef Stroganoff hot, and prepare for the compliments to roll in! The combination of tender beef, earthy mushrooms, and rich, creamy sauce is truly irresistible.

Tips for the Perfect Beef Stroganoff

Making Beef Stroganoff is relatively simple, but there are a few tips to ensure that your dish turns out perfectly every time:

  1. Choose the Right Beef: While any cut of beef can technically be used for Beef Stroganoff, tender cuts like sirloin, tenderloin, or ribeye are the best choices. These cuts cook quickly and stay tender, ensuring a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

  2. Don’t Overcook the Beef: Beef Stroganoff is all about the beef being tender. Cook it quickly over medium-high heat to brown it, and then simmer it in the sauce. Overcooking the beef can lead to a tough, dry texture.

  3. Use Full-Fat Sour Cream: For the creamiest, richest sauce, opt for full-fat sour cream. It will create the luxurious texture that makes this dish so comforting. Low-fat sour cream can sometimes cause the sauce to curdle.

  4. Add Wine for Extra Flavor: If you want to elevate the flavor of your Beef Stroganoff, consider deglazing the pan with a splash of white wine after sautéing the mushrooms and onions. The wine adds depth to the sauce and complements the beef beautifully.

  5. Make It Ahead: Beef Stroganoff is an excellent make-ahead meal. The flavors meld and intensify as it sits, making it perfect for meal prep or dinner parties. Just reheat it gently on the stove over low heat, adding a splash of beef broth if necessary to loosen the sauce.

  6. Customize with Veggies: While mushrooms are traditional in Beef Stroganoff, feel free to experiment with other vegetables. Some people like to add peas, spinach, or bell peppers for added texture and flavor.

Variations of Beef Stroganoff

Though the classic Beef Stroganoff recipe is perfect as is, you can easily customize it to suit your tastes or dietary preferences. Here are some variations you might want to try:

  • Chicken Stroganoff: Swap the beef for chicken breast or thighs for a lighter version. Chicken pairs beautifully with the creamy sauce and still creates a rich, comforting dish.
  • Vegetarian Stroganoff: For a meat-free version, replace the beef with hearty vegetables like cauliflower, zucchini, or even tempeh. A plant-based sour cream substitute can also be used to keep the dish vegetarian.
  • Spicy Stroganoff: Add a kick of heat by incorporating red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce into the sauce. This will give the dish a fiery flavor while still maintaining its creamy texture.
  • Mushroom Stroganoff: If you’re a mushroom lover, try using a mix of wild mushrooms like shiitake, cremini, and portobello. The different flavors and textures of the mushrooms will elevate the dish.

Conclusion

Beef Stroganoff is a timeless, comforting dish that’s easy to make and always delivers on flavor. With just a few simple ingredients and steps, you can create a rich, creamy, and satisfying meal that your family and friends will love. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or a cozy weeknight dinner, Beef Stroganoff is sure to become a favorite in your recipe rotation.

So next time you’re looking for a meal that’s both indulgent and simple, give this classic Beef Stroganoff recipe a try. You’ll be rewarded with a delicious, hearty dish that’s sure to impress. Happy cooking!
