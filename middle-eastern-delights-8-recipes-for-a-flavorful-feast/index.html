Middle Eastern Delights: 8 Recipes for a Flavorful Feast


The Middle East, a region brimming with history, culture, and diverse culinary traditions, offers a rich variety of dishes that tantalize the taste buds. From smoky grilled meats to aromatic rice dishes, fresh salads, and flavorful dips, Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its complex spices and unique flavor profiles. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just beginning your culinary journey, these 8 recipes will take you on a gastronomic adventure that showcases the heart and soul of Middle Eastern cooking. These dishes celebrate the abundant use of fresh ingredients, bold spices, and time-honored techniques, promising to deliver a vibrant and unforgettable feast.

1. Hummus: A Creamy, Flavorful Dip

A cornerstone of Middle Eastern cuisine, hummus is a creamy and smooth dip made from pureed chickpeas, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil. Perfect for any occasion, this beloved appetizer has become a global favorite due to its versatility and rich flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned chickpeas (or 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight)
  • 1/4 cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for desired consistency)
  • Olive oil, paprika, and fresh parsley (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. If using dried chickpeas, cook them in water until tender (about 1 to 1.5 hours). If using canned chickpeas, drain and rinse them.
  2. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and cumin. Blend until smooth.
  3. Add water a little at a time to achieve your desired consistency.
  4. Season with salt and adjust lemon juice to taste.
  5. Transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with paprika, and garnish with parsley.
  6. Serve with pita bread, vegetable sticks, or crackers for dipping.

2. Falafel: Crispy Chickpea Fritters

Falafel, a deep-fried ball made of ground chickpeas and a blend of herbs and spices, is a popular street food across the Middle East. With a crunchy exterior and soft, fluffy interior, these crispy fritters are often served in pita bread with a variety of toppings like tomatoes, cucumbers, and tahini sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-6 tablespoons flour (or as needed)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas in water for 12 hours or overnight. Drain and rinse them well.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, paprika, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is well combined but not pureed.
  3. Add baking powder and flour, mixing to form a dough. The dough should hold together but not be too sticky.
  4. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  5. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel in batches, turning occasionally until golden and crisp, about 3-5 minutes per batch.
  6. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve hot in pita bread with lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and a drizzle of tahini sauce.

3. Shawarma: Flavorful Spiced Meat Wraps

Shawarma is one of the most famous dishes in the Middle East. Traditionally made with lamb, chicken, or beef, the meat is marinated in a blend of spices, grilled, and then thinly sliced to be served in a flatbread wrap. It’s perfect for a hearty meal packed with delicious flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs (or beef/lamb)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons yogurt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground paprika
  • 1 tablespoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pita bread or flatbreads for wrapping
  • Sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and lettuce for garnish
  • Tahini or garlic sauce (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine olive oil, yogurt, garlic, cumin, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, allspice, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Add the chicken thighs to the marinade, ensuring the meat is evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for 6-7 minutes per side until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. Remove from the heat and let rest for a few minutes before slicing the chicken thinly.
  5. Warm the pita bread, and then stuff with sliced chicken, tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and lettuce.
  6. Drizzle with tahini or garlic sauce, if desired, and serve immediately.

4. Tabouleh: A Fresh, Herbaceous Salad

Tabouleh is a vibrant and refreshing salad made from bulgur wheat, parsley, mint, tomatoes, and cucumbers. Dressed with olive oil, lemon juice, and a touch of salt, this dish is both nutritious and bursting with flavors. It’s perfect as a side dish or light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the bulgur in a large bowl and cover it with boiling water. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes until softened. Drain any excess water and fluff with a fork.
  2. Add the chopped parsley, mint, tomatoes, and cucumber to the bulgur. Mix gently to combine.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss everything together and adjust the seasoning to taste.
  5. Let the salad sit for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld before serving.

5. Baba Ganoush: Smoky Eggplant Dip

Baba Ganoush is another classic Middle Eastern dip made from roasted eggplant, tahini, garlic, and lemon juice. The smoky flavor of the roasted eggplant adds depth to the creamy texture of the dip. This dish pairs wonderfully with pita bread or fresh vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Paprika, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce the eggplants with a fork and place them on a baking sheet.
  2. Roast the eggplants in the oven for 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  3. Once roasted, let the eggplants cool slightly before peeling off the skin.
  4. Place the flesh of the eggplants in a food processor along with tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and olive oil. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  5. Season with salt and adjust the flavor as needed.
  6. Transfer to a bowl, drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with paprika, and garnish with fresh parsley.
  7. Serve with pita or fresh vegetables.

6. Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolma)

Dolma is a dish consisting of vine leaves stuffed with a mixture of rice, pine nuts, and herbs. Often served as an appetizer or snack, dolmas are light, flavorful, and make for a perfect accompaniment to any Middle Eastern meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar grape leaves in brine (about 20-30 leaves)
  • 1 cup short-grain rice
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the grape leaves thoroughly to remove the brine and set them aside to drain.
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and pine nuts and sauté until the onion is soft and the pine nuts are golden.
  3. Add the rice and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat and stir in parsley, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Place a grape leaf on a flat surface with the vein side up. Spoon about a tablespoon of the rice mixture onto the leaf and fold in the sides before rolling it tightly.
  5. Repeat with the remaining grape leaves and rice mixture.
  6. Arrange the stuffed grape leaves in a pot, stacking them as needed. Cover with water and drizzle with a bit of olive oil.
  7. Bring to a boil, reduce the heat, and simmer for about 45 minutes until the rice is cooked.
  8. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

7. Kebabs: Grilled Meat Skewers

Kebabs are a staple of Middle Eastern grilling. Whether made with chicken, lamb, or beef, these skewers are marinated in flavorful spices and grilled to perfection. Serve them with rice or flatbread for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless chicken thighs (or lamb/beef)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, salt, and pepper.
  2. Cut the chicken (or meat) into bite-sized chunks and marinate in the mixture for at least 2 hours.
  3. Preheat the grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated meat onto the skewers, making sure to leave space between each piece.
  5. Grill the kebabs for about 4-5 minutes per side or until fully cooked and lightly charred.
  6. Serve the kebabs with rice, flatbread, or a simple salad.

8. Baklava: A Sweet, Flaky Pastry

Baklava is a sweet, rich dessert made from layers of flaky phyllo dough, chopped nuts, and a syrup made from honey, sugar, and lemon juice. It’s one of the most beloved sweets in the Middle East, known for its crisp texture and delightful sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios, almonds), chopped
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Ground cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush a baking dish with melted butter.
  2. Layer 8 sheets of phyllo dough in the dish, brushing each sheet with butter before adding the next.
  3. Sprinkle a thin layer of chopped nuts over the phyllo dough and continue layering with buttered phyllo sheets until all the nuts are used.
  4. Finish with a final layer of phyllo dough, using about 8 more sheets.
  5. Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes or until golden and crispy.
  7. While baking, make the syrup: Combine sugar, water, honey, and lemon juice in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  8. Once the baklava is done, pour the hot syrup over the hot pastry. Let it cool completely before serving.

These 8 Middle Eastern dishes will bring a delightful array of flavors to your table. From savory dips and salads to grilled meats and sweet desserts, they represent the vibrant and diverse culinary heritage of the region. Enjoy creating these delicious dishes at home and indulge in the flavors of the Middle East!
