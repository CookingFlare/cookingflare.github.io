Classic Pound Cake: Simple Elegance in Every Slice


Are you in the mood for a classic dessert that never goes out of style? Look no further than the timeless and elegant Classic Pound Cake. This rich and buttery treat has been a favorite for generations, and it’s not hard to see why. With its dense yet tender crumb and subtle vanilla flavor, this cake is the epitome of comfort and indulgence.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch loaf pan, ensuring all sides are well-coated.

  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. This can be done using an electric mixer on medium-high speed for about 5 minutes. The mixture should become pale and airy.

  3. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Make sure each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. This will ensure a smooth and uniform batter.

  4. Stir in the vanilla extract, combining it thoroughly with the butter-sugar mixture. The aroma of vanilla will start to fill your kitchen, adding to the anticipation of the final result.

  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the butter-sugar mixture, alternating with the milk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing just until combined after each addition. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense texture.

  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Give the pan a gentle tap on the countertop to remove any air bubbles.

  7. Bake for 60 to 70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The top of the cake should be golden brown and the edges slightly pulled away from the sides of the pan.

  8. Once the cake is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing. This will allow the flavors to fully develop and the texture to set.

  9. Serve your Classic Pound Cake on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream and fresh berries for a delightful twist. Whether enjoyed as an afternoon snack, a dessert after dinner, or even as the centerpiece of a special occasion, this cake is sure to impress.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of citrus, add the zest of one lemon or orange to the batter.
  • Experiment with different extracts, such as almond or coconut, to create unique flavor profiles.
  • To make a glaze, mix together powdered sugar and lemon juice until smooth and drizzle it over the cooled cake.
  • This cake can be stored at room temperature for up to 3 days or frozen for longer shelf life.

So, if you’re looking for a dessert that embodies simplicity and elegance, give the Classic Pound Cake a try. Its rich flavor and moist texture will have you coming back for seconds, and maybe even thirds. This is a recipe that has stood the test of time, and it’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Happy baking!
