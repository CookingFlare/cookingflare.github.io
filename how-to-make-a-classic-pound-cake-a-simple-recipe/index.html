How to Make a Classic Pound Cake: A Simple Recipe


Pound cake, with its rich texture and buttery flavor, stands as a timeless dessert cherished by generations. The name ‘pound cake’ originates from its traditional recipe—one pound each of butter, sugar, eggs, and flour. Although this classic formula laid the foundation, bakers today often tweak the proportions and incorporate additional ingredients to personalize their pound cakes. Whether you’re a baking novice or seasoned aficionado, mastering a classic pound cake can elevate your baking repertoire and satisfy those cravings for a moist, tender slice of heaven.

Why Classic Pound Cake?

What sets a pound cake apart from other cakes is its dense yet tender crumb and that deep buttery flavor that lingers with each bite. Unlike airy sponge cakes that rely heavily on whipped egg whites or chemical leaveners, pound cake achieves its texture through the careful creaming of butter and sugar, coupled with the gradual incorporation of eggs and flour. This creates a lush, velvety interior perfect for everything from a simple afternoon tea to an elegant dessert buffet.

The beauty of a classic pound cake lies in its simplicity—the ingredients are familiar pantry staples, requiring no exotic additions. This makes it accessible for bakers of all levels and an excellent base for creative embellishments such as glazes, fresh fruit, or a dusting of powdered sugar.

Ingredients for a Classic Pound Cake

  • Unsalted butter: 1 cup (226 grams), softened
  • Granulated sugar: 1 cup (200 grams)
  • Large eggs: 4, at room temperature
  • All-purpose flour: 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons (approximately 130 grams), sifted
  • Vanilla extract: 2 teaspoons
  • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Baking powder: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for slight lift)

Ingredient Notes

  • Using unsalted butter allows you control over the salt content. Make sure your butter is softened but not melted to achieve the ideal batter texture.
  • Room temperature eggs blend more easily, ensuring even distribution and better emulsification.
  • Although traditional pound cake recipes omit leavening agents, a small amount of baking powder helps the cake rise just enough to lighten the dense crumb without compromising its signature texture.

Essential Equipment

  • 9x5-inch loaf pan or a Bundt pan
  • Electric mixer (stand or hand-held)
  • Rubber spatula
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Cooling rack

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Pound Cake

1. Prepare Your Pan

Begin by thoroughly greasing your loaf or Bundt pan with butter or non-stick spray, then dust it lightly with flour. Tapping out the excess flour prevents the cake from sticking and ensures it releases cleanly after baking.

2. Preheat the Oven

Set your oven to 350°F (175°C) to allow it ample time to reach the perfect baking temperature. This even heat is critical for a golden crust and tender interior.

3. Cream Butter and Sugar

In the mixing bowl, beat the softened butter on medium speed for about 1 minute until smooth and creamy. Gradually add the granulated sugar, increasing the speed to medium-high, and continue beating for 4–5 minutes. This step is crucial—it incorporates air into the mixture, contributing to a lighter crumb.

4. Add Eggs Gradually

Beat the eggs in, one at a time, making sure each is fully incorporated before adding the next. This slow addition stabilizes the batter and prevents it from curdling, preserving its creamy texture.

5. Mix in Vanilla and Salt

Add the vanilla extract and salt into the batter, folding them gently with a rubber spatula to infuse the cake with aromatic flavor and balance sweetness.

6. Sift and Incorporate Flour and Baking Powder

Sift together the all-purpose flour and baking powder to ensure even distribution. Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter-egg mixture. Use the electric mixer on low speed or fold in by hand to avoid overmixing, which could develop the gluten excessively and make the cake tough.

7. Transfer Batter to Pan

Scrape the batter carefully into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Give the pan a gentle tap on the counter to eliminate any air bubbles.

8. Bake to Perfection

Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 50-60 minutes. Start checking around 45 minutes by inserting a toothpick or skewer into the center—if it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, the cake is done.

9. Cooling

Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 15 minutes. Then transfer it onto a wire rack to cool completely. This step prevents moisture from accumulating at the bottom, keeping the crust firm.

Serving Suggestions and Variations

  • Classic with a Dusting: Once cooled, sprinkle a light dusting of powdered sugar over the cake for an elegant, simple finish.

  • Glazed Twist: For a shiny, flavored top, drizzle a lemon or orange glaze made from powdered sugar and citrus juice.

  • Berry Companion: Serve slices with fresh berries and a dollop of whipped cream for a refreshing contrast.

  • Chocolate Swirl: For those seeking decadence, gently fold in a handful of chocolate chips or swirl in some cocoa powder to the batter before baking.

  • Spiced Upgrade: Add cinnamon, nutmeg, or cardamom for a cozy, aromatic profile.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Dense or Heavy Cake: Make sure you cream your butter and sugar sufficiently to trap air. Avoid overmixing once flour is added as this develops gluten and toughens the cake.

  • Cake Sinking: Do not open the oven door frequently during baking as temperature changes cause the cake to collapse. Verify that your baking powder is fresh.

  • Dry Texture: Avoid overbaking; start checking early to prevent loss of moisture. Also, ensure accurate measuring of ingredients—too much flour or too little butter dries out the cake.

Storing Pound Cake

Wrap the pound cake tightly in plastic wrap or store it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, the cake can be frozen for up to 3 months. Allow it to thaw at room temperature before serving.

Final Thoughts

Making a classic pound cake is more than following a recipe—it’s about understanding the balance between ingredients and technique. This simple, straightforward recipe yields a cake that’s rich in flavor, with a moist, tender crumb that melts in your mouth. Every slice tells a story of tradition, craft, and culinary satisfaction.

Whether you enjoy it plain, crowned with berries, or turned into an elegant dessert, mastering this classic pound cake is a rewarding baking experience that will impress friends and family alike. So preheat your oven, grab your mixing bowl, and delight in the timeless pleasure of homemade pound cake.
