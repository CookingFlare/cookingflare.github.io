How to Make Authentic Chinese Ma Lai Go (Steamed Cake)


Ma Lai Go, also known as Chinese steamed cake, is a beloved dessert in Cantonese cuisine. Its soft, fluffy texture, combined with a slightly sweet flavor, makes it a delightful treat for any occasion. Whether you’re making it for a special celebration or simply to satisfy your sweet tooth, this traditional cake is easy to prepare and incredibly satisfying. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through a step-by-step guide to creating an authentic Ma Lai Go at home, along with helpful tips and variations to customize the recipe to your taste.

What is Ma Lai Go?

Ma Lai Go, or 馬來糕 in Mandarin, is a popular steamed cake that originates from Chinese-speaking regions, especially Hong Kong and Guangdong. The cake is known for its light and airy texture, which is achieved through the steaming process, as opposed to baking in the oven. It’s typically made with a few simple ingredients: flour, sugar, eggs, and a leavening agent. While the cake itself is subtly sweet, it can be flavored with a variety of ingredients like pandan, coconut milk, or even red bean paste to suit different tastes.

Ma Lai Go is a perfect dessert for afternoon tea, a family gathering, or a festive occasion like Chinese New Year. Its popularity lies in its simplicity and the unique method of steaming, which gives the cake its characteristic moist and tender crumb.

Ingredients for Ma Lai Go

To make authentic Chinese Ma Lai Go, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (approximately 120g)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (approximately 100g)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (for added richness, optional)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

Tools You’ll Need

  • A steaming pot or wok (or a large pot with a lid)
  • A round or square cake pan (preferably non-stick)
  • A mixing bowl
  • A whisk or electric mixer
  • A spatula or spoon
  • A strainer (to sift the flour)

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Ma Lai Go

Step 1: Prepare the Steamer

Before you begin mixing the ingredients, it’s essential to prepare your steaming setup. You can either use a traditional Chinese steamer, a wok with a steaming rack, or simply a large pot with a tight-fitting lid. Fill the bottom of the steamer with water, ensuring that the water level doesn’t touch the cake pan once it is placed inside. Bring the water to a boil while you prepare the cake batter.

Step 2: Sift the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, sift the all-purpose flour to ensure that there are no lumps. This step is crucial for the texture of the cake, as it helps incorporate air into the flour and ensures the cake will be light and fluffy. Add the baking powder and a pinch of salt to the flour and sift again to evenly distribute the leavening agent.

Step 3: Whisk the Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and sugar together until the mixture becomes light and frothy. This process is vital to achieve a soft, airy texture in the cake. You can use an electric mixer for this step if you want a smoother result. Once the egg and sugar mixture is well combined, add the coconut milk (if using), vegetable oil (or melted butter), and vanilla extract, if desired. Continue to whisk until everything is evenly incorporated.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Slowly add the wet ingredients into the dry mixture, stirring gently with a spatula. It’s important to avoid over-mixing at this point. The batter should be smooth and slightly thick, with no lumps. If the batter is too thick, you can add a little bit of water, a tablespoon at a time, until the desired consistency is achieved. The batter should be slightly pourable but still thick enough to hold its shape.

Step 5: Pour the Batter into the Cake Pan

Lightly grease the cake pan with a small amount of vegetable oil or butter to ensure the cake doesn’t stick. Pour the batter into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles from the batter, which can create holes or uneven texture in the cake.

Step 6: Steam the Cake

Once your cake pan is ready, carefully place it into the steaming pot or wok. Cover the steamer with a tight-fitting lid and steam the cake over medium-high heat for about 30 to 40 minutes. It’s important to check the water level in your steamer during the steaming process and add more water if necessary to ensure consistent steam.

After about 30 minutes, check the cake by inserting a toothpick or skewer into the center. If it comes out clean, the cake is ready. If the batter is still sticky, continue to steam for another 5-10 minutes.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Once the Ma Lai Go is fully steamed, carefully remove the cake pan from the steamer. Allow the cake to cool for a few minutes before flipping it out of the pan onto a serving plate. You can slice it into small squares or wedges for serving. Ma Lai Go is best enjoyed warm, but it can also be kept at room temperature for a day or two.

Tips for Making Perfect Ma Lai Go

  • Sifting the flour and baking powder: This step is crucial to ensure that the dry ingredients are evenly distributed and to incorporate air into the flour. This will help create the soft, airy texture that Ma Lai Go is known for.

  • Steaming at the right temperature: Steaming at a medium-high heat ensures the cake cooks evenly without being overcooked or undercooked. Avoid steaming on too high a heat, as it may cause the cake to crack on top.

  • Texture: If you prefer a denser, more moist cake, you can adjust the amount of coconut milk or use more egg yolks. For a lighter cake, use more water and ensure the egg mixture is whisked until frothy.

  • Flavor variations: Although traditional Ma Lai Go is simple, you can experiment by adding ingredients like pandan paste for a fragrant, green-colored cake, or red bean paste for a sweet filling. For a more exotic twist, consider adding shredded coconut or a hint of citrus zest.

  • Toppings: You can top your steamed cake with powdered sugar or drizzle it with sweetened condensed milk for extra sweetness. Additionally, fresh fruits such as strawberries or mangoes can be used as toppings for a refreshing touch.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-mixing the batter: When combining the wet and dry ingredients, mix gently and avoid overworking the batter. Over-mixing can lead to a dense and chewy texture, which is not characteristic of Ma Lai Go.

  • Under-steaming: If the cake is not steamed long enough, the texture may be too wet or gooey. On the other hand, over-steaming can dry the cake out. It’s important to check for doneness by inserting a skewer into the center.

  • Not checking the water level in the steamer: Steaming is a delicate process, and running out of water can lead to uneven results. Keep an eye on the water level and top up as needed.

Variations of Ma Lai Go

While the traditional version of Ma Lai Go is simple and delicious, there are several variations you can try to add your personal touch to this classic Chinese dessert.

  1. Pandan Ma Lai Go: Pandan leaves are commonly used in Southeast Asian desserts to impart a fragrant, slightly sweet flavor and a vibrant green color. To make pandan Ma Lai Go, simply add 1-2 teaspoons of pandan paste to the batter before steaming.

  2. Coconut Ma Lai Go: For a more tropical flavor, replace the coconut milk with fresh grated coconut. You can also sprinkle shredded coconut on top of the cake before steaming to add texture.

  3. Red Bean Ma Lai Go: To add a twist of flavor, incorporate sweetened red bean paste into the batter or layer it in between two layers of cake. The red bean paste adds a creamy, earthy flavor that balances the sweetness of the cake.

  4. Savory Ma Lai Go: For a savory variation, omit the sugar and add a pinch of salt and a dash of soy sauce. This results in a unique, savory steamed cake that can be enjoyed as a snack or appetizer.

Conclusion

Making authentic Ma Lai Go at home is a fun and rewarding experience. With its soft, fluffy texture and simple ingredients, this classic Chinese steamed cake is sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you stick to the traditional recipe or get creative with flavors, Ma Lai Go offers endless possibilities for customization. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the steamer, and enjoy a delicious slice of this beloved Cantonese treat. Happy cooking!
