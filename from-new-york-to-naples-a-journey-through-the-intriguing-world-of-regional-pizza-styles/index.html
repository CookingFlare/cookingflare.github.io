From New York to Naples: A Journey Through the Intriguing World of Regional Pizza Styles


Pizza has an enigmatic quality that transcends borders and cultures. With its humble origins in Naples, Italy, this culinary gem has evolved into a beloved dish with hundreds of unique styles, each reflective of the region from which it comes. From the sodium-thrashed, foldable slices of New York to the syrupy sweetness of a Hawaiian pie, there is no shortage of variation.

As we embark on our journey through the world of regional pizza styles, let’s put on our aprons and embark on a sensory adventure, exploring how diverse ingredients & methods have weaved their way into the heart of pizza-making. We are about to uncover hidden layers of each regional pizza in this multi-part series. Let us start by exploring two iconic styles: Neapolitan and New York pizza.

Neapolitan Pizza – The Authentic Cradle

Our journey begins in Naples, a city that prides itself on a deep history and rivalry of being the birthplace of pizza. The first documented case of “pizza” being served dates back to 1889, during the celebration of the unification of Italy. Since then, Neapolitan pizza has become synonymous with Italy’s culinary treasure.

Neapolitan pizza, sometimes referred to as “Neapolitan margherita,” is distinguished by its leopard-like crust, chewy center, and slightly soupy undercarriage, thanks to the high hydration of its dough. The pies are baked in a wood-fired oven at a scorching 800-900°F (425-475°C) for a maximum of 60-90 seconds. For a real Neapolitan experience, the preparation involves obsidian-black San Marzano tomatoes, fresh basil from the Campania region, and Pecorino Romano modified into a powder called “pomodorini.”

In recent years, Neapolitan pizzas have successfully gained recognition when in October 2017, it received a prestigious Protected Designation of Origin (P.D.O.) certification.

New York-Style Pizza – The Big Apple’s Slice

Moving north to the bustling streets of New York City, one can find another celebrated style that is uniquely its own. In comparison to Neapolitan’s dedicated bead of bubbling cheese, New York-style pizza boasts a larger, crispier crust with very little garnishing. Delicate and airy, the dough is rolled thin to create wide, generous slices.

The Watery, wet, and droopy nature of New York pizza hailed from Italian-American immigrants, who adapted the pizza into a food that was easily transportable, using delivery workers and snatch-and-go customers. Pizza in New York City thrived and changed in the pizzerias that were scattered throughout urban islands of the city, becoming larger and accommodating more than a slice, selling individually like slices of pie from a deli case.

New Yorkers are quite polarized about sauce; some like it light-hearted and minimal, while others prefer a dunk of robust tomato sauce that leaves your thumb and forefinger relatively saucy. In New York, the controversy lies between the wet-bottom market, offering an Italian-American touch with the addition of garlic, oregano, and a gooey clump of mozzarella that bubbles to create the iconic “raindrop” effect.

Embracing the Variations Beyond Borders

The broader world is home to a kaleidoscope of pizza styles, all worthy of a place at the table. From Chicago’s deep-dish that resembles a pie to Detroit’s pan pizza, with its crispy edges, and even the Californian gourmet pizza introducing the concept of “create-your-own” experience, each style holds its own charm and alluring history.

These unique regional variations tell stories of their hometowns and the cultures that surround them, making pizza more than just a food; it becomes a narrative of tradition, flavor, and passion. By joining the dots between Naples and New York City, one can appreciate how regional ingredients, methods, and stories combine to create a wealth of flavors and textures that continue to astonish our culinary imagination.

As we continue to travel and adapt our palates, we realize that pizza itself is like a cultural melting pot, settling in the hearts of people and nations, transforming, evolving, becoming a shared language of taste regardless of nationality or tradition.

Ready for your next culinary journey? Choose your favorite style, gather some fresh ingredients, and unleash your inner pizzaiolo. Embrace the diversity, find your personal affinity, and let the fun, delicious, aromatically adventure unfold!
