Blueberry Coconut Muffins: A Perfectly Portable Breakfast Option

3 min · 620 words

Who doesn’t love waking up to the smell of freshly baked muffins? The combination of juicy blueberries and tropical coconut makes these Blueberry Coconut Muffins the ultimate breakfast treat. Not only are they delicious, but they are also incredibly portable, making them the perfect on-the-go breakfast option. So let’s dive in and learn how to make these mouthwatering muffins!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup melted coconut oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or lightly grease the cups.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and shredded coconut. Make sure all the ingredients are well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, melted coconut oil, eggs, and vanilla extract until smooth.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.

  5. Gently fold in the blueberries, making sure they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  6. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each one about 2/3 full.

  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The muffins should be golden brown on top.

  8. Remove the muffins from the oven and allow them to cool in the tin for 5 minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  9. Once cooled, these Blueberry Coconut Muffins are ready to be enjoyed! They are delicious on their own, but you can also serve them with a dollop of coconut yogurt or a sprinkle of powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness.

Tips and Variations:

  • If you prefer a sweeter muffin, you can add an additional 1/4 cup of granulated sugar to the batter.

  • Feel free to customize these muffins by adding your favorite mix-ins. Chopped nuts or white chocolate chips would be a delicious addition.

  • To make these muffins dairy-free, you can substitute the eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water per egg) and use a dairy-free milk alternative.

  • If you don’t have fresh blueberries on hand, you can use frozen blueberries. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before adding them to the batter.

These Blueberry Coconut Muffins are a delightful combination of flavors and textures. They are soft, moist, and bursting with juicy blueberries. The subtle hint of coconut adds a tropical twist that will transport your taste buds to paradise. Whether you’re enjoying them at home or taking them on the go, these muffins are sure to become a breakfast favorite. Give them a try and let us know what you think!
