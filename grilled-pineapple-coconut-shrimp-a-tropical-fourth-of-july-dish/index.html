Grilled Pineapple Coconut Shrimp: A Tropical Fourth of July Dish


summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to fire up the grill and throw a Fourth of July party that your guests will never forget. But why settle for the same old burgers and hot dogs when you can impress your friends and family with a dish that’s as exotic as it is delicious? Look no further than grilled pineapple coconut shrimp - a tropical twist on a classic summer treat that’s sure to be the star of your Independence Day celebration.

The Perfect Combination

The key to this dish is the perfect combination of sweet and savory flavors. The juicy pineapple, caramelized to perfection on the grill, provides a natural sweetness that pairs perfectly with the savory flavor of the shrimp. Add in the creamy coconut flakes and a hint of spice from the cayenne pepper, and you have a flavor combination that’s both familiar and exotic.

The Recipe

To make this dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 ripe pineapple, cut into 1-inch wedges
  • 1/4 cup coconut flakes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, honey, soy sauce, and cayenne pepper. Add the shrimp and toss to coat, making sure they’re evenly coated with the marinade. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to 2 hours.

Step 2: Grill the Pineapple

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the pineapple wedges from the refrigerator and brush them with a little bit of olive oil. Place the pineapple on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until caramelized and slightly charred. Remove the pineapple from the grill and set it aside.

Step 3: Grill the Shrimp

Remove the shrimp from the marinade and place them on the grill. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until pink and cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the grill and set them aside with the pineapple.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

To assemble the dish, place a cooked shrimp on each plate. Top the shrimp with a piece of grilled pineapple, then sprinkle with coconut flakes. Garnish with a sprig of fresh cilantro and serve immediately.

Tips and Variations

  • To add a little extra flavor to your dish, try adding a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds or chopped fresh mint to the coconut flakes.
  • If you prefer a spicier dish, you can increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the marinade or add a diced jalapeno pepper to the pineapple.
  • To make this dish more substantial, try serving it with a side of quinoa or brown rice and a simple green salad.

Conclusion

Grilled pineapple coconut shrimp is a dish that’s sure to impress your friends and family this Fourth of July. With its perfect combination of sweet and savory flavors, it’s a twist on a classic summer treat that’s sure to become a new favorite. So why not fire up the grill and give it a try? Your taste buds - and your guests - will thank you.
