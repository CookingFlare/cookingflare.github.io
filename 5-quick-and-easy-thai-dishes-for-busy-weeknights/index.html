5 Quick and Easy Thai Dishes for Busy Weeknights


When the clock is ticking and your schedule is packed, the last thing you want to do is spend hours in the kitchen. However, Thai cuisine offers a delightful array of flavors that can be prepared quickly and easily, making it perfect for busy weeknights. In this post, we’ll explore five quick and easy Thai dishes that are not only delicious but also simple enough to whip up in no time. Let’s dive into the vibrant world of Thai cooking!

1. Thai Basil Chicken (Pad Krapow Gai)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground chicken
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving
  • Fried egg (optional), for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Add the minced garlic and chopped chilies, stirring for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Add the ground chicken to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spatula. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add Sauces: Stir in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Mix well and let it cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Incorporate Basil: Remove the skillet from heat and fold in the Thai basil leaves until they wilt.
  6. Serve: Serve the Thai basil chicken over jasmine rice, topped with a fried egg if desired.

Why It’s Great for Busy Nights:

This dish is not only quick to prepare but also packed with flavor. The combination of savory sauces and fresh basil creates a satisfying meal that can be on your table in under 30 minutes.

2. Quick Thai Green Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 pound chicken breast, thinly sliced (or tofu for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 cup bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup zucchini, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Fresh basil and cilantro, for garnish
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat Coconut Milk: In a large pot, pour in the coconut milk and bring it to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add Curry Paste: Stir in the green curry paste, mixing until well combined.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Add the sliced chicken (or tofu) and cook for about 5 minutes until it’s cooked through.
  4. Add Vegetables: Toss in the bell peppers and zucchini, cooking for an additional 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season: Stir in the fish sauce and brown sugar, adjusting to taste.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil and cilantro, and serve with cooked rice.

Why It’s Great for Busy Nights:

This green curry is a one-pot wonder that comes together in less than 30 minutes. The creamy coconut milk and aromatic curry paste create a comforting dish that’s perfect for any night of the week.

3. Thai Shrimp Salad (Yum Goong)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1-2 Thai bird chilies, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Shrimp: In a pot of boiling water, cook the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes until they turn pink. Drain and let cool.
  2. Prepare the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, and chopped chilies.
  3. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the cooked shrimp, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and cilantro.
  4. Dress the Salad: Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve: Serve immediately or chill for a refreshing meal.

Why It’s Great for Busy Nights:

This salad is light, refreshing, and requires minimal cooking. It’s perfect for those hot evenings when you want something healthy and satisfying without the fuss.
