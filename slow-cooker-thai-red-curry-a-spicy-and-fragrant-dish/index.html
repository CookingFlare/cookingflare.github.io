Slow Cooker Thai Red Curry: A Spicy and Fragrant Dish

4 min · 656 words

If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine and the enticing blend of spicy, sweet, and savory flavors, then Slow Cooker Thai Red Curry is a dish that belongs in your recipe collection. This aromatic and vibrant dish is a perfect harmony of rich coconut milk, red curry paste, and an array of fresh vegetables and protein, creating a tantalizing experience for your taste buds. The best part? You can effortlessly prepare this culinary delight in your slow cooker, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully while you go about your day. Let’s dive into the tantalizing world of Slow Cooker Thai Red Curry and discover how to create this delectable dish from the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Red Curry Paste:

  • 4-6 Thai red chilies
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 small shallot
  • 1 stalk of lemongrass
  • 1-inch piece of galangal or ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime

For the Curry:

  • 1 1/2 pounds of chicken, tofu, or your choice of protein
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 red bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 large carrot, sliced
  • 1 small eggplant, diced
  • 1 cup of snap peas
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Red Curry Paste:

    • In a food processor, combine the red chilies, garlic, shallot, lemongrass, galangal or ginger, ground coriander, ground cumin, ground white pepper, tomato paste, fish sauce, and the zest and juice of one lime. Blend until a smooth paste forms. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the Slow Cooker:

    • If your slow cooker has a sauté function, heat it and add a tablespoon of oil. If not, you can use a separate pan for this step. Add the prepared red curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Combine Ingredients:

    • Add the chicken, tofu, or chosen protein to the slow cooker, followed by the coconut milk, chicken or vegetable broth, red bell peppers, carrot, eggplant, snap peas, brown sugar, and soy sauce. Stir to combine all the ingredients and coat them with the red curry paste.

  4. Slow Cook:

    • Cover the slow cooker and set it to cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours. This slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a rich and aromatic curry.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Once the cooking time is complete, taste the curry and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve the Slow Cooker Thai Red Curry over steamed jasmine rice, and garnish with fresh basil leaves for a burst of color and additional aroma.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian or vegan version, simply omit the meat and use tofu or a medley of vegetables as the protein base.
  • Feel free to adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or fewer Thai red chilies to suit your palate.
  • Experiment with different vegetables such as bamboo shoots, baby corn, or Thai eggplants to add variety to your curry.

Final Thoughts

Slow Cooker Thai Red Curry is a dish that not only delights the senses but also offers the convenience of slow cooking, making it a wonderful option for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. The combination of exotic spices, creamy coconut milk, and the freshness of the vegetables creates a symphony of flavors that will transport you to the bustling streets of Thailand. Whether you’re a seasoned Thai food enthusiast or a newcomer to this culinary tradition, Slow Cooker Thai Red Curry is an invitation to savor the bold and aromatic essence of Thai cuisine in the comfort of your own home. So, gather your ingredients, set your slow cooker, and get ready to embark on a flavorful journey that will leave you craving more with every tantalizing bite.
