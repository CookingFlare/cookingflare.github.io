Top Grilled Seafood Recipes for Your Fourth of July BBQ


When the Fourth of July rolls around, it’s time to fire up the grill and indulge in mouthwatering, smokey treats. While traditional BBQ fare like burgers and hot dogs often steal the spotlight, grilled seafood offers a flavorful and refreshing twist for your summer cookout. Whether you’re planning to serve a crowd or simply want a delectable meal to enjoy with family and friends, seafood is the perfect option for a summer BBQ.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best grilled seafood recipes that will make your Fourth of July BBQ a hit. From succulent shrimp to tender fish fillets, these dishes are easy to prepare, bursting with flavor, and sure to impress your guests. So, grab your tongs, heat up the grill, and get ready for a seafood feast that will have everyone coming back for more!

Grilled Shrimp Skewers with Garlic Butter

Shrimp is a popular seafood choice for grilling due to its quick cook time and sweet, juicy flavor. Grilling shrimp on skewers makes for a fun and easy-to-eat dish that’s perfect for outdoor gatherings. The garlic butter marinade adds a rich, savory note that pairs perfectly with the shrimp’s natural sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine melted butter, minced garlic, lemon juice, smoked paprika, parsley, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  2. Thread the shrimp onto the skewers, ensuring they’re spaced evenly.
  3. Brush the shrimp generously with the garlic butter mixture.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are pink and opaque.
  6. Serve immediately with extra garlic butter for dipping.

These grilled shrimp skewers will quickly become a crowd favorite at your Fourth of July BBQ. The garlic butter adds richness, while the lemon juice provides a zesty freshness that’s perfect for a summer cookout.

Grilled Lobster Tails with Lemon Herb Butter

Lobster is the epitome of luxury, and grilling lobster tails elevates its natural sweetness while adding a smoky depth of flavor. This recipe combines the richness of lobster with a zesty lemon herb butter that complements the delicate meat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 lobster tails, split lengthwise
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Using kitchen scissors, carefully cut the lobster tails lengthwise down the center.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, lemon juice, parsley, garlic powder, red pepper flakes, and salt.
  4. Brush the lobster meat with the lemon herb butter mixture, ensuring it’s evenly coated.
  5. Place the lobster tails on the grill, flesh side down, and cook for 5-6 minutes.
  6. Flip the tails and brush with more butter. Continue grilling for another 3-4 minutes, or until the meat is opaque and tender.
  7. Serve with additional lemon wedges and a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

Grilled lobster tails are a show-stopping dish that will elevate your Fourth of July BBQ to the next level. The tangy, buttery sauce perfectly complements the sweet, succulent lobster meat, making it a delightful treat for any seafood lover.

Grilled Salmon with Maple Mustard Glaze

Salmon is another fantastic fish for grilling, thanks to its firm texture and rich flavor. When paired with a sweet and tangy maple mustard glaze, this grilled salmon is sure to be a hit at your BBQ. The glaze caramelizes slightly as it cooks, giving the salmon a beautiful, glossy finish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets, skin on
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, Dijon mustard, soy sauce, olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Brush the salmon fillets with the maple mustard glaze, coating them evenly on both sides.
  4. Place the salmon fillets on the grill, skin side down, and cook for 4-5 minutes.
  5. Flip the fillets and brush with more glaze. Grill for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Serve with extra glaze and fresh herbs on top.

The combination of sweet maple syrup and tangy mustard creates a balanced glaze that highlights the natural flavor of the salmon. This dish is perfect for your Fourth of July celebration and is sure to leave a lasting impression.

Grilled Scallops with Lemon Garlic Sauce

Grilled scallops are a luxurious addition to any BBQ menu, and they cook up quickly on the grill. Their tender, slightly sweet flavor is enhanced by a zesty lemon garlic sauce that adds a punch of flavor without overwhelming the delicate seafood.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large sea scallops, cleaned and patted dry
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine melted butter, minced garlic, lemon juice, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Season the scallops with salt and pepper.
  4. Lightly oil the grill grates to prevent sticking, then place the scallops on the grill.
  5. Grill the scallops for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and opaque.
  6. Drizzle the lemon garlic sauce over the grilled scallops and serve immediately.

These grilled scallops are the perfect bite-sized treat for your BBQ. The buttery lemon garlic sauce elevates the scallops’ natural sweetness, creating a dish that’s both refreshing and indulgent.

Grilled Fish Tacos with Mango Salsa

Fish tacos are a fun and flavorful option for your Fourth of July BBQ. Grilled white fish fillets, such as tilapia or mahi-mahi, are topped with a zesty mango salsa that brings a tropical flair to the party. This recipe is light, fresh, and perfect for a summer gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 4 white fish fillets (tilapia, mahi-mahi, or cod)
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup mango, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the fish fillets with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the fish for 3-4 minutes per side, or until it easily flakes with a fork.
  4. While the fish is grilling, make the mango salsa by combining the mango, red onion, cilantro, jalapeño, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Warm the corn tortillas on the grill for 1-2 minutes per side.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing a portion of grilled fish on each tortilla and topping with a generous spoonful of mango salsa.

These grilled fish tacos are bursting with bright, fresh flavors. The sweet mango salsa adds a tropical sweetness that pairs beautifully with the smoky grilled fish, making them a perfect choice for your Fourth of July celebration.

Grilled Calamari with Lemon and Herbs

Calamari, or squid, is another excellent seafood option for grilling. Its mild flavor and chewy texture become wonderfully tender when grilled over high heat, and the addition of lemon and fresh herbs gives it a Mediterranean flair.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh calamari, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the calamari rings with olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the calamari onto skewers or use a grill basket for easy flipping.
  4. Grill the calamari for 2-3 minutes, turning frequently to ensure even cooking.
  5. Remove from the grill and garnish with fresh parsley.

Grilled calamari is a quick and flavorful seafood option that brings a touch of sophistication to your BBQ. The lemon and herbs enhance the natural flavor of the squid, creating a light yet satisfying dish.

Conclusion

Grilled seafood is a perfect addition to any Fourth of July BBQ, offering a refreshing and flavorful alternative to traditional grill fare. From shrimp skewers and lobster tails to salmon and fish tacos, there are endless possibilities for creating delicious and impressive seafood dishes that will wow your guests. These recipes are easy to follow, bursting with flavor, and ideal for summer grilling.

So, this Fourth of July, take a break from the usual burgers and hot dogs and treat your guests to an unforgettable seafood feast. Whether you’re serving juicy shrimp, tender lobster, or succulent fish, these grilled seafood recipes will surely make your BBQ the highlight of the holiday. Enjoy the summer flavors and the good company!
