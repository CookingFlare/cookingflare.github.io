Preserving the Flavors: Recipes for Canning Herbs


Canning herbs is a wonderful way to preserve their flavors and extend their shelf life. Whether you have a bountiful herb garden or simply want to save some fresh herbs for later use, canning is a fantastic method that allows you to enjoy the vibrant flavors of herbs all year round. In this blog post, we will explore some easy and delicious recipes for canning herbs.

Herb-Infused Oils

One of the simplest ways to preserve herbs is by infusing them into oils. Herb-infused oils not only add a burst of flavor to your dishes but also make beautiful and thoughtful gifts. Here’s how you can make your own herb-infused oils:

  1. Gather your fresh herbs of choice, such as basil, rosemary, thyme, or dill. Rinse them thoroughly and pat them dry.

  2. Sterilize your jars by washing them in hot, soapy water and then boiling them for 10 minutes. Let them air dry completely.

  3. Pack the jars tightly with your herbs, leaving about an inch of headspace at the top.

  4. Pour a high-quality oil, like olive or grapeseed oil, over the herbs, making sure they are completely submerged.

  5. Seal the jars tightly and store them in a cool, dark place for about two weeks to allow the flavors to infuse.

  6. After two weeks, strain the oil through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the herbs. Transfer the flavored oil into clean, sterilized bottles or jars.

Use your herb-infused oils in dressings, marinades, or simply drizzle them over roasted vegetables for a burst of flavor.

Herb Butters

Another delightful way to preserve herbs is by making herb-infused butter. Herb butters are incredibly versatile and can be used to enhance the flavors of savory dishes or spread onto freshly baked bread. Here’s a simple recipe for herb butter:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, chives, or tarragon)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the softened butter and finely chopped herbs. Mix well until the herbs are evenly distributed throughout the butter.

  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and mix again to incorporate the seasoning.

  3. Transfer the herb butter onto a sheet of plastic wrap or parchment paper. Roll it into a log shape, twisting the ends to secure it.

  4. Place the herb butter log in the refrigerator for at least two hours, or until firm.

  5. Once firm, slice the herb butter into rounds and use it to top grilled meats, steamed vegetables, or melt it over a hot baked potato.

Herb Salt

If you’re looking for a simple yet flavorful way to preserve herbs, herb salt is the answer. Herb salt can be used as a seasoning in various dishes and adds a delightful aroma and taste. Here’s how you can make your own herb salt:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coarse sea salt
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh herbs (such as thyme, sage, or oregano)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the coarse sea salt and finely chopped herbs. Mix well to evenly distribute the herbs throughout the salt.

  2. Spread the mixture onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and let it air dry for a few days, stirring occasionally to break up any clumps.

  3. Once the herb salt is completely dry, transfer it to an airtight container.

Sprinkle your homemade herb salt on roasted vegetables, grilled meats, or use it to season your favorite dishes for an extra burst of flavor.

Conclusion

Canning herbs is a fantastic way to preserve their flavors and enjoy them throughout the year. Whether you choose to infuse them into oils, blend them into butter, or create herb salts, these preserved herbs will add a touch of freshness and vibrancy to your culinary creations. So, don’t let your herb garden go to waste! Get started with these simple and delicious recipes for canning herbs and savor the flavors all year long.
