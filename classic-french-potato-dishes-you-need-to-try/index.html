Classic French Potato Dishes You Need to Try


French cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, elegant presentation, and exquisite eateries. When it comes to potato dishes, the French have particularly mastered the art of elevating this humble ingredient into a culinary experience like no other. From comforting casseroles to decadent sauces, potatoes in French cuisine are not only delicious but also versatile. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of classic French potato dishes that you absolutely need to try.

  1. Ployes (Potato Pancakes) Ployes is a traditional flatbread originating from the Brittany region of France. The recipe consists of a pancake-like preparation made from a mixture of grated potatoes, flour, and seasonings. These crispy and earthy-fresh disks are a staple in many Breton households. To prepare Ployes, start by grating 3 large potatoes and squeezing out excess liquid. Combine the grated potatoes with 1 cup of all-purpose flour, a pinch of salt, and 1/2 cup of lukewarm water. Knead the mixture for a few minutes until a smooth dough forms. Divide the dough into 6 equal parts and shape each into a round disk. Heat a non-stick skillet or a cast-iron pan with a small amount of oil until it's hot. Place a ploye disk in the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes on either side or until golden brown.
  2. Gratin Potatoes (Gratin Dauphinois) Gratin Potatoes, also known as Gratin Dauphinois, is a classic French dish that consists of thinly sliced potatoes layered in a baking dish and topped with cream, cheese, and breadcrumbs. This creamy, cheesy, and comforting gratin is a staple in many French households, especially during the winter months. To prepare Gratin Potatoes, start by peeling 4 large potatoes and slicing them thinly. Butter a 9x13 inch baking dish and sprinkle the bottom with a pinch of salt. Begin layering the sliced potatoes, starting and ending with a layer of potatoes. In between each layer of potatoes, spread a mixture of 1 cup heavy cream, 2 tablespoons grated Gruyère cheese, and 1 tablespoon unsalted butter. Once you have used up all the potatoes and the cream mixture, top the gratin with breadcrumbs and grated cheese.
  3. Pommes Anna (Potato and Mushroom Tart) Pommes Anna, meaning 'Anna's apples,' is a traditional French potato and mushroom dish made in a single layer in a large skillet. This delicate and flavorful tart is a showpiece for any dinner party. To prepare Pommes Anna, peel and thinly slice 3 large potatoes, then set aside. In a skillet, sauté 1 cup chopped mushrooms in butter until softened. In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Arrange a layer of potatoes in the skillet, overlapping them slightly. Repeat this process until you have used up all the potatoes and the skillet is filled to the top. Top the potatoes with the sautéed mushrooms and a layer of grated Gruyère cheese. Fold the potatoes up over the filling and cook for a few more minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Ratatouille (Vegetable and Potato Stew) Ratatouille is a hearty vegetable stew originating from the south of France. Though often associated with vegetables, ratatouille can be made with a combination of vegetables and potatoes, creating a truly satisfying meal. To prepare Ratatouille, peel and slice 2 large potatoes and 1 large onion. Chop 2 cups of tomatoes and 2 cups of bell peppers into small pieces, as well. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet and sauté the sliced onion until softened. Next, add the sliced potatoes, chopped tomatoes, and bell peppers, and season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar. Cook for approximately 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes and vegetables are tender and lightly browned.
  5. Côtes de Potatoes (Twice-Baked Potatoes Côtes) Côtes de Potatoes are miniature baked potatoes made by par-cooking the potatoes until softened before finishing them off in the oven. This method makes them easy to peel and results in a smooth, creamy interior. To prepare Côtes de Potatoes, start by baking 2-3 large potatoes at 375°F for 30-40 minutes, or until slightly softened. Remove the potatoes from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Skewer the warm potatoes and peel them from the skin end using a small tool, starting from the bottom. Brush the potatoes with butter, season with salt and pepper, and sprinkle with grated cheese. Return the potatoes to the oven for an additional 5-7 minutes or until the cheese is melted and golden brown.
  6. Bourguignon Potatoes (French Potatoes Cooked in a Red Wine Sauce) Bourguignon Potatoes is a hearty, comforting dish from the Burgundy region that consists of diced potatoes cooked in a rich red wine sauce. This classic winter dish is perfect for a cold evening in, especially if paired with braised meats. To prepare Bourguignon Potatoes, start by cooking 2 large potatoes in boiling water until slightly tender. Drain the potatoes and dice them into small cubes. In a large saucepan, cook 1 pound of ground beef until browned. Next, add 1 large onion, chopped into small pieces, and cook until softened. Stir in 1 cup of red wine and 1 cup of beef broth, and season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Add the diced potatoes to the saucepan, cover it with a lid, and continue cooking on low heat for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and the liquid has almost completely evaporated. Tips and Tricks

Use seasonal ingredients whenever possible for the best flavors and textures. To avoid sticky potatoes, boil them for 10-15 minutes before adding potatoes to the oil or butter for gratins. Ployes and Côtes de Potatoes keep well at room temperature for a short period, while the other dishes are best served immediately after cooking.

Conclusion French potato dishes offer a wide variety of cooking techniques, flavor combinations, and the undeniable charm of French cuisine. Whether you are in a solo cooking adventure or entertaining guests, mastering these classic potato dishes will elevate your table and leave your taste buds yearning for more. So embark on this culinary journey, practice, experiment, and delight in the rich landscape of French potato recipes!
