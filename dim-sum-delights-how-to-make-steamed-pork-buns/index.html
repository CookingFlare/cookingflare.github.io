Dim Sum Delights: How to Make Steamed Pork Buns

3 min · 574 words

If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you’ve probably tried steamed pork buns at your favorite dim sum restaurant. These fluffy, savory buns are a staple of dim sum menus and are loved by people all over the world. Today, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of making these delicious steamed pork buns right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the filling:

  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Mix until a dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

  4. While the dough is rising, prepare the filling. In a mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green onions. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  5. After the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Transfer it back to the floured surface and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a small ball.

  6. Take one dough ball and flatten it with your palm. Use a rolling pin to roll it into a circle about 4 inches in diameter. Place a spoonful of the pork filling in the center of the dough circle.

  7. Gather the edges of the dough circle and pinch them together to seal the bun. Repeat this process for the remaining dough balls and filling.

  8. Place the filled buns on individual squares of parchment paper and arrange them in a steamer basket, leaving some space between each bun. Cover the steamer basket with a lid.

  9. Fill a wok or a large pot with about 2 inches of water and bring it to a boil. Once the water is boiling, place the steamer basket on top and steam the buns for about 15 minutes, or until the dough is cooked through.

  10. Carefully remove the steamer basket from the pot and let the buns cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy these delightful steamed pork buns with soy sauce or your favorite dipping sauce.

Conclusion

Making steamed pork buns at home might seem like a daunting task, but with this easy-to-follow recipe, you’ll be able to recreate the flavors of your favorite dim sum restaurant in no time. The fluffy dough filled with savory pork filling is a match made in heaven. So why not gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and embark on a culinary adventure right in your own kitchen? Dim sum delights await!
