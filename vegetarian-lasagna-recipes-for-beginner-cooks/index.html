Vegetarian Lasagna Recipes for Beginner Cooks


Welcome to our blog post dedicated to delicious and easy-to-make vegetarian lasagna recipes! Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this collection of recipes will help you create mouthwatering lasagnas that are perfect for vegetarians and meat-lovers alike. So grab your apron, preheat your oven, and let’s get cooking!

Classic Spinach and Ricotta Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 ½ cups of ricotta cheese
  • 1 ½ cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 cups of marinara sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions and set them aside.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped spinach, ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, beaten egg, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly combined.
  3. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom. Place three lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  4. Spread half of the spinach and ricotta mixture over the noodles. Repeat the layering process with more marinara sauce, noodles, and the remaining spinach and ricotta mixture.
  5. Finish by topping the lasagna with marinara sauce and a generous sprinkle of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  7. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before serving. Slice and enjoy!

Eggplant and Zucchini Lasagna Roll-Ups

Ingredients:

  • 8 lasagna noodles
  • 1 medium eggplant, thinly sliced
  • 2 small zucchinis, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of marinara sauce
  • 1 ½ cups of ricotta cheese
  • 1 ½ cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ¼ cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions and set them aside.
  2. In a large frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced eggplant and zucchini, and sauté until they become tender. Season with salt, pepper, and minced garlic.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  4. Lay out the lasagna noodles on a flat surface. Spread a spoonful of the cheese mixture onto each noodle, followed by a layer of sautéed eggplant and zucchini.
  5. Roll up each lasagna noodle tightly and place them seam-side down in a baking dish. Pour the marinara sauce over the roll-ups, covering them evenly.
  6. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top of the roll-ups.
  7. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes or until the cheese is melted and golden.
  8. Allow the lasagna roll-ups to cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve with a side of salad for a complete meal.

Butternut Squash and Sage Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 3 cups of butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 ½ cups of ricotta cheese
  • 1 ½ cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • ½ cup of fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • 2 cups of béchamel sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions and set them aside.
  2. In a large frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced butternut squash and minced garlic, and sauté until the squash becomes tender. Season with salt, pepper, and chopped sage leaves.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  4. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of béchamel sauce on the bottom. Place three lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  5. Spread half of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles, followed by a layer of sautéed butternut squash and sage.
  6. Repeat the layering process with more béchamel sauce, noodles, the remaining ricotta cheese mixture, and the remaining sautéed butternut squash and sage.
  7. Finish by topping the lasagna with béchamel sauce and a sprinkle of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  9. Allow the lasagna to cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve with a side of roasted vegetables for a delicious and comforting meal.

We hope you enjoyed these vegetarian lasagna recipes for beginner cooks. Whether you choose the classic spinach and ricotta, the eggplant and zucchini roll-ups, or the butternut squash and sage variation, these recipes are sure to impress your family and friends. So go ahead, get creative in the kitchen, and enjoy the wonderful flavors of these vegetarian lasagnas!
