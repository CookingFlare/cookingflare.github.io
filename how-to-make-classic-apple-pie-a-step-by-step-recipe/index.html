How to Make Classic Apple Pie: A Step-by-Step Recipe


Apple pie is a timeless dessert that evokes feelings of nostalgia and warmth. Whether it’s a holiday gathering or a cozy Sunday afternoon, nothing quite compares to the delightful aroma of a homemade apple pie baking in the oven. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of crafting a classic apple pie from scratch, ensuring that you achieve that perfect balance of sweet and tart flavors with a flaky, golden crust.

Ingredients

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

For the Apple Filling:

  • 6 to 8 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith and Honeycrisp)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon butter (to dot the filling)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon milk (for egg wash)

Equipment Needed

  • Mixing bowls
  • Rolling pin
  • Pie dish (9-inch)
  • Knife and cutting board
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Whisk or fork (for mixing the egg wash)
  • Parchment paper (optional)

Step 1: Prepare the Pie Crust

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and granulated sugar. Whisk these dry ingredients together until they are well blended.

  2. Cut in the Butter
    Add the chilled, diced butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Itâ€™s important to keep the butter cold to achieve a flaky crust.

  3. Add Ice Water
    Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing with a fork until the dough begins to come together. You may not need all the water, so add just enough to form a cohesive dough.

  4. Form the Dough
    Divide the dough into two equal portions. Shape each portion into a disc and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least one hour (or overnight) to allow the dough to chill and the gluten to relax.

Step 2: Prepare the Apple Filling

  1. Peel and Slice the Apples
    While the dough is chilling, peel, core, and slice the apples. Aim for uniform slices to ensure even cooking. You can use a combination of tart and sweet apples to achieve a balanced flavor.

  2. Mix the Filling Ingredients
    In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples with granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss the ingredients together until the apples are evenly coated. Let the mixture sit for about 15-20 minutes to release some juices.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Prepare the Surface
    On a lightly floured surface, remove one disc of dough from the refrigerator. Let it sit for a few minutes until it softens slightly.

  2. Roll Out the Dough
    Using a rolling pin, roll out the dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Make sure to keep the thickness even. If the dough sticks to the surface, sprinkle a little more flour.

  3. Transfer to Pie Dish
    Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to a 9-inch pie dish. Gently press the dough into the bottom and sides of the dish. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges, leaving about an inch to fold over later.

Step 4: Assemble the Pie

  1. Add the Apple Filling
    Pour the apple filling into the prepared pie crust, making sure to distribute the apples evenly. Dot the filling with small pieces of butter to enhance the flavor.

  2. Roll Out the Top Crust
    Take the second disc of dough from the refrigerator and roll it out in the same manner as the bottom crust. Once rolled out, you can either place it directly over the filling or cut it into strips for a lattice crust.

  3. Create a Lattice Top (Optional)
    If you choose to make a lattice top, cut the rolled-out dough into strips about 1 inch wide. Lay half of the strips horizontally across the filling, then weave the remaining strips vertically, alternating over and under.

  4. Seal the Edges
    Fold the overhanging edge of the bottom crust over the top crust (or the edges of the lattice) and crimp to seal. This step is crucial for keeping the filling from leaking out during baking.

Step 5: Prepare for Baking

  1. Make the Egg Wash
    In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and milk to create an egg wash. This will give your pie a beautiful, golden-brown finish.

  2. Brush the Pie
    Gently brush the egg wash over the top crust of the pie. This step not only adds color but also helps to create a shiny finish.

  3. Cut Slits for Venting
    If you opted for a solid top crust, make a few slits with a sharp knife to allow steam to escape during baking. This prevents the crust from becoming soggy.

Step 6: Bake the Pie

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 425Â°F (220Â°C). This high temperature helps the crust to crisp up beautifully.

  2. Bake the Pie
    Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350Â°F (175Â°C) and continue baking for 35 to 45 minutes. The pie is done when the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  3. Cool the Pie
    Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours. Cooling helps the filling to set, making it easier to slice.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Slice the Pie
    After cooling, slice the pie into wedges using a sharp knife. Serve it plain or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra treat.

  2. Store Leftovers
    If you have leftovers, cover the pie with plastic wrap or aluminum foil and store it at room temperature for up to two days. For longer storage, refrigerate the pie for up to a week.

Tips for the Perfect Apple Pie

  • Choose the Right Apples: Mixing sweet and tart apples enhances the flavor. Consider using a combination of Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, and Fuji apples.

  • Donâ€™t Overwork the Dough: Overworking the dough can lead to a tough crust. Handle it gently and aim for a flaky texture.

  • Let the Filling Rest: Allowing the apple filling to sit for a bit helps release some juices, reducing the risk of a soggy bottom crust.

  • Experiment with Spices: Feel free to customize the spices in your filling. A pinch of ginger or allspice can add a unique twist.

  • Serve Warm: Apple pie is best enjoyed warm, making the flavors more vibrant.

Conclusion

Making a classic apple pie is a rewarding experience that brings the warmth of home into your kitchen. With its flaky crust and delicious apple filling, this dessert is sure to impress at any gathering or simply as a treat for yourself. Follow these step-by-step instructions, and you’ll have a homemade apple pie that captures the essence of fall and tradition in every bite. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and start baking your way to a slice of heaven!
