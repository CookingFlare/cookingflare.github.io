Sous Vide Coconut Rice with Mango Salsa


Sous vide cooking has revolutionized how we prepare meals, offering precise temperature control and exceptional consistency. Pairing this technique with vibrant tropical flavors, like coconut and mango, creates an unforgettable dish that captures the essence of paradise. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through crafting perfectly cooked coconut rice in a sous vide bag, paired with a refreshing mango salsa. The result is a meal that’s equal parts fragrant, colorful, and delicious—ideal for dinner parties, meal prep, or even a laid-back weeknight dinner.

Why Sous Vide for Coconut Rice?

Traditional methods of cooking rice often lead to uneven results—sometimes overcooked, other times undercooked, depending on the heat source and cookware you use. By switching to sous vide, you sidestep many of these challenges. Sous vide cooking offers consistent heat distribution, ensuring that every grain of rice absorbs the perfect amount of liquid, yielding fluffy, perfectly textured coconut rice every time. Additionally, you enhance the flavor by sealing ingredients into a vacuum bag, locking in the subtle but essential undertones of coconut milk.

Combining the sous vide method with bright, fresh mango salsa transforms this humble rice dish into a tropical celebration. The juxtaposition of creamy, coconut-infused rice and tangy salsa makes it a wonderfully satisfying culinary experience. Ready to learn how it’s done?

Ingredients For Sous Vide Coconut Rice with Mango Salsa

Coconut Rice Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice (or basmati rice for a nuttier flavor)
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk (unsweetened)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil or butter (optional, for added richness)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to highlight the coconut flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt

Mango Salsa Ingredients:

  • 1 large ripe mango, peeled and diced into small cubes
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely diced
  • 1 small jalapeño pepper, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 handful fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • Pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper

Equipment Needed:

  • Sous vide immersion circulator
  • Large stockpot or container for water bath
  • Vacuum-seal bags or high-quality resealable freezer bags
  • Knife and cutting board
  • Mixing bowls and utensils

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Setting Up Your Sous Vide

Begin by preheating your sous vide water bath to 190°F (88°C)—the ideal temperature for cooking rice. It’s a slightly higher temperature compared to sous vide proteins, but it ensures the grains soften fully while absorbing the creamy coconut liquid.

Step 2: Preparing the Coconut Rice Mixture

In a large mixing bowl, combine the jasmine rice, coconut milk, water, coconut oil (if using), sugar, and sea salt. Stir together until evenly mixed. Taste the mixture briefly; it should already hint at the tropical richness you’re aiming for.

Transfer the rice mixture to a vacuum-seal bag or resealable freezer bag. If using a resealable bag, make sure to press out as much air as possible before sealing. For vacuum-sealed bags, use your machine to ensure a tight seal.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking the Rice

Once the sous vide reaches the preheated temperature, lower the bagged rice mixture into the water bath. Ensure the bag is fully submerged and weighted down if necessary. Cook for 45–60 minutes, ensuring the water circulates evenly around the bag for consistent results.

As the rice cooks, the grains will absorb the coconut milk, transforming into perfectly creamy rice with a rich depth of flavor. You don’t need to stir or monitor the process—that’s one of the magical benefits of sous vide cooking.

Step 4: Preparing Mango Salsa

While the rice is cooking, turn your attention to the mango salsa. In a medium mixing bowl, combine the diced mango, red bell pepper, red onion, and jalapeño. Add the fresh cilantro, lime juice, and olive oil. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.

Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to your preference. If you enjoy extra heat, consider adding more jalapeño or a dash of chili flakes.

Pro tip: For the best flavor, let the salsa sit for at least 10–15 minutes before serving. The rest period allows the lime juice and spices to meld with the other ingredients.

Step 5: Finishing the Coconut Rice

After the cooking time is up, remove the sous vide bag from the water bath and carefully open it. Fluff the rice with a fork until it’s airy and fragrant. The rice should have absorbed the liquid fully, yielding tender, moist grains infused with coconut flavor. If there’s any excess liquid remaining, gently drain it off.

Assembly and Serving

To serve, spoon the sous vide coconut rice onto individual plates or a large serving dish. Top generously with the mango salsa for a pop of color and a burst of bright, tangy flavor. Garnish with additional fresh cilantro if desired.

This dish pairs beautifully with grilled seafood, like shrimp or tilapia, for a complete island-inspired meal. For a vegetarian option, consider serving it alongside grilled tofu or roasted seasonal vegetables.

Storage and Meal Prep Tips

This recipe is perfect for meal prep, as both components store well in the refrigerator. Allow the rice to cool before placing it in an airtight container; it can be refrigerated for up to 5 days. The mango salsa is best eaten within 2–3 days before the fresh ingredients lose their vibrancy.

If you want to make the dish ahead for a party, prepare the rice sous vide and refrigerate it. Reheat the rice gently by steaming or microwaving, then top with freshly made salsa for the best presentation and flavor.

A Feast for the Senses

Sous vide coconut rice with mango salsa is everything you’d want in a tropical-inspired dish—fluffy, fragrant rice; sweet and tangy salsa; a balance of creamy richness and zesty brightness. This recipe showcases how sous vide cooking can elevate everyday ingredients into something extraordinary while allowing you to lock in flavors and achieve consistent results.

Whether you’re preparing this for yourself or entertaining guests, this festive dish will leave everyone asking for seconds. Try the recipe today and transport your taste buds to paradise!
