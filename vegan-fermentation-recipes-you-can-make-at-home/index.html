Vegan Fermentation Recipes You Can Make at Home


Fermentation has been a cornerstone of human food preservation and flavor development for centuries. While traditionally associated with dairy, meat, and alcoholic beverages, fermentation has also become a popular practice in plant-based cuisine. Whether you are new to vegan cooking or an experienced plant-based chef, fermenting your own foods at home is a fun, creative, and highly rewarding way to enhance your meals.

In this guide, we will explore several easy and delicious vegan fermentation recipes you can make at home. From tangy kimchi to creamy cashew cheese, these recipes will introduce you to the world of fermented plant-based foods, enriching your diet with probiotics, enzymes, and distinctive flavors.

What is Fermentation?

Fermentation is a natural metabolic process where microorganisms like bacteria, yeast, or molds convert carbohydrates—such as sugars and starches—into alcohol, gases, or acids. This process preserves food, develops new flavors, and increases its nutritional value. In vegan cooking, fermentation can transform a variety of plant-based ingredients into delicious and healthful foods that are rich in beneficial probiotics, which promote gut health.

Why Should You Try Vegan Fermentation at Home?

Fermenting foods at home has several advantages:

  1. Health Benefits: Fermented foods are packed with probiotics, which support digestion and help maintain a healthy gut microbiome.
  2. Control Over Ingredients: Making your own fermented foods allows you to control the ingredients, making it easy to avoid preservatives, artificial flavors, and excess sugar or salt.
  3. Customization: You can experiment with different flavors, spices, and herbs to make the perfect batch of fermented foods that suit your taste.
  4. Cost-Effective: While store-bought fermented foods can be pricey, making your own at home is a cost-effective way to enjoy probiotic-rich meals.

Now that you understand the benefits, let’s dive into some delicious and simple vegan fermentation recipes you can try at home.

1. Vegan Kimchi

Kimchi, a staple in Korean cuisine, is a spicy and tangy fermented vegetable dish typically made with napa cabbage and radishes. It’s packed with flavor and probiotics, making it a great addition to many meals.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium napa cabbage, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, toss the chopped cabbage with sea salt. Massage the salt into the cabbage until it begins to soften, and let it sit for about 1-2 hours to draw out moisture.
  2. Rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold water and drain.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the grated ginger, minced garlic, gochugaru, sugar, soy sauce, water, and rice vinegar to create the kimchi paste.
  4. Add the cabbage to the paste and mix well, making sure the cabbage is fully coated. If you like, you can also add julienned carrots for extra texture.
  5. Pack the kimchi into a clean glass jar, pressing it down to remove any air pockets.
  6. Seal the jar and leave it at room temperature for 3-5 days to ferment, depending on how tangy you prefer it.
  7. Once the kimchi is fermented to your liking, store it in the fridge to slow down the fermentation process.

Enjoy your homemade vegan kimchi as a side dish, in soups, or even on top of rice and noodles!

2. Fermented Cashew Cheese

Cashew cheese is a creamy, dairy-free alternative to traditional cheese. By fermenting cashews, you can create a tangy, spreadable cheese that adds a savory depth to many dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw cashews, soaked in water for 6-8 hours
  • 1/4 cup water (or more for desired consistency)
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon probiotic powder (optional, but recommended for fermentation)

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked cashews. Place them in a high-speed blender or food processor.
  2. Add water, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, salt, and probiotic powder (if using).
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy, adding more water as needed to achieve your desired consistency.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a clean jar or airtight container, cover loosely with a lid or cheesecloth to allow airflow, and let it sit at room temperature for 12-24 hours to ferment.
  5. Once the cheese has fermented to your liking, store it in the refrigerator. It will thicken up as it chills.

This vegan cashew cheese is perfect for spreading on crackers, using as a dip, or adding to your favorite pasta dishes.

3. Sauerkraut

Sauerkraut is a classic fermented food made from cabbage and salt. It’s incredibly simple to make and full of gut-friendly probiotics. It’s a tangy, crunchy topping that pairs wonderfully with a variety of dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cabbage, shredded
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon caraway seeds (for flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Shred the cabbage finely using a knife or food processor.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cabbage and sea salt. Massage the salt into the cabbage for about 5-10 minutes to release its moisture.
  3. If desired, add caraway seeds to the cabbage mixture for additional flavor.
  4. Pack the cabbage tightly into a clean mason jar, pressing down firmly to eliminate any air pockets.
  5. Ensure the cabbage is submerged in its own liquid. If necessary, add a little water to cover the cabbage completely.
  6. Cover the jar with a clean cloth or loose lid to allow gases to escape.
  7. Leave the jar at room temperature for 5-7 days, checking daily to make sure the cabbage remains submerged in its liquid. Taste it every day, and once it has fermented to your liking, seal the jar and store it in the refrigerator.

Sauerkraut is delicious on sandwiches, in salads, or as a side dish to your favorite meal.

4. Vegan Coconut Yogurt

Coconut yogurt is a creamy, dairy-free alternative to traditional yogurt. It’s rich in probiotics and can be used in smoothies, parfaits, or enjoyed with fruit and granola.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or sweetener of your choice)
  • 1 probiotic capsule (or 1 tablespoon dairy-free yogurt starter culture)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the coconut milk and maple syrup over low heat until warm, but not boiling.
  2. Open the probiotic capsule and sprinkle the powder into the warm coconut milk. Stir gently to combine.
  3. Pour the mixture into a clean glass jar or container and cover with a lid or cheesecloth.
  4. Leave the jar in a warm area, like an oven with the light on or a dehydrator, for 12-24 hours. The longer it ferments, the tangier it will become.
  5. Once the yogurt has thickened and developed a tangy flavor, refrigerate it for at least 4 hours before serving.

You can customize your coconut yogurt by adding fruits, nuts, or seeds after fermentation, creating a delicious and healthy breakfast or snack.

5. Pickled Vegetables

Pickled vegetables are a great way to preserve the flavors of your favorite veggies while introducing them to the health benefits of fermentation. You can pickle almost any vegetable, from cucumbers to carrots, and enjoy them as a tangy snack or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, cucumbers, cauliflower, etc.)
  • 1 tablespoon sea salt
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns (optional)
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed

Instructions:

  1. Cut the vegetables into bite-sized pieces or slices.
  2. In a jar, layer the vegetables with mustard seeds, peppercorns, and smashed garlic.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the sea salt, apple cider vinegar, and water. Stir until the salt dissolves.
  4. Pour the brine over the vegetables, making sure they are fully submerged.
  5. Seal the jar and leave it at room temperature for 2-3 days to ferment. The pickles will develop their flavor during this time.
  6. Once fermented, transfer the pickles to the refrigerator.

These pickled vegetables are perfect as a snack or an accompaniment to sandwiches, burgers, or salads.

Final Thoughts

Vegan fermentation is a fantastic way to enhance your plant-based meals, adding flavor, texture, and beneficial probiotics. Whether you’re making your own vegan kimchi, cashew cheese, or coconut yogurt, the process of fermenting foods at home is not only fun but also provides you with nutrient-dense, gut-friendly foods. Experiment with different spices, vegetables, and grains to create your own unique fermented dishes, and enjoy the delicious benefits of home fermentation!

So, roll up your sleeves, get creative, and start fermenting your way to better health and tastier meals!
