Easy Dessert Recipes for Beginners: Start Baking Today


Baking is an enjoyable and rewarding activity that can transform simple ingredients into delicious desserts. Whether you’re new to the kitchen or looking to explore the world of sweet treats, this guide offers a collection of easy dessert recipes that are perfect for beginners. Baking can be intimidating, but with the right recipes and a little patience, you’ll be creating delightful desserts in no time.

In this post, we’ll walk you through simple yet mouth-watering dessert ideas that anyone can make, regardless of experience. From cookies to cakes and beyond, these recipes use everyday ingredients and require minimal equipment. Let’s dive in and start baking today!

1. Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are a timeless favorite, and they’re one of the easiest desserts to make. These soft, chewy treats are perfect for beginners because they require minimal ingredients and steps. Once you get the hang of them, you’ll be able to bake a batch of cookies in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup (230g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup (150g) brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 1/4 cups (280g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups (340g) semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or lightly grease it.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Add the vanilla extract and eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  6. Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop rounded balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Leave enough space between each cookie for spreading.
  7. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until the edges are golden brown but the centers are still soft.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Simple Vanilla Cupcakes

Cupcakes are a great beginner-friendly dessert, and their versatility makes them a favorite for all occasions. This vanilla cupcake recipe yields fluffy, moist cupcakes that pair well with any frosting or topping of your choice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups (190g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) whole milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-cup muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter, eggs, and vanilla extract until light and fluffy.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with the milk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, frost with your favorite frosting, such as buttercream or cream cheese frosting, and decorate as desired.

3. No-Bake Cheesecake

If you’re a beginner baker, the thought of making a cheesecake may seem daunting. But with this easy no-bake cheesecake recipe, you can enjoy a creamy, indulgent dessert without the need for a complicated process. The best part? It doesn’t require turning on your oven.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups (150g) graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted

For the filling:

  • 16 oz (450g) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated.
  2. Press the mixture into the bottom of a springform pan or pie dish to form a crust. Use the back of a spoon to pack it tightly.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy.
  4. In another bowl, whip the heavy cream and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  5. Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture until fully combined.
  6. Pour the filling onto the prepared crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  7. Refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow it to set.
  8. Before serving, top the cheesecake with fresh fruit, chocolate ganache, or your favorite toppings.

4. Apple Crisp

Apple crisp is a comforting dessert that combines the sweet-tart flavor of apples with a buttery, crumbly topping. It’s simple to make and perfect for beginners. This recipe requires just a few ingredients, making it ideal for a quick and satisfying treat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups (600g) apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup (45g) old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (50g) brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup (60g) unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8-inch baking dish or similar-sized pan.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice, granulated sugar, and ground cinnamon. Spread the apple mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, brown sugar, and salt. Cut in the butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are tender.
  6. Allow the apple crisp to cool slightly before serving. It’s delicious on its own or served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

5. Banana Bread

Banana bread is a beginner-friendly recipe that results in a moist, flavorful loaf. It’s a great way to use up overripe bananas, and it requires minimal ingredients and equipment. Plus, it’s incredibly versatile—add chocolate chips, nuts, or dried fruit for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup (75g) melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups (190g) all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 4x8-inch loaf pan or line it with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth.
  3. Stir in the melted butter, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Add the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.
  5. Gradually fold in the flour until just incorporated.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan.
  7. Bake for 60-65 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the bread comes out clean.
  8. Let the banana bread cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Final Thoughts

Baking doesn’t have to be complicated or intimidating. With these simple and easy dessert recipes, you can start baking today and create delicious treats with minimal effort. Whether you’re making chocolate chip cookies, vanilla cupcakes, or a no-bake cheesecake, you’ll be amazed at how quickly you can master the art of baking.

These beginner-friendly recipes are a perfect starting point for anyone interested in baking. Once you feel more confident, feel free to experiment with new flavors, ingredients, and techniques. Baking is all about creativity, so have fun and enjoy the process!

Start with these recipes, and soon enough, you’ll be creating impressive desserts that will delight your friends and family. Happy baking!
