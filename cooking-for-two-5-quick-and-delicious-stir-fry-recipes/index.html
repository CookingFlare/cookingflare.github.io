Cooking for Two: 5 Quick and Delicious Stir-Fry Recipes


Cooking for two can be an enjoyable and intimate experience, especially when you have quick and flavorful recipes on hand. Stir-fry is a perfect choice for this scenario—it’s fast, versatile, and bursting with vibrant flavors. Whether you’re in the mood for something light and healthy or something more indulgent, these five stir-fry recipes are sure to impress. From chicken to vegetables to shrimp, there’s a stir-fry recipe for every craving. Let’s dive into the world of stir-fry cooking and explore some easy yet delicious dishes that you can whip up in no time.

1. Classic Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry

This classic chicken and vegetable stir-fry is the ultimate comfort food that’s healthy and full of flavor. Packed with lean protein, colorful veggies, and a savory sauce, this dish comes together in under 30 minutes and is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • Cooked rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken and Vegetables: Begin by slicing the chicken into thin strips. Prepare your vegetables by slicing the bell peppers, zucchini, and carrots. Mince the garlic and grate the ginger to have them ready for use.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the chicken and stir-fry for about 4-5 minutes until it’s fully cooked and golden brown. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same skillet, add the garlic and ginger, cooking for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the bell peppers, zucchini, and carrots to the skillet. Stir-fry for about 4-5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Combine and Make the Sauce: While the vegetables are cooking, mix the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, and cornstarch in a small bowl. Once the vegetables are ready, return the chicken to the skillet and pour the sauce mixture over everything. Stir well to coat the chicken and vegetables.

  5. Serve: Allow everything to cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy. Serve your chicken and vegetable stir-fry over a bed of steamed rice for a complete meal.

Tips:

  • Feel free to swap out the vegetables depending on what you have on hand. Broccoli, snow peas, and mushrooms all work wonderfully in this dish.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, add a dash of red pepper flakes or a sliced chili pepper to the stir-fry.

2. Shrimp and Snow Pea Stir-Fry

If you’re craving seafood, this shrimp and snow pea stir-fry will hit the spot. With a delightful balance of sweetness from the shrimp and crunch from the snow peas, this stir-fry is light yet satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas, trimmed
  • 1 small carrot, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili garlic sauce (optional for heat)
  • Cooked jasmine rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Shrimp and Vegetables: Peel and devein the shrimp, then pat them dry with a paper towel. Slice the onion and carrot and trim the snow peas.

  2. Cook the Shrimp: Heat the sesame oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set them aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same pan, add the onion and cook for about 2 minutes until softened. Add the carrots and snow peas, and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until they are tender but still crisp.

  4. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and chili garlic sauce (if using). Pour the sauce into the pan with the vegetables and bring to a simmer. Stir everything well to coat the vegetables with the sauce.

  5. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked shrimp back into the pan and toss to combine. Cook for an additional minute to heat through. Serve the shrimp and snow pea stir-fry over jasmine rice.

Tips:

  • For extra flavor, sprinkle a little toasted sesame seeds on top before serving.
  • If you can’t find snow peas, snap peas are a great alternative.

3. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Beef and broccoli is a classic stir-fry that never goes out of style. This recipe features tender strips of beef and crisp broccoli, all coated in a savory soy-based sauce that brings it all together.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Cooked brown rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef and Vegetables: Slice the flank steak thinly against the grain for maximum tenderness. Prepare the broccoli florets by cutting them into bite-sized pieces. Slice the onion and mince the garlic.

  2. Cook the Beef: Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the beef and stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until browned and cooked through. Remove the beef from the pan and set aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same skillet, add the onion and garlic. Cook for about 1 minute until fragrant, then add the broccoli. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes, or until the broccoli is tender yet still bright green.

  4. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and cornstarch until smooth.

  5. Combine and Serve: Return the beef to the skillet, pour the sauce over the beef and broccoli, and stir to coat everything evenly. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens and the beef and broccoli are well-coated. Serve over brown rice.

Tips:

  • To add some crunch, top with chopped peanuts or cashews before serving.
  • For a deeper flavor, marinate the beef in soy sauce, garlic, and ginger for 20 minutes before cooking.

4. Vegetarian Tofu Stir-Fry

This tofu stir-fry is packed with protein and vegetables, making it an excellent meatless option. The crispy tofu pairs perfectly with stir-fried vegetables and a flavorful sauce for a satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Cooked quinoa, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tofu and Vegetables: Drain the tofu and press it to remove excess moisture. Cut it into cubes. Slice the bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms, and mince the garlic.

  2. Crisp the Tofu: Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until the tofu is golden brown and crispy on all sides. Remove the tofu from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same skillet, add the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil.

  5. Combine and Serve: Add the crispy tofu back into the skillet and pour the sauce over the tofu and vegetables. Stir everything well to combine. Serve the tofu stir-fry over quinoa.

Tips:

  • If you prefer extra crispy tofu, toss the cubes in cornstarch before frying them.
  • For more depth of flavor, add a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds before serving.

5. Pork and Asparagus Stir-Fry

This pork and asparagus stir-fry is a simple yet elegant dish. The tender pork and fresh asparagus are enhanced by a sweet and savory sauce that makes each bite irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb pork tenderloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • Cooked rice noodles, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pork and Asparagus: Slice the pork tenderloin thinly against the grain. Trim and cut the asparagus into 2-inch pieces.

  2. Cook the Pork: Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the pork slices and stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes, or until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the pork from the pan and set it aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Asparagus: In the same skillet, add the garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the asparagus and stir-fry for 4-5 minutes, or until it’s tender but still vibrant green.

  4. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and cornstarch.

  5. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked pork back into the skillet with the asparagus and pour the sauce over the top. Stir well to coat everything in the sauce and cook for another 2 minutes until the sauce thickens. Serve over rice noodles.

Tips:

  • You can substitute pork with chicken or beef if preferred.
  • If you want a bit of heat, add some red pepper flakes to the sauce.

Stir-fries are an excellent choice for anyone cooking for two. They’re quick, customizable, and offer endless possibilities for combinations. Whether you’re in the mood for chicken, shrimp, tofu, or pork, these five recipes offer a variety of flavors and textures that will satisfy your cravings. Keep these recipes in your arsenal for busy weeknights, and you’ll always have a tasty meal in no time. Enjoy your cooking!
