Cinnamon Roll Cake: Indulge in a Breakfast Classic


When it comes to breakfast, few things can rival the comforting, nostalgic allure of cinnamon rolls. The warm, gooey swirls of cinnamon and sugar, paired with a drizzle of sweet icing, create a delightful treat that many of us cherish from our childhood. But what if you could take that delicious experience and transform it into a cake? Enter the Cinnamon Roll Cake—a delightful fusion that captures all the flavors of the classic breakfast pastry while offering the simplicity and ease of a cake.

The Origin of Cinnamon Rolls

Cinnamon rolls have a rich history that dates back to ancient times. Their origins can be traced to Swedish and Danish baking traditions, where they are known as “kanelbullar” in Sweden and “kanelsnegle” in Denmark. These sweet rolls gained popularity in the United States in the early 20th century, often served as a decadent breakfast or brunch item. Over the years, they have evolved into a beloved comfort food, and their versatility has inspired countless variations.

Why Choose Cinnamon Roll Cake?

The beauty of Cinnamon Roll Cake lies in its convenience and indulgence. Unlike traditional cinnamon rolls that require time-consuming preparation and meticulous rolling, this cake is simple to make and can feed a crowd. It combines all the familiar flavors in a fluffy cake that’s perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even dessert. Imagine waking up to the aroma of cinnamon wafting through your kitchen, and being able to slice into a warm, gooey cake in mere minutes.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this mouthwatering Cinnamon Roll Cake, gather the following ingredients:

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt

For the Cinnamon Swirl:

  • ½ cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Cream Cheese Glaze:

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare Your Oven and Pan

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or non-stick cooking spray to prevent the cake from sticking.

2. Make the Cake Batter

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This process typically takes about 2-3 minutes. Next, add the eggs one at a time, ensuring each is fully incorporated before adding the next. Stir in the vanilla extract.

In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with buttermilk. Begin and end with the flour mixture, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are perfectly fine.

3. Create the Cinnamon Swirl

In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar and ground cinnamon. Pour the melted butter into the mixture and stir until crumbly. This will be your delicious cinnamon swirl that adds that authentic cinnamon roll flavor to the cake.

4. Assemble the Cake

Spread half of the cake batter evenly into the prepared baking dish. Sprinkle the cinnamon swirl mixture over the batter, making sure to cover it evenly. Then, dollop the remaining cake batter over the cinnamon layer. Using a knife or a skewer, gently swirl the batter to create beautiful marbled patterns. Avoid over-mixing, as you want distinct swirls.

5. Bake the Cake

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Keep an eye on it as baking times can vary based on your oven.

6. Prepare the Cream Cheese Glaze

While the cake is baking, prepare the cream cheese glaze. In a medium bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract, mixing well until you achieve a pourable consistency. If the glaze is too thick, add a little more milk until you reach your desired thickness.

7. Cool and Glaze

Once the cake is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, drizzle the cream cheese glaze generously over the top of the cake.

Serving Suggestions

Your Cinnamon Roll Cake is now ready to be served! This cake is best enjoyed warm, but it can also be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to three days. For an extra indulgent experience, consider serving it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Pairing Ideas

To elevate your breakfast experience, consider pairing your Cinnamon Roll Cake with:

  • Freshly brewed coffee or espresso
  • A hot cup of tea
  • Fresh fruit, such as strawberries or bananas
  • A side of crispy bacon or sausage for a savory contrast

Variations to Try

While the classic Cinnamon Roll Cake is undeniably delicious, here are a few variations you can try to mix things up:

1. Nutty Delight

Incorporate chopped walnuts or pecans into the cinnamon swirl for a delightful crunch. This nutty addition complements the flavors of cinnamon and sugar perfectly.

2. Chocolate Chip

Add a handful of chocolate chips to the batter or sprinkle them alongside the cinnamon swirl for a chocolatey twist that will satisfy any sweet tooth.

3. Pumpkin Spice

For a seasonal treat, substitute 1 cup of buttermilk with 1 cup of pumpkin puree and add 1 teaspoon of pumpkin spice to the dry ingredients. This will give you a rich, autumnal flavor that’s perfect for fall.

4. Fruit-Infused

Add fresh berries or diced apples to the batter for a fruity spin on this classic. Blueberries, raspberries, or chopped apples will add moisture and a burst of flavor.

Storing Leftovers

If you find yourself with leftover Cinnamon Roll Cake, it can be easily stored for future enjoyment. Allow the cake to cool completely before slicing it into squares. Store the pieces in an airtight container at room temperature for about three days or refrigerate for up to a week. You can also freeze individual slices for up to three months. Just ensure they are wrapped tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil before placing them in a freezer-safe bag.

Final Thoughts

The Cinnamon Roll Cake is more than just a dessert; it’s a celebration of flavors and memories that can brighten any breakfast table. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, enjoying a cozy morning at home, or simply craving a sweet treat, this cake is sure to impress. So grab your ingredients, preheat that oven, and indulge in this delightful breakfast classic that combines the best of both worlds—the beloved cinnamon roll and the simplicity of a cake.

Embrace the warmth, comfort, and sweetness of Cinnamon Roll Cake and make it a staple in your breakfast repertoire. Your taste buds will thank you!
