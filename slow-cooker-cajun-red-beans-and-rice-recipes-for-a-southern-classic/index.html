Slow Cooker Cajun Red Beans and Rice Recipes for a Southern Classic


If you’re a fan of Cajun cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this Slow Cooker Cajun Red Beans and Rice recipe! This Southern classic is packed with flavor and will transport you straight to the heart of Louisiana. The best part? It’s incredibly easy to make in your slow cooker, allowing the flavors to develop and meld together over time.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dried red kidney beans
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by rinsing and sorting through the dried red kidney beans. Remove any debris or stones, then place the beans in a large bowl and cover them with water. Let them soak overnight, or for at least 8 hours. Drain and rinse the beans before using.

  2. In your slow cooker, combine the soaked and drained kidney beans, diced onion, diced bell pepper, diced celery, minced garlic, sliced smoked sausage, bay leaves, dried thyme, dried oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Give everything a good stir to ensure that the flavors are evenly distributed.

  3. Pour in the chicken broth, making sure that the beans and vegetables are fully submerged. If needed, add more broth or water to cover them. Place the lid on the slow cooker and cook on low for 8-10 hours, or on high for 4-6 hours. The longer you cook them, the creamier the beans will become.

  4. Once the cooking time is up, remove the bay leaves and give the beans a good stir. At this point, you can use a spoon to mash some of the beans against the side of the slow cooker to thicken the mixture. If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can also use an immersion blender to partially blend the beans.

  5. Serve the Cajun red beans over a bed of cooked white rice, garnished with chopped green onions. The combination of the creamy beans, smoky sausage, and aromatic spices will have your taste buds singing!

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, simply omit the smoked sausage and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. You can also add some diced tomatoes for extra flavor.
  • If you prefer a spicier dish, increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add a few dashes of hot sauce to the slow cooker.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. The flavors will continue to develop over time, making the leftovers even more delicious.

So there you have it, a mouthwatering Slow Cooker Cajun Red Beans and Rice recipe that will transport you straight to the heart of the South. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply want to indulge in some comfort food, this Southern classic is sure to satisfy. So fire up your slow cooker and get ready to enjoy a taste of Louisiana right in your own kitchen. Happy cooking!
