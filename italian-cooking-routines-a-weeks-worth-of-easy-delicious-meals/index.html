Italian Cooking Routines: A Week's Worth of Easy, Delicious Meals


Italian cuisine is loved across the globe for its delectable flavors, versatile ingredients, and enriching textures. Traditional Italian dishes possess a certain charm that transcends cultures and time. In this blog post, I’ll endeavor to share my absolute favorite Italian recipes, perfect for preparing a week’s worth of easy, tasty and slightly modified Italian cooking routines.

Monday: Classic Marinara with Spaghetti

Kick starting your week with a classic Italian pasta dish - Marinara with Spaghetti. This dish is perfect for hectic weekdays as it doesn’t require much time and yet screams authentic Italian taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Pound Spaghetti
  • 2 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 4 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 Can Diced Tomatoes ( approximately 14.5 Oz)
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Fresh Basil Leaves

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Spaghetti as per the package instructions.
  2. In a separate pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and sauté for a minute.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cook until the sauce thickens, about 15 minutes.
  6. Combine the cooked spaghetti with the marinara sauce.
  7. Finish by topping it with freshly grated parmesan cheese and a sprig of freshly torn basil leaves.

Tuesday: Positano Pasta with Zucchini

A classic recipe from the Amalfi Coast, the Positano Pasta with Zucchini is a healthy and delightful twist to traditional Italian pasta. The lovely bright green color and mild flavor of the zucchini, combined with the rich and comforting pasta, creates a meal in itself.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Pound Pasta
  • 2 Zucchinis, spiralized or finely julienne
  • 2 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 4 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Red Pepper Flakes (optional)
  • Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Fresh basil Leaves

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions.
  2. In a separate saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook until it becomes aromatic.
  4. Add the spiralized zucchini, sprinkle salt, and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Combine cooked pasta with zucchini mixture and toss well.
  6. Serve steaming hot, garnished with freshly grated parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves. Don’t forget to make it spicy if you wish, with a pinch of red pepper flakes.

Wednesday: Risotto Milanese

One of the most famous risotto recipes- Risotto Milanese- is an elegant and exquisite dish on any table. The saffron-infused risotto topped with fried perch or dried saffron-infused chicken, is a meal you don’t want to miss.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 Cup Arborio Rice
  • 4 Cups Hot Chicken Stock
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Saffron Threads
  • 1 1/2 Cups Dry White Wine
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 1/2 Cup Crumbled Mascarpone Cheese or Cream
  • 2 Tablespoons Butter
  • Freshly Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Freshly Chopped Parsley

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat up the chicken stock and keep it warm.
  2. In a separate pot, heat a bit of olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cook until the onion becomes translucent.
  4. Add the Arborio rice, stirring to coat the rice evenly.
  5. Add the saffron threads, then pour in the white wine and stir until the wine is absorbed.
  6. Add a ladle of hot chicken stock, stirring gently until the liquid is absorbed before adding more.
  7. Repeat this process until all the stock is used, cooking until the rice is creamy and al dente.
  8. Stir in Mascarpone cheese or cream and butter, and season with salt and pepper.
  9. Serve with freshly grated parmesan cheese and fresh chopped parsley on top.

Thursday: Chicken Parmesan

Switch gears from the expertly made risotto, and dive into the world of Chicken Parmesan. A juicy chicken cutlet coated in breadcrumbs, baked to crisp, then smothered in marinara sauce and melted mozzarella. Who could say no?

Ingredients:

  • 4 Chicken Breasts
  • 1 1/2 Cups All-Purpose Flour
  • 2 Large Eggs
  • 1 Cup Breadcrumbs
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 2 Cups Marinara Sauce
  • 1 1/2 Cups Shredded Mozzarella Cheese
  • Freshly Chopped Parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F.
  2. In one dish, mix together the flour, salt, and pepper.
  3. In another dish, beat the eggs.
  4. In a third dish, combine the breadcrumbs.
  5. Pat your chicken breasts flat and coat each in the flour mixture, shaking off the excess.
  6. Next, dip in the beaten egg, then in the breadcrumbs, making sure to coat well.
  7. Place the coated chicken breasts on a baking sheet, and bake for 25 minutes until cooked through.
  8. Pour the marinara sauce on top of the chicken, followed by the shredded mozzarella cheese.
  9. Place back into the oven for additional 5 minutes, until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  10. Serve with a sprinkle of freshly chopped parsley.

Friday: Weekend-ready Bruschetta

Make your weekend delightful with a flavorful and elegant bruschetta. With a simple yet delicious combination of ripe tomatoes, fresh basil, and balsamic vinegar on crispy toasted bread, this dish is the perfect mix of taste, texture, and freshness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Baguette
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Balsamic Vinegar
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Fresh Chopped Basil
  • Garlic, for toasting the bread

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F
  2. Slice the baguette into 1/2-inch thick pieces, then place them on a baking sheet.
  3. Rub the baguette slices with garlic, drizzle them with olive oil, then put them in the oven until golden brown.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix the chopped tomatoes, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  5. Top the toasted bread slices with the tomato mixture, then sprinkle with freshly chopped basil.

Saturday: Classic Lasagna

Indulge in the comforting Italian classic that never fails to impress- Lasagna. Perhaps considered the ultimate comfort food, lasagna is a mouthwatering combination of layered pasta, ricotta, and mozzarella, smothered in a rich tomato sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 Box Lasagna Noodles
  • 2 Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 1 Pound Ground Beef or Italian Sausage
  • 1 Large Onion, finely chopped
  • 3 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 Can Crushed Tomatoes
  • 1 Can Tomato Paste
  • 3 Cups Ricotta Cheese
  • 2 Large Eggs
  • 1 Teaspoon Dried Basil
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • 2 Cups Shredded Mozzarella
  • Freshly Chopped Parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F
  2. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the ground beef or sausage and cook until the meat is browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
  4. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cook until they become soft.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and season with salt, pepper, and dried basil.
  6. Simmer for at least 30 minutes while you prepare the lasagna layers.
  7. In a separate bowl, mix the ricotta cheese, eggs, and dried basil.
  8. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  9. Assemble the lasagna by layering: a thin layer of the meat sauce, a layer of the ricotta mixture, and a layer of cooked lasagna noodles. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of the meat sauce and mozzarella cheese on top.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  11. Allow to cool for a few minutes before serving, garnished with freshly chopped parsley.

Sunday: Prosciutto-wrapped Chicken with Sage

Savor your Sunday with a delicious Prosciutto-wrapped Chicken with Sage. This elegant dish is packed with flavor from the savory prosciutto, fragrant sage, and succulent chicken breast. The combination of the flavors is simply divine.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Boneless Skinless Chicken Breasts
  • 12 Slices Prosciutto
  • Fresh Sage Leaves
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Olive Oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F
  2. With a chicken breast in between two sheets of Prosciutto, wrap the chicken tightly and secure with toothpicks.
  3. Brush the wrapped chicken with olive oil and season both sides with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the chicken in a baking dish and roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through.
  5. The sage leaves added to the roasting pan combine with dripping and add a lovely aromatic touch.

And there you have it- a week’s worth of easy, delicious Italian meals! The beauty of these meals is that they not only taste amazing, but they also help you dive into the rich culinary heritage of Italy. So, bundle up your loved ones, pull out the good wine, and start cooking. Buon Appetito!
