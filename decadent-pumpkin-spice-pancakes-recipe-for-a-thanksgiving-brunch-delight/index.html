Decadent Pumpkin Spice Pancakes Recipe for a Thanksgiving Brunch Delight


If you’re looking for a delicious and festive treat to serve at your Thanksgiving brunch, look no further than these Decadent Pumpkin Spice Pancakes. Combining the warm flavors of pumpkin and spices, these pancakes are sure to be a hit with your family and friends. They’re fluffy, flavorful, and perfect for a cozy autumn morning. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 cup milk
  • ½ cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves. Make sure all the dry ingredients are well combined.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, pumpkin puree, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth and well incorporated.

  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to tough pancakes.

  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with butter or cooking spray.

  5. Using a ¼ cup measuring cup, pour the pancake batter onto the skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until bubbles start to form on the surface of the pancake.

  6. Flip the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.

  7. Repeat with the remaining batter, adding more butter or cooking spray as needed.

  8. Serve the pancakes warm with your favorite toppings. Maple syrup, whipped cream, chopped nuts, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar are all great options.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, you can add chocolate chips or chopped pecans to the pancake batter before cooking.
  • If you prefer a lighter texture, you can substitute some of the all-purpose flour with cake flour.
  • Feel free to adjust the spices to your taste. If you love cinnamon, add a bit more. If you’re not a fan of cloves, you can omit them altogether.
  • To keep the pancakes warm while you finish cooking the rest, place them on a baking sheet in a low oven (around 200°F/93°C) until ready to serve.

These Decadent Pumpkin Spice Pancakes are the perfect way to start your Thanksgiving morning. The combination of pumpkin and spices will fill your kitchen with the aroma of fall, and the fluffy texture will make every bite melt in your mouth. Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or enjoying a cozy brunch for two, these pancakes are guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat your skillet, and get ready to indulge in the ultimate Thanksgiving brunch delight. Your loved ones will thank you for it! Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
