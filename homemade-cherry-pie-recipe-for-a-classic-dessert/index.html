Homemade Cherry Pie Recipe for a Classic Dessert


A homemade cherry pie is more than just a dessert—it’s a nostalgic experience wrapped in a flaky crust and ooey-gooey fruit filling. Few things compare to the joy of pulling a warm pie fresh from the oven, letting its irresistible aroma fill the air, and savoring a slice with loved ones. Whether you’re preparing it for a holiday gathering, a weekend treat, or simply to celebrate the joy of baking, this homemade cherry pie recipe promises to deliver a rich, tangy-sweet flavor with the perfect balance of tartness and sweetness.

Why Make Cherry Pie at Home?

Store-bought desserts can never replicate the authentic taste and charm of a pie made from scratch. Homemade cherry pie not only allows you to control the quality of the ingredients but also enables you to tailor the flavor and texture exactly to your liking. Whether you use sweet Bing cherries, tart Montmorency cherries, or a combination of the two, this dessert lets the cherries truly shine while the buttery, flaky crust acts as the perfect complement.

The beauty of this recipe lies in its versatility. You can use fresh, frozen, or canned cherries based on what’s available or in season. Additionally, mastering this cherry pie recipe can give you the confidence to experiment with other fruits, spices, and personal touches in the future.

Ingredients

To make the ultimate homemade cherry pie, you’ll need two main components: the crust and the filling. Here’s everything you’ll need:

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups (310g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (226g) unsalted butter, very cold and cut into small cubes
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Cherry Filling:

  • 5 cups fresh or frozen pitted cherries (if using canned, drain and rinse thoroughly)
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup (30g) cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract (optional but highly recommended for enhanced flavor)
  • Pinch of salt

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • Coarse sugar for topping (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Pie Crust

The foundation of any beautiful pie is its crust. A homemade crust may seem intimidating at first, but with this simple method, you’ll nail that golden, flaky texture every time.

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. You should still have small pea-sized lumps of butter throughout the dough. These lumps will create the flakiness when baked.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, while gently mixing with a fork until the dough holds together but isn’t sticky. Avoid overworking the dough.
  4. Divide the dough into two equal portions, shape each into a disc, and wrap in plastic wrap. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or up to 2 days.

2. Make the Cherry Filling

The heart of the pie lies in its filling. This recipe preserves the juicy freshness of cherries while creating a thick, luscious consistency.

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, almond extract (if using), and salt.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture starts to thicken and bubble—this typically takes about 10-15 minutes. If your cherries are overly juicy, it may take a little longer for the filling to thicken.
  3. Remove the filling from heat and allow it to cool completely. Cooling prevents it from making your bottom crust soggy during baking.

3. Assemble the Pie

Now comes the fun and creative part: assembling the pie! This is where you can show off your decorative skills with a lattice top or other creative patterns.

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of chilled dough into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer it to a 9-inch pie pan, pressing it gently into the sides and bottom without stretching.
  3. Pour the cooled cherry filling into the prepared crust, spreading it out evenly.
  4. Roll out the second dough disc into another 12-inch circle. Here, you can either place it on top as a solid crust or cut it into strips for a classic lattice design. If opting for a solid crust, make sure to cut a few small slits for steam to escape.
  5. Trim and seal the edges where the top and bottom crusts meet, then crimp or flute for a decorative finish.

4. Add the Finishing Touch

To achieve a glossy, golden-brown crust, brush the top of the pie with an egg wash made by whisking together the beaten egg and milk. Sprinkle with coarse sugar for a sparkling, crunchy finish if desired.

5. Bake Your Cherry Pie

Place the pie on the center rack of your preheated oven and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes. Then, reduce the temperature to 375°F (190°C) and continue baking for an additional 35-40 minutes. The crust should be golden, and the filling should be bubbling.

If the edges of your crust start browning too quickly, cover them with foil or a pie crust shield to prevent burning.

6. Cool and Serve

Once baked, allow the pie to cool at room temperature for at least 2 hours. This cooling time helps the filling to set properly so that it doesn’t ooze out when sliced.

Serve warm or at room temperature. For extra indulgence, pair each slice with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips for the Perfect Cherry Pie

  • Choosing the Right Cherries: Fresh cherries are ideal for their flavor and texture. If you’re using frozen cherries, don’t thaw them; use them directly to prevent excess moisture. When using jarred or canned cherries, ensure you rinse them thoroughly to reduce the syrupy sweetness.
  • Thickening the Filling: Cornstarch works well to thicken the filling, but you can also use tapioca starch or flour if you prefer.
  • Keeping the Crust Flaky: Make sure your butter and water are extremely cold to achieve a tender, flaky crust. If the dough starts to warm up, pop it back in the refrigerator for a few minutes.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers, store them, loosely covered, in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. To reheat, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and warm the pie for 10-15 minutes to restore its freshly baked taste and texture.

Additionally, you can freeze a fully baked and cooled cherry pie for up to 3 months. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil to prevent freezer burn. Simply thaw it overnight in the refrigerator and reheat as needed.

Conclusion

There’s something truly magical about making a classic cherry pie from scratch—it’s a labor of love that pays off in every bite. With its buttery, homemade crust and perfectly sweet filling, this dessert is bound to become a staple for family meals, celebrations, or even quiet evenings with a cup of tea.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather some cherries, and create your own masterpiece. Once you’ve tried this recipe, homemade cherry pie will forever hold a special place in your heart (and your recipe box).
