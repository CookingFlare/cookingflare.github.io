Spring Vegetable Frittata: A Fresh Twist for Easter


As Easter approaches, it’s the perfect time to celebrate the arrival of spring with a delicious and vibrant dish that showcases the season’s freshest produce. A spring vegetable frittata is a versatile and satisfying option that can be served for brunch, lunch, or even a light dinner. Packed with the goodness of colorful vegetables and protein-rich eggs, this frittata is not only a treat for the taste buds but also a feast for the eyes.

Ingredients

Before we delve into the cooking process, let’s gather our ingredients. For the spring vegetable frittata, you will need:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup of fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup of fresh peas
  • 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs such as parsley or dill for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Vegetables

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Set this aside while you prepare the vegetables.

In a 10-inch oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped red onion and garlic, and sauté until they turn translucent and fragrant. Then, add the asparagus, cherry tomatoes, and peas to the skillet. Sauté the vegetables for a few minutes until they begin to soften but still retain their vibrant colors. Finally, add the fresh spinach leaves to the skillet and cook until they are just wilted.

Step 2: Assembling the Frittata

Once the vegetables are cooked to perfection, evenly distribute them in the skillet. Then, carefully pour the egg mixture over the vegetables, ensuring that the eggs cover the entire skillet. Allow the frittata to cook undisturbed for a couple of minutes until the edges start to set.

Step 3: Finishing in the Oven

Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese over the top of the frittata and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Let the frittata bake for 12-15 minutes or until the eggs are fully set and the top is lightly golden.

Step 4: Serving and Garnishing

Once the frittata is cooked to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Carefully slide a spatula around the edges of the skillet to loosen the frittata. Gently slide the frittata onto a serving plate or cutting board. Sprinkle some fresh herbs over the top for a burst of color and added freshness.

Conclusion

A spring vegetable frittata is a delightful dish that captures the essence of the season. The combination of tender asparagus, sweet cherry tomatoes, and vibrant green peas, all enveloped in fluffy eggs and tangy feta cheese, creates a symphony of flavors in every bite. Whether you’re hosting a festive Easter brunch or simply craving a taste of spring, this frittata is sure to impress.

So, this Easter, embrace the spirit of the season with a spring vegetable frittata that celebrates the beauty of fresh produce and the joy of gathering around the table with loved ones. It’s a dish that not only nourishes the body but also feeds the soul.
