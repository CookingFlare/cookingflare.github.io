Authentic Pad Thai Recipe with Shrimp and Tofu


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, then you’re probably no stranger to the delightful flavors of Pad Thai. This iconic dish is a harmonious blend of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors, combined with the satisfying textures of tender shrimp, firm tofu, and chewy rice noodles. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating an authentic Pad Thai dish that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Thailand.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz (225g) rice noodles
  • 8 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz (225g) firm tofu, cubed
  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice Noodles: Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes, or until they are soft but still slightly firm. Drain and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix the tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar until the sugar is dissolved. Set the sauce aside.

  3. Cook the Shrimp and Tofu: In a large wok or skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and tofu, and cook until the shrimp turns pink and the tofu is lightly browned. Remove the shrimp and tofu from the wok and set them aside.

  4. Create the Base: In the same wok, heat the remaining oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant. Crack the eggs into the wok and scramble them until they are partially set.

  5. Add Noodles and Sauce: Increase the heat to high and add the soaked rice noodles to the wok. Pour the prepared sauce over the noodles and toss everything together until the noodles are evenly coated.

  6. Incorporate the Shrimp and Tofu: Return the cooked shrimp and tofu to the wok, and stir to combine with the noodles.

  7. Finish and Serve: Add the bean sprouts and half of the chopped green onions to the wok, and toss them with the noodles for a minute. Remove the wok from the heat and transfer the Pad Thai to serving plates. Garnish with crushed peanuts, the remaining green onions, and a wedge of lime. Serve with fresh cilantro on the side.

Tips for Success

  • Use a wok or a large skillet with high sides to accommodate the tossing and stirring of the ingredients.
  • Have all your ingredients prepped and ready to go, as Pad Thai cooks quickly once you start the process.
  • Adjust the sweetness and tanginess of the Pad Thai sauce to your preference by adding more palm sugar or tamarind paste.

Final Thoughts

With its vibrant flavors and contrasting textures, Pad Thai with shrimp and tofu is a dish that never fails to impress. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining guests, this authentic recipe is sure to be a hit. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your wok, and embark on a culinary journey to create a mouthwatering Pad Thai experience in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
