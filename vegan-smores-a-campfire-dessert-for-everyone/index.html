Vegan S'mores: A Campfire Dessert for Everyone


Summer nights are made for campfires, laughter, and the delicious aroma of toasted marshmallows. Among the classic campfire treats, nothing quite compares to the timeless s’more—a delightful concoction of graham crackers, gooey marshmallows, and rich chocolate. But what if you’re following a vegan lifestyle? Fear not! This blog post will guide you through the process of making vegan s’mores that everyone can enjoy, whether they’re plant-based eaters or not.

Understanding the Ingredients

The charm of s’mores lies in their simplicity. Traditional recipes usually require three main components: graham crackers, marshmallows, and chocolate. However, with a vegan twist, we’ll look for plant-based alternatives that maintain the essence of this beloved dessert.

1. Vegan Graham Crackers

The base of any good s’more starts with graham crackers. Fortunately, many brands offer vegan-friendly options, typically free from honey and made with wholesome ingredients. When shopping for graham crackers, look for those made with whole grains for added nutrition and flavor.

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can even make your own vegan graham crackers! Here’s a quick recipe:

Homemade Vegan Graham Crackers

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/3 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons non-dairy milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, almond flour, coconut sugar, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and non-dairy milk until well combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms.
  5. Roll out the dough between two sheets of parchment paper to about 1/8 inch thick. Cut into squares or rectangles.
  6. Place the crackers on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  7. Let them cool completely before using.

2. Vegan Marshmallows

Traditional marshmallows are made with gelatin, which is derived from animal collagen. Luckily, there are several brands that offer vegan marshmallows made from plant-based ingredients like agar-agar or carrageenan. Brands such as Dandies or Sweet & Sara are popular choices that can be found in most grocery stores or health food stores.

If you want to make homemade vegan marshmallows, here’s a simple recipe:

Homemade Vegan Marshmallows

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water, divided
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light corn syrup
  • 1 tablespoon agar-agar powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine 1/2 cup of water with the agar-agar powder. Let it sit for about 5 minutes to bloom.
  2. In a separate saucepan, combine the granulated sugar, corn syrup, and the remaining 1/2 cup of water. Bring to a boil, stirring until the sugar is dissolved.
  3. Add the bloomed agar-agar mixture to the sugar mixture, and let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract and salt.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased 8x8 inch baking dish and let it sit at room temperature for at least 4 hours or until set.
  6. Once set, dust with powdered sugar and cut into squares.

3. Vegan Chocolate

The final element of a s’more is, of course, chocolate. Many dark chocolates are vegan, but always check the label to ensure there are no dairy ingredients. Brands like Enjoy Life and Hu Chocolate offer delicious vegan options that pair beautifully with your s’mores.

Putting It All Together

Now that we have our vegan graham crackers, marshmallows, and chocolate ready, it’s time to assemble the perfect vegan s’more. Whether you’re at a campsite or in your backyard, the process remains the same.

The Assembly

Ingredients:

  • Vegan graham crackers
  • Vegan marshmallows
  • Vegan chocolate

Instructions:

  1. Start with one graham cracker as your base.
  2. Place a piece of vegan chocolate on the cracker.
  3. Roast a vegan marshmallow over the fire until it’s golden brown and gooey (or use a microwave for a quick option).
  4. Place the roasted marshmallow on top of the chocolate.
  5. Top with another graham cracker and gently press down to create a sandwich.
  6. Enjoy your vegan s’more while it’s warm!

Tips for the Perfect Vegan S’more

  1. Toast Your Marshmallows: Achieving the perfect roast on your marshmallow is key. You want it to be golden brown on the outside and melty on the inside. Use a long skewer for safety and hold it over the fire, rotating slowly.

  2. Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to switch things up! Try flavored chocolate bars, add peanut butter or almond butter, or even introduce fresh fruits like strawberries or bananas into the mix for a fun twist.

  3. Make it a S’more Bar: If you’re hosting a gathering, set up a s’more bar where guests can customize their treats. Provide a variety of graham crackers, marshmallows, chocolates, and toppings to create unique combinations.

  4. Use a Grill or Oven: If you don’t have access to a fire, you can still enjoy vegan s’mores! Simply use a grill or an oven to toast your marshmallows and melt the chocolate.

Why Vegan S’mores Are for Everyone

Vegan s’mores are not just for those following a plant-based diet; they are a delicious treat that can be enjoyed by everyone. They offer a guilt-free way to indulge in a classic dessert without compromising on taste or texture. Plus, making vegan s’mores can be a fun bonding activity for family and friends, encouraging everyone to get involved in the process.

Health Benefits

Switching to vegan ingredients can also provide health benefits. Vegan marshmallows and chocolates often have fewer additives and preservatives than their traditional counterparts. Additionally, using whole grain graham crackers can add fiber and essential nutrients to your dessert.

Conclusion

Vegan s’mores are a delightful treat that everyone can enjoy, whether you’re gathered around a campfire, hosting a backyard barbecue, or simply satisfying your sweet tooth at home. With just a few simple substitutions, you can create a s’more that is not only delicious but also aligns with a vegan lifestyle.

So, gather your ingredients, invite some friends, and embark on a s’more-making adventure. You might just find that these vegan s’mores are even better than the classic version! Enjoy the process, savor every bite, and create memories that will last a lifetime. Happy toasting!
