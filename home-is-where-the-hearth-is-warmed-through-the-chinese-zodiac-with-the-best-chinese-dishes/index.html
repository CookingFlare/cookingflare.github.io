Home is Where the Hearth is: Warmed Through the Chinese Zodiac With the Best Chinese Dishes


Every Chinese New Year, families gather together to celebrate the Lunar New Year. And while there are various customs and traditions observed during this time, specific dishes are always associated with the Chinese Zodiac, believing that these dishes encourage good fortune. So without further ado, let’s embark on a mouth-watering journey through the animal signs and their respective iconic dishes.

Rat (鼠 - Shǔ): Rats are associated with the winter season and the Chinese believe they symbolize longevity, wealth, and good fortune. So, it is only fitting to start with a dish that embodies these qualities – the ever-popular sticky rice cake (糯米糕, Nuò Mí Gāo). The soft, chewy texture of the rice cake signifies the prosperity of wealth.

Ox (牛 - Niú): The Ox represents perseverance, determination, and hard work. It is fitting that the Ox is associated with beef dishes. A staple dish from the Liangshan Yi ethnic group, the Steamed Beef and Red Bean Soup (红豆炖牛肉, Hóng Dòu Dùn Niú Ròu), is perfect for the Ox. This comforting soup symbolizes balance and is believed to assist in overcoming any career stagnation.

Tiger (虎 - Hǔ): The Tiger is a symbol of bravery and power. To honor this regal creature, we welcome the Bold and Spicy Numbing Beef (麻辣烫, Málà Tàng) into the mix. This delectable hot pot is packed full with tender slices of meat, vegetables, and spices that embodies the fieriness of the Tiger.

Rabbit (兔 - Tù): Associated with grace, kindness, and fertility, the Rabbit finds its representation in the ‘Golden Duck with Six Spices’ (六味索子, Liù Wèi Suǒ Zǐ). A sweet, savory treat adorned with essences of six extravagantly fragrant spices, this dish increases the prosperity of relationships, happiness and longevity.

Dragon (龙 - Lóng): Powerful, heroic, and dynamic - a true embodiment of the Dragon. Owing to these traits, the Dragon is recognized with green vegetable and the appetite-whetting Stir-fried Dace Fish with Green Onions (葱爆鳢鱼, Cōng Bào Yá Yú) that entices nobility and safeguards wealth.

Snake (蛇 - Shé): The Snake signifies wisdom and transformation. Hence the dish known as ‘Sliced Fish and Leek Thick Soup’ (葱爆鳢鱼, Cōng Bào Yá Yú) derives its essence, symbolizing the gradual growth and development in wisdom.

Horse (马 - Mǎ): Known for their vitality and strength, the Horse gets its ideal meal in the form of the invigorating ‘Stir-fried Dry Noodles’ (炒乾面, Chǎo Gān Miàn). This dish is a powerhouse of energy, perfectly capturing the exuberance of the Horse.

** Goat (羊 - Yáng):** The Goat is all about harmony and balance. A fitting dish indeed is the well-balanced “Change Sauce Fish” (酱爆鱼, Jiàng Bào Yú). The gentle, succulent fish is fried to a golden perfection and smothered in change sauce, evoking a sense of tranquility and moderation.

Monkey (猴 - Hóu): Associated with intelligence, adaptability, and curiosity, the Monkey’s dish of choice is the Stir-fried Dried Shrimps with Cashews (锅巴辣虾, Guō Bā Là Xiā). The addictive spices and crunchiness of nuts bring a sense of adventurous learning to the banquet.

Rooster (鸡 - Jī): The Rooster is symbolic for its communication skills. Therefore, the ‘Fried Rice with Leftover Chicken’ (麻婆鸡 τά飯, Má Pó Jī Táofàn) is the ideal dish, connecting family members and adding a sense of unity to the gathering.

Dog (狗 - Gǒu): Dogs represent loyalty and faithfulness. An auspicious dish that bestows loyalty is the Steamed Chicken with Oyster Sauce (蚵墨鸡, Kè Mò Jī). The tender, juicy chicken is steamed to perfection, drenched in a beautifully authentic, scrumptious oyster sauce.

Pig (猪 - Zhū): Representing prosperity and abundance, the Pig compliments the glistening ‘Red Cooked Pork with Radish’ (红烧排骨, Hóng Shāo Pái Gǔ). Swimming in a luscious, sweet and savory sauce, this dish guarantees a better and more prosperous future.

To wrap up our Chinese Zodiac food journey, it is clear that each zodiac sign represented by a creature is linked to a dish that shares their traits and significance. So, as you prepare for the Chinese New Year bash, remember to cater to your Zodiac sign and bring in a new age of prosperity, luck, and happiness. After all, the heart of home during Chinese New Year lies in the delectable dishes that bring the family together.
