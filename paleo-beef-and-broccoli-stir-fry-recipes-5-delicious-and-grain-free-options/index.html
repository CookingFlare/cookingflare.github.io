Paleo Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


In the world of paleo cooking, stir-fries are a staple dish that can be easily customized to suit your taste preferences. When it comes to beef and broccoli, this classic combination is a match made in heaven. In this post, we’ll explore 5 delicious and grain-free paleo beef and broccoli stir-fry recipes that are sure to become a regular in your meal rotation.

Recipe 1: Classic Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

This recipe is a great starting point for those new to paleo cooking. It’s simple, yet packed with flavor.

  • 1 pound beef strips (sirloin or ribeye work well)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (make sure it’s free from grains)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

In a large skillet or wok, heat the coconut oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside. Add the broccoli, garlic, and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the broccoli is tender-crisp. Return the beef to the skillet and stir in the soy sauce and sesame oil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Recipe 2: Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry with Ginger and Green Onions

This recipe adds a fresh and aromatic twist to the classic combination.

  • 1 pound beef strips (sirloin or ribeye work well)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (make sure it’s free from grains)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

In a large skillet or wok, heat the coconut oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside. Add the broccoli, garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the broccoli is tender-crisp. Return the beef to the skillet and stir in the soy sauce, sesame oil, and green onions. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
