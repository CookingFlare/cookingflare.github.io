Instant Pot Chicken Soup: The Ultimate Comfort Food Recipe


If you’re looking for a warm, comforting bowl of soup that’s quick and easy to make, then Instant Pot chicken soup is the answer. This delicious, hearty dish combines tender chicken, aromatic vegetables, and savory broth to create the perfect meal for any occasion. Whether you’re nursing a cold, craving something cozy, or simply need a quick and healthy dinner, this recipe will surely hit the spot.

Why Choose Instant Pot Chicken Soup?

The Instant Pot has revolutionized the way we cook by reducing cooking times significantly without compromising flavor or texture. Chicken soup, traditionally simmered for hours, can now be prepared in under an hour, thanks to the magic of the Instant Pot. The pressure cooking method locks in the flavors, making the broth rich and savory while ensuring the chicken is perfectly tender.

Beyond the time-saving benefits, Instant Pot chicken soup is an incredibly versatile dish. You can adjust the recipe to fit your dietary preferences, add your favorite vegetables, or use different types of meat for variety. Plus, it’s a great option for meal prepping, as it stores well in the fridge and freezes beautifully for future meals.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Chicken Soup

The beauty of chicken soup lies in its simplicity. This recipe uses a handful of ingredients that you likely already have in your pantry. Here’s what you need:

Main Ingredients:

  • Chicken: Bone-in, skinless chicken thighs or breasts work well. Bone-in chicken gives the broth a deeper, richer flavor, but boneless skinless chicken will cook a bit faster and is easier to shred.
  • Carrots: Fresh, peeled carrots are a must. They add natural sweetness and a vibrant color to the soup.
  • Celery: A classic addition, celery provides a subtle earthiness that enhances the overall flavor.
  • Onion: Adds aromatic depth to the soup.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic brings a warm, aromatic note that complements the other vegetables.
  • Broth: Use low-sodium chicken broth for the base. You can substitute with vegetable broth if you prefer a vegetarian version.
  • Herbs and Spices: Fresh or dried thyme, bay leaves, parsley, and salt and pepper are essential for seasoning the soup. Feel free to add a pinch of dried rosemary or oregano for extra flavor.

Optional Add-ins:

  • Noodles or Rice: Adding noodles (egg noodles work great) or rice makes the soup heartier and more filling.
  • Spinach or Kale: For a nutritious boost, consider adding some leafy greens toward the end of the cooking process.
  • Lemon: A squeeze of lemon juice brightens up the flavors and adds a bit of acidity to balance the richness of the broth.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Instant Pot Chicken Soup

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you start cooking, chop all your vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Dice the onions, slice the carrots and celery, and mince the garlic. If you’re using chicken thighs with skin, remove the skin before cooking to reduce fat content.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables and Garlic

Turn your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” mode and allow it to heat up. Once hot, add a tablespoon of olive oil. Add the diced onions, carrots, and celery to the pot. Sauté the vegetables for about 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they begin to soften and the onions become translucent. Add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional 30 seconds, until fragrant.

This step helps develop the base flavor of the soup and makes sure the garlic doesn’t burn, which can make the broth taste bitter.

Step 3: Add the Chicken and Broth

Once your vegetables are sautéed, add the chicken pieces to the pot. Pour in the chicken broth and make sure the chicken is fully submerged. Stir to combine, scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon (these bits add flavor to the broth).

Add the herbs, including thyme, bay leaves, and parsley, along with salt and pepper to taste. If you’re adding noodles or rice, you can add them now, or you can wait until after the pressure cooking phase, depending on your preference.

Step 4: Pressure Cook the Soup

Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to the “Sealing” position. Select the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” mode and set the cooking time for 15 minutes on high pressure. The Instant Pot will take a few minutes to come to pressure before the countdown begins.

During the pressure cooking, the chicken will become tender and the flavors will meld together beautifully.

Step 5: Release the Pressure

Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 5-10 minutes, then perform a quick release to release any remaining pressure. Carefully open the lid. You should see a beautifully cooked chicken soup with a fragrant broth.

Step 6: Shred the Chicken

Using tongs or a slotted spoon, remove the chicken from the pot and place it on a cutting board. Let it cool slightly, then use two forks to shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and stir to combine with the soup. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed, adding more salt, pepper, or herbs as desired.

Step 7: Add Noodles or Rice (Optional)

If you haven’t already added noodles or rice, you can do so now. For noodles, stir in about 1-2 cups of your preferred variety (egg noodles, rotini, or even spiralized zucchini). For rice, add about ½ to 1 cup of uncooked white or brown rice. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode again and let the soup simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the noodles or rice are tender and fully cooked.

Step 8: Finish and Serve

Once your noodles or rice are cooked, turn off the Instant Pot and discard the bay leaves. If desired, stir in a handful of fresh spinach or kale for added nutrition. Serve your Instant Pot chicken soup hot with a sprinkle of fresh parsley or a squeeze of lemon juice for extra flavor.

Tips for the Best Instant Pot Chicken Soup

  • Use Bone-In Chicken for More Flavor: While boneless, skinless chicken breasts cook quickly and are easy to shred, bone-in chicken thighs or breasts will provide a richer, deeper flavor to the broth.
  • Vegetable Variations: Feel free to add or swap out vegetables based on what you have on hand. Potatoes, parsnips, or leeks can all be great additions.
  • Adjust Consistency: If you prefer a thicker soup, you can use an immersion blender to puree a portion of the soup. This creates a creamy texture without the need for heavy cream.
  • Spice It Up: If you like a little heat, try adding a pinch of red pepper flakes or a small chopped jalapeño to the soup.
  • Make it Ahead: This soup is perfect for meal prepping. Make a big batch and store it in the fridge for up to 4 days or freeze it for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply thaw in the fridge overnight and reheat on the stove or in the microwave.

Health Benefits of Instant Pot Chicken Soup

Chicken soup is not only delicious but also packed with health benefits. The ingredients used in this recipe provide a range of nutrients that support your immune system, promote hydration, and aid in digestion. Here’s a quick breakdown of the key health benefits:

  • Chicken: A great source of lean protein, which helps repair tissues and supports muscle health.
  • Vegetables: Rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, vegetables like carrots, celery, and onions provide essential nutrients that support overall health and wellness.
  • Garlic: Known for its immune-boosting properties, garlic has been used for centuries as a natural remedy for colds and flu.
  • Herbs: Fresh thyme and parsley contain antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds that promote overall health.
  • Broth: A warm, comforting bowl of soup made with bone broth is not only soothing, but it also contains collagen, which supports joint health, skin elasticity, and gut health.

Conclusion

Instant Pot chicken soup is the ultimate comfort food that’s easy to make, full of flavor, and incredibly nourishing. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a soothing meal to heal your soul, this soup is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. With its versatility, you can make it your own by adding different vegetables, grains, or seasonings to suit your taste.

The Instant Pot makes this recipe a breeze, reducing the time it takes to make a homemade chicken soup without sacrificing any of the depth of flavor. So the next time you’re craving a warm, comforting meal, reach for your Instant Pot and make this satisfying chicken soup. Your taste buds—and your family—will thank you!
