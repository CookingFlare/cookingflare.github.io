New England Clam Chowder: A Creamy Seafood Classic


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can compare to the rich and hearty goodness of New England Clam Chowder. This creamy seafood classic has been a favorite in the northeastern United States for generations, and it’s not hard to see why. With its velvety texture, savory flavors, and briny undertones, New England Clam Chowder is the perfect dish to warm you up on a chilly evening or serve as the star of a special meal.

Ingredients

To make this delectable New England Clam Chowder, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of fresh clams
  • 4 slices of bacon, diced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 stalks of celery, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 3 cups of potatoes, diced
  • 2 cups of clam juice
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Begin by cleaning the clams. Scrub the shells thoroughly under cold running water to remove any dirt or grit. Discard any clams with cracked shells or that do not close when tapped.

  2. In a large pot, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.

  3. Add the chopped onion, celery, and minced garlic to the pot. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the diced potatoes and clam juice to the pot. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 10-15 minutes.

  5. While the potatoes are cooking, steam the clams. Place them in a separate pot with a few inches of water. Cover and steam over high heat until the clams open, about 5-7 minutes. Discard any clams that do not open.

  6. Remove the clams from their shells, reserving a few in their shells for garnish if desired. Coarsely chop the clams and set them aside.

  7. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour and a splash of milk to make a slurry. Set aside.

  8. Once the potatoes are tender, add the chopped clams, reserved bacon, milk, and heavy cream to the pot. Stir to combine.

  9. Gradually whisk in the flour slurry, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Continue to cook the chowder over medium heat until it thickens slightly, about 5 minutes. Be sure to stir frequently to prevent scorching.

  10. Season the chowder with salt and pepper to taste. Remember that the clams and bacon will add some saltiness, so be cautious when adding additional salt.

  11. Serve the New England Clam Chowder hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley and a few reserved clams in their shells for presentation.

Conclusion

New England Clam Chowder is a timeless classic that brings the flavors of the sea to your table. From the briny sweetness of the clams to the smoky richness of the bacon, every spoonful of this creamy soup is a delight. Whether you’re craving a comforting meal on a rainy day or looking to impress your guests with a taste of New England, this recipe is a must-try. So grab your pot and ladle, and get ready to indulge in the comforting embrace of New England Clam Chowder.
