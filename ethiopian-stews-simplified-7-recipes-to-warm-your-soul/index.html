Ethiopian Stews Simplified: 7 Recipes to Warm Your Soul


Ethiopian cuisine is as diverse and vibrant as the country’s rich culture and history. Known for its intricate flavors, traditional culinary heritage, and communal dining style, Ethiopian food offers a unique experience that warms the soul through every bite. At the heart of Ethiopian fare lie its incredible stews, or “wats,” which are bursting with bold spices and nourishing ingredients. These dishes are typically served atop injera, a soft, sour fermented flatbread, making every meal a combination of layers, textures, and deep flavors. Whether you’re new to Ethiopian cuisine or looking to expand your culinary repertoire, simplifying these stews can help you bring authentic taste to your kitchen. Below, we dive into seven soul-warming Ethiopian stew recipes simplified for home cooks.

1. Doro Wat - The Iconic Chicken Stew

Doro Wat is Ethiopia’s most famous dish and is often hailed as the country’s national stew. Rich, spicy, and comforting, this chicken stew is cooked with a generous amount of onions that are slow-cooked until caramelized. Traditionally, Doro Wat relies on berbere spice mix, a complex blend of chili powders, fenugreek, ginger, garlic, and more.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 2 pounds chicken (thighs or drumsticks work well)
    • 3 large onions
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
    • 3 tablespoons berbere spice mix
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 3 tablespoons ghee or butter
    • 2 cups chicken broth
    • Hard-boiled eggs (optional, for serving)

  2. Instructions:

    • In a large pot, sauté finely chopped onions on medium heat, stirring constantly, until caramelized (about 20 minutes). No oil is needed initially as the onions release their own moisture.
    • Add garlic, ginger, and berbere spice mix. Cook for 2–3 minutes until aromatic.
    • Stir in tomato paste and ghee. Let the mixture combine into a thick base.
    • Add the chicken and coat thoroughly. Pour chicken broth over and reduce heat to low. Simmer for 45–60 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
    • Serve with hard-boiled eggs and injera or rice.

2. Misir Wat - Red Lentil Stew

Misir Wat is a hearty, vegetarian Ethiopian staple that features red lentils simmered in a fragrant berbere-spiced sauce. It’s vibrant in both color and taste, offering a satisfying dish even for those seeking meat-free options.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 1 cup red lentils
    • 2 medium onions
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
    • 2 tablespoons berbere spice mix
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    • 3 cups water or vegetable broth
    • Salt, to taste

  2. Instructions:

    • Sauté chopped onions in oil on medium heat until softened. Add garlic, ginger, berbere spice mix, and tomato paste. Cook until fragrant.
    • Rinse lentils and add them to the pot. Stir to coat with the aromatic mixture.
    • Pour in the water or broth, bring to a boil, then lower the heat to simmer. Cook for 25–30 minutes until the lentils are tender, stirring occasionally.
    • Serve warm with injera or crusty bread for a lighter pairing.

3. Shiro Wat - Comforting Chickpea Stew

Shiro Wat captures the essence of Ethiopian comfort food: creamy, rich, and deeply spiced. Made from ground chickpea flour, it’s a smooth stew that’s quick to prepare and perfect for dipping or spreading on injera.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 1 cup chickpea flour
    • 2 medium onions
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
    • 2 tablespoons berbere spice mix
    • 3 tablespoons olive oil
    • 3 cups water or vegetable broth
    • Salt, to taste

  2. Instructions:

    • In a large skillet, sauté chopped onions with olive oil. Add garlic, ginger, and berbere spice mix once softened. Stir until fragrant.
    • Gradually whisk chickpea flour into the water or vegetable broth, ensuring no lumps. Add this mixture to the skillet.
    • Cook over low heat, stirring continuously, until the stew thickens and takes on a creamy consistency (about 20 minutes).
    • Adjust seasoning with salt and serve warm.

4. Tikil Gomen - Cabbage and Potato Stew

This lighter, milder stew is typically made with cabbage, potatoes, and carrots. Tikil Gomen doesn’t rely heavily on berbere; instead, it embraces earthy turmeric for warmth and color.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 1/2 head of cabbage, chopped
    • 3 medium potatoes, cubed
    • 2 carrots, sliced
    • 1 medium onion
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 cup water
    • Salt and pepper, to taste

  2. Instructions:

    • Heat oil in a large pot and sauté onions, garlic, and turmeric until fragrant.
    • Add cabbage, potatoes, and carrots. Stir to coat in the turmeric mixture.
    • Add water and cover. Cook on low heat for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender.
    • Serve as a light accompaniment to other stews or as a stand-alone meal.

5. Kik Alicha - Mild Split Pea Stew

Kik Alicha provides a gentler flavor palate for those who prefer mild dishes. With yellow split peas and a touch of turmeric, this dish is nutritious and flavorful without being overpowering.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 1 cup yellow split peas
    • 1 medium onion
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric
    • 2 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • Salt, to taste

  2. Instructions:

    • Rinse split peas and set aside.
    • Heat oil in a pot and sauté onion, garlic, and turmeric. Add split peas and stir to combine.
    • Pour in water, bring to a boil, then lower heat to simmer. Cook for 30–35 minutes until tender.
    • Adjust seasoning with salt, and serve with bread or injera.

6. Fasolia Wat - Ethiopian Green Bean Stew

Fasolia Wat incorporates green beans and carrots in a bright, tangy stew. It’s fresh and lighter in texture, making it a great side dish to accompany heavier wats.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 1 pound green beans, trimmed
    • 2 carrots, sliced
    • 1 medium tomato, diced
    • 1 medium onion
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • Juice from 1 lemon
    • Salt and pepper, to taste

  2. Instructions:

    • Sauté onions in olive oil until softened. Add garlic, tomato, green beans, and carrots.
    • Pour lemon juice over and season with salt and pepper. Stir and cover to let the vegetables steam for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    • Serve warm.

7. Siga Wat - Beef Stew

Siga Wat, or beef stew, is robust and deeply flavorful, anchored by chunky beef cuts simmered in a berbere-spiced sauce. It’s perfect for hearty meals and gatherings.

Simplified Recipe:

  1. Ingredients:

    • 2 pounds beef, cubed
    • 2 medium onions
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
    • 3 tablespoons berbere spice mix
    • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 3 cups beef broth
    • 4 tablespoons ghee or butter

  2. Instructions:

    • Brown beef in a large pot with ghee. Remove and set aside.
    • Sauté onions until softened, adding garlic, ginger, and berbere spice mix for flavor.
    • Stir in tomato paste, return beef to the pot, and pour in beef broth. Simmer for 1–1.5 hours on low heat.
    • Serve atop injera or rice.

Final Thoughts

Ethiopian cuisine brings families and friends together with its rich, communal dining traditions, where stews steal the show. Whether you’re crafting a fiery Doro Wat or a mellow Kik Alicha, these simplified recipes ensure that you can explore the flavors of Ethiopia without overcomplicating the process.

Warm, nourishing, and vibrantly seasoned, these stews will transport you to the bustling kitchens of Ethiopia with every spoonful. Serve them alongside injera or your favorite accompaniments and enjoy not just a meal, but a cultural culinary experience. Happy cooking!
