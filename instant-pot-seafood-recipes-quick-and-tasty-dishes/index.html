Instant Pot Seafood Recipes: Quick and Tasty Dishes


Seafood is one of the most versatile and flavorful protein choices when it comes to cooking. From the depth of flavors offered by shrimp and scallops to the rich taste of salmon and mussels, seafood can transform your dining experience into something unforgettable. But let’s face it—cooking seafood can sometimes feel intimidating, especially if you’re trying to whip up a delicious dinner on a tight schedule. That’s where your trusty Instant Pot comes in!

The Instant Pot is a time-saving kitchen marvel, capable of cooking complex and flavorful meals in a fraction of the traditional time. If you’re a seafood lover looking to make quick, delicious, and restaurant-quality meals at home, you’re in for a treat. Below, we’ll dive into mouthwatering Instant Pot seafood recipes that are perfect for weeknight dinners, date nights, or any special occasion.

1. Instant Pot Shrimp Scampi

Shrimp scampi is a classic dish that’s all about simplicity packed with bold flavors. With your Instant Pot, you can recreate this Italian-inspired favorite in under 20 minutes!

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth (or seafood broth for richer flavor)
  • ½ cup white wine (optional; substitute with broth if preferred)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Cooked pasta or rice for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Start by setting your Instant Pot to sauté mode and adding butter and olive oil. Once melted, add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Stir in the chicken broth, white wine (if using), lemon juice, salt, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Add the shrimp to the pot and gently stir to coat them with the sauce. Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to manual pressure cook for 1 minute.
  4. After the cooking cycle is complete, perform a quick release of the pressure. Open the lid carefully and sprinkle parsley on top.
  5. Serve the shrimp scampi over pasta, rice, or enjoy it on its own as a light and flavorful dish.

2. Creamy Instant Pot Seafood Chowder

A warm bowl of chowder is the ultimate comfort food, and making it in an Instant Pot is a breeze. This recipe combines shrimp, scallops, and cod for a rich, hearty seafood medley that takes less than 30 minutes from start to finish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb cod or haddock, cut into chunks
  • ½ lb scallops, cleaned
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups chicken or seafood broth
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup frozen or fresh corn kernels
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to sauté mode and cook the bacon until crisp. Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon and set it aside, leaving the drippings in the pot.
  2. Add the diced onion and garlic to the bacon drippings, sautéing until softened, around 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the broth, potatoes, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well and close the Instant Pot lid. Set to pressure cook on high for 5 minutes.
  4. Perform a quick release, then open the lid and stir in the cream, corn, shrimp, cod, and scallops. Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode and simmer for 3-5 minutes, until the seafood is cooked through.
  5. Ladle into bowls, top with the crispy bacon bits, and garnish with fresh parsley.

3. Instant Pot Garlic Butter Salmon

Salmon comes out perfectly tender and flaky when cooked in an Instant Pot. This garlic butter version is simple, delicious, and sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets (skin-on or skinless)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper.
  2. In a small microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter and mix it with minced garlic and lemon juice. Pour this mixture over the salmon fillets.
  3. Add water to the Instant Pot and place a trivet inside. Lay the salmon fillets on the trivet, skin-side down if applicable.
  4. Close the lid and pressure cook on high for 3 minutes. Perform a quick release once the timer is up.
  5. Carefully remove the salmon and serve with a sprinkle of fresh dill or parsley. Pair with steamed vegetables or a simple salad for a complete meal.

4. Instant Pot Mussels in White Wine Broth

Mussels may seem like a fancy dish reserved for date night, but the Instant Pot simplifies the cooking process, allowing you to enjoy them anytime.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs fresh mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup chicken or seafood broth
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Crusty bread for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. With your Instant Pot on sauté mode, warm the olive oil and cook the garlic and shallot for 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant.
  2. Stir in the white wine, broth, salt, and pepper. Add the mussels to the pot and mix well to coat them in the liquid.
  3. Close the lid and pressure cook on high for 1 minute. Perform a quick release when done.
  4. Open the lid and discard any mussels that didn’t open. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with crusty bread to soak up the delicious broth.

5. Instant Pot Cajun Seafood Boil

The Instant Pot transforms the traditional labor-intensive seafood boil into a quick and easy dish. This spicy and flavorful recipe is perfect for a crowd or any occasion where you want to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb crab legs (optional but highly recommended)
  • 1 lb small red potatoes
  • 2 ears of corn, cut into thirds
  • 1 smoked sausage, sliced into rounds
  • 3 tbsp Cajun seasoning
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 cup chicken or seafood broth
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes, sausage, and corn in your Instant Pot. Sprinkle with half the Cajun seasoning and add the minced garlic.
  2. Pour the broth into the pot, then place the shrimp and crab legs (if using) on top. Squeeze the juice from the lemon halves over everything and drop the squeezed lemon into the pot.
  3. Sprinkle with the remaining Cajun seasoning. Secure the lid and pressure cook on high for 5 minutes.
  4. Perform a quick release, then open the lid and carefully transfer everything to a large serving dish. Drizzle with melted butter and garnish with fresh parsley.

Final Tips for Cooking Seafood in the Instant Pot

  • Use Fresh or Frozen Seafood: The Instant Pot works well with both fresh and frozen seafood. If using frozen seafood, you may need to add an extra minute of cook time for best results.
  • Don’t Overcook: Seafood is delicate and cooks quickly. Follow the recommended times carefully to ensure perfect results.
  • Season Generously: The pressure cooking process can sometimes mute flavors, so don’t be shy with your herbs, spices, and marinades.

These Instant Pot seafood recipes are the perfect way to bring the flavors of the ocean to your table without spending hours in the kitchen. Whether you’re a fan of shrimp, cod, scallops, or mussels, there’s a dish on this list to satisfy your cravings. Grab your Instant Pot and dive into a world of quick, tasty, and seafood-packed dishes!
