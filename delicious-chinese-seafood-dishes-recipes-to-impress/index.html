Delicious Chinese Seafood Dishes: Recipes to Impress


If you’re a seafood lover and want to explore the rich and vibrant flavors of Chinese cuisine, you’re in for a treat! Chinese seafood dishes are known for their delicate balance of flavors, enticing aromas, and beautiful presentation. In this blog post, we’ll explore three delicious Chinese seafood recipes that are sure to impress your family and friends. So let’s dive right in!

1. Steamed Whole Fish with Ginger and Scallions

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole fish (such as sea bass or red snapper), cleaned and scaled
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, julienned
  • 3-4 stalks of scallions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine (Chinese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the fish under cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Make three shallow diagonal cuts on each side of the fish.
  3. Place the fish on a heatproof plate that fits inside your steamer.
  4. Scatter the ginger and scallions over the fish.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, and a pinch of salt. Pour this mixture over the fish.
  6. Fill a wok or large pot with water and bring to a boil. Place the plate with the fish into the steamer and steam for about 10-12 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily.
  7. Carefully remove the plate from the steamer and garnish with some additional julienned ginger and scallions. Serve hot with steamed rice.

2. Salt and Pepper Squid

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the flour, cornstarch, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to 375°F (190°C).
  3. Toss the squid rings in the flour mixture until evenly coated.
  4. Shake off any excess flour and carefully lower the squid rings into the hot oil, a few at a time. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the fried squid to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  6. Repeat the process with the remaining squid.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot with a squeeze of lime juice.

3. Cantonese-style Lobster

Ingredients:

  • 2 lobsters, about 1 1/2 pounds each, halved lengthwise
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine (Chinese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, mixed with 2 tablespoons of water
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ginger and stir-fry for about 30 seconds, until fragrant.
  3. Add the lobster halves, flesh side down, and cook for 2-3 minutes until they start to turn red.
  4. Flip the lobster halves and add the oyster sauce, soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, until the lobster shells turn bright red.
  5. Add the cornstarch mixture and stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  6. Transfer the lobster to a serving platter, garnish with fresh cilantro leaves, and serve hot with steamed rice.

These three Chinese seafood recipes are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a flavorful meal. The delicate steamed fish, crispy salt and pepper squid, and succulent Cantonese-style lobster will surely impress your guests and leave them wanting more. So go ahead, explore the wonders of Chinese seafood cuisine in your own kitchen and enjoy a fantastic dining experience!
