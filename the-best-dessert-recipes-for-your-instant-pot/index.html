The Best Dessert Recipes for Your Instant Pot


When it comes to whipping up quick, delicious desserts, the Instant Pot has transformed the way many home cooks approach sweet treats. This marvel of modern kitchen technology doesn’t just make savory meals a breeze; it excels in producing moist, perfectly cooked desserts with minimal effort. Whether you’re new to using the Instant Pot for desserts or a seasoned pro looking to expand your repertoire, this comprehensive guide presents an array of standout dessert recipes that promise to satisfy every sweet tooth.

From cheesecakes that rival your favorite bakery to rich puddings and moist cakes, these Instant Pot desserts are not only delectable but also surprisingly straightforward. You’ll discover tips, tricks, and tricks of the trade sprinkled throughout to help you make the most of your pressure cooker. Let’s dive right into these crowd-pleasing recipes that turn your Instant Pot into a dessert powerhouse.

1. Classic Instant Pot Cheesecake

Cheesecake is often considered a challenging dessert, but the Instant Pot demystifies the process, delivering creamy, dense perfection every time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 24 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sour cream

Method:

  1. Make the crust: Mix the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar until evenly combined. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a 7-inch springform pan. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the filling: Beat the softened cream cheese with the sugar until smooth and creamy. Add eggs one at a time, blending fully after each addition. Stir in vanilla extract and sour cream until fully incorporated.
  3. Assemble: Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust in the springform pan. Cover the pan tightly with aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in.
  4. Pressure cook: Place a trivet in the Instant Pot, add 1 cup of water, and carefully place the cheesecake on top. Lock the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 35 minutes.
  5. Natural release: Let the pressure naturally release for 15 minutes, then release any remaining pressure manually.
  6. Chill: Remove the cheesecake and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight, to allow it to set completely.

Pro tip: For an extra special touch, top your cheesecake with fresh berries or a drizzle of homemade caramel sauce before serving.

2. Instant Pot Chocolate Lava Cakes

For those moments when you crave molten, gooey chocolate cake, Instant Pot Lava Cakes can satisfy that desire in under half an hour.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 6 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of salt
  • Butter and cocoa powder for ramekins

Method:

  1. Prep ramekins: Butter four 6-ounce heatproof ramekins and dust them with cocoa powder, ensuring they are thoroughly coated.
  2. Melt chocolate and butter: In a microwave-safe bowl, heat butter and chocolate in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth.
  3. Mix batter: Whisk powdered sugar into the chocolate mixture. Add eggs and egg yolks one at a time, beating until smooth. Stir in flour and salt until just combined.
  4. Fill ramekins: Divide the batter evenly among ramekins.
  5. Pressure cook: Pour one cup of water into the Instant Pot and place a trivet. Set ramekins on the trivet, secure the lid, and cook on high pressure for 8 minutes.
  6. Quick release: Carefully perform a quick pressure release and remove the ramekins.
  7. Serve: Let cool for 5 minutes, then gently run a knife around edges and invert onto plates. Enjoy immediately for that luscious lava center.

Insider advice: Adding a scoop of vanilla ice cream elevates this dessert to gourmet status.

3. Instant Pot Rice Pudding

Comforting, creamy rice pudding is a classic dessert that benefits greatly from the consistent heat and moisture of the Instant Pot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: raisins or chopped nuts

Method:

  1. Combine ingredients: In the Instant Pot, mix rice, milk, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt. Stir well.
  2. Cook: Seal the lid, set valve to sealing, and pressure cook on high for 10 minutes.
  3. Natural release: Allow natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then open the valve to release any remaining pressure.
  4. Finish pudding: Stir the pudding thoroughly. If it’s too thin, use the sauté function to thicken for additional minutes while stirring continuously.
  5. Add extras: Stir in raisins or nuts if desired. Serve warm or chilled.

Note: This recipe is forgiving — feel free to experiment with coconut milk or almond milk for a dairy-free twist.

4. Instant Pot Sticky Toffee Pudding

The rich, sticky toffee pudding is an English classic that emerges from your Instant Pot moist and deeply flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped dates, soaked in 1 cup boiling water + 1 teaspoon baking soda (allow to cool)
  • For the toffee sauce:
    • 1/2 cup brown sugar
    • 1/2 cup heavy cream
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter

Method:

  1. Prepare batter: In a bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In a mixer, cream butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla.
  2. Add dates: Stir in soaked dates along with the soaking liquid. Gradually fold in dry ingredients.
  3. Assemble: Pour batter into a greased 7-inch pan. Cover with foil.
  4. Pressure cook: Place a trivet in the Instant Pot with 1 cup water. Set pan on trivet, lock lid, and cook on high pressure for 25 minutes.
  5. Make toffee sauce: While the pudding cooks, combine sugar, cream, and butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir until smooth and slightly thickened.
  6. Serve: Remove pudding, let cool slightly, and serve with generous amounts of warm toffee sauce.

5. Instant Pot Cheesecake Brownies

Why choose between cheesecake and brownies when you can have both? This decadent dessert combines rich chocolatey brownies with a smooth cheesecake swirl.

Ingredients:

  • For brownies:
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • For cheesecake swirl:
    • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
    • 1/4 cup sugar
    • 1 large egg
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Method:

  1. Brownie batter: Melt butter and combine with sugar. Add eggs and beat well. Mix in cocoa powder, flour, salt, and baking powder.
  2. Cheesecake mixture: Beat cream cheese, sugar, egg, and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Layer and swirl: Pour half the brownie batter into a greased 7-inch pan. Dollop half the cheesecake mixture in spoonfuls. Repeat layers. Swirl lightly with a knife.
  4. Cook: Cover pan tightly with foil and pressure cook in Instant Pot with 1 cup water on high for 35 minutes.
  5. Cool: Allow natural pressure release for 10 minutes. Chill brownies before cutting for clean slices.

Tips for Perfect Instant Pot Desserts Every Time

  • Use a springform pan or silicone molds: These are ideal for easy removal and even cooking.
  • Seal the pan: Wrapping the cake pan in foil helps keep water out and maintains texture.
  • Adjust cooking times: Smaller pans may cook faster. Use natural pressure release for custards and cheesecakes to prevent cracks.
  • Experiment with flavors: Instant Pot desserts adapt well to add-ins like nuts, fruits, and extracts.
  • Moisture is key: Always add at least 1 cup of water for pressure cooking to build steam properly.

Exploring desserts in the Instant Pot elevates weeknight sweets and special occasions alike. The combination of speed, consistent results, and versatility makes this appliance your best friend in the kitchen when it’s time to indulge.

So, heat up that Instant Pot, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself — the perfect dessert doesn’t have to be complicated, just pressure-cooked with love. Happy cooking!
