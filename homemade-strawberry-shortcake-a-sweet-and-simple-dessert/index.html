Homemade Strawberry Shortcake: A Sweet and Simple Dessert


There’s nothing quite like the taste of fresh, juicy strawberries on a warm summer day. And what better way to showcase their natural sweetness than with a homemade strawberry shortcake? This classic dessert is a crowd-pleaser, perfect for any occasion, from backyard barbecues to elegant dinner parties. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create a delicious and visually stunning dessert that will leave your guests asking for seconds.

Ingredients

For the shortcake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 2/3 cup milk

For the strawberries:

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar

For the whipped cream:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  2. Add the cold butter to the dry ingredients. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Pour in the milk and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough shortcakes.

  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times to bring it together. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick round.

  5. Using a round biscuit cutter or a glass, cut out individual shortcakes from the dough. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving a bit of space between each one.

  6. Bake the shortcakes for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Remove them from the oven and let them cool completely.

  7. While the shortcakes are cooling, prepare the strawberries. In a medium bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and sugar. Toss gently to coat the strawberries in the sugar. Let them sit for about 15 minutes to macerate, allowing the strawberries to release their natural juices.

  8. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract together until soft peaks form.

  9. To assemble the strawberry shortcakes, slice each cooled shortcake in half horizontally. Place the bottom half on a plate, spoon some of the macerated strawberries on top, and add a dollop of whipped cream. Place the top half of the shortcake on top and finish with more strawberries and whipped cream.

  10. Serve immediately and enjoy the heavenly combination of tender shortcake, juicy strawberries, and fluffy whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For added flavor, consider adding a splash of lemon juice or a sprinkle of lemon zest to the macerated strawberries.
  • If you prefer a sweeter shortcake, you can increase the amount of sugar in the dough or sprinkle some sugar on top before baking.
  • Feel free to get creative with the presentation of your strawberry shortcakes. You can garnish them with mint leaves, chocolate shavings, or a drizzle of chocolate sauce.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use store-bought whipped cream instead of making your own.

Final Thoughts

Homemade strawberry shortcake is a timeless dessert that never fails to impress. With its buttery, tender shortcakes, juicy strawberries, and billowy whipped cream, it’s a treat that captures the essence of summer. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a sweet indulgence, this recipe is sure to satisfy your taste buds. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create a dessert that will leave a lasting impression on your loved ones. Enjoy!
