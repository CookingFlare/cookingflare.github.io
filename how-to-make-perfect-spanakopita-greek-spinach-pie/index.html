How to Make Perfect Spanakopita: Greek Spinach Pie


Spanakopita, the beloved Greek spinach pie, is a dish that encapsulates the vibrant flavors of Greek cuisine. Made with layers of flaky phyllo pastry, a rich and savory spinach filling, and a touch of creamy feta cheese, this timeless recipe is the star of many kitchen tables. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or looking to elevate your dinner game, mastering spanakopita will not only impress your guests but also transport your taste buds to the Mediterranean.

In this post, I’ll guide you through every step to make perfect spanakopita at home. You’ll learn tips for working with delicate phyllo dough, how to create a flavorful filling, and the secrets to balancing textures and flavors. Let’s dive right in and unlock the art of Greek spinach pie.

The Magic of Spanakopita

Spanakopita is one of Greece’s most famous dishes, loved for its versatility and deeply satisfying taste. Often served as an appetizer or a main dish, this pie combines fresh spinach, creamy feta, and aromatic herbs wrapped in layers of golden, crispy phyllo pastry. While the recipe calls for humble ingredients, the result is nothing short of exceptional.

What makes spanakopita special is its balance of textures: the crunch of the phyllo against the creamy spinach filling creates a symphony that pleases the palate. Plus, the dish can be served hot, warm, or even cold, making it perfect for gatherings, lunches, or even as a snack.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we jump into the recipe, it’s important to gather all your ingredients. Spanakopita relies on quality produce and proper preparation to shine. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Filling:

  • 2 pounds fresh spinach, washed, stemmed, and chopped (or 16 ounces frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 scallions, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • ½ cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 8 ounces feta cheese, crumbled
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

For the Phyllo Pastry:

  • 12 sheets of phyllo dough, thawed according to package instructions
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted (or olive oil for brushing)
  • 1 tablespoon breadcrumbs (optional, to prevent sogginess in the crust)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Spinach Filling

Start by making the flavorful filling. If using fresh spinach, blanch it briefly in boiling water until wilted, then drain and chop. For frozen spinach, ensure it’s fully thawed and squeeze out excess water. The goal is to eliminate moisture to keep the pie from becoming soggy.

In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the chopped onion and scallions until soft and fragrant, about 3 minutes. Add the garlic (if using) and cook for one minute. Stir in the spinach and cook for 5 minutes, allowing any remaining liquid to evaporate. Remove from heat.

While the spinach mixture cools slightly, in a large bowl combine parsley, dill, eggs, and crumbled feta cheese. Season with salt and pepper—remember that feta is salty, so go light on the added salt. Add the cooled spinach mixture to the bowl and stir thoroughly to combine.

2. Prepare the Phyllo Pastry

Phyllo dough can be tricky to work with, so it’s important to handle it with care. Make sure the dough is fully thawed and remove it from the package right before use. Keep sheets covered with a clean, damp towel to prevent them from drying out.

Brush a rectangular baking dish (approximately 9x13 inches) with some melted butter or olive oil. Place the first sheet of phyllo in the dish, letting the edges overlap slightly. Brush the phyllo lightly with melted butter, ensuring full coverage but not saturating it. Repeat this process with six sheets of phyllo, layering them one at a time and brushing each layer with butter.

3. Assemble the Spanakopita

Spread the prepared spinach filling evenly over the layered phyllo base. Use a spatula to smooth the filling so it reaches the edges of the dish.

Layer the remaining six phyllo sheets on top of the filling, brushing each sheet with melted butter as you go. Tuck the edges of the phyllo into the dish to seal the pie. Brush the top layer generously with butter to ensure a golden crust.

For added crispness and to prevent the top layers from puffing up, score the top of the spanakopita with a sharp knife. You can divide it into squares or diamond shapes, but cut only through the top layers of phyllo—not all the way to the filling.

4. Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the spanakopita for 45-50 minutes, or until the phyllo is golden brown and crisp. The aroma of butter and herbs will fill your kitchen as it bakes.

Once finished, remove it from the oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes before serving. This resting period helps the filling set and makes it easier to slice into neat portions.

Tips for Success

1. Handle Phyllo Dough Gently

Phyllo is delicate and prone to tearing. If a sheet tears, don’t worry—layer it as smoothly as possible and continue assembling the pie. Imperfections in the dough won’t affect the flavor.

2. Drain Spinach Thoroughly

Excess moisture is spanakopita’s enemy. Always squeeze out as much liquid as possible from the spinach to keep the pie crisp and flavorful.

3. Use Fresh Herbs

Fresh dill and parsley elevate the dish with their bright, earthy flavors, but if you’re in a pinch, dried versions can be substituted. Adjust the quantity—dried herbs are more concentrated, so use about one-third the amount of fresh.

4. Customize the Filling

Traditional spanakopita features spinach and feta, but some variations include ricotta, cream cheese, or even meats like ground lamb. Feel free to adapt the recipe to suit your personal tastes.

Serving Suggestions

Spanakopita is incredibly versatile. Serve it warm as a main dish alongside a crisp green salad and some tzatziki sauce. For appetizers, cut the pie into smaller squares for bite-sized portions. It pairs beautifully with roasted vegetables, grilled meats, or a glass of refreshing white wine.

This spinach pie is equally delightful as leftovers; you can reheat slices in the oven or enjoy them cold. Spanakopita’s flavors deepen with time, making it a worthwhile dish for meal prep or entertaining a crowd.

Bringing Greek Traditions Home

With its vibrant flavors and decadent textures, spanakopita is a celebration of Greek culinary traditions. This recipe takes time and care, but the reward is a dish that transcends the ordinary. Each bite carries the unmistakable taste of the Mediterranean, reminding us that the simplest ingredients can create something extraordinary.

Prepare your perfect spanakopita today, and relish in the joy of homemade cuisine infused with history, culture, and love. Whether you’re cooking for family or friends, this iconic Greek spinach pie will undoubtedly become one of your cherished recipes.
