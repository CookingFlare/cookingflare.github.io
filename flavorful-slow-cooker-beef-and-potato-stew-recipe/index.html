Flavorful Slow Cooker Beef and Potato Stew Recipe


Are you looking for a comforting and hearty meal that practically cooks itself? Look no further than this delicious Slow Cooker Beef and Potato Stew. Packed with tender beef, flavorful vegetables, and savory broth, this stew is the perfect way to warm up on a chilly day. The best part? You can set it and forget it, allowing the slow cooker to work its magic while you go about your day. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to make this mouthwatering dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cubed
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef: In a large bowl, season the beef stew meat with salt, pepper, and coat with all-purpose flour.

  2. Sear the Beef: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches and sear on all sides until browned. Transfer the beef to the slow cooker.

  3. Saute the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and garlic. Saute for a few minutes until fragrant. Transfer the onion and garlic to the slow cooker.

  4. Combine Ingredients: Add the diced potatoes, sliced carrots, bay leaves, and dried thyme to the slow cooker.

  5. Make the Broth: In a separate bowl, whisk together the beef broth, red wine (if using), and tomato paste. Pour the mixture over the ingredients in the slow cooker.

  6. Cook the Stew: Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, or until the beef and vegetables are tender.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stew is ready, taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Discard the bay leaves and serve the stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired.

Tips for Success

  • For an extra layer of flavor, you can add a splash of Worcestershire sauce or balsamic vinegar to the broth mixture.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water and stir the mixture into the stew during the last 30 minutes of cooking.
  • This stew can be easily customized with additional vegetables such as celery, parsnips, or sweet potatoes.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

The slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a rich and savory stew that will have your whole house smelling amazing. The tender beef, soft potatoes, and hearty vegetables create a satisfying meal that’s perfect for a cozy night in or for feeding a crowd. Plus, the convenience of using a slow cooker makes this recipe a time-saving option for busy days.

So, whether you’re looking for a comforting dinner for your family or a dish to impress your guests, this Flavorful Slow Cooker Beef and Potato Stew is sure to become a new favorite in your recipe repertoire. Give it a try and savor the deliciousness of this classic comfort food!
