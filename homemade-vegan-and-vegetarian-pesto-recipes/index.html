Homemade Vegan and Vegetarian Pesto Recipes


Pesto has long been a favorite among food lovers for its vibrant flavor, versatility, and easy preparation. Traditionally made with basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil, this classic Italian sauce can be tailored to suit various diets, including vegan and vegetarian lifestyles. By substituting or omitting certain ingredients, you can create delicious plant-based versions to accompany a wide range of dishes, from pasta and pizzas to sandwiches and roasted vegetables.

Below, we explore different homemade vegan and vegetarian pesto recipes that celebrate fresh, wholesome ingredients while delivering incredible taste and texture. Whether you’re craving a classic basil pesto or intriguing alternatives using kale, sun-dried tomatoes, or even avocado, these recipes are sure to impress your taste buds.

Classic Vegan Basil Pesto

A rich, herbaceous basil pesto is timeless. By swapping out Parmesan cheese for nutritional yeast or vegan cheese, this recipe keeps the traditional flavor intact while catering to plant-based preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves, tightly packed
  • 3 tablespoons nutritional yeast (or vegan Parmesan cheese)
  • 1/3 cup raw cashews or walnuts (alternative to pine nuts)
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add basil, nutritional yeast, cashews, garlic, and lemon juice to a food processor.
  2. Pulse ingredients until finely chopped.
  3. Slowly drizzle in olive oil while blending, scraping down the sides as necessary.
  4. Continue blending until the pesto reaches your desired creamy consistency. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  5. Store in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to one week. Add a thin layer of olive oil on top to keep the pesto fresh.

Nut-Free Vegan Pesto with Sunflower Seeds

For those with nut allergies, sunflower seeds offer a fantastic alternative while providing the same creamy texture and nutty flavor in pesto recipes. Pair this flavorful pesto with roasted veggies, zoodles, or grilled tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh spinach or kale (or a mix)
  • 1/3 cup roasted sunflower seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Juice of 1 lime or lemon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Water as needed
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine spinach (or kale), roasted sunflower seeds, garlic, and lime juice in a food processor.
  2. Pulse ingredients until finely minced.
  3. Blend while slowly adding olive oil, adjusting thickness with small amounts of water if needed.
  4. Season with salt and pepper according to taste.
  5. Serve immediately or store in an airtight container for up to a week.

Vegetarian Caper and Olive Pesto

This Mediterranean-inspired pesto is perfect for those seeking bold flavors. While vegetarian, it uses Pecorino or Parmesan cheese to achieve a salty, umami kick.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups fresh parsley leaves
  • 1/4 cup capers, drained and rinsed
  • 1/3 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 1/3 cup grated Pecorino or Parmesan cheese
  • 1 small garlic clove
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Pepper to taste, salt as needed (capers and cheese are already salty)

Instructions:

  1. Place parsley, capers, olives, cheese, and garlic in a food processor.
  2. Blend until everything is finely chopped.
  3. During blending, gradually incorporate olive oil to create a smooth texture.
  4. Adjust seasoning with pepper and a little salt if necessary.
  5. Use over pasta, spread on toasted bread, or pair with roasted potatoes.

Creamy Vegan Avocado Pesto

Elevate your pesto game with avocado! This vegan recipe delivers a beautifully creamy texture without relying on dairy. It’s perfect for summer dishes, adding a fresh and buttery element to salads, bread, or veggie bowls.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado, pitted and peeled
  • 2 cups fresh cilantro or basil
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine avocado, cilantro (or basil), garlic, lime juice, and half of the olive oil.
  2. Blend until smooth, adding the remaining olive oil gradually to reach desired consistency.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Best used immediately to preserve the vibrant green color.

Kale and Walnut Vegan Pesto

Perfect for colder months, this nutrient-packed pesto offers a light bitterness from kale balanced by rich, earthy walnuts. High in antioxidants and heart-healthy fats, this recipe is both delicious and nourishing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh kale leaves (stems removed)
  • 1/3 cup toasted walnuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place kale, walnuts, garlic, lemon juice, and nutritional yeast in a food processor.
  2. Pulse until ingredients are finely chopped.
  3. Continue blending and slowly pour in olive oil until smooth.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Use as a pasta sauce, or layer onto grain bowls for added zest.

Vegan Red Pepper and Cashew Pesto

Roasted red peppers take center stage in this vibrant, smoky pesto. Combined with creamy cashews, this unique recipe becomes an irresistible option for dipping, spreading, or drizzling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large roasted red peppers (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/3 cup raw cashews, soaked in water for 30 minutes and drained
  • 1 small garlic clove
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine roasted red peppers, cashews, garlic, and balsamic vinegar.
  2. Blend until ingredients are well combined.
  3. Add olive oil in a steady stream while blending to achieve a creamy consistency.
  4. Adjust seasoning with salt and black pepper as needed.
  5. Use this pesto for sandwiches, as a dip, or drizzled over roasted vegetables.

Versatile Ways to Use Vegan and Vegetarian Pesto

Once you’ve mastered these exciting pesto recipes, the possibilities for using them are endless! Here are some creative ideas to inspire your culinary adventures:

  • Pasta Sauces: Toss pesto with cooked spaghetti, penne, or even zucchini noodles for an easy, vibrant meal.
  • Sandwich Spreads: Replace mayonnaise or mustard with pesto to add a punch of flavor to sandwiches or wraps.
  • Pizza Base: Swap out traditional tomato sauce for pesto to create a unique pizza flavor profile.
  • Condiments for Proteins: Drizzle pesto over roasted tofu, tempeh, or even vegetarian burgers for a flavorful twist.
  • Salad Dressings: Thin out pesto with a little extra olive oil or lemon juice and use it as a zesty salad dressing.

Final Thoughts

Homemade vegan and vegetarian pesto recipes provide a brilliant avenue for experimenting with bold flavors and fresh ingredients. The flexibility of pesto makes it a perfect fit for virtually any dish, while catering to those who value plant-based eating.

Next time you’re looking to liven up your meals, skip the store-bought jars and whip up your own pesto creations at home. Armed with these recipes, you’ll be ready to take your cooking repertoire to the next level while impressing friends, family, and even yourself with the flavors you can create from scratch.
