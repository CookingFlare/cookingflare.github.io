Savory Nut-Free Stuffed Bell Peppers Recipe


If you’re looking for a flavorful and allergy-friendly dish that’s both delicious and satisfying, then you’re in for a treat with this savory nut-free stuffed bell peppers recipe. Whether you have a nut allergy or simply prefer to avoid nuts in your meals, this recipe is a perfect choice for a wholesome and hearty meal that everyone will love.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking dish with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the bell peppers: Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. If the peppers don’t stand upright, you can slice a thin layer off the bottom to create a flat surface.

  3. Cook the filling: In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground beef, breaking it up with a spoon, until it’s no longer pink. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and continue to cook until the onion is soft and translucent.

  4. Combine the ingredients: Stir in the cooked quinoa, diced tomatoes, oregano, basil, paprika, salt, and pepper. Let the mixture simmer for a few minutes until the flavors meld together.

  5. Stuff the bell peppers: Spoon the filling into the prepared bell peppers, pressing down gently to pack the filling. Place the stuffed peppers in the prepared baking dish.

  6. Bake the stuffed peppers: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.

  7. Add the cheese: Remove the foil and sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the tops of the peppers. Return the dish to the oven and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  8. Serve and garnish: Once the stuffed peppers are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian option, you can substitute the ground beef with cooked lentils or your favorite meat alternative.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese to suit your taste preferences.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the filling mixture.

Final Thoughts

These savory nut-free stuffed bell peppers are a fantastic option for a wholesome family dinner or a meal-prep idea for the week. Packed with protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, this recipe is both satisfying and versatile. Whether you have dietary restrictions or simply enjoy a hearty and flavorful meal, these stuffed bell peppers are sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. Enjoy!

By incorporating a combination of savory ingredients and allergy-friendly options, this recipe offers a delicious and satisfying dish for all to enjoy. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or preparing meals for the week, these nut-free stuffed bell peppers are a wholesome and flavorful choice. With a balance of protein, vegetables, and aromatic herbs, this recipe provides a versatile and satisfying option for any occasion.
