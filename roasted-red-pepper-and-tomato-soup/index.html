Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup


There’s something about a bowl of homemade soup that can instantly lift your spirits, especially when the weather turns cold or the days feel a little too hectic. If you’re in the mood for a warm, flavorful meal, look no further than roasted red pepper and tomato soup. This delightful recipe combines the sweetness of roasted red peppers with the rich, tangy goodness of tomatoes, creating a smooth and velvety soup that’s both comforting and satisfying.

Why Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup?

Roasted red peppers bring a unique depth of flavor to any dish. When paired with ripe, juicy tomatoes, they create a perfect balance of sweetness and acidity. The roasting process caramelizes the natural sugars in the peppers, giving them a smoky richness that transforms an ordinary tomato soup into something extraordinary.

This soup is not only packed with flavor, but it’s also incredibly easy to make. Whether you’re looking for a simple weeknight dinner or a dish to impress guests at your next gathering, this roasted red pepper and tomato soup fits the bill. Plus, it’s naturally vegetarian and can easily be made vegan by substituting dairy ingredients. It’s a healthy, wholesome meal that can be enjoyed by everyone.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this mouthwatering roasted red pepper and tomato soup:

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes – fresh, juicy, and in-season for the best flavor.
  • 2 red bell peppers – the stars of the show, adding a smoky, sweet flavor.
  • 1 medium onion – a key aromatic that adds sweetness to the soup.
  • 3 cloves garlic – for a bit of punch and richness.
  • 4 cups vegetable broth – you can also use chicken broth for a deeper flavor.
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil – for roasting the vegetables and adding richness.
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil – a classic herb that complements both tomatoes and peppers.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano – another staple herb that enhances the overall flavor.
  • Salt and pepper – to taste.
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar – optional, but adds a touch of acidity that balances the sweetness.
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream – optional, for a creamy finish (use coconut milk for a dairy-free version).
  • Fresh basil leaves – for garnish, to add a burst of color and freshness.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Roasting the Red Peppers and Tomatoes

The first step in making this roasted red pepper and tomato soup is to roast the vegetables, as this process brings out their natural sweetness and deepens their flavors. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

Start by halving the red bell peppers, removing the seeds and membranes, and placing them cut-side down on a baking sheet. Do the same with the tomatoes, cutting them in half and placing them skin-side down on the same baking sheet. Drizzle the vegetables with a little olive oil and season them with a pinch of salt and pepper.

Roast the vegetables in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes or until the skins of the tomatoes are slightly charred and the peppers have softened and started to blister. The smell of roasting vegetables will fill your kitchen, setting the stage for a delicious soup.

2. Sautéing the Onion and Garlic

While the peppers and tomatoes are roasting, heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until the onions become translucent and soft. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the garlic to release its fragrance without burning.

The sautéed onion and garlic provide a savory foundation for the soup, enhancing the natural sweetness of the roasted vegetables.

3. Peeling the Roasted Peppers and Tomatoes

Once the peppers and tomatoes are roasted, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool for a few minutes. The skins of the tomatoes should peel off easily, and the peppers will have softened enough to remove their skins as well. Use your fingers or a knife to peel off the charred skin of the peppers.

Chop the peeled roasted red peppers into small pieces, and set them aside. Squeeze out any excess juice from the tomatoes and chop them into chunks as well. This step is essential to getting the perfect consistency for your soup.

4. Blending the Soup

Now that the vegetables are roasted and ready, add the chopped tomatoes and red peppers to the sautéed onions and garlic in the pot. Stir everything together, allowing the ingredients to mix and start building a rich, flavorful base.

Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. Let it cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If you prefer a thicker soup, you can cook it a bit longer to reduce the broth and concentrate the flavors.

Once the soup has simmered, use an immersion blender to puree the mixture directly in the pot until it reaches a smooth, velvety texture. If you don’t have an immersion blender, you can carefully transfer the soup to a regular blender in batches. Be sure to let the soup cool slightly before blending to avoid splattering.

5. Finishing Touches

Once the soup is smooth and well-blended, stir in the dried basil, oregano, and a splash of balsamic vinegar (if using). Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

If you prefer a creamier texture, now is the time to stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk. This will add richness and a velvety texture to the soup, making it even more comforting.

6. Serving and Garnishing

Ladle the hot soup into bowls and garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and a burst of freshness. If you like, you can drizzle a little extra cream on top or sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese for an added touch of decadence.

Serve your roasted red pepper and tomato soup with a side of crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich for the ultimate comforting meal. It’s perfect for a cozy lunch, a family dinner, or even as a starter for a more formal gathering.

Tips for the Perfect Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup

  • Use fresh, in-season tomatoes: The quality of tomatoes plays a significant role in the flavor of the soup. When tomatoes are in season, they’ll be juicier and more flavorful, leading to a better overall result.
  • Adjust the sweetness: If you find that the soup is too sweet from the peppers, you can balance it by adding a bit more salt or a splash of lemon juice to cut through the richness.
  • Make it ahead: This soup actually tastes even better the next day after the flavors have had time to meld together. You can make it in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It also freezes well, so consider making a double batch for future meals.
  • Spice it up: For a little kick, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce when blending the soup.

Health Benefits of Roasted Red Pepper and Tomato Soup

Not only is this roasted red pepper and tomato soup incredibly delicious, but it’s also packed with nutrients that can benefit your health.

Rich in Antioxidants

Both tomatoes and red peppers are rich in antioxidants, particularly vitamin C and lycopene. Lycopene is a powerful antioxidant found in tomatoes that is known for its heart-health benefits and potential cancer-fighting properties. Red peppers are also high in beta-carotene, which is converted into vitamin A in the body and supports healthy vision and immune function.

Good for Your Skin

The vitamin C in red peppers and tomatoes helps boost collagen production, promoting healthy, glowing skin. It also helps fight free radicals, which can contribute to the aging process.

Low in Calories, High in Flavor

This soup is a great option for anyone looking to enjoy a flavorful, satisfying meal without consuming too many calories. By using vegetable broth and avoiding heavy cream (unless desired), you can keep the soup light and healthy while still enjoying a rich, comforting dish.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re a soup lover or just looking for a comforting meal to enjoy on a chilly evening, roasted red pepper and tomato soup is a winning choice. With its smoky, sweet flavor, velvety texture, and health benefits, this soup is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

So next time you’re in the mood for something warm and delicious, fire up your oven, roast those peppers and tomatoes, and treat yourself to a bowl of homemade soup that’s full of love and flavor.
