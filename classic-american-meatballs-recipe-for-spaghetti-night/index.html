Classic American Meatballs: Recipe for Spaghetti Night


Spaghetti night is a cherished tradition in many American households, and at the heart of this culinary celebration lies the classic American meatball. Whether you’re hosting a family dinner, preparing a cozy meal for yourself, or planning a gathering with friends, the combination of tender meatballs simmered in rich tomato sauce served over al dente spaghetti is simply irresistible. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of meatballs, share an easy yet delicious recipe, and provide tips to elevate your spaghetti night to the next level.

The History of Meatballs

The meatball, a humble yet beloved dish, boasts a rich history that dates back centuries. While variations exist across different cultures, the American meatball has its roots in Italian cuisine. Italian immigrants brought their family recipes to the United States, where the dish evolved to reflect local tastes and ingredients.

In America, meatballs became a staple in Italian-American cuisine, often served in marinara sauce with spaghetti. Today, they are enjoyed across the nation, transcending cultural boundaries and becoming a comfort food favorite.

The Perfect Meatball Recipe

Now, let’s dive into the recipe for classic American meatballs that are sure to impress. This dish is simple enough for beginners yet flavorful enough to satisfy seasoned cooks. Gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

For the meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 blend recommended for optimal flavor)
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (preferably Italian-style)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for heat)

For the sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Fresh basil for garnish

For serving:

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • Extra grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Meatballs

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped parsley, minced garlic, egg, salt, black pepper, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes. Use your hands to gently mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough meatballs.

  2. Shape the Meatballs: Once mixed, shape the mixture into meatballs, approximately 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. You can use a cookie scoop or your hands for consistent sizing. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  3. Chill the Meatballs: For best results, cover the meatballs with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This helps them hold their shape while cooking.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet or saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, until softened and translucent. Add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional minute, stirring frequently to avoid burning.

  2. Add the Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir to combine. Add the sugar, salt, black pepper, oregano, and basil. Bring the sauce to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 3: Cook the Meatballs

  1. Brown the Meatballs: In a separate skillet, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium-high heat. Working in batches, add the meatballs to the skillet, being careful not to overcrowd them. Brown the meatballs on all sides for about 5 minutes. They don’t need to be cooked through at this stage, as they will finish cooking in the sauce.

  2. Simmer in Sauce: Once browned, transfer the meatballs to the simmering tomato sauce. Cover the skillet and let the meatballs simmer in the sauce for about 25-30 minutes, or until they are fully cooked (internal temperature should reach 160°F).

Step 4: Cook the Spaghetti

  1. Boil the Pasta: While the meatballs are simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.

Step 5: Serve

  1. Combine and Plate: If the sauce is too thick, add a bit of the reserved pasta water to reach your desired consistency. Toss the cooked spaghetti with the sauce until well coated.

  2. Garnish and Enjoy: Serve the spaghetti topped with meatballs, garnished with fresh basil and extra grated Parmesan cheese. Enjoy your classic American meatball spaghetti night!

Tips for the Perfect Meatball Spaghetti

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of your meatballs and sauce greatly depends on the quality of your ingredients. Opt for fresh herbs, high-quality ground meat, and good canned tomatoes.

  • Don’t Skip the Chill: Chilling the meatballs before cooking helps them retain their shape and ensures a tender texture.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to experiment with different seasonings or add-ins, such as chopped bell peppers, grated carrots, or even a splash of Worcestershire sauce to the meat mixture for an added kick.

  • Make-Ahead: Meatballs can be made ahead of time and frozen for a quick weeknight meal. Simply shape them, freeze on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag. Cook from frozen in the sauce, adding a little extra time to ensure they’re heated through.

  • Pair with Sides: Enhance your spaghetti night with sides like garlic bread, a fresh garden salad, or roasted vegetables. These additions turn your meal into a feast.

Conclusion

Classic American meatballs served over spaghetti is a dish that brings comfort and joy to any table. With this easy-to-follow recipe, you can recreate this beloved dish in your own kitchen, impressing your family and friends with your culinary skills. Whether it’s a casual family dinner or a festive gathering, this meatball spaghetti will surely become a staple in your meal rotation.

So, gather your loved ones, embrace the aroma of simmering sauce, and enjoy a delightful spaghetti night filled with laughter, good food, and cherished memories. Happy cooking!
