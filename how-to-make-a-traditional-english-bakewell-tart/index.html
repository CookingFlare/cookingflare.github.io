How to Make a Traditional English Bakewell Tart


The Bakewell tart is a quintessentially British dessert that hails from the charming market town of Bakewell in Derbyshire. With its buttery pastry, rich almond frangipane, and a delightful layer of raspberry jam, this tart is a celebration of classic English baking. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, making a Bakewell tart is an enjoyable and rewarding experience. In this guide, we’ll take you step-by-step through the process of creating this delicious treat, along with tips and tricks to ensure your tart turns out perfectly every time.

Ingredients

For the Pastry

  • 225g (8 oz) plain flour
  • 125g (4.5 oz) unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 75g (3 oz) icing sugar
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons cold water

For the Frangipane

  • 150g (5 oz) unsalted butter, softened
  • 150g (5 oz) caster sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 150g (5 oz) ground almonds
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 tablespoon plain flour

For the Filling

  • 200g (7 oz) raspberry jam (or your preferred jam)
  • Flaked almonds, for decoration (optional)

For the Glaze (optional)

  • 150g (5 oz) icing sugar
  • 1-2 tablespoons water or lemon juice

Step 1: Preparing the Pastry

Make the Pastry Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the plain flour and icing sugar. This will create a light, sweet base for your pastry.

  2. Add the Butter: Add the chilled, cubed unsalted butter to the flour mixture. Using your fingertips, rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. This step is crucial as it creates a flaky texture for your pastry.

  3. Incorporate the Egg Yolk: Add the egg yolk to the mixture and stir until combined. If the mixture appears too dry, add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough; you want a tender pastry.

  4. Chill the Dough: Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in cling film, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This helps to firm up the butter and makes rolling easier.

Step 2: Blind Baking the Pastry

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

  2. Roll Out the Pastry: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry to about 3mm thick. Carefully transfer it to a 23cm (9-inch) tart tin, pressing it into the edges. Trim any excess pastry from the edges.

  3. Blind Bake: Line the pastry with baking paper and fill it with baking beans or uncooked rice to weigh it down. Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes, then remove the paper and beans and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the pastry is lightly golden. Allow it to cool completely.

Step 3: Preparing the Frangipane Filling

  1. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In a mixing bowl, beat the softened unsalted butter and caster sugar together until light and fluffy. This process incorporates air into the mixture, creating a light frangipane.

  2. Add the Eggs: Gradually add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. If the mixture starts to curdle, add a tablespoon of the ground almonds to help stabilize it.

  3. Incorporate the Dry Ingredients: Gently fold in the ground almonds, almond extract, and plain flour until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to a dense texture.

Step 4: Assembling the Tart

  1. Spread the Jam: Once the pastry case has cooled, spread an even layer of raspberry jam over the base. The tartness of the jam beautifully complements the sweetness of the frangipane.

  2. Add the Frangipane: Spoon the frangipane filling over the jam, smoothing it out with a spatula. Use a fork to create a decorative pattern on the surface if desired.

  3. Decorate (Optional): If you like, sprinkle flaked almonds over the top for added texture and flavor.

Step 5: Baking the Tart

  1. Bake: Place the assembled tart in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the frangipane is golden brown and set in the center. A skewer inserted into the middle should come out clean.

  2. Cool: Once baked, remove the tart from the oven and let it cool in the tin for about 10 minutes. Carefully transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 6: Glazing the Tart (Optional)

  1. Prepare the Glaze: In a small bowl, mix the icing sugar with enough water or lemon juice to achieve a smooth, pourable consistency.

  2. Glaze the Tart: Once the tart has cooled, drizzle the glaze over the top, allowing it to set before slicing.

Step 7: Serving Your Bakewell Tart

Your traditional English Bakewell tart is now ready to be enjoyed! Serve it at room temperature, either plain or with a dollop of clotted cream or a scoop of ice cream. It makes a delightful addition to afternoon tea, family gatherings, or simply as a sweet treat to enjoy with a cup of tea or coffee.

Tips for Perfecting Your Bakewell Tart

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of your Bakewell tart will be determined by the quality of your ingredients, especially the butter and almonds. Opt for high-quality unsalted butter and fresh ground almonds for the best results.

  • Experiment with Flavors: While raspberry jam is traditional, feel free to experiment with other flavors such as apricot, cherry, or even lemon curd for a refreshing twist.

  • Store Properly: If you have leftovers (though they might not last long!), store your Bakewell tart in an airtight container at room temperature for up to three days. For longer storage, you can freeze it, but be sure to wrap it tightly in cling film.

  • Presentation Matters: For an elegant touch, dust the cooled tart with icing sugar before serving. Fresh berries or edible flowers can also make beautiful decorations.

Conclusion

Making a traditional English Bakewell tart is a delightful baking project that results in a stunning dessert. With its flaky pastry, luscious frangipane, and sweet jam filling, it’s no wonder this tart has become a beloved classic. Follow the steps outlined in this guide, and you’ll have a beautiful Bakewell tart that is sure to impress your family and friends. Happy baking!
