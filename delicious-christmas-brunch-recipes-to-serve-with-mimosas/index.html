Delicious Christmas Brunch Recipes to Serve with Mimosas


When the holiday season arrives, nothing sets the tone better than a festive Christmas brunch shared with family and friends. Pairing your meal with delightful mimosas adds a touch of sparkle and celebration to an already heartwarming occasion. Whether you’re hosting a lively crowd or enjoying a cozy morning, these brunch recipes bring the perfect blend of comfort, elegance, and indulgence. Let’s explore an array of mouthwatering dishes that complement the light, bubbly freshness of mimosas and transform your Christmas morning into a delicious feast.

The Magic of Christmas Brunch

Christmas brunch is a wonderful opportunity to balance savory and sweet flavors while keeping the mood relaxed yet festive. The trick lies in crafting dishes that are easy to prepare ahead of time and can be assembled or finished quickly. Consider incorporating seasonal ingredients like cranberries, cinnamon, citrus, and fresh herbs to evoke the holiday spirit. Of course, mimosas bring a citrusy fizz that enhances the taste of many brunch classics — from buttery pastries to hearty egg dishes.

1. Cranberry Orange Scones with Whipped Maple Butter

Start your brunch with warm, flaky scones that showcase the vibrant flavors of cranberry and orange. Slightly tart cranberries and zesty orange zest blend beautifully, offering a burst of holiday cheer in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 3/4 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1 tablespoon orange zest
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Whipped Maple Butter:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gently fold in cranberries and orange zest.
  5. In a small bowl, mix cream and vanilla extract, then pour into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  6. Turn dough onto a floured surface, shape into a round disc about 1 inch thick, and cut into eight wedges.
  7. Place wedges on the baking sheet and bake for 18–20 minutes until golden.
  8. While the scones cool, beat softened butter with maple syrup and sea salt until fluffy.
  9. Serve scones warm with the whipped maple butter spread generously.

These scones pair wonderfully with the citrus notes in mimosas, creating a balanced palate that wakes up the senses.

2. Savory Herb and Cheese Strata

This savory strata brings layers of flavor with fresh herbs, sharp cheese, and custardy eggs. You can make it the night before and bake it fresh in the morning, making brunch prep stress-free.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups cubed day-old bread (French or sourdough recommended)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup grated Gruyère cheese
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme leaves
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, cubed

Instructions:

  1. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with butter.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk eggs, milk, mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the cubed bread, cheeses, and fresh herbs, folding gently to combine.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared dish, pressing down to ensure bread soaks the custard.
  5. Dot the top with butter cubes.
  6. Cover with foil and refrigerate overnight.
  7. The next morning, preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake covered for 30 minutes.
  8. Remove foil and bake an additional 20 minutes until the top is golden and the center is set.
  9. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before serving.

This rich, herbaceous baked egg dish is a fantastic savory contrast to the crisp sweetness of mimosas and complements the brunch table beautifully.

3. Smoked Salmon Avocado Toast with Dill and Capers

For a fresh and elegant option, smoked salmon on avocado toast brings a luxurious feel without complicated prep. The creamy avocado and briny capers pair exquisitely with the effervescent bubbly of mimosas.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices good-quality rustic bread, toasted
  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 8 ounces smoked salmon slices
  • 2 tablespoons capers, drained
  • Fresh dill sprigs for garnish
  • Red onion slices (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mash the avocados with lemon juice, salt, and black pepper until creamy but slightly chunky.
  2. Spread avocado evenly onto each toasted bread slice.
  3. Layer smoked salmon generously on top.
  4. Sprinkle capers and thin slices of red onion if used.
  5. Garnish with fresh dill.
  6. Serve immediately so the bread remains crisp.

The light creaminess and delicate smokiness make this dish a refined pairing for a citrus mimosa, lending festive sophistication to your table.

4. Gingerbread Pancakes with Cinnamon Maple Syrup

Turn your traditional pancake routine into a festive celebration with the warm spices of gingerbread. These pancakes are fluffy, aromatic, and full of holiday flavor that complements mimosas well.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 cups buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • Butter or oil for cooking

Cinnamon Maple Syrup:

  • 1 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk dry ingredients: flour, sugar, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, mix buttermilk, eggs, molasses, and melted butter.
  3. Pour wet ingredients into dry and stir until just combined; don’t overmix.
  4. Heat a nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake and cook until bubbles form on top, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Flip and cook another 2 minutes until golden.
  7. For syrup, combine maple syrup and cinnamon in a small saucepan, warm gently.
  8. Serve pancakes stacked with a drizzle of cinnamon maple syrup.

These spiced pancakes add warmth and nostalgia to your brunch, bringing out the complementary orange flavors in your mimosas.

5. Pear and Brie Tart with Honey and Thyme

For a slightly more sophisticated palate, a pear and brie tart shines with its mix of sweet, savory, and earthy flavors. This dish feels special and looks gorgeous if you want to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 2 ripe pears, thinly sliced
  • 8 ounces brie cheese, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll pastry out on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Score a one-inch border around the edges, being careful not to cut through.
  4. Layer the brie slices inside the border, followed by the pear slices.
  5. Drizzle honey evenly over the pears.
  6. Sprinkle thyme leaves and a little black pepper.
  7. Brush the border of pastry with beaten egg for a golden edge.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes until pastry is puffed and golden.
  9. Serve warm or at room temperature.

The honey’s floral sweetness and creamy brie beautifully contrast with the crisp bubbles and acidity of a mimosa, elevating your brunch with layered flavors.

6. Christmas Mimosa Cocktail Variations

Since the star of your brunch spreads is the mimosa, offering a few creative twists provides variety and excitement.

Classic Mimosa:

  • Equal parts chilled champagne and freshly squeezed orange juice.
  • Serve in a flute glass with an orange twist for garnish.

Pomegranate Mimosa:

  • Replace orange juice with pomegranate juice for a tart, jewel-toned version.
  • Garnish with pomegranate seeds or rosemary sprigs.

Blood Orange Mimosa:

  • Use blood orange juice for a vibrant color and slightly sweeter taste.
  • Add a splash of triple sec for extra depth.

Cranberry Ginger Mimosa:

  • Mix cranberry juice with ginger ale and top with champagne.
  • Garnish with fresh cranberries and a lime wedge.

Each version pairs beautifully with the various dishes we’ve explored, creating an inviting, festive atmosphere.

Seasonal Ingredients and Tips

  • Citrus: Oranges, blood oranges, and lemons are abundant in winter, adding bright, fresh notes to dishes and drinks.
  • Fresh Herbs: Dill, thyme, chives, and parsley provide lively green accents and aromatic flavors.
  • Baking Ahead: Scones, strata, and tarts are excellent for preparing the day before, reducing morning stress.
  • Presentation: Use festive serving platters, fresh garnishes, and colorful napkins to enhance the holiday vibe.
  • Dietary Considerations: Offer alternative milk options for the strata or gluten-free flour for scones if needed.

Bringing It All Together

A memorable Christmas brunch served with a variety of flavorful dishes and sparkling mimosas captures the joy of the season. From the first bite of cranberry orange scones to the last sip of a blood orange mimosa, each recipe adds layers of warmth and celebration. These recipes balance sweet, savory, spicy, and fresh elements to satisfy diverse tastes and create a festive feast everyone will savor.

Gather your loved ones, pop open the bubbly, and indulge in this enchanting brunch toasts to the most wonderful time of the year. Cheers to delicious memories and sparkling moments!
