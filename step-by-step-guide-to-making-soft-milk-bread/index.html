Step-by-Step Guide to Making Soft Milk Bread


Achieving soft and pillowy milk bread at home is possible with just a few simple ingredients and techniques. This rich and tender bread originates from Asia and is a favorite worldwide due to its sweet aroma, fluffy texture, and versatility. Perfect for sandwiches, breakfast toast, or even desserts, milk bread is a must-try for anyone who loves baking.

In this guide, you’ll learn how to bake soft milk bread from scratch. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, every step here is crafted to help you master this delightful recipe. All you need is patience, careful attention, and the willingness to enjoy a rewarding journey in the kitchen!

Ingredients

To make soft milk bread, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups (360g) all-purpose or bread flour
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon (6g) salt
  • 2 teaspoons (7g) instant yeast or active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) warm milk (around 110°F or 43°C)
  • 1/3 cup (80ml) heavy cream or full-fat milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 4 tablespoons (60g) unsalted butter, softened

Optional Glaze:

  • 1 egg yolk mixed with 1 tablespoon milk (for a golden crust)

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools ready:

  • Mixing bowls
  • A kitchen scale (optional but recommended for precision)
  • Dough scraper (optional)
  • Stand mixer or hand mixer with dough hooks (optional)
  • Rolling pin
  • Loaf pan (standard size, approximately 9x5 inches)
  • Plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel

The Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by combining the dry ingredients. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. If you’re using active dry yeast, dissolve it in the warm milk beforehand and let it sit for about 5 minutes until foamy.

Next, add the wet ingredients: warm milk, heavy cream, and the egg. Mix everything together using a wooden spoon or spatula until the dough begins to come together. If you have a stand mixer, use the dough hook attachment for this step.

Add the softened butter one tablespoon at a time, kneading or mixing in-between additions until the butter is fully incorporated into the dough. The dough should be soft, smooth, and slightly sticky.

Step 2: Knead the Dough

If using a stand mixer, increase the speed to medium and knead for 8–10 minutes until the dough becomes elastic and pulls away from the sides of the bowl. If kneading by hand, transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and work it with the heel of your hands for 12–15 minutes until smooth and stretchy.

A great way to check if the dough is ready is the “windowpane test.” Stretch a small portion of the dough between your fingers and see if it forms a translucent film without tearing easily. If it does, it’s ready.

Step 3: First Rise

Form the dough into a ball and place it into a lightly greased mixing bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel. Let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free environment for 1–2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

If your kitchen is cold, place the bowl in a slightly warm oven (turn it on for a couple of minutes and then off) to help the dough rise.

Step 4: Shape the Dough

Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it onto a lightly floured surface. Divide the dough into three equal portions (or more if you prefer smaller rolls).

Roll each portion into a ball, and then flatten each into an oval using a rolling pin. Fold the top third of the oval down toward the center, and then fold the bottom third up over the first fold. Turn the folded dough seam-side down, and then roll it slightly to smooth it out. Repeat this for all the portions.

Step 5: Arrange in the Loaf Pan

Grease your loaf pan with a thin layer of butter or oil. Place the dough portions into the pan, seam-side down, side-by-side. Cover the pan loosely with a clean towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise for a second time until it doubles in size. This should take about 45–60 minutes.

Step 6: Preheat the Oven

While the dough is undergoing its second rise, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). If desired, brush the top of the dough with the egg yolk and milk mixture for a glossy golden crust.

Step 7: Bake

Place the loaf pan in the oven and bake for 25–30 minutes, or until the milk bread turns a rich golden brown. You’ll know it’s done when the bread sounds hollow when tapped on the top. If the top browns too quickly during baking, loosely cover it with aluminum foil and continue baking.

Step 8: Cool

Remove the bread from the oven and let it sit in the pan for 10–15 minutes. Then, transfer it onto a wire rack to cool completely. Avoid slicing the bread while it’s still warm, as this could make it tear and lose its fluffy texture.

Tips for Perfect Milk Bread

  • Use warm liquids: Always use warm milk to activate the yeast properly. Too hot, and it may kill the yeast; too cold, and the dough won’t rise efficiently.
  • Consider tangzhong: For extra softness, you can incorporate a tangzhong (a cooked paste made with flour and milk or water). Simply whisk 2 tablespoons flour with 1/4 cup water and heat until it thickens. Let it cool and add it to your dough in Step 1.
  • Experiment with flavors: Add a touch of vanilla extract, almond extract, or even a small amount of honey to the dough for added depth of flavor.

Serving Ideas

Soft milk bread is incredibly versatile! Use it for breakfast paired with your favorite jam and butter, as the base for hearty sandwiches, or transform it into French toast. For a decadent dessert, drizzle slices with condensed milk or layer them with whipped cream and fruits.

Why You’ll Love Homemade Milk Bread

Aside from the joy of baking, homemade milk bread is far superior to store-bought alternatives. Its unmatched softness, subtle sweetness, and customizable flavor make it worth every moment of preparation. Plus, the beautiful golden crust adds visual appeal and aromatic delight to your kitchen!

Take your time with this recipe, and enjoy the process of creating something delicious from scratch. Freshly baked soft milk bread is sure to become a staple in your home that friends and family will love.
