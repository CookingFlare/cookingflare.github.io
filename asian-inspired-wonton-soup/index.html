Asian-Inspired Wonton Soup


Wonton soup is a beloved dish in many parts of Asia, especially in Chinese cuisine, offering a delicious combination of delicate dumplings filled with savory ingredients, served in a rich and aromatic broth. This versatile dish can be enjoyed as a light meal or as an appetizer, and it has earned its place in kitchens around the world due to its incredible flavor and comforting qualities.

In this recipe, we’ll walk you through making homemade wontons from scratch, creating a flavorful broth, and assembling it all to create a bowl of soothing, satisfying soup that will transport your taste buds straight to Asia. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this Asian-inspired wonton soup is perfect for any occasion.

What Is Wonton Soup?

Wonton soup is a traditional Chinese dish consisting of wontons (dumplings) filled with ground meat, typically pork, shrimp, or chicken, which are boiled and served in a savory broth. The soup is known for its soothing and flavorful qualities, making it a perfect dish to enjoy during colder weather, or simply when you’re craving a comforting meal.

Wontons are typically made with thin dough wrappers that enclose a mixture of meat, herbs, and spices. The broth itself is often made with a base of chicken or pork stock, seasoned with soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, and garlic to provide a perfect balance of savory, earthy, and umami flavors.

Why Make Your Own Wonton Soup?

While you can easily buy pre-made wontons and pre-made broth at the store, making your own wonton soup from scratch gives you complete control over the ingredients and flavors. Homemade wontons have a much fresher and tastier taste compared to store-bought options, and crafting the soup yourself allows you to adjust the seasonings to your liking.

Additionally, preparing wonton soup from scratch is a fun and rewarding experience, especially when you share the process with friends or family. Once you’ve tried making it yourself, it’s likely that you won’t want to go back to the store-bought version.

The Components of Wonton Soup

To make the perfect wonton soup, there are three essential components that you’ll need: the wontons (dumplings), the broth, and the garnishes. Let’s break down each element in detail so you can make the best possible wonton soup at home.

How to Make Wontons

Ingredients for the Wonton Filling:

  • 1/2 pound ground pork – This is the traditional meat for wontons, though you can substitute it with shrimp or chicken if desired.
  • 1/4 pound shrimp, finely chopped – Adding shrimp brings a light, delicate flavor that complements the pork perfectly.
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped – These provide a subtle, savory freshness.
  • 2 tablespoons grated ginger – Ginger brings a warm, slightly spicy kick that is key to authentic wonton flavor.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced – Garlic offers a rich, aromatic undertone that deepens the flavor of the filling.
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce – Soy sauce adds depth and umami, making the filling more savory.
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil – A little sesame oil enhances the wonton filling with a rich, nutty aroma.
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper – White pepper has a slightly milder, less pungent flavor than black pepper, making it ideal for wonton fillings.
  • 1 egg – Acts as a binder for the filling, holding all the ingredients together.

Wonton Wrappers:

  • You can buy wonton wrappers from most grocery stores or Asian supermarkets. Alternatively, you can make your own if you prefer. However, for convenience, store-bought wrappers work just fine for this recipe.

How to Assemble the Wontons:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped shrimp, green onions, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, white pepper, and egg. Mix everything together thoroughly until the filling is smooth and well combined. You can use your hands to make sure everything is evenly incorporated.

  2. Prepare the wrappers: Lay out the wonton wrappers on a clean surface. Place a teaspoon of the filling in the center of each wrapper. Be careful not to overstuff them, as they may tear during cooking.

  3. Shape the wontons: To fold the wontons, dip your finger in water and run it around the edges of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper in half to form a triangle, pressing the edges together tightly to seal. For added security, bring the two corners of the triangle together to form a pouch shape and pinch them to seal. Repeat this process for the remaining wrappers and filling.

  4. Prepare for cooking: Once all the wontons are filled and shaped, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. If you’re not planning to cook them immediately, you can freeze the wontons at this stage to use later.

Making the Broth

The broth is a vital part of wonton soup, as it provides the base flavor that enhances the wontons and brings everything together.

Ingredients for the Broth:

  • 4 cups chicken stock – You can use homemade chicken stock for the best flavor, or store-bought stock if you’re in a pinch. Either way, the stock should be rich and flavorful.
  • 2 cups water – To dilute the stock slightly and create a more balanced broth.
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce – Adds saltiness and umami to the broth.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil – Imparts a toasty, nutty flavor that complements the wontons.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced – For a rich aromatic base in the broth.
  • 1 small piece of ginger, sliced thinly – Ginger adds a warm, slightly spicy flavor that complements the wontons.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar – A small amount of rice vinegar provides a hint of acidity, helping to balance the richness of the broth.
  • 2-3 sprigs cilantro – Adds a fresh, herbaceous note that brightens up the entire dish.
  • Salt and pepper to taste – Adjust the seasoning of the broth to your preference.

How to Make the Broth:

  1. Prepare the base: In a large pot, combine the chicken stock and water. Bring it to a gentle boil over medium heat.

  2. Add aromatics: Add the garlic, ginger, and cilantro sprigs to the pot. Allow the broth to simmer for about 15-20 minutes so the flavors can infuse and develop.

  3. Season: Once the broth has simmered, add the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and salt and pepper to taste. Stir to combine, then taste the broth and adjust the seasonings if necessary. You want a savory, slightly tangy broth with a deep umami flavor.

  4. Strain the broth: Remove the ginger, garlic, and cilantro sprigs from the broth using a fine-mesh strainer. This will leave you with a clean, flavorful broth.

Cooking the Wontons

Now that you’ve prepared your wontons and broth, it’s time to cook the dumplings and assemble the soup.

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil: In a separate pot, bring a large amount of water to a boil. Add the wontons to the boiling water in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot. Gently stir to prevent the wontons from sticking together.

  2. Cook the wontons: Boil the wontons for about 4-5 minutes, or until they float to the surface and are cooked through. When done, remove the wontons from the water with a slotted spoon and set them aside.

  3. Assemble the soup: Divide the cooked wontons between serving bowls. Ladle the hot broth over the wontons, making sure they’re submerged in the flavorful liquid.

Garnishes and Serving Suggestions

Wonton soup is often topped with simple garnishes that add flavor, color, and texture. Here are some great options:

  • Chopped green onions: Adds freshness and a mild onion flavor.
  • Cilantro leaves: Provides a fresh, aromatic note that contrasts with the rich broth.
  • Chili oil or sliced fresh chili peppers: Adds a spicy kick if you prefer some heat.
  • Toasted sesame seeds: For a bit of crunch and an extra touch of nuttiness.

Serve your wonton soup with a side of steamed rice or a simple vegetable stir-fry to complete the meal. This dish is perfect for sharing with friends and family or enjoying on your own for a cozy dinner at home.

Tips for the Best Wonton Soup

  • Make-ahead wontons: You can prepare the wontons ahead of time and freeze them. When you’re ready to cook them, just drop them directly into boiling water from the freezer – no need to thaw them first.
  • Broth variations: You can adjust the flavors of the broth by adding things like star anise, soy sauce, or miso paste to enhance its complexity. For a more intense flavor, consider simmering the broth for an hour or two to concentrate the flavors.
  • Customize the filling: While pork and shrimp are classic, feel free to experiment with different fillings. You can use ground beef, turkey, or even tofu for a vegetarian version.
  • Adjust the texture: If you prefer your wontons a little chewier, use less water in the filling, or if you like them softer, add a bit more moisture.

Final Thoughts

Asian-inspired wonton soup is a dish that’s both comforting and flavorful, offering a perfect balance of textures and tastes. From the tender wontons to the rich and savory broth, every bite is a little taste of heaven. By making everything from scratch, you can enjoy a much fresher, tastier version than store-bought options, and you’ll be able to customize it to suit your tastes perfectly.

Whether you’re serving it as a starter for a dinner party or enjoying it solo as a light meal, this recipe for wonton soup is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, get ready to roll up your sleeves, and dive into the world of homemade wonton soup—you won’t regret it!
