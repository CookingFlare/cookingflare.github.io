Zesty Lemongrass Pork Stir Fry


Are you craving a delicious and flavorful dish that will tantalize your taste buds? Look no further than this mouthwatering Zesty Lemongrass Pork Stir Fry recipe. Packed with bold Asian-inspired flavors and vibrant colors, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household. The combination of lemongrass, tender pork, and an array of fresh vegetables creates a harmonious and satisfying meal that is perfect for any day of the week.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork tenderloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, finely minced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 100g snow peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Pork:

    • In a bowl, combine the sliced pork tenderloin with the minced lemongrass, garlic, soy sauce, and fish sauce. Mix well to ensure the pork is evenly coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables:

    • Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add a splash of oil and stir-fry the sliced bell peppers, onion, carrot, and snow peas until they are slightly tender yet still crisp. Remove the vegetables from the wok and set aside.

  3. Cook the Pork:

    • In the same wok, add a bit more oil if needed. Stir-fry the marinated pork mixture until the meat is cooked through and slightly caramelized.

  4. Combine and Season:

    • Return the cooked vegetables to the wok with the pork. Add the oyster sauce, brown sugar, and chili flakes. Toss everything together until well combined and the sauce coats the ingredients evenly.

  5. Serve:

    • Garnish the Zesty Lemongrass Pork Stir Fry with fresh basil leaves for an extra burst of flavor. Serve hot over steamed rice and enjoy the delightful blend of aromas and tastes.
