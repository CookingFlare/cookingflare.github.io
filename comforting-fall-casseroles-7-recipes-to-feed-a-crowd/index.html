Comforting Fall Casseroles: 7 Recipes to Feed a Crowd


As the leaves start to change and the crispness of fall settles in, nothing beats the comfort of a hearty casserole. These dishes are not only warm and filling but also perfect for feeding a crowd. Whether you’re hosting a family dinner, a casual get-together, or looking to bring something to a potluck, fall casseroles have you covered. From savory to creamy, these recipes will soon become favorites in your fall cooking repertoire. Here are seven comforting casseroles to feed a crowd and bring a little extra warmth to your autumn gatherings.

1. Classic Mac and Cheese Casserole

Mac and cheese is a quintessential comfort food, and when it’s baked into a casserole, it becomes even more indulgent. This mac and cheese casserole is loaded with creamy cheese sauce and a crunchy breadcrumb topping, making it the ultimate dish for fall gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Cook the macaroni according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Add flour and whisk to form a roux. Cook for about 1 minute until the mixture is bubbly.
  4. Slowly pour in the milk and heavy cream, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Bring to a simmer and cook until the sauce thickens, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cheddar cheese, mustard powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Continue stirring until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce is smooth.
  6. Add the cooked macaroni to the sauce, mixing until evenly coated. Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish.
  7. In a small bowl, combine panko breadcrumbs with melted butter and sprinkle over the casserole.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Chicken and Wild Rice Casserole

This chicken and wild rice casserole is a delicious blend of tender chicken, earthy wild rice, and a creamy sauce that will have everyone coming back for seconds. It’s a comforting, hearty dish that works well as a main course or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked wild rice
  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and diced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the onion and garlic in a little olive oil over medium heat until softened, about 3-4 minutes. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked wild rice, chicken, sautéed vegetables, peas, chicken broth, heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  5. Top with breadcrumbs and bake for 25-30 minutes or until the casserole is bubbly and the top is golden brown.
  6. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

3. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Casserole

This sweet potato and black bean casserole is perfect for a vegetarian or plant-based crowd. It’s packed with flavor, nutrients, and a good balance of textures. The combination of creamy sweet potatoes and savory black beans creates a filling dish that’s sure to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups cooked black beans (or one 15-ounce can, drained and rinsed)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Boil the cubed sweet potatoes in a large pot of salted water until tender, about 15 minutes. Drain and mash them with a fork or potato masher.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, black beans, corn, salsa, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread the mixture into the prepared baking dish, smoothing it out into an even layer.
  5. Sprinkle the shredded Monterey Jack cheese evenly on top.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired, and serve hot.

4. Beef and Potato Casserole

This beef and potato casserole is a great comfort food option, with layers of seasoned ground beef, tender potatoes, and a rich, cheesy sauce. It’s filling, hearty, and sure to please even the hungriest crowd.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cups thinly sliced potatoes
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef and onion over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Drain excess fat and season with garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the cream of mushroom soup with milk until smooth.
  4. In the prepared baking dish, layer half of the sliced potatoes, followed by half of the ground beef mixture, and then pour half of the soup mixture over the top. Repeat the layers.
  5. Cover the casserole with aluminum foil and bake for 40 minutes.
  6. Remove the foil, sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese on top, and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

5. Broccoli and Cheddar Rice Casserole

This broccoli and cheddar rice casserole is a creamy, cheesy delight that makes a fantastic side dish or a light main course. The combination of tender broccoli, fluffy rice, and sharp cheddar cheese makes for a winning comfort food recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets, steamed
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, steamed broccoli, shredded cheddar cheese, cream of chicken soup, sour cream, and chicken broth. Stir to combine and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top.
  4. Sprinkle the Parmesan cheese on top and bake for 25-30 minutes or until the casserole is bubbly and the top is golden.
  5. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

6. Turkey and Stuffing Casserole

This turkey and stuffing casserole is the perfect way to repurpose leftover turkey, especially after a fall holiday like Thanksgiving. It’s a simple yet delicious dish, with savory stuffing and tender turkey topped with a creamy sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 4 cups prepared stuffing (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of chicken soup
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the shredded turkey, prepared stuffing, cream of chicken soup, chicken broth, sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the casserole is heated through and the top is golden brown.

7. Butternut Squash and Spinach Lasagna

For a vegetarian twist on a classic, this butternut squash and spinach lasagna is the perfect fall casserole. The sweetness of the butternut squash pairs beautifully with the rich spinach and creamy ricotta cheese layers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 9 lasagna noodles, cooked
  • 3 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Boil the butternut squash cubes in a large pot of salted water until soft, about 15 minutes. Drain and mash.
  3. Sauté the spinach in a pan with a little olive oil until wilted.
  4. In a bowl, mix the mashed squash, ricotta cheese, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  5. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce in the bottom of the baking dish.
  6. Layer lasagna noodles, the squash-ricotta mixture, spinach, mozzarella cheese, and marinara sauce. Repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes to brown the top.

These seven comforting fall casseroles are the perfect way to feed a crowd and embrace the flavors of the season. Whether you’re craving something creamy, cheesy, or a bit more hearty, there’s a casserole recipe here to satisfy every taste. These dishes are simple to make, versatile, and sure to be a hit at any autumn gathering. Happy cooking, and enjoy the warmth and comfort that comes with a homemade casserole!
