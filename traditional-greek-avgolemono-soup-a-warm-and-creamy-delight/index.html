Traditional Greek Avgolemono Soup: A Warm and Creamy Delight


This recipe for Traditional Greek Avgolemono Soup will transport you to the sunny shores of Greece with its rich flavors and comforting warmth. Made with simple ingredients and bursting with lemony goodness, this soup is a classic dish that has been enjoyed for generations. Whether you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or just looking to try something new, this Avgolemono Soup is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 4 cups water
  • 4 large eggs
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add the chicken pieces, onion, carrots, celery, chicken broth, and water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 1 hour, or until the chicken is fully cooked and tender. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  3. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside to cool. Once cooled, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces and discard the bones.
  4. Return the shredded chicken back to the pot and add the rice. Cook for an additional 15 minutes, or until the rice is tender.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and lemon juice until well combined. Slowly ladle a cup of hot broth from the pot into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to temper the eggs.
  6. Gradually pour the egg mixture back into the pot, stirring gently to combine. Be careful not to let the soup come to a boil, as this may cause the eggs to curdle.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and let the soup simmer for another 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  8. Serve the Avgolemono Soup hot, garnished with fresh parsley. The soup can be enjoyed on its own or accompanied by crusty bread for a heartier meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, you can replace the chicken broth with vegetable broth and omit the chicken. Add more vegetables such as zucchini or spinach to enhance the flavor and nutritional value.
  • If you prefer a thicker consistency, you can add a cornstarch slurry to the soup. Mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water until smooth, then slowly stir it into the soup and let it simmer for a few more minutes until thickened.
  • To make the soup even more flavorful, you can add a bay leaf, a clove of garlic, or a pinch of nutmeg during the cooking process.
  • Leftover soup can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop, adding a little extra broth or water if needed.

Enjoy the delicious taste of Traditional Greek Avgolemono Soup and let its comforting flavors transport you to the vibrant streets of Greece. This recipe is a true celebration of Greek cuisine and its ability to bring people together over a warm and creamy bowl of soup. Share this delightful dish with your loved ones and create lasting memories around the dinner table.
