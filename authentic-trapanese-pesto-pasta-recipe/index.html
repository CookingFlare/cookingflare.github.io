Authentic Trapanese Pesto Pasta Recipe


If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, you’ve probably tried traditional pesto, the bright and aromatic sauce made with basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan, and olive oil. However, if you want to elevate your pesto game and explore a unique regional variation, then Trapanese pesto, hailing from the beautiful island of Sicily, is a must-try. This variation, known for its rich, nutty flavor and vibrant color, is a deliciously fresh twist on the classic. In this blog post, we will delve into how to make authentic Trapanese pesto pasta and take your culinary skills to the next level.

What is Trapanese Pesto?

Trapanese pesto, also known as “Pesto alla Trapanese,” is a traditional Sicilian sauce that dates back to the 18th century. Unlike the typical Genoese pesto, which uses basil as its primary herb, Trapanese pesto is made with fresh, ripe tomatoes, almonds, and garlic. The ingredients are blended together to create a pesto that’s creamy yet tangy, savory yet subtly sweet, and incredibly aromatic. This distinctive sauce pairs wonderfully with a variety of pastas, but its true charm comes to life when combined with short pasta varieties like busiate (Sicilian twisted pasta), fusilli, or orecchiette.

The Key Ingredients for Trapanese Pesto

The magic of Trapanese pesto lies in its simplicity. Let’s break down the essential ingredients for this authentic Sicilian sauce:

  • Ripe Tomatoes: The base of Trapanese pesto is made from ripe, juicy tomatoes, which give the sauce its vibrant red color and slightly sweet, tangy flavor. You can use fresh, in-season tomatoes if you’re making this recipe during the summer months, or opt for high-quality canned tomatoes if fresh ones are unavailable.

  • Almonds: The nuts that give this pesto its unique texture and flavor are almonds, often sourced from Sicily. The rich, buttery taste of almonds adds a distinctive depth to the pesto.

  • Garlic: Garlic is another important ingredient, providing that pungent, aromatic kick that balances the sweetness of the tomatoes and the richness of the almonds.

  • Basil: While basil isn’t the main ingredient in Trapanese pesto, it still plays a supporting role in bringing freshness and a peppery bite to the sauce.

  • Olive Oil: A good-quality extra virgin olive oil is crucial for bringing all the ingredients together and giving the pesto its silky consistency.

  • Pecorino Cheese: Pecorino, a sharp, salty cheese made from sheep’s milk, is used to add complexity and a salty kick to the sauce. Some recipes may also include Parmigiano Reggiano as a complement.

  • Salt and Pepper: These basic seasonings are essential to balance the flavors and bring out the richness of the pesto.

With these ingredients, you’re ready to create an authentic Trapanese pesto pasta that will transport you straight to the sun-drenched shores of Sicily.

How to Make Authentic Trapanese Pesto

Step 1: Prepare the Tomatoes

Start by blanching your tomatoes to remove the skins. To do this, bring a pot of water to a boil and make small “X” incisions at the bottom of each tomato. Once the water is boiling, add the tomatoes and let them cook for about 30 seconds, or until the skin starts to peel away. Remove the tomatoes from the boiling water and transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.

Once cooled, peel off the skins and roughly chop the tomatoes. You should have about two cups of chopped tomatoes to form the base of your pesto.

Step 2: Toast the Almonds

In a dry pan over medium heat, toast the almonds lightly. Stir constantly to ensure they don’t burn. Toasting the almonds intensifies their flavor and adds a slightly smoky note to the pesto. Once toasted, remove them from the heat and let them cool down before using them in the pesto.

Step 3: Prepare the Garlic and Basil

Peel the garlic cloves and set them aside. If you’re using fresh basil, rinse the leaves and pat them dry. For an extra fragrant pesto, you can lightly bruise the basil leaves with your fingers to release their oils.

Step 4: Blend the Pesto

Now it’s time to bring all the ingredients together. In a food processor or blender, add the peeled tomatoes, toasted almonds, garlic cloves, fresh basil, and a generous pinch of salt and pepper. Pulse a few times until everything is roughly chopped and combined.

Next, slowly add the extra virgin olive oil in a steady stream, continuing to pulse until you achieve a smooth, creamy consistency. Be careful not to overblend; you want the pesto to have a little texture and not be too runny.

Finally, add the Pecorino cheese and pulse one more time to incorporate it. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more salt, pepper, or olive oil if needed.

Step 5: Cook the Pasta

While making the pesto, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add your pasta of choice, such as busiate, fusilli, or orecchiette, and cook according to the package instructions, ensuring the pasta is al dente.

Once cooked, reserve about a cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.

Step 6: Combine the Pasta with Pesto

Return the drained pasta to the pot or a large mixing bowl. Add the freshly made Trapanese pesto, stirring gently to coat the pasta. If the pesto seems too thick, you can add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up and create a silkier texture. Toss until all the pasta is evenly coated with the vibrant red sauce.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

To serve, plate the pasta in individual bowls. You can top it off with a sprinkle of extra Pecorino cheese, a drizzle of olive oil, and a few extra basil leaves for garnish. Serve with a side of crusty bread to mop up any remaining pesto sauce.

Why Trapanese Pesto Works So Well

The unique combination of ingredients in Trapanese pesto results in a flavor profile that is unlike any other pesto variation. The tomatoes bring a juicy, natural sweetness that is balanced by the nuttiness of the almonds and the subtle bitterness of the basil. The garlic offers a savory punch that complements the rich texture of the extra virgin olive oil. Finally, the Pecorino adds a sharpness that cuts through the richness, elevating the sauce to new heights.

Moreover, the texture of Trapanese pesto is something to behold. It’s thicker than Genoese pesto, which is often more liquid in consistency, yet still spreadable and smooth. The almonds give it a slight graininess, which adds an interesting element to the dish.

Trapanese pesto also has an advantage over traditional pesto in terms of versatility. While pesto alla Genovese is often reserved for pasta, Trapanese pesto works well as a topping for grilled vegetables, meat, or even as a spread on sandwiches. It can be used in a wide variety of dishes, making it a staple in Sicilian cooking.

Pairing Trapanese Pesto Pasta

This Trapanese pesto pasta pairs beautifully with a range of drinks, both alcoholic and non-alcoholic. If you’re opting for a wine, consider a light and crisp white wine, such as a Sicilian Grillo or a Verdicchio. These wines will complement the richness of the pesto without overpowering the delicate flavors. For something a little bolder, a dry Rosé can also be an excellent match.

If you prefer a non-alcoholic option, an iced herbal tea with lemon or a sparkling water infused with fresh citrus will provide a refreshing contrast to the richness of the pesto.

Trapanese Pesto Variations

While the basic recipe for Trapanese pesto is delightful as is, feel free to experiment with different variations to make it your own. Here are a few ideas:

  • Roasted Red Peppers: Add roasted red peppers to the pesto for a smokier, slightly sweet flavor.
  • Sun-Dried Tomatoes: Use sun-dried tomatoes for a more concentrated, intense tomato flavor.
  • Other Nuts: If you don’t have almonds on hand, you can substitute them with pine nuts or walnuts for a different flavor profile.
  • Vegan Version: To make this recipe vegan, simply skip the Pecorino cheese or replace it with a plant-based cheese substitute. Nutritional yeast can also be a great addition for that cheesy, umami flavor.

Conclusion

Trapanese pesto pasta is an incredibly flavorful and simple dish that showcases the vibrant culinary heritage of Sicily. With its combination of fresh tomatoes, almonds, garlic, and Pecorino, this pesto offers a unique twist on the classic Italian sauce that will delight your taste buds. Whether you’re looking to impress guests with an authentic Italian dish or simply want to explore the flavors of Sicily, this Trapanese pesto pasta recipe is a must-try.

So, next time you’re in the mood for pasta, step away from the traditional pesto and indulge in the bright, rich, and nutty flavors of Trapanese pesto. Your palate will thank you!
