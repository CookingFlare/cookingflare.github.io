Indian Samosas with Spiced Potatoes and Peas Recipe


Samosas are a popular Indian snack that is loved by people all over the world. These delicious, crispy pastries are filled with a flavorful mixture of spiced potatoes and peas, making them the perfect appetizer or tea-time treat. In this recipe, I will guide you through the process of making homemade samosas that are sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • For the dough:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • Water, as needed

  • For the filling:

    • 3 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1/2 cup peas, boiled
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
    • Salt, to taste
    • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

Preparing the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
  2. Add the vegetable oil and mix it into the flour until it resembles crumbs.
  3. Gradually add water, a little at a time, and knead until you have a smooth, pliable dough.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Making the Filling:

  1. Heat some vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Add the chopped onions and green chilies to the pan and sauté until the onions turn translucent.
  4. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  5. Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala to the pan. Mix well.
  6. Add the mashed potatoes and boiled peas to the pan. Season with salt.
  7. Mix everything together until well combined and cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  8. Remove the pan from heat and let the filling cool completely.

Assembling the Samosas:

  1. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  2. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, approximately 6-7 inches in diameter.
  3. Cut the circle in half, creating two semi-circles.
  4. Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, overlapping the edges to seal it.
  5. Fill the cone with a spoonful of the prepared filling, ensuring not to overfill it.
  6. Moisten the edges of the dough cone with water and pinch them together to seal the samosa.
  7. Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling.

Frying the Samosas:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or kadhai over medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide a few samosas into the pan.
  3. Fry the samosas until they turn golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  4. Remove the fried samosas from the oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  5. Repeat the frying process with the remaining samosas.

Serving Suggestions

Serve the hot and crispy samosas with your favorite chutneys, such as mint chutney or tamarind chutney. They can be enjoyed as a standalone snack or as a part of a meal. These samosas are perfect for parties, potlucks, or even as a quick evening snack.

Conclusion

Making Indian samosas at home may seem like a daunting task, but with this easy and delicious recipe, you can recreate the authentic flavors of India in your own kitchen. The combination of spiced potatoes and peas encased in a crispy pastry is simply irresistible. So, why not give it a try and surprise your loved ones with these delectable treats? Enjoy!
