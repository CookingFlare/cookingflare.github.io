Easter Dinner Made Easy: One-Pan Recipes for a Stress-Free Meal


Are you tired of spending hours in the kitchen preparing an elaborate Easter dinner? Do you want to enjoy the day with your loved ones rather than being stuck at the stove? Look no further! In this blog post, we bring you a collection of one-pan recipes that will make your Easter dinner a breeze. These recipes are not only delicious but also easy to prepare, leaving you with more time to relax and enjoy the holiday festivities.

One-Pan Honey Glazed Ham

Let’s start with the star of the Easter dinner - the ham. This one-pan honey glazed ham recipe is not only mouthwatering but also incredibly simple to make. Begin by preheating your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the ham in a roasting pan and pour one cup of water into the bottom of the pan. Cover the ham with aluminum foil and bake for two hours.

In a small saucepan, combine ½ cup of honey, ¼ cup of brown sugar, 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard, 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar, and a pinch of ground cloves. Heat the mixture over medium heat until the sugar has dissolved. Remove the foil from the ham and brush the glaze all over the surface. Return the ham to the oven, uncovered, and bake for an additional 30 minutes, or until the glaze is caramelized and sticky.

One-Pan Lemon Herb Roasted Chicken

If you prefer poultry over ham, this one-pan lemon herb roasted chicken recipe is sure to impress. Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a small bowl, mix together 2 tablespoons of melted butter, the juice of one lemon, and a teaspoon each of dried thyme, rosemary, and oregano.

Place a whole chicken (about 4 pounds) in a roasting pan and season it generously with salt and pepper. Pour the lemon herb mixture over the chicken, making sure to coat it evenly. Add quartered potatoes, carrots, and onions around the chicken for a complete meal. Roast for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until the chicken is golden brown and the vegetables are tender.

One-Pan Spring Vegetable Medley

No Easter dinner is complete without a vibrant and fresh vegetable side dish. This one-pan spring vegetable medley is packed with seasonal flavors and is a breeze to make. Start by heating some olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add sliced carrots, snap peas, asparagus, and cherry tomatoes to the skillet. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of red pepper flakes for a kick.

Sauté the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. Finish off with a squeeze of lemon juice and a sprinkle of chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley or dill. Serve this colorful medley alongside your main course for a burst of freshness on your Easter table.

One-Pan Lemon Bars

Finally, let’s not forget about dessert! These one-pan lemon bars are a tangy and sweet treat that will satisfy your Easter sweet tooth. Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (180°C). In a mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour, ½ cup of powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt. Cut in ½ cup of cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

Press the mixture into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch baking dish and bake for 15 minutes, or until lightly golden. Meanwhile, whisk together 2 large eggs, 1 cup of granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, and the zest of one lemon. Pour the mixture over the baked crust and return it to the oven for an additional 20 minutes, or until set.

Conclusion

This Easter, treat yourself to a stress-free and delicious dinner with these one-pan recipes. From a succulent honey glazed ham to a vibrant spring vegetable medley, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and leave you with more time to enjoy the festivities. And don’t forget to indulge in a tangy and sweet one-pan lemon bar for dessert. Happy Easter and happy cooking!
