Easy Shrimp Lo Mein in 20 Minutes


If you’re looking for a quick and flavorful dinner that is packed with savory goodness, look no further than this Easy Shrimp Lo Mein. This recipe is perfect for busy weeknights when you want to enjoy a delicious, satisfying meal without spending hours in the kitchen. With simple ingredients and a preparation time of just 20 minutes, you can enjoy a mouthwatering shrimp lo mein that rivals takeout.

Why This Recipe Is Perfect for Busy Days

This shrimp lo mein recipe is not only easy to make, but it also delivers on flavor and nutrition. Here’s why you’ll love it:

  1. Fast and Simple: You can have this meal ready in 20 minutes. It’s an excellent option when you don’t want to compromise on taste or spend too much time cooking.
  2. Flavorful: The combination of tender shrimp, crisp vegetables, and savory sauce creates a dish bursting with flavor. The umami of the soy sauce and the slight sweetness of the hoisin sauce work together to create the perfect balance.
  3. Healthy Option: Packed with protein from the shrimp and filled with colorful veggies, this shrimp lo mein is a wholesome meal that’s both delicious and nutritious.
  4. Customizable: Want to add more vegetables or change the protein? This recipe is incredibly versatile. You can swap the shrimp for chicken, beef, or tofu, or toss in extra vegetables like mushrooms, snow peas, or bell peppers.

Now that you know why this shrimp lo mein is a must-try, let’s dive into how to make it!

Ingredients

For this recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Shrimp Lo Mein:

  • 8 oz lo mein noodles (or spaghetti if you can’t find lo mein noodles)
  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium carrots, julienned
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions (scallions), for garnish

For the Lo Mein Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (low-sodium preferred)
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons of water (to thicken the sauce)

How to Make Easy Shrimp Lo Mein in 20 Minutes

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by preparing all your ingredients before you start cooking. This will make the process smooth and quick.

  • Cook the Noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the lo mein noodles (or spaghetti if you’re using that), and cook according to the package instructions, usually about 4-6 minutes. Once cooked, drain the noodles and set them aside. If you want to prevent the noodles from sticking together, toss them with a bit of oil after draining.
  • Prep the Shrimp and Veggies: While the noodles are cooking, peel and devein the shrimp. Slice the onion, bell pepper, and carrots into thin strips. Mince the garlic. Set everything aside and have them ready for cooking.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and black pepper. Stir until the sugar is dissolved. Add the cornstarch slurry (cornstarch mixed with water) to the bowl and whisk everything together. This will help thicken the sauce as it cooks. Set the sauce aside.

Step 3: Cook the Shrimp and Vegetables

In a large skillet or wok, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and are cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set them aside.

In the same skillet, add the sliced onions, bell peppers, and carrots. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 3-4 minutes until they start to soften but still retain some crunch. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds, until fragrant.

Step 4: Combine Everything

Once the vegetables are cooked, return the shrimp to the skillet. Add the cooked noodles and the sauce to the pan, stirring everything together until well combined. The sauce will begin to thicken and coat the noodles and shrimp. Stir for an additional 2 minutes to ensure everything is heated through and evenly coated in sauce.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Remove the skillet from the heat and sprinkle the dish with sliced green onions. You can also add some sesame seeds for extra texture and flavor, if desired. Serve the shrimp lo mein hot and enjoy!

Tips for the Best Shrimp Lo Mein

  • Don’t Overcook the Shrimp: Shrimp cooks quickly, so be careful not to overcook it. Once it turns pink and opaque, it’s done. Overcooked shrimp can become tough and rubbery, so be mindful of the cooking time.
  • Customize the Veggies: Feel free to add more vegetables based on what you have on hand. Mushrooms, snap peas, or broccoli would all make excellent additions.
  • Use Fresh Noodles: If you can find fresh lo mein noodles at your grocery store, they will add an extra level of authenticity and flavor to the dish. However, dried noodles work perfectly fine and are more commonly available.
  • Make It Spicy: If you like a bit of heat, add a teaspoon of chili paste or some red pepper flakes to the sauce. Adjust the level of spice according to your taste preferences.
  • Prep Ahead: To save even more time, you can prep the vegetables and shrimp ahead of time. Store the shrimp in the refrigerator and the pre-sliced veggies in an airtight container. When you’re ready to cook, it will be a breeze to put everything together.

Nutritional Benefits of Shrimp Lo Mein

This shrimp lo mein is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients that can support a balanced diet:

  • Shrimp: Shrimp is a lean source of protein and low in calories. It’s also rich in essential nutrients like vitamin B12, iodine, and selenium.
  • Vegetables: The onions, bell peppers, and carrots provide vitamins A, C, and various antioxidants that contribute to a healthy immune system.
  • Noodles: Lo mein noodles provide carbohydrates for energy, and if you choose whole wheat noodles, you’ll increase the fiber content for a more filling meal.

Serving Suggestions

This shrimp lo mein can be enjoyed as a standalone meal or paired with a variety of sides. Here are a few ideas:

  • Steamed Vegetables: Serve with a side of steamed broccoli, snow peas, or bok choy to add more color and nutrients to the meal.
  • Egg Rolls or Spring Rolls: For an appetizer or side dish, egg rolls or vegetable spring rolls complement the flavors of the lo mein perfectly.
  • Crispy Tofu: If you prefer a vegetarian option, add some crispy tofu cubes for extra protein and texture.

Conclusion

In just 20 minutes, you can create a flavorful and satisfying shrimp lo mein that is perfect for busy weeknights. With its tender shrimp, vibrant vegetables, and savory sauce, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Plus, it’s highly customizable, so feel free to adjust the ingredients and spice level to suit your taste.

Whether you’re in the mood for something comforting or simply need a quick dinner option, this easy shrimp lo mein recipe checks all the boxes. Give it a try tonight and treat yourself to a delicious homemade meal that’s just as good, if not better, than takeout!
