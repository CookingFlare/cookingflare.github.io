Delectable Thanksgiving Pumpkin Pie: A Timeless Dessert for the Holidays


Thanksgiving is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with a slice of warm and comforting pumpkin pie? This classic dessert has been a staple on Thanksgiving tables for generations, and for good reason. With its smooth, creamy filling and delicate spices, it’s the perfect ending to a hearty holiday meal. In this recipe, I’ll share with you a foolproof method for creating a delectable Thanksgiving pumpkin pie that will have your guests begging for seconds.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 4-6 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups evaporated milk

For the whipped cream:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the crust

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  • Shape the dough into a disk and wrap it in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Roll out the crust

  • Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle that’s about 12 inches in diameter.
  • Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish. Press it firmly into the bottom and sides of the dish, trimming any excess dough. Crimp the edges for a decorative touch.

Step 3: Prepare the filling

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, sugar, salt, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, nutmeg, and eggs. Mix until well combined.
  • Gradually add the evaporated milk, stirring until the mixture is smooth and creamy.

Step 4: Bake the pie

  • Pour the pumpkin filling into the prepared pie crust. Smooth the top with a spatula.
  • Place the pie on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes.
  • Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for another 40-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  • Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack.

Step 5: Make the whipped cream

  • In a chilled mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until soft peaks form.
  • Serve each slice of pumpkin pie with a dollop of whipped cream on top.

Enjoy the Taste of Tradition

There you have it! A timeless Thanksgiving pumpkin pie recipe that will surely become a family favorite. The combination of rich pumpkin filling and buttery crust is simply irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying an intimate meal, this classic dessert is a must-have for the holiday season. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aroma of spices fill your kitchen as you create this delectable treat. Happy baking and happy Thanksgiving!
