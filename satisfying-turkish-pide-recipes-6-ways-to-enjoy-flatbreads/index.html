Satisfying Turkish Pide Recipes: 6 Ways to Enjoy Flatbreads


Turkish pide, often referred to as Turkish flatbreads, is a beloved staple in Turkish cuisine. These delicious, boat-shaped breads are not only visually appealing but also versatile and satisfying. From savory fillings to sweet toppings, Turkish pide offers a world of culinary possibilities that can please any palate. In this blog post, we will explore six distinct ways to enjoy Turkish pide, ensuring that you can savor this delightful dish in various forms.

What is Turkish Pide?

Turkish pide is a type of flatbread that is traditionally baked in a stone oven. It has a soft, chewy texture and is characterized by its unique boat shape. The dough is made from simple ingredients like flour, water, yeast, and salt, making it easy to prepare at home. What sets pide apart is its versatility; it can be filled with an array of ingredients, ranging from meats and vegetables to cheeses and herbs, making each bite an explosion of flavor.

1. Classic Minced Meat Pide

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 250 grams minced lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, sugar, and yeast. Gradually add warm water and olive oil, mixing until a dough forms. Knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover with a cloth and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour.

  2. Make the Filling: In a skillet, sauté the onions and garlic until translucent. Add the minced meat, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook until the meat is browned and fully cooked. Stir in fresh parsley and set aside.

  3. Shape the Pide: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each into an oval shape. Place a generous amount of meat filling in the center, leaving the edges free. Fold the edges over to create a boat shape.

  4. Bake: Place the pide on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown. Serve hot, garnished with extra parsley.

This classic minced meat pide is a crowd-pleaser, perfect for family gatherings or a cozy night in. The combination of spices and juicy meat creates a hearty filling that pairs beautifully with the soft flatbread.

2. Spinach and Feta Cheese Pide

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 200 grams fresh spinach, chopped
  • 100 grams feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Follow the same steps as in the minced meat pide recipe to create the dough.

  2. Make the Filling: In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the onions until soft. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. Stir in feta cheese, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Shape the Pide: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Roll out the dough into ovals, then place the spinach and feta filling in the center. Fold the edges to form a boat shape.

  4. Bake: Brush the edges with egg wash for a golden finish. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown.

This vegetarian option is a delightful way to enjoy Turkish pide, combining the earthy flavors of spinach with the creamy, tangy feta cheese. It’s a nutritious choice that doesn’t compromise on flavor.

3. Turkish Sausage and Egg Pide

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 150 grams sucuk (Turkish sausage), thinly sliced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Create the dough as previously described.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a skillet, lightly sauté the sucuk until it begins to release its oils. Set aside.

  3. Shape the Pide: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Roll out the dough into oval shapes. Arrange the cooked sucuk in the center, and create a small well for each egg.

  4. Add Eggs: Crack an egg into each well, season with salt, pepper, and paprika. Fold the edges to form a boat shape.

  5. Bake: Bake for 12-15 minutes until the edges are golden and the eggs are cooked to your liking. Garnish with dill or parsley before serving.

This pide is perfect for breakfast or brunch, offering a savory combination of spicy sausage and runny eggs that will satisfy your morning cravings.

4. Sweet Nutella and Banana Pide

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ cup Nutella
  • 2 ripe bananas, sliced
  • Chopped hazelnuts (optional)
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Follow the dough preparation method as described earlier.

  2. Shape the Pide: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Roll out the dough into ovals. Spread a generous layer of Nutella in the center, and top with banana slices.

  3. Fold and Bake: Fold the edges over to create a boat shape, leaving the center exposed. Bake for 10-12 minutes until the edges are golden.

  4. Serve: Sprinkle with chopped hazelnuts and powdered sugar before serving. This sweet version of pide is a delightful treat for dessert or a special breakfast.

The combination of warm Nutella and fresh bananas creates a heavenly experience that is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

5. Mediterranean Veggie Pide

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 100 grams black olives, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Make the dough as described in previous recipes.

  2. Make the Filling: In a bowl, combine the bell pepper, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, olives, oregano, salt, and pepper.

  3. Shape the Pide: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Roll out the dough into ovals. Fill the center with the veggie mixture and fold the edges to form a boat shape.

  4. Bake: Brush the edges with egg wash and bake for 12-15 minutes until golden brown.

This Mediterranean veggie pide is a colorful and healthy option, perfect for those looking to enjoy a lighter meal without sacrificing flavor.

6. Cheese and Herb Pide

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 200 grams mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 100 grams feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Follow the previous dough preparation instructions.

  2. Make the Filling: In a bowl, mix mozzarella, feta, dill, mint, salt, and pepper.

  3. Shape the Pide: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Roll out the dough into ovals and fill with the cheese mixture. Fold the edges to create a boat shape.

  4. Bake: Brush the edges with egg wash and bake for 12-15 minutes until the pide is golden and the cheese is bubbling.

This cheese and herb pide is a delightful cheesy treat, perfect as an appetizer or a main dish. The fresh herbs elevate the flavors, making it a favorite among cheese lovers.

Conclusion

Turkish pide is a versatile and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed in many ways. Whether you prefer savory options like minced meat or Mediterranean veggies, or sweet delights like Nutella and banana, there’s a pide for everyone. These recipes not only bring the flavors of Turkey to your kitchen but also allow you to experiment with different ingredients to suit your taste.

So, roll up your sleeves, prepare the dough, and indulge in these delicious Turkish flatbreads. Enjoy the process of making them with family and friends, and savor every bite of these satisfying pide creations!
