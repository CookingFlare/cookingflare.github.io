Instant Pot Beef Stroganoff: A Rich and Creamy Recipe


Beef Stroganoff is one of those classic comfort foods that never goes out of style. Whether you’re preparing it for a family dinner, a special occasion, or just a cozy meal after a long day, this dish is sure to impress. Traditionally, Beef Stroganoff involves tender beef in a rich and creamy sauce, often served over egg noodles or rice. But, if you want to take your Beef Stroganoff to the next level of convenience and flavor, using an Instant Pot can help you achieve a dish that’s both quick and delicious.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through a step-by-step process for making Beef Stroganoff in an Instant Pot. It’s easy to prepare, packed with flavor, and ready in no time. Say goodbye to slaving over a stovetop for hours, and hello to a creamy, melt-in-your-mouth dinner made effortlessly with your Instant Pot.

Why Use an Instant Pot for Beef Stroganoff?

An Instant Pot is a game-changer in the kitchen. It combines the functions of a pressure cooker, slow cooker, rice cooker, and more, all in one convenient appliance. By using the Instant Pot for Beef Stroganoff, you not only save time but also retain maximum flavor and tenderness in the beef. The pressure cooking function seals in all the delicious juices, creating a rich and flavorful sauce that infuses the meat.

Additionally, the sauté function allows you to brown your meat and vegetables before pressure cooking, adding a depth of flavor that would normally require multiple cooking steps. In other words, you can make Beef Stroganoff in a fraction of the time it would traditionally take on the stove, without sacrificing any of the rich taste.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Beef Stroganoff

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather all the ingredients needed for this creamy, flavorful beef stroganoff recipe:

For the Beef Stroganoff:

  • 1.5 pounds beef stew meat (or sirloin tips, cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces cremini or white mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika (optional, for a smoky flavor)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup sour cream (or Greek yogurt for a healthier twist)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 8 ounces egg noodles (or any pasta of your choice)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Optional Add-ins:

  • 1 tablespoon brandy or white wine (for an extra layer of flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme or 1 teaspoon fresh thyme (for a herby touch)

How to Make Beef Stroganoff in the Instant Pot

Now that we have all our ingredients ready, let’s walk through the process of making this incredible dish. The beauty of the Instant Pot is that it simplifies the entire cooking process while still delivering that classic, rich flavor we all love.

Step 1: Sauté the Beef and Vegetables

Start by turning your Instant Pot to the Sauté function. Allow it to heat up for a minute or two, and then add the olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the beef stew meat in batches, ensuring the beef has enough room to sear. Brown the beef on all sides, which should take about 5 minutes per batch.

Once the beef is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside on a plate. You may need to do this in two batches depending on the size of your Instant Pot.

Next, add the chopped onion to the pot and sauté for 2-3 minutes, scraping up any brown bits left from the beef. The caramelized bits on the bottom of the pot add flavor to your sauce, so don’t skip this step.

After the onions are softened, add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional 30 seconds. Then, stir in the sliced mushrooms and cook for another 3-4 minutes, until the mushrooms have released their moisture and begun to brown.

Step 2: Deglaze the Pot

Once the mushrooms are cooked down, pour in beef broth to deglaze the pot. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up any remaining browned bits from the bottom. These flavorful remnants will make your sauce even richer. Add the Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, paprika, salt, and pepper to the pot, stirring to combine.

If you’re using brandy or white wine, add it now and allow it to cook off for 1-2 minutes. This adds complexity to the sauce and complements the beef perfectly.

Step 3: Pressure Cook the Beef

Now that your base is ready, return the browned beef to the Instant Pot. Stir to coat the beef in the sauce mixture. Add the egg noodles directly on top of the beef, making sure they are as even as possible. Do not stir.

Lock the lid in place and set the Instant Pot to Manual or Pressure Cook on High for 5 minutes. After the cooking time is complete, allow the Instant Pot to naturally release pressure for about 5 minutes before switching the valve to the venting position to release any remaining pressure.

Step 4: Finish the Stroganoff Sauce

Once the pressure has been fully released, carefully remove the lid. Stir everything together gently to combine the beef, noodles, and sauce. The noodles will have absorbed much of the liquid, but there will still be a rich, creamy sauce left.

To thicken the sauce, if desired, mix flour with a tablespoon of water to make a slurry and stir it into the sauce. If you prefer a slightly thinner sauce, skip this step. Add sour cream (or Greek yogurt) and stir until the sauce is creamy and smooth.

Step 5: Serve and Garnish

Once everything is well combined, taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with extra salt and pepper if needed. Serve the Beef Stroganoff over a bed of the creamy sauce, garnished with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color.

Enjoy the rich, velvety texture and the savory depth of flavor in every bite!

Tips for the Best Instant Pot Beef Stroganoff

  • Choose the Right Cut of Beef: While stew meat works well for this recipe, you can also use sirloin, tenderloin, or other cuts of beef. Just ensure the beef is cut into bite-sized pieces for even cooking.

  • Don’t Skip Browning the Beef: Searing the beef is crucial for developing that rich flavor. The Maillard reaction from browning meat creates a deep, savory flavor that you can’t replicate by simply tossing everything in the Instant Pot.

  • Use Low-Sodium Broth: If you’re watching your sodium intake, choose a low-sodium beef broth to avoid making the dish too salty. You can always adjust the seasoning at the end of cooking.

  • Customize the Sauce: If you prefer a tangier sauce, add a little more mustard or a squeeze of lemon juice before serving. For extra richness, you can even stir in a dollop of cream cheese along with the sour cream.

  • Make it Gluten-Free: For a gluten-free version, swap the flour for cornstarch or a gluten-free flour blend. Additionally, choose gluten-free noodles or serve the Stroganoff over mashed potatoes or rice instead.

Variations of Instant Pot Beef Stroganoff

While the classic beef stroganoff is delicious, there are plenty of ways to customize the dish to suit your preferences.

1. Chicken Stroganoff:

Replace the beef with boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs. Chicken cooks faster, so you’ll only need 3-4 minutes of pressure cooking. Follow the same instructions as the beef version, but with chicken as the main protein.

2. Vegetarian Stroganoff:

For a vegetarian version, swap out the beef for mushrooms or another plant-based protein, such as tofu or tempeh. Make sure to use vegetable broth instead of beef broth.

3. Spicy Beef Stroganoff:

Add a little heat to your dish by incorporating some crushed red pepper flakes, cayenne pepper, or even a small amount of hot sauce into the sauce.

4. Instant Pot Beef Stroganoff with Veggies:

Add in extra vegetables such as carrots, peas, or spinach for more color and nutrition. Simply toss them into the pot along with the beef and broth.
