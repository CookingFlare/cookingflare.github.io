Greek Greens and Spanaki with Olive Oil Recipe


Greek cuisine is celebrated for its use of fresh, seasonal produce and simple yet flavorful preparations that highlight the natural goodness of ingredients. Among the many treasures of Greek culinary tradition, leafy greens often take center stage, lending vibrant color, unparalleled texture, and deeply satisfying flavors to dishes. One standout preparation is Spanaki, or sautéed spinach with olive oil—a dish that combines the rich history of Mediterranean cooking with the essence of wholesome eating.

This recipe takes inspiration from the age-old tradition of making plants the heroes of the dining table. The best part? Greek greens aren’t limited to spinach alone. Bitter greens, winter greens, sweet chards, and even tender wild herbs all boast their place in Greek kitchens. Through this post, we’ll explore how you can recreate this quintessential dish at home with ease, capturing its pure flavors while returning to the simplicity that has defined Greek cuisine for centuries.

A Legacy of Leaves: The Significance of Greens in Greek Cooking

Leafy greens, or “horta” as they are known in Greece, symbolize the ideal marriage between sustenance and sustainability. For centuries, Greeks have relied on the lush vegetation of their landscapes to craft hearty, affordable meals full of nutrients. From dense collards to wild dandelion greens, Greeks embraced a “cook-with-what-you-have” philosophy, often foraging for wild greens on their own. These greens offer a subtle bitterness and robust aroma that balances wonderfully with vibrant olive oil, tangy lemon, and flavorful herbs.

The traditional Greek preparation of Spanaki brings natural sweetness to spinach by gently sautéing it in olive oil, often with additions such as garlic, onions, or fresh dill. While fresh lemon juice tends to frame the dish with its refreshing acidity, variations include crumbled feta cheese, pine nuts, or ground nutmeg, all of which add their own flair. When paired with rustic bread or grilled meats, Spanaki delivers an irresistible taste of Greece straight to your table.

Ingredients You’ll Need to Make Greek Greens and Spanaki

For the Greens:

  • 1 pound (500g) of fresh spinach, thoroughly washed and trimmed
  • 2 cups of mixed greens (such as Swiss chard, kale, or dandelion greens)
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh dill, chopped
  • Optional: ½ cup of cooked wild greens (e.g., purslane or chicory)

For the Olive Oil Mix:

  • 3–4 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil (Greek olive oil is preferred for authenticity and flavor depth)
  • 3 cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • ½ small onion, chopped finely
  • Juice from one medium lemon
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: Nutmeg (¼ teaspoon, freshly grated)

Optional Garnishes:

  • Crumbled feta cheese
  • Toasted pine nuts
  • Lemon wedges

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Preparation

  1. Wash and Prep the Greens: Begin by rinsing the spinach and other leafy greens thoroughly under cold water to remove all traces of dirt. Once clean, pat them dry using paper towels or a kitchen towel. Trim any coarse stems and chop into manageable pieces for cooking.
  2. Prepare Additional Flavor Elements: Finely chop garlic and onions, ensuring they are ready for the sauté step. Set aside your herbs like dill and gather all other seasonings for easy access during cooking.

Cooking the Dish

Sauté the Base:

  1. Heat a large skillet or sauté pan over medium heat. Add the olive oil and allow it to warm gently without smoking.
  2. Add garlic and onions to the pan, stirring frequently. Cook for about 2–3 minutes, or until the onions begin to soften and the garlic releases its aromatic oils.

Add the Greens:

  1. Slowly incorporate the spinach and mixed greens into the pan in batches, stirring constantly as they wilt down. The greens should shrink significantly as they cook, leaving ample space in the skillet. If using wild greens, add them last for optimum texture.
  2. Season lightly with salt and black pepper at this stage, allowing the flavors to meld evenly.

Enhance with Lemon and Spices:

  1. Once the greens are fully wilted and tender (after approximately 5–7 minutes of sautéing), drizzle fresh lemon juice over the mixture. If desired, sprinkle a pinch of grated nutmeg for a gentle, peppery complexity.
  2. Include chopped dill near the end of cooking to preserve its grassy brightness.

Optional Additions:

  1. Sprinkle pine nuts or crumbled feta cheese over the finished dish for added richness. These optional elements complement the natural sweetness of spinach while creating textural contrast.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Greens and Spanaki are versatile enough to be enjoyed as part of nearly any meal. Serve as:

  • A light appetizer alongside crusty bread for scooping up the greens and soaking up the olive oil.
  • A healthy side dish accompanying roasted chicken, grilled lamb chops, or seared fish.
  • A main course in vegetarian meals when topped generously with feta cheese or an extra drizzle of olive oil.

Round out your serving with wedges of lemon for extra zest and a sprinkle of freshly chopped parsley for visual appeal. The dish can be served warm for maximum aroma or at room temperature for a more relaxed presentation, mimicking the charm of Greek mezze spreads.

Pro Tips for Making the Perfect Spanaki

  • Choose Quality Olive Oil: Greek olive oil is highly prized for its intense flavor, grassy undertones, and low acidity. A robust, extra virgin variety will elevate this recipe significantly.
  • Don’t Overcook the Greens: Leafy greens are best enjoyed tender but still vibrant in color. Overcooking spinach can create a soggy texture and dull its flavor—so aim for a swift sauté.
  • Batch Cooking: If your pan feels crowded while adding greens, cook in batches. This helps retain heat and ensures even cooking without steaming or boiling the greens.
  • Experiment with Herbs: While dill is traditional, consider experimenting with mint, parsley, or oregano for alternative flavor profiles and aromas.

Why Spanaki is a Nutritional Powerhouse

Besides its unforgettable taste, Greek Greens and Spanaki offer exceptional nutritional benefits. Spinach is revered for its high content of iron, vitamin K, and folate. When paired with olive oil—rich in heart-healthy monounsaturated fats—the dish becomes a well-rounded choice for maintaining a balanced diet.

The addition of garlic and lemon juice enhances the nutritional profile further, infusing the dish with antioxidants and aiding digestion. Whether you’re looking to incorporate more greens into your life or searching for a lighter, wholesome alternative to traditional side dishes, Spanaki shines as a delicious and health-conscious choice.

A Celebration of Simplicity

Greek Greens and Spanaki beautifully reflect the essence of Mediterranean cooking—simple ingredients, bold flavors, and undeniable freshness. By prioritizing quality produce and authentic techniques, this recipe allows you to explore the rich culinary heritage of Greece without the need for complicated or extravagant preparations. Whether you’re feeding family, entertaining guests, or indulging in a moment of self-care, this dish invites you to savor the blissful harmony between nourishment and enjoyment.

Dive into the world of Greek greens today and transform humble spinach into a feast that nourishes the body and soul.
