Savor the Best of Chinese Cuisine with These Oven Recipes


Chinese cuisine is renowned globally for its diverse flavors, healthy ingredients, and rich cultural heritage. From succulent Kung Pao Chicken to the delicate simplicity of Steamed Fish, Chinese food has captured the hearts and taste buds of people all over the world. Today, we introduce you to delectable Chinese dishes that you can recreate in your own kitchen using your oven. Embrace the rich and diverse world of Chinese cuisine with these mouth-watering oven recipes.

  1. Oven-Baked Pork Dumplings (Shengjianbao)

These steamed-to-perfection pork dumplings combine savory, umami flavors with a satisfying crunch from the fried bottom. Serve these scrumptious dumplings with a side of vinegar, ginger paste, and sesame seeds for an authentic experience.

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground pork
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely minced
  • 3 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 24 round dumpling wrappers
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix the ground pork, ginger, green onions, soy sauce, cornstarch, and sesame oil in a large bowl. Set aside for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. Take one dumpling wrapper and place a tablespoon of the pork mixture in the center. Fold and seal the wrapper, ensuring the filling is encased securely.
  4. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Arrange dumplings on the sheet, spacing them evenly and ensuring they don’t touch each other.
  5. Brush each dumpling with vegetable oil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Reduce heat to 350°F (175°C) and bake an additional 5-7 minutes or until bottoms of the dumplings are golden-brown.
  6. Serve hot alongside the dipping sauce of your choice.

  1. Sweet and Sour Oven-Baked Pork

This classic dish is perfect for a family meal or for when you’re looking to impress friends. A mouthwatering combo of crispy pork, bell peppers, and pineapple, this sweet and sour pork dish is a crowd-pleaser, sure to leave everyone craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds pork, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 2 cups rice wine vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 2 bell peppers, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 pineapple, cut into bite-sized pieces

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F (235°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the pork pieces with half of the flour and cornstarch combined. Toss until the pieces are evenly coated. Shake off any excess and place the coated pork on the lined baking sheet. Bake for 15 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the rice wine vinegar, sugar, soy sauce, and ketchup. Set aside.
  4. Remove the pork from the oven, reduce heat to 400°F (205°C). Add the bell peppers and pineapple to the baking sheet, tossing with the pork to distribute evenly.
  5. Mix 2 tablespoons of the sauce with the pork, peppers, and pineapple. Bake for an additional 20 minutes.
  6. Pour the remaining sauce over the pork, peppers, and pineapple, ensuring everything is evenly coated. Return to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes.
  7. Remove from the oven, garnish with sesame seeds and cilantro, and serve over hot steamed rice.

  1. Kung Pao Chicken in the Oven

The spicy, classic Chinese dish of Kung Pao Chicken is made even easier with this oven-friendly recipe. Savory, tender chicken, crispy peanuts, and bell peppers dance in a mouth-watering sauce – all ready to be enjoyed on your dinner table.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup unsalted peanuts
  • 1 cup red bell peppers, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, finely minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste
  • 1 egg white
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the chicken pieces with 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil, cornstarch, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Spread the chicken on the lined baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes, until cooked through.
  3. While the chicken is baking, prepare the sauce. Combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and chili paste in a small bowl.
  4. In another bowl, whisk together egg white and water. In a separate bowl, combine peanuts and 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil.
  5. Once the chicken is cooked, remove from the oven. Add peanuts, bell peppers, green onions, ginger, and garlic. Drizzle with the sauce, gently tossing to combine.
  6. Return the dish to the oven and bake for an additional 10 minutes, until the peanuts are toasted and the veggies are tender-crisp.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with sesame seeds and steamed rice.

Conclusion

These mouth-watering oven recipes bring the bold, complex flavors of Chinese cuisine to your kitchen. From satisfying dumplings to delectable Sweet and Sour Pork, these dishes will tantalize your taste buds and keep you coming back for more. Create a taste of China right at home with these irresistible and easy-to-follow oven recipes.
