Easter Recipes for a Brunch Buffet: Feed a Crowd


Easter is one of those holidays that brings people together, and what better way to celebrate than with a delicious brunch buffet? Hosting a crowd can feel daunting, but with a little planning and a menu filled with a mix of sweet and savory dishes, you’ll have everything you need to impress your friends and family. From make-ahead casseroles to festive springtime desserts, there’s something everyone will love. Let’s dive into some tried-and-true recipes perfect for an Easter brunch buffet.

1. Classic Ham and Cheese Quiche

A quiche is the epitome of brunch food—rich, flavorful, and easy to prepare ahead. This classic ham and cheese quiche is a perfect anchor dish for your buffet table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup diced cooked ham
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced green onions
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie pan and crimp the edges.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  3. Scatter the diced ham, shredded cheese, and green onions evenly over the pie crust.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the top until everything is covered.
  5. Bake for 40–45 minutes, or until the center is set and lightly golden.
  6. Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

This dish can be served warm or at room temperature, making it a versatile choice for your buffet.

2. Lemon Ricotta Pancakes

Pancakes add a sweet and decadent element to your Easter spread, and these lemon ricotta pancakes bring a springtime vibe with their light, tangy flavor. They’re fluffy, delicate, and pair perfectly with fresh berries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • Zest and juice of 1 large lemon
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • Butter or oil for the skillet

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, ricotta, lemon zest, lemon juice, sugar, and eggs until smooth.
  3. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined; avoid overmixing.
  4. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a touch of butter or oil.
  5. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake onto the skillet and cook until bubbles form on top, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown.
  6. Serve with maple syrup, whipped cream, and a scattering of fresh berries.

For added efficiency, consider using a warming tray to keep the pancakes hot while you focus on other dishes.

3. Spinach and Feta Phyllo Cups

Take your savory bites up a notch with spinach and feta phyllo cups. These bite-sized treats are easy to eat, packed with flavor, and look impressive on the table.

Ingredients:

  • 12 sheets of phyllo dough (thawed if frozen)
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 cup frozen chopped spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 clove minced garlic
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 large egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a mini muffin tin.
  2. Cut phyllo dough sheets into squares about 3x3 inches. Layer 4 squares per muffin tin slot, brushing melted butter between each layer.
  3. In a bowl, mix spinach, feta, Parmesan, garlic, nutmeg, and beaten egg.
  4. Spoon the mixture into each phyllo cup.
  5. Bake for 15–20 minutes until golden and crispy.

These can be made a few hours in advance and reheated for a few minutes just before serving.

4. Asparagus and Prosciutto Tart

This elegant tart is a celebration of spring’s best ingredients and is surprisingly easy to make thanks to store-bought puff pastry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 6–8 slices of prosciutto
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out the puff pastry onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Mix ricotta, Parmesan, and lemon zest in a bowl. Spread this mixture evenly over the puff pastry, leaving a 1-inch border.
  3. Lay the asparagus and prosciutto on top of the cheese mixture. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  4. Fold the edges of the puff pastry over slightly to create a border.
  5. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and puffed.

Slice into squares before serving to make it easier for your guests to grab.

5. Deviled Eggs with a Twist

No Easter buffet is complete without deviled eggs, but why not give this classic dish a modern touch? Adding smoked paprika and crispy bacon to the filling takes them to the next level.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika, plus more for garnish
  • 2 slices of crispy bacon, crumbled
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the boiled eggs in half lengthwise and scoop out the yolks into a mixing bowl.
  2. Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites.
  4. Sprinkle each egg with crumbled bacon and an extra pinch of smoked paprika.

These can be prepared a few hours in advance and chilled until ready to serve.

6. Mini Carrot Cake Muffins

For a sweet finish, these mini carrot cake muffins are moist, spiced, and perfectly portioned, making them a great grab-and-go dessert option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • Cream cheese frosting (optional for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line a mini muffin tin.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix the eggs, oil, sugars, and vanilla until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix just until combined. Fold in the grated carrots and nuts (if using).
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the mini muffin tin and bake for 12–15 minutes.
  6. Cool completely before topping with cream cheese frosting, if desired.

These bite-sized treats will disappear quickly, so consider doubling the batch!

Hosting Tips for Easter Brunch Buffet

To pull off a stress-free Easter brunch buffet, keep these tips in mind:

  • Plan a balanced menu with a mix of sweet and savory options. Include vegetarian dishes to accommodate different dietary preferences.
  • Set up a self-serve drink station with coffee, tea, juice, and a fun springtime mimosa bar.
  • Incorporate make-ahead dishes, like the quiche and deviled eggs, to minimize last-minute prep.
  • Decorate with simple touches like pastel napkins, fresh flowers, or tiny decorative eggs scattered across the table.

With these recipes and hosting tips, you’re sure to impress your guests and create a memorable Easter celebration. From savory bites to sweet treats, this brunch buffet strikes the perfect balance to feed a crowd without overwhelming the host. Celebrate Easter with good food, great company, and the joy of spring’s arrival.
