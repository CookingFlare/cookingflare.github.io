Simple Christmas Recipes for Last-Minute Holiday Prep


The holiday season often brings a flurry of excitement, joy, and, yes, stress. As Christmas approaches, many find themselves scrambling to prepare delicious meals for family and friends. But fear not! With a little creativity and some simple recipes, you can whip up a delightful holiday feast without breaking a sweat. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of simple Christmas recipes that are perfect for last-minute holiday prep. Whether you’re hosting a festive dinner or attending a potluck, these dishes will surely impress your guests and keep your stress levels at bay.

Appetizers That Wow

1. Cranberry Brie Bites

These delightful bites combine the creamy richness of brie cheese with the tartness of cranberry sauce, all wrapped up in a flaky pastry. They’re easy to make and are always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 wheel of brie cheese, cut into small cubes
  • 1 cup of cranberry sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh rosemary, for garnish
  • Egg wash (1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon of water)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface and cut it into squares (about 3 inches).
  3. Place a small cube of brie in the center of each square, top with a spoonful of cranberry sauce, and fold the corners of the pastry over the filling.
  4. Brush the tops with the egg wash.
  5. Place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Garnish with fresh rosemary before serving.

2. Stuffed Mushrooms

These stuffed mushrooms are savory and satisfying, making them the perfect appetizer for any holiday gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large mushrooms, cleaned and stems removed
  • 1 cup of cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup of minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the cream cheese, Parmesan, garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Fill each mushroom cap with the cream cheese mixture.
  4. Place the stuffed mushrooms on a baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is bubbly.

Main Courses to Savor

3. Honey Glazed Ham

A honey-glazed ham is a classic centerpiece for any Christmas dinner. It’s simple to prepare and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-cooked ham (about 5-7 pounds)
  • 1 cup of honey
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, and ground cloves. Stir until the mixture is smooth.
  3. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern and place it in a roasting pan.
  4. Brush half of the glaze over the ham.
  5. Cover with foil and bake for 1.5 to 2 hours, basting with the remaining glaze every 30 minutes.
  6. Remove the foil during the last 30 minutes of cooking to caramelize the glaze.

4. Herb-Roasted Turkey Breast

For those who prefer turkey over ham, this herb-roasted turkey breast is both simple and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless turkey breast (about 4 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of dried thyme
  • 2 teaspoons of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rub the turkey breast with olive oil, then season with thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the turkey breast in a roasting pan and roast for about 1.5 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

Side Dishes to Delight

5. Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Creamy mashed potatoes are a must-have side dish for any holiday meal. Adding garlic elevates the flavor to a whole new level.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 1 cup of milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  2. In a separate pan, melt the butter and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  4. Mash the potatoes and mix in the garlic butter and milk.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

6. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

These roasted Brussels sprouts are crispy, smoky, and packed with flavor, making them a perfect side for your holiday feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, bacon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the sprouts are crispy and the bacon is cooked.

Desserts to Indulge

7. Peppermint Bark

This festive treat is quick to make and brings the holiday spirit to any dessert table.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces of dark chocolate, chopped
  • 12 ounces of white chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of peppermint extract
  • Crushed peppermint candies, for topping

Instructions:

  1. Melt the dark chocolate in a microwave or double boiler until smooth.
  2. Spread the dark chocolate onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and let it cool.
  3. Melt the white chocolate and stir in the peppermint extract.
  4. Pour the white chocolate over the cooled dark chocolate and spread evenly.
  5. Sprinkle crushed peppermint candies on top.
  6. Let it set in the refrigerator for about 1 hour before breaking into pieces.

8. Spiced Eggnog Cake

This moist and flavorful cake is perfect for holiday gatherings and is a delightful twist on traditional eggnog.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup of eggnog
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, then stir in the eggnog and vanilla.
  4. In another bowl, mix the flour, nutmeg, cinnamon, and baking powder.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture until just combined.
  6. Pour the batter into a greased cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.

Final Touches

With these simple Christmas recipes, you can enjoy a stress-free holiday season filled with delicious food and cherished memories. Remember, the key to last-minute prep is organization. Make a list of what you need, gather your ingredients ahead of time, and don’t hesitate to ask for help from family and friends. After all, the holiday season is about togetherness and joy.

So, roll up your sleeves, put on your favorite holiday tunes, and get ready to impress your guests with these delightful dishes. Happy cooking and Merry Christmas!
