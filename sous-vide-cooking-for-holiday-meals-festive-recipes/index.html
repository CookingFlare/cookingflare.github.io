Sous Vide Cooking for Holiday Meals: Festive Recipes


As the holiday season approaches, many of us are scrambling to come up with new and exciting recipes to impress our loved ones. If you’re tired of the same old traditional dishes, why not try something new and innovative? Sous vide cooking is a game-changer for holiday meals, offering unparalleled precision and control over the cooking process. In this post, we’ll explore some festive recipes that showcase the versatility and ease of sous vide cooking.

Glazed Ham with a Twist

Sous vide cooking is particularly well-suited for cooking large cuts of meat like ham. With a sous vide machine, you can achieve a perfectly glazed ham with a tender, juicy interior. Here’s a recipe that adds a twist to the classic glazed ham:

Ingredients:

  • 1 (5-6 pound) bone-in ham
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup bourbon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 130°F (54°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, honey, mustard, vinegar, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the glaze all over the ham, making sure to coat it evenly.
  4. Place the ham in a sous vide bag with the pineapple juice, orange juice, and bourbon (if using).
  5. Seal the bag and cook for 24 hours.
  6. Remove the ham from the bag and let it rest for 30 minutes before slicing and serving.

Sous Vide Turkey Breast with Herbs

Sous vide cooking is also ideal for cooking delicate cuts of meat like turkey breast. This recipe adds a flavorful twist to the classic roasted turkey breast:

Ingredients:

  • 2 (1-2 pound) boneless, skinless turkey breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup white wine

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 130°F (54°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, thyme, rosemary, sage, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the herb mixture all over the turkey breasts, making sure to coat them evenly.
  4. Place the turkey breasts in a sous vide bag with the white wine.
  5. Seal the bag and cook for 4-6 hours.
  6. Remove the turkey breasts from the bag and let them rest for 30 minutes before slicing and serving.

Sous Vide Green Bean Casserole

Who says green bean casserole has to be boring? Sous vide cooking can elevate this classic side dish to new heights. Here’s a recipe that adds a creamy, cheesy twist:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup French-fried onions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 180°F (82°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the green beans with the butter until they’re evenly coated.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the cheddar and mozzarella cheese.
  4. In a sous vide bag, combine the green beans, cheese mixture, flour, heavy cream, and whole milk.
  5. Seal the bag and cook for 1-2 hours.
  6. Remove the green beans from the bag and top with the French-fried onions. Serve hot.

Sous Vide Sweet Potato Casserole

Sweet potato casserole is a staple of many holiday meals, but it can be so much more than just a simple marshmallow-topped dish. Sous vide cooking can add a rich, creamy texture to this classic recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup marshmallows
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 180°F (82°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sweet potatoes, butter, brown sugar, heavy cream, whole milk, and vanilla extract.
  3. In a sous vide bag, combine the sweet potato mixture and seal the bag.
  4. Cook for 2-3 hours.
  5. Remove the sweet potatoes from the bag and top with the marshmallows and pecans. Serve hot.

Sous Vide Eggnog Cheesecake

Dessert is often the most anticipated part of the holiday meal, and sous vide cooking can elevate even the most classic desserts to new heights. Here’s a recipe for a creamy, indulgent eggnog cheesecake:

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 pounds cream cheese, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup eggnog
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sour cream

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your sous vide machine to 160°F (71°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, mix together the graham cracker crumbs and sugar.
  3. Add the melted butter and stir until the mixture is evenly moistened.
  4. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
  5. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth.
  6. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  7. Beat in the granulated sugar, eggnog, vanilla extract, and salt.
  8. Pour the cheesecake batter into the prepared pan.
  9. Place the pan in a sous vide bag and seal the bag.
  10. Cook for 2-3 hours.
  11. Remove the cheesecake from the bag and let it cool completely.
  12. Top with sour cream and serve.

With these festive recipes, you’ll be the talk of the town this holiday season. Sous vide cooking offers a level of precision and control that’s hard to achieve with traditional cooking methods, making it the perfect way to impress your loved ones. So go ahead, get creative, and make this holiday season one to remember!
