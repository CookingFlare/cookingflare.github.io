The Best Fall Recipes for Meal Prep


As the crisp autumn air sets in and the leaves start to change colors, it’s the perfect time to embrace the season’s bounty with some hearty and comforting meals. Fall is all about cozy flavors—think roasted vegetables, root vegetables, warming spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves, and the indulgence of hearty stews and soups. This is the season when we crave nutritious, satisfying meals that will fuel our days and keep us warm during the cooler months.

Meal prepping during the fall is not only a great way to make sure you always have a delicious, wholesome meal ready but also a time-saver for the busy weeks ahead. By prepping fall-inspired dishes in advance, you can ensure you’re eating seasonally, enjoying the flavors of the season, and staying on top of your health goals. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best fall recipes for meal prep that are both delicious and nutritious.

1. Butternut Squash and Chickpea Curry

When the weather turns cooler, there’s nothing better than a warm, comforting curry. This butternut squash and chickpea curry is perfect for meal prep as it’s packed with rich flavors and can be enjoyed for days. The creamy texture of the squash pairs beautifully with the hearty chickpeas, and the spices provide that familiar autumn warmth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and cinnamon. Stir to coat the squash with the spices.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and chickpeas. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
  5. Divide the curry into meal prep containers and store in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat before serving.

2. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Chili

Sweet potatoes are a fall favorite, and when paired with black beans, they create a hearty, satisfying chili that is both filling and nutritious. This recipe is a great way to incorporate more plant-based meals into your meal prep routine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper, cooking until softened.
  2. Add the diced sweet potatoes, chili powder, cumin, and smoked paprika. Stir well to coat the vegetables in the spices.
  3. Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce heat and cook for 25-30 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Add the black beans and cook for an additional 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Portion the chili into meal prep containers and store in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat before serving.

3. Apple Cider Braised Chicken

Apple cider is a quintessential fall flavor, and braising chicken in it infuses the meat with a delicate sweetness. This dish combines the savory taste of chicken with the tangy, fruity flavor of apple cider, making it perfect for fall meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 cups apple cider
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper and brown them in the skillet for about 5-7 minutes per side.
  3. Remove the chicken and set it aside. Add the sliced onion and garlic to the skillet, cooking for about 3 minutes until softened.
  4. Pour in the apple cider, scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. Add the thyme and return the chicken to the pan.
  5. Transfer the skillet to the oven and braise for 40-45 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  6. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving. Store in meal prep containers, and enjoy throughout the week.

4. Roasted Root Vegetables with Quinoa

This is the ultimate fall meal prep dish—colorful, nutritious, and full of hearty flavors. Roasted root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and beets pair perfectly with quinoa, making for a balanced and filling meal. It’s also easily customizable with any root vegetables you have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and chopped
  • 1 beet, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon ground thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the carrots, parsnips, and beet with olive oil, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Spread the vegetables on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  3. Roast for 30-35 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the quinoa according to the package instructions.
  5. Once the vegetables and quinoa are done, combine them in a large bowl. Toss together and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Portion into meal prep containers and store in the fridge for up to 4 days.

5. Pumpkin and Lentil Soup

Nothing screams fall like pumpkin, and this pumpkin and lentil soup is a healthy, comforting option for meal prep. The combination of pumpkin, lentils, and warming spices makes for a rich, filling soup that will keep you cozy all season long.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pumpkin puree (canned or fresh)
  • 1 cup dried green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until softened.
  2. Add the cumin, ginger, and cinnamon, and stir for about 1 minute to toast the spices.
  3. Pour in the pumpkin puree, diced tomatoes, lentils, and vegetable broth. Stir to combine.
  4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and adjust the flavors to your liking.
  6. Allow the soup to cool before storing in meal prep containers. It will keep in the fridge for up to 5 days, and the flavors will develop even more with time.

6. Autumn Salad with Maple Vinaigrette

Salads aren’t just for summer—this autumn salad is a perfect choice for meal prep during the fall months. Roasted squash, cranberries, and a maple vinaigrette dressing bring the season’s flavors together in a light yet hearty dish that can be enjoyed on its own or as a side.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups mixed greens (spinach, arugula, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tablespoons feta cheese (optional)

For the Maple Vinaigrette:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  2. While the squash roasts, prepare the salad dressing by whisking together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Once the squash is done, let it cool slightly before assembling the salad. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, roasted squash, cranberries, pecans, and feta.
  4. Drizzle with the maple vinaigrette and toss gently.
  5. Store the salad in meal prep containers, but keep the dressing separate to prevent wilting. When ready to eat, pour the dressing over the salad and enjoy.

Conclusion

Fall is the perfect season for hearty, comforting meals that warm you up and nourish your body. From savory stews and curries to roasted vegetables and pumpkin-filled dishes, these fall-inspired recipes will help you create satisfying meal prep options that last throughout the week. By planning ahead and preparing these dishes in advance, you’ll save time, reduce stress, and ensure you’re eating delicious, seasonal meals all fall long.
