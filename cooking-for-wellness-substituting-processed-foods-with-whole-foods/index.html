Cooking for Wellness: Substituting Processed Foods with Whole Foods


In recent years, people have become increasingly aware of the connection between diet and overall health. As more individuals seek to improve their well-being through nutritional changes, the popularity of whole foods has surged. This approach involves using minimally processed, unrefined, and nutrient-dense ingredients obtained from natural sources, such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats. In this article, we will explore simple substitutions to help you swap processed foods for whole foods in your daily cooking and enhance your wellness journey.

Replacing Processed Meats

One of the most significant changes you can make in your diet is substituting processed meats with healthier, whole-food alternatives. Instead of hot dogs and deli meats, opt for lean cuts of poultry, such as turkey or chicken, or try plant-based options like tofu, tempeh, and legumes.

Try this Braised Tofu recipe, which incorporates aromatic Asian flavors to produce a heart-healthy and appetizing dish:

Braised Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block (14 oz) of firm tofu, drained and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 inch of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of cornstarch
  • Water (2 tablespoons)
  • 1 scallion, thinly sliced
  • Chopped cilantro leaves

Instructions:

  1. In a medium-size bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, mirin, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, sugar, and cornstarch.
  2. Add tofu cubes to the marinade and let it sit for 30 minutes, turning occasionally.
  3. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Make sure to coat with a thin layer of sesame oil.
  4. Add tofu and marinade to the skillet. Cook until the tofu is golden brown and tender.
  5. Mix water and cornstarch together, then slowly pour the mixture into the skillet.
  6. Cook until the sauce thickens and the tofu is coated evenly.
  7. Garnish with sliced scallion and chopped cilantro leaves.
  8. Serve braised tofu with rice, steamed vegetables, or your favorite whole grain.

Choosing Whole Grain Alternatives

Another easy substitution to make in your cooking routine is replacing refined grains with whole-grain options. Whole grains offer a range of nutrients, like fiber, B vitamins, antioxidants, and minerals, which benefit your overall health. Swap white rice, pasta, and bread for brown, wild, red, and black varieties, and incorporate more quinoa, amaranth, farro, and buckwheat.

For example, try making a Wholesome Farro Salad, bursting with vibrant flavors and textures:

Wholesome Farro Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of farro, rinsed
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 bunch of radishes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of feta cheese or goat cheese, crumbled
  • Chopped fresh parsley, mint, or basil
  • Lemon zest and juice from 1 lemon
  • 6 oz of marinated artichokes, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Extra-virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring water and rinsed farro to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until tender, around 20-30 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooked farro, chopped vegetables, marinated artichokes, and crumbled cheese. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  3. Prepare the dressing by whisking lemon zest and juice, extra-virgin olive oil, and a pinch of salt in a small bowl.
  4. Pour the dressing over the farro salad and gently mix.
  5. Garnish with freshly chopped herbs and additional crumbled cheese.
  6. Wholesome Farro Salad tastes great as a side dish or a meatless main course, and it keeps well refrigerated for several days.

Including More Fresh Produce

Lastly, aim to incorporate an abundance of fresh produce in your meals. Vegetables, fruits, and herbs not only provide essential vitamins and minerals but also promote overall wellness via their vibrant colors and organized molecules called phytonutrients.

For a delightful and nutrient-packed Cottage Cheese and Berry Salad, try this recipe:

Cottage Cheese and Berry Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 oz of low-fat cottage cheese
  • 1 cup of mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, and strawberries)
  • 1 cup of spinach or arugula
  • Chopped fresh mint or basil
  • 1 tablespoon of extra-virgin olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Seasoning
  • Honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine mixed berries, spinach or arugula, and chopped herbs. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk cottage cheese with extra-virgin olive oil and lemon juice. Adjust seasoning according to preference.
  3. Gently fold the cheese mixture into the berry salad.
  4. Drizzle with honey if desired.
  5. Cottage Cheese and Berry Salad can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or a delightful snack.
  6. Feel free to vary the berries and greens based on seasonality and personal preference.

By embracing whole foods and making simple substitutions in your kitchen, you can embark on a journey towards improved wellness through cooking. Remember, cooking with whole ingredients not only benefits your health but also enhances the flavors and satisfaction of your meals. Happy cooking, and enjoy the wonderful world of whole foods!
