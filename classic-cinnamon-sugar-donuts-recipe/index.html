Classic Cinnamon Sugar Donuts Recipe


There’s something magical about the combination of warm, soft donuts coated in cinnamon sugar that immediately evokes a sense of comfort and nostalgia. Whether you’re planning to serve them at a special breakfast gathering, enjoy them with your morning coffee, or simply indulge in a homemade treat, classic cinnamon sugar donuts never disappoint.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to create perfectly fluffy, sweet cinnamon sugar donuts that will leave everyone coming back for more. Whether you’re using a donut pan or frying them the traditional way, you’ll be able to make these mouthwatering treats right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make these delicious cinnamon sugar donuts, you’ll need a few simple ingredients, many of which you might already have in your pantry. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Donut Batter:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk (or your preferred milk alternative)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional, but it adds a lovely depth of flavor)

For the Cinnamon Sugar Coating:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted (for coating the donuts)

Equipment Needed

While this recipe can be made using either a donut pan or a deep fryer, let’s break down both methods so you can choose whichever works best for you.

Option 1: Donut Pan

If you have a donut pan, this method will be a breeze. Donut pans come in various sizes, but a standard 6-cavity donut pan works well for this recipe. They give your donuts a beautiful, uniform shape and allow for a healthier, baked alternative to frying.

Option 2: Deep Fryer (or Frying in a Pot)

If you prefer the crispy, golden crunch of fried donuts, a deep fryer or a large pot for frying will do the trick. Frying your donuts creates that irresistible texture that many people associate with traditional donut shops. The best part? They can still be coated in cinnamon sugar for that unbeatable flavor!

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cinnamon Sugar Coating

Before you even start making the batter, it’s a good idea to prepare the cinnamon sugar coating. This way, once your donuts are ready, you can quickly coat them while they’re still warm.

In a shallow bowl, mix together 1/2 cup of granulated sugar and 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon. This will create your cinnamon sugar mixture. Set aside.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients for the donut batter: 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 3/4 cup of granulated sugar, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, and 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg (if using). Make sure the dry ingredients are evenly combined before moving on.

Step 3: Combine the Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, beat 2 large eggs until they are light and frothy. Then, add the 1/2 cup of milk, 1/4 cup of melted unsalted butter, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract. Whisk the wet ingredients until they are fully incorporated and smooth.

Step 4: Combine the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the wet mixture. Stir everything together using a spatula or spoon until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make your donuts dense. The batter should be thick but still spoonable.

Step 5: Prepare the Donut Pan or Fryer

For Baking (Donut Pan):

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease your donut pan with non-stick cooking spray or lightly butter it. Spoon the batter into each cavity of the pan, filling each about 3/4 full. Use a spoon or your fingers to smooth out the batter evenly.

For Frying (Deep Fryer or Pot):

Fill your deep fryer or a large pot with vegetable oil (or any frying oil of your choice). You’ll want enough oil to submerge the donuts completely. Heat the oil to 350°F (175°C) to ensure that your donuts fry evenly and become golden brown on the outside while staying soft and fluffy inside.

Step 6: Bake or Fry the Donuts

Baking:

Place the donut pan into the preheated oven and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into a donut comes out clean. The donuts should be golden brown around the edges and spring back when lightly pressed.

Frying:

If you’re frying your donuts, carefully drop each donut into the hot oil, one at a time. Fry for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Use a slotted spoon to remove the donuts from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Step 7: Coat the Donuts in Cinnamon Sugar

While the donuts are still warm, dip them into the melted butter, making sure to coat them evenly. Then, roll them in the cinnamon sugar mixture, ensuring that they are fully coated with that sweet, spicy goodness.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Once your donuts are coated in cinnamon sugar, they’re ready to serve! You can enjoy them warm straight from the oven or fryer, or let them cool for a few minutes. Either way, they are sure to be a hit.

Tips for Perfect Cinnamon Sugar Donuts

  1. Don’t Overmix the Batter: When combining the wet and dry ingredients, mix just until incorporated. Overmixing will result in dense, tough donuts instead of light, fluffy ones.

  2. Fry at the Right Temperature: If you’re frying, be sure your oil is at the correct temperature (350°F/175°C). Too hot, and the donuts will burn on the outside before cooking through; too cool, and they’ll be greasy and soggy.

  3. Use Fresh Cinnamon: For the best flavor, always use fresh cinnamon in your donuts and cinnamon sugar coating. This ensures that your donuts have the most vibrant and aromatic flavor.

  4. Try Variations: Feel free to experiment with different flavors. Add a dash of vanilla extract, try a different spice like cardamom or cloves, or even include small bits of chopped fruit, such as apples or pears, in the batter for a unique twist.

  5. Glaze It Up: While cinnamon sugar is the classic coating, you can also glaze your donuts. Simply make a glaze by combining powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract, and drizzle it over the donuts after they’ve been fried or baked.

How to Store Leftover Donuts

While these donuts are best enjoyed fresh, you can store any leftovers for later. To keep them as fresh as possible:

  • For Baked Donuts: Place your leftover donuts in an airtight container at room temperature. They’ll stay fresh for up to 2 days. If you want to preserve them for a little longer, freeze them and reheat them in the oven or microwave.

  • For Fried Donuts: Store fried donuts in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 day. For longer storage, freeze them. When you’re ready to enjoy, you can reheat them in the oven for a few minutes to restore some of the crispiness.

Conclusion

Making classic cinnamon sugar donuts at home is an enjoyable and rewarding experience. With just a few basic ingredients, a little time, and a dash of love, you can create a batch of donuts that are just as good as any bakery’s. Whether you choose to bake them for a healthier option or fry them for that perfect golden crunch, these cinnamon sugar donuts are sure to bring joy to your taste buds. Enjoy them warm with a cup of coffee or tea, and savor the moment as you take a bite into a timeless treat.

So, next time you’re craving something sweet, consider making this easy and satisfying recipe. They’ll be gone before you know it, and you’ll be left thinking about your next batch. Happy baking (or frying)!
