The Science Behind Keto

The ketogenic (keto) diet is a low-carb, high-fat, moderate-protein diet that aims to put the body into a metabolic state called ketosis. By lowering carbohydrate intake and replacing it with fat, the body starts to burn fat for fuel instead of relying on glucose from carbohydrates. When the body is in ketosis, it produces ketones in the liver, which become an alternate source of energy for the brain and other vital organs.

The Benefits of Keto

  1. Weight Loss: Because the body is burning fat for fuel, dieters tend to lose weight rapidly on the keto diet.
  2. Increased Energy: Ketones are an efficient source of fuel for the body, and many people report feeling more energized on the keto diet.
  3. Improved Mental Clarity: By fueling the brain with ketones, many people report improved focus and concentration.
  4. Disease Prevention: The keto diet has been shown to help manage chronic health conditions like Type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers.
