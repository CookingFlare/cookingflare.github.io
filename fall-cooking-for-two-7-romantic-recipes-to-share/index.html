Fall Cooking for Two: 7 Romantic Recipes to Share


As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, fall invites us to cozy up with our loved ones and indulge in the warmth of home-cooked meals. Cooking for two during this enchanting season can be a delightful experience, filled with flavors that evoke comfort and romance. In this blog post, we’ll explore seven romantic recipes perfect for sharing with that special someone. Each dish is designed to be simple yet elegant, allowing you to savor the moments together while enjoying the bounty of fall.

1. Pumpkin Risotto with Sage

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the vegetable broth over low heat.
  2. In a separate large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice, allowing it to toast for about 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the white wine, stirring until it’s mostly absorbed.
  5. Gradually add the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until the liquid is absorbed before adding more.
  6. After about 15 minutes, stir in the pumpkin puree and fresh sage. Continue cooking until the rice is creamy and al dente.
  7. Remove from heat and mix in the Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve warm, garnished with additional sage if desired.

This creamy pumpkin risotto is not only comforting but also a beautiful representation of fall flavors. The combination of pumpkin and sage creates a warm, inviting dish that’s perfect for a romantic dinner.

2. Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts and Bacon

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup pecans, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the Brussels sprouts on a baking sheet and sprinkle the chopped bacon over them.
  4. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the Brussels sprouts are tender and the bacon is crispy.
  5. Drizzle the maple syrup over the roasted Brussels sprouts and bacon, tossing to coat.
  6. Return to the oven for an additional 5 minutes.
  7. If using, sprinkle with chopped pecans before serving.

This dish combines the earthy flavors of Brussels sprouts with the sweetness of maple syrup and the savory crunch of bacon. It’s a perfect side dish to complement any fall entrée and adds a touch of romance to your table.

3. Apple Cider Glazed Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine apple cider, Dijon mustard, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and reduce until thickened, about 10 minutes.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  3. Add the chicken to the skillet and cook for 6-7 minutes on each side, or until cooked through.
  4. Pour the apple cider glaze over the chicken, allowing it to simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve the chicken drizzled with the glaze, alongside your favorite fall vegetables.

This apple cider glazed chicken is a delightful main course that captures the essence of fall. The sweet and tangy glaze pairs beautifully with the tender chicken, making it a dish that’s sure to impress.

4. Butternut Squash Soup with Coconut Milk

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
