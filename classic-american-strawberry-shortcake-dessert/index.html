Classic American Strawberry Shortcake Dessert


Strawberry shortcake is a beloved dessert in America, epitomizing the sweet and tangy flavors of fresh strawberries combined with the light, buttery richness of biscuits and the smooth, creamy texture of whipped cream. Whether served at a summer picnic, a backyard BBQ, or as a delicious treat after dinner, this dessert never fails to impress. The balance of textures and flavors in strawberry shortcake makes it the perfect dish to enjoy in the warmer months when strawberries are at their peak.

This classic American dessert has been around for centuries, evolving from simple biscuit recipes and fruit compotes to the refined treat we know and love today. With its layers of tender, flaky biscuits, juicy strawberries, and rich whipped cream, strawberry shortcake is the ultimate combination of simplicity and indulgence. Let’s dive into how you can create this timeless dessert from scratch in your kitchen.

History of Strawberry Shortcake

The origins of strawberry shortcake can be traced back to England, where it was known as “strawberry cream cake.” In the 16th century, English bakers created sponge cakes and biscuits that were paired with sweetened cream and strawberries. Over time, as the dessert made its way to America, it underwent a few changes, with American bakers opting for a more biscuit-like base rather than a sponge cake. The dessert’s popularity soared, especially in the 19th and 20th centuries, and it became an iconic part of American summer dining.

Today, strawberry shortcake is still a symbol of summer, served at everything from picnics to family gatherings. Whether you make it from scratch or opt for a quick store-bought shortcut, this dessert is always a hit.

Ingredients

For this traditional strawberry shortcake recipe, you’ll need:

For the Biscuits:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 2/3 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Strawberries:

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice (optional)

For the Whipped Cream:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Preparing the Strawberries

Before making the biscuits, start by preparing the strawberries. Slice the strawberries into thin pieces and place them in a medium bowl. Sprinkle them with sugar and lemon juice (if using). Stir the strawberries gently to combine, and let them sit for about 30 minutes. The sugar will draw out the juices from the berries, creating a sweet strawberry syrup that will soak into the biscuits and infuse the dessert with rich, fruity flavor.

If you want a more intense strawberry flavor, you can even prepare the strawberries a few hours in advance, allowing them to marinate in the fridge. The longer the strawberries sit, the more flavorful the syrup will be.

Making the Biscuits

While the strawberries are macerating, it’s time to make the biscuits. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This will ensure the biscuits bake evenly and don’t stick to the pan.

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. This dry mixture will form the base of your biscuit dough.

  2. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter, two forks, or your fingers, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized pieces of butter. The key to a flaky biscuit is keeping the butter cold and not overworking the dough.

  3. Pour in the milk and vanilla extract, stirring gently until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense biscuits. The dough should be soft and slightly sticky.

  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently pat it into a rectangle about 1-inch thick. Fold the dough in half, then pat it out again. Repeat this process a few times to create layers. This will help make your biscuits tender and flaky.

  5. Use a round biscuit cutter or a glass (about 2 inches in diameter) to cut out your biscuits. Place the biscuits on the prepared baking sheet, ensuring they are close together to help them rise evenly.

  6. Bake the biscuits in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they are golden brown on top and cooked through. Once baked, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool slightly before assembling the dessert.

Making the Whipped Cream

While the biscuits cool, prepare the whipped cream. In a mixing bowl, combine the heavy whipping cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the cream on medium-high speed until stiff peaks form. Be careful not to overwhip the cream, as it can turn into butter.

Once the whipped cream is ready, set it aside in the fridge to keep it cold while you assemble the shortcakes.

Assembling the Strawberry Shortcake

Now comes the fun part—putting everything together!

  1. Cut each biscuit in half horizontally to create two layers.

  2. Spoon a generous amount of the macerated strawberries (along with some of the syrup) onto the bottom half of each biscuit.

  3. Top the strawberries with a dollop of fresh whipped cream. You can be as generous as you like here; the cream adds a luxurious touch to each bite.

  4. Place the top half of the biscuit on top of the whipped cream and strawberries, gently pressing down to secure the layers.

  5. If you like, you can add a little extra whipped cream on top for decoration or even garnish with a whole strawberry for a festive touch.

Serving

Serve your homemade strawberry shortcake immediately while the biscuits are still warm. The contrast of the warm biscuit with the cold whipped cream and sweet, juicy strawberries creates a perfect balance of flavors and textures. This dessert is best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, they can be stored in the fridge for a day or two.

If you’re looking for variations, you can also try adding a layer of lemon curd or a drizzle of chocolate ganache to elevate the flavors. Alternatively, you can substitute the whipped cream for mascarpone cheese or Greek yogurt for a slightly tangier twist.

Tips for the Perfect Strawberry Shortcake

  • Use fresh, ripe strawberries: The quality of the strawberries is crucial for making a great strawberry shortcake. Opt for strawberries that are in season for the best flavor. Look for berries that are firm, fragrant, and deep red in color.
  • Chill the butter: Cold butter is essential for creating flaky biscuits. Make sure the butter stays chilled until it’s incorporated into the dough.
  • Don’t overmix the dough: The key to light and fluffy biscuits is not overworking the dough. Mix it just enough to bring it together, and don’t worry if it looks a little rough—this will only add to the texture of the final product.
  • Adjust the sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter biscuit, you can increase the amount of sugar in the biscuit dough or the whipped cream. However, the sweetness of the strawberries usually balances everything out.
  • Add texture with toppings: If you’re feeling adventurous, consider topping your shortcakes with toasted nuts, like slivered almonds or crushed pistachios, for added crunch.

Variations on Classic Strawberry Shortcake

While the classic version is always a favorite, there are many ways to put your own spin on strawberry shortcake. Here are some ideas to try:

Strawberry Shortcake with Angel Food Cake

For a lighter version of strawberry shortcake, swap out the biscuit for angel food cake. The fluffy, airy texture of the cake pairs wonderfully with the strawberries and whipped cream, giving you a refreshing take on this dessert.

Vegan Strawberry Shortcake

To make this dessert vegan-friendly, you can replace the butter with coconut oil or vegan butter in the biscuits, and use non-dairy whipping cream in place of the heavy cream. For the biscuit dough, you can substitute the milk with almond milk or coconut milk, which will add a unique flavor to the dessert.

Gluten-Free Strawberry Shortcake

For those with gluten sensitivities, you can make gluten-free strawberry shortcake by using a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend in place of the regular flour. Make sure your whipped cream is made with gluten-free ingredients, and check the labels on any other ingredients to ensure they are safe for a gluten-free diet.

Conclusion

Strawberry shortcake is more than just a dessert; it’s a tradition that brings people together. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply enjoying the bounty of fresh strawberries during the summer months, this classic dessert never goes out of style. With its buttery biscuits, sweet strawberries, and whipped cream, it’s a treat that’s sure to satisfy anyone with a sweet tooth. By following this recipe and tips, you can create the perfect strawberry shortcake that will have your friends and family asking for seconds. Enjoy!
