Vietnamese Pho Beef Noodle Soup Recipe


If you’ve ever been to Vietnam or visited a Vietnamese restaurant, you’ve probably heard of pho. This iconic dish is a staple of Vietnamese cuisine and is beloved all over the world for its rich, complex flavors and comforting warmth. Today, I’m going to share with you my take on the classic Vietnamese Pho Beef Noodle Soup recipe, so you can recreate this delicious dish in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

For the Broth:

  • 4 pounds beef marrow bones
  • 2 pounds beef brisket
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 4-inch piece of ginger, halved lengthwise
  • 5 star anise
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 3-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 black cardamom pod
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce
  • 1/4 cup rock sugar or regular sugar

For the Soup:

  • 1 pound dried rice noodles
  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced onions
  • 3 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup sawtooth herb or cilantro
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Sriracha and hoisin sauce for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth:

    • Preheat the oven to 450°F. Place the beef bones and brisket on a roasting pan and roast for 30 minutes, until nicely browned. This step will enhance the flavor of the broth.
    • In a large stockpot, add the onion and ginger halves, star anise, cloves, cinnamon stick, cardamom pod, coriander seeds, and fennel seeds. Toast the spices over medium heat until fragrant.
    • Add the roasted bones and brisket to the pot, along with 6 quarts of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer, uncovered, for at least 4 hours. Skim off any foam and fat that rises to the surface.
    • After 4 hours, remove the beef brisket and set it aside. Continue simmering the broth for another 2 hours.
    • Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve, discarding the solids. Season the broth with salt, fish sauce, and rock sugar. Keep the broth warm while you prepare the other components of the soup.

  2. Prepare the Soup Ingredients:

    • Soak the rice noodles in hot water for 20 minutes, then drain and set aside.
    • Thinly slice the beef brisket that was set aside earlier.
    • Arrange the beef sirloin, sliced onions, and green onions on a platter. Set aside the cilantro, bean sprouts, Thai basil, and sawtooth herb in separate bowls.

  3. Assemble the Pho Soup:

    • Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the soaked rice noodles and cook for 1-2 minutes, until tender but still firm. Drain the noodles and divide them among serving bowls.
    • Arrange a few slices of the cooked brisket and raw sirloin on top of the noodles in each bowl. Ladle the hot broth over the meat, which will cook it to perfection.
    • Serve the soup immediately, accompanied by the platter of fresh herbs, lime wedges, and condiments such as sriracha and hoisin sauce.

Tips for Serving:

  • Pho is traditionally served with a variety of fresh herbs and condiments, allowing diners to customize their bowls to their liking. Feel free to experiment with different combinations of herbs and sauces to find your favorite flavor profile.
  • The key to a great bowl of pho lies in the broth, so don’t rush the process of simmering the bones and spices. The longer you simmer, the richer and more flavorful the broth will be.
  • When it comes to slicing the beef, remember to cut it as thinly as possible. This will ensure that the hot broth cooks the beef to a perfect, tender texture.

Now that you have this authentic Vietnamese Pho Beef Noodle Soup recipe in your arsenal, you can bring the flavors of Vietnam into your own home. Whether you’re serving it for a cozy night in or impressing guests at a dinner party, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. Enjoy the aromatic broth, tender beef, and the satisfaction of mastering this beloved Vietnamese classic.
