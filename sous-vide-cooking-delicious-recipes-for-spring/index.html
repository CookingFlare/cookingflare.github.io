Sous Vide Cooking: Delicious Recipes for Spring


As the weather starts to warm up, our taste buds begin to crave lighter, fresher flavors that reflect the season’s bounty. Sous vide cooking is the perfect way to prepare a variety of dishes that showcase the sweetness of spring. In this post, we’ll explore some mouth-watering recipes that highlight the versatility of sous vide cooking and the flavors of the season.

Asparagus and Lemon Risotto

Spring is the perfect time to enjoy the tender shoots of asparagus, and pairing it with a creamy risotto is a match made in heaven. Sous vide cooking allows us to cook the asparagus to the perfect texture, while the risotto absorbs the flavors of lemon and parmesan.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth, warmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 190°F (88°C).
  2. In a large pot, sauté the garlic and asparagus in olive oil until tender. Set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the warmed chicken broth, 1/2 cup at a time, stirring continuously and allowing each portion to absorb before adding the next.
  5. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the parmesan and lemon juice.
  6. Add the cooked asparagus to the risotto and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Cook the risotto in the sous vide water bath for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Spring Pea and Mint Soup

The sweet flavor of spring peas pairs perfectly with the freshness of mint, making this soup a refreshing and light option for the season. Sous vide cooking allows us to cook the peas to the perfect texture, while the mint adds a bright and herbaceous note.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh spring peas
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 180°F (82°C).
  2. In a blender or food processor, puree the peas, chicken broth, and heavy cream until smooth.
  3. In a large pot, sauté the garlic and mint in olive oil until fragrant.
  4. Add the pureed pea mixture to the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Cook the soup in the sous vide water bath for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Sous Vide Egg and Prosciutto Wrap

Eggs are a staple of spring, and wrapping them in prosciutto and herbs is a simple yet elegant way to enjoy them. Sous vide cooking allows us to cook the eggs to the perfect texture, while the prosciutto adds a salty and savory note.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 slices prosciutto
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C).
  2. Crack the eggs into a sous vide bag or a heat-proof container and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Add the prosciutto, parsley, and chives to the bag or container.
  4. Cook the eggs in the sous vide water bath for 45-60 minutes, or until the whites are set and the yolks are cooked to your desired level of doneness.
  5. Serve the eggs wrapped in prosciutto and garnished with additional herbs, if desired.

Sous Vide Chicken and Strawberry Salad

The sweetness of strawberries pairs perfectly with the richness of chicken, making this salad a refreshing and light option for spring. Sous vide cooking allows us to cook the chicken to the perfect texture, while the strawberries add a pop of color and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1 cup sliced strawberries
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the sous vide water bath to 160°F (71°C).
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook the chicken in the sous vide water bath for 30-40 minutes, or until cooked through.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, sliced strawberries, crumbled feta cheese, and chopped pecans.
  5. Slice the cooked chicken breasts and add them to the bowl.
  6. Drizzle the olive oil and balsamic vinegar over the salad and season with salt and pepper to taste.

These sous vide recipes showcase the versatility and ease of cooking with this method, and highlight the flavors and ingredients of the spring season. Whether you’re looking for a light and refreshing soup, a creamy risotto, or a simple yet elegant egg dish, sous vide cooking has something for everyone. So why not give one of these recipes a try and experience the delicious results for yourself?
