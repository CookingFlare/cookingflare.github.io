Easter Recipes for a Spring Fling: Celebrate the Season

Appetizers to Start the Celebration

Deviled Eggs with a Twist

Deviled eggs are a classic Easter appetizer, but why not elevate them with a unique twist? Start by boiling a dozen eggs until hard-boiled. Once cooled, peel and slice them in half. Remove the yolks and mix them with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, a splash of apple cider vinegar, and a pinch of smoked paprika. For a fun twist, add diced pickles or crumbled bacon. Spoon the mixture back into the egg whites and garnish with fresh chives or microgreens. These deviled eggs are sure to be a hit at your Easter gathering!

Spring Vegetable Platter with Herb Dip

Celebrate the bounty of spring with a colorful vegetable platter. Choose a variety of seasonal vegetables such as radishes, snap peas, baby carrots, and bell peppers. Arrange them beautifully on a large platter. For the herb dip, blend Greek yogurt with fresh herbs like dill, parsley, and chives, along with a squeeze of lemon juice and a pinch of salt. This refreshing dip pairs perfectly with the crisp vegetables, making it a healthy and vibrant appetizer.

Mini Quiches with Asparagus and Goat Cheese

Mini quiches are versatile and can be made ahead of time, making them perfect for Easter brunch. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Line a muffin tin with pastry shells or phyllo dough. Fill each shell with a mixture of sautéed asparagus, crumbled goat cheese, and the egg mixture. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the quiches are set and golden. These bite-sized delights are sure to impress your guests!

Main Dishes for a Festive Feast

Honey-Glazed Ham with Pineapple

A succulent honey-glazed ham is a centerpiece that never fails to impress. Start with a fully cooked ham and score the surface in a diamond pattern. In a saucepan, combine honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, and pineapple juice. Brush this glaze generously over the ham and place it in a preheated oven at 325°F (160°C). Bake for about 15 minutes per pound, basting occasionally. Serve with pineapple rings for a sweet and savory dish that embodies the spirit of Easter.

Lemon Herb Roasted Chicken

For a lighter main dish, consider a lemon herb roasted chicken. Rub a whole chicken with olive oil, salt, pepper, and a mixture of fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, and parsley. Stuff the cavity with lemon halves and garlic cloves. Roast in a preheated oven at 425°F (220°C) for about 1.5 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). The result is a juicy, flavorful chicken that pairs beautifully with spring vegetables.

Vegetarian Stuffed Peppers

For a vegetarian option, stuffed peppers are a colorful and satisfying choice. Halve bell peppers and remove the seeds. In a skillet, sauté onions, garlic, and diced tomatoes. Add cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, and spices like cumin and chili powder. Fill each pepper with the mixture and top with shredded cheese. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 30-35 minutes. These stuffed peppers are not only delicious but also a feast for the eyes!
