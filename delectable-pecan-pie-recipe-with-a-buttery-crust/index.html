Delectable Pecan Pie Recipe with a Buttery Crust


There’s something undeniably comforting about a slice of pecan pie. The rich, buttery crust paired with the sweet, nutty filling creates a symphony of flavors that is sure to delight your taste buds. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this pecan pie recipe is a must-try. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let’s dive into the art of creating the perfect pecan pie.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 2-4 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups pecan halves

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled butter pieces and using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough with a fork just until it holds together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  3. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, corn syrup, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. Stir in the pecan halves until they are evenly coated with the sugar mixture.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish and press it gently into the bottom and sides of the dish.
  2. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges as desired.
  3. Pour the pecan filling into the prepared crust and spread it out evenly.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  2. If the edges of the crust start to darken too much during baking, you can cover them with aluminum foil to prevent burning.
  3. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool completely on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This delectable pecan pie is best enjoyed slightly warm or at room temperature. You can elevate the experience by serving it with a dollop of freshly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. And for those who enjoy a touch of indulgence, a drizzle of warm caramel sauce adds an extra layer of decadence.

Conclusion

There’s no denying the allure of a well-made pecan pie. The combination of a buttery crust and a luscious pecan filling makes it a timeless dessert that’s perfect for any occasion. So, whether you’re hosting a holiday gathering or simply craving a sweet treat, this pecan pie recipe is a surefire way to impress your guests and satisfy your cravings. So, go ahead, give it a try, and bask in the warm, nutty goodness of this classic dessert.
