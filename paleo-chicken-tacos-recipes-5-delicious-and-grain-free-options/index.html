Paleo Chicken Tacos Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


Tacos are a staple in many cuisines, and for good reason. They’re easy to make, versatile, and can be customized to suit any taste or dietary requirement. However, traditional tacos often rely heavily on grains like tortillas, which can be a problem for those following a paleo diet. Fear not, dear paleo enthusiasts! We’ve got you covered with these 5 delicious and grain-free paleo chicken tacos recipes.

Recipe 1: Spicy Chicken Tacos with Avocado Salsa

This recipe is a twist on traditional tacos, with a spicy kick from the chipotle peppers in adobo sauce. To make it paleo-friendly, we’ll be using lettuce wraps instead of tortillas.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup chipotle peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together chipotle peppers, lime juice, garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the chicken to the bowl and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the avocado salsa by combining diced avocado, tomatoes, cilantro, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing a cooked chicken breast on a lettuce leaf, topping with avocado salsa, and serving immediately.

Recipe 2: Korean-Style Chicken Tacos with Gochujang Slaw

This recipe takes inspiration from Korean cuisine, with a sweet and spicy gochujang slaw to top our grain-free tacos.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro
  • 2 tbsp gochujang
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, green onions, and cilantro.
  3. Add the chicken to the bowl and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the gochujang slaw by combining shredded cabbage, cilantro, gochujang, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing a cooked chicken breast on a lettuce leaf, topping with gochujang slaw, and serving immediately.

Recipe 3: Chicken Tacos with Mango Salsa and Cilantro Lime Rice

This recipe is a tropical twist on traditional tacos, with the sweetness of mango and the brightness of lime.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 1 cup cooked white rice (make sure it’s paleo-friendly!)
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together mango, lime juice, garlic, cumin, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the chicken to the bowl and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the cilantro lime rice by combining cooked rice, lime juice, cumin, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing a cooked chicken breast on a lettuce leaf, topping with mango salsa, and serving with a side of cilantro lime rice.

Recipe 4: Chicken Tacos with Roasted Vegetables and Chimichurri Sauce

This recipe is a hearty and flavorful option, with roasted vegetables and a tangy chimichurri sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as broccoli, cauliflower, and carrots)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the mixed vegetables with olive oil, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the vegetables on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the chimichurri sauce by combining parsley, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Assemble the tacos by placing a cooked chicken breast on a lettuce leaf, topping with roasted vegetables and chimichurri sauce, and serving immediately.

Recipe 5: Chicken Tacos with Bacon and Avocado

This recipe is a rich and satisfying option, with crispy bacon and creamy avocado.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together lime juice, garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the chicken to the bowl and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the taco toppings by combining cooked bacon, diced avocado, cilantro, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing a cooked chicken breast on a lettuce leaf, topping with taco toppings, and serving immediately.

These paleo chicken tacos recipes offer a delicious and grain-free twist on traditional tacos. Whether you’re in the mood for something spicy, sweet, or savory, there’s a recipe here for you. So go ahead, get creative, and enjoy your grain-free tacos!
