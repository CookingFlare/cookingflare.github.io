From the Grill to the Table: Delicious Fourth of July BBQ Recipes to Make Your Mouth Water


Summer is in full swing, and what better way to celebrate Independence Day than with a mouthwatering BBQ? The Fourth of July is a time for family, friends, and delicious food. Get ready to fire up the grill and impress your guests with these delectable BBQ recipes that will leave everyone begging for seconds!

1. Grilled Honey Mustard Chicken Skewers

Prep time: 10 minutes Cook time: 15 minutes Serves: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the chicken chunks onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  4. Brush the honey mustard mixture evenly over the chicken skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  6. Remove from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

Serve these tender and flavorful chicken skewers with a side of grilled vegetables and enjoy the perfect balance of sweet and tangy flavors.

2. Smoky BBQ Ribs

Prep time: 15 minutes Cook time: 2.5 - 3 hours Serves: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs baby back pork ribs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (your favorite brand)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Aluminum foil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper to create a dry rub.
  3. Pat the ribs dry with a paper towel and remove the membrane from the back of the ribs.
  4. Rub the dry rub all over the ribs, ensuring an even coating.
  5. Wrap the ribs tightly in aluminum foil and place them on the grill.
  6. Cook the ribs for 2.5 - 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and starts to pull away from the bones.
  7. Carefully remove the ribs from the foil and brush them generously with your favorite BBQ sauce.
  8. Return the ribs to the grill and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce is caramelized and sticky.

These smoky BBQ ribs will be the star of your Fourth of July gathering. Serve them with cornbread and coleslaw for a classic BBQ feast.

3. Grilled Pineapple Salsa

Prep time: 10 minutes Cook time: 5 minutes Makes: 2 cups

Ingredients:

  • 1 small pineapple, peeled and cored
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeno, seeds removed and finely diced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Cut the pineapple into thick rounds.
  3. Grill the pineapple rounds for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they have grill marks.
  4. Remove the pineapple from the grill and let it cool slightly.
  5. Dice the grilled pineapple and place it in a medium-sized bowl.
  6. Add the diced red bell pepper, red onion, jalapeno, lime juice, and cilantro to the bowl.
  7. Stir everything together until well combined.
  8. Season with salt to taste.

This grilled pineapple salsa is a refreshing and tangy accompaniment to any BBQ dish. Serve it with tortilla chips or as a topping for grilled meats.

4. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Prep time: 10 minutes Cook time: 15 minutes Serves: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, chopped parsley, chives, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the herb butter mixture evenly over each ear of corn.
  4. Grill the corn for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until it is tender and slightly charred.
  5. Remove from the grill and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

This grilled corn on the cob with herb butter will be a hit at your Fourth of July BBQ. The combination of the smoky grilled corn and the flavorful herb butter is simply irresistible.

Conclusion

With these delicious BBQ recipes, your Fourth of July celebration is sure to be a hit. Whether you’re grilling up some honey mustard chicken skewers, smoky BBQ ribs, or serving a refreshing grilled pineapple salsa, your guests will be impressed by your culinary skills. Don’t forget to include some grilled corn on the cob with herb butter on the side for the ultimate summer feast. Happy grilling and happy Fourth of July!
