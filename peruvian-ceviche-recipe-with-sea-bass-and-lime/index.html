Peruvian Ceviche Recipe with Sea Bass and Lime


If there’s one dish that captures the vibrant flavors of Peru, it’s the classic Peruvian ceviche. This iconic dish is a perfect blend of fresh seafood, tangy lime juice, and a burst of flavors from Peruvian ingredients. Today, we’re going to explore the art of making Peruvian ceviche, with a twist of using sea bass for a delightful culinary experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh sea bass, skinned and deboned
  • 6-8 limes, juiced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 aji amarillo peppers, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 sweet potato, boiled and sliced
  • 1 ear of corn, boiled and sliced
  • 1-2 tablespoons chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce leaves for serving
  • A handful of cancha (toasted corn nuts) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sea Bass: Cut the sea bass into small, bite-sized pieces and place them in a glass or ceramic bowl. Season with salt and pepper.

  2. Marinate the Sea Bass: Pour the freshly squeezed lime juice over the sea bass, making sure the pieces are fully submerged. The acidity of the lime juice will “cook” the fish. Let it marinate in the refrigerator for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Add the Flavor: Stir in the thinly sliced red onion, aji amarillo peppers, and minced garlic. These ingredients will infuse the ceviche with a delightful kick of flavor and a hint of heat.

  4. Let it Chill: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the ceviche chill in the refrigerator for another 10-15 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld and the fish to reach the perfect texture.

  5. Serve and Garnish: Once the ceviche is chilled, it’s ready to be served. Arrange the lettuce leaves on a platter and spoon the ceviche on top. Garnish with slices of boiled sweet potato, boiled corn, and a sprinkle of chopped cilantro. For an extra crunch, add a handful of cancha on top.

  6. Enjoy: Serve the Peruvian sea bass ceviche immediately and savor the vibrant flavors of the sea, the tang of the lime, and the warmth of Peruvian spices.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Freshness is Key: When making ceviche, it’s crucial to use the freshest sea bass available. Look for firm, translucent flesh with no fishy odor for the best results.
  • Balancing the Flavors: Adjust the amount of aji amarillo peppers to suit your spice preference. These peppers add a unique flavor profile to the ceviche, so feel free to experiment with the quantity.
  • Chill Time: Allowing the fish to marinate and chill is essential for the flavors to develop. Don’t rush this step for the best tasting ceviche.

Peruvian sea bass ceviche with lime is a celebration of fresh ingredients, zesty flavors, and the culinary heritage of Peru. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the sea, this recipe is sure to impress with its vibrant colors and refreshing taste. So, roll up your sleeves, grab your knife, and embark on a culinary journey to the heart of Peru with this delightful sea bass ceviche recipe.
