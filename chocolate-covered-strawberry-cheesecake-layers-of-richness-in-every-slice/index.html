Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake: Layers of Richness in Every Slice


When it comes to desserts that wow both in flavor and presentation, few can compete with a rich and indulgent Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake. This delightful treat is an exquisite combination of creamy cheesecake, luscious chocolate, and the fresh, fruity taste of strawberries. Each slice offers an enticing balance of flavors and textures, making it the perfect centerpiece for any celebration or a special treat for yourself. In this post, we’ll explore how to make this decadent dessert, layer by layer, and share tips for achieving the perfect chocolate-covered strawberries to crown your cheesecake.

The Allure of Cheesecake

Cheesecake has long been a beloved dessert, cherished for its creamy texture and rich flavor. Originating from ancient Greece, this delightful treat has evolved over the centuries, with numerous variations emerging around the world. The base of a classic cheesecake typically includes cream cheese, sugar, and eggs, creating a smooth and velvety filling that can be paired with a myriad of toppings.

The Classic Cheesecake Base

For our Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake, we will start with a classic New York-style cheesecake base. This creamy filling benefits from a buttery graham cracker crust that provides a satisfying crunch to offset the smoothness of the cheesecake. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients for the Cheesecake Base

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
    • ¼ cup granulated sugar
    • Pinch of salt

  • For the cheesecake filling:

    • 4 (8-ounce) packages of cream cheese, softened
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • ½ cup sour cream
    • ½ cup heavy whipping cream
    • 4 large eggs
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Creating the Perfect Crust

  1. Preheat the Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 325°F (160°C). This temperature is ideal for baking the cheesecake evenly without cracking.

  2. Prepare the Crust: In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, sugar, and salt. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and resemble wet sand.

  3. Press into the Pan: Grease a 9-inch springform pan. Press the crumb mixture firmly into the bottom of the pan and slightly up the sides. Ensure it’s evenly distributed for a consistent texture.

  4. Bake the Crust: Bake the crust in the preheated oven for about 10 minutes, or until it’s lightly golden. Allow it to cool while you prepare the cheesecake filling.

Crafting the Creamy Cheesecake Filling

  1. Beat the Cream Cheese: In a large mixing bowl, use an electric mixer to beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy. This ensures a lump-free filling.

  2. Add Sugar and Sour Cream: Gradually add the granulated sugar and sour cream, mixing until well combined.

  3. Incorporate the Heavy Cream: Pour in the heavy whipping cream and vanilla extract, mixing until fully incorporated. The heavy cream adds richness and a velvety texture.

  4. Add Eggs and Flour: Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing on low speed after each addition. Finally, gently fold in the flour to stabilize the filling.

  5. Pour into the Crust: Carefully pour the cheesecake filling over the cooled crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.

  6. Bake the Cheesecake: Bake in the 325°F oven for 60-70 minutes, or until the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle. This slight jiggle will set as it cools, preventing cracking.

  7. Cool and Chill: Turn off the oven, crack the door open, and allow the cheesecake to cool in the oven for about an hour. Then, remove it and let it cool to room temperature before refrigerating for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

The Chocolate Component

No Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake would be complete without a rich chocolate layer. This layer can be added either as a ganache or melted chocolate drizzled over the top. Here’s how to create a silky chocolate ganache:

Ingredients for Chocolate Ganache

  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (optional for shine)

Making the Chocolate Ganache

  1. Heat the Cream: In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer.

  2. Add Chocolate: Remove from heat and pour the chocolate chips into the hot cream. Let it sit for a minute to soften the chocolate.

  3. Stir Until Smooth: Add the butter, if using, and stir until the mixture is completely smooth and glossy.

  4. Cool Slightly: Allow the ganache to cool for a few minutes before pouring it over the cheesecake.

The Strawberries: The Crowning Glory

Fresh strawberries are the star of our dessert. Chocolate-covered strawberries add a burst of flavor and visual appeal. Here’s how to make them perfectly:

Ingredients for Chocolate-Covered Strawberries

  • 1 pound fresh strawberries, washed and dried
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (optional for smoother chocolate)

Creating Chocolate-Covered Strawberries

  1. Melt the Chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the chocolate chips and coconut oil. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until melted and smooth.

  2. Dip the Strawberries: Hold each strawberry by the stem and dip it into the melted chocolate, allowing any excess to drip off.

  3. Set on Wax Paper: Place the dipped strawberries on a wax paper-lined baking sheet. Allow them to set at room temperature or refrigerate for faster setting.

Assembling Your Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake

Now that we have all the components ready, it’s time to assemble your masterpiece!

  1. Pour Ganache Over Cheesecake: Once the cheesecake has cooled and chilled, remove it from the springform pan. Place it on a serving platter and pour the cooled chocolate ganache over the top, allowing it to drip down the sides.

  2. Decorate with Strawberries: Arrange the chocolate-covered strawberries on top of the cheesecake, creating a beautiful display.

  3. Chill Again: Refrigerate the assembled cheesecake for an additional hour to allow the ganache to set completely.

Serving Suggestions

When it’s time to serve your Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake, consider the following tips:

  • Slice with Care: Use a sharp knife, running it under hot water before each cut for clean slices.
  • Garnish: Consider adding whipped cream or a dusting of cocoa powder for an extra touch.
  • Pairing: This cheesecake pairs wonderfully with a cup of coffee or a glass of dessert wine.

Final Thoughts

Creating a Chocolate Covered Strawberry Cheesecake is a rewarding culinary adventure. The layers of rich creaminess, the indulgence of chocolate, and the freshness of strawberries come together to create a dessert that’s not only a feast for the taste buds but also a visual delight. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging in a well-deserved treat, this cheesecake is sure to impress.

Experiment with variations, such as using different fruits or flavors in the cheesecake, to make it your own. The possibilities are endless, but one thing is for sure: each slice will bring joy and satisfaction to all who partake. Happy baking!
