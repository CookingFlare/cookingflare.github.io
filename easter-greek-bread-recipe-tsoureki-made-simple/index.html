Easter Greek Bread Recipe (Tsoureki) Made Simple


Greek Easter traditions are known for their vibrant celebrations, distinctive flavors, and cherished customs that bring families together. Among the many culinary delights that grace tables during this festive season, Tsoureki—a soft, slightly sweet braided bread—holds a special place of honor. This traditional Greek bread is as symbolic as it is delicious. Its unique flavor, enriched with hints of orange zest, vanilla, and the aromatic mastic or mahleb spices, makes it a central part of Easter festivities.

The process of making Tsoureki may seem daunting at first glance, but this simplified recipe breaks it down into manageable steps, ensuring that even novice bakers can create a beautiful masterpiece worthy of celebrating. Ready to embrace the rich cultural heritage of Greek Easter? Let’s dive into this rewarding baking experience!

What is Tsoureki?

Tsoureki is much more than a bread; it’s a tradition and a symbol of celebration. This braided bread is typically served during Easter and carries symbolic meaning. The three-strand braid is said to represent the Holy Trinity, while the red-dyed eggs often nestled into the dough symbolize the blood of Christ and rebirth. Its slightly sweet flavor and tender texture make it irresistible, and its lightly spiced, aromatic profile captures the essence of springtime in Greece.

While Tsoureki is traditionally flavored with unique ingredients like mahleb (ground cherry seed) and mastic resin, these can sometimes be tricky to find outside Greek stores. Fortunately, this simplified recipe provides easy-to-source alternatives without sacrificing the authentic taste.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Gathering the right ingredients is key to crafting a perfect Tsoureki. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Dough:

  • 4½ cups (540g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 package (2¾ tsp) active dry yeast
  • ½ cup (120ml) lukewarm milk
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup (60ml) lukewarm water
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp orange zest (or lemon zest for variation)
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • Optional: ½ tsp ground mahleb or ½ tsp crushed mastic resin for traditional flavor

For Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tbsp milk

For Garnish:

  • Slivered almonds or sesame seeds (optional)
  • Red-dyed hard-boiled eggs (optional, for decoration)

Step-By-Step Instructions

Making Tsoureki requires patience, as the dough needs time to rise and develop its flavors. Follow these simplified steps, and soon you’ll have a stunning, golden-brown braided bread ready to impress your family and friends.

1. Activate the Yeast

In a bowl, combine the lukewarm water and active dry yeast, along with 1 teaspoon of sugar. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes until it begins to foam. This step ensures your yeast is alive and ready to work its magic.

2. Prepare the Dough Mixture

In a large mixing bowl or the bowl of a stand mixer, whisk together the lukewarm milk, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, orange zest, and cinnamon. If you’re using mahleb or mastic, add them at this stage for the authentic flavor profile. Once the yeast mixture is foamy, add it to the bowl.

Gradually incorporate the flour, one cup at a time, mixing on low speed or using a wooden spoon. Continue until the mixture forms a sticky dough. Switch to kneading—either by hand or using a mixer fitted with a dough hook—and add the softened butter in small chunks as you go. Knead for about 8–10 minutes (or longer if kneading by hand) until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

3. Allow the Dough to Rise

Transfer the dough to a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free environment for 1½ to 2 hours. The dough should double in size.

Pro Tip: If you’re struggling to find a warm spot, preheat your oven to 85°F (30°C), turn it off, and place the bowl inside.

4. Shape and Braid the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release air bubbles and transfer it to a floured surface. Divide the dough into three equal portions. Roll each piece into a long rope, roughly 15–18 inches in length. Align the ropes side by side and braid them loosely, tucking the ends underneath to secure the shape.

If desired, place dyed red eggs gently into the braid, press them slightly so they’re nestled but not completely embedded in the dough.

5. Second Rise

Place the braided dough on a parchment-lined baking sheet or greased round pan. Cover with a towel or plastic wrap and let it rise again for 30–45 minutes. This second rise gives Tsoureki its signature fluffy texture.

6. Egg Wash and Garnish

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Whisk together the egg yolk and milk to make an egg wash. Gently brush it over the surface of the risen dough for a beautiful golden finish. Sprinkle with almonds or sesame seeds if desired.

7. Bake

Bake the Tsoureki in the preheated oven for 25–30 minutes or until the top is rich golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Keep an eye on it, as overbaking can dry out the bread.

If the top browns too quickly, tent it loosely with aluminum foil for the remainder of the baking time.

8. Cool and Serve

Once baked, allow the Tsoureki to cool on a wire rack before slicing. While traditionally served as part of Easter celebrations, it’s equally delightful as a breakfast or snack paired with coffee, tea, or a spread of jam.

Tips for Perfect Tsoureki

Creating Tsoureki isn’t just about following the recipe; it’s also about honing your baking technique. Here are some tips to ensure success:

  • Use room-temperature ingredients: This helps the dough mix smoothly and rise effectively.
  • Patience is key: Allow plenty of time for the dough to rise. Skipping this step can result in dense bread lacking the characteristic fluffiness.
  • Don’t overwork the dough: While kneading is important for developing gluten, overworking it can lead to tough bread.
  • Experiment with flavors: If mahleb or mastic are unavailable, orange zest and vanilla create a wonderfully aromatic profile. Almond or coconut extracts can also be used for unique twists.
  • Store leftovers properly: Wrap the cooled Tsoureki tightly and store at room temperature for up to three days. It can also be frozen for longer preservation.

A Tradition Worth Celebrating

Making Tsoureki is more than baking; it’s participating in a centuries-old tradition that unites loved ones through shared experiences and flavors. While it’s most prominent during Easter, Tsoureki’s appeal extends well beyond the Mediterranean spring celebrations. Its soft texture, lightly sweet taste, and braided elegance make it suitable for numerous occasions.

Whether you’re reviving a family tradition or simply exploring the rich culinary heritage of Greece, baking Tsoureki is immensely rewarding. This simplified approach ensures that every step is manageable and enjoyable, so you’re left with not only a delicious loaf but also a sense of accomplishment.

Ready to embrace the joy of baking? Gather your ingredients, find your rhythm, and prepare to bring a touch of Greece into your kitchen. As the rich aroma fills your home, you’ll understand why Tsoureki is such a cherished part of Easter celebrations—it’s a true labor of love!
