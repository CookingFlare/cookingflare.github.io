Cider and Food Pairing Pleasures: Exploring the World of Hard Ciders


When it comes to pairing food with drink, few beverages offer as much diversity and complexity as hard cider. With its rich history dating back to ancient times, cider has evolved over the centuries to become a staple in many cuisines around the world. From sweet and fruity to dry and tangy, hard ciders can complement a wide range of dishes, from savory to sweet. In this article, we’ll delve into the world of hard ciders and explore the art of pairing them with food.

The Basics of Hard Cider

Before we dive into the world of pairing, it’s essential to understand the basics of hard cider. Hard cider is made from fermented apple juice, and its flavor profile can vary greatly depending on factors such as the type of apples used, the level of sweetness, and the method of fermentation. There are several styles of hard cider, including:

  • Sweet ciders: These ciders are made with a higher concentration of sugar and are typically served chilled. They pair well with sweet and savory dishes, such as desserts and cheese plates.
  • Dry ciders: As the name suggests, dry ciders have a lower sugar content and a more acidic taste. They pair well with rich and savory dishes, such as roasted meats and stews.
  • Tannic ciders: These ciders have a high level of tannins, which give them a dry and astringent taste. They pair well with fatty and rich dishes, such as cheese and charcuterie.

Pairing Hard Cider with Food

Now that we’ve covered the basics of hard cider, let’s explore some pairing options. Here are a few of our favorite combinations:

  • Apple and Cheddar: A classic combination that’s hard to beat. Sweet ciders pair perfectly with sharp cheddar cheese, while dry ciders complement the rich flavor of aged cheddar.
  • Cider-Glazed Pork Chops: Sweet ciders are a natural match for pork chops glazed with a mixture of cider, honey, and spices. The sweetness of the cider complements the savory flavor of the pork.
  • Roasted Brussels Sprouts: Dry ciders pair well with roasted Brussels sprouts tossed with bacon and balsamic vinegar. The acidity of the cider cuts through the richness of the dish.
  • Cheese and Charcuterie: Tannic ciders are a great match for cheese and charcuterie plates. The dryness of the cider complements the richness of the cheese and meats.
  • Spiced Apple Cider Donuts: Sweet ciders are a perfect match for spiced apple cider donuts. The sweetness of the cider complements the sweetness of the donuts, while the spices add a warm and cozy touch.

Exploring the World of Hard Ciders

Hard cider is a global beverage, with producers from around the world offering their own unique takes on the classic drink. Here are a few of our favorite hard cider producers:

  • France: French ciders are known for their complexity and nuance. Try the Cidre Bouche from the Loire Valley for a dry and tangy cider.
  • England: English ciders are often sweeter and fruitier than their French counterparts. Try the Strongbow from Somerset for a classic English cider.
  • United States: American ciders are known for their diversity and creativity. Try the cider from the 2 Towns Ciderhouse in Oregon for a dry and tannic cider.

Conclusion

Hard cider is a versatile and complex beverage that can be paired with a wide range of dishes. Whether you prefer sweet or dry ciders, there’s a style out there for you. By exploring the world of hard ciders and learning about the different styles and pairing options, you can elevate your cooking and entertaining game. So next time you’re planning a dinner party or just looking for a new drink to try, consider reaching for a glass of hard cider. Your taste buds will thank you.
