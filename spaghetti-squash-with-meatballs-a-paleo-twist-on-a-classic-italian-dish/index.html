Spaghetti Squash with Meatballs: A Paleo Twist on a Classic Italian Dish


Spaghetti squash is a game-changer for anyone looking to reduce their carb intake or simply mix things up in the kitchen. When paired with homemade meatballs, you get a dish that’s not only delicious, but also paleo-friendly. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a twist on the classic Italian dish that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.

The Beauty of Spaghetti Squash

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s talk about the star of the show: spaghetti squash. This type of squash is a great source of fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making it a nutritious addition to any meal. When cooked, its flesh naturally separates into strands that resemble spaghetti, hence the name. It’s a great low-carb alternative to traditional pasta, and it’s also gluten-free, making it a great option for those with dietary restrictions.

The Meatballs

Now that we’ve established the importance of spaghetti squash, let’s talk about the meatballs. Ground beef is a classic choice for meatballs, but for this recipe, we’ll be using a combination of ground beef and pork. The pork adds a richness and tenderness to the meatballs that’s hard to beat. We’ll also be using some finely chopped onion, garlic, and parsley to add some extra flavor.

Here’s the recipe for the meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

The Assembly

Now that we have our meatballs and spaghetti squash, it’s time to bring everything together. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds. Place the squash on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Roast the squash in the oven for about 45 minutes, or until it’s tender and easily shreds with a fork.

While the squash is cooking, form the meatball mixture into balls, about 1 1/2 inches (3.8 cm) in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and drizzle with olive oil. Bake the meatballs in the oven for about 18-20 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and lightly browned on the outside.

The Finishing Touches

Once the squash and meatballs are cooked, it’s time to assemble the dish. Remove the squash from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Use a fork to shred the squash into strands. Add the meatballs to the squash and toss everything together. You can add some marinara sauce to the dish if you like, but we prefer to keep things simple and let the natural flavors of the squash and meatballs shine through.

The Verdict

Spaghetti squash with meatballs is a game-changing recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your household. It’s a paleo-friendly twist on a classic Italian dish that’s both delicious and nutritious. The spaghetti squash adds a fun and unique texture to the dish, while the meatballs provide a rich and savory flavor. Whether you’re a meatball lover or just looking for a new way to cook spaghetti squash, this recipe is sure to please.

Tips and Variations

  • Use different types of meat for the meatballs, such as turkey or chicken, to change up the flavor.
  • Add some red pepper flakes to the meatball mixture for an extra kick of heat.
  • Use fresh basil instead of parsley for a different flavor.
  • Add some diced bell peppers or mushrooms to the meatball mixture for extra flavor and nutrients.
  • Serve the dish with some roasted vegetables, such as broccoli or Brussels sprouts, for a well-rounded meal.
