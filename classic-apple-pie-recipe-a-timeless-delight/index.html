Classic Apple Pie Recipe: A Timeless Delight


Nothing quite compares to the comforting aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the air. With its flaky golden crust, tender cinnamon-laced apples, and a touch of sweetness, the classic apple pie has stood the test of time as a beloved dessert. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to create your very own homemade apple pie that will leave your taste buds dancing with delight.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ½ cup ice-cold water

For the filling:

  • 6-7 medium-sized apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the topping:

  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt for the crust. Add the cold, cubed butter and, using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing it into the flour-butter mixture until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape each portion into a disk, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the apple filling. In a separate bowl, combine the thinly sliced apples, granulated sugar, all-purpose flour, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss everything together until the apples are evenly coated.

  5. Roll out one of the chilled dough disks on a lightly floured surface until it forms a circle large enough to fit your pie dish. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides.

  6. Fill the pie crust with the prepared apple filling, spreading it out evenly.

  7. Roll out the second chilled dough disk to form the top crust. You can either place the whole sheet of dough on top of the filling or create a lattice pattern by cutting the dough into strips and weaving them together.

  8. Once the top crust is in place, trim any excess dough hanging over the edges of the pie dish. Fold and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  9. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg, and sprinkle the granulated sugar over it for a beautiful golden finish.

  10. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips, and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  11. Once the pie is done, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for at least 2 hours before serving. This will help the filling set and ensure clean slices.

  12. Slice and serve your homemade apple pie warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat.

Tips and Variations

  • To save time, you can use store-bought pie crust instead of making your own from scratch. However, the homemade crust adds an extra level of flavor and flakiness to the pie.

  • Experiment with different types of apples to find your favorite combination. Some apples are better for baking, while others retain their shape and texture. Mixing different varieties can result in a more complex flavor profile.

  • Add a twist to your classic apple pie by incorporating additional ingredients such as caramel, raisins, or nuts. These additions can elevate the flavor and texture of the pie.

  • Serve the apple pie warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a drizzle of caramel sauce for an irresistible dessert that will impress your family and friends.

There you have it—a classic apple pie recipe that will transport you back to simpler times with every bite. Gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a delightful baking adventure. Whether enjoyed as a comforting winter dessert or a centerpiece on your Thanksgiving table, this timeless apple pie is sure to become a beloved favorite for years to come.
