How to Make Homemade Hummus: A Beginner's Recipe


Hummus is a delicious and versatile dip that has its origins in the Middle East, loved for its creamy texture and rich flavor. This beginner-friendly recipe will take you through the simple steps of making homemade hummus, allowing you to impress your friends and family with your culinary skills while enjoying a nutritious snack. Whether you’re serving it at a party, enjoying it as a midday snack, or using it as a spread in your wraps and sandwiches, homemade hummus is always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients You Will Need

To create your own hummus, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) of chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup of tahini (sesame seed paste)
  • 2-3 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice (about 1 medium lemon)
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic, minced (adjust based on preference)
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed for desired consistency)
  • Paprika (for garnish, optional)
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish, optional)

Optional Add-Ins

Feel free to experiment with your hummus by adding some of the following ingredients for a unique twist:

  • Roasted red peppers: For a smoky flavor, blend in a few strips of roasted red pepper.
  • Spices: Experiment with spices like smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, or sumac for added flavor.
  • Herbs: Fresh herbs like cilantro or basil can add a refreshing touch.
  • Olives: Chopped olives can enhance the savory flavor profile of your hummus.

Tools You Will Need

To make your homemade hummus, gather the following tools:

  • Food processor or high-powered blender
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Rubber spatula
  • Serving bowl

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you dive into the blending process, it’s essential to prepare your ingredients. Drain and rinse the chickpeas under cold water to remove excess sodium and canning liquid, which can affect the flavor and texture of your hummus. If you have the time, removing the skins from the chickpeas will result in an even creamier hummus, but this step is optional.

Step 2: Blend the Base

In your food processor, combine the drained chickpeas, tahini, minced garlic, lemon juice, ground cumin, and salt. Blend the mixture on high until it becomes a smooth paste. You may need to stop the processor occasionally to scrape down the sides with a rubber spatula to ensure all ingredients are mixed well.

Step 3: Add Olive Oil and Water

Once your base is smooth, slowly drizzle in the olive oil while the food processor is running. This will help to emulsify the oil into the mixture, creating a creamy consistency.

Next, add water a tablespoon at a time, blending thoroughly after each addition. The amount of water you need will depend on how thick or thin you prefer your hummus. For a thicker hummus, use less water; for a creamier, more spreadable texture, add more.

Step 4: Taste and Adjust

After blending for a few minutes, taste your hummus and adjust the seasoning as needed. You may want to add more lemon juice for tartness, additional salt, or even more garlic for a stronger flavor. Remember, the flavors will deepen as the hummus sits, so it’s often best to start with a little and add more if necessary.

Step 5: Serve and Garnish

Once you’re satisfied with the flavor and texture, transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. Drizzle with a little extra virgin olive oil on top for added richness, and sprinkle with paprika and chopped fresh parsley if desired. This not only enhances the flavor but also adds a pop of color to your dish.

Storing Your Homemade Hummus

If you have leftovers (which is rare because it’s so delicious!), store your homemade hummus in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will keep well for about 4-7 days. You can also freeze hummus for up to three months. When ready to use, simply thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and give it a good stir before serving.

Ways to Enjoy Hummus

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making homemade hummus, let’s explore some delicious ways to enjoy it:

  1. As a Dip: Serve your hummus with an array of fresh vegetables such as carrots, cucumber, bell peppers, and celery for a healthy snack.

  2. In Wraps and Sandwiches: Spread hummus on pita or whole grain wraps, and layer with fresh veggies, grilled chicken, or falafel for a filling meal.

  3. With Pita Bread: Warm some pita bread in the oven and cut it into triangles for a classic pairing with hummus.

  4. As a Salad Dressing: Thin out your hummus with a bit of water or olive oil and use it as a creamy salad dressing.

  5. On Toast: Spread hummus on whole-grain toast and top with sliced avocado, cherry tomatoes, or a poached egg for a nutritious breakfast.

Nutritional Benefits of Hummus

Not only is hummus delicious, but it also packs a nutritional punch. Here are some reasons to indulge in this tasty dip:

  • High in Protein: Chickpeas are an excellent source of plant-based protein, making hummus a great choice for vegetarians and vegans.

  • Rich in Fiber: Hummus contains dietary fiber, which promotes digestive health and can help you feel full longer.

  • Healthy Fats: The olive oil and tahini in hummus provide healthy fats, which are essential for heart health and can help maintain cholesterol levels.

  • Vitamins and Minerals: Hummus is rich in essential vitamins and minerals such as iron, folate, and magnesium, contributing to overall health and wellness.

Conclusion

Making homemade hummus is not only easy but also incredibly rewarding. With just a handful of ingredients and simple steps, you can create a delicious dip that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you keep it classic or add your own unique twist, homemade hummus is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Enjoy experimenting with flavors, share it with friends and family, and most importantly, savor every bite of your homemade creation. Happy cooking!
