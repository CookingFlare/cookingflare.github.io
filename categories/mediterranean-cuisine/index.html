Mediterranean-Inspired Meatless Monday Recipes

Welcome to our Mediterranean-inspired Meatless Monday recipe collection! Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these recipes are bursting with flavor, nutrition, and the vibrant colors of the Mediterranean. From hearty salads to savory stews, these dishes are not only delicious but also a celebration of the diverse and wholesome ingredients found in Mediterranean cuisine. 1. Greek-Style Stuffed Peppers Ingredients: 4 large bell peppers 1 cup cooked quinoa 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional for vegans) 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper to taste Olive oil for drizzling Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese (if using), parsley, garlic, and oregano. Season with salt and pepper. Stuff the bell peppers with the quinoa mixture and place them in a baking dish. Drizzle with olive oil. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the peppers are tender and slightly charred. 2. Eggplant and Lentil Moussaka Ingredients: 2 large eggplants, sliced lengthwise 1 cup dry green or brown lentils 1 onion, finely chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 can crushed tomatoes 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional for vegans) Salt and pepper to taste Olive oil for cooking Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the eggplant slices on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes until tender. In a saucepan, cook the lentils according to the package instructions until tender but not mushy. In a separate pan, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until softened. Add the crushed tomatoes, cinnamon, oregano, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes. In a greased baking dish, layer the roasted eggplant, cooked lentils, and tomato sauce, repeating until all ingredients are used. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese (if using). Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden and bubbling. 3. Mediterranean Chickpea Salad Ingredients: 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1 cucumber, diced 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved 1/2 cup pitted Kalamata olives, halved 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional for vegans) 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, olives, red onion, and parsley. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper. Pour the dressing over the chickpea mixture and toss to combine. Sprinkle with crumbled feta cheese if desired, and serve chilled. Wholesome Mediterranean Lentil Recipes for a Hearty Meal

The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its heart-healthy components, and lentils are a staple ingredient in many Mediterranean dishes. Packed with plant-based protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, lentils are a versatile and nutritious addition to any meal. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delightful and wholesome Mediterranean lentil recipes that are not only delicious but also perfect for a hearty, satisfying meal. 1. Greek Lentil Soup (Fakes) Ingredients: 1 cup green or brown lentils 1 onion, finely chopped 2 carrots, diced 2 celery stalks, chopped 3 garlic cloves, minced 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes 4 cups vegetable broth 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper to taste Fresh parsley for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute. Stir in the lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, and oregano. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30-35 minutes, or until the lentils are tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh parsley. 2. Lebanese Lentil Salad (Mujadara) Ingredients: 1 cup brown or green lentils 1 cup basmati rice 2 large onions, thinly sliced 4 cups water 3 tablespoons olive oil 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon cinnamon Salt and pepper to taste Fresh mint leaves for garnish Instructions: Rinse the lentils and place them in a pot with 4 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender but not mushy. In a separate pot, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are caramelized and crispy. This may take around 20-25 minutes. In another pot, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the rice and sauté for a few minutes. Stir in the cumin, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Add the cooked lentils to the rice and gently combine. Serve the mujadara topped with the caramelized onions and fresh mint leaves. 3. Italian Lentil Stew Ingredients: 1 cup brown lentils 1 onion, diced 2 carrots, diced 2 celery stalks, chopped 3 garlic cloves, minced 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes 4 cups vegetable broth 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar 1 teaspoon dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste Fresh basil for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, sauté the onions, carrots, and celery in olive oil until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute. Stir in the lentils, crushed tomatoes, vegetable broth, balsamic vinegar, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 35-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the Italian lentil stew hot, garnished with fresh basil. These wholesome Mediterranean lentil recipes are not only delicious but also nutritious and satisfying.
