Indulge in Middle Eastern Basbousa: A Sweet Semolina Cake


If you have a sweet tooth and a love for Middle Eastern desserts, then you’re in for a treat! Basbousa, also known as revani or hareeseh, is a delectable sweet semolina cake that hails from the Middle East. This moist and aromatic cake is soaked in a flavorful sugar syrup, making it an irresistible delight for anyone with a penchant for desserts.

The Origin of Basbousa

Basbousa has its roots in Egypt and is a beloved dessert across the Middle East, Turkey, and Greece. The name “basbousa” is derived from the Arabic word for “just a kiss,” which perfectly encapsulates the delicate and tender nature of this dessert. Each bite of basbousa is like a sweet embrace, leaving a lingering taste that beckons for more.

The Ingredients

To embark on this culinary journey, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of coarse semolina
  • 1 cup of desiccated coconut
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • Almonds or pine nuts for garnish
  • For the sugar syrup:
    • 1 ½ cups of sugar
    • 1 ½ cups of water
    • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

The Preparation

Step 1: Making the Batter

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the semolina, desiccated coconut, and sugar.
  2. Add the plain yogurt and melted butter to the dry ingredients.
  3. Mix everything together until well combined and let the batter rest for 15 minutes to allow the semolina to absorb the liquids.
  4. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

Step 2: Baking the Basbousa

  1. Grease a baking dish with butter and transfer the batter into the dish, spreading it out evenly.
  2. Using a knife, score the batter into diamond or square shapes, and place an almond or pine nut in the center of each piece.
  3. Bake the basbousa in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 3: Making the Sugar Syrup

  1. While the basbousa is baking, prepare the sugar syrup by combining the sugar, water, and lemon juice in a saucepan.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until it slightly thickens.
  3. Once the basbousa is out of the oven, immediately pour the warm sugar syrup over the hot cake, ensuring that it is evenly distributed.

Serving and Enjoying Basbousa

Allow the basbousa to cool and absorb the syrup for at least 2 hours before serving. This sweet semolina cake is best enjoyed with a cup of hot tea or aromatic Arabic coffee. The combination of the moist, fragrant cake and the sweet, citrusy syrup is a match made in dessert heaven.

In Conclusion

Indulging in the delightful flavors of basbousa is a sensory experience that transports you to the heart of the Middle East. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a sweet treat, this easy-to-make dessert is sure to impress and satisfy. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the enchanting taste of Middle Eastern basbousa.
