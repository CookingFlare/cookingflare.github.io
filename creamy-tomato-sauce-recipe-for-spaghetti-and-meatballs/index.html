Creamy Tomato Sauce Recipe for Spaghetti and Meatballs


Who doesn’t love a good plate of spaghetti and meatballs? It’s a classic dish that brings comfort and satisfaction with every bite. And what’s better than a delicious plate of spaghetti and meatballs? One that is topped with a creamy tomato sauce bursting with flavor! In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make a creamy tomato sauce that will take your spaghetti and meatballs to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a little kick)
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the crushed tomatoes to the saucepan, along with the dried basil, dried oregano, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir well to combine.

  3. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. After 20 minutes, stir in the heavy cream. Continue to simmer the sauce for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Remove the sauce from the heat and let it cool slightly. At this point, you can use an immersion blender or a regular blender to blend the sauce until smooth. This step is optional, but it will give your sauce a velvety texture.

  6. Return the sauce to the heat and simmer for another 5 minutes to heat it through. If the sauce appears too thick, you can add a splash of water to thin it out.

  7. While the sauce is simmering, cook your spaghetti according to the package instructions, until al dente. Drain the spaghetti and set it aside.

  8. Serve the creamy tomato sauce over a plate of spaghetti and meatballs. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for an extra pop of flavor and color.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a chunkier sauce, you can use diced tomatoes instead of crushed tomatoes.

  • For a vegetarian version, you can omit the meatballs and serve the sauce over your favorite plant-based protein or simply enjoy it with spaghetti.

  • Feel free to add other ingredients to the sauce, such as sautéed mushrooms, bell peppers, or grated Parmesan cheese, to customize it to your liking.

  • Leftover sauce can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it on the stovetop or in the microwave before serving.

So there you have it – a creamy tomato sauce recipe that will elevate your spaghetti and meatballs to a whole new level. The combination of the rich and velvety sauce with the tender meatballs and al dente spaghetti is simply irresistible. Give this recipe a try and prepare to be amazed by the burst of flavors in every bite. Enjoy!
