Vietnamese Pho Ga with Chicken and Rice Noodle Soup Recipe

If you’re a fan of Vietnamese cuisine, you’ve probably heard of the famous and comforting dish called Pho. Traditionally made with beef, Pho also has a chicken version known as Pho Ga, which is equally delicious and satisfying. This aromatic and flavorful soup is perfect for any occasion, whether you’re craving a warm and nourishing meal or looking to impress your dinner guests with an exotic culinary delight. Ingredients For the Broth: 1 whole chicken, about 3-4 pounds 1 large onion, peeled and halved 3-inch piece of fresh ginger, halved lengthwise 2 cinnamon sticks 3 star anise 4 cloves 1 cardamom pod 1 teaspoon coriander seeds 8 cups of chicken broth 2 tablespoons fish sauce 1 tablespoon sugar Salt, to taste For the Soup: 1 pound rice noodles 2 cups shredded cooked chicken (from the broth) 1 onion, thinly sliced 4 green onions, thinly sliced 1 cup bean sprouts Fresh cilantro, basil, and Thai basil for garnish Lime wedges Sriracha or chili sauce (optional) Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Broth Rinse the chicken under cold water and place it in a large pot. Add enough water to cover the chicken, then bring it to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface. Remove the chicken from the pot and discard the water. Rinse the pot and return the chicken to it. Add the onion, ginger, cinnamon sticks, star anise, cloves, cardamom, and coriander seeds to the pot. Pour in the chicken broth and add fish sauce and sugar. Bring the broth to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, uncovered, for about 1 hour. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface. Once the broth is done, carefully remove the chicken from the pot and let it cool. Shred the meat and set it aside. Step 2: Prepare the Rice Noodles While the broth is simmering, soak the rice noodles in hot water according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside. Step 3: Assemble the Soup Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot, discarding the solids. Bring the strained broth to a boil and season with salt to taste. Divide the cooked rice noodles among serving bowls and top with shredded chicken, sliced onion, and green onions. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and chicken. The hot broth will cook the chicken and warm the noodles. Serve the Pho Ga hot, garnished with bean sprouts, cilantro, basil, Thai basil, lime wedges, and a side of Sriracha or chili sauce for those who like it spicy. Enjoy the Authentic Flavors of Pho Ga! This Vietnamese Pho Ga recipe offers a delightful combination of aromatic herbs, tender chicken, and silky rice noodles in a rich and flavorful broth. The complex blend of spices and the freshness of the garnishes create a harmonious balance of flavors that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Vietnam. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting to explore Vietnamese cuisine, this Pho Ga recipe is a must-try for anyone who appreciates the warmth and comfort of a steaming bowl of soup. ...
