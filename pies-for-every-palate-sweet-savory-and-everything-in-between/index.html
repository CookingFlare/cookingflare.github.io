Pies for Every Palate: Sweet, Savory, and Everything In Between


Pies have a remarkable ability to evoke warmth, nostalgia, and joy. Whether it’s a slice of classic apple pie that transports you back to childhood family gatherings or a rich meat pie that fills your kitchen with irresistible aromas, pies come in diverse flavors and styles that cater to every palate. In this post, we’ll explore the delightful world of pies, ranging from sweet to savory, and even some unique combinations that may surprise you. Get ready to roll up your sleeves and dive into the art of pie-making!

The Art of Pie Crust

Before we delve into specific recipes, let’s talk about the foundation of any great pie: the crust. A flaky, buttery crust can elevate any pie, whether it’s sweet or savory. Here’s a basic pie crust recipe to get you started.

Basic Pie Crust Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for sweet pies)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar (if using).
  2. Add the cubed butter. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, blend the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together.
  4. Divide the dough into two discs, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before rolling out.

Sweet Pies: A Slice of Heaven

1. Classic Apple Pie

Nothing says comfort like a classic apple pie. With a perfectly spiced filling and a golden crust, this pie is a timeless favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 6 to 8 medium apples (Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or a mix)
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Peel, core, and slice the apples. In a large bowl, mix apples with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
  3. Roll out the bottom crust and place it in a pie dish. Fill with the apple mixture and dot with butter.
  4. Roll out the top crust, place it over the filling, and crimp the edges to seal. Cut slits for steam to escape.
  5. Brush the top crust with egg wash and sprinkle with sugar.
  6. Bake for 15 minutes in the preheated oven, then reduce temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 30-35 minutes, until the apples are tender and the crust is golden brown.

2. Rich Chocolate Cream Pie

For chocolate lovers, a rich chocolate cream pie is a must-try. This dessert is indulgent and perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust (see the basic pie crust recipe)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ⅓ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 2 oz. bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the cream, milk, sugar, cocoa powder, cornstarch, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and begins to bubble.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped chocolate and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Pour the chocolate filling into the pre-baked pie crust. Let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  4. Serve with whipped cream on top.

Savory Pies: Comfort in Every Bite

3. Classic Chicken Pot Pie

Warm, hearty, and comforting, chicken pot pie is a dish that brings everyone to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup diced potatoes
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • ½ cup butter
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • 1 pie crust (top and bottom)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions and cook until translucent.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook for 1 minute. Gradually add chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly until thickened.
  4. Add the cooked chicken, carrots, peas, potatoes, and seasonings. Mix until well combined.
  5. Roll out the bottom crust and place it in a pie dish. Fill with the chicken mixture, then cover with the top crust.
  6. Cut slits in the top crust for steam to escape. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the crust is golden brown.

4. Spinach and Feta Pie (Spanakopita)

This Greek-inspired pie is a delightful combination of spinach, feta, and herbs wrapped in flaky phyllo dough.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. fresh spinach (or 10 oz. frozen)
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • ½ cup fresh dill (or 1 tablespoon dried dill)
  • 1 package phyllo dough (16 oz.)
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. If using fresh spinach, sauté it with the onion and garlic until wilted. Let it cool, then squeeze out excess moisture.
  3. In a bowl, combine the spinach mixture, feta, eggs, dill, salt, and pepper.
  4. Layer 5 sheets of phyllo dough in a greased pie dish, brushing each layer with melted butter.
  5. Spread the spinach filling over the phyllo and fold the edges over. Layer more phyllo sheets on top, brushing with butter.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until golden brown.

Unique Pies: A Twist on Tradition

5. Pear and Blue Cheese Tart

This unique pie combines the sweetness of pears with the tanginess of blue cheese, creating a sophisticated dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust (see the basic pie crust recipe)
  • 3 ripe pears, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup crumbled blue cheese
  • ¼ cup walnuts, chopped
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Arrange the sliced pears in the pre-baked pie crust. Sprinkle with blue cheese and walnuts.
  3. Drizzle honey over the top and sprinkle with rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until the pears are tender and the cheese is melted.

6. Thai Chicken Curry Pie

Infuse your pie-making with exotic flavors by trying a Thai chicken curry pie. It’s a fusion dish that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 cup diced vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, peas)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 pie crust (top and bottom)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine coconut milk, red curry paste, fish sauce, and lime juice. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Add shredded chicken and vegetables, cooking until heated through.
  4. Roll out the bottom crust and place it in a pie dish. Fill with the chicken curry mixture and cover with the top crust.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the crust is golden brown.

Pie Tips and Tricks

  • Chill Your Ingredients: For a flakier crust, make sure your butter and water are cold.
  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Mix until just combined to avoid tough crusts.
  • Use a Pie Weights: When blind-baking crusts, use pie weights or dried beans to prevent bubbling.
  • Let It Cool: Allow your pie to cool before slicing for cleaner cuts and better flavor.

Conclusion

Pies are more than just desserts or meals; they are a celebration of flavors and cultures that can be shared with loved ones. From the sweetness of traditional apple pie to the savory richness of chicken pot pie, there is a pie for every palate. So whether you’re hosting a dinner, celebrating a special occasion, or simply treating yourself, embrace the joy of pie-making. With a little creativity and the right recipes, you can craft pies that are not only delicious but also memorable. Happy baking!
