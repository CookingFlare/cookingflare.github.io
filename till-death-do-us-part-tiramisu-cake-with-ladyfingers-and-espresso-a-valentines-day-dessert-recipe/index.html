Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso: A Valentine's Day Dessert Recipe


This Valentine’s Day, surprise your loved one with a decadent and romantic dessert that will truly make their heart skip a beat. Our Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso is the perfect treat to celebrate your love and create lasting memories. This twist on the classic Italian dessert will have your taste buds dancing with delight.

Ingredients

For the cake:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • ½ cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of boiling water

For the filling:

  • 1 ½ cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of mascarpone cheese
  • ½ cup of powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

For the espresso syrup:

  • 1 cup of brewed espresso or strong coffee
  • ¼ cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of coffee liqueur (optional)

For the decoration:

  • Cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well to ensure everything is evenly distributed.

  3. Add the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Mix on medium speed for 2 minutes, until the batter is smooth. Stir in the boiling water, which will make the batter thin.

  4. Pour the batter equally into the prepared cake pans. Bake for approximately 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean. Remove from the oven and let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Transfer the cakes to wire racks to cool completely.

  5. While the cakes are cooling, prepare the filling. In a medium mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream until soft peaks form. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mascarpone cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until well combined. Set aside.

  6. To make the espresso syrup, combine the brewed espresso or strong coffee with the granulated sugar in a small saucepan. Heat over medium heat until the sugar has dissolved. Remove from the heat and stir in the coffee liqueur, if using.

  7. Once the cakes have cooled, slice each cake horizontally into two equal layers. You will have a total of four cake layers.

  8. Place one cake layer on a serving plate and lightly brush it with the espresso syrup. Spread a generous amount of the filling evenly over the cake layer. Repeat this process with the remaining cake layers, syrup, and filling.

  9. Dust the top of the cake with cocoa powder using a fine-mesh sieve. You can also decorate the sides of the cake with cocoa powder, if desired.

  10. Refrigerate the cake for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to allow the flavors to meld together and the cake to firm up.

  11. When ready to serve, slice the Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake into generous portions and enjoy the luscious layers of ladyfingers, espresso-infused cake, and creamy mascarpone filling.

A Romantic Treat for Valentine’s Day

This Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso is the epitome of romance. The combination of rich chocolate cake, velvety mascarpone filling, and the subtle kick of espresso will transport you and your loved one to dessert heaven. The tender ladyfingers soaked in espresso syrup add a delightful texture and depth of flavor.

Whether you’re celebrating Valentine’s Day or simply want to surprise your special someone with a sweet treat, this Tiramisu Cake is sure to impress. The layers of love and devotion that go into creating this cake make it a true labor of love. So, go ahead and indulge in this decadent delight and make your Valentine’s Day celebration truly unforgettable.
