Argentine Chimichurri Masterclass: 7 Pairings to Try


Chimichurri is an iconic Argentine condiment that has captured the hearts and palates of food lovers around the world. This versatile sauce, which typically consists of parsley, garlic, vinegar, olive oil, and a variety of spices, offers a fresh and tangy flavor that can transform any dish. Whether you’re grilling steak, serving seafood, or even looking for a unique way to elevate your vegetables, chimichurri is the perfect accompaniment. In this masterclass, we will explore the history of chimichurri and provide seven delicious pairings to help you make the most out of this vibrant sauce.

The History of Chimichurri

Chimichurri’s origins are deeply rooted in Argentina’s culinary tradition, and its history is as flavorful as the sauce itself. While the exact origins remain somewhat unclear, it is widely believed that chimichurri was introduced by immigrants from Europe, particularly from the Basque region. Over time, the sauce evolved into the beloved Argentine condiment we know today. Though chimichurri is often associated with grilled meats, particularly beef, its versatility has made it popular across many cuisines worldwide.

Ingredients That Make Chimichurri Special

At its core, chimichurri is a simple yet powerful blend of ingredients that work harmoniously to create a rich, aromatic sauce. Here’s a breakdown of the essential components:

  • Parsley: Fresh parsley is the primary herb used in chimichurri, contributing a vibrant green color and earthy flavor.
  • Garlic: Adds depth and a slight pungency, balancing the freshness of the parsley.
  • Olive Oil: This forms the base of the sauce and adds a smooth, rich texture.
  • Vinegar: Red wine vinegar is traditionally used for a tangy and acidic kick.
  • Spices: Red pepper flakes, oregano, and cumin are common additions that bring heat and complexity to the sauce.
  • Salt and Pepper: These seasonings round out the flavor profile, making chimichurri both savory and bright.

While these ingredients form the classic base for chimichurri, many variations exist, including adding lemon, shallots, or even a touch of honey for sweetness.

7 Amazing Pairings for Chimichurri

Now that we understand what makes chimichurri such a delicious and essential sauce, let’s dive into some of the best pairings you can try. These pairings showcase the versatility of chimichurri and how it can enhance a wide range of dishes.

1. Grilled Steak

It’s no secret that chimichurri and steak are a match made in culinary heaven. The bold flavors of the grilled meat pair perfectly with the bright and herby sauce. Argentine cuisine is famous for its beef, and chimichurri is the condiment of choice for enhancing the rich, smoky flavor of a perfectly grilled steak.

Whether you’re cooking a ribeye, flank steak, or even a tenderloin, chimichurri provides the perfect balance of freshness and acidity to cut through the richness of the meat. Drizzle the sauce generously over the steak or serve it on the side for dipping. The robust flavors of chimichurri elevate the meat while complementing its natural umami.

2. Grilled Vegetables

Chimichurri doesn’t just shine when paired with meat; it also makes a wonderful accompaniment to grilled vegetables. The vibrant herbaceous flavors of chimichurri work especially well with smoky vegetables, like bell peppers, zucchini, eggplant, and mushrooms.

To make this pairing, grill your vegetables of choice until they’re tender and slightly charred. Once off the grill, toss them with chimichurri for an extra punch of flavor. You can even marinate the vegetables in chimichurri before grilling for a deeper infusion of flavors.

3. Roasted Chicken

For a lighter pairing, chimichurri is an excellent choice for roasted chicken. The sauce’s tangy acidity cuts through the savory, roasted chicken skin, while the fresh herbs brighten up the entire dish. You can either brush the chicken with chimichurri before roasting or serve it on the side as a dipping sauce.

If you’re looking for a bit of extra flavor, consider marinating the chicken in chimichurri overnight to allow the sauce to penetrate the meat. This will infuse the chicken with all the wonderful flavors of garlic, parsley, and vinegar, creating a truly irresistible dish.

4. Fish and Seafood

While chimichurri is most commonly paired with beef, it’s also a fantastic accompaniment for fish and seafood dishes. The acidity of the vinegar and the brightness of the parsley provide a refreshing contrast to the delicate flavors of the fish.

Try chimichurri with grilled salmon, shrimp skewers, or seared scallops for a delightful and flavorful pairing. For a particularly impressive dish, serve chimichurri over grilled octopus or a light white fish like halibut or cod. The sauce brings a zesty, vibrant element to the seafood without overpowering its natural sweetness.

5. Empanadas

Empanadas are another Argentine staple that pairs beautifully with chimichurri. These golden, flaky pastries can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as beef, chicken, or cheese. When served with a side of chimichurri, they become a flavorful snack or appetizer that’s perfect for any occasion.

The rich, savory filling of the empanada and the herby freshness of chimichurri create an irresistible combination. You can drizzle the chimichurri over the empanada or serve it in a small dish for dipping. Either way, the sauce adds a burst of flavor that complements the flaky crust and filling.

6. Grilled Sausages

Another quintessential Argentine dish that pairs wonderfully with chimichurri is grilled sausage, or “choripán.” This Argentine street food features a chorizo sausage served in a crusty bread roll and topped with chimichurri. The combination of the smoky, spicy sausage and the tangy, herby sauce is a flavor explosion that will leave your taste buds dancing.

If you can’t get your hands on chorizo, any type of grilled sausage will work beautifully with chimichurri. Whether you prefer pork, beef, or chicken sausage, the chimichurri adds an incredible depth of flavor. Serve it with some grilled peppers and onions for a complete, satisfying meal.

7. Potato Salad

Chimichurri can even make a refreshing addition to potato salad. Instead of the typical mayonnaise-based dressing, try tossing your boiled potatoes in chimichurri for a lighter, tangier twist. The bright, herbaceous sauce pairs beautifully with the creamy potatoes, creating a dish that is both rich and refreshing.

For a more substantial salad, you can add additional ingredients like hard-boiled eggs, olives, or fresh herbs. The possibilities are endless, but one thing is certain—chimichurri will elevate your potato salad to the next level.

Tips for Making Perfect Chimichurri

While chimichurri is relatively easy to make, there are a few tips that will ensure your sauce is as flavorful as possible:

  • Use fresh herbs: The freshness of the parsley is essential to creating the vibrant flavor that defines chimichurri. Avoid using dried parsley, as it will not provide the same punch.
  • Balance the acidity: The vinegar is what gives chimichurri its signature tang, but it’s important not to overpower the sauce. Taste as you go and adjust the vinegar to your liking.
  • Let it rest: After making chimichurri, let the sauce sit for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. This resting time also allows the garlic and herbs to infuse the oil and vinegar, resulting in a more flavorful sauce.
  • Store it properly: If you have leftovers, store your chimichurri in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will last for up to a week and can be used on everything from grilled meats to roasted vegetables.

Conclusion

Chimichurri is a deliciously versatile sauce that can elevate a wide variety of dishes. Whether you’re grilling steak, roasting chicken, or serving seafood, chimichurri adds a burst of freshness and acidity that enhances the natural flavors of the food. By experimenting with these seven pairings, you can explore the many ways this iconic Argentine sauce can transform your meals. So, next time you’re preparing a meal, consider making a batch of chimichurri and discovering just how it can take your dishes to new heights!
