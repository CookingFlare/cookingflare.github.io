Tidal Trends: Refreshing Recipes Bringing Seafood Restaurants into the Future


As the world of culinary arts evolves right alongside of technology, seafood restaurants and chefs alike are consistently seeking new ways to impress their guests with innovative dishes that incorporate the freshest ingredients available. Inspired by the constantly-changing tides and the bountiful aquatic ecosystem, here are some refreshing recipes that showcase the flavors and textures of seafood in exciting and unconventional ways. These creations bring classic and modern influences, blending traditional techniques with cutting-edge culinary trends to elevate the dining experience.

1. Seared Scallops with Smoky Hass Avocado Cream

Key Ingredients:

  • 12 large scallops
  • 2 ripe Hass avocados
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Smoked paprika for garnish

In this dish, delicate seared scallops sit atop a velvety avocado cream, bringing a creamy texture and smoky flavor that are sure to impress. Here’s a simple guide on how to make it.

  1. Sear scallops in a hot pan with olive oil and butter until golden brown. Season with salt and pepper, and set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine the ripe Hass avocados, one tablespoon of olive oil, and two tablespoons of unsalted butter. Blend until smooth.
  3. Pass the avocado mixture through a fine mesh strainer to remove the avocado lumps while retaining the creamy texture.
  4. Heat the strained avocado cream in a saucepan, and lightly smoke the cream using a smoking gun or a portable smoker.
  5. For a richer flavor, add a touch of smoked paprika to the avocado cream.
  6. To serve, place avocado cream on a plate and top with seared scallops, then sprinkle with smoked paprika for garnish.

2. Sustainable Seafood Chowder with Cauliflower “Croutons”

Key Ingredients:

  • 2 cups diced sustainable fish (cod, haddock, or pollock)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 peeled and diced potatoes
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable or fish stock
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tsp Old Bay seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into small florets
  • Olive oil for frying

This heartwarming chowder combines texture and sustainability, incorporating gluten-free cauliflower florets that are fried until crispy, similar to traditional croutons. Here is how to make it.

  1. In a large pot, sauté onions and garlic until fragrant over medium heat, adding a bit of olive oil if necessary.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and carrots to the pot, followed by fish stock, Old Bay seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  3. Bring the soup to a simmer and let it cook until the vegetables are tender. Then, add the cubed sustainable fish to the pot, stirring gently.
  4. Once the fish is cooked through and tender, add the canned coconut milk to the chowder and stir well.
  5. For the cauliflower “croutons,” heat a few inches of olive oil in a shallow pan. Fry the cauliflower florets until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Ladle the chowder into bowls, and garnish with cauliflower “croutons” and a sprinkle of Old Bay seasoning.

3. Pan-Seared Swordfish with Miso-Butter Glaze

Key Ingredients:

  • 4 swordfish steaks
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 thinly sliced garlic cloves
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Swordfish, known for its dense texture and robust flavor, benefits from the savory and umami-packed miso glaze in this dish. Here’s the simple guide to make it.

  1. Mix the white miso paste, honey, minced garlic, lemon juice, and melted unsalted butter in a small bowl.

  2. Season the swordfish steaks with salt and pepper, and then brush the miso-butter mixture generously onto both sides of the fish steaks.

  3. Heat a splash of olive oil in a cast iron skillet over high heat, then add the swordfish steaks, miso-butter side down.

  4. Cook for 4-5 minutes, then flip and cook the other side using the same technique.

  5. To make the garlic aioli, blend a head of roasted garlic with 1 cup of mayonnaise, then season with lemon juice and salt.

  6. Serve the pan-seared swordfish with the garlic aioli on the side.

As we navigate the sea of culinary trends and exploration, these refreshing recipes bring a new wave of creativity and sustainability into seafood restaurants. As we embrace innovative techniques and return to the basics of flavor and simplicity, these recipes represent the future of seafood cuisine. Let your creativity flow with the tides, and always strive to make each dish an opportunity to delight and surprise your guests.
