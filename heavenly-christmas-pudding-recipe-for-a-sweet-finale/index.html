Heavenly Christmas Pudding Recipe for a Sweet Finale


December is here, and that means it’s time to start planning your Christmas feast. While the main course and side dishes often steal the spotlight, let’s not forget about the grand finale – the dessert. And what better way to end a festive meal than with a heavenly Christmas pudding? This traditional British dessert is rich, moist, and packed with flavors that will leave your taste buds dancing with joy. So, let’s dive straight into the recipe and create a sweet masterpiece that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1 cup currants
  • 1 cup sultanas
  • 1 cup chopped dates
  • 1 cup candied peel
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup shredded suet
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon mixed spice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chopped almonds
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 4 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup stout or dark beer

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the raisins, currants, sultanas, chopped dates, and candied peel. Give them a good mix to distribute the fruits evenly.

  2. Add the breadcrumbs, brown sugar, all-purpose flour, shredded suet, cocoa powder, mixed spice, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, salt, and chopped almonds to the fruit mixture. Stir well to combine all the dry ingredients.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the lemon zest, orange zest, lemon juice, orange juice, beaten eggs, and stout or dark beer. Whisk until everything is well incorporated.

  4. Pour the wet mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Stir everything together until you have a thick, sticky batter. Make sure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  5. Now, it’s time to prepare the pudding basin. Grease a 2-pint (1.2-liter) pudding basin with butter or cooking spray. Line the bottom with a circle of parchment paper.

  6. Spoon the pudding batter into the prepared basin, pressing it down firmly. Smooth the top with the back of a spoon.

  7. Take a large piece of parchment paper and fold a pleat in the center. Place it over the basin, with the pleat in the middle, and secure it tightly with kitchen twine or a rubber band.

  8. Create a handle by tying twine around the basin, making a loop to lift the pudding in and out of the pot.

  9. Fill a large pot with enough boiling water to come halfway up the sides of the pudding basin. Place a heatproof plate or a trivet at the bottom to prevent direct heat.

  10. Carefully lower the pudding basin into the pot, making sure the water doesn’t spill into the pudding. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and let it steam on low heat for 6 hours. Check the water level occasionally and top up with boiling water if needed.

  11. After 6 hours, remove the pudding basin from the pot and let it cool completely. Once cool, remove the parchment paper and replace it with a fresh one. Cover the basin tightly with plastic wrap or foil and store it in a cool, dry place until Christmas day.

  12. On the day you plan to serve the pudding, steam it again for 2 hours following the same process as before.

  13. Once the pudding is steamed, carefully remove it from the pot and let it cool slightly. Run a knife around the edge of the basin to loosen the pudding, then turn it out onto a serving plate.

  14. Serve your heavenly Christmas pudding warm with a dollop of brandy butter, a drizzle of warm custard, or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Enjoy the rich, indulgent flavors and let the sweetness of the pudding bring a delightful end to your Christmas feast.

Conclusion

Christmas is a time for celebration, joy, and indulgence. And what better way to indulge than with a heavenly Christmas pudding? This traditional dessert will not only satisfy your sweet tooth but also bring a sense of nostalgia and warmth to your holiday table. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and create a pudding that will leave everyone in awe. May your Christmas be filled with love, laughter, and the sweetness of this delightful dessert. Happy holidays and happy cooking!
