Slow Cooker Hawaiian Pulled Pork: Sweet and Savory


If you’re looking for a delicious and easy-to-make dish that will transport your taste buds to a tropical paradise, then look no further than this Slow Cooker Hawaiian Pulled Pork recipe. This mouthwatering dish combines the sweetness of pineapple with the savory flavors of pork, creating a tantalizing culinary experience that is sure to impress your family and friends. Plus, the convenience of using a slow cooker makes this recipe a perfect choice for busy weeknights or weekend gatherings. Let’s dive into the details of this delightful dish and learn how to bring a taste of Hawaii to your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pounds of pork shoulder, trimmed of excess fat
  • 1 cup of pineapple juice
  • 1/2 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of ketchup
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional, for a hint of heat)
  • Sliced pineapple, for serving
  • Hawaiian rolls or your favorite sandwich buns, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork: Start by trimming any excess fat from the pork shoulder and then placing it in the slow cooker.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a mixing bowl, combine the pineapple juice, soy sauce, ketchup, brown sugar, rice vinegar, minced garlic, grated ginger, sesame oil, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Whisk the ingredients together until well combined.

  3. Pour the Sauce over the Pork: Pour the sauce over the pork in the slow cooker, making sure to coat the meat evenly.

  4. Cook the Pork: Cover the slow cooker and cook the pork on low for 8-10 hours, or on high for 4-5 hours, until the meat is tender and easily shreds with a fork.

  5. Shred the Pork: Once the pork is cooked, use two forks to shred the meat directly in the slow cooker, allowing it to soak up the flavorful sauce.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: To serve, spoon the Hawaiian pulled pork onto the sliced pineapple and sandwich buns. The sweet and savory flavors of the pork pair perfectly with the freshness of the pineapple and the softness of the buns, creating a truly delightful eating experience.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a splash of lime juice or a sprinkle of fresh cilantro to the finished pulled pork.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can transfer the cooking liquid to a saucepan after shredding the pork and simmer it over medium heat until it reaches your desired consistency, then pour it back over the meat.

Final Thoughts

This Slow Cooker Hawaiian Pulled Pork recipe is a celebration of tropical flavors and the convenience of slow cooking. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, a family gathering, or simply craving a taste of the islands, this dish is a surefire way to elevate any meal. The tender, juicy pork infused with the sweetness of pineapple and the zing of ginger and garlic will make this recipe a favorite in your culinary repertoire. So, grab your slow cooker, gather the ingredients, and get ready to savor the irresistible blend of sweet and savory in every bite.
