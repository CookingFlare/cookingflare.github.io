The Best Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich Recipe


If there’s one iconic sandwich that has captured the hearts (and taste buds) of food enthusiasts across America, it’s the legendary Philly cheesesteak. Originating from Philadelphia, this hearty sandwich is a delicious combination of thinly sliced steak, melted cheese, and sautéed onions, all nestled within a soft hoagie roll. Whether you’re a fan of gooey cheese, savory beef, or just downright delicious comfort food, mastering the art of making a Philly cheesesteak at home is a culinary adventure worth undertaking.

Ingredients

For the Steak:

  • 1 lb ribeye steak, thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

For the Sandwich:

  • 4 hoagie rolls, sliced open
  • 2 cups provolone cheese, shredded or sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Begin by thinly slicing the ribeye steak against the grain. This ensures tender meat that’s easy to bite through in the sandwich. Season the steak generously with salt and pepper, ensuring each piece is coated evenly.

Slice the onions and bell peppers into thin strips. These vegetables add a sweet and savory flavor profile to the sandwich.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large skillet or cast-iron pan, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions (and bell peppers if using) to the skillet and sauté until they are caramelized and golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper to enhance the flavors. Once done, transfer the onions and peppers to a plate and set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Steak

Using the same skillet, heat another tablespoon of vegetable oil over high heat until it just begins to smoke. Add the seasoned steak slices in a single layer, ensuring they are not overcrowded. Cook the steak for 2-3 minutes per side, allowing it to develop a golden-brown crust while remaining tender inside. Avoid overcooking to maintain juiciness.

Step 4: Assemble the Sandwiches

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Split the hoagie rolls lengthwise and arrange them on a baking sheet. Place a generous amount of provolone cheese on one side of each roll.

Divide the cooked steak evenly among the rolls, spreading it over the cheese. Top the steak with the sautéed onions and bell peppers, distributing them evenly for each sandwich.

Step 5: Bake the Sandwiches

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the sandwiches for 5-7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the bread is lightly toasted.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Remove the sandwiches from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. Optionally, you can add condiments like ketchup, hot sauce, or mayo to taste. Serve hot and enjoy the deliciousness of homemade Philly cheesesteak sandwiches!

Tips for the Perfect Philly Cheesesteak

  • Choose the Right Cut: Ribeye steak is traditionally used for Philly cheesesteaks due to its marbling and flavor. If unavailable, you can use sirloin or flank steak as alternatives.

  • Cheese Options: While provolone is classic, feel free to experiment with other cheeses like American or Whiz for a different flavor profile.

  • Sautéing Technique: Ensure your skillet is hot enough to caramelize the onions and peppers quickly without burning them, adding depth to the sandwich’s flavor.

  • Bread Matters: Authentic hoagie rolls are soft yet sturdy enough to hold the filling. If unavailable, opt for a soft Italian or French bread.

  • Customize to Taste: Feel free to add mushrooms, jalapeños, or your favorite toppings to personalize your Philly cheesesteak.

Mastering the art of making a Philly cheesesteak sandwich at home is not just about following a recipe; it’s about embracing a culinary tradition and creating a dish that delights the senses. With a few quality ingredients and some kitchen finesse, you can savor the authentic flavors of Philadelphia right in your own home. Whether it’s game day, a family gathering, or simply a craving for comfort food, a homemade Philly cheesesteak sandwich is sure to hit the spot. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the skillet, and get ready to enjoy one of America’s most beloved sandwiches!
