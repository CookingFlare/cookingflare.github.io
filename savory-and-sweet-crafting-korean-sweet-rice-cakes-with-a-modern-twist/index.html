Savory and Sweet: Crafting Korean Sweet Rice Cakes with a Modern Twist


Rice cakes, or tteok, are a beloved traditional Korean snack that has been enjoyed for centuries. Made from glutinous rice flour, these chewy and delicious cakes come in various shapes and flavors. While the classic sweet rice cakes are a staple, it’s always exciting to experiment with new flavors and give them a modern twist. In this recipe, we will explore how to create both savory and sweet Korean rice cakes with a contemporary twist.

Savory Rice Cake: Kimchi and Cheese Tteok

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 cup kimchi, finely chopped
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water for boiling

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour with water, following the instructions on the packaging. Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix together the chopped kimchi, shredded mozzarella cheese, soy sauce, sesame oil, minced garlic, sugar, salt, and pepper. Ensure that the ingredients are well combined.
  3. Take a small portion of the rice dough and flatten it into a disc shape. Place a spoonful of the kimchi and cheese mixture in the center of the disc.
  4. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling and seal it tightly, ensuring that no filling is exposed. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.
  5. Bring a pot of water to a boil and gently drop the rice cakes into the boiling water. Cook for about 5-7 minutes or until the rice cakes float to the surface.
  6. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the cooked rice cakes to a plate and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

The combination of the tangy kimchi and gooey melted cheese creates a unique and delicious twist on the traditional savory rice cake. These kimchi and cheese tteok are perfect as a snack or as an appetizer for your next Korean-inspired gathering.

Sweet Rice Cake: Matcha and Red Bean Tteok

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder
  • 1 cup sweetened red bean paste
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Water for boiling

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour with water, following the instructions on the packaging. Knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  2. Divide the dough into two equal portions. In one portion, incorporate the matcha powder, kneading until the dough turns a vibrant green color.
  3. Take a small portion of the plain rice dough and flatten it into a disc shape. Place a spoonful of sweetened red bean paste in the center of the disc.
  4. Take a small portion of the matcha rice dough and flatten it into a thin disc. Place it on top of the plain rice dough with the red bean paste.
  5. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling and seal it tightly, ensuring that no filling is exposed. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.
  6. Bring a pot of water to a boil and gently drop the rice cakes into the boiling water. Cook for about 5-7 minutes or until the rice cakes float to the surface.
  7. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the cooked rice cakes to a plate and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

The combination of the earthy matcha flavor and sweet red bean paste creates a delightful contrast of tastes in these modern sweet rice cakes. Enjoy them as a dessert or as a treat with a cup of tea.

Conclusion

By infusing traditional Korean rice cakes with modern flavors, we can create unique and exciting variations that are sure to impress. Whether you prefer the savory combination of kimchi and cheese or the sweet harmony of matcha and red bean, these contemporary rice cakes are a fantastic way to explore the versatility of this beloved Korean snack. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure with these savory and sweet rice cakes with a modern twist.
