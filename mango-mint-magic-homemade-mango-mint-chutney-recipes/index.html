Mango Mint Magic: Homemade Mango Mint Chutney Recipes


Chutneys are a versatile, delicious, and traditional part of Indian cuisine. Throwing together an assortment of ingredients to create mouthwatering flavors is what chutneys are all about. One exciting and refreshing combination is mango mint chutney, packed with a burst of sweetness and a cool aftertaste. This Homemade Mango Mint Chutney Recipe will delight your taste buds and tantalize your culinary experiences.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 cup raw, unsalted peanuts
  • 1 teaspoon red chilli flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup jaggery or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • A splash of vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: First things first: get all your ingredients together. Rinse the mangoes before peeling and cubing them. Make sure to thoroughly wash the mint leaves and pat them dry.

  2. Make your mango-mint puree: Blend together the mangoes and mint leaves until it forms a smooth puree. This is your base for the chutney.

  3. Roast your peanuts: Heat a tablespoon of the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the peanuts and roast until lightly browned, stirring occasionally. Allow them to cool to room temperature.

  4. Begin chutney preparation: Enjoy the smell of the peanuts as they infuse the kitchen with their nutty scent. Now it’s time to assemble the chutney!

  5. Heat the oil: Heat the remaining oil in a large skillet over medium heat.

  6. Add spices and let them sizzle: Add the red chilli flakes to the pan and let them sizzle in the oil for about thirty seconds. This will ensure the spice flavors infuse the chutney.

  7. Add the mango-mint puree: Stir in your mango-mint puree to the skillet and allow it to cook for approximately five minutes. Keep stirring to prevent sticking.

  8. Introduce the roasted peanuts: Include the cooled roasted peanuts, adding them to the skillet, and let them infuse into the chutney.

  9. Bring the chutney to a boil: Turn up the heat and bring the ingredients to a rolling boil. You’ll notice a beautiful aroma as the chutney comes to life.

  10. Reduce heat and simmer: Lower the heat, letting the chutney simmer while slowly thickening.

  11. Sweeten your chutney: Once the chutney has thickened, add the jaggery or brown sugar. Give it a taste and adjust sweetness as desired.

  12. Finally, add lemon juice, salt, and mix well: Incorporate the lemon juice and salt, stirring thoroughly to ensure the chutney is seasoned to perfection.

  13. Let your chutney rest: Remove the chutney from heat, and allow it to rest for at least an hour. This will give the ingredients time to marry and deepen in flavor.

Now you have a delicious homemade Mango Mint Chutney ready to pair with a wide variety of dishes, from Indian bread to grilled meats.

Enjoy the refreshing character of Mango Mint Chutney, perfect as a sidekick to your favorite flatbread, a gourmet topping on your burgers, or a palate-cleansing contrast to biryanis and curries. With this simple recipe, you’ll be experimenting with other chutney creations and impressing your friends and family in no time!
