Creative Leftover Recipes for After Christmas Dinner


The holiday season brings with it an abundance of joy, cheer, and, let’s be honest, a mountain of delicious food. After the Christmas feast, many of us find ourselves with heaps of leftovers that can seem daunting. Instead of letting those culinary creations languish in the fridge, why not transform them into something new and exciting? From savory casseroles to delectable sandwiches, let’s explore some creative leftover recipes that will inspire you to make the most of your Christmas dinner remnants.

1. Festive Turkey and Cranberry Sliders

Ingredients:

  • Leftover turkey, shredded or sliced
  • Cranberry sauce
  • Slider buns
  • Cream cheese (optional)
  • Fresh arugula or spinach
  • Dijon mustard (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sliders: Cut slider buns in half. If desired, spread a thin layer of cream cheese on the bottom half for added richness.
  2. Layer the Ingredients: On the cream cheese layer, add shredded turkey and spoon a generous dollop of cranberry sauce on top.
  3. Add Greens: Top with fresh arugula or spinach for a pop of color and nutrition.
  4. Finish and Serve: Place the top half of the slider bun on the stack and serve with a side of Dijon mustard for those who enjoy a little kick.

These sliders are not only quick to prepare but also a delightful way to blend the flavors of Christmas in a fun and portable format.

2. Christmas Leftover Soup

Ingredients:

  • Leftover turkey or ham, diced
  • Carrots, diced
  • Celery, diced
  • Onion, chopped
  • Garlic, minced
  • Vegetable or chicken broth
  • Leftover stuffing (optional)
  • Fresh herbs (like thyme or parsley)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add carrots, celery, onion, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add Broth and Protein: Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Stir in the diced turkey or ham and let it cook for another 10 minutes.
  3. Season and Serve: Add salt, pepper, and fresh herbs to taste. If you want to thicken the soup, crumble in some leftover stuffing. Serve hot with crusty bread on the side.

This comforting soup is perfect for those chilly winter days and a great way to warm up while utilizing leftovers.

3. Cheesy Potato and Brussels Sprout Bake

Ingredients:

  • Leftover mashed potatoes
  • Cooked Brussels sprouts, halved
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella work well)
  • Milk or cream
  • Salt and pepper
  • Bread crumbs (optional for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix the Base: In a large bowl, combine leftover mashed potatoes, a splash of milk or cream, salt, and pepper. Mix until creamy.
  3. Layer the Ingredients: In a greased baking dish, layer the mashed potato mixture, followed by the halved Brussels sprouts. Sprinkle shredded cheese on top and, if desired, add a layer of bread crumbs for crunch.
  4. Bake: Bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly. Let it cool slightly before serving; this dish pairs wonderfully with roasted meats.

This bake is a fantastic way to elevate ordinary leftovers into a cheesy, comforting dish that everyone will love.

4. Spiced Sweet Potato and Turkey Hash

Ingredients:

  • Leftover sweet potatoes, cubed
  • Diced leftover turkey
  • Onion, chopped
  • Bell pepper, diced
  • Olive oil
  • Cumin and paprika
  • Eggs (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Base: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and bell pepper, sautéing until soft.
  2. Add Sweet Potatoes and Turkey: Stir in the cubed sweet potatoes and diced turkey. Season with cumin and paprika, cooking until heated through and slightly crispy.
  3. Optional Egg Topping: If you’d like, you can make a well in the hash and crack an egg into it. Cover and cook until the egg is set to your liking.
  4. Garnish and Serve: Top with fresh cilantro and serve warm.

This hash is a hearty breakfast or brunch option that beautifully utilizes your holiday leftovers while bringing new flavor profiles to the table.

5. Leftover Ham and Cheese Quiche

Ingredients:

  • Leftover ham, diced
  • Eggs
  • Milk or cream
  • Shredded cheese (Swiss or cheddar)
  • Pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • Spinach or kale (optional)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, whisk together eggs and milk; season with salt and pepper. Stir in diced ham, shredded cheese, and optional greens.
  3. Assemble the Quiche: Pour the filling into the pie crust, spreading it evenly.
  4. Bake: Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden. Allow it to cool slightly before slicing.

This quiche is perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, providing a delicious way to repurpose your holiday ham.

6. Cranberry and Brie Puff Pastry Bites

Ingredients:

  • Leftover cranberry sauce
  • Brie cheese, cut into small pieces
  • Puff pastry sheets
  • Egg wash (egg mixed with water)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare the Puff Pastry: Roll out puff pastry on a floured surface and cut into squares (about 3x3 inches).
  3. Assemble the Bites: Place a small piece of brie and a teaspoon of cranberry sauce in the center of each square. Fold the pastry over to create pockets and seal the edges with a fork.
  4. Brush and Bake: Brush the tops with egg wash and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.

These delightful bites make for a fantastic appetizer or snack, combining the sweet and savory flavors of Christmas in a flaky pastry.

7. Leftover Stuffing Frittata

Ingredients:

  • Leftover stuffing
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Shredded cheese (optional)
  • Vegetables (like bell peppers, onions, or spinach)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Whisk the Eggs: In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Stir in the leftover stuffing and any additional vegetables you want to include.
  3. Bake: Pour the mixture into a greased oven-safe skillet and sprinkle cheese on top if desired. Bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the frittata is set and slightly golden.
  4. Serve: Let it cool slightly before slicing into wedges. This frittata can be served warm or at room temperature.

A frittata is a versatile and hearty dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, brunch, or dinner, showcasing the flavors of your holiday feast.

Conclusion

Leftovers don’t have to be boring or repetitive. With a little creativity and inspiration, you can transform your Christmas dinner remnants into delightful new dishes that will impress your family and friends. From sliders and soups to fancy quiches and playful puff pastries, these recipes provide a delicious way to savor the flavors of the holidays long after the last plate has been cleared. So, gather your leftovers and get cooking—there’s a world of culinary possibilities waiting for you!
