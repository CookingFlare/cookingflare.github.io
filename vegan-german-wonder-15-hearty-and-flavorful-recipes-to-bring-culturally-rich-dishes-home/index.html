Vegan German Wonder: 15 Hearty and Flavorful Recipes to Bring Culturally Rich Dishes Home


Germany is renowned for its rich culinary heritage, characterized by hearty and sumptuous cuisines that satisfy the soul. While traditionally, German fare heavily features meat-based dishes, this doesn’t deter vegan enthusiasts from savoring the unique flavors the nation has to offer. Say hello to vegan German wonderland! Here are 15 delightful, hearty, and flavorful vegan German recipes that are sure to leave you feeling culturally enriched and satiated.

1. Vegan Sauerkraut

Say goodbye to meat, hello sauerkraut! This tangy and delightful traditional German delicacy, sauerkraut, can be enjoyed by vegans too. What’s even more delightful is that it’s a breeze to make at home.

Ingredients
1 medium cabbage head
2 tablespoons sea salt
2 tablespoons caraway seeds
Enough water to cover the cabbage
  1. Cut the cabbage into wedges, remove the core, and thinly shred it using a mandoline slicer. Place the cabbage in a large bowl, mix in the salt, and massage it until the cabbage starts to soften and wilts.
  2. Let the cabbage sit for about 10 minutes to release excess liquids from the cabbage.
  3. Shape the cabbage into rolls and place them snugly into a clean 1-liter mason jar or fermentation crock.
  4. Press down the cabbage rolls with a clean board until the brine starts to come up and cover them, now add the caraway seeds over the top.
  5. Pour enough water over the cabbage rolls to submerge them entirely. Attach a breathable lid, like a cheesecloth secured with a rubber band or a fermented food lid, and let ferment at room temperature for 2-4 weeks in a dark corner.

2. Vegan Kartoffelpuffer

For those with a penchant for pancakes, kartoffelpuffer (German potato pancakes) is a must-try. This vegan take on the beloved German delicacy transcends expectations as it serves up a nutritious, gluten-free treat.

Ingredients
2 lbs potatoes, peeled and boiled
2 large onions, thinly sliced
1/2 cup all-purpose gluten-free flour
1 teaspoon salt
1/4 cup vegetable oil for frying
  1. Drain and mash the boiled potatoes well until no lumps are left.
  2. In a separate frying pan, sauté the thinly sliced onions in a little oil until caramelized.
  3. Once cooled, mix in the caramelized onions, gluten-free flour, and salt to the mashed potatoes, making sure everything’s well combined.
  4. Heat up about 1/4 cup of vegetable oil in a large frying pan. Using 2 spoons, form medium-sized, flat, pancake-like shapes with the potato mixture.
  5. Fry each kartoffelpuffer until crispy and golden brown on both sides, which usually takes about 5-6 minutes per batch. Drain the fried kartoffelpuffer on paper towels, and serve warm with green applesauce if desired.

3. Vegan Reisfeldsalat

Colorful and delightful, this vegan reisfeldsalat (rice field salad) offers a delightful blend of local and imported ingredients, creating an assortment of textures and flavors to enjoy.

Ingredients
3 cups cooked basmati rice, cooled
1/2 cup thinly-sliced red cabbage
1/2 cup thinly-sliced green bell pepper
1 cucumber, chopped
1/4 cup chopped scallions
1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
2 tablespoons olive oil
2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
1 tablespoon honey (or maple syrup for a vegan alternative)
Salt and pepper, to taste
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled basmati rice, sliced red cabbage, green bell pepper, cucumber, scallions, and cilantro.
  2. Whisk together olive oil, apple cider vinegar, honey (or maple syrup), salt, and pepper in a separate small bowl.
  3. Pour the dressing over the rice mixture, toss gently, and allow the salad to marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator before serving.
