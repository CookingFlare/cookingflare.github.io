How to Make Dairy-Free Whipped Cream: A Step-by-Step Guide


Whipped cream is a beloved topping for desserts, beverages, and various sweet treats. However, for those who are lactose intolerant, vegan, or simply prefer to avoid dairy, traditional whipped cream can be off-limits. The good news is that making dairy-free whipped cream is not only possible but also incredibly simple and delicious! In this guide, we’ll explore the best methods, ingredients, and tips to create a fluffy and creamy dairy-free whipped cream that will elevate your favorite desserts.

Understanding Dairy-Free Alternatives

Before diving into the recipes, it’s important to understand the various dairy-free alternatives available. The most common substitutes for traditional whipped cream include:

  • Coconut Cream: The thick part of canned coconut milk, coconut cream is rich and creamy, making it an ideal base for whipped cream. It has a subtle coconut flavor that works well with many desserts.

  • Aquafaba: The liquid from canned chickpeas, aquafaba is a fantastic vegan alternative that can whip up into a light and airy cream. It’s virtually flavorless, making it highly versatile.

  • Cashew Cream: Soaked cashews blended with water create a creamy base that can be whipped into a thick mixture. This option is nutty in flavor and can be sweetened for a delightful treat.

  • Soy or Almond Cream: Many brands offer whipped cream alternatives made from soy or almond milk. These products are often pre-sweetened and flavored, making them easy to use.

With these options in mind, let’s explore how to make dairy-free whipped cream using coconut cream and aquafaba, two of the most popular and effective alternatives.

Recipe 1: Coconut Cream Whipped Cream

Ingredients

  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk (chilled overnight)
  • 2-4 tablespoons powdered sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • A pinch of salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. Chill the Coconut Milk: Start by placing a can of full-fat coconut milk in the refrigerator overnight. This allows the cream to separate from the liquid, making it easier to whip.

  2. Prepare Your Tools: Gather a mixing bowl and a hand mixer or stand mixer. It’s important to use a cold bowl to help the whipped cream maintain its structure. You can chill the bowl in the freezer for about 10 minutes before use.

  3. Scoop the Coconut Cream: After chilling, open the can of coconut milk without shaking it. You’ll notice that the cream has risen to the top. Carefully scoop out the thick coconut cream into your chilled mixing bowl, leaving the liquid behind.

  4. Whip the Cream: Using your hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the coconut cream on medium-high speed until it becomes fluffy and light. This process usually takes about 2-3 minutes.

  5. Add Sweetener and Flavor: Gradually add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt if desired. Continue to whip the mixture until all ingredients are well combined and the cream reaches your desired level of sweetness and fluffiness.

  6. Taste and Adjust: Taste your whipped cream and adjust the sweetness or flavor as needed. If it’s too thick, you can add a tablespoon of the reserved coconut milk to achieve your desired consistency.

  7. Serve or Store: Your coconut cream whipped cream is now ready to serve! Use it immediately as a topping for cakes, pies, or fruit. If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. Note that the cream may thicken further upon chilling, so give it a quick whip before serving again.

Recipe 2: Aquafaba Whipped Cream

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup aquafaba (liquid from canned chickpeas)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar (optional)
  • 2-4 tablespoons powdered sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Aquafaba: Open a can of chickpeas and drain the liquid into a measuring cup. You will need about 1/2 cup of aquafaba. This liquid is what will whip up into a delightful foam.

  2. Chill Your Bowl: Just like with the coconut cream, it’s a good idea to chill your mixing bowl for at least 10 minutes in the freezer.

  3. Whip the Aquafaba: Pour the aquafaba into your chilled mixing bowl and add the cream of tartar if using. Using your hand mixer or stand mixer, beat the aquafaba on medium-high speed. It may take 5-10 minutes to reach stiff peaks, similar to whipping egg whites.

  4. Add Sweetener and Flavor: Once the aquafaba is whipped to stiff peaks, gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract while continuing to mix. This should take another minute or two.

  5. Taste and Adjust: Just like with the coconut whipped cream, taste your aquafaba whipped cream and adjust the sweetness or flavor as needed.

  6. Serve or Store: Serve your aquafaba whipped cream immediately on desserts or store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. Be aware that it may lose some volume over time, so a quick whip may be needed before serving.

Tips for Perfect Dairy-Free Whipped Cream

  • Use Full-Fat Coconut Milk: For the best results, always choose full-fat coconut milk. Low-fat versions will not whip well.

  • Chill Everything: Keeping all your tools and ingredients cold is essential for achieving the right texture.

  • Experiment with Sweeteners: While powdered sugar is a common choice, feel free to experiment with other sweeteners like maple syrup, agave, or coconut sugar. Just remember that liquid sweeteners may affect the consistency.

  • Flavor Variations: Get creative with flavors! Try adding cocoa powder for chocolate whipped cream, or incorporate different extracts like almond or peppermint for a unique twist.

  • Stability Enhancers: If you need your whipped cream to hold up for an extended period, consider adding a stabilizer like cornstarch or using a tablespoon of powdered gelatin (vegan gelatin alternatives are available) dissolved in water.

Conclusion

Making dairy-free whipped cream at home is an easy and rewarding process. With just a few simple ingredients and these step-by-step instructions, you can create a delicious topping that is perfect for all your favorite desserts. Whether you choose the rich and creamy coconut whipped cream or the light and airy aquafaba version, you’ll be able to enjoy a delightful dairy-free alternative that satisfies your sweet tooth. So go ahead, whip up some dairy-free whipped cream and elevate your desserts to the next level!
