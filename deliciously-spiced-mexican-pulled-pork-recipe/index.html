Deliciously Spiced Mexican Pulled Pork Recipe


If you’re looking for a mouthwatering dish that combines tender meat with vibrant flavors, look no further than this Deliciously Spiced Mexican Pulled Pork Recipe. This dish, often referred to as carnitas, is a traditional Mexican preparation that brings together the best of spices, slow cooking, and the joy of sharing great food with loved ones. Whether you serve it in tacos, burritos, or on its own with rice and beans, this pulled pork is sure to impress. Let’s dive deep into the ingredients, preparation, and cooking techniques that make this recipe a standout.

Ingredients

For the Pork

  • 4 to 5 pounds of pork shoulder (also known as pork butt): The marbling in pork shoulder renders down beautifully during cooking, resulting in tender, juicy pulled pork.
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt: Essential for seasoning the meat and enhancing its natural flavors.
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper: Adds a subtle kick to the dish.
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin: A warm spice that gives a distinct earthy flavor.
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika: Adds a rich depth and smoky flavor to the meat.
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder: For a touch of heat and color.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano: This herb complements the other spices and adds an aromatic quality.
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced: Fresh garlic infuses the meat with incredible flavor.
  • 1 large onion, diced: Adds sweetness and depth to the dish.
  • 1 cup orange juice: The acidity tenderizes the meat while providing a sweet citrus flavor.
  • 1/4 cup lime juice: Brightens the overall dish and balances the richness of the pork.
  • 1 to 2 jalapeños, diced (optional): For those who enjoy some heat, add diced jalapeños for an extra kick.

For Serving

  • Corn tortillas: Perfect for making tacos.
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped: A fresh garnish that adds a burst of flavor.
  • Sliced avocados or guacamole: Creaminess that complements the spices.
  • Pico de gallo or salsa: Fresh toppings that enhance the dish.
  • Lime wedges: A squeeze of lime adds freshness.

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Season the Pork

Start by preparing your pork shoulder. Pat it dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, which helps in achieving a nice sear. In a small bowl, mix together the kosher salt, black pepper, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, and dried oregano. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork shoulder, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

Step 2: Sear the Pork

In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat a couple of tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the seasoned pork shoulder in the pan. Sear it on all sides until it develops a deep golden-brown crust, which should take about 4-5 minutes per side. This step is essential as it locks in the juices and adds an extra layer of flavor.

Step 3: Add Aromatics

Once the pork is beautifully seared, remove it from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until it becomes translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and diced jalapeños (if using), cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant. This mixture will create a flavorful base for the pulled pork.

Step 4: Combine Ingredients

In a slow cooker or a large pot, combine the seared pork shoulder, sautéed onion, garlic, and jalapeños. Pour in the orange juice and lime juice, ensuring the pork is well coated in the liquid. This not only tenderizes the pork but also infuses it with a citrusy flavor that is characteristic of Mexican cuisine.

Step 5: Slow Cook the Pork

If you’re using a slow cooker, cover it and cook on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-6 hours until the meat is fork-tender. If you’re using a Dutch oven, cover it and place it in a preheated oven at 300°F (150°C) for about 4-5 hours. It’s important to check the pork periodically, adding a little water or broth if the liquid level gets too low.

Shredding the Pork

Once the pork is done cooking, carefully remove it from the cooker or pot and place it on a large cutting board. Let it rest for about 10-15 minutes before shredding. Use two forks to pull the meat apart into bite-sized pieces. This is where the magic happens; the pork should be incredibly tender and easy to shred.

Step 6: Crisping the Pulled Pork (Optional)

For an extra layer of texture and flavor, you can crisp up the pulled pork. In the same skillet used for searing, add a little oil and heat it on medium-high. Add the shredded pork in batches, allowing it to sear and develop crispy edges. This step is optional, but it adds a delightful crunch that contrasts beautifully with the tender meat.

Serving Suggestions

Now that your Deliciously Spiced Mexican Pulled Pork is ready, it’s time to serve! Here are some fantastic ways to enjoy it:

  1. Tacos: Warm some corn tortillas and fill them with the pulled pork. Top with fresh cilantro, diced onions, sliced avocados, and a squeeze of lime juice.

  2. Burritos: Layer the pulled pork with rice, beans, cheese, and your favorite toppings, then wrap it up in a large tortilla.

  3. Nachos: Spread tortilla chips on a baking sheet, layer with pulled pork, cheese, jalapeños, and bake until the cheese is melted. Serve with guacamole and salsa.

  4. Salads: For a lighter option, serve the pulled pork over a bed of greens with fresh veggies, avocado, and a zesty vinaigrette.

  5. Rice Bowls: Combine the pulled pork with rice, black beans, corn, and your favorite toppings for a hearty meal.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers (which is often the case because this dish is so hearty), store the pulled pork in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. It can also be frozen for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply thaw in the refrigerator overnight and warm it in a skillet over medium heat, adding a splash of broth or water to keep it moist.

Conclusion

This Deliciously Spiced Mexican Pulled Pork Recipe is not only a feast for the senses but also a celebration of flavors that capture the essence of Mexican cuisine. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply enjoying a cozy meal at home, this dish is sure to be a crowd-pleaser. With its tender, juicy meat infused with spices and citrus, it’s a recipe that you’ll want to make again and again. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in a culinary adventure that will transport you straight to the heart of Mexico! Enjoy!
