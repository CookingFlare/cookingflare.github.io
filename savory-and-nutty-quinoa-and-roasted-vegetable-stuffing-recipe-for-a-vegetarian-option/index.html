Savory and Nutty Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Stuffing Recipe for a Vegetarian Option


Stuffing is a classic dish that often takes center stage during holiday feasts and family gatherings. Traditionally, stuffing recipes rely heavily on bread as the base ingredient. However, if you’re looking for a vegetarian option or simply want to switch things up, this Savory and Nutty Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Stuffing is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness. In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the rinsed quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the quinoa is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Set aside.

  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant.

  4. Add the diced carrots, celery, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, and zucchini to the skillet. Cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  5. Stir in the dried thyme, dried rosemary, dried sage, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional minute to allow the flavors to meld.

  6. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa and the sautéed vegetable mixture. Mix well to ensure the vegetables are evenly distributed throughout the quinoa.

  7. Transfer the quinoa and vegetable mixture to a baking dish. Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes.

  8. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes, or until the top is golden brown.

  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly. Sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Savory and Nutty Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Stuffing is incredibly versatile and can be served as a main dish for a vegetarian meal or as a side dish alongside roasted meats or poultry. It pairs well with a variety of sauces and gravies, adding a flavorful and nutritious element to your plate.

Additional Tips

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables or incorporating different herbs and spices. The options are endless!
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Final Thoughts

This Savory and Nutty Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Stuffing recipe offers a delightful twist on the classic stuffing dish. With its combination of wholesome quinoa, roasted vegetables, and aromatic herbs, it’s sure to impress even the most discerning palates. Whether you’re looking for a vegetarian option or want to add a new dimension to your holiday feast, this stuffing recipe is a winner. Give it a try and let your taste buds soar!
