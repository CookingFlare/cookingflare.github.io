One-Pan Marvels: 20 Easiest Vegan Recipes for Lazy Weeknight Cooking


The hustle and bustle of daily life leaves us with little time to spare in the kitchen, especially when we’re craving a warm and satisfying meal. But fear not! Vegan cooking has never been more accessible or enjoyable with these twenty, effortless, one-pan recipes designed for that lazy weeknight cooking. So, let’s bring out the simplicity in vegan cooking and inspire your palate!

1. Vegan One-Pan Rice and Beans

This dish might just be your new kitchen staple. Begin by warming coconut oil on medium heat. Then, gently sauté onions and garlic. Add some crushed tomatoes, rice, and seasonings (such as cumin, salt, and pepper), making sure to mix well. Pour in vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Gently stir in the beans and cover the pan with foil. Leave it to cook for 25-30 minutes, until the rice is fluffy and the flavors have melded together.

2. Vegan Sausage and Peppers One-Pan Skillet

Saute sliced vegan sausage links with sliced bell peppers and onions in a large, deep skillet until lightly browned. Pour in a jar of vegan marinara sauce and add seasonings like dried oregano and basil. Simmer for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Serve over a bed of cooked pasta for an explosion of flavors in every bite!

3. Vegan One-Pan Quinoa Pilaf

To make the most of your one-pan wonder, begin by sauteing onions and garlic in olive oil. Stir in quinoa and vegetable broth, making sure to cover the quinoa evenly. Add some diced bell peppers, carrots, and peas for a colorful twist. Bring everything to a boil before reducing heat and letting it simmer with a lid on for 18-20 minutes. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper for a delicious and filling meal.

4. Vegan One-Pan Nachos

Layer tortilla chips on a baking sheet and top with a layer of refried beans, followed by shredded lettuce, chopped tomatoes, and sliced jalapenos. Season with cumin, chili powder, and salt to taste. Sprinkle vegan cheese shreds generously over the top and bake for about 10 minutes or until the cheese melts. Garnish with fresh cilantro and sliced avocado for a mouth-watering treat.

5. Vegan Lemon Garlic One-Pan Pasta

No, there’s no mistake. One-pan pasta does, in fact, exist! Start by heating olive oil in a large, deep skillet. Add in pasta, garlic, and lemon juice. Pour in vegetable broth, just enough to cover the pasta. Stir occasionally until the pasta is al dente and the liquid has reduced to a sauce-like consistency. Toss in some cherry tomatoes and let them cook in the warmth for 3-4 minutes. Finish it off with fresh basil, a sprinkle of salt and pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil if needed.

6. Vegan One-Pan Tomato and Basil Frittata

In a well-seasoned cast iron skillet, whisk together almond milk, nutritional yeast, turmeric, salt, and black pepper until well combined. Gently pour in the whisked mixture and evenly scatter diced tomatoes, sliced bell peppers, and fresh basil leaves across the surface. Cover with a lid and bake in a preheated oven for 35-40 minutes. Remove from the oven, let it cool down slightly, and slice into wedges for a delicious vegan breakfast or brunch dish.

7. Vegan One-Pan Chili Tofu Skillet

In a large skillet, sauté sliced onions, bell peppers, and jalapenos until tender. Add crumbled firm tofu, black beans, and corn, cooking until heated through. Stir in a dollop of vegan sour cream and sprinkle of favorite chili seasoning, and allow it to cook for a few more minutes. Served alongside warm tortillas, this is a satisfying and versatile dish that keeps hunger at bay.

8. Vegan One-Pan Creamy Penne Pasta

In a large skillet, sauté garlic and onions in olive oil before adding selected penne pasta and vegetable broth. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the pasta is almost done. Stir in a layer of cashew cream (soaked raw cashews and water blended until smooth), vegan cream cheese, nutritional yeast, and spinach. Allow to cook until the spinach has wilted, and the sauce is hot and creamy. Mix in cherry tomatoes and let them simmer in the sauce for 3-4 minutes. Season, if needed, and serve with crusty bread on the side.

These deceptively easy vegan recipes will undoubtedly become your go-to’s when busy weeknights leave you short on time and ideas. Not only do they bring a broad array of flavors and textures to the palette, but they may also provide a food revolution of one-pan, no-fuss vegan meals that guarantee deliciousness for every meal occasion. Enjoy your cooking journey with these vegan one-pan marvels!
