Cooking for Kids: Kid-Friendly Indian Recipes


Indian cuisine is known for its rich flavors, aromatic spices, and diverse range of dishes. While some might assume that Indian food is too spicy or complex for children, there are plenty of kid-friendly recipes that can introduce them to the wonderful world of Indian flavors. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of easy-to-make and delicious Indian recipes that are sure to please even the pickiest eaters.

Vegetable Biryani

Biryani is a flavorful rice dish that is loved by people of all ages. To make a kid-friendly version, we can load it with colorful vegetables and keep the spices mild. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, beans)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan and add the cumin seeds. Once they start to splutter, add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan and cook for another minute.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and mushy.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables to the pan and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. Now, add the soaked and drained rice to the pan and gently mix it with the vegetables.
  7. Pour 4 cups of water into the pan and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan with a lid, and let it cook for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  8. Once the biryani is ready, fluff the rice with a fork and garnish it with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot.

Paneer Tikka

Paneer tikka is a popular Indian appetizer that can be easily adapted into a kid-friendly recipe. Paneer, a type of Indian cottage cheese, is marinated in a flavorful yogurt-based mixture and then grilled or baked. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup thick yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, gram flour, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth paste.
  2. Add the paneer cubes, bell pepper chunks, and onion chunks to the marinade. Make sure all the pieces are coated evenly. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) or heat a grill pan on medium heat.
  4. Thread the marinated paneer, bell pepper, and onion onto skewers.
  5. If using the oven, place the skewers on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and brush them with oil. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the paneer is golden brown.
  6. If using a grill pan, brush it with oil and cook the skewers for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the paneer is cooked and has grill marks.
  7. Serve the paneer tikka hot with mint chutney or ketchup.

Mango Lassi

No Indian meal is complete without a refreshing beverage, and mango lassi is a favorite among kids and adults alike. This creamy and delicious drink is made with ripe mangoes, yogurt, and a touch of sweetness. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and pitted
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons honey or sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Crushed ice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the ripe mangoes, yogurt, milk, and honey/sugar. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. If desired, add some crushed ice to the blender and blend for a few seconds to make it chilled.
  3. Pour the mango lassi into glasses and serve immediately.

Conclusion

Introducing your kids to Indian cuisine can be a fun and exciting experience. With these kid-friendly recipes, you can create delicious meals that are packed with flavors and nutrients. From the aromatic vegetable biryani to the flavorful paneer tikka and the refreshing mango lassi, these dishes will surely become favorites in your household. So, get your little ones involved in the kitchen and enjoy the journey of exploring the vibrant world of Indian cooking together. Happy cooking!
