Sous Vide Cheesecake: Creamy and Foolproof


Cheesecake is one of those classic desserts that never goes out of style. The rich, creamy texture combined with a smooth, velvety finish makes it a favorite for so many occasions. But let’s face it, achieving the perfect cheesecake—one that’s not too dry, too dense, or cracked—can sometimes feel like an impossible task. That’s where sous vide comes into play.

Sous vide, a French cooking technique meaning “under vacuum,” involves cooking food in a precisely controlled water bath. This method, primarily used for meats, has now found its way into the dessert world, and for good reason. It creates an incredibly consistent temperature, resulting in desserts that are perfectly cooked, every single time. When it comes to cheesecake, sous vide gives you a creamy, smooth, and foolproof result that will elevate your baking game.

In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to make a perfect sous vide cheesecake—from the equipment you’ll need, the ingredients required, and a step-by-step guide to creating this deliciously creamy dessert. Let’s get started!

Why Sous Vide for Cheesecake?

Before diving into the recipe, it’s important to understand why sous vide is such a great method for making cheesecake. Traditionally, cheesecakes are baked in an oven, often leading to uneven cooking and the dreaded cracks on top. Sous vide eliminates these issues by cooking the cheesecake gently and evenly in a water bath, ensuring that it cooks uniformly and maintains its smooth, creamy texture.

The sous vide method also prevents overcooking, which can dry out the cheesecake. Since the temperature of the water bath is precisely controlled, there’s no risk of the cheesecake getting too hot and curdling. You can relax and know that every bite will be as creamy as the last.

Equipment You’ll Need

To get started, you’ll need a few key pieces of equipment. While some of these may seem specialized, don’t worry. They’re easy to find, and you’ll be able to use them for many other sous vide recipes in the future.

  1. Sous Vide Precision Cooker: This is the heart of the sous vide method. The precision cooker allows you to set an exact temperature for the water bath, ensuring your cheesecake cooks perfectly every time.

  2. Large Pot or Container for the Water Bath: You’ll need a vessel to hold the water for the sous vide cooker. A large pot or container that can hold both the water and the jars (or baking pans) is essential.

  3. Jars or Small Ramekins: Since sous vide cooking requires food to be submerged in water, we’ll be using mason jars or small ramekins to cook the cheesecake. These containers keep the cheesecake batter in place while allowing the water to cook the dessert evenly.

  4. Lid or Foil: If you’re using mason jars, it’s a good idea to cover them with lids or foil to prevent water from seeping into the jars during cooking.

  5. Blender or Food Processor: To achieve the creamy consistency of the cheesecake batter, you’ll need a blender or food processor to mix the ingredients.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Cheesecake

Here’s what you’ll need to make a delicious, creamy cheesecake using sous vide:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs (you can substitute with crushed digestive biscuits or your preferred cookie)
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • ¼ tsp ground cinnamon (optional)

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  • 24 oz cream cheese (3 packages), softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream
  • A pinch of salt

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Sous Vide Cheesecake

Now that you have everything ready, let’s get into the fun part: the cooking process. Don’t worry—it’s easy, and the results are totally worth it.

Step 1: Prepare the Water Bath

Start by filling your large pot or container with water. Attach your sous vide precision cooker to the side of the pot, set the temperature to 176°F (80°C), and let the water come to temperature. This temperature is ideal for a creamy, perfectly set cheesecake.

Step 2: Prepare the Crust

In a medium-sized bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, granulated sugar, and ground cinnamon (if using). Add the melted butter and stir until the mixture resembles wet sand. This will be your cheesecake crust.

Take your mason jars or ramekins (depending on the number of servings you want) and evenly distribute the crust mixture among them. Press the crumbs down gently to form a compact layer. You can use the back of a spoon or a small cup to help press down the crust.

Once the crust is ready, set the jars or ramekins aside while you make the cheesecake filling.

Step 3: Make the Cheesecake Filling

In a blender or food processor, combine the softened cream cheese, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, sour cream, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt. Blend until the mixture is smooth and creamy, with no lumps. You want a thick but pourable batter. If the mixture looks too thick, add a little more cream to reach the desired consistency.

Step 4: Pour the Filling into the Jars

Carefully pour the cheesecake filling into each jar or ramekin, leaving a little space at the top for expansion. The filling should be evenly distributed across all jars. Tap each jar gently on the counter to release any air bubbles.

Once all jars are filled, cover them with lids or aluminum foil to prevent any water from getting inside during the sous vide process.

Step 5: Sous Vide Cook the Cheesecake

Place the jars with the cheesecake filling into the preheated water bath. Ensure they’re fully submerged, but not overcrowded. You may need to cook in batches depending on the size of your containers.

Cook the cheesecakes in the sous vide bath for about 1 to 1.5 hours. The beauty of sous vide is that you don’t need to worry about overcooking—just set the timer and let the precision cooker do its magic. The cheesecake will gently cook, maintaining its creamy texture.

Step 6: Chill and Set

Once the cheesecakes have finished cooking, carefully remove the jars from the water bath. Let them cool at room temperature for about 15 minutes, then transfer them to the fridge to chill for at least 4 hours (overnight is best). This chilling period helps the cheesecakes set and gives them that perfectly smooth texture.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy!

After the cheesecakes have chilled and set, you can serve them directly in the jars or remove them carefully if you used ramekins. You can top your sous vide cheesecake with fresh fruit, whipped cream, or even a drizzle of chocolate or caramel sauce for an added touch.

Tips for Success

  • Soften Your Cream Cheese: Ensure that the cream cheese is fully softened before blending. Cold cream cheese can result in lumps, which will affect the smoothness of your cheesecake filling.

  • Don’t Overfill the Jars: Leave a little space at the top of the jars to allow for expansion during cooking.

  • Chill Thoroughly: For the best texture, be sure to chill the cheesecake for several hours before serving. The cheesecake will firm up as it cools, giving it that perfect consistency.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to get creative with the flavor of your cheesecake! Add lemon zest, chocolate chips, or fruit purees to the batter to customize your dessert.

Why Sous Vide Cheesecake Is a Game Changer

Sous vide cheesecake is a game-changer for anyone who has struggled to get that perfect cheesecake texture. With its precise temperature control, sous vide ensures that your cheesecake cooks evenly without the risk of cracking or overcooking. Plus, the ability to cook in individual portions makes it ideal for serving guests or enjoying a slice all to yourself.

The result is a rich, smooth, and creamy cheesecake with a perfect, melt-in-your-mouth texture. Whether you’re new to sous vide cooking or a seasoned pro, this method will make your cheesecake-making experience simple, stress-free, and absolutely delicious.

Conclusion

Making cheesecake sous vide-style is not only easy, but it guarantees a deliciously creamy dessert every time. The gentle water bath ensures that the cheesecake is perfectly cooked, without any of the common issues like cracks or dryness. Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion or simply indulging in a homemade treat, sous vide cheesecake is sure to impress.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a decadent dessert, skip the oven and grab your sous vide precision cooker. This foolproof method will change the way you think about cheesecakes forever!
