Baking with Kids: Simple and Delicious Chocolate Chip Cookies


Baking with kids can be a fun and rewarding experience for both children and parents. Not only does it provide an opportunity to bond and create lasting memories, but it also helps kids develop important skills such as following instructions, measuring ingredients, and understanding the science of baking. One of the best recipes to start with when baking with kids is simple and delicious chocolate chip cookies. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through a step-by-step guide on how to make these scrumptious cookies with your little ones.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (220g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (220g) packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups (340g) semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture into the wet mixture, mixing until well combined.
  5. Finally, fold in the chocolate chips until evenly distributed throughout the dough.

Step 2: Shaping the Cookies

  1. Using a spoon or a cookie scoop, drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between each cookie for spreading.
  2. If your kids are old enough, they can help with this step and get creative with shaping the dough into fun, irregular shapes.

Step 3: Baking the Cookies

  1. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 10 to 12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  2. Remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips for Baking with Kids

  • Assign Age-Appropriate Tasks: Depending on the age of your kids, assign them tasks that are suitable for their skill level. Younger children can help with mixing ingredients, while older kids can assist with shaping the dough and placing it on the baking sheet.
  • Embrace the Mess: Baking with kids can be messy, but it’s all part of the fun! Encourage your kids to get involved and don’t stress about the mess. It’s the experience that counts.
  • Educational Opportunities: Use baking as a learning opportunity for your kids. Discuss the ingredients, measurements, and the science behind baking. This can make the experience both fun and educational for them.

Conclusion

Baking chocolate chip cookies with kids is a wonderful way to spend quality time together while creating something delicious. The joy and sense of accomplishment they’ll feel when biting into a warm, freshly baked cookie is truly priceless. So, gather your little sous chefs and embark on this delightful baking adventure. Happy baking!
