Greek Moussaka Recipe with Eggplant and Ground Beef


Moussaka is a classic Greek dish that combines layers of eggplant, ground beef, and a rich tomato sauce, topped with a creamy béchamel sauce. It’s a hearty and comforting dish that is perfect for a family dinner or a gathering with friends. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to create a delicious and authentic Greek Moussaka.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

For the Béchamel Sauce

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the eggplants by slicing them lengthwise into 1/4-inch thick slices. Sprinkle salt on both sides of the slices and let them sit for about 20 minutes. This will help remove any bitterness from the eggplant. After 20 minutes, rinse the eggplant slices and pat them dry with a paper towel.

  3. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside.

  4. In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of olive oil and sauté the onions and garlic until they become translucent and fragrant. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes.

  5. While the sauce is simmering, heat another tablespoon of olive oil in a separate skillet over medium heat. Cook the eggplant slices in batches until they become golden brown on both sides. Remove the cooked slices from the skillet and set them aside.

  6. To make the béchamel sauce, melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously for about 2 minutes until the mixture turns golden brown. Gradually add the milk while whisking constantly to avoid any lumps from forming. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens. Stir in the nutmeg, salt, and pepper.

  7. Remove the saucepan from the heat and gradually whisk in the beaten eggs. This will give the béchamel sauce a rich and creamy texture.

  8. Now it’s time to assemble the moussaka. In a greased baking dish, arrange a layer of eggplant slices on the bottom. Top with a layer of the ground beef mixture, followed by another layer of eggplant slices. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of eggplant slices on top.

  9. Pour the béchamel sauce over the top layer of eggplant, making sure it covers the entire surface. Smooth out the sauce with a spatula.

  10. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 45 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the moussaka is bubbling around the edges.

  11. Remove the moussaka from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to slice.

  12. Serve the moussaka warm, and enjoy the delicious combination of flavors and textures.

Final Thoughts

Greek Moussaka is a dish that brings together the flavors of the Mediterranean in a comforting and satisfying way. The layers of eggplant, ground beef, and tomato sauce, topped with a creamy béchamel sauce, create a harmony of flavors that will delight your taste buds. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, this Greek Moussaka recipe is sure to impress. Give it a try and experience the taste of Greece in your own kitchen!
