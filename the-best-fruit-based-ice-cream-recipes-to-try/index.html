The Best Fruit-Based Ice Cream Recipes to Try


When it comes to indulging in a creamy, refreshing treat, there’s nothing quite like a bowl of homemade ice cream, especially when it’s made with fresh, juicy fruits. Fruit-based ice creams offer a delightful combination of sweetness and tang, with natural flavors that are both vibrant and satisfying. Whether you’re looking to beat the summer heat, surprise guests with a delicious homemade dessert, or simply want a healthier alternative to traditional ice cream, fruit-based ice cream recipes are the way to go.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best fruit-based ice cream recipes that you can easily make at home, using a variety of fruits for unique flavors. From tropical options like mango and pineapple to classic favorites such as strawberry and peach, you’re sure to find a new favorite treat.

1. Mango Sorbet Ice Cream

Mango sorbet is a popular fruit-based ice cream that’s known for its smooth texture and tropical flavor. The sweetness of ripe mangoes, combined with the slight tartness from a touch of lime, makes this sorbet a refreshing treat, especially during hot summer days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 cup sugar (or more to taste)
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Place the mangoes, lime juice, sugar, and water in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth.
  2. Taste the mixture and add more sugar if desired, depending on the sweetness of your mangoes.
  3. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Once the sorbet reaches a creamy consistency, transfer it to a container and freeze for at least 3 hours before serving.

The result is a refreshing, smooth, and fruity treat that will transport you straight to a tropical paradise.

2. Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream

For those who enjoy the rich, tangy flavors of cheesecake, this strawberry cheesecake ice cream is the perfect combination of creamy, fruity, and indulgent. The addition of fresh strawberries gives it a burst of natural sweetness, while the cream cheese adds a rich, velvety texture that mimics the classic dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the strawberries and 1/4 cup of sugar. Cook over medium heat until the strawberries break down and release their juices (about 5-7 minutes).
  2. Mash the strawberries with a fork or potato masher to create a chunky sauce. Let it cool.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, the remaining sugar, and vanilla extract.
  4. Fold in the cooled strawberry sauce and softened cream cheese, making sure the mixture is smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

This ice cream offers the best of both worlds: the sweet, tart flavor of strawberries and the rich, indulgent texture of cheesecake.

3. Peach and Honey Ice Cream

The natural sweetness of peaches, combined with the floral notes of honey, creates an ice cream that’s perfect for late summer days when peaches are in season. This recipe has a delightful balance of creamy texture and refreshing fruitiness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the peaches and honey. Blend until smooth.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, milk, and vanilla extract.
  3. Fold the peach and honey mixture into the cream mixture until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  5. Once the ice cream is ready, transfer it to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours.

The peach and honey ice cream is incredibly smooth, with the natural sweetness of the fruit and honey coming through in every bite. This is a must-try recipe for peach lovers!

4. Blueberry Lemon Ice Cream

Blueberries and lemons are a match made in heaven. The sweet, slightly tart flavor of blueberries complements the zesty freshness of lemon, making this ice cream both bright and refreshing. This recipe is perfect for anyone who enjoys a combination of sweet and tangy flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the blueberries and sugar. Cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes, until the blueberries start to release their juices and soften.
  2. Mash the blueberries with a fork or potato masher, then add the lemon zest and juice. Let the mixture cool to room temperature.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and whole milk.
  4. Fold the blueberry mixture into the cream and milk mixture until fully combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

This ice cream is a perfect balance of tart and sweet, with the blueberries adding a burst of color and flavor.

5. Pineapple Coconut Ice Cream

For a tropical treat, this pineapple coconut ice cream is a must-try. The sweetness of ripe pineapple pairs perfectly with the creamy richness of coconut milk, creating a smooth, indulgent ice cream that feels like a mini vacation in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh pineapple, chopped
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the pineapple and sugar. Blend until smooth.
  2. Add the coconut milk, heavy cream, and vanilla extract to the pineapple mixture and blend again until fully combined.
  3. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Once the ice cream is ready, transfer it to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

This tropical ice cream is creamy, flavorful, and completely satisfying, with the coconut giving it an exotic twist that perfectly complements the pineapple.

6. Raspberry Lime Sorbet

If you prefer a dairy-free option, raspberry lime sorbet is a fantastic choice. The sweet-tart flavor of raspberries is complemented by the refreshing zest of lime, creating an icy dessert that’s both light and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the raspberries, sugar, lime juice, and water. Blend until smooth.
  2. Taste the mixture and add more sugar or lime juice if desired.
  3. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Transfer the sorbet to a container and freeze for at least 3 hours before serving.

The raspberry lime sorbet is light, tangy, and refreshing—perfect for when you want a dessert that’s not too heavy but still full of flavor.

7. Blackberry Mint Ice Cream

Blackberries, with their deep, rich flavor, are the star of this ice cream. The mint adds a refreshing touch that elevates the flavor profile, making this ice cream an exceptional choice for those who enjoy complex, layered tastes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh blackberries
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the blackberries and sugar. Cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes, until the blackberries begin to break down.
  2. Mash the blackberries and then strain the mixture to remove the seeds.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the cream, milk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the blackberry puree and fresh mint to the cream mixture and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

The blackberry mint ice cream offers a delightful blend of sweet, tart, and fresh flavors, making it a sophisticated and delicious dessert choice.

Conclusion

Fruit-based ice cream is an incredibly versatile and refreshing treat that can be enjoyed year-round. Whether you’re craving something tropical, tangy, or indulgent, there’s a fruit ice cream recipe to satisfy your taste buds. From the tropical richness of mango sorbet to the creamy indulgence of peach and honey ice cream, these recipes are sure to become favorites in your household.

So, gather your ingredients, break out the ice cream maker, and get ready to indulge in some of the best homemade fruit-based ice creams you’ll ever taste!
