Indian Palak Paneer with Spinach and Cheese Recipe


Palak Paneer is a classic Indian dish that combines the vibrant flavors of spinach and paneer, a type of Indian cheese. This vegetarian dish is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Whether you’re a fan of Indian cuisine or looking to try something new, this Palak Paneer recipe is sure to impress your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves, washed and finely chopped
  • 200 grams paneer, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the ghee or vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.

  2. Add the finely chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

  3. Once the onion is cooked, add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Now, it’s time to add the spinach leaves. Stir well and cook until the spinach wilts and reduces in volume. This usually takes around 5-7 minutes.

  5. Once the spinach is cooked, remove the pan from heat and let it cool slightly. Transfer the mixture to a blender or food processor and blend it into a smooth puree. Set aside.

  6. In the same pan, heat a little more ghee or oil and add the paneer cubes. Fry them until they turn golden brown on all sides. Remove the paneer from the pan and set it aside.

  7. Return the pan to the heat and pour in the spinach puree. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Stir well to combine all the flavors.

  8. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If the mixture is too thick, you can add a little water to achieve the desired consistency.

  9. Finally, add the fried paneer cubes to the spinach mixture. Gently stir to coat the paneer evenly with the spinach sauce. Simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes.

  10. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.

Serve the Palak Paneer hot with naan bread or steamed rice for a complete and satisfying meal. This dish pairs well with a side of raita, a cooling yogurt-based condiment, to balance the flavors.

Final Thoughts

Palak Paneer is a delightful dish that showcases the versatility and richness of Indian cuisine. The combination of spinach and paneer creates a creamy and flavorful gravy that perfectly complements the soft and chewy texture of the cheese. This vegetarian recipe is not only a treat for the taste buds but also a great way to incorporate nutrient-rich spinach into your diet.

Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, Palak Paneer is sure to impress your guests. Its vibrant green color and aromatic flavors will transport you to the streets of India. So, put on your apron, gather the ingredients, and get ready to indulge in the delightful flavors of this Indian classic.
