Best Thanksgiving Turkey Leftover Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time of gratitude, family, and, of course, incredible food. But once the holiday feast has ended, chances are you’re left with a mountain of turkey that no one could quite manage to finish. While a traditional turkey sandwich is always a good fallback, why not get creative and elevate those leftovers into delicious, crowd-pleasing dishes? Transform your post-Thanksgiving meals into culinary masterpieces with these thoughtfully crafted recipes that not only help reduce food waste but give your turkey a second life.

1. Creamy Turkey & Wild Rice Soup

There’s something about the rich aroma of a homemade soup simmering away on the stove that makes it perfect autumn comfort food. This hearty turkey and wild rice soup utilizes your leftover turkey and any remaining herbs you may have from Thanksgiving prep.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded turkey
  • 1 cup cooked wild rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups chicken or turkey broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add diced onion, celery, and carrots. Sauté for 5 minutes until softened.
  2. Stir in the garlic and cook for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
  3. Add the turkey broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to a simmer, stir in the wild rice, and let cook for 20 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and flour until smooth. Slowly pour the mixture into the soup, stirring constantly.
  5. Add the shredded turkey, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well and let simmer for another 10 minutes. Serve hot with a side of crusty bread.

2. Turkey Enchiladas

Bring south-of-the-border flair to your Thanksgiving leftovers with turkey enchiladas. This vibrant dish is full of cheesy goodness and packed with flavor, making it the perfect way to spice up your holiday scraps.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups chopped turkey
  • 8 corn or flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 small red onion, finely diced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Olive oil for greasing
  • Optional: diced jalapeño for added spice

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine turkey, 1 cup of cheddar cheese, sour cream, cilantro, red onion, cumin, and paprika. Mix well.
  3. Spoon the turkey mixture into each tortilla, rolling them up tightly. Place seam-side down in the greased baking dish.
  4. Pour the enchilada sauce evenly over the tortillas and sprinkle the remaining cheddar cheese on top.
  5. Bake uncovered for 20 minutes, or until bubbly and golden brown. Garnish with extra cilantro before serving.

3. Thanksgiving Turkey Shepherd’s Pie

This turkey shepherd’s pie is the ultimate comfort food, combining tender turkey, savory gravy, and fluffy mashed potatoes into one delicious casserole. Bonus: you can even use leftover mashed potatoes to top it all off!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded turkey
  • 2 cups leftover mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 cup turkey or chicken gravy
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Melt butter in a saucepan and sauté the onion and garlic until translucent, about 3 minutes.
  3. Stir in the frozen peas and carrots and cook for another minute.
  4. Add shredded turkey and gravy to the mixture, stirring well. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Transfer the turkey filling to a greased casserole dish and spread it evenly. Top with mashed potatoes, smoothing the surface with a spoon or spatula.
  6. Bake for 25 minutes or until the top begins to turn golden brown. Let cool slightly before serving.

4. Turkey Cranberry Quesadillas

Quesadillas are a quick and easy option, and this recipe combines sweet and savory elements to create an unexpectedly delicious dish. The cranberry sauce adds a tangy twist, while gooey melted cheese pulls it all together.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded turkey
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella or white cheddar cheese
  • Butter or cooking spray for greasing
  • Optional: diced green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat. Grease lightly with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Place a tortilla on the griddle and layer half with shredded turkey, cranberry sauce, and cheese. Fold the tortilla over to form a half-moon shape.
  3. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the cheese has melted and the tortilla is golden brown. Remove from the skillet and repeat with the remaining tortillas.
  4. Cut into wedges and garnish with diced green onions if desired. Serve warm.

5. Turkey Pot Pie

Turn your leftover turkey into a classic pot pie with a flaky, buttery crust. This recipe is a sure-fire way to breathe new life into your holiday staples.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups shredded turkey
  • 1 refrigerated pie crust (or homemade if you have time)
  • 1 1/2 cups frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 cup turkey or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 1 minute to form a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in broth and heavy cream, stirring until the mixture thickens. Add rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stir in shredded turkey and frozen vegetables until evenly coated in the creamy filling.
  5. Pour the filling into a greased pie dish and place the pie crust on top, pressing down the edges to seal. Cut small slits in the crust to allow steam to escape.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Let stand for 5 minutes before serving.

6. Turkey Fried Rice

If you’re craving a quick and flavorful dish, turkey fried rice is a fantastic way to repurpose those leftovers. It’s simple to customize based on your pantry staples.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, diced
  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Optional: sriracha for added heat

Instructions

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add ginger and sauté for 1 minute.
  2. Push the ginger to one side of the skillet and scramble the eggs on the other side. Once cooked, mix the eggs with the ginger.
  3. Add the diced turkey, frozen vegetables, and rice to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  4. Pour in soy sauce and mix thoroughly, ensuring the rice is evenly coated. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions and, if desired, drizzle with sriracha. Serve hot.

By reinvigorating your leftovers with these creative recipes, this Thanksgiving can extend beyond a single evening. Not only will you save money and time, but these dishes offer a chance to experiment with flavors and elevate your cooking game. Enjoy the simplicity and satisfaction of adapting your holiday classics into meals your family will love throughout the week. It’s the perfect way to keep the spirit of Thanksgiving alive, one delicious bite at a time.
