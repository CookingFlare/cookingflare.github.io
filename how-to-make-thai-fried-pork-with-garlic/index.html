How to Make Thai Fried Pork with Garlic


When it comes to quick, irresistible street food from Thailand, Thai Fried Pork with Garlic reigns supreme. This dish perfectly balances aromatic garlic, tender pork, and a burst of savory seasoning, all fried to crispy perfection in hot oil. If you’ve ever wondered how to bring this iconic Thai flavor into your own kitchen, you’re about to discover the step-by-step method to crafting authentic Thai fried pork with garlic that will wow your taste buds and impress friends at your dinner table.

What Makes Thai Fried Pork with Garlic So Special?

At its heart, this dish is a tantalizing combination of soy sauce, aromatic garlic, and succulent pork, fried until the outside is golden brown and the inside juicy and flavorful. The key lies not only in the sauce but also the marinating process and the frying technique.

Garlic is front and center — not just as a flavoring but as a crunchy garnish, giving each bite a garlicky punch that clamps perfectly with the smokiness of the fried pork. The use of oyster sauce, fish sauce, and a touch of sugar lends layers of umami depth and subtle sweetness that balance each other beautifully. Traditionally, it’s served with steamed jasmine rice and a side of fresh cucumber or a tangy dipping sauce to cut through the richness.

Ingredients for Thai Fried Pork with Garlic

Before diving into cooking, make sure to gather these staples:

  • Pork: Boneless pork shoulder or loin, thinly sliced or cut into bite-sized strips
  • Garlic: Fresh cloves, finely minced (or crushed), used liberally
  • Soy Sauce: Light soy sauce as the primary salty seasoning
  • Oyster Sauce: Adds a luscious, savory depth
  • Fish Sauce: For authentic Thai saltiness and complexity
  • Sugar: A pinch of palm sugar if available, or light brown sugar
  • Black Pepper: Freshly ground to taste
  • Cornstarch or Rice Flour: For coating the pork and achieving that crispy crust
  • Oil: Neutral oil with a high smoke point, such as vegetable or peanut oil, for frying
  • Optional Garnishes: Fresh Thai basil, sliced chili, and green onions

Preparation: Setting the Stage for Crispy Perfection

Step 1: Selecting and Preparing the Pork

Picking the right cut of pork is crucial. Pork shoulder or loin works best due to a good balance of fat and tenderness. Trim any excess fat and slice it thin or into 1-inch strips. The thin slices will fry evenly and develop a tantalizing crispy crust.

Step 2: Marinating

Combine light soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, black pepper, and half of your minced garlic in a mixing bowl. Toss the pork pieces into this mixture, ensuring each piece is well-coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, but ideally a few hours in the refrigerator. This step infuses flavor and tenderizes the pork.

Step 3: Coating for Crunch

Right before frying, toss the marinated pork pieces in cornstarch or rice flour. This dusting provides the necessary texture when fried. Shake off any excess to avoid clumping in the oil.

Cooking Method: The Secret to Crispy, Garlicky Goodness

Step 4: Preparing the Garlic

Garlic is the heart of this dish, so don’t skimp. Mince or crush the remaining garlic cloves finely. When fried, this garlic becomes golden and crunchy, elevating the dish exponentially.

Step 5: Frying the Pork

Heat a generous amount of oil in a wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Test the temperature by dropping a small bit of pork — if it sizzles immediately, the oil is ready.

Fry the pork strips in batches to avoid overcrowding, ensuring even cooking and crispiness. Turn occasionally until they turn a beautiful golden brown, roughly about 4-6 minutes per batch depending on thickness. Use a slotted spoon to remove them and set on paper towels to drain excess oil.

Step 6: Garlic Frying

In the same hot oil (reduce heat if necessary), add the remaining minced garlic. Fry it carefully, stirring constantly, until golden brown and fragrant but not burnt. Burnt garlic tastes bitter and will spoil the dish, so watch this closely.

Once golden, remove the garlic and drain it on paper towels too.

Step 7: Combining

Add all the fried pork back into the wok or skillet, gently tossing to meld flavors. Then sprinkle the crispy fried garlic on top, mixing it in lightly or reserving some for garnish at serving.

Finish with a quick splash of soy sauce if needed, a pinch more fresh black pepper, or a few drops of lime juice to cut through the richness.

Tips for Maximum Flavor and Texture

  • Garlic Lovers Unite: If you want that garlicky hit intensified, double the amount of garlic used or consider adding thinly sliced garlic chips.
  • Optimal Pork Thickness: Slices too thick won’t crisp well; too thin may overcook and become dry. Aim for about ¼ inch thickness.
  • Oil Temperature: Keeping your oil hot but not smoking ensures the pork crisps quickly without absorbing excess oil.
  • Marinating Time: The longer marination allows meat to soak in more flavor and improves tenderness.
  • Finishing Touches: Garnishing with freshly chopped herbs like Thai basil or cilantro adds a fresh, aromatic contrast.

Serving Suggestions

The best way to enjoy Thai fried pork with garlic is fresh and hot, served alongside steaming jasmine rice. Rounded out with a crisp, refreshing cucumber salad or pickled vegetables, it becomes a balanced meal with textural contrasts.

Other sides like fried rice, sticky rice, or a fresh green papaya salad (som tam) complement the flavors wonderfully. Don’t forget a dipping sauce like a spicy chili-lime fish sauce dip for added zing.

Variations You Can Try at Home

  • Spicy Version: Add minced Thai bird’s eye chilies into the marinade or sprinkle chili flakes on top for heat.
  • Sweet and Savory: Incorporate a touch more sugar or honey in the marinade to develop caramel notes during frying.
  • Herb Infused: Toss in Thai holy basil or mint leaves during the final toss for an herbal twist.
  • Double Garlic: Fry thin garlic slices separately until crispy and use alongside minced garlic to layer garlic flavors and textures.
  • Gluten-Free: Use tamari or coconut aminos instead of soy sauce, and substitute rice flour for cornstarch.

Wrapping Up

Making Thai fried pork with garlic at home is surprisingly straightforward but rewarding. The key to replicating that vibrant street food vibe lies in fresh ingredients, skillful marinating, perfect frying technique, and, above all, generous use of garlic. From casual weeknight dinners to festive gatherings, this dish will bring authentic Thai flair to your table and keep everyone asking for seconds.

Now that you have the complete guide, gather your ingredients, heat up your wok, and dive into crafting this aromatic, savory sensation. Soon, your kitchen will be filled with the irresistible aroma of garlicky fried pork reminiscent of bustling Thai night markets.

Enjoy your cooking adventure! If you try this at home, I’d love to hear how it turns out or any creative spins you put on it. Happy frying!
