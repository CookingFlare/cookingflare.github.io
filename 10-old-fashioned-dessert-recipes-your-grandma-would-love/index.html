10 Old-Fashioned Dessert Recipes Your Grandma Would Love


Got a sweet tooth? Indulge in these timeless treats that are sure to bring back fond memories of grandma’s cozy kitchen.

1. Apple Crisp

Apple Crisp is a cozy, old-fashioned dessert that brings the taste of autumn into your kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups thinly sliced tart apples
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the apples and 1/2 cup of the flour.
  3. In another bowl, mix the remaining flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon.
  4. Cut in the butter until crumbly.
  5. Sprinkle over apple mixture.
  6. Pour in water.
  7. Bake for 45-55 minutes, until golden brown.

2. Lemon Pudding

This simple yet delicious dessert is an ideal way to satisfy cravings for something sweet yet light.

Ingredients:

  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the egg yolks, sugar, and flour.
  2. Add water, lemon zest, and milk slowly, stirring constantly.
  3. Cook over medium heat until it coats a spoon.
  4. Remove from the heat, and stir in vanilla.

3. Meringue Cookies

Tangy, light, and crispy, these adorable little desserts are a delightful treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large egg whites
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 250°F.
  2. In a bowl, whip the egg whites until fluffy.
  3. Gradually add sugar, and continue to whip.
  4. Gently mix in vanilla.
  5. Drop in spoonfuls onto parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 1 hour.

4. Shoofly Pie

With a sweet, dark caramel flavor, this pie is a surefire crowd pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup packed dark brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup boiling water
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped walnuts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a saucepan, combine butter and brown sugar.
  3. Add the boiling water, stirring until sugar dissolves.
  4. In a bowl, mix the flour and baking soda.
  5. Stir in milk, and then the walnuts.
  6. Pour into a 9-inch pie plate.
  7. Bake for 45 minutes.

5. Banana Drop Cookies

Creamy, fluffy, and ridiculously delicious, these cookies are sure to become a go-to dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 bananas, mashed
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a bowl, mix the mashed bananas and flour.
  3. Add the butter and brown sugar.
  4. Stir in the egg.
  5. Fold in the almonds.
  6. Drop onto parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes.

6. Pecan Pie

This unapologetically rich dessert is simply divine.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 275°F.
  2. In a bowl, mix the sugars and cinnamon.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, corn syrup, and cream.
  4. Fold in the flour.
  5. Fill a 9-inch pie plate with the pecans.
  6. Pour on the egg mixture.
  7. Bake for 1 hour.

7. Chocolate Drops

A chocolate lover’s dream, these gooey, decadent treats never fail to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. In a bowl, sift together flour, cocoa, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream the butter, then alternate adding the sugars.
  4. Fold in the flour mixture.
  5. Drop onto parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes.

8. Vanilla Custard

This creamy, silky dessert is a no-brainer on its own, but also pairs well with fruits or pastries.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large egg yolks

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla.
  2. In a saucepan, warm the milk.
  3. Add the warm milk slowly into the egg mixture, stirring constantly.
  4. Pour back into the saucepan.
  5. Cook over medium heat until it coats a spoon.

9. Pineapple Upside-Down Cake

This inverted cake is a perfect blend of flavors: a hint of caramel and a dash of pineapple goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2/3 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cake and pastry flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup canned crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1/2 cup chopped maraschino cherries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a bowl, combine the sugars, flours, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream the butter, then add the eggs.
  4. Fold in the fruit.
  5. Pour into a 9-inch cake pan.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes.

10. Chess Pie

This classic southern dessert has a delightful caramel flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F.
  2. In a bowl, mix the sugar and spices.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour and butter.
  4. Stir in the vanilla.
  5. Spread into a 9-inch pie plate.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes.

From comforting classics to indulgent treats, bring back a taste of grandma’s kitchen with these unforgettable dessert recipes!
