Tasty and Allergy-Friendly Stir-Fry Recipes for Busy Weeknights


Are you tired of spending hours in the kitchen after a long day at work? Do you want to enjoy a delicious and healthy meal without the hassle? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share with you some mouthwatering stir-fry recipes that are not only tasty but also allergy-friendly. These recipes are perfect for busy weeknights when you want a quick and nutritious meal that everyone in the family can enjoy.

1. Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup snap peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add garlic, broccoli, bell pepper, carrot, and snap peas. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. Return the chicken to the skillet and pour the teriyaki sauce over the stir-fry. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken and vegetables.
  5. Remove from heat and garnish with sesame seeds. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

2. Shrimp and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, halved and sliced
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add shrimp and cook until pink and cooked through. Remove shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add onion, bell peppers, zucchini, and sugar snap peas. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Return the shrimp to the skillet and add soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the sauce coats the shrimp and vegetables.
  5. Remove from heat and serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

3. Tofu and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 package firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 head broccoli, cut into florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add tofu and cook until browned on all sides. Remove tofu from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add broccoli, bell pepper, carrot, and garlic. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, cornstarch, and water. Pour the sauce into the skillet and cook for 2 minutes until it thickens.
  4. Return the tofu to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes, stirring gently to coat the tofu and vegetables with the sauce.
  5. Remove from heat and drizzle with sesame oil. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

These allergy-friendly stir-fry recipes are not only quick and easy to make but also packed with flavor and nutrients. Feel free to customize the ingredients according to your taste and dietary needs. Whether you’re a meat lover, seafood enthusiast, or a vegetarian, there’s a stir-fry recipe for everyone. So, next time you’re in a hurry but still crave a delicious meal, give these recipes a try and savor the flavors of a homemade stir-fry without any worries about allergies or time-consuming preparations. Bon appétit!
