Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze


Are you searching for an easy-to-follow yet delectable side dish that’s guaranteed to delight both your family and guests? Look no further than roasted Brussels sprouts with balsamic glaze!

Why choose this dish? Not only is it incredibly scrumptious, but it also boasts a wide array of health benefits. Reddish-purple in color, Brussels sprouts belong to the cruciferous vegetable family, alongside broccoli and cauliflower. Packed with nutritional value, these little sprouts provide essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber that contribute to overall well-being. Furthermore, they boast anti-inflammatory properties and boast a significant amount of antioxidants, which promotes heart health and supports a healthy immune system.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs. Brussels sprouts
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1.5 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp grated garlic (about 2 cloves)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F.

  2. Rinse the Brussels sprouts thoroughly and trim the stems. Cut each sprout in half vertically, removing any damaged outer leaves.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, salt, and pepper; then gently toss in the Brussels sprouts, ensuring even coating.

  4. Spread the prepared sprouts evenly in a single layer on a large baking sheet.

  5. Roast them for around 25 minutes or until the edges are crispy and browned. Halfway through the roasting process, turn the sprouts to promote even crisping and browning.

  6. Meanwhile, prepare your balsamic glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar, honey, Dijon mustard, garlic, and water. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the glaze thickens, about 10 minutes.

  7. When the sprouts are done roasting, remove them from the oven. Dot each sprout with unsalted butter and drizzle the balsamic glaze over the top, ensuring even distribution.

  8. Return the sprouts and glaze to the oven and cook for another 5-7 minutes to allow the butter and glaze to blend and enhance the flavors.

Serve your delectable roasted Brussels sprouts garnished with extra glazed baked butter, sprinkled with freshly ground black pepper and/or chopped fresh parsley. This tantalizing dish can serve as a delightful side to poultry, fish, or pork dishes.

Roasting brings out the nutty and slightly sweet flavor of the Brussels sprouts while retaining their vibrant colors. The balsamic glaze introduces a pleasant tangy-sweet contrast, while the dijon mustard amps up the flavor profile with its pungent zest.

Quick Tips:

  • Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet. Ensure the sprouts have adequate space to crisp evenly.
  • Keep a watchful eye on your sprouts as they roast. Brussels sprouts cook quickly and can easily over-brown.
  • Feel free to experiment with other flavors! Consider adding crushed walnuts or bacon bits for an extra punch.

Introducing roasted Brussels sprouts with balsamic glaze to your plates will not only amaze your taste buds but also satisfy your guests and loved ones. Happy cooking and savoring!
