Homemade S'mores Ice Cream: A Campfire Favorite


Nothing says summer like the taste of s’mores around a cozy campfire. The combination of gooey marshmallows, melted chocolate, and crunchy graham crackers is simply irresistible. But what if you could enjoy that classic s’mores flavor in a cool and creamy dessert? Enter Homemade S’mores Ice Cream, the perfect treat to beat the summer heat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1 cup crushed graham crackers
  • 1/2 cup milk chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream and whole milk. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it reaches a gentle simmer. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and egg yolks until well combined and slightly thickened.

  3. Slowly pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to prevent curdling. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5 minutes.

  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.

  5. Once the ice cream base is chilled, it’s time to add the s’mores goodness. Remove the mixture from the refrigerator and stir in the mini marshmallows, crushed graham crackers, and milk chocolate chips. Make sure they are evenly distributed throughout the ice cream base.

  6. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes, or until the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  7. Transfer the churned ice cream to a lidded container, gently folding in any remaining marshmallows, graham crackers, and chocolate chips. Place the container in the freezer and allow the ice cream to harden for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

  8. When ready to serve, scoop the Homemade S’mores Ice Cream into bowls or cones. If desired, garnish with extra marshmallows, graham cracker crumbs, and a drizzle of melted chocolate.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra smoky flavor, try toasting the marshmallows before adding them to the ice cream base. Simply place the marshmallows on a baking sheet and broil them in the oven until golden brown.

  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make this recipe. After step 6, pour the ice cream base into a lidded container and freeze for about 2 hours. Remove from the freezer and vigorously stir the mixture with a fork or whisk to break up any ice crystals. Repeat this process every hour for about 4-5 hours, or until the ice cream reaches the desired consistency.

  • Experiment with different mix-ins to create your own unique twist on this classic dessert. Try adding chopped nuts, caramel sauce, or even a swirl of peanut butter.

  • To make the ice cream more kid-friendly, you can substitute the milk chocolate chips with semisweet or dark chocolate chips.

Now you can enjoy the nostalgic taste of s’mores in a refreshing and indulgent ice cream. Whether you’re gathered around a campfire or lounging in your backyard, Homemade S’mores Ice Cream is sure to be a hit with family and friends. So grab your ingredients, start churning, and savor every spoonful of this campfire favorite transformed into a frozen delight.
