How to Make Korean Grilled Beef Short Ribs (Galbi) at Home


When it comes to Korean barbecue, few dishes rival the bold, juicy flavors of grilled beef short ribs, or galbi. This iconic dish is a cornerstone of Korean cuisine and a favorite among food lovers worldwide. With its savory-sweet marinade and tender meat, galbi takes Korean BBQ night to the next level. The good news? You don’t have to visit a restaurant to enjoy perfectly grilled galbi. With fresh ingredients and some preparation, you can make restaurant-quality beef short ribs at home.

In this in-depth guide, we’ll walk you through how to select the right short ribs, prepare an authentic marinade using everyday ingredients, achieve tender meat, and grill up these flavorful cuts to perfection. Whether you have a charcoal grill, gas grill, or stovetop, this recipe will help you master the art of cooking galbi in your own kitchen.

Selecting the Right Beef Short Ribs

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to understand the backbone of this dish: the beef short ribs. Galbi is traditionally made using a specific cut of beef called “flanken-style short ribs,” which are thinly sliced across the bone. This cut ensures tenderness and allows the marinade to deeply penetrate the meat, delivering maximum flavor.

When purchasing beef short ribs, look for cuts with a good balance of meat and fat. Marbling is key for achieving juicy, flavorful results. Many Asian grocery stores sell pre-sliced flanken-style ribs that are perfect for this recipe. If you can’t find these at your local market, ask the butcher to slice the short ribs thinly (about ¼ inch thick) across the bones.

Ingredients for Authentic Galbi Marinade

The heart of galbi’s irresistible flavor lies in its marinade—a carefully balanced combination of savory, sweet, and umami elements. Korean cooking often incorporates jang (sauces and pastes) and fresh produce to create depth of flavor.

Here’s what you’ll need to make the marinade for approximately 2 lbs of beef short ribs:

  • Soy Sauce: ½ cup
  • Brown Sugar: 3 tablespoons
  • Honey: 2 tablespoons
  • Asian Pear or Red Apple: 1 medium (grated)
  • Garlic: 5 cloves (minced)
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon (grated)
  • Sesame Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Rice Wine or Mirin: 2 tablespoons
  • Black Pepper: ½ teaspoon
  • Green Onions: 2 stalks (thinly sliced)
  • Sesame Seeds: 1 tablespoon

The addition of Asian pear or apple is crucial, as it adds natural sweetness to the marinade while tenderizing the meat. Don’t skip this step—it’s the secret to melt-in-your-mouth galbi. If you can’t find Asian pear, a regular pear or apple works just as well.

Marinating the Short Ribs

Once you’ve gathered all your ingredients, it’s time to prepare the marinade and soak the short ribs in flavor. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Combine the Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, honey, grated pear or apple, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, rice wine or mirin, and pepper. Add the green onions and sesame seeds for extra texture and aroma.

  2. Prep the Ribs: Rinse the short ribs under cold water to remove any excess surface bone fragments. Pat them dry with paper towels to ensure the marinade clings to the meat.

  3. Marinate the Meat: Place the short ribs into a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the ribs, making sure each piece is evenly coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap. Allow the short ribs to marinate for at least 4 hours in the refrigerator, but overnight for optimal flavor.

While the meat marinates, the sugar and fruit enzymes work their magic, enhancing tenderness and infusing the beef with deep flavors.

Preparing to Grill

Now that the short ribs are infused with bold flavors, it’s time to prepare for grilling. The smoky char from grilling contributes to galbi’s signature taste. Choose your grilling method based on the tools you have available:

Charcoal Grill

For the most authentic flavor, use a charcoal grill. Start by lighting the charcoal and letting it burn until the coals are ashy white. Arrange them evenly at the bottom of the grill. Before placing your ribs on the grill, lightly oil the grates to prevent sticking.

Gas Grill

If you’re using a gas grill, preheat it to medium-high heat and oil the grates.

Stovetop or Grill Pan

No outdoor space for grilling? No problem. A heavy-duty grill pan works beautifully. Heat the pan over medium-high heat and brush it lightly with oil before adding the ribs.

Grilling the Galbi

Follow these steps to achieve perfectly grilled galbi:

  1. Add the Ribs to the Grill: Remove the short ribs from the marinade and shake off any excess liquid. Place them directly on the hot grill grates or grill pan.

  2. Cook Time: Grill the ribs for about 3–4 minutes per side, depending on their thickness. Keep an eye on them to prevent burning since the marinade contains sugars that caramelize quickly.

  3. Look for Signals: You’ll know the galbi is ready once the meat is beautifully caramelized, with slightly charred edges and grill marks on both sides.

  4. Rest Before Serving: Transfer the cooked short ribs to a plate and let them rest for a couple of minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions for Your Galbi Feast

Korean cuisine is all about community and sharing, so serve the galbi alongside complementary side dishes and accompaniments to create a complete meal. Here are some ideas to elevate your table:

  • Rice: A bowl of steamed rice is essential. It balances the rich flavors of the short ribs and provides some much-needed starch.
  • Kimchi: No Korean BBQ meal is complete without this tangy, fermented side dish. The spiced cabbage works as a perfect palate cleanser between bites of savory meat.
  • Ssam: Pair your galbi with lettuce wraps (ssam) for a fresh, crunchy contrast. Add a dollop of ssamjang (fermented soybean paste mixed with hot pepper paste) for a burst of umami.
  • Banchan: Enhance your spread with small side dishes like seasoned spinach (sigeumchi namul), pickled radish, or marinated bean sprouts (kongnamul).

Tips for Perfect Galbi

  1. Don’t Skip the Marinade Time: While you may be tempted to grill the short ribs right away, marinating them overnight ensures maximum tenderness and flavor.

  2. Monitor the Grill Heat: The high sugar content of the marinade can lead to burning if your grill is too hot. Make sure the grill is medium-high to prevent charring.

  3. Consider Adding Smoky Charcoal Flavor: If you’re using a gas grill, add a smoker box or wood chips to mimic the authentic smoky flavor of charcoal.

  4. Double Your Recipe: These grilled beef short ribs tend to disappear quickly! Marinate extra to ensure everyone gets their fill.

Conclusion

Making Korean grilled beef short ribs (galbi) at home is a rewarding culinary experience. With a mouthwatering marinade, tender beef, and the smoky char from grilling, this dish brings Korean barbecue flavors right to your table. Once you master this recipe, it’s bound to become a favorite in your household.

Whether served as part of a festive dinner or just a casual BBQ night, galbi embodies the communal joy and bold flavors of Korean cuisine. Gather your ingredients, fire up the grill, and get ready to transport your taste buds to Korea. Happy cooking!
