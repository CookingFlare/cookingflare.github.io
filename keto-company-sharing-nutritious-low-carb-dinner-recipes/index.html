1. Creamy Garlic Chicken

An absolute winner in the low-carb dinner arena, the Creamy Garlic Chicken dish is similar to being served a gourmet meal at a cozy restaurant. Cooked in a delectable garlic and heavy cream sauce, the flavor of this dish is heavenly.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper and sear them in hot olive oil in a large skillet for 5-6 minutes each side.
  2. Remove chicken from the skillet and add minced garlic, cooking until fragrant.
  3. In the same skillet, add chicken broth and deglaze the pan, scraping the bits from the bottom.
  4. Stir in heavy cream, allowing it to thicken slightly.
  5. Add the chicken back to the skillet and pour the sauce over it. Simmer until the chicken is cooked thoroughly.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

2. Cheesy Cauliflower Casserole

An excellent side for any low-carb meal, the Cheesy Cauliflower Casserole incorporates a vegetable-rich ingredient that is high in fiber and low in carbs. It’s a family-friendly dish that’s sure to become a staple in your household.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, chopped into florets
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup cream or half-and-half
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the cauliflower florets in boiling salted water for about 5 minutes or until they’re tender.
  3. Drain the florets and mash them in the skillet.
  4. Stir in shredded cheddar cheese, butter, cream or half-and-half, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased casserole dish, top with grated Parmesan cheese, and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden and bubbly.

3. Leek and Mushroom Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

Vegetarians, brace yourselves, because these stuffed Portobello mushrooms are next-level good. With earthy flavors, this low-carb dish will have your taste buds singing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Portobello mushrooms, stems and gills removed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 leek, diced
  • 4 oz white mushrooms, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3/4 cup cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. Arrange the Portobello mushrooms on a lined baking sheet, gill-side up.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and cook the leek, garlic, white mushrooms, and a pinch of salt and pepper until the vegetables are soft.
  3. Slowly mix in the cream and bring to a simmer, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  4. Spoon the leek and mushroom mixture into the Portobello mushrooms, and top with shredded cheddar cheese.
  5. Bake the stuffed mushrooms for 20-25 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.
