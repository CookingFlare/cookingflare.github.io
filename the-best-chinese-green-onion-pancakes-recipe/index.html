The Best Chinese Green Onion Pancakes Recipe


If you’ve ever wandered the streets of China or explored an authentic Chinese restaurant, you’ve likely encountered the irresistible scent of sizzling Chinese green onion pancakes. These crispy, savory, and slightly chewy delights are a popular street food across China and are a favorite for many food enthusiasts around the world. The dish is a perfect balance of texture and flavor, with the rich aroma of green onions mixing with the crunch of a perfectly fried exterior.

In this post, we’ll guide you through making the best Chinese green onion pancakes from scratch. You’ll learn everything from the basics of the dough to the secrets behind getting the ideal crispy texture, as well as tips on serving and storing these mouthwatering pancakes.

What Are Chinese Green Onion Pancakes?

Chinese green onion pancakes, also known as Cong You Bing (葱油饼), are savory flatbreads made from dough that’s rolled up with a mixture of finely chopped green onions, salt, and sometimes other seasonings. The dough is then pan-fried until golden and crispy. Unlike the American pancake, which is soft and fluffy, Chinese green onion pancakes have a more textured, chewy center with a crispy outer layer. The pancakes are often enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or side dish.

Green onion pancakes are known for their deliciously complex flavor despite having only a few simple ingredients. The green onions add a fresh, slightly sharp taste that complements the crispy texture of the pancake.

Why You Should Make Your Own Chinese Green Onion Pancakes

Making these pancakes from scratch at home offers several benefits:

  • Freshness: Homemade pancakes are always fresher than store-bought or restaurant versions. You can taste the difference in every crispy bite.
  • Customization: By making them yourself, you can adjust the amount of green onions, salt, and oil to your taste. You can even experiment with adding other seasonings, such as sesame oil or chili flakes.
  • Control Over Ingredients: You have complete control over the quality of ingredients you use. No preservatives or artificial flavors here – just wholesome ingredients.
  • Fun to Make: Preparing green onion pancakes can be an enjoyable cooking experience. You’ll get to stretch and roll the dough, layer in the flavorful green onions, and enjoy the satisfaction of turning out perfectly crispy pancakes.

Ingredients for Chinese Green Onion Pancakes

To make these delicious pancakes, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 3/4 cup hot water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the Filling:

  • 1 bunch of green onions (also called scallions)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or sesame oil for added flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • A pinch of white pepper (optional)
  • Extra vegetable oil for frying

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Chinese Green Onion Pancakes

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s dive into the steps of preparing your homemade Chinese green onion pancakes.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by making the dough for the pancakes. The key to a good green onion pancake lies in the texture of the dough – it should be soft but not too sticky.

  1. Mix the flour and salt: In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour with the salt. Stir gently to distribute the salt evenly throughout the flour.

  2. Add the hot water: Slowly pour the hot water into the flour mixture, stirring with chopsticks or a wooden spoon as you go. The water should be just hot enough to help activate the flour, but not boiling.

  3. Knead the dough: Once the water is added, you’ll notice the mixture starting to form a shaggy dough. Turn it out onto a clean work surface and knead it for about 5-10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle in a little more flour, but avoid adding too much – it should remain soft.

  4. Let the dough rest: After kneading, cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. This resting period helps the gluten relax, making it easier to roll out the dough later.

Step 2: Prepare the Green Onion Filling

While the dough is resting, you can prepare the green onion filling. This part is simple but crucial, as it adds flavor and texture to your pancakes.

  1. Chop the green onions: Take your bunch of green onions and chop them into small pieces, about 1/2 inch in length. You want enough green onions to give the pancakes plenty of flavor, so be generous with your chopping.

  2. Season the onions: Place the chopped green onions in a bowl, then add 1/2 teaspoon of salt and the white pepper (if using). Pour in the vegetable oil or sesame oil. The oil helps to bind the green onions together and adds richness to the flavor.

  3. Set aside: Stir the green onions and set the bowl aside for later use.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

After the dough has rested, it’s time to roll it out. You’ll want to stretch it as thin as possible to achieve that deliciously crispy texture.

  1. Divide the dough: Turn the dough out onto your work surface and divide it into four equal portions. This will make it easier to work with and help ensure the pancakes are uniform in size.

  2. Roll out each portion: Take one portion of the dough and roll it into a long, thin sheet. Aim for about 1/8 inch in thickness. You may need to sprinkle a little flour on your work surface and rolling pin to prevent the dough from sticking.

  3. Brush with oil and sprinkle green onions: Once you’ve rolled out the dough, use a pastry brush to lightly coat the surface with a little more vegetable oil. Then, evenly sprinkle a portion of the green onion mixture across the dough.

Step 4: Shape the Pancake

Now it’s time to shape the pancake. This step is where the magic happens!

  1. Roll it up: Starting from one end of the dough, roll it up tightly into a log or cylinder shape. Be careful not to roll it too tightly, as you want the dough to have space to expand when frying.

  2. Shape the log into a coil: Once you have your dough rolled into a log, take one end and coil it inward to form a spiral. This shape will create the layers in the pancake and give it that desirable texture.

  3. Flatten the coil: Use your palms to gently flatten the spiral-shaped dough into a disc about 6 inches in diameter. Be gentle so as not to squish out the filling.

Step 5: Fry the Pancakes

The best part is frying the pancakes to achieve that crispy, golden exterior.

  1. Heat the pan: Place a non-stick or cast-iron skillet over medium heat and add a generous amount of vegetable oil to the pan. Let it heat up until it’s shimmering but not smoking.

  2. Cook the pancake: Carefully place the rolled dough disc into the hot pan. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes on one side until it turns golden brown and crispy. You’ll know it’s time to flip when the pancake has a nice crisp texture and the edges begin to lift.

  3. Flip and cook the other side: Use a spatula to flip the pancake gently. Cook the other side for another 2-3 minutes until it’s golden and crisp as well.

  4. Repeat with the remaining dough: Remove the cooked pancake from the skillet and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Repeat the process with the remaining portions of dough.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once all your pancakes are cooked, serve them hot and enjoy them immediately while they’re crispy! Chinese green onion pancakes are often enjoyed with a simple dipping sauce, such as soy sauce, rice vinegar, and a touch of chili oil for a little heat.

Tips for the Perfect Chinese Green Onion Pancakes

  • Use hot water for the dough: Hot water helps to soften the flour, making the dough easier to roll out and ensuring a flaky texture.
  • Roll the dough as thin as possible: The thinner you roll out the dough, the crispier and flakier the pancake will be.
  • Rest the dough: Don’t skip the resting period – it’s essential for making the dough smooth and easy to work with.
  • Fry in enough oil: The oil helps to crisp up the dough and create those beautiful layers. Don’t skimp on the oil when frying.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you happen to have leftovers (though it’s unlikely!), you can store the pancakes in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2-3 days. To reheat them, simply place them in a hot skillet for 1-2 minutes on each side to restore their crispiness.

Alternatively, you can freeze the pancakes before frying them. Once they’re fully assembled and coiled, freeze them on a baking sheet. Once frozen solid, transfer them to a freezer-safe bag or container. When ready to cook, simply fry them straight from the freezer – they’ll turn out just as delicious!

Conclusion

Chinese green onion pancakes are a delightful and flavorful snack that’s perfect for any occasion. With the right technique, these crispy, flaky pancakes will impress anyone who takes a bite. Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or an experienced home cook, this recipe is simple to follow and yields satisfying results.

Now that you have the full guide, it’s time to gather your ingredients and start cooking! There’s no better way to enjoy a piece of Chinese street food culture than by making these savory pancakes in the comfort of your own kitchen. Enjoy!
