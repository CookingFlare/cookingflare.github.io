The Best BBQ Brisket Recipe You'll Ever Try


If you’re a fan of smoky, tender, and melt-in-your-mouth BBQ brisket, then you’re in for a treat. Today, I’m going to share with you my secret recipe for the best BBQ brisket you’ll ever try. This recipe has been passed down through generations in my family, and it never fails to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole brisket (10-12 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 cup beef broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat your smoker to 225°F (107°C). If you don’t have a smoker, you can use a charcoal grill with indirect heat.

  2. In a small bowl, mix together the salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, chili powder, and cayenne pepper. This will be your dry rub.

  3. Trim any excess fat from the brisket, leaving about 1/4 inch of fat on the surface. This will help keep the meat moist during the long cooking process.

  4. Generously season the brisket with the dry rub, making sure to coat all sides. Massage the rub into the meat, ensuring it adheres well.

  5. Place the seasoned brisket on the smoker, fat side up. Close the lid and let it smoke for about 6-8 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 190-205°F (88-96°C). This slow and low cooking method will ensure the brisket becomes tender and flavorful.

  6. Every hour or so, spritz the brisket with beef broth to keep it moist and add extra flavor.

  7. Once the brisket reaches the desired temperature, carefully remove it from the smoker and wrap it tightly in aluminum foil. This will allow the meat to rest and continue cooking in its own juices for about 1-2 hours.

  8. After the resting period, unwrap the brisket and let it cool slightly. Then, using a sharp knife, slice the brisket against the grain into thin slices. This will ensure maximum tenderness.

  9. Serve the BBQ brisket with your favorite BBQ sauce and enjoy it with some coleslaw, pickles, and buns. You can also serve it with cornbread or baked beans for a complete BBQ feast.

Tips and Tricks

  • The key to a great BBQ brisket is low and slow cooking. Maintain a steady temperature throughout the cooking process to achieve the best results.
  • Use a meat thermometer to accurately monitor the internal temperature of the brisket. This will help you determine when it’s done.
  • Let the brisket rest after cooking. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a juicier and more tender meat.
  • Experiment with different wood chips or chunks to add various flavors to your brisket. Hickory and mesquite are popular choices for BBQ brisket.
  • Leftover brisket can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. It also freezes well, so you can enjoy it later.

Now that you have the secret recipe for the best BBQ brisket, it’s time to fire up your smoker and impress your friends and family with your BBQ skills. Get ready for a mouthwatering, finger-licking experience that will leave everyone craving for more. Happy cooking!
