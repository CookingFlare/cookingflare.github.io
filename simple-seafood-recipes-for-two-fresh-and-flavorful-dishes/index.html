Simple Seafood Recipes for Two: Fresh and Flavorful Dishes


When it comes to preparing meals for two, seafood offers an excellent choice. Versatile, quick to cook, and brimming with natural flavor, seafood can transform a regular dinner into an elegant and memorable experience. Whether you’re planning a romantic date night or simply want a nutritious yet delicious homemade meal, these simple seafood recipes will impress with their freshness and flair.

This guide shares a handful of fresh, flavorful seafood dishes perfect for two people. From zesty shrimp pasta to herbed salmon baked to perfection, you’ll find something to suit your palate. These recipes emphasize simplicity and bold flavors while letting the natural character of seafood shine. Let’s dive in.

1. Garlic Butter Shrimp with Lemon Zest

Shrimp is a perfect option for a quick, satisfying meal. The combination of garlic, butter, and lemon enhances its natural sweetness, creating a dish that’s simple yet irresistibly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • ½ pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the butter and let it melt.
  2. Sauté the minced garlic for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Toss in the shrimp, ensuring they’re in a single layer for even cooking. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side or until they turn pink and opaque.
  5. Add the lemon zest and juice, mixing well to coat the shrimp evenly.
  6. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and serve with a side of crusty bread or over a bed of rice.

This dish tastes best when served immediately. The vibrant lemon pairs beautifully with the richness of garlic and butter, making it a delightful seafood centerpiece.

2. Pan-Seared Scallops in White Wine Sauce

Pan-seared scallops offer elegance and simplicity. This recipe uses a white wine sauce to further elevate their natural sweetness, creating a restaurant-quality dish you can make at home.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large sea scallops, cleaned and dried
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a heavy skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Sprinkle the scallops with salt and pepper, then place them in the hot skillet.
  3. Sear the scallops for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Lower the heat to medium and add butter to the skillet. Once melted, stir in the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  5. Deglaze the skillet with white wine, scraping up the brown bits for added flavor. Simmer for 2 minutes, then stir in the heavy cream if desired for a richer sauce.
  6. Return the scallops to the skillet briefly to warm them up and coat them in the sauce.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

Pair these delicate scallops with sautéed asparagus or baby potatoes for a sophisticated yet effortless meal.

3. Baked Salmon with Dijon Mustard and Herbs

Salmon is a versatile fish that works well with various flavors. The combination of Dijon mustard, fresh herbs, and a hint of lemon creates a beautifully balanced dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets, about 6 ounces each
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons chopped fresh dill
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the Dijon mustard, olive oil, lemon juice, dill, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the salmon fillets skin-side down on the baking sheet and spread the mustard-herb mixture evenly over the top.
  4. Bake for 12-15 minutes, depending on the thickness of the fillets, until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Serve the salmon with a side of roasted vegetables or a simple mixed greens salad.

This baked salmon recipe is quick, healthy, and packed with flavor, making it an ideal choice for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion.

4. Crab Cakes with a Zesty Remoulade Sauce

Juicy lumps of crab meat bound together with simple ingredients create irresistible crab cakes. The remoulade sauce adds a tangy kick, completing the dish.

Ingredients for Crab Cakes:

  • ½ pound lump crab meat
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • ½ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • ½ teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil for frying

Ingredients for Remoulade Sauce:

  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon hot sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine all the crab cake ingredients except the oil. Mix gently to avoid breaking up the crab meat.
  2. Form the mixture into 4 small patties and chill in the refrigerator for 15 minutes to firm up.
  3. Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat and fry the crab cakes for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  4. For the remoulade sauce, mix all the ingredients in a small bowl until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Serve the crab cakes warm with the sauce on the side and a fresh citrusy slaw.

5. Mediterranean Seafood Pasta

This colorful and flavorful pasta dish combines shrimp and scallops with classic Mediterranean ingredients like tomatoes, olives, and herbs. It’s hearty yet light, making it an excellent choice for any seafood enthusiast.

Ingredients:

  • ½ pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • ½ pound sea scallops
  • 8 ounces spaghetti or linguine
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¼ cup pitted Kalamata olives, halved
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • ½ cup white wine or seafood stock
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh basil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water, then drain and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp and scallops to the skillet, seasoning with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes. Cook until shrimp are pink and scallops are lightly golden, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the cherry tomatoes and olives. Sauté briefly, then deglaze the pan with white wine or seafood stock. Simmer for 3 minutes.
  5. Return the seafood to the skillet and toss in the cooked pasta, mixing everything together. Add reserved pasta water, a splash at a time, to create a light sauce.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil and optional Parmesan cheese before serving.

This Mediterranean-inspired dish is a crowd-pleaser, offering a delightful mix of textures and flavors.

Each of these recipes celebrates the simple beauty of fresh seafood. They’re straightforward enough for a weeknight dinner but refined enough to impress on a special occasion. By focusing on quality ingredients and minimal preparation, these dishes deliver maximum flavor while allowing you to spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying your meal.
