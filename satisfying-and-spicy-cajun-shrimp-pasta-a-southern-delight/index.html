Satisfying and Spicy Cajun Shrimp Pasta: A Southern Delight


Are you craving a deliciously satisfying and spicy meal that will transport your taste buds to the southern states? Look no further than our mouthwatering Cajun Shrimp Pasta recipe! Bursting with flavor and easy to make, this dish is perfect for a cozy dinner at home or when you want to impress your friends and family with a taste of the South.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of medium-sized shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 ounces of linguine or fettuccine pasta
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and season with Cajun seasoning, paprika, garlic powder, dried thyme, dried oregano, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the sliced bell peppers, onion, and minced garlic. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables become tender and slightly caramelized.

  4. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and bring it to a simmer. Allow the broth to reduce by half, which should take about 5 minutes.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer the sauce for an additional 2-3 minutes until it thickens slightly. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  6. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss it gently in the creamy Cajun sauce until well-coated. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the pasta is heated through.

  7. Finally, return the cooked shrimp to the skillet and give everything a good toss to combine. Cook for an additional minute to ensure the shrimp is warmed.

  8. Serve the Cajun Shrimp Pasta hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the Cajun seasoning mixture.
  • If you prefer a lighter version, you can substitute the heavy cream with half-and-half or whole milk.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables such as cherry tomatoes, mushrooms, or spinach to the dish for added flavor and nutritional value.
  • This pasta dish pairs well with a side of garlic bread or a fresh green salad.

Now, sit back, savor the flavors, and enjoy this satisfying and spicy Cajun Shrimp Pasta. It’s a true Southern delight that will transport you to the heart of Louisiana with every bite. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining guests, this recipe is sure to impress. Happy cooking!
