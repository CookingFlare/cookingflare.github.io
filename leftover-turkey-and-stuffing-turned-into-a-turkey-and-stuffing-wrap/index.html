Leftover Turkey and Stuffing Turned into a Turkey and Stuffing Wrap


As the holiday season approaches, one of the most beloved traditions is gathering around the table with family and friends to enjoy a hearty feast. With turkey as the centerpiece, it’s no wonder that Thanksgiving and Christmas dinners often result in an abundance of leftovers. But what do you do with all that leftover turkey and stuffing? Instead of letting it languish in the fridge, why not transform it into a delectable turkey and stuffing wrap? This creative recipe not only minimizes food waste but also revitalizes your leftovers into a mouthwatering meal that everyone will love.

The Art of Using Leftovers

Leftovers can often seem uninspiring, sitting in the fridge waiting for someone to give them a second chance. However, with a little creativity and some simple ingredients, you can breathe new life into those remnants of holiday feasts. The turkey and stuffing wrap is the perfect answer, combining the savory flavors of your Thanksgiving or Christmas dinner into a convenient and satisfying meal.

Why Wrap It Up?

Wraps are a wonderful way to enjoy your food on the go, making them an ideal lunch or light dinner option. They are versatile, allowing you to customize them with various sauces, vegetables, and cheeses, based on your preferences. The turkey and stuffing wrap is no exception, offering a delightful blend of textures and flavors that will have your taste buds singing.

Ingredients

To create the perfect turkey and stuffing wrap, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Wrap:

  • 2 large tortillas or wraps (whole wheat or spinach for added nutrition)
  • 1 cup of leftover turkey, shredded or sliced
  • 1 cup of leftover stuffing (traditional or cornbread stuffing works well)
  • 1/2 cup of cranberry sauce (optional but recommended for a tangy kick)
  • 1/2 cup of shredded cheese (cheddar or mozzarella)
  • Fresh greens (spinach, lettuce, or arugula)
  • 1 tablespoon of mayonnaise or Greek yogurt (optional, for creaminess)

For the Dressing (optional):

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prep Your Ingredients

Before you start assembling your wraps, gather all your ingredients and prepare them for easy access. Shred or slice your leftover turkey, crumble your stuffing, and set aside your greens and cheese. If you plan to use the optional dressing, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.

Step 2: Warm the Wraps

To enhance the flavor and texture, consider warming your tortillas or wraps slightly. You can do this in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds on each side, or you can microwave them for about 10-15 seconds. Warming helps the wraps become more pliable and easier to roll.

Step 3: Assemble the Wraps

Lay your warmed tortilla on a clean surface or plate. Start layering your ingredients in the following order:

  1. Spread: If desired, spread a thin layer of mayonnaise or Greek yogurt on the tortilla for added creaminess.

  2. Turkey: Place a generous amount of shredded or sliced turkey in the center of the wrap.

  3. Stuffing: Add a hearty scoop of leftover stuffing on top of the turkey. This is where the flavors really shine!

  4. Cranberry Sauce: Drizzle cranberry sauce over the stuffing for a burst of sweetness and acidity.

  5. Cheese: Sprinkle shredded cheese over the top for melty goodness.

  6. Greens: Finally, add a handful of fresh greens to provide a crisp contrast to the warm filling.

Step 4: Roll It Up

Once your fillings are layered, it’s time to roll your wrap. Start by folding in the sides of the tortilla and then carefully roll from the bottom up, tucking in the fillings as you go. Aim for a tight roll to keep everything together. If you’re having trouble, you can secure the wrap with a toothpick or wrap it tightly in foil.

Step 5: Slice and Serve

Once rolled, slice the wrap in half on a diagonal to showcase the colorful filling inside. Serve immediately, or if you’re meal prepping, wrap it in foil or parchment paper to enjoy later. If you made the optional dressing, drizzle some over the top for an extra layer of flavor.

Variations to Try

The beauty of the turkey and stuffing wrap is its versatility. Here are a few variations to consider:

Southwest Turkey Wrap

Mediterranean Turkey Wrap

Incorporate sliced cucumbers, feta cheese, and kalamata olives with a drizzle of tzatziki sauce. This variation offers a refreshing and tangy flavor profile that contrasts beautifully with the traditional stuffing.

BBQ Turkey Wrap

Mix shredded turkey with your favorite BBQ sauce and add coleslaw for crunch. This wrap is perfect for those who love a smoky flavor with a touch of sweetness.

Storing Leftovers

If you have leftover wraps, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, unwrap and place in a skillet over medium heat until warmed through, or microwave for a quick option.

Final Thoughts

The turkey and stuffing wrap is a fantastic way to repurpose holiday leftovers into a delicious and satisfying meal. Not only does it save food from going to waste, but it also brings a delightful twist to traditional flavors. So next time you find yourself with leftover turkey and stuffing, remember this easy wrap recipe. It’s a quick solution that your taste buds will thank you for!

Enjoy this simple yet flavorful dish that captures the essence of holiday feasting while providing a convenient and modern meal option. Happy cooking!
