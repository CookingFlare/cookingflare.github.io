Spring Vegetable Risotto Recipes to Try Now


When the first signs of spring begin to fill the air—fresh blooms, longer days, and a bounty of vibrant vegetables—there’s no better way to celebrate than by creating dishes that highlight the season’s freshest flavors. One of the most soothing, versatile, and flavor-packed ways to enjoy spring produce is through risotto. This classic Italian comfort dish, with its creamy texture and welcoming warmth, offers a perfect canvas to showcase the bright, crisp tastes of spring vegetables.

Whether you’re a seasoned risotto maker or a curious novice, these spring vegetable risotto recipes will help you embrace the season’s harvest while crafting a dish that is both impressive and nourishing. Let’s dive deep into the world of spring vegetable risottos, uncover tips and tricks for perfect results, and explore variations that will suit every palate.

Why Risotto with Spring Vegetables?

Risotto is often associated with autumn or winter comfort foods, but spring risottos bring a fresh twist by incorporating a medley of delicate, tender vegetables like asparagus, peas, fava beans, radishes, and baby artichokes. The creaminess of the arborio rice combined with bright, crisp, and slightly sweet vegetables creates a harmonious balance — light yet satisfying.

Spring vegetables typically have a mild bitterness or fresh earthiness that contrasts beautifully with the buttery, cheesy richness of risotto. This season of renewal is all about contrast and balance, and this comes through beautifully in your risotto bowl.

The Basics of a Perfect Risotto

Before we explore recipes, understanding the fundamental technique behind risotto will elevate your results.

1. Choose the Right Rice

Arborio, Carnaroli, or Vialone Nano are the quintessential risotto rices because of their high starch content. They give risotto its characteristic creamy consistency while allowing for a slight al dente bite.

2. Building Flavor with Stock

Using a homemade or good quality vegetable or chicken stock adds depth. Warm the stock beforehand; adding cold liquid interrupts the cooking process and can make the risotto clumpy.

3. Cook with Patience

Risotto is not rushed. Slowly adding stock one ladle at a time, gently stirring after each addition, and allowing the rice to absorb the liquid fully is what creates that luscious creaminess.

4. Finish with Fat

Butter and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese added at the end create a silky, luxurious finish.

Essential Spring Vegetables to Use in Risotto

  • Asparagus: Look for thin, tender stalks that will cook quickly and add a mild grassy sweetness.
  • Peas: Sweet and crisp, peas provide pops of freshness.
  • Fava Beans: Shelled and blanched, these have a buttery texture.
  • Baby Artichokes: Slightly nutty and tender when cooked gently.
  • Radishes: When cooked lightly or added at the end, they add crunch and a peppery kick.
  • Green Garlic & Scallions: Early season aromatics to brighten the dish.
  • Spinach or Baby Kale: Add subtle earthiness and vibrant color.

1. Classic Asparagus and Pea Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 bunch thin asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • 4 cups warm vegetable stock
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Zest of 1 lemon (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Blanch asparagus and peas in boiling salted water for 2 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté aromatics: In a large skillet or deep pan, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, cooking until translucent.
  3. Toast rice: Add Arborio rice and stir to coat with the butter and onions. Cook for 1-2 minutes until rice is slightly translucent at the edges.
  4. Deglaze with wine: Pour in white wine, stirring until almost fully absorbed.
  5. Add stock incrementally: Begin adding warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Allow the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more. Continue this process for about 18 minutes.
  6. Add vegetables: When rice is nearly al dente (just tender with a slight bite), stir in the asparagus and peas. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to warm through.
  7. Finish risotto: Remove from heat and stir in remaining butter, Parmigiano-Reggiano, and lemon zest. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with chopped parsley for a fresh pop of color.

2. Rustic Spring Fava Bean and Green Garlic Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Carnaroli rice
  • 1 cup shelled fava beans (blanched and peeled)
  • 3 stalks green garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 small shallot, finely chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable stock, warmed
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup grated pecorino Romano cheese
  • Salt and cracked black pepper
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prep fava beans: Blanch for 1 minute and peel to remove tough skins. Set aside.
  2. Sauté aromatics: Heat olive oil in pan, sauté shallots and green garlic until soft.
  3. Toast rice: Add Carnaroli rice, stirring for about 2 minutes.
  4. Deglaze: Pour in white wine and stir until mostly absorbed.
  5. Add stock slowly: Use warm stock, adding one ladle at a time until rice is creamy and tender, about 18-20 minutes.
  6. Add fava beans: Stir them in during the last 5 minutes of cooking to let their flavor infuse.
  7. Finish: Remove from heat, fold in pecorino cheese, adjust salt and pepper.
  8. Serve with fresh mint leaves scattered on top for an herbaceous punch.

3. Creamy Baby Artichoke and Spinach Risotto with Lemon

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Vialone Nano rice
  • 8 baby artichokes, trimmed and thinly sliced
  • 2 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable stock, warmed
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare artichokes: Rub trimmed artichokes with lemon juice to prevent browning and sauté in olive oil over medium heat until slightly tender, about 5 minutes.
  2. Cook aromatics: In a separate pan, heat butter, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Toast rice: Add rice and stir until coated and slightly translucent on edges.
  4. Deglaze: Pour in white wine, stirring until almost absorbed.
  5. Add stock slowly: Add warm stock ladle by ladle, stirring constantly until rice is al dente (about 18 minutes).
  6. Add greens and artichokes: Stir in spinach and sautéed artichokes during the last 5 minutes.
  7. Finish risotto: Remove from heat and stir in Parmigiano cheese, lemon zest, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve immediately, drizzled with a touch of good olive oil.

Tips for Elevating Your Spring Risotto Experience

  • Fresh Herbs: Adding fresh herbs such as mint, basil, tarragon, or parsley right before serving can elevate the freshness of your dish.
  • Acidity: A splash of fresh lemon juice or a drizzle of vinaigrette can cut through the richness and brighten the flavors.
  • Textural Contrast: Garnish with toasted nuts like pine nuts or almonds to add crunch.
  • Cheese Choices: Don’t hesitate to experiment with cheeses like aged pecorino, ricotta salata, or even a touch of fresh burrata.
  • Wine Pairing: A crisp, light white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio complements the delicate flavors of spring vegetable risottos.

Variations to Try

  • Spring Mushroom Risotto: Incorporate wild mushrooms like morels or chanterelles forearthy depth alongside peas and asparagus.
  • Lemon and Herb Risotto: Use a blend of fresh herbs with lemon zest and juice for a bright, refreshing risotto that pairs perfectly with spring vegetables.
  • Vegan Spring Risotto: Swap butter with olive oil or vegan spread and omit cheese or use nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor boost.

Wrapping Up

Spring vegetable risottos are a celebration of freshness and nurturing comfort all in one bowl. They invite creativity, from choosing the freshest seasonal vegetables to garnishing with bright herbs and cheeses. As you experiment with these recipes, you’ll discover the unique harmony that comes from marrying creamy risotto with the crisp, bright, and tender bounty of spring.

Next time you crave something fulfilling yet light, turn to a spring vegetable risotto — your taste buds, and your guests, will thank you.
