How to Make Christmas Muffins: Sweet and Savory Recipes


When Christmas is in the air, the aroma of holiday treats wafts through kitchens everywhere. And what could be better than muffins—those delightfully versatile bites of joy—to bring festive cheer to your holiday table? Whether you’re entertaining guests, gifting homemade goodies, or simply indulging in seasonal flavors, Christmas muffins are the perfect option for both sweet and savory palates. Below, we’ll guide you through creative sweet and savory muffin recipes that are easy to make and sure to wow your friends and family.

Sweet Christmas Muffin Recipes

Let’s begin with the sweeter side of Christmas. Imagine the goodness of classic holiday flavors like cinnamon, nutmeg, orange zest, and cranberries baked into warm, fluffy muffins. These options are perfect for breakfast, dessert, or an afternoon snack by the fire.

1. Cranberry Orange Muffins

Cranberries and oranges bring together tangy and sweet notes that scream holiday magic. This recipe is simple yet packed with festive flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • Zest of one orange
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil or melted butter
  • 1 cup fresh or dried cranberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with liners or grease it lightly.
  2. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Whisk together the egg, orange juice, and orange zest in a separate bowl. Gradually add the oil or melted butter.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry mixture. Stir gently until combined—avoid overmixing! Fold in the cranberries.
  5. Spoon the batter into the muffin tins, filling each about three-quarters full.
  6. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the tops are golden and a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  7. Cool before serving, although these muffins taste heavenly when slightly warm.

2. Gingerbread Muffins

What’s Christmas without gingerbread? This recipe gives you that quintessential holiday flavor in muffin form with a deliciously moist texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup molasses
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup buttermilk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare a muffin tin with liners.
  2. Sift together the flour, baking soda, salt, ginger, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Beat the softened butter, molasses, and brown sugar together until creamy. Add the egg and beat until smooth.
  4. Gradually fold in the dry ingredients, alternating with the buttermilk, until the batter is well-combined.
  5. Fill each muffin cup about ¾ full, and bake for 20–25 minutes. Allow the muffins to cool before enjoying.
  6. Optional: Dust with powdered sugar or drizzle with a simple vanilla glaze for extra holiday flair.

3. Chocolate Peppermint Muffins

If you’re a chocolate lover, this recipe delivers festive decadence with a hint of refreshing peppermint.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • ½ tsp peppermint extract
  • ½ cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin or grease it lightly.
  2. Sift the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a mixing bowl. Set aside.
  3. In another bowl, whisk the sugar, egg, milk, vegetable oil, and peppermint extract.
  4. Gradually combine the wet ingredients with the dry mix. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Spoon the batter into the muffin tins until they’re two-thirds full.
  6. Bake for 18–22 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

Savory Christmas Muffin Recipes

For those who lean toward savory treats, these muffins are a fantastic addition to your holiday spread. Think herbs, cheese, and even seasonal vegetables baked into warm, fluffy muffins.

1. Cheddar and Chive Muffins

Bursting with cheesy goodness and the subtle bite of fresh chives, these muffins double as appetizers or breakfast bites.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp paprika or cayenne (optional for a kick)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped chives
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • ¼ cup melted butter or olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a muffin tin or use liners.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and paprika or cayenne.
  3. Add the shredded cheddar cheese and chopped chives to the dry mix.
  4. In another bowl, beat the egg before adding milk and melted butter or olive oil.
  5. Combine the wet ingredients with the dry mixture, stirring gently.
  6. Fill the muffin cups about ¾ full and bake for 20–25 minutes. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

2. Savory Herb and Parmesan Muffins

Perfect for pairing with soups and salads, these muffins showcase the fragrant flavors of rosemary, thyme, and nutty Parmesan.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp finely chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ cup plain yogurt or sour cream
  • ¼ cup olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare your muffin tin with liners.
  2. Mix the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, garlic powder, rosemary, thyme, and Parmesan in a bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, yogurt, and olive oil.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients just until mixed—don’t overdo it.
  5. Spoon the batter into the muffin tins, filling each about three-quarters of the way.
  6. Bake for 18–22 minutes or until golden brown. Serve warm for maximum flavor.

3. Bacon and Spinach Muffins

Rich, savory, and packed with protein, these muffins make great snacks while blending the feeling of indulgence with festive comfort.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup cooked and crumbled bacon
  • 1 cup chopped fresh spinach (frozen works, but thaw and drain it first)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or Swiss cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk or buttermilk
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and prepare a muffin tin with liners.
  2. Mix the flour, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl. Add the crumbled bacon, chopped spinach, and cheese.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and whisk them together with milk and vegetable oil.
  4. Combine the wet and dry mixes and stir gently until just blended.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin tins, filling about ¾ full.
  6. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the tops are golden and firm. Serve at room temperature or warm.

Tips for Perfect Christmas Muffins

  • Use Seasonal Ingredients: For sweet muffins, incorporate spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger and fruits like oranges and cranberries. For savory options, lean into seasonal herbs like rosemary and thyme.
  • Don’t Overmix: Whether sweet or savory, overmixing leads to dense, tough muffins. Mix just until combined.
  • Experiment with Toppings: Crumb toppings, drizzles, or a sprinkle of grated cheese can elevate your muffins.
  • Freeze Extras: Muffins freeze beautifully and can be reheated for fresh holiday treats.

Christmas muffins are a delightful way to get creative in the kitchen while embracing the flavors of the season. From the tangy sparkle of cranberry orange muffins to the earthy richness of savory bacon and spinach muffins, there’s something here to brighten everyone’s holiday taste buds. Gather your ingredients, warm up that oven, and fill your home with the irresistible aroma of Christmas muffins. Happy baking!
