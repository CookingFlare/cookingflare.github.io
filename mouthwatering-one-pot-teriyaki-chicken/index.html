Mouthwatering One Pot Teriyaki Chicken


If you’re looking for a flavorful and easy dish to add to your cooking repertoire, then this One Pot Teriyaki Chicken recipe is just what you need. Packed with savory and sweet flavors, this dish is a perfect balance of tender chicken, fresh vegetables, and a delicious homemade teriyaki sauce. The best part? It all comes together in just one pot, making cleanup a breeze. Let’s dive into the details and get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Start by seasoning the chicken thighs with salt and pepper. In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken thighs and cook until golden brown on both sides, about 4-5 minutes per side. Once browned, remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Make the Teriyaki Sauce: In the same pot, add the garlic and ginger, and sauté for a minute until fragrant. Then, add the soy sauce, water, honey, and rice vinegar. Stir the mixture well and let it come to a simmer.

  3. Cook the Vegetables: Once the sauce is simmering, add the bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, and mushrooms to the pot. Stir to coat the vegetables in the sauce. Cover the pot and let the vegetables cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp.

  4. Simmer with Chicken: Return the chicken thighs to the pot, nestling them among the vegetables. Allow the chicken and vegetables to simmer in the teriyaki sauce for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened slightly.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Once the chicken is cooked through, remove the pot from the heat. Serve the One Pot Teriyaki Chicken over cooked white rice, spooning the vegetables and sauce over the chicken. Garnish with sesame seeds for an extra crunch and visual appeal.

Tips for Success

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the teriyaki sauce.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetable mix based on your preferences. Snow peas, snap peas, or baby corn can also be great additions.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water in a small bowl, then stir it into the sauce before adding the chicken back to the pot.

Conclusion

This One Pot Teriyaki Chicken recipe is a fantastic option for a quick and satisfying meal. The combination of tender chicken, crisp vegetables, and the rich, savory-sweet teriyaki sauce is sure to impress your taste buds. Whether you’re a busy parent, a college student, or simply someone who appreciates a delicious, fuss-free dinner, this recipe is a winner. Try it out for your next meal and savor the delightful flavors of homemade teriyaki chicken!
