Baked Easter Recipes: From Ham to Casseroles


Easter is a time of celebration, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious food. As the spring season blooms, it brings with it a variety of fresh ingredients that can elevate your holiday meals. One of the most cherished traditions during Easter is the feast, often centered around a beautifully baked ham, accompanied by an array of delightful casseroles and side dishes. In this blog post, we will explore a selection of baked Easter recipes that will not only impress your guests but also create lasting memories around the dining table.

The Star of the Show: Baked Easter Ham

Honey-Glazed Baked Ham

A succulent baked ham is often the centerpiece of Easter dinner. The sweet and savory flavors of a honey-glazed ham make it a favorite among families. Here’s how to prepare a mouthwatering honey-glazed baked ham:

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Whole cloves (for studding the ham)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Prepare the Ham: Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern and stud it with whole cloves.
  3. Make the Glaze: In a saucepan, combine honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, and ground cinnamon. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Bake the Ham: Place the ham in a roasting pan and brush it generously with the glaze. Cover loosely with aluminum foil and bake for about 1.5 to 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the remaining glaze.
  5. Finish Baking: Remove the foil during the last 30 minutes of baking to allow the glaze to caramelize. The ham is done when it reaches an internal temperature of 140°F (60°C).
  6. Serve: Let the ham rest for 15 minutes before slicing. Serve with your favorite sides.

Maple-Glazed Baked Ham

For a twist on the traditional honey glaze, try a maple-glazed ham. The rich flavor of maple syrup adds a unique sweetness that pairs beautifully with the savory ham.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Whole cloves (for studding the ham)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Prepare the Ham: Score the surface of the ham and stud it with whole cloves.
  3. Make the Glaze: In a saucepan, combine maple syrup, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, ground ginger, and ground nutmeg. Heat until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Bake the Ham: Place the ham in a roasting pan, brush with the glaze, cover with foil, and bake for 1.5 to 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes.
  5. Finish Baking: Remove the foil for the last 30 minutes to caramelize the glaze. Ensure the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).
  6. Serve: Let the ham rest before slicing and serve with your favorite sides.
