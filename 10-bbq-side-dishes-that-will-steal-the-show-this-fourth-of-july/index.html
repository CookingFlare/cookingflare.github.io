10 BBQ Side Dishes That Will Steal the Show This Fourth of July


As the Fourth of July approaches, the excitement of summer barbecues, family gatherings, and outdoor parties fills the air. While the main event, whether it’s ribs, burgers, or grilled vegetables, often takes center stage, it’s the BBQ side dishes that can elevate your gathering to the next level. A good side dish has the power to complement the smoky flavors of the grilled meats, add vibrant colors to the table, and make your feast unforgettable.

Whether you’re a seasoned griller or a newcomer to the BBQ scene, these 10 side dishes are sure to steal the show this Fourth of July. From creamy coleslaw to zesty potato salad and even fresh fruit salads, these dishes promise to bring bold flavors and make your holiday spread one for the books. Let’s dive into these mouthwatering recipes that will wow your guests and leave them asking for the recipe.

1. Classic Creamy Coleslaw

No BBQ is complete without a refreshing, crunchy side of coleslaw. This classic creamy coleslaw combines crisp cabbage, shredded carrots, and a tangy dressing to balance out the smoky and savory flavors of grilled meats. It’s light, refreshing, and has the perfect amount of creaminess that makes it an ideal side for any BBQ dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small head of green cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and grated
  • 1/2 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, grated carrot, and sliced red onion.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, and honey until smooth.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cabbage mixture and toss until the vegetables are evenly coated.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Chill the coleslaw in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving. This allows the flavors to meld together for the best taste.

2. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Grilled corn on the cob is a quintessential BBQ side that brings a sweet, smoky flavor to the table. This version takes it up a notch with a rich herb butter that melts into every kernel. The combination of the charred corn and the herbed butter creates a perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the corn for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are charred and tender.
  3. While the corn grills, prepare the herb butter by mixing the softened butter, parsley, basil, garlic, lemon zest, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Once the corn is ready, remove it from the grill and spread the herb butter over each ear of corn. Serve immediately.

3. Zesty Potato Salad

Potato salad is another BBQ staple, and this version incorporates a tangy mustard dressing with a hint of vinegar to balance the richness of the potatoes. It’s creamy, hearty, and perfect for a summer barbecue, offering both flavor and comfort in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon celery, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the halved potatoes and cook until tender, about 10 minutes.
  2. Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly before transferring them to a large mixing bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, white vinegar, dill, celery, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add the dressing to the potatoes and stir gently to combine. Fold in the chopped hard-boiled eggs.
  5. Chill the potato salad in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving for the best flavor.

4. Caprese Salad Skewers

Bright, fresh, and visually stunning, Caprese salad skewers are the perfect BBQ side dish to add color to your spread. The combination of ripe tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fresh basil, drizzled with balsamic glaze, offers a burst of flavor that pairs wonderfully with grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 package fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini or ciliegine)
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Thread cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, and fresh basil leaves onto skewers in a repeating pattern.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the balsamic vinegar over medium heat. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until it thickens into a syrupy consistency.
  3. Arrange the skewers on a platter, drizzle with olive oil and balsamic reduction, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.

5. Grilled Veggie Skewers

Grilled vegetable skewers are not only vibrant and colorful but also packed with nutrients and flavor. This simple yet delicious side dish is perfect for vegetarian guests and pairs wonderfully with any BBQ main dish. You can use a variety of vegetables based on your preferences, but bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and red onions are classic choices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into thick rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 8-10 whole mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating them to ensure even grilling.
  3. Drizzle the vegetable skewers with olive oil and sprinkle with oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Serve the skewers hot off the grill as a delicious side.

6. Grilled Pineapple with Honey and Lime

For a sweet and tropical twist, grilled pineapple with honey and lime adds a burst of fruity goodness to your BBQ spread. The caramelized pineapple, combined with the tangy lime and sweet honey, makes for a refreshing side that perfectly complements the smoky flavors of the grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pineapple, peeled, cored, and sliced into rings
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the pineapple slices for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until grill marks appear and the fruit begins to caramelize.
  3. While the pineapple grills, mix honey, lime juice, and a pinch of cayenne (if using) in a small bowl.
  4. Brush the grilled pineapple with the honey-lime mixture and serve immediately.

7. Baked Macaroni and Cheese

A creamy and cheesy baked macaroni and cheese is a crowd-pleaser at any BBQ. The crispy, golden top, combined with the smooth and velvety interior, offers comfort in every bite. Add a sprinkle of smoked paprika or garlic powder for an extra layer of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 4 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cook the macaroni according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until it forms a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in the milk and cook until the sauce thickens.
  5. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and season with salt and pepper. Once the cheese has melted, combine the sauce with the cooked macaroni.
  6. Transfer the macaroni and cheese to a baking dish and top with breadcrumbs.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.

8. Cucumber and Tomato Salad with Feta

A light and refreshing cucumber and tomato salad with feta cheese is the perfect antidote to the richness of grilled meats. The cool cucumbers, juicy tomatoes, and salty feta come together with a simple vinaigrette for a refreshing and easy-to-make side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cucumbers, sliced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cucumbers, tomatoes, red onion, and crumbled feta.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate for an hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

9. Cornbread with Honey Butter

Soft, buttery cornbread with a touch of sweetness is a perfect BBQ side dish. The crumbly texture of the cornbread pairs wonderfully with smoky grilled meats and adds a comforting touch to your Fourth of July feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup honey, for the butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, melted butter, and eggs.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into a greased baking dish and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. For the honey butter, mix softened butter and honey until smooth. Serve with the cornbread.

10. Watermelon, Feta, and Mint Salad

End your BBQ feast with a refreshing and light watermelon salad that features juicy, sweet watermelon, salty feta, and fresh mint. This salad is the perfect way to cleanse the palate after a flavorful meal, and its vibrant colors make it a visual delight.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the watermelon cubes, feta cheese, and chopped mint in a large bowl.
  2. Drizzle with lime juice and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Toss gently to combine and serve chilled.

With these 10 BBQ side dishes, you’re sure to create a memorable Fourth of July celebration that everyone will enjoy. These dishes bring bold flavors, vibrant colors, and the perfect balance to any BBQ feast. From the creamy coleslaw to the tangy potato salad, and the refreshing watermelon salad, your guests will be raving about your spread long after the fireworks are over. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to host a BBQ that will steal the show!
