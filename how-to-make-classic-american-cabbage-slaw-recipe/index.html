How to Make Classic American Cabbage Slaw: Recipe


Cabbage slaw, a quintessential side dish in American cuisine, brings a delightful crunch and a burst of flavor to any meal. Whether served alongside a barbecue, as a topping for sandwiches, or enjoyed on its own, this classic slaw is both easy to prepare and highly versatile. In this guide, we will delve into the steps to create the perfect American cabbage slaw, exploring its ingredients, preparation methods, and serving suggestions.

Ingredients

To create a delicious classic American cabbage slaw, gather the following ingredients:

For the Slaw:

  • 1 medium head of green cabbage (approximately 2 to 3 pounds)
  • 1 medium head of purple cabbage (optional for color contrast)
  • 2 large carrots
  • 1 small red onion (or 1/2 large onion)
  • 1 bell pepper (any color, optional)
  • 1 cup of chopped fresh parsley (optional for garnish)

For the Dressing:

  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar (or white vinegar)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard (optional for added flavor)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Vegetables

1.1. Cabbage:
Start by removing the outer leaves of the cabbage. Cut the head into quarters and remove the core. Slice the cabbage halves into thin shreds. You can use a sharp knife, a mandoline slicer, or a food processor fitted with a shredding disk for this task. Using both green and purple cabbage not only enhances the visual appeal of the slaw but also adds a range of nutrients.

1.2. Carrots:
Peel the carrots and use a grater or a food processor to shred them finely. Carrots add a natural sweetness and vibrant color to the slaw.

1.3. Onion and Bell Pepper:
Finely slice the red onion and bell pepper, if using. The onion adds a zesty kick, while the bell pepper contributes a sweet crunch.

Step 2: Mixing the Vegetables

In a large mixing bowl, combine the shredded green and purple cabbage, shredded carrots, sliced onion, and bell pepper. Toss the vegetables together gently to ensure an even distribution of colors and textures.

Step 3: Making the Dressing

3.1. Combine Ingredients:
In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, sugar, and Dijon mustard (if using). The mayonnaise serves as the creamy base, while the vinegar adds tanginess. Adjust the sugar according to your desired sweetness; remember that cabbage can have a naturally slightly bitter flavor, which sugar counterbalances.

3.2. Seasoning:
Add salt and black pepper to taste. Start with a pinch of each and adjust to your liking after mixing. The seasoning will enhance the overall flavor of the slaw.

Step 4: Bringing It All Together

Pour the dressing over the mixed vegetables. Use a pair of tongs or a large spoon to gently toss the slaw until all ingredients are well coated with the dressing. Ensure that each shred of cabbage and carrot gets a taste of the creamy goodness!

Step 5: Chilling the Slaw

For the best flavor, cover the slaw with plastic wrap or transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving. This chilling time allows the flavors to meld and the cabbage to soften slightly, creating a more enjoyable texture.

Step 6: Serving Your Slaw

Once chilled, give the slaw a good stir before serving. You can garnish it with fresh chopped parsley for added freshness and color. This classic American cabbage slaw is perfect alongside grilled meats, as a topping for pulled pork sandwiches, or even as a light and refreshing snack.

Tips for the Perfect Cabbage Slaw

  • Freshness is Key: Use fresh, crisp cabbage and vegetables for the best texture and flavor. Avoid wilted or soft vegetables.

  • Customize Your Slaw: Feel free to add other ingredients such as diced apples, raisins, or nuts for added texture and flavor. Each variation brings a unique twist to the classic recipe.

  • Make It Ahead: Cabbage slaw can be made a day ahead of time, allowing the flavors to develop even further. Just be sure to keep it refrigerated until serving.

  • Adjust the Creaminess: If you prefer a lighter slaw, reduce the amount of mayonnaise or substitute with Greek yogurt for a tangy twist.
