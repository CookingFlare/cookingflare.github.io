Homemade Greek Honey Bread (Tsoureki) Recipe


Greek Honey Bread, or Tsoureki, embodies the heart and soul of Greek baking. This slightly sweet, aromatic loaf is traditionally reserved for festive occasions, often gracing the tables of Easter celebrations or family gatherings. However, its unique blend of flavors and irresistible texture make it a treat worth enjoying all year round. Whether you are looking to indulge in a slice with morning coffee or serve it as a centerpiece for a dinner party, this Greek bread recipe offers the perfect balance of sweetness and warm spices. Today, we’ll walk you through the process of crafting this incredible dish right in your kitchen—a simplified yet authentic take on a timeless classic.

Origins and Importance of Tsoureki

Tsoureki traces back to centuries of culinary tradition, steeped in both cultural and religious significance. Historically, this braided bread held symbolic meaning, often representing themes of renewal and resurrection. A distinctive element of Tsoureki is its flavor profile, crafted with spices such as mahleb and mastic, along with hints of citrus and honey. These ingredients not only add depth but also carry a unique charm, making Greek Honey Bread truly unforgettable.

While mahleb is a powdered spice derived from cherry seeds and mastic is a resin from the mastic tree, these ingredients are now widely available in specialty stores or online, ensuring that you can give the authentic recipe a try, no matter where you’re located.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of Greek Honey Bread lies in its simplicity, requiring a handful of accessible ingredients. Here’s a breakdown of everything you’ll need for this recipe:

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups (500g) all-purpose flour, sifted
  • ½ cup (120ml) lukewarm milk
  • ½ cup (120ml) lukewarm water
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup (60g) unsalted butter, softened
  • ⅓ cup (75g) sugar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 large egg + 1 egg yolk
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground mahleb
  • ¼ teaspoon ground mastic
  • Zest of 1 orange

For the Honey Syrup:

  • ⅓ cup (80ml) honey
  • ⅓ cup (80ml) water
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon orange blossom water (optional, for added aroma)

For the Egg Wash:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon milk

Optional Toppings:

  • Slivered almonds
  • Sesame seeds

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making homemade Greek Honey Bread requires a bit of time and patience, but the reward is well worth the effort. Follow these steps carefully to achieve the perfect loaf:

1. Activate the Yeast

Begin by activating your yeast, ensuring the dough will rise properly. In a small bowl, combine the lukewarm water, a tablespoon of sugar, and the active dry yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This step is crucial, as it guarantees the yeast is fresh and alive.

2. Prepare the Wet Ingredients

In a medium saucepan, gently warm the milk until slightly warm to the touch but not too hot. Add the butter, letting it melt into the milk completely before removing from heat.

In a mixing bowl, whisk the egg, egg yolk, and honey together. Slowly add the warm milk mixture into the bowl, followed by the yeast mixture. Whisk until everything is well combined.

3. Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a large bowl, sift together the flour, mahleb, mastic, and salt. Add the sugar and orange zest, tossing lightly to distribute the flavors evenly. Create a small well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour the wet mixture into the middle.

4. Knead the Dough

Using clean hands or a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook, begin kneading the mixture into a smooth, elastic dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky—add extra flour gradually if needed. Knead consistently for about 10–12 minutes to achieve the right texture.

5. Proof the Dough

Once your dough is ready, transfer it to a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel. Let it rest in a warm area for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until it doubles in size. While waiting, you can prepare the honey syrup (see below).

6. Prepare the Syrup

To make the honey syrup, combine the honey, water, and cinnamon stick in a small saucepan. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Allow it to simmer for 5 minutes before removing the cinnamon stick and adding orange blossom water (if using). Let the syrup cool completely before use.

7. Shape the Dough

Once proofed, punch the dough down to release air and divide it into three equal portions. Roll each portion into long ropes, about 16–18 inches in length. Braid the ropes together, ensuring a tight weave, and tuck the ends neatly underneath. Transfer the braid to a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Cover the bread loosely with a towel and let it rise again for 30–45 minutes.

8. Egg Wash and Topping

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Brush the bread gently with the egg wash, ensuring full coverage without deflation. Sprinkle toppings such as slivered almonds or sesame seeds for added texture and flavor.

9. Bake and Glaze

Bake the bread in the preheated oven for 30–35 minutes, or until golden brown. When done, remove from the oven and brush the warm honey syrup generously over the bread. Allow it to cool slightly before serving.

Tips for Success

Choose Quality Ingredients

Mahleb and mastic are small players that wield big flavor, so sourcing good-quality spices is key to achieving the authentic taste of Tsoureki.

Perfect Kneading

The elasticity of the dough is crucial for the airy, fluffy texture of this bread. Ensure you knead consistently to develop the gluten fully.

Test Your Yeast

If your yeast doesn’t froth when activated, it’s likely expired. Always check the date before starting the recipe.

How to Serve and Store

Tsoureki is best served fresh—slice it up and enjoy it with a smear of butter or a drizzle of extra honey. Alternatively, pair it with a cup of Greek coffee for a truly authentic experience. If you have leftovers, store the bread in an airtight container or wrap it tightly in plastic to maintain freshness. You can refrigerate it or freeze slices for later enjoyment.

A Slice of Tradition at Home

With this homemade Greek Honey Bread recipe, you’re not just crafting a meal; you’re participating in a time-honored tradition that connects culinary art with cultural heritage. The subtle sweetness, accented by warm spices and citrus, creates a loaf that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether braided into a classic shape or adorned with modern variations, Tsoureki never fails to impress.

Baking this bread is as much about the process as it is about the final product. From preparing the dough to weaving the beautiful braid, it offers moments of mindfulness and creativity. Bake a loaf today, and savor both the flavors and the experience.
