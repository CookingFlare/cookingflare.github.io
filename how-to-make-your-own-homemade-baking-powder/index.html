How to Make Your Own Homemade Baking Powder


Baking powder is a staple ingredient in many recipes, from fluffy pancakes to soft cakes and cookies. It’s used to make baked goods rise, creating a light, airy texture. However, you might find yourself in a situation where you’re out of baking powder and don’t want to make a trip to the store. Or perhaps you’re aiming for a more natural, homemade version without any artificial additives. Luckily, making your own baking powder at home is simple, affordable, and effective.

In this post, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of making your own homemade baking powder, why you might want to consider doing so, and some handy tips to ensure your baked goods turn out perfectly every time.

What is Baking Powder?

Before we dive into how to make your own, let’s take a moment to understand what baking powder is and how it works.

Baking powder is a leavening agent, which means it helps dough or batter rise by releasing gas when it’s mixed with liquid and heated. This creates tiny bubbles in the mixture, making the final baked product light and fluffy.

Baking powder usually contains three key ingredients:

  1. Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate) – This is the alkaline ingredient that reacts with an acid.
  2. Acidic component – Usually cream of tartar or another acid that helps activate the baking soda.
  3. Starch (like cornstarch or arrowroot) – This prevents the two active ingredients from reacting prematurely during storage.

There are two types of baking powder available in the market: single-acting and double-acting. Single-acting baking powder releases gas as soon as it’s mixed with liquid, while double-acting baking powder releases gas in two stages, once when mixed with liquid and again when exposed to heat. Most store-bought baking powders are double-acting.

Now, let’s explore how to make your own version using ingredients you may already have in your pantry.

Ingredients for Homemade Baking Powder

To make homemade baking powder, you only need three basic ingredients:

  1. Baking soda – This is the base ingredient in baking powder. It needs an acid to activate it, which is where the next ingredient comes in.
  2. Cream of tartar – This is an acidic powder that combines with baking soda to create the chemical reaction needed to make your baked goods rise.
  3. Cornstarch – The cornstarch helps to keep the baking soda and cream of tartar from reacting with each other prematurely and allows for longer shelf life.

Homemade Baking Powder Recipe

To make a simple, reliable homemade baking powder, follow this recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 part baking soda
  • 2 parts cream of tartar
  • 1 part cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the baking soda and cream of tartar. This combination forms the leavening agents.
  2. Add the cornstarch: The cornstarch helps keep the mixture stable by preventing the baking soda and cream of tartar from reacting with each other too soon. Add it to the dry ingredients and mix well.
  3. Store the homemade baking powder: Transfer the mixture to an airtight container. Store it in a cool, dry place, away from heat and moisture.

How Much Homemade Baking Powder to Use?

When substituting your homemade baking powder for store-bought, you can use the same amount. The general rule of thumb is:

  • For each teaspoon of baking powder required in your recipe, use 1/3 teaspoon of baking soda, 2/3 teaspoon of cream of tartar, and a pinch of cornstarch (to prevent premature reactions).

For example, if a recipe calls for 1 teaspoon of baking powder, you would use 1/3 teaspoon of baking soda, 2/3 teaspoon of cream of tartar, and a small amount of cornstarch to keep it stable.

Double-Acting Baking Powder Variation

If you prefer the benefits of double-acting baking powder, you can make a variation that mimics the effect of double-acting baking powder. Double-acting baking powder releases gas in two stages: first, when mixed with liquid, and second, when exposed to heat.

To make a version that behaves like double-acting baking powder, use the following proportions:

  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch

Store this mixture just as you would the standard version, and use it in the same amount as you would double-acting baking powder in your recipes.

Why Make Your Own Baking Powder?

There are several reasons you might choose to make your own baking powder instead of buying it at the store. Here are a few benefits:

1. Control Over Ingredients

Many commercially available baking powders contain additives and preservatives, such as sodium aluminum sulfate or monocalcium phosphate. While these ingredients are generally recognized as safe by food safety authorities, some people prefer to avoid them for health or environmental reasons.

By making your own baking powder, you have complete control over what goes into it, allowing you to create a cleaner, more natural product.

2. Avoiding Additives

Commercial baking powders often contain cornstarch or other starches to prevent clumping. However, some brands may use modified starches or other ingredients that aren’t ideal for those with dietary sensitivities. By making your own baking powder, you can choose the exact starch or other fillers you prefer (or skip them entirely).

3. Customization

Making your own baking powder means you can adjust the formula to suit your preferences. For example, if you want a baking powder that’s more acidic or milder in its reaction, you can tweak the ratio of ingredients. This is helpful if you’re working with specific dietary needs, like low-sodium or gluten-free baking.

4. Cost-Effective

Commercial baking powder can add up over time, especially if you’re a regular baker. Making your own is very affordable, and the ingredients needed are typically pantry staples. Plus, the shelf life of homemade baking powder is relatively long, making it an economical choice for most bakers.

5. Freshness

When you make your own baking powder, you ensure it’s fresh and potent. Store-bought baking powder loses its effectiveness over time, and older baking powder may not produce the same rise in your baked goods. By mixing your own in small batches, you can ensure you’re always using the freshest leavening agent.

Troubleshooting Homemade Baking Powder

While making your own baking powder is simple, there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure it works perfectly in your recipes:

1. Ensure Proper Ratios

The key to effective homemade baking powder is getting the proportions right. Too much cream of tartar can make the dough overly acidic, while too much baking soda may leave an unpleasant taste in your finished product. Stick to the ratios outlined in the recipes above to get the best results.

2. Keep It Dry

Moisture is the enemy of baking powder. If your mixture gets wet, the chemical reaction will occur prematurely, and your baking powder will lose its potency. Make sure to store your homemade baking powder in an airtight container and keep it in a cool, dry place.

3. Test Your Homemade Baking Powder

Before using homemade baking powder in a recipe, it’s a good idea to test it. Simply add a small amount of baking powder to warm water. If it bubbles and fizzes vigorously, it’s still good to use. If the reaction is weak or nonexistent, it’s time to make a fresh batch.

4. Shelf Life

Homemade baking powder has a shelf life of about 3-6 months, depending on how it’s stored. As mentioned, moisture and humidity can cause it to lose potency over time. Store it in an airtight container in a cool, dry place, and be sure to test it before using it in your baking projects.

Conclusion

Making your own homemade baking powder is a simple, effective, and cost-efficient way to ensure your baked goods rise perfectly every time. Whether you’re trying to avoid additives, looking for a more natural alternative, or just running low on store-bought baking powder, this homemade version is easy to prepare and works just as well in your favorite recipes.

Now that you know how to make your own, you can experiment with different variations and ingredients to create a custom baking powder suited to your preferences. From cakes and cookies to pancakes and muffins, you’ll be ready to bake with confidence and control over the ingredients you use.

So, next time you find yourself in need of baking powder, skip the store and make your own—it’s as easy as 1-2-3! Happy baking!
