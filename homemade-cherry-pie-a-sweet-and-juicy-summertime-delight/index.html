Homemade Cherry Pie: A Sweet and Juicy Summertime Delight


Are you looking for the perfect dessert to showcase the vibrant flavors of summer? Look no further! Homemade Cherry Pie is the answer to your cravings. Bursting with sweet and juicy cherries, encased in a flaky crust, this delightful treat is sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating a mouthwatering cherry pie that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of fresh cherries, pitted
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon of almond extract
  • 2 pie crusts (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon of butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • A sprinkle of sugar (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large bowl, combine the pitted cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and almond extract. Stir gently until the cherries are evenly coated. Let the mixture sit for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. Roll out one of the pie crusts and gently transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish. Press the crust firmly against the bottom and sides of the dish, trimming any excess dough.

  4. Pour the cherry filling into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly. Dot the filling with small pieces of butter to add richness to the pie.

  5. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the cherry filling. Trim off any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  6. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg, and sprinkle a little sugar on top for a golden and slightly crunchy finish.

  7. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any juices that may overflow) and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. If the crust starts to brown too quickly, cover the edges with aluminum foil.

  8. Once the pie is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours. This will allow the filling to set and prevent it from being too runny when sliced.

  9. Serve your homemade cherry pie with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra touch of indulgence. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • If fresh cherries are not in season, you can use frozen cherries instead. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before using.

  • For an added twist, you can incorporate other fruits such as blueberries, raspberries, or even peaches into the filling. Experiment with different combinations to create your own unique pie.

  • If you prefer a lattice crust, simply cut the second pie crust into thin strips and weave them over the cherry filling.

  • To make the pie ahead of time, you can prepare the filling and crust separately and store them in the refrigerator. Assemble the pie just before baking to ensure a crisp crust.

  • Leftover pie can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat individual slices in the microwave for a few seconds or enjoy them cold.

Now that you have the recipe for Homemade Cherry Pie, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! Whether you’re enjoying it at a picnic, a barbecue, or a cozy summer gathering, this sweet and juicy summertime delight is guaranteed to be a hit. So, grab your ingredients, preheat that oven, and let the aroma of freshly baked cherry pie fill your kitchen. Happy baking!
