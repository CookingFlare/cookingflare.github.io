Flatout Adventure: Exploring Ancient Flatbread Recipes from Around the World


When embarking on a culinary journey, nothing quite compares to the flavors of ancient flatbread recipes which spice up the taste buds and stir the senses. These timeless food treasures hail from far-reaching corners of the world, each with its own magical heritage that plays with different grains, techniques, and spices for a mouthwatering result. Ready to embark on the Flatout Adventure?

1. The Crusty Charm of Italian Focaccia

Planting its roots firmly from the Liguria region of Italy, Focaccia is a delicious flatbread that will charm your palate with its crispy crust, fluffy interior and flavorful toppings. To recreate these ancient Italian flavors, here’s a simple recipe that retains its essence.

Focaccia Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 ¼ cups lukewarm water
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • For toppings: rosemary, cherry tomatoes, olives, and sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix the flour, instant yeast, sugar, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Gradually add lukewarm water, and 2 tablespoons of olive oil to the dry mixture and knead to form a smooth, elastic dough.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rise in a warm place for approximately 1 hour or until it doubles in size.
  4. Punch down the dough, transfer it to a greased or floured baking sheet, and flatten it with your hands. Create dimples on the dough using your fingertips.
  5. Drizzle over the remaining olive oil and let the dough rise again for about 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  7. Top the dough with rosemary, cherry tomatoes, olives, and sea salt according to your personal preference. Allow the flavors to meld for about 10 minutes.
  8. Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until it turns a beautiful golden-brown.

2. From the Deserts of Levant: Lavash

Lavash is an ancient Armenian flatbread, thinner than paper and widely enjoyed across the Levant, a region that includes modern day Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Lebanon, Palestine, Syria, and Turkey. This unleavened bread is prepared using basic ingredients that highlight the flavors of its ancient heritage.

Lavash Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add warm water and oil to the dry mixture. Knead until smooth and slightly sticky.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  4. Roll out the dough very thinly using a rolling pin on a lightly floured surface.
  5. Set your oven to high heat (around 500°F / 260°C). Bake each lavash for a short period, usually a few minutes, until it puffs up and acquires a slight golden color.

3. The Heart-warming Afghan Naan

Naan is a ubiquitous leavened flatbread found in many countries of the Middle East, Central Asia, andNorthwest China. However, when it comes to heart-warming comfort, few compare to the Afghan Naan, made with a touch of ghee or oil which lends a smoky flavor.

Afghan Naan Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 3 tbsp ghee (or oil)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix dry ingredients, including flour, salt, sugar, and yeast.
  2. Add warm water, milk, and ghee to the dry mixture and knead until the dough becomes soft and smooth.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rise until doubled, usually for about an hour.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls, roll them out, and roll each ball in ghee before shaping.
  5. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and place the naans on a greased baking sheet.
  6. Bake the naans for 10-15 minutes until they turn golden-brown.

The Flatout Adventure has just begun! Be inspired by the flavors from various regions and don’t hesitate to add your personal touch to these ancient recipes. Your taste buds are in for a thrilling ride as you explore the centuries-old secrets of ancient flatbreads; Ancient recipies open up a world of possibilities. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves and let the Flatout Adventure begin!
