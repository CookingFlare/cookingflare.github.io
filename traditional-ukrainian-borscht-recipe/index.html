Traditional Ukrainian Borscht Recipe


Borscht is much more than just a soup in Ukraine – it’s a cultural treasure, a symbol of warmth, family gatherings, and tradition. This hearty, rich, and flavorful dish has roots that stretch back centuries, with each region, family, and cook adding their own unique spin. The traditional Ukrainian borscht is a deep, vibrant red soup made with beets, cabbage, potatoes, and a mix of other vegetables, often served with a dollop of sour cream and accompanied by rye bread. It’s perfect for cold winter days but loved year-round for its robust flavor and satisfying qualities.

In this post, we will guide you step by step through the process of making authentic Ukrainian borscht. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow and will yield a borscht that’s as close as you can get to the traditional dish enjoyed in Ukrainian homes.

Ingredients for Traditional Ukrainian Borscht

The beauty of borscht is in its simplicity. While there are countless variations, this traditional Ukrainian recipe incorporates a mix of fresh vegetables, savory meats, and herbs that are typical of the dish’s history and regional preferences.

Essential Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized beets (peeled and grated)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and grated
  • 1 large potato, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 small head of cabbage, shredded
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar (apple cider vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 2 tablespoons sunflower oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 6 cups beef or chicken broth (preferably homemade for the best flavor)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons sour cream (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika (optional for a smoky flavor)
  • Rye bread (for serving, ideally with a garlic rub)

Optional Meat Ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef short ribs (or any other cut of beef with bones for a deeper broth flavor) or
  • 1 chicken breast or thighs, if you prefer a lighter version of the soup.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Ukrainian Borscht

Step 1: Prepare the Meat (If Using)

If you are using beef or chicken in your borscht, start by preparing the broth. Place your beef short ribs or chicken in a large pot, add about 6 cups of cold water, and bring to a boil. Skim off any foam or impurities that float to the surface. Reduce the heat to a simmer, add a pinch of salt, and cook for 1-2 hours to develop a rich and flavorful broth. Once done, remove the meat and strain the broth. If using beef, shred the meat into small pieces once cooled, and set aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large pot, heat the sunflower oil over medium heat. Start by sautéing the chopped onions until they are translucent and slightly golden, which will bring out their sweetness. Add the grated carrots and continue to sauté for about 5 minutes until they soften. Then, add the grated beets and cook for an additional 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. This step is crucial because it helps to develop the deep, earthy flavor that is characteristic of borscht.

Step 3: Add Tomato Paste and Vinegar

Next, add the tomato paste to the sautéed vegetables. This ingredient gives the borscht its signature tangy richness. Stir it in thoroughly, letting the flavors combine for about 2 minutes. Then, add the vinegar and sugar, which balance the sweetness of the beets and the acidity from the tomatoes.

Step 4: Cook the Broth

Now, pour the prepared beef or chicken broth into the pot with the vegetables. Bring everything to a gentle boil. Add the diced potatoes, shredded cabbage, bay leaves, peppercorns, and salt to taste. Let the soup simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender. Stir occasionally, and check the seasoning. You may want to adjust the salt and pepper at this stage to suit your taste.

Step 5: Add Garlic, Dill, and Smoked Paprika

Once the vegetables are cooked through, add the minced garlic, fresh dill, and smoked paprika (if using) to the soup. These ingredients will infuse the borscht with a fragrant aroma and elevate the depth of flavors. Let the soup simmer for an additional 10 minutes to allow the herbs and spices to infuse into the broth.

Step 6: Final Adjustments

Taste the borscht and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, vinegar, or sugar if needed. The balance between sweetness and tanginess is what makes borscht truly special, so take the time to get it just right.

Step 7: Serve the Borscht

Ladle the hot borscht into bowls and top each serving with a generous dollop of sour cream. Garnish with extra fresh dill and parsley for an added burst of flavor. Borscht is traditionally served with crusty rye bread, which can be lightly toasted and rubbed with a garlic clove for extra flavor.

Tips for the Best Ukrainian Borscht

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: The key to a great borscht lies in the quality of its ingredients, especially the beets. Fresh, organic beets will bring out the vibrant color and rich flavor of the soup.
  • Homemade Broth: Although store-bought broth can work in a pinch, homemade broth made from simmered meat or bones will add a depth of flavor that you can’t achieve with anything else.
  • Let It Sit: Like many soups, borscht tastes even better the next day. If you can, let the soup rest for several hours or overnight. This allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.
  • Play with Toppings: Though sour cream is a classic topping, you can experiment with other garnishes like chopped scallions, hard-boiled eggs, or pickled vegetables, all of which pair beautifully with the soup.
  • Adjust the Tanginess: Some people like their borscht to have more tang, while others prefer it a bit sweeter. Adjust the vinegar and sugar to suit your taste.
  • Smoked Meat Option: For a deeper, more complex flavor, try using smoked meats or smoked paprika in your borscht. This adds an extra layer of smokiness that pairs wonderfully with the sweetness of the beets.

The Tradition Behind Ukrainian Borscht

Ukrainian borscht is not just food; it’s a way of life. The dish is steeped in history and tradition, having been passed down from generation to generation. In Ukraine, borscht is often considered a symbol of home and family. It’s a dish that brings people together, whether it’s shared over a festive holiday meal or a simple weeknight dinner.

Every family has its own version of borscht, with variations that might include different meats, a mix of additional vegetables, or special herbs. Some prefer a richer, meat-heavy broth, while others keep it lighter with just the vegetables. The beauty of borscht lies in its adaptability – it can be made to suit any preference while still retaining its essential identity.

In Ukraine, borscht is often eaten with garlic bread or pampushki (soft Ukrainian garlic rolls). It is common to serve it alongside a variety of pickles, which balance the richness of the soup with their sharp acidity. The dish is perfect for feeding a crowd, and leftovers always taste better the next day.

Nutritional Value of Ukrainian Borscht

Borscht is not only comforting but also packed with nutrients. The beets are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, and they give the soup its signature color. Cabbage adds fiber and vitamin C, while the potatoes provide starch to make the soup filling. If you use a meat-based broth, the soup also contains protein, making it a well-rounded, wholesome meal.

Nutritional Breakdown (per serving, without sour cream):

  • Calories: ~200-250 (depending on meat and portion size)
  • Protein: 10g (with meat)
  • Fat: 7g
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fiber: 7g
  • Vitamin C: 40% of your daily intake

The inclusion of sour cream adds a touch of richness and creaminess that makes the borscht even more satisfying. For those watching their calorie intake, you can opt for a lighter version using low-fat sour cream or skipping it altogether.

Conclusion

Ukrainian borscht is a beloved dish that has stood the test of time. Its deep, earthy flavor and vibrant color make it a crowd-pleaser at any meal. With its rich history, complex flavors, and nourishing ingredients, borscht is a true representation of Ukrainian cuisine at its finest. Whether you’re enjoying it on a cold winter’s day or at a family gathering, borscht will undoubtedly bring comfort and warmth to your table.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start cooking! A bowl of this traditional Ukrainian borscht is sure to become a favorite in your household, carrying with it the warmth of Ukrainian hospitality and culture.
