Turtle Doves: Heartwarming Seafood Recipes Perfect for Valentine's Day


As Valentine’s Day approaches, there’s no better way to show your loved one how much you care than by preparing a heartwarming meal together. And what better way to celebrate this romantic holiday than with a protein that comes straight from the ocean: turtle doves, also known as scallops! This delicate and succulent seafood makes for a perfect, luxurious Valentine’s Day dinner. Read on for our romantic recipe ideas featuring the star ingredient - turtle doves.

1. Scallops with Lemon-Roquefort Risotto

A dish that balances sweet and salty flavors, the Lemon-Roquefort Risotto would make for an incredible culinary experience with your beloved. Roquefort cheese adds an exquisite tangy taste to the creamy, buttery risotto that forms the base of this dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 4 cups warm chicken or seafood stock
  • 8 oz Roquefort cheese, crumbled
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, chopped
  • Olive oil and butter for cooking
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and white pepper to taste
  • 24 turtle doves (scallops)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the chicken or seafood stock to a simmer.
  2. In a separate large saucepan, melt the butter and add the olive oil. Cook the shallots and garlic until aromatic, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the rice, toast it for a couple of minutes, and then pour in the wine. Stir until the liquid is absorbed.
  4. Add the hot stock, one ladleful at a time, stirring continuously and waiting for each ladleful to be absorbed before adding the next. This will take approximately 20 minutes.
  5. Once the rice is al dente, remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the Roquefort and lemon zest. Add pepper and salt to taste.
  6. In a separate frying pan, cook the scallops in melted butter until golden brown.
  7. Serve the scallops over the Risotto, garnishing with fresh parsley and a drizzle of lemon juice.

2. Baked Scallops with Spinach and Béchamel Sauce

This beloved French recipe combines delicious seafood with a healthy dose of leafy greens, making it the perfect Valentine’s Day entrée for those wanting a lighter option. Give this classic a twist by using turtle doves, and indulge in the flavors of the Mediterranean during your candlelit dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 24 turtle doves (scallops)
  • 1 lb fresh spinach, washed and drained
  • 4 cups béchamel sauce
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil or cooking spray for greasing
  • 1 small white onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and grease a baking dish with olive oil or cooking spray.
  2. Cook the spinach in a large pan over medium heat until tender. Add chopped onions and cook until transparent.
  3. In a separate saucepan, heat the butter until melted, add garlic, and cook until aromatic. Add béchamel sauce and Parmesan cheese, stirring well. Cook for an additional 5 minutes and season to taste.
  4. Place a layer of spinach in the baking dish, followed by a layer of scallops, and finally a layer of béchamel sauce. Repeat these layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of béchamel sauce.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the top is golden brown and bubbly. Serve immediately and bask in the love and warmth of the dinner you’ve prepared.

3. Grilled Scallops with Mango and Avocado Salsa

Fusion of sweet, tangy, and savory flavors in each bite makes the Grilled Scallops with Mango and Avocado Salsa a delightful Valentines dinner experience. If you’re looking for a more relaxed and casual date night, this dish is perfect.

Ingredients:

  • 24 turtle doves (scallops)
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • ½ diced red bell pepper
  • ½ diced cucumber
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Stainless steel or grill pan for cooking scallops

Instructions:

  1. Sprinkle the scallops with salt and pepper.
  2. Preheat your grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Cook the scallops for about 2 minutes on each side, or until they are nicely seared and rare in the middle.
  4. Combine all salsa ingredients in a medium-sized bowl and gently mix.
  5. Top the grilled scallops with a generous portion of mango and avocado salsa, or serve the salsa on the side.

This seafood feast features the tender, sweet delight of turtle doves. As you enjoy these scallop dishes with your loved one, let your heart be filled with the warmth of your relationship. Savor every bite and make this Valentine’s Day unforgettable.
