Exploring Middle Eastern Breakfast: 5 Recipes to Start Your Day


The Middle Eastern breakfast table is a feast for the senses—a colorful spread of flavors, textures, and aromatic touches that bring people together. Rooted in cultural tradition, these morning meals offer nourishment, energy, and a comforting sense of togetherness. From savory to sweet, these recipes represent a blend of simplicity and complexity that makes every bite memorable. Let’s dive into five authentic Middle Eastern breakfast dishes and discover how they can transform the way you start your day.

1. Shakshuka: Eggs Poached in a Spicy Tomato Sauce

Shakshuka, a classic North African and Middle Eastern dish, has become a beloved breakfast highlight worldwide. This hearty meal features eggs poached in a rich tomato sauce, spiced with a medley of warm flavors and often served with crusty bread on the side.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 large ripe tomatoes, chopped (or one 14-oz can of diced tomatoes)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4-5 large eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sauté until softened.
  2. Add the diced bell pepper and cook for another 4-5 minutes until tender.
  3. Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, cumin, paprika, coriander, salt, and black pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  4. Make small wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-8 minutes, or until the eggs reach your preferred doneness.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro, and serve with warm pita bread or a crusty baguette.

2. Ful Medames: A Protein-Packed Fava Bean Dish

Ful medames is a beloved Middle Eastern breakfast dish, particularly popular in Egypt. Made with cooked fava beans, this dish is rich in protein and fiber, leaving you energized for the day ahead.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned or cooked fava beans
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, mashed into a paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish
  • Optional toppings: chopped tomatoes, hard-boiled eggs, tahini, or sliced onions

Instructions:

  1. Warm the fava beans in a small saucepan over low heat, mashing them lightly with a fork to release some of the starches.
  2. Transfer the beans to a bowl and mix in olive oil, lemon juice, garlic paste, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Garnish with parsley and your choice of toppings, and serve with pita bread.

Ful medames can be a customizable dish, allowing you to add layers of spices and toppings that suit your taste. Paired with a cup of tea, this dish embodies comfort and sustenance.

3. Manakish Za’atar: Middle Eastern Breakfast Flatbread

Manakish za’atar is a simple flatbread brushed with a fragrant mixture of za’atar and olive oil. Popular across the Levant region, it’s a quick and satisfying breakfast choice.

Ingredients:

  • 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (plus extra for topping)
  • 3 tablespoons za’atar spice blend

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, salt, sugar, and yeast in a mixing bowl. Gradually add warm water and olive oil, mixing to form a soft dough.
  2. Knead the dough for 7-8 minutes on a floured surface. Place it in a greased bowl, cover with a cloth, and let rise for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Divide the dough into 4 smaller portions and roll each into a circle.
  4. Mix za’atar with olive oil to form a paste. Spread the paste evenly on each dough circle.
  5. Bake for 7-10 minutes, until the edges are golden and crisp. Serve warm.

Manakish pair well with labneh (strained yogurt) or fresh vegetables, creating a balanced breakfast that’s both flavorful and healthy.

4. Labneh with Fresh Vegetables and Olives

Labneh, a creamy strained yogurt, is a staple of the Middle Eastern breakfast table. It’s tart, refreshing, and pairs beautifully with an assortment of fresh produce and olives for a light, nutritious start to the day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups plain full-fat yogurt
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Fresh vegetables: cucumber slices, cherry tomatoes, bell peppers
  • Kalamata olives or green olives

Instructions:

  1. To make labneh, mix yogurt with salt and strain through a cheesecloth or fine mesh sieve for 6-8 hours in the refrigerator.
  2. Transfer the thickened labneh to a serving plate. Drizzle with olive oil and surround with fresh vegetables and olives for dipping.

This dish is served with fresh pita bread or toasted flatbread for a delightful contrast of textures. Its simplicity is key, highlighting the natural flavors of its components.

5. Knafeh: Sweet Cheese Pastry for a Special Morning Treat

Knafeh is a decadent and sweet breakfast dish that’s more commonly enjoyed during festive occasions. This iconic Palestinian dessert features layers of shredded phyllo dough filled with creamy cheese, soaked in floral syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded phyllo dough (kataifi)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 tablespoon orange blossom water or rosewater
  • Crushed pistachios for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with butter.
  2. Mix the phyllo dough with melted butter until evenly coated. Spread half the dough into the baking dish.
  3. Combine ricotta and mozzarella cheese, then spread the mixture over the dough. Top with the remaining phyllo.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until golden brown.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until slightly thickened. Stir in orange blossom water.
  6. Pour the syrup over the warm knafeh and garnish with crushed pistachios. Serve immediately.

Knafeh offers a delightful balance of sweetness and creaminess, making it a celebratory breakfast indulgence.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern breakfast traditions showcase an incredible diversity of flavors and textures, from the rich savoriness of shakshuka and ful medames to the subtle sweetness of knafeh. Each of these dishes brings people together, telling a story of heritage, hospitality, and love for food. Whether you’re preparing for a busy day ahead or looking to spend a leisurely morning with loved ones, these recipes provide both nourishment and a sense of connection to one of the most vibrant culinary traditions in the world.
