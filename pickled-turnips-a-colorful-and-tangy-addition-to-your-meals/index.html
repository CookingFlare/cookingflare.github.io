Pickled Turnips: A Colorful and Tangy Addition to Your Meals


If you’re looking to add a burst of flavor and a pop of color to your meals, look no further than pickled turnips. These tangy and vibrant vegetables are not only delicious but also incredibly easy to make. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, pickled turnips are a must-try addition to your culinary repertoire.

What are Pickled Turnips?

Pickled turnips are a traditional Middle Eastern dish that has gained popularity around the world for its unique flavor and versatility. Made by soaking sliced or diced turnips in a mixture of vinegar, salt, and spices, pickled turnips offer a delightful combination of tanginess and crunch.

The Benefits of Pickled Turnips

Aside from their delicious taste, pickled turnips also offer several health benefits. Turnips are a low-calorie vegetable that is rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are particularly high in vitamin C, which supports a healthy immune system, and vitamin K, which is essential for blood clotting and bone health. Additionally, the pickling process enhances the turnips’ natural probiotic content, promoting a healthy gut and aiding in digestion.

How to Make Pickled Turnips

Making pickled turnips at home is a straightforward process that requires minimal ingredients and equipment. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 3 large turnips
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 2 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds

Instructions:

  1. Peel the turnips and cut them into thin slices or small cubes.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, salt, garlic, peppercorns, and coriander seeds. Bring the mixture to a boil, then remove it from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
  3. Place the turnip slices or cubes in a sterilized jar, ensuring they are tightly packed.
  4. Pour the cooled pickling liquid over the turnips, making sure they are completely submerged. If needed, add more vinegar and water in equal proportions to cover the turnips.
  5. Seal the jar tightly and store it in the refrigerator for at least 48 hours to allow the flavors to develop. The pickled turnips will keep for several weeks in the fridge.

Serving Suggestions

Pickled turnips are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • As a condiment: Add pickled turnips to sandwiches, wraps, or burgers for an extra tangy kick.
  • In salads: Toss pickled turnips into green salads or grain bowls to add a burst of color and flavor.
  • Alongside Middle Eastern dishes: Serve pickled turnips as a side dish with falafel, hummus, or kebabs for an authentic Middle Eastern experience.
  • In tacos or burritos: Use pickled turnips as a tasty topping for your favorite Mexican dishes.

Final Thoughts

Pickled turnips are a delightful addition to any meal, offering a tangy and colorful twist that is sure to impress your taste buds. With their ease of preparation and numerous serving options, pickled turnips are a versatile ingredient that can elevate the flavor profile of a wide range of dishes. So why not give them a try? Your taste buds will thank you!

Note: Remember to experiment with different spices and flavor combinations to tailor your pickled turnips to your personal taste. Enjoy the process of creating your own unique variation of this delightful dish!
