The Secrets of Chinese Rice Cakes: Sticky and Sweet Recipes


If you’ve ever indulged in the delightful world of Chinese cuisine, you’ve likely encountered the enticing allure of Chinese rice cakes. These chewy, sticky, and sweet treats hold a special place in Chinese culinary traditions, often enjoyed during festive occasions such as Lunar New Year. But what are the secrets behind these delectable rice cakes? How can you bring the authentic flavors of Chinese rice cakes into your own kitchen? In this blog post, we’ll unravel the mysteries and share some irresistible recipes that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of China.

Understanding Chinese Rice Cakes

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s take a moment to understand what Chinese rice cakes are all about. Known as “nian gao” in Mandarin, these treats are made from glutinous rice flour, which gives them their signature chewy texture. Traditionally, Chinese rice cakes are symbolic of prosperity and good luck, making them a cherished part of celebratory feasts. Their sticky and sweet nature represents the hope for a “sticky” and harmonious family bond, as well as the sweetness of life in the coming year.

Classic Chinese Rice Cake Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the glutinous rice flour and 1 1/4 cups of water to form a smooth batter.
  2. Grease a shallow heatproof dish with vegetable oil.
  3. Pour the batter into the dish and steam over high heat for 30-40 minutes, or until set.
  4. In a saucepan, dissolve the brown sugar in 1/2 cup of water over low heat to make a syrup.
  5. Once the rice cake is cooked, pour the syrup over the cake and allow it to cool and set.

Savory Stir-Fried Rice Cakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Chinese rice cakes
  • 4 ounces pork, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups Napa cabbage, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice cakes in warm water for 15-20 minutes to soften them.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat some oil over medium-high heat and stir-fry the pork until cooked.
  3. Add the garlic and Napa cabbage, and stir-fry until the cabbage wilts.
  4. Drain the rice cakes and add them to the wok, along with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken broth.
  5. Stir-fry until the rice cakes are tender and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and sesame oil, and garnish with chopped green onions before serving.

Sweet Red Bean Rice Cakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sweet red bean paste
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup toasted sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Mix the glutinous rice flour and water to form a smooth dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls and flatten them into discs.
  3. Spoon a small amount of sweet red bean paste onto each disc and seal the edges, shaping them into cakes.
  4. Steam the rice cakes for 15-20 minutes, or until they are cooked through.
  5. Roll the cooked rice cakes in granulated sugar and toasted sesame seeds for a delightful finish.

Conclusion

In Chinese culture, the preparation and consumption of rice cakes are rich with symbolism and tradition. By exploring these sticky and sweet recipes, you can not only savor the delightful flavors of Chinese rice cakes but also partake in the cultural significance they hold. Whether you’re indulging in a classic sweet rice cake or savoring the savory stir-fried variation, each bite is a celebration of heritage and culinary craftsmanship. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the secrets of Chinese rice cakes, and embark on a culinary journey that’s as rich in tradition as it is in taste.
