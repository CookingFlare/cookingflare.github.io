Lemon Poppy Seed Bread: A Refreshing Citrus Delight


Are you ready to embark on a culinary journey that combines the zesty, invigorating flavor of lemons with the delightful crunch of poppy seeds? If you’re a fan of citrus-infused treats, then this Lemon Poppy Seed Bread recipe is tailor-made for you. The tangy aroma of fresh lemons combined with the subtle crunch of poppy seeds creates a delightful symphony of flavors in every bite. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, preparing a delicious snack, or simply satisfying your sweet tooth, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your baking repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Bread:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons poppy seeds
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice

For the Glaze:

  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prep: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch loaf pan.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and poppy seeds. Set aside.

  3. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, cream together the granulated sugar and softened butter until light and fluffy.

  4. Incorporate Eggs and Zest: Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the lemon zest.

  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients, alternating with the plain yogurt, and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.

  6. Add Lemon Juice: Gently fold in the fresh lemon juice until evenly distributed.

  7. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Prepare Glaze: While the bread is cooling, prepare the glaze by whisking together the confectioners’ sugar and enough lemon juice to achieve a drizzling consistency.

  9. Drizzle and Serve: Once the bread has cooled for about 10 minutes, drizzle the glaze over the top. Allow the bread to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Tips and Tricks

  • Fresh is Best: Use freshly squeezed lemon juice and zest for the most vibrant citrus flavor.
  • Don’t Overmix: When combining the wet and dry ingredients, mix until just combined to avoid a tough texture.
  • Storage: Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Final Thoughts

The Lemon Poppy Seed Bread offers a delightful balance of tangy citrus and the subtle crunch of poppy seeds. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is a perfect way to infuse some sunshine into your kitchen and delight your taste buds. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the irresistible aroma of freshly baked Lemon Poppy Seed Bread. It’s a citrus-infused treat that’s sure to brighten your day!
