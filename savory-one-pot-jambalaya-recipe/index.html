Savory One Pot Jambalaya Recipe


Jambalaya, a classic dish originating from Louisiana, is a staple of Southern American cuisine. This one-pot wonder is a harmonious blend of flavors, textures, and aromas, making it a crowd-pleaser. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating a savory one-pot jambalaya that will transport your taste buds to the heart of the Bayou.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound smoked sausage, such as Andouille or kielbasa, sliced
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the Pot: In a large, heavy pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced sausage and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Remove the sausage from the pot and set aside.
  2. Brown the Chicken: Add the chicken to the pot and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside with the sausage.
  3. Soften the Onions: Reduce the heat to medium and add the chopped onions to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onions are translucent and starting to caramelize, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Add the Aromatics: Add the minced garlic, diced red bell pepper, and cooked chicken to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the Spices and Rice: Stir in the thyme, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, black pepper, cumin, and coriander. Cook for 1 minute. Add the long-grain white rice and stir to combine.
  6. Add the Liquid: Add the chicken broth, water, and browned sausage to the pot. Stir to combine, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  7. Simmer the Jambalaya: Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 25-30 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  8. Finish with Flavor: Remove the pot from the heat and let it rest for 5 minutes. Fluff the jambalaya with a fork and stir in the chopped scallions, parsley, and crumbled feta cheese (if using).
  9. Serve and Savor: Serve the jambalaya hot, garnished with additional scallions, parsley, and feta cheese if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier jambalaya, add more cayenne pepper or use hot sausage.
  • To add some heat, stir in some diced jalapeños or serrano peppers.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the sausage with roasted vegetables, such as zucchini, eggplant, and red bell peppers.
  • To make the jambalaya more substantial, add some cooked shrimp, crawfish, or andouille sausage.

Conclusion

This savory one-pot jambalaya recipe is a flavorful and satisfying dish that’s perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. With its rich aroma and bold flavors, it’s sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. So, gather your ingredients and get cooking – your taste buds will thank you!
