The Best Vegan Pie Recipes for Every Season


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the warm, flaky delight of a pie. Whether it’s a sweet dessert or a savory main dish, pies are versatile, delicious, and can be made vegan without compromising on flavor. With each season bringing its own bounty of fresh ingredients, there’s a perfect vegan pie to celebrate every time of the year. Join us as we explore the best vegan pie recipes that capture the essence of each season, ensuring your table is graced with delightful flavors year-round.

Spring: Fresh and Floral

As the earth awakens from its winter slumber, spring offers a vibrant array of fruits and vegetables. It’s the ideal time to create refreshing and light pies that reflect the season’s bounty.

1. Lemon Lavender Cream Pie

This pie is a delightful blend of tart and sweet, bringing together the zing of fresh lemons with the aromatic essence of lavender.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups almond flour
    • 1 cup oats
    • 3 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
    • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
    • Pinch of salt

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup soaked cashews (soaked for at least 4 hours)
    • ½ cup fresh lemon juice
    • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
    • 1/3 cup maple syrup
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 teaspoon culinary lavender

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a mixing bowl, combine almond flour, oats, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and salt. Press the mixture into a pie dish evenly. Bake for 10-12 minutes until lightly golden. Let it cool.

  2. Make the filling: In a blender, combine soaked cashews, lemon juice, lemon zest, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and lavender. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the filling into the cooled crust. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours to set. Serve garnished with fresh berries or edible flowers.

2. Asparagus and Mushroom Savory Pie

Spring vegetables like asparagus and mushrooms are at their peak during this season, making them perfect for a savory pie.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • ½ cup vegetable oil
    • 6 tablespoons cold water
    • Pinch of salt

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup asparagus, chopped
    • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 cup coconut cream

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a bowl, mix whole wheat flour and salt. Add vegetable oil and cold water. Knead until a dough forms. Roll out and fit into a pie dish.

  2. Cook the filling: Sauté onion and garlic in a pan until translucent. Add mushrooms, asparagus, thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook until vegetables are tender. Stir in coconut cream.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the filling into the crust. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25-30 minutes until golden. Let it cool before serving.

Summer: Bright and Fruity

The summer months are filled with an abundance of juicy fruits and colorful vegetables. It’s the perfect time to whip up pies that celebrate the season’s sweetness.

3. Classic Berry Pie

Nothing says summer like a pie bursting with fresh berries. This classic berry pie is both simple and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 cup vegan butter, chilled
    • ½ cup sugar
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1/4 cup ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
    • ½ cup sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a bowl, mix flour and sugar. Cut in vegan butter until crumbly. Add lemon juice and ice water, mixing until a dough forms. Divide and chill for 30 minutes.

  2. Make the filling: In a saucepan, combine mixed berries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat until bubbly and thickened.

  3. Assemble the pie: Roll out half the crust and fit into a pie dish. Pour in the berry filling. Roll out the second half and create a lattice top. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 45-50 minutes until bubbly.

4. Peach and Ginger Pie

This pie is a sweet and spicy delight, perfect for summer gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust: (Use the same crust recipe as the berry pie)

  • For the filling:

    • 4 cups peaches, sliced
    • ½ cup sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Follow the same steps as the classic berry pie.

  2. Make the filling: In a bowl, combine peaches, sugar, cornstarch, ginger, and lemon juice. Toss to coat.

  3. Assemble the pie: Follow the same assembly instructions as the berry pie but use the peach filling. Bake as previously instructed.

Fall: Warm and Comforting

As the days grow shorter and cooler, fall calls for hearty pies filled with warm spices and comforting flavors.

5. Pumpkin Spice Pie

A quintessential fall dessert, pumpkin pie is easy to make vegan and is always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust: (Use the classic pie crust recipe)

  • For the filling:

    • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
    • 1 cup coconut milk
    • ¾ cup brown sugar
    • 2 teaspoons pumpkin pie spice
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Use the classic pie crust recipe to prepare your pie shell.

  2. Make the filling: In a mixing bowl, whisk together pumpkin puree, coconut milk, brown sugar, pumpkin pie spice, vanilla extract, and cornstarch until smooth.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the filling into the prepared crust and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45-50 minutes until set. Chill before serving.

6. Sweet Potato and Sage Pie

This savory pie combines the creamy texture of sweet potatoes with fragrant sage, creating a delightful dish perfect for fall feasts.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust: (Use the same crust recipe as the berry pie)

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups mashed sweet potatoes
    • 1 onion, diced
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, chopped
    • ½ cup coconut milk
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Use the classic pie crust recipe.

  2. Make the filling: Sauté onion and garlic until translucent. In a bowl, mix mashed sweet potatoes, sautéed onion, garlic, sage, coconut milk, salt, and pepper.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the filling into the prepared crust and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30-35 minutes. Let it cool before serving.

Winter: Cozy and Indulgent

Winter is the season for rich, indulgent pies that warm the soul and satisfy cravings.

7. Chocolate Hazelnut Cream Pie

Decadent and luscious, this chocolate pie is perfect for holiday gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust: (Use the same crust recipe as the berry pie)

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup hazelnut butter
    • 1 cup coconut milk
    • ½ cup cocoa powder
    • ½ cup maple syrup
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Follow the same instructions as the berry pie.

  2. Make the filling: In a bowl, whisk together hazelnut butter, coconut milk, cocoa powder, maple syrup, and vanilla until smooth.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the filling into the crust and chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours to set.

8. Apple Cinnamon Pie

A classic winter dessert, apple pie is comforting and full of warm spices.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust: (Use the classic pie crust recipe)

  • For the filling:

    • 6 cups apples, peeled and sliced
    • ¾ cup sugar
    • 2 tablespoons flour
    • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Use the classic pie crust recipe.

  2. Make the filling: In a bowl, mix sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, and lemon juice until well coated.

  3. Assemble the pie: Pour the apple filling into the prepared crust and cover with a top crust. Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 45-50 minutes until golden and bubbly.

Conclusion

Vegan pies are a fantastic way to celebrate the changing seasons while enjoying delicious, plant-based meals.
