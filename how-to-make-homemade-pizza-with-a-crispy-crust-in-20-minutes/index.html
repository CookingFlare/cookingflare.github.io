How to Make Homemade Pizza with a Crispy Crust in 20 Minutes


There’s nothing quite like a hot, fresh pizza straight from the oven—especially when it’s made right at home. Homemade pizza allows you to have full control over every ingredient, and the best part? You can make it in just 20 minutes, with a perfectly crispy crust that rivals any pizzeria. In this post, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to achieve that crispy, golden-brown crust while ensuring your toppings are fresh, delicious, and perfectly balanced.

Ingredients for Homemade Pizza with Crispy Crust

Before we get started, make sure you have all the ingredients on hand. The beauty of this homemade pizza recipe is that it uses simple, easy-to-find ingredients that you might already have in your pantry or fridge. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • ¾ cup warm water (about 110°F)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp salt

For the Sauce:

  • ½ cup canned tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Toppings (Choose your favorites):

  • 1 ½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup sliced pepperoni or sausage (optional)
  • ½ cup sliced bell peppers, onions, or mushrooms (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Preparation Time Breakdown

Making a pizza in 20 minutes might seem impossible, but with the right techniques and ingredients, it can be done! Here’s the timeline we’ll follow:

  • Dough Preparation: 5 minutes
  • Dough Rising: 5 minutes (while you prepare toppings and sauce)
  • Pizza Assembly: 5 minutes
  • Baking: 5 minutes

So, let’s break it down and make that perfect crispy crust!

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, mix together the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy. This indicates that the yeast is active and ready to use.

While the yeast is activating, take a large mixing bowl and combine the flour and salt. Make a small well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Stir with a spoon until a dough forms, and then turn it out onto a lightly floured surface.

Knead the dough for about 1-2 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You don’t need to overdo it here; just enough to incorporate the ingredients and develop the gluten for that crispy texture.

Step 2: Let the Dough Rise for 5 Minutes

Now that the dough is ready, we’ll allow it to rise for a short 5 minutes. This step is crucial for getting that airy, crispy texture in the crust. While the dough is rising, you can begin preparing your sauce and toppings. The dough doesn’t need to double in size—just a slight puffing up will do.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

A great pizza sauce is simple but packed with flavor. To make the sauce, heat the olive oil in a small pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Then, pour in the canned tomato sauce, oregano, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Simmer the sauce for about 3-4 minutes until it’s heated through and slightly thickened. Don’t worry about cooking it for a long time—this step is just to infuse the flavors together quickly. Once done, remove the sauce from the heat and set it aside.

Step 4: Preheat Your Oven

While the sauce is simmering and the dough is rising, preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C). A very hot oven is key to achieving that crispy crust in such a short amount of time. If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well. The stone will help to distribute heat evenly and ensure the crust cooks perfectly.

Step 5: Roll Out the Dough

Once your dough has had its 5-minute rise, it’s time to roll it out. Lightly flour your surface and rolling pin, and roll the dough into your desired pizza shape. Aim for a thin crust, as this will help it cook quickly and become crispy. Don’t worry if it’s not a perfect circle—homemade pizza has charm in its imperfections!

If you’re looking for extra crispy edges, you can brush the rolled dough with a little olive oil before adding the toppings. This will help crisp up the outer edges even more during baking.

Step 6: Assemble the Pizza

Now comes the fun part—assembling your pizza! Transfer your rolled dough onto a piece of parchment paper or directly onto your preheated pizza stone or baking sheet. Here’s how to top it:

  1. Spread the sauce: Using a spoon, spread an even layer of your prepared sauce over the dough, leaving about a half-inch border around the edges for the crust.
  2. Add the cheese: Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella evenly over the sauce. Feel free to add more or less depending on how cheesy you like your pizza!
  3. Add toppings: Now, add your favorite toppings. Whether you like pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, or even a classic Margherita with just basil and mozzarella, the choice is yours. Don’t overload the pizza, though—keeping the toppings light will ensure a crispy crust.

Step 7: Bake the Pizza

Once your pizza is fully assembled, it’s time to bake. Place your pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 5-7 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy, and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned. Keep an eye on it, as different ovens can vary slightly in temperature.

If you’re using a pizza stone, the crust should cook even faster. The high heat of the oven will quickly crisp up the crust, while the cheese melts to perfection. If you prefer extra crispy edges, you can bake the pizza a bit longer—just be sure not to burn the cheese.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy!

Once your pizza is out of the oven, let it cool for a minute before slicing. This helps the cheese set and prevents any scorching of your mouth when you take that first bite. For an extra burst of flavor, sprinkle some fresh basil leaves, red pepper flakes, or a little grated Parmesan over the top before serving.

Tips for Achieving a Crispy Crust

While this recipe will give you a delicious, crispy crust in 20 minutes, there are a few tips you can keep in mind to ensure it’s even crispier:

1. Use High Heat

The hotter your oven, the crispier the crust. Preheat your oven to at least 500°F (260°C), and don’t be afraid to go even higher if your oven allows it. A hot oven ensures a quick cook time and allows the crust to crisp up before the toppings get overcooked.

2. Roll the Dough Thin

A thin crust allows for more crispiness. Avoid thick dough that will take longer to cook and can end up soft in the center. A thinner crust cooks faster and becomes delightfully crispy.

3. Use a Pizza Stone or Baking Steel

If you have a pizza stone or baking steel, use it! These tools retain and distribute heat better than a regular baking sheet, creating a crispier pizza crust. If you don’t have one, a regular baking sheet will still do the job.

4. Brush the Edges with Olive Oil

For even crispier edges, brush the outer crust with a little olive oil before baking. This adds flavor and promotes browning, ensuring that your crust is golden and crispy.

5. Don’t Overload the Pizza

Too many toppings can weigh down the pizza and prevent the crust from crisping up properly. Keep the toppings light and balanced to achieve that perfect crispy pizza base.

Final Thoughts

Homemade pizza with a crispy crust in just 20 minutes is achievable with the right ingredients, techniques, and a bit of practice. This recipe gives you the freedom to customize the pizza to your taste, all while enjoying a fast and delicious meal. The combination of a quick dough rise, a hot oven, and minimal toppings creates a pizza that’s crispy, flavorful, and perfect for a busy weeknight or casual weekend dinner.

With this simple recipe, there’s no need to order takeout. You can have a fresh, homemade pizza with a crispy crust ready in just 20 minutes. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and enjoy the ultimate pizza-making experience!
