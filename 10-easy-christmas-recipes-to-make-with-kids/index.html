10 Easy Christmas Recipes to Make with Kids


The holiday season is a perfect time to bring the family together, and what better way to bond than in the kitchen? Christmas recipes that involve kids can be both fun and festive. From sweet treats to savory snacks, these easy-to-make recipes will not only bring joy to your home but also create lasting memories with your little ones.

In this post, we’ve gathered 10 simple and creative Christmas recipes that kids can help make. These recipes require minimal prep time, are perfect for holiday gatherings, and are great for sparking a love of cooking in children. Let’s dive into these delicious and kid-friendly Christmas treats!

1. Santa Claus Pancakes

Start your holiday morning with a festive twist on traditional pancakes! Santa Claus pancakes are a delightful breakfast treat that’s easy for kids to assemble. You only need a few ingredients, and your little ones will enjoy building their own “Santa” face on top of the pancakes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of pancake mix
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 2 bananas (sliced)
  • Strawberries (sliced)
  • Mini chocolate chips
  • Whipped cream

Directions:

  1. Prepare the pancake mix according to the instructions on the box.
  2. Pour the batter onto a hot griddle, forming a round shape for Santa’s face.
  3. Cook until golden brown on both sides, then transfer to plates.
  4. Let the kids decorate their Santa faces by adding banana slices for the eyes, a strawberry for the nose, and whipped cream for the beard.
  5. Use mini chocolate chips for the eyes and a slice of strawberry for the Santa hat.

These pancakes will surely get your family into the Christmas spirit!

2. Christmas Tree Pizzas

Who says you can’t have pizza for Christmas? Christmas tree pizzas are a creative way to make dinner fun for the whole family. Kids will love decorating their own personal pizzas with toppings that form the shape of a Christmas tree.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Tomato sauce
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Assorted toppings (pepperoni, olives, green bell pepper, mushrooms, etc.)

Directions:

  1. Roll out the pizza dough and cut it into a triangle shape to resemble a Christmas tree.
  2. Spread a layer of tomato sauce over the dough.
  3. Let the kids sprinkle mozzarella cheese over the sauce, forming the “snow” on the tree.
  4. Use toppings like green peppers for the tree’s branches and small round slices of pepperoni or olives to create ornaments.
  5. Bake in the oven according to the dough package directions, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

These personalized pizzas are sure to be a hit with your little chefs!

3. Reindeer Sugar Cookies

Sugar cookies are a classic Christmas treat, and this reindeer version takes them to the next level. Decorating the cookies to look like reindeer faces adds a fun and festive touch that kids will love.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 ½ cups unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 ½ cups white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp almond extract
  • ¼ cup milk
  • Pretzels (for antlers)
  • Red M&Ms (for the nose)
  • Mini chocolate chips (for the eyes)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix the butter and sugar until creamy. Add the egg, vanilla, and almond extract, and continue mixing until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the flour and baking soda together.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut out round shapes using a cookie cutter.
  6. Place the cookies on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 8-10 minutes.
  7. Once the cookies have cooled, let the kids decorate them by adding pretzels for antlers, a red M&M for the nose, and mini chocolate chips for the eyes.

These reindeer sugar cookies are a fun activity for kids and a tasty treat for the entire family!

4. Snowman Cheese Balls

For a savory option, these snowman cheese balls are a great appetizer for your Christmas party. Easy to make and fun to decorate, kids will enjoy assembling these festive little snowmen.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz cream cheese (softened)
  • 8 oz shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tbsp ranch seasoning mix
  • 1 tbsp chopped green onions
  • Pretzel sticks (for arms)
  • Black olives (for eyes and buttons)
  • Baby carrots (for the nose)

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the cream cheese, shredded cheddar cheese, ranch seasoning, and green onions. Mix until well combined.
  2. Roll the cheese mixture into two balls: one large and one small, to form the body and head of the snowman.
  3. Stick a pretzel into each side of the larger cheese ball for the arms.
  4. Use black olives for the eyes and buttons, and a baby carrot for the nose.
  5. Refrigerate the snowman cheese balls for at least 30 minutes before serving.

These snowman cheese balls are both adorable and delicious – a perfect holiday snack for kids and adults alike!

5. Gingerbread Houses

Building a gingerbread house is a holiday tradition that children absolutely adore. It’s a fun way for them to use their creativity while celebrating Christmas. While it can be a bit more time-consuming, the results are worth it!

Ingredients:

  • Gingerbread house kit (or homemade gingerbread pieces)
  • Royal icing (for decorating)
  • Assorted candy (gumdrops, candy canes, sprinkles, etc.)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the gingerbread house kit or bake homemade gingerbread pieces and allow them to cool.
  2. Assemble the house using royal icing as glue.
  3. Let the kids decorate the house with their favorite candies—gumdrops for the roof, candy canes for the walls, and sprinkles for extra sparkle.
  4. Once the house is decorated, let the icing dry completely before displaying it.

This is a wonderful project that can be enjoyed by kids of all ages and will add a touch of holiday charm to your home.

6. Christmas Candy Bark

Candy bark is incredibly easy to make and perfect for holiday snacking. Let the kids help with spreading the chocolate and adding colorful toppings for a festive treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dark or milk chocolate chips
  • 1 cup white chocolate chips
  • Assorted candy toppings (M&Ms, crushed candy canes, sprinkles, etc.)

Directions:

  1. Melt the dark or milk chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl, stirring every 30 seconds until fully melted.
  2. Pour the melted chocolate onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and spread it into an even layer.
  3. Melt the white chocolate chips and drizzle them over the dark chocolate.
  4. Let the kids sprinkle the candy toppings over the melted chocolate before it sets.
  5. Refrigerate the candy bark for 1 hour or until hardened, then break it into pieces to serve.

This candy bark is colorful, sweet, and easy to make – perfect for a fun and festive activity with kids!

7. Mini Christmas Cupcakes

Cupcakes are a favorite among children, and these mini Christmas cupcakes are perfect for a holiday treat. The kids can decorate their cupcakes with festive toppings like sprinkles, edible glitter, and holiday-themed toppers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of chocolate or vanilla cake mix
  • 1 cup of butter (softened)
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Green food coloring
  • Red and green sprinkles

Directions:

  1. Prepare the cupcakes according to the cake mix instructions.
  2. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely.
  3. In a bowl, beat the softened butter with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth. Add a few drops of green food coloring to create green frosting.
  4. Let the kids pipe or spread the frosting onto each cupcake.
  5. Decorate the cupcakes with red and green sprinkles, and if desired, add festive toppers like mini candy canes or holiday-themed sprinkles.

These mini cupcakes are sure to be a hit with kids, and the decorating is an enjoyable activity that allows for plenty of creativity!

8. Candy Cane Brownies

Brownies are a crowd favorite, and this candy cane version adds a peppermint twist to a classic treat. Kids can help with stirring the batter and adding crushed candy canes to make these brownies even more festive.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of brownie mix (or homemade brownie recipe)
  • 2-3 crushed candy canes
  • 1 cup of chocolate chips
  • ½ cup of chopped walnuts (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the brownie mix according to the package instructions.
  2. Pour the brownie batter into a greased baking pan.
  3. Add chocolate chips and crushed candy canes on top of the batter.
  4. Bake according to the instructions on the box, then allow the brownies to cool before cutting them into squares.

These candy cane brownies are a delicious treat that will make your holiday gatherings even sweeter!

9. Christmas Fruit Kabobs

For a healthier Christmas treat, fruit kabobs are a great choice. These simple and colorful kabobs are easy to make and fun to eat.

Ingredients:

  • Red and green grapes
  • Strawberries
  • Kiwi
  • Marshmallows
  • Wooden skewers

Directions:

  1. Cut the kiwi into small rounds.
  2. Skewer a mix of red and green grapes, strawberries, kiwi, and marshmallows.
  3. Let the kids arrange the fruit and marshmallows in a festive pattern, alternating colors to make the kabobs more fun.

These fruit kabobs are both nutritious and festive, making them the perfect snack for kids to enjoy during the holiday season.

10. Frosted Christmas Pretzels

Pretzels are a great snack to make with kids during the holidays. These frosted Christmas pretzels are simple to make, and kids will love dipping them in chocolate and decorating them with sprinkles.

Ingredients:

  • Pretzel rods
  • White chocolate chips
  • Red and green sprinkles

Directions:

  1. Melt the white chocolate chips in the microwave, stirring every 30 seconds until smooth.
  2. Dip the pretzel rods into the melted white chocolate, covering them halfway.
  3. Let the kids sprinkle red and green sprinkles over the chocolate-covered pretzels.
  4. Place the pretzels on a parchment-lined baking sheet and refrigerate for 15-20 minutes until the chocolate hardens.

These frosted pretzels are a festive snack that’s sure to become a family favorite!

Whether you’re baking cookies, making pizzas, or assembling fruit kabobs, these 10 easy Christmas recipes are perfect for spending quality time in the kitchen with your kids. Get ready for a season filled with delicious food, laughter, and cherished memories!
