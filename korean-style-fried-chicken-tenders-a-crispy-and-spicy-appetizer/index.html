Korean-Style Fried Chicken Tenders: A Crispy and Spicy Appetizer


If you’re a fan of fried chicken and love a little kick in your appetizers, then you’re in for a treat. This recipe for Korean-Style Fried Chicken Tenders will satisfy your cravings for a crispy and spicy dish that will leave you wanting more. With its unique blend of flavors and textures, this appetizer is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a game night with friends or a family gathering.

Ingredients

For the chicken tenders:

  • 1 pound chicken tenders
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Korean-style sauce:

  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

For the garnish:

  • Sesame seeds
  • Chopped green onions

Instructions

  1. In a shallow bowl, mix together the flour, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and paprika. Set aside.

  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large skillet over medium heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C).

  3. Dip each chicken tender into the flour mixture, making sure it’s evenly coated. Shake off any excess flour.

  4. Carefully place the coated chicken tenders into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.

  5. While the chicken tenders are frying, prepare the Korean-style sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together the gochujang, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger until well combined.

  6. Once the chicken tenders are cooked, remove them from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate. Let them cool for a few minutes.

  7. In a large mixing bowl, pour the Korean-style sauce over the chicken tenders. Toss them gently until they are evenly coated with the sauce.

  8. Serve the Korean-Style Fried Chicken Tenders on a platter, garnished with sesame seeds and chopped green onions. They can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a side of steamed rice or kimchi for a complete meal.

Tips

  • For extra crispy chicken tenders, you can double coat them by dipping them in the flour mixture, then in beaten eggs, and finally back into the flour mixture before frying.

  • If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of gochujang in the sauce and adjust according to your taste.

  • Make sure to use a deep-fry thermometer to monitor the oil temperature and maintain it at 350°F (175°C) throughout the frying process.

  • If you don’t have gochujang, you can substitute it with sriracha or any other chili paste of your choice.

So there you have it, a delicious and flavorful recipe for Korean-Style Fried Chicken Tenders. With its crispy coating and spicy sauce, this appetizer will surely be a hit at your next gathering. Give it a try and enjoy the mouthwatering flavors of Korean cuisine right in your own home.
