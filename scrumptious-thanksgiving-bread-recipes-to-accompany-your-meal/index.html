Scrumptious Thanksgiving Bread Recipes to Accompany Your Meal


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and, of course, indulging in delicious food. While the turkey takes center stage on the Thanksgiving table, let’s not forget about the bread! Bread is a versatile and essential part of any Thanksgiving feast, and it can truly elevate your meal to new heights. In this blog post, we’ll be sharing some scrumptious Thanksgiving bread recipes that will perfectly complement your holiday spread.

1. Savory Herb Pull-Apart Rolls

These savory herb pull-apart rolls are a delightful twist on the classic dinner roll. They are soft, buttery, and packed with aromatic herbs that will tantalize your taste buds. To make these delectable rolls, you’ll need:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or parsley), finely chopped
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
  2. Gradually add the warm milk and melted butter to the dry ingredients. Mix until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Punch down the risen dough and divide it into small, equally-sized pieces.
  6. Roll each piece into a ball and dip it in the melted butter, then roll it in the chopped herbs.
  7. Place the herb-coated dough balls in a greased baking dish, leaving a little space between each roll.
  8. Cover the dish with a kitchen towel and let the rolls rise for another 30 minutes.
  9. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  10. Bake the rolls for 20-25 minutes, or until they turn golden brown.
  11. Remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving.

2. Pumpkin Cornbread

Pumpkin cornbread is a seasonal twist on the classic cornbread recipe. It adds a subtle sweetness and a beautiful golden color to your Thanksgiving table. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup pumpkin purée
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9-inch square baking pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin purée, milk, melted butter, and eggs.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cornbread to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

3. Cranberry Walnut Bread

If you’re looking for a bread with a burst of tangy sweetness and a satisfying crunch, look no further than cranberry walnut bread. It’s a delightful combination of tart cranberries and nutty walnuts. Gather these ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries, coarsely chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 3/4 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Stir in the chopped cranberries and walnuts.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the orange juice, melted butter, and beaten egg.
  5. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
  7. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Conclusion

These scrumptious Thanksgiving bread recipes are sure to impress your guests and add a delightful touch to your holiday feast. Whether you choose to make savory herb pull-apart rolls, pumpkin cornbread, or cranberry walnut bread, these breads will perfectly complement your Thanksgiving meal. So, roll up your sleeves, put on your apron, and let the aroma of freshly baked bread fill your kitchen this Thanksgiving!
