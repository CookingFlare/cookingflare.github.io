A Taste of the Middle East: 10 Lebanese-inspired Recipes for Food Presentation


The Middle East, a region known for its rich culinary heritage, is a treasure trove of flavors, aromas, and presentation styles that can inspire any food enthusiast. Lebanese cuisine, in particular, is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and stunning presentation. In this blog post, we’ll take you on a journey through the streets of Beirut and beyond, showcasing 10 Lebanese-inspired recipes that will elevate your food presentation game.

Recipe 1: Fattoush Salad with Pomegranate and Pine Nuts

Fattoush, a classic Lebanese salad, is a symphony of textures and flavors. To give it a modern twist, add some crispy pomegranate seeds and toasted pine nuts. Simply combine 4 cups of mixed greens, 1 cup of crumbled feta cheese, 1/2 cup of chopped cucumber, 1/2 cup of chopped tomatoes, 1/4 cup of chopped red onion, 1/4 cup of chopped parsley, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Top with pomegranate seeds and pine nuts.

Recipe 2: Stuffed Grape Leaves with Rice and Herbs

Dolmas, or stuffed grape leaves, are a staple in Lebanese cuisine. To add a pop of color, use a combination of fresh herbs like parsley, mint, and cilantro. Simply combine 1 cup of cooked rice, 1/2 cup of chopped fresh herbs, 1/4 cup of chopped scallions, 1/4 cup of chopped parsley, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Stuff the mixture into grape leaves and serve with a dollop of yogurt.

Recipe 3: Chicken Shawarma with Tzatziki Sauce

Shawarma, a popular street food in the Middle East, is a must-try. To give it a Lebanese twist, serve it with a refreshing tzatziki sauce made with yogurt, cucumber, garlic, and dill. Simply marinate 1 pound of chicken breast in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and oregano. Grill the chicken and serve with a side of tzatziki sauce.

Recipe 4: Grilled Halloumi Cheese with Honey and Thyme

Halloumi, a Cypriot cheese, is a favorite in Lebanese cuisine. To give it a sweet and savory twist, grill it with a drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of thyme. Simply grill 1 block of halloumi cheese for 2-3 minutes on each side. Drizzle with honey and sprinkle with thyme.

Recipe 5: Spinach and Feta Phyllo Triangles

Phyllo triangles are a popular appetizer in Lebanese cuisine. To give them a healthy twist, add some wilted spinach and crumbled feta cheese. Simply combine 1 cup of wilted spinach, 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese, 1/4 cup of chopped scallions, 1/4 cup of chopped parsley, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place the mixture in phyllo triangles and bake until golden brown.

Recipe 6: Lemon and Herb Roasted Chicken

Roasted chicken is a staple in many Middle Eastern cuisines. To give it a bright and citrusy twist, add some lemon juice and herbs like thyme and rosemary. Simply rub 1 whole chicken with a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, thyme, and rosemary. Roast the chicken in the oven until cooked through.

Recipe 7: Stuffed Bell Peppers with Quinoa and Herbs

Stuffed bell peppers are a popular side dish in Lebanese cuisine. To give them a nutritious twist, add some quinoa and herbs like parsley and cilantro. Simply combine 1 cup of cooked quinoa, 1/2 cup of chopped fresh herbs, 1/4 cup of chopped scallions, 1/4 cup of chopped parsley, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Stuff the mixture into bell peppers and bake until tender.

Recipe 8: Baba Ghanoush with Garlic and Lemon

Baba ghanoush, a popular dip in Lebanese cuisine, is a must-try. To give it a bright and citrusy twist, add some garlic and lemon juice. Simply combine 2 roasted eggplants, 1/4 cup of garlic, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, 1/4 cup of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and serve with pita bread.

Recipe 9: Grilled Eggplant with Tahini Sauce

Grilled eggplant is a popular side dish in Lebanese cuisine. To give it a creamy and nutty twist, serve it with a tahini sauce made with tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and water. Simply grill 2 eggplants for 2-3 minutes on each side. Serve with a side of tahini sauce.

Recipe 10: Baklava with Pistachios and Rose Water

Baklava, a popular dessert in Lebanese cuisine, is a must-try. To give it a unique twist, add some pistachios and rose water. Simply combine 1 package of phyllo dough, 1 cup of chopped pistachios, 1/2 cup of chopped walnuts, 1/4 cup of rose water, 1/4 cup of honey, and 1/4 cup of sugar. Layer the ingredients and bake until golden brown.

In conclusion, Lebanese cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors, aromas, and presentation styles that can inspire any food enthusiast. From the bold flavors of fattoush salad to the stunning presentation of baklava, these 10 recipes will elevate your food presentation game and take you on a culinary journey through the streets of Beirut and beyond.
