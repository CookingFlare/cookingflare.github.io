Flavorful Indian Samosa Recipes for Cooking for Two


Samosas are a beloved snack in Indian cuisine, known for their flaky pastry and rich fillings. Perfect as appetizers or snacks, these delightful treats can be tailored to suit any palate. Whether you’re craving something spicy, savory, or even sweet, making samosas at home allows you to explore various flavors while enjoying a cozy cooking experience, especially when preparing for two. Here, we will delve into some flavorful Indian samosa recipes that are perfect for a small gathering or date night.

The Perfect Samosa Dough

Before diving into the delicious fillings, let’s start with the samosa dough. A good samosa begins with a flaky, crisp pastry. Here’s a simple recipe to create the perfect dough for two servings.

Ingredients for Samosa Dough:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, carom seeds, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Incorporate the Fat: Add ghee or vegetable oil to the flour mixture. Mix well until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Knead the Dough: Gradually add water, little by little, kneading until you achieve a smooth and firm dough. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

Classic Potato and Pea Samosas

One of the most popular samosa fillings is a classic mixture of spiced potatoes and peas. This recipe is simple yet bursting with flavors.

Ingredients for Filling:

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a pan, heat oil over medium heat and add cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
    • Add the mashed potatoes and green peas to the pan. Mix well.
    • Stir in coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for about 5 minutes until everything is well combined.
    • Remove from heat, add lemon juice, and garnish with fresh cilantro. Let the mixture cool.

  2. Assemble the Samosas:

    • Divide the rested dough into two equal parts and roll each into a ball.
    • Roll out each ball into a thin oval shape. Cut the oval in half to create two semicircles.
    • Take one semicircle, fold it into a cone shape, sealing the edge with a little water. Pinch to secure.
    • Fill the cone with the potato and pea mixture, then seal the open edge by pinching it together. Repeat for the second samosa.

  3. Fry the Samosas:

    • Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Once hot, gently slide in the samosas, being careful not to overcrowd the pan.
    • Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Drain on paper towels.

Spicy Lentil Samosas

For a healthier twist, these spicy lentil samosas are a great option. Packed with protein and flavor, they are sure to impress.

Ingredients for Filling:

  • 1/2 cup red lentils (masoor dal), soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Lentils: Drain the soaked lentils and cook them in a saucepan with enough water until soft, about 15-20 minutes. Drain any excess water and let them cool.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and green chili. Sauté until the onions are translucent.
    • Stir in ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few more minutes.
    • Add the cooked lentils to the pan and mix well. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, then remove from heat and garnish with fresh coriander.

  3. Assemble and Fry: Follow the same assembling and frying instructions as the potato samosas.

Sweet Coconut and Raisin Samosas

To satisfy your sweet tooth, try these delightful coconut and raisin samosas. They are a unique dessert option that will leave your taste buds dancing.

Ingredients for Filling:

  • 1 cup grated fresh coconut (or desiccated coconut)
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon ghee

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a pan, melt ghee and add grated coconut, raisins, sugar, and cardamom powder.
    • Cook for about 5-7 minutes until the mixture is heated through and sugar dissolves. Set aside to cool.

  2. Assemble and Fry: Use the same dough and assembling instructions as mentioned before. Fry until golden brown.

Tips for Perfect Samosas

  1. Resting the Dough: Allowing the dough to rest is crucial for achieving a flaky texture. Ensure it’s covered to prevent drying out.

  2. Oil Temperature: The oil should be hot enough for frying but not smoking. If the oil is too hot, the samosas will burn on the outside while remaining uncooked on the inside.

  3. Experiment with Fillings: Don’t hesitate to experiment with various fillings such as paneer, mixed vegetables, or even non-vegetarian options. The possibilities are endless!

  4. Serve with Chutneys: Samosas are best enjoyed with a variety of chutneys. Mint chutney, tamarind sauce, or yogurt dip can enhance the eating experience.

Conclusion

Cooking samosas for two is not only a delightful culinary adventure but also a wonderful way to share a meal filled with flavors and warmth. These recipes provide a perfect balance of tradition and creativity, allowing you to explore the rich tapestry of Indian cuisine right in your own kitchen. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy the art of samosa-making with your loved one. Whether you choose the classic potato and pea, the spicy lentil, or the sweet coconut variation, each bite promises to be a flavorful journey that you both will cherish. Happy cooking!
