Exploring the Boulevardier: A Classic Whiskey Cocktail


The Boulevardier is a classic whiskey cocktail that is rich in history and flavor. Made with bourbon or rye whiskey, sweet vermouth, and Campari, this drink offers a perfect balance of sweetness, bitterness, and complexity. Whether you’re a whiskey connoisseur or a cocktail enthusiast, the Boulevardier is a must-try for any drink lover.

Ingredients

To create the perfect Boulevardier, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 ounces bourbon or rye whiskey
  • 1 ounce sweet vermouth
  • 1 ounce Campari
  • Orange peel, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.
  2. Add the bourbon or rye whiskey, sweet vermouth, and Campari.
  3. Stir gently for about 30 seconds to combine the ingredients and chill the drink.
  4. Strain the mixture into a chilled glass filled with fresh ice.
  5. Garnish with a twist of orange peel for an extra touch of flavor.

The History of the Boulevardier

The Boulevardier cocktail has an intriguing history that dates back to the 1920s. It was created by Harry MacElhone, an American bartender who moved to Paris and opened the famous Harry’s New York Bar. The bar quickly became a popular gathering place for expatriates and artists, attracting influential figures like Ernest Hemingway and F. Scott Fitzgerald.

The Boulevardier was originally published in MacElhone’s 1927 cocktail book, “Barflies and Cocktails.” The recipe called for equal parts bourbon, sweet vermouth, and Campari, making it a close relative of the Negroni cocktail. However, the use of whiskey instead of gin gives the Boulevardier a distinct character.

Tips for a Perfect Boulevardier

  • Use high-quality ingredients: Since the Boulevardier is a relatively simple cocktail, using top-notch bourbon or rye whiskey, as well as a good-quality sweet vermouth and Campari, will greatly enhance the taste.
  • Experiment with different whiskeys: While bourbon is the traditional choice for a Boulevardier, you can also try using rye whiskey for a spicier and drier flavor profile. Don’t be afraid to experiment and find your preferred whiskey combination.
  • Don’t skip the orange peel: The orange peel garnish adds a delightful citrusy aroma to the drink. Simply twist a piece of orange peel over the cocktail and drop it in for a visually appealing and aromatic finish.

Serving and Pairing Suggestions

The Boulevardier is a versatile cocktail that can be enjoyed on various occasions. Here are a few serving and pairing suggestions to enhance your drinking experience:

  • Serve it on the rocks: The Boulevardier is traditionally served over ice, allowing the flavors to develop and mellow as the ice slowly melts. This cooling effect balances the bitterness of the Campari and the sweetness of the vermouth, creating a well-rounded taste.

  • Pair it with charcuterie: The bold and complex flavors of the Boulevardier make it an excellent companion for a charcuterie board. The smoky and savory notes of cured meats, paired with the cocktail’s bitterness, create a harmonious flavor combination.

  • Enjoy it as an aperitif: The Boulevardier’s bitter and herbal undertones make it a perfect aperitif, stimulating the appetite before a meal. Sip on this classic cocktail while preparing a delicious dinner, and let the flavors awaken your taste buds.

Conclusion

The Boulevardier is a classic whiskey cocktail that deserves a place in every cocktail lover’s repertoire. Its rich history, balanced flavors, and versatility make it a timeless choice for any occasion. Whether you’re sipping it on a cozy evening or impressing guests at a cocktail party, the Boulevardier is sure to satisfy your palate. So, grab your favorite bourbon or rye whiskey, gather your ingredients, and embark on a journey to explore the depths of this captivating cocktail. Cheers!
