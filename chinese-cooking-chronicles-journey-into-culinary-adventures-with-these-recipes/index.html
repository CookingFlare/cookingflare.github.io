1. Spicy Kung Pao Chicken

The spicy Kung Pao Chicken originated from the imperial kitchens of the Qing Dynasty. This dish is a true representation of the bold and lively Chinese cuisine, passionately built upon a harmonious blend of contrasting tastes: salty, sweet, sour, and spicy.

  • Ingredients:

    • 600g boneless chicken, cut into small cubes
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • A handful of peanuts
    • 250g dried chilies, soaked and sliced into halves
    • 1 onion, thinly sliced
    • 2 tbsp Sichuan peppercorns
    • 2 tbsp soy sauce
    • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
    • 1.5 tbsp sugar
    • 1 tbsp cooking wine (optional)
    • A pinch of salt to taste
    • 2 tbsp water
    • Coriander for garnishing

  • Instructions:

    1. Heat oil in a wok on high heat. Add and let the Sichuan peppercorns, garlic, and chilies sizzle until they release their fragrances.
    2. Add the chicken cubes and spread them evenly in the wok. Let it brown on one side without turning it, then flip to cook the other side.
    3. Add the onions and peanuts. Stir-fry everything together for a couple of minutes, until the onions turn slightly golden.
    4. Combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, cooking wine, salt, and water in a small bowl to make the sauce.
    5. Pour the sauce over the chicken and stir until everything is well-coated.
    6. Lower the heat to simmer for 3-4 minutes until the sauce has slightly thickened.
    7. Give a taste test and adjust the seasoning to your preference.
    8. Lastly, garnish with coriander and serve hot with steamed rice.

2. Dumpling Delights: Xiao Long Bao

Who doesn’t love comforting themselves with a warm, juicy dumpling filled with aromatic soup? Xiao Long Bao, also known as soup dumplings, takes that joy to the next level. These delectable dumplings are works of art wrapped in tender dough with a luscious, meaty filling ready to burst.

  • Ingredients:

    • 200g all-purpose flour
    • 2 tbsp tapioca starch
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 100g water
    • 300g pork belly, minced
    • 2 tbsp minced ginger
    • 2 tbsp soy sauce
    • 1.5 tbsp rice wine
    • 1 tbsp sesame oil
    • 1 tsp white pepper
    • 2-3 chinese soup spoon of chilled stock or as needed

  • Instructions:

    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, tapioca starch, salt, and water. Mix and knead until a smooth and slightly elastic dough forms. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest for 30 minutes.
    2. While your dough is resting, prepare the filling. Combine all the filling ingredients and mix well. It’s essential that the pork filling be super smooth and gooey for a perfect soup dumpling.
    3. To assemble the dumplings, take one piece of dough about the size of a walnut and roll it into a ball. Place it between two pieces of plastic wrap and flatten it slightly. Use a rolling pin to roll the piece out into a round wrapper. Ensure the wrapper’s thickness is uniform throughout.
    4. Place 1-2 teaspoons of filling in the center of your wrapper. Wet the edges with water and fold the wrapper carefully to create a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges to seal the dumpling well.
    5. Continue to assemble all dumplings by repeating the steps above.
    6. To cook, steam or pan-fry the dumplings. For cooking steamed dumplings, place them in a steamer with some parchment paper over them. Steam for about 15 minutes or until fully cooked.
    7. For pan-fried dumplings, heat a little oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the dumplings and cook for 2-3 minutes until bottoms are crispy and browned. Then add 1/4 cup of water (adjust depending on the number of dumplings), cover, and lower heat to let them steam until all the water has evaporated.

3. Stir-fried Broccoli with Oyster Sauce

A healthy and delightful dish full of crunchy broccoli that’s packed with nutrients and smothered in a savory-sweet oyster sauce. This flavorful dish is sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

  • Ingredients:

    • 400g broccoli
    • 2 tbsp oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • Salt and sugar to taste
    • 100ml oyster sauce
    • 2 tbsp water
    • 1 tsp sesame oil
    • Cooked rice for serving

  • Instructions:

    1. Separate the broccoli into individual florets and cut them into bite-size pieces. Cut the stems into slim slices. Rinse them under water and drain.
    2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add garlic and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
    3. Add the broccoli florets and sauce for 1-2 minutes.
    4. Then add the broccoli stems. Season with salt and sugar. Cook and stir until the broccoli is crispy-tender.
    5. Combine the oyster sauce and water in another bowl.
    6. Pour the combined sauce mixture over the broccoli and give it a good stir for about 1-2 minutes to allow the sauce to coat the broccoli uniformly.
    7. Stir in the sesame oil and switch off flame.
    8. Serve hot with steaming rice and enjoy the dish.
