Spring into Salad: Seasonal Recipes to Bust Winter Blues & Celebrate New Beginnings


The arrival of spring breathes life into our cooking, ushering in an abundance of fresh produce that tastes like nature’s own celebration. After months of cozy, hearty stews and warm baked goods, our palates yearn for a lighter fare. Spring salads serve not just as a palate cleanser but as a culinary gateway to the vibrant flavors and fresh colors that spring imparts to our plates.

Take inspiration from the season with these four tantalizing salad recipes, each using an array of fresh, local ingredients that spring forth from the earth. These recipes are designed to invigorate and nourish, helping us leave the winter blues behind and embrace the promise of new beginnings.

  1. Spring Pea and Prosciutto Salad

With the first spring peas making their luscious appearance, this salad captures the essence of the season in its sweet simplicity. The burst of peas contrast beautifully with the salty crunch of prosciutto, while pecorino cheese adds a sharp, nutty dimension.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh spring peas
  • 1/4 pound prosciutto, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup grated pecorino cheese
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanche the peas for a few minutes in boiling salted water until they are just tender but still brightly colored. Remove from heat, drain, and rinse in cold water to set their vibrant green hue.

  2. In a large bowl, gently combine the peas, prosciutto slices, and pecorino cheese.

  3. Dress the salad with olive oil, noting that the pecorino also contributes to the flavor profile. Season with salt and pepper according to your taste.

  4. Gently toss to combine and serve immediately.

  5. Asparagus, Feta, and Toasted Pine Nut Salad

Asparagus season is a passionate affair of tender stalks and a subtle sweetness that pairs perfectly with the saltiness of feta and the nuts’ buttery crunch. This salad reflects the heightened flavors and textures of spring.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice from 1 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Trim the asparagus by snapping off the woody ends. Cut the stalks into 1-inch lengths, keeping some whole for textural variety.

  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the asparagus and cook until tender-crisp, approximately 3-5 minutes. Stirring occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  3. Drain the asparagus under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside to drain thoroughly.

  4. In a bowl, combine the cooked asparagus, crumbled feta, and toasted pine nuts.

  5. Prepare a simple lemon vinaigrette by whisking together olive oil and lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  6. Pour the dressing over the salad, gently toss to combine, and serve immediately.

  7. Radish, Avocado, and Mint Farro Salad

This sophisticated salad combines the earthiness of farro, the peppery zest of fresh radishes, and the richness of avocado, resulting in a satisfying and well-rounded meal. Mint adds a refreshing touch that sprawls across the palate.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup farro, quadruple rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups fresh radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Extra virgin olive oil, for drizzling
  • Lemon juice, for dressing
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring 4 cups of water, with a pinch of salt, to a boil. Add the rinsed farro and simmer slowly for approximately 15-20 minutes, or until all the water has been absorbed, and the farro is al dente. Remove from heat.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked farro, sliced radishes, diced avocado, and chopped mint.

  3. Prepare a tangy-lemony dressing by whisking together a little olive oil and lemon juice; taste and adjust as needed. Salt and pepper generously.

  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad, and toss gently to combine flavors. Adjust seasoning to taste.

  5. Allow the salad to sit for 10-15 minutes before serving to let the flavors meld together. Serve at room temperature.

  6. Dandelion Greens with Oranges and Toasted Walnuts

Familiar yet slightly unexpected, dandelion greens are nutrient-packed and have a slightly bitter flavor that contrasts nicely with the natural sweetness of oranges and the agreeable crunch of toasted walnuts. This simple masterpiece is reflective of spring’s new beginnings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch dandelion greens, washed and torn
  • 1 orange, supremes peel and segmented separately
  • 1/4 cup toasted walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the torn dandelion greens, and cook just until they wilt, no more than 2-3 minutes. Remove the pan from heat and allow the greens to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, gently toss the cooled greens with the orange supremes and chopped toasted walnuts.
  3. Prepare a tangy-rustic dressing by whisking together red wine vinegar with a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad, gently tossing until the ingredients are well-coated and evenly distributed.
  5. Serve promptly to ensure the crisp texture of the greens and fresh oranges.

As spring unfolds before us, the colors and flavors of the season have much to say. These refreshing salad recipes embrace the soul of spring with their simplicity and purity of ingredients. They welcome the season while inviting us to enjoy the renewed flavor combinations that celebrate life’s most exquisite moments.
