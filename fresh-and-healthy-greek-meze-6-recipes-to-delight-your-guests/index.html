Fresh and Healthy Greek Meze: 6 Recipes to Delight Your Guests


Greek cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and healthy cooking techniques. One of the most beloved aspects of Greek cuisine is the meze, a selection of small dishes that are perfect for sharing and indulging in a variety of flavors. In this blog post, we will explore six delicious and healthy Greek meze recipes that are guaranteed to delight your guests. From tangy dips to savory pastries, these recipes are easy to make and will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Greece.

1. Tzatziki

No Greek meze spread is complete without a bowl of refreshing tzatziki. This creamy yogurt and cucumber dip is packed with fresh flavors and pairs perfectly with warm pita bread or crunchy vegetables. To make this classic Greek dip, you will need:

  • 1 cucumber, grated and drained
  • 2 cups Greek yogurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. In a bowl, combine the grated and drained cucumber, Greek yogurt, minced garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  2. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Refrigerate for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld together.
  5. Serve chilled with pita bread or fresh vegetables.

2. Spanakopita

Spanakopita is a traditional Greek pastry filled with spinach and feta cheese. It’s a perfect combination of flaky pastry and savory filling, making it a crowd-pleaser at any gathering. Here’s how you can make this delicious meze:

  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
  4. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted.
  5. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  6. In a bowl, combine the spinach mixture with the crumbled feta cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Lay a sheet of phyllo dough on a clean surface and brush it with olive oil. Repeat this step with five more sheets.
  8. Spoon the spinach and feta mixture onto the phyllo dough, leaving a border around the edges.
  9. Fold the phyllo dough over the filling, creating a triangle shape.
  10. Repeat this step with the remaining phyllo dough and filling.
  11. Place the spanakopita triangles on a baking sheet and brush them with olive oil.
  12. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy.
  13. Serve warm as a delightful meze.

3. Melitzanosalata

Melitzanosalata is a smoky eggplant dip that is bursting with flavor. It’s a perfect addition to any Greek meze platter and can be enjoyed with warm pita bread or as a spread on sandwiches. Here’s how you can make this delicious dip:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the eggplants on a baking sheet and pierce them with a fork.
  3. Roast the eggplants in the oven for 45-60 minutes, or until they become soft and the skin is charred.
  4. Remove the eggplants from the oven and let them cool.
  5. Peel the skin off the eggplants and discard.
  6. In a bowl, mash the roasted eggplant flesh with a fork.
  7. Add the minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  8. Refrigerate for at least an hour to allow the flavors to meld together.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

4. Dolmades

Dolmades are stuffed grape leaves that are both delicious and visually appealing. The combination of rice, herbs, and lemon-infused grape leaves creates a delightful burst of flavors. Here’s how you can make this classic Greek meze:

  • 1 jar grape leaves, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. In a pot, bring water to a boil and blanch the grape leaves for a few minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a separate pot, cook the rice according to the package instructions. Let it cool.
  3. In a bowl, combine the cooked rice, chopped onion, dill, mint, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Take one grape leaf and place it flat on a clean surface. Spoon a small amount of the rice mixture onto the leaf, near the stem.
  5. Fold the sides of the leaf inward, and roll it tightly into a small cigar shape.
  6. Repeat this step with the remaining grape leaves and rice mixture.
  7. Place the dolmades in a steamer basket and steam them for 20-25 minutes.
  8. Let them cool before serving.

5. Fava

Fava is a creamy yellow split pea dip that is a staple in Greek cuisine. It’s simple to make and is often enjoyed as a spread on bread or as a side dish. Here’s how you can make this delightful meze:

  • 1 cup yellow split peas
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  1. Rinse the split peas under cold water.
  2. In a pot, bring water to a boil and add the split peas. Cook until they become tender.
  3. Drain the split peas and set them aside.
  4. In a pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  5. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
  6. Add the cooked split peas to the pan and cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  8. In a food processor, blend the split pea mixture until smooth and creamy.
  9. Add the lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  10. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

6. Greek Salad Skewers

For a refreshing and healthy addition to your Greek meze platter, try these Greek salad skewers. They are a colorful and appetizing way to enjoy the flavors of a Greek salad. Here’s how you can make these delightful skewers:

  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber, cut into chunks
  • Feta cheese, cut into cubes
  • Kalamata olives
  • Red onion, cut into wedges
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Thread the cherry tomatoes, cucumber chunks, feta cheese cubes, kalamata olives, and red onion wedges onto skewers.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the skewers.
  4. Serve chilled as a refreshing meze option.

Conclusion

With these six fresh and healthy Greek meze recipes, you can create a vibrant and flavorful spread that will impress your guests. From the tangy tzatziki to the savory spanakopita, each dish showcases the best of Greek cuisine. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Greece. Enjoy!
