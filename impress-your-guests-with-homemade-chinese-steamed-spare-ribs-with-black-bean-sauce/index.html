Impress Your Guests with Homemade Chinese Steamed Spare Ribs with Black Bean Sauce


If you’re looking to impress your dinner guests with a dish that’s bursting with flavor and cultural authenticity, then Chinese Steamed Spare Ribs with Black Bean Sauce is the way to go. This classic Chinese dish combines tender spare ribs with a savory and aromatic black bean sauce, creating a mouthwatering culinary experience that will leave your guests asking for seconds. While this recipe may seem complex at first glance, it’s surprisingly simple to make and requires just a few key ingredients. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to elevate your culinary skills to new heights as we dive into the art of preparing this delectable Chinese delicacy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork spare ribs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons fermented black beans, rinsed and finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons ginger, finely grated
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 red chili, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Spare Ribs: Start by preparing the spare ribs. If your butcher hasn’t already done so, cut the spare ribs into bite-sized pieces. Rinse the ribs under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.

  2. Marinate the Spare Ribs: In a large bowl, combine the spare ribs with the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and half of the minced garlic and grated ginger. Mix well to ensure that the spare ribs are evenly coated. Cover the bowl and let the ribs marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  3. Prepare the Black Bean Sauce: In a separate bowl, mix together the fermented black beans, the remaining minced garlic and grated ginger, oyster sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and cornstarch. Stir until the ingredients are well combined.

  4. Assemble the Dish: Once the spare ribs have marinated, arrange them in a steaming dish in a single layer. Pour the black bean sauce over the spare ribs, ensuring that they are evenly coated with the flavorful sauce. Sprinkle the chopped green onions and sliced red chili (if using) over the top.

  5. Steam the Spare Ribs: Fill a wok or large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Carefully place the steaming dish with the spare ribs into the wok, ensuring that the water does not touch the bottom of the dish. Cover the wok with a lid and steam the spare ribs over high heat for approximately 30-40 minutes, or until the ribs are tender and cooked through.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the spare ribs are done steaming, carefully remove the dish from the wok. Garnish the spare ribs with fresh cilantro leaves for a pop of color and additional flavor. Serve the spare ribs hot, accompanied by steamed rice or your choice of greens, and watch as your guests savor every delicious bite.

Tips and Tricks:

  • Choosing the Right Spare Ribs: When selecting spare ribs for this dish, opt for ribs with a good meat-to-fat ratio. This will ensure that the spare ribs are tender and succulent after steaming.
  • Enhance the Flavor: If you want to add an extra depth of flavor to the dish, consider marinating the spare ribs overnight to allow the flavors to intensify.
  • Adjusting the Spice Level: If you prefer a spicier kick, increase the amount of red chili or add a sprinkle of chili flakes to the black bean sauce.

Chinese Steamed Spare Ribs with Black Bean Sauce is a dish that embodies the essence of traditional Chinese cuisine, and by mastering this recipe, you’re sure to leave a lasting impression on your guests. So, take the plunge into the world of Chinese cooking and treat your loved ones to a culinary experience they won’t soon forget.
