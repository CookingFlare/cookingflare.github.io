Korean Spicy Braised Tofu: A Vegan Delight Bursting with Flavor


If you’re looking for a vegan dish that packs a punch of flavor, then look no further than Korean Spicy Braised Tofu. This dish combines the delicate texture of tofu with the bold and spicy flavors of Korean cuisine. It’s a perfect balance of heat, sweetness, and umami that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

To make this delicious vegan delight, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced (for garnish)
  • 1 teaspoon of toasted sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tofu. Drain the tofu and gently press it between paper towels to remove excess moisture. Cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes and set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and vegetable broth. Pour the sauce into the skillet with the onions and garlic, and stir well to combine.

  4. Carefully add the tofu cubes to the skillet, making sure they are evenly coated with the spicy sauce. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the tofu simmer for about 15 minutes, allowing it to absorb all the flavors.

  5. After 15 minutes, remove the lid and give the tofu a gentle stir. Continue simmering uncovered for an additional 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.

  6. Once the tofu is tender and the sauce has thickened, remove the skillet from the heat. Transfer the spicy braised tofu to a serving dish and garnish with sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds.

  7. Serve the Korean Spicy Braised Tofu hot with steamed rice or noodles for a complete meal. The combination of the spicy tofu and the fluffy rice is simply irresistible.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder spice level, you can reduce the amount of gochujang in the recipe. Alternatively, if you’re a heat-seeker, feel free to add more gochujang to amp up the spice.
  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add some vegetables to the dish. Sliced bell peppers, carrots, or mushrooms would be great additions.
  • If you’re not a fan of tofu, you can try substituting it with tempeh or seitan for a different texture and taste.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in a skillet or microwave before serving.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Braised Tofu is a vegan delight that will satisfy your cravings for bold and spicy flavors. With its tender tofu, fiery sauce, and aromatic garnishes, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household. Give it a try and let your taste buds dance with joy!
