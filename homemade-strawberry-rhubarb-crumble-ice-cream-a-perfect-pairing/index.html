Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble Ice Cream: A Perfect Pairing


If you’re looking for a delightful summer treat that combines the sweetness of strawberries with the tartness of rhubarb, then look no further than this Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble Ice Cream recipe. This refreshing and creamy dessert brings together the best of both worlds – the classic flavors of a fruit crumble and the cool indulgence of ice cream. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a delightful homemade dessert, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Strawberry Rhubarb Compote:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and quartered
  • 1 cup rhubarb, diced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Crumble:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

Making the Strawberry Rhubarb Compote:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the strawberries, rhubarb, granulated sugar, water, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the fruits have softened and the mixture has thickened, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the compote to cool to room temperature.

Preparing the Crumble:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Using your fingers, work the cold butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Spread the crumble mixture onto a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown. Allow the crumble to cool completely.

Making the Ice Cream Base:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the mixture for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.

Assembling the Ice Cream:

  1. Once the ice cream base is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. During the last few minutes of churning, spoon in the cooled strawberry rhubarb compote and the crumble pieces, allowing them to swirl into the ice cream.
  3. Transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

Serving Suggestions

Serve the Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble Ice Cream in chilled bowls, topped with additional crumble pieces and a fresh strawberry for a garnish. This delightful dessert pairs perfectly with a warm summer evening or as a sweet finish to a dinner party.

Conclusion

Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble Ice Cream is a delightful fusion of flavors that captures the essence of summer. The tangy sweetness of the strawberry rhubarb compote combined with the buttery crunch of the crumble creates a symphony of taste and texture in every spoonful. Whether enjoyed on its own or as a complement to a variety of desserts, this ice cream is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. So, gather the freshest ingredients, embrace the joy of homemade desserts, and indulge in the irresistible charm of this perfect pairing.
