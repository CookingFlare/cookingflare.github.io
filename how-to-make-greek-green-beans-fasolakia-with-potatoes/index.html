How to Make Greek Green Beans (Fasolakia) with Potatoes


Greek cuisine is renowned for its simple, hearty dishes that embrace the flavors of fresh vegetables, olive oil, and aromatic herbs. One such classic dish is Greek Green Beans with Potatoes, or Fasolakia me patates. This humble yet incredibly flavorful recipe combines tender green beans, creamy potatoes, and a robust tomato-based sauce infused with olive oil and spices. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting to explore Mediterranean recipes, this hearty vegan dish is easy to prepare, delicious, and packed with wholesome nutrients.

Let’s dive into the process of making Fasolakia while exploring the traditional techniques used in Greek households to achieve the perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make Fasolakia with potatoes, the ingredients are delightfully simple. Here’s what you’ll require for a batch that serves 4-6:

Fresh Produce:

  • 1 pound (450g) of fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 3 medium waxy potatoes, peeled and diced into bite-sized chunks
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4-5 ripe tomatoes, grated (or use one 14-ounce can of diced tomatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste (optional for a deeper flavor)
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and sliced (optional but adds a slight sweetness).

Pantry Staples:

  • 1/3 cup of extra-virgin olive oil (Greek olive oil preferred for authenticity)
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth (or water)
  • Kosher salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (if the tomatoes are overly acidic).

Herbs & Spices:

  • 2-3 sprigs of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • (Optional) A pinch of cinnamon for an added layer of warmth.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Preparing Fasolakia

Here’s how you can make this comforting Greek dish from scratch:

1. Prep Your Ingredients

Before you get started, ensure all your ingredients are prepped. Trim the green beans by snapping off the ends, peel and dice the potatoes, and finely chop the onion and garlic. If using fresh tomatoes, grate them and discard the peels for a smoother consistency.

2. Sauté Aromatics

In a large pot or dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 4-5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute, letting its aroma fill the kitchen.

3. Build the Base

Add the grated tomatoes (or canned diced tomatoes) and tomato paste to the pot. Stir well to combine, and let the mixture cook down for about 10 minutes. This step helps concentrate the natural sweetness of the tomatoes.

4. Add the Vegetables

Stir in the green beans, potatoes, and carrot slices (if using). Season with salt, pepper, and sugar (if needed to balance the acidity of the tomatoes). Sprinkle in the dried oregano, drop in the bay leaf, and if desired, add a small pinch of cinnamon to enhance the warmth of the dish.

5. Simmer in Olive Oil and Broth

Pour in the vegetable broth and give everything a good stir, ensuring the liquid partially covers the vegetables. Cover the pot with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and let the mixture simmer for 35-45 minutes. The slow simmer allows the green beans to become tender while the potatoes absorb the rich flavors of the sauce.

6. Check for Doneness

After 35 minutes, check the vegetables for doneness. Both the green beans and the potatoes should be melt-in-your-mouth tender, but not mushy. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of water or broth and continue cooking until everything is perfectly cooked.

7. Finish with Fresh Herbs

Remove the bay leaf from the pot and stir in the freshly chopped parsley. The vibrant parsley adds a burst of freshness and rounds out the flavors of the dish.

Tips for Making Authentic Fasolakia

  • Choose Fresh Green Beans: For the best flavor and texture, use fresh, tender green beans. If fresh beans are unavailable, frozen whole green beans are a suitable alternative.
  • Low and Slow Cooking: The key to perfect Fasolakia lies in simmering the dish slowly. This allows the flavors to meld together and ensures the vegetables absorb the rich tomato sauce.
  • Olive Oil is Essential: Don’t skimp on the olive oil—it’s a cornerstone of Greek cooking and contributes to the luxurious texture of the sauce. Opt for high-quality extra-virgin olive oil for the best results.
  • Adjust Seasonings: Taste the sauce halfway through cooking and adjust salt, pepper, or sugar levels as needed. If you want to introduce some heat, sprinkle a few chili flakes into the dish for a mild kick.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Green Beans with Potatoes is traditionally served warm as a main dish. Pair it with crusty bread to soak up the flavorful sauce, and consider serving it alongside some of these classic accompaniments:

  • A Bowl of Greek Salad: A bright salad of chopped tomatoes, cucumbers, red onion, Kalamata olives, and feta makes the perfect pairing for this dish.
  • Greek Yogurt Dip: A dollop of tzatziki or plain Greek yogurt adds a cooling contrast to the vibrant tomato-based sauce.
  • Lemon Wedges: A squeeze of fresh lemon juice over the finished dish adds brightness and a hint of acidity.

If you’re hosting a gathering, Fasolakia can also be featured as part of a mezze feast alongside dishes like stuffed grape leaves (dolmades), spinach pie (spanakopita), or creamy hummus.

Storing and Reheating Fasolakia

One of the best things about Fasolakia is that it tastes even better the day after cooking as the sauce continues to develop its flavors. Here’s how to store and enjoy leftovers:

  1. Refrigeration: Transfer cooled Fasolakia to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 3-4 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of water or broth to loosen the sauce.
  2. Freezing: If you’d like to freeze the dish, portion it into freezer-safe containers and freeze for up to 3 months. Defrost overnight in the refrigerator and reheat as instructed above.

Why Fasolakia Is Worth Making

Greek Green Beans with Potatoes is much more than a simple vegetable stew—it’s a celebration of Mediterranean simplicity. With every bite, you’ll experience the magic of fresh vegetables transformed into something extraordinary by humble ingredients like olive oil, tomatoes, and herbs. It’s a dish that resonates with history, tradition, and a deep love of natural flavors.

Whether you enjoy it as a vegan main, a side dish, or part of a larger feast, Fasolakia offers both comfort and nutrition in every bowl. So gather your ingredients, embrace the Mediterranean way of cooking, and savor this timeless dish that has warmed the hearts and kitchens of Greece for generations.

Greek Green Beans with Potatoes is a simple yet deeply satisfying dish that brings people together. Share it with family and friends, and feel free to adapt the recipe to suit your preferences. Once you master this dish, you’ll see why it holds its place as one of the most cherished recipes in the Greek culinary tradition. Happy cooking!
