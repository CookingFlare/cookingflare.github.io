Blueberry Coconut Granola: A Crunchy and Nutritious Snack


Granola is a versatile, delicious, and wholesome snack that has become a staple in many households. Whether you’re looking for a quick breakfast option, a midday snack, or a topping for your yogurt, granola fits the bill perfectly. Today, we’ll dive into a delightful recipe for Blueberry Coconut Granola. This crunchy and nutritious snack combines the sweet and tangy flavor of dried blueberries with the tropical taste of coconut, making it an irresistible treat.

Why Blueberry Coconut Granola?

Granola is often praised for its nutritional benefits. It’s typically made from whole grains, nuts, seeds, and dried fruits, providing a good source of fiber, healthy fats, and essential nutrients. The addition of blueberries and coconut in this recipe not only enhances the flavor but also boosts the nutritional profile.

Health Benefits of Blueberries

Blueberries are known as a superfood due to their high antioxidant content. They are rich in vitamins C and K, manganese, and dietary fiber. Antioxidants help combat oxidative stress in the body, which can reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

Benefits of Coconut

Coconut adds a wonderful flavor and texture to granola. It’s rich in medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs), which are a type of fat that may boost metabolism and provide a quick source of energy. Coconut is also a good source of dietary fiber, iron, and potassium.

Ingredients for Blueberry Coconut Granola

To make the perfect batch of Blueberry Coconut Granola, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 3 cups old-fashioned rolled oats: The base of our granola, providing fiber and a hearty texture.
  • 1 cup sliced almonds: Adds crunch and a dose of healthy fats.
  • 1 cup shredded unsweetened coconut: Brings in that tropical flavor.
  • 1/2 cup sunflower seeds: Adds additional crunch and nutrition.
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds: A powerhouse of omega-3 fatty acids and fiber.
  • 1/2 cup honey or maple syrup: Natural sweeteners that bind the granola together.
  • 1/3 cup coconut oil, melted: Ensures a crispy texture.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: Adds a depth of flavor.
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt: Enhances all the flavors.
  • 1 cup dried blueberries: The star of our recipe, providing a burst of sweetness and antioxidants.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Creating this delicious Blueberry Coconut Granola is straightforward. Follow these steps to ensure your granola turns out perfectly crunchy and flavorful every time.

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (163°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat to prevent sticking and make cleanup easier.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, sliced almonds, shredded coconut, sunflower seeds, and chia seeds. Stir well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

Step 3: Prepare the Wet Ingredients

In a separate microwave-safe bowl, combine the honey (or maple syrup), melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, and sea salt. Microwave for about 30 seconds, just enough to melt the coconut oil if it hasn’t already liquefied. Stir the mixture until well combined.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients. Use a spatula or your hands (if you don’t mind getting a bit sticky) to thoroughly mix everything together. Make sure all the dry ingredients are coated with the wet mixture; this will help the granola clump together and bake evenly.

Step 5: Spread on Baking Sheet

Spread the granola mixture evenly onto the prepared baking sheet. Use the back of a spoon or spatula to press it down gently, ensuring it’s in an even layer. This helps the granola bake uniformly and creates those desirable clusters.

Step 6: Bake the Granola

Bake the granola in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through to ensure even baking. Keep a close eye on it during the last few minutes to prevent burning. The granola is done when it’s golden brown and fragrant.

Step 7: Add Dried Blueberries

Once the granola is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool completely on the baking sheet. After it has cooled, mix in the dried blueberries. Cooling the granola before adding the blueberries helps them stay chewy rather than becoming too hard.

Step 8: Store and Enjoy

Transfer the cooled granola to an airtight container. It can be stored at room temperature for up to two weeks, although it rarely lasts that long! You can also freeze the granola for up to three months.

Serving Suggestions

Blueberry Coconut Granola is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in numerous ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Breakfast Bowl: Serve the granola with Greek yogurt and fresh fruit for a satisfying breakfast.
  • On-the-Go Snack: Pack some granola in a small container or baggie for a quick, nutritious snack during the day.
  • Smoothie Topper: Sprinkle granola on top of your favorite smoothie for added crunch and nutrients.
  • Dessert: Use granola as a topping for ice cream or a fruit parfait.

Customizing Your Granola

One of the great things about making granola at home is the ability to customize it to suit your preferences. Here are a few ideas to personalize your Blueberry Coconut Granola:

  • Different Nuts and Seeds: Substitute or add other nuts and seeds like pecans, walnuts, or pumpkin seeds.
  • Spices: Add a teaspoon of cinnamon or a pinch of nutmeg for extra flavor.
  • Sweeteners: Experiment with different natural sweeteners like agave nectar or brown rice syrup.
  • Dried Fruits: Mix in other dried fruits such as cranberries, raisins, or apricots.

Tips for Perfect Granola

Making granola is simple, but a few tips can help ensure it turns out perfectly every time:

  • Even Layer: Spreading the granola in an even layer on the baking sheet helps it bake uniformly and prevents burning.
  • Stirring: Stirring the granola halfway through baking ensures that it bakes evenly and doesn’t burn on the edges.
  • Cooling: Letting the granola cool completely on the baking sheet allows it to crisp up nicely.

Nutritional Information

While the exact nutritional content can vary based on specific ingredients and portion sizes, here’s a general idea of the nutrition provided by Blueberry Coconut Granola:

  • Calories: Approximately 150-200 calories per 1/4 cup serving.
  • Fiber: Thanks to the oats, coconut, and chia seeds, this granola provides a good amount of dietary fiber.
  • Healthy Fats: The almonds, coconut, and sunflower seeds contribute beneficial fats, including omega-3 fatty acids from the chia seeds.
  • Protein: Nuts and seeds add a decent amount of plant-based protein to the granola.
  • Antioxidants: Blueberries offer a significant boost of antioxidants, which are important for combating oxidative stress in the body.

Conclusion

Blueberry Coconut Granola is a delightful and nutritious snack that is easy to make at home. With its crunchy texture, sweet-tart blueberries, and tropical hint of coconut, it’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Not only is it delicious, but it also provides numerous health benefits, making it a great addition to your diet.

So why not give this recipe a try? Whether you enjoy it for breakfast, as a snack, or even as a dessert topping, Blueberry Coconut Granola is a versatile treat that will keep you coming back for more. Happy cooking!
