Slow Cooker Beef and Vegetable Stew: A Hearty Meal


There’s nothing quite like coming home to the aroma of a hearty beef and vegetable stew simmering away in the slow cooker. This classic comfort food is not only delicious but also incredibly easy to prepare. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a rich and savory dish that will warm your soul. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a cozy meal for yourself, this slow cooker beef and vegetable stew is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

For the Stew:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Garnish (optional):

  • Chopped fresh parsley
  • Crusty bread for serving

Instructions

  1. Coat the Beef: In a large bowl, toss the beef cubes with the flour until evenly coated. This will help thicken the stew as it cooks.

  2. Sear the Beef: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes in batches and cook until browned on all sides. Transfer the browned beef to the slow cooker.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the garlic and onion, and sauté until softened and fragrant. Transfer the mixture to the slow cooker.

  4. Combine Ingredients in the Slow Cooker: Add the carrots, celery, and potatoes to the slow cooker with the beef and aromatics. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). Add the bay leaves and dried thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Give everything a good stir to combine.

  5. Cook the Stew: Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 8 hours or on high for 4-5 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stew is ready, discard the bay leaves. Ladle the stew into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired. Serve with crusty bread for a complete meal.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a deeper flavor, you can sear the beef in the same skillet used for sautéing the aromatics. This helps to develop a rich, caramelized crust on the beef.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can create a slurry by mixing equal parts flour and water, then stirring it into the stew during the last hour of cooking.

Why Slow Cooker Beef and Vegetable Stew?

Slow cooker beef and vegetable stew is a versatile dish that can be customized to suit your preferences. You can easily add your favorite vegetables, such as mushrooms, peas, or bell peppers, to make it your own. The long, slow cooking process allows the flavors to intensify, resulting in a deeply satisfying meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

This stew is also a great make-ahead option. You can prepare it the night before, refrigerate the insert of the slow cooker, and simply pop it into the base and turn it on in the morning for a hassle-free dinner.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re cooking for a busy weeknight, a family gathering, or simply treating yourself to a comforting meal, slow cooker beef and vegetable stew is a timeless classic that never disappoints. With its rich flavors and tender beef, this hearty stew is bound to become a staple in your recipe collection. So, dust off that slow cooker and get ready to savor the warm, satisfying goodness of this delightful dish.
