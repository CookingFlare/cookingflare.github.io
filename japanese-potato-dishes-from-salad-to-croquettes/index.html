Japanese Potato Dishes: From Salad to Croquettes


Japan is a country with diverse and rich culinary traditions. While rice often takes the spotlight, potatoes also have found a cherished place in Japanese cuisine. Their versatility allows for an array of dishes that range from refreshing salads to comforting croquettes. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of Japanese potato dishes that exemplify Japanese flavors and cooking techniques, providing mouthwatering recipes along the way. The Role of Potatoes in Japanese Cuisine Potatoes were introduced to Japan in the late 18th century by Dutch traders. Initially met with some skepticism, they gradually became a staple in Japanese households. Known for their adaptability, potatoes make a seamless transition between Western and Eastern culinary practices. They can be fried, boiled, or mashed, and they absorb flavors beautifully. In Japanese cooking, potatoes feature prominently in soups, salads, and side dishes, often paired with umami-rich ingredients like soy sauce and miso.

  1. Japanese Potato Salad (ジャガイモサラダ) Japanese potato salad is a creamy, comforting dish that showcases the unique flavor profiles found in Japanese cuisine. Unlike Western potato salads that often feature mayonnaise heavily, this version is lighter and incorporates a medley of seasonal vegetables for added texture. Ingredients:

500g of Japanese potatoes (such as Hokkaido or Okinawa) 1/2 cup of Japanese mayonnaise 1/4 cup of finely diced cucumber 1/4 cup of finely diced carrot 2 tablespoons of diced red onion 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar Salt and pepper to taste Optional: Chopped scallions for garnish

Instructions:

Prepare the Potatoes: Begin by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into chunks. Place them in a pot of salted water and bring to a boil. Cook until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and let cool.

Make the Dressing: In a separate bowl, combine the mayonnaise, rice vinegar, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

Combine Ingredients: Once the potatoes are cool enough to handle, cut them into bite-sized pieces. In a large mixing bowl, add the potatoes, cucumbers, carrots, and red onion. Pour the dressing over the ingredients and gently mix until everything is coated.

Serve: Transfer the potato salad to a serving dish and sprinkle chopped scallions on top for a fresh finish. Chill for about 30 minutes in the refrigerator before serving.

Tips:

For added creaminess, consider mixing in a little plain yogurt. Customize your salad by including other seasonal vegetables like corn or bell peppers.

  1. Niku-Jaga (肉じゃが) Niku-jaga, or "meat and potatoes," is a comforting one-pot dish that is iconic in Japanese home cooking. The combination of tender beef, potatoes, and onions simmered in a sweet-savory sauce makes this dish a favorite among families. Ingredients:

300g thinly sliced beef (such as sirloin or ribeye) 4 medium-sized potatoes, cut into wedges 2 onions, sliced 2 carrots, cut into rounds 1/4 cup of soy sauce 2 tablespoons of sugar 1/4 cup of mirin 1 tablespoon of sake 2 cups of dashi broth or water Green beans for garnish

Instructions:

Sauté the Beef: In a large pot or deep skillet, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the sliced beef and sauté until it's browned.

Add Aromatics: Incorporate the sliced onions and cook until they are translucent.

Simmer the Vegetables: Add the potatoes and carrots to the pot, cooking for a few minutes. Then, pour in the dashi broth (or water), soy sauce, sugar, mirin, and sake. Bring to a simmer.

Cook: Lower the heat and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the flavors meld together.

Garnish and Serve: Before serving, add blanched green beans for color and presentation. Serve hot over rice for a complete meal.

Tips:

For a vegetarian alternative, replace the beef with mushrooms or tofu. Enhance the dish by adding konjac noodles.

  1. Korokke (コロッケ) Korokke, or Japanese croquettes, are a beloved street food made from mashed potatoes and other ingredients, coated in panko breadcrumbs, and fried until crispy. They're perfect as a snack or side dish. Ingredients:

500g of Japanese potatoes 1/2 cup of minced onion 1/2 cup of finely chopped meat (ground beef or pork, optional) 1/4 cup of milk 1 tablespoon of butter Salt and pepper to taste 1 cup of panko breadcrumbs Oil for frying

Instructions:

Cook the Potatoes: Peel and boil the potatoes as you did for the salad, then mash them in a bowl.

Sauté Onions (and Meat): In a frying pan, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions (and ground meat, if using) and sauté until soft. Combine with the mashed potatoes, adding milk, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let cool.

Shape the Croquettes: Once the mixture is cool enough, shape it into small patties or rounds.

Coat and Fry: Dip each patty into the panko breadcrumbs to coat. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the croquettes until golden brown on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side.

Serve Hot: Drain on paper towels and serve with tonkatsu sauce for dipping.

Tips:

Add shredded cabbage on the side for crunch. Experiment by incorporating different vegetables or cheeses into the potato mixture.

  1. Potato Chips with Wasabi This simple yet electrifying dish elevates regular potato chips to fuel your taste buds with a punch of umami. The wasabi adds a delightful heat that complements the natural crunch. Ingredients:

2 large potatoes Vegetable oil for frying 2 tablespoons of wasabi powder (adjust to taste) 1 tablespoon of salt

Instructions:

Slice Potatoes: Thinly slice the potatoes using a mandolin for even cuts. Rinse in cold water to remove excess starch.

Prepare Wasabi Mixture: Mix the wasabi powder with a little water to create a thick paste. Add salt and set aside.

Heat Oil: In a large pot or deep fryer, heat oil to 175°C (350°F).

Fry Potatoes: Fry the potato slices in batches until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels.

Coat with Wasabi: Toss warm chips with the wasabi mixture to coat evenly.

Serve Warm: Enjoy with a beverage of your choice.

Tips:

Experiment with different seasoning blends for varieties like garlic or curry. Serve alongside creamy dipping sauces to balance the heat.

Conclusion Japanese potato dishes encompass an exciting spectrum of flavors and techniques. From the creamy, comforting texture of potato salad to the hearty satisfaction of niku-jaga, these recipes offer something for everyone to enjoy. By incorporating potatoes into your meals, you can experience not only the joy of cooking but also the delightful symphony of Japanese cuisine. Next time you're in the kitchen, consider reaching for those humble potatoes. Whether frying them, mashing them, or soaking them in savory sauces, you’ll be creating dishes that welcome warmth and comfort into any meal. So gather your ingredients, channel your inner Japanese chef, and embark on a flavorsome journey with these potato dishes!
