Around My French Table: Paris & Provence for Kids Culinary Adventure


Visiting the enchanting cities of Paris and Provence with your little gastronomes in tow? Why not embark on a delightful culinary adventure, inspired by the renowned cookbook Around My French Table: Paris & Provence? Your trip will not only be filled with laughter, cherished memories, but also gastronomic delights! Here are three fun and educational recipes that celebrate the rich flavors and culinary traditions of the region with a focus on kid-friendly fare.

Recipe 1: Le Gateau De Crepe, a Flavorful Adventure

Parisian cuisine is nothing short of magical, and what can be more captivating for young adventurers than creating le gateau de crepe at home? Our fragrant stack starts with the velvety base of a soothing crepe batter, flavored with lavender, an ingredient you can source either at a local French market or online. Combine a whisper of sweetness in our batter with a little brown sugar, for your young chefs to discover the subtleties of French culinary art.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons culinary lavender

Instructions:

  1. Begin by making the batter: Whisk together flour, milk, melted butter, brown sugar, honey, and lavender buds in a bowl until entirely smooth. Allow the mix to rest for an hour, giving the flavors a chance to mingle.

  2. Warm a non-stick skillet over medium heat and pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the heated surface. Swirl to achieve an evenly thin layer.

  3. Cook for approximately 2 minutes, or until the crepe turns lightly golden at its edges. Carefully flip it over with a spatula and cook the other side for an additional minute.

  4. Transfer the crepe to a plate, and repeat the process with the remaining batter. Stack the cooked crepes on top of each other, ensuring they maintain their soft texture.

  5. Give children the creative freedom to assemble their own “gâteau de crêpes” with an assortment of toppings: sweet mascarpone cheese, fresh berries, a whisper of powdered sugar, or even chocolate hazelnut spread.

  6. Delight in the sweet aroma while witnessing lightning-fast flips, mastering the art of swirling, and savoring each tender bite of culinary Paris!

Recipe 2: A La Rousse Provençal Ratatouille, the Heart of Provence

Embrace the sunshine of Provence and unveil the secrets behind one of France’s most beloved vegetarian dishes – Ratatouille. Inspired by Julia Child’s recipe, our Ratatouille tells a story of colorful vegetables that dance together in harmony. This vegetarian delight is packed with talents waiting to be discovered – from vegetable peeling to flavoring. Here’s a simplified recipe that is sure to be a hit at home.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 medium eggplant, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 2 yellow squash, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme or herbs de Provence

Instructions:

  1. Begin by heating olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Toss in diced onions and cook until they turn soft and golden.

  2. Next, introduce the eggplant, red bell pepper, zucchini, and yellow squash, allowing them enough time to release their moisture and gently caramelize.

  3. After ten minutes, incorporate minced garlic and cook for an additional 3 minutes to infuse the flavors.

  4. Add crushed tomatoes and season with salt, freshly ground black pepper, and a sprinkle of fresh thyme or herbs de Provence.

  5. Simmer the ratatouille over low heat for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing the sun to slowly bring all the ingredients together.

  6. This rustic dish can be paired with a myriad of main courses, such as chicken, fish, or simply enjoyed on toast for a child-friendly presentation.

  7. Encourage young chefs to experiment with flavors and textures – a pinch of this and a sprinkle of that – and witness their creations come to life!

Recipe 3: A Taste of Tarte Tropézienne in Provence

Who can resist the voluptuous layers of yellow and chocolate cream encased in the delicate yeast dough of a Tarte Tropézienne? This delectable dessert, originating from St. Tropez, is a surefire way to wrap up an extraordinary culinary adventure in Provence.

Ingredients (For the Cream):

  • 8 oz cream cheese, room temperature
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar

Chocolate Cream Ingredients:

  • 2 oz semisweet chocolate
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • Confectioners’ sugar, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preparing the cream: Beat the cream cheese and butter until light and fluffy. Pour in the heavy cream, vanilla, and confectioners’ sugar, and mix until well combined.

  2. For chocolate lovers, a simple yet sublime chocolate cream can be made by heating the cream and semisweet chocolate together until smooth. Allow it to cool and sweeten to taste with confectioners’ sugar.

  3. Prepare the yeast dough and follow your preferred method, ensuring to form it into a perfect ball, et voilà – the base of your Tarte Tropézienne is complete.

  4. With young hands, combine the dough into tender layers, constructing the body and preparing for a loving embrace of luscious cream.

  5. Fill the center with yellow cream, and for chocolate enthusiasts? Devote a smaller telltale mound of chocolate cream alongside.

  6. Now, let your creations rise and bake until golden, then drizzle with melted dark chocolate, lightly swirl, and voila’ – a fresh taste of Tarte Tropézienne that will leave your kitchen fragrant with memories of Provence.

In closing, this magical culinary exploration of Paris and Provence enables families to create shared memories while embracing the art of French cooking! The harmony of flavors, the scents, and textures are certain to enchant and delight taste buds of all ages. So grab your food-safe aprons, tie on your chef hats, and start your very own kids’ culinary adventure of a lifetime. Bon appétit, mes amis!
