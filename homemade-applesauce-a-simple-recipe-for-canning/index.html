Homemade Applesauce: A Simple Recipe for Canning


Nothing beats the taste and aroma of homemade applesauce. It’s a versatile and delicious accompaniment to countless dishes. Plus, making your own applesauce allows you to control the ingredients and customize the flavors to suit your palate. In this blog post, we will guide you through a simple recipe for canning homemade applesauce, ensuring you can enjoy this delectable treat all year round.

Ingredients

  • 10 pounds of apples (use a mix of sweet and tart varieties)
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • Lemon juice (optional, for preserving color)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Apples: Wash, peel, core, and dice the apples. If you prefer a chunkier texture, leave the apples in larger pieces. For a smoother applesauce, dice them into smaller chunks.

  2. Cook the Apples: In a large pot, combine the diced apples, water, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-30 minutes until the apples are tender.

  3. Puree the Apples: Once the apples are soft, remove the pot from the heat and let it cool slightly. Using a blender or an immersion blender, carefully puree the cooked apples until you achieve your desired consistency. If you prefer a chunkier sauce, blend it for a shorter time. For a smoother texture, blend it for a longer time.

  4. Preserve the Applesauce: If you want to can the applesauce for long-term storage, now is the time to do it. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Fill the hot jars with the hot applesauce, leaving a 1/2-inch headspace at the top. Wipe the rims clean and seal the jars with the lids and bands.

  5. Process the Jars: Place the filled jars in a water bath canner or a large pot with a rack on the bottom. Make sure the jars are covered by at least 1 inch of water. Bring the water to a gentle boil and process the jars for 15 minutes. Adjust the processing time according to your altitude if necessary.

  6. Cool and Store: After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner and place them on a towel-lined countertop. Allow the jars to cool undisturbed for 12-24 hours. This cooling period ensures a proper seal. Check the lids for a tight seal by pressing down on the center of each one. If the lid pops back, it didn’t seal properly, and you should refrigerate that jar for immediate use.

  7. Enjoy Your Homemade Applesauce: Once the jars have cooled and sealed correctly, store them in a cool, dark place. Properly sealed jars can be stored for up to a year. Refrigerate any unsealed jars and consume them within a week or so.

Tips for Customizing Your Applesauce

  • Add Sweeteners: If you prefer a sweeter applesauce, increase the amount of sugar or try substituting it with honey, maple syrup, or brown sugar.
  • Spice It Up: Experiment with different spices like ginger, cloves, or allspice to add a warm and aromatic twist to your applesauce.
  • Mix Apple Varieties: Combining different apple varieties will give your applesauce a more complex flavor profile. Try using a mix of Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Fuji apples for a well-balanced sauce.
  • Preserving Color: To prevent your applesauce from browning during the cooking process, add a splash of lemon juice to the mixture. The citric acid in the lemon juice helps preserve the bright color of the applesauce.

Now that you have a simple and foolproof recipe for homemade applesauce, it’s time to get cooking! Enjoy the process of creating this delightful treat and savor the delicious results. Whether you enjoy it on its own, as a topping for pancakes, or as a side with roast pork, homemade applesauce is sure to be a hit. Happy canning!
