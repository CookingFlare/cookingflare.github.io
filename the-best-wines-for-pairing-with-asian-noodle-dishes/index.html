The Best Wines for Pairing with Asian Noodle Dishes


Asian noodle dishes are a celebration of flavors, textures, and aromas, offering a delightful blend of spices, herbs, and sauces that create truly memorable meals. From the rich broth of ramen to the zest of pad Thai or the savory tang of lo mein, these dishes often achieve a perfect harmony of sweet, sour, salty, and umami. But have you ever considered the impact of an exquisitely paired wine on your noodle experience? Selecting the right wine can elevate Asian noodle dishes to new heights, balancing bold flavors and enhancing subtler notes. Here’s how to expertly pair wines with some of your favorite Asian noodle dishes.

Understanding the Flavors of Asian Noodle Dishes

Before diving into wine recommendations, it’s crucial to understand the complex flavor profiles these dishes offer:

  1. Sweetness: Often derived from sugar, tamarind, hoisin sauce, or sweet soy sauces.
  2. Savory or Umami: Found in ingredients like soy sauce, miso, mushrooms, and fermented products.
  3. Sourness: Lime, vinegar, or other citrusy elements add brightness.
  4. Spice: Chili oils, peppers, and pastes contribute heat.
  5. Bitterness: Sometimes from herbs like cilantro or bitter greens.

These diverse flavors mean that no one-size-fits-all wine exists for all Asian noodle dishes. Instead, thoughtful selection based on the specific dish and its dominant flavors is key.

Wine Pairing Principles for Asian Cuisine

To pair wine with Asian noodle dishes, consider these principles:

  1. Balance Acidity: Choose wines with vibrant acidity to cut through richness and balance spicy or sweet notes.
  2. Match Intensity: Pair lighter wines with delicate dishes and more robust wines with bold or spicy meals.
  3. Complement or Contrast: Either complement the dish’s flavors (e.g., citrusy wines with sour notes) or create contrast (e.g., slightly sweet wines to offset spicy heat).
  4. Low Tannins: Avoid heavy tannins that can clash with salty or spicy elements, creating an unpleasant bitterness.
  5. Sweetness Matters: Slightly off-dry wines work well with both heat and sweet undertones, softening overpowering spice and enhancing delicate flavors.

Using these principles as a guide, let’s explore wine pairings for some of the most popular Asian noodle dishes.

Ramen and Wine Pairings

Ramen is as versatile as it is indulgent, with broth-based variations like tonkotsu (pork), shoyu (soy sauce), miso, and shio (salt). Its rich flavors call for wines that can match or balance its complexity.

  • Tonkotsu Ramen: The creamy, pork-based broth pairs wonderfully with fuller-bodied whites like Chardonnay (especially unoaked options) or Viognier. The slightly creamy texture of these wines mirrors the richness of the broth without overpowering it.

  • Shoyu Ramen: With its salty, umami-forward soy-based broth, this ramen shines with a dry Riesling or Pinot Gris. Their acidity cuts through the saltiness and enhances the savory notes.

  • Miso Ramen: Miso’s earthy flavor pairs beautifully with Gewürztraminer. Its slight sweetness harmonizes with the umami richness, and its floral notes add intrigue.

  • Spicy Ramen: For heat-driven ramen, opt for a lightly sweet Moscato or an off-dry sparkling wine like Prosecco. These wines provide a refreshing, cooling balance to spicy heat.

Pad Thai and Wine Pairings

Pad Thai, with its sweet, sour, savory, and nutty elements, is a perennial favorite. The lime tang, tamarind sweetness, peanut garnish, and protein options (from shrimp to chicken) present multiple pairing opportunities.

  • Sweet Whites: A chilled Riesling (off-dry) or Chenin Blanc complements the dish’s tangy-sweet notes while also softening any spice from chili flakes.

  • Sparkling Wines: A crisp Cava or Brut Champagne accentuates the bright acidity and provides a refreshing counterbalance to the dish’s richness.

  • Rosé Wines: For a slightly savory pairing, opt for a dry Rosé. Its red fruit notes enhance the dish’s sweetness without overwhelming it.

Lo Mein and Wine Pairings

Lo mein’s soy-based, stir-fried goodness often incorporates savory meats or vegetables, making it a deeply umami-driven dish. Its fat and saltiness require wines with cleansing acidity.

  • Pinot Noir: This light-bodied red has bright red fruit flavors and soft tannins, making it an excellent match for soy sauce’s umami depth.

  • Beaujolais: Particularly the Beaujolais-Villages variety, this fruity red wine adds freshness that balances the dish’s richness and highlights its savoriness.

  • Sauvignon Blanc: The wine’s herbal and citrusy notes contrast the soy-based saltiness while refreshing the palate.

Pho and Wine Pairings

Pho, the iconic Vietnamese noodle soup, boasts a fragrant broth seasoned with star anise, cinnamon, and cloves. Whether it’s beef-based pho bò or chicken-focused pho gà, the wine pairing depends on its spices and freshness.

  • Dry Riesling: A crisp Riesling balances the aromatic spices and complements fresh herbs like cilantro and basil.

  • Grüner Veltliner: This Austrian white wine, with its herbaceous and peppery notes, pairs exceptionally well with pho’s complex flavors.

  • Pinot Gris: The slight floral undertones of Pinot Gris can enhance pho’s delicate yet layered flavor.

Spicy Noodle Dishes and Cooling Wine Pairings

Many Asian noodle dishes, such as Korean jjajangmyeon or Singapore noodles, incorporate bold spices that demand wines capable of soothing the heat.

  • Moscato d’Asti: Its effervescent sweetness provides relief from the spice while complementing the dish’s sweet and savory flavors.

  • Gewürztraminer: This aromatic wine pairs effortlessly with heavily spiced dishes due to its inherent sweetness and fruity nature.

  • Lambrusco: A slightly sweet, sparkling red wine like Lambrusco adds a unique pairing for spicy, saucy noodle dishes.

Yaki Udon and Wine Pairings

Yaki udon, a Japanese stir-fried noodle dish, features thick, chewy noodles coated in a savory soy-based sauce, often with vegetables and meat or seafood.

  • Chardonnay: A lightly oaked Chardonnay’s subtle creaminess and balanced acidity pair seamlessly with yaki udon’s rich, savory flavors.

  • Sake: While not a wine, sake is an excellent choice, as its rich umami characteristics align harmoniously with soy-based sauces.

Vegetarian Noodle Dishes and Wine Pairings

Vegetarian noodle dishes like soba noodles in sesame sauce or vegetable chow mein often rely on earthy and nutty ingredients. Wines that are light and fresh accentuate their natural qualities.

  • Provence Rosé: This light, dry rosé pairs beautifully with sesame or peanut-based sauces, balancing earthy flavors with bright acidity.

  • Albariño: A crisp, citrusy Spanish white wine, Albariño provides a refreshing contrast to nutty or vegetable-based noodle dishes.

  • Sparkling Wine: A vibrant sparkling wine cuts through richer vegetarian sauces and highlights fresh herbs or greens.

Final Tips for Asian Noodle and Wine Pairing

  1. Temperature Matters: Serve white and sparkling wines well-chilled to offer refreshing relief for spiced dishes.
  2. Experiment: Flavors are subjective, so don’t hesitate to try unconventional pairings to discover your preferences.
  3. Portion Size: If the dish is particularly rich, smaller portions with a sip of well-paired wine leave a balanced and satisfying impression.

Asian noodle dishes and wines are a match made in culinary heaven, offering endless opportunities for exploration and delight. By considering the dish’s flavor profile and employing a touch of creativity, you can craft pairings that complement your favorite noodles while refreshing your palate with each sip. So, uncork a bottle, grab your chopsticks, and savor the harmony of exquisite food and wine. Bon appétit—or, in this case, kanpai!
