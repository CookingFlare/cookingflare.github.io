The Classic Sazerac Ice Cream: A Spicy and Boozy Dessert


If you’re a fan of the classic Sazerac cocktail, then you’re in for a real treat with this unique dessert twist. Introducing the Classic Sazerac Ice Cream, a spicy and boozy delight that will leave your taste buds begging for more. This recipe combines the bold flavors of rye whiskey, absinthe, and aromatic bitters with a rich and creamy ice cream base. Get ready to indulge in a dessert that’s sure to impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons rye whiskey
  • 2 teaspoons absinthe
  • 1 teaspoon aromatic bitters

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Place the saucepan over medium heat and stir until the sugar has dissolved completely. Do not let the mixture come to a boil.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks until smooth and creamy. Slowly pour about 1/2 cup of the warm cream mixture into the bowl with the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper the eggs. This will prevent them from curdling.

  3. Pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream mixture. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. This will help remove any lumps or bits of cooked egg.

  5. Stir in the rye whiskey, absinthe, and aromatic bitters until well combined. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, making sure it touches the surface of the mixture to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld.

  6. Once the mixture is chilled, transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes.

  7. Transfer the churned ice cream to a lidded container and freeze for an additional 2 hours to firm up.

  8. Serve the Classic Sazerac Ice Cream in bowls or cones, and garnish with a sprinkle of aromatic bitters or a drizzle of absinthe for an extra touch of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For a stronger absinthe flavor, you can add an extra teaspoon of absinthe to the mixture before churning.

  • If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make this recipe. After step 5, pour the mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer. Every 30 minutes, remove the dish from the freezer and stir vigorously with a fork to break up any ice crystals. Repeat this process for about 3-4 hours, or until the mixture reaches a creamy consistency.

  • Feel free to experiment with different bitters flavors, such as orange or lavender, to create your own unique twist on this recipe.

  • This ice cream pairs beautifully with a warm slice of apple pie or a decadent chocolate brownie.

Now that you have the recipe for this delicious and indulgent treat, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get started. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a special dessert, the Classic Sazerac Ice Cream is sure to impress. So go ahead, take a scoop and savor the spicy and boozy flavors that make this dessert truly unforgettable. Enjoy!
