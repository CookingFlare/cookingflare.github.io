Poultry Recipes for the Perfect Holiday Dinner


As the holiday season approaches, there’s a palpable excitement in the air: the smell of cinnamon and pine, the sparkle of twinkling lights, and the warmth of gathering around the table with loved ones. That table isn’t complete without a show-stopping dish, and for many, poultry takes center stage during festive feasts. Whether you’re crafting a holiday dinner that’s elegant, comforting, or unique, poultry recipes provide versatility, flavor, and a sense of tradition that captures the essence of the holidays.

This post dives into a curated selection of poultry-based recipes that are perfect for an unforgettable holiday dinner. From golden roasted turkey to richly flavored chicken dishes, these recipes are designed to help you shine as the host or hostess with the most. Let’s get started!

Herb Butter Roasted Turkey

The holiday turkey is a timeless classic, synonymous with celebratory dinners. This herb butter roasted turkey recipe combines aromatic herbs with rich butter to create a juicy bird with golden, crispy skin.

Ingredients:

  • 12–14 pound turkey (thawed)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh sage
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper (to taste)
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 2 onions, quartered
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth or turkey stock

Instructions:

  1. Prep the turkey: Remove the giblets and pat the turkey dry with paper towels. Place on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan.
  2. Make the herb butter: In a medium bowl, mix softened butter, rosemary, thyme, sage, garlic, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Rub the turkey: Spread the herb butter generously under the skin, over the skin, and inside the cavity.
  4. Add aromatics: Stuff the cavity with lemon halves and onion quarters for additional flavor.
  5. Roast: Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C). Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan to keep the turkey moist during cooking. Roast the turkey according to its weight (approximately 15 minutes per pound), basting occasionally.
  6. Rest before slicing: Let the turkey rest for at least 20 minutes before carving to preserve juices.

Serve this turkey alongside cranberry sauce and classic sides for a dinner centerpiece that guests will rave about.

Crispy Herb-Stuffed Cornish Game Hens

For hosts seeking a more intimate holiday dinner, Cornish game hens are a charming alternative to a full-sized turkey. Their smaller size allows faster cooking, making them perfect for elegant yet manageable holiday meals.

Ingredients:

  • 4 Cornish game hens
  • 2 cups cornbread stuffing mix (or homemade stuffing of choice)
  • ½ cup melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the stuffing: Make the stuffing according to package directions or your preferred recipe. Let it cool slightly before use.
  2. Stuff the hens: Carefully stuff each hen cavity with the prepared stuffing.
  3. Season the hens: Rub the hens all over with melted butter, olive oil, garlic powder, onion powder, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the hens in a roasting pan and bake for 45–50 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  5. Rest and serve: Let the hens rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with lemon wedges for added flavor.

Pair these charming birds with roasted vegetables and a drizzle of gravy for a stunning plate presentation.

Maple Mustard Glazed Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs offer rich, succulent meat that’s perfect for capturing bold flavors. This maple mustard glaze blends sweet and tangy elements, giving your holiday menu a contemporary twist.

Ingredients:

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • ⅓ cup pure maple syrup
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Set your oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a baking sheet or line it with parchment paper.
  2. Make the glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together maple syrup, Dijon mustard, olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Season and coat chicken: Arrange chicken thighs on the prepared baking sheet. Brush the glaze generously over each thigh.
  4. Bake: Roast for 35–40 minutes, or until the chicken’s internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) and the skin is caramelized.
  5. Serve: Transfer the chicken thighs to a platter and garnish with fresh herbs, if desired.

Serve these flavorful chicken thighs with mashed potatoes or wild rice pilaf and sautéed green beans for a well-rounded dish.

Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Roulades

For a more refined holiday poultry dish, chicken roulades make a sophisticated statement. This recipe incorporates spinach and cheese, rolled into tender chicken breasts for an impressive presentation.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • ½ cup ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare chicken: Butterfly the chicken breasts carefully, ensuring they’re evenly sliced but not broken through completely. Pound them gently to about ½-inch thickness.
  2. Create the filling: In a medium bowl, mix together spinach, ricotta, Parmesan, garlic powder, onion powder, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff and roll: Spread the filling onto each chicken breast. Roll tightly, securing the ends with toothpicks.
  4. Sear and bake: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the roulades until golden, then transfer to a baking dish. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes.
  5. Serve: Remove toothpicks carefully before plating. Slice each roulade for a visually striking presentation.

Serve with garlic mashed potatoes and a rich holiday sauce for extra decadence.

Duck Breast with Orange Glaze

For those looking to deviate from traditional turkey or chicken, duck provides a rich, luxurious addition to the holiday menu. Pair it with an orange glaze to create a dish that’s both festive and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless duck breasts
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • ⅓ cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme

Instructions:

  1. Prep the duck: Score the duck skin in a crosshatch pattern without cutting the meat. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Cook the duck: Heat a skillet over medium heat and place the duck breasts skin-side down. Render the fat until the skin is crispy, then flip and cook the other side for 3–5 minutes.
  3. Make the glaze: In a saucepan, combine orange juice, honey, soy sauce, and thyme. Simmer until slightly thickened.
  4. Glaze and serve: Brush the duck breasts with the orange glaze. Slice thinly and serve.

Duck is best paired with roasted root vegetables or a bright citrus salad for a perfect holiday plate balance.

Final Thoughts

Poultry recipes are the heart and soul of many holiday menus, offering endless possibilities to suit any culinary style while bringing people together around the table. Whether you stick with the traditional roast turkey, opt for smaller Cornish game hens, or surprise your guests with duck breasts or stuffed roulades, the most important ingredient is the love and effort you put into the process. These dishes are sure to make your holiday dinner one to remember.

Experiment, enjoy the seasonal flavors, and savor every moment spent with family and friends. After all, the holidays are about connection, and nothing connects people quite like a meal cooked with care.
