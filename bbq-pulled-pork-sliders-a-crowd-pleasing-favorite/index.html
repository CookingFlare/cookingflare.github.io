BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders: A Crowd-Pleasing Favorite


When it comes to hosting gatherings, there’s one dish that never fails to delight: BBQ pulled pork sliders. These mouthwatering little sandwiches pack a punch with their bold flavors and tender, juicy meat, making them an irresistible treat for friends and family alike. Whether you’re planning a backyard barbecue, a game-day party, or a casual get-together, BBQ pulled pork sliders are sure to be a hit with your guests. What Makes BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders So Special? BBQ pulled pork sliders offer a variety of textures and flavors that perfectly complement each other. The slow-cooked pork, typically seasoned with a robust rub, becomes incredibly tender as it cooks, allowing it to be easily shredded with a fork. Tossed in a rich, homemade barbecue sauce, each slider delivers a delightful balance of sweetness and tanginess that keeps everyone coming back for more. Serve them on fluffy slider buns, and don’t forget the toppings! The versatility of BBQ pulled pork sliders means you can customize them to suit your crowd’s tastes. Whether your guests prefer a classic approach or a creative twist, these sliders can be adapted to fit any occasion. Let’s dive into the details of crafting the perfect BBQ pulled pork sliders! Ingredients for BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders For the Pulled Pork

4-5 lbs pork shoulder (also known as pork butt) 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon smoked paprika 1 tablespoon garlic powder 1 tablespoon onion powder 1 tablespoon brown sugar 1 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon black pepper 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat) 1 cup chicken broth (or apple cider for extra sweetness)

For the BBQ Sauce

1 cup ketchup 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar 1/4 cup brown sugar 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce 1 tablespoon mustard (yellow or Dijon) 1 tablespoon smoked paprika 1 teaspoon garlic powder Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving

Slider buns (mini brioche buns work wonderfully) Coleslaw (for added crunch and flavor) Pickles (optional, for a tangy kick)

Equipment Needed

Slow cooker or Instant Pot Mixing bowls Forks (for shredding the pork) Serving platter

Tips for Choosing the Right Cut of Pork When selecting your pork shoulder, look for a piece with a good amount of marbling. The fat will render during cooking, keeping the meat moist and flavorful. Don’t shy away from a little fat; it contributes to the richness of the pulled pork. Preparing the Pulled Pork Step 1: Seasoning

Prepare the rub. In a mixing bowl, combine the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper. Mix until well combined.

Coat the pork. Pat the pork shoulder dry with paper towels. Rub the olive oil over the meat and then generously apply the spice mixture, ensuring all sides are well covered.

Step 2: Cooking the Pork Slow Cooker Method:

Place the pork in the slow cooker. Pour the chicken broth (or apple cider) into the bottom of the slow cooker, then add the seasoned pork on top.

Cook. Cover and cook on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-6 hours, until the pork is fork-tender.

Instant Pot Method:

Sear the pork. Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté function. Add a little olive oil and sear the pork on all sides until browned (about 4-5 minutes per side).

Add broth. Pour in the chicken broth, then close the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 60 minutes. Allow a natural release for 15 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.

Step 3: Shredding the Pork Once the pork is cooked, use two forks to shred it directly in the slow cooker or Instant Pot. If there’s excess fat, feel free to discard it. Now, you have a mound of deliciously tender pulled pork just ready to be slathered in BBQ sauce. Crafting the Perfect BBQ Sauce While you could use a store-bought sauce, making your own adds a personal touch and allows you to customize the flavor. Here’s how to whip up a simple and tasty BBQ sauce.

Combine ingredients. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until smooth.

Heat the sauce. Transfer the mixture to a saucepan over medium heat. Stir occasionally and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Add sauce to pork. Once the sauce has thickened slightly, mix it with the shredded pork in the slow cooker or Instant Pot. Stir until the pork is nicely coated.

Assembly of the Sliders Now that you have your succulent pulled pork ready, it’s time to make those sliders!

Prepare the buns. If you’d like, lightly toast the slider buns on a grill or in the oven for added texture.

Layer the pulled pork. Place a generous scoop of the BBQ pulled pork on the bottom half of each slider bun.

Add toppings. Top with a spoonful of coleslaw for crunch and freshness. Optional: add pickles for an extra tangy bite.

Cap it off. Place the top half of the slider bun on each assembled slider.

Serve! Arrange your BBQ pulled pork sliders on a serving platter and watch as your guests dive in.

Pairing Ideas To make your gathering complete, consider some side dishes that will complement your BBQ pulled pork sliders:

Classic potato salad – Creamy with the right amount of seasoning, it’s a BBQ staple. Corn on the cob – Fire-grilled or boiled, corn is a sweet addition to the meal. Baked beans – The sweetness and smokiness pair perfectly with the pulled pork. Chips and dips – Fresh salsa or guacamole can add a refreshing contrast to the richness of the sliders.

Storing Leftovers If you find yourself with leftover pulled pork (although it’s unlikely!), store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It can also be frozen for up to 2 months. When ready to enjoy, simply reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop, adding a bit of barbeque sauce for moisture. Conclusion BBQ pulled pork sliders are a versatile, crowd-pleasing favorite that can elevate any event. With their combination of smoky flavors, tender meat, and customizable toppings, they are sure to be a hit at your next gathering. Whether you make the pulled pork from scratch or opt for a simple preparation, these sliders will definitely have your guests raving. And while they are perfect for summer barbecues, they can be enjoyed year-round, serving as a hearty and comforting meal. So fire up your slow cooker or Instant Pot and get ready to indulge in the ultimate BBQ pulled pork sliders — your taste buds will thank you!
