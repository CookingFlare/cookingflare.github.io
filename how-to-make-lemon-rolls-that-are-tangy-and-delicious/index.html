How to Make Lemon Rolls That Are Tangy and Delicious


Lemon rolls are the perfect combination of sweet, tangy, and buttery goodness. These fluffy, soft rolls are infused with a fresh lemon flavor that balances out the sweetness, creating an unforgettable treat for any occasion. Whether you’re baking them for breakfast, brunch, or as a dessert, lemon rolls are sure to become a favorite in your household. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about how to make lemon rolls that are tangy, delicious, and sure to impress.

Why Lemon Rolls?

Lemon rolls are an exciting variation of traditional cinnamon rolls. The zesty citrus flavor provides a refreshing contrast to the sweetness of the dough and glaze, making each bite a burst of sunshine. These rolls are soft and airy, with a tangy filling that’s balanced with just the right amount of sweetness. The best part? They are incredibly versatile. You can enjoy them warm out of the oven or have them as a snack throughout the day.

If you’re looking for a way to add a little twist to your baking routine, lemon rolls are a perfect choice. Here’s everything you need to know to make these delightful rolls from scratch.

Ingredients for Lemon Rolls

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup milk (warm)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter (melted)
  • 2 large eggs

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)

For the Lemon Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon milk or cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon zest (for garnish)

These ingredients are easy to find and will ensure you get the most flavorful lemon rolls. Once you have everything ready, it’s time to start making your dough.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Lemon Rolls

1. Prepare the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk (around 110°F) and a teaspoon of sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the milk and let it sit for about 5 minutes. You should see it become frothy as the yeast activates. If it doesn’t foam, the yeast may be expired, and you’ll need to start with fresh yeast.

While the yeast is activating, in a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Once the yeast is ready, add the melted butter and eggs to the milk mixture, stirring well to combine. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients while mixing. Stir until the dough comes together.

Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes. The dough should be smooth and elastic. If it feels too sticky, add a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much, as you want the dough to remain soft and pliable.

Once kneaded, form the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover it with a clean kitchen towel and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

2. Make the Lemon Filling

While the dough is rising, you can prepare the lemon filling. In a bowl, combine the softened butter, sugar, lemon zest, lemon juice, and cinnamon (if using). Mix until smooth and creamy. This lemon filling is the heart of the rolls and will add that delicious tanginess that makes these rolls stand out.

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface.

3. Roll Out the Dough

Using a rolling pin, roll the dough into a large rectangle, approximately 12x18 inches. The dough should be about 1/4-inch thick. Once the dough is rolled out, spread the lemon filling evenly over the surface of the dough. Be sure to cover it completely, leaving a small border around the edges.

4. Roll and Slice the Dough

Starting from one long edge of the rectangle, carefully roll the dough into a tight log. Once rolled up, slice the dough into 12 equal-sized pieces using a sharp knife or dental floss. If you use dental floss, slide it under the roll, then cross the ends over the top and pull tightly to cut cleanly through the dough without squishing it.

Place the sliced rolls in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, making sure they are close together but not touching. This will allow them to rise and bake together into one deliciously soft roll.

5. Let the Dough Rise Again

Cover the pan with a clean towel and let the rolls rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until they have puffed up and are nearly touching each other. This second rise ensures the rolls will be light and airy.

6. Bake the Lemon Rolls

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Once the rolls have finished rising, place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the rolls are golden brown on top. The smell of freshly baked lemon rolls will fill your kitchen and have everyone eagerly waiting for a taste!

7. Prepare the Lemon Glaze

While the rolls are baking, make the lemon glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, lemon juice, milk, and zest until smooth. The glaze should be thick but pourable. If it’s too thick, add a little more milk; if it’s too runny, add more powdered sugar until you reach the desired consistency.

8. Glaze the Rolls

Once the rolls are out of the oven and still warm, drizzle the lemon glaze generously over the top. The glaze will seep into the warm rolls, adding an extra layer of tangy sweetness that enhances the overall flavor.

9. Serve and Enjoy

Allow the rolls to cool slightly before serving. These lemon rolls are best enjoyed warm, but they also make a wonderful treat the next day. They’re perfect for breakfast, brunch, or as an afternoon snack with a cup of tea or coffee.

Tips for Perfect Lemon Rolls

  • Use fresh lemons: Fresh lemon juice and zest are key to getting that bright, tangy flavor that makes these rolls so special. Avoid bottled lemon juice if possible, as it doesn’t have the same fresh taste.
  • Don’t skip the second rise: The second rise is essential for making the rolls light and fluffy. Be patient and let the dough rise fully before baking.
  • Butter is your friend: Use unsalted butter for both the dough and the filling for the best flavor. The richness of the butter helps balance the acidity of the lemon.
  • Store leftovers properly: If you have any leftovers, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. You can also freeze them for up to a month. Just reheat them in the oven for a few minutes to get them warm and soft again.

Variations and Add-Ins

If you want to experiment with flavors, there are a few variations you can try with your lemon rolls:

  • Lemon Poppy Seed Rolls: Add 2-3 tablespoons of poppy seeds to the lemon filling for a delightful crunch.
  • Blueberry Lemon Rolls: Add a handful of fresh or frozen blueberries to the lemon filling for an added burst of flavor.
  • Cream Cheese Filling: For a richer filling, combine cream cheese with the lemon butter filling. This will give the rolls a smooth, tangy center.
  • Lemon and Lavender Rolls: Add a touch of dried culinary lavender to the lemon filling for a fragrant, floral note.

Conclusion

Lemon rolls are a delicious, tangy, and sweet treat that can brighten up any occasion. With their fluffy texture, zesty lemon flavor, and sweet glaze, they’re perfect for breakfast, brunch, or dessert. By following the steps outlined above, you’ll be able to make these incredible lemon rolls from scratch and share them with your family and friends. Don’t forget to get creative with variations and enjoy this delightful citrus-infused twist on a classic favorite.

Happy baking!
