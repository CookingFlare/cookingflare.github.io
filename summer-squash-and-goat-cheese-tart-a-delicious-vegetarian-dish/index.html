Summer Squash and Goat Cheese Tart: A Delicious Vegetarian Dish


Summer is in full swing, and with it comes an abundance of fresh and flavorful vegetables. One of the most underrated and versatile summer squash varieties is the yellow crookneck. Its sweet and slightly nutty flavor makes it a perfect addition to a variety of dishes, including this delicious vegetarian tart.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large yellow crookneck squash, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Make the Pastry Crust

In a large bowl, combine the flour, confectioners’ sugar, and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the beaten egg, crumbled goat cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, and chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Assemble the Tart

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges and press the dough into the corners of the pan.

Spread the goat cheese mixture evenly over the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Arrange the sliced summer squash in a concentric circle pattern on top of the cheese mixture. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Bake the Tart

Bake the tart for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack.

Serve and Enjoy

Serve the Summer Squash and Goat Cheese Tart warm or at room temperature. It’s perfect as a main course or as a side dish for a summer dinner party. You can also serve it as a brunch item or as a light lunch.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the tart more substantial, you can add some cooked sausage or bacon to the filling.
  • You can also use other types of summer squash, such as zucchini or pattypan, in place of the yellow crookneck.
  • Experiment with different cheeses, such as feta or ricotta, for a unique flavor profile.
  • Serve the tart with a side salad or roasted vegetables for a well-rounded meal.

Conclusion

The Summer Squash and Goat Cheese Tart is a delicious and easy-to-make vegetarian dish that’s perfect for summer. The combination of sweet and nutty summer squash, tangy goat cheese, and flaky pastry crust is a match made in heaven. Whether you’re looking for a main course or a side dish, this tart is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the flavors of summer!
