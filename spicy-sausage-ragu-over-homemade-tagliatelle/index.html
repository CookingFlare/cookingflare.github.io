Spicy Sausage Ragu Over Homemade Tagliatelle


When it comes to classic Italian dishes, few meals are as satisfying as a plate of fresh, homemade pasta topped with a rich, flavorful ragu. If you’re craving a hearty, comforting dish with a touch of heat, look no further than Spicy Sausage Ragu over Homemade Tagliatelle. This recipe combines the perfect balance of spices, savory sausage, and homemade tagliatelle that will transport you straight to Italy with each bite.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through making this incredible meal from scratch—starting with the sausage ragu, followed by preparing the tagliatelle, and finally, combining them for a meal that’s sure to impress your family and friends. So, let’s dive into this mouthwatering recipe!

What Makes Spicy Sausage Ragu Special?

Sausage ragu is a traditional Italian sauce that is typically simmered low and slow to develop deep, rich flavors. The key to a good ragu lies in its ingredients—quality sausage, fresh tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic herbs. In our spicy version, we’ll be adding a bit of heat, making it the perfect option for those who love a little extra kick in their meals.

The sausage in this ragu provides a deep, savory base that is enhanced by the sweetness of fresh tomatoes, the richness of olive oil, and the aromatic notes from garlic, onions, and herbs. By adding a touch of chili flakes or fresh chili peppers, we bring an additional layer of warmth and spice to the sauce.

Pairing this bold and flavorful ragu with homemade tagliatelle, a type of pasta that is light yet sturdy enough to hold up to the hearty sauce, is the ultimate comfort food experience.

Ingredients You’ll Need for the Sausage Ragu

To make a perfectly balanced spicy sausage ragu, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Sausage Ragu:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil – for sautéing the aromatics.
  • 1 onion – finely chopped.
  • 4 cloves garlic – minced.
  • 1 carrot – grated (this helps balance the acidity of the tomatoes).
  • 2 pounds Italian sausage – remove the casing if using links. You can use mild or spicy sausage depending on your preference.
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili flakes – adjust to your spice tolerance. Alternatively, use fresh chopped chili peppers.
  • 1 cup red wine – adds depth of flavor.
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes – or fresh tomatoes, chopped.
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste – helps thicken the sauce.
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano – for a Mediterranean herb flavor.
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried basil – another essential Italian herb.
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme – adds earthy notes.
  • Salt and pepper – to taste.
  • Fresh basil – optional, for garnish.

For the Homemade Tagliatelle:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour – or “00” flour, which is ideal for pasta.
  • 3 large eggs – room temperature.
  • A pinch of salt – to enhance the pasta’s flavor.
  • A few tablespoons of water – for adjusting dough consistency if necessary.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Spicy Sausage Ragu Over Homemade Tagliatelle

1. Preparing the Sausage Ragu

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics
Start by heating olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until it softens. Add the minced garlic and grated carrot, and continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes until the garlic becomes fragrant.

Step 2: Brown the Sausage
Add the sausage to the pan, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon. Cook for 6-8 minutes until the sausage is browned and cooked through. You want to get some caramelization on the meat to develop the flavors.

Step 3: Add the Heat
Once the sausage is browned, sprinkle in the red chili flakes (or fresh chili peppers) to bring in that spicy kick. Stir well, making sure the sausage is evenly coated with the spices.

Step 4: Deglaze with Wine
Pour in the red wine, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any browned bits stuck to the surface. Allow the wine to cook off for about 2-3 minutes until it reduces slightly.

Step 5: Add the Tomatoes and Seasonings
Now, add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together to combine and bring it to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce will thicken and develop an incredibly rich flavor.

Step 6: Taste and Adjust
After 45 minutes, taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if needed. You may want to add more chili flakes if you like it spicier, or a pinch more salt and pepper for balance. If the sauce is too thick, add a little water or chicken broth to reach your desired consistency.

2. Making Homemade Tagliatelle

While the ragu is simmering and developing flavors, it’s the perfect time to prepare the fresh pasta. Making tagliatelle from scratch is surprisingly simple, and it elevates this dish to an entirely new level of authenticity.

Step 1: Create the Pasta Dough
On a clean surface, create a mound with the flour, then make a well in the center. Crack the eggs into the well, add a pinch of salt, and whisk the eggs with a fork, slowly incorporating the flour from the edges.

Step 2: Knead the Dough
Once the mixture begins to come together, use your hands to knead the dough. It should take about 10 minutes to form a smooth, elastic dough. If it’s too dry, add a tablespoon of water at a time until the dough comes together. If it’s too wet, sprinkle a little more flour.

Step 3: Rest the Dough
Wrap the dough in plastic wrap or cover it with a damp towel and let it rest for at least 30 minutes. Resting the dough helps the gluten relax, making it easier to roll out later.

Step 4: Roll Out the Dough
After the dough has rested, roll it out into thin sheets using a rolling pin or a pasta machine. Tagliatelle is typically about 1/8-inch thick. Once rolled out, dust the sheets with a bit of flour and fold them loosely.

Step 5: Cut the Tagliatelle
Use a sharp knife to cut the pasta into wide ribbons, about 1/4-inch in width. Unfold the ribbons and dust them with flour to prevent sticking.

3. Cooking the Tagliatelle

Step 1: Boil the Water
Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Salt is crucial for pasta cooking as it enhances the flavor of the pasta.

Step 2: Cook the Tagliatelle
Add the fresh tagliatelle to the boiling water and cook for about 2-3 minutes. Fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried, so keep a close eye on it. Once the pasta is al dente, drain it, reserving a little bit of the cooking water.

4. Combining the Sausage Ragu and Tagliatelle

Step 1: Toss the Pasta with the Sauce
Add the freshly cooked tagliatelle directly to the pan with the sausage ragu. Toss the pasta in the sauce, adding a little reserved pasta water if necessary to help the sauce coat the noodles. The starch from the pasta water helps the sauce adhere better.

Step 2: Final Touches
Serve the spicy sausage ragu over the homemade tagliatelle, and garnish with fresh basil if desired. A sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese would also be a fantastic addition to this hearty dish.

Why Homemade Tagliatelle Makes a Difference

While store-bought pasta can certainly work in a pinch, there’s something truly special about homemade tagliatelle. The texture is unmatched, with a soft but firm bite that’s perfect for soaking up the rich, spicy sausage ragu. Fresh pasta is also lighter than its dried counterpart, allowing the flavors of the sauce to shine through without overwhelming the dish.

Additionally, making pasta from scratch is a rewarding and therapeutic experience. It doesn’t require any special equipment aside from a rolling pin and a sharp knife, and the results are well worth the effort. Once you try it, you may never go back to store-bought pasta again!

Tips for Success

  • Use quality sausage: For the best ragu, choose a high-quality Italian sausage. If possible, look for sausages with a bit of fat as this will add richness to the sauce.
  • Let the sauce simmer: The longer the ragu simmers, the better the flavor. Don’t rush it—let the sauce develop its depth over time.
  • Don’t skip the resting time for the dough: Resting the dough allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out and resulting in a smoother pasta.
  • Add pasta water for sauce consistency: The starchy pasta water helps thicken the sauce and makes it cling to the pasta better.

Final Thoughts

Spicy Sausage Ragu over Homemade Tagliatelle is the perfect dish for a cozy dinner or a special occasion. The hearty ragu, bursting with flavors and a touch of heat, pairs beautifully with the tender homemade pasta. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or a group of friends, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy.

Enjoy this dish with a glass of red wine, and you’ll feel like you’ve just stepped into a traditional Italian trattoria. Buon appetito!
