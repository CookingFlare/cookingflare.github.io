Saltwater Secrets: Quaint Seafood Recipes Passed Down Through Generations


For generations, coastal families have cherished the art of seafood cooking, with recipes and techniques passed on from one family member to another. From the crisp, cool Atlantic to the sunny, warm Pacific, these seafood recipes stand the test of time. Discover the incredible flavors of the sea with this carefully curated collection of traditional yet delectable recipes that’ll transport your taste buds straight to the coast.

  1. Grandmother’s Crispy-Skinned Seared Salmon

There’s nothing quite like biting into perfectly seared salmon with its indulgent crispy skin. Let’s explore a family-favorite recipe to bring a touch of nostalgia to your table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 (6 ounce) salmon fillets, skin on
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves removed from stems
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Pat damp salmon fillets with paper towels and season both sides with salt and black pepper.

  2. In a large non-stick skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium-high heat. Once melted, add the seasoned salmon fillets, skin-side down, and cook for 6-8 minutes until the skin is crispy and golden. Try not to move it around too much.

  3. Flip the salmon fillets and add the thyme leaves and remaining butter to the pan. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through but still tender.

  4. Drizzle with lemon juice, garnish with extra thyme leaves, and serve immediately.

  5. Great Aunt Mary’s Grilled Shrimp Skewers

One of the best-kept secrets to mouth-watering grilled shrimp is the marinade. Here’s a recipe guaranteed to transform your backyard BBQ game.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 6 bamboo skewers soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.

  2. Add the shrimp to the bowl, ensuring they are well-coated with the marinade. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to 2 hours for the best flavor.

  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated shrimp onto the prepared skewers, around 6-8 per stick.

  4. Grill the skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the shrimp are pink, cooked through, and slightly charred.

  5. Remove from the grill, let rest for a minute, and serve hot.

  6. Uncle John’s Hearty Fish Chowder

Warm and comforting, this traditional fish chowder recipe will become a go-to for any chilly night.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound potatoes, diced
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground red pepper
  • 1 pound white fish (such as cod or haddock), diced
  • 3 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the potatoes, red pepper, and diced fish to the pot, stirring to combine.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Stir in the heavy cream, salt, and black pepper. Continue simmering for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped parsley. Serve the chowder hot, garnished with additional parsley if desired.

Discover the enticing depths of the sea with these generations-old seafood recipes. Each comforting dish is guaranteed to transport you to the coastal settings of your family tree, evoking the memories and stories behind each delicious bite. Whether your taste buds crave crispy-skinned salmon, plump and smoky grilled shrimp, or the warmth of a hearty fish chowder, indulge in the ocean’s treasures and reconnect with your culinary roots.

Remember, it’s the little family secrets and cherished recipes that add that special je ne sais quoi to each dish. Keep these recipes alive and let them flourish for generations to come. Happy cooking!
