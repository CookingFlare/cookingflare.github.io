How to Make Your Own Za'atar Spice Blend: Recipes Included


Za’atar is a fragrant, earthy, and tangy Middle Eastern spice blend that has become a staple in kitchens around the world. With its distinctive combination of herbs, sesame seeds, and sumac, za’atar enhances a variety of dishes—from meats and vegetables to flatbreads and dips. While you can purchase pre-made za’atar in stores, making your own za’atar blend at home gives you the freedom to adjust the proportions of the spices and herbs to your taste. In this blog post, we’ll explore how to make your own za’atar spice blend and provide some exciting recipes to try it out in.

What Is Za’atar?

Za’atar is a traditional spice mix originating from the Middle East, particularly popular in countries like Lebanon, Syria, Jordan, and Palestine. The blend’s core ingredients typically include dried thyme, oregano, sumac, and sesame seeds. The balance of these ingredients creates a flavorful combination that is aromatic, tangy, and slightly nutty. Some variations may also include cumin, coriander, fennel seeds, or salt. Za’atar is incredibly versatile—it can be used as a seasoning for meats, sprinkled over salads, or mixed with olive oil to make a dip.

While za’atar’s exact origins are debated, the name “za’atar” actually refers to the thyme-like herb used in the blend, and it has been a part of Middle Eastern cooking for centuries.

Ingredients for Homemade Za’atar Spice Blend

Making za’atar at home is easy and requires just a few simple ingredients. Depending on the region, the ingredients and their proportions may vary, but the basic elements remain consistent. Here’s what you need:

Traditional Za’atar Ingredients:

  1. Dried Thyme – The herb that gives za’atar its signature earthy flavor. Thyme is the backbone of the blend and provides a strong herbal note.
  2. Sumac – A tangy, citrusy spice made from dried sumac berries. Sumac gives za’atar its signature tartness and adds depth to the flavor profile.
  3. Sesame Seeds – These add a mild, nutty flavor and a slight crunch. Toasting the sesame seeds brings out their flavor.
  4. Oregano – Adds a slightly bitter, aromatic, and earthy flavor. Some variations of za’atar use oregano in place of thyme, or sometimes in combination with it.
  5. Salt – Enhances the flavors and ties everything together.

Optional Ingredients:

Depending on your personal preferences or regional variations, you can add other spices or herbs to your za’atar blend:

  • Cumin – For a warm, earthy note that complements the herbal flavors.
  • Coriander – For a slightly citrusy undertone that brightens up the blend.
  • Marjoram – For a sweeter, milder herb flavor that can soften the harshness of the thyme.
  • Fennel Seeds – Adds a hint of licorice-like sweetness, a popular addition in some variations of za’atar.

How to Make Za’atar at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Ingredients

Start by collecting the essential spices: dried thyme, sumac, sesame seeds, oregano, and salt. If you’d like to try a variation, include cumin, coriander, or fennel seeds as well. You can adjust the quantities based on your taste preferences or what you have on hand.

Step 2: Toast the Sesame Seeds

The sesame seeds are an integral part of za’atar, and toasting them enhances their nutty flavor. Place a dry skillet over medium heat and add the sesame seeds. Stir frequently to prevent burning, and toast them for about 3-5 minutes until they turn golden brown and become fragrant. Set them aside to cool before adding them to the spice blend.

Step 3: Mix the Herbs and Spices

Once your sesame seeds have cooled, it’s time to combine the other ingredients. In a bowl, add the dried thyme, sumac, oregano, and salt. If you’re using any additional spices like cumin, coriander, or fennel seeds, add them to the mix as well. Stir everything together to ensure the spices are well distributed.

Step 4: Add the Toasted Sesame Seeds

Once the spices are mixed, stir in the toasted sesame seeds. These will give the za’atar its signature texture and crunch.

Step 5: Store Your Za’atar

Za’atar can be stored in an airtight container or a glass jar. It will keep for several months in a cool, dry place. Make sure to store it away from direct sunlight to preserve its freshness. You can also keep it in the fridge for longer shelf life, though it’s best used within a few months to maintain its vibrant flavors.

How to Use Za’atar in Your Cooking

Za’atar is incredibly versatile, so once you’ve made your homemade blend, the options for using it in cooking are endless. Here are some ideas on how to incorporate za’atar into your meals:

1. Za’atar Chicken

Za’atar works wonderfully as a seasoning for roasted or grilled chicken. To make za’atar chicken, mix olive oil with a few tablespoons of za’atar, and rub it all over your chicken. Let it marinate for at least an hour, then roast it in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for 30-40 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). The za’atar adds a delightful, herby flavor to the chicken while keeping it juicy and tender.

2. Za’atar Flatbreads (Manakish)

Manakish is a popular Middle Eastern flatbread topped with za’atar and olive oil. To make za’atar flatbread, roll out dough (you can use pizza dough or make your own from scratch), brush it with olive oil, and generously sprinkle za’atar over the top. Bake it in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for 10-12 minutes, or until the bread is golden and crispy. Serve it as a snack, appetizer, or side dish.

3. Za’atar Hummus

Za’atar can elevate your hummus to a whole new level. Simply sprinkle a generous amount of za’atar over the top of your homemade or store-bought hummus. Drizzle with olive oil and serve with pita bread or vegetable crudités. The tanginess of the sumac and the earthiness of the thyme blend beautifully with the creamy hummus.

4. Za’atar Roasted Vegetables

Za’atar is a great addition to roasted vegetables. Toss your favorite vegetables, such as cauliflower, carrots, or sweet potatoes, in olive oil, salt, and za’atar. Roast them in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for 20-30 minutes or until they are golden and tender. The za’atar adds a savory, herbaceous note to the vegetables, making them irresistibly delicious.

5. Za’atar Labneh Dip

Labneh is a creamy strained yogurt that pairs perfectly with za’atar. To make a quick za’atar labneh dip, spread a thick layer of labneh on a platter, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle generously with za’atar. Serve with pita chips or fresh vegetables for a refreshing and flavorful snack.

6. Za’atar Eggs

For a quick and easy breakfast, scramble or fry eggs and sprinkle za’atar on top. You can also make a za’atar omelet by adding the spice blend directly into the egg mixture. This adds an earthy, herby flavor that complements the richness of the eggs.

7. Za’atar and Olive Oil Dip

Za’atar mixed with olive oil makes a simple yet flavorful dip for bread. Just pour a little olive oil into a shallow dish, sprinkle with za’atar, and dip your favorite bread (like pita or baguette) into the mixture. The richness of the olive oil and the aromatic spices create a delightful contrast.

Za’atar Variations

While the traditional za’atar blend is delicious on its own, feel free to experiment with other spices and herbs to make your own unique version. Here are a few variations you can try:

1. Za’atar with Garlic

Add a little garlic powder or freshly minced garlic to your za’atar blend for a fragrant, savory kick. This works particularly well in marinades for meats or on roasted vegetables.

2. Za’atar with Cumin and Coriander

For a spicier, deeper flavor, mix in ground cumin and coriander. These two spices bring warmth and complexity to the za’atar, making it a perfect match for grilled meats or stews.

3. Za’atar with Chili Flakes

If you like a bit of heat, try adding red chili flakes or cayenne pepper to your za’atar. This fiery version is great for spicing up dips or sprinkling over roasted potatoes.

4. Za’atar with Orange Zest

For a fresh, citrusy twist, add dried orange zest to your za’atar blend. This variation pairs beautifully with seafood dishes or roasted vegetables.

Conclusion

Making your own za’atar at home is an easy and rewarding way to add a burst of flavor to your meals. With its vibrant combination of herbs, sesame seeds, and sumac, za’atar is a versatile spice blend that can be used in a wide variety of dishes, from roasted meats and vegetables to dips and flatbreads. By crafting your own za’atar, you can experiment with the proportions and ingredients to suit your taste. So, get creative, and enjoy the delicious flavors of homemade za’atar in your kitchen!
