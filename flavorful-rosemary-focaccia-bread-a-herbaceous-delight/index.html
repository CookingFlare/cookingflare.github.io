Flavorful Rosemary Focaccia Bread: A Herbaceous Delight


Focaccia bread, a classic Italian staple, is a delight to the senses. The aroma of freshly baked bread, the softness of the dough, and the subtle tang of herbs all come together to create a culinary experience like no other. In this recipe, we’ll take the traditional focaccia to new heights by infusing it with the piney flavor of rosemary. This herbaceous delight is perfect for accompanying soups, stews, or simply served on its own as a snack.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sea salt

Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Let the mixture sit for 5-7 minutes, or until the yeast becomes frothy and bubbly.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

Add the olive oil, salt, and 2 cups of flour to the bowl. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Gradually add the remaining 2 cups of flour, 1/2 cup at a time, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. Knead the dough for 10-15 minutes, until it becomes slightly sticky.

Step 4: Add the Rosemary and Sea Salt

Add the chopped rosemary and coarse sea salt to the dough. Mix until the herbs are evenly distributed.

Step 5: Let the Dough Rise

Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 6: Shape the Dough

Punch down the dough and shape it into a rectangle or circle, depending on your preference.

Step 7: Let the Dough Rise Again

Place the shaped dough on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes.

Step 8: Bake the Focaccia

Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes, or until it’s golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 9: Cool and Serve

Remove the focaccia from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for 5-10 minutes. Slice and serve warm, or let it cool completely and store it in an airtight container for up to 3 days.

Tips and Variations

  • To enhance the rosemary flavor, you can increase the amount of rosemary to 3-4 tablespoons.
  • If you prefer a stronger herb flavor, you can add a sprinkle of dried rosemary on top of the focaccia before baking.
  • To make a garlic rosemary focaccia, simply add 1-2 cloves of minced garlic to the dough along with the rosemary.
  • You can also experiment with different herbs, such as thyme or oregano, to create unique flavor profiles.

Conclusion

Rosemary focaccia bread is a delightful addition to any meal. The piney flavor of the rosemary pairs perfectly with the soft, pillowy texture of the bread. Whether you’re serving it as a side dish or using it as a base for sandwiches, this flavorful bread is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the joy of freshly baked bread infused with the subtle beauty of rosemary.
