Instant Pot Recipes for Kids: Fun and Tasty Meals


When it comes to feeding kids, parents often face a trifecta of challenges: creating meals that are nutritious, delicious, and easy to prepare. The Instant Pot, a versatile kitchen gadget that has revolutionized home cooking, is the perfect solution for tackling those busy weeknight dinners or lazy weekend lunches. With its ability to pressure cook, sauté, and more, you can whip up meals in a fraction of the time while still delivering flavors that your little ones will love. Here are some fun and tasty Instant Pot recipes specifically designed to appeal to kids while keeping things simple for busy parents.

1. Cheesy Chicken and Rice

This creamy, cheesy dish is a kid-approved favorite and a complete meal all in one pot. Packed with protein, wholesome grains, and ooey-gooey cheese, it’s both comforting and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pound chicken breasts (diced into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup basmati rice (rinsed)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • ½ cup frozen peas
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and heat the olive oil. Add the diced chicken and cook until lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Season with garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Cancel the “Sauté” function and stir in the rinsed rice, chicken broth, and frozen peas.
  3. Secure the lid, ensuring the valve is set to “Sealing.” Pressure cook on high for 8 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and creamy. Serve warm, and watch your kids light up with joy!

2. Sloppy Joe Pasta

Combine the flavors of a classic Sloppy Joe with kid-friendly pasta for a dinner full of fun and flavor. This Instant Pot twist keeps the mess to a minimum.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound lean ground beef
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 2 ½ cups beef broth
  • 1 ½ cups tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheddar cheese for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and brown the ground beef with the onion and bell pepper. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Stir in the elbow macaroni, beef broth, tomato sauce, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  3. Secure the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Pressure cook on high for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  4. Open the lid, stir well, and serve topped with shredded cheddar cheese if desired.

3. Kid-Approved Mac and Cheese

Who doesn’t love mac and cheese? This Instant Pot version eliminates the need for boxed mixes, creating a creamy, homemade dish that’s ready in a snap.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ cups whole milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ½ teaspoon mustard powder (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Add the elbow macaroni, water, and salt to the Instant Pot. Secure the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Pressure cook on high for 4 minutes, followed by a quick release.
  2. Switch to “Sauté” mode. Stir in the milk, shredded cheese, butter, and mustard powder (if using).
  3. Mix until the cheese is completely melted and the macaroni is creamy. Serve immediately and watch it vanish!

4. Sweet and Tangy Meatballs

Perfect as a main dish or snack, these bite-sized meatballs are coated in a sweet and tangy sauce that kids will adore.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey or beef
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce
  • ½ cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the ground turkey (or beef), breadcrumbs, egg, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Roll into small meatballs.
  2. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” mode and brown the meatballs slightly (they don’t need to cook fully yet). Remove and set aside.
  3. In the Instant Pot, whisk together the barbecue sauce, pineapple juice, and honey (or brown sugar).
  4. Return the meatballs to the pot, ensuring they are coated in the sauce. Secure the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Pressure cook on high for 8 minutes, then allow a natural release for 5 minutes before opening.
  5. Serve warm with rice or pasta, or let the kids eat them as finger food!

5. Applesauce Oatmeal

Start the day on a sweet note with this hearty, fruit-filled oatmeal. It’s a healthy way to give kids the energy they need to tackle the day ahead.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup steel-cut oats
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • ½ cup milk
  • Fresh fruit for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Add the steel-cut oats, water, applesauce, and cinnamon to the Instant Pot. Stir to combine.
  2. Secure the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Pressure cook on high for 8 minutes, followed by a natural release for 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the maple syrup (if using) and milk until creamy. Top with fresh fruit, and serve!

6. Rainbow Veggie Quinoa

If you’re looking to sneak more veggies into your kids’ meals, this colorful quinoa dish is perfect. It’s vibrant, fun to eat, and full of nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 1 ¾ cups vegetable broth
  • ½ cup diced red bell pepper
  • ½ cup diced yellow bell pepper
  • ½ cup shredded carrots
  • ½ cup frozen corn
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the quinoa, vegetable broth, bell peppers, carrots, corn, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper to the Instant Pot.
  2. Secure the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Pressure cook on high for 1 minute, followed by a natural release for 10 minutes.
  3. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and serve as a colorful side dish or main course.

Cooking for kids doesn’t have to be a chore. With these fun and tasty Instant Pot recipes, you can create meals that everyone in the family will enjoy. Whether you’re making dinner, breakfast, or a quick snack, your Instant Pot is your best ally in the kitchen. So put on that apron, gather the ingredients, and let the magic of kid-friendly cooking begin!
