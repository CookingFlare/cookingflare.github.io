The Joy of Jelly: Recipes for Canning Sweet Spreads


Welcome to The Joy of Jelly, where we celebrate the art of canning sweet spreads! If you’re a fan of fruity, flavorful, and versatile spreads, you’ve come to the right place. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of delicious jelly recipes that you can easily make at home. Get ready to embark on a sweet and satisfying journey!

Strawberry Delight

Let’s kick things off with a classic: Strawberry Delight jelly. Bursting with the natural sweetness of ripe strawberries, this jelly is sure to become a favorite in your household. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 4 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup of lemon juice
  • 1 package of powdered pectin

  1. In a large pot, combine the strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to allow the strawberries to release their juices.

  2. Place the pot on the stove over medium heat and bring the mixture to a boil. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Once the mixture reaches a rolling boil, add the powdered pectin and stir well. Continue boiling for another minute, then remove from heat.

  4. Skim off any foam that may have formed on the surface of the jelly. This will help ensure a smooth and clear final product.

  5. Ladle the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, then seal with lids and rings.

  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes to ensure proper sealing and preservation.

  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place. The jelly will continue to set as it cools.

Blueberry Bliss

Next up, we have a luscious Blueberry Bliss jelly that captures the essence of summer. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 cups of fresh blueberries
  • 4 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of lemon juice
  • 1 package of powdered pectin

  1. In a large pot, combine the blueberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Crush the blueberries slightly to release their juices.

  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it comes to a boil. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Once the mixture reaches a rolling boil, add the powdered pectin and stir well. Boil for an additional minute, then remove from heat.

  4. Skim off any foam from the surface of the jelly.

  5. Pour the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace. Clean the rims, then seal with lids and rings.

  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.

  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Peachy Keen

Last but not least, let’s indulge in the sweet and fragrant Peachy Keen jelly. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 cups of ripe peaches, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of lemon juice
  • 1 package of powdered pectin

  1. In a large pot, combine the peaches, sugar, and lemon juice. Let the mixture sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it reaches a boil. Stir occasionally.

  3. Add the powdered pectin and stir well. Boil for another minute, then remove from heat.

  4. Skim off any foam from the surface of the jelly.

  5. Carefully ladle the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving a 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, then seal with lids and rings.

  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.

  7. Once cooled, store the jars in a cool, dark place.

Conclusion

There you have it - three delightful jelly recipes to bring a touch of sweetness to your pantry. Whether you prefer the classic allure of strawberries, the vibrant burst of blueberries, or the fragrant essence of peaches, these homemade jellies are sure to satisfy your cravings. Enjoy them spread on toast, swirled into yogurt, or even as a topping for ice cream. The possibilities are endless!

Remember, canning your own sweet spreads allows you to control the ingredients and flavors, ensuring the freshest and most delicious results. So go ahead, unleash your inner jelly enthusiast, and experience the joy of homemade preserves. Happy canning!

Note: The recipes provided are for personal use and should be followed carefully to ensure safe canning practices. Always consult reputable sources for detailed canning instructions and guidelines.
