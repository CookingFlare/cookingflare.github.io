Zesty and Tangy Lemon Garlic Shrimp Scampi Linguine: A Seafood Lover's Delight


If you’re a seafood enthusiast, there’s nothing quite like the delightful combination of zesty lemon, pungent garlic, succulent shrimp, and al dente linguine. This recipe for Lemon Garlic Shrimp Scampi Linguine is a symphony of flavors that will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of the Mediterranean. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a restaurant-quality meal at home, this dish is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of linguine
  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • Zest and juice of 2 lemons
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions

  1. Begin by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the linguine and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the cooking water, and set aside.

  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes, and sauté for about a minute until the garlic is fragrant but not browned.

  3. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the shrimp to the skillet. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp, as they can become rubbery.

  4. Once the shrimp are cooked, remove them from the skillet and set them aside. Deglaze the skillet with the white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.

  5. Add the lemon zest and juice to the skillet, and let the mixture simmer for a couple of minutes to reduce slightly.

  6. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet, along with the cooked linguine and chopped parsley. Toss everything together, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if the mixture seems too dry.

  7. Season the dish with salt and black pepper to taste, and give it a final toss to ensure all the ingredients are well combined.

  8. Serve the Lemon Garlic Shrimp Scampi Linguine in individual pasta bowls, garnished with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and an extra drizzle of olive oil if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a lighter version of this dish, you can use zucchini noodles or spaghetti squash in place of the linguine.
  • If you prefer a bit of heat, increase the amount of red pepper flakes or add a dash of hot sauce to the shrimp mixture.
  • This recipe can easily be adapted to include other seafood such as scallops or crab meat for a luxurious twist.

Conclusion

Lemon Garlic Shrimp Scampi Linguine is a versatile dish that can be served as a main course or as a sophisticated starter for a multi-course meal. Its vibrant flavors and elegant presentation make it a standout option for any occasion. So, next time you’re in the mood for a seafood extravaganza, give this recipe a try and let the tangy, zesty flavors of the Mediterranean elevate your dining experience. Cheers to good food and great company!
