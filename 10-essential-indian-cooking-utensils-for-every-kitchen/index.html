10 Essential Indian Cooking Utensils for Every Kitchen


The art of cooking Indian cuisine requires a wide variety of cooking utensils to bring together flavors, textures, and aromas of diverse dishes. Having the right utensils in your kitchen will make cooking a smooth and enjoyable experience, ensuring that your culinary creations are the perfect blend of tradition and innovation. In this article, we will focus on the top 10 essential Indian cooking utensils that you must have in your kitchen.

  1. Carafes and Vessel Set For cooking and serving Indian recipes, the right carafes and vessel set is essential. A carafe set typically includes a kettle, teapot, and a stand for fermenting vegetables or garlic. A stainless steel or copper vessel set is ideal for cooking curries, allowing for the even heat distribution that gives a rich flavor to the dish. Purchase a set that contains vessels of different sizes to accommodate a variety of cooking needs.
  2. Well-seasoned Cast Iron Skillets Well-seasoned cast-iron skillets are favorites in many Indian kitchens. Skillets are perfect for searing meats, cooking pancakes and chapatis, or even as a serving bowl. The depth of cast iron provides heat retention, allowing for precise control over the cooking process. To achieve this well-seasoned effect, apply oil and dry each side of the skillet multiple times.
  3. Tawa and Griddle The non-stick tawa and cast-iron griddle is a staple in the Indian kitchen. This cooking vessel can be used for cooking rotis, making dosas, and cooking parathas. Each item requires a delicate touch while being cooked, so it is essential to have a cooking utensil that does not scratch these items easily. Invest in a cast-iron or non-stick tawa to ensure seamless cooking.
  4. Mixing Utensils and Measuring Bucket Proper preparation of curries, chutneys, and sauces begins with appropriate measuring. A set of measuring spoons will serve you well in creating precise quantities of herbs, spices, and masalas. You'll also want a mixing vessel, such as a mixing bowl or a stand-alone expert tool, in order to effectively combine wet and dry ingredients.

Citrus fruits and their juice instantly boost fragrant and distinct flavors to fit your personality.

Essential Indian Cooking Utensils for Every Kitchen The art of cooking Indian cuisine requires a wide variety of cooking utensils to bring together flavors, textures, and aromas of diverse dishes. Having the right utensils in your kitchen will make cooking a smooth and enjoyable experience, ensuring that your culinary creations are the perfect blend of tradition and innovation. In this article, we will focus on the top 10 essential Indian cooking utensils that you must have in your kitchen.

  1. Carafes and Vessel Set For cooking and serving Indian recipes, the right carafes and vessel set is essential. A carafe set typically includes a kettle, teapot, and a stand for fermenting vegetables or garlic. A stainless steel or copper vessel set is ideal for cooking curries, allowing for the even heat distribution that gives a rich flavor to the dish. Purchase a set that contains vessels of different sizes to accommodate a variety of cooking needs.
  2. Well-Seasoned Cast Iron Skillets Well-seasoned cast-iron skillets are favorites in many Indian kitchens. Skillets are perfect for searing meats, cooking pancakes and chapatis, or even as a serving bowl. The depth of cast iron provides heat retention, allowing for precise control over the cooking process. To achieve this well-seasoned effect, apply oil and dry each side of the skillet multiple times.
  3. Tawa and Griddle The non-stick tawa and cast-iron griddle is a staple in the Indian kitchen. This cooking vessel can be used for cooking rotis, making dosas, and cooking parathas. Each item requires a delicate touch while being cooked, so it is essential to have a cooking utensil that does not scratch these items easily. Invest in a cast-iron or non-stick tawa to ensure seamless cooking.
  4. Mixing Utensils and Measuring Bucket Proper preparation of curries, chutneys, and sauces begins with appropriate measuring. A set of measuring spoons will serve you well in creating precise quantities of herbs, spices, and masalas. You'll also want a mixing vessel, such as a mixing bowl or a stand-alone expert tool, to effectively combine wet and dry ingredients.
  5. Lemon and Citrus Juicer Squeezing fresh lime or lemon juice instantly boosts fragrant and distinct flavors to your dish. A suitable manually squeezed/crafted citrus juicer is a convenient addition to any Indian kitchen.
  6. Pastry Rolling Pin For making samosas and other Indian pastries, a suitable rolling pin is a must-have in your kitchen. It helps you to roll dough based items evenly.
  7. Vegetable Chopper/Cutcelerator Chopping onions, tomatoes, and other vegetables is an essential part of Indian cooking. A vegetable chopper with multiple blades is a versatile tool to have in your kitchen.
  8. Garlic Knives Indian cuisine often requires chopping garlic at the last minute. A set of sharp garlic knives will make chopping garlic a breeze, and you can ensure that the flavor of the garlic is released while cooking.
  9. Tongs and Ladles Tongs are perfect for flipping items in a pan, and ladles are used to serve soup, dal, or curries. Having the right tongs and ladles will make serving a more enjoyable and convenient experience.
  10. Spice Grinders and Extractors Indian cuisine often requires pre-ground spices or unique spice blends. Use a spice grinder or spice extractor to ensure the right consistency and flavor of the spices in your recipes. By incorporating these essential Indian cooking utensils in your kitchen, you will be well-equipped to create a variety of traditional Indian dishes and experiment with new recipes.
