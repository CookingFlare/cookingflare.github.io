Sous Vide Swordfish: Perfectly Cooked Fish Every Time


Are you tired of overcooking or undercooking your swordfish? Look no further! Sous vide cooking is the secret to achieving perfectly cooked swordfish every time. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the step-by-step process of sous vide swordfish, ensuring a tender, juicy, and flavorful result that will impress your guests and elevate your culinary skills.

Ingredients

  • 2 swordfish steaks (about 1 inch thick)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your water bath or sous vide machine to 130°F (54°C).

  2. Season the swordfish steaks generously with salt and pepper on both sides.

  3. In a skillet over medium-high heat, heat the olive oil. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, until fragrant.

  4. Remove the skillet from heat and stir in the fresh lemon juice and chopped parsley. Set aside.

  5. Place each seasoned swordfish steak in a separate vacuum-sealed bag or a ziplock bag, ensuring they are flat and not overlapping.

  6. Gently lower the bags into the preheated water bath, making sure they are fully submerged. If using ziplock bags, use the water displacement method to remove the air before sealing.

  7. Cook the swordfish in the water bath for 45 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the thickness of the steaks. This cooking time will ensure the fish is cooked to a perfect medium-rare doneness.

  8. Once the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the swordfish steaks from the bags and pat them dry with paper towels.

  9. Heat a grill or skillet over high heat. Brush the steaks with the reserved garlic-lemon mixture.

  10. Sear the swordfish steaks for about 1-2 minutes on each side, until they develop a golden crust.

  11. Remove the swordfish from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

  12. Serve the sous vide swordfish with lemon wedges on the side for an extra burst of freshness.

Tips and Tricks

  • If you prefer your swordfish cooked to a different level of doneness, adjust the cooking time accordingly. For a medium doneness, cook for 1 hour and 15 minutes, and for a well-done result, cook for 1 hour and 30 minutes.

  • Sous vide cooking allows for precise temperature control, resulting in a consistently cooked fish. This method ensures the swordfish retains its moisture and natural flavors.

  • Experiment with different seasonings and marinades to customize the flavor profile of your swordfish. Consider adding herbs like thyme or rosemary, or incorporating spices like paprika or cayenne pepper.

  • When searing the swordfish, make sure the grill or skillet is extremely hot. This will help create a flavorful crust while maintaining the desired doneness of the fish.

  • Serve the sous vide swordfish with a side of roasted vegetables, a fresh salad, or even a citrusy salsa for a complete and delicious meal.

Now that you’ve mastered the art of sous vide swordfish, you can enjoy perfectly cooked fish every time. Impress your friends and family with this foolproof technique that guarantees a tender and flavorful dining experience. Happy cooking!
