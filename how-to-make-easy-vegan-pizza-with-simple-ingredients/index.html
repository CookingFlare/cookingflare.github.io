How to Make Easy Vegan Pizza with Simple Ingredients


If you’re craving a delicious pizza that’s not only easy to make but also plant-based and satisfying, then you’ve come to the right place! Vegan pizza is a fantastic option for anyone looking to enjoy the rich flavors of pizza while keeping things cruelty-free, healthy, and free from animal products. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the entire process of making a mouthwatering vegan pizza, from the crust to the toppings, using simple ingredients you likely already have in your kitchen.

Whether you’re vegan, lactose-intolerant, or just looking to try something new, this vegan pizza recipe is a perfect choice. It’s easy to customize with your favorite toppings, and it’s guaranteed to impress even non-vegan pizza lovers.

Ingredients for the Pizza Crust

For the dough:

  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast (one packet)
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp garlic powder (optional)

For the tomato sauce:

  • 1 cup canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional, helps to balance acidity)

Toppings:

  • 1 cup vegan mozzarella cheese (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup fresh vegetables, such as bell peppers, mushrooms, onions, spinach, and zucchini
  • 1 tbsp fresh basil leaves, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional, for a spicy kick)

Equipment You’ll Need:

  • Mixing bowl
  • Rolling pin (or hands, if you prefer)
  • Pizza stone or baking sheet
  • Small saucepan
  • Oven

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Pizza

Step 1: Prepare the Pizza Dough

The first step in making your vegan pizza is to prepare the dough. This dough is simple, quick, and requires only a few basic ingredients.

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm water (around 110°F or 43°C) and sugar. Stir to dissolve the sugar, then sprinkle the active dry yeast on top. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy. This step is crucial as it ensures the yeast is active and ready to make your dough rise.

  2. Mix the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, olive oil, salt, and garlic powder (if using). Once the yeast mixture is frothy, pour it into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Stir until a dough forms.

  3. Knead the dough: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour; if it’s too dry, add a small amount of water.

  4. Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size. You can also let it rise for a longer period if you prefer a more flavorful dough.

Step 2: Make the Tomato Sauce

While the dough is rising, you can prepare the tomato sauce for your vegan pizza. This simple homemade sauce adds a fresh and tangy flavor that pairs perfectly with your pizza toppings.

  1. Cook the garlic: In a small saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, or until it becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn it!

  2. Add the tomatoes and seasonings: Pour in the canned tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes. Stir in the oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and sugar (if using). Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust the seasonings as needed. You can add more salt or a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity of the tomatoes.

  3. Blend (optional): If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can blend the mixture with an immersion blender or transfer it to a blender. This step is optional and depends on your personal texture preference.

Once the sauce is ready, set it aside to cool slightly before using it on your pizza.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

Now that your dough has risen and your sauce is prepared, it’s time to shape your pizza crust. You can either use a rolling pin or your hands to stretch the dough.

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you’re using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats to ensure that it gets hot and helps create a crispy crust.

  2. Shape the dough: Punch down the dough to release the air, then transfer it to a floured surface. Use a rolling pin to roll it out into a round shape, or simply stretch it with your hands for a more rustic look. The dough should be about 12 inches in diameter for a standard pizza, but feel free to adjust the size based on your preferences.

  3. Transfer to baking sheet or stone: If you’re using a baking sheet, place a piece of parchment paper on it and transfer the rolled-out dough onto the sheet. If you’re using a pizza stone, carefully transfer the dough onto the preheated stone.

Step 4: Add the Sauce and Toppings

Now comes the fun part—creating your vegan pizza masterpiece! Start by spreading a generous amount of the homemade tomato sauce evenly over the pizza dough. Leave about an inch of space around the edges for the crust.

  1. Spread the sauce: Use a spoon or spatula to spread the tomato sauce in an even layer, covering the dough completely.

  2. Add the cheese: Sprinkle the vegan mozzarella cheese over the sauce. You can use store-bought vegan cheese or make your own from cashews or other plant-based ingredients. Make sure to cover the sauce evenly with cheese, but feel free to use as much or as little as you like.

  3. Top with vegetables: Add your favorite veggies to the pizza. Popular choices include bell peppers, mushrooms, onions, spinach, zucchini, and olives. You can use a variety of fresh or roasted vegetables—anything you like! Be sure not to overload the pizza, as this can affect the cooking time and result in a soggy crust.

  4. Season: For extra flavor, sprinkle a pinch of salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if you like spice) over the top. You can also add a drizzle of olive oil or a few fresh basil leaves for a touch of freshness.

Step 5: Bake the Pizza

Once your pizza is topped to perfection, it’s time to bake it.

  1. Bake the pizza: Place the pizza in the preheated oven. If you’re using a pizza stone, it should only take about 10-12 minutes to cook. If you’re using a baking sheet, it might take a bit longer—around 12-15 minutes.

  2. Check for doneness: The pizza is ready when the crust is golden and crispy, and the cheese is melted and bubbling. If you like your crust extra crispy, you can let it bake for a few more minutes. Keep an eye on it to avoid burning the toppings.

  3. Cool slightly: Once the pizza is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. This will help the cheese set and make it easier to slice.

Step 6: Slice and Serve

After the pizza has cooled slightly, it’s time to slice it up and enjoy! You can use a pizza cutter or a sharp knife to slice your pizza into wedges.

For an extra touch, you can sprinkle some fresh basil on top just before serving. This will add a burst of flavor and a pop of color to your pizza.

Tips for Customizing Your Vegan Pizza

One of the best things about making your own vegan pizza is how customizable it is. Here are some ideas to take your pizza to the next level:

  1. Cheese options: You can use different types of vegan cheese, such as cashew-based cheese, coconut-based cheese, or almond cheese. You can also make your own cheese at home using simple ingredients like nuts and nutritional yeast.

  2. Add protein: If you want to make your vegan pizza more filling, you can add plant-based protein sources such as chickpeas, tempeh, tofu, or vegan sausage.

  3. Spices and herbs: Experiment with different spices and herbs to change up the flavor. Try adding thyme, rosemary, or even a drizzle of balsamic vinegar for a tangy touch.

  4. Gluten-free crust: If you’re gluten-free, you can easily swap the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend. You can also use cauliflower crust or almond flour for a low-carb version.

  5. Sweet and savory: Try adding some fruit to your pizza, such as pineapple, figs, or even apples. The combination of sweet and savory can be surprisingly delicious.

Conclusion

Making vegan pizza at home is a fun and rewarding experience that allows you to enjoy a delicious meal while sticking to a plant-based diet. With this easy recipe, you can create a flavorful pizza from scratch with simple ingredients that you can customize to your taste. Whether you’re feeding a crowd or enjoying a quiet night in, vegan pizza is the perfect dish to satisfy your cravings.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat the oven, and get ready to enjoy a vegan pizza that will surely become your new favorite!
