Vegan Coconut Macaroons: Sweet and Chewy


If you’re craving a simple, delicious, and naturally vegan treat, look no further than vegan coconut macaroons. These sweet, chewy delights offer the perfect balance of crispy edges and soft, coconut-packed centers. Whether you’re new to vegan baking or an experienced pro, these macaroons are incredibly easy to make and a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Coconut macaroons are a classic treat that can be made in many variations. This vegan version replaces traditional ingredients like egg whites and dairy with plant-based alternatives, making them suitable for those following a vegan lifestyle or anyone with dietary restrictions. Not only are these macaroons rich in coconut flavor, but they’re also gluten-free, dairy-free, and egg-free, making them an inclusive dessert that almost everyone can enjoy.

What Makes Vegan Coconut Macaroons Special?

One of the standout features of these macaroons is the unique texture. While the outside becomes golden and crisp, the inside remains delightfully chewy and moist. The coconut flavor shines through with every bite, making each macaroon a sweet, aromatic treat. These macaroons can also be customized with chocolate, nuts, or spices to make them even more decadent and fun.

What sets these macaroons apart from other cookies and desserts is their versatility. They’re perfect for almost any occasion—whether you’re preparing them for a family gathering, a potluck, or simply enjoying them with a cup of tea. Plus, they require minimal ingredients, making them both convenient and affordable.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create these delicious vegan coconut macaroons, gather the following ingredients:

  • Shredded Coconut: The star of the show! Shredded coconut provides both the texture and the tropical flavor.
  • Aquafaba: This is the magic ingredient that replaces egg whites. Aquafaba is the liquid found in canned chickpeas and works as an excellent binder for the macaroons.
  • Maple Syrup: For natural sweetness. Maple syrup also adds a lovely depth of flavor that enhances the coconut’s natural taste.
  • Vanilla Extract: This will bring a subtle but comforting flavor that pairs perfectly with the coconut.
  • Salt: Just a pinch of salt enhances all the flavors in the recipe.
  • Almond Meal (Optional): A small amount of almond meal helps give the macaroons a slightly denser texture. This is an optional addition, but it adds a nice richness.
  • Coconut Oil: Coconut oil is used to add moisture to the mixture and a slight coconut flavor, ensuring the macaroons hold together while baking.

These ingredients come together in the simplest way to create the chewy, golden treats we all crave.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat to prevent the macaroons from sticking.

Step 2: Prepare the Aquafaba

To replace egg whites in the traditional recipe, we’ll use aquafaba. Drain a can of chickpeas and save the liquid. You’ll need about 3 tablespoons of aquafaba for this recipe, which will provide the right amount of binding and structure for the macaroons. If you prefer, you can also use the aquafaba from cooking your own chickpeas. Use a hand mixer or whisk to whip the aquafaba until it forms soft peaks. This step may take a few minutes, so be patient.

Step 3: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In a large bowl, combine the whipped aquafaba, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and coconut oil. Stir the mixture until everything is well combined. You should have a smooth, slightly runny base that will soon be transformed into macaroons.

Step 4: Add the Dry Ingredients

Now, fold in the shredded coconut, salt, and almond meal (if using). The shredded coconut is the base of your macaroons, so make sure it’s evenly distributed throughout the mixture. Stir until the coconut is completely coated in the wet ingredients. The batter should be slightly sticky but not too wet—if it feels too runny, add a little extra shredded coconut to achieve the right consistency.

Step 5: Form the Macaroons

Using a spoon or a cookie scoop, scoop out spoonfuls of the coconut mixture and form them into small mounds on the prepared baking sheet. Space them about 1 inch apart to ensure they don’t stick together while baking. Press each mound down lightly with the back of the spoon to compact it slightly.

If you want a more refined look, you can shape the macaroons into a rounded pyramid shape or use your hands to form them. But if you’re after simplicity, the traditional mound shape works just as well!

Step 6: Bake the Macaroons

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the macaroons for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. Keep an eye on them, as oven temperatures can vary, and you want to make sure they don’t overcook.

Once baked, remove the macaroons from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for about 5 minutes. This will help them firm up a little bit before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 7: Optional: Drizzle with Chocolate

For an extra special touch, drizzle melted vegan chocolate over the cooled macaroons. Simply melt some dark chocolate in the microwave or over a double boiler, then use a spoon or piping bag to drizzle it over each macaroon. Allow the chocolate to set before serving.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Coconut Macaroons

  1. Use Unsweetened Shredded Coconut: To avoid overwhelming sweetness, always opt for unsweetened shredded coconut. This allows the natural flavor of the coconut to shine through without being cloyingly sweet.

  2. Whip the Aquafaba Well: For the best texture, be sure to whip the aquafaba until it forms soft peaks. This will give the macaroons a light, airy texture in the center while keeping the edges crisp.

  3. Let Them Cool Completely: Macaroons can be fragile while hot. Allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes, and then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before handling.

  4. Customize Your Macaroons: Don’t be afraid to get creative! You can add dried fruits like cranberries or apricots for added sweetness and texture, or sprinkle in some cinnamon or nutmeg for a warm, spicy twist.

  5. Make Mini Macaroons: If you prefer smaller portions, you can easily make mini macaroons by using a smaller cookie scoop. Just adjust the baking time slightly and keep an eye on them to avoid overbaking.

Serving Suggestions

Vegan coconut macaroons are perfect for a variety of occasions. Serve them at your next party, potluck, or family gathering. You can even pack them up as a thoughtful homemade gift for friends or loved ones. Pair them with a cup of coconut milk, almond milk, or your favorite tea to create a delightful afternoon snack.

These macaroons also store well in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week, so you can enjoy them for several days. If you’d like to extend their shelf life, they can be frozen for up to three months. Just make sure they are completely cooled before freezing.

Why You’ll Love These Vegan Coconut Macaroons

Not only are these coconut macaroons irresistibly delicious, but they also come with several health benefits. They’re naturally high in fiber and healthy fats, thanks to the coconut. With a few simple swaps, these macaroons can easily become your go-to dessert for any occasion. The fact that they’re vegan, gluten-free, and refined sugar-free means that they’re a healthier treat for both you and your guests.

Whether you’re a vegan, someone with food allergies, or simply a lover of all things coconut, these macaroons are sure to hit the spot. Their chewy texture and sweet coconut flavor are bound to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire.

So, next time you’re in the mood for a sweet, tropical treat, whip up these vegan coconut macaroons. They’re simple, satisfying, and a fun way to bring a little sunshine into your day!
