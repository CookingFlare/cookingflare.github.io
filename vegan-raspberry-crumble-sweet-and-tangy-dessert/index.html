Vegan Raspberry Crumble: Sweet and Tangy Dessert


If you’re craving a dessert that’s both refreshing and indulgent, a Vegan Raspberry Crumble is the perfect choice. This sweet and tangy treat is not only incredibly delicious but also easy to make. Packed with vibrant raspberries and topped with a crispy, golden crumble, it is the ideal dessert to enjoy after a hearty meal or as a delightful snack.

This recipe takes the classic crumble dessert and gives it a plant-based twist, making it suitable for vegans and anyone who enjoys a healthier alternative without compromising on taste. Let’s dive into how you can make this mouthwatering Vegan Raspberry Crumble that will surely become a favorite in your dessert repertoire.

The Perfect Balance of Sweet and Tangy

The star of this dessert is the raspberries, which bring a beautiful burst of tartness to contrast the sweetness of the crumble topping. Raspberries are not only delicious but also packed with vitamins, antioxidants, and fiber, making them a nutritious addition to any dessert.

The crumble topping is where the magic happens. It combines oats, flour, and plant-based butter to create a crunchy, golden layer that pairs perfectly with the soft, juicy raspberries. A touch of brown sugar and cinnamon adds extra warmth and sweetness to the dish, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.

Ingredients for Vegan Raspberry Crumble

Before you get started, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need for this Vegan Raspberry Crumble:

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • Fresh or frozen raspberries – 3 cups (If using frozen raspberries, make sure they’re thawed and drained of excess liquid)
  • Maple syrup or agave nectar – 2 tablespoons (for natural sweetness)
  • Lemon juice – 1 tablespoon (adds brightness to the raspberries)
  • Cornstarch or arrowroot powder – 1 tablespoon (helps to thicken the raspberry filling)
  • Vanilla extract – 1 teaspoon (for added depth of flavor)

For the Crumble Topping:

  • Old-fashioned rolled oats – 1 1/2 cups (for that perfect crunchy texture)
  • All-purpose flour – 3/4 cup (you can also use whole wheat flour for a heartier crumble)
  • Brown sugar – 1/4 cup (or coconut sugar for a more natural option)
  • Cinnamon – 1/2 teaspoon (for that warm, comforting flavor)
  • Salt – a pinch (to balance out the sweetness)
  • Plant-based butter – 1/2 cup (softened or melted; coconut oil also works well)
  • Almond meal (optional) – 2 tablespoons (for added texture and flavor)

How to Make Vegan Raspberry Crumble

Step 1: Prepare the Raspberry Filling

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) so it’s ready to go once the crumble is assembled.

Next, prepare the raspberry filling. In a medium bowl, combine the raspberries with the maple syrup, lemon juice, cornstarch, and vanilla extract. Stir everything gently until the raspberries are well-coated. The cornstarch will help thicken the juices as the filling cooks, creating a deliciously gooey and sweet layer.

Once your raspberries are mixed, transfer the filling into a greased 9-inch round or square baking dish. Make sure the raspberries are spread evenly at the bottom of the dish. Set it aside while you work on the crumble topping.

Step 2: Make the Crumble Topping

In a separate bowl, combine the rolled oats, flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Give the dry ingredients a quick stir to mix them together. Then, add the softened plant-based butter to the bowl. Using your hands or a pastry cutter, cut the butter into the dry mixture until it forms a crumbly texture. The goal is to have small, pea-sized bits of butter scattered throughout the oats and flour.

If you prefer an extra layer of flavor and texture, you can mix in the almond meal at this stage. It adds a nutty richness to the crumble topping, but it’s completely optional.

Once the crumble mixture is ready, sprinkle it evenly over the raspberry filling, making sure it covers the fruit completely. You want the crumble topping to form a nice, even layer that will bake up golden and crispy.

Step 3: Bake the Crumble

Place the assembled crumble in the preheated oven and bake for about 35-40 minutes. Keep an eye on it after the 30-minute mark. The crumble topping should be golden brown and crispy, and the raspberry filling should be bubbling up around the edges.

If you like your crumble extra crispy, you can broil it for an additional 2-3 minutes at the end of baking, but be sure to watch it carefully so it doesn’t burn.

Step 4: Let it Cool and Serve

Once the Vegan Raspberry Crumble is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool for about 10 minutes before serving. The filling will be hot, and giving it some time to set will make it easier to scoop and serve.

This dessert is perfect when served warm, but it can also be enjoyed at room temperature. To make it extra special, you can serve it with a scoop of vegan vanilla ice cream, coconut whipped cream, or a dollop of non-dairy yogurt.

Variations and Tips for Your Raspberry Crumble

While this recipe is already delicious as is, there are plenty of ways to customize your Vegan Raspberry Crumble to suit your tastes or what you have available in your pantry. Here are some ideas to try:

Add Different Fruits

Raspberries are the star of this crumble, but you can easily swap them out for other berries or fruits. Blackberries, blueberries, strawberries, and even cherries make wonderful alternatives. You can also mix and match to create a berry medley crumble.

Make it Gluten-Free

If you need a gluten-free version of this dessert, simply swap out the all-purpose flour for a gluten-free flour blend. Make sure to check that your oats are certified gluten-free as well.

Spice it Up

While cinnamon is the classic spice for a crumble topping, you can experiment with other warming spices like nutmeg, ginger, or cardamom. Adding a little ground ginger will give the crumble a delightful warmth that pairs wonderfully with the tangy raspberries.

Sweeten to Your Taste

The maple syrup in the raspberry filling adds just the right amount of sweetness, but you can adjust it based on your preferences. If you prefer a sweeter filling, feel free to add more maple syrup, agave nectar, or even a little coconut sugar. Just be sure to taste as you go so you don’t overpower the natural tartness of the raspberries.

Add Nuts or Seeds

For extra texture and flavor, consider mixing in some chopped nuts or seeds into the crumble topping. Walnuts, almonds, or even pumpkin seeds would add a delightful crunch and a dose of healthy fats.

Make Individual Servings

If you want to serve this dessert in individual portions, simply divide the filling and crumble topping into small ramekins or baking dishes. This makes for a more elegant presentation and allows each guest to have their own personal crumble.

Health Benefits of Vegan Raspberry Crumble

Not only is this Vegan Raspberry Crumble a delicious dessert, but it also offers several health benefits thanks to the use of wholesome ingredients.

  • Raspberries are an excellent source of vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants, which help support immune health and reduce inflammation.
  • Oats provide a great source of fiber, which is essential for digestive health. They also help keep you feeling full longer, making this a more satisfying dessert.
  • Plant-based butter is a healthier alternative to dairy-based butter, and using it in this crumble provides healthy fats without any cholesterol.
  • Maple syrup is a natural sweetener that contains essential minerals like zinc and manganese, which are beneficial for overall health.

Of course, this crumble is meant to be enjoyed as an occasional treat, but by using whole plant-based ingredients, it’s a much healthier choice than many traditional desserts.

Final Thoughts on Vegan Raspberry Crumble

This Vegan Raspberry Crumble is a delightful dessert that’s easy to prepare and perfect for any occasion. The combination of tart raspberries and a sweet, crumbly topping makes it a crowd-pleaser, and it’s versatile enough to be adapted to your taste preferences. Whether you enjoy it warm with a scoop of vegan ice cream or as a simple, satisfying treat, it’s sure to become a favorite in your dessert collection.

By swapping out traditional butter and dairy ingredients for plant-based options, this recipe offers a delicious and healthier alternative for those following a vegan lifestyle or anyone looking to reduce their intake of animal products. So, grab some fresh raspberries (or frozen if that’s what you have), gather your ingredients, and get baking! Your taste buds will thank you.
