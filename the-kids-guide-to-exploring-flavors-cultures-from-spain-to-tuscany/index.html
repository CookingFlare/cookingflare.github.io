The Kids' Guide to Exploring Flavors & Cultures: from Spain to Tuscany


Are you eager to introduce your little ones to the magic of different cuisines and cultures from around the world? Cooking together is an enjoyable and interactive way for parents and kids to explore new tastes, textures, and traditions. Get ready to embark on a culinary journey, as we take you from the sun-drenched streets of Spanish towns, to the rolling hills of Tuscany.

When exploring flavors and cultures with kids, it’s essential to begin with simple recipes that demonstrate the core ingredients, flavor profiles, and elements specific to each region. Here’s an exciting adventure in the world of flavor profiles, presented via three engaging recipes. Let’s start the journey with a culinary escapade to Spain!

1. Spanish Tortilla

The first dish we’ll explore is the Spanish Tortilla, a beloved classic consisting of eggs and potatoes that can be enjoyed any time of day. This recipe is perfect for introducing children to the world of Latin cooking, and it’s sure to be a crowd-pleaser!

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium-sized potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Fry the sliced potatoes until soft and slightly browned. Season with a pinch of salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs together with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Add the cooked potatoes to the bowl with the beaten eggs, and mix gently.
  5. Clean the frying pan and heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Pour the egg and potato mixture into the pan.
  6. Let the tortilla cook undisturbed for 5-7 minutes or until the bottom is set and nicely golden.
  7. Slide the tortilla onto a plate, slide it back into the pan, uncooked side down, and cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  8. Carefully slide the tortilla back onto the plate, and serve hot or at room temperature.

2. Pasta alla Carbonara

Next, let’s transport ourselves to the charming countryside of Tuscany, where we’ll cook up a savory Pasta alla Carbonara. This hearty dish is sure to have your little ones delighting in its creamy, cheesy goodness!

Ingredients:

  • 400 grams of spaghetti
  • 150 grams of pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 5 large eggs
  • 100 grams of pecorino cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook until al dente, according to package instructions.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat the pancetta or bacon in a pan over medium heat until crispy. Set aside.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and pecorino cheese together. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. When the pasta is done, drain it, reserving 1 cup of cooking water.
  5. Transfer the pasta back to the pot, and add the crispy pancetta.
  6. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, and toss quickly so that the hot pasta cooks the eggs gently.
  7. Add the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, until reaching the desired consistency. Serve immediately, garnished with extra pecorino cheese.

3. Empanadillas (Mini Spanish Hand Pies)

Round off your worldly culinary tour with everyone’s favorite handheld treat - the Empanadilla. These mini savory pies are filled with deliciousness, folding into a perfect parcel of pastry goodness. An inviting appetizer or satisfying snack, these empanadillas are sure to become a hit!

Ingredients:

For the pastry:

  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/3 cup of cold water
  • 1/2 cup of cold vegetable shortening

For the filling:

  • 2 cups of cooked chicken, finely chopped
  • 1 cup of canned corn kernels, drained
  • 1/2 cup of canned black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 small bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. For the pastry, mix the flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add the cold water, and knead until the dough comes together. Wrap it in plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for 30 minutes.
  2. While the dough rests, prepare the filling. In a pan, cook the onion and bell pepper in oil over medium heat until softened. Add the cooked chicken, corn, black beans, oregano, salt, and pepper, and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  4. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut out 4-inch circles. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of each circle, and bring the edges together, pinching to seal. Brush the empanadillas with the beaten egg.
  5. Place the empanadillas on the prepared baking sheet, and bake for 20-25 minutes, until golden brown. Serve warm with a side of Chimichurri sauce for dipping.

As you embark on these gastronomic adventures with your children, try experimenting with different recipes and flavors from various regions worldwide. Engaging with world cuisines fosters a greater understanding and appreciation of new cultures, deliciously increasing kids’ lifetime food horizons. Remember, the most essential ingredient in these culinary adventures is the joy shared in creating new memories together – and making every meal an adventure to look forward to!
