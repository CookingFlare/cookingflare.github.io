Steaming Ahead: Steamy Seafood Recipes that'll Whet Your Appetite


When it comes to seafood, there’s nothing quite like tender, juicy bites of fish, shrimp, and other delicacies from the depths of the ocean. While there are several cooking methods, such as pan-searing, grilling, or baking, steaming remains one of the healthiest and easiest ways to prepare your favorite seafood dishes. This method not only locks in the natural flavors of the ingredients but also keeps them moist and succulent. So, grab your bamboo steamer, heatproof bowl, and saucepan, and let’s dive into some steamy seafood recipes that’ll make your taste buds rejoice!

  1. Steamed Garlic and Ginger Miso Glazed Salmon

Ingredients

  • 4 (6 oz) salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Green onions, sliced, for garnish
  • Cooked rice or vegetables, to serve

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together miso paste, minced ginger, minced garlic, mirin, sake, soy sauce, and sesame oil.

  2. Arrange the salmon fillets in a single layer in a heatproof bowl that’s large enough to fit over your steamer.

  3. Pour the miso mixture over the salmon, ensuring that each piece is well coated.

  4. Set up your steamer – line the steamer tray with parchment paper if needed – and place it over a saucepan filled with 1-2 inches of water. Bring the water to a simmer.

  5. Place the bowl with the glazed salmon in the steamer, cover, and steam for 8-10 minutes, or until the salmon is just cooked through.

  6. Use caution when removing the hot bowl and serve the salmon with some extra sauce from the bowl. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve with rice or vegetables on the side.

  7. Steamed Lemon Herb Shrimp

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooked rice or vegetables, to serve

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, minced garlic, chopped parsley, dill, and thyme. Add the shrimp and toss to coat evenly with the marinade.

  2. Prepare your steamer – line the steamer tray with parchment paper if needed – and place it over a saucepan filled with 1-2 inches of water. Bring the water to a simmer.

  3. Place the shrimp in the steamer, cover, and steam for 5-7 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.

  4. While the shrimp steam, whisk together the lemon zest and juice, salt, and pepper in a separate bowl.

  5. Drizzle the lemon mixture over the cooked shrimp and toss to coat. Serve with rice or vegetables on the side.

  6. Steamed Miso Glazed Cod

Ingredients

  • 4 (6 oz) cod fillets
  • 2 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Cooked rice or vegetables, to serve

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together miso paste, minced ginger, mirin, sake, soy sauce, and minced garlic.
  2. Arrange the cod fillets in a single layer in a heatproof bowl that’s large enough to fit over your steamer.
  3. Pour the miso mixture over the cod, ensuring that each piece is well coated.
  4. Set up your steamer – line the steamer tray with parchment paper if needed – and place it over a saucepan filled with 1-2 inches of water. Bring the water to a simmer.
  5. Place the bowl with the glazed cod in the steamer, cover, and steam for 6-8 minutes, or until the cod is just cooked through.
  6. Use caution when removing the hot bowl and serve the cod with some extra sauce from the bowl. Serve with rice or vegetables on the side.

Conclusion

Steaming is a fantastic method for preparing seafood dishes that retain their natural flavors while remaining tender and moist. Not only is this technique healthy, but it’s also quite simple to execute. In no time, you can have a delicious, restaurant-quality seafood meal on your table, impressing your family and friends while enjoying the delicacy of the ocean’s finest offerings. Get your steamer ready and explore exciting new recipes today!
