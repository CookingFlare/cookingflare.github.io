How to Make Thanksgiving Baked Macaroni and Cheese


As Thanksgiving approaches, the anticipation of a feast full of rich, flavorful dishes fills the air. Amid the turkey, cranberry sauce, and stuffing, one dish stands out as a crowd favorite: macaroni and cheese. Whether you’re serving it as a side or enjoying it as a comforting main course, baked macaroni and cheese is a timeless dish that brings warmth and joy to the table.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of preparing a mouth-watering baked macaroni and cheese that will leave your guests asking for seconds. From creamy cheese sauce to golden, bubbly perfection, this recipe is sure to become a Thanksgiving tradition in your household.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, it’s important to gather the right ingredients. The key to making a delicious baked macaroni and cheese lies in the perfect combination of cheeses, pasta, and seasoning. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Mac and Cheese:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni (or any pasta shape of your choice)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese (or another mild, melty cheese)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder (optional, but adds great depth of flavor)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional, adds a subtle warmth)

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably panko for extra crunch)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup extra shredded cheese (such as cheddar or a blend of your choice)
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika (for a hint of color and flavor)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Thanksgiving Baked Macaroni and Cheese

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by boiling the macaroni according to the package instructions. You’ll want it to be slightly undercooked, as it will continue cooking in the oven. Follow these steps:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the macaroni to the boiling water and cook for 7-8 minutes (or until al dente).
  3. Drain the pasta and set it aside in a colander.

Tip: It’s essential to salt the water, as it helps the pasta absorb flavor. Don’t overcook the pasta, as it will continue to cook in the cheese sauce during the baking process.

Step 2: Prepare the Cheese Sauce

Now, let’s move on to the creamy cheese sauce. This is where the magic happens, so don’t rush this part. Follow these steps to create a luscious, velvety sauce:

  1. In a large saucepan or Dutch oven, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat.
  2. Once the butter is fully melted, sprinkle in the flour, stirring constantly to make a smooth roux (a flour-butter paste). Cook for about 2 minutes, making sure the mixture doesn’t brown.
  3. Gradually add the milk to the roux, stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Keep stirring until the sauce thickens and reaches a smooth consistency, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Once the sauce is thickened, reduce the heat to low and add in the shredded cheddar, Gruyère, and Parmesan cheeses. Stir until the cheese is completely melted and the sauce is smooth.
  5. Add the garlic powder, mustard powder, salt, pepper, and optional nutmeg to taste. Adjust seasoning as necessary to get the perfect balance of flavors.

Tip: For extra creaminess, you can swap some of the milk for heavy cream or half-and-half.

Step 3: Combine the Pasta and Sauce

Now that your cheese sauce is ready, it’s time to bring it all together.

  1. Add the drained macaroni to the cheese sauce, stirring gently to coat each piece of pasta evenly with the creamy mixture. Be sure to mix well to ensure every bite is filled with cheesy goodness.
  2. Once combined, transfer the macaroni and cheese into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.

Tip: If you want to get a little extra indulgent, you can layer some extra cheese in the middle for even more cheesiness!

Step 4: Add the Topping

The crispy, golden topping is what sets baked macaroni and cheese apart from stovetop versions. Here’s how to achieve that perfect crunch:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs and melted butter. Stir until the breadcrumbs are evenly coated.
  2. Sprinkle the buttery breadcrumbs over the top of the macaroni and cheese.
  3. Sprinkle an additional 1/2 cup of shredded cheese over the breadcrumbs. The cheese will melt into the breadcrumbs, creating a crispy, cheesy topping.
  4. Finally, add a dash of paprika over the top for color and a hint of smokiness.

Tip: If you want your topping to be extra crispy, broil the dish for the last few minutes of baking.

Step 5: Bake the Mac and Cheese

It’s time to bake your macaroni and cheese to perfection:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbling and the topping is golden brown.
  3. If you prefer a more crispy top, switch the oven to broil during the last 2-3 minutes of baking. Watch it closely, as the broiler can burn things quickly.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Once your baked macaroni and cheese is out of the oven, let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This allows the cheese to set slightly and makes it easier to serve. Slice, scoop, and enjoy the rich, cheesy deliciousness!

Tips for Making the Best Thanksgiving Baked Macaroni and Cheese

  • Use high-quality cheese: The flavor of your macaroni and cheese depends heavily on the cheese you use. Opt for sharp cheddar for a tangy, bold flavor and Gruyère for a smooth, melt-in-your-mouth texture. Feel free to experiment with other cheeses like mozzarella, fontina, or Monterey Jack for a unique twist.
  • Make ahead: You can prepare the macaroni and cheese the day before Thanksgiving. Just assemble the dish, cover it tightly with plastic wrap or foil, and refrigerate. On the day of, bake it in the oven as directed. You may need to add a few extra minutes to the baking time if it’s coming straight from the fridge.
  • Add extra ingredients: For a flavor boost, consider adding cooked bacon, caramelized onions, or sautéed garlic. These ingredients complement the richness of the cheese and add a savory depth of flavor.
  • Balance the texture: If you prefer a creamier macaroni and cheese, increase the amount of milk or use cream instead of half-and-half. If you like a firmer texture, reduce the amount of milk slightly for a thicker sauce.

Common Variations

While the classic baked macaroni and cheese is always a hit, there are endless variations that can add a unique touch to your Thanksgiving spread. Consider trying one of these ideas to put your own spin on the dish:

  1. Spicy Mac and Cheese: Add diced jalapeños, cayenne pepper, or hot sauce to the cheese sauce for a kick of heat.
  2. Buffalo Chicken Mac and Cheese: Mix in shredded cooked chicken and toss it in buffalo sauce before baking.
  3. Vegetarian Mac and Cheese: For a veggie twist, add sautéed spinach, mushrooms, or roasted cauliflower to the dish.
  4. Truffle Mac and Cheese: Drizzle a bit of truffle oil over the top before baking for an indulgent, luxurious flavor.

Conclusion

Baked macaroni and cheese is a versatile, comforting dish that will complement your Thanksgiving meal perfectly. Whether you stick to the traditional recipe or customize it with your favorite ingredients, this dish is guaranteed to please your guests and become a staple on your holiday table. With its creamy cheese sauce, tender pasta, and crispy topping, it’s a dish that embodies the warmth and joy of Thanksgiving.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and start preparing a baked macaroni and cheese that will wow your family and friends this Thanksgiving. Enjoy the flavors, the memories, and the joy of sharing a delicious meal together!
