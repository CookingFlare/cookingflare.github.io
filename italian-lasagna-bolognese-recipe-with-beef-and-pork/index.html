Italian Lasagna Bolognese Recipe with Beef and Pork


Lasagna Bolognese is a classic Italian dish that is loved by many for its rich flavors and comforting layers. This recipe combines the goodness of beef and pork, creating a succulent and flavorful lasagna that will leave you wanting more. So, let’s dive right into it!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and pork, and cook until browned. Make sure to break up the meat into small pieces as it cooks.

  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic to the skillet. Sauté for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened.

  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, red wine (if using), beef broth, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. While the sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  6. To assemble the lasagna, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles on top, followed by another layer of meat sauce. Sprinkle some shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese over the sauce.

  7. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and a generous amount of cheese on top.

  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

  9. Once the lasagna is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes before serving. This allows the lasagna to set and makes it easier to cut into neat portions.

  10. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve hot. The lasagna can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a crisp green salad and some garlic bread.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize this recipe to your liking. You can add other vegetables like bell peppers or mushrooms for an extra burst of flavor and nutrition.

  • If you prefer a milder sauce, you can reduce or omit the red pepper flakes.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It also freezes well, so you can make a larger batch and save some for later.

  • For a vegetarian version, you can substitute the beef and pork with plant-based ground meat alternatives.

Enjoy the hearty and delicious Italian Lasagna Bolognese with Beef and Pork! This recipe is a labor of love, but the end result is well worth the effort. Impress your family and friends with this classic Italian dish that is sure to become a favorite. Buon appetito!
