Slow Cooker Thai Noodle Recipes for a Taste of Thailand


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’re in for a treat. Today, I’ll be sharing some delightful slow cooker Thai noodle recipes that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Thailand. These recipes are not only easy to prepare but also bursting with the vibrant flavors of Southeast Asia. So, grab your slow cooker and let’s embark on a culinary journey to Thailand!

Slow Cooker Pad Thai

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 1/4 cup peanuts, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the chicken broth, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Add the chicken, bell pepper, and bean sprouts to the slow cooker and stir to coat with the sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 2-3 hours, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  5. Once the chicken is cooked, stir in the cooked rice noodles and green onions.
  6. Serve the Pad Thai garnished with chopped peanuts, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges.

Slow Cooker Drunken Noodles

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces wide rice noodles
  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the oyster sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, fish sauce, garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Add the sliced flank steak and bell pepper to the slow cooker and stir to coat with the sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 3-4 hours, or until the steak is tender.
  4. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  5. Once the steak is tender, stir in the cooked rice noodles and Thai basil leaves.
  6. Serve the Drunken Noodles garnished with fresh cilantro and lime wedges.

Slow Cooker Coconut Curry Noodles

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup red curry paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the coconut milk, red curry paste, fish sauce, and brown sugar.
  2. Add the shrimp, bell pepper, and snap peas to the slow cooker and stir to coat with the curry mixture.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 1-2 hours, or until the shrimp is pink and cooked through.
  4. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  5. Once the shrimp is cooked, stir in the cooked rice noodles and fresh basil leaves.
  6. Serve the Coconut Curry Noodles with lime wedges for a refreshing burst of citrus.

Conclusion

There you have it - three tantalizing slow cooker Thai noodle recipes that will bring the exotic flavors of Thailand right to your dinner table. Whether you’re craving the classic Pad Thai, the fiery Drunken Noodles, or the creamy Coconut Curry Noodles, these recipes are sure to satisfy your Thai food cravings. So, dust off your slow cooker, stock up on the essential ingredients, and get ready to savor the taste of Thailand in the comfort of your own home. Happy cooking!
