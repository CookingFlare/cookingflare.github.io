Preserving the Fall: Recipes for Canning Pumpkins and Squash


As the crisp air of autumn settles in and the vibrant foliage paints a picturesque landscape, it’s time to embrace the bounty of the season. Pumpkins and squash are not just staples of fall decor; they’re versatile ingredients that can be preserved for enjoyment long after the leaves have fallen. Canning is an excellent way to capture the essence of fall, allowing you to savor the flavors of pumpkins and squash throughout the year. In this guide, we’ll explore various methods for canning these delicious ingredients, offering recipes that will delight your palate and fill your pantry.

Understanding the Basics of Canning

Before diving into our delicious recipes, it’s essential to understand the basics of canning. This method of preserving food involves sealing jars of food and heating them to kill bacteria, yeasts, and molds. The heat creates a vacuum seal that prevents contamination, allowing you to store your preserved goods safely.

Key Equipment Needed for Canning

To begin your canning journey, gather the following essential equipment:

  • Canning Jars: Use glass jars specifically designed for canning. They come in various sizes, but pint or quart jars work best for pumpkin and squash.
  • Lids and Bands: Ensure your lids are new to maintain a proper seal, while the bands can be reused.
  • Canning Pot: A large pot with a lid and a rack to hold the jars in boiling water.
  • Jar Lifter: A handy tool to safely remove hot jars from boiling water.
  • Funnel: A wide-mouth funnel helps in filling jars without mess.
  • Bubble Remover: A flat tool that helps eliminate air bubbles in the jars.

Safety First

Canning can be a rewarding process, but it’s crucial to follow updated safety guidelines from reputable sources like the USDA. Always work with tested recipes to ensure the safety and quality of your preserved foods.

Canning Pumpkin Puree

Pumpkin puree is a versatile ingredient that can be used in soups, pies, muffins, and more. Here’s how to can your own pumpkin puree.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 small sugar pumpkins (or any other variety suitable for cooking)
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pumpkins: Start by washing the pumpkins thoroughly. Cut them in half and scoop out the seeds and strings. You can save the seeds for roasting later!

  2. Cook the Pumpkins: There are several methods to cook the pumpkins:

    • Boiling: Place the pumpkin halves in a large pot filled with water. Boil for about 30-45 minutes or until the flesh is tender.
    • Baking: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the pumpkin halves cut side down on a baking sheet. Bake for about 45 minutes or until soft.

  3. Puree the Flesh: Once cooked, scoop out the flesh and place it in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth.

  4. Fill the Jars: Using a funnel, fill your sterilized canning jars with the pumpkin puree, leaving about 1 inch of headspace. Use a bubble remover to eliminate any air bubbles.

  5. Seal and Process: Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth to ensure a good seal. Place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands until they are fingertip-tight. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 90 minutes for pint jars and 75 minutes for half-pint jars.

  6. Cool and Store: Once processed, carefully remove the jars and allow them to cool on a clean towel. After 24 hours, check the seals. Any jars that haven’t sealed properly should be refrigerated and used within a week.

Canning Squash Relish

Squash relish is a tangy addition to sandwiches, burgers, and hot dogs. Here’s a simple recipe for canning squash relish.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups diced yellow squash
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seed
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric
  • 1 tablespoon salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and dice the squash, onion, and bell pepper.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In a large pot, combine the diced vegetables with sugar, vinegar, mustard seed, turmeric, and salt. Stir well to combine.

  3. Cook the Mixture: Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to a simmer. Let it cook for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The vegetables should become tender, and the relish should thicken slightly.

  4. Fill the Jars: Using a funnel, fill sterilized jars with the hot squash relish, leaving a ½ inch headspace. Remove air bubbles and wipe the rims of the jars.

  5. Seal and Process: Apply the lids and bands, then process the jars in a boiling water bath for 15 minutes.

  6. Cool and Store: As with the pumpkin puree, allow the jars to cool completely on a towel. Check seals after 24 hours and store in a cool, dark place.

Canning Pumpkin Pie Filling

Why not capture the flavors of pumpkin pie in a jar? This canning method allows you to enjoy the essence of fall in an easy-to-use format.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups pumpkin puree (prepared as above)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup evaporated milk (canned pumpkin pie filling is not safe for canning, so make sure to add this later when you are ready to bake)

Instructions

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the pumpkin puree, sugar, pumpkin pie spice, salt, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  2. Fill the Jars: Using a funnel, fill sterilized jars with the pumpkin mixture, leaving 1 inch of headspace.

  3. Seal and Process: Wipe the rims, apply lids and bands, and process in a boiling water bath for 90 minutes.

  4. Cool and Store: Follow the cooling and storage instructions as mentioned in previous recipes.

When you’re ready to make your pumpkin pie, simply add a cup of evaporated milk and eggs to the jar’s contents, and you’re good to go!

Canning Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash soup is a favorite fall dish, and canning it allows you to enjoy its creamy goodness even in the dead of winter.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup onion, chopped
  • 1 cup carrot, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Wash, peel, and chop the butternut squash, onion, and carrots.

  2. Cook the Vegetables: In a large pot, combine the squash, onion, carrot, vegetable broth, garlic powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 20-30 minutes.

  3. Blend the Soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, or transfer in batches to a regular blender.

  4. Fill the Jars: Fill sterilized jars with the hot soup, leaving 1 inch of headspace. Remove air bubbles and wipe the rims.

  5. Seal and Process: Apply lids and bands, then process in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 60 minutes.

  6. Cool and Store: Allow jars to cool completely, check seals, and store in a cool, dark place.

Tips for Successful Canning

  • Choose Fresh Produce: Always select ripe, fresh pumpkins and squash for the best flavor and texture.
  • Stay Organized: Have all your equipment ready before starting the canning process to streamline your efforts.
  • Label Your Jars: Always label your jars with the contents and date so you can keep track of what you have stored.

Conclusion

Canning pumpkins and squash is a fantastic way to preserve the flavors of fall for enjoyment throughout the year. With a little effort and the right recipes, you can create a pantry full of delicious, homemade goods that bring comfort and warmth to your meals. Whether you’re savoring pumpkin puree in a pie or enjoying squash relish on a burger, you’ll be reminded of those cozy autumn days. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start preserving the fall bounty today!
