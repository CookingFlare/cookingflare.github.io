Fourth of July BBQ: 10 Sizzling Side Dishes to Complement Your Grilling


The Fourth of July is a time for family gatherings, fireworks, and, of course, mouthwatering BBQs. Whether you’re hosting a casual backyard cookout or a full-on outdoor celebration, the focus often shifts to the main dishes on the grill. But what makes a BBQ truly memorable isn’t just the perfectly cooked steak, juicy ribs, or sizzling burgers — it’s the sides that elevate the entire meal. If you want your Fourth of July celebration to stand out, it’s essential to pair your grilled meats with some exceptional side dishes.

In this blog post, we’ve compiled 10 sizzling side dishes that will complement your grilling and have everyone at the BBQ asking for seconds. These dishes are perfect for celebrating Independence Day, bringing flavor, variety, and color to your outdoor feast.

1. Classic Coleslaw: A Cool and Crisp Companion

Coleslaw is a BBQ classic that never goes out of style. This crunchy, creamy side dish offers a refreshing contrast to the smoky, savory flavors of grilled meats. The balance of tangy dressing and crisp cabbage makes it a perfect complement to heavy BBQ dishes. Whether you prefer a traditional coleslaw made with mayonnaise or a tangier vinegar-based version, this dish brings cool relief to your palate, making it an essential side for your Fourth of July celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of green cabbage, shredded
  • 1 medium head of purple cabbage, shredded
  • 1 large carrot, shredded
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded green cabbage, purple cabbage, and carrot.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, and honey until smooth.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cabbage mixture and toss until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Refrigerate for at least an hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

2. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herbed Butter

Nothing says summer like corn on the cob, and grilling it adds an irresistible smokiness that will have your guests coming back for more. Topped with a dollop of herbed butter, this side dish is simple to make but packs a punch of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place the corn on the grill and cook, turning occasionally, for about 10-12 minutes, until the kernels are tender and lightly charred.
  3. While the corn is grilling, mix the softened butter with the fresh herbs, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  4. Once the corn is done, remove it from the grill and slather each ear with the herbed butter mixture.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with extra herbs if desired.

3. BBQ Baked Beans: Sweet, Smoky, and Savory

No BBQ spread is complete without a hearty helping of baked beans. This dish combines sweet, smoky, and savory flavors, perfectly balancing the richness of grilled meats. The best part? You can prepare the beans in advance and let them simmer while you focus on your grilling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) of baked beans
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 slices of bacon, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the skillet.
  2. Add the diced onion to the skillet and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. In a large pot, combine the baked beans, ketchup, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and sautéed onions. Stir to combine.
  4. Bring the beans to a simmer over low heat and cook for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Stir in the crispy bacon and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Keep warm until ready to serve.

4. Grilled Veggie Skewers: Colorful and Flavorful

Grilled veggie skewers are not only a visually appealing side dish but also a delicious one. By grilling a variety of fresh vegetables, you bring out their natural sweetness and smokiness, making them the perfect complement to BBQ meats. Plus, they’re easy to make and can be customized based on what’s in season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between the bell peppers, zucchini, onion, and cherry tomatoes.
  3. Drizzle the skewers with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, oregano, and garlic powder.
  4. Grill the veggie skewers for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  5. Serve immediately.

5. Potato Salad with Dijon Dressing

Potato salad is another quintessential BBQ side dish, and this version, with its tangy Dijon mustard dressing, brings a zesty twist to the classic. Creamy, flavorful, and hearty, this side pairs beautifully with grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the halved baby potatoes and cook for about 10 minutes, or until tender. Drain and let cool slightly.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the cooked potatoes, chopped hard-boiled eggs, and fresh dill to the bowl, then toss everything together gently until well combined.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving.

6. Grilled Asparagus with Lemon Zest

Grilled asparagus is a sophisticated yet simple side that pairs perfectly with just about anything coming off the grill. The natural flavor of the asparagus is enhanced by a burst of citrusy lemon zest, making it a refreshing complement to your BBQ spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Drizzle the asparagus with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the asparagus for about 6-8 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and slightly charred.
  4. Remove from the grill and sprinkle with lemon zest before serving.

7. Corn and Avocado Salad

A light, refreshing salad made with sweet corn and creamy avocado is a great way to balance out the heaviness of grilled meats. The vibrant flavors of this salad make it a hit at any BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ears of corn, husked and cooked
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Grill the corn until charred, about 10-12 minutes. Let cool slightly, then cut the kernels off the cob.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the grilled corn, diced avocado, chopped onion, and cilantro.
  3. Drizzle with lime juice and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss gently and serve immediately.

8. Macaroni and Cheese

Creamy, cheesy, and utterly comforting, macaroni and cheese is a beloved side dish that pairs perfectly with the smoky flavors of grilled BBQ. It’s a crowd-pleaser that never goes out of style.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Cook the macaroni according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until it forms a roux.
  3. Gradually whisk in the milk and cook until the sauce thickens, about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the cheddar and Parmesan cheeses until melted and smooth.
  5. Toss the cooked macaroni with the cheese sauce, season with salt and pepper, and serve.

9. Cucumber and Tomato Salad

A simple, fresh cucumber and tomato salad is an excellent light side dish for a BBQ. The crispness of the cucumbers and the juiciness of the tomatoes make it a refreshing palate cleanser between bites of smoky grilled meat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Combine the cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and red onion in a bowl.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and red wine vinegar, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Toss gently and serve immediately.

10. Grilled Pineapple with Honey and Lime

For a sweet ending to your BBQ feast, grilled pineapple offers a tropical, caramelized finish. The natural sweetness of the pineapple, combined with a drizzle of honey and a squeeze of lime, makes for a refreshing and delightful side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • A pinch of cayenne pepper (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the pineapple slices for 3-4 minutes on each side, until charred and tender.
  3. Drizzle with honey, squeeze lime juice over the top, and sprinkle with cayenne pepper if desired.
  4. Serve warm.

These 10 side dishes will bring color, flavor, and variety to your Fourth of July BBQ, perfectly complementing your grilled meats and adding that extra touch that will make your gathering unforgettable. Whether you’re craving something creamy, tangy, or fresh, these dishes are sure to impress your guests and elevate your outdoor feast.
