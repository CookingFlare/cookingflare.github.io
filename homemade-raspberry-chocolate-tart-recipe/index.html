Homemade Raspberry Chocolate Tart Recipe


Chocolate and raspberry are two ingredients that harmonize beautifully together, creating a decadent dessert that is both indulgent and refreshing. A homemade raspberry chocolate tart is the perfect combination of rich, smooth chocolate and the bright, tart flavor of fresh raspberries, all encased in a buttery, crisp pastry shell. Whether you’re preparing for a special occasion, a holiday gathering, or simply craving a sweet treat, this tart is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Tart Shell:

  • 1 1/4 cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (30g) cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup (113g) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups (225g) fresh raspberries
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Chocolate Ganache:

  • 8 oz (227g) high-quality dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For Garnish (Optional):

  • Fresh raspberries
  • Shaved chocolate
  • A sprinkle of powdered sugar

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Tart Shell

  1. Make the dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour, cocoa powder, and sugar. Add the cubed cold butter to the dry ingredients and, using a pastry cutter or your hands, work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized pieces of butter. This is the key to getting a flaky tart crust.

  2. Add the egg yolk and water: Add the egg yolk and 2 tablespoons of ice water. Stir with a fork until the dough begins to come together. If the dough seems too dry, add another tablespoon of water. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  3. Chill the dough: Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it into a ball. Flatten it into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and chill it in the fridge for at least 30 minutes. This step helps the dough firm up and makes it easier to roll out.

  4. Roll out the dough: Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and let it sit for a few minutes at room temperature. Roll the dough out on a floured surface until it’s about 1/8 inch thick. Transfer the dough to a tart pan with a removable bottom (approximately 9 inches in diameter). Gently press the dough into the pan, making sure it fits snugly against the sides.

  5. Blind bake the crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork and line it with parchment paper or aluminum foil. Fill the crust with pie weights, dried beans, or rice to prevent it from puffing up while baking. Bake for about 15 minutes. After 15 minutes, remove the weights and parchment paper, and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the crust is fully set and slightly golden.

  6. Cool the crust: Once the tart shell is done baking, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool completely before filling.

Step 2: Making the Raspberry Filling

  1. Cook the raspberries: In a medium saucepan, combine the fresh raspberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the raspberries break down and the mixture thickens, about 5-7 minutes. You can use the back of a spoon to mash the raspberries if you prefer a smoother filling.

  2. Strain the filling: For a smoother texture, you can strain the raspberry mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the seeds. This step is optional, but it will give you a velvety-smooth raspberry filling.

  3. Cool the raspberry filling: Allow the raspberry filling to cool completely before spreading it over the tart crust.

Step 3: Preparing the Chocolate Ganache

  1. Heat the cream: In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it starts to simmer. Be sure to watch it closely so it doesn’t boil over.

  2. Pour over the chocolate: Place the chopped dark chocolate in a heatproof bowl. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate, making sure all the chocolate is covered. Let it sit for a minute to allow the chocolate to soften.

  3. Stir to combine: After a minute, stir the chocolate and cream together until smooth and glossy. Add the butter, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt, and stir until the butter is fully melted and the ganache is smooth.

  4. Cool the ganache: Let the ganache cool for a few minutes before using it to top the tart. This will ensure that it doesn’t melt the raspberry filling when it’s poured on.

Step 4: Assembling the Tart

  1. Layer the raspberry filling: Once the tart shell has cooled completely, spread the cooled raspberry filling evenly over the base of the tart shell. The filling should form a smooth layer that covers the entire bottom.

  2. Pour the chocolate ganache: Carefully pour the cooled chocolate ganache over the raspberry filling. Use a spatula to gently spread the ganache into an even layer. You can also tilt the tart pan slightly to ensure the ganache evenly covers the raspberry filling.

  3. Chill the tart: Place the tart in the refrigerator for at least 2-3 hours to allow the ganache to set. If you’re in a hurry, you can speed up the process by placing the tart in the freezer for 30-45 minutes.

Step 5: Garnishing and Serving

  1. Garnish the tart: Before serving, you can garnish the tart with fresh raspberries, shaved chocolate, or a light dusting of powdered sugar for an elegant touch.

  2. Serve and enjoy: Once the tart has chilled and the ganache has set, slice it into wedges and serve. This tart is perfect for a special occasion, but it’s also a wonderful dessert for any time you want to indulge in something rich and luxurious.

Tips and Tricks for the Perfect Raspberry Chocolate Tart

  • Use quality chocolate: Since the ganache is a key element of this tart, using high-quality dark chocolate will make a big difference in flavor. Look for chocolate that’s at least 70% cocoa for a rich, deep chocolate flavor that balances well with the tartness of the raspberries.

  • Raspberry substitution: While fresh raspberries are ideal for this recipe, you can also use frozen raspberries. Just be sure to thaw and drain them well before using them in the filling.

  • Let the tart cool completely: It’s important to let the tart cool completely before slicing it. This allows the ganache to set properly and makes it easier to slice into clean, neat pieces.

  • Customize with different fruits: While raspberries and chocolate are a classic pairing, you can experiment with other fruits like strawberries, blackberries, or even cherries. Just keep in mind that some fruits may release more moisture than others, so you may need to adjust the filling accordingly.

Conclusion

This homemade raspberry chocolate tart is a showstopper dessert that combines the best of both worlds—rich chocolate ganache and the fresh, tangy flavor of raspberries. With a buttery cocoa crust, a smooth raspberry filling, and a luscious chocolate topping, this tart is sure to be a hit at any gathering. Whether you’re an experienced baker or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is simple to follow and guarantees a delicious result. Enjoy the perfect balance of sweet, tart, and chocolatey goodness with each bite!
