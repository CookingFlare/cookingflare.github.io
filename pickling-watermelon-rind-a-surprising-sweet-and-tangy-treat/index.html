Pickling Watermelon Rind: A Surprising Sweet and Tangy Treat


Who would have thought that the humble watermelon rind could be transformed into a delicious sweet and tangy treat through the magic of pickling? This often overlooked part of the watermelon is actually a treasure trove of flavor and texture waiting to be explored. Today, we’re going to delve into the art of pickling watermelon rind and discover the delightful results that await.

Understanding Watermelon Rind

Before we embark on our pickling adventure, let’s take a moment to appreciate the unique qualities of watermelon rind. The rind, with its crisp texture and subtle sweetness, holds great potential for culinary exploration. When pickled, it undergoes a delightful transformation, becoming a vibrant mixture of sweet, tangy, and slightly crunchy goodness.

The Pickling Process

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of watermelon rind, peeled and cut into small cubes
  • 2 cups of white vinegar
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon of allspice
  • 1 teaspoon of whole peppercorns

Instructions

  1. Begin by peeling the tough green skin from the watermelon rind and removing any remaining pink flesh.
  2. Cut the rind into small, uniform cubes and set aside.
  3. In a large pot, combine the white vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and spices, and bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Once the mixture has reached a rolling boil, carefully add the watermelon rind cubes.
  5. Reduce the heat and simmer the rind in the pickling liquid for about 45 minutes, or until the cubes become translucent and tender.
  6. Remove the pot from the heat and allow the pickled watermelon rind to cool to room temperature.
  7. Once cooled, transfer the pickled rind and its liquid into sterilized jars, making sure the rind is fully submerged in the liquid.
  8. Seal the jars and store them in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours before enjoying the fruits of your labor.

Serving Suggestions

Pickled watermelon rind can be enjoyed in a variety of ways, adding a unique twist to both sweet and savory dishes. Here are a few suggestions to get you started:

  • Charcuterie Board: Arrange pickled watermelon rind alongside an assortment of cheeses, cured meats, and crackers for a delightful addition to your next charcuterie board.
  • Salads: Toss pickled watermelon rind into a fresh summer salad for a burst of unexpected flavor and texture.
  • Sandwiches: Add a few slices of pickled watermelon rind to your favorite sandwich for a sweet and tangy kick.
  • Cocktails: Elevate your cocktail game by using pickled watermelon rind as a garnish for your next refreshing beverage.

Final Thoughts

Pickling watermelon rind opens up a world of culinary possibilities, transforming a often-discarded part of the fruit into a delightful and versatile ingredient. Whether enjoyed on its own or incorporated into a variety of dishes, pickled watermelon rind is a surprising sweet and tangy treat that is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

So, the next time you find yourself enjoying a juicy watermelon, don’t let the rind go to waste. Instead, embark on a pickling adventure and savor the delightful results of this surprisingly delicious treat!
