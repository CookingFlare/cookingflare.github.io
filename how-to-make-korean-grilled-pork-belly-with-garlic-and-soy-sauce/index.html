How to Make Korean Grilled Pork Belly with Garlic and Soy Sauce


Korean cuisine offers a diverse range of flavors, with dishes known for their balance of savory, sweet, salty, and umami. One of the standout dishes in Korean barbecue is Samgyeopsal, or grilled pork belly. This delicious and aromatic dish combines tender slices of pork belly, which are grilled to perfection and then paired with a rich, flavorful marinade made from garlic and soy sauce. It’s a mouthwatering, satisfying dish that’s perfect for sharing with friends and family. Whether you’re new to Korean food or a long-time fan, this recipe will guide you step-by-step on how to make Korean grilled pork belly with garlic and soy sauce.

Ingredients for Korean Grilled Pork Belly

To make this dish, you’ll need a few basic ingredients. The key to achieving that unique Korean flavor lies in the marinade, which includes garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, and other components that elevate the taste. Here’s a list of ingredients for the recipe:

Pork Belly

  • 1 pound of pork belly (fresh or frozen)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Pepper (to taste)

Marinade

  • 4 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes, optional, for a bit of heat)
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped green onions (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger (minced, optional for an extra zing)

Dipping Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon gochujang (Korean chili paste, optional)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • Fresh chili slices (optional for added heat)

Side Dishes (for serving)

  • Lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
  • Kimchi (for an authentic touch)
  • Sliced garlic (for serving, optional)
  • Steamed rice (to balance the richness)

Preparing the Pork Belly

1. Choose the Right Pork Belly

When it comes to making Korean grilled pork belly, selecting the right cut of pork is essential. Fresh pork belly, with its layers of fat and meat, will provide the juiciness and tenderness necessary for this dish. If you can, buy pork belly that’s already sliced into thin strips (about 1/4 inch thick). Thin slices are perfect because they grill quickly and absorb the marinade flavors more effectively. If you’re unable to find pre-sliced pork belly, simply ask your butcher to slice it for you, or you can slice it yourself at home.

2. Prep the Pork Belly

Before marinating the pork belly, you want to season it lightly with salt and pepper to bring out the natural flavors of the meat. Lay the pork belly slices out on a tray, and sprinkle them with a pinch of salt and pepper on both sides. You don’t need to overdo it with the seasoning at this stage, as the marinade will provide additional flavor.

3. Make the Marinade

In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, honey or sugar, rice vinegar, gochugaru (if using), green onions (optional), and ginger (optional). Whisk everything together until the honey or sugar dissolves completely. The soy sauce will give it a rich, salty depth, while the sesame oil brings a nutty, aromatic flavor that’s typical of Korean cooking. The garlic adds a punch of flavor, and the rice vinegar brings a nice balance to the overall marinade with its tangy acidity.

4. Marinate the Pork Belly

Place your seasoned pork belly slices into a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow bowl, and pour the marinade over the pork. Seal the bag or cover the bowl, and let the pork belly marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, although overnight marinating will give the meat even more flavor. If you’re short on time, a quick 30-minute marination will still provide plenty of taste, but longer marinating will allow the flavors to really penetrate the meat.

Grilling the Pork Belly

Once your pork belly is marinated and ready, it’s time to fire up the grill or heat your stovetop grill pan. Here’s how you can grill it perfectly.

1. Prepare the Grill

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a grill pan on the stovetop, heat it over medium-high heat and lightly oil it to prevent the pork belly from sticking. You can also use a cast-iron skillet for a similar effect. The goal is to achieve a good sear on the pork belly slices while ensuring they cook evenly.

2. Grill the Pork Belly

Place the marinated pork belly slices on the grill or in the pan, ensuring they’re not overcrowded. Grill them for about 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until they’re nicely caramelized and cooked through. Pork belly has a high fat content, so it will release some fat as it cooks, adding flavor and moisture to the meat. Be sure to flip the pork belly slices once or twice to ensure even grilling on both sides.

3. Check for Doneness

Because pork belly is fatty and has a relatively high moisture content, it can take a bit longer to cook than leaner cuts of meat. You’ll know it’s done when the edges are crispy and the meat is tender. The outside should be slightly golden and crispy, while the inside remains juicy and flavorful.

Preparing the Dipping Sauce

While the pork belly is grilling, you can prepare a simple dipping sauce to accompany your grilled pork. This sauce will provide a nice contrast to the richness of the meat and add an extra layer of flavor.

1. Combine the Ingredients

In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, sugar, and gochujang (if using). Whisk everything together until the sugar dissolves, and then sprinkle some sesame seeds on top for added texture. You can also add fresh chili slices for some extra heat, depending on your preference.

2. Adjust to Taste

Taste the dipping sauce and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you like it sweeter, add a little more sugar or honey. If you want more heat, go ahead and increase the amount of gochujang or fresh chili slices. The dipping sauce should have a good balance of savory, sweet, and tangy flavors.

Serving the Korean Grilled Pork Belly

Now that your pork belly is grilled to perfection and your dipping sauce is ready, it’s time to serve everything up!

1. Serve with Lettuce Wraps

Korean grilled pork belly is often served with fresh lettuce leaves, which act as a base for wrapping the meat. Lay the grilled pork belly slices on the lettuce leaves and spoon a little of the dipping sauce over the top. You can also add other accompaniments like sliced garlic, a dollop of kimchi, or even a bit of rice for added texture and flavor.

2. Enjoy with Side Dishes

To create an authentic Korean barbecue experience, serve the grilled pork belly alongside side dishes such as kimchi, pickled vegetables, or a simple bowl of steamed rice. The pickled vegetables and kimchi will add a refreshing crunch and tang to balance out the richness of the pork belly.

3. Wrap and Eat

To enjoy, take a piece of the grilled pork belly, place it in the center of a lettuce leaf, add some of the dipping sauce, and wrap it up. You can eat the wrap as is or dip it in the dipping sauce for an extra burst of flavor. The combination of the smoky pork, the crisp lettuce, and the savory dipping sauce creates a harmonious blend of textures and tastes that is truly delightful.

Tips for Success

  • Grill on high heat: Make sure your grill or grill pan is hot before placing the pork belly on it. This will give the pork a nice sear, locking in the juices and creating a crispy, flavorful crust.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pork: Allow enough space between each piece of pork belly while grilling to ensure that they cook evenly and don’t steam.
  • Use fresh ingredients: For the best flavor, use fresh garlic, ginger, and green onions. Fresh ingredients can make a noticeable difference in the taste of the marinade.
  • Adjust the marinade to your liking: If you prefer a sweeter marinade, increase the honey or sugar content. If you like it spicier, add more gochugaru or gochujang.
  • Serve immediately: Korean grilled pork belly is best enjoyed hot off the grill. The pork belly will lose its juiciness if it sits for too long.

Final Thoughts

Making Korean grilled pork belly at home is a rewarding and fun experience. With a bit of preparation and the right ingredients, you can enjoy the authentic flavors of Korean barbecue right in your own kitchen. This dish is perfect for gatherings, weekend meals, or simply when you’re in the mood for something indulgent and flavorful. Whether you’re wrapping it in lettuce or serving it with rice and kimchi, Korean grilled pork belly with garlic and soy sauce is sure to impress anyone who tastes it.
