In Season: 5 Healthy and Light Recipes with Fresh Summer Produce


Summer is here, and that means an abundance of fresh and delicious produce is available at your local farmers’ market or grocery store. Take advantage of the vibrant flavors and nutritional benefits of seasonal fruits and vegetables with these five healthy and light recipes. Packed with nutrients and bursting with flavor, these dishes will keep you feeling satisfied and energized all summer long.

1. Grilled Watermelon Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 small watermelon, cut into wedges
  • 2 cups arugula
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place watermelon wedges on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until grill marks appear.
  3. Remove watermelon from the grill and let it cool slightly.
  4. In a large bowl, combine arugula, feta cheese, and chopped mint leaves.
  5. Add the grilled watermelon to the bowl and drizzle with balsamic glaze.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and toss gently to combine.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy the refreshing flavors of this unique summer salad.

2. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 2 large zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine basil leaves, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  2. Pulse until smooth and creamy, adding more olive oil if needed.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  5. Add zucchini noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes, until tender.
  6. Remove from heat and toss with pesto sauce.
  7. Garnish with extra Parmesan cheese and serve warm.

3. Mango Salsa Stuffed Avocado

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the avocados in half lengthwise and remove the pits.
  2. In a bowl, combine diced mango, red onion, cherry tomatoes, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spoon the mango salsa into the avocado halves.
  4. Serve immediately as a refreshing and nutritious appetizer or light lunch.

4. Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into cubes
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or parsley)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, chopped herbs, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and let them sit for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken cubes onto skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  6. Remove from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.
  7. Enjoy these flavorful and tender chicken skewers with a side of grilled vegetables or a fresh salad.

5. Berry Chia Pudding

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine mixed berries and almond milk.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and stir in chia seeds, honey or maple syrup (if desired), and vanilla extract.
  4. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight, until the chia seeds have absorbed the liquid and the mixture has thickened.
  5. Stir the pudding before serving and top with additional berries, if desired.
  6. Enjoy this nutritious and delicious summer dessert or breakfast option.

Take advantage of the fresh and flavorful summer produce with these five healthy and light recipes. Incorporating seasonal ingredients into your meals not only enhances the taste but also provides essential nutrients to support your overall well-being. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply enjoying a meal at home, these dishes are sure to impress and delight your taste buds. So, head to the nearest farmers’ market and start cooking up a storm with the best of summer’s bounty!
