Arroz a la Cubana: A Simple and Flavorful Spanish Rice Dish


Arroz a la Cubana is a classic Spanish dish that combines the vibrant flavors of rice, tomato sauce, fried eggs, and plantains. This simple yet flavorful dish is a staple in many Spanish households and is loved for its versatility and delicious taste. Whether you’re looking to impress your guests or simply want to indulge in a comforting and satisfying meal, Arroz a la Cubana is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

To make Arroz a la Cubana, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked white rice
  • 1 cup of tomato sauce
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup of green peas
  • 4 ripe plantains, sliced and fried
  • 4 eggs, fried
  • Olive oil for cooking
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic and sauté until translucent.

  2. Add the diced red bell pepper and carrot to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Stir in the tomato sauce and green peas, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5 minutes, until the flavors are well combined.

  4. Add the cooked white rice to the skillet and mix well, ensuring that the rice is evenly coated with the tomato sauce and vegetables. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. While the rice is cooking, heat some olive oil in a separate pan and fry the plantain slices until golden brown on both sides. Remove from heat and set aside.

  6. In the same pan, fry the eggs to your desired doneness. For a traditional Arroz a la Cubana, it is recommended to fry the eggs sunny-side up or over-easy.

  7. Once the rice is cooked and the eggs and plantains are ready, it’s time to plate the dish. Divide the rice mixture into individual serving plates and top each portion with a fried egg and a few slices of fried plantains.

  8. Serve the Arroz a la Cubana hot and enjoy the explosion of flavors with each bite. The combination of the rich tomato sauce, fluffy rice, creamy egg yolk, and sweet plantains is sure to satisfy any craving.

Variations

While Arroz a la Cubana is traditionally made with white rice, you can experiment with different types of rice to add a unique twist to the dish. Brown rice or wild rice can add a nutty flavor and a healthier touch to the recipe.

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also add some protein to the dish. Sautéed shrimp, grilled chicken, or even ground beef can be excellent additions to make the dish heartier and more filling.

For those who love a bit of heat, adding a dash of hot sauce or a sprinkle of chili flakes can give the Arroz a la Cubana a spicy kick.

Conclusion

Arroz a la Cubana is a simple yet flavorful Spanish rice dish that is loved for its combination of vibrant flavors and comforting textures. With its versatile nature, you can easily customize the dish to suit your preferences and dietary needs. Whether you enjoy it as a main course or a side dish, Arroz a la Cubana is sure to please your taste buds and transport you to the sunny streets of Spain.
