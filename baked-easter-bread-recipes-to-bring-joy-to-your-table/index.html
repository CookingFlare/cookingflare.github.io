Baked Easter Bread Recipes to Bring Joy to Your Table


Easter is a time of celebration, renewal, and gathering with friends and family. Among the many traditions that fill the season with warmth and sweetness, baked Easter bread stands out as a symbol of joy, prosperity, and togetherness. Whether you’re hosting a festive brunch or simply looking to add a homemade touch to your holiday table, the enticing aroma and rich texture of freshly baked Easter bread make it a perfect centerpiece.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of delicious baked Easter bread recipes designed to inspire your culinary creativity and bring joy to your table. From classic Italian Easter breads to unique and flavorful twists, there is something here to please every palate. Each recipe is detailed for your baking pleasure, followed by tips and variations to make each loaf your own.

The Tradition of Easter Bread

Easter bread has a long history in many cultures, often steeped in symbolism and seasonal significance. It is typically a sweet, enriched dough, sometimes braided, studded with dried fruit or nuts and often crowned with colorful sprinkles or dyed eggs. This bread isn’t just about taste; it represents hope, rebirth, and the sweetness of life’s new beginnings.

Classic Italian Easter Bread

The Italian Easter bread is a brilliant place to start. Lightly sweet and softly textured, it usually features vibrant dyed eggs nestled in the dough, making for a festive presentation.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm milk (about 110°F)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs (2 for dough, 1 for egg wash)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon or orange
  • Colored Easter eggs (hard-boiled)
  • Sprinkles or coarse sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm milk, a pinch of sugar, and yeast. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Make the dough: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, salt, and zest. Create a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, melted butter, and 2 eggs. Stir until a sticky dough forms.
  3. Knead: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, until smooth and elastic. Alternatively, use a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook.
  4. First rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean towel, and let it rise in a warm spot until doubled in size (about 1-2 hours).
  5. Shape the bread: Punch down the dough and divide it into 3 equal portions. Roll each into long ropes and braid them together on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Form a circle, pinching the ends to seal.
  6. Place eggs: Nestle the colored hard-boiled eggs into the braid, pressing lightly.
  7. Second rise: Cover with a towel and let it rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Egg wash and decorate: Beat the remaining egg and brush it over the bread. Sprinkle with coarse sugar or sprinkles if desired.
  9. Bake: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown and sound hollow when tapped.
  10. Cool and serve: Let cool on a wire rack and enjoy the sweet, bright flavors of this festive loaf.

Greek Tsoureki: Aromatic and Fluffy

Another popular Easter bread is Greek Tsoureki, known for its fragrant notes of mahleb and mastic, spices rarely found in other breads. Its soft, brioche-like crumb and beautiful braided shape make it a wonderful addition to your Easter table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon mahleb (ground cherry pits)
  • 1/2 teaspoon mastic resin, ground (optional)
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Red-dyed hard-boiled eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Yeast prep: Dissolve yeast in warm milk with a tablespoon of sugar; rest until bubbly.
  2. Combining dry ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt, mahleb, and mastic.
  3. Mix wet ingredients: Whisk eggs, orange zest, and softened butter.
  4. Forming the dough: Stir yeast mixture and egg mixture into dry ingredients until dough begins to form.
  5. Kneading: Knead by hand or stand mixer for 10 minutes until elastic and smooth.
  6. Rising: Cover and allow dough to rise in a warm place until doubled (around 2 hours).
  7. Divide and braid: Divide dough into three equal parts. Roll each into a rope, braid, and shape into a ring or leave as a loaf.
  8. Adding eggs: Place red-dyed eggs into the braid if using, securing gently.
  9. Final proof: Cover and let rise for 30 minutes again.
  10. Egg wash and seeds: Brush with beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  11. Baking: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for approximately 30 minutes until golden.
  12. Cooling: Cool before slicing and savor this uniquely spiced bread.

Sweet Russian Paska Bread

Paska, a traditional Easter bread in Eastern European households, is often taller and more cake-like but still wonderfully bread-like. It is sweet, rich, and can be adorned with elaborate icing or simple sugar glazes.

Ingredients:

  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • 1/2 cup raisins or dried cranberries
  • Icing: powdered sugar and lemon juice or egg whites for glaze

Instructions:

  1. Prepare yeast: Mix yeast with warm milk and a teaspoon of sugar, allow to bloom.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: Combine flour, sugar, and salt.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: Beat eggs, vanilla, almond extract, and melted butter.
  4. Form dough: Add wet ingredients and yeast mixture to dry ingredients and stir to mix. Add raisins.
  5. Knead: Knead on a floured surface or mixer for about 10 minutes.
  6. First rising: Place dough in greased bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled, around 1.5-2 hours.
  7. Shape: Punch down and shape into a round loaf or place in a tall, greased pan.
  8. Second rise: Let dough rise again for 45 minutes.
  9. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 45-50 minutes or until golden and a toothpick comes out clean.
  10. Cool and glaze: Let cool before drizzling with icing or egg white glaze for a traditional finish.

Tips for Perfect Easter Bread Every Time

  • Room temperature ingredients: Make sure eggs, butter, and milk are at room temperature for better rise and dough development.
  • Patience during rising: The dough should double in size during proofs. Slower, cooler rises can enhance flavor.
  • Use a kitchen thermometer: Yeast activates best around 105°F-115°F; avoid overheating your milk or liquid which can kill the yeast.
  • Flavor additions: Experiment with citrus zest, spices like cardamom or nutmeg, and extracts to customize your bread.
  • Decorations: From dyed eggs woven into the braids to sugar glazes, consider visual appeal to make your bread festive and inviting.
  • Storage: Easter breads are best enjoyed fresh but can be stored tightly wrapped for 2-3 days at room temperature. Freeze for longer storage.

Variations to Try

  • Savory Easter Bread: Substitute the sugar with fresh herbs, omit dried fruit, and add cheese or olives for a savory twist.
  • Gluten-Free or Vegan Easter Bread: Use gluten-free baking mixes with added xanthan gum, and replace dairy and eggs with plant-based alternatives like almond milk and flax eggs.
  • Mini loaves or rolls: Perfect for individual servings and easier sharing.
  • Add nuts: Almonds, walnuts, or pistachios provide a satisfying crunch and flavor boost.

Bringing Easter Bread to Your Table

Baking Easter bread is more than a culinary task — it is a way to connect with tradition, creativity, and those you love. Whether enjoyed plain, with butter, or alongside your favorite springtime dishes, these loaves deliver warmth and sweetness in every bite. Try these recipes this season to fill your kitchen with the joy of baking and your home with the spirit of Easter celebration.

May your holidays be rich with flavor, friendship, and the simple pleasure of breaking bread together. Happy baking!
