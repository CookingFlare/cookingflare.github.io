Mouthwatering Christmas Ham Recipe for Your Holiday Table


The holiday season is the perfect time to gather with loved ones and indulge in delicious food. And what better centerpiece for your holiday table than a succulent and mouthwatering Christmas ham? This classic dish is a crowd-pleaser that never fails to impress. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create a truly unforgettable Christmas ham that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

To create the perfect Christmas ham, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in, fully cooked ham (around 10-12 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the ham in a large baking dish or roasting pan, fat side up.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, maple syrup, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Mix well until the ingredients are fully combined.

  3. Using a brush or spoon, generously coat the ham with the glaze mixture, making sure to cover the entire surface. Reserve some of the glaze for basting later.

  4. Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil, making sure to leave some space for the heat to circulate. This will help keep the ham moist and prevent it from drying out.

  5. Place the ham in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 15 minutes per pound. For a 10-12 pound ham, this will be around 2.5 to 3 hours. Baste the ham with the reserved glaze every 30 minutes to add extra flavor and moisture.

  6. About 30 minutes before the ham is done, remove the foil to allow the top to brown and caramelize. This will create a beautiful and appetizing glaze.

  7. Once the internal temperature of the ham reaches 140°F (60°C), it is ready to be taken out of the oven. Use a meat thermometer to ensure accuracy.

  8. Let the ham rest for about 15 minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute and ensure a tender and juicy result.

  9. Carve the ham into thin slices and serve it on a platter. Garnish with fresh rosemary sprigs, if desired, for a festive touch.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of flavor, you can add pineapple rings or cloves to the ham before baking. Simply press the cloves into the fat layer of the ham or arrange pineapple rings on top, securing them with toothpicks.

  • If you prefer a sweeter glaze, you can increase the amount of brown sugar or maple syrup in the recipe. Adjust the ingredients to suit your taste preferences.

  • Leftover ham can be used in a variety of recipes, such as sandwiches, soups, or casseroles. Be sure to store it properly in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

  • Feel free to experiment with different spices and herbs to customize the flavor of your Christmas ham. Thyme, garlic, or even a hint of cayenne pepper can add an interesting twist.

Conclusion

This mouthwatering Christmas ham recipe is sure to become a holiday favorite among your family and friends. The combination of sweet and savory flavors, along with the tender and juicy meat, creates a truly irresistible centerpiece for your holiday table. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying a cozy celebration, this ham will undoubtedly add a touch of festive magic to your holiday season. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to create a Christmas ham that will leave everyone craving for more. Happy cooking and happy holidays!
