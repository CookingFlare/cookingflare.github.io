The Best Sous Vide Recipe for Lamb Shanks


Lamb shanks have always been a staple in many cuisines around the world, and for good reason. The slow-cooked, fall-off-the-bone tender meat is a treat for the senses, and the rich, gamey flavor is a perfect match for a variety of herbs and spices. But cooking lamb shanks can be a challenge, especially for those who are new to cooking. That’s where sous vide comes in.

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking method that involves sealing food in airtight bags and then cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method allows for unparalleled control over the cooking process, ensuring that your lamb shanks are cooked to perfection every time.

In this recipe, we’ll be using the sous vide method to cook lamb shanks to tender, juicy perfection. We’ll also be adding a variety of aromatics and spices to give the dish a rich, complex flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 lamb shanks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

First, preheat your sous vide water bath to 130°F (54°C). While the water is heating up, season the lamb shanks with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat, and sear the lamb shanks on all sides until they’re nicely browned. Remove the lamb shanks from the skillet and set them aside.

Next, add the diced onion to the skillet and cook until it’s softened and translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute. Add the red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf to the skillet, and stir to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes, or until it’s reduced by half.

Once the liquid has reduced, add the lamb shanks back to the skillet and stir to coat them evenly with the sauce. Remove the skillet from the heat and let it cool slightly.

Once the water bath has reached the correct temperature, place the lamb shanks in airtight bags and seal them with a vacuum sealer or the displacement method. Place the bags in the water bath and cook for 24 hours.

After 24 hours, remove the lamb shanks from the water bath and remove them from the bags. Strain the cooking liquid and discard the solids. Skim off any excess fat from the surface of the liquid and discard it.

To serve, place the lamb shanks on a plate and spoon some of the cooking liquid over the top. Serve with your choice of sides, such as roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes.

Tips and Variations:

  • To add some extra flavor to the dish, you can add some aromatics like carrots, celery, and onions to the sous vide bag with the lamb shanks.
  • If you prefer a stronger flavor, you can increase the amount of red wine and beef broth in the recipe.
  • To make the dish more substantial, you can serve the lamb shanks with some crusty bread or over a bed of pasta.
  • If you’re looking for a vegetarian option, you can substitute the lamb shanks with beef or pork shanks.

Conclusion:

Cooking lamb shanks sous vide is a game-changer for anyone who loves slow-cooked, fall-off-the-bone tender meat. The method allows for unparalleled control over the cooking process, ensuring that your lamb shanks are cooked to perfection every time. With this recipe, you’ll be able to create a rich, complex flavor profile that’s sure to impress your guests. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the magic of sous vide cooking for yourself.
