Thai Peanut Shrimp Stir-Fry: A Nutty and Seafood-Filled Delight


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine and seafood, then you’re in for a treat with this Thai Peanut Shrimp Stir-Fry recipe. This dish is a delightful combination of succulent shrimp, vibrant vegetables, and a rich, nutty sauce that will tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a cooking enthusiast or someone looking to impress their guests with a flavorful and exotic meal, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients:

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of sugar snap peas
  • 1 cup of shredded carrots
  • 1 cup of broccoli florets
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup of creamy peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup of low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup of water

For Serving:

  • Cooked white or brown rice
  • Chopped green onions and chopped peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sauce:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, grated ginger, and red pepper flakes until smooth. Gradually add water to achieve the desired consistency. Set aside.

  2. Cook the Shrimp:

    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and stir for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the shrimp to the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables:

    • In the same pan, add the sliced bell peppers, sugar snap peas, shredded carrots, and broccoli florets. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Combine and Simmer:

    • Return the cooked shrimp to the pan with the vegetables. Pour the prepared sauce over the shrimp and vegetables. Gently toss everything together until well coated. Allow the stir-fry to simmer for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly and the shrimp and vegetables are heated through.

  5. Serve and Garnish:

    • Serve the Thai Peanut Shrimp Stir-Fry over cooked rice. Garnish with chopped green onions and chopped peanuts for an added crunch and burst of freshness.

Tips for Success:

  • Prep Ahead: You can prepare the sauce and chop the vegetables in advance to streamline the cooking process.
  • Customize the Heat: Adjust the amount of red pepper flakes in the sauce to suit your heat preference.
  • Protein Swap: If you’re not a fan of shrimp, you can easily substitute it with chicken, tofu, or even a mix of your favorite seafood.

Final Thoughts:

The Thai Peanut Shrimp Stir-Fry is a harmonious blend of textures and flavors, from the tender shrimp to the crisp vegetables, all brought together by the luscious nutty sauce. The dish offers a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and spicy notes, making it a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or a special gathering, this recipe is a surefire way to elevate your culinary skills and transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Thailand. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your wok, and embark on a culinary journey with this delightful Thai Peanut Shrimp Stir-Fry!
