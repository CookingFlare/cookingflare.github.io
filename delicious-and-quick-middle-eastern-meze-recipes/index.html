Delicious and Quick Middle Eastern Meze Recipes


Middle Eastern cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, rich textures, and vibrant colors. One of the most beloved ways to enjoy this diverse culinary tradition is through meze—a collection of small dishes that are often shared among friends and family. Meze offers a fantastic way to experience a variety of flavors, from savory dips to warm grilled meats. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just looking to create a casual meal at home, these quick and delicious Middle Eastern meze recipes are perfect for any occasion.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a selection of traditional and modern meze dishes that are easy to prepare, full of flavor, and sure to impress your guests. From creamy hummus to crispy falafel, we’ll guide you through each recipe step by step so you can bring the flavors of the Middle East to your table.

1. Hummus: The Classic Middle Eastern Dip

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (garbanzo beans)
  • 3 tablespoons tahini (sesame paste)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, to adjust consistency
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Paprika, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas: Drain and rinse the chickpeas from the can. For a smoother texture, you can remove the skins of the chickpeas by gently pinching them, but this step is optional.

  2. Blend the Ingredients: In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, cumin, and a pinch of salt. Process until smooth, adding water gradually to reach your desired consistency.

  3. Taste and Adjust: Taste your hummus and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you like it more tangy, add more lemon juice, or add extra garlic for a punch.

  4. Serve: Transfer the hummus to a serving dish, drizzle with olive oil, and garnish with chopped parsley and a sprinkle of paprika. Serve with warm pita bread, crackers, or fresh vegetables.

Hummus is not only easy to make, but it also packs a punch of flavor. It’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a dip or spread, and it pairs well with a variety of other meze dishes.

2. Baba Ganoush: Smoky Eggplant Dip

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Pomegranate seeds (optional), for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Eggplants: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Pierce the eggplants with a fork and place them on a baking sheet. Roast for about 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the eggplant is soft.

  2. Cool and Peel: Let the roasted eggplants cool for a few minutes. Once cool enough to handle, peel off the skin and discard it. Scoop out the flesh and place it in a bowl.

  3. Blend the Dip: In a food processor, combine the eggplant flesh, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  4. Serve: Transfer the baba ganoush to a serving dish and drizzle with olive oil. Garnish with fresh parsley and pomegranate seeds for a touch of sweetness and color. Serve with pita bread or vegetables.

Baba ganoush offers a deliciously smoky flavor that pairs beautifully with the earthy tahini and tangy lemon. It’s a great addition to any meze platter and an excellent alternative to hummus.

3. Falafel: Crispy Chickpea Fritters

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (garbanzo beans), drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons flour (or chickpea flour for gluten-free)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Falafel Mixture: In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, baking powder, flour, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is coarse but holds together when pressed.

  2. Chill the Mixture: Transfer the mixture to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. This will help the falafel hold together better when frying.

  3. Form the Falafel: Once chilled, form the falafel mixture into small balls or patties using your hands or a scoop.

  4. Fry the Falafel: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, fry the falafel in batches, turning them occasionally, until golden brown and crispy on all sides (about 3-4 minutes per side).

  5. Drain and Serve: Remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Serve with a side of tahini sauce or yogurt dip.

Falafel is one of the most iconic Middle Eastern dishes. With its crunchy exterior and soft, flavorful interior, it’s a satisfying option for any meze spread. Serve them with a side of fresh vegetables or pita bread for a complete experience.

4. Tabbouleh: Fresh Herb Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 bunch fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bulgur: Place the bulgur wheat in a bowl and cover it with warm water. Let it sit for about 10 minutes, or until it softens. Drain any excess water and fluff the bulgur with a fork.

  2. Combine the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, cucumber, parsley, and mint.

  3. Dress the Salad: Add the soaked bulgur to the vegetables and mix well. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Serve: Serve the tabbouleh immediately, or refrigerate it for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Tabbouleh is a light, refreshing salad that’s full of fresh herbs and vegetables. It’s the perfect side dish to balance out the richness of other meze offerings like hummus and falafel.

5. Muhammara: Spicy Red Pepper and Walnut Dip

Ingredients:

  • 2 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Roasted Peppers: Roast the red bell peppers under a broiler or on an open flame until the skin is charred. Place the peppers in a bowl, cover with a cloth, and let them steam for a few minutes. Peel off the skins, remove the seeds, and set the flesh aside.

  2. Blend the Ingredients: In a food processor, combine the roasted peppers, toasted walnuts, olive oil, pomegranate molasses, garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.

  3. Serve: Transfer the muhammara to a serving dish and drizzle with olive oil. Serve with pita bread, crackers, or fresh vegetables.

Muhammara is a smoky, spicy dip that’s perfect for those who love bold flavors. The combination of roasted peppers, walnuts, and pomegranate molasses creates a unique and unforgettable taste.

6. Labneh: Creamy Yogurt Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups plain Greek yogurt (full-fat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Olive oil, for drizzling
  • Dried za’atar or sumac, for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Strain the Yogurt: Place the Greek yogurt in a fine mesh strainer over a bowl and let it drain for several hours or overnight to remove excess liquid. The longer it drains, the thicker it will become.

  2. Season the Labneh: Once the yogurt has thickened, transfer it to a serving dish and stir in the salt.

  3. Serve: Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with za’atar or sumac. Serve with pita bread, olives, or fresh vegetables.

Labneh is a velvety smooth, tangy yogurt cheese that’s perfect for dipping. It’s often served as part of a larger meze spread and pairs wonderfully with bread or vegetables.

Conclusion

These Middle Eastern meze recipes are a celebration of the vibrant and diverse flavors of the region. Whether you’re in the mood for a creamy dip like hummus or baba ganoush, or a savory snack like falafel, these dishes are easy to prepare and sure to impress. The beauty of meze is that it encourages sharing and allows you to enjoy a wide range of flavors in one sitting.

Next time you’re hosting a dinner or simply craving something delicious and healthy, whip up these meze recipes to bring the flavors of the Middle East to your home. With their fresh ingredients, rich textures, and vibrant colors, these dishes are bound to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Enjoy!
