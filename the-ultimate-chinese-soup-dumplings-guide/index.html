The Ultimate Chinese Soup Dumplings Guide


Soup dumplings, or xiao long bao (小笼包), are one of the most iconic dim sum dishes in Chinese cuisine. Loved for their delicate wrapper, flavorful filling, and steamy soup tucked inside, they are a testament to culinary craftsmanship and an absolute delight for the taste buds. If you’ve ever wondered how these little pockets of joy are made or dreamed of perfecting them at home, you’re in the right place. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about soup dumplings, from their origins to step-by-step instructions to master them.

A Brief History of Xiao Long Bao

Xiao long bao originated in China’s Jiangnan region, specifically in Shanghai and its surrounding areas. Traditionally, they were served in wooden steamers (hence the name “xiao long,” meaning “little steaming basket”). Over time, these dumplings found their way from local markets into worldwide dining establishments, becoming a global favorite.

The beauty of xiao long bao lies in its ingenious design. Encased in thin dough, this treat contains a juicy filling with a burst of hot soup. But where does the soup come from? It’s all thanks to collagen-rich meat or aspic—gelatinized broth that liquefies into soup when steamed. This small culinary marvel is what sets soup dumplings apart from their peers.

Tools and Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to gather the right tools and ingredients. The art of making soup dumplings lies in precision and preparation.

Kitchen Tools

  • Rolling Pin: A smaller rolling pin works best for precision in crafting the wrappers.
  • Steamer Basket: Bamboo steamers are authentic and widely used, but metal steamers work just as well.
  • Dumpling Press or Silicone Mat: To ensure consistency in preparation.
  • Measuring Cups and Scale: Accurate measurements contribute to even texture and flavor.
  • Knife and Cutting Board: Useful for preparing ingredients finely.

Key Ingredients

  • Pork: Ground pork is the classic choice for the dumpling filling. Its fat content keeps the filling moist.
  • Gelatin or Collagen-Rich Broth: These create the soup pocket inside the dumpling. Homemade broth made from chicken and pork bones is ideal.
  • All-Purpose Flour or Dumpling Flour: Used for the dough wrappers, which should be soft yet stretchy.
  • Soy Sauce: Adds umami to the filling.
  • Shaoxing Wine: A staple in Chinese cooking, it enhances the depth of flavor.
  • Ginger and Green Onions: Typical aromatics for dumpling filling.
  • Sesame Oil: Provides that iconic nutty aroma and taste.
  • Salt and Sugar: Balances the flavor of the filling.

Step-By-Step Recipe: How to Make Xiao Long Bao

Making xiao long bao at home requires patience and attention to detail, but the payoff is immense. Follow these steps to create perfect soup dumplings from scratch.

Step 1: Prepare the Soup Stock

The “soup” element comes from gelatinized stock, which melts into liquid during steaming. Here’s how you make it:

  1. Ingredients for the Broth:

    • 500g pork bones
    • 500g chicken bones
    • 1 piece of ginger
    • 2 green onions
    • 8 cups of water

  2. Instructions:

    • Combine pork and chicken bones in a large pot of boiling water for about 5 minutes to blanch them.
    • Discard the water, rinse the bones thoroughly, then transfer them to a clean pot.
    • Add ginger, green onions, and fresh water. Simmer on low heat for 3-4 hours.
    • Strain the broth to remove solids and let it cool. Refrigerate overnight until the liquid turns into gelatin.

Step 2: Make the Dough

The wrapper is key to creating the perfect soup dumpling—it must be thin yet durable enough to hold the soup filling.

  1. Ingredients for the Dough:

    • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
    • 2/3 cup of warm water
    • Pinch of salt

  2. Instructions:

    • Combine flour and salt in a mixing bowl.
    • Gradually add warm water while stirring to create a shaggy dough.
    • Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
    • Cover with a damp cloth and let rest for 1 hour.

Step 3: Make the Filling

The filling combines juicy pork and flavorful seasonings. Here’s the process:

  1. Ingredients for the Filling:

    • 300g ground pork
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine
    • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
    • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
    • 1 green onion, finely chopped
    • Salt and sugar to taste
    • 1/2 cup gelatinized broth (cubed)

  2. Instructions:

    • In a bowl, mix the ground pork with soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, ginger, and green onions.
    • Season with a pinch of salt and sugar, ensuring the mixture is well-combined.
    • Fold the gelatinized broth cubes into the pork mixture gently, avoiding overmixing.

Step 4: Assemble the Dumplings

Here’s where precision comes into play.

  1. Instructions:
    • Roll out the rested dough into a long log and divide it into equal portions (about 2 inches wide).
    • Flatten each dough piece with a rolling pin to create thin, circular wrappers. Keep the edges thinner than the center to accommodate the filling.
    • Place a spoonful of filling (including a cube or two of gelatinized broth) in the center of each wrapper.
    • Carefully pleat the edges around the filling, pinching to seal the dumpling. Aim for 12-18 pleats per dumpling for a traditional look.

Step 5: Steam the Dumplings

Steaming brings everything together—cooking the dough, melting the broth, and infusing the dumplings with heavenly aromas.

  1. Instructions:
    • Line your steamer basket with parchment paper or Napa cabbage leaves to prevent sticking.
    • Arrange dumplings in the steamer basket with space between them.
    • Steam over boiling water for 8-10 minutes, ensuring the soup warms but doesn’t erupt out of the wrappers.

Tips for Perfect Soup Dumplings

  1. Keep the Wrappers Thin: While rolling the dough, focus on creating thin yet sturdy wrappers. Overly thick wrappers can detract from the dumpling experience.

  2. Work Quickly: Once filled, steam dumplings promptly to preserve the gelatin’s integrity.

  3. Serve Fresh: Soup dumplings are best enjoyed straight off the steamer, while the soup inside is warm and flavorful.

  4. Master the Pleats: Pleating isn’t just decorative—it ensures the dumplings seal properly to trap the soup inside.

  5. Pair with Dipping Sauce: Traditional dipping sauces feature a mix of vinegar, soy sauce, and freshly grated ginger. This complements the richness of the dumplings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my dumplings leak?

Leaking dumplings often result from wrappers being too thin or improper sealing. Take extra care while pleating and use consistent pressure.

Can I freeze soup dumplings?

Yes! Assemble the dumplings, arrange them on a parchment-lined tray, and freeze them uncooked. Steam directly from frozen for about 12-15 minutes.

What’s the best way to eat soup dumplings?

To eat xiao long bao, use a spoon to cradle the dumpling and nibble a small opening in the skin. Let the steam escape and sip the soup before devouring the rest.

Final Thoughts

Making soup dumplings from scratch may be a labor of love, but their impressive flavors and textures are well worth the effort. Whether you’re hosting a party, impressing guests, or indulging in homemade comfort food, the pride of perfecting xiao long bao will add a layer of joy to every bite.

Dive into the artistry of Chinese cuisine and give these beloved dumplings a place at your table. Practice makes perfect—and with this guide by your side, you’re set for success.
