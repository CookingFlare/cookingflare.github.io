5 Paleo-Friendly Recipes for a Quick and Easy Dinner


Eating Paleo doesn’t have to be daunting or overly time-consuming. The Paleo diet emphasizes whole foods, healthy fats, lean proteins, vegetables, nuts, and seeds while eliminating processed foods, grains, and dairy. Thankfully, with a little creativity, you can whip up meals that are not only delicious and nutritious but also quick and easy to prepare. Whether you’re a Paleo enthusiast or just looking to make healthier dinner choices, these five recipes are sure to bring convenience and flavor to your weeknight meals.

1. Garlic Lemon Herb Chicken Thighs with Roasted Vegetables

This hearty and flavorful dish is a perfect balance of protein and vegetables, and it only takes about 30 minutes to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 lb baby carrots
  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix together olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, lemon zest, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the mixture over the chicken thighs and set them aside to marinate for 10 minutes.
  4. Toss the carrots and Brussels sprouts with a bit of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them on a large baking tray.
  5. Place the chicken thighs on top of the vegetables.
  6. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C) and the vegetables are tender.
  7. Serve hot with an extra squeeze of lemon juice for added brightness.

2. Easy Sweet Potato and Ground Beef Skillet

This one-pan recipe is packed with savory flavors and is perfect for a busy weeknight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add diced sweet potatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, until slightly softened (about 5-7 minutes). Remove from skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add ground beef and cook until browned and cooked through. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  4. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic to the skillet and sauté for 3-4 minutes, until fragrant and softened.
  5. Return the sweet potatoes to the skillet and season with smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  6. Stir well to combine, cover, and cook for another 5 minutes until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

3. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto Shrimp

For a Paleo-friendly twist on pasta night, zucchini noodles paired with garlic shrimp and fresh pesto make for a light yet satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchini, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • ¼ cup pine nuts
  • ¼ cup olive oil (for pesto)
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the pesto by blending basil, pine nuts, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a food processor until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add minced garlic and shrimp, cooking until the shrimp turn pink and opaque (about 3-4 minutes). Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the spiralized zucchini and sauté for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened but still firm.
  4. Toss the zucchini noodles with the prepared pesto sauce.
  5. Serve the noodles topped with shrimp, garnished with fresh basil if desired.

4. Coconut-Crusted Salmon with Asparagus

Crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, this salmon dish is paired with roasted asparagus for an effortless Paleo dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 tbsp coconut flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lb asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, sliced for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Season salmon fillets with salt and pepper.
  3. Set up a dredging station: In one bowl, place the coconut flour. In another bowl, beat the eggs. In a third bowl, place shredded coconut.
  4. Dip each salmon fillet first in coconut flour, then in the beaten eggs, and finally coat with shredded coconut.
  5. Place the salmon on one side of the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Toss asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and arrange it on the other side of the baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  8. Serve with lemon slices for added freshness.

5. Stuffed Bell Peppers with Cauliflower Rice

A Paleo take on a classic comfort food, these stuffed bell peppers are filling, flavorful, and incredibly easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 lb ground turkey or ground beef
  • 2 cups cauliflower rice
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, cooking until fragrant and softened.
  4. Add ground turkey (or beef), cooking until browned. Stir in the tomato, cauliflower rice, paprika, oregano, coconut aminos, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5 minutes until combined and the cauliflower rice is tender.
  5. Stuff the bell peppers with the mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
  7. Remove from the oven and serve immediately.

These five Paleo-friendly dinner recipes are a testament to just how versatile and satisfying whole, unprocessed ingredients can be. By keeping these simple yet delicious meal ideas in rotation, you’ll find it easier to stay on track with your Paleo lifestyle while enjoying flavors that make dinnertime something to look forward to. Happy cooking!
