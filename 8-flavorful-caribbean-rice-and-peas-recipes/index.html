8 Flavorful Caribbean Rice and Peas Recipes


Rice and peas is a staple dish in many Caribbean countries, with each island nation offering its unique twist on this classic comfort food. From the salty shores of Jamaica to the sun-kissed beaches of Barbados, every region has its special way of preparing this beloved dish. In this post, we'll take you on a culinary journey across the Caribbean, exploring 8 flavorful rice and peas recipes that showcase the diversity and richness of the region.

  1. Jamaican Stewed Rice and Peas Located on the northern coast of Jamaica, the town of Port Antonio is famous for its spicy stewed peas and rice, which is often served with a side of fried fish. To make this hearty dish, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Then, sauté your peas with ginger, bell peppers, and a hint of curry powder before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside your favorite Jamaican dish. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped 1 tablespoon curry powder 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup coconut milk 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

  1. Trinidadian Curry Rice and Peas Trinidad and Tobago is a melting pot of cultures, with its cuisine reflecting the influences of Indian, African, and European traditions. To make this flavorful curry rice and peas, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and curry leaves. Then, sauté your peas with ginger, cumin, and coriander, followed by the addition of coconut cream and a sprinkle of cayenne pepper. Finish the dish with a handful of chopped cilantro and a squeeze of fresh lime juice. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 curry leaves 1 tablespoon cumin 1 tablespoon coriander 1/2 cup coconut cream 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt and pepper to taste Fresh cilantro, chopped Fresh lime juice

  1. Guyanese Hoppin' Rice and Peas Located on the northern coast of South America, Guyana is a British colony with a unique fusion of cuisines. To make this traditional Guyanese dish, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Then, sauté your peas with thyme, carrots, and a hint of nutmeg before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside a side of fried pepper pot soup. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped 1 tablespoon thyme 1 carrot, chopped 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg 1/2 cup coconut milk Salt to taste

  1. Bahamian Pea Jumbie and Rice The Bahamian archipelago is a British colony with a unique blend of African and European influences. To make this classic Bahamian dish, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and thyme. Then, sauté your peas with lemon juice, bell peppers, and a hint of cumin before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside a side of grilled fish. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon thyme 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1 bell pepper, chopped 1 teaspoon cumin 1/2 cup coconut milk Salt to taste

  1. Barbadian Stewed Rice and Green Peas Barbados is a small island nation located in the eastern Caribbean, with a cuisine that reflects its British colonial past. To make this classic Barbadian dish, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and thyme. Then, sauté your green peas with ginger, carrots, and a hint of nutmeg before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside a side of fried fish. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup green peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon thyme 2 inches ginger 1 carrot, chopped 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg 1/2 cup coconut milk Salt to taste

  1. Haitian Manioc Rice and Peas Located on the western Caribbean island of Hispaniola, Haiti is a French colony with a unique blend of African and European influences. To make this traditional Haitian dish, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Then, sauté your peas with coconut milk, cumin, and a hint of lime juice before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside a side of fried meat. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped 1/2 cup coconut milk 1 tablespoon cumin 1 tablespoon lime juice Salt to taste

  1. Dominica Ginger Rice and Peas Located on the northern Caribbean island of Dominica, this tiny nation is a Nature Island paradise with a cuisine that reflects its French, African, and British influences. To make this flavorful ginger rice and peas, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Then, sauté your peas with ginger, thyme, and a hint of curry powder before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside a side of grilled fish. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped 2 inches ginger 1 tablespoon thyme 1 tablespoon curry powder 1/2 cup coconut milk Salt to taste

  1. Aruban Coconut Rice and Peas Located in the southern Caribbean, Aruba is a tiny island nation with a unique fusion of cuisines. To make this creamy coconut rice and peas, start by cooking your rice with onions, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. Then, sauté your peas with coconut milk, cumin, and a hint of cayenne pepper before adding them to the rice. Finish the dish with a splash of coconut milk and a pinch of salt, and serve alongside a side of grilled meat. Ingredients:

1 cup rice 2 cups water 1 cup peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped 1 cup coconut milk 1 tablespoon cumin 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt to taste

Each of these Caribbean rice and peas recipes offers a unique twist on this classic comfort food. Whether you prefer the bold flavors of Jamaica or the delicate spices of Barbados, there's something for every palate on this culinary journey across the region.
