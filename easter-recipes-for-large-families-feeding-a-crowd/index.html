Easter Recipes for Large Families: Feeding a Crowd


Gathering a big family for Easter is a joyous occasion filled with laughter, stories, and, of course, a feast that accommodates every hungry guest. When the table is overflowing with relatives and friends, planning a meal that satisfies all tastes and dietary needs can feel daunting. The key is to craft a menu that’s not only delicious but also scalable, straightforward to prepare, and bursting with spring flavors that celebrate the spirit of the season.

In this comprehensive guide, you’ll find a curated selection of Easter recipes designed for large families—meals that bring comfort and elegance to the table while streamlining preparation. From succulent mains and vibrant sides to irresistible desserts, this post provides a roadmap to feeding a crowd with grace and gusto.

Planning a Large-Family Easter Menu: Tips and Tricks

Before diving into recipes, some strategic planning helps everything run smoothly:

  • Focus on Recipes That Scale Well: Choose dishes that can be easily increased in quantity without sacrificing taste or texture.
  • Opt for Make-Ahead or Slow-Cooker Options: These lighten the cooking load on Easter morning and free you up for family time.
  • Balance the Menu: Incorporate a balance of proteins, fresh vegetables, carbs, and sweets to satisfy diverse palates.
  • Account for Dietary Preferences: Include options that cater to vegetarians, gluten-free guests, or those with allergies.
  • Think About Presentation and Serving: Buffet-style layouts and easily portioned dishes keep traffic flowing and minimize fuss.

The Star of the Table: Main Course Ideas

1. Herb-Crusted Roasted Leg of Lamb (Feeds 10-12)

Lamb is a traditional Easter centerpiece, and roasting a leg to perfection is surprisingly approachable.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole leg of lamb (about 7-8 lbs), bone-in
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp thyme leaves
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and cracked black pepper

Method:

  1. Bring the lamb to room temperature, pat dry, and make small incisions to insert garlic slivers.
  2. Mix rosemary, thyme, lemon zest, olive oil, salt, and pepper into a paste.
  3. Rub the paste generously over the lamb.
  4. Roast in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for approximately 1.5 to 2 hours, depending on desired doneness.
  5. Let rest for 15 minutes before carving.

Serving tip: Pair with mint sauce or a red wine reduction for that extra layer of flavor.

2. Honey-Glazed Baked Ham (Feeds 15-20)

Ham is a crowd-pleaser and relatively easy to prepare in large quantities.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in spiral ham (about 10-12 lbs)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern.
  3. Mix honey, mustard, brown sugar, and vinegar to create the glaze.
  4. Place ham in a roasting pan, brush half the glaze, and cover loosely with foil.
  5. Bake for about 2 hours, basting every 20 minutes with remaining glaze in the last hour.
  6. Remove foil for the final 20 minutes to allow a caramelized crust to form.

3. Spring Vegetable & Ricotta Tart (Vegetarian, feeds 8-10)

A colorful tart offers a lighter alternative and adds an elegant touch to the Easter spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 sheets puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and roasted
  • 1 cup peas
  • 1 cup thinly sliced radishes
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Line a large tart/pizza pan with puff pastry, trim edges.
  2. Mix ricotta, parmesan, eggs, salt, and pepper. Spread evenly on pastry.
  3. Arrange roasted asparagus, peas, and radishes on top.
  4. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 25-30 minutes or until golden and puffed.

Serve warm or at room temperature for easy buffet-style serving.

Vibrant Sides to Complement Your Easter Feast

1. Roasted Spring Vegetables with Lemon and Thyme

Think baby carrots, fingerling potatoes, and snap peas, all kissed with bright citrus and fragrant thyme.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs baby carrots
  • 2 lbs fingerling potatoes, halved
  • 1 lb snap peas
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Toss potatoes and carrots with olive oil, lemon zest, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Roast at 400°F (200°C) for 30-40 minutes, turning halfway.
  3. During last 10 minutes, add snap peas to the pan for a slight roast.
  4. Finish with a squeeze of lemon juice before serving.

2. Classic Deviled Eggs (Feeds 12-16)

Deviled eggs are a staple for Easter, and making double or triple batches ensures no one misses out.

Ingredients:

  • 18 large eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • Paprika for garnish
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. Halve eggs and carefully remove yolks.
  2. Mash yolks with mayo, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pipe or spoon filling back into whites.
  4. Dust with paprika and chill until ready.

3. Spring Greens Salad with Goat Cheese and Candied Pecans

Add a fresh, crunchy side to counterbalance roasted meats.

Ingredients:

  • Mixed baby greens (arugula, spinach, watercress)
  • 4 oz goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup pecans, candied
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • Balsamic vinaigrette dressing

Delectable Desserts to End on a Sweet Note

1. Carrot Cake Sheet Pan with Cream Cheese Frosting

Ideal for feeding a crowd, this moist carrot cake can be prepared the day before.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups shredded carrots
  • 3 1/2 cups flour
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups chopped walnuts (optional)

For Frosting:

  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a large sheet pan.
  2. Mix all dry ingredients together.
  3. Whisk oil, eggs, and vanilla in a separate bowl.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients, fold in carrots and nuts.
  5. Pour batter into sheet pan; bake for 40-45 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Beat frosting ingredients until creamy and smooth.
  7. Cool cake completely before spreading frosting.

2. Lemon Blueberry Trifle

A no-bake layered dessert that looks stunning and serves many.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large pound cake or store-bought angel food cake, cubed
  • 2 cups lemon curd
  • 4 cups whipped cream (fresh or store-bought)
  • 3 cups fresh blueberries
  • Fresh mint for garnish

Method:

  1. Layer cake cubes, lemon curd, whipped cream, and blueberries in a large glass bowl or trifle dish.
  2. Repeat layers until ingredients are used.
  3. Top with whipped cream, extra berries, and mint leaves.
  4. Chill for at least an hour before serving.

Drinks to Toast the Occasion

  • Sparkling Lemonade with Fresh Herbs: Mix homemade or store-bought lemonade with sparkling water and a splash of elderflower syrup, garnish with rosemary or thyme.
  • Iced Mint Green Tea: Brew strong green tea, chill, and serve with fresh mint and lemon slices.
  • Classic Mimosas: Champagne with fresh orange juice is always a festive choice.

Final Thoughts on Feeding a Large Family

Easter is about coming together, sharing stories, and making memories. A meal that accommodates a big crowd doesn’t have to be stressful or complicated. By choosing recipes that scale easily, can be prepared ahead, and highlight seasonal ingredients, you create a feast that’s effortless and delightful.

Remember, the joy of a family gathering lies as much in the company and the laughter as it does in the food. With these recipes, you’ll serve both in generous measure—ensuring your Easter celebration is remembered for warmth, flavor, and abundant happiness.

Whether you’re hosting twenty or just ten, these Easter recipes for large families will help you create a festive spread full of vibrant tastes and comforting classics. Happy Easter and happy cooking!
