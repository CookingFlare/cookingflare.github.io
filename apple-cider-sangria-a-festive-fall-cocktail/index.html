Apple Cider Sangria: A Festive Fall Cocktail


Fall is the perfect season to indulge in cozy drinks that warm your soul and delight your taste buds. And what better way to celebrate the crisp autumn air than with a refreshing glass of Apple Cider Sangria? This festive cocktail combines the flavors of apple cider, fresh fruit, and a hint of spice to create a drink that is both comforting and invigorating. Whether you’re hosting a fall gathering or simply want to enjoy a special treat, this Apple Cider Sangria recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups apple cider
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup brandy
  • 1/4 cup orange liqueur
  • 1 apple, thinly sliced
  • 1 pear, thinly sliced
  • 1 orange, thinly sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 whole cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Ice cubes

Instructions

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the apple cider, white wine, brandy, and orange liqueur. Stir well to combine.

  2. Add the apple slices, pear slices, and orange slices to the pitcher. Gently muddle the fruit with a wooden spoon to release their natural juices.

  3. Add the cinnamon stick, cloves, nutmeg, and sugar to the pitcher. Stir well to dissolve the sugar and infuse the flavors.

  4. Place the pitcher in the refrigerator and let the sangria chill for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. When ready to serve, fill glasses with ice cubes and pour the chilled Apple Cider Sangria over the ice. Garnish with additional apple slices or cinnamon sticks, if desired.

  6. Sip and savor the delightful combination of sweet apple cider, tangy fruit, and aromatic spices. The crispness of the apple and pear slices complements the warmth of the cinnamon and cloves, creating a truly harmonious blend of flavors.

  7. This Apple Cider Sangria is the perfect addition to any fall gathering or cozy night in. Its vibrant colors and enticing aroma will set the mood for a memorable experience.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of autumn flavor, consider adding a splash of maple syrup to the sangria mixture. The rich, sweet taste will complement the apple cider beautifully.

  • If you prefer a non-alcoholic version, simply omit the white wine, brandy, and orange liqueur. Increase the amount of apple cider to compensate for the missing liquids.

  • Experiment with different types of fruit to personalize your Apple Cider Sangria. Pomegranate seeds, cranberries, or even sliced figs can add a unique twist to the recipe.

  • To make a large batch of Apple Cider Sangria for a crowd, simply double or triple the ingredients and adjust the chilling time accordingly.

Conclusion

Apple Cider Sangria is the epitome of fall in a glass. Its combination of seasonal flavors and vibrant colors make it a festive addition to any autumn gathering. With the crispness of the apple and pear slices, the warmth of cinnamon and cloves, and the sweetness of apple cider, this cocktail is a true delight for the senses. So, gather your loved ones, pour a glass of this delightful concoction, and raise a toast to the beauty of fall.
