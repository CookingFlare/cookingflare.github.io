How to Make a Delicious Philly Cheesesteak Sandwich


The Philly cheesesteak sandwich is a culinary icon that hails from the streets of Philadelphia. Known for its rich flavors and satisfying textures, this sandwich has become a beloved dish across the United States and beyond. Whether you’re a lifelong fan or trying it for the first time, making a delicious Philly cheesesteak at home is easier than you might think. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a mouthwatering cheesesteak that rivals any deli in Philly.

The History of the Philly Cheesesteak

Before diving into the recipe, it’s worth exploring the origins of this iconic sandwich. The Philly cheesesteak was invented in the 1930s by Pat and Harry Olivieri, who ran a hot dog stand in Philadelphia. One day, they decided to grill some beef and serve it on an Italian roll. The sandwich quickly gained popularity, and soon, local patrons were flocking to their stand for a taste. Today, the cheesesteak is a staple of Philadelphia cuisine, with countless variations and styles.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make an authentic Philly cheesesteak, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Sandwich

  • 1 pound ribeye steak (thinly sliced)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 green bell pepper (sliced, optional)
  • 4 slices provolone cheese (or Cheez Whiz for a classic touch)
  • 4 hoagie rolls (preferably Amoroso’s or any soft Italian rolls)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Olive oil (for cooking)

Optional Toppings

  • Sautéed mushrooms
  • Hot peppers
  • Ketchup or mayonnaise (for serving)

Equipment You’ll Need

  • Skillet or griddle (preferably cast iron)
  • Sharp knife (for slicing the steak)
  • Cutting board
  • Spatula
  • Tongs

Preparing the Ingredients

Choosing the Right Steak

The key to a great Philly cheesesteak is the meat. Ribeye steak is the traditional choice due to its marbling and tenderness. When selecting your ribeye, look for a piece that has a good amount of fat, as this will add flavor and juiciness to your sandwich. If you can’t find ribeye, sirloin or flank steak can be used as alternatives, but they may not be as tender.

Slicing the Steak

For the best results, the steak should be sliced thinly. To make this easier, place the ribeye in the freezer for about 30 minutes before slicing. This will firm up the meat, making it easier to cut. Use a sharp knife to slice the steak against the grain into thin strips, about 1/8 inch thick.

Preparing the Vegetables

While the steak is chilling, slice the onion and bell pepper (if using). The vegetables will add sweetness and crunch to the sandwich, balancing the richness of the meat and cheese. You can also prepare any additional toppings you’d like, such as sautéed mushrooms or hot peppers.

Cooking the Philly Cheesesteak

Step 1: Sauté the Vegetables

Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and bell peppers, and sauté them until they are soft and caramelized, about 5-7 minutes. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper to enhance the flavors. Once cooked, remove the vegetables from the skillet and set them aside.

Step 2: Cook the Steak

In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of olive oil and increase the heat to medium-high. Add the thinly sliced ribeye steak to the skillet in a single layer. Avoid overcrowding the pan; you may need to cook the steak in batches. Season the meat with salt and pepper.

Cook the steak for about 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it is browned and cooked through. The goal is to achieve a nice sear while keeping the meat tender. If you prefer your steak well done, you can cook it a little longer.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

Once the steak is cooked, return the sautéed onions and bell peppers to the skillet. Mix everything together, allowing the flavors to meld for an additional minute. If you’re using Cheez Whiz, you can add it at this stage, stirring until it’s melted and evenly distributed. If you prefer provolone cheese, place the slices on top of the steak and cover the skillet with a lid for a minute or two until the cheese is melted.

Step 4: Toast the Hoagie Rolls

While the steak and cheese are finishing up, it’s time to prepare the hoagie rolls. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Slice the rolls lengthwise, but don’t cut all the way through.
