Homemade Garlic Bread Knots: A Flavorful Side Dish

3 min · 543 words

Garlic bread is a classic side dish that perfectly complements any meal. But have you ever tried making your own garlic bread knots? These homemade garlic bread knots are not only delicious, but they also add a touch of elegance to your table. They are soft, garlicky, and full of flavor. In this recipe, I will guide you through the process of making these delectable knots from scratch. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt, for sprinkling

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Make a well in the center and add the yeast.

  2. Pour the warm water into the well and let it sit for 5 minutes, or until the yeast becomes foamy.

  3. Add the olive oil to the mixture and stir until a dough forms.

  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  6. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).

  7. Punch down the dough and divide it into small portions. Roll each portion into a long rope, about 10 inches in length.

  8. Tie each rope into a knot, tucking the ends underneath.

  9. Place the knots onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving some space between each knot.

  10. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic and melted butter.

  11. Brush the garlic butter mixture over the knots, making sure to coat them evenly.

  12. Sprinkle the chopped parsley and a pinch of salt over the knots for added flavor.

  13. Bake the knots in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes, or until they turn golden brown.

  14. Remove the knots from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

  15. Serve the homemade garlic bread knots warm as a flavorful side dish to accompany your favorite pasta, soup, or main course.

Tips and Variations

  • For a cheesy twist, you can sprinkle grated Parmesan or mozzarella cheese over the knots before baking.

  • To add some heat, you can mix a pinch of red pepper flakes into the garlic butter mixture.

  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor of your garlic bread knots. Basil, oregano, or even a sprinkle of paprika can add an extra layer of deliciousness.

  • If you prefer a softer crust, you can brush the knots with melted butter immediately after removing them from the oven.

  • These garlic bread knots are best enjoyed fresh, but you can store any leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days.

Now that you have the recipe for these homemade garlic bread knots, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! These knots are not only a delightful side dish but also a crowd-pleasing appetizer. So next time you’re looking to impress your guests or simply indulge in a flavorful treat, give these garlic bread knots a try. You won’t be disappointed!
