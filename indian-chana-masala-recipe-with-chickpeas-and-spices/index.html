Indian Chana Masala Recipe with Chickpeas and Spices


Chana Masala is a popular Indian dish made with chickpeas and a blend of aromatic spices. It is a hearty and flavorful dish that can be enjoyed as a main course or as a side dish. In this recipe, we will explore how to make authentic Chana Masala from scratch, using simple ingredients that are readily available in your pantry.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 medium-sized onions (finely chopped)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit lengthwise)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves (for garnishing)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse them thoroughly under cold water. In a pressure cooker, add the rinsed chickpeas along with enough water to cover them. Pressure cook the chickpeas for about 20-25 minutes until they are soft and tender. Set aside.

  2. Heat oil in a large pan or skillet over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them splutter. Then, add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan. Sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Now, add the finely chopped tomatoes and slit green chilies to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and mushy, stirring occasionally.

  5. Reduce the heat to low and add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to combine the spices with the tomato-onion mixture.

  6. Cook the spice mixture for a minute or two until the flavors are well blended.

  7. Add the cooked chickpeas along with the water (from pressure cooking) to the pan. Stir well to coat the chickpeas with the spice mixture.

  8. Cover the pan and let the Chana Masala simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  9. Sprinkle garam masala over the Chana Masala and give it a final stir. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences.

  10. Garnish the Chana Masala with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, naan bread, or roti. Squeeze some lemon juice on top for an extra zing.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have a pressure cooker, you can cook the chickpeas in a regular pot. Just simmer them in water for about 1-2 hours until they are tender.
  • For a creamier texture, you can mash a few chickpeas with the back of a spoon during the cooking process.
  • Feel free to adjust the spice levels according to your taste. Add more or less chili powder and green chilies as per your preference.
  • You can also add other vegetables like spinach or bell peppers to enhance the nutritional value and taste of the dish.
  • Leftover Chana Masala can be refrigerated and enjoyed the next day. The flavors tend to deepen and intensify with time.

Chana Masala is a versatile dish that can be customized to suit your taste. It is a wholesome and delicious meal that will leave you craving for more. So, go ahead and try this authentic Indian recipe in your kitchen. Your taste buds will thank you!
