10 International Appetizer Recipes to Spice Up Your Menu


When it comes to hosting a dinner party or gathering with friends, the key to a successful evening is often the appetizers. A delicious and well-curated selection of small plates can set the tone for a memorable and enjoyable experience. But, with so many options out there, it can be difficult to decide which recipes to try. That’s why we’ve put together this list of 10 international appetizer recipes to spice up your menu.

1. Bruschetta Italiano

Inspired by the flavors of Italy, this classic appetizer is a crowd-pleaser. Toasted bread rubbed with garlic and topped with diced tomatoes, basil, and mozzarella cheese is a simple yet elegant combination. To take it to the next level, drizzle with a high-quality extra virgin olive oil and a sprinkle of salt.

2. Spring Rolls with Peanut Sauce

Take a trip to Southeast Asia with these crispy spring rolls filled with shredded vegetables and served with a creamy peanut sauce. The combination of textures and flavors is addictive, and the sauce can be made ahead of time to simplify preparation.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package spring roll wrappers
  • 1/2 cup shredded vegetables (carrots, cucumber, bell pepper)
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Fill spring roll wrappers with shredded vegetables and seal.
  2. Fry in hot oil until crispy and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a blender or food processor, combine peanut butter, soy sauce, honey, and water. Blend until smooth.
  4. Serve spring rolls with peanut sauce for dipping.

3. Empanadas with Chimichurri

These savory pastries from Argentina are filled with ground beef and onions, and topped with a tangy chimichurri sauce. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the empanadas can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package empanada dough
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chimichurri sauce (see below)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Chimichurri Sauce:

  • 1 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 cup chopped oregano
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Roll out empanada dough and cut into squares.
  3. Fill squares with ground beef and onion mixture, and seal edges.
  4. Brush with egg wash and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  5. Serve with chimichurri sauce for dipping.

4. Falafel with Tahini Sauce

Inspired by the flavors of the Middle East, these crispy falafel are served with a creamy tahini sauce and a side of pita bread. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the falafel can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tahini sauce (see below)

Tahini Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, parsley, cilantro, garlic, flour, and breadcrumbs. Process until coarsely chopped.
  3. Shape into patties and brush with olive oil.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until crispy and golden brown.
  5. Serve with tahini sauce for dipping.

5. Samosas with Chutney

Inspired by the flavors of India, these crispy samosas are filled with spiced potatoes and peas, and served with a tangy chutney. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the samosas can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package samosa wrappers
  • 1 cup cooked potatoes
  • 1/2 cup cooked peas
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chutney (see below)

Chutney:

  • 1 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 cup chopped green chilies
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Fill samosa wrappers with potato and pea mixture, and seal edges.
  2. Fry in hot oil until crispy and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a blender or food processor, combine cilantro, green chilies, onion, lemon juice, and water. Blend until smooth.
  4. Serve samosas with chutney for dipping.

6. Quesadillas with Guacamole

Inspired by the flavors of Mexico, these crispy quesadillas are filled with melted cheese and served with a creamy guacamole. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the quesadillas can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Guacamole (see below)

Guacamole:

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1/2 red onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Place tortillas on a baking sheet and sprinkle with cheese.
  3. Fold tortillas in half and brush with ghee or oil.
  4. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until crispy and melted.
  5. Serve with guacamole for dipping.

7. Spring Onion Pancakes with Dipping Sauce

Inspired by the flavors of Japan, these crispy spring onion pancakes are served with a tangy dipping sauce. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the pancakes can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup grated ginger
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Dipping sauce (see below)

Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, cornstarch, scallions, cilantro, and ginger.
  2. Gradually add water and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead dough for 5-10 minutes, or until smooth.
  4. Divide dough into small balls and roll out into thin circles.
  5. Fry in hot oil until crispy and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve with dipping sauce for dipping.

8. Grilled Halloumi with Honey and Thyme

Inspired by the flavors of Greece, this grilled halloumi cheese is served with a drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of thyme. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the halloumi can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block halloumi cheese
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Slice halloumi cheese into thin slices.
  3. Grill for 2-3 minutes per side, or until crispy and golden brown.
  4. Drizzle with honey and sprinkle with thyme.
  5. Serve immediately.

9. Steamed Dumplings with Soy Sauce

Inspired by the flavors of China, these steamed dumplings are filled with pork and vegetables, and served with a savory soy sauce. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the dumplings can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package dumpling wrappers
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground pork, cabbage, scallions, and soy sauce.
  2. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix.
  3. Place dumpling wrappers on a clean surface and place a tablespoon of filling in the center of each wrapper.
  4. Fold wrappers into triangles and press edges together to seal.
  5. Steam for 10-15 minutes, or until cooked through.
  6. Serve with soy sauce for dipping.

10. Crostini with Goat Cheese and Fig Jam

Inspired by the flavors of France, these crispy crostini are topped with creamy goat cheese and sweet fig jam. The combination of flavors and textures is a winner, and the crostini can be made ahead of time and reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese
  • 1/4 cup fig jam
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Slice baguette into thin slices and place on a baking sheet.
  3. Top each slice with a dollop of goat cheese and a spoonful of fig jam.
  4. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Serve immediately.

With these 10 international appetizer recipes, you’ll be well on your way to creating a menu that will impress your friends and family. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just want to spice up your regular menu, these recipes are sure to be a hit. So go ahead, get creative, and bon appétit!
