Italian Cooking Hacks: Tips and Tricks to Streamline YourKitchen


Are you tired of spending hours in the kitchen, trying to perfect your Italian recipes? Or perhaps you’re yearning for some efficiency, without sacrificing the authentic flavors Italy is known for? In this blog post, we’re diving headfirst into the world of Italian cooking hacks, all designed to bring out the best in your culinary creations. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

First up, we’ve got marinara sauce – freaking amazing when imported directly from Italy, but have you ever considered making it at home? Ah yes, we can see that puzzled look on your face. Fear not, for we’ve got a fantastic two-ingredient hack. And guess what? Those ingredients are tomato sauce and water! As the mixture gently simmers in a saucepan, slowly introduce the water, a little at a time, until you achieve the desired consistency. Trust us, your sauce will taste remarkably similar to those delightful, sun-kissed recipes you can find in the Mediterranean. For an extra piquant twist, include a pinch of dried oregano or a dash of dried basil while sautéing.

Feeling ambitious enough to tinker with the classics? Give your homemade pasta a new depth of flavor in just 10 minutes! Yeah, you read that right! Simply sprinkle your dry pasta with some fine sea salt, then go ahead and cover it all with boiling water. Cook for about nine minutes, or until your pasta is al dente, then drain well. But wait, there’s more! Strain a little of the pasta cooking water into a bowl and whisk in some extra-virgin olive oil. This infused olive oil is perfect for drizzling over your cooked pasta, along with any sauce. It not only brings every flavor to life but also prevents your pasta from becoming a sticky, chewy mess.

Feeling overwhelmed with agreeing each serving size on food and ingredients? Well, here’s some mind-blowing news. Transfer all your frustration into a food scale, weigh your chosen protein (be it chicken, beef, or fish) and let your pasta weigh half of that. Isn’t that so simple and satisfying? The same ratio applies to portions: weigh the same amount for each person, making your life so much easier when hosting dinner parties!

Now, let’s talk cheese, specifically, how to store ricotta. For many, storing ricotta proves to be a real pickle. But fret not, for we have the ideal solution. Spoon your ricotta into airtight containers and press lightly with a spatula to release any trapped air. Drizzle a thin layer of olive oil over the top to create a barrier between the cheese and air. This will maintain its freshness for up to five days in your refrigerator. What’s more, you can use the leftover olive oil for your salad dressing!

Isn’t everyone a sucker for perfectly cooked risotto grains? Here’s a handy tip to nail that texture: constantly stirring the risotto in opposite directions. This ensures even cooking and makes each grain tender and delicious. Plus, leaving your risotto on the heat too long is a culinary taboo. The best way to avoid that is by removing the pan from the heat when the rice is still slightly al dente.

Let’s face it; the term “organize” and “Italian kitchen” don’t usually go hand-in-hand. So, here’s a life-changing tip – invest in those clear, plastic food storage containers. Keep them on the counter and clearly label each with an ingredient name, cooking method, and date. No more “pantry raiding” with this hack, and you’ll never forget which half-eaten package of farro you bought six months ago!

Last but not least, how to you roll out pasta dough without cracking your back and lifting floors? News flash: the solution to your torment is right there in the skillet! Simply roll out your freshly-prepared dough over the warm skillet, working from the center outward. In no time, you’ll have perfectly shaped, even pasta ready for sauce.

There you have it – a selection of Italian cooking hacks, tips, and tricks (by accident, not design) to revolutionize your kitchen and streamline your culinary creations. With these brilliant hacks up your sleeve, you’ll be dishing up authentic pasta as if you were transported directly from your home to the heart of Tuscany. Buon appetito!
