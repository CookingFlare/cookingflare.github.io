How to Prepare Perfectly Flaky Mexican Empanadas


Have you ever bit into an empanada filled with tender beef, gooey cheese, and a hint of spices, only to find the pastry crumby and flavorless? We've all been there. Making perfectly flaky empanadas can be a daunting task, but with a few simple tips and tricks, you can become an empanada expert in no time. The Art of Empanada Making Empanadas have been a staple in Mexican cuisine for centuries, filled with everything from chicken to fish, and even sweet fillings like strawberries and cream. However, the art of making these savory pastries is both time-consuming and requires patience. The key to a perfectly flaky empanada is the dough, which requires careful mixing and rolling to achieve that golden, flaky texture. Ingredients To make your empanada dough from scratch, you'll need the following ingredients:

3 cups all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon salt 1 tablespoon granulated sugar 1/2 cup lard or vegetable shortening 1 large egg, beaten 1/4 cup warm water

For the beef filling, you'll need:

1 pound ground beef 1 medium onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon cumin 1 teaspoon paprika Salt and pepper to taste 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Step 1: Make the Empanada Dough In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and lard or vegetable shortening. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the fat into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

Gradually add the beaten egg, mixing with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Gradually add the warm water, mixing with a fork until the dough is smooth and elastic. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it is smooth and pliable. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Make the Beef Filling In a large pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.

Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes. Add the ground beef, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until the beef is evenly browned. Stir in the chopped cilantro and cook for an additional 1 minute.

Step 3: Assemble the Empanadas Divide the dough into 8-10 equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a thin circle, about 1/8 inch thick.

Place a tablespoon or two of the beef filling in the center of the dough circle. Fold the dough over the filling, forming a half-moon shape. Press the edges together to seal the empanada. Use a fork to crimp the edges and create a decorative border.

Step 4: Fry the Empanadas Heat 2-3 inches of oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Fry the empanadas for 2-3 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown. Step 5: Bake the Empanadas Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown. Step 6: Serve Serve the empanadas warm, garnished with additional cilantro if desired. You can also serve them alongside a side of salsa, guacamole, or sour cream for a delicious and authentic Mexican experience. Tips for Flaky Empanadas

Make sure to keep the dough cold, as this will help to create a flaky texture. Don't overwork the dough, as this can make it tough and chewy. Use a light touch when rolling out the dough, as too much pressure can cause it to tear. Use a fork to crimp the edges of the empanada, as this will help to create a flaky texture. Don't overcrowd the oil when frying the empanadas, as this can cause them to stick together.

Conclusion Making perfectly flaky empanadas is a true culinary art. With these simple tips and tricks, you can create delicious, flaky empanadas that are sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you're serving them at a dinner party or a family gathering, empanadas are sure to be a hit.
