Soft and Fluffy Potato Rolls: Perfect for Dinner


Creating the perfect dinner isn’t just about having a delicious main course. It’s also about the side dishes, the bread, and anything that takes the meal to the next level. So, how about trying something unique yet delightful? Introducing potato rolls - the soft, fluffy, and delicious counterpart to any meal!

Before we dive into the recipe proper, it’s essential to understand why potato rolls are special. Unlike traditional rolls, potato rolls include mashed potatoes as a significant ingredient. This seemingly simple change brings about a world of difference in the final product. The potatoes - rich in starch and fluffy in texture - help the rolls retain their soft and springy character even after the bread cools down.

Now, let’s get straight into preparing these delightful potato rolls.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for kneading
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and boiled (about 2 cups of mashed potatoes)
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine warm water, honey, and yeast. Let them sit for about five minutes, until foamy. This mixture is what we call ‘starter.’ It provides the necessary food for the yeast to grow and helps with fermentation.

  2. While the yeast mixture is working, prepare the potatoes. Boil and mash two large potatoes until smooth. Allow them to cool sufficiently, then mix them in a separate bowl with 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter.

  3. Once the yeast mixture has reached a good level of foaminess, mix the mashed potatoes into the yeast starter. Keep the mixture light and combine it with 2 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 tablespoon of salt.

  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth and elastic. You may need extra flour, so don’t be shy about adding more as needed.

  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise in a warm spot for about an hour or until doubled in size. Keep in mind: yeast loves warmth, so the better the spot, the better the rolls.

  6. Deflate the risen dough gently and divide it into the number of rolls you want - say, 12. Shape them into smooth rolls and place them into a buttered baking dish. You may also use a baking sheet, but make sure to line it with parchment paper to prevent the rolls from sticking.

  7. Allow the shaped rolls to rise again for about 20 minutes.

  8. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the rolls for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.

  9. Immediately remove from the oven to keep the rolls soft and fluffy. Serve your potato rolls with anything from soups, stews to your favorite sandwiches, and enjoy the wonderful and pleasant burst of flavors!

While simple in their nature, potato rolls perfectly complement any dinner, whether as sandwiches or served alongside soup, stews, and even barbecue dishes. So, why not add some warmth and joy to your next meal by taking a chance on the versatile, delectable world of potato rolls? Happy cooking!
