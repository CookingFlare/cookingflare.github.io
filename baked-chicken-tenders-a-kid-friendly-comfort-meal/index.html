Baked Chicken Tenders: A Kid-Friendly Comfort Meal


Chicken tenders are a classic comfort food that both kids and adults love. They are crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and perfect for dipping into all sorts of delicious sauces. While fried chicken tenders are a popular choice, baking them is a healthier alternative that doesn’t compromise on taste. In this recipe, we will show you how to make crispy and flavorful baked chicken tenders that your whole family will enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of chicken tenders
  • 1 cup of breadcrumbs (preferably panko)
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 eggs, beaten

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil.

  2. In a shallow dish, combine the breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

  3. Dip each chicken tender into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off, then coat it in the breadcrumb mixture. Press the chicken tender firmly into the mixture to ensure it is fully coated.

  4. Place the coated chicken tenders onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a little space between each one. This will allow them to bake evenly and become crispy on all sides.

  5. Bake the chicken tenders for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. You can test for doneness by cutting into one of the tenders to ensure there is no pink inside.

  6. Once the chicken tenders are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. This will help them retain their crispiness.

  7. Serve the baked chicken tenders with your favorite dipping sauces, such as ketchup, barbecue sauce, or honey mustard. You can also pair them with a side of fries or a fresh salad for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For extra crispy chicken tenders, you can spray them lightly with cooking spray before baking. This will help them brown and become even more crunchy.

  • To add more flavor, you can experiment with different spices and herbs. Try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper for a spicy kick or some dried rosemary for a fragrant twist.

  • If you want to make a larger batch of chicken tenders, simply double or triple the recipe. You can freeze any leftovers for a quick and easy meal later on.

  • If you don’t have chicken tenders, you can use chicken breasts instead. Just cut them into strips of similar size to create your own chicken tenders.

  • Get the kids involved in the preparation process! Let them help with dipping the chicken tenders into the breadcrumb mixture or whisking the eggs. It’s a fun and interactive way to get them excited about cooking.

Baked chicken tenders are a versatile and delicious meal that the whole family will enjoy. Whether you’re serving them for dinner, a party, or a quick snack, these crispy treats are sure to be a hit. So, go ahead and give this recipe a try – you won’t be disappointed!
