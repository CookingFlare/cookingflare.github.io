Authentic Chinese Mango Pudding Recipe


Mango pudding is a beloved dessert that graces many Asian tables, especially in Chinese cuisine. This creamy and smooth dessert is bursting with the natural sweetness of ripe mangoes, offering a refreshing conclusion to a hearty meal. Whether you’re preparing for a special occasion or simply want to indulge in a fruity treat, this authentic Chinese mango pudding recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings.

What is Mango Pudding?

Mango pudding is a sweet, velvety dessert that combines mango puree with a gelatinous or custard-like consistency. Popular in Cantonese cuisine, it’s often served at the end of a meal in Chinese restaurants, particularly dim sum establishments. The bright yellow hue and tropical flavor make it an irresistible choice, and it’s an easy-to-make dish that requires only a few ingredients.

Unlike the Western version of mango mousse, Chinese mango pudding has a thicker texture, often achieved through the use of gelatin or agar-agar, giving it a custard-like consistency. The base of the pudding usually includes fresh mangoes, coconut milk, sugar, and a setting agent. The best part? It’s a no-bake dessert, which means it’s quick to prepare and can be made ahead of time!

Ingredients for Authentic Chinese Mango Pudding

To make a truly authentic Chinese mango pudding, fresh mangoes are essential. It’s important to choose ripe, sweet mangoes for the best flavor. Here’s what you’ll need for this delicious dessert:

  • 2 large ripe mangoes (about 450g to 500g)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (full-fat or light, depending on your preference)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste, depending on the sweetness of your mangoes)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 teaspoons agar-agar powder (or gelatin if you prefer)
  • 2 tablespoons milk (whole milk or dairy-free alternatives)
  • A pinch of salt (to balance sweetness)
  • Optional garnishes: Fresh mango cubes, whipped cream, or coconut flakes for decoration

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Mango Pudding

Now, let’s walk through the steps to create this velvety, tropical dessert. The process is simple and doesn’t require advanced cooking skills, making it a perfect option for beginner cooks and experienced chefs alike.

1. Prepare the Mangoes

Start by peeling the mangoes and cutting them into small cubes. Discard the pit and any tough fibers. Place the mango cubes in a blender or food processor and blend them into a smooth puree. Depending on the size of your mangoes, you should have about 1 1/2 to 2 cups of mango puree. Set aside for later use.

2. Dissolve the Agar-Agar

Agar-agar is a plant-based gelling agent, which is commonly used in Asian desserts. If you’re using gelatin instead, the process will be slightly different, but the result will be the same.

In a small saucepan, combine the agar-agar powder with 1/2 cup of water. Stir gently, and bring it to a boil over medium heat. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 3-4 minutes, or until the agar-agar is completely dissolved. Be sure to stir constantly to prevent any lumps from forming. Once dissolved, remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

3. Combine the Mango Puree and Coconut Milk

In a large mixing bowl, combine the freshly made mango puree with the coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Stir the mixture until the sugar is dissolved. The coconut milk adds a rich, creamy texture that enhances the tropical flavor of the mangoes, making it an essential ingredient in this dish.

4. Add the Agar-Agar Mixture

Slowly pour the dissolved agar-agar mixture into the mango-coconut mixture. Stir well to ensure that the agar-agar is evenly incorporated. The warm agar-agar will help everything blend together smoothly. At this point, you should have a creamy, slightly runny mixture that will thicken as it cools.

5. Pour into Molds

Now, it’s time to transfer the mango mixture into your desired molds. You can use small individual molds, a large baking dish, or even silicone molds for fun shapes. If you’re planning to serve the pudding in a more elegant way, small glass cups or bowls work wonderfully as well. Make sure to leave a little space at the top of each mold for easy removal later.

6. Let it Set

Once you’ve poured the mixture into your molds, place them in the refrigerator to set. Allow the mango pudding to chill for at least 4 hours, or until it has completely firmed up. This step is essential for achieving that perfect, pudding-like texture. If you’re in a rush, you can also speed up the process by placing the molds in the freezer for 1-2 hours.

7. Serve and Garnish

Once the pudding has fully set, remove it from the fridge. If you used silicone molds or cups, gently run a knife around the edges of the pudding to help release it from the mold. You can invert the molds onto a plate or serve them directly in the bowls. For an extra touch of elegance, top your mango pudding with fresh mango cubes, whipped cream, or even a sprinkle of toasted coconut flakes.

Tips for the Perfect Mango Pudding

  1. Choose Ripe Mangoes: The sweetness and flavor of your mango pudding depend on the quality of the mangoes. Make sure your mangoes are fully ripe, soft to the touch, and bursting with flavor.

  2. Adjust Sweetness: If your mangoes are extra sweet, feel free to reduce the sugar amount. If they’re less ripe, you may want to increase the sugar a little to balance the tartness.

  3. Vegan Option: To make a vegan version, simply substitute the milk and coconut milk for plant-based alternatives like almond milk or oat milk. Agar-agar is already vegan-friendly, so this substitution will work perfectly.

  4. Make it Ahead: Mango pudding is a fantastic dessert to prepare in advance. You can make it a day or two ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator until you’re ready to serve.

  5. Texture Variations: If you prefer a smoother, creamier texture, you can increase the amount of coconut milk or use full-fat coconut milk. If you want the pudding to have a firmer texture, slightly increase the agar-agar amount.

  6. Fresh Mango Topping: For an added burst of mango flavor, use fresh mango cubes as a topping right before serving. This adds a fresh and juicy contrast to the creamy pudding.

Why is Mango Pudding So Popular?

Mango pudding is more than just a dessert—it’s a cultural icon in Chinese cuisine. Its vibrant color and tropical flavors evoke a sense of summer and freshness, making it a favorite in restaurants, especially after a hearty meal like dim sum or hotpot.

Mangoes are also associated with good fortune and prosperity in many cultures, particularly in Asia, making this dessert an excellent choice for celebrations and special occasions. Whether it’s a Chinese New Year gathering or a casual family dinner, mango pudding adds a touch of elegance to any table.

Variations of Mango Pudding

While the basic recipe for mango pudding is quite simple, there are many ways you can personalize it to suit your tastes or dietary preferences.

  1. Mango Coconut Pudding: For those who love the tropical combination of mango and coconut, adding extra coconut milk or even shredded coconut to the pudding base will deepen the flavor.

  2. Mango Agar-Agar Bars: If you prefer a firmer version of the pudding that’s easier to slice and serve, you can increase the amount of agar-agar and pour the mixture into a large baking dish, allowing it to set into bars.

  3. Mango Lassi-Inspired Pudding: For a tangy twist, try adding a bit of yogurt to the pudding mix, which will give it a flavor reminiscent of mango lassi, the popular Indian yogurt-based drink.

  4. Tropical Fruit Salad Pudding: Serve the mango pudding with an assortment of tropical fruits, such as papaya, pineapple, and kiwi, to create a fresh fruit salad that complements the sweetness of the pudding.

Health Benefits of Mangoes in Pudding

Apart from being delicious, mangoes offer numerous health benefits. Packed with vitamins, fiber, and antioxidants, mangoes are not only a tasty tropical fruit but also a nutritious one. Mangoes are an excellent source of Vitamin C, which supports your immune system, skin health, and overall well-being.

Additionally, mangoes are rich in beta-carotene, which can help protect your vision and promote healthy skin. Their high fiber content supports digestion, while antioxidants like lutein and zeaxanthin contribute to heart health.

Although the pudding contains sugar, the natural sugars in the mangoes provide a healthier, more natural sweetness compared to processed sugars, making it a guilt-free treat.

Conclusion

Chinese mango pudding is a classic dessert that captures the essence of tropical flavors in every bite. Its rich, smooth texture and sweet, fruity taste make it an irresistible treat that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re preparing it for a festive celebration or enjoying it as an everyday dessert, this recipe will surely impress your guests and leave them wanting more.

So, gather your ingredients, prepare this delightful dessert, and experience the refreshing taste of authentic Chinese mango pudding today!
