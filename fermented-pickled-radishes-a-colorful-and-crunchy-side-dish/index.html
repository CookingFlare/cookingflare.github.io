Fermented Pickled Radishes: A Colorful and Crunchy Side Dish


Fermented pickled radishes are a flavorful and nutritious side dish that can elevate any meal. The process of fermentation brings out the natural sweetness of the radishes, while the pickling liquid adds a tangy and crunchy texture. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make fermented pickled radishes at home, using simple ingredients and minimal equipment.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) water
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat)
  • 1/4 cup (15g) chopped fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)

Equipment

  • A large glass jar with a wide mouth (at least 1 quart/1 liter capacity)
  • A fermentation weight or a small plate
  • Cheesecloth or a clean, thin towel
  • A rubber band or string

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Radishes: Wash the radishes thoroughly, and then slice them into thin rounds. Remove any leaves or stems, as they can be bitter. Place the sliced radishes in a colander and sprinkle with salt. Let them sit for 30 minutes to allow the salt to draw out excess moisture.
  2. Create the Brine: In a small saucepan, combine the water, vinegar, sugar, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, stirring occasionally. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the sugar and salt have dissolved.
  3. Assemble the Fermentation Jar: Rinse the fermentation jar with hot water and dry it thoroughly. Pack the sliced radishes into the jar, leaving about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of space at the top. Pour the hot brine over the radishes, making sure they are completely covered.
  4. Add the Fermentation Weight: Place the fermentation weight or a small plate on top of the radishes to keep them submerged under the brine. Cover the jar with cheesecloth or a clean, thin towel, securing it with a rubber band or string.
  5. Ferment the Pickles: Store the jar in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or cupboard. The fermentation process usually takes 3-5 days, depending on the temperature and the desired level of sourness. Check the pickles daily to ensure they are fermenting properly. You can check for fermentation by tasting the pickles or by looking for bubbles on the surface of the brine.
  6. Store the Pickles: Once the pickles have reached the desired level of sourness, remove the fermentation weight and cover the jar with a tight-fitting lid. Store the pickles in the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. They will keep for several months in the fridge.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense flavor, you can increase the amount of sugar or vinegar in the brine.
  • To add some crunch, you can include some sliced carrots or daikon radish in the fermentation jar.
  • For a spicy kick, you can add some sliced jalapeños or serrano peppers to the brine.
  • Fermented pickled radishes make a great topping for tacos, salads, or sandwiches. You can also use them as a side dish for grilled meats or as a crunchy snack on their own.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled radishes are a delicious and healthy addition to any meal. With this simple recipe, you can create a flavorful and crunchy side dish that’s perfect for any occasion. So why not give it a try and experience the tangy, sweet, and sour flavors of fermented pickled radishes for yourself?
