Homemade Peanut Butter Cups: Better than Store-Bought

3 min · 567 words

If you’re a fan of peanut butter and chocolate, then you’re in for a treat! Today, I’m going to share with you my recipe for homemade peanut butter cups that are so delicious, they’ll put store-bought ones to shame. These sweet treats are incredibly easy to make and require only a few simple ingredients. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • ¼ cup milk chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Sea salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized mixing bowl, combine the creamy peanut butter, powdered sugar, and melted unsalted butter. Mix until well combined and smooth. This will be the filling for our peanut butter cups.

  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the semisweet chocolate chips and milk chocolate chips. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring after each interval, until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth. Be careful not to overheat the chocolate as it can burn easily.

  3. Add the vegetable oil to the melted chocolate and stir until well combined. The vegetable oil will help give the chocolate a smooth and glossy finish.

  4. Line a muffin tin with paper liners. Spoon a small amount of melted chocolate into each liner, just enough to cover the bottom. Use the back of a spoon to spread the chocolate evenly along the bottom and slightly up the sides of the liner.

  5. Place a small dollop of the peanut butter filling onto the center of each chocolate-lined liner. Flatten the dollop slightly with the back of a spoon.

  6. Spoon more melted chocolate over the peanut butter filling, covering it completely. Use the back of a spoon to smooth the chocolate and ensure it reaches the edges of the liner.

  7. If desired, sprinkle a pinch of sea salt on top of each peanut butter cup for a sweet and salty flavor combination.

  8. Place the muffin tin in the refrigerator and allow the peanut butter cups to set for at least 2 hours, or until the chocolate is firm.

  9. Once the peanut butter cups have set, remove them from the muffin tin and peel off the paper liners. Store the homemade peanut butter cups in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Tips and Variations

  • For a crunchy twist, add crushed pretzels or chopped peanuts to the peanut butter filling before spooning the melted chocolate over it.
  • Experiment with different types of chocolate, such as dark chocolate or white chocolate, to create unique flavor combinations.
  • If you prefer a sweeter filling, you can add more powdered sugar to the peanut butter mixture. Adjust the amount to suit your taste.
  • Get creative with the toppings! Sprinkle crushed Oreos, shredded coconut, or mini chocolate chips on top of the melted chocolate before it sets.

Conclusion

There you have it – a simple yet delectable recipe for homemade peanut butter cups that will leave you craving more. With just a handful of ingredients and a little bit of time, you can create a treat that surpasses any store-bought version. So, why settle for ordinary when you can indulge in extraordinary? Give this recipe a try and let your taste buds rejoice in the delightful combination of peanut butter and chocolate. Enjoy!
