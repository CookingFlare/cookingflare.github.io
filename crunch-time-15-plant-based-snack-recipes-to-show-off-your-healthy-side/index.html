Crunch Time: 15 Plant-Based Snack Recipes to Show Off Your Healthy Side


When you’ve got a hankering for a tasty snack, but also want to maintain your healthy lifestyle, plant-based options are the answer. Not only are they good for you, but they’re also versatile, budget-friendly, and scrumptious. Here are our top 15 plant-based snack recipes to show off your healthy side, delight your taste buds, and satisfy your cravings.

  1. Kale Chips

These crispy, flavorful treats are an excellent way to consume your greens.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large bunch of kale, washed, patted dry, and torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Toss the kale with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Spread the kale in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 8-12 minutes, until crispy.
  1. Roasted Chickpeas

These crunchy little bites are high in protein and fiber and are an easy snack to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and patted dry
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Toss the chickpeas with olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  • Spread the chickpeas in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 20 minutes, stirring halfway through, until crispy.
  1. Cucumber Rounds with Hummus

A fresh and cool snack, utilizing the goodness of cucumbers and hummus.

Ingredients:

  • 1 English cucumber, sliced into rounds
  • Hummus, to serve

Instructions:

  • Arrange the cucumber rounds on a plate.
  • Serve with hummus for dipping.
  1. Fruit Skewers with Yogurt Dip

Fruit skewers are a great way to enjoy a mix of your favorite fruits.

Ingredients:

  • Various fruits like kiwi, pineapple, strawberries, and grapes
  • Greek yogurt, to serve

Instructions:

  • Thread the fruits onto skewers.
  • Serve with Greek yogurt for dipping.
  1. Apple Slices with Almond Butter

A classic combination showcasing the perfect balance of sweet and savory.

Ingredients:

  • 2 apples, cored and sliced
  • Almond butter, to serve

Instructions:

  • Arrange the apple slices on a plate.
  • Serve with almond butter for dipping.
  1. Veggie Sticks with Guacamole

Load up on your veggies and enjoy them with a healthy guacamole dip.

Ingredients:

  • Various vegetables like carrots, celery, bell peppers, and cucumber
  • Guacamole, to serve

Instructions:

  • Slice the vegetables into sticks.
  • Serve with guacamole for dipping.
  1. Quinoa Salad Stuffed Avocado

A filling and nutritious snack that tastes as good as it looks.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, halved and pitted
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped cucumber
  • Juice from 1 freshly squeezed lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • Cut around the avocado pit and remove it.
  • In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa, halved cherry tomatoes, chopped cucumber, and lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper.
  • Stuff each avocado half with the quinoa salad.
  1. Chickpea and Spinach Salad

This protein-packed salad is a perfect on-the-go snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped cucumber
  • Juice from 1 freshly squeezed lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • In a bowl, combine chickpeas, spinach, red onion, and cucumber.
  • Drizzle with lemon juice and season with salt and pepper.
  1. Hemp Seed Energy Bites

Loaded with protein and good fats, these wholesome bites keep you energized.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup hemp seeds
  • 1/2 cup almond butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds

Instructions:

  • In a bowl, mix rolled oats and hemp seeds.
  • In a separate bowl, combine almond butter and maple syrup. Mix well.
  • Add wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until combined.
  • Fold in chia seeds.
  • Using your hands, form the mixture into small bites and place them on a plate.
  • Refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  1. Zucchini Chips

A savory and crispy snack, full of veggie goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large zucchinis, sliced into thin rounds
  • 2 tablespoons almond flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil, for brushing

Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 200 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • In a bowl, combine almond flour, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  • Brush the zucchini slices with olive oil and dip into the almond flour mixture.
  • Arrange the coated zucchini slices on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  • Bake for 1-2 hours, flipping halfway through, until crispy.
  1. Sweet Potato Wedges

Skip the fries and opt for a wholesome version made from sweet potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika

Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Toss the sweet potato wedges with olive oil, salt, pepper, and paprika.
  • Arrange the wedges in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 35-45 minutes, until tender.
  1. powering Mix Trail Mix

Throw together this mix of seeds, nuts, and dried fruit for a nutrient-packed snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup almonds
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 1/2 cup pecans
  • 1/2 cup dried fruit

Instructions:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the almonds, pumpkin seeds, pecans, and dried fruit.
  • Mix well and store in an airtight container.
  1. Cinnamon Roasted Squash Seeds

Make the most of your winter squash and enjoy these spicy seeds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons squash seeds, removed from your winter squash
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  • Toss the squash seeds with olive oil, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  • Spread the seeds in a single layer on a baking sheet and bake for 5-10 minutes, until lightly toasted.
  1. Edamame Dip

A fun and healthy alternative to guacamole or hummus.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked edamame
  • Juice from 1 freshly squeezed lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • In a food processor, blend edamame, lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Pulse until the desired consistency is reached.
  1. Raw Veggie Rolls

A fun and fresh snack that showcases the goodness of raw veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package rice paper wrappers
  • 1 cup julienned vegetables (carrots, cucumbers, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro

Instructions:

  • Fill a shallow dish with warm water.
  • Soak one rice paper wrapper in the water for 10 seconds, until it becomes pliable.
  • Arrange some vegetables and cilantro in the center of the wrapper, fold, and roll.
  • Repeat with the remaining wrappers and vegetables.

These plant-based snack recipes will not only satisfy your cravings but also nourish your body and boost your overall health. Give some, or all, of these yummy snacks a try and enjoy a guilt-free snacking experience. Just remember, moderation is still key, even when it comes to healthy snacks. Happy snacking!
