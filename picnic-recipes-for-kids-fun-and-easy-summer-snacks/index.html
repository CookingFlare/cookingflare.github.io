Picnic Recipes for Kids: Fun and Easy Summer Snacks


Summer is the perfect time for outdoor adventures, and what better way to make them even more enjoyable than by packing some fun, delicious, and easy-to-make picnic snacks for the kids? Whether you’re heading to the park, the beach, or just enjoying a day in the backyard, these kid-friendly picnic recipes will keep everyone happy, full, and entertained. The best part is, these recipes are simple to prepare, full of flavor, and packed with nutrients that will fuel your little ones through a day of fun and play.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of picnic recipes that are not only tasty but also easy for kids to enjoy. From fun finger foods to refreshing snacks, you’ll find the perfect additions to your next summer outing.

1. DIY Sandwich Kabobs

Sometimes the simplest things are the most fun, and DIY sandwich kabobs are a great way to let kids customize their own meal. These bite-sized kabobs are loaded with all the delicious ingredients that make up a traditional sandwich, but in a fun, portable format that’s perfect for a picnic.

Ingredients:

  • Whole wheat or white bread (sliced into squares or rounds)
  • Sliced deli meats (turkey, ham, or chicken)
  • Cheese cubes (cheddar, mozzarella, or Swiss)
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber slices
  • Lettuce or spinach leaves
  • Skewers or toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. Start by cutting the bread into small squares or rounds, depending on the shape you prefer.
  2. Layer the bread with deli meats, cheese, and fresh veggies.
  3. Assemble the kabobs by alternating the bread, meat, cheese, and veggies on each skewer or toothpick.
  4. Repeat until you have enough for everyone. Serve with a side of dip or a light vinaigrette for extra flavor.

These kabobs are easy to hold, making them perfect for kids to snack on while running around or relaxing at the picnic table. They’re also highly customizable, so picky eaters can create their ideal sandwich without any fuss.

2. Mini Veggie and Hummus Wraps

If you’re looking for a nutritious snack that’s both tasty and easy to pack, mini veggie and hummus wraps are an excellent choice. Packed with fresh vegetables and creamy hummus, these wraps are a great way to get your kids excited about eating healthy.

Ingredients:

  • Whole wheat or spinach wraps
  • Hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • Sliced bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange)
  • Sliced cucumber
  • Shredded carrots
  • Baby spinach leaves
  • Cherry tomatoes (sliced in half)

Instructions:

  1. Lay the wrap flat on a clean surface.
  2. Spread a generous layer of hummus across the center of the wrap.
  3. Layer the fresh vegetables on top of the hummus, starting with spinach, then adding bell peppers, cucumber, carrots, and tomatoes.
  4. Roll up the wrap tightly, folding in the sides as you go.
  5. Slice the wrap into bite-sized pinwheels and serve.

These wraps are full of color and flavor, making them both visually appealing and delicious. They’re also a fantastic way to sneak in extra veggies, and the creamy hummus helps balance out the freshness of the ingredients.

3. Fruit Skewers with Yogurt Dip

No picnic is complete without something sweet, and fruit skewers are a great way to satisfy that craving. You can make them with a variety of fresh, seasonal fruits and serve them with a tasty yogurt dip for an extra touch of creaminess.

Ingredients:

  • Strawberries, hulled and halved
  • Grapes (red, green, or both)
  • Pineapple chunks
  • Cantaloupe or honeydew melon cubes
  • Blueberries
  • Skewers

For the yogurt dip:

  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the fruits by cutting them into bite-sized pieces, and thread them onto the skewers in any combination of colors and flavors you like.
  2. For the dip, mix the Greek yogurt, honey, and vanilla extract in a bowl until smooth.
  3. Arrange the fruit skewers on a plate and serve with the yogurt dip on the side.

These fruit skewers are not only refreshing but also packed with vitamins and antioxidants. The yogurt dip adds a creamy texture that pairs perfectly with the sweetness of the fruit, making it a favorite for kids and adults alike.

4. Cheesy Veggie Quesadillas

Quesadillas are always a hit with kids, and they’re easy to prepare in advance for a picnic. These cheesy veggie quesadillas are filled with nutrient-packed vegetables and melted cheese, making them both satisfying and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • Flour tortillas (small or medium)
  • Shredded cheddar cheese or Mexican blend cheese
  • Diced bell peppers
  • Sliced mushrooms
  • Chopped spinach or kale
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat and lightly brush with olive oil.
  2. Place a tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle with cheese and a mix of the diced bell peppers, mushrooms, and spinach.
  3. Top with another tortilla and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until the cheese is melted and the tortillas are golden brown.
  4. Remove from the skillet and allow to cool slightly before slicing into wedges.

Cheesy veggie quesadillas are the perfect handheld snack for kids to enjoy on the go. They’re customizable with any veggies you have on hand, and the gooey cheese adds just the right amount of indulgence.

5. Apple Sandwiches with Peanut Butter and Granola

Looking for a snack that’s both fun and healthy? Apple sandwiches with peanut butter and granola are a great way to add some crunch and protein to your picnic spread. The sweet, tart apple pairs beautifully with the creamy peanut butter, while the granola adds a satisfying crunch.

Ingredients:

  • Apples (any variety, but Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  • Peanut butter (or almond butter, if preferred)
  • Granola (choose your favorite variety)
  • Honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Core and slice the apples into rings, about ¼ inch thick.
  2. Spread a generous layer of peanut butter on one side of each apple ring.
  3. Sprinkle granola on top of the peanut butter.
  4. Sandwich the apple slices together to form a delicious snack.
  5. Drizzle with a little honey for extra sweetness, if desired.

These apple sandwiches are a great alternative to traditional sandwiches and provide a good mix of sweetness and crunch. They’re also a great option for those with nut allergies if you substitute peanut butter with a nut-free spread.

6. Frozen Yogurt Bark

If you’re looking for a fun and refreshing treat for a hot summer day, frozen yogurt bark is the way to go. This easy-to-make snack is a great way to enjoy yogurt in a new, exciting form. It’s packed with healthy ingredients like fresh fruit and granola, making it both delicious and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Fresh fruit (berries, sliced kiwi, or banana slices)
  • Granola
  • Shredded coconut (optional)
  • Dark chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Spread the Greek yogurt evenly on the baking sheet, creating a thin layer.
  3. Drizzle the honey over the yogurt and swirl it in gently.
  4. Sprinkle the fresh fruit, granola, shredded coconut, and dark chocolate chips (if using) over the yogurt.
  5. Freeze for 2-3 hours or until the yogurt is firm.
  6. Break into pieces and serve.

Frozen yogurt bark is a treat that everyone will love, and it’s an excellent way to cool off during a summer picnic. The combination of tangy yogurt, sweet fruit, and crunchy granola creates a flavor explosion in every bite.

7. Mini Pita Pockets with Chicken Salad

For a more filling option that still feels light and fresh, mini pita pockets filled with chicken salad are a great choice. This is an easy and portable snack that’s perfect for kids, and it can be made ahead of time, so you’re all set for your picnic.

Ingredients:

  • Mini pita pockets
  • 2 cups cooked chicken breast, shredded
  • ¼ cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Chopped celery
  • Chopped apples
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, Greek yogurt, mayonnaise, mustard, celery, apples, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Cut the mini pita pockets in half and stuff each one with a spoonful of the chicken salad mixture.
  3. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to pack.

These mini pita pockets are an easy-to-hold snack that’s perfect for picnics. The creamy chicken salad filling is both satisfying and tasty, and the added crunch of celery and apples gives it an extra burst of flavor.

8. No-Bake Energy Bites

For a snack that will keep the kids energized throughout the day, no-bake energy bites are a perfect option. These little bites of goodness are packed with wholesome ingredients like oats, nut butter, and honey, providing a good mix of protein, fiber, and healthy fats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup peanut butter or almond butter
  • ¼ cup honey
  • ¼ cup mini chocolate chips or raisins
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds or flax seeds
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all the ingredients and mix until well combined.
  2. Roll the mixture into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter.
  3. Place the energy bites on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set.
  4. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week.

These no-bake energy bites are a great option for kids who need a quick pick-me-up. They’re easy to make, and their small size makes them perfect for packing in a picnic basket.

Conclusion

These picnic recipes are not only delicious but also simple to make and pack, making them perfect for a summer outing with kids. Whether you’re packing fruit skewers, mini sandwiches, or refreshing frozen yogurt bark, these snacks are sure to keep the little ones satisfied while they play and explore. The best part is that you can easily customize them to suit your family’s tastes, ensuring that everyone has something they enjoy. So grab your picnic blanket, pack up these fun and easy snacks, and enjoy the sunny days ahead!
