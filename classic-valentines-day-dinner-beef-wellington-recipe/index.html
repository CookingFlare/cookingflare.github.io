Classic Valentine's Day Dinner: Beef Wellington Recipe


Are you looking to impress your special someone this Valentine’s Day? Look no further than the classic and elegant Beef Wellington. With its tender beef fillet, flavorful mushroom duxelles, and flaky puff pastry, this dish is sure to melt hearts and create a memorable dining experience. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create a mouthwatering Beef Wellington that will leave your loved one in awe.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds beef tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Season the beef tenderloin with salt and pepper, ensuring an even coating on all sides.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until browned, about 2 minutes per side. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside to cool.

  3. In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced shallot and garlic, sautéing until fragrant and translucent. Then, add the finely chopped cremini mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become golden brown.

  4. Pour in the dry white wine and cook until the liquid has evaporated. Stir in the fresh parsley and season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the mushroom duxelles from the heat and let it cool completely.

  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the puff pastry into a large rectangle, about 1/8 inch thick. Spread the cooled mushroom duxelles evenly over the puff pastry, leaving a border around the edges.

  6. Place the seared beef tenderloin on top of the mushroom duxelles, positioning it in the center. Carefully wrap the puff pastry around the beef, sealing all edges. Trim off any excess pastry if necessary.

  7. Brush the entire surface of the puff pastry with the beaten egg, ensuring a glossy and golden finish when baked. Use a sharp knife to create decorative patterns on the pastry, if desired.

  8. Transfer the Beef Wellington to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the puff pastry is golden brown and the beef reaches your desired level of doneness.

  9. Remove the Beef Wellington from the oven and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing. This will help the juices redistribute and result in a more tender and flavorful dish.

  10. Serve the Beef Wellington as the centerpiece of your romantic Valentine’s Day dinner. Pair it with roasted vegetables, creamy mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

The classic Beef Wellington is a show-stopping dish that will make your Valentine’s Day celebration truly special. The combination of tender beef, savory mushroom duxelles, and flaky puff pastry creates a symphony of flavors and textures that is sure to impress. Take your time with each step, savoring the process of creating this culinary masterpiece. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is approachable and rewarding. So, light some candles, set the table, and get ready to indulge in a romantic and delicious dinner with your loved one. Happy Valentine’s Day!
