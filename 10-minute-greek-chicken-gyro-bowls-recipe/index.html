10-Minute Greek Chicken Gyro Bowls Recipe


If you’re looking for a quick and delicious meal that’s packed with flavor, look no further than these 10-Minute Greek Chicken Gyro Bowls! This recipe is perfect for those busy weeknights when you want something nutritious and satisfying without spending hours in the kitchen. With a combination of fresh ingredients and bold Mediterranean flavors, these gyro bowls will leave you wanting more. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Tzatziki sauce, for serving

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the chicken. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, dried oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a marinade.

  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and massage the chicken to ensure it’s evenly coated. Let it marinate for about 5 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.

  3. Heat a grill pan or skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated chicken breasts and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked through and nicely browned. Remove from heat and let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing.

  4. While the chicken is resting, assemble the gyro bowls. Divide the cooked quinoa evenly between two bowls. Top with sliced chicken, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, Kalamata olives, and crumbled feta cheese.

  5. Serve the gyro bowls with a generous dollop of tzatziki sauce on top. You can also garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or dill for an extra burst of flavor.

  6. Enjoy your 10-Minute Greek Chicken Gyro Bowls straight away and savor the vibrant Mediterranean flavors!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian option, you can replace the chicken with grilled halloumi cheese or marinated tofu.
  • Feel free to customize your gyro bowls with your favorite toppings. You can add roasted red peppers, avocado, or even a squeeze of lemon juice for a tangy kick.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Simply reheat in the microwave or enjoy them cold.

Final Thoughts

These 10-Minute Greek Chicken Gyro Bowls are not only quick and easy to make, but they’re also incredibly delicious and nutritious. The combination of tender grilled chicken, fresh vegetables, and tangy tzatziki sauce creates a harmonious medley of flavors that will transport your taste buds straight to the Mediterranean. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or feeding a crowd, this recipe is sure to impress. So, give it a try and enjoy a taste of Greece right in the comfort of your own home!

Remember, cooking is all about experimenting and making it your own, so don’t be afraid to get creative with your gyro bowls. And most importantly, enjoy the process and the delicious results that await you. Happy cooking!
