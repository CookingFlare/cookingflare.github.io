Tantalizing Tuna Tartare

Delight your guests with this sophisticated appetizer that takes advantage of the firm, yet tender texture of fresh tuna. Crafted with care and packed with umami, this dish showcases a symphony of flavors and is sure to leave a lasting impression.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound sashimi-grade tuna, finely diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely minced
  • 1 jalapeno, seeded and finely minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha
  • Glossy, black sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Begin by finely diced the tuna into 1/4-inch pieces, taking care to maintain the integrity of the fish. Place in a large mixing bowl.

  2. Dice the red onion and jalapeño, then add to the bowl with the tuna. Mix well to combine.

  3. To create the tangy, spicy dressing, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, and sriracha in a small bowl. Season with a pinch of salt and white pepper to taste.

  4. Add the dressing to the tuna mixture and gently toss to ensure all pieces are evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. To plate, take a small, ring-shaped mold or cookie cutter, and firmly press it into the tuna mixture. Gently remove the mold to reveal a perfectly shaped and evenly packed tartare.

  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro and a sprinkle of glossy, black sesame seeds.

  7. Serve with toasted baguette slices or rice crackers for a taste of the sea you’ll never forget.

Seared Scallops with Lemon Risotto

For this exquisite dish, we pair buttery, golden seared scallops with a comforting, one-pot risotto that boasts a burst of zesty lemon flavor. The perfect dish for a memorable meal, this recipe showcases a balance of sophisticated textures and invigorating citrus that will leave your taste buds dancing in delight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large scallops
  • Salt and white pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 1/2 cups grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 lemons, zested and juiced

Instructions:

  1. Pat the scallops dry with a paper towel, remove the Muscle, then season with salt and white pepper on both sides.

  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the scallops and sear for 1-2 minutes on each side, until golden brown. Remove scallops from the skillet and set aside, then reduce heat to medium.

  3. In a separate large saucepan, heat the chicken or vegetable stock and keep warm over low heat.

  4. In the same skillet used for searing the scallops, sauté the onion and garlic until translucent and fragrant. Add the arborio rice, stirring to coat the grains completely with the oil and onions.

  5. Deglaze the skillet with white wine, stirring as it evaporates.

  6. Slowly pour in the hot chicken or vegetable stock, 1/2 cup at a time, allowing the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more. Continuously stir the risotto, which should thicken and become creamy, over the 20-25 minutes it takes to cook.

  7. When the rice is cooked through but still al dente, remove the pot from the heat. Stir in Parmigiano-Reggiano, chives, parsley, and unsalted butter, followed by the lemon zest and juice. Season with salt and white pepper to taste.

  8. To plate, arrange the seared scallops atop the lemon risotto and garnish with extra chives, lemon zest, and Parmigiano-Reggiano shavings for an exquisite finish.
