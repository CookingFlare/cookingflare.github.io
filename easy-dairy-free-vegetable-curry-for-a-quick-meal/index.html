Easy Dairy-Free Vegetable Curry for a Quick Meal


If you’re looking for a flavorful and satisfying meal that’s quick to make and dairy-free, then you’re in for a treat. This easy vegetable curry recipe is not only bursting with vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients, but it’s also incredibly simple to prepare. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to become a go-to favorite in your meal rotation.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to whip up this delicious dairy-free vegetable curry:

  • 2 tablespoons of coconut oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 teaspoons of curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 2 cups of cauliflower florets
  • 2 cups of diced carrots
  • 1 cup of green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup of diced bell peppers
  • 2 cups of baby spinach
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or naan bread for serving

Instructions

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s get cooking!

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté for 3-4 minutes, or until the onions are soft and translucent.

  2. Add the Spices: Once the aromatics are fragrant, sprinkle in the curry powder, ground cumin, and ground turmeric. Stir well to coat the onions and garlic with the spices, and let them cook for another minute to release their flavors.

  3. Pour in the Liquid: Next, pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, stirring to combine everything into a fragrant, golden-hued sauce.

  4. Add the Vegetables: Add the cauliflower florets, diced carrots, green beans, and bell peppers to the pot. Give everything a good stir, ensuring that the vegetables are well-coated with the flavorful sauce.

  5. Simmer to Perfection: Cover the pot and let the curry simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still have a slight bite to them.

  6. Finish with Spinach: Once the vegetables are cooked to perfection, stir in the baby spinach until it wilts into the curry. Season with salt and pepper to taste, adjusting the seasoning as needed.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Your dairy-free vegetable curry is now ready to be served. Spoon it over a bed of fluffy rice or pair it with warm naan bread for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips for Success

  • Feel free to customize the vegetable selection based on your preferences or what’s in season. Broccoli, peas, or sweet potatoes would also work wonderfully in this curry.
  • For an added kick of heat, sprinkle in some red pepper flakes or diced fresh chili when adding the spices.
  • If you’re a fan of protein in your curry, consider adding chickpeas, tofu, or your choice of cooked meat to make it a heartier dish.

In Conclusion

This easy dairy-free vegetable curry is a fantastic option for those busy weeknights when you want a nourishing meal without spending hours in the kitchen. Packed with vibrant vegetables and aromatic spices, it’s a dish that’s as wholesome as it is delicious. Plus, it’s a great way to incorporate more plant-based goodness into your diet. So, the next time you’re craving a quick and satisfying meal, give this recipe a try and savor the delightful flavors of this dairy-free vegetable curry.
