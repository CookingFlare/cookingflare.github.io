Delectable Apple Pie: A Traditional Christmas Dessert


Apple pie is a classic and beloved dessert that has been enjoyed for generations. With its buttery crust and sweet, cinnamon-spiced filling, it’s no wonder that apple pie has become a staple on Christmas dinner tables around the world. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating a delectable apple pie that will leave your guests begging for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water
  • 6-8 medium-sized apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work best)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Additional granulated sugar for sprinkling

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and 1 tablespoon of granulated sugar. Mix well.

  3. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. The dough should be slightly crumbly, but still hold together when pressed.

  5. Divide the dough into two equal portions. Flatten each portion into a disk shape and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  6. While the dough is chilling, peel, core, and slice the apples. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, ¾ cup of granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.

  7. Take one portion of the chilled dough and roll it out on a lightly floured surface. Transfer the rolled dough to a 9-inch pie dish, making sure it covers the bottom and sides of the dish.

  8. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly. Drizzle the melted butter over the filling.

  9. Roll out the second portion of the chilled dough and place it on top of the apple filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  10. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg wash and sprinkle with granulated sugar.

  11. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  12. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set.

  13. Serve the delectable apple pie warm or at room temperature. It pairs perfectly with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream.

Conclusion

There’s something magical about the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie filling the air during the holiday season. This traditional Christmas dessert is not only delicious but also a symbol of warmth and togetherness. With this recipe, you can create a delectable apple pie that will make your Christmas celebrations even more memorable. So gather your loved ones, roll up your sleeves, and let the sweet scent of apples and cinnamon fill your home this holiday season. Happy baking!
