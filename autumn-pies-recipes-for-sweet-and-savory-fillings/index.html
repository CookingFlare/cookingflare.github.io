Autumn Pies: Recipes for Sweet and Savory Fillings


Autumn is a season of warm colors, cozy sweaters, and comforting flavors. One of the best ways to embrace the spirit of fall is by baking delicious pies with seasonal ingredients. Whether you prefer sweet or savory fillings, there’s a pie recipe to satisfy every palate. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering autumn pie recipes that are sure to impress your family and friends.

1. Spiced Apple Pie

Preparation time: 30 minutes

Baking time: 45 minutes

Total time: 1 hour 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 package of ready-made pie crusts (or homemade if you prefer)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt. Mix well.

  4. Add the dry mixture to the apples and toss until all the slices are evenly coated.

  5. Line a pie dish with one of the pie crusts. Pour the apple filling into the crust and distribute the butter pieces on top.

  6. Cover the pie with the second pie crust, crimping the edges to seal. Make a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  7. Place the pie dish on a baking sheet and bake for approximately 45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  8. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for at least 30 minutes before serving. Enjoy with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence!

2. Butternut Squash and Sage Pie

Preparation time: 40 minutes

Baking time: 1 hour

Total time: 1 hour 40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package of ready-made pie crusts (or homemade if you prefer)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Place the butternut squash cubes on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast in the oven for about 25 minutes, or until tender.

  3. In a skillet, sauté the chopped onion, minced garlic, and fresh sage leaves in olive oil until fragrant and the onion is translucent.

  4. In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted butternut squash, sautéed onion mixture, vegetable broth, heavy cream, eggs, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.

  5. Line a pie dish with one of the pie crusts. Pour the butternut squash filling into the crust.

  6. Cover the pie with the second pie crust, crimping the edges to seal. Make a few slits on the top crust.

  7. Place the pie dish on a baking sheet and bake for approximately 1 hour, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set.

  8. Allow the pie to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. This savory pie pairs perfectly with a side of mixed greens for a complete autumn meal.

3. Salted Caramel Pecan Pie

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Baking time: 55 minutes

Total time: 1 hour 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups pecan halves
  • 1 package of ready-made pie crusts (or homemade if you prefer)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, corn syrup, melted butter, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined.

  3. Arrange the pecan halves on the bottom of the pie crust.

  4. Pour the sugar mixture over the pecans, making sure they are evenly coated.

  5. Cover the pie with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes.

  6. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 25 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly jiggles in the center.

  7. Allow the pie to cool completely before slicing. Serve with a drizzle of salted caramel sauce for an irresistible autumn treat.

Autumn pies are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of the season. Whether you’re craving a classic apple pie, a savory butternut squash and sage pie, or a sweet and salty pecan pie, these recipes are sure to bring warmth and comfort to your table. So, don your apron, gather your ingredients, and let the aromas of autumn fill your kitchen. Happy baking!
