Greek Grilled Lamb Riblets with Rosemary and Lemon


Grilled lamb riblets are a savory, succulent, and flavorful dish that brings the essence of Greek cuisine right into your kitchen. The combination of tender lamb, aromatic rosemary, and zesty lemon creates a perfect harmony of flavors. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, preparing a family dinner, or simply craving something delicious, these Greek-style lamb riblets with rosemary and lemon will surely satisfy your appetite and elevate your cooking experience.

In this post, we will explore the step-by-step process of making these mouthwatering lamb riblets. We will cover everything from the ingredients you’ll need to the grilling techniques that ensure the perfect balance of tenderness and flavor. Let’s dive into this exquisite Greek-inspired recipe that you can prepare with ease and confidence!

Ingredients for Greek Grilled Lamb Riblets with Rosemary and Lemon

Before we start, gather all of the following ingredients to ensure you have everything needed to make these delicious grilled lamb riblets:

  • 2 pounds of lamb riblets
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh oregano, finely chopped (optional but highly recommended)
  • Juice and zest of 1 large lemon
  • 1 tablespoon of red wine vinegar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs, for garnish

These ingredients combine to create a marinade that enhances the rich flavor of the lamb while also providing the freshness of lemon and the earthiness of rosemary. The olive oil ensures that the lamb remains moist and juicy while grilling.

Preparing the Lamb Riblets

The key to preparing perfect Greek grilled lamb riblets is in the marinade. The longer you let the lamb sit in the marinade, the more flavorful the riblets will be. Here’s how you can get started:

1. Trim the Riblets

First, begin by trimming any excess fat from the lamb riblets. While a little fat is necessary for flavor, trimming the excess ensures a cleaner grilling experience and avoids flare-ups. You should leave a thin layer of fat for flavor and moisture retention.

2. Prepare the Marinade

In a large mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, finely chopped rosemary, minced garlic, fresh oregano (if using), lemon juice and zest, red wine vinegar, salt, and black pepper. Stir until everything is well mixed.

The olive oil and red wine vinegar serve as the base of the marinade, helping to tenderize the lamb and allow the herbs and spices to infuse deeply into the meat. The fresh rosemary and oregano bring that signature Greek flavor, while the garlic adds a savory, aromatic depth. The lemon juice and zest brighten the dish, balancing the richness of the lamb with a fresh tang.

3. Marinate the Riblets

Place the lamb riblets into the bowl with the marinade. Toss them gently to ensure that each piece is fully coated in the flavorful mixture. For the best results, cover the bowl and refrigerate the riblets for at least 2 hours, though overnight is ideal for maximum flavor. The longer they marinate, the more the meat will absorb the herbs, lemon, and garlic, resulting in an irresistibly tasty final dish.

Grilling the Lamb Riblets

Grilling is an essential step to achieving the smoky, slightly charred exterior while keeping the lamb riblets tender and juicy on the inside. The grill marks and caramelization bring out the flavors, making it a perfect cooking method for this dish.

1. Preheat the Grill

Heat your grill to medium-high heat, ensuring it is properly preheated before placing the riblets on it. Whether you’re using a gas or charcoal grill, a consistent medium-high heat ensures even cooking and helps develop a beautiful crust on the lamb.

2. Grill the Riblets

Once the grill is hot, place the marinated lamb riblets on the grates. If using a charcoal grill, you may want to arrange the riblets over direct heat for a good sear and then move them to a cooler side of the grill to finish cooking. For a gas grill, simply adjust the temperature to maintain a medium-high heat.

Grill the riblets for 4 to 5 minutes per side. Turn the lamb riblets every few minutes to ensure they cook evenly on all sides. You’re aiming for a slightly charred exterior with a juicy, tender interior. Lamb is best cooked medium-rare to medium, so be sure to check the internal temperature. It should reach 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare or 160°F (71°C) for medium.

3. Baste for Extra Flavor

As the riblets grill, you can occasionally brush them with extra marinade for a boost of flavor. However, be careful not to use too much, as the marinade contains olive oil, which can cause flare-ups. A light brush will suffice and will add an additional layer of aromatic goodness.

4. Let the Riblets Rest

Once the lamb riblets are grilled to your desired doneness, remove them from the grill and let them rest for 5 to 10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring they remain tender and moist when you cut into them.

Serving Greek Grilled Lamb Riblets

These Greek grilled lamb riblets are best served immediately after grilling, accompanied by some refreshing sides and garnishes. Here are some delicious ways to serve your lamb riblets:

1. Garnish with Fresh Herbs

After resting, sprinkle some freshly chopped rosemary or parsley over the lamb riblets to add an extra burst of flavor. The fresh herbs not only enhance the taste but also provide a vibrant color contrast to the grilled meat.

2. Pair with Greek Salad

A Greek salad featuring cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, Kalamata olives, and feta cheese is a perfect accompaniment to the lamb riblets. The cool, crisp salad balances the richness of the lamb, and the tangy dressing complements the lemony flavor of the meat.

3. Serve with Tzatziki Sauce

No Greek dish is complete without tzatziki sauce! The cool, creamy yogurt-based dip with cucumber, garlic, dill, and lemon pairs beautifully with the grilled lamb. Serve a generous spoonful of tzatziki alongside the lamb riblets for a refreshing contrast.

4. Add Some Warm Pita Bread

Warm, soft pita bread is ideal for scooping up tzatziki sauce or soaking up any juices from the lamb riblets. Its mild flavor complements the boldness of the grilled lamb and makes for a hearty, satisfying meal.

5. Lemon Wedges for an Extra Zing

Serve the lamb riblets with lemon wedges on the side so guests can squeeze a bit more fresh lemon juice on their meat for an extra burst of brightness. The acidity of the lemon enhances the grilled flavor of the lamb and adds a refreshing finish to each bite.

Tips for Perfect Greek Grilled Lamb Riblets

  1. Use Fresh Herbs: While dried herbs can be used in a pinch, fresh rosemary, oregano, and garlic will provide a more vibrant flavor that truly shines in this dish.

  2. Don’t Overcrowd the Grill: Give each lamb riblet enough space on the grill to ensure even cooking. Overcrowding can result in uneven grilling and prevents the lamb from developing a nice crust.

  3. Check the Temperature: Use a meat thermometer to ensure your lamb is cooked to the desired doneness. Lamb can dry out if overcooked, so be mindful of the temperature.

  4. Experiment with Marinades: While rosemary and lemon are classic flavors for Greek-style lamb, feel free to experiment with other herbs like thyme, mint, or even a touch of cumin for a different flavor profile.

  5. Consider Using a Grill Basket: If you’re worried about small riblets falling through the grates, a grill basket can help keep them secure while allowing for even cooking.

Conclusion

Greek grilled lamb riblets with rosemary and lemon are an irresistible dish that showcases the rich flavors of Mediterranean cuisine. Whether you’re preparing a special meal for family and friends or just want to indulge in something savory, these lamb riblets will surely impress. The combination of tender lamb, fragrant rosemary, and the tangy brightness of lemon creates a flavor profile that’s truly unforgettable.

By following this recipe and grilling with care, you’ll enjoy perfectly cooked lamb riblets with an aromatic, smoky exterior and a juicy, flavorful interior. Pair them with fresh sides like Greek salad, tzatziki sauce, and warm pita for a complete and satisfying meal that captures the essence of Greek cooking. Enjoy your cooking adventure and savor every bite of these mouthwatering grilled lamb riblets!
