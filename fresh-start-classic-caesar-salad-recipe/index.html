Fresh Start: Classic Caesar Salad Recipe


Salads are a versatile and refreshing addition to any meal. Whether you’re looking for a light lunch or a side dish to complement your main course, a classic Caesar salad is a timeless choice. The crisp romaine lettuce, savory dressing, and crunchy croutons come together to create a dish that’s both satisfying and full of flavor. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make a classic Caesar salad from scratch, including the iconic dressing that makes this dish truly unforgettable.

Ingredients

For the Dressing:

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Salad:

  • 1 large head of romaine lettuce, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup croutons
  • Additional grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dressing:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the minced garlic, chopped anchovy fillets, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice until well combined.
    • Add the mayonnaise and grated Parmesan cheese to the bowl, and continue whisking until the dressing is smooth and creamy.
    • Season the dressing with salt and pepper to taste, and adjust the flavors as needed. Set the dressing aside while you prepare the salad.

  2. Assemble the Salad:

    • Place the chopped romaine lettuce in a large serving bowl.
    • Drizzle the prepared dressing over the lettuce, using just enough to lightly coat the leaves. Toss the salad gently to ensure that the dressing is evenly distributed.
    • Add the croutons to the salad, and toss once more to combine all the ingredients.
    • Sprinkle additional grated Parmesan cheese over the top of the salad for a final touch of flavor.

  3. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Once the salad is assembled, it’s ready to be served. Divide the Caesar salad among individual plates and serve immediately as a delicious starter or side dish.

Tips for Success

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, consider adding grilled chicken, shrimp, or even salmon to turn this side dish into a satisfying main course.
  • To make homemade croutons, toss cubes of day-old bread with olive oil, salt, and your choice of dried herbs, then bake in a preheated oven until golden and crisp.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version of the dressing, simply omit the anchovies and adjust the seasoning to your taste.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of making a classic Caesar salad at home is a rewarding experience that will impress your family and friends. The combination of fresh, crisp lettuce, flavorful dressing, and crunchy croutons makes this dish a timeless favorite. By following this recipe, you can create a restaurant-quality Caesar salad right in your own kitchen. Whether it’s for a light lunch, a dinner party, or a quick and easy meal, this classic recipe is sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire. Enjoy the fresh flavors and satisfying crunch of a homemade Caesar salad, and embrace the joy of creating delicious, wholesome dishes from scratch.
