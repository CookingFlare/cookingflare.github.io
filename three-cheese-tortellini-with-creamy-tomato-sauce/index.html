Three-Cheese Tortellini with Creamy Tomato Sauce


If you’re craving a rich and comforting pasta dish that combines velvety cheese, luscious tomato sauce, and the perfect al dente bite, look no further than Three-Cheese Tortellini with Creamy Tomato Sauce. This dish delivers the ideal balance of flavors, from the savory cheese-filled tortellini to the smooth and indulgent creaminess of the tomato sauce. It’s a meal that will impress guests at dinner parties, satisfy your family’s hunger, or even serve as a perfect weeknight dinner.

Why This Recipe?

Three-Cheese Tortellini with Creamy Tomato Sauce is the ultimate pasta dish for those who love the combination of cheese and tomato. It’s a step up from a classic marinara or plain pasta, thanks to the richness of the cream and the depth of flavor the cheese adds. Here’s why you’ll want to make this recipe again and again:

  1. Quick and Easy: Despite its luxurious taste, this dish is simple to make, requiring only a handful of ingredients.
  2. Cheese Lovers’ Dream: The blend of three cheeses—ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan—makes every bite rich and satisfying.
  3. Comfort Food: The creamy tomato sauce perfectly complements the pillowy tortellini, making it a perfect comfort food.
  4. Customizable: You can adjust the flavors or add extra ingredients like fresh basil, spinach, or even chicken to make the dish your own.

Ingredients

For this recipe, you’ll need a few fresh ingredients that come together to create a rich, cheesy experience:

  • For the Tortellini:

    • 1 package of refrigerated three-cheese tortellini (about 9 oz)

  • For the Creamy Tomato Sauce:

    • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 can (14.5 oz) of crushed tomatoes
    • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
    • 1/4 cup of tomato paste
    • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)
    • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
    • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
    • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

  • For the Cheese Mixture:

    • 1/2 cup of ricotta cheese
    • 1/2 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
    • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese

  • For Garnishing:

    • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
    • Extra Parmesan cheese for sprinkling

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Tortellini

The first step in preparing this dish is cooking the tortellini. Since most tortellini is pre-cooked and only needs reheating, you can follow the package instructions. Typically, this involves bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Once boiling, drop the tortellini in and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the tortellini rises to the surface and is al dente. Once cooked, drain the tortellini and set it aside. Make sure to reserve a cup of pasta water to adjust the sauce later if needed.

Step 2: Prepare the Tomato Sauce

While the tortellini is cooking, start working on the creamy tomato sauce. Heat the olive oil in a large pan or skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. This step adds a savory foundation to the sauce.

Next, add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn it. Garlic is crucial for adding depth to the sauce, so don’t rush this step.

Once the onions and garlic are perfectly sautéed, add the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Stir to combine and let it simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together and creates a thick, tomatoey base for the sauce.

After the tomatoes have simmered, add the heavy cream, red pepper flakes, dried basil, and dried oregano. Stir everything well to combine. Let the sauce simmer for another 5 minutes, allowing the cream to integrate fully and the sauce to thicken slightly. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.

Step 3: Combine the Cheese Mixture

While the sauce is simmering, it’s time to prepare the cheese mixture. In a separate bowl, combine the ricotta, shredded mozzarella, and grated Parmesan cheese. This blend of cheeses will give your tortellini a creamy, gooey filling that perfectly complements the richness of the creamy tomato sauce. Stir the mixture until well combined, ensuring the cheeses are evenly distributed.

Step 4: Stuff the Tortellini

If your tortellini is not pre-stuffed (though most three-cheese tortellini is), you can stuff each piece by placing a small spoonful of the cheese mixture inside. However, if you’re using pre-made three-cheese tortellini, you can skip this step and move straight to the next.

Step 5: Combine Tortellini with Sauce

Once the sauce has thickened and the tortellini is cooked, it’s time to bring the two together. Gently toss the cooked tortellini into the creamy tomato sauce, stirring carefully so that each piece is coated in the rich sauce. If the sauce appears too thick, add a little reserved pasta water to reach your desired consistency. The starch in the pasta water will help thicken the sauce and create a silky finish.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

After everything is combined, transfer the Three-Cheese Tortellini with Creamy Tomato Sauce to serving plates. To finish, garnish with fresh basil leaves and a sprinkle of extra Parmesan cheese for an added touch of flavor. The freshness of the basil pairs perfectly with the creamy tomato sauce, elevating the dish to a new level.

Tips and Variations

  1. Make it Spicy: For those who enjoy a little heat, add extra red pepper flakes to the sauce or drizzle some chili oil over the finished dish.
  2. Fresh Tomatoes: If you want to go for a more vibrant and fresh flavor, substitute the canned crushed tomatoes for fresh tomatoes. Simmer them down until they break apart and blend into a smooth sauce.
  3. Add Vegetables: Fresh spinach or mushrooms would make great additions to this dish. Simply sauté them with the onions and garlic before adding the tomatoes.
  4. Chicken or Shrimp: To make this dish more filling, consider adding grilled chicken or shrimp on top for an added protein boost.
  5. Make it Ahead: This dish can be made ahead and stored in the fridge for 1-2 days. Reheat on the stove with a little bit of cream or pasta water to bring it back to life.
