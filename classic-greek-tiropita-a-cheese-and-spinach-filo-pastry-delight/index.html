Classic Greek Tiropita: A Cheese and Spinach Filo Pastry Delight


If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or simply looking to try something new, then Classic Greek Tiropita is a must-try dish. This mouthwatering combination of cheese and spinach wrapped in layers of delicate filo pastry will surely delight your taste buds. With its crispy exterior and flavorful filling, Tiropita is a crowd-pleaser that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

To make this delectable Greek treat, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 package of filo pastry sheets
  • 500 grams of feta cheese, crumbled
  • 250 grams of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Now that we have gathered all the ingredients, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making Classic Greek Tiropita:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F) and grease a baking dish with olive oil.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the crumbled feta cheese, chopped spinach, eggs, milk, olive oil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Take a sheet of filo pastry and place it on a clean, flat surface. Brush it lightly with olive oil. Repeat this step with several more sheets, layering them on top of each other.
  4. Spread a generous amount of the cheese and spinach mixture evenly over the filo pastry sheets.
  5. Carefully roll the pastry into a log shape, ensuring that the filling is tightly enclosed.
  6. Place the rolled pastry into the greased baking dish, seam side down. Repeat the process with the remaining filo pastry sheets and filling until the dish is filled.
  7. Brush the top of the Tiropita with olive oil to ensure it browns evenly during baking.
  8. Using a sharp knife, gently score the top of the Tiropita into individual servings.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for approximately 35-40 minutes, or until the pastry turns golden brown and crispy.
  10. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Classic Greek Tiropita is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed on its own or as part of a larger meal. Here are a few serving suggestions to enhance your culinary experience:

  • Serve warm as an appetizer or snack at a gathering or party.
  • Pair with a fresh Greek salad and tzatziki sauce for a light and satisfying lunch.
  • Enjoy alongside other traditional Greek dishes such as Souvlaki, Moussaka, or Spanakopita for a complete Greek feast.

Conclusion

Classic Greek Tiropita is a beloved dish that captures the essence of Greek cuisine. Its combination of cheese, spinach, and flaky filo pastry creates a delightful balance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply looking to indulge in a delicious treat, Tiropita is sure to impress. So don’t wait any longer – gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and savor the taste of Greece right in your own kitchen.

Note: The recipe provided above is a classic interpretation of Greek Tiropita. Feel free to customize the filling by adding herbs, spices, or other ingredients to suit your taste preferences. Enjoy experimenting and making this recipe your own!
