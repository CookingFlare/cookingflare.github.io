Rustic Tuscan Bread Soup Recipe - Italian Comfort in a Bowl


When it comes to Italian comfort food, few dishes encapsulate the warmth and heartiness of the Tuscan countryside quite like the rustic Tuscan bread soup, also known as “Ribollita.” This simple yet flavorful dish has been a staple in Tuscan kitchens for centuries, offering a delicious way to repurpose stale bread and celebrate the abundance of fresh vegetables. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating this soul-soothing dish that warms the body and soul.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) whole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand
  • 1 bunch Lacinato kale, stemmed and chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 4 cups stale bread, torn into pieces
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, and cook until softened, about 8 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional 2 minutes.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Greens: Stir in the crushed tomatoes and kale, and cook until the kale is wilted, about 5 minutes.

  3. Simmer with Beans and Broth: Add the cannellini beans, broth, and torn bread to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the bread breaks down and the soup thickens.

  4. Season and Serve: Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Ladle the soup into bowls and serve with a generous sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top.

Tips for a Perfect Ribollita:

  • Use stale bread: The key to achieving the perfect texture in this soup is to use stale bread. Fresh bread will turn mushy too quickly, while stale bread adds body and thickness to the soup.

  • Let it rest: Ribollita actually means “reboiled” in Italian. Traditionally, this soup is made a day ahead and reheated, allowing the flavors to meld and intensify.

  • Customize the veggies: Feel free to incorporate other seasonal vegetables such as zucchini, cabbage, or Swiss chard for a unique twist on this classic recipe.

The Comfort of Tuscan Tradition

As you savor each spoonful of this rustic Tuscan bread soup, you’re not just enjoying a meal – you’re partaking in a tradition that has been passed down through generations. The simplicity of ingredients and the robust flavors are a true testament to the Tuscan way of life, where food is not just sustenance but a celebration of the earth’s bounty and the warmth of community.

Whether you’re curled up on a chilly evening or hosting a gathering of loved ones, this Tuscan bread soup is sure to bring comfort and joy to all who taste it. So, channel your inner Italian nonna, gather your ingredients, and let the aroma of this timeless dish fill your kitchen with the essence of Tuscan tradition. Buon appetito!
