Peanut Butter and Jelly Cupcakes: A Childhood Favorite


Who doesn’t love the classic combination of peanut butter and jelly? It’s a flavor duo that brings back childhood memories and never fails to satisfy. Now, imagine that delicious combination in the form of a moist, fluffy cupcake. Yes, you heard that right – Peanut Butter and Jelly Cupcakes! This recipe takes a beloved childhood favorite and transforms it into a delightful treat that will have both kids and adults reaching for seconds.

Ingredients

For the Cupcakes:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • ½ cup creamy peanut butter
  • ¾ cup whole milk

For the Jelly Filling:

  • ½ cup your favorite fruit jelly or jam

For the Peanut Butter Frosting:

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups confectioners’ sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract and peanut butter.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients.

  5. Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Cool the cupcakes completely.

  6. Using a small knife or a cupcake corer, create a small well in the center of each cupcake. Fill the well with your favorite fruit jelly or jam.

  7. For the peanut butter frosting, beat together the peanut butter and butter until smooth. Gradually add the confectioners’ sugar, alternating with the milk and vanilla extract, until the frosting reaches a spreadable consistency.

  8. Frost the filled cupcakes with the peanut butter frosting, using a piping bag or offset spatula.

  9. Enjoy these delightful Peanut Butter and Jelly Cupcakes with a glass of cold milk or a hot cup of coffee.

Tips and Variations

  • Variation: For a crunchy twist, sprinkle some chopped roasted peanuts on top of the frosting.
  • Storage: Store the cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
  • Allergy-Friendly: To make these cupcakes nut-free, substitute the peanut butter with sunflower seed butter.

Final Thoughts

Peanut Butter and Jelly Cupcakes are a nostalgic and indulgent treat that brings back fond memories of childhood lunches and after-school snacks. With the perfect balance of sweet, nutty, and fruity flavors, these cupcakes are sure to be a hit at birthday parties, potlucks, or simply as a special homemade treat. So, the next time you’re craving a taste of nostalgia, whip up a batch of these delightful Peanut Butter and Jelly Cupcakes and let the flavors take you on a trip down memory lane.
