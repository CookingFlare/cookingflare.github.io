Saucy and Delicious: BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwich Recipe


BBQ pulled pork sandwiches are the epitome of comfort food, combining tender, flavorful meat with a rich, tangy sauce, all nestled between soft, pillowy buns. This recipe not only delivers on taste but also brings a delightful experience to your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, enjoying a cozy family dinner, or simply indulging in a personal treat, this pulled pork sandwich recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Pulled Pork

  • 4 pounds of pork shoulder (also known as pork butt)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 1 cup of chicken broth

For the BBQ Sauce

  • 1 cup of ketchup
  • 1/2 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of mustard (yellow or Dijon)
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving

  • Soft burger buns (preferably brioche)
  • Coleslaw (optional, for topping)
  • Pickles (optional, for topping)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

  1. Trim the Fat: Start by trimming excess fat from the pork shoulder. While some fat is necessary for flavor and moisture, too much can lead to greasy pulled pork.

  2. Season the Meat: In a small bowl, combine smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork shoulder, ensuring an even coating.

  3. Sear the Pork: In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, sear the pork shoulder on all sides until golden brown. This process locks in flavors and adds depth to the dish.

Step 2: Slow Cook the Pork

  1. Transfer to Slow Cooker: Once seared, transfer the pork shoulder to a slow cooker. Pour in the chicken broth, which will keep the meat moist during cooking.

  2. Cook: Cover the slow cooker and set it to low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-5 hours. The goal is to cook the pork until it is tender and shreds easily with a fork.

Step 3: Make the BBQ Sauce

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, mix together ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder. Stir well to combine.

  2. Simmer the Sauce: Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Step 4: Shred the Pork

  1. Check for Doneness: Once the pork is done cooking, carefully remove it from the slow cooker. Allow it to rest for 10-15 minutes.

  2. Shred the Meat: Using two forks, shred the pork into bite-sized pieces. Remove any large chunks of fat and return the shredded meat to the slow cooker.

  3. Mix with BBQ Sauce: Pour the prepared BBQ sauce over the shredded pork and stir until well combined. Let it cook for an additional 30 minutes in the slow cooker to soak up the flavors.

Step 5: Assemble the Sandwiches

  1. Prepare the Buns: Lightly toast the burger buns for added texture and flavor.

  2. Build Your Sandwich: Place a generous scoop of the BBQ pulled pork onto the bottom half of each bun. For an extra crunch and flavor, top with coleslaw and pickles if desired.

  3. Serve: Place the top half of the bun over the filling and serve immediately. These sandwiches are best enjoyed with a side of fries, potato salad, or chips.

Tips for the Perfect BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwich

  • Choose the Right Cut: Pork shoulder is ideal for pulled pork due to its marbling and connective tissue, which breaks down during the cooking process, resulting in juicy, tender meat.

  • Low and Slow: The key to great pulled pork is patience. Cooking it low and slow allows the flavors to develop and the meat to become tender.

  • Experiment with Sauces: Feel free to experiment with different BBQ sauces. You can try adding honey, chipotle, or even a splash of bourbon for a unique twist.

  • Make Ahead: This pulled pork can be made ahead of time. Store the shredded pork and BBQ sauce separately in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat before serving.

  • Freeze for Later: You can also freeze the pulled pork for future meals. Thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and reheat on the stove or in the microwave.

Pairing Suggestions

A BBQ pulled pork sandwich pairs beautifully with a variety of sides and beverages. Here are some suggestions:

  • Sides: Coleslaw, corn on the cob, baked beans, or potato salad make excellent accompaniments.
  • Drinks: Consider pairing your meal with iced tea, lemonade, or a light beer to complement the flavors.

Conclusion

There you have it—an incredibly tasty BBQ pulled pork sandwich recipe that’s perfect for any occasion. With its tender, flavorful pork and zesty BBQ sauce, this dish will surely become a favorite for you and your loved ones. So gather your ingredients, fire up the slow cooker, and prepare for a delicious meal that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Happy cooking!
