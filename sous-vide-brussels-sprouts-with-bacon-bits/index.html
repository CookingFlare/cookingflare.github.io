Sous Vide Brussels Sprouts with Bacon Bits


Brussels sprouts—small, earthy, and tender—are one of those vegetables you either love or hate. But when cooked correctly, their nutty flavor shines, and their texture transforms into something magical. Combine them with crispy bacon bits, and you’ve got a dish that might just win over even the staunchest critics. Cooking Brussels sprouts sous vide allows you to control their texture and preserve their natural sweetness better than any other method. This recipe will guide you step-by-step, ensuring you create the perfect side dish packed with mouthwatering flavor.

Why Sous Vide Is Perfect for Brussels Sprouts

If you’ve ever cooked Brussels sprouts on the stove or roasted them in the oven, you know it can be tricky to properly balance tenderness with caramelization. Boiling often blunts their flavor, while sautéing or roasting runs the risk of burning the edges before the interior cooks through. Sous vide, however, eliminates these issues. By cooking the Brussels sprouts in a precisely controlled temperature water bath, you can achieve fork-tender sprouts without losing their vibrant flavor.

Sous vide also lets you experiment with seasoning and fat additions, infusing the vegetables with bacon, butter, or garlic as they gently cook. This technique locks in moisture and creates the perfect blank canvas for finishing them off with a crispy, seared exterior.

Now that you know the benefits of using sous vide for Brussels sprouts, let’s dive into this irresistible recipe.

Ingredients

For the Sous Vide Brussels Sprouts:

  • 1 pound fresh Brussels sprouts (trimmed and halved)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional, for added richness)

For the Bacon Bits:

  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional, for caramelized sweetness)

For Garnish:

  • Zest of ½ lemon (optional, for brightness)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Equipment Needed

  • Sous vide immersion circulator
  • Large pot or sous vide container
  • Vacuum-sealed or resealable freezer bags
  • Skillet or cast-iron pan
  • Tongs
  • Paper towels

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Water Bath

Fill your sous vide container with water and attach the immersion circulator. Set the temperature to 185°F (85°C)—the sweet spot for tender, fully cooked Brussels sprouts. While the water bath heats up, prepare the sprouts.

Step 2: Prep the Brussels Sprouts

Trim off the tough ends of the sprouts and peel away any yellow or damaged outer leaves. Cutting them in half allows them to cook evenly and absorb the flavors of the seasonings.

Place the halved sprouts in a vacuum-sealed or resealable freezer bag. Add the butter, kosher salt, black pepper, and garlic powder. Seal the bag using the water displacement method if you’re not using a vacuum sealer: submerge the bag slowly into the water bath to press out air before sealing it tightly.

Step 3: Sous Vide the Brussels Sprouts

Once the water bath is ready, place the sealed bag of Brussels sprouts into the container. Ensure it’s fully submerged; use a clip to keep the bag from floating. Cook the sprouts for 60 minutes to achieve a perfectly fork-tender texture.

Step 4: Cook the Bacon

While your Brussels sprouts are cooking, prepare the bacon. Heat a skillet over medium heat and add the bacon slices in a single layer. Cook the strips for about 6–8 minutes, flipping occasionally, until crispy and golden brown.

Once the bacon is done, transfer it to a plate lined with paper towels to remove excess grease. Once cooled, chop the bacon into small bits. To add a touch of sweetness, toss the bacon pieces with a tablespoon of maple syrup—this will enhance the dish’s flavor and complement the Brussels sprouts beautifully.

Step 5: Finish with a Sear

After an hour in the sous vide, remove the bag from the water bath. Carefully cut it open and drain the sprouts, reserving the liquid if you want to use it later for extra moisture or flavor.

Heat a skillet or cast-iron pan over high heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil (or use some of the rendered bacon fat for extra flavor). Once the pan is hot, add the sous-vide-cooked Brussels sprouts, flat side down. Sear them for 2–3 minutes until they develop a golden-brown crust. Flip and sear the other side for an additional 1–2 minutes.

Step 6: Assemble the Dish

Transfer the seared Brussels sprouts to a serving dish. Sprinkle the crispy bacon bits generously over the top, ensuring every bite gets that smoky crunch. For a final touch, garnish with freshly chopped parsley and a sprinkle of lemon zest, which adds a layer of brightness to balance the richness of the butter and bacon.

Serving Suggestions

This dish pairs beautifully with a variety of main courses. Serve it alongside roasted chicken, pan-seared salmon, or even a perfectly grilled steak. The crispy bacon and tender Brussels sprouts also make a festive addition to holiday spreads or dinner parties.

Looking to round out the presentation? Consider adding toasted nuts like slivered almonds or crushed pecans for even more crunch and texture. A drizzle of balsamic glaze can also complement the earthy flavors of the sprouts while offering a hint of sweetness.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Brussels Sprouts

  1. Preheat Your Water Bath Properly: Make sure your water bath has reached its desired temperature before adding the sealed bag. Precision is key.
  2. Don’t Overcrowd the Bag: Leave room for the sprouts to expand slightly as they cook, ensuring even heat distribution.
  3. Use High-Quality Bacon: Thick-cut bacon provides the best results, with a hearty texture that stands up to the Brussels sprouts.
  4. Finish Immediately Before Serving: Searing the sprouts right before plating ensures they stay crisp and caramelized without steaming on the plate.

Sous vide Brussels sprouts with bacon bits is more than just a side dish—it’s a game-changer. By combining precise sous vide cooking with crispy seared edges and smoky bacon pieces, you’re creating a dish that’s indulgent yet balanced. The sous vide method preserves the sprouts’ freshness and enhances their natural sweetness, while the bacon adds a salty bite that makes every mouthful irresistible.

This recipe is proof that Brussels sprouts can be so much more than a divisive vegetable; with the right technique and flavors, they can become an enjoyable staple on your table.
