Flavorful and Easy Picnic Recipes for a Summer Escape


Summer is here, and it’s the perfect time to pack a picnic basket and head outdoors to enjoy the sunshine, the fresh air, and some delicious food. Whether you’re lounging in a park, at the beach, or exploring a hiking trail, having a well-packed picnic makes for a fun and memorable experience. But packing the perfect picnic spread can be tricky if you don’t know where to start. Fret not, we’ve got you covered with a selection of flavorful and easy picnic recipes that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

1. Classic Chicken Salad Sandwiches

A picnic isn’t complete without a classic sandwich, and chicken salad sandwiches are the perfect choice. They’re easy to make, satisfying, and incredibly versatile. You can make the chicken salad ahead of time, making it an ideal dish for busy mornings when you’re trying to get ready for your outing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked, shredded chicken (rotisserie chicken works great)
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries or grapes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 slices of whole-grain bread (or any bread of your choice)
  • Lettuce leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice.
  2. Add the chopped celery, onion, and dried cranberries or grapes, mixing everything together until well combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread a generous amount of the chicken salad mixture on one slice of bread, then top with a lettuce leaf if desired.
  5. Place another slice of bread on top to form a sandwich. Slice the sandwiches into halves or quarters for easy serving.

Tips:

  • For an extra crunch, add a handful of chopped nuts such as almonds or walnuts to the chicken salad mixture.
  • If you’re looking for a lighter version, substitute the mayonnaise with Greek yogurt or avocado.

2. Caprese Skewers

These colorful and fresh Caprese skewers are an ideal picnic snack, offering the perfect combination of juicy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil. The bite-sized portions make them incredibly easy to eat on the go, and they’re a hit with both kids and adults alike.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry or grape tomatoes
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella balls (ciliegine)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Toothpicks or small skewers

Instructions:

  1. Thread a cherry tomato, a fresh basil leaf, and a mozzarella ball onto each skewer or toothpick.
  2. Repeat the process until all ingredients are used up, creating small bite-sized skewers.
  3. Arrange the skewers on a serving platter, drizzle with balsamic glaze, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Tips:

  • For added flavor, drizzle with olive oil before serving.
  • You can prepare these skewers a few hours in advance and store them in the fridge until it’s time to leave for your picnic.

3. Mediterranean Quinoa Salad

Packed with fresh vegetables, grains, and a tangy lemon dressing, this Mediterranean quinoa salad is a light yet filling picnic option. It’s great for those looking for a healthy dish that’s full of vibrant flavors. The best part is that it’s easily customizable with whatever veggies you have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the quinoa and reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for about 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is fluffy and the water is absorbed.
  2. Remove from heat and let the quinoa cool to room temperature.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooled quinoa, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, olives, and feta cheese.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour the dressing over the quinoa mixture and toss everything together until well combined.

Tips:

  • You can swap out the feta cheese for goat cheese if you prefer.
  • Add some chopped fresh parsley or mint to enhance the freshness of this salad.

4. Veggie Wraps with Hummus

For a refreshing and healthy picnic option, try making veggie wraps filled with hummus. These wraps are full of crunchy veggies, creamy hummus, and a satisfying tortilla that’s perfect for packing. Plus, they can be made in advance and stored in the fridge until it’s time to leave.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large whole-wheat tortillas
  • 1/2 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 carrot, shredded
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup spinach leaves
  • 1/4 cup roasted red pepper strips
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Lay the tortillas flat on a clean surface.
  2. Spread a generous amount of hummus on each tortilla.
  3. Layer the veggies (carrot, cucumber, avocado, spinach, and roasted red peppers) on top of the hummus.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Carefully roll up each tortilla tightly, securing the fillings inside.
  6. Slice the wraps into halves or thirds for easy serving.

Tips:

  • Use different types of hummus for variety, such as roasted garlic or spicy harissa.
  • For added protein, include some grilled chicken or chickpeas in the wraps.

5. Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

Nothing says summer like a refreshing fruit salad. Packed with a variety of colorful fruits, this salad is both nutritious and delicious. The honey-lime dressing brings everything together with a touch of sweetness and tang. It’s the perfect side dish to balance out heavier main courses.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • 1 cup watermelon, cubed
  • 1 orange, peeled and segmented
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all the fruit.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, lime juice, and lime zest.
  3. Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the fruit and toss gently to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving.

Tips:

  • You can add any fruit of your choice such as mango, peaches, or grapes.
  • For extra flavor, sprinkle some chia seeds or coconut flakes on top.

6. Pasta Salad with Pesto and Sun-Dried Tomatoes

This pasta salad is the perfect blend of savory pesto, tangy sun-dried tomatoes, and fresh mozzarella. It’s a dish that can be served cold, making it ideal for your picnic basket. You can make it the day before, allowing the flavors to meld together for a richer taste.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta (fusilli, penne, or farfalle works well)
  • 1/2 cup pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mozzarella pearls or cubed mozzarella
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooled pasta, pesto, sun-dried tomatoes, mozzarella, and pine nuts.
  3. Toss everything together until the pasta is well coated.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips:

  • For extra flavor, you can add roasted vegetables like zucchini or bell peppers to the pasta salad.
  • If you want more protein, grilled chicken or shrimp can be added to the salad.

7. Lemon Bars

End your picnic with something sweet but refreshing. These lemon bars are the perfect treat, with a tangy lemon flavor and a buttery, crumbly crust. They’re easy to make and will definitely satisfy your sweet tooth without feeling too heavy.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • For the filling:
    • 4 large eggs
    • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
    • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. For the crust, combine the flour and powdered sugar in a bowl. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared dish.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the crust is lightly golden.
  5. For the filling, whisk together the eggs, sugar, flour, baking powder, lemon juice, and lemon zest until smooth.
  6. Pour the filling over the baked crust and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and lightly golden on top.
  7. Allow to cool completely before dusting with powdered sugar and slicing into squares.

Tips:

  • These bars can be made a day in advance and stored in the fridge for an extra cool treat.
  • Garnish with additional lemon zest for an added pop of flavor.

Conclusion

Planning the perfect picnic menu doesn’t have to be difficult. With these flavorful and easy-to-make picnic recipes, you’ll be able to create a spread that’s fresh, delicious, and satisfying. From savory chicken salad sandwiches to refreshing fruit salads, each dish offers a unique combination of tastes and textures that will please everyone. So pack your basket, grab a blanket, and enjoy the ultimate summer escape!
