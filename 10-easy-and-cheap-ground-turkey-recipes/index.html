10 Easy and Cheap Ground Turkey Recipes


Ground turkey is an incredibly versatile and affordable protein that can be used in a wide range of dishes, from tacos to pasta sauces. In this post, we’ll explore 10 easy and cheap ground turkey recipes that are perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or a special occasion.

Recipe 1: Ground Turkey Tacos

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 packet of taco seasoning
  • 8-10 taco shells
  • Shredded cheese, lettuce, and any other taco toppings you like

Brown the ground turkey in a skillet with the onion and garlic, then add the taco seasoning and cook according to the package instructions. Serve the turkey mixture in the taco shells and top with your favorite toppings.

Recipe 2: Ground Turkey Meatballs

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Marinara sauce and pasta for serving

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, breadcrumbs, egg, Parmesan cheese, parsley, and basil. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Form into meatballs and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until cooked through. Serve with marinara sauce and pasta.

Recipe 3: Ground Turkey Stuffed Bell Peppers

  • 4 bell peppers, any color
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheese for topping

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, onion, garlic, cooked rice, diced tomatoes, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Stuff each bell pepper with the turkey mixture and top with shredded cheese. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the bell peppers are tender.

Recipe 4: Ground Turkey and Veggie Burgers

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Buns and toppings for serving

Preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, onion, garlic, peas and carrots, breadcrumbs, egg, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Form into patties and cook for 5-7 minutes per side or until cooked through. Serve on buns with your favorite toppings.

Recipe 5: Ground Turkey and Mushroom Gravy over Egg Noodles

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup egg noodles
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions. In a large skillet, cook the ground turkey over medium-high heat until browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Add the onion and mushrooms and cook until the vegetables are tender. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Sprinkle the flour over the turkey mixture and cook for 1 minute. Gradually add the chicken broth, whisking constantly, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes or until the gravy has thickened. Serve the turkey mixture over the egg noodles.

Recipe 6: Ground Turkey and Spinach Stuffed Shells

  • 12 jumbo pasta shells
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cooked spinach
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Marinara sauce for serving

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the pasta shells according to the package instructions. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, onion, garlic, cooked spinach, ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, and egg. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Stuff each pasta shell with the turkey mixture and place in a baking dish. Cover with marinara sauce and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Recipe 7: Ground Turkey and Black Bean Chili

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheese and sour cream for serving

Cook the ground turkey in a large pot over medium-high heat until browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Add the onion and garlic and cook until the vegetables are tender. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes, kidney beans, chili powder, and cumin. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the flavors have melded together. Serve hot with shredded cheese and sour cream.

Recipe 8: Ground Turkey and Broccoli Fritters

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, onion, broccoli, panko breadcrumbs, egg, and olive oil. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Form into patties and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until cooked through. Serve hot with lemon wedges.

Recipe 9: Ground Turkey and Mushroom Crepes

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, onion, mushrooms, and garlic. Mix well with your hands until just combined. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, eggs, milk, and melted butter. Add the turkey mixture to the batter and mix well. Heat a small non-stick pan over medium heat. Pour in a small amount of the batter and tilt the pan to coat the bottom evenly. Cook for 1-2 minutes or until the edges start to curl. Loosen with a spatula and flip. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes or until cooked through. Serve hot with fresh parsley.

Recipe 10: Ground Turkey and Spinach Quesadillas

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
  • 4 large tortillas
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Salsa and sour cream for serving

Cook the ground turkey in a large skillet over medium-high heat until browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Add the onion and garlic and cook until the vegetables are tender. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. In a separate pan, warm the tortillas over medium heat for 30 seconds on each side. Sprinkle a quarter of the cheese on half of each tortilla, then top with a quarter of the turkey mixture. Fold the tortillas in half and cook for 2-3 minutes or until the cheese is melted and the tortillas are crispy. Serve hot with salsa and sour cream.

These 10 easy and cheap ground turkey recipes are perfect for a quick and delicious meal. Whether you’re looking for a taco filling, a meatball, or a quesadilla, ground turkey is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of dishes. With these recipes, you’ll be able to create a new meal every week without breaking the bank.
