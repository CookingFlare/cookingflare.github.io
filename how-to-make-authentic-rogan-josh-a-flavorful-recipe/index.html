How to Make Authentic Rogan Josh: A Flavorful Recipe


Rogan Josh, a Kashmiri dish, is a rich and flavorful stew made with tender lamb, fragrant spices, and rich gravy. This beloved recipe has its roots in the Mughal era, when it was perfected by skilled cooks in the royal kitchens of Kashmir. Today, it remains one of the most popular dishes in Indian cuisine, and its delicious flavors and rich history have made it a staple in many homes and restaurants around the globe. Ingredients:

500g lamb or beef, preferably mutton, cut into small pieces 2 tablespoons of ghee or vegetable oil 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 teaspoon of ginger paste 1 tablespoon of cumin seeds 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon of ground cardamom 1 teaspoon of ground cumin 1 teaspoon of coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper 1 teaspoon of garam masala Salt, to taste 2 tomatoes, finely chopped 2 tablespoons of tomato puree 2 tablespoons of Rogan Josh paste (adapt or custom make homemade version) 1 cup of water Fresh cilantro, to garnish Lemon wedges, to serve

Preparation Prep Time: 45 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Servings: 4-6 Instructions: To begin, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil in a large, heavy-based saucepan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds, and allow them to sizzle until fragrant. The secret to deep, satisfying flavors lies in their subtle whiff. Once the cumin seeds have released their aroma, add the finely chopped onion and sauté until it starts to brown. Next, add the minced garlic and ginger paste, and cook until the mixture becomes fragrant, releasing the pungent aroma of the paste. Once fragrant, add the ground cinnamon, cardamom, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and garam masala. Stir to combine, allowing the spices to infuse with the rich essence of the onions, garlic, and ginger. Add the marinated mutton or lamb pieces and cook until it starts to brown, allowing it to cook gradually. Add the sliced tomatoes and sauté for a few minutes until they lose their shape. Now, its time to add the tomato puree and Rogan Josh paste. Stir them well to combine with the meat and spices, with their bold, rich flavors mingling with the seasoned cumin-based oil mixture. Next, add the water, and blend with a whisk or a wooden spoon, along with salt and other spices. Ensure the flavors are sour, spicy, and prohibitively industrious—typically the secret nuances of an excellent Rogan Josh. Stir continuously to combine. Close the lid, observing the tempting steam rise from the openings in the dish. Allow the preparation to simmer at low heat for at least 20 minutes, letting the juices inside permeate the gravy, making the whole atmosphere under the lid nagat overhead lights enjoyable. 500g lamb or beef, preferably mutton, cut into small pieces 2 tablespoons of ghee or vegetable oil 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 teaspoon of ginger paste 1 tablespoon of cumin seeds 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon of ground cardamom 1 teaspoon of ground cumin 1 teaspoon of coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper 1 teaspoon of garam masala Salt, to taste 2 tomatoes, finely chopped 2 tablespoons of tomato puree 2 tablespoons of Rogan Josh paste 1 cup of water Fresh cilantro, to garnish Lemon wedges, to serve

Instructions:

Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee or oil in a large, heavy-based saucepan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and allow them to sizzle until fragrant. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it starts to brown. Add the minced garlic and ginger paste, and cook until the mixture becomes fragrant. Add the ground cinnamon, cardamom, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and garam masala. Stir to combine. Add the marinated mutton or lamb pieces and cook until it starts to brown. Add the sliced tomatoes and sauté for a few minutes until they lose their shape. Add the tomato puree and Rogan Josh paste. Stir well to combine. Add the water and blend with a whisk or a wooden spoon. Add salt and other spices to taste. Close the lid and allow the dish to simmer for at least 20 minutes. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice.

Tips for Creating the Perfect Flavorful Rogan Josh:

Spice Blending: Balance the spices to create a distinct flavor. Marination: Marinate the lamb or mutton for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour to make it tender. Use High-Quality Spices: Use authentic and aromatic spices to give the dish a rich flavor. Simmering Time: Allow the dish to simmer for at least 20 minutes to meld the flavors. Garnish: Garnish the dish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lemon juice to add freshness and brightness to the dish.
