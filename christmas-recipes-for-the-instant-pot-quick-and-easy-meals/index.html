Christmas Recipes for the Instant Pot: Quick and Easy Meals


Christmas is a time for joy, celebration, and gathering with loved ones. As the holiday season approaches, the hustle and bustle of preparing festive meals can often lead to stress. But fear not! The Instant Pot is here to save the day, allowing you to whip up delicious Christmas recipes in a fraction of the time. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of quick and easy Instant Pot recipes that are perfect for your holiday gatherings. From main courses to sides and desserts, let’s dive into the world of festive cooking!

Why Choose the Instant Pot for Your Christmas Cooking?

The Instant Pot has revolutionized home cooking. It combines multiple kitchen appliances into one, allowing you to pressure cook, slow cook, sauté, steam, and even make yogurt—all with a single device. This versatility is particularly beneficial during the busy holiday season when time is of the essence. With the Instant Pot, you can prepare hearty meals without spending hours in the kitchen, leaving more time for family and festivities.

Main Courses

Instant Pot Honey Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked ham (about 6-8 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 cup apple juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ham: Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern. This will help the glaze penetrate and create a beautiful crust.

  2. Make the Glaze: In a bowl, mix honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, cinnamon, and cloves until well combined.

  3. Set Up the Instant Pot: Pour the apple juice into the Instant Pot and place the trivet inside. Set the ham on top of the trivet.

  4. Glaze the Ham: Brush half of the glaze mixture over the ham.

  5. Cook: Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 15 minutes per pound. For an 8-pound ham, that’s about 120 minutes. Allow for a natural release.

  6. Finishing Touches: After releasing the pressure, brush the remaining glaze over the ham. Optional: To caramelize the glaze, transfer the ham to a baking dish and broil in the oven for 5-10 minutes.

Instant Pot Beef Bourguignon

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 2 cups mushrooms, quartered
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Beef: Set your Instant Pot to ‘Sauté’ mode. Add olive oil, then brown the beef cubes in batches. Remove and set aside.

  2. Sauté Vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion and garlic. Cook until softened. Add carrots and mushrooms, stirring occasionally.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Return the beef to the pot. Add red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.

  4. Cook: Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 35 minutes. Allow for a natural release.

  5. Serve: This dish can be served over mashed potatoes, rice, or crusty bread. It’s rich, hearty, and perfect for a holiday feast.

Side Dishes

Instant Pot Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Potatoes: Place cubed potatoes and garlic in the Instant Pot. Add chicken broth.

  2. Cook: Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. Quick release the pressure.

  3. Mash: Drain excess liquid if needed. Add butter, heavy cream, salt, and pepper. Mash until smooth and creamy.

Instant Pot Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook Bacon: Set the Instant Pot to ‘Sauté’ mode. Add bacon and cook until crispy. Remove bacon and set aside, leaving the grease in the pot.

  2. Sauté Brussels Sprouts: Add Brussels sprouts to the pot and sauté for 3-4 minutes until slightly browned.

  3. Add Liquid: Pour in chicken broth and balsamic vinegar. Stir to combine, scraping any browned bits from the bottom.

  4. Cook: Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 4 minutes. Quick release the pressure.

  5. Finish: Stir in the cooked bacon, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Desserts

Instant Pot Chocolate Lava Cake

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup butter
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Melt Chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, melt butter and chocolate chips together until smooth.

  2. Mix Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk eggs, egg yolks, sugar, and vanilla. Slowly add the melted chocolate mixture, then fold in flour.

  3. Prepare Ramekins: Grease four ramekins and pour the batter evenly into them.

  4. Set Up the Instant Pot: Pour 1 cup of water into the pot and place a trivet inside. Stack the ramekins on top.

  5. Cook: Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. Allow for a natural release for 5 minutes, then quick release.

  6. Serve: Invert the ramekins onto plates and serve warm with ice cream or whipped cream.

Tips for Successful Instant Pot Cooking

  1. Read the Manual: Familiarize yourself with your Instant Pot before starting. Each model may have slightly different features.

  2. Use the Right Liquid: Pressure cooking requires liquid to create steam. Always include enough broth or water in your recipes.

  3. Don’t Overfill: Avoid overfilling the Instant Pot. Keep the contents below the maximum fill line to ensure safe cooking.

  4. Adjust Cooking Times: Cooking times can vary based on the size of ingredients and your Instant Pot model. Always check for doneness.

  5. Experiment: The Instant Pot is versatile, so don’t hesitate to experiment with your favorite holiday recipes.

Conclusion

The holiday season doesn’t have to be stressful when it comes to meal preparation. With these quick and easy Instant Pot recipes, you can enjoy delicious Christmas meals without spending all day in the kitchen. From a succulent honey glazed ham to decadent chocolate lava cake, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Embrace the convenience of your Instant Pot and make this Christmas a memorable one filled with good food and great company. Happy cooking!
