Inexpensive and Delicious Homemade Teriyaki Sauce Recipes


Teriyaki sauce is a staple in Japanese cuisine, known for its sweet and savory flavor profile. While store-bought teriyaki sauce can be convenient, making your own at home is not only cost-effective but also allows you to customize the flavors to your liking. In this blog post, we will share three easy and delicious homemade teriyaki sauce recipes that won’t break the bank. Let’s get started!

Classic Teriyaki Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1/4 cup sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger. Stir well to combine.
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve cornstarch in water, then add it to the saucepan.
  3. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring occasionally.
  4. Cook for about 5 minutes or until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  5. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool before transferring it to a bottle or jar.
  6. Store in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

Honey Teriyaki Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

  5. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool before transferring it to a bottle or jar.
  6. Store in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

Pineapple Teriyaki Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, pineapple juice, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger. Stir well to combine.
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve cornstarch in water, then add it to the saucepan.
  3. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring occasionally.
  4. Cook for about 5 minutes or until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  5. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool before transferring it to a bottle or jar.
  6. Store in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

Conclusion

Making your own teriyaki sauce at home is not only budget-friendly but also allows you to experiment with different flavors. Whether you prefer the classic version, a honey-infused twist, or a tangy pineapple variation, these homemade teriyaki sauce recipes are sure to elevate your dishes.
