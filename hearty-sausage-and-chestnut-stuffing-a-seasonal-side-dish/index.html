Hearty Sausage and Chestnut Stuffing: A Seasonal Side Dish


Stuffing is an essential part of any holiday meal, adding warmth and flavor to the table. This recipe for Hearty Sausage and Chestnut Stuffing takes traditional stuffing to a whole new level with the addition of savory sausage and earthy chestnuts. The combination of these ingredients creates a dish that is both comforting and indulgent, perfect for any festive occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of sausage, casings removed
  • 1 cup of diced onion
  • 1 cup of diced celery
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of chestnuts, roasted and peeled
  • 8 cups of day-old bread, cubed
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried sage
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a large baking dish and set aside.

  2. In a large skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Use a wooden spoon to break up the sausage into small crumbles. Once cooked, transfer the sausage to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess grease.

  3. In the same skillet, add the diced onion, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the roasted and peeled chestnuts to the skillet and cook for an additional 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked sausage, sautéed vegetables, bread cubes, parsley, thyme, sage, salt, and black pepper. Toss everything together until well combined.

  6. Slowly pour the chicken or vegetable broth over the stuffing mixture, stirring gently to evenly distribute the liquid.

  7. Drizzle the melted butter over the stuffing mixture and toss once more to incorporate.

  8. Transfer the stuffing mixture to the greased baking dish, spreading it out in an even layer.

  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes.

  10. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.

  11. Once baked, remove the stuffing from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

  12. Serve the Hearty Sausage and Chestnut Stuffing alongside your favorite holiday dishes and enjoy the warm, comforting flavors!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, simply omit the sausage and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • Feel free to add additional herbs or spices to suit your taste. Thyme, rosemary, and sage are all great options.
  • For a touch of sweetness, you can stir in some dried cranberries or raisins before baking.
  • Leftover stuffing can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Conclusion

This Hearty Sausage and Chestnut Stuffing is the perfect side dish to complement your holiday feast. The combination of savory sausage, roasted chestnuts, and fragrant herbs creates a stuffing that is rich in flavor and texture. Whether you’re serving it alongside roast turkey or as a standalone vegetarian dish, this stuffing is sure to impress your family and friends. So, get ready to savor the warmth and indulgence of this seasonal favorite. Happy cooking!
