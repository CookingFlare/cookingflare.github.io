Brunch Cocktail Recipes That Are Both Sweet and Savory


Whether you’re hosting a weekend brunch or looking to elevate your midday indulgence, crafting cocktails that strike the perfect sweet-and-savory balance is a surefire way to impress your guests. Combining the rich, robust flavors of herbs and spices with hints of sweetness creates drinks that feel well-rounded, refreshing, and endlessly satisfying. Brunch, after all, is an occasion where culinary creativity reigns supreme—and your drink menu should reflect that spirit.

Below, we’ll walk you through a collection of sweet-and-savory brunch cocktail recipes that will transform your gathering into a flavorful feast. Each recipe blends bold flavors with subtle sweetness, offering something unique for every palate.

1. The Maple Rosemary Gin Fizz

This cocktail bridges the gap between spicy herbal notes and syrupy sweetness. Fresh rosemary pairs beautifully with gin, while maple syrup brings warmth and depth to the drink.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz gin
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 1 oz maple syrup
  • 1 egg white (optional)
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • Sparkling water
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. In a cocktail shaker, muddle one sprig of rosemary to release its oils.
  2. Add the gin, lemon juice, maple syrup, and egg white to the shaker. If you’re skipping the egg white, simply proceed to the next step.
  3. Shake vigorously for 15 seconds without ice to create a frothy texture, then add ice cubes and shake again.
  4. Strain into a chilled glass and top with sparkling water.
  5. Garnish with a sprig of rosemary for a rustic and aromatic touch.

2. Spicy Tomato Honey Bloody Mary

No brunch is complete without the classic Bloody Mary. This recipe introduces subtle sweetness from honey to temper the spiciness of hot sauce and the tartness of tomato juice, resulting in a beautifully balanced cocktail.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz vodka
  • 4 oz tomato juice
  • 1 tsp honey
  • ½ tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • ½ tsp hot sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • Pinch of smoked paprika
  • Celery salt (for rimming the glass)
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional garnishes: celery stalk, pickled vegetables, olives

Instructions:

  1. Rim a tall glass with celery salt by moistening the rim with lemon juice and dipping it in the salt.
  2. In a shaker, combine vodka, tomato juice, honey, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, lemon juice, and smoked paprika.
  3. Add ice cubes and shaker contents; stir or gently shake to combine the flavors.
  4. Pour the mixture into the rimmed glass filled with ice.
  5. Garnish with your preferred accompaniments, such as celery stalks, olives, or pickled vegetables.

3. Bacon-Washed Bourbon Old Fashioned

For a savory twist on the classic Old Fashioned, try this bacon-washed bourbon cocktail. This technique imparts the smoky, salty essence of bacon into rich bourbon, complemented by a hint of sweetness from brown sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz bacon fat-washed bourbon (see instructions below)
  • 1 tsp brown sugar syrup
  • 2 dashes bitters
  • Orange peel
  • Ice cubes

To Make Bacon-Washed Bourbon:

  1. Cook 2 slices of bacon and save 1 tbsp of the rendered fat.
  2. Once the fat cools slightly, pour it into a jar of bourbon and let it infuse for 4-6 hours at room temperature.
  3. Freeze the mixture for several hours until the fat solidifies; skim off the hardened fat and strain the bourbon to remove any residual solids.

Instructions for Cocktail:

  1. In a mixing glass, combine bacon-washed bourbon, brown sugar syrup, and bitters.
  2. Add ice and stir for 20 seconds.
  3. Strain into a rocks glass with a large ice cube.
  4. Express an orange peel over the cocktail for added complexity and garnish.

4. Honey-Lavender Bellini

For those who enjoy floral notes in their libations, this Honey-Lavender Bellini balances the perfume-like subtleties of lavender with sparkling sweetness from peach purée. It’s a sophisticated choice for brunch cocktails.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz honey-lavender syrup (see instructions below)
  • 2 oz peach purée
  • Prosecco or champagne to top
  • Ice cubes

To Make Honey-Lavender Syrup:

  1. Combine ½ cup honey, ½ cup water, and 1 tbsp dried lavender in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Simmer for 5 minutes, then let it cool and strain out the lavender.

Instructions for Cocktail:

  1. In a flute or coupe glass, combine the honey-lavender syrup and peach purée.
  2. Top with chilled Prosecco, stirring gently to mix.
  3. Garnish with fresh lavender sprigs for an aromatic touch.

5. Smoky Pineapple Mezcal Margarita

Bring tropical flair to your brunch table with this smoky, sweet margarita. Mezcal’s bold, smoky character melds wonderfully with pineapple and a salty-spicy rim.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz mezcal
  • 1 oz triple sec
  • 2 oz fresh pineapple juice
  • ½ oz lime juice
  • Tajín seasoning and kosher salt for rim
  • Ice cubes
  • Pineapple slice or jalapeño for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rim a glass with a mix of Tajín and kosher salt for a zesty kick.
  2. In a shaker, combine mezcal, triple sec, pineapple juice, and lime juice.
  3. Add ice and shake vigorously for about 20 seconds.
  4. Strain into the rimmed glass over fresh ice.
  5. Garnish with a slice of pineapple or a sliced jalapeño for a savory garnish.

6. Salted Caramel Irish Coffee

Warm, cozy, and utterly decadent, this cocktail is brunch perfection for cooler mornings. The sweetness of caramel pairs beautifully with the savoriness of sea salt and the robustness of Irish whiskey.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz Irish whiskey
  • 1 oz salted caramel syrup (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup hot brewed coffee
  • Whipped cream
  • Pinch of sea salt for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a coffee mug, combine Irish whiskey and salted caramel syrup.
  2. Add freshly brewed coffee and stir until well mixed.
  3. Top with a generous dollop of whipped cream.
  4. Sprinkle a pinch of sea salt over the whipped cream for a delicious finish.

Final Thoughts

Sweet and savory cocktails are a wonderful addition to any brunch plan. The interplay of these contrasting flavors keeps guests intrigued and delighted with every sip. Whether you prefer something smoky like the Bacon-Washed Old Fashioned, floral and light like the Honey-Lavender Bellini, or bold and spicy like the Tomato Honey Bloody Mary, there’s a recipe here to complement your brunch favorites.

Elevate your brunch game by incorporating these unique cocktails that cater to diverse tastes and leave your guests raving about your creativity! Try experimenting with the recipes—mix and match ingredients, adjust sweetness or spice levels, and personalize the garnishes. After all, brunch is all about merging indulgence with comfort, and these cocktails deliver the perfect balance every time. Cheers to delicious mornings!
