Sour Cream Coffee Cake: Comfort in Every Bite


Have you ever craved the comforting taste of a delicious sour cream coffee cake early in the morning? With a warm cup of coffee in hand, there’s nothing quite like sinking your teeth into the soft, moist crumb topped with brown sugar and cinnamon. Not only is this recipe easy to follow, but it’s a delightful treat to enjoy with friends and family. Let’s get started on making one of the most comforting cakes you’ll ever taste.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 1/3 cups white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Cinnamon Streusel Topping:
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 1/2 cup golden brown sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (177°C). Grease a 10-inch Bundt pan or a 9-inch round cake pan, ensuring you coat the inside well with cooking spray or butter.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together 2 cups of flour, 1 1/2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Set aside.

  3. Beat 3/4 cup of softened unsalted butter and 1 1/3 cups of white sugar in a large mixing bowl until light and fluffy. This should take about 3 minutes. Add 2 eggs, one at a time, to the mixture, beating well after each addition.

  4. Gradually merge in the dry ingredients by alternating between adding 1 1/2 cups of sour cream and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, making sure each ingredient is well-incorporated before adding the next. Be cautious not to overmix the batter.

  5. For the cinnamon streusel topping, combine 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup of golden brown sugar, and 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon in a separate bowl. Pour in 1/4 cup of melted unsalted butter and combine the ingredients using a fork or a pastry cutter, until the mixture resembles coarse sand.

  6. Spoon half of the cake batter into the prepared Bundt or cake pan, smoothing it out into an even layer. Sprinkle two-thirds of the cinnamon streusel topping mixture over the batter, ensuring the mixture coats the entire surface.

  7. Carefully spread the remaining cake batter in dollops over the streusel layer, leaving some space between each dollop to promote even baking. Smooth out the top layer of the batter before gently adding the rest of the streusel mixture on top, finishing with a decorative swirl pattern, if desired.

  8. Bake the cake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick or skewer inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. Your oven may vary in cooking time, so do not hesitate to keep an eye on the cake and adjust accordingly.

  9. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for about 20 minutes before gently running a knife around the edges, if you used a Bundt pan, to ensure easy removal. If you used a round cake pan, simply let it cool in the pan for an additional 10 minutes before transferring the cake to a serving plate.

  10. Serve your Sour Cream Coffee Cake warm or at room temperature, ideally partnered with a steaming cup of coffee or tea. This buttery, flavorful dessert is perfect for a weekend breakfast or an after-dinner treat. Savor each comforting bite and customize your serving by adding a dollop of whipped cream, vanilla ice cream, or a drizzle of caramel sauce for an extra touch of indulgence.

As you enjoy this classic Sour Cream Coffee Cake, reminisce on why it’s truly become a beloved comfort food for generations. It’s the melt-in-your-mouth goodness, the aroma that fills your home, and most significantly, the joy that comes from creating and sharing a timeless dessert with loved ones.

Now that your kitchen is filled with the rich scent of freshly baked cake, don’t forget to share this recipe with family and friends to spread the love and warmth of Sour Cream Coffee Cake. Happy baking!
