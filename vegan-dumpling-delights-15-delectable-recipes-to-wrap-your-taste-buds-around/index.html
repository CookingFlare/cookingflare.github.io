Vegan Dumpling Delights: 15 Delectable Recipes to Wrap Your Taste Buds Around


Dumplings are one of the world’s most beloved foods, offering a comforting and satisfying bite that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether served steamed, boiled, fried, or pan-fried, dumplings offer endless versatility in their fillings, making them ideal for any dietary preference. For those following a plant-based lifestyle, vegan dumplings are a fantastic way to indulge in this beloved dish without compromising on flavor.

In this post, we’re diving into 15 delicious vegan dumpling recipes that will wrap your taste buds in a world of savory goodness. From classic steamed options to creative fillings and dipping sauces, these dumplings are perfect for gatherings, meal prep, or a cozy night in.

1. Classic Vegan Dumplings

A classic dumpling is always a great place to start. For a traditional flavor, try a filling of sautéed mushrooms, cabbage, and carrots, seasoned with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a touch of garlic and ginger. The key to these vegan dumplings is ensuring the filling is well-seasoned and moist. Simply wrap the filling in dumpling wrappers and either steam, boil, or pan-fry.

Ingredients:

  • Dumpling wrappers (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 cup carrots, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the mushrooms in sesame oil until soft. Add the cabbage, carrots, garlic, and ginger, cooking until tender.
  2. Season with soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place a spoonful of the filling in each dumpling wrapper and seal.
  4. Steam or fry until golden and crispy.

2. Tofu and Spinach Dumplings

Tofu brings a creamy texture to dumpling fillings, while spinach offers a boost of nutrition and color. These tofu and spinach dumplings are packed with protein, iron, and flavor. The key here is pressing the tofu to remove excess moisture so the filling doesn’t become soggy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and crumbled
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the garlic in sesame oil, then add the spinach and cook until wilted.
  2. Stir in crumbled tofu, soy sauce, and nutritional yeast.
  3. Place the filling into wrappers and fold.
  4. Steam or pan-fry until golden brown.

3. Jackfruit and Sweet Potato Dumplings

Jackfruit is often used in plant-based cooking as a meat substitute due to its texture, and when combined with sweet potato, it makes a filling, sweet, and savory dumpling. The sweetness of the potato balances out the subtle flavor of the jackfruit.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can young jackfruit, drained and shredded
  • 1 medium sweet potato, cooked and mashed
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the jackfruit with soy sauce and spices until lightly crispy.
  2. Mix the cooked sweet potato with the jackfruit mixture.
  3. Spoon the filling into the wrappers and seal.
  4. Steam or fry until golden.

4. Vegan Buffalo Cauliflower Dumplings

Spicy, tangy, and utterly satisfying, these buffalo cauliflower dumplings are perfect for anyone craving a bold flavor. Cauliflower works wonderfully as a vegan filling, absorbing the tangy buffalo sauce while providing a bit of texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small head of cauliflower, chopped into small florets
  • 2 tbsp buffalo sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped green onions
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Roast the cauliflower with olive oil until tender.
  2. Toss the cauliflower in buffalo sauce and let it cool slightly.
  3. Place the buffalo cauliflower into dumpling wrappers and fold.
  4. Steam or pan-fry.

5. Miso and Eggplant Dumplings

The umami-packed flavors of miso and eggplant are perfect for a savory dumpling filling. The eggplant softens during cooking, providing a rich texture that’s complemented by the salty depth of the miso paste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium eggplant, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp miso paste
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the eggplant with sesame oil until soft.
  2. Stir in miso paste, soy sauce, and garlic powder.
  3. Fill the dumpling wrappers with the mixture and fold.
  4. Steam or pan-fry.

6. Vegan Kimchi Dumplings

Kimchi brings a fiery, tangy flavor to vegan dumplings, making them a delightful option for those who love bold tastes. This recipe combines the fermented, spicy goodness of kimchi with a simple vegetable filling for a unique dumpling experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup kimchi, chopped
  • 1 cup cabbage, finely shredded
  • 1/2 cup carrots, grated
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Mix the chopped kimchi, cabbage, and carrots with soy sauce and sesame oil.
  2. Spoon the mixture into dumpling wrappers and seal.
  3. Steam or fry until crispy.

7. Vegan Mushroom and Leek Dumplings

Mushrooms are a classic dumpling filling, and when paired with leeks, they offer a perfect balance of savory flavors. The mushrooms provide earthiness while the leeks add a subtle sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 leek, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the mushrooms and leeks in olive oil until soft.
  2. Season with soy sauce and let the mixture cool.
  3. Spoon the filling into dumpling wrappers and fold.
  4. Steam or pan-fry.

8. Thai-Inspired Vegan Dumplings

For a flavorful twist, these Thai-inspired dumplings are filled with a combination of tofu, Thai basil, and lime for a zesty, aromatic experience. Pair them with a peanut dipping sauce for an ultimate culinary adventure.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block tofu, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup fresh Thai basil, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Combine tofu, basil, and lime zest in a bowl, adding soy sauce to taste.
  2. Fill the dumpling wrappers with the mixture and seal.
  3. Steam or pan-fry and serve with peanut dipping sauce.

9. Vegan Sweet and Sour Dumplings

For a fun, sweet and savory twist on dumplings, try this filling made with pineapple, bell peppers, and tofu, topped off with a tangy sweet and sour sauce. These dumplings are sure to please those who love a touch of sweetness in their savory dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pineapple, chopped
  • 1/2 cup bell peppers, chopped
  • 1/2 block tofu, crumbled
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté tofu, pineapple, and bell peppers with soy sauce and rice vinegar.
  2. Allow the mixture to cool before placing it into dumpling wrappers.
  3. Steam or fry and serve with sweet and sour sauce.

10. Vegan Tempeh and Cabbage Dumplings

Tempeh is a protein-packed ingredient that’s perfect for a hearty dumpling. This recipe combines tempeh with cabbage and a touch of garlic for a satisfying bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup tempeh, crumbled
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the garlic, tempeh, and cabbage until tender.
  2. Add soy sauce for seasoning and let the mixture cool.
  3. Fill the dumpling wrappers with the mixture and steam or fry.

11. Vegan Spicy Seitan Dumplings

Seitan is often used in vegan cooking as a meat substitute, and when spiced with chili paste, it makes a flavorful filling for dumplings. This spicy option is perfect for those who crave a little heat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup seitan, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp chili paste
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the seitan with chili paste, soy sauce, and sesame oil.
  2. Let the filling cool before placing it in dumpling wrappers.
  3. Steam or fry until golden.

12. Vegan Coconut Curry Dumplings

The rich flavors of coconut and curry powder combine for a creamy, comforting dumpling filling that is sure to satisfy. This tropical-inspired recipe is perfect for anyone craving something different.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 cup carrots, grated
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Cook the carrots and peas with coconut milk and curry powder until soft.
  2. Let the filling cool before placing it into dumpling wrappers.
  3. Steam or fry until crispy.

13. Vegan Avocado and Black Bean Dumplings

The creamy texture of avocado pairs perfectly with the hearty black beans for a filling that’s both satisfying and nutritious. These dumplings offer a mild and delicious flavor with a subtle touch of spice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado, mashed
  • 1 cup black beans, mashed
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Mix the mashed avocado, black beans, lime juice, and cumin together.
  2. Fill the wrappers with the mixture and seal.
  3. Steam or fry until golden.

14. Vegan Pumpkin and Sage Dumplings

Pumpkin brings a naturally sweet flavor to vegan dumplings, and when paired with sage, it creates a warm and earthy filling that’s perfect for fall or any time of year.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Mix the pumpkin puree with sage, nutmeg, and soy sauce.
  2. Place the filling in dumpling wrappers and fold.
  3. Steam or pan-fry.

15. Vegan Mediterranean Dumplings

A delicious blend of olives, tomatoes, and chickpeas creates a fresh, tangy filling for these Mediterranean-inspired dumplings. Served with a creamy tahini dipping sauce, these dumplings are full of vibrant flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chickpeas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, chopped
  • 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

  1. Combine the chickpeas, olives, sun-dried tomatoes, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  2. Place the mixture into the dumpling wrappers and seal.
  3. Steam or pan-fry and serve with tahini dipping sauce.

Conclusion

Vegan dumplings are not only versatile but also incredibly delicious, offering a wide range of flavors and textures that will keep you coming back for more. From traditional flavors to inventive fillings inspired by global cuisines, there’s a vegan dumpling recipe for every taste. Whether you prefer them steamed, fried, or boiled, these dumplings will undoubtedly satisfy your cravings for comfort food while keeping your meals plant-based and full of flavor.

Next time you’re in the mood for something savory and satisfying, grab some dumpling wrappers and get creative with one of these delectable vegan fillings. Your taste buds will thank you!
