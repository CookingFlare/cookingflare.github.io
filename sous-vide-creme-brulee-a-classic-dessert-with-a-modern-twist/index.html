Sous Vide Creme Brulee: A Classic Dessert with a Modern Twist


Creme Brulee is a timeless dessert loved by many for its rich, creamy texture and contrasting crispy caramelized sugar topping. Traditionally, this delectable treat is prepared by baking a custard base in the oven and then broiling sugar on top to achieve the signature crackly caramel crust. However, with the advent of modern cooking techniques, we can now take this classic dessert to new heights using the sous vide method.

The Magic of Sous Vide

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves vacuum-sealing ingredients in a bag and cooking them in a temperature-controlled water bath. This method ensures precise temperature control and even cooking, resulting in consistently perfect dishes every time.

Elevating Creme Brulee with Sous Vide

By utilizing the sous vide technique, we can achieve a luxurious and velvety custard for our creme brulee, with the added benefit of a perfectly caramelized sugar crust. The secret lies in the gentle and controlled cooking process that the sous vide method provides.

Ingredients:

  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Additional granulated sugar for caramelizing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your water bath to 175°F (80°C) using your sous vide machine.

  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until the mixture becomes pale and creamy.

  3. Slowly pour in the heavy cream while continuing to whisk until well combined. Be careful not to incorporate too much air into the mixture.

  4. Add the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt, and whisk until everything is evenly distributed.

  5. Pour the custard mixture into individual ramekins or mason jars, leaving a small gap at the top for expansion.

  6. Tightly seal each ramekin or jar with a lid or plastic wrap to prevent water from seeping in during the sous vide cooking process.

  7. Gently place the sealed containers into the preheated water bath, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cook for 1 hour to 1 hour and 15 minutes, depending on the desired consistency. The longer you cook, the firmer the custard will be.

  8. Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the ramekins or jars from the water bath and let them cool at room temperature for about 30 minutes.

  9. Refrigerate the custards for at least 2 hours, or overnight, to allow them to set completely.

  10. When ready to serve, sprinkle a thin and even layer of granulated sugar on top of each custard.

  11. Using a kitchen torch, carefully caramelize the sugar until it turns golden brown and forms a crisp crust. Make sure to move the torch constantly to avoid burning the sugar.

  12. Allow the caramelized sugar to harden for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Serve the sous vide creme brulee as is for a classic presentation.
  • Add a dollop of whipped cream and a sprinkle of cinnamon for a touch of indulgence.
  • Garnish with fresh berries or mint leaves to add a burst of color and freshness.

Conclusion

With the sous vide technique, we can elevate the traditional creme brulee to new heights. The precise temperature control ensures a velvety custard, while the caramelized sugar crust adds a delightful crunch. Impress your guests with this modern twist on a classic dessert. So, go ahead and give this sous vide creme brulee recipe a try. It’s guaranteed to leave a lasting impression!
