Mexican Empanadas with Beef and Onions Recipe


If you’re craving a taste of Mexico, these authentic Mexican empanadas with beef and onions are sure to transport your taste buds south of the border. Empanadas are a beloved staple in Mexican cuisine, with their flaky crust and savory fillings. This recipe combines the richness of beef with the sweetness of caramelized onions, creating a flavor profile that will leave you craving more. Let’s dive into the details of creating these delectable treats in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup cold water

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

For Frying:

  • Vegetable oil

Instructions:

Making the Dough:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use your fingers to rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. In a small bowl, beat the egg with the cold water. Gradually add the egg mixture to the flour mixture, stirring until the dough comes together. If the dough is too dry, add a little more water, a teaspoon at a time.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for a few minutes until smooth. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Making the Filling:

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the ground beef until browned. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the chopped onions and cook until they are soft and translucent. Add the minced garlic, ground cumin, and paprika, and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  3. Return the cooked ground beef to the skillet and mix well with the onion and spice mixture. Season with salt and pepper, and stir in the chopped cilantro. Remove from heat and allow the filling to cool.

Assembling the Empanadas:

  1. Preheat the vegetable oil in a deep skillet or pot to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness. Use a round cutter to cut out circles of dough, approximately 5 inches in diameter.
  3. Place a spoonful of the beef and onion filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape, then use a fork to crimp the edges and seal the empanadas.
  4. Carefully place the assembled empanadas in the hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove from the oil and drain on a paper towel-lined plate.

Serving:

These Mexican empanadas with beef and onions are best enjoyed warm, with a side of salsa or guacamole for dipping. They make a fantastic appetizer for a Mexican-themed dinner or a satisfying snack for any time of day.

Conclusion:

With their flaky, golden crust and flavorful beef and onion filling, these Mexican empanadas are a true delight for the senses.
