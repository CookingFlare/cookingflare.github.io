Simple and Cheap Desserts: Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth on a Budget


Craving a sweet treat but don’t want to break the bank? You’re in the right place! Whether you’re looking for a quick snack to satisfy your sweet tooth or something to impress at your next gathering, delicious desserts don’t have to come with a hefty price tag. In this guide, we’ll explore a variety of simple and budget-friendly desserts that will leave you feeling indulged without draining your wallet. Get ready to discover easy-to-make desserts that use minimal ingredients, most of which you probably already have in your pantry!

1. Classic Rice Pudding

Rice pudding is a comforting, creamy dessert that’s as simple as it is delicious. The best part? It’s made from just a handful of ingredients, and you can make it in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of rice (white or brown)
  • 2 cups of milk (or plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • Ground cinnamon or nutmeg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the rice, milk, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil over medium heat.
  3. Once it starts to boil, reduce the heat to low and simmer, uncovered, for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the sugar and vanilla extract. Continue simmering, stirring often, until the mixture thickens to a creamy consistency (about 10-15 more minutes).
  5. Once it has reached the desired texture, remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  6. Sprinkle with cinnamon or nutmeg for an extra touch of flavor and serve warm or chilled.

Rice pudding is a classic dessert that’s adaptable. You can even add raisins or dried fruit to give it an extra layer of sweetness.

2. No-Bake Chocolate Oatmeal Cookies

Who says you need an oven to make cookies? This no-bake chocolate oatmeal cookie recipe is fast, easy, and perfect for those times you want to whip up something sweet without turning on the oven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of oats
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup of butter
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the sugar, cocoa powder, butter, and milk. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  2. Once the mixture is boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the oats, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Mix until well combined.
  4. Drop spoonfuls of the cookie mixture onto a parchment-lined tray.
  5. Let the cookies set at room temperature for about 30 minutes, or place them in the fridge to speed up the process.

These cookies are chewy, chocolaty, and full of texture from the oats. Plus, they don’t require any baking—making them a great last-minute dessert.

3. Banana Ice Cream

Have ripe bananas lying around and don’t know what to do with them? Turn them into a delicious, creamy ice cream that’s a healthier alternative to store-bought options. This banana ice cream requires just one main ingredient!

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice the bananas into small chunks.
  2. Place the banana slices on a baking sheet and freeze them for about 2 hours or until completely frozen.
  3. Once frozen, blend the banana slices in a food processor until they become creamy and smooth. You might need to scrape down the sides of the bowl a couple of times.
  4. Once the mixture is smooth, you can enjoy the banana ice cream immediately for a soft-serve consistency, or freeze it for an hour for a firmer texture.

Optional Add-ins:

  • A spoonful of peanut butter for a rich, nutty flavor
  • A dash of vanilla extract
  • Chocolate chips or crushed nuts for added crunch

This healthy, creamy treat is perfect for those hot days when you’re craving ice cream but don’t want to splurge on store-bought varieties.

4. Peanut Butter and Jelly Bars

If you love peanut butter and jelly, these easy-to-make bars will quickly become a favorite. With just a few basic ingredients, you can whip up a nostalgic dessert that’s perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of peanut butter
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup of your favorite jam or jelly (strawberry, grape, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease an 8x8-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the peanut butter, sugar, egg, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. Spread the mixture evenly into the prepared baking dish.
  4. Spoon small dollops of jam over the peanut butter mixture and use a knife or spoon to swirl it into the batter.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the bars are golden brown and a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. Let the bars cool completely before cutting them into squares.

These peanut butter and jelly bars are a great way to enjoy a sweet treat that’s both familiar and delicious.

5. Apple Crisp

Apple crisp is a simple yet satisfying dessert that doesn’t require any fancy ingredients. It’s a perfect way to use up apples and enjoy the comforting flavors of cinnamon and oats.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup of rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup of flour
  • 1/4 cup of butter, softened
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, mix the sliced apples, sugar, and cinnamon, then pour them into the prepared baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, combine the oats, flour, butter, and salt. Use your hands or a pastry cutter to mix everything until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples.
  5. Bake for 30-40 minutes or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.

Serve this warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra special treat.

6. Chocolate Mug Cake

When you’re craving chocolate cake but don’t want to bake a whole one, a mug cake is the answer. This recipe gives you a perfectly portioned dessert in just a few minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 4 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon of baking powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • 3 tablespoons of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe mug, mix the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add the milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth and well-combined.
  3. Microwave on high for 1 minute and 30 seconds. If it’s not fully cooked, continue microwaving in 20-second intervals.
  4. Let it cool slightly before digging in.

You can top your mug cake with whipped cream, ice cream, or even a drizzle of chocolate syrup to make it even more indulgent.

7. Lemon Sorbet

Sorbet is a refreshing and easy dessert to prepare, and it doesn’t require any special equipment like an ice cream maker. With just a few ingredients, you can enjoy a sweet and tangy treat that’s perfect for a hot day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 4-5 lemons)
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the water and sugar. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the syrup cool for a few minutes.
  3. Stir in the lemon juice and lemon zest.
  4. Pour the mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer.
  5. Every 30 minutes, use a fork to scrape the mixture to break up the ice crystals. Continue this process for about 2-3 hours until the sorbet is frozen and fluffy.

Lemon sorbet is a refreshing dessert that’s both sweet and tangy, and it’s incredibly easy to make. Plus, it’s a great way to use up fresh lemons!

Conclusion

Dessert doesn’t have to be expensive or complicated to be delicious. These simple and cheap dessert recipes offer a variety of options for satisfying your sweet tooth without spending a lot of money. From the creamy comfort of rice pudding to the refreshing chill of lemon sorbet, there’s something for everyone. So next time you’re in the mood for a treat, try one of these easy recipes—you’ll be amazed at how simple it can be to enjoy a sweet indulgence on a budget!
