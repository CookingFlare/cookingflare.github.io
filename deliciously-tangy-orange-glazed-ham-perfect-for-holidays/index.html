Deliciously Tangy Orange Glazed Ham: Perfect for Holidays


Holidays are a time for gatherings, laughter, and of course, delicious food. If you’re looking for the perfect centerpiece for your holiday feast, look no further than this mouthwatering recipe for Deliciously Tangy Orange Glazed Ham. This recipe combines the savory richness of ham with the bright, citrusy flavors of orange, creating a dish that is sure to impress your guests. Whether it’s for Easter, Christmas, or any special occasion, this ham is guaranteed to steal the show. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in fully cooked ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup orange marmalade
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ham: Preheat the oven to 325°F. Place the ham on a cutting board and score the top in a diamond pattern, about 1/4 inch deep. This will allow the glaze to penetrate the meat, giving it a delicious flavor.

  2. Make the Glaze: In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the orange marmalade, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, orange juice, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, and ground cinnamon. Stir the mixture until the sugar has dissolved and the glaze is well combined.

  3. Glaze the Ham: Place the ham on a rack in a roasting pan. Brush a third of the glaze over the ham, making sure to get into the scored lines. Cover the ham with foil and bake for 1 hour.

  4. Continue Glazing: After the first hour, remove the foil and brush another third of the glaze over the ham. Return it to the oven, uncovered, and continue baking for another 30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F, basting with the pan juices every 10 minutes.

  5. Rest and Serve: Once the ham is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 15-20 minutes before carving. Serve the ham with the remaining glaze on the side for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For an extra citrusy kick, add some orange zest to the glaze.
  • If you prefer a spicier glaze, sprinkle in a pinch of red pepper flakes.
  • Leftover glazed ham makes fantastic sandwiches or can be diced and added to omelets and salads.

With its succulent, tender meat and the irresistible tang of the orange glaze, this Deliciously Tangy Orange Glazed Ham is the perfect addition to any holiday table. The combination of sweet and savory flavors will leave your guests coming back for seconds and asking for the recipe. So, elevate your holiday feast with this show-stopping dish and create lasting memories with your loved ones.

Whether it’s a festive Easter brunch or a cozy Christmas dinner, this ham is sure to be the star of the show. The aroma of the ham roasting in the oven, the tantalizing blend of orange and spices, and the joy of sharing a delicious meal with family and friends will make any holiday gathering even more special. So, gather your loved ones, set the table, and let this Deliciously Tangy Orange Glazed Ham take center stage at your next holiday celebration. Cheers to good food, great company, and unforgettable moments.
