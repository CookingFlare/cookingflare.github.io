Homemade Thai Peanut Tofu: A Nutty Vegan Option


When it comes to vegan cuisine, few dishes encapsulate the spirit of flavor and creativity quite like Thai peanut tofu. This scrumptious dish blends the rich, nutty flavors of peanut sauce with the protein-packed goodness of tofu, making it not only delightful to the palate but also a nutritious choice suitable for any meal. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to spruce up your dinner table with something different, this recipe is bound to impress.

The Allure of Thai Cuisine

Thai cuisine is renowned for its intricate balance of flavors, combining sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements in each dish. This recipe for homemade Thai peanut tofu stands out by showcasing the creamy richness of peanut butter, enhancing the tofu’s texture while infusing it with bold flavors. The beauty of this dish lies not only in its taste but also in its versatility. It can be served over rice, quinoa, or even as a standalone dish alongside a fresh salad.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To prepare this delicious Thai peanut tofu, gather the following ingredients:

For the Tofu:

  • 1 block (14 oz) firm or extra-firm tofu
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil for frying

For the Peanut Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup (or agave syrup)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (or lime juice)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-2 teaspoons sriracha (adjust for spice preference)
  • Water (as needed for consistency)

For Garnish:

  • Chopped green onions
  • Crushed peanuts
  • Fresh cilantro or basil leaves
  • Lime wedges

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preparing the Tofu

  1. Press the Tofu: Begin by draining the tofu and pressing it to remove excess moisture. Wrap the block in a clean kitchen towel and place a heavy pan on top for about 20-30 minutes. This step is crucial for achieving a crispy texture when frying.

  2. Cut the Tofu: Once pressed, cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes.

  3. Marinate the Tofu: In a bowl, gently toss the tofu cubes with soy sauce, ensuring they are evenly coated. Allow the tofu to marinate for at least 15 minutes to absorb the flavor.

2. Making the Peanut Sauce

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the peanut butter, soy sauce, maple syrup, rice vinegar, sesame oil, grated ginger, minced garlic, and sriracha.

  2. Add Water: Stir the mixture until well combined. Gradually add water, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired sauce consistency. It should be creamy but pourable.

3. Cooking the Tofu

  1. Coat with Cornstarch: Sprinkle the corn starch over the marinated tofu and gently toss to coat. This will help create a crispy crust when fried.

  2. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet or frying pan, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat.

  3. Fry the Tofu: Once the oil is hot, add the tofu cubes in a single layer. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Avoid overcrowding the pan; you may need to do this in batches.

  4. Drain Excess Oil: Once cooked, transfer the tofu to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil.

4. Combine Tofu and Sauce

  1. Toss the Tofu: In a large mixing bowl, add the crispy tofu and pour the peanut sauce over it. Gently toss until the tofu is well-coated.

  2. Heat Through: If desired, return the tofu and sauce mixture to the skillet and cook on low heat for a few minutes to warm through, stirring occasionally.

5. Serve and Garnish

  1. Plate Your Dish: Serve the Thai peanut tofu over steamed rice or quinoa.

  2. Add Toppings: Garnish with chopped green onions, crushed peanuts, and fresh herbs like cilantro or basil. Serve with lime wedges on the side for an added burst of flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Thai Peanut Tofu

  • Choose the Right Tofu: For the best texture, use firm or extra-firm tofu. Silken tofu will not hold up well during frying.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to customize your peanut sauce by adding ingredients like coconut milk for creaminess or lime juice for a zesty kick.

  • Add Vegetables: Amp up the nutritional value by adding saut√©ed vegetables like bell peppers, broccoli, or snap peas to the dish.

  • Storage and Reheating: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat gently in the microwave or on the stovetop.

Nutritional Benefits of Thai Peanut Tofu

This homemade Thai peanut tofu is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Tofu is an excellent source of plant-based protein, making it a staple in vegan diets. It contains essential amino acids, iron, and calcium, supporting muscle health and bone density.

Peanut butter, while rich in calories, is high in healthy fats and protein, providing energy and promoting satiety. The addition of fresh herbs and vegetables adds vitamins and minerals, making this dish a well-rounded option for any meal.
