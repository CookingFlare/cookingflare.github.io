Mummy Dogs with Gory Blood Mustard Sauce: An Easy Halloween Dinner Recipe


Happy Halloween! Are you searching for a fun and spooky dinner idea for your frightful feast? Look no further than this delightful Mummy Dogs with Gory Blood Mustard Sauce recipe. It’s a great twist on a classic dish, perfect for your Halloween party or family gathering.

Ingredients

To make this creepy creation, you’ll need:

For the Mummy Dogs:

  • 6 hot dogs
  • 1 can of crescent roll dough
  • 2 tablespoons of mustard (use your favorite variety, but we recommend dark corn or whole grain for the gory appearance)

For the Blood Mustard Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup of mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon of whole grain mustard
  • A few drops of red food coloring

Additional Items:

  • Black and/or red food coloring
  • Water
  • Additional mustard for more “bloody” accents (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven according to the crescent roll dough package instructions, usually around 375°F (190°C).

  2. Unroll the crescent roll dough and separate into 6 rectangles. Gently stretch each rectangle so they are a bit bigger, wide enough to wrap around your hot dogs as smoothly as possible.

  3. Spread a thin layer of mustard along the length of each hot dog. Place a hot dog at the narrow end of each dough rectangle and roll it up, enclosing the hot dog entirely. Make sure there are no gaps where mustard is visible.

  4. Carefully pinch and wrap the dough to create a mummy-like appearance. Tuck any excess dough under the wrapped portions.

  5. Place the wrapped hot dogs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a silicone mat, and arrange them about 2 inches apart from each other.

  6. In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise and whole grain mustard for the Blood Mustard Sauce. If you want it to look more appetizingly gory, add a few drops of red food coloring and stir until the desired color is achieved.

  7. Bake the Mummy Dogs for 12-15 minutes, or until the crescent roll dough is golden brown.

  8. Once baked, remove the Mummy Dogs from the oven and let them cool for a couple of minutes on the baking sheet.

  9. Using a toothpick or a small knife, create small “wounds” on the Dough Mummy Wraps, and add a few dots of additional mustard to mimic more blood flow. Optionally, you can also draw some ominous facial features or symbols on the dough in the same way.

  10. Drizzle or pipe the Blood Mustard Sauce generously over the Mummy Dogs to complete the gory presentation.

Voilà! Your Mummy Dogs are ready to be devoured by ghouls and ghoulies alike. Send shivers down your guests’ spines with this creepy and delicious twist on a classic favorite.

Serving Suggestions

  • For an extra spooky touch, consider creating a “graveyard” presentation by arranging the Mummy Dogs on a platter with “decorative tombstones” made from small cookies, gummy candies, or even decorated sugar cookies.

  • If you’re looking to fully embrace the scary-hack vibe, you can serve your Boneless Wings with a side of soggy chips and gory dips for a delightful gut-munching experience. Just imagine the look on your guests’ faces when they see you serving something that looks almost real enough to be human flesh!

  • If you need to cater to a crowd, you can easily double or even triple the ingredients to create more Mummy Dogs, ensuring there’s enough for everyone to indulge in this fun and frightful delicacy.
