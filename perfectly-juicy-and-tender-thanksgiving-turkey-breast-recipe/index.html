Perfectly Juicy and Tender Thanksgiving Turkey Breast Recipe


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and enjoying a delicious feast. While the traditional whole turkey is often the star of the show, sometimes you may find yourself hosting a smaller gathering or simply prefer the taste of turkey breast. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to create a perfectly juicy and tender Thanksgiving turkey breast that will impress your guests and leave them craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 bone-in turkey breast (around 6 pounds)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 teaspoons dried sage
  • 2 teaspoons dried rosemary
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the turkey breast on a rack in a roasting pan, skin side up.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, dried thyme, dried sage, dried rosemary, kosher salt, and black pepper. Mix well until the herbs and spices are evenly distributed throughout the butter.

  3. Using your hands, carefully lift the skin of the turkey breast, being careful not to tear it. Spread half of the herb butter mixture directly onto the meat, under the skin. Massage the butter into the meat, ensuring it covers the entire breast.

  4. Next, spread the remaining herb butter mixture over the skin of the turkey breast, rubbing it in gently to create a flavorful crust.

  5. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the roasting pan. This will help keep the turkey breast moist during cooking and create a flavorful base for gravy later on.

  6. Place the roasting pan with the turkey breast in the preheated oven. Cook for about 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature of the thickest part of the breast reaches 165°F (74°C) when measured with a meat thermometer.

  7. Every 30 minutes, baste the turkey breast with the pan juices to ensure it stays moist and flavorful.

  8. Once the turkey breast reaches the desired temperature, remove it from the oven and tent it loosely with aluminum foil. Let it rest for at least 15 minutes before carving. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute within the meat, resulting in a juicier and more tender turkey breast.

  9. Carve the turkey breast into thin slices and serve alongside your favorite Thanksgiving sides. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a boneless turkey breast, you can use the same herb butter mixture and cooking instructions. Simply adjust the cooking time accordingly, as boneless turkey breasts tend to cook faster than bone-in ones.

  • Feel free to customize the herb butter mixture with your favorite herbs and spices. Adding garlic, lemon zest, or even a touch of cayenne pepper can elevate the flavors and add a personal touch to your turkey breast.

  • Don’t forget to save the pan juices to make a delicious gravy to accompany your turkey breast. Simply strain the juices into a saucepan, bring to a simmer, and thicken with a cornstarch slurry or a roux.

With this recipe, you can create a perfectly juicy and tender Thanksgiving turkey breast that will be the highlight of your holiday meal. Whether you’re cooking for a smaller gathering or simply prefer the taste of turkey breast, this recipe will ensure a delicious and memorable Thanksgiving feast. Enjoy and happy cooking!
