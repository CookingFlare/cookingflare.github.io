Strawberry Sage Sensation: Homemade Strawberry Sage Jam Recipes


Summer, the season of abundant sunshine, is also the time when strawberries ripen and burst in their juicy, sweet flavor. I find it a perfect moment to experiment with culinary creations that showcase the true essence of nature’s sweetness. In this post, I will share my secret recipe for a delightful concoction - strawberry sage jam!

Combining sweet strawberries and the fragrant, herbal notes of sage brings an extraordinary flavor profile to your palate. This homemade strawberry sage jam is not just a delicious, versatile topping for toast, biscuits, and pastries, but also a novel addition to your cheese board, accompanying your favorite artisanal cheeses as an elegant touch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled, and chopped
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 2 tbsp of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp of honey
  • 1 tsp of minced fresh sage
  • 1 tsp of pectin (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp of salt

Preparation:

  1. Begin by preparing the strawberries. Rinse the fruit under running water, then hull and chop them into small pieces, being careful not to puree or mash them. Set them aside in a stainless steel mixing bowl.
  2. Add the sugar, lemon juice, and honey to the mixing bowl with the strawberries.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the minced fresh sage and 1/4 cup of water. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat and cook until the sage leaves soften and release their aromatic oils, about 5 minutes. Strain the sage-infused water and add it to the strawberry mixture.
  4. Combine the pectin (if using) and the salt with the strawberry mixture, strengthening the jam’s natural gelling properties.
  5. Prepare a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan by greasing it gently with butter or cooking spray. Add the strawberry mixture to the saucepan and slow cook it, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  6. As the mixture comes to a steady boil, cook it for precisely 10 minutes, monitoring the heat to prevent scorching. Stir it from time to time and use a candy thermometer to measure the jam’s consistency, which should reach 220°F (104°C) for a glossy, perfect jam.
  7. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool just slightly before transferring it into sterilized jars or containers.
  8. Seal the jars, allowing the jam to rest for several days before tasting. This allows the flavors to meld and deepen, providing the tastiest jam possible.

Using your homemade strawberry sage jam is an invitation to taste a unique combination that’s as delightful as it is surprising. Try it on your morning toast, as a spread for sandwiches, or as a topping for your pancakes or waffles. Alternatively, you can use your imagination and incorporate this versatile concoction into savory dishes such as grilled cheese, crostini, or a savory-spiced chicken dish. This sweet-meets-herb sensation will undoubtedly become a summer favorite in no time.

Conclusion

In this post, I’ve shared a simple, elegant method to create this delicious and unique jam, perfect for savoring the fleeting flavors of the summer. This recipe’s versatility makes it essential for both novice and experienced cooks, as it showcases the best of what nature offers and leads to a fruitful, pleasurable culinary adventure. So, the next time you encounter a plethora of strawberries, don’t hesitate to give this strawberry sage jam a try – it’s the perfect touch for your culinary repertoire!
