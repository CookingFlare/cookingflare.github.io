The Aviation: A Classic Gin Cocktail That Takes You to New Heights


When it comes to classic cocktails, the Aviation stands out as a timeless favorite among gin enthusiasts. This elegant and refreshing drink has a fascinating history and a unique blend of flavors that make it a must-try for anyone looking to elevate their cocktail game. In this blog post, we will delve into the origins of the Aviation, explore its key ingredients, and provide you with a step-by-step guide to creating this delightful libation at home.

The History of the Aviation Cocktail

The Aviation cocktail has a rich and intriguing history that dates back to the early 20th century. It is believed to have been created by Hugo Ensslin, a bartender at the Hotel Wallick in New York City, around 1916. Ensslin first documented the recipe for the Aviation in his book “Recipes for Mixed Drinks,” where he outlined the cocktail’s distinctive combination of gin, lemon juice, maraschino liqueur, and a touch of violet liqueur.

Over the years, the Aviation has undergone some variations, particularly in the use of violet liqueur, which was difficult to find for a long time. However, with the resurgence of craft cocktails and the availability of artisanal liqueurs, the Aviation has experienced a revival, allowing mixologists and home bartenders to recreate this classic drink with authenticity.

Key Ingredients

The key to crafting a perfect Aviation lies in the careful selection of high-quality ingredients. Here are the essential components that come together to create this exquisite cocktail:

  • Gin: The backbone of the Aviation, gin provides the spirituous base for the drink. Opt for a dry gin with a well-balanced botanical profile to complement the other flavors in the cocktail.

  • Lemon Juice: Freshly squeezed lemon juice adds a bright and zesty element to the Aviation, balancing the floral notes of the other ingredients.

  • Maraschino Liqueur: This clear, cherry-flavored liqueur lends a subtle sweetness and complexity to the cocktail, contributing to its smooth and slightly fruity character.

  • Violet Liqueur: The inclusion of violet liqueur, such as Crème de Violette, gives the Aviation its signature pale lavender hue and imparts a delicate floral aroma to the drink.

Crafting the Perfect Aviation Cocktail

Now that we’ve laid the groundwork for the Aviation, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of creating this classic cocktail at home. To get started, you will need the following equipment and ingredients:

Equipment

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Jigger or measuring tool
  • Strainer
  • Coupe glass or martini glass

Ingredients

  • 2 oz. gin
  • ¾ oz. fresh lemon juice
  • ½ oz. maraschino liqueur
  • ¼ oz. violet liqueur
  • Maraschino cherry or lemon twist, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Fill a cocktail shaker with ice to chill the ingredients and the vessel.

  2. Add the gin, fresh lemon juice, maraschino liqueur, and violet liqueur to the shaker.

  3. Secure the shaker and shake vigorously for about 15 seconds to thoroughly chill and combine the ingredients.

  4. Place a strainer over the shaker and strain the cocktail into a chilled coupe glass or martini glass.

  5. Garnish the Aviation with a maraschino cherry or a twist of lemon peel for a touch of elegance and flavor.

  6. Serve and enjoy this classic gin cocktail.
