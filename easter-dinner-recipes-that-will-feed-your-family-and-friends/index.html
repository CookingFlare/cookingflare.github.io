Easter Dinner Recipes That Will Feed Your Family and Friends


Easter is more than just a day for colorful eggs and sweet treats—it’s a perfect occasion to gather your family and friends around the table and celebrate with a meal that satisfies and delights. Whether you prefer traditional dishes or a modern twist, this Easter dinner menu promises hearty, flavorful recipes with enough variety to please every palate. From succulent mains to vibrant sides and delectable desserts, you’ll find everything you need to create a memorable feast.

Planning the Perfect Easter Dinner

When it comes to Easter dinner, the key is to balance indulgence with freshness. Think of rich roasts complemented by crisp spring vegetables, and desserts that end the meal on a sweet, light note. Timing and preparation also matter. Many dishes benefit from advance prep, so you’re not overwhelmed on the day itself.

Here’s a complete breakdown to guide your culinary journey:

  • Main Courses: Roasts that carve easily and are packed with flavor.
  • Side Dishes: Seasonal vegetables and salads that add color and nutrition.
  • Breads and Starters: Warm, comforting, and full of character.
  • Desserts: Traditional and contemporary sweets perfect for Easter.

Main Courses

Herb-Crusted Roast Leg of Lamb

Lamb has long been an Easter staple, symbolizing renewal and spring. This herb-crusted roast delivers juicy, tender meat with an aromatic crust that will enrapture the senses.

Ingredients:

  • 1 leg of lamb (about 6-7 lbs)
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Combine garlic, rosemary, thyme, lemon zest, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  3. Pat the lamb dry, then rub the herb mixture all over the surface of the meat.
  4. Place the lamb on a roasting rack and roast for about 1 hour 30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (medium-rare).
  5. Rest the lamb for 15 minutes before carving.

The combination of fresh herbs and lemon offers brightness that balances the rich lamb flavor, making it irresistible.

Honey-Glazed Ham with Pineapple

For a crowd-pleaser, a honey-glazed ham offers both sweetness and texture; the pineapple adds a tangy tropical twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked ham (about 8 lbs)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • Fresh pineapple rings
  • Whole cloves for studding

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern and stud with cloves.
  3. Mix honey, mustard, and brown sugar in a bowl.
  4. Place pineapple rings on the ham, securing with toothpicks.
  5. Brush the honey glaze over the ham generously.
  6. Bake uncovered for about 1 hour 30 minutes, basting every 20 minutes.
  7. Let rest before slicing.

The sticky-sweet glaze caramelizes beautifully in the oven, elevating the presentation and flavor.

Side Dishes

Roasted Spring Vegetables with Garlic and Thyme

Easter celebrates spring, so vibrant vegetables take center stage. This simple roasting method brings out natural sweetness and adds savory touches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups baby carrots
  • 2 cups asparagus spears, trimmed
  • 1 lb new potatoes, halved
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss all vegetables with olive oil, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread evenly on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until vegetables are tender and lightly caramelized.
  5. Serve immediately, removing garlic cloves before plating.

Roasting separately like this allows each vegetable to maintain its unique texture and flavor.

Spring Pea and Mint Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

A refreshing salad is a great counterpoint to heavier dishes, and peas plus mint scream spring.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh or frozen peas (thawed)
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, roughly chopped
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Directions:

  1. Blanch peas for 2 minutes in boiling water, then shock in ice water.
  2. Drain and pat dry.
  3. Whisk lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper to make dressing.
  4. Toss peas, mint, and green onions with the vinaigrette.
  5. Chill until ready to serve.

This salad complements savory mains with its cool, zesty freshness.

Breads and Starters

Cheddar and Chive Scones

Scones aren’t just for tea—they’re an excellent accompaniment to savory Easter spreads.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Stir in cheese and chives.
  5. Add buttermilk, mixing until just combined.
  6. Turn dough out onto floured surface, pat into 1-inch thick disk.
  7. Cut into 8 triangles and place on baking sheet.
  8. Bake for 12-15 minutes, until golden.

Serve warm with butter or alongside the main course.

Deviled Eggs with Smoked Paprika

No Easter meal feels complete without deviled eggs. A dusting of smoked paprika adds depth and color.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs, hard boiled
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Smoked paprika, for garnish

Directions:

  1. Peel the eggs and cut each in half lengthwise.
  2. Remove yolks and mash with mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spoon or pipe the mixture back into egg whites.
  4. Sprinkle with smoked paprika before serving.

These elegant bites pair well with cocktails or as an appetizer before the main feast.

Desserts

Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Nothing says Easter like a classic carrot cake—moist with a hint of spice and topped with tangy cream cheese frosting.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/4 cups vegetable oil
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Frosting:

  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat oil, brown sugar, and granulated sugar.
  4. Add eggs, one at a time, mixing well.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients.
  6. Fold in carrots and walnuts.
  7. Divide batter between pans and bake for 30-35 minutes.
  8. Cool completely before frosting.
  9. For frosting, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth.
  10. Add powdered sugar and vanilla; beat until fluffy.
  11. Frost cake layers and assemble.

This cake doubles as a centerpiece and a sweet celebration of spring.

Lemon Poppy Seed Bundt Cake

Light and zesty, this cake is an excellent alternative for those seeking a bright dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a bundt pan.
  2. Whisk together flour, poppy seeds, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Beat butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then vanilla and lemon zest.
  5. Alternate adding dry ingredients and buttermilk, beginning and ending with dry ingredients.
  6. Stir in lemon juice.
  7. Pour batter into pan and bake for 50-60 minutes.
  8. Let cool 15 minutes, then invert onto a rack.
  9. Mix glaze ingredients and drizzle over cake.

The tangy glaze makes each slice fresh and inviting, perfect for the spring table.

Final Tips for a Stress-Free Easter Dinner

  • Advance Prep: Make desserts and side salads a day ahead to ease your workload.
  • Timing: Start roasting main dishes early so they can rest properly before serving.
  • Table Setting: Use simple spring-themed decor like pastel linens or fresh flowers to enhance your festive mood.
  • Beverages: Consider a crisp white wine, such as Sauvignon Blanc, or a sparkling water infused with lemon and mint for non-alcoholic refreshment.

This Easter, fill your home with the aromas of freshly cooked meals and fill your hearts with warmth and gratitude. By following these recipes and planning tips, you’ll create an unforgettable feast that nourishes body and soul alike.

Wishing you a joyous and delicious Easter celebration surrounded by your favorite people!
