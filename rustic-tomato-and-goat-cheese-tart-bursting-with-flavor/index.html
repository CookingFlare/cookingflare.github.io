Rustic Tomato and Goat Cheese Tart: Bursting with Flavor


As the summer months approach, the aroma of fresh tomatoes and herbs wafts through the air, beckoning us to create dishes that showcase their vibrant flavors. One such delight is the rustic tomato and goat cheese tart, a simple yet elegant creation that embodies the essence of summer. This recipe combines the sweetness of caramelized tomatoes with the tanginess of goat cheese, all wrapped up in a flaky pastry crust.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 2 large tomatoes, cored and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and cold butter. Using a pastry blender or your fingertips, work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Transfer the dough to a 9-inch (23 cm) tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges and press the dough into the corners of the pan.

In a small bowl, mix together the crumbled goat cheese, chopped thyme, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Spread the mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch (2.5 cm) border around the edges.

In a separate bowl, toss the chopped tomatoes with the olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Spread the tomato mixture over the goat cheese, making sure to stay within the border.

Roll out the remaining dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Use a pastry cutter or a knife to cut the dough into strips, about 1/2 inch (1 cm) wide. Place the strips on top of the tart, weaving them into a lattice pattern. Trim the edges and press the strips gently to adhere them to the tart.

Brush the egg wash over the lattice top and sprinkle with a pinch of salt. Bake the tart for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly and slightly caramelized.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more rustic look, don’t worry too much about the lattice pattern. Simply place the strips on top of the tart in a loose, crisscross pattern.
  • Substitute other types of cheese, such as feta or ricotta, for the goat cheese.
  • Add some heat to the tart by sprinkling a pinch of red pepper flakes over the goat cheese.
  • Use fresh herbs like basil or oregano instead of thyme for a different flavor profile.
  • Make the tart ahead of time and refrigerate it for up to 24 hours before baking. Simply let it come to room temperature before baking.

Conclusion

The rustic tomato and goat cheese tart is a true summer delight, perfect for warm-weather gatherings and outdoor meals. Its simplicity belies its complexity of flavors, with the sweetness of the tomatoes, the tanginess of the goat cheese, and the crunch of the pastry crust all working together in harmony. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your repertoire. So go ahead, get cooking, and let the flavors of summer burst forth in every bite!
