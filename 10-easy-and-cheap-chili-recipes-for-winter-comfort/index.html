10 Easy and Cheap Chili Recipes for Winter Comfort


Winter is the perfect time to cozy up with a warm bowl of chili. Whether you’re a meat lover or a vegetarian, there’s a chili recipe for everyone. And the best part? These recipes are not only delicious but also budget-friendly. So let’s dive into the world of chili and explore 10 easy and cheap recipes that will warm your soul this winter.

1. Classic Beef Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground beef until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve hot and garnish with your favorite toppings like shredded cheese, sour cream, or chopped green onions.

2. Vegetarian Lentil Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the lentils according to the package instructions.
  2. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add the cooked lentils, diced tomatoes, black beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  4. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve hot and top with avocado slices, cilantro, or a squeeze of lime juice.

3. White Chicken Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until softened.
  2. Add the cooked and shredded chicken, white beans, chicken broth, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  3. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Serve hot and garnish with chopped cilantro, shredded cheese, or a dollop of sour cream.

4. Spicy Turkey Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground turkey until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, chili powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve hot and top with sliced jalapenos, diced avocado, or a squeeze of lime juice.

5. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Chili

Ingredients:

  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the diced sweet potatoes, black beans, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  3. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Serve hot and garnish with chopped cilantro, diced avocado, or a dollop of Greek yogurt.

6. Smoky Chipotle Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can pinto beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground beef until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, pinto beans, chili powder, cumin, chipotle pepper, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve hot and top with sliced green onions, shredded cheese, or a dollop of sour cream.

7. Vegan Quinoa Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the cooked quinoa, black beans, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  3. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Serve hot and garnish with chopped cilantro, sliced avocado, or a squeeze of lime juice.

8. Three Bean Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until softened.
  2. Add the kidney beans, black beans, cannellini beans, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  3. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Serve hot and top with chopped green onions, shredded cheese, or a dollop of Greek yogurt.

9. Chunky Vegetable Chili

Ingredients:

  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced carrots, celery, onion, and minced garlic until softened.
  2. Add the diced tomatoes, kidney beans, black beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  3. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Serve hot and garnish with chopped parsley, a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese, or a drizzle of olive oil.

10. Slow Cooker Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the ground beef until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Transfer the cooked ground beef to a slow cooker.
  3. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, diced tomatoes, kidney beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker.
  4. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  5. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours.
  6. Serve hot and top with your favorite toppings like shredded cheese, sour cream, or chopped green onions.

Enjoy these 10 easy and cheap chili recipes that will warm your body and soul during the winter months. Experiment with different ingredients and spice levels to find your perfect bowl of chili. So grab a ladle, gather your loved ones, and indulge in the comforting flavors of homemade chili. Stay warm and happy cooking!
