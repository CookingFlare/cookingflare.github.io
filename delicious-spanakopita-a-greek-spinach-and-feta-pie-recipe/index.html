Delicious Spanakopita: A Greek Spinach and Feta Pie Recipe


Spanakopita, the quintessential Greek dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people around the world. This savory pie is a staple in Greek cuisine, made with a flaky crust, a generous helping of spinach, and a tangy sprinkle of feta cheese. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making a delicious Spanakopita that’s sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of filo dough (usually found in the freezer section of most supermarkets)
  • 1 bunch of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted, about 3-4 minutes. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Step 2: Prepare the Crust

Thaw the filo dough according to the package instructions. Cut the dough into four equal pieces and place each piece on a flat surface. Brush each piece with some olive oil to prevent the dough from sticking together.

Step 3: Assemble the Spanakopita

Place one piece of filo dough on a flat surface and brush with some olive oil. Spoon a quarter of the spinach mixture onto the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Sprinkle a quarter of the crumbled feta cheese and grated ricotta cheese over the spinach. Top with a quarter of the chopped parsley.

Step 4: Fold the Dough

Fold the 1-inch border over the filling to create a seal, then fold the opposite side over the filling, creating a triangle. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling ingredients.

Step 5: Bake the Spanakopita

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the Spanakopita triangles on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch of space between each pie. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is hot and bubbly.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Remove the Spanakopita from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Serve warm, garnished with additional parsley if desired. This Spanakopita is perfect as an appetizer or side dish, and it’s sure to become a favorite in your household.

Tips and Variations

  • To make individual Spanakopita, simply use smaller pieces of filo dough and adjust the cooking time accordingly.
  • Add some diced onions or mushrooms to the filling for extra flavor.
  • Use goat cheese or feta cheese with a higher fat content for an even tangier flavor.
  • Serve the Spanakopita with a side of tzatziki sauce or a simple green salad for a refreshing and satisfying meal.

With this recipe, you’ll be well on your way to creating a delicious Spanakopita that’s sure to impress your family and friends. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the authentic flavors of Greece in the comfort of your own home.
