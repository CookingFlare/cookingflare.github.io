Homestyle Chicken and Dumpling Soup: Comfort in a Bowl


There’s nothing quite like a steaming bowl of homestyle chicken and dumpling soup to warm the soul and comfort the spirit. This classic dish has been a staple in kitchens for generations, and for good reason – it’s hearty, flavorful, and incredibly satisfying. Whether you’re looking to whip up a cozy meal for a rainy day or simply craving a taste of nostalgia, this recipe is sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

For the Soup:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 carrots, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme and parsley for garnish

For the Dumplings:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine the chicken pieces, onions, carrots, celery, garlic, chicken broth, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Let the soup simmer for about 1 hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  2. While the soup is simmering, prepare the dumplings. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, mix the milk and melted butter. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.

  3. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and shred the meat using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and bring the soup back to a simmer.

  4. Using a spoon, drop spoonfuls of the dumpling batter into the simmering soup. Cover the pot with a lid and let the dumplings cook for about 15 minutes, or until they are cooked through and fluffy.

  5. Once the dumplings are cooked, remove the pot from the heat and discard the bay leaves. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  6. Ladle the homestyle chicken and dumpling soup into bowls, garnish with fresh thyme and parsley, and serve hot.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a shortcut, you can use rotisserie chicken instead of cooking a whole chicken from scratch.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables like peas, corn, or potatoes to the soup for extra flavor and texture.
  • If you prefer a thicker soup, you can stir in a mixture of cornstarch and water during the last few minutes of cooking.

The Magic of Homestyle Chicken and Dumpling Soup

There’s something truly magical about the process of making homestyle chicken and dumpling soup. The aroma that fills the kitchen as the soup simmers, the tender shreds of chicken, and the pillowy dumplings all come together to create a dish that’s more than just food – it’s a hug in a bowl.

Whether you have fond memories of enjoying this soup with loved ones or you’re discovering it for the first time, there’s no denying the comforting power of homestyle chicken and dumpling soup. So, the next time you’re in need of some culinary comfort, give this recipe a try and savor the warmth and coziness that it brings.
