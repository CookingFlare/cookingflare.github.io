Fermented Pickled Turnips: A Tangy and Crunchy Appetizer Idea


If you’re looking for a unique and tangy appetizer to add some zing to your meals, fermented pickled turnips might just be the ticket. These crunchy, brine-soaked turnips are a delicious addition to salads, sandwiches, or simply enjoyed on their own. Not only do they add a burst of flavor to your dishes, but they also bring a host of health benefits due to the fermentation process. In this blog post, we’ll explore the process of making fermented pickled turnips at home, including the ingredients, equipment, and step-by-step instructions. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients:

  • 4 large turnips, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, peeled and crushed
  • 1 tablespoon of coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon of mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon of sea salt
  • Filtered water

Equipment:

  • Glass jars with lids
  • Weight or fermentation weights
  • Cheesecloth or coffee filter
  • Rubber bands

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Turnips: After peeling and cutting the turnips into sticks, place them in a large bowl.

  2. Create the Brine: In a separate bowl, mix the sea salt with filtered water to create a brine solution. Ensure that the salt is completely dissolved.

  3. Add Flavorings: Add the crushed garlic, coriander seeds, and mustard seeds to the bowl of turnip sticks. Toss the turnips and flavorings together to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  4. Pack the Jars: Pack the turnip sticks and flavorings tightly into the glass jars, leaving some headspace at the top.

  5. Pour in the Brine: Pour the prepared brine over the turnips, ensuring that they are completely submerged. Leave about an inch of headspace at the top of the jar.

  6. Weigh Down the Turnips: Place a weight or fermentation weight on top of the turnips to keep them submerged in the brine.

  7. Cover and Ferment: Cover the jars with cheesecloth or a coffee filter and secure with rubber bands. Place the jars in a cool, dark place to ferment for 5-7 days, depending on your desired level of tanginess.

  8. Check for Fermentation: After 5 days, start tasting the turnips. Once they reach your preferred level of tanginess, remove the weights, seal the jars with lids, and refrigerate.

Serving Suggestions:

Fermented pickled turnips can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. Serve them as a tangy side dish with grilled meats, add them to salads or sandwiches for an extra kick, or simply enjoy them as a standalone snack. The crunchy texture and vibrant flavor of these pickled turnips are sure to elevate any dish they accompany.

Health Benefits:

Apart from their delicious taste, fermented pickled turnips also offer numerous health benefits. The fermentation process enhances the nutritional value of turnips, making them a good source of probiotics that support gut health. Additionally, they are rich in vitamins and minerals, adding an extra nutritional boost to your diet.

Conclusion:

Fermented pickled turnips are a delightful way to incorporate a tangy and crunchy element into your meals while reaping the benefits of fermentation. By following this simple recipe, you can create a batch of pickled turnips that will elevate your culinary creations and promote your well-being. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a flavorful journey with homemade fermented pickled turnips!
