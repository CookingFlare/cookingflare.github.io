The Best Christmas Dinner Recipes for Under $20


Christmas is a time for joy, warmth, and delicious food shared with loved ones. However, holiday meals can sometimes feel financially overwhelming. Fear not! With a bit of planning, creativity, and smart shopping, you can craft a festive, mouthwatering Christmas dinner without breaking the bank. This guide unveils some of the best Christmas dinner recipes for under $20—flavorful, memorable dishes that stretch every dollar while embracing traditional and innovative twists.

Why Budget-Friendly Christmas Dinners Are Absolutely Possible

The holidays often come with expectations of extravagant feasts—roasts, multiple sides, rich desserts—but affordability doesn’t have to mean sacrificing indulgence or celebration. Many ingredients can be transformed into holiday-worthy dishes, and cooking from scratch dramatically lowers costs. Budget-friendly meals also open the door to focusing on quality rather than quantity, and the joy of crafting every portion with care.

Planning Your Under $20 Christmas Dinner

To keep your holiday dinner under $20, consider the following tips:

  • Prioritize the protein: Choose affordable proteins like whole chickens, turkey legs, or plant-based alternatives.
  • Embrace seasonal produce: Root vegetables such as carrots, potatoes, and parsnips are inexpensive and perfect for festive sides.
  • Make it from scratch: Homemade gravies, bread, and desserts cut down on costs and add personal touches.
  • Shop smart: Look for sales, use coupons, and buy in bulk where reasonable.
  • Limit complex ingredients: Stick to pantry staples and simple flavor enhancers like herbs and citrus.

With this in mind, let’s dive into a thoughtfully curated Christmas dinner menu that’ll delight everyone around the table without the holiday budget blues.

Starter: Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

A vibrant, creamy soup is a wonderful way to kick off your Christmas dinner. Butternut squash, rich in flavor and naturally sweet, is budget-friendly especially when buying whole squash.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash ($3–4)
  • 1 large onion, diced ($0.50)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced ($0.10)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth or water with bouillon cubes ($1.50)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter ($0.30)
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the butternut squash in half, scoop out seeds, and drizzle with 1 tablespoon olive oil. Roast face-down on a baking sheet for 40 minutes or until tender.
  3. In a pot, sauté the onion and garlic in remaining oil until translucent.
  4. Scoop roasted squash flesh into the pot.
  5. Add vegetable broth and bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Blend the soup using an immersion blender or standard blender until smooth.
  7. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  8. Serve warm with a swirl of cream or a sprinkle of fresh herbs (optional).

Tip: Make this soup a day ahead—the flavors deepen overnight!

Main Course: Herb-Roasted Whole Chicken with Garlic and Lemon

A whole chicken is a classic centerpiece that’s surprisingly affordable and feeds several people. Adding fresh herbs, garlic, and lemon brings holiday elegance to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (3-4 lbs) ($8–10)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced ($0.20)
  • 1 lemon, halved and sliced ($0.50)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil ($0.30)
  • A few sprigs of thyme, rosemary, or sage (fresh or dried, $1)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Pat the chicken dry and rub it inside and out with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff the cavity with lemon halves and herbs.
  4. Place chicken breast-side up on a roasting pan.
  5. Surround the bird with extra lemon slices and sprinkle with more herbs.
  6. Roast for about 1 hour and 20 minutes until juices run clear and internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  7. Let rest for 10 minutes before carving.

Pro tip: Reserve the drippings for a simple yet flavorful pan gravy.

Side Dishes

1. Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a holiday staple, and by infusing them with garlic, you add festive complexity.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs potatoes (Russet or Yukon Gold) ($3)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced ($0.20)
  • 1/2 cup milk or cream ($0.50)
  • 2 tablespoons butter ($0.50)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel and cube potatoes.
  2. Boil until tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Drain and mash with butter, milk, garlic, salt, and pepper until creamy.
  4. Serve hot.

2. Honey-Roasted Carrots and Parsnips

Root vegetables bring warmth and color to the plate, and roasting them with honey adds sweetness and a caramelized finish that feels luxurious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced ($1)
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and sliced ($1)
  • 2 tablespoons honey ($0.40)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil ($0.15)
  • Salt, pepper, and fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the carrots and parsnips with olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast 25-30 minutes, stirring once.
  4. Garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

3. Simple Green Beans Almondine

Green beans bring vibrance and crunch. Tossed with toasted almonds, they feel festive yet stay within budget.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh green beans, trimmed ($2)
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds ($1)
  • 1 tablespoon butter ($0.25)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanch green beans in boiling water for 3-4 minutes; drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, melt butter over medium heat.
  3. Add almonds and toast until golden.
  4. Add green beans, toss, add lemon zest, and season to taste.
  5. Serve immediately.

Dessert: Classic Cinnamon Apple Crisp

To complement the savory dishes, this easy apple crisp captures holiday warmth at a low cost, taking advantage of budget-friendly apples in season.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups sliced apples ($2)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice ($0.25)
  • 1/2 cup oats ($0.50)
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar ($0.50)
  • 1/4 cup flour ($0.20)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup butter, cold and cubed ($0.50)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Toss apples with lemon juice and cinnamon; spread in a baking dish.
  3. Combine oats, brown sugar, and flour in a bowl.
  4. Cut butter into the oat mixture until crumbly.
  5. Sprinkle crumb topping evenly over apples.
  6. Bake 35-40 minutes until topping is golden and apples are bubbly.

Serve warm, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream if budget allows.

Putting It All Together: Serving Suggestions and Tips

This entire menu—roasted chicken, garlic mashed potatoes, honey-roasted carrots and parsnips, green bean almondine, and cinnamon apple crisp—comfortably fits under the $20 mark if you shop wisely. Pair it with simple sparkling water or homemade punch to complete the festive experience.

  • Delegate: If sharing your Christmas meal with family or friends, suggest each person bring a dish to spread out the workload and cost.
  • Prep Ahead: Many components can be made ahead (soup, dessert, even mashed potatoes), allowing you to enjoy the day stress-free.
  • Elevate with presentation: Use fresh herbs, colorful platters, or even simple DIY place cards to make the meal feel extra special.

Final Thoughts

Crafting an affordable Christmas dinner isn’t just about cutting costs; it’s about focusing on what truly matters—flavor, togetherness, and celebration. These recipes prove that simple ingredients, prepared thoughtfully, can deliver festive dishes that rival any lavish holiday spread.

This year, give yourself the gift of a flavorful, joyful, and budget-savvy Christmas dinner. Your wallet—and your loved ones—will thank you.
