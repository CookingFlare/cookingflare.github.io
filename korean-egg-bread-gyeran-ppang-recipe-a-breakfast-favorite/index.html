Korean Egg Bread (Gyeran-ppang) Recipe: A Breakfast Favorite


Korean Egg Bread, known as Gyeran-ppang, is a delightful street food that has captured the hearts and taste buds of many. This fluffy, savory treat is not only a popular breakfast option in Korea but also a perfect snack for any time of the day. With its golden crust and soft, egg-filled center, Gyeran-ppang is a dish that combines simplicity with comfort, making it a must-try for anyone looking to explore Korean cuisine.

What is Gyeran-ppang?

Gyeran-ppang translates to “egg bread” in English, and it is precisely that—a delicious bread filled with a whole egg. Traditionally, this dish is made using a sweet, fluffy bread dough that is baked until golden brown. The egg is cracked directly into the dough before baking, resulting in a warm, gooey center that is both satisfying and nutritious. Gyeran-ppang is often enjoyed fresh from the oven, with its aroma wafting through the air, enticing passersby to indulge in this delightful treat.

The History of Gyeran-ppang

Gyeran-ppang has its roots in Korean street food culture, where vendors sell various snacks to busy city dwellers. The dish gained popularity in the 1980s and has since become a staple in Korean bakeries and street stalls. Its rise in fame can be attributed to its simplicity, affordability, and the comforting flavors that resonate with many. Today, Gyeran-ppang is not only enjoyed in Korea but has also made its way to international food markets, where it continues to win over new fans.

Ingredients for Gyeran-ppang

To make authentic Gyeran-ppang at home, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Bread Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm milk (about 110°F or 43°C)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg (for the dough)

For the Filling:

  • 4 large eggs (one for each bread piece)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: chopped green onions, cheese, or bacon bits

Equipment Needed

Before you start, gather the following equipment:

  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Baking tray
  • Parchment paper
  • Oven

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm milk and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This indicates that the yeast is active.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Once the yeast mixture is frothy, add it to the flour mixture along with the melted butter and one egg. Mix until a dough forms.

  4. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour as needed.

  5. First Rise: Place the kneaded dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Shape the Bread

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.

  2. Divide the Dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Divide the dough into four equal pieces.

  3. Shape the Dough: Take one piece of dough and flatten it into a small circle, about 4-5 inches in diameter. Make sure the edges are slightly thicker than the center to hold the egg.

  4. Add the Egg: Carefully crack an egg into the center of the dough circle. Season with salt and pepper, and add any optional toppings you desire.

  5. Seal the Dough: Gently fold the edges of the dough over the egg, pinching to seal it. Place the filled dough seam-side down on the prepared baking tray. Repeat this process for the remaining pieces of dough.

Step 3: Second Rise

Cover the shaped dough with a kitchen towel and let them rise for another 20-30 minutes. This second rise will help create a light and fluffy texture.
