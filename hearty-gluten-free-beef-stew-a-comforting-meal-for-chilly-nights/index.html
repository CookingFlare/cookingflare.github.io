Hearty Gluten-Free Beef Stew: A Comforting Meal for Chilly Nights


Nothing warms the soul quite like a hearty bowl of beef stew on a chilly night. And if you’re following a gluten-free diet, you don’t have to miss out on this comfort food classic. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a delicious gluten-free beef stew that will satisfy your cravings and keep you cozy all night long.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 stalks celery, sliced
  • 1 parsnip, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. This will give your stew a rich flavor. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onions are translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the carrots, celery, parsnip, and mushrooms to the pot. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables start to soften.

  4. Return the beef to the pot and pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). The red wine adds depth to the flavor, but you can omit it if you prefer. Add the bay leaves, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper.

  5. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and simmer for about 1 ½ to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  6. Remove the bay leaves from the pot. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with a little water and stir it into the stew. Cook for an additional 10 minutes, or until the stew has thickened.

  7. Ladle the gluten-free beef stew into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot with your favorite gluten-free bread or over a bed of fluffy white rice.

Tips and Variations

  • For a heartier stew, you can add potatoes or sweet potatoes to the recipe. Simply peel and dice them, and add them to the pot along with the other vegetables.
  • If you don’t have red wine on hand, you can substitute it with an equal amount of beef broth.
  • To make this stew in a slow cooker, follow steps 1 and 2 as directed. Then, transfer everything to your slow cooker, add the remaining ingredients, and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours.
  • This gluten-free beef stew is the perfect make-ahead meal. It tastes even better the next day, so feel free to prepare it in advance and let the flavors develop overnight in the refrigerator.

So there you have it – a comforting and gluten-free beef stew recipe that will warm you up from the inside out. This hearty meal is perfect for those chilly nights when you crave something soul-soothing. Give it a try and enjoy the delicious flavors of this classic dish.
