Satisfying Shrimp and Crab Linguine in White Wine Sauce


If you’re looking for a luxurious and satisfying seafood pasta dish, look no further than this delectable Shrimp and Crab Linguine in White Wine Sauce recipe. This dish is perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in, and it’s surprisingly easy to make. The combination of succulent shrimp, tender crab meat, and a flavorful white wine sauce will surely impress your family and friends. So, let’s dive into the recipe and create a memorable dining experience right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of linguine
  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 ounces of lump crab meat
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup of dry white wine
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup of freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes for a hint of heat (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Linguine: Cook the linguine according to the package instructions in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped shallot, and sauté until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes.

  3. Cook the Seafood: Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until they turn pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes per side. Then, gently fold in the lump crab meat and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes. Remove the seafood from the skillet and set aside.

  4. Make the White Wine Sauce: In the same skillet, pour in the white wine and bring it to a simmer, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Let the wine reduce by half, then stir in the heavy cream and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  5. Finish the Dish: Add the cooked linguine to the skillet with the white wine sauce. Toss everything together until the pasta is well coated. Then, add the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter, Parmesan cheese, and chopped parsley. Gently toss until the butter has melted and the sauce has thickened slightly. Season with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if desired.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Divide the shrimp and crab linguine among serving plates, garnish with extra Parmesan and parsley if desired, and serve immediately.

Tips and Tricks

  • For the best flavor, use a good quality dry white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio.
  • Don’t overcook the seafood to keep it tender and juicy.
  • If you prefer a lighter version, you can substitute half-and-half for the heavy cream.

Conclusion

This Shrimp and Crab Linguine in White Wine Sauce is a true showstopper that will elevate any dinner table. The rich and creamy white wine sauce complements the delicate sweetness of the seafood, creating a harmonious blend of flavors with every bite. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a gourmet meal at home, this recipe is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in a truly satisfying culinary experience!
