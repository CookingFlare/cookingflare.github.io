How to Make Homemade Fruit Tarts


There’s something incredibly rewarding about making homemade fruit tarts. The combination of a buttery crust, creamy filling, and vibrant fruit topping creates a dessert that is as indulgent as it is beautiful. Whether you’re preparing them for a special occasion or simply want to serve a dessert that celebrates fresh, seasonal fruits, this recipe is sure to impress.

In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the step-by-step process of making perfect fruit tarts at home. From mastering the crust to selecting the best fruit combinations, this detailed tutorial will ensure you achieve bakery-quality results every time.

Step 1: Gather Your Ingredients

Every great recipe begins with quality ingredients. For fruit tarts, you’ll need the following:

For the Tart Crust (Pâte Sablée):

  • 1 ¾ cups (220g) all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened and cubed
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Filling (Pastry Cream):

  • 2 cups (500ml) whole milk
  • ½ cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup (30g) cornstarch
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Optional: Zest from half a lemon for added freshness

For the Fruit Topping:

  • Fresh fruit of your choice, sliced or diced: Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, kiwi, peaches, mango, blackberries—whatever is in season works best.
  • Optional: Apricot jam or honey for glazing

Step 2: Prepare the Tart Crust

Making the tart crust is all about achieving a melt-in-your-mouth texture that complements the filling and topping. Follow these steps carefully:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: Combine the flour, sugar, and salt in a large mixing bowl.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the cubed butter to the bowl and, using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture has a sandy, crumb-like consistency.

  3. Incorporate the Wet Ingredients: Mix the egg yolk and vanilla extract together in a small bowl, then add it to the crumbly mixture. Gently knead with your hands until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough; you want it to remain tender.

  4. Chill the Dough: Once the dough forms a ball, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Chilling allows the butter to firm up, making it easier to work with later.

  5. Roll Out the Dough: After chilling, roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness. Carefully transfer it into tart pans or a large tart tin. Press the dough gently into the edges. Trim any excess and prick the bottom of the tart shells with a fork to prevent puffing during baking.

  6. Blind Bake the Crust: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 12–15 minutes, then remove the weights and bake for an additional 5 minutes until lightly golden. Let the crust cool completely before filling.

Step 3: Make the Pastry Cream

The filling is the heart of any fruit tart, and a rich, velvety pastry cream serves as the perfect base for fresh fruit.

  1. Heat the Milk: In a medium saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it begins to simmer. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the sugar and cornstarch to prevent lumps.

  3. Whisk the Egg Yolks: Add the egg yolks to the sugar and cornstarch mixture, whisking vigorously until smooth.

  4. Temper the Eggs: Slowly pour a small amount of the warm milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to prevent curdling. Gradually add the remaining milk while continuing to whisk.

  5. Cook the Pastry Cream: Return the mixture to the saucepan. Cook over medium heat, whisking continuously, until the cream thickens. This should take 5–7 minutes.

  6. Add Flavor: Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract, butter, and optional lemon zest for a hint of brightness. Transfer the cream to a bowl and cover with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly onto the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour.

Step 4: Assemble the Fruit Tarts

Now comes the fun part—assembling your masterpiece!

  1. Fill the Tart Shells: Spoon the chilled pastry cream into the cooled tart crusts, spreading it evenly with the back of a spoon or an offset spatula.

  2. Prepare the Fruits: Wash and slice your fruits if necessary. Pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.

  3. Arrange the Fruits: Let your creativity shine by arranging the fruit on top of the pastry cream. You can create intricate patterns or keep it simple; either way, aim for a balanced mix of colors and textures.

  4. Glaze the Fruits (Optional): For added shine and to preserve the freshness of the fruit, gently heat apricot jam or honey with a splash of water until thin. Brush the glaze onto the fruit using a pastry brush.

Step 5: Chill and Serve

Once assembled, refrigerate the fruit tarts for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and ensure the pastry cream is set. Serve chilled, slicing carefully to preserve the appearance of the fruit.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Fruit Tarts

  1. Use Seasonal Fruits: Seasonal produce not only tastes better but also enhances the overall freshness and sweetness of your tart.

  2. Line Your Crust Properly: Always ensure the parchment paper and pie weights cover the entire base during blind baking to prevent uneven cooking.

  3. Customize the Filling: While pastry cream is traditional, you could use whipped mascarpone cheese, lemon curd, or even a layer of chocolate ganache for a twist.

  4. Experiment with Shapes: Mini tartlets are great for parties, while full-sized tarts make an elegant centerpiece for a gathering.

  5. Don’t Skip the Glaze: A simple glaze helps seal the fruit’s surface and keeps it looking fresh for longer.

Why Homemade Fruit Tarts are Worth the Effort

Making fruit tarts from scratch may seem daunting at first, but the process is endlessly rewarding. The buttery crust, creamy filling, and vibrant fruit topping come together to create a dessert that is not only delicious but also visually striking. Plus, the flexibility of the recipe lets you experiment with flavors and customize it based on your preferences.

Homemade fruit tarts bring elegance to any table and highlight the beauty of fresh fruits. By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll create desserts that rival those from professional bakeries. Whether you’re entertaining guests or treating yourself to a special dessert, these tarts are sure to impress.

Give this recipe a try and enjoy the satisfaction of baking something truly extraordinary!
