Allergy-Friendly Oatmeal Cookies: A Chewy and Nutritious Snack


If you’re on the lookout for a delicious and allergy-friendly treat, look no further than these mouthwatering oatmeal cookies. Packed with wholesome ingredients and free from common allergens, these cookies are perfect for anyone with dietary restrictions or allergies. Plus, they’re incredibly easy to make and oh-so-chewy!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gluten-free rolled oats
  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup creamy almond butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup dairy-free chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free rolled oats, oat flour, ground cinnamon, and salt. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the unsweetened applesauce, maple syrup, almond butter, and vanilla extract until smooth and well combined.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until a thick and sticky dough forms. If desired, fold in the dairy-free chocolate chips for an extra burst of flavor.

  5. Using a tablespoon or a cookie scoop, drop rounded portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Flatten each portion slightly with the back of a spoon or your fingers to create a cookie shape.

  6. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. Keep an eye on them to ensure they don’t overbake and become too crispy.

  7. Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes. Transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before enjoying.

Serving Suggestions

These allergy-friendly oatmeal cookies are perfect for any occasion. Enjoy them as an on-the-go breakfast, a satisfying midday snack, or even as a dessert. Pair them with a glass of almond milk or your favorite hot beverage for the ultimate treat.

Storage Tips

If you manage to resist devouring all the cookies at once, you can store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to five days. For longer storage, place them in the refrigerator for up to two weeks or freeze them for up to three months.

Allergy-Friendly Substitutions

This recipe is specifically designed to be allergy-friendly and can be easily customized to suit your dietary needs. Here are a few substitutions you can make:

  • Gluten-Free: Ensure that the oats and oat flour you use are certified gluten-free.
  • Nut-Free: Swap the almond butter for sunflower seed butter or another nut-free alternative.
  • Soy-Free: Double-check that your dairy-free chocolate chips are soy-free, or omit them altogether.
  • Sugar-Free: Replace the maple syrup with a sugar-free sweetener of your choice.

Feel free to experiment with different flavors and add-ins to make these cookies your own. You can toss in dried fruits, chopped nuts, or even a sprinkle of shredded coconut for added texture and taste.

So, whether you have dietary restrictions or just love a good oatmeal cookie, give these allergy-friendly treats a try. They’re chewy, nutritious, and sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. Happy baking!
