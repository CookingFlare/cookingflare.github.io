How to Can and Preserve Sweet Potatoes for Winter Meals


Sweet potatoes are a staple of fall and winter meals, and preserving them can help you enjoy their rich flavor and nutrients all year long. Canning sweet potatoes is an excellent way to keep them ready for use in soups, casseroles, pies, and a variety of other comforting dishes. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps for safely canning and preserving sweet potatoes, ensuring you have a ready supply of this nutritious vegetable when the weather turns cold.

Why Preserve Sweet Potatoes?

Sweet potatoes are not only delicious but also packed with vitamins, minerals, and fiber. They’re an excellent source of beta-carotene, vitamin C, potassium, and manganese, making them a highly nutritious food to include in your diet. Canning sweet potatoes offers the advantage of having them on hand throughout the winter months, ready to add to meals whenever you need them.

Preserving sweet potatoes through canning allows you to enjoy their taste and health benefits without the need for freezing, which can sometimes affect their texture. Properly canned sweet potatoes can last for up to a year when stored in a cool, dark, and dry place, making them a long-lasting addition to your pantry.

What You’ll Need to Can Sweet Potatoes

Before you begin the canning process, it’s important to gather the necessary tools and ingredients. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

Equipment

  • Pressure canner: Sweet potatoes require pressure canning because they are a low-acid food. This helps prevent the growth of harmful bacteria, such as botulism, which thrives in low-acid foods when not processed correctly.
  • Canning jars: Use standard canning jars, such as Mason jars, with new lids to ensure a proper seal.
  • Jar lifter: A jar lifter helps you safely remove jars from the hot water without burning yourself.
  • Canning funnel: This tool helps you fill jars without spilling, ensuring that the jars stay clean.
  • Lid magnet: A small tool to easily lift the sterilized canning lids from the hot water.
  • Large pot: For boiling the sweet potatoes before canning them.
  • Cutting board and knife: For peeling and cutting the sweet potatoes into chunks or slices.

Ingredients

  • Sweet potatoes: Choose firm, mature sweet potatoes without any soft spots or blemishes.
  • Water or syrup: Depending on whether you want to preserve your sweet potatoes in water or a light syrup.
  • Canning salt (optional): If you plan to can sweet potatoes in water, you may want to add a small amount of salt for flavor, though it’s not necessary.

Step-by-Step Guide to Canning Sweet Potatoes

Step 1: Prepare the Sweet Potatoes

Start by thoroughly washing the sweet potatoes to remove any dirt or debris. You’ll want to peel the sweet potatoes next. To make this easier, you can blanch them in boiling water for 3-5 minutes to loosen the skins before peeling. Once peeled, cut the sweet potatoes into uniform pieces. You can slice them, cube them, or cut them into chunks depending on your preference and how you plan to use them later.

Step 2: Pre-Cook the Sweet Potatoes

After the sweet potatoes are peeled and cut, it’s time to cook them before canning. There are two main options for pre-cooking: boiling or steaming.

  1. Boiling: Fill a large pot with water, bring it to a boil, and then add the sweet potato pieces. Cook them for about 5-10 minutes, or until they are just tender but not falling apart. Drain the sweet potatoes and allow them to cool slightly.

  2. Steaming: If you prefer to steam the sweet potatoes, place them in a steam basket over boiling water and steam for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are tender. This method retains more of the sweet potatoes’ nutrients since they are not immersed in water.

Step 3: Sterilize the Jars and Lids

While the sweet potatoes are cooling, it’s a good time to sterilize your canning jars and lids. Wash the jars in hot, soapy water, and rinse them well. Place the jars in a large pot of boiling water for 10 minutes to sterilize them, or run them through a dishwasher on the hottest setting.

The lids should also be heated but not boiled. Place the lids in a small pot of simmering water for about 5 minutes. This softens the rubber seal and ensures a proper fit on the jar.

Step 4: Pack the Jars

Once the sweet potatoes have cooled enough to handle, it’s time to pack them into the sterilized jars. Using a canning funnel, carefully pack the sweet potato pieces into each jar, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top. Be sure to pack the sweet potatoes tightly to minimize air pockets, but don’t crush them.

If you’re preserving the sweet potatoes in water, fill the jars with water, leaving that same 1-inch headspace. For a sweeter taste, you can use a light syrup made from sugar and water. For every 4 cups of water, you can add 1 cup of sugar to make the syrup.

Optionally, you can add 1/4 teaspoon of canning salt per quart jar for added flavor, though this is entirely up to you.

Step 5: Remove Air Bubbles

After packing the jars, it’s important to remove any air bubbles that may have formed between the sweet potato pieces. To do this, use a non-metallic utensil, like a plastic spatula, and gently slide it around the inside of the jar. This will help release any trapped air bubbles.

Step 6: Wipe the Jar Rims and Seal

Next, use a clean cloth or paper towel to wipe the rims of the jars. This step is crucial for ensuring a proper seal, as any residue left on the rim can prevent the lid from sealing tightly. Place the sterilized lids on the jars, and screw on the metal rings just until they are fingertip-tight. Do not overtighten, as this can prevent the air from escaping during the canning process.

Step 7: Process the Jars in a Pressure Canner

Sweet potatoes must be processed in a pressure canner to ensure they are safely preserved. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Add about 2-3 inches of water to the bottom of the pressure canner. Make sure you follow the manufacturer’s instructions for your particular canner.

  2. Place the jars into the canner using a jar rack or a canning basket. The jars should not touch each other, as this could cause uneven processing.

  3. Lock the lid onto the pressure canner and set the pressure according to your altitude. Typically, for quart jars, you will need to can sweet potatoes at 10-11 pounds of pressure. For pint jars, 10 pounds is usually sufficient.

  4. Once the canner has reached the correct pressure, process the jars for 90 minutes for quart jars or 75 minutes for pint jars. Be sure to maintain the proper pressure throughout the processing time.

Step 8: Allow Jars to Cool

After the processing time is complete, turn off the heat and allow the pressure canner to cool naturally. Do not open the canner until the pressure gauge reads zero. Carefully remove the jars from the canner using a jar lifter and set them on a clean towel or cooling rack. Let the jars cool completely for 12-24 hours.

Step 9: Check the Seals

Once the jars are cool, check the seals by pressing down on the center of each lid. If the lid doesn’t pop back, the jar is sealed correctly. Any jars that didn’t seal properly should be refrigerated and used within a few days.

Step 10: Store the Jars

Once you’ve confirmed that the jars are sealed, remove the metal rings to prevent rusting and store the jars in a cool, dark, and dry location. A pantry, cellar, or cupboard works well. Properly canned sweet potatoes can be stored for up to a year.

How to Use Canned Sweet Potatoes

Canned sweet potatoes are incredibly versatile and can be used in a variety of dishes. Some popular ways to incorporate them into your meals include:

  • Sweet Potato Pie: Use canned sweet potatoes as the base for a rich and creamy sweet potato pie filling.
  • Mashed Sweet Potatoes: Simply open a jar, drain the liquid, and mash the sweet potatoes with butter, cinnamon, and brown sugar for a quick side dish.
  • Sweet Potato Soup: Add canned sweet potatoes to soups and stews for added flavor and nutrition.
  • Casseroles: Use canned sweet potatoes in a savory casserole with marshmallows or pecans on top.

The possibilities are endless, and you’ll be glad to have this nutritious vegetable available during the colder months when fresh options are limited.

Final Thoughts

Canning and preserving sweet potatoes is an excellent way to enjoy their natural sweetness and nutritional benefits all year long. With the right preparation and equipment, the process is relatively simple and yields a long-lasting supply of this versatile vegetable. Whether you’re making pies, soups, or casseroles, you’ll have the convenience of having sweet potatoes ready to go, no matter the season.

Remember that proper canning techniques are essential to ensure food safety, so always follow the recommended processing times and pressure settings for your area. With these tips in hand, you’re ready to can sweet potatoes and add a little comfort to your winter meals!
