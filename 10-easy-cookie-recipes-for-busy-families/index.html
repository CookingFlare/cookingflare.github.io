10 Easy Cookie Recipes for Busy Families


When life gets hectic, indulging in homemade treats can be a comforting anchor amidst the chaos. Cookies are not only a delightful snack but also simple to make, especially with recipes tailored for families with packed schedules. Whether you need a quick dessert for dinner or a treat to brighten up lunchboxes, these cookie recipes are here to save the day. Each recipe below combines convenience with flavor, ensuring you spend less time in the kitchen without sacrificing quality or enjoyment.

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

The undisputed favorite for families everywhere, chocolate chip cookies never fail to deliver. This foolproof recipe takes minimal time and is ideal for busy evenings or last-minute bake sales.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • A pinch of salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until smooth. Add eggs and vanilla, mixing until combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually combine with the wet ingredients.
  4. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop batter onto the baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes or until edges are lightly golden.

Peanut Butter Blossom Cookies

Combining the creamy richness of peanut butter with a chocolatey twist, these cookies are sure to be a hit. The combination requires minimal prep but packs maximum flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 20-25 chocolate kisses

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line your baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix peanut butter, sugar, and egg until smooth.
  3. Shape into small balls and place on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 8-10 minutes and press a chocolate kiss onto each cookie while still warm.

Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Looking for a healthy yet indulgent choice? Oatmeal raisin cookies offer sweetness with heartier ingredients, perfect for busy mornings or post-school snacks.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 3/4 cup raisins

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar in a bowl. Add egg and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Combine dry ingredients and mix into the wet ingredients. Fold in oats and raisins.
  4. Scoop onto the prepared sheet and bake for 9-11 minutes.

Snickerdoodles

With their sugary-crispy coating and soft interior, snickerdoodles are timeless and quick to make. They’re wonderful for any family that loves a bit of cinnamon spice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar (divided)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and 1 cup of sugar together. Add eggs and vanilla.
  3. Whisk together flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and cinnamon, then mix into wet ingredients.
  4. Roll dough into balls and coat them with the remaining sugar and cinnamon mixture.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes until lightly golden.

Sugar Cookies

Ideal for cutouts or simple rounds, sugar cookies are versatile, quick to assemble, and perfect for custom decorations with the kids!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 1/3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugar together, then add egg and vanilla.
  3. Gradually mix in flour and baking powder. Chill dough for 30 minutes.
  4. Roll out, shape as desired, and bake for 8-10 minutes.

No-Bake Chocolate Oatmeal Cookies

For the busiest of days, these no-bake treats are lifesavers. They’re quick, indulgent, and completely fuss-free.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 6 tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 3 cups oats
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Stir in sugar, cocoa, and milk; bring to a boil for 1 minute.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in oats, peanut butter, and vanilla.
  3. Spoon onto wax paper and let cool.

Double Chocolate Cookies

Rich, fudgy, and indulgent, double chocolate cookies are ideal for those dedicated chocoholics in the family.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugar, then add egg and vanilla.
  3. Mix flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt; combine with wet ingredients. Fold in chocolate chips.
  4. Drop dough onto the sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes.

Coconut Macaroons

Light, chewy, and tropical, coconut macaroons are quick to prepare and require only a handful of ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded sweetened coconut
  • 2/3 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix coconut, condensed milk, and vanilla in a bowl.
  3. Form small mounds on the sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden.

Monster Cookies

Packed with oats, peanut butter, and candy-coated chocolate, monster cookies are the perfect grab-and-go treat for energetic families!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups oats
  • 3/4 cup chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup candy-coated chocolates

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter, sugar, and peanut butter. Add eggs and vanilla.
  3. Mix in oats, chocolate chips, and candies. Scoop onto the baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes.

Shortbread Cookies

Simple, buttery, and dependable, shortbread cookies require only three ingredients while offering maximum flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugar together until fluffy. Slowly add flour until a crumbly dough forms.
  3. Press dough into desired shapes and bake for 18-20 minutes.

Final Thoughts

No matter how busy you are, baking cookies doesn’t have to be complicated. With these 10 delicious recipes, you can whip up treats that your whole family will love. From the timeless chocolate chip cookies to quick no-bakes, there’s something here for every moment of the day. Make the most of these recipes to turn even the busiest of days into a memorable baking experience for the family.
