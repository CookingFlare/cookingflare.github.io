Leftover Makeover: 10 Recipes for Creative Casseroles


If you’ve ever found yourself staring at a fridge full of leftovers and wondering how to transform them into something exciting, you’re not alone. Leftovers are often seen as an afterthought, something to eat in a rush or when you’re too tired to cook. But with a little creativity, those leftovers can be turned into delicious, hearty casseroles that will impress your family and save you time and effort in the kitchen. Casseroles are the perfect comfort food, and they can be customized to incorporate just about any leftover ingredients you have on hand.

In this post, we’ll walk through 10 unique and mouthwatering casserole recipes that take leftovers to the next level. Whether you have leftover chicken, vegetables, pasta, or even mashed potatoes, these recipes will help you use what you have in a delicious and satisfying way.

1. Leftover Roast Chicken and Rice Casserole

After a Sunday roast, there’s usually some chicken left, but don’t let it go to waste! This casserole combines tender chicken with cooked rice, vegetables, and a creamy sauce to create a comforting, flavorful dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 cups cooked rice (white, brown, or any preferred variety)
  • 1 cup frozen peas or mixed vegetables
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or your favorite type)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, rice, vegetables, cream of mushroom soup, and sour cream. Stir until all ingredients are well coated.
  3. Add salt, pepper, and garlic powder to taste.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and spread it out evenly.
  5. Sprinkle shredded cheese on top and drizzle with olive oil.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Mashed Potato Casserole with Bacon and Cheddar

Mashed potatoes are a staple at any family dinner, and they make an excellent base for a creative casserole. This recipe adds crispy bacon, cheddar cheese, and a little bit of sour cream for richness and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups leftover mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, sour cream, crumbled bacon, and chopped green onions. Stir in butter, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mixture into a greased casserole dish and top with shredded cheddar cheese.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and golden brown.
  5. Serve with a side of steamed vegetables for a complete meal.

3. Leftover Spaghetti Pie

If you have leftover spaghetti, turn it into a fun and unique casserole with this spaghetti pie recipe. The pasta forms a delicious, crispy crust, while the savory tomato sauce, cheese, and meat filling make for a rich and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups leftover spaghetti
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1/2 lb ground beef or sausage (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. If using ground beef or sausage, cook it in a skillet over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat and stir in the marinara sauce. Set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, beat the egg and combine with ricotta cheese, mozzarella, and Parmesan. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Mix the leftover spaghetti with the ricotta cheese mixture, then press it into the bottom of a greased casserole dish to form a “crust.”
  5. Pour the meat sauce over the spaghetti mixture, spreading it evenly.
  6. Bake for 25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil and serve hot.

4. Vegetarian Casserole with Roasted Vegetables and Quinoa

For those with leftover roasted vegetables, this healthy and hearty vegetarian casserole is the perfect solution. The quinoa adds a nice texture and serves as the perfect base for the roasted vegetables, cheese, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover roasted vegetables (such as carrots, zucchini, and bell peppers)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the roasted vegetables, quinoa, vegetable broth, and olive oil. Season with dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and sprinkle with mozzarella or feta cheese.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the casserole is heated through.
  5. Serve with a fresh side salad for a light and nutritious meal.

5. Leftover Chili Mac Casserole

Chili and mac and cheese are two comfort foods that go perfectly together. If you have leftover chili and cooked macaroni, this casserole is an easy and satisfying way to bring them together in one dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover chili
  • 2 cups cooked macaroni or any pasta of your choice
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the leftover chili, cooked macaroni, and sour cream. Mix well.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and sprinkle with shredded cheddar cheese.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot.

6. Leftover Beef Stroganoff Casserole

Beef stroganoff is a rich and creamy dish, and if you have leftovers, it makes an excellent base for a casserole. Paired with egg noodles and a crispy topping, this casserole is sure to be a hit.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover beef stroganoff (with sauce)
  • 2 cups cooked egg noodles
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (for topping)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the leftover beef stroganoff with cooked egg noodles and sour cream.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and top with grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. In a small bowl, melt butter and toss with breadcrumbs. Sprinkle the breadcrumb mixture on top of the casserole.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

7. Leftover Turkey Tetrazzini

Turkey tetrazzini is a classic casserole, perfect for repurposing any leftover turkey. Creamy and savory, this dish combines turkey, pasta, and a delicious white sauce topped with melted cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover turkey, shredded
  • 2 cups cooked spaghetti or egg noodles
  • 1 can cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup peas (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the leftover turkey, cooked pasta, cream of chicken soup, sour cream, and peas. Stir until well combined.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and top with shredded mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  5. Serve with a side of garlic bread for a full meal.

8. Leftover Ham and Cheese Breakfast Casserole

If you have leftover ham from dinner, this breakfast casserole is a great way to use it up the next morning. With eggs, cheese, and bread, it’s a comforting and filling dish to start your day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover ham, diced
  • 4 cups cubed bread (day-old works best)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard powder (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a greased casserole dish, layer the cubed bread and diced ham.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and mustard powder (if using). Pour the egg mixture evenly over the bread and ham.
  4. Sprinkle with shredded cheddar cheese.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the casserole is set and golden brown on top.
  6. Serve with fresh fruit for a complete breakfast.

9. Leftover Veggie and Rice Casserole

This casserole is perfect for those who have leftover vegetables and rice from a previous meal. With a creamy sauce and a cheesy topping, it’s a great way to use up your leftovers while making a hearty and nutritious dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover cooked rice
  • 2 cups leftover roasted vegetables
  • 1 can cream of celery soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix the cooked rice, roasted vegetables, cream of celery soup, and milk. Stir until well combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and top with shredded mozzarella cheese.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve hot as a side or main dish.

10. Leftover Pizza Casserole

If you have leftover pizza, this casserole takes pizza night leftovers to the next level. The combination of pizza crust, sauce, cheese, and toppings makes this casserole a fun and flavorful meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 slices leftover pizza, chopped into pieces
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Chop the leftover pizza into small pieces.
  3. In a greased casserole dish, layer the pizza pieces and pour marinara sauce over the top.
  4. Sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese, then sprinkle dried oregano over the top.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and the casserole is heated through.
  6. Serve with a side salad or garlic bread.

With these 10 creative casserole recipes, you’ll never look at leftovers the same way again. Casseroles are a fantastic way to take what you already have and turn it into something new and exciting. So the next time you have leftovers sitting in your fridge, don’t just reheat them—transform them into a delicious casserole!
