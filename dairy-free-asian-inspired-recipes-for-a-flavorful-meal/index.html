Dairy-Free Asian Inspired Recipes for a Flavorful Meal


Are you a fan of Asian cuisine, but struggle with dairy allergies or intolerances? Don’t worry, because today we have some exciting and delicious dairy-free Asian inspired recipes that will satisfy your cravings and take your taste buds on a flavorful journey. From vibrant stir-fries to comforting soups, these recipes are packed with all the authentic Asian flavors you love, without the dairy. So, let’s dive right in and explore these mouthwatering dishes!

1. Coconut Curry Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, and snap peas)
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Cooked rice or noodles, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the coconut oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until fragrant and the onion is translucent, about 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the red curry paste, coconut milk, chicken broth, and soy sauce. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked chicken back to the skillet along with the mixed vegetables. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve the coconut curry chicken over rice or noodles, garnished with fresh cilantro. Enjoy!

2. Vegan Pad Thai

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons tamari or soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha (optional, for heat)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet or wok, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion, garlic, and red bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the tamari or soy sauce, lime juice, maple syrup, rice vinegar, and sriracha (if using) to make the sauce.
  4. Add the cooked rice noodles, shredded carrots, and bean sprouts to the skillet. Pour the sauce over the noodles and vegetables. Toss everything together until well coated and heated through, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve the vegan pad Thai garnished with chopped peanuts, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges on the side. Enjoy this flavorful and satisfying dish!

3. Spicy Garlic Shrimp Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha (adjust to taste)
  • Green onions, sliced, for garnish
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger. Sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  2. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the red bell pepper and snow peas. Sauté until the vegetables are crisp-tender, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, and sriracha.
  5. Pour the sauce over the vegetables in the skillet and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until heated through.
  6. Add the cooked shrimp back to the skillet and toss everything together until well coated.
  7. Serve the spicy garlic shrimp stir-fry over cooked rice, garnished with sliced green onions. Enjoy the bold flavors of this dish!

Conclusion

With these dairy-free Asian inspired recipes, you can enjoy the rich and diverse flavors of Asian cuisine without any worries about dairy allergies or intolerances. Whether you’re craving a creamy coconut curry, a tangy vegan pad Thai, or a spicy garlic shrimp stir-fry, these recipes have got you covered. So, gather your ingredients, get cooking, and treat yourself to a flavorful and satisfying meal!
