Taste of Vietnam: Tangy and Spicy Fish Sauce Recipes


If you’re a fan of bold and vibrant flavors, then Vietnamese cuisine is sure to captivate your taste buds. One of the key ingredients that adds a punch of flavor to many Vietnamese dishes is fish sauce. Known for its tangy and savory taste, fish sauce is a staple in Vietnamese cooking. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious and easy-to-make recipes that showcase the taste of Vietnam through tangy and spicy fish sauce.

1. Tangy Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together fish sauce, lime juice, honey, garlic, cilantro, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss to coat them evenly with the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the shrimp onto skewers.
  4. Grill the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and opaque.
  5. Serve the tangy grilled shrimp skewers hot with a side of steamed rice and a squeeze of lime juice.

2. Spicy Vietnamese Caramel Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1-2 Thai chili peppers, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice tolerance)
  • Chopped green onions and sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together fish sauce, soy sauce, brown sugar, and rice vinegar. Set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the garlic, ginger, and chili peppers. Cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces to the skillet and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Pour the fish sauce mixture over the chicken and stir well to coat. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 10-12 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Serve the spicy Vietnamese caramel chicken over steamed jasmine rice, garnished with chopped green onions and sesame seeds.

3. Tangy and Spicy Vietnamese Beef Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 Thai chili pepper, finely chopped
  • Mixed salad greens
  • Fresh herbs (such as mint, cilantro, and Thai basil)
  • Chopped peanuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together fish sauce, lime juice, sugar, vegetable oil, garlic, and chili pepper.
  2. Place the beef slices in a shallow dish and pour half of the marinade over them. Let it marinate for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Heat a grill pan or skillet over high heat. Cook the beef slices for about 2 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  4. In a large bowl, toss together the salad greens and fresh herbs. Drizzle the remaining marinade over the greens and toss to coat.
  5. Arrange the grilled beef slices on top of the salad greens. Sprinkle with chopped peanuts for an added crunch.
  6. Serve the tangy and spicy Vietnamese beef salad as a refreshing and light meal.

These tangy and spicy fish sauce recipes are just a glimpse into the vibrant flavors of Vietnamese cuisine. By incorporating fish sauce into your cooking, you can elevate your dishes and experience the authentic taste of Vietnam. Whether it’s grilled shrimp skewers, caramel chicken, or a refreshing beef salad, these recipes are sure to impress your family and friends. So, get ready to embark on a culinary journey and savor the taste of Vietnam right in your own kitchen!

Remember to adjust the spice levels according to your preference, and feel free to experiment with different herbs and vegetables to add your own twist to these recipes. Enjoy the burst of flavors and the unique tanginess that fish sauce brings to your dishes. Happy cooking!

Note: Fish sauce is widely available in most grocery stores and Asian markets. Look for a good-quality fish sauce with no additives for the best results.
