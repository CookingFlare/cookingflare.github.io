Spicy and Savory Gumbo Recipes Straight from the South


If there’s one dish that truly encapsulates the rich and diverse flavors of the American South, it’s gumbo. This hearty, spicy, and savory stew has its roots in Louisiana, where it has been a beloved staple for generations. Gumbo is a dish that reflects the unique cultural heritage of the region, blending French, Spanish, African, and Native American influences into a melting pot of flavors. In this blog post, we’re going to explore the art of creating two distinct yet equally delicious gumbo recipes: traditional seafood gumbo and rich chicken and sausage gumbo.

Seafood Gumbo

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 stalks of celery, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 pound of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound of crab meat
  • 1 quart of fish or seafood stock
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked white rice for serving
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Gradually whisk in the flour to make a roux, stirring constantly until it reaches a dark caramel color.

  2. Add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery to the roux, and cook until the vegetables are softened.

  3. Stir in the minced garlic, diced tomatoes, bay leaves, thyme, and Cajun seasoning. Pour in the fish or seafood stock, and bring the mixture to a simmer.

  4. Add the shrimp and crab meat to the pot, and let the gumbo simmer for about 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Season the gumbo with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the bay leaves.

  6. Serve the seafood gumbo over cooked white rice, and garnish with chopped green onions.

Chicken and Sausage Gumbo

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 3 stalks of celery, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 pound of Andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 quart of chicken stock
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked white rice for serving
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Gradually whisk in the flour to make a roux, stirring constantly until it reaches a dark caramel color.

  2. Add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery to the roux, and cook until the vegetables are softened.

  3. Stir in the minced garlic, diced tomatoes, bay leaves, thyme, and paprika. Pour in the chicken stock, and bring the mixture to a simmer.

  4. Add the diced chicken and sliced Andouille sausage to the pot, and let the gumbo simmer for about 30-40 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop.

  5. Season the gumbo with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the bay leaves.

  6. Serve the chicken and sausage gumbo over cooked white rice, and garnish with chopped green onions.

Gumbo is a dish that rewards patience and attention to detail. Whether you opt for the rich flavors of the seafood gumbo or the comforting warmth of the chicken and sausage gumbo, these recipes are sure to transport you to the heart of the South with every delicious spoonful. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the taste of the South with these soul-warming gumbo recipes.
