Classic Eggs Benedict: A Brunch Favorite Recipe


Brunch is a beloved mealtime tradition that combines the best of both breakfast and lunch. And when it comes to brunch, there’s one dish that stands out as a true classic: Eggs Benedict. This delectable dish is perfect for those lazy weekend mornings when you want to treat yourself to a luxurious and indulgent meal. In this blog post, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of creating the perfect Classic Eggs Benedict.

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 English muffins, split and toasted
  • 8 slices of Canadian bacon
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • Hollandaise sauce:
    • 3 egg yolks
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the Hollandaise sauce. In a heatproof bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and lemon juice until well combined. Place the bowl over a saucepan of simmering water, making sure the bottom of the bowl doesn’t touch the water.

  2. Slowly drizzle in the melted butter while whisking constantly. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens to a creamy consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Set aside and keep warm.

  3. Fill a large saucepan with water and bring it to a gentle simmer. Add the white vinegar to the water and stir gently. The vinegar helps to coagulate the egg whites, ensuring a perfectly poached egg.

  4. Crack one egg into a small bowl or ramekin. Create a gentle whirlpool in the simmering water using a spoon. Carefully slide the egg into the center of the whirlpool, allowing the water to envelop the egg. Poach the egg for about 3-4 minutes until the white is set but the yolk is still runny.

  5. Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove the poached egg from the water and set it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess water. Repeat this process with the remaining eggs.

  6. Take the toasted English muffin halves and place two slices of Canadian bacon on each half.

  7. Carefully place a poached egg on top of each Canadian bacon slice.

  8. Spoon a generous amount of Hollandaise sauce over each poached egg, allowing it to cascade down the sides.

  9. Serve the Classic Eggs Benedict immediately, garnished with a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper and a pinch of chopped parsley.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian version of Eggs Benedict, you can replace the Canadian bacon with sautéed spinach or avocado slices.
  • For added flavor, you can sprinkle a pinch of paprika or cayenne pepper on top of the Hollandaise sauce.
  • To make the process easier, you can prepare the Hollandaise sauce in advance and reheat it gently before serving.

Now that you have the recipe for Classic Eggs Benedict, it’s time to gather your ingredients and bring this brunch favorite to life in your own kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a brunch gathering or simply treating yourself to a special weekend meal, Eggs Benedict is sure to impress. So, put on your chef’s hat and get ready to indulge in this classic and delicious dish. Enjoy!
