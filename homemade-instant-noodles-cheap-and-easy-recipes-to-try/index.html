Homemade Instant Noodles: Cheap and Easy Recipes to Try


Instant noodles have long been a staple in households around the world, offering convenience, comfort, and quick satisfaction on busy days. But did you know you can elevate this simple dish by making homemade instant noodles? Preparing your own version of this pantry classic gives you the freedom to customize flavors, control ingredients, and even save money. Plus, it’s fun, creative, and surprisingly easy! Whether you’re looking for a family-friendly culinary project or just want to reduce your reliance on store-bought products, this guide will walk you through step-by-step recipes for homemade instant noodles that are cost-effective, delicious, and wholesome.

Why Make Instant Noodles From Scratch?

There’s nothing wrong with store-bought instant noodles, but making them at home comes with a variety of benefits:

  1. Customization: When you make your own instant noodles, you can control the flavorings to match your dietary preferences. Whether you prefer a spicy broth, creamy consistency, or vegan options, homemade is the way to go.
  2. Healthier Ingredients: Packaged instant noodles often contain high sodium and artificial preservatives. With homemade noodles, you can select fresh and nutritious ingredients.
  3. Reduced Costs: Buying individual ingredients in bulk is often cheaper than purchasing pre-packaged meals, especially if you plan to make this a staple in your kitchen.
  4. Sustainable Option: Homemade noodles eliminate the wasteful single-use packaging often associated with store-bought instant noodles.

The process might seem complicated at first, but armed with the right recipes and techniques, you’ll soon discover how simple it can be to create flavorful instant noodles right in your own kitchen.

Basic Components of Homemade Instant Noodles

Before diving into recipes, let’s break down the components of homemade instant noodles:

1. Noodles

The foundation of any instant noodle dish is, well, the noodles themselves. You can use fresh noodles or dried noodles, depending on your preference. Common options include:

  • Egg noodles for a richer flavor and chewy texture.
  • Rice noodles for a gluten-free alternative.
  • Wheat noodles for a traditional option.

If you’re ambitious, you can even make your own noodles from scratch using just flour, water, and eggs (optional). Homemade noodles give you complete control over texture and taste.

2. Flavor Base

The seasoning packet is often the heart of store-bought instant noodles. To recreate this at home, prepare a flavorful blend that can be stored for later use. Popular ingredients for the flavor base include:

  • Bouillon powder or cubes
  • Soy sauce
  • Miso paste
  • Garlic and onion powder
  • Chili flakes
  • Ginger powder
  • Dried spices (like cumin or coriander)

3. Toppings

Toppings make your homemade noodles even more exciting. Keep a selection of fresh vegetables, proteins, and garnishes handy:

  • Vegetables: Greens like spinach, bok choy, or kale, as well as shredded carrots, mushrooms, or corn.
  • Proteins: Boiled eggs, shredded chicken, tofu, or prawns.
  • Garnishes: Fresh scallions, sesame seeds, fried garlic, or nori.

By preparing the noodles, broth seasoning, and toppings ahead of time, you’ll have everything you need for a “just-add-water” instant noodle experience.

Recipes for Homemade Instant Noodles

Recipe 1: Classic Soy Garlic Instant Noodles

This recipe combines the savory flavors of soy sauce and garlic for a timeless noodle dish that’s ready in minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 servings of dried wheat noodles
  • 1 tbsp instant soy sauce powder or liquid soy sauce
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Boiled water
  • Optional toppings: sliced green onions, soft-boiled egg, or sautéed mushrooms

Instructions:

  1. Cook your dried noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small airtight container, mix the soy sauce powder, garlic powder, onion powder, sugar, and sesame oil. Store this flavor mix for future use, if desired.
  3. When ready to assemble, place the cooked noodles in a bowl. Add 1 tbsp of the seasoning mix and pour boiling water over the noodles. Adjust water quantities to your preferred broth consistency.
  4. Stir well until the flavors are combined.
  5. Garnish with green onions, boiled egg, or mushrooms before serving. Enjoy your soy garlic instant noodles!

Recipe 2: Vegan Coconut Curry Instant Noodles

This recipe features creamy coconut milk and bold curry spices, making it a perfect vegan comfort food option.

Ingredients:

  • 2 servings of rice noodles
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp dried ginger powder
  • 2 tbsp coconut milk powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Boiled water
  • Optional toppings: tofu cubes, chopped cilantro, and lime wedge

Instructions:

  1. Prepare your rice noodles following the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Mix the curry powder, turmeric, ginger powder, coconut milk powder, and salt in a small jar. Store this spice mix for later if you want to make multiple servings.
  3. To assemble, place cooked rice noodles in a bowl. Add 2 tbsp of the spice mix and pour boiling water over the noodles.
  4. Stir thoroughly until you achieve a creamy broth and noodles are well-coated.
  5. Top with tofu, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice for extra flavor.

Recipe 3: Spicy Kimchi Instant Noodles

For those who like a kick in their food, this homemade instant noodle recipe takes inspiration from Korean favorites.

Ingredients:

  • 2 servings of egg noodles
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped kimchi
  • Boiled water
  • Optional toppings: bean sprouts, boiled egg, and nori strips

Instructions:

  1. Boil egg noodles until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a jar, mix gochujang, chili flakes, garlic powder, sesame oil, and chopped kimchi. Store in the refrigerator for later use.
  3. To prepare your noodles, place cooked egg noodles in a bowl. Add 2 tbsp of the kimchi mix and pour boiling water over the noodles.
  4. Stir until well-combined and fragrant.
  5. Garnish with bean sprouts, egg, and nori strips before serving.

Tips for Storing Your Homemade Instant Noodle Ingredients

One of the best parts of making homemade instant noodles is the ability to prep ahead and store your ingredients. Here are some tips for long-term storage:

  • Noodles: Cook and dry your own noodles, or buy fresh ones and freeze them in portion-sized bundles. Dried noodles can be stored in a cool, dry cupboard.
  • Flavor Bases: Prepare seasoning mixes in bulk and store them in airtight containers or resealable jars in the pantry. Consider vacuum-sealing for longer shelf life.
  • Toppings: Freeze pre-chopped vegetables, proteins, and garnishes individually, or store fresh veggies in the fridge for up to a week.

With these storage tips, you’ll always have everything on hand for a quick and satisfying homemade instant noodle meal.

Experimenting with Flavors

The beauty of homemade instant noodles lies in their versatility. Once you have the basics down, don’t be afraid to experiment with flavors and textures. Here are some ideas to inspire you:

  • Add peanut butter and soy sauce for a nutty twist.
  • Use lemongrass and lime to create a tangy Southeast Asian-inspired broth.
  • Toss in barbecue sauce and shredded chicken for a smoky flavor profile.

Conclusion

Homemade instant noodles are more than just a meal—they’re a way to take control of your cooking, experiment with flavors, and create something uniquely yours. By following these easy recipes and storage tips, you’ll be able to whip up affordable and delicious dishes anytime. Goodbye processed packets, and hello fresh, homemade comfort! It’s time to rethink instant noodles and embrace the magic of cooking them from scratch.
