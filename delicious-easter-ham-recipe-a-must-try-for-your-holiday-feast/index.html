Delicious Easter Ham Recipe: A Must-Try for Your Holiday Feast


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious food. One dish that often takes center stage at any Easter feast is a succulent ham. The sweet, savory, and smoky flavors of a well-prepared ham can elevate your holiday meal and create lasting memories. In this blog post, we will guide you through a mouthwatering Easter ham recipe that is not only easy to prepare but will also impress your guests and leave them asking for seconds!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create a delectable Easter ham, gather the following ingredients:

For the Ham:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Whole cloves (for studding the ham)

For the Glaze:

  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger

Preparing the Ham

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Before you start cooking, preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). This temperature allows the ham to heat through without drying out, ensuring that it remains juicy and flavorful.

Step 2: Score the Ham

Once your oven is preheated, place the ham on a sturdy cutting board. Use a sharp knife to score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern. Make shallow cuts about 1 inch apart, cutting through the skin and fat but not into the meat. This scoring will allow the glaze to penetrate the ham, enhancing the flavor.

Step 3: Stud the Ham

Take whole cloves and stud the intersections of your diamond pattern. This not only adds a lovely presentation but also infuses the ham with a subtle clove flavor.

Making the Glaze

Step 4: Combine Ingredients

In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine the brown sugar, honey, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and black pepper. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is well combined.

Step 5: Simmer

Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally. Allow it to bubble for about 5-10 minutes until it thickens slightly. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.

Baking the Ham

Step 6: Prepare the Baking Dish

Place the ham in a large roasting pan, flat side down. If you have any leftover glaze, brush it generously over the ham, ensuring you get into the scored areas for maximum flavor.

Step 7: Bake the Ham

Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for about 15-18 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C). Remove the foil during the last 30 minutes of baking to allow the glaze to caramelize beautifully.

Step 8: Prepare the Orange Maple Glaze

While the ham is baking, prepare the orange maple glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the orange juice, orange zest, maple syrup, and ground ginger. Bring to a simmer and cook for about 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside.

Step 9: Baste the Ham

During the last 30 minutes of baking, baste the ham every 10 minutes with the orange maple glaze. This will create a shiny, flavorful crust on the ham that your guests will adore.

Serving the Ham

Step 10: Rest the Ham

Once the ham reaches the desired temperature, remove it from the oven and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and tender ham when you carve it.

Step 11: Carve the Ham

Using a sharp carving knife, slice the ham against the grain into even pieces. Serve it warm on a large platter, drizzling any remaining glaze over the top for extra flavor.

Pairing Suggestions

A delicious Easter ham pairs beautifully with a variety of side dishes. Consider serving:

  • Roasted Spring Vegetables: A colorful medley of asparagus, carrots, and bell peppers roasted with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Scalloped Potatoes: Creamy, cheesy scalloped potatoes that complement the savory ham perfectly.
  • Honey-Glazed Carrots: Sweet glazed carrots that add a lovely touch of sweetness to your plate.
  • Classic Deviled Eggs: A timeless appetizer that never fails to impress.
  • Fresh Spring Salad: A light salad with mixed greens, strawberries, feta cheese, and a balsamic vinaigrette.

Leftovers and Storage Tips

One of the best things about preparing a large ham is the delicious leftovers! Here are a few ideas for using your leftover ham:

  • Ham Sandwiches: Layer slices of ham on your favorite bread with cheese and mustard for a quick and satisfying lunch.
  • Ham and Cheese Quiche: Whip up a savory quiche loaded with chopped ham, cheese, and veggies for a tasty brunch option.
  • Ham and Bean Soup: Simmer leftover ham with beans, vegetables, and broth for a hearty and comforting soup.
  • Ham Fried Rice: Stir-fry diced ham with rice, vegetables, and soy sauce for a quick dinner.

To store leftover ham, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil and refrigerate. Properly stored, it should last for up to 5 days. You can also freeze leftover ham for up to 2 months; just make sure to slice it before freezing for easier use later.

Conclusion

This delicious Easter ham recipe is sure to be the highlight of your holiday feast. With its sweet and savory glaze, perfectly cooked meat, and stunning presentation, it’s a dish that will impress your guests and create cherished memories. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying an intimate family dinner, this ham is a must-try for your Easter celebration. Happy cooking, and may your holiday be filled with joy, laughter, and, of course, delicious food!
