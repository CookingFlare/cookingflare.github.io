The Best Italian Casserole Recipes: Comfort Food Done Right


Italian casserole dishes have long been cherished for their hearty, comforting flavors, amalgamating a symphony of tastes and traditions from across the beautiful country. In this guide, you’ll find a handful of our favorite Italian casserole recipes, each one substantially more enticing than the last.

Read on to take your culinary skills to the next level, cue the aroma that will linger in the air!

1. Classic Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (15 oz.) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 oz.) can tomato paste
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned and fully cooked, breaking it apart as it cooks, about 7-10 minutes. Drain excess fat.

  2. Add onion and garlic to the saucepan, cooking for 3-4 minutes, stirring often, until onions are translucent.

  3. Stir in tomato sauce and tomato paste, then simmer for 15 minutes.

  4. In a separate bowl, mix ricotta cheese and 1 ½ cups of mozzarella cheese.

  5. To assemble, spread a bit of meat sauce onto the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish, then layer with 6 lasagna noodles, half the ricotta mixture, and 1 cup of meat sauce. Continue with another layer of 6 noodles, remaining ricotta mixture, and meat sauce, finishing with a layer of noodles, the rest of the meat sauce, and lastly the remaining mozzarella cheese.

  6. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake at 375°F for 25 minutes.

  7. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden-brown.

2. Eggplant Parmesan Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 2 cups Italian-seasoned breadcrumbs
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the sauce:

  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 (15 oz.) cans tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 oz.) can tomato paste
  • 1 tsp. dried basil
  • 1 tsp. dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the topping:

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

Instructions:

  1. For the eggplant, slice them into ½-inch rounds and salt lightly on both sides. Place the slices in a colander, and let them stand for about 30 minutes to draw out excess moisture.
  2. In three separate shallow dishes, set up the breading process: flour, whisked eggs, and breadcrumbs mixed with half the Parmesan.
  3. Bread the eggplant slices by dipping them first into the flour, then the eggs, and finishing with the breadcrumb mixture.
  4. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add enough oil to reach a depth of ¼ inch. Fry breaded eggplant, in batches if necessary, until golden-brown on both sides, about 4-5 minutes per side.

Next, prepare the sauce by sautéing onions and garlic in a saucepan until tender. Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, dried basil, and oregano, then simmer for about 15 minutes.

To assemble, spread a layer of sauce onto the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish, then add layers of fried eggplant, more sauce, and Parmesan cheese. Repeat until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of sauce and mozzarella cheese.

Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake at 375°F for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until cheese is bubbly and golden-brown.

3. Baked.aspectratiotedSpaghetti alla Carbonara Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. spaghetti
  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente.

  2. Cook bacon in a large skillet over medium heat until crispy, about 7-10 minutes.

  3. Remove bacon from the skillet, leaving behind the rendered bacon fat. Add onion and garlic to the skillet, cooking until soft and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

  4. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.

  5. Drain pasta, then return it to its pot.

  6. Mix in cooked bacon, onions, and garlic, then gently toss with the egg mixture.

  7. Pour the pasta mixture into a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  8. Drizzle the skillet remaining bacon fat over the pasta mixture.

  9. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake at 375°F for 20 minutes.

  10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until cheese is bubbly and golden-brown.

Final Thoughts

These Italian casserole dishes are the ultimate comfort food, perfect for family and friends gatherings. With layers of deep flavors, you’ll create memories and juggle the aroma of your culinary skills. Explore, cook, and enjoy these treasured recipes!
