Peeling Perfection: Techniques for Every Fruit and Veggie


When you pick up a fruit or vegetable, how often do you pause, wondering how best to remove those stubborn skins? From apples to zucchini, peeling produces perfectly prepped produce, ready for use, and can be both a science and a skill. This guide presents several tried-and-true techniques to ensure you are an expert in peeling perfection.

For a knife-wielding experience, make sure to use a sharp, well-fitted knife. Hold the fruit securely, ideally on a cutting board with a non-slip surface to prevent any accidents. Apply gentle pressure, moving your knife around the outside of the produce in smooth strokes rather than jabbing motions. It’s tempting to cut right through vigorous skins, but patience is your friend - take time to remove the peel slowly to prevent bruising the fruit beneath.

If your knife skills need fine-tuning, more advanced techniques can be a natural progression, such as shaving vegetables or fruit with a speed cutter or bundt knife. If a hot kitchen is a deterrent to rolling with the peeling techniques, opt for a steaming element instead. Place your produce in a steaming basket over boiling water and cover with a lid. Continue the process for a few minutes, and the skins will loosen. This enhanced technique is notable for its gentle approach and reduced heat exposure.
