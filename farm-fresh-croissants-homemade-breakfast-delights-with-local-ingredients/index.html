Farm Fresh Croissants: Homemade Breakfast Delights with Local Ingredients


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked croissants wafting through your kitchen in the morning. The delicate layers of buttery pastry, golden and flaky, make for a truly indulgent breakfast treat. But have you ever considered taking your croissants to the next level by using farm fresh, locally sourced ingredients? In this recipe, I’ll show you how to create homemade croissants that not only taste incredible but also support local farmers and producers.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 package (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup farm fresh butter, cold

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and set aside.

  2. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water. Let it sit for a few minutes until frothy.

  3. Pour the yeast mixture into the well of the dry ingredients. Add the softened butter and milk. Mix until a sticky dough forms.

  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  5. Place the dough back into the mixing bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

  6. While the dough is rising, prepare the farm fresh butter. On a sheet of parchment paper, shape the cold butter into a 6-inch square. Fold the parchment paper over the butter and use a rolling pin to flatten and shape it. Place the butter back into the refrigerator until ready to use.

  7. Once the dough has doubled in size, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Roll it into a 12-inch square.

  8. Place the farm fresh butter square in the center of the dough square, diagonally. Fold the corners of the dough over the butter, overlapping them slightly to seal it inside.

  9. Roll the dough out into a rectangle, about 20 inches long and 10 inches wide. Fold the bottom third of the dough up, then fold the top third down, like folding a letter.

  10. Rotate the dough 90 degrees and roll it out again into a rectangle. Fold it in thirds again. Repeat this process two more times, for a total of four folds.

  11. Wrap the dough tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.

  12. Once the dough has chilled, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  13. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the croissant dough to a 1/4-inch thickness. Cut the dough into triangles, and gently stretch each triangle to elongate it slightly.

  14. Starting from the wide end of each triangle, roll up the dough towards the point. Place the croissants on the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between them for expansion.

  15. Brush the croissants with beaten egg, which will give them a beautiful golden color as they bake.

  16. Bake the croissants for 15-20 minutes, or until they are puffed and golden brown.

  17. Remove the croissants from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before serving.

Conclusion

These farm fresh croissants are a labor of love, but the end result is well worth the effort. By using locally sourced ingredients, you not only support your community’s farmers and producers but also elevate the flavor and quality of your breakfast. The combination of flaky layers, buttery richness, and the freshness of farm-fresh ingredients will make these croissants a breakfast delight that you’ll want to savor again and again. So why not treat yourself and your loved ones to a homemade croissant experience that celebrates local flavors and supports sustainable food practices? Happy baking!
