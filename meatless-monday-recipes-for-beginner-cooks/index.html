Meatless Monday Recipes for Beginner Cooks


Are you a beginner cook looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet? Look no further than Meatless Monday recipes! These delicious and easy-to-make dishes are perfect for those who are new to cooking or simply want to explore the world of meatless meals. In this blog post, we’ll be sharing three simple and flavorful recipes that will make your Meatless Mondays a breeze. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Creamy Mushroom Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. pasta of your choice
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the mushrooms and garlic in a little olive oil until the mushrooms are golden brown.
  3. Add the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cook for 5 minutes until the broth has reduced slightly.
  4. Stir in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce has thickened.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss until well coated in the creamy mushroom sauce.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

2. Veggie Stir-Fry with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and cornstarch until smooth. Add the tofu cubes and gently toss to coat. Let it marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the bell pepper, zucchini, carrot, and broccoli. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the skillet. Cook for an additional minute.
  5. Return the tofu to the skillet and stir-fry for another minute to heat through.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the veggie stir-fry over steamed rice or noodles.

3. Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, any color
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, cheddar cheese, cilantro, cumin, and chili powder. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Stuff the quinoa mixture into the bell peppers, packing it tightly.
  4. Place the stuffed bell peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil. Bake for 25-30 minutes until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.
  5. Remove the foil and sprinkle additional cheese on top if desired. Bake for another 5 minutes until the cheese has melted.
  6. Serve the quinoa stuffed bell peppers hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Conclusion

Meatless Monday recipes are a fantastic way to explore the world of plant-based cooking, even if you’re a beginner in the kitchen. These three recipes are simple, flavorful, and will leave you satisfied without missing the meat. So, gather your ingredients and give these recipes a try. Happy cooking!
