Instant Pot Chicken Stir-Fry: A Quick and Healthy Recipe


Are you craving a delicious, healthy, and quick meal that you can whip up in no time? If so, an Instant Pot chicken stir-fry is the perfect recipe for you! Packed with vibrant vegetables, tender chicken, and savory sauces, this meal is a wholesome choice for busy weeknights. The Instant Pot allows you to cook everything quickly while retaining the flavors and nutrients of the ingredients. Whether you’re meal prepping for the week or simply looking for a flavorful dinner, this Instant Pot chicken stir-fry will become your new go-to recipe.

Why Choose Instant Pot Chicken Stir-Fry?

Chicken stir-fry is a versatile dish that can be tailored to suit any taste or dietary preference. By cooking it in an Instant Pot, you significantly cut down on cooking time, making it a great option for those who are short on time but still want a healthy, homemade meal. The Instant Pot seals in all the flavors and moisture, ensuring your chicken comes out tender and juicy every time.

The best part? This stir-fry recipe is incredibly adaptable. You can use a variety of vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, broccoli, snow peas, and more. Plus, the sauce is a savory blend of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a touch of honey for sweetness. It’s a simple yet flavorful combination that enhances the overall taste of the dish.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you begin cooking, it’s important to gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need for this easy Instant Pot chicken stir-fry:

Protein

  • Chicken Breasts or Thighs: Chicken breasts are a great lean protein choice, but if you prefer more flavorful meat, chicken thighs work just as well. Be sure to cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces for even cooking.

Vegetables

  • Bell Peppers: Use any color bell peppers you like. Red, yellow, and orange peppers add a nice sweetness and color to the dish.
  • Broccoli Florets: Broccoli is a classic stir-fry vegetable that pairs well with chicken. It adds crunch and a healthy dose of fiber and vitamins.
  • Carrots: Sliced thinly, carrots cook quickly and provide a nice texture contrast.
  • Snap Peas: These crunchy peas add a burst of sweetness and texture to the stir-fry.
  • Onion: A small yellow or white onion adds aromatic depth to the flavor.

Sauce

  • Soy Sauce: The base of the stir-fry sauce. Use low-sodium soy sauce to keep the dish healthier.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic cloves minced finely to bring a rich flavor to the sauce.
  • Ginger: Fresh ginger adds a spicy, aromatic kick to the sauce.
  • Honey: A little sweetness balances the savory soy sauce and enhances the flavor of the stir-fry.
  • Sesame Oil: Adds a nutty flavor that complements the other ingredients beautifully.
  • Rice Vinegar: A touch of acidity to balance the flavors and add brightness.
  • Cornstarch: A little cornstarch mixed with water helps to thicken the sauce and create a silky texture.

Garnishes (Optional)

  • Sesame Seeds: Adds a delightful crunch and a bit of extra flavor to the finished dish.
  • Green Onions: Sliced thinly for garnish, they bring a mild onion flavor and fresh color.
  • Red Pepper Flakes: For those who like a bit of heat, a sprinkle of red pepper flakes will elevate the flavor.

How to Make Instant Pot Chicken Stir-Fry

Now that you have everything ready, let’s dive into the steps for preparing this flavorful Instant Pot chicken stir-fry. Follow the instructions carefully, and you’ll have a delicious meal in no time.

Step 1: Sauté the Chicken

Start by turning your Instant Pot to the Sauté setting. Add a small amount of sesame oil to the pot and allow it to heat up. Once the oil is hot, add the chicken pieces. Season the chicken with salt and pepper to taste.

Cook the chicken for about 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it is browned on all sides. Since the Instant Pot cooks at a higher temperature, this step helps the chicken sear quickly and locks in the juices. Once browned, remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

Without turning off the Sauté function, add a little more sesame oil to the pot. Toss in the sliced onions, carrots, bell peppers, and snap peas. Sauté the vegetables for 2-3 minutes, just long enough to soften them slightly while maintaining some of their crunch.

Don’t worry about fully cooking the vegetables during this step, as they’ll continue to cook under pressure in the Instant Pot. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

Step 3: Make the Sauce

While the vegetables are sautéing, prepare the stir-fry sauce. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, minced garlic, grated ginger, honey, rice vinegar, and a teaspoon of cornstarch mixed with a tablespoon of water. Stir the sauce well to ensure the cornstarch is fully dissolved.

Once the vegetables are slightly softened, pour the sauce over them and stir to combine. The sauce will start to simmer, filling your kitchen with amazing aromas.

Step 4: Add the Chicken Back In

Now that the sauce is mixed in with the vegetables, add the cooked chicken back into the pot. Stir everything together to make sure the chicken is well coated in the sauce. The Instant Pot will build up pressure during the next step, so it’s important to mix the ingredients thoroughly to ensure even cooking.

Step 5: Pressure Cook

Seal the Instant Pot lid, making sure the pressure valve is set to Sealing. Set the Instant Pot to Manual or Pressure Cook on high pressure for 3 minutes. Yes, only 3 minutes! The Instant Pot will come to pressure quickly, and the chicken will cook perfectly in this short time.

Once the cooking time is up, perform a quick release by carefully turning the pressure valve to Venting. Be cautious of the hot steam.

Step 6: Thicken the Sauce (Optional)

If you prefer a thicker sauce, turn the Instant Pot back to the Sauté setting after releasing the pressure. Let the sauce simmer for a minute or two until it thickens to your desired consistency. You can also add a little extra cornstarch slurry (1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 1 tbsp water) if necessary.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Once the sauce has thickened, your Instant Pot chicken stir-fry is ready to serve! Scoop the stir-fry onto plates or into bowls. Garnish with a sprinkle of sesame seeds, sliced green onions, and a pinch of red pepper flakes for added flavor and a pop of color.

Step 8: Enjoy!

This Instant Pot chicken stir-fry pairs wonderfully with steamed rice, cauliflower rice, or even quinoa if you’re looking for a low-carb option. The combination of juicy chicken, crispy vegetables, and savory sauce makes for a wholesome, satisfying meal.

Tips for Making the Perfect Instant Pot Chicken Stir-Fry

While this recipe is straightforward, there are a few tips and tricks to make sure you get the best results every time:

1. Use Fresh Ingredients

Fresh vegetables and high-quality chicken will elevate the flavor of your stir-fry. Look for crisp vegetables with bright colors, and choose boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs for the best texture.

2. Customize the Vegetables

Feel free to use whatever vegetables you have on hand. Broccoli, mushrooms, zucchini, baby corn, or even bok choy can make great additions or substitutes in this stir-fry.

3. Adjust the Sauce to Your Taste

The sauce in this recipe is customizable. If you prefer a sweeter stir-fry, add more honey. If you like it spicier, incorporate some sriracha or red chili paste into the sauce.

4. Don’t Overcook the Chicken

Since the chicken cooks quickly under pressure, be sure not to overcook it. If you’re worried about it becoming dry, you can always cook for less time or use chicken thighs, which tend to stay juicier.

5. Keep it Healthy

This stir-fry is already a healthy option, but you can further reduce the calorie count by using a lower-sodium soy sauce or omitting the honey if you prefer a sugar-free version.

Variations You Can Try

While the classic Instant Pot chicken stir-fry is delicious on its own, you can experiment with different ingredients to make the recipe your own. Here are a few variations you might like:

Chicken and Shrimp Stir-Fry

Mix things up by adding shrimp along with the chicken. Shrimp cooks very quickly, so be sure to add it after pressure cooking to prevent overcooking.

Sweet and Sour Chicken Stir-Fry

Add a bit of pineapple to the stir-fry for a tropical twist. The pineapple’s natural sweetness complements the savory soy sauce and makes for a delightful sweet-and-sour flavor.

Spicy Chicken Stir-Fry

If you enjoy a bit of heat, add a tablespoon of chili garlic sauce, sriracha, or red pepper flakes to the stir-fry sauce. You can also use fresh chili peppers for an extra spicy kick.

Vegetarian Stir-Fry

Skip the chicken and use tofu or tempeh as your protein. This is a great option for vegetarians or vegans, and it’s just as tasty!

Conclusion

This Instant Pot chicken stir-fry is the perfect solution for busy nights when you want something quick, healthy, and delicious. The Instant Pot’s pressure cooking technology ensures that the chicken is juicy and tender, while the vegetables retain their crunch and nutrients. With a few simple ingredients, you can create a wholesome meal in under 30 minutes. Whether you stick with the classic version or experiment with variations, this recipe is a must-try for anyone looking to make a healthy and satisfying stir-fry. Enjoy!
