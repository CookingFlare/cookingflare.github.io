Tuscan Focaccia with Rosemary: A Fragrant Italian Bread

3 min · 539 words

Focaccia, a beloved Italian bread, is known for its pillowy texture and enticing aroma. The Tuscan version, in particular, stands out with its simple yet flavorful combination of olive oil, rosemary, and sea salt. Today, we bring you a recipe that will transport your taste buds to the sun-drenched hills of Tuscany.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 400ml warm water
  • 80ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs
  • Coarse sea salt

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Make a well in the center and add the yeast. Gradually pour in the warm water while stirring gently with a wooden spoon until the dough starts to come together.

  2. Once the dough forms a shaggy mass, pour in the olive oil. Continue to mix until the dough becomes smooth and elastic, about 10 minutes. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much.

  3. Lightly grease another bowl with olive oil and transfer the dough to it. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

  4. Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set it aside.

  5. Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it to the prepared baking sheet and press it into a rectangular shape, about 1 inch thick. If the dough springs back, let it rest for a few minutes and then continue shaping it.

  6. Using your fingertips, make dimples all over the surface of the dough. This will create those characteristic pockets that will hold the olive oil and rosemary.

  7. Drizzle the focaccia generously with olive oil, making sure to fill each dimple. Sprinkle fresh rosemary leaves over the top, pressing them gently into the dough. Finish off with a sprinkle of coarse sea salt for that extra burst of flavor.

  8. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the focaccia turns golden brown. The aroma of freshly baked bread and rosemary will fill your kitchen, tempting you to devour it right away.

  9. Once the focaccia is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for a few minutes. Resist the urge to cut into it immediately, as this will allow the flavors to settle and the bread to become even more delicious.

  10. Serve the Tuscan focaccia warm or at room temperature, alongside a bowl of vibrant tomato sauce or a selection of Italian cheeses. The bread’s soft, chewy interior, combined with the fragrant rosemary and the subtle hint of sea salt, will transport you to the heart of Tuscany with every bite.

Final Thoughts

Tuscan Focaccia with Rosemary is a delightful bread that captures the essence of Italian cuisine. Its simplicity and use of quality ingredients make it a perfect addition to any meal or a satisfying snack on its own. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to indulge in the flavors of Tuscany, this fragrant Italian bread will not disappoint. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to the sun-soaked hills of Tuscany with this delicious Tuscan Focaccia recipe. Buon appetito!
