Smoky Sea Sessions: Grilled Seafood Recipes with a Smokin' Taste


There’s nothing quite like a meal straight off the grill! The smoky flavors that seep into the food, melding with its natural taste, elevates any dish to new heights. Make your seafood sizzle with this irresistible sauce. And with the warm days ahead, now is the perfect time to bring the sea to your backyard barbecue! In today’s blog, we’re diving into our smoky sea sessions, sharing some of the most delightful grilled seafood recipes infused with smokin’ taste!

  1. Seafood Skewers with Smoky Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb mixed seafood (shrimp, scallops, and chunks of fish like salmon or swordfish)
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into squares
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 long skewers

Instructions:

a. In a medium bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, honey, minced garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.

b. Marinate the mixed seafood in half of the mixture for at least 30 minutes.

c. Preheat your grill on medium-high heat, and lightly oil the grates.

d. Skewer the marinated seafood, zucchini rounds, cherry tomatoes, and red bell pepper squares, alternating between the ingredients.

e. Grill for about 5-7 minutes on each side, basting occasionally with the rest of the marinade.

  1. Grilled Lime-Cilantro Shrimp Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 3 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 limes, juiced
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Warm corn tortillas
  • Sliced red onions
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Salsa, sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

a. In a bowl, whisk together lime juice, olive oil, chopped cilantro, minced garlic, chili powder, ground cumin, and salt.

b. Marinate the shrimp in the mixture for 20 minutes.

c. Preheat your grill on medium heat.

d. Thread the shrimp onto skewers, and grill for about 3-4 minutes on each side.

e. Serve the grilled shrimp on warm corn tortillas, topped with sliced red onions, avocado, salsa, sour cream or Greek yogurt, and a lime wedge.

  1. Smoky BBQ Salmon Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs salmon fillets
  • 1/2 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pie crust, rolled out
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme

Instructions:

a. Preheat your grill on medium-high heat.

b. Season the salmon fillets with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper, then brush with BBQ sauce.

c. Grill the salmon for about 5 minutes on each side, until it flakes easily with a fork.

d. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Whisk in the shredded cheddar cheese until fully melted.

e. Flake the grilled salmon into a baked pie crust, then pour the cheese sauce over the top. Sprinkle the green onions and thyme over the pie.

f. Bake at 375°F for 20-25 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the sauce bubbles.
