Easter Breakfast Casseroles: Hearty Dishes to Start the Day


Easter morning calls for a special breakfast that brings family and friends together. What better way to kick off the day’s festivities than with a delicious and hearty breakfast casserole? This versatile dish not only saves time in the kitchen but also allows for ample creativity in its preparation. From savory to sweet, there’s a breakfast casserole to suit every taste. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering Easter breakfast casserole recipes that will elevate your morning spread and delight your loved ones.

Cheesy Hash Brown Breakfast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 package frozen hash browns
  • 1 pound breakfast sausage
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 8 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup diced onions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a skillet, cook the breakfast sausage until browned and crumbled. Drain any excess grease.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
  4. Layer the frozen hash browns in the prepared baking dish. Top with the cooked sausage, bell peppers, onions, and shredded cheeses.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the layered ingredients in the baking dish.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the casserole is set and the top is golden brown.
  7. Allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Spinach and Feta Breakfast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 8 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking spray

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a skillet, sauté the diced onions and minced garlic until softened. Add the fresh spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread the spinach mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese on top.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the spinach and feta.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the casserole is set and the edges are golden brown.
  7. Let the casserole cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Blueberry French Toast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf French bread, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 8 eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish and arrange the bread cubes in the dish. Sprinkle the blueberries over the bread.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Pour the egg mixture over the bread and blueberries, ensuring all the bread is coated. Press down on the bread to absorb the liquid.
  4. Cover the baking dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight.
  5. The next morning, preheat the oven to 350°F and remove the plastic wrap from the baking dish.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the casserole is puffed and golden.
  7. Let the casserole cool for a few minutes, dust with powdered sugar, and serve with additional maple syrup if desired.

Conclusion

Easter breakfast casseroles are a fantastic way to simplify your morning meal prep while still serving a delightful and satisfying dish. Whether you opt for the comforting Cheesy Hash Brown Breakfast Casserole, the flavorful Spinach and Feta Breakfast Casserole, or the indulgent Blueberry French Toast Casserole, you’re sure to impress your guests with these hearty dishes. Get creative with your favorite ingredients and make this Easter morning a memorable one with a delightful breakfast casserole. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
