Homemade Cinnamon Rolls: A Sweet Comfort Food Recipe


Cinnamon rolls are the epitome of comfort food, a delightful treat that brings warmth and joy to any kitchen. The aroma of freshly baked cinnamon rolls wafting through the house is enough to make anyone feel at home. Whether enjoyed for breakfast, brunch, or as a sweet snack, these rolls are a beloved classic that can be made from scratch with just a few simple ingredients. In this post, we will guide you through the process of making homemade cinnamon rolls, from the dough to the gooey filling and the luscious icing that ties it all together.

The History of Cinnamon Rolls

Cinnamon rolls have a rich history that dates back centuries. Originating in Northern Europe, these sweet pastries were initially made with cardamom, a spice that was more common in Scandinavian countries. Over time, the recipe evolved, and cinnamon became the star ingredient, especially in the United States, where they gained immense popularity. Today, cinnamon rolls are a staple in many households, often associated with family gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make the perfect homemade cinnamon rolls, gather the following ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

For the Filling:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (optional)

For the Icing:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm milk (about 110°F) and the active dry yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This indicates that the yeast is active and ready to use.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, and salt.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and then add the melted butter and the activated yeast mixture. Mix until well combined.

  4. Form the Dough: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until a dough begins to form. Once combined, knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. Let It Rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough is rising, prepare the filling:

  1. Mix the Filling Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the softened butter, brown sugar, ground cinnamon, and chopped nuts (if using). Mix until it forms a smooth paste.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Punch Down the Dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Turn it out onto a floured surface.

  2. Roll the Dough: Using a rolling pin, roll the dough into a rectangle about 1/4 inch thick. Aim for a size of approximately 16x24 inches.

  3. Spread the Filling: Evenly spread the cinnamon filling over the rolled-out dough, leaving a small border around the edges.

Step 4: Shape the Rolls

  1. Roll It Up: Starting from one long edge, carefully roll the dough into a tight log. Make sure to roll it evenly to ensure uniform rolls.

  2. Cut the Rolls: Using a sharp knife or dental floss, cut the log into 12 equal pieces. Place the rolls in a greased baking dish, leaving some space between each roll for expansion.

Step 5: Second Rise

  1. Let the Rolls Rise Again: Cover the baking dish with a kitchen towel and let the rolls rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until they have puffed up.

Step 6: Bake the Rolls

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Bake: Once the rolls have risen, bake them in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.

Step 7: Prepare the Icing

While the rolls are baking, prepare the icing:

  1. Mix the Icing Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.
