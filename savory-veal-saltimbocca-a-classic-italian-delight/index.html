Savory Veal Saltimbocca: A Classic Italian Delight


When it comes to Italian cuisine, few dishes evoke the same level of admiration and craving as Veal Saltimbocca. This classic dish, with its delicate layers of flavor and elegant presentation, is a true representation of the simplicity and sophistication of Italian cooking. If you’re looking to impress your family or guests with a dish that embodies both tradition and taste, look no further. Here’s everything you need to know about preparing Savory Veal Saltimbocca.

The Origins of Saltimbocca

Saltimbocca, which translates to “jump in the mouth,” is a dish that hails from Rome, Italy. Traditionally made with veal, the dish is layered with prosciutto and fresh sage, creating a savory combination that is both aromatic and flavorful. The dish has roots in Italian culinary history that showcase the use of simple, high-quality ingredients that come together to create something extraordinary.

The Key Ingredients

To create the perfect Veal Saltimbocca, it’s essential to use high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Veal Saltimbocca

  • Veal Cutlets: Look for thin cuts of veal, ideally about 1/4 inch thick. The tenderness of the meat is crucial for the dish.
  • Prosciutto: This Italian dry-cured ham adds a rich, salty flavor that complements the veal beautifully.
  • Fresh Sage Leaves: The aromatic quality of sage is a hallmark of this dish. Fresh leaves are recommended for the best flavor.
  • Flour: A light dusting of flour helps to create a golden crust when the veal is sautéed.
  • Olive Oil: Use high-quality extra virgin olive oil for cooking. It adds depth to the flavor profile of the dish.
  • Butter: A couple of tablespoons of butter will enhance the richness and help in browning the cutlets.
  • White Wine: A dry white wine is ideal for deglazing the pan and creating a luscious sauce.
  • Salt and Pepper: Simple seasonings that will elevate the overall flavor.

For the Sauce

  • Chicken or Vegetable Broth: Adds moisture and depth to the sauce.
  • Lemon Juice: A splash of fresh lemon juice brightens up the dish and balances the richness of the meat.

Preparing the Veal Saltimbocca

Step 1: Prepare the Veal

Begin by placing the veal cutlets between two sheets of plastic wrap. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, gently pound the veal to a uniform thickness of about 1/4 inch. This step ensures even cooking and tenderness.

Step 2: Assemble the Cutlets

Take a slice of prosciutto and lay it on top of each veal cutlet. Next, place a fresh sage leaf on top of the prosciutto. To secure everything together, use a toothpick or a small skewer to hold the layers in place.

Step 3: Dredge in Flour

Lightly coat each assembled cutlet in flour, tapping off any excess. This not only helps with browning but also contributes to a slight crispiness that pairs wonderfully with the tender meat.

Step 4: Sauté the Cutlets

In a large skillet, heat a combination of olive oil and butter over medium-high heat. Once the butter has melted and started to foam, carefully add the veal cutlets to the pan, sage side up. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown. Remove the cutlets from the pan and set them aside on a plate.

Step 5: Create the Sauce

In the same skillet, add a splash of white wine to deglaze the pan. Scrape up any browned bits from the bottom - this adds incredible flavor to your sauce. After a minute, pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and let it simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly. Finish the sauce with a splash of lemon juice, salt, and pepper to taste.

Step 6: Combine and Serve

Return the veal cutlets to the skillet, spooning some sauce over them. Allow everything to simmer together for an additional 2-3 minutes to heat through. Serve the veal saltimbocca hot, garnished with additional fresh sage and a drizzle of the sauce.

Tips for Perfecting Your Veal Saltimbocca

  • Quality Matters: Since the ingredients are simple, using high-quality veal, prosciutto, and olive oil will make a noticeable difference in flavor.
  • Pound Evenly: Ensuring that your veal is pounded to an even thickness will help it cook uniformly.
  • Don’t Overcook: Veal is best when it’s cooked to a tender state. Overcooking can lead to dryness. Aim for a light golden brown color.
  • Experiment with Herbs: While sage is traditional, feel free to experiment with other herbs like thyme or rosemary for a twist on the classic recipe.

Serving Suggestions

Veal Saltimbocca is a versatile dish that pairs beautifully with various sides. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Polenta: Creamy polenta provides a wonderful base for the veal and absorbs the savory sauce.
  • Roasted Vegetables: A medley of seasonal roasted vegetables adds color and nutrition to the plate.
  • Pasta: Serve alongside a light pasta tossed in olive oil and garlic for a complete meal.
  • Salad: A simple arugula salad with lemon vinaigrette can provide a refreshing contrast to the rich flavors of the saltimbocca.

Wine Pairing

When it comes to pairing wine with Veal Saltimbocca, a crisp white wine works best. Look for a Pinot Grigio or a Sauvignon Blanc to complement the dish’s flavors. If you prefer red wine, a light-bodied Chianti can also be a great choice.

Conclusion

Savory Veal Saltimbocca is not just a meal; it’s an experience that transports you to the heart of Italy with every bite. This dish encapsulates the essence of Italian cooking: simple, high-quality ingredients brought together to create something truly special. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a quiet meal at home, Veal Saltimbocca is a dish that promises to impress and satisfy. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on this culinary journey to Italy right from your kitchen. Buon appetito!
