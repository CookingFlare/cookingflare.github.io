Springtime Quiche Recipes: Perfect for Any Meal


As the days grow longer and the flowers begin to bloom, spring brings with it a sense of renewal and freshness. What better way to celebrate the season than with a delightful quiche? This versatile dish can be enjoyed for breakfast, brunch, lunch, or even dinner, making it a perfect addition to your springtime menu. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of springtime quiche recipes that highlight seasonal ingredients, ensuring that each bite is a celebration of spring.

The Versatility of Quiche

Quiche is a savory pie made with a flaky crust and a rich filling, typically consisting of eggs, cream, and cheese. The beauty of quiche lies in its versatility; you can customize it with a wide range of ingredients, from vegetables to meats, and even seafood. This adaptability makes quiche an ideal dish for any meal, allowing you to incorporate seasonal produce and cater to various dietary preferences.

The Perfect Crust

Before diving into the recipes, let’s talk about the crust. A classic quiche crust is made from flour, butter, and a pinch of salt. However, if you’re short on time, you can easily use a store-bought pie crust. For those looking for a healthier option, consider using a whole wheat crust or even a crustless quiche, which is just as delicious and lighter.

Basic Quiche Crust Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Add the chilled butter and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together.
  4. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Prick the bottom with a fork and pre-bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10-15 minutes until lightly golden.

Now that we have our crust ready, let’s explore some delicious springtime quiche recipes!

1. Asparagus and Goat Cheese Quiche

Asparagus is one of the first vegetables to appear in spring, making it a perfect star ingredient for your quiche.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked quiche crust
  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the asparagus and sauté for about 5 minutes until tender. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Spread the sautéed asparagus evenly over the pre-baked crust. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese on top.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the asparagus and cheese.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden on top. Allow to cool for a few minutes before slicing.

2. Spinach and Feta Quiche

Spinach is another springtime favorite, and when paired with feta cheese, it creates a delightful flavor combination.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked quiche crust
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped spinach and sauté until wilted, about 3-4 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Spread the sautéed spinach evenly over the pre-baked crust. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese on top.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the spinach and feta.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly golden. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

3. Tomato and Basil Quiche

Nothing says spring quite like fresh tomatoes and fragrant basil. This quiche is a celebration of classic Italian flavors.
