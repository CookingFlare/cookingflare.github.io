With Wine-Poached Pears Over Gorgonzola Pasta


Few dishes marry the sophisticated interplay of sweet and savory quite like wine-poached pears served over creamy gorgonzola pasta. This culinary masterpiece balances the mellow sweetness of poached fruit with the tangy richness of blue cheese-infused sauce, creating a harmony of flavors that is both luxurious and comforting. It’s a recipe born for dinner parties, romantic evenings, or even a solo indulgence, offering an experience that elevates everyday dining into something truly memorable.

Why This Dish Works

The magic of this dish lies in the way each element complements the others. Poaching pears in wine adds depth to their natural fruitiness, introducing subtle notes of spice and acidity that are both aromatic and indulgent. When paired with gorgonzola — a cheese renowned for its robust tang and creamy texture — the sweetness of the pears is amplified, creating a symphony of contrasts. Add pasta as the canvas for these bold flavors, and the result is an elegant yet approachable dish that deserves a regular spot in your culinary repertoire.

Beyond its flavor profile, this dish also shines in its ability to impress with minimal effort. The process of poaching fruit, while seemingly advanced, is remarkably simple. Combining that with a buttery, cheese-laden pasta sauce ensures you can create a restaurant-worthy dish in the comfort of your own kitchen.

With that in mind, let’s dive into the details.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Pears:

  • 2 firm Bosc or Anjou pears
  • 2 cups dry red wine (such as Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 star anise (optional)
  • Zest of 1 lemon

For the Pasta:

  • 12 ounces fettuccine, pappardelle, or any long pasta of choice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 5 ounces gorgonzola cheese, crumbled (adjust to taste)
  • 3 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Garnishes:

  • Fresh thyme leaves or parsley for garnish
  • Crushed walnuts or candied pecans (optional)

Cooking Steps

Step 1: Poaching the Pears

Start by preparing the pears. Wash, peel, and halve the pears lengthwise. Use a small spoon to remove the cores, ensuring the halves remain intact for presentation purposes.

In a medium-sized pot, combine the wine, sugar, cinnamon stick, cloves, star anise (if using), and lemon zest. Heat the mixture over medium-high heat, stirring gently until the sugar dissolves completely. Lower the heat to medium and place the pear halves into the poaching liquid.

Allow the pears to cook gently for 15–20 minutes while flipping them occasionally to ensure even coloring. The goal is for the pears to become tender yet retain their shape, and they should take on a lovely deep crimson hue from the wine. Once done, remove them from the poaching liquid and set them aside. Reserve the liquid by straining out the spices — you’ll use it for finishing the dish.

Step 2: Cooking the Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve ½ cup of the pasta water before draining the rest.

Step 3: Preparing the Gorgonzola Sauce

In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, pour in the heavy cream and allow it to warm slightly — but do not let it boil. Gradually add the gorgonzola cheese, stirring continuously to achieve a creamy consistency. To enhance the complexity of the sauce, mix in the grated Parmesan cheese, which complements the bold flavors of the gorgonzola. Season with cracked black pepper and salt to taste, keeping in mind the saltiness of the cheese.

If the sauce feels too thick, loosen it with the reserved pasta water, adding it a few splashes at a time until the desired texture is achieved.

Step 4: Assembling the Dish

Toss the drained pasta directly into the skillet containing the gorgonzola sauce, ensuring the noodles are well-coated. Plate individual servings on wide pasta bowls or shallow plates to allow for a clean presentation.

Once the pasta is plated, carefully place a wine-poached pear half on top of each serving. For extra flair, drizzle a small amount of the reserved poaching liquid over the pears and pasta.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Sprinkle fresh thyme leaves or parsley over the dish to bring a hint of herby freshness. For a touch of crunch, scatter crushed walnuts or candied pecans over the top. Serve immediately while the pasta is warm and creamy, and prepare for rave reviews.

Tips for Perfect Results

  1. Selecting the Right Pears: Opt for firm pears such as Bosc or Anjou, as these varieties hold their shape well during the poaching process.
  2. Choosing the Wine: A dry red wine with medium body works best for poaching. Avoid overly sweet options, as this can make the dish overwhelmingly sweet. Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, or Zinfandel offer excellent results.
  3. Balancing the Gorgonzola: If you’re not accustomed to bold cheeses, start with a lesser amount and taste-test as you go. Add more gorgonzola gradually until you hit your preferred flavor balance.
  4. Make in Advance: The poached pears can be prepared ahead of time and stored in their poaching liquid in the refrigerator for up to two days. Reheat gently before serving.

A Little History Behind the Ingredients

With its roots in French and Italian cuisine, combining poached fruits with savory dishes has long been a hallmark of sophisticated dining. Pears, cherished for their velvety texture and natural sweetness, have a long-standing association with wine in both sweet and savory dishes. Meanwhile, gorgonzola cheese hails from northern Italy, where its production dates back thousands of years. Known for its creamy texture and hearty flavor, gorgonzola’s bold personality contrasts beautifully with the milder, sweeter flavors of poached fruit. The addition of pasta as a neutral base ties these elements together in a way that feels both upscale and inviting.

Variations to Experiment With

For adventurous cooks, the beauty of this dish lies in its adaptability. While wine-poached pears and gorgonzola pasta form a solid foundation, you can experiment with variations to suit your tastes or dietary needs.

  • Alternative Cheese: Swap out gorgonzola for Stilton, Roquefort, or goat cheese for slightly different flavor profiles.
  • White Wine Pears: For a lighter take, poach the pears in white wine instead of red. Pair this with a milder cheese like Brie or mascarpone.
  • Non-Alcoholic Version: Prepare the pears in a mixture of cranberry juice and pomegranate juice rather than wine. The result is equally stunning and suitable for all ages.
  • Nut-Free Option: Skip the walnuts or pecans entirely and replace them with toasted breadcrumbs or crispy shallots as a textural garnish.

A Meal Meant To Be Savored

Wine-poached pears over gorgonzola pasta serve as a reminder that food can be art — a celebration of flavors, textures, and colors combined to delight the senses. The pairing of tangy cheese, silky pasta, and fruity elegance isn’t just a dish; it’s an experience that transforms the ordinary into the extraordinary. By following this recipe, you’ll not only master a meal fit for special occasions but also gain the confidence to innovate and refine your culinary skills further.

So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and let your taste buds embark on an unforgettable journey. Dinner has never looked (or tasted) so good!
