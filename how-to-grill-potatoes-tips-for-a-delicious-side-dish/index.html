How to Grill Potatoes: Tips for a Delicious Side Dish


Grilling potatoes is one of the best ways to prepare this versatile vegetable. Whether you’re serving them as a side dish for a summer barbecue or a cozy dinner, grilled potatoes bring a smoky, crispy exterior with a soft and creamy interior that is simply irresistible. This guide will walk you through how to grill potatoes perfectly, from selecting the right potatoes to grilling techniques and seasoning ideas.

Why Grill Potatoes?

Grilling potatoes brings out a depth of flavor that other cooking methods can’t quite match. The heat from the grill caramelizes the sugars on the surface of the potatoes, creating a crispy, golden-brown exterior. The inside stays tender, making each bite a delightful contrast of textures. Furthermore, grilling potatoes is easy and quick, making it a go-to method for both seasoned chefs and beginners.

The smoky aroma that permeates the potatoes as they grill is also a major perk. It adds a distinct flavor that pairs beautifully with other grilled foods, such as meats, vegetables, or even grilled corn on the cob.

Types of Potatoes to Grill

Not all potatoes are created equal, and some types are better suited for grilling than others. Here are the most common types of potatoes you’ll encounter, and how they fare on the grill:

1. Yukon Gold Potatoes

These potatoes are a favorite for grilling due to their waxy texture and rich, buttery flavor. They hold their shape well, making them ideal for grilling whole or in slices. Yukon Golds have a thin skin that crisps up nicely when grilled.

2. Red Potatoes

Red potatoes have a smooth, thin skin and a creamy interior, making them another great choice for grilling. They also hold their shape well during cooking and offer a mildly sweet flavor when grilled. Red potatoes are perfect for grilling as wedges or whole.

3. Russet Potatoes

Although Russets are often used for baking, they can be grilled as well. They have a fluffier texture compared to waxy potatoes and can turn out crispy on the outside when grilled. Russets are typically best when cut into wedges for grilling.

4. Fingerling Potatoes

These small, elongated potatoes are perfect for grilling due to their size and shape. Fingerlings have a firm texture and hold up well on the grill. They’re often grilled whole and are a delightful addition to any grilled platter.

Preparing Potatoes for the Grill

Before you fire up the grill, it’s important to properly prepare your potatoes to ensure they cook evenly and develop that perfect crispy exterior.

Step 1: Wash and Scrub

Start by washing your potatoes under cold water to remove any dirt. Use a vegetable brush to scrub the skins, especially if you’re grilling with the skin on. If you prefer to peel your potatoes, feel free to do so, but keep in mind that the skin adds flavor and helps the potatoes hold their shape while grilling.

Step 2: Cut the Potatoes (Optional)

Depending on your preferred method, you can either grill your potatoes whole or cut them into pieces. Cutting the potatoes into wedges or slices allows for quicker cooking and gives more surface area for that crispy exterior.

If you’re cutting the potatoes, try to make the pieces roughly the same size to ensure even cooking. For example, cut larger potatoes into wedges, and slice smaller potatoes into rounds or halves. If you want to grill whole potatoes, skip this step and proceed to the next.

Step 3: Parboil (Optional)

While this step is optional, parboiling your potatoes before grilling can help them cook faster and more evenly on the grill. Simply place the potatoes (whether whole or cut) into a pot of boiling water and cook for about 5-7 minutes. The potatoes should be slightly tender but not fully cooked. After parboiling, drain and let them cool for a few minutes before grilling. This method helps prevent undercooked or overcooked potatoes on the grill.

Grilling Techniques

Once your potatoes are prepared, it’s time to move on to the grilling process. There are several methods to grill potatoes, each yielding a slightly different texture and flavor.

1. Direct Grilling (For Wedges and Slices)

Direct grilling is the most common method for grilling potatoes, especially when they are cut into wedges or slices. This technique involves placing the potatoes directly on the grill grates over medium-high heat.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (around 375°F - 400°F).
  2. Brush the potatoes with olive oil or your preferred cooking oil. This helps them achieve a crispy texture and prevents sticking.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and your favorite herbs or spices.
  4. Place the potatoes directly on the grill, cut side down, and cook for 5-7 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy on the outside. Be sure to flip the potatoes halfway through to ensure even grilling.
  5. Remove from the grill once they are tender and crispy.

2. Indirect Grilling (For Whole Potatoes)

For whole potatoes, indirect grilling is the way to go. This method involves placing the potatoes on the cooler side of the grill, away from direct heat, allowing them to cook through without burning.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat (about 350°F).
  2. Coat the whole potatoes with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and any other seasonings you desire.
  3. Place the potatoes on the cooler side of the grill, away from direct flames.
  4. Cover the grill and cook the potatoes for 30-45 minutes, depending on their size, turning occasionally. The potatoes are done when you can easily pierce them with a fork or knife.
  5. For extra crispiness, you can finish the potatoes on direct heat for a minute or two per side to achieve that golden, crispy exterior.

3. Grilling in Foil (For Soft Potatoes)

If you prefer soft and tender potatoes, grilling them in foil is an excellent option. This method locks in moisture and flavor, giving you a softer interior while still maintaining some grill marks and flavor.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Wrap the prepared potatoes in heavy-duty aluminum foil, sealing the edges tightly.
  3. Place the foil-wrapped potatoes on the grill and cook for 35-45 minutes, depending on the size of the potatoes.
  4. After grilling, unwrap the potatoes carefully and serve.

Flavorful Seasonings and Marinades

One of the best things about grilled potatoes is that they’re incredibly versatile when it comes to seasoning. Here are some tasty ideas to elevate your grilled potatoes.

1. Classic Herb and Garlic

A simple seasoning of fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, and parsley combined with minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper is always a crowd-pleaser. This combination enhances the natural flavor of the potatoes and adds a fragrant, savory touch.

2. Smoky BBQ

For a smoky flavor, toss your potatoes with a bit of smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. For extra richness, add a drizzle of your favorite barbecue sauce toward the end of grilling.

3. Cheesy Herb

Grilled potatoes and cheese are a match made in heaven. After grilling, sprinkle shredded cheddar, parmesan, or a combination of both on the hot potatoes. Let the cheese melt slightly, then top with fresh chopped chives or green onions for a creamy, savory side dish.

4. Spicy Kick

If you enjoy a little heat, try adding chili powder, cayenne pepper, or crushed red pepper flakes to your grilled potatoes. You can also mix in lime juice and cilantro for a zesty twist.

5. Lemon and Dill

For a refreshing, tangy flavor, try seasoning your potatoes with lemon zest, lemon juice, fresh dill, olive oil, and salt. This light and zesty flavor combination is perfect for warm-weather grilling.

Serving Suggestions

Grilled potatoes are versatile and can be served alongside a wide variety of dishes. Here are some great ways to pair them:

  • Grilled meats: Grilled chicken, steak, burgers, or ribs make the perfect companions to grilled potatoes.
  • Vegetables: Serve your potatoes with other grilled vegetables like asparagus, bell peppers, and zucchini for a well-rounded meal.
  • Salads: Grilled potatoes pair wonderfully with fresh salads, especially those with a tangy vinaigrette dressing.

Conclusion

Grilling potatoes is a simple yet delicious way to elevate any meal. With the right preparation, grilling techniques, and seasoning, you can create crispy, tender, and flavorful potatoes that will steal the show at your next cookout or family dinner. Whether you’re grilling whole potatoes, wedges, or slices, the key is to ensure even cooking and balance between crispy and soft textures. Don’t be afraid to get creative with seasonings and try out different flavor profiles. Grilled potatoes are a crowd favorite and will quickly become a go-to side dish in your grilling repertoire.
