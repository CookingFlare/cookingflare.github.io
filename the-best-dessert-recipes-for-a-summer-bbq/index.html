The Best Dessert Recipes for a Summer BBQ


Summer BBQs are a perfect way to gather friends and family to enjoy delicious grilled food, refreshing drinks, and a fun atmosphere. But what makes a BBQ truly unforgettable is the sweet ending — dessert! Whether you’re hosting a backyard BBQ, a picnic in the park, or a poolside gathering, the right dessert can elevate the entire experience. From fruity, chilled treats to rich, decadent confections, the possibilities are endless. In this post, we’ll explore the best dessert recipes for a summer BBQ that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

1. Grilled Peaches with Honey and Cinnamon

Nothing says summer like ripe, juicy peaches, and grilling them takes their sweetness to a whole new level. Grilled peaches are easy to prepare, making them the perfect dessert for a BBQ. The caramelization from the grill enhances the natural sugars of the fruit, and a drizzle of honey with a sprinkle of cinnamon adds a warm, inviting flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • A pinch of sea salt (optional)
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the peach halves with a little bit of oil or melted butter to prevent sticking.
  3. Place the peaches cut-side down on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes, until grill marks appear and the fruit starts to soften.
  4. Flip the peaches and grill for an additional 2-3 minutes, until they are tender but still holding their shape.
  5. Drizzle with honey, sprinkle with cinnamon and a pinch of salt (optional), and garnish with fresh mint leaves.
  6. Serve immediately, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent touch.

This easy-to-make grilled dessert is sure to impress your guests, offering a perfect balance of sweetness, tartness, and a touch of spice.

2. Strawberry Shortcake Cups

Strawberry shortcake is a classic summer dessert, and individual cups make it even easier to serve at a BBQ. These handheld desserts are made with fluffy sponge cake, fresh strawberries, and a generous layer of whipped cream. They’re portable, fun to eat, and the perfect treat for a hot day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 box of shortcake biscuits or sponge cake (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup of heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tablespoon of powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the sliced strawberries with sugar. Toss well and let the strawberries sit for 10-15 minutes to macerate.
  2. While the strawberries are resting, whip the cream in a large bowl with the powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form.
  3. Slice the shortcake biscuits or sponge cake into small rounds or cubes.
  4. To assemble, layer the shortcake pieces at the bottom of serving cups or jars. Top with a spoonful of macerated strawberries and a generous dollop of whipped cream.
  5. Repeat the layering process until the cups are filled.
  6. Garnish with a few extra strawberry slices on top and serve chilled.

These mini strawberry shortcake cups are the perfect refreshing bite after a hearty BBQ meal. They’re light, creamy, and bursting with fresh, sweet flavors.

3. No-Bake Lemon Cheesecake Bars

For those who want to skip the oven on a hot summer day, no-bake desserts are a lifesaver. These lemon cheesecake bars are rich, creamy, and tangy with a smooth texture that practically melts in your mouth. The zesty lemon flavor is perfect for summer, and the buttery graham cracker crust adds the ideal crunch to balance the creamy filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of melted butter
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup of powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup of sour cream
  • 1/4 cup of fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter in a bowl. Mix until the crumbs are fully coated with the butter and form a slightly wet mixture.
  2. Press the graham cracker mixture into the bottom of an 8x8-inch pan lined with parchment paper to create an even crust. Chill in the refrigerator while you prepare the filling.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Add the powdered sugar, sour cream, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract. Mix until fully combined and creamy.
  4. Spread the cheesecake filling over the chilled graham cracker crust, smoothing the top with a spatula.
  5. Refrigerate the bars for at least 4 hours, or until they are firm enough to slice.
  6. Cut into squares and serve cold. Garnish with extra lemon zest or fresh berries, if desired.

These lemon cheesecake bars are a bright and refreshing treat that will be a hit at any summer BBQ.

4. S’mores Dip

If you love the classic campfire treat, you’ll adore this S’mores dip. It’s easy to make and perfect for a BBQ where you want to skip the open fire but still indulge in the classic gooey chocolate and marshmallow combination. Serve it with graham crackers, pretzels, or fruit for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of chocolate chips
  • 1 cup of mini marshmallows
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • A pinch of sea salt (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a cast iron skillet or a heatproof dish, add the chocolate chips and butter. Place the dish on the grill and heat until the chocolate is melted, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once the chocolate has melted, top with the mini marshmallows.
  4. Cover and cook for about 5-7 minutes, until the marshmallows are golden and slightly toasted.
  5. Remove from the grill and sprinkle with a pinch of sea salt, if desired.
  6. Serve with graham crackers, pretzels, or fresh fruit for dipping.

This S’mores dip is a simple yet indulgent dessert that captures the essence of summer in every bite.

5. Watermelon Sorbet

For a refreshing and cooling treat on a hot summer day, watermelon sorbet is the perfect option. It’s made with fresh watermelon, lime, and just a touch of sweetness to enhance the natural flavors. This light, frozen dessert is incredibly refreshing and the ideal way to end a BBQ on a sweet, hydrating note.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of cubed seedless watermelon
  • 1/2 cup of sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup of water

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the watermelon cubes, sugar, lime juice, and water. Blend until smooth.
  2. Taste the mixture and adjust the sweetness by adding more sugar, if desired.
  3. Pour the mixture into a shallow baking dish and freeze for about 4 hours, or until firm.
  4. Once frozen, scrape the sorbet with a fork to create a fluffy, shaved texture.
  5. Serve in small bowls or glasses, and garnish with fresh mint leaves or a squeeze of lime juice for an extra burst of freshness.

This watermelon sorbet is a deliciously hydrating dessert that’s naturally sweet and perfect for hot summer days.

6. Chocolate-Dipped Frozen Banana Bites

Bananas are a favorite fruit for many, and when frozen and dipped in chocolate, they become a delightful frozen treat that’s both satisfying and refreshing. These chocolate-dipped frozen banana bites are easy to make, and they offer the perfect combination of creamy banana and rich chocolate.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas, peeled and cut into 1-inch slices
  • 8 oz semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (optional)
  • Chopped nuts, sprinkles, or shredded coconut for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Arrange the banana slices on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Melt the chocolate chips in a heatproof bowl over a double boiler, or microwave in 30-second intervals until fully melted. Stir in the coconut oil, if using, to make the chocolate smoother.
  3. Dip each banana slice into the melted chocolate, coating it generously. Return the banana slices to the baking sheet.
  4. Optional: Sprinkle with chopped nuts, sprinkles, or shredded coconut before the chocolate hardens.
  5. Freeze the banana bites for at least 1 hour, or until the chocolate is set and the bananas are frozen.
  6. Serve immediately or store in an airtight container in the freezer for later enjoyment.

These chocolate-dipped frozen banana bites are the perfect combination of creamy and crunchy, making them a fun and easy dessert for your summer BBQ.

Conclusion

Whether you’re grilling up savory dishes or just enjoying the warm weather with friends, the right dessert can make a summer BBQ truly memorable. From fresh, fruity options like grilled peaches and strawberry shortcake cups to indulgent treats like s’mores dip and lemon cheesecake bars, these dessert recipes will satisfy any sweet tooth. The best part? They’re easy to prepare and perfect for enjoying outside in the sunshine. So fire up the grill, whip up one of these desserts, and get ready to create lasting memories with loved ones at your next summer BBQ.
