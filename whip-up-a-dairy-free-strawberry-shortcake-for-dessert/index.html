Whip Up a Dairy-Free Strawberry Shortcake for Dessert


Craving a delicious and refreshing dessert that is both dairy-free and bursting with the flavors of summer? Look no further than this irresistible Dairy-Free Strawberry Shortcake recipe! With its light and fluffy layers, sweet and juicy strawberries, and creamy coconut whipped cream, this dessert is sure to be a hit at any gathering or simply as a treat for yourself. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the shortcake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup dairy-free milk (such as almond or oat milk)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the strawberry filling:

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar

For the coconut whipped cream:

  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk, refrigerated overnight
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt until well combined.

  3. Add the cold cubed dairy-free butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Make a well in the center of the mixture and pour in the dairy-free milk and vanilla extract. Stir until just combined, being careful not to overmix.

  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick rectangle.

  6. Using a round biscuit cutter or a glass, cut out shortcakes from the dough and place them onto the prepared baking sheet. Gather any remaining dough scraps and repeat the process until all the dough is used.

  7. Bake the shortcakes in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they are golden brown. Remove from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack.

  8. While the shortcakes are cooling, prepare the strawberry filling. In a medium bowl, toss together the sliced strawberries and granulated sugar. Let them sit for about 15 minutes to release their natural juices.

  9. To make the coconut whipped cream, open the can of refrigerated coconut milk and carefully scoop out the solid coconut cream that has separated at the top. Place it in a mixing bowl, leaving the coconut water behind.

  10. Using an electric mixer, beat the coconut cream on high speed for a minute until it becomes fluffy. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to beat until fully combined and smooth.

  11. Once the shortcakes have cooled, slice them in half horizontally. Place the bottom halves on serving plates, and spoon a generous amount of the strawberry filling onto each one.

  12. Top the strawberry filling with a dollop of coconut whipped cream, and then place the remaining shortcake halves on top.

  13. Finish off each shortcake with another spoonful of strawberry filling and a final dollop of coconut whipped cream. Feel free to garnish with a fresh strawberry or mint leaves for an extra touch.

  14. Serve immediately and enjoy this delightful dairy-free Strawberry Shortcake!

Conclusion

With this easy-to-follow recipe, you can indulge in a scrumptious dairy-free Strawberry Shortcake that will impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more. The combination of the tender shortcake, juicy strawberries, and creamy coconut whipped cream is a match made in dessert heaven. So why wait? Gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and whip up this delightful treat for your next special occasion or simply as a sweet ending to any meal. Bon appétit!
