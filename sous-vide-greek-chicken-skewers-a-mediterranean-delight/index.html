Sous Vide Greek Chicken Skewers: A Mediterranean Delight


If you’re looking for a flavorful and tender chicken dish that captures the essence of the Mediterranean, then look no further than Sous Vide Greek Chicken Skewers. This recipe combines the traditional flavors of Greece with the modern cooking technique of sous vide, resulting in succulent and perfectly cooked chicken every time. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply craving a taste of Greece, these skewers are sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the marinade:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

For the chicken:

  • 1.5 lbs (680g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

For serving:

  • Tzatziki sauce
  • Pita bread
  • Fresh lemon wedges
  • Chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and black pepper. This flavorful marinade will infuse the chicken with the delicious taste of Greece. Set aside.

  2. Place the cubed chicken in a resealable plastic bag or a vacuum-sealed bag. Pour the marinade over the chicken, making sure it’s evenly coated. Seal the bag and massage the marinade into the chicken, ensuring every piece is well coated. Place the bag in the refrigerator and let the chicken marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for even more intense flavors.

  3. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 145°F (63°C). This low and slow cooking method will ensure the chicken remains juicy and tender while allowing the flavors to develop.

  4. Remove the chicken from the marinade, shaking off any excess. Thread the chicken onto the soaked wooden skewers, making sure not to overcrowd them. This will allow the chicken to cook evenly.

  5. Place the skewers in a single layer in a sous vide bag or a vacuum-sealed bag. Make sure to leave enough space between each skewer for the water to circulate. Seal the bag, removing as much air as possible.

  6. Gently place the bag of skewers into the preheated water bath. Ensure the bag is fully submerged, and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours. The sous vide technique will guarantee perfectly cooked chicken that is tender and full of flavor.

  7. Once the chicken is done cooking, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Take out the skewers and pat them dry with a paper towel.

  8. Optional step: For an extra burst of flavor, you can finish the chicken skewers on a hot grill or under the broiler for a few minutes, just until they develop a slightly charred exterior.

  9. Serve the Sous Vide Greek Chicken Skewers with tzatziki sauce, pita bread, fresh lemon wedges, and a sprinkle of chopped fresh parsley. The cool and refreshing tzatziki perfectly complements the tender chicken, while the pita bread adds a delightful crunch. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the skewers for an added zing.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Greek Chicken Skewers are a true taste of the Mediterranean. The combination of the traditional Greek marinade and the precision cooking technique of sous vide results in chicken that is juicy, tender, and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re hosting a Greek-themed party or simply craving a delicious and healthy meal, these skewers are sure to transport your taste buds to the sun-soaked shores of Greece. So fire up your sous vide machine, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in this Mediterranean delight. Opa!
