Step-by-Step Guide to Making Korean Steamed Egg


Welcome to our cooking blog, where we explore unique and delicious recipes from around the world. Today, we’re diving into the vibrant and flavorful world of Korean cuisine with a step-by-step guide to making Korean Steamed Egg. This dish is a true delight, combining the silky texture of a custard with the savory flavors of Korean seasonings. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

For this recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 scallion (green onion), finely chopped
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Steaming Setup: Fill a large pot with water and place a steamer basket inside. Bring the water to a boil over medium heat.

  2. Whisk the Eggs: In a mixing bowl, crack the eggs and whisk them until well beaten. Make sure the yolks and whites are fully incorporated.

  3. Add the Broth: Slowly pour the chicken or vegetable broth into the beaten eggs, while continuing to whisk. This will help create a smooth and silky texture.

  4. Season the Mixture: Add the soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and a pinch of salt to the egg mixture. Give it a good stir, ensuring all the seasonings are well combined.

  5. Prepare the Steaming Dish: Lightly grease a heatproof dish or individual ramekins with cooking spray or a small amount of oil. This will prevent the steamed egg from sticking.

  6. Pour the Mixture: Pour the egg mixture into the prepared dish(es). If using ramekins, divide the mixture equally among them.

  7. Steam the Egg: Carefully place the dish(es) into the steamer basket. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and steam for approximately 10-12 minutes, or until the eggs are set.

  8. Garnish and Serve: Once the eggs are cooked, remove the dish(es) from the steamer and let them cool slightly. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve warm.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more flavorful steamed egg, you can add other ingredients such as cooked shrimp, diced vegetables, or even kimchi.
  • To make a vegetarian version, simply substitute the chicken broth with vegetable broth.
  • Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences. You can add more soy sauce, sesame oil, or sugar if desired.
  • If you don’t have a steamer, you can use a large skillet with a lid. Fill the skillet with water, place a heatproof dish inside, cover, and steam over medium-low heat.

Final Thoughts

Korean Steamed Egg is a delightful dish that showcases the simplicity and elegance of Korean cuisine. With its silky texture and savory flavors, it’s a perfect addition to any meal. Whether you’re a fan of Korean food or looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to impress.
