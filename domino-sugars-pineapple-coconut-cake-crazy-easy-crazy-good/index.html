Domino Sugar's Pineapple Coconut Cake: Crazy Easy, Crazy Good


Ah, the delightful, tropical combination of pineapple and coconut – two flavors that always remind me of a blissful beach vacation. And what better way to transport your taste buds to a sunny paradise than with a slice of Pineapple Coconut Cake? In this blog post, I’ll be sharing a delicious recipe sure to please your palate and put a smile on your face. Let’s dive into the wonderful world of sweet desserts with a twist as we create the Domino Sugar’s Pineapple Coconut Cake.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Domino Sugar
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 (8 oz) package cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cups shredded coconut
  • 1 can (20 oz) crushed pineapple, drained
  • 1/2 cup Domino Granulated Sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

Now that we’ve gathered the ingredients, let’s start making our Pineapple Coconut Cake. It will challenge your culinary skills without making you sweat over complicated methods. Here we go:

Step 1: Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Batter

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This is the first step to creating that perfect, moist cake. The heat will be evenly distributed, baking your cake to perfection.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened unsalted butter and Domino Sugar until smooth. This will help create a light and fluffy batter.
  3. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Incorporating eggs gradually prevents the cake batter from curdling.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the butter mix, alternating with the buttermilk, and continue beating until everything is well-combined. It’s okay if the batter looks slightly curdled at this point; it will smooth out.

Step 2: Add Flavor with Pineapple and Coconut

  1. Combine the shredded coconut, crushed pineapple (drained), and 1/2 cup of Domino Granulated Sugar in a large mixing bowl. The pineapple adds a burst of tropical sweetness, while the shredded coconut brings a delightful texture.
  2. Fold together this fruity mixture into the cake batter until it’s evenly distributed. The pineapple’s juices will add more moisture, enhancing the cake’s deliciousness.

Step 3: Bake till the Cake is Golden

  1. Pour the batter evenly into two greased and floured 9-inch round cake pans. Creating two layers will make assembling the cake a breeze.
  2. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The delightful aroma of pineapple and coconut will fill your kitchen, signaling that your cake is ready.
  3. Cool the cakes in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. This will ensure that the cake firmer and easier to frost.

Step 4: Cream Cheese Frosting for Extra Delight

  1. Beat the cream cheese and softened unsalted butter in a large mixing bowl until creamy. This frosting is rich, smooth and complements the cake’s tropical flavors perfectly.
  2. Add the vanilla extract, then gradually incorporate the rest of the powdered sugar (from the original 2 cups). Whip until the frosting is fluffy and spreadable.
  3. Once the cake has cooled, apply a thin layer of frosting to the top of the first layer, and then place the second layer on top. Frost the sides and top of the cake, then garnish with additional shredded coconut, if desired.

And there you have it - the Crazy Easy, Crazy Good Pineapple Coconut Cake! It’s a testament to the creative powers of cooking, combining simplicity, delicious flavors, and an ode to tropical vacations.

This lovely cake is bound to please your guests and bring joy to any gathering. Don’t forget to take a photo before it disappears, and share the love by passing the recipe along. Happy baking, and best wishes on your culinary adventures to purer bliss: Domino Sugar’s Pineapple Coconut Cake!
