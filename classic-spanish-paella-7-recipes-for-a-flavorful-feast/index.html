Classic Spanish Paella: 7 Recipes for a Flavorful Feast


Paella, a quintessential Spanish dish, is as diverse and rich as the country’s culinary traditions. Originating from the Valencia region on Spain’s eastern coast, this iconic dish has evolved over the centuries into a variety of regional styles, each with its own unique twist. Whether you’re a paella purist or someone looking to experiment with new flavors, there’s a paella recipe for everyone. In this blog post, we’ll explore seven different ways to prepare this beloved dish, each offering a flavorful and authentic experience.

1. Traditional Valencian Paella

The Valencian Paella, known as Paella Valenciana, is the original version of this dish and a must-try for anyone looking to experience the roots of Spanish cuisine. It combines a variety of fresh ingredients and is characterized by its distinct blend of savory and slightly sweet flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice (preferably Bomba rice)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup green beans
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/2 pound chicken thighs, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 pound rabbit (optional), cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup artichoke hearts (optional)
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large paella pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant. Stir in grated tomatoes and cook for a few minutes until they release their juices.

  2. Cook the Meat: Add chicken and rabbit pieces to the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Brown the meat on all sides, allowing it to develop a rich, golden color.

  3. Add Vegetables and Rice: Toss in green beans, peas, and artichokes. Stir everything together, then add the rice, allowing it to toast for about 2 minutes.

  4. Add Liquid and Simmer: Pour in the broth, saffron, and white wine. Stir to combine, then let the mixture simmer over low heat without stirring for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice has absorbed the liquid and become tender.

  5. Rest and Serve: Remove the paella from heat and cover with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Serve hot and enjoy!

This traditional paella recipe is a perfect example of the vibrant flavors of Spain and an excellent starting point for anyone exploring the world of paella.

2. Seafood Paella

Seafood Paella, or Paella de Mariscos, is one of the most popular versions of this dish, especially along Spain’s Mediterranean coast. With the fresh bounty of the sea, this recipe bursts with flavor and highlights the delicate taste of shrimp, clams, mussels, and squid.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Bomba rice
  • 4 cups seafood broth (or chicken broth)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 pound squid, cleaned and sliced
  • 1/2 pound mussels
  • 1/2 pound clams
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sofrito: In a large paella pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until fragrant. Stir in the grated tomato and bell pepper, allowing it to cook down for about 5 minutes.

  2. Cook the Rice: Add the rice to the pan, toasting it lightly for a couple of minutes. Pour in the saffron, white wine, and seafood broth, then season with salt and pepper.

  3. Add the Seafood: Bring the mixture to a simmer. After about 10 minutes, add the shrimp, squid, mussels, and clams. Stir gently and allow the seafood to cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the shellfish have opened and the shrimp are pink.

  4. Rest and Serve: Remove from heat and cover with a clean towel. Let the paella rest for 5-10 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld and ensures the rice is perfectly tender.

The sweet seafood flavor, combined with the rich broth and saffron, makes this a showstopper that will delight your taste buds.

3. Vegetarian Paella

For those who prefer a plant-based version of this dish, the Vegetarian Paella is an excellent choice. It features a colorful array of vegetables that create a fresh and satisfying alternative without sacrificing flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1/2 cup artichoke hearts, quartered
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/2 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, sautéing for about 5 minutes until softened. Stir in the grated tomato, zucchini, and artichoke hearts, cooking for another 5 minutes.

  2. Add Rice and Broth: Stir in the rice, letting it toast for a minute or two. Add the vegetable broth, saffron, white wine, salt, and pepper, stirring to combine.

  3. Simmer: Let the paella simmer without stirring for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed. Add peas and green beans in the last 10 minutes of cooking.

  4. Let Rest and Serve: Once the rice is fully cooked, remove the pan from the heat and cover it with a kitchen towel. Allow it to rest for 5 minutes before serving.

This vegetarian version brings out the natural sweetness of the vegetables and offers a lighter but equally delicious take on the classic dish.

4. Chicken and Chorizo Paella

The Chicken and Chorizo Paella is a robust variation that combines the rich flavors of Spanish sausage and tender chicken. The smokiness from the chorizo gives the dish a unique and deep flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Bomba rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 2 chicken breasts, cut into pieces
  • 2 chorizo sausages, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken and Chorizo: Heat olive oil in a paella pan over medium heat. Brown the chicken pieces on all sides, then remove and set aside. In the same pan, cook the chorizo slices until they release their oils and become crispy.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper to the pan, cooking until softened. Stir in the grated tomato and cook for 5 minutes.

  3. Add Rice and Broth: Stir in the rice, allowing it to toast for a couple of minutes. Pour in the chicken broth, saffron, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken back into the pan, then bring the mixture to a simmer.

  4. Simmer and Serve: Let the paella cook for 20-25 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed. Add peas in the last 10 minutes. Remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes.

This hearty dish combines the savory, smoky flavors of chorizo with the tenderness of chicken for a satisfying meal.

5. Black Paella (Paella Negra)

One of the most striking and unique variations of paella is the Black Paella, or Paella Negra, which uses cuttlefish ink to give the rice a deep, dramatic black color. This version is often served in coastal regions and is a favorite among seafood lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice
  • 4 cups seafood broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 pound squid, sliced
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons cuttlefish ink
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Seafood: Heat olive oil in a large pan and add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened. Add the squid and shrimp, cooking for 3-4 minutes until just tender.

  2. Prepare the Rice: Add the rice to the pan, stirring to combine with the seafood. Pour in the seafood broth, saffron, and cuttlefish ink, then bring to a simmer.

  3. Simmer and Serve: Cook the paella for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed. Stir in the peas in the final 10 minutes. Let it rest for 5 minutes before serving.

The cuttlefish ink gives this paella its signature color and adds a unique, briny flavor that pairs perfectly with the tender seafood.

6. Mixed Paella

For those who love variety, the Mixed Paella offers a blend of both meat and seafood, providing a delicious balance of flavors. It’s a great way to enjoy the best of both worlds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 1/2 pound chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 pound mussels
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: Heat olive oil in a large paella pan. Brown the chicken pieces and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Sofrito: Add onion and garlic to the pan, sautéing until softened. Stir in grated tomato and cook for another 5 minutes.

  3. Add Rice and Broth: Add the rice to the pan, allowing it to toast for a couple of minutes. Pour in the chicken broth, saffron, and seasoning, then bring to a simmer.

  4. Add the Seafood: After 15 minutes, add shrimp and mussels to the pan. Allow everything to cook until the seafood is tender and the rice is fully cooked.

  5. Rest and Serve: Let the paella rest for a few minutes before serving.

This mixed paella is an excellent way to enjoy the different textures and flavors of both land and sea.

7. Paella with Chanterelle Mushrooms

A unique take on the classic, this recipe incorporates chanterelle mushrooms to create an earthy, savory paella that feels both luxurious and comforting. The combination of wild mushrooms and rich broth elevates this dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron
  • 1/2 pound chanterelle mushrooms
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, grated
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Mushrooms: Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté the mushrooms until tender and browned. Set them aside.

  2. Prepare the Base: In the same pan, sauté onion and garlic until softened. Stir in grated tomato and cook for a few minutes.

  3. Add Rice and Broth: Stir in the rice and cook for a minute. Pour in vegetable broth, saffron, and season with salt and pepper.

  4. Simmer: Let the paella simmer for 20 minutes, adding the chanterelle mushrooms and peas in the final 10 minutes of cooking.

  5. Rest and Serve: Let the paella rest for 5 minutes before serving.

The earthy flavor of chanterelle mushrooms adds a delightful twist to the traditional paella recipe, making it perfect for mushroom lovers.

Whether you prefer seafood, meat, or a vegetable-packed version, paella offers endless possibilities for experimentation. These seven recipes provide a comprehensive look into the diverse ways this dish can be prepared, each delivering rich flavors and a touch of Spanish authenticity. So gather your ingredients, heat your paella pan, and get ready to create a flavorful feast for your next special occasion or family dinner.
