Satisfy Your Cravings with Traditional Chinese Lion's Head Meatballs


Lion’s Head Meatballs, also known as Shi Zi Tou in Mandarin, are a popular dish in Chinese cuisine. These meatballs are named after their large size and resemblance to the head of a lion. Made with a combination of ground pork and various spices, Lion’s Head Meatballs are known for their tender texture and rich flavor. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating these delectable meatballs from scratch.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1/4 cup water chestnuts, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, water chestnuts, green onions, minced garlic, grated ginger, soy sauce, rice wine, cornstarch, salt, white pepper, and sugar. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking, then shape the meat mixture into large meatballs, about the size of a tennis ball. Place the meatballs on a plate or tray.

  3. Preheat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the vegetable oil and heat it until shimmering. Carefully add the meatballs to the pan, ensuring they are evenly spaced.

  4. Cook the meatballs for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown. This will help seal in the flavors and give them a nice crispy exterior.

  5. Once the meatballs are browned, carefully pour the chicken broth into the skillet or wok. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the meatballs are cooked through and tender.

  6. Remove the lid and increase the heat to medium-high. Allow the broth to reduce and thicken slightly, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.

  7. Serve the Lion’s Head Meatballs hot, garnished with fresh green onions. They can be enjoyed on their own as a main dish, or served with steamed rice and vegetables for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier alternative, you can use lean ground turkey or chicken instead of pork.
  • Feel free to add your own twist to the recipe by incorporating additional spices or herbs such as cilantro or chili flakes.
  • To enhance the flavors, you can marinate the meat mixture for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator before shaping into meatballs.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can mix an additional tablespoon of cornstarch with water and add it to the skillet during the final minutes of cooking.

Conclusion

Traditional Chinese Lion’s Head Meatballs are a delightful dish that will satisfy your cravings for authentic Chinese cuisine. With their juicy and flavorful texture, these meatballs are sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you serve them as a main course or as part of a larger meal, Lion’s Head Meatballs are a delicious addition to any dining table. Give this recipe a try and experience the wonderful flavors of Chinese cooking in the comfort of your own home. Enjoy!
