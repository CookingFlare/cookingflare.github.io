Cooking with Fresh Tarragon: Flavorful Recipes for Any Occasion


Tarragon, with its delicate anise-like aroma and slightly sweet, licorice flavor, is a standout herb that deserves a place in every kitchen. While it may not be as ubiquitous as parsley or basil, fresh tarragon’s distinctive taste can elevate a wide range of dishes. Whether you’re experimenting in the kitchen or preparing a tried-and-true family recipe, tarragon brings nuance and depth to your culinary creations.

In this blog post, we’ll explore the magic of cooking with fresh tarragon, offering flavorful recipes and practical tips to inspire your next meal. From sauces and marinades to main courses and beverages, these recipes will showcase the versatility of this underappreciated herb.

The Allure of Fresh Tarragon

Fresh tarragon stands apart from its dried counterpart due to its vibrant and subtle flavor profile. As part of the herb mix “fines herbes,” it is widely used in French cuisine, but its potential reaches far beyond European kitchens. Tarragon pairs beautifully with poultry, fish, eggs, and vegetables, and it shines equally in creamy sauces, tangy dressings, and even cocktails.

When selecting fresh tarragon at the market or harvesting from your garden, look for bright green, tender leaves with a slightly glossy appearance. The stems should be firm yet pliable, and the herb should exude its characteristic licorice scent. Once you have your tarragon, the culinary possibilities are endless.

Tarragon Mustard Marinade for Grilled Chicken

This versatile marinade infuses chicken with bold flavors while keeping it tender and juicy. It’s perfect for grilling, baking, or even pan-searing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons fresh tarragon, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional, for a hint of sweetness)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the tarragon, Dijon mustard, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, honey (if using), salt, and pepper until smooth.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them. Ensure the chicken is evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. When ready to cook, preheat your grill (or oven) to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with additional fresh tarragon if desired.

This recipe pairs wonderfully with a crisp green salad or roasted seasonal vegetables.

Creamy Tarragon Sauce for Fish and Vegetables

This elegant tarragon sauce is a must-have for delicate fish like cod, halibut, or salmon. It’s also an excellent companion for steamed vegetables like asparagus, broccoli, or green beans.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons fresh tarragon, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 shallots, finely minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced shallots and cook until soft and translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer until reduced by half.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the cream. Simmer gently for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly.
  4. Add the fresh tarragon and lemon zest. Cook for an additional minute to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, adjusting to taste.

Serve this luscious sauce drizzled over grilled fish or alongside roasted vegetables for a sophisticated and flavorful dish.

Tarragon and Lemon Potato Salad

Give the classic potato salad a fresh twist with the addition of tarragon and a bright lemon vinaigrette. This version is lighter than the traditional mayonnaise-based salad yet equally satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds small new potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons fresh tarragon, finely chopped
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and let cool slightly before cutting them into halves or quarters.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to create the vinaigrette.
  3. In a large bowl, toss the cooked potatoes with the vinaigrette while they are still warm to allow them to absorb the flavors.
  4. Fold in the fresh tarragon and green onions. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Serve warm or at room temperature.

This zesty potato salad is a crowd-pleaser at picnics, barbecues, and casual dinners alike.

Tarragon-Infused Lemonade

Tarragon’s subtle licorice notes make it a surprising yet delicious addition to homemade lemonade. This refreshing beverage is perfect for summer gatherings or as an afternoon pick-me-up.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh lemon juice (about 6-8 lemons)
  • 1 cup sugar or honey
  • 4 cups water
  • 3-4 sprigs fresh tarragon
  • Ice cubes for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the sugar (or honey) with 1 cup of water. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely to create a simple syrup.
  2. Remove from heat and add the tarragon sprigs to the saucepan. Let the syrup cool to room temperature, allowing the tarragon to infuse its flavor.
  3. Strain the tarragon syrup into a pitcher and discard the sprigs. Add the lemon juice and the remaining water to the pitcher. Stir well and refrigerate until chilled.
  4. Serve over ice, garnished with a sprig of tarragon or a lemon slice for a touch of elegance.

This tarragon-infused lemonade balances sweet, tart, and herbal flavors perfectly, offering a unique take on a classic drink.

Cooking Tips for Fresh Tarragon Success

  1. Use it fresh when possible: While dried tarragon works in some recipes, fresh tarragon’s flavor is more vibrant and nuanced, making it suitable for salads, garnishes, and delicate sauces.
  2. Add it at the right time: Tarragon’s flavor can diminish with prolonged cooking, so add it toward the end of the cooking process for best results.
  3. Pair with complementary ingredients: Tarragon pairs well with citrus, mustard, cream, butter, and white wine, as well as proteins like chicken, fish, and eggs.
  4. Store properly: To keep fresh tarragon at its best, wrap it in a damp paper towel and place it in a plastic bag in the refrigerator. It will last up to a week when stored this way.

Fresh tarragon is a culinary gem that can elevate a variety of dishes. Whether you’re creating a show-stopping entrée or a simple side, this herb brings a unique flavor profile to the table. As you explore the recipes above, you’ll discover that cooking with tarragon is not only delicious but also endlessly inspiring. So, grab a bunch of fresh tarragon and start experimenting—you might just discover your new favorite ingredient!
