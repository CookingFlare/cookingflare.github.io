Burrito-licious: Showstopping Recipes for Any Occasion!


A burrito is more than just a tortilla filled with deliciousness. It’s a celebration of flavors, textures, and convenience. Ideal for any occasion, be it a casual gathering or a festive fiesta, burritos have the remarkable ability to satisfy and delight. In this blog post, we’re diving deep into the world of burritos, bringing you three showstopping recipes that will make your next gathering truly unforgettable.

Recipe #1: Spicy Chicken Burritos

Prep time: 20 minutes Cook time: 15 minutes Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 can (15.5 oz.) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 medium bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 6 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, guacamole, chopped cilantro

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the chicken, 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Heat the remaining oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the chicken for 5-7 minutes, until fully cooked.
  3. In a separate skillet, cook the black beans, red onion, and bell pepper over medium heat for 3-5 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Warm the tortillas according to the package instructions. Lay out each tortilla on a flat surface, and evenly distribute the cooked chicken, bean-vegetable mixture, salsa, and shredded cheese.
  5. Roll up the burritos tightly and place them seam-side down on a lined baking sheet. Lightly spray with oil, and bake at 375°F for 10-15 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  6. Let your guests customize their burritos with the optional toppings! Enjoy.

Recipe #2: Vegetarian Black Bean Burritos

Prep time: 20 minutes Cook time: 15 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup uncooked white rice
  • 1 can (15.5 oz.) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, guacamole, hot sauce

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions. Set aside to cool.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and zucchini, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the black beans, corn, chili powder, cumin, and salt. Cook for 3-5 minutes, until heated through.
  4. Warm the tortillas according to the package instructions. Evenly distribute the cooked rice mixture and shredded cheese onto each tortilla.
  5. Roll up the burritos tightly and place them seam-side down on a lined baking sheet. Lightly spray with oil, and bake at 375°F for 10-15 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  6. Serve the burritos with the optional toppings. Enjoy.

Recipe #3: Slow Cooker Carnitas Burritos

Prep time: 10 minutes Cook time: 3-4 hours Serves: 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. pork shoulder or carnitas
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz.) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, guacamole, chopped cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onions until softened.
  2. In a slow cooker, combine the cooked onions, pork, diced tomatoes, chicken broth, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, and salt.
  3. Cook on low for 3-4 hours, until the pork is tender and falls apart easily.
  4. Shred the pork using two forks, discarding any excess fat.
  5. Warm the tortillas according to the package instructions. Divide the shredded pork evenly among the tortillas, along with shredded cheese.
  6. Roll up the burritos tightly and place them seam-side down on a lined baking sheet. Lightly spray with oil, and bake at 375°F for 10-15 minutes, until golden and crispy.
  7. Allow your guests to add their favorite toppings. Enjoy.

Final Thoughts

Burritos are versatile creations, and the possibilities are endless when it comes to ingredient combinations. Challenge yourself to experiment with new flavors and textures, customize your recipes to suit your audience, and witness as your guests fall in love with the world of burritos! Happy cooking and banqueting!
