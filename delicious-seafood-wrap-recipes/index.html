Delicious Seafood Wrap Recipes


Seafood wraps offer a delightful combination of freshness, zest, and satisfying textures, perfect for lunches, picnics, or light dinners. Whether you’re craving the sweet crunch of crispy shrimp, the smoky flavor of grilled fish, or the creamy richness of crab meat, seafood wraps bring a wonderful diversity of tastes wrapped up in a convenient, portable package. Today, we’ll explore a range of delicious seafood wrap recipes, peppered with tips for perfect seasoning, creative fillings, and accompaniments that will elevate your seafood wrap experience.

Why Choose Seafood Wraps?

Seafood wraps are not just delicious—they’re versatile, nutritious, and quick to prepare. Seafood, such as shrimp, crab, fish, and scallops, is rich in omega-3 fatty acids and high-quality protein, making these wraps both wholesome and satisfying. Wrapping these ingredients in soft tortillas, crisp lettuce leaves, or even seaweed sheets allows you to enjoy a meal that’s easy to eat on the go, with endless variations to keep your palate intrigued.

Essential Ingredients for Tasty Seafood Wraps

Crafting flavor-packed seafood wraps starts with choosing fresh seafood and the right blend of complementary ingredients. Here are the building blocks for dynamic wraps:

  • Fresh Seafood: Shrimp, crab meat, grilled or pan-seared white fish (like cod or tilapia), smoked salmon, or scallops.
  • Tortilla or Wrap Base: Flour tortillas, whole wheat, spinach or tomato wraps, lettuce leaves, or even rice paper for an Asian-inspired twist.
  • Crunchy Fillings: Shredded cabbage, julienned carrots, cucumber slices, bell peppers, or radishes.
  • Creamy Elements: Avocado, cream cheese, yogurt-based sauces, or aioli.
  • Fresh Herbs: Cilantro, dill, basil, or chives.
  • Citrus and Zest: Lemon, lime, or orange zest to brighten flavors.
  • Spices and Seasonings: Paprika, cayenne, Old Bay seasoning, cumin, or black pepper.
  • Sauces and Dressings: Sriracha mayo, garlic aioli, chipotle sauce, teriyaki glaze, or a tangy vinaigrette.

Recipe 1: Spicy Shrimp Avocado Wrap

A spicy, creamy combination bursting with fresh flavors, this wrap is perfect for a light yet satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 10 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • ½ cup diced tomatoes
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 large flour tortillas
  • 3 tbsp spicy mayo (mix mayonnaise with Sriracha to taste)
  • Juice of ½ lime

Instructions

  1. Season the Shrimp: In a bowl, toss the shrimp with smoked paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
  2. Cook the Shrimp: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque.
  3. Prepare the Wrap: Lay out the tortillas and spread a tablespoon of spicy mayo on each.
  4. Layer the Fillings: Add shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cilantro, and avocado slices.
  5. Add Shrimp and Lime Juice: Place the cooked shrimp on top and drizzle with fresh lime juice.
  6. Wrap it Up: Fold the sides of the tortilla and roll tightly.
  7. Serve: Cut in half and serve immediately or wrap in foil for an on-the-go meal.

Pro tip: For extra crunch, toss some thinly sliced radishes or cucumbers inside before wrapping.

Recipe 2: Crab and Mango Citrus Wrap

The sweet, buttery crab paired with the tropical tang of mango makes this wrap a flavorful standout.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh crab meat (lump or claw meat)
  • 1 ripe mango, diced
  • 1 cup mixed greens
  • ¼ cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh basil
  • 2 large spinach wraps
  • 3 tbsp Greek yogurt
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Make the Citrus Yogurt Sauce: Combine Greek yogurt, honey, orange zest, and orange juice in a small bowl. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Prepare the Wrap: Spread the citrus yogurt sauce evenly over each spinach wrap.
  3. Layer the Ingredients: Add the mixed greens, crab meat, diced mango, red onion, and fresh basil.
  4. Wrap It Up: Roll snugly, tucking in the sides as you go.
  5. Chill and Serve: For best flavor, chill the wraps for 15 minutes before serving to let the citrus sauce meld with the crab.

Tip: If fresh crab meat isn’t available, good-quality canned crab can substitute well when drained properly.

Recipe 3: Grilled Fish Tacos with Cilantro Lime Slaw

Taking inspiration from the classic fish taco, this wrap combines smoky grilled fish with zesty slaw for contrast.

Ingredients

  • 2 white fish fillets (cod, tilapia, or halibut)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups shredded cabbage
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 2 large whole wheat tortillas

Instructions

  1. Season the Fish: Rub fish with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill the Fish: Preheat grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Cook fish about 4 minutes per side until flaky.
  3. Prepare the Slaw: Combine shredded cabbage, chopped cilantro, lime juice, and mayonnaise in a bowl.
  4. Assemble the Wraps: Lay tortillas flat, place fish fillet on each, and top with cilantro lime slaw.
  5. Serve: Roll up and serve with extra lime wedges.

Pro tip: Warm tortillas before assembling for the best texture and flavor.

Recipe 4: Smoked Salmon and Cucumber Wrap with Dill Cream Cheese

A refined yet simple wrap that’s perfect for brunch or a quick lunch, bursting with delicate and fresh flavors.

Ingredients

  • 4 oz smoked salmon, thinly sliced
  • 1 large cucumber, peeled into ribbons using a vegetable peeler
  • 4 tbsp cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tbsp capers, drained
  • 2 large tomato basil wraps or flour tortillas
  • Fresh ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Make the Dill Cream Cheese: Mix cream cheese with chopped dill until well combined.
  2. Prepare the Wrap: Spread a generous layer of dill cream cheese on each wrap.
  3. Layer the Salmon and Cucumber: Arrange smoked salmon slices evenly on top, then add cucumber ribbons.
  4. Add Capers and Pepper: Sprinkle capers and freshly ground black pepper over the filling.
  5. Roll and Serve: Roll each wrap tightly and slice on the diagonal.

Suggestion: Serve with a side of lemon wedges or a light green salad.

Recipe 5: Spicy Thai Scallop and Mango Rice Paper Wraps

For a refreshing and light option, rice paper wraps filled with scallops and mango strike the perfect balance.

Ingredients

  • 12 sea scallops, cleaned and patted dry
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp chili paste (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 mango, julienned
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • ½ cup fresh mint leaves
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 6 rice paper wrappers
  • 2 cups mixed greens or shredded lettuce

Instructions

  1. Marinate and Cook Scallops: Marinate scallops in fish sauce, chili paste, and lime juice for 10 minutes. Sear scallops in a hot pan for 2 minutes per side.
  2. Prepare Rice Papers: Soak rice paper wrappers in warm water until soft, then lay flat.
  3. Assemble Wraps: On each wrapper, place a few mixed greens, carrot, mango, fresh herbs, and 2 scallops.
  4. Wrap Tightly: Fold sides and roll up like a burrito.
  5. Serve with Dipping Sauce: Pair with a sweet chili or peanut dipping sauce for extra zest.

Tip: Ensure no excess water remains on the rice paper before rolling to prevent sogginess.

Complementary Sides and Beverages for Seafood Wraps

To complete your seafood wrap meal, consider pairing your wrap with:

  • Sides: Sweet potato fries, roasted corn, fresh fruit salad, or light vegetable soup.
  • Beverages: A crisp white wine, citrus-infused sparkling water, light beer, or iced green tea.

Tips for Making the Best Seafood Wraps Every Time

  • Freshness Matters: Always use the freshest seafood available for the best taste and texture.
  • Balance Flavors: Combine creamy elements with acidic and spicy components to create depth.
  • Texture Contrast: Include crunchy vegetables to contrast with tender seafood.
  • Don’t Overfill: Keep fillings moderate for easier rolling and eating.
  • Warm Tortillas: Lightly warming wraps makes them pliable and enhances taste.

Seafood wraps marry the savory richness of ocean flavors with fresh, vibrant ingredients wrapped in a handheld feast. Whether you’re a seafood aficionado or just discovering the joys of fish and shellfish, these wraps provide endless opportunities to experiment and savor every bite. From spicy shrimp to smooth smoked salmon to zesty scallop wraps, your taste buds are in for a festivity of flavors.

Get creative, have fun with ingredients and spices, and let your culinary imagination sail into the flavorful waters of seafood wraps!
