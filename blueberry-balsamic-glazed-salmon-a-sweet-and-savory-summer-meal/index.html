Blueberry Balsamic Glazed Salmon: A Sweet and Savory Summer Meal


As the summer season approaches, many of us start to crave lighter, fresher, and more refreshing meals that still pack a punch of flavor. One dish that perfectly captures the essence of summer is blueberry balsamic glazed salmon. This sweet and savory recipe is a perfect blend of flavors and textures that will leave you wanting more. In this post, we’ll dive into the details of this mouth-watering dish and provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to prepare it.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 ounces each)
  • 1 cup blueberries, fresh or frozen
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze (you can make your own or store-bought)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Cooking the Salmon

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil, leaving enough room between each fillet for even cooking.

In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, garlic, and honey. Place the salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet and brush the honey-garlic mixture evenly over each fillet.

The Blueberry Balsamic Glaze

In a small saucepan, combine the blueberries, balsamic glaze, and a pinch of salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the blueberries have broken down and the mixture has thickened into a syrupy glaze, about 10-12 minutes.

Assembling the Dish

Once the blueberry balsamic glaze is ready, brush it evenly over the salmon fillets. You can use a spoon or a brush to get the glaze into all the nooks and crannies of the fish.

Place the baking sheet in the oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.

Serving Suggestions

Once the salmon is cooked, remove it from the oven and sprinkle with chopped parsley, if desired. Serve immediately, garnished with additional blueberries and a drizzle of the blueberry balsamic glaze.

This dish pairs perfectly with a side of roasted vegetables, quinoa, or a simple green salad. You can also serve it with a side of garlic bread or a crusty baguette for a more indulgent meal.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this dish more substantial, add some sautéed spinach or roasted asparagus to the plate.
  • Swap out the blueberries for other berries, such as raspberries or blackberries, for a different flavor profile.
  • If you prefer a lighter glaze, use less balsamic glaze or add a splash of lemon juice to the mixture.
  • Make this dish ahead of time by preparing the blueberry balsamic glaze and cooking the salmon up to a day in advance. Simply reheat the glaze and assemble the dish before serving.

Conclusion

Blueberry balsamic glazed salmon is a summer meal that’s sure to impress. With its sweet and savory flavors, this dish is perfect for a dinner party or a quick weeknight meal. By following this recipe, you’ll be able to create a dish that’s both refreshing and flavorful, with the perfect balance of sweet and savory. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the taste of summer!
