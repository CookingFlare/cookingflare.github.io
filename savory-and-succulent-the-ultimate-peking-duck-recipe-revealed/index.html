Savory and Succulent: The Ultimate Peking Duck Recipe Revealed


If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of Peking Duck. This iconic dish is known for its crispy skin, succulent meat, and flavorful seasoning. Today, I’m going to reveal the ultimate Peking Duck recipe that will leave you craving for more. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 whole duck (around 5-6 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons of Chinese five-spice powder
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 8-10 Mandarin pancakes (store-bought or homemade)
  • Spring onions, thinly sliced
  • Cucumber, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Begin by preparing the duck. Remove the giblets and excess fat from the cavity. Rinse the duck thoroughly with cold water and pat it dry with paper towels.

  3. In a small bowl, combine the Chinese five-spice powder, honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper. Mix well to create a marinade.

  4. Rub the marinade all over the duck, making sure to coat it evenly. Don’t forget to rub some of the marinade inside the cavity as well. Let the duck marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight in the refrigerator for maximum flavor.

  5. After marinating, place the duck on a wire rack set inside a roasting pan. This will allow the fat to render off and the skin to crisp up evenly.

  6. In a small bowl, mix together the baking powder and a little bit of water to create a paste. Brush this paste all over the duck, focusing on the skin. This step will help achieve that crispy texture we all love in Peking Duck.

  7. Place the duck in the preheated oven and roast for about 2 hours, or until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Make sure to turn the duck halfway through cooking to ensure even browning.

  8. While the duck is roasting, prepare the Mandarin pancakes according to the package instructions or make them from scratch if you prefer. These thin, soft pancakes are the perfect vessel for the succulent Peking Duck.

  9. Once the duck is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in tender and juicy meat.

  10. To serve, slice the duck into thin pieces, similar to how you would slice a roasted chicken. Arrange the duck slices on a plate alongside the Mandarin pancakes, spring onions, and cucumber.

  11. To enjoy, simply take a Mandarin pancake, spread a little hoisin sauce on it, add a few slices of duck, and top it off with some spring onions and cucumber. Roll it up and savor the explosion of flavors and textures in every bite.

Conclusion

There you have it, the ultimate Peking Duck recipe that will impress your family and friends. With its crispy skin, succulent meat, and delicious seasoning, this dish is a true crowd-pleaser. So, the next time you’re craving some Chinese delicacy, give this recipe a try and transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Beijing. Enjoy!
