Delicious Sri Lankan Recipes: 7 Dishes for Spice Lovers


Sri Lanka, an island nation steeped in rich cultural heritage, offers a symphony of flavors that dance on the palate. Renowned for its vibrant spices, this cuisine caters to those who love to indulge in bold and aromatic dishes. If you’re a spice lover eager to explore the tantalizing world of Sri Lankan cooking, you’re in for a treat! Here, we present seven must-try recipes that showcase the essence of Sri Lankan cuisine, all bursting with flavor and character.

1. Sri Lankan Chicken Curry

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tablespoons Sri Lankan curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2-3 sprigs curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Stir in garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a few minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and cook until they are no longer pink.
  4. Sprinkle in the curry powder and turmeric, mixing well to coat the chicken.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and add curry leaves. Bring to a simmer.
  6. Season with salt and let it cook for 20-30 minutes until the chicken is tender and the flavors meld.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving. Pair with steamed rice or roti for a delightful meal.

2. Daal Curry (Parippu Curry)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup yellow split peas (moong dal)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the split peas under cold water and boil them in a pot with enough water until soft, about 25-30 minutes.
  2. In a separate pan, heat coconut oil and sauté the onions until translucent.
  3. Add garlic and green chilies, cooking for another few minutes.
  4. Stir in the turmeric, followed by the cooked split peas. Mix well.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and let it simmer for 10 minutes, adjusting the seasoning with salt.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander before serving. This curry pairs beautifully with rice or bread.

3. Kottu Roti

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 roti (flatbreads), chopped into small pieces
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, cabbage, and peas)
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green chilies for heat (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, cooking until fragrant.
  2. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for about 5 minutes until they soften.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the space. Scramble until cooked through.
  4. Add the chopped roti to the pan and mix everything together.
  5. Drizzle with soy sauce and season with salt, pepper, and green chilies if using. Stir-fry for another 5 minutes.
  6. Serve hot as a street-food-inspired meal!

4. Coconut Sambol

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 2-3 red chilies, dried
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a dry pan, lightly toast the dried red chilies until fragrant, then grind them into a fine powder.
  2. In a bowl, combine grated coconut, ground chilies, finely chopped onion, lime juice, and salt.
  3. Mix well, adjusting the lime and salt to taste.
  4. Serve as a condiment with rice and curry dishes for an added kick of flavor.

5. Fish Ambul Thiyal (Sour Fish Curry)

Ingredients:

  • 500g firm white fish (like tuna or snapper), cut into chunks
  • 2 tablespoons goraka (dried Garcinia) or tamarind paste
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1-2 teaspoons chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the fish with turmeric, chili powder, and salt. Let it marinate for 15 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the onions, garlic, and ginger until golden.
  3. Add the marinated fish to the pan, followed by goraka or tamarind paste. Stir to combine.
  4. Cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, gently turning the fish occasionally.
  5. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with rice. This dish’s sour notes and spice make it a standout!

6. Eggplant Moju

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, diced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Curry leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Once they start to splutter, add onion and green chilies, sautéing until the onion is soft.
  2. Add diced eggplants and turmeric, stirring to combine. Cook until the eggplants are tender.
  3. Stir in vinegar, sugar, and salt, cooking for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Garnish with curry leaves if desired and serve as a side dish with rice.

7. Watalappan (Sri Lankan Coconut Pudding)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup jaggery (or brown sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of salt
  • Chopped cashews or raisins (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, jaggery, and salt until well combined.
  3. Gradually mix in the coconut milk and cardamom powder.
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish, adding cashews or raisins if using.
  5. Bake for about 30-40 minutes or until set and slightly golden on top.
  6. Allow to cool before slicing. Serve chilled for a delightful dessert!

Conclusion

Exploring Sri Lankan cuisine is a journey into a world of vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and comforting dishes. From rich curries to refreshing sambols and sweet desserts, each recipe offers a unique taste of the island’s culinary heritage. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out, these seven dishes will surely delight your taste buds and satisfy your craving for spice. So gather your ingredients, embrace the heat, and enjoy creating these delicious Sri Lankan recipes in your kitchen!
