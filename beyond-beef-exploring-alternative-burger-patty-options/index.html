Beyond Beef: Exploring Alternative Burger Patty Options


When it comes to grilling and enjoying a good old-fashioned burger, many of us are on the lookout for healthier, more sustainable alternatives to traditional beef patties. That’s where Beyond Beef comes in. This plant-based ground “meat” has taken the culinary world by storm, offering a delicious and versatile option for those looking to reduce their meat consumption or explore new flavors. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the world of Beyond Beef and explore some creative and mouthwatering recipes for alternative burger patties that will tantalize your taste buds.

The Rise of Beyond Beef

Beyond Beef is a plant-based ground “meat” made from a blend of pea protein, rice protein, mung bean protein, and other natural ingredients. It has gained immense popularity for its ability to mimic the taste, texture, and juiciness of traditional beef, making it an excellent choice for vegans, vegetarians, and anyone looking to cut back on their meat intake. Beyond Beef is also celebrated for being a more sustainable and environmentally friendly option, as it requires fewer resources to produce compared to beef.

Recipe 1: Spicy Black Bean and Beyond Beef Patty

Ingredients:

  • 1 package Beyond Beef
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your choice of toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mash the black beans with a fork or potato masher until mostly smooth.
  2. Add the Beyond Beef, breadcrumbs, red onion, garlic, cumin, paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the bowl.
  3. Mix all the ingredients until well combined.
  4. Divide the mixture into equal portions and form them into burger patties.
  5. Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat and cook the patties for 4-5 minutes per side, or until browned and heated through.
  6. Serve the spicy black bean and Beyond Beef patties on burger buns with your favorite toppings.

This recipe offers a delightful combination of the smoky flavors of black beans and the savory goodness of Beyond Beef, creating a patty that’s bursting with texture and spice.

Recipe 2: Mediterranean Chickpea and Beyond Beef Patty

Ingredients:

  • 1 package Beyond Beef
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your choice of toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, pulse the chickpeas until finely chopped.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the chopped chickpeas, Beyond Beef, panko breadcrumbs, parsley, red bell pepper, tahini, garlic, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Mix the ingredients until well incorporated.
  4. Form the mixture into burger patties of your desired size.
  5. Heat a grill or skillet over medium heat and cook the patties for 4-5 minutes per side, or until golden and cooked through.
  6. Serve the Mediterranean chickpea and Beyond Beef patties on burger buns with your preferred toppings.

This recipe showcases the delightful marriage of Mediterranean flavors with the succulence of Beyond Beef, resulting in a patty that’s both aromatic and satisfying.

Recipe 3: Umami Portobello and Beyond Beef Patty

Ingredients:

  • 1 package Beyond Beef
  • 4 large portobello mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Burger buns and your choice of toppings

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, whisk together the soy sauce, balsamic vinegar, garlic, and olive oil.
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in the marinade, turning to coat evenly. Let them marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill for 3-4 minutes per side, or until tender.
  5. While the mushrooms are grilling, shape the Beyond Beef into patties of similar size to the mushrooms.
  6. Cook the Beyond Beef patties for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are nicely seared and heated through.
