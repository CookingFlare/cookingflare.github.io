Pro Protest: How to Protest Kitchen Anxiety and Master That Iconic Pizzeria Pizza


Do you feel intimidated when stepping into the kitchen, particularly when it comes to par-baking pizza dough or tossing pizza in the air with finesse? I understand. Kitchen anxiety can be overwhelming, and the pursuit of perfection can seem like an uphill battle. Fear not, for we’ve woven together a Pro-Test to guide you through creating your own iconic pizzeria-style pizza. Let’s wipe away kitchen anxiety and conquer that dream-like pizzeria pizza, one slice at a time.

1. Embrace Procrastination: Plan Before You Start

Rather than diving in headfirst, take a moment to plan and collect recipes, learn about the ingredients, and gather tools. This careful preparation will grant you the confidence to confidently claim your very own pizza-making domain.

Ingredients You’ll Need:

  • Yeast
  • Flour
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Olive oil
  • Tomato sauce
  • Toppings of your choice
  • Grated mozzarella cheese

Tools You’ll Need:

  • Stand mixer or hand mixer
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Pizza peel or a large flat plate
  • Baking stone
  • Parchment paper
  • Oven mitts
  • Rubber spatula

2. Dough Your Best: Making restaurant-quality pizza dough

Ah, the cornerstone of any authentic pizzeria pizza: the dough. Begin with a yeasted dough that achieves that perfect mixture of crispiness and chewiness. Here’s a simple recipe you can follow:

For the Yeasted Pizza Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 1/2 cup lukewarm water, 5 grams of active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Let the mixture sit for 5 minutes or until it becomes frothy.

  2. Add 480 grams of all-purpose flour and 9 grams of salt to the yeast mixture and blend until everything is well-incorporated.

  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes. Gradually add 30 milliliters of olive oil into the mix while kneading.

  4. Create a round ball with the dough and place it in a large, oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean towel and sit it in a warm spot for approximately 2 hours – this allows your dough to rise and creates a perfectly puffed, airy crust.

  5. Once the dough has risen, carefully punch it down and divide it into two even portions. You can either use them right away for two small pizzas or save one in the fridge for later.

  6. Prepare for shaping by lightly flouring your work surface and hands. This assists in preventing your dough from sticking and helps make that classic pizzeria pizza shape.

3. Stretching the Dough with Confidence

A crucial aspect of pizza-making is giving your dough that perfect shape. Tossing or spinning the dough can be intimidating, don’t worry, it takes practice. Here is an alternative method for you:

  1. Start by stretching and pressing the dough into a circle using your hands.

  2. Treat this like a blanket and carefully slide your hands under the crust, stretching and thinning it as you go.

  3. If you want leeway from the usual tossing procedures or you’re still developing that skill, you can also use a rolling pin to create a uniform thickness.

  4. While giving your dough that signature pizzeria pizza shape, always remember to let it rest for about twenty minutes before baking. This rest period will relax the gluten and make your crust an absolute delight.

4. Bake Like a Pro

Par-baking your crust before adding toppings creates a delicious, crispy pizza crust. Here’s how:

  1. Preheat your oven to 260°C / 500°F with a baking stone inside.

  2. Stretch your dough to your desired thickness, then transfer it onto a pizza peel or a large flat plate.

  3. Lightly brush your crust with the remaining olive oil and brush a thin layer of tomato sauce evenly around the crust.

  4. Now, generously sprinkle your cheese and add desired toppings. Be mindful not to overstuff or over-crowd your pizza.

  5. Carefully slide your masterpiece onto the preheated baking stone in the oven. Bake for approximately 8-10 minutes until your crust develops a delicious, golden-brown color.

  6. Remove your pizza from the oven with oven mitts and set it aside. The next step ensures the perfect crust: broil your pizza! Move the rack to the highest shelf of your oven and set the broiler on high. Keep a close watch and rotate the pizza if necessary. Broil for 1-2 minutes or until the cheese bubbles and the crust color deepens.

  7. Remove your pizzeria-worthy creation from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Slice and serve hot.

And there you have it! How to embrace your Pro-Protest and conquer kitchen anxiety to make your very own iconic pizzeria-style pizza that rivals the best pizzerias out there.
