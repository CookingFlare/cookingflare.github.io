Christmas Recipes for the Busy Cook: Fast and Fabulous


As the holiday season approaches, the hustle and bustle of Christmas preparations can feel overwhelming. Between shopping for gifts, decorating the house, and attending festive gatherings, finding the time to plan and prepare delicious meals can be a challenge. Fear not! This guide is packed with quick and fabulous Christmas recipes that busy cooks can whip up without compromising on flavor or presentation. Let’s dive into some delightful dishes that will impress your family and friends while saving you time in the kitchen.

Starters and Appetizers

1. Cranberry Brie Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry (thawed)
  • 1 wheel of Brie cheese
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh rosemary for garnish
  • Egg wash (1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon water)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and cut it into squares (about 3x3 inches).
  3. Place the puff pastry squares on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Cut the Brie into small pieces and place one piece in the center of each square.
  5. Top the cheese with a teaspoon of cranberry sauce.
  6. Fold the corners of the pastry over the filling, pinching to seal.
  7. Brush the tops with egg wash for a golden finish.
  8. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the pastry is puffed and golden. Garnish with rosemary before serving.

2. Stuffed Mini Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 12 mini sweet peppers
  • 1 cup cream cheese (softened)
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup cooked bacon bits (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Slice the mini peppers in half lengthwise and remove the seeds.
  3. In a bowl, mix the softened cream cheese, cheddar cheese, bacon bits, and garlic powder until well combined.
  4. Spoon the cheese mixture into each pepper half.
  5. Arrange the stuffed peppers on a baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

Main Courses

3. Honey Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked ham (about 5-7 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Place the ham in a roasting pan and score the surface in a diamond pattern.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine honey, brown sugar, ground mustard, cloves, and cinnamon. Heat over medium until sugar dissolves.
  4. Brush the glaze over the ham, ensuring it gets into the scored areas.
  5. Bake uncovered for about 1.5 to 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the remaining glaze.
  6. Let the ham rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.

4. One-Pan Garlic Butter Shrimp and Asparagus

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 bunch asparagus (trimmed)
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 lemon (juiced)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp and asparagus to the skillet. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until the shrimp are pink and opaque.
  5. Squeeze lemon juice over the shrimp and asparagus, and toss to coat.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Side Dishes

5. Maple Roasted Brussels Sprouts

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts (halved)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup toasted pecans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the sprouts on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes until golden brown and tender, stirring halfway through.
  5. If desired, sprinkle toasted pecans over the top before serving.

6. Creamy Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in a large pot of salted water until tender (about 15-20 minutes).
  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  3. Add heavy cream, butter, salt, and pepper. Mash until smooth and creamy.
  4. Adjust seasoning to taste and garnish with fresh chives.

Desserts

7. Chocolate Peppermint Bark

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate (chopped)
  • 12 ounces white chocolate (chopped)
  • 1/2 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/2 cup crushed candy canes

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Melt dark chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second intervals until smooth.
  3. Pour the melted dark chocolate onto the prepared baking sheet and spread evenly.
  4. Melt white chocolate in the same manner, then stir in peppermint extract.
  5. Pour the white chocolate over the dark chocolate layer and swirl with a knife for a marbled effect.
  6. Sprinkle crushed candy canes on top before the chocolate sets.
  7. Refrigerate for 1 hour until firm, then break into pieces.

8. Eggnog Cheesecake

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup butter (melted)
  • 3 (8-ounce) packages cream cheese (softened)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup eggnog
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan.
  3. In a large bowl, beat cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Pour in eggnog, nutmeg, and vanilla extract. Mix until combined.
  5. Pour the cheesecake filling over the crust and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 55-60 minutes or until the center is set. Let cool before refrigerating for at least 4 hours or overnight.

Final Thoughts

This Christmas, embrace the joy of cooking with these quick and fabulous recipes designed for busy cooks. Each dish is simple to prepare yet stunning enough to make your holiday celebrations memorable. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or bringing a dish to share, these recipes will help you create a festive feast without the stress. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the holiday cooking begin! Happy cooking and merry Christmas!
