Som Tam (Papaya Salad) Recipe with Shrimp and Peanuts


If you’re looking for a refreshing, flavorful, and healthy dish to spice up your meals, look no further than Som Tam, a traditional Thai papaya salad. This vibrant dish is a perfect blend of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors, and when combined with succulent shrimp and crunchy peanuts, it becomes a delightful explosion of taste and texture. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to create this tantalizing Som Tam with shrimp and peanuts that will surely impress your taste buds and those of your loved ones.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 10-12 medium-sized shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, coarsely chopped
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons palm sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup long beans, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup carrot, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons dried shrimp (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Shrimp: In a pot of boiling water, cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. Drain and set aside to cool.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, mix the fish sauce, tamarind paste, palm sugar, lime juice, garlic, and Thai bird’s eye chilies. Stir well until the sugar dissolves.

  3. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the shredded papaya, cherry tomatoes, long beans, julienned carrots, and dried shrimp. Toss gently to mix.

  4. Assemble the Salad: Add the cooked shrimp to the mixing bowl and pour the prepared dressing over the ingredients. Toss the salad thoroughly to ensure the dressing coats all the ingredients evenly.

  5. Serve: Transfer the Som Tam to a serving platter and sprinkle the coarsely chopped peanuts over the top.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, simply omit the shrimp and add extra roasted peanuts or fried tofu for protein.
  • Adjust the amount of Thai bird’s eye chilies according to your heat preference. Be cautious as they can be very spicy!
  • If green papaya is not available, you can substitute with green mango for a slightly different but equally delicious variation.
  • Feel free to customize the salad with additional ingredients such as grated coconut, green beans, or even grilled chicken for a heartier meal.

Final Thoughts

Som Tam with shrimp and peanuts is a delightful dish that marries bold flavors with a satisfying crunch. The combination of the sweet and tangy dressing with the fresh papaya, succulent shrimp, and crunchy peanuts creates a harmonious symphony of tastes and textures that will leave you craving for more. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, looking for a light lunch, or simply want to explore the vibrant world of Thai cuisine, this recipe is a must-try. So, roll up your sleeves, get your ingredients ready, and embark on a culinary adventure with this tantalizing Som Tam creation!
