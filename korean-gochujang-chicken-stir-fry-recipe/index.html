Korean Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re looking to spice up your weeknight dinners with a dish that’s bold, flavorful, and easy to make, look no further than this Korean Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry. This dish is a perfect fusion of savory, sweet, and spicy flavors, all coming together in a quick and simple stir-fry. The key ingredient here is Gochujang, a traditional Korean chili paste that packs a punch and brings a unique umami flavor that enhances the chicken, vegetables, and sauce.

Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or just want to try something new, this recipe is sure to satisfy your taste buds. With its perfect balance of heat and sweetness, the Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry will become a new favorite in your dinner rotation.

Ingredients

Before diving into the preparation, let’s gather all the ingredients you need for this delicious stir-fry. The beauty of this recipe lies in how easy it is to make with simple ingredients you can find at most grocery stores.

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g (1 lb) boneless, skinless chicken thighs – Chicken thighs are ideal for stir-fries because they stay juicy and tender. You can substitute with chicken breasts if preferred.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce – This adds saltiness and depth to the dish.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar – A touch of acidity to balance the richness.
  • 1 tablespoon Gochujang (Korean chili paste) – This is the heart of the dish, providing that spicy, umami flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil – Adds a subtle nutty flavor to the marinade.
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar – For a hint of sweetness to complement the spice.

For the Stir-Fry Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon Gochujang – Adds the signature spicy kick.
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce – For that salty umami flavor.
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar – A perfect balance of tartness.
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce – Adds a deep umami richness.
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil – For extra flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon water or chicken broth – To thin out the sauce to your desired consistency.

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil – For cooking the chicken and vegetables.
  • 1 onion, sliced thinly – Adds sweetness and crunch.
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced into strips – A colorful addition for flavor and texture.
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons – Adds a mild, crisp texture.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced – A key aromatic to deepen the flavor.
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated – For a subtle zing.
  • 2 spring onions (green onions), sliced – For garnish and freshness.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds – For garnish and an added crunch.

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Start by marinating the chicken to infuse it with all those delicious Korean flavors. In a mixing bowl, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, Gochujang, sesame oil, and honey or brown sugar. Stir until everything is well mixed, and the sugar is dissolved.

Cut the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces. Add the chicken to the marinade and mix until every piece is coated evenly. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes. If you have time, marinate it for up to 2 hours in the refrigerator to allow the flavors to develop even further.

Step 2: Prepare the Stir-Fry Sauce

While the chicken is marinating, it’s time to prepare the stir-fry sauce. In a separate bowl, whisk together the Gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and water or chicken broth. Mix well until smooth and set aside.

This sauce will add an irresistible depth of flavor to the stir-fry, so don’t skip this step!

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the pan is hot, add the vegetable oil. Swirl the oil around to coat the bottom of the pan.

Add the sliced onion and bell pepper to the pan. Stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until the vegetables begin to soften. Then, add the zucchini, garlic, and ginger. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, ensuring that the vegetables remain crisp-tender and not overcooked. Transfer the vegetables to a plate and set them aside.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken

In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed, and toss in the marinated chicken. Let the chicken cook undisturbed for a minute or two so that it can develop a crispy, golden-brown exterior. Then, stir-fry the chicken for about 5-7 minutes until it is cooked through and browned on all sides. If necessary, work in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.

Step 5: Combine Chicken and Vegetables

Once the chicken is fully cooked, return the stir-fried vegetables to the pan. Pour in the prepared stir-fry sauce and toss everything together, making sure that the chicken and vegetables are coated evenly with the sauce. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly and become glossy.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Remove the pan from the heat. Transfer the Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry to a serving platter. Garnish with sliced spring onions and sesame seeds for an extra pop of flavor and texture. Serve immediately with steamed rice or noodles.

Tips for the Perfect Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry

  • Adjust the spice level: Gochujang can vary in heat. If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the amount of Gochujang in both the marinade and the sauce. Conversely, add more if you like it spicier!
  • Add more veggies: Feel free to customize the vegetables based on what’s in season or what you have in your fridge. Mushrooms, broccoli, and carrots are excellent additions.
  • Use chicken breast for a leaner option: While chicken thighs are ideal for stir-fries due to their juiciness, you can easily substitute with chicken breasts if you prefer a leaner meat.
  • Make it gluten-free: Swap out soy sauce for tamari or coconut aminos for a gluten-free alternative.
  • Meal prep friendly: This recipe makes for great leftovers. You can store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.

Why You’ll Love This Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry

This Korean Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry is a fantastic dish that brings together the best of Korean flavors. The combination of savory Gochujang, sweet honey, and the nutty richness of sesame oil creates a harmonious balance that’s hard to beat.

Not only does this dish have an incredible taste, but it’s also easy to prepare, making it a perfect option for busy weeknights. With just a few simple steps and ingredients, you’ll have a restaurant-quality meal on the table in no time.

Moreover, this recipe is highly adaptable. Whether you want to add more vegetables, adjust the heat, or serve it over a bed of noodles or rice, this stir-fry can be customized to suit your personal preferences. It’s versatile, delicious, and guaranteed to become a go-to dish in your cooking repertoire.

Gochujang Chicken Stir-Fry is the ultimate representation of Korean flavors – spicy, savory, and slightly sweet. By using Gochujang, a fermented chili paste that’s rich in umami, this recipe offers a depth of flavor that’s hard to replicate with traditional stir-fry sauces.

The addition of vegetables not only enhances the dish in terms of flavor but also provides added nutrition and texture. The crisp, fresh vegetables contrast beautifully with the tender, juicy chicken, making every bite satisfying and balanced.
