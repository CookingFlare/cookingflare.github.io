Instant Pot Shrimp and Grits: A Comforting Southern Recipe


Shrimp and grits is a dish steeped in Southern tradition, a timeless comfort food that blends rich flavors with satisfying textures. With the Instant Pot, preparing this iconic combination becomes faster and easier while still delivering the authentic flavors we know and love. Whether you’re looking for an elevated weeknight dinner or a dish to impress guests, Instant Pot shrimp and grits hit the mark every time.

The Story Behind Shrimp and Grits

Shrimp and grits originated in coastal Southern regions, marrying fresh seafood with ground hominy grits, a staple of Native American cuisine. Originally a breakfast dish for fishermen, this meal evolved over time, gaining popularity in restaurants and homes alike. Today, the dish epitomizes Southern comfort food and makes a versatile canvas for creative flavors, while still honoring its humble roots.

Why Use the Instant Pot?

Traditional shrimp and grits require careful attention when cooking the grits for the perfect creamy texture, balancing the flavors of the shrimp, spices, and broth. With the Instant Pot, you can streamline the process and save time without sacrificing flavor or consistency. The pressure cooking function ensures that the grits cook to perfection every time, eliminating the guesswork while amplifying the flavors of the shrimp and seasonings.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For this Instant Pot shrimp and grits recipe, you’ll gather both classic and bold ingredients designed to deliver layers of flavor. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Grits:

  • 1 cup stone-ground grits
  • 2 ½ cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian option)
  • ½ cup heavy cream (optional for creamier grits)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

For the Shrimp:

  • 1 pound large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 bell pepper (diced; choose any color you prefer)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon Cajun seasoning
  • ½ cup andouille sausage, diced (optional for added heat and flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley (chopped for garnish)
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these detailed steps to create Instant Pot shrimp and grits that will wow your taste buds. This recipe is designed to be foolproof, so even if you’re a beginner in the kitchen, you can whip up an impressive meal in no time.

Step 1: Preparing the Grits

  1. Add Ingredients to the Instant Pot: Start by placing the stone-ground grits, chicken broth, butter, salt, and black pepper in the Instant Pot. Stir to combine.
  2. Pressure Cook: Seal the lid and set the Instant Pot to “Pressure Cook” mode on high for 10 minutes. Ensure the valve is set to sealing.
  3. Natural Release: After the cooking is complete, let the pressure release naturally for about 5 minutes, then carefully release any remaining pressure manually. Remove the lid.
  4. Enhance Creaminess: Stir in the heavy cream (if using), cheddar cheese, and additional butter while the grits are still warm. Mix until the cheese has melted completely and the grits are creamy. Set aside and keep warm.

Step 2: Cooking the Shrimp

  1. Preheat the Instant Pot: Wipe out the inner pot, then set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” mode. Heat the olive oil.
  2. Sauté Aromatics and Vegetables: Add the chopped onion, garlic, and diced bell pepper. Cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and fragrant. If you’re incorporating andouille sausage, add it now and sauté until browned.
  3. Season the Shrimp: Sprinkle the shrimp with smoked paprika and Cajun seasoning, tossing to coat evenly.
  4. Sear the Shrimp: Add the shrimp to the pot and sauté for 2-3 minutes, just until they turn pink and are cooked through. Be cautious not to overcook, as shrimp can become rubbery.
  5. Finish with Lemon: Squeeze the juice of half a lemon over the shrimp and stir.

Step 3: Assemble the Dish

  1. Plate the Grits: Spoon the warm, creamy grits onto individual plates or shallow bowls.
  2. Top with Shrimp Mixture: Ladle the sautéed shrimp and vegetable mixture over the grits, allowing the juices to soak into the grits for added flavor.
  3. Garnish: Sprinkle chopped parsley on top for a fresh and vibrant finish. For extra flair, consider adding a dash of hot sauce or a sprinkle of green onions.

Tips for Perfect Shrimp and Grits in the Instant Pot

  • Use Stone-Ground Grits: While quick grits can save time, stone-ground grits offer a richer texture and deeper flavor that pairs perfectly with shrimp.
  • Adjust Spices: The Cajun seasoning provides a kick that can be adjusted to suit your taste. Add more or less depending on your spice preference, or opt for Old Bay seasoning for a classic seafood flavor.
  • Fresh or Frozen Shrimp: Either will work, but fresh shrimp tend to offer better texture and flavor. If using frozen shrimp, thaw them completely before cooking.
  • Cheese Variation: Cheddar cheese is classic, but feel free to experiment with other cheeses such as Parmesan or Gouda for a unique spin.

Why This Recipe Will Become a Staple

Shrimp and grits combine the comforting heartiness of creamy grits with the bright and savory appeal of succulent shrimp. This Instant Pot version simplifies the process, enabling you to enjoy this restaurant-quality meal at home with minimal effort. The combination of robust spices, cheesy grits, and tender shrimp makes this recipe a standout for casual family dinners or special occasions alike.

Pairing Suggestions

To round out your meal, here are a few pairing options:

  • Side Dish: Steamed collard greens or roasted asparagus add a pop of color and balance the richness of the shrimp and grits.
  • Beverage: A chilled glass of Sauvignon Blanc or a refreshing lemonade complements the bold flavors in the dish.
  • Dessert: For a truly Southern experience, a slice of pecan pie or banana pudding wraps up the meal on a sweet note.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (though it’s hard to believe this dish won’t be devoured immediately), store the grits and shrimp separately for best results. Keep them in airtight containers and refrigerate for up to three days. When reheating, add a splash of broth or water to the grits to restore their creamy consistency, and warm gently on the stove or microwave. Shrimp should be reheated in a pan over low heat just until warmed through; overcooking during reheating can make them rubbery.

Shrimp and grits may have originated as humble fare, but this Instant Pot rendition brings it firmly into contemporary kitchens with ease and elegance. If you’ve been searching for a dish that’s flavorful, comforting, and delightfully simple to prepare, this recipe delivers all that and more. Celebrate a taste of the South with this modern yet soulful approach to shrimp and grits—you’ll find yourself coming back for seconds (and thirds).
