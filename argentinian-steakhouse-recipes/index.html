Argentinian Steakhouse Recipes


If you’re a meat lover, then you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’re going to explore the mouthwatering world of Argentinian steakhouse recipes. Argentina is famous for its delicious beef, and their steakhouse culture is second to none. So, let’s fire up the grill and get cooking!

Chimichurri Sauce

No Argentinian steakhouse experience is complete without the famous chimichurri sauce. This vibrant and flavorsome condiment is the perfect accompaniment to grilled meats. Here’s how you can make it at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the parsley, garlic, red wine vinegar, and dried oregano.
  2. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking continuously to emulsify the sauce.
  3. Add the red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  4. Let the chimichurri sauce sit for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

Asado (Grilled Beef Ribs)

Asado is the quintessential Argentinian barbecue dish. It’s all about slow-cooking beef ribs over an open flame, resulting in tender and juicy meat with a smoky flavor. Here’s how you can make your own mouthwatering asado:

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 pounds beef ribs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Start by preparing your grill for indirect grilling. You want a medium-low heat for slow-cooking the ribs.
  2. Season the beef ribs generously with salt and pepper, making sure to coat all sides.
  3. Place the ribs on the grill, bone-side down, away from direct heat. Close the grill lid and let the ribs cook for about 2-3 hours, flipping occasionally.
  4. Test the doneness by inserting a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the meat. The internal temperature should reach around 145°F for medium-rare or 160°F for medium.
  5. Once cooked to your desired doneness, remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

Provoleta (Grilled Provolone Cheese)

Provoleta is a popular appetizer in Argentinian steakhouses. It’s a simple yet incredibly delicious dish that features grilled provolone cheese topped with a sprinkle of oregano. Here’s how you can make it:

Ingredients:

  • 1 provolone cheese wheel, about 1 inch thick
  • Dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Slice the provolone cheese wheel into individual portions, about 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Place the cheese slices directly on the grill grates and let them cook for about 2 minutes on each side, or until they develop a golden crust.
  4. Sprinkle the grilled provolone slices with dried oregano while they’re still hot.
  5. Serve immediately with warm bread or as a side dish to your grilled meats.

Conclusion

Argentinian steakhouse recipes are all about celebrating the incredible flavors of beef. From the tangy chimichurri sauce to the melt-in-your-mouth asado and the indulgent provoleta, these recipes will transport you to the vibrant culinary world of Argentina. So, grab your apron, fire up the grill, and get ready to impress your friends and family with these authentic creations. ¡Buen provecho!
