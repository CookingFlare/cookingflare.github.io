Kitsune Udon: A Classic Japanese Noodle Dish


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, then you’ve likely come across the delicious and comforting dish known as Kitsune Udon. This classic Japanese noodle dish features thick udon noodles in a flavorful broth, topped with a slice of fried tofu. Kitsune Udon is not only a popular choice among noodle lovers, but it also holds a special place in Japanese folklore.

The Legend of Kitsune

Before we delve into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the folklore behind the name “Kitsune.” In Japanese mythology, Kitsune refers to a fox with magical abilities. According to legend, these mischievous creatures have the power to shape-shift into human form. They are often depicted as intelligent and cunning, known for their love of indulgent pleasures, including fine food.

The Ingredients

To recreate this traditional dish at home, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 250g udon noodles
  • 2 cups dashi (Japanese fish stock)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 piece of aburaage (fried tofu)
  • Green onions, thinly sliced, for garnish

The Recipe

  1. Start by preparing the udon noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and set aside.

  2. In a medium-sized pot, combine the dashi, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. While the broth simmers, prepare the aburaage by cutting it into thin slices.

  4. In a separate pan, heat a small amount of oil over medium-high heat. Add the aburaage slices and fry them until they turn golden brown and crispy on both sides.

  5. Once the aburaage is fried, transfer it to a paper towel-lined plate to remove any excess oil.

  6. Now, it’s time to assemble the dish. Divide the cooked udon noodles into individual serving bowls. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles, ensuring they are fully submerged.

  7. Place a slice of fried aburaage on top of each bowl of udon, allowing it to float gracefully on the surface.

  8. Garnish the Kitsune Udon with thinly sliced green onions to add a touch of freshness and color.

  9. Serve the dish immediately, allowing the flavors to meld together as you savor the warm and comforting combination of noodles, broth, and fried tofu.

The Magic of Kitsune Udon

Kitsune Udon is a dish that embodies the essence of Japanese comfort food. The thick, chewy udon noodles soak up the savory broth, while the fried tofu adds a delightful crunch to each bite. The combination of flavors and textures is truly magical, making this dish a favorite among both young and old.

Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or someone looking to explore the world of Japanese cuisine, Kitsune Udon is a dish that should not be missed. So gather your ingredients, embrace the legend of the mischievous Kitsune, and embark on a culinary adventure that will transport you to the heart of Japan.

Remember, cooking is not just about following a recipe; it’s about infusing your own personal touch and passion into every dish you create. So go ahead, channel your inner Kitsune, and let the magic unfold in your kitchen as you prepare this classic Japanese noodle dish. Enjoy!
