How to Make a Pizza with Fresh Mozzarella and Tomatoes


Pizza is one of the most beloved comfort foods around the world, enjoyed by people of all ages and backgrounds. Whether you’re indulging in a classic slice at your local pizzeria or making it at home, there’s something undeniably satisfying about a well-made pizza. One of the best variations to try is the pizza with fresh mozzarella and tomatoes. This simple, yet flavorful, combination is perfect for those who appreciate the taste of fresh ingredients that come together in harmony.

Making a pizza with fresh mozzarella and tomatoes at home can be a rewarding and enjoyable experience. The key to success lies in using quality ingredients and mastering a few essential techniques that will elevate your pizza to the next level. In this blog post, we will guide you through every step of the process, from preparing the dough to adding the perfect toppings and baking the pizza to perfection.

Ingredients for the Perfect Pizza

The foundation of any great pizza lies in the ingredients you use. For a pizza with fresh mozzarella and tomatoes, you’ll need the following items:

For the Dough:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 ¼ teaspoons)
  • 1 cup warm water (around 110°F or 43°C)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the Sauce:

  • 1 ½ cups crushed tomatoes (preferably San Marzano)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • A pinch of salt and pepper

For the Toppings:

  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, preferably buffalo mozzarella
  • 1 large tomato, sliced thinly
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • A drizzle of olive oil
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • Optional: A sprinkle of red pepper flakes for some heat

Preparing the Dough

Step 1: Activating the Yeast

The first step in making pizza dough is activating the yeast. Begin by adding the warm water to a bowl. Sprinkle the sugar over the water and stir until dissolved. Then, add the active dry yeast to the water, stirring gently to combine. Let the mixture sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy and bubbly. This shows that the yeast is active and ready to go.

Step 2: Mixing the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Once the yeast has activated, pour it into the flour mixture along with the olive oil. Stir with a spoon until a dough begins to form. Once the dough is somewhat combined, transfer it to a floured surface and begin kneading.

Step 3: Kneading the Dough

Kneading is a crucial step for developing the gluten in the dough, which will give your pizza its chewy, airy texture. Use the heel of your hands to push the dough forward, then fold it over and rotate. Continue this process for about 8-10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, add a bit more flour; if it’s too dry, add a splash of water.

Step 4: Letting the Dough Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. During this time, the dough will develop flavor and texture, so don’t rush this important step.

Preparing the Sauce

Step 1: Sautéing the Garlic

While the dough is rising, it’s the perfect time to prepare the pizza sauce. Begin by heating the olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1-2 minutes, just until it becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it will turn bitter.

Step 2: Cooking the Sauce

Once the garlic is fragrant, add the crushed tomatoes to the saucepan. Stir in the dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can blend it with an immersion blender to achieve a finer texture.

Step 3: Letting the Sauce Cool

Once your sauce is ready, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. It’s important not to use the sauce while it’s still boiling hot, as this could affect the texture of your dough.

Preparing the Toppings

Step 1: Slicing the Fresh Mozzarella

Fresh mozzarella is a key component of this pizza, contributing a creamy and mild flavor that perfectly complements the tomatoes. To prepare the mozzarella, drain it from any excess liquid and slice it into thick rounds. You can either use a serrated knife or your hands to tear the cheese into pieces, which can add a rustic feel to your pizza.

Step 2: Slicing the Tomatoes

Tomatoes are essential for this pizza, and using the right variety makes all the difference. Opt for fresh, ripe tomatoes—Roma or heirloom varieties work wonderfully. Slice the tomatoes thinly, aiming for about ¼-inch slices. If the tomatoes are particularly juicy, you can place them on a paper towel to absorb some of the excess moisture.

Step 3: Preparing Fresh Basil

Fresh basil is the finishing touch for this pizza, adding aromatic and slightly sweet notes. Pluck a few basil leaves from the stem, and set them aside. If desired, you can tear them into smaller pieces right before serving to avoid them wilting during baking.

Assembling the Pizza

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Before you start assembling your pizza, make sure your oven is preheated to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, now is the time to place it in the oven to heat up. A pizza stone helps create a crispier crust, but a baking sheet will work just fine if you don’t have one.

Step 2: Rolling Out the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer it to a floured surface and use a rolling pin to roll it out into a thin, round shape. Aim for about 12 inches in diameter. If you prefer a thicker crust, you can leave the dough slightly thicker.

Step 3: Adding the Sauce

Spread a generous amount of your homemade tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust. Be careful not to overload the pizza with sauce, as this can make the crust soggy. A thin layer is all you need to get a balanced flavor.

Step 4: Adding the Mozzarella

Place the slices of fresh mozzarella evenly over the sauce, ensuring that each slice of pizza will get a generous helping of cheese. If you’re using torn pieces, scatter them in a way that provides coverage across the entire pizza.

Step 5: Adding the Tomato Slices

Arrange the sliced tomatoes on top of the mozzarella. Depending on your preference, you can overlap them slightly or leave some space between each slice. The tomatoes will release some moisture as the pizza bakes, so don’t worry if they look a little juicier than usual.

Step 6: Baking the Pizza

Carefully transfer the assembled pizza to the preheated oven. If you’re using a pizza stone, a pizza peel will help slide the pizza onto the stone. Bake the pizza for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbling. Keep a close eye on the pizza during the last few minutes of baking to ensure it doesn’t burn.

Finishing Touches

Step 1: Adding Fresh Basil

Once the pizza is out of the oven, scatter fresh basil leaves over the top. The heat from the pizza will release the oils in the basil, creating a fragrant aroma that will elevate the dish. You can either leave the basil whole or tear it into smaller pieces for a more rustic feel.

Step 2: Drizzling Olive Oil

For an added layer of richness, drizzle a little extra virgin olive oil over the pizza. This will enhance the flavors of the fresh mozzarella and tomatoes, giving the pizza a luxurious finish.

Step 3: Seasoning with Salt

Finally, sprinkle a pinch of sea salt over the pizza to bring out the natural flavors of the ingredients. If you enjoy a little heat, you can also sprinkle some red pepper flakes over the top.

Serving the Pizza

Once your pizza is complete, it’s time to slice and enjoy! Use a sharp pizza cutter to slice the pizza into wedges, and serve immediately while the crust is still crispy and the cheese is melty. This pizza pairs wonderfully with a crisp glass of white wine, such as Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc, or a refreshing iced tea.

Tips for the Perfect Pizza

  1. Use High-Quality Ingredients: The flavor of your pizza will be directly impacted by the quality of the ingredients. Choose fresh mozzarella, ripe tomatoes, and high-quality olive oil for the best results.
  2. Don’t Overload the Pizza: It’s easy to go overboard with toppings, but too many ingredients can make the pizza soggy. Less is more when it comes to a classic pizza like this.
  3. Preheat Your Oven: A hot oven is key to achieving a crispy crust. Make sure your oven is preheated to the right temperature before baking the pizza.
  4. Experiment with Variations: While fresh mozzarella and tomatoes are a classic combination, feel free to experiment with different toppings. Arugula, prosciutto, or a drizzle of balsamic glaze can add a new dimension to your pizza.

Conclusion

Making a pizza with fresh mozzarella and tomatoes is a rewarding experience that brings together simple, fresh ingredients for a delicious meal. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced home cook, this recipe is easy to follow and yields impressive results. The combination of the creamy mozzarella, juicy tomatoes, and fragrant basil creates a pizza that’s bursting with flavor, making it a perfect dish for family gatherings, casual dinners, or special occasions.

So, next time you’re in the mood for pizza, skip the takeout and try your hand at making this homemade masterpiece. With a little patience and care, you’ll be able to enjoy the authentic taste of pizza straight from your oven. Happy cooking!
