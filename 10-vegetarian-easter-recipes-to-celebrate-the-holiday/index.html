10 Vegetarian Easter Recipes to Celebrate the Holiday


Easter is a time of renewal, celebration, and gathering with loved ones. Whether you’re hosting a festive brunch, a lavish lunch, or an intimate dinner, serving delicious and creative vegetarian dishes can elevate your holiday menu and delight everyone at the table. With spring’s vibrant ingredients bursting at their freshest, this collection of ten vegetarian Easter recipes promises to deliver color, flavor, and seasonal freshness.

Let’s dive into these inspired, meat-free creations that honor the spirit of Easter while showcasing the best of spring vegetables, herbs, and comforting textures.

1. Spring Vegetable and Herb Frittata

A frittata bursts with springtime freshness while offering a satisfying, fluffy texture. This recipe embraces tender asparagus, sweet peas, and fresh herbs like dill and chives, folded into rich eggs and aged parmesan.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup fresh peas (or thawed frozen peas)
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives
  • 1/4 cup chopped dill
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Heat olive oil in an ovenproof skillet over medium heat.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic until soft and translucent. Add asparagus and peas, cooking until just tender.
  3. Whisk eggs in a large bowl, stir in parmesan, herbs, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet. Cook without stirring for 3-4 minutes until edges begin to set.
  5. Transfer skillet to the oven and bake for 12-15 minutes or until the frittata is fully set and golden.
  6. Allow to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

This frittata can be served warm or at room temperature, making it ideal for brunch or picnic-style Easter celebrations.

2. Roasted Carrot and Feta Tart with Thyme

Carrots go beyond the ordinary in this stunning tart with flaky pastry, tangy feta, and fragrant fresh thyme. It looks as impressive as it tastes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 pound carrots, peeled and sliced into thin strips
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 tsp honey
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss carrot strips with olive oil, honey, thyme, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  2. Roll out puff pastry on parchment paper. Score a 1-inch border around edges.
  3. Spread feta evenly inside the border, then arrange roasted carrots on top.
  4. Brush the pastry edges with beaten egg to achieve a golden crust.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes until pastry is puffed and golden brown.

This tart makes for a sophisticated centerpiece perfect for a light lunch or appetizer.

3. Deviled Eggs – Classic With a Twist

Deviled eggs land at every Easter table traditionally. Give yours a twist with creamy avocado and a sprinkle of paprika for added depth and vibrancy.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Paprika for garnish
  • Fresh chives, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Hard boil eggs, cool, and peel. Slice eggs in half lengthwise and carefully remove yolks into a bowl.
  2. Mash yolks and avocado together, add mustard, lemon juice, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Pipe or spoon mixture back into egg whites. Sprinkle with paprika and chives.

These avocado deviled eggs add natural creaminess and a fresh twist to a classic.

4. Spring Pea and Mint Soup

Celebrate green goodness with this vibrant chilled pea and mint soup — refreshing and perfect for pre-meal sipping or a light first course.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 small onion, quartered
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, packed
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Greek yogurt or crème fraîche for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onion in butter or olive oil until translucent.
  2. Add peas and vegetable broth; simmer for 10 minutes until peas are tender.
  3. Remove from heat, stir in mint leaves, and blend until smooth using an immersion blender or food processor.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Chill if serving cold.
  5. Garnish with a dollop of yogurt or crème fraîche.

The fresh mint elevates this soup with a cooling and aromatic note ideal for a spring holiday.

5. Cheesy Hot Cross Buns

Infuse tradition with cheese in this savory take on classic hot cross buns. Enjoy fluffy buns dotted with cheese and warming spices, perfect toasted with butter.

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp allspice
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 egg, beaten (for dough)
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1/4 cup flour mixed with water for crosses

Instructions:

  1. Activate yeast in warm milk with a teaspoon of sugar for 10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Mix flour, sugar, salt, and spices in a bowl. Add yeast mixture, beaten egg, and melted butter; knead into a dough.
  3. Fold in cheese, knead briefly to combine. Let dough rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  4. Shape dough into small buns, place on a baking tray, and let rise again for 30 minutes.
  5. Pipe crosses on buns using flour paste; brush with egg wash.
  6. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes until golden.

Serve warm and enjoy the cheesy twist on this Easter staple.

6. Roasted Beet and Goat Cheese Salad with Walnuts

Earthy roasted beets paired with tangy goat cheese and toasted walnuts create a salad laden with flavor and a burst of color perfect for Easter tables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium beets, scrubbed and trimmed
  • 4 oz goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup toasted walnuts
  • Mixed greens (arugula, spinach, or your choice)
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Wrap beets in foil and roast for 45-60 minutes until tender. Let cool, then peel and slice.
  2. In a bowl, whisk balsamic vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss greens with dressing, arrange beet slices on top.
  4. Sprinkle goat cheese and walnuts over the salad.

This dish offers a balance of sweet, tangy, and crunchy elements that brighten your banquet with fresh textures.

7. Mushroom and Spinach Stuffed Pastry Puffs

Golden puff pastry envelops a savory blend of mushrooms, spinach, garlic, and herbs. These decadent bites are perfect as appetizers or side dishes for Easter dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 8 oz mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup cream cheese
  • 1 tsp thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet, sauté onions and garlic until fragrant. Add mushrooms, cook until moisture evaporates. Stir in spinach, cook until wilted.
  2. Remove from heat, stir in cream cheese and thyme. Season with salt and pepper. Let cool.
  3. Cut puff pastry into squares, spoon filling onto the center of each. Fold into desired shapes, seal edges.
  4. Brush tops with egg wash, bake at 400°F (200°C) for 15-20 minutes until puffed and golden.

Serve warm for a rich, indulgent starter.

8. Lemon and Lavender Shortbread Cookies

Bright and floral, these shortbread cookies are an elegant ending to your Easter feast or a delightful treat alongside afternoon tea.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest
  • 1 tbsp culinary lavender buds, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Cream butter and powdered sugar together until light and fluffy.
  2. Mix in lemon zest, lavender, vanilla, and salt. Gradually add flour until dough forms.
  3. Roll dough into a log, wrap in plastic wrap, chill for 30 minutes.
  4. Slice dough into 1/4-inch thick rounds, place on baking sheet.
  5. Bake at 325°F (165°C) for 15-20 minutes until edges just begin to color.

These cookies combine the brightness of lemon with the subtle floral notes of lavender for a sophisticated treat.

9. Lemon Garlic Roasted Potatoes with Rosemary

Tender on the inside, crispy on the outside, and bursting with lemon and rosemary fragrance, these roasted potatoes are a crowd-pleaser side perfect for any Easter table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss potatoes with olive oil, garlic, rosemary, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes until golden and crispy, stirring halfway through.

These potatoes offer fragrant, zesty flavor that pairs well with any main dish on your Easter menu.

10. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

No Easter celebration feels complete without a classic carrot cake. Moist, spiced, and topped with luscious cream cheese frosting, this homemade cake is a showstopper.

Ingredients for Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Ingredients for Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. Whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt together.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat eggs, sugars, oil, and vanilla until smooth. Slowly mix in dry ingredients. Fold in grated carrots and walnuts.
  4. Divide batter evenly between pans and bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Cool completely.
  5. For frosting, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla until fluffy.
  6. Frost cooled cakes, layering and topping evenly.

Take bite after bite of this crowd-pleasing dessert — perfect for finishing your Easter feast on a sweet note.

Final Thoughts

Vegetarian Easter recipes offer diverse, flavorful options that highlight spring’s best ingredients while accommodating a variety of tastes. From hearty frittatas and savory pastries to vibrant salads and indulgent sweets, these dishes ensure your holiday celebration is memorable and satisfying for vegetarians and omnivores alike.

Prepare these recipes for your Easter gathering and enjoy the seasonal bounty, shared moments, and culinary creativity that make the holiday special. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
