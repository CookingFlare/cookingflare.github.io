7 Unique Recipes Inspired by the Flavors of the Philippines


The Philippines, an archipelago of over 7,000 islands, is a melting pot of culinary delights. Its cuisine is a fusion of Spanish, Chinese, Malay, and American flavors, resulting in a rich and diverse gastronomic experience. Here are 7 unique recipes inspired by the flavors of the Philippines, showcasing the country's vibrant and aromatic flavors. Recipe 1: Adobo-Style Braised Short Ribs with Pineapple and Coconut Adobo, a classic Filipino stew, is a staple in many Filipino households. This recipe puts a modern twist on the traditional dish by adding short ribs and a sweet and tangy glaze made from pineapple and coconut milk. The result is a tender and flavorful meat that will leave you wanting more. Ingredients:

2 pounds beef short ribs 1/4 cup fish sauce 2 tablespoons vinegar 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 tablespoon garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger, grated 1 cup pineapple juice 1 cup coconut milk 1 tablespoon brown sugar 2 tablespoons sesame oil 2 cloves garlic, minced Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). In a large Dutch oven, combine fish sauce, vinegar, black pepper, garlic, and ginger. Bring to a boil, then add short ribs and cook for 2 minutes. Transfer the pot to the oven and braise for 2 1/2 hours or until the meat is tender. In a small saucepan, combine pineapple juice, coconut milk, and brown sugar. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Add the sesame oil and garlic to the saucepan and stir to combine. To serve, place the short ribs on a platter and spoon the pineapple-coconut sauce over the top. Garnish with chopped cilantro, if desired.

Recipe 2: Lechon Kawali with Spicy Mango Salsa Lechon kawali, a popular Filipino dish, consists of crispy fried pork belly served with a spicy mango salsa. The combination of textures and flavors is a testament to the Philippines' love for bold and vibrant flavors. Ingredients:

2 pounds pork belly, skin removed 1/2 cup cornstarch 1/4 cup fish sauce 2 tablespoons vinegar 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 tablespoon garlic, minced 1 tablespoon grated ginger Vegetable oil for frying Spicy Mango Salsa (see below for recipe)

Instructions:

Cut the pork belly into 2-inch pieces and coat with cornstarch. Heat 2 inches of vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer over medium-high heat. Fry the pork until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the pork from the oil and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. To serve, place the lechon kawali on a platter and spoon the spicy mango salsa over the top.

Spicy Mango Salsa:

2 cups diced fresh mango 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped 1 tablespoon lime juice Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe 3: Sinigang-Style Soup with Pork and Vegetables Sinigang is a sour and savory Filipino stew made with a variety of ingredients such as pork, vegetables, and tamarind broth. This recipe puts a modern twist on the traditional dish by adding a variety of colorful vegetables. Ingredients:

2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into 2-inch pieces 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as tomatoes, okra, and bok choy) 1 cup tamarind broth 1/4 cup fish sauce 2 tablespoons vinegar 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 tablespoon garlic, minced 1 tablespoon grated ginger

Instructions:

In a large pot, combine pork, mixed vegetables, tamarind broth, fish sauce, vinegar, black pepper, garlic, and ginger. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the pork is tender. To serve, ladle the sinigang into bowls and garnish with chopped cilantro and a sprinkle of salt.

Recipe 4: Chicken Inasal with Grilled Pineapple and Cilantro Rice Chicken inasal, a popular Filipino dish, is a marinated chicken dish grilled to perfection. This recipe adds a sweet and tangy twist by serving the chicken with grilled pineapple and cilantro rice. Ingredients:

2 pounds chicken breast or thighs, bone-in 1/4 cup vinegar 2 tablespoons fish sauce 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 tablespoon garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger, grated 1 cup marinade (see below for recipe) 4 cups cooked rice Grilled pineapple (optional) Cilantro leaves (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. In a large bowl, combine chicken, vinegar, fish sauce, black pepper, garlic, and ginger. Marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight. Remove the chicken from the marinade and grill for 5-7 minutes per side or until cooked through. Serve the chicken with grilled pineapple and cilantro rice. Garnish with chopped cilantro leaves, if desired.

Marinade:

1 cup vinegar 2 tablespoons fish sauce 1 tablespoon black pepper 1 tablespoon garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger, grated

Recipe 5: Lumpia-Style Spring Rolls with Shrimp and Vegetables Lumpia, a popular Filipino dish, consists of deep-fried spring rolls filled with a variety of ingredients. This recipe adds a twist by serving the spring rolls with a sweet and sour sauce made from tamarind and palm sugar. Ingredients:

2 cups mixed vegetables (such as cabbage, carrots, and bean sprouts) 1 cup peeled and deveined shrimp 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 cup cornstarch 1 egg, beaten Vegetable oil for frying Tamarind-Palm Sugar Sauce (see below for recipe)

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine mixed vegetables, shrimp, flour, cornstarch, and egg. Mix well to combine. Heat 2 inches of vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer over medium-high heat. Fry the spring rolls until golden brown, about 3-5 minutes. Remove the spring rolls from the oil and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. To serve, place the lumpia on a platter and spoon the tamarind-palm sugar sauce over the top.

Tamarind-Palm Sugar Sauce:

1 cup tamarind broth 2 tablespoons palm sugar 1 tablespoon vinegar 1 tablespoon fish sauce 1 tablespoon black pepper

Recipe 6: Pancit-Style Stir-Fried Noodles with Vegetables and Shrimp Pancit, a popular Filipino dish, consists of stir-fried noodles and vegetables. This recipe adds a twist by serving the noodles with a variety of seafood such as shrimp and squid. Ingredients:

2 cups pancit noodles 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as cabbage, carrots, and bean sprouts) 1 cup peeled and deveined shrimp 1 cup diced squid 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger, grated Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Cook the pancit noodles according to package instructions. In a large wok or frying pan, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the mixed vegetables, shrimp, and squid to the wok and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes. Add the cooked noodles to the wok and stir-fry for another minute. To serve, ladle the pancit into bowls and garnish with chopped cilantro leaves and a sprinkle of salt.

Recipe 7: Ube Halaya-Style Purple Yams with Coconut Cream and Langka Ube halaya, a popular Filipino dish, consists of purple yams cooked in coconut cream and langka. This recipe adds a twist by serving the yams with a variety of fresh fruits and nuts. Ingredients:

2 cups purple yams, peeled and grated 1/2 cup coconut cream 2 tablespoons langka (jackfruit) 1 tablespoon brown sugar 1 tablespoon grated ginger 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro Fresh fruits and nuts (optional)

Instructions:

In a large saucepan, combine purple yams, coconut cream, langka, brown sugar, and ginger. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes or until the yams are tender. To serve, place the ube halaya in a serving bowl and garnish with chopped fresh cilantro and a sprinkle of sugar. Serve with a variety of fresh fruits and nuts, if desired.

These 7 unique recipes inspired by the flavors of the Philippines are a testament to the country's rich and vibrant culinary heritage. From spicy and sour dishes to sweet and savory desserts, these recipes showcase the diversity and complexity of Filipino flavors. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and take you on a culinary journey through the Philippines.
