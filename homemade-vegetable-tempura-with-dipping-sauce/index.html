Homemade Vegetable Tempura with Dipping Sauce


Craving a crunchy and delightful Japanese dish? Look no further than this homemade vegetable tempura with a delicious dipping sauce. Tempura is a popular Japanese dish consisting of lightly battered and deep-fried vegetables, perfect for a light and crispy meal or as a delightful appetizer. The secret to a successful tempura lies in the crispiness of the batter and the freshness of the vegetables. Let’s dive into how you can create your own crispy and flavorful vegetable tempura at home!

Ingredients:

For Vegetable Tempura:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • Assorted fresh vegetables (such as zucchini, sweet potatoes, bell peppers, and broccoli), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Oil for deep frying

For Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Grated daikon radish (optional)
  • Grated ginger (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dipping Sauce:

    • In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, water, mirin, and sugar.
    • Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves.
    • Remove from heat and let it cool. Add grated daikon radish and ginger for extra flavor if desired.

  2. Prepare the Vegetable Tempura:

    • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt.
    • Gradually add ice-cold water and mix until the batter is smooth. Be careful not to overmix; some lumps are okay.

  3. Fry the Vegetable Tempura:

    • Heat oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (180°C).
    • Dip the vegetable pieces into the batter, ensuring they are well coated.
    • Carefully place the battered vegetables into the hot oil and fry until golden and crispy. Do not overcrowd the pan.
    • Remove the tempura with a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Serve the hot vegetable tempura immediately with the prepared dipping sauce on the side.
    • Enjoy the crunchy texture and delightful flavors of the homemade vegetable tempura.

Tips for Perfect Tempura:

  • Use ice-cold water in the batter to ensure a crispy texture.
  • Do not overmix the batter to avoid a heavy coating on the vegetables.
  • Fry the vegetables in batches to maintain the oil temperature and ensure even cooking.
  • Experiment with different vegetable combinations for a colorful and flavorful tempura.

Now that you have mastered the art of making homemade vegetable tempura, treat yourself and your loved ones to this Japanese delicacy. Whether as a snack, appetizer, or main dish, vegetable tempura is sure to impress with its light and crispy goodness. Get creative with your choice of vegetables and enjoy the crunchy satisfaction of this delightful dish!
