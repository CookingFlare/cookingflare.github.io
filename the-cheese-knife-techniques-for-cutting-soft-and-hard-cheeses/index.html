The Cheese Knife: Techniques for Cutting Soft and Hard Cheeses


If you’re a cheese lover like me, you know that the right knife can make all the difference when it comes to enjoying your favorite cheeses. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply indulging in a cheese plate for one, having the proper techniques for cutting both soft and hard cheeses is essential. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of cheese cutting and share some tips and tricks for using the cheese knife effectively.

The Cheese Knife: A Versatile Tool

The cheese knife is a versatile tool specifically designed for cutting and serving cheese. Its unique features make it the perfect companion for any cheese connoisseur. The blade of the cheese knife is typically narrow and slightly curved, allowing for precise and controlled cuts. The sharp edge effortlessly slices through both soft and hard cheeses without crushing or crumbling them.

Techniques for Cutting Soft Cheeses

Soft cheeses, such as Brie, Camembert, and Goat Cheese, require a delicate touch to achieve perfect slices. Here are some techniques to master when cutting soft cheeses:

  1. Use a cheese wire: A cheese wire is a handy tool for cutting soft cheeses with precision. Simply place the wire beneath the cheese and pull it through to create even slices.

  2. Use a cheese plane: A cheese plane is another excellent tool for cutting soft cheeses. Hold the cheese firmly and glide the cheese plane across the surface to create thin, uniform slices.

  3. Use a warm, damp knife: For certain soft cheeses, like Mozzarella or Burrata, a warm, damp knife can help achieve clean cuts. Dip the knife in hot water, wipe it dry, and then slice through the cheese smoothly.

  4. Serve with a cheese spatula: Once you’ve cut your soft cheese into slices, use a cheese spatula to carefully transfer them onto a serving plate or cheese board. This ensures that your beautifully cut cheese maintains its shape and presentation.

Techniques for Cutting Hard Cheeses

Hard cheeses, such as Cheddar, Parmesan, and Gouda, require a bit more effort to cut through their firm texture. Here are some techniques to help you master cutting hard cheeses:

  1. Use a cheese knife: The cheese knife’s sharp and sturdy blade is perfect for tackling hard cheeses. Hold the cheese firmly and apply gentle pressure to slice through the cheese smoothly.

  2. Use a cheese cleaver: A cheese cleaver is a heavy-duty knife with a thick blade, ideal for cutting through hard cheeses like Parmesan. Position the cleaver on top of the cheese and apply downward pressure to crack it into smaller, more manageable pieces.

  3. Use a cheese plane: Despite being primarily used for soft cheeses, a cheese plane can also be effective in cutting thin and even slices from hard cheeses. Hold the cheese firmly and glide the plane across the surface to achieve your desired thickness.

  4. Serve with a cheese fork: Once you’ve cut your hard cheese into slices or chunks, a cheese fork can be used to serve them. The fork’s prongs allow for easy picking and serving, maintaining the integrity of the cheese.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of cutting cheese is crucial for any cheese enthusiast. With the right techniques and tools, you can elevate your cheese experience and impress your guests with beautifully cut and presented cheese. Remember to choose the appropriate knife for the cheese you’re cutting, whether it be a soft Brie or a hard Parmesan. So, the next time you indulge in a cheese platter, put your new skills to the test and savor every flavorful bite.

Happy cheese cutting!
