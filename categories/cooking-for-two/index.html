Allergy-Friendly Fare: Allergy-Friendly Cooking for Two Recipes

Are you and your loved one dealing with food allergies? Do you find it challenging to cook delicious meals that cater to your dietary restrictions? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share some mouthwatering allergy-friendly recipes specifically designed for couples. Whether you’re both dealing with allergies or just one of you, these recipes are guaranteed to bring joy and flavor to your table. 1. Creamy Butternut Squash Pasta Ingredients: 1 small butternut squash, peeled and diced 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 cup of vegetable broth 1/2 cup of coconut milk 2 tablespoons of nutritional yeast 1 teaspoon of dried sage Salt and pepper to taste 8 ounces of gluten-free pasta Instructions: Boil the diced butternut squash until tender. Drain and set aside. In a blender, combine the cooked butternut squash, minced garlic, vegetable broth, coconut milk, nutritional yeast, dried sage, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy. Cook the gluten-free pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside. In a large saucepan, heat the butternut squash sauce over medium heat until warmed through. Add the cooked pasta to the sauce and toss until well coated. Serve hot and garnish with fresh herbs if desired. 2. Lemon Herb Grilled Chicken Ingredients: 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts Juice of 1 lemon Zest of 1 lemon 2 tablespoons of olive oil 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 teaspoon of dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, lemon zest, olive oil, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Place the chicken breasts in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Make sure the chicken is evenly coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken breasts for approximately 6-8 minutes on each side, or until they reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve the grilled chicken with your favorite allergy-friendly side dishes. 3. Berry Quinoa Salad Ingredients: 1 cup of cooked quinoa 1 cup of mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries) 1/4 cup of chopped fresh mint leaves 1/4 cup of chopped almonds 2 tablespoons of lemon juice 2 tablespoons of olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, mixed berries, chopped fresh mint leaves, chopped almonds, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well. Refrigerate the salad for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve chilled as a refreshing side dish or a light lunch option. 4. Chocolate Avocado Mousse Ingredients: 2 ripe avocados 1/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder 1/4 cup of maple syrup 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract Pinch of salt Fresh berries for garnish (optional) Instructions: Cut the avocados in half, remove the pits, and scoop out the flesh into a blender or food processor. Add the cocoa powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and salt to the blender. Blend until smooth and creamy. Transfer the mousse to serving bowls and refrigerate for at least 1 hour to set. Garnish with fresh berries if desired before serving. Conclusion Cooking for two can be a delightful experience, even when dealing with food allergies.
