Tasty Thanksgiving Spinach and Artichoke Dip Recipe


Thanksgiving is the perfect time to indulge in rich, savory dishes, and one appetizer that never fails to impress guests is spinach and artichoke dip. This creamy, flavorful dip combines the earthiness of spinach, the tang of artichokes, and a luscious, cheesy base, making it an ideal snack to serve while guests gather before the big feast. Whether you’re hosting or attending a Thanksgiving gathering, this spinach and artichoke dip recipe is a sure way to bring a touch of comfort and joy to your table.

Why This Spinach and Artichoke Dip is Perfect for Thanksgiving

Thanksgiving meals are all about abundance, rich flavors, and sharing memorable dishes. While the turkey, stuffing, and mashed potatoes are always the main attractions, it’s the appetizers and sides that keep everyone nibbling and satisfied as they wait for the main event. Spinach and artichoke dip is a beloved dish, but its versatility makes it perfect for any holiday table. With a balance of textures and flavors that’s creamy, cheesy, and a little bit tangy, it strikes the perfect note for a Thanksgiving appetizer.

Not only does this spinach and artichoke dip complement the other rich dishes, but it also offers a lighter contrast to the heaviness of the turkey and stuffing. Whether served with crispy crackers, crunchy baguette slices, or fresh vegetables for dipping, this dish will have everyone reaching for more. Its rich creaminess and savory flavor profile are both comforting and decadent, but it’s still fresh enough to be enjoyed in between bites of heartier dishes.

Ingredients for Thanksgiving Spinach and Artichoke Dip

To make this delicious spinach and artichoke dip, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Dip:

  • 1 (10-ounce) package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1 (14-ounce) can artichoke hearts, drained and chopped
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for sautéing)

For Serving:

  • Crackers, crostini, or sliced baguette
  • Fresh vegetable crudités (carrots, celery, cucumber, etc.)

These ingredients create a perfectly balanced dip that’s creamy and full of flavor, with just the right amount of cheese and seasoning to make it a crowd favorite.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Spinach and Artichoke Dip

1. Prepare the Spinach

Begin by thawing the frozen spinach. You can do this by placing it in the refrigerator the night before, or for a quicker method, defrost it in the microwave. Once thawed, use a clean kitchen towel or paper towels to wring out as much liquid as possible. The key to a good spinach dip is to ensure there’s no excess moisture, which could make the dip watery. After wringing out the spinach, set it aside.

2. Sauté the Garlic

In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until it becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can become bitter.

3. Combine the Main Ingredients

Once the garlic is aromatic, add the thawed and drained spinach to the skillet and stir to combine. Cook the spinach for about 3 minutes, just to warm it up and allow the flavors to meld. Next, add the chopped artichoke hearts, stirring to combine. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, making sure everything is evenly mixed and heated through.

4. Prepare the Creamy Base

In a separate bowl, combine the softened cream cheese, sour cream, and mayonnaise. Use a hand mixer or whisk to blend the ingredients together until smooth and creamy. Add the shredded mozzarella cheese and grated Parmesan cheese, mixing until fully incorporated. The cheeses will give the dip a rich, velvety texture that’s impossible to resist.

5. Mix Everything Together

Now that you have both the vegetable mixture and the creamy base ready, it’s time to combine them. Add the spinach and artichoke mixture to the cream cheese mixture and stir until everything is well combined. Make sure the vegetables are evenly distributed in the creamy cheese mixture.

6. Season the Dip

Season the dip with onion powder, dried basil (optional), salt, and pepper. Taste the dip and adjust the seasoning as needed. Depending on your preference, you can add more salt or pepper to balance the flavors.

7. Bake the Dip

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Transfer the prepared spinach and artichoke dip into a 9-inch baking dish or a similar oven-safe dish. Use a spatula to spread the mixture evenly across the top.

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 25 to 30 minutes, or until the dip is bubbling and the top is golden brown and slightly crispy. You’ll know it’s done when the edges are slightly crispy and the cheese is melted to perfection.

8. Serve and Enjoy

Once your spinach and artichoke dip is out of the oven, let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve the dip warm with an assortment of crackers, crostini, or fresh vegetable crudités for dipping. The creamy texture and rich flavor make it perfect for pairing with any of your favorite Thanksgiving side dishes.

Tips for Perfecting Your Spinach and Artichoke Dip

While this recipe is already foolproof, there are a few tips to ensure that your spinach and artichoke dip is as perfect as possible:

  • Use Fresh Spinach (Optional): If you prefer to use fresh spinach instead of frozen, simply sauté about 4 cups of fresh spinach until it wilts and then chop it up before using it in the dip.

  • Try Different Cheeses: While mozzarella and Parmesan are the classic choices for this dip, feel free to experiment with other cheeses like Gouda, cheddar, or even a bit of cream cheese for extra richness.

  • Make Ahead and Refrigerate: You can prepare the dip ahead of time and refrigerate it before baking. Just assemble the dip as instructed, cover it with plastic wrap or foil, and store it in the fridge for up to 24 hours. When ready to bake, simply pop it in the oven and bake as directed.

  • Add a Crunchy Topping: For an added layer of flavor and texture, sprinkle some extra Parmesan cheese on top of the dip before baking, or top it with breadcrumbs for a crispy, golden finish.

  • Season to Taste: Adjust the seasoning to your personal preference. If you like a bit of heat, feel free to add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the dip for a spicy kick.
