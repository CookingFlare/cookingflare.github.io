How to Make Korean Spicy Beef Soup (Yukgaejang)


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors and hearty dishes, and few recipes exemplify this better than Yukgaejang, a traditional spicy beef soup. This dish is beloved for its rich broth, spicy kick, and nourishing ingredients, making it a perfect meal for colder weather or when you need a hearty comfort food.

Yukgaejang is not only packed with flavors but is also a healthy dish, thanks to the use of lean beef, vegetables, and a variety of spices that come together in a complex, savory broth. Whether you’re making it for the first time or you’re a seasoned pro in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through every step, ensuring a delicious bowl of Yukgaejang.

The History of Yukgaejang

Yukgaejang has deep roots in Korean history, dating back to the time of the Joseon Dynasty. The dish was originally a food for soldiers who needed high-energy, protein-rich meals to sustain them during long days on the battlefield. Over time, it evolved into a beloved comfort food, cherished for its hearty ingredients and satisfying flavor profile.

This dish has become popular worldwide, especially among Korean food enthusiasts, as it offers a perfect balance of heat, umami, and freshness. Today, it’s commonly found in Korean restaurants and is often enjoyed during family gatherings, or as a rejuvenating dish after a long day.

Ingredients for Yukgaejang

To make an authentic bowl of Yukgaejang, you need the right ingredients. While you might find different variations depending on the region or personal preferences, the core ingredients remain the same.

Main Ingredients

  • Beef Brisket or Shank – This is the most common cut used for Yukgaejang because of its rich flavor and tender texture when cooked slowly. It provides the deep umami base that the broth needs.
  • Korean Red Pepper Flakes (Gochugaru) – These give the soup its signature spiciness. The flavor profile of Gochugaru is milder compared to other chili flakes, offering a smoky heat that doesn’t overpower the dish.
  • Korean Soy Sauce – A staple in Korean cooking, this adds depth and saltiness to the broth.
  • Garlic – Fresh garlic imparts a rich aroma and flavor, balancing the spice.
  • Ginger – Fresh ginger adds a slight sweetness that complements the other flavors.
  • Korean Chili Paste (Gochujang) – A fermented paste made from red chili peppers, it gives the soup a complex, savory, and slightly sweet taste.
  • Sesame Oil – This oil adds a nutty aroma and richness to the dish.
  • Scallions (Green Onions) – Chopped scallions not only bring a fresh, slightly spicy bite but also provide a burst of color.
  • Bean Sprouts – These add texture and a mild crunch, as well as a refreshing contrast to the spicy broth.

Vegetables

  • Shiitake Mushrooms – Known for their earthy flavor, shiitake mushrooms are often used in Korean soups. They give the soup a subtle umami depth and absorb the flavors of the broth beautifully.
  • Korean Radish (Mu) – This white radish, often used in kimchi and soups, has a mild, slightly peppery flavor that pairs perfectly with the spicy elements in Yukgaejang.
  • Spinach – Fresh spinach adds a vibrant green color and is often used in the last stages of cooking to provide a contrast in texture and flavor.

Seasoning and Garnish

  • Salt – To taste, salt is added to balance out the flavors and season the broth.
  • Pepper – A small amount of ground black pepper enhances the spiciness and deepens the soup’s flavor.

How to Prepare Yukgaejang

Step 1: Preparing the Beef

Start by preparing the beef. For Yukgaejang, you will want to use a tough cut of beef that becomes tender when boiled, such as beef brisket or shank. Here’s how you can prepare it:

  1. Trim the Beef: If you’re using brisket or shank, remove any excess fat, leaving some to help with the richness of the broth.
  2. Boil the Beef: Place the beef in a large pot and cover it with water. Bring it to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, lower the heat and let it simmer for about an hour or until the beef becomes tender.
  3. Shred the Beef: Once the beef is cooked and tender, remove it from the pot and let it cool slightly. Shred the beef into thin strips using a fork or your hands. This shredded beef will form the heart of your soup.

Step 2: Preparing the Broth

While the beef is simmering, you can prepare the flavorful broth. The broth is the base of the dish and provides the foundation for all of the flavors to come together.

  1. Sauté Garlic and Ginger: In a separate pot, heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Add finely chopped garlic and ginger and sauté them until fragrant, which should take about 1-2 minutes.
  2. Add Soy Sauce and Gochujang: Stir in a couple of tablespoons of Korean soy sauce and gochujang (Korean chili paste). These two ingredients will give the broth its rich, umami flavor and a little kick of heat. Let the mixture cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the Beef Broth: After simmering the beef, strain the broth into the pot with the garlic, ginger, and seasoning mixture. Bring the broth to a simmer, allowing the flavors to meld together. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Step 3: Adding Vegetables and Spices

Now it’s time to add vegetables and finish building the soup’s flavor.

  1. Add the Radish and Mushrooms: Peel and slice the Korean radish and add it to the simmering broth. Then, add sliced shiitake mushrooms for an earthy depth. Let these cook in the broth for about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Add the Shredded Beef: Once the vegetables are tender, add the shredded beef into the pot. Stir everything together, ensuring the beef is thoroughly mixed with the vegetables and broth.
  3. Season the Soup: At this point, you can add gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes) to the broth, adjusting the amount depending on how spicy you want the soup to be. Let the soup simmer for another 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop further.

Step 4: Final Touches

  1. Add Bean Sprouts: For a bit of crunch and freshness, add bean sprouts to the soup and let them cook for just a few minutes.
  2. Add Spinach: Add fresh spinach at the very end of cooking. This will preserve its vibrant green color and ensure it doesn’t become too wilted.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the soup is ready, it’s time to plate and serve. Ladle the soup into bowls, making sure each serving has a good balance of beef, vegetables, and broth. Garnish with chopped scallions for a touch of freshness, and if you like, drizzle a little extra sesame oil on top for added flavor.

Yukgaejang is best enjoyed with a bowl of steamed white rice and some kimchi on the side to balance the heat and add more layers of flavor.

Tips for Making the Perfect Yukgaejang

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: To get the best flavor, try to use fresh vegetables and high-quality beef. The freshness of the ingredients will enhance the flavor of the soup.
  2. Adjust the Spice Level: If you’re not a fan of too much heat, you can reduce the amount of gochugaru or gochujang. However, the spiciness is one of the defining features of Yukgaejang, so don’t shy away from it entirely!
  3. Slow Cooking for Tender Meat: The key to achieving tender, melt-in-your-mouth beef is to cook it slowly. Don’t rush the simmering process; let the beef cook for at least an hour for the best texture.
  4. Let the Soup Rest: Like many soups and stews, Yukgaejang often tastes even better the next day. Allowing it to sit in the fridge for a few hours or overnight lets the flavors deepen and meld together.

Conclusion

Yukgaejang is a hearty, flavorful, and satisfying dish that encapsulates the spirit of Korean cuisine. With its spicy broth, tender beef, and a variety of vegetables, this soup is perfect for those who enjoy bold and savory flavors. The best part is that this dish is easy to customize – whether you prefer it milder or spicier, there’s always room to adjust the seasonings to your liking.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for something comforting and flavorful, try making this delicious Korean spicy beef soup at home. With a little patience and the right ingredients, you’ll be able to enjoy this traditional dish that’s loved by many across the world. Happy cooking!
