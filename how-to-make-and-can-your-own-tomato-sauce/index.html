How to Make and Can Your Own Tomato Sauce


Tomato sauce is a staple in many kitchens, used as a base for pastas, pizzas, soups, and a myriad of other dishes. While store-bought options are convenient, making and canning your own tomato sauce allows you to control the ingredients, customize the flavors, and enjoy the satisfaction of preserving the fruits of your labor. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about making and canning your own tomato sauce, from selecting the best tomatoes to the canning process itself.

Selecting the Right Tomatoes

The first step to making great homemade tomato sauce is choosing the right type of tomato. While you can use any variety of tomato, certain types are better suited for making sauce due to their flavor and consistency. Here are the top choices:

Roma Tomatoes (Plum Tomatoes)

Roma tomatoes are one of the best varieties for making sauce. They have fewer seeds, a firmer flesh, and lower water content, which makes them perfect for creating a thick, rich sauce. Their slightly tangy flavor adds depth to any dish.

San Marzano Tomatoes

Considered the gold standard for Italian tomato sauce, San Marzano tomatoes are known for their sweet flavor and low acidity. They’re often grown in the volcanic soil of Italy, which adds a unique depth to their taste. If you can get your hands on authentic San Marzano tomatoes, they will elevate your sauce to the next level.

Beefsteak Tomatoes

Beefsteak tomatoes are large, juicy, and flavorful, making them an excellent choice for sauce if you prefer a slightly chunkier texture. Their high water content means you’ll need to simmer the sauce longer to achieve the desired consistency.

Heirloom Tomatoes

If you want a more complex flavor profile, heirloom tomatoes are an intriguing option. These tomatoes come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, each offering unique flavors. They are typically grown without the use of modern pesticides or fertilizers, which can also appeal to those looking for organic options.

Cherry and Grape Tomatoes

For a sweeter sauce, consider using cherry or grape tomatoes. These small tomatoes pack a punch of flavor and can be great for adding a hint of sweetness to your sauce.

Tips for Tomato Selection:

  • Choose tomatoes that are ripe but not overripe.
  • Avoid tomatoes with large cracks, blemishes, or signs of rot.
  • If possible, buy from local farmers or farmers’ markets to ensure you’re getting the freshest tomatoes available.

Preparing the Tomatoes

Once you’ve selected your tomatoes, the next step is to prepare them for sauce-making. Here’s a simple process to follow:

Step 1: Blanching the Tomatoes

Blanching helps remove the skins and makes the tomatoes easier to peel. To blanch your tomatoes:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  2. Cut a small “X” on the bottom of each tomato with a sharp knife.
  3. Place the tomatoes into the boiling water for 30 seconds to 1 minute, or until the skins begin to peel away.
  4. Remove the tomatoes from the boiling water and immediately transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.
  5. Peel off the skins, which should come off easily.

Step 2: Removing the Seeds (Optional)

While many people like to leave the seeds in their sauce for added texture, you can remove them if you prefer a smoother consistency. To do this:

  1. Cut the peeled tomatoes in half and gently squeeze out the seeds.
  2. Alternatively, you can scoop out the seeds with a spoon or your fingers.

Step 3: Chopping the Tomatoes

Once your tomatoes are peeled and seeded, chop them into chunks. You can choose the size of the chunks based on your preference for a chunky or smoother sauce.

Making the Tomato Sauce

Now comes the fun part—turning your fresh tomatoes into a flavorful sauce! Here’s a basic recipe to guide you through the process:

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds of ripe tomatoes (Roma, San Marzano, or a combination)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for heat)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional for added flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Olive Oil: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Sauté the Onions and Garlic: Add the chopped onions and garlic to the pot. Sauté for 3-4 minutes, or until they are soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the Tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and stir to combine.
  4. Simmer the Sauce: Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook uncovered for 45 minutes to 1 hour, stirring occasionally. The sauce will thicken as it reduces. If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the sauce to your desired consistency.
  5. Season the Sauce: Add salt, pepper, oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes (if using). Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning. If the sauce is too acidic, add a tablespoon of sugar to balance the flavors.
  6. Add Fresh Basil (Optional): For a fresh, fragrant twist, add a few sprigs of fresh basil leaves towards the end of cooking.

Once your tomato sauce is thickened and perfectly seasoned, it’s ready to be used. If you’re planning to can the sauce, let it cool to room temperature before moving on to the canning process.

Canning the Tomato Sauce

Canning your homemade tomato sauce ensures that you can enjoy it all year long, even when fresh tomatoes are out of season. It’s important to follow safe canning practices to avoid contamination and spoilage. Here’s how to can your tomato sauce properly:

Supplies Needed:

  • Canning jars with lids (pint or quart size)
  • Canning pot or large stockpot with a rack
  • Jar lifter or tongs
  • Canning funnel
  • Ladle
  • Clean kitchen towels
  • Vinegar (for sterilizing)
  • A large pot for the sauce

Step 1: Sterilizing the Jars

Before you begin the canning process, it’s essential to sterilize your jars to ensure the sauce stays safe for long-term storage. To sterilize:

  1. Place the jars in a large pot of boiling water for 10-15 minutes.
  2. Remove the jars carefully with a jar lifter and place them on a clean towel to dry.

Step 2: Preparing the Lids

Place the lids in a small pot of hot (not boiling) water for a few minutes to soften the sealing compound. This helps create an airtight seal during the canning process.

Step 3: Filling the Jars

Once your jars and lids are ready, it’s time to fill the jars with tomato sauce. Using a canning funnel, ladle the hot tomato sauce into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of space at the top (also called headspace). Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth to remove any sauce residue that might prevent a proper seal.

Step 4: Sealing the Jars

Place the warm lids on top of the jars and screw on the metal bands until they are finger-tight. Don’t overtighten, as the air needs to escape during the canning process.

Step 5: Processing the Jars

To ensure the tomato sauce is properly sealed and safe for storage, process the jars in a hot water bath:

  1. Fill a canning pot with enough water to fully submerge the jars.
  2. Bring the water to a boil and carefully place the jars into the pot using a jar lifter.
  3. Boil the jars for 35-40 minutes, depending on the size of the jars.
  4. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool on a clean towel.

Step 6: Checking the Seal

After the jars have cooled, check the seals by pressing down in the center of each lid. If the lid doesn’t pop back, the jar has sealed properly. If the lid pops up and down, the jar hasn’t sealed, and you should refrigerate the sauce and use it within a few days.

Step 7: Storing the Canned Sauce

Store the sealed jars in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or cellar. Properly canned tomato sauce can last for up to a year, and it will maintain its flavor and quality if stored correctly.

Tips for Using Your Canned Tomato Sauce

Once your tomato sauce is canned and stored, it’s time to put it to good use! Here are some ideas for using your homemade tomato sauce:

  • Pasta Dishes: Use your homemade sauce for classic pasta dishes like spaghetti, lasagna, or penne arrabbiata.
  • Pizza Sauce: Spread a layer of tomato sauce on your pizza dough for a homemade pizza night.
  • Soups and Stews: Add your tomato sauce to soups, stews, or chili for a rich base.
  • Dipping Sauce: Serve your tomato sauce as a dip for breadsticks, mozzarella sticks, or fried vegetables.
  • Shakshuka: Use the sauce as a base for shakshuka, a Middle Eastern dish with poached eggs in a tomato and pepper sauce.

Conclusion

Making and canning your own tomato sauce is a rewarding process that allows you to enjoy the fresh taste of summer tomatoes all year long. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can create a delicious, homemade tomato sauce that will elevate your meals and impress your friends and family. Whether you’re a seasoned canner or a first-time maker, these tips and techniques will help you produce a high-quality, flavorful tomato sauce you can be proud of. So grab your tomatoes, start cooking, and get ready to enjoy your homemade sauce for months to come!
