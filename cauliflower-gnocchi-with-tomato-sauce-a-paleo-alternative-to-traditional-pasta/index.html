Cauliflower Gnocchi with Tomato Sauce: A Paleo Alternative to Traditional Pasta


Are you tired of sacrificing flavor for a healthier diet? Do you miss the comforting taste of traditional pasta dishes but struggle to find a paleo-friendly alternative? Look no further! Cauliflower gnocchi with tomato sauce is a game-changer for anyone seeking a delicious and nutritious meal that aligns with paleo principles.

The Power of Cauliflower

Cauliflower has become a staple in many paleo households due to its versatility and nutritional benefits. This cruciferous vegetable is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making it an excellent addition to any meal. When used as a substitute for traditional pasta, cauliflower offers a lower carb count, fewer calories, and more fiber than its wheat-based counterpart.

Preparing Cauliflower Gnocchi

To make cauliflower gnocchi, you’ll need:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 egg

Rinse the cauliflower and remove the leaves and stem. Pulse the head in a food processor until it resembles rice. Transfer the cauliflower “rice” to a microwave-safe dish, cover it with a microwave-safe lid or plastic wrap, and cook on high for 4-5 minutes. Allow the cauliflower to cool, then squeeze out as much liquid as possible using a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth.

In a large bowl, combine the cooled cauliflower, almond flour, coconut flour, salt, and pepper. Mix well until the ingredients are fully incorporated. Add the olive oil and egg, and stir until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and pliable. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Cooking the Cauliflower Gnocchi

Divide the chilled dough into 4 equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a long rope, then cut it into 1-inch pieces. You should end up with around 20-25 gnocchi.

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the gnocchi for 3-5 minutes or until they float to the surface. Remove the cooked gnocchi with a slotted spoon and drain off any excess water.

Tomato Sauce Recipe

For the tomato sauce, you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups canned crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

Add the crushed tomatoes, chicken broth, dried basil, and dried oregano to the saucepan. Stir well to combine, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

Assembly and Serving

To assemble the dish, place the cooked cauliflower gnocchi on a plate or in a bowl. Spoon the tomato sauce over the gnocchi, then top with your choice of protein (such as grilled chicken, sautéed shrimp, or pan-seared pork chops). Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a sprinkle of parmesan cheese, if desired.

Conclusion

Cauliflower gnocchi with tomato sauce is a delicious and nutritious paleo alternative to traditional pasta dishes. With its low carb count, fewer calories, and more fiber than traditional pasta, this recipe is perfect for anyone seeking a healthier meal option. By following these simple steps, you can enjoy a flavorful and satisfying meal that aligns with your paleo lifestyle.
