How to Cook Delicious Ramen Beyond the Instant Pack


Ramen, a humble yet beloved dish, is more than just the pre-packaged noodles stacked on grocery store shelves. With its roots in Japan, ramen has grown into a culinary art form, featuring a symphony of rich broths, springy noodles, and flavorful toppings. If you’ve only known ramen from its instant form, it’s time to elevate your experience. This guide will teach you how to cook delicious, restaurant-worthy ramen right in your own kitchen, allowing you to ditch the prepackaged seasoning packets and embrace the craft of making this soul-warming meal.

The Secrets of Great Ramen

The magic of ramen lies in its harmony of four distinct components: the broth, the tare (flavor base), the noodles, and the toppings. To create a bowl of authentic and satisfying ramen, each of these elements must shine on their own while complementing the others. While this might sound complex, with a little preparation and experimentation, you’ll soon master the art of crafting a bowl of ramen that rivals your favorite noodle shop.

Step 1: Choose Your Ramen Broth Base

Ramen broth is the foundation of the dish, and there are several types to choose from depending on your flavor preferences. Here are the most popular varieties and how to make them:

1. Shoyu (Soy Sauce) Broth

Shoyu ramen features a soy sauce-based broth with a clear and savory flavor profile. It’s the most common style of ramen and pairs well with a variety of toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups chicken stock (or vegetable stock for vegetarian options)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, peeled and sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat.
  2. Sauté the garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken stock, water, soy sauce, and mirin.
  4. Bring the broth to a simmer and let it cook for 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Strain before serving.

2. Tonkotsu (Pork Bone) Broth

Tonkotsu ramen, famed for its creamy and rich texture, is made by boiling pork bones for hours to release their collagen and marrow.

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 pounds pork bones (cleaned and rinsed)
  • 12 cups water
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 head of garlic, halved
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons salt

Instructions:

  1. Place the pork bones in a large pot of water. Bring to a boil for 10 minutes and then discard the water to remove impurities.
  2. Add fresh water to the pot along with the onion, garlic, and ginger.
  3. Simmer uncovered for 6-8 hours, occasionally skimming off any foam.
  4. Strain the broth through a fine sieve and season with salt.

3. Miso Broth

Miso ramen, originating from Hokkaido, utilizes fermented soybean paste to create a nutty, umami-packed base.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 3 tablespoons red miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon white miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili paste (optional, for heat)

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a pot and sauté the garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add both types of miso paste and stir to dissolve.
  3. Pour in the stock and whisk until the miso is fully incorporated.
  4. Simmer for 10 minutes and adjust seasoning as needed.

Step 2: Mastering the Tare (Flavor Base)

The tare is the concentrated flavoring agent added to the broth right before assembling your ramen. It ensures the broth has depth and balance. Each type of broth pairs with a different tare:

  • Shoyu tare: Combine soy sauce, sake, mirin, and a touch of sugar.
  • Miso tare: Mix miso paste, sesame paste, and a splash of chili oil for extra richness.
  • Shio (salt) tare: Stir together sea salt, kombu (dried kelp), and a dash of lemon juice for brightness.

Step 3: Perfecting the Noodles

The noodles in ramen are just as important as the broth. Chewy, springy, and flavorful noodles elevate the dish, and they can come fresh, frozen, or dried.

Making Fresh Noodles: If you’re feeling adventurous, fresh ramen noodles are easy to make at home with just a few ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups bread flour (high-protein flour)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2/3 cup warm water

Instructions:

  1. Mix the baking soda, salt, and warm water until dissolved. Slowly incorporate the mixture into the flour until it forms a shaggy dough.
  2. Knead the dough for 10 minutes until smooth, wrap it in plastic, and let it rest for one hour.
  3. Roll the dough thinly and cut into noodles using a pasta or knife. Boil the noodles for 2-3 minutes before adding them to your ramen bowl.

If fresh noodles feel too time-intensive, many Asian grocery stores carry high-quality pre-packaged ramen noodles that can be boiled and used straight away.

Step 4: Selecting Toppings

Ramen toppings allow you to customize your bowl and add layers of flavor and texture. Here are some popular options:

  • Soft-boiled eggs (ajitsuke tamago): Marinate boiled eggs in a mix of soy sauce, mirin, and water for a couple of hours or overnight.
  • Chashu pork: Braised slices of pork belly cooked with soy sauce, sake, and sugar melt in your mouth.
  • Seaweed (nori): A classic topping that adds a briny flavor.
  • Corn and butter: This Hokkaido specialty enriches the ramen with sweetness and creaminess.
  • Bamboo shoots (menma): Fermented bamboo provides an earthy taste and crunchy texture.
  • Scallions: Thinly sliced green onions add freshness and a pop of color.

Step 5: Assembling Your Ramen

Once you’ve prepared the broth, tare, noodles, and toppings, it’s time to assemble your ramen bowl.

  1. Place a couple of tablespoons of tare at the bottom of the bowl.
  2. Ladle in the hot broth, mixing it with the tare to distribute the flavor.
  3. Add the noodles, ensuring they sit just under the broth but are visible.
  4. Arrange the toppings artfully over the noodles.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the fruits of your labor.

Bonus Tips for Ramen Success

  1. Make the broth ahead of time: Broth improves with a day or two in the fridge as the flavors meld. It also cuts down on stress at dinner time.
  2. Invest in a noodle strainer or basket: This makes portioning out boiled noodles easier and ensures even cooking.
  3. Experiment with spice: Try adding chili oil, rayu, or gochujang (Korean chili paste) to your bowl for some heat.

Ramen, at its heart, is about personalization and exploration. With the knowledge of these ramen-making basics, you have the tools to customize your bowl to your tastes, from the richness of the broth to the toppings that adorn it. Cooking delicious ramen from scratch is a rewarding process that promises not only a heartwarming bowl but also the satisfaction of creating something truly handmade. Step away from the instant ramen and dive into the craft of real ramen — your taste buds will thank you.
