Indian Palak Paneer Tikka Recipe with Spinach and Cheese


Indian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and diverse regional specialties. One dish that has gained immense popularity worldwide is Palak Paneer Tikka, a creamy and tangy spinach-based curry with marinated paneer (Indian cheese). In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of making Palak Paneer Tikka with a twist – adding a layer of cheese to the traditional spinach sauce.

Marinating the Paneer

The journey begins with preparing the paneer, which is a crucial component of this recipe. To make the paneer more tender and flavorful, we’ll marinate it in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper. These spices will not only enhance the flavor of the paneer but also help to tenderize it.

Preparing the Spinach Sauce

While the paneer is marinating, we’ll prepare the spinach sauce. Fresh spinach leaves will be blended with garlic, ginger, green chilies, and a pinch of salt to create a smooth and creamy paste. This sauce will serve as the base for the Palak Paneer Tikka.

Adding the Cheese

The next step involves adding a layer of cheese to the spinach sauce. We’ll use a blend of mozzarella and ricotta cheese to create a creamy and velvety texture. The cheese will not only add richness to the dish but also help to balance the flavors.

Cooking the Palak Paneer Tikka

Once the spinach sauce and cheese are ready, it’s time to cook the Palak Paneer Tikka. The marinated paneer will be grilled to perfection and then simmered in the spinach sauce with the added cheese. The sauce will thicken slightly, coating the paneer in a rich and creamy glaze.

Final Touches

The Palak Paneer Tikka is now ready to be served. We’ll garnish it with fresh cilantro leaves and a dollop of raita (a yogurt-based side dish) to balance the spiciness. This dish is perfect for a special occasion or a casual dinner with family and friends.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cut into small cubes
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, minced
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Raita (yogurt-based side dish), for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper. Add the paneer cubes and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the spinach leaves, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Cook until the spinach is wilted, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the mozzarella and ricotta cheese to the pan and stir well to combine. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the sauce is smooth. Season with salt to taste.
  4. Remove the paneer from the marinade, allowing any excess to drip off. Grill the paneer until it’s golden brown and slightly charred.
  5. Add the grilled paneer to the spinach sauce and simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  6. Serve the Palak Paneer Tikka hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves and a dollop of raita.

This Indian Palak Paneer Tikka Recipe with Spinach and Cheese is a unique and flavorful twist on the traditional Palak Paneer. The addition of cheese adds a richness and creaminess to the dish, making it a perfect combination of flavors and textures.
