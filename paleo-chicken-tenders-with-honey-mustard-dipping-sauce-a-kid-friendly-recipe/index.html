Paleo Chicken Tenders with Honey Mustard Dipping Sauce: A Kid-Friendly Recipe


Are you tired of serving the same old chicken tenders to your kids, only to have them complain about the breading and processed ingredients? Look no further! This recipe for Paleo Chicken Tenders with Honey Mustard Dipping Sauce is a game-changer. Not only are they easy to make, but they’re also a healthier alternative to traditional chicken tenders. Plus, the honey mustard dipping sauce is a kid-friendly favorite.

Ingredients

For the chicken tenders:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into strips
  • 1/2 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika

For the honey mustard dipping sauce:

  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken Tenders

In a shallow dish, mix together the coconut flour, almond flour, and a pinch of salt. In a separate dish, whisk together the melted coconut oil, eggs, and a pinch of salt. Dip each chicken strip into the egg mixture, then coat in the coconut flour mixture, pressing gently to adhere. Place the coated chicken tenders on a plate or tray.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken Tenders

Heat a large skillet or sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add 1-2 tablespoons of coconut oil to the pan. When the oil is hot, add 3-4 chicken tenders to the pan (do not overcrowd). Cook for 5-6 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through and reaches an internal temperature of 165°F. Repeat with the remaining chicken tenders.

Step 3: Make the Honey Mustard Dipping Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, and mayonnaise until smooth. Stir in the chopped parsley and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 4: Serve

Serve the Paleo Chicken Tenders hot with the Honey Mustard Dipping Sauce. You can also add some fresh fruit or a side salad for a well-rounded meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra crispy coating, try adding some chopped nuts or seeds to the coconut flour mixture.
  • If your kids prefer a spicier dipping sauce, you can add some hot sauce or red pepper flakes to the honey mustard mixture.
  • To make this recipe more substantial, serve the chicken tenders with some roasted vegetables or a side of quinoa or brown rice.

Conclusion

This recipe for Paleo Chicken Tenders with Honey Mustard Dipping Sauce is a kid-friendly favorite that’s easy to make and packed with nutrients. By using coconut flour and almond flour, we’re able to create a crispy coating without the need for processed breadcrumbs. And with the honey mustard dipping sauce, we’re adding a sweet and tangy flavor that kids will love. Give this recipe a try and see how your kids react!
