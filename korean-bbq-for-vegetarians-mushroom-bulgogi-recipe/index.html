Korean BBQ for Vegetarians: Mushroom Bulgogi Recipe


Are you a vegetarian who loves the bold flavors and sizzling excitement of Korean BBQ? Look no further than this Mushroom Bulgogi recipe! Bursting with umami and packed with wholesome ingredients, this vegetarian twist on a Korean classic will surely satisfy your cravings. So, fire up your grill and get ready for a mouthwatering adventure!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of fresh mushrooms (such as shiitake or cremini)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional garnish: sesame seeds and sliced green onions

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the marinade. In a bowl, whisk together the minced garlic, sesame oil, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice wine vinegar, grated ginger, and gochujang. Set the marinade aside for later.

  2. Clean the mushrooms thoroughly and remove any dirt or debris. Slice the mushrooms into thin, even pieces. This will ensure that they cook evenly and absorb all the delicious flavors.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced mushrooms, thinly sliced onion, and the marinade. Toss everything together until the mushrooms and onions are well-coated. Allow the mixture to marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours, in the refrigerator. This will allow the flavors to meld together beautifully.

  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you don’t have a grill, a stovetop grill pan or a regular frying pan will work just fine.

  5. Drizzle a bit of vegetable oil onto the grill grates or frying pan to prevent sticking. Once the grill is hot, carefully place the marinated mushroom and onion mixture onto the grill. Cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the mushrooms are tender and slightly charred. Be sure to flip them gently to avoid breaking apart the mushrooms.

  6. Once cooked to perfection, remove the mushrooms from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. This will allow the flavors to settle and intensify.

  7. Serve your Mushroom Bulgogi hot, garnished with sesame seeds and sliced green onions if desired. This dish pairs perfectly with steamed rice, kimchi, and a side of fresh lettuce leaves for wrapping.

Get Creative!

Feel free to experiment with different types of mushrooms to find your favorite combination. Portobello mushrooms can add a meaty texture, while oyster mushrooms bring a delicate and slightly sweet flavor. You can also add thinly sliced bell peppers or carrots to the marinade for an extra crunch and burst of color.

Final Thoughts

This Mushroom Bulgogi recipe is a fantastic vegetarian alternative to traditional Korean BBQ. The mushrooms soak up the savory marinade, creating a delightful umami experience in every bite. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, this dish will leave you completely satisfied.

So, gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and enjoy the flavors of Korea with this delectable Mushroom Bulgogi. It’s a surefire way to impress your loved ones and introduce them to the wonders of vegetarian Korean cuisine. Happy grilling!
