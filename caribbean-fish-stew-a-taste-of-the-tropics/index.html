Caribbean Fish Stew: A Taste of the Tropics


If you’re looking to add a touch of the tropics to your dinner table, look no further than Caribbean Fish Stew. Bursting with flavors and vibrant colors, this dish is a true reflection of the Caribbean cuisine. The combination of fresh fish, aromatic spices, and tropical ingredients creates a symphony of taste that will transport you to a sunny paradise. So, let’s dive into this recipe and bring a taste of the Caribbean to your kitchen!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of fresh fish fillets (snapper, grouper, or mahi-mahi)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, seeded and minced (optional for a spicy kick)
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 2 cups of fish or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing your fish fillets. Rinse them under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Cut the fillets into bite-sized pieces and set aside.

  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the sliced bell peppers to the pot and cook for another 3-4 minutes until they begin to soften. If you like it spicy, this is the perfect time to add the minced scotch bonnet pepper.

  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, paprika, ground cumin, thyme leaves, and cilantro. Cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes release their juices and the spices become fragrant.

  5. Pour in the coconut milk and fish or vegetable broth, and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Carefully add the fish fillets to the pot, making sure they are submerged in the liquid. Reduce the heat to low and let the stew simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and tender.

  7. Once the fish is cooked, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if needed. Remember, Caribbean cuisine is all about bold and vibrant flavors, so feel free to add more spices or herbs according to your taste preferences.

  8. Serve the Caribbean Fish Stew hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves. This dish pairs perfectly with steamed rice or crusty bread to soak up all the delicious flavors.

Dive into the Caribbean Delight

Caribbean Fish Stew is a delightful culinary journey that brings the exotic flavors of the tropics to your plate. The combination of fresh fish, aromatic spices, and creamy coconut milk creates a harmonious blend of flavors that is hard to resist. Whether you’re a seafood lover or simply looking to spice up your dinner routine, this recipe is sure to impress. So, grab your apron, gather the ingredients, and embark on a flavorful adventure with Caribbean Fish Stew. Bon appétit!

Please note: This recipe can be customized to suit your taste preferences. Feel free to add other vegetables such as okra or carrots, or experiment with different types of fish. The key is to embrace the vibrant and bold flavors of the Caribbean cuisine and make it your own.
