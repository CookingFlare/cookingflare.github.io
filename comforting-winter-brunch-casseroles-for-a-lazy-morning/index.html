Comforting Winter Brunch Casseroles for a Lazy Morning


Winter mornings call for cozy and comforting meals that warm both the body and soul. And what better way to start a lazy morning than with a delicious brunch casserole? These easy-to-make dishes are perfect for feeding a crowd or simply indulging in a lazy day breakfast. In this blog post, we’ll explore three comforting winter brunch casserole recipes that will make your mornings extra special. So, grab your favorite blanket, pour yourself a hot cup of coffee, and let’s dive into these mouthwatering recipes!

1. Cheesy Hash Brown Casserole

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 package frozen hash browns
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 can cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the hash browns, shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, cream of chicken soup, melted butter, diced onion, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish.
  4. Bake for 45 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is bubbly.
  5. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

2. Sausage and Egg Breakfast Casserole

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage
  • 6 slices bread, cubed
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon ground mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a skillet, cook the breakfast sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess grease.
  3. In the prepared baking dish, layer the cubed bread, cooked sausage, and shredded cheddar cheese.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, ground mustard, salt, and pepper. Pour the mixture over the ingredients in the baking dish.
  5. Cover the dish with foil and let it sit in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  6. Bake the casserole, covered, for 45 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for another 15 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is set.
  7. Allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

3. Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole

Prep Time: 25 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Total Time: 1 hour 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf French bread, cubed
  • 4 cups sliced apples
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cubed French bread, sliced apples, brown sugar, melted butter, and ground cinnamon. Toss until well coated.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Spread the bread and apple mixture evenly in the prepared baking dish. Pour the egg mixture over the top.
  5. Press down gently with a fork to ensure all the bread is soaked in the egg mixture.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is set.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

These comforting winter brunch casseroles are sure to make your lazy mornings extra special. Whether you’re hosting a brunch gathering or simply treating yourself to a delicious breakfast, these recipes are easy to prepare and packed with flavor. So, next time you crave a cozy and satisfying morning meal, give one of these casseroles a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
