How to Make a Sweet Flatbread with Cinnamon and Sugar


If you’re looking for a delicious, easy-to-make treat that fills your kitchen with a warm, inviting aroma, look no further than sweet flatbread with cinnamon and sugar. This soft, lightly crispy flatbread is the perfect snack or dessert, whether you’re craving something sweet in the afternoon or need a quick treat to impress your guests. The combination of warm cinnamon and sugary sweetness creates a comforting dessert that’s bound to become a household favorite. Best of all, it requires only a few simple ingredients and a bit of your time in the kitchen.

In this post, we will guide you through the process of creating this mouthwatering flatbread step by step, from preparing the dough to the final sprinkle of cinnamon sugar. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned home baker, you’ll find this recipe simple, rewarding, and incredibly tasty.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this cinnamon sugar flatbread, you won’t need any complex or hard-to-find ingredients. Most of these items are probably already in your pantry! Here’s what you’ll need:

  • For the dough:

    • 2 cups of all-purpose flour (you can also use whole wheat for a heartier texture)
    • 1/4 cup of warm water (about 110°F/43°C)
    • 2 tablespoons of sugar (this helps activate the yeast and adds a little sweetness to the dough)
    • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
    • 1 tablespoon of active dry yeast
    • 3 tablespoons of olive oil (or melted butter, for a richer flavor)

  • For the cinnamon sugar topping:

    • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
    • 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon
    • 1 tablespoon of melted butter (to brush on the flatbread before adding the cinnamon sugar)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Sweet Flatbread with Cinnamon and Sugar

1. Preparing the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water and sugar. Sprinkle the active dry yeast over the top, and give it a gentle stir. Allow the mixture to sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This step is essential to make sure your yeast is alive and capable of helping your dough rise.

Once the yeast is activated, in a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a small well in the center, and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Stir everything together until a rough dough forms.

2. Kneading the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and begin kneading. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour as needed. On the other hand, if it feels too dry, add small splashes of water until it reaches the right consistency.

Once the dough has been kneaded, shape it into a ball and place it back in the bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and allow it to rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it has doubled in size. The warmth encourages the yeast to work its magic, creating a fluffy and airy dough.

3. Rolling Out the Dough

Once the dough has risen, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and punch it down to remove the air. Divide the dough into equal portions, depending on the size of flatbread you want to make. You can make smaller individual flatbreads or one large flatbread. Using a rolling pin, roll each portion of dough into a thin circle or oval shape, about 1/4 inch thick.

4. Cooking the Flatbread

To cook the flatbread, you can either use a griddle, a cast-iron skillet, or a flat baking sheet. Preheat your skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. If you’re using a skillet or griddle, no oil is necessary, as the dough will cook dry, developing a light, crispy texture. However, if you’re using a baking sheet, lightly grease it with oil or cooking spray.

Place your rolled dough onto the preheated skillet or griddle and cook for 2-3 minutes on one side, until the surface begins to bubble slightly and the underside is golden brown. Flip the flatbread and cook the other side for another 2-3 minutes, until both sides are golden and crispy.

Remove the flatbread from the skillet or griddle and set it aside to cool slightly.

5. Preparing the Cinnamon Sugar Topping

While the flatbread is cooling, melt the butter in a small bowl. In another bowl, mix together the cinnamon and sugar. The cinnamon-sugar topping is the secret to transforming this flatbread into a sweet indulgence.

Once the flatbread has cooled enough to handle, brush one side with the melted butter. Generously sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar mixture over the buttered side, ensuring that every inch of the flatbread is covered with that sugary, cinnamon goodness.

6. Serving the Sweet Flatbread

Once you’ve added the cinnamon sugar topping, slice the flatbread into wedges, similar to how you would slice a pizza. The flatbread can be served warm or at room temperature, making it a versatile treat for any occasion.

Sweet flatbread with cinnamon and sugar is perfect on its own or can be paired with a variety of dips and sides. If you like, serve it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream to make it even more decadent.

Tips for Perfecting Your Sweet Flatbread

  • Use high-quality cinnamon: The quality of the cinnamon you use can make a big difference in the flavor. Opt for Ceylon cinnamon if possible, as it has a more delicate, sweet flavor compared to the more common Cassia cinnamon.

  • Don’t overcook the flatbread: Since the flatbread cooks quickly, be sure to keep an eye on it while it’s in the skillet or on the griddle. Overcooking can lead to a dry flatbread, so take it off the heat as soon as it’s golden and crispy.

  • Experiment with toppings: While cinnamon and sugar are a classic combination, you can get creative with your toppings. Try adding chopped nuts, a drizzle of honey, or even a sprinkle of chocolate chips for extra flavor.

  • Make it gluten-free: If you need a gluten-free option, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend. Just make sure the flour you choose has a good structure to hold the dough together.

  • Reheat for a crispy finish: If you have leftover flatbread, you can easily reheat it by placing it back on the griddle or in a skillet for a minute or two. This will bring back the crispy texture while warming up the cinnamon sugar topping.

Why This Recipe Works

This sweet flatbread with cinnamon and sugar works so well because of its simplicity and the balance between the rich, fluffy dough and the sweet, spiced topping. The dough itself is easy to prepare and doesn’t require hours of rising time like traditional bread, making it a fast and fun option for baking with little effort.

The combination of the crisped-up dough with the melt-in-your-mouth cinnamon-sugar topping creates a perfect harmony of textures and flavors. The sweet sugar contrasts beautifully with the savory notes of the olive oil in the dough, while the cinnamon adds warmth and complexity. The result is a treat that’s both indulgent and comforting, with just the right amount of sweetness.

The Versatility of Sweet Flatbread

One of the reasons this recipe is so fantastic is its versatility. You can easily adapt it to fit your mood or your dietary preferences. If you’re a fan of fruit, consider topping the flatbread with sliced apples, pears, or berries before sprinkling on the cinnamon sugar. You can also experiment with adding a little vanilla extract to the dough or a pinch of nutmeg for extra flavor.

For an even more luxurious treat, consider drizzling a cream cheese glaze over the top once the flatbread has cooled. You could also serve it alongside your favorite hot beverage, such as tea, coffee, or hot chocolate, making it the ideal choice for brunch, dessert, or a cozy afternoon snack.

Final Thoughts

Making sweet flatbread with cinnamon and sugar is a rewarding experience, and the result is a delicious, comforting treat that’s sure to please everyone. Whether you’re preparing it for a family gathering, a special occasion, or just a simple snack, this recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. With just a few basic ingredients and simple steps, you can create a warm, sweet indulgence that’s perfect for any time of day.

Give this recipe a try the next time you’re craving something sweet, and you’ll see just how easy and satisfying homemade flatbread can be. Happy baking!
