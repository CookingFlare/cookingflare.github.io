10 Budget-Friendly Dinner Recipes Under $10


Finding delicious, nutritious meals that don’t break the bank can feel like a challenge, especially when time is tight and you’re working within a limited budget. But the truth is, you don’t need a huge grocery bill to put together flavorful and satisfying meals for yourself or your family. With a little planning, creativity, and a focus on simple ingredients, you can whip up amazing dinners that cost less than $10. Here are ten budget-friendly recipes that prove eating well doesn’t have to drain your wallet.

1. Hearty Lentil and Vegetable Soup

Lentils are an inexpensive and versatile pantry staple packed with protein and fiber. This hearty lentil and vegetable soup is perfect for cold nights or whenever you need a quick, easy dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dry lentils (red or green)
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 medium carrots, sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • Spices: salt, pepper, cumin, and paprika

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onion, garlic, carrots, and celery in a splash of oil for 5 minutes.
  2. Add broth, lentils, diced tomatoes, and spices. Bring to a boil.
  3. Simmer for 25-30 minutes until lentils are tender.
  4. Serve with a slice of crusty bread or over cooked rice for a heartier meal.

2. Homemade Veggie Fried Rice

Transform leftover rice and odds-and-ends veggies into a quick, delicious fried rice dish. It’s a great way to use what you have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably chilled)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (frozen peas, carrots, or whatever you have)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Optional: green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Scramble eggs in a pan, then set aside.
  2. Heat oil in the pan, add garlic, and sauté.
  3. Add mixed veggies and cook for 5-6 minutes. Add rice.
  4. Mix in soy sauce and scrambled eggs. Stir until well-combined.
  5. Garnish with green onions, if available.

3. Sloppy Joe Sweet Potatoes

A fun twist on a classic that keeps the cost down by using sweet potatoes as the base instead of buns.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes
  • 1/2 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • Spices: salt, pepper, smoked paprika

Instructions:

  1. Bake sweet potatoes at 400°F (200°C) for 40-50 minutes, or until tender.
  2. In a skillet, sauté onion, then add ground meat. Cook until browned.
  3. Stir in tomato sauce, Worcestershire sauce, and seasonings. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Cut the sweet potatoes in half and top with the sloppy joe mixture.

4. Black Bean and Corn Tacos

These tacos are simple, fresh, and incredibly budget-friendly thanks to inexpensive canned goods.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup frozen or canned corn
  • 8 small tortillas
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Optional toppings: salsa, shredded cheese, or cilantro

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, combine black beans, corn, chili powder, and cumin. Heat until warmed through.
  2. Warm tortillas in a separate pan.
  3. Spoon the bean mixture into the tortillas and add any desired toppings.

5. Cabbage Stir-Fry with Noodles

Cabbage is incredibly affordable and surprisingly versatile. This quick stir-fry pairs it with noodles for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 head of cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 8 oz spaghetti or rice noodles
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add garlic, cabbage, and carrot. Stir-fry for 5-6 minutes.
  3. Add soy sauce and cooked noodles. Toss until fully mixed.

6. Baked Ziti

This classic pasta bake is the ultimate comfort food, and you can make it cheaply with just a few ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz ziti or penne pasta
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • 1 cup ricotta or cottage cheese (optional)
  • Spices: Italian seasoning, garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions and drain.
  2. Mix pasta with marinara sauce and ricotta, if using.
  3. Layer in a baking dish: pasta mixture, mozzarella, and Parmesan.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes, until bubbly.

7. Stuffed Bell Peppers

Stuffed bell peppers are a colorful and delicious way to use grains and leftovers creatively.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, tops removed and seeded
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • Spices: salt, pepper, garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Sauté onion and ground meat until browned.
  3. Mix rice, tomato sauce, and spices with the cooked meat.
  4. Stuff the peppers with the mixture and place them in a baking dish with 1/2 cup water at the bottom.
  5. Bake 30-40 minutes until the peppers are soft.

8. Vegetarian Chili

Chili is hearty, flavorful, and easy to customize with pantry staples.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1 onion, diced
  • Spices: chili powder, cumin, garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté onion with spices.
  2. Add beans, tomatoes, and corn. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Serve on its own or with a side of cornbread.

9. Eggplant Parmesan

A delicious vegetarian option that’s full of flavor without a hefty price tag.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium eggplant
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 1 egg
  • Spices: Italian seasoning, garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Slice eggplant into rounds and dip in beaten egg, then breadcrumbs.
  2. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 15 minutes until crisp.
  3. Layer baked eggplant with marinara and mozzarella in a baking dish.
  4. Bake for another 15 minutes until bubbly.

10. Chicken and Potato Sheet Pan Dinner

Simple, hearty, and affordable, this sheet pan meal pairs juicy chicken thighs with roasted potatoes and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in chicken thighs
  • 4 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 2 cups chopped vegetables (carrots, zucchini, etc.)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Spices: salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss potatoes and vegetables with oil and spices.
  3. Place chicken thighs and vegetables on a sheet pan.
  4. Roast for 35-45 minutes, flipping halfway through.

These ten wallet-friendly recipes prove that delicious dinners don’t have to cost a fortune. With a little planning and some basic ingredients, you can create meals that are big on flavor, easy to prepare, and perfect for any weeknight. Keep these ideas on hand for those nights when you’re looking for something affordable yet satisfying. Happy cooking!
