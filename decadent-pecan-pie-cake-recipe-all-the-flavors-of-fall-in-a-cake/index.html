Decadent Pecan Pie Cake Recipe: All the Flavors of Fall in a Cake


As the leaves turn vibrant shades of amber and crimson, and the air becomes crisp with the promise of winter, it’s time to embrace the flavors of fall. One of the most beloved autumn treats is pecan pie, a classic dessert that evokes memories of family gatherings and cozy evenings. But what if you could take the essence of this traditional pie and transform it into a cake? Enter the decadent pecan pie cake—a dessert that captures all the sweet, nutty goodness of pecan pie, layered into a moist, flavorful cake. Let’s embark on a culinary journey that promises to delight your taste buds and impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

To create the perfect pecan pie cake, you’ll need a mix of simple yet flavorful ingredients. Here’s what you’ll require:

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

For the Pecan Pie Filling:

  • 1 cup corn syrup (light or dark, depending on your preference)
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1½ cups whole pecans

For the Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Chopped pecans for garnish

Equipment Needed

Before you get started, gather the following tools to streamline your baking process:

  • Mixing bowls
  • Electric mixer
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Rubber spatula
  • 9-inch round cake pans (2)
  • Cooling rack
  • Saucepan for the filling
  • Piping bag (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cake

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C) and greasing your cake pans with butter or non-stick spray.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk them together until well blended.

  3. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In another bowl, use an electric mixer to cream the softened butter and granulated sugar until it becomes light and fluffy. This typically takes about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Incorporate the Eggs and Vanilla: Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Then, stir in the vanilla extract.

  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet mixture, alternating with the buttermilk. Begin and end with the flour mixture. Mix until just combined.

  6. Fold in the Pecans: Gently fold in the chopped pecans, ensuring they’re evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  7. Pour and Bake: Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Once baked, allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring them to a cooling rack to cool completely.

Step 2: Prepare the Pecan Pie Filling

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the corn syrup, brown sugar, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth and thoroughly combined.

  2. Add Whole Pecans: Gently fold in the whole pecans, ensuring they are evenly coated in the filling mixture.

  3. Cook the Filling: Cook the mixture over medium heat until it begins to thicken, about 5-7 minutes. Be careful not to let it boil. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

Step 3: Assemble the Cake

  1. Layer the Cakes: Once the cakes have cooled, place one layer on a serving plate. Spread a generous amount of the pecan pie filling over the top, allowing some to spill over the edges for a rustic look.

  2. Add the Second Layer: Place the second cake layer on top of the first and top it with more pecan pie filling.

  3. Frost the Cake: In a mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract, mixing until the frosting is smooth and fluffy. Frost the top and sides of the cake, smoothing it out as you go.

  4. Garnish: Sprinkle chopped pecans on the top of the cake for an added crunch and visual appeal.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Slice the cake and serve it as is, or pair it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. This decadent pecan pie cake is perfect for fall gatherings, holiday celebrations, or simply as a comforting treat to enjoy at home.

Tips for the Perfect Pecan Pie Cake

  • Use Fresh Pecans: For the best flavor and texture, use fresh pecans. If possible, toast them in the oven for a few minutes to enhance their nuttiness.

  • Let the Cake Cool Completely: Ensure that your cakes are completely cooled before adding the frosting and pecan pie filling. This will help prevent melting and ensure a clean presentation.

  • Make It Ahead: This cake can be made a day in advance. In fact, the flavors meld beautifully when it sits overnight. Just store it in an airtight container in the fridge.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to add your twist to the cake. A pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg in the cake batter can add a warm spice note that complements the pecans beautifully.

Conclusion

The decadent pecan pie cake is a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of fall, combining the best elements of a classic dessert into a moist, rich cake. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply indulging in a sweet treat at home, this cake is sure to impress. With its layers of flavor, rich frosting, and generous pecan pie filling, it captures the essence of autumn in every bite. So, gather your ingredients, embrace your inner baker, and treat yourself to this scrumptious pecan pie cake. Your taste buds will thank you!
