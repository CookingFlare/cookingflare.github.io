How to Make Homemade Dijon Mustard from Scratch


Crafting your own homemade Dijon mustard is a journey into the art of condiments that will transform your kitchen game. With a combination of simple ingredients, a touch of patience, and a bit of technique, you can create a deliciously complex mustard that adds flavor to everything from sandwiches to salad dressings. Not only is it easy to prepare, but it’s also an incredibly rewarding project for anyone who loves making things from scratch.

What Makes Dijon Mustard Unique?

Dijon mustard originated in the Dijon region of France, earning its name from this culinary capital. What sets it apart from other mustards is its distinct tangy and sharp flavor, as well as its smooth, creamy consistency. Traditional Dijon mustard is made using brown or black mustard seeds and verjuice—a tart juice made from unripe grapes—though modern versions often use white wine or white wine vinegar. These ingredients come together to create a condiment that’s not just a flavor enhancer but a statement of sophistication on your plate.

Making Dijon mustard at home allows you to control the quality of ingredients, adjust the flavor to your liking, and perhaps most importantly, skip unnecessary additives and preservatives found in many store-bought varieties. Let’s dive into the details and learn how to create this classic pantry staple in your own kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of homemade Dijon mustard lies in its simplicity. Here’s what you’ll need to get started:

Core Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mustard seeds (a mix of yellow and brown for balance)
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (like a Sauvignon Blanc)
  • 1/4 cup white wine vinegar (for that iconic tang)
  • 2–3 tablespoons water (to adjust consistency)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (to enhance flavor)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making Dijon mustard from scratch involves a simple process of soaking, blending, and resting. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Soak the Mustard Seeds

  • Combine the mustard seeds, white wine, and white wine vinegar in a small bowl.
  • Stir to ensure the seeds are evenly coated with the liquids.
  • Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid, and let the mixture sit at room temperature for 24–48 hours. This soaking period softens the seeds and mellows the bitterness, allowing the flavors to develop into the smooth taste we associate with Dijon mustard.

Step 2: Blend the Mixture

  • Transfer the soaked mustard seeds and their liquid into a blender or food processor.
  • Add the salt and honey (if using) to the mixture. Begin blending at a low speed, gradually increasing as the seeds start to break down.
  • Continue blending until you achieve your desired consistency. Some people prefer a coarser mustard with visible seed flecks, while others like it ultra-smooth.

Step 3: Adjust Consistency

  • If the mustard is too thick, gradually add water, one tablespoon at a time, blending or stirring in between until the desired consistency is reached. Keep in mind that the mustard will thicken further as it rests, so don’t be afraid to make it slightly thinner than you think you need.

Step 4: Let It Rest

  • Once blended, transfer the mustard to a sterilized glass jar or an airtight container.
  • Allow the mustard to rest for at least 24 hours (and up to a week) in the refrigerator before using. This resting period allows the flavors to mature and settle, transforming the sharpness into a more balanced profile.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Dijon Mustard

  1. Experiment with Seed Ratios: Using a mix of yellow and brown mustard seeds creates a mustard that’s both flavorful and versatile. Brown seeds contribute a deeper, spicier flavor, while yellow seeds offer a milder, smoother note.

  2. Try Different Liquids: While white wine and white wine vinegar are traditional, you can experiment with other acids like apple cider vinegar or lemon juice to create unique variations. For a non-alcoholic option, substitute the wine with additional vinegar or water.

  3. Play with Sweetness: The addition of honey or maple syrup is entirely optional, but a hint of sweetness can beautifully offset the mustard’s tanginess. Adjust this to suit your personal taste or omit it altogether for a more traditional approach.

  4. Be Patient: Freshly made mustard can taste overly sharp right after blending. Resting it in the fridge softens its edges and enhances its complexity. Resist the urge to skip this step—your taste buds will thank you.

  5. Store Properly: Homemade Dijon mustard stores well in the refrigerator for up to 2–3 months. Use a sterilized jar to keep it fresh for as long as possible.

Using Your Homemade Dijon Mustard

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making Dijon mustard, the possibilities for using it are practically endless. Here are a few ways to incorporate your creation into your cooking:

  • Salad Dressings: Whisk together your mustard with olive oil, vinegar, and a touch of honey for a quick and flavorful vinaigrette.
  • Marinades: Combine it with garlic, herbs, and lemon juice to create a marinade that infuses meats and vegetables with a zingy, aromatic flavor.
  • Sandwiches and Wraps: Spread a generous layer onto sandwiches or wraps to elevate even the simplest of recipes.
  • Mustard Crusted Meats: Coat chicken, pork, or fish in your Dijon mustard before adding breadcrumbs and baking for a golden, crunchy crust.
  • Dipping Sauce: Mix with mayonnaise or yogurt for a creamy dip perfect for roasted vegetables or pretzels.

Why Make Dijon Mustard at Home?

The satisfaction that comes with creating something from scratch cannot be overstated. When you make Dijon mustard at home, you gain full control over the ingredients, ensuring a tastier and healthier end product. Moreover, it’s a completely customizable process—you can make the mustard milder or spicier, sweeter or more acidic, depending on your preferences.

Additionally, homemade Dijon mustard is almost always more cost-effective than store-bought gourmet versions. With just a handful of pantry staples, you can produce batches of mustard that rival anything commercially available, all while avoiding unnecessary additives.

Final Thoughts

Making Dijon mustard at home may seem like a small endeavor, but the rewards are huge. Not only will you have a fresh, flavorful condiment to add to your culinary repertoire, but you’ll also gain a deeper appreciation for a beloved classic. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, preparing a weekday lunch, or crafting a festive charcuterie board, your homemade mustard will undoubtedly become a talking point. Once you’ve experienced the bright, tangy intensity of mustard you made yourself, you might never go back to store-bought options.

So, gather your ingredients, carve out a bit of time, and let your inner artisan shine. Making Dijon mustard from scratch is as enjoyable as it is delicious—your dishes (and taste buds) will thank you.
