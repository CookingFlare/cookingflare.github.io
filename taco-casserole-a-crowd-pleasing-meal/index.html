Taco Casserole: A Crowd-Pleasing Meal


Taco night is always a hit, but sometimes you want to switch things up a bit. That’s where this Taco Casserole comes in! It’s a delicious twist on the classic taco, perfect for feeding a crowd or meal prepping for the week. Packed with all the flavors you love, this casserole is sure to become a family favorite. Plus, it’s easy to make and can be customized to suit your taste. Let’s dive into the recipe and get ready to impress your friends and family with this mouthwatering dish!

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 cup salsa
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup crushed tortilla chips
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with olive oil and set aside.

  2. In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and red bell pepper, and sauté until softened.

  3. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Once the beef is cooked through, add the taco seasoning and stir to coat the meat and vegetables evenly.

  5. Stir in the black beans and corn, and cook for another 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through.

  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the salsa and sour cream until well combined.

  7. Transfer the beef mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.

  8. Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese over the top of the beef mixture, followed by the crushed tortilla chips.

  9. Cover the dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes.

  10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  11. Once done, remove the casserole from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.

  12. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot, with additional salsa and sour cream on the side if desired.

Tips for Customization:

  • Feel free to add your favorite toppings such as sliced jalapeños, diced tomatoes, or even a dollop of guacamole.
  • For a vegetarian version, swap the ground beef for a meatless crumble or additional veggies like zucchini and mushrooms.

Serving Suggestions:

This Taco Casserole is a complete meal on its own, but you can also serve it with a side of Mexican rice, a fresh green salad, or some homemade guacamole and tortilla chips.

Conclusion:

This Taco Casserole is a versatile and tasty dish that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a casual dinner party, want to meal prep for the week, or just craving a comforting and flavorful meal, this casserole has got you covered. It’s easy to make, customizable, and sure to please even the pickiest eaters. So, give this recipe a try and watch it become a staple in your kitchen! Enjoy!
