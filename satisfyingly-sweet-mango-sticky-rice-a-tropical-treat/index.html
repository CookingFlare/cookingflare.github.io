Satisfyingly Sweet Mango Sticky Rice: A Tropical Treat


If you’re looking for a dessert that transports you to a tropical paradise with every bite, then mango sticky rice is the answer. This Thai delicacy combines the creaminess of coconut-infused sticky rice with the sweetness of ripe mangoes, resulting in a dessert that’s both satisfying and refreshing. Whether you’re a fan of Asian cuisine or simply love indulging in sweet treats, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Sticky Rice:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt

For the Mango Topping:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • A pinch of salt
  • Sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Soak the Rice

  1. Rinse the glutinous rice in cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Place the rice in a bowl and cover it with water. Let it soak for at least 4 hours or preferably overnight. This will ensure that the rice cooks evenly and becomes delightfully sticky.

Step 2: Steam the Rice

  1. After soaking, drain the rice and transfer it to a steamer lined with cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel.
  2. Steam the rice for 25-30 minutes, or until it becomes tender and slightly translucent.

Step 3: Prepare the Coconut Sauce

  1. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves and the sauce is smooth. Set aside.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

  1. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a bowl and pour half of the prepared coconut sauce over it. Gently fold the sauce into the rice until it’s well coated. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 5: Prepare the Mango Topping

  1. In a separate saucepan, heat the coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt over low heat.
  2. Stir until the sugar dissolves, then remove the saucepan from the heat.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  1. To serve, place a generous portion of the coconut-infused sticky rice on a plate.
  2. Arrange the sliced mangoes on top or alongside the rice.
  3. Drizzle the remaining coconut sauce over the mangoes and rice.
  4. Optionally, sprinkle sesame seeds over the dish for a delightful crunch and visual appeal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist, try adding a splash of pandan extract to the coconut sauce for a fragrant and vibrant green color.
  • If mangoes are not in season, you can substitute them with other tropical fruits such as ripe papaya or even fresh pineapple.
  • Leftover mango sticky rice can be refrigerated and enjoyed cold the next day, making it a perfect make-ahead dessert for gatherings.

Conclusion

Mango sticky rice is a delightful marriage of flavors and textures that captures the essence of tropical indulgence. Its creamy, sweet, and slightly salty profile makes it a beloved dessert not only in Thailand but around the world. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of the tropics, this recipe is guaranteed to impress and satisfy. So, grab some ripe mangoes, sticky rice, and coconut milk, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the lusciousness of mango sticky rice.
