The Perfect Pair: Wine and Taco/Burrito Pairings


Tacos and burritos are beloved dishes enjoyed by many around the world. They offer a delightful combination of flavors and textures that make them incredibly versatile. From savory meats to fresh vegetables, there are endless possibilities when it comes to fillings and toppings. But have you ever considered pairing these delicious creations with wine? In this blog post, we will explore the art of pairing wine with tacos and burritos, and discover some unexpected flavor combinations that will elevate your dining experience.

Understanding Flavor Profiles

Before we dive into the world of wine and taco/burrito pairings, it’s important to understand the concept of flavor profiles. Each wine and food item has its own unique combination of flavors, including sweetness, acidity, bitterness, and richness. When pairing wine with tacos and burritos, we want to create a harmonious balance between the flavors of the dish and the wine.

Classic Pairings

  1. Fish Tacos with Sauvignon Blanc: The light and crisp flavors of a Sauvignon Blanc complement the delicate flavors of fish tacos beautifully. The wine’s acidity cuts through the richness of the fish, while its herbal notes enhance the freshness of the dish.

  2. Beef Barbacoa Burritos with Malbec: The bold and robust flavors of a Malbec pair wonderfully with the rich and savory beef barbacoa. The wine’s tannins and dark fruit flavors add depth to the dish, creating a truly satisfying combination.

  3. Vegetarian Tacos with Pinot Grigio: For those who prefer vegetarian options, a light and refreshing Pinot Grigio is an excellent choice. Its crisp acidity and citrus notes complement the fresh flavors of the vegetables, creating a delightful balance.

  4. Spicy Chicken Tacos with Riesling: When it comes to spicy dishes, a slightly sweet and aromatic Riesling is a fantastic match. The wine’s sweetness helps to tame the heat, while its acidity and fruity flavors provide a refreshing contrast.

Unexpected Pairings

While the classic pairings mentioned above are tried and true, it’s always fun to experiment with unexpected combinations. Here are a few unconventional pairings that might surprise you:

  1. Carnitas Tacos with Chardonnay: The richness of the carnitas calls for a wine with some weight, and a buttery Chardonnay fits the bill perfectly. Its creamy texture and notes of vanilla and oak complement the succulent pork, creating a luxurious pairing.

  2. Vegan Burritos with Rosé: Don’t let the absence of meat deter you from enjoying a fantastic wine pairing. A dry and fruity Rosé works wonders with vegan burritos, as its vibrant flavors and acidity enhance the freshness of the ingredients.

  3. Shrimp Tacos with Sparkling Wine: Sparkling wine is not just for celebrations! The effervescence and acidity of a sparkling wine make it a delightful companion to shrimp tacos. The bubbles help to cleanse the palate and highlight the delicate flavors of the shrimp.

  4. Pork Belly Tacos with Syrah: The rich and indulgent flavors of pork belly call for a wine with some intensity. A bold and peppery Syrah provides the perfect balance, as its dark fruit flavors and smoky notes complement the pork belly’s richness.

Wrapping Up

Pairing wine with tacos and burritos opens up a world of exciting flavor combinations. Whether you stick to the classic pairings or venture into uncharted territory, the key is to experiment and find what works best for your taste buds. Remember, wine and food should always be enjoyed together, so don’t be afraid to have some fun and discover your own perfect pairings. Cheers to the delicious journey ahead!
