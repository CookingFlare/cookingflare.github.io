Japanese Ramen with Pork and Soft-Boiled Egg Recipe


Ramen is a beloved Japanese dish that has gained popularity all over the world. Its rich and flavorful broth, paired with tender noodles and various toppings, makes it a comforting and satisfying meal. In this recipe, we will be making a delicious bowl of Japanese ramen with succulent pork and a perfectly soft-boiled egg. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of pork belly
  • 4 large eggs
  • 6 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of mirin
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of chili paste (optional)
  • 4 portions of ramen noodles
  • 4 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 sheets of nori, cut into small strips

Instructions

  1. Preparing the Pork:

    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the pork belly. Boil for 5 minutes to remove any impurities. Drain the water and rinse the pork under cold water.
    • In the same pot, combine the chicken or vegetable broth, soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, brown sugar, and chili paste (if desired). Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.
    • Add the pork belly to the pot and simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily shreds with a fork. Remove the pork from the broth and set it aside.
    • Once the pork has cooled slightly, slice it into thin strips.

  2. Soft-Boiling the Eggs:

    • In a medium pot, bring water to a boil. Gently lower the eggs into the boiling water and cook for exactly 6 minutes.
    • After 6 minutes, carefully transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water and let them cool for a few minutes. This will stop the cooking process and make it easier to peel the eggs later.
    • Once the eggs have cooled, gently tap them on a hard surface to crack the shell. Peel the eggs, being careful not to damage the soft-boiled yolk. Set the eggs aside.

  3. Cooking the Ramen Noodles:

    • Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water to remove excess starch. Set the noodles aside.

  4. Assembling the Ramen Bowl:

    • Divide the cooked noodles into 4 serving bowls. Top each bowl with slices of the cooked pork belly, a soft-boiled egg, chopped green onions, and nori strips.
    • Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and toppings, ensuring that all ingredients are submerged in the flavorful broth.
    • Serve immediately and enjoy the comforting flavors of this homemade Japanese ramen!

Tips and Variations

  • For added flavor, you can marinate the pork belly in the broth mixture overnight before cooking it. This will infuse the meat with even more deliciousness.
  • Feel free to customize your ramen bowl with additional toppings such as corn, bean sprouts, bamboo shoots, or mushrooms.
  • If you prefer a spicier ramen, you can add more chili paste or even sprinkle some chili flakes on top.
  • If you’re vegetarian, you can substitute the pork belly with tofu or other plant-based protein options.

Ramen is a versatile dish that allows for endless creativity, so don’t be afraid to experiment with different flavors and toppings. Whether you’re a ramen enthusiast or new to this delightful cuisine, this Japanese Ramen with Pork and Soft-Boiled Egg Recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. Enjoy!
