Wholesome and Nutritious Palak Paneer Recipe

Palak Paneer is a beloved dish in Indian cuisine, known for its vibrant green color and rich flavor. This wholesome and nutritious recipe combines fresh spinach (palak) with soft, creamy paneer, creating a dish that is not only delicious but also packed with vitamins and minerals. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a beginner looking to explore Indian flavors, this Palak Paneer recipe is sure to impress. Ingredients For the Spinach Puree: 500 grams fresh spinach leaves 2 cups water (for blanching) 1 tablespoon oil (for sautéing) 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 medium onion, finely chopped 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste) 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1 medium tomato, pureed 1 teaspoon garam masala Salt to taste 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional) 2 tablespoons heavy cream (optional, for richness) For the Paneer: 250 grams paneer, cubed 1 tablespoon butter or ghee 1 teaspoon cumin seeds Fresh coriander leaves for garnish Preparation Steps Step 1: Blanching the Spinach Boil Water: In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add Spinach: Once boiling, add the fresh spinach leaves and blanch for 2-3 minutes until they wilt. Ice Bath: Immediately transfer the spinach to an ice bath (a bowl filled with cold water) to stop the cooking process and retain the vibrant green color. Drain and Blend: After a few minutes, drain the spinach and blend it into a smooth puree using a blender. Set aside. Step 2: Preparing the Base Heat Oil: In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Sauté Cumin Seeds: Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add Onions: Incorporate the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Chilies and Ginger-Garlic Paste: Stir in the chopped green chilies and ginger-garlic paste, cooking for another 2 minutes until fragrant. Tomato Puree: Add the tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from the base. Step 3: Combining Spinach and Paneer Add Spinach Puree: Pour the spinach puree into the pan and mix well with the onion-tomato base. Seasoning: Add garam masala and salt to taste. Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add Paneer: Gently fold in the cubed paneer, ensuring it is well coated with the spinach sauce. Creaminess: If you prefer a richer taste, stir in heavy cream and lemon juice. Allow the dish to simmer for an additional 5 minutes. Step 4: Final Touches Adjust Consistency: If the gravy is too thick, add a splash of water to reach your desired consistency. Garnishing: Finish with fresh coriander leaves for a burst of flavor and color. Serving Suggestions Palak Paneer is best enjoyed hot with a side of naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice. Pair it with a refreshing cucumber raita or a simple salad to balance the meal. This dish is perfect for family gatherings, special occasions, or a comforting weeknight dinner. ...
