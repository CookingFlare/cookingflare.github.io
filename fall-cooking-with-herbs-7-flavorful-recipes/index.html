Fall Cooking with Herbs: 7 Flavorful Recipes


As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, fall invites us to embrace the warmth of our kitchens. This season is not just about pumpkin spice lattes and apple pies; it’s also a time to explore the rich flavors that fresh herbs can bring to our meals. Herbs are nature’s flavor enhancers, and when combined with the hearty ingredients of fall, they create dishes that are both comforting and vibrant. In this post, we’ll explore seven flavorful recipes that celebrate the bounty of fall and the aromatic herbs that elevate them.

1. Herb-Infused Butternut Squash Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: a splash of cream for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until the onion is translucent.
  2. Cook the Squash: Add the cubed butternut squash to the pot, stirring to combine. Cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the squash to soften slightly.
  3. Add Broth and Herbs: Pour in the vegetable broth and add the thyme and sage. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  4. Blend the Soup: Using an immersion blender, puree the soup until smooth. If you prefer a chunkier texture, blend only half of the soup and mix it back in.
  5. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, drizzled with a splash of cream if desired.

This herb-infused butternut squash soup is a perfect starter for any fall gathering, warming your guests from the inside out.

2. Rosemary Roasted Root Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and chopped
  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the carrots, parsnips, sweet potato, and red onion. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with rosemary, salt, and pepper. Toss until the vegetables are well coated.
  3. Roast: Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are tender and caramelized.
  4. Serve: Transfer to a serving dish and enjoy as a side dish or a hearty vegetarian main.

The earthy flavors of rosemary paired with the sweetness of roasted root vegetables make this dish a fall favorite.

3. Thyme and Garlic Chicken Thighs

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the Chicken: In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, thyme, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the chicken thighs.
  3. Sear the Chicken: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat a little olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken thighs, skin-side down, and sear for about 5-7 minutes until the skin is golden brown.
  4. Bake: Flip the chicken thighs and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the juices run clear.
  5. Serve: Serve with lemon wedges for a bright finish.

This dish is a delightful way to enjoy the robust flavors of thyme, making it a perfect centerpiece for a fall dinner.
