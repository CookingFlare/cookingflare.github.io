The Best Rice and Grain Recipes for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with family and friends, sharing gratitude over a table laden with delicious food. While traditional dishes like turkey, mashed potatoes, and stuffing dominate the feast, incorporating rice and grains into your menu can offer unique textures, flavors, and even healthier options for your guests. Here are some of the best rice and grain recipes to elevate your Thanksgiving spread and leave everyone asking for seconds.

Savory Wild Rice and Mushroom Pilaf

Wild rice is a timeless autumnal grain, pairing perfectly with the earthy flavors of mushrooms. This dish is hearty, nutty, and visually appealing, making it a beautiful addition to any Thanksgiving table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup wild rice (rinse well)
  • 3 cups vegetable broth or chicken stock
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup cremini or button mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • ½ cup chopped pecans (optional)
  • ¼ cup dried cranberries (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the wild rice: In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the rinsed wild rice, reduce the heat to a simmer, and cover until rice is tender, about 40-45 minutes. Drain any excess liquid if necessary.
  2. Prepare the mushrooms: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and shallots, cooking until softened and slightly caramelized. Stir in garlic and thyme for the final minute of cooking.
  3. Combine and serve: Mix the cooked wild rice with the mushroom mixture in a serving dish. Add chopped pecans and dried cranberries if desired for a festive touch, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

This dish is both gluten-free and vegan-friendly, ensuring everyone at the table can enjoy it.

Creamy Barley Risotto with Butternut Squash

For a twist on classic risotto, substitute arborio rice with barley. Barley’s chewy texture and nutty flavor make it an excellent base for Thanksgiving-inspired ingredients like butternut squash and sage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pearled barley
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons butter (or olive oil for vegan option)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1½ cups diced butternut squash
  • ¼ cup dry white wine
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the squash: Roast the butternut squash at 400°F for 20 minutes until tender, or sauté it in a skillet with olive oil until caramelized and soft.
  2. Cook the barley: In another pan, sauté the onion in butter, then add the barley and stir until lightly toasted. Pour in white wine and let it simmer until absorbed. Gradually add the stock, one cup at a time, stirring frequently, until the barley is tender and creamy (about 35-40 minutes).
  3. Finish and serve: Stir the roasted butternut squash into the barley mixture, then fold in Parmesan and fresh sage. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

This dish is rich and comforting, and the creamy texture is bound to rival any traditional sides.

Sweet Potato and Farro Salad

A vibrant farro salad packed with sweet potatoes and herbs can break up heavier dishes on your Thanksgiving table and serve as a refreshing palate cleanser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup farro
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ cup pomegranate arils
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
  • ¼ cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the sweet potatoes: Toss the diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Roast at 400°F for 25 minutes or until golden.
  2. Cook the farro: Bring water to a boil, add farro, and simmer until tender, about 25 minutes. Drain and transfer to a large mixing bowl.
  3. Toss the salad: Combine the farro with roasted sweet potatoes, pomegranate arils, parsley, and feta cheese if using. Finish with a drizzle of lemon juice and adjust seasoning.

This dish is festive and colorful, perfect for balancing the richness of Thanksgiving classics.

Spiced Pumpkin Rice

Pumpkin and warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg are synonymous with autumn and festivities. Infuse your rice dish with these flavors for a unique and aromatic side.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups basmati rice
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (canned or homemade)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ¼ teaspoon cloves
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly, then cook with coconut milk, pumpkin puree, cinnamon stick, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves. Stir frequently to prevent sticking. Add more water if needed during cooking.
  2. Sweeten and serve: Once rice is fully cooked, remove the cinnamon stick and stir in maple syrup. Adjust the salt to balance sweetness, then serve warm.

This fragrant dish works well as a side or even a lightly sweet dessert.

Quinoa Stuffing with Apples and Sausage

Stuffing typically takes center stage during Thanksgiving, but replacing traditional bread with quinoa adds a nutrient-packed twist while keeping the irresistible flavors intact.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa (rinse well)
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 Granny Smith apple, cubed
  • ½ pound ground sausage (or a plant-based alternative)
  • ½ teaspoon dried sage
  • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¼ cup chopped walnuts or pecans
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the quinoa: In a saucepan, bring stock to a boil, then add quinoa. Cover and let simmer until quinoa is tender and absorbs the liquid, about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Sauté onion, celery, apple, and sausage in a skillet until browned and fragrant. Stir in sage and thyme.
  3. Combine and finish: Mix the cooked quinoa with the sausage mixture, folding in nuts for added crunch. Adjust seasoning before serving.

This stuffing-inspired quinoa dish is hearty, filling, and perfect for those seeking a gluten-free option.

Conclusion

Rice and grains are often overlooked for Thanksgiving, but their versatility offers endless possibilities to create standout dishes. Whether you want a savory wild rice pilaf, creamy barley risotto, or vibrant farro salad, these recipes add both flavor and nutrition to your table. Adding unique dishes like spiced pumpkin rice and quinoa stuffing ensures there’s something for every palate.

Experiment with these recipes and make this Thanksgiving meal one that goes beyond the ordinary!
