Korean Steamed Egg: A Silky Smooth Side Dish


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine, you’ve probably come across the delightful and comforting dish known as Korean steamed egg. This simple yet satisfying side dish is a staple in Korean households and is often served alongside a variety of main courses. With its silky smooth texture and delicate flavor, Korean steamed egg is a versatile dish that pairs well with just about anything. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is a must-try for anyone looking to expand their culinary repertoire.

The Magic of Korean Steamed Egg

Korean steamed egg, or “gaeran jjim,” is a custard-like dish that’s made from a mixture of eggs, water, and seasoning. The result is a velvety, smooth texture that practically melts in your mouth with each spoonful. What sets Korean steamed egg apart from other egg dishes is its unique cooking method. Instead of being scrambled or fried, the eggs are gently steamed, which creates a luxurious and delicate texture.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To make Korean steamed egg, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups of water
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • Chopped scallions for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the Steaming Equipment: Begin by setting up a steaming apparatus. This can be a steamer basket, a heatproof dish with a lid, or any other setup that allows for gentle steaming.

  2. Whisk the Eggs: In a mixing bowl, whisk the eggs until they are well combined. Be careful not to overwhisk, as this can create a foamy texture in the steamed egg.

  3. Add Water and Seasoning: Gradually add the water to the whisked eggs, stirring gently to combine. Then, add the salt and sesame oil, continuing to stir until everything is evenly mixed.

  4. Strain the Mixture: For an extra smooth texture, consider straining the egg mixture through a fine-mesh sieve before steaming.

  5. Steam the Eggs: Pour the egg mixture into the prepared steaming apparatus. Cover with a lid and steam over medium heat for 10-15 minutes, or until the eggs are just set.

  6. Garnish and Serve: Once the eggs are cooked, remove them from the heat and garnish with chopped scallions. Serve the Korean steamed egg hot as a side dish alongside your favorite Korean main course.

Tips for Perfect Korean Steamed Egg

  • Use a gentle steaming method to ensure the eggs cook evenly and maintain their silky texture.
  • Experiment with different seasonings such as soy sauce, garlic, or even kimchi to add depth of flavor to the dish.
  • For a richer version, you can substitute some of the water with chicken or vegetable broth.

Embracing Korean Cuisine

Korean steamed egg is a testament to the beauty of Korean cuisine. Its simplicity and elegance make it a perfect addition to any meal, and its gentle flavor pairs well with a wide variety of dishes. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned enthusiast, this recipe is a wonderful introduction to the world of Korean flavors.

So, the next time you’re craving a comforting and silky smooth side dish, give Korean steamed egg a try. Its delicate texture and subtle flavor are sure to win you over, and you’ll find yourself coming back to this recipe time and time again. Enjoy the magic of Korean steamed egg, and let it transport you to the heart of Korean culinary tradition.
