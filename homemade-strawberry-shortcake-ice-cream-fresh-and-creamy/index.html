Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream: Fresh and Creamy


Are you ready to indulge in the delightful flavors of summer with a homemade treat that will leave your taste buds craving for more? Look no further than this luscious and creamy Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream recipe. With the perfect balance of fresh strawberries, crumbly shortcake, and velvety ice cream, this dessert is a celebration of everything sweet and summery.

Ingredients

For the Strawberry Swirl:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Shortcake Crumble:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 large egg yolks

Instructions

Strawberry Swirl:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the sliced strawberries, granulated sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the strawberries break down and the mixture thickens, about 10-12 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and allow it to cool. Once cooled, puree the mixture until smooth and set it aside.

Shortcake Crumble:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold cubed butter and use your fingers to rub the butter into the dry ingredients until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Spread the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown.
  5. Let the crumble cool completely before breaking it into small pieces.

Ice Cream Base:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and half of the granulated sugar.
  2. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining sugar until pale and slightly thickened.
  4. Gradually pour the hot cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly.
  5. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  6. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl and let it cool completely.

Assembly:

  1. Once the ice cream base has cooled, churn it in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. During the last minute of churning, gently fold in the strawberry swirl and shortcake crumble, reserving some for topping.
  3. Transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container, layering with the remaining strawberry swirl and shortcake crumble.
  4. Freeze for at least 4 hours, or until the ice cream is firm.

Serving Suggestions

Scoop the Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream into bowls and garnish with fresh strawberries, a dollop of whipped cream, and a sprinkle of shortcake crumble for an extra crunch. This delightful dessert is perfect for hot summer days, backyard barbecues, or any occasion that calls for a refreshing and indulgent treat.

Conclusion

Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream is a celebration of summer’s bounty, bringing together the vibrant flavors of ripe strawberries and the comforting sweetness of shortcake in a creamy, dreamy concoction. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a special dessert, this recipe is sure to impress with its fresh and indulgent flavors. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to churn up a batch of this delightful ice cream that will have everyone asking for seconds.
