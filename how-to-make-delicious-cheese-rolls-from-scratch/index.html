How to Make Delicious Cheese Rolls from Scratch


There’s something undeniably comforting about the soft, golden crust of freshly baked cheese rolls. Whether you’re preparing them as a companion to a hearty soup, as a satisfying snack, or simply to impress friends and family, cheese rolls made from scratch elevate any meal occasion with their rich, irresistible flavor and tender texture. Here, you’ll find an easy-to-follow recipe that combines simple ingredients and techniques to yield perfect cheese rolls every time. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To prepare these delightful cheese rolls from scratch, gather the following ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups (360g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp salt
  • 2 ¼ tsp instant yeast (1 packet)
  • ¾ cup whole milk (warm)
  • 1/3 cup water (warm)
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter (softened)

For the Cheese Filling:

  • 1 ½ cups shredded cheese (choose your favorite: cheddar, mozzarella, Gruyère, or a combination)
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 3 tbsp cream cheese (softened)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (optional, adds a subtle aroma to the filling)

Additional Ingredients:

  • 1 large egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tbsp water (to mix with the egg)
  • Optional garnish: sesame seeds, dried herbs (oregano or parsley), or a sprinkle of more cheese for topping

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough

Start by making the dough—this forms the base of your cheese rolls.

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. If using active dry yeast instead of instant yeast, ensure you activate it by dissolving it in the warm water first and letting it sit until foamy (about 5 minutes).
  • Gradually add the warm milk and water to the dry mix. Stir using a wooden spoon or with the dough hook attachment of a stand mixer until the ingredients bind.
  • Add the softened butter and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. This step can take about 8-10 minutes by hand or 5 minutes with a mixer.
  • Once the dough is ready, shape it into a ball and place it in a lightly greased bowl. Cover with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, then let it rise in a warm, draft-free area for 1–2 hours or until it doubles in size.

2. Make the Cheese Filling

While the dough is rising, it’s time to prepare the ooey-gooey cheese filling that turns these rolls into a delectable treat.

  • In a medium bowl, mix the shredded cheese(s), Parmesan, and cream cheese until well combined. If using garlic powder or other desired spices, incorporate them into the mixture.
  • Set the filling aside until the dough is ready. Keep it covered to maintain freshness.

3. Assemble the Rolls

Once your dough has risen, it’s time to transform it into rolls:

  • Punch the dough down lightly to release the air, then transfer it to a floured surface.
  • Roll out the dough into a rectangular shape, approximately 14x10 inches, and about ¼ inch thick.
  • Spread your cheese filling evenly across the surface of the dough, leaving about ½ inch of space around the edges to prevent spillage while rolling.
  • Starting on one of the longer edges, tightly roll the dough into a log. Once rolled, pinch the seam to seal.

4. Slice and Arrange

Here’s where you create the signature roll shape:

  • Using a serrated knife, cut the log into equal slices—aim for around 12 rolls, depending on thickness.
  • Arrange the rolls in a greased baking dish or on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Leave some space between each roll for expansion as they rise again.
  • Cover the rolls loosely and let them rise for another 30–45 minutes until puffy.

5. Egg Wash and Bake

For a glossy finish and an enhanced bake, prepare the egg wash:

  • Beat the egg with 1 tablespoon of water and brush this mixture lightly over the tops of the rolls.
  • (Optional) Sprinkle the tops with sesame seeds, dried herbs, or an additional handful of shredded cheese for visual and flavor appeal.

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) while the rolls are finishing their second rise. Once the oven is ready:

  • Bake the rolls for 18–22 minutes, or until their tops turn golden brown and the cheese filling bubbles lightly.

6. Cool and Serve

Remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool for about 10 minutes in the pan to avoid breaking apart. Serve them warm as they are, or pair them with a side of marinara sauce or sour cream for dipping.

Additional Tips for Perfect Cheese Rolls

  1. Experiment with Cheese: Different cheeses bring unique flavors to your rolls. For bold flavors, try sharp cheddar; for gooey texture, mozzarella is a winner; Gruyère adds a nutty depth.
  2. Herbs for Extra Flavor: Incorporating fresh or dried herbs like rosemary, thyme, or parsley into the dough or filling can elevate your rolls to gourmet status.
  3. Make-Ahead Option: Prepare the dough in advance and let it rise overnight in the refrigerator. Allow it to come to room temperature before assembling and baking the rolls.
  4. Freezing: Cheese rolls freeze beautifully! Once baked, let them cool completely, then store in an airtight container or freezer-safe bag for up to 3 months. Reheat in the oven when ready to serve.

Why Homemade Cheese Rolls Are Worth the Effort

When you bake cheese rolls from scratch, every bite reflects the effort and care you’ve infused into your creation. Unlike store-bought versions, homemade rolls allow complete control over the ingredients, which means no preservatives or additives—just wholesome, tasty goodness. The added customization ensures you can tailor them to satisfy your personal preferences, whether you like them intensely cheesy, subtly buttery, or flavored with fragrant herbs.

Homemade cheese rolls are pocketed bliss wrapped in dough—a dish that works well any time of the day. Baking them from scratch is easier than it seems, and the reward is unmatchable. So ditch the store-bought version, roll up your sleeves, and give this recipe a try. Your kitchen is waiting, and so is the irresistible aroma of freshly baked cheese rolls. Once you’ve made them, be prepared: they may just become the most requested dish in your home!
