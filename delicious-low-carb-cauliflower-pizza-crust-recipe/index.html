Delicious Low Carb Cauliflower Pizza Crust Recipe

4 min · 685 words

Are you looking for a healthy and delicious alternative to traditional pizza crust? Look no further! This low carb cauliflower pizza crust is the perfect solution. Made with simple ingredients and packed with flavor, it’s a guilt-free way to enjoy your favorite pizza toppings. Plus, it’s gluten-free and keto-friendly!

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Cut the cauliflower into florets and pulse them in a food processor until they resemble rice. You can also grate the cauliflower using a cheese grater if you don’t have a food processor.

  3. Place the cauliflower rice in a microwave-safe bowl and microwave on high for 5 minutes. This will help remove excess moisture from the cauliflower.

  4. Transfer the cooked cauliflower rice to a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth. Allow it to cool slightly, then squeeze out as much liquid as possible. This step is crucial for achieving a crispy crust.

  5. In a large bowl, combine the cauliflower rice, beaten egg, grated Parmesan cheese, dried oregano, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until everything is evenly incorporated.

  6. Transfer the cauliflower mixture to the prepared baking sheet and shape it into a round or rectangular crust, depending on your preference. Aim for a thickness of about 1/4 inch.

  7. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until it becomes golden brown and firm to the touch.

  8. Once the crust is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. At this point, you can add your favorite pizza toppings, such as marinara sauce, cheese, vegetables, and meats.

  9. Return the pizza to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  10. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a crispier crust, you can place the baked crust under the broiler for a couple of minutes after adding the toppings.

  • Feel free to experiment with different seasonings and spices to customize the flavor of your crust. Try adding dried basil, thyme, or even a pinch of red pepper flakes for some heat.

  • If you prefer a dairy-free option, you can replace the Parmesan cheese with nutritional yeast or a dairy-free cheese alternative.

  • This cauliflower pizza crust is a great way to sneak in some extra veggies into your diet. You can even add grated zucchini or carrots to the cauliflower mixture for added nutrition.

  • Leftover crust can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or toaster oven before adding your desired toppings.

Now that you have the recipe for this delicious low carb cauliflower pizza crust, it’s time to get creative with your toppings! Whether you prefer classic margherita, loaded veggie, or meat lovers, this crust will provide the perfect base for your culinary creations. Enjoy your guilt-free pizza night!
