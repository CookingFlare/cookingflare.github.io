How to Make the Perfect Focaccia with Olive Oil and Herbs


Focaccia is a beloved Italian bread that is often compared to pizza dough because of its similar texture, but with a softer and fluffier crumb. The addition of olive oil and fresh herbs makes it a flavorful, versatile bread that pairs wonderfully with soups, salads, or simply served with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of salt. The process of making the perfect focaccia may seem intimidating at first, but with the right ingredients and techniques, it’s surprisingly easy to prepare. Let’s dive into how to make the perfect focaccia with olive oil and herbs at home!

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create a traditional, flavor-packed focaccia, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Flour: 4 cups of all-purpose flour is ideal for focaccia as it yields a soft and chewy texture. You can also use bread flour if you want a slightly more robust crumb.
  • Yeast: 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast, which will help the dough rise and develop the signature light texture.
  • Warm Water: 1 1/2 cups of warm water, which activates the yeast. Make sure the water is warm but not too hot, around 110°F (43°C).
  • Olive Oil: 1/4 cup of high-quality extra virgin olive oil for the dough and additional oil for drizzling on top. The rich flavor of olive oil is key to the depth of flavor in this recipe.
  • Salt: 1 1/2 teaspoons of salt to season the dough.
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon of sugar to help activate the yeast.
  • Herbs: 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, thyme, or a combination of both. Fresh herbs are preferred for their vibrant, aromatic flavor, but dried herbs can be used if fresh ones aren’t available.
  • Toppings: Coarse sea salt, additional olive oil for drizzling, and optionally, garlic or onion slices for extra flavor.

Tips for the Best Ingredients

  • Olive Oil: Use a high-quality extra virgin olive oil for both the dough and as a topping. Olive oil is the heart and soul of focaccia, so it’s important to choose one that adds richness and depth of flavor to the bread.
  • Herbs: Fresh rosemary is the classic herb used in focaccia. However, thyme, oregano, and sage are also excellent options to customize the flavor profile.
  • Flour: Though all-purpose flour works well, for an even better texture, you can blend it with some bread flour. Bread flour has a higher protein content, which gives the bread more structure.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making the Perfect Focaccia

Step 1: Prepare the Yeast Mixture

The first step in making focaccia is activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Stir gently and let the mixture sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This shows that the yeast is active and ready to work its magic.

Step 2: Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Mix them together to ensure the salt is evenly distributed. You want to make sure the yeast mixture doesn’t come into direct contact with the salt, as it can inhibit the yeast’s ability to rise. Creating a well in the center of the flour mixture will give you space to add the yeast mixture and the olive oil.

Step 3: Make the Dough

Pour the activated yeast mixture into the well in the dry ingredients. Add the olive oil as well, and begin mixing with a wooden spoon or your hands. Once the dough starts to come together, transfer it to a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels sticky, you can add a small amount of flour, but be careful not to overdo it. The dough should be soft, but not too sticky.

Step 4: First Rise

After kneading, form the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let the dough rise for about 1-2 hours in a warm, draft-free area. The dough should double in size. This process is crucial for developing the air bubbles that give focaccia its soft and light texture.

Step 5: Shape the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Transfer it to a well-oiled baking sheet or a large baking pan (about 9x13 inches works well). Gently stretch the dough to fit the size of the pan. You can use your fingers to dimple the dough, which will create the classic indentations seen in focaccia bread.

Step 6: Second Rise

Cover the shaped dough with a cloth and let it rise again for about 30 minutes. This second rise allows the dough to become even fluffier and ensures a light crumb. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

Step 7: Add Olive Oil and Herbs

After the dough has risen for the second time, it’s time to drizzle it with more olive oil. Be generous, as this will give the bread its rich, golden color and soft, moist texture. Sprinkle the dough with freshly chopped rosemary, thyme, or any other herbs of your choice. Press the herbs gently into the dough with your fingers, ensuring they’re well distributed. Optionally, you can also sprinkle some garlic, sea salt, or sliced onions for extra flavor.

Step 8: Bake the Focaccia

Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the focaccia is golden brown on top and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. You can also check the internal temperature of the bread; it should be around 190°F (88°C) when fully baked.

Step 9: Cool and Serve

Once the focaccia is baked to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. While still warm, drizzle a little extra olive oil on top for added flavor. Cut it into squares or strips and serve immediately.

Tips for Perfect Focaccia Every Time

  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Focaccia is meant to be light and airy. Avoid over-kneading the dough, as this can lead to a dense texture.
  • Use Olive Oil Generously: The key to a soft, flavorful focaccia is in the olive oil. Don’t be afraid to use plenty of it both in the dough and as a topping.
  • Experiment with Toppings: Focaccia is incredibly versatile. Try adding olives, cherry tomatoes, or sun-dried tomatoes before baking. The options are endless!
  • Let the Dough Rise: Be patient during the rising stages. The dough needs time to develop its flavors and texture, so don’t rush this process.
  • Use a Hot Oven: Make sure your oven is fully preheated before baking the focaccia. A hot oven will help it bake evenly and achieve a golden-brown crust.

Variations of Focaccia to Try

While the classic rosemary focaccia is a favorite, there are many other variations you can experiment with:

  • Olive Focaccia: Add black or green olives into the dough or use them as a topping for a Mediterranean twist.
  • Cheese Focaccia: Sprinkle grated Parmesan, mozzarella, or cheddar over the dough before baking for a cheesy variation.
  • Tomato and Herb Focaccia: Slice cherry tomatoes and add them to the dough before baking, along with a handful of fresh basil.
  • Garlic Focaccia: For garlic lovers, scatter minced garlic over the dough before baking. You can also infuse the olive oil with garlic for an extra punch of flavor.

Storing and Reheating Focaccia

Focaccia is best enjoyed fresh, but if you have leftovers, you can store it at room temperature for 1-2 days. To keep it from drying out, wrap it in a kitchen towel or store it in an airtight container. If you want to reheat it, simply place it in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 5-10 minutes, until warm and crisp again.

Conclusion

Making the perfect focaccia with olive oil and herbs is easier than you might think. With the right ingredients, some patience, and a few simple steps, you can create this delicious, aromatic bread at home. Whether you serve it alongside a soup, salad, or enjoy it as a snack on its own, focaccia with olive oil and herbs is sure to become a staple in your baking repertoire.
