Impressive Beef Wellington Recipe for a Stylish Christmas Dinner


Christmas is a time for celebration, and what better way to impress your guests than with a mouthwatering Beef Wellington? This classic dish is a showstopper that combines tender beef, savory mushrooms, and flaky puff pastry. With its elegant presentation and delicious flavors, it’s the perfect centerpiece for a stylish Christmas dinner. So let’s dive right into the recipe and create a culinary masterpiece!

Ingredients

For the Beef Wellington:

  • 1 ½ pounds (700g) beef tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 8-10 slices of prosciutto
  • 8-10 ounces (225-280g) mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

For the Red Wine Sauce:

  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Season the beef tenderloin generously with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over high heat and sear the beef on all sides until browned. Remove from the skillet and set aside to cool.

  3. In the same skillet, add the mushrooms, shallot, garlic, and thyme. Cook until the mushrooms release their moisture and the mixture becomes dry. Season with salt and pepper, then remove from heat and let it cool.

  4. Lay out a sheet of plastic wrap and arrange the prosciutto slices in a rectangle, slightly overlapping each other. Spread a thin layer of Dijon mustard on top of the prosciutto.

  5. Spread the mushroom mixture evenly on top of the prosciutto.

  6. Place the seared beef tenderloin in the center of the prosciutto and mushroom layer. Carefully roll the prosciutto and mushrooms around the beef, using the plastic wrap to help shape it into a tight log. Chill in the refrigerator for 15 minutes.

  7. Roll out the puff pastry into a large rectangle. Remove the plastic wrap from the beef and place it in the center of the puff pastry. Wrap the pastry around the beef, sealing the edges by brushing them with the beaten egg.

  8. Place the Beef Wellington on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the entire pastry with the egg wash.

  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.

  10. While the Beef Wellington is resting, prepare the red wine sauce. In a saucepan, combine the red wine and beef stock. Bring to a simmer and cook until the liquid has reduced by half. Stir in the butter and season with salt and pepper.

  11. Slice the Beef Wellington into thick slices and serve with the red wine sauce. Enjoy!

Conclusion

Impress your guests this Christmas with a Beef Wellington that is sure to leave a lasting impression. The combination of tender beef, savory mushrooms, and flaky puff pastry creates a symphony of flavors that will delight everyone at your stylish dinner. So take your culinary skills to the next level and create a masterpiece that will make this Christmas truly memorable. Happy cooking and happy holidays!
