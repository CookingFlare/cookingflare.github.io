Gluten-Free Raspberry Thumbprint Muffins: A Sweet and Tangy Treat


If you’re looking for a delightful gluten-free treat that’s bursting with sweet and tangy flavors, look no further than these Gluten-Free Raspberry Thumbprint Muffins. These muffins are not only a perfect addition to your breakfast or brunch spread, but they also make for a delightful snack any time of the day. The combination of the moist, tender muffin base and the vibrant raspberry filling creates a flavor explosion that will leave you wanting more. Plus, they are gluten-free, making them suitable for those with dietary restrictions or preferences.

Ingredients

For the Muffins:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or dairy-free)

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • 1/2 cup raspberry jam
  • Fresh raspberries for topping

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease the wells with cooking spray.

Step 2: Make the Muffin Batter

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, almond flour, baking powder, and salt until well combined.
  2. In a separate large bowl, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together using a hand mixer or a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Then, mix in the vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry flour mixture to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk, and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.

Step 3: Fill the Muffin Tin

  1. Divide the muffin batter evenly among the prepared muffin tin wells, filling each about two-thirds full.

Step 4: Create the Raspberry Thumbprints

  1. Using the back of a spoon or your thumb, make an indentation in the center of each muffin to create a well for the raspberry filling.

Step 5: Add the Raspberry Filling

  1. Spoon a small amount of raspberry jam into each indentation, filling it almost to the top.
  2. Place a fresh raspberry on top of the raspberry jam in each muffin.

Step 6: Bake and Enjoy

  1. Bake the muffins in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the muffin comes out clean.
  2. Once baked, remove the muffins from the oven and allow them to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Serving and Storing

These Gluten-Free Raspberry Thumbprint Muffins are best enjoyed fresh on the day they are made. However, you can store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. They can also be frozen for up to one month. Simply thaw them at room temperature or reheat them in the microwave before enjoying.

Make a batch of these delightful muffins and watch them disappear in no time. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, packing a lunch, or simply craving a homemade treat, these Gluten-Free Raspberry Thumbprint Muffins are sure to brighten your day with their sweet and tangy flavors. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to bake up a storm in your kitchen. Happy baking!
