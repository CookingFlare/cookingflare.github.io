Budget-Friendly Indian Vegetarian Recipes: Spicy and Affordable


Are you looking for delicious Indian vegetarian recipes that won’t break the bank? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore a variety of budget-friendly Indian dishes that are not only packed with flavor but also easy on the wallet. Whether you are a student on a tight budget or simply trying to save some money, these recipes will satisfy your taste buds without compromising on taste. So, let’s dive in!

1. Rajma Masala (Kidney Bean Curry)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the kidney beans overnight and pressure cook them until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they start to splutter, add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the cooked kidney beans along with the water they were cooked in. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

2. Aloo Gobi (Potato and Cauliflower Curry)

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 small cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they start to splutter, add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the cubed potatoes and cauliflower florets. Mix until well-coated with the spice mixture.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat until the potatoes and cauliflower are tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with roti or naan.

3. Chana Masala (Chickpea Curry)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (chana), soaked overnight and boiled until tender
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Once they start to splutter, add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  3. Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the boiled chickpeas and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

4. Vegetable Pulao

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • Assorted vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, bell peppers), diced
  • 2 medium-sized onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds, cloves, green cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the thinly sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute.
  4. Add the diced vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pan. Mix well.
  6. Add 2 cups of water and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Conclusion

There you have it! Four budget-friendly Indian vegetarian recipes that are spicy, delicious, and won’t break the bank. These recipes are perfect for those who want to enjoy the flavors of Indian cuisine without spending a fortune. So, go ahead and give them a try. Your taste buds will thank you!

Remember, cooking on a budget doesn’t mean compromising on taste. With a little creativity and some pantry staples, you can create flavorful and satisfying meals that are easy on your wallet. Enjoy and happy cooking!

Note: The recipes provided are approximate measurements. Feel free to adjust the quantities according to your taste preferences.
