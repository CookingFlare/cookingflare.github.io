Senior Cooking: 7 Easy and Delicious Breakfast Muffin Recipes


As we age, it becomes more important than ever to maintain a healthy and balanced diet. Breakfast is often hailed as the most important meal of the day, and what better way to start your morning than with a delicious and nutritious muffin? In this article, we will share seven easy and mouthwatering breakfast muffin recipes that are perfect for seniors. These recipes are not only packed with flavor but also designed to meet the unique nutritional needs of older adults. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Blueberry Oatmeal Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • ½ cup Greek yogurt
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the oats, flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the mashed bananas, Greek yogurt, honey, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the blueberries.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before enjoying.

2. Spinach and Feta Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups baby spinach, chopped
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the spinach and feta cheese.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flours, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  4. In another bowl, whisk the buttermilk, olive oil, and eggs.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  6. Gently fold in the spinach and feta mixture.
  7. Spoon the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about ¾ full.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown and a toothpick comes out clean.
  9. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

  8. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

4. Banana Nut Muffins

  • ¼ tsp salt

5. Zucchini and Carrot Muffins

  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup unsweetened applesauce

6. Chocolate Chip Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup milk
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the milk, vegetable oil, egg, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the chocolate chips.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about ¾ full.
  7. Bake for 15-18 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

7. Peanut Butter and Jelly Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ cup creamy peanut butter
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup jelly (your choice of flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the peanut butter, melted butter, milk, and egg.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Spoon half of the batter into the muffin cups.
  6. Add a small dollop of jelly to the center of each muffin cup.
  7. Spoon the remaining batter over the jelly, covering it completely.
  8. Bake for 15-18 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

These seven easy and delicious breakfast muffin recipes are sure to delight your taste buds while providing the essential nutrients you need to start your day off right. Whether you prefer fruity flavors or indulgent chocolate chips, there’s a muffin here for everyone. So, put on your apron, preheat the oven, and get ready to enjoy a homemade breakfast treat that’s perfect for seniors. Happy baking!
