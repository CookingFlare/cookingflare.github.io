Indulgent Picnic Desserts to Sweeten Your Summer


As the warm sunshine beckons and the days grow longer, summer picnics become a delightful way to enjoy time with friends, family, or even just a relaxing solo retreat. What better way to elevate your picnic experience than with indulgent desserts that satisfy your sweet tooth while adding a bit of luxury to your outdoor gathering? In this post, we explore a variety of picnic-perfect desserts that are not only scrumptious but also designed to be convenient for transporting and serving in the great outdoors.

The Ultimate Picnic Dessert Guide

1. Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars

When you think of summer, the refreshing combination of lemon and blueberries often comes to mind. These cheesecake bars perfectly capture that essence, offering a balance of tangy citrus, creamy cheese filling, and sweet blueberries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups of graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup of sugar
  • 6 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 2 packages (8 oz each) of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon zest
  • 1 ½ cups of fresh blueberries
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch (for the blueberry topping)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). In a medium-sized bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and press them firmly into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch pan. Bake the crust for about 8-10 minutes or until lightly golden, then set aside to cool.

  2. Make the cheesecake filling: In a large mixing bowl, beat together the softened cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, ensuring each is fully incorporated before adding the next. Stir in the vanilla extract and lemon zest.

  3. Bake the cheesecake: Pour the cheesecake filling over the prepared crust and spread it out evenly. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the center is set but slightly jiggly. Let it cool to room temperature before transferring it to the fridge for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible.

  4. Prepare the blueberry topping: In a saucepan, combine fresh blueberries and cornstarch over medium heat. Stir gently until the blueberries release their juice and the mixture thickens. Allow the topping to cool, then spread it evenly over the chilled cheesecake bars. Cut into squares and serve.

These cheesecake bars are perfect for packing up and enjoying under the open sky, offering both the creaminess of cheesecake and the vibrant flavors of summer fruits.

2. Chocolate-Dipped Strawberry Tart

A classic tart meets indulgence with this chocolate-dipped strawberry version. The combination of a buttery crust, rich chocolate ganache, and fresh, juicy strawberries makes this dessert an irresistible choice for your picnic basket.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made tart shell (or homemade)
  • 8 oz dark chocolate
  • ½ cup of heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1 pint of fresh strawberries, hulled
  • ½ cup of chopped almonds or pistachios (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the tart shell: If you’re using a store-bought tart shell, bake according to the package instructions and allow it to cool completely. If you’re making your own, use a simple shortcrust pastry recipe and bake it until golden.

  2. Make the chocolate ganache: In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and add the chopped dark chocolate, stirring until the mixture is smooth and glossy. Stir in the honey to enhance the flavor. Let the ganache cool slightly.

  3. Assemble the tart: Pour the chocolate ganache into the cooled tart shell and spread it out evenly. Arrange the hulled strawberries on top, making a beautiful pattern that will entice your picnic guests.

  4. Garnish: If desired, sprinkle chopped almonds or pistachios over the tart for an added crunch. Allow the tart to set in the fridge for at least 2 hours before serving.

The combination of the creamy ganache and fresh strawberries makes this dessert a showstopper, perfect for summer celebrations.

3. Peach Cobbler Muffins

Peach cobbler is a beloved Southern classic, but these peach cobbler muffins bring all the flavor in a convenient, portable format. They’re sweet, soft, and have a beautiful crumbly topping, making them a picnic favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups fresh peaches, diced
  • ½ cup brown sugar (for topping)
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat and prepare the muffin tin: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or lightly grease the cups.

  2. Make the muffin batter: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon. In a separate bowl, combine the melted butter, egg, milk, and vanilla extract. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Gently fold in the diced peaches.

  3. Prepare the topping: In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar and flour for the topping. Sprinkle this mixture over the muffin batter before baking.

  4. Bake the muffins: Spoon the muffin batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about ¾ of the way full. Sprinkle the brown sugar topping evenly over each muffin. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

These peach cobbler muffins are an easy-to-make dessert that is perfect for picnics. The juicy peaches combined with the warm, spiced crumble topping make these muffins a comfort food that’s easy to enjoy on the go.

4. No-Bake S’mores Bars

S’mores are an iconic summer treat, and these no-bake s’mores bars capture all the flavors without the need for a campfire. Perfect for those picnics where you want the experience of s’mores without the hassle.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cups mini marshmallows
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • ½ cup peanut butter (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a large bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated and the mixture is crumbly. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch pan.

  2. Melt the chocolate and marshmallows: In a saucepan over low heat, melt the chocolate chips and peanut butter (if using), stirring frequently. Once melted and smooth, remove from heat and fold in the mini marshmallows. The marshmallows should soften but not completely melt.

  3. Assemble the bars: Pour the chocolate-marshmallow mixture over the graham cracker crust and spread it evenly. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until set.

  4. Cut and serve: Once the bars have set, cut them into squares and enjoy! These bars are crunchy, gooey, and packed with the irresistible flavors of s’mores.

These no-bake s’mores bars are a crowd-pleaser, with all the nostalgia of a traditional s’more but in an easy, portable form perfect for a picnic.

5. Raspberry Sorbet Popsicles

For a lighter option that still satisfies your craving for something sweet, these raspberry sorbet popsicles are the perfect treat. Made with fresh raspberries and just a few simple ingredients, they are refreshing and cool, ideal for a hot summer day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • Juice of one lemon

Instructions:

  1. Make the syrup: In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat over medium heat until the sugar has dissolved completely, then remove from heat and let it cool.

  2. Prepare the raspberry mixture: In a blender, combine fresh raspberries and lemon juice. Blend until smooth. Strain the mixture through a fine mesh sieve to remove seeds, if desired.

  3. Combine the syrup and raspberry puree: Mix the cooled syrup with the raspberry puree. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds and freeze for at least 4 hours or until fully frozen.

  4. Serve and enjoy: Run warm water over the outside of the popsicle molds for a few seconds to loosen the popsicles, then remove and serve immediately.

These raspberry sorbet popsicles are the perfect balance of sweet and tart, and their vibrant color will brighten any picnic spread.

Conclusion

These indulgent picnic desserts not only capture the essence of summer but also ensure your picnic is memorable and full of delicious treats. From fruity and refreshing sorbet popsicles to the decadent chocolate-dipped strawberry tart, these recipes offer a variety of flavors that will satisfy every palate. Whether you’re enjoying a sunny day at the park or lounging by the lake, these desserts will sweeten your summer and elevate your picnic experience. Happy picnicking!
