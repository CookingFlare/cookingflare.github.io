Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole: A Complex and Flavorful Dish


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you’ve likely heard of mole. This rich and complex sauce is a staple in Mexican cooking and is often used in dishes like enchiladas and tamales. One of the most popular variations is the Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole, which combines tender chicken with a sauce made from a blend of chili peppers, spices, and chocolate. The result is a dish that is both spicy and sweet, with layers of flavor that will leave you craving more.

Ingredients

To make Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 dried ancho chilies, seeds and stems removed
  • 2 dried guajillo chilies, seeds and stems removed
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup of raisins
  • 1/4 cup of almonds
  • 1/4 cup of sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  2. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for an additional 2 minutes.

  3. In a separate pan, toast the dried ancho and guajillo chilies over medium heat until they become fragrant, about 1 minute per side. Remove from heat and let them cool slightly. Once cool, roughly chop the chilies.

  4. In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped chilies, chicken broth, raisins, almonds, sesame seeds, cocoa powder, cumin, cinnamon, and oregano. Blend until smooth.

  5. Pour the chili mixture into the skillet with the onions and tomatoes. Stir to combine. Add the browned chicken pieces back into the skillet and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  7. Serve the Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole over rice or with warm tortillas. Garnish with fresh cilantro, if desired.

The Complexity of Flavor

Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole is a dish that showcases the depth and complexity of Mexican cuisine. The combination of dried chilies, spices, and chocolate creates a sauce that is both spicy and slightly sweet. The raisins add a touch of natural sweetness, while the almonds and sesame seeds provide a nutty undertone. The result is a sauce that is rich and flavorful, with each ingredient contributing to the overall taste.

The Art of Mole Making

Making Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole is truly an art form. From toasting the chilies to blending the sauce, each step requires attention to detail and a delicate balance of flavors. While it may seem daunting at first, the end result is well worth the effort. The complex flavors and aroma of the mole will transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico, where this dish is a beloved tradition.

Final Thoughts

Spicy Mexican Chicken Mole is a dish that embodies the essence of Mexican cuisine. Its complex flavors and rich sauce make it a standout dish that is sure to impress. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is worth giving a try. So gather your ingredients, embrace the art of mole making, and prepare to indulge in a truly unforgettable culinary experience.
