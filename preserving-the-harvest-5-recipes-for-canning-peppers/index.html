Preserving the Harvest: 5 Recipes for Canning Peppers


As summer comes to a close, it’s time to make the most of the bountiful harvest of peppers from your garden or local farmer’s market. Canning peppers is a fantastic way to preserve their vibrant flavors, ensuring you can enjoy them throughout the year. In this article, we’ll explore five delicious recipes for canning peppers, each with its own unique twist. So let’s dive in and get started!

1. Spicy Pickled Jalapenos

If you crave a little heat in your dishes, pickled jalapenos are a must-have pantry staple. They add a tangy and fiery kick to everything from nachos to tacos. To make this recipe, slice jalapenos into rounds or leave them whole if you prefer a milder flavor. Pack them tightly into sterilized jars, along with garlic cloves and a few sprigs of fresh cilantro. In a saucepan, combine equal parts water and vinegar, a pinch of salt, and a teaspoon of sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil, then pour it over the peppers, leaving a small headspace. Seal the jars and process them in a water bath canner for 10 minutes. Allow the pickled jalapenos to rest for a few weeks before indulging in their spicy goodness.

2. Sweet and Tangy Pepper Relish

For a versatile condiment that brings a burst of flavor to sandwiches, burgers, and charcuterie boards, try making a sweet and tangy pepper relish. Begin by finely chopping a mix of bell peppers, red onions, and chili peppers. In a large pot, combine these vegetables with white vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, and a pinch of salt. Cook the mixture over medium heat until it reaches a thick, syrupy consistency. Ladle the hot relish into sterilized jars, leaving a half-inch headspace. Process the jars in a water bath canner for 15 minutes and allow them to cool before storing. This relish will add a delightful zing to any dish.

3. Smoky Chipotle Salsa

If you’re a fan of smoky flavors, this chipotle salsa is a game-changer. Start by roasting red bell peppers and chipotle peppers until they’re charred and blistered. Remove the skins and blend the peppers with tomatoes, garlic, lime juice, and a pinch of salt until smooth. Transfer the salsa to a saucepan and simmer for 10 minutes to meld the flavors. Pour the hot salsa into sterilized jars, leaving a quarter-inch headspace. Process the jars in a water bath canner for 20 minutes. Your homemade chipotle salsa will be a hit at your next gathering.

4. Tangy Pepper Jelly

Pepper jelly is a delightful balance of sweetness and tanginess, perfect for pairing with cheese or spreading on crackers. To make this jelly, finely chop a mix of bell peppers and hot peppers. In a large pot, combine the peppers with apple cider vinegar, sugar, and pectin. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil, stirring constantly, and then let it simmer for a few minutes. Ladle the hot jelly into sterilized jars, leaving a quarter-inch headspace. Process the jars in a water bath canner for 10 minutes. Allow the jelly to cool and set before enjoying its unique flavor profile.

5. Roasted Red Pepper Soup

While canning peppers is typically associated with preserving in jars, you can also create delicious soups to enjoy all year round. Roasted red pepper soup is a comforting and flavorful option. Start by charring red bell peppers under the broiler until their skins are blackened. Let them cool, then remove the skins and seeds. In a large pot, sauté onions, garlic, and a pinch of salt until translucent. Add the roasted peppers, vegetable broth, and a dash of cream. Simmer the soup for 10 minutes, then blend until smooth. Ladle the hot soup into sterilized jars, leaving a half-inch headspace. Process the jars in a water bath canner for 30 minutes. This soup will warm you up on cold winter days.

Conclusion

Preserving the harvest by canning peppers allows you to savor the flavors of summer long after the growing season has ended. From spicy pickled jalapenos to tangy pepper jelly, these recipes offer a variety of options to suit every taste. So roll up your sleeves, gather your fresh peppers, and start canning these delicious recipes. Your future self will thank you for the taste of summer in every bite. Happy canning!
