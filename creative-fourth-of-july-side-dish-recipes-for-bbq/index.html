Creative Fourth of July Side Dish Recipes for BBQ


The Fourth of July is right around the corner, and that means it’s time to fire up the grill and celebrate with friends and family. While burgers and hot dogs are always a hit, why not take your BBQ game to the next level with some creative and delicious side dishes? From grilled vegetables to refreshing salads, we’ve got you covered with these unique Fourth of July side dish recipes.

Grilled Corn and Avocado Salad

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the corn until lightly charred, about 8-10 minutes. Let it cool, then cut the kernels off the cob.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the grilled corn, diced avocados, red bell pepper, red onion, and cilantro.
  4. Squeeze the lime juice over the salad and season with salt and pepper. Gently toss to combine.
  5. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Watermelon and Feta Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 small seedless watermelon, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup feta cheese, cut into cubes
  • Fresh mint leaves, torn
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Thread a cube of watermelon, a cube of feta, and a mint leaf onto a skewer. Repeat until all the ingredients are used.
  2. Arrange the skewers on a platter and drizzle with balsamic glaze.
  3. Serve immediately and savor the delightful combination of sweet watermelon, salty feta, and refreshing mint.

Grilled Vegetable Pasta Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 red onion, cut into thick slices
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and quartered
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 8 oz penne pasta
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the zucchini, yellow squash, red onion, red bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the vegetables until tender and slightly charred, about 5-6 minutes per side. Remove from the grill and let them cool.
  4. Cook the penne pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  5. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  6. Add the grilled vegetables, cooked pasta, and torn basil leaves to the bowl. Toss gently to coat everything with the dressing.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

Grilled Pineapple Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 1 pineapple, peeled, cored, and cut into rings
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the pineapple rings until they have grill marks, about 2-3 minutes per side. Let them cool, then chop into small pieces.
  3. In a bowl, combine the grilled pineapple, diced red onion, minced jalapeno pepper, lime juice, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  4. Mix well and let the flavors meld together for at least 30 minutes before serving.
  5. Serve alongside grilled meats or as a refreshing dip with tortilla chips.

Conclusion

These creative Fourth of July side dish recipes will take your BBQ to a whole new level. From the Grilled Corn and Avocado Salad to the Grilled Pineapple Salsa, these dishes are bursting with flavor and will impress your guests. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and celebrate Independence Day with these delicious and unique side dishes. Happy Fourth of July!
