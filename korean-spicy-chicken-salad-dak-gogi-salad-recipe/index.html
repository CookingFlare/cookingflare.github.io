Korean Spicy Chicken Salad (Dak Gogi Salad) Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors, unique combinations, and the perfect balance between sweet, spicy, sour, and savory. One dish that captures the essence of these flavors is Dak Gogi Salad, also known as Korean Spicy Chicken Salad. This delightful salad is a fusion of tender, spicy chicken, crunchy vegetables, and a variety of seasonings, making it a refreshing and satisfying dish perfect for any occasion.

Whether you are looking for a light meal, a crowd-pleasing side dish, or something to spice up your weeknight dinner, Dak Gogi Salad is a delicious option that is easy to prepare and packed with flavor.

In this blog post, we will walk you through the ingredients, step-by-step instructions, and tips to prepare the best Korean Spicy Chicken Salad that will make your taste buds dance with joy.

What is Dak Gogi Salad?

Dak Gogi Salad (닭고기 샐러드) is a traditional Korean dish that features spicy, marinated chicken served over a bed of fresh, crisp vegetables. The term “Dak Gogi” literally translates to “chicken” in Korean, while “Salad” refers to the mixed vegetables that accompany the chicken. The combination of tender chicken, the spicy kick of gochujang (Korean red chili paste), and the crunchiness of fresh vegetables creates an irresistible dish that can be served as a main course or as a side.

The beauty of Dak Gogi Salad lies in its versatility. You can customize the salad to your taste by adjusting the spice level, adding different vegetables, or including extra toppings like sesame seeds or sliced boiled eggs. The salad is often served with steamed rice or can be enjoyed on its own for a low-carb, high-protein meal.

Ingredients for Dak Gogi Salad

To make a truly authentic and mouthwatering Dak Gogi Salad, it is essential to use the right ingredients. Below are the key components you will need to prepare this flavorful dish:

For the Chicken:

  • Chicken breast or thigh fillets (about 1 pound): The chicken should be boneless and skinless to ensure easy cooking and to maintain tenderness.
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): This thick, spicy paste is the key to achieving the signature heat and umami flavor in the dish.
  • Soy sauce: Adds saltiness and depth to the marinade.
  • Sesame oil: Gives the chicken a nutty aroma and flavor.
  • Garlic (2-3 cloves, minced): Adds a sharp, aromatic flavor.
  • Honey or sugar: Balances out the heat from the gochujang and provides a slight sweetness.
  • Rice vinegar or apple cider vinegar: Provides acidity to balance the flavors.
  • Ginger (1-inch piece, grated): Adds warmth and complexity to the marinade.
  • Black pepper: For seasoning.

For the Salad:

  • Mixed salad greens (such as romaine lettuce, spinach, and arugula): These greens serve as the base for the salad and add freshness.
  • Cucumber (1 medium, julienned): Adds crunch and freshness.
  • Carrot (1 large, julienned): For a touch of sweetness and color.
  • Red cabbage (optional, thinly sliced): Adds a vibrant color and extra crunch.
  • Bell peppers (1-2, sliced): Sweet bell peppers complement the heat from the chicken and bring additional texture.
  • Green onions (2-3 stalks, chopped): Adds a fresh, mild onion flavor.
  • Cilantro (optional, a handful): Adds a herby and fresh note to the salad.
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish): Adds a nutty, crunchy topping.

For the Dressing (optional):

  • Gochujang (1 tablespoon): To enhance the spiciness and depth.
  • Soy sauce (1 tablespoon): To provide a salty umami flavor.
  • Sesame oil (1 teaspoon): For a nutty aroma.
  • Honey (1 teaspoon): To balance out the acidity and spiciness.
  • Rice vinegar (1 tablespoon): Adds brightness and tang to the dressing.
  • Water (1 tablespoon): To adjust the consistency of the dressing.

Instructions to Make Dak Gogi Salad

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken Marinade

The first step in making Dak Gogi Salad is to marinate the chicken. This allows the flavors to penetrate the chicken, making it tender and flavorful.

  1. In a bowl, combine gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, honey, and rice vinegar. Mix well until the marinade is smooth and consistent.
  2. Season the mixture with black pepper to taste.
  3. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces or strips. This ensures that the chicken cooks evenly and can easily be mixed into the salad later.
  4. Add the chicken to the marinade and toss it until fully coated. Cover the bowl and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes. For maximum flavor, marinate it for 1-2 hours or even overnight in the refrigerator.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

Once the chicken has marinated, it’s time to cook it.

  1. Heat a non-stick skillet or pan over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of oil, just enough to coat the bottom of the pan.
  2. Once the pan is hot, add the marinated chicken pieces and cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure the chicken is evenly cooked. The chicken should be golden brown and cooked through.
  3. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pan and set it aside to cool slightly.

Step 3: Prepare the Salad Ingredients

While the chicken is cooking, prepare the fresh vegetables for the salad.

  1. Wash and dry the salad greens thoroughly. Tear the lettuce or greens into bite-sized pieces and place them in a large bowl.
  2. Julienne the cucumber, carrot, and bell peppers. If using red cabbage, thinly slice it as well.
  3. Chop the green onions and cilantro (if using). These will add a burst of fresh flavor to the salad.
  4. Once all the vegetables are prepared, add them to the large salad bowl with the salad greens.

Step 4: Assemble the Dak Gogi Salad

Now that the chicken and vegetables are ready, it’s time to assemble the salad.

  1. Add the cooked chicken to the salad bowl with the vegetables.
  2. Gently toss the chicken and vegetables together until they are well combined. Be careful not to break up the chicken too much.
  3. If desired, drizzle a small amount of the optional dressing over the salad and toss again to coat the ingredients evenly.
  4. Garnish the salad with sesame seeds, green onions, and cilantro for added flavor and visual appeal.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!

Once everything is tossed together, the Dak Gogi Salad is ready to be served. You can serve it immediately while the chicken is still warm, or you can refrigerate the salad and serve it cold for a refreshing meal.

Tips for Making the Best Dak Gogi Salad

  1. Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder version of Dak Gogi Salad, you can reduce the amount of gochujang in the marinade or substitute it with a milder chili paste. On the other hand, if you love spice, feel free to add more gochujang or even some chili flakes to amp up the heat.
  2. Marinate the chicken longer: For the best flavor, marinate the chicken for at least 30 minutes, but if possible, allow it to sit in the marinade for 1-2 hours or overnight. The longer the chicken marinates, the more flavorful it will be.
  3. Add more vegetables: Feel free to add other vegetables like radishes, spinach, or cabbage for extra crunch and nutrition. The beauty of this salad is that it can be customized to your liking.
  4. Make it ahead of time: You can prepare the chicken and vegetables ahead of time. Store the marinated chicken separately in the refrigerator and assemble the salad just before serving. This will save time on busy days.
  5. Serve with rice or noodles: While Dak Gogi Salad is a filling dish on its own, it pairs wonderfully with steamed rice or even cold noodles for a more substantial meal.

Why You’ll Love Dak Gogi Salad

Dak Gogi Salad is a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that make every bite exciting. The tender, spicy chicken contrasts beautifully with the crisp, refreshing vegetables, creating a satisfying and well-balanced meal. It’s also a versatile dish that you can adjust to your taste and dietary preferences.

The combination of spicy, sweet, and sour elements makes this salad a favorite among those who enjoy bold flavors. Plus, it’s packed with protein, fiber, and vitamins, making it a nutritious and guilt-free option for anyone looking to eat healthily without sacrificing taste.

Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or just looking for a new, flavorful dish to add to your repertoire, Dak Gogi Salad is sure to impress. It’s simple to make, full of fresh ingredients, and will leave you craving more.

Final Thoughts

Korean Spicy Chicken Salad, or Dak Gogi Salad, is a delightful dish that brings together bold flavors and fresh ingredients in one bowl. The spicy chicken, mixed with crunchy vegetables, creates a perfect balance that’s both satisfying and refreshing. It’s a great option for anyone looking to explore Korean flavors or simply enjoy a healthy, delicious meal. Try it today and experience the magic of Korean cuisine right at home!
