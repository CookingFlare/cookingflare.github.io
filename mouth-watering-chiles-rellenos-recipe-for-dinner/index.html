Mouth-Watering Chiles Rellenos Recipe for Dinner


Are you ready to spice up your dinner routine with a delicious and flavorful dish? Look no further than the classic Mexican favorite - Chiles Rellenos! This mouth-watering recipe combines the perfect blend of roasted poblano peppers, savory stuffing, and a light, fluffy egg batter that will surely tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a cooking enthusiast looking to impress your dinner guests, this Chiles Rellenos recipe is guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • 1 cup of shredded Oaxaca cheese
  • 1 cup of shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup of corn kernels
  • 1/4 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup of diced onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Roast and Peel the Peppers:

    • Place the poblano peppers under a broiler or directly over a gas flame until the skins are charred.
    • Transfer the charred peppers to a bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let them steam for 10 minutes.
    • Peel off the skins, make a small slit down the side of each pepper, and remove the seeds.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a mixing bowl, combine the Oaxaca cheese, Monterey Jack cheese, corn kernels, cilantro, onion, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
    • Mix well to combine all the ingredients.

  3. Stuff the Peppers:

    • Carefully stuff each poblano pepper with the cheese and corn mixture, ensuring they are evenly filled.

  4. Prepare the Batter:

    • In a separate bowl, separate the eggs and beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form.
    • Gently fold in the egg yolks until just combined.

  5. Coat and Fry the Peppers:

    • Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Lightly coat each stuffed pepper in flour, then dip it into the egg batter.
    • Fry the peppers in the hot oil until golden brown on all sides.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Remove the fried Chiles Rellenos from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
    • Serve hot with your favorite salsa or sauce on the side.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For a healthier alternative, you can bake the stuffed peppers in the oven instead of frying them.
  • Customize the filling by adding cooked ground meat, beans, or different types of cheese to suit your taste preferences.
  • Make a large batch of Chiles Rellenos and freeze the leftovers for a quick and satisfying meal later on.
