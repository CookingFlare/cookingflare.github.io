Fall Pizzas: Tasty Ideas with Seasonal Squash and Bacon

3 min · 536 words

As the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and gold and the air becomes crisper, it’s the perfect time to embrace the flavors of fall in your cooking. One of the most delightful ways to do this is by creating delicious, seasonal pizzas that celebrate the bounties of autumn. In this blog post, we’ll explore some creative and mouthwatering pizza recipes featuring the rich, earthy flavors of seasonal squash and the savory, smoky goodness of bacon.

Butternut Squash and Maple Bacon Pizza

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza dough
  • 1 cup butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 6 strips maple bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • Fresh sage leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then roast them in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until tender and lightly caramelized.
  3. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness, then transfer it to a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle the pizza dough with a bit of olive oil and sprinkle the shredded mozzarella over the surface.
  5. Arrange the roasted butternut squash, crumbled maple bacon, red onion slices, and dollops of goat cheese on top of the cheese.
  6. Drizzle the pizza with maple syrup and scatter a few fresh sage leaves over the toppings.
  7. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

This Butternut Squash and Maple Bacon Pizza is a delightful blend of sweet and savory flavors, with the creamy goat cheese balancing out the smoky bacon and the caramelized squash adding a delightful sweetness.

Acorn Squash and Bacon Jam Pizza

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pizza dough
  • 1 small acorn squash, sliced into thin rounds
  • 8 slices bacon
  • 1 cup fontina cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup caramelized onions
  • 1/4 cup bacon jam
  • Fresh thyme leaves
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Lay the bacon slices on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes or until crispy. Once done, crumble the bacon into small pieces.
  3. In a skillet, cook the acorn squash slices in a bit of olive oil over medium-high heat until they are tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface and transfer it to a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  5. Brush the pizza dough with a bit of olive oil and sprinkle the shredded fontina cheese over the surface.
  6. Arrange the cooked acorn squash slices, caramelized onions, and bacon jam on top of the cheese, then sprinkle the crumbled bacon over the toppings.
  7. Place the pizza in the oven and bake for 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Drizzle the baked pizza with balsamic glaze and sprinkle with fresh thyme leaves before serving.

This Acorn Squash and Bacon Jam Pizza is a delightful medley of flavors, with the sweet and tangy bacon jam complementing the earthy acorn squash and the crispy bacon adding a satisfying crunch to every bite.

Conclusion

These fall-inspired pizzas with seasonal squash and bacon are a wonderful way to savor the flavors of autumn. Whether you’re hosting a cozy gathering with friends or simply craving a comforting meal on a chilly evening, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and warm your soul. Get creative with your toppings, experiment with different cheeses and herbs, and make the most of the abundant produce that the fall season has to offer. Enjoy the process of crafting these delicious pizzas, and savor every delicious slice!

So there you have it, two delectable fall pizza recipes that are perfect for celebrating the flavors of the season. We hope you enjoy making and, of course, eating these delicious creations. Happy cooking!
