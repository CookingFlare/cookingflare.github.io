Traditional Italian Easter Recipes: A Feast for the Senses


Easter in Italy is a time of joy, family, and flavorful food, filled with age-old traditions and delightful aromas. Discover some of the most popular Italian Easter dishes that will tantalize your taste buds and bring warmth to your celebration.

Lamb and Artichoke Terrine

Lamb is a key component of Italian Easter feasts, symbolizing Jesus’ sacrifice. This elegant terrine showcases the best of Italian cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground lamb
  • 2 cups chopped artichokes
  • ½ cup finely diced carrots
  • ½ cup finely diced celery
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 slices of bread, day-old, soaked and dried
  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, grated

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery, and cook until tender.
  2. Stir in the ground lamb and cook until browned.
  3. Add the chopped artichokes, chicken broth, and dried oregano. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Press the lamb mixture into a terrine or loaf pan, distributing it evenly.
  6. Layer the soaked bread slices on top and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake for 35 minutes, until heated through and the cheese is golden.
  8. Allow the terrine to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Easter Pie

This traditional Italian pie is a sweet and savory, indulgent delight, made with rich layers of flavors that will wow your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 2 cups cherry Jam
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 oz Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1 cup diced ham
  • 1 cup chopped pitted green olives
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh basil
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface and transfer it to a greased cookie sheet.
  2. In a bowl, mix the mascarpone cheese, cherry jam, ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, diced ham, chopped green olives, chopped hard-boiled eggs, parsley, basil, and oregano.
  3. Spread the filling evenly over the pizza dough and bake for 25 to 30 minutes, until golden and bubbly.
  4. Allow the pie to cool slightly before cutting and serving.

Easter Rice Pudding

This classic Italian dessert is a beloved Easter treat, light and comforting, with an aromatic, creamy texture that is irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 vanilla beans, split and scraped
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the Arborio rice, milk, sugar, vanilla beans and their pulp, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil and then reduce the heat to low.
  2. Simmer the rice pudding, stirring occasionally, for about 45 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the pudding is thick.
  3. Remove the vanilla beans and discard.
  4. Stir in the unsalted butter and let the pudding cool slightly.
  5. Serve the rice pudding warm, topped with a sprinkle of cinnamon and nutmeg, or chilled for a refreshing dessert.

As you gather with family and friends to celebrate the joyous festivities of Easter, indulge in these delicious and authentic Italian dishes that are sure to delight your senses and create a warm, hospitable atmosphere for all to enjoy. Buona Pasqua!
