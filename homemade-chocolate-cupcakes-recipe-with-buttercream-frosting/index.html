Homemade Chocolate Cupcakes Recipe with Buttercream Frosting


If you’re craving something sweet and indulgent, there’s nothing like a fresh batch of homemade chocolate cupcakes with creamy buttercream frosting. These cupcakes are soft, moist, and have a rich chocolate flavor that will surely satisfy your sweet tooth. Whether you’re baking for a birthday, a special celebration, or simply to treat yourself, this homemade recipe is the perfect choice.

In this post, we’ll guide you through each step of creating the most delicious chocolate cupcakes, from the moist cupcake base to the perfect buttercream frosting that will leave everyone asking for the recipe.

Ingredients for Chocolate Cupcakes

Before you get started, gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s a list of everything you’ll need for the cupcakes:

  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder (preferably Dutch-process)
  • 1 ½ tsp baking powder
  • 1 ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup brown sugar (light or dark)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil (or canola oil)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • ½ cup sour cream (room temperature)

Ingredients for Buttercream Frosting

For the buttercream frosting, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 4 cups powdered sugar (sifted)
  • 2-3 tbsp heavy cream (or whole milk)
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

How to Make Homemade Chocolate Cupcakes

Step 1: Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Pan

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 12-cup muffin pan with cupcake liners to ensure easy removal after baking. This is a crucial step as it ensures your cupcakes won’t stick to the pan and keeps them intact when you take them out of the oven.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: all-purpose flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisking them together evenly distributes the ingredients, ensuring your cupcakes rise uniformly. It also helps prevent clumps of cocoa powder that can affect the texture of the cupcakes.

Step 3: Combine the Wet Ingredients

In another mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, whole milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. This step allows the sugar to dissolve and the eggs to incorporate fully, making sure the cupcakes turn out light and fluffy.

Step 4: Add the Dry Ingredients to the Wet Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter, as doing so can cause the cupcakes to be dense. Once the batter is smooth and well combined, add the sour cream and mix until it is fully incorporated. The sour cream helps keep the cupcakes moist and tender, giving them a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Step 5: Add the Boiling Water

Now, slowly pour in the boiling water into the batter. Yes, you read that correctly! The hot water thins out the batter, giving the cupcakes a super moist and soft texture. Stir carefully until the water is fully incorporated into the batter. The batter will be thin, but that’s completely normal.

Step 6: Fill the Cupcake Liners

Using a spoon or an ice cream scoop, fill each cupcake liner about ¾ of the way full. This ensures that the cupcakes have room to rise without overflowing. Be sure to evenly distribute the batter to ensure all cupcakes bake evenly.

Step 7: Bake the Cupcakes

Place the muffin pan into the preheated oven and bake for 18-20 minutes. Keep an eye on them toward the end, and use a toothpick or cake tester to check for doneness. Insert it into the center of a cupcake – if it comes out clean or with just a few crumbs, they’re done. Remove the cupcakes from the oven and let them cool in the pan for about 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

How to Make the Perfect Buttercream Frosting

Now that your chocolate cupcakes are cooling down, it’s time to make the frosting. A rich, creamy buttercream frosting is the perfect complement to these moist cupcakes. Follow these easy steps to create a smooth, decadent buttercream.

Step 1: Beat the Butter

In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter on medium speed using an electric mixer or stand mixer. Beat the butter until it’s light and fluffy, which usually takes about 3 minutes. This process incorporates air into the butter, creating a light texture for your frosting.

Step 2: Add Powdered Sugar

Gradually add the sifted powdered sugar, one cup at a time, mixing on low speed to prevent a sugar cloud. Once all the sugar is added, increase the speed to medium-high and beat the mixture for another 3-4 minutes. This will help achieve the smooth and creamy texture you want for the frosting.

Step 3: Add Vanilla Extract and Salt

Next, add the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt to the frosting mixture. The vanilla adds depth and flavor, while the salt balances the sweetness. Mix well until everything is fully incorporated.

Step 4: Adjust Consistency with Cream

If the frosting seems too thick, add heavy cream, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach the desired consistency. If the frosting is too thin, add a little more powdered sugar. You want the frosting to be smooth and easily spreadable but not too runny.

Step 5: Beat the Frosting

Once the frosting has reached the right consistency, beat it on medium-high for an additional 1-2 minutes. This final step will make the frosting light and airy.

How to Frost the Cupcakes

Now that both your cupcakes and frosting are ready, it’s time to frost these beauties!

Step 1: Prepare the Piping Bag

Place a piping bag with a star tip (or any tip you like) inside a tall glass or a cup. Fold the edges of the piping bag over the glass to make filling easier. Spoon the buttercream frosting into the bag, then twist the top of the bag to seal it.

Step 2: Pipe the Frosting

Once the cupcakes are completely cooled, begin piping the frosting onto the cupcakes in a circular motion. Start from the center of the cupcake and spiral outward, finishing with a small swirl at the top. This classic technique creates a beautiful and professional look.

Step 3: Add Decorations

If desired, sprinkle some chocolate shavings, sprinkles, or even small candy pieces on top of the frosting for extra texture and decoration. This is where you can get creative and personalize your cupcakes to suit any occasion.

Tips for Making the Best Chocolate Cupcakes

  1. Use High-Quality Cocoa Powder: The cocoa powder is one of the most important ingredients in the cupcakes, so make sure to use a good-quality unsweetened cocoa powder for the best flavor. Dutch-process cocoa gives the cupcakes a richer, deeper chocolate taste.

  2. Don’t Overmix the Batter: When combining the dry and wet ingredients, mix until just combined. Overmixing can cause the cupcakes to be tough instead of light and fluffy.

  3. Let the Cupcakes Cool Completely: Before frosting, make sure the cupcakes have cooled completely. If they are still warm, the buttercream may melt and slide off the cupcakes.

  4. Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure that your butter, eggs, and sour cream are at room temperature before mixing. This helps them blend more easily and creates a smoother batter and frosting.

  5. Storage: If you have any leftover cupcakes, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. If you need to store them for a longer period, keep them in the fridge for up to a week, but allow them to come to room temperature before serving.

Conclusion

There you have it – a simple yet delicious homemade chocolate cupcakes recipe with a creamy buttercream frosting. These cupcakes are perfect for any occasion, from birthdays to casual gatherings, or just as a treat to enjoy with a cup of coffee or tea. The rich chocolate flavor combined with the smooth, buttery frosting creates a perfect balance that will keep you coming back for more.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get baking – these chocolate cupcakes are sure to be a hit with friends, family, and anyone with a sweet tooth!
