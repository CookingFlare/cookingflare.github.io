Budget-Friendly Mexican Side Dishes: Affordable and Flavorful


When it comes to cooking, Mexican cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors and bold combinations. From tangy salsas to hearty enchiladas, there’s something for everyone. However, you don’t have to break the bank to enjoy delicious Mexican dishes. In this blog post, we’ll explore some budget-friendly Mexican side dishes that are not only affordable but also packed with flavor. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Mexican Street Corn (Elote)

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup crumbled cotija cheese
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the corn until lightly charred, turning occasionally, for about 10 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise and sour cream.
  4. Once the corn is cooked, brush the mayonnaise-sour cream mixture onto each ear of corn.
  5. Sprinkle the cotija cheese and chili powder evenly over the corn.
  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro.
  7. Serve with lime wedges for squeezing over the corn.

2. Mexican Rice (Arroz Mexicano)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain well.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  4. Add the rice to the skillet and cook, stirring frequently, until the rice is lightly toasted, about 5 minutes.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth, tomato sauce, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with chopped cilantro, if desired.

3. Refried Beans (Frijoles Refritos)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups pinto beans, cooked and drained
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add the cooked pinto beans to the skillet and mash them with a potato masher or fork until they reach your desired consistency.
  4. Stir in the cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve hot as a side dish or as a filling for tacos, burritos, or quesadillas.

4. Mexican Coleslaw

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, mayonnaise, lime juice, chopped cilantro, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Toss well to coat the cabbage evenly with the dressing.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Serve chilled as a refreshing side dish.

5. Mexican Street Fries

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Russet potatoes, cut into fries
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Crumbled cotija cheese, for garnish
  • Chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the cut potatoes with vegetable oil, chili powder, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper until well coated.
  3. Spread the fries in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping the fries halfway through, until they are golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove from the oven and sprinkle with crumbled cotija cheese and chopped cilantro.
  6. Serve hot with lime wedges for squeezing over the fries.

These budget-friendly Mexican side dishes are not only easy to make but also bursting with flavors that will transport your taste buds to the streets of Mexico. Whether you’re hosting a Mexican-themed dinner party or simply want to add some spice to your weeknight meals, these recipes are sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, put on your sombrero, and get cooking! ¡Buen provecho!
