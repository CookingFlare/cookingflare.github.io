The Perfect Oatmeal: Top Techniques and Delightful Flavors


Oatmeal is a versatile and nutritious breakfast staple that never fails to satisfy. From classic rolled oats to quick-cooking varieties, choosing the right oats and implementing top techniques will result in a perfect bowl of oatmeal every time. To add even more delight, consider experimenting with a variety of flavors. In this post, we’ll share our top techniques and a few of our favorite oatmeal flavor combinations.

Choosing the Right Oats

The first step to making the perfect oatmeal is selecting the right oats. Rolled oats are the most commonly used variety and the ones we’ll be focusing on in this post. Here’s a quick overview of the different types of oats:

  1. Old-fashioned oats: These are the classic, whole, and unbroken rolled oats. They take the longest to cook but have the BEST texture.
  2. Quick oats: These oats are cut into smaller pieces, resulting in a faster cook time. They’re great for those with limited time in the morning, but their texture isn’t quite as pleasing as old-fashioned oats.
  3. Steel-cut oats: These oats are chopped into thick slices and take a bit longer to cook than rolled oats. They have a chewier texture and are often used in overnight oats recipes.
  4. Instant oats: As the name implies, these oats can be cooked in mere minutes. Instant oats have been pre-steamed and are more tender than other rolled oats. Some people prefer their texture, though many claim that they lack the satisfying texture of other oats.

For the perfect oatmeal, opt for old-fashioned or steel-cut oats, as they have the best texture. Steel-cut oats may need to be pre-soaked for a few hours to properly cook, but old-fashioned oats can go straight from the box into the pot.

Cooking Techniques

Once you have your oats chosen, the next step is to cook them perfectly. Here are a few techniques to ensure success:

  1. Ratio: Use a 1:2 ratio of oats to liquid. This means for every cup of oats, you’ll need 2 cups of liquid. Different liquids work, including water, milk, or a combination. You can even get creative and use almond, soy, or oat milk for added flavor.
  2. Boil and simmer: Boil the liquid and oats together, then reduce the heat to a low simmer. Stir often to prevent sticking and to ensure even cooking.
  3. Add-ins: Experiment with a variety of add-ins such as nuts, seeds, fruits, maple syrup, or chocolate chips. Get creative and make it your own! You can even cook your oats in the microwave for a faster option.

Favorite Oatmeal Flavor Combos

Now that you know the key techniques, let’s dive into a few of our favorite oatmeal flavor combinations to inspire your creations:

  1. Classic: Hot water, rolled oats, pinch of salt, and a drizzle of honey. Top with sliced bananas and chopped nuts.
  2. Apple Cinnamon: Cooked in cinnamon-infused milk and topped with diced apples and a sprinkle of cinnamon.
  3. Lemon Blueberry: Swirl in some freshly squeezed lemon juice and top with plump, fresh blueberries.
  4. Chocolate Chai: Warm spices like cinnamon, cardamom, and ginger mixed into milk with cocoa powder. Top with raisins and a dollop of whipped cream for an indulgent treat.
  5. Coconut Lime: A dash of coconut extract in the cooking liquid, along with fresh lime zest and shredded coconut. Yum!

Now that you’ve armed yourself with techniques and flavor combinations, start experimenting with your oatmeal. Don’t be afraid to get creative and make it your own! Remember, the perfect oatmeal is all about personal preference. Happy cooking!
