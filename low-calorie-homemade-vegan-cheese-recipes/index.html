Low-Calorie Homemade Vegan Cheese Recipes


Cheese is undeniably one of the most beloved food items worldwide, but for those following a vegan lifestyle or aiming to reduce their calorie intake, traditional cheese may not fit into the equation. Fortunately, creating your own low-calorie vegan cheese at home is easier than you might think. With a few simple ingredients and basic techniques, you can enjoy creamy, flavorful alternatives that are not only healthier but also kinder to the environment.

In this post, you’ll find delicious homemade vegan cheese recipes that satisfy your craving for cheesy goodness without compromising your dietary goals. We’ll dive into creative ways to replicate the textures, flavors, and versatility of traditional cheese—all while keeping it nutritious and low in calories.

The Basics of Vegan Cheese

Before jumping into the recipes, let’s cover some fundamentals that will help you create successful vegan cheese every time. Most vegan cheeses rely on plant-based ingredients to mimic the creamy texture and nuanced flavors of dairy cheese. Commonly used components include nuts, seeds, non-dairy milk or yogurt, tofu, nutritional yeast, and tapioca starch. These are combined with various seasonings, acids, and thickeners to create different types of cheese, ranging from spreadable cream cheese to firmer slicing cheeses.

Key Ingredients

  1. Nuts and Seeds: Cashews, almonds, macadamia nuts, and sunflower seeds are popular bases for creamy vegan cheese. They offer richness without being overly caloric. If you’re looking for ultra-low-calorie options, try silken tofu or cooked cauliflower instead.

  2. Nutritional Yeast: This pantry staple gives vegan cheeses their cheesy and nutty flavor. It’s also packed with vitamins, especially B12, making it an essential ingredient for plant-based eaters.

  3. Acids: Lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, and sometimes white wine vinegar provide the tanginess necessary to create a cheese-like flavor profile.

  4. Thickeners: Tapioca starch or agar-agar can yield stretchy and melty textures ideal for pizzas and grilled cheese sandwiches.

  5. Spices and Seasonings: Garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, pepper, and fresh herbs like chives or dill are used to customize the flavor.

Recipe 1: Cashew-Based Cream Cheese Spread

Perfect for bagels, crackers, or as a dip for fresh vegetables, this creamy cashew-based cheese is a crowd-pleaser. It uses simple pantry staples to achieve a silky consistency and delicious flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked in water for 4-6 hours)
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2-3 tablespoons water (adjust for consistency)

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked cashews thoroughly.
  2. In a high-speed blender or food processor, combine the cashews, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, garlic (if using), salt, and 2 tablespoons of water.
  3. Blend until smooth. If necessary, add another tablespoon of water to achieve a creamy spreadable texture.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning as desired.
  5. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week.

Recipe 2: Tofu Feta Cheese

This tofu-based recipe is tangy, crumbly, and perfect for salads, grain bowls, or as a topping for roasted vegetables. It’s lower in calories compared to nut-based vegan cheeses but still packs plenty of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block (14 oz) firm tofu (pressed to remove excess water)
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 3 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive brine (or water mixed with 1/4 teaspoon salt)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the pressed tofu into small cubes or crumble it into bite-sized pieces, depending on your preference.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, olive brine, oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt.
  3. Place the tofu pieces in a shallow dish or jar and pour the mixture over them until fully submerged.
  4. Cover and let marinate in the refrigerator for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight.
  5. Drain any excess liquid and serve as needed.

Recipe 3: Stretchy Tapioca Mozzarella

This stretchy vegan mozzarella is perfect for pizza, lasagna, or grilled cheese sandwiches. The combination of tapioca starch and creamy ingredients creates a melt-worthy cheese you’ll love.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup raw cashews (soaked in water for 4 hours)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened non-dairy milk
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon tapioca starch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked cashews.
  2. Combine the cashews, non-dairy milk, water, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, tapioca starch, and salt in a blender. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the mixture into a small saucepan and heat over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  4. Within a few minutes, the mixture will thicken and become stretchy. Adjust the heat as needed to prevent burning.
  5. Serve immediately while warm for optimal stretchiness, or refrigerate for later use. Reheat gently on the stovetop for best results.

Recipe 4: Cauliflower Cheese Sauce

If you’re looking for ultra-low-calorie cheesy goodness, this cauliflower-based sauce is a fantastic option. It’s creamy, light, and versatile enough to drizzle over pasta, baked potatoes, or steamed veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cauliflower florets (steamed until soft)
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened non-dairy milk
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets until tender, then transfer them to a blender or food processor.
  2. Add the non-dairy milk, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy. If necessary, add a bit more milk for a thinner sauce.
  4. Serve warm over your favorite dishes, or store in the refrigerator for up to three days.

Tips for Perfecting Homemade Vegan Cheese

  1. Experiment with Consistency: The texture of your cheese can be adjusted to suit specific purposes. For spreads, blend the ingredients until perfectly smooth. For firmer cheeses, use agar-agar or refrigerate the mixture for several hours.

  2. Customize the Flavor: Don’t be afraid to play around with seasonings. Add fresh herbs, sun-dried tomatoes, or spices to create unique variations.

  3. Use High-Quality Ingredients: The flavor of vegan cheese largely depends on the quality of ingredients, so opt for organic produce and fresh spices whenever possible.

  4. Storage: Homemade vegan cheese usually has a shorter shelf life than commercial versions. Store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator and consume within a week.

Final Thoughts

Making vegan cheese at home offers endless possibilities for customization, creativity, and health-conscious eating. Whether you’re seeking a protein-packed spread or a flavorful sauce, these recipes provide a fantastic starting point for exploring the world of plant-based cheeses. By swapping out calorie-dense dairy cheese with one of these vibrant alternatives, you’ll not only stay on track with your wellness goals but also enjoy incredible depths of flavor.

Try these recipes today and discover how versatile vegan cheese can be! Share your creations with friends and family, and watch even the most devoted dairy lovers become fans of these delicious alternatives.
