Korean Grilled Shrimp (Saeu Gui) Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, unique combinations of savory and spicy ingredients, and the creative use of grilling methods. One dish that stands out for its deliciousness and simplicity is Korean Grilled Shrimp (Saeu Gui). This dish showcases tender, juicy shrimp marinated in a savory-sweet marinade and grilled to perfection, making it a perfect choice for any grilling occasion.

In this recipe, we will explore every step of making Saeu Gui, from marinating the shrimp to the best grilling techniques, and how you can enjoy this dish at home. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue, craving a quick dinner, or looking to explore Korean food, this recipe will guide you through a delicious experience.

Ingredients Youâ€™ll Need

For the marinade:

  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce: The saltiness of soy sauce gives the shrimp a savory base flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil: Adds a nutty, fragrant note to the marinade.
  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar: Provides a gentle sweetness to balance the saltiness.
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine or mirin: This adds a touch of acidity and depth to the flavor profile.
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red chili paste): Gives the dish a mild heat and depth.
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced): Fresh garlic enhances the savory flavors and adds a bit of sharpness.
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (minced): Ginger provides a warming, aromatic note to the dish.
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper: A subtle spice that complements the other flavors.

For the shrimp:

  • 1 pound medium to large shrimp: Fresh or thawed shrimp works best for grilling, with the shells removed and deveined.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds: Optional, for garnish.
  • 1-2 green onions: Optional, thinly sliced, for garnish.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Korean Grilled Shrimp (Saeu Gui)

1. Prepare the Shrimp

Start by preparing the shrimp. If you’re using frozen shrimp, ensure they are fully thawed before cooking. Remove the shells and devein the shrimp if they haven’t been cleaned already. Leave the tails on, as they add a nice touch when serving, and they make handling the shrimp easier on the grill.

Rinse the shrimp under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Dry shrimp will absorb the marinade better and grill more evenly.

2. Prepare the Marinade

In a medium-sized bowl, mix together the soy sauce, sesame oil, honey (or sugar), rice wine (or mirin), gochujang, garlic, ginger, and black pepper. Stir until all the ingredients are fully combined. The marinade should have a nice balance of salty, sweet, and spicy flavors, with a hint of umami from the gochujang.

Once the marinade is prepared, taste it and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you prefer a sweeter marinade, add a bit more honey. For more spice, you can increase the amount of gochujang or add a pinch of Korean chili flakes (gochugaru).

3. Marinate the Shrimp

Place the cleaned shrimp into a large zip-top plastic bag or shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the shrimp and toss to coat each piece evenly. Seal the bag or cover the dish, and let the shrimp marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, but for the best flavor, aim for 1 to 2 hours.

While marinating, the shrimp will absorb all the delicious flavors from the marinade, allowing the gochujang and sesame oil to infuse them with a savory and slightly spicy profile.

4. Preheat the Grill

While the shrimp is marinating, prepare your grill. Whether you’re using a charcoal grill, a gas grill, or a grill pan, make sure it’s preheated to medium-high heat. For a charcoal grill, let the coals burn down until they are covered with white ash, indicating a consistent, medium heat. For a gas grill, set it to medium-high heat and allow it to warm up for about 10-15 minutes.

If you’re using a grill pan indoors, heat it over medium-high heat, lightly oiling the pan to prevent sticking.

5. Grill the Shrimp

Once your grill is ready, it’s time to cook the shrimp. Skewer the shrimp onto metal or wooden skewers (if using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes before use to prevent burning). Place the shrimp on the grill in a single layer, ensuring they are not overcrowded.

Grill the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn pink and opaque. Shrimp cooks very quickly, so keep a close eye on them to avoid overcooking. The grilling time will depend on the size of your shrimp and the heat of your grill, but they should develop a nice char on the outside while remaining tender and juicy on the inside.

6. Serve and Garnish

Once grilled to perfection, remove the shrimp from the skewers and place them on a serving plate. If you like, sprinkle the shrimp with toasted sesame seeds and garnish with thinly sliced green onions for a touch of color and flavor.

Saeu Gui is often served with a side of steamed rice, pickled vegetables, or a refreshing salad, making it a perfect dish for any meal. You can also enjoy it with a bowl of warm soup or a Korean-style dipping sauce.

Tips for Perfect Korean Grilled Shrimp

  • Marinating time: If you have the time, let the shrimp marinate for longer. The flavors will become more intense, and the shrimp will absorb more of the marinade. However, if you’re in a rush, even 30 minutes will do the trick.
  • Donâ€™t overcook: Shrimp can become tough and rubbery if overcooked, so be sure to watch the grill carefully. As soon as they turn pink and opaque, theyâ€™re ready to be removed.
  • Use a grill basket: If you’re concerned about shrimp falling through the grill grates, use a grill basket designed for seafood or a piece of foil with holes poked through. This allows you to cook the shrimp without worrying about them slipping through.
  • Customizing the marinade: The marinade for Saeu Gui is highly customizable. If you prefer a sweeter marinade, increase the honey. For a more pronounced heat, add extra gochujang or even a pinch of dried chili flakes. You can also experiment with adding citrus zest or fresh herbs for an added twist.
  • Side dishes: Serve Saeu Gui with traditional Korean side dishes like kimchi, sautÃ©ed spinach, or a bowl of warm soup. You can also add a small bowl of ssamjang (a thick dipping sauce made from gochujang and fermented soybean paste) for an extra burst of flavor.

Pairing Saeu Gui with Drinks

Korean Grilled Shrimp pairs beautifully with a variety of drinks, enhancing the overall dining experience. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Soju: This popular Korean distilled spirit is a great match for grilled shrimp. Its clean, neutral flavor helps balance the spices in the dish.
  • Makgeolli: A sweet, slightly tangy rice wine, Makgeolli complements the umami of the shrimp and is perfect for those looking for a less potent alternative to soju.
  • Beer: If you prefer beer, go for a crisp, light lager or a pilsner. The refreshing qualities of the beer pair well with the grilled shrimp, balancing out the flavors.
  • White Wine: A light, dry white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio can also work well with Saeu Gui. The acidity and crispness of the wine will cut through the richness of the shrimp.
