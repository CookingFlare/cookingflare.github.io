Quick and Easy Thanksgiving Side Dishes for Busy Cooks


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. But as a busy cook, you may find yourself overwhelmed with all the preparations that come with hosting this special holiday. That’s why I’m here to help! In this blog post, I’ll share some quick and easy Thanksgiving side dish recipes that will impress your guests without taking up too much of your precious time. So let’s get cooking!

1. Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in a large pot of salted water until they are fork-tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. In a separate saucepan, heat the milk until warm.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked potatoes, garlic butter, and warm milk. Mash together until smooth and creamy.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.

2. Honey Glazed Carrots

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of baby carrots
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Steam or boil the baby carrots until they are tender but still slightly crisp. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the honey and thyme leaves, stirring until well combined.
  3. Add the cooked carrots to the skillet and toss until they are evenly coated with the honey glaze.
  4. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the glaze has thickened slightly.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve warm.

3. Cranberry Pecan Salad

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups of mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup of dried cranberries
  • 1 cup of chopped pecans
  • 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • Balsamic vinaigrette dressing

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, and crumbled feta cheese.
  2. Drizzle with your favorite balsamic vinaigrette dressing. Toss until all the ingredients are well coated.
  3. Serve immediately.

4. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper until they are evenly coated.
  3. Spread the Brussels sprouts out in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, until they are tender and lightly browned.
  5. Remove from the oven and sprinkle with the crumbled bacon. Serve hot.

5. Cornbread Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups of cornbread, crumbled
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 teaspoons of dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and celery, and cook until they are softened.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the crumbled cornbread, cooked onion and celery mixture, chicken broth, dried sage, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a greased baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve warm.

These quick and easy Thanksgiving side dishes are sure to be a hit at your holiday gathering. With minimal effort and maximum flavor, you can impress your guests without spending hours in the kitchen. So go ahead and enjoy a stress-free Thanksgiving feast with your loved ones. Happy cooking!
