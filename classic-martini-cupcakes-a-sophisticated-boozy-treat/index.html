Classic Martini Cupcakes: A Sophisticated Boozy Treat


If you’re looking for a unique and sophisticated treat to serve at your next gathering, look no further than Classic Martini Cupcakes. These delightful little cakes are infused with the flavors of a classic martini, making them a perfect indulgence for cocktail lovers. With a moist and tender crumb, a subtle boozy kick, and a tangy citrus twist, these cupcakes are sure to impress both your taste buds and your guests.

Ingredients

For the cupcakes:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup vodka
  • 2 tablespoons dry vermouth
  • Zest of 1 lemon

For the martini buttercream frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vodka
  • 1 tablespoon dry vermouth
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake pan with paper liners.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This should take about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  5. In a separate small bowl, combine the milk, vodka, dry vermouth, and lemon zest.

  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the milk mixture. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.

  7. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full.

  8. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean.

  9. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the martini buttercream frosting. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy.

  10. Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, beating well after each addition.

  11. Stir in the vodka, dry vermouth, lemon juice, and lemon zest until well combined.

  12. Once the cupcakes are completely cool, generously frost each one with the martini buttercream frosting.

  13. For an extra touch of elegance, garnish each cupcake with a twist of lemon zest or a small cocktail pick.

Serving Suggestions

These Classic Martini Cupcakes are perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a sophisticated cocktail party or a casual get-together with friends. Serve them alongside your favorite martini cocktail for a truly indulgent experience. The tangy citrus flavors of the cupcakes pair beautifully with the creamy and boozy buttercream frosting, creating a harmonious balance that will leave your guests craving more.

Storage Tips

If you have any leftover cupcakes (which is unlikely!), store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. However, it’s best to enjoy them fresh to fully appreciate their moist texture and vibrant flavors.

Conclusion

Classic Martini Cupcakes are a delightful twist on a classic cocktail, bringing together the flavors of a martini in a sweet and sophisticated treat. With their moist crumb, subtle boozy kick, and tangy citrus twist, these cupcakes are sure to impress your guests and become a favorite in your dessert repertoire. Enjoy them at your next gathering and let the compliments roll in!
