Best Summer Picnic Recipes for a Crowd


When the sun is shining and the weather is warm, there’s nothing quite like gathering with friends and family for a summer picnic. Whether you’re celebrating a birthday, enjoying a long weekend, or just savoring the season, picnics are an ideal way to relax and indulge in delicious food surrounded by nature. However, planning a picnic for a crowd can be challenging—especially when you want to make sure everyone is satisfied with the variety and flavors on offer.

The key to a successful summer picnic lies in choosing dishes that are not only tasty but also easy to transport, serve, and eat outdoors. If you’re tasked with organizing the ultimate picnic for a large group, this collection of best summer picnic recipes is here to help. From refreshing salads and savory sandwiches to sweet treats and refreshing beverages, these recipes are guaranteed to be crowd-pleasers.

1. Classic Chicken Salad

A classic chicken salad is always a crowd favorite, and for good reason. It’s versatile, easy to make, and can be enjoyed in a variety of ways—whether as a sandwich filling, a topping for a salad, or a simple side dish. This recipe uses rotisserie chicken to save time, but you can also cook your chicken from scratch if you prefer.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cooked and shredded chicken (rotisserie or homemade)
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup celery, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup red grapes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice.
  2. Add in the celery, grapes, and green onions, and mix until well combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve the chicken salad on a bed of lettuce or as a filling for sandwiches, wraps, or croissants.

This chicken salad is a perfect balance of creamy, crunchy, and sweet flavors, making it an excellent choice for a summer picnic.

2. Caprese Pasta Salad

A pasta salad is an excellent picnic dish because it can be made ahead and served cold. The Caprese pasta salad is fresh, vibrant, and packed with flavor from the combination of ripe tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pasta (such as fusilli or rotini)
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella, cubed
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions. Drain and rinse with cold water to cool it down.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooled pasta, cherry tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the dressing over the pasta salad and toss until everything is evenly coated.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.

This dish is incredibly refreshing and simple, with the balsamic vinegar adding a tangy sweetness that complements the fresh ingredients.

3. Grilled Veggie Skewers

For those looking to incorporate more vegetables into their picnic spread, grilled veggie skewers are a great option. The smoky flavor from the grill combined with the freshness of the vegetables creates a delightful combination. You can use whatever vegetables are in season, but bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and onions are always great choices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 bell peppers, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 oz mushrooms, whole
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating the different veggies.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the skewers with the marinade, making sure each vegetable is coated.
  5. Grill the skewers for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature.

These veggie skewers are not only easy to prepare, but they’re also healthy and packed with flavor. You can even prep them ahead of time and cook them on-site for added convenience.

4. Ultimate Picnic Sandwiches

When it comes to sandwiches, there’s no one-size-fits-all option. For a crowd, it’s best to offer a variety of sandwiches to satisfy different tastes. The following ultimate picnic sandwich assortment will please everyone—from meat lovers to vegetarians.

Ingredients:

  • Whole wheat or white sandwich bread
  • Sliced deli meats (turkey, ham, roast beef)
  • Cheese (cheddar, Swiss, provolone)
  • Lettuce, tomato, and pickles
  • Hummus and avocado for spreading
  • Mustard, mayonnaise, and other condiments

Instructions:

  1. Layer the deli meats, cheese, lettuce, tomato, and pickles on slices of bread.
  2. Add condiments like mustard or mayonnaise as desired.
  3. For vegetarian options, spread hummus or mashed avocado on the bread and top with veggies such as cucumbers, sprouts, and shredded carrots.
  4. Slice the sandwiches into smaller portions for easy serving.

This customizable sandwich platter ensures that there’s something for everyone at your picnic. Plus, they’re simple to assemble and easy to eat on the go.

5. Watermelon Feta Salad

A refreshing salad that embodies the flavors of summer, watermelon feta salad is light, sweet, and savory. It’s perfect for hot days and pairs wonderfully with grilled meats and sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cubed watermelon, crumbled feta, and chopped mint.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and lime juice.
  3. Gently toss to combine and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve chilled.

This salad is the epitome of summer, with its juicy watermelon and creamy feta creating a balance of flavors. It’s simple, light, and a hit for large groups.

6. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herbed Butter

Grilled corn on the cob is a timeless summer favorite, and adding herbed butter takes it to the next level. The smoky grill flavor paired with the rich and fragrant butter makes this dish irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the corn for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until the kernels are charred and tender.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter, parsley, chives, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Once the corn is grilled, brush with the herbed butter.
  5. Serve immediately.

The addition of fresh herbs and garlic elevates the flavor of the corn, making it a standout dish at any picnic.

7. Lemonade with Fresh Herbs

No picnic is complete without a refreshing beverage, and homemade lemonade is always a favorite. By infusing it with fresh herbs like mint or basil, you can add an extra layer of flavor that will make it even more refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups water
  • 1 cup fresh lemon juice (about 6 lemons)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint or basil leaves
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

This lemonade is the perfect thirst quencher, and the herbs add an extra burst of flavor that’s perfect for summer.

8. Chocolate-Dipped Fruit

For a sweet finish to your picnic, chocolate-dipped fruit is an easy yet indulgent treat. The rich, smooth chocolate pairs wonderfully with the fresh, juicy fruit, making it the perfect picnic dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups strawberries, washed and dried
  • 2 bananas, sliced
  • 1 cup dark or milk chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt the chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl, stirring every 30 seconds until smooth.
  2. Dip each piece of fruit into the melted chocolate, making sure it’s coated.
  3. Place the dipped fruit on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate until the chocolate hardens.
  4. Serve chilled.

This dessert is a fun and delicious way to enjoy fruit, and it’s simple to prepare ahead of time.

Conclusion

A summer picnic for a crowd is all about offering a variety of delicious, easy-to-eat dishes that will please everyone. From savory dishes like grilled veggie skewers and classic chicken salad to refreshing options like watermelon feta salad and lemonade, these recipes are perfect for outdoor gatherings. Each recipe is simple to prepare, transport, and serve, ensuring that your picnic is a memorable and enjoyable experience for all.

Remember, the best picnics are the ones where you can relax, enjoy good company, and savor great food. So gather your friends, pack your basket, and head outdoors to enjoy these delightful summer picnic recipes!
