A Culinary Tour of Southeast Asia: 8 Recipes to Try


Southeast Asia is a vibrant region known for its rich culinary heritage, blending diverse ingredients, spices, and cooking techniques. From the bustling street markets of Thailand to the tropical islands of Indonesia, Southeast Asian cuisine offers a unique combination of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy flavors that will delight any food enthusiast. Whether you’re an experienced chef or a beginner looking to expand your palate, these eight dishes will transport you to the heart of Southeast Asia.

1. Pad Thai (Thailand)

Pad Thai is arguably one of the most famous Thai dishes outside of Thailand. This stir-fried noodle dish is bursting with flavor, thanks to its sweet and sour tamarind sauce, salty fish sauce, and a hint of spiciness from chili. Traditionally served with shrimp, chicken, or tofu, Pad Thai is a perfect blend of textures and tastes that make it a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 egg
  • 100g shrimp (or tofu for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon chili flakes (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or wok. Add the garlic and stir-fry for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Push the garlic to one side of the pan, and crack the egg into the other side. Scramble the egg until cooked.
  4. Add the shrimp (or tofu) to the pan and cook until they turn pink and are cooked through.
  5. Toss in the noodles, followed by tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, and lime juice. Stir everything together, ensuring the noodles are well-coated with the sauce.
  6. Stir in the bean sprouts and chili flakes (if using), then remove from heat.
  7. Plate the dish, sprinkle with crushed peanuts, and garnish with fresh cilantro and a lime wedge.

This dish is perfect for an easy weeknight dinner or as part of a larger spread at a dinner party.

2. Pho (Vietnam)

No exploration of Southeast Asian cuisine is complete without a mention of Pho, the iconic Vietnamese noodle soup. Known for its fragrant broth, this dish is typically enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or lunch. Pho is made with beef or chicken and is served with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and lime. The secret to a great Pho lies in the slow-simmered broth that infuses deep, savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef bones (or chicken carcass for a lighter broth)
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 piece of ginger (about 4 inches), halved
  • 2 star anise
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g beef sirloin (or chicken breast)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Fresh cilantro
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Sriracha sauce

Instructions:

  1. Char the onion and ginger over an open flame or on a hot pan until they are lightly blackened. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, add the beef bones (or chicken) and enough water to cover them. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 2-3 hours, skimming off any impurities that float to the top.
  3. After 2-3 hours, add the charred onion and ginger, star anise, cloves, and cinnamon stick to the pot. Continue to simmer for another hour, adding fish sauce for seasoning.
  4. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  5. Slice the beef (or chicken) into thin pieces.
  6. To serve, place the cooked noodles in a bowl, add the raw slices of beef on top, and ladle the hot broth over the top. The heat from the broth will cook the beef.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and lime wedges. Serve with hoisin and Sriracha sauce on the side for additional flavor.

Pho is perfect for a cozy meal on a cold day and offers an authentic taste of Vietnam.

3. Nasi Lemak (Malaysia)

Considered the national dish of Malaysia, Nasi Lemak is a fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk, often served with a variety of accompaniments such as fried anchovies, hard-boiled eggs, cucumber, and a spicy sambal sauce. While Nasi Lemak can be enjoyed as a breakfast dish, it is also commonly eaten throughout the day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 pandan leaf (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 cup dried anchovies
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1/2 cup sambal (spicy chili paste)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the jasmine rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain.
  2. In a large pot, combine the rice, coconut milk, water, and salt. If using, add the pandan leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.
  3. In a small pan, fry the dried anchovies until crispy and golden brown. Set aside.
  4. To serve, scoop a portion of coconut rice onto a plate. Top with fried anchovies, a hard-boiled egg, cucumber slices, and a generous spoonful of sambal.
  5. Enjoy this dish as a hearty breakfast or a delicious lunch.

Nasi Lemak is a delightful dish that captures the essence of Malaysian cuisine with its harmonious balance of flavors and textures.

4. Bánh Xèo (Vietnam)

Bánh Xèo is a savory Vietnamese pancake filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts. It is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, offering a perfect contrast in texture. Typically eaten as a snack or light meal, Bánh Xèo is served with fresh herbs and dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 100g pork belly, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • Fresh herbs (mint, basil, cilantro)
  • Fish sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together rice flour, coconut milk, turmeric, salt, and water to make the batter.
  2. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a little oil.
  3. Pour a thin layer of batter into the pan, swirling it to cover the bottom. Cook until the edges become crispy.
  4. Add the shrimp, pork belly, and bean sprouts to one side of the pancake. Cover with a lid and cook for 2-3 minutes until the filling is cooked through and the pancake is crispy.
  5. Fold the pancake in half and transfer to a plate.
  6. Serve with fresh herbs and fish sauce for dipping.

Bánh Xèo is a fun and interactive dish, perfect for sharing with friends and family.

5. Laksa (Malaysia/Singapore)

Laksa is a spicy noodle soup that combines the flavors of Southeast Asia in a single bowl. The creamy coconut milk broth or tangy tamarind-based broth, paired with noodles, shrimp, and chicken, makes for a rich and comforting dish. Laksa is a must-try for anyone exploring the culinary traditions of Malaysia and Singapore.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g shrimp (or chicken)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • Fresh cilantro, mint, and lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the curry paste until fragrant, then add coconut milk, tamarind paste, and fish sauce. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the shrimp (or chicken) and cook until they are just done.
  4. To serve, place a portion of noodles in a bowl, pour the soup over the noodles, and top with boiled eggs, fresh herbs, and lime wedges.

Laksa is a flavorful and satisfying dish that captures the essence of the region’s love for bold, aromatic broths.

6. Soto Ayam (Indonesia)

Soto Ayam is a traditional Indonesian chicken soup made with turmeric and served with rice, boiled eggs, and sometimes vermicelli. It’s a hearty, comforting dish perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 2 tablespoons turmeric powder
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, chopped
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • 200g cooked rice
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Boil the chicken with turmeric, garlic, shallots, lemongrass, and bay leaf in a large pot. Let it simmer until the chicken is cooked and the broth is flavorful.
  2. Shred the chicken and return it to the pot. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper.
  3. To serve, place a scoop of cooked rice in a bowl, add the shredded chicken and pour the broth over the top. Garnish with boiled eggs, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges.

Soto Ayam offers a comforting and aromatic meal, perfect for any occasion.

7. Satay (Indonesia/Malaysia)

Satay is a popular Southeast Asian street food consisting of skewered and grilled meat, usually served with a rich peanut sauce. While it’s commonly made with chicken, beef or lamb is also used.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thighs, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Skewers for grilling

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the chicken with soy sauce, brown sugar, turmeric powder, and vegetable oil for at least 1 hour.
  2. Thread the marinated chicken onto the skewers.
  3. Grill the chicken over medium heat for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  4. In a small saucepan, mix peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, and brown sugar. Heat until the sauce is smooth and thick.
  5. Serve the grilled satay with peanut sauce for dipping.

Satay is a great appetizer or party snack, and its smoky, savory flavor will surely impress your guests.

8. Khao Pad (Thailand)

Khao Pad, or Thai fried rice, is a flavorful and easy dish made with jasmine rice, vegetables, and your choice of protein. This dish is often served as a quick meal in Thailand and can be customized based on what ingredients are available.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, peas, corn)
  • 2 eggs
  • 100g shrimp (or chicken)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Heat a pan and scramble the eggs until cooked. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, stir-fry shrimp (or chicken) with mixed vegetables until cooked.
  3. Add the cooked rice, fish sauce, soy sauce, and lime juice. Stir-fry everything together until well-mixed and heated through.
  4. Garnish with fresh cilantro and lime wedges before serving.

Khao Pad is a simple yet delicious dish that brings the flavors of Thailand right into your home.

Southeast Asian cuisine is a vibrant fusion of flavors that can transform any meal into an unforgettable experience. Whether you’re seeking the fiery heat of Thai curries or the soothing warmth of Vietnamese soups, these eight recipes will give you a true taste of the region. The beauty of Southeast Asian cooking lies in its simplicity, variety, and boldness of flavors, making it easy to recreate these dishes in your own kitchen. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary journey that will transport your taste buds to the heart of Southeast Asia!
