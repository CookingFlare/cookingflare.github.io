Easy and Delicious Chicken Enchiladas: A Simple Mexican Treat


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you’ll love this easy and delicious chicken enchiladas recipe. Packed with flavor and simple to make, this dish is a perfect choice for a family dinner or a gathering with friends. Let’s dive into the details of this mouthwatering Mexican treat.

Ingredients

For the Enchiladas:

  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken
  • 1 can (10 ounces) red enchilada sauce
  • 1 can (4 ounces) chopped green chilies
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 8 flour tortillas (8 inches)
  • 2 cups shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, chopped fresh cilantro, sliced green onions

For the Enchilada Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 can (8 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Enchilada Sauce

  1. In a saucepan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the flour and chili powder, and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add the remaining ingredients for the enchilada sauce, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, 1/2 cup of the enchilada sauce, green chilies, chopped onion, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.

Step 3: Assemble the Enchiladas

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Spread 1/2 cup of the enchilada sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  3. Warm the tortillas in the microwave for a few seconds to make them pliable.
  4. Spoon about 1/3 cup of the chicken filling onto each tortilla, then sprinkle with a small handful of Monterey Jack cheese.
  5. Roll up the tortillas and place seam-side down in the baking dish.
  6. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the top, then sprinkle with the remaining cheese.

Step 4: Bake and Serve

  1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  2. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.
  3. Let the enchiladas cool for a few minutes, then serve with optional toppings such as sour cream, chopped fresh cilantro, and sliced green onions.

Tips and Variations

  • For a time-saving option, you can use store-bought rotisserie chicken and pre-made enchilada sauce.
  • Feel free to customize the filling with additional ingredients such as black beans, corn, or diced tomatoes.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, add a dash of hot sauce or extra chopped green chilies to the filling.

In Conclusion

These easy and delicious chicken enchiladas are sure to become a staple in your recipe collection. With simple ingredients and a straightforward cooking process, you can enjoy the flavors of Mexico right in your own kitchen. Whether it’s a busy weeknight or a casual weekend gathering, this dish is a crowd-pleaser that will have everyone coming back for seconds. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to savor this simple Mexican treat!
