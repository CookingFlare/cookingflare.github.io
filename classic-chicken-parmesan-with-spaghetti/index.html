Classic Chicken Parmesan with Spaghetti


There’s something deeply satisfying about the combination of crispy breaded chicken, savory marinara sauce, gooey melted cheese, and perfectly al dente spaghetti. Chicken Parmesan, or Chicken Parmigiana, is one of those dishes that bridges generations and continents, always bringing a sense of warmth and comfort to the table. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner, a special occasion, or just craving a hearty meal, this classic dish delivers on flavor and texture.

In this extensive guide, we’re going to dive deep into everything you need to know about making an exceptional Chicken Parmesan with spaghetti. From selecting the right ingredients to mastering the technique of breading and frying the chicken, and crafting the perfect tomato sauce and spaghetti to accompany it—consider this your ultimate reference.

Why Chicken Parmesan With Spaghetti Reigns Supreme

Chicken Parmesan has roots in both Italy and America, evolving as Italian immigrants adapted traditional recipes with the ingredients available to them in the New World. It’s a dish that’s both humble and indulgent—a perfect balance of crispy texture and rich flavor.

The marriage of crispy chicken cutlets and tender pasta creates a satisfying one-two punch. Plus, the melted cheese and bright tomato sauce elevate the flavors in a way that is both nostalgic and timeless.

Ingredients: The Building Blocks of a Stellar Chicken Parmesan

Great results start with great ingredients. While there are many variations, here are the essentials and recommended components for a thoroughly classic preparation.

For the Chicken:

  • Chicken breasts: Boneless, skinless, ideally pounded thin to about ½ inch for even cooking.
  • All-purpose flour: For dredging.
  • Eggs: Beaten, as a binder for the breading.
  • Breadcrumbs: Italian-style or fresh homemade breadcrumbs for extra crunch.
  • Parmesan cheese: Finely grated, added to the breadcrumb mixture for a nutty flavor.
  • Seasonings: Salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and dried Italian herbs (such as basil and oregano).
  • Olive oil and butter: For frying—combining these gives excellent flavor and helps with browning.

For the Sauce:

  • San Marzano tomatoes (or good quality crushed tomatoes): The base.
  • Onion: Finely chopped.
  • Garlic: Minced.
  • Olive oil: For sautéing.
  • Fresh basil and oregano: For authentic Italian flavor.
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar: To balance acidity.
  • Crushed red pepper flakes (optional): For a subtle spicy kick.

For Assembly:

  • Fresh mozzarella cheese: Sliced.
  • Parmesan cheese: More for topping.
  • Fresh basil leaves: For garnish.
  • Spaghetti: Cooked al dente.

Preparing Chicken Parmesan: Step-by-Step

1. Preparing the Chicken Cutlets

Start by trimming any excess fat from the chicken breasts. To ensure even cooking, place each breast between two sheets of plastic wrap or inside a zip-top bag and pound gently with a meat mallet or rolling pin until the chicken is about ½ inch thick.

2. Setting Up the Breading Station

Use three shallow dishes arranged side by side:

  • The first contains all-purpose flour, seasoned lightly with salt and pepper.
  • The second holds the beaten eggs, whisked with a tablespoon of water or milk to thin slightly.
  • The third dish contains the breadcrumbs mixed with grated Parmesan cheese and the dried Italian herbs.

3. Bread the Chicken

  • Dredge each chicken cutlet first in the flour, coating evenly and shaking off excess.
  • Dip into the beaten eggs, ensuring the entire surface is covered.
  • Finally, press into the breadcrumb mixture, turning so both sides adhere well.

For a thicker, crunchier coating, some like to double dip by repeating the egg and breadcrumb steps.

4. Frying the Chicken

Use a generous amount of olive oil and a bit of butter in a large skillet over medium heat. When the oil is shimmering (but not smoking), add the breaded cutlets.

Fry each side for about 3-4 minutes until golden brown. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure even frying and to keep the coating crispy.

Once cooked, transfer the chicken to a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil.

Making the Marinara Sauce from Scratch

While jarred marinara works in a pinch, a homemade sauce can transform this dish completely.

1. Sauté Aromatics

Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and sauté gently until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant.

2. Add Tomatoes and Seasonings

Pour in crushed San Marzano tomatoes, stir in fresh basil leaves, oregano, salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar to cut acidity. If you like a little heat, sprinkle in some red pepper flakes.

3. Simmer

Bring sauce to a gentle simmer, cover partially, and cook for at least 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This allows flavors to meld and sauce to thicken.

Cooking the Spaghetti

While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente—still firm to the bite but fully cooked. Drain and toss lightly with a tablespoon of olive oil to prevent sticking.

Assembling the Classic Chicken Parmesan

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

Place the fried chicken cutlets on a baking sheet lined with parchment or in a shallow baking dish.

2. Layer the chicken with marinara sauce and cheese.

Spoon a generous amount of marinara over each cutlet. Top with slices of fresh mozzarella and sprinkle some shredded Parmesan on top.

3. Bake

Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and slightly golden.

Plating Like a Pro

  • Twirl a generous serving of spaghetti onto each plate.
  • Place a chicken cutlet on top.
  • Spoon extra marinara around the pasta for a beautiful presentation.
  • Garnish with torn fresh basil leaves and a light drizzle of good quality olive oil.
  • Add extra parmesan on the side, in case someone wants to add a little more cheesy goodness.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Parmesan Every Time

  • Even thickness matters: Pounding the chicken ensures it cooks evenly and remains juicy.
  • Breadcrumb texture: Use Italian-style breadcrumbs or make your own by toasting day-old bread and pulsing in a food processor.
  • Oil temperature: Medium heat is key. Too hot, and the breadcrumbs burn before chicken cooks through; too low, and you get soggy breading.
  • Use fresh cheese: Fresh mozzarella melts beautifully and adds creaminess.
  • Sauce variations: Feel free to add sautéed mushrooms or bell peppers to your sauce for extra flavor.
  • Make it ahead: The sauce can be prepared a day early and refrigerated, allowing flavors to deepen.

Variations to Explore

  • Eggplant Parmesan: Substitute chicken for thin slices of breaded and fried eggplant for a vegetarian twist.
  • Gluten-free version: Use gluten-free breadcrumbs and pasta.
  • Spicy Chicken Parmesan: Add some cayenne or chili flakes to the breadcrumb mix or sauce for a spicy kick.
  • Different pasta: While spaghetti is traditional, penne or linguine also complement the dish well.

Why This Recipe Works

The success of Chicken Parmesan lies in combining textures and flavors that balance each other. The crispy, seasoned breading contrasts with soft, melted cheese. The tangy sweetness of the marinara cuts through richness, while pasta serves as a neutral backdrop that brings the whole dish together. When each element is thoughtfully prepared, the final result is both comforting and impressive.

Final Thoughts

Classic Chicken Parmesan with spaghetti is more than just a meal; it’s a culinary experience that showcases the beautiful simplicity of Italian-American cooking. By taking time to make each component carefully—from the breaded chicken to the homemade sauce—you can create a dish that’s bursting with flavor and guaranteed to please any crowd.

So roll up your sleeves, get cooking, and enjoy one of the most beloved dishes to grace any dinner table!

Printable Recipe Summary

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (pounded thin)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 ½ cups Italian breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese (plus extra for topping)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp dried Italian herbs
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil and butter for frying
  • 4 cups marinara sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions

  1. Pound chicken breasts until ½ inch thick.
  2. Season flour with salt and pepper; dredge chicken first in flour, then eggs, then breadcrumb/Parmesan mixture.
  3. Fry chicken in olive oil and butter until golden, about 3-4 minutes each side. Drain.
  4. Simmer marinara sauce.
  5. Cook spaghetti until al dente.
  6. Place fried chicken on baking dish; top with marinara, mozzarella, and Parmesan.
  7. Bake at 400°F for 10-15 minutes until cheese melts.
  8. Serve chicken over spaghetti; garnish with basil.

Enjoy your meal!

Now, imagine the aroma filling your kitchen as that bubbling cheese melds with savory sauce and crispy chicken—a dish this good deserves to be made again and again!
