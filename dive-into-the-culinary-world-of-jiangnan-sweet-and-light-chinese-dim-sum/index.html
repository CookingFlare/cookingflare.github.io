Dive into the Culinary World of Jiangnan: Sweet and Light Chinese Dim Sum


Nestled in the watery wonderland of Southern China lies the alluring region of Jiangnan. Renowned for its delicate flavors and rich history, the cuisine of Jiangnan has become synonymous with exquisite Chinese culinary experiences. This land, abundant with rivers and lakes, has contributed greatly to the development of China’s dim sum culture, the delightful art of sharing small plate meals. Join us as we dive into the sweet and light world of Jiangnan and unveil the secrets behind its popular dim sum selections. Here’s a collection of recipes, stories, and traditions that make up this unique regional cuisine.

1. Refreshing Taro Puffs

No Jiangnan dim sum feast is complete without a basket of freshly made taro puffs. This savory pastry will intrigue your taste buds with a filling of succulent shrimp, wrapped in a flaky, golden crust. Take a bite and get a mouthful of tender taro mashed together with the delightful, briny shrimp. The flavors will transport you to the water towns of Jiangnan, where you can almost hear the bubbling of the canals beneath the soft morning light.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb taro, peeled and cut into 1-inch chunks
  • 1 cup Chinese dried shrimp, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger, finely grated
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and add taro chunks. Cook for approximately 20 minutes or until taro is soft and fully cooked.
  2. Drain taro and mash until smooth. Allow it to cool down before assembling the puffs.
  3. Combine taro mash, shrimp, green onions, and ginger in a large bowl. Mix well and season with salt to taste.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix the flour and water to create a smooth dough. Roll the dough out on a floured surface to approximately 1/8-inch thickness.
  5. Using round cookie cutters or lids, cut the dough into circular shapes. Place a spoonful of the taro and shrimp filling onto the center of each circular piece of dough.
  6. Fold each dough circle in half and pinch the edges to seal the filling inside. Make sure to press the edges well to avoid any leakage.
  7. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or deep fryer until it reaches approximately 325째F (165째C). Deep fry the taro puffs in batches for 3-4 minutes or until they have a lovely golden color.
  8. Serve warm, and enjoy the explosion of flavors with every bite!

2. Sweet Rice Dumplings with Purple Sweet Potato

These dainty dumplings showcase the delightful union of sweet and savory flavors inspired by the lavish ingredients of Jiangnan. The vibrant purple hue from the sweet potato filling will leave you longing for more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup purple sweet potatoes, cooked and mashed
  • 1/2 cup red bean paste
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Combine rice flour and water in a large bowl, whisking well to remove lumps. Heat the mixture in a wok or pot over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens into a smooth dough. The dough should be slightly tacky and pliable.
  2. Allow the dough to cool before kneading in the mashed sweet potatoes, red bean paste, and sugar. Mix well and continue to knead until the dough becomes soft and smooth, incorporating the vegetable oil.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Roll the dough into a long log, then divide the log into smaller portions. Shape each portion into a flat disc, and wrap a small piece of red bean paste in the center. Seal the edges, making sure the filling is well covered.
  5. Bring a medium pot of water to a boil and add the dumplings. Simmer for approximately 10 minutes or until they become soft and cooked through.
  6. Serve the sweet rice dumplings warm, garnished with some tapioca pearls or a sprinkle of sesame seeds for extra texture and flavor.

3. Heavenly Osmanthus Seed Flan

To end the journey through the cuisine of Jiangnan, treat yourself to a sweet and floral haven with this divine osmanthus seed flan. Luxuriate in the soothing aroma and the tender texture of this classic dessert. Osmanthus plants are abundant throughout the region and often found in traditional culinary delights due to their naturally sweet and intoxicating fragrance.

Ingredients:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 2 tsp osmanthus seeds, steeped in boiling water for 15 minutes
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch, dissolved in 1/4 cup cold water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350째F (175째C) and prepare a water bath (also known as a bain-marie) by placing a roasting pan on the lower rack of the oven.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine sugar and 3/4 cup of water. Bring to a boil and let it cook without stirring until it reaches an amber color.reduce the heat to low and carefully add the osmanthus seed-infused water. Be cautious as the mixture will bubble and steam. Stir well.
  3. In a separate large bowl, whisk together egg yolks, sweetened condensed milk, and dissolved cornstarch.
  4. Gradually add the osmanthus caramel sauce to the egg mixture, whisking constantly to avoid scrambling the eggs.
  5. In another saucepan, scald the heavy cream by heating it until small bubbles appear at the surface.
  6. Pour the hot cream over the egg mixture in slow, steady streams while whisking constantly. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into ramekins or a flan mold.
  7. Place the ramekins or mold into the prepared water bath in the oven, ensuring that the water reaches halfway up their sides. Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the custard is set but still trembling slightly in the center.
  8. Carefully remove the ramekins or mold from the water bath and let them cool. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or, preferably, overnight.
  9. To serve, gently run a knife around the edge of each ramekin to loosen the custard. Invert the flan onto a plate, allowing the osmanthus caramel sauce to drip down the sides. Enjoy this elegant and fragrant dessert, complete with a taste of Jiangnan’s predestined harmony of flavors.

The culinary world of Jiangnan is a treasure trove of sweet and light flavors, influenced by its geography and local culture. By mastering these dim sum recipes, you’ll immerse yourself in the tantalizing realm of traditional Jiangnan cuisine and its delectable creations. From taro puffs to osmanthus seed flan, each dish brings a small taste of the region’s rich, textured history and works of excellence to your table.
