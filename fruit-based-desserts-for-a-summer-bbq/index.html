Fruit-Based Desserts for a Summer BBQ


Summer BBQs are the perfect occasion to enjoy delicious food, lively conversations, and the warmth of the season. While grilled meats and savory dishes often take center stage, the dessert table is just as important in creating a memorable dining experience. For a BBQ, there’s no better way to wrap up a meal than with light, refreshing, and naturally sweet fruit-based desserts.

Whether you’re hosting a BBQ in your backyard or attending one at a friend’s place, offering fruit-inspired desserts adds a burst of color and flavor that complements the smoky, savory dishes. From fruity sorbets to crisp tarts and fresh fruit salads, these desserts are not only satisfying but also easy to prepare.

In this post, weâ€™ll explore several fruit-based desserts that are perfect for any summer BBQ. These treats highlight the natural sweetness and freshness of seasonal fruits while offering a delightful way to cool down on a hot day.

1. Mixed Berry Parfait with Whipped Cream

One of the easiest and most visually appealing fruit-based desserts to make for a BBQ is a mixed berry parfait. This layered dessert is versatile, quick to prepare, and can be customized to suit your preferences. You can use a variety of berries such as strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries, which are in season during the summer months.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 pint blueberries
  • 1 pint raspberries
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup granola (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Whipped Cream: In a chilled mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  2. Assemble the Parfait: In serving glasses, alternate layers of whipped cream, fresh berries, and granola (if using) for added crunch.
  3. Chill and Serve: Let the parfaits chill in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes before serving. Garnish with extra berries or a sprig of mint if desired.

This dessert is not only refreshing but also a great way to showcase the beauty of fresh, in-season berries. The sweet whipped cream complements the tartness of the berries, while the granola adds texture and depth.

2. Grilled Peaches with Honey and Cinnamon

Grilling fruit brings out its natural sweetness, and peaches are one of the best fruits to grill. The caramelization that happens when peaches are grilled creates a delightful smoky-sweet flavor that pairs beautifully with honey and a dash of cinnamon.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Prepare the Peaches: Brush the cut sides of the peaches with honey, then sprinkle with cinnamon and a pinch of sea salt.
  3. Grill the Peaches: Place the peaches on the grill cut side down. Grill for about 3-4 minutes until the peaches have grill marks and are slightly softened.
  4. Serve: Remove from the grill and drizzle with more honey. Garnish with fresh mint leaves for added freshness.

Grilled peaches are a simple yet elegant fruit-based dessert that will impress your guests. The natural sweetness of the peaches is enhanced by the grill, and the honey and cinnamon add a perfect finishing touch.

3. Watermelon Sorbet

Watermelon is a quintessential summer fruit, and turning it into a sorbet makes for an incredibly refreshing dessert. Watermelon sorbet is not only hydrating but also light and bursting with flavor. Plus, it’s naturally vegan and gluten-free!

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cubed watermelon
  • 1/4 cup fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 cup honey or agave syrup (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest

Instructions:

  1. Puree the Watermelon: Place the cubed watermelon in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth.
  2. Add Lime and Sweetener: Add the fresh lime juice, honey or agave syrup (if using), and lemon zest to the watermelon puree. Blend again until combined.
  3. Freeze the Sorbet: Pour the mixture into a shallow dish or a popsicle mold and freeze for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  4. Serve: Once frozen, scrape the sorbet with a fork to create a fluffy texture. Serve in bowls or as popsicles.

Watermelon sorbet is a great way to keep your guests cool during a hot summer BBQ. Its vibrant color and refreshing taste make it an instant crowd-pleaser. Plus, it can be prepared in advance, making it a convenient dessert option for busy BBQ hosts.

4. Tropical Fruit Salad with Coconut Lime Dressing

A tropical fruit salad is a fun and colorful way to incorporate a variety of fruits into your BBQ dessert spread. The combination of sweet pineapple, juicy mango, and tangy citrus is the perfect balance of flavors. Top it off with a coconut lime dressing, and you have a dessert that’s as refreshing as it is flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, peeled and cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 kiwis, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup coconut yogurt or regular yogurt (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/4 cup shredded coconut (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Fruits: In a large mixing bowl, combine the pineapple, mango, and kiwi.
  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut yogurt (if using), lime juice, and honey.
  3. Toss the Salad: Pour the dressing over the fruit and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve: Garnish with shredded coconut for a tropical touch. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.

This tropical fruit salad is a deliciously vibrant way to end your BBQ meal. The tangy lime dressing and creamy coconut yogurt complement the sweet fruits, making each bite a refreshing burst of flavor.

5. Strawberry Shortcake Skewers

Strawberry shortcake is a classic summer dessert, but on a BBQ, itâ€™s even more fun when served on skewers. These bite-sized treats are easy to assemble and perfect for guests to grab and go.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint strawberries, hulled
  • 1 pound sponge cake or pound cake, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup whipped cream (or vanilla yogurt)
  • Fresh mint for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Assemble the Skewers: Alternate threading the cake cubes and strawberries onto wooden skewers.
  2. Top with Cream: Serve the skewers with whipped cream or vanilla yogurt on the side for dipping.
  3. Garnish: Garnish with fresh mint leaves for a touch of green and extra flavor.

These strawberry shortcake skewers are a playful twist on a traditional dessert. Theyâ€™re perfect for a casual summer BBQ where guests can enjoy a sweet treat without needing utensils.

6. Mango Coconut Chia Pudding

Chia pudding is a healthy and indulgent dessert that is both filling and refreshing. By adding mango and coconut, you can create a tropical-inspired chia pudding thatâ€™s perfect for a summer BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup chia seeds
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced
  • Shredded coconut for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chia Pudding: In a bowl, mix the chia seeds, coconut milk, and honey or maple syrup. Stir well to combine.
  2. Refrigerate: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to allow the chia seeds to absorb the liquid and form a pudding-like texture.
  3. Serve: Spoon the pudding into serving glasses, then top with diced mango and a sprinkle of shredded coconut.

Mango coconut chia pudding is a healthy yet indulgent dessert option thatâ€™s both satisfying and nutritious. Itâ€™s naturally dairy-free, vegan, and can be made ahead of time, making it a great option for BBQ hosts.
