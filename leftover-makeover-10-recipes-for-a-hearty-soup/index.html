Leftover Makeover: 10 Recipes for a Hearty Soup


Leftover food often finds its way to the back of the fridge, forgotten and sometimes, left to go to waste. However, with a little creativity, those leftovers can be transformed into hearty and flavorful soups that will make your meals exciting and delicious once again. Whether it’s a bit of roasted chicken from last night’s dinner, some vegetables that are starting to lose their freshness, or that half-cup of rice you have sitting around, all of these can play a key role in creating a comforting, filling soup.

In this post, we’re sharing 10 different ways to take your leftovers and turn them into rich, satisfying soups that can easily become your new go-to meals. Perfect for cozy nights, meal prep, or simply reducing food waste, these leftover soup ideas will inspire you to rethink how you use what you already have in your kitchen.

1. Chicken and Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover roasted chicken, shredded
  • 2 cups of leftover roasted or steamed vegetables (carrots, potatoes, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup of water (if needed)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)

Instructions:

  1. Start by sautéing the diced onion and minced garlic in a large pot over medium heat until fragrant and softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the leftover vegetables to the pot and cook for an additional 2 minutes to warm them up.
  3. Pour in the chicken broth and water, bringing everything to a gentle simmer.
  4. Stir in the shredded roasted chicken and fresh herbs. Let the soup simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If the soup seems too thick, add more water or broth until you reach the desired consistency.

This chicken and vegetable soup is a fantastic way to use up leftover chicken and vegetables. The broth is full of savory flavors, and the vegetables add texture and heartiness.

2. Tomato and Rice Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of leftover cooked rice
  • 2 cups of canned tomatoes (or leftover roasted tomatoes)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and basil to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, diced carrot, and garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in the canned or leftover tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Add the leftover cooked rice and let the soup simmer for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to combine and the rice to absorb some of the broth.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and fresh basil to taste.

This soup is perfect for using up leftover rice and tomatoes. It’s a comforting, rich, and slightly tangy soup that’s sure to please anyone looking for a hearty, low-effort meal.

3. Beef and Potato Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover beef (steak, roast, or stew)
  • 2 cups of leftover mashed potatoes
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup of frozen peas (or any leftover veggies you have)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic in a little olive oil until fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the beef broth and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the leftover mashed potatoes and beef, letting the potatoes break down and thicken the soup. If the soup is too thick, you can add a bit more broth or water.
  4. Add in the frozen peas (or any leftover vegetables you have) and simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with fresh parsley.

This beef and potato soup is rich, creamy, and incredibly filling. The mashed potatoes act as a thickener, making this soup especially cozy and satisfying.

4. Lentil and Ham Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of leftover cooked ham, diced
  • 1 cup of leftover cooked lentils
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the broth, bay leaf, and leftover lentils. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the diced ham and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This hearty lentil and ham soup is full of flavor, and the ham provides a savory touch that complements the earthy lentils perfectly. It’s a great way to transform those leftover ham scraps into a comforting meal.

5. Vegetable and Quinoa Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover cooked quinoa
  • 2 cups of leftover roasted vegetables (zucchini, bell peppers, squash, etc.)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat a little olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the vegetable broth, leftover quinoa, roasted vegetables, cumin, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a simmer.
  3. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the quinoa to absorb some of the broth and the vegetables to soften further.
  4. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Packed with protein and fiber from the quinoa, this soup is a fantastic way to use up leftover grains and vegetables. It’s healthy, hearty, and perfect for meal prep.

6. Chicken and Corn Chowder

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup of leftover cooked corn (or canned corn)
  • 2 cups of leftover mashed potatoes
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic in some olive oil until softened.
  2. Add the chicken broth, leftover mashed potatoes, chicken, corn, and thyme. Bring everything to a simmer.
  3. Let the soup cook for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This creamy, comforting chowder is perfect for using up both leftover chicken and mashed potatoes. The corn adds a sweetness that balances out the savory flavors of the chicken and potatoes.

7. Butternut Squash and Carrot Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover roasted butternut squash
  • 1 cup of leftover roasted carrots
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A dash of cream or coconut milk (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion in olive oil until softened.
  2. Add the roasted butternut squash, carrots, and vegetable broth. Bring everything to a simmer.
  3. Let it simmer for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you prefer a creamy texture, add a splash of cream or coconut milk.
  5. Season with cinnamon, salt, and pepper to taste.

This butternut squash and carrot soup is a sweet, savory, and comforting choice for using up roasted vegetables. The hint of cinnamon adds warmth and depth to the flavor.

8. Minestrone Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of leftover pasta
  • 2 cups of leftover vegetables (beans, zucchini, spinach, etc.)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until fragrant.
  2. Add the diced tomatoes, leftover vegetables, and vegetable broth. Bring the soup to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the leftover pasta and let the soup simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing everything to heat through and absorb the flavors.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning.

Minestrone soup is a great way to use up any leftover vegetables and pasta you may have. It’s a colorful, nutrient-packed soup that’s easy to make and full of flavor.

9. Chicken Tortilla Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 cup of crushed tortilla chips
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onion in a little olive oil until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken broth, diced tomatoes, black beans, chili powder, and cumin. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Stir in the shredded chicken and crushed tortilla chips, allowing the chips to thicken the soup slightly.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and garnish with fresh cilantro.

This chicken tortilla soup is a flavorful and hearty option that transforms your leftover chicken into a zesty, satisfying meal.

10. Potato Leek Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of leftover mashed potatoes
  • 2 leeks, cleaned and sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the leeks, onion, and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the vegetable broth, mashed potatoes, and thyme. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Let the soup cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the mashed potatoes to incorporate into the broth and create a creamy texture.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This potato leek soup is a simple, creamy dish that uses up leftover mashed potatoes and transforms them into a silky, comforting soup.

Conclusion

Transforming leftovers into hearty soups is an easy way to reduce waste while enjoying a delicious meal. Whether you have leftover meats, grains, vegetables, or starches, there are countless ways to create satisfying, flavorful soups from what you already have in your fridge. These 10 recipes offer a great variety of flavors and ingredients that can be adjusted based on what you have available. So, the next time you’re wondering what to do with your leftovers, consider turning them into one of these comforting, nourishing soups!
