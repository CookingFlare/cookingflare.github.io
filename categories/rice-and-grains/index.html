Today, we’re diving into a delicious and nutritious recipe that brings together the wholesome goodness of quinoa with a medley of roasted vegetables. This Mediterranean-inspired quinoa salad is perfect for a healthy lunch, a side dish, or even a light dinner. Let’s get started! Ingredients For the Salad: 1 cup of quinoa 2 cups of water or vegetable broth 1 red bell pepper, diced 1 yellow bell pepper, diced 1 zucchini, chopped 1 eggplant, chopped 1 red onion, sliced 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped For the Dressing: 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar 1 tablespoon lemon juice 1 clove garlic, minced 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Step 1: Preparing the Quinoa Rinse the quinoa under cold water using a fine-mesh sieve. This helps to remove the natural coating called saponin, which can make the quinoa taste bitter. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed quinoa and water (or vegetable broth for added flavor). Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until all the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is tender. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for an additional 5 minutes. Fluff the cooked quinoa with a fork and set aside to cool. Step 2: Roasting the Vegetables Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). On a large baking sheet, spread out the diced red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, chopped zucchini, chopped eggplant, and sliced red onion. Drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Toss the vegetables to ensure they are evenly coated with oil and seasoning. Roast in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized, stirring halfway through the cooking time. Remove from the oven and allow the roasted vegetables to cool slightly. Step 3: Assembling the Salad In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, cherry tomatoes, Kalamata olives, crumbled feta cheese, chopped parsley, and chopped basil. In a small bowl, whisk together the extra virgin olive oil, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper to create the dressing. Pour the dressing over the quinoa and vegetable mixture, and gently toss to combine all the ingredients thoroughly. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary, adding more salt, pepper, or lemon juice as needed. Fresh and Light Quinoa and Tomato Salad

Quinoa is renowned for its nutritional benefits and versatility in the kitchen. When paired with vibrant, juicy tomatoes and a medley of fresh herbs, it transforms into a delightful salad that’s perfect for summer or anytime you crave a light and nourishing meal. Ingredients 1 cup quinoa, rinsed thoroughly 2 cups water or vegetable broth 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved 1 cucumber, diced 1/2 red onion, finely chopped 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar or lemon juice Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions Cooking Quinoa In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed quinoa and water or vegetable broth. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is tender and has absorbed all the liquid. Remove from heat and let it sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and transfer to a large mixing bowl to cool. Preparing the Salad While the quinoa is cooking, prepare the vegetables. Halve the cherry tomatoes, dice the cucumber, and finely chop the red onion. In the same mixing bowl with the cooled quinoa, add the cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, chopped parsley, basil, and mint. Drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil and red wine vinegar or lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mixing and Serving Gently toss all the ingredients together until well combined and evenly coated with the dressing. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Serve immediately, or refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together before serving. Tips for the Perfect Quinoa Salad Rinsing Quinoa: Always rinse quinoa thoroughly before cooking to remove its natural coating, which can make it taste bitter. Vegetable Broth: Using vegetable broth instead of water adds extra flavor to the quinoa. Fresh Herbs: Fresh herbs like parsley, basil, and mint bring a burst of freshness and aroma to the salad. Make-Ahead: This salad can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Add fresh herbs just before serving to maintain their flavor. Variations Add Protein: Mix in grilled chicken, shrimp, or tofu for added protein. Cheese: Sprinkle feta or goat cheese over the salad for a creamy tangy touch. Nuts and Seeds: Toasted pine nuts, almonds, or pumpkin seeds add crunch and extra nutrients. Enjoy Your Quinoa and Tomato Salad! Spicy and Tangy Mexican Rice with Pork

Mexican cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors and vibrant spices that tantalize the taste buds. One dish that embodies this culinary tradition perfectly is Spicy and Tangy Mexican Rice with Pork. This hearty and satisfying meal combines tender pieces of pork with fragrant rice, tomatoes, and a medley of spices that create a symphony of flavors in every bite. Ingredients 1 lb pork shoulder, diced 2 cups long-grain rice 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes 1 onion, finely chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 bell pepper, diced (any color) 2 jalapeño peppers, seeded and minced 2 cups chicken broth 1/4 cup tomato paste 1 tablespoon chili powder 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon paprika Salt and pepper, to taste Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish) Lime wedges (for serving) Instructions 1. Prepare the Pork Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of oil. Season the diced pork shoulder with salt and pepper. Add the pork to the skillet and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the pork from the skillet and set aside. 2. Sauté Aromatics In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of oil if needed. Sauté the chopped onion, garlic, bell pepper, and jalapeños until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the diced tomatoes with their juices and tomato paste. Stir to combine. 3. Cook the Rice Stir in the long-grain rice, chili powder, cumin powder, and paprika. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the rice is lightly toasted. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. 4. Combine and Simmer Return the browned pork shoulder to the skillet. Stir everything together well. Reduce the heat to low, cover with a lid, and simmer gently for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. 5. Garnish and Serve Once the rice is cooked through, taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with freshly chopped cilantro. Serve hot with lime wedges on the side for an extra burst of citrusy flavor. Tips for Success Choosing Pork: Pork shoulder is ideal for this dish as it becomes tender and flavorful when simmered with the rice. Spice Level: Adjust the amount of jalapeños and chili powder to suit your spice preference. Tomato Paste: Tomato paste adds richness and depth to the dish, enhancing the overall flavor profile. Resting Time: Allowing the rice to rest after cooking ensures a fluffy texture and allows the flavors to develop fully. Variations Vegetarian Option: Replace the pork with black beans or tofu for a meatless version. Additions: Customize with corn kernels, diced carrots, or black olives for additional texture and flavor. Toppings: Serve with avocado slices, sour cream, or shredded cheese for extra indulgence. Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto: A Fall Favorite Comfort Food

Risotto lovers rejoice! This Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto recipe is the perfect comfort food to warm your soul on chilly fall evenings. The creamy texture of the risotto pairs perfectly with the sweet and nutty flavor of butternut squash, while the earthy aroma of sage adds a touch of elegance to this hearty dish. Prepare to fall in love with the ultimate fall comfort food. Ingredients 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 small onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice 1/2 cup white wine (optional) 1 tablespoon fresh sage leaves, finely chopped 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese Salt and pepper to taste Instructions In a large pot, bring the vegetable or chicken broth to a simmer. Keep it warm on low heat. Heat the olive oil in a separate large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they become translucent and fragrant. Stir in the Arborio rice, coating each grain with the oil, onion, and garlic mixture. Cook for about 2 minutes until the rice starts to toast slightly. If using white wine, pour it into the saucepan and stir continuously until the liquid has been absorbed by the rice. Begin adding the warm broth to the rice, one ladleful at a time, stirring continuously. Allow each ladleful to be absorbed by the rice before adding the next. After about 10 minutes, add the cubed butternut squash to the risotto. Continue adding the broth and stirring until the rice and squash are cooked, and the risotto has reached a creamy consistency. This should take about 20-25 minutes in total. Stir in the freshly chopped sage leaves and grated Parmesan cheese, ensuring they are well incorporated into the risotto. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and give the risotto a final stir. Remove the risotto from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. Serve the Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto hot, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh sage leaves and an extra sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top. Tips and Variations For added richness, you can stir in a tablespoon of butter just before serving. If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can use an immersion blender to partially puree the cooked butternut squash before adding it to the risotto. To make this dish vegan-friendly, simply substitute the Parmesan cheese with nutritional yeast or a vegan cheese alternative. For an extra burst of flavor, you can roast the cubed butternut squash in the oven before adding it to the risotto. Simply toss the squash in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast at 400°F (200°C) for about 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized. Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile of your risotto. Thyme, rosemary, or even a pinch of nutmeg can all be delightful additions. In Conclusion As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, indulge in the comforting flavors of fall with this Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto. This dish is a celebration of seasonal produce and traditional Italian cooking techniques. The creamy texture, nutty sweetness, and aromatic sage combine to create a symphony of flavors that will warm both your belly and your heart.
