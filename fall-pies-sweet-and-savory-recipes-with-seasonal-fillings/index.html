Fall Pies: Sweet and Savory Recipes with Seasonal Fillings


Fall is the time of year when the air turns crisp, the leaves change colors, and the aroma of freshly baked pies fills the kitchen. There’s something magical about the combination of warm spices, seasonal fruits, and buttery crusts that makes fall pies irresistible. Whether you prefer sweet or savory flavors, we’ve got you covered with these delicious fall pie recipes. So grab your apron and let’s get baking!

Apple Cinnamon Pie

Nothing says fall quite like the classic combination of apples and cinnamon. This apple cinnamon pie is a true crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of thinly sliced apples
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 package of ready-made pie crusts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice. Mix well.
  3. Roll out one pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess dough.
  4. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust.
  5. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  6. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  8. Allow the pie to cool before serving.

Butternut Squash and Sage Pie

For a savory option, try this butternut squash and sage pie. It’s the perfect blend of earthy flavors and creamy goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package of ready-made pie crusts

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the butternut squash cubes on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add the roasted butternut squash cubes to the skillet. Mash them slightly with a fork or potato masher.
  5. Stir in the vegetable broth, heavy cream, chopped sage, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until heated through.
  6. Roll out one pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess dough.
  7. Pour the butternut squash mixture into the pie crust.
  8. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  9. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  10. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.
  11. Allow the pie to cool before serving.

Conclusion

Fall pies are the perfect way to celebrate the flavors of the season. Whether you prefer the comforting sweetness of apple cinnamon or the savory goodness of butternut squash and sage, these recipes are sure to impress your family and friends. So embrace the fall spirit, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in the warmth and deliciousness of fall pies. Happy baking!
