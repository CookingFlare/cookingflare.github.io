Best Wines to Pair with Savory Pies and Tarts


When it comes to culinary delights, savory pies and tarts occupy a special place in the hearts and palates of food lovers. From the flaky crust to the rich fillings, these dishes evoke comfort and indulgence. However, the experience of enjoying a savory pie or tart can be elevated to new heights with the right wine pairing. In this guide, we’ll explore the best wines to complement various types of savory pies and tarts, making your dining experience not only delicious but also memorable.

Understanding the Basics of Wine Pairing

Wine pairing is an art that balances flavors, textures, and aromas. The goal is to enhance both the food and the wine, creating a harmonious dining experience. Here are some fundamental principles to keep in mind:

  • Complementary Flavors: Choose wines that echo the flavors of the dish. For instance, a rich, meaty pie may pair well with a bold red wine that has similar flavor notes.

  • Contrast: Sometimes, contrasting flavors can create a delightful balance. A zesty white wine can cut through the richness of a creamy tart, providing a refreshing contrast.

  • Weight and Texture: The body of the wine should match the weight of the dish. Heavier dishes call for fuller-bodied wines, while lighter dishes are best enjoyed with lighter wines.

  • Acidity: High-acid wines can brighten up rich, savory dishes, while low-acid wines might work better with milder flavors.

Now, let’s delve into specific types of savory pies and tarts and discover which wines pair beautifully with them.

Classic Meat Pies

Shepherd’s Pie

A comforting classic, Shepherd’s Pie consists of minced lamb and vegetables topped with creamy mashed potatoes. The robust flavors of the lamb demand a wine that can stand up to them.

Wine Pairing: A full-bodied red wine, such as a Shiraz or Malbec, complements the savory, meaty flavors of the Shepherd’s Pie. The fruity undertones and peppery notes of these wines enhance the dish’s richness, making each bite even more enjoyable.

Chicken Pot Pie

Chicken Pot Pie is a hearty dish filled with tender chicken and vegetables in a creamy sauce, all encased in a flaky crust.

Wine Pairing: A Chardonnay, especially one that has undergone malolactic fermentation, works wonders with Chicken Pot Pie. Its buttery texture and notes of apple and pear complement the creaminess of the filling, creating a delightful pairing.

Savory Tarts

Quiche Lorraine

This French classic features a buttery crust filled with eggs, cream, cheese, and crispy bacon. Its rich flavor profile requires a wine that can enhance the dish without overpowering it.

Wine Pairing: An Alsace Pinot Gris is a fantastic match for Quiche Lorraine. Its rich, aromatic profile and hints of spice beautifully complement the savory notes of the bacon and cheese.

Spinach and Feta Tart

A vibrant tart filled with fresh spinach and tangy feta cheese offers a delightful mix of flavors.

Wine Pairing: A Sauvignon Blanc is an excellent choice here. Its bright acidity and herbal notes balance the richness of the feta, while the wine’s citrus undertones highlight the freshness of the spinach.

Seafood Pies and Tarts

Salmon and Leek Pie

This delectable dish features flaky salmon and tender leeks, all enveloped in a creamy sauce.

Wine Pairing: A Pinot Noir pairs beautifully with Salmon and Leek Pie. The wine’s light body and red fruit flavors harmonize with the richness of the salmon, while its subtle earthiness complements the leeks.

Seafood Tart

A mix of shellfish and fish encased in a delicate pastry crust creates a luxurious seafood tart.

Wine Pairing: A crisp Champagne or Sparkling Wine is a fantastic match for seafood tarts. The bubbles cleanse the palate, while the wine’s acidity enhances the flavors of the seafood.

Vegetarian Pies and Tarts

Mushroom and Goat Cheese Tart

This tart features earthy mushrooms and tangy goat cheese, creating a rich and satisfying flavor profile.

Wine Pairing: An Italian Barbera complements the earthy flavors of the mushrooms while balancing the tartness of the goat cheese. Its moderate acidity and fruity notes enhance the dish without overwhelming it.

Ratatouille Tart

A colorful blend of vegetables like eggplant, zucchini, and bell peppers makes Ratatouille Tart a vibrant choice for a vegetarian dish.

Wine Pairing: A Rosé is a delightful pairing for Ratatouille Tart. Its fresh acidity and berry flavors marry well with the medley of vegetables, providing a refreshing contrast to the dish’s savory elements.

Personal Favorites and Unique Pairings

Bacon and Cheese Pie

This indulgent pie is packed with smoky bacon and rich cheese, making it a guilty pleasure for many.

Wine Pairing: A Zinfandel is an unexpected but excellent choice. Its bold fruit flavors and hints of spice complement the salty, savory notes of the bacon and cheese.

Curried Vegetable Pie

A pie filled with spiced vegetables offers a unique flavor profile that can be challenging to pair.

Wine Pairing: An off-dry Riesling works remarkably well with Curried Vegetable Pie. The wine’s sweetness balances the heat of the curry, while its acidity refreshes the palate.

Conclusion

Pairing wine with savory pies and tarts can elevate your dining experience to new heights. By understanding the flavors, textures, and characteristics of both the dishes and wines, you can create harmonious combinations that enhance every meal. Whether you’re enjoying a comforting Shepherd’s Pie or a refined Quiche Lorraine, the right wine will not only complement the dish but also enrich your overall culinary adventure. Explore these pairings, experiment with different wines, and most importantly, enjoy the journey of discovering the perfect match for your savory pies and tarts. Cheers to delicious dining!
