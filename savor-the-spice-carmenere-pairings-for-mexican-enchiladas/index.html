Savor the Spice: Carmenere Pairings for Mexican Enchiladas


Mexican cuisine is a symphony of flavors, with each dish a harmonious blend of spices, herbs, and chilies. Enchiladas, in particular, are a staple of Mexican cooking, with their tender tortillas wrapped around savory fillings and smothered in a rich, spicy sauce. But what makes enchiladas truly exceptional is the perfect pairing of wine. In this article, we’ll explore the art of matching Carmenere, a Chilean varietal, with the bold flavors of Mexican enchiladas.

The Perfect Pairing: Carmenere and Enchiladas

Carmenere is a wine that defies convention. Native to Bordeaux, it was once believed to be Merlot, but its unique flavor profile soon revealed its true identity. With notes of dark fruit, spice, and a hint of smokiness, Carmenere is the perfect companion to the bold, spicy flavors of Mexican enchiladas.

The key to pairing Carmenere with enchiladas lies in its balance of acidity and tannins. The acidity cuts through the richness of the cheese and sauce, while the tannins provide a subtle bitterness to balance the heat of the chilies. The dark fruit flavors of the wine also complement the bold, savory flavors of the fillings, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.

Enchiladas Rojas: A Classic Carmenere Pairing

Enchiladas Rojas, or red enchiladas, are a staple of Mexican cuisine. Tender tortillas are wrapped around shredded chicken or beef, then smothered in a rich, spicy tomato sauce and melted cheese. The bold flavors of this dish are perfectly complemented by Carmenere’s dark fruit and spice notes.

To pair Carmenere with Enchiladas Rojas, look for a wine with a moderate level of acidity and tannins. A wine with a slight smokiness or oakiness will also complement the bold flavors of the dish. Some recommended Carmenere wines for Enchiladas Rojas include:

  • Concha y Toro Carmenere
  • Montes Carmenere
  • Santa Rita Carmenere

Enchiladas Verdes: A Refreshing Carmenere Pairing

Enchiladas Verdes, or green enchiladas, are a refreshing twist on the classic dish. Tortillas are wrapped around tender chicken or beef, then smothered in a tangy, herby green sauce and topped with sour cream and avocado. The bright, citrusy flavors of this dish are perfectly complemented by Carmenere’s crisp acidity.

To pair Carmenere with Enchiladas Verdes, look for a wine with a high level of acidity and a crisp, refreshing flavor profile. A wine with a hint of green pepper or herb notes will also complement the bold flavors of the dish. Some recommended Carmenere wines for Enchiladas Verdes include:

  • Casillero del Diablo Carmenere
  • Undurraga Carmenere
  • Viña Maipo Carmenere

Conclusion

Carmenere is a wine that is often overlooked, but its unique flavor profile makes it the perfect pairing for Mexican enchiladas. Whether you prefer the bold, spicy flavors of Enchiladas Rojas or the bright, citrusy flavors of Enchiladas Verdes, Carmenere is a wine that will complement and enhance the flavors of these iconic dishes. So the next time you’re cooking up a batch of enchiladas, don’t forget to reach for a glass of Carmenere – your taste buds will thank you.
