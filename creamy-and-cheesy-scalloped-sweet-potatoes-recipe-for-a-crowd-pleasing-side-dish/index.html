Creamy and Cheesy Scalloped Sweet Potatoes Recipe for a Crowd-Pleasing Side Dish


If you’re looking for a show-stopping side dish that will have your guests coming back for seconds, look no further than this creamy and cheesy scalloped sweet potatoes recipe. Perfect for holidays, special occasions, or any time you want to elevate your meal, this dish combines the natural sweetness of sweet potatoes with a luscious, creamy sauce and a generous topping of melted cheese. Let’s dive into the details of this crowd-pleasing recipe.

Ingredients

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish and set it aside.

  2. Prepare the Sweet Potatoes: Peel the sweet potatoes and slice them into thin rounds, about 1/8 inch thick. You can use a mandoline for even slices, but a sharp knife works just as well.

  3. Make the Creamy Sauce: In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Sprinkle the flour over the butter and whisk continuously to form a smooth paste, cooking for 2-3 minutes. Gradually pour in the milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook the sauce until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Season with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.

  4. Layer the Sweet Potatoes: Arrange a layer of sweet potato slices in the prepared baking dish, slightly overlapping. Pour a portion of the creamy sauce over the sweet potatoes, then sprinkle with a handful of Gruyère cheese. Repeat the layers until all the sweet potatoes, sauce, and cheese are used, finishing with a generous layer of cheese on top.

  5. Bake to Perfection: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 40 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender, the sauce is bubbly, and the cheese is golden and bubbly.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Let the scalloped sweet potatoes rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves for a pop of color and an extra burst of flavor. This dish pairs beautifully with roasted meats, poultry, or even as a standout vegetarian option.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra layer of flavor, add thinly sliced onions or crispy bacon between the layers of sweet potatoes.
  • Experiment with different cheeses such as sharp cheddar, fontina, or even a touch of blue cheese for a unique twist.
  • If you’re short on time, parboil the sweet potato slices for a few minutes before layering them in the baking dish to speed up the cooking process.

In Conclusion

Creamy, cheesy, and irresistibly delicious, these scalloped sweet potatoes are sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. Whether you’re entertaining a crowd or simply treating your family to a special meal, this side dish brings comfort and elegance to the table. The rich, velvety sauce enveloping tender sweet potatoes, topped with a golden layer of melted cheese, is a true celebration of flavor and texture. Give this recipe a try and watch it become a cherished addition to your culinary creations. Cheers to good food and great company!
