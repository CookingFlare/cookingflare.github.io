Delicious Fourth of July BBQ Fish Recipes for Seafood Lovers


Are you a seafood lover? Do you enjoy the smoky flavors of a barbeque? Well, you’re in luck because today, we’re diving into the world of Fourth of July BBQ fish recipes that will make your taste buds dance with delight. Whether you’re planning a backyard cookout or a beachside gathering, these delicious recipes will add a touch of elegance and flavor to your Independence Day celebrations. So, fire up those grills and let’s get cooking!

Grilled Lemon Herb Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 lemons, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Place each salmon fillet on a sheet of aluminum foil.
  3. Drizzle olive oil over each fillet and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Top each fillet with a couple of lemon slices and sprinkle with chopped dill and parsley.
  5. Fold the aluminum foil to create a packet, ensuring it is tightly sealed.
  6. Place the packets on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  7. Carefully remove the packets from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

Spicy Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss to coat them evenly with the marinade. Let them marinate for about 15 minutes.
  4. Thread the shrimp onto the soaked wooden skewers, leaving a small space between each shrimp.
  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until the shrimp turn pink and opaque.
  6. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them cool for a minute before serving.

Grilled Halibut Tacos with Mango Salsa

Ingredients:

  • 4 halibut fillets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small tortillas
  • 1 cup mango salsa (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the halibut fillets with the spice mixture, making sure to coat them evenly.
  4. Place the fillets on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Remove the fillets from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.
  6. Warm the tortillas on the grill for about 30 seconds per side.
  7. To assemble the tacos, place a halibut fillet on each tortilla and top with mango salsa.
  8. Serve the tacos with additional salsa on the side.

Grilled Swordfish Steaks with Lemon Butter Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 4 swordfish steaks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • For the lemon butter sauce:
    • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub the swordfish steaks with olive oil and season them with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the steaks on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the fish is cooked through and has grill marks.
  4. While the steaks are grilling, prepare the lemon butter sauce. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is heated through and well combined.
  5. Remove the swordfish steaks from the grill and drizzle them with the lemon butter sauce.
  6. Let the steaks rest for a few minutes before serving.

Conclusion

This Fourth of July, take your BBQ game to the next level with these delicious fish recipes. From the smoky flavors of grilled salmon and shrimp skewers to the refreshing taste of halibut tacos and swordfish steaks, these dishes are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your seafood cravings. So, grab your apron, fire up the grill, and enjoy a mouthwatering seafood feast this Independence Day. Happy cooking and happy Fourth of July!
