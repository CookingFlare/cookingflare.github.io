Leftover Veggie Stir-Fry Turned into a Flavorful Soup


Welcome to today’s recipe blog post! If you’re like me, you love finding creative ways to use up leftovers and create delicious meals. Today, we’re going to turn leftover veggie stir-fry into a flavorful soup that will warm your soul and tantalize your taste buds. This recipe is not only a great way to reduce food waste but also a fantastic option for a quick and satisfying meal. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

To make this leftover veggie stir-fry soup, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Leftover veggie stir-fry (about 2 cups)
  • Vegetable broth (4 cups)
  • Onion (1 medium, diced)
  • Garlic cloves (2, minced)
  • Ginger (1-inch piece, grated)
  • Soy sauce (2 tablespoons)
  • Sesame oil (1 tablespoon)
  • Sriracha sauce (1 tablespoon, optional)
  • Green onions (2, chopped)
  • Fresh cilantro (a handful, chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent, around 5 minutes.

  2. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot. Sauté for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Now, it’s time to bring out the star of the show - the leftover veggie stir-fry! Add it to the pot and stir well, allowing the flavors to blend together.

  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, soy sauce, and sriracha sauce (if using). Give it all a good stir and let it come to a gentle boil.

  5. Once the soup is boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and create a rich and flavorful base.

  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember, the leftover veggie stir-fry may already have some seasoning, so be sure to taste before adding more salt.

  7. Just before serving, sprinkle the chopped green onions and fresh cilantro on top. These fresh herbs will add a burst of freshness and color to the soup.

  8. Ladle the soup into bowls and serve hot. You can enjoy it as is or pair it with some crusty bread or a side salad for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize this recipe based on your preferences and the ingredients you have on hand. You can add other leftover vegetables, such as bell peppers or mushrooms, to the soup for extra flavor and texture.

  • If you prefer a thicker soup, you can puree a portion of the soup using an immersion blender or a regular blender. This will give the soup a velvety texture while still maintaining the chunky goodness of the leftover veggie stir-fry.

  • For a heartier meal, you can add cooked noodles or rice to the soup. This will transform it into a filling and satisfying one-pot meal.

  • If you want to add a touch of creaminess to the soup, you can stir in a tablespoon of coconut milk or cashew cream just before serving. This will add a luscious and indulgent element to the dish.

Conclusion

And there you have it - a simple and delicious way to transform leftover veggie stir-fry into a flavorful soup. Not only does this recipe help reduce food waste, but it also allows you to enjoy a comforting and nourishing meal. So, the next time you find yourself with some leftover stir-fry in the fridge, don’t hesitate to whip up this delightful soup. Your taste buds will thank you!
