Savory Chicken and Rice Casserole Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the heartwarming appeal of a savory chicken and rice casserole. This classic recipe combines tender chicken, fluffy rice, and a medley of vegetables, all enveloped in a creamy sauce that brings everything together. Perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or meal prep, this casserole is not only delicious but also easy to make. Let’s dive into the details of creating this delightful dish that will surely become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

To prepare this savory chicken and rice casserole, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Casserole

  • 2 cups cooked chicken (shredded or diced)
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice (uncooked)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup cream of chicken soup (or homemade)
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 1 medium onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional, for topping)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for sautéing)

For the Topping (Optional)

  • 1 cup crushed crackers (like Ritz or saltines)
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that your casserole cooks evenly and thoroughly.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until it becomes translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring frequently to prevent burning. The aroma of sautéed onions and garlic will fill your kitchen, setting the stage for a delicious meal.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked chicken, uncooked rice, chicken broth, cream of chicken soup, frozen mixed vegetables, sautéed onions and garlic, dried thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together until well combined. The mixture should be creamy and fragrant, with the rice evenly distributed throughout.

Step 4: Transfer to Baking Dish

Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with cooking spray or a little olive oil. Pour the chicken and rice mixture into the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly. If you’re using cheese, sprinkle half of the shredded cheddar cheese over the top of the mixture for added flavor.

Step 5: Bake the Casserole

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for 45 minutes. This allows the rice to cook and absorb the flavors of the chicken and broth. After 45 minutes, remove the foil and check the casserole. If the rice is tender and the liquid is mostly absorbed, it’s time to add the topping.

Step 6: Add the Topping

In a small bowl, mix the crushed crackers with melted butter and grated Parmesan cheese. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top of the casserole. If you’re a cheese lover, feel free to add the remaining cheddar cheese on top as well.

Step 7: Final Bake

Return the casserole to the oven, uncovered, and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and crispy. The cheese should be melted and bubbly, creating a delicious crust that contrasts beautifully with the creamy filling.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Once the casserole is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish is best enjoyed warm, and it pairs wonderfully with a simple green salad or some crusty bread. Scoop generous portions onto plates and watch as your family digs in with delight.

Tips for the Perfect Casserole

  1. Use Leftover Chicken: This recipe is a fantastic way to use up leftover rotisserie chicken or any cooked chicken you have on hand. It saves time and adds great flavor.

  2. Customize Your Vegetables: Feel free to swap out the frozen mixed vegetables for your favorites. Broccoli, bell peppers, or even spinach can add a fresh twist to the dish.

  3. Make It Ahead: This casserole can be prepared ahead of time. Simply assemble it, cover it tightly with foil, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours before baking. Just add an extra 10-15 minutes to the baking time if it’s coming straight from the fridge.

  4. Add Some Heat: If you enjoy a little spice, consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or some diced jalapeños to the mixture for a kick.
