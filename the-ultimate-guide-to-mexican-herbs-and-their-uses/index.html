The Ultimate Guide to Mexican Herbs and Their Uses


When it comes to Mexican cuisine, the aroma of vibrant herbs is an integral part of the culinary experience. Aromatic and flavorful, Mexican herbs have the ability to elevate even the most basic dishes into mouth-watering masterpieces. From the bright citrus notes of lime to the warm earthy undertones of oregano, Mexican herbs have been a cornerstone of the country's rich culinary heritage.

  1. Cilantro: The Flavor of Mexico Cilantro is one of the most versatile Mexican herbs, used in both fresh and dried forms. Also known as coriander, cilantro has been used in Mexican cooking for centuries, particularly in salsas, salads, and sauces. It pairs particularly well with the zesty flavor of lime juice, providing a beautiful harmony of citrus and herbal notes. Cilantro is an essential herb in many traditional Mexican dishes, including guacamole and salsas. In addition to its flavorful uses, cilantro has also been found to have several health benefits, including:

Reducing the risk of infections by producing antioxidants that eliminate free radicals from the body Reducing inflammation, making it a savior for people who experience chronic pain and inflammation Supporting bone health with high levels of minerals such as calcium and magnesium

One of the popular Mexican recipes that feature cilantro is the traditional sauce called Chipotle Cilantro Sauce. This recipe combines flavorful chipotle peppers, cilantro, lime juice, garlic, and sour cream to create a delicious and warming sauce perfect for tacos. Chipotle Cilantro Sauce Recipe

1/4 cup mayonnaise 1/4 cup sour cream 1 tablespoon chipotle peppers in adobo sauce 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro 1 teaspoon lime juice 1 small garlic clove Salt, to taste

Combine all the ingredients and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to combine. 2. Oregano: Warming the Mexican Soul Mexican oregano has been used in traditional medicine and cooking for centuries, providing a host of health benefits and enticing flavors. The flavor profile of oregano is warm, earthy, and herbaceous, with a slight pungency that pairs well with a wide range of ingredients. Use it in combination with fresh chilies or sliced chilies in your salsas and it;s referred to as fresh Chili-o. In traditional Mexican cooking, oregano is used to make a variety of sauces and salsas, adding a hint of earthy flavor that complements the bright and fresh ingredients. One of the most popular dishes that feature oregano is chile con carne, a traditional Mexican dish made with chilies, beef, onions, garlic, and oregano. 3. Epazote: Shedding Some Light on Traditional Medicine Epazote is a lesser-known Mexican herb, but it has been a cornerstone of traditional Mexican medicine for thousands of years. Its unique flavor and aroma have made it a popular ingredient in a variety of dishes, from soups and stews to salads and salsas. This Mexican herb pairs particularly well with beans, adding a hint of bitterness that balances the rich flavor of beans. One of the popular Mexican recipes that feature epazote is a hearty and flavorful chili soup called Pozole. This traditional soup combines epazote, shredded pork, hominy, tomatoes, onions, and chilies to create a delicious and nutritious meal. Pozole Soup Recipe

2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon dried epazote 2 cups low-sodium broth 1 cup suceeds home ground pork or chicken 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes 1 tablespoon sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper Optional: epazote leaves, chopped onion and hominy for garnish

Heat oil in a large pot and sauté the onion and garlic until they're soft. Add the ancho chilies, cumin, and epazote and stir. Add the broth, bring to a boil, add the pork or chicken, and cook until tender. Add the diced tomatoes, sugar, salt, pepper, and hominy, and simmer for 10 minutes.
