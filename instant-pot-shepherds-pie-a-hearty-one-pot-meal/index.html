Instant Pot Shepherd's Pie: A Hearty, One-Pot Meal


When you’re craving a comforting, filling meal that’s both easy to make and packed with flavor, Instant Pot Shepherd’s Pie is a perfect choice. This classic dish has long been a favorite for family dinners and gatherings, and using the Instant Pot makes it quicker and simpler than ever. Whether you’re new to using an Instant Pot or are a seasoned pro, this recipe will guide you step-by-step to create a delicious shepherd’s pie that’s rich in flavor and texture, all in one pot.

What is Shepherd’s Pie?

Before diving into the recipe, it’s important to know what makes Shepherd’s Pie such a beloved dish. Traditionally, Shepherd’s Pie is a savory casserole made with a base of ground meat, often lamb or beef, mixed with vegetables and topped with creamy mashed potatoes. The dish is then baked until golden brown and deliciously crispy on top.

While the original version has roots in the United Kingdom and Ireland, Shepherd’s Pie has evolved and adapted over the years to suit various tastes and dietary needs. With the Instant Pot, we can recreate this hearty comfort food in less time and with less effort, without sacrificing any of the flavor.

Why Use an Instant Pot for Shepherd’s Pie?

Using an Instant Pot to make Shepherd’s Pie has several advantages over traditional methods. Here’s why it’s an excellent choice for this dish:

  1. Speed: The Instant Pot significantly reduces cooking time. You can have a piping hot, homemade Shepherd’s Pie ready in under an hour, a fraction of the time it would take if you were to prepare it using a stovetop and oven.

  2. One-Pot Meal: The Instant Pot is a game-changer for anyone looking to save on cleanup time. Since everything is cooked in one pot, there’s no need to juggle multiple pans. From browning the meat to cooking the mashed potatoes, the Instant Pot handles it all.

  3. Flavor Locking: The sealed pressure cooking environment ensures that all the flavors meld together beautifully, making for a dish that’s rich, savory, and satisfying.

  4. Versatility: While traditional Shepherd’s Pie is made with lamb or beef, you can easily swap in turkey, chicken, or even plant-based ground meat if you want to cater to specific dietary preferences.

Instant Pot Shepherd’s Pie Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make Instant Pot Shepherd’s Pie:

For the Meat Filling:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef or lamb (or ground turkey/chicken for a leaner option)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Mashed Potatoes:

  • 2 pounds (900g) russet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup milk (or more, depending on desired creaminess)
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • Fresh parsley or chives for garnish (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that we have all the ingredients ready, it’s time to get cooking! Here’s how to make Shepherd’s Pie in your Instant Pot from start to finish:

Step 1: Cook the Meat Filling

  1. Sauté the Meat: Turn your Instant Pot on to the “Sauté” setting. Once hot, add the ground beef (or lamb) and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Drain any excess fat if necessary.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: Add the diced onion and garlic to the pot and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onions soften and become translucent.

  3. Add Vegetables and Seasonings: Add the diced carrots and peas to the pot. Stir in the tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the beef broth (or vegetable broth), scraping the bottom of the pot with a spatula to loosen any browned bits. These bits add rich flavor to the dish.

  5. Pressure Cook: Secure the lid on the Instant Pot and set the valve to “Sealing.” Use the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” setting to cook for 8 minutes on high pressure. Once the cooking cycle finishes, allow the pressure to naturally release for about 10 minutes before turning the valve to “Venting” to release the remaining pressure.

  6. Stir the Filling: Open the lid, give everything a good stir, and taste. Adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Set the meat mixture aside in a bowl.

Step 2: Make the Mashed Potatoes

  1. Cook the Potatoes: While the meat is cooking, place the diced potatoes into the Instant Pot. Add enough water to cover the potatoes, and sprinkle with a pinch of salt.

  2. Pressure Cook the Potatoes: Secure the lid on the Instant Pot, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook the potatoes on high pressure for 8 minutes. Once done, allow the pressure to naturally release for 5 minutes before manually venting the remaining pressure.

  3. Mash the Potatoes: Drain the cooked potatoes and return them to the Instant Pot. Add the butter, milk, garlic powder (if using), salt, and pepper. Mash until smooth and creamy, adding more milk if needed to reach your desired consistency.

Step 3: Assemble the Shepherd’s Pie

  1. Layer the Meat Filling: Spread the cooked meat mixture evenly in the bottom of the Instant Pot insert.

  2. Top with Mashed Potatoes: Carefully spoon the mashed potatoes over the meat mixture, spreading them out to cover the filling completely. Use a spatula to smooth the top. If you prefer a more decorative top, you can use a fork to create a pattern or ridges in the potatoes.

Step 4: Final Cooking

  1. Pressure Cook Again: Close the Instant Pot lid (no need to seal the vent), and set the Instant Pot to “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” for 5 minutes on low pressure. This step helps to blend the flavors and gives the mashed potatoes a golden top.

  2. Quick Release: Once the time is up, perform a quick release by turning the valve to “Venting” and carefully releasing the pressure.

  3. Serve: Open the lid, and your Instant Pot Shepherd’s Pie is ready to serve! Garnish with freshly chopped parsley or chives for a burst of color and extra flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For Extra Flavor: Add a splash of red wine to the meat filling after browning the meat and allow it to cook down before adding the broth. This adds depth and complexity to the dish.

  • Vegetarian Shepherd’s Pie: Swap out the meat for lentils or plant-based crumbles for a hearty, vegetarian version of Shepherd’s Pie. You can also use a mix of vegetables like mushrooms, zucchini, and bell peppers for a satisfying, meat-free option.

  • Topping Variations: While mashed potatoes are traditional, you can get creative with the topping. Try a mixture of mashed cauliflower for a lower-carb version, or add shredded cheese on top of the potatoes before broiling for a cheesy, golden finish.

  • Freezing: Shepherd’s Pie is a great make-ahead meal. If you want to freeze it, assemble the pie in a freezer-safe container, but skip the final cooking step. Instead, cover the pie and freeze it for up to 3 months. To cook from frozen, simply cook in the Instant Pot for 15-20 minutes on high pressure, then follow the final steps.

Serving Ideas

Instant Pot Shepherd’s Pie is a filling, one-pot meal that doesn’t require much in terms of side dishes, but if you’re looking to round out the meal, here are a few ideas:

  • Steamed Greens: A side of steamed broccoli, spinach, or green beans adds a light, fresh contrast to the rich Shepherd’s Pie.
  • Salad: A simple green salad with a tangy vinaigrette can help cut through the richness of the pie.
  • Bread: Serve with some warm, crusty bread for dipping into any leftover gravy or meat sauce.

Final Thoughts

Instant Pot Shepherd’s Pie is the ultimate comfort food made easy. With just a few simple steps, you can create a flavorful, hearty meal that’s perfect for any weeknight or special occasion. The best part? You only need one pot to make it, making cleanup a breeze. Whether you stick with the classic beef filling or try a different variation, this dish is sure to satisfy everyone at your table. Happy cooking!
