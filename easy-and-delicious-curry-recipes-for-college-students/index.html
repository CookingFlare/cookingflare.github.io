Easy and Delicious Curry Recipes for College Students


As a college student, you’re often balancing classes, assignments, social life, and a variety of other responsibilities. Amid all that, finding time to cook a meal that’s quick, satisfying, and flavorful can feel like a challenge. Enter curry – a versatile, delicious dish that’s easy to make, customizable to your taste, and perfect for meal prep. Whether you’re craving a rich and creamy chicken curry or a vegetarian delight, curry recipes are your ticket to a meal that doesn’t just fill you up, but also fuels your busy day.

Here, we’ve gathered a collection of easy curry recipes that will take you no time to whip up and will leave your taste buds wanting more. Whether you’re a novice cook or just looking to save time, these recipes are perfect for the college kitchen, with ingredients that are both affordable and readily available.

1. Simple Chicken Curry

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional)
  • 1 can of coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes (400 g)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (optional)
  • Rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced chicken to the pan and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until browned on all sides.
  4. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and chili powder (if using). Cook for another minute to let the spices bloom.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and diced tomatoes. Stir everything together and bring to a simmer.
  6. Reduce the heat and cook for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the curry to thicken and the flavors to meld. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve over cooked rice and garnish with fresh cilantro if desired.

This simple chicken curry is comforting, aromatic, and satisfying. You can adjust the level of spice to your preference by adding more chili powder or opting for a mild curry powder blend. It’s also perfect for meal prepping – make a big batch at the start of the week, and you’ve got lunches and dinners sorted.

2. Vegetarian Chickpea Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (400 g), drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 can of coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes (400 g)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh spinach (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Rice or naan bread, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for 5-6 minutes, until soft and translucent.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, cooking for another 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, cumin, and smoked paprika. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chickpeas, diced tomatoes, and coconut milk to the pan. Stir well to combine.
  5. Bring to a simmer, reduce the heat, and cook for 15-20 minutes, letting the curry thicken and the flavors blend together.
  6. Add spinach in the last few minutes of cooking, letting it wilt into the curry.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve the curry over rice or with warm naan bread.

This vegetarian chickpea curry is a great option for those who want a quick, plant-based meal. The combination of coconut milk and spices creates a creamy, rich base that complements the chickpeas beautifully. It’s filling, nutritious, and easy on the wallet.

3. One-Pot Spicy Lentil Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (400 g)
  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Rice or flatbread, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until soft and golden, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, cumin, cinnamon, and turmeric. Cook for 1 minute to toast the spices.
  4. Add the rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes, coconut milk, and vegetable broth. Stir to combine.
  5. Bring the curry to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, uncovered, for about 25-30 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve with rice or flatbread and garnish with fresh cilantro.

This one-pot lentil curry is not only budget-friendly but also high in protein and fiber. It’s a great option for college students looking to make a meal that’s both hearty and healthy. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity – just throw everything into one pot and let it cook! Perfect for a busy weeknight or for prepping in advance.

4. Easy Beef and Potato Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (400 g)
  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 2 cups beef broth or water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the beef stew meat and brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger to the pot and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, paprika, and cinnamon, cooking for another minute to release the spices’ flavors.
  4. Add the diced potatoes, diced tomatoes, coconut milk, and beef broth to the pot. Stir everything together.
  5. Bring the curry to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the beef is tender and the potatoes are fully cooked.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the curry over rice and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro.

This beef and potato curry is a comforting, filling dish that’s perfect for colder days or when you need something hearty to keep you energized. It’s easy to make and doesn’t require too many ingredients, making it ideal for the college kitchen.

5. Quick Thai Green Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon green curry paste
  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 cup sliced bell peppers (any color)
  • 1/2 cup chopped bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh basil or cilantro for garnish
  • Rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the sliced chicken and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Stir in the red and green curry pastes and cook for 1-2 minutes to release the flavors.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk, fish sauce, and brown sugar, stirring to combine.
  4. Add the sliced bell peppers and bamboo shoots (if using). Bring the curry to a simmer and cook for about 10-15 minutes, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the curry over rice and garnish with fresh basil or cilantro.

This Thai green curry is perfect for when you’re craving something a bit more exotic. It’s packed with bold flavors, from the creamy coconut milk to the heat of the curry pastes. Plus, it’s quick to make, so you can have a flavorful, restaurant-style dish in no time.

Final Thoughts

These curry recipes are perfect for college students who want to cook something delicious without spending hours in the kitchen. The best part about curry is its versatility – you can use whatever protein or vegetables you have on hand, and customize the spices to your taste. Whether you’re into creamy coconut-based curries or rich tomato-based ones, there’s a recipe here for everyone.

Curry also makes an excellent meal prep option. You can cook a large batch, store it in the fridge, and enjoy leftovers throughout the week. With these simple recipes, you’ll be able to enjoy homemade, flavorful meals that are as easy on your schedule as they are on your budget.

So, grab your ingredients, fire up the stove, and start cooking – these easy curry recipes will quickly become your go-to dishes for the semester.
