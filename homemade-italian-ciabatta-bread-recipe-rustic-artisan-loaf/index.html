Homemade Italian Ciabatta Bread Recipe - Rustic Artisan Loaf


Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re about to embark on a delicious journey to make Homemade Italian Ciabatta Bread - a rustic artisan loaf that will transport you straight to the streets of Italy. This bread is known for its airy, hole-ridden interior and crispy, golden crust. Get ready to impress your family and friends with your baking skills, as we guide you through the step-by-step process of creating this mouthwatering bread. So, roll up your sleeves, put on your apron, and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 500g bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 400ml water

Instructions

  1. Mixing the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, and instant yeast. Make a well in the center and pour in the water. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a sticky dough forms. Don’t worry if it looks shaggy at this stage, that’s what we want.

  2. Kneading the dough: Lightly flour a clean surface and turn out the dough onto it. Begin kneading the dough by stretching it away from you, then folding it back onto itself. Repeat this motion for about 10-15 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, you can sprinkle a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much.

  3. First rise: Place the dough back into the mixing bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the dough has doubled in size. This slow rise will help develop the bread’s flavor.

  4. Shaping the loaves: Once the dough has risen, generously flour your work surface and gently turn out the dough. Divide it into two equal portions. Working with one portion at a time, shape it into a rectangular shape by folding the sides towards the center, and then folding the top and bottom towards the center. Carefully transfer the shaped dough onto a floured baking sheet or a proofing basket, seam side down. Repeat the process with the second portion of dough.

  5. Second rise: Cover the shaped dough with a kitchen towel and let it rise for another 45 minutes to 1 hour. During this time, the loaves will increase in size but won’t double like in the first rise.

  6. Preheating the oven: While the dough is on its second rise, preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a baking stone, place it on the middle rack of the oven to preheat as well. If not, a regular baking sheet will do just fine.

  7. Baking the bread: Once the oven is preheated, carefully transfer the risen loaves onto a baking stone or baking sheet. If you’re using a baking sheet, you can bake both loaves at the same time. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the bread has a deep golden crust. You can also tap the bottom of the bread to check if it sounds hollow, which is a good indicator that it’s done.

  8. Cooling and serving: Remove the loaves from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for at least 15 minutes before slicing. Ciabatta bread is best enjoyed fresh, so serve it warm or at room temperature with your favorite dips, spreads, or soups.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve successfully created your very own Homemade Italian Ciabatta Bread - a rustic artisan loaf that’s as delicious as it is impressive. The airy interior, chewy texture, and crispy crust make this bread a standout in any gathering. Whether you’re enjoying it on its own or using it as a base for sandwiches, this Ciabatta bread will elevate your culinary skills to new heights. So, go ahead and savor every bite of this homemade delight. Buon appetito!
