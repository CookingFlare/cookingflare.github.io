10 Authentic Indian Recipes Every Home Cook Should Try

Indian cuisine is a rich tapestry of flavors, with a bewildering variety of spices, herbs, and cooking techniques that cater to every palate. From the spicy kick of the chilli-infused curries to the delicate nuances of the creamy kormas, Indian cooking has something to offer for every occasion. In this article, we will delve into ten authentic Indian recipes that are a must-try for every home cook. Chettinad Chicken Located in the southern state of Tamil Nadu, Chettinad cuisine is known for its bold use of spices and eclectic combinations. This iconic dish from the region is a staple of South Indian cuisine and is known for its complex blend of spices and tangy flavor. The secret to making the perfect Chettinad chicken lies in the use of a unique spice blend, which typically includes cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and star anise. To make Chettinad chicken, start by marinating chicken pieces in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, and spices. Then, heat some oil in a pan and sauté the onions, ginger, and garlic before adding the marinated chicken. Once the chicken is cooked, add a mix of spices including coriander, cumin, turmeric, cayenne pepper, and finally a blend of curry leaves and cardamom. Simmer the curry for at least 20 minutes before garnishing it with fresh cilantro and serving it with steamed rice or roti. Saag Aloo A popular Punjabi dish from the Indian state of Punjab, Saag Aloo is a tangy and slightly spicy potato curry. This simple yet flavorful recipe is a staple of Indian cuisine and can be easily made by any home cook. To make the curry, start by sautéing onions and garlic in oil before adding a mix of mustard and jaggery (a type of unrefined sugar). Then, add the grated ginger and chilies, followed by some chopped tomatoes and a sprinkle of garam masala. Cook the mixture for at least 15 minutes before adding diced potatoes and a variety of winter greens like kale and spinach. The secret to making a good Saag Aloo lies in the use of mustard oil, which gives the dish a distinct nutty flavor. Simply chop the potatoes and the greens into small pieces and sauté them in oil before adding the curry mixture made with the onions, garlic, ginger, and spices. Once the potatoes and greens are cooked, serve them with a dollop of raita (yogurt sauce) or hang thecori. Naan Bread Naan bread is a staple of Indian cuisine and a fundamental element of any Indian meal. This leavened flatbread is a perfect accompaniment to a variety of curries and is easy to make at home. To make naan bread, start by dissolving yeast in warm water and mixing it with flour, yeast, and other dry ingredients. Knead the dough for at least 10 minutes before letting it rest for 2 hours. when the dough has risen, divide it into small portions and shape each portion into a circular disk. Cook the naan in a skillet with a little oil or ghee until they are golden brown and puffed up. Serve the naan hot with a dollop of butter, melted ghee, or some chutney. Kashmiri Rogan Josh The name Rogan Josh translates to "reduced fire" and is a classic Kashmiri dish that is known for its rich and creamy texture. This popular Kashmiri recipe is made with lamb or goat, coconut milk, and possibly some cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and cinnamon. To make Rogan Josh, start by marinating lamb pieces in a mixture of spices and yogurt before cooking them in oil. Add the cumin and coriander, followed by the coconut milk and cayenne pepper, and stir the mixture for at least 20 minutes until it thickens. The secret to making a good Rogan Josh lies in the use of Kashmiri chilies, which are known for their mild heat and smoky flavor. Sauté some onions, garlic, and ginger before adding the marinated lamb mixture, chopped tomatoes, and the Kashmiri chilies. Stir-fry the mixture for a few minutes before adding the coconut milk and spices. Let the curry simmer for at least 30 minutes before garnishing it with some fresh cilantro and serving it with basmati rice or naan. Mumbai-Style Vada Pav Vada pav is a popular street food from the Indian state of Maharashtra and is essentially a deep-fried potato dumpling placed in a slice of bread (pav). To make Mumbai-style Vada Pav, start by boiling some potatoes before mashing them with spices and onions. Shape the potato mixture into small dumplings and deep-fry them until they are golden brown. Meanwhile, toast some bread and spread green chutney on it. Assemble the Vada pav by placing the fried potato dumpling in the bread slice. To make it more authentic, add some onions, chutneys, or local masalas, and some sliced cucumber and carrots. Chettinad Rasam Chettinad Rasam is a popular liquor from the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, and its medicinal properties are well-documented in Ayurveda. The original recipe of Chettinad rasam is a spicy and sour broth made with tamarind, onions, and a mix of spices. It is served as a digestive after every meal and is a staple in South Indian cuisine. To make the authentic Chettinad Rasam, start by roasting some spices like coriander seeds, mustard seeds, and cumin seeds. Grind the spices with cumin, coriander, and dried mint to form a fine paste. Dissolve the paste in the tamarind water, and sauté the onions, chilies, and garlic in oil before adding the Chettinad rasam paste. Let the Rasam simmer for at least 15 minutes before adding jaggery and a dash of lime juice. Dal Makhani Dal Makhani is a popular Punjabi dish made from black lentils and kidney beans cooked in cream. The secret ingredient of Dal Makhani is macadamia nut oil, which gives the dish a rich and creamy texture. To make Dal Makhani, begin by soaking the lentils and soaking some black lentils for 4 hours before sautéing them in ghee. Once the lentils are cooked, mash them in a blender to remove some texture. Heat some oil in a pan before adding some chilies, cumin seeds, and onions. Here's a revised version of the original post: Exploring the Flavors of India: 10 Essential Dishes Every Home Cook Should Try Indian cuisine is a rich tapestry of flavors, with a bewildering variety of spices, herbs, and cooking techniques that cater to every palate. From the spicy kick of the chilli-infused curries to the delicate nuances of the creamy kormas, Indian cooking has something to offer for every occasion. In this article, we will delve into ten authentic Indian recipes that are a must-try for every home cook. Chettinad Chicken Located in the southern state of Tamil Nadu, Chettinad cuisine is known for its bold use of spices and eclectic combinations. This iconic dish from the region is a staple of South Indian cuisine and is known for its complex blend of spices and tangy flavor. The secret to making the perfect Chettinad chicken lies in the use of a unique spice blend, which typically includes cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and star anise. To make Chettinad chicken, start by marinating chicken pieces in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, and spices. Then, heat some oil in a pan and sauté the onions, ginger, and garlic before adding the marinated chicken. Once the chicken is cooked, add a mix of spices including coriander, cumin, turmeric, cayenne pepper, and finally a blend of curry leaves and cardamom. Simmer the curry for at least 20 minutes before garnishing it with fresh cilantro and serving it with steamed rice or roti. Saag Aloo A popular Punjabi dish from the Indian state of Punjab, Saag Aloo is a tangy and slightly spicy potato curry. This simple yet flavorful recipe is a staple of Indian cuisine and can be easily made by any home cook. To make the curry, start by sautéing onions and garlic in oil before adding a mix of mustard and jaggery (a type of unrefined sugar). Then, add the grated ginger and chilies, followed by some chopped tomatoes and a sprinkle of garam masala. Cook the mixture for at least 15 minutes before adding diced potatoes and a variety of winter greens like kale and spinach. Naan Bread Naan bread is a staple of Indian cuisine and a fundamental element of any Indian meal. This leavened flatbread is a perfect accompaniment to a variety of curries and is easy to make at home. To make naan bread, start by dissolving yeast in warm water and mixing it with flour, yeast, and other dry ingredients. Knead the dough for at least 10 minutes before letting it rest for 2 hours. Kashmiri Rogan Josh The name Rogan Josh translates to "reduced fire" and is a classic Kashmiri dish that is known for its rich and creamy texture. This popular Kashmiri recipe is made with lamb or goat, coconut milk, and possibly some cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and cinnamon. To make Rogan Josh, start by marinating lamb pieces in a mixture of spices and yogurt before cooking them in oil. Add the cumin and coriander, followed by the coconut milk and cayenne pepper, and stir the mixture for at least 20 minutes until it thickens. Mumbai-Style Vada Pav Vada pav is a popular street food from the Indian state of Maharashtra and is essentially a deep-fried potato dumpling placed in a slice of bread (pav). To make Mumbai-style Vada Pav, start by boiling some potatoes before mashing them with spices and onions. Shape the potato mixture into small dumplings and deep-fry them until they are golden brown. Meanwhile, toast some bread and spread green chutney on it. Chettinad Rasam Chettinad Rasam is a popular liquor from the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, and its medicinal properties are well-documented in Ayurveda. The original recipe of Chettinad rasam is a spicy and sour broth made with tamarind, onions, and a mix of spices. It is served as a digestive after every meal and is a staple in South Indian cuisine. Dal Makhani Dal Makhani is a popular Punjabi dish made from black lentils and kidney beans cooked in cream. The secret ingredient of Dal Makhani is macadamia nut oil, which gives the dish a rich and creamy texture. To make Dal Makhani, begin by soaking the lentils and soaking some black lentils for 4 hours before sautéing them in ghee. Lauki Hara Bhara Korma Lauki Hara Bhara Korma is a popular North Indian recipe made with bottle gourd, paneer, and a medley of spices. This creamy curry is a staple of North Indian cuisine and is a perfect substitute for rich and heavy curries. To make Lauki Hara Bhara Korma, start by slicing some bottle gourd and sautéing it in ghee until tender. Then, mash some paneer and heat a mixture of spices like cumin seeds and coriander, then add garam masala, cardamom, and cinnamon. Palak Paneer Palak Paneer is a popular North Indian recipe made with spinach, paneer, and a medley of spices. This creamy curry is a staple of North Indian cuisine and is a perfect substitute for rich and heavy curries. To make Palak Paneer, start by blanching some spinach and blending it with ginger and green chilies. Then, heat a mixture of spices like cumin seeds and coriander, then add garam masala, cardamom, and cinnamon. Murgh Biryani Murgh Biryani is a popular North Indian recipe made with chicken, basmati rice, and a variety of spices. This flavorful rice dish is a staple of North Indian cuisine and is a perfect accompaniment to a variety of curries. To make Murgh Biryani, start by marinating chicken pieces in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, and spices. Then, heat some oil in a pan and sauté the onions, ginger, and garlic before adding the marinated chicken. Once the chicken is cooked, layer the cooked rice, chicken, and spices in a pot and steam it for at least 20 minutes before serving.
