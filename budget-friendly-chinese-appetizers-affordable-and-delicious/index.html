Budget-Friendly Chinese Appetizers: Affordable and Delicious


When it comes to Chinese cuisine, people often think that it’s expensive and complicated to make. However, that’s not entirely true. With a little creativity and some budget-friendly ingredients, you can create delicious and authentic Chinese appetizers that won’t break the bank.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the most popular and affordable Chinese appetizers that are perfect for any occasion. From crispy fried wontons to savory egg rolls, we’ll cover it all.

Wonton Soup

Wonton soup is a classic Chinese dish that’s both comforting and flavorful. The best part? It’s incredibly easy to make and can be customized to suit your taste preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of wonton wrappers
  • 1/2 pound of ground pork
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup of chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped cabbage, chopped scallions, garlic, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  2. Lay a wonton wrapper on a flat surface and place a tablespoon of the pork filling in the center. Dip your finger in water and run it along the edges of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle and press the edges together to seal the wonton.
  3. Repeat the process until all the wrappers are used up. You should end up with around 20-25 wontons.
  4. In a large pot, combine the chicken broth and wontons. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the wontons float to the surface.
  5. Serve the wontons hot, garnished with chopped scallions and soy sauce.

Egg Rolls

Egg rolls are a staple in Chinese cuisine and can be made in a variety of ways. This recipe is for a classic vegetable egg roll that’s crispy on the outside and savory on the inside.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of egg roll wrappers
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup of chopped carrots
  • 1/2 cup of chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped cabbage, carrots, scallions, garlic, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  2. Lay an egg roll wrapper on a flat surface and place a tablespoon of the vegetable filling in the center. Dip your finger in water and run it along the edges of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle and press the edges together to seal the egg roll.
  3. Repeat the process until all the wrappers are used up. You should end up with around 10-12 egg rolls.
  4. Heat about 1-2 inches of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add the egg rolls and fry until they’re golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Remove the egg rolls from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Potstickers

Potstickers are a type of dumpling that’s popular in Northern China. They’re typically filled with a mixture of ground pork and vegetables, and then pan-fried until crispy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of potsticker wrappers
  • 1/2 pound of ground pork
  • 1/2 cup of chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup of chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped cabbage, chopped scallions, garlic, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  2. Lay a potsticker wrapper on a flat surface and place a tablespoon of the pork filling in the center. Dip your finger in water and run it along the edges of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper into a triangle and press the edges together to seal the potsticker.
  3. Repeat the process until all the wrappers are used up. You should end up with around 20-25 potstickers.
  4. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add about 1-2 tablespoons of vegetable oil to the pan and swirl it around to coat the bottom.
  5. When the oil is hot, add the potstickers to the pan and cook until they’re crispy and golden brown on the bottom, about 3-4 minutes.
  6. Add about 1/4 cup of chicken broth to the pan and cover with a lid. Steam the potstickers for 5-7 minutes, or until they’re cooked through and the liquid has been absorbed.
  7. Serve the potstickers hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

Conclusion

In this post, we’ve covered three delicious and affordable Chinese appetizers that are perfect for any occasion. From crispy wontons to savory egg rolls, and from pan-fried potstickers to steamed dumplings, there’s something for everyone. With these recipes, you can create authentic Chinese dishes that are both delicious and budget-friendly. So next time you’re planning a dinner party or just want to try something new, give these recipes a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
