How to Make Homemade Nut Brittle: A Crunchy Delight


Are you tired of the same old candies and chocolates? Looking for something unique, homemade, and irresistible to satisfy your sweet tooth? If yes, then nut brittle might be just the perfect treat for you. In this blog post, we’ll explore how to make a delicious, crunchy, and mouthwatering homemade nut brittle. So, buckle up and get ready to enjoy the delightful taste of this classic confection.

Ingredients: • 2 cups mixed nuts (almonds, peanuts, cashews, hazelnuts, etc.) • 1 cup granulated sugar • 3 tablespoons corn syrup • 1/4 cup unsalted butter • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract • Optional: 1 cup chocolate chips • Optional: sprinkle toppings of your choice (coarse sea salt, chopped dried fruits, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large bowl, mix together the nuts and set aside.

  3. In a medium saucepan, combine the sugar, corn syrup, and butter. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the butter and sugar have melted and the mixture reaches a rolling boil. This may take about 10-12 minutes.

  4. Once the mixture reaches a boil, continue to stir frequently and cook until it reaches the “soft crack” stage. To test this, drop a small amount of the mixture into cold water; if it forms a hard, but pliable ball that can be flattened into a crescent shape, it has reached the right consistency. This will usually take around 10-15 minutes.

  5. Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Immediately pour the mixture over the nuts arranged on the prepared baking sheet. Be careful as it will be extremely hot.

  6. Using a spatula, spread the mixture evenly across the baking sheet. It’s crucial to do this while it’s still warm and pliable; otherwise, it will be difficult to get that lovely brittle effect.

  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center looks almost dry. Allow the brittle to cool completely on the baking sheet.

  8. Break the brittle into irregular, chunky pieces. This can be done by tapping the baking sheet against your work surface; however, take extra caution so as not to shatter the brittle or cut yourself while breaking it.

Optional Chocolate and Toppings Steps:

If you’d like a chocolate-dipped nut brittle, follow these steps:

  1. Once the brittle is cool and hard, break it into smaller, more manageable pieces.

  2. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or microwave, stirring frequently until smooth.

  3. Dip the brittle pieces into the melted chocolate completely or just halfway, depending on your preference.

  4. Place the dipped brittle on wax paper and immediately add your sprinkle toppings before the chocolate sets.

  5. Allow the chocolate to set completely before storing.

Conclusion: Homemade nut brittle is a show-stopping treat with its delightful crunch and irresistible sweetness. Once you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll never want to stop making it. Store your nut brittle in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two weeks or freeze it for longer storage. So, indulge in this delightful, homemade concoction, and impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. Happy cooking!
