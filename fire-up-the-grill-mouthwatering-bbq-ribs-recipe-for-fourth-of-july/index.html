Fire up the Grill: Mouthwatering BBQ Ribs Recipe for Fourth of July


As the Fourth of July approaches, the smell of sizzling BBQ ribs wafts through the air, tantalizing our taste buds and beckoning us to gather ‘round the grill. There’s something special about firing up the grill on this patriotic holiday, surrounded by family and friends, and indulging in a plate of tender, fall-off-the-bone ribs. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of slow-cooking ribs to perfection, infusing them with a rich, tangy BBQ sauce that will leave your guests begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 racks of pork ribs (about 4 pounds total)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Preparation

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s talk about the importance of prepping our ribs. A good rub is essential in developing the rich, caramelized crust that we all know and love. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs (this will help the rub penetrate deeper into the meat) and sprinkle the mixture evenly over both racks. Massage the rub into the meat, making sure to get it into all the nooks and crannies.

Slow-Cooking the Ribs

Preheat your grill to 225°F (110°C), using your preferred type of heat source (gas, charcoal, or wood). Once the grill is hot, place the ribs on the grill, bone-side down. Close the lid and let the magic happen. Cook the ribs for 4-5 hours, or until they reach an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C). Every hour, spritz the ribs with apple cider vinegar and flip them over to ensure even cooking.

Glazing the Ribs

After 4-5 hours of slow-cooking, it’s time to add the glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the BBQ sauce and olive oil. Brush the glaze evenly over both racks of ribs, making sure to get it into all the crevices. Return the ribs to the grill and cook for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the glaze is caramelized and sticky.

Serving

Once the ribs are cooked to your liking, remove them from the grill and let them rest for 5-10 minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute, making the meat even more tender and flavorful. Cut the ribs into individual portions and serve with your favorite sides, such as coleslaw, baked beans, or corn on the cob.

Tips and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor, try adding wood chips or chunks to your grill while cooking the ribs. This will infuse the meat with a rich, smoky flavor.
  • If you prefer a spicier rib, add a dash of hot sauce to the glaze before brushing it over the ribs.
  • For a more intense BBQ flavor, try using a combination of BBQ sauces in your glaze. Experiment with different types and ratios to find your perfect blend.

As you fire up the grill this Fourth of July, remember that the key to mouthwatering BBQ ribs is slow-cooking and patience. By following this recipe, you’ll be rewarded with a plate of tender, fall-off-the-bone ribs that will leave your guests in awe. So go ahead, gather ‘round the grill, and get ready to indulge in the ultimate American summer classic.
