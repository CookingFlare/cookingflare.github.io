The Art of Making Homemade Goma-ae: Japanese Sesame Spinach Salad


If there’s one thing that Japanese cuisine is known for, it’s the harmonious balance of flavors and textures. And no dish exemplifies this better than Goma-ae, a traditional Japanese sesame spinach salad. Made with just a handful of ingredients, Goma-ae is a delicious and nutritious side dish that will elevate any meal.

Ingredients

To make this delectable dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of fresh spinach
  • 2 tablespoons of toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the spinach. Rinse the spinach thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities. Trim off the tough stems and discard them.

  2. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a pinch of salt to the boiling water. Blanch the spinach by placing it in the boiling water for about 30 seconds, or until the leaves are wilted and vibrant green.

  3. Once the spinach is blanched, drain it and immediately transfer it to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process and preserve its vibrant color. Let the spinach sit in the ice water for a few minutes, then drain it well and gently squeeze out any excess water. Set aside.

  4. In a small skillet over medium heat, toast the sesame seeds until they turn golden brown and become fragrant. This should take about 2-3 minutes. Remove the skillet from heat and let the sesame seeds cool for a few minutes.

  5. Using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder, grind the toasted sesame seeds until they form a coarse powder. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can grind the sesame seeds longer until they become a paste-like consistency.

  6. In a small bowl, combine the ground sesame seeds, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Mix well until the sugar is dissolved and the sauce is smooth and well-combined.

  7. Now it’s time to bring everything together. In a large mixing bowl, add the blanched spinach and pour the sesame sauce over it. Gently toss the spinach with the sauce, making sure each leaf is coated evenly.

  8. Transfer the dressed spinach to a serving dish. You can enjoy Goma-ae immediately, or for even better flavor, let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  9. Serve the Goma-ae as a side dish alongside your favorite Japanese meal, or enjoy it on its own as a refreshing and healthy snack.

The Perfect Balance of Flavors

Goma-ae is a dish that beautifully balances the nuttiness of toasted sesame seeds with the subtle sweetness of mirin and the savory umami of soy sauce. The blanched spinach adds a refreshing and vibrant element, making this salad a delight to the eyes and the taste buds.

Not only is Goma-ae a delicious addition to any meal, but it also packs a nutritional punch. Spinach is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as iron and fiber. The sesame seeds provide healthy fats, protein, and a host of minerals, making this salad a wholesome choice for both vegans and non-vegans alike.

Experiment and Personalize

While the traditional Goma-ae recipe calls for spinach, you can experiment with different greens such as kale or Swiss chard for a unique twist. You can also add a splash of rice vinegar or a sprinkle of chili flakes to customize the flavors to your liking. Don’t be afraid to get creative and make this dish your own.

Conclusion

Goma-ae is a testament to the art of Japanese cooking. With its simple yet flavorful combination of ingredients, this sesame spinach salad is a true delight. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking for a healthy and tasty side dish, Goma-ae is a must-try. So why not gather the ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure in your own kitchen? Your taste buds will thank you.
