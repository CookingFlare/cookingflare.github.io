The Best Korean Desserts: 5 Sweet Treats to Try


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors and unique ingredients, but it’s the desserts that often leave a lasting impression. From chewy rice cakes to refreshing shaved ice, Korean desserts offer a delightful array of textures and tastes that are sure to satisfy any sweet tooth. In this blog post, we will explore five of the best Korean desserts that you absolutely must try. Each treat has its own story and cultural significance, making them not just delicious but also a window into Korea’s rich culinary heritage.

1. Bingsu (빙수)

What is Bingsu?

Bingsu is a popular Korean shaved ice dessert that has gained international fame for its refreshing qualities, especially during the hot summer months. Traditionally, bingsu is made with finely shaved ice topped with a variety of ingredients, including sweetened red beans, fruits, condensed milk, and even ice cream. The combination of textures and flavors makes bingsu a delightful treat that can be customized to suit any palate.

The History of Bingsu

The origins of bingsu can be traced back to the Joseon Dynasty, where it was known as “patbingsu” (팥빙수), meaning “red bean shaved ice.” It was originally a royal dessert enjoyed by the elite, but over time, it became a beloved treat for everyone. Today, bingsu has evolved into numerous variations, with modern twists that include flavors like matcha, mango, and even chocolate.

How to Make Bingsu at Home

Making bingsu at home is easier than you might think. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of ice
  • 1 cup of sweetened red beans (pat)
  • 1 cup of diced fresh fruits (mango, strawberries, or kiwi)
  • 1/2 cup of condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup of ice cream (optional)
  • Toppings: nuts, mochi, or chocolate syrup

Instructions:

  1. Shave the Ice: Use a blender or ice shaver to create finely shaved ice. If using a blender, pulse the ice until it resembles snow.
  2. Layer the Ingredients: In a bowl, layer the shaved ice, sweetened red beans, and diced fruits.
  3. Add Condensed Milk: Drizzle condensed milk over the top for sweetness.
  4. Top It Off: Add a scoop of ice cream if desired, and finish with your choice of toppings.
  5. Serve Immediately: Enjoy your homemade bingsu right away for the best texture.

2. Tteok (떡)

What is Tteok?

Tteok refers to a variety of Korean rice cakes made from glutinous rice flour. These cakes can be steamed, pounded, or pan-fried, and they come in many shapes and sizes. Tteok is often enjoyed during celebrations and special occasions, symbolizing good fortune and prosperity.

The Significance of Tteok

Tteok has a deep-rooted cultural significance in Korea. It is often served during holidays, birthdays, and weddings. Each type of tteok has its own meaning; for example, baekseolgi (white rice cake) is associated with celebrations and new beginnings, while songpyeon (half-moon shaped rice cake) is traditionally made during the Chuseok harvest festival.

Popular Types of Tteok

  • Songpyeon: These are half-moon shaped rice cakes filled with sweetened sesame seeds or red beans, often made during the Chuseok festival.
  • Baekseolgi: A fluffy, steamed rice cake that is often used for birthdays and celebrations.
  • Injeolmi: Chewy rice cakes coated in powdered soybean flour, offering a nutty flavor.

How to Make Songpyeon

Here’s a simple recipe for making songpyeon at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of sweet rice flour (glutinous rice flour)
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • Fillings: sweetened sesame seeds or red bean paste
  • Pine needles (optional, for aroma)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a bowl, mix sweet rice flour, water, and sugar until a smooth dough forms. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. Shape the Cakes: Take a small piece of dough and flatten it in your palm. Place a small amount of filling in the center and fold the dough over to form a half-moon shape. Pinch the edges to seal.
  3. Steam the Cakes: Line a steamer with pine needles (if using) and place the songpyeon on top. Steam for about 20 minutes until cooked through.
