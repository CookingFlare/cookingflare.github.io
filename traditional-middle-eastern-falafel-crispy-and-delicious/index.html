Traditional Middle Eastern Falafel: Crispy and Delicious


In the heart of the Middle East, falafel has been a staple food for centuries. This crispy and flavorful street food is a beloved treat for locals and tourists alike. With its rich history and cultural significance, falafel has become an integral part of Middle Eastern cuisine. In this blog post, we will delve into the traditional recipe of falafel and explore the secrets behind its crispy exterior and tender interior.

The History of Falafel

Falafel is believed to have originated in Egypt around the 10th century. The word “falafel” is derived from the Arabic word “ta’amiya,” which means “to fry.” Initially, falafel was made with fava beans, but over time, chickpeas became the preferred ingredient. The dish spread throughout the Middle East and became a popular street food, particularly in Egypt, Syria, Lebanon, and Turkey.

Ingredients

To make traditional Middle Eastern falafel, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight and drained
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions (green onions)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mash the chickpeas using a fork or a potato masher until they are coarsely chopped.
  2. Add the minced garlic, chopped parsley, cilantro, scallions, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper to the bowl with the chickpeas. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  3. Using your hands, shape the mixture into small patties, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter and 1/2 inch thick. You should end up with around 8-10 patties.
  4. Heat the olive oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. When the oil is hot, add a few falafel patties to the pan (do not overcrowd the pan).
  5. Fry the falafel for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Repeat with the remaining patties.
  6. Using a slotted spoon, remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Tips and Variations

  • To achieve the perfect crispiness, make sure the oil is hot enough before frying the falafel. You can test the oil by dropping a small piece of the mixture into the pan. If it sizzles and rises to the surface, the oil is ready.
  • For a crisper exterior, you can chill the falafel patties in the refrigerator for 30 minutes before frying.
  • To bake the falafel instead of frying, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the patties on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until crispy and golden brown.
  • Experiment with different spices and herbs to give your falafel a unique flavor. Some options include paprika, sumac, and dried oregano.

Conclusion

Falafel is a beloved Middle Eastern dish that is easy to make and packed with flavor. By following this traditional recipe, you can create crispy and delicious falafel that is sure to impress your friends and family. Whether you choose to fry or bake your falafel, this recipe is a great starting point for any falafel enthusiast. So go ahead, give it a try, and taste the magic of the Middle East!
