Wholesome Chicken and Dumplings: A Healthier Recipe


Chicken and dumplings is a classic comfort food that warms the heart and soul. Traditionally rich and hearty, this dish can often be laden with heavy creams and refined ingredients. However, in this healthier version, we’ll create a nourishing and wholesome chicken and dumplings recipe that does not compromise on flavor or comfort. Packed with lean protein, wholesome vegetables, and lightened-up dumplings, this dish will delight your taste buds while keeping your health in check. Let’s dive into the ingredients, preparation methods, and tips for making an irresistible, healthier chicken and dumplings.

Ingredients

For the Chicken and Broth

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs for more flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

For the Dumplings

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder (optional)
  • ½ cup nonfat Greek yogurt (for creaminess)
  • ¼ cup low-fat milk (or dairy-free alternative)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (for flavor)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, sliced carrots, and chopped celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are softened and the onion is translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  2. Add Chicken and Broth: Place the chicken breasts into the pot along with the low-sodium chicken broth. Ensure the chicken is submerged in the liquid. Add the dried thyme, dried rosemary, bay leaf, and season with salt and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through. Use a meat thermometer to ensure the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

Step 2: Shred the Chicken

  1. Remove the Chicken: Once the chicken is cooked, carefully remove it from the pot and place it on a cutting board. Let it cool for a few minutes.

  2. Shred the Chicken: Using two forks, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.

Step 3: Make the Dumplings

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and garlic powder (if using). Whisk together until well combined.

  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Stir in the nonfat Greek yogurt and low-fat milk until a soft dough forms. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.

Step 4: Combine and Cook

  1. Combine Chicken and Broth: Return the shredded chicken to the pot with the broth and vegetables. If using, stir in the frozen peas at this stage. Bring the mixture back to a gentle simmer.

  2. Dumpling Time: Drop spoonfuls of the dumpling mixture into the simmering broth. Cover the pot with a lid and allow the dumplings to steam for about 10-15 minutes, or until they have puffed up and are cooked through. Avoid lifting the lid during this time to ensure proper steaming.

Step 5: Finish and Serve

  1. Taste and Adjust Seasoning: Before serving, taste the broth and adjust seasoning if necessary. Remove the bay leaf.

  2. Garnish: Ladle the chicken, dumplings, and broth into bowls. Sprinkle with fresh parsley for a pop of color and added flavor.

  3. Enjoy: Serve warm and watch as this wholesome chicken and dumplings dish brings comfort to your table.

Health Benefits of This Recipe

This healthier version of chicken and dumplings is not just delicious; it’s also packed with nutritional benefits:

  • Lean Protein: Chicken is a great source of lean protein, essential for muscle repair and overall health.
  • Whole Grains: Using whole wheat flour in the dumplings adds fiber, which aids in digestion and helps keep you full longer.
  • Vegetables Galore: The combination of carrots, celery, and peas provides essential vitamins and minerals, along with antioxidants that support overall health.
  • Lower in Fat: By using Greek yogurt instead of heavy cream, we reduce the overall fat content while still achieving a creamy, satisfying texture.

Tips for Success

  1. Customize Your Vegetables: Feel free to add or substitute vegetables based on what you have on hand. Kale, spinach, or mushrooms can be excellent additions.
  2. Make it Ahead: This dish can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat gently on the stove or microwave.
  3. Freezer-Friendly: You can freeze leftovers for a quick meal later. Just be aware that the dumplings may become softer upon reheating.

Conclusion

Wholesome chicken and dumplings is the perfect dish for a cozy night in or a family gathering. With its comforting flavors and nutritious ingredients, you can enjoy a classic favorite without the guilt. This healthier recipe is not only satisfying but also a great way to nourish your body with wholesome goodness. Try it out and watch as it becomes a staple in your home, bringing warmth and joy to every meal. Happy cooking!
