Delicious Greek Spinach and Feta Stuffed Zucchini Boats


When it comes to delicious and healthy Mediterranean-inspired dishes, Greek Spinach and Feta Stuffed Zucchini Boats are a standout option. This recipe combines the fresh flavors of zucchini, the heartiness of spinach, and the rich tang of feta cheese to create a dish that is as satisfying as it is nutritious. Whether you’re looking for a vegetarian main course or a flavorful side dish, these zucchini boats are sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium zucchinis
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 5 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the zucchinis: Cut the zucchinis in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds to create a hollow “boat” shape. Place the zucchini halves in a baking dish.

  3. Prepare the filling: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 3-4 minutes. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes. Remove the skillet from the heat and let the mixture cool slightly.

  4. Assemble the boats: In a large bowl, combine the spinach mixture with the crumbled feta, Parmesan cheese, and chopped dill. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Spoon the filling into the hollowed-out zucchini halves, mounding it generously.

  5. Bake the boats: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake the zucchini boats for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the zucchinis are tender and the filling is golden and bubbly.

  6. Serve and enjoy: Once the zucchini boats are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Then, serve them warm and enjoy the delicious flavors of the Mediterranean!

Tips and Variations

  • For a heartier version, you can add cooked quinoa or brown rice to the filling mixture.
  • Feel free to customize the filling with your favorite herbs and spices, such as oregano, basil, or red pepper flakes.
  • If you’re a fan of melted cheese, sprinkle some mozzarella or provolone on top of the stuffed zucchini boats before baking for an extra gooey finish.

Conclusion

Greek Spinach and Feta Stuffed Zucchini Boats are a delightful way to incorporate fresh vegetables and Mediterranean flavors into your meal. With their vibrant colors, savory filling, and satisfying texture, these zucchini boats are a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a main course, a side dish, or even as a tasty appetizer. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply treating yourself to a flavorful meal, these zucchini boats are a must-try recipe that will surely become a favorite in your kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to the sunny shores of Greece with this delightful recipe!
