All-Time Favorite: Classic Mac and Cheese Recipe


If there’s one dish that’s universally loved, it’s classic mac and cheese. Creamy, cheesy, and oh-so-comforting, this timeless favorite has been a staple in households for generations. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner, a potluck, or just craving some indulgent comfort food, mac and cheese never fails to hit the spot. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make the ultimate classic mac and cheese that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz (450g) elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups (960ml) whole milk
  • 2 cups (200g) sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup (100g) mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 teaspoon dry mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup (100g) breadcrumbs
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Cook the Macaroni: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the macaroni and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a large saucepan, melt the 1/2 cup of butter over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in the flour to create a roux. Cook the roux for a few minutes, stirring constantly, until it turns a light golden color. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Continue cooking and stirring until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.

  3. Add the Cheese: Reduce the heat to low and add the shredded cheddar and mozzarella cheese to the sauce, stirring until the cheese is completely melted and the sauce is smooth. Stir in the dry mustard, cayenne pepper (if using), and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Combine the Macaroni and Cheese Sauce: Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and gently fold until the macaroni is evenly coated with the creamy cheese sauce.

  5. Bake the Mac and Cheese: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to a greased baking dish. In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs with the melted butter and sprinkle the mixture over the top of the mac and cheese.

  6. Bake for Perfection: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden and the edges are bubbly.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once out of the oven, allow the mac and cheese to cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley if desired and enjoy the creamy, cheesy perfection!

Tips for Perfection

  • Feel free to experiment with different cheese combinations to suit your taste preferences. Gouda, gruyere, or fontina can add a unique twist to the classic recipe.
  • For extra flavor, consider adding crispy bacon, diced ham, or sautéed mushrooms to the mac and cheese before baking.
  • If you’re a fan of a crispy top, broil the mac and cheese for a couple of minutes at the end of baking to achieve a golden crust.

There you have it - an irresistible classic mac and cheese recipe that’s sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re a cheese purist or love to customize with additional mix-ins, this dish is endlessly versatile and undeniably delicious. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create a bowl of pure comfort and satisfaction. After all, classic mac and cheese has a timeless appeal that never goes out of style. Enjoy!
