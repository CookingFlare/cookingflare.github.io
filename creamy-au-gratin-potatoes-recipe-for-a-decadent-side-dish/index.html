Creamy Au Gratin Potatoes Recipe for a Decadent Side Dish


If you’re looking for a rich and indulgent side dish to accompany your main course, look no further than this creamy and flavorful Au Gratin Potatoes recipe. With its layers of tender potatoes, rich cream sauce, and a golden, cheesy crust, this dish is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving for more. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of grated Gruyere cheese
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.

  3. Sprinkle the flour over the butter and garlic mixture, stirring continuously until well combined. Cook for an additional minute to cook off the raw flour taste.

  4. Slowly pour in the heavy cream while whisking constantly to prevent any lumps from forming. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens and comes to a gentle simmer.

  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the grated Gruyere and Parmesan cheeses. Stir until the cheeses have melted and the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season with salt, pepper, and dried thyme to taste.

  6. Arrange a layer of thinly sliced potatoes in the greased baking dish, slightly overlapping each other. Pour a layer of the creamy cheese sauce over the potatoes, making sure to cover them evenly.

  7. Repeat the layering process with the remaining potatoes and sauce, finishing with a layer of sauce on top. Ensure that the potatoes are fully coated with the sauce to prevent them from drying out during baking.

  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 40 minutes. This will allow the potatoes to cook and become tender.

  9. After 40 minutes, remove the foil and sprinkle some additional grated cheese on top for an extra cheesy and golden crust.

  10. Return the dish to the oven and bake uncovered for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the potatoes are fork-tender.

  11. Once done, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the sauce to thicken slightly.

  12. Serve the Creamy Au Gratin Potatoes as a decadent side dish alongside your favorite main course. This dish pairs well with roasted meats, grilled vegetables, or even a simple green salad.

Enjoy the creamy, cheesy goodness of these Au Gratin Potatoes, and let your taste buds revel in the decadence of this side dish. With its creamy sauce, tender potatoes, and golden crust, this recipe is sure to become a favorite among your family and friends. So go ahead, indulge and savor every bite of this delicious dish!
