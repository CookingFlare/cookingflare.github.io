Simple and Delicious Meatball Recipes for Cooking for Seniors


As we age, our taste buds may change, but our love for good food remains the same. Cooking for seniors can be a joyful experience, especially when you have simple and delicious recipes up your sleeve. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering meatball recipes that are perfect for seniors. These recipes are not only easy to prepare but also packed with flavor and nutrition, making them ideal for the older generation.

Classic Italian Meatballs

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 24-ounce jar marinara sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, parsley, egg, garlic, salt, black pepper, oregano, and red pepper flakes. Mix until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs, about 1 inch in diameter, and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through.
  5. In a large skillet, heat the marinara sauce over medium heat. Add the cooked meatballs to the sauce and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Serve the meatballs with your choice of pasta or crusty bread. Garnish with additional Parmesan cheese and parsley if desired.

Turkey and Spinach Meatballs

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 24-ounce jar marinara sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, spinach, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, garlic, salt, black pepper, basil, and nutmeg. Mix until well combined.
  3. Form the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through.
  5. In a saucepan, heat the marinara sauce over medium heat. Add the cooked meatballs to the sauce and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Serve the meatballs with steamed vegetables or a light salad for a healthy and satisfying meal.

Sweet and Sour Meatballs

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks, drained
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup sweet and sour sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the ground pork, breadcrumbs, onion, egg, garlic powder, ginger, salt, and black pepper. Form the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  3. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through.
  4. In a large skillet, combine the pineapple chunks, bell peppers, and sweet and sour sauce. Heat over medium heat until the sauce begins to bubble.
  5. Add the cooked meatballs to the skillet and stir gently to coat them with the sweet and sour sauce.
  6. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the sweet and sour meatballs over steamed rice for a delightful combination of flavors and textures.

Conclusion

These simple and delicious meatball recipes are perfect for cooking for seniors.
