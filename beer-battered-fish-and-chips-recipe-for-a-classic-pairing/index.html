Beer-Battered Fish and Chips Recipe for a Classic Pairing


There are few dishes as iconic and satisfying as a perfectly crispy beer-battered fish served alongside golden, fluffy chips. This classic pairing has stood the test of time and continues to be a favorite in pubs and homes around the world. The crispy exterior of the fish, enveloped in a flavorful beer-infused batter, paired with the soft interior of the chips, makes for a delightful meal that is loved by many. If you’re looking to recreate this classic dish at home, look no further. Here is a step-by-step guide to making delicious beer-battered fish and chips in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cod fillets
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup beer (lager works well)
  • 2 cups vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into thick strips
  • Salt, to taste
  • Malt vinegar (optional, for serving)
  • Tartar sauce (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chips:

    • Start by rinsing the potato strips under cold water to remove excess starch.
    • Pat them dry with a kitchen towel.
    • In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil to 325°F (165°C).
    • Carefully add the potato strips in batches and fry for about 3-4 minutes until they are soft but have not taken on any color.
    • Remove the partially cooked chips and set them aside to drain on paper towels.

  2. Prepare the Fish:

    • In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
    • Slowly pour in the beer while whisking until the batter is smooth.
    • Let the batter rest for about 15 minutes.
    • In the meantime, heat the oil to 375°F (190°C) in a deep fryer or a heavy-bottomed pot.

  3. Fry the Fish:

    • Dip each cod fillet into the batter, allowing any excess to drip off.
    • Carefully lower the fillets into the hot oil and fry for 4-5 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
    • Once done, remove the fish from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain.

  4. Finish the Chips:

    • Increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C).
    • Re-fry the par-cooked chips in batches until they are crisp and golden brown.
    • Remove the chips from the oil and drain any excess oil on paper towels.

  5. Serve:

    • Season the chips with salt while they are still hot.
    • Serve the beer-battered fish and chips together with malt vinegar and tartar sauce on the side.

Enjoy this classic beer-battered fish and chips dish with your favorite cold beverage for a truly satisfying meal. The crispy texture of the fish and chips, coupled with the savory flavors, make it a timeless recipe that never fails to impress. Whether you’re hosting a casual gathering or simply craving a taste of the pub at home, this recipe is sure to hit the spot. So, roll up your sleeves, grab a cold beer, and get ready to indulge in this delicious and comforting dish!

Remember, the key to achieving the perfect beer-battered fish and chips lies in using fresh ingredients, maintaining the right oil temperature, and ensuring your batter is well-seasoned and airy. With a little practice and patience, you’ll be serving up restaurant-quality fish and chips in no time. Cheers to good food and memorable meals!
