Indulge in Indian Spices: 4 Marinades for Tandoori Chicken


Tandoori chicken, a classic Indian dish that has gained popularity worldwide for its tender, juicy, and flavorful meat. The secret to achieving this mouth-watering dish lies in the marinade, a blend of aromatic spices and yogurt that tenderizes the meat and infuses it with a rich, creamy flavor. In this blog post, we will explore four different marinade recipes for tandoori chicken, each with its unique blend of spices and flavors.

Marinade 1: Classic Tandoori Chicken Marinade

This is a traditional Indian-style marinade that combines the quintessential spices of cumin, coriander, garam masala, and cayenne pepper with the tanginess of yogurt and lemon juice.

  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 2: Spicy Tandoori Chicken Marinade

For those who like a little heat in their food, this marinade adds a kick of chili flakes and cayenne pepper to the classic recipe.

  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 3: Creamy Tandoori Chicken Marinade

This marinade adds a richness to the chicken by incorporating heavy cream and butter into the yogurt mixture.

  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 4: Herby Tandoori Chicken Marinade

This marinade adds a fresh and herbaceous flavor to the chicken by incorporating cilantro, parsley, and mint into the yogurt mixture.

  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint

Combine all the ingredients in a bowl and mix well. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Once you have chosen your marinade, you can cook the chicken in a tandoor oven or a conventional oven. Simply place the chicken on a skewer or in a baking dish and cook until it reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). Serve hot with basmati rice, naan bread, and a dollop of raita to cool down the spice.

In conclusion, these four marinades offer a range of flavor profiles and spice levels to suit every taste. Whether you prefer a classic and traditional flavor or a spicy and adventurous one, there’s a marinade on this list for you. So go ahead, indulge in the world of Indian spices and discover the perfect marinade for your tandoori chicken.
