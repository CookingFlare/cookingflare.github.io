Vegan Oatmeal Raisin Cookies: Chewy and Wholesome


If you’re looking for a delicious and wholesome treat that’s both vegan and packed with flavor, then you’re in for a treat! These Vegan Oatmeal Raisin Cookies are the perfect combination of chewy goodness and wholesome ingredients. They’re easy to make and even easier to devour. So, let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • ½ cup almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ½ cup maple syrup
  • ¼ cup almond butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup raisins

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, whole wheat flour, almond flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Mix well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, almond butter, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  4. Pour the wet mixture into the dry mixture and stir until a thick dough forms. Fold in the raisins until they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.

  5. Using a cookie scoop or tablespoon, drop spoonfuls of the dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Flatten each cookie slightly with the back of a spoon or your fingers.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. The cookies will still be soft when you take them out of the oven, but they will firm up as they cool.

  7. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Once cooled, these Vegan Oatmeal Raisin Cookies are ready to be enjoyed! Serve them with a glass of almond milk or your favorite hot beverage.

Tips and Variations

  • For a nut-free version, replace the almond flour with an equal amount of rolled oats or whole wheat flour.

  • Feel free to add a handful of chopped walnuts or pecans to the dough for an extra crunch.

  • If you prefer your cookies to be sweeter, you can increase the amount of maple syrup to ¾ cup. Alternatively, you can also add a tablespoon of coconut sugar or brown sugar to the dough.

  • These cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week. They also freeze well, so you can make a big batch and enjoy them whenever you crave a wholesome treat.

Final Thoughts

These Vegan Oatmeal Raisin Cookies are a testament to the fact that vegan baking can be just as delicious as its non-vegan counterpart. The combination of oats, raisins, and warm spices creates a cookie that is both chewy and comforting. Plus, with the use of wholesome ingredients like coconut oil and almond flour, you can indulge in these treats without any guilt.

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to bake up a batch of these Vegan Oatmeal Raisin Cookies. Trust me, your taste buds will thank you!
