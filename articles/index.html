Nutritious Paleo Lemon Chicken Skillet Recipe

Are you looking for a delicious and healthy recipe that will satisfy your taste buds and keep you on track with your Paleo lifestyle? Look no further! This Nutritious Paleo Lemon Chicken Skillet Recipe is the perfect dish to add to your repertoire. Packed with flavor and wholesome ingredients, this recipe will leave you feeling nourished and satisfied. Ingredients 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts 2 tablespoons coconut oil 1 onion, diced 3 cloves of garlic, minced 1 cup chicken broth Juice of 2 lemons Zest of 1 lemon 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon black pepper Fresh parsley, for garnish Instructions Start by seasoning the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides. Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breasts to the skillet and cook until browned on both sides, about 4-5 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes. Pour in the chicken broth and lemon juice, scraping the bottom of the skillet to release any browned bits. Bring the mixture to a simmer. Return the chicken breasts to the skillet and sprinkle with dried oregano, dried thyme, and lemon zest. Cover the skillet and let the chicken simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until cooked through and no longer pink in the center. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the skillet and transfer to a serving platter. Let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. Meanwhile, increase the heat under the skillet and let the sauce reduce for about 5 minutes, until slightly thickened. Pour the sauce over the chicken and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot and enjoy! Why Choose Paleo? The Paleo diet focuses on consuming foods that our ancestors would have eaten during the Paleolithic era. This means that grains, legumes, and processed foods are off the table, while lean meats, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds are encouraged.
