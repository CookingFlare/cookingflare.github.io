Herbal Brunch Tartlets: Bite-Sized Delights with Fresh Herb Fillings


Are you looking for a delightful and flavorful brunch recipe that will impress your guests? Look no further than these Herbal Brunch Tartlets! These bite-sized delights are filled with fresh herbs, creating a burst of aromatic flavors in every bite. Whether you’re hosting a brunch party or simply want to treat yourself to something special, these tartlets are the perfect choice.

Ingredients

For the Tartlet Shells:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup cold water

For the Herb Fillings:

  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 cup fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 cup fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by making the tartlet shells. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the cold water, a little at a time, while mixing the dough with a fork. Continue until the dough starts to come together. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it gently until smooth. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Take the chilled dough out of the refrigerator and roll it out on a floured surface until it’s about 1/8 inch thick. Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out circles that will fit into the cups of a mini muffin tin. Press the circles into the muffin tin cups, shaping them into tartlet shells. Prick the bottoms of the shells with a fork to prevent them from puffing up during baking.

  4. Bake the tartlet shells in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes, or until they turn golden brown. Remove them from the oven and let them cool completely on a wire rack.

  5. While the tartlet shells are cooling, prepare the herb fillings. In a mixing bowl, combine the finely chopped basil, dill, parsley, and chives. Add the ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Stir well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  6. Once the tartlet shells have cooled, spoon a generous amount of the herb filling into each shell, making sure to fill them to the top. You can use a piping bag or a spoon to do this.

  7. Serve the Herbal Brunch Tartlets immediately and enjoy the burst of fresh herb flavors in every bite. These tartlets are perfect for brunch, afternoon tea, or any occasion where you want to impress your guests with a unique and delicious appetizer.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different herb combinations to suit your taste. You can also add other ingredients like sun-dried tomatoes, olives, or caramelized onions to the filling for added flavor.
  • If you don’t have a mini muffin tin, you can use a regular-sized muffin tin and make larger tartlets. Adjust the baking time accordingly.
  • These tartlets can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Simply reheat them in the oven for a few minutes before serving.
  • Serve the tartlets with a side of fresh salad or a drizzle of balsamic glaze for an extra touch of elegance.

Conclusion

Herbal Brunch Tartlets are the perfect combination of flavors and textures, making them a delightful addition to any brunch menu. With their flaky tartlet shells and aromatic herb fillings, these bite-sized delights are sure to impress your guests and leave them craving more. So go ahead, give this recipe a try and elevate your brunch experience to a whole new level of deliciousness!
