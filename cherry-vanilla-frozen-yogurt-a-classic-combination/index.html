Cherry Vanilla Frozen Yogurt: A Classic Combination


Who doesn’t love the delightful combination of sweet, juicy cherries and the comforting aroma of vanilla? Today, we’re bringing these classic flavors together in a refreshing and creamy treat – Cherry Vanilla Frozen Yogurt. This recipe is a perfect way to indulge in a guilt-free dessert that’s bursting with fruity goodness and a hint of comforting vanilla.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 2 cups of Greek yogurt
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Cherries: If using fresh cherries, wash and pit them. If using frozen cherries, ensure they are thawed. Place the cherries in a food processor or blender and pulse until they are roughly chopped.

  2. Make the Yogurt Mixture: In a large bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, whole milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Whisk the mixture until the sugar is completely dissolved and everything is well combined.

  3. Blend the Ingredients: Add the chopped cherries to the yogurt mixture. Using a hand blender or regular blender, blend the mixture until the cherries are well incorporated, and the mixture is smooth and creamy.

  4. Chill the Mixture: Once blended, cover the mixture and place it in the refrigerator for about 1-2 hours to chill. This step helps the flavors to meld and intensify.

  5. Churn the Frozen Yogurt: After the mixture has chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes until the frozen yogurt reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  6. Freeze the Yogurt: Transfer the churned frozen yogurt into a freezer-safe container. Cover it with a lid or plastic wrap, ensuring it touches the surface of the frozen yogurt to prevent ice crystals from forming. Freeze for at least 4-6 hours, or until the frozen yogurt is firm.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: When ready to serve, take the frozen yogurt out of the freezer and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly. Scoop the Cherry Vanilla Frozen Yogurt into bowls, garnish with fresh mint leaves if desired, and savor every spoonful of this delightful treat.

Tips and Variations

  • For a dairy-free version, you can use coconut milk or almond milk yogurt in place of Greek yogurt and whole milk.
  • Experiment with different fruits such as strawberries, blueberries, or peaches for a variety of fruity frozen yogurt flavors.
  • Add a crunchy texture by stirring in some chopped nuts or granola before freezing the yogurt.

Cherry Vanilla Frozen Yogurt is not only a delicious dessert but also a healthier alternative to traditional ice cream. With the sweetness of cherries and the warmth of vanilla, this frozen yogurt is sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re hosting a summer get-together or simply craving a cool and creamy treat, this recipe is a delightful way to enjoy the classic combination of cherry and vanilla in a whole new form. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to whip up a batch of this delightful frozen yogurt – your taste buds will thank you!
