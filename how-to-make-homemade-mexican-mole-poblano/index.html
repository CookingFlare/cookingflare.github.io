How to Make Homemade Mexican Mole Poblano


Are you ready to embark on a culinary adventure that will take your taste buds on a journey to Mexico? Look no further than homemade Mexican Mole Poblano, a rich and flavorful sauce that is a staple in Mexican cuisine. In this recipe, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of creating this traditional and mouth-watering dish. Get your apron on and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 3 dried ancho chilies
  • 3 dried pasilla chilies
  • 2 dried mulato chilies
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds, toasted
  • 2 tomatoes, roasted
  • 2 tomatillos, roasted
  • 2 tablespoons raisins
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 tablet Mexican chocolate, grated
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Begin by preparing the dried chilies. Remove the stems and seeds from the chilies, then toast them in a dry skillet over medium heat for a few seconds on each side. Be careful not to burn them. Once toasted, place the chilies in a bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for about 15 minutes until they soften.

  2. While the chilies are soaking, heat the vegetable oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. In a blender or food processor, combine the toasted almonds, sesame seeds, roasted tomatoes, roasted tomatillos, raisins, cocoa powder, cinnamon, cloves, coriander, cumin, and the soaked chilies (drained). Blend until you achieve a smooth paste-like consistency.

  4. Add the chili paste to the saucepan with the sautéed onion and garlic. Stir well to combine and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Gradually pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, stirring continuously to incorporate the liquid into the sauce. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 30 minutes, allowing it to thicken slightly.

  6. After 30 minutes, add the grated Mexican chocolate to the sauce and stir until it melts completely. The chocolate adds depth and richness to the mole, giving it a unique and authentic flavor. Season with salt to taste.

  7. Allow the mole poblano to simmer for an additional 10 minutes, ensuring all the flavors are well combined.

  8. Finally, remove the sauce from the heat and let it cool slightly before serving. The mole can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week, allowing the flavors to intensify over time.

Serving Suggestions

Mole Poblano is traditionally served over roasted chicken or turkey, but it can also be used as a sauce for enchiladas, tacos, or even drizzled over grilled vegetables for a vegetarian option. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve with warm tortillas and rice for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You have successfully made homemade Mexican Mole Poblano. This complex and flavorful sauce will surely impress your family and friends. The combination of dried chilies, spices, and chocolate creates a unique and indulgent taste that is synonymous with Mexican cuisine. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and let the magic of Mole Poblano transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico. Enjoy!
