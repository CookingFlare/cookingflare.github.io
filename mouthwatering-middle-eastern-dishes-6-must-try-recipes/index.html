Mouthwatering Middle Eastern Dishes: 6 Must-Try Recipes


If you’re a fan of bold flavors, aromatic spices, and rich culinary traditions, then Middle Eastern cuisine is a treasure trove of culinary delights waiting to be explored. From savory kebabs to fragrant rice dishes, Middle Eastern cooking is a celebration of fresh ingredients and time-honored recipes that have been passed down through generations. In this article, we’ll take a journey through the vibrant and diverse world of Middle Eastern cuisine, featuring six must-try recipes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds and transport you to the bustling markets and bustling streets of the Middle East.

1. Hummus with Za’atar

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon za’atar
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, cumin, and salt.
  2. Blend until smooth, adding a little water if needed to reach your desired consistency.
  3. Transfer the hummus to a serving dish and create a well in the center.
  4. Drizzle olive oil over the top and sprinkle with za’atar.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with warm pita bread or crunchy vegetables.

2. Falafel

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak the chickpeas in water overnight. Drain and rinse.
  2. In a food processor, pulse the chickpeas, onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, parsley, cilantro, salt, and pepper until coarsely ground.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and stir in the baking powder and flour.
  4. Form the mixture into small balls and flatten slightly.
  5. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep pan and fry the falafel until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve with pita bread, tahini sauce, and a fresh salad.

3. Shawarma

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pita bread, sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and tahini sauce for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, garlic, olive oil, cumin, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken slices to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.
  4. Heat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat and cook the chicken until charred and cooked through.
  5. Warm the pita bread and assemble the shawarma with the chicken, tomatoes, cucumbers, and a drizzle of tahini sauce.
