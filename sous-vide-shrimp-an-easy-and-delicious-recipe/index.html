Sous Vide Shrimp: An Easy and Delicious Recipe


If you’re looking for a way to elevate your shrimp dishes with minimal effort and maximum flavor, sous vide shrimp might just be your new best friend in the kitchen. Cooking shrimp using the sous vide method guarantees perfectly tender, juicy shrimp every time. Whether you’re a novice or an experienced home chef, this technique is simple, reliable, and results in shrimp that are packed with flavor. Let’s dive into how to make the perfect sous vide shrimp, including tips, tricks, and variations to keep your meals exciting.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide (pronounced “soo-veed”) is a French cooking technique that translates to “under vacuum.” It involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature for a prolonged period. This method allows for a level of precision that traditional cooking methods can’t match, resulting in perfectly cooked food every time. For shrimp, sous vide ensures that they stay juicy, tender, and never overcooked, which is a common problem with traditional shrimp preparation methods.

Why Choose Sous Vide for Shrimp?

Shrimp is notoriously easy to overcook, which leads to rubbery, dry texture. By using the sous vide method, the shrimp are cooked slowly at a constant temperature, which means they cook evenly and retain all their natural juices. Here are some reasons why sous vide shrimp should be your go-to cooking method:

  • Perfect Texture: Unlike traditional pan-searing or grilling, sous vide shrimp never becomes overcooked. The low and slow cooking process ensures the shrimp are tender, moist, and flavorful.

  • Consistency: Sous vide cooking provides precise temperature control, so every shrimp will cook exactly the same, resulting in consistent texture and taste.

  • Hands-Off Cooking: Once you set up your sous vide bath and seal the shrimp, you can walk away and focus on other aspects of your meal. It’s ideal for busy weeknights or when you want to prepare other side dishes simultaneously.

  • Flavor Infusion: Sous vide shrimp are cooked in a sealed bag, which traps the shrimp’s natural juices and any added seasonings or marinades, making them extra flavorful.

Now that you understand why sous vide shrimp is such a game-changer, let’s walk through the process of making it at home.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Shrimp

The beauty of sous vide shrimp lies in its simplicity. You don’t need much to make a delicious dish, and you can customize it to your taste. Here’s what you’ll need for the basic recipe:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil (or melted butter for a richer flavor)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika (or regular paprika)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs like parsley, thyme, or dill (optional)

For Optional Marinade (for added flavor):

  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • A pinch of chili flakes (for a touch of heat)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce or Worcestershire sauce

Preparing the Shrimp for Sous Vide

The first step in the sous vide shrimp cooking process is prepping the shrimp. You can either leave the shells on or remove them, depending on your preference. However, many people prefer to peel and devein the shrimp for ease of eating. After this, follow these steps:

  1. Seasoning: Season the shrimp with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, and lemon zest. The paprika will give the shrimp a lovely smoky flavor, while the lemon zest will enhance the fresh, clean taste of the shrimp.

  2. Garlic and Olive Oil: Add the minced garlic and drizzle the olive oil (or melted butter) over the shrimp. The fat will help to keep the shrimp moist and add richness to the flavor. If you’re using an optional marinade, mix the Dijon mustard, honey, soy sauce, and chili flakes in a bowl and coat the shrimp.

  3. Bagging the Shrimp: Once the shrimp is seasoned, it’s time to seal it in a vacuum bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a zipper-lock bag and the water displacement method. Simply place the shrimp in the bag, press out as much air as possible, and seal it tightly. The bag should be free of any air pockets to ensure even cooking.

Cooking Sous Vide Shrimp

The next step is the actual sous vide cooking process. The key to sous vide cooking is maintaining a consistent water temperature for the right amount of time. Here’s how you can do it:

Step 1: Set the Sous Vide Bath

Fill your sous vide container with water and set your immersion circulator to a temperature of 140°F (60°C) for a perfect texture. You can cook shrimp at slightly higher or lower temperatures, but 140°F will give you consistently tender and juicy shrimp.

Step 2: Place the Shrimp in the Water Bath

Once the water has reached the desired temperature, place the sealed bag of shrimp into the water bath. Make sure the shrimp is fully submerged, and it’s a good idea to clip the bag to the side of the container to prevent it from floating. The water bath should maintain a steady temperature for 30-45 minutes.

Step 3: Finish the Shrimp

After the shrimp has finished cooking, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. The shrimp will already be perfectly cooked, but if you like your shrimp a little crispy or golden, you can quickly sear them in a hot pan.

Searing the Shrimp (Optional)

Heat a little olive oil or butter in a pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the shrimp and sear them for about 1-2 minutes on each side until they develop a nice golden crust. Be careful not to overdo it, as the shrimp is already cooked through from the sous vide process, and you just want to add a bit of color and texture.

How to Serve Sous Vide Shrimp

Sous vide shrimp is versatile and can be served in a variety of ways. Here are a few ideas:

  • As an appetizer: Serve the shrimp with a dipping sauce such as cocktail sauce, aioli, or a tangy lemon-garlic butter sauce.

  • In pasta: Toss the shrimp with pasta, olive oil, garlic, and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese for a quick and satisfying meal.

  • In salads: Add the shrimp to a fresh salad with greens, avocado, and a lemon vinaigrette for a light and refreshing dish.

  • In tacos: Season the shrimp with cumin, chili powder, and lime juice, and serve them in soft tortillas with fresh salsa and avocado for a delicious taco night.

  • As a main dish: Pair the shrimp with roasted vegetables or a side of rice for a complete meal.

Tips for the Best Sous Vide Shrimp

  • Use Fresh Shrimp: Whenever possible, opt for fresh, high-quality shrimp. Frozen shrimp works as well, but fresh shrimp will have a better texture and flavor.

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Bag: Make sure there’s enough room in the bag for the shrimp to cook evenly. If you need to cook a large batch, it’s better to use multiple bags than to overcrowd one.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t be afraid to experiment with different seasonings and marinades. Adding herbs, citrus zest, or even a touch of white wine can elevate the flavor of the shrimp.

  • Adjust Cooking Time: Shrimp cooks quickly, and the sous vide method ensures that they stay tender even with extended cooking times. However, if you’re short on time, you can cook shrimp for as little as 15-20 minutes. If you have more time, you can leave them in the water bath for up to an hour without overcooking them.

Sous Vide Shrimp Variations

While the basic sous vide shrimp recipe is already flavorful, you can take things up a notch by adding your personal twist. Here are some variations to try:

  • Garlic Butter Shrimp: Add a few tablespoons of garlic butter to the shrimp before sealing them in the bag. This will infuse the shrimp with an extra layer of richness and flavor.

  • Spicy Shrimp: For a spicy kick, add chili flakes, cayenne pepper, or hot sauce to the shrimp before sealing them. The slow cooking method will infuse the heat throughout the shrimp.

  • Lemon Herb Shrimp: Add fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or basil, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for a light, refreshing taste.

  • Curry Shrimp: If you’re a fan of bold flavors, try marinating the shrimp in a coconut milk-based curry sauce before sealing them in the bag for a unique twist.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide shrimp is a game-changer for anyone who loves seafood but struggles with achieving the perfect texture. With the precise temperature control and slow cooking process, you’re guaranteed tender, flavorful shrimp every time. Whether you’re making a quick weeknight dinner or planning an elegant dinner party, sous vide shrimp is an excellent addition to your culinary repertoire.

So, next time you’re craving shrimp, skip the traditional methods and give sous vide cooking a try. You’ll never look at shrimp the same way again!
