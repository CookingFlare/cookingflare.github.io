Budget-Friendly Italian Pasta Sauce Recipes


Are you a pasta lover on a tight budget? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore a variety of budget-friendly Italian pasta sauce recipes that will satisfy your cravings without breaking the bank. From classic marinara to creamy Alfredo, these recipes are not only delicious but also easy to make. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Classic Marinara Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sauté until translucent.
  2. Add the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool slightly before serving over your favorite pasta.

2. Creamy Alfredo Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Reduce heat to low and pour in the heavy cream. Stir continuously until the cream starts to thicken.
  3. Gradually add the grated Parmesan cheese, stirring until melted and smooth.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool slightly before tossing it with your cooked pasta.

3. Pesto Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the basil leaves, grated Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, and garlic. Pulse until finely chopped.
  2. Gradually add the olive oil while the food processor is running, until the mixture forms a smooth paste.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Toss the pesto sauce with your cooked pasta and enjoy!

4. Arrabbiata Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and dried red pepper flakes, sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the diced tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove from heat and let the sauce cool slightly before tossing it with your cooked pasta.

5. Carbonara Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup diced pancetta or bacon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the diced pancetta or bacon until crispy. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain, reserving a small amount of pasta water.
  5. Immediately toss the hot pasta with the cooked pancetta or bacon, garlic, and the egg mixture. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If the sauce seems too thick, add a splash of reserved pasta water to loosen it up.

Enjoy these budget-friendly Italian pasta sauce recipes that are sure to satisfy your taste buds without putting a strain on your wallet. Feel free to experiment with different ingredients and add your own personal touch. Bon appétit!
