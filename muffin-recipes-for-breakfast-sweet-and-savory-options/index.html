Muffin Recipes for Breakfast: Sweet and Savory Options


Muffins have long been a popular breakfast choice, offering a versatile and delicious way to start the day. Whether you’re in the mood for something sweet to satisfy your morning cravings or a savory option to fuel your busy schedule, muffins are the perfect solution. With their easy-to-make nature and ability to be packed with various ingredients, muffins can be customized to suit every taste.

In this blog post, we will explore a variety of muffin recipes for breakfast, ranging from indulgent sweet treats to hearty savory delights. Whether you’re a novice baker or a seasoned pro, these muffin recipes will give you the inspiration you need to bake up something amazing.

The Appeal of Muffins for Breakfast

Before diving into the recipes, it’s important to understand why muffins make such an excellent choice for breakfast. Muffins are:

  • Convenient: Muffins are portable and easy to eat on the go, which is ideal for busy mornings.
  • Customizable: You can adjust the ingredients to suit dietary preferences, such as gluten-free, dairy-free, or low-sugar.
  • Balanced: A good muffin recipe can offer a balance of carbohydrates, fiber, healthy fats, and protein, making it an ideal breakfast option.
  • Quick to Prepare: Muffins are relatively simple to make, often requiring less than an hour from start to finish.

Now that we’ve highlighted why muffins are a great breakfast option, let’s explore some of the best sweet and savory muffin recipes that you can try.

Sweet Muffin Recipes for Breakfast

1. Classic Blueberry Muffins

Blueberry muffins are a timeless favorite for a reason – their sweet, juicy bursts of flavor combined with a tender crumb make them irresistible. These muffins are perfect for breakfast, whether you’re enjoying a quiet morning at home or rushing out the door.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1 tbsp flour (for dusting blueberries)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, eggs, milk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the blueberries, ensuring they’re evenly distributed in the batter. Dust the blueberries with 1 tablespoon of flour before adding them to prevent them from sinking to the bottom of the muffin.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Banana Nut Muffins

Banana nut muffins are a comforting and satisfying choice for breakfast. The combination of ripe bananas and crunchy nuts creates a hearty and flavorful muffin that is perfect for those mornings when you need something filling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas with a fork until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, brown sugar, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  4. Add the melted butter, egg, milk, and vanilla extract to the mashed bananas and mix until well combined.
  5. Stir in the dry ingredients until just incorporated, being careful not to overmix.
  6. Fold in the chopped walnuts or pecans.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 full.
  8. Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  9. Let the muffins cool for a few minutes before removing them from the tin. Allow them to cool completely on a wire rack.

3. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

If you love the combination of apples and cinnamon, these apple cinnamon muffins will be your new favorite breakfast treat. The natural sweetness from the apples, combined with the warming spices of cinnamon, creates a muffin that’s perfect for any time of the year.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup chopped apple (peeled and cored)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, cinnamon, baking powder, baking soda, salt, granulated sugar, and brown sugar.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, egg, vanilla extract, and buttermilk.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the chopped apples.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool the muffins in the tin for a few minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Savory Muffin Recipes for Breakfast

If you’re not a fan of sweets in the morning, savory muffins are a great alternative. Packed with cheese, herbs, and even vegetables, savory muffins are hearty and satisfying, providing all the nourishment you need to kickstart your day.

1. Cheddar and Chive Muffins

Cheddar and chive muffins are the perfect savory breakfast treat. These muffins are cheesy, flavorful, and light, making them a great side dish for a larger breakfast or a quick snack on the go.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh chives, chopped
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a muffin tin or line it with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and garlic powder.
  3. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and chopped chives.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, egg, and milk.
  5. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Let the muffins cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

2. Spinach and Feta Muffins

For a more sophisticated flavor, try spinach and feta muffins. These savory muffins are packed with healthy spinach and rich feta cheese, making them a perfect addition to your breakfast routine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Stir in the crumbled feta cheese and chopped spinach.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, egg, and milk.
  5. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool before serving.

3. Bacon and Cheese Muffins

If you’re a fan of bacon, these bacon and cheese muffins will quickly become your go-to breakfast option. The crispy bacon combined with melted cheese gives these muffins a savory, satisfying flavor that will keep you full until your next meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 3/4 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Stir in the crumbled bacon and shredded cheddar cheese.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, egg, and milk.
  5. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Cool the muffins before serving.

Conclusion

Muffins are a wonderful and versatile option for breakfast, whether you prefer something sweet or savory. The recipes we’ve shared in this post will help you create delicious, nutritious muffins that are perfect for any morning. From the classic blueberry muffin to the savory bacon and cheese muffin, there’s a muffin recipe for every taste.
