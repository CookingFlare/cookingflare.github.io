Traditional Apple Cider Glazed Ham for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. If you’re looking to add a touch of tradition to your Thanksgiving table this year, consider preparing a mouthwatering Traditional Apple Cider Glazed Ham. This timeless dish is sure to impress your loved ones and elevate your holiday feast to new heights. In this recipe, we’ll explore the step-by-step process of creating a succulent ham infused with the rich, sweet flavor of apple cider glaze.

Ingredients

  • 1 bone-in ham, approximately 8-10 pounds
  • 2 cups apple cider
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ham: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the ham in a roasting pan, making sure to score the top with shallow cuts in a diamond pattern. This will allow the glaze to penetrate the meat, ensuring a flavorful result.

  2. Create the Glaze: In a saucepan, combine the apple cider, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Allow the glaze to cook for 15-20 minutes until it thickens slightly. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Glaze the Ham: Using a basting brush, generously coat the ham with the apple cider glaze, making sure to cover the entire surface. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper to enhance the flavors.

  4. Bake to Perfection: Place the glazed ham in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 15-18 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C). Baste the ham with the remaining glaze every 30 minutes to ensure a caramelized, flavorful crust.

  5. Let it Rest: Once the ham is cooked to perfection, remove it from the oven and allow it to rest for 15-20 minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in a moist and tender finished product.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Carve the ham into generous slices and serve alongside your favorite Thanksgiving sides. The combination of savory ham and sweet apple cider glaze is a match made in holiday heaven, sure to leave your guests coming back for seconds.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a splash of maple syrup to the glaze.
  • If you prefer a smokier profile, you can prepare the ham on a grill using indirect heat, adding a unique charred essence to the dish.
  • Leftover ham can be repurposed into sandwiches, salads, and soups, ensuring that no delicious morsel goes to waste.

Conclusion

With its warm, comforting flavors and classic appeal, Traditional Apple Cider Glazed Ham is a quintessential addition to any Thanksgiving celebration. This recipe allows you to channel the spirit of the season and create a memorable dining experience for your loved ones. As you gather around the table, the tantalizing aroma and delectable taste of this timeless dish will undoubtedly become a cherished part of your holiday traditions. So, roll up your sleeves, embrace the joy of cooking, and savor the magic of Thanksgiving with this remarkable recipe. Cheers to a festive and flavorful feast!
