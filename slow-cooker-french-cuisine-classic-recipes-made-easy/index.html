Slow Cooker French Cuisine: Classic Recipes Made Easy


French cuisine is known for its elegance and sophistication, but it can also be time-consuming and labor-intensive. However, with the help of a slow cooker, you can recreate classic French dishes with ease. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious slow cooker recipes that will transport you to the heart of France.

Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a traditional French dish that combines tender chicken, red wine, and aromatic herbs. It’s a perfect dish for a cozy dinner or a special occasion. Here’s how to make it in a slow cooker:

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Transfer the chicken to the slow cooker.
  3. Add the onion, garlic, and carrot to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Deglaze the skillet with red wine, scraping the bottom to release any browned bits. Pour the wine mixture into the slow cooker.
  5. Add the chicken broth, tomato paste, bay leaf, thyme, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  6. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  7. Serve the Coq au Vin with crusty bread or over mashed potatoes. Sprinkle the crispy bacon on top for an added crunch.

Beef Bourguignon

Beef Bourguignon is another classic French dish that is rich in flavor and perfect for cooler days. The slow cooker version simplifies the cooking process without compromising on taste. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside.
  2. In the same skillet, brown the beef cubes in batches. Transfer the beef to the slow cooker.
  3. Add the onion, garlic, and carrots to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Deglaze the skillet with red wine, scraping the bottom to release any browned bits. Pour the wine mixture into the slow cooker.
  5. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, bay leaf, thyme, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  6. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-6 hours, until the beef is tender.
  7. Serve the Beef Bourguignon with mashed potatoes or crusty bread. Sprinkle the crispy bacon on top for an extra burst of flavor.

Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a vibrant and colorful vegetable stew that represents the essence of French cuisine. It’s a versatile dish that can be served as a main course or as a side dish. Here’s how to make it in a slow cooker:

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until softened.
  2. Transfer the onion and garlic mixture to the slow cooker.
  3. Add the diced eggplant, zucchinis, bell peppers, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  4. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve the Ratatouille as a main course with crusty bread or as a side dish to accompany grilled meat or fish.

Conclusion

With these slow cooker recipes, you can bring the flavors of French cuisine into your kitchen without spending hours in front of the stove. From the rich Coq au Vin to the comforting Beef Bourguignon and the vibrant Ratatouille, these classic French dishes made easy will impress your family and friends. So go ahead, dust off your slow cooker, and embark on a culinary journey to France. Bon appétit!
