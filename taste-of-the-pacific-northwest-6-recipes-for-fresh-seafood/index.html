Taste of the Pacific Northwest: 6 Recipes for Fresh Seafood


The Pacific Northwest is a treasure trove for seafood lovers. With its pristine waters and abundant marine life, the region offers some of the freshest and most flavorful seafood in the world. From tender Dungeness crab to wild-caught salmon, Pacific oysters, and hearty halibut, the culinary possibilities are endless. Let’s dive into six mouthwatering recipes that celebrate the bounty of this coastal paradise.

Cedar Plank Grilled Salmon

The Pacific Northwest is synonymous with wild-caught salmon, and cooking it on a cedar plank is a tradition that infuses the fish with smoky, woody flavors. This preparation is easy, impressive, and perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large salmon fillet (about 2 lbs.), skin on
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cedar plank, soaked in water for at least 2 hours

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat (about 375°F).
  2. Pat the salmon dry and rub it with olive oil, garlic, lemon zest and juice, dill, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the prepared salmon skin-side down on the soaked cedar plank.
  4. Set the plank on the grill, cover, and cook for 20-25 minutes or until the salmon is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Serve with grilled vegetables or a zesty slaw for a complete meal.

Dungeness Crab Cakes with Aioli

This recipe highlights Dungeness crab, a staple of the Pacific Coast. These delicate yet flavorful crab cakes are a true crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

For the Crab Cakes:

  • 1 lb. Dungeness crab meat, picked clean
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for frying)

For the Aioli:

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Pinch of smoked paprika

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all crab cake ingredients (except the oil). Form into small patties (about 8).
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry the crab cakes until golden brown on each side, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  3. For the aioli, mix the mayonnaise, garlic, lemon juice, and smoked paprika in a small bowl.
  4. Serve the crab cakes hot, paired with the aioli and a green salad.

Pacific Northwest Clam Chowder

No seafood feast is complete without a warm bowl of clam chowder. This thick, creamy soup perfectly captures the essence of fresh shellfish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 dozen clams, scrubbed and cleaned
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cups diced potatoes
  • 3 cups clam juice or seafood stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt, pepper, and fresh thyme, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Steam the clams in 1 cup of water until they open. Reserve the liquid and remove the clam meat from the shells.
  2. In a large pot, cook bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside, leaving the drippings in the pot.
  3. Sauté onion and celery in the drippings until softened. Add the potatoes, clam juice, and reserved steaming liquid, cooking until potatoes are tender.
  4. In a separate pan, melt butter and whisk in flour to create a roux. Slowly stir in cream, then add the mixture to the soup.
  5. Stir in the clams, bacon, and thyme. Simmer for 5-10 minutes, adjusting seasoning as needed. Serve with warm crusty bread.

Pan-Seared Halibut with Herb Butter

Halibut’s firm, mild-flavored flesh makes it a versatile centerpiece for any meal. This simple pan-seared preparation lets the fresh fish shine.

Ingredients:

  • 4 halibut fillets (about 6 oz. each)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Pat the halibut fillets dry and season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the fillets for 3-4 minutes per side until golden and fully cooked.
  3. Reduce heat, then melt butter in the pan. Add garlic, parsley, and thyme, basting the fish with the herb butter for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Serve the halibut immediately, garnished with lemon wedges and fresh herbs.

Smoked Oysters with Garlic Butter

Pacific oysters are prized for their rich, briny flavor. This smoked oyster recipe is an easy way to elevate your seafood experience.

Ingredients:

  • 2 dozen fresh oysters
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare a smoker and heat it to 225°F.
  2. Shuck the oysters, discarding the top shells and placing the oyster meat with the bottom shell on a smoking tray.
  3. Melt butter in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring in garlic, parsley, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Transfer the oysters to the smoker and drizzle each with the melted garlic butter. Smoke for 25-30 minutes until the edges begin to curl.
  5. Serve smoky oysters hot with fresh lemon wedges.

Grilled Spot Prawns with Chili-Lime Marinade

Spot prawns are a regional delicacy in the Pacific Northwest, and grilling them enhances their natural sweetness. This chili-lime marinade adds the perfect balance of tangy and spicy flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. spot prawns, shell-on
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest and juice of 2 limes
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lime zest and juice, chili powder, paprika, garlic powder, and salt.
  2. Toss the prawns in the marinade and chill for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Thread the prawns onto skewers.
  4. Grill the prawns for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque.
  5. Garnish with cilantro and serve immediately.

The Pacific Northwest’s seafood is an exceptional gift from its waters. Rooted in sustainability and freshness, these recipes embrace the region’s culinary heritage while offering modern flavors and techniques. A taste of the Pacific Northwest is truly unforgettable, and these dishes bring its charm directly to your kitchen. Whether you’re cooking for family, friends, or just for yourself, these six recipes guarantee a seafood meal to remember. Happy cooking!
