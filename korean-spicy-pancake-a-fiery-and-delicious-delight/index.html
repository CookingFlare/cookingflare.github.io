Korean Spicy Pancake: A Fiery and Delicious Delight


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or just looking to add some spicy flair to your cooking repertoire, then Korean Spicy Pancake, also known as “Pajeon,” is a must-try dish. Pajeon is a savory pancake filled with a variety of ingredients and seasoned with a spicy kick that will tantalize your taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making this fiery and delicious delight, from the essential ingredients to the step-by-step cooking process. So, grab your apron and let’s dive into the world of Korean Spicy Pancake!

Ingredients:

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup chopped scallions
  • 1 cup sliced kimchi
  • 1 cup small shrimp or squid (optional)
  • 1/2 cup sliced onions
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced carrots
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Dipping Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped scallions
  • 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Pancake Batter

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, water, egg, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Whisk the ingredients together until a smooth batter forms. Set the batter aside while you prepare the filling.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a separate bowl, mix the chopped scallions, sliced kimchi, small shrimp or squid (if using), sliced onions, and thinly sliced carrots. Season the filling with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 3: Combine the Batter and Filling

  1. Gently fold the prepared filling into the pancake batter until well combined.

Step 4: Cook the Pancake

  1. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add a generous amount of vegetable oil.
  2. Pour a ladleful of the pancake batter into the skillet, spreading it out evenly to form a round pancake.
  3. Cook the pancake for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.

Step 5: Make the Dipping Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame seeds, finely chopped scallions, garlic, and sugar. Set the dipping sauce aside for serving.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Once the pancake is cooked to perfection, transfer it to a cutting board and slice it into wedges.
  2. Serve the Korean Spicy Pancake with the prepared dipping sauce on the side.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For an extra spicy kick, add a teaspoon of Korean chili powder to the pancake batter.
  • Experiment with different fillings such as mushrooms, zucchini, or thinly sliced beef to customize your Korean Spicy Pancake.
  • Ensure the skillet is hot enough before pouring the batter to achieve a crispy texture.

Korean Spicy Pancake is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as an appetizer, snack, or even a main course. Its fiery flavor and crispy texture make it a standout dish that will impress your family and friends. So, why not add a touch of Korean spice to your next culinary adventure and savor the delightful flavors of Pajeon? Happy cooking!
