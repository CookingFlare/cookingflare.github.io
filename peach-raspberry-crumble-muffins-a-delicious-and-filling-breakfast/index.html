Peach Raspberry Crumble Muffins: A Delicious and Filling Breakfast


When it comes to breakfast, there’s nothing quite like the comfort of a warm, freshly baked muffin. These Peach Raspberry Crumble Muffins are not only delicious but also packed with the goodness of juicy fruits and a crunchy topping, making them the perfect way to start your day. Whether you’re enjoying them with a cup of coffee on a lazy Sunday morning or grabbing one on the go, these muffins will surely become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

For the Muffins:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups diced fresh peaches (about 2 medium peaches)
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries

For the Crumble Topping:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ⅓ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

Instructions

Preparing the Crumble Topping

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the flour, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and ground cinnamon.
  2. Add the cold, cubed butter into the mixture.
  3. Using a fork or pastry cutter, blend the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Set aside the crumble topping while you prepare the muffin batter.

Making the Muffin Batter

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners or grease the cups with cooking spray.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. This ensures an even distribution of the leavening agents and spices.
  3. In another bowl, beat together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, and eggs until well combined. The mixture should be slightly thick and pale.
  4. Gradually whisk in the buttermilk, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold together using a spatula. Be careful not to overmix; it’s okay if there are a few lumps remaining.
  6. Gently fold in the diced peaches and raspberries, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

Assembling and Baking the Muffins

  1. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about three-quarters full.
  2. Sprinkle the crumble topping generously over each muffin, pressing it lightly to adhere.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs attached.
  4. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tips for Perfect Muffins

  • Use Fresh Fruits: Fresh peaches and raspberries give these muffins their irresistible flavor and moist texture. If fresh fruits are not available, you can use frozen ones, but make sure to thaw and drain them well before adding to the batter.
  • Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure that your eggs, buttermilk, and melted butter are at room temperature. This helps in achieving a smooth batter and even baking.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the batter can result in tough muffins. Mix just until the dry ingredients are incorporated.
  • Crumble Topping: For an extra crunchy topping, you can add a handful of chopped nuts, such as pecans or walnuts, to the crumble mixture.

Variations and Substitutions

  • Vegan Option: Substitute eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water for each egg) and use a non-dairy milk like almond or soy milk in place of buttermilk. Replace butter with coconut oil or vegan margarine.
  • Gluten-Free: Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of regular flour. Ensure that the baking powder and other ingredients are certified gluten-free.
  • Different Fruits: Feel free to experiment with other fruits like blueberries, strawberries, or apples. Adjust the quantity to ensure the right balance of flavors and moisture.

Serving Suggestions

These Peach Raspberry Crumble Muffins are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven, but they also store well. Here are a few ideas on how to enjoy them:

  • Warm with Butter: Slice a warm muffin in half and spread a pat of butter on each side. The butter will melt into the muffin, adding richness and flavor.
  • With Yogurt: Serve a muffin alongside a bowl of Greek yogurt for a protein-packed breakfast. You can even crumble the muffin and sprinkle it over the yogurt for a delicious parfait.
  • On the Go: These muffins make an excellent grab-and-go breakfast or snack. Wrap them individually in plastic wrap or store in an airtight container for easy access throughout the week.

Storing and Freezing

  • At Room Temperature: Store the muffins in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. To keep them fresh longer, place a paper towel at the bottom of the container to absorb excess moisture.
  • In the Refrigerator: If you prefer to keep them longer, refrigerate the muffins for up to a week. Warm them in the microwave for about 20 seconds before serving to restore their soft texture.
  • Freezing: These muffins freeze beautifully. Place the cooled muffins in a single layer on a baking sheet and freeze until solid. Transfer them to a freezer-safe bag or container and store for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator or reheat in the microwave.

Nutritional Benefits

These Peach Raspberry Crumble Muffins are not only tasty but also offer some nutritional benefits:

  • Whole Wheat Flour: Incorporating whole wheat flour adds fiber, which is beneficial for digestion and helps keep you full longer.
  • Fresh Fruits: Peaches and raspberries are rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and dietary fiber. They contribute to overall health and provide natural sweetness.
  • Moderate Sugar Content: While these muffins do contain sugar, the amount is balanced with the natural sweetness of the fruits. You can also reduce the sugar slightly if you prefer a less sweet muffin.

Conclusion

Peach Raspberry Crumble Muffins are a delightful addition to any breakfast or brunch spread. Their combination of juicy peaches, tart raspberries, and a crunchy crumble topping makes them irresistibly delicious. With simple ingredients and easy preparation steps, these muffins are perfect for both novice and experienced bakers.

Whether you’re baking them for a special occasion or simply to enjoy during the week, these muffins are sure to bring a smile to your face and warmth to your kitchen. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and treat yourself to the comforting taste of homemade Peach Raspberry Crumble Muffins. Happy baking!
