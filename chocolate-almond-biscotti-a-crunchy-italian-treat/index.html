Chocolate Almond Biscotti: A Crunchy Italian Treat


If you’re a fan of Italian treats, then you’re in for a delightful journey with this Chocolate Almond Biscotti recipe. Biscotti, also known as cantuccini, are twice-baked cookies that originated in the city of Prato. These crunchy delights are perfect for dipping into a cup of coffee or enjoying on their own. The addition of rich chocolate and nutty almonds takes this classic biscotti to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole almonds, toasted and coarsely chopped
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.
  3. In a separate large bowl, beat the eggs, sugar, and vanilla extract until light and fluffy.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a stiff dough forms.
  5. Fold in the chopped almonds and chocolate chips until evenly distributed.

Step 2: Shaping the Biscotti

  1. Divide the dough in half. With lightly floured hands, shape each half into a log about 12 inches long and 2 inches wide. Place the logs on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them a few inches apart.
  2. Gently flatten the logs with the palm of your hand to about 1-inch thickness.

Step 3: First Bake

  1. Bake the logs in the preheated oven for 25 minutes, or until set. Remove from the oven and let cool for 5 minutes.
  2. Using a serrated knife, carefully slice the logs diagonally into 1-inch thick pieces.

Step 4: Second Bake

  1. Arrange the biscotti cut side down on the baking sheet and return them to the oven.
  2. Bake for an additional 10 minutes, then flip the biscotti over and bake for another 10 minutes until they are crisp.
  3. Transfer the biscotti to a wire rack and let them cool completely.

Step 5: Enjoy!

Once the biscotti have cooled, they are ready to be enjoyed. Store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two weeks. These delightful treats also make a great homemade gift for friends and family. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this Chocolate Almond Biscotti recipe is sure to impress and satisfy your sweet cravings.

Final Thoughts

Chocolate Almond Biscotti is a beloved Italian treat that combines the richness of chocolate with the crunch of almonds, creating a delightful symphony of flavors and textures. Whether you’re savoring them with a cup of coffee or indulging in them as a standalone treat, these biscotti are guaranteed to become a favorite in your baking repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a delicious journey to create these irresistible Chocolate Almond Biscotti!
