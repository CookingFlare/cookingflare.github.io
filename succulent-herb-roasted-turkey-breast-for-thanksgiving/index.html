Succulent Herb-Roasted Turkey Breast for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. If you’re looking to impress your loved ones with a mouthwatering dish this Thanksgiving, look no further than this succulent herb-roasted turkey breast recipe. Perfect for smaller gatherings or as a complement to a whole turkey, this dish is sure to be a hit at your holiday table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole bone-in turkey breast (about 6-8 pounds)
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh sage, finely chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 carrots, roughly chopped
  • 2 stalks of celery, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Rinse the turkey breast under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Place the turkey breast on a cutting board, and using a sharp knife, carefully remove any excess skin and fat. Tuck the wing tips under the breast and tie the legs together with kitchen twine for even cooking.

  2. Herb Rub: In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, and sage. Season the mixture with salt and black pepper to taste. Rub the herb mixture all over the turkey breast, making sure to coat it evenly. Squeeze the lemon halves over the turkey and place them in the cavity along with the quartered onion.

  3. Vegetables and Broth: Scatter the chopped carrots, celery, and any remaining onion around the turkey breast in a roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth and white wine (if using) into the pan, creating a flavorful base for the turkey.

  4. Roasting: Place the turkey breast in the preheated oven and roast for about 2 to 2 1/2 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) when measured with a meat thermometer. Baste the turkey breast with the pan juices every 30 minutes to ensure it stays moist and flavorful.

  5. Resting: Once the turkey breast reaches the desired temperature, carefully remove it from the oven and transfer it to a cutting board. Cover the turkey loosely with aluminum foil and let it rest for at least 20 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a juicier and more tender turkey.

  6. Carving and Serving: After the resting period, remove the kitchen twine and carve the turkey breast into thin slices. Arrange the slices on a serving platter, along with the roasted vegetables. Serve with your favorite Thanksgiving sides and enjoy!

Tips and Tricks:

  • For added flavor, consider brining the turkey breast in a solution of water, salt, sugar, and herbs for 12-24 hours before roasting.
  • If you prefer a crispy skin, you can increase the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C) during the last 30 minutes of roasting.
  • Use the leftover turkey breast to make sandwiches, salads, or hearty soups for delicious post-Thanksgiving meals.

With its aromatic herb rub and tender, juicy meat, this succulent herb-roasted turkey breast is a delightful centerpiece for your Thanksgiving feast. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or simply want to add variety to your holiday menu, this recipe is sure to please. Give it a try and savor the flavors of the season with this delectable dish. Happy Thanksgiving!
