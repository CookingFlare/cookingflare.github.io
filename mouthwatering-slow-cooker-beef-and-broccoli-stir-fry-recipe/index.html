Mouthwatering Slow Cooker Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious, easy-to-make meal that will have your family asking for seconds, look no further than this mouthwatering slow cooker beef and broccoli stir-fry recipe. This dish combines tender, juicy beef with crisp broccoli and a savory sauce, creating a delightful harmony of flavors. Perfect for busy weeknights, this recipe allows you to set it and forget it, making dinner time a breeze. Let’s dive into the ingredients, preparation, and some tips to elevate your stir-fry game!

Ingredients

For the Beef and Broccoli

  • 1.5 pounds of flank steak: Flank steak is ideal for stir-frying as it becomes incredibly tender when cooked slowly.
  • 4 cups of broccoli florets: Fresh broccoli is best, but you can use frozen if you’re in a pinch.
  • 1 medium onion: Thinly sliced. It adds a nice sweetness to the dish.
  • 2 cloves of garlic: Minced for that aromatic flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger: Minced or grated, ginger adds a wonderful zing to the dish.

For the Marinade and Sauce

  • 1/3 cup of soy sauce: A key ingredient that brings depth and umami to the dish.
  • 1/4 cup of beef broth: This will enhance the flavors and keep the beef moist.
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar: Adds a touch of sweetness to balance the savory elements.
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch: Used to thicken the sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil: Provides a nutty flavor that complements the beef beautifully.
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper: For a bit of heat.
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar: Gives a nice tang and helps to tenderize the meat.

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

  1. Slice the Flank Steak: Begin by slicing the flank steak against the grain into thin strips. This will ensure that the beef remains tender after cooking. Aim for slices about 1/4 inch thick.

  2. Marinate the Beef: In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, beef broth, brown sugar, sesame oil, black pepper, and rice vinegar. Add the sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well coated. Cover and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to 4 hours in the fridge for maximum flavor.

Step 2: Prepare the Slow Cooker

  1. Layer the Ingredients: Place the sliced onions at the bottom of the slow cooker. This acts as a bed for the beef and prevents it from sticking to the bottom.

  2. Add the Beef: Pour the marinated beef along with the marinade over the onions. Spread it out evenly.

  3. Add the Garlic and Ginger: Sprinkle the minced garlic and ginger over the beef. These aromatics will infuse the dish with incredible flavor.

Step 3: Cooking Time

  1. Set Your Slow Cooker: Cover the slow cooker and set it to low heat. Let the beef cook for about 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours. The longer you cook it, the more tender the beef will become.

Step 4: Add the Broccoli

  1. In the Last 30 Minutes: About 30 minutes before serving, add the broccoli florets to the slow cooker. Stir gently to combine, ensuring the broccoli is coated in the sauce. Cover again and continue cooking until the broccoli is bright green and tender, about 20-30 minutes.

Step 5: Thicken the Sauce

  1. Create a Slurry: In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a few tablespoons of cold water until smooth.

  2. Thicken the Sauce: About 10 minutes before serving, stir the cornstarch slurry into the slow cooker. This will thicken the sauce and give it a luscious texture. Stir gently to combine and allow it to cook for the remaining time.

Serving Suggestions

Once your slow cooker beef and broccoli stir-fry is ready, it’s time to serve! Here are some ideas to elevate your meal:

  • Serve Over Rice: A classic choice, serve the beef and broccoli over fluffy white rice or brown rice. The rice will soak up the delicious sauce.

  • Pair with Noodles: For a twist, serve it over cooked rice noodles or lo mein for a hearty meal.

  • Top with Sesame Seeds: Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds on top for added crunch and flavor.

  • Garnish with Green Onions: Chopped green onions add a fresh touch and a pop of color to your dish.

Tips for the Best Slow Cooker Beef and Broccoli

  1. Sear the Beef First: For added depth of flavor, consider searing the beef in a skillet before adding it to the slow cooker. This step caramelizes the meat and enhances its taste.

  2. Fresh Ingredients: Using fresh broccoli and high-quality beef will make a noticeable difference in the final dish.

  3. Adjust the Sauce: Feel free to tweak the sauce ingredients to your taste. If you prefer a spicier kick, add red pepper flakes or a splash of chili oil.

  4. Make It Ahead: This dish can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Just reheat gently on the stove or in the microwave.

  5. Freezing Leftovers: If you have leftovers, they freeze well! Store in an airtight container for up to 3 months. Just thaw and reheat when you’re ready to enjoy it again.

Nutritional Information

This slow cooker beef and broccoli stir-fry is not only delicious but also offers some nutritional benefits:

  • Protein: Beef is a great source of protein, essential for muscle repair and growth.
  • Vitamins: Broccoli is packed with vitamins C and K, as well as fiber, which is beneficial for digestion.
  • Low in Carbs: When served without rice or noodles, this dish can be a low-carb option, making it suitable for various dietary needs.

Conclusion

This mouthwatering slow cooker beef and broccoli stir-fry recipe is perfect for any night of the week. With its tender beef, vibrant broccoli, and savory sauce, it’s sure to become a family favorite. The slow cooking process not only enhances the flavors but also allows you to spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying your meal with loved ones. So grab your slow cooker, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor a delicious homemade dinner that’s both easy and satisfying!
