Turn Up the Heat: DIY Chinese Chili Sauce Recipes for Extra Kick


Are you ready to spice up your dishes and transport your taste buds to new heights? Just as music can ignite a party, Chinese chili sauces have the incredible power to bring your meals alive with a burst of flavor and heat. Discover the secrets of Chinese culinary mastery with these DIY Chinese chili sauce recipes, perfect for adding that infamous kick to your favorite dishes.

  1. Fermented Bean Paste Chili Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fermented broad beans (douchi)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 Thai bird’s eye chilies, deseeded and minced
  • 1 tablespoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a wok or skillet, gently fry the Sichuan peppercorns over medium heat for 3-4 minutes until fragrant.

  2. Add the vegetable oil and heat until smoking. Lower the heat and carefully add the douchi and garlic. Fry until the douchi begins to caramelize, stirring constantly.

  3. Add the chilies and continue frying for another minute. Strain the mixture and reserve the oil.

  4. In a mini food processor or mortar and pestle, grind the fried ingredients to a paste.

  5. Return the paste to the reserved oil, and stir-fry for a minute. Add water and simmer on low heat for 5 minutes. Taste and adjust the spiciness as desired.

  6. Allow to cool before transferring to a sealable jar. Store in the refrigerator for up to a month.

  7. Hua Jiao Chili Oil

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups peanut oil
  • 1/4 cup Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 cup dried red chilies, stemmed and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons crushed Sichuan pickled mustard greens (zha cai) (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon whole Sichuan peppercorns

Instructions:

  1. In a wok or saucepan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add both the whole and crushed Sichuan peppercorns and stir-fry until fragrant and slightly browned.

  2. Add the dried chilies and zha cai (if using) and fry until the chilies have darkened and the oil turns red.

  3. Strain the oil through a fine-mesh sieve to separate the solids from the liquid.

  4. Allow the oil to cool before transferring it to a jar. The Hua Jiao Chili Oil will keep for up to 3 months in the refrigerator.

  5. Hot Pot Chili Oil

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups vegetable oil
  • 1 cup Sichuan Pickled Chilies (Dieda)
  • 2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 cup fermented soybean paste (miso paste can be substituted)
  • 1 tablespoon wheat flour

Instructions:

  1. In a small pot, heat the oil over low heat. Cook until it smokes, add the Sichuan peppercorns, turn off the heat, and let the oil cool for a few minutes.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the fermented soybean paste and wheat flour. Slowly whisk 1 cup of the hot oil into the mixture, followed by the remaining oil.
  3. In a saucepan, bring the oil mixture to a low simmer. Add the pickled chilies (dieda).
  4. Reduce heat and stir gently for about 10 minutes as the mixture thickens.
  5. Taste and adjust the spiciness as desired. Strain the developed Hot Pot Chili Oil into a sealable jar. Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 months.

Now that you have concocted your homemade Chinese chili sauces, the world of spicy Chinese cooking awaits. Try these DIY creations with your favorite dishes and enjoy the sultry heat they bring. Don’t be afraid to experiment and adapt these recipes to your taste – the essence of Chinese cooking is all about seasoning to your heart’s delight. Happy cooking, and may your meals be ignited by the siren call of chili!
