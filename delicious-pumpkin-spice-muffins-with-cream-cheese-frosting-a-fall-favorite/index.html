Delicious Pumpkin Spice Muffins with Cream Cheese Frosting: A Fall Favorite


Fall is the season for cozy sweaters, colorful leaves, and of course, delicious pumpkin treats. One of the most beloved fall favorites is the pumpkin spice muffin with creamy, tangy cream cheese frosting. These muffins are the epitome of comfort and warmth, with their aromatic spices and moist, tender crumb. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to create these delectable pumpkin spice muffins from scratch, along with a luscious cream cheese frosting that will elevate them to a whole new level of indulgence.

Ingredients

For the Pumpkin Spice Muffins:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/3 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Pumpkin Spice Muffins:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and ginger.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vegetable oil until well combined. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the pumpkin puree, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  7. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely before frosting.

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  1. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and beat until light and fluffy.
  3. Once the muffins are completely cooled, generously frost them with the cream cheese frosting.

Tips and Variations

  • For a nutty twist, add 1/2 cup of chopped pecans or walnuts to the muffin batter.
  • Sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon or pumpkin spice on top of the cream cheese frosting for an extra pop of flavor.
  • If you prefer a lighter frosting, reduce the amount of powdered sugar to your liking.

Serving and Storing

These pumpkin spice muffins with cream cheese frosting are best enjoyed fresh, but they can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Bring them to room temperature before serving for the best taste and texture.

Whether you’re hosting a fall brunch, celebrating a special occasion, or simply craving a cozy treat, these pumpkin spice muffins with cream cheese frosting are sure to delight your taste buds and warm your soul. Embrace the flavors of the season with this irresistible fall favorite!
