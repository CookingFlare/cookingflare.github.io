Cooking for Two: 5 Easy and Flavorful Risotto Recipes


Cooking for two can be one of the most enjoyable experiences in the kitchen. You can experiment with flavors, ingredients, and textures while keeping things manageable and personalized. If you’re in the mood for a comforting and satisfying dish, risotto is an excellent choice. This creamy, flavorful dish is easy to make and can be customized to suit a wide variety of tastes. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, risotto is perfect for a romantic dinner, a cozy night in, or simply a quick yet impressive meal.

In this post, we’re going to share five delicious risotto recipes that are perfect for two. These recipes are not only full of flavor but also easy to prepare, allowing you to create an unforgettable meal without spending hours in the kitchen. Let’s dive into the world of creamy risotto!

1. Classic Parmesan Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a small saucepan, heat the chicken or vegetable broth over low heat, keeping it warm throughout the process.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: In a large pan, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Toast the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the pan and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice is lightly toasted and opaque around the edges.

  4. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine and stir until it has mostly absorbed into the rice.

  5. Add the Broth: Begin adding the warm broth one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly. Wait until the liquid is absorbed before adding more broth. Continue this process until the rice is tender and creamy, about 18-20 minutes.

  6. Finish the Risotto: Once the rice is cooked, stir in the remaining butter and grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Serve: Spoon the risotto into two bowls, garnish with fresh parsley, and serve immediately.

2. Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1 1/2 cups mixed mushrooms (e.g., cremini, shiitake, button), sliced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Mushrooms: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until golden brown and tender, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Toast the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and cook, stirring frequently, for 1-2 minutes.

  4. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine and stir until the liquid has mostly evaporated.

  5. Add the Broth: Gradually add the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Wait for the liquid to be absorbed before adding more broth. Continue until the rice is creamy and al dente, about 18 minutes.

  6. Combine the Ingredients: Stir in the cooked mushrooms, Parmesan cheese, and fresh thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Serve: Spoon the risotto into two bowls and serve hot, garnished with extra thyme if desired.

3. Lemon and Asparagus Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Asparagus: Bring a small pot of water to a boil. Add the asparagus pieces and blanch them for 2-3 minutes, until bright green and tender. Drain and set aside.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.

  3. Toast the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the pan and cook, stirring, for 1-2 minutes until the rice is lightly toasted.

  4. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine, stirring until it’s mostly absorbed.

  5. Add the Broth: Add the warm broth one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing the liquid to absorb before adding more. Continue until the rice is creamy and tender, about 18 minutes.

  6. Combine the Ingredients: Stir in the blanched asparagus, lemon zest, lemon juice, and grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.

  7. Serve: Spoon the risotto into two bowls, and garnish with extra lemon zest if desired.

4. Shrimp and Garlic Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1/2 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups seafood broth, kept warm
  • 1/4 cup freshly chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Shrimp: In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: In the same skillet, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Toast the Rice: Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing the rice to lightly toast.

  4. Deglaze with Wine: Add the white wine and stir until mostly absorbed.

  5. Add the Broth: Gradually add the warm seafood broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Allow the liquid to absorb before adding more. Continue until the rice is creamy and tender, about 18-20 minutes.

  6. Finish the Dish: Stir in the cooked shrimp, chopped parsley, and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Serve: Serve the risotto hot, garnished with extra parsley or Parmesan if desired.

5. Butternut Squash Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 2 cups roasted butternut squash (peeled and cubed)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Butternut Squash: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast for 25-30 minutes until soft and caramelized.

  2. Cook the Aromatics: In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Toast the Rice: Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, until the rice is lightly toasted.

  4. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine and stir until the wine has mostly evaporated.

  5. Add the Broth: Add the warm vegetable broth one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly. Wait for the liquid to absorb before adding more. Continue until the rice is creamy and al dente, about 18 minutes.

  6. Finish the Dish: Stir in the roasted butternut squash, ground sage, and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.

  7. Serve: Spoon the risotto into two bowls, and garnish with additional Parmesan cheese if desired.

Conclusion

Risotto is a versatile and delicious dish that is perfect for cooking for two. These five recipes offer a variety of flavors and ingredients, from the classic Parmesan risotto to the savory mushroom and shrimp variations. Whether you prefer the freshness of lemon and asparagus or the warmth of butternut squash, there’s a risotto recipe here that’s sure to impress.

The key to a perfect risotto is patience. Stirring the rice constantly and adding the broth slowly allows the rice to release its natural starches, creating that signature creamy texture. With these recipes, you can enjoy a comforting, restaurant-quality dish in the comfort of your own home. Enjoy the process of cooking, and more importantly, enjoy the delicious results!
