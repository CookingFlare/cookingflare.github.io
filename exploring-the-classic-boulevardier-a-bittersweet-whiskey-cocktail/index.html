Exploring the Classic Boulevardier: A Bittersweet Whiskey Cocktail


The Boulevardier is a classic cocktail that combines the rich flavors of whiskey with the bittersweet notes of Campari and sweet vermouth. It’s a sophisticated and timeless drink that has been enjoyed by cocktail enthusiasts for decades. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of the Boulevardier and explore its history, ingredients, and the art of making this bittersweet whiskey cocktail.

The Origins of the Boulevardier

The Boulevardier cocktail is believed to have originated in the 1920s, during the Prohibition era in the United States. It was created by an American expatriate named Erskine Gwynne, who published a literary magazine called “The Boulevardier” in Paris. Inspired by the vibrant cocktail culture of the city, Gwynne sought to create a drink that would captivate the palates of his sophisticated readers.

The Ingredients

To make a classic Boulevardier, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 ½ ounces of bourbon whiskey
  • 1 ounce of Campari
  • 1 ounce of sweet vermouth
  • Orange twist or cherry (for garnish)

Mixing the Boulevardier

  1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.
  2. Add the bourbon whiskey, Campari, and sweet vermouth to the mixing glass.
  3. Stir gently for about 20-30 seconds to combine the ingredients and chill the drink.
  4. Strain the mixture into a chilled coupe or old-fashioned glass.
  5. Garnish with an orange twist or a cherry.

The Art of Tasting

The Boulevardier is a complex cocktail that offers a delightful balance of flavors. When sipping this drink, take a moment to appreciate the interplay between the robustness of the bourbon, the bitterness of the Campari, and the sweetness of the vermouth. Allow the flavors to unfold on your palate, and notice how they evolve with each sip.

Variations and Customizations

While the classic Boulevardier recipe is a tried-and-true favorite, there are endless possibilities for customization. Here are a few variations you can explore:

  • The Smoky Boulevardier: Substitute the bourbon with a smoky Islay Scotch whisky for a unique twist on the original recipe.
  • The Spiced Boulevardier: Add a dash of aromatic bitters or muddle some fresh herbs, such as rosemary or thyme, to infuse the drink with additional flavors.
  • The Sparkling Boulevardier: Top off your cocktail with a splash of sparkling wine or club soda for a refreshing effervescence.

Feel free to experiment with different ratios of the ingredients to suit your personal taste preferences. The joy of mixology lies in the ability to create something uniquely yours.

The Perfect Pairings

The Boulevardier is a versatile cocktail that pairs well with a variety of dishes. Its bittersweet profile makes it an excellent companion for rich and savory foods. Consider serving the Boulevardier alongside:

  • A charcuterie board with cured meats and aged cheeses
  • Grilled steaks or roasted meats
  • Dark chocolate desserts

The complex flavors of the Boulevardier will complement and elevate these dishes, creating a harmonious dining experience.

Final Thoughts

The Boulevardier is more than just a cocktail; it’s a testament to the art of mixology and the timeless appeal of classic drinks. With its bittersweet notes and smooth finish, this whiskey cocktail is sure to captivate your senses and leave you craving more. So, gather your ingredients, master the art of mixing, and embark on a journey of exploring the classic Boulevardier. Cheers!
