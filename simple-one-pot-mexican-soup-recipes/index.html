Simple One-Pot Mexican Soup Recipes


If you’re looking for hearty, flavorful, and easy-to-make meals, one-pot Mexican soups are a great option to consider. These soups are not only packed with vibrant flavors and spices, but they also offer the convenience of minimal cleanup. Whether you’re preparing a meal for a busy weeknight or hosting a gathering with family and friends, these simple one-pot Mexican soup recipes are sure to impress.

In this post, we’ll dive into some delicious and easy-to-follow recipes that bring the bold, comforting flavors of Mexican cuisine straight to your kitchen. From traditional options like Tortilla Soup and Pozole to unique twists like Chicken and Corn Soup, you’ll find something for every taste preference.

Why Choose One-Pot Soups?

One-pot meals are incredibly practical, especially for busy individuals or families. When it comes to Mexican soups, the beauty lies in the fact that you can combine a variety of fresh ingredients in a single pot, letting the flavors meld together without much effort. There are several benefits to making these soups, such as:

  • Minimal Cleanup: Cooking everything in one pot means less washing up afterward.
  • Full of Flavor: Cooking everything together allows the ingredients to release their flavors into the broth, creating a more harmonious dish.
  • Customizable: You can easily adjust ingredients based on your preferences or what you have on hand.
  • Comforting: The combination of broth, spices, and hearty ingredients in Mexican soups provides both warmth and nourishment.

So, let’s get started with some of the best and easiest one-pot Mexican soups that you can make!

1. Classic Mexican Tortilla Soup

A staple in Mexican cuisine, tortilla soup is rich, comforting, and packed with bold flavors. With its combination of tender chicken, crispy tortilla strips, and flavorful broth, this is the ultimate comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (4 oz) diced green chilies
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded (rotisserie chicken works well)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1-2 corn tortillas, cut into strips
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Sour cream, for topping
  • Shredded cheese, for topping

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Soup Base: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for about 5 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Broth: Stir in the diced tomatoes (with juices), green chilies, chicken broth, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to combine.

  3. Add the Chicken and Corn: Stir in the shredded chicken and corn, allowing the soup to simmer for another 10 minutes.

  4. Crisp the Tortillas: While the soup is simmering, heat a small amount of oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the tortilla strips and fry until crispy and golden, about 3-4 minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  5. Finish the Soup: Stir in the lime juice, adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper, if needed.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls, topping with crispy tortilla strips, fresh cilantro, a dollop of sour cream, and shredded cheese.

2. Chicken and Corn Soup

This comforting chicken and corn soup has a creamy base and is perfect for those chilly evenings when you need something filling and hearty. The combination of tender chicken and sweet corn in a savory broth is both satisfying and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and carrot. Cook for about 5-6 minutes until the vegetables soften.

  2. Add Corn and Chicken: Stir in the corn kernels and shredded chicken. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

  3. Pour in Broth and Cream: Add the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Add Cream and Seasonings: Stir in the heavy cream, ground cumin, and smoked paprika. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

  5. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve with warm tortillas or crusty bread on the side.

3. Pozole: A Mexican Classic

Pozole is a traditional Mexican soup made with hominy (dried corn kernels treated with lime) and can be prepared with either pork, chicken, or even a vegetarian version. This dish is often served during special occasions and celebrations, but it’s easy to make at home for a filling and delicious meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lb pork shoulder, cut into chunks (or chicken thighs)
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) hominy, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 2 dried ancho chilies, seeds removed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh lime wedges, for serving
  • Sliced radishes, for garnish
  • Shredded lettuce, for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chilies: In a small skillet, toast the ancho chilies over medium heat for about 2 minutes until fragrant. Remove from heat and soak the chilies in hot water for about 10 minutes. Once softened, blend them with a little bit of the soaking water until smooth.

  2. Cook the Pork (or Chicken): In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the pork (or chicken) and brown on all sides, about 7-8 minutes.

  3. Add the Broth and Hominy: Pour in the chicken broth, hominy, and the blended ancho chili mixture. Stir in the cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender.

  4. Finish and Serve: Once the meat is tender, adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve the pozole hot, garnished with sliced radishes, shredded lettuce, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges on the side.

4. Spicy Mexican Black Bean Soup

This black bean soup is a rich and hearty dish, perfect for anyone who loves a little heat in their meal. It’s simple, delicious, and packs a punch of flavor, with earthy black beans, aromatic spices, and a touch of smoky heat from the chipotle.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chipotle chili powder
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Crumbled tortilla chips, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking for 5 minutes until soft and fragrant.

  2. Add the Beans and Spices: Stir in the black beans, vegetable broth, ground cumin, chipotle chili powder, and diced tomatoes. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop.

  3. Blend the Soup: Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth, or for a chunkier texture, blend just half of the soup.

  4. Season and Serve: Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro, crumbled tortilla chips, and lime wedges.

Conclusion

One-pot Mexican soups are the perfect way to enjoy flavorful, comforting meals with minimal effort. Whether you’re making a classic tortilla soup, a creamy chicken and corn soup, or a hearty pozole, these recipes are sure to become your go-to dishes. With their rich flavors, vibrant ingredients, and easy cleanup, these soups are perfect for any occasion.

So, next time you’re in the mood for something comforting and flavorful, give these simple one-pot Mexican soup recipes a try. They’re not only delicious but also bring the warmth and spice of Mexico right into your home.
