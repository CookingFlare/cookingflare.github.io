Nepalese Momos and Spicy Curries


Welcome to our culinary journey through the vibrant flavors of Nepal! In this blog post, we will explore the delightful world of Nepalese Momos and Spicy Curries. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds with a fusion of spices, textures, and aromas that will transport you to the beautiful Himalayan nation.

The Art of Making Momos

Momos, a popular street food in Nepal, are a type of dumpling that originated in Tibet but have since become an integral part of Nepalese cuisine. These bite-sized delights are made by stuffing a thin dough wrapper with a savory filling, which can vary from vegetables to meat.

Ingredients

To make the perfect momos, you will need:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of minced meat (chicken, pork, or beef)
  • 1 cup of finely chopped vegetables (cabbage, carrots, and onions)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, water, and salt. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  2. In a separate bowl, mix the minced meat, chopped vegetables, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, vegetable oil, salt, and pepper. Ensure the filling is well combined.

  3. Take a small portion of the dough and roll it into a thin circle using a rolling pin. Place a spoonful of the filling in the center of the circle.

  4. Fold the edges of the dough towards the center, creating pleats along the way. Pinch the top to seal the momo.

  5. Repeat the process until all the dough and filling are used.

  6. Steam the momos in a steamer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the dough is cooked and the filling is tender.

  7. Serve hot with a spicy tomato chutney or a tangy dipping sauce of your choice.

Exploring Spicy Curries

In addition to momos, Nepalese cuisine boasts a wide variety of aromatic and spicy curries that are sure to awaken your senses. From hearty lentil curries to fiery chicken masalas, the flavors are as diverse as the country’s landscape.

Chicken Masala

One of the most beloved curries in Nepal is the Chicken Masala. This dish combines succulent pieces of chicken with a fragrant blend of spices, creating a rich and flavorful curry.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan and add the cumin seeds. Let them splutter for a few seconds.

  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.

  3. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for another minute.

  4. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Stir well to coat the onions with the spices.

  5. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.

  6. Add the chicken pieces and season with salt. Cook on medium heat until the chicken is tender and cooked through.

  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with steamed rice or naan bread.
