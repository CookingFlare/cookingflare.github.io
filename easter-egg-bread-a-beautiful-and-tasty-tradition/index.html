Easter Egg Bread: A Beautiful and Tasty Tradition


Easter is a time for celebration, and what better way to mark the occasion than with a beautiful and delicious Easter Egg Bread? This tradition has been passed down through generations, and it’s not only a feast for the taste buds but also a stunning centerpiece for any Easter table. In this blog post, I’ll share with you the history of Easter Egg Bread, its significance, and of course, a delightful recipe for you to try at home.

The History and Significance

Easter Egg Bread, also known as “tsoureki” in Greek, “cozonac” in Romanian, and “kulich” in Russian, has its roots deeply embedded in the customs of Eastern European and Mediterranean cultures. It’s typically made during Easter, symbolizing the end of Lent and the celebration of Christ’s resurrection. The bread’s braided shape represents the Holy Trinity, and the eggs nestled within the dough symbolize new life and rebirth.

The process of making Easter Egg Bread is a labor of love, often involving the entire family. From kneading the dough to carefully braiding it and decorating it with colorful eggs, every step is a meaningful tradition that is cherished and passed down from one generation to the next.

The Recipe

Now, let’s dive into the delightful recipe for Easter Egg Bread. This recipe will yield a beautifully golden loaf with a soft, slightly sweet interior, and a glossy, egg-adorned crust.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 eggs, plus 1 for egg wash
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground mahleb (optional, for a unique flavor)
  • 5-6 dyed Easter eggs
  • Optional: sesame seeds for sprinkling

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm milk and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and mahleb.
  3. Add the melted butter, eggs, vanilla extract, and orange zest to the yeast mixture. Stir to combine.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
  7. Punch down the dough and divide it into three equal portions. Roll each portion into a long rope, then braid the ropes together.
  8. Place the braid on a parchment-lined baking sheet, tucking the dyed Easter eggs into the braids as you go.
  9. Cover the braid with a kitchen towel and let it rise for an additional 30-45 minutes.
  10. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Beat the remaining egg and brush it over the risen bread. Sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired.
  11. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
  12. Allow the bread to cool before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

Easter Egg Bread is delightful on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it with flavored butter, jam, or honey. It also makes a wonderful addition to a brunch spread, alongside fresh fruit and cheeses.

In Conclusion

Easter Egg Bread is not just a recipe; it’s a tradition that brings families together and adds a touch of beauty and flavor to the Easter festivities. Whether you’re continuing a long-standing tradition or starting a new one, the process of making this bread is as rewarding as the taste itself. So, gather your loved ones, roll up your sleeves, and create a beautiful and tasty Easter Egg Bread to share and enjoy. Happy baking!
