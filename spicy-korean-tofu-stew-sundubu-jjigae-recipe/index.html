Spicy Korean Tofu Stew (Sundubu Jjigae) Recipe


Sundubu Jjigae, a popular Korean dish, is a spicy tofu stew that’s rich, hearty, and packed with bold flavors. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or trying it for the first time, Sundubu Jjigae is a perfect introduction to the vibrant and satisfying world of Korean stews. This dish features silky soft tofu, savory broth, and a complex combination of spices, vegetables, and proteins, making it a comforting and satisfying meal.

In this post, we’ll guide you through every step of making Sundubu Jjigae at home, from preparing the ingredients to achieving the ideal level of heat and flavor balance. So, let’s dive into how you can prepare this comforting stew in your kitchen!

What is Sundubu Jjigae?

Sundubu Jjigae, or Korean Spicy Tofu Stew, is a comforting dish traditionally served with rice. It’s made with uncurdled, soft tofu known as “sundubu” and typically features a spicy broth seasoned with gochujang (Korean chili paste) and gochugaru (Korean chili flakes). What makes Sundubu Jjigae stand out is its versatility in terms of the ingredients used. You can add vegetables, seafood, beef, or even pork, making it a flexible dish suited to all tastes.

The hallmark of a great Sundubu Jjigae is the balance of flavors – from the umami-rich broth to the spicy kick of the chili paste and the mild, delicate taste of the tofu. This stew is best served bubbling hot in a stone pot (dolsot), which keeps it sizzling right until the last bite.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create a delicious bowl of Sundubu Jjigae at home, here’s a list of ingredients you’ll need:

For the Broth:

  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)

For the Protein:

  • 1/2 pound of protein (you can use pork, beef, or seafood like shrimp or clams)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for cooking the protein)

For the Tofu:

  • 1 block (about 14 oz) of soft or silken tofu (sundubu)

Additional Ingredients:

  • 1/2 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms (shiitake or button mushrooms), sliced
  • 1 small green chili pepper, sliced (optional for extra heat)
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green onions for garnish
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Preparing the Broth

The base of Sundubu Jjigae is its flavorful broth. Start by heating the sesame oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic, sautéing them for about 2-3 minutes, or until they become fragrant and slightly softened.

Next, add the gochujang (Korean chili paste) and gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) to the pot. Stir the spices in the oil until they become fragrant and the oil turns a deep red color. This step is crucial, as the gochujang provides a savory base with a kick of heat, while the gochugaru adds depth and flavor.

Once the chili paste and flakes are well incorporated, add the vegetable or chicken broth to the pot. Bring the broth to a simmer and cook for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Add soy sauce for additional umami and a touch of salt to enhance the overall flavor profile. If you’d like a hint of sweetness to balance the heat, add a teaspoon of sugar (optional).

Adding the Protein

While the broth simmers, it’s time to prepare the protein you’ve chosen. Traditionally, Sundubu Jjigae can include pork, beef, or seafood. If you’re using seafood, shrimp or clams work wonderfully as they absorb the flavors of the broth.

In a separate pan, heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium-high heat. If you’re using pork or beef, slice the meat thinly, and quickly stir-fry it in the pan until it’s browned and cooked through. For shrimp or clams, simply cook them until they turn pink or open up (for clams). Once the protein is ready, set it aside.

Preparing the Tofu and Vegetables

Now, it’s time to prepare the tofu. Gently cut the soft tofu into chunks, as it’s delicate and can break easily. The tofu is the star of this dish, so treat it with care. Once the tofu is cut, set it aside.

Next, slice the zucchini and mushrooms. These vegetables add texture and a mild flavor that complements the heat from the broth. Add the zucchini and mushrooms to the simmering broth, cooking for another 5 minutes until they are tender. You can also add a sliced green chili pepper at this point if you prefer an extra kick of heat.

Bringing Everything Together

Once the vegetables are tender and the broth has reached the right flavor balance, gently add the tofu to the pot. Stir gently, being careful not to break up the tofu. Let the tofu warm up in the broth for 3-4 minutes, allowing it to absorb the spicy, flavorful broth.

At this stage, you can crack an egg into the stew for added richness and texture. Simply create a little well in the center of the stew and crack the egg into it. Cover the pot and allow the egg to poach in the bubbling broth for a few minutes until it’s cooked to your liking.

Serving the Sundubu Jjigae

Once your Sundubu Jjigae is ready, serve it hot, ideally in a traditional stone pot (dolsot) to keep it bubbling. Garnish with freshly chopped green onions for a burst of freshness and color. This dish is best enjoyed with a bowl of steaming white rice to balance out the heat.

Sundubu Jjigae can be served as a stand-alone meal or as part of a Korean feast with other side dishes (banchan). The combination of spicy broth, tender tofu, vegetables, and protein makes it a comforting and satisfying dish, especially on a cold day.

Tips for Making the Perfect Sundubu Jjigae

  1. Choose the Right Tofu: The type of tofu you use will greatly affect the texture of the stew. Silken or soft tofu (sundubu) is the best choice, as it gives the stew a creamy, delicate texture. Avoid using firm tofu, as it won’t absorb the broth in the same way.

  2. Adjust the Spice Level: The spice level of Sundubu Jjigae is easily adjustable. If you prefer a milder stew, use less gochujang and gochugaru. You can also reduce the number of chili peppers or remove them entirely if you’re not a fan of too much heat.

  3. Customize with Protein: While pork and beef are the most traditional proteins used in Sundubu Jjigae, you can also make it with seafood like shrimp or clams for a lighter variation. Tofu can be the only protein in a vegetarian version.

  4. Use Fresh Vegetables: Fresh zucchini and mushrooms bring a great texture and flavor to the dish. While it’s common to add other vegetables like onions or spinach, feel free to get creative based on what you have on hand.

  5. Don’t Overcook the Tofu: Tofu is delicate, so avoid overcooking it. Once it’s added to the broth, allow it to warm up, but don’t let it cook for too long as it can break apart.

  6. Serve Immediately: Sundubu Jjigae is best enjoyed immediately after it’s prepared, while the stew is still hot and bubbling. If you’re serving it in a stone pot, it will stay sizzling for longer.

Conclusion

Sundubu Jjigae is a delicious and hearty Korean dish that’s perfect for any occasion. The combination of soft tofu, flavorful broth, and spicy seasonings makes it a dish that warms the soul and satisfies the taste buds. Whether you’re making it for a weeknight dinner or sharing it with friends and family, this dish is sure to become a favorite. With a few simple ingredients and a bit of care, you can create an authentic bowl of Sundubu Jjigae in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
