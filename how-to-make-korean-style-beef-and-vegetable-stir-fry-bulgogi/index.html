How to Make Korean Style Beef and Vegetable Stir-Fry (Bulgogi)


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and enticing aromas, with bulgogi standing as one of its most beloved dishes. Bulgogi, which translates to “fire meat,” is a flavorful, tender beef dish marinated to perfection and accompanied by an array of fresh vegetables. This Korean-style beef and vegetable stir-fry is not only easy to make but also delivers a healthy, balanced, and mouthwatering meal that’s packed with every bite. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned enthusiast, this recipe is a beautiful way to bring the flavors of Korea to your kitchen.

Ingredients for Korean Bulgogi Stir-Fry

Creating a delicious bulgogi doesn’t require complex or hard-to-find ingredients, making it an approachable meal for everyday cooking. Below is the list of essentials you’ll need:

For the Beef:

  • 1 lb (450 grams) of thinly sliced beef sirloin or ribeye: Look for cuts that are tender and well-marbled for the best flavor. If your beef isn’t pre-sliced, freeze it slightly to make thin slicing easier.
  • 2-3 tablespoons of soy sauce: This gives the marinade a savory, umami-filled foundation.
  • 1 tablespoon of white or brown sugar: Adds balance to the salty and tangy flavors.
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil: For that nutty, aromatic finish.
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine or mirin: Enhances depth while tenderizing the meat.
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced): A key flavor component in Korean cooking.
  • ½ teaspoon grated ginger: Adds warmth and a touch of spice.
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper: For seasoning.
  • 1 tablespoon of pear puree or grated Asian pear: Helps tenderize the meat and provides natural sweetness.

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil: Use a neutral oil like canola or sunflower for cooking.
  • 1 medium onion (sliced thinly): Adds natural sweetness to the dish.
  • 2-3 carrots (julienned): For crunch and vibrant color.
  • 1 bell pepper (sliced thinly): Choose red, yellow, or orange for sweetness and brightness.
  • 1 small zucchini (cut into thin slices): Balances both texture and fresh flavors.
  • 1 cup fresh mushrooms (sliced): Shiitake is the traditional choice, but button or cremini works well too.
  • 2 scallions (sliced): Garnish that brings a fresh oniony lift.
  • Sesame seeds: For a crunchy garnish and nutty flavor.
  • Optional: Spinach or cabbage (lightly wilted): Adds a healthy, leafy green element.

Preparing Your Bulgogi Marinade

The hallmark of bulgogi lies in its perfectly balanced marinade. Follow these steps to craft a marinade that transforms ordinary beef into tender, flavorful perfection:

  1. Combine the base ingredients: In a medium-sized bowl, mix the soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, rice wine (or mirin), minced garlic, grated ginger, black pepper, and pear puree. Whisk until the sugar is fully dissolved and the ingredients are evenly distributed.
  2. Marinate the beef: Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring each piece is coated thoroughly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or transfer to an airtight container. Let the beef marinate for at least 30 minutes (longer is better—2 hours or overnight will yield deeper flavors).
  3. Optional shortcuts: If you’re pressed for time, massage the marinade into the beef with your hands to speed up absorption.

Preparing the Vegetables

While the beef marinates, prep your vegetables to save time later. Keep your slices uniform for even cooking. Once cut:

  • Separate the onions and bell peppers into individual strips.
  • Keep the carrots julienned thin for a consistent stir-fry texture.
  • Clean and slice mushrooms, removing stems if needed.
  • Chop any additional vegetables you’d like to include, like spinach or napa cabbage.

Cooking the Korean Beef and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Cooking bulgogi stir-fry is a quick and rewarding process that yields a restaurant-quality meal in minutes. Here’s how to bring everything together:

Step 1: Searing the Beef

Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wide skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once sizzling hot, drain excess marinade from the beef slices and add them to the pan in small batches. Avoid overcrowding the skillet. Cook each batch for about 2-3 minutes or until the beef develops a caramelized, golden-brown finish. Remove cooked beef and set aside.

Step 2: Cooking the Vegetables

Add the remaining oil to the pan and toss in the onions, carrots, mushrooms, bell peppers, and zucchini. Stir-fry for 4-5 minutes, or until the vegetables become tender but still retain a slight crispness. If adding spinach or cabbage, toss them into the pan during the last 1-2 minutes to wilt. Season with a small pinch of salt to enhance their flavor.

Step 3: Combining the Beef and Vegetables

Reduce the heat to medium. Return the cooked beef to the pan and stir everything together, allowing the flavors to meld for 1-2 minutes. For a saucier finish, you can add any leftover marinade to the pan, letting it reduce slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Bulgogi stir-fry is versatile, making it ideal for pairing with different sides and accompaniments. Serve it piping hot and enjoy these serving ideas:

  • Steamed rice: Traditional white rice completes the dish and soaks up the rich flavors beautifully.
  • Kimchi: For a tangy, spicy kick that complements the sweetness of the bulgogi.
  • Noodles: Toss bulgogi into cooked glass noodles or ramen to create a hearty noodle bowl.
  • Lettuce wraps: Scoop bulgogi into lettuce leaves for a fresh, low-carb option.
  • Egg topping: Add a sunny-side-up or fried egg on top for an extra layer of indulgence.

Cooking Tips and Variations

To keep your stir-fry fresh and exciting, adapt the recipe with these ideas:

  • Vegetarian option: Substitute beef with firm tofu or tempeh and increase your vegetable variety, such as broccoli or snap peas.
  • Spicy twist: For heat lovers, add gochujang (Korean chili paste) to the marinade or stir-fry.
  • Pan alternatives: If you don’t have a wok, use a cast-iron skillet for excellent results.
  • Meal prep: Marinate beef portions ahead of time and freeze them to save time on busy cooking days.

Final Thoughts

Mastering Korean-style beef and vegetable stir-fry (bulgogi) is sure to elevate your cooking repertoire. It’s a dish that combines rich flavor, satisfying textures, and nourishing ingredients in a way few meals can rival. Once you try this recipe, it’s bound to become a weekly favorite, leaving everyone at the table craving more. With these step-by-step instructions, you’ll create bulgogi that’s as authentic as it is delectable. So grab your skillet, prepare your chopsticks, and savor the aroma filling your kitchen—you’re about to create a masterpiece!
