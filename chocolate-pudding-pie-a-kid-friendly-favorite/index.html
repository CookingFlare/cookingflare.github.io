Chocolate Pudding Pie: A Kid-Friendly Favorite


If you’re looking for a delicious dessert that will satisfy both kids and adults alike, look no further than Chocolate Pudding Pie. This classic dessert is a crowd-pleaser, and with its rich, creamy texture and decadent chocolate flavor, it’s sure to become a family favorite. Best of all, it’s easy to make and requires just a few simple ingredients. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups of chocolate cookie crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 3 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons of powdered sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the chocolate cookie crumbs, melted butter, and granulated sugar. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated with the butter and sugar.

  3. Press the crumb mixture into the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie dish, forming an even crust. Make sure to press the crumbs firmly to create a solid base for the pie.

  4. Place the pie crust in the preheated oven and bake for 10 minutes, or until the crust is firm and golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool completely.

  5. In a saucepan, whisk together the cornstarch, salt, and sugar. Gradually whisk in the milk until the mixture is smooth and free of lumps.

  6. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil. Continue to cook for 1-2 minutes, then remove from heat.

  7. Add the chocolate chips to the hot pudding mixture and stir until the chocolate has melted and the mixture is smooth. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  8. Pour the chocolate pudding into the cooled pie crust and smooth the top with a spatula. Place the pie in the refrigerator and let it chill for at least 4 hours, or until the pudding is set.

  9. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream and powdered sugar until soft peaks form. Spread the whipped cream over the chilled pudding pie, covering the entire surface.

  10. Return the pie to the refrigerator and let it chill for an additional hour to allow the flavors to meld.

  11. When ready to serve, slice the chocolate pudding pie into wedges and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra twist, you can add a layer of sliced bananas or strawberries to the bottom of the pie crust before pouring in the chocolate pudding.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use a store-bought graham cracker crust instead of making your own chocolate cookie crust.
  • Get creative with toppings! Sprinkle some crushed chocolate cookies or shaved chocolate on top of the whipped cream for added texture and flavor.
  • If you prefer a lighter version of this pie, you can use low-fat milk and whipped cream instead of whole milk and heavy cream.

So there you have it - a delicious and kid-friendly recipe for Chocolate Pudding Pie. With its smooth and creamy texture and irresistible chocolatey taste, this dessert is sure to be a hit at your next gathering. Whether you’re serving it at a birthday party or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this pie is bound to put a smile on everyone’s face. Give it a try and watch as it quickly becomes a family favorite!
