Homemade Eggnog Pancakes: A Festive Breakfast for the Holiday Season

As the holiday season approaches, our kitchens become filled with the aromas of sweet treats and comforting breakfast dishes. One of the most beloved holiday breakfast traditions is the warm, fluffy pancake. But why settle for ordinary pancakes when you can elevate them with the rich flavors of eggnog? In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make homemade eggnog pancakes that will become a new family favorite. Ingredients 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder 1 teaspoon salt 1 cup granulated sugar 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted 1 cup eggnog 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Butter or oil for greasing the pan Instructions In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, melted butter, eggnog, eggs, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. The batter should still be slightly lumpy. Let the batter rest for 10-15 minutes to allow the flour to absorb the liquid. Preheat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Grease the pan with butter or oil. Using a 1/4 cup measuring cup, scoop the batter onto the pan. Cook the pancakes for 2-3 minutes, until bubbles appear on the surface and the edges start to dry. Flip the pancakes and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until golden brown. Serve the pancakes warm with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, whipped cream, or fresh fruit. Tips and Variations For an extra boost of holiday cheer, add 1/4 cup of chopped pecans or walnuts to the batter for added texture and flavor. If you prefer a stronger eggnog flavor, you can add an additional 1/4 cup of eggnog to the batter. To make the pancakes more festive, use a cookie cutter to cut out fun shapes from the batter before cooking. If you’re short on time, you can prepare the batter the night before and refrigerate it overnight. Simply give it a quick stir before cooking in the morning. History of Eggnog Eggnog, a creamy and sweet beverage made with milk, cream, sugar, and eggs, has a long history dating back to the 18th century. It was originally a drink reserved for the wealthy, as it required expensive ingredients like cream and sugar.
