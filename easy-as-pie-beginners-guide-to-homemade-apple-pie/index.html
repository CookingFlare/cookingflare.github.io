Easy as Pie: Beginner's Guide to Homemade Apple Pie


If you’re a beginner in the kitchen and have always wanted to try your hand at baking, there’s no better place to start than with a classic homemade apple pie. With its flaky crust and sweet, cinnamon-spiced apple filling, apple pie is a timeless dessert that is sure to impress your family and friends. And the best part? It’s easier to make than you might think!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 6 cups apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt for the crust. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, starting with 1/4 cup, and mix until the dough comes together. If needed, add more water, a tablespoon at a time, until the dough holds together when pinched.

  4. Divide the dough in half and shape each half into a disc. Wrap them in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  5. In another mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice for the filling. Toss until the apples are well coated.

  6. Roll out one disc of dough on a lightly floured surface until it is about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it into the bottom and sides.

  7. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust, spreading it out evenly.

  8. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it on top of the apple filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  9. Cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  10. Optional: Brush the top crust with a beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar for a golden, shiny finish.

  11. Place the pie on a baking sheet and bake for 50 to 60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  12. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set.

  13. Serve your homemade apple pie warm or at room temperature, and enjoy it on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For a shortcut, you can use store-bought pie crust instead of making your own. Just follow the package instructions for baking.
  • Experiment with different types of apples to find your favorite combination. Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, and Fuji apples are all great options.
  • Add a handful of raisins or chopped walnuts to the filling for extra texture and flavor.
  • Sprinkle some cinnamon sugar on top of the crust before baking for an extra touch of sweetness.
  • If you don’t have a pastry cutter, you can use two knives or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour.

Now that you have the beginner’s guide to homemade apple pie, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! Don’t be intimidated by the process - just follow the steps and trust your instincts. Before you know it, you’ll have a delicious apple pie that will make you proud. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and let the aroma of warm cinnamon and baked apples fill your kitchen. Happy baking!
