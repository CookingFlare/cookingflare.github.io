1. Classic Buttermilk Pancakes

The foundation of all great pancakes starts with the classic buttermilk pancake recipe. The tangy buttermilk creates a tender crumb with golden, crisp edges, perfect for piling high with butter and maple syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, combine buttermilk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour wet ingredients into dry and stir until just combined; some lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a skillet over medium heat, lightly grease, and pour 1/4 cup of batter per pancake.
  5. Cook until bubbles form on top, flip, and cook until golden on the other side.

Serve stacked with warm maple syrup and butter for the ultimate comforting breakfast.

2. Blueberry Lemon Pancakes

Bursting with fresh blueberries and a zing of lemon, these pancakes are a bright and refreshing take on the classic.

Key Tips: Use fresh blueberries to prevent batter discoloration; fold gently to keep berries intact.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk milk, butter, eggs, and lemon zest.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients; fold in blueberries.
  4. Cook pancakes on a greased griddle until golden, flipping carefully.

Top with extra blueberries and a drizzle of honey or lemon syrup.

3. Chocolate Chip Pancakes with a Hint of Cinnamon

Sweet, warm, and a little indulgent, this chocolate chip pancake recipe is perfect for satisfying a weekend sweet tooth.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Combine dry ingredients in a bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk buttermilk, eggs, and butter.
  3. Mix wet and dry ingredients; fold in chocolate chips.
  4. Cook pancakes on a hot skillet till golden on both sides.

Serve with whipped cream or a dusting of powdered sugar for an extra special touch.

4. Vegan Banana Pancakes

Deliciously fluffy with naturally sweet bananas, this vegan recipe excludes eggs and dairy but never compromises on flavor or texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix almond milk, mashed bananas, vanilla, and oil.
  3. Stir wet ingredients into the dry just until combined.
  4. Cook on a greased skillet over medium heat until golden.

This recipe pairs beautifully with maple syrup and fresh fruit.

5. Savory Herb and Parmesan Pancakes

For those who prefer savory over sweet, these herb-infused pancakes with parmesan are satisfying and unique for breakfast or brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh herbs (chives, parsley, thyme)
  • 2 tbsp melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk milk, egg, melted butter, parmesan, and herbs.
  3. Mix wet ingredients into dry until just combined.
  4. Cook on a hot griddle until golden and cooked through.

Serve with sour cream or a dollop of yogurt and smoked salmon for a decadent brunch twist.

6. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Fall flavors extend all the way into weekend breakfasts with this warmly spiced pumpkin pancake recipe. The pumpkin puree makes pancakes rich and moist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 tbsp melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Stir together the dry ingredients in a bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk pumpkin, milk, eggs, and butter.
  3. Combine both and mix until smooth.
  4. Cook on medium heat until pancakes hold together and edges are set.

Top with whipped cream and a drizzle of maple syrup for the ultimate cozy feel.

7. Oatmeal Pancakes with Honey and Walnuts

A hearty option, oatmeal pancakes mix the wholesome goodness of oats with crunch from walnuts and the natural sweetness of honey.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/3 cup chopped walnuts

Instructions:

  1. Blend oats into a coarse flour or use as-is for texture.
  2. Mix oats, flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Whisk milk, eggs, butter, and honey together.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients, fold in walnuts carefully.
  5. Cook each pancake until golden brown on both sides.

Perfect with a pat of butter and extra honey for drizzling.

8. Ricotta and Lemon Pancakes

Light, fluffy, and slightly tangy, ricotta pancakes carry a rich and creamy dimension thatâ€™s hard to beat on a relaxed morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, combine ricotta, buttermilk, eggs, lemon zest, and juice.
  3. Fold dry ingredients into wet just until mixed.
  4. Cook pancakes over medium heat until lightly browned.

Serve with fresh berries and a dusting of powdered sugar.

9. Whole Wheat Banana Walnut Pancakes

A healthier twist on banana pancakes, whole wheat flour makes these more filling, paired with crunchy walnuts for added texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1/3 cup chopped walnuts

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, baking powder, salt, and brown sugar in a bowl.
  2. Whisk milk, mashed bananas, egg, and butter in another.
  3. Fold wet into dry ingredients, then add walnuts.
  4. Cook pancakes until golden and set.

Enjoy with a drizzle of maple syrup or nut butter.

10. Matcha Green Tea Pancakes

These vibrant green pancakes are infused with matcha powder for a subtle earthiness and beautiful color.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp matcha green tea powder
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 tbsp melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Sift flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and matcha powder.
  2. Whisk milk, egg, and butter.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
  4. Cook on a hot griddle until bubbles form.

Serve with honey or a light drizzle of sweetened condensed milk.

11. Apple Cinnamon Pancakes

Comfort in pancake form, apples and cinnamon come together for a warm and nostalgic flavor pairing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 apple, peeled and finely diced

Instructions:

  1. Mix dry ingredients in a bowl.
  2. Whisk milk, egg, and butter separately.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients and fold in diced apples.
  4. Cook pancakes over medium heat until golden and fragrant.

Serve with warm syrup or softened butter.

12. Peanut Butter and Jelly Pancakes

Turn the classic sandwich into a fun pancake variant that kids and adults alike will adore.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • Peanut butter and your choice of jelly for topping

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Whisk milk, eggs, and butter.
  3. Combine wet and dry; cook pancakes on griddle.
  4. Serve topped with peanut butter spread and berry jelly.

The combination makes breakfast feel playful and nostalgic.

13. Cornmeal Pancakes with Honey Butter

Adding cornmeal lends a bit of crunch and a rustic taste. Paired with honey butter, these pancakes are a southern-inspired delight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornmeal
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter

Honey Butter:

  • 4 tbsp butter, softened
  • 2 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, cornmeal, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Whisk milk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Combine, then cook pancakes till golden brown.
  4. Mix butter and honey for topping.

Serve pancakes warm with a generous smear of honey butter.

14. Coconut Flour Pancakes (Gluten-Free)

Perfect for gluten-sensitive pancakes lovers, coconut flour adds fiber and a subtle tropical flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup coconut flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk (or coconut milk)
  • 2 tbsp melted coconut oil
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Combine coconut flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Whisk eggs, milk, coconut oil, and maple syrup.
  3. Mix wet and dry until batter thickens.
  4. Cook small pancakes over medium heat due to batter thickness.

Serve with fresh berries or a drizzle of coconut cream.

15. Sweet Potato Pancakes

A nutrient-packed alternative with a delicious orange hue and warm spice notes, sweet potatoes make pancakes hearty and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mashed cooked sweet potato
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Whisk sweet potato, milk, eggs, and butter.
  3. Stir wet ingredients into dry.
  4. Cook pancakes on a hot griddle until golden.

Serve with a pat of butter and syrup or a dollop of yogurt.
