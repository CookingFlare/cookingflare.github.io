Gluten-Free Holiday Recipes That Taste Amazing


The holiday season is a time for celebration, loved ones, and dining together. Unfortunately, this can be a challenging time for those who adhere to a gluten-free diet. But don't worry, we've got you covered with some mouth-watering and easily adaptable gluten-free recipes that are sure to become new family favorites. These holiday recipes are not only flavorful and satisfying but also packed with nutrients to keep your loved ones healthy and happy throughout the festive season. Gluten-Free Cranberry Apple Crumble There's nothing quite like a warm and comforting fruit crisp to cozy up with during the holidays. This gluten-free version combines juicy apples, sweet cranberries, and crunchy oat topping, all made from wholesome ingredients that even the gluten-intolerant can enjoy. Ingredients

1 cup rolled oats 1/2 cup almond flour 1/4 cup brown sugar 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces 6 cups mixed apples, peeled and sliced 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries 1/4 cup honey 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine oats, almond flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Stir in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Combine sliced apples and cranberries in a separate bowl. Sprinkle honey and lemon juice over the fruit and gently toss to coat. Place the fruit mixture in a 9x9-inch baking dish and top with the oat mixture. Bake for 40-45 minutes or until golden brown and the fruit is bubbly.

Santa's Favorite Gluten-Free Gingerbread Cookies Who says you can't have your gingerbread cake and eat it too? These easy-to-make cookies use a simple gluten-free milk mixture and sweet honey to ensure they're both delicious and safe to enjoy. Ingredients

1 1/2 cups peanut butter 1/2 cup coconut sugar 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon ginger 1 teaspoon cinnamon 1/2 cup coconut milk 2 cups almond flour 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon baking soda 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar 1/4 cup molasses Decorations (gingerbread gel icing and candy pieces)

Instructions

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Combine peanut butter, coconut sugar, eggs, ginger, cinnamon, and coconut milk in a large bowl. Stir in almond flour, salt, baking soda, and cream of tartar. Add molasses and mix until a dough forms. Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to approximately 1/4 inch thickness. Use cookie cutters to cut out your favorite shapes and bake for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown. Allow the cookies to cool and then frost with gingerbread gel icing and decorate as desired.

Green Chile and Southwestern Roasted Sweet Potatoes Whether you're hosting family game night, an epic holiday party, or a casual dinner, these roasted sweet potatoes are sure to delight. A stunning addition to any menu, these addictive roasted spuds combine the intense flavors of Southwestern spices with rich, creamy chile pepper. Ingredients

2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges 2 tablespoons chili oil 1 cup vegan sour cream 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1 tablespoon cumin 1 tablespoon chili powder 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional) Salt and pepper to taste Chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, combine cut sweet potatoes, chili oil, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, paprika, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Toss to coat until all potatoes are evenly seasoned. Bake the potatoes for 1 hour or until tender and slightly caramelized. Remove from oven and brush with vegan sour cream. Top with shredded cheddar cheese and bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, until cheese is melted and golden. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro for a finishing touch.
