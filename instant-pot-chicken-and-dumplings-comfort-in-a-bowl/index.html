Instant Pot Chicken and Dumplings: Comfort in a Bowl


Few dishes evoke the feeling of comfort and warmth quite like chicken and dumplings. This classic comfort food brings together tender chicken, hearty vegetables, and fluffy dumplings in a savory broth that’s perfect for any occasion. With the help of the Instant Pot, you can enjoy this soul-soothing dish in a fraction of the time it traditionally takes, without sacrificing any of its delicious flavors.

Ingredients

To make Instant Pot Chicken and Dumplings, you’ll need:

For the Chicken Stew:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Dumplings:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

Step 1: Sauté the Chicken and Vegetables

  1. Set your Instant Pot to ‘Sauté’ mode. Heat olive oil in the pot and add diced onion, sliced carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  2. Add minced garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds until fragrant. Season with salt, pepper, dried thyme, and dried parsley.

  3. Add the bite-sized chicken thigh pieces to the pot. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until the chicken is lightly browned on all sides.

Step 2: Pressure Cook the Chicken Stew

  1. Pour in chicken broth and give everything a good stir to combine. Ensure no browned bits are stuck to the bottom of the pot.

  2. Close the lid of the Instant Pot and set the valve to ‘Sealing’. Cancel the ‘Sauté’ mode and select the ‘Pressure Cook’ or ‘Manual’ setting. Cook on high pressure for 5 minutes.

  3. Once the cooking cycle is complete, allow for a natural pressure release for 10 minutes. Then carefully switch the valve to ‘Venting’ to release any remaining pressure.

Step 3: Make the Dumpling Dough

  1. While the chicken stew is cooking, prepare the dumpling dough. In a mixing bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.

  2. Add melted butter and milk to the dry ingredients. Stir until just combined. The dough will be sticky.

Step 4: Add Dumplings to the Pot

  1. Open the lid of the Instant Pot once pressure has fully released. Stir in frozen peas to the chicken stew.

  2. Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough evenly over the top of the stew. You should have about 8-10 dumplings.

  3. Close the lid of the Instant Pot again. Set the valve to ‘Sealing’ and cook on high pressure for 8 minutes.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Once the cooking cycle is complete, perform a quick pressure release by carefully turning the valve to ‘Venting’.

  2. Open the lid and gently stir the chicken and dumplings together. The dumplings should be fluffy and cooked through.

  3. Serve hot in bowls, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy the comforting warmth of Instant Pot Chicken and Dumplings!

Tips for Perfect Chicken and Dumplings

  • Chicken Thighs: Using boneless, skinless chicken thighs ensures tender and juicy pieces of chicken in the stew.
  • Vegetables: Feel free to customize with your favorite vegetables like potatoes or green beans.
  • Dumpling Dough: Handle the dumpling dough gently and avoid overmixing to keep the dumplings light and fluffy.
  • Seasoning: Adjust salt, pepper, and herbs according to your taste preferences.

Serving Suggestions

Instant Pot Chicken and Dumplings is a complete meal on its own but pairs wonderfully with a side salad or crusty bread for a heartier meal.

Side Salad Ideas:

  • Mixed Green Salad: Toss together mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, and a simple vinaigrette.
  • Cucumber Tomato Salad: Combine sliced cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and feta cheese with a lemon-herb dressing.

Bread Ideas:

  • Garlic Bread: Slice a baguette, spread with garlic butter, and toast until golden brown.
  • Biscuits: Serve with warm, flaky biscuits for a comforting addition.

Storing Leftovers

Store any leftover Instant Pot Chicken and Dumplings in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove or in the microwave until heated through.

Conclusion

Instant Pot Chicken and Dumplings is the ultimate comfort food that’s quick and easy to prepare with the help of your trusty Instant Pot. Whether you’re craving a cozy dinner for the family or looking to warm up on a chilly evening, this dish delivers wholesome flavors and satisfying textures in every spoonful. Embrace the comfort of homemade chicken and dumplings with this simple yet delightful recipe—it’s sure to become a favorite in your household!
