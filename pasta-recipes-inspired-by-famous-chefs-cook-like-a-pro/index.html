Pasta Recipes Inspired by Famous Chefs: Cook Like a Pro


Pasta is a universal favorite. Its versatility, simplicity, and ability to hold complex flavors make it a staple ingredient in kitchens around the world. Unsurprisingly, many renowned chefs have elevated pasta into an art form by infusing their expertise, creativity, and deep respect for ingredients. In this blog post, we’ll explore pasta recipes inspired by some of the world’s most celebrated chefs. These recipes are easy to follow yet sophisticated, allowing you to cook like a culinary professional in the comfort of your home.

1. Cacio e Pepe Inspired by Massimo Bottura

Massimo Bottura, the Michelin-starred Italian chef and owner of Osteria Francescana, is a firm believer in celebrating simplicity with high-quality ingredients. His take on Cacio e Pepe is an homage to traditional Roman cuisine, featuring only a handful of components executed to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti or tonnarelli
  • 200g Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated
  • 2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ cup reserved pasta cooking water

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook until al dente, reserving about ½ cup of the pasta water.
  2. Meanwhile, toast freshly ground black pepper in a large pan over medium heat until fragrant.
  3. In a mixing bowl, blend the grated Pecorino Romano with a small amount of warm water to form a creamy paste.
  4. Drain the pasta and add it to the pan with the toasted pepper. Toss to coat the strands in the aromatic oils.
  5. Gradually add the creamy cheese mixture into the pasta while tossing, incorporating the reserved pasta water as needed to achieve a silky sauce.
  6. Plate immediately and serve with an extra sprinkle of Pecorino Romano on top.

This deceptively simple dish highlights the importance of technique and ingredient selection—an approach Bottura champions in his cooking.

2. Pappardelle with Ragu Inspired by Gordon Ramsay

Known for his bold flavors and elevated comfort food, Gordon Ramsay’s take on ragu transforms a classic Italian dish into something unforgettable. His use of fresh herbs, slow-cooked meats, and a touch of elegance makes it approachable yet refined.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fresh pappardelle pasta
  • 500g beef chuck, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp tomato paste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh Parmesan cheese, grated (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add the diced carrot, celery, and onion, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute. Stir in the beef, breaking it up and browning it evenly.
  3. Deglaze the pan with the red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Let the alcohol evaporate for a couple of minutes.
  4. Add the tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, bay leaf, and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cover and simmer on low for 2–3 hours, stirring occasionally and adding a little water if the sauce thickens too much.
  6. Cook the pappardelle in salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and toss with the rich, meaty ragu.
  7. Serve with freshly grated Parmesan and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.

Ramsay’s attention to layering flavors brings out the depth in this rustic yet sophisticated dish.

3. Lemon Ricotta Ravioli Inspired by Giada De Laurentiis

Giada De Laurentiis, famed for her fresh and vibrant Italian-American style, often uses ingredients that celebrate brightness and balance. Her lemon ricotta ravioli is an elegant yet achievable dish that showcases her signature approach.

Ingredients:

For the pasta dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • Pinch of salt

For the filling:

  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/3 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the sauce:

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 10 fresh sage leaves
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Instructions:

  1. To make the pasta dough, combine the flour and salt on a clean work surface. Make a well in the center, crack in the eggs, and gently incorporate the flour using a fork. Knead until the dough is smooth and elastic. Wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. For the filling, mix ricotta, lemon zest, Parmesan, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Set aside.
  3. Roll out the dough thinly and cut into squares. Spoon a small amount of filling onto each square, fold, and seal the edges to form ravioli.
  4. Bring a pot of salted water to a gentle boil. Cook the ravioli until they float, about 2–3 minutes.
  5. In a pan, melt butter over medium heat and add sage leaves. Sauté until the sage becomes crisp and the butter turns golden brown. Add lemon juice for a bright finish.
  6. Toss the cooked ravioli in the sauce and plate with an additional sprinkle of Parmesan.

This dish is perfect for dinner parties or intimate evenings, capturing De Laurentiis’ love for light and flavorful cuisine.

4. Tagliatelle Carbonara Inspired by Jamie Oliver

Jamie Oliver’s food philosophy revolves around making wholesome recipes that everyone can try. His take on carbonara involves accessible ingredients while maintaining the integrity of this beloved Italian classic.

Ingredients:

  • 400g tagliatelle
  • 100g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 whole eggs + 2 yolks
  • ½ cup Pecorino Romano, grated
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the tagliatelle in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water.
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk the eggs, yolks, Pecorino Romano, and black pepper.
  3. In a large pan over medium heat, cook the pancetta until crisp. Remove from heat.
  4. Toss the drained pasta into the pan with pancetta. Slowly add the egg mixture, stirring constantly to create a creamy texture. Avoid applying direct heat to prevent scrambling.
  5. Add reserved pasta water gradually for the desired consistency.
  6. Serve warm with additional Pecorino Romano and freshly cracked pepper.

Jamie’s emphasis on simplicity shines through in this comforting, restaurant-quality meal you can easily recreate.

Conclusion

Cooking pasta like a pro doesn’t require years of culinary training or access to obscure ingredients. By drawing inspiration from these renowned chefs and their signature styles, you can transform your kitchen into a world-class trattoria. Elevate your pasta game with these recipes, focusing on high-quality ingredients, precise techniques, and a touch of creativity. Whether preparing a quick weeknight dinner or an elaborate feast, these dishes are sure to impress and delight.
