Savory and Satisfying: Meatball Sub Sandwich Recipe


If you’re craving a hearty, comforting meal that’s packed with flavor, look no further than the classic meatball sub sandwich. This culinary gem combines juicy meatballs, rich marinara sauce, and melting cheese, all nestled within a soft, crusty sub roll. Perfect for lunch, dinner, or even a casual gathering, this recipe will satisfy your hunger and impress your family and friends. Let’s dive into the world of meatball subs and explore how to make this delightful dish from scratch.

Ingredients

For the Meatballs

  • 1 pound ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for added flavor)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (Italian seasoned for an extra flavor kick)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 large egg
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for some heat)

For the Marinara Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance the acidity of the tomatoes)
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembly

  • 4 sub rolls (preferably hoagie rolls)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)
  • Extra Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Meatballs

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan, parsley, egg, minced garlic, onion powder, oregano, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. Mix everything thoroughly until well combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to tough meatballs.

  2. Shape the Meatballs: Use your hands to form the mixture into meatballs, approximately 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper for easy cleanup.

  3. Bake the Meatballs: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Bake the meatballs for about 20 minutes or until they are browned and cooked through. You can check their doneness by inserting a meat thermometer; it should read 160°F (70°C) for beef.

Step 2: Make the Marinara Sauce

  1. Sauté Aromatics: In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, being careful not to let it burn.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Seasoning: Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir in the sugar, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will allow the flavors to meld beautifully.

  3. Combine Meatballs and Sauce: Once the meatballs are cooked, carefully add them to the marinara sauce. Let them simmer together for another 5-10 minutes, absorbing the flavors of the sauce.

Step 3: Assemble the Meatball Sub Sandwiches

  1. Prep the Rolls: While the meatballs are simmering, slice the sub rolls in half lengthwise. If desired, you can lightly toast them in the oven for a few minutes to enhance their texture.

  2. Load the Meatballs: Using tongs or a slotted spoon, carefully place 2-3 meatballs onto each sub roll. Spoon over some of the marinara sauce to ensure each bite is packed with flavor.

  3. Add Cheese: Generously sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over the meatballs. If you like your cheese extra bubbly and golden, you can place the assembled sandwiches under the broiler for a few minutes until the cheese melts.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Remove the sandwiches from the oven and garnish with fresh basil leaves and an extra sprinkle of Parmesan cheese if desired. Serve hot and enjoy the savory satisfaction of your homemade meatball sub!

Tips for the Perfect Meatball Sub

  • Meat Variety: Mixing ground beef with ground pork or turkey can add complexity to the flavor and texture of the meatballs.

  • Cheese Options: While mozzarella is the classic choice, feel free to experiment with provolone or even a spicy pepper jack for a twist.

  • Make It Healthier: For a lighter version, consider using lean ground turkey or chicken, and whole grain sub rolls.

  • Storage and Reheating: Leftover meatballs and sauce can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat on the stove or in the microwave before assembling your subs.

  • Meal Prep: You can make a large batch of meatballs and marinara sauce ahead of time. They freeze well, so you can enjoy a quick and satisfying meal on busy days.

Pairing Suggestions

While meatball subs are delicious on their own, consider pairing them with a side of crispy fries or a fresh salad for a complete meal. A light vinaigrette salad can balance the richness of the sandwich, while sweet potato fries add a delightful twist. For beverages, a cold soda, iced tea, or a light beer can complement the flavors beautifully.

Conclusion

The meatball sub sandwich is a timeless classic that brings comfort and satisfaction with every bite. With its juicy meatballs, flavorful marinara sauce, and gooey cheese, it’s no wonder this dish has earned a permanent spot in the hearts (and stomachs) of many. Whether you’re whipping it up for a weeknight dinner or serving it at a gathering, this recipe is sure to please. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create a savory and satisfying meatball sub that will have everyone coming back for seconds! Enjoy your delicious creation, and happy cooking!
