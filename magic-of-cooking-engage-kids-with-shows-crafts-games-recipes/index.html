Magic of Cooking: Engage Kids with Shows, Crafts, Games & Recipes


Cooking is not just a skill to be acquired, but an adventure to be experienced! While passing on the art of cooking to children might seem like a challenging task, it doesn’t have to be. With the right approach, you’ll be astounded by the enthusiasm and curiosity kids can bring to your kitchen. Dive into our guide to ignite their passion for cooking, and learn how to blend education, fun, and delicious dishes along the way!

1. Turn On the TV

If your little ones are easily distracted, consider tuning into their favorite cooking shows while making a dish together. Your budding chef will be enchanted by the world of Willy Wonka as well as the magic of Julia Child. Even children who don’t show keen interest in helping might end up sticking around to learn more. Remember to mute the content if necessary, to avoid any unpleasant surprises.

2. Decorate and Personalize

Who said cooking has to be done in a drab, ordinary kitchen? Transform your workspace into a whimsical culinary playground! From colorful aprons to decorative cutting boards, children will feel empowered and have fun while engaged in preparing delicious dishes. Fun kitchen utensils like pizza cutters can transform their potatoes into delightful stars.

3. Have a Theme Day

Make it exciting by picking a theme every week. The theme could range from Italian Night to a My Little Pony Themed Paella Party. When they focus on developing a menu, they’d naturally learn more about different cuisines and expand their culinary vocabulary. Don’t forget to let your kids help in picking the theme.

4. Incorporate Crafts

Take inspiration from the book, ‘The Ugly Dumpling,’ and make food-shaped crafts to accompany your cooking experience. Kids will learn about different shapes and their correlation to the component they represent. Plan a gingerbread-house building competition, where your children can unleash their creativity and artistic skills. Remember to let them taste their edible masterpieces.

5. Play ‘Guess The Ingredient’

Strengthen their observational skills by playing ‘Guess The Ingredient.’ Blindfold them and allow them to touch and smell various unknown kitchen ingredients. This activity facilitates the development of senses that will lead to better understanding of cooking.

6. Set Up a Fun Game

Introduce cooking-related games to enhance their knowledge, increase their skill set, and have a good time. Timed cooking contests, instructed by fun rules, will get hearts racing, and neurotransmitters pumping. The excitement builds higher when they see their parents competing in such a delightful contest.

7. Experiment with Yummy Edible Play-Doh

Transform flour and water into edible play-doh by adding food coloring and other mix-ins. This activity will keep kids entertained for hours, with an added bonus of them learning about different textures that occur in cooking. When they eat their creation, they would know how food texture directly affects taste.

8. Family-Favorite Recipe Spotlight

Delighting the palate can be reached through experimentation with mouthwatering family-favorite recipes. When the final dish is cooked, sit down and discuss the entire process - from what worked well, to what needs improvement. This will trigger deeper learning about meal preparation, retention, and evaluation.

9. Read (Culinary) Books Together

Reading with your children about cooking-related books will open up their minds to an array of unique stories, recipes, and cultural aspects. Classic picture books like, ‘Hooray for Diffendoofer Day!’ by Dr. Seuss, and ‘A River of Words: The Story of William Vollmann,’ will help them appreciate the value of words.

10. Create a Cooking Diary

Encourage the young culinary experts to keep a cooking diary, where they can jot down their recipes and experiences. This activity fosters a love for documenting, creativity, and practical experimentation, which also strengthens their language skills.

In conclusion, involving children in cooking will not only create happy memories, foster creativity, and stoke their excitement, but also help them build a strong foundation for healthy eating habits. While you put the magic back into cooking, don’t forget to support their experimentation, creativity, and indulgence. Soon enough, you’ll discover a budding chef in your midst!
