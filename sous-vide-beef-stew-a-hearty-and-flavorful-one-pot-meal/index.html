Sous Vide Beef Stew: A Hearty and Flavorful One-Pot Meal


Are you craving a comforting and delicious meal that will warm your soul? Look no further than Sous Vide Beef Stew! This hearty and flavorful one-pot meal is sure to become a favorite in your household. With tender beef, vibrant vegetables, and a rich broth, it’s the perfect dish to enjoy on a chilly evening. In this recipe, we’ll explore the step-by-step process of cooking this delectable stew using the sous vide method.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your water bath to 135°F (57°C) using your sous vide machine.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and sear on all sides until browned. This will help to develop a rich flavor in the stew.

  3. Once the beef is seared, remove it from the skillet and set it aside. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  4. Add the carrots, celery, and mushrooms to the skillet. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  5. Deglaze the skillet by adding the red wine. Stir well, scraping the bottom of the skillet to release any browned bits. Let the wine simmer for a minute to reduce slightly.

  6. Transfer the contents of the skillet into a large resealable bag, along with the seared beef. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaves, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Seal the bag, making sure to remove any excess air.

  7. Place the bag into the preheated water bath, ensuring it is fully submerged. Cook the beef stew at 135°F (57°C) for 24 hours. This slow-cooking process will result in tender and flavorful meat.

  8. Once the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Open the bag and transfer the beef stew to a serving dish, discarding the bay leaves.

  9. Garnish the stew with fresh parsley for a pop of color and added freshness.

  10. Serve the Sous Vide Beef Stew hot with crusty bread or over a bed of creamy mashed potatoes. The choice is yours!

Conclusion:

Sous Vide Beef Stew is a true labor of love that rewards you with a rich and comforting meal. The sous vide method allows the flavors to meld together and the beef to become incredibly tender. This one-pot wonder is perfect for a cozy family dinner or for impressing guests with its depth of flavor. Give this recipe a try, and you’ll find yourself coming back to it time and time again. Enjoy!

Remember to adjust the cooking time according to your preference. If you prefer a firmer texture, reduce the cooking time slightly. Feel free to experiment with different vegetables and seasonings to make this Sous Vide Beef Stew your own. Happy cooking!
