The Best Nut-Free Cookie Recipes for Allergen-Friendly Treats


Are you craving some delicious cookies but need to avoid nuts due to allergies? Look no further! We have compiled a list of the best nut-free cookie recipes that are perfect for those with nut allergies, or anyone looking for tasty allergen-friendly treats. Whether you have a sweet tooth or want to impress your friends and family with your baking skills, these recipes are sure to satisfy your cookie cravings.

1. Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Who can resist the timeless charm of a classic chocolate chip cookie? This nut-free version is just as delicious as the original, with a chewy texture and bursts of chocolate in every bite. Make sure to use dairy-free chocolate chips if you have dairy allergies as well.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Fold in the dairy-free chocolate chips.
  6. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake for 9-11 minutes or until the edges are golden brown. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

For a healthier twist on the classic oatmeal raisin cookie, try this nut-free recipe. These cookies are packed with wholesome oats and sweet raisins, making them the perfect guilt-free treat for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups old-fashioned oats
  • 1 cup raisins

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, ground cinnamon, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Fold in the oats and raisins.
  6. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Lemon Shortbread Cookies

If you’re in the mood for something zesty and refreshing, these lemon shortbread cookies are the perfect choice. With a delicate crumbly texture and a burst of citrus flavor, they are an absolute delight to indulge in.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, powdered sugar, lemon zest, and lemon juice.
  3. Gradually add in the flour and salt, mixing until the dough comes together.
  4. Roll the dough into small balls and place them on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Flatten each ball slightly with the back of a fork.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These nut-free cookie recipes are not only delicious but also safe for those with nut allergies. Share them with your loved ones and enjoy the scrumptious flavors without worrying about allergies. Happy baking!
