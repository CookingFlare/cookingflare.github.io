Pumpkin Spice Granola: Crunchy Fall Snack


Have you ever smelled the crisp air and felt the chill of autumn? Me neither, but you better believe I’m enjoying my fair share of pumpkin-spiced creations in my own little world. As the leaves tumble and the days grow shorter, who doesn’t want a warming, satisfying snack to accompany their pumpkin-spiced lip balm and candles?

Enter: Pumpkin Spice Granola – the perfect crunchy companion to your squash-filled dreams. Made with a blend of nuts, seeds, and dried fruits, this DIY snack is customizable to your heart’s content, and is far superior to the prepackaged version you’ll find at the grocery store. Trust me; I’ve compared notes with a few autumnal mammals and we’re all in agreement: homemade is the way to go.

Gathering Your Ingredients

First things first: gather up those ingredients! Your essentials will include:

  • 1 cup raw almonds
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin seeds (also known as pepitas)
  • 1/2 cup sunflower seeds
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries or raisins
  • 1/2 cup walnuts (or substitute your favorite nut)
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup pure pumpkin puree
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons pumpkin pie spice (cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, cloves, and allspice in harmony)
  • 1 teaspoon ground Himilayan pink salt
  • Optional, but persuasive: 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips (for those cocoa crunch needs)

Whipping Up Your Pumpkin Spice Granola

No fancy equipment required! Just a mixing bowl, a baking sheet, and your trusty hands. Follow these steps:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). You’ll be baking this granola, so your oven needs to be awake and prepared to do some work. If you have a convection oven, feel free to use that – it’ll cut about 25°F (14°C) off the equation.
  2. In a very large bowl, combine all your nuts, seeds, and oats. Feel free to mix and match – swap out nuts you love and dislike, or add synergy-enhancing extras like coconut flakes or hemp seeds.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together your honey, pumpkin puree, maple syrup, pumpkin pie spice, and pink salt. This is what will get your ingredients tasting all autumnal and cozy.
  4. Pour the wet mixture over your dry mixture and blend well. You may eyeball this and manually mix until everything is evenly coated in tasty goo.
  5. Spread your coated ingredients onto an ungreased baking sheet. Make sure to give ’em some space – granola needs to crisp up and get all toasty, and overcrowding will lead to hard-to-chew collections instead of crunchy little clusters.
  6. Into that preheated oven your granola goes! Bake for about 30 minutes, or until the desired level of golden brown is achieved (it’s okay to peak in on it from time to time). Be mindful not to overbake, as it’ll turn into unpleasantly, well, burnt.
  7. Out of the oven and onto the cooling rack! Give your granola about 30 minutes to cool down before you try to handle it. Trust me, patience is a virtue in this scenario.
  8. Stir in your dried fruit and/or chocolate chips (if you chose to go that route) after the granola has had time to cool down.
  9. Store your finished pumpkin spice granola in an airtight container, preferably at room temperature, to maintain mouthwatering crispiness. It’ll last for weeks that way!

And that’s it! Now you’ll have a no-fuss, customizable autumn snack at the ready whenever you need. Who needs store-bought when you can have homemade pumpkin spice granola? Happy snacking, and happy fall!
