Creamy Nut-Free Potato Salad Recipe


When it comes to summer picnics, barbecues, or family gatherings, a classic potato salad is always a hit. However, finding the perfect recipe that caters to dietary restrictions can sometimes be challenging. If you’re looking for a creamy, delicious, nut-free potato salad recipe that’s sure to delight everyone, you’ve come to the right place. This recipe combines simplicity with flavor, offering a dish that’s both easy to prepare and satisfying to eat.

Ingredients

For the Salad:

  • 2 pounds of potatoes (Yukon Gold or red potatoes work best)
  • 1 cup of celery, finely chopped
  • 1 cup of pickles, diced (dill pickles preferred)
  • 1/2 cup of red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional)

For the Dressing:

  • 1 cup of mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons of pickle juice (from the jar of pickles)
  • 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes

  1. Wash and peel the potatoes. While peeling is optional, removing the skin helps achieve a smoother texture.
  2. Cut the potatoes into bite-sized pieces, ensuring they are roughly uniform in size for even cooking.
  3. Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Add a generous pinch of salt to the water.
  4. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to medium and cook until the potatoes are tender but not mushy, about 10-12 minutes.
  5. Drain the potatoes in a colander and let them cool slightly. You can spread them out on a baking sheet to speed up the cooling process.

Step 2: Prepare the Dressing

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, pickle juice, apple cider vinegar, and sugar.
  2. Whisk until smooth and well-combined.
  3. Season the dressing with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to start with a small amount and adjust as needed.

Step 3: Assemble the Salad

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled potatoes, celery, pickles, red onion, and fresh parsley. If you’re including chopped hard-boiled eggs, add them now.
  2. Pour the dressing over the potato mixture.
  3. Gently toss everything together until the potatoes and vegetables are evenly coated with the creamy dressing.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

  1. Cover the potato salad with plastic wrap or transfer it to an airtight container.
  2. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving. Chilling allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.
  3. Serve chilled as a side dish at your next gathering.

Tips and Variations

  • Vegan Option: Substitute the mayonnaise with a vegan mayo alternative and omit the hard-boiled eggs.
  • Extra Crunch: Add chopped bell peppers or radishes for additional texture and flavor.
  • Herb Variations: Experiment with different herbs like dill or chives to give the salad a unique twist.
  • Make Ahead: This potato salad can be made up to 24 hours in advance, making it a convenient option for busy hosts.

Nutritional Information

While specific nutritional information will vary based on portion sizes and exact ingredients used, here’s a general overview per serving (assuming 8 servings):

  • Calories: ~250
  • Protein: 3g
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fat: 13g
  • Fiber: 3g
  • Sugar: 2g

This creamy nut-free potato salad is not only a crowd-pleaser but also a versatile dish that can be tailored to fit various dietary needs. Whether you’re hosting a summer BBQ or simply craving a comforting side dish, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire.

Enjoy your creamy, delicious, and totally nut-free potato salad!
