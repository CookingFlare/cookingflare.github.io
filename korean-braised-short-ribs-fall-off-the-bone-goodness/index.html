Korean Braised Short Ribs: Fall-Off-The-Bone Goodness


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique cooking techniques, and one dish that perfectly embodies these characteristics is Korean Braised Short Ribs. This melt-in-your-mouth delicacy, also known as Galbi Jjim, combines tender beef ribs with a rich and savory sauce that will leave you craving for more. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to create this succulent and satisfying dish in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds beef short ribs
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup rice wine (mirin)
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 8 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the beef short ribs. Trim off any excess fat and rinse them under cold water. Pat them dry with a paper towel and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, rice wine, sesame oil, and minced garlic. Mix until the sugar has dissolved and the ingredients are well combined.

  3. Place the beef short ribs in a large pot or Dutch oven. Pour the marinade over the ribs, making sure they are fully coated. Cover the pot and let the ribs marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.

  4. Once the ribs have marinated, remove them from the refrigerator and let them come to room temperature.

  5. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).

  6. In the same pot or Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onion and carrots, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the carrots start to soften.

  7. Add the marinated short ribs to the pot, along with any remaining marinade. Pour in the beef broth, making sure the ribs are partially submerged. If needed, add more beef broth or water to cover the ribs.

  8. Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the ribs simmer for about 2 hours, or until they become tender and easily fall off the bone.

  9. After 2 hours, add the cubed potatoes to the pot. Cover and continue simmering for another 30 minutes, or until the potatoes are fully cooked and tender.

  10. Once the ribs and potatoes are cooked, carefully remove them from the pot and transfer them to a serving dish. Garnish with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds.

  11. Serve the Korean Braised Short Ribs hot, accompanied by steamed rice and kimchi for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Korean Braised Short Ribs are a true masterpiece of flavors and textures. The slow braising process allows the beef to become incredibly tender, while the rich and savory sauce infuses it with a depth of flavor that is simply irresistible. This dish is perfect for special occasions or when you want to treat yourself to something truly extraordinary. So why not bring the flavors of Korea to your kitchen and indulge in this Fall-Off-The-Bone Goodness?
