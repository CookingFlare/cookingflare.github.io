Gluten-Free Lemon Bars: A Tangy and Refreshing Dessert


If you’re a fan of citrusy treats, you’re in for a real treat with these gluten-free lemon bars. Bursting with zesty flavor and a buttery, crumbly crust, these bars are the perfect combination of sweet and tangy. Whether you have a gluten intolerance or simply want to explore new flavors, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire. Let’s dive into the details of how to create these irresistible lemon bars!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • A pinch of salt

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, granulated sugar, melted butter, and a pinch of salt. Mix until the ingredients come together to form a crumbly dough.
  3. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan.
  4. Bake the crust for 15-18 minutes, or until it turns golden brown. Once done, remove it from the oven and set it aside to cool.

Step 2: Making the Lemon Filling

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and granulated sugar until well combined.
  2. Add the fresh lemon juice and lemon zest to the egg mixture, stirring until smooth.
  3. Sift in the almond flour and baking powder, then mix until the filling is silky and free of lumps.
  4. Pour the lemon filling over the cooled crust, spreading it out evenly.

Step 3: Baking the Bars

  1. Reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (160°C) and bake the bars for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.
  2. Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and allow the lemon bars to cool completely at room temperature.
  3. For an extra touch, dust the bars with powdered sugar before cutting them into squares.

Serving and Storing

These gluten-free lemon bars are best enjoyed chilled, so refrigerate them for at least 2 hours before serving. The tangy lemon flavor combined with the buttery crust creates a delightful contrast that will leave your taste buds craving more. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days for optimal freshness.

Final Thoughts

With their bright, citrusy flavor and gluten-free nature, these lemon bars are a crowd-pleaser for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a summer picnic, a brunch with friends, or simply satisfying your own sweet tooth, this recipe is a must-try. The balance of tartness and sweetness in every bite is a true testament to the magic of homemade desserts. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the delightful tang of these gluten-free lemon bars!
