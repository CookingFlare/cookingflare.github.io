Cooking with Fresh Rosemary: 7 Must-Try Recipes


Rosemary is one of the most fragrant and versatile herbs that can elevate any dish, whether it’s a simple roast chicken or a sophisticated lamb dish. Known for its robust flavor, rosemary can bring out rich, savory notes in many recipes, making it a must-have herb in your culinary arsenal. In this blog post, we’ll explore seven must-try recipes that showcase the versatility of fresh rosemary. Whether you’re an experienced home cook or just starting out, these dishes will inspire you to cook with this aromatic herb.

1. Rosemary Garlic Roasted Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper for easy cleanup.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the halved baby potatoes with olive oil, minced garlic, chopped rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the potatoes evenly on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are golden brown and crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Stir the potatoes halfway through cooking for even crispness.
  5. For a citrusy finish, drizzle with lemon juice before serving.

This dish is the perfect side for any meal and pairs wonderfully with roasted meats like chicken, beef, or lamb. The rosemary infuses the potatoes with its piney, earthy flavor, while the garlic adds a savory depth.

2. Rosemary Lemon Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken cubes, rosemary, lemon zest, olive oil, honey, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix until the chicken is evenly coated.
  2. Thread the chicken onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium heat. Grill the skewers for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is fully cooked and has nice grill marks.
  4. Serve the chicken skewers with a side of couscous, rice, or a fresh salad.

The combination of rosemary and lemon brings a refreshing, aromatic element to the chicken, making it a perfect dish for a summer cookout or a light dinner.

3. Rosemary and Parmesan Bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and chopped rosemary. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil, stirring until a dough forms.
  3. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and shape it into a round loaf. Place it on a parchment-lined baking sheet and let it rise for another 30 minutes.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Before baking, sprinkle the top of the loaf with Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake the bread for 25-30 minutes, or until golden brown. Let it cool before slicing.

This rosemary and Parmesan bread is perfect for dipping in olive oil or serving alongside soups, stews, or pasta dishes. The rosemary provides an aromatic undertone, while the Parmesan adds a savory richness.

4. Rosemary Infused Olive Oil

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 cloves garlic (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the olive oil over low heat.
  2. Add the rosemary sprigs and garlic (if using) and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, being careful not to overheat the oil.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the rosemary infuse for an additional 15 minutes.
  4. Strain the oil into a bottle or jar, discarding the rosemary sprigs and garlic.
  5. Optionally, add a pinch of chili flakes for a bit of heat.

This rosemary-infused olive oil can be used as a drizzle for vegetables, pasta, or salads. It also makes a great gift when stored in a decorative bottle. The rosemary flavor will infuse the oil with its rich and aromatic taste, making any dish it touches feel more special.

5. Rosemary and Honey Glazed Carrots

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound baby carrots
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, bring salted water to a boil. Add the carrots and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender but still crisp. Drain the carrots and set aside.
  2. In the same saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the rosemary and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the honey and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked carrots to the saucepan and toss to coat with the honey and rosemary glaze. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the glazed carrots as a side dish with roasted meats or a fresh salad.

The sweet honey and fragrant rosemary complement each other perfectly in this dish, creating a balance of savory and sweet that makes these carrots a standout side.

6. Rosemary-Infused Roast Lamb

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 pound leg of lamb
  • 4 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine the rosemary, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the rosemary mixture all over the lamb, ensuring it is well-coated.
  4. Place the lamb on a roasting rack in a roasting pan. Roast for 1 ½ - 2 hours, or until the lamb reaches your desired level of doneness.
  5. (Optional) Halfway through roasting, pour red wine into the roasting pan for added flavor.
  6. Let the lamb rest for 15-20 minutes before slicing and serving.

This rosemary-infused roast lamb is the perfect centerpiece for a special occasion or holiday dinner. The rosemary enhances the rich flavor of the lamb, while the garlic and lemon bring brightness and depth.

7. Rosemary and Blue Cheese Stuffed Mushrooms

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large cremini or white mushrooms, stems removed
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the cream cheese, chopped rosemary, blue cheese, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spoon the mixture into the mushroom caps, pressing gently to fill each one.
  4. Arrange the stuffed mushrooms on the baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the stuffing is golden.
  5. Serve as an appetizer or side dish.

These rosemary and blue cheese stuffed mushrooms are savory, creamy, and packed with flavor. The rosemary pairs beautifully with the tangy blue cheese, creating an unforgettable bite.

Conclusion

Fresh rosemary is a powerful herb that can enhance both simple and sophisticated dishes. From roasted vegetables to savory meats, these recipes will show you how to harness the full potential of rosemary in your cooking. Whether you’re making a fragrant bread, a honey-glazed side dish, or a succulent roast, rosemary will add depth and complexity to your meals. Give these recipes a try, and let this herb elevate your culinary creations.
