10 Delicious Preserves to Make for Winter


Preserving the vibrant flavors of summer and fall is one of the most satisfying ways to prepare for the colder months. Whether you’re savoring the taste of ripe fruits or capturing the spice of garden vegetables, homemade preserves bring warmth to winter days. With a simple mix of ingredients and a little patience, you can stock your pantry with jars of goodness that will elevate your meals and satisfy your cravings. Below are ten preserve recipes that combine practicality with joy, offering you a delightful culinary adventure this winter.

1. Classic Raspberry Jam

A timeless favorite, raspberry jam is the perfect way to repurpose freshly picked raspberries from summer. Its tangy sweetness is delightful smothered on toast or swirled into yogurt during chilly mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh raspberries
  • 2 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Combine raspberries and sugar in a large pot over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Add lemon juice and bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Allow the jam to simmer, occasionally stirring and skimming off any foam. This takes about 12-15 minutes, or until the consistency thickens.
  4. Pour the jam into sterilized jars, seal tightly, and let it cool.

2. Spiced Apple Butter

Rich and fragrant, spiced apple butter encapsulates the essence of autumn. It’s a versatile spread that pairs beautifully with scones or as a glaze for roasted meats.

Ingredients:

  • 5 lbs apples (peeled, cored, and sliced)
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Place apples in a slow cooker and simmer on low for about 6-8 hours.
  2. Stir in sugar, spices, and lemon juice, then cook for another hour.
  3. Blend the mixture until smooth and transfer to jars for storage.

3. Cranberry Orange Marmalade

Bright and zesty, cranberry orange marmalade is festive and perfect for winter celebrations. The bold citrus notes make it a great topping for pancakes or a filling in pastries.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups fresh cranberries
  • 2 oranges (zest and juice)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • ½ cup water

Instructions:

  1. Combine cranberries, orange juice, zest, sugar, and water in a saucepan.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Blend lightly for a chunky texture and pour into sterilized jars.

4. Pickled Beets

Earthy and slightly tangy, pickled beets are an excellent preserve to enjoy as a salad topper or side dish during winter.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium-sized beets
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • ¾ cup water
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander

Instructions:

  1. Boil beets for 30-40 minutes, then peel and slice thinly.
  2. Prepare brine by combining vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and coriander in a saucepan. Heat until dissolved.
  3. Layer beets into jars and pour hot brine over them. Seal jars, allow to cool, and refrigerate.

5. Carrot Cake Jam

Capture the essence of dessert in a jar with this unique carrot cake jam. It’s sweet, spiced, and creates true comfort when spread on toast or mixed with cream cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded carrots
  • 2 ½ cups crushed pineapple
  • ½ cup raisins
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 3 cups sugar
  • ½ box pectin

Instructions:

  1. Combine carrots, pineapple, raisins, and lemon juice in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add cinnamon and sugar, stirring constantly. Bring to a boil.
  3. Stir in pectin, cook for another 1-2 minutes, then transfer to jars and seal.

6. Garlic Herb Jelly

For lovers of savory preserves, garlic herb jelly is a standout. It pairs beautifully with cheeses and makes a welcome addition to charcuterie boards.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons crushed garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • 1 tablespoon thyme leaves
  • 3 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 box pectin

Instructions:

  1. Combine vinegar, garlic, herbs, and sugar in a saucepan. Heat the mixture until it reaches a rolling boil.
  2. Stir in pectin and boil for one additional minute.
  3. Strain if desired and pour into jars to set.

7. Spicy Tomato Relish

Tomato relish strikes a balance between sweet and spicy, making it a versatile addition to sandwiches, eggs, or roasted vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 5 cups chopped tomatoes
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup cider vinegar
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes
  • 2 teaspoons mustard seeds

Instructions:

  1. Heat a saucepan and cook tomatoes and onions until softened.
  2. Stir in vinegar, sugar, chili flakes, and mustard seeds. Simmer for about 40 minutes on low heat.
  3. Spoon relish into jars and let it cool before sealing.

8. Plum Chutney

Plum chutney is a tangy, slightly spicy preserve that pairs perfectly with meats, curries, or grain bowls.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups plums (pitted and chopped)
  • 1 cup red onion (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a large pot and bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat and simmer for approximately 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Transfer to jars while warm, seal, and store.

9. Lemon Curd

Tangy, creamy, and decadent, lemon curd is a preserve that feels like pure luxury. Enjoy it atop pancakes, tarts, or as a filling for cakes.

Ingredients:

  • 5 egg yolks
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (cubed)

Instructions:

  1. Whisk yolks, sugar, lemon juice, and zest in a saucepan over low heat.
  2. Add butter cubes gradually, stirring constantly for about 10 minutes until mixtures thickens.
  3. Strain if desired and pour curd into jars to chill.

10. Hot Pepper Jelly

For a kick of spice that warms cold days, hot pepper jelly adds depth to cheeses, crackers, and even grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 bell peppers (assorted colors)
  • 2 jalapeño peppers (seeded)
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 1 box pectin

Instructions:

  1. Finely chop peppers and combine them with vinegar and sugar in a pot. Bring to a boil.
  2. Stir in pectin and cook for 2-3 minutes more.
  3. Transfer to jars and let the jelly set for 24 hours.

Stocking up on homemade preserves transforms your winter pantry into an oasis of flavor and comfort. Each recipe connects you to the seasons and brings joy to mealtime, whether you’re stirring spiced apple butter into your oatmeal or adding zing to your cheeseboard with hot pepper jelly. These preserves are easy to make, satisfying to eat, and perfect for sharing with friends and family during the colder months.
