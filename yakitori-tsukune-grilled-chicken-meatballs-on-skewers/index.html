Yakitori Tsukune: Grilled Chicken Meatballs on Skewers

3 min · 582 words

If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve likely come across yakitori, a popular type of skewered chicken grilled to perfection. One of the most beloved variations of yakitori is Tsukune, flavorful chicken meatballs that are juicy on the inside and caramelized on the outside. Today, I’m excited to share with you a fantastic recipe for Yakitori Tsukune that will transport your taste buds to the streets of Tokyo.

Ingredients

For the meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground chicken
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

Additional:

  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground chicken, panko breadcrumbs, egg, scallions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sake, mirin, salt, and pepper. Mix the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.

  2. With damp hands, shape the chicken mixture into small meatballs, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter, and place them on a plate.

  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, thread the chicken meatballs onto the soaked bamboo skewers, about 3-4 meatballs per skewer.

  4. In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar for the sauce. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has completely dissolved. Remove from heat and set aside.

  5. Once the grill is hot, place the skewers on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until the meatballs are cooked through and nicely charred on the outside.

  6. While the meatballs are grilling, brush the sauce over the meatballs, turning them occasionally to ensure they are evenly coated with the delicious glaze.

  7. Once the meatballs are cooked through and glazed to perfection, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.

  8. Serve the Yakitori Tsukune hot, garnished with extra chopped scallions and a side of steamed rice or mixed greens for a complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicy kick, add a teaspoon of chili paste to the meatball mixture.
  • You can also pan-fry the meatballs if grilling is not an option.
  • Feel free to customize the sauce by adjusting the sweetness or saltiness to suit your taste.

Yakitori Tsukune is a delightful dish that captures the essence of Japanese street food culture. The juicy, succulent meatballs combined with the sweet and savory glaze make for a truly irresistible combination. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a taste of Japan, this recipe is sure to impress. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the flavors of Yakitori Tsukune!
