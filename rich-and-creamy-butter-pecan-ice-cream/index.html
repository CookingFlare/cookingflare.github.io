Rich and Creamy Butter Pecan Ice Cream


Indulge in the velvety goodness of homemade butter pecan ice cream that will leave your taste buds craving for more. This recipe combines the richness of butter, the crunchiness of toasted pecans, and the creaminess of a classic ice cream base. It’s a delightful treat that is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon butter extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chopped pecans, toasted

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ice Cream Base:

    • In a medium-sized saucepan, combine the heavy cream and whole milk over medium heat. Heat the mixture until it reaches a gentle simmer, stirring occasionally.
    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and egg yolks until well combined and slightly thickened.
    • Slowly pour about half of the heated cream mixture into the bowl with the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling.
    • Pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining cream mixture. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of the spoon, about 5-7 minutes. Do not let it boil.
    • Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the vanilla extract, butter extract, and salt. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Toast the Pecans:

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
    • Spread the chopped pecans on a baking sheet in a single layer.
    • Toast the pecans in the preheated oven for 8-10 minutes, or until fragrant and lightly browned.
    • Remove from the oven and let them cool completely.

  3. Churn the Ice Cream:

    • Once the ice cream base is chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
    • During the last few minutes of churning, add the toasted pecans and continue churning until evenly distributed.
    • Transfer the churned ice cream into a lidded container, making sure to spread the pecans throughout the mixture.
    • Place the container in the freezer for at least 4 hours, or until the ice cream is firm.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Once the ice cream is fully frozen, scoop it into bowls or cones.
    • For an extra touch of decadence, drizzle some warm caramel sauce over the top and sprinkle with additional toasted pecans.
    • Indulge in the rich and creamy butter pecan ice cream, savoring every spoonful of the velvety texture and the delightful crunch of the pecans.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, try adding a splash of bourbon or rum to the ice cream base before churning.
  • If you prefer a smoother texture, strain the chilled ice cream base through a fine-mesh sieve before churning to remove any lumps or cooked egg bits.
  • Customize your ice cream by adding in other mix-ins like chocolate chips or a swirl of caramel sauce during the last few minutes of churning.
  • Experiment with the level of toasting for the pecans to achieve your desired level of crunchiness and flavor.

Now you have a delicious recipe for rich and creamy butter pecan ice cream that will impress your friends and family. Whether enjoyed on its own or as a delightful addition to a warm slice of pie, this homemade treat is sure to become a favorite. Give it a try and indulge in the ultimate ice cream experience!
