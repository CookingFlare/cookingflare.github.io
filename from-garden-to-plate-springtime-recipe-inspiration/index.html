From Garden to Plate: Springtime Recipe Inspiration


Springtime signifies new beginnings and rejuvenation – a perfect season to revamp our culinary routines! Incorporate fresh, locally sourced ingredients, and enjoy the bounty of the season by cultivating your very own kitchen garden. Transform your garden’s harvest into delectable and wholesome meals with these simple yet mouthwatering recipes.

Fresh Asparagus Quiche

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 600g fresh asparagus, thinly sliced

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together milk, heavy cream, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Season with salt and black pepper.
  5. Fold in half of the Gruyère and feta cheese.
  6. Layer the sliced asparagus over the pie crust.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the asparagus.
  8. Sprinkle the remaining cheese over the top.
  9. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the center is set and the crust is golden.
  10. Allow to cool before slicing and serving.

This quiche pairs well with a simple mixed greens salad and a glass of white wine on a beautiful spring day.

Garden Herb Bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/4 cup fresh herbs (parsley, dill, basil, rosemary)
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
  2. Stir in the fresh herbs.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix warm water and olive oil.
  4. Gradually add the water mixture to the flour mixture, stirring until a soft dough forms.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth and elastic.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about an hour.
  7. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  8. Punch down the dough, shape into a loaf, and place it in a greased loaf pan.
  9. Allow the bread to rise again for about 30 minutes.
  10. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until golden brown.
  11. Allow the bread to cool before slicing and serving.

Serve this herbaceous bread alongside a hearty soup or as a versatile side for your springtime meals.

Easter Egg Nests with Carrot Tops Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked peas
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped carrot tops
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend peas and Parmesan together until a paste forms.
  2. Stir in the chopped carrot tops and basil.
  3. Stream in the olive oil, pulsing until combined.
  4. Season with salt and black pepper.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the mayonnaise and half of the pesto.
  6. Line a muffin tin with papers or foil.
  7. Spoon the pea pesto into the muffin cups, creating a nest-like shape.
  8. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

These edible nests are a whimsical and health-conscious addition to any springtime celebration.

As the days grow longer and the temperatures rise, our gardens come alive with vibrant produce, and our plates can reflect the colors of the season. Embrace the fleeting beauty of spring and savor these delicious and nutritious recipes that celebrate the harvest of your own kitchen garden. Happy cooking!
