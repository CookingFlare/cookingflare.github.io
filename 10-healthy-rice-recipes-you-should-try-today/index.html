10 Healthy Rice Recipes You Should Try Today


Rice is a versatile and nutritious staple found in many kitchens worldwide. Packed with essential nutrients, fiber, and a great source of energy, rice can be used in a variety of dishes. If you’re looking to add some healthy and exciting rice recipes to your meal plan, you’ve come to the right place. These ten recipes are not only delicious but also packed with vitamins, minerals, and other essential nutrients to fuel your body.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a range of healthy rice dishes that cater to different tastes and dietary preferences. From nutrient-rich brown rice bowls to plant-based stir-fries, there’s something for everyone. So, grab your apron and get ready to discover your new favorite rice recipes!

1. Quinoa & Brown Rice Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 cup brown rice
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup diced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup diced bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the dressing:

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water. In a medium pot, combine quinoa, brown rice, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 20 minutes or until the grains are tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, garlic, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa and brown rice, diced vegetables, red onion, and parsley. Toss everything together.
  4. Drizzle the lemon vinaigrette over the salad and toss to coat. Sprinkle with feta cheese if using. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

This quinoa and brown rice salad is full of healthy fats, fiber, and antioxidants from the vegetables, making it an excellent addition to any meal.

2. Cauliflower Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower, grated into rice-sized pieces
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce (or coconut aminos for a gluten-free option)
  • 1 tbsp green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add the diced carrots, peas, onion, and garlic. Cook for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  2. Add the cauliflower rice to the pan and stir well to combine. Cook for an additional 5 minutes until the cauliflower is tender and slightly crispy.
  3. Push the cauliflower mixture to the side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked and then mix them into the cauliflower rice.
  4. Stir in the soy sauce (or coconut aminos), green onions, salt, and pepper. Continue cooking for another 2-3 minutes, then serve hot.

This cauliflower fried rice is a great low-carb alternative to traditional fried rice and is packed with essential vitamins and fiber.

3. Lentil and Rice Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, tops removed and seeds discarded
  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup cooked lentils
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, lentils, onion, tomatoes, cumin, paprika, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff each bell pepper with the rice and lentil mixture, pressing down gently to pack the filling inside.
  4. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish. If desired, sprinkle with shredded cheese on top.
  5. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.
  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro before serving.

These lentil and rice stuffed bell peppers are a deliciously healthy option for lunch or dinner. Packed with plant-based protein, fiber, and vitamins, this dish is both satisfying and nutritious.

4. Chickpea & Spinach Rice Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp tahini (for dressing)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp paprika (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the basmati rice according to the package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chickpeas and spices (cumin, turmeric, garlic powder, salt, and pepper). Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chickpeas are slightly crispy.
  3. Add the chopped spinach to the pan and cook for another 2 minutes until wilted.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, and a little water to thin it out into a dressing.
  5. To assemble, place the cooked rice in a bowl, top with the chickpea and spinach mixture, and drizzle with tahini dressing. Sprinkle with paprika if desired.

This chickpea and spinach rice bowl is a powerhouse of protein, fiber, and vitamins, making it a satisfying and wholesome meal option for lunch or dinner.

5. Mango Chicken Rice Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the jasmine rice according to the package instructions and set aside.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then grill or pan-sear them until fully cooked, about 6-7 minutes per side. Slice the chicken into thin strips.
  3. In a small bowl, combine diced mango, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper to create a fresh mango salsa.
  4. To assemble, place the cooked rice in a bowl, top with the sliced chicken, and spoon the mango salsa on top. Serve immediately.

The combination of sweet mango and savory chicken over jasmine rice is a delightful treat. This dish is high in lean protein and antioxidants, making it an ideal choice for a healthy, flavorful meal.

6. Sweet Potato & Black Bean Rice Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown rice
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup avocado, diced
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the sweet potato cubes with olive oil, smoked paprika, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender.
  3. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, cook the brown rice according to the package instructions.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, roasted sweet potatoes, black beans, diced avocado, and lime juice. Toss gently to combine.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This sweet potato and black bean rice bowl is a nutrient-packed meal filled with fiber, vitamins, and healthy fats. It’s a perfect option for a vegan or vegetarian meal.

7. Vegetable Biryani with Brown Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups brown basmati rice
  • 1 tbsp ghee or olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1/2 cup chopped cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup chopped green beans
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat ghee or olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add the garlic, ginger, turmeric, cumin, cinnamon, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick. Cook for about 2 minutes to release the spices’ fragrance.
  3. Add the chopped vegetables (carrot, peas, cauliflower, and green beans) and sauté for another 5 minutes.
  4. Add the brown rice, salt, and water (about 3 cups). Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes or until the rice is cooked.
  5. Remove the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and bay leaf. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This vegetable biryani with brown rice is a fragrant, flavorful dish full of spices and healthy vegetables. It’s perfect for a comforting dinner that’s both nutritious and satisfying.

8. Zucchini & Mushroom Rice Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari (for gluten-free)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the jasmine rice according to the package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the zucchini, mushrooms, onion, and garlic. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Stir in the cooked rice, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 3-4 minutes, stirring frequently to combine all the flavors.
  4. Serve immediately as a light, healthy lunch or dinner option.

This zucchini and mushroom rice stir-fry is low in calories but high in flavor and nutrients, making it an ideal healthy rice dish.

9. Eggplant and Tomato Rice Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown rice
  • 2 medium eggplants, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil over medium heat until softened.
  3. Add the diced eggplant, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Cook for 7-10 minutes until the eggplant is tender.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes and cooked brown rice, then transfer the mixture to a greased casserole dish.
  5. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese if using, then bake for 20 minutes until heated through.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

This eggplant and tomato rice casserole is a comforting, hearty dish full of vitamins and minerals. It’s perfect for those who enjoy rich, savory flavors.

10. Spicy Thai Basil Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce (optional)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the jasmine rice according to the package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and chili, and sauté for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the cooked rice, soy sauce, fish sauce, and basil. Stir well to combine and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  4. Garnish with lime wedges and serve immediately.

This spicy Thai basil rice is a flavorful dish that brings heat, freshness, and umami to the table. It’s a great side dish or a quick main course.

Whether you’re looking for a light salad, a comforting casserole, or a hearty rice bowl, these recipes have you covered. Try them out today and enjoy the delicious flavors and health benefits rice can offer!
