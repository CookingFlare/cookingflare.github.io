Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells Recipe


There’s something magical about pasta stuffed with a creamy, cheesy filling and smothered in a rich, savory marinara sauce. Spinach and ricotta stuffed shells are one of those beloved dishes that feel both comforting and elegant, ideal for everything from family dinners to special gatherings. This recipe showcases the perfect marriage of tender jumbo pasta shells, fresh spinach, creamy ricotta, and a flavorful homemade sauce to tie it all together.

Not only does this dish wow with its flavors, but it’s also incredibly versatile and surprisingly easy to prepare. Whether you’re making it ahead for a stress-free weeknight meal or want to turn it into a crowd-pleasing vegetarian entrée, spinach and ricotta stuffed shells are bound to become a staple in your kitchen.

The Beauty of Spinach and Ricotta

Spinach and ricotta have long been culinary partners-in-crime, creating creamy, earthy flavors that complement pasta flawlessly. Ricotta brings a mild, creamy softness that’s essential for stuffing shells without overwhelming other flavors. Meanwhile, spinach balances the richness of the cheese with a bright, vegetal note—plus, it offers a boost of nutrients.

One of the key advantages of this pairing is its ability to integrate easily into dishes that feel hearty yet refined. Combined with a robust marinara sauce, spinach and ricotta become the stars that elevate the simple jumbo shell pasta into a restaurant-quality meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Gathering the ingredients beforehand will ensure a smooth cooking process. Here’s everything you’ll need to create your spinach and ricotta stuffed shells:

For the filling:

  • 1 box of jumbo pasta shells (about 8 oz.)
  • 2 cups fresh spinach (or 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg (optional, for depth of flavor)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the marinara sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 cups canned crushed tomatoes (or a jar of preferred marinara sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon dried basil
  • ½ teaspoon sugar (to cut acidity)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For assembly and topping:

  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep the Jumbo Shells

Start by cooking the pasta shells. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, then add the jumbo shells and cook them until they’re al dente—typically around 9 to 11 minutes, depending on the package instructions. Be careful not to overcook the shells, as they’ll continue to soften in the oven later.

Once cooked, drain the shells and spread them out on a baking sheet to cool. This prevents sticking and ensures they’re ready to be stuffed.

Step 2: Make the Filling

While the shells are cooling, prepare the spinach and ricotta mixture. For fresh spinach, sauté it in a small pan with a drizzle of olive oil until wilted, then set it aside to cool. If using frozen spinach, ensure it’s thoroughly thawed and squeezed of excess water.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, minced garlic, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Mix until smooth and creamy, then fold in the spinach, ensuring everything is evenly incorporated.

Step 3: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

A homemade marinara sauce elevates this dish, but if you’re short on time, you can use your favorite store-bought sauce.

To make the sauce from scratch, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant and softened. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, sugar, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce gently for 15 to 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Taste and adjust seasoning as needed, then remove from heat. Set the sauce aside while you assemble the shells.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Spread a generous layer of marinara sauce onto the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Stuff each cooled pasta shell with a heaping spoonful of the spinach and ricotta mixture, then nestle the stuffed shells into the sauce-filled dish close together. Repeat until all the shells are stuffed and arranged neatly in the dish.

Pour the remaining marinara sauce evenly over the shells, ensuring each one is coated. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan on top for a melty, golden finish.

Step 5: Bake to Perfection

Cover the baking dish loosely with foil and bake the shells in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. After this initial cook time, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes. This allows the cheese to melt fully and achieve a slight golden crust.

Remove the dish from the oven and allow it to cool for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil, if desired.

Tips for Success

Achieving perfect spinach and ricotta stuffed shells doesn’t have to be complicated. Here are some tips to simplify the process and ensure great results:

1. Choose Quality Cheese

Opt for high-quality ricotta and freshly grated Parmesan and mozzarella to enhance flavor and texture.

2. Avoid Overcooked Pasta

Cook the shells al dente to prevent them from becoming too soft when baked. Slightly firm shells are easier to stuff and hold their shape beautifully.

3. Make Ahead

Stuffed shells can be made ahead and refrigerated or frozen. If freezing, assemble the dish up to the baking step, then cover tightly and freeze. Bake straight from frozen, adding an additional 20 minutes to cooking time.

4. Customize the Filling

Feel free to experiment with add-ins! Some great options include crumbled cooked sausage, chopped mushrooms, or even fresh herbs like parsley or oregano.

Serving Suggestions

Spinach and ricotta stuffed shells pair wonderfully with a crisp green salad and a side of garlic bread. For a heartier meal, add roasted vegetables or a grilled protein like chicken or shrimp.

This dish also shines as the centerpiece of a buffet-style gathering. With vegetarian-friendly ingredients and crowd-pleasing flavors, it’s sure to impress guests.

Wrapping Up

Spinach and ricotta stuffed shells are a celebration of comfort food at its finest. From the creamy, savory filling to the vibrant marinara sauce that complements every bite, this recipe delivers a satisfying meal that’s rich in flavor, texture, and presentation.

Whether baked fresh or prepared ahead, this dish is a testament to the joy that comes from simple yet thoughtfully crafted ingredients. Perfect for family gatherings, casual dinners, or bringing something impressive to the table, spinach and ricotta stuffed shells are bound to become a regular feature in your culinary repertoire.

Make it tonight, or bookmark this recipe for your next gathering—it’s guaranteed to impress and bring smiles to the table!
