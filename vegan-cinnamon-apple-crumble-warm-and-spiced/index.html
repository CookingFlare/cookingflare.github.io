Vegan Cinnamon Apple Crumble: Warm and Spiced


As the autumn breeze whispers through the streets, the aroma of warm spices and caramelized apples fills the air, beckoning us to gather around the table. In this season of cozying up by the fire, there’s no better way to welcome the chilly evenings than with a warm and spiced vegan cinnamon apple crumble. This comforting dessert is a symphony of flavors and textures, with the sweetness of apples, the warmth of cinnamon, and the crunch of oat topping.

The Perfect Combination

The secret to this crumble’s success lies in the harmonious blend of ingredients. Tart Granny Smith apples are the star of the show, providing a delightful contrast to the sweetness of the cinnamon and sugar. The addition of a hint of nutmeg and cardamom adds depth and complexity to the flavor profile, while the crunchy oat topping provides a satisfying textural contrast.

The Recipe

To make this vegan cinnamon apple crumble, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 6-8 Granny Smith apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, cardamom, and salt. Toss until the apples are evenly coated with the dry ingredients.

In a separate bowl, mix together the rolled oats, melted vegan butter, and chopped walnuts (if using). Spread the mixture evenly over the apple filling, making sure to cover the entire surface.

Baking and Serving

Place the crumble in a 9x9-inch baking dish and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are tender. Remove the crumble from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve warm, topped with a dollop of coconut whipped cream or a scoop of your favorite vegan ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe more indulgent, drizzle with a caramel sauce made from dates and coconut cream.
  • Substitute the Granny Smith apples with other varieties, such as Honeycrisp or Fuji, for a sweeter flavor.
  • Add a sprinkle of sea salt on top of the crumble before baking for a touch of savory flavor.
  • Experiment with different spices, such as ginger or allspice, to create a unique flavor profile.

Conclusion

In this season of cozying up by the fire, there’s no better way to warm the heart and soul than with a warm and spiced vegan cinnamon apple crumble. This comforting dessert is a symphony of flavors and textures, with the sweetness of apples, the warmth of cinnamon, and the crunch of oat topping. With its simple ingredients and straightforward preparation, this recipe is sure to become a new favorite in your household. So go ahead, gather your loved ones around the table, and indulge in the warm and comforting goodness of this vegan cinnamon apple crumble.
