How to Make Labneh: A Creamy Middle Eastern Yogurt Recipe


Labneh is a luxurious and creamy Middle Eastern yogurt spread that has won the hearts of food lovers around the world. Known for its rich, tangy flavor and thick texture, labneh is versatile in both savory and sweet dishes. Whether served as a dip, spread, or side dish, it adds a delightful touch to your meals. If you’ve ever wondered how to make labneh from scratch, you’re in for a treat. In this recipe, we’ll guide you step-by-step through the process of making this traditional Middle Eastern delight at home.

What Is Labneh?

Labneh is a strained yogurt that originates from the Middle East, popular in countries such as Lebanon, Syria, and Palestine. It’s often used as a spread, dip, or condiment, and its smooth, creamy texture and tangy taste make it a beloved staple in Mediterranean cuisine. The process of making labneh involves draining regular yogurt to remove excess whey, resulting in a thick and luscious consistency.

While traditional labneh is made with cow’s milk, it’s also possible to make it with goat’s milk or sheep’s milk for a different flavor profile. Labneh is often served with a drizzle of olive oil, a sprinkle of za’atar, and a side of pita bread or vegetables. It’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in numerous ways, from appetizers to breakfast spreads.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make homemade labneh, you’ll need just a few simple ingredients:

  • Whole milk yogurt – You’ll want to use full-fat, plain yogurt for the best texture and flavor. Look for a good-quality, unsweetened yogurt that doesn’t contain added preservatives or artificial ingredients.
  • Salt – This helps enhance the flavor of the yogurt and aids in the straining process. Use a fine sea salt or kosher salt for a cleaner taste.
  • Olive oil (optional) – For serving, drizzle extra virgin olive oil on top for added richness.
  • Za’atar or herbs (optional) – Za’atar is a popular Middle Eastern spice blend that adds a wonderful earthy flavor to the labneh. You can also use fresh herbs such as thyme, parsley, or mint.
  • Pita bread or fresh vegetables – Labneh pairs perfectly with warm pita bread, cucumber slices, carrot sticks, or other fresh vegetables for dipping.

How to Make Labneh from Scratch

Making labneh at home is simple and requires minimal effort. While it does take some time to drain the yogurt, the hands-on portion of the process is quick and easy. Here’s a step-by-step guide to making labneh:

Step 1: Prepare the Yogurt

Start with good-quality plain yogurt. If you prefer a creamier and richer flavor, opt for whole milk yogurt, as it provides a thicker texture and fuller taste. For a tangier version, you can use yogurt that has a bit of a sour flavor, but for a smoother consistency, choose yogurt with a milder flavor.

Place the yogurt in a mixing bowl and stir it gently to break up any lumps and smooth it out.

Step 2: Add Salt

Once the yogurt is smooth, add a pinch of salt. The salt not only enhances the flavor but also helps with the draining process. Stir it in well to ensure it is evenly distributed throughout the yogurt. If you prefer a more pronounced flavor, you can add a little extra salt, but be mindful not to overdo it.

Step 3: Set Up for Straining

To strain the yogurt and make it thick and creamy, you will need a fine-mesh strainer, cheesecloth, or a clean kitchen towel. Set the strainer over a large bowl, ensuring there’s enough space between the strainer and the bowl for the whey to drain.

Place a piece of cheesecloth over the strainer, folding the edges to form a loose pouch. You can also use a clean dish towel instead of cheesecloth if you don’t have one. Pour the salted yogurt into the cheesecloth-lined strainer.

Step 4: Let the Yogurt Drain

Now, the straining process begins. Allow the yogurt to drain in the refrigerator for several hours or overnight. The longer you let it drain, the thicker the labneh will be. For a creamy and spreadable consistency, drain the yogurt for about 12 to 24 hours. If you prefer a firmer, more cheese-like texture, leave it for up to 48 hours. The excess whey will slowly drip out, leaving you with the desired thick consistency.

If you notice that the cheesecloth or towel is collecting too much liquid, you may want to gently wring it out to speed up the process. Make sure to check the consistency regularly to ensure it’s as thick as you like.

Step 5: Transfer the Labneh

Once the yogurt has drained and reached the consistency you desire, carefully remove the labneh from the cheesecloth or towel. Transfer it into a clean bowl. At this point, the labneh will have a thick, creamy texture that’s perfect for spreading or serving as a dip.

If you find that the labneh is still too thick or dry, you can mix in a little bit of milk or water to loosen it up to your preferred consistency.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Labneh is best enjoyed fresh, but it can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week. To serve, drizzle a little olive oil over the top and sprinkle with your choice of herbs or spices. Za’atar is a popular seasoning for labneh and adds a wonderful aromatic and herbal flavor. You can also sprinkle some fresh herbs like mint, thyme, or parsley for a refreshing twist.

Labneh can be served as a dip with pita bread or fresh vegetables like cucumber, carrot sticks, or cherry tomatoes. It can also be spread on toast or used as a topping for salads and roasted vegetables. For a more indulgent treat, labneh can even be paired with honey or fruit for a sweet snack or dessert.

Variations and Flavor Ideas

While the classic labneh recipe is simple and delicious, you can experiment with different flavors and add-ins to create your own variations. Here are a few ideas:

  • Garlic Labneh: Add a few crushed garlic cloves to the yogurt before straining to infuse the labneh with a savory, aromatic flavor. Garlic labneh pairs wonderfully with grilled meats or roasted vegetables.
  • Herbed Labneh: Mix in fresh herbs like dill, mint, or basil to give the labneh a refreshing herbal flavor. This variation is perfect for serving alongside mezze platters or Mediterranean-inspired dishes.
  • Lemon Labneh: For a zesty twist, mix in lemon zest or a few teaspoons of fresh lemon juice before straining. The bright citrus flavor complements the tanginess of the labneh.
  • Spicy Labneh: Add a dash of chili flakes, cayenne pepper, or a small amount of hot sauce for a spicy kick. This variation is great for those who enjoy a bit of heat with their dip.
  • Sweet Labneh: Drizzle honey over the top and add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg for a sweet take on labneh. Serve it as a dessert or with fresh fruit for a delightful treat.

Storing Labneh

Homemade labneh can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week. If you’ve made a large batch, you can portion it into smaller containers for easy access. To preserve the flavor, it’s best to consume it within a few days of making it.

To keep the labneh moist and fresh, you can drizzle a bit of olive oil over the surface before sealing the container. The olive oil helps to preserve the texture and flavor, keeping the labneh creamy and smooth.

Health Benefits of Labneh

Labneh is not only delicious, but it also offers several health benefits. Since it’s made from yogurt, it’s packed with probiotics, which support a healthy digestive system. The fermentation process involved in making yogurt also makes labneh easier to digest for those who are lactose intolerant.

Labneh is rich in protein, calcium, and healthy fats, making it a satisfying addition to your diet. It can help promote strong bones, boost immunity, and provide sustained energy throughout the day.

Conclusion

Making labneh at home is a simple yet rewarding process. With just a few ingredients and a bit of patience, you can create a creamy, tangy yogurt spread that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you serve it as a dip, spread, or topping, labneh adds a touch of Middle Eastern flair to your meals. With endless possibilities for flavor variations, labneh is a versatile and delicious dish that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Try making it yourself and discover the joys of this creamy, flavorful delight!
