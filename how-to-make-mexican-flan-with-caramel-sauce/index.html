How to Make Mexican Flan with Caramel Sauce


If you’re a fan of rich, creamy desserts with a touch of sweetness, then Mexican flan with caramel sauce is a must-try. This classic dessert is a staple in Mexican cuisine, known for its smooth custard and decadent caramel topping. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating this indulgent treat right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Flan:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Caramel Sauce:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Making the Caramel Sauce:

  1. Start by making the caramel sauce. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water over medium heat. Stir gently until the sugar dissolves.

  2. Once the sugar has dissolved, stop stirring and allow the mixture to come to a boil. Let it boil, without stirring, until it turns a deep amber color. This should take about 10-12 minutes.

  3. As soon as the caramel reaches the desired color, remove the saucepan from the heat and carefully pour in the heavy cream. Be cautious as the mixture will bubble up.

  4. Stir the caramel gently until the cream is fully incorporated. Then, stir in the vanilla extract. Set the caramel sauce aside to cool while you prepare the flan mixture.

Making the Flan:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place a large, deep baking dish into the oven and fill it with about 1 inch of water. This will be your water bath for baking the flan.

  2. In a separate saucepan, heat the 1 cup of granulated sugar over medium heat. Stir constantly until the sugar melts and turns into a golden-brown caramel.

  3. Once the caramel is ready, carefully pour it into the bottom of a 9-inch round cake pan, tilting the pan to ensure the bottom is evenly coated. Set the pan aside to allow the caramel to harden.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  5. Pour the flan mixture over the hardened caramel in the cake pan.

  6. Carefully transfer the filled cake pan into the water bath in the preheated oven. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set, but the center still jiggles slightly when gently shaken.

  7. Once the flan is baked, remove it from the oven and the water bath. Allow it to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight.

  8. When ready to serve, run a knife around the edge of the cake pan to loosen the flan. Place a serving plate over the pan and quickly invert it to release the flan with the caramel sauce on top.

Serving and Enjoying

Serve the Mexican flan with caramel sauce chilled, allowing the silky smooth texture and rich caramel flavor to shine. This dessert pairs beautifully with a cup of freshly brewed coffee or a drizzle of extra caramel sauce for an added touch of indulgence.

Now that you have this authentic Mexican flan recipe in your repertoire, you can impress your friends and family with this delightful dessert. Bon appétit!
