Savory Thanksgiving Quinoa Recipes for a Healthy Meal


Quinoa, a versatile and nutritious grain, has become increasingly popular in recent years. With its high protein content and numerous health benefits, it’s no wonder that quinoa has become a staple in many kitchens around the world. This Thanksgiving, why not incorporate quinoa into your holiday feast? In this blog post, we will introduce you to three savory Thanksgiving quinoa recipes that are not only delicious but also perfect for a healthy meal.

1. Quinoa Stuffed Acorn Squash

This quinoa stuffed acorn squash recipe is a delightful combination of flavors and textures. The sweetness of the acorn squash, the nuttiness of the quinoa, and the earthiness of the herbs and spices create a symphony of tastes in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squashes, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side down, and roast them in the oven for about 30-40 minutes or until tender.
  3. In the meantime, rinse the quinoa under cold water.
  4. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
  5. Add the rinsed quinoa to the saucepan and toast it for a couple of minutes.
  6. Pour in the vegetable broth and add the dried thyme and sage. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Once the acorn squash halves are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly.
  8. Fill each acorn squash half with a generous amount of the cooked quinoa mixture.
  9. Return the stuffed acorn squash halves to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  10. Serve warm and enjoy!

2. Quinoa and Mushroom Pilaf

This quinoa and mushroom pilaf is a hearty and flavorful side dish that pairs well with any Thanksgiving main course. The combination of earthy mushrooms, aromatic herbs, and fluffy quinoa creates a dish that will impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and minced garlic to the saucepan and cook until the mushrooms release their moisture and become tender.
  4. Add the rinsed quinoa to the saucepan and toast it for a couple of minutes.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth and add the dried thyme and rosemary. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the quinoa and mushroom pilaf as a side dish alongside your Thanksgiving main course.

3. Quinoa and Kale Salad

This quinoa and kale salad is a refreshing and nutritious addition to your Thanksgiving table. Packed with vitamins and antioxidants, this salad will add a burst of color and flavor to your holiday spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 4 cups kale, stems removed and leaves thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • For the dressing:
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
    • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the vegetable broth over medium heat until it comes to a boil. Add the rinsed quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, sliced kale, cherry tomatoes, dried cranberries, crumbled feta cheese, and chopped walnuts.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the quinoa and kale mixture and toss to combine.
  6. Let the salad sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the quinoa and kale salad as a refreshing side dish or as a light main course.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, surprise your friends and family with these savory quinoa recipes that are not only delicious but also nutritious. Whether you choose the quinoa stuffed acorn squash, quinoa and mushroom pilaf, or quinoa and kale salad, these dishes will add a healthy twist to your holiday feast. Enjoy the flavors and the benefits of quinoa while creating a memorable Thanksgiving meal. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
