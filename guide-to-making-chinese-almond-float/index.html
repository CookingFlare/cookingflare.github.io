Guide to Making Chinese Almond Float


Chinese Almond Float is a beloved dessert that combines delicate almond-flavored jelly with syrupy sweetness. This delightful dish is commonly served in Chinese restaurants, often as a refreshing end to a satisfying meal. Whether you’ve enjoyed it at a restaurant or discovered it while browsing through Asian food blogs, the good news is that it’s quite simple to prepare at home. This guide will take you through the process of making the perfect Chinese Almond Float, explaining every step in detail, from selecting ingredients to serving the dessert in a traditional, restaurant-style presentation.

What Is Chinese Almond Float?

Chinese Almond Float, also known as “杏仁豆腐” (Xìng rén dòufu), is a traditional Chinese dessert made from almond-flavored jelly. Contrary to what the name might suggest, it does not contain actual almonds in the form of chunks or pieces. Instead, the “almond” flavor is derived from almond extract, which gives it that distinctive, delicate taste. The jelly texture is soft and smooth, providing a gentle contrast to the sweet, syrupy base.

In many Chinese households, Almond Float is served as a sweet finish to a large meal, especially after consuming savory dishes. It’s particularly popular during festivals and special gatherings. While the dessert is generally made using agar-agar or gelatin, the texture is often more wobbly and tender than traditional gelatin-based jelly.

One of the key characteristics of this dish is the addition of fruit cocktail or lychee, which enhances its refreshing nature. The fruit complements the almond jelly perfectly, creating a delightful balance of flavors.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Making Chinese Almond Float at home requires only a few ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Almond powder: This is the base for the almond flavor. It’s available in most Asian supermarkets or online, and can be found as “almond meal” or “sweet almond powder.”
  • Agar-agar powder: This is a natural gelling agent that helps the almond jelly set. You can find it in most Asian or health food stores, and it’s often used in many traditional Asian desserts.
  • Sugar: For sweetness, you’ll need sugar. Depending on your preference, you can adjust the amount of sugar to your liking. Traditional recipes call for regular granulated sugar, but you can experiment with alternatives like rock sugar or honey for unique variations.
  • Almond extract: This is the key to giving the dessert its almond flavor. Make sure you use almond extract, not almond essence, for the best results.
  • Milk: While some recipes may use water for the base of the jelly, milk provides a creamier and richer texture to the dessert.
  • Fruit cocktail or lychee: This is typically served alongside the almond jelly. Fruit cocktail is often used, but fresh or canned lychee can be an excellent alternative for a more luxurious taste.
  • Water: Used in combination with the milk to dissolve the agar-agar and almond powder.

Now that you know what you’ll need, let’s walk through the step-by-step process to make Chinese Almond Float at home.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Chinese Almond Float

Step 1: Preparing the Almond Jelly Mixture

The first thing you need to do is create the base for your almond jelly. Start by mixing 1/4 cup of almond powder with 1/4 cup of water. Whisk this mixture together until the almond powder is dissolved. It’s important to get rid of any lumps to ensure a smooth texture for your jelly.

In a saucepan, combine 1 1/2 cups of water with 1/2 cup of milk. This combination provides a balance of creaminess and lightness. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat. Once the liquid starts heating up, stir in the dissolved almond powder mixture.

Once the almond mixture is well incorporated into the liquid, add 2 teaspoons of agar-agar powder. Agar-agar is a natural gelling agent that sets at room temperature, unlike gelatin, which needs to be refrigerated to set. Stir the mixture well to ensure that the agar-agar is completely dissolved.

Step 2: Sweetening the Jelly

Next, add sugar to sweeten the jelly. You can use about 1/3 cup of sugar, but feel free to adjust according to your preference. Stir continuously while heating the mixture until the sugar is completely dissolved. The key here is to make sure the sweetness is just right, as the almond flavor should be the star of the show, not the sugar.

Once the sugar has dissolved, bring the mixture to a boil and let it simmer for about 5 minutes, ensuring that the agar-agar is fully activated. This will help create a smooth and firm texture once it sets.

Step 3: Adding Almond Extract

Now it’s time to add the almond extract, which gives this dessert its signature flavor. Add about 1 teaspoon of almond extract to the mixture. Stir well to ensure the extract is evenly distributed throughout the liquid. Be careful not to add too much almond extract, as it can overpower the delicate flavor of the dessert.

Once the almond extract is fully incorporated, remove the saucepan from the heat. The mixture should now have a lovely almond fragrance, which is a key part of this dessert’s appeal.

Step 4: Pouring the Jelly Mixture into Molds

After the almond jelly mixture is ready, it’s time to pour it into molds. You can use individual molds or a larger pan, depending on how you want to serve the dessert. Pour the mixture into the molds carefully, ensuring that it’s evenly distributed.

Allow the almond jelly to cool at room temperature for about 10 to 15 minutes. Once it’s cool enough, place the molds in the refrigerator and let the jelly set for at least 2 hours, or until it has firmed up completely. The gelatinous consistency will depend on how much agar-agar you use, but it should have a smooth, soft, and wobbly texture once it’s done.

Step 5: Preparing the Fruit

While the almond jelly is setting, it’s time to prepare the fruit that will accompany it. If you’re using canned fruit cocktail, drain the syrup and rinse the fruit gently. Fruit cocktail typically includes peaches, pears, pineapples, and cherries, providing a colorful and juicy contrast to the smooth jelly.

Alternatively, if you’re using lychee, peel and pit the fruit, cutting them into small pieces. Lychee adds a refreshing sweetness that pairs beautifully with the almond flavor, making it a great option for a more exotic twist.

Step 6: Assembling the Chinese Almond Float

Once the almond jelly has set and the fruit is ready, it’s time to assemble the dessert. If you used a larger pan for the jelly, you’ll need to cut it into bite-sized cubes. For individual molds, simply invert them onto serving plates, and the jelly should slide out easily.

Place a few pieces of the fruit cocktail or lychee around the jelly on the plate. The fruit adds a touch of sweetness and provides an eye-catching presentation.

Step 7: Serving the Dessert

Finally, it’s time to serve the Chinese Almond Float. Traditionally, the dish is served with a light sugar syrup or sweetened condensed milk poured over the top. If you prefer a lighter syrup, you can mix 1/4 cup of sugar with 1/4 cup of water, heating it until the sugar dissolves.

For an extra touch, garnish the dessert with a few mint leaves or a drizzle of condensed milk. If you’re serving the dessert during a special occasion, feel free to use a decorative bowl or glass to elevate the presentation.

Chinese Almond Float is a versatile dessert. You can enjoy it chilled on a hot day or serve it as a refreshing treat after a hearty meal. Its subtle almond flavor combined with the natural sweetness of fruit makes it an ideal dish to satisfy any sweet tooth.

Tips for Making the Perfect Chinese Almond Float

  • Adjust sweetness to taste: If you prefer a less sweet dessert, reduce the amount of sugar or opt for a sugar substitute.
  • Use fresh fruit: If you can get your hands on fresh lychee or other tropical fruits, they will add an extra level of freshness to the dish.
  • Experiment with flavorings: While almond extract is the traditional choice, you can experiment with adding vanilla or coconut extract to give the dessert a new twist.
  • Serve chilled: The jelly will have a firmer texture when chilled, so it’s best to serve the dessert after it has been in the refrigerator for a couple of hours.

Conclusion

Chinese Almond Float is a simple yet elegant dessert that brings the perfect balance of texture and flavor. Its smooth, wobbly almond jelly combined with sweet, juicy fruits creates a refreshing treat that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re new to Chinese desserts or a long-time fan, making this dish at home is easy, rewarding, and sure to impress your guests. With just a few ingredients and some patience, you can recreate the beloved dessert in your own kitchen.
