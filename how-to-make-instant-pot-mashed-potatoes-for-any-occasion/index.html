How to Make Instant Pot Mashed Potatoes for Any Occasion


Mashed potatoes hold a special place in the culinary heart, complementing everything from a slow-roasted turkey on Thanksgiving to a hearty Sunday pot roast. While the traditional stovetop method has long been a trusted way to prepare this comfort food, the Instant Pot has ushered in a quicker, more convenient alternative that delivers creamy, fluffy mashed potatoes every time. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just treating yourself, this Instant Pot recipe guarantees delicious results with minimal effort. Let’s dive in and explore the step-by-step process for making perfect mashed potatoes with your trusty Instant Pot and some simple ingredients.

Why Use an Instant Pot for Mashed Potatoes?

Before we get into the recipe, it’s important to understand why the Instant Pot excels at making mashed potatoes. This versatile kitchen gadget employs high-pressure steam to cook potatoes evenly, retaining their natural flavors and nutrients in the process. Unlike boiling potatoes on the stove, which often leads to waterlogged tubers if overcooked, the Instant Pot ensures consistent texture without sacrificing flavor. Additionally, the hands-free nature of pressure cooking allows you to focus on other dishes or simply relax while your potatoes cook to perfection. It’s an ideal solution for busy home cooks seeking efficiency without compromising quality.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Mashed Potatoes

These are all the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 2–3 pounds of potatoes (Yukon Gold for creaminess or Russet for fluffiness)
  • 1 cup of liquid (water, chicken broth, or vegetable broth for added flavor)
  • 4–6 tablespoons of unsalted butter (adjust to taste)
  • 1/3–1/2 cup of milk or heavy cream (for desired creaminess)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Optional: Garlic cloves, sour cream, or chopped fresh herbs (like parsley or chives) for extra flavor.

Feel free to scale up or modify quantities depending on how many servings you need. This recipe will comfortably serve 4–6 people.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Instant Pot Mashed Potatoes

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes

Start by peeling the potatoes if you prefer smooth and creamy mashed potatoes. For a rustic texture, leave the skins on—especially if using Yukon Golds, as their thinner skin blends well when mashed. Once peeled (or unpeeled), chop the potatoes into uniform chunks, roughly 1–2 inches in size. This ensures even cooking and prevents some pieces from turning mushy before others are fully done.

Step 2: Add Potatoes and Liquid to the Instant Pot

Place the chopped potatoes into the stainless-steel liner of your Instant Pot. Add one cup of liquid—water, chicken broth, or vegetable broth—depending on your flavor preference. Broth adds subtle depth to the mashed potatoes, but water works perfectly fine if simplicity is your goal.

Step 3: Pressure Cook

Seal the lid of your Instant Pot and set the valve to the “Sealing” position. Select the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” setting and cook the potatoes on high pressure for 8 minutes. Remember, the Instant Pot requires a few minutes to reach full pressure, so factor this into your timing.

Once the timer completes, perform a quick release by carefully shifting the valve to the “Venting” position. Steam will escape rapidly, so use a towel or wooden utensil to avoid burns.

Step 4: Drain and Mash

Carefully remove the lid once the pressure pin drops. Check if the potatoes are fork-tender by gently piercing a few chunks. Drain any remaining liquid, if necessary, using a colander or leave it in if you want extra moisture in your mashed potatoes. Return the potatoes to the Instant Pot liner.

Step 5: Add Butter and Mash

While the potatoes are still hot, add 4–6 tablespoons of unsalted butter. Use a potato masher or a hand mixer to begin mashing. The heat will help melt the butter into the potatoes evenly, creating a smooth base. For ultra-creamy mashed potatoes, opt for a ricer or food mill.

Step 6: Incorporate Milk or Cream

Slowly pour in your milk or heavy cream, starting with 1/3 cup. Mix thoroughly, adding more liquid as needed to achieve your desired consistency. For tangy richness, you can also mix in a dollop of sour cream.

Step 7: Season to Taste

Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. This step ensures your mashed potatoes are flavorful and well-balanced. Taste frequently as you mix to fine-tune the seasoning. If you want to take things up a notch, consider blending roasted garlic, Parmesan cheese, or fresh herbs into the mix. Fold in these extras carefully to maintain the texture.

Tips for Perfect Instant Pot Mashed Potatoes

  1. Choose the Right Potato Variety: Yukon Gold potatoes offer a buttery flavor and naturally creamy texture, while Russets are starchier and yield fluffier mash. Both work well depending on your preference.

  2. Don’t Overmix: Once the potatoes are mashed, mix in additional ingredients gently. Overmixing can activate the starches in potatoes, making them gluey.

  3. Keep Warm Before Serving: If you’re not serving immediately, use your Instant Pot’s “Keep Warm” feature to hold the mashed potatoes at the perfect temperature without drying them out.

  4. Customize for the Occasion: For a casual family dinner, stick with classic mashed potatoes. For holiday meals or special gatherings, elevate them with add-ins like truffle oil, chives, or cream cheese.

Serving Suggestions

Instant Pot mashed potatoes are a versatile side dish that pairs beautifully with nearly any protein. Serve them alongside roast beef, grilled chicken, glazed ham, or even seared salmon. You can also spoon them over shepherd’s pie or slather them on a plate with rich gravy. For lighter fare, pair them with steamed vegetables or fresh green salad to balance their indulgent creaminess. No matter the occasion, these mashed potatoes will be the star of the plate.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have leftover mashed potatoes, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, gently warm them in a saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of milk or broth to bring them back to their original creamy texture. For larger quantities, consider reheating them in the oven at 350°F, covered with foil, until warmed through.

Mashed potatoes are more than just a side—they’re a staple dish that evokes comfort, joy, and connection. Using the Instant Pot not only simplifies the preparation but also guarantees a consistent and delectable outcome. Whether you’re crafting a special holiday menu or whipping up a quick weeknight dinner, this Instant Pot mashed potatoes recipe is a foolproof option that belongs in every home cook’s repertoire. Enjoy experimenting, sharing, and savoring this timeless classic!
