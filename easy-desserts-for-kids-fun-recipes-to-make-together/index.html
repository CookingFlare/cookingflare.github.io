Easy Desserts for Kids: Fun Recipes to Make Together


When it comes to creating lasting memories with your little ones, nothing beats spending quality time together in the kitchen. One of the best ways to bond with your children is by making fun and simple desserts that everyone can enjoy. Not only do these sweet treats taste amazing, but they also offer an opportunity for kids to get involved, learn new skills, and feel a sense of accomplishment.

If you’re looking for easy, kid-friendly dessert ideas to whip up together, this list has got you covered. From no-bake options to baked goods that will have your kitchen smelling heavenly, here are some delightful dessert recipes to enjoy with your children.

1. Fun Cookie Decorating

One of the easiest and most enjoyable desserts for kids is to make cookies and let them get creative with decorating. The best part? You don’t need to make complicated recipes. Simple sugar cookies are perfect for decorating, and the toppings can be as colorful and fun as your child’s imagination.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Assorted toppings (sprinkles, colored sugar, mini chocolate chips, icing, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In another bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg and vanilla extract, and mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until a dough forms.
  5. Roll the dough into balls (about 1 inch in size) and flatten them slightly on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are golden.
  7. Once the cookies are cool, let the kids decorate them using icing, sprinkles, or any other fun toppings. The possibilities are endless!

This recipe is perfect for children who enjoy expressing themselves creatively. It’s also a great opportunity to teach them about different colors, shapes, and textures. Plus, they get to eat their artwork once they’re done!

2. No-Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Bars

If you’re in the mood for something sweet and savory, these no-bake chocolate peanut butter bars are a perfect treat. This recipe is incredibly simple, and kids will love helping out with each step.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the peanut butter, powdered sugar, and graham cracker crumbs until well combined.
  2. Press the mixture into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch pan, creating an even layer.
  3. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the chocolate chips and butter together in 30-second intervals, stirring after each interval until smooth.
  4. Pour the melted chocolate mixture over the peanut butter layer, spreading it evenly with a spatula.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until the bars are firm and set.
  6. Cut into squares and serve.

These bars are perfect for kids to help with. They can mix, press, and even drizzle the chocolate topping. Not only is this a no-bake dessert, but it’s also a great way to introduce children to the concept of layering flavors and textures.

3. Fruity Frozen Yogurt Bark

A healthier dessert option that kids will love is fruity frozen yogurt bark. This fun treat is not only easy to make but also packed with delicious fruits and creamy yogurt. You can customize the toppings based on what your kids enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups plain or vanilla Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • 1 cup mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup granola
  • 1/4 cup mini chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Spread the yogurt evenly across the baking sheet, creating a thick layer.
  3. Drizzle honey or maple syrup on top if you’d like a touch of sweetness.
  4. Scatter the mixed berries, granola, and chocolate chips (if using) evenly over the yogurt.
  5. Freeze for at least 2-3 hours or until the bark is completely frozen.
  6. Break the bark into pieces and serve.

This frozen yogurt bark is a fun, hands-on dessert for kids to help with. They can sprinkle the fruit and granola on top, making it a creative, interactive snack. It’s perfect for hot days or as a refreshing treat after school.

4. Mini Cupcakes with Whipped Cream Frosting

Cupcakes are a classic dessert that everyone loves, and mini cupcakes are perfect for little hands. These tiny treats are easy to make and offer endless opportunities for decorating with frosting and toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk

For the frosting:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a mini muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a larger bowl, cream the butter and sugar together until fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk. Mix until just combined.
  5. Fill each cupcake liner about 2/3 full with batter.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. While the cupcakes are cooling, whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  8. Once the cupcakes have cooled, pipe the whipped cream frosting on top and decorate with sprinkles or any other fun toppings.

These mini cupcakes are a hit for birthday parties or just as a fun afternoon project. The kids can help fill the cupcake liners, frost the cupcakes, and add sprinkles or candies to make each one unique.

5. Banana Ice Cream

No need for an ice cream maker or long freezing times—banana ice cream is a simple, healthy, and delicious treat to make with kids. You can mix in your favorite flavors or toppings to make it your own!

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 1/4 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional mix-ins: chocolate chips, peanut butter, berries, or crushed nuts

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice the bananas into small pieces. Place the banana slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze for at least 2 hours.
  2. Once frozen, blend the bananas in a food processor or blender until smooth, adding the milk and vanilla extract to help the mixture come together.
  3. If you’d like to add mix-ins, such as chocolate chips or peanut butter, pulse them in after the ice cream has become smooth.
  4. Serve immediately for a soft-serve texture, or freeze for another 30 minutes for a firmer consistency.

Banana ice cream is a fantastic option for kids who love creamy desserts but need a healthier alternative. It’s also a great way to use up ripe bananas, and kids will enjoy helping you blend and add their favorite flavors.

6. Rice Krispies Treats with a Twist

Rice Krispies treats are a classic dessert, but why not take them up a notch by adding some fun mix-ins or toppings? These crispy, marshmallow treats are easy to make and even easier to enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 package (10 oz) mini marshmallows
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Optional mix-ins: M&Ms, chocolate chips, sprinkles, pretzels, etc.

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the marshmallows and stir until completely melted.
  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the Rice Krispies cereal into the marshmallow mixture and stir until evenly coated.
  4. Press the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch pan, making sure it’s packed down firmly.
  5. Allow the treats to cool for 30 minutes before cutting them into squares.
  6. For extra fun, add your favorite mix-ins while the mixture is still warm, or top the treats with sprinkles once they’ve set.

Rice Krispies treats are incredibly versatile and offer a fun way for kids to help out in the kitchen. Let them stir, press the mixture into the pan, and choose the toppings that will make each batch uniquely theirs.

Conclusion

Making desserts with your children is not only a fun way to spend time together but also a chance to teach them valuable skills in the kitchen. Whether you’re making cookies, cupcakes, or even healthy frozen treats, these easy recipes will inspire you to create delicious memories with your little ones. The best part is, you don’t need fancy ingredients or complicated instructions to make something delightful—just a bit of creativity and a lot of love. Happy baking!
