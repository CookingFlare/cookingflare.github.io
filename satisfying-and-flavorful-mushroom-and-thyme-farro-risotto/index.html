Satisfying and Flavorful Mushroom and Thyme Farro Risotto


If you’re looking for a hearty, satisfying, and flavorful dish that’s perfect for both weeknight dinners and special occasions, then this Mushroom and Thyme Farro Risotto is just what you need. This dish is a delightful twist on the classic Italian risotto, featuring earthy mushrooms, aromatic thyme, and nutty farro. Not only is it delicious, but it’s also packed with nutrients and can be customized to suit your taste preferences. Let’s dive into the details of this mouthwatering recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup farro
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 10 ounces mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, or oyster), sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme sprigs for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable or chicken broth to a simmer. Once simmering, reduce the heat to low to keep it warm.

  2. In a separate large, deep skillet or pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they are golden brown and have released their moisture, about 8-10 minutes. Stir in the fresh thyme leaves and cook for another minute to let the flavors meld.

  4. Stir in the farro and cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing it to toast slightly. Pour in the white wine and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid has been absorbed.

  5. Begin adding the warm broth to the skillet, one ladleful at a time, stirring frequently and allowing the farro to absorb the liquid before adding more. Continue this process until the farro is tender and has a creamy consistency, which should take about 30-35 minutes.

  6. Once the farro is cooked to your desired consistency, stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and black pepper to taste.

  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and let the risotto rest for a few minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld and the texture to become even creamier.

  8. Serve the Mushroom and Thyme Farro Risotto in individual bowls, garnished with fresh thyme sprigs for a pop of color and added aroma.

Customization Tips

  • If you’re a fan of a more intense mushroom flavor, consider adding a splash of mushroom broth or a small amount of dried porcini mushrooms to the cooking liquid.
  • For a dairy-free version, simply omit the Parmesan cheese or substitute it with a sprinkle of nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of mushrooms to create your own unique flavor profile.

Final Thoughts

This Mushroom and Thyme Farro Risotto is a delightful and wholesome dish that brings together the earthy flavors of mushrooms, the aromatic essence of thyme, and the nuttiness of farro into one satisfying meal. Whether you’re cooking for a cozy dinner at home or entertaining guests, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy. Embrace the process of making risotto, enjoy the aromas that fill your kitchen, and savor every spoonful of this comforting dish. Bon appétit!
