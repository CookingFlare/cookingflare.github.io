Homemade Raspberry Swirl Cheesecake Bars: A Creamy and Fruity Delight


If you’re a fan of cheesecake and love the sweet and tangy flavor of raspberries, then you’re in for a treat with these homemade raspberry swirl cheesecake bars. This delightful dessert combines the rich creaminess of cheesecake with a burst of fruity goodness from fresh raspberries. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply indulging in a sweet craving, these cheesecake bars are sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  • 3 packages (24 oz) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup sour cream

For the Raspberry Swirl:

  • 1 ½ cups fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon water

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C). Line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.
  2. In a bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until well combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan, creating an even layer.
  3. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and let it cool while you prepare the cheesecake filling.

Step 2: Making the Cheesecake Filling

  1. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  3. Stir in the vanilla extract and sour cream until the mixture is smooth and well combined.
  4. Pour the cheesecake filling over the cooled crust, spreading it evenly.

Step 3: Creating the Raspberry Swirl

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the fresh raspberries, sugar, and water. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the raspberries break down and the mixture thickens slightly, about 5 minutes.
  2. Remove the raspberry mixture from the heat and strain it through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the seeds, pressing down to extract as much raspberry puree as possible.

Step 4: Swirling the Raspberry Puree

  1. Drop spoonfuls of the raspberry puree onto the cheesecake filling.
  2. Use a knife or a toothpick to create swirls by gently dragging it through the raspberry puree and cheesecake filling, creating a marbled pattern.

Step 5: Baking and Chilling

  1. Bake the cheesecake bars for 40-45 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  2. Remove the cheesecake bars from the oven and let them cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight to set.

Serving and Storing

Once the cheesecake bars are fully set, use the parchment paper overhang to lift them out of the pan and onto a cutting board. Cut them into bars of your desired size and serve chilled. These raspberry swirl cheesecake bars can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, making them a perfect make-ahead dessert for any occasion.

The combination of velvety smooth cheesecake and the bright, vibrant flavor of fresh raspberries makes these homemade raspberry swirl cheesecake bars a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is sure to impress your family and friends. Enjoy the creamy and fruity goodness of these cheesecake bars, and savor every delectable bite!
