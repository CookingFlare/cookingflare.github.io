How to Make Korean Rice Cake Soup (Tteokguk) for the New Year


Korean Rice Cake Soup, known as Tteokguk, is a beloved dish that marks the beginning of the Korean New Year, celebrated on the first day of the lunar calendar. This warm, comforting soup is more than just a delicious meal – it’s also a symbol of longevity and a fresh start. The dish consists of thin, chewy rice cakes (tteok) cooked in a savory broth, often accompanied by sliced beef, egg garnishes, and various vegetables. In Korea, eating tteokguk on New Year’s Day is an important tradition, and it is said that eating the soup will help you age a year in wisdom and good health.

In this guide, we will walk you through how to make Tteokguk, from preparing the ingredients to serving it with a perfect balance of flavors. Whether you’re celebrating the Korean New Year or simply craving a comforting bowl of soup, this recipe will deliver an authentic taste of Korean culture straight to your kitchen.

Key Ingredients for Tteokguk

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients needed for this dish:

1. Rice Cakes (Tteok)

The most important ingredient in Tteokguk is, of course, the rice cakes. These thin, oval-shaped cakes are typically made with rice flour and are either purchased fresh or dried. For Tteokguk, it’s best to use the thin, disc-shaped variety known as baekseolgi tteok (white rice cakes). If using dried rice cakes, you’ll need to soak them in water for a few hours before cooking.

2. Broth

The broth forms the base of Tteokguk and can be made using either beef, chicken, or anchovies. Traditionally, beef is used for its rich, savory flavor. You’ll need some beef brisket or shank, which is boiled to make a rich and hearty broth.

3. Beef

A few slices of thinly cut beef (typically sirloin or brisket) are used as garnish for the soup. The beef is briefly cooked in the broth or stir-fried before being added to the soup for extra flavor and texture.

4. Egg

A lightly seasoned egg is scrambled and then sliced thinly to decorate the soup. The egg provides color contrast and a delicate richness to the dish.

5. Seasonings

The seasonings in Tteokguk are simple but essential for flavor. You’ll need soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. These ingredients help bring together the savory components of the dish and provide a well-rounded, satisfying taste.

6. Garnishes

A variety of garnishes can be added for extra texture and visual appeal. These can include green onions (chopped), sesame seeds, and sometimes pickled radish or kimchi. Each adds a slight variation in flavor that complements the soup.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Tteokguk

1. Prepare the Broth

Start by making a flavorful broth. If you’re using beef, follow these steps:

  1. Boil the Beef: Place your beef brisket or shank in a large pot and cover it with water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 to 1.5 hours. Skim off any impurities that rise to the top during the boiling process.

  2. Add Aromatics: Once the beef has cooked, add a few cloves of garlic, a small onion (optional), and a piece of dried kelp (kombu) to the pot for an added depth of flavor. Continue to simmer for 30 minutes.

  3. Season the Broth: After simmering, strain the broth to remove the beef and aromatics. Return the clear broth to the pot and add soy sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. A dash of sesame oil can also be added for a richer, nuttier flavor.

If you’re using chicken or anchovies for the broth, you would follow a similar process, adjusting the cooking time based on the type of meat or seafood you’re using.

2. Prepare the Rice Cakes

If you’re using dried rice cakes, they will need to be soaked in water for a few hours to soften them. Once softened, rinse the rice cakes under cold water and set them aside.

Fresh rice cakes do not need to be soaked, so you can add them directly to the simmering broth once it’s ready.

3. Prepare the Beef Garnish

While the broth is simmering, prepare the beef garnish:

  1. Slice the Beef: Cut your beef into thin strips. If you prefer, you can also stir-fry the beef in a little sesame oil and soy sauce to enhance the flavor.

  2. Cook the Beef: Add the beef slices into the broth and cook them for 5-7 minutes, just until they’re tender. Remove from the pot and set aside for later use.

4. Cook the Rice Cakes

Once the broth is ready and seasoned to your liking, add the rice cakes to the pot. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. The rice cakes should cook for about 5-10 minutes or until they become soft and chewy.

If you’re using fresh rice cakes, they will cook relatively quickly. Dried rice cakes will take a bit longer, so be sure to check their texture as they cook.

5. Prepare the Egg Garnish

While the rice cakes are cooking, prepare the egg garnish.

  1. Scramble the Eggs: Beat two eggs in a bowl. In a hot pan, drizzle a small amount of sesame oil and pour the beaten eggs into the pan. Gently swirl the pan to spread the egg evenly, creating a thin layer. Once the egg is set, flip it over to cook the other side briefly.

  2. Slice the Egg: Once the egg is cooked, remove it from the pan and let it cool for a minute. Then, cut the egg into thin strips to use as garnish for the soup.

6. Assemble the Soup

Once everything is cooked, it’s time to assemble the soup:

  1. Add the Beef Back into the Broth: Add your cooked beef slices back into the pot along with the rice cakes. Let them simmer together for a few more minutes to combine the flavors.

  2. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as needed, adding more soy sauce, sesame oil, or salt if necessary.

  3. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls. Top with the egg garnish, chopped green onions, and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for extra flavor and crunch. You can also add a few pieces of pickled radish on the side to balance out the savory flavor of the soup.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Once your Tteokguk is beautifully garnished, it’s ready to serve. Pair it with a side of kimchi or other Korean side dishes (banchan) for a complete meal. Enjoy the warm, comforting bowl of soup and feel the joy of welcoming the New Year with this cherished Korean tradition.

Tips for Making the Perfect Tteokguk

  • Rice Cake Texture: If the rice cakes are too soft or mushy, it can affect the texture of the soup. Be sure to cook them just long enough to become tender and chewy without overcooking them.
  • Broth Variation: You can switch up the flavor of the broth by using chicken or anchovy stock. Each variation will give the soup a slightly different but equally delicious taste.
  • Topping Ideas: While the egg and beef garnish are traditional, you can also get creative with toppings. Adding a few slices of sautéed mushrooms or a drizzle of gochujang (Korean chili paste) for a spicy kick can add variety to the dish.

Conclusion

Tteokguk is more than just a dish – it’s a part of Korean New Year traditions that celebrates family, health, and a fresh start. Making this soup at home is a rewarding experience that brings warmth to both your heart and your kitchen. With its rich broth, chewy rice cakes, and simple yet elegant garnishes, Tteokguk is a dish that will make your New Year celebrations (or any special occasion) even more memorable.

Whether you are familiar with Korean cuisine or trying Tteokguk for the first time, this recipe offers a genuine taste of Korean culture. Embrace the tradition, enjoy the soup, and take a moment to reflect on the new year ahead.
