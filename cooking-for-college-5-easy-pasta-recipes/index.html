Cooking for College: 5 Easy Pasta Recipes


Balancing classes, assignments, extracurricular activities, and a social life can make cooking in college feel like a daunting chore. But with a few simple pasta recipes in your arsenal, you can create satisfying, affordable, and quick meals without breaking the bank or spending hours in the kitchen. Pasta has long been a college student’s best friend—it’s inexpensive, versatile, and hearty enough to fuel long study sessions. Below, we’ll walk through five easy pasta recipes that will elevate your dorm-room dinners and make you feel like a culinary pro.

1. One-Pot Garlic Butter Pasta

This recipe is perfect for when you’re pressed for time but don’t want to sacrifice flavor. It all cooks in one pot, which means less cleanup—a boon for busy students.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp butter (or use olive oil for a lighter option)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: crushed red pepper flakes or fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the pasta and water. Bring to a boil and cook according to the pasta’s package instructions, stirring occasionally. Do not drain the water entirely—leave about ½ cup of starchy pasta water in the pot.
  2. Reduce the heat to low. Add butter, minced garlic, and Parmesan cheese directly into the pot. Stir until the butter melts and the cheese creates a creamy sauce.
  3. Season with salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if you like a little heat.
  4. Serve immediately and garnish with parsley if desired. This dish pairs beautifully with a simple side salad or garlic bread.

Pro Tip: Garlic butter pasta is a great base dish—toss in cooked mushrooms, spinach, or leftover rotisserie chicken to make it more filling!

2. Cheesy Pesto Pasta Bake

When you have a bit more time or want to meal prep, this cheesy pesto pasta bake is a rewarding choice. It’s comforting, flavorful, and easy to reheat throughout the week.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz penne or rigatoni pasta
  • 1 cup store-bought pesto (or homemade if you’re ambitious!)
  • 1 ½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: cherry tomatoes, spinach, or shredded cooked chicken

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook your pasta in salted boiling water until just al dente. Drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
  3. Stir in the pesto and a handful of spinach or cherry tomatoes if using.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the ricotta cheese with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Spread half of the pasta mixture into a lightly greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Dollop half the ricotta cheese mixture over the pasta, then sprinkle with shredded mozzarella.
  6. Add the rest of the pasta mixture on top and repeat the layering with ricotta and mozzarella. Sprinkle the Parmesan cheese evenly over the top.
  7. Bake for 20–25 minutes or until bubbling and golden on top.
  8. Let it cool slightly, then serve.

Pro Tip: Double the recipe and store individual portions in the freezer for quick, future meals.

3. Creamy Tomato and Spinach Tortellini

This recipe uses store-bought cheese tortellini, making it an easy and flavorful meal to whip up on weeknights. The creamy tomato sauce is made from scratch but takes just minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 10 oz fresh or frozen cheese tortellini
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 3 cups fresh spinach
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the cheese tortellini according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the garlic until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes (including their juice), heavy cream, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the spinach to the skillet and cook until it wilts (about 2 minutes).
  5. Combine the cooked tortellini with the sauce, tossing gently to coat evenly.
  6. Serve warm with grated Parmesan cheese on top.

Pro Tip: If you’re a fan of spice, try adding a pinch of red chili flakes to the sauce for an extra kick.

4. Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a classic Italian pasta dish that requires just a handful of ingredients, most of which you probably already have in your kitchen. It’s quick, light, and incredibly satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve ¼ cup of pasta water, then drain.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and sauté until just golden (be careful not to let it burn).
  3. Add the red pepper flakes to the skillet and stir. Let it cook for about 30 seconds.
  4. Toss the cooked spaghetti into the skillet, adding the reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce. Mix well and season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh parsley and Parmesan cheese.

Pro Tip: This is a great dish for improvisation—throw in some sautéed shrimp, a handful of halved cherry tomatoes, or even cooked zucchini for extra texture.

5. Peanut Butter Sesame Pasta

If you’re craving something different, this peanut butter sesame pasta is a unique and flavorful twist on typical pasta dishes. It’s also a fantastic option for meal prep!

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti or soba noodles
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter (creamy or crunchy)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar (or lime juice)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger (optional)
  • 1 cup shredded carrots, cucumber, or other veggies
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, chopped peanuts, or cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti or soba noodles according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, and ginger to create the sauce.
  3. Toss the cooked noodles with the sauce until thoroughly coated.
  4. Mix in shredded veggies such as carrots or cucumber for added crunch and color.
  5. Serve the dish cold or at room temperature and top with sesame seeds, chopped peanuts, or cilantro for garnish.

Pro Tip: This recipe is a great way to sneak in more vegetables! Try it with bell peppers, edamame, or shredded cabbage for extra nutrition.

Final Thoughts

Mastering these five pasta recipes will not only save you time and money but also improve your cooking confidence. Each dish is endlessly customizable, making it easy to adapt based on what ingredients you have on hand. Plus, pasta dishes tend to reheat well, making them excellent for meal prepping and leftovers—a key survival tactic for college life.

Whether you’re craving classic comfort food or something a bit more adventurous, these recipes prove that pasta is the ultimate weeknight hero. Give them a try, and before you know it, you’ll be impressing not just your roommates but even yourself with your culinary skills. Happy cooking!
