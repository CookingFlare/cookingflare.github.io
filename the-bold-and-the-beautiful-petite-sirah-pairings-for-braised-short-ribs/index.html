The Bold and the Beautiful: Petite Sirah Pairings for Braised Short Ribs


As the sun sets over the hills of Tuscany, the air is filled with the aroma of slow-cooked braised short ribs, perfectly paired with a glass of Petite Sirah. The bold and the beautiful, a perfect match made in culinary heaven. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of pairing Petite Sirah with braised short ribs, a match that will leave you wanting more.

The Wines

Petite Sirah, a varietal known for its bold and full-bodied flavors, is the perfect complement to the rich and tender short ribs. With its dark fruit flavors, subtle hints of spice, and smooth tannins, Petite Sirah is the perfect wine to pair with the bold and rich flavors of the short ribs.

The Recipe

For this recipe, we’ll be using a classic braised short ribs recipe, with a few tweaks to enhance the flavors and textures. We’ll be using a combination of red wine, beef broth, and aromatics to create a rich and flavorful sauce, perfect for serving with the short ribs.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup aromatics (such as carrot, celery, and onion)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C).
  2. Season the short ribs with salt, pepper, and thyme.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the short ribs until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove the short ribs from the pot and set aside.
  4. Add the diced onion to the pot and cook until caramelized, about 10 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the red wine, beef broth, and aromatics to the pot. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
  6. Return the short ribs to the pot, cover, and transfer to the preheated oven. Braise for 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls off the bone.
  7. Remove the pot from the oven and stir in the flour to thicken the sauce. Add the butter and stir until melted.
  8. Serve the short ribs with the rich and flavorful sauce spooned over the top.

The Pairing

As you sit down to enjoy your braised short ribs, take a sip of the Petite Sirah. The bold and full-bodied flavors of the wine perfectly complement the rich and tender short ribs. The dark fruit flavors and subtle hints of spice in the wine enhance the flavors of the short ribs, while the smooth tannins provide a velvety texture that complements the tender meat.

Tips for Pairing:

  • When pairing Petite Sirah with braised short ribs, look for a wine with dark fruit flavors and subtle hints of spice.
  • The bold and full-bodied flavors of the wine should complement the rich and tender short ribs.
  • The smooth tannins in the wine should provide a velvety texture that complements the tender meat.

As the evening comes to a close, and the last bite of short ribs is savored, take a moment to appreciate the beauty of the pairing. The bold and the beautiful, a match made in culinary heaven.
