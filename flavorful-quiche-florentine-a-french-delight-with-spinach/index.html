Flavorful Quiche Florentine: A French Delight with Spinach


If you’re looking for a dish that’s both elegant and comforting, look no further than the classic French quiche. And when it comes to quiche, one of the most beloved variations is the Quiche Florentine. This delightful dish brings together the richness of eggs and cream with the earthy goodness of spinach, all encased in a buttery, flaky crust. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create a flavorful Quiche Florentine that will impress your guests and tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 5 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup Gruyère cheese, shredded
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Add the chilled, cubed butter to the flour mixture.
  3. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Drizzle the ice water over the mixture and mix with a fork until the dough begins to come together.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a clean surface and knead it gently until it forms a cohesive ball.
  6. Flatten the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion is soft and translucent.
  4. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted.
  5. Remove the skillet from the heat and let the spinach mixture cool.
  6. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  7. Stir in the shredded Gruyère cheese.

Assembling the Quiche:

  1. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish.
  2. Press the dough into the pie dish, trimming any excess dough from the edges.
  3. Spread the cooled spinach mixture evenly over the bottom of the crust.
  4. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the spinach.

Baking the Quiche:

  1. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  2. Remove the quiche from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Flavorful Quiche Florentine pairs beautifully with a simple green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette. It’s the perfect dish for a leisurely brunch, a special lunch, or a light dinner. The creamy, custardy filling combined with the earthy spinach and nutty Gruyère cheese make for a truly delightful eating experience.

Conclusion

Quiche Florentine is a timeless classic that never fails to impress. Its versatility and comforting flavors make it a staple in French cuisine and a favorite in households around the world. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of France, this recipe is sure to please. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the flavors of a traditional French delight - the Flavorful Quiche Florentine.
