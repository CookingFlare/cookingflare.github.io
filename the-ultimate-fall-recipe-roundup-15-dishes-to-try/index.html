The Ultimate Fall Recipe Roundup: 15 Dishes to Try


As the crisp autumn air rolls in and the leaves begin to change colors, it’s the perfect time to indulge in hearty, comforting dishes that reflect the season. Fall is a time to embrace rich flavors like cinnamon, nutmeg, roasted vegetables, and slow-cooked meats. From savory soups to decadent desserts, there’s no shortage of recipes to try. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering or simply enjoying a cozy evening at home, this ultimate fall recipe roundup is sure to inspire your next meal. Here are 15 delicious dishes you should try this season.

1. Butternut Squash Soup

Start your fall culinary adventure with a bowl of velvety butternut squash soup. This dish is the epitome of comfort food with its rich, earthy flavor and creamy texture. To make this soup, roast chunks of butternut squash until tender and caramelized, then blend with onions, garlic, vegetable broth, and a dash of cream. A sprinkle of roasted pumpkin seeds and a swirl of sour cream can add a delightful crunch and tang to each spoonful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for roasting

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed squash in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast on a baking sheet for 25-30 minutes, or until soft and caramelized.
  3. In a large pot, sauté onions and garlic in olive oil until soft.
  4. Add the roasted squash and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Blend the soup until smooth and creamy, then stir in the cream.
  6. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, and serve hot with your favorite toppings.

2. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Fall mornings call for something special, and pumpkin spice pancakes are the perfect choice. These fluffy pancakes are packed with the flavors of fall, including cinnamon, ginger, and nutmeg, with the rich taste of pumpkin puree. Top them with maple syrup, whipped cream, or even a sprinkle of candied pecans for an indulgent breakfast experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp ginger
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • ½ cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Butter for cooking

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the dry ingredients: flour, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, milk, pumpkin puree, brown sugar, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  4. Heat a griddle or skillet over medium heat and add a little butter.
  5. Pour ¼ cup of batter onto the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
  6. Serve with maple syrup, whipped cream, and any additional toppings you desire.

3. Roasted Root Vegetable Medley

A medley of roasted root vegetables is the ultimate side dish for any fall meal. With a mix of sweet potatoes, carrots, parsnips, and beets, this dish is bursting with flavor and nutrition. Roasting these vegetables brings out their natural sweetness and caramelizes them to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 2 beets, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the vegetables with olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 30-40 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are tender and caramelized.
  5. Serve hot as a side dish to complement your main meal.

4. Apple Crisp

When it comes to fall desserts, apple crisp is a classic. The sweet, spiced apples paired with a buttery, crumbly topping make for a perfect end to any fall meal. Serve it warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for extra indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups apples, peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • ½ cup flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cubed
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with granulated sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Spread the apples evenly in a baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, combine oats, flour, brown sugar, butter, and salt. Use a pastry cutter or your hands to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat topping over the apples.
  5. Bake for 45-50 minutes, until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.
  6. Serve warm with ice cream or whipped cream.

5. Beef Stew with Red Wine

A hearty beef stew is the ultimate comfort food for fall. Slow-cooked in red wine and broth, this dish is full of tender beef, vegetables, and a deep, rich flavor that develops over time. Perfect for a cozy evening or a weekend family dinner, this stew will warm you from the inside out.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches, removing it from the pot and setting it aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic until softened.
  3. Add the beef back to the pot along with red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, and bay leaves.
  4. Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat and cook for 2-3 hours, until the beef is tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.

6. Stuffed Acorn Squash

Stuffed acorn squash is a beautiful and flavorful dish that showcases the best of fall produce. Acorn squash is roasted and filled with a savory mixture of quinoa, cranberries, nuts, and spices. This dish is perfect as a main course for vegetarians or as a side dish to a larger meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 acorn squash, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • ½ cup dried cranberries
  • ½ cup pecans, chopped
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Drizzle the cut sides of the squash with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast the squash halves, cut side down, for 40-45 minutes, until tender.
  4. In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa, cranberries, pecans, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt.
  5. Stuff each squash half with the quinoa mixture and return to the oven for 10 minutes.
  6. Serve warm, garnished with fresh herbs or additional nuts.

7. Maple Glazed Brussels Sprouts

Brussels sprouts are a quintessential fall vegetable, and when they’re glazed with maple syrup, they become a sweet and savory side dish that pairs perfectly with any main course. The caramelized edges of the sprouts combined with the rich maple glaze will have you coming back for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through, until crispy and golden brown.
  4. Drizzle with maple syrup and return to the oven for another 5 minutes.
  5. Serve hot as a side dish.

8. Mushroom Risotto

Creamy risotto with earthy mushrooms is a fall dish that delivers both comfort and sophistication. The combination of sautéed mushrooms, Arborio rice, and Parmesan cheese creates a dish that’s rich in flavor and perfect for cozy fall evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened.
  2. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their juices and become golden brown.
  3. Stir in the rice and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Gradually add the vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Allow the liquid to be absorbed before adding more.
  5. Once the rice is cooked and creamy, stir in the Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve hot as a main dish or a side.

9. Cranberry Orange Bread

The combination of tart cranberries and zesty orange is a hallmark of fall flavors. This moist cranberry orange bread is perfect for breakfast, snacks, or even a light dessert. Its bright citrus flavor and tangy berries make it a refreshing yet comforting treat for the season.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: flour, baking powder, baking soda, and cinnamon.
  3. In another bowl, mix the sugar, egg, orange juice, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, then gently stir in the cranberries.
  5. Pour the batter into a greased loaf pan and bake for 60-70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. Cool before slicing and serving.

10. Sweet Potato Casserole

Sweet potato casserole is a must-have side dish for any fall gathering. With its creamy mashed sweet potatoes and a crunchy, sweet topping, it’s both comforting and flavorful. Topped with toasted marshmallows or pecans, this dish can even double as a dessert!

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in salted water until tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Mash the sweet potatoes with butter, brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg until smooth.
  4. Transfer to a baking dish and top with marshmallows or pecans, if desired.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the topping is golden brown.

11. Spiced Pear Tart

A spiced pear tart is a perfect fall dessert. The buttery, flaky crust paired with the sweet, tender pears and warm spices will make this tart a standout at any fall gathering. It’s a great way to showcase seasonal fruits in a delicious, elegant way.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • 3 ripe pears, peeled and sliced
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp butter

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a baking sheet and prick it with a fork.
  3. Arrange the pear slices on the pastry, overlapping them slightly.
  4. Sprinkle with sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg, and dot with butter.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the pastry is golden and the pears are tender.
  6. Let cool slightly before serving.

12. Caramelized Onion and Goat Cheese Flatbread

For a savory appetizer or snack, try a caramelized onion and goat cheese flatbread. The combination of sweet, caramelized onions and creamy goat cheese on a crispy flatbread crust makes for a delicious bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package pizza dough
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 oz goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a baking sheet.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and cook until soft and caramelized, about 15 minutes.
  4. Top the pizza dough with the caramelized onions and crumbled goat cheese.
  5. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar, season with salt and pepper, and bake for 12-15 minutes, until the crust is golden.
  6. Slice and serve.

13. Slow Cooker Chili

Chili is a fall favorite, and when made in a slow cooker, it becomes even more flavorful. The long cooking time allows all the spices to meld together perfectly, creating a rich, hearty dish. Add beans, tomatoes, and your choice of meat for a meal that’s perfect for chilly nights.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cans kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Brown the ground meat in a skillet, then transfer to a slow cooker.
  2. Add the remaining ingredients to the slow cooker, stirring to combine.
  3. Cook on low for 6-8 hours, or high for 3-4 hours, until the chili is thick and flavorful.
  4. Serve with cornbread or tortilla chips.

14. Autumn Roasted Chicken with Apples

A whole roasted chicken with apples is a classic fall dish that’s both simple and elegant. The apples caramelize in the oven, infusing the chicken with a sweet and savory flavor that pairs perfectly with roasted potatoes and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 4 apples, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rub the chicken with olive oil, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff the chicken cavity with the garlic and apples.
  4. Roast for 1-1.5 hours, until the chicken is golden and cooked through.
  5. Serve with roasted vegetables and potatoes.

15. Pecan Pie

End your fall feast with a slice of classic pecan pie. The rich, nutty filling and flaky crust make it an irresistible dessert. Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups pecans

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the corn syrup, brown sugar, eggs, butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Stir in the pecans and pour the mixture into the pie crust.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden.
  5. Cool completely before serving.

With these 15 fall recipes, you’ll have plenty of delicious dishes to make the most of the season. Whether you’re craving something savory or sweet, these recipes capture the best flavors autumn has to offer. Enjoy the warm, comforting tastes of fall with your loved ones and create lasting memories around the dinner table.
