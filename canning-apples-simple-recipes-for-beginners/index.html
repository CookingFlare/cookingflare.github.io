Canning Apples: Simple Recipes for Beginners


There’s something truly magical about capturing the crisp, sweet-tart essence of fresh apples in a jar. Whether you’ve just returned home with a bushel of apples from the orchard or want to preserve the taste of fall a little longer, canning is an excellent way to enjoy this versatile fruit year-round. If you’re new to preserving food and have always wanted to try canning, apples are the perfect way to get started. They’re easy to work with, forgiving for beginners, and transform beautifully into a variety of delicious recipes.

This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about canning apples, including tips, tools, and several beginner-friendly recipes like canned apple slices, applesauce, and apple butter. Let’s dive right into it!

Preparing for Canning: What You Need to Know

Before we get into the recipes, it’s essential to familiarize yourself with the basics of canning. Canning ensures that your preserved apples remain safe to eat over time while locking in flavor and nutrients.

Essential Tools for Canning

To successfully can apples, you’ll need to gather the following tools:

  • Canning Jars: Use mason jars with new lids and bands. Make sure the jars are clean and free of cracks.
  • Water Bath Canner: A large pot with a rack to hold jars in place. This method is suitable for high-acid foods like apples.
  • Jar Lifter: A tool to safely remove hot jars from boiling water.
  • Funnel: Handy for pouring liquid or filling jars cleanly.
  • Bubble Remover/Headspace Tool: Use to remove air bubbles and measure the space at the top of the jar.
  • Ladle and Spoon: For filling jars with apples and syrup.

Key Steps to Safe Canning

  1. Sanitizing Jars: Wash your jars, lids, and bands with hot, soapy water. Sterilize them in boiling water or the dishwasher for maximum safety.
  2. Preparing Apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples as needed for your recipes. Working with fresh and firm apples will give you the best results.
  3. Creating These Recipes: Whether you’re making slices, sauce, or butter, ensure you follow tested recipes to maintain a safe pH level for canning.
  4. Sealing and Processing: After filling your jars, ensure the lids are sealed properly and process them in a water bath to preserve them.

Now that you’re ready, let’s get into the delicious recipes!

Recipe 1: Canned Apple Slices in Light Syrup

Canned apple slices are incredibly versatile. You can use them straight out of the jar for pies, crisps, or even as a quick snack. The process is simple and perfect for beginner canners.

Ingredients

  • 12 medium apples (about 8–10 pounds)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 tablespoons bottled lemon juice (per jar)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples into uniform pieces. To prevent browning, place apple slices in a bowl of water with a splash of lemon juice.
  2. Make the Syrup: In a large pot, bring water and sugar to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Reduce heat and simmer.
  3. Fill the Jars: Pack apple slices tightly into sterilized jars. Pour hot syrup over the apples, leaving ½ inch of headspace. Add 2 tablespoons of bottled lemon juice to each jar to maintain acidity.
  4. Remove Air Bubbles: Use a bubble remover to ensure there are no air pockets. Wipe the rims of the jars clean and secure the lids.
  5. Process the Jars: Place jars in a water bath canner, ensuring they’re fully submerged. Process for 20 minutes for pint jars and 25 minutes for quart jars.
  6. Cool and Store: Once processed, remove the jars and cool them on a towel. Check the seals before storing.

Recipe 2: Homemade Applesauce

Applesauce is a quick and easy recipe that’s nearly impossible to mess up, making it a beginner-friendly favorite. Plus, it’s a crowd-pleaser for kids and adults alike.

Ingredients

  • 6 pounds of apples (a mix of sweet and tart varieties)
  • 1 cup water
  • Sugar to taste
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples. If you prefer a more rustic texture, you can leave the skins on.
  2. Cook the Apples: In a large pot, combine the apples and water. Cover and cook on medium heat until the apples soften, about 15–20 minutes.
  3. Mash or Blend: Use a potato masher or an immersion blender to achieve your desired applesauce texture. Add sugar and cinnamon to taste.
  4. Fill the Jars: Ladle hot applesauce into sterilized jars, leaving ½ inch of headspace. Wipe rims clean and secure the lids.
  5. Process the Jars: Process in a water bath canner for 15 minutes for pint jars and 20 minutes for quart jars.
  6. Cool and Store: Remove jars, cool completely, and ensure seals are intact before storing.

Recipe 3: Spiced Apple Butter

If you adore the flavors of autumn, apple butter is a must-try. Its rich, spiced flavor and smooth texture make it perfect for spreading on toast, muffins, or even swirled into yogurt.

Ingredients

  • 6 pounds apples (peeled, cored, and chopped)
  • 1 cup apple cider or water
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon allspice

Instructions

  1. Cook the Apples: Place the apples and cider in a large pot. Cover and cook over medium heat until the apples are soft, about 20 minutes.
  2. Blend the Mixture: Use an immersion blender or food processor to puree the apples until smooth.
  3. Add Spices and Sugar: Return the apple puree to the pot. Stir in sugar and spices. Cook uncovered on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens, about 1–2 hours.
  4. Fill the Jars: Spoon the hot apple butter into sterilized jars, leaving ¼ inch of headspace. Wipe rims clean and secure lids.
  5. Process the Jars: Process in a water bath canner for 10 minutes for both pint and half-pint jars.
  6. Cool and Store: Let the jars cool and check the seals. Properly sealed jars can be stored for up to a year.

Troubleshooting Common Canning Issues

Even with the simplest recipes, beginners might encounter a few challenges. Here are solutions to common canning obstacles:

  • Floating Fruit: This happens when air remains in the fruit. Pack the apples tightly and remove air bubbles before sealing.
  • Syrup Leakage: If liquid seeps out during processing, ensure you leave the correct headspace and tighten the lids adequately.
  • Lid Did Not Seal: Always check the seals after the jars cool. If a jar doesn’t seal, refrigerate it and consume the contents within a week or reprocess it with a new lid.

Storing and Enjoying Your Canned Apples

Once your jars are cooled and sealed, label them with the date and contents. Store them in a cool, dark place like a pantry or cellar. Properly canned apples can be enjoyed for up to a year.

The beauty of canned apples lies in their versatility. Use your apple slices for quick desserts, your applesauce as a snack or baking ingredient, and your apple butter as a rich spread packed with autumnal spices.

Final Thoughts

Canning apples is a rewarding and satisfying process that allows you to preserve the delicious taste of the season. By starting with these simple recipes, you’ll build the skills and confidence to try more advanced canning projects in the future. Whether you’re preparing warm apple pie in February or spreading apple butter on toast in July, these jars of apples will remind you of the crisp, comforting joy that autumn brings.

Grab those apples and let’s get canning! Happy preserving!
