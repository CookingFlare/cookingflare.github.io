French Cooking for Beginners: Easy Recipes to Start With


If you’ve always been intrigued by French cuisine, but never quite knew where to start, you’re in luck! French cooking may seem intimidating at first, with its rich flavors and intricate techniques, but with a few basic recipes, you can begin your culinary journey and impress your family and friends. In this blog post, we’ll explore some easy and delicious French recipes that are perfect for beginners. So, put on your apron, grab your chef’s hat, and let’s get cooking!

1. Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a classic French dish that showcases the vibrant flavors of summer vegetables. It’s a hearty and versatile dish that can be served as a main course or a side dish. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion and garlic to the pan and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the diced eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and tomatoes to the pan.
  4. Sprinkle the dried thyme, salt, and pepper over the vegetables.
  5. Cook the ratatouille over low heat for about 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Coq au Vin

Coq au Vin is a classic French dish that combines tender chicken with rich red wine and aromatic vegetables. It’s a comforting and flavorful dish that will transport you straight to the streets of Paris. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of red wine
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon of all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large pot, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until it becomes crispy.
  3. Remove the bacon from the pot and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, add the chicken thighs and brown them on both sides.
  5. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  6. Add the diced onion, carrots, and minced garlic to the pot and sauté until they become tender.
  7. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and cook for a minute, stirring constantly.
  8. Add the red wine, chicken broth, and tomato paste to the pot, and bring to a simmer.
  9. Return the chicken and bacon to the pot.
  10. Cover the pot and let the Coq au Vin simmer for about 1 hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  11. Serve hot with crusty bread or mashed potatoes.

3. Crème Brûlée

No French cooking experience is complete without a sweet treat. Crème Brûlée is a classic French dessert that combines silky smooth custard with a caramelized sugar topping. It’s a simple yet elegant dessert that will impress your guests. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons of brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer. Remove from heat.
  4. Slowly pour the heated cream into the egg mixture, whisking constantly.
  5. Place four ramekins in a baking dish and divide the custard mixture evenly among them.
  6. Fill the baking dish with hot water, about halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
  7. Carefully transfer the baking dish to the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the custard is set but still slightly jiggly in the center.
  8. Remove the ramekins from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature.
  9. Chill the custards in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours.
  10. Just before serving, sprinkle 1 tablespoon of brown sugar evenly over each custard.
  11. Using a kitchen torch, carefully melt and caramelize the sugar until it forms a crispy topping.
  12. Allow the sugar to harden for a few minutes before serving.

There you have it! Three easy and delicious French recipes to kickstart your culinary adventure. Remember, French cooking is all about taking your time, savoring the flavors, and enjoying the process. So, don’t be afraid to experiment and make these recipes your own. Bon appétit!
