Vegan and Vegetarian Gravy Recipes for Your Holiday Meals


The holidays are a time for family gatherings, festive feasts, and indulging in rich, comforting dishes. Whether you’re celebrating Thanksgiving, Christmas, or any other special occasion, gravy is often the centerpiece of many meals, adding flavor and texture to everything from mashed potatoes to stuffing and roasted vegetables. However, for those following a vegan or vegetarian diet, traditional gravies made with meat drippings aren’t an option. Fortunately, there are plenty of delicious and easy-to-make vegan and vegetarian gravy recipes that can elevate your holiday spread without compromising on taste.

In this blog post, we will explore several mouthwatering gravy options for those who want to make their holiday meals cruelty-free, flavorful, and satisfying. These recipes can be made with simple plant-based ingredients and are sure to impress your guests, whether they follow a vegan, vegetarian, or omnivorous diet. Let’s dive into these savory, rich, and indulgent gravy recipes that are perfect for any holiday table.

Classic Vegan Mushroom Gravy

Mushrooms are a fantastic base for vegan gravy, offering a rich umami flavor that mimics the depth of traditional meat-based gravies. This classic vegan mushroom gravy is savory, smooth, and perfect for drizzling over mashed potatoes, stuffing, or roasted vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of sliced mushrooms (cremini or white button mushrooms work well)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegan butter or olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegan butter or olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the minced garlic and sliced mushrooms to the pan and sauté for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the mushrooms have released their moisture and are lightly browned.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir well to coat. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the flour to toast slightly.
  4. Slowly pour in the vegetable broth while stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Add the soy sauce, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring the gravy to a simmer, stirring occasionally, until it thickens to your desired consistency (about 5-10 minutes).
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve the mushroom gravy warm over your holiday dishes and enjoy!

This hearty and rich mushroom gravy is perfect for adding depth of flavor to plant-based roasts, mashed potatoes, or stuffing. The earthy mushrooms, combined with savory soy sauce and fragrant thyme, create a comforting and satisfying sauce that pairs beautifully with a variety of dishes.

Vegan Cashew Gravy

Cashews create a smooth, creamy base for vegan gravy, making this cashew gravy a luxurious addition to your holiday meals. The rich, nutty flavor of cashews adds a velvety texture to the gravy, which works wonderfully with mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a plant-based loaf.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup raw cashews (soaked in water for 4-6 hours or overnight)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked cashews and add them to a high-speed blender or food processor. Add the vegetable broth, nutritional yeast, soy sauce, ground sage, salt, and pepper. Blend until completely smooth and creamy.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened and slightly golden.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  4. Pour the cashew mixture into the saucepan with the sautéed onions and garlic. Stir well to combine. Bring the gravy to a simmer over low heat, stirring occasionally, for 5-10 minutes, or until the gravy has thickened to your desired consistency.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. If the gravy is too thick, add a little more vegetable broth to reach the desired texture.

The creamy, velvety texture of this cashew gravy makes it a decadent choice for your holiday meals. It’s especially wonderful when paired with mashed potatoes, stuffing, or a vegan loaf. The nutty cashews add a rich flavor that will delight both vegans and non-vegans alike.

Vegan Onion Gravy

Onion gravy is a classic choice for many holiday meals, and it’s easy to make vegan by using vegetable broth and plant-based butter. The caramelized onions in this gravy provide a deep, sweet flavor that perfectly complements mashed potatoes, vegan roasts, and roasted vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegan butter or olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegan butter or olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring frequently, for about 15-20 minutes, until the onions are deeply caramelized and golden brown. Be patient during this step—the longer you cook the onions, the sweeter and more flavorful they will become.
  2. Once the onions are caramelized, sprinkle the flour over them and stir to combine. Cook for another 1-2 minutes to remove the raw flour taste.
  3. Slowly pour in the vegetable broth while stirring constantly. Add the soy sauce, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring the gravy to a simmer and cook for 5-10 minutes until it thickens.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. If you prefer a smoother gravy, you can blend the gravy using an immersion blender or regular blender.
  5. Serve this savory onion gravy warm over your holiday dishes.

This onion gravy is the perfect addition to any holiday table. The sweet, caramelized onions combined with the savory soy sauce and thyme create a rich and flavorful gravy that pairs wonderfully with mashed potatoes, vegan roasts, or stuffing.

Vegan Brown Gravy

For those who love a traditional brown gravy, this vegan version is a delicious alternative. It’s made with simple pantry ingredients like vegetable broth, soy sauce, and a little bit of flour to thicken it up. This savory and umami-packed brown gravy will complement everything from mashed potatoes to vegan meatloaf.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary or thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened and translucent.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the onion and garlic mixture and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly to avoid burning.
  4. Slowly pour in the vegetable broth, stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming. Add the soy sauce, dried rosemary (or thyme), salt, and pepper. Bring the gravy to a simmer and cook for 5-10 minutes until thickened.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the gravy is too thick, add a bit more vegetable broth to thin it out to your desired consistency.

This rich and savory brown gravy is ideal for drizzling over mashed potatoes, stuffing, or vegan meatloaf. The combination of soy sauce and herbs gives it a depth of flavor that is perfect for your holiday meal.

Vegan Cranberry Gravy

For a unique twist on traditional gravy, consider making vegan cranberry gravy. This tangy, slightly sweet gravy is a great alternative to the classic savory options and pairs wonderfully with vegan roasts, mashed potatoes, or stuffing. It brings a burst of color and flavor to your holiday spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or agave syrup
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cranberries, vegetable broth, maple syrup, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to a simmer.
  2. Cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens slightly. You can mash some of the cranberries with a spoon to create a smoother texture.
  3. If you prefer a thicker gravy, dissolve the cornstarch in a small amount of cold water and add it to the pan. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until thickened.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

This cranberry gravy adds a beautiful contrast of sweetness and tang to your holiday meals. It’s perfect for those who enjoy a fruit-based sauce alongside savory dishes and is a great addition to your festive spread.

Conclusion

Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, or simply looking to explore new flavors during the holidays, these vegan and vegetarian gravy recipes are the perfect addition to your holiday meals. From rich mushroom gravies to sweet cranberry sauces, there’s something for every palate. These gravies are packed with flavor, simple to prepare, and guaranteed to impress your guests.

The best part about these vegan gravies is that they’re versatile, so you can drizzle them over mashed potatoes, stuffing, roasted vegetables, or any other dish that could use a little extra richness. These recipes prove that you don’t need meat-based ingredients to create hearty, satisfying gravies that everyone will love. So, this holiday season, make your meals even more memorable with these delicious plant-based gravies.
