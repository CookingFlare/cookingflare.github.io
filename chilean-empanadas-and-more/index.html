Chilean Empanadas and More


When it comes to South American cuisine, Chilean empanadas stand out as a flavorful and beloved dish. These savory pastries, filled with a variety of ingredients, are a staple in Chilean culture and a delight to the taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making traditional Chilean empanadas, along with some other delightful Chilean dishes that are sure to tantalize your senses.

The Art of Chilean Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of lard or vegetable shortening
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of pitted black olives, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Cut in the lard or vegetable shortening until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • In a separate bowl, beat the egg and water together. Gradually add this mixture to the flour, stirring until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface until smooth, then cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • In a skillet, cook the ground beef and onion over medium heat until the meat is no longer pink. Drain any excess fat.
    • Add the cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper to the meat mixture. Allow it to cool slightly.

  3. Assemble the Empanadas:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    • Roll out the dough to 1/8 inch thickness. Using a round cutter, cut out circles of dough.
    • Place a spoonful of the meat mixture in the center of each circle. Top with a slice of hard-boiled egg and a few slices of black olives.
    • Moisten the edges of the dough with water, then fold it over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together to seal, then crimp with a fork.

  4. Bake and Enjoy:

    • Place the empanadas on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
    • Let the empanadas cool for a few minutes before serving. These delicious pastries are perfect for a snack or a meal, and they can be enjoyed hot or at room temperature.

More Chilean Delights

1. Pastel de Choclo

This traditional Chilean dish is a savory pie made with ground beef, onions, chicken, raisins, black olives, and a creamy corn topping. It’s a comforting and hearty meal that showcases the rich flavors of Chilean cuisine.

2. Cazuela

Cazuela is a traditional Chilean soup that features a flavorful broth, chunks of meat, potatoes, pumpkin, corn on the cob, and rice. It’s a nourishing and wholesome dish that reflects the agricultural heritage of Chile.

3. Pebre

Pebre is a zesty Chilean condiment made with ingredients such as cilantro, onion, garlic, chili pepper, and vinegar. It’s often served alongside grilled meats, empanadas, or bread, adding a burst of flavor to every bite.

In conclusion, Chilean empanadas and the array of other dishes from Chile offer a delightful exploration of flavors and culinary traditions. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a curious food enthusiast, these recipes are an invitation to savor the essence of Chilean cuisine and bring a taste of South America to your table. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that celebrates the rich heritage of Chilean gastronomy.
