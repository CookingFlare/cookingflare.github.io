Bringing Korea to Your Kitchen: How to Make Bossam


Are you craving the flavors of Korea but can’t make it to a restaurant? Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this blog post, we’ll show you how to bring the taste of Korea to your own kitchen with a delicious and authentic recipe for bossam. This traditional Korean dish is a favorite among locals and tourists alike, and now you can enjoy it from the comfort of your own home. So let’s dive right in and get cooking!

Ingredients

To make bossam, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1.5 kilograms of pork belly
  • 1 head of garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of soybean paste (doenjang)
  • 2 tablespoons of red pepper paste (gochujang)
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds
  • 1 bunch of green onions, chopped
  • Lettuce leaves, for serving
  • Kimchi, for serving
  • Steamed rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the pork belly. Rinse it under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels. Then, slice it into thin strips, about 1-inch wide.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped onion, soybean paste, red pepper paste, sesame oil, sugar, salt, black pepper, rice vinegar, sesame seeds, and green onions. Mix well to form a paste.

  3. Place the pork belly strips in a large pot and add enough water to cover them. Bring the water to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the pork belly is tender.

  4. Once the pork belly is cooked, remove it from the pot and let it cool slightly. Then, using your hands, rub the paste mixture all over the pork belly, making sure to coat it evenly.

  5. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the coated pork belly on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and roast for about 15 minutes, or until the outside is crispy and golden brown.

  6. Remove the pork belly from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into bite-sized pieces.

  7. To serve, place the sliced pork belly on a platter along with lettuce leaves, kimchi, and steamed rice. Encourage your guests to wrap the pork belly in lettuce leaves, adding kimchi and a spoonful of rice for extra flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can marinate the pork belly in the paste mixture overnight before cooking it. This will result in a more intense and flavorful bossam.

  • If you prefer a milder version of bossam, you can reduce the amount of red pepper paste (gochujang) in the recipe.

  • Feel free to experiment with different toppings and condiments to personalize your bossam. Some popular choices include pickled radishes, garlic slices, and ssamjang sauce.

  • Bossam is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a main course or as part of a larger Korean feast. It pairs well with other traditional Korean dishes such as japchae (stir-fried noodles) and kimchi jjigae (kimchi stew).

Conclusion

Bringing the taste of Korea to your kitchen has never been easier with this delicious recipe for bossam. With its tender and flavorful pork belly, combined with the vibrant and spicy paste, bossam is sure to satisfy your cravings for authentic Korean cuisine. So gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and get ready to enjoy a taste of Korea from the comfort of your own home. Happy cooking!
