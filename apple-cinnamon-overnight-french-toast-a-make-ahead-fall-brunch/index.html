Apple Cinnamon Overnight French Toast: A Make-Ahead Fall Brunch


Fall is the perfect time to gather with loved ones and indulge in a comforting brunch. And what better way to start your day than with a warm and delicious Apple Cinnamon Overnight French Toast? This make-ahead recipe is not only easy to prepare but also guarantees a flavorful and satisfying meal that will impress your guests. So, without further ado, let’s dive into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of French bread, preferably stale or day-old
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Bread

  1. Start by greasing a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Slice the French bread into thick slices, about 1-inch thick.
  3. Arrange the bread slices in a single layer in the prepared baking dish, slightly overlapping each other.

Step 2: Make the Custard Mixture

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and salt until well combined.
  2. Pour the custard mixture evenly over the bread slices, making sure to soak each slice.
  3. Gently press down on the bread to ensure it absorbs the custard mixture.

Step 3: Add the Apple Cinnamon Topping

  1. In a separate bowl, toss the thinly sliced apples with brown sugar and ground cinnamon until well coated.
  2. Arrange the apple slices evenly over the soaked bread, pressing them slightly into the custard mixture.
  3. Drizzle the melted butter over the top of the apple slices, ensuring they are well coated.

Step 4: Refrigerate Overnight

  1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and refrigerate overnight, allowing the flavors to meld together and the bread to absorb the custard mixture.

Step 5: Bake and Serve

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Remove the aluminum foil from the baking dish and bake for 45-50 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the custard is set.
  3. Once baked, remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.
  4. Slice into individual portions and serve with a dusting of powdered sugar, a drizzle of maple syrup, or a dollop of whipped cream, if desired.

This Apple Cinnamon Overnight French Toast is a delightful combination of warm spices, tender apples, and custard-soaked bread. The make-ahead aspect of this recipe makes it perfect for entertaining, as you can simply pop it in the oven in the morning and enjoy a stress-free brunch with your loved ones. The aroma of cinnamon and apples wafting through your kitchen will surely add to the cozy fall vibes.

So, the next time you’re looking for a delicious and convenient fall brunch dish, give this Apple Cinnamon Overnight French Toast a try. Your taste buds will thank you, and your guests will be asking for seconds!
