How to Make Homemade Marshmallows from Scratch


Homemade marshmallows are a delicious treat that adds a special touch to any occasion. Whether you’re making s’mores, enjoying a hot cocoa, or just looking for a fun and creative baking project, marshmallows made from scratch are far superior to store-bought ones in both flavor and texture. With the right ingredients and a bit of patience, you can create soft, pillowy marshmallows that are perfect for any celebration or casual snack.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the entire process of making marshmallows from scratch, from preparing the ingredients to the final step of cutting them into perfect squares. Let’s dive into the details and discover how easy it is to make this homemade delight.

Ingredients for Homemade Marshmallows

Before you begin making your marshmallows, it’s important to gather the necessary ingredients. While the list might seem long, they are all readily available at most grocery stores. Here’s what you’ll need:

Essential Ingredients:

  1. Gelatin – This is the key ingredient that gives marshmallows their signature texture. You’ll need unflavored gelatin.
  2. Granulated Sugar – This will be the primary sweetener for your marshmallows.
  3. Corn Syrup – This adds smoothness and prevents the sugar from crystallizing, ensuring the marshmallows have a soft and smooth texture.
  4. Water – Water helps dissolve the gelatin and sugar and is essential for creating the proper texture.
  5. Vanilla Extract – For that wonderful marshmallow flavor.
  6. Salt – A small pinch of salt helps balance the sweetness and enhances the flavor.

For Dusting:

  1. Powdered Sugar – To coat your marshmallows after they’re made to prevent sticking.
  2. Cornstarch – Often combined with powdered sugar, cornstarch creates a non-stick coating that keeps the marshmallows separate and prevents them from becoming too sticky.

Optional Flavorings:

While classic vanilla marshmallows are delicious, you can get creative and add different flavorings to your marshmallows. Here are a few ideas:

  • Cocoa Powder for chocolate-flavored marshmallows.
  • Cinnamon for a spiced kick.
  • Peppermint Extract for a minty twist.
  • Fruit Extracts such as strawberry or lemon.

Now that you have everything ready, let’s start making the marshmallows!

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Homemade Marshmallows

Step 1: Prepare the Gelatin

The first thing you need to do is activate the gelatin. This will ensure that your marshmallows have the perfect texture. Place the gelatin in the bowl of a stand mixer or a large mixing bowl. Add 1/2 cup of cold water to the gelatin and let it sit for about 10 minutes. The gelatin will absorb the water and bloom, which is essential for the next step.

Step 2: Cook the Sugar Syrup

While the gelatin is blooming, you can begin preparing the sugar syrup. In a medium saucepan, combine 1 1/2 cups of granulated sugar, 1 cup of corn syrup, and 1/2 cup of water. Place the saucepan over medium heat and stir gently to dissolve the sugar. Once the sugar has dissolved, increase the heat to medium-high and bring the mixture to a boil. Do not stir once it starts boiling.

Using a candy thermometer, monitor the temperature of the syrup. You want the syrup to reach 240°F (115°C), also known as the soft-ball stage. This stage is critical for creating the ideal texture for your marshmallows.

Step 3: Combine the Gelatin and Sugar Syrup

Once the sugar syrup has reached the correct temperature, carefully remove the saucepan from the heat. Take the bloomed gelatin and pour the hot sugar syrup over it. The gelatin will dissolve into the syrup. Be cautious, as the syrup will be very hot.

Using the whisk attachment on your stand mixer (or a hand mixer if you don’t have a stand mixer), start mixing on low speed. Gradually increase the speed to medium-high and beat the mixture for about 10-15 minutes. The mixture will gradually thicken and transform into a fluffy, glossy meringue-like texture. At this point, you can add 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract and a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor.

Step 4: Prepare the Pan and Transfer the Mixture

While the marshmallow mixture is whipping, prepare your pan. Lightly grease an 8x8-inch or 9x9-inch baking pan with non-stick spray or a thin layer of oil. Dust the bottom and sides of the pan with a mixture of powdered sugar and cornstarch to prevent the marshmallows from sticking to the pan.

Once the marshmallow mixture has reached its desired consistency and looks fluffy, pour it into the prepared pan. Use a spatula or a spoon to spread the mixture evenly, ensuring it reaches all corners of the pan. Don’t worry if the top looks a bit uneven; you’ll be dusting it with powdered sugar later to make it look pristine.

Step 5: Let the Marshmallows Set

This is the hardest part — waiting! You need to allow the marshmallows to set at room temperature for at least 4 hours, but ideally overnight. The marshmallows will firm up and become spongy during this time.

Step 6: Cutting the Marshmallows

After your marshmallows have set and become firm, it’s time to cut them into pieces. To make this process easier, dust a cutting surface with powdered sugar and cornstarch. You can also dust your knife with the mixture to prevent the marshmallows from sticking.

Carefully cut the marshmallows into squares or rectangles. The size is entirely up to you, but standard marshmallows are usually around 1-inch squares. If you’d like to get creative, you can use cookie cutters to make fun shapes like stars, hearts, or even festive holiday-themed marshmallows.

Step 7: Coat the Marshmallows

To ensure the marshmallows don’t stick together, toss each piece in a mixture of powdered sugar and cornstarch. Coat all sides thoroughly to create a non-stick layer that will help the marshmallows stay fresh and separate.

Step 8: Enjoy Your Homemade Marshmallows

Once your marshmallows are coated and cut, they’re ready to be enjoyed! These homemade marshmallows are perfect for making s’mores, adding to hot cocoa, or simply eating on their own as a sweet treat. They also make wonderful gifts when packaged in cute jars or bags.

Tips for Making Perfect Marshmallows

1. Use a Candy Thermometer

One of the most important tools for making marshmallows is a candy thermometer. Without it, it can be difficult to know when the sugar syrup has reached the right temperature. Investing in a good-quality thermometer will make the process much easier.

2. Don’t Skip the Resting Time

Allowing the marshmallows to set properly is key to achieving the right texture. Don’t try to rush this process by refrigerating them; room temperature is best for allowing the marshmallows to firm up without becoming too dense or soggy.

3. Experiment with Flavors

Feel free to experiment with different flavorings and extracts. Adding a few tablespoons of cocoa powder will give you chocolate marshmallows, or a few drops of peppermint extract will add a festive touch. The possibilities are endless!

4. Make It a Family Activity

Making marshmallows from scratch is a fun and interactive process. Get the whole family involved by having everyone help with the mixing, cutting, and coating of the marshmallows. It’s a great way to spend time together and create something delicious.

5. Store Properly

To keep your marshmallows fresh for longer, store them in an airtight container at room temperature. They will stay soft and fresh for up to 2 weeks. If you’re making a large batch, you can also freeze marshmallows for longer storage. Just be sure to coat them well with powdered sugar to prevent them from sticking together in the freezer.

Conclusion

Making homemade marshmallows from scratch is a rewarding and fun process that results in a delectable treat. With just a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can create light, fluffy, and flavorful marshmallows that are far superior to store-bought ones. Whether you’re using them to top off a dessert or enjoying them by themselves, homemade marshmallows are sure to be a hit. So why not give it a try? The results are sweet, soft, and oh-so-satisfying.
