Fresh and Zesty: Lemon Asparagus Risotto for a Springtime Treat


As the last wisps of winter’s chill dissipate, the arrival of spring brings with it a sense of renewal and rejuvenation. The season’s fresh flavors and vibrant colors are the perfect inspiration for a light and zesty dish that captures the essence of the season. In this recipe, we’ll combine the sweetness of asparagus with the brightness of lemon and the creamy richness of risotto, creating a truly springtime treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the asparagus: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch the asparagus for 4-5 minutes, or until tender but still crisp. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the onion and garlic: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  3. Cook the Arborio rice: Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice is coated in oil and slightly toasted.
  4. Add the white wine (if using): If using white wine, add it to the skillet and cook until the liquid is almost completely absorbed, stirring constantly.
  5. Add the warmed broth: Add 1 cup of warmed broth to the skillet and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is mostly absorbed. Repeat this process, adding the broth in 1-cup increments, until the rice is cooked and creamy, about 20-25 minutes.
  6. Add the lemon juice and butter: Stir in the freshly squeezed lemon juice and unsalted butter until the butter is melted and the rice is coated in the lemony goodness.
  7. Combine the asparagus and risotto: Add the cooked asparagus to the risotto and stir to combine.
  8. Finish with Parmesan cheese: Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until melted and well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Garnish with parsley (if desired): Sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top of the risotto, if desired, for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added burst of citrus flavor, use lemon zest in place of some of the grated Parmesan cheese.
  • Substitute other spring vegetables, such as peas or bell peppers, for the asparagus.
  • Add some protein to the dish by stirring in cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu.
  • Make the risotto ahead of time and refrigerate or freeze for up to 3 days. Reheat gently before serving.

Conclusion

This Lemon Asparagus Risotto is the perfect springtime treat, with its bright and zesty flavors and creamy, comforting texture. Whether you’re serving it as a main course or as a side dish, this recipe is sure to delight your taste buds and leave you feeling refreshed and rejuvenated. So go ahead, give it a try, and let the flavors of springtime transport you to a brighter, more vibrant world.
