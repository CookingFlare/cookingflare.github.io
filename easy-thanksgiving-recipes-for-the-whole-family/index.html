Easy Thanksgiving Recipes for the Whole Family


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with loved ones, enjoy hearty meals, and celebrate gratitude. While the holiday brings joy, it can also be stressful when you’re tasked with preparing a feast. The pressure to make everything perfect can be overwhelming, especially when you’re trying to balance time, effort, and taste. But don’t worry! You don’t need to spend all day in the kitchen to create a memorable meal. These easy Thanksgiving recipes are perfect for the whole family and will leave everyone at the table asking for seconds.

In this post, we will explore simple, yet delicious dishes that will make your Thanksgiving dinner a breeze to prepare. From classic main courses to satisfying sides and delightful desserts, you’ll find recipes that cater to all tastes and dietary preferences. So, let’s dive in!

1. Oven-Roasted Turkey with Herb Butter

The star of any Thanksgiving meal is the turkey. However, roasting a turkey doesn’t have to be complicated. By using a few simple ingredients and techniques, you can create a moist, flavorful bird that will impress everyone.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (10-12 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Pat the turkey dry with paper towels. This helps achieve crispy skin.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter with garlic, rosemary, thyme, sage, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Carefully lift the skin of the turkey and rub half of the herb butter directly on the meat. Spread the remaining butter on the outside of the turkey.
  5. Stuff the turkey cavity with the lemon halves and onion quarters.
  6. Place the turkey on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the pan.
  7. Roast the turkey for about 13 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C) in the thickest part of the thigh.
  8. Let the turkey rest for at least 20 minutes before carving.

This simple herb butter turkey will have a crispy, golden skin and tender, juicy meat—sure to be the highlight of your meal.

2. Creamy Mashed Potatoes

No Thanksgiving dinner is complete without mashed potatoes. This recipe is creamy, buttery, and perfectly seasoned, making it a perfect side dish to complement the turkey.

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds russet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • Fresh chives, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  2. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.
  3. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream and butter over low heat until the butter is melted.
  4. Mash the potatoes with a potato masher or a hand mixer until smooth. Slowly add the warm cream and butter mixture, mashing until desired creaminess is achieved.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic (if using). Stir in fresh chives for a burst of color and flavor.

These creamy mashed potatoes will melt in your mouth and pair beautifully with your roasted turkey.

3. Classic Green Bean Almondine

Green beans are a classic Thanksgiving side dish, but this recipe takes them to the next level with a simple almondine twist. The toasted almonds add a nutty crunch that complements the tender green beans perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the green beans and cook for 4-5 minutes, until they are tender-crisp. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced almonds and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the almonds are golden brown and toasted.
  3. Add the cooked green beans to the skillet and toss to coat in the buttery almond mixture. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
  4. Serve immediately, garnished with additional almonds if desired.

This dish is an elegant and easy way to elevate your Thanksgiving table, offering a delightful balance of flavors and textures.

4. Sweet Potato Casserole with Marshmallow Topping

Sweet potatoes are another Thanksgiving favorite, and this casserole with a marshmallow topping brings out the natural sweetness of the potatoes in a comforting, indulgent way. It’s a crowd-pleaser that’s as easy to make as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups mini marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil the sweet potatoes in a large pot of water for about 15-20 minutes, until tender. Drain the water and mash the potatoes until smooth.
  3. Stir in the brown sugar, melted butter, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until well combined.
  4. Transfer the sweet potato mixture to a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Spread the mixture evenly in the dish.
  5. Top with mini marshmallows and bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden and puffy.
  6. Let cool slightly before serving.

This sweet potato casserole is a guaranteed hit, with the gooey marshmallows offering the perfect contrast to the rich and creamy sweet potatoes.

5. Cranberry Sauce with Orange and Cinnamon

Homemade cranberry sauce is far superior to the canned variety, and it’s surprisingly easy to make. This recipe combines the tartness of fresh cranberries with the bright flavors of orange and a touch of cinnamon for a refreshing and aromatic sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the cranberries, orange juice, orange zest, sugar, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.
  3. Remove from heat and let cool to room temperature. The sauce will continue to thicken as it cools.
  4. Transfer to a serving dish and refrigerate until ready to serve.

This homemade cranberry sauce adds a fresh and tangy component to your Thanksgiving spread and is easy to make ahead of time.

6. Pumpkin Pie with Gingersnap Crust

No Thanksgiving meal is complete without dessert, and pumpkin pie is a must-have. This version is made with a gingersnap crust for an added burst of flavor that complements the classic spiced pumpkin filling perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade if preferred)
  • 1 1/2 cups canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup gingersnap cookies, crushed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the crushed gingersnap cookies with a tablespoon of sugar and a pinch of salt. Press the mixture into the bottom of a pie dish to form the crust.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, brown sugar, eggs, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, cloves, and salt until smooth.
  4. Pour the pumpkin filling into the gingersnap crust and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the center is set and a toothpick comes out clean.
  5. Let the pie cool completely before slicing and serving.

This pumpkin pie is a delightful finish to your Thanksgiving meal, with the spicy gingersnap crust offering a unique twist on a classic dessert.

7. Apple Crisp

If you’re looking for another dessert option that’s just as comforting and delicious, apple crisp is a great choice. It’s easy to make, and the combination of tart apples and a buttery oat topping is simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss the apple slices with granulated sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Transfer the apples to a greased 9x9-inch baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, butter, brown sugar, and salt. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix until the mixture forms crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.
  6. Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for extra indulgence.

This apple crisp is the perfect autumn dessert, warm and sweet, and a great way to end your Thanksgiving feast.

Conclusion

Thanksgiving doesn’t have to be a stressful affair in the kitchen. With these easy recipes, you can create a mouthwatering spread that will satisfy your entire family. From a simple, flavorful turkey to comforting mashed potatoes, vibrant green beans, and irresistible desserts, these dishes offer everything you need to make your Thanksgiving dinner a success. Enjoy the holiday, cherish your time with loved ones, and most importantly—dig into these delicious dishes!
