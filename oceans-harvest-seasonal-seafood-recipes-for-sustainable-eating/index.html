Ocean's Harvest: Seasonal Seafood Recipes for Sustainable Eating


In a world where our oceans are facing unprecedented challenges, it’s getting more and more important to consume seafood in a sustainable manner. This flavorful adventure into the depths of the sea welcomes you to the bountiful world of Ocean’s Harvest, a seasonal seafood recipe collection that will take you on a culinary journey while promoting sustainable eating habits. Dive in and explore how you can bring the ocean’s bounty to your dining table with recipes that are not only delectable but also eco-conscious.

Spring: The Acme of Bounty

The spring season heralds the arrival of a multitude of seafood delicacies that are perfect for your palate and the planet. The cooler waters bring a plethora of thriving choices.

1. Roussettes Meunière with Lemon Garlic Linguine

Quiver in delight at the site of the roussettes meunière, a delicate flatfish that deserves a starring role on your plates. Paired with the nutty warmth of lemon garlic linguine, this dish truly captures the essence of springtime flavors. Verve your taste buds with this simple yet exquisite recipe.

  • For 4 servings:

Ingredients:

  • 8-10 roussettes (blackback flounder or dab)
  • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups vegetable oil
  • Juice and zest from 1 lemon
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 cup flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  • 1 pound linguine noodles
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 3 lemons, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large frying pan to 360°F.
  2. Dredge the roussettes in flour, patting off the excess, and gently slip into the oil. Fry until golden brown, about 7-8 minutes, and then flip, frying for another 5 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter until creamy. Set aside.
  4. In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook the linguine according to package instructions. In a separate pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat, adding the garlic, and cook until lightly golden. Add the lemon slices in a single layer and cook for about 3 minutes.
  5. Drain the linguine, reserving 1 cup of pasta water. Add the linguine to the lemon and garlic pan, tossing to coat with the oil. Slowly pour in the lemon-butter sauce, adding pasta water if necessary to achieve a silky sauce.
  6. Plate the linguine, and place the meunière roussettes on top. Sprinkle with parsley and grated Parmesan, and serve immediately.

2. Saffron Cauliflower “Sushi” Rolls

Substitute rice with cauliflower for a healthier twist on traditional sushi rolls. The ocean’s counterpart, kombu dashi, brings out the umami in this dish, wrapped in nori and served atop a bed of fragrant saffron infused watercress salad.

  • For 4 servings:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cauliflower, grated
  • 1 cup kombu dashi
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup sushi rice, cooked
  • 8 sheets of nori
  • 1 avocado
  • 8 ounces soft tofu
  • 8 cooked shrimp, deveined and peeled

Instructions:

  1. Place the kombu dashi, sake, mirin, rice vinegar, and salt into a saucepan, heating gently until salt dissolves, and the mixture starts to steam. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly before pouring over the grated cauliflower. Set aside to cool completely.
  2. Squeeze out excess moisture from the cauliflower-rice mixture and stir in the cooked sushi rice. Mixture should hold together when pressed.
  3. Lay out a sheet of nori and spread a thin layer of the cauliflower sushi rice on it, leaving a margin at the top edge. Place a line of sliced avocado, tofu, and shrimp in the center.
  4. Roll up the nori tightly, using a bamboo sushi mat to secure the roll. Repeat with the remaining nori and fillings. Use a sharp knife to slice the rolls into bite-sized pieces.
  5. For the saffron watercress salad, in a small bowl, mix together 1/8 teaspoon of saffron strands with 2 tablespoons of warm water, allowing the saffron to bloom for about 10 minutes. In a separate bowl, combine 1 cup of watercress, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and 1 tablespoon of the saffron-infused water, massaging the watercress leaves to coat them thoroughly.
  6. Plate the watercress salad on a flat surface, and arrange the sushi rolls on top for a visually stunning and healthy meal.

Summer: Sun-Kissed Seafood

When the heat of the sun is in full force, indulge in the refreshing and cooling offerings from the vast ocean.

3. Hibachi-Grilled Tilapia with Yuzu Matcha Slaw

Experience the fusion of flavors in this zesty dish where yuzu and matcha meet in a bright and bold slaw to accompany the simplicity of grilled tilapia.

  • For 4 servings:

Ingredients:

  • 4 tilapia fillets
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper

For the Yuzu Matcha Slaw:

  • 4 cups shredded napa cabbage
  • Juice of 1 yuzu (or 1 lemon)
  • 1 tablespoon matcha powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together the soy sauce, mirin, matcha, honey, salt, and pepper in a shallow dish. Add the tilapia fillets, cover, and refrigerate for 1-2 hours.
  2. Preheat your hibachi grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush the grates with oil, then remove the tilapia from the marinade and place it on the grill. Cook for 4-5 minutes on each side, until lightly charred and cooked through.
  3. While the tilapia is grilling, prepare the slaw. Whisk together the yuzu juice, matcha, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss the shredded napa cabbage with the dressing, ensuring an even coating of flavor.
  4. Plate the yuzu matcha slaw, and place the hibachi-grilled tilapia on top. This dish offers a delightful taste of summer with its harmony of freshness and heat, leaving a lasting impression on any seafood lover’s palate.

Conclusion

As we welcome the bounty the ocean offers along the ebbs and flows of the seasons, let’s be mindful of the sustainable practices required for a healthy future. Embrace Ocean’s Harvest with flavorful, seasonally-specific recipes, and savor the ocean’s gifts while preserving its delicate balance for generations to come. Happy cooking!
