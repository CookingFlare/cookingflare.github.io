Grilled Swordfish Steaks: A Taste of the Sea


Swordfish, an underutilized yet delicious and healthy option, is gaining popularity among seafood lovers. Rich in protein and heart-healthy Omega-3 fatty acids, swordfish is an excellent addition to any diet. One of the best ways to enjoy this delectable seafood is by grilling it to perfection. In this guide, we’ll be sharing our foolproof recipe for succulent and mouthwatering grilled swordfish steaks.

The Perfect Grilled Swordfish Steak

To highlight the natural flavors and texture of the swordfish, we’ll be using basic yet delicious marinade ingredients. This way, you’ll let the seafood shine while still adding a layer of flavor that complements the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 swordfish steaks
  • Salt
  • Ground black pepper
  • Olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil

Directions:

  1. Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. This will ensure the perfect sear for your fish – not too low to leave it uncrisped, but not too high to allow it to burn.

  2. Season the swordfish steaks generously on both sides with salt and black pepper. It’s essential to properly season the fish since it’s such a lean protein; the spices will add flavor and help the steaks stick to the grill.

  3. In a shallow dish or bowl, mix one tablespoon of olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, oregano, and basil.

  4. Place the swordfish steaks in a resealable plastic bag or a dish with a lid. Pour the marinade over the steaks, ensuring they’re well-coated. The swordfish needs to marinate at least an hour to absorb the flavors adequately. Longer is even better, up to 6 hours.

  5. Once your grill is adequately heated, remove the swordfish steaks from their marinade and shake off any excess. Place them on the grill, and resist the urge to play with the fish during the first few minutes while it seals.

  6. Keep the steaks on the grill for approximately six minutes per side, depending on their thickness. They’ll be done when they ace the internal temperature of 145°F. Be attentive, as swordfish steaks can easily dry out and overcook.

  7. Once removed from the grill, let the steaks rest for about five minutes, enabling the juices to redistribute throughout the fish. Doing so will encourage a juicier final product.

  8. Serve your grilled swordfish steaks with a refreshing side salad, zesty tzatziki, or a well-made pasta dish to round out your meal.

Tips for Success

  • Buy the best fish: Buy the freshest swordfish available. It should smell briny, not fishy, and eyes should be clear, not clouded.

  • Rest the Fish: Allowing the swordfish to rest after grilling helps retain those beautiful flavors. It’s an essential step often overlooked.

  • Choose Big Steaks: If you’re not confident in your grilling abilities or have a smaller grill, opt for larger steaks. They’re more forgiving and provide a larger margin for error.

Your grilled swordfish steaks are a testament to the beauty of fresh seafood, prepared simply and with love. Whether you’re hosting a romantic dinner for two or a summer gathering, this satisfying dish leaves your guests longing for more. So lure them into the realm of under-utilized swordfish – we guarantee they’ll be coming back for seconds.
