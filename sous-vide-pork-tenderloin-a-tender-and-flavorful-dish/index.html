Sous Vide Pork Tenderloin: A Tender and Flavorful Dish


If you’re a fan of tender, succulent pork that practically melts in your mouth, then you’re in for a treat with this sous vide pork tenderloin recipe. Sous vide, a cooking technique that involves vacuum-sealing food in a bag and cooking it to a precise temperature in a water bath, is a game-changer when it comes to achieving perfect doneness and flavor. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create a mouthwatering sous vide pork tenderloin that will have your taste buds singing.

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil
  • Optional: your choice of seasoning or rub

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Sous Vide Water Bath: Fill a large pot or container with water and attach your sous vide precision cooker. Set the temperature to 140°F (60°C) for a medium finish or 150°F (65.5°C) for a more well-done texture.

  2. Prepare the Pork Tenderloin: Season the pork tenderloins with salt, pepper, and your choice of seasoning or rub. Place the seasoned pork in a vacuum-sealable bag with the minced garlic, fresh rosemary, and thyme. Drizzle with a touch of olive oil to enhance the flavors. Vacuum-seal the bag, ensuring a tight seal to prevent any water from entering.

  3. Sous Vide Cooking: Once the water bath reaches the desired temperature, carefully submerge the sealed pork tenderloins into the water. Ensure that the pork is fully submerged. Cook for 2-3 hours for a perfectly tender result.

  4. Finishing Touch: After the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the pork tenderloins from the water bath. Gently pat dry with paper towels. Optionally, you can sear the pork in a hot skillet for a minute on each side to create a flavorful crust.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Slice the sous vide pork tenderloin into medallions and serve with your favorite sides. Whether it’s roasted vegetables, a fresh salad, or creamy mashed potatoes, this tender and flavorful dish pairs well with a variety of accompaniments.

Tips and Tricks

  • Seasoning: Feel free to experiment with different seasoning blends to customize the flavor profile of your pork tenderloin. From smoky paprika to zesty citrus rubs, the options are endless.
  • Searing: While optional, a quick sear after sous vide cooking adds a delightful caramelization to the pork and enhances its visual appeal.
  • Temperature: Adjust the sous vide cooking temperature based on your desired level of doneness. For a blush pink center, aim for 140°F (60°C), while 150°F (65.5°C) will yield a more uniformly cooked result.

Conclusion

Sous vide pork tenderloin is not only a show-stopping main course, but it’s also a testament to the magic of precision cooking. By harnessing the power of sous vide, you can elevate this humble cut of pork into a dish that’s worthy of the finest dining establishments. The tenderness and flavor that this cooking method imparts will make it a staple in your culinary repertoire. So, fire up your sous vide precision cooker, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the delectable results of this sous vide pork tenderloin recipe. Cheers to creating memorable dining experiences right in your own kitchen!
