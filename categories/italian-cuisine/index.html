Pasta alle Vongole (Clams Pasta) Ingredients: 1 pound spaghetti 2 pounds littleneck clams, scrubbed Extra-virgin olive oil 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced 1/2 cup dry white wine 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley 1 1/2 tablespoons red pepper flakes Grated Pecorino Romano cheese Instructions: In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook pasta according to package directions until al dente. In a large skillet, heat extra-virgin olive oil over medium heat. Add sliced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add clams and white wine, cover and cook until clams open, about 5-7 minutes. Once pasta is cooked, drain and add to the skillet. Toss to combine with the clam mixture. Add parsley, red pepper flakes, and drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil. Toss to coat. Serve pasta with a generous sprinkling of grated Pecorino Romano cheese. Tiramisu Ingredients: 1 pound mascarpone cheese, room temperature 1/4 cup granulated sugar 4 large egg yolks 1/4 cup whole milk 1/2 cup strong brewed coffee, warm 1/4 cup dark rum 12 ounces ladyfingers 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder Instructions: In a medium bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale, about 2-3 minutes. Whisk in milk and set the bowl over a saucepan of simmering water, making sure the water does not touch the bottom of the bowl. Whisk constantly until the mixture thickens and reaches 160°F (71°C), 2-4 minutes. Remove the bowl from the heat and whisk in the mascarpone until smooth. In a small bowl, stir together coffee and rum and arrange ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of a 9-inch square baking dish. Spoon half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, then drizzle with half the coffee mixture. Arrange another layer of ladyfingers on top and spoon the remaining mascarpone mixture. Drizzle in the remaining coffee mixture and dust the top with cocoa powder. Let tiramisu chill for at least 4 hours before serving. Italian-Style Chicken and Mushroom Risotto with White Wine

Risotto, a classic Italian dish, is a staple of many Italian restaurants and homes. The creamy texture, rich flavors, and variety of ingredients used in risotto make it a versatile and satisfying meal. In this recipe, we will combine the flavors of chicken, mushrooms, and white wine to create a delicious and authentic Italian-Style Chicken and Mushroom Risotto. Ingredients 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces 2 cups mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake), cleaned and sliced 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup Arborio rice 4 cups chicken broth, warmed 1/2 cup white wine (dry) 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1 tablespoon butter Salt and pepper, to taste Fresh parsley, chopped (optional) Instructions Prepare the ingredients: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5 minutes. Add the onion and garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Cook the rice: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the chicken broth over low heat. Add the Arborio rice and cook, stirring constantly, for 1-2 minutes. The rice should be coated in the broth and slightly toasted. Add the wine and cook: Add the white wine to the pot and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is almost completely absorbed, about 2-3 minutes. Add the chicken and mushrooms: Add the cooked chicken and mushrooms to the pot and stir to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Add the Parmesan cheese: Add the Parmesan cheese to the pot and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until the cheese is melted and the risotto is creamy. Finish with butter and season: Remove the pot from the heat and stir in the butter until melted. Season the risotto with salt and pepper to taste. Serve: Serve the risotto immediately, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired. Tips and Variations For a creamier risotto, add 1-2 tablespoons of heavy cream or half-and-half towards the end of cooking. To add more flavor to the risotto, sauté some chopped shallots or scallions along with the onion and garlic. For a vegetarian version, substitute the chicken with roasted or sautéed vegetables, such as bell peppers, zucchini, or eggplant. To make the risotto ahead of time, cook the rice and chicken mixture, then refrigerate or freeze until ready to reheat and finish with butter and Parmesan cheese. Conclusion Italian-Style Chicken and Mushroom Risotto with White Wine is a delicious and authentic Italian dish that is perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in. The combination of creamy risotto, tender chicken, and earthy mushrooms is a match made in heaven.
