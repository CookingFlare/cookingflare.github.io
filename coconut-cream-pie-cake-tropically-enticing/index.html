Coconut Cream Pie Cake: Tropically Enticing


Imagine a dessert that combines your love for coconut cream pie with the richness of a classic cake. Now picture tasting the creamy layers of dreamy coconut custard, the airy whipped cream to top it off, and finally, the delightful crunch from the cake layer. Ladies and gentlemen, let me introduce you to the Coconut Cream Pie Cake – a heavenly masterpiece that will whisk your taste buds to tropical zen.

The Indulgent History

The metaphorical love story between coconuts and desserts dates back to the ancient Polynesians, who were known to indulge in various coconut-based treats likesticky rice pudding. Fast-forward a few millennia, and the coconut cream pie was born, gaining popularity in Americasoutheastthanks to its creamy filling and sweet, flaky crust. As for the cake itself, it’s a classic in most households. But when you blend them together, you’re given a heavenly tropical delight. And that, my dear readers, is the story of Coconut Cream Pie Cake – the tropical enticing dessert that will switch on your taste buds to “entertain” mode!

Sumptuous Preparation

Now, who said preparing that luscious Coconut Cream Pie Cake would put you through the culinary wringer, huh? Although the recipe may seem quite informal with its gushing layers of flavor, it’ll require some precision to pull it off. Don’t fret; this guide will take you through the process step by step.

Ingredients:

  • 1 & 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 2/3 cup full-fat milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 & 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 3 cups unsweetened fresh coconut, toasted
  • 1 & 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 & 1/4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 4 cups cold heavy whipping cream
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Additional granulated sugar for sprinkling
  • Additional coconut flakes for sprinkle

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease three 8-inch cake pans and line the bottom with parchment paper.

  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the room-temperature butter and mix using a fork or pastry cutter, until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Gradually pour this mixture into the dry ingredients, mixing until the batter is smooth, free of lumps, and slightly thick.

  4. Fold in the toasted coconut flakes and divide the batter evenly among the three prepared pans. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the center is set. Let them cool on a wire rack for 10 minutes before removing them from the pans and cooling them completely.

  5. While the cakes are cooling, prepare the coconut custard filling. In a saucepan, combine the sugar and egg yolks, whisking until the mixture is pale yellow.

  6. Add the flour and gradually whisk in the milk, making sure you break up any lumps in the process.

  7. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens.

  8. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the unsalted butter and vanilla extract until the butter is fully incorporated and the mixture is smooth.

  9. Pour the custard mixture over the cooled cakes, making sure it’s evenly distributed. Refrigerate the cakes for about an hour while you prepare the whipped cream topping.

  10. In a large mixing bowl, beat the whipping cream, sugar, and cocoa powder until stiff peaks form.

  11. Once the custard layer has set on the cake layers, place one layer on a serving plate. Spread a generous layer of whipped cream and repeat the process with the second and third layers.

  12. Crumble some extra toasted coconut flakes and sprinkle them over the top of the cake. For a gourmet touch, sprinkle a bit of additional granulated sugar. Chill the Coconut Cream Pie Cake for an hour in the fridge before serving. Enjoy!
