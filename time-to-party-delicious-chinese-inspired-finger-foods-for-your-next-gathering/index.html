Time to Party: Delicious Chinese-inspired Finger Foods for Your Next Gathering


Are you ready to spice up your next gathering with an array of mouth-watering, Chinese-inspired finger foods? These appetizers bring the flavors of China effortlessly to your next party, ensuring your guests will be coming back for seconds. From savory dumplings to sweet red bean buns, let’s dive into these tasteful recipes tailored for easy entertaining.

1. Crispy Spring Rolls

Start your Chinese-inspired appetizer spread with these addictive Crispy Spring Rolls. These tasty morsels pack a delightful assortment of vegetables and are perfect for both vegetarians and meat-lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup finely shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup finely shredded carrots
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bamboo shoots
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped water chestnuts
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped celery
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 40 spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, sauté cabbage, carrots, onions, bell peppers, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, water chestnuts, and celery for 5-7 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  2. Add the optional protein of your choice, soy sauce, sesame oil, and garlic powder. Cook for an additional 2 minutes, stirring well to combine.
  3. Allow the filling to cool before assembling. Take a spring roll wrapper, place it diagonally with one corner pointing towards you. Place about 2 tablespoons of filling in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the bottom corner over the filling, tuck it in, then fold in the sides. Roll the wrapper away from you and slightly moisten the remaining corner with water to seal. Repeat until all the spring rolls are assembled.
  5. In a large pan or deep fryer, heat the cooking oil to 375°F (190°C). Fry spring rolls in batches for 2-3 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Drain well and serve hot with your choice of sweet chili sauce or tangy ketchup for dipping.

2. Dumpling Trio: Pork & Chive, Vegetable, and Spicy Shrimp

Dumplings are a favorite finger food at any gathering, and these three varieties are a delightful assortment of flavors and textures. Whether omnivores, vegetarians, or but seafood-lovers, the Dumpling Trio has something for everyone.

Ingredients:

Pork & Chive Dumplings:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 cup finely chopped chives
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped ginger
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 20 dumpling wrappers

Vegetable Dumplings:

  • 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 1 cup finely chopped carrots
  • 1 cup finely chopped spinach
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped water chestnuts
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bamboo shoots
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 20 dumpling wrappers

Spicy Shrimp Dumplings:

  • 1 pound shrimp, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped ginger
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red bell peppers
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped garlic
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons chili sauce
  • 20 dumpling wrappers

Instructions:

Pork & Chive Dumplings:

  1. Mix together the ground pork, chives, green onions, ginger, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Set aside.
  2. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of a dumpling wrapper. Wet the edges with water, then fold the dumpling to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges together to seal.
  3. Repeat with the remaining dumpling wrappers and filling.

Vegetable Dumplings:

  1. Mix together the cabbage, carrots, spinach, onions, mushrooms, water chestnuts, bamboo shoots, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Set aside.
  2. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of a dumpling wrapper. Wet the edges with water, then fold the dumpling to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges together to seal.
  3. Repeat with the remaining dumpling wrappers and filling.

Spicy Shrimp Dumplings:

  1. Mix together the shrimp, green onions, ginger, red bell peppers, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, and chili sauce. Set aside.
  2. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of a dumpling wrapper. Wet the edges with water, then fold the dumpling to form a half-moon shape, pressing the edges together to seal.
  3. Repeat with the remaining dumpling wrappers and filling.

Steaming Instructions:

  1. Fill a steamer pot with about 2 inches of water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Place the dumplings on a parchment-lined steamer tray, ensuring they do not touch each other.
  3. Steam for 8-10 minutes, or until the filling is cooked through and the wrappers are translucent.

Pan-Frying Instructions:

  1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and add a tablespoon of cooking oil.
  2. Place the dumplings in the pan, making sure they do not touch each other.
  3. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the bottoms are golden brown.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of water to the pan and cover with a lid. Cook for an additional 5-6 minutes, or until the water has evaporated, and the dumplings are cooked through.

Serve the dumplings with soy sauce, chili oil, or your favorite dipping sauce!

3. Red Bean Buns

These sweet and savory Red Bean Buns are an irresistible twist on a Chinese classic. A perfect finger food for any party, they are sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup bread flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup red bean paste (homemade or store-bought)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the all-purpose flour, bread flour, instant yeast, warm water, sugar, and salt until a shaggy dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough for 8-10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.
  3. Gradually add the melted butter to the dough and continue to knead until the dough is soft, supple, and slightly sticky.
  4. Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out on a lightly floured surface into a 12x15-inch rectangle.
  6. Spread the red bean paste evenly over the dough, leaving about 1-inch of space on all sides.
  7. Starting from the long side, roll the dough tightly into a log, pressing the edges to seal.
  8. Cut the log into 1-inch pieces and place them cut-side up on a lightly greased or parchment-lined baking sheet.
  9. Cover the buns with a damp cloth and let them rise for an additional 30 minutes.
  10. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the buns for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.
  11. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving.

These Red Bean Buns are scrumptious on their own, but serve them alongside a cup of Chinese tea or a steaming cup of coffee if you’d like!

The Chinese-inspired finger foods shared above promise a delightful sensory journey for your party-goers. Brimming with flavor, these dishes are sure to make your next gathering unforgettable. So gather your ingredients, inspire your guests with these enticing recipes, and get ready to create unforgettable memories. Happy cooking and hosting!
