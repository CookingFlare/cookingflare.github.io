A Day in Rome: Discover the Charm of Roman-style Flatbreads and Pizza Recipes


Ah, Rome! The Eternal City with its storied streets, waiting to be explored and savored one bite at a time. This charming city, brimming with history, art, and culture, also takes pride in its culinary treasures. One cannot talk about Roman cuisine without mentioning the mouthwatering Roman-style flatbreads and pizzas that evoke the aroma and flavors of Rome in every bite. Buckle up and join us on a gastronomic journey as we uncloak the secrets of these delicious mainstays.

The Roman-style Flatbread: Schiacciata

Our first stop to explore the tantalizing world of Roman-style flatbreads is a delightful rectangle named Schiacciata. This flavorful, crisp-on-the-outside and soft-on-the-inside bread is often topped with olive oil, rosemary, and coarse salt, providing a taste reminiscent of the fragrant breeze that wafts off Rome’s cobblestoned alleyways. The simplicity of this bread’s ingredients is what makes its taste beyond compare.

Schiacciata with Sardinian Tomato Pesto

  1. Start off by heating 2 tablespoons olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Once heated, add 2 garlic cloves, finely minced, and cook for a minute.
  3. Stir in ½ cup of sun-dried tomatoes that have been chopped and let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add ½ cup of pine nuts and sauté until they are golden brown.
  5. Incorporate 1 cup of fresh basil leaves along with ⅓ cup of grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese into the pan, mixing well.
  6. Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool, then blend all the ingredients together into a smooth paste.
  7. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and generously brush a sheet of parchment paper with olive oil.
  8. Roll out a piece of fresh dough on the oiled parchment paper into a rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick.
  9. Stretch the dough carefully from the edges to create a larger surface area for your topping.
  10. Spread the cooled tomato pesto mixture evenly on the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  11. Drizzle extra virgin olive oil on top of the schiacciata.
  12. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and crisp.
  13. Slice into pieces and serve warm. Your Schiacciata with Sardinian Tomato Pesto is ready!

Roman Pizza: The Piadina Romana

Every great pizza story needs a star, and in Rome, that’s the wonderful Piadina Romana. This flatbread, similar to the schiacciata but with a more delicate texture, is traditionally made from a mixture of wheat flour, water, salt, and olive oil. Yields a thin, foldable bread that wraps around a diversity of fillings to make it suitable for any time of the day.

Piadina Romana with Porchetta

  1. Start by gathering 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 cup of whole wheat flour, and ½ cup of fine semolina flour. Mix gently.
  2. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and 1 tablespoon of cooking oil to the flour mixture, then gradually pour in 1-1½ cups of lukewarm water.
  3. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic. Cover and let it rest for an hour.
  4. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal-sized pieces, then roll each into a ball.
  5. Flatten each ball gently with your hands, cover, and let it rest for another 30 minutes.
  6. In a separate bowl, prepare the porchetta filling: mix 2 lbs of boneless pork shoulder, 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 teaspoon of black pepper, ⅛ teaspoon of nutmeg, 1 tablespoon of whole fennel seeds, 2 cloves of garlic, and small chopped onion.
  7. Marinate the pork shoulder in the mixture for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  8. Preheat your pizza stone or oven-safe skillet to a high temperature.
  9. Roll your piadina dough thinly on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch.
  10. Cook the dough on the hot stone/pan for about 2-3 minutes on each side, until puffed and slightly charred.
  11. Layer the cooked piadina with slices of spicy porchetta, provolone cheese, arugula, and a dollop of garlic aioli.
  12. Wrap the piadina into a neat package and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Delizioso!

The Roman-style flatbreads and pizzas are testaments to Rome’s love for delicious, simple meals. So, grab your skillet, gather your ingredients, and bring a piece of the Eternal City into your kitchen by creating these wonderful Rome-inspired recipes. The incredible culture of Rome will undoubtedly transport you to its storied streets as you enjoy each savory bite.

Keep savoring the flavors and immerse yourself in the culinary traditions Rome has to offer. Your culinary adventure into Rome’s flavors awaits, so don’t hesitate to become a master of its pizza and flatbreads, one delicious bite at a time. Buon appetito!
