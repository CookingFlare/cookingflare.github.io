10 Best Japanese Sushi Recipes for Beginners


Sushi, a beloved dish that has gained global popularity, is often seen as a culinary masterpiece. Whether you’re a novice cook or a seasoned pro, making sushi at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. If you’ve ever wanted to learn how to make sushi but didn’t know where to begin, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll explore 10 beginner-friendly Japanese sushi recipes that will help you create delicious sushi rolls in your own kitchen.

1. California Roll - A Staple for Beginners

The California roll is one of the most popular sushi rolls worldwide, and it’s a great starting point for beginners. It’s a maki sushi roll, which means it’s made by rolling ingredients in a seaweed sheet with rice on the outside. The California roll is often made with imitation crab, avocado, cucumber, and a dash of sesame seeds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 4 ounces imitation crab (or real crab meat)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice according to package instructions. Once done, mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let the rice cool.
  2. Place a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface and put a sheet of nori, shiny side down.
  3. Wet your hands to prevent rice from sticking. Spread a thin layer of rice evenly on the nori, leaving a 1-inch gap at the top.
  4. Lay the avocado slices, cucumber, and imitation crab across the center of the rice.
  5. Carefully roll the sushi using the bamboo mat, applying gentle pressure to form a tight roll.
  6. Sprinkle sesame seeds on the outside of the roll, then cut into bite-sized pieces.

2. Tuna Nigiri - Simple Yet Flavorful

Nigiri is a classic form of sushi that consists of a small mound of rice topped with a slice of fish or seafood. Tuna nigiri is particularly popular for its fresh, clean taste and simplicity. This recipe is perfect for beginners who want to explore sushi beyond rolls.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sushi rice
  • 1 ¼ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 ounces fresh tuna (sashimi grade)
  • Wasabi (optional)
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Slice the fresh tuna into thin pieces, about ¼ inch thick.
  3. Wet your hands with water and form small mounds of rice, about the size of a golf ball.
  4. Place a slice of tuna on top of each rice mound. If you like, spread a small amount of wasabi on the tuna for extra flavor.
  5. Serve with soy sauce on the side for dipping.

3. Vegetarian Sushi Roll - A Plant-Based Delight

For those who prefer a vegetarian option, this sushi roll is a great choice. It’s made with a variety of fresh vegetables, making it light, nutritious, and full of flavor. The key ingredients are avocado, cucumber, and pickled radish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons pickled radish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice, then mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.
  3. Spread a thin layer of rice on the nori, leaving about an inch of space at the top.
  4. Lay the avocado slices, cucumber, and pickled radish across the center of the rice.
  5. Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, then slice into bite-sized pieces.

4. Salmon Roll - A Fresh and Flavorful Classic

Salmon is one of the most popular fish for sushi, and for good reason. Its rich, buttery flavor pairs perfectly with the slight tang of vinegared rice. This sushi roll is a wonderful way to introduce yourself to fish-based sushi.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 4 ounces fresh salmon (sashimi grade), sliced into strips
  • 1 small cucumber, julienned
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Lay a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.
  3. Spread an even layer of rice over the nori.
  4. Place the fresh salmon slices and cucumber along the center of the rice.
  5. Roll the sushi tightly using the mat and slice into bite-sized pieces.

5. Shrimp Tempura Roll - Crispy and Crunchy

Shrimp tempura is a popular addition to sushi rolls, offering a crispy contrast to the soft rice and vegetables. This sushi recipe is perfect for beginners who want to make a sushi roll with a crunchy element.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 6 shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Tempura batter mix (or make your own with flour and cornstarch)
  • ½ cucumber, julienned
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Prepare the tempura batter and dip the shrimp into the batter. Fry in hot oil until golden and crispy.
  3. Lay a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.
  4. Spread a thin layer of rice over the nori.
  5. Place the shrimp, cucumber, and any other desired fillings along the center.
  6. Roll the sushi tightly and slice into pieces.

6. Dragon Roll - A Visually Stunning Roll

The Dragon Roll is as much about aesthetics as it is about taste. This sushi roll is decorated to resemble a dragon, often featuring eel, avocado, and a drizzle of eel sauce on top. It’s a fantastic roll for beginners who want to try something a little more ambitious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 4 ounces eel (unagi), grilled and sliced
  • ½ avocado, thinly sliced
  • Eel sauce for drizzling
  • Cucumber, julienned

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.
  3. Spread a thin layer of rice on the nori.
  4. Add eel, cucumber, and avocado along the center.
  5. Roll the sushi tightly and drizzle eel sauce on top. Slice into pieces and serve.

7. Spider Roll - A Crunchy and Spicy Treat

The Spider Roll is made with soft-shell crab, which provides a unique flavor and crunchy texture. This roll often includes spicy mayo and avocado for an extra kick. It’s perfect for those who enjoy a bit of spice and seafood.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 4 ounces soft-shell crab (fried)
  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • Spicy mayo (sriracha mixed with mayonnaise)
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Fry the soft-shell crab until crispy.
  3. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.
  4. Spread a layer of rice over the nori.
  5. Add the soft-shell crab, avocado, and a drizzle of spicy mayo in the center.
  6. Roll the sushi tightly, then slice into pieces.

8. Sushi Burrito - A Modern Take on Sushi

The sushi burrito combines the flavors of sushi with the convenience of a burrito. It’s a fun, larger roll that’s perfect for a meal. Fillings can vary, but a typical sushi burrito features rice, fish, and veggies wrapped in a large sheet of nori.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large sheet nori
  • ½ avocado, sliced
  • 4 ounces tuna or salmon, diced
  • Cucumber, julienned
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Lay a large sheet of nori on a flat surface.
  3. Spread a thick layer of rice on the nori.
  4. Add tuna or salmon, avocado, and cucumber on top.
  5. Roll it up tightly like a burrito and slice it in half.

9. Maki Sushi with Eel - Sweet and Savory

Eel, or unagi, is often grilled and brushed with a sweet soy-based sauce, making it an irresistible sushi filling. This simple maki sushi roll is easy to make and offers a delicious combination of savory and sweet flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 4 ounces grilled eel (unagi)
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.
  3. Spread a layer of rice on the nori.
  4. Add grilled eel along the center of the rice.
  5. Roll tightly and slice into bite-sized pieces.

10. Ebi Nigiri - A Simple, Yet Elegant Option

Ebi nigiri features shrimp on top of sushi rice, creating a delicate and tasty sushi option. It’s easy to make and doesn’t require much preparation, making it a great choice for beginners.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sushi rice
  • 1 ¼ cups water
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 6 cooked shrimp (ebi)
  • Soy sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and mix it with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Let it cool.
  2. Place a small mound of rice on your hand, then gently press a shrimp on top.
  3. Serve with soy sauce for dipping.

These 10 sushi recipes are perfect for anyone who’s just starting their sushi-making journey. Whether you prefer vegetarian rolls, seafood-based sushi, or a unique fusion roll, these recipes offer a variety of options for every palate. Start with the basics, then experiment with different fillings and flavors to create your own sushi masterpieces. Enjoy making sushi at home and impress your friends and family with your newfound skills!
