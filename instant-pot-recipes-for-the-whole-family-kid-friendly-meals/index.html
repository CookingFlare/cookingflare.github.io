Instant Pot Recipes for the Whole Family: Kid-Friendly Meals


When it comes to preparing meals for the whole family, it can feel like an impossible challenge to create something that’s both appealing to the kids and satisfying for the adults. Add a busy schedule into the mix, and dinner often becomes a race against the clock. That’s where the Instant Pot comes in—a kitchen superhero that combines efficiency, versatility, and ease of use to help you craft delicious meals for everyone at the table.

Below, we’ve gathered a selection of wholesome, kid-approved recipes that can be made quickly and conveniently using your Instant Pot. From hearty pasta dishes to comforting soups, these recipes are perfect for weeknight dinners, weekend family gatherings, or any time you want to serve up a meal that’s bound to bring smiles to everyone’s faces.

Creamy Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

Few dishes are as universally loved by kids as mac and cheese, and this version is both creamy and deeply satisfying. Plus, it skips the artificial boxed flavors and is ready in a fraction of the time it takes to make traditional stovetop or baked recipes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb elbow macaroni or shell pasta
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (sharp or mild, depending on preference)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 4 oz cream cheese, cubed
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the macaroni and water to your Instant Pot. Seal the lid, set the valve to ‘Sealing,’ and select Manual or Pressure Cook for 4 minutes.
  2. Once the timer goes off, perform a quick release carefully. Open the lid and stir.
  3. Turn the Instant Pot to its ‘Sauté’ setting. Stir in the milk, cheeses, garlic powder, and onion powder. Keep stirring until the cheese has fully melted and the sauce is creamy.
  4. Add salt and pepper to taste, serve immediately, and watch those little faces light up.

Instant Pot Chicken and Rice Casserole

This classic casserole has been a dinner staple for generations, and now you can have it on the table in under 30 minutes. It’s one of those one-pot meals that even picky eaters adore.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ cups long-grain white rice
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tsp dried parsley
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to ‘Sauté’ mode. Add a drizzle of oil, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant, then add the diced chicken. Season with salt, pepper, paprika, and parsley. Cook until the chicken is barely browned.
  2. Stir in the rice and chicken broth, making sure the rice is fully submerged. Cancel the ‘Sauté’ function, seal the lid, and set the pot to Manual or Pressure Cook for 10 minutes.
  3. Once the cooking cycle completes, allow a natural release for 5 minutes, then carefully quick-release any remaining pressure.
  4. Stir in the frozen vegetables, then add the shredded cheese. Let the residual heat melt the cheese and heat the vegetables through. Serve warm, and enjoy the comforting flavors of this family-friendly dish.

Instant Pot Sloppy Joe Sliders

Turn a retro favorite into an easy weeknight meal with this Instant Pot recipe. These sloppy joe sliders are sweet, tangy, and just messy enough for kids to love without creating chaos.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • ½ onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 2 tbsp mustard
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • ½ cup water
  • Slider buns

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to ‘Sauté.’ Brown the ground meat with the onion and garlic, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess grease if necessary.
  2. Add the ketchup, mustard, Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, and water to the pot. Stir to combine, then seal the lid. Set the Instant Pot to Manual or Pressure Cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Perform a quick release once finished. If the mixture is too thin, use the ‘Sauté’ function to simmer it until it thickens to your liking.
  4. Spoon the sloppy joe mixture onto slider buns, and serve warm. Pair with a side of fruit or veggies for a complete meal.

Instant Pot Vegetable Soup

Encouraging kids to eat their vegetables can be a bit easier when those veggies are part of a hearty, flavorful soup. This cozy vegetable soup is simple, nutritious, and adaptable based on your family’s preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced into rounds
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 medium zucchini, chopped
  • 2 cups diced potatoes
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken stock
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Select the ‘Sauté’ option on your Instant Pot. Heat the olive oil, then sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until softened.
  2. Add the zucchini, potatoes, green beans, corn, and diced tomatoes. Stir in the stock, dried herbs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Seal the lid, set the valve to ‘Sealing,’ and select Manual or Pressure Cook for 7 minutes.
  4. Once the cooking is done, perform a quick release, and open the lid. Stir the soup and check for seasoning, adjusting as needed.
  5. Serve warm with a side of crusty bread or crackers—this is comfort food the whole family can get behind.

Pro Tips for Instant Pot Success

Cooking for kids often means balancing convenience with flavor and nutrition. Here are a few tips to make the most out of your Instant Pot:

  1. Adjust seasonings: Kids’ taste buds are more sensitive to bold flavors, so go easy on spices and herbs, but don’t shy away from fresh ingredients.
  2. Prep ahead: Pre-chop vegetables or portion out ingredients in advance to save even more time at dinner.
  3. Double up: Many Instant Pot recipes are freezer-friendly, so consider doubling a recipe and storing portions for busy nights.

An Instant Pot can truly transform the way both you and your kids experience dinnertime. With its efficiency and versatility, you can explore endless possibilities for satisfying meals that bring the family together. From cheesy pasta to hearty soups, these kid-friendly recipes are just the beginning of all the delicious memories you can create around the table.
