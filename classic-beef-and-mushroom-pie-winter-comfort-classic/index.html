Classic Beef and Mushroom Pie: Winter Comfort Classic


Winter is here, and what better way to warm up than with a hearty and comforting classic beef and mushroom pie? This recipe is a crowd-pleaser and is sure to become a staple in your winter cooking repertoire. With tender chunks of beef, earthy mushrooms, and a flaky pastry crust, this dish is the epitome of comfort food. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 500g beef, diced into bite-sized chunks
  • 250g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of plain flour
  • 250ml beef stock
  • 250ml red wine
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (180°C fan-forced).

  2. In a large, oven-safe skillet or casserole dish, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced beef and brown it on all sides. This step helps to seal in the juices and ensures a tender result. Once the beef is browned, remove it from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  4. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they have softened and released their moisture.

  5. Sprinkle the flour over the mushrooms and onions, stirring well to coat everything evenly. This will help thicken the sauce later on.

  6. Slowly pour in the beef stock and red wine, stirring continuously to avoid any lumps. This will create a rich and flavorful sauce.

  7. Stir in the tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme leaves, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.

  8. Return the browned beef to the skillet, making sure it is coated evenly in the sauce. Cover the skillet with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Allow the pie to bake for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  9. While the pie is cooking, prepare the puff pastry. Roll out the sheet of pastry on a lightly floured surface until it is large enough to cover the skillet.

  10. Once the beef is cooked, remove the skillet from the oven and carefully place the pastry over the top, tucking in the edges to seal in the filling. Brush the pastry with the beaten egg to give it a glossy finish.

  11. Return the skillet to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy.

  12. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the filling to set slightly and make it easier to slice.

  13. Serve the classic beef and mushroom pie hot, with a side of mashed potatoes and steamed greens. The combination of tender beef, earthy mushrooms, and buttery pastry is a match made in comfort food heaven.

Enjoy this winter comfort classic with loved ones, and let the flavors warm your soul. This classic beef and mushroom pie is a timeless dish that will keep you cozy throughout the colder months. So, grab your apron, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in the ultimate comfort food experience!
