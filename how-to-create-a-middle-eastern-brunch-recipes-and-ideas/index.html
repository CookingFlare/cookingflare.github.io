How to Create a Middle Eastern Brunch: Recipes and Ideas


Brunch is the perfect fusion of breakfast and lunch, offering a laid-back, versatile option for any gathering. If you’re looking to elevate your brunch game, Middle Eastern cuisine offers a stunning array of bold flavors, vibrant colors, and wholesome ingredients that will impress any crowd. From creamy dips to fragrant pastries, crafting a Middle Eastern brunch is an exciting culinary adventure. Let’s break down everything you need to create a spread that embodies the warmth and tradition of Middle Eastern hospitality.

The Foundation: Mezze Platters

A Middle Eastern brunch starts with an assortment of mezze, small plates perfect for sharing. These dishes set the stage for the rest of the meal.

Hummus

A velvety, nutty hummus is essential. To make it at home, blend the following ingredients in a food processor until smooth:

  • 1 can (15 oz) of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 2–3 tablespoons olive oil
  • A pinch of cumin and salt Drizzle the hummus with olive oil, sprinkle with paprika, and garnish with fresh parsley before serving. Serve it alongside fresh pita bread or crudités.

Mutabbal (Baba Ghanoush)

To complement the hummus, prepare mutabbal. This smoky, luscious dip made from roasted eggplants adds depth to any spread. Here’s a quick recipe:

  • Roast 2 large eggplants until their skins are charred and flesh is tender.
  • Scoop out the flesh and blend it with 3 tablespoons tahini, 1 minced garlic clove, 2 tablespoons lemon juice, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  • Season with salt and optionally add a pinch of chili flakes for heat.

Labneh

Creamy, tangy labneh—strained yogurt—is another pillar of Middle Eastern brunch. To create an aromatic version, swirl olive oil on top and sprinkle za’atar, a blend of dried thyme, sumac, and sesame seeds. Serve with pita or fresh vegetables.

Adding Variety: Salads and Pickles

Bright, crunchy salads and tangy pickles balance the richness of the dips and bring freshness to your brunch table.

Fatoush Salad

Fatoush is an iconic Middle Eastern salad with a medley of garden vegetables, herbs, and crispy pita chips. For an authentic version, toss these ingredients together:

  • Chopped cucumbers, tomatoes, radishes, and greens (such as arugula or romaine)
  • Fresh parsley and mint
  • Lemon juice and olive oil for the dressing
  • Toasted or fried pieces of pita bread
  • A sprinkle of sumac for a citrusy kick

Pickled Vegetables

Pickled turnips, cucumbers, and olives are classic accompaniments. These tangy additions are readily available at stores or can be made at home with simple brine recipes.

Hearty Mains: Bringing Depth to Your Brunch

While mezze and salads are essential, no brunch is complete without hearty dishes. Middle Eastern cuisine offers plenty of flavorful mains that are perfect for late-morning meals.

Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a show-stopping one-pan dish of poached eggs in a spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce. To prepare shakshuka:

  1. Sauté onions, garlic, and diced red bell peppers in a deep skillet with olive oil until softened.
  2. Add canned tomatoes, a teaspoon of ground cumin, paprika, and a pinch of chili flakes. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Make wells in the sauce and crack in eggs. Cover the skillet and cook until the eggs are just set.
  4. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve with fresh bread.

Manakish

Manakish, a soft flatbread topped with za’atar, cheese, or minced meat, makes a delightful addition to brunch. Here’s a simple za’atar topping recipe:

  • Mix 2 tablespoons za’atar with 3 tablespoons olive oil. Spread the mixture over flatbread dough, bake in a preheated oven at 475°F for 8–10 minutes, and serve warm.

Incorporating Proteins: Meat and Vegetarian Options

Protein-rich dishes amplify the meal and keep everyone satisfied.

Kofta Kebabs

Kofta kebabs are minced meat patties infused with spices and herbs. To make them:

  1. Mix 1 lb ground lamb or beef with grated onion, minced parsley, ground cumin, coriander, and cinnamon. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Form the mixture into oval patties or thread onto skewers.
  3. Grill or cook on a stovetop grill pan until done. Serve alongside tzatziki or tahini sauce.

Falafel

For a vegetarian option, falafel is a must-have. These crispy chickpea fritters are packed with flavor. Homemade falafel can be made by blending soaked chickpeas (not canned) with onions, garlic, parsley, cilantro, and spices before frying until golden. Serve with tahini sauce.

Perfect Side Breads and Accompaniments

Bread is central to Middle Eastern cuisine. Include a variety to soak up sauces and dips.

  • Pita Bread: Fluffy and versatile, pita is a classic choice. It’s perfect for scooping dips or wrapping around kofta and falafel.
  • Markook: A thin, crepe-like bread ideal for spreading labneh or rolling shakshuka ingredients into a handheld bite.
  • Bagels with a Twist: Try Simit—a Middle Eastern sesame-encrusted bread circle that’s like a cross between a bagel and pretzel.

Sweet Touch: Desserts and Beverages

End your Middle Eastern brunch experience with desserts and traditional beverages that highlight the region’s love for intricate, aromatic flavors.

Baklava

Baklava, layers of phyllo pastry filled with chopped pistachios and drenched in a honey syrup, is timeless. If you’re short on time, consider sourcing some from a local bakery.

Maamoul

These delicate, date-filled cookies are perfect for nibbling with coffee or tea. They’re flavored with orange blossom or rose water for a fragrant touch.

Turkish Coffee or Mint Tea

The drinks are as important as the food in Middle Eastern hospitality. Turkish coffee, brewed thick and rich with cardamom, or Moroccan mint tea, sweetened and poured dramatically, are must-tries.

Presentation and Ambiance

Finally, brunch is about more than just food—ambiance matters. Decorate your table with vibrant ceramics, woven baskets, and fresh flowers. Use small bowls and platters to arrange the items beautifully. The inviting aromas of za’atar, roasted eggplant, and fresh herbs will do the rest to evoke the spirit of Middle Eastern culture.

A Middle Eastern brunch is more than just a meal: it’s an expression of tradition, hospitality, and bold flavors that bring people together. With these recipes and ideas, you’re set to host an unforgettable feast that delights both the eyes and the palate. Enjoy the process and savor every bite!
