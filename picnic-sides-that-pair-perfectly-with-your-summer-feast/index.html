Picnic Sides That Pair Perfectly with Your Summer Feast


When the sun is shining, the air is warm, and the days stretch long, nothing beats a picnic with family and friends. Whether you’re gathering at a park, by the beach, or in your own backyard, the right sides can elevate your summer feast and make every bite feel like a celebration. From fresh salads to creamy dips, savory bites to zesty slaws, this collection of picnic side dishes will perfectly complement your main courses and keep everyone coming back for more.

1. Classic Potato Salad

No summer picnic is complete without the comforting, creamy goodness of potato salad. It’s a timeless side dish that pairs wonderfully with grilled meats, sandwiches, and more. The key to an unforgettable potato salad is using the right potatoes—waxier varieties like Yukon Gold or red potatoes hold their shape better and have a smooth, buttery texture once boiled.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 boiled eggs, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh dill (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until fork-tender, about 12-15 minutes. Drain and let them cool before dicing them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and sugar to create a creamy dressing.
  3. Gently fold the potatoes, chopped eggs, red onion, and celery into the dressing. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. For an extra touch of flavor, sprinkle some fresh dill on top, or let the salad chill in the fridge for a few hours to let the flavors meld together.

2. Zesty Coleslaw

Coleslaw is another picnic staple that offers a crunchy, refreshing contrast to rich and smoky barbecue dishes. With its tangy dressing and crisp veggies, coleslaw provides the perfect balance of flavors. While you can go traditional with cabbage, feel free to mix in other colorful vegetables to add texture and variety.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small green cabbage, shredded
  • 1 small purple cabbage, shredded
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and shredded
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage and carrots.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, honey, and celery seeds until smooth.
  3. Pour the dressing over the cabbage mixture and toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Let the slaw sit for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to develop. You can also make this the day before to save time on the day of your picnic.

3. Fresh Tomato and Cucumber Salad

For a lighter, more refreshing option, a tomato and cucumber salad is a perfect choice. It’s vibrant, crunchy, and packed with flavor, making it an excellent side for grilled chicken, seafood, or even burgers. The addition of fresh herbs and a zesty vinaigrette takes this salad from simple to extraordinary.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion in a large bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the vegetables and toss gently to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh basil and parsley, then serve immediately or let it chill for 30 minutes before serving.

4. Creamy Macaroni Salad

Macaroni salad is another picnic favorite that’s easy to make and even easier to enjoy. With its creamy dressing and tender pasta, it’s the ultimate side dish to bring to a cookout. Customize it with your favorite ingredients like peas, carrots, or even bacon to add a fun twist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni, cooked and drained
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package directions, drain, and let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooled pasta with mayonnaise, mustard, and apple cider vinegar.
  3. Stir in the chopped celery, bell pepper, and green onions.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving to let the flavors blend.

5. Grilled Vegetable Skewers

For a healthy and delicious side, grilled vegetable skewers are a fantastic choice. They are versatile, easy to prepare, and full of smoky flavor. Plus, they make a colorful addition to your picnic spread. Feel free to experiment with different vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, mushrooms, and cherry tomatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between the zucchini, bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, and red onion.
  3. Drizzle the skewers with olive oil and sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Grill the skewers for 6-8 minutes, turning occasionally until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Serve the skewers hot or at room temperature.

6. Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Corn on the cob is a summer classic that’s simple to prepare yet packed with flavor. Grilling the corn enhances its natural sweetness, while a dollop of herb-infused butter adds richness and complexity. This side pairs well with almost anything, from burgers to grilled chicken to seafood.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the corn for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until it’s lightly charred and cooked through.
  3. While the corn is grilling, mix the softened butter, parsley, thyme, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  4. Once the corn is done, remove it from the grill and slather it with the herb butter. Serve immediately.

7. Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

If you’re looking for a salad that’s packed with protein and flavor, this Mediterranean chickpea salad is the way to go. With its combination of chickpeas, cucumbers, tomatoes, red onion, and Kalamata olives, it’s a vibrant and satisfying side that pairs wonderfully with grilled meats or sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and chopped
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, olives, and feta.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate for 30 minutes to let the flavors meld.

8. Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

To finish your picnic meal on a sweet note, a fresh fruit salad is the perfect dessert option. This fruit salad is full of colorful, juicy fruits like watermelon, strawberries, and pineapple, and the honey-lime dressing adds a zesty sweetness that’s impossible to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup pineapple, cubed
  • 1/2 cup blueberries
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh mint leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all of the fruit.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together honey and lime juice until smooth.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the fruit and gently toss to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving.

Conclusion

Picnics are all about enjoying good food in the great outdoors, and with these delicious sides, you can take your summer feast to the next level. Whether you’re serving up classic potato salad, zesty slaw, or refreshing fruit salad, these picnic sides are sure to satisfy every guest. They’re easy to make, packed with flavor, and perfect for pairing with your favorite grilled meats and cold beverages. So, the next time you plan a picnic, be sure to include these sides—they’ll become a favorite part of your summer tradition!
