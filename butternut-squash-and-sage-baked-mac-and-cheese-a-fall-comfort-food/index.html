Butternut Squash and Sage Baked Mac and Cheese: A Fall Comfort Food


Fall is upon us, and what better way to embrace the cozy season than with a comforting bowl of Butternut Squash and Sage Baked Mac and Cheese? This creamy and flavorful twist on the classic dish is sure to warm your soul and satisfy your cravings. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash
  • 1 pound macaroni noodles
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups grated sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh sage leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Breadcrumbs for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. Start by preparing the butternut squash. Peel it, remove the seeds, and cut it into small cubes. Place the cubes on a baking sheet and roast them in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes or until they are tender. Once roasted, remove from the oven and set aside.

  3. While the butternut squash is roasting, cook the macaroni noodles according to the package instructions. Make sure to cook them al dente, as they will continue to cook in the oven later on. Drain the noodles and set them aside.

  4. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk continuously for about 2 minutes to create a roux.

  5. Gradually pour in the milk, whisking constantly to prevent any lumps from forming. Continue to cook the mixture until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and add the grated cheddar cheese, Parmesan cheese, chopped sage leaves, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Stir until the cheeses have melted and the sauce is smooth and creamy.

  7. Add the roasted butternut squash to the sauce and mix well, gently mashing some of the squash cubes with the back of a spoon.

  8. Add the cooked macaroni noodles to the sauce and toss until they are well coated with the creamy butternut squash mixture.

  9. Transfer the mac and cheese mixture to the greased baking dish and spread it out evenly. Sprinkle breadcrumbs on top to create a crispy crust.

  10. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the edges are bubbling.

  11. Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to portion.

  12. Serve the Butternut Squash and Sage Baked Mac and Cheese hot, and enjoy the creamy, cheesy goodness with a hint of nutty sweetness from the butternut squash.

This Butternut Squash and Sage Baked Mac and Cheese is the perfect dish to enjoy during the fall season. The combination of the creamy butternut squash sauce, the sharpness of the cheddar cheese, and the aromatic sage creates a flavor profile that is both comforting and delicious. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking for a cozy meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead and indulge in this ultimate fall comfort food!
