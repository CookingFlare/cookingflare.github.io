Grilled Sides: Delicious Accompaniments to Your BBQ


When the sun shines and the weather beckons, there’s nothing quite like gathering around a grill for a barbecue. While the main event often centers on perfectly grilled meats, the sides are what truly elevate your outdoor feast. Grilled sides not only enhance the flavors of grilled dishes but also add vibrant colors and textures to your plate. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of delicious grilled side dishes that are sure to impress your guests and complement your BBQ spread.

The Importance of Grilled Sides

Grilled sides are an essential part of any barbecue. They provide balance to the meal, offering freshness and crunch alongside the smoky flavors of grilled meats. A well-rounded plate that includes grilled vegetables, salads, and starches not only pleases the palate but also creates a visually appealing feast. Let’s dive into some mouthwatering grilled side recipes that will make your BBQ unforgettable.

1. Grilled Garlic Butter Corn on the Cob

Corn on the cob is a summertime classic that shines on the grill. The natural sweetness of corn caramelizes beautifully when grilled, and when slathered in garlic butter, it becomes irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Make the Garlic Butter: In a small bowl, mix the melted butter, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the Corn: Place the corn on the grill, turning occasionally, until charred and tender (about 10-15 minutes).
  4. Baste and Serve: Remove the corn from the grill, brush with the garlic butter, and sprinkle with fresh herbs before serving.

2. Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Grilled vegetable skewers are colorful, healthy, and easy to customize. You can use any combination of vegetables you have on hand, making this dish versatile for any BBQ gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup mushrooms, whole
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Soak Skewers: If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning.
  2. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, mix olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Skewer the Veggies: Thread the vegetables onto the skewers, alternating colors for visual appeal.
  4. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat and grill the skewers for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally until charred and tender.

3. Grilled Asparagus with Lemon Zest

Asparagus is a fantastic side that’s quick to prepare and packed with nutrients. Grilling asparagus enhances its natural flavors and adds a delightful smokiness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Asparagus: Toss the asparagus with olive oil, lemon zest, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and grill the asparagus for 5-7 minutes, turning until tender and slightly charred.
  3. Serve: Remove from the grill and serve with lemon wedges for an extra burst of flavor.

4. Grilled Potato Wedges

Crispy grilled potato wedges are a fantastic alternative to traditional French fries. They are seasoned to perfection and can be dipped in your favorite sauces.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium-sized russet potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes: In a large bowl, toss the potato wedges with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the wedges directly on the grill grates and cook for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally until golden brown and crispy.
  3. Garnish and Serve: Remove from the grill, garnish with chopped chives, and serve with your favorite dipping sauce.

5. Grilled Caprese Salad

A twist on a classic salad, grilling the components of a Caprese salad adds a smoky depth that enhances the flavors. This dish is perfect for those hot summer days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large tomatoes, sliced
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the Tomatoes: Lightly brush tomato slices with olive oil and grill for 2-3 minutes on each side.
  3. Assemble the Salad: On a platter, layer the grilled tomatoes, mozzarella slices, and fresh basil. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.

6. Grilled Peaches with Honey

For a sweet ending to your BBQ, grilled peaches are a delightful treat. The caramelization from the grill intensifies their sweetness, making them a perfect dessert or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Honey (for drizzling)
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Peaches: Brush the cut sides of the peaches with olive oil.
  2. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat and grill the peaches cut side down for about 4-5 minutes until grill marks appear.
  3. Serve: Drizzle with honey and serve warm, optionally with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

7. Grilled Stuffed Bell Peppers

Stuffed bell peppers are a fun and flavorful side dish that can be filled with a variety of ingredients. Grilling them adds a smoky flavor that complements the stuffing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa or rice
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Monterey Jack)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Peppers: Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds.
  2. Make the Filling: In a bowl, combine quinoa or rice, black beans, corn, cumin, chili powder, and half of the cheese.
  3. Stuff the Peppers: Fill each bell pepper with the mixture and top with the remaining cheese.
  4. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat and place the stuffed peppers on the grill. Cover and cook for about 20-25 minutes until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted.

8. Grilled Sweet Potatoes with Lime

Sweet potatoes are nutritious and delicious, and grilling them amplifies their natural sweetness. Adding lime gives a refreshing twist that perfectly balances the flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, sliced into rounds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Sweet Potatoes: In a bowl, toss the sweet potato rounds with olive oil, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium heat. Grill the sweet potatoes for about 5-7 minutes on each side until tender and charred.
  3. Garnish and Serve: Remove from the grill, garnish with fresh cilantro, and serve warm.

Conclusion

Grilled sides are the unsung heroes of any barbecue gathering. They bring not only flavor and texture but also a variety of nutrients and colors to your plate. From grilled corn on the cob to stuffed bell peppers, these delicious accompaniments will undoubtedly impress your guests and elevate your BBQ experience. Next time you fire up the grill, don’t forget to include these mouthwatering sides in your menu. Happy grilling!
