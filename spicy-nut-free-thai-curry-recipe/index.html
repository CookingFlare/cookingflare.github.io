Spicy Nut-Free Thai Curry Recipe


Are you a fan of Thai cuisine but have a nut allergy? Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered with this delicious and spicy nut-free Thai curry recipe! Packed with flavor and easy to make, this dish will satisfy your cravings without compromising your dietary restrictions. So let’s jump right into it!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  2. Add the sliced red and green bell peppers, carrot, and zucchini to the pan. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Stir in the Thai red curry paste and cook for another minute, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the vegetables to absorb the flavors.

  5. Add the soy sauce, lime juice, and brown sugar to the pan. Stir well to combine and let the curry simmer for an additional 5 minutes.

  6. Taste the curry and season with salt if needed. Adjust the spice level by adding more curry paste if desired.

  7. Once the curry is ready, remove it from the heat and serve hot. Garnish with fresh cilantro for an extra burst of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

This nut-free Thai curry is incredibly versatile and can be served with various accompaniments. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Jasmine rice: Serve the curry over a bed of fluffy jasmine rice for a satisfying meal.
  • Noodles: Try pairing the curry with rice noodles or vermicelli for a different texture.
  • Naan bread: Enjoy the curry with some warm and fluffy naan bread to soak up all the delicious sauce.
  • Fresh vegetables: Serve the curry alongside a side of fresh vegetables, such as cucumber slices or bean sprouts, for a refreshing contrast.

Final Thoughts

This nut-free Thai curry recipe is a great option for those with nut allergies who still want to enjoy the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine. The combination of aromatic spices, creamy coconut milk, and a medley of vegetables creates a dish that is both satisfying and nourishing. So go ahead and give it a try – your taste buds will thank you!

Remember to adjust the spice level according to your preference, and feel free to experiment with different vegetables to make this dish your own. Enjoy the flavors of Thailand in the comfort of your own kitchen with this delicious and nut-free Thai curry recipe.

Note: Please consult with your doctor or allergist if you have severe nut allergies to ensure these ingredients are safe for you to consume.
