Mouthwatering Mexican Street Corn: A Fiesta on Your Plate


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to take a deep dive into the world of mouthwatering Mexican street corn. This delicious and vibrant dish is a true fiesta on your plate, bursting with flavors that will transport you straight to the bustling streets of Mexico.

The Origins of Mexican Street Corn

Mexican street corn, also known as Elote, is a beloved street food in Mexico. It’s a simple yet incredibly satisfying snack that combines the natural sweetness of corn with tangy and savory flavors. Traditionally, it is grilled over an open flame to enhance its natural smokiness, then slathered with a mixture of mayonnaise, crema, and spices, and finally topped with crumbled cheese and a squeeze of lime.

The Perfect Grilled Corn

To achieve that smoky flavor that makes Mexican street corn so irresistible, it’s best to grill the corn on a charcoal or gas grill. Start by removing the husks and silk from the corn, leaving the stem intact for easy handling. Brush the corn with a generous amount of olive oil and sprinkle it with salt and pepper. Grill the corn over medium heat, turning occasionally, until it’s lightly charred and cooked through. This should take about 10-15 minutes.

The Flavors of Mexico

Now, let’s move on to the star of the show—the flavor-packed toppings that make Mexican street corn truly unforgettable. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Mayonnaise: Slather the grilled corn with a thin layer of mayonnaise. This will add a creamy and tangy element to the dish.

  • Crema: Crema is a Mexican-style sour cream that’s slightly thinner and tangier than regular sour cream. Drizzle it over the mayo-coated corn for an extra burst of flavor.

  • Spices: To give your Mexican street corn a spicy kick, sprinkle it with chili powder, smoked paprika, and a pinch of cayenne pepper. Adjust the spice level to your liking.

  • Cheese: Traditional Mexican street corn is topped with crumbled cotija cheese, a salty and crumbly cheese that perfectly complements the other flavors. If you can’t find cotija cheese, feta cheese or grated Parmesan will work just as well.

  • Lime: Finish off your Mexican street corn with a squeeze of fresh lime juice. The acidity of the lime cuts through the richness of the toppings and adds a refreshing twist.

Serving and Enjoying Mexican Street Corn

Once you’ve assembled all the toppings, it’s time to serve and enjoy your mouthwatering Mexican street corn. You can either serve it on the cob or cut the kernels off and serve it in a bowl. If you prefer a mess-free option, you can also skewer the corn before grilling and serve it as corn on the stick.

Mexican street corn is perfect for backyard barbecues, summer parties, or even as a side dish for your next Mexican-inspired feast. It’s a crowd-pleaser that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of Mexico, give this vibrant and flavorful dish a try. With its irresistible combination of smoky grilled corn, creamy toppings, and zesty lime juice, Mexican street corn is sure to be a hit at your next gathering. ¡Buen provecho!

Note: Mexican street corn is a versatile dish, and you can customize it to suit your taste. Feel free to experiment with additional toppings like chopped cilantro, minced garlic, or hot sauce. Let your creativity run wild and make this fiesta on your plate truly your own!
