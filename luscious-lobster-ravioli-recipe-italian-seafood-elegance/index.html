Luscious Lobster Ravioli Recipe - Italian Seafood Elegance


There’s something undeniably luxurious about lobster, and when it’s enveloped in delicate pasta pockets and bathed in a luscious sauce, the result is simply exquisite. Today, we’re diving into the world of Italian seafood elegance with a recipe for homemade lobster ravioli that will transport your taste buds to the shores of the Mediterranean.

The Perfect Pasta Dough

To begin this culinary journey, we must first craft the perfect pasta dough. In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt. Create a well in the center and crack in 3 large eggs. Using a fork, gradually incorporate the flour into the eggs until a shaggy dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Creating the Lobster Filling

While the pasta dough rests, let’s prepare the star of the show – the lobster filling. Begin by poaching two fresh lobster tails in a pot of simmering water for 8-10 minutes until the meat is opaque and cooked through. Remove the lobster meat from the shells and finely chop it. In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped lobster meat with 1/2 cup of ricotta cheese, a tablespoon of freshly chopped parsley, a squeeze of lemon juice, salt, and pepper to taste. Gently fold the mixture together until well combined.

Rolling and Filling the Ravioli

After the dough has rested, divide it into four equal portions. Working with one portion at a time, roll the dough through a pasta machine, gradually decreasing the thickness setting until you achieve a thin, even sheet. Spoon small mounds of the lobster filling onto the pasta sheet, leaving enough space between each mound. Brush the edges of the pasta with water, then carefully lay a second sheet of pasta over the filling. Press around each mound of filling to seal the ravioli, ensuring there are no air pockets. Use a pastry wheel to cut the ravioli into individual pieces.

Cooking and Serving

Once the ravioli are assembled, bring a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil. Carefully drop the ravioli into the water and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until they float to the surface. While the ravioli cook, melt a generous amount of butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add a clove of minced garlic and a handful of cherry tomatoes, allowing them to sauté until fragrant. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the cooked ravioli directly from the pot to the skillet. Toss the ravioli gently in the garlic-infused butter, allowing them to soak up the flavors.

A Finishing Touch

To elevate this dish to true Italian seafood elegance, sprinkle the lobster ravioli with a handful of freshly chopped basil and a dusting of grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese. Serve the ravioli immediately, allowing the decadent flavors to speak for themselves. Accompany this dish with a crisp white wine, such as Pinot Grigio or Chardonnay, to complement the richness of the lobster and buttery sauce.

In Conclusion

This luscious lobster ravioli recipe embodies the essence of Italian seafood elegance. With each tender bite, you’ll experience the sweet, delicate flavor of lobster harmonizing with the creamy ricotta, all encased in silky pasta. Whether you’re hosting a special dinner or simply indulging in a culinary adventure, this dish is sure to captivate and delight. Buon appetito!
