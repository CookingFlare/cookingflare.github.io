Hearty Nut-Free Beef Stew Recipe


When the weather turns chilly, there’s nothing quite like a comforting bowl of beef stew to warm you up from the inside out. This hearty nut-free beef stew recipe is a perfect balance of savory flavors, tender beef, and hearty vegetables. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a cozy meal for yourself, this recipe is sure to satisfy.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 4 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and pepper. Add the beef stew meat and toss to coat evenly.

  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.

  3. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is soft and translucent.

  4. Return the browned beef to the pot. Add the beef broth, red wine, tomato paste, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and cook for 1 1/2 hours, stirring occasionally.

  5. After 1 1/2 hours, add the chopped carrots, potatoes, and celery to the pot. Cover and continue to cook for an additional 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the beef is fork-tender.

  6. Stir in the frozen peas and cook for an additional 5 minutes, until the peas are heated through.

  7. Remove the bay leaves from the stew and discard. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  8. Ladle the hearty nut-free beef stew into bowls, garnish with chopped fresh parsley, and serve hot.

Tips for Success

  • For the best flavor, use a well-marbled cut of beef stew meat such as chuck or round.
  • If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mix an additional tablespoon of flour with water and stir it into the stew during the last 15 minutes of cooking.
  • This stew can be made ahead of time and reheated, allowing the flavors to meld even further.

Serving Suggestions

This hearty nut-free beef stew pairs beautifully with a crusty loaf of bread, dinner rolls, or a simple green salad. It’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed on its own or as part of a larger meal.

Conclusion

With its rich, savory flavors and wholesome ingredients, this hearty nut-free beef stew is a true classic that never goes out of style. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner, a potluck, or a cozy night in, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy the comforting aroma and delicious taste of homemade beef stew.
