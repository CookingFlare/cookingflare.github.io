Strawberry Rhubarb Pie: A Perfect Balance of Sweet and Tangy


As the summer months approach, the sweet aroma of fresh strawberries and tart rhubarb fills the air, beckoning us to create something truly special in the kitchen. And what better way to do so than with a classic strawberry rhubarb pie? This beloved dessert is a masterclass in balancing sweet and tangy flavors, with a delicate dance between the two ingredients that will leave your taste buds singing.

The Perfect Union

At its core, a strawberry rhubarb pie is all about the harmonious marriage of two distinct flavors. Sweet, juicy strawberries provide the base note, while tart, slightly bitter rhubarb adds a welcome contrast. Together, they create a flavor profile that is at once familiar and exciting, comforting and innovative.

The Recipe

To create this masterpiece, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup fresh rhubarb, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup ice-cold water
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and cut rhubarb. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, flour, and salt. Pour the sugar mixture over the strawberry-rhubarb mixture, and toss until the fruit is evenly coated.

Step 2: Make the Crust

In a separate bowl, combine the cold butter and ice-cold water. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the water until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the flour, salt, and sugar, stirring until the dough comes together in a ball.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake the Pie

Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish, trimming the edges to fit. Fill the pie crust with the strawberry-rhubarb filling, mounding it slightly in the center.

Roll out the remaining dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a pastry cutter or a knife to cut the dough into strips for a lattice-top crust. Place the strips on top of the filling, weaving them into a lattice pattern. Trim the edges of the strips and press the edges of the lattice to seal.

Brush the egg wash over the lattice top and sprinkle with sugar. Bake the pie in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

The Finishing Touches

Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes. In a small bowl, whisk together the milk and white vinegar. Brush the mixture over the lattice top to give the pie a glossy finish.

Serving Suggestions

Serve the pie warm or at room temperature, garnished with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream if desired. The perfect balance of sweet and tangy flavors makes this pie a crowd-pleaser for any gathering, whether it’s a summer barbecue, a family dinner, or a casual brunch.

Conclusion

A strawberry rhubarb pie is more than just a dessert – it’s an experience. With its perfect balance of sweet and tangy flavors, it’s a taste sensation that will leave you wanting more. So why not give it a try? With this recipe, you’ll be well on your way to creating a masterpiece that will be the star of any gathering.
