Harvest Pumpkin Chili Recipe


Autumn is in the air, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a fragrant, flavorful, and wholesome harvest pumpkin chili? This unique take on a classic comfort food radiates the warmth and coziness of fall, while delivering a nutritious punch. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let’s embark on a culinary journey together!

üçΩ ingredients

  • 1 large pumpkin (about 3 pounds), peeled, seeded, and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 pound ground beef or turkey
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2-3 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 (15.5-ounce) can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (15.5-ounce) can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • Optional toppings: cilantro, avocado, sour cream, green onion, hot sauce

üå∞ instructions

  1. Prepare the pumpkin: Preheat your oven to 400¬∞F (200¬∞C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Arrange the pumpkin cubes on the sheet, drizzle lightly with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Roast in the oven for about 25-30 minutes or until tender and golden, tossing occasionally. Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. Cook the ground meat: In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the ground beef or turkey over medium-high heat until browned and no longer pink. Drain off the excess fat, if necessary.
  3. Saut√© the veggies: Add the diced onion, garlic, bell peppers, and carrots to the pot. Saut√© until the onion and garlic are fragrant and the vegetables are starting to soften, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the seasonings: Add the roasted pumpkin, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, chicken or vegetable broth, and water. Season with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Mix well ensuring all components are integrated.
  5. Simmer the chili: Bring the chili to a simmer over medium-low heat. Partially cover the pot, and let the flavors meld, stirring occasionally, for about 45-60 minutes or until the chili thickens and reaches the desired consistency.
  6. Add the beans: Stir in the drained and rinsed kidney beans and black beans, cooking for an additional 10-15 minutes to fully heat through.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning: Taste your finished chili and adjust seasonings as needed. If the consistency is too thick, thin the chili with more broth or water.
  8. Serve and garnish: Serve your harvest pumpkin chili hot with your choice of toppings, such as cilantro, avocado, sour cream, green onion, or hot sauce. Enjoy your homemade harvest pumpkin chili as a comforting and nutritious dinner or packed lunch throughout the fall season!

Experience the fusion of comforting fall flavors and the delightful satisfaction of a hearty meal with this harvest pumpkin chili. Your taste buds will dance to the rhythm of the season, and your family and friends will revel in the warming sensations of this special recipe.
