Authentic Mexican Sopes: A Delicious Handheld Delight


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Authentic Mexican Sopes. These delicious handheld delights are a traditional Mexican dish that combines a crispy base with flavorful toppings, creating a burst of flavors in every bite. Whether you’re looking to impress your friends at a gathering or simply craving a tasty snack, these sopes are the perfect choice.

Ingredients

For the Sopes:

  • 2 cups masa harina (corn flour)
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup refried beans
  • 1 cup shredded chicken or beef
  • 1 cup lettuce, shredded
  • 1 cup tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cup queso fresco, crumbled
  • ½ cup sour cream
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the masa harina, warm water, and salt. Mix well until a soft dough forms. If the dough feels too dry, add a little more water, a tablespoon at a time. Conversely, if it feels too wet, add more masa harina.

  2. Divide the dough into small golf ball-sized portions. Using your hands, shape each portion into a small disc, about ¼ inch thick and 3 inches in diameter.

  3. Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Place the sopes on the hot surface and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they develop a light golden brown color and become slightly crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. To assemble the sopes, start by spreading a generous layer of refried beans on each sope, leaving a small border around the edges.

  5. Next, add your choice of shredded chicken or beef on top of the beans, followed by shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, crumbled queso fresco, sour cream, and a sprinkle of fresh cilantro.

  6. Repeat the process with the remaining sopes until all are assembled.

  7. Serve the sopes immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, top your sopes with some homemade salsa or a drizzle of hot sauce.

  • Feel free to get creative with the toppings. Add some sliced jalapenos for a spicy twist or experiment with different types of cheese.

  • If you’re vegetarian, you can substitute the shredded chicken or beef with grilled vegetables or sautéed mushrooms for a delicious meat-free option.

  • Sopes are best enjoyed fresh, but you can store any leftover sopes in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two days. To reheat, simply place them in a preheated oven at 350°F for about 5-7 minutes, or until warmed through.

  • Serve your sopes with a side of Mexican rice and a refreshing agua fresca for a complete and satisfying meal.

Now that you have the recipe for Authentic Mexican Sopes, it’s time to gather your ingredients, get cooking, and experience the mouthwatering flavors of this traditional Mexican dish. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, these sopes are sure to impress and satisfy your taste buds. So grab your apron, put on some festive music, and enjoy the process of creating this handheld delight that’s bursting with authentic Mexican flavors. ¡Buen provecho!
