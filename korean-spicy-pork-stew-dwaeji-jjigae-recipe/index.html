Korean Spicy Pork Stew (Dwaeji Jjigae) Recipe


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, and one of the most beloved dishes that embodies this richness is Dwaeji Jjigae, or Korean Spicy Pork Stew. This hearty and flavorful stew features tender pork, aromatic vegetables, and a balance of spicy, savory, and slightly tangy flavors. Perfect for any time of year, especially during colder months, Dwaeji Jjigae is a comforting, satisfying meal that brings warmth and depth to the table.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to prepare this delicious dish at home, from selecting the best ingredients to cooking it to perfection. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned chef looking to expand your recipe collection, this guide will help you master the art of making Dwaeji Jjigae.

What is Dwaeji Jjigae?

Dwaeji Jjigae is a traditional Korean stew made with pork, typically pork shoulder or belly, simmered in a rich, spicy broth made with gochujang (Korean chili paste), gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), garlic, and other key seasonings. The dish is cooked with a variety of vegetables, including onions, potatoes, and napa cabbage, which enhance the stew’s depth and texture. It’s often served with a bowl of steamed rice, and the combination of spicy, savory, and umami flavors makes it an irresistible meal.

Why You Should Try This Recipe

Dwaeji Jjigae is a perfect choice for a family meal or when hosting friends. It’s hearty, packed with flavor, and comforting. Here’s why you should give it a try:

  • Bold, Spicy Flavors: The gochujang and gochugaru create a deliciously spicy and slightly sweet broth that packs a punch.
  • Tender Pork: When slow-cooked, the pork becomes tender and absorbs the rich flavors of the broth, making every bite succulent.
  • Customizable: You can adjust the level of spiciness to your liking and add or omit ingredients based on what you have available.
  • Hearty and Filling: This stew is a complete meal with vegetables, meat, and a rich broth, perfect for colder days or when you’re craving something comforting.

Now, let’s get into the recipe!

Ingredients for Dwaeji Jjigae

For the Stew Base:

  • 1 lb pork shoulder or pork belly (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 medium onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 medium potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 2 cups napa cabbage (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) – adjust based on your spice preference
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons doenjang (Korean soybean paste)
  • 4 cups water or low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, for balancing the flavors)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon green onions (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

For the Optional Additions:

  • 1 small zucchini (sliced)
  • 1/2 block tofu (cubed)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (for extra umami flavor)

How to Make Dwaeji Jjigae

Step 1: Prepare the Pork and Vegetables

Start by preparing your pork. If you’re using pork shoulder, it’s best to cut it into small, bite-sized pieces to ensure it cooks evenly and absorbs the flavors of the stew. If you’re using pork belly, the slightly fatty cuts will melt into the stew, making it richer.

Peel and cube your potatoes into bite-sized chunks. Slice your onion thinly, and chop your napa cabbage into bite-sized pieces as well. For added texture and flavor, you can also include sliced zucchini and tofu, which will soak up the flavorful broth.

Step 2: Sauté the Pork and Aromatics

In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the pork and sauté it until it’s lightly browned on all sides. This step helps seal in the juices and enhance the flavor of the pork.

Add the sliced onion, garlic, and ginger to the pot. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the onions become translucent and aromatic. The fragrance of ginger and garlic will fill your kitchen, adding a wonderful depth to the stew.

Step 3: Add the Seasonings

Next, it’s time to add the star ingredients that give Dwaeji Jjigae its signature flavor profile. Stir in the gochujang, gochugaru, and doenjang. These ingredients are the heart and soul of the stew, bringing the heat, umami, and depth of flavor.

After the pastes are well incorporated, pour in the soy sauce and water (or chicken broth for a richer flavor). Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. At this point, you can adjust the level of spiciness by adding more gochugaru if you like it hotter.

Step 4: Simmer and Cook

Add the cubed potatoes to the pot, and let the stew simmer for about 30 minutes or until the potatoes are tender. During this time, the pork will become tender, and the flavors will meld together beautifully. If the broth evaporates too much, feel free to add more water or broth to maintain the liquid level.

Once the potatoes are tender, add the napa cabbage, zucchini, and tofu (if using). Continue to simmer for another 10 minutes, or until the cabbage is wilted and the zucchini is tender.

Step 5: Final Seasoning and Adjustments

Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning as needed. You can add salt, pepper, or a touch of sugar to balance the spiciness. If you want a deeper umami flavor, a tablespoon of fish sauce can be added at this point.

For an extra burst of flavor, drizzle in 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar to brighten the stew and bring a slight tang that complements the spiciness.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Once everything is cooked to perfection, serve the Dwaeji Jjigae hot, garnished with sesame seeds and chopped green onions. Pair it with a bowl of steamed white rice for the ultimate comfort meal. The rich, spicy broth, tender pork, and soft vegetables come together in a way that is truly irresistible.

Tips for Perfecting Your Dwaeji Jjigae

  • Choose the right cut of pork: Pork shoulder or pork belly are the most commonly used cuts for Dwaeji Jjigae. Pork shoulder offers leaner meat, while pork belly adds a richer, fatty texture to the stew. Both work beautifully, so choose based on your preference.
  • Adjust the spice level: The amount of gochugaru and gochujang you add determines the spice level of the dish. If you’re sensitive to spice, start with a smaller amount and gradually increase it. You can also add some sugar to balance the heat.
  • Simmer low and slow: The key to a flavorful Dwaeji Jjigae is slow simmering. Allow the pork to cook gently, which will make it incredibly tender and ensure the flavors meld together perfectly.
  • Add extra vegetables: Feel free to add other vegetables like mushrooms, carrots, or even spinach for added texture and flavor.
  • Make it ahead: Dwaeji Jjigae can be made in advance and stored in the fridge for a day or two. The flavors continue to develop, making it even better the next day.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Pork Stew, or Dwaeji Jjigae, is a dish that brings warmth, comfort, and bold flavors to the table. Whether you’re craving something spicy, savory, or just looking to try a new recipe, this stew is sure to satisfy your taste buds. With its tender pork, vibrant vegetables, and spicy broth, it’s the perfect meal to enjoy with family and friends.

Now that you have the recipe, there’s no excuse not to dive into the world of Korean cuisine and create this flavorful stew right in your kitchen. Enjoy the process, and most importantly, enjoy the delicious results!

Happy cooking!
