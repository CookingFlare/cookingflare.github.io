Loaded Nachos with Homemade Cheese Sauce: A Crowd Favorite

Are you ready to take your nacho game to the next level? Look no further than these loaded nachos with homemade cheese sauce. This crowd-pleasing dish is perfect for game day gatherings, movie nights, or simply satisfying your craving for something cheesy and delicious. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create the ultimate loaded nachos experience right in your own kitchen. Ingredients For the Homemade Cheese Sauce: 2 tablespoons unsalted butter 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1 cup whole milk 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder Salt and pepper to taste For the Loaded Nachos: 1 bag of your favorite tortilla chips 1 pound ground beef or turkey 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed 1 cup diced tomatoes 1/2 cup chopped red onion 1/4 cup sliced jalapeños 1/4 cup sliced black olives 1/2 cup sour cream Fresh cilantro for garnish Optional: salsa, guacamole, or any other desired toppings Instructions Homemade Cheese Sauce: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute, until the mixture is golden and bubbling. Gradually whisk in the milk and continue cooking, whisking constantly until the mixture thickens, about 5 minutes. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until smooth and creamy. Season the cheese sauce with garlic powder, salt, and pepper to taste. Keep warm until ready to use. Loaded Nachos: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground beef or turkey until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat. Arrange the tortilla chips in a single layer on a large baking sheet or oven-safe dish. Sprinkle the cooked meat, black beans, diced tomatoes, red onion, jalapeños, and black olives over the tortilla chips. Drizzle the homemade cheese sauce generously over the nachos, ensuring even coverage. Place the loaded nachos in the preheated oven and bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and the nachos are heated through. Serving and Enjoying Once the loaded nachos are out of the oven, it’s time to add the finishing touches. Dollop spoonfuls of sour cream over the hot, cheesy nachos, and sprinkle fresh cilantro on top for a burst of color and flavor. For those who love a little extra heat, feel free to drizzle some salsa or add a scoop of guacamole on the side. ...
