Indian Samosas Recipe with Spiced Potatoes and Peas


If there’s one Indian snack that’s beloved worldwide, it’s the crispy, flavorful samosa. These triangular pockets of joy are filled with a delicious mixture of spiced potatoes and peas, creating a perfect balance of savory goodness. Whether you’re hosting a gathering, craving a tasty snack, or simply want to try your hand at Indian cuisine, making samosas at home is a rewarding experience. So, let’s dive into the art of creating these delectable treats right in your kitchen!

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water

For the Filling:

  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and roughly mashed
  • 1 cup green peas, boiled
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, finely chopped

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and vegetable oil.
  2. Gradually add water and knead the mixture to form a smooth, firm dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
  3. Stir in the grated ginger and minced green chilies, sautéing for a minute.
  4. Add the boiled peas and mashed potatoes, mixing well.
  5. Sprinkle in the coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Combine thoroughly.
  6. Cook the mixture for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Remove from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  8. Once cooled, add the chopped coriander leaves and mix well.

Step 3: Shape the Samosas

  1. Divide the rested dough into equal-sized balls.
  2. Roll each ball into a thin oval or round shape.
  3. Cut each oval in half to form two semi-circles.

Step 4: Assemble the Samosas

  1. Take one semi-circle and fold it into a cone shape, using water to seal the edges.
  2. Fill the cone with the prepared potato and pea mixture, then seal the open edge using water, creating a triangular pocket.
  3. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.

Step 5: Fry the Samosas

  1. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the prepared samosas in batches.
  3. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, then remove and drain on paper towels.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Your homemade Indian samosas with spiced potatoes and peas are ready to be enjoyed!
  2. Serve them hot with your favorite chutney or dipping sauce.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve just mastered the art of making authentic Indian samosas with spiced potatoes and peas. These golden, crispy delights are a true testament to the rich and diverse flavors of Indian cuisine. Whether you’re savoring them as a snack, appetizer, or part of a meal, the satisfaction of creating these treats from scratch is truly unmatched. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the aromas of Indian spices, and embark on a culinary journey that will surely delight your taste buds and those of your loved ones. Happy cooking!
