Savory Thanksgiving Ratatouille Recipes


Ratatouille is a classic French dish that combines the flavors and textures of various vegetables. This Thanksgiving, why not add a savory twist to your traditional ratatouille recipe? In this blog post, we will explore three delicious savory Thanksgiving ratatouille recipes that will impress your guests and add a unique touch to your holiday feast. So, let’s dive right in!

Recipe 1: Herbed Ratatouille with Roasted Garlic

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, diced
  • 2 bell peppers, diced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the diced eggplants, zucchinis, bell peppers, onion, garlic, and tomatoes.
  3. Drizzle the olive oil over the vegetables and toss them gently to coat evenly.
  4. Sprinkle the fresh thyme leaves, rosemary, salt, and pepper over the vegetables and toss again.
  5. Transfer the seasoned vegetables to a baking dish and spread them out in an even layer.
  6. Roast the ratatouille in the preheated oven for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  7. Serve hot as a side dish or with crusty bread for a hearty meal.

Recipe 2: Ratatouille Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  4. Add the diced eggplant, zucchini, bell pepper, onion, and garlic to the skillet and sauté for 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  5. Stir in the diced tomatoes, balsamic vinegar, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Spoon the ratatouille mixture into the hollowed-out acorn squash halves.
  7. Cover the baking sheet with foil and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the acorn squash is tender.
  8. Serve the stuffed acorn squash as a main course or as a side dish alongside your Thanksgiving turkey.

Recipe 3: Ratatouille Tart with Puff Pastry

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 medium eggplant, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface to fit a baking sheet.
  3. Transfer the puff pastry to the baking sheet and fold in the edges to create a border.
  4. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat.
  5. Add the sliced eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, onion, and garlic to the skillet and sauté for 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  6. Stir in the balsamic vinegar, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Spoon the ratatouille mixture onto the puff pastry, spreading it evenly.
  8. Brush the edges of the puff pastry with the beaten egg to create a golden crust.
  9. Drizzle the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil over the vegetables.
  10. Bake the ratatouille tart in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the puff pastry is golden and crispy.
  11. Allow the tart to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

These savory Thanksgiving ratatouille recipes are sure to be a hit at your holiday gathering. Whether you choose to serve them as a side dish or as a main course, they will add a burst of flavor and color to your Thanksgiving table. So, don’t hesitate to give them a try and impress your guests with your culinary skills. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
