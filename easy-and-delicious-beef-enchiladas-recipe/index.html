Easy and Delicious Beef Enchiladas Recipe


Beef enchiladas are a classic comfort food that combines the rich, savory flavors of seasoned beef with the satisfying, cheesy goodness of melted cheese and tangy enchilada sauce, all wrapped up in soft corn tortillas. This easy beef enchilada recipe is perfect for a weeknight dinner, a family gathering, or even meal prep for the week ahead. With a few simple ingredients and a little prep time, you can create a dish that’s bursting with flavor and sure to please everyone at the table.

Whether you’re new to cooking or an experienced home chef, this recipe is approachable and offers a delicious way to enjoy a Mexican-inspired meal from the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you start cooking, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients. This recipe is simple yet flavorful, featuring a combination of beef, spices, tortillas, cheese, and a delicious enchilada sauce. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Beef Filling:

  • 1 lb ground beef (use lean beef for a healthier option)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (4 oz) diced green chilies (optional for a little extra heat)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup beef broth or water (to moisten the filling)
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional for extra cheesy filling)

For the Enchiladas:

  • 8 small corn tortillas (you can use flour tortillas if you prefer)
  • 1 can (15 oz) enchilada sauce (choose your preferred spiciness level)
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a mix of both)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)
  • Sour cream for serving (optional)
  • Sliced jalapeños for added spice (optional)

Instructions for Preparing Beef Enchiladas

Making beef enchiladas might seem complicated at first, but the process is quite straightforward. Follow the steps below to create an easy and flavorful beef enchilada dish that your whole family will love!

Step 1: Prepare the Beef Filling

  1. Cook the Ground Beef: In a large skillet over medium heat, add the ground beef. Break it apart with a spoon as it cooks, ensuring it cooks evenly on all sides. You’ll want the beef to brown and become crumbly. This should take about 5 to 7 minutes.

  2. Add Onions and Garlic: Once the beef is browned, add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet. Stir them into the beef and cook for an additional 3 to 4 minutes, until the onion becomes soft and translucent.

  3. Season the Beef: Sprinkle the ground cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper over the beef mixture. Stir to coat the beef evenly with the spices. If you want a bit more heat, you can also add the diced green chilies at this point.

  4. Moisten the Filling: Pour in the beef broth or water and stir to combine. Let the mixture simmer for 5 to 7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the liquid to reduce slightly. If you want an extra cheesy filling, add 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese into the beef mixture, and stir until melted and fully incorporated.

  5. Taste and Adjust: Taste the beef mixture and adjust the seasonings if needed. Once you’re satisfied with the flavor, remove the skillet from the heat and set it aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Tortillas

Before assembling your enchiladas, you need to soften the tortillas so they can easily roll without tearing.

  1. Heat the Tortillas: In a dry skillet or griddle, heat each tortilla for 10 to 20 seconds per side. This will soften them up, making them more pliable for rolling. If you prefer, you can lightly fry the tortillas in hot oil to achieve a crispier texture, but this step is optional.

  2. Keep Warm: Once the tortillas are heated, stack them on a plate and cover them with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm until you’re ready to assemble the enchiladas.

Step 3: Assemble the Beef Enchiladas

Now comes the fun part—assembling the enchiladas! You’ll layer the beef mixture and cheese inside each tortilla, then roll them up and arrange them in a baking dish.

  1. Prepare the Baking Dish: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with cooking spray or a small amount of oil to prevent the enchiladas from sticking.

  2. Fill the Tortillas: Take one tortilla at a time and spoon about 2 to 3 tablespoons of the beef mixture into the center. Add a small amount of shredded cheese on top of the beef mixture if you’d like an extra cheesy layer. Roll the tortilla tightly around the filling and place it seam-side down in the prepared baking dish. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and beef mixture.

  3. Top with Enchilada Sauce and Cheese: Once all the tortillas are rolled and placed in the baking dish, pour the enchilada sauce evenly over the top of the rolled tortillas. Be sure to cover all the enchiladas with sauce for the best flavor. Sprinkle the shredded cheese over the sauce.

Step 4: Bake the Enchiladas

  1. Bake the Enchiladas: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the enchiladas are heated through. The sauce should be slightly bubbling at the edges of the dish.

  2. Garnish and Serve: Once the enchiladas are done, remove them from the oven. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro, and serve with a dollop of sour cream and sliced jalapeños on the side if you like a little extra kick.

Tips for the Best Beef Enchiladas

  • Choose Your Protein: While this recipe uses ground beef, you can easily substitute it with ground turkey, chicken, or even a vegetarian filling such as beans or sautéed vegetables. The key is to ensure the filling is flavorful, so season it well with spices.

  • Homemade Enchilada Sauce: If you want to take your beef enchiladas to the next level, consider making your own enchilada sauce. It’s easy to make from scratch with ingredients like tomatoes, chili powder, garlic, and onions. Homemade sauce adds a personalized touch that can make the dish even more special.

  • Cheese Variety: While cheddar is a popular choice, try using a blend of cheeses like Monterey Jack, Mexican blend, or even cotija for an authentic flavor. The meltability and texture of different cheeses can elevate the dish.

  • Make Ahead: Beef enchiladas can be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge for up to 2 days before baking. Simply assemble the enchiladas, cover with plastic wrap or foil, and refrigerate. When you’re ready to bake, remove the cover and bake as directed, adding an extra 5-10 minutes if the dish is chilled.

  • Freezer-Friendly: You can also freeze the enchiladas for a later meal. After assembling them, wrap them tightly in foil or plastic wrap and store them in an airtight container. When ready to bake, thaw them in the fridge overnight, and bake as usual.

Variations and Customization

While the classic beef enchiladas are delicious on their own, there are endless ways to customize this dish. Here are some variations you can try:

  1. Vegetarian Enchiladas: Swap out the beef for a variety of vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, onions, and black beans. Sauté them with your favorite spices for a flavorful vegetarian version.

  2. Spicy Beef Enchiladas: If you like things hot, try adding more chili powder, cayenne pepper, or even some diced jalapeños to the beef filling. You can also switch out the regular enchilada sauce for a spicy chipotle or verde sauce for an extra punch.

  3. Cheese Lovers Enchiladas: Add more layers of cheese to the filling itself, or use multiple types of cheese in the sauce for a gooey, cheesy experience. Try blending Monterey Jack, pepper jack, and cheddar for a fun mix.

  4. Green Enchiladas: For a tangy twist, use green enchilada sauce made from tomatillos and green chilies instead of the traditional red sauce. This adds a fresh, bright flavor to the dish.

Serving Suggestions

Beef enchiladas are a hearty dish that can be served with various sides to complete the meal. Here are some tasty options to accompany your enchiladas:

  • Mexican Rice: A flavorful side of Mexican rice, made with tomatoes, garlic, and cumin, complements the richness of the beef enchiladas.
  • Refried Beans: Serve with a side of creamy refried beans for a classic Mexican meal.
  • Guacamole and Salsa: Fresh guacamole with a hint of lime, along with a zesty salsa, will add a refreshing contrast to the warmth of the enchiladas.
  • Shredded Lettuce and Sour Cream: For a lighter touch, serve the enchiladas with a side of shredded lettuce and a dollop of sour cream to balance out the richness.

Conclusion

Beef enchiladas are a wonderful dish that brings together savory, spicy, and cheesy flavors in every bite. Whether you’re preparing them for a casual weeknight dinner or a special family occasion, this easy recipe will impress your guests and satisfy your hunger. The combination of seasoned beef, soft tortillas, melted cheese, and enchilada sauce creates a meal that’s simple, delicious, and perfect for any time of year. Happy cooking!
