Trinidadian Doubles and Street Eats


Trinidadian cuisine is a beautiful blend of diverse cultural influences, resulting in an explosion of flavors and aromas that captivate the senses. One iconic street food that embodies the essence of Trinidadian culinary heritage is Doubles. Originating from the bustling streets of Trinidad and Tobago, Doubles are a beloved snack that has captured the hearts and palates of locals and visitors alike.

The Origins of Doubles

Doubles are a quintessential street food, commonly found in the vibrant markets and bustling street corners of Trinidad. This delectable dish consists of two fluffy, fried flatbreads known as “bara,” generously filled with a savory chickpea curry, and topped with an array of chutneys, hot sauces, and pickled vegetables. The origins of Doubles can be traced back to the Indian immigrant community in Trinidad, where culinary traditions melded with local ingredients and flavors, giving birth to this beloved delicacy.

Authentic Trinidadian Doubles Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of chickpeas
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Water
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Tamarind chutney
  • Mango chutney
  • Hot pepper sauce
  • Pickled green mango

Instructions:

  1. Soak the chickpeas in water overnight. Drain and rinse the chickpeas the next day.
  2. In a large pot, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, cumin, turmeric, curry powder, coriander, cayenne pepper, and salt. Cover with water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer until the chickpeas are tender and the mixture has thickened into a flavorful curry.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a soft dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into thin, flat discs.
  5. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Fry the flat discs until they puff up and turn golden brown.
  6. To assemble the Doubles, place a generous spoonful of the chickpea curry between two bara. Top with tamarind chutney, mango chutney, hot pepper sauce, and pickled green mango.

The Experience of Street Eats in Trinidad

When you indulge in Trinidadian Doubles, you not only savor a delightful culinary creation but also immerse yourself in the lively street food culture of Trinidad. The aroma of sizzling frying pans, the rhythmic clang of utensils, and the vibrant colors of the condiments create an unforgettable sensory experience. As you take your first bite, the medley of spices, textures, and flavors dances on your taste buds, leaving an indelible impression of Trinidad’s rich gastronomic tapestry.

Exploring the Streets for Culinary Delights

While Doubles hold a special place in Trinidad’s street food scene, the culinary adventure doesn’t end there. As you wander through the vibrant streets, you’ll encounter an array of enticing street eats, each offering a unique glimpse into the local food culture. From mouthwatering roti filled with tender meats and fragrant spices to crispy, golden-fried fish cakes, the diversity of street eats in Trinidad reflects the nation’s melting pot of culinary traditions.

Conclusion

Trinidadian Doubles and street eats exemplify the heart and soul of Trinidad’s culinary landscape. Through these beloved dishes, one can truly appreciate the fusion of cultural influences that have shaped Trinidad’s gastronomic identity. Whether you’re savoring Doubles on a bustling street corner or venturing into the labyrinth of flavors in a local market, Trinidad’s street eats promise a journey of discovery and delight for food enthusiasts and curious travelers alike.

Experience the vibrant tapestry of Trinidadian cuisine through the lens of street eats, and let the flavors and aromas transport you to the heart of this enchanting Caribbean island.
