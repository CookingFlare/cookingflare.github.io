Rustic Thanksgiving Recipes for a Cozy Feast


Thanksgiving is a time for family gatherings, love, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. Often, Thanksgiving feasts are centered around rich flavors and hearty ingredients. In this blog post, I’ll be sharing rustic Thanksgiving recipes to fulfill your craving for that cozy, comforting meal.

Rustic Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a staple of any Thanksgiving meal, and this rustic version is sure to be a crowd-pleaser. The secret is to use a combination of potatoes and yam or sweet potato for added sweetness and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound russet potatoes
  • 1 medium sweet potato
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons whole milk
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Peel and chop the potatoes and sweet potato into equal-sized pieces.
  2. Place them in a large pot with ample water to cover, and add a pinch of salt.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer, and cook until tender, around 15-20 minutes.
  4. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot, then add the butter and milk. Mash until smooth.
  5. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.

Braised Balsamic Cabbage

Cabbage is an affordable, versatile, and nutritious side dish that pairs perfectly with the richness of Thanksgiving fare. This flavorful braise creates a savory and slightly sweet accompaniment that gives a unique twist to the traditional cooked cabbage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cabbage, cored and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cupptimeless balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or braiser over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions and a pinch of salt, cooking until softened, about 8 minutes.
  3. Stir in the balsamic vinegar and chicken broth, then bring to a simmer.
  4. Add the sliced cabbage, and cook until wilted and caramelized, around 15 minutes.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme, and continue cooking for an additional 5 minutes to meld flavors.

Savory Sage and Lemon Stock Turkey Breast

A smaller turkey may have smaller priorities, but this turkey will be the star of your feast. The use of sage and lemon infuses the turkey with both rustic and bright flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in turkey breast
  • Salt & freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh sage leaves
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oven to 325°F.
  2. Pat the turkey breast dry, then season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Heat the olive oil in an oven-safe pan over medium heat, and brown the turkey breast on both sides, about 7 minutes total.
  4. In a small bowl, combine sage, lemon juice, and garlic, then brush over the turkey breast.
  5. Place the pan in the oven and cook until the internal temperature of the turkey reaches 165°F, about 1 hour and 15 minutes.
  6. Allow to rest for 10 minutes before slicing.

Rustic Pumpkin Pie

This rustic pumpkin pie takes you back to the old-fashioned flavors of childhood, with a buttery, flaky crust that’s a perfect base for the spiced pumpkin filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 refrigerated pie crust
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Roll out the pie crust, and transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk pumpkin puree, sugar, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, salt, eggs, and heavy cream.
  4. Pour the filling into the pie crust.
  5. Dot the top with melted butter, and bake for 15 minutes.
  6. Reduce the oven temperature to 350°F, and bake for an additional 45-50 minutes, until the filling is set.
  7. Allow the pie to cool before slicing and serving.

As the heart of autumn celebrations, Thanksgiving is a perfect opportunity to share your love for friends and family through the simple pleasures of good food. These rustic recipes are sure to bring a touch of comfort and warmth to your feast. So gather your loved ones, and enjoy a wonderful Thanksgiving!
