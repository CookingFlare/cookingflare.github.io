Keftedes: Juicy Greek Meatballs Recipe


When it comes to traditional Greek cuisine, few dishes capture the heart of Mediterranean flavors quite like keftedes, the juicy and aromatic Greek meatballs. Perfect as an appetizer, a snack, or part of a hearty main course, keftedes bring together the perfect balance of herbs, spices, and tender meat to create an unforgettable dish that’s beloved by food enthusiasts around the world.

What Makes Keftedes Special?

Keftedes stand out thanks to their unique combination of fresh ingredients and well-rounded flavors. Unlike your ordinary meatballs, Greek keftedes are seasoned with an array of Mediterranean ingredients like fresh mint, oregano, parsley, and garlic that provide distinct herbal notes. Bread soaked in milk is often added to enhance their tenderness, giving these meatballs their melt-in-your-mouth texture. Traditional keftedes are often pan-fried for that signature crispy exterior, but they remain juicy and flavorful on the inside.

This recipe honors the classic preparation while adding a few tips and tricks to ensure your meatballs come out perfectly every time. Whether you’re new to Greek cooking or a seasoned chef looking to refine your techniques, this keftedes recipe is sure to become a favorite in your repertoire.

Ingredients You’ll Need

You don’t need a long list of exotic ingredients to make authentic and delicious keftedes. Many of the items are everyday essentials. Here’s everything you’ll need to bring these Greek meatballs to life:

For the Meatballs:

  • 500g (1 lb) ground beef – You can also mix beef with ground pork or lamb for extra flavor.
  • 1 medium white onion – Grated or finely minced for a smooth texture.
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced – Fresh garlic is key for depth of flavor.
  • 2 slices of stale bread, crust removed – Soaked in milk to keep the meatballs moist.
  • 60ml (1/4 cup) milk – For softening the bread.
  • 1 large egg – Acts as a binder to hold the meatballs together.
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped – Adds a fresh, herbal note.
  • 2 tbsp fresh mint, chopped – Essential for that authentic flavor.
  • 1 tsp dried oregano – A classic seasoning in Greek cuisine.
  • 1 tsp ground cumin – Adds a subtle warm undertone.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste.
  • Olive oil, for frying – Use a good quality olive oil to stay true to Greek cooking traditions.

For Serving:

  • Lemon wedges – A squeeze of lemon brings brightness to the dish.
  • Tzatziki sauce – A creamy yogurt and cucumber dip that pairs beautifully with keftedes.
  • Pita bread or a simple green salad – Optional, but complements the dish perfectly.

Step-By-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Bread Mixture

Start by soaking the stale bread in milk for about 5 minutes. Once softened, squeeze out the excess milk and crumble the bread into a large mixing bowl. This step ensures your meatballs are tender and moist instead of dense.

2. Build the Flavor Base

Add the grated onion, minced garlic, parsley, mint, oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper to the bowl. Stir until evenly combined. These herbs and spices are the foundation of the bold and fresh flavors that make keftedes irresistible.

3. Combine the Meat and Egg

Add the ground beef (or mixed ground meat) and the egg to the mixing bowl. Using your hands or a wooden spoon, gently combine all the ingredients until they’re evenly distributed. Avoid overmixing, as this can make the meatballs tough.

4. Shape the Meatballs

Take small portions of the mixture and roll them into balls about the size of a golf ball. Place each meatball on a tray or plate as you work. You should end up with approximately 20-24 meatballs, depending on the size.

5. Chill the Meatballs

Pop the tray into the fridge for around 20–30 minutes. Chilling helps the meatballs hold their shape while cooking and allows the flavors to meld together for a stronger taste.

6. Pan-Fry to Golden Perfection

Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the meatballs, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them for 6–8 minutes, turning occasionally, until they’re golden brown on all sides. You may need to fry them in batches to avoid steaming them instead of getting that crispy exterior.

7. Drain Excess Oil

Remove the cooked meatballs with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil. This step ensures your keftedes remain crispy without being greasy.

Tips for Perfect Keftedes

  1. Fresh Herbs Are Key – While dried herbs can be used, fresh mint and parsley bring a brightness that takes the dish to another level.
  2. Don’t Skip the Bread – Soaking bread in milk is a cornerstone of this recipe. If you skip this step, you risk losing the signature soft texture.
  3. Use Quality Olive Oil – Since olive oil plays a huge role in frying and flavoring the meatballs, using a high-quality, extra-virgin olive oil will make a noticeable difference.
  4. Control the Heat – Fry the meatballs over medium heat to ensure they cook evenly without burning or drying out.

Serving Suggestions

Keftedes are incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in many ways:

  • Serve them on a platter with a drizzle of fresh lemon juice and a side of tzatziki sauce for dipping.
  • Pair them with pita bread and a medley of fresh vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onion for a satisfying meal.
  • Add them atop a bed of fluffy rice pilaf or orzo for a comforting, homestyle dinner.
  • Make a wrap by stuffing them along with some tzatziki into warm pita bread.

Whether you’re serving them at a party or during a laid-back dinner at home, keftedes always steal the show with their bold yet comforting flavors.

The Heart of Greek Hospitality

Keftedes are more than just a dish; they’re an embodiment of Greek hospitality and culinary heritage. In Greece, food is often the center of gatherings, celebrations, and meaningful conversations, and keftedes are always welcome on the table. Their mix of simplicity and sophistication feels like a warm hug in the form of food.

By making this keftedes recipe, you’re not just cooking meatballs—you’re connecting with a rich tradition that’s been passed down through generations. Whether you’re serving them to loved ones or simply treating yourself, keftedes are bound to impress.

So fire up your skillet and let these juicy Greek meatballs transport you straight to the sun-soaked shores of Greece, where every bite is a celebration of the Mediterranean spirit!
