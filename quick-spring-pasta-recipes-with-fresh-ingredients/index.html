Quick Spring Pasta Recipes with Fresh Ingredients


As the chill of winter recedes and the world bursts into color, it’s time to embrace the freshness and vibrancy of spring in our kitchens. One of the most satisfying ways to celebrate this seasonal shift is by cooking quick, flavorful pasta dishes that highlight fresh ingredients. Spring produce like asparagus, peas, artichokes, baby spinach, and herbs breathe new life into classic pasta, turning simple meals into memorable culinary experiences. These recipes are fast enough for weeknight dinners but elegant enough to impress your guests.

Let’s dive into a collection of recipes that combine the quick-cooking nature of pasta with the bright, clean flavors of spring’s best offerings.

1. Lemon Asparagus Spaghetti with Parmesan and Mint

The bright tang of lemon and the tender crunch of asparagus create a delightful contrast in this easy pasta dish. Garlic and Parmesan add depth, while mint introduces a refreshing herbal note.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 1 bunch asparagus (trimmed, cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package instructions.
  2. In the last 3 minutes of cooking, add asparagus pieces to the boiling pasta water.
  3. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and saut√© for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Drain pasta and asparagus, reserving a cup of pasta water.
  5. Toss spaghetti and asparagus with garlic oil, lemon zest, and juice. Add reserved pasta water gradually to loosen the sauce if necessary.
  6. Stir in Parmesan and mint, seasoning with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan sprinkled on top.

Notes:

This dish shines in its simplicity, where the fresh lemon brightens every bite. Mint adds an unexpected fresh punch that complements the sweetness of asparagus. Using the pasta cooking water helps create a silky sauce that clings perfectly to the strands.

2. Spring Pea and Ricotta Rotini with Basil Pesto

Sweet spring peas blended with creamy ricotta and vibrant pesto make a colorful and nutritious pasta that comes together in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 10 oz rotini pasta
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 cup whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook rotini in salted boiling water until al dente. In the last 2 minutes, add peas to the pot.
  2. Drain pasta and peas, reserving a bit of pasta water.
  3. In a bowl, combine ricotta and pesto. Gradually mix in reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce.
  4. Toss the pasta and peas with the ricotta-pesto mixture and olive oil.
  5. Garnish with toasted pine nuts and freshly cracked pepper before serving.

Notes:

The sweetness of peas paired with the rich creaminess of ricotta offers a satisfying mouthfeel. Basil pesto adds herby layers without overpowering. For a nutty crunch, pine nuts are an excellent addition but feel free to skip if you prefer.

3. Garlic and Herb Fettuccine with Baby Spinach and Cherry Tomatoes

Bursting with a medley of spring herbs and juicy tomatoes, this garlic-forward pasta pairs perfectly with tender baby spinach. The creaminess from a hint of mascarpone adds subtle indulgence without heaviness.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fettuccine
  • 3 cups baby spinach leaves
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • 4 cloves garlic (thinly sliced)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup mascarpone cheese
  • Fresh parsley and chives (finely chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook fettuccine in boiling salted water until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a wide skillet over medium heat. Add garlic slices and cook until lightly golden.
  3. Add cherry tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes until they begin to soften.
  4. Toss in baby spinach; cook until just wilted.
  5. Drain pasta and add to the skillet. Mix in mascarpone cheese and stir well until pasta is coated.
  6. Finish by folding in chopped parsley and chives. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve with a drizzle of olive oil and freshly grated Parmesan if desired.

Notes:

Mascarpone provides a velvety texture that elevates this spring pasta beyond simple olive oil-based dishes. The combination of fresh herbs captures the essence of the season perfectly, making it both comforting and refreshing.

4. Artichoke and Lemon Orzo Salad with Feta and Dill

This orzo recipe doubles as a warm salad or light main dish, bringing together tender artichoke hearts, zesty lemon, and pungent dill for an irresistibly fresh meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup orzo pasta
  • 1 can (14 oz) artichoke hearts (drained and quartered)
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill (chopped)
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook orzo pasta according to package directions, drain and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together lemon juice, zest, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add orzo, artichokes, feta, and dill to the bowl; toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve slightly warm or chilled.

5. Pea Shoot and Prosciutto Farfalle with Aged Balsamic

This elegant farfalle recipe features tender pea shoots and thin slices of salty prosciutto, balanced by the sweet complexity of aged balsamic vinegar.

Ingredients:

  • 10 oz farfalle pasta
  • 2 cups pea shoots
  • 4 oz prosciutto (sliced thin)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons aged balsamic vinegar
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook farfalle according to package instructions.
  2. While pasta cooks, gently heat olive oil in a skillet and saut√© garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add prosciutto slices for 1-2 minutes to warm through but not crisp.
  4. Drain pasta and reserve some cooking water.
  5. Toss pasta with garlic oil, prosciutto, pea shoots, and a splash of pasta water.
  6. Drizzle balsamic vinegar over the pasta and grind fresh black pepper on top.
  7. Serve immediately.

Notes:

Pea shoots offer a delicate, fresh flavor that plays well with prosciutto‚Äôs saltiness. The aged balsamic vinegar provides sweetness and acidity that heightens the dish‚Äôs appeal, making it a quick yet sophisticated choice for spring evenings.

Tips for Cooking with Fresh Spring Produce in Pasta

  • Timing is everything: Most spring vegetables cook quickly. Adding them in the last few minutes of pasta boiling times is a clever way to save time and retain their crunch and color.
  • Use pasta water: This starchy, slightly salty water helps bind sauces and portions the perfect consistency, without heavy cream or excess oil.
  • Embrace herbs: Spring herbs like mint, basil, dill, and chives brighten dishes naturally without added fat or salt.
  • Balance flavors: The freshness of vegetables often pairs beautifully with rich cheeses like Parmesan, ricotta, or mascarpone.
  • Don‚Äôt overdo it: Simple seasoning and fresh ingredients often speak for themselves. Avoid overwhelming dishes with too many components.
