Honey and Lavender Cake: Unconventional Flavor Combo


Indulge in a taste of nature with this exquisite Honey and Lavender Cake. A delightful fusion of floral and sweet, every slice is sure to delight the senses. Let’s embark on a culinary journey to explore one of the most unconventional yet delectable flavor combinations.

The first time I came across the idea of mixing honey and lavender in a cake was during a serene countryside vacation at a quaint bed and breakfast. The aroma of lavender fields filled the air, and local honey from a nearby beekeeper’s farm left an indelible impression on me. The duo had the potential to create something truly unforgettable, and I knew I had to give it a try.

Welcome to the land of perfect harmony, where the floral notes of lavender embrace the sweet richness of honey, resulting in a unique and sophisticated cake. Combining these two natural ingredients creates a perfect balance of flavors that is both comforting and luxurious.

What You Need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • Generous pinch of salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon lavender buds, finely ground
  • 1 cup local raw honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • Lavender buttercream (recipe below)
  • Optional: Fresh lavender sprigs for garnish

What to Do:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C) and grease two 9-inch round cake pans with butter. Dust the bottom with flour and tap out any excess.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.

  3. Cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy in a large stand mixer or a bowl using an electric hand mixer. This should take about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the ground lavender and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the milk and honey until well incorporated.

  6. Reduce the mixer speed to low and add half the flour mixture to the butter mixture. Alternate with the honey mixture, ending with the flour. Mix until the batter is smooth. Then, fold in the Greek yogurt using a spatula.

  7. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans, smooth the tops, and place them on the middle rack of the preheated oven. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean. Cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring the cakes to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Once your cakes have cooled, make your lavender buttercream. Start by beating the butter in a large bowl until creamy. Gradually add in the powdered sugar while continuously mixing. Add the lavender buds and beat until combined. Finally, add in the lemon juice and mix until the desired consistency is reached.

  9. Apply a layer of lavender buttercream on one of the cake layers. Place the second layer on top and cover the entire cake with an even layer of buttercream. Garnish with fresh lavender sprigs if desired.
