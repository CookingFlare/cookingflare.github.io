Delicious Homemade Chimichurri Sauce for Grilled Meat


Chimichurri sauce is a vibrant and flavorful condiment that pairs beautifully with grilled meats. Originating from Argentina, this fresh, herbaceous sauce has become a staple in many kitchens worldwide. Whether you’re grilling steak, chicken, or even vegetables, chimichurri adds a zesty and aromatic kick that elevates your dish to new heights.

In this post, we’ll guide you through making a homemade chimichurri sauce that will take your grilling game to the next level. We’ll explore the ingredients, preparation methods, and tips for using it with your favorite grilled dishes. So, fire up your grill, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into making this easy and delicious chimichurri sauce!

What is Chimichurri Sauce?

Chimichurri sauce is a condiment made with fresh herbs, garlic, vinegar, and oil, typically used in Argentine and Uruguayan cuisine. It is often served as an accompaniment to grilled meats, but it can also be used as a marinade or dressing. The sauce has a vibrant green color, thanks to the combination of parsley and oregano, with a tangy kick from red wine vinegar. The garlic and chili flakes add an aromatic depth, while the olive oil brings everything together, making this sauce incredibly versatile and addictive.

What makes chimichurri stand out is its freshness and complexity of flavors. Unlike heavy, creamy sauces, chimichurri is light and bright, allowing the flavors of your grilled meat to shine while adding its own distinct taste.

Ingredients for Homemade Chimichurri Sauce

To make chimichurri, you’ll need a handful of fresh ingredients. The beauty of this sauce is that it doesn’t require any fancy or hard-to-find items—just a few basics that you likely already have in your kitchen. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need:

Fresh Parsley

The foundation of chimichurri is fresh parsley. It provides a refreshing and herbal flavor that forms the base of the sauce. Be sure to use flat-leaf parsley for the most authentic flavor.

Fresh Oregano

Oregano adds a warm, earthy note that complements the parsley. Fresh oregano is preferred for its more vibrant flavor, but you can use dried oregano if fresh is unavailable. Just reduce the amount since dried herbs are more concentrated.

Garlic

Garlic is the aromatic star of the sauce, providing a bold and savory flavor. A few cloves are all you need to give your chimichurri a delightful punch.

Red Wine Vinegar

The tangy acidity of red wine vinegar balances out the rich flavors of the herbs and garlic. It’s essential for creating that signature zesty kick in chimichurri.

Olive Oil

A high-quality extra virgin olive oil is the best choice for chimichurri. The oil binds the ingredients together and gives the sauce a smooth, luscious texture. It also adds a subtle fruity flavor.

Red Pepper Flakes

To give chimichurri some heat, red pepper flakes are often added. The amount can be adjusted based on your heat preference. Some people prefer a mild sauce, while others like it spicy.

Salt and Black Pepper

Simple seasoning with salt and freshly ground black pepper helps bring all the flavors together and enhances the overall taste of the sauce.

Lemon Juice (Optional)

Some variations of chimichurri include a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for extra acidity. This step is optional, but it can brighten the sauce and provide an additional layer of flavor.

How to Make Chimichurri Sauce

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, it’s time to prepare your chimichurri sauce. Don’t worry if you’ve never made it before—it’s quick and simple! Follow these steps, and you’ll have a delicious homemade sauce in no time.

Step 1: Prepare the Herbs

Start by finely chopping the parsley and oregano. You want to get the herbs as finely chopped as possible, as this will help them blend seamlessly with the other ingredients. Some people prefer to use a food processor for this step, but hand-chopping gives the sauce a rustic, textured quality that many people love.

Step 2: Mince the Garlic

Next, finely mince the garlic cloves. You want the garlic to be small enough to release all its flavor into the sauce but not so small that it turns into a paste. A garlic press works great here if you have one.

Step 3: Combine the Ingredients

In a medium bowl, combine the chopped parsley, oregano, and minced garlic. Add in the red pepper flakes, salt, and pepper. Mix everything together to combine the dry ingredients evenly.

Step 4: Add the Wet Ingredients

To the bowl of herbs, add the red wine vinegar and olive oil. Stir everything well, ensuring the oil and vinegar coat all the herbs and garlic. If you’re using lemon juice, add it at this point as well.

Step 5: Adjust the Flavor

Taste your chimichurri and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you want more acidity, add a bit more vinegar or lemon juice. For extra heat, toss in a few more red pepper flakes. You can also add more salt and pepper to suit your preference.

Step 6: Let It Sit

For the best flavor, let your chimichurri sit for at least 30 minutes before serving. This resting time allows the flavors to meld together and develop. Ideally, you want to let it sit for a few hours, but even 30 minutes can make a noticeable difference.

Tips for Making the Best Chimichurri Sauce

  1. Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh herbs, garlic, and high-quality olive oil make a big difference in the flavor of your chimichurri. Opt for fresh over dried whenever possible to achieve the most vibrant taste.

  2. Customize the Heat: If you like your chimichurri with a bit more spice, you can add more red pepper flakes or even a small minced chili pepper. Alternatively, if you prefer a milder sauce, reduce the amount of pepper flakes or skip them altogether.

  3. Let It Rest: The longer chimichurri sits, the better it tastes. The flavors continue to meld, and the sauce becomes even more flavorful over time. If you have the time, make your chimichurri a few hours in advance or even the day before serving.

  4. Don’t Overdo It with the Vinegar: While vinegar is essential for the tangy flavor of chimichurri, too much vinegar can overpower the other ingredients. Start with the suggested amount and adjust to your taste.

  5. Storage: Chimichurri can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Just be sure to let it come to room temperature before serving, as the olive oil may solidify when chilled.

Using Chimichurri Sauce with Grilled Meat

Chimichurri sauce is most commonly served with grilled meats, particularly steaks. The fresh, zesty sauce complements the smoky flavors of grilled beef and enhances its natural taste. Here are some ideas for pairing chimichurri with your grilled meats:

Grilled Steak

A classic pairing for chimichurri sauce is a juicy, grilled steak. The acidity and freshness of the chimichurri cut through the richness of the beef, creating a perfect balance of flavors. Try it with a ribeye, flank steak, or skirt steak for a true Argentine-inspired meal.

Grilled Chicken

Chimichurri sauce works wonderfully with grilled chicken. The bright, herbaceous flavors bring out the best in the chicken, especially when it’s cooked with a bit of smokiness on the grill. For added flavor, marinate the chicken in some chimichurri before grilling.

Grilled Lamb

Lamb is another excellent choice for chimichurri. The bold, earthy flavors of lamb pair beautifully with the fresh, tangy sauce. Try it with lamb chops or a grilled leg of lamb for a delicious and impressive meal.

Grilled Vegetables

Chimichurri isn’t just for meat—it’s also fantastic with grilled vegetables. The sauce’s vibrant flavors work well with smoky, charred veggies like bell peppers, zucchini, and eggplant. For a vegetarian option, serve chimichurri with grilled portobello mushrooms or cauliflower steaks.

Grilled Fish

While chimichurri is traditionally associated with red meat, it also complements grilled fish, especially oily fish like salmon or trout. The herbaceous sauce adds a fresh contrast to the rich flavor of the fish.

Conclusion

Making your own chimichurri sauce at home is incredibly easy, and the result is a flavorful, vibrant condiment that takes your grilled meats to the next level. The combination of fresh herbs, garlic, and vinegar creates a sauce that enhances the natural flavors of meat while adding a zesty, aromatic kick. Whether you’re grilling steak, chicken, lamb, or vegetables, chimichurri is the perfect accompaniment.

So, the next time you fire up your grill, make sure to whip up a batch of this delicious homemade chimichurri sauce. It’s a simple yet powerful way to elevate your meal and impress your guests. Enjoy!
