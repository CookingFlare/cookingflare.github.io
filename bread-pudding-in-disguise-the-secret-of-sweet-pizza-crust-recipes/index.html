Bread Pudding in Disguise: The Secret of Sweet Pizza Crust Recipes


If you’ve ever craved the sinful allure of bread pudding but couldn’t commit to making two entirely separate desserts, fear not. Enter sweet pizza crust recipes – your secret weapon to transform humble dough into an indulgent bread pudding experience. With this delicious twist, you can now enjoy an irresistible blend of textures and flavors, right at home.

But what, exactly, makes these crusts so divine? In this post, we accept no shortcuts, taking you through the process of making your very own sweet pizza crust recipe. By weaving together savant techniques and expert tips, we empower you to indulge in a flavorful bread pudding without any preconceived notions.

Ingredients

To embark on our sweet pizza crust adventure, gather the following ingredients:

  1. 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 1 teaspoon sugar
  3. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  4. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  5. 1/2 cup warm water
  6. 1 package active dry yeast (about 2 1/4 teaspoons)
  7. 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  8. 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  9. 2 eggs
  10. 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  11. 3/4 cup milk
  12. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  13. 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  14. 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  15. 3 cups stale French bread, cubed
  16. 2 cups chopped pears
  17. 2 cups fresh strawberries, halved
  18. 1 cup brown sugar
  19. 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  20. 1/4 cup heavy whipping cream

Instructions

For the Sweet Pizza Crust

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Stir to evenly distribute the ingredients.
  2. Add the olive oil, warm water, and yeast; mix well to create a soft dough.
  3. On a lightly floured surface, knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. This should take about 10 minutes.
  4. Place the dough in a well-oiled bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rest in a warm place for an hour or until it doubles in size.
  5. Once the dough has doubled, punch it down and fold in the shredded mozzarella, Parmesan, eggs, and softened butter. Knead until well combined.
  6. Divide the dough into two equal parts and shape them into rounds. Cover them and let them rest for an additional 30 minutes.
  7. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and lightly oil a pizza pan or baking sheet.

For the Bread Pudding Filling

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the milk, sugar, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and nutmeg until smooth.
  2. Toss the cubed French bread, pears, and strawberries in the milk mixture until fully coated. Let the bread soak up the mixture for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Once the dough has completed its second resting period, roll out one of the dough rounds to your desired thickness. Place it on your prepared pizza pan or baking sheet.
  4. Spread the soaked bread mixture evenly onto the dough, followed by the chopped pears and a layer of halved strawberries.
  5. Evenly distribute the brown sugar and butter pieces on top of the fruit.
  6. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the remaining dough to form the top crust. Lay it over the filling, tucking the edges under the bottom crust to seal.
  7. Brush the top crust with heavy cream and use a sharp knife to create a few vents in the dough for steam to escape.
  8. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

Enjoy!

Once your sweet pizza crust bread pudding is fresh from the oven, let it cool for a few minutes to set before serving. Slice into wedges and indulge in the delicious taste of bread pudding disguised as an innovative dessert. Serve with a dollop of whipped cream or scoop of vanilla ice cream, and enjoy this scrumptious spin on a classic dessert.

Find yourself craving sweet pizza crust recipes again and again, no longer constrained by traditional dessert expectations. Elevate your home baked offerings in ways you never thought possible! Happy cooking!
