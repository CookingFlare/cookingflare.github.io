Easy Spring Desserts: Sweet Treats for the Season


Spring is a time of renewal and rejuvenation, and what better way to celebrate this vibrant season than with a selection of easy spring desserts? As the weather warms and flowers bloom, lighter, fruit-infused, and refreshing treats take center stage in kitchens around the world. Whether you’re hosting a garden party, a spring picnic, or simply looking to brighten up your dessert table, there are plenty of delicious options to choose from. From delicate fruit tarts to creamy mousse and zesty lemon cakes, these springtime desserts are as delightful as the season itself.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of spring-inspired desserts that are not only easy to make but will also bring joy and flavor to your gatherings. Let’s dive in!

1. Lemon Raspberry Cheesecake Bars

These tangy and sweet lemon raspberry cheesecake bars are the perfect balance of creamy and fruity. The zesty lemon pairs beautifully with the tartness of fresh raspberries, while the graham cracker crust adds a delightful crunch. These bars are easy to prepare and require no baking, making them a hassle-free choice for spring gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
    • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar

  • For the cheesecake layer:

    • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
    • 1 cup sour cream
    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
    • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
    • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract

  • For the raspberry topping:

    • 1 cup fresh raspberries
    • 1 tablespoon honey

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar. Press this mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared pan to form the crust. Bake for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sour cream until smooth and creamy. Add in the powdered sugar, eggs, lemon zest, lemon juice, and vanilla extract, mixing until combined.
  4. Pour the cheesecake mixture over the cooled crust. Smooth out the top with a spatula and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the cheesecake is set around the edges but slightly jiggly in the center.
  5. Let the cheesecake cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow it to fully set.
  6. While the cheesecake is chilling, make the raspberry topping by gently heating the raspberries and honey in a small saucepan over low heat for about 5 minutes until the raspberries break down into a sauce.
  7. Once the cheesecake has chilled, spread the raspberry sauce over the top and cut into squares. Serve chilled.

2. Strawberry Shortcake Trifle

This classic dessert is a crowd-pleaser and an excellent way to showcase fresh, juicy strawberries. Layers of sponge cake, whipped cream, and sweetened berries come together to create a light yet indulgent treat that feels like spring in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 store-bought or homemade sponge cake, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and granulated sugar. Stir gently and set aside for about 30 minutes to allow the strawberries to release their natural juices.
  2. In a separate mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  3. To assemble the trifle, layer the sponge cake cubes, sweetened strawberries, and whipped cream in a large glass trifle dish. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used up.
  4. Chill the trifle in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving, so the flavors can meld together.
  5. Garnish with additional fresh strawberries on top and serve chilled.

3. Lemon Poppy Seed Cake with Glaze

Lemon poppy seed cake is a timeless spring dessert. The citrusy freshness of lemon combined with the nutty crunch of poppy seeds makes this cake a true delight. The glaze adds a sweet finish that enhances the cake’s tangy flavor. It’s perfect for brunches or afternoon tea.

Ingredients:

  • For the cake:

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
    • ½ teaspoon baking soda
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 cup buttermilk
    • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • For the glaze:

    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and poppy seeds. Set aside.
  3. In a separate large bowl, beat together the sugar and butter until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the buttermilk. Mix until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. While the cake is baking, make the glaze by whisking together the powdered sugar and lemon juice until smooth.
  7. Allow the cake to cool for 10 minutes in the pan before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, drizzle the glaze over the top of the cake.
  8. Slice and serve.

4. Peach Cobbler with Biscuit Topping

Peach cobbler is a quintessential summer dessert, and it can easily transition into spring with fresh, juicy peaches. This easy-to-make cobbler features a soft biscuit topping that pairs wonderfully with sweet, caramelized peaches.

Ingredients:

  • For the filling:

    • 4 cups fresh or frozen peaches, sliced
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
    • Pinch of salt

  • For the biscuit topping:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 teaspoons baking powder
    • ¼ cup granulated sugar
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, cold and cubed
    • ¾ cup whole milk

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, lemon juice, and salt. Toss gently to coat the peaches, then transfer the mixture into a 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt for the biscuit topping. Cut the cold butter into the dry ingredients using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Add the milk to the biscuit mixture and stir until just combined. Spoon dollops of the biscuit dough over the peach filling, covering most of the surface.
  4. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the biscuit topping is golden and the peach filling is bubbling around the edges.
  5. Let the cobbler cool slightly before serving. It’s perfect on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

5. Mint Chocolate Chip Ice Cream Cake

If you’re looking for a showstopper dessert for a spring celebration, this mint chocolate chip ice cream cake is the way to go. A cool, creamy dessert with layers of mint ice cream and rich chocolate cake is sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box chocolate cake mix (or homemade)
  • 1 ½ quarts mint chocolate chip ice cream, softened
  • 1 cup whipped cream
  • ½ cup chocolate chips (optional)

Directions:

  1. Bake the chocolate cake according to the package instructions and let it cool completely.
  2. Once the cake has cooled, cut it into two equal layers. Place the first layer at the bottom of a springform pan.
  3. Spread a layer of softened mint chocolate chip ice cream over the cake layer, then top with the second cake layer.
  4. Cover the top of the cake with more ice cream, smoothing it out with a spatula.
  5. Freeze the cake for 2-3 hours, or until the ice cream is firm.
  6. Before serving, top with whipped cream and sprinkle chocolate chips over the top if desired.

These easy spring desserts are sure to bring a smile to everyone’s face. Whether you’re craving a fruity treat, a creamy confection, or a refreshing ice cream dessert, these recipes capture the essence of spring with fresh flavors and vibrant colors. The best part is that they’re all simple to make, so you can spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the beautiful weather and great company. Happy baking!
