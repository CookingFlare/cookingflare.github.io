How to Make Homemade Syrian Kebab with Tomato Sauce


Kebab has long been a favorite dish across the Middle East, and Syria is no exception. Syrian kebab is a flavorful, juicy, and aromatic dish, where spiced ground meat is grilled or cooked on skewers. When paired with a rich tomato sauce, it becomes a satisfying meal that balances the savory flavors of the meat with the tanginess of the sauce. In this post, we’ll walk you through making homemade Syrian kebab with tomato sauce, an exquisite dish that will surely impress your family and guests.

Ingredients for Syrian Kebab

For the Kebab:

  • 500 grams of ground lamb (you can use beef if preferred)
  • 1 medium onion, finely grated or finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon allspice
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint, chopped (optional)
  • Wooden or metal skewers for grilling

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A pinch of sugar (optional, to balance the acidity)
  • Fresh herbs like parsley or basil for garnish

Preparing the Kebab Mixture

Step 1: Preparing the Meat

To make the perfect Syrian kebab, you’ll want to start by selecting high-quality ground lamb or beef. Ground lamb is the traditional choice, as it offers a richer flavor and a tender texture. However, you can substitute it with beef if desired.

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat with finely grated onion. The onion not only adds flavor but also helps keep the kebab juicy. To prevent the kebab mixture from being too runny, you can lightly press out any excess moisture from the grated onion using a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth.

Step 2: Adding the Spices

Now comes the fun part—adding the spices! Syrian kebab is known for its aromatic spice blend. Add cumin, coriander, cinnamon, allspice, black pepper, and salt to the meat mixture. These spices give the kebab its signature flavor profile. The cinnamon adds a slight sweetness, while the cumin and coriander create a warm, earthy depth.

Feel free to adjust the spice levels according to your personal preferences. If you enjoy a more intense flavor, you can increase the quantity of spices, especially cumin or allspice.

Step 3: Binding the Mixture

To help bind the meat together and create a smooth texture, add a tablespoon of olive oil. Olive oil helps maintain moisture during cooking and adds richness to the kebab. For added freshness, incorporate some finely chopped fresh parsley and mint. This not only brightens up the kebab’s flavor but also adds a slight herbal aroma that’s very characteristic of Middle Eastern cuisine.

Step 4: Shaping the Kebabs

Once the meat mixture is well-seasoned and bound, it’s time to shape the kebabs. The traditional way of shaping kebabs is by molding the meat onto skewers. Wet your hands slightly with water to prevent the meat from sticking. Take about 2 tablespoons of the kebab mixture and mold it around the skewer, ensuring it’s evenly spread along the length of the skewer.

For an even cook and an aesthetically pleasing result, shape the kebabs with a slight taper at both ends. If you don’t have skewers, you can shape the kebab mixture into patties and cook them on a grill pan or skillet.

Cooking the Kebab

Step 1: Preheating the Grill

For authentic flavor, grilling the kebabs over charcoal is the best option. The smoky aroma from the charcoal adds a wonderful depth to the dish. Preheat your grill or barbecue until the coals are glowing red, and the heat is medium-high.

If you’re using a gas grill, make sure it’s preheated to medium-high heat, or you can opt for a grill pan indoors for convenience.

Step 2: Grilling the Kebabs

Place the kebabs on the grill, making sure there is enough space between them so they cook evenly. Grill the kebabs for about 3-4 minutes per side, turning them occasionally. The kebabs should be browned and slightly charred on the outside, while remaining juicy on the inside.

If you’re using a grill pan, cook the kebabs for a similar amount of time over medium-high heat, turning them frequently to ensure an even sear on all sides.

Step 3: Resting the Kebabs

Once the kebabs are fully cooked, remove them from the grill and allow them to rest for a few minutes. Resting helps retain the juices within the kebab, ensuring each bite is tender and succulent.

Preparing the Tomato Sauce

While the kebabs are grilling, you can begin preparing the delicious tomato sauce to complement the kebabs. The richness of the tomato sauce balances the savory spices in the kebabs, making the dish even more irresistible.

Step 1: Sautéing the Aromatics

In a saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté the onions until they’re softened and translucent, which should take about 5 minutes. The garlic will release a fragrant aroma, indicating that it’s ready for the next step.

Step 2: Adding the Spices

To infuse the sauce with depth, add cumin, coriander, and paprika to the onions and garlic. Stir the spices to toast them lightly for about 30 seconds, allowing the flavors to bloom in the oil. This step enhances the overall taste of the sauce and adds a warm, earthy backdrop to the fresh tomato flavors.

Step 3: Cooking the Tomatoes

Next, add the finely chopped or pureed tomatoes to the pan. Stir everything together, and let the sauce simmer on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If you like a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender to puree it into a velvety consistency.

Step 4: Balancing the Sauce

Once the sauce has thickened slightly, taste it and adjust the seasoning as necessary. You may want to add a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity of the tomatoes, especially if they are particularly tangy. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and keep simmering the sauce for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to fully integrate.

Step 5: Garnishing the Sauce

Once the sauce is finished, sprinkle some freshly chopped parsley or basil over the top for a burst of freshness and color. This garnish not only adds a vibrant touch to the dish but also enhances the flavor with a bit of herbal brightness.

Serving the Syrian Kebab with Tomato Sauce

Once both the kebabs and the tomato sauce are ready, it’s time to plate your dish. Serve the kebabs on a large platter, and spoon the rich tomato sauce over the top or on the side as a dipping sauce. Garnish with additional parsley or mint for a lovely contrast in color.

Syrian kebabs are traditionally served with flatbreads like pita or lavash, which are perfect for soaking up the flavorful tomato sauce. You can also serve the kebabs with a side of rice pilaf or a fresh salad for a well-rounded meal.

This dish pairs beautifully with a glass of chilled yogurt drink or a fresh cucumber salad to complement the warm, savory flavors of the kebabs.

Tips for the Perfect Syrian Kebab

  • Use fatty meat: When making kebabs, it’s important to use a meat with some fat content, as this ensures the kebabs stay juicy. Ground lamb typically works best for this reason.
  • Don’t overwork the meat: When mixing the meat with the spices, don’t overwork the mixture, as it can make the kebabs tough. Mix gently until everything is just combined.
  • Soak the skewers: If you’re using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes before grilling to prevent them from burning.
  • Rest the kebabs: Allowing the kebabs to rest after cooking helps retain their juices, making them more flavorful and tender.
  • Customize the sauce: While tomato sauce is the classic choice, feel free to experiment with adding extra vegetables like bell peppers or eggplant to make the sauce richer and more complex.

Conclusion

Making homemade Syrian kebab with tomato sauce is a rewarding experience that brings together bold flavors and textures. The combination of aromatic spices, juicy grilled meat, and rich tomato sauce creates a dish that’s both comforting and satisfying. Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion or a casual family meal, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire.
