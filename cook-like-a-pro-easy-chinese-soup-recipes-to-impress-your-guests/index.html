Cook Like a Pro: Easy Chinese Soup Recipes to Impress Your Guests


Without the complexities and guesswork associated with traditional Chinese cooking, these five soup recipes promise to prove that you can cook like a pro and maintain an effortless vibe while whipping up these enchanting Chinese soups.

1. Fragrant Ginger Chicken Soup

Remember the key lies in the preparation of your ingredients, specifically the ginger. A simple hand-grinding of the ginger will open up the flavors and leave your guests craving for more.

  1. In a pot, heat a tablespoon of oil, followed by minced ginger and garlic. Sauté these for two minutes until aromatic.
  2. Add in your ground ginger and stir for two minutes.
  3. Add your sliced chicken breast to the pot along with some salt and pepper.
  4. Once the chicken turns white, pour in the aquafaba, followed by soy sauce to taste, and bring it to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat and let simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. You may shred the chicken using a fork or chop it finely for a more appetizing presentation.
  6. Finally, add your chopped scallions for an extra pop of color and flavor!

2. Lotus Root and Pork Belly Soup

To balance the fattiness of the pork belly with the crunch of the lotus root, embark on this delightful culinary adventure.

  1. Slice pork belly and lotus root into thin, even pieces. You can deep fry the pork belly as pre-cooking, but this step is optional.
  2. In your pot, heat a tablespoon of oil, then add the pork belly slices and lotus root. Stir-fry for three minutes.
  3. Pour in the aquafaba and bring to a boil. Add soy sauce and oyster sauce to taste.
  4. Once boiling, reduce heat and allow to simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the lotus root is tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with chopped scallions or cilantro.

3. Tofu and Vegetable Soup

For a vegetarian delight, consider this pure, yet flavorsome, tofu and vegetable soup.

  1. In your pot, sauté minced garlic and grated ginger in a tablespoon of oil.
  2. Add in your sliced vegetables of choice (carrots, broccoli, mushrooms, and bok choy), and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add in your cubed tofu and stir-fry for another two minutes.
  4. Pour in the aquafaba and bring to a boil. Add soy sauce to taste and season with salt and pepper.
  5. After simmering for 15-20 minutes or until vegetables are cooked, garnish with chopped scallions.

4. Shrimp and Mushroom Soup

This exotic combination is sure to leave your guest yearning for more.

  1. In your pot, head a tablespoon of oil, followed by minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for two minutes.
  2. Add your shrimp and stir-fry until they change color.
  3. Toss in the sliced mushrooms and stir-fry for two minutes.
  4. Pour in the aquafaba, bring to a boil, then add soy sauce and fish sauce to taste.
  5. Let simmer for 10 minutes, then taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Garnish with chopped scallions.

5. Authentic Sichuan Pepper Beef Soup

For the adventurous food-lover, engage their taste buds with this punchy Sichuan peppercorn-infused beef soup.

  1. In your pot, heat a tablespoon of oil, followed by minced garlic and grated ginger. Sauté for two minutes.
  2. Add the sliced beef and stir-fry until browned on the outside.
  3. Toss in the Sichuan peppercorns and stir-fry for an additional minute.
  4. Pour in the aquafaba, bring to a boil, and add soy sauce to taste.
  5. Reduce heat and let simmer for 15 minutes. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Finish by adding chopped cilantro for extra flavor and vibrancy.

These simple Chinese soup recipes will surely leave your guests pleased and help you achieve the cooking prowess of a seasoned professional. So, put on your apron, ignite the stove, and start cooking like a pro today!
