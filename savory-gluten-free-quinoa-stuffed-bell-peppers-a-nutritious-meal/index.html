Savory Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers: A Nutritious Meal


Are you on the lookout for a delicious and nutritious meal that is also gluten-free? Look no further! These savory gluten-free quinoa stuffed bell peppers are the perfect combination of flavors and textures that will leave your taste buds satisfied and your body nourished. Packed with wholesome ingredients, this dish is not only delicious but also a great option for those following a gluten-free lifestyle. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1 zucchini, grated
  • 1 cup mushrooms, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place the bell peppers in a baking dish, standing upright.

  3. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness. In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  4. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant.

  5. Add the grated carrot, zucchini, and chopped mushrooms to the skillet. Cook for about 5 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

  6. Stir in the cooked quinoa, dried oregano, dried basil, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all the flavors.

  7. Spoon the quinoa mixture into the hollowed bell peppers, packing it tightly.

  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30-35 minutes or until the bell peppers are tender and slightly charred.

  9. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5 minutes to allow the tops to brown slightly.

  10. Remove from the oven and let the stuffed bell peppers cool for a few minutes.

  11. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables and spices to suit your taste preferences. You can add corn, black beans, or even chopped spinach to the quinoa stuffing for added flavor and nutritional benefits.

  • If you prefer a cheesy twist, sprinkle some grated cheese on top of the stuffed bell peppers before baking for a melty, gooey finish.

  • These stuffed bell peppers can be enjoyed as a main course or served as a side dish alongside grilled chicken or fish.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. They can be reheated in the microwave or oven for a quick and easy meal.

Final Thoughts

These savory gluten-free quinoa stuffed bell peppers are not only visually appealing but also incredibly delicious and nutritious. The combination of quinoa, vegetables, and spices creates a well-balanced and satisfying meal that will leave you feeling nourished and satisfied. Whether you’re following a gluten-free diet or simply looking to incorporate more wholesome ingredients into your meals, this recipe is a winner. So go ahead and give it a try – your taste buds and body will thank you!
