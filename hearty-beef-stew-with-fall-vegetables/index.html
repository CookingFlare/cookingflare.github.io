Hearty Beef Stew with Fall Vegetables


When the weather starts to cool and the leaves begin to change, it’s the perfect time to cozy up with a warm, hearty bowl of beef stew. This classic dish is a comforting and satisfying meal that brings together the rich flavors of tender beef and the earthy goodness of fall vegetables. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a delicious and nourishing beef stew that will warm your soul and fill your home with irresistible aromas.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 4 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 parsnips, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 cups butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup pearl onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Beef: In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes in batches and brown them on all sides. This step is crucial for developing rich flavor in the stew.

  2. Saute the Aromatics: Once the beef is browned, add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot. Saute until the onions are translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  3. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the beef broth and red wine, if using, and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. This will infuse the stew with deep, savory flavors.

  4. Add the Herbs and Vegetables: Stir in the bay leaves, dried thyme, and dried rosemary. Then, add the carrots, parsnips, butternut squash, and pearl onions to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Simmer to Perfection: Bring the stew to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the stew simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are soft and flavorful.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stew is ready, discard the bay leaves and ladle it into bowls. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and freshness. Pair it with crusty bread or fluffy mashed potatoes for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips for Success:

  • For the most flavorful stew, take the time to properly brown the beef before adding the other ingredients.
  • Don’t rush the simmering process – slow and low is the key to achieving melt-in-your-mouth beef and perfectly cooked vegetables.
  • This stew tastes even better the next day, so consider making it ahead of time for a convenient and delicious meal prep option.

Final Thoughts

Hearty beef stew with fall vegetables is a timeless dish that brings warmth and comfort to any table. With its rich, savory flavors and nourishing ingredients, it’s the perfect way to embrace the changing seasons and indulge in a satisfying home-cooked meal. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aroma of this delicious stew fill your kitchen with the promise of a heartwarming dining experience.
