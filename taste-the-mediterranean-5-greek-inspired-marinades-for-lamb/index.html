Taste the Mediterranean: 5 Greek-Inspired Marinades for Lamb


The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its emphasis on fresh vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. One of the most iconic and flavorful proteins in this culinary tradition is lamb, which is often marinated in a blend of herbs, spices, and citrus juice to enhance its natural flavor. In this post, we’ll explore five Greek-inspired marinades for lamb that will transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed hills of Greece.

Marinade 1: Classic Greek-Style

This marinade is a timeless favorite, combining the brightness of lemon juice with the depth of olive oil and the pungency of garlic.

  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and whisk until smooth. Place the lamb in a large zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 2: Spicy Feta and Mint

This marinade adds a bold and refreshing twist to the classic Greek-style, with the tang of feta cheese and the cooling effect of mint.

  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1-2 teaspoons red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat)

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and whisk until smooth. Place the lamb in a large zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 3: Cumin and Coriander

This marinade takes inspiration from the rich and aromatic spices of the Middle East, with the warm and earthy flavors of cumin and coriander.

  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and whisk until smooth. Place the lamb in a large zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 4: Lemon and Herb

This marinade is a bright and zesty celebration of the Mediterranean’s love affair with lemon and herbs.

  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and whisk until smooth. Place the lamb in a large zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 5: Ouzo and Capers

This marinade is a nod to the bold and savory flavors of Greece’s national spirit, ouzo, paired with the salty tang of capers.

  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons ouzo
  • 2 tablespoons chopped capers
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Combine all ingredients in a bowl and whisk until smooth. Place the lamb in a large zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over it. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a culinary novice, these five Greek-inspired marinades for lamb are sure to elevate your cooking game and transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed hills of Greece. So go ahead, get creative, and taste the Mediterranean!
