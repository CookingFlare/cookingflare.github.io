Ricotta and Spinach Stuffed Shells: A Vegetarian Delight


If you’re in the mood for a comforting and satisfying meal, look no further than ricotta and spinach stuffed shells. This vegetarian dish combines the creamy goodness of ricotta cheese with the nutrient-packed benefits of spinach, all wrapped in tender pasta shells and smothered in a rich marinara sauce. Whether you’re planning a family dinner, a cozy date night, or just want to treat yourself, this recipe is sure to please. Let’s dive into the details of creating this delightful dish!

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Shells

  • 20 jumbo pasta shells
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese (plus extra for topping)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups fresh spinach (or 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional, but recommended)

For the Marinara Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

  1. Sauté the Aromatics
    In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, ensuring it doesn’t burn.

  2. Add the Tomatoes
    Pour in the crushed tomatoes, followed by the oregano, basil, salt, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if you’re using them. Stir well to combine. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for 15-20 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld beautifully.

Step 2: Cook the Pasta Shells

While the marinara sauce is simmering, cook the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions. Aim for al dente, as they will continue to cook in the oven later. Once cooked, drain the shells and set them aside to cool slightly.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

  1. Sauté the Spinach
    If using fresh spinach, sauté it in a pan with a splash of olive oil until wilted, about 3-4 minutes. If using frozen spinach, ensure it’s well-drained and chopped.

  2. Mix the Cheeses
    In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, sautéed spinach, garlic powder, salt, black pepper, and nutmeg. Mix until all the ingredients are well incorporated.

Step 4: Assemble the Stuffed Shells

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Stuff the Shells
    Spread about 1 cup of marinara sauce evenly in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Take each cooked pasta shell and fill it generously with the ricotta and spinach mixture. Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish, open side up.

  3. Top with Sauce and Cheese
    Once all the shells are filled and arranged in the dish, pour the remaining marinara sauce over the top. Sprinkle additional mozzarella cheese on top for that extra cheesy goodness.

Step 5: Bake the Dish

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. After that, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once baked, allow the dish to cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley for a pop of color and added flavor. Serve your ricotta and spinach stuffed shells with a side salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.

Tips for Perfection

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling and stuff the shells a day in advance. Just cover the assembled dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate. When you’re ready to bake, remove the plastic wrap and bake as instructed, adding a few extra minutes if needed.

  • Variations: Feel free to mix it up! Add sautéed mushrooms, roasted red peppers, or even a layer of sautéed zucchini for extra veggies. You can also swap out the cheeses for your favorites.

  • Freezing: This dish freezes beautifully. Assemble the stuffed shells but do not bake them. Cover tightly with foil and freeze for up to three months. When you’re ready to enjoy, thaw in the fridge overnight and bake as usual.

Nutritional Benefits

Ricotta and spinach stuffed shells not only taste delicious but they also offer a variety of nutritional benefits.

  • Spinach: Packed with vitamins A, C, and K, spinach is a powerhouse of antioxidants and helps support overall health.
  • Ricotta: This cheese is lower in fat compared to others and is a good source of protein, calcium, and riboflavin.
  • Whole Grains: If you opt for whole wheat pasta shells, you’ll add an extra boost of fiber to your meal.

Conclusion

Ricotta and spinach stuffed shells are a delightful and comforting vegetarian dish that can be enjoyed by everyone, regardless of dietary preferences. The combination of creamy cheese, savory marinara, and tender pasta creates a meal that feels indulgent yet wholesome. The best part? It’s easy to prepare and can be customized to suit your taste. So the next time you’re in search of a satisfying dish, give these stuffed shells a try. They’re bound to become a favorite in your household!

Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just for yourself, this recipe is sure to impress. Dig in and enjoy the delicious flavors of ricotta and spinach stuffed shells, a true vegetarian delight!
