Healthy and Delicious Thanksgiving Soups and Stews


Thanksgiving is a time of gratitude, gathering, and of course, indulgent feasts. While many look forward to roasted turkeys, velvety mashed potatoes, and sweet cranberry sauces, adding warm and hearty soups and stews to the menu can elevate the meal to a new level. Not only are these dishes comfort foods that embrace the spirit of the season, but they can also provide a lighter, healthier counterbalance to the rich main courses and desserts typically served.

Soups and stews are versatile, easy to prepare ahead of time, and can be customized to suit a variety of dietary preferences. Whether you’re looking for a plant-based option, a fiber-packed starter, or even a main dish that celebrates the season’s flavors, there’s a recipe to suit your needs. Below, you’ll find a collection of Thanksgiving soups and stews that are both nutritious and undeniably delicious.

Butternut Squash and Apple Soup

This velvety butternut squash and apple soup is the epitome of fall in a bowl. The natural sweetness of squash combined with the tartness of apples creates a beautifully balanced flavor, while warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg offer a hint of holiday cheer.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 2 medium apples (Granny Smith works well), peeled, cored, and chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or avocado oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Coconut milk (optional, for added creaminess)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add the diced butternut squash and apples to the pot. Saute for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour in vegetable broth and season with cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20–25 minutes or until the squash and apples are tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer to a blender in batches.
  5. For extra creaminess, stir in coconut milk before serving.

This soup can be paired with a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds or crispy croutons for a satisfying crunch.

Hearty Turkey and Vegetable Stew

Transform leftover Thanksgiving turkey into a hearty, nutrient-packed stew that’s perfect for the holiday weekend. Loaded with seasonal vegetables and aromatic herbs, this dish is as wholesome as it is filling.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded or cubed
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 medium parsnip, diced
  • 1 large potato, cubed
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 6 cups turkey or chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Stir in carrots, celery, parsnip, and potato. Cook for about 5 minutes to allow the vegetables to soften slightly.
  3. Pour in turkey broth and sprinkle in thyme and rosemary. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 20 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the cooked turkey and cook for an additional 5 minutes to heat through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, then serve with fresh parsley or a crusty whole-grain bread for dipping.

Creamy Cauliflower and Leek Soup (Dairy-Free)

This creamy cauliflower and leek soup is a lighter alternative to traditional cream-based dishes. Cauliflower gives the soup its smooth texture, while leeks add subtle sweetness.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 large leek, cleaned and sliced (white and light green parts only)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any dairy substitute)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Paprika or smoked paprika (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add leeks and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the cauliflower florets and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 15–20 minutes until the cauliflower is soft.
  3. Puree the soup with an immersion blender or in a standard blender until smooth.
  4. Stir in almond milk for creaminess and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  5. Garnish with a sprinkle of paprika for a touch of color and a hint of smokiness.

This soup works wonderfully as a light appetizer or a comforting side dish to your main Thanksgiving meal.

Lentil and Sweet Potato Stew

For a plant-based, protein-packed option, this lentil and sweet potato stew delivers on both nutrition and flavor. Lentils bring earthy richness, while sweet potatoes provide a hint of natural sweetness.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups kale or spinach, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, carrots, and spices, cooking until the vegetables are softened and fragrant.
  2. Stir in the lentils, sweet potatoes, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  3. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer for 25–30 minutes, or until the lentils and sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Stir in the chopped kale or spinach and let it wilt for 2–3 minutes.
  5. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper, then serve topped with fresh parsley or cilantro.

This stew makes for a hearty, satisfying dish that vegetarians and meat-eaters alike will enjoy.

Golden Turmeric Pumpkin Soup

Celebrate the season’s favorite ingredient—pumpkin—with this anti-inflammatory golden turmeric pumpkin soup. It’s a showstopper with its vibrant color and warming spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) pure pumpkin puree
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Stir in turmeric and cinnamon, allowing the spices to bloom for 1 minute.
  3. Add pumpkin puree and vegetable broth. Blend well, then bring to a simmer.
  4. Stir in coconut milk and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve warm with a garnish of toasted pepitas or a swirl of extra coconut milk.

Adding soups and stews to your Thanksgiving menu not only complements the holiday meal but also provides an opportunity to use seasonal produce in a way that’s both healthy and comforting. These recipes are perfect for entertaining or for enjoying as leftovers throughout the weekend. Warm, nourishing, and filled with fall goodness, they’ll surely become Thanksgiving staples.
