Discover the Spice of Life: Ethnic Breakfast Dishes for Brunch


Brunch, the delightful marriage of breakfast and lunch, has long been a favorite among food enthusiasts. But why settle for the same ol’ boring breakfast dishes when you can embark on a flavorful journey around the world? Embrace the uniqueness of ethnic breakfast dishes and blend their delectable flavors into your next brunch gathering.

Let’s embark on this culinary adventure, shall we?

Moroccan B’Stilla

Whisk your taste buds off to Morocco with this scrumptious and slightly spicy B’Stilla, a traditional pastry filled with an aromatic blend of sautéed onions, eggs, and delicate spices.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup almond flakes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground saffron threads
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon freshly grated orange zest
  • ½ cup finely chopped fried onions
  • 4 dried apricots, soaked and chopped
  • ¼ cup chopped dates
  • 1 small egg, beaten
  • ¼ cup melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Mix the flour, eggs, almond flakes, spices, sugar, and orange zest in a large bowl.
  2. Add the chopped onions, apricots, and dates. Mix well to combine.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a non-stick frying pan. Drop spoonfuls of the mixture into the pan and fry until golden-brown, about 3 minutes per side.
  4. Dip each crêpe into the beaten egg, coat with the butter, and arrange in a circular pattern in a buttered dish.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 20 minutes until crisp.

Japanese Shakshuka

Feel the cultural fusion of Japanese and Middle Eastern cuisines in this scrumptious Shakshuka. Poaching eggs in a rich tomato sauce topped with savory bonito flakes and the essence of Japan.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • ½ medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons sake
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 (14-ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon chopped scallions
  • 1 tablespoon bonito flakes

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Stir in sake, soy sauce, sugar, and ginger. Mix in tomatoes, salt, and cumin.
  3. Add chickpeas and simmer until the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded.
  4. Create 2-3 wells in the sauce and crack the eggs into each well.
  5. Cook until the whites are set, approximately 3-4 minutes.
  6. Top with chopped scallions and bonito flakes before serving.

Açai Bowl - Brazilian Brunch

Take a samba dance break into the vibrant streets of Rio with this refreshing Açai Bowl. A traditional Brazilian frozen fruit mix scooped into a bowl and topped with an assortment of healthier ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup frozen açai fruit puree
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ cup sliced strawberries
  • ¼ cup diced pineapple
  • ¼ cup diced kiwi
  • ¼ cup blueberries
  • 1 tablespoon chopped hazelnuts

Instructions:

  1. Combine açai puree, honey, and cinnamon in a food processor. Blend until creamy and smooth.
  2. Scoop the mixture into bowls, making a depression in the center of each.
  3. Fill the bowls with sliced strawberries, pineapple, kiwi, and blueberries.
  4. Top with chopped hazelnuts before serving.

Feeling adventurous in the kitchen? Embrace these enticing ethnic breakfast dishes and awaken your senses. Rest assured, your taste buds will thank you! Now go ahead and plan your next cultural brunch experience. Don’t forget the spices!
