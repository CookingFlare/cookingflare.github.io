Vegan Spanish Recipes: Plant-Based Takes on Classic Dishes


When it comes to Spanish cuisine, the vibrant flavors and rich traditions have captivated food lovers around the world. From the famous paella to the beloved tapas, Spanish dishes offer a culinary journey that is both satisfying and indulgent. However, as the plant-based movement gains momentum, many are exploring how to adapt these classic recipes into vegan versions without sacrificing taste or authenticity. In this post, we’ll delve into a selection of delightful vegan Spanish recipes that bring a plant-based twist to traditional favorites. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these recipes are sure to excite your taste buds and inspire your cooking adventures.

1. Vegan Paella

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice (or any short-grain rice)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads (optional but recommended)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. In a large paella pan or skillet, heat a generous drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until they soften and become fragrant.
  2. Stir in the diced red and yellow bell peppers, cooking for an additional 5 minutes until they begin to soften.
  3. Add the arborio rice to the pan, stirring to coat the rice with the oil and vegetables. Cook for about 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, smoked paprika, saffron, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
  5. Add the green beans and peas, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pan with a lid or aluminum foil and let it cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and has absorbed the liquid.
  6. Once cooked, remove from heat and let it sit for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges for a zesty finish.

2. Vegan Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup chickpea flour
  • 1 1/4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced potatoes and onion, seasoning with salt and pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and lightly golden.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together chickpea flour, water, turmeric, salt, and pepper until smooth. This mixture will serve as the egg substitute.
  3. Once the potatoes and onions are cooked, drain any excess oil and transfer them to the bowl with the chickpea mixture. Stir to combine.
  4. Wipe the skillet clean and add a bit more olive oil. Pour the potato mixture back into the skillet, spreading it evenly.
  5. Cook over medium heat for about 5-7 minutes, then carefully flip using a plate or lid and cook the other side for another 5-7 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Slice into wedges and serve warm or at room temperature.

3. Vegan Gazpacho

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and diced
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, and garlic.
  2. Add the vegetable broth, olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  3. Adjust the seasoning to taste and blend again if necessary. For a chunkier texture, blend for a shorter time.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours to let the flavors meld together.
  5. Serve chilled, garnished with fresh basil or parsley.

4. Vegan Churros with Chocolate Sauce

Ingredients for Churros:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil (melted)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Cinnamon sugar (for coating: mix sugar with cinnamon to taste)

Ingredients for Chocolate Sauce:

  • 1 cup dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, coconut oil, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil and then remove from heat.
  2. Stir in the flour until a dough forms. Let it cool for a few minutes, then add the vanilla extract.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan. Prepare a piping bag with a star nozzle and fill it with the churro dough.
  4. Pipe strips of dough into the hot oil, cutting with scissors to form desired lengths. Fry until golden brown, then drain on paper towels.
  5. Roll the churros in cinnamon sugar while still warm.
  6. For the chocolate sauce, heat coconut milk in a saucepan, then remove from heat and stir in chocolate chips until melted and smooth.
  7. Serve churros warm with chocolate sauce for dipping.

5. Vegan Pisto (Spanish Ratatouille)

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), chopped
  • 2 zucchinis, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  2. Add the bell pepper and eggplant, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the zucchini and continue to cook for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Adjust seasoning as needed and garnish with fresh herbs before serving.

Conclusion

Exploring vegan Spanish recipes is a delightful way to enjoy the rich culinary heritage of Spain while embracing a plant-based lifestyle. Each dish captures the essence of traditional flavors, making them accessible to everyone, regardless of dietary preferences. From hearty paellas to sweet churros, these plant-based twists on classic dishes prove that you can enjoy the vibrant tastes of Spain without compromising your values.

As you embark on your culinary journey, don’t hesitate to experiment with these recipes. Whether you choose to serve them at a gathering or enjoy them solo, these vegan Spanish recipes will not only satisfy your hunger but also offer a glimpse into the heart of Spain’s gastronomic culture. So gather your ingredients, turn up the music, and get cooking! ¡Buen provecho!
