Seafood Jambalaya Recipe with Shrimp, Andouille Sausage, and Rice


Jambalaya is a classic Creole dish that originated in Louisiana. It’s a one-pot wonder that combines the flavors of the Holy Trinity (onions, bell peppers, and celery) with a variety of proteins and spices. In this recipe, we’ll be focusing on a seafood jambalaya that features succulent shrimp, smoky andouille sausage, and fluffy rice. Get ready to embark on a flavor-packed culinary journey!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 stalks of celery, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the andouille sausage and cook until browned and crispy. Remove the sausage from the pot and set it aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Return the andouille sausage to the pot and add the diced tomatoes (with their juice), chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, dried thyme, dried oregano, and bay leaf. Stir well to combine all the flavors.

  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Add the rice to the pot and stir until it’s well coated with the sauce. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and has absorbed all the liquid.

  6. While the rice is cooking, season the shrimp with salt and pepper. In a separate pan, sauté the shrimp over medium heat until they are pink and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side. Be careful not to overcook them, as they can become tough and rubbery.

  7. Once the rice is done, remove the bay leaf from the pot and gently fold in the cooked shrimp. Allow the jambalaya to sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  8. Serve the seafood jambalaya hot, garnished with fresh parsley. It’s a complete meal on its own, but you can also accompany it with a side of crusty bread or a simple green salad.

Final Thoughts

This Seafood Jambalaya Recipe with Shrimp, Andouille Sausage, and Rice is a delightful blend of flavors and textures. The combination of succulent shrimp, smoky andouille sausage, and aromatic vegetables creates a symphony of tastes that will transport you straight to the vibrant streets of New Orleans.

Feel free to adjust the spices according to your taste preferences. If you like it spicier, add a dash of cayenne pepper or a few drops of hot sauce. If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of Cajun seasoning.

So, gather your ingredients, prepare your taste buds for an explosion of flavors, and enjoy this hearty and satisfying Seafood Jambalaya Recipe with Shrimp, Andouille Sausage, and Rice. It’s a dish that will surely impress your family and friends. Bon appétit!
