Middle Eastern Breakfast: How to Make Shakshuka


Shakshuka is a staple of Middle Eastern breakfasts, celebrated not only for its bold flavors but also for its simplicity and versatility. Whether you’re gathering your loved ones for an indulgent brunch or preparing a quick yet satisfying meal on a busy weekday morning, this dish will deliver comfort and nourishment with every bite. Perfectly poached eggs nestled in a spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce make Shakshuka a dish that’s packed with flavor and character. Let’s dive into how you can prepare this Middle Eastern classic right at home.

What is Shakshuka?

Shakshuka is a rustic dish believed to have origins in North Africa and the Middle East. It is built around a base of ripe tomatoes, onions, bell peppers, garlic, and warm, aromatic spices. Eggs are added to the mixture, cooked until the whites are set but the yolks remain delightfully runny. While traditionally prepared with minimal ingredients, Shakshuka welcomes variation, making it adaptable to dietary preferences or the ingredients you have on hand.

This hearty, one-pan breakfast is fantastic as the star of a weekend spread or as a quick, wholesome meal enjoyed with a crusty loaf of bread, pita, or naan. Its flavors are earthy, warming, and just the right mix of spicy and savory, making it a universal crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients for Shakshuka

To prepare the perfect Shakshuka, you’ll need simple, pantry-friendly ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil – for sautéing and enriching the flavor of the dish.
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped – adds sweetness and depth.
  • 1 bell pepper, diced – green, red, or yellow for a perfect texture.
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced – provides the robust, savory undertone.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin – a quintessential Middle Eastern spice.
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika – for a hint of smokiness.
  • ½ teaspoon chili flakes (optional) – for a mildly spicy kick.
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes or 6 fresh tomatoes, chopped – the primary base for the sauce.
  • Salt and pepper, to taste – for seasoning.

To finish the dish:

  • 4-6 eggs – free-range eggs work best for their rich flavor.
  • A handful of fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped – for garnish.
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional) – enhances the creaminess of the dish.
  • Bread of choice – warm pita or crusty sourdough are ideal accompaniments.

Step-by-Step Guide to Make Shakshuka

1. Prepare Your Vegetables

Begin by finely chopping your onion, bell pepper, and garlic. A consistent dice ensures even cooking and a great flavor profile. If you’re using fresh tomatoes instead of canned, peel and dice them ahead of time.

2. Heat the Pan

Select a large, deep skillet or a cast-iron pan for optimal cooking. Heat the olive oil over medium heat, allowing it to shimmer before adding your onion and bell pepper. Stir them gently as they sauté, softening beautifully and releasing their natural sweetness.

3. Build the Flavor Base

Once the onions and bell peppers are softened, add the minced garlic. Stir frequently to prevent burning, as garlic cooks quickly. Follow with the ground cumin, smoked paprika, and chili flakes (if you’re aiming for a little heat). These aromatic spices elevate the sauce, giving it that distinct Middle Eastern flair.

4. Add the Tomatoes

Pour the diced tomatoes into the pan, stirring to combine with the sautéed vegetables and spices. If using fresh tomatoes, allow them to simmer until they break down into a sauce-like consistency. Lower the heat and let the mixture cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. During this time, the flavors will meld beautifully, creating a rich and hearty base. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

5. Create Nesting Spots for Eggs

Using a spoon, make small wells in the thick tomato sauce, leaving enough space for each egg. The depth of the wells ensures that the eggs stay nestled and cook evenly.

6. Add the Eggs

Crack one egg into each well, taking care not to break the yolks. If you’re worried about any stray shell fragments, crack the eggs into a separate bowl first, then gently pour them into the sauce.

7. Cover and Cook

Cover the pan and allow the eggs to cook gently. Depending on your preference for the yolk’s doneness, cook the eggs for 5–8 minutes. For a soft and runny yolk, aim for the shorter side of this range; for firmer yolks, cook slightly longer.

8. Finish the Dish

When the eggs are cooked to your liking, remove the pan from the heat. Sprinkle chopped parsley or cilantro over the top for a burst of fresh flavor and color. If you enjoy cheese, crumble some feta on top—it perfectly complements the acidic tomato base and creamy eggs.

How to Serve Shakshuka

Shakshuka is best enjoyed straight from the pan, making it the ultimate communal dish. Place the skillet at the center of the table and serve it with slices of warm pita bread, crusty sourdough, or naan on the side. It’s perfect for dipping into the rich tomato sauce and scooping up the creamy eggs.

Pair your Shakshuka with a side of fresh greens, hummus, or a simple cucumber and yogurt salad to round out your breakfast. Some prefer a bold cup of Turkish coffee or a glass of fresh pomegranate juice to balance the richness of the dish.

Variations to Explore

Shakshuka is endlessly versatile, and there are numerous ways to customize it to suit your taste. Here are a few ideas:

  • Add more vegetables: Incorporate spinach, kale, or zucchini for extra nutrients.
  • Spice it up: Use harissa paste for an extra kick of heat and complexity.
  • Include protein: Add crumbled spicy sausage, ground lamb, or chickpeas for a heartier meal.
  • Go green: Swap the tomato base for a green spinach and herb sauce for a unique twist.

The flexibility of Shakshuka ensures it remains a favorite dish in households worldwide.

Tips for the Perfect Shakshuka

  1. The pan matters: A cast-iron skillet retains heat beautifully and cooks the dish evenly.
  2. Fresh ingredients are key: Plump tomatoes and fresh herbs enhance the flavor considerably.
  3. Don’t rush: Allow the sauce to cook slowly to ensure the spices fully infuse.
  4. Cover the pan effectively: To cook the eggs evenly, ensure the lid fits snugly on the pan.
  5. Reheating tip: Consider warming leftovers gently on the stovetop rather than microwaving, as the eggs and sauce retain their best texture in the skillet.

Why Shakshuka Should Be on Your Table

Shakshuka encapsulates all the elements of a comforting homemade meal—simple ingredients, vivid flavors, and a cooking process that’s as enjoyable as the end result. It’s a dish steeped in history, bringing friends and families together to share in its warmth and vibrancy.

Make your mornings special with a freshly prepared Shakshuka, and don’t hesitate to add your personal twist to the recipe. There’s nothing like starting the day with such a flavorful and nourishing dish that’s tied to rich cultural traditions.

So grab your skillet and a few fresh ingredients, and turn your kitchen into a hub of Middle Eastern magic.
