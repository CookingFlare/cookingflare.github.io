Easy Easter Brunch Frittatas: Simple and Delicious Egg Dishes for Your Table


Easter is a time for family, friends, and delicious food. If you’re looking for a simple and tasty dish to serve at your Easter brunch, look no further than these easy frittatas. Frittatas are a versatile dish that can be customized to suit your taste and preferences. They are also a great way to use up leftover ingredients. In this blog post, we’ll explore some simple and delicious frittata recipes that are perfect for your Easter table.

Classic Ham and Cheese Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup diced ham
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9-inch pie dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Stir in the diced ham and shredded cheddar cheese.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the greased pie dish.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the frittata is set and golden brown on top.
  6. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

Veggie Delight Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup baby spinach
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9-inch pie dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Stir in the diced bell peppers, onion, mushrooms, and baby spinach.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the greased pie dish.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the frittata is set and lightly browned.
  6. Remove from the oven and sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese on top.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  8. Serve warm.

Smoked Salmon and Dill Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup smoked salmon, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9-inch pie dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Stir in the chopped smoked salmon, fresh dill, and diced red onion.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the greased pie dish.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the frittata is set and lightly golden on top.
  6. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  7. Serve with lemon wedges for a burst of freshness.

Conclusion

These easy Easter brunch frittatas are simple to make and bursting with flavors. Whether you prefer classic ham and cheese, a veggie-packed delight, or a tangy smoked salmon and dill frittata, there’s something for everyone at your table. Serve these delicious egg dishes alongside a fresh salad or some crusty bread, and you have a complete Easter brunch menu that will impress your guests. So, this Easter, try out these easy frittata recipes and make your brunch extra special. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
