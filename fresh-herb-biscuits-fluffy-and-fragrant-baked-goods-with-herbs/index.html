Fresh Herb Biscuits: Fluffy and Fragrant Baked Goods with Herbs


If you’re a fan of homemade biscuits, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to share with you a recipe for fresh herb biscuits that are not only fluffy and flavorful but also incredibly fragrant. These biscuits are the perfect addition to any meal, whether it’s a hearty breakfast, a comforting brunch, or a delicious side dish for dinner. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make these delightful fresh herb biscuits:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, dried thyme, dried rosemary, dried oregano, and dried basil. Make sure all the ingredients are well combined.

  3. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry mixture. Using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial for achieving a light and flaky texture in your biscuits.

  4. Make a well in the center of the mixture and pour in the buttermilk. Gently stir the mixture until the dough starts to come together. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough biscuits.

  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently a few times until it forms a cohesive ball. Again, be mindful not to overwork the dough.

  6. Roll out the dough to about 1/2-inch (1.25 cm) thickness. Using a biscuit cutter or a round cookie cutter, cut out biscuits from the dough. Place the biscuits onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a small gap between each one.

  7. Bake the biscuits in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top. Keep an eye on them, as baking times may vary depending on your oven.

  8. Once the biscuits are done, remove them from the oven and transfer them to a wire rack to cool slightly. Serve them warm and enjoy the fragrant aroma of the herbs.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different fresh herbs instead of using dried ones. Chopped fresh thyme, rosemary, oregano, and basil will add an extra burst of flavor to your biscuits.

  • For a cheesy twist, sprinkle some grated Parmesan or cheddar cheese on top of the biscuits before baking. The cheese will melt and create a deliciously crispy crust.

  • If you don’t have buttermilk, you can make your own by mixing 3/4 cup of regular milk with 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar. Let the mixture sit for 5 minutes before using it in the recipe.

  • These fresh herb biscuits are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven. However, you can store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. To reheat, simply warm them in a toaster oven or microwave.

Final Thoughts

Fresh herb biscuits are a delightful way to elevate your baking game and add a touch of freshness and fragrance to your favorite bread. With their fluffy texture and aromatic herbs, these biscuits are sure to impress your family and friends. So, why wait? Gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in the irresistible goodness of fresh herb biscuits. Happy baking!

Please note that the baking times and temperatures mentioned in this recipe may vary depending on your specific oven. Always keep a close eye on your baked goods to ensure they are cooked to perfection.
