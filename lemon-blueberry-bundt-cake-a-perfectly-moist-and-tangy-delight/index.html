Lemon Blueberry Bundt Cake: A Perfectly Moist and Tangy Delight

3 min · 620 words

If you’re looking for a delightful dessert that perfectly balances tangy and sweet flavors, then you’re in for a treat with this Lemon Blueberry Bundt Cake recipe. This cake is not only visually stunning but also incredibly moist and bursting with the flavors of fresh lemon and juicy blueberries. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, celebrating a special occasion, or simply craving a delicious dessert, this Lemon Blueberry Bundt Cake is sure to impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 cups fresh blueberries, tossed in 1 tablespoon of flour

For the Glaze:

  • 1 1/2 cups confectioners’ sugar
  • 3-4 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prepare: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and generously grease a 10-inch bundt pan with butter or non-stick cooking spray. Dust the greased pan with a light coating of flour, tapping out any excess.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.

  3. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.

  4. Add Eggs and Flavorings: Beat in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract, lemon zest, and fresh lemon juice.

  5. Alternate Mixing: Gradually add the dry flour mixture into the wet ingredients, alternating with the Greek yogurt, until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  6. Fold in Blueberries: Gently fold the flour-coated blueberries into the batter, being careful not to break the berries.

  7. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared bundt pan and spread it out evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Cool and Glaze: Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 15 minutes before carefully transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. While the cake is cooling, prepare the glaze by whisking together the confectioners’ sugar, fresh lemon juice, and lemon zest until smooth.

  9. Drizzle with Glaze: Once the cake has cooled, drizzle the glaze over the top, allowing it to cascade down the sides of the cake.

  10. Serve and Enjoy: Slice the Lemon Blueberry Bundt Cake and serve it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra special treat.

Tips and Tricks

  • Make sure to coat the blueberries with flour before adding them to the batter. This will prevent them from sinking to the bottom of the cake during baking.
  • For an extra lemony flavor, you can add a few drops of lemon extract to the batter.
  • If you don’t have a bundt pan, you can use a 9x13-inch baking pan, but adjust the baking time accordingly.

Conclusion

The Lemon Blueberry Bundt Cake is a show-stopping dessert that is perfect for any occasion. Its moist and tender crumb, combined with the bright flavors of lemon and bursts of juicy blueberries, make it a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is sure to impress and delight. So, go ahead and give it a try – your taste buds will thank you!
