Decadent Pecan Pie Recipe for Your Thanksgiving Menu


Thanksgiving is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate this festive occasion than with a mouthwatering dessert? If you’re looking to impress your family and friends with a decadent treat, then look no further than this irresistible Pecan Pie recipe. With its buttery crust and gooey, caramel-like filling, this dessert is sure to be the star of your Thanksgiving menu.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 1 ½ cups pecan halves
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt for the crust. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Form the dough into a disk and wrap it in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Carefully transfer the dough to the pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and up the sides. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges.

  5. Spread the pecan halves evenly over the bottom of the pie crust.

  6. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, corn syrup, melted butter, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined.

  7. Pour the filling mixture over the pecans in the pie crust, making sure to cover them completely.

  8. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any potential spills and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly jiggly in the center.

  9. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack before serving. This will allow the filling to set further.

  10. Serve the decadent pecan pie at room temperature, or for an extra special touch, warm it up slightly and serve it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic pecan pie, you can add a tablespoon of bourbon or a teaspoon of cinnamon to the filling mixture.
  • If you prefer a deeper caramel flavor, you can substitute half of the corn syrup with dark corn syrup.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of nuts, such as walnuts or almonds, or even mix them together for a unique flavor combination.
  • To make the pie in advance, you can prepare the crust and filling separately and store them in the refrigerator. When you’re ready to bake, simply assemble the pie and follow the instructions as usual.

Now that you have this decadent pecan pie recipe in your arsenal, you can confidently add it to your Thanksgiving menu. Your guests will be in awe of the rich flavors and buttery crust, making it a truly memorable dessert. So go ahead, indulge in this irresistible treat and create lasting memories with your loved ones this Thanksgiving!
