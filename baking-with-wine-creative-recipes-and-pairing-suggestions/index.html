Baking with Wine: Creative Recipes and Pairing Suggestions


Baking with wine is a delightful and unexpected twist that elevates your culinary creations. The complexity of flavors that wine introduces can transform ordinary recipes into extraordinary desserts and savory treats. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, incorporating wine into your baking repertoire can yield delicious results. In this blog post, we will explore creative recipes that utilize wine in their ingredients and offer pairing suggestions that harmonize beautifully with your baked goods.

The Benefits of Baking with Wine

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a moment to discuss why baking with wine is a wonderful idea. Wine not only adds depth and richness to your dishes, but it also acts as a natural tenderizer. The acidity in wine can enhance flavor profiles, while the alcohol evaporates during baking, leaving behind a subtle essence that can elevate your desserts and savory items.

Additionally, the world of wine offers a vast array of flavors, from fruity and floral to robust and earthy. This versatility allows bakers to experiment with different types of wine to complement or contrast with their baked goods.

Creative Recipes Featuring Wine

1. Red Wine Chocolate Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red wine (Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon)
  • 1 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¾ cup cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a medium saucepan, heat the red wine over medium heat until it reduces to about half (approximately ½ cup). Allow it to cool slightly.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  4. In another bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy, then add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract and cooled wine.
  5. Gradually combine the wet and dry ingredients, mixing until just incorporated.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cake to cool before serving. Dust with powdered sugar or serve with a red wine reduction sauce for an elegant touch.

2. White Wine Poached Pear Tart

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe pears, peeled, halved, and cored
  • 1 bottle of dry white wine (Sauvignon Blanc works well)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 pre-made pastry shell

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the white wine, sugar, vanilla extract, and cinnamon. Bring to a simmer.
  2. Add the pear halves to the simmering wine mixture and poach for about 15-20 minutes, or until tender. Remove the pears and set them aside to cool.
  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Place the pastry shell in a tart pan and arrange the poached pears on top.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown.
  6. Serve warm, drizzled with some of the poaching liquid reduced to a syrupy consistency.

3. Spicy Red Wine Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine (Zinfandel adds a nice spice)
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, brown the ground meat over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for another minute.
  4. Add the kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and red wine. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or sour cream.

4. Wine-Infused Banana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ¼ cup white wine (Chardonnay adds a lovely flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a bowl, combine the mashed bananas, granulated sugar, brown sugar, egg, melted butter, vanilla extract, and white wine.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and baking soda.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Allow to cool before slicing. Enjoy with a glass of the same wine used in the recipe.

5. Rosé Wine Fruit Cake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed dried fruits (raisins, apricots, cherries)
  • 1 cup rosé wine
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Soak the mixed dried fruits in rosé wine overnight.
  2. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and grease a bundt pan.
  3. In a bowl, cream the butter and sugar together until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one by one, mixing well after each.
  4. In another bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, followed by the soaked fruits (with any remaining wine).
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared bundt pan and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until a skewer comes out clean.
  7. Let cool before serving. This cake pairs beautifully with a glass of chilled rosé.

Pairing Suggestions for Your Wine-Infused Baked Goods

Baking with wine not only enhances the flavors of your recipes, but it also opens up a world of pairing possibilities. Here are some suggestions for wines that complement the dishes mentioned above:

  • Red Wine Chocolate Cake: Pair with a rich glass of Merlot. The chocolate and red fruit flavors will create a delightful harmony.

  • White Wine Poached Pear Tart: Serve with a dessert wine like Sauternes or a sparkling wine to balance the sweetness of the pears.

  • Spicy Red Wine Chili: A robust Zinfandel complements the spiciness of the chili and enhances the savory notes.

  • Wine-Infused Banana Bread: Enjoy with a glass of dessert wine such as Moscato, which will enhance the sweetness of the bananas.

  • Rosé Wine Fruit Cake: Pair with a light and fruity rosé to highlight the flavors of the dried fruits in the cake.

Conclusion

Baking with wine is a creative way to explore new flavors and make your dishes stand out. From decadent desserts to savory delights, wine can be a wonderful ingredient that adds depth and complexity to your culinary creations. Experiment with different wines and discover how they can elevate your baking game. Cheers to delicious adventures in the kitchen!
