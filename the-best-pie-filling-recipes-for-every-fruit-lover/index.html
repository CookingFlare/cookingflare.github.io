The Best Pie Filling Recipes for Every Fruit Lover


When it comes to desserts, few things can rival the joy of a freshly baked pie. The crisp, flaky crust paired with a luscious fruit filling makes for a delightful treat that can satisfy any sweet tooth. Whether you prefer the tartness of berries, the sweetness of peaches, or the comforting taste of apples, there’s a pie filling recipe for every fruit lover. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of pie filling recipes that celebrate the natural flavors of fruit. From classic to creative, these fillings will inspire you to bake your next pie masterpiece.

1. Classic Apple Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 6 to 8 cups of peeled and sliced apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Apples: In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning.
  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, combine granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. Combine: Pour the sugar mixture over the apples and toss to coat evenly. Let the mixture sit for about 15 minutes to allow the juices to release.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out your pie crust and place it in a pie dish. Fill with the apple mixture and dot with butter. Cover with a top crust, sealing the edges.
  5. Bake: Cut slits in the top crust for steam to escape. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for 35-45 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the crust is golden.

2. Blueberry Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Blueberries: If using frozen blueberries, allow them to thaw and drain excess liquid.
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and salt. Stir gently to combine.
  3. Cook the Filling: Cook until the mixture begins to bubble and thicken, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Use your favorite pie crust recipe, and pour the blueberry filling into the prepared crust. Dot with butter and cover with another crust if desired.
  5. Bake: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden and juices are bubbling.

3. Cherry Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 4 cups pitted tart cherries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Prepare Cherries: If using frozen cherries, thaw and drain the excess liquid.
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a saucepan, combine cherries, sugar, cornstarch, almond extract, lemon juice, and salt.
  3. Cook the Filling: Over medium heat, cook until the mixture starts to bubble and thicken, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Roll out your pie crust and pour in the cherry filling. Dot with butter and cover with a top crust if desired.
  5. Bake: Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbly.

4. Peach Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 6 cups peeled and sliced fresh peaches (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Prepare Peaches: If using frozen peaches, allow them to thaw and drain excess liquid.
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine peaches, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla, and cinnamon. Toss gently to combine.
  3. Cook the Filling: Transfer the mixture to a saucepan over medium heat. Cook until the peaches are tender and the mixture thickens, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Fill your pie crust with the peach mixture, dot with butter, and cover with another crust if desired.
  5. Bake: Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden and juices are bubbling.

5. Mixed Berry Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 2 cups strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup raspberries
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Prepare Berries: Rinse and prepare all berries, hulled and sliced as necessary.
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine all berries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla. Toss to coat.
  3. Cook the Filling: Transfer the mixture to a saucepan over medium heat. Cook until the berries start to release their juices and the mixture thickens, about 7-10 minutes.
  4. Assemble the Pie: Pour the mixed berry filling into your prepared pie crust, dot with butter, and cover with a top crust if desired.
  5. Bake: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is bubbly and the crust is golden brown.

6. Lemon Meringue Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 3 large egg yolks, beaten
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a saucepan, whisk together sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually stir in water.
  2. Cook the Mixture: Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and begins to bubble.
  3. Add Egg Yolks: Slowly whisk a small amount of the hot mixture into the beaten egg yolks to temper them, then return to the saucepan and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Add Lemon and Butter: Remove from heat and stir in lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter until smooth.
  5. Assemble the Pie: Pour the lemon filling into a pre-baked pie crust. Let it cool slightly while you prepare the meringue.
  6. Make the Meringue: Beat 3 egg whites until soft peaks form, then gradually add 6 tablespoons of sugar, beating until stiff peaks form. Spread over the lemon filling.
  7. Bake: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-12 minutes, or until the meringue is golden.

Conclusion

Creating your own pie filling is a wonderful way to showcase the flavors of the season. With these recipes, you can explore the delightful world of fruit pies and impress your family and friends with delicious homemade desserts. Each filling brings its own unique taste and aroma, ensuring there’s always something to satisfy every fruit lover’s craving. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the pie-making adventure begin! Happy baking!
