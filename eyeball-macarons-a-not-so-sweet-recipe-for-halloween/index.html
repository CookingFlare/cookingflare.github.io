Eyeball Macarons: A Not-So-Sweet Recipe for Halloween


Halloween is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with some frightfully delicious treats? This year, give your spooky creations an edible twist with Eyeball Macarons - a unique dessert that is sure to entice both kids and adults alike. The best part? It’s much easier to make than one would think, and they look positively macabre!

Ingredients:

For the Macaron Shells:

  • 1 cup icing sugar
  • 3/4 cup almond flour
  • 3 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Gel food coloring (black or purple)
  • Gel food coloring (red)

For the Buttercream Filling and Eyeballs:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup whole milk
  • Lollipop sticks or skewers
  • Gel food coloring (black)
  • Candy eyeballs (small to medium size)

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Macaron Shells

  1. Sift the almond flour and icing sugar together into a bowl, discarding any lumps.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites with an electric mixer until they become foamy. Gradually add in the granulated sugar, and continue to beat until stiff peaks form.
  3. Gently fold the flour mixture into the egg white mixture, using a spatula, in a cutting motion until the batter is smooth. It should resemble raw egg white consistency; do not overmix.
  4. Divide your batter into several bowls and add gel food coloring to each bowl, one color per bowl. Mix well for vibrant macaron shells.
  5. Transfer the batter to piping bags fitted with a round tip. Pipe a circle of batter onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper (about 1-inch diameter).
  6. Tap the baking sheet on the counter a few times to release any air bubbles, then let your macarons rest for 30 minutes until a skin forms on the surface. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) as they rest.
  7. Bake the macarons for 12-15 minutes, or until they are set but not browned. They should easily lift away from the parchment paper. Allow them to cool before removing from the baking sheet.

Step 2: Prepare the Buttercream Filling

  1. In a large bowl, cream the softened butter with an electric mixer until it’s smooth and fluffy.
  2. Gradually add the powdered sugar, followed by the vanilla extract and whole milk. Continue to beat until the mixture is creamy and pipeable.
  3. Add food coloring and mix well for vibrant buttercream. The consistency should be firm enough to hold its shape. If it’s too thin, add more powdered sugar. If it’s too thick, add a little more milk.

Step 3: Assemble the Eyeball Macarons

  1. Once your macarons are completely cool, pair them by size and sandwich two shells together with buttercream filling.
  2. Place the filled macaron on your serving tray, with the flat side facing up. Use the remaining buttercream to attach the candy eyeballs on top.
  3. If desired, stick a lollipop stick or skewer through the bottom (flat side) of the macaron to make them more life-like – this also adds a convenient handle for Trick-or-Treaters.

Step 4: Presentation

Serve these delightful, spooky treats to your guests and watch them disappear. Store uneaten macarons in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. If you’re sending them to a location, box them carefully to avoid damage, but trust us when we say they won’t last long enough to make it there!

Closing Thoughts

Whether you’re entertaining for a Halloween party, or just wanting to practice your baking skills, Eyeball Macarons are a fun alternative that will definitely make your dessert table stand out. A little creativity mixed with culinary expertise will bring a drop-dead-gorgeous addition to any spooky feast this Halloween. So, gather your ingredients, grab a piping bag, and unleash your macabre baker inside! Happy haunting!
