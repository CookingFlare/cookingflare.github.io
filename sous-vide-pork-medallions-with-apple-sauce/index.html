Sous Vide Pork Medallions with Apple Sauce


Sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we approach preparing meat. It allows for precise temperature control, ensuring that every cut of meat is cooked to perfection, retaining its natural flavors and tenderness. If you’ve ever struggled with overcooking or drying out pork, sous vide is a game changer. This recipe for sous vide pork medallions with apple sauce is not only simple but guarantees melt-in-your-mouth pork, complemented by a rich, slightly tart apple sauce that enhances the natural sweetness of the pork.

Why Sous Vide Pork Medallions?

Pork medallions are a tender cut from the pork tenderloin, known for their juicy texture and mild flavor. However, like most lean meats, they can dry out if cooked improperly. Traditional methods, such as pan-searing or grilling, can often result in uneven cooking, with some parts of the meat overdone while others are undercooked. Sous vide cooking solves this problem by cooking the pork evenly at a precise temperature, ensuring that the entire medallion remains perfectly tender from edge to edge.

The sous vide method also allows for flavor infusions. In this recipe, we will infuse the pork medallions with a simple yet effective marinade, which enhances the taste without overwhelming the natural pork flavor. Once cooked, the pork is seared in a hot pan to create a golden, crispy exterior, while the inside remains perfectly juicy and tender.

Pairing the pork with a homemade apple sauce adds a sweet and savory contrast, balancing the richness of the meat. The combination of the sous vide method and the apple sauce elevates this dish, making it an impressive yet easy option for any dinner party or family meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make sous vide pork medallions with apple sauce, gather the following ingredients:

For the Pork Medallions:

  • 4 pork medallions (about 6 oz each)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried rosemary)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

For the Apple Sauce:

  • 2 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • A pinch of salt

Equipment:

  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • Vacuum seal bags or resealable zipper bags
  • Heavy-bottomed skillet (preferably cast iron)
  • A blender or immersion blender for the apple sauce

Preparing the Pork Medallions for Sous Vide Cooking

The key to sous vide cooking is ensuring that your pork is well-seasoned and properly vacuum-sealed before cooking. Here’s how to prepare the pork:

  1. Season the Pork: Start by seasoning your pork medallions with salt, pepper, and rosemary. The rosemary imparts a fragrant, earthy flavor that pairs beautifully with pork. Add garlic to bring a subtle depth of flavor. Drizzle the medallions with a little olive oil and brush each piece with Dijon mustard and apple cider vinegar. The mustard adds a hint of sharpness, while the vinegar helps tenderize the pork and enhances the flavors.

  2. Vacuum Seal or Bag the Pork: For sous vide cooking, vacuum sealing the pork medallions ensures that they cook evenly and prevents water from entering the bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a resealable zipper bag. Place the seasoned pork medallions in the bag, making sure they are not overlapping. If using a zipper bag, use the water displacement method to remove the air: seal the bag almost all the way, then slowly lower it into the water bath, allowing the water pressure to push out the remaining air. Seal the bag completely once the air is out.

  3. Set the Sous Vide Temperature: Preheat your sous vide water bath to 140°F (60°C) for perfectly tender pork. If you prefer your pork medallions a little more done, you can increase the temperature to 145°F (62.7°C) for a medium doneness. This low and slow cooking process ensures the pork remains juicy and tender, never dry.

  4. Cook the Pork: Submerge the sealed bag of pork medallions into the preheated water bath. Cook for 1 to 1.5 hours. The beauty of sous vide is that the pork will cook evenly and retain its moisture, so you don’t need to worry about overcooking.

Making the Apple Sauce

While your pork is cooking to perfection in the sous vide bath, take the time to prepare the apple sauce. The apple sauce is a simple, yet flavorful accompaniment that adds a natural sweetness to balance the savory pork.

  1. Peel and Core the Apples: Begin by peeling, coring, and chopping the apples into small chunks. Granny Smith apples are a popular choice because of their tartness, which pairs nicely with the richness of the pork. However, you can also use Honeycrisp apples for a sweeter sauce.

  2. Cook the Apples: In a medium saucepan, add the chopped apples, water, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt. Stir to combine and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. Cook the apples for about 15 minutes, or until they have softened and broken down into a chunky sauce.

  3. Blend the Sauce: Once the apples are soft, remove the saucepan from the heat. Use an immersion blender to puree the apple sauce directly in the pan, or transfer it to a blender. Blend until smooth and creamy. Add the lemon juice to brighten the flavor and adjust the sweetness by adding more sugar, if desired.

  4. Keep Warm: Once your apple sauce is blended, cover it and keep it warm on the stove until ready to serve. The sauce can also be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Searing the Pork

After the pork has finished cooking in the sous vide bath, it’s time to give it that golden, crispy exterior. This step is crucial because it adds texture and a delightful caramelized flavor to the meat.

  1. Preheat the Pan: Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of olive oil or butter and allow it to get hot. The oil should shimmer but not smoke.

  2. Sear the Pork: Remove the pork medallions from the bag and pat them dry with paper towels. This step is important because any moisture on the surface will prevent the pork from searing properly. Once the pan is hot, place the pork medallions in the skillet and sear each side for about 1-2 minutes, or until a golden-brown crust forms. Don’t overcrowd the pan; you may need to do this in batches.

  3. Rest the Pork: After searing, transfer the pork medallions to a plate and let them rest for a few minutes. This helps the juices redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring a moist and tender bite.

Serving the Dish

Now that both the pork medallions and apple sauce are ready, it’s time to plate your dish. Here’s how to serve it beautifully:

  1. Plate the Pork: Arrange the pork medallions on a serving plate, ensuring they are evenly spaced for a professional presentation.

  2. Spoon the Apple Sauce: Generously spoon the warm apple sauce over the top of the pork medallions. The sweet and tart flavors of the apple sauce will create a lovely contrast to the savory richness of the pork.

  3. Garnish and Serve: If desired, garnish the dish with a sprinkle of fresh herbs, such as parsley or thyme, for added color and freshness. Serve with a side of roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes for a complete, satisfying meal.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Pork Medallions

  • Choose Quality Pork: Opt for fresh, high-quality pork medallions from your local butcher or grocery store. The better the quality of the meat, the more flavorful your dish will be.

  • Seasoning Variations: Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices. Thyme, sage, or even a bit of paprika can add unique flavors to the pork medallions.

  • Apple Sauce Adjustments: If you like a smoother apple sauce, you can blend the sauce even more to reach a silky consistency. For a spicier kick, try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or a splash of bourbon to the sauce for a deeper flavor.

  • Sous Vide Cooking Times: While 1 to 1.5 hours is perfect for tender pork, sous vide cooking is quite forgiving. You can leave the pork in the water bath for up to 4 hours without it becoming overcooked, making it an excellent dish to prepare in advance.

Conclusion

Sous vide pork medallions with apple sauce is a perfect example of how modern cooking techniques can elevate traditional flavors. The sous vide method ensures that the pork medallions are cooked to perfection, with a tender, juicy texture that is difficult to achieve with other cooking methods. The apple sauce adds a fresh, sweet contrast that balances the richness of the pork, creating a dish that’s both sophisticated and comforting.

Whether you’re a seasoned sous vide cook or just starting out, this recipe is approachable and guarantees delicious results. Impress your friends and family with this simple yet elegant meal, and enjoy the perfect pairing of savory pork and sweet apple sauce.
