9. Butternut Squash Soup

For a light and flavorful side, butternut squash soup is a fantastic option. This velvety soup is made by roasting the squash and blending it with onions, garlic, and vegetable broth for a smooth, comforting texture. A hint of nutmeg and cinnamon adds warmth, making this soup a perfect start to your Thanksgiving meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roast the butternut squash on a baking sheet for 25-30 minutes until tender.
  3. In a pot, sauté the onion and garlic in a little oil until softened.
  4. Add the roasted squash, vegetable broth, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Blend the soup until smooth and season with salt and pepper.

10. Roasted Garlic and Herb Potatoes

These roasted garlic and herb potatoes are a fantastic side dish that pairs well with turkey and other meats. The crispy, golden edges of the potatoes combined with the savory garlic and fresh herbs make this dish irresistible. It’s simple to prepare, but the flavor is extraordinary.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the halved potatoes with olive oil, garlic, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast on a baking sheet for 25-30 minutes, until golden and crispy.

11. Cauliflower Gratin

Cauliflower gratin is a creamy, cheesy side that brings a touch of sophistication to your Thanksgiving spread. The cauliflower is lightly blanched before being smothered in a rich cheese sauce and topped with a golden breadcrumb crust. It’s a perfect way to enjoy this cruciferous vegetable with a creamy twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Blanch the cauliflower in boiling water for 5 minutes, then drain.
  3. In a saucepan, melt butter and whisk in flour. Gradually add milk, whisking until thickened.
  4. Stir in cheese, salt, and pepper until melted and smooth.
  5. Toss the cauliflower with the cheese sauce and transfer to a baking dish.
  6. Top with breadcrumbs and bake for 20 minutes, until golden and bubbly.

12. Pumpkin Risotto

Pumpkin risotto brings the warmth of fall flavors into a creamy, comforting dish. The combination of Arborio rice, pumpkin puree, and Parmesan cheese creates a rich, savory side dish that complements turkey and other holiday mains beautifully. A dash of sage enhances the earthy flavors of the pumpkin, making this risotto a standout dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, melt butter over medium heat. Add the Arborio rice and toast for 2 minutes.
  2. Gradually add the white wine and stir until absorbed.
  3. Slowly add the vegetable broth, stirring frequently until the liquid is absorbed.
  4. Stir in the pumpkin puree and Parmesan cheese, cooking until creamy.
  5. Season with sage, salt, and pepper before serving.
