How to Make Vegan Tarts and Galettes: Flaky and Delicious


When it comes to baking, few things are as versatile and delightful as tarts and galettes. These flaky pastries can be filled with a plethora of sweet or savory ingredients, making them the perfect dish for any occasion. Whether you’re looking to impress guests at a dinner party or simply want to indulge in a cozy treat at home, vegan tarts and galettes are a fantastic choice. In this post, we’ll dive deep into the art of creating these delicious pastries from scratch, ensuring your baking experience is both enjoyable and rewarding.

What Are Tarts and Galettes?

Before we get into the nitty-gritty of baking, let’s clarify the differences between tarts and galettes.

  • Tarts are typically made in a shallow, round pan with a removable bottom, featuring a structured pastry crust. They are often filled with custards, fruits, or savory mixtures and can be served either hot or cold.

  • Galettes, on the other hand, are rustic, free-form pastries that require no special pan. They are made by rolling out the dough and folding the edges over the filling, creating a charming, casual appearance. Galettes are often filled with seasonal fruits or vegetables and can be savory or sweet.

Both options are perfect for showcasing fresh ingredients and can easily be made vegan by substituting traditional ingredients for plant-based alternatives.

Essential Ingredients for Vegan Tarts and Galettes

Creating the perfect vegan tart or galette starts with selecting the right ingredients. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need:

For the Pastry Dough

  1. Flour: All-purpose flour works well, but you can also use whole wheat flour or gluten-free alternatives if desired.
  2. Plant-based Butter: Look for vegan butter sticks for the best texture. Coconut oil can also be used, but it may impart a slight coconut flavor.
  3. Powdered Sugar: This sweetener helps create a tender crust. Regular granulated sugar can be used, but it may result in a slightly different texture.
  4. Salt: Just a pinch to enhance the flavors.
  5. Ice Water: This is crucial for bringing the dough together without warming the butter too much.

For the Filling

The filling can vary widely based on your preferences. Here are some ideas:

  • Sweet Fillings: Fresh fruits (like berries, peaches, or apples), vegan custard, chocolate ganache, or nut butter spreads.
  • Savory Fillings: Seasonal vegetables (like zucchini, tomatoes, or mushrooms), vegan cheese, herbs, and spices.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Tarts and Galettes

Step 1: Prepare the Pastry Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine 1 and 1/4 cups of flour, 1/4 cup of powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt.

  2. Cut in the Plant-based Butter: Add 1/2 cup of cold, diced vegan butter to the dry ingredients. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add Ice Water: Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently until the dough begins to come together. You may need about 3 to 5 tablespoons of ice water, but be careful not to overwork the dough.

  4. Chill the Dough: Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This chilling step helps the fats solidify, resulting in a flakier crust.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough is chilling, you can prepare your filling. Here are two delicious filling options to consider:

Sweet Berry Filling

  • Ingredients: 2 cups of mixed berries (fresh or frozen), 1/4 cup of maple syrup, 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.

  • Instructions: In a bowl, combine the berries, maple syrup, cornstarch, and vanilla extract. Toss gently until the berries are coated. Let this mixture sit for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Savory Vegetable Filling

  • Ingredients: 1 cup of chopped seasonal vegetables (like zucchini, bell peppers, and onions), 1 tablespoon of olive oil, 1 teaspoon of dried herbs (such as thyme or oregano), salt, and pepper to taste.

  • Instructions: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the vegetables and sauté until they are tender, about 5-7 minutes. Season with herbs, salt, and pepper, then let cool.

Step 3: Assemble the Tart or Galette

For a Tart

  1. Roll Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle, about 1/8 inch thick. Transfer it to a tart pan, pressing it into the edges. Trim any excess dough.

  2. Pre-bake the Crust: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and place the tart crust in the oven for about 10 minutes, until just set but not browned.

  3. Add the Filling: Remove the crust from the oven and fill it with your prepared berry or vegetable filling.

  4. Bake the Tart: Return the tart to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is bubbling and the crust is golden brown.

For a Galette

  1. Roll Out the Dough: Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface into a rough circle, about 1/8 inch thick.

  2. Add the Filling: Spoon your filling into the center of the dough, leaving about 2 inches of border.

  3. Fold the Edges: Gently fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pleating as needed to create a rustic look.

  4. Bake the Galette: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake the galette for 30-35 minutes, until the crust is golden and the filling is cooked through.

Step 4: Cool and Serve

Once baked, allow your tart or galette to cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing. This resting period allows the filling to set, ensuring clean slices. Serve warm or at room temperature, and consider adding a dollop of vegan whipped cream or a scoop of dairy-free ice cream for an extra treat!

Tips for Perfect Vegan Tarts and Galettes

  • Keep Everything Cold: Cold butter and water are key to a flaky crust. Use chilled utensils and work quickly to prevent the butter from melting.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t be afraid to get creative with your fillings. Seasonal fruits and vegetables are perfect for showcasing fresh flavors.

  • Use a Silpat or Parchment Paper: To prevent sticking, line your baking sheet with a Silpat mat or parchment paper when baking galettes.

  • Make Ahead: The pastry dough can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for up to a month. Just be sure to thaw it in the fridge before rolling it out.

Conclusion

Vegan tarts and galettes are not only delicious but also incredibly versatile. With a flaky, tender crust and endless filling possibilities, these pastries can be tailored to suit any taste or occasion. Whether you opt for a sweet berry tart or a savory vegetable galette, your culinary creation is sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy the process of making these delightful pastries from scratch. Happy baking!
