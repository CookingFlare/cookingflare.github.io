Sous Vide Creme Brulee: Creamy and Luxurious Dessert


Crème brûlée is a classic dessert that never fails to impress. Its velvety custard base and caramelized sugar topping create a symphony of flavors and textures that are hard to resist. While the traditional method of making crème brûlée involves baking the custard in a water bath, the sous vide technique takes it to a whole new level of perfection. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make a sous vide crème brûlée that is unbelievably creamy and luxurious.

Ingredients

  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • Pinch of salt
  • Extra granulated sugar for caramelizing

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 176°F (80°C).

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined.

  3. Slowly pour the heavy cream into the egg mixture, whisking continuously to ensure everything is well incorporated.

  4. Once the custard mixture is smooth and silky, strain it through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. This step is crucial to remove any lumps or impurities, resulting in a silky-smooth texture.

  5. Divide the custard evenly among four ramekins or oven-safe jars. Place the ramekins in a baking dish or a sous vide container.

  6. Carefully pour hot water into the baking dish or sous vide container, making sure it reaches about three-quarters of the way up the sides of the ramekins. This will create a gentle and uniform cooking environment for the custards.

  7. Seal the baking dish or sous vide container with plastic wrap or a lid, ensuring that no water can enter the ramekins.

  8. Gently place the baking dish or sous vide container into the preheated water bath and cook for 60-75 minutes. The custards should be set around the edges but still have a slight jiggle in the center.

  9. Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the ramekins from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature. Then, transfer them to the refrigerator and chill for at least 2 hours, or overnight for the best results.

  10. When you’re ready to serve, sprinkle a thin layer of granulated sugar evenly over the surface of each custard. Using a kitchen torch, carefully caramelize the sugar until it turns a deep golden brown. Make sure to move the torch in a circular motion to evenly distribute the heat.

  11. Allow the custards to sit for a few minutes to let the sugar harden into a crispy topping. Serve immediately and enjoy the decadent delight that is sous vide crème brûlée!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra layer of flavor, you can infuse the cream with different ingredients such as vanilla bean, citrus zest, or even a hint of espresso.
  • Don’t skip the step of straining the custard mixture. It ensures a smooth and silky texture by removing any bits of cooked egg or other impurities.
  • If you don’t have a kitchen torch, you can use the broiler function in your oven to caramelize the sugar topping. Simply place the custards under the broiler for a few minutes, keeping a close eye on them to prevent burning.
  • Feel free to get creative with the toppings! Add a sprinkle of cinnamon, a drizzle of chocolate sauce, or even a dollop of whipped cream to take your sous vide crème brûlée to the next level.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide crème brûlée is the epitome of indulgence. The slow and gentle cooking method ensures a perfectly creamy custard every time, while the caramelized sugar topping adds a delightful crunch. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself, this luxurious dessert is sure to impress. So grab your sous vide machine, gather the ingredients, and get ready to dive into a world of creamy deliciousness with sous vide crème brûlée!
