Greek Galaktoboureko with Custard and Phyllo Recipe


Are you in the mood for a sweet and creamy dessert that will transport you to the sunny shores of Greece? Look no further than Galaktoboureko! This traditional Greek delicacy combines the richness of custard with the flaky goodness of phyllo pastry. With its golden crust and luscious filling, Galaktoboureko is a dessert fit for any occasion.

Ingredients

For the Custard:

  • 1 liter of milk
  • 150 grams of fine semolina
  • 200 grams of sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon

For the Syrup:

  • 400 grams of sugar
  • 500 milliliters of water
  • Juice of 1/2 a lemon
  • 1 cinnamon stick

For the Phyllo:

  • 250 grams of phyllo pastry sheets
  • 150 grams of melted butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

  2. In a large saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it starts to simmer. Be careful not to let it boil.

  3. While the milk is heating, in a separate bowl, whisk together the semolina, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, and lemon zest until well combined.

  4. Slowly pour the semolina mixture into the simmering milk, stirring continuously to avoid any lumps. Cook the custard mixture for about 10 minutes, or until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon.

  5. Remove the custard from the heat and let it cool slightly.

  6. While the custard is cooling, prepare the syrup by combining the sugar, water, lemon juice, and cinnamon stick in a saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.

  7. Take your phyllo pastry sheets and cut them to fit the size of your baking dish. Brush each sheet with melted butter before layering them in the dish. Repeat this process until you have about half of the phyllo sheets layered.

  8. Pour the cooled custard over the phyllo sheets, spreading it evenly.

  9. Continue layering the remaining phyllo sheets on top of the custard, brushing each sheet with melted butter.

  10. Using a sharp knife, carefully score the top layer of phyllo into diamond or square shapes. This will make it easier to cut the Galaktoboureko later.

  11. Bake the Galaktoboureko in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.

  12. Once baked, remove the Galaktoboureko from the oven and immediately pour the cooled syrup over it. The hot pastry will absorb the syrup, infusing it with sweetness.

  13. Allow the Galaktoboureko to cool completely before serving. This dessert is best enjoyed chilled, so refrigerate it for a few hours or overnight for the flavors to meld together.

Serving Suggestions

Galaktoboureko is a versatile dessert that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a variety of accompaniments. Here are a few serving suggestions to enhance your Galaktoboureko experience:

  • Dust the top of the Galaktoboureko with a sprinkle of powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness.
  • Serve each slice with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
  • Garnish with a few fresh berries or a drizzle of honey for a burst of freshness.

Conclusion

Indulge in the decadent flavors of Greece with this Galaktoboureko recipe. The combination of creamy custard and crispy phyllo pastry is a match made in dessert heaven. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet treat, Galaktoboureko is sure to impress. Follow this recipe, and let your taste buds embark on a culinary journey to the sun-drenched Mediterranean. Opa!
