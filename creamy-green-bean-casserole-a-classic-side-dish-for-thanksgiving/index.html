Creamy Green Bean Casserole: A Classic Side Dish for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is just around the corner, and many of us are busy planning the perfect menu for our family gatherings. While turkey is often the centerpiece of the meal, it’s the side dishes that really make the occasion special. One classic side dish that never fails to impress is the creamy green bean casserole. This comforting and flavorful dish is a staple of many Thanksgiving tables, and for good reason. In this post, we’ll explore the recipe and techniques for making the perfect creamy green bean casserole.

The History of Creamy Green Bean Casserole

The creamy green bean casserole has a rich history that dates back to the 1950s. It’s believed to have originated from a Campbell’s Soup Company recipe, which was published in the company’s cookbook, “The Campbell’s Cookbook”, in 1955. The recipe quickly became popular, and it’s been a staple of many American holiday tables ever since.

The Recipe

This creamy green bean casserole recipe is a classic take on the traditional dish. It’s easy to make, uses common ingredients, and is sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup French-fried onions
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch the green beans for 5-7 minutes, or until they’re tender but still crisp. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, sauté the chopped onion in butter until it’s softened and translucent.
  4. In a separate mixing bowl, combine the milk, cream of mushroom soup, black pepper, and salt. Stir until well combined.
  5. Add the cooked green beans, sautéed onion, and grated cheddar cheese to the mixing bowl. Stir until the cheese is melted and the mixture is well combined.
  6. Pour the mixture into a 9x13 inch baking dish. Top with French-fried onions.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the onions are golden brown and the casserole is hot and bubbly.

Tips and Variations

While this recipe is a classic, there are many ways to customize it to your taste. Here are a few tips and variations to try:

  • Use fresh or frozen green beans: While fresh green beans are ideal, frozen beans can be used as a substitute. Simply thaw and pat dry before using.
  • Add some spice: If you like a little heat, add some red pepper flakes or diced jalapeños to the mixture for an extra kick.
  • Mix and match cheeses: While cheddar is traditional, you can experiment with other cheeses like Parmesan, Swiss, or even feta.
  • Add some crunch: Sprinkle some chopped nuts or crushed crackers on top of the casserole for added texture.

Conclusion

The creamy green bean casserole is a classic side dish that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. With its rich history, easy recipe, and endless variations, it’s a dish that’s sure to become a staple of your Thanksgiving table. So why not give it a try this year? Your family and friends are sure to thank you.
