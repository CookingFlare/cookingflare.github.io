Vegan and Dairy-Free Sauces for BBQs


Summertime means BBQs, outdoor gatherings, and delicious meals shared with family and friends. But for those embracing a vegan lifestyle or avoiding dairy, it can sometimes feel like traditional BBQ menus lack options that cater to your needs. Whether you’re hosting the cookout or bringing a dish as a guest, crafting mouthwatering vegan and dairy-free sauces can elevate any BBQ spread.

Below, you’ll find a collection of homemade vegan sauce recipes that are perfect for slathering on grilled veggies, plant-based proteins, or for dipping alongside crispy sides. These sauces are packed with flavor, easy to make, and free of animal products or dairy—but don’t worry, even non-vegans will be asking for seconds!

Why Create Your Own Vegan Sauces?

While store-bought condiments are convenient, whipping up your own sauces allows you to avoid hidden animal byproducts (like casein or honey) and control the ingredients. Not only is this important for staying true to your dietary preferences, but it also ensures fresher flavors and the ultimate customization.

Homemade sauces are simple to make and adaptable based on what’s in your pantry. Need something creamy, tangy, smoky, or spicy? You’re bound to find inspiration in the recipes below.

1. Smoky Cashew-Based BBQ Sauce

This smoky BBQ sauce has it all—depth of flavor, velvety texture, and a subtle tang that pairs well with grilled mushrooms, tofu, zucchini, and beyond. Instead of relying on butter or egg-based thickeners, this recipe uses cashews to create a creamy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews, soaked for at least 4 hours
  • 1 cup crushed canned tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked cashews and blend them in a high-speed blender until smooth.
  2. Add the crushed tomatoes, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, smoked paprika, chili powder, tamari, garlic, and salt.
  3. Continue blending until combined and silky. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed.
  4. Serve cold or warm gently on low heat. Use generously with grilled veggies or vegan burgers.

2. Zesty Lemon-Tahini Dressing

A creamy yet refreshing sauce, this lemon-tahini dressing is perfect for drizzling over fresh veggies or as a dip for roasted sweet potato wedges. Packed with healthy fats and bright citrus notes, it’s a crowd-pleaser that balances smoky BBQ flavors beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • ¼ cup warm water
  • Juice of 1 large lemon
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, finely grated or minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together tahini and warm water until smooth and creamy. Adjust the water quantity for your desired thickness.
  2. Stir in lemon juice, olive oil, garlic, cumin (if using), and a pinch of salt with a crack of black pepper.
  3. Taste and tweak to your preference—more lemon juice for tang or salt to enhance flavors.
  4. Serve immediately, or store covered in the fridge for up to 3 days.

3. Classic Vegan Ranch Dip

Creamy, herby, and packed with flavor, this vegan ranch dip is a staple that works across many BBQ settings. Pair it with fresh veggie platters, grilled kebabs, or use it as a base for a dairy-free coleslaw.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup unsweetened vegan yogurt or coconut cream
  • 2 tablespoons almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the vegan yogurt or coconut cream with almond milk and apple cider vinegar in a mixing bowl. Whisk until smooth.
  2. Stir in parsley, dill, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Chill the sauce for at least 20 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  4. Serve generously with crispy cauliflower bites or BBQ jackfruit sliders.

4. Spicy Chipotle Mayo

Want to add a fiery twist to your BBQ offerings? This vegan chipotle mayo brings creaminess, heat, and a hint of smokiness. It’s ideal for sandwiches, wraps, or even drizzling over grilled corn.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup vegan mayo of your choice
  • 1-2 chipotle peppers in adobo, minced (adjust for heat level)
  • Juice of ½ lime
  • 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  • 1 teaspoon agave nectar
  • Pinch of smoked paprika
  • A dash of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the vegan mayo with the minced chipotle peppers.
  2. Add lime juice, garlic, agave nectar, smoked paprika, and salt. Stir until well incorporated.
  3. Refrigerate for 15-30 minutes to let the flavors merge.
  4. Serve as a dipping sauce or a spread for BBQ sandwiches.

5. Fresh Avocado Chimichurri

Chimichurri is traditionally a herby and piquant Argentine sauce, but this twist adds avocado for a creamy yet refreshing base. Perfect for drizzling on smoky veggies or grilled tempeh steaks.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large ripe avocado
  • 1 cup chopped fresh parsley (stems removed)
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Scoop out the avocado into a food processor. Add parsley, cilantro, red wine vinegar, olive oil, lime juice, and garlic.
  2. Pulse until smooth but slightly chunky for texture.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary.
  4. Serve promptly for maximum freshness or refrigerate briefly.

Pro Tips for Perfect BBQ Sauces

  1. Storage: Many of these sauces can be prepared ahead of time and stored in airtight containers in the refrigerator. Fresh sauces like avocado chimichurri are best enjoyed the day they’re made but can be kept fresh for up to 1 day.
  2. Adjust to Your Preference: Each sauce recipe can be tweaked based on your taste buds. Want more spice? Add chili flakes. Need something sweeter? A touch of maple syrup works wonders.
  3. Pair Thoughtfully: Match sauces with grilled foods that complement their flavor. For example, chimichurri works exceptionally well with smoky eggplant and bell peppers.

With these vegan and dairy-free sauce options, you’ll never need to settle for bland BBQ spreads again. Combining bold flavors and rich textures, these homemade condiments are sure to impress your guests while keeping your meals plant-based and cruelty-free. Whether you’re feeding vegans or omnivores, these recipes prove that BBQs can cater to all tastes without compromising on flavor.
