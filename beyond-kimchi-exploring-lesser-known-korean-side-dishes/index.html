Beyond Kimchi: Exploring Lesser-Known Korean Side Dishes


When it comes to Korean cuisine, kimchi is often the first dish that comes to mind. And for good reason – it’s a staple condiment that adds flavor and spice to many Korean meals. However, kimchi is just one part of the rich and diverse world of Korean side dishes. In this post, we’ll explore some of the lesser-known side dishes that are just as delicious and interesting as kimchi.

Gyeranjjim () - Steamed Eggs

Gyeranjjim is a simple yet flavorful side dish made with steamed eggs, sesame oil, and soy sauce. It’s often served as a palate cleanser between bites of spicy food. To make gyeranjjim, beat 2-3 eggs in a bowl and season with salt and black pepper. Then, steam the eggs in a steamer basket until they’re cooked through. Drizzle with sesame oil and soy sauce, and garnish with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds.

Oi Muchim () - Cucumber Salad

Oi muchim is a refreshing side dish that’s perfect for hot summer days. It’s made with thinly sliced cucumbers, vinegar, sugar, and sesame oil. To make oi muchim, slice 2-3 cucumbers into thin rounds and place them in a bowl. In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons of vinegar, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and 1 tablespoon of sesame oil. Pour the dressing over the cucumbers and toss to combine. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Sigeumchi () - Boiled Spinach

Sigeumchi is a classic Korean side dish that’s made by boiling spinach in salt water. To make sigeumchi, bring a pot of water to a boil and add 1-2 tablespoons of salt. Add 1 bunch of fresh spinach to the pot and cook until the leaves are wilted. Drain the spinach and rinse it with cold water to stop the cooking process. Serve the sigeumchi hot, garnished with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Gyeran-mari () - Korean-Style Rolled Omelette

Gyeran-mari is a popular Korean side dish that’s made with rolled omelette, vegetables, and sometimes meat. To make gyeran-mari, beat 2 eggs in a bowl and season with salt and black pepper. Heat a small non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of oil. Pour the eggs into the pan and cook until the edges start to set. Add your choice of vegetables (such as carrots, zucchini, and bell peppers) and sometimes meat (such as ham or bacon) to the eggs. Roll the omelette into a tight cylinder and slice it into bite-sized pieces. Serve the gyeran-mari hot, garnished with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Bokkeumbap () - Korean-Style Fried Rice

Bokkeumbap is a popular Korean side dish that’s made with leftover rice, vegetables, and sometimes meat. To make bokkeumbap, heat a small non-stick pan over medium heat and add a small amount of oil. Add 1 cup of leftover rice to the pan and stir-fry until it’s lightly toasted. Add your choice of vegetables (such as carrots, zucchini, and bell peppers) and sometimes meat (such as ham or bacon) to the rice. Stir-fry everything together until the vegetables are tender and the rice is heated through. Serve the bokkeumbap hot, garnished with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Kongnamul Muchim () - Bean Sprout Salad

Kongnamul muchim is a refreshing side dish that’s made with bean sprouts, vinegar, sugar, and sesame oil. To make kongnamul muchim, rinse 1 cup of bean sprouts and drain them well. In a small bowl, whisk together 2 tablespoons of vinegar, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and 1 tablespoon of sesame oil. Pour the dressing over the bean sprouts and toss to combine. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Conclusion

In this post, we’ve explored some of the lesser-known Korean side dishes that are just as delicious and interesting as kimchi. From steamed eggs to bean sprout salad, these side dishes offer a variety of flavors and textures that can elevate any Korean meal. Whether you’re a seasoned foodie or just looking to try something new, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds. So next time you’re cooking up a Korean meal, consider adding one of these side dishes to the menu. Your taste buds will thank you!
