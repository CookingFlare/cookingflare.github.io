How to Make Spanish Tomato Bread: A Simple and Delicious Recipe


When it comes to Spanish cuisine, one of the simplest yet most delicious dishes you can prepare is Spanish tomato bread, or pan con tomate. This rustic and flavorful appetizer has been a staple of Spanish households for centuries, particularly in the region of Catalonia. The recipe is not only quick and easy but also requires very few ingredients, making it perfect for any time of day—whether as a light snack, a tapa for a gathering, or even as part of a hearty breakfast.

In this post, we’ll guide you through the steps to make this classic Spanish tomato bread at home. You’ll also learn a bit about its origins, variations, and tips for taking your pan con tomate to the next level.

What Is Spanish Tomato Bread?

Spanish tomato bread, known as pan con tomate in Spanish, is a simple yet delicious dish made by rubbing ripe tomatoes onto slices of toasted bread. Traditionally, it is seasoned with olive oil, garlic, and salt. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity and the quality of ingredients. It’s a perfect example of how the most basic ingredients can come together to create something extraordinary.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make authentic Spanish tomato bread, you need just a handful of ingredients. The key is to use fresh, high-quality items. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Bread: Traditional Spanish bread like pan de pueblo (country-style bread) or baguette works best. You want a hearty, crusty loaf that can hold up to the toppings.
  • Tomatoes: Fresh, ripe tomatoes are essential. Heirloom or vine-ripened tomatoes are ideal, as they provide a rich, juicy texture that’s perfect for the dish.
  • Garlic: A clove of garlic adds a sharp, aromatic flavor that enhances the overall taste.
  • Olive oil: Extra virgin olive oil is the best choice for this recipe. It adds a fruity, slightly peppery flavor that balances the richness of the tomatoes and garlic.
  • Salt: A pinch of good quality salt helps bring out the sweetness of the tomatoes.
  • Optional toppings: For an extra touch of flavor, you can also add toppings such as fresh basil, a drizzle of balsamic vinegar, or even some thinly sliced cured ham.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Spanish Tomato Bread

Making Spanish tomato bread is as easy as it sounds. Follow these simple steps to prepare this delicious Spanish treat:

1. Prepare the Bread

Start by selecting a good-quality loaf of bread. The bread should have a firm, crusty exterior with a soft, airy interior. If you’re using a baguette or any other long loaf, slice it into pieces about half an inch thick. You can also use smaller pieces of bread if you prefer individual servings.

Toast the bread: Heat a grill pan or your oven to medium-high heat. Lightly brush the bread slices with olive oil and toast them until they are golden brown and crispy. If you’re using an oven, bake the slices for about 5-7 minutes or until they are lightly crisped. The goal is to achieve a crunchy surface that can hold up to the juicy tomatoes without becoming soggy.

2. Prepare the Tomatoes

Once your bread is toasted to perfection, it’s time to prepare the tomatoes. You want to use ripe, juicy tomatoes for the best flavor. Cut the tomatoes in half and use a grater to grate the flesh into a bowl. This will help extract all the juices and pulp while leaving the skins behind.

If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can finely chop the tomatoes instead of grating them. However, grating them allows the juice to infuse the bread more easily, creating a deliciously moist base.

3. Add Garlic and Olive Oil

After grating the tomatoes, rub a clove of garlic onto the warm toasted bread. The heat of the bread will release the garlic’s natural oils, giving the bread a mild, aromatic garlic flavor. You can also lightly crush the garlic and rub it if you prefer a more intense flavor.

Next, drizzle extra virgin olive oil over the toasted bread. Use about 1-2 teaspoons per slice, depending on your preference. The oil adds richness and depth to the dish, enhancing the overall taste of the tomato.

4. Top with Grated Tomatoes

Now it’s time to pile on the grated tomatoes. Spoon the tomato pulp generously over the toasted bread, making sure to cover every inch of the surface. The juicy, ripe tomatoes should seep into the bread slightly, giving each bite a burst of flavor.

5. Season with Salt

Sprinkle a pinch of sea salt over the tomatoes. The salt will draw out the natural sweetness of the tomatoes and elevate the overall flavor. You can also add some freshly ground black pepper if you like a bit of heat.

6. Optional Toppings

While traditional pan con tomate is simply made with tomatoes, olive oil, and salt, there are many variations of this dish that add extra flavor. You can top your tomato bread with:

  • Fresh basil: A few fresh basil leaves will add a lovely herbal note that complements the tomatoes.
  • Cured ham: Thin slices of jamón serrano or Iberian ham can be added for a savory, salty touch that pairs beautifully with the sweetness of the tomatoes.
  • Balsamic vinegar: A drizzle of balsamic vinegar can provide a tangy contrast to the sweetness of the tomatoes and olive oil.
  • Cheese: Shavings of Manchego or another Spanish cheese can take this dish to the next level, adding richness and depth.

7. Serve and Enjoy

Once your Spanish tomato bread is ready, serve it immediately. Pan con tomate is best enjoyed while the bread is still warm, with the juices from the tomatoes soaking into the crispy toast. It’s a simple yet satisfying dish that is perfect as a light meal or snack.

Tips for Making the Perfect Spanish Tomato Bread

While making pan con tomate is straightforward, a few tips can help ensure you get the most out of this delicious dish.

Use the Right Tomatoes

The quality of your tomatoes will significantly impact the flavor of your pan con tomate. Choose tomatoes that are ripe and juicy, as these will create the most flavorful base. Avoid using bland, watery tomatoes as they won’t give you the same depth of flavor.

Toast the Bread Properly

The bread should be toasted just enough to make it crispy but not so much that it becomes too hard to eat. You want the bread to have a good crunch without being overly dry. Brushing the bread with olive oil before toasting adds extra flavor and ensures the bread doesn’t dry out.

Don’t Skip the Garlic

Garlic plays a crucial role in enhancing the overall flavor of the bread. Rubbing the garlic onto the warm toast will give it a subtle, aromatic taste that elevates the dish. If you prefer a milder flavor, you can always reduce the amount of garlic or even use roasted garlic for a sweeter taste.

Experiment with Toppings

While traditional pan con tomate is simple, there’s no harm in experimenting with additional toppings. A sprinkle of red pepper flakes, some fresh herbs, or even a drizzle of good-quality balsamic vinegar can add new dimensions to this dish.

Variations of Spanish Tomato Bread

There are several variations of pan con tomate, depending on the region of Spain. Here are a few popular variations:

Pan con Tomate y Jamón

This variation adds thin slices of jamón serrano or jamón ibérico to the bread. The rich, salty ham perfectly complements the fresh tomato, making this version more filling and indulgent.

Pan con Tomate y Queso

In this version, the toasted bread is topped with grated Manchego cheese or another Spanish cheese, such as Idiazábal. The cheese adds a creamy, savory flavor that balances the acidity of the tomatoes.

Pan con Tomate al Horno

In this variation, the bread is topped with tomatoes and olive oil, then baked in the oven for a few minutes. This results in a warm, roasted tomato flavor that adds depth and complexity to the dish.

Pan con Tomate con Aceite de Trufa

For an extra indulgent touch, drizzle the toast with truffle oil instead of regular olive oil. This variation adds a luxurious earthy flavor that elevates the dish to a whole new level.

Pairing Spanish Tomato Bread with Drinks

While pan con tomate is delicious on its own, it also pairs wonderfully with a variety of drinks. Here are a few suggestions for perfect pairings:

  • Red wine: A glass of Spanish red wine, such as Rioja or Tempranillo, pairs beautifully with the rich flavors of the bread and tomatoes.
  • Sherry: A dry sherry, like Fino or Amontillado, complements the dish’s savory flavors without overwhelming the palate.
  • Cerveza: A cold Spanish lager or ale can provide a refreshing contrast to the rich, savory flavors of the pan con tomate.
  • Fresh juice: For a non-alcoholic option, fresh orange juice or a light cucumber cooler can add a refreshing balance to the dish.

Conclusion

Spanish tomato bread is a quintessential dish that highlights the simplicity and beauty of Mediterranean ingredients. With just a few basic elements—bread, tomatoes, olive oil, and garlic—you can create a dish that bursts with flavor and is perfect for any occasion. Whether you enjoy it as a snack, a side dish, or a light meal, pan con tomate is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So next time you’re craving something fresh, savory, and delicious, try making this easy and satisfying Spanish classic!
