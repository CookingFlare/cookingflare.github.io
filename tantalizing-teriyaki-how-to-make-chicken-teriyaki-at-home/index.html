Tantalizing Teriyaki: How to Make Chicken Teriyaki at Home


Teriyaki is a classic Japanese dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people all around the world. The combination of sweet and savory flavors, along with the tender and juicy chicken, makes it a truly tantalizing dish. The good news is, you don’t have to visit a restaurant to enjoy this delectable dish. With a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can make chicken teriyaki right in the comfort of your own kitchen. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

To make chicken teriyaki, you will need:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1/4 cup sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the marinade. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Set aside.

  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over them. Make sure the chicken is evenly coated. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, but ideally for 2 hours or overnight. This will allow the flavors to penetrate the chicken and make it extra delicious.

  3. Once the chicken has marinated, remove it from the fridge and let it sit at room temperature for about 10 minutes. This will ensure that the chicken cooks evenly.

  4. In a small bowl, mix together the cornstarch and water until smooth. This will help thicken the teriyaki sauce later.

  5. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off, and place it in the skillet. Cook the chicken for about 6-7 minutes on each side, or until it is cooked through and nicely browned.

  6. While the chicken is cooking, pour the remaining marinade into a small saucepan and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 5 minutes. Add the cornstarch mixture to the saucepan and stir until the sauce thickens. Remove from heat.

  7. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the skillet and let it rest for a few minutes. Then, slice it into thin strips.

  8. Serve the chicken teriyaki over a bed of steamed rice or alongside some stir-fried vegetables. Drizzle the thickened teriyaki sauce over the chicken and garnish with some sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an extra touch of flavor and visual appeal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic chicken teriyaki, you can add some sliced bell peppers, onions, or even pineapple chunks to the skillet along with the chicken. This will add a burst of color and extra flavor to the dish.

  • If you prefer a spicier teriyaki sauce, you can add a teaspoon of sriracha or chili flakes to the marinade.

  • To make this dish vegetarian, you can substitute the chicken with tofu or tempeh. Simply follow the same steps, but adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • If you’re looking for a healthier option, you can use skinless chicken thighs instead of breasts. Thigh meat tends to be juicier and more flavorful.

Now that you have the recipe for homemade chicken teriyaki, you can impress your family and friends with this delicious dish. It’s a perfect choice for a quick weeknight dinner or a special gathering. So put on your apron, gather the ingredients, and get ready to create a tantalizing teriyaki experience right in your own kitchen!

Note: This recipe serves 4 people. Feel free to adjust the quantities based on your needs.
