From Pizza Dough to Pasta, Nail That Perfect Italian Texture Every Time


As a long-time enthusiast of Italian cuisine, nothing quite compares to the texture of fresh, homemade pasta. The secret lies in the dough - simple yet versatile, allowing you to create an array of delicious dishes. Here is a foolproof guide to achieving that perfect Italian texture every time.

Choosing Your Ingredients

Eschew the use of a kitchen aid or stand mixer for now and gather your ingredients by hand. For every 2 cups of flour, you will need 1 large egg and 1/2 cup of water. To enrich your pasta dough, consider incorporating a pinch of salt and 2 tablespoons of olive oil. Be cautious with adding too much salt or oil, as they can affect the pasta’s texture negatively.

Preparing Your Equipment

Before diving into the dough preparation, ensure readiness with these essential tools:

  1. Large mixing bowl
  2. Work surface - wooden or marble
  3. Pasta extruder or meat tenderizer for cutting
  4. Pasta drying rack
  5. Freshwater pasta pot

Mixing the Dough

Place your flour on the work surface, creating a well in the center to accommodate your wet ingredients. Crack the egg directly into the well, followed by a generous drizzle of water mixed with a dash of olive oil and a pinch of salt. Gradually incorporate the mixture from the well into the flour by hand. Use firm circular motions to knead your dough. Avoid overworking the dough to retain its elasticity.

Once evenly mixed, cover your dough with a damp towel and allow it to rest for about twenty minutes. This allows the gluten to relax.

Shaping the Dough

After resting, divide the dough into manageable portions. Depending on the width of your pasta, a standard amount ranges from 4-6 ounces. Roll out your dough with a rolling pin, gently applying pressure from the center and working your way outward.

Transition to a pasta extruder or utilize a meat tenderizer to slice the rolled dough into desired pasta cuts. If further drying is necessary, adhere to a thin layer of pasta on a drying rack.

Cooking the Pasta to Perfection

Before cooking, ensure adequate salt in your pasta water to impart flavor evenly. An average of 4 quarts per pound of pasta should suffice, but you can adjust according to taste.

Allow your pasta to cook uncovered, under gently boiling water for one minute less than your preferred cooking time. When immersed in the water, it will continue to cook for an additional minute or two. Drain thoroughly using a colander or strainer to maintain an al dente texture.

Finally, add the pasta directly to your sauce or prepare it separately. And voila! A perfect plate of homemade Italian pasta.

Variety is the Spice of Life

The beauty of handmade pasta lies in its versatility. Experiment by adapting your dough with different flours, bean or spinach purees, eggs, and various types of cheese. Unleash your creativity and venture beyond the traditional Italian variety.

In conclusion, making perfect pasta may appear daunting, but with proper ingredients, tools, and a little elbow grease, you’ll soon be impressing family and friends alike. Buon appetito!
