Cooking with Asparagus: 7 Delicious Spring Recipes


Spring has finally arrived, bringing with it a vibrant selection of fresh produce. Among the seasonal delights, asparagus stands out as a culinary gem, celebrated for its unique flavor, versatility, and health benefits. Rich in vitamins A, C, E, and K, as well as fiber and antioxidants, asparagus is not just a delicious addition to any meal; it’s also a powerhouse of nutrition. Whether grilled, roasted, or incorporated into salads, this tender green vegetable can elevate any dish. In this blog post, we’ll explore seven scrumptious asparagus recipes that will ignite your spring cooking and showcase the beauty of this seasonal ingredient.

1. Lemon Garlic Roasted Asparagus

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the asparagus, olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Toss until the asparagus is evenly coated.
  3. Spread the asparagus in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 15-20 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender and slightly crispy.
  5. For an extra touch of flavor, sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the asparagus before serving.

Chef’s Tip:

For a smoky flavor, consider adding a dash of smoked paprika to the mix before roasting.

2. Asparagus and Goat Cheese Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup asparagus, chopped
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup fresh chives, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
  3. In an oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped asparagus and sauté for 3-5 minutes until tender.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the asparagus and cook for 2-3 minutes until the edges begin to set.
  5. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese and fresh chives over the top.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the frittata is fully set and golden brown.
  7. Let cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Chef’s Tip:

Feel free to add other vegetables such as bell peppers or spinach for a heartier frittata.

3. Asparagus and Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water and drain.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine quinoa and water or vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, for 15 minutes or until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let sit for 5 minutes.
  3. While the quinoa is cooking, bring a small pot of water to a boil. Blanch the asparagus pieces for 2-3 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, blanched asparagus, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and feta cheese.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.

Chef’s Tip:

This salad can be served warm or chilled, making it a great option for meal prep.

4. Creamy Asparagus Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed and chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  2. Add the chopped asparagus and season with salt and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes until the asparagus begins to soften.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk and return to heat until warmed through.
  5. Adjust seasoning as needed before serving.

Chef’s Tip:

For added depth, sauté a few slices of potato with the onions for a thicker soup.

5. Grilled Asparagus with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place asparagus on the grill and cook for 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and grill marks appear.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic glaze before serving.

Chef’s Tip:

For a more intense flavor, marinate the asparagus in the olive oil and balsamic glaze for an hour before grilling.

6. Asparagus and Mushroom Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil and sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and asparagus to the skillet, cooking for 5-7 minutes until tender.
  4. Stir in the soy sauce, salt, and pepper, cooking for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with sesame seeds if desired.

Chef’s Tip:

Serve this stir-fry over rice or noodles for a complete meal.

7. Asparagus and Prosciutto Flatbread

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made flatbread or pizza dough
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 4 ounces prosciutto, torn into pieces
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Roll out your flatbread or pizza dough on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Brush the dough with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Spread the ricotta cheese evenly over the dough, followed by the asparagus and prosciutto.
  5. Sprinkle the mozzarella cheese on top.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the edges are golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  7. Let cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Chef’s Tip:

Top with fresh arugula or a drizzle of olive oil before serving for an extra layer of flavor.

Asparagus is not only a versatile ingredient but also an excellent way to introduce fresh flavors to your meals this spring. From savory frittatas to vibrant salads and hearty soups, these seven recipes showcase the best of asparagus. So, roll up your sleeves, head to the farmers’ market, and enjoy the bounty of springtime cooking with these delightful dishes. Happy cooking!
