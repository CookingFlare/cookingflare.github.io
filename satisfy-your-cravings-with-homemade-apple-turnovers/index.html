Satisfy Your Cravings with Homemade Apple Turnovers


There’s something undeniably comforting about biting into a warm, flaky pastry filled with sweet, spiced apples. Apple turnovers are a delightful treat that can be enjoyed any time of the year. This blog post will guide you through the process of making homemade apple turnovers, from choosing the right apples to perfecting your pastry dough. Get ready to satisfy your cravings with this delicious recipe that will impress friends and family alike!

Why You’ll Love Homemade Apple Turnovers

Homemade apple turnovers not only taste better than store-bought varieties, but they also give you the freedom to customize the filling to your liking. You can control the sweetness, spice levels, and even the type of pastry dough used. Plus, making them from scratch is a rewarding experience that allows you to connect with the ingredients and the process.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To create the perfect apple turnovers, gather the following ingredients:

For the Pastry Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Apple Filling:

  • 4 medium-sized apples (Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or a mix)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust based on the sweetness of the apples)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon milk (for egg wash)
  • Extra sugar for sprinkling

Choosing the Right Apples

The type of apple you choose plays a crucial role in the success of your apple turnovers. For the best flavor and texture, opt for a mix of tart and sweet apples. Granny Smith apples provide a nice tartness that balances well with sugar, while Honeycrisp apples add sweetness and juiciness. Other great options include Fuji, Braeburn, or Jonagold. Aim for a combination that suits your personal taste preferences.

Preparing the Pastry Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and sugar until well combined.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. You want to see small pea-sized pieces of butter throughout the mixture.

  3. Add Ice Water: Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently until the dough begins to come together. Be careful not to overmix; the dough should be slightly crumbly but hold together when pressed.

  4. Chill the Dough: Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Chilling the dough helps to solidify the butter, resulting in a flakier pastry.

Making the Apple Filling

  1. Prepare the Apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples into small cubes. Aim for uniform pieces to ensure even cooking.

  2. Mix the Filling: In a large bowl, combine the apple cubes, sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg (if using), cornstarch, and vanilla extract. Toss everything together until the apples are well coated.

  3. Cook the Filling: For an extra layer of flavor, you can cook the apple filling on the stovetop. In a skillet over medium heat, add the apple mixture and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the apples begin to soften. Remove from heat and let it cool.

Assembling the Turnovers

  1. Roll Out the Dough: Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disk of dough to about 1/8 inch thick. Use a sharp knife or pizza cutter to cut the dough into squares or triangles, approximately 4-6 inches in size.

  2. Fill the Pastry: Place a generous spoonful of the apple filling in the center of each square or triangle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the turnovers to burst during baking.

  3. Seal the Edges: Fold the pastry over the filling to create a triangle or rectangle shape. Press the edges together firmly with your fingers, and then use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative touch. Make sure to poke a few holes in the top of each turnover to allow steam to escape.

  4. Prepare for Baking: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  5. Egg Wash: In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and milk to create an egg wash. Brush the tops of the turnovers with the egg wash and sprinkle with extra sugar for added sweetness and crunch.

Baking Your Turnovers

  1. Bake: Place the assembled turnovers on the prepared baking sheet, leaving space between each one. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until they are golden brown and flaky.

  2. Cool: Once baked, remove the turnovers from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for a few minutes. This will help them set and prevent the filling from spilling out.

Enjoying Your Homemade Apple Turnovers

Homemade apple turnovers are best enjoyed warm, fresh out of the oven. They can be served as a delightful dessert or a sweet breakfast treat. Pair them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an indulgent experience. You can also dust them with powdered sugar for an elegant touch.

Storing and Freezing Turnovers

If you’re lucky enough to have leftovers (though they’re unlikely!), you can store your apple turnovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. To keep them fresh for longer, consider freezing them before baking. Simply assemble the turnovers, place them on a baking sheet, and freeze until solid. Once frozen, transfer them to a freezer-safe bag or container. When you’re ready to bake, no need to thaw! Just bake them straight from the freezer, adding a few extra minutes to the baking time.

Variations to Try

While the classic apple turnover is delightful, feel free to experiment with different fillings and flavors:

  • Mixed Fruit Turnovers: Combine apples with berries or pears for a fruity twist.
  • Spiced Pumpkin Filling: Substitute pumpkin puree and spices for a seasonal variation.
  • Nutty Add-ins: Incorporate chopped nuts like pecans or walnuts for added texture and flavor.
  • Cheese Fillings: Try a cream cheese filling with cinnamon for a rich and creamy dessert.

Conclusion

Homemade apple turnovers are a delightful way to satisfy your sweet tooth and impress your loved ones. With their flaky pastry and warm apple filling, they embody the essence of comfort food. The process of making them from scratch is not only enjoyable but also allows for creativity and personalization. Whether you choose to stick with the classic recipe or explore new variations, these turnovers are sure to become a beloved treat in your household.

So, roll up your sleeves and gather your ingredients—it’s time to embark on a delicious journey of baking homemade apple turnovers that will warm your heart and satisfy your cravings!
