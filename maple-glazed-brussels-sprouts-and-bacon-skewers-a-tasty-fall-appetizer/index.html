Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts and Bacon Skewers: A Tasty Fall Appetizer


Fall is a season of warmth, comfort, and delicious flavors. And what better way to embrace the spirit of autumn than with a delectable and easy-to-make appetizer that embodies the essence of the season? These Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts and Bacon Skewers are the perfect combination of savory and sweet, making them an ideal dish to serve at fall gatherings, holiday parties, or even just as a cozy night in treat. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create this delightful appetizer that will surely impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 8 slices of bacon, cut into halves
  • 1/4 cup of pure maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Prepare the Brussels Sprouts: In a large bowl, toss the halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper until they are evenly coated.

  3. Assemble the Skewers: Take a half slice of bacon and thread it onto a wooden skewer, followed by a Brussels sprout half. Repeat this process, alternating between bacon and Brussels sprouts, until the skewer is filled. Place the assembled skewers on the prepared baking sheet.

  4. Make the Glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup and balsamic vinegar. Brush this glaze generously over the assembled skewers, ensuring that each piece is coated.

  5. Bake the Skewers: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the bacon is crispy and the Brussels sprouts are tender, turning the skewers once halfway through the cooking time.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the skewers are cooked to perfection, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Arrange the skewers on a serving platter and drizzle any remaining glaze over the top. These Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts and Bacon Skewers are best enjoyed warm, so serve them immediately and watch them disappear!

Tips and Tricks

  • To make this appetizer vegetarian-friendly, simply substitute the bacon with strips of smoked tofu or tempeh for a smoky, meat-free alternative.
  • Experiment with different glaze variations by adding a pinch of cayenne pepper for a spicy kick or a sprinkle of smoked paprika for an extra layer of flavor.
  • If you’re short on time, you can prepare the skewers in advance and refrigerate them until you’re ready to bake. This makes them a convenient option for entertaining.

Embrace the Fall Flavors

The combination of caramelized Brussels sprouts, smoky bacon, and the sweet, earthy notes of maple syrup in these skewers is a true celebration of fall flavors. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply indulging in a cozy night at home, these Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts and Bacon Skewers are sure to be a hit. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the warmth of the season infuse your kitchen as you create this delightful appetizer. Your taste buds and your guests will thank you!
