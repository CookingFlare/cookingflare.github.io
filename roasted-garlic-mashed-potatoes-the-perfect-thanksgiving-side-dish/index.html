Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes: The Perfect Thanksgiving Side Dish


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and, of course, indulging in a delicious feast. While the turkey often takes center stage, the side dishes are equally important in creating a memorable meal. One dish that never fails to impress is the classic Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes. Creamy, flavorful, and oh-so-comforting, this side dish is sure to be a hit at your Thanksgiving table.

The Magic of Roasted Garlic

The secret to elevating your mashed potatoes to a whole new level lies in the addition of roasted garlic. Roasting garlic brings out its natural sweetness and imparts a rich, nutty flavor that pairs perfectly with the creamy potatoes. The process is simple, yet the results are extraordinary.

To roast garlic, start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Take a whole head of garlic and slice off the top to expose the cloves. Place the garlic on a sheet of aluminum foil and drizzle it with olive oil, ensuring that each clove is coated. Wrap the garlic tightly in the foil and place it in the oven for about 45 minutes, or until the cloves are soft and golden brown. Once roasted, the garlic cloves will be soft and spreadable, ready to infuse your mashed potatoes with their irresistible flavor.

The Perfect Mashed Potatoes

Now that you have your roasted garlic ready, it’s time to create the perfect mashed potatoes. For this recipe, you will need:

  • 4 pounds (1.8 kg) of Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 cup (240 ml) of whole milk
  • ½ cup (113 g) of unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 head of roasted garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Start by placing the peeled and cut potatoes in a large pot of salted water. Bring the water to a boil and cook the potatoes until they are tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.

In a small saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it is warm but not boiling. Add the softened butter to the warm milk and stir until it melts completely.

Now, it’s time to add the star ingredient – the roasted garlic. Squeeze the cloves out of the roasted garlic head and add them to the potatoes. Using a potato masher or a fork, mash the potatoes and garlic together until they are smooth and well combined.

Slowly pour the warm milk and butter mixture into the mashed potatoes, stirring as you go. The milk will help create a creamy texture while the butter adds richness. Continue mashing until the desired consistency is reached. Some prefer their mashed potatoes smooth and silky, while others enjoy a slightly chunky texture. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and give it a final stir to ensure all the flavors are evenly distributed.

Serving Suggestions

Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes are incredibly versatile and can complement a wide range of Thanksgiving dishes. Here are a few serving suggestions to inspire you:

  • Serve alongside a perfectly roasted turkey, drizzled with homemade gravy.
  • Pair with roasted vegetables, such as Brussels sprouts or carrots, for a colorful and nutritious plate.
  • Create a classic shepherd’s pie by layering the mashed potatoes with cooked ground meat and vegetables, then baking until golden brown.
  • For a vegetarian option, top the mashed potatoes with sautéed mushrooms and caramelized onions, then bake until bubbly and golden.

No matter how you choose to serve them, one thing is for certain – these Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes will steal the show and have everyone coming back for seconds.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, treat your loved ones to a side dish that is both comforting and full of flavor. With its creamy texture and the irresistible aroma of roasted garlic, these mashed potatoes will make your holiday feast truly memorable. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create the perfect Thanksgiving side dish. Your taste buds will thank you!
