Flavorful Dairy-Free Thai Green Curry Recipe


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine but are looking for a dairy-free alternative to the traditional green curry, you’ve come to the right place. This flavorful and aromatic Thai green curry recipe is not only dairy-free but also packs a punch of authentic Thai flavors that will tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or new to the world of Thai cooking, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your culinary repertoire.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp green curry paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 block firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 2-3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 1-2 Thai red chili peppers, sliced (optional for extra heat)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tofu:

    • Start by draining the tofu and pressing it between paper towels to remove excess moisture.
    • Once drained, cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics:

    • Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
    • Add the green curry paste to the skillet and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Add the Vegetables and Tofu:

    • Add the sliced red and yellow bell peppers to the skillet and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
    • Next, add the cubed tofu and snap peas to the skillet, continuing to stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes.

  4. Simmer with Coconut Milk:

    • Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, stirring to combine all the ingredients.
    • Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Season and Garnish:

    • Add the soy sauce, brown sugar, and kaffir lime leaves to the curry, stirring well.
    • Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning with salt as needed.
    • Just before serving, stir in the Thai basil leaves and sliced Thai red chili peppers for an extra kick of heat.

  6. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Remove the curry from the heat and discard the kaffir lime leaves.
    • Serve the dairy-free Thai green curry over steamed jasmine rice or rice noodles, garnishing with additional Thai basil leaves and chili slices if desired.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a protein boost, you can substitute the tofu with seitan, tempeh, or your choice of protein.
  • If you prefer a thicker curry, you can reduce the amount of vegetable broth or simmer the curry for a longer duration.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetable selection based on your preferences and seasonal availability.

Final Thoughts:

This dairy-free Thai green curry recipe offers a delightful balance of creamy coconut milk, aromatic herbs, and zesty flavors that are characteristic of Thai cuisine. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a comforting and exotic meal, this recipe is a wonderful way to bring the vibrant flavors of Thailand to your own kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure that will transport you to the bustling streets of Bangkok with every savory spoonful.
