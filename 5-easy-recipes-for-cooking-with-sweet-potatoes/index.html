5 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Sweet Potatoes


Sweet potatoes are a nutritional powerhouse and a versatile ingredient that can elevate your meals. Whether you’re looking for a comforting side dish, a filling main course, or a healthy snack, sweet potatoes can fit the bill. Their natural sweetness and creamy texture make them suitable for both savory and sweet dishes. Here, we’ll explore five easy recipes that celebrate the wonderful flavors of sweet potatoes, perfect for any occasion.

1. Sweet Potato Mash

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (or olive oil for a vegan option)
  • 1/4 cup milk (or almond milk)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A pinch of cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sweet Potatoes: Start by peeling the sweet potatoes and cutting them into uniform chunks. This ensures even cooking.

  2. Boil: Place the sweet potato chunks into a large pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat and let them simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until tender.

  3. Drain: Once the sweet potatoes are cooked, drain the water and return them to the pot.

  4. Mash: Add butter and milk to the sweet potatoes and mash them using a potato masher or fork until you reach your desired consistency. For a creamier texture, use an electric mixer.

  5. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste. If you want a hint of warmth, sprinkle in a pinch of cinnamon.

  6. Serve: This sweet potato mash makes a delightful side dish, perfect alongside roasted meats or as part of a vegetarian meal.

2. Baked Sweet Potato Fries

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper for easy cleanup.

  2. Cut Sweet Potatoes: Wash and peel the sweet potatoes (if desired). Cut them into even-sized sticks, resembling fries.

  3. Season: In a large bowl, toss the sweet potato sticks with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.

  4. Bake: Spread the fries in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet. Bake for about 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are golden and crispy.

  5. Serve: These baked sweet potato fries are a healthier alternative to regular fries and pair wonderfully with dipping sauces like ketchup or aioli.

3. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 large sweet potato, diced
  • 1 can black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Avocado, diced (for topping)
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Cook Sweet Potatoes: In a large skillet, heat a splash of olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced sweet potato and season with cumin and chili powder. Cook for about 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sweet potatoes are tender.

  2. Add Black Beans: Stir in the black beans and cook until heated through, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat.

  3. Warm Tortillas: In a separate skillet, warm the corn tortillas over medium heat for about 30 seconds on each side until they are pliable.

  4. Assemble Tacos: Fill each tortilla with the sweet potato and black bean mixture. Top with diced avocado and fresh cilantro.

  5. Serve: Serve these delicious tacos with lime wedges on the side for a burst of fresh flavor.

4. Sweet Potato Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Optional toppings: Greek yogurt or coconut cream, chives, or pumpkin seeds

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add Sweet Potatoes: Stir in the diced sweet potatoes, ginger, and turmeric. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.

  3. Simmer: Pour in the vegetable broth, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the sweet potatoes are tender.

  4. Blend: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer it in batches to a blender. Be cautious with hot liquids!

  5. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and top with a dollop of Greek yogurt or coconut cream, and a sprinkle of chives or pumpkin seeds for added texture.

5. Sweet Potato Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mashed sweet potatoes (about 1 large sweet potato)
  • 1/2 cup flour (whole wheat or all-purpose)
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk (or plant-based milk)
  • 1 egg (or flax egg for vegan option)
  • Butter or oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Sweet Potatoes: If you don’t have mashed sweet potatoes ready, bake or steam a sweet potato until tender, then mash it.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.

  3. Combine Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together the mashed sweet potatoes, milk, and egg until smooth.

  4. Combine Mixtures: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are fine.

  5. Cook Pancakes: Heat a skillet over medium heat and add a little butter or oil. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown on both sides.

  6. Serve: Serve warm with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, yogurt, or fresh fruit.

Conclusion

Sweet potatoes are more than just a seasonal favorite; they are a versatile ingredient that can be used in various dishes throughout the year. From savory tacos to sweet pancakes, these recipes are simple and delicious. Incorporating sweet potatoes into your meals not only enhances flavor but also adds a nutritious boost to your diet. Try these five easy recipes, and enjoy the delightful taste and health benefits of sweet potatoes in your kitchen!
