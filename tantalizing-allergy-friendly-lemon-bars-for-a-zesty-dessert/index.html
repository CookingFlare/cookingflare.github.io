Tantalizing Allergy-Friendly Lemon Bars for a Zesty Dessert


If you’re a fan of citrusy desserts but have dietary restrictions or food allergies, you’ll be thrilled to discover these tantalizing allergy-friendly lemon bars. Bursting with zesty flavor and a delightful balance of sweet and tart, these lemon bars are not only delicious but also free from common allergens such as gluten, dairy, and nuts. Whether you have dietary restrictions or simply want to try a new twist on a classic dessert, these allergy-friendly lemon bars are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 cup gluten-free flour blend
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free butter, melted

For the lemon filling:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup gluten-free flour blend
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 3 large eggs

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend and powdered sugar for the crust. Gradually pour in the melted dairy-free butter and stir until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Press the crust mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake for 15 minutes or until lightly golden brown.

  4. While the crust is baking, prepare the lemon filling. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, and salt. Add in the freshly squeezed lemon juice and lemon zest, followed by the eggs. Whisk until well combined and smooth.

  5. Pour the lemon filling over the hot crust and return the pan to the oven. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.

  6. Remove the pan from the oven and let the lemon bars cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the bars to fully set.

  7. Once chilled, carefully lift the bars out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang. Place them on a cutting board and slice into squares or rectangles.

  8. Serve the allergy-friendly lemon bars chilled, garnished with a dusting of powdered sugar or a dollop of dairy-free whipped cream, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For added texture and flavor, consider adding a handful of shredded coconut or chopped almonds to the crust mixture.
  • To make these lemon bars vegan-friendly, substitute the eggs with a suitable egg replacer or try using applesauce or mashed bananas as a binding agent.
  • Experiment with different citrus fruits such as limes or oranges to create unique variations of these allergy-friendly bars.

These tantalizing allergy-friendly lemon bars are the perfect dessert for those with dietary restrictions or food allergies, as well as anyone who appreciates a zesty and refreshing treat. Enjoy them as a summertime dessert, a sweet indulgence after a meal, or even as a delightful addition to a brunch spread. With their tangy lemon flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture, these bars are sure to become a new favorite in your repertoire of allergy-friendly recipes. Give them a try and discover a whole new world of delicious, allergy-friendly desserts!
