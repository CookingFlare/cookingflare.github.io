Exploring the Flavors of Vietnam: 6 Pho Recipes You Must Try


Vietnamese cuisine is renowned for its rich, vibrant flavors and meticulous balance of taste, texture, and aroma. Among its most celebrated dishes, pho stands out as a national treasure — a dish that embodies the heart and soul of Vietnam. This iconic noodle soup, traditionally enjoyed any time of day, features a harmonious blend of savory broth, rice noodles, fresh herbs, and protein toppings, offering endless possibilities for customization.

Pho is much more than a meal; it’s a culinary experience steeped in history and culture. Its preparation is an art, requiring attention to detail and the use of high-quality ingredients. In this culinary journey, let’s explore six distinct pho recipes that pay homage to the diversity of this beloved dish. Whether you’re a seasoned home chef or a novice cook, these recipes are sure to inspire your next culinary adventure.

1. Traditional Pho Bo (Beef Pho)

Pho Bo is the quintessential beef noodle soup that captures the essence of Vietnamese cooking. This warm and nourishing dish features a clear, aromatic broth infused with the flavors of spices such as star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ lbs beef bones (marrow or knuckle bones preferred)
  • 1 lb beef brisket, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, halved
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced lengthwise
  • 5-star anise pods
  • 3 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro, Thai basil, and green onions
  • Bean sprouts, lime wedges, and chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Roast the beef bones, onion, and ginger in a preheated oven at 375°F for about 20 minutes until fragrant and slightly charred.
  2. Simmer the roasted bones, onion, and ginger in a large pot of water for 4-6 hours, skimming off any impurities.
  3. Toast the spices (star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom) in a dry pan until aromatic, then add them to the broth.
  4. Strain the broth, season with fish sauce and sugar, and adjust to taste.
  5. To serve, assemble cooked rice noodles in a bowl, top with thinly sliced beef brisket, and pour the hot broth over. Garnish with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime, and chili.

This classic recipe is a masterclass in simplicity and depth, offering a comforting dish that’s hard to resist.

2. Pho Ga (Chicken Pho)

Pho Ga is a lighter, yet equally flavorful alternative to beef pho. It’s especially beloved for its soothing broth, which carries the essence of poached chicken and vibrant herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4 lbs), cleaned
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 4-inch piece of ginger, sliced lengthwise
  • 3-star anise pods
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro, Thai basil, and green onions
  • Lime wedges and chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Poach the whole chicken in a large pot of water, adding the onion, ginger, and spices midway through the cooking process.
  2. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and shred the meat, reserving the bones for further simmering.
  3. Strain the broth and season with fish sauce and sugar.
  4. Assemble cooked rice noodles in a bowl, top with shredded chicken, and ladle hot broth over everything.
  5. Garnish generously with herbs, lime, and chili.

Perfect for a quick weeknight meal, Pho Ga offers a delicate yet satisfying experience.

3. Pho Chay (Vegetarian Pho)

For plant-forward diners, Pho Chay provides all the depth and complexity of traditional pho, sans the meat. Packed with fresh vegetables and tofu, it’s a nourishing and guilt-free option.

Ingredients:

  • 1-onion, halved
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced lengthwise
  • 5-star anise pods
  • 3-cinnamon sticks
  • 4-cloves
  • 8 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 block of firm tofu, cubed and pan-fried
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles
  • Vegetables: mushrooms, carrots, bok choy, and bean sprouts
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro and Thai basil
  • Lime wedges and chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Char the onion and ginger under the broiler, then simmer with the spices in vegetable broth for about 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the broth and season with soy sauce and sugar.
  3. Sauté vegetables and tofu until tender and golden.
  4. Combine noodles, sautéed vegetables, and tofu in a bowl. Pour over the hot broth.
  5. Add fresh herbs, lime wedges, and chili as desired.

Pho Chay is a celebration of fresh, wholesome ingredients that showcases pho’s versatility.

4. Pho Tai (Rare Beef Pho)

For those craving a more delicate beef pho, Pho Tai features thinly sliced rare beef that gently cooks in the steaming hot broth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ lbs beef bones
  • 1 lb eye-round steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion
  • 3-inch piece of ginger
  • 5-star anise pods
  • 3 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro, Thai basil, and green onions
  • Lime wedges and chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the broth using beef bones, roasted onion, ginger, and spices as in the Pho Bo recipe.
  2. To serve, place uncooked slices of steak atop the cooked noodles in each bowl, then pour steaming hot broth over to cook the beef.
  3. Finish with fresh herbs, lime, and chili.

The tender, slightly rare beef in Pho Tai makes it a favorite for those who love bold, authentic flavors.

5. Pho Seafood

Seafood pho adds a coastal twist to the traditional recipe, featuring shrimp, squid, and fresh fish that create a light, briny broth.

Ingredients:

  • 8 cups chicken or seafood broth
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 3-inch piece of ginger, sliced lengthwise
  • 2-star anise pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 lb seafood mix (shrimp, squid, fish fillets)
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro and Thai basil
  • Lime wedges and chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Simmer the onion, ginger, and spices in the chicken/seafood broth for 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the broth, then season with fish sauce and sugar.
  3. Add seafood mix to the hot broth, cooking only until just tender.
  4. Assemble cooked noodles in a bowl, add seafood, and pour over the broth.
  5. Garnish to your liking with herbs, lime, and chili.

This unique take on pho is perfect for seafood enthusiasts.

6. Pho Sate (Spicy Pho)

A fiery variation for heat lovers, Pho Sate adds a spicy kick with Sriracha and chili oil, turning the comforting bowl of pho into an exhilarating flavor adventure.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ lbs beef bones
  • 1 lb beef brisket, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion
  • 3-inch piece of ginger
  • 5-star anise pods
  • 3 cinnamon sticks
  • 2 tbsp chili oil
  • 2 tbsp Sriracha
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 lb fresh rice noodles
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro, Thai basil, and green onions
  • Lime wedges and chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Make the broth following the Pho Bo method.
  2. Stir in chili oil, Sriracha, and adjust spice levels to your preference.
  3. Serve with noodles, sliced beef brisket, and a touch of spicy broth. Garnish with herbs, lime, and additional chili for extra heat.

Pho Sate brings the heat while maintaining the comforting essence of Vietnamese cuisine.

These six pho recipes offer a glimpse into the incredible diversity of this beloved dish. From the robust flavors of Pho Bo to the spicy excitement of Pho Sate or the meat-free indulgence of Pho Chay, there’s a bowl for every palate. Now it’s your turn to embark on this flavorful journey — explore, experiment, and discover your favorite take on pho in the comfort of your kitchen!
