10 Simple Canning Recipes to Stock Your Pantry


When it comes to preserving food, canning has remained one of the most reliable methods for generations. Not only does it allow you to make the most of seasonal produce, but it also ensures that your pantry is filled with delicious, homemade options year-round. Whether you’re looking to save money, eat healthier, or simply enjoy the satisfaction of creating your own preserves, these 10 simple canning recipes are the perfect way to get started.

1. Classic Strawberry Jam

Strawberry jam is a staple for any pantry, and making it yourself is surprisingly simple. Start by washing and hulling fresh strawberries, then mash them slightly to release their juices. Combine them with sugar and a touch of lemon juice in a large pot and simmer until thickened. To ensure a proper set, use pectin as indicated in your recipe. Ladle the hot jam into sterilized jars, leaving a little headspace, and process in a water bath for 10 minutes. This sweet treat is perfect for spreading on toast, baking in pastries, or even adding to yogurt.

2. Pickled Cucumbers

Homemade pickles are both crunchy and tangy, offering a perfect side dish or snack. Prepare your cucumbers by slicing them vertically or into rounds. For the brine, mix vinegar, water, sugar, and your favorite blend of spices (like dill, garlic, and mustard seeds) in a pot and bring the mixture to a boil. Pack the cucumber slices tightly into sterilized jars, pour the hot brine over the top, and seal them. Process jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. After curing for a few weeks, you’ll have delicious homemade pickles ready to enjoy.

3. Tomato Basil Sauce

If you love pasta night, a homemade tomato basil sauce is a must for your canning collection. Start by blanching and peeling fresh tomatoes, then simmer them with garlic, onions, basil, olive oil, and a touch of salt. Blend the sauce until smooth and adjust the seasoning to taste. Ladle the sauce into clean jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace, and process in a water bath to preserve the fresh flavors of summer tomatoes. This sauce pairs wonderfully with pasta, pizza, or hearty stews.

4. Peach Preserves

Capture the essence of summer with homemade peach preserves. Begin by peeling and pitting fresh peaches, then chop them into small chunks or slices. Simmer the fruit with sugar and a splash of lemon juice, stirring regularly until the mixture thickens. Add additional spices like cinnamon or nutmeg for a warm flavor twist. Pack the preserves into hot sterilized jars, leaving space at the top, then process in a boiling water bath. Peach preserves are fantastic atop pancakes, waffles, or even spooned over ice cream.

5. Green Beans in Brine

Preserved green beans are a salty snack or side dish that’s quick to prepare. Wash and trim the beans, then pack them into sterilized jars upright for a clean look. Simmer a mixture of water, vinegar, and salt to create the brine, pouring it over the beans until covered. If desired, add garlic, dill, or chili peppers for extra flavor. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. These crunchy treats can be enjoyed straight out of the jar or as an accompaniment to charcuterie boards.

6. Homemade Apple Butter

For a rich and spiced spread, apple butter is an excellent choice. Cook peeled, cored, and chopped apples until they’re softened. Purée the mixture, then simmer with sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves for several hours until it thickens into a spreadable consistency. Ladle the fragrant apple butter into sterilized jars and process them in a water bath. This deeply flavored condiment works beautifully on toast, biscuits, or as a filling for pastries.

7. Canned Salsa

Homemade salsa is a pantry essential that can jazz up any meal. Dice fresh tomatoes, peppers, onions, and cilantro, then stir in lime juice, garlic, and a pinch of salt. Simmer the mixture for 10 minutes to blend the flavors, then ladle into sterilized jars, leaving space at the top. Seal and process the jars in a boiling water bath. Whether you use it for dipping chips or topping tacos, this fresh salsa is sure to be a hit.

8. Sweet Pickled Beets

Pickled beets add a sweet and earthy flavor to salads and sides alike. Roast or boil fresh beets until tender, then peel and slice them. Make a brine with vinegar, sugar, cloves, cinnamon sticks, and a pinch of salt. Pack the beets into sterilized jars and pour the hot brine over them. Seal the jars and process in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. The vibrant color and tangy sweetness of these pickled treats make them irresistible.

9. Chicken Stock

Canning homemade chicken stock is a brilliant way to ensure you always have a flavorful base for soups, stews, and sauces on hand. Start by simmering chicken bones, vegetables like carrots and celery, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns in water for several hours. Skim off the fat and strain the stock before ladling it into sterilized jars. Pressure canning is essential for safely preserving low-acid foods like stock, so follow your pressure canner’s instructions closely.

10. Spiced Pears

For a dessert-ready preserve, spiced pears are a delicious option. Peel and slice fresh pears, placing them into sterilized jars. Simmer a syrup made of sugar, water, cinnamon sticks, cloves, and nutmeg, then pour it over the pears until they’re completely submerged. Seal the jars and process in a water bath for 20 minutes. These spiced pears can be enjoyed on their own, as a topping for cakes, or paired with whipped cream.

Canning Tips for Beginners

Before you dive into any of these canning recipes, keep in mind some essential tips:

  • Use fresh ingredients: High-quality produce will yield the best results. Choose fruits and vegetables at their peak ripeness for optimal flavor.
  • Sterilize your jars: Properly sterilizing jars is crucial to eliminate bacteria and ensure your canned goods stay safe and fresh.
  • Follow heating guidelines: Whether you’re using a water bath or pressure canning method, always follow the recommended processing times to prevent spoilage.
  • Label clearly: Add labels with both the preservation date and the name of the recipe to each jar.

By keeping these basics in mind, you’ll be well-equipped to stock your pantry with homemade goodies.

Final Thoughts

Canning is an incredible way to enjoy seasonal flavors year-round, reduce food waste, and gain a sense of accomplishment in the kitchen. These 10 simple recipes serve as a starting point for beginners and seasoned home preservers alike. Once you get the hang of it, the possibilities are truly endless—from jams and sauces to soups and pickled vegetables. So roll up your sleeves, grab your mason jars, and start filling your pantry with delicious homemade creations!
