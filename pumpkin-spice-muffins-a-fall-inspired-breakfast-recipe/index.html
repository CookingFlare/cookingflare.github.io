Pumpkin Spice Muffins: A Fall-Inspired Breakfast Recipe


As the leaves start to change and the air becomes crisp, it’s time to embrace the flavors of fall. One of the most beloved autumn treats is the classic pumpkin spice muffin. These moist, flavorful delights are the perfect way to start your day, whether you enjoy them with a hot cup of coffee or as a grab-and-go breakfast on a busy morning. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create the ultimate pumpkin spice muffins that will fill your home with the comforting scents of cinnamon, nutmeg, and, of course, pumpkin.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups canned pumpkin puree

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease the cups with non-stick cooking spray.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and ginger until well combined.

  3. Mix Wet Ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vegetable oil until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract and pumpkin puree until the mixture is smooth and well combined.

  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix the batter, as this can result in tough muffins.

  5. Fill Muffin Cups: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each cup about 2/3 full.

  6. Bake: Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

  7. Cool and Enjoy: Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, these pumpkin spice muffins are ready to be enjoyed!

Serving Suggestions

These pumpkin spice muffins are delicious on their own, but you can take them to the next level with a few creative serving suggestions. Spread a dollop of cream cheese or butter on a warm muffin for a decadent treat, or pair them with a drizzle of honey for a touch of sweetness. If you’re feeling adventurous, consider adding a handful of chopped nuts or dried cranberries to the batter for added texture and flavor.

Final Thoughts

Pumpkin spice muffins are a quintessential fall treat that brings warmth and comfort to any breakfast table. With their rich, aromatic spices and moist texture, these muffins are sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re baking a batch for a cozy weekend brunch or to share with friends and family, these pumpkin spice muffins are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of the season. So, embrace the spirit of autumn and indulge in the simple pleasure of a homemade pumpkin spice muffin.

I hope this blog post helps you to create delicious pumpkin spice muffins that will become a staple in your fall recipe collection. Happy baking!
