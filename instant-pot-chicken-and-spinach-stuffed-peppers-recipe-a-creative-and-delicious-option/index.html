Instant Pot Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Peppers Recipe: A Creative and Delicious Option


As the weather starts to cool down, our taste buds crave warm and comforting dishes that can be easily prepared at home. One such recipe that fits the bill perfectly is Instant Pot Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Peppers. This creative and delicious option is a game-changer for those looking for a healthy and satisfying meal that can be prepared in no time.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers, any color
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into small pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 cup cooked white rice
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Peppers

  1. Rinse the bell peppers and cut off the tops. Remove the seeds and membranes, and place them in the Instant Pot.
  2. Add 1 cup of water to the Instant Pot, and close the lid. Make sure the valve is set to “sealing”.
  3. Press the “manual” or “pressure cook” button and set the cooking time to 5 minutes at high pressure.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken and Spinach

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, spinach, paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the skillet. Cook until the spinach is wilted.
  3. Stir in the cooked rice, diced tomatoes, and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

Step 3: Assemble the Stuffed Peppers

  1. Once the peppers are cooked, remove them from the Instant Pot and set them aside.
  2. Add the cooked chicken and spinach mixture to the peppers, dividing it evenly among the four peppers.
  3. Top each pepper with shredded cheddar cheese (if using).

Step 4: Finish Cooking the Stuffed Peppers

  1. Close the Instant Pot and set the valve to “sealing”. Press the “manual” or “pressure cook” button and set the cooking time to 5 minutes at high pressure.
  2. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  3. Serve the stuffed peppers hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley or cilantro (if desired).

Tips and Variations

  • Use any combination of bell pepper colors you like, or a mix of colors for a festive look.
  • Substitute ground turkey or beef for the chicken if you prefer.
  • Add some heat to the dish by using hot sauce or red pepper flakes.
  • Serve the stuffed peppers with a side of steamed vegetables or a green salad for a well-rounded meal.

Conclusion

Instant Pot Chicken and Spinach Stuffed Peppers is a creative and delicious recipe that is perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. With its easy-to-follow instructions and minimal ingredients, it’s a great option for busy home cooks. Give it a try and enjoy the warm and comforting flavors of this unique recipe!
