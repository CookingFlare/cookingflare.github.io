Korean Spicy Chicken Stew (Dakdoritang) Recipe


Korean Spicy Chicken Stew, known as Dakdoritang, is a beloved dish that combines bold flavors, rich textures, and hearty ingredients into the ultimate comfort food experience. This stew encapsulates Korea’s culinary philosophy: vibrant, nourishing, and harmonious. Whether you’re an avid lover of Korean cuisine or exploring spicy Korean recipes for the first time, Dakdoritang is a perfect dish to master in your kitchen. Its fiery yet balanced flavor profile and simple preparation make it a crowd-pleaser and a staple in countless Korean households.

With tender chicken simmered in a deeply flavorful, spicy broth filled with potatoes, carrots, and aromatics like garlic and ginger, Dakdoritang creates an unforgettable dish that’s equal parts soothing and exciting. Let’s dive into the details of creating this iconic recipe, step by step, and uncover a bit of the cultural significance behind this irresistible feast.

Ingredients for Dakdoritang

To recreate the traditional flavor of Dakdoritang, you’ll need a thoughtfully curated list of ingredients. Each plays a vital role in building layers of flavor for the stew.

Main Ingredients:

  • Chicken: Bone-in, skin-on chicken drumsticks or thighs work best for tender, juicy meat. Use approximately 2 pounds.
  • Potatoes: 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks. Starchy potatoes work well as they absorb the flavorful broth.
  • Carrots: 2 medium carrots, peeled and cut into thick slices or chunks.
  • Onion: 1 large onion, roughly chopped for added sweetness and depth.

For the Spicy Sauce:

  • Gochujang (Korean red chili paste): 3 tablespoons. This ingredient is the backbone of the stew’s vibrant, spicy flavor.
  • Gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes): 1-2 tablespoons, depending on your spice tolerance.
  • Soy Sauce: 3 tablespoons for a savory, umami note.
  • Sugar or Honey: 1 tablespoon for subtle sweetness that balances the heat.
  • Minced Garlic: 6-7 cloves for robust, aromatic depth.
  • Grated Ginger: 1 tablespoon for warmth and complexity.
  • Water or Chicken Broth: About 2-3 cups, depending on how thick you want the stew.
  • Sesame Oil: 1 teaspoon for a rich, nutty aroma.

Optional Garnish:

  • Green Onion: Thinly sliced.
  • Sesame Seeds: Lightly toasted for texture and nuttiness.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep the Ingredients

Before cooking, ensure all your ingredients are prepared and ready to use. Cut the chicken into smaller pieces if necessary, allowing the flavors to penetrate evenly. Peel and chop the vegetables. Potatoes should be cut into relatively thick chunks to prevent overly soft textures after simmering.

Step 2: Make the Spicy Sauce

In a mixing bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar (or honey), minced garlic, grated ginger, and sesame oil. Gradually add 2 cups of water or chicken broth, stirring as you go, to create the base of your flavorful broth. Adjust the seasoning to your taste; if you prefer less heat, reduce the amount of gochugaru slightly.

Step 3: Brown the Chicken

Heat a large pot or a deep skillet over medium heat. Add a drizzle of neutral oil and sear the chicken pieces for 2-3 minutes on each side until they develop a light golden crust. This step locks in the juices and adds depth to the final dish. Remove the chicken and set aside.

Step 4: Layer the Vegetables and Chicken

Using the same pot, layer the chopped onions on the bottom as a natural base for the stew. Add the browned chicken on top of the onions, followed by the chunks of potatoes and carrots. This layered approach ensures even cooking and flavor distribution.

Step 5: Pour in the Sauce

Pour the spicy sauce mixture over the layers of chicken and vegetables. The sauce should cover most of the ingredients but does not need to fully submerge them. Add a little extra broth or water if needed.

Step 6: Simmer and Cook

Cover the pot and bring the stew to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes. Check periodically, stirring gently to ensure the sauce doesn’t stick to the bottom of the pot and to help the flavors meld together beautifully. Adjust the amount of liquid during cooking if needed.

Step 7: Finish and Garnish

When the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender, remove the stew from the heat. Taste the broth and adjust seasoning if necessary; you can add a splash of soy sauce, a pinch of sugar, or additional spice to suit your palate. Serve the stew hot, garnished with thinly sliced green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for added flavor and presentation.

Tips for Success

  • Control the Spice: Dakdoritang is traditionally spicy, but you can easily adjust the heat level. Use less gochugaru or opt for a milder chili paste if you prefer a gentler kick.
  • Choose the Right Chicken Cuts: Bone-in chicken pieces are recommended for their juicy texture and robust flavor. If you prefer boneless, use thighs as they stay tender.
  • Consistency of the Broth: If you prefer a thinner broth, add more water or chicken stock. For a thicker, richer consistency, let the stew simmer uncovered toward the end of cooking.
  • Pair with Rice: Dakdoritang pairs beautifully with steamed white rice, which balances the spiciness of the broth and provides a neutral base for savoring every bite.

Cultural Significance of Dakdoritang

Dakdoritang holds a special place in Korean cuisine and family gatherings. This dish is often served for casual weekday meals or shared with loved ones on colder days, as its hearty nature brings warmth and comfort. The fusion of spicy, sweet, and savory flavors reflects Korea’s affinity for balance in food, making this dish symbolic of the country’s culinary heritage.

The term “Dakdoritang” itself has an interesting history. While “dak” means chicken in Korean, “doritang” combines “dori” (derived from the Japanese word for bird, “tori”) and “tang,” which means soup or stew in Korean. Today, Dakdoritang is simply embraced as a flavorful celebration of Korean home cooking.

Bring the Essence of Korea to Your Kitchen

Making Dakdoritang at home invites the rich flavors of Korean cuisine into your own space. This spicy chicken stew requires just a handful of readily available ingredients and is perfect for feeding family and friends. Its bold flavors and comforting textures make it a dish that’s worth the effort, and once you master it, it might become one of your go-to recipes for satisfying cravings or introducing others to the wonders of Korean food.

So gather your ingredients and embrace this culinary journey that showcases Korea’s warmth and vibrancy through every bite. Dakdoritang isn’t just a meal—it’s a delicious story told through layers of spice, sweetness, and comfort. You’re sure to fall in love with its soul-soothing elegance, one bowl at a time.
