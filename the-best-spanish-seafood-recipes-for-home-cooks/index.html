The Best Spanish Seafood Recipes for Home Cooks


Spanish cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, rich history, and diverse ingredients. Among its most celebrated offerings are seafood dishes that capture the essence of coastal living. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, these Spanish seafood recipes are sure to impress and transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Spain. Let’s dive into some of the best seafood recipes that you can easily replicate at home.

1. Paella de Mariscos (Seafood Paella)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Bomba or Arborio rice
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb mussels, cleaned
  • 1 lb calamari, sliced into rings
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the broth: In a saucepan, heat the seafood stock and add the saffron threads. Let it simmer on low heat to infuse the flavors.
  2. Sauté the vegetables: In a large paella pan or skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until they are translucent. Stir in the red bell pepper and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  3. Cook the rice: Add the grated tomatoes and paprika, stirring for a minute. Then, add the rice, ensuring it is well-coated with the vegetable mixture. Pour in the saffron-infused seafood stock and season with salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for about 10 minutes.
  4. Add the seafood: Arrange the shrimp, mussels, and calamari on top of the rice and cover the pan. Cook for an additional 10-12 minutes until the seafood is cooked through and the rice has absorbed the liquid.
  5. Rest and serve: Remove from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with parsley and serve with lemon wedges.

2. Gambas al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil: In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking until the garlic is fragrant but not browned.
  2. Cook the shrimp: Add the shrimp to the skillet in a single layer. Season with salt and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque.
  3. Garnish and serve: Remove from heat, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve immediately with crusty bread to soak up the delicious garlic oil.

3. Pulpo a la Gallega (Galician-Style Octopus)

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs octopus, cleaned
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into rounds
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the octopus: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the octopus and cook for about 45 minutes or until tender. Remove and let it cool, then slice into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Boil the potatoes: In the same pot, add the potato rounds and cook until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Assemble the dish: On a serving platter, arrange the potato rounds and top them with the octopus slices. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with smoked paprika and salt, and garnish with parsley. Serve warm.

4. Calamares a la Romana (Battered Fried Calamari)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh calamari, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for frying
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the batter: In a bowl, whisk the eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper. In another bowl, place the flour, and in a third bowl, the breadcrumbs.
  2. Coat the calamari: Dip each calamari ring in flour, then into the egg mixture, and finally coat with breadcrumbs.
  3. Fry the calamari: In a deep skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Fry the calamari in batches for about 2-3 minutes until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Serve: Arrange the calamari on a platter with lemon wedges for squeezing over before enjoying.

5. Arroz Negro (Black Rice)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Bomba rice
  • 4 cups fish or seafood stock
  • 1/2 lb squid, cleaned and sliced
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup black squid ink
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the vegetables: In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until translucent. Stir in the grated tomatoes and cook for another few minutes.
  2. Add the rice and stock: Add the rice, stirring until well-coated. Pour in the stock and add the black squid ink. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook the seafood: Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. After about 10 minutes, add the squid and shrimp, cooking for another 10 minutes until the rice is tender and has absorbed the liquid.
  4. Serve: Remove from heat, let it rest for 5 minutes, and serve with lemon wedges and a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

6. Pisto with Tuna

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium zucchinis, diced
  • 4 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 can of tuna in olive oil, drained
  • Olive oil, for frying
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and bell peppers, cooking until soft. Stir in the zucchini and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  2. Add the tomatoes: Mix in the chopped tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and cook until the mixture thickens, about 15 minutes.
  3. Incorporate the tuna: Gently fold in the tuna, cooking for another 2-3 minutes until warmed through.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil and serve warm as a side dish or light main course.

7. Boquerones en Vinagre (Marinated Anchovies)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh anchovies, cleaned and filleted
  • 1 cup white wine vinegar
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the anchovies: Place the anchovy fillets in a shallow dish. Pour the white wine vinegar over the top, ensuring they are fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for at least 24 hours.
  2. Prepare the dressing: In a small bowl, combine olive oil, garlic slices, and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
  3. Serve: After marinating, drain the anchovies and arrange them on a platter. Drizzle with the olive oil mixture and garnish with chopped parsley. Serve as an appetizer or tapas.

Conclusion

Cooking Spanish seafood dishes at home allows you to explore the rich culinary traditions of Spain while enjoying fresh and flavorful ingredients. From the iconic paella to simple yet elegant garlic shrimp, these recipes offer something for everyone. With a little practice and the right ingredients, you can bring the taste of Spain to your dining table, impressing family and friends with your culinary skills. So, gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and let these best Spanish seafood recipes inspire your next cooking adventure!
