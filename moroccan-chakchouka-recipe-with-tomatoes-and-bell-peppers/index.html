Moroccan Chakchouka Recipe with Tomatoes and Bell Peppers


Chakchouka, a traditional Moroccan dish, is a flavorful and vibrant recipe that combines the rich flavors of tomatoes and bell peppers with aromatic spices. This vegetarian dish is not only delicious but also easy to prepare, making it a perfect option for a quick and satisfying meal. Let’s dive into the details of this mouthwatering Moroccan Chakchouka recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 bell peppers (red, green, or yellow), thinly sliced
  • 4 large tomatoes, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 to 6 eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and sauté until it becomes translucent and slightly caramelized.

  2. Add the sliced bell peppers to the skillet and continue sautéing for a few minutes until they start to soften.

  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, minced garlic, ground cumin, paprika, ground turmeric, salt, and pepper. Cook the mixture for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes break down and release their juices.

  4. Create small wells in the mixture and crack the eggs into them. Cover the skillet and let the eggs cook until they reach your desired level of doneness. If you prefer a runny yolk, it will take around 5 minutes, while fully cooked eggs will take a bit longer.

  5. Once the eggs are cooked to your liking, remove the skillet from the heat. Sprinkle fresh parsley or cilantro on top for added freshness and aroma.

  6. Serve the Chakchouka hot, straight from the skillet. It pairs well with crusty bread or warm pita, making it a satisfying and complete meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier kick, you can add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a chopped chili pepper to the Chakchouka.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste preferences. Some popular additions include smoked paprika, oregano, or even a hint of cinnamon.
  • If you want to add some protein to your dish, you can include cooked chickpeas or crumbled feta cheese before adding the eggs.
  • Chakchouka can be enjoyed for any meal of the day. It makes a hearty breakfast, a satisfying brunch, or a flavorful dinner option.

Conclusion

Moroccan Chakchouka is a delightful dish that showcases the vibrant flavors and spices of Moroccan cuisine. Its combination of tomatoes, bell peppers, and eggs creates a harmonious blend of textures and tastes. With this easy-to-follow recipe, you can effortlessly recreate this traditional Moroccan delicacy in your own kitchen. So gather your ingredients, heat up your skillet, and get ready to savor the rich flavors of Chakchouka. Bon appétit!
