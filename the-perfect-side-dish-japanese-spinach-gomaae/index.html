The Perfect Side Dish: Japanese Spinach Gomaae


If you’re looking for a delicious and healthy side dish to accompany your Japanese-inspired meals, look no further than Japanese Spinach Gomaae. This simple yet flavorful dish features blanched spinach dressed in a creamy sesame sauce, making it the perfect addition to any meal. Whether you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine or simply looking to add more greens to your diet, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

To make Japanese Spinach Gomaae, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of fresh spinach
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the spinach. Rinse the spinach leaves thoroughly under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities. Trim off the tough stems and discard them.

  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt to the boiling water. Carefully place the spinach leaves into the boiling water and blanch them for about 1 minute, or until they are wilted and vibrant green.

  3. Once the spinach is cooked, immediately transfer it to a bowl filled with ice water. This will stop the cooking process and help the spinach retain its bright green color. Allow the spinach to cool in the ice water for a few minutes.

  4. While the spinach is cooling, it’s time to prepare the sesame sauce. In a dry skillet, toast the sesame seeds over medium heat until they become fragrant and slightly golden. This should take about 2-3 minutes. Remove the toasted sesame seeds from the skillet and transfer them to a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder. Grind the sesame seeds until they turn into a coarse powder.

  5. In a small bowl, combine the ground sesame seeds, soy sauce, sugar, mirin, and sesame oil. Stir well to combine, ensuring that the sugar is dissolved and the ingredients are well incorporated.

  6. Once the spinach has cooled, squeeze out any excess water and roughly chop it into bite-sized pieces.

  7. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped spinach and the sesame sauce. Toss the spinach gently to coat it evenly with the sauce.

  8. Transfer the dressed spinach to a serving dish and garnish with a sprinkle of additional toasted sesame seeds for added flavor and visual appeal.

Serving Suggestions

Japanese Spinach Gomaae is traditionally served as a side dish alongside steamed rice and grilled fish or meat. Its refreshing and nutty flavors perfectly complement the main course. You can also enjoy it as part of a bento box or as a standalone salad. Feel free to get creative and experiment with different proteins or vegetables to create your own unique twist on this classic dish.

Health Benefits

Not only is Japanese Spinach Gomaae delicious, but it’s also packed with nutritional benefits. Spinach is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as iron and folate. It is known for its antioxidant properties and is believed to support healthy digestion and cardiovascular health. The sesame seeds in the sauce provide a good source of healthy fats, protein, and essential minerals like calcium and magnesium.

Conclusion

Japanese Spinach Gomaae is a versatile and flavorful side dish that adds a touch of elegance to any Japanese-inspired meal. With its vibrant green color and creamy sesame sauce, it’s sure to impress your family and friends. Give this recipe a try and discover the perfect balance of flavors that will leave you wanting more. Enjoy!
