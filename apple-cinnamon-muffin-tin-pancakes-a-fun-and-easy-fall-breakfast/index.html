Apple Cinnamon Muffin Tin Pancakes: A Fun and Easy Fall Breakfast


Are you ready to kick-start your fall mornings with a delicious and comforting breakfast? Look no further than these Apple Cinnamon Muffin Tin Pancakes! Bursting with warm flavors and a hint of sweetness, these fluffy pancakes are the perfect way to start your day.

Why Muffin Tin Pancakes?

You might be wondering, why make pancakes in a muffin tin? Well, this innovative technique not only adds a fun twist to the classic pancake recipe but also makes them easy to grab and enjoy on the go. Plus, the individual portions make it convenient for serving and portion control.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 apple, peeled and finely diced
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a muffin tin with cooking spray or butter.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Make sure all the ingredients are well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold them together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough pancakes.

  5. Gently fold in the diced apple, ensuring it is evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  6. Spoon the pancake batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about 3/4 full. Smooth the tops with the back of a spoon to ensure even baking.

  7. Bake the pancakes in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. To check if they’re done, insert a toothpick into the center of a pancake. If it comes out clean, they’re ready!

  8. Remove the muffin tin from the oven and let the pancakes cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack.

  9. Serve these delightful Apple Cinnamon Muffin Tin Pancakes warm, drizzled with maple syrup. You can enjoy them as they are or top them with your favorite pancake toppings like whipped cream, chopped nuts, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar.

Final Thoughts:

These Apple Cinnamon Muffin Tin Pancakes are a delightful twist on traditional pancakes, perfect for fall mornings or any time you crave a cozy breakfast. The combination of sweet apples, warm cinnamon, and fluffy pancakes is simply irresistible. Plus, the muffin tin technique makes them a fun and convenient breakfast option for the whole family.

So, why not treat yourself to a plateful of these delicious pancakes? They’re easy to make, bursting with flavor, and will make your mornings extra special. Embrace the fall vibes and indulge in this delightful breakfast treat!
