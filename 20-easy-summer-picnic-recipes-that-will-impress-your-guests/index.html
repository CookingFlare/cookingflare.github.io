20 Easy Summer Picnic Recipes That Will Impress Your Guests


Summer is the perfect time to gather your friends and family for a picnic. Whether you’re heading to the beach, a park, or just enjoying your backyard, a great picnic requires delicious food that’s easy to prepare, pack, and share. From refreshing salads to savory snacks and sweet treats, these 20 easy summer picnic recipes will elevate your outdoor gatherings and leave a lasting impression on your guests.

1. Crispy Baked Chicken Wings

For a picnic, you need a finger food that everyone can enjoy. These crispy baked chicken wings are perfect. Coated in a flavorful dry rub and baked to golden perfection, they’re a healthy alternative to fried wings while still offering that satisfying crunch. You can make them ahead of time and enjoy them cold or reheat them at your picnic spot.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken wings
  • Olive oil
  • Paprika
  • Garlic powder
  • Onion powder
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the chicken wings with olive oil and season with paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Arrange the wings on a baking sheet and bake for 35-40 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  4. Serve with a side of ranch or blue cheese dipping sauce.

2. Mediterranean Pasta Salad

A pasta salad is a picnic classic, and this Mediterranean version is loaded with fresh vegetables and a tangy dressing that makes it the perfect dish to bring to your gathering. With ingredients like cucumbers, olives, cherry tomatoes, and feta, it’s a flavor-packed side dish that pairs well with almost anything.

Ingredients:

  • Cooked pasta (penne or rotini works best)
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber
  • Kalamata olives
  • Red onion
  • Feta cheese
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta, chopped vegetables, olives, and feta cheese.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the pasta and toss everything together.
  4. Chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving.

3. Caprese Skewers

Caprese skewers are simple to make, refreshing, and visually stunning. These bite-sized skewers combine fresh mozzarella, ripe tomatoes, and fragrant basil, drizzled with balsamic glaze for a little extra sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh mozzarella balls
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze

Instructions:

  1. Skewer a mozzarella ball, a basil leaf, and a cherry tomato onto each skewer.
  2. Arrange the skewers on a serving platter and drizzle with balsamic glaze just before serving.

4. Grilled Veggie Sandwiches

These grilled veggie sandwiches are full of flavor and easy to assemble. Perfect for a vegetarian-friendly picnic, the combination of grilled vegetables like zucchini, eggplant, and peppers on whole grain bread makes for a satisfying and healthy option.

Ingredients:

  • Zucchini
  • Eggplant
  • Bell peppers
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Whole grain sandwich bread
  • Hummus or goat cheese

Instructions:

  1. Slice the vegetables and drizzle with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill the vegetables until tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Spread hummus or goat cheese on slices of bread, then layer with the grilled vegetables.
  4. Press the sandwich together and slice into halves for serving.

5. Watermelon Feta Salad

Watermelon is synonymous with summer, and when paired with feta cheese, mint, and a squeeze of lime, it becomes a refreshing and unexpected salad. This sweet and salty combination is perfect for a hot day and will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • Cubed watermelon
  • Crumbled feta cheese
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Lime juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the watermelon, feta cheese, and mint.
  2. Drizzle with lime juice and season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Toss everything together gently and serve chilled.

6. Classic Deviled Eggs

Deviled eggs are always a crowd-pleaser. Creamy, tangy, and perfectly bite-sized, they’re a great addition to any picnic spread. For a twist, you can experiment with different fillings or toppings like bacon bits or paprika.

Ingredients:

  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Mayonnaise
  • Dijon mustard
  • Vinegar
  • Salt and pepper
  • Paprika (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cut the hard-boiled eggs in half and remove the yolks.
  2. Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites.
  4. Garnish with paprika and serve chilled.

7. Mini Quiches

Mini quiches are great for a picnic because they’re portable and can be eaten warm or cold. You can make them with a variety of fillings, such as spinach, mushrooms, or bacon, making them perfect for any taste.

Ingredients:

  • Pre-made pie crust
  • Eggs
  • Heavy cream
  • Cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or goat cheese)
  • Spinach, mushrooms, or bacon (optional)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and cut it into small rounds to fit muffin tins.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add your desired fillings to the crusts and pour the egg mixture over the top.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the quiches are set and lightly golden.

8. Pulled Pork Sliders

Pulled pork is a picnic favorite, and these sliders are perfect for small bites. The tender, slow-cooked pork, combined with a tangy BBQ sauce and a soft bun, makes for a satisfying dish that will be a hit at your gathering.

Ingredients:

  • Pulled pork (slow-cooked)
  • BBQ sauce
  • Slider buns
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the pulled pork and mix with BBQ sauce.
  2. Toast the slider buns and spoon the pulled pork onto each bun.
  3. Top with coleslaw for extra crunch and flavor.

9. Fruit Skewers

Fruit skewers are a simple yet delicious way to enjoy summer fruits. Combine a variety of seasonal fruits like pineapple, strawberries, grapes, and melon for a sweet and colorful addition to your picnic.

Ingredients:

  • Pineapple
  • Strawberries
  • Grapes
  • Melon
  • Skewers

Instructions:

  1. Cut the fruit into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Skewer the fruit, alternating between different varieties.
  3. Serve immediately or chill for a refreshing treat.

10. Chicken Caesar Wraps

For a portable version of a classic Caesar salad, try chicken Caesar wraps. Tender chicken, romaine lettuce, Parmesan, and Caesar dressing wrapped in a tortilla make a satisfying and easy-to-eat picnic meal.

Ingredients:

  • Grilled chicken breast
  • Romaine lettuce
  • Caesar dressing
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Flour tortillas

Instructions:

  1. Slice the grilled chicken into thin strips.
  2. Toss the chicken with romaine lettuce, Caesar dressing, and Parmesan cheese.
  3. Wrap the mixture in flour tortillas and slice into halves.

11. Spinach and Artichoke Dip

Creamy spinach and artichoke dip is a comforting and crowd-pleasing appetizer that’s perfect for a picnic. Serve with crackers, pita chips, or fresh vegetables for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • Frozen spinach (thawed and drained)
  • Artichoke hearts (chopped)
  • Cream cheese
  • Sour cream
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the spinach, artichokes, cream cheese, sour cream, Parmesan cheese, and garlic powder.
  3. Transfer to a baking dish and bake for 25-30 minutes, until hot and bubbly.
  4. Serve with dipping options of your choice.

12. Avocado Toast Bites

Avocado toast is a trendy and healthy choice, and these mini avocado toast bites are perfect for a picnic. Topped with creamy avocado, a sprinkle of salt, and a dash of lemon, they’re simple but delicious.

Ingredients:

  • Sliced baguette or crusty bread
  • Avocados
  • Lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread slices until golden and crispy.
  2. Mash the avocados and spread them on the toasted bread.
  3. Season with lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Serve immediately or pack for later.

13. Hummus and Veggie Platter

For a healthy, refreshing, and easy-to-assemble picnic dish, try a hummus and veggie platter. Pair creamy hummus with colorful vegetables like carrots, cucumber, and bell peppers for a light and satisfying snack.

Ingredients:

  • Hummus
  • Carrots
  • Cucumber
  • Bell peppers
  • Cherry tomatoes

Instructions:

  1. Arrange the fresh veggies on a platter alongside bowls of hummus for dipping.
  2. Serve immediately or chill until ready to serve.

14. Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Grilled corn on the cob is a summer favorite, and with a generous dollop of herb butter, it’s even better. The smoky flavor from the grill pairs perfectly with the fresh, creamy butter.

Ingredients:

  • Corn on the cob
  • Butter
  • Fresh herbs (parsley, thyme, rosemary)

Instructions:

  1. Grill the corn on medium heat, turning occasionally, until charred and tender.
  2. In a bowl, mix softened butter with finely chopped herbs.
  3. Brush the corn with the herb butter and serve.

15. Smoked Salmon and Cream Cheese Bagels

For an upscale picnic option, smoked salmon and cream cheese bagels are an elegant choice. With the richness of cream cheese and the delicate flavor of smoked salmon, these bagels are sure to impress your guests.

Ingredients:

  • Bagels
  • Cream cheese
  • Smoked salmon
  • Capers
  • Red onion slices

Instructions:

  1. Spread a layer of cream cheese on each bagel half.
  2. Top with smoked salmon, capers, and red onion slices.
  3. Serve immediately.

16. Zucchini Fritters

Zucchini fritters are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, making them an excellent snack for picnics. They’re easy to make and can be eaten hot or cold, so they’re a great make-ahead option.

Ingredients:

  • Zucchini
  • Flour
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Grate the zucchini and squeeze out excess moisture.
  2. In a bowl, mix the zucchini with flour, eggs, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat oil in a pan and fry spoonfuls of the mixture until golden brown on both sides.
  4. Serve with a yogurt or tzatziki dipping sauce.

17. Pasta Primavera

Pasta primavera is a fresh and vibrant dish that’s perfect for summer. Loaded with seasonal vegetables like tomatoes, peas, and bell peppers, this dish is light yet filling.

Ingredients:

  • Pasta
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Bell peppers
  • Zucchini
  • Olive oil
  • Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions.
  2. Sauté the vegetables in olive oil until tender.
  3. Toss the pasta and vegetables together, then top with Parmesan cheese.
  4. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

18. Chilled Cucumber Soup

This cool and refreshing cucumber soup is the perfect way to beat the summer heat. Blended with yogurt, garlic, and fresh herbs, it’s light and creamy, making it an ideal appetizer for any picnic.

Ingredients:

  • Cucumbers
  • Yogurt
  • Garlic
  • Fresh dill
  • Lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Blend the cucumbers with yogurt, garlic, dill, and lemon juice until smooth.
  2. Chill the soup in the fridge for at least an hour before serving.
  3. Garnish with additional dill and serve cold.

19. Peach Cobbler

End your picnic on a sweet note with a delicious peach cobbler. Made with fresh peaches and a buttery biscuit topping, this dessert is a comforting way to finish your outdoor meal.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh peaches
  • Sugar
  • Flour
  • Baking powder
  • Butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Slice the peaches and toss them with sugar.
  3. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and butter to form a dough.
  4. Pour the peaches into a baking dish and top with the biscuit dough.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, until golden and bubbly.

20. Iced Tea Lemonade

To wash down all the delicious food, nothing beats a refreshing iced tea lemonade. This sweet and tart drink is the perfect summer beverage to complement your picnic spread.

Ingredients:

  • Iced tea
  • Lemon juice
  • Sugar or honey
  • Fresh mint

Instructions:

  1. Brew a strong batch of iced tea.
  2. Mix with freshly squeezed lemon juice and sweeten with sugar or honey.
  3. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve over ice.

These 20 easy summer picnic recipes are sure to impress your guests with their fresh flavors, vibrant colors, and ease of preparation. Whether you’re in the mood for savory snacks, refreshing salads, or sweet treats, these dishes will elevate your outdoor gatherings and make for a memorable picnic experience.
