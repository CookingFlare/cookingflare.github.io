How to Make a Delicious French Dip Sandwich


A French Dip Sandwich is a classic that combines tender slices of beef, a crusty baguette, and a savory au jus dipping sauce. Perfect for a hearty lunch or an indulgent dinner, this sandwich is a delightful treat that never fails to please. Whether it’s your first time making one or you’re looking to refine your technique, this guide will walk you through how to prepare a sublime French Dip Sandwich from scratch.

Ingredients for the Ultimate French Dip Sandwich

To craft an authentic French Dip, you’ll need a combination of fresh ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need for the sandwich and the au jus:

For the Sandwich:

  • 1 pound of thinly sliced roast beef (preferably cooked medium-rare)
  • 4 crusty French rolls or baguettes, split lengthwise
  • 4 slices of provolone, Swiss, or Gruyère cheese (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced

For the Au Jus (the dipping sauce):

  • 3 cups beef broth or stock (low-sodium preferred)
  • 1/4 cup dry red wine (optional but recommended)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)

These ingredients come together in perfect harmony, with bold flavors designed to elevate your sandwich into a rich, savory masterpiece.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Au Jus

The au jus is the heart of a French Dip Sandwich. Here’s how to make a flavorful dipping sauce that complements the sandwich without overpowering it:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics:
    In a medium-sized saucepan, heat a tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.

  2. Deglaze the Pan:
    If you’re using red wine, pour it into the pan to deglaze. Scrape the bottom of the saucepan to release the brown bits. Allow the wine to simmer for 1-2 minutes, reducing slightly.

  3. Simmer the Broth:
    Add the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, dried thyme, salt, and pepper to the saucepan. Stir well to combine.

  4. Cook the Au Jus:
    Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer gently for 10-15 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Strain the au jus through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl or serving container to remove onion and garlic bits, leaving a silky smooth dipping sauce.

Preparing the Sandwich

Once your au jus is ready, it’s time to assemble the sandwich. This part is quick but requires attention to detail for the perfect balance of textures and flavors.

  1. Toast the Rolls:
    Preheat the broiler in your oven. Lightly butter the inside of each roll and sprinkle with minced garlic. Place them on a baking sheet, cut side up, and broil for 2-3 minutes. Keep an eye on them to prevent burning—they should be golden brown and slightly crisp.

  2. Warm the Meat:
    In a skillet over medium-low heat, add the thinly sliced roast beef and a few tablespoons of au jus. Heat for 2-3 minutes until just warmed through. Avoid overcooking, as the beef can become tough.

  3. Melt the Cheese (Optional):
    If you enjoy cheese on your French Dip Sandwich, place a slice of provolone, Swiss, or Gruyère on the bottom half of the roll. Return the rolls to the broiler for an additional 1-2 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbling.

  4. Assemble the Sandwich:
    Layer the warmed roast beef generously onto the bottom half of the rolls. Use tongs to avoid adding too much liquid, which can make the bread soggy. Top with the other half of the roll.

Serving the French Dip Sandwich

A French Dip is incomplete without its signature element: dipping it into the luscious au jus. Here’s how to serve it up for maximum enjoyment:

  1. Portion the Au Jus:
    Serve the warm au jus in small bowls or ramekins, one for each person. This ensures everyone can dip their sandwich to their liking.

  2. Add a Side:
    French Dip Sandwiches pair wonderfully with french fries, a simple mixed green salad, or a handful of crisps. The sides add variety while keeping the focus on the sandwich.

  3. Don’t Forget Napkins:
    A French Dip is a hands-on meal that can get a little messy, but that’s all part of the fun. Make sure to have plenty of napkins nearby!

Tips for the Perfect French Dip Sandwich

  1. Choose Quality Beef:
    High-quality, tender roast beef is essential. If you’re cooking your roast beef at home, aim for a medium-rare doneness to keep the meat juicy.

  2. Experiment with Rolls:
    The bread plays a key role in texture. A French baguette or an artisan roll with a sturdy crust and soft interior works best, as it can absorb the au jus without falling apart.

  3. Customize the Flavor:
    Feel free to tweak the seasoning in your au jus to suit your taste. Adding a pinch of smoked paprika or a dash of hot sauce can create a unique spin on the classic.

  4. Serve Immediately:
    French Dip Sandwiches are best enjoyed fresh and warm. Assemble them right before serving to prevent the bread from becoming soggy.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I make a French Dip Sandwich ahead of time?
While it’s best to assemble the sandwich just before serving, you can prepare the au jus and roast beef ahead of time. Store them separately in sealed containers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently before assembling.

2. What if I don’t have beef broth?
Vegetable broth or chicken broth can work in a pinch, but beef broth gives the authentic rich flavor that defines a traditional French Dip.

3. Can I use a slow cooker to make the au jus?
Absolutely! Combine the au jus ingredients in a slow cooker and cook on low for 4-6 hours. This method extracts even more flavor.

Conclusion

A French Dip Sandwich is a timeless comfort food that delivers layers of satisfaction—from the hearty beef to the crisp roll and the flavorful au jus. Whether you’re preparing it for a quiet evening at home or serving a crowd, this sandwich is a guaranteed hit. By following these steps, you’ll not only master the art of making a French Dip but also inspire others to share in its irresistible charm. So fire up your stove, gather your ingredients, and dive into creating this delectable, crowd-pleasing dish. Bon appétit!
