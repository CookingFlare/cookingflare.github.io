Cold Pasta Salad with Sweet Corn and Herbs


When the weather heats up, nothing beats a cold pasta salad to refresh and nourish you. Whether it’s for a picnic, a backyard BBQ, or just a quick and healthy meal at home, a cold pasta salad can be a perfect dish. In this post, we will dive into a simple yet flavorful recipe for a Cold Pasta Salad with Sweet Corn and Herbs. This recipe is packed with vibrant ingredients, making it an ideal choice for warm weather.

Why You Should Make This Cold Pasta Salad

Cold pasta salads are an excellent choice for busy days, summer gatherings, and meal prepping. They are versatile, easy to make, and can be prepared ahead of time. This particular recipe is not only delicious but also nutritious, with the sweet corn providing a natural sweetness that pairs beautifully with fresh herbs. It’s also a great way to incorporate more vegetables into your diet while still enjoying a satisfying meal.

The combination of pasta, sweet corn, and herbs creates a balance of textures and flavors that will make your taste buds dance. This dish is also highly customizable, allowing you to add or swap ingredients based on your preferences or dietary needs. Plus, it’s a great way to enjoy corn, which is at its peak during the summer months.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this cold pasta salad with sweet corn and herbs, gather the following ingredients:

For the Salad:

  • 2 cups of pasta (penne, fusilli, or rotini work best)
  • 1 cup of fresh sweet corn kernels (or use frozen if fresh isn’t available)
  • 1 medium cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (red or yellow for color)
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled (optional)
  • 1/4 cup toasted sunflower seeds (for a nice crunch)

For the Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar (or lemon juice)
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional for sweetness)
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

How to Make Cold Pasta Salad with Sweet Corn and Herbs

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by cooking the pasta according to the package instructions. Opt for al dente pasta, as it will hold its shape better when mixed with the other ingredients. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and rinse it under cold water to stop the cooking process. This also helps to cool down the pasta quickly, making it perfect for a cold salad.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables and Corn

While the pasta is cooling, prepare the other ingredients. If you’re using fresh corn, remove the kernels from the cob. You can do this by standing the cob upright on a cutting board and running a sharp knife down the sides. If you don’t have fresh corn, frozen corn will work just as well—just make sure to thaw it beforehand.

Dice the cucumber, bell pepper, and red onion. Chop the fresh parsley and basil finely, and set them aside for later.

Step 3: Make the Dressing

In a small bowl or jar, combine the olive oil, apple cider vinegar (or lemon juice), Dijon mustard, honey, and minced garlic. Whisk or shake well to combine all the ingredients. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. The dressing should be tangy, slightly sweet, and well-balanced. If you like more acidity, add an extra splash of vinegar or lemon juice.

Step 4: Combine Everything

In a large mixing bowl, add the cooled pasta, sweet corn, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, and fresh herbs. If you’re adding feta cheese and sunflower seeds, include them now as well. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Be sure to coat all the ingredients evenly with the dressing.

Step 5: Chill and Serve

Once everything is mixed together, cover the bowl and refrigerate the pasta salad for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. The salad will be even better if allowed to chill for a few hours or overnight. This makes it perfect for meal prepping or preparing ahead of time for a gathering.

Before serving, give the salad a final toss and taste one last time to check for seasoning. You can add a little more salt, pepper, or lemon juice, depending on your preference.

Variations to Try

This Cold Pasta Salad with Sweet Corn and Herbs is highly adaptable. Feel free to make these adjustments to suit your tastes or dietary needs:

Protein Additions:

  • Grilled Chicken: Add diced grilled chicken breast for extra protein and a smoky flavor.
  • Chickpeas: If you prefer a vegetarian option, roasted or canned chickpeas are a great plant-based protein addition.
  • Shrimp: For a seafood twist, toss in some cooked shrimp for a light and flavorful protein.

Vegetables:

  • Tomatoes: Cherry or grape tomatoes add a burst of juicy sweetness that complements the other ingredients.
  • Avocado: Add some creamy avocado for a rich texture and extra nutrition.
  • Carrots: Shredded carrots can provide a crunchy contrast and a sweet flavor.

Cheese Alternatives:

  • Goat Cheese: If you’re not a fan of feta, goat cheese is a great alternative that adds a tangy and creamy texture.
  • Parmesan: Freshly grated Parmesan can also add a salty, umami flavor to the salad.

Herbs and Seasonings:

  • Mint: Fresh mint pairs wonderfully with sweet corn and adds a refreshing note.
  • Oregano or Thyme: For a more Mediterranean flavor, fresh oregano or thyme can bring an earthy depth to the salad.

Gluten-Free Option:

If you’re looking for a gluten-free alternative, simply swap out the regular pasta for gluten-free pasta made from rice, quinoa, or corn.

The Benefits of Corn in Your Diet

Sweet corn is not only delicious, but it also comes with a variety of health benefits. It’s a great source of dietary fiber, which promotes digestive health and helps to maintain a healthy weight. Corn is also rich in antioxidants like lutein and zeaxanthin, which are beneficial for eye health. Additionally, it provides essential vitamins and minerals like Vitamin B, magnesium, and folate.

Incorporating sweet corn into this cold pasta salad not only enhances the flavor but also adds a nutritious punch. Whether it’s fresh or frozen, sweet corn is a summer staple that can elevate your salad with its natural sweetness and satisfying crunch.

Meal Prep and Storage Tips

This cold pasta salad is perfect for meal prepping. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, making it an excellent choice for busy weeks. To keep it fresh, store it in an airtight container, and make sure to keep the dressing separate if you plan to make it ahead of time. This will prevent the salad from becoming soggy. Simply toss the dressing in right before serving.

If you plan on keeping it for multiple days, you may want to add any delicate ingredients, like avocado, just before serving to maintain their freshness and texture.

Why This Recipe is Perfect for Summer

This cold pasta salad with sweet corn and herbs is the ultimate summer dish. It’s light, refreshing, and full of seasonal ingredients. The vibrant colors of the vegetables, the sweetness of the corn, and the fresh herbs make this salad visually appealing as well as delicious.

Moreover, this recipe is versatile enough to be served at a variety of occasions. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue, going on a picnic, or simply want a quick, no-cook meal for a hot day, this pasta salad will always be a crowd-pleaser. It’s simple to make, yet impressive enough to serve guests, and you can adjust the ingredients to suit your tastes or dietary preferences.

Final Thoughts

This Cold Pasta Salad with Sweet Corn and Herbs is a must-try recipe that combines simplicity with flavor. The sweet corn and fresh herbs create a beautiful contrast, while the light dressing brings everything together. It’s a dish that celebrates the flavors of summer and makes for a great addition to any meal. Whether you’re enjoying it on a sunny afternoon or prepping it for the week ahead, this pasta salad is sure to become a favorite in your recipe rotation.

With endless possibilities for variations and customization, this cold pasta salad is bound to become a staple in your summer meal planning. So grab some pasta, fresh corn, and herbs, and enjoy this deliciously refreshing dish today!
