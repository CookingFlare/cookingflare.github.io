How to Make Instant Pot Beef and Vegetable Soup


Nothing hits the spot quite like a warm bowl of beef and vegetable soup, brimming with savory broth, tender chunks of meat, and colorful, nutrient-packed veggies. It’s hearty, comforting, and satisfying—a dish that warms both your heart and your kitchen. Best of all, using an Instant Pot allows you to achieve high-quality flavors with minimal effort and time compared to traditional stovetop or slow-cooker methods. In this post, we’ll walk you through crafting a delicious Instant Pot beef and vegetable soup that will become a favorite for busy weeknights and cozy weekends alike.

Ingredients

The key to developing a delicious soup lies in selecting fresh, high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Protein:

  • Beef stew meat, cubed (about 1.5 pounds): Choose well-marbled pieces that will become tender during cooking. You can also opt for chuck roast and cut it into bite-sized portions.

For the Vegetables:

  • Carrots (2-3, sliced into rounds): A sweet and earthy touch that pairs beautifully with the beef.
  • Celery (2-3 stalks, diced): Adds crunch and subtle flavor that complements the soup.
  • Onion (1 medium, diced): The aromatic base of the dish.
  • Potatoes (3 medium, peeled and chopped into chunks): Perfect for giving the soup a filling, starchy component. Yukon gold or red potatoes work best for their creamy texture.
  • Frozen green beans (1 cup): Alternatively, fresh green beans work well.
  • Frozen peas (½ cup): Adds pops of color and slight sweetness to balance the savory components.

For the Base:

  • Beef broth (5 cups): Use a rich, high-quality broth for deep, hearty flavor.
  • Tomato paste (2 tablespoons): Adds depth and enhances the soup’s savory and slightly tangy notes.
  • Diced tomatoes (1 can, about 14.5 ounces): Provide brightness and texture to the broth.

Seasonings and Aromatics:

  • Minced garlic (3 cloves): Essential for balancing and enhancing flavors.
  • Bay leaves (2): Impart a subtle yet aromatic depth to the soup.
  • Dried thyme (1 teaspoon): Adds earthiness that complements the beef.
  • Paprika (1 teaspoon): For warmth and a subtle smoky undertone.
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste): Essential for seasoning and bringing the soup’s flavors alive.

Optional Add-Ins:

  • Fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish): Brightens up the finished dish while elevating presentation.
  • Crusty bread: Serve alongside your soup for dipping or pairing.

Instructions

Follow these step-by-step directions to make this wonderfully flavorful beef and vegetable soup in your Instant Pot. Remember, the process is straightforward, but the results are deeply satisfying.

Step 1: Prep Your Ingredients

Before diving into cooking, take the time to prepare your ingredients. Chop all vegetables into uniform pieces to ensure even cooking. If your beef isn’t pre-cut, trim and dice it into bite-sized cubes. Gather your seasonings and set everything near the Instant Pot to streamline the process.

Step 2: Sear the Beef

Turn on your Instant Pot’s sauté function and let it heat up. Add a drizzle of vegetable oil or olive oil to the pot and wait for it to shimmer. Carefully place the beef cubes in the pot in a single layer (you may need to work in batches to avoid overcrowding). Season the meat with salt and pepper as it sears. Brown the beef on all sides—this step adds a rich layer of flavor to your soup base. Once browned, remove the beef and set it aside.

Step 3: Build Flavor in the Pot

Without turning off the sauté function, add diced onion to the pot and stir frequently until softened. Follow with minced garlic, cooking for an additional 30 seconds until fragrant. Add tomato paste and stir well, letting it caramelize slightly. Deglaze the pot with a splash of beef broth, scraping up the browned bits stuck to the bottom. These bits, known as fond, are packed with flavor and will elevate your soup.

Step 4: Add Remaining Ingredients

Return the browned beef to the Instant Pot. Next, add carrots, celery, potatoes, green beans, peas, diced tomatoes (including the juice), bay leaves, thyme, and paprika. Pour in the beef broth, ensuring that the ingredients are covered with liquid. Stir gently to combine everything evenly.

Step 5: Pressure Cook

Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Select the manual pressure-cook option, set the timer to 22 minutes on high pressure, and let the Instant Pot work its magic. The pressure cooking process allows the beef to become tender while melding the flavors of the vegetables and broth.

Step 6: Natural Pressure Release

Once the timer on the Instant Pot completes, perform a natural pressure release for about 10 minutes. This ensures the soup continues to cook gently and avoids splattering when you eventually release the remaining pressure manually. After the 10-minute natural release, carefully switch the valve to “Venting” and let out the remaining steam.

Step 7: Final Touches

Unlock and remove the Instant Pot lid. Give the soup a good stir and taste to adjust seasoning with additional salt or pepper if needed. Remove the bay leaves before serving. If desired, sprinkle chopped parsley on top for a fresh, herbal finish.

Serving Suggestions

This Instant Pot beef and vegetable soup is versatile and pairs beautifully with various sides. Serve it in large bowls alongside crusty bread, warm dinner rolls, or even rice for a heartier meal. For added indulgence, you can top the soup with a dollop of sour cream or shredded cheese.

The soup also works perfectly as leftovers—the flavors deepen overnight, making it an even tastier option for lunch the next day. Simply store the soup in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days or freeze for longer storage.

Tips for Success

Choose the Right Beef

The quality of the beef you use plays a significant role in the soup’s outcome. Look for cuts like chuck roast or stew meat, which become incredibly tender over long cooking periods. Avoid leaner cuts that may dry out or turn tough in the Instant Pot.

Layer Flavors Strategically

Cooking in layers—searing the beef, sautéing aromatics, and caramelizing tomato paste—creates depth and complexity in the soup that’s hard to beat. You’ll notice a remarkable difference if you take the time to follow these steps.

Customize Your Vegetables

While the recipe provides a solid mix of vegetables, you can easily swap out or add different ones based on your preferences or seasonal availability. Consider including parsnips, sweet potatoes, zucchini, or corn for unique twists.

Use High-Quality Broth

A good beef broth serves as the backbone of this soup, making it robust and savory. Invest in a high-quality store-bought variety or use homemade broth to take your soup to the next level.

Why Use an Instant Pot?

The Instant Pot is a game-changer for soup recipes. Traditional beef and vegetable soup can take hours to simmer, but with pressure cooking, you can achieve complex, slow-cooked flavors in a fraction of the time. The Instant Pot also locks in nutrients and aromas, resulting in a soup that’s as healthy as it is flavorful.

Conclusion

Making Instant Pot beef and vegetable soup is a perfect way to unite ease, taste, and nutrition in one comforting dish. Whether you’re cooking for a busy family or simply craving homey flavors, this recipe offers exactly what you need. With fresh ingredients, simple techniques, and a time-saving Instant Pot, you can create a filling and delicious soup that will leave everyone asking for seconds.

So, next time you’re looking to whip up something hearty and wholesome, pull out your Instant Pot and let this recipe guide you to a standout meal. Happy cooking!
