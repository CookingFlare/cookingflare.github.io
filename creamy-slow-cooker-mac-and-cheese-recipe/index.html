Creamy Slow Cooker Mac and Cheese Recipe


If you’re a fan of comfort food and easy cooking, then you’re in for a treat with this creamy slow cooker mac and cheese recipe. Imagine coming home to the irresistible aroma of cheesy goodness wafting through your kitchen. This recipe is the ultimate combination of convenience and indulgence, making it perfect for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. Let’s dive into the details of this delightful dish.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz (450g) elbow macaroni
  • 4 cups (950ml) whole milk
  • 12 oz (340g) evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 cups (300g) shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups (200g) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup (50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp (30g) all-purpose flour
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (50g) breadcrumbs for topping

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Slow Cooker: Begin by greasing the slow cooker with a light coating of cooking spray or butter to prevent the mac and cheese from sticking.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the whole milk, evaporated milk, melted butter, shredded cheddar, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese. Add the garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Whisk in the flour until the mixture is smooth and well-combined.

  3. Add the Pasta: Pour the uncooked elbow macaroni into the slow cooker, then pour the cheese and milk mixture over the pasta. Gently stir to ensure the pasta is evenly coated with the cheesy mixture.

  4. Set and Forget: Cover the slow cooker and set it to cook on low for 2-3 hours. It’s important to resist the temptation to lift the lid and stir the mac and cheese, as this can affect the cooking process.

  5. Check for Doneness: After 2 hours, check the mac and cheese for doneness. The pasta should be tender, and the cheese should be melted and creamy. If the mixture appears too dry, you can add a splash of milk and gently stir to adjust the consistency.

  6. Optional Crispy Topping: If you desire a crispy topping, sprinkle breadcrumbs over the mac and cheese during the last 30 minutes of cooking.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the mac and cheese is ready, serve it hot and garnish with a sprinkle of paprika or fresh herbs for a pop of color and flavor.

Why Choose the Slow Cooker Method?

Using a slow cooker for mac and cheese offers several advantages. Firstly, it requires minimal effort - simply combine the ingredients, set it, and forget it until it’s time to eat. This makes it a fantastic option for busy individuals or families. Additionally, the slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in an incredibly creamy and rich mac and cheese that’s sure to impress.

Tips for Success:

  • Cheese Selection: While the recipe specifies cheddar, mozzarella, and Parmesan, feel free to experiment with your favorite cheeses to create a custom blend.

  • Consistency: For a creamier texture, you can add more milk or cream towards the end of the cooking time to achieve your desired consistency.

  • Customization: Get creative with add-ins such as crispy bacon, diced ham, or sautéed vegetables to add extra depth of flavor to your mac and cheese.

In Conclusion:

This creamy slow cooker mac and cheese recipe is a game-changer for anyone who appreciates a hassle-free, decadent meal. Whether you’re hosting a casual gathering or craving a hearty dinner, this recipe is a surefire way to please the crowd without breaking a sweat. Embrace the simplicity and deliciousness of slow cooker cooking with this delightful mac and cheese dish. Happy cooking!
