Homemade Mexican Refried Beans: A Classic Side Dish

4 min · 820 words

There’s something undeniably comforting about a warm, flavorful side of refried beans. Whether you’re serving them alongside your favorite Mexican dishes or as a standalone dip, homemade Mexican refried beans are a classic that never goes out of style. In this recipe, I’ll walk you through the process of creating rich, creamy refried beans right in your own kitchen. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried pinto beans
  • 1 medium white onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Beans

Start by rinsing the pinto beans under cold water to remove any dirt or debris. In a large pot, place the beans and cover them with water. Allow the beans to soak overnight or for at least 8 hours. This soaking process will help soften the beans and reduce their cooking time.

Step 2: Cook the Beans

After soaking, drain the beans and return them to the pot. Add enough water to cover the beans by about 2 inches. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook the beans for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until they are tender. Be sure to check the water level periodically and add more if needed to keep the beans submerged.

Step 3: Sauté the Aromatics

In a separate skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is soft and translucent. Stir in the ground cumin, chili powder, and paprika, allowing the spices to toast for a minute or two, releasing their flavors.

Step 4: Mash the Beans

Once the beans are tender, remove them from the heat and drain off any excess cooking liquid, reserving some of the liquid. Transfer the cooked beans to a large bowl and mash them using a potato masher or the back of a spoon. For a creamier texture, you can use a food processor or blender.

Step 5: Refry the Beans

Add the sautéed aromatics to the mashed beans, stirring to combine. Place the bean mixture back into the skillet and cook over medium heat. Gradually add some of the reserved cooking liquid as needed to achieve your desired consistency. Continue cooking and stirring the beans until they thicken and develop a creamy texture.

Step 6: Season and Serve

Season the refried beans with salt to taste, and adjust the seasoning as needed. Once the beans reach the perfect creamy consistency, they’re ready to be served. Whether as a side dish, a filling for burritos, or a dip for tortilla chips, homemade Mexican refried beans are sure to be a hit!

Final Thoughts

With their velvety texture and robust flavor, homemade Mexican refried beans are a versatile and delicious addition to any meal. While the process of cooking dried beans from scratch requires a bit of time and patience, the end result is well worth the effort. So, next time you’re craving the comforting flavors of Mexican cuisine, consider whipping up a batch of these flavorful refried beans. Your taste buds will thank you!

Enjoy your culinary adventure in the kitchen, and savor the satisfaction of creating a classic side dish that’s as enjoyable to make as it is to eat. Cheers to homemade Mexican refried beans!
