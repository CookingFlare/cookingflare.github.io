5 Iconic Dishes from the Italian Riviera to Savor


The Italian Riviera, with its stunning coastal scenery and charming villages, is a culinary treasure trove waiting to be explored. This region, known as “La Liguria,” boasts a rich tapestry of flavors influenced by its Mediterranean climate, fresh seafood, and local produce. Here, we delve into five iconic dishes that embody the essence of the Italian Riviera, each telling a story of tradition, culture, and passion for food.

1. Trofie al Pesto

A Taste of Tradition

Trofie al Pesto is perhaps the most famous dish from the Ligurian region. This simple yet vibrant pasta dish showcases the rich flavors of fresh basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, and extra virgin olive oil, blended together to create a luscious pesto sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 400g trofie pasta (or any short pasta)
  • 100g fresh basil leaves
  • 50g pine nuts, toasted
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 120ml extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: Potatoes and green beans

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pesto: In a food processor, combine the basil leaves, garlic, pine nuts, and Parmesan cheese. Pulse until finely chopped. Gradually add the olive oil while blending until a smooth paste forms. Season with salt to taste.

  2. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. If using, dice the potatoes and add them to the water along with the trofie. After about 5 minutes, add the green beans, if using, and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente.

  3. Combine: Drain the pasta and vegetables, reserving a cup of the cooking water. In a large bowl, mix the pasta with the pesto, adding a splash of the reserved water to achieve the desired creaminess.

  4. Serve: Divide into bowls and drizzle a little extra olive oil on top. Garnish with additional pine nuts and Parmesan if desired.

Savoring the Dish

Trofie al Pesto is not just a dish; it’s a celebration of Liguria’s vibrant agriculture. Pair it with a glass of local white wine, such as Vermentino, to enhance the meal’s freshness.

2. Focaccia di Recco

A Cheesy Delight

Focaccia di Recco hails from the town of Recco and is a unique take on traditional focaccia. What sets this dish apart is its thin, crispy texture filled with creamy cheese, typically Stracchino, making it an irresistible treat.

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 250ml water
  • 50ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 250g Stracchino cheese (or similar soft cheese)
  • Additional olive oil for brushing
  • Sea salt for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Gradually add water and olive oil, mixing until a smooth dough forms. Knead for about 10 minutes until elastic. Let the dough rest for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Assemble the Focaccia: Divide the dough into two equal portions. Roll out one portion to form a thin round. Place it on a greased baking sheet. Spread the Stracchino cheese evenly over the dough, leaving a border around the edges.

  3. Cover and Bake: Roll out the second portion of dough and place it over the cheese. Pinch the edges to seal. Brush the top with olive oil and sprinkle with sea salt. Bake in a preheated oven at 220°C (428°F) for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

  4. Serve Hot: Allow to cool slightly before slicing. Enjoy it warm as a snack or appetizer.

Enjoying a Slice

Focaccia di Recco is best enjoyed fresh out of the oven. Its cheesy, crispy goodness pairs beautifully with a chilled glass of local white wine or a refreshing Ligurian spritz.

3. Cappon Magro

A Festive Seafood Platter

Cappon Magro is a traditional Ligurian dish that exemplifies the region’s love for seafood. This elaborate seafood platter is typically served during festive occasions and showcases a variety of shellfish and fish arranged beautifully with colorful vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 500g mixed seafood (shrimps, mussels, clams, squid)
  • 200g white fish fillets (like cod or sole)
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • For the Green Sauce:
    • 100g fresh parsley
    • 50g basil
    • 2 cloves garlic
    • 100ml extra virgin olive oil
    • 50ml white wine vinegar
    • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Seafood: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the seafood and cook until just cooked through. Remove and let cool. Retain the cooking water.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: In the same water, boil the potatoes until tender. Add the carrots, zucchini, and broccoli, cooking until just tender. Drain and let cool.

  3. Make the Green Sauce: In a food processor, combine parsley, basil, garlic, olive oil, vinegar, and salt. Blend until smooth.

  4. Assemble the Platter: On a large serving dish, arrange the seafood and vegetables artistically. Drizzle with green sauce and enjoy!

A Culinary Experience

Cappon Magro is not just a meal; it’s a visual masterpiece. This dish is a feast for the eyes as much as it is for the palate, often enjoyed with a glass of Rosé or a sparkling wine to celebrate its vibrant flavors.

4. Farinata

A Chickpea Flour Delight

Farinata, a simple yet delicious dish, is a staple of Ligurian cuisine made from chickpea flour. Its origins trace back to ancient times, and it is considered a rustic favorite, ideal as a snack or appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 250g chickpea flour
  • 750ml water
  • 60ml extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Fresh rosemary (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Batter: In a large bowl, whisk together chickpea flour, water, salt, and half the olive oil until smooth. Let it rest for at least 2 hours.

  2. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 220°C (428°F). Place a round baking pan in the oven to heat.

  3. Bake the Farinata: Carefully take out the hot pan and pour in the remaining olive oil, ensuring it coats the bottom. Pour the rested batter into the pan, spreading evenly. Sprinkle with black pepper and rosemary, if using.

  4. Cook Until Golden: Bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are crispy and the top is golden brown.

  5. Serve: Slice into wedges and serve hot or at room temperature.

A Versatile Dish

Farinata is naturally gluten-free and can be enjoyed plain or topped with various toppings like caramelized onions, olives, or even sautéed mushrooms. Pair it with a local beer or a light white wine for a delightful experience.

5. Torta Pasqualina

A Savory Easter Pie

Torta Pasqualina is a traditional Ligurian pie that celebrates the arrival of spring, often enjoyed during Easter festivities. This savory dish is filled with greens, ricotta, and eggs, encased in a flaky crust.

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 250ml water
  • 100ml olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 500g ricotta cheese
  • 300g spinach or Swiss chard, cooked and chopped
  • 4 eggs
  • 100g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Nutmeg, salt, and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: Mix flour, salt, water, and olive oil in a bowl to form a dough. Knead until smooth. Divide into two portions, wrap in plastic, and let rest for 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, mix the ricotta cheese, chopped greens, Parmesan, and seasonings. Add 3 beaten eggs, reserving one for brushing the crust.

  3. Assemble the Pie: Roll out one portion of dough to fit a pie dish. Place it in the dish and add the filling. Make indentations in the filling and crack the reserved egg into each indentation. Roll out the second portion of dough and cover the pie, sealing the edges.

  4. Bake the Pie: Brush the top with beaten egg and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 40-50 minutes until golden brown.

  5. Serve: Let cool slightly before slicing. Enjoy warm or at room temperature.

Celebrating with Torta Pasqualina

This pie embodies the flavors of spring and is perfect for festive gatherings. Pair it with a crisp salad and a light-bodied white wine to enhance its rich flavors.

Conclusion

The Italian Riviera is a culinary paradise, with each dish reflecting the region’s natural bounty and rich traditions. From the fresh, aromatic Trofie al Pesto to the festive Cappon Magro, these five iconic dishes invite you to savor Liguria’s flavors. As you explore these recipes, let the spirit of the Italian Riviera inspire your culinary adventures at home. Buon appetito!
