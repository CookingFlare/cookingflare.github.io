Savory Nut-Free Stuffed Bell Pepper Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious and wholesome meal that’s both nut-free and packed with flavor, you’ve come to the right place! This savory stuffed bell pepper recipe is sure to satisfy your taste buds while ensuring the safety of those with nut allergies. With a combination of nutritious ingredients and a burst of delightful flavors, these stuffed bell peppers will become a new family favorite. So, let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color you prefer)
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for those who like it spicy)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Prepare a baking dish by lightly greasing it with oil or non-stick spray.

  2. Start by preparing the bell peppers. Cut off the tops and remove the seeds and membranes. Rinse them thoroughly and set aside.

  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.

  4. Add the diced onions and minced garlic to the skillet and sauté for a few minutes until they become fragrant and translucent.

  5. Stir in the diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, black pepper, paprika, chili powder, and cayenne pepper (if using). Let the mixture simmer for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Add the cooked rice to the skillet and mix well, ensuring all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  7. Spoon the filling into each bell pepper, packing it tightly. Place the stuffed bell peppers upright in the prepared baking dish.

  8. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for another 10-15 minutes, or until the bell peppers have softened and slightly charred.

  9. Once the stuffed bell peppers are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.

  10. Serve the stuffed bell peppers on a plate, garnished with freshly chopped parsley for an added burst of freshness.

Enjoy Your Nut-Free Delight!

These savory nut-free stuffed bell peppers are perfect for a comforting weeknight dinner or a delightful dish to impress your guests. The combination of juicy ground beef, aromatic herbs, and tender bell peppers creates a symphony of flavors that will leave everyone craving for more. Plus, with the added benefit of being nut-free, it’s a great option for those with nut allergies or dietary restrictions.

Feel free to experiment with different fillings or add your own twist to this recipe. You can also serve these stuffed bell peppers alongside a side salad or your favorite grain for a complete and satisfying meal. Enjoy the deliciousness and share it with your loved ones!

Remember, cooking is all about creativity and personalization, so don’t be afraid to make it your own. Happy cooking!
