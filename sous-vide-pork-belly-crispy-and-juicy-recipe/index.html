Sous Vide Pork Belly: Crispy and Juicy Recipe


Pork belly, a delicious and tender cut of meat, has gained popularity for its rich flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture. If you’ve ever wondered how to prepare this delectable dish to perfection, then sous vide cooking is your answer. This method ensures the pork belly remains incredibly tender and juicy, with the added bonus of a crispy, golden-brown skin that brings out the full potential of the meat.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of cooking sous vide pork belly—step by step—so that you can enjoy a restaurant-quality meal in the comfort of your own home. Whether you’re an experienced sous vide chef or a first-timer, this recipe will help you create a dish that’s sure to impress.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, a French term meaning “under vacuum,” is a method of cooking food in a temperature-controlled water bath. The food is sealed in an airtight bag, then cooked at a precise and consistent temperature for an extended period. This results in perfectly cooked meat, poultry, or vegetables with unparalleled tenderness and flavor.

The sous vide process allows the pork belly to retain its moisture while ensuring it’s cooked evenly from edge to edge. No more dry or overcooked pork belly. The result is a juicy, tender, and flavorful bite that’s simply irresistible.

Why Sous Vide is Perfect for Pork Belly

Pork belly is a fatty cut of meat, and sous vide cooking is perfect for rendering down that fat while keeping the meat tender and moist. The beauty of sous vide is that you can cook the pork belly low and slow, allowing the fat to break down and infuse the meat with flavor. By using sous vide, you can ensure the pork belly reaches that ideal, melt-in-your-mouth consistency while achieving a crispy exterior.

The traditional method of cooking pork belly usually involves roasting it at high heat to crisp up the skin, but it can often result in uneven cooking and tough meat. Sous vide ensures that every inch of the pork belly is cooked to perfection before you finish it off with a quick sear.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this crispy and juicy sous vide pork belly, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds pork belly, skin-on
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary (optional)
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Equipment

  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • Large pot or container for the water bath
  • Vacuum sealer or zip-lock bags
  • Cast-iron skillet or heavy pan (for searing)
  • Tongs
  • Knife for scoring the pork belly

Preparing the Pork Belly

The first step in preparing sous vide pork belly is to prep the meat itself. Since pork belly comes with skin, it’s important to score the skin to allow the fat to render and crisp up during the final searing step.

Step 1: Score the Skin

Place the pork belly on a cutting board, skin-side up. Using a sharp knife, make shallow cuts across the skin, about 1-inch apart. Be careful not to cut into the meat itself—only the skin and fat layer should be scored. This will help the fat render more easily, resulting in a crispy skin.

Step 2: Season the Pork Belly

Season the pork belly generously with salt and black pepper. If you’d like to add a little extra flavor, you can rub minced garlic onto the meat, along with fresh rosemary and thyme. These herbs add an aromatic touch to the dish.

If you prefer a slightly sweet and savory finish, you can also drizzle the pork belly with a bit of soy sauce and honey. This will caramelize during the searing step, enhancing the flavor and adding a beautiful glaze to the pork belly.

Step 3: Vacuum Seal the Pork Belly

Once the pork belly is seasoned, it’s time to vacuum-seal it. If you have a vacuum sealer, place the seasoned pork belly inside a vacuum bag, ensuring it’s laid flat. Seal the bag tightly to remove as much air as possible. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use the water displacement method with a zip-lock bag.

To use the water displacement method, place the pork belly in a large zip-lock bag. Seal the bag almost all the way, leaving just a small opening. Submerge the bag in a container of water, and as the water pushes out the air, seal the bag completely.

Cooking the Pork Belly Sous Vide

Step 4: Set Up Your Sous Vide Water Bath

Fill a large pot or container with water and attach your sous vide precision cooker. Set the temperature to 170°F (77°C) for a tender and juicy pork belly. The low and slow temperature will allow the pork belly to cook evenly and retain its moisture.

Once the water has reached the desired temperature, place the vacuum-sealed bag with the pork belly into the water bath. Make sure the bag is fully submerged, and clip it to the side of the container if necessary to ensure it stays in place.

Step 5: Cook the Pork Belly

Let the pork belly cook in the sous vide bath for about 10 to 12 hours. Yes, you read that right—this is a long cooking time, but it’s necessary to break down the fat and ensure the pork belly becomes tender. Don’t rush this process—low and slow is the way to go!

After 10 to 12 hours, the pork belly will be fully cooked, tender, and infused with all the flavors from the seasoning and herbs.

Crispying the Skin

Step 6: Sear the Pork Belly

Once the pork belly has finished cooking, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully take the pork belly out of the bag. The meat will be very tender at this point, so be gentle when handling it.

To achieve that irresistible crispy skin, heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of olive oil and let it heat up until shimmering. Place the pork belly, skin-side down, into the hot pan. Press down gently to ensure the skin makes contact with the pan, and let it sear for about 3-5 minutes.

You’ll know it’s ready when the skin becomes golden brown and crispy. If needed, you can move the pork belly around the pan to get an even sear. The fat will render out during this step, and the skin will become beautifully crispy.

Step 7: Rest and Slice

Once the pork belly is perfectly crispy, remove it from the pan and let it rest for a few minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring each bite is tender and juicy.

After resting, slice the pork belly into bite-sized pieces. You’ll notice the contrast in texture between the tender meat and the crispy skin—it’s a perfect combination that will have your taste buds dancing.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide pork belly is incredibly versatile and pairs well with many sides. Here are a few ideas for serving your pork belly:

  • Over rice: Serve the pork belly over a bed of steamed rice for a simple and comforting dish. The richness of the pork pairs wonderfully with the mild rice.
  • In tacos: Thinly slice the pork belly and serve it in soft tortillas with fresh salsa, guacamole, and a squeeze of lime. It makes for a delicious taco filling.
  • With roasted vegetables: Pair the pork belly with roasted vegetables such as carrots, potatoes, and Brussels sprouts for a hearty and satisfying meal.
  • On a salad: For a lighter option, add slices of crispy pork belly to a fresh salad with mixed greens, avocado, and a tangy vinaigrette.

The possibilities are endless when it comes to serving sous vide pork belly. Experiment with different sides and sauces to find your perfect combination!

Tips and Tricks for the Perfect Pork Belly

  • Time is key: Don’t rush the cooking time. The 10-12 hours of sous vide cooking allow the pork belly to become incredibly tender and juicy. If you try to cook it faster, the fat might not render properly, resulting in a less-than-perfect texture.
  • Use a heavy pan: For the best crispy skin, use a cast-iron skillet or a heavy pan. It retains heat well and ensures even browning of the skin.
  • Rest the meat: After searing the pork belly, let it rest before slicing. This helps lock in the juices and ensures each bite is as flavorful as possible.
  • Experiment with flavors: Feel free to get creative with your seasonings. Adding a bit of brown sugar or maple syrup to the seasoning mix can enhance the caramelization and create a deliciously sweet crust.

Conclusion

Sous vide pork belly is a game-changer when it comes to cooking this delicious, fatty cut of meat. The combination of perfectly tender, juicy pork belly with a crispy, golden-brown skin is unbeatable. By using the sous vide method, you can ensure your pork belly is cooked to perfection every time, with minimal effort and guaranteed results.

Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or just craving a comforting meal, this sous vide pork belly recipe is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, set up your sous vide bath, and prepare yourself for a culinary experience that’s both effortless and indulgent. Enjoy!
