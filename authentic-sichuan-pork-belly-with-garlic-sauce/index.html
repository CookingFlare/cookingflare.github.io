Authentic Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce


When it comes to bold flavors and dynamic culinary traditions, Sichuan cuisine stands out as one of China’s most iconic and celebrated regional styles. Known for its fiery spices, rich aromas, and harmonious balance of flavors, Sichuan dishes have captivated the taste buds of food enthusiasts across the globe. Among these dishes, Authentic Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce exemplifies everything that makes this cuisine so remarkable: tender slices of pork belly, an aromatic garlic-infused sauce, and the hallmark balance of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet notes.

In this blog post, we’re diving deep into how to create this dish from scratch. Whether you’re looking to recreate this Sichuan classic at home or simply learn more about its cultural significance, this guide will walk you through every step with engaging details, helpful tips, and cooking techniques for success. Prepare yourself for a gastronomic adventure filled with bold flavors and authentic charm!

A Brief History of Sichuan Cuisine

Before we dive into the recipe, it’s important to understand why Sichuan cuisine is so revered. With its origins rooted in the Sichuan Province of southwestern China, this cuisine is renowned for its liberal use of garlic, ginger, chili peppers, and the unmistakable Sichuan peppercorn, which imparts a signature tingly, numbing sensation to the palate. This unique flavor profile—referred to locally as málà (麻辣), meaning “numbing and spicy”—makes Sichuan dishes globally recognizable.

Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce (also called 蒜泥白肉, pronounced suàn ní bái ròu) embodies everything that Sichuan cuisine stands for. The dish combines indulgently fatty pork belly slices with a robust sauce featuring fresh garlic, chili oil, vinegar, soy sauce, and sesame seeds. Each bite is a celebration of contrasts: the rich, silky pork is balanced beautifully by the punchy sauce, making this dish a festive addition to any meal.

Ingredients for Authentic Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce

Creating an authentic version of this dish requires attention to detail and the use of high-quality ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pork Belly:

  • 500g pork belly, skin-on
  • 2 scallions (whole), cut into large sections
  • 5 slices of fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine (or dry cooking wine)
  • Water, enough to cover the pork belly in a pot

For the Garlic Sauce (Suàn Ní Jiàng):

  • 6 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Chinese black vinegar (or rice vinegar)
  • 1½ tablespoons chili oil, with sediment (La Jiao You)
  • ½ tablespoon sesame paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped cilantro, for garnish
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds

Step-by-Step Recipe: How to Make Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce

1. Prepare and Cook the Pork Belly

Start with the pork belly, the star of the dish. Wash the pork belly thoroughly under cold running water to remove any impurities. Place it in a large pot and add the scallions, ginger slices, Sichuan peppercorns, and Shaoxing wine. Fill the pot with enough water to fully submerge the pork belly.

Bring the pot to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a simmer. Cook the pork belly for 35–40 minutes or until fully tender. You can check doneness by piercing the meat with a chopstick; it should slide through easily without resistance.

Once the pork belly is cooked, remove it from the pot and let it cool slightly. To ensure even slicing, refrigerate the cooked pork for 20–30 minutes to firm it up. This step is optional but useful for achieving neat slices.

2. Prepare the Garlic Sauce

While the pork belly is cooling, prepare the sauce. Mince the garlic finely or use a garlic press for a smoother texture. In a small mixing bowl, combine the garlic with soy sauce, black vinegar, chili oil, sesame paste, and sugar. Stir the ingredients until fully integrated, creating a rich and aromatic sauce.

Taste the sauce and adjust the balance of flavors as needed. If you prefer the sauce spicier, add more chili oil. For a sweeter profile, increase the sugar slightly. The goal is to achieve harmony between the salty savoriness of the soy sauce, the tangy acidity of the vinegar, and the fragrant heat of the chili oil.

3. Slice the Pork Belly

Once the pork belly is firmed up, use a sharp knife to slice it into thin, even pieces. Traditional 蒜泥白肉 recipes recommend slicing the pork belly as thinly as possible. Each piece should have a visible layer of melt-in-your-mouth fat, lean meat, and the chewy skin.

Arrange the slices neatly in a serving dish, either in a layered circular pattern or stacked elegantly. Presentation matters, as this dish is as much visual as it is flavorful!

4. Assemble and Garnish

Pour the prepared garlic sauce generously over the arranged pork belly slices, ensuring every piece is coated. Garnish the dish with finely chopped cilantro and toasted sesame seeds for added texture and fragrance.

Tips for the Perfect Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce

  1. Choose Quality Pork Belly: Look for pork belly with evenly distributed layers of fat and meat for the best texture. Fresh is always better!
  2. Balance the Sauce: The garlic sauce is the soul of this dish, so take your time perfecting it. Adjust the ratios to suit your palate, keeping the balance of salty, tangy, and spicy flavors.
  3. Use Homemade Chili Oil: Store-bought chili oil works, but making your own chili oil elevates the flavor even further. Use dried chili flakes, Sichuan peppercorns, garlic, and neutral oil for an authentic homemade version.
  4. Serve at Room Temperature: Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce is typically served slightly chilled or at room temperature, allowing the flavors to shine through.

Pairing Suggestions and Serving Ideas

This dish is versatile and works beautifully as an appetizer, side dish, or even the centerpiece of a multi-course Sichuan feast. Pair it with steamed rice or accompany it with classic Sichuan dishes like Kung Pao Chicken, Ma Po Tofu, or Dandan Noodles to round out the meal.

For beverages, consider serving this dish with lightly sweetened Chinese tea, which complements the bold, garlicky flavors while balancing the richness of the pork belly.

Final Thoughts: Why You’ll Love This Dish

Authentic Sichuan Pork Belly with Garlic Sauce is more than a recipe—it’s a window into the culinary traditions of Sichuan Province. Each bite tells a story of bold innovation and cultural heritage. Recreating this dish in your kitchen allows you to experience these flavors while mastering the techniques behind one of Sichuan’s most beloved classics.

Whether you’re new to Sichuan cuisine or an experienced home cook looking to expand your repertoire, this dish promises to impress. Its vibrant garlic sauce, tender pork belly, and stunning presentation make it both a crowd-pleaser and a personal indulgence.

So roll up your sleeves, bring the aromas of Sichuan to your kitchen, and get ready to delight your senses with this authentic recipe. Your next culinary adventure awaits!
