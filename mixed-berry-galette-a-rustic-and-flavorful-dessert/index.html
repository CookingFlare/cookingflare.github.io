Mixed Berry Galette: A Rustic and Flavorful Dessert


Are you looking for a delicious and impressive dessert that captures the essence of summer? Look no further than the Mixed Berry Galette. This rustic dessert is not only visually stunning but also bursting with the flavors of fresh berries. With its buttery crust and juicy filling, this galette is sure to become a favorite in your repertoire.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the egg wash:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon milk

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the crust

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. Add the cold butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Slowly add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Form the dough into a disc and wrap it in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Make the filling

  1. In another bowl, gently toss the mixed berries with the sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Let the mixture sit for about 15 minutes to allow the berries to release their juices.

Step 3: Assemble the galette

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter. Transfer the dough to the prepared baking sheet.

  3. Spoon the mixed berry filling onto the center of the dough, leaving a border of about 2 inches. Gently fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pleating as necessary.

Step 4: Bake and serve

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg and milk to make the egg wash. Brush the edges of the dough with the egg wash.

  2. Bake the galette for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

  3. Serve the Mixed Berry Galette warm or at room temperature. You can enjoy it on its own or add a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra touch of indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different combinations of berries to create your own unique flavor profile. Try adding some sliced peaches or nectarines for a delightful twist.
  • If you prefer a sweeter filling, increase the amount of sugar in the filling.
  • For a gluten-free version, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

The Mixed Berry Galette is a versatile dessert that can be enjoyed year-round. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving something sweet, this galette is sure to impress. The combination of buttery crust and juicy berries creates a harmonious balance of flavors and textures. So, grab your apron and get ready to indulge in this rustic and flavorful dessert!
