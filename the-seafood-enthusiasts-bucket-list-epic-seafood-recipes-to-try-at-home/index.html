The Seafood Enthusiast's Bucket List: Epic Seafood Recipes to Try at Home


Welcome seafood lovers, fellow food enthusiasts, and kitchen explorers! If you gaze into the deep blue sea, captivated by the diverse wonders and flavors it offers, you’re in for a treasured culinary adventure. In today’s post, we’ll embark on a quest to master the art of the ocean’s finest dishes – all from the comfort of your kitchen. Fire up your imagination and get ready to dive deep into a world where plump shrimp, succulent scallops, delicate lobster, and other sea treasures await your culinary prowess.

1. Sizzling Seafood Paella

Embark on a taste voyage to Spain with this vibrant, flavorful Paella. This hearty dish puts the spotlight on aromatic saffron, juicy mussels, plump shrimp, and tender calamari, all elevated by the traditional socarrat – that golden crust forming at the dish’s bottom.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Bomba or Calrose rice
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 12 mussels, cleaned
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 lb calamari, cut into rings
  • 1/4 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup frozen artichoke hearts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chorizo, cooked and sliced (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the stock and saffron for about 10 minutes to allow the essence of saffron to infuse.
  2. In an 18-inch paella pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onion, bell pepper, and garlic until softened.
  3. Add the tomato and cook until the liquid has reduced to a sauce-like consistency.
  4. Stir in the rice, ensuring each grain is well-coated with the sauce, and cook for about 2 minutes.
  5. Pour in the saffron-infused stock until it covers half the rice’s height. Add artichoke hearts and chorizo, if using. Let the rice simmer for about 20 minutes.
  6. Distribute mussels, shrimp, and calamari over the rice. Depending on your preference, you may push them under the rice for them to steam or let them cook on top for that authentic char-grilled flavor.
  7. Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes or until the rice is al dente and the seafood is cooked through. Garnish with frozen peas to retain their crunch.
  8. Allow the paella to rest for about 5 minutes before serving to achieve that irresistible socarrat.

2. Lobster Mac and Cheese

Lobster meets macaroni in an elegant twist of comfort food. Twice-baked decadence – creamy, buttery, and brimming with succulent lobster goodness – will make this a new iconic classic on your culinary cooking résumé.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups grated sharp cheddar cheese
  • Salt and cayenne pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp grated nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 lb cooked lobster meat
  • 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Cook macaroni according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a large, oven-safe dish.
  3. In a large, deep skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add flour, and whisk until smooth, creating a blonde roux, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Gradually pour in milk and heavy cream, whisking to incorporate. Cook until the sauce has thickened, about 4-5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the cheddar cheese, salt, cayenne pepper, nutmeg, Worcestershire sauce, and cooked macaroni. Mix thoroughly until all macaroni is evenly coated.
  6. Fold in the cooked lobster meat and transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish.
  7. Combine panko breadcrumbs and Parmesan cheese. Sprinkle evenly over the lobster mac and cheese.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until golden brown and bubbling. Allow to rest for 10 minutes before serving.

3. Pan-Seared Scallops with Mango-Avocado Salsa

Impress your dinner guests or wow your household with this bright and zesty dish combining perfectly seared scallops with a refreshing mango-avocado salsa. This dish triumphs in simplicity while expressing the tender succulence of scallops layered with the lively flavors of the Caribbean and Latin America.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs dry sea scallops
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Mango-Avocado Salsa (recipe below)

Mango-Avocado Salsa:

  • 1 mango, diced
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon honey

Instructions:

  1. Pat-dry sea scallops. Season both sides of the scallops with salt and pepper. If needed, remove the muscle from the scallops.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add scallops in a single layer, making sure they are not overcrowded. Sear for 1-2 minutes per side until golden brown with a slightly seared crust.
  3. Remove scallops from the skillet and place them on a clean plate. Immediately top scallops with a generous spoonful of mango-avocado salsa.
