Best Classic Carrot Cake Recipe with Cream Cheese Frosting


Few desserts evoke a sense of nostalgia quite like a classic carrot cake. It’s a timeless treat that combines the wholesome goodness of carrots with the decadence of cream cheese frosting. Perfect for celebrations, family gatherings, or simply to indulge your sweet tooth, this carrot cake delivers rich flavors and a moist texture that is hard to resist.

This recipe captures the essence of tradition while incorporating modern touches to ensure consistent results every time. From choosing the right ingredients to assembling the final masterpiece, this guide takes you through each step of the process. Let’s dive right in and create the best carrot cake you’ll ever bake.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Carrot Cake

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 cup vegetable oil or melted coconut oil
  • 1 cup unsweetened applesauce (or crushed pineapple for a tropical twist)
  • 4 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups grated carrots, firmly packed (about 4-5 medium carrots)
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional but highly recommended)
  • ½ cup raisins (optional)

For the Cream Cheese Frosting

  • 8 ounces full-fat cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1–2 tablespoons whole milk (if needed for smoother consistency)

Step-by-Step Directions

Preparing the Carrot Cake Batter

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line two 9-inch round cake pans with parchment paper to ensure easy removal later. Alternatively, you can use a 9x13-inch pan if you prefer a single-layer cake.

  1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. These warming spices are key to the classic flavor profile of carrot cake.

  2. Combine the wet ingredients: In another bowl, stir together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, vegetable oil, applesauce, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined. This mixture forms the foundation of the batter, ensuring it’s tender and moist.

  3. Fold the dry into the wet: Gradually add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture while stirring gently. Be careful not to overmix, as this can affect the cake’s texture. Your goal is to create a smooth batter without streaks of flour.

  4. Add the carrots and extras: Fold in the grated carrots, walnuts or pecans, and raisins. The grated carrots not only provide sweetness but also ensure the cake stays moist. If you’re using nuts and raisins, they add an extra layer of texture and flavor.

Baking the Cake

Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans. Smooth the tops with a spatula to ensure an even rise.

Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean with only a few moist crumbs attached. If using a 9x13-inch pan, you may need to extend the baking time slightly.

Once baked, remove the pans from the oven and allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes. Then transfer them to wire racks to cool completely before frosting. Believe me, frosting a warm cake is a recipe for disaster, so patience is key!

Making the Cream Cheese Frosting

The frosting is the crowning glory of carrot cake. Its tangy sweetness balances the spiced layers perfectly.

  1. Cream the butter and cream cheese: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth and creamy. Use an electric mixer on medium speed for the best results.

  2. Incorporate the sugar: Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, while mixing on low speed. Once all the sugar is incorporated, increase the mixer speed to medium-high and beat until fluffy.

  3. Flavor and adjust: Stir in the vanilla extract. If the frosting is too thick, add milk 1 tablespoon at a time until you reach the desired consistency. You’re looking for a creamy texture that’s sturdy enough to spread smoothly.

Assembling the Cake

Now comes the fun part: turning your carrot cake layers into a gorgeous masterpiece.

  1. Level the layers: If your cakes have domed tops, use a serrated knife to gently trim them for a flat surface. This step is optional, but it helps create a stable and visually appealing cake.

  2. Frost between the layers: Spread a generous layer of cream cheese frosting on top of the first cake layer. Place the second layer on top and gently press down to secure it.

  3. Cover the cake: Frost the top and sides of the cake with the remaining frosting. Use an offset spatula for smooth coverage or create decorative swirls for added charm.

  4. Decorate: If desired, sprinkle chopped nuts on the sides of the cake or add a dusting of cinnamon on top. You can even garnish with carrot-shaped marzipan pieces for a whimsical touch.

Tips for Success

  • Use fresh carrots: Pre-shredded carrots are convenient, but freshly grated carrots yield a moister cake and have better flavor.

  • Don’t skimp on the spices: The cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger in this recipe are the backbone of its flavor. Adjust these spices as needed to suit your taste preferences.

  • Choose the right pan: If making a layered cake feels intimidating, go for the 9x13-inch option. It’s easier to frost and serve for casual occasions.

  • Room-temperature ingredients: Ensure the eggs, cream cheese, and butter are at room temperature for smooth mixing and optimal texture.

  • Chilling the cake: For cleaner slices, chill the frosted cake in the refrigerator for 30 minutes before serving.

Serving and Storing

This carrot cake is best served slightly chilled or at room temperature. Slice it up and watch as your friends and family savor every bite—a testament to the balanced flavors and irresistible cream cheese frosting.

If you have leftovers (a rare occurrence!), store them covered in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. The cake’s flavors will deepen with time, making it even more delicious. You can also freeze individual slices for up to 3 months. Thaw them in the fridge overnight before enjoying.

Want to make it your own? Experiment by adding shredded coconut or swapping walnuts for pecans. Small tweaks can make the cake uniquely yours while keeping the classic charm intact.

Final Thoughts

Baking the perfect carrot cake is a delightful adventure that combines wholesome ingredients, warm spices, and creamy frosting into a timeless dessert. Whether you’re new to baking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is simple enough to follow yet special enough to impress. With every bite, you’ll savor the moist layers, balanced sweetness, and irresistible tang of cream cheese frosting—a true celebration of tradition.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat the oven, and let the aromatic journey begin. A classic carrot cake awaits, ready to steal the spotlight at your next gathering or to brighten your day as a cozy indulgence!
