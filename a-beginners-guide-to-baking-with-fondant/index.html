A Beginner's Guide to Baking with Fondant


Fondant is a versatile and beautiful ingredient used in many cake decorating projects, from simple birthday cakes to elaborate wedding designs. If you’re new to baking and cake decorating, fondant may seem intimidating, but with the right guidance, you can master it and create stunning cakes. This guide will walk you through the basics of working with fondant, from understanding what it is to the step-by-step process of applying it to your cake.

What is Fondant?

Fondant is a smooth, pliable sugar dough used to cover cakes and create decorations. It has a smooth, satin finish that provides a flawless, polished look, which is why it’s popular for professional cakes. There are two main types of fondant: rolled fondant and poured fondant.

  • Rolled Fondant: This is the most common type used for covering cakes. It’s rolled out into thin sheets and draped over cakes to create a smooth, flawless finish. Rolled fondant can also be molded into shapes and figures to add creative decorations.
  • Poured Fondant: Poured fondant is a glossy, thicker icing that is often used to coat smaller cakes, candies, and pastries.

For beginners, rolled fondant is the best option since it’s easy to use and offers more creative possibilities.

Essential Tools for Working with Fondant

Before diving into the process of working with fondant, it’s important to have the right tools. Here’s a list of essentials you’ll need:

  • Rolling Pin: A rolling pin is necessary for rolling the fondant into an even, thin layer.
  • Fondant Smoothers: These tools help smooth out wrinkles and bumps when applying fondant to cakes, giving them a flawless appearance.
  • Fondant Cutter or Pizza Cutter: A sharp cutter ensures clean edges when trimming excess fondant around your cake.
  • Cornstarch or Powdered Sugar: To prevent the fondant from sticking to your work surface or rolling pin, lightly dust it with cornstarch or powdered sugar.
  • Fondant Tools for Shaping: These include small cutters, molders, and veiners, which allow you to shape the fondant into intricate designs like flowers, bows, and other decorations.
  • Rolling Mat: This provides a non-stick surface to work on and can be especially useful for preventing messes.
  • Cake Turntable: A turntable allows you to rotate your cake smoothly while applying fondant, helping to ensure a consistent and even layer.

With these tools, you’ll be well-equipped to begin your fondant adventure.

Preparing the Cake

Before applying fondant to your cake, it’s essential to prepare your cake properly. Here’s how to get your cake ready for fondant coverage:

1. Bake and Cool Your Cake

Start by baking your cake as usual, following your recipe instructions. After baking, allow the cake to cool completely. Fondant should never be applied to a warm cake because it will melt and become sticky. Place your cake on a flat surface and allow it to cool to room temperature. If your cake has a domed top, you can use a serrated knife to level it off, creating a smooth, even surface for the fondant to sit on.

2. Apply a Crumb Coat

The crumb coat is a thin layer of buttercream or ganache that helps to seal in the crumbs and provides a smooth surface for the fondant to adhere to. Spread the crumb coat evenly over the cake using an offset spatula, ensuring that the entire cake is covered.

Once the crumb coat is applied, refrigerate the cake for at least 30 minutes to set the buttercream and firm it up. This will make it easier to work with the fondant and ensure a smooth finish.

Preparing the Fondant

Before you can apply fondant to your cake, you’ll need to prepare it properly. Here’s how:

1. Knead the Fondant

Fondant often comes in pre-colored blocks, but it might be a bit stiff when you first open it. Begin by kneading the fondant with your hands to soften it. This will make it more pliable and easier to roll out. If you find the fondant is too sticky, dust it with a bit of powdered sugar or cornstarch to help with the consistency.

2. Roll Out the Fondant

Lightly dust your work surface with cornstarch or powdered sugar to prevent the fondant from sticking. Using a rolling pin, roll the fondant into a large, even circle. Aim for a thickness of about 1/8 to 1/4 inch. The rolled-out fondant should be large enough to cover your cake completely, so measure your cake before rolling the fondant to ensure you have enough.

You can measure the diameter of your cake and then roll the fondant out to a size that’s about 2 inches larger than the cake’s diameter. This will give you enough fondant to cover the sides and top of the cake.

3. Check for Even Thickness

As you roll out the fondant, make sure it remains an even thickness throughout. If some areas are too thin, they may tear when you try to cover the cake. If the fondant is sticking to the rolling pin or surface, dust it with a bit more powdered sugar or cornstarch.

Applying the Fondant

Once your fondant is prepared, it’s time to cover your cake. Follow these steps for a smooth, flawless finish:

1. Lift the Fondant

Carefully roll the fondant onto the rolling pin, using it like a big cylinder. This will help you lift the fondant without tearing it. Gently unroll the fondant over the cake, positioning it in the center.

2. Smooth the Fondant

Starting from the top of the cake, gently smooth the fondant over the surface. Use your hands or a fondant smoother to push out any air bubbles or wrinkles. Work your way down the sides of the cake, smoothing the fondant as you go. A cake turntable is especially useful at this stage, as it allows you to rotate the cake smoothly as you apply the fondant.

3. Trim Excess Fondant

Once the fondant is smoothed over the cake, use a sharp fondant cutter or pizza cutter to trim the excess fondant around the base of the cake. Leave a little excess fondant to tuck under the cake for a neat finish.

4. Smooth the Bottom Edge

To finish, smooth the bottom edges of the fondant to ensure it sits neatly around the base of the cake. If you like, you can add a decorative border to the base using fondant or edible ribbon.

Decorating the Cake with Fondant

Now that your cake is covered in fondant, you can get creative with decorating! Fondant can be used to create all sorts of designs, from simple patterns to intricate decorations. Here are a few ideas for decorating your fondant-covered cake:

1. Fondant Flowers and Shapes

Fondant can be molded into all kinds of shapes, from flowers to ribbons to bows. Use small cutters or molds to create detailed fondant shapes that can be placed on the cake. For a more intricate design, try using fondant tools to add texture or details to your decorations.

2. Fondant Painting

For a more artistic look, you can paint fondant with edible food colors. Use food-grade paintbrushes and gel food coloring mixed with a little bit of alcohol or lemon extract to create painted designs on your fondant-covered cake. This technique works especially well for creating watercolor effects or delicate designs.

3. Adding Texture

You can also use fondant to add texture to your cake. Fondant impression mats or embossing tools can be used to create intricate patterns or textures on the surface of the fondant. Simply roll the tool over the surface of the fondant before you apply it to the cake.

4. Create a Fondant Topper

Another fun way to decorate your cake is by creating a fondant topper. This could be a 3D sculpture, like a fondant animal or figurine, that you place on top of the cake. These can be made ahead of time and placed on the cake just before serving.

Storing Your Fondant Cake

Once your cake is decorated, it’s important to store it properly to keep it fresh and intact. Fondant-covered cakes should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. If your cake has perishable fillings or frosting, it’s important to refrigerate it. However, be mindful that refrigeration can cause condensation to form on the fondant, which may cause it to lose its smooth appearance. Always allow your cake to come to room temperature before serving.

Common Fondant Mistakes to Avoid

As a beginner, you might encounter a few challenges while working with fondant. Here are some common mistakes and how to avoid them:

  • Fondant is Too Sticky: If your fondant is too sticky to work with, simply knead in a little more powdered sugar or cornstarch until it reaches the right consistency.
  • Tearing the Fondant: If you roll your fondant too thin, it may tear when you try to apply it to your cake. Aim for an even thickness and be gentle when lifting and applying the fondant.
  • Wrinkles or Bubbles: If you see wrinkles or air bubbles in your fondant, use a fondant smoother to gently press them out. You can also use a pin to pop small bubbles before smoothing the fondant.

Conclusion

Baking with fondant may seem like a daunting task at first, but with the right tools, practice, and a bit of patience, you’ll be able to create stunning cakes that will impress your friends and family. Remember, the more you work with fondant, the more comfortable and skilled you’ll become. Enjoy the process, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different decorations and techniques. Happy baking!
