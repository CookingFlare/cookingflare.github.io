Spicy Instant Pot Korean Beef Bulgogi Recipes


When it comes to Korean cuisine, few dishes hold as much popularity as bulgogi. Known for its tender beef marinated in a sweet and savory sauce, bulgogi is truly a delightful experience for the palate. Today, we’re taking this beloved classic to the next level by preparing it in a Spicy Instant Pot, which not only enhances the flavors but also significantly reduces cooking time. Let’s dive into this delicious Spicy Instant Pot Korean Beef Bulgogi recipe that is sure to impress your family and friends!

What is Bulgogi?

Bulgogi, which translates to “fire meat,” is a traditional Korean dish made from thinly sliced marinated beef. The marinade typically includes soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and pepper. The beauty of bulgogi lies in its versatility; it can be grilled, stir-fried, or in our case, cooked in an Instant Pot. The result is a dish that boasts a rich flavor profile, tender meat, and a hint of sweetness balanced by spiciness.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Marinade:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

For the Beef:

  • 2 pounds beef (sirloin or ribeye), thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup beef broth or water

Optional for Serving:

  • Cooked rice
  • Lettuce leaves (for wraps)
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Kimchi

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Marinade

Begin by making the marinade, which is the heart of this dish. In a large mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, gochugaru, gochujang, minced garlic, minced ginger, rice vinegar, sesame seeds, and black pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until the sugar dissolves completely, and the marinade is well-blended.

Step 2: Marinate the Beef

Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring each piece is coated evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes at room temperature. For the best results, you can marinate it for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator. This allows the flavors to penetrate the meat, resulting in a more flavorful dish.

Step 3: Sauté in the Instant Pot

Once the beef is marinated, set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function. Add the vegetable oil to the pot, allowing it to heat for a few moments. Then, add the sliced onions and carrots, sautéing them for about 3-4 minutes until they start to soften.

Step 4: Add the Beef

Carefully add the marinated beef along with the marinade into the Instant Pot. Stir everything together, ensuring the beef is evenly distributed. This is where the magic begins as the flavors meld together beautifully.

Step 5: Pressure Cook

Pour in the beef broth or water into the pot. This will create the steam needed for pressure cooking. Close the lid securely and set the valve to the “Sealing” position. Select the “Pressure Cook” function and set the timer for 15 minutes. Once the cooking time is complete, allow for a natural pressure release for about 10 minutes before carefully switching the valve to “Venting” to release any remaining steam.

Step 6: Serve

Once the pressure has been released, open the lid and give the bulgogi a gentle stir. The beef should be tender and infused with the aromatic flavors of the marinade. Serve the Spicy Instant Pot Korean Beef Bulgogi over a bed of steaming rice, garnishing with chopped green onions. For an interactive dining experience, consider serving it family-style with lettuce leaves and assorted side dishes.

Tips for the Best Bulgogi

  • Meat Selection: Choose well-marbled cuts like sirloin or ribeye for the best flavor and tenderness. The thinly sliced meat absorbs the marinade quickly, enhancing the overall taste.

  • Spice Level: Adjust the amount of gochugaru and gochujang to suit your spice preference. If you prefer a milder version, reduce the quantities or omit them altogether.

  • Marinating Time: For optimal flavor, marinate the beef overnight if time allows. This not only enhances the flavor but also results in incredibly tender meat.

  • Instant Pot Variants: If you don’t have an Instant Pot, this recipe can also be adapted for stovetop cooking. Just sauté the ingredients in a skillet and allow them to simmer until the beef is tender.

  • Serving Suggestions: Pair your bulgogi with a side of kimchi, pickled vegetables, or a refreshing cucumber salad to balance the heat.

Conclusion

This Spicy Instant Pot Korean Beef Bulgogi recipe is not only quick and easy but also bursting with flavor. The Instant Pot allows you to achieve that beautifully tender beef in a fraction of the time compared to traditional methods. With its sweet and spicy notes, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you serve it over rice, in lettuce wraps, or as part of a larger Korean feast, your culinary adventure will be a hit. So gather your ingredients, fire up your Instant Pot, and prepare to indulge in this delectable dish that brings a taste of Korea right to your kitchen! Enjoy your cooking, and don’t forget to share this delightful recipe with your friends and family!
