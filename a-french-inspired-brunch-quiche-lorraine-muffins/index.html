A French-Inspired Brunch: Quiche Lorraine Muffins


Bonjour, foodies! Today, we’re bringing the charm of French cuisine to your brunch table with a delightful twist on a classic dish - Quiche Lorraine Muffins. These savory, perfectly portioned treats are the ideal addition to any brunch spread, offering the rich flavors of the traditional quiche in a convenient, handheld form. Whether you’re hosting a gathering with friends or simply treating yourself to a leisurely weekend brunch, these muffins are sure to impress. So, don your imaginary beret and let’s embark on a culinary journey to France right in your kitchen!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 4-5 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Filling:

  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • ½ cup chopped shallots
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • A pinch of nutmeg

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the chilled, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice-cold water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix gently until the dough comes together.
  3. Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.
  3. Add the crumbled bacon, shredded Gruyère cheese, and chopped shallots to the egg mixture, and stir to evenly distribute the ingredients.

Assembling the Quiche Lorraine Muffins:

  1. After chilling, roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to about 1/8 inch thickness. Using a round cutter, cut out circles of dough slightly larger than the size of the muffin tin openings.
  2. Gently press the dough circles into the greased muffin tin, ensuring that the dough comes up the sides of each opening.
  3. Pour the prepared filling into each muffin cup, filling them almost to the top.

Baking and Serving:

  1. Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the muffins are set and the crust is golden brown.
  2. Allow the quiche lorraine muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before carefully removing them.
  3. Serve the muffins warm or at room temperature, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh herbs if desired.

Bon Appétit!

These Quiche Lorraine Muffins are a delightful marriage of flaky crust, creamy custard, savory bacon, and the nutty richness of Gruyère cheese. They embody the essence of French cuisine with their elegant simplicity and irresistible flavors. This recipe allows you to effortlessly bring the sophistication of a French-inspired brunch to your own home, allowing you to savor each bite with a touch of Parisian charm.

So, gather your loved ones or indulge in a moment of self-care, and enjoy these Quiche Lorraine Muffins as the centerpiece of your next brunch affair. Pair them with a crisp, refreshing salad and a glass of your favorite wine, and transport yourself to a quaint French café without leaving the comfort of your home. Voilà, the art of French cooking has never been more accessible!
