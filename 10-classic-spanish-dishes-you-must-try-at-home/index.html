10 Classic Spanish Dishes You Must Try at Home


Spain, a country renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, and delectable cuisine, offers a culinary landscape that is as diverse as its regions. From the sun-drenched shores of the Mediterranean to the lush green hills of Galicia, each area boasts unique flavors and traditional dishes that tell the story of its people. If you’re looking to embark on a culinary journey, here are ten classic Spanish dishes you simply must try at home. With simple ingredients and straightforward instructions, you’ll be able to bring the spirit of Spain to your kitchen.

1. Paella

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of Bomba rice (or any short-grain rice)
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup of green beans, chopped
  • 1 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup of cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup of mussels (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a paella pan or large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  2. Stir in the red bell pepper and green beans, cooking for another 5 minutes until slightly softened.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and saffron, cooking for a few minutes until the tomatoes break down.
  4. Pour in the rice and stir to coat, then add the broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 15 minutes.
  5. Add the chicken, shrimp, and mussels, pressing them into the rice. Cover and cook for an additional 10 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the seafood is cooked through.
  6. Remove from heat, let it rest for 5 minutes, then serve with lemon wedges.

2. Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the potatoes (and onion if using) and cook, stirring occasionally, until soft and slightly golden, about 15 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, beat the eggs and season with salt. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain excess oil and mix them with the eggs.
  3. Wipe the skillet clean and add a little olive oil. Pour in the egg and potato mixture, cooking on medium-low heat for about 5 minutes until the edges start to set.
  4. Carefully flip the tortilla using a plate, cooking the other side for another 5 minutes until fully set and golden.
  5. Let it cool slightly before slicing into wedges. Serve warm or at room temperature.

3. Gazpacho

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 cups of tomato juice
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. Blend until smooth.
  2. Add the tomato juice, olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Blend again until well combined.
  3. Chill the gazpacho in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Serve cold, garnished with fresh basil or parsley.

4. Churros with Chocolate Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 cup of dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • Extra sugar for coating

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat and stir in flour until a dough forms.
  2. Stir in the vegetable oil and vanilla extract, mixing until smooth. Let the dough cool slightly.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot. Transfer the dough to a piping bag fitted with a star tip.
  4. Squeeze strips of dough into the hot oil, frying until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels, then roll in sugar.
  5. For the chocolate sauce, heat the cream in a saucepan until simmering. Pour over the chopped chocolate and stir until melted and smooth.
  6. Serve the churros warm with chocolate sauce for dipping.

5. Pisto Manchego

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 zucchini, diced
  • 2 cups of ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, sautéing until soft.
  2. Stir in the zucchini and cook for 5 minutes. Add the tomatoes and smoked paprika, cooking for another 10-15 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  3. Season with salt and pepper. If desired, create wells in the pisto and crack an egg into each. Cover and cook until the eggs are set to your liking.
  4. Serve warm with crusty bread.

6. Pulpo a la Gallega (Galician-Style Octopus)

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of octopus
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika (sweet or smoked)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the octopus and cook for about 40 minutes until tender. Remove and let it cool.
  2. In the same pot, add the potato slices and cook until tender, about 15 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Slice the octopus into rounds. Arrange potato slices on a plate, top with octopus, and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with paprika and garnish with parsley.
  4. Serve at room temperature.

7. Fabada Asturiana (Asturian Bean Stew)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of fabes (white beans) or cannellini beans
  • 1 chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 blood sausage (morcilla), sliced
  • 200g of pork shoulder, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the beans overnight in cold water. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until translucent.
  3. Add the soaked beans, chorizo, blood sausage, pork shoulder, bay leaf, and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer.
  4. Cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beans are tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot, accompanied by crusty bread.

8. Croquetas de Jamón (Ham Croquettes)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of diced ham (preferably Iberian)
  • 1/2 cup of onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of butter
  • 1/4 cup of flour
  • 2 cups of milk
  • Salt and nutmeg to taste
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Eggs for coating
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add onion and sauté until soft.
  2. Stir in the flour, cooking for 2 minutes. Gradually whisk in the milk, cooking until thickened.
  3. Add diced ham and season with salt and nutmeg. Pour the mixture into a dish and let it cool completely.
  4. Once cooled, form small balls or patties. Dip in egg and coat with breadcrumbs.
  5. Heat oil in a frying pan and fry the croquettes until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

9. Bacalao al Pil-Pil (Cod in Garlic Sauce)

Ingredients:

  • 4 pieces of salted cod, soaked overnight
  • 1 cup of olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat olive oil over low heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes, cooking gently until the garlic is golden.
  2. Remove the garlic and set aside. In the same oil, add the soaked cod pieces, cooking for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally.
  3. As the cod cooks, stir the oil with a wooden spoon to emulsify, creating a thick sauce.
  4. Once the cod is cooked through, plate and drizzle with the pil-pil sauce. Garnish with the reserved garlic and fresh parsley.
