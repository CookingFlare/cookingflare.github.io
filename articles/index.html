Homemade Spinach and Ricotta Ravioli: A Cheesy and Flavorful Treat

There’s something truly special about homemade pasta. The taste, the texture, and the satisfaction of creating something delicious from scratch are unmatched. If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, you’ll love this recipe for homemade spinach and ricotta ravioli. It’s a dish that’s both comforting and elegant, making it perfect for a cozy dinner at home or a special occasion with loved ones. Ingredients For the Pasta Dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour 3 large eggs 1 tablespoon olive oil 1/2 teaspoon salt For the Filling: 1 1/2 cups fresh spinach, chopped 1 cup ricotta cheese 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1 clove garlic, minced Salt and pepper to taste For the Sauce: 4 tablespoons unsalted butter 3-4 fresh sage leaves 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Step 1: Make the Pasta Dough On a clean work surface, mound the flour and create a well in the center. Crack the eggs into the well, add the olive oil, and sprinkle with salt. Using a fork, gradually incorporate the flour into the eggs until a dough starts to form. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes. Step 2: Prepare the Filling In a skillet, sauté the chopped spinach over medium heat until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool. In a bowl, combine the cooled spinach, ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. Step 3: Roll Out the Dough After the dough has rested, cut it into four equal pieces. Working with one piece at a time, flatten the dough into a rough rectangle shape. Set a pasta machine to the widest setting and pass the dough through it. Fold the dough into thirds and pass it through the machine again. Repeat this process, gradually decreasing the thickness settings, until the dough is thin and smooth. Step 4: Assemble the Ravioli Lay the rolled-out dough on a floured surface. Place small spoonfuls of the filling about 1 inch apart on the dough. Brush water around each mound of filling. Lay another sheet of rolled-out dough over the filling and press around each mound to seal. Use a fluted pastry wheel to cut the ravioli into individual pieces. Step 5: Cook the Ravioli Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the ravioli into the boiling water and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until they float to the surface. Remove the ravioli with a slotted spoon and transfer to a plate. Step 6: Make the Sauce In a skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sage leaves and cook until the butter is golden and the sage leaves are crisp. Remove the skillet from the heat and discard the sage leaves. Toss the cooked ravioli in the brown butter sauce and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. Enjoy Your Homemade Spinach and Ricotta Ravioli! The combination of tender pasta, creamy ricotta, and earthy spinach, all enveloped in a nutty brown butter sauce, makes this homemade spinach and ricotta ravioli a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re a seasoned pasta maker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy. So, roll up your sleeves, embrace the process, and treat yourself to this cheesy and flavorful Italian classic made with love from your own kitchen. Buon appetito! ...

Savory and Satisfying Sausage and Mushroom Penne: A Hearty Meal

Are you tired of the same old pasta dishes? Looking for something hearty, flavorful, and oh-so-satisfying? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll be combining the rich flavors of sausage and mushrooms with penne pasta to create a dish that will leave you craving for more. Ingredients 8 ounces of penne pasta 1 pound of Italian sausage, casing removed 1 small onion, diced 3 cloves of garlic, minced 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes 1 cup of chicken broth 1 teaspoon of dried oregano 1/2 teaspoon of dried basil 1/2 teaspoon of dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish Fresh basil leaves for garnish Instructions Cook the penne pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside. In a large skillet, cook the Italian sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Break the sausage into small pieces as it cooks. Remove the cooked sausage from the skillet and set aside. Leave about 1 tablespoon of the rendered fat in the skillet. In the same skillet, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they have softened and released their moisture. Return the cooked sausage to the skillet and mix well with the onion, garlic, and mushrooms. Pour in the diced tomatoes and chicken broth. Stir in the dried oregano, basil, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Add the cooked penne pasta to the skillet and toss everything together until the pasta is well coated with the sauce. Serve the sausage and mushroom penne in bowls, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves. Enjoy the Flavorful Delight!
