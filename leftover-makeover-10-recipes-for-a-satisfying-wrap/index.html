Leftover Makeover: 10 Recipes for a Satisfying Wrap


There’s something magical about wrapping up a mix of ingredients into a neat little package that’s as satisfying to the eyes as it is to the stomach. Not only are wraps versatile, but they’re also the perfect solution to transform last night’s leftovers into today’s lunch or dinner masterpiece. Whether you’re trying to reduce food waste or simply looking for a creative way to reimagine a meal, these ten recipes will show you just how far your leftovers can go.

1. Roasted Chicken Caesar Wrap

Roast chicken is a classic leftover that can be reinvented in so many ways. For this wrap, combine shredded roasted chicken with crisp romaine lettuce, grated Parmesan cheese, and your favorite Caesar dressing. Add a handful of croutons for some crunch, and wrap it all up in a flour tortilla. This combination is creamy, tangy, and irresistibly satisfying.

Tip: If you don’t have Caesar dressing on hand, a quick mix of mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, garlic powder, and anchovy paste can create a similar taste.

2. BBQ Pulled Pork Wrap

If your last barbecue left behind some leftover pulled pork, you’re in for a treat. Start with a large tortilla and layer it with pulled pork, a dollop of coleslaw, and a drizzle of extra BBQ sauce for added zing. If you’re feeling adventurous, add some pickled red onions or dill pickles to punch up the flavor.

This wrap takes the smoky, tangy essence of barbecue and condenses it into a portable, hand-held meal.

3. Mediterranean Falafel Wrap

Got leftover falafel from a takeout night? Reimagine it with a Mediterranean-inspired wrap. Use a soft pita bread or a flatbread and spread a layer of creamy hummus. Add the falafel, along with sliced cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, and a sprinkling of fresh parsley. For an extra layer of flavor, drizzle some tzatziki or tahini sauce on top.

Not only is this wrap bursting with fresh flavors, but it’s also vegetarian-friendly and incredibly filling.

4. Breakfast Burrito Wrap

Transform leftover scrambled eggs, bacon, or roasted veggies into a hearty breakfast burrito. Start by warming up a large tortilla, then load it with scrambled eggs, crispy bacon bits, and some diced roasted potatoes or sauteed vegetables. Sprinkle shredded cheese and a spoonful of salsa or hot sauce before wrapping it up.

This is the ideal grab-and-go breakfast for busy mornings, and it makes great use of breakfast leftovers.

5. Pizza Wrap

Yes, you read that right—turn your leftover pizza toppings into a wrap! Start by spreading a thin layer of marinara sauce on a tortilla. Then, add the leftover toppings, such as pepperoni, diced vegetables, and shredded mozzarella cheese. Roll it up and lightly toast the wrap in a skillet or a panini press.

This is an excellent way to enjoy that cheesy, saucy pizza goodness in a new and exciting form.

6. Teriyaki Rice Wrap

Leftover stir-fry or teriyaki rice makes for a flavorful and filling wrap. Scoop your rice and stir-fried veggies onto a tortilla and add any proteins you might have, such as chicken or tofu. Top with a drizzle of teriyaki sauce or soy sauce for extra flavor.

Wrap it tightly and enjoy this Asian-inspired meal that captures the sweet-savory balance of a classic takeaway dish.

7. Taco Wrap

Taco night leftovers make for the ultimate no-effort lunch the next day. Take your leftover taco meat—whether it’s ground beef, shredded chicken, or pork carnitas—and layer it in a tortilla with some black beans, shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, cheese, and sour cream. A squeeze of lime or some chopped cilantro will elevate the flavors even further.

This wrap is as vibrant as it is convenient and offers all the taste of taco night minus the mess.

8. Thanksgiving Turkey Cranberry Wrap

Leftover turkey from your Thanksgiving feast doesn’t have to sit in the fridge until it dries out. Slice the turkey thinly and place it on a tortilla. Add a smear of cranberry sauce, some stuffing, and a dash of gravy. For a touch of freshness, layer in some spinach or arugula.

The result is a savory-sweet wrap that tastes just like Thanksgiving dinner but without the need for a plate and fork.

9. Greek Gyro Wrap

Leftover roasted lamb or beef is perfect for recreating the flavors of a classic Greek gyro. Use a pita or flatbread as your base and spread a dollop of tzatziki sauce. Layer in the meat, along with sliced cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, and crumbled feta cheese. A sprinkle of oregano will tie it all together.

This wrap is loaded with Mediterranean flavor and is perfect when you’re craving something hearty yet fresh.

10. Veggie Stir-Fry Lettuce Wraps

For a lighter option, ditch the tortilla and opt for crisp lettuce leaves as your wrap base. Grab your leftover veggie stir-fry and spoon portions into the lettuce cups. Add a sprinkle of chopped peanuts or sesame seeds, and finish with a drizzle of soy sauce or a squeeze of lime for an umami-packed bite.

This wrap strikes the perfect balance between light and satisfying, and it’s a great choice for using up vegetable-heavy leftovers.

Wrap It Up (Pun Intended)

Repurposing leftovers doesn’t have to be boring or repetitive. With these wrap recipes, you can easily turn yesterday’s meal into a fresh, flavorful creation that feels entirely new. From barbecue to breakfast burritos to Mediterranean-inspired wraps, the possibilities are endless when you think outside the box.

The beauty of wraps lies in their flexibility—mix and match ingredients, incorporate sauces, and customize them to your taste. Not only do these recipes help reduce food waste, but they also make meal prep quick and stress-free. So the next time you open your fridge and spot remnants of last night’s dinner, grab some tortillas, flatbreads, or lettuce leaves and get creative. You just might discover your new favorite wrap combination.
