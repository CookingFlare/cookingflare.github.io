5 Hearty Fall Stews to Keep You Cozy


As the crisp air of fall sets in and the days get shorter, there’s nothing quite like a comforting bowl of stew to warm you up. Fall is the perfect season to embrace hearty, flavorful meals that nourish the body and soul. The beauty of stews lies in their versatility—whether you’re in the mood for something meaty, vegetarian, or a combination of both, there’s a stew for everyone. With rich flavors and tender ingredients, these five fall stews will fill your home with the aroma of autumn and keep you cozy throughout the season.

In this post, we’ll share five delightful and easy-to-make fall stew recipes that you can enjoy during chilly evenings. These stews are not only perfect for dinner but also make great leftovers for lunch the next day. Let’s dive into the delicious world of fall stews!

1. Classic Beef Stew with Root Vegetables

There’s something timeless about a classic beef stew that just feels like home. Rich and comforting, this stew combines tender beef, root vegetables, and a savory broth, creating a satisfying meal that is perfect for fall.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat, cut into cubes
  • 4 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 carrots, sliced into rounds
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional, for added depth of flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon flour (for thickening)

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Beef: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and sear on all sides until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Once browned, remove the beef and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the chopped onions and garlic. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened and fragrant. Add the tomato paste and cook for another minute, stirring to incorporate.

  3. Deglaze the Pot: If using wine, pour it in now to deglaze the pot. Scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot using a wooden spoon. This adds a lot of flavor to the stew.

  4. Add the Broth and Spices: Return the beef to the pot, along with the potatoes, carrots, parsnips, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Pour in the beef broth and bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the stew simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.

  6. Thicken the Stew: If you prefer a thicker stew, mix 1 tablespoon of flour with a little cold water to form a slurry. Stir this mixture into the stew and cook for another 10 minutes until the stew thickens.

  7. Serve: Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary, then serve the stew hot with some crusty bread on the side.

This classic beef stew is a perfect meal for chilly fall nights, offering hearty ingredients and a rich, savory flavor that will keep you coming back for more.

2. Chicken and Sweet Potato Stew

If you’re looking for a lighter yet equally satisfying option, this chicken and sweet potato stew is a must-try. The sweetness of the potatoes balances out the savory chicken and vegetables, creating a harmonious dish that is both comforting and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Chicken: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and season with salt, pepper, cumin, and smoked paprika. Cook the chicken until browned, about 5-7 minutes, then remove it from the pot and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion, garlic, celery, and carrots. Cook for about 5 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Add the Sweet Potatoes and Broth: Add the cubed sweet potatoes and chicken broth to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Simmer: Reduce the heat and cover the pot. Let the stew simmer for about 40-45 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and the flavors have melded together.

  5. Return the Chicken to the Pot: Add the browned chicken back into the pot and stir to combine. Let the stew cook for an additional 10 minutes to ensure everything is heated through.

  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley before serving. Enjoy this hearty, slightly sweet, and savory stew with a side of warm bread.

This chicken and sweet potato stew is perfect for those who prefer a healthier option without sacrificing flavor. The tender chicken, creamy sweet potatoes, and aromatic spices make this stew a fall favorite.

3. Butternut Squash and Lentil Stew

For a plant-based option that still packs a punch in terms of flavor and satisfaction, this butternut squash and lentil stew is a great choice. The earthy lentils pair beautifully with the sweet, creamy butternut squash, making this stew a cozy and filling dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Onion and Garlic: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté for 3-4 minutes, until fragrant.

  2. Add the Spices and Squash: Stir in the turmeric, cumin, and cinnamon. Add the cubed butternut squash and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Add the Lentils and Broth: Add the lentils, vegetable broth, and diced tomatoes (with their juice) to the pot. Bring to a boil.

  4. Simmer: Once boiling, reduce the heat and cover the pot. Let the stew simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the lentils are tender and the squash is soft.

  5. Season and Serve: Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This stew is perfect for fall, offering a hearty and nourishing meal that is also vegan and gluten-free. The combination of lentils and squash provides a wholesome and satisfying dish that is both filling and delicious.

4. Pork and Apple Stew

Pork and apples are a classic combination, especially in fall when apples are in season. This pork and apple stew balances savory and sweet flavors, making it an unforgettable dish. The pork becomes tender and juicy while the apples add a subtle sweetness that enhances the overall flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 4 apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground sage
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Pork: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the pork chunks and brown them on all sides, about 7-10 minutes. Once browned, remove the pork and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Onion and Garlic: In the same pot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté for about 5 minutes until softened.

  3. Add the Apples and Spices: Stir in the apples, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the apples to soften.

  4. Deglaze with Chicken Broth: Add the chicken broth to the pot and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Return the pork to the pot and stir to combine.

  5. Simmer: Cover and simmer the stew on low for 1.5 to 2 hours, until the pork is tender and the apples have broken down into a sauce-like consistency.

  6. Finish with Apple Cider Vinegar: Stir in the apple cider vinegar and cook for an additional 5 minutes to balance the flavors.

  7. Serve: Serve hot with a side of mashed potatoes or rustic bread for a complete meal.

This pork and apple stew is an autumn delight, offering a comforting mix of savory, sweet, and tangy flavors that will warm you up on the coldest fall evenings.

5. Vegetarian Mushroom and Barley Stew

Mushrooms are a fantastic addition to any fall stew, adding rich umami flavor and a meaty texture. This vegetarian mushroom and barley stew is hearty, satisfying, and packed with earthy flavors, making it a perfect choice for plant-based eaters or anyone craving a comforting, meatless meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cremini or button mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Mushrooms and Vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and cook for about 5-7 minutes until they release their moisture and begin to brown. Add the onions, carrots, and garlic, and sauté for an additional 3-4 minutes.

  2. Add the Barley and Broth: Stir in the pearl barley, vegetable broth, and wine (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the stew simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the barley is tender and the flavors have developed.

  4. Season and Serve: Stir in soy sauce, thyme, and a bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot and enjoy this earthy, filling stew.

This mushroom and barley stew is a delightful vegetarian option that will satisfy even the most devout meat-eaters. The combination of mushrooms, barley, and aromatic herbs creates a stew that’s rich in flavor and texture.

Conclusion

Fall is the perfect time to indulge in warm, comforting stews that fill your home with delicious aromas. Whether you prefer the classic flavors of beef and root vegetables, the sweet and savory combo of chicken and sweet potatoes, or the earthy depth of mushroom and barley, these five fall stews will keep you cozy all season long. Each recipe is simple to make, versatile, and full of hearty ingredients that make every spoonful a delight.

So gather your ingredients, get your pots ready, and enjoy these satisfying stews that make the best of what fall has to offer. Happy cooking!
