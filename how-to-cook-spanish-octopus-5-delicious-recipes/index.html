How to Cook Spanish Octopus: 5 Delicious Recipes


When it comes to seafood delicacies, Spanish octopus stands out for its unique texture, rich flavor, and versatility in cooking. Known for its tender yet slightly chewy bite, octopus has been a prized ingredient in Mediterranean and Spanish cuisine for centuries. Cooking Spanish octopus perfectly can be a bit intimidating for first-timers, but with the right methods and recipes, it’s incredibly rewarding. This post will guide you through five delectable ways to prepare Spanish octopus at home, helping you achieve tender results and tantalizing flavors every time.

Understanding Spanish Octopus

Before diving into cooking, it’s helpful to know what makes Spanish octopus special. The octopus commonly used in Spain is often harvested from the Atlantic Ocean, where cold waters contribute to its firm texture. The fresh octopus typically weighs between 2 to 5 pounds and features thick tentacles that are a culinary delight once properly cooked.

Octopus has a tendency to be tough if not prepared correctly. The key is slow, gentle cooking to break down the connective tissue and tenderize the flesh. The cooking process involves blanching, simmering, or roasting depending on the recipe. With patience and the right techniques, octopus can transform into a sumptuous dish perfect for any occasion.

Preparing Spanish Octopus for Cooking

The preparation stage is crucial for the best outcome. Start by gently rinsing the octopus under cold running water. Remove the beak, located at the center of the tentacles, if your supplier hasn’t done so already. Some cooks recommend a quick freezing or pounding procedure to further tenderize the meat, but this step is optional if you plan on simmering the octopus for a long time.

Many traditional Spanish recipes begin by soaking the octopus in cold water for about an hour, which helps clean it and remove any excess salt. Once cleaned, bring a large pot of water to boil. Some chefs add bay leaves, peppercorns, garlic, or wine corks to the boiling water, believing these additions tenderize the octopus; however, the cork trick remains a debated myth. The important factor is to cook slowly and steadily.

How to Cook Spanish Octopus: Basic Cooking Method

  1. Blanching the Octopus
    Lower the octopus’s head and tentacles into boiling water three times in quick succession – this technique, often called “scaring,” helps curl the tentacles beautifully. After the last dip, leave the octopus submerged.

  2. Simmering
    Reduce the heat and let the octopus simmer gently for 45 minutes to an hour or until the flesh becomes tender when pierced with a fork. Avoid boiling vigorously as it can make the octopus rubbery.

  3. Cooling
    Remove the octopus from the pot and let it cool on a plate. Once cooled, you can use it immediately for your recipes, or refrigerate if you plan to prepare the dish later.

1. Pulpo a la Gallega (Galician-Style Octopus)

One of Spain’s most loved octopus dishes originates from Galicia in the northwest. Simple yet full of flavor, Pulpo a la Gallega highlights tender octopus with just a few seasoning essentials.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cooked Spanish octopus (about 3-4 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, preferably Spanish extra virgin
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika (pimentón)
  • Coarse sea salt to taste
  • 3-4 medium potatoes, peeled and boiled

Instructions:

  1. Slice the cooked octopus tentacles into 1/2-inch thick rounds.
  2. Cut boiled potatoes into thick slices or cubes.
  3. Arrange the potatoes on a serving dish and place the octopus slices on top.
  4. Drizzle generously with olive oil.
  5. Sprinkle smoked paprika evenly over the octopus.
  6. Add coarse sea salt to taste.

Serve immediately as a tapa or appetizer with a crisp white wine.

2. Grilled Spanish Octopus with Lemon and Garlic

Grilling adds a smoky edge that enhances the natural sweetness of the octopus, creating a delightful contrast of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cooked Spanish octopus
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 large lemon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. After cooking and cooling your octopus, cut the tentacles into manageable pieces.
  2. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  3. In a bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss the octopus pieces in this marinade and let rest for 15 minutes.
  5. Place the octopus on the grill for 3-4 minutes on each side or until slightly charred.
  6. Once grilled, garnish with chopped fresh parsley and an extra squeeze of lemon if desired.

This dish pairs excellently with grilled vegetables or a rustic loaf of bread.

3. Spanish Octopus and Chickpea Stew

This hearty stew combines the succulent octopus with earthy chickpeas, tomatoes, and bold spices, making it perfect for cool evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cooked Spanish octopus (cut into chunks)
  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (or 1 can, drained)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup fish or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add smoked paprika and cumin; cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in diced tomatoes and chickpeas; simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour in broth and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Add the octopus chunks, reduce heat, and simmer uncovered for 15-20 minutes to meld flavors.
  6. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs before serving.

Serve warm with crusty bread to soak up the delicious broth.

4. Spanish Octopus Carpaccio with Olive Oil and Capers

For a fresh and elegant presentation, try octopus carpaccio. Thinly sliced octopus dressed simply highlights its tender texture and subtle flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cooked Spanish octopus, cooled and cleaned
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon capers, rinsed
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Sea salt to taste
  • Fresh arugula or microgreens (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Using a sharp knife or mandoline, slice the octopus into very thin rounds.
  2. Arrange the slices evenly on a large flat plate.
  3. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice evenly.
  4. Scatter capers over the carpaccio.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Garnish with arugula or microgreens if desired.

This dish makes a stunning appetizer and works well with a chilled glass of Albariño.

5. Spanish Octopus Paella

Octopus adds a luxurious touch to the classic Spanish rice dish, blending seamlessly with saffron, vegetables, and seafood.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cooked Spanish octopus, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups Bomba or Arborio rice
  • 1/2 pound peeled shrimp (optional)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 large tomato, grated or finely chopped
  • 4 cups seafood broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • A pinch of saffron threads (soaked in warm water)
  • 1/4 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a wide paella pan or large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper until softened.
  3. Add the tomato, smoked paprika, and saffron with its soaking water; cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the rice and stir to coat grains evenly.
  5. Pour in seafood broth and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add octopus pieces and peas, distributing evenly.
  7. Simmer uncovered for 15-18 minutes without stirring, until rice is cooked and liquid absorbed.
  8. In the last 5 minutes, add shrimp if using and cook until pink.
  9. Remove from heat; cover with a cloth and rest for 5 minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges.

Serve this paella with a chilled rosé or a light Spanish red wine for a well-rounded meal.

Tips for Perfectly Cooking Spanish Octopus

  • Slow and Low: Avoid boiling octopus at high temperatures; gentle simmering prevents rubbery texture.
  • Test for Doneness: Tenderness can be checked by piercing with a fork or skewer.
  • Resting Time: Letting cooked octopus rest enhances flavor absorption if marinated afterward.
  • Pairings: Olive oil, smoked paprika, lemon, garlic, and fresh herbs complement octopus beautifully.
  • Leftovers: Cooked octopus can be refrigerated for up to 2 days and works well cold in salads or reheated in any of these recipes.

Spanish octopus offers a world of flavor and culinary adventure. Whether you enjoy it simply grilled, in a robust stew, or creatively incorporated into a paella, mastering how to cook octopus transforms your kitchen into a vibrant Spanish seaside bodega. Try these recipes on your next cooking adventure for a taste that’s truly unforgettable. Bon appétit!
