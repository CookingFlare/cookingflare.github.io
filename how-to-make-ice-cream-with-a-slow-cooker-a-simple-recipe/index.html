How to Make Ice Cream with a Slow Cooker: A Simple Recipe


Ice cream is a beloved treat enjoyed by people of all ages. The creamy texture and delightful flavors make it a perfect dessert for any occasion. While traditional ice cream making can be a labor-intensive process, using a slow cooker simplifies the task significantly. In this post, we will guide you through a simple recipe for making ice cream with a slow cooker, ensuring that you can enjoy homemade ice cream without the fuss.

Why Use a Slow Cooker for Ice Cream?

Using a slow cooker to make ice cream offers several advantages:

  1. Ease of Preparation: The slow cooker does most of the work for you. You can simply combine the ingredients, set the cooker, and let it do its magic.
  2. Consistent Temperature: Slow cookers maintain a steady temperature, which is crucial for creating a smooth and creamy ice cream base.
  3. Versatility: You can experiment with various flavors and mix-ins, allowing you to customize your ice cream to suit your taste.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make delicious ice cream in your slow cooker, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream: This will provide the rich and creamy base for your ice cream.
  • 1 cup whole milk: Whole milk adds creaminess and helps balance the richness of the heavy cream.
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar: This sweetener will enhance the flavor of your ice cream.
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract: For a classic vanilla flavor, or you can substitute with other extracts for different flavors.
  • A pinch of salt: This enhances the overall flavor of the ice cream.
  • Optional mix-ins: Chocolate chips, crushed cookies, fruit, or nuts can be added for extra flavor and texture.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Slow Cooker

Before you start mixing your ingredients, ensure your slow cooker is clean and ready to use. If your slow cooker has a removable insert, you can take it out for easier mixing.

Step 2: Combine the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Whisk the mixture until the sugar is fully dissolved. This step is crucial for achieving a smooth texture in your ice cream.

Step 3: Pour the Mixture into the Slow Cooker

Once your mixture is well combined, pour it into the slow cooker insert. If you’re using a slow cooker with a removable insert, you can place it back into the cooker.

Step 4: Cook on Low

Set your slow cooker to the low setting and cover it with the lid. Allow the mixture to cook for about 2 to 3 hours. During this time, the mixture will thicken and develop a custard-like consistency. Stir the mixture every 30 minutes to ensure even cooking and prevent any sticking to the sides.

Step 5: Cool the Mixture

After the cooking time is complete, turn off the slow cooker and remove the insert (if applicable). Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature. This step is essential because adding the mixture to an ice cream maker while it’s still hot can lead to undesirable results.

Step 6: Chill in the Refrigerator

Once the mixture has cooled, transfer it to an airtight container and place it in the refrigerator. Chill the mixture for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. Chilling the mixture thoroughly will help it freeze more evenly in the next step.

Step 7: Churn the Ice Cream

After chilling, it’s time to churn your ice cream. If you have an ice cream maker, pour the chilled mixture into the machine and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This process typically takes about 20 to 30 minutes, depending on your machine.

If you don’t have an ice cream maker, you can still make ice cream without one. Pour the chilled mixture into a shallow dish and place it in the freezer. Every 30 minutes, stir the mixture vigorously with a fork to break up any ice crystals. Repeat this process for about 2 to 3 hours until the ice cream reaches your desired consistency.

Step 8: Add Mix-Ins

If you want to add mix-ins like chocolate chips, crushed cookies, or fruit, do so during the last few minutes of churning in the ice cream maker. If you’re using the manual method, fold in the mix-ins after the ice cream has reached a soft-serve consistency.

Step 9: Freeze the Ice Cream

Once your ice cream is churned and mixed, transfer it to an airtight container. Smooth the top with a spatula and cover it with a lid. Place the container in the freezer for at least 4 hours to allow the ice cream to firm up.

Step 10: Serve and Enjoy

After the ice cream has frozen to your desired consistency, it’s time to serve!
