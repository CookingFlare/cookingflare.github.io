Greek Orange Semolina Cake: A Citrusy and Moist Dessert


If you’re a fan of citrusy desserts, then this Greek Orange Semolina Cake is a must-try! Bursting with the vibrant flavors of orange, this cake is not only delicious but also incredibly moist. Made with semolina flour, this unique dessert has a slightly grainy texture that adds a delightful twist to the traditional cake experience.

Ingredients

  • 3 large oranges
  • 1 cup fine semolina flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a round cake pan with butter or cooking spray.

  2. Wash the oranges thoroughly and grate the zest of two oranges. Set the zest aside for later use. Juice all three oranges and set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.

  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Make sure to scrape down the sides of the bowl to ensure even mixing.

  5. In a separate bowl, combine the semolina flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.

  6. Gradually add the dry ingredient mixture to the butter mixture, alternating with the freshly squeezed orange juice. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing well after each addition.

  7. Finally, fold in the orange zest, ensuring it is evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  8. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula.

  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  10. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  11. Once the cake has cooled, dust it with powdered sugar for an elegant finishing touch.

Serving Suggestions

This Greek Orange Semolina Cake is perfect on its own or served with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The citrusy flavor pairs beautifully with a cup of hot tea or coffee, making it an ideal dessert for any time of the day.

Storage

To keep your cake fresh, store it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to three days. Alternatively, you can refrigerate it for up to a week. Just make sure to bring it to room temperature before serving to enhance its flavor and texture.

Final Thoughts

The Greek Orange Semolina Cake is a delightful dessert that will impress your friends and family with its unique taste and texture. The combination of citrusy orange and grainy semolina flour creates a cake that is both refreshing and satisfying. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet treat, this recipe is sure to be a hit. So, grab your apron and get ready to experience the zesty goodness of this moist and flavorful Greek Orange Semolina Cake!
