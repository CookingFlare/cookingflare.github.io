Farm to Table Grilling Recipes: Fire up the Flavor


Summer is here, and it’s time to fire up the grill! There’s something special about cooking over an open flame, don’t you think? The smoky aroma, the sizzling sounds, and the anticipation of biting into a perfectly grilled meal. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best farm-to-table grilling recipes that will make your taste buds dance with joy.

Grilled Peach and Prosciutto Salad

Summer is the perfect time to enjoy the sweetness of peaches, and pairing them with crispy prosciutto and fresh greens creates a salad that’s both refreshing and satisfying. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, sliced into wedges
  • 6 slices of prosciutto, torn into strips
  • 4 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the peach slices for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they develop a nice char.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, prosciutto, feta cheese, and mint.
  4. Top the salad with the grilled peaches and drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve immediately.

Grilled Steak Fajitas

There’s nothing quite like the flavor of a grilled steak, and when you pair it with sautéed onions and bell peppers, you’ve got a dish that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs flank steak, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 large onion, sliced into thin rings
  • 2 large bell peppers (any color), sliced into thin strips
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 small flour tortillas
  • Optional toppings: diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, sour cream, cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the steak strips for 3-4 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  3. Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the sliced onions and bell peppers, and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until they’re tender and lightly charred.
  4. Add the garlic, cumin, and smoked paprika to the skillet, and cook for an additional minute.
  5. Warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds.
  6. Assemble the fajitas by slicing the grilled steak into thin strips, and serving it with the sautéed onions and bell peppers, along with your desired toppings.

Grilled Corn with Herbs and Cheese

Summer is the perfect time to enjoy the sweetness of corn on the cob, and when you add some fresh herbs and melted cheese, you’ve got a side dish that’s sure to become a family favorite. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked and silked
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Grill the corn ears for 10-12 minutes, turning every 2-3 minutes, or until they’re lightly charred and tender.
  3. Meanwhile, mix the softened butter, garlic, parsley, and cheddar cheese in a small bowl.
  4. Once the corn is cooked, brush the herb butter mixture evenly onto each ear of corn.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve immediately.

Conclusion

Grilling is all about experimentation and having fun, so don’t be afraid to try new ingredients and flavor combinations. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or just starting out, these farm-to-table grilling recipes are sure to inspire you to fire up the grill and create some delicious meals for your family and friends. Happy grilling!
