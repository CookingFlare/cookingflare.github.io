Instant Pot Mongolian Beef: A Sweet and Savory Recipe


Are you craving a dish that combines the perfect balance of sweetness and savory flavors, with a rich and tender texture that melts in your mouth? Look no further than Instant Pot Mongolian Beef. This mouthwatering recipe delivers everything you love about Mongolian cuisine: a sticky, sweet sauce infused with ginger and garlic, paired with tender strips of beef that soak up all the delicious flavors. And the best part? You can make it in a fraction of the time using your trusty Instant Pot.

Whether you’re new to cooking with an Instant Pot or a seasoned pro, this recipe is simple, straightforward, and sure to impress. If you’re ready to elevate your weeknight dinners with a dish that rivals your favorite takeout, let’s dive into this Instant Pot Mongolian Beef recipe!

Why Choose Instant Pot for Mongolian Beef?

The Instant Pot is a game-changer when it comes to cooking time and flavor. By using the pressure cooker setting, your beef cooks in a fraction of the time it would take with traditional methods, all while maintaining its juiciness and tenderness. This means less time spent cooking and more time enjoying a delicious, homemade meal.

The beauty of cooking Mongolian beef in the Instant Pot lies in its ability to seal in the flavors. The combination of soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and brown sugar creates a luscious sauce that penetrates every bite of beef, giving you a rich, sweet, and savory flavor in every mouthful. Plus, the Instant Pot allows you to cook the beef quickly without overcooking it, ensuring it stays juicy and tender.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s make sure you have all the ingredients to create the perfect Mongolian beef. Here’s everything you’ll need for this recipe:

  • Beef: The cut of beef you choose is crucial to the texture of the dish. Flank steak, skirt steak, or sirloin are ideal for Mongolian beef. These cuts are tender and cook well in a short amount of time under pressure.
  • Soy Sauce: Soy sauce is a key ingredient that brings a salty, umami flavor to the sauce.
  • Hoisin Sauce: Hoisin sauce adds a subtle sweetness and depth of flavor to the dish.
  • Brown Sugar: Brown sugar gives the dish its signature sweetness, helping to balance the salty flavors of soy and hoisin sauce.
  • Garlic and Ginger: Fresh garlic and ginger are a must for this recipe. They infuse the sauce with a delightful aromatic quality.
  • Cornstarch: Cornstarch is used to thicken the sauce, giving it that glossy, sticky texture.
  • Sesame Oil: This oil adds a nutty, aromatic flavor that elevates the dish.
  • Green Onions: Green onions (scallions) are used as a garnish and provide a refreshing contrast to the rich sauce.
  • Red Pepper Flakes (optional): If you like a little heat in your Mongolian beef, add red pepper flakes to taste.

For the Beef:

  • 1 pound of flank steak (or your preferred cut of beef)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 1 tablespoon garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for thickening)
  • 1/2 cup water (to help build the sauce base)
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Garnish:

  • Green onions (scallions), chopped
  • Sesame seeds (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide to Instant Pot Mongolian Beef

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

Begin by slicing your beef into thin strips. The key to Mongolian beef’s tender texture is the way the beef is sliced. Make sure to cut against the grain to help break down the muscle fibers and ensure the meat stays tender after cooking. Aim for strips about 1/4 inch thick to ensure they cook evenly and absorb the sauce nicely.

Once your beef is sliced, season it lightly with salt and pepper. While this step is optional, it helps to add a bit of flavor right to the beef itself before it soaks up the sauce.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

Turn your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function. Add the sesame oil to the pot and let it heat up. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and ginger. Stir frequently, allowing them to sauté for about 1-2 minutes, just until fragrant. Be careful not to let the garlic burn, as it can turn bitter.

This step infuses the oil with the garlic and ginger’s aromatic flavors, which will then carry through to the sauce.

Step 3: Add the Beef

Add the sliced beef to the Instant Pot, stirring it well to coat the beef in the garlic, ginger, and sesame oil. Sauté the beef for about 3-4 minutes, just until it starts to brown slightly. This step helps to lock in the flavors and gives the beef a bit of a sear, adding depth to the overall dish.

Step 4: Make the Sauce

Once the beef has browned, it’s time to add the ingredients for the sauce. Add the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, brown sugar, and water to the pot. Stir everything together, making sure the brown sugar dissolves completely.

In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry. Add this mixture to the Instant Pot to help thicken the sauce as it cooks. If you like a bit of heat in your Mongolian beef, sprinkle in the red pepper flakes at this point.

Give the sauce a good stir to combine all of the ingredients, making sure the beef is well coated.

Step 5: Pressure Cook

Now it’s time to cook everything to perfection. Secure the lid on the Instant Pot and set the valve to the sealing position. Press the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” button and set the time for 10 minutes on high pressure.

Once the cooking time is complete, allow the Instant Pot to naturally release pressure for about 5 minutes before switching the valve to “venting” to release any remaining pressure.

Step 6: Final Touches

Carefully remove the lid and give the Mongolian beef a good stir. The sauce should have thickened up nicely, and the beef should be tender and perfectly cooked.

Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a sweeter dish, you can add a little more brown sugar. If you like it saltier, add an extra splash of soy sauce. This is where you can customize the flavor to suit your preferences.

Step 7: Serve

Once you’re satisfied with the flavor, transfer the Mongolian beef to a serving dish. Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds for an added touch of freshness and crunch.

Serve your Instant Pot Mongolian Beef over a bed of steamed rice, or pair it with stir-fried vegetables for a complete and satisfying meal. The sticky, sweet, and savory sauce will soak into the rice, creating an irresistible dish that will have everyone coming back for more.

Tips for Perfect Mongolian Beef

  1. Cut the Beef Thinly: For the best texture, slice the beef as thinly as possible. This will help it cook quickly and absorb the sauce more effectively.

  2. Customize the Sauce: Don’t be afraid to adjust the sweetness and saltiness of the sauce to match your taste. Some people like their Mongolian beef extra sweet, while others prefer a more savory profile.

  3. Avoid Overcrowding: If you’re making a larger batch, consider cooking the beef in batches to avoid overcrowding the Instant Pot. This will help ensure that the beef cooks evenly and that the sauce has room to thicken properly.

  4. Add Vegetables: You can easily make this a one-pot meal by adding vegetables like bell peppers, broccoli, or snap peas. Simply add them during the last few minutes of pressure cooking, and they will cook perfectly in the sauce.

  5. Use Leftovers for Meal Prep: Instant Pot Mongolian Beef makes fantastic leftovers. The flavors only deepen as the dish sits, making it a great option for meal prep. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
