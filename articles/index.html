Satisfying Gluten-Free Chicken and Rice Casserole: A Comforting Dish

Are you in the mood for a delicious and comforting meal that won’t leave you feeling bloated or guilty? Look no further! Our Satisfying Gluten-Free Chicken and Rice Casserole is the perfect dish to satisfy your cravings without compromising your dietary needs. Ingredients 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded 2 cups cooked brown rice 1 cup broccoli florets 1 cup carrots, diced 1 cup peas 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup chicken broth 1 cup unsweetened almond milk 2 tablespoons gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a casserole dish with cooking spray. In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent. Add the diced carrots, broccoli florets, and peas to the skillet. Cook for 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are slightly tender. In a separate bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, almond milk, gluten-free all-purpose flour, dried thyme, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Ensure that the flour is fully dissolved. Pour the mixture into the skillet with the vegetables. Stir well to combine. Add the cooked chicken and brown rice to the skillet. Stir until all the ingredients are evenly coated with the sauce. Transfer the mixture to the greased casserole dish, spreading it out evenly. Cover the casserole dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. After 30 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbling. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serving Suggestions This Gluten-Free Chicken and Rice Casserole is a complete meal on its own, but you can also serve it with a fresh green salad or steamed vegetables for added nutrition. Gluten-Free Sushi and Sashimi for Japanese Dining

Japanese cuisine has been loved by many for its simplicity and focus on using fresh ingredients. This culinary tradition extends beyond rice, noodles, and tempura to encompass sushi and sashimi. If you’re wanting to serve a gloriously gluten-free meal, indulge in this traditional Japanese delicacy with a twist: gluten-free sushi and sashimi. Ingredients for Gluten-Free Sushi Gluten-free nori sheets: These sheets, manufactured using rice or seaweed flour instead of wheat, are the base for sushi rolls. Grated daikon – Sushi’s signature tangy aroma comes from daikon radish. It’s delightfully crisp when eaten raw, making it a staple in sushi rolls. Avocado – This creamy and buttery fruit is rich in fiber, antioxidants, and essential nutrients like potassium and magnesium. Cooked sweet potato – Sweet potatoes have a warming, almost buttery flavor that melds well with sushi rice. They’re also nutritious, packed with vitamins A and C. Tuna – This protein is righteously lean and serves as a perfect canvas for the other ingredients. Rice vinegar – This ingredient acidifies sushi rice, providing a refreshing tang, and helps to bring out the inherent flavors of the rice. Rice – Gluten-free short-grain rice is preferable because it has a chewy, clingy texture that sustains when pressed. Sticky rice is crucial for sushi rolls. Water – For sushi rice, it’s necessary to use the right ratio of water to achieve a perfect texture. Packed brown sugar – This ingredient plays an essential part in sushi rice seasoning found in natural kitchens. Ingredients for Gluten-Free Sashimi Sushi-grade tuna or salmon – Though fresh fish is vital in sashimi, sushi-grade fish guarantees that parasites are eliminated. Toxic-free soy sauce – Gluten-free soy sauce makes the taste perfect for sashimi without the addition of gluten. Wasabi paste – The hot, green paste excites the taste buds with its fiery flavor. Preparing Gluten-Free Sushi Preparing sushi rice Rinse one cup (240 ml) of rice in a fine mesh colander under cold water, allowing the water to drain away. Transfer rice to a medium saucepan, add 1.5 cups (360 ml) water and let it rest for 30 minutes. Tuna and cook the rice over low heat until all water has been absorbed. In a small saucepan, combine two tablespoons rice vinegar, packed brown sugar, and a pinch of salt. Cook over medium heat stirring until fully dissolved. Pour the seasoned rice vinegar over the cooked rice and quickly fold it gently with a wooden rice paddle, spatula, or the back of a spoon. Cover and allow the rice to cool. Preparing sushi rolls Lay a sheet of gluten-free nori on a sushi mat or a piece of plastic wrap spread out on your work surface. Spread one-third of seasoned rice per sheet on the nori. Starting from the bottom edge of the nori, begin to roll. Place one slice each of avocado and sweet potato on the top third of the rice bed. Add a thin ribbon of tuna along them. Pull the bottom edge of the mat over the ingredients and roll the sushi tightly into cylinder shapes. Seal with a little bit of rice water. Slice the sushi rolls into five equal portions with a sharp wet knife. Preparing Gluten-Free Sashimi Thoroughly wash any potential parasites away from the fish by soaking sushi-grade fish in water. Slice the fish thinly against the grain. Prepare sushi-quality soy sauce. Serving Your Gluten-Free Treat Sushi served with an appetizing dish, such as miso soup or pickled cucumber slices, makes a light and nutritious meal that your taste buds will appreciate. Gluten-free sashimi is visually appealing and each piece of fish melts away, making you feel refreshed with every bite. ...
