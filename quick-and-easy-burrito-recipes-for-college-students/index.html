Quick and Easy Burrito Recipes for College Students


Welcome to our quick and easy burrito recipes for college students! We understand that as a college student, you’re often short on time and budget, but that doesn’t mean you have to compromise on delicious and satisfying meals. Burritos are a perfect go-to meal that’s not only convenient but also versatile and customizable to suit your taste preferences. Whether you’re craving a hearty breakfast, a filling lunch, or a satisfying dinner, these burrito recipes have got you covered. Let’s dive into these simple yet flavorful recipes that will become staples in your college cooking repertoire.

Breakfast Burrito

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 large flour tortillas
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Salsa (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a non-stick skillet over medium heat, sauté the bell peppers and onions until they soften.
  2. Crack the eggs into the skillet and scramble them with the sautéed vegetables until they are fully cooked.
  3. Season the eggs with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Warm the flour tortillas in a separate skillet or microwave for a few seconds until they are pliable.
  5. Divide the scrambled eggs evenly between the two tortillas.
  6. Sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese over the eggs.
  7. If desired, add a spoonful of salsa for an extra kick.
  8. Fold the sides of the tortillas over the filling, then roll them up tightly.
  9. Serve the breakfast burritos immediately or wrap them in foil for an on-the-go meal.

Veggie and Black Bean Burrito

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 2 large flour tortillas
  • Sour cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the black beans, cooked rice, diced tomatoes, red onions, cilantro, ground cumin, and chili powder. Mix well to incorporate the flavors.
  2. Warm the flour tortillas in a skillet or microwave until they are pliable.
  3. Divide the black bean and rice mixture evenly between the two tortillas.
  4. If desired, add a dollop of sour cream for a creamy element.
  5. Fold the sides of the tortillas over the filling, then roll them up tightly.
  6. For a warm burrito, heat the assembled burritos in a skillet for a few minutes on each side until they are golden brown and crispy.
  7. Alternatively, enjoy the burritos cold for a refreshing meal on a hot day.

Chicken and Avocado Burrito

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked, shredded chicken
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup diced red bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup diced red onions
  • 1/4 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 2 large flour tortillas
  • Lime wedges (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the cooked, shredded chicken in a bowl and season it with salt and pepper.
  2. Arrange the sliced avocado, diced red bell peppers, and red onions on a clean work surface.
  3. Warm the flour tortillas until they are pliable.
  4. Divide the seasoned chicken evenly between the two tortillas.
  5. Layer the avocado slices, bell peppers, and onions over the chicken.
  6. Sprinkle shredded Monterey Jack cheese over the filling.
  7. Squeeze a lime wedge over each burrito for a burst of citrus flavor, if desired.
  8. Fold the sides of the tortillas over the filling, then roll them up tightly.
  9. Serve the chicken and avocado burritos with extra lime wedges for an added zing.

These quick and easy burrito recipes are perfect for college students who are looking for satisfying meals that can be prepared in a flash. Whether you prefer a breakfast burrito to kickstart your day, a veggie and black bean burrito for a wholesome lunch, or a chicken and avocado burrito for a protein-packed dinner, these recipes are sure to become your go-to favorites. Feel free to customize the fillings to suit your taste and dietary preferences, and don’t forget to share these recipes with your fellow college students. Happy cooking!
