Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Butter: A Halloween Dinner Recipe


As the crisp autumn air settles in and leaves begin to turn shades of orange and gold, it’s time to embrace the cozy flavors of the season. What better way to celebrate Halloween than with a delightful dish of Pumpkin Ravioli adorned with a rich Sage Butter sauce? This recipe not only captures the essence of fall but also brings a festive touch to your dinner table. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through crafting these delicious ravioli from scratch, paired with a fragrant sage butter that elevates the dish to new heights.

Ingredients

For the Ravioli Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 3 large eggs
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the Pumpkin Filling

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (canned or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sage Butter Sauce

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 10-12 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Making the Ravioli Dough

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and crack the eggs into it. Add the olive oil. Using a fork, beat the eggs gently, slowly incorporating the flour from the edges of the well. Once combined, knead the mixture by hand for about 8-10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. Wrap it in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature.

  2. Roll Out the Dough: After resting, divide the dough into quarters. Keep three pieces covered while you work with one. On a floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/16-inch thickness, or as thin as possible without tearing. If you have a pasta machine, you can use it to achieve an even thickness.

Preparing the Pumpkin Filling

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium bowl, mix the pumpkin puree, ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, nutmeg, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. The filling should be creamy and flavorful, with a perfect balance of sweetness and spice.

Assembling the Ravioli

  1. Cut the Dough: Lay out one sheet of rolled dough on a floured surface. Using a round cutter or a glass, cut out circles of dough, about 3 inches in diameter.

  2. Fill the Ravioli: Place a teaspoon of the pumpkin filling in the center of each circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the ravioli to burst during cooking.

  3. Seal the Ravioli: Moisten the edges of the dough circle with a little water. Carefully fold the dough in half over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges together firmly to seal, ensuring no air pockets remain. You can also crimp the edges with a fork for an added decorative touch.

  4. Repeat the Process: Continue this process with the remaining dough and filling, laying the finished ravioli on a floured baking sheet.

Cooking the Ravioli

  1. Boil Water: In a large pot, bring salted water to a gentle boil. Be careful not to let it reach a rolling boil, as this can break the delicate ravioli.

  2. Cook the Ravioli: Carefully drop the ravioli into the boiling water, cooking them in batches if necessary. They should take about 3-4 minutes to cook. The ravioli are done when they float to the surface. Use a slotted spoon to gently remove them from the pot and transfer them to a plate.

Making the Sage Butter Sauce

  1. Melt the Butter: In a skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat until it begins to foam.

  2. Add the Sage: Once the butter is foamy, add the fresh sage leaves. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, allowing the sage to infuse the butter and turn a lovely golden brown. Be cautious not to burn the butter, as it can develop a bitter flavor.

  3. Season the Sauce: Add salt and pepper to taste. If desired, you can also add a splash of lemon juice for brightness.

Serving the Dish

  1. Plate the Ravioli: Place the cooked pumpkin ravioli on a serving platter or individual plates. Drizzle the sage butter sauce generously over the top, ensuring each ravioli is coated in that delicious, fragrant sauce.

  2. Garnish: For an extra touch, sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese over the ravioli and garnish with additional fresh sage leaves.

  3. Enjoy: Serve your Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Butter warm, paired with a crisp autumn salad or a glass of your favorite white wine. This dish is perfect for Halloween gatherings, family dinners, or simply indulging in the season’s flavors.

Tips and Variations

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the ravioli in advance and freeze them before cooking. Just make sure to separate each layer with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

  • Spice It Up: For a spicy twist, consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or crushed red pepper flakes to the pumpkin filling for a bit of heat.

  • Herb Alternatives: While sage works beautifully with pumpkin, feel free to experiment with other herbs such as thyme or rosemary for a different flavor profile.

  • Vegan Option: To make this dish vegan, substitute the ricotta and Parmesan with plant-based alternatives and use aquafaba or a flax egg in place of the regular eggs in the dough.

Conclusion

Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Butter is more than just a dish; it’s a celebration of the flavors of fall, and a perfect way to impress your guests during the Halloween season. The combination of sweet pumpkin filling and the aromatic sage butter creates a comforting yet elegant meal that is sure to become a staple in your autumn menu. Whether you’re hosting a Halloween party or enjoying a cozy dinner at home, this recipe will bring warmth and joy to your table. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the cooking begin! Happy Halloween!
