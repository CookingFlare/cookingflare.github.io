Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken: A Juicy and Flavorful Main Course


If you’re looking for a delicious and impressive main course that will leave your guests begging for seconds, look no further than Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken. This classic Mediterranean dish is bursting with flavors, thanks to the combination of zesty lemon, fragrant herbs, and tender roasted chicken. Not only is it incredibly tasty, but it’s also a breeze to prepare. So roll up your sleeves and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 4 lemons, divided
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon of dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Start by preparing the marinade. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, dried oregano, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, black pepper, and the juice of 2 lemons. Stir well to combine.
  3. Place the whole chicken in a large roasting pan or baking dish. Use a sharp knife to score the chicken’s skin, creating small slits all over the surface. This will allow the marinade to penetrate the meat and infuse it with flavor.
  4. Pour the marinade over the chicken, making sure to coat it evenly. Use your hands to massage the marinade into the chicken, ensuring every nook and cranny is covered.
  5. Slice the remaining 2 lemons into thin rounds. Place the lemon slices on top of the chicken, tucking them into the slits you made earlier. These lemon slices will not only add a beautiful presentation but also infuse the chicken with even more citrusy goodness.
  6. Drizzle the extra virgin olive oil over the chicken, giving it a glossy sheen and enhancing the flavors.
  7. Cover the roasting pan with aluminum foil, sealing it tightly. This will help create a steamy environment that ensures a juicy and tender result.
  8. Place the roasting pan in the preheated oven and roast the chicken for about 1 hour and 30 minutes.
  9. After the initial cooking time, remove the aluminum foil and continue roasting for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the chicken’s skin turns golden brown and crispy.
  10. Once the chicken is fully cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.
  11. Carve the chicken into serving pieces and serve it with the pan juices and roasted lemon slices. The tangy and aromatic flavors of the Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken are best enjoyed alongside a side of roasted potatoes or a fresh Greek salad.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of freshness, sprinkle some chopped fresh parsley or dill over the roasted chicken before serving.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes to the marinade.
  • To ensure an evenly cooked chicken, use a meat thermometer to check for doneness. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C).
  • If you have leftovers, Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken makes a fantastic filling for sandwiches or wraps. Simply slice the cooked chicken and stuff it into pita bread or tortillas along with some Greek tzatziki sauce and fresh vegetables.

Now that you have the recipe for Greek Lemon Roasted Chicken, it’s time to gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure. This succulent and flavorful main course is perfect for special occasions or even a simple weeknight dinner. So, fire up your oven, embrace the Mediterranean flavors, and get ready to indulge in a taste of Greece like never before.

Enjoy your meal!
