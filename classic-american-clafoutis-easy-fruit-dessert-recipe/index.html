Classic American Clafoutis: Easy Fruit Dessert Recipe


Clafoutis is a delightful dessert that has captured the hearts and taste buds of many. Originating from the Limousin region of France, this simple yet elegant dish has made its way into American kitchens, where it has been embraced and adapted to include a variety of fruits. This Classic American Clafoutis recipe combines the traditional custard-like batter with fresh, seasonal fruits that make it a must-try for dessert lovers everywhere. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply indulging in a sweet treat at home, this easy fruit dessert is sure to impress.

What is Clafoutis?

At its core, clafoutis is a baked custard filled with fruit. The traditional French version uses black cherries, but over time, different regions have put their own spin on this classic recipe. In America, clafoutis has evolved to include a wide array of fruits like blueberries, strawberries, peaches, and even apples.

The beauty of clafoutis lies in its versatility. You can customize it according to the fruits in season or your personal preferences. The basic components—a simple batter of eggs, milk, and flour—create a tender, pancake-like texture that envelops the fruit, resulting in a comforting and delicious dessert.

The History Behind Clafoutis

Clafoutis has a rich and storied history, dating back to the 19th century. The dish was originally created in the Limousin region, where locals would bake fresh cherries in a simple batter. The name “clafoutis” comes from the Occitan word “clafotir,” which means “to fill.” Traditionally, clafoutis was made with unpitted cherries, as the pits added a unique almond flavor to the dish.

As the recipe spread beyond France, it underwent various adaptations to accommodate local ingredients and tastes. In America, clafoutis has become a popular choice for brunches and dessert gatherings, showcasing a variety of fruits that celebrate the bountiful harvest.

Ingredients for Classic American Clafoutis

To make a Classic American Clafoutis, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Fresh Fruit: 2 cups of your preferred fruit (cherries, blueberries, strawberries, or peaches)
  • Eggs: 3 large eggs
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup granulated sugar, plus extra for dusting
  • Milk: 1 cup whole milk
  • Flour: 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Butter: 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • Powdered Sugar: For dusting (optional)

Choosing the Right Fruit

When selecting fruit for your clafoutis, opt for the freshest and ripest options available. Seasonal fruits work best, as they offer the most flavor. Here are some popular choices:

  • Cherries: The classic choice, sweet or tart, with pits for added flavor.
  • Blueberries: Juicy and sweet, they create a lovely contrast with the custard.
  • Strawberries: Sliced strawberries add a bright color and refreshing taste.
  • Peaches: Tender and sweet, they provide a delightful flavor profile.

Feel free to mix and match fruits to create your unique version of clafoutis!

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that the clafoutis will bake evenly and achieve a lovely golden color.

Step 2: Prepare the Baking Dish

Grease a 9-inch round pie dish or a similar-sized baking dish with butter. This will help prevent the clafoutis from sticking and make for an easier cleanup. For added flavor, you can sprinkle a little granulated sugar in the dish after buttering it.

Step 3: Prepare the Fruit

Wash and dry your chosen fruit. If you are using cherries, it’s best to pit them. If you’re using larger fruits like peaches or strawberries, slice them into manageable pieces. Distribute the prepared fruit evenly in the bottom of the greased baking dish.

Step 4: Make the Batter

In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and granulated sugar until the mixture is light and frothy. Gradually add the milk, melted butter, vanilla extract, and salt, mixing until well combined. Finally, sift in the flour, stirring gently until there are no lumps. The batter should be smooth and slightly thick.

Step 5: Pour the Batter

Carefully pour the batter over the arranged fruit in the baking dish. Make sure the fruit is evenly submerged in the batter.

Step 6: Bake

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 35-45 minutes, or until the clafoutis is puffed up and golden brown. A toothpick inserted into the center should come out clean, indicating that it is fully cooked.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the clafoutis from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes. It will deflate slightly as it cools, which is normal. Dust the top with powdered sugar before slicing and serving. Clafoutis can be enjoyed warm, at room temperature, or even chilled.

Tips for Making the Perfect Clafoutis

  1. Let the batter rest: For an even fluffier texture, let your batter rest for at least 15 minutes before pouring it over the fruit. This allows the flour to hydrate fully and helps create a lighter custard.

  2. Experiment with flavors: Feel free to add spices such as cinnamon or nutmeg to the batter for an extra layer of flavor. A splash of almond extract can also enhance the dish, especially when using stone fruits like cherries and peaches.

  3. Add a twist: For a more decadent dessert, consider adding a layer of cream cheese or ricotta cheese mixed with sugar between the fruit and batter for added richness.

  4. Serve with accompaniments: Clafoutis pairs beautifully with whipped cream, vanilla ice cream, or a drizzle of warm caramel sauce. You can also accompany it with fresh berries for a pop of color and flavor.

  5. Storage: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Variations on Clafoutis

While the classic version of clafoutis is delicious, there are many variations you can try to keep things interesting:

  • Chocolate Clafoutis: Add cocoa powder to the batter for a rich, chocolatey twist. Pair it with raspberries or cherries for a delightful combination.
  • Savory Clafoutis: Substitute the sugar for herbs and cheese to create a savory version. Spinach, feta, and sun-dried tomatoes can make for a delicious twist on this traditional dish.
  • Gluten-Free Clafoutis: Substitute all-purpose flour with almond flour or a gluten-free blend to cater to those with dietary restrictions.

Conclusion

Classic American Clafoutis is a wonderfully versatile dessert that celebrates the beauty of seasonal fruits. With its easy preparation and delightful results, it’s an ideal choice for any occasion. Whether you’re enjoying it for brunch, dessert, or a sweet snack, this clafoutis recipe is sure to become a favorite in your repertoire. So gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and prepare to indulge in this delightful fruit dessert that’s both simple and sophisticated!
