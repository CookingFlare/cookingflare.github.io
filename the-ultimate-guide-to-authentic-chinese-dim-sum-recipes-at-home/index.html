The Ultimate Guide to Authentic Chinese Dim Sum Recipes at Home


Chinese dim sum is a delightful culinary experience that transcends time. It is a beloved tradition practiced for centuries, enjoyed by families and friends as they gather around tables laden with delicate, bite-sized masterpieces. While dim sum is traditionally enjoyed at a restaurant, it is surprisingly easy to create authentic dim sum dishes in the comfort of your home. In this ultimate guide, we reveal easy-to-follow recipes for signature dishes, from unforgettable dumplings to flaky egg tarts.

HAR GAU (Pork Dumplings)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. ground pork
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 2 tablespoons grated ginger
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 package of round dumpling wraps (about 20 wrappers)
  • Water (for sealing the dumplings)
  • Dipping sauce (mix equal parts soy sauce and sesame oil)

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, green onions, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. Mix well and set aside.
  2. To assemble the dumplings, lay out a dumpling wrapper on a clean surface. Place a small amount of filling in the center of the wrapper (about 1 tablespoon) and moisten the edges with water.
  3. Fold the wrapper in half and press the edges together to seal. Repeat this process with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  4. To cook, heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a small amount of oil. Place the dumplings in the skillet, leaving enough space between each dumpling. Cook until the bottom layer is golden brown (about 3-4 minutes).
  5. Add 1/4 cup of water to the skillet and cover with a tight-fitting lid. Steam the dumplings for about 8 minutes or until all the water has evaporated.
  6. Remove the lid and allow the dumplings to cook for an additional 2 minutes or until the bottoms are crispy.
  7. Serve hot with your homemade dipping sauce.

SHU MAI (Pork and Shrimp Shumai)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. ground pork
  • 1/2 lb. shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 4 green onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cups of shumai wrappers
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch (mixed with 1/4 cup of water for sealing)

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, pulse the shrimp until they reach a paste-like consistency. Transfer to a mixing bowl and add the ground pork, green onions, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Mix well and set aside.
  2. To assemble the shumai, spoon a small amount of the filling mixture into each shumai wrapper.
  3. Brush the edges of the wrapper with the cornstarch slurry and roll the filling into a small log. Repeat this process with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  4. Place the shumai in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper and steam for about 10-12 minutes or until cooked through.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

CHAR SIU BAO (BBQ PORK BUNS)

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. pork belly (cut into cubes)
  • 2 tablespoons five-spice powder
  • 3 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Shaoxing wine
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • qb. honey
  • Parmesan cheese (for steaming)
  • 2 cups of bun dough

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the pork belly, five-spice powder, hoisin sauce, Shaoxing wine, sesame oil, soy sauce, and honey. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place the marinated pork in a single layer on the baking sheet and bake for 25 minutes or until the pork is tender and caramelized.
  3. Let the pork cool before chopping it into small pieces.
  4. To assemble the buns, roll out the bun dough into small balls and flatten them slightly. Place a small amount of the chopped pork in the center of each dough ball and fold the dough around the pork, sealing the edges.
  5. Steam the buns in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper for about 15 minutes. Brush the buns with some sesame oil and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese to enhance the crust.
  6. Serve warm with a side of hoisin sauce.

EGG TARTS (Dan Tat)

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (cold and cubed)
  • 3/4 cup ice water
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 whole eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 3/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Heavy cream (to brush the crust)

Instructions:

  1. To make the crust, combine the flour and butter in a food processor and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water and pulse until the dough comes together.
  2. Flatten the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C) and line a muffin tin with parchment paper or cooking spray.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about 1/4-inch thickness. Using a circle cutter, cut out rounds large enough to fit into the muffin tin.
  5. Place the dough rounds in the muffin tin and gently press the dough into the bottom and sides. Refrigerate for 10 minutes.
  6. To make the filling, whisk together the sugar, whole eggs, egg yolks, and vanilla extract in a mixing bowl.
  7. Brush the crust with heavy cream and pour the filling into each crust.
  8. Bake for about 20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set.
  9. Allow the egg tarts to cool before serving.
