10 Easy Recipes for Cooking with Tofu


Tofu is often seen as a simple, bland ingredient, but it’s actually an incredibly versatile and nourishing food. Whether you’re a seasoned vegetarian, vegan, or just looking to try something different, tofu can easily take on a variety of flavors and textures. With this guide to ten easy tofu recipes, you’ll discover how to transform this humble ingredient into dishes that excite your taste buds and satisfy your hunger.

Why Cook with Tofu?

Before diving into the recipes, it’s worth highlighting the benefits of cooking with tofu. Made from soybeans, tofu is packed with plant-based protein, low in calories, and rich in important minerals like calcium and iron. Its neutral flavor means it can effortlessly absorb marinades and spices, making it an excellent canvas for all sorts of dishes — from hearty meals to light snacks.

Now, let’s explore a set of 10 easy tofu recipes that maximize flavor and simplicity.

1. Crispy Pan-Fried Tofu

If you’re craving crunch, this crispy pan-fried tofu is your answer. It’s perfect as a topping for salads or grain bowls or served alongside steamed vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika

Instructions:

  1. Drain the tofu and press it between paper towels to remove excess water. Cut into 1-inch cubes.
  2. In a medium bowl, toss tofu with soy sauce.
  3. Sprinkle cornstarch, garlic powder, and smoked paprika; toss again until evenly coated.
  4. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Cook tofu cubes for about 3-4 minutes on each side until golden and crispy.
  6. Remove from heat and serve immediately.

2. Tofu Stir-Fry with Vegetables

A quick and colorful stir-fry loaded with fresh vegetables and flavorful tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz extra-firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, sliced thin
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp honey or maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu and cut into cubes.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  3. Add tofu cubes and cook until lightly browned on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, add garlic and ginger; sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Add bell pepper, broccoli, and carrot; stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until crisp-tender.
  6. Return tofu to the pan. Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, and honey or maple syrup.
  7. Toss well and cook for another 2 minutes.
  8. Garnish with green onions and serve over rice or noodles.

3. Silken Tofu Smoothie Bowl

Start your day with a creamy and nutrient-packed tofu smoothie bowl. Silken tofu creates a velvety texture and adds protein without dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup silken tofu
  • 1 banana
  • 1/2 cup frozen berries (blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1/4 cup almond milk (or other plant milk)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or agave (optional)
  • Toppings: granola, sliced fruits, nuts, coconut flakes

Instructions:

  1. Blend silken tofu, banana, frozen berries, almond milk, chia seeds, and sweetener until smooth.
  2. Pour into a bowl and garnish with your favorite toppings.
  3. Enjoy as a refreshing breakfast or snack.

4. Tofu Scramble with Spinach

Replace scrambled eggs effortlessly with this savory tofu scramble, packed with vibrant veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, crumbled
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1/2 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric (for color)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt & pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and bell pepper, sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Crumble tofu with your hands and add to the skillet.
  4. Stir in turmeric, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  5. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Toss in fresh spinach and cook until wilted.
  7. Serve as a breakfast side or in a wrap.

5. Baked Tofu Nuggets

Homemade tofu nuggets that are crispy on the outside and tender on the inside—a crowd-pleaser for both kids and adults.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup bread crumbs
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika

Instructions:

  1. Marinate tofu pieces in soy sauce for 20 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. In a bowl, combine bread crumbs, garlic powder, and smoked paprika.
  4. Dip tofu pieces into olive oil, then coat thoroughly with bread crumb mixture.
  5. Place on baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and crispy.
  6. Serve with your favorite dipping sauce.

6. Tofu and Mushroom Stroganoff

A comforting, creamy stroganoff made entirely plant-based by swapping meat for tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, cubed
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup vegan sour cream or cashew cream
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp flour (for thickening)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooked noodles or rice, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add onion and garlic, cooking until translucent.
  3. Add mushrooms and cook until soft.
  4. Toss in tofu cubes and soy sauce; sauté for 5 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle flour and paprika, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  6. Slowly add vegetable broth, stirring to combine.
  7. Let sauce simmer for 5 minutes until thickened.
  8. Stir in vegan sour cream, then cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. Season to taste and serve over noodles or rice.

7. BBQ Tofu Sandwiches

Juicy and flavorful BBQ tofu makes for a delicious plant-based sandwich option.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, sliced
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 4 burger buns
  • Coleslaw or pickles for topping
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Press tofu to remove water, then slice into cutlets.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  3. Cook tofu slices until browned on both sides.
  4. Brush generously with BBQ sauce and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Toast buns lightly.
  6. Assemble sandwiches with tofu, extra BBQ sauce, and coleslaw or pickles.

8. Thai Peanut Tofu Noodles

This dish balances creamy peanut sauce with the satisfying texture of tofu and noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, cubed
  • 8 oz rice noodles or spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter (natural and creamy)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Chopped peanuts and cilantro for garnish
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions; drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan and fry tofu until golden.
  3. Whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, maple syrup, ginger, and garlic in a bowl.
  4. Toss noodles, tofu, and sauce together until fully coated.
  5. Garnish with chopped peanuts and cilantro before serving.

9. Tofu and Veggie Skewers

Grilled tofu skewers are perfect for a summer barbecue or an easy weeknight dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz firm tofu, cubed
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Zucchini slices
  • Red onion chunks
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine olive oil, soy sauce, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Toss tofu cubes and vegetables in the marinade; let sit for 30 minutes.
  3. Thread tofu and vegetables alternately onto skewers.
  4. Grill or broil skewers for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally.
  5. Serve hot for a light, fresh meal.

10. Tofu Chocolate Mousse

End your meal on a sweet note with this velvety chocolate mousse made from silken tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup silken tofu
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips, melted
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup or agave
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Melt chocolate chips gently using a double boiler or microwave.
  2. In a blender, combine silken tofu, melted chocolate, sweetener, vanilla, and salt.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour.
  5. Serve topped with fresh berries or coconut whipped cream.

Final Thoughts

Tofu’s adaptability is its biggest strength. Once you understand how to press it and which cooking methods complement its texture, the possibilities become nearly endless. From savory mains to sweet treats, these straightforward recipes serve as a launchpad for experimenting in the kitchen. Enjoy discovering your next favorite way to cook with tofu!

Happy cooking!
