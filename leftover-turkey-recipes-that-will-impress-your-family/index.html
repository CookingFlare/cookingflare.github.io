Leftover Turkey Recipes That Will Impress Your Family


The holidays often leave us with one delicious challenge: what to do with all that leftover turkey. While turkey sandwiches and basic casseroles might come to mind first, leftover turkey truly has the potential to shine in a variety of creative recipes that will delight your family long after the holiday feast is over. From hearty soups and vibrant salads to comforting pastas and exotic curries, these dishes breathe new life into your leftovers, transforming them into unforgettable meals.

1. Creamy Turkey and Wild Rice Soup

When the chill lingers and comfort is the ultimate craving, a bowl of creamy turkey and wild rice soup fits the bill perfectly. This recipe blends tender turkey chunks, earthy wild rice, and a rich creamy broth, making it a soothing yet satisfying option.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded or cubed
  • 1 cup wild rice, cooked
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or turkey broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add onions, carrots, and celery; sauté until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Stir in garlic and thyme, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Pour in broth and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add cooked wild rice and turkey, letting it warm and infuse the broth.
  5. Lower heat and stir in the cream, seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Heat through gently, avoiding boiling after adding cream.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

This soup not only makes the turkey go further but offers a balance of textures and flavors that refresh your post-holiday palate.

2. Turkey and Cranberry Quesadillas

For a fun and unexpectedly delicious twist on leftover turkey, quesadillas bring together the savory richness of turkey and the sweet tang of cranberry sauce. Quick to assemble and perfect for a casual meal, these quesadillas will get rave reviews.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded cooked turkey
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup leftover cranberry sauce
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • Butter or oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place one tortilla in the skillet, sprinkle half with cheese, turkey, and dollops of cranberry sauce.
  3. Fold the tortilla over and press gently.
  4. Cook until the bottom is golden brown, then flip carefully to toast the other side.
  5. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
  6. Slice into wedges and serve with a dollop of sour cream or salsa.

The contrast between the melted cheese and cranberry tartness adds a burst of flavor that can renew your excitement for turkey leftovers.

3. Turkey Pot Pie with a Flaky Herb Crust

Few dishes say comfort like pot pie, and using leftover turkey elevates this classic. Creamy filling bundled under a flaky, herb-infused crust creates a hearty, satisfying meal that’s sure to win family approval.

Ingredients:

For the filling:

  • 3 cups cooked turkey, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup milk or cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme

For the crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 3-5 tablespoons ice water
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary or thyme (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a saucepan, melt butter and sauté onions and garlic until softened.
  3. Stir in flour and cook for 2 minutes to form a roux.
  4. Gradually whisk in broth and milk, simmering until thickened.
  5. Add cooked turkey, peas and carrots, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  6. For the crust, mix flour, salt, and herbs. Cut in butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  7. Add ice water little by little until the dough clumps together. Chill for 15 minutes.
  8. Roll out dough and fit over a pie dish filled with turkey mixture.
  9. Cut slits in the crust for steam to escape.
  10. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until crust is golden brown.

This homemade touch turns your turkey into a heartwarming stand-alone dinner with elegance and rustic appeal.

4. Turkey and Sweet Potato Enchiladas

Enchiladas stuffed with turkey and sweet potato offer an exciting way to enjoy leftovers with a Southwestern flair. The combination strikes the perfect balance between smoky, savory, and slightly sweet, coated in a vibrant red enchilada sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 2 cups cooked sweet potatoes, mashed or cubed
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce
  • 8 corn or flour tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro and sliced avocado for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat oil and sauté onions with cumin and smoked paprika.
  3. Combine turkey, sweet potatoes, onion mixture, and half the cheese in a bowl.
  4. Spread a small amount of enchilada sauce in a baking dish.
  5. Fill each tortilla with the turkey mixture, roll tightly and place seam side down in the dish.
  6. Pour remaining enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas and sprinkle with the remaining cheese.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes until bubbly and cheese is melted.
  8. Garnish with cilantro and avocado slices before serving.

You’ll find these enchiladas both comforting and refreshingly different, making them a new favorite way to prepare turkey.

5. Asian-Inspired Turkey Stir-Fry

Leftover turkey can quickly be transformed into a flavorful Asian stir-fry packed with colorful veggies and a tangy, savory sauce. This recipe is a fresh and healthy way to enjoy turkey on a busy weeknight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups turkey, sliced thin
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Cooked rice or noodles, to serve
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a wok or large skillet, heat vegetable oil on high.
  2. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  3. Toss in bell peppers, snap peas, and broccoli; stir-fry until just tender.
  4. Add turkey slices and cook until heated through.
  5. Stir in soy sauce, hoisin, and sesame oil.
  6. Remove from heat and garnish with green onions and sesame seeds.
  7. Serve immediately over rice or noodles.

This quick stir-fry offers a welcome burst of brightness and variety after holiday heaviness and shows how versatile turkey can be.

6. Turkey and Spinach Stuffed Shells

For a make-ahead crowd-pleaser, stuffed pasta shells filled with turkey, spinach, and a blend of cheeses bring the holiday flavors together in a hearty Italian dish.

Ingredients:

  • 20 jumbo pasta shells, cooked al dente
  • 2 cups cooked turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 egg
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian herbs to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix turkey, ricotta, mozzarella, Parmesan, spinach, egg, salt, pepper, and herbs in a bowl.
  3. Spread half the marinara sauce in a baking dish.
  4. Stuff cooked shells with the turkey mixture and arrange them in the dish.
  5. Top with remaining sauce and sprinkle with extra mozzarella.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10 minutes to brown cheese.
  7. Let cool slightly before serving.

These stuffed shells elegantly combine creamy and savory flavors that make turkey second helpings eagerly awaited.

Pro Tips for Using Leftover Turkey

  • Moisture matters: Turkey can dry out quickly when reheated. Incorporate creamy sauces, broths, or moisture-rich ingredients to maintain juicy bites.
  • Size is everything: Slice or shred leftover turkey evenly so it heats uniformly and blends well into your new dish.
  • Spice it up: Introducing bold spices or different cuisines can completely transform the flavor profile of your turkey, keeping meals exciting.
  • Freeze for later: Turkey freezes well. Portion and freeze leftovers to expand your recipe options later in the month.

Leftover turkey is a treasure trove of potential that goes far beyond typical sandwiches. Whip up any one of these recipes, and you’ll never dread leftover turkey again. The creativity, warmth, and bold flavor combinations showcased here will impress your family and make each meal feel special.

So next time you face a fridge full of turkey, embrace the challenge with these inspired dishes — your taste buds (and your loved ones) will thank you.
