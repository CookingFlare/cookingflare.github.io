Low-Carb Asian-Inspired Recipes You'll Love


If you’re on a low-carb journey and are craving bold, flavorful dishes, you’re in luck! Asian cuisine is filled with aromatic spices, fresh ingredients, and mouthwatering flavors, making it a fantastic option for anyone looking to reduce their carb intake while still enjoying delicious meals. Whether you’re new to low-carb eating or a seasoned pro, these Asian-inspired recipes will satisfy your taste buds without breaking your dietary goals.

In this blog post, we’ve gathered a collection of low-carb recipes that draw from various Asian cooking traditions, including Chinese, Japanese, Thai, and Korean. These dishes are not only healthy but also easy to prepare and full of flavor. From savory stir-fries to refreshing salads and satisfying soups, there’s something here for everyone.

1. Keto-Friendly Thai Basil Chicken (Pad Krapow Gai)

One of Thailand’s most beloved street foods, Pad Krapow Gai is typically made with ground chicken, Thai basil, garlic, and chili peppers, served over a bed of rice. To make this dish low-carb, we’ll skip the rice and replace it with cauliflower rice, a fantastic low-carb alternative.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground chicken
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, finely chopped (adjust based on spice preference)
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon low-sugar sweetener (e.g., monk fruit or erythritol)
  • 1/2 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 1 cup cauliflower rice (optional, but recommended)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic and chilies, sautéing until fragrant (about 1-2 minutes).
  3. Add the ground chicken and cook until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce, soy sauce, and sweetener. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the Thai basil leaves and toss until they wilt and release their fragrance.
  6. Serve the chicken mixture over a bed of cauliflower rice for a low-carb meal that’s packed with flavor.

Why You’ll Love It: The combination of aromatic basil, garlic, and chilies creates a balance of savory, spicy, and slightly sweet flavors that are incredibly satisfying. This dish is not only low in carbs but also incredibly rich in protein, making it a perfect keto-friendly option.

2. Japanese Zucchini Noodles with Miso Peanut Sauce

This recipe is a creative, low-carb take on traditional Japanese soba noodles. Using zucchini noodles (or “zoodles”) instead of wheat-based noodles, you can enjoy the comforting texture of noodles without the carbs. The miso peanut sauce adds a creamy, savory punch that will leave you coming back for more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter (unsweetened)
  • 1 tablespoon white miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili paste or sriracha (optional, for a bit of heat)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the peanut butter, miso paste, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and chili paste. Whisk until smooth.
  3. In a large pan, heat the zucchini noodles over medium heat for 2-3 minutes, just until they soften slightly (don’t overcook to avoid sogginess).
  4. Pour the miso peanut sauce over the zucchini noodles and toss to coat.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions before serving.

Why You’ll Love It: This dish is creamy, rich, and full of umami from the miso and peanut butter. It’s the perfect low-carb alternative for those who love noodle-based dishes but are looking to cut out the carbs.

3. Korean Beef Lettuce Wraps (Bulgogi)

Bulgogi, a classic Korean dish, is typically made with marinated beef grilled or stir-fried. In this recipe, we’ll serve the flavorful beef in lettuce wraps instead of rice or bread, making it a refreshing and low-carb option that’s still full of savory goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb thinly sliced beef (sirloin or ribeye works well)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon low-sugar sweetener
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 bunch of butter lettuce or romaine lettuce (for wraps)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, sweetener, garlic, ginger, and black pepper. Stir until well-mixed.
  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade, tossing to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 2 hours for more flavor).
  3. Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated beef and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef is cooked through.
  4. Serve the cooked beef in lettuce wraps and sprinkle with sesame seeds.

Why You’ll Love It: These lettuce wraps are a fun and healthy alternative to traditional tacos or wraps. The savory-sweet marinade and tender beef make each bite satisfying without the carbs.

4. Chinese Broccoli Beef Stir-Fry (Low-Carb Version)

A popular Chinese takeout dish, broccoli beef stir-fry is typically served with rice or noodles. In this low-carb version, we’ll skip the carbs and focus on the combination of tender beef and crisp broccoli, all cooked in a flavorful, garlicky sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb flank steak, thinly sliced against the grain
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil or avocado oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional for extra flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water or low-sodium beef broth

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced beef and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring often, until browned.
  4. Add the broccoli, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chili flakes. Stir to combine.
  5. Add the water or beef broth to create a sauce and cook for another 3-5 minutes until the broccoli is tender and the sauce thickens.

Why You’ll Love It: This stir-fry is full of savory flavors and crunch, making it a satisfying low-carb meal. It’s quick, easy, and great for meal prepping.

5. Thai Coconut Soup (Tom Kha Gai)

This creamy, tangy Thai coconut soup is full of bright flavors from lemongrass, lime leaves, and galangal, with tender chicken simmered in coconut milk. It’s a perfect low-carb comfort food that will warm you up without the carbs.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced thinly
  • 2 cans (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, smashed
  • 4-5 lime leaves
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon low-sugar sweetener (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the coconut milk and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add the lemongrass, lime leaves, chilies, and chicken breasts. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  3. Stir in the fish sauce, lime juice, and sweetener. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

Why You’ll Love It: This soup has a rich, creamy base with a burst of freshness from the lime and herbs. It’s incredibly satisfying and the perfect dish to enjoy as a warm, low-carb appetizer or main course.

6. Vegetable Stir-Fry with Tofu (Low-Carb Version)

Stir-fries are a staple in Asian cuisine, and this vegetable stir-fry with tofu is a great option for a low-carb, plant-based meal. Packed with colorful vegetables and protein-rich tofu, this dish is as nutritious as it is flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili paste (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro or green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Press the tofu to remove excess moisture, then cut it into cubes.
  2. Heat the sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the tofu cubes and cook for 5-6 minutes until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Add the vegetables to the same pan and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  5. Return the tofu to the pan and pour in the soy sauce, rice vinegar, and chili paste. Stir well to coat.
  6. Serve the stir-fry hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or green onions.

Why You’ll Love It: This stir-fry is vibrant, full of texture, and packed with healthy veggies and protein. It’s a great low-carb, plant-based option that’s perfect for vegetarians or anyone looking to reduce meat consumption.

Conclusion

Asian cuisine offers a rich tapestry of flavors, textures, and cooking techniques that are perfect for creating low-carb meals that don’t sacrifice taste. Whether you’re in the mood for a zesty stir-fry, a refreshing salad, or a comforting soup, these low-carb Asian-inspired recipes will keep your meals exciting, satisfying, and aligned with your health goals. These dishes are quick to prepare, full of fresh ingredients, and packed with the bold flavors that make Asian cuisine so irresistible.

So, the next time you’re craving something flavorful yet low-carb, turn to these recipes. Not only will you be treating yourself to a delicious meal, but you’ll also be nourishing your body with nutritious, wholesome ingredients. Happy cooking!
