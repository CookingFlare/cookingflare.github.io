Blackberry Custard Pie: A Summertime Sensation


Blackberry custard pie, a classic dessert that never goes out of style. This sweet treat is a staple of summertime gatherings, and for good reason. The combination of tart blackberries and creamy custard is a match made in heaven, and the aroma that fills the air as it bakes is simply divine.

To make this pie, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen blackberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 pie crust (homemade or store-bought)

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the blackberries, granulated sugar, flour, and salt. Mix until the blackberries are evenly coated with the dry ingredients. Add the melted butter and mix until the berries are well coated.

In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, and vanilla extract until well combined. Pour the egg mixture over the blackberry mixture and stir until everything is well combined.

Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the blackberry mixture and spread it out evenly.

Bake the pie for 40-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. You may need to cover the edges of the crust with foil to prevent them from burning.

Once the pie is baked, let it cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before serving. You can serve it warm or at room temperature, depending on your preference.

This blackberry custard pie is perfect for summer gatherings, picnics, or potlucks. It’s a crowd-pleaser, and it’s sure to be a hit with your friends and family. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the sweet taste of summer.

There are a few secrets to making this pie truly special. First, make sure to use fresh or frozen blackberries, as they will give the pie a more intense flavor and texture. Second, don’t overmix the filling, as this can cause the blackberries to break down and the pie to become too dense. Finally, make sure to let the pie cool completely before serving, as this will allow the flavors to meld together and the filling to set properly.

If you want to add a little something extra to your blackberry custard pie, you can try one of these variations:

  • Add a splash of lemon juice to the filling to give it a bit of brightness and balance out the sweetness.
  • Use a combination of blackberries and other berries, such as raspberries or blueberries, to create a unique flavor profile.
  • Add a layer of whipped cream or meringue on top of the pie before baking to give it a decorative touch.

These are just a few ideas, but feel free to experiment and come up with your own unique variations.
