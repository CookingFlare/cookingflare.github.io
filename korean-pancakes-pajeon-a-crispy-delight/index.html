Korean Pancakes (Pajeon): A Crispy Delight


Few dishes capture the essence of Korean comfort food quite like Pajeon, a savory pancake that boasts incredible flavor, texture, and versatility. Whether enjoyed as a snack, an appetizer, or as a main dish, this crispy delight has earned a special place in Korean cuisine and in the hearts of food enthusiasts around the world.

What Is Pajeon?

Pajeon (파전) literally translates to “scallion pancake,” with “pa” meaning scallions and “jeon” referring to foods that are pan-fried or battered. Unlike the sweeter pancakes more common in Western cuisines, Pajeon is savory, packed with fresh ingredients, and often enjoyed alongside an array of dipping sauces and accompaniments.

Its defining characteristics are the crisp, golden exterior and the tender, flavorful interior. The pancakes can include just about any fresh ingredients you have on hand, with green onions being the star of the show. Other popular additions are seafood, kimchi, or thinly sliced vegetables, which make it endlessly customizable.

The History Behind Pajeon

Pajeon has long been a staple of Korean cuisine, with roots dating back to the Joseon Dynasty (1392–1897). Historically, various types of “jeon” were prepared as offerings at ancestral rites or served during significant celebrations. Over time, Pajeon evolved from a ceremonial dish to an everyday comfort food enjoyed at street markets, homes, and restaurants.

One fun cultural tidbit: Pajeon is often associated with rainy days in Korea. The sound of rain hitting pavement apparently reminds people of the sizzling sound of frying jeon, leading to a popular tradition of enjoying pancakes with a glass of makgeolli (a traditional Korean rice wine) on gloomy days.

Why Pajeon Is Loved Around the World

So what makes Pajeon stand out? It’s not just the simplicity—it’s the combination of crisp edges, chewy textures, and bursts of bold flavors in every bite. The intermingling of the herbs and vegetables with savory seasonings creates a deeply satisfying dish that appeals to a global palate.

Moreover, Pajeon is incredibly adaptable. Whether you’re following a vegan or gluten-free diet or looking to experiment with seasonal produce, it’s an easy recipe that can be tailored to suit your preferences. Its universal appeal is bolstered by the accessibility of key ingredients and its straightforward preparation process.

Pajeon is also a social dish, often cooked communally and shared in Korean households. It’s the perfect food to bring people together over a hot skillet and some laughter, making it as much about the experience as it is the flavor.

How To Make Korean Scallion Pancakes: Essential Tips and Techniques

While Pajeon may seem like a simple dish, achieving the perfect crispy texture with just the right thickness requires mastering a few basic techniques. Below, we’ll walk you through helpful tips to make your Pajeon extraordinary.

Choosing the Right Ingredients

The foundation of any great Pajeon lies in the batter and the fresh fillings. Korean pancakes typically consist of a simple batter made from flour, water, and sometimes an egg. For added authenticity, panjeon flour mix, which has seasonings blended in, is available at many Asian grocery stores.

The key ingredient here is scallions. Thinly sliced scallions or green onions provide the signature flavor of Pajeon. Seafood lovers often add squid, shrimp, or oysters for an umami boost, while vegetarians can stick with zucchini, carrots, or mushrooms.

Secret to the Crunch: Frying Technique

Achieving that irresistible crunch is all about the frying method. Hot vegetable oil is essential for uniformly crispy edges, while a shallow pan ensures the pancake cooks evenly. Keep these important steps in mind:

  1. Hot Pan, Cold Batter: A hot pan means instant, satisfying sizzle that forms the golden-brown crust. The batter should also be chilled, which allows for a cohesive texture.
  2. Spread Wisely: Spread the batter evenly across the pan, allowing just enough room for scallions and other fillings to peek out.
  3. Flip with Confidence: Once the edges turn golden brown, loosen the entire pancake with a spatula and flip swiftly to lock in crispness on both sides.

Step-by-Step Recipe for Classic Pajeon

Here’s how you can master the art of Korean scallion pancakes at home:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rice flour (optional for added crispiness)
  • 1 1/4 cups cold water
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1 bunch of scallions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup seafood mix (shrimp or squid, optional)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Salt (to taste)

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey or sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon chopped scallions
  • Optional: A pinch of red chili flakes for heat

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter
    In a large mixing bowl, whisk together all-purpose flour, rice flour (if using), and cold water until there are no lumps. Incorporate the egg for added fluffiness. Season with a pinch of salt to taste.

  2. Prep the Ingredients
    Slice scallions into 2-inch pieces. If adding seafood, ensure it’s cleaned and pat dried to avoid excess moisture.

  3. Heat the Pan
    Place a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat and add a generous layer of vegetable oil. Heat until shimmering.

  4. Cook the Pajeon
    Pour a ladleful of batter into the pan, spreading it evenly. Scatter scallions and any additional ingredients over the batter. Use another small amount of batter to lightly cover the fillings.

  5. Fry and Flip
    Allow the pancake to cook for 4-5 minutes or until the edges turn crunchy and golden brown. Carefully loosen with a spatula and flip the pancake to cook the other side for another 3-4 minutes.

  6. Drain Excess Oil
    Transfer the pancake to a cooling rack or plate lined with paper towels to remove excess oil.

  7. Make the Dipping Sauce
    Mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, and scallions in a small bowl. Serve alongside the freshly cooked Pajeon for dipping.

  8. Serve and Enjoy
    Cut the pancake into bite-sized pieces and serve warm. Complement the dish with a glass of makgeolli or your favorite drink for an authentic Korean experience.

Creative Variations of Pajeon You Need To Try

Once you’ve mastered the scallion pancake, why not mix things up? Here are a few variations to inspire your next Pajeon adventure.

Kimchi Pajeon

For a spicy twist, incorporate chopped kimchi into the batter. The tangy, peppery flavor of kimchi pairs beautifully with the crisp pancake.

Seafood Pajeon

Add shrimp, squid, oysters, or even fish slices for a seafood-laden version that’s perfect for special occasions.

Vegetarian Pajeon

Opt for seasonal veggies like zucchini, carrots, mushrooms, or bell peppers. The vibrant colors and mixed textures make this healthy and delicious.

Bulgogi Pajeon

Top the batter with thinly sliced beef marinated in bulgogi sauce to create a meaty, flavorful variation.

Final Tips for Making Pajeon Like a Pro

  1. Use Cast Iron Pans for Crunch: A cast-iron pan retains heat evenly, ensuring the perfect crunch level.
  2. Don’t Overmix the Batter: Overmixing leads to dense pancakes. Aim for a lump-free yet airy consistency.
  3. Pair It with Side Dishes: Pajeon tastes even better when served alongside Korean classics like kimchi or pickled radishes.

Bringing Korean Pancakes to Your Table

Pajeon isn’t just a dish—it’s an experience. From the sizzling sound of the batter hitting the hot oil to the first crispy bite, every moment is captivating. Whether you’re hosting friends, cooking for your family, or treating yourself to a solo culinary project, Pajeon is guaranteed to bring joy to your table.

Give this easy recipe a try, experiment with different variations, and immerse yourself in the flavors of Korea. Because once you taste Pajeon, you’ll understand why it has become a beloved dish worldwide.
