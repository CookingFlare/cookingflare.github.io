Wholesome and Filling: Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Peppers

Are you looking for a delicious and nutritious meal that is both gluten-free and filling? Look no further than these wholesome Quinoa Stuffed Peppers! Packed with flavor and loaded with wholesome ingredients, this recipe is perfect for those following a gluten-free diet or anyone who wants to enjoy a hearty and satisfying meal. Ingredients 4 large bell peppers (any color) 1 cup quinoa 2 cups vegetable broth 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 onion, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 zucchini, diced 1 cup mushrooms, sliced 1 cup spinach, chopped 1 can diced tomatoes, drained 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1 teaspoon dried basil Salt and pepper to taste Grated cheese (optional, for topping) Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place the peppers in a baking dish, standing upright. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness. In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. While the quinoa is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant and translucent. Add the zucchini, mushrooms, and spinach to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender and any excess moisture has evaporated. Stir in the diced tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Remove the skillet from the heat and add the cooked quinoa. Mix well to combine all the ingredients. Spoon the quinoa mixture into the bell peppers, filling them to the top. If desired, sprinkle grated cheese on top of each pepper. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is golden and bubbly. Remove from the oven and let the stuffed peppers cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy! Hearty Gluten-Free Lentil Soup: A Nourishing Bowl of Goodness

Are you craving a warm and comforting bowl of soup that is not only delicious but also gluten-free? Look no further! This Hearty Gluten-Free Lentil Soup is packed with nourishing ingredients and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re following a gluten-free diet or simply looking for a healthy and satisfying meal, this recipe is sure to satisfy your taste buds and keep you feeling satisfied. Ingredients 1 cup dried lentils (any variety) 1 onion, diced 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 carrots, diced 2 celery stalks, diced 1 medium potato, peeled and diced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 can diced tomatoes 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon turmeric Salt and pepper to taste Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish) Instructions Rinse the lentils under cold water and remove any debris. Set aside. In a large pot, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent. Add the diced carrots, celery, and potato to the pot. Cook for about 5 minutes, until the vegetables begin to soften. Add the rinsed lentils to the pot, followed by the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes. Stir well to combine. Season the soup with cumin, paprika, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Give it a good stir to distribute the flavors evenly. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender and the flavors have melded together. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. If the soup is too thick, you can add more vegetable broth or water to achieve your desired consistency. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley. Serve hot and enjoy!
