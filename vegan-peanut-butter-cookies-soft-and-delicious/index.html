Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies: Soft and Delicious


If you’re looking for a dessert that’s both simple and indulgent, vegan peanut butter cookies are the perfect treat. Not only are these cookies easy to prepare, but they are also rich in flavor, soft in texture, and completely plant-based. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or simply looking to cut back on dairy and eggs, this recipe is a must-try.

Peanut butter cookies are a classic favorite, and turning them into a vegan version doesn’t compromise on taste or texture. With just a few ingredients, you can make a batch of these cookies that are soft, chewy, and satisfy that peanut butter craving without any animal products.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make vegan peanut butter cookies, you won’t need to hunt down any obscure ingredients. Most of the items required are kitchen staples you probably already have in your pantry. Here’s a quick rundown of what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup peanut butter: Use a natural peanut butter, ideally one with just peanuts and salt for the cleanest taste. If you prefer a sweeter cookie, creamy peanut butter works well too.
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar or brown sugar: These sugars offer a deep sweetness and a slightly molasses-like flavor, perfect for pairing with peanut butter.
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup: A natural sweetener that also adds a hint of caramelized flavor.
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy milk: Almond milk, oat milk, or soy milk work best, but feel free to use what you have on hand.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: Adds a layer of warmth and depth to the overall flavor of the cookies.
  • 1 cup rolled oats: For texture and chewiness.
  • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour or all-purpose flour: Whole wheat flour gives the cookies a hearty feel, but regular flour can be used if you prefer a lighter texture.
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda: This helps the cookies rise and gives them a light, airy texture.
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt: Enhances the flavor of the peanut butter and balances the sweetness.

Optional:

  • 1/4 cup dark chocolate chips: For an extra chocolatey twist, add some dairy-free chocolate chips into the dough.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies

Step 1: Prepare Your Oven and Baking Sheets

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper to prevent the cookies from sticking and to make cleanup easier. If you don’t have parchment paper, lightly grease the baking sheet with a little coconut oil or vegetable oil.

Step 2: Mix the Wet Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the peanut butter, maple syrup, non-dairy milk, and vanilla extract. Stir until the ingredients are fully combined, and the mixture is smooth. This is where the peanut butter’s creamy texture comes into play, so make sure to mix well to achieve a nice, smooth batter.

Step 3: Add the Dry Ingredients

Next, add the rolled oats, flour, baking soda, and salt to the wet mixture. Stir everything together until a thick dough forms. You might need to use your hands to knead it slightly, but this dough should not be too sticky. If it’s too wet, add a tablespoon of extra flour. Conversely, if the dough is too dry, add a small splash of non-dairy milk until it reaches the desired consistency.

Step 4: Form the Cookie Dough

Once the dough is ready, it’s time to shape the cookies. Using a spoon, scoop out small portions of the dough and roll them into balls, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. Place each ball on the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 2 inches of space between each one.

Step 5: Flatten the Cookies

To get the signature peanut butter cookie crisscross pattern, use a fork to gently press down on each dough ball. First, press down vertically, then turn the fork 90 degrees and press again, creating a crisscross effect. This also helps the cookies cook evenly.

Step 6: Bake the Cookies

Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for about 8-10 minutes. The cookies should be golden brown around the edges, and the centers will look slightly soft but firm up as they cool. Keep a close eye on them, as they can quickly go from golden to overbaked if left too long.

Step 7: Let the Cookies Cool

Once your cookies are done, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. Afterward, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. This step ensures that the cookies set properly and don’t break apart when you try to move them.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies

  1. Use Natural Peanut Butter: If you want to keep these cookies as healthy as possible, opt for a natural peanut butter with just peanuts and salt. Many commercial peanut butters contain added sugar and preservatives, which can affect the flavor of your cookies.

  2. Experiment with Sweeteners: While maple syrup and coconut sugar are great natural sweeteners, you can experiment with alternatives like agave syrup or date sugar for different flavor profiles.

  3. Keep the Dough Cool: If you find that the dough is too soft or sticky to handle, place it in the refrigerator for 10-15 minutes before shaping the cookies. This will make it easier to roll and shape the dough.

  4. Add-ins for Extra Flavor: You can make these cookies your own by adding your favorite mix-ins. Try adding dairy-free chocolate chips, chopped nuts, or dried fruit. Just make sure to not overwhelm the dough with too many ingredients, or the texture might change.

  5. Storage: These vegan peanut butter cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. If you want to keep them for longer, freeze them for up to 3 months. Just be sure to let them cool completely before storing.

Health Benefits of Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies

Peanut butter is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here are some of the benefits of incorporating it into your cookie recipe:

  • Protein Power: Peanut butter is a great source of plant-based protein, which helps in muscle repair and overall body function. It’s a fantastic addition to a vegan diet.

  • Healthy Fats: The healthy fats in peanut butter help to support brain function and heart health. Unlike processed fats, the fats in peanut butter come with beneficial nutrients like vitamins E and K.

  • Fiber: Oats provide fiber, which supports digestion and helps maintain a feeling of fullness. This makes these cookies a better snack option compared to many other sweet treats.

  • No Dairy: Since this recipe is completely dairy-free, it’s suitable for those who are lactose intolerant or have dairy allergies.

Why These Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies Are the Best

Vegan peanut butter cookies are one of the easiest and most satisfying plant-based treats you can make. The rich, nutty flavor of peanut butter pairs perfectly with the natural sweetness of maple syrup and coconut sugar. These cookies are soft and chewy, with the perfect balance of sweetness and saltiness.

They’re perfect for every occasion, from afternoon tea to an after-dinner dessert. Plus, they come together quickly with minimal ingredients, making them an excellent choice when you’re craving something homemade but don’t want to spend a lot of time in the kitchen.

Whether you’re baking for yourself, sharing with friends, or bringing a treat to a gathering, these cookies are guaranteed to be a hit. They’re also easy to customize, so feel free to make them your own with different mix-ins or toppings.

Conclusion

These vegan peanut butter cookies are the ideal combination of indulgent and nutritious. Made with simple, wholesome ingredients, they’re a treat you can feel good about eating. Whether you’re vegan or just trying to enjoy a more plant-based lifestyle, this recipe is one you’ll want to keep in your baking rotation. The soft, chewy texture and rich peanut butter flavor make these cookies a crowd-pleaser, and they’re just as delicious as their non-vegan counterparts.

Give this recipe a try, and enjoy a batch of these soft and delicious vegan peanut butter cookies that are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth!
