Preserving the Fall Feast: Recipes for Canning Cranberry Chutney


Cranberry chutney is a delightful condiment that perfectly captures the flavors of fall. With its tangy and sweet taste, it pairs well with a variety of dishes, from roasted turkey to cheese platters. But what if you could enjoy this delicious treat all year round? That’s where canning comes in. In this post, we’ll dive into the art of canning cranberry chutney, providing you with two mouthwatering recipes that will preserve the flavors of the fall feast for months to come.

Recipe 1: Classic Cranberry Chutney

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh cranberries
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup chopped red onion
  • 1 cup golden raisins
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the cranberries, sugar, vinegar, red onion, raisins, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt.
  2. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the chutney thickens and the cranberries burst, remove the saucepan from the heat.
  5. Carefully transfer the hot chutney into sterilized canning jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  6. Wipe the jar rims clean, seal the jars with lids and rings, and process them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  7. After processing, remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool completely on a wire rack.
  8. Once cooled, check the seals, label the jars, and store them in a cool, dark place for up to a year.

Recipe 2: Spiced Cranberry Chutney

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh cranberries
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon grated orange zest
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the cranberries, brown sugar, vinegar, red bell pepper, dried cranberries, red onion, lemon juice, orange zest, mustard seeds, cinnamon, allspice, and salt.
  2. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. As the chutney thickens and the cranberries soften, remove the saucepan from the heat.
  5. Ladle the hot chutney into sterilized canning jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  6. Use a clean knife or skewer to remove any air bubbles from the jars, then wipe the rims clean and seal them with lids and rings.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 15 minutes, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  8. Once processed, carefully remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool on a towel-lined surface.
  9. Once cooled, check the seals, label the jars, and store them in a cool, dark place for up to a year.

Enjoy the Flavors of Fall All Year Round

Canning cranberry chutney is not only a practical way to preserve the fall feast, but it’s also an opportunity to savor the vibrant flavors of cranberries, spices, and other seasonal ingredients long after the harvest season ends. With these two delicious recipes, you can elevate your meals, impress your guests, and bring a taste of autumn to any occasion. So, grab your canning jars, embrace the art of preservation, and enjoy the fruits of your labor throughout the year. Happy canning!

*Note: When canning, always follow proper safety guidelines and ensure your equipment is clean and in good condition.
