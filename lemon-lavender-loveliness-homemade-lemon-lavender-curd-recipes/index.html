Lemon Lavender Loveliness: Homemade Lemon Lavender Curd Recipes


Lemon and lavender, two ingredients that, when combined, create a magical sensory experience, a burst of flavor that leaves your taste buds desiring more. Homemade lemon lavender curd is a delightful and unique recipe that’s perfect for culinary enthusiasts wanting to try something different and Instagram-worthy.

Lavender, a calming herb often associated with spas and relaxation, adds an aromatic touch to traditional lemon curd. With a mellow sweetness and floral notes to balance the tartness of lemons, it’s a versatile condiment that can be enjoyed in various ways:

  1. A spread on toast, benefiting from the deliciously smooth texture.
  2. Mixed into creams, frostings, and other baked goods, offering a creamy and fragrant addition.
  3. Drizzled over pancakes and waffles for an unforgettable breakfast experience.
  4. As a topping for desserts, complementing the sweetness of fruit and rich chocolate.

Preparing lemon lavender curd is both satisfying and simple. With only a handful of ingredients, you’ll have a jar full in no time. Let’s dive into the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 10-12 lemons)
  • 3/4 cup castor sugar
  • 2/3 cup unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon dried lavender
  • 1/4 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of kosher salt

Procedure

  1. Begin by preparing your lemons. You’ll want to strain the juice to rid any seeds and unwanted pulpy bits. A fine-mesh sieve works well for this.
2. In a medium-sized saucepan, combine lemon juice, sugar, and salt. Whisk until sugar is thoroughly dissolved.
  1. Add the cubed butter and toss in the lavender and vanilla extract. Cook over medium heat, stirring consistently to combine all ingredients smoothly.
4. As the mixture heats, it will begin to thicken. Bring the temperature to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low to maintain a slow simmer.
  1. Stir in lavender and butter until fully incorporated. Let it cook, stirring frequently to prevent sticking and burning, until the mixture has thickened sufficiently. This may take up to 10-12 minutes. When you see tracks on the back of a spoon, or it has reached a custard-like consistency, it’s ready.
6. Remove from heat, incorporate vanilla extract, and let the mixture cool slightly. The curd will thin as it cools, so let it cool completely before storage or further use.

7. Once cooled, pour or spoon the lemon lavender curd into a clean jar or container. Cover, and refrigerate. Homemade lemon lavender curd should keep in the fridge for up to two weeks.

## Tips for Success

1. Avoid using candle-grade lavender. Though it appears the same, it contains oils not suitable for consumption.
2. Select organic, edible lavender varieties to ensure a safe culinary experience.
3. Strain the lavender before incorporating it into the curd for a smoother, debris-free product.
4. For a true lavender boost, try growing your own lavender or seeking out a local, trusted grower.

Experience this delightful combination of flavors and elevate your culinary journey with lemon lavender curd. Whether you're a fan of lemon, lavender, or both, these homemade recipes will delight your taste buds and leave you craving more. Once you've tried it, you'll understand the lemon lavender loveliness, and you'll soon find yourself experimenting with other herbaceous additions. Bon appétit!
