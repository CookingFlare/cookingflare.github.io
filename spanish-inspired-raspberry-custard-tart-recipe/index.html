Spanish Inspired Raspberry Custard Tart Recipe


When it comes to desserts that capture the essence of summer, few can rival the delightful harmony of a Spanish-inspired raspberry custard tart. This recipe combines the creamy richness of custard with the tartness of fresh raspberries, all enveloped in a buttery pastry crust. Perfect for warm gatherings or as a sweet end to a dinner party, this tart will transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed landscapes of Spain.

Ingredients

For the Pastry Crust

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2-3 tablespoons cold water
  • A pinch of salt

For the Custard Filling

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • A pinch of salt

For the Raspberry Topping

  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Mint leaves (for garnish)

Equipment Needed

  • 9-inch tart pan
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Saucepan
  • Rubber spatula
  • Baking paper
  • Pie weights or dried beans

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pastry Crust

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, powdered sugar, and a pinch of salt until well combined.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the chilled, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, blend until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add the Egg Yolk: In a small bowl, whisk the egg yolk with 2 tablespoons of cold water. Add this to the flour mixture and mix until the dough begins to come together. If the dough is too dry, add an additional tablespoon of water.

  4. Chill the Dough: Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This helps the butter firm up, ensuring a flaky crust.

Step 2: Blind Bake the Crust

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Roll Out the Dough: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to fit your 9-inch tart pan. Gently press the dough into the pan, trimming any excess.

  3. Prepare for Baking: Line the crust with baking paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. This prevents the crust from puffing up during baking.

  4. Bake the Crust: Bake the crust in the preheated oven for about 15 minutes. Remove the weights and paper, then bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Remove from the oven and allow to cool completely.

Step 3: Make the Custard Filling

  1. Heat the Milk: In a medium saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it is steaming but not boiling. Remove from heat.

  2. Whisk the Egg Yolks: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon zest, and a pinch of salt until smooth.

  3. Temper the Egg Mixture: Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking continuously to prevent the eggs from cooking. Once combined, return the mixture to the saucepan.

  4. Cook the Custard: Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly with a rubber spatula, until it thickens and coats the back of the spatula (about 5-7 minutes). Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.

  5. Cool the Custard: Pour the custard filling into the cooled pastry crust. Smooth the top with a spatula. Allow it to cool at room temperature for about 30 minutes, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours to set.

Step 4: Prepare the Raspberry Topping

  1. Mix the Raspberries: In a small bowl, combine the fresh raspberries, granulated sugar, and lemon juice. Gently toss to coat the berries. Allow them to sit for about 15 minutes, which will release some of their juices.

Step 5: Assemble the Tart

  1. Top the Custard: Once the custard has set, remove the tart from the refrigerator. Carefully arrange the marinated raspberries on top of the custard, creating a beautiful design.

  2. Garnish: For an extra touch of elegance, garnish the tart with fresh mint leaves.

Serving Suggestions

This Spanish-inspired raspberry custard tart is best served chilled. Slice it into wedges and serve it on its own or complement it with a dollop of whipped cream for added indulgence. Pair it with a glass of sweet dessert wine or a refreshing sparkling beverage for the perfect ending to any meal.

Tips for Success

  • Fresh Ingredients: Use the freshest raspberries you can find for optimal flavor. If raspberries are out of season, consider substituting with other berries like strawberries or blueberries.

  • Chill Time: Allowing the custard to set in the refrigerator is crucial for a well-defined tart. Don’t rush this step!

  • Baking the Crust: Keep an eye on your crust while it’s baking. Oven temperatures can vary, so adjust the time as necessary to avoid burning.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to infuse the custard with other flavors. A splash of orange blossom water or a hint of almond extract can add a delightful twist.

Storage

The raspberry custard tart can be stored in the refrigerator, covered, for up to 3 days. However, for the best texture and taste, it’s recommended to consume it within the first couple of days.

Conclusion

This Spanish-inspired raspberry custard tart is a delectable dessert that combines rich flavors and beautiful presentation. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply indulging in a sweet treat, this tart is sure to impress. With its creamy custard and vibrant raspberry topping, it’s a true celebration of summer flavors. Enjoy creating this delightful dessert and sharing it with friends and family for a taste of Spain right at your table!
