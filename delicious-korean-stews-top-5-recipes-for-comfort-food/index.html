Delicious Korean Stews: Top 5 Recipes for Comfort Food


Korean cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and comforting dishes. Among the many incredible recipes in Korean cuisine, stews hold a special place. Known for their hearty ingredients and deeply satisfying flavors, these stews are perfect for any season but especially cherished during colder months. They offer the perfect balance of spicy, savory, and umami flavors, making them a go-to for a warm, comforting meal.

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, these five Korean stew recipes will help you recreate the heartwarming, authentic tastes of Korea right in your own home. Each stew brings its own unique flavor and history, so let’s dive into the wonderful world of Korean stews.

1. Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개) – Kimchi Stew

Kimchi jjigae is perhaps the most iconic of all Korean stews, a beloved comfort food that’s often eaten with rice. This stew is typically made with old, fermented kimchi, which gives it a tangy, spicy depth of flavor. It’s the ultimate in simple, satisfying meals that can be whipped up quickly and is packed with nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of well-fermented kimchi, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1/2 pound of pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 block of tofu, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 4 cups of water or anchovy stock (for more depth of flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Pork Belly: In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced pork belly and cook until it turns golden and crispy on the edges. This step will enhance the rich flavor of the stew.

  2. Add Onion and Garlic: Toss in the sliced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onion softens and becomes fragrant.

  3. Incorporate Kimchi: Add the chopped kimchi and continue to stir-fry for another 5 minutes. This helps the kimchi release its flavors into the stew.

  4. Simmer the Stew: Add the gochugaru, gochujang, soy sauce, and anchovy stock (or water). Stir everything to combine and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for about 10 minutes.

  5. Add Tofu and Final Simmer: Gently add the tofu cubes into the pot and let them cook for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the tofu to absorb the savory broth.

  6. Finish the Dish: Drizzle in the sesame oil, season with salt and pepper to taste, and garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot with steamed rice on the side.

Kimchi Jjigae is the perfect combination of spicy, savory, and tangy, and it’s a fantastic dish to use up any leftover kimchi. It’s incredibly easy to make and delivers a punch of flavor in every bite.

2. Banchan-style Doenjang Jjigae (된장찌개) – Soybean Paste Stew

Doenjang jjigae is a classic Korean stew made with doenjang, a thick and fermented soybean paste that has a strong, savory umami flavor. This stew is often considered the Korean equivalent of miso soup but with a richer, earthier taste. It is frequently enjoyed in Korean households as a staple, eaten daily or alongside various side dishes, known as banchan.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons of doenjang (fermented soybean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1/2 pound of beef (typically beef brisket or stew cuts), thinly sliced
  • 1 medium zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 block of tofu, cut into cubes
  • 4 cups of water or anchovy stock
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Stock: In a large pot, combine water (or anchovy stock) with the doenjang and gochujang. Stir until both pastes dissolve and form a smooth base.

  2. Cook the Beef: Add the sliced beef and minced garlic to the pot, then bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the beef to tenderize and the flavors to meld.

  3. Add Vegetables: Toss in the sliced zucchini, onion, and potato. Stir gently, and let the vegetables simmer in the broth until they become tender, around 10 minutes.

  4. Add Tofu and Final Seasoning: Add tofu cubes to the stew and let them cook for an additional 5 minutes. Season the stew with soy sauce, sesame oil, fish sauce (optional), salt, and pepper.

  5. Serve and Garnish: Once everything is cooked through and the stew has thickened slightly, garnish with fresh green onions. Serve alongside a bowl of hot steamed rice.

Doenjang Jjigae is perfect for anyone who enjoys deep, earthy flavors. It’s a great vegetarian dish (if you omit the beef) and a must-try for anyone who wants to explore the heart of Korean comfort food.

3. Galbitang (갈비탕) – Short Rib Soup

Galbitang is a nourishing, clear broth soup made with short ribs, a popular dish in Korea, especially during colder months. The long boiling process helps extract all the flavors from the meat and bones, creating a rich, clean broth. This dish is traditionally served with rice and kimchi on the side.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef short ribs (cut into pieces)
  • 10 cups of water
  • 4 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 1 small onion, peeled
  • 1 piece of Korean radish (mu), sliced into thin rounds
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped for garnish
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the Short Ribs: Start by blanching the short ribs in boiling water for about 5 minutes. This step helps remove impurities and keeps the broth clear. Drain the ribs and rinse them under cold water.

  2. Simmer the Broth: In a large pot, add the short ribs, water, garlic, and onion. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Simmer for at least 1.5 to 2 hours to extract the full flavor from the beef bones.

  3. Add Radish: Once the broth has developed a rich flavor, add the sliced radish and cook for another 20 minutes until the radish softens.

  4. Season the Soup: Add soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper to the broth. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

  5. Serve: Once everything is cooked through, serve the galbitang hot with a bowl of steamed rice and garnish with chopped green onions. For a complete meal, side dishes like kimchi are often served alongside.

Galbitang is perfect for those who love clean, comforting broths and tender beef. The soup’s simplicity allows the natural flavors to shine, making it an incredibly satisfying dish.

4. Chueotang (추어탕) – Loach Soup

Chueotang is a traditional Korean stew made with loach (a type of freshwater fish) and is considered a nutritious and healing food. This unique dish features a thick, earthy broth that’s both comforting and packed with flavor. Though it’s not as commonly made at home, it holds significant cultural importance.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of loach fish (fresh or frozen)
  • 5 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of soybean paste (doenjang)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of perilla leaves, chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Loach: In a pot, combine water and loach fish. Bring to a boil and simmer for about 30 minutes. Skim off any impurities that rise to the surface.

  2. Add the Seasonings: Once the broth is clear, add the soybean paste, gochugaru, and minced garlic. Stir until well combined and simmer for another 10 minutes.

  3. Add Vegetables: Add zucchini and perilla leaves (if using) and continue simmering for another 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  4. Final Touches: Drizzle in the sesame oil, season with salt and pepper, and adjust the flavors to your liking. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Chueotang is an acquired taste but is immensely nutritious, making it a beloved dish for those looking for something out of the ordinary in their Korean stew repertoire.

5. Sundubu Jjigae (순두부찌개) – Soft Tofu Stew

Sundubu jjigae is a soft tofu stew that’s a staple in Korean homes and restaurants alike. This dish is made with uncurdled tofu, which gives the stew a smooth, creamy texture. The combination of spicy, savory flavors with the mild tofu makes for a comforting meal perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of soft tofu (sundubu)
  • 1/2 pound of ground pork or beef
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (chili flakes)
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 3 cups of anchovy stock or water
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Meat and Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the ground pork or beef and cook until browned. Add the garlic and onion, and sauté until fragrant.

  2. Add the Seasonings: Stir in gochujang and gochugaru, mixing well to coat the meat and vegetables. Pour in the anchovy stock (or water), and bring it to a boil.

  3. Add Tofu: Gently add the soft tofu into the pot, breaking it up into smaller chunks. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to heat through and absorb the flavors.

  4. Finish the Stew: Crack an egg into the stew (if using) and let it cook for another 3-4 minutes until the egg is cooked to your preference.

  5. Serve: Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

Sundubu jjigae is perfect for those who enjoy a mild yet spicy stew with a velvety texture. It’s quick to make and provides an incredibly soothing, flavorful experience.

Conclusion

Korean stews are the epitome of comfort food, offering a variety of flavors, textures, and rich, satisfying broth that will warm you from the inside out. Whether you’re craving the tangy heat of Kimchi Jjigae, the earthy depth of Doenjang Jjigae, or the tender beef of Galbitang, there’s a stew for every taste and occasion. These dishes reflect the heart of Korean cooking, bringing people together over a shared meal, filled with bold, comforting flavors. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and start cooking these amazing stews. Enjoy the flavors of Korea in your own kitchen today!
