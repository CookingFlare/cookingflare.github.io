Cooking for Allergies: 12 Simple Recipes for the Whole Family


Navigating food allergies while preparing meals that everyone in the family can enjoy is no small feat. It requires thoughtful planning, creative substitutions, and a keen eye to ensure all ingredients are safe. But the good news is: eating delicious, allergy-friendly meals doesn’t mean compromising on taste or variety. In this guide, we’ll explore 12 simple and satisfying allergy-conscious recipes that cater to common allergen restrictions such as nuts, dairy, gluten, eggs, or soy. These recipes are designed to please the entire family, blending nutrition and flavor for a truly harmonious dining experience.

1. Dairy-Free Creamy Tomato Soup

A warm and comforting bowl of tomato soup doesn’t have to include cream. This dairy-free version uses coconut milk for richness and is perfect for anyone avoiding dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28 oz) can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant and softened.
  2. Add the canned tomatoes and vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Blend until smooth using an immersion blender or standard blender.
  4. Return to the pot, stir in coconut milk, and season with salt and pepper. Simmer for a few minutes before serving.

2. Egg-Free Pancakes

If eggs are a no-go in your household, don’t worry—you can still enjoy fluffy and delicious pancakes with this simple swap.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose or gluten-free flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 cup dairy-free milk (such as almond or oat milk)
  • 1 tablespoon melted coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and baking powder.
  2. Slowly add the dairy-free milk and melted coconut oil, stirring until just combined.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and pour small ladles of batter onto the pan. Flip once bubbles appear and cook until golden.

3. Gluten-Free Chicken Nuggets

Chicken nuggets are a family favorite, and this version keeps them crispy and gluten-free. Perfect for little ones with wheat allergies!

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ½ cup gluten-free breadcrumbs
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 egg or egg alternative (e.g., flax egg)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix gluten-free breadcrumbs with paprika and garlic powder in a bowl.
  3. Dip chicken pieces into the egg (or substitute), then coat with the breadcrumb mixture.
  4. Place nuggets on the baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes, turning once.

4. Soy-Free Teriyaki Stir-Fry

Soy-free teriyaki sauce pairs beautifully with fresh vegetables and rice for a fast and flavorful dinner.

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup coconut aminos (a soy sauce alternative)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Assorted veggies (e.g., broccoli, carrots, bell peppers)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Cooked rice

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together the coconut aminos, honey, and grated ginger in a small bowl.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a skillet and sauté the veggies until tender-crisp.
  3. Add the sauce and stir to coat. Serve over cooked rice.

5. Nut-Free Pesto Pasta

Traditional pesto often relies on nuts, but this version swaps them for seeds, maintaining all the vibrant flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil
  • ⅓ cup sunflower or pumpkin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove
  • ½ cup dairy-free Parmesan cheese or nutritional yeast
  • Pasta of choice (gluten-free as needed)

Instructions:

  1. Blend basil, seeds, olive oil, garlic, and cheese substitute in a food processor until smooth.
  2. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, toss it with the pesto, and serve immediately.

6. Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

These cookies are dairy-free, egg-free, and just as indulgent as traditional recipes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose or gluten-free flour
  • ½ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup dairy-free chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix all the ingredients in a bowl until a dough forms.
  3. Scoop dough onto the baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes.

7. Dairy-Free Mac and Cheese

Kids and adults alike will love this creamy, dairy-free twist on a classic comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta (gluten-free for allergy considerations)
  • 1 cup butternut squash puree
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric for color
  • Nutritional yeast (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions.
  2. In a large pan, warm the squash puree, coconut milk, and turmeric. Stir in nutritional yeast for a cheesier flavor and mix with the pasta.

8. Allergy-Friendly Tacos

Tacos are incredibly versatile and easy to customize for any allergy in your household.

Ingredients:

  • Corn or cassava tortillas
  • Protein of choice (e.g., ground turkey, beans, or tofu substitute)
  • Toppings: lettuce, tomatoes, dairy-free cheese

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the protein according to your preferences.
  2. Assemble the tacos with tortillas, protein, and toppings. Serve immediately.

9. Soy-Free Miso Soup

Miso soup without soy? It’s possible! Use chickpea miso to create this enjoyable starter.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 3 tablespoons chickpea miso
  • ½ cup cubed tofu alternative or cooked chickpeas
  • Wakame seaweed

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable broth in a saucepan.
  2. Stir in the chickpea miso until dissolved, then add tofu alternative and seaweed. Serve warm.

10. Nut-Free Granola

This easy granola recipe skips nuts but keeps all the crunch your family loves.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups oats (certified gluten-free, if needed)
  • ½ cup sunflower seeds
  • ½ cup shredded coconut
  • ¼ cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 300°F.
  2. Toss all the ingredients together, spread on a baking sheet, and bake for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.

11. Egg-Free Veggie Quiche

This quiche uses a chickpea flour base, making it egg-free and packed with plant-based protein.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chickpea flour
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., spinach, mushrooms)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
  2. Mix chickpea flour, water, turmeric, and salt, then stir in the veggies.
  3. Pour into a greased pie dish and bake for 30-35 minutes.

12. Dairy-Free Strawberry Smoothie

A refreshing and creamy smoothie, completely dairy-free and loaded with natural sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup frozen strawberries
  • 1 banana
  • 1 cup almond or oat milk
  • 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:

  1. Blend all ingredients until smooth and creamy. Pour into glasses and enjoy.

Whether it’s breakfast, lunch, dinner, or snack time, these allergy-friendly recipes prove the kitchen can remain a space for creativity and joy. Taking the time to prepare meals that accommodate allergies not only keeps your family safe but also encourages inclusivity at the table. By using these simple recipes as inspiration, you’ll discover that allergy-friendly cooking can be as delicious as it is nourishing!
