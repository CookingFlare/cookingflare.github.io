Leftover Sweet Potato Turned into a Sweet Potato Gnocchi


If you’re looking for a creative way to use up your leftover sweet potatoes, I’ve got just the recipe for you – Sweet Potato Gnocchi! This pillowy pasta alternative is a delightful twist on traditional gnocchi and a perfect way to make the most of those extra sweet potatoes sitting in your pantry. Plus, it’s a great way to add some color and nutrition to your plate. So let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups leftover sweet potatoes, mashed
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, flour, beaten egg, salt, and ground nutmeg. Mix well until a soft dough forms.

  2. Dust a clean surface with flour and transfer the dough onto it. Knead the dough gently for a few minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  3. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a long rope-like shape, about 1/2 inch in diameter.

  4. Cut the ropes into bite-sized pieces, about 1 inch long. You can leave them as is or give them a traditional gnocchi shape by pressing each piece against the tines of a fork, creating ridges.

  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the gnocchi into the boiling water and cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until they float to the surface. This indicates that they are cooked through.

  6. Using a slotted spoon, remove the cooked gnocchi from the water and transfer them to a plate or serving dish.

  7. At this point, you can choose to enjoy your Sweet Potato Gnocchi as is or take it a step further and add your favorite sauce. A simple brown butter and sage sauce or a creamy garlic Parmesan sauce would complement the flavors beautifully.

  8. Serve the Sweet Potato Gnocchi hot and garnish with some fresh herbs or grated Parmesan cheese for an extra touch of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • If your leftover sweet potatoes are on the drier side, you may need to add a little more flour to the dough to achieve the right consistency. Just add a tablespoon at a time until the dough comes together.

  • Sweet Potato Gnocchi can be frozen for later use. After shaping the gnocchi, place them on a baking sheet and freeze until firm. Then, transfer them to a freezer bag or container. Cook them directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

  • Get creative with your sauces! Sweet Potato Gnocchi pairs well with a variety of flavors. Try a spicy tomato sauce, a creamy pesto, or a tangy lemon and herb sauce to switch things up.

  • For added texture and flavor, you can lightly pan-fry the cooked gnocchi in some olive oil until golden and crispy. It adds a delightful crunch to each bite.

So there you have it – a delicious and unique way to use up those leftover sweet potatoes. Sweet Potato Gnocchi is a versatile dish that will impress your taste buds and add a pop of color to your dinner table. Give it a try and let me know how it turns out! Happy cooking!
