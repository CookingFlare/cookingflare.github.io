Stout Suppers: Hearty Dishes to Pair with Dark Beers


When the days grow shorter and the air turns crisp, nothing quite warms the heart like a hearty meal paired with a rich, dark stout. These robust beers, with their deep flavors of roasted malt, chocolate, and coffee, offer a unique and delightful complement to a variety of dishes. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply enjoying a comforting dinner at home, stout suppers are the perfect way to embrace the season. Let’s explore some delectable recipes that highlight the rich flavors of stout and provide a satisfying dining experience.

Understanding Stout Beer

Before diving into the recipes, it’s essential to understand what stout beer is and why it pairs so well with certain hearty dishes. Originally brewed in England, stouts are dark beers made using roasted malt or roasted barley, giving them their characteristic color and flavor. The most famous types of stouts include:

  • Dry Stout: Often associated with Guinness, this variety is smooth with a slight bitterness.
  • Sweet Stout: Known for its creaminess and sweetness, this stout often contains lactose.
  • Imperial Stout: A stronger version with higher alcohol content, rich flavors, and a complex profile.
  • Oatmeal Stout: Incorporating oats gives this stout a silky mouthfeel and additional depth.

The complex flavors of stout can enhance the taste of hearty dishes, creating a perfect harmony of flavors. Now, let’s discover some recipes that will elevate your stout supper experience.

1. Beef and Stout Stew

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 2 parsnips, sliced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup stout beer
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Brown the Beef: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and brown on all sides. Remove from the pot and set aside.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add the onion and garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent. Add the carrots and parsnips and sauté for another 5 minutes.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Return the beef to the pot. Stir in the beef broth, stout beer, tomato paste, thyme, bay leaves, salt, and pepper.

  4. Simmer: Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for 2 to 3 hours, or until the beef is tender.

  5. Serve: Remove bay leaves before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley and enjoy with a glass of stout.

2. Stout-Braised Short Ribs

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs beef short ribs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups stout beer
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons dried rosemary
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • Fresh thyme, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prep the Ribs: Season the short ribs with salt and pepper. In a large Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the ribs on all sides until browned. Remove and set aside.

  2. Cook Vegetables: In the same pot, add onion, carrots, and garlic. Sauté until the onions are softened.

  3. Deglaze: Pour in the stout beer, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Bring to a simmer.

  4. Add Remaining Ingredients: Return the short ribs to the pot. Add beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, rosemary, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.

  5. Braise: Cover and let it simmer for 2 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.

  6. Serve: Plate the short ribs and spoon the sauce over them. Garnish with fresh thyme and serve with mashed potatoes or crusty bread.

3. Stout and Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced (such as cremini or shiitake)
  • 1 cup stout beer
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Sauté Base: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, cooking until softened. Stir in the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture.

  2. Toast Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the skillet, stirring to coat it in the oil. Cook for about 2 minutes, until the rice is slightly translucent.

  3. Deglaze with Stout: Pour in the stout beer, stirring until it’s mostly absorbed by the rice.

  4. Add Broth Gradually: Begin adding the warmed vegetable broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until the liquid is absorbed before adding more. Continue this process for about 20 minutes or until the rice is creamy and al dente.

  5. Finish Risotto: Remove from heat and stir in the Parmesan cheese and butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Serve: Spoon the risotto into bowls, garnish with fresh parsley, and enjoy with a glass of stout.

4. Stout Chocolate Cake

Ingredients

  • 1 cup stout beer
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  3. Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together the stout, eggs, buttermilk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.

  4. Combine Mixtures: Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. If desired, fold in chocolate chips.

  5. Bake: Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Cool and Frost: Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Frost with your favorite chocolate frosting and serve.

5. Stout BBQ Pulled Pork

Ingredients

  • 4 lbs pork shoulder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup stout beer
  • 2 cups BBQ sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Buns for serving
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Instructions

  1. Season the Meat: Rub the pork shoulder with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the pork on all sides until browned.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: In a slow cooker, add onion and garlic. Place the seared pork on top.

  3. Add Liquids: Pour in the stout beer, BBQ sauce, and apple cider vinegar. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours or until the pork is tender and easily shredded.

  4. Shred the Pork: Remove the pork from the slow cooker, shred it with two forks, and return it to the sauce. Stir to combine.

  5. Serve: Serve the pulled pork on buns, topped with coleslaw if desired, and enjoy with a cold stout.

Conclusion

Stout suppers are a wonderful way to embrace the rich, hearty flavors that dark beers have to offer. From comforting stews and savory short ribs to creamy risottos and decadent desserts, these dishes are sure to impress your guests and satisfy your cravings. So, the next time you crack open a bottle of stout, consider whipping up one of these delicious recipes for a truly memorable dining experience. Cheers to hearty meals and the warmth of good company!
