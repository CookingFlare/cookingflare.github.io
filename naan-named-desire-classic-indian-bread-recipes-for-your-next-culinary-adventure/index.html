Naan-named Desire: Classic Indian Bread Recipes for Your Next Culinary Adventure


Getting down to the essence of Indian cuisine, it wouldn’t be an understatement to say that bread dishes play an integral part in it. Among all Indian bread recipes, there’s one that stands out like the perfect symphony in a composition—the hallowed Naan. It’s an incredibly versatile bread, deeply loved across borders. Much like the mirage of the pioneer’s dream, it lures you into a culinary adventure, unfamiliar and yet beckoning you to explore its cobblestoned lanes. Today, let’s loosen our apron strings, throw caution to the wind, and venture into the heart of classic Indian bread, armed with our trusty Naan.

Tawa Naan

When gas stoves were a distant dream, the tawa or griddle was the solitary savior of our foremothers back in the day. Tawa Naan, deep dented and charred with love, is reminiscent of those simpler times. Simply put, Tawa Naan is the quintessential workhorse you’d want on your side to power through lunch and dinner. Just follow the simple steps given below:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Wheat Flour
  • 1/2 cup Warm Water
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp Instant Yeast
  • Salt (A pinch)

Instructions:

  1. First off, in a large mixing bowl, bring together the wheat flour, sugar, and instant yeast. Gradually pour in the warm water while ensuring the ingredients form a pliable dough. Continue kneading until you attain the consistency of a tight pizza dough.
  2. Cover and set the dough aside for about an hour, granting it a chance to split hairs and bask in the glow of yeast’s magic.
  3. Grease your palms, and subsequently, your hands portion the dough into smaller marble-sized balls.
  4. Roll them out, careful not to cry foul at their rebellious urge to stick. That’s just them asserting their Indian spirit.
  5. One by one, place the rolled Naan on the heated griddle, dance around it with a spatula, applying gentle pressure, loosening secrets from its tender belly.
  6. Once you detect those telltale bubbles crowing the bread, flip it over. Let it brown, fondling it tenderly with the tip of your knife.
  7. Brush the Naan with ghee or butter or garlic paste, and you have a tawa naan fit for a king.

Garlic Naan

Flirtatious, tempting, and ever so seductive—Garlic Naan morphs into an understanding partner on your culinary odyssey. It’s the kind of dance partner you don’t want to miss. This recipe is for the garlic lovers in the crowd:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Wheat Flour
  • 1/2 cup Warm Water
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp Instant Yeast
  • 2 cloves Garlic, Crushed
  • Salt (A pinch)
  • 1 tbsp Ghee or Oil

Instructions:

  1. Combine the wheat flour, sugar, and instant yeast in a deep vessel. Pour in the warm water as you churn the sea of flour with your desire and intention.
  2. Knead it until the dough eludes signs of dryness and starts clinging onto your palms like a dear friend.
  3. Cover the dough and let it slumber until it’s mature enough—about an hour.
  4. After an hour, portion the dough into smaller marble-sized balls, humble and unassuming.
  5. Knead the crushed garlic into the dough and roll it out carefully, not too thin, not too thick.
  6. On a heated tawa or skillet, lay the rolled Naan. Dance around it, coerce its secrets to the surface but remember, too much zeal might deport an undesirable char.
  7. Flip it over, let it brown, and apply a whisper of ghee or oil. Pull away from the heat, and voilà—a garlic Naan license to satisfy and entice.

Getting down and crumbly, breaking down the cultural barriers with a roll, Naan is mandatory on the table, as life is incomplete without it. Along with a dollop of butter chicken or a spoonful of dal makhani, the value of Naan elatedly transcends. So, pan your naan now, arm yourself with your trusty tawa or skillet, and let’s all take a bow on this fascinating culinary adventure together. Happy cooking!
