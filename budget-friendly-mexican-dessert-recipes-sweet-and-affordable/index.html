Budget-Friendly Mexican Dessert Recipes: Sweet and Affordable


When it comes to satisfying your sweet tooth without breaking the bank, Mexican desserts stand out as a deliciously rich option. Combining vibrant flavors with simple ingredients, these recipes are not only easy on the wallet but also incredibly delightful. In this post, we’ll explore several budget-friendly Mexican dessert recipes that will transport you to a fiesta of flavors, all while keeping your budget in check.

1. Churros

Churros are a classic Mexican treat that are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. This delightful dessert is often served with chocolate sauce or sprinkled with cinnamon sugar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine water, oil, and salt. Bring to a boil.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in flour and baking powder until the mixture forms a ball.
  3. Let it cool slightly before adding eggs one at a time, stirring until smooth.
  4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Fill a piping bag with the churro dough and pipe 4-6 inch lengths into the hot oil.
  6. Fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes on each side.
  7. Mix sugar and cinnamon in a bowl and roll the churros in this mixture after frying.
  8. Serve warm with a side of chocolate sauce for dipping.

2. Tres Leches Cake

Tres Leches Cake, or “Three Milks Cake,” is an indulgent dessert that is surprisingly affordable to make. This moist cake is soaked in three types of milk, creating a rich and creamy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Whipped cream for topping
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  4. Gradually add the flour mixture until combined, then pour into the prepared dish.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let it cool.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream.
  7. Poke holes in the cooled cake with a fork and pour the milk mixture evenly over the top.
  8. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  9. Top with whipped cream and a sprinkle of cinnamon before serving.

3. Flan

Flan is a beloved Mexican dessert known for its smooth texture and caramel flavor. Making flan at home is simple, and it requires minimal ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt sugar until it turns golden brown. Quickly pour the caramel into a round baking dish, tilting to coat the bottom evenly.
  3. In a blender, combine eggs, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla. Blend until smooth.
  4. Pour the mixture over the caramel in the baking dish.
  5. Place the baking dish in a larger pan filled with water (water bath) and bake for about 50-60 minutes.
  6. Once set, remove from the oven and allow it to cool. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  7. To serve, run a knife around the edges and invert onto a plate.

4. Arroz con Leche

Arroz con Leche, or rice pudding, is a comforting dessert that is not only filling but also extremely budget-friendly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine rice and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 20 minutes until rice is tender.
  2. Add evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and cinnamon stick to the cooked rice. Stir and cook for another 10-15 minutes on low heat.
  3. Remove the cinnamon stick and stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with a sprinkle of ground cinnamon.

5. Buñuelos

Buñuelos are crispy fried pastries that can be enjoyed as a sweet treat or a savory snack. They are often topped with sugar or syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil for frying
  • Sugar and cinnamon for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Mix well.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together melted butter and egg. Add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, mixing until a soft dough forms. If the dough is too dry, add a little water.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into thin circles.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the dough circles until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels. Dust with sugar and cinnamon before serving.

6. Mexican Hot Chocolate

This dessert drink is rich and warming, perfect for chilly evenings or as a sweet finish to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups milk (or water for a lighter version)
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • A pinch of chili powder (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine milk, cocoa powder, sugar, cinnamon stick, and chili powder (if using).
  2. Heat over medium heat, whisking until the mixture is smooth and heated through.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Serve hot in mugs, optionally topped with whipped cream or marshmallows.

7. Capirotada (Mexican Bread Pudding)

Capirotada is a traditional Mexican bread pudding that is often made during Lent but can be enjoyed year-round.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups stale bread, cubed
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (such as pecans)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil, stirring until sugar dissolves. Remove from heat and add cinnamon and vanilla.
  2. In a greased baking dish, layer half of the cubed bread. Sprinkle with half of the raisins and nuts.
  3. Pour half of the syrup over the layers. Repeat with the remaining bread, raisins, nuts, and syrup. If using cheese, sprinkle it on top.
  4. Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 30-40 minutes until set.
  5. Serve warm, optionally with a drizzle of cream.

Conclusion

These budget-friendly Mexican dessert recipes are not only easy to make but also bring a taste of Mexico right to your kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to indulge in something sweet, these delightful treats will surely impress. The best part? They are all made with affordable ingredients, proving that you don’t have to spend a fortune to enjoy delicious desserts. So gather your ingredients and start creating these mouthwatering recipes today!
