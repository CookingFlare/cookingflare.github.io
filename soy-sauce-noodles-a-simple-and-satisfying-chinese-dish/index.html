Soy Sauce Noodles: A Simple and Satisfying Chinese Dish


If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to delve into the wonderful world of Soy Sauce Noodles, a simple and satisfying dish that is sure to tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a novice in the kitchen or a seasoned chef, this recipe is easy to follow and guarantees a flavorful outcome.

Ingredients

  • 250g of fresh or dried noodles
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of white pepper
  • 2 spring onions, finely chopped
  • Optional: sliced chicken, beef, or vegetables for added protein and texture

Instructions

  1. Boil a pot of water and cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and set aside.

  2. In a wok or large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, being careful not to burn it.

  3. If you’re adding protein or vegetables to your dish, now is the time to cook them. Simply stir-fry them in the wok until cooked through and set aside.

  4. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and white pepper. Mix well until the sugar has dissolved.

  5. Add the cooked noodles to the wok and pour the sauce mixture over them. Toss the noodles gently, ensuring they are evenly coated with the sauce. If you’re using protein or vegetables, add them back into the wok and toss everything together.

  6. Continue to cook the noodles for a few more minutes, allowing them to absorb the flavors of the sauce. If the noodles start to stick to the pan, you can add a splash of water or chicken broth to loosen them up.

  7. Once the noodles are heated through, transfer them to a serving dish and garnish with the chopped spring onions. The vibrant green color and fresh flavor of the spring onions will add a delightful finishing touch to your dish.

  8. Serve your Soy Sauce Noodles hot and enjoy the wonderful combination of flavors and textures. The umami-rich soy sauce, the hint of sweetness from the sugar, and the aromatic garlic will leave your taste buds craving for more.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of noodles such as egg noodles, rice noodles, or even udon noodles. Each variety will bring its own unique texture and taste to the dish.

  • Customize your Soy Sauce Noodles by adding your favorite proteins or vegetables. Sliced chicken, beef, shrimp, or a medley of colorful stir-fried vegetables will elevate the dish and make it a complete meal.

  • For a spicier kick, add a dash of chili oil or sprinkle some red pepper flakes over the finished dish.

  • If you’re a fan of extra saucy noodles, double the sauce ingredients and adjust the seasoning to your liking.

Soy Sauce Noodles are a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a quick weeknight meal or as part of a larger Chinese feast. The simplicity of the recipe allows you to experiment with different ingredients and make it your own. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the wok, and prepare to indulge in a bowl of deliciousness. Happy cooking!
