Homemade English Muffins: Perfect for Breakfast


There’s something undeniably comforting about the smell of freshly baked bread in the morning, especially when it comes to English muffins. These delightful little bread circles are perfect for toasting, slathering with butter and jam, or using as the base for a delicious breakfast sandwich. While it’s easy to grab a pack of English muffins from the store, there’s a special joy in making them from scratch at home. Not only do you get to customize the flavor and texture, but the process of making them is incredibly rewarding. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through a step-by-step recipe for homemade English muffins that will elevate your breakfast game to a whole new level.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) of active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of warm water (around 110°F)
  • 1 cup of warm milk (around 110°F)
  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, softened
  • Cornmeal, for sprinkling

Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently to dissolve the yeast and let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the yeast mixture, warm milk, and 2 cups of flour. Stir until well combined. Add the salt and softened butter, then gradually add the remaining flour until a soft dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook for this step.

Step 4: First Rise

Place the dough in a greased bowl, turning once to coat the dough. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1 hour or until doubled in size.

Step 5: Shape the Muffins

Punch down the dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Roll the dough to about 1/2 inch thickness. Using a round cutter or a glass, cut the dough into circles.

Step 6: Second Rise

Sprinkle a baking sheet with cornmeal and place the cut dough circles onto the sheet, leaving some space between each one. Sprinkle the tops of the muffins with additional cornmeal. Cover the muffins with a kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30-45 minutes.

Step 7: Cook the Muffins

Heat a griddle or a large skillet over medium heat. Carefully transfer the risen muffins to the griddle and cook for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.

Step 8: Enjoy!

Once the muffins are cooked, let them cool on a wire rack. Then, split them open with a fork to reveal their nooks and crannies, perfect for catching melted butter or your favorite spreads. These homemade English muffins are best enjoyed fresh, but you can also store them in an airtight container for a few days.

Conclusion

Making homemade English muffins is a delightful way to start your day. Not only are they delicious, but the process of making them from scratch is incredibly satisfying. With this simple recipe, you can elevate your breakfast experience and impress your family and friends with your baking skills. So, why not give it a try and savor the joy of homemade English muffins for your next breakfast?
