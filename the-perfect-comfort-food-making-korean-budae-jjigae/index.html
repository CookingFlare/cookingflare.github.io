The Perfect Comfort Food: Making Korean Budae Jjigae


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and hearty portions. Among the many dishes that have gained popularity worldwide, Budae Jjigae, also known as Army Stew, stands out as a quintessential comfort food that warms the hearts and bellies of Koreans and food enthusiasts alike. This delectable, spicy, and savory stew is a staple in Korean households, especially during the colder months when a warm, comforting meal is just what the doctor ordered.

A Brief History of Budae Jjigae

Budae Jjigae, which translates to “army stew,” has its roots in the Korean War era. During the 1950s, Korean soldiers stationed in the countryside would often cook a hearty stew using scraps of food they found in the abandoned homes of the wealthy. The dish was a creation born out of necessity, as it allowed soldiers to make a nutritious meal using whatever ingredients they could find. Over time, the recipe evolved, and Budae Jjigae became a beloved national dish, celebrated for its rich flavors and comforting warmth.

Ingredients and Equipment

To make the perfect Budae Jjigae, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of diced ham or bacon
  • 1 cup of diced sausage (such as Korean chorizo or Chinese sausage)
  • 2 cups of diced potatoes
  • 1 cup of diced carrots
  • 1 cup of diced zucchini
  • 2 cups of kimchi, chopped
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped green onions, for garnish

You’ll also need a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven with a lid.

Cooking the Stew

To begin, heat the vegetable oil in the pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until it’s translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

Next, add the diced ham or bacon and cook until it’s crispy, about 5 minutes. Remove the cooked ham or bacon from the pot and set it aside.

Add the diced sausage to the pot and cook until it’s browned, about 5 minutes. Remove the cooked sausage from the pot and set it aside with the ham or bacon.

Add the diced potatoes, carrots, and zucchini to the pot and cook until they’re slightly tender, about 10 minutes. Add the chopped kimchi and cook for an additional 2 minutes, until the kimchi is heated through.

In a small bowl, whisk together the beef broth, soy sauce, Gochujang, and ground black pepper. Pour the broth mixture over the vegetables in the pot and bring the mixture to a boil.

Reduce the heat to low and simmer the stew, covered, for 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender. Add the cooked ham or bacon and sausage back into the pot and simmer for an additional 5 minutes.

Season the stew with salt to taste, then garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot over a bed of steamed rice.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the stew more substantial, add some cooked noodles, such as rice noodles or udon, to the pot.
  • For an extra kick of heat, add more Gochujang to the broth mixture.
  • To make the stew more vegetarian-friendly, omit the ham or bacon and sausage and add more kimchi and vegetables.
  • Budae Jjigae is best served immediately, but it can be refrigerated for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 2 months. Reheat the stew over low heat, adding more broth if necessary.

Conclusion

Budae Jjigae is a true comfort food that warms the heart and soul. With its rich, savory flavors and hearty portions, it’s no wonder this dish has become a staple in Korean cuisine. By following this recipe, you’ll be able to create a delicious and authentic Budae Jjigae that’s sure to become a favorite in your household. So gather your ingredients, fire up your stove, and get ready to indulge in the perfect comfort food.
