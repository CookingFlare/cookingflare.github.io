How to Make Spanish Chicken with Chorizo: A Flavorful One-Pan Recipe


Spanish cuisine is known for its rich, bold flavors and the harmonious combination of ingredients that often define its culinary traditions. One of the most satisfying and flavorful dishes to come from Spain is Spanish chicken with chorizo. This one-pan recipe is not only simple to make, but it also packs a punch with savory, spicy, and smoky flavors. Whether you’re preparing a hearty family dinner or impressing guests with your cooking skills, this dish is sure to be a hit.

In this blog post, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to create this mouthwatering Spanish chicken with chorizo, using easy-to-find ingredients and a straightforward cooking method. Get ready for an unforgettable meal that will transport you straight to the heart of Spain!

What is Spanish Chicken with Chorizo?

Spanish chicken with chorizo is a rustic, comforting dish that combines succulent chicken thighs with flavorful chorizo sausage, all cooked together in one pan. The chicken becomes infused with the rich flavors of the chorizo as it crisps up in the pan, while the chorizo releases its savory juices and spices, adding depth to the entire dish. It’s typically accompanied by potatoes, peppers, and onions, which soak up the delicious juices and become perfectly tender during cooking.

This dish is inspired by the traditional Spanish “paella,” but it simplifies the process by using fewer ingredients and cooking it all in one pan. It’s a versatile recipe that can be customized to your taste, allowing for variations like adding more vegetables or adjusting the spice level to suit your preferences.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  • One-pan simplicity: Less cleanup and more time to enjoy your meal.
  • Bold flavors: Chorizo adds a unique depth of flavor to the chicken.
  • Versatility: Feel free to add extra vegetables or adjust the spices to suit your taste.
  • Comforting and satisfying: Hearty and filling, this dish is perfect for any occasion.
  • Minimal ingredients: A few key ingredients create a dish that’s bursting with flavor.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this Spanish chicken with chorizo recipe, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken and Chorizo:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (or boneless, if preferred)
  • 2-3 Spanish chorizo sausages, sliced into rounds (or use a different type of chorizo if needed)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for frying)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (for an extra smoky flavor)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Vegetables:

  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes (yukon gold or red potatoes work well)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved (optional but adds a burst of freshness)

For the Broth:

  • 1 cup chicken broth (low sodium recommended)
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (optional but adds depth of flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped
  • Lemon wedges for a zesty touch

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Spanish Chicken with Chorizo

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by gathering all your ingredients and preparing them for cooking.

  1. Cut the vegetables: Peel and cube the potatoes, slice the bell pepper and onion, and halve the cherry tomatoes if using. Mince the garlic and set it aside.
  2. Slice the chorizo: Slice the Spanish chorizo sausages into rounds, ensuring they’re ready to be added to the pan as soon as the cooking begins.

Step 2: Brown the Chicken Thighs

In a large, heavy-bottomed skillet or frying pan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. While the oil is heating, pat the chicken thighs dry with a paper towel and season them generously with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika on both sides.

Once the oil is hot, place the chicken thighs in the pan, skin side down, and cook for 4-5 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the chicken thighs and cook for another 4-5 minutes on the other side. The goal here is to sear the chicken and lock in the flavor. Don’t worry about fully cooking the chicken yet, as it will finish cooking with the other ingredients later.

Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside for now.

Step 3: Cook the Chorizo and Vegetables

In the same pan, add the sliced chorizo and cook over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chorizo begins to release its oil and becomes slightly crispy. This process will infuse the oil with the rich, smoky flavors of the chorizo.

Next, add the diced potatoes, onions, and red bell pepper to the pan. Stir the vegetables around to coat them in the chorizo oil and cook for 5-7 minutes until they start to soften. Don’t forget to add the minced garlic in the last minute of cooking, as garlic burns quickly.

At this point, the kitchen should start to smell absolutely delicious, and the chorizo’s smoky, spicy aroma will be filling the air.

Step 4: Add Broth and Wine

Once the vegetables are softened, it’s time to add the liquid. Pour in the chicken broth and dry white wine (if using), scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. These little bits of flavor will add richness to the dish.

Add fresh thyme and cayenne pepper (if desired for a bit of heat). Stir everything together and bring the mixture to a simmer.

Step 5: Return the Chicken to the Pan

Place the browned chicken thighs back into the pan, skin side up. Nestle them into the vegetables and chorizo so they’re partially submerged in the liquid. Cover the pan with a lid and reduce the heat to low. Let everything simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked through (the internal temperature should reach 165°F/75°C).

During this time, the chicken will cook in the flavorful broth, and the potatoes will become tender and infused with the delicious chorizo flavor. You can check the chicken halfway through to ensure the pan doesn’t dry out; if needed, add a bit more broth.

Step 6: Final Touches

Once the chicken is fully cooked, remove the lid and let the pan simmer uncovered for a few more minutes, allowing the sauce to reduce slightly and the flavors to concentrate. Add the cherry tomatoes at this point, if using, and cook for another 2-3 minutes until they soften and burst slightly.

Taste the dish and adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, or more cayenne if you like a spicier kick.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Remove the pan from the heat and let the dish rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley or cilantro for a burst of color and freshness. Squeeze a little lemon juice over the top to balance out the richness and enhance the flavors.

Serve your Spanish chicken with chorizo hot, straight from the pan, with crusty bread or over a bed of rice. This dish pairs wonderfully with a light green salad on the side to balance the hearty, savory flavors.

Tips for the Best Spanish Chicken with Chorizo

  • Use bone-in chicken thighs: Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs work best in this recipe because they retain moisture during cooking, making them juicy and tender. Boneless chicken thighs or breasts can be substituted, but the cooking time may vary slightly.
  • Don’t skip the chorizo: The chorizo is the star of the dish, so choose a good-quality Spanish chorizo for the best flavor. You can use either fresh or cured chorizo, but fresh will release more flavor as it cooks.
  • Customize the vegetables: While potatoes, onions, and peppers are traditional, feel free to add or swap out other vegetables like zucchini, mushrooms, or peas. Just be mindful of the cooking times to ensure everything cooks evenly.
  • Add heat to taste: If you love spicy food, feel free to increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add a chopped fresh chili when cooking the vegetables.
  • Let the flavors develop: This dish is even more flavorful the next day, so consider making it ahead of time for an easy meal. Reheat gently on the stove with a splash of chicken broth to keep it moist.

Why This Dish is a Winner

Not only is Spanish chicken with chorizo bursting with flavor, but it’s also incredibly versatile and adaptable to your personal tastes. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, preparing a comforting family meal, or looking for a dish that you can prep in advance, this one-pan recipe is the perfect solution. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a flavorful and satisfying meal that brings the essence of Spanish cuisine right into your home.

Give this recipe a try, and you’ll see why it’s such a beloved dish in Spanish cooking. It’s comforting, easy to make, and packed with the bold, vibrant flavors that are so characteristic of Spain’s culinary heritage.

Enjoy your meal with a glass of Spanish wine or a chilled pitcher of sangria for the full Spanish experience. ¡Buen provecho!
