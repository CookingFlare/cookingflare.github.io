Simple French Recipes for Busy Families


Making a delicious and sophisticated meal can be a challenge, especially for busy families. With the demands of work and school, it's easy to rely on takeout or convenience foods. However, with a few simple French recipes, you can create mouth-watering dishes without sacrificing your family's taste buds or your sanity. Simplifying French Cuisine French cuisine is famous for its complex flavors and techniques, but these recipes prove that you don't need to be a culinary expert to make them. By breaking down recipes into their simplest form and using everyday ingredients, you'll be whipping up French masterpieces in no time. Monday: French Onion Soup This classic soup is a staple of French cuisine and a crowd-pleaser. It's also incredibly easy to make and can be ready in under 30 minutes. Ingredients

2 tablespoons butter 1 large onion 2 cloves garlic 4 cups beef broth 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 6 slices of baguette 1 cup grated cheese (such as gruyère or cheddar)

Instructions

Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until the onion is translucent, about 8-10 minutes. Pour in the beef broth, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for 15 minutes or until hot and bubbly. Preheat your broiler. Rub the bread slices with a little butter and place on a baking sheet. Broil slices of bread until golden brown, then sprinkle with cheese and return to the broiler until the cheese is golden and bubbly. Ladle the hot soup over the bread slices and serve with a sprinkle of additional cheese if desired.

Quick Tuesday: Quiche Lorraine Quiche is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. It's also a fantastic way to use up leftover vegetables and cheese. Ingredients

1 pie crust (homemade or store-bought) 2 tablespoons butter 1 onion, diced 2 cloves garlic 1 cup heavy cream 2 large eggs 1 cup shredded cheese (such as cheddar, cheddar, or Swiss) 1/2 cup chopped ham Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

Roll out the pie crust and place in a pie dish. Bake the crust in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 15-18 minutes or until lightly browned. In a separate skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Cook the onion and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, eggs, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Sprinkle the cooked onion mixture and shredded cheese into the baked pie crust. Pour the cream mixture over the filling and top with chopped ham. Return the quiche to the oven and bake for 35-40 minutes or until a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.

Wednesday: Chicken Cordon Bleu Chicken Cordon Bleu is a classic dish that combines chicken, ham, and cheese to make a show-stopping dinner. It's also relatively quick and easy to prepare. Ingredients

4 boneless chicken breasts 4 slices of ham 4 slices of Swiss cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup butter, softened Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Lay a slice of ham on the cutting board. Arrange sliced cheese on the ham, leaving a small border around the edges. Fold the chicken breast in half to cover the ham and cheese, securing with toothpicks if needed. In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Brown the chicken breasts on both sides for about 5 minutes or until lightly browned. Transfer the browned chicken to a baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until cooked through.

Thursday: Coq au Vin Coq au Vin is a hearty dish that slowly cooks chicken in a rich wine and mushroom sauce. While it may seem complicated, this simplified version cuts the cooking time by half. Ingredients

2 boneless chicken breasts 1 cup white wine 1 cup beef broth 2 tablespoons butter 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup sliced mushrooms 1/2 cup chopped onion 1 teaspoon dried thyme Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

In a skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Brown the chicken breasts on both sides for about 5 minutes or until lightly browned. In the same skillet, add the white wine, beef broth, garlic, and mushrooms. Bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add the browned chicken to the skillet and cook for 5-10 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through.

Friday: Crêpes Savory or sweet, crêpes are a healthier, versatile alternative to pancakes. Ingredients

1 cup milk 1 cup water 2 eggs 2 tablespoons sugar 3 tablespoons melted butter 2 cups all-purpose flour Fillings of choice (ham, eggs, vegetables, cheese, or fruit)

Instructions

In a blender, combine milk, water, eggs, sugar, and melted butter. Blend until smooth. Gradually add the flour to the mixture and blend until smooth. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Pour in a small amount of the batter, tilting the skillet to coat evenly. Cook the crêpe for 1-2 minutes or until the edges start to curl. Fold in half to enclose fillings, then serve.

Creating a French-inspired meals for your family doesn't have to be difficult or time-consuming. These simple recipes not only showcase the flavors and techniques of French cuisine but also use everyday ingredients that can be found in your local grocery store. The key is to keep it simple and avoid overcomplicating meals. Try your hand at the classic recipes outlined above and give your family the taste of France without sacrificing your sanity.
