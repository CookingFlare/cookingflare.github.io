Middle Eastern Vegan Recipes: Delicious Plant-Based Options


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its rich history, diverse flavors, and colorful ingredients. It is a cuisine that relies heavily on vegetables, grains, legumes, and aromatic spices, making it a perfect match for plant-based diets. From creamy hummus to spiced lentils and vibrant vegetable stews, Middle Eastern vegan dishes are packed with flavors that can excite even the most dedicated carnivores.

In this blog post, we’ll dive into a selection of delicious Middle Eastern vegan recipes that you can easily prepare at home. These dishes not only highlight the best of plant-based eating but also offer a taste of the rich cultural heritage of the Middle East.

1. Hummus – The Classic Middle Eastern Dip

No Middle Eastern meal is complete without hummus. This creamy, rich dip is made primarily from chickpeas, tahini (sesame seed paste), lemon, garlic, and olive oil. It’s incredibly versatile and can be used as a spread, a dip for vegetables or pita bread, or even as a side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (or 1.5 cups cooked chickpeas)
  • 3 tbsp tahini
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed for desired consistency)
  • Olive oil, paprika, and parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas if using canned. If you’re using fresh chickpeas, cook them until soft.
  2. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, and salt.
  3. Blend until smooth, adding water a little at a time until you reach your desired consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more lemon juice or cumin as needed.
  5. Transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, and garnish with a sprinkle of paprika and fresh parsley.
  6. Serve with pita bread, vegetables, or as part of a mezze platter.

This recipe is not only easy to make but also provides a creamy, comforting base for various Middle Eastern dishes.

2. Falafel – Fried Chickpea Balls of Flavor

Falafel is another staple of Middle Eastern cuisine, often found in street food stalls and restaurants. These crispy, golden balls are made from chickpeas, fresh herbs, and spices, then deep-fried to perfection. They are naturally vegan, making them an excellent choice for anyone seeking plant-based meals.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground paprika
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp flour (or chickpea flour for a gluten-free option)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas. Place them in a food processor with the onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, paprika, and baking powder.
  2. Process the mixture until it becomes a coarse dough. If it feels too wet, add flour a little at a time until it holds together.
  3. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste, then refrigerate for at least an hour.
  4. Shape the dough into small balls or patties.
  5. Heat the olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel in batches, turning occasionally until golden and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  6. Remove from oil and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve with tahini sauce, pickled vegetables, and a side of fresh salad or in pita bread.

Falafel is a great source of protein and fiber, making it a satisfying and nutritious meal.

3. Mujaddara – Lentils and Rice with Caramelized Onions

Mujaddara is a beloved dish in many Middle Eastern countries, and it’s one of the simplest yet most flavorful meals you can prepare. It’s a combination of lentils, rice, and caramelized onions, providing a hearty and nutritious meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown or green lentils
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the lentils under cold water and cook them in a pot with 3 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 20-25 minutes until the lentils are tender but not mushy.
  2. While the lentils are cooking, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they are deeply caramelized and golden brown. This will take about 20 minutes.
  3. In another pot, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the rice and cook for 2-3 minutes until lightly toasted.
  4. Add the cooked lentils (along with any leftover cooking water) to the rice, along with the cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  5. Cover the pot and cook the rice and lentils together for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and fully cooked.
  6. Serve the mujaddara in a large bowl, topped with the caramelized onions and garnished with fresh parsley.

Mujaddara is a perfect example of how simple ingredients can be transformed into something both comforting and flavorful. It’s vegan, high in protein, and pairs wonderfully with a side of yogurt or fresh salad.

4. Fattoush – A Vibrant Middle Eastern Salad

Fattoush is a refreshing salad made with crisp vegetables, fresh herbs, and toasted pita bread. It’s often dressed with a tangy lemon and olive oil dressing, making it the ideal dish to complement other Middle Eastern vegan recipes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed greens (romaine, arugula, or any leafy greens)
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup radishes, thinly sliced
  • 2 pita bread rounds
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 1 tsp ground sumac (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Tear the pita bread into pieces and place them on a baking sheet. Drizzle with a tablespoon of olive oil and toast in the oven for 5-7 minutes until crispy and golden.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the mixed greens, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, bell pepper, and radishes.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining olive oil, lemon juice, pomegranate molasses, ground sumac, salt, and pepper.
  4. Just before serving, add the toasted pita pieces to the salad and drizzle the dressing over the top.
  5. Toss gently to combine and serve immediately.

Fattoush is an incredibly light yet flavorful salad that pairs well with any Middle Eastern meal. The crispy pita adds a nice crunch, while the tangy dressing brings everything together in a perfect balance of flavors.

5. Baba Ganoush – Smoky Eggplant Dip

Baba ganoush is another classic Middle Eastern dip that’s similar to hummus but made with roasted eggplant instead of chickpeas. Its smoky flavor, combined with tahini, lemon, and garlic, makes it a delicious addition to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 3 tbsp tahini
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Pomegranate seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Prick the eggplants with a fork a few times, then place them on a baking sheet and roast for 35-40 minutes, turning halfway through, until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
  2. Remove the eggplants from the oven and let them cool slightly. Once cooled, scoop out the soft flesh into a bowl and discard the skins.
  3. In a food processor, combine the eggplant flesh, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more lemon juice or salt if needed.
  5. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh parsley and pomegranate seeds, if using.
  6. Serve with warm pita bread or vegetables for dipping.

Baba ganoush is the perfect smoky, creamy dip to enjoy as part of a mezze spread. Its rich flavor and velvety texture make it irresistible to anyone who tries it.

6. Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolma)

Dolma is a traditional Middle Eastern dish where grape leaves are stuffed with a mixture of rice, herbs, and sometimes vegetables. The flavors are infused as they cook, making each bite a burst of aromatic goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar of grape leaves (about 30 leaves), drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup rice (preferably short-grain)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (optional)
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in the rice, pine nuts (if using), parsley, dill, allspice, cinnamon, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  3. Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. Lay one grape leaf flat on a clean surface. Place about a tablespoon of the rice mixture near the stem end of the leaf.
  5. Fold in the sides and roll up the leaf tightly, like a small burrito.
  6. Repeat with the remaining leaves and rice mixture.
  7. Arrange the stuffed grape leaves in a pot, layering them as you go. Add enough water to cover the dolmas, along with the lemon juice.
  8. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the grape leaves are tender.
  9. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Dolma is a savory, aromatic dish that’s perfect for serving at gatherings or as part of a larger meal. Its tender grape leaves and fragrant rice filling are always a crowd-pleaser.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern vegan cuisine is a treasure trove of delicious, plant-based dishes that showcase the best flavors of the region. Whether you’re enjoying creamy hummus, crispy falafel, or aromatic mujaddara, there’s no shortage of satisfying meals that cater to a vegan lifestyle. The use of fresh herbs, spices, and healthy ingredients ensures that each dish is not only delicious but also nourishing.

So, next time you’re craving something flavorful and wholesome, look to Middle Eastern cuisine for inspiration. With these recipes, you can bring the flavors of the region to your own kitchen and enjoy plant-based meals that are bursting with flavor and tradition.
