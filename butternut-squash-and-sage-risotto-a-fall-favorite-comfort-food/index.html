Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto: A Fall Favorite Comfort Food


Risotto lovers rejoice! This Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto recipe is the perfect comfort food to warm your soul on chilly fall evenings. The creamy texture of the risotto pairs perfectly with the sweet and nutty flavor of butternut squash, while the earthy aroma of sage adds a touch of elegance to this hearty dish. Prepare to fall in love with the ultimate fall comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage leaves, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring the vegetable or chicken broth to a simmer. Keep it warm on low heat.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a separate large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, sautéing until they become translucent and fragrant.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice, coating each grain with the oil, onion, and garlic mixture. Cook for about 2 minutes until the rice starts to toast slightly.
  4. If using white wine, pour it into the saucepan and stir continuously until the liquid has been absorbed by the rice.
  5. Begin adding the warm broth to the rice, one ladleful at a time, stirring continuously. Allow each ladleful to be absorbed by the rice before adding the next.
  6. After about 10 minutes, add the cubed butternut squash to the risotto. Continue adding the broth and stirring until the rice and squash are cooked, and the risotto has reached a creamy consistency. This should take about 20-25 minutes in total.
  7. Stir in the freshly chopped sage leaves and grated Parmesan cheese, ensuring they are well incorporated into the risotto.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and give the risotto a final stir.
  9. Remove the risotto from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
  10. Serve the Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto hot, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh sage leaves and an extra sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top.

Tips and Variations

  • For added richness, you can stir in a tablespoon of butter just before serving.
  • If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can use an immersion blender to partially puree the cooked butternut squash before adding it to the risotto.
  • To make this dish vegan-friendly, simply substitute the Parmesan cheese with nutritional yeast or a vegan cheese alternative.
  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can roast the cubed butternut squash in the oven before adding it to the risotto. Simply toss the squash in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast at 400°F (200°C) for about 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile of your risotto. Thyme, rosemary, or even a pinch of nutmeg can all be delightful additions.

In Conclusion

As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, indulge in the comforting flavors of fall with this Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto. This dish is a celebration of seasonal produce and traditional Italian cooking techniques. The creamy texture, nutty sweetness, and aromatic sage combine to create a symphony of flavors that will warm both your belly and your heart. So grab your apron, gather the ingredients, and experience the magic of this fall favorite comfort food. Bon appétit!
