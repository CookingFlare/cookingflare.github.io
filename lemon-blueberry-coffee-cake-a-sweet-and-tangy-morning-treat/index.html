Lemon Blueberry Coffee Cake: A Sweet and Tangy Morning Treat


There’s something undeniably delightful about the combination of lemon and blueberry, especially when it comes together in a tender, moist coffee cake. This recipe is perfect for those leisurely weekend mornings when you want to treat yourself and your loved ones to a homemade breakfast that feels special.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh or frozen blueberries (if using frozen, do not thaw)

For the Streusel Topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces

For the Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat and Prepare: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x13-inch baking pan or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.

  2. Make the Streusel Topping: In a small bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and lemon zest for the streusel topping. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Set aside.

  3. Prepare the Cake Batter: In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Mix in the sour cream and vanilla extract until smooth.

  4. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and lemon zest.

  5. Mix Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. Gently fold in the blueberries.

  6. Assemble the Cake: Spread half of the batter into the prepared baking pan, smoothing it out evenly. Sprinkle half of the streusel topping over the batter. Repeat with the remaining batter and streusel topping.

  7. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean and the top is golden brown.

  8. Make the Glaze: While the cake is baking, prepare the glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar and lemon juice until smooth. Adjust the consistency by adding more sugar or lemon juice as needed.

  9. Cool and Glaze: Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes, then drizzle the glaze over the warm cake. Let it cool completely before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Lemon Blueberry Coffee Cake pairs beautifully with a hot cup of coffee or tea. The tangy lemon zest and juicy bursts of blueberries complement the moist, buttery cake perfectly. Serve it as a brunch centerpiece or enjoy it as an afternoon snack with friends and family.

Storage Tips

To keep your coffee cake fresh, store it in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. You can also freeze individual slices wrapped in plastic wrap and foil for longer storage. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before serving.

Final Thoughts

Baking this Lemon Blueberry Coffee Cake is not just about creating a delicious treat; it’s about creating memories around the table. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply enjoying a lazy Sunday morning, this recipe is sure to bring smiles and satisfaction. Embrace the flavors of fresh lemon and plump blueberries in every bite, and savor the joy of homemade goodness.
