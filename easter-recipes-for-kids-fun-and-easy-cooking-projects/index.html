Easter Recipes for Kids: Fun and Easy Cooking Projects


Easter is a time for family, fun, and creativity, especially when it comes to celebrating with kids. The holiday brings a mix of joy and excitement, from Easter egg hunts to festive meals, and it’s the perfect time to get little hands involved in the kitchen. If you’re looking for fun and easy Easter recipes that will not only keep your kids entertained but also make delicious treats to enjoy, look no further! This guide includes a variety of simple, hands-on cooking projects that kids will love to make and, of course, eat.

1. Bunny-Shaped Sugar Cookies

What better way to start your Easter cooking adventures than by baking a batch of bunny-shaped sugar cookies? These cookies are soft, sweet, and perfect for decorating.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Pink icing and candy eyes (for decoration)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the dough: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg and vanilla extract, and mix well. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture and mix until a dough forms.
  2. Roll out the dough: Lightly flour your countertop and roll out the dough to about ¼ inch thickness. Use a bunny-shaped cookie cutter to cut out the cookies.
  3. Bake the cookies: Place the cut-out cookies on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 8–10 minutes or until the edges are golden. Let the cookies cool completely before decorating.
  4. Decorate: Once the cookies have cooled, let the kids decorate them with pink icing for the nose and candy eyes. You can also use colorful sprinkles for extra flair.

These bunny-shaped sugar cookies will not only make your kitchen smell delicious, but they also create a fun bonding experience for you and your kids while decorating.

2. Easter Egg Rice Krispies Treats

Rice Krispies treats are always a hit with kids, and making them into Easter egg shapes adds an extra level of fun to this classic snack. This is a no-bake recipe, making it a perfect choice for young chefs who are just getting the hang of cooking.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 package (10 oz) mini marshmallows
  • Food coloring (pastel shades)
  • Sprinkles (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the treats: In a large saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the marshmallows and stir until they are completely melted and smooth.
  2. Mix the cereal: Add the Rice Krispies cereal to the melted marshmallow mixture, stirring until the cereal is evenly coated.
  3. Color the treats: Divide the mixture into separate bowls and add a few drops of food coloring to each bowl. Stir well until the mixture turns into pastel colors like pink, blue, yellow, and green.
  4. Form the eggs: Allow the colored mixtures to cool slightly, then use your hands (lightly greased with butter or non-stick spray) to form small oval shapes resembling Easter eggs. Let the treats cool completely.
  5. Decorate: If you like, you can dip the treats in sprinkles or additional candy decorations to add extra color and texture.

This treat not only looks festive but is also easy to prepare and fun for kids to shape into egg forms. Plus, it’s a great way to incorporate some creativity into the cooking process!

3. Bunny Pancakes

Why settle for plain pancakes when you can create cute bunny pancakes for Easter breakfast? These bunny pancakes are easy to make, and kids will love the fun shapes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups pancake mix
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 banana (for ears)
  • Chocolate chips (for eyes and nose)
  • Maple syrup

Directions:

  1. Prepare the pancake batter: In a large bowl, whisk together the pancake mix, milk, egg, and melted butter until smooth.
  2. Cook the pancakes: Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly grease it with butter or cooking spray. Pour a small amount of pancake batter onto the griddle to form a circle for the bunny’s head. To make the ears, pour two smaller circles on either side of the head.
  3. Flip the pancakes: Once bubbles start to form on the surface, flip the pancake to cook the other side for about 1-2 minutes. Remove from the griddle and place on a plate.
  4. Assemble the bunny face: Slice the banana into thin pieces to create the bunny ears. Use chocolate chips to form the eyes and nose. You can also use a small piece of fruit, like a strawberry slice, for the mouth if desired.
  5. Serve: Drizzle the pancakes with maple syrup and serve immediately.

These bunny pancakes are the perfect way to start Easter morning with a smile. Kids will enjoy assembling their own bunny faces and, of course, eating them afterward!

4. Carrot-Shaped Fruit Kabobs

Get your kids to eat more fruit by turning it into fun carrot-shaped kabobs! These fruity creations are not only delicious but also make a healthy snack or side dish for your Easter celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large orange (for the “carrot”)
  • Green grapes (for the “greens”)
  • Skewers or wooden sticks
  • Small cookie cutter (optional)

Directions:

  1. Prepare the fruit: Peel the orange and cut it into wedges. For added creativity, you can use a small cookie cutter to cut the orange into shapes that resemble carrot tops.
  2. Assemble the kabobs: Take a skewer or wooden stick and thread the orange wedges onto it. Leave some space at the top for the “greens.” Add a few green grapes at the top to form the leafy part of the carrot.
  3. Serve: Arrange the fruit kabobs on a serving platter and enjoy this healthy, fun snack that kids will love.

These carrot-shaped fruit kabobs not only provide a colorful addition to your Easter spread but also encourage your kids to enjoy healthy, fresh fruit in a fun and playful way.

5. Easter Bunny Cupcakes

Cupcakes are always a favorite, and these Easter bunny cupcakes add a creative twist to a classic dessert. With a few simple decorations, you can transform regular cupcakes into adorable bunny faces that will be the star of your Easter celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of cake mix (or your favorite homemade cake recipe)
  • 1 cup butter (softened)
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pink food coloring
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Coconut flakes (optional)
  • Jelly beans (for noses)

Directions:

  1. Bake the cupcakes: Prepare the cake mix according to the instructions on the box. Bake the cupcakes and allow them to cool completely.
  2. Make the frosting: In a mixing bowl, beat together the softened butter and powdered sugar until smooth. Add the vanilla extract and a few drops of pink food coloring to create a pastel pink frosting.
  3. Frost the cupcakes: Frost each cupcake with the pink buttercream frosting, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  4. Decorate the bunny face: Place two mini marshmallows on each cupcake for the bunny’s cheeks. Use a small piece of marshmallow for the nose, and add a jelly bean on top to create the bunny’s nose. If you like, you can use coconut flakes to mimic fur and add extra texture.
  5. Serve and enjoy: Once the cupcakes are decorated, they’re ready to serve. These bunny cupcakes are sure to delight both kids and adults alike!

These cupcakes are both delicious and adorable. Plus, they’re a great way to involve kids in the kitchen by letting them help decorate the cupcakes and add their personal touch.

6. Easter Egg Sandwiches

If you’re looking for something a little more savory, these Easter egg sandwiches are an excellent option. You can make a simple sandwich and cut it into an egg shape to celebrate Easter in a fun and edible way.

Ingredients:

  • Bread (whole wheat or white)
  • Deli meat (turkey, ham, or chicken)
  • Cheese slices
  • Lettuce or spinach
  • Mustard or mayonnaise

Directions:

  1. Prepare the sandwich fillings: Lay out a slice of bread and spread a thin layer of mustard or mayonnaise. Add a slice of cheese, followed by the deli meat and a layer of lettuce or spinach.
  2. Cut the sandwich into an egg shape: Use an egg-shaped cookie cutter to cut the sandwich into a festive shape. You can also use a knife to trim the edges if you don’t have a cookie cutter.
  3. Serve: Serve these Easter egg sandwiches as a lunch option that’s both nutritious and fun for the kids to enjoy.

These Easter egg sandwiches are not only a creative twist on a classic dish but also a great way to get kids excited about eating healthy ingredients.

Conclusion

Easter is the perfect time to have fun in the kitchen with your kids, and these recipes are sure to make your celebrations more memorable. Whether you’re baking sweet treats like bunny cookies or preparing savory Easter egg sandwiches, these projects will help bring the whole family together for a festive and enjoyable cooking experience. Remember, the most important part is having fun, so let your kids get creative and enjoy making—and eating—these delicious Easter-inspired recipes.
