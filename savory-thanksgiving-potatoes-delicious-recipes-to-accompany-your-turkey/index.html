Savory Thanksgiving Potatoes: Delicious Recipes to Accompany Your Turkey


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, indulging in delicious food. While the turkey may steal the spotlight, let’s not forget about the unsung heroes of the Thanksgiving feast – the savory potatoes. Whether mashed, roasted, or au gratin, these versatile spuds can elevate your Turkey Day spread to new heights. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering potato recipes that are sure to impress your guests. Let’s dive in!

1. Creamy Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes and minced garlic in a large pot. Cover with cold water and add a generous pinch of salt.
  2. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer until the potatoes are fork-tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Drain the potatoes and garlic, then return them to the pot.
  4. In a small saucepan, heat the milk and butter over low heat until the butter is melted and the mixture is warm.
  5. Using a potato masher or a handheld mixer, mash the potatoes and garlic until smooth.
  6. Slowly pour in the warm milk and butter mixture, continuing to mash until the potatoes are creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Transfer the mashed potatoes to a serving dish and garnish with chopped fresh chives. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Crispy Roasted Rosemary Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the halved baby potatoes, olive oil, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper. Toss until the potatoes are evenly coated.
  3. Transfer the potatoes to a baking sheet, spreading them out in a single layer.
  4. Roast the potatoes in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. The crispy roasted rosemary potatoes are now ready to be enjoyed!

3. Cheesy Scalloped Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and dried thyme, and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the heavy cream to the saucepan and bring it to a simmer. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Grease a baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  5. Arrange a layer of sliced potatoes in the bottom of the baking dish. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and a generous amount of shredded cheddar cheese. Repeat the layers until all the potatoes and cheese are used, ending with a layer of cheese on top.
  6. Pour the infused cream mixture over the potatoes, ensuring it covers them completely.
  7. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  8. Allow the dish to cool for a few minutes before serving. The cheesy scalloped potatoes are now ready to be devoured!

Conclusion

These savory Thanksgiving potato recipes are the perfect accompaniment to your turkey feast. Whether you prefer the creamy goodness of garlic mashed potatoes, the crispy perfection of roasted rosemary potatoes, or the cheesy indulgence of scalloped potatoes, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, try out these delectable recipes and add a delicious twist to your Thanksgiving celebration. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
