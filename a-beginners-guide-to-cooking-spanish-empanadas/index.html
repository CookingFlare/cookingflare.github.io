A Beginner's Guide to Cooking Spanish Empanadas


Spanish empanadas are a delightful culinary treasure, combining flaky pastry with a rich, savory filling that sings with bold flavors. Whether you’re a novice eager to dive into homemade international cooking or simply searching for a new project in the kitchen, crafting these little pockets of joy is both satisfying and approachable. In this guide, you’ll find a step-by-step exploration of how to make authentic Spanish empanadas from scratch—including dough preparation, traditional fillings, tips for baking or frying, and serving suggestions.

What Are Spanish Empanadas?

Empanadas trace their roots to Spain, with influences that span across Latin America and beyond. Unlike the bite-size empanadillas or turnovers you might find, Spanish empanadas are often larger, sometimes presented as a pie or large pastry, sliced into wedges. These handheld wonders culminate in a golden crust encasing a mixture of ingredients like meats, vegetables, and spices, creating a harmonious balance of textures and tastes.

The Anatomy of a Classic Spanish Empanada

To craft a proper Spanish empanada, understanding its basic components is key:

  1. Dough: A tender, flaky pastry crust that can be baked or fried, traditionally made with flour, olive oil, yeast or baking powder, and a touch of salt.
  2. Filling: Usually a savory mixture combining proteins such as tuna, chicken, or beef with vegetables like onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes, spiced with paprika, garlic, and sometimes olives or hard-boiled eggs.
  3. Assembly and Cooking Technique: The dough sheet envelops the filling, sealed with care to prevent leakage and achieve a satisfying crust crunch after cooking.

Ingredients for the Dough

Dough simplicity is part of what makes Spanish empanadas accessible for beginners. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder

The use of olive oil instead of butter reflects the Mediterranean influence and adds a subtle fruitiness.

Step-by-Step Dough Preparation

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, sift together the flour, salt, and baking powder.
  2. Add oil: Pour in the olive oil and rub it into the flour with your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Incorporate water: Gradually add warm water, mixing with a fork or your hand to form a soft dough.
  4. Knead: Transfer to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Rest dough: Cover with a damp cloth and allow it to rest for at least 30 minutes. This step softens the dough and makes rolling easier.

Classic Spanish Empanada Fillings

Tuna and Pepper Filling

One of the most traditional fillings hails from Galicia, where empanadas with tuna and roasted red peppers are cherished. This filling is vibrant and robust, combining savory tuna with sweet peppers and mild spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (7 oz) tuna in olive oil, drained
  • 2 roasted red bell peppers, diced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Preparation:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onions and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add diced roasted peppers and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in tomato sauce and smoked paprika, cooking for a few more minutes until the mixture thickens.
  4. Remove from heat, transfer to a bowl, and fold in the drained tuna.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.

Chicken and Chorizo Filling

For those craving something a bit heartier, this combination of shredded chicken and spicy chorizo layers strong flavors and a satisfying texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked shredded chicken breast
  • 1/2 cup chorizo, diced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Preparation:

  1. Heat olive oil and cook the chorizo until it releases its oils and browns slightly.
  2. Add onions, garlic, and bell pepper, cooking until soft.
  3. Stir in tomato sauce, paprika, and cumin. Simmer briefly.
  4. Add shredded chicken, mix thoroughly, and season to taste.

Assembling the Empanadas

  1. Divide the dough into two equal portions.
  2. Roll each portion on a floured surface into a large circle, about 10-12 inches in diameter.
  3. Transfer one circle to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Spoon the filling evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border.
  5. Brush the border with water or beaten egg to seal.
  6. Carefully place the second dough circle over the filling.
  7. Press edges firmly to seal, then crimp with a fork or twist for a traditional fluted edge.
  8. Poke small holes or slits on top to allow steam to escape during baking.
  9. Brush the top with beaten egg for a glossy finish.

Baking Your Empanadas

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the empanadas for 30-40 minutes or until the crust turns a beautiful golden brown. Allow cooling slightly before slicing—this helps the filling set and makes for cleaner cuts.

Alternative: Frying Empanadas

While baking is typical for Spanish empanadas, frying offers a crispy, indulgent variation.

  • Heat two inches of oil in a deep pan to about 350°F (175°C).
  • Fry empanadas in batches, turning once until golden and cooked through (about 3-4 minutes per side).
  • Drain excess oil on paper towels before serving.

Tips for Success

  • Don’t overfill: Too much filling can cause the empanada to burst during cooking.
  • Keep the dough cool: Warm dough is sticky and hard to handle.
  • Rest after assembling: Letting the empanadas rest for 15 minutes before baking helps the dough relax and hold its shape.
  • Experiment with fillings: From Spanish-style spinach and egg to pork and chorizo blends, empanadas are perfect for creativity.

Serving Suggestions

Spanish empanadas are versatile. Serve warm as an appetizer or with a green salad for a light lunch. Some like pairing them with a bowl of rich Spanish-style gazpacho or simple roasted vegetables.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use store-bought dough?
Absolutely! While making dough from scratch ensures authenticity and freshness, puff pastry or pie crusts can be convenient substitutes.

How far ahead can I prepare the empanadas?
Fill and seal empanadas can be refrigerated for up to 24 hours before baking, or frozen for up to a month. Bake directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

Can empanadas be made gluten-free?
Yes. Consider using gluten-free flours formulated for pastry, keeping in mind adjustments might be needed for moisture and texture.

Final Thoughts

Mastering Spanish empanadas offers an entry point into a rich culinary tradition that’s both forgiving for beginners and rewarding in complexity. From the warm waft of paprika to the joyous crackle of a golden crust, these empanadas deliver big on flavor and satisfaction. Whether baked or fried, stuffed with seafood, meat, or veggies, empanadas invite you to create, share, and savor one bite at a time.

Enjoy the process, and bon appétit!
