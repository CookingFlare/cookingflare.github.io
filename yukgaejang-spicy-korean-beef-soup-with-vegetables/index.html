Yukgaejang: Spicy Korean Beef Soup with Vegetables


Yukgaejang is a beloved Korean soup known for its rich, spicy flavor and hearty ingredients. This soul-warming dish is a staple in Korean cuisine, especially during the colder months, and it’s no wonder why – the combination of tender beef, vibrant vegetables, and fiery red broth makes it a comforting and satisfying meal. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to create your own homemade Yukgaejang that’s sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

For the Broth:

  • 1.5 lbs beef brisket, thinly sliced
  • 10 cups water
  • 6-8 dried Korean red chili peppers
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 5 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Korean red chili pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • Salt, to taste

For the Soup:

  • 1 cup fernbrake (gosari), soaked in water and drained
  • 1 cup sliced Korean radish (mu)
  • 1 cup sliced onion
  • 1 cup sliced carrot
  • 1 cup mung bean sprouts
  • 1 cup sliced shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 cup sliced zucchini
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 4 eggs

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Broth:

    • In a large pot, bring the water to a boil.
    • Add the beef brisket and boil for 5 minutes. Then, discard the water and rinse the beef under cold water to remove any impurities.
    • Return the beef to the pot and add the dried Korean red chili peppers, garlic, onion, and green onions.
    • Pour in the soy sauce, Korean red chili pepper flakes, and sesame oil.
    • Add salt to taste.
    • Simmer over medium heat for 1.5 to 2 hours, until the beef is tender and the broth is rich and flavorful.

  2. Prepare the Soup:

    • Once the broth is ready, add the fernbrake, Korean radish, onion, carrot, mung bean sprouts, shiitake mushrooms, and zucchini.
    • Simmer for an additional 20-30 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Serve:

    • In a separate pot, bring water to a boil and cook the eggs for 7-8 minutes. Then, transfer them to cold water and peel.
    • Slice the boiled eggs in half.
    • Ladle the Yukgaejang into individual bowls and top each with a halved boiled egg and a sprinkle of chopped green onions.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra depth of flavor, you can add a small piece of Korean dried seaweed (dashima) to the broth.
  • Adjust the spiciness of the soup by adding more or less Korean red chili pepper flakes according to your preference.
  • Yukgaejang tastes even better the next day, so consider making a large batch and enjoy the leftovers.

Final Thoughts

Yukgaejang is not only a delicious soup but also a reflection of the rich and diverse flavors of Korean cuisine. The combination of spicy, savory, and hearty elements makes it a truly satisfying dish that warms both the body and the soul. Whether you’re a fan of Korean food or looking to explore new flavors, Yukgaejang is a must-try recipe that will undoubtedly become a favorite in your home. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aromas of this incredible soup fill your kitchen with warmth and comfort. Enjoy!
