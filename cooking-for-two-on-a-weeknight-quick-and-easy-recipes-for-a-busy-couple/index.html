Cooking for Two on a Weeknight: Quick and Easy Recipes for a Busy Couple


Are you and your partner looking for delicious and hassle-free recipes to enjoy after a long day at work? Cooking for two on a weeknight doesn’t have to be a daunting task. With the right recipes and a bit of planning, you can whip up delectable meals that are not only satisfying but also easy to prepare. Whether you’re both seasoned cooks or just starting out in the kitchen, these quick and easy recipes are perfect for a busy couple looking to enjoy a relaxing evening together.

1. Lemon Garlic Shrimp Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of linguine
  • 8 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cook the linguine according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until they turn pink and opaque.
  4. Toss the cooked linguine into the skillet with the shrimp.
  5. Add the lemon juice and zest, then toss to combine.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

This refreshing lemon garlic shrimp pasta comes together in under 30 minutes, making it an ideal weeknight dinner for two.

2. One-Pan Chicken and Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1 small yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and half of the Italian seasoning.
  3. In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Sear the chicken breasts for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  5. Add the cherry tomatoes, zucchini, yellow squash, and bell pepper to the skillet.
  6. Sprinkle the remaining Italian seasoning over the vegetables.
  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

This one-pan chicken and vegetables dish is not only flavorful but also a breeze to clean up, leaving you with more time to enjoy each other’s company.

3. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces of flank steak, sliced thinly
  • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
  • 3 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, marinate the sliced flank steak with 2 tablespoons of soy sauce for 10-15 minutes.
  2. In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over high heat.
  3. Add the marinated flank steak and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until browned. Remove from the wok and set aside.
  4. In the same wok, add the broccoli florets, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  5. Return the cooked flank steak to the wok.
  6. Add the remaining soy sauce and oyster sauce, then toss to combine.
  7. Serve the beef and broccoli stir-fry hot over steamed rice.

This savory beef and broccoli stir-fry is a quick and easy way to enjoy a taste of Asia without the hassle.

Conclusion

Cooking for two on a weeknight doesn’t have to be stressful. These quick and easy recipes are perfect for a busy couple looking to enjoy delicious meals without spending hours in the kitchen. With a little creativity and the right ingredients, you can turn an ordinary weeknight into a delightful culinary experience. So, roll up your sleeves, get cooking, and savor every moment together!
