Buttery Baked Lemon Herb Halibut: A Perfectly Flaky Fish


There’s nothing quite like the delicate taste and flaky texture of halibut. And when you combine it with a medley of zesty lemon and fragrant herbs, you have a dish that is simply irresistible. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a buttery baked lemon herb halibut that will leave your taste buds dancing with delight.

Ingredients

  • 4 halibut fillets
  • 4 tablespoons of butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking dish with parchment paper or lightly grease it to prevent sticking.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. Place the halibut fillets in the prepared baking dish, skin side down. Spoon the butter and herb mixture over the fillets, making sure to coat them thoroughly.

  4. Bake the halibut in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. The exact cooking time may vary depending on the thickness of your fillets, so keep a close eye on them to avoid overcooking.

  5. Once the halibut is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the fish to firm up slightly.

  6. Serve the buttery baked lemon herb halibut hot, garnished with fresh lemon wedges for an extra burst of citrusy goodness. This dish pairs perfectly with a side of steamed vegetables or a light salad.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a more intense lemon flavor, you can add a bit of lemon zest to the butter and herb mixture.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste. Fresh herbs like parsley, dill, or basil can add a vibrant touch to the dish.
  • This recipe works well with other types of white fish, such as cod or tilapia. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly, as thinner fillets may cook faster.
  • To ensure your halibut cooks evenly, try to choose fillets that are of similar thickness. If necessary, you can fold thinner sections under to create a more uniform piece.

Why We Love Buttery Baked Lemon Herb Halibut

There’s something truly special about the combination of buttery halibut, tangy lemon, and aromatic herbs. The richness of the butter complements the mild, sweet flavor of the fish, while the lemon adds a refreshing citrusy note. The herbs bring depth and complexity to the dish, elevating its overall taste.

Not only does this recipe offer a burst of flavor, but it also provides a healthy and nutritious meal option. Halibut is a lean fish that is packed with essential nutrients such as omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and vitamins. By baking the fish instead of frying it, you can enjoy its natural goodness without adding unnecessary calories.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a seafood dish that is both elegant and easy to prepare, give this buttery baked lemon herb halibut a try. You won’t be disappointed by its perfectly flaky texture and tantalizing flavors.
