How to Make Greek Savory Cornbread with Feta Cheese


If you’re looking to explore the rich culinary traditions of Greece while trying something new, Greek savory cornbread with feta cheese is the recipe you’ve been missing. This hearty bread combines the rustic appeal of cornbread with the tangy creaminess of feta cheese, resulting in a dish that’s robustly flavored, versatile, and perfect for any occasion. Whether served as part of a mezze spread, alongside soups and stews, or as a standalone snack, Greek cornbread is a delightful addition to your repertoire.

The History Behind Greek Cornbread

Cornbread as a dish has ancient roots, with variations stretching across cultures and continents. While most associate cornbread with Southern American cuisine, Greece also has a rich tradition of savory breads made with cornmeal. Known as “Bobota” in older times, this cornbread emerged as a staple food in rustic village kitchens, often made when wheat flour was scarce but cornmeal was plentiful. Over the years, modern forms of the bread evolved to include beloved Greek ingredients like feta cheese, olive oil, and fresh herbs, ensuring that Bobota retained its place in Greek gastronomy.

What Makes Greek Cornbread Special?

Unlike the sweeter, buttery cornbreads often found in the United States, Greek savory cornbread focuses on salty and tangy notes that complement its rustic flavor. Feta cheese gives the bread its signature creaminess and tang, while olive oil and yogurt provide moisture and richness. Herbs such as oregano and dill can be incorporated for a Mediterranean twist, and the result is a bread with a moist, crumbly interior and a beautifully golden crust.

Ingredients to Gather

When it comes to Greek cornbread, simplicity is key. This recipe uses ingredients traditionally found in Greek cooking, providing both authenticity and ease. Before you begin, gather the following:

Dry Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper (optional)

Wet Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt (full-fat preferred)
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons honey (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
  • 1 cup milk (whole milk preferred)

Add-Ins:

  • ¾ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh dill or parsley (optional, for added flavor)
  • A sprinkle of dried oregano (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prep Your Workspace

Before diving into the recipe, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan or a similarly sized square baking dish with olive oil or butter. If you prefer, you can line it with parchment paper to ensure the bread releases cleanly once baked.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and black pepper. Whisk them together thoroughly to ensure even distribution of leavening agents and seasonings.

3. Blend the Wet Ingredients

In another medium bowl, whisk the eggs until smooth. Add Greek yogurt, olive oil, honey (if using), and milk. Continue whisking until the mixture is fully combined and creamy.

4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Slowly pour the wet mixture into the bowl of dry ingredients, stirring gently with a spatula or wooden spoon. Fold the ingredients together until just incorporated—be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to a dense texture.

5. Add the Feta and Herbs

Now comes the star of the recipe: feta cheese! Gently fold the crumbled feta into the batter, ensuring even distribution. If you’re adding fresh dill, parsley, or oregano, fold these in at the same time. These elements will add vibrant bursts of flavor throughout the bread.

6. Pour and Smooth

Transfer the batter into your greased or lined baking dish. Use a spatula to spread it evenly to the edges, smoothing out the top for a level finish.

7. Bake to Golden Perfection

Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. If the edges or top of the bread begin to brown too quickly, cover the dish lightly with aluminum foil.

Cooling and Serving Suggestions

Once baked, remove the cornbread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Afterward, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing. The bread can be served warm or at room temperature.

Greek savory cornbread with feta cheese pairs wonderfully with a variety of dishes. Here are some serving ideas:

  • As a Side Dish: Serve alongside roasted vegetables, lemon herb chicken, or a steaming bowl of lentil soup.
  • On a Mezze Platter: Pair this cornbread with other Greek staples like tzatziki, olives, roasted red pepper dips, and grape leaves.
  • For Breakfast or Brunch: Top slices of cornbread with a fried egg and fresh tomatoes for a filling start to the day.

Tips for Success

  • Choose High-Quality Feta: Opt for authentic Greek feta cheese, which tends to be creamier and more flavorful than generic varieties. Sheep’s milk feta is traditional and offers a rich, tangy profile.
  • Don’t Overmix: To avoid tough bread, mix your ingredients until just combined. Overworking the batter activates the gluten in the flour, which can interfere with achieving that tender crumb.
  • Experiment with Herbs: If dill or parsley isn’t your thing, try swapping them for thyme, rosemary, or chives. The flexibility of this recipe allows you to tailor it to your personal taste.
  • Storage: Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days, or in the refrigerator for up to five days. To enjoy later, reheat slices in the oven to restore their texture.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

Greek savory cornbread with feta cheese is the epitome of comfort food with a Mediterranean twist. Its unique combination of hearty cornmeal, tangy feta, and aromatic herbs makes it a crowd-pleaser that works well for both casual meals and festive gatherings. Whether you’re exploring a new cuisine or searching for a creative spin on classic cornbread, this recipe is sure to become a go-to favorite.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and bring a little piece of Greece into your kitchen. Happy baking!
