Bunuelos de Bacalao: Crispy Salt Cod Fritters from Spain


If you’re looking to add a touch of Spanish flair to your culinary repertoire, then Bunuelos de Bacalao, or Crispy Salt Cod Fritters, are a must-try. This traditional Spanish dish is a beloved tapas favorite, often enjoyed as a savory snack or appetizer. The crispy exterior gives way to a tender, flavorful interior, making it a delightful treat for any occasion. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the history of Bunuelos de Bacalao, explore the key ingredients and steps to prepare this dish, and provide tips to ensure your fritters turn out perfect every time.

History of Bunuelos de Bacalao

Bunuelos de Bacalao has a rich history in Spanish cuisine, with origins that can be traced back to the coastal regions of Spain. Salt cod, a staple ingredient in this dish, has been a part of Spanish gastronomy for centuries, particularly in regions where fresh fish was not readily available. The preservation of cod in salt allowed it to be stored for extended periods, making it a valuable source of sustenance for coastal communities.

The tradition of preparing salt cod fritters emerged as a way to make the most of this preserved fish, transforming it into a delectable and satisfying dish. Over time, Bunuelos de Bacalao became a cherished part of Spanish culinary heritage, celebrated for its unique blend of flavors and textures.

Key Ingredients

To prepare Bunuelos de Bacalao, you will need the following key ingredients:

  • Salt Cod: Look for high-quality salt cod, as it forms the foundation of this dish. Soaking the salt cod in water to remove excess salt is a crucial step in the preparation process.

  • Potatoes: Potatoes add a soft, creamy texture to the fritter mixture, binding the ingredients together and imparting a subtle sweetness.

  • Onion and Garlic: These aromatic ingredients provide depth of flavor and enhance the overall taste of the fritters.

  • Parsley: Fresh parsley lends a vibrant, herbal note to the fritters, elevating their complexity.

  • Eggs: Eggs act as a binding agent, ensuring that the fritters hold their shape during frying.

  • Flour: All-purpose flour is used to coat the fritters before frying, creating a crispy outer layer.

  • Baking Powder: Baking powder helps the fritters achieve a light, airy texture.

  • Olive Oil: High-quality olive oil is essential for frying the fritters to golden perfection.

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Salt Cod

Begin by soaking the salt cod in cold water for at least 24 hours, changing the water several times to remove excess salt. Once the cod has been desalted, drain and pat it dry before flaking it into small pieces.

Step 2: Boil and Mash the Potatoes

Peel and dice the potatoes, then boil them until tender. Mash the cooked potatoes until smooth and set aside.

Step 3: Sauté the Aromatics

In a skillet, sauté finely chopped onion and garlic until translucent and fragrant. Allow the mixture to cool before adding it to the mashed potatoes.

Step 4: Combine the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flaked salt cod, mashed potatoes, sautéed aromatics, chopped parsley, eggs, and a pinch of baking powder. Mix the ingredients thoroughly to ensure an even distribution of flavors.

Step 5: Form and Fry the Fritters

Using your hands, shape the fritter mixture into small, uniform balls. Roll each ball in flour to coat them evenly. In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat and carefully fry the fritters until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once the fritters are golden and crispy, transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve the Bunuelos de Bacalao hot, accompanied by a dipping sauce or a squeeze of lemon for a burst of freshness.

Tips for Success

  • Ensure the salt cod is thoroughly desalted to avoid an overly salty end result.

  • Consistency is key when forming the fritters to ensure even cooking.

  • Use a high-quality olive oil for frying to impart a rich, savory flavor to the fritters.

  • Serve the fritters immediately to enjoy them at their crispy best.

Bunuelos de Bacalao are a delightful representation of Spanish culinary craftsmanship, offering a harmonious blend of textures and flavors that are sure to captivate your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of Spain, these crispy salt cod fritters are a delightful addition to any menu. Embrace the tradition and savor the culinary heritage of Spain with this beloved dish. Buena apetito!
