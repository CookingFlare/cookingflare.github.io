Heirloom Tomato and Mozzarella Salad Recipe


Summer is the perfect time to showcase the beauty of heirloom tomatoes in a simple yet elegant salad. The sweetness of the tomatoes pairs perfectly with the creaminess of the mozzarella, and the fresh basil adds a bright and herbaceous note to the dish.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 heirloom tomatoes, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced into 1/4-inch thick rounds
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)

Instructions

  1. Arrange the tomato slices on a large platter or individual plates.
  2. Top each tomato slice with a round of mozzarella cheese.
  3. Drizzle the olive oil over the salad, making sure each piece of tomato and mozzarella is coated with a thin layer of oil.
  4. Sprinkle the balsamic vinegar over the salad, using about 1-2 tbsp per serving.
  5. Sprinkle the chopped basil leaves over the salad, using about 1-2 tbsp per serving.
  6. Season the salad with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. If desired, sprinkle a pinch of sugar over the salad to balance out the acidity of the tomatoes and vinegar.
  8. Serve the salad immediately, garnished with additional basil leaves if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more substantial salad, add some sliced prosciutto or grilled chicken to the dish.
  • To add some crunch to the salad, top the tomatoes with some chopped pecans or pistachios.
  • For a more intense flavor, use a high-quality balsamic glaze instead of balsamic vinegar.
  • To make the salad more substantial, add some mixed greens such as arugula or spinach to the dish.
  • For a vegetarian option, omit the cheese and add some sliced avocado or grilled portobello mushrooms to the dish.

History of Heirloom Tomatoes

Heirloom tomatoes have a rich history that dates back to the early 20th century. These unique tomatoes were grown by small-scale farmers and gardeners who saved seeds from their favorite varieties and replanted them year after year. Over time, these seeds developed into distinct and colorful varieties that were passed down from generation to generation.

In the 1970s and 1980s, heirloom tomatoes experienced a resurgence in popularity as gardeners and chefs began to appreciate their unique flavors and textures. Today, heirloom tomatoes are grown and enjoyed around the world, and are a staple in many farmers’ markets and specialty grocery stores.

Conclusion

Heirloom Tomato and Mozzarella Salad is a simple yet elegant recipe that showcases the beauty of heirloom tomatoes. The sweetness of the tomatoes pairs perfectly with the creaminess of the mozzarella, and the fresh basil adds a bright and herbaceous note to the dish. Whether you’re serving this salad at a summer dinner party or enjoying it as a light and refreshing lunch, it’s sure to be a hit with your friends and family.
