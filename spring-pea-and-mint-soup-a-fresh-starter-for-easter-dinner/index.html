Spring Pea and Mint Soup: A Fresh Starter for Easter Dinner


As the days grow longer and the weather begins to warm, Easter brings with it a sense of renewal and celebration. It’s the perfect time to gather with loved ones and enjoy a delicious meal together. For a fresh and vibrant starter that captures the essence of spring, look no further than this delightful Spring Pea and Mint Soup. With its bright green color and delicate flavors, this soup is a celebration of the season and a perfect way to kick off your Easter dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves, plus extra for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional)
  • Fresh lemon zest, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 2 minutes, stirring frequently.

  2. Add the Peas and Broth: Once the aromatics are tender, add the peas to the pot. If using fresh peas, cook for 3-4 minutes until they are bright green and tender. If using frozen peas, simply heat them through. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  3. Infuse with Mint: Stir in the fresh mint leaves, reserving a few for garnish. Let the soup simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. Blend to Smooth Perfection: Using an immersion blender or a stand blender, carefully blend the soup until smooth and velvety. If using a stand blender, work in batches and be cautious of the hot liquid. Once blended, return the soup to the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Finish with Cream (optional): For an extra touch of luxury, swirl in the heavy cream to add a creamy richness to the soup. This step is optional, but it adds a wonderful silkiness to the texture.

  6. Serve and Garnish: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with a sprinkle of fresh lemon zest and a few torn mint leaves. The bright citrusy zest and the aromatic mint will elevate the flavors and make your soup visually stunning.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a dairy-free version, you can skip the heavy cream and embrace the soup’s pure, vibrant flavors.
  • To add a little heat, consider incorporating a pinch of red pepper flakes or a drizzle of chili oil.
  • This soup can be served warm or chilled, making it a versatile addition to your Easter menu.
  • Consider serving the soup alongside some crusty bread or a light salad to round out the meal.

Embrace the Freshness of Spring

This Spring Pea and Mint Soup is a celebration of the season’s bounty, and it’s a wonderful way to welcome guests to your Easter table. The combination of sweet peas and fragrant mint creates a light and refreshing flavor profile that is sure to impress. Whether you’re hosting a grand Easter feast or simply enjoying a quiet meal with family, this soup is a delightful way to embrace the freshness of spring.

So, this Easter, elevate your dinner with a bowl of this vibrant and delicious soup. It’s a feast for the eyes and the palate, and it’s a beautiful representation of the new beginnings that spring brings. Happy cooking, and happy Easter!
