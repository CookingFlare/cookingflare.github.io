How to Make Ice Cream Sandwiches from Scratch


When the sun is blazing and the craving for something cold and sweet hits, few desserts can compete with the charm of a homemade ice cream sandwich. Soft, chewy cookies hugging creamy, dreamy ice cream—this classic treat is a delightful combination that’s fun to make and even better to eat. Making ice cream sandwiches from scratch is easier than you think, and customizing every element allows you to create a personal masterpiece perfect for any occasion.

Whether you’re planning a backyard party, looking for a cool dessert, or simply wanting to impress your family with a homemade delight, this comprehensive guide will walk you through crafting ice cream sandwiches like a pro—from the cookie dough to the ice cream filling and even creative add-ons.

Why Make Ice Cream Sandwiches from Scratch?

It’s tempting to buy pre-made ice cream sandwiches at the store, but baking your own cookies and churning or selecting your favorite ice cream opens up a world of possibilities:

  • Flavor Control: Choose your cookie flavors—from classic chocolate chip to oatmeal, snickerdoodle, or even double chocolate.
  • Ice Cream Customization: Use any flavor ice cream you like, dairy or non-dairy.
  • Texture Perfection: Homemade cookies can be baked perfectly to be soft and chewy or thin and crisp—whatever you prefer.
  • Special Diet Options: You can make gluten-free cookies, vegan ice cream, or lower-sugar versions that still taste amazing.
  • Presentation and Fun: Get creative with toppings and shapes that store-bought versions don’t offer.

Step 1: Choosing Your Cookie Base

The cookie component is the heart of the sandwich. The best ice cream sandwich cookies have a subtle flavor but strong enough texture to hold the ice cream without crumbling or getting soggy too fast. Typically, softer cookies with a bit of chewiness work best, but some people prefer thinner, crispier options. Here are some great cookie bases:

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

The iconic choice. Use a dough recipe rich with butter and just enough sugar to get golden, tender cookies. Add good-quality chocolate chips or chunks for melty goodness.

Double Chocolate Cookies

For chocolate lovers, using cocoa powder and chocolate bits gives the cookie a deep chocolatey flavor that pairs well with vanilla or mint chip ice cream.

Snickerdoodles

Cinnamon sugar cookies add a cozy, spicy note that contrasts beautifully with creamy ice cream.

Oatmeal Cookies

Chewy oatmeal cookies bring heartiness and texture, and they go wonderfully with fruity or caramel ice cream.

Sugar Cookies

Simple, sweet, and customizable with sprinkles or zest, sugar cookies allow the ice cream flavor to take center stage.

Step 2: Crafting the Perfect Cookie Dough

Here’s a versatile chocolate chip cookie dough recipe ideal for ice cream sandwiches:

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips

Directions

  1. Cream Butter and Sugars: Using a mixer, beat the butter and sugars until fluffy and creamy (about 3 minutes).
  2. Add Eggs and Vanilla: Beat in the eggs one at a time, then add vanilla extract.
  3. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Combine: Slowly add dry ingredients to wet, mixing until just combined.
  5. Fold in Chocolate Chips: Stir chocolate chips into the dough.
  6. Chill: Cover dough and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (this helps prevent too much spreading during baking).

Step 3: Baking Cookies for Ice Cream Sandwiches

  • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  • Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  • Using a cookie scoop, portion dough into 2-tablespoon balls and place evenly spaced on the sheet.
  • Gently flatten each ball to about 2¼ inches wide.
  • Bake for 10–12 minutes until edges are golden and centers appear set but soft.
  • Let cool on the sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

This size and thickness create a perfect cookie to sandwich the ice cream.

Step 4: Selecting or Making the Ice Cream Filling

The best part! You can pick your favorite store-bought ice cream or make your own homemade version. Here are a few crowd-pleasing ice cream ideas:

  • Vanilla Bean: Classic and versatile.
  • Mint Chocolate Chip: Refreshing and indulgent.
  • Salted Caramel: Sweet and salty goodness.
  • Strawberry Swirl: Fruity and colorful.
  • Cookie Dough: Adding texture and flavor.

If you want to make homemade ice cream, use a basic vanilla ice cream or experiment with flavors like honey lavender or espresso.

Step 5: Assembling Ice Cream Sandwiches Like a Pro

Step-by-Step Assembly

  1. Scoop Ice Cream: Using an ice cream scoop or spoon, place a generous scoop (about ½ cup) of ice cream onto the flat side of one cookie.
  2. Shape the Ice Cream: Quickly flatten the ice cream gently with your hand or a spoon to create an even layer.
  3. Top with Another Cookie: Place a second cookie flat side down on top of the ice cream.
  4. Press Gently: Press just enough to spread the ice cream to the edges, creating a neat sandwich.
  5. Freeze to Set: Wrap each sandwich individually in plastic wrap or wax paper to prevent freezer burn. Freeze for at least an hour to firm up.

Step 6: Creative Twists and Toppings

To elevate your ice cream sandwiches, try these delicious add-ons:

  • Roll Edges: Roll the edges of the ice cream in mini chocolate chips, sprinkles, crushed nuts, or toasted coconut flakes.
  • Drizzle Sauce: Before wrapping, drizzle caramel, fudge, or berry compote over the ice cream.
  • Mix Cookie Flavors: Use different cookie flavors for the top and bottom for a flavor contrast.
  • Make Mini Sandwiches: For parties or kid-friendly snacks, make bite-sized sandwiches with small cookies.
  • Add Crunch: Layer thin chocolate bark or cookie crumbs between the ice cream and cookie.

Step 7: Storage Tips

Keep your desserts fresh and flavorful with proper storage:

  • Always wrap ice cream sandwiches individually with plastic wrap or parchment.
  • Store in an airtight container or freezer-safe bag to avoid freezer odors.
  • Eat within a week for best flavor and texture. Ice cream quality declines over prolonged freezing.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Cookies Too Hard: Reduce baking time slightly or add a bit more brown sugar or honey to the dough to retain moisture.
  • Cookies Spread Too Much: Chill dough longer and ensure your baking soda is fresh.
  • Ice Cream Too Soft or Melting: Use firm, slightly softened ice cream for easier spreading but still maintains shape.
  • Sandwiches Falling Apart: Make sure cookies are fully cooled and ice cream is good and firm before assembly.

Summary: An Easy Recipe for Joy

Making ice cream sandwiches from scratch is a delightful way to enjoy a beloved classic with your own twist. From mix-and-match cookie doughs to inventive ice cream flavors and playful toppings, this simple homemade treat will wow family and friends, and it’s perfect all year round—not just summer.

If you start with the perfect cookie dough and use creamy, high-quality ice cream, you’ll be rewarded with an ultra-satisfying dessert experience. Plus, it’s a fun kitchen project that allows endless creativity and brings smiles with every bite.

Bonus: Quick Recipe Recap for Perfect Ice Cream Sandwiches

  • Make soft and chewy cookies (like chocolate chip) with your favorite recipe.
  • Cool completely before assembling.
  • Use firm ice cream, scooped and gently flattened.
  • Sandwich ice cream between two cookies, press lightly.
  • Wrap and freeze to set.
  • Add creative coatings or toppings if desired.

Enjoy your homemade ice cream sandwiches with family and friends or savor them all by yourself—you’ve earned it!

If you give these recipes a try or have your own signature ice cream sandwich twist, share your ideas and experiences. There’s no limit to how much fun making and enjoying these sweet treats can be!
