Tender and Juicy: Herb-Marinated Grilled Pork Tenderloin


There’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of sinking your teeth into a perfectly grilled, herb-marinated pork tenderloin. The combination of tender meat infused with aromatic herbs and the smoky flavor from the grill is a culinary experience to savor. In this recipe, we’ll walk through the steps to achieve a succulent and flavorful grilled pork tenderloin that will have your taste buds singing.

Ingredients

  • 2 pork tenderloins (about 1 pound each)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh sage, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1/4 cup of balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Herb Marinade

In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, sage, black pepper, salt, olive oil, balsamic vinegar, and Dijon mustard. Whisk the ingredients together until they are well combined.

Step 2: Marinate the Pork Tenderloin

Place the pork tenderloins in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the herb marinade over the pork, ensuring that the meat is well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse into the meat.

Step 3: Preheat the Grill

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, about 375-400°F (190-200°C). Make sure the grates are clean and lightly oiled to prevent the pork from sticking.

Step 4: Grill the Pork Tenderloin

Remove the pork tenderloins from the marinade and discard the excess marinade. Place the pork on the grill and cook for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C). Use a meat thermometer to ensure the pork is cooked to perfection.

Step 5: Rest and Serve

Once the pork tenderloin is grilled to perfection, remove it from the grill and let it rest for 5-10 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a juicy and tender result. After resting, slice the pork tenderloin into 1/2-inch slices and serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

This herb-marinated grilled pork tenderloin pairs beautifully with a variety of sides. Consider serving it with roasted vegetables, a fresh green salad, or a side of garlic mashed potatoes. The versatility of this dish makes it a perfect centerpiece for any occasion, from casual weeknight dinners to special gatherings with friends and family.

Conclusion

Grilling a pork tenderloin to perfection is a rewarding and delicious endeavor. The herb-marinated flavors combined with the smokiness from the grill elevate this dish to a whole new level. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or just starting out, this recipe is sure to impress and delight. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a tender and juicy herb-marinated grilled pork tenderloin that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Cheers to good food and great company!
