Busy-Day Banana Split Cake: A Classic in Mini Size


Ah, banana split cakes! Who could resist the temptations of a delectable blend of bananas, whipped cream, and chocolate fudge sauce, all neatly packed into a single, scrumptious dessert? And now, imagine having the joy of this classic dessert in a brand new, easy-to-make mini size. It’s simply perfect for busy days when you’re craving something sweet yet don’t have the time or energy to slave away in the kitchen.

So, let’s dive right into this scrumptious world of miniature banana split cakes! Here’s a simple yet irresistible recipe for you to try out, and in no time, you’ll be diving into a delectable, miniature version of your favorite classic dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 18 muffin-sized paper cups or metal muffin liners
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

For the Whipped Cream Frosting:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

For the Chocolate Ganache:

  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy whipping cream

Instructions:

Preparing the Cake:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and position your oven rack in the middle. Grease your muffin tins or use paper liners and set them aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the dry ingredients: flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix them well and then set the bowl aside.

  3. In another mixing bowl, mash the ripe bananas. Add the sugar, oil, eggs, and vanilla extract; whisk everything until smooth and well-mixed.

  4. Now, add the banana mixture to the dry ingredients and whisk until everything is just combined. It’s crucial not to overmix the batter as it will result in tough cakes.

  5. Once your batter is ready, divide it equally among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full.

  6. Pop these little beauties into the oven and let them bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

  7. After baking, remove your mini cakes from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes in their tins. Then, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Preparing the Whipped Cream Frosting:

  1. As your mini cakes are cooling, prepare the whipped cream frosting. Place the heavy whipping cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract in a large bowl, and whip until it forms smooth peaks.

Preparing the Chocolate Ganache:

  1. While the whipped cream is chilling, make the chocolate ganache. Combine chocolate chips with heavy whipping cream in a microwave-safe bowl. Microwave for about 1 minute, stir well, and then return to the microwave for another 20 seconds if needed. Continue microwaving in 10-second intervals, stirring until smooth and glossy.

Assembling the Mini Banana Split Cakes:

  1. Now that you’ve got all your components ready, it’s time for the fun part—assembly! Take a mini banana cake and slice it in half horizontally. Frost the bottom half with whipped cream, drizzle with chocolate ganache, and add a banana slice. Place the top half of the mini cake on top.

  2. Repeat this process until all your mini cakes have been turned into little banana-split wonders.

  3. Serve your mini banana split cakes immediately, and watch them disappear as your family and friends indulge in this delightful fusion of a classic dessert.

Pro Tips:

  • Keep your bananas ripe but firm to prevent too much moisture from seeping into your cakes.
  • For a more intense banana flavor, you can even toss in a few finely chopped walnuts or pecans into the batter.
  • When frosting the mini cakes, go ahead and indulge in a little extra whipped cream or chocolate ganache drizzle; nobody’s judging here!

Wow, and there you have it! A delightful twist on a classic dessert, perfect for busy days or any occasion. This mini version of the banana split cake might just be your new go-to recipe for when you’re craving a taste of nostalgia in a sweet, fun, and easy-to-make format. So, try it out, and let us know how it goes!
