The Art of Canning Rhubarb: A Tart Delight


Rhubarb, with its vibrant red stalks and tangy flavor, is a versatile ingredient that can be enjoyed in various culinary creations. One of the best ways to preserve its unique taste and extend its shelf life is through the art of canning. In this blog post, we will explore the step-by-step process of canning rhubarb and share some delicious recipe ideas to make the most of this tart delight.

Why Can Rhubarb?

Canning rhubarb not only allows you to enjoy its distinct flavor all year round but also helps to reduce food waste. By preserving this versatile ingredient, you can add a touch of tartness to your dishes even when rhubarb is out of season. Canning also provides a convenient way to store rhubarb, making it readily available for your cooking experiments and culinary delights.

Step-by-Step Guide to Canning Rhubarb

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin the canning process, make sure you have all the necessary supplies on hand. You will need:

  • Fresh rhubarb stalks
  • Canning jars with lids and bands
  • A large pot for sterilizing the jars
  • A water bath canner or a large pot for processing the jars
  • A canning funnel
  • A ladle
  • A clean kitchen towel
  • A jar lifter
  • A bubble remover or a non-metallic utensil

Step 2: Prepare the Rhubarb

Start by washing the rhubarb stalks thoroughly under cold water. Trim off the ends and any leaves, as they are toxic and should never be consumed. Cut the stalks into pieces of your desired size, keeping in mind that smaller pieces will cook faster and create a smoother texture in your canned rhubarb.

Step 3: Sterilize the Jars

Place the canning jars in a large pot and fill it with enough water to cover the jars completely. Bring the water to a boil and let the jars simmer for 10 minutes to ensure they are sterile. Remove the jars from the pot using a jar lifter and place them upside down on a clean kitchen towel to drain and dry.

Step 4: Prepare the Syrup

While the jars are sterilizing, prepare a simple syrup to enhance the flavor and preserve the rhubarb. In a separate pot, combine equal parts water and sugar, and bring the mixture to a boil. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely, and then remove the pot from the heat.

Step 5: Fill the Jars

Using a canning funnel, carefully fill the sterilized jars with the prepared rhubarb pieces, leaving about an inch of headspace at the top. Pour the hot syrup over the rhubarb, ensuring that all the pieces are covered. Use a bubble remover or a non-metallic utensil to remove any air bubbles trapped in the jars.

Step 6: Seal and Process the Jars

Wipe the jar rims clean with a damp cloth to remove any syrup residue. Place the lids and bands on the jars, tightening them securely but not too tightly. Lower the filled jars into the water bath canner or a large pot, ensuring they are fully submerged in water. Process the jars according to the USDA guidelines for canning rhubarb, typically boiling them for 10-15 minutes.

Step 7: Store and Enjoy

After processing, carefully remove the jars from the canner and place them on a kitchen towel to cool. As they cool, you will hear a satisfying “pop” sound, indicating a successful seal. Allow the jars to cool completely before checking the seals by pressing down on the center of each lid. If the lid does not flex or pop, your jars are sealed properly. Store the canned rhubarb in a cool, dark place and consume within a year for the best quality and flavor.

Recipe Ideas Using Canned Rhubarb

Now that you have mastered the art of canning rhubarb, let’s explore some delightful recipes that will make your taste buds dance:

1. Rhubarb Compote

This simple yet delicious compote can be enjoyed on its own, drizzled over pancakes, or used as a topping for ice cream. Combine canned rhubarb with a touch of sugar, a squeeze of lemon juice, and a pinch of cinnamon. Simmer the mixture on the stovetop until it thickens slightly, and let it cool before serving.

2. Rhubarb Crisp

Take your crisp game to the next level with the addition of canned rhubarb. In a baking dish, combine the canned rhubarb with a sprinkle of sugar and a dash of vanilla extract. In a separate bowl, mix together oats, flour, brown sugar, and melted butter to create the crisp topping. Sprinkle the topping over the rhubarb mixture and bake until golden and bubbly.

3. Rhubarb Jam

Spread the tangy goodness of rhubarb jam on toast, scones, or even use it as a filling for pastries. Combine canned rhubarb with sugar, lemon zest, and a splash of water in a saucepan. Cook the mixture over medium heat until it thickens and reaches a jam-like consistency. Pour the hot jam into sterilized jars and seal for future enjoyment.

Conclusion

Canning rhubarb is not only a practical way to preserve this tart delight but also a gateway to a world of culinary possibilities. Whether you choose to enjoy it in a compote, crisp, or jam, canned rhubarb will always add a touch of tanginess and excitement to your dishes, no matter the season. So embrace the art of canning and savor the flavors of rhubarb all year round. Happy canning!
