The Delightful Taste of Japanese Shiso Tempura


Japanese cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors and textures, and one dish that stands out is shiso tempura. This delightful treat not only showcases the unique flavor of shiso leaves but also highlights the delicate art of tempura frying. If you’re looking to impress your friends and family or simply indulge in a culinary adventure, this guide will take you through the process of creating perfect shiso tempura.

What is Shiso?

Shiso, also known as perilla, is an herb that belongs to the mint family. Its leaves are vibrant green and can also be found in purple varieties. Shiso has a distinct flavor profile that can be described as a mix of mint, basil, and anise, making it an intriguing addition to various dishes. In Japanese cuisine, shiso is often used as a garnish, an ingredient in salads, or a wrapping for sushi.

The History of Tempura

Tempura has a rich history in Japan, believed to have originated from Portuguese missionaries in the 16th century. Initially, it was a method of cooking fish and vegetables in a light batter and frying them in oil. Over time, tempura evolved into the beloved dish we know today. The key to exceptional tempura lies in the batter, which should be light and crispy, allowing the natural flavors of the ingredients to shine.

Ingredients for Shiso Tempura

To prepare shiso tempura, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Tempura Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cold sparkling water (or ice-cold water)
  • 1 egg yolk (optional)
  • A pinch of salt

For the Shiso Tempura:

  • 20 fresh shiso leaves
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1 tablespoon grated daikon radish (optional)
  • A sprinkle of sesame seeds (optional)

Preparing the Shiso Leaves

Before diving into the tempura preparation, it’s essential to prepare your shiso leaves properly. Start by rinsing the leaves under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities. Gently pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel or use a salad spinner. It’s crucial to ensure the leaves are dry to achieve the perfect crispy texture when frying.

Making the Tempura Batter

Creating the ideal tempura batter is a vital step in ensuring your shiso tempura is light and crispy. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, sift the all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt. Sifting helps aerate the flour, resulting in a lighter batter.

  2. Combine with Liquid: In a separate bowl, whisk together the cold sparkling water and the egg yolk (if using). The sparkling water adds bubbles to the batter, contributing to its fluffiness.

  3. Blend Together: Pour the liquid mixture into the flour mixture and gently stir until just combined. It’s important not to overmix; a few lumps are perfectly fine. The key to tempura is to keep the batter cold and minimal.

Frying the Shiso Tempura

Now that you have your shiso leaves and batter ready, it’s time to fry them to perfection. Follow these steps for successful frying:

  1. Heat the Oil: In a deep skillet or frying pan, pour enough vegetable oil to submerge the shiso leaves. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches around 350°F (175°C). To test if the oil is ready, drop a small amount of batter into the oil. If it sizzles and rises to the surface, it’s time to start frying.

  2. Batter the Shiso Leaves: Dip each shiso leaf into the tempura batter, ensuring it’s fully coated. Allow any excess batter to drip off.

  3. Fry the Leaves: Carefully place the battered shiso leaves into the hot oil, a few at a time. Avoid overcrowding the pan, as this can lower the oil temperature and result in soggy tempura. Fry for about 2-3 minutes or until the leaves turn golden brown and crispy.

  4. Drain Excess Oil: Using a slotted spoon, remove the fried shiso tempura from the oil and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil.

Preparing the Dipping Sauce

A delicious dipping sauce elevates your shiso tempura experience. Here’s a quick recipe for a simple yet flavorful sauce:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce and mirin. Mix well to combine.
  2. If desired, add the grated daikon radish for a refreshing touch.
  3. Sprinkle sesame seeds on top for added texture and flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Present your shiso tempura beautifully on a serving platter. You can garnish it with additional shiso leaves or serve it alongside a bowl of dipping sauce for an authentic touch. Pair it with a chilled glass of sake or green tea to complete your Japanese culinary experience.

Tips for Perfect Shiso Tempura

  • Keep Everything Cold: For the crispiest tempura, ensure that your batter ingredients are cold. You can even chill your mixing bowl before preparing the batter.
  • Monitor Oil Temperature: Use a thermometer to keep track of the oil temperature. If the oil is too hot, the batter will burn; if it’s too cool, the tempura will absorb too much oil.
  • Experiment with Other Ingredients: While shiso leaves are extraordinary, feel free to experiment with other vegetables, such as sweet potatoes, zucchini, or mushrooms, to create a varied tempura platter.

Conclusion

Shiso tempura is a delightful dish that encapsulates the essence of Japanese cuisine—a perfect balance of flavors and textures. With its crispy exterior and aromatic herb filling, this dish is sure to impress anyone who tries it. Whether served as an appetizer, a side dish, or a part of a larger meal, shiso tempura is a versatile addition to your culinary repertoire.

Now that you have all the knowledge and tips to create this delightful treat, grab your ingredients and start frying! Enjoy the process, and savor the delightful taste of Japanese shiso tempura in the comfort of your own home.
