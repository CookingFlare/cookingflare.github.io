How to Make Greek Kefta Burgers on the Grill


If you’re looking to spice up your barbecue game this summer, look no further than Greek Kefta Burgers. These flavorful, juicy burgers are made from a mixture of ground meat and aromatic spices, giving you a taste of Mediterranean cuisine right in your backyard. Perfect for grilling, these burgers are quick to prepare and will impress your family and friends with their unique flavor profile. Let’s dive into how to make these delicious Greek Kefta Burgers on the grill!

What Are Kefta Burgers?

Kefta, or kefta, are flavorful meatballs or patties that originate from Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cuisines. Traditionally made with ground lamb or beef, they are seasoned with a variety of herbs and spices, which give them a distinctive taste. When transformed into burgers, Kefta delivers an exciting twist to the classic American barbecue.

Ingredients for Greek Kefta Burgers

To make your own Greek Kefta Burgers, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Kefta Patties

  • 1 lb ground beef (or a mix of beef and lamb for more flavor)
  • 1 small onion, finely grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon (optional, for warmth)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (to brush on the grill)

For Serving

  • Pita bread or burger buns
  • Tzatziki sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Feta cheese, crumbled (optional)

Equipment You’ll Need

  • Grill (gas or charcoal)
  • Mixing bowl
  • Measuring spoons
  • Measuring cups
  • Grill spatula
  • Meat thermometer (optional, but recommended)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Greek Kefta Burgers

Step 1: Prepare the Kefta Mixture

  1. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground meat, grated onion, minced garlic, chopped parsley, and mint. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. Add the Spices: Sprinkle in the ground cumin, coriander, paprika, and cinnamon (if using). Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Use your hands to mix everything together until you have a cohesive mixture. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the burgers tough.

Step 2: Form the Patties

  1. Shape the Patties: Divide the mixture into equal portions (about 4 to 6, depending on your preferred burger size). Wet your hands slightly to prevent sticking, and shape each portion into a patty about 1/2 inch thick.

  2. Chill the Patties: Place the patties on a plate and cover them with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before grilling. This helps the patties hold their shape and enhances the flavors.

Step 3: Preheat the Grill

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (around 400°F to 450°F). If you’re using a charcoal grill, allow the coals to burn down until they’re covered in white ash.

  2. Oil the Grill Grates: Brush the grill grates with olive oil to prevent sticking. This is especially important for the Kefta burgers, as they contain less fat than traditional beef burgers.

Step 4: Grill the Kefta Burgers

  1. Grill the Patties: Carefully place the patties on the preheated grill. Grill for about 4 to 5 minutes on one side without moving them. This helps to develop a nice crust.

  2. Flip and Cook: Using a grill spatula, gently flip the patties over. Grill for another 4 to 5 minutes on the other side. The burgers should be browned on the outside and cooked to your desired doneness on the inside. You can use a meat thermometer to check for doneness; the internal temperature should reach 160°F for ground beef.

Step 5: Assemble Your Burgers

  1. Prepare the Pita or Buns: While the burgers are grilling, warm pita bread or burger buns on the grill for a minute or two until lightly toasted.

  2. Add Toppings: Once the Kefta burgers are cooked, place them on the toasted pita or buns. Top with tzatziki sauce, sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, red onion, and crumbled feta cheese if desired.

  3. Serve Immediately: Serve the burgers hot with your favorite side dishes, such as Greek salad, potato wedges, or grilled vegetables.

Tips for Perfect Kefta Burgers

  • Meat Choice: While ground beef is commonly used, a mix of ground lamb and beef can add even more flavor to your Kefta burgers. Adjust the seasoning according to the meat you choose.

  • Customize Your Spices: Feel free to adjust the spices based on your preferences. Adding chili flakes can give your burgers a spicy kick, while fresh herbs can add brightness.

  • Make Ahead: Kefta patties can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours or frozen for up to 3 months. Just ensure they are well-wrapped to avoid freezer burn.

  • Cooking Method: If you don’t have a grill, you can also cook Kefta burgers in a skillet or under a broiler. Just ensure the patties are cooked through.

Conclusion

Greek Kefta Burgers are a fantastic way to bring a taste of the Mediterranean to your summer grilling. With their aromatic spices and juicy texture, these burgers are sure to be a hit at your next barbecue. The combination of fresh toppings and tzatziki adds a refreshing element that complements the warm spices perfectly.

Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or simply craving something different for dinner, these Kefta burgers are easy to make and incredibly satisfying. So fire up the grill and enjoy a delicious meal that transports you to the sunny shores of Greece!
