Thai Chicken Satay Recipe with Peanut Sauce


If you’re looking to add some bold and vibrant flavors to your culinary repertoire, then Thai chicken satay with peanut sauce is a must-try dish. This classic Thai street food is a crowd-pleaser, featuring tender, marinated chicken skewers served with a rich and creamy peanut dipping sauce. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of Thailand, this recipe is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the details of creating this delicious and aromatic dish.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Satay:

  • 1.5 lbs (680g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon (5ml) turmeric
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) vegetable oil
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

For the Peanut Sauce:

  • 1 cup (240ml) coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup (120g) creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) red curry paste
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon (5ml) fish sauce

Instructions

  1. Begin by preparing the chicken. Cut the chicken thighs into thin, evenly-sized strips and place them in a bowl.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the coconut milk, soy sauce, fish sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, curry powder, turmeric, and minced garlic. Mix well to create the marinade.

  3. Pour the marinade over the chicken strips, ensuring that each piece is well coated. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse.

  4. While the chicken is marinating, it’s time to prepare the peanut sauce. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the coconut milk, peanut butter, red curry paste, soy sauce, and brown sugar. Whisk the ingredients together until the sauce is smooth and well combined.

  5. Bring the peanut sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the sauce has thickened slightly, remove it from the heat and stir in the lime juice and fish sauce. Set the peanut sauce aside to cool.

  6. When you’re ready to cook the chicken, preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.

  7. Thread the marinated chicken strips onto the soaked bamboo skewers, shaking off any excess marinade. Brush the chicken with vegetable oil to prevent sticking.

  8. Place the chicken skewers on the preheated grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has a slightly charred exterior.

  9. Once the chicken is cooked, transfer the skewers to a serving platter and garnish with chopped cilantro and a sprinkle of sesame seeds.

  10. Serve the Thai chicken satay with the prepared peanut sauce on the side for dipping, and enjoy the explosion of flavors with each bite.

Tips and Tricks

  • To achieve tender and juicy chicken, avoid overcooking the satay skewers.
  • If you’re using wooden skewers, remember to soak them in water beforehand to prevent burning on the grill.
  • Feel free to customize the level of spiciness in the peanut sauce by adjusting the amount of red curry paste to suit your taste preferences.

Final Thoughts

Thai chicken satay with peanut sauce is a delightful combination of savory, sweet, and tangy flavors that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Thailand. Whether you’re grilling up a storm at a summer barbecue or simply indulging in a flavorful weeknight dinner, this recipe is a winner. Embrace the aromatic spices, creamy peanut goodness, and succulent chicken to elevate your culinary experience with this authentic Thai dish. So, fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the irresistible allure of Thai chicken satay with peanut sauce.
