Middle Eastern Shawarma Recipe with Lamb and Tzatziki Sauce


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve likely encountered the tantalizing aroma and flavors of shawarma. This iconic dish is a staple street food in the Middle East, known for its succulent, spiced meat and creamy sauces. While shawarma is traditionally made with chicken or beef, today we’re going to put a unique twist on this classic recipe by preparing it with tender lamb and serving it with a refreshing tzatziki sauce. Get ready to embark on a culinary journey to the heart of the Middle East as we explore the art of making lamb shawarma with a delightful tzatziki twist.

Ingredients

For the Lamb Shawarma:

  • 2 pounds of boneless lamb shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tzatziki Sauce:

  • 1 cucumber, grated and drained
  • 1 1/2 cups of Greek yogurt
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Warm pita bread
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Chopped fresh parsley

Instructions

Marinating the Lamb:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the thinly sliced lamb, red onion, minced garlic, olive oil, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Toss the ingredients until the lamb is well coated with the spices. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Preparing the Tzatziki Sauce:

  1. Start by grating the cucumber and then place it in a fine-mesh sieve. Sprinkle the grated cucumber with a pinch of salt and let it sit for 10 minutes to release excess moisture. Afterward, press the cucumber to remove any remaining liquid.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the grated and drained cucumber, Greek yogurt, minced garlic, fresh dill, fresh mint, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Stir the ingredients until well combined, then cover the bowl and refrigerate the tzatziki sauce until ready to serve.

Cooking the Lamb:

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated lamb and cook for 5-6 minutes, or until the meat is tender and caramelized, stirring occasionally.
  2. Once the lamb is cooked through, remove the skillet from the heat and allow the meat to rest for a few minutes.

Assembling the Shawarma:

  1. Warm the pita bread in a dry skillet or microwave.
  2. Place a generous portion of the cooked lamb on the warm pita bread, then top it with sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and a dollop of tzatziki sauce.
  3. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley and fold the pita bread to create a delicious shawarma wrap.

Serving and Enjoying:

Now that your lamb shawarma with tzatziki sauce is ready, it’s time to savor the delightful flavors of this Middle Eastern masterpiece. Serve the shawarma wraps alongside a crisp salad or some roasted vegetables for a complete and satisfying meal. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the Middle East, this lamb shawarma recipe is sure to impress your guests and tantalize your taste buds.

Conclusion

The art of making lamb shawarma with tzatziki sauce is a celebration of Middle Eastern flavors and culinary traditions. The tender, spiced lamb combined with the cool, creamy tzatziki sauce creates a harmonious balance of textures and tastes, making it a truly irresistible dish. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the aromas of the Middle East, and embark on a culinary adventure with this exquisite lamb shawarma recipe. Whether you’re an experienced home cook or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to elevate your cooking repertoire and leave a lasting impression on anyone who has the pleasure of enjoying it. Bon appétit!
