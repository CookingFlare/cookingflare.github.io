Paleo Meatloaf with Ketchup Glaze: A Hearty and Delicious Recipe for Fall


As the leaves start to change colors and the crisp autumn air sets in, our taste buds begin to crave heartier, comforting dishes that warm the soul. One classic recipe that never goes out of style is meatloaf, and today, we’re putting a Paleo twist on this beloved favorite. Introducing Paleo Meatloaf with Ketchup Glaze, a recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your fall cooking repertoire.

The Perfect Blend of Flavors

This Paleo Meatloaf is a masterclass in balance and harmony. The combination of ground beef, pork, and turkey ensures a rich, meaty flavor profile, while the addition of sautéed onions, garlic, and mushrooms adds an depth of umami. Meanwhile, the ketchup glaze provides a tangy, sweet contrast that elevates the dish to new heights.

The Recipe

To make this Paleo Meatloaf, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 pound ground turkey
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and button), sliced
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown, about 5 minutes.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, ground turkey, cooked onion and mushroom mixture, egg, almond flour, salt, black pepper, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Mix well with your hands or a wooden spoon until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Transfer the meat mixture to a loaf pan or shape it into a loaf shape on a baking sheet. Smooth the top with your hands or a spatula.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, and a pinch of salt. Brush the glaze all over the top of the meatloaf.
  7. Place the meatloaf in the oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C).
  8. Remove the meatloaf from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

The Verdict

This Paleo Meatloaf with Ketchup Glaze is a game-changer. The combination of flavors and textures is nothing short of magic, and the ketchup glaze adds a sweet and tangy element that elevates the dish to new heights. Whether you’re serving it for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your fall cooking repertoire.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, top the meatloaf with some chopped bacon or chopped fresh herbs like parsley or thyme before baking.
  • If you prefer a leaner meatloaf, you can substitute the ground pork with additional ground turkey or beef.
  • To make this recipe gluten-free, be sure to use gluten-free almond flour.
  • This recipe freezes well, so feel free to make it ahead of time and freeze it for up to 3 months. Simply thaw it overnight in the fridge and reheat it in the oven before serving.

With its rich flavors and comforting warmth, this Paleo Meatloaf with Ketchup Glaze is the perfect recipe to cozy up to this fall. Give it a try and experience the magic for yourself!
