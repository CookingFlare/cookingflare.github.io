Festive Thanksgiving Pancake Recipes for a Fun Breakfast


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. While the traditional Thanksgiving feast often centers around turkey, stuffing, and cranberry sauce, why not kick off the day with a festive twist on breakfast? This year, surprise your loved ones with a fun and flavorful Thanksgiving pancake breakfast. From pumpkin spice to apple cinnamon, these pancake recipes are sure to set the mood for a memorable morning. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, buttermilk, egg, and melted butter. Mix until well combined.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
  5. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Serve the pumpkin spice pancakes with warm maple syrup.

2. Apple Cinnamon Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 1/4 cups milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 cup grated apple
  • Caramel sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the milk, egg, melted butter, and grated apple. Mix until well combined.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Again, avoid overmixing.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
  5. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Serve the apple cinnamon pancakes with a drizzle of warm caramel sauce.

3. Cranberry Orange Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the milk, orange zest, orange juice, egg, and melted butter. Mix until well combined.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  4. Fold in the dried cranberries.
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Dust the cranberry orange pancakes with powdered sugar before serving.

Final Thoughts

These festive Thanksgiving pancake recipes are a delightful way to start your holiday morning. Whether you prefer the warm flavors of pumpkin spice, the comforting combination of apple and cinnamon, or the tangy sweetness of cranberry and orange, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, gather your loved ones around the breakfast table and savor the flavors of the season. Happy Thanksgiving!
