Sous Vide Maple Glazed Carrots: A Sweet and Buttery Side


Carrots are a versatile vegetable that can be prepared in many delicious ways. One method that brings out their natural sweetness and preserves their vibrant color is sous vide cooking. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating tender and flavorful Sous Vide Maple Glazed Carrots. The result is a side dish that is both sweet and buttery, making it the perfect complement to any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of carrots, peeled and cut into uniform pieces
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your water bath to 185°F (85°C).

  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrots with the butter, maple syrup, salt, pepper, and cinnamon (if using). Make sure the carrots are evenly coated.

  3. Transfer the seasoned carrots into a vacuum-sealed bag. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  4. Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, carefully place the bag of carrots into the water. Make sure the bag is fully submerged.

  5. Cook the carrots sous vide for 1 hour. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the carrots to become tender.

  6. After 1 hour, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully open it. Be cautious of any hot steam that may escape.

  7. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Transfer the cooked carrots from the bag to the skillet using a slotted spoon or tongs. Reserve the cooking liquid.

  8. Cook the carrots in the skillet for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they start to caramelize and develop a slightly golden color.

  9. Pour the reserved cooking liquid into the skillet and continue cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes, allowing the liquid to reduce and glaze the carrots.

  10. Remove the skillet from heat and transfer the glazed carrots to a serving dish.

  11. Serve the Sous Vide Maple Glazed Carrots as a delicious side dish alongside your favorite main course. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, try adding a pinch of nutmeg or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to the carrots before sealing them in the bag.
  • If you prefer a sweeter glaze, you can increase the amount of maple syrup in the recipe.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile of the glazed carrots. Thyme, rosemary, or even a hint of cayenne pepper can add a unique twist.
  • If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a zip-top bag and the water displacement method. Simply place the carrots and seasoning in the bag, seal it most of the way, then lower it into the water bath, allowing the water to push out the remaining air before sealing it completely.
  • Leftover glazed carrots can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat them in a skillet over medium heat until warmed through.

Sous Vide Maple Glazed Carrots are a delightful side dish that brings out the natural sweetness of the carrots. The sous vide cooking method ensures that they are perfectly tender, while the maple glaze adds a touch of richness and depth of flavor. Whether you’re serving them alongside a holiday roast or a simple weeknight dinner, these sweet and buttery carrots are sure to impress. Enjoy the simplicity and deliciousness of this recipe!
