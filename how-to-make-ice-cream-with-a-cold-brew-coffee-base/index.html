How to Make Ice Cream with a Cold Brew Coffee Base


Who can resist the creamy decadence of homemade ice cream? Pairing it with the robust and smooth flavor of cold brew coffee takes this frozen treat to an entirely new level of sophistication. Cold brew coffee is celebrated for its mellow, slightly sweet taste, and when used as the foundation for ice cream, it offers a fantastic blend of indulgence and caffeine—perfect for coffee lovers everywhere. In this guide, we’ll walk through the steps to create an irresistible homemade cold brew coffee ice cream that you can enjoy any time of the year.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Creating this ice cream requires a mix of pantry staples and the cold brew itself, which will serve as the flavor powerhouse for the recipe. Here’s what you need:

For the Cold Brew Coffee Base:

  • 1 cup of coarsely ground coffee beans (choose a medium or dark roast for more depth of flavor)
  • 4 cups of cold water

For the Ice Cream:

  • 1 cup of cold brew coffee (prepared in advance)
  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of pure vanilla extract
  • 6 large egg yolks (for a custard-based ice cream)
  • A pinch of salt

Step 1: Create the Cold Brew Coffee

Cold brew coffee is the star of this recipe, and it’s essential to prepare it correctly in order to extract all the nuanced flavors. Unlike iced coffee made with hot brewing methods, cold brew takes time but rewards you with a smoother finish.

Instructions:

  1. Combine the coarsely ground coffee beans with 4 cups of cold water in a large jar or pitcher. Stir the mixture gently to ensure the grounds are fully submerged.
  2. Cover the jar or pitcher and let the coffee steep at room temperature or in the refrigerator for 12-24 hours. The longer it steeps, the stronger the flavor will be.
  3. Strain the coffee using a fine mesh sieve or a coffee filter to remove the grounds. The result is a beautifully rich cold brew concentrate. Set aside 1 cup of this cold brew for the ice cream, and reserve the rest for another use (think iced coffee or additional recipes!).

Step 2: Make the Ice Cream Base

The hallmark of truly wonderful ice cream is a luscious custard base. This step incorporates the cold brew coffee while ensuring the texture remains silky and satisfying.

Instructions:

  1. Whisk the egg yolks and sugar: In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until the mixture becomes pale and fluffy. This step is crucial for achieving the signature richness of custard-based ice cream.
  2. Heat the milk and cream: In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and a pinch of salt. Set the heat to medium and warm the mixture until it’s steaming but not boiling, stirring occasionally to prevent scorching. Add the vanilla extract and cold brew coffee, stirring until fully incorporated.
  3. Temper the eggs: Gradually pour about 1 cup of the warm milk mixture into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly. This step, called tempering, prevents the eggs from scrambling when combined with the hot liquid.
  4. Combine and cook: Pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan with the rest of the milk mixture. Cook over low heat while stirring continuously with a wooden spoon or silicone spatula. The goal is to thicken the mixture into a custard—this happens when it reaches around 170–175°F or is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  5. Strain and cool: Once your custard base is ready, strain it through a fine mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any cooked egg bits. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly against the surface to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the custard in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or until fully cooled.

Step 3: Churn the Ice Cream

Here’s where the magic happens—turning the coffee-infused custard base into creamy ice cream.

Instructions:

  1. Prepare your ice cream maker: Ensure your ice cream machine bowl is properly frozen (if required by your model) before starting. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. Churn the ice cream: Pour the chilled custard mixture into the ice cream maker and churn according to the machine’s settings. Depending on your model, this step typically takes 20–30 minutes.
  3. Achieve your desired consistency: At this stage, the ice cream will have a soft-serve texture. Transfer it into an airtight container and freeze for several hours or overnight, allowing it to firm up. This step ensures a scoopable consistency.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy!

The beauty of homemade ice cream lies in its versatility. Whether served solo, paired with cookies, or topped with chocolate or caramel sauce, cold brew coffee ice cream is bound to impress with its bold flavor and velvety texture.

For extra flair, consider garnishing your ice cream with chocolate shavings, crushed coffee beans for crunch, or even a swirl of whipped cream. Each bite delivers a harmonious blend of rich coffee flavor and creamy sweetness.

Tips for Perfect Cold Brew Coffee Ice Cream

  • Choose the right coffee beans: A dark roast or espresso blend works beautifully, but feel free to experiment with lighter roasts if you prefer subtler coffee notes.
  • Don’t rush the steeping process: Cold brew coffee requires time, so don’t shortcut the brewing period. Proper extraction ensures the coffee’s low acidity.
  • Experiment with flavors: Add a touch of cocoa powder, cinnamon, or even a splash of coffee liqueur to complement the cold brew coffee base.
  • Use quality ingredients: Because this recipe relies heavily on the flavor of the coffee, high-quality beans will make a noticeable difference.
  • Storage matters: Store your ice cream in an airtight container to prevent ice crystals from forming and preserve its texture.

Final Thoughts

Making ice cream with a cold brew coffee base marries the irresistible creaminess of homemade ice cream with the bold, nuanced flavor of cold brew coffee. It’s the perfect treat for coffee enthusiasts craving a caffeinated dessert option or anyone looking to elevate their ice cream game. Once you master this recipe, you can personalize it to suit your taste preferences and share it with friends and family, earning you plenty of requests for more!

Homemade cold brew coffee ice cream isn’t just a dessert; it’s a culinary experience—a blend of rich, smooth, and utterly delightful flavors that will leave everyone asking for seconds.
