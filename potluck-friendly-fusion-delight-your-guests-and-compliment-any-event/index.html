1. Charred Corn & Tomato Salad Salsa Fusion

Who says salsa can’t be artistic and Artesian?

Ingredients:

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cups fresh corn kernels
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat your grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the corn with a little olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the corn, turning every once in a while until it’s charred all over. The smoke signals, my friends, are a sign of flavor.
  4. Once it’s grilled, let it cool down to room temperature before cutting the kernels off the cobs.
  5. Toss the tomatoes, corn, onion, cilantro, lime juice, and remaining olive oil together in a large bowl. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Mix in the zest of the lime for that extra Zest of life.
  6. Pile it onto a platter and serve as your primary colorful addition to the potluck array!

2. Heirloom Quinoa Salad

Bring a little loom to your bowl!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth (or water)
  • 1/2 cup red quinoa
  • 1/2 cup black quinoa
  • 1/2 cup white quinoa
  • 2 cups colorful heirloom cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted

Instructions:

  1. Rinse your quinoa and then cook it in 2 1/2 cups of vegetable broth or water (it’s perfect for vegetable broth, allowing the quinoa to absorb the flavor). Cook as you would rice, bringing it to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and let it simmer for about 12 minutes. Fluff with a fork and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. In a big bowl, combine the three quinoa varieties, blending them like a beautiful stew of life itself.
  3. Add the tomatoes, cucumber, red bell pepper, herbs, lemon zest, and juice, then pour the olive oil over top.
  4. Season to taste with salt and pepper, then fold everything together gently.
  5. Sprinkle with the toasted, golden pine nuts for a divine crunch.

3. Fusion Fire Roasted Vegetables

A burst of flavor by the fire!

Ingredients:

  • 2 bell peppers (any combination of colors)
  • 2 portobello mushrooms, stems removed and sliced
  • 1 large eggplant, cubed
  • 3 ears of corn, shucked
  • 1 red onion, cut in wedges
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh herbs (rosemary, thyme, and oregano)
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan. Rub the vegetables with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of fresh herbs.
  2. Fire-roast your assorted veggies over the high heat of the grill, basting them with the remaining oil and turning occasionally until tender and slightly charred. The smokey layer on the vegetables tells the story of the feast that is about to begin.
  3. Once cooked, let them cool a bit then slice the vegetables into chunks.
  4. Drizzle your roasted vegetable bounty with balsamic vinegar and serve in a beautifully arranged platter.

4. Fused-up Vegan Falafels

An adventure to a far east in every bite!

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup frozen corn
  • 1/2 cup canned black beans, drained
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons tahini paste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Pita bread, lettuce leaves, or toothpicks

Instructions:

  1. Place the chickpeas, corn, black beans, garlic, onion, parsley, cilantro, tahini, olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, lemon juice, and salt and pepper in a food processor and pulse until well combined.
  2. Form the mixture into small patties, packing them down firmly to ensure they hold together well on the grill.
  3. Heat the remaining tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Cook the patties until crispy and heated through.
