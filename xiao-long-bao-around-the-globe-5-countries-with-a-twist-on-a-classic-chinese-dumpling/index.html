Xiao Long Bao Around the Globe: 5 Countries with a Twist on a Classic Chinese Dumpling


Xiao Long Bao, or soup dumplings, not only come in various forms within China itself, but they’ve also been embraced across the world. These delicate and scrumptious dumplings pair tender pork or crab meat with a rich and flavorful broth inside a thin and delicate wrapping. As people grow more and more enthusiastic about international cuisine, inspiring chefs around the globe are experimenting with this well-loved dish, infusing it with unique flavors and ingredients that breathe new life into the classic.

Let’s take a culinary journey through five countries and explore their twist on the traditional xiao long bao recipe.

Local Twist #1: Saffron Xiao Long Bao - India

India, with its rich and diverse culinary landscape, has warmly welcomed the xiao long bao. In their version, chefs embellish the dumpling with saffron from the Kashmir region, known for its unique fragrance and culinary value. To harmonize the delicate floral flavor with the rich pork filling, spices like cumin and coriander are added to the dough for a touch of local heat.

Local Twist #2: Emmental Xiao Long Bao - Switzerland

Who would have thought that the xiao long bao would even make its way to the land of majestic mountains and delicious Swiss chocolate? Emmentaler, the iconic cheese from Switzerland, adds a unique twist to the soup dumpling. The chefs blend chunks of the melted, tangy cheese within the savory pork filling, elevating the creamy taste and texture experience while keeping the classic dough intact.

Local Twist #3: Haggis Xiao Long Bao - Scotland

Taking inspiration from the famous Scottish haggis, local chefs have infused a distinctly Scottish touch into the humble xiao long bao. Known for its intense, earthy flavors, haggis is made from sheep’s heart, liver, and lungs, mixed with oats, spices, and traditionally encased in a sheep’s stomach. To create the perfect fusion, this unlikely ingredient is incorporated into the filling, which is further enriched by Scottish whisky and a generous dollop of mustard.

Local Twist #4: Sour Cream and Caviar Xiao Long Bao - Russia

The land of lovely winters and black caviar offers yet another interesting take on xiao long bao. By wrapping the dumplings in a thin dough enriched with sour cream, the dish takes on a luxurious twist. Furthermore, bursts of caviar add a delightful briny taste alongside the typical pork filling, making every bite of this dumpling a royal feast.

Local Twist #5: Wasabi Xiao Long Bao - Japan

As East meets West, the world of flavors comes together cohesively with the Japanese-inspired version of the xiao long bao. The inclusion of wasabi creates an unmatched adrenaline rush for the taste buds. At the same time, the earthiness of the filling and the zesty pop of flavor complement the pickled radish, oroshi, that traditionally accompanies the dish.

Savor the xiao long bao journey around the globe with these globally-inspired recipes, adding a unique touch to an already loved classic. Each twist on the traditional dumpling will excite your taste buds and invite you to explore a world of flavors that burst from the familiar wrapper. Let this adventure inspire you to create further variations and showcase both local and international inspirations in your personal kitchen.

Get ready to bring a taste of the world into your plate with one clever gastronomical experience – Xiao Long Bao, traverse the globe, all on a single bite!
