Sous Vide Garlic Herb Butter Steak: A Flavorful Indulgence

Are you ready to take your steak game to the next level? Look no further than this mouthwatering Sous Vide Garlic Herb Butter Steak recipe. With its tender and juicy texture, infused with the flavors of garlic, herbs, and butter, this steak is sure to impress even the most discerning palates. Ingredients 1.5 pounds of steak (preferably ribeye or New York strip) 4 cloves of garlic, minced 2 tablespoons of fresh herbs (such as rosemary, thyme, or parsley), finely chopped 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter, softened Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Preheat your sous vide water bath to 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare steak, or adjust the temperature according to your preference. Season the steak generously with salt and pepper on both sides. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped herbs, and softened butter. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly incorporated. Rub the garlic herb butter mixture all over the steak, ensuring that every inch is coated with the flavorful goodness. Place the seasoned steak in a vacuum-sealable bag or a ziplock bag, making sure to remove as much air as possible before sealing. Carefully place the bagged steak into the preheated water bath, ensuring that it is fully submerged. Cook the steak in the sous vide water bath for 1 to 2 hours, depending on the desired level of doneness. Remember, the longer you cook it, the more tender the steak will become. Once the cooking time is up, remove the steak from the water bath and carefully take it out of the bag. Preheat a cast-iron skillet or grill over high heat. Sear the steak for 1 to 2 minutes on each side, or until a beautiful crust forms. Transfer the steak to a cutting board and let it rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute. Slice the steak against the grain into thick, juicy slices. Serve your Sous Vide Garlic Herb Butter Steak with your favorite sides, such as roasted potatoes and sautéed vegetables. Enjoy the flavorful indulgence! Tips and Tricks Experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile of your steak. Consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper for a hint of heat or a squeeze of lemon juice to brighten the flavors. For an extra burst of flavor, allow the steak to marinate in the garlic herb butter mixture for a few hours or overnight before cooking it sous vide. If you don’t have a sous vide machine, you can still achieve a similar result by using a digital thermometer to monitor the internal temperature of the steak as it cooks in a low-temperature oven. Don’t skip the searing step. It adds a delicious caramelized crust to the steak and enhances the overall flavor and appearance. In Conclusion Sous Vide Garlic Herb Butter Steak is the epitome of a flavorful indulgence. By cooking the steak sous vide, you ensure that every bite is tender, juicy, and perfectly cooked to your liking. The garlic herb butter infusion adds a depth of flavor that elevates this classic dish to new heights.
