Tasty Thanksgiving Bread Stuffing Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. One dish that always takes center stage on the Thanksgiving table is the beloved bread stuffing. This classic side dish is a must-have for many, and with good reason. The combination of flavorful bread, aromatic herbs, and savory ingredients makes for a truly scrumptious addition to any Thanksgiving feast. In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering bread stuffing recipes that are sure to impress your family and friends this holiday season.

1. Traditional Herb and Sausage Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of day-old French bread, cubed
  • 1 pound of ground sausage
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the onion, celery, and garlic until they become tender and fragrant.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread cubes, cooked sausage, sautéed vegetables, parsley, sage, and thyme. Mix well.
  5. Gradually add the chicken broth to the bread mixture, stirring until the bread is moistened but not soggy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish.
  7. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Cornbread and Cranberry Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of cornbread mix, prepared according to package instructions and cooled
  • 1 cup of dried cranberries
  • 1 cup of pecans, chopped
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Crumble the cooled cornbread into a large mixing bowl.
  3. Add the dried cranberries, chopped pecans, diced onion, diced celery, minced garlic, parsley, rosemary, and thyme to the bowl. Mix well.
  4. Gradually add the chicken broth to the cornbread mixture, stirring until the mixture is moistened but not soggy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish.
  6. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

3. Mushroom and Herb Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of day-old Italian bread, cubed
  • 1 pound of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the mushrooms, onion, celery, and garlic, and sauté until they become tender and golden brown.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread cubes, sautéed mushrooms and vegetables, parsley, thyme, and rosemary. Mix well.
  4. Gradually add the vegetable broth to the bread mixture, stirring until the bread is moistened but not soggy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish.
  6. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

These three bread stuffing recipes are guaranteed to elevate your Thanksgiving feast to new heights. Whether you prefer the classic combination of herbs and sausage, the sweet and tangy flavors of cornbread and cranberries, or the earthy richness of mushrooms and herbs, there’s a stuffing recipe here that will satisfy every palate. So gather your ingredients, tie on your apron, and get ready to create a Thanksgiving meal that will leave everyone craving more. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
