Charming Apricot Almond Bars: A Fruity Delight


If you’re a fan of fruity, sweet treats, then you’re in for a real treat with this recipe for Charming Apricot Almond Bars. These delightful bars are the perfect combination of tangy apricot and nutty almond flavors, all wrapped up in a buttery, crumbly crust. Whether you’re hosting a summer picnic, a brunch, or just want to indulge in a delicious dessert, these bars are sure to impress. So, let’s jump right into it and whip up a batch of these charming apricot almond bars!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups dried apricots, chopped
  • 1/2 cup apricot preserves
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on two sides for easy removal later.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, almond extract, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Press the crust mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Set aside to cool while you prepare the filling.

Step 2: Making the Apricot Filling

  1. In a saucepan, combine the chopped dried apricots, apricot preserves, granulated sugar, water, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the mixture comes to a simmer.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and continue to cook for 10-12 minutes, or until the apricots are softened and the mixture has thickened. Stir in the almond extract and remove the filling from the heat.

Step 3: Assembling the Bars

  1. Spread the apricot filling evenly over the cooled crust. Sprinkle the sliced almonds over the top of the filling, gently pressing them down.
  2. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the almonds are lightly toasted.
  3. Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan before using the parchment paper overhang to lift them out. Transfer to a cutting board and slice into bars of your desired size.

Serving and Storing

These Charming Apricot Almond Bars are best enjoyed at room temperature. They make a delightful addition to any dessert spread and pair beautifully with a cup of tea or coffee. Store any leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Final Thoughts

With their delightful balance of fruity sweetness and nutty crunch, these Charming Apricot Almond Bars are sure to become a favorite in your baking repertoire. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to the world of homemade treats, this recipe is a wonderful way to showcase the delicious flavors of apricot and almond. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to charm your taste buds with these delightful bars!

So, what are you waiting for? Let’s get baking and indulge in this fruity delight!
