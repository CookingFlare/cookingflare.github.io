Delicious Tofu Banh Mi: A Vegan Take on a Vietnamese Classic


Are you craving a flavorful and satisfying sandwich that’s both vegan and packed with delicious Vietnamese flavors? Look no further than this recipe for Delicious Tofu Banh Mi! This vegan twist on a classic Vietnamese sandwich will leave your taste buds tingling and your belly happy. With the perfect balance of textures and flavors, it’s a recipe that’s sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

For the pickled vegetables:

  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 daikon radish, julienned

For the tofu marinade:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha (optional)
  • 1 block firm tofu, sliced into 1/2-inch thick slabs

For the sandwich:

  • Baguette, cut into individual sandwich-sized pieces
  • Vegan mayonnaise
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Thinly sliced jalapenos (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Pickle the vegetables

In a small bowl, whisk together the rice vinegar, water, sugar, and salt until the sugar and salt have dissolved. Add the julienned carrot and daikon radish, making sure they are fully submerged in the pickling liquid. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it sit at room temperature for at least 1 hour, or refrigerate overnight for more intense flavor.

Step 2: Marinate the tofu

In another bowl, combine the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, lime juice, minced garlic, sesame oil, maple syrup, and sriracha (if using). Whisk until well combined. Place the tofu slabs in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, making sure they are fully coated. Let the tofu marinate for at least 30 minutes, or longer if desired.

Step 3: Cook the tofu

Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly grease it with cooking spray or a small amount of oil. Remove the tofu from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off, and place it in the pan. Cook the tofu for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until it is golden brown and slightly crispy.

Step 4: Assemble the sandwich

Slice the baguette pieces lengthwise, leaving one side intact to create a pocket. Spread a generous amount of vegan mayonnaise on both sides of the baguette. Place a few slices of the cooked tofu inside the pocket, followed by a handful of pickled vegetables. Top with fresh cilantro leaves and thinly sliced jalapenos if you like a bit of heat. Repeat with the remaining baguette pieces.

Step 5: Enjoy!

Serve your Delicious Tofu Banh Mi sandwiches immediately and enjoy the explosion of flavors and textures. The combination of the tangy pickled vegetables, savory tofu, and fresh herbs is truly a delight for the senses. These sandwiches make a great lunch or dinner option, and they are perfect for picnics or gatherings with friends.

Conclusion

Delicious Tofu Banh Mi is a vegan take on a Vietnamese classic that truly delivers on taste and satisfaction. With its flavorful marinated tofu, tangy pickled vegetables, and fresh herbs, it’s a sandwich that will impress vegans and non-vegans alike. Plus, it’s easy to make and customizable to suit your taste preferences. So why not give this recipe a try and add a new favorite to your repertoire? Your taste buds will thank you!
