Baking with Apples: 6 Delicious Fall Recipes


As the crisp air of fall settles in, the kitchen becomes a haven for cozy, comforting aromas. One of the most beloved seasonal ingredients is the humble apple. With its sweet-tart flavor and versatility, apples make the perfect addition to any fall recipe. Whether you prefer them baked in a pie, nestled in a cake, or paired with buttery streusel, apples bring a burst of flavor that perfectly complements the autumn season.

In this post, we will explore six mouthwatering fall recipes that celebrate apples in their most delicious forms. From traditional classics like apple pie to creative twists on the original, there’s something here for everyone. So, preheat your oven and get ready to fill your home with the irresistible smell of baked apples.

1. Classic Apple Pie

There’s no dessert more synonymous with fall than a warm, flaky apple pie. The buttery crust, paired with a sweet, spiced apple filling, creates the perfect balance of textures and flavors. While it may seem like a daunting task to make from scratch, a classic apple pie is easier than you think.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples (Granny Smith and Honeycrisp work great)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts or homemade pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples into 1/4-inch thick slices. Place them in a large bowl and toss with lemon juice to prevent browning.
  2. Make the filling: In a separate bowl, mix the granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and flour. Pour this mixture over the apples and toss until the apples are evenly coated.
  3. Assemble the pie: Roll out one pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Fill the crust with the apple mixture, mounding the apples in the center. Dot the filling with butter. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the apples. Crimp the edges together to seal the pie. Cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  4. Egg wash: Beat the egg and brush it over the top crust to give it a golden finish.
  5. Bake: Place the pie in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Cool and serve: Let the pie cool for at least an hour before slicing. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra treat.

This classic apple pie is sure to be a hit at any fall gathering and will fill your kitchen with the warm, comforting scents of cinnamon and apples.

2. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

Muffins are a quick and easy way to enjoy the flavors of fall, and these apple cinnamon muffins are a crowd-pleaser. They’re perfect for breakfast or a snack, and the apple chunks add a burst of freshness to every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 cups peeled and diced apples (preferably tart varieties like Granny Smith)
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, salt, and both sugars.
  3. Mix wet ingredients: In another bowl, whisk together the melted butter, egg, vanilla extract, and milk.
  4. Combine: Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Fold in apples and walnuts: Gently fold in the diced apples and chopped walnuts, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Bake: Spoon the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about two-thirds of the way. Bake for 18-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  7. Cool and enjoy: Let the muffins cool in the tin for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

These apple cinnamon muffins are soft, flavorful, and perfect for starting your fall mornings on a delicious note.

3. Apple Crisp

Apple crisp is a classic dessert that is as easy to make as it is delicious. With a warm, spiced apple filling and a crunchy, buttery topping, this dish is a perfect way to showcase the natural sweetness of apples. It’s a cozy treat that’s sure to satisfy your fall cravings.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 medium-sized apples (preferably a mix of tart and sweet)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples, then toss them with lemon juice, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and cornstarch. Transfer the apple mixture to a greased 9x9-inch baking dish.
  2. Make the topping: In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, and brown sugar. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your hands until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Assemble the crisp: Sprinkle the oat mixture evenly over the apples.
  4. Bake: Place the dish in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are tender.
  5. Serve: Let the apple crisp cool slightly before serving. It’s fantastic on its own or served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Apple crisp is one of those fall desserts that never goes out of style. It’s comforting, easy to make, and perfect for any occasion.

4. Apple Galette

For a rustic twist on the traditional pie, try making an apple galette. This freeform tart has a golden, flaky crust that encases the tender apples, with no need for a pie dish or intricate crimping. It’s a simple yet stunning dessert that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crusts or homemade pie dough
  • 4-5 medium apples (Granny Smith, Fuji, or Gala)
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples thinly. In a bowl, toss the apples with sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, cornstarch, and lemon juice.
  2. Prepare the dough: Roll out the pie dough on a floured surface to form a rough circle about 12 inches in diameter. Transfer it to a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Assemble the galette: Pile the apple mixture into the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border around the edges. Fold the edges of the dough over the apples, pleating the dough as you go to form a rustic crust.
  4. Egg wash: Beat the egg and brush it over the exposed dough to give it a shiny, golden finish.
  5. Bake: Place the galette in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the apples are tender and the crust is golden brown.
  6. Serve: Let the galette cool slightly before slicing. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a drizzle of caramel sauce.

This apple galette is the perfect dessert for anyone who loves the taste of apple pie but prefers a more laid-back, rustic approach to baking.

5. Apple Strudel

This flaky pastry, stuffed with a sweet, spiced apple filling, is a popular treat in many European countries. Apple strudel is a bit more involved than some other recipes, but the result is well worth the effort. The crisp, buttery layers of dough paired with tender apples create a dessert that’s simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of phyllo dough (about 20 sheets)
  • 5-6 medium apples (Granny Smith or Golden Delicious)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs, toasted
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the apples: Peel, core, and slice the apples thinly. Toss them with sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Let the apples sit for 10 minutes to release their juices.
  2. Prepare the strudel filling: In a large bowl, combine the apples with the toasted breadcrumbs and raisins.
  3. Assemble the strudel: Lay a sheet of phyllo dough on a clean surface and brush it with melted butter. Place another sheet of dough on top, and repeat the process with 8-10 sheets. Spoon the apple mixture along the center of the dough, then fold in the sides and roll the dough up into a log.
  4. Bake: Place the strudel on a parchment-lined baking sheet and brush the top with melted butter. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35-40 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Dust with powdered sugar: Let the strudel cool slightly, then dust with powdered sugar before slicing and serving.

Apple strudel is a sophisticated, yet comforting dessert that combines the best of fall flavors in every flaky bite.

6. Apple Cider Donuts

No fall baking list would be complete without apple cider donuts. These cake-like donuts are infused with the rich flavor of apple cider and rolled in cinnamon sugar, making them a perfect fall treat for breakfast or dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup apple cider (reduce to 1/4 cup if too liquid)
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the batter: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. In another bowl, mix the brown sugar, apple cider, buttermilk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  2. Combine the dry and wet ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. The batter will be thick.
  3. Fry the donuts: Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C). Scoop spoonfuls of the batter and carefully drop them into the hot oil. Fry for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown.
  4. Cinnamon sugar coating: Mix the granulated sugar and cinnamon in a shallow dish. Immediately coat the warm donuts in the cinnamon sugar mixture.
  5. Serve: Let the donuts cool slightly before enjoying them with a hot cup of cider or coffee.

These apple cider donuts are a beloved fall treat that captures the essence of autumn in every bite.

Apple-based desserts are an essential part of fall baking, and these six recipes highlight just how versatile apples can be in the kitchen. Whether you enjoy the classic apple pie or something a little more adventurous like apple cider donuts, these recipes will warm your home with the perfect autumnal flavors. Happy baking!
