Homemade Apple Cider Donuts: A Fall Favorite


Fall is finally here, and what better way to embrace the season than with a batch of homemade apple cider donuts? These delightful treats are a fall favorite, and they’re perfect for enjoying with a warm cup of apple cider. With their soft and moist texture, and the delicious blend of apple and cinnamon flavors, these donuts are sure to become a new autumn tradition in your household.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a donut pan with non-stick cooking spray and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. Ensure that all the ingredients are well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the apple cider, buttermilk, melted butter, egg, and vanilla extract until smooth and well incorporated.

  4. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense donuts.

  5. Spoon the batter into a piping bag or a zip-top bag with the corner snipped off. Pipe the batter into the prepared donut pan, filling each cavity about two-thirds full.

  6. Place the donut pan in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the donuts are lightly golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  7. Once baked, remove the donuts from the oven and allow them to cool in the pan for a few minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. While the donuts are cooling, prepare the cinnamon sugar coating. In a shallow bowl, combine 1/2 cup granulated sugar and 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon. Mix well.

  9. Once the donuts have cooled, dip each one in the cinnamon sugar mixture, ensuring that they are coated evenly. Gently shake off any excess sugar.

  10. Serve the homemade apple cider donuts immediately and enjoy their warm, comforting flavors. These donuts are best enjoyed on the day they are made, but they can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of apple flavor, you can add 1/2 cup of finely chopped apples to the batter before baking.
  • If you don’t have a donut pan, you can use a muffin tin instead. Simply spoon the batter into the greased muffin tin and adjust the baking time accordingly.
  • To make these donuts even more indulgent, you can drizzle them with a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and apple cider.
  • If you prefer a gluten-free option, you can substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend. Just make sure to check that all your other ingredients are also gluten-free.

Now that you have the recipe for homemade apple cider donuts, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! These delightful treats are the perfect way to celebrate the flavors of fall and enjoy a cozy moment with your loved ones. So grab a cup of apple cider, put on your favorite sweater, and savor the warm and comforting taste of homemade apple cider donuts. Happy baking!
