How to Make Crispy Korean Fried Chicken at Home


Craving for some delicious, crispy Korean fried chicken but don’t want to leave the comfort of your home? Look no further! In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to make mouthwatering, crispy Korean fried chicken right in your own kitchen. Get ready to impress your taste buds and your guests!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of chicken wings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for an extra kick)
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper. This combination of spices will give your chicken that signature Korean fried chicken flavor.

  2. Dip each chicken wing into the buttermilk, ensuring it is fully coated. Then, roll the chicken wing in the flour mixture, making sure it is evenly coated. Repeat the process for all the chicken wings.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot or deep fryer to 350°F (175°C). Make sure there is enough oil to fully submerge the chicken wings.

  4. Carefully place the coated chicken wings into the hot oil, working in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot. Fry the wings for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy.

  5. Once the chicken wings are cooked, transfer them to a wire rack or a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

  6. For an extra crispy coating, you can double-fry the chicken wings. Simply let them cool for a few minutes, then return them to the hot oil for another 5 minutes, or until they reach your desired level of crispiness.

  7. Serve your homemade crispy Korean fried chicken with your favorite dipping sauce, such as sweet chili sauce, soy garlic sauce, or spicy gochujang sauce. You can also garnish the chicken with some sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an authentic Korean touch.

  8. Enjoy your crispy Korean fried chicken immediately while it’s still hot and crunchy. It pairs perfectly with a side of steamed rice and pickled radishes for a complete Korean meal experience.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier alternative, you can bake the chicken wings instead of frying them. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and place the coated chicken wings on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 40-45 minutes, flipping the wings halfway through, until they are crispy and cooked through.

  • Feel free to adjust the level of spiciness by increasing or decreasing the amount of cayenne pepper in the flour mixture. You can also add some hot sauce or Korean chili paste to the buttermilk for an extra kick.

  • If you prefer boneless chicken, you can use chicken breast or chicken thigh meat instead of chicken wings. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure the chicken is cooked through.

Now that you know the secret to making crispy Korean fried chicken at home, it’s time to put your culinary skills to the test. Gather the ingredients, follow the steps, and get ready to enjoy a plate of irresistibly crispy and flavorful chicken. Happy cooking!
