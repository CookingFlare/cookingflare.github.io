A French Delight: Lemon Madeleines with Earl Grey Glaze


Bonjour, mes amis! Today, we’re diving into the delightful world of French baking with a recipe that’s as charming as it is delicious: Lemon Madeleines with Earl Grey Glaze. These delicate little treats are a perfect balance of zesty lemon flavor and the subtle floral notes of Earl Grey tea. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is sure to impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

For the Madeleines:

  • 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon

For the Earl Grey Glaze:

  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon strong brewed Earl Grey tea, cooled
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Madeleine Batter: In a small bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and granulated sugar until pale and thick. Add the vanilla extract and lemon zest, then gently fold in the dry ingredients. Finally, fold in the melted butter until just combined. Cover the bowl and chill the batter for at least 1 hour.

  2. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease the madeleine molds with butter and dust with flour, tapping out any excess.

  3. Bake the Madeleines: Spoon the chilled batter into the prepared molds, filling each about 3/4 full. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the centers spring back when lightly pressed. Transfer the madeleines to a wire rack to cool completely.

  4. Prepare the Earl Grey Glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, cooled brewed Earl Grey tea, and lemon juice until smooth. If the glaze is too thick, add a little more tea; if it’s too thin, add more powdered sugar.

  5. Glaze the Madeleines: Once the madeleines are completely cooled, dip the curved side of each madeleine into the Earl Grey glaze, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the glazed madeleines back on the wire rack and let the glaze set for about 15 minutes.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Arrange the glazed madeleines on a serving platter and watch them disappear in no time. These delightful treats are best enjoyed with a cup of tea or coffee, allowing the flavors to truly shine.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a twist, you can add a pinch of culinary lavender to the glaze for a floral infusion.
  • Don’t have madeleine molds? You can use a mini muffin tin as a substitute.

The Art of Madeleine Baking

The beauty of making madeleines lies in their simplicity and elegance. These little shell-shaped cakes have been a beloved French pastry for centuries, and for good reason. The process of chilling the batter allows the gluten to relax, resulting in the signature hump that forms in the center during baking. This hump is the hallmark of a perfectly baked madeleine, and achieving it is a true art form.

The Earl Grey Connection

Pairing the citrusy brightness of lemon with the fragrant, bergamot-infused Earl Grey tea creates a match made in culinary heaven. The floral and slightly astringent notes of the tea complement the tangy lemon, elevating the flavor profile of the madeleines to new heights.

Final Thoughts

Lemon Madeleines with Earl Grey Glaze are a delightful combination of French tradition and modern elegance. Whether you’re serving them at a sophisticated afternoon tea or simply indulging in a moment of self-care, these little treats are bound to bring a touch of joy to any occasion. So, don your apron, brew a pot of tea, and let’s bake up some French charm together. À bientôt!
