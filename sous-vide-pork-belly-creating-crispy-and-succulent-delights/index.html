Sous Vide Pork Belly: Creating Crispy and Succulent Delights


If you’re a fan of tender, succulent pork belly with a crispy, golden-brown exterior, then sous vide cooking is about to become your new best friend. Sous vide, a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a plastic pouch and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature, is perfect for achieving the ideal texture and flavor for pork belly.

Why Sous Vide Pork Belly?

Sous vide cooking allows the pork belly to cook evenly and retain its natural juices, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture that is simply irresistible. The low and consistent temperature of the water bath ensures that the pork belly becomes tender while remaining moist and flavorful. This method also provides the opportunity to infuse the pork belly with aromatic herbs and seasonings, enhancing its depth of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. pork belly, skin on
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil

Instructions:

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Belly

  1. Start by ensuring that the pork belly is clean and pat it dry with paper towels.
  2. Using a sharp knife, score the skin of the pork belly in a crisscross pattern, being careful not to cut into the meat.
  3. Season the pork belly generously with salt and pepper, making sure to rub the seasoning into the scores on the skin.

Step 2: Flavor Infusion

  1. Place the seasoned pork belly in a large resealable bag, along with the minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, and olive oil.
  2. Seal the bag, ensuring that the pork belly is evenly coated with the seasonings and aromatics.
  3. Place the bag in the refrigerator to marinate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to fully permeate the pork belly.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 165°F (74°C).
  2. Place the marinated pork belly in a vacuum-sealed bag, ensuring that it is arranged in a single layer to ensure even cooking.
  3. Submerge the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 12-24 hours, depending on your desired level of tenderness. The longer cooking time will result in an even more tender pork belly.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

  1. Once the pork belly has finished cooking, carefully remove it from the bag and place it on a wire rack to air dry for 30 minutes. This will help to further dry out the skin, resulting in a crispier texture when seared.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and place the pork belly skin-side down to sear for 3-5 minutes, or until the skin is golden and crispy.
  3. Remove the pork belly from the skillet and allow it to rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide pork belly pairs beautifully with a variety of accompaniments. Whether served over a bed of creamy mashed potatoes, alongside a vibrant salad, or as part of a gourmet sandwich, the succulent and crispy pork belly is sure to be a showstopper at any meal.

In Conclusion

Sous vide pork belly offers the perfect balance of tenderness and crispiness, making it a versatile and indulgent dish that is well worth the time and effort. Embrace the art of sous vide cooking and treat yourself to the delight of perfectly cooked, succulent pork belly with a crispy exterior that will leave your taste buds longing for more.
