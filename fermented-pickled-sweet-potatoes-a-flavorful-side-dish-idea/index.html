Fermented Pickled Sweet Potatoes: A Flavorful Side Dish Idea


When it comes to side dishes, many of us tend to stick to the usual suspects: steamed vegetables, roasted potatoes, or a simple green salad. But what if you could add a little excitement to your meal routine with a flavorful and nutritious option that’s also surprisingly easy to make? Enter fermented pickled sweet potatoes – a game-changing side dish that’s sure to become a new favorite.

In this recipe, we’ll explore the world of fermented pickled sweet potatoes, a dish that’s been gaining popularity in recent years due to its unique blend of tangy, sweet, and savory flavors. By combining the natural sweetness of sweet potatoes with the tanginess of fermented vegetables, we’ll create a dish that’s both familiar and exotic, perfect for adventurous eaters and foodies alike.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 large sweet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup (250ml) water
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) salt
  • 1 tablespoon (15g) sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1.25g) black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon (1.25g) red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat)
  • 1/2 cup (120g) chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup (30g) toasted sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Create the Brine

In a large bowl, combine the water, white vinegar, rice vinegar, salt, sugar, black pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir until the salt and sugar have dissolved, creating a brine.

Step 2: Soak the Sweet Potatoes

Add the sliced sweet potatoes to the brine, making sure they’re fully submerged. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and refrigerate for at least 24 hours or up to 48 hours.

Step 3: Ferment the Sweet Potatoes

After the soaking period, drain the sweet potatoes and rinse them under cold running water to remove any excess brine. In a clean glass jar or container with a wide mouth, create a layer of sweet potatoes. Sprinkle a pinch of salt over the sweet potatoes, followed by a pinch of sugar. Repeat this process until all the sweet potatoes have been used, finishing with a layer of salt on top.

Step 4: Allow Fermentation

Cover the jar with a clean cloth or paper towel and secure it with a rubber band. Place the jar in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or cupboard, and allow it to ferment for 3-5 days. You may start to notice bubbles forming on the surface of the sweet potatoes and a slightly sour smell – this is a sign of fermentation.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the fermentation process is complete, remove the jar from the cool, dark place and transfer it to the refrigerator. Allow the sweet potatoes to chill for at least 2 hours before serving. Just before serving, garnish with chopped scallions and toasted sesame seeds.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier flavor, increase the amount of red pepper flakes or add some diced jalapeños to the brine.
  • To add some crunch, sprinkle some chopped nuts or seeds over the sweet potatoes before serving.
  • Experiment with different types of vinegar, such as apple cider vinegar or balsamic vinegar, for a unique flavor profile.
  • For a more intense flavor, allow the sweet potatoes to ferment for an additional day or two.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled sweet potatoes are a game-changing side dish that’s sure to add some excitement to your meal routine. With its unique blend of tangy, sweet, and savory flavors, this dish is perfect for adventurous eaters and foodies alike. By following these simple steps and experimenting with different variations, you’ll be on your way to creating a delicious and nutritious side dish that’s sure to become a new favorite.
