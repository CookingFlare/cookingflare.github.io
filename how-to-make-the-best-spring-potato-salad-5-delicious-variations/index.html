How to Make the Best Spring Potato Salad: 5 Delicious Variations


Spring is the perfect time to enjoy the vibrant flavors of fresh produce, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a delicious potato salad? This classic dish is a crowd-pleaser, and with a few creative twists, you can elevate it to a whole new level. In this article, we will explore five mouth-watering variations of spring potato salad that are sure to impress your family and friends.

1. Lemon Herb Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of baby potatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh herbs (such as parsley, dill, and chives), chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the baby potatoes in salted water until tender. Drain and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, and fresh herbs.
  3. Add the cooled potatoes to the bowl and gently toss to coat them with the dressing.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.

2. Greek Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of Yukon Gold potatoes, cubed
  • 1/2 cup of Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup of red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the Yukon Gold potatoes in salted water until tender. Drain and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, lemon juice, red wine vinegar, and minced garlic.
  3. Add the cooled potatoes, Kalamata olives, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and fresh parsley to the bowl. Gently toss to combine.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.

3. Dijon Bacon Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of red potatoes, cubed
  • 1/4 cup of mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup of cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup of green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the red potatoes in salted water until tender. Drain and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and apple cider vinegar.
  3. Add the cooled potatoes, crumbled bacon, and chopped green onions to the bowl. Gently toss to combine.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.

4. Roasted Asparagus and Radish Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of fingerling potatoes, halved
  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch of radishes, quartered
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1/4 cup of fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the fingerling potatoes, asparagus, and radishes with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, and honey.
  3. Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a baking sheet and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and golden brown.
  5. Transfer the roasted vegetables to a large bowl and sprinkle with fresh mint. Gently toss to combine.
  6. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.

5. Avocado and Corn Potato Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of yellow potatoes, cubed
  • 2 avocados, diced
  • 1 cup of corn kernels, cooked and cooled
  • 1/4 cup of red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup of red onion, diced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the yellow potatoes in salted water until tender. Drain and let cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the diced avocados, cooked corn kernels, diced red bell pepper, diced red onion, and chopped cilantro.
  3. Add the cooled potatoes to the bowl and gently toss to combine.
  4. Squeeze the lime juice over the salad and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least one hour before serving.

These five variations of spring potato salad offer a delightful mix of flavors and textures that will brighten up any meal. Whether you prefer the tangy freshness of lemon herb, the Mediterranean twist of Greek, the smoky goodness of Dijon bacon, the roasted goodness of asparagus and radish, or the creamy richness of avocado and corn, there’s a potato salad to suit every taste. So, grab your apron, head to the kitchen, and get ready to impress with the best spring potato salad variations!
