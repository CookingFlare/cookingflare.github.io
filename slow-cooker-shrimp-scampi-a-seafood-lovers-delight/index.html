Slow Cooker Shrimp Scampi: A Seafood Lover's Delight


If you’re a seafood enthusiast who loves the bold flavors of shrimp, then this slow cooker shrimp scampi recipe is about to become your new favorite. Not only is it incredibly flavorful, but it’s also a breeze to make, thanks to the convenience of a slow cooker. This recipe is perfect for busy weeknights when you want a delicious meal without spending hours in the kitchen. So, grab your slow cooker and get ready to indulge in a dish that’s bursting with garlic, butter, and succulent shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup of dry white wine
  • 2 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon zest
  • 1/4 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup of freshly chopped parsley
  • 8 ounces of cooked linguine or your favorite pasta

Instructions:

  1. In the slow cooker, combine the shrimp, minced garlic, chicken broth, white wine, lemon juice, lemon zest, red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Gently stir the ingredients to ensure the shrimp are well coated with the flavorful mixture.
  3. Place the cubed butter on top of the shrimp mixture.
  4. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the shrimp are opaque and cooked through.
  5. Once the shrimp are cooked, carefully remove them from the slow cooker and set them aside.
  6. Add the freshly chopped parsley to the remaining liquid in the slow cooker and stir well.
  7. Prepare the linguine or pasta according to the package instructions.
  8. Serve the shrimp scampi over the cooked pasta, generously spooning the flavorful sauce over the top.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For an extra kick of flavor, add a splash of white wine vinegar to the slow cooker before cooking.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can create a slurry by mixing a tablespoon of cornstarch with a tablespoon of cold water and stirring it into the sauce in the slow cooker.
  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables, such as cherry tomatoes or spinach, during the last 30 minutes of cooking.

Why Slow Cooker Shrimp Scampi?

This slow cooker shrimp scampi recipe is a game-changer for seafood lovers. The slow cooking process allows the shrimp to absorb all the wonderful flavors of garlic, butter, and tangy lemon, resulting in a dish that’s truly irresistible. The convenience of the slow cooker also means that you can set it and forget it, returning to a mouthwatering meal that’s ready to be enjoyed.

The richness of the butter, the bright citrus notes, and the hint of heat from the red pepper flakes create a harmonious symphony of flavors that will have you reaching for seconds. Whether you’re cooking for your family or hosting a dinner party, this slow cooker shrimp scampi is sure to impress even the most discerning palates.

In conclusion, slow cooker shrimp scampi is a seafood lover’s dream come true. It’s a dish that embodies the essence of indulgence, yet it’s surprisingly simple to prepare. With minimal effort and maximum flavor, this recipe is a must-try for anyone who appreciates the delectable taste of shrimp. So, fire up your slow cooker and treat yourself to a luxurious dining experience right at home.
