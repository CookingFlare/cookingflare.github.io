Vegan Comfort Food: Hearty Recipes for a Filling Feast


Comfort food is all about indulging in rich, satisfying, and often nostalgic dishes that warm the heart and soul. Traditionally, comfort food has been associated with meat-based and dairy-laden recipes, but that doesn’t mean vegans can’t enjoy a hearty and filling feast. In this post, we’ll explore the world of vegan comfort food, featuring recipes that are sure to become new favorites.

Creamy Mashed Potato Gratin

There’s nothing quite like a warm, creamy mashed potato dish to cozy up to. This vegan version of the classic recipe uses a cashew-based cream instead of dairy, and is topped with a crispy, golden-brown breadcrumb mixture.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup cashew cream (see note)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast

Note: To make cashew cream, soak 1 cup of cashews in water for at least 4 hours. Drain and rinse, then blend with 1/2 cup fresh water until smooth.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pot, combine potatoes, cashew cream, olive oil, onion, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until potatoes are tender.
  3. Transfer pot to a blender or food processor, and blend until smooth.
  4. Transfer mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish, and top with breadcrumb mixture.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until top is golden brown.

Lentil and Mushroom Shepherd’s Pie

Shepherd’s pie is a classic comfort food dish that’s easily adapted to a vegan lifestyle. This recipe uses lentils and mushrooms instead of ground meat, and is topped with a crispy, mashed potato crust.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown or green lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups mashed potatoes (see note)

Note: To make mashed potatoes, boil 2-3 large potatoes until tender. Drain and mash with 1/4 cup non-dairy milk and 1 tablespoon olive oil.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pot, combine lentils, vegetable broth, olive oil, onion, garlic, mushrooms, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until lentils are tender.
  3. Transfer lentil mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  4. Top with mashed potato crust, spreading evenly.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until top is golden brown.

Vegan Mac and Cheese

Macaroni and cheese is a comforting, indulgent dish that’s easy to make vegan. This recipe uses a cashew-based cheese sauce and a variety of non-dairy milks to create a creamy, cheesy sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound macaroni
  • 2 cups non-dairy milk (see note)
  • 1/2 cup cashew cream (see note)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast

Note: To make non-dairy milk, combine 1 cup water with 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar or lemon juice. Let sit for 5-10 minutes, then strain and use.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook macaroni according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a blender or food processor, combine non-dairy milk, cashew cream, olive oil, onion, garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  4. In a large pot, combine blended sauce and cooked macaroni. Stir until combined.
  5. Transfer macaroni mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish, and top with breadcrumb mixture.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until top is golden brown.

Vegan Chili

Chili is a comforting, warming dish that’s perfect for a cold winter’s night. This vegan version uses a variety of beans and vegetables to create a hearty, filling chili.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cups mixed beans (see note)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Note: To make mixed beans, combine 1 cup cooked kidney beans, 1 cup cooked black beans, and 1 cup cooked pinto beans.

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and red bell pepper, and cook until tender.
  2. Add mixed beans, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until chili is hot and bubbly.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro if desired.

These hearty, comforting recipes are sure to become new favorites in your vegan kitchen. Whether you’re looking for a satisfying breakfast, a filling lunch, or a cozy dinner, these dishes are sure to hit the spot. So go ahead, indulge in these vegan comfort foods and enjoy the warmth and comfort they bring to your table.
