Authentic Middle Eastern Shawarma: Tips for Assembling Sandwiches


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices, and one of the most beloved dishes is the authentic Middle Eastern shawarma. A shawarma is a flavorful and juicy meat dish that is traditionally cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The tantalizing aroma of the meat roasting on the spit is enough to make anyone’s mouth water. Once the meat is cooked to perfection, it’s time to assemble the shawarma sandwiches, and that’s where the magic happens.

Choosing the Right Bread

The foundation of a great shawarma sandwich is the bread. In the Middle East, pita bread or flatbread is commonly used for shawarma sandwiches. The bread should be soft yet sturdy enough to hold the flavorful fillings without falling apart. If you can’t find traditional Middle Eastern bread, you can use a soft, fluffy wrap or a similar type of bread as a substitute.

Layering the Flavors

The key to a truly delicious shawarma sandwich is the layering of flavors. Start by spreading a thin layer of creamy garlic sauce or tahini on the bread. This will add a rich, savory flavor to the sandwich and help hold the fillings in place. Then, add a generous portion of thinly sliced shawarma meat. The meat should be tender, juicy, and bursting with aromatic spices.

Next, add a layer of crunchy and tangy pickles. Traditional Middle Eastern pickles are often made with cucumbers, turnips, or even mixed vegetables, and they add a delightful contrast to the rich, savory meat. Finally, top it off with a handful of fresh, crisp lettuce and slices of ripe tomatoes. These fresh ingredients add a burst of color and texture to the sandwich, making each bite a delightful medley of flavors.

Adding a Flavorful Kick

For those who enjoy a bit of heat, consider adding a sprinkle of sumac or a drizzle of spicy harissa sauce to the sandwich. Sumac adds a tangy, citrusy flavor, while harissa brings a fiery kick that will elevate the overall taste of the shawarma sandwich. If you prefer a milder option, a dollop of creamy hummus can also add a delightful creaminess and depth of flavor to the sandwich.

Wrapping it Up

Once all the flavorful components are layered on the bread, it’s time to wrap up the shawarma sandwich. Carefully fold the sides of the bread over the fillings, then roll it up tightly. This will help keep all the delicious ingredients in place and make it easier to handle and eat. Some people prefer to lightly toast the wrapped sandwich for a warm, crispy exterior, but this is optional.

Serving Suggestions

Authentic Middle Eastern shawarma sandwiches are often served with a side of tangy pickles, a generous portion of crispy French fries, and a refreshing salad. To complete the experience, consider serving the sandwiches with a cooling and creamy tzatziki sauce for dipping.

In Conclusion

Assembling authentic Middle Eastern shawarma sandwiches is a delightful and flavorful experience. The combination of tender, spiced meat, creamy sauces, crunchy pickles, and fresh vegetables creates a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of the Middle East. Whether you’re enjoying a shawarma sandwich as a quick meal or as part of a festive gathering, these tips will help you create a truly authentic and mouthwatering Middle Eastern culinary experience. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the irresistible taste of authentic Middle Eastern shawarma sandwiches.
