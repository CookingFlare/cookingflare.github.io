10 Delicious Fall Recipes for a Cozy Brunch


When the crisp autumn air settles in and the leaves start turning shades of gold, it’s time to embrace the cozy comfort of fall flavors. Fall is the perfect season to gather with friends and family over a warm brunch that celebrates seasonal ingredients and spices. Whether you’re hosting a casual get-together or simply indulging in a quiet morning, these 10 fall recipes will fill your home with warmth and make your brunch table a seasonal delight.

1. Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Nothing says fall quite like pumpkin, and these fluffy pumpkin spice pancakes are a delightful way to start your brunch. The combination of pumpkin puree, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves creates a warming and aromatic batter that pairs perfectly with maple syrup and whipped cream.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves.
  2. In another bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined (be careful not to overmix).
  4. Heat a griddle or nonstick skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or cooking spray.
  5. Pour about ¼ cup of batter onto the skillet and cook until bubbles form on the surface, about 2-3 minutes. Flip and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Serve the pancakes with maple syrup and a dollop of whipped cream for extra indulgence.

2. Apple Cinnamon French Toast Casserole

This apple cinnamon French toast casserole is the ultimate fall breakfast bake. With layers of soft, custardy bread soaked in a spiced apple mixture, it’s a showstopper dish that’s perfect for feeding a crowd. Plus, it can be prepared ahead of time, making it an easy option for busy mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of challah bread or brioche, cut into cubes
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups whole milk
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 apple, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • ¼ cup brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Arrange the cubed bread in the prepared dish. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, heavy cream, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Pour the custard mixture over the bread cubes, pressing the bread down gently to ensure it’s soaked.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the sliced apple with the melted butter and brown sugar. Scatter the apple mixture over the top of the casserole.
  5. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the top is golden and crispy.
  6. Serve with a dusting of powdered sugar and a drizzle of maple syrup.

3. Butternut Squash and Sage Quiche

This savory quiche combines the rich sweetness of roasted butternut squash with the earthy flavor of fresh sage. Perfect for a cozy brunch, this quiche is a wonderful combination of texture and taste, with a buttery, flaky crust that holds it all together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 2 cups roasted butternut squash (peeled, cubed, and roasted)
  • ½ cup ricotta cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  2. In a blender or food processor, blend the roasted butternut squash, ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, eggs, heavy cream, and chopped sage until smooth.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust.
  4. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and golden on top.
  5. Allow the quiche to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

4. Sweet Potato Hash with Sausage

For a hearty and savory fall brunch dish, try this sweet potato hash with sausage. Roasted sweet potatoes and caramelized onions are paired with savory sausage and bell peppers for a delicious, well-rounded dish. This one-pan recipe is as simple as it is satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 lb breakfast sausage
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 eggs (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, paprika, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender.
  2. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, cook the sausage in a large skillet over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Remove the sausage from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the onion and bell pepper until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the roasted sweet potatoes and sausage to the skillet and stir to combine. Cook for another 5 minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  5. If desired, fry eggs in the skillet to top the hash, cooking until the whites are set but the yolks remain runny.
  6. Serve the hash hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

5. Pear and Gorgonzola Salad

This crisp and refreshing salad features the perfect balance of sweetness from the pears and sharpness from the Gorgonzola cheese. It’s a simple yet elegant dish that makes for a great side at a fall brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed salad greens (arugula, spinach, etc.)
  • 2 pears, cored and sliced
  • ½ cup crumbled Gorgonzola cheese
  • ¼ cup candied pecans
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the salad greens with the pear slices, Gorgonzola, and candied pecans.
  2. Drizzle the olive oil and balsamic vinegar over the salad and toss to combine.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve immediately.

6. Cinnamon Roll Muffins

These cinnamon roll muffins combine the soft, fluffy texture of muffins with the rich, spiced filling of cinnamon rolls. The result is a bite-sized treat that’s perfect for a fall brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup powdered sugar (for glaze)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the cinnamon and brown sugar.
  5. Spoon a little batter into each muffin cup, then sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar mixture on top. Add another spoonful of batter and repeat.
  6. Swirl the cinnamon-sugar mixture into the batter using a toothpick, then bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Drizzle the muffins with a simple glaze made by mixing powdered sugar with a splash of milk, then serve warm.

7. Cranberry Orange Scones

These cranberry orange scones are light, tender, and bursting with fall flavors. The zesty orange pairs beautifully with the tart cranberries, making this a perfect addition to your fall brunch spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 stick cold butter, cubed
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries, chopped
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cut the cold butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Stir in the chopped cranberries and orange zest.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, heavy cream, and vanilla extract. Add this to the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and shape it into a disc. Cut the dough into 8 wedges and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until the scones are golden brown.
  8. Let cool slightly, then serve with clotted cream or jam.

8. Spiced Apple Cider Mimosa

For a refreshing beverage to accompany your fall brunch, try a spiced apple cider mimosa. This twist on the classic mimosa uses apple cider and a touch of cinnamon to give it a fall-inspired flavor. Perfect for a celebratory brunch!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 1 bottle champagne or sparkling wine
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 apple slices, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pour the apple cider into a champagne flute, filling about a third of the glass.
  2. Top with champagne or sparkling wine, then stir in the cinnamon.
  3. Garnish with apple slices and serve immediately.

9. Maple Pecan Oatmeal

Start your brunch with a warm, hearty bowl of maple pecan oatmeal. This dish is simple yet comforting, with the natural sweetness of maple syrup and the crunch of toasted pecans.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 2 cups milk
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • ¼ cup chopped pecans, toasted
  • Pinch of cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the milk to a simmer.
  2. Stir in the oats and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the oats are tender.
  3. Add the maple syrup, cinnamon, and salt, and stir to combine.
  4. Top with toasted pecans before serving.

10. Pumpkin Cream Cheese Muffins

These pumpkin cream cheese muffins are a decadent treat that combines the richness of pumpkin with a sweet cream cheese filling. Perfect for those who love a sweet and savory breakfast!

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, sugar, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, eggs, oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. In a small bowl, beat the cream cheese with powdered sugar until smooth.
  6. Spoon half of the muffin batter into each muffin cup, then add a dollop of cream cheese mixture. Top with the remaining batter.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
