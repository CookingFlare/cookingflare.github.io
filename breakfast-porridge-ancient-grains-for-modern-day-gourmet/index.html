Breakfast Porridge: Ancient Grains for Modern-day Gourmet


Porridge has been a staple breakfast dish around the world for thousands of years. Variations of this comforting and easy-to-make meal have been enjoyed by many, using ancient grains like barley, millet, and farro. Today, we’re going to explore the versatility and health benefits of these grains, and share two delicious porridge recipes with you.

Why Ancient Grains?

Ancient grains have begun to make their way into modern kitchens due to their diverse nutritional profiles and unique flavors. Unlike their modern counterparts, these grains have largely remained unchanged by human intervention. This means they preserve essential nutrients and an impressive amount of fiber, making them a popular option for health-conscious eaters.

Let’s dive into the benefits of a few ancient grains:

  1. Farro: This grain, dating back to Egyptian times, boasts a high fiber content, helping to keep you full and satisfied. It is also a rich source of antioxidants and vitamin B.
  2. Barley: Another ancient grain with a rich history, barley has been used as a staple in many cultures. It is high in fiber and iron, and provides a variety of nutrients and minerals.
  3. Millet: This tiny grain is packed with nutrients, including essential amino acids, fiber, and minerals, and it’s perfect for those with dietary restrictions, such as gluten-free or grain-free diets.

Recipe 1: Farro and Pear Porridge

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Farro, rinsed and drained
  • 4 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 large pear, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the farro, almond milk, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Reduce the heat to low, and let the farro simmer for about 20 minutes or until tender.
  3. While the farro is cooking, sauté chopped almonds in a pan with a little bit of oil until golden brown. Set aside.
  4. Once the farro is cooked, stir in the diced pears, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, and ground nutmeg. Cook for additional 5 minutes.
  5. Taste and adjust the sweetness with a tablespoon of honey if desired.
  6. Serve warm, topped with the prepared almonds.

Recipe 2: Summer Berry and Farro Porridge

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Farro, rinsed and drained
  • 4 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 1/2 cups mixed summer berries
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the farro, almond milk, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Reduce the heat to low, and let the farro simmer for about 20 minutes or until tender.
  3. While the farro is cooking, sauté chopped almonds in a pan with a little bit of oil until golden brown. Set aside.
  4. Once the farro is cooked, stir in the mixed summer berries and vanilla extract, followed by the ground cinnamon and nutmeg. Cook for additional 5 minutes.
  5. Serve warm, topped with the prepared almonds.

Conclusion

Porridge is an excellent option for those in search of a satisfying and nutritious breakfast to start their day. By incorporating ancient grains like farro, barley, and millet, you can enjoy the distinct textures and flavors, as well as the added health benefits these grains provide. Try out these two delicious porridge recipes and start your day with an ancient grain boost.
