Homemade Condiments: Recipes for Canning


Are you tired of store-bought condiments that lack that homemade touch? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore the world of homemade condiments and provide you with easy-to-follow recipes for canning. From tangy ketchup to fiery hot sauce, these condiments will elevate your meals and add a burst of flavor to any dish. So grab your apron and let’s get started!

Tangy Tomato Ketchup

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds ripe tomatoes
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Score the bottom of each tomato with a small “X” and blanch them in boiling water for 1 minute. Remove the tomatoes and place them in a bowl of ice water to cool.
  2. Peel the tomatoes and remove the seeds. Chop the tomatoes into small pieces.
  3. In a separate pot, sauté the onion and bell pepper until they are soft and translucent.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, vinegar, brown sugar, salt, cloves, cinnamon, and allspice to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  5. Simmer the mixture on low heat for 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  6. Use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree the mixture until smooth.
  7. Return the mixture to the pot and continue simmering for another 1 hour, until it reaches the desired thickness.
  8. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  9. Ladle the hot ketchup into the jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth to remove any spills.
  10. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 15 minutes. Remove from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

Fiery Jalapeno Hot Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound jalapeno peppers
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Wash the jalapeno peppers and pat them dry. Place them on the prepared baking sheet and roast in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the skins are blistered and charred.
  3. Remove the peppers from the oven and let them cool. Once cooled, remove the stems and seeds.
  4. Place the roasted peppers, garlic, vinegar, salt, and sugar in a blender or food processor. Blend until smooth.
  5. Pour the mixture into a saucepan and bring it to a boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  7. Carefully ladle the hot sauce into the jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth.
  8. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. Remove from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

Sweet and Spicy Pickle Relish

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cucumbers, finely chopped
  • 1 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups white vinegar
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 tablespoon celery seeds
  • 1 tablespoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chopped cucumbers, onion, and bell pepper.
  2. In a separate pot, bring the vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, celery seeds, and salt to a boil. Stir until the sugar has dissolved.
  3. Pour the hot vinegar mixture over the cucumber mixture. Stir well to combine.
  4. Let the relish sit at room temperature for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  5. Sterilize your canning jars and lids according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Ladle the relish into the jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes. Remove from the water bath and let them cool completely before storing.

Conclusion

Homemade condiments are the perfect way to add a personal touch to your meals. With these easy-to-follow recipes for canning, you can enjoy the flavors of summer all year round. From tangy ketchup to fiery hot sauce and sweet and spicy pickle relish, these homemade condiments are sure to impress your family and friends. So why settle for store-bought when you can create your own delicious condiments? Get canning and elevate your meals to a whole new level!
