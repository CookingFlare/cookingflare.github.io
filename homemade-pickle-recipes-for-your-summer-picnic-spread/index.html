Homemade Pickle Recipes for Your Summer Picnic Spread


Summer is the perfect time for picnics, and no picnic spread is complete without a jar of homemade pickles. Whether you’re a fan of tangy dill pickles or spicy bread and butter pickles, making your own pickles at home is easier than you might think. Not only are homemade pickles delicious, but they also make a fantastic addition to sandwiches, salads, and charcuterie boards. In this article, we’ll explore some easy and flavorful homemade pickle recipes that will elevate your summer picnic spread.

Classic Dill Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 pickling cucumbers
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons pickling salt
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons dill seeds
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds

Instructions:

  1. Begin by washing the cucumbers thoroughly and cutting them into spears or slices.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the water, vinegar, and pickling salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the salt dissolves.
  3. In each sterilized jar, place 2 cloves of garlic, 1 tablespoon of dill seeds, 1/2 tablespoon of black peppercorns, and 1/2 tablespoon of mustard seeds.
  4. Pack the cucumber spears or slices into the jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of space at the top.
  5. Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  6. Seal the jars and let them cool to room temperature before refrigerating. The pickles will be ready to eat in about 24 hours.

Spicy Bread and Butter Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 5-6 pickling cucumbers
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Wash the cucumbers and cut them into thin slices. Thinly slice the onion as well.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the apple cider vinegar, granulated sugar, brown sugar, mustard seeds, turmeric, celery seeds, and red pepper flakes. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Add the cucumber slices and onion to the saucepan and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Using a slotted spoon, pack the cucumbers and onions into sterilized jars.
  5. Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers, ensuring they are fully covered.
  6. Seal the jars and let them cool to room temperature before refrigerating. These pickles will be ready to enjoy after 48 hours.

Sweet and Tangy Bread and Butter Pickles

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 pickling cucumbers
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup pickling salt
  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes

Instructions:

  1. Wash the cucumbers and thinly slice them. Thinly slice the onion as well.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cucumber slices, sliced onion, and pickling salt. Cover with ice and let stand for 2 hours. Rinse and drain thoroughly.
  3. In a large saucepan, combine the apple cider vinegar, granulated sugar, mustard seeds, celery seeds, turmeric, and red pepper flakes. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Add the cucumber slices and onion to the saucepan and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Using a slotted spoon, pack the cucumbers and onions into sterilized jars.
  6. Pour the hot brine over the cucumbers, ensuring they are fully covered.
  7. Seal the jars and let them cool to room temperature before refrigerating. These pickles will be ready to eat after 48 hours.

Conclusion

Homemade pickles are a delightful addition to any summer picnic spread. With these easy and delicious recipes, you can create your own flavorful pickles that will impress your family and friends. Whether you prefer classic dill pickles, spicy bread and butter pickles, or sweet and tangy bread and butter pickles, there’s a homemade pickle recipe for everyone. So, roll up your sleeves, grab some fresh cucumbers, and get ready to pickle your way to picnic perfection!
