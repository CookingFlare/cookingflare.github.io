Thai Stir Fry Recipe with Beef and Vegetables

Thai cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, aromas, and spices, and one of the most popular dishes is the stir fry. In this recipe, we’ll be combining the richness of beef with the vibrant colors and flavors of a variety of vegetables, all cooked to perfection in a savory sauce. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. Ingredients 200g beef strips (sirloin or ribeye work well) 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 onion, thinly sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 bell pepper, sliced 1 cup mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, and shiitake) 1 cup snow peas, sliced 1 cup Thai basil leaves 2 tablespoons tamarind paste 2 tablespoons fish sauce 1 tablespoon soy sauce 1 tablespoon palm sugar 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt and black pepper, to taste 2 tablespoons water 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (optional) Instructions Prepare the ingredients: Cut the beef into thin strips and season with salt. Cut the onion, bell pepper, and mushrooms into thin slices. Mince the garlic and chop the snow peas into bite-sized pieces. Cook the beef: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add the beef and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until browned and cooked through. Remove the beef from the wok and set aside. Cook the aromatics: In the same wok, add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced onion and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning. Add the vegetables: Add the sliced bell pepper and mushrooms to the wok. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Add the snow peas and cook for an additional minute. Add the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, soy sauce, palm sugar, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper. Add the sauce to the wok and stir to combine. Combine the beef and vegetables: Add the cooked beef back into the wok and stir to combine with the vegetables and sauce. Season and serve: Season the stir fry with salt and black pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped fresh cilantro, if desired. Serve immediately over steamed rice or noodles. Tips and Variations For a spicier stir fry, add more cayenne pepper or use Thai chili peppers instead of bell peppers. Substitute chicken or pork for the beef, if preferred. Add other vegetables, such as baby corn, bamboo shoots, or Thai eggplant, to the stir fry. Use different types of mushrooms, such as oyster or shiitake, for added depth of flavor. Serve the stir fry with a fried egg or crispy fried shallots for added texture and flavor. Conclusion This Thai stir fry recipe with beef and vegetables is a flavorful and nutritious meal that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. With its bold flavors and aromas, it’s perfect for a quick and easy dinner or a special occasion. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the delicious combination of beef and vegetables in a savory Thai-inspired sauce. ...
