Everyday Peruvian Dishes: 8 Recipes to Impress


Peruvian cuisine is a vibrant celebration of flavors, textures, and history. With influences from native traditions, Spanish colonization, African roots, Japanese techniques, and Chinese ingredients, it is no surprise that Peru’s culinary scene has garnered global recognition. From tangy marinades to deeply comforting rice dishes, the magic of Peruvian cooking lies in its balance of bold flavors and fresh ingredients.

If you’re looking to infuse your kitchen with the essence of Peru, these eight everyday recipes will give you a taste of the heart and soul of this diverse cuisine. Whether you’re prepping for a family dinner or hosting a special occasion, these dishes will leave your guests impressed and begging for seconds.

1. Lomo Saltado: Stir-Fried Beef with Peruvian Flair

Lomo Saltado is a beloved Peruvian dish that perfectly blends Asian and South American flavors. This stir-fry of tender beef, onions, tomatoes, and French fries is served with white rice for a balanced and comforting meal. The key lies in the sauce—a soy sauce and vinegar-based blend that brings rich umami and tangy brightness at every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced into thick wedges
  • 2 large tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 2 cups cooked French fries
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups cooked white rice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add oil and sear beef slices until browned; remove and set aside.
  2. Add onions and garlic to the skillet, stir-frying until fragrant.
  3. Toss in tomatoes, soy sauce, vinegar, salt, and pepper. Stir-fry briefly, allowing tomatoes to soften slightly.
  4. Return the beef to the skillet and combine with cooked French fries. Stir and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Serve the stir-fry over a mound of steamed white rice, garnished with freshly chopped cilantro.

2. Papa a la Huancaína: Creamy Potatoes in Spicy Sauce

This appetizer is simple yet irresistible. Slices of boiled potatoes are drenched in a creamy, spicy sauce made from ají amarillo peppers, cheese, and milk, served over lettuce and garnished with olives and boiled eggs. Papa a la Huancaína is a quintessential starter for any Peruvian meal.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium potatoes, boiled and peeled
  • ½ cup ají amarillo paste
  • 4 ounces queso fresco (or feta cheese)
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Lettuce leaves for serving
  • Hard-boiled eggs and black olives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Blend ají amarillo paste, queso fresco, garlic, and evaporated milk until you achieve a smooth and creamy sauce.
  2. Arrange lettuce leaves on plates and place potato slices over them.
  3. Pour the sauce generously over the potatoes and garnish with boiled eggs and olives.
  4. Chill before serving for a refreshing appetizer.

3. Ceviche: Peru’s Signature Dish

No Peruvian spread is complete without ceviche. This zesty dish is all about fresh fish marinated in lime juice, spiced with chili, and complemented by crunchy onions, sweet potatoes, and corn. It’s a celebration of freshness and balance.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh white fish (sea bass or tilapia), cut into small cubes
  • Juice of 8 limes
  • 1 medium red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 ají amarillo or Serrano chili, diced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Boiled sweet potato and corn as side accompaniments

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, season the fish cubes with salt and chili, ensuring an even coating.
  2. Pour fresh lime juice over the fish and let it marinate for 15–20 minutes; the acid will cook the fish.
  3. Add onions and cilantro to the marinated fish, stirring gently.
  4. Serve immediately with slices of boiled sweet potato and corn on the side.

4. Aji de Gallina: Creamy Chicken Stew

Aji de Gallina is a classic comfort food in Peruvian cuisine, combining shredded chicken with a velvety yellow chili sauce enhanced by bread crumbs and nuts. Its depth of flavor and soothing texture makes it an ideal dish for family gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • ½ cup ají amarillo paste
  • 2 slices of white bread, soaked in milk
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • ½ cup evaporated milk
  • ¼ cup Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Walnuts or pecans, finely chopped
  • Boiled eggs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Blend the soaked bread slices with chicken stock to create a smooth mixture.
  2. In a pan, sauté garlic and ají amarillo paste until fragrant.
  3. Add the bread mixture to the pan, followed by shredded chicken, evaporated milk, and Parmesan cheese. Stir and cook until thickened.
  4. Serve with white rice and garnish with boiled eggs and chopped nuts.

5. Quinoa Chaufa: Peruvian-Style Fried Quinoa

Quinoa has been a staple food in Peru for centuries, earning its title as a superfood. In this dish, it replaces the traditional rice of Chinese-style fried rice to create a nutrient-packed, flavorful alternative.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked quinoa
  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 1 cup cooked chicken or shrimp, diced
  • ½ cup chopped scallions
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a skillet and sauté garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add chicken or shrimp, followed by scrambled eggs, stirring everything together.
  3. Mix in cooked quinoa and toss with soy sauce and scallions.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with fresh scallions.

6. Pollo a la Brasa: Peruvian Roast Chicken

Pollo a la Brasa is a smoky, savory roast chicken that epitomizes Peruvian street food. Marinated in a blend of spices and cooked to perfection, this dish is complemented by a side of French fries and salad.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 2 tablespoons cumin
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • ½ cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine cumin, paprika, garlic paste, soy sauce, vinegar, salt, and pepper to create a marinade.
  2. Rub the marinade all over the chicken, ensuring even coverage. Let it marinate for at least 2 hours (preferably overnight).
  3. Roast the chicken at 375°F for 1–1½ hours, basting regularly.
  4. Serve with French fries and a crisp salad for a traditional pairing.

7. Tacu-Tacu: Rice and Beans Delight

Tacu-Tacu is a hearty dish inspired by leftovers, combining rice and beans into a flavorful patty, often served with fried plantains and a fried egg. It’s a deeply satisfying and versatile meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 cup cooked beans (black or lentils)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Mash beans and mix them with cooked rice, cumin, garlic, onion, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet, shape the mixture into a patty, and fry until golden on both sides.
  3. Serve with fried plantains and a fried egg atop for a complete meal.

8. Anticuchos: Skewered Beef Hearts

Anticuchos are a popular street food in Peru, consisting of marinated beef heart skewers grilled to smoky perfection. They’re seasoned with ají panca paste, garlic, and vinegar, making them a must-try dish for adventurous eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound beef heart, sliced into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons ají panca paste
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers

Instructions:

  1. Combine ají panca paste, vinegar, garlic, salt, and pepper to create a marinade.
  2. Coat beef heart pieces with the marinade and let them rest for at least 2 hours.
  3. Assemble meat cubes on skewers and grill over medium-high heat until perfectly charred.
  4. Serve with boiled potatoes and ají amarillo sauce for dipping.

Final Thoughts

Peruvian cuisine is an adventure for the senses and a journey into a culture rich in tradition. Incorporating these dishes into your culinary repertoire will not only allow you to embrace the versatility of Peruvian cooking but also impress anyone who gets a forkful of your creations. The beauty of these recipes is their adaptability for everyday cooking while bringing bold and unforgettable flavors to your table.
