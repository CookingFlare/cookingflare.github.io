How to Use Leftover Bacon Creatively


Bacon is undeniably a beloved ingredient in many kitchens, known for its crispy texture, savory flavor, and its ability to elevate nearly any dish. But what happens when you make too much? Leftover bacon can sometimes go overlooked or tossed into the fridge without a clear idea of how to use it. Rather than letting that delicious, crispy goodness go to waste, there are countless creative ways to repurpose your leftover bacon into dishes that will surprise and delight your taste buds.

If you’ve ever found yourself with extra bacon after breakfast or a family brunch, you’re in luck. This post will guide you through 10 inventive and tasty ways to use leftover bacon creatively, from savory main dishes to sweet treats. Let’s dive into these ideas that will take your leftover bacon game to the next level!

1. Bacon and Avocado Grilled Cheese

A grilled cheese sandwich is comfort food at its finest, but when you add bacon and avocado into the mix, you elevate the entire experience. Using leftover bacon in a grilled cheese is an easy way to repurpose it without much effort, but it adds a whole new level of richness and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of your favorite bread (sourdough or whole grain works well)
  • 2-3 slices of leftover bacon, crumbled
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 2 slices of cheese (cheddar, Swiss, or mozzarella)
  • Butter for toasting

Directions:

  1. Spread butter on one side of each bread slice.
  2. Layer a slice of cheese on the unbuttered side of one bread slice, followed by crumbled bacon and slices of avocado.
  3. Top with the second slice of cheese and then place the other slice of bread on top, buttered side facing out.
  4. Grill the sandwich on medium heat, flipping until both sides are golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  5. Slice in half and enjoy a crunchy, creamy, and savory delight.

This sandwich works wonderfully for breakfast, lunch, or even a comforting dinner.

2. Bacon-Wrapped Stuffed Mushrooms

If you want to add a touch of elegance to your leftovers, bacon-wrapped stuffed mushrooms are an excellent appetizer or party dish. These savory bites are filled with a flavorful stuffing, then wrapped in crispy bacon for an extra punch of taste.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large mushrooms, stems removed
  • 8-10 slices of leftover bacon, diced
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or thyme), chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a pan, cook the diced bacon until it becomes crispy. Remove from the pan and drain on a paper towel.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the cream cheese, Parmesan, breadcrumbs, cooked bacon, herbs, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Stuff the mushroom caps with the cream cheese mixture, pressing it in gently to fill each one.
  5. Wrap each stuffed mushroom with a slice of bacon and place them on a baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the bacon is crispy and the mushrooms are tender.

These bacon-wrapped stuffed mushrooms are perfect for impressing guests at your next dinner party or serving as a flavorful snack.

3. Bacon and Egg Breakfast Burritos

There’s nothing quite like a breakfast burrito, and using leftover bacon can take your morning meal from ordinary to extraordinary. This recipe combines the smoky flavor of bacon with eggs, cheese, and vegetables for a filling breakfast that’s easy to make and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 3-4 slices of leftover bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Monterey Jack)
  • 1/4 cup diced bell pepper and onion
  • 1 large flour tortilla
  • Salsa or sour cream for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. In a skillet, sauté the diced bell pepper and onion until softened.
  2. Scramble the eggs in a separate pan, then combine with the cooked bacon and sautéed vegetables.
  3. Lay the tortilla flat and spoon the egg mixture in the center. Add shredded cheese on top.
  4. Roll up the tortilla into a burrito, folding the sides in as you go to secure the filling.
  5. Optionally, you can toast the burrito on a skillet to give it a crispy exterior.
  6. Serve with salsa or sour cream for added flavor.

This bacon and egg breakfast burrito is the perfect on-the-go meal or a satisfying start to your day.

4. Bacon Carbonara

Pasta lovers know that carbonara is a classic Italian dish, but have you ever thought of adding leftover bacon to it? This simple yet delicious pasta dish combines crispy bacon with eggs, cheese, and garlic for an indulgent meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti or fettuccine
  • 4-5 slices of leftover bacon, chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the garlic for about a minute until fragrant.
  4. In a bowl, whisk the eggs with the Parmesan cheese, then season with salt and pepper.
  5. Toss the drained pasta into the skillet with the garlic, then quickly mix in the egg mixture, adding pasta water a little at a time to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Stir in the crispy bacon and garnish with fresh parsley.

Bacon carbonara is a quick, satisfying, and flavorful way to enjoy your leftover bacon in a hearty pasta dish.

5. Bacon Salad

Bacon can bring an unexpected crunch and flavor to a fresh salad. Whether you’re enjoying a simple side salad or a hearty main dish, crumbled bacon adds a touch of savory indulgence that perfectly complements the freshness of vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • Mixed greens (spinach, arugula, and romaine)
  • 3-4 slices of leftover bacon, crumbled
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Cucumber, sliced
  • Blue cheese or goat cheese (optional)
  • Dressing of choice (balsamic vinaigrette or ranch)

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss together the mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and avocado.
  2. Top the salad with crumbled bacon and cheese if desired.
  3. Drizzle with your favorite dressing and toss to combine.

This bacon salad is light yet satisfying, with the crispy bacon providing an irresistible crunch that takes the salad to the next level.

6. Bacon Mac and Cheese

Mac and cheese is a classic comfort food, and when you add bacon to the mix, it becomes an even more indulgent treat. Using leftover bacon in this creamy, cheesy dish is an excellent way to give your mac and cheese a smoky, savory twist.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni or any pasta shape
  • 4 slices of leftover bacon, crumbled
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup milk or heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions, then drain.
  2. In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy, then remove and set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, melt the butter and whisk in the flour to create a roux. Gradually add milk or cream, whisking continuously to prevent lumps.
  4. Stir in the shredded cheese and season with salt and pepper. Once the sauce is smooth, toss in the cooked pasta.
  5. Stir in the crumbled bacon and mix until well combined.

Bacon mac and cheese is a rich, comforting dish that pairs perfectly with a side salad or some crusty bread.

7. Bacon and Potato Soup

When the weather turns cooler, a hearty soup is the perfect way to use up your leftover bacon. This bacon and potato soup is creamy, comforting, and packed with flavor. The bacon adds a nice smoky kick that pairs beautifully with the richness of the soup.

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 large potatoes, diced
  • 6 slices of leftover bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the chopped bacon until crispy, then remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  3. Add the diced potatoes and broth, bringing the mixture to a boil. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Using an immersion blender, puree the soup to your desired consistency (smooth or chunky).
  5. Stir in the milk or cream and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Top with crispy bacon and fresh chives before serving.

This bacon and potato soup is a perfect way to use leftover bacon in a warming, satisfying meal.

8. Bacon-Wrapped Asparagus

Bacon-wrapped asparagus is a delicious, easy-to-make side dish that elevates the simple vegetable to new heights. The smoky bacon flavor pairs beautifully with the tender asparagus, making it a great addition to any dinner table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 6-8 slices of leftover bacon
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Wrap each asparagus spear with a slice of bacon, securing the bacon in place.
  3. Place the wrapped asparagus on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the bacon is crispy and the asparagus is tender.

Bacon-wrapped asparagus is an elegant side dish that works perfectly with roasted meats or grilled fish.

9. Bacon-Topped Pizza

Take your homemade pizza to the next level by adding crumbled leftover bacon to the toppings. Bacon pairs wonderfully with other ingredients like caramelized onions, mushrooms, and cheese, creating a mouthwatering pizza that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1-2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 3-4 slices of leftover bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup caramelized onions (optional)
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven according to the pizza dough instructions.
  2. Roll out the dough and spread the tomato sauce evenly over the surface.
  3. Top with shredded mozzarella cheese, crumbled bacon, and caramelized onions if using.
  4. Bake the pizza according to the dough’s instructions, until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and serve.

A bacon-topped pizza is a fun and flavorful way to enjoy your leftover bacon in a totally new form.

10. Bacon Chocolate Chip Cookies

For a truly unique and unexpected way to use bacon, consider adding it to dessert! Bacon’s salty and smoky flavor complements the sweetness of chocolate chips in these indulgent cookies, creating a perfect balance of savory and sweet.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 4-5 slices of leftover bacon, crumbled

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cream together the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until fluffy.
  3. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips and crumbled bacon.
  7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown.

These bacon chocolate chip cookies offer a playful twist on the classic treat, and the savory bacon provides an unforgettable contrast to the sweetness of the cookie.

Conclusion

Leftover bacon doesn’t have to be a one-dimensional ingredient. From savory dishes like pasta and sandwiches to more unexpected uses in salads and cookies, there are endless creative ways to incorporate bacon into your meals. Whether you’re making a hearty soup, a flavorful pizza, or even a sweet treat, bacon adds a richness and depth of flavor that can transform any dish.

Next time you have leftover bacon, don’t just toss it in the fridge—turn it into something new and delicious. With these recipes, you can enjoy the smoky, savory goodness of bacon in a variety of creative ways that will have everyone asking for seconds.
