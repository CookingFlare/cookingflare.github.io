Instant Pot BBQ Recipes: Smoky Flavors Without the Grill


Barbecue season is a time for smoky flavors, tender meats, and sticky-sweet sauces. But what if you don’t have access to a grill? Whether you live in an apartment, don’t have time to fire up the coals, or simply want an easier way to cook your barbecue favorites, the Instant Pot is here to save the day. This magical appliance makes it possible to whip up juicy, flavorful barbecue that rivals your favorite smokehouse—all without stepping outside. From fall-off-the-bone ribs to savory pulled pork, the Instant Pot offers a quicker, less messy way to satisfy your barbecue cravings. Let’s dive into some mouthwatering recipes that deliver those iconic smoky flavors straight from your kitchen.

Why Choose the Instant Pot for BBQ Recipes?

The Instant Pot is a multifunctional kitchen gadget that combines pressure cooking, sautéing, steaming, and slow cooking into a single device. What sets it apart for barbecue recipes is its ability to tenderize proteins quickly while locking in juices and flavors. Additionally, you can infuse barbecue sauces and spices directly into the meat, ensuring every bite is seasoned to perfection.

For barbecue lovers, the Instant Pot provides an indoor solution that mimics the tenderness you’d achieve with hours of low-and-slow smoking. Plus, it cuts down on prep and cleanup time. While you won’t replicate the exact charred, flame-kissed flavor of a grill, there are ways to enhance smokiness with the right ingredients—think liquid smoke, smoked paprika, or even a quick broil session to add that coveted caramelization.

Essential Tips for Instant Pot BBQ Success

Before jumping into the recipes, let’s go over some essential tips and tricks to master Instant Pot barbecue:

1. Use the Sauté Function

Many barbecue recipes benefit from searing the meat before pressure cooking. Browning the meat using the sauté function helps lock in juices and creates a flavorful crust. This extra step is especially helpful for ribs, pork shoulder, and chicken thighs.

2. Don’t Skip the Natural Release

For tender barbecue meats like ribs and brisket, always allow the Instant Pot to release pressure naturally for 10-15 minutes before performing a quick release. This gradual decompression helps relax the fibers in the protein, ensuring a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

3. Add Liquid Smoke for Smoky Depth

Since the Instant Pot doesn’t generate smoke like a barbecue smoker, incorporating a small amount of liquid smoke can provide that authentic smoky undertone. Smoked spices like chipotle powder, smoked paprika, or ancho chili powder are also great alternatives.

4. Build Layers of Flavor

Barbecue is all about bold, layered flavors. Use a combination of dry rubs, marinades, and sauces to create depth. Don’t hesitate to baste the cooked meat in more sauce and caramelize it under the broiler for a finishing touch.

Instant Pot BBQ Recipes to Try

### Recipe 1: Instant Pot Baby Back Ribs

Tender, saucy ribs are a barbecue classic, and the Instant Pot delivers perfect results in a fraction of the time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • ½ cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon liquid smoke
  • 1 ½ cups barbecue sauce (your favorite brand or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Remove the silver membrane from the back of the ribs for optimal tenderness.
  2. Combine the brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, salt, and black pepper in a small bowl. Generously rub the seasoning all over the ribs.
  3. Position a trivet inside the Instant Pot, and add the apple cider vinegar, water, and liquid smoke to the pot.
  4. Place the ribs on the trivet in a standing position, curving around the pot.
  5. Seal the lid, set the valve to sealing, and pressure cook on high for 25 minutes.
  6. Once cooking is complete, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes before venting any remaining pressure.
  7. Brush both sides of the ribs with barbecue sauce and transfer them to a baking sheet. Broil in the oven for 5-7 minutes to caramelize the sauce, then serve.

Recipe 2: Instant Pot Pulled Pork

Pulled pork is the ultimate barbecue comfort food, and it couldn’t be easier to make in the Instant Pot. Perfect for sandwiches, tacos, or loaded baked potatoes!

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds pork shoulder (Boston butt), cut into large chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon liquid smoke
  • 1 ½ cups barbecue sauce

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the smoked paprika, garlic powder, cayenne pepper, brown sugar, salt, and black pepper. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork.
  2. Turn on the sauté function of the Instant Pot. Add the olive oil, then sear the pork pieces on all sides until browned. Work in batches if necessary.
  3. Once all the pork is browned, add the chicken broth and liquid smoke to the pot, scraping the bottom to deglaze any browned bits.
  4. Cancel the sauté function. Return the pork to the pot, seal the lid, and set to pressure cook on high for 60 minutes.
  5. Allow the pressure to release naturally for 15 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
  6. Remove the pork and shred it with two forks. Toss it with barbecue sauce and serve.

Recipe 3: Instant Pot BBQ Chicken Wings

Juicy, saucy wings are the perfect party food, and the Instant Pot makes it easy to feed a crowd.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds chicken wings
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Season the wings with smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the trivet in the Instant Pot and add the water to the pot. Arrange the seasoned wings on the trivet.
  3. Seal the lid, set the valve to sealing, and pressure cook on high for 8 minutes.
  4. Perform a quick pressure release. Remove the wings from the pot and toss them in barbecue sauce mixed with honey (if using).
  5. For crispy, sticky wings, broil them in the oven for 5 minutes or until the sauce bubbles and caramelizes.

Add Your Signature Style

These recipes are a great starting point, but a big part of the fun of Instant Pot barbecue is experimenting. Mix up your own dry rubs, try different sauces, or play with side dishes like Instant Pot baked beans or cornbread to round out your meal. With a little creativity and your trusty Instant Pot, smoky flavors without the grill are just a button-push away.

Take the hassle out of barbecue without sacrificing flavor, and let your Instant Pot deliver smoky, savory goodness—all year round.
