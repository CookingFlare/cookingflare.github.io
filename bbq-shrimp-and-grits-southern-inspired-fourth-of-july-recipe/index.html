BBQ Shrimp and Grits: Southern-Inspired Fourth of July Recipe


When it comes to celebrating the Fourth of July, there’s nothing quite like the sizzle of a barbecue and the comforting flavors of Southern cuisine. This year, why not add a touch of Southern charm to your Independence Day celebration with a delicious BBQ Shrimp and Grits recipe? This iconic dish is a perfect blend of smoky, spicy, and savory flavors that will wow your guests and leave them craving for more. So, fire up the grill, dust off your favorite cast-iron skillet, and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

For the Shrimp:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Grits:

  • 1 cup stone-ground grits
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

For the BBQ Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Garnish:

  • Fresh chopped parsley
  • Sliced green onions

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the BBQ Sauce

In a small saucepan, combine all the BBQ sauce ingredients. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the sauce has thickened slightly, remove it from the heat and set it aside.

Step 2: Cook the Grits

In a large pot, bring the water and a pinch of salt to a boil. Slowly add the grits, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once the grits are creamy and tender, stir in the milk, butter, and cheddar cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then cover and keep warm.

Step 3: Grill the Shrimp

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. In a bowl, combine the shrimp, minced garlic, paprika, cayenne pepper, black pepper, olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Toss to coat the shrimp evenly. Thread the shrimp onto skewers and grill for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are pink and slightly charred. Remove the shrimp from the grill and set aside.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

Divide the creamy grits among serving bowls. Top with the grilled shrimp, then drizzle with the homemade BBQ sauce. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley and sliced green onions for a burst of color and freshness.

Enjoy Your Southern Feast!

There you have it - a mouthwatering BBQ Shrimp and Grits recipe that’s sure to be the star of your Fourth of July celebration. The smoky flavors from the grill, the creamy richness of the grits, and the tangy kick of the BBQ sauce come together to create a dish that embodies the spirit of Southern cooking. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or a cozy indoor gathering, this Southern-inspired recipe is guaranteed to impress your guests and leave them coming back for seconds. So, fire up the grill, get those grits simmering, and treat yourself and your loved ones to a taste of the South this Independence Day! Happy Fourth of July!
