Seafood Recipes with Coconut Milk


When it comes to marrying the flavors of the sea with the creamy, tropical richness of coconut milk, the result is nothing short of extraordinary. Seafood and coconut milk have a long-standing partnership in various coastal cuisines, especially in Southeast Asian, Caribbean, and South American cooking traditions. This combination not only enhances seafood’s natural sweetness but also imparts a velvety texture and exotic aroma that elevate any dish from simple to sublime.

In this guide, we’ll dive deep into a collection of diverse and exciting seafood recipes bathed in coconut milk. From fragrant curries to delicate stews, these dishes showcase how coconut milk creates harmony with fish, shrimp, crab, and shellfish. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or exploring new flavors for the first time, these recipes will inspire you to infuse your seafood repertoire with creamy, tropical goodness.

Why Coconut Milk Works Perfectly with Seafood

Seafood has its delicate and sometimes briny flavor that can be easily overpowered by aggressive spices or heavy sauces. Coconut milk offers a subtle sweetness and a silky mouthfeel that balances pierces of heat and tanginess often associated with tropical seafood dishes. Its fat content carries fat-soluble flavors, allowing herbs and spices like lemongrass, ginger, garlic, turmeric, and chili to bloom beautifully.

Moreover, coconut milk provides a natural richness without the heaviness of dairy, making it ideal for light yet satisfying meals. The slight nutty flavor adds complexity without masking the seawater freshness of fish or shellfish. This versatility means coconut milk can be used in a range of cooking techniques including simmering, stewing, and even grilling marinades.

Essential Ingredients for Coconut Milk Seafood Dishes

Before we jump into the recipes, here are some ingredients commonly paired for these tropical seafood marvels:

  • Coconut milk: Fresh or canned, full-fat for creaminess
  • Seafood: Firm white fish (snapper, cod, halibut), shellfish (shrimp, crab, mussels), squid, and clams
  • Aromatics: Garlic, shallots, lemongrass, ginger, galangal
  • Herbs: Cilantro, Thai basil, curry leaves, kaffir lime leaves
  • Spices: Turmeric, coriander, cumin, chili peppers, paprika
  • Acid: Lime juice, tamarind paste, or vinegar to balance richness
  • Vegetables: Bell peppers, tomatoes, okra, spinach, eggplant
  • Seasonings: Fish sauce, soy sauce, salt, and pepper

Recipe 1: Thai Coconut Curry Fish Stew

This fragrant and comforting curry is a classic example of coconut milk’s magic with fish. Layers of lemongrass, ginger, and chili elevate tender white fish in a spicy, creamy broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb firm white fish fillets, cut into chunks (such as cod or snapper)
  • 2 cups coconut milk (full-fat)
  • 1 cup fish stock or water
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised and chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 small red chilies, sliced (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh Thai basil or cilantro for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add a splash of oil. Sauté the onion, garlic, lemongrass, ginger, and red chilies until fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Stir in red curry paste and cook for another minute to release aromas.
  3. Pour in the coconut milk and fish stock. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add the fish chunks and bell pepper. Simmer gently for about 8 minutes or until the fish is cooked through but still tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning as desired.
  6. Remove lemongrass stalks before serving.
  7. Garnish with fresh Thai basil or cilantro.

Serve this curry with jasmine rice or sticky rice to soak up every bit of the luscious sauce.

Recipe 2: Caribbean Coconut Crab Curry

Caribbean cuisine boasts some of the richest uses of coconut milk with seafood. This crab curry recipe captures that sweet heat, blending turmeric, allspice, and scotch bonnet peppers for a dish bursting with island spirit.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh crab, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper, finely chopped (remove seeds for less heat)
  • 1 tbsp allspice
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a deep skillet or pot. Add onions, garlic, scotch bonnet pepper, and thyme. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  2. Stir in turmeric, allspice, and cinnamon. Cook spices for 1-2 minutes to release fragrance.
  3. Add diced tomatoes and brown sugar, cooking until tomatoes soften into a sauce.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add crab pieces, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Season with lime juice, salt, and pepper just before serving.

Pair with fluffy coconut rice or fried dumplings for a full Caribbean feast.

Recipe 3: Coconut & Lime Shrimp Soup

Sometimes simple is sublime. This light and zesty shrimp soup highlights the refreshing tang of lime balanced against the creamy coconut milk base, enriched with ginger and lemongrass.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 cups coconut milk
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised and chopped
  • 1-inch fresh ginger, sliced thin
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves (optional)
  • 1 red chili, sliced
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro and green onions to garnish

Instructions

  1. In a pot, combine coconut milk, lemongrass, ginger, garlic, lime leaves, and chili. Bring to a gentle simmer for 10 minutes to infuse flavors.
  2. Remove lemongrass stalks and lime leaves.
  3. Add mushrooms and cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Add shrimp and cook until pink and opaque, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. Stir in lime juice and fish sauce. Adjust seasoning to balance creamy, sour, and salty notes.
  6. Garnish with cilantro and green onions before serving.

This soup is refreshing yet comforting, perfect as a starter or light main meal.

Recipe 4: Malaysian Coconut Squid Rendang

Rendang is a slow-cooked dry curry with roots in Indonesia and Malaysia, traditionally made with beef but equally decadent with squid in coconut milk and spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb squid, cleaned and cut into rings
  • 2 cups thick coconut milk
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 red chilies, deseeded and chopped
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 star anise
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Blend onions, garlic, ginger, and chilies into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a wok or pot and fry the paste until fragrant and oil separates.
  3. Add coriander, cumin, turmeric, cinnamon stick, and star anise, frying for another 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Stir in tamarind paste and palm sugar.
  6. Add squid rings and cook uncovered on medium-low heat, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens and coats the squid (about 20-25 minutes).
  7. Adjust salt to taste, remove cinnamon and star anise before serving.

Serve with steamed rice or roti for a rich, aromatic experience.

Recipe 5: Creamy Coconut Mussels with Garlic and Herbs

A simple yet luxurious dish, mussels steamed in garlic-infused coconut milk with fresh herbs makes for a spectacular appetizer or light main course.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh mussels, cleaned and debearded
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup white wine or water
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • 1 small chili (optional), finely chopped
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat oil and sauté shallots and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add white wine and bring to a simmer.
  3. Pour in coconut milk, add chili, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Add mussels and cover pot.
  5. Cook for 5-7 minutes, shaking pot occasionally, until all mussels open (discard any unopened).
  6. Stir in lemon juice and sprinkle with fresh parsley.

Serve with crusty bread to mop up the fragrant broth.

Tips for Cooking with Coconut Milk and Seafood

  • Quality matters: Use full-fat coconut milk for the richest, creamiest texture.
  • Don’t overcook seafood: Most seafood cooks quickly; overcooking can make it tough and rubbery.
  • Balance is key: Coconut milk is rich, so balance dishes with acidity like lime, tamarind, or vinegar.
  • Spice it up cautiously: Add chili gradually to accommodate heat tolerance.
  • Infuse flavors: Simmer aromatics in coconut milk before adding seafood for maximum depth.
  • Fresh is best: Use fresh herbs and lime juice at the end for brightness.

Final Thoughts

Seafood recipes with coconut milk open up a luscious world of flavor that is both comforting and adventurous. From the light, tangy soups of Thailand to the slow-simmered crab curries of the Caribbean and the spicy rendangs of Malaysia, coconut milk acts as a versatile canvas for a global palette of spices and fresh seafood.

These dishes invite creativity and experimentation—try adding your favorite vegetables, tweaking spice levels, or swapping seafood types. Whether celebrating a special occasion or simply spicing up weeknight dinners, the creamy, fragrant harmony of coconut milk and seafood never fails to impress.

Craving a tropical twist in your kitchen? Dive into these recipes and savor the exquisite pairing of sea and coconut—one delicious spoonful at a time.
