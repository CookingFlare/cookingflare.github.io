Korean Kimchi Stew (Kimchi Jjigae) Recipe with Pork


If you’ve ever had the pleasure of tasting Korean cuisine, you’ve likely encountered Kimchi Jjigae, a beloved and hearty dish made with kimchi as its star ingredient. This flavorful stew, which is known for its savory, spicy, and slightly sour profile, has a unique ability to warm you up on a cold day or satisfy your cravings for something comforting. Adding pork to this dish enhances the umami flavors and makes it even more satisfying.

In this recipe, I’ll guide you step by step to create an authentic and delicious Kimchi Jjigae with Pork that you can easily make at home. This dish is quick to prepare, uses simple ingredients, and delivers a bold punch of flavors that will make it one of your go-to comfort food recipes.

What is Kimchi Jjigae?

Kimchi Jjigae, also known as Korean Kimchi Stew, is a staple in Korean households. It’s a versatile dish that can be made using various proteins like tofu, beef, and pork, but the classic and most popular version is made with pork. The dish relies on the fermented, spicy, and sour flavors of kimchi, which imparts a deep richness to the stew. Typically, the stew is served as a main course alongside a bowl of steamed rice, making it a perfect comfort meal.

The stew is traditionally made by simmering pork with kimchi, garlic, onion, tofu, and sometimes vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms. The broth is often seasoned with gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), gochujang (Korean chili paste), and sometimes doenjang (fermented soybean paste), which adds to the stew’s complex depth of flavor. The longer the stew simmers, the better the flavors meld together, creating a rich, savory, and spicy broth.

Key Ingredients for Kimchi Jjigae with Pork

1. Kimchi (Fermented Cabbage)

The key ingredient in Kimchi Jjigae is kimchi, which is made from fermented napa cabbage, radishes, garlic, ginger, and a variety of seasonings. You can use homemade or store-bought kimchi, but using mature, well-fermented kimchi is crucial. The fermentation gives the kimchi its distinct sour flavor, which is essential for the stew.

2. Pork

In this version of Kimchi Jjigae, we use pork belly or pork shoulder for its tender, juicy texture. Pork belly is rich in fat, making the stew hearty and flavorful. The pork complements the tangy kimchi and balances the heat from the gochujang and gochugaru.

3. Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste)

Gochujang is a fermented Korean chili paste made from red chili peppers, glutinous rice, fermented soybeans, and salt. It adds heat, depth, and a touch of sweetness to the stew, enhancing the flavor profile. It is a staple in many Korean dishes and adds that characteristic spicy and slightly sweet kick to the stew.

4. Gochugaru (Korean Chili Flakes)

Gochugaru is another important ingredient in Kimchi Jjigae. It adds a different type of heat compared to gochujang and helps to elevate the spice level of the dish. Gochugaru also gives the stew a vibrant red color, making it visually appealing.

5. Tofu

Tofu is often added to Kimchi Jjigae to balance the richness of the pork and add a soft, silky texture to the stew. Firm tofu is best, as it holds up well in the broth without falling apart.

6. Onion and Garlic

Onions and garlic are essential aromatics that add a sweet, savory, and pungent depth of flavor to the stew. Sautéing these ingredients with the pork helps to build the base flavor for the broth.

7. Vegetables (Optional)

While not strictly necessary, adding vegetables such as zucchini, shiitake mushrooms, or potatoes can give the dish extra texture and flavor. These vegetables absorb the rich broth, making each bite a burst of flavor.

8. Seasonings

A few seasonings like soy sauce, sesame oil, fish sauce, and salt are added to further enhance the stew’s complexity. These ingredients balance the saltiness, umami, and savory elements in the dish.

How to Make Kimchi Jjigae with Pork

Preparation Time: 10-15 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Serves: 4-6 people

Ingredients:

  • 300g (10 oz) of pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of well-fermented kimchi (about 1/2 small jar), chopped into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 block of firm tofu, sliced into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional, but recommended for added umami)
  • 1 tablespoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 medium zucchini (optional), sliced into half-moons
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms or other mushrooms (optional), sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, to balance the acidity of the kimchi)
  • 2 tablespoons green onions (for garnish)
  • Steamed rice (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    • Start by slicing the pork belly or pork shoulder into thin, bite-sized pieces. Set aside.
    • Chop the kimchi into bite-sized pieces if it isn’t already cut.
    • Slice the tofu into 1-inch cubes and set aside.
    • Slice the onion, zucchini, and mushrooms (if using).

  2. Sauté the Aromatics and Pork:

    • In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil over medium heat. Once hot, add the sliced onions and sauté until softened and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
    • Add the minced garlic and sauté for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
    • Add the sliced pork belly to the pot and cook until the pork is browned and crispy around the edges, about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Add Kimchi and Seasonings:

    • Stir in the chopped kimchi, gochujang, and gochugaru. Let the kimchi sauté for 2-3 minutes to release its flavors and heat up the chili paste.
    • Add the soy sauce, fish sauce (if using), and a pinch of sugar (optional). Stir everything to combine and coat the pork and kimchi with the seasonings.

  4. Simmer the Stew:

    • Pour in the vegetable broth (or water) and bring the mixture to a boil. Once it boils, reduce the heat to medium-low and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes. This will allow the pork to tenderize and the flavors to meld together.
    • If you like your stew extra spicy, feel free to add more gochugaru at this point. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with additional salt or soy sauce if needed.

  5. Add Tofu and Vegetables:

    • After the stew has simmered for 25-30 minutes, add the tofu, zucchini, and mushrooms (if using). Let the stew simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and the tofu has absorbed some of the broth’s flavors.

  6. Final Adjustments:

    • Taste the stew one last time to check the seasoning. You can add more gochujang for heat, soy sauce for saltiness, or sugar to balance the acidity. Adjust as desired.

  7. Serve:

    • Once everything is cooked and the flavors are well-developed, remove the pot from the heat. Ladle the Kimchi Jjigae into bowls, garnishing with chopped green onions for a fresh touch.
    • Serve with a bowl of steamed rice and enjoy!

Tips for Making the Best Kimchi Jjigae

  • Use well-fermented kimchi: The longer the kimchi has been fermented, the more intense the sourness and umami flavors will be. Older kimchi is perfect for making stew as it adds more depth.
  • Pork belly is ideal: Pork belly provides the perfect balance of fat and meat for the stew. However, if you prefer a leaner option, pork shoulder works well too.
  • Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder stew, reduce the amount of gochujang and gochugaru. Conversely, if you love spicy food, feel free to add extra chili flakes.
  • Add vegetables of your choice: Feel free to experiment with adding other vegetables like potatoes, carrots, or even leafy greens like napa cabbage or spinach for more texture and nutrition.
  • Simmer longer for richer flavor: The longer the stew simmers, the more the flavors will develop. You can even make the stew a day ahead and reheat it for even better taste.

Conclusion

Kimchi Jjigae with pork is a must-try Korean comfort food that packs a punch of bold, savory, and spicy flavors. Whether you’re making it on a chilly evening or looking to try something new, this dish will surely become a family favorite. With its simple ingredients, hearty texture, and depth of flavor, Kimchi Jjigae is a true representation of the delicious versatility of Korean cuisine. Enjoy it with a bowl of steamed rice for the ultimate comforting meal!
