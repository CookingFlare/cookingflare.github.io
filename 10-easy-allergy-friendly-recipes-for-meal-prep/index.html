10 Easy Allergy-Friendly Recipes for Meal Prep


Meal prepping is a game-changer for busy individuals looking to maintain a healthy lifestyle, especially for those managing food allergies. When you have specific dietary restrictions, planning your meals ahead of time is essential to ensure you’re not only eating safely but also enjoying delicious, nutritious meals. In this post, we’ll explore 10 easy allergy-friendly recipes that are perfect for meal prep. These recipes are free from common allergens like gluten, dairy, nuts, and eggs, making them suitable for a variety of dietary needs. Whether you’re new to allergy-friendly eating or just looking for some fresh ideas to add to your rotation, these recipes will help keep you on track and satisfied all week long.

1. Quinoa and Chickpea Salad

A fresh, hearty salad that’s full of plant-based protein and fiber, this quinoa and chickpea salad is perfect for lunch or dinner. It’s packed with colorful veggies and a zesty dressing, making it not only allergy-friendly but also highly nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chickpeas, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, and cherry tomatoes.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

This salad can be stored in an airtight container for up to four days in the fridge. It’s a refreshing, filling meal that requires minimal prep time and is free of common allergens.

2. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Burrito Bowls

If you’re craving something hearty and satisfying, sweet potato and black bean burrito bowls are a great option. These bowls are not only allergy-friendly but also versatile. You can easily swap out ingredients depending on your preferences or what you have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the sweet potato cubes with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the sweet potatoes in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly crispy.
  4. In a bowl, layer the roasted sweet potatoes, black beans, avocado, and cilantro.
  5. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the top before serving.

These burrito bowls can be stored in meal prep containers for up to four days. They’re perfect for a filling lunch or dinner and are packed with vitamins, fiber, and healthy fats.

3. Lentil and Veggie Stir-Fry

Lentils are a fantastic source of plant-based protein and fiber, making them an excellent option for any meal. This easy stir-fry combines lentils with a variety of colorful vegetables, making it a satisfying and allergy-friendly dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils, rinsed and cooked
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons gluten-free soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, sautéing for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the broccoli, carrot, bell pepper, and zucchini. Cook, stirring occasionally, for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Add the cooked lentils to the skillet and stir to combine.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and a pinch of salt.
  6. Pour the sauce over the lentil and veggie mixture, stirring to coat evenly.
  7. Serve warm, or divide into meal prep containers for later use.

This stir-fry can be stored in the fridge for up to five days, making it a great option for a make-ahead lunch or dinner. It’s high in protein, fiber, and packed with vitamins.

4. Chia Pudding with Berries and Coconut

This chia pudding is a simple and nutritious breakfast option for anyone following an allergy-friendly diet. It’s dairy-free, gluten-free, and full of fiber, making it a great way to start your day.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any dairy-free milk)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries)
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened shredded coconut

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine chia seeds, almond milk, vanilla extract, and maple syrup (if using).
  2. Stir well to ensure the chia seeds are evenly distributed.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to allow the chia seeds to absorb the liquid and form a pudding-like consistency.
  4. Before serving, top with mixed berries and shredded coconut.

Chia pudding is an excellent option for meal prep. You can make several servings at once and store them in individual containers in the fridge for up to five days.

5. Grilled Chicken with Quinoa and Roasted Vegetables

This balanced meal is rich in protein, fiber, and healthy fats, and it’s simple to prepare for a week of delicious lunches or dinners. The grilled chicken pairs perfectly with quinoa and roasted vegetables for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 sweet potato, cubed
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium heat.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and dried oregano.
  3. Grill the chicken for 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
  4. While the chicken is grilling, toss the zucchini, bell pepper, and sweet potato with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Spread the vegetables on a baking sheet and roast at 400°F (200°C) for 25-30 minutes, or until tender.
  6. Serve the grilled chicken alongside the roasted vegetables and quinoa, and drizzle with fresh lemon juice.

This meal can be stored in individual containers for up to four days, making it perfect for meal prep. It’s balanced and satisfying, with a great mix of lean protein, healthy fats, and fiber.

6. Baked Falafel with Tahini Sauce

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from chickpeas and herbs, and it’s perfect for meal prep. This baked version is lower in fat than traditional fried falafel and just as delicious. Pair it with a tangy tahini sauce for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup gluten-free breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the tahini sauce:

  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1-2 tablespoons water (to thin)
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, parsley, cilantro, onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, lemon juice, breadcrumbs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pulse until the mixture is well combined but still slightly chunky.
  4. Roll the mixture into small balls and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden brown and crispy.
  6. For the tahini sauce, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, water, and salt until smooth.
  7. Serve the falafel with the tahini sauce on the side.

These baked falafels can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to five days, making them a great meal prep option for lunch or dinner.

7. Zucchini Noodles with Pesto

Zucchini noodles are a fantastic gluten-free, low-carb alternative to traditional pasta. Paired with a fresh, homemade pesto, this dish is light yet satisfying and perfect for meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (or sunflower seeds for a nut-free version)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (optional)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, nutritional yeast, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Pulse until smooth, scraping down the sides as needed.
  3. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add the zucchini noodles.
  4. Cook for 2-3 minutes, just until the noodles are tender but still firm.
  5. Toss the noodles with the pesto sauce and serve.

Zucchini noodles with pesto can be stored in an airtight container for up to three days. They’re light, refreshing, and full of flavor—perfect for a quick meal prep solution.

8. Stuffed Bell Peppers

Stuffed bell peppers are a classic comfort food, and they’re easy to make allergy-friendly. This version is filled with quinoa, black beans, and spices, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, chili powder, cumin, corn, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stuff the bell peppers with the quinoa mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  4. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
  5. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes.

These stuffed peppers are perfect for meal prep. Store them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to five days.

9. Coconut Curry Vegetables

This coconut curry is rich, creamy, and full of flavor. It’s made with simple ingredients like coconut milk, curry paste, and mixed vegetables. It’s a comforting, allergy-friendly dish that’s perfect for meal prepping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 can coconut milk (full-fat)
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, carrot, bell pepper, and zucchini, sautéing for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the coconut milk, curry paste, turmeric, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the flavors are well combined.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This curry can be stored in an airtight container for up to four days, making it a perfect addition to your meal prep routine.

10. Apple Cinnamon Overnight Oats

For a quick and easy breakfast, overnight oats are a fantastic option. This apple cinnamon version is both comforting and nutritious, and it’s easy to prepare ahead of time.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 apple, diced
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a mason jar or airtight container, combine the oats, almond milk, apple, cinnamon, and maple syrup.
  2. Stir well to combine, then cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. In the morning, stir again and enjoy cold or heat it up for a warm breakfast.

These overnight oats can be stored in the fridge for up to five days. They’re a quick and easy breakfast solution that’s both allergy-friendly and delicious.

These 10 easy allergy-friendly recipes are perfect for meal prep and will help you stay on track with your healthy eating goals. Each recipe is simple, nutritious, and free from common allergens, making them ideal for anyone with dietary restrictions. Whether you’re looking for a hearty lunch, a satisfying dinner, or a quick breakfast, these recipes have you covered.
