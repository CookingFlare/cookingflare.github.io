Unique Ice Cream Flavors You Need to Try This Summer


As the summer sun blazes down, there’s nothing quite as satisfying as a scoop (or two) of ice cream. While classic flavors like vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry will always have a special place in our hearts, why not shake things up this season? This summer, we invite you to step outside the box and indulge in some unique ice cream flavors that promise to tantalize your taste buds and make your ice cream experience truly unforgettable.

1. Lavender Honey

If you’ve never tried lavender in your desserts, you’re in for a delightful surprise. Lavender honey ice cream combines the floral notes of lavender with the sweetness of honey, creating a refreshing and aromatic treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon dried culinary lavender
  • 4 egg yolks
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream, milk, and lavender over medium heat until it begins to steam. Remove from heat and let steep for about 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the lavender buds.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and honey until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the warm cream mixture to the egg yolks, whisking constantly.
  5. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat until it thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  6. Pour into a bowl, cover, and chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  7. Churn in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

2. Avocado Lime

Creamy, rich avocado blended with zesty lime creates an ice cream flavor that is both refreshing and indulgent. This unique combination is perfect for those hot summer days when you crave something a bit different.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the avocados, coconut milk, sugar, lime juice, lime zest, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  2. Taste and adjust sweetness as needed.
  3. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Transfer to a container and freeze for at least 2 hours before serving.

3. Spicy Chocolate

For those who love a kick, spicy chocolate ice cream will certainly satisfy your cravings. Infusing chocolate with chili powder or cayenne pepper creates a bold and exciting flavor that will leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, sugar, cocoa powder, cayenne pepper, and salt over medium heat. Stir until the mixture is smooth and heated through.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  3. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours.
  4. Pour into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

4. Earl Grey Tea

Earl Grey tea ice cream offers a sophisticated flavor profile that’s perfect for tea lovers. The bergamot citrus flavor pairs beautifully with the creaminess of ice cream, resulting in a unique and aromatic dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 4 tablespoons loose Earl Grey tea (or 4 tea bags)
  • 4 egg yolks
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Add the Earl Grey tea and heat until steaming. Remove from heat and let steep for 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the tea leaves or bags.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the egg yolks.
  4. Gradually add the warm cream mixture to the egg yolks, whisking constantly.
  5. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat until it thickens.
  6. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, then churn in an ice cream maker.

5. Sweet Corn and Black Raspberry

This sweet corn and black raspberry ice cream is a delightful summer treat that combines the sweetness of fresh summer corn with the tartness of black raspberries. It’s a flavor that’s both surprising and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh corn kernels (about 4 ears)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 cup black raspberries
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine corn, heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Heat until steaming, then let steep for about 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the corn kernels.
  3. In a blender, combine the strained mixture with the black raspberries and blend until smooth.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, then churn in an ice cream maker.

6. Basil and Lemon

Basil may seem like an odd ingredient for ice cream, but its fresh and herbaceous flavor pairs wonderfully with the brightness of lemon. This refreshing treat is perfect for hot summer days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Juice and zest of 2 lemons
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Add the basil leaves and heat until steaming. Remove from heat and let steep for 30 minutes.
  2. Strain the mixture to remove the basil.
  3. Stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, and salt.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, then churn in an ice cream maker.

7. Maple Bacon

For those who love the sweet and salty combination, maple bacon ice cream is a must-try. The rich flavor of maple syrup paired with the savory crunch of bacon creates an indulgent experience.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup maple syrup
  • ½ cup cooked and crumbled bacon
  • 4 egg yolks
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, milk, and maple syrup. Heat until steaming.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the egg yolks.
  3. Gradually add the warm cream mixture to the egg yolks, whisking constantly.
  4. Return to the saucepan and cook until thickened.
  5. Stir in the crumbled bacon and chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours. Churn in an ice cream maker.

8. Matcha Green Tea

Matcha green tea ice cream offers a unique twist on traditional flavors. The earthy flavor of matcha combined with creamy ice cream makes for a refreshing and health-conscious treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons matcha green tea powder
  • 4 egg yolks
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, milk, and sugar. Heat until steaming.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and matcha powder until smooth.
  3. Gradually add the warm cream mixture to the egg yolks, whisking constantly.
  4. Return to the saucepan and cook until thickened.
  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, then churn in an ice cream maker.

9. Coconut Curry

Coconut curry ice cream is for the adventurous palate. The richness of coconut cream paired with the spices of curry creates a truly unique flavor that will surprise and delight.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine coconut cream, whole milk, sugar, curry powder, and salt. Heat until steaming.
  2. Whisk until smooth and combined.
  3. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, then churn in an ice cream maker.

10. Blue Cheese and Fig

For the cheese lovers among us, blue cheese and fig ice cream is a decadent treat. The sharpness of blue cheese beautifully contrasts with the sweetness of figs, resulting in a complex and delicious flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ½ cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1 cup dried figs, chopped
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine heavy cream, whole milk, and sugar. Heat until steaming.
  2. Stir in the crumbled blue cheese until melted.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in the chopped figs and salt.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, then churn in an ice cream maker.

Conclusion

This summer, don’t settle for the same old flavors. Embrace the opportunity to experiment with unique ice cream recipes that will elevate your dessert game. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply treating yourself, these flavors will impress and delight. So grab your ice cream maker and get ready to scoop up some creativity this season!
