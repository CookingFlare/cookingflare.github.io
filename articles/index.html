Impress Your Guests: Fourth of July BBQ Recipe Ideas

The Fourth of July is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with a sizzling BBQ for your friends and family? If you’re looking to impress your guests with some mouthwatering dishes, we’ve got you covered. From succulent grilled meats to fresh and vibrant salads, these Fourth of July BBQ recipes will take your celebration to the next level. Grilled Honey Mustard Chicken Skewers Ingredients: 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts 1/2 cup honey 1/4 cup Dijon mustard 2 tablespoons soy sauce 2 cloves garlic, minced Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto skewers. In a bowl, whisk together the honey, Dijon mustard, soy sauce, garlic, salt, and pepper. Brush the honey mustard mixture over the chicken skewers, ensuring they are well coated. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and place the skewers on the grill. Grill for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a delicious charred exterior. Smoked BBQ Pulled Pork Sliders Ingredients: 3 lbs pork shoulder 1 cup BBQ sauce 1 tablespoon smoked paprika 1 tablespoon brown sugar 1 teaspoon garlic powder Slider buns Coleslaw for topping Instructions: Rub the pork shoulder with the smoked paprika, brown sugar, and garlic powder. Prepare your smoker according to manufacturer instructions and smoke the pork shoulder at 225°F for 6-8 hours, or until it easily shreds with a fork. Once the pork is smoked, shred it using two forks and mix in the BBQ sauce. Toast the slider buns on the grill and assemble the sliders with the BBQ pulled pork and a dollop of fresh coleslaw. Grilled Corn and Avocado Salad Ingredients: 4 ears of corn, husked 2 ripe avocados, diced 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped Juice of 2 limes 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat the grill to medium heat and grill the corn for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until charred in spots. Once the corn is cool enough to handle, cut the kernels off the cob and place them in a large bowl. Add the diced avocado, halved cherry tomatoes, red onion, and cilantro to the bowl with the corn. In a small bowl, whisk together the lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to combine. Grilled Watermelon and Feta Skewers Ingredients: 1 small seedless watermelon, cut into cubes 8 oz feta cheese, cut into cubes Fresh mint leaves Balsamic glaze for drizzling Instructions: Thread the watermelon cubes, feta cheese cubes, and mint leaves onto skewers. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and place the skewers on the grill. Grill for 1-2 minutes on each side, just until the watermelon and feta have grill marks.
