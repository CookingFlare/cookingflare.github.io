Whip up Traditional Chinese Desserts with these Recipe Ideas


Chinese desserts are a heavenly concoction of sweet and savory flavors that never fail to tantalize taste buds. These delightful treats are not just limited to dim sum and noodles; prepare to indulge your sweet tooth with an array of diverse and scrumptious traditional Chinese desserts. This blog will take you on a culinary journey, introducing you to a few exotic recipe ideas that evoke feelings of warmth and nostalgia. Let’s journey beyond the ordinary and elevate your dessert game with these traditional Chinese delicacies.

1. Red Bean Soup

A popular Chinese dessert that never loses its charm, red bean soup is a humble and warming delight. Known as “hong dou tang” in Chinese, it is made from sweet red beans cooked in a rich syrup, sugar, and various accoutrements such as dried winter melon or lotus seeds. The resulting dish is a luscious, comforting spoonful of sweet heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red beans, soaked overnight
  • 3 cups water
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1 piece dried winter melon or dried lotus seeds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine soaked red beans with water, brown sugar, and any additional ingredients (dried winter melon or lotus seeds).
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 2 hours or until the red beans are tender.
  3. Serve hot in small bowls.

While red bean soup may sound simple, it embodies the essence of natural goodness and showcases the harmonious blend of flavors that elevate this comforting dessert to new heights.

2. Sweet Rice Dumplings

Also known as “tang shui baow)(“汤水 عبد”), sweet rice dumplings are a sumptuous Chinese dessert that is as delectable as it is fragrant. The dumplings are made with savory glutinous rice, mung beans, and red beans, wrapped in a tender rice flour wrapper, and boiled in a sweet syrup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • ½ cup red beans
  • ½ cup mung beans
  • 4 cups water
  • 3 cups sugar
  • 6 cups water (for rice wrappers)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, cook glutinous rice, red beans, and mung beans in water until tender.
  2. Once cooked, mash the mixture with a fork or potato masher until the consistency resembles a coarse paste.
  3. Mix in sugar until evenly distributed. Set aside the filling.
  4. Prepare thin rice flour sheets by mixing rice flour with water and cooking the mixture to create a soft dough. Roll out the dough into thin sheets.
  5. Place a scoop of filling in the center of each rice flour wrapper and fold to encase the filling. Secure the edges.
  6. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and carefully place the dumplings into the boiling water for about 3-5 minutes.
  7. Remove from the water, transfer to a bowl of cold water, and allow them to soak for a minute.
  8. Serve the sweet rice dumplings hot or cold, depending on your preference.

3. Mango Pudding

For a refreshing twist, indulge in the glorious creation that is mango pudding. Merging the velvety smoothness of the pudding and the tropical sweetness of ripe mangoes, this dessert captures a burst of vibrant flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangoes
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons jelly powder (mango or strawberry flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon agar-agar powder

Instructions:

  1. Blend ripe mangoes in a blender to achieve a smooth puree.
  2. In a pot, combine mango puree, water, jelly powder, sugar, and agar-agar powder.
  3. Stir continuously over medium heat until the mixture comes to a boil. Once boiling, reduce heat and allow it to simmer for 2 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and strain the mixture through a sieve into a bowl.
  5. Allow the pudding to cool before chilling in the refrigerator for at least an hour.
  6. Serve chilled, garnished with diced fresh mango pieces.

Each of these desserts offers a unique taste and a delightfully captivating experience in Thai culinary tradition. Whether you’re looking to satisfy a sweet craving or aim to impress your dinner guests, incorporating these recipes will bring a touch of authentic flavor and touch of antiquity to your dessert table. Explore the many enchanting charms of Chinese desserts and create unforgettable memories with each bite.
