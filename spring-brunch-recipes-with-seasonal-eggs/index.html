Spring Brunch: Recipes with Seasonal Eggs


Spring is the perfect time to gather friends and family for a delightful brunch. And what better ingredient to highlight during this season than eggs? With their vibrant colors and fresh flavors, seasonal eggs can take your brunch dishes to the next level. In this blog post, we will explore three delicious recipes that celebrate the versatility of eggs in the springtime. So, let’s get cracking!

1. Asparagus and Goat Cheese Frittata

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Cooking time: 25 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 ounces of goat cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the asparagus and red onion, and sauté for 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the edges start to set.
  5. Sprinkle the crumbled goat cheese evenly over the frittata.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20 minutes until the frittata is puffed and golden.
  7. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serve this asparagus and goat cheese frittata with a side salad or crusty bread for a satisfying spring brunch option.

2. Smoked Salmon and Dill Deviled Eggs

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 6 hard-boiled eggs
  • 2 ounces of smoked salmon, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cut the hard-boiled eggs in half lengthwise. Carefully remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
  2. Mash the egg yolks with a fork until smooth. Add the smoked salmon, mayonnaise, dill, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Spoon the egg yolk mixture back into the egg white halves.
  4. Garnish with a sprig of dill or a small piece of smoked salmon.
  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

These smoked salmon and dill deviled eggs are a sophisticated and flavorful addition to any spring brunch spread.

3. Lemon Blueberry Dutch Baby Pancake

Preparation time: 10 minutes | Cooking time: 25 minutes | Serves: 2-4

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of fresh blueberries
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a blender, combine the eggs, milk, flour, sugar, lemon zest, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  3. In a 10-inch cast-iron skillet or oven-safe pan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  4. Add the blueberries to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes until they start to burst.
  5. Pour the batter over the blueberries in the skillet.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20 minutes until the pancake is puffed and golden.
  7. Remove from the oven, dust with powdered sugar, and serve immediately.

This lemon blueberry Dutch baby pancake is a show-stopping dish that will impress your brunch guests with its fluffy texture and bright flavors.

Final Thoughts

Spring brunches are a wonderful opportunity to celebrate the season’s bounty, and eggs are a fantastic ingredient to incorporate into your dishes. Whether you prefer savory or sweet, these recipes showcase the versatility of eggs and their ability to elevate any brunch spread. So, gather your loved ones, set a beautiful table, and enjoy the fresh flavors of spring with these delightful egg-based recipes. Happy brunching!
