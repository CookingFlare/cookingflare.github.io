Picnic Vegetables: Grilled and Marinated Goodness


Welcome to our latest recipe for picnic vegetables! In this post, we’ll be exploring the delicious world of grilled and marinated veggies that are perfect for your next outdoor gathering. Whether you’re planning a picnic in the park or a backyard barbecue, these flavorful and colorful vegetables will be a hit with your family and friends. So let’s get started!

Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Grilled vegetable skewers are a classic picnic dish that is not only visually appealing but also bursting with flavors. Here’s how you can make your own mouthwatering grilled vegetable skewers:

Ingredients:

  • Bell peppers (red, yellow, and green)
  • Zucchini
  • Eggplant
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Red onion
  • Olive oil
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Garlic powder
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Cut the bell peppers, zucchini, and eggplant into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between different colors and varieties for an attractive presentation.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper to create a marinade.
  5. Brush the marinade over the vegetable skewers, ensuring that each piece is well-coated.
  6. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  7. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them cool slightly before serving.

These grilled vegetable skewers are not only a feast for the eyes but also packed with smoky flavors. Serve them as a colorful side dish or as a vegetarian main course option at your next picnic.

Marinated Summer Salad

If you’re looking for a refreshing and tangy salad to accompany your grilled meats or sandwiches, this marinated summer salad is the perfect choice. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • Cucumbers
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Red onion
  • Kalamata olives
  • Feta cheese
  • Fresh parsley
  • Lemon juice
  • Olive oil
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Slice the cucumbers, halve the cherry tomatoes, finely chop the red onion, and roughly chop the fresh parsley.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, red onion, Kalamata olives, and crumbled feta cheese.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper to create a dressing.
  4. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat all the ingredients.
  5. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Before serving, garnish the salad with fresh parsley for an extra burst of freshness.

This marinated summer salad is a light and refreshing addition to any picnic spread. The combination of tangy feta cheese, juicy cherry tomatoes, and crisp cucumbers will keep you coming back for more.

Grilled Corn on the Cob

No picnic is complete without some juicy and smoky grilled corn on the cob. Here’s how you can make this classic picnic favorite:

Ingredients:

  • Fresh corn on the cob
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Paprika
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Peel back the husks of the corn, leaving them attached at the base.
  3. Remove the silk from the corn and then fold the husks back over the corn.
  4. Soak the corn in cold water for about 10 minutes.
  5. Remove the corn from the water and pat it dry.
  6. Place the corn on the preheated grill and cook for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and slightly charred.
  7. While the corn is grilling, melt the butter and mix it with salt and paprika in a small bowl.
  8. Remove the corn from the grill and carefully peel back the husks.
  9. Brush the butter mixture over the corn and squeeze fresh lime juice over the top.
  10. Serve the grilled corn on the cob immediately.

Grilled corn on the cob is a beloved picnic staple that will be sure to satisfy your taste buds. The combination of smoky flavors, creamy butter, and tangy lime creates a truly irresistible treat.

Conclusion

With these delicious recipes for grilled and marinated picnic vegetables, you’ll be well-prepared to impress your guests at your next outdoor gathering. Whether you choose to make the vibrant grilled vegetable skewers, the refreshing marinated summer salad, or the classic grilled corn on the cob, your picnic spread will be bursting with flavors and colors. So fire up that grill, pack your picnic basket, and enjoy the delightful goodness of these tasty vegetable dishes!
