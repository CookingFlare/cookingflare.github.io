Creamy and Tangy: Homemade Caesar Dressing


If there’s one thing that can take your Caesar salad to the next level, it’s a creamy and tangy homemade Caesar dressing. Forget about the store-bought stuff with its preservatives and additives – making your own dressing from scratch is not only healthier, but it also allows you to customize the flavors to your liking. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a luscious dressing that will elevate your salad game.

Ingredients

To make this delicious homemade Caesar dressing, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped anchovy fillets (if using), Dijon mustard, grated Parmesan cheese, and Worcestershire sauce. Make sure all the ingredients are well combined.

  2. Season the dressing with salt and pepper to taste. Keep in mind that the anchovies and Parmesan cheese already add some saltiness to the dressing, so go easy on the salt at first and adjust later if needed.

  3. Once the dressing is thoroughly mixed, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. This step is crucial for achieving the perfect balance of creaminess and tanginess.

  4. After chilling, give the dressing a quick stir to ensure it’s well-blended. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  5. Your creamy and tangy homemade Caesar dressing is now ready to be used. Drizzle it over crisp romaine lettuce, sprinkle with additional Parmesan cheese, and toss with croutons for a classic Caesar salad. You can also use it as a dip for fresh vegetables or as a spread for sandwiches and wraps.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version of this dressing, simply omit the anchovy fillets. The other ingredients will still create a flavorful and creamy dressing.

  • If you prefer a lighter dressing, you can use Greek yogurt or sour cream in place of some of the mayonnaise. This will give the dressing a tangy twist while reducing the overall fat content.

  • Feel free to experiment with additional flavors to customize your Caesar dressing. Some popular additions include a dash of hot sauce for a spicy kick, a teaspoon of honey for a touch of sweetness, or a tablespoon of chopped fresh herbs like parsley, basil, or chives for added freshness.

  • If you’re making a large batch of dressing, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to one week. Just give it a good shake before using to recombine any separated ingredients.

With this homemade Caesar dressing recipe, you’ll never go back to store-bought. The creamy texture, tangy flavors, and customizable nature of this dressing will make your salads shine. So, go ahead and take your Caesar salad to the next level with a dressing that’s made with love and fresh ingredients. Enjoy!
