How to Make Middle Eastern Spinach and Feta Pies (Sfeeha)


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its rich flavors, aromatic spices, and delicious combinations of ingredients that offer both a delightful experience and a connection to centuries of culinary tradition. One such dish is the beloved Spinach and Feta Pies, also known as Sfeeha in some regions. These savory pies are popular throughout the Middle East and are often served as a snack, appetizer, or part of a larger meal. Their crisp pastry shell and flavorful, aromatic filling make them a favorite among people of all ages.

In this blog post, we’ll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to prepare these delectable pies at home. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or just craving a tasty snack, Spinach and Feta Pies will be the star of your table. Let’s dive into the recipe, explore the ingredients, and perfect this Middle Eastern classic.

What Are Sfeeha?

Sfeeha are savory pastries that are typically filled with a variety of ingredients, but one of the most common fillings is spinach mixed with feta cheese. The name Sfeeha can be used to refer to a wide variety of Middle Eastern pies, and in some regions, it refers to meat-filled pies. However, the spinach and feta variation is a lighter, vegetarian version that is both nutritious and satisfying.

The filling for Sfeeha combines the earthy flavor of fresh spinach with the tangy, creamy taste of feta cheese, which is balanced by herbs like dill, parsley, and a hint of lemon for brightness. The pastry, which is either soft or crispy, complements the savory filling perfectly.

Ingredients for Spinach and Feta Pies (Sfeeha)

To make Sfeeha, you’ll need to gather a few essential ingredients that come together to create the perfect balance of flavors. Here’s what you’ll need for the spinach and feta filling and the dough:

For the Dough:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for rolling)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 packet of dry yeast (2 ¼ teaspoons)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin (optional for added flavor)
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 egg (for egg wash, optional)

For the Filling:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ pounds fresh spinach, chopped (or you can use frozen spinach, but fresh is preferred for the best flavor)
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh dill (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Spinach and Feta Pies (Sfeeha)

The process of making Sfeeha involves two key components: preparing the dough and making the filling. We will walk you through each step, so you can recreate this tasty treat with ease.

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

The dough for Sfeeha is soft, slightly chewy, and easy to work with. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and dry yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5 to 10 minutes, until the mixture becomes frothy. This step ensures that the yeast is activated and will help the dough rise.

  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, sift the flour, salt, and ground cumin (if using). If you are using a stand mixer, attach the dough hook, and if you’re mixing by hand, use a wooden spoon.

  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Add the activated yeast mixture, olive oil, and lemon juice to the dry ingredients. Stir until everything is well combined, and a dough begins to form. If you find that the dough is too sticky, you can add a bit more flour (a tablespoon at a time) until the dough is smooth and no longer sticks to your hands.

  4. Knead the dough: Once the dough comes together, knead it on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes. The dough should become soft, elastic, and smooth.

  5. Let the dough rise: Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

Step 2: Make the Spinach and Feta Filling

While the dough is rising, you can prepare the spinach and feta filling. Here’s how:

  1. Sauté the onions and garlic: In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes, until the onions are soft and translucent.

  2. Cook the spinach: Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook for about 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally. If you’re using fresh spinach, it will wilt down considerably. Cook until all the moisture has evaporated, leaving you with dry, tender spinach. If you’re using frozen spinach, ensure it’s thawed and drained of excess water before adding it to the skillet.

  3. Mix the ingredients: Remove the spinach mixture from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Once it’s cool enough to handle, transfer it to a large mixing bowl. Add the crumbled feta cheese, parsley, dill, lemon juice, and salt and pepper to taste. Stir everything together until well combined.

Step 3: Assemble the Pies

Now that the dough has risen and the filling is ready, it’s time to assemble the Sfeeha:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Shape the dough: Punch down the dough to release the air. Divide the dough into small portions, each about the size of a golf ball. Roll each piece into a ball and then flatten it out into a small circle, about 4 inches in diameter. Use a rolling pin to achieve an even thickness.

  3. Fill the dough: Spoon about 2-3 tablespoons of the spinach and feta filling into the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as it may cause the pies to burst during baking.

  4. Seal the pies: Fold the edges of the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape or pleated circle. Pinch the edges together to seal the filling inside. If you prefer a more rustic look, you can leave the edges slightly open.

  5. Egg wash (optional): For a beautiful golden finish, brush the tops of the pies with a beaten egg.

Step 4: Bake the Pies

Place the assembled pies onto the prepared baking sheet. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until the dough is golden brown and crispy. Keep an eye on the pies to ensure they don’t burn, as oven temperatures can vary.

Once baked, remove the pies from the oven and allow them to cool slightly before serving.

Tips for Perfect Sfeeha

  • Fresh Spinach: If you’re using fresh spinach, be sure to wash and dry it thoroughly before cooking. Excess moisture can make the filling soggy.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling a day in advance and store it in the refrigerator. This allows the flavors to meld and develop even further.

  • Freezing: Sfeeha can be frozen both before and after baking. If you freeze them before baking, make sure to wrap each pie tightly in plastic wrap or foil and store them in an airtight container or freezer bag. When ready to bake, simply pop them straight from the freezer into the oven, adjusting the baking time accordingly.

  • Herbs: The combination of parsley and dill adds a fresh, vibrant flavor to the filling, but feel free to experiment with other herbs like mint or thyme to personalize your pies.

Serving Sfeeha

Sfeeha can be served in many ways. These pies are fantastic as appetizers at a party, a light snack with a side of yogurt or tahini sauce, or as part of a mezze spread. The creamy feta and earthy spinach filling pair wonderfully with a squeeze of lemon juice or a drizzle of olive oil on top. You can also serve them alongside a fresh salad, or simply enjoy them on their own.

If you are making a large batch of these pies, they are perfect for meal prep or for sharing with family and friends. Whether you choose to serve them warm or at room temperature, they will be enjoyed by all.

Conclusion

Making Middle Eastern Spinach and Feta Pies (Sfeeha) at home is a rewarding and delicious experience. The combination of crispy dough, flavorful spinach and feta filling, and aromatic herbs is simply irresistible. With a little patience and attention to detail, you can recreate this classic dish that’s rich in tradition and flavor.

By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to make Sfeeha from scratch and enjoy a taste of the Middle East in the comfort of your own home. Whether for a casual snack or a special occasion, these spinach and feta pies are sure to impress. Happy baking!
