Summer Sorbet: Fruity Sorbet Mocktail Mix


If you’re looking for a refreshing and healthy treat to beat the summer heat, look no further! Introducing the Fruity Sorbet Mocktail Mix – a delightful and easy-to-make sorbet recipe that’ll satisfy your taste buds without the guilt. Perfect for kids and adults alike, this fruity sorbet is a great alternative to sugary snacks during warm weather.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups water
  • 3 cups white grape juice (or any fruit juice of your choice)
  • 2 cups fresh seasonal fruits (520g)
  • ½ cup sugar (200g)
  • 1 lemon
  • 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • a handful of mint leaves
  • Optional: a few dashes of Grenadine syrup (for added color and flavor)
  • Ice molds

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the fruit juice: In a large pot, combine water, white grape juice, and sugar. Stir until the sugar is dissolved. Set aside and allow the mixture to cool.

  2. Preparing the fruit blend: Wash and chop your choice of seasonal fruits, such as watermelon, strawberries, blueberries, or mangoes. Remove any inedible parts like seeds and skin, and set aside.

  3. Adding fresh citrus: Zest one lemon and one lime, and set the zest aside. Squeeze the juice from both the lemon and lime into the sugar-infused watermelon juice. Mix well.

  4. Incorporating flavors: Stir in the honey and mint leaves into the fruit juice mixture. Smell the delicious aroma wafting from the pot as it blends with the juicy fruit.

  5. Chilling the mixture: Allow the fruit juice mixture to cool entirely before placing it in the refrigerator for several hours or overnight. The key is to keep it cold.

  6. Preparing the molds: While the mixture chills, prepare your ice molds. You can use a small ice cube tray or an ice cream mold for a more elegant presentation. If you prefer spheres, you can use silicone sphere molds, which are available at most kitchen stores.

  7. Creating the sorbet: Once the fruit juice is chilled, pour the mixture into your molds. If desired, add a few dashes of Grenadine syrup for color and visual appeal, but this is completely optional.

  8. Freezing the sorbet: Place the filled molds in the freezer, ensuring that they are evenly filled to avoid any air bubbles. After a few hours, give the sorbet a gentle stir to prevent the formation of ice crystals. To make the sorbet more creamy, you can stir in a small amount of softened ice cream or frozen yogurt.

  9. Enjoying your fruit sorbet mocktail: Once fully frozen, remove the sorbet from the molds and serve immediately. Enjoy the refreshing fruity sorbet in a cool glass, perfect for sipping on a warm summer day.

In conclusion, this delightful Fruity Sorbet Mocktail Mix is a fantastic way to cool down and enjoy the sweet and tangy flavors of summer. It’s simple, healthy, and requires no fancy equipment or ingredients. With this recipe, you’ll feel good about indulging in a tasty and wholesome dessert. Don’t miss the opportunity to surprise your friends and family with this refreshing treat – give it a try this summer!
