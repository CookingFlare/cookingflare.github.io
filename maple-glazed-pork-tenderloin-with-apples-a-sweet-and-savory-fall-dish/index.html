Maple-Glazed Pork Tenderloin with Apples: A Sweet and Savory Fall Dish


Welcome to our kitchen, where we love experimenting with unique flavor combinations that tantalize the taste buds. Today, we bring you a delightful recipe that perfectly captures the essence of fall – Maple-Glazed Pork Tenderloin with Apples. This dish combines the sweetness of maple syrup with the savory flavors of tender pork and crisp apples. It’s a match made in culinary heaven!

Ingredients

  • 1 pork tenderloin (approximately 1.5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 apples, cored and sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Set aside.

  3. Heat the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Season the pork tenderloin with salt and pepper, then sear it on all sides until golden brown. This will help seal in the juices and add a lovely caramelized flavor.

  4. Once seared, remove the pork tenderloin from the skillet and set it aside on a plate.

  5. In the same skillet, add the sliced apples and red onion. Sauté them for about 5 minutes, or until they start to soften and caramelize slightly.

  6. Return the pork tenderloin to the skillet, nestling it among the apples and onions. Brush the maple syrup mixture over the pork, making sure to coat it evenly.

  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast for approximately 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the pork reaches 145°F (63°C). Remember to baste the pork with the glaze every 10 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful.

  8. Once cooked, remove the skillet from the oven and let the pork rest for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures a tender and juicy result.

  9. Slice the pork tenderloin into medallions and serve it alongside the sautéed apples and onions. Drizzle any remaining glaze over the top for an extra burst of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Maple-Glazed Pork Tenderloin with Apples pairs beautifully with a variety of side dishes. Here are a few suggestions to complete your fall feast:

  • Creamy mashed potatoes: The smooth and buttery texture of mashed potatoes complements the sweet and savory flavors of the pork.

  • Roasted Brussels sprouts: The slight bitterness of Brussels sprouts provides a nice contrast to the sweetness of the maple glaze.

  • Steamed green beans: The crispness of steamed green beans adds a refreshing element to the meal.

  • Warm dinner rolls: Soft and fluffy dinner rolls are perfect for soaking up any leftover glaze.

Conclusion

There you have it – a delectable Maple-Glazed Pork Tenderloin with Apples recipe that will leave your taste buds craving more. The combination of maple syrup, pork, and apples creates a symphony of flavors that perfectly captures the essence of fall. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the oven, and indulge in this sweet and savory dish that is sure to become a seasonal favorite. Happy cooking!
