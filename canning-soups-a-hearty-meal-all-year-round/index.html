Canning Soups: A Hearty Meal All Year Round


Looking for a delicious and convenient way to enjoy homemade soups throughout the year? Look no further than canning soups! With the right ingredients, equipment, and a little bit of time, you can whip up a variety of hearty soups that will warm your soul on even the coldest of days. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of canning soups, from selecting the best ingredients to storing them for long-term enjoyment.

Why Can Soups?

Canning soups offers several benefits that make it a worthwhile endeavor for any home cook. Here are just a few reasons why you should consider canning soups:

  1. Convenience: Imagine having a delicious, homemade soup ready to eat whenever you want, without the need for lengthy preparation. Canning soups allows you to preserve your favorite recipes in individual servings, making it easy to enjoy a hot bowl of soup at a moment’s notice.

  2. Preservation: By canning soups, you’re extending their shelf life significantly. Properly canned soups can last for up to a year or even longer, meaning you can enjoy the flavors of summer well into the winter months.

  3. Nutrition: Canning soups at home allows you to control the quality and freshness of the ingredients. You can use seasonal produce, high-quality meats, and homemade stocks, ensuring that your soups are packed with nutrients and free from preservatives.

Getting Started

Before you embark on your canning adventure, it’s important to gather the necessary equipment. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

  • Canning jars with lids and bands
  • A large stockpot or canner
  • A canning rack or a folded towel
  • A ladle or a large spoon
  • A funnel
  • A jar lifter or tongs
  • A clean kitchen towel
  • Labels and a permanent marker for labeling your jars

Once you have your equipment ready, it’s time to select your soup recipes. Choose soups that have a good balance of flavors and textures, and that will hold up well during the canning process. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Classic Chicken Noodle Soup
  • Hearty Beef and Vegetable Stew
  • Creamy Tomato Basil Soup
  • Spicy Lentil and Sausage Soup

The Canning Process

Now that you have your equipment and recipes, it’s time to start canning your soups. Follow these steps for a successful canning experience:

  1. Prepare your ingredients: Chop vegetables, cook meats, and make your soup base according to your recipe. Ensure that all ingredients are fresh and of high quality.

  2. Sterilize your jars: Wash your jars, lids, and bands in hot, soapy water and rinse them thoroughly. Place them in a large pot of boiling water for 10 minutes to sterilize.

  3. Fill your jars: Using a ladle and a wide-mouth funnel, carefully fill your sterilized jars with your prepared soup, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top. Wipe the rims of the jars clean with a damp kitchen towel.

  4. Seal your jars: Place the lids on the jars and screw on the bands just until they are fingertip tight. Do not overtighten. This allows air to escape during the canning process.

  5. Process your jars: Place your filled jars in a canner or large stockpot filled with enough boiling water to cover the jars by at least 1 inch. Process the jars according to the recommended processing time for your recipe and altitude.

  6. Cool and store: Once the processing time is complete, carefully remove the jars from the canner using tongs or a jar lifter. Place them on a clean kitchen towel and allow them to cool completely. Check the seals on the lids after the jars have cooled. Store your properly sealed jars in a cool, dark place for long-term storage.

Enjoying Your Canned Soups

When you’re ready to enjoy your homemade canned soups, simply remove a jar from storage and heat it on the stovetop or in the microwave until hot. Serve it with your favorite crusty bread or a side salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

Remember to label your jars with the date and the type of soup to keep track of their freshness. Properly canned soups can last for up to a year or longer, but it’s always a good idea to use them within a reasonable timeframe to ensure the best flavor and quality.

So, why not give canning soups a try? With a little bit of planning and preparation, you can stock your pantry with a variety of delicious soups that will bring comfort and warmth to your table all year round. Happy canning!
