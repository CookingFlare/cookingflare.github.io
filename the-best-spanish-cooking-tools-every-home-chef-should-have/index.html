The Best Spanish Cooking Tools Every Home Chef Should Have


Spanish cuisine is celebrated worldwide for its bold flavors, rich history, and variety of regional dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a home cook looking to explore new culinary horizons, having the right tools is essential to mastering Spanish cooking. From paella to tapas, there are specific tools that can elevate your dishes and make the cooking process smoother.

In this post, we’ll dive into the essential Spanish cooking tools every home chef should have, breaking down their uses, benefits, and how they can help you bring the flavors of Spain into your kitchen.

1. Paella Pan (Paellera)

No discussion of Spanish cooking would be complete without the mention of the paella pan, or paellera. This wide, shallow pan is specifically designed for making paella, the iconic Spanish dish that combines rice, saffron, seafood, and meats into a vibrant and flavorful meal.

The key to making an authentic paella lies in the socarrat, the crispy, golden layer of rice that forms at the bottom of the pan. A good paella pan ensures that the heat is distributed evenly, which is crucial to achieving this coveted texture. Paella pans are usually made from carbon steel, which retains heat well and allows for even cooking. They come in various sizes, so you can choose one that suits the number of servings you want to prepare.

In addition to making paella, this versatile pan can also be used for frying, grilling, and even baking, making it a valuable tool for any Spanish-inspired meal.

2. Cast Iron Skillet

A cast iron skillet is a must-have for many Spanish dishes, particularly when it comes to dishes like tortilla española (Spanish omelette) or pisto (a vegetable stew). Cast iron retains and distributes heat evenly, ensuring that your dishes cook thoroughly and evenly.

For tortilla española, a cast iron skillet provides the perfect balance of heat retention for the eggs and potatoes to cook gently and evenly. If you’re making churros or searing meats for stews like cocido madrileño, a cast iron skillet can also serve as a durable, high-heat cooking surface.

A well-seasoned cast iron skillet is a kitchen staple that can last for generations, making it an investment worth considering.

3. Terracotta Cazuela

The cazuela is a traditional Spanish cooking vessel that is often made from terracotta. This shallow, round dish is perfect for cooking stews, braises, and slow-cooked dishes like fabada asturiana (Asturian bean stew) or cordero al horno (roast lamb).

The beauty of the cazuela lies in its ability to retain and distribute heat evenly. It can be used on the stovetop or in the oven, and it’s ideal for dishes that require long cooking times to develop deep, rich flavors. Many Spanish chefs prefer cazuelas for their rustic look and their ability to infuse dishes with a unique flavor due to the porous nature of terracotta.

When using a cazuela, be sure to season it before your first use to prevent cracking and improve its non-stick properties.

4. Spanish Mortar and Pestle (Molinillo)

The molinillo is the Spanish equivalent of a traditional mortar and pestle. This tool is used for grinding and mixing spices, herbs, and garlic to create flavorful pastes or rubs that are often featured in Spanish cooking.

In Spain, a molinillo is often used to prepare romesco sauce, a Catalan staple made from roasted peppers, tomatoes, nuts, and garlic. Whether you’re grinding dried saffron, almonds for turrón (Spanish nougat), or creating a homemade garlic paste, the molinillo helps release the essential oils and flavors from ingredients, resulting in a more aromatic and flavorful dish.

It’s a great tool for any home chef who loves to use fresh, ground spices in their cooking. Additionally, the molinillo comes in various sizes, allowing you to find the one that best suits your needs.

5. Chopping and Cutting Board (Tabla de Cortar)

A sturdy cutting board (or tabla de cortar) is an essential tool for any kitchen, but in Spanish cooking, it holds particular importance. Spain is home to a variety of fresh vegetables, cured meats, and seafood, all of which need to be prepared with care.

The chopping board should be large enough to handle everything from slicing vegetables for pisto to chopping seafood for paella. It’s important to invest in a high-quality cutting board made from wood, bamboo, or plastic, depending on your preference.

Many Spanish chefs also prefer a board with a well for catching juices, especially when working with tomatoes or cured meats like jamón ibérico. A good cutting board ensures precise, clean cuts and protects your knives from damage.

6. Serrated Knife for Tomatoes

Spain is known for its juicy, flavorful tomatoes, which are often used in dishes like salmorejo (a cold tomato soup) or pan con tomate (tomato bread). To achieve the perfect slice, you’ll need a serrated knife.

A serrated knife has a jagged edge that allows you to cut through delicate ingredients like tomatoes without squashing them. This is particularly important for dishes like salmorejo, where the texture of the tomatoes is critical to the success of the dish.

While a serrated knife is useful for many fruits and vegetables, it’s indispensable when preparing dishes that rely on the fresh, vibrant flavor of tomatoes.

7. Corkscrew (Sacacorchos)

A good corkscrew is not just a tool for opening wine; it’s an essential item for any Spanish kitchen, where wine plays a central role in meals. Spain produces some of the world’s best wines, and whether you’re pairing a glass of Rioja with your paella or enjoying a glass of cava with your tapas, a corkscrew is a must-have tool.

Look for a corkscrew with a comfortable handle and a long screw to ensure that it easily opens bottles of all types, from corked wine bottles to bottles of olive oil, which are also common in Spanish kitchens.

8. Paella Ladle

The paella ladle is designed specifically for serving paella, ensuring that the rice is spooned out without disturbing the delicate socarrat at the bottom of the pan. This ladle usually has a wide, shallow bowl to accommodate the large portions of paella, making it perfect for scooping up the perfect serving of rice, seafood, and meats.

Having a paella ladle makes serving your paella a breeze while ensuring that the presentation is perfect. It’s an essential tool for anyone who regularly prepares paella, allowing you to serve this dish with precision and care.

9. Spanish Olive Oil Dispenser (Aceitera)

Spain is the world’s largest producer of olive oil, and the aceitera is the perfect tool to keep this essential ingredient on hand. An aceitera is a ceramic or glass dispenser designed to pour olive oil in a controlled manner, preventing spills and wastage.

In Spanish cooking, olive oil is used liberally in everything from frying to dressing salads. An aceitera ensures that you can pour just the right amount of olive oil every time, making it easier to use in recipes without overdoing it. The wide mouth of many aceiteras also allows for easy refills, ensuring that you always have fresh olive oil at your fingertips.

10. Paella or Tapas Serving Dishes

For tapas or smaller Spanish dishes, tapas serving dishes are indispensable. These small, traditional dishes, often made from ceramic or terracotta, are used to serve individual portions of various tapas, such as patatas bravas, croquetas, or tortilla española.

They come in various shapes and sizes, from small bowls to larger plates, and are designed to highlight the presentation of the dish. Using these dishes not only adds authenticity to your meal but also helps you serve tapas in the traditional style that encourages sharing and enjoying food together.

11. Pressure Cooker

A pressure cooker can be a game-changer in Spanish cooking, particularly when making dishes that require long cooking times, such as cocido madrileño or fabada asturiana. The pressure cooker helps tenderize tough cuts of meat and beans quickly, saving time while still allowing you to create rich, flavorful stews.

Spanish chefs rely on pressure cookers to prepare hearty dishes in a fraction of the time it would take using traditional cooking methods. If you’re looking to replicate the depth of flavor found in classic Spanish stews, a pressure cooker is a must-have addition to your kitchen arsenal.

Conclusion

Spanish cooking is about more than just recipes; it’s about bringing the flavors and traditions of Spain into your home. The right tools can make all the difference, allowing you to prepare authentic Spanish dishes with ease. Whether you’re preparing a classic paella, a rich stew, or fresh tapas, having these essential Spanish cooking tools in your kitchen will help you achieve the perfect results every time.

Investing in these tools will not only enhance your cooking experience but also deepen your appreciation of the rich culinary heritage that Spanish cuisine offers. Whether you’re making a simple tortilla or hosting a grand paella dinner, these tools will help you cook like a true Spanish chef.
