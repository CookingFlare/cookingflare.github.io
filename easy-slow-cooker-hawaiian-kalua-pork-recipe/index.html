Easy Slow Cooker Hawaiian Kalua Pork Recipe


If you’re searching for an effortless yet delicious dish that brings the flavors of Hawaii to your kitchen, look no further! This Easy Slow Cooker Hawaiian Kalua Pork recipe is perfect for busy weeknights or gatherings with family and friends. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity and the rich, smoky flavor that comes from slow cooking. Let’s dive into this fantastic recipe that is sure to transport your taste buds straight to the islands!

What is Kalua Pork?

Kalua Pork is a traditional Hawaiian dish that is typically cooked in an underground oven called an “imu.” The pork is seasoned with salt and wrapped in banana leaves, then slow-cooked until it’s tender and flavorful. The slow cooking process allows the meat to absorb an incredible depth of flavor, making it a beloved staple in Hawaiian cuisine. While the traditional cooking method requires time and effort, this easy slow cooker version simplifies the process while still retaining the essence of the original dish.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To prepare this Easy Slow Cooker Hawaiian Kalua Pork, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4-5 pounds of pork shoulder (also known as pork butt)
  • 1-2 tablespoons of Hawaiian sea salt or kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon of liquid smoke (for that authentic smoky flavor)
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • Optional: 2-3 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: 1 cup of banana leaves (if you want to replicate the traditional method)

Choosing the Right Cut of Meat

Selecting the right cut of pork is crucial for achieving melt-in-your-mouth tenderness. Pork shoulder is the ideal choice due to its marbling and fat content. As the pork cooks low and slow, the fat renders down, keeping the meat moist and flavorful. You can ask your butcher for a bone-in or boneless pork shoulder, but remember that bone-in will typically yield a richer flavor.

Preparing the Kalua Pork

Step 1: Season the Pork

  1. Begin by rinsing the pork shoulder under cold water and patting it dry with paper towels. This helps to remove any excess moisture and allows the seasoning to adhere better.
  2. Rub the Hawaiian sea salt evenly over the entire surface of the pork. If you like a little extra kick, you can also sprinkle black pepper for added flavor.
  3. If you have banana leaves, you can wrap the seasoned pork in them, which will help retain moisture and impart a subtle sweetness to the dish. However, this step is optional, and you can still achieve great results without them.

Step 2: Add Liquid Smoke

  1. Place the seasoned pork shoulder in the slow cooker.
  2. Drizzle the liquid smoke over the meat, ensuring it’s evenly coated. This will give it that signature smoky flavor reminiscent of traditional Kalua Pork.

Step 3: Slow Cook the Pork

  1. Cover the slow cooker with its lid and set it to low. Allow the pork to cook for 8-10 hours or until the meat is fork-tender and easily shreds apart.
  2. Avoid lifting the lid during cooking, as this can release heat and extend cooking time. Trust the process and let the slow cooker do its magic!

Shredding the Pork

Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the pork shoulder from the slow cooker. It will be extremely tender and may fall apart as you lift it. Use two forks to shred the meat into bite-sized pieces, discarding any large pieces of fat or bone if present.

Step 4: Mix with Juices

After shredding, return the pork to the slow cooker and mix it with the cooking juices. This step ensures that the meat absorbs all the delicious flavors. You can also taste and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding more salt or black pepper to suit your preference.

Serving Suggestions

Hawaiian Kalua Pork is incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways. Here are a few delicious ideas to inspire your meal:

1. Classic Kalua Pork Plate

Serve the shredded pork over a bed of steamed white rice. Accompany it with traditional Hawaiian sides like macaroni salad and steamed vegetables for a complete meal.

2. Kalua Pork Tacos

For a fun twist, use the Kalua Pork as a filling for tacos. Warm corn tortillas and top them with shredded pork, fresh pineapple salsa, and a drizzle of lime juice for a refreshing bite.

3. Kalua Pork Sandwiches

Make a delightful sandwich by placing the shredded pork on a soft bun. Add some coleslaw for crunch and a tangy barbecue sauce for an extra layer of flavor.

4. Kalua Pork Bowls

Create a hearty bowl by layering the Kalua Pork with brown rice, avocado, black beans, and a sprinkle of cilantro. This option is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients.

5. Kalua Pork Salad

For a lighter option, toss the shredded pork on a bed of mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, and cucumbers. Drizzle with a light vinaigrette for a refreshing salad.

Storing Leftovers

If you find yourself with leftover Kalua Pork, you’re in for a treat! The flavors deepen and improve after a day in the fridge. Store the shredded pork in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 3-4 days. You can reheat it in the microwave or on the stovetop, adding a splash of water or broth to prevent it from drying out.

Freezing Kalua Pork

If you want to save some for later, you can freeze the leftover Kalua Pork. Allow it to cool completely, then transfer it to a freezer-safe bag or container. It can be stored in the freezer for up to three months. When you’re ready to enjoy it, simply thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and reheat.

Tips for the Perfect Kalua Pork

  • Don’t Rush the Cooking Time: Slow cooking is key to achieving that tender, flavorful pork. Resist the temptation to cook it on high for a shorter period; the low and slow method is what makes this dish special.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to customize the seasoning to your liking. You can add garlic powder, onion powder, or even a hint of cayenne pepper for added heat.

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The quality of your pork and seasonings can significantly impact the final dish. Whenever possible, opt for organic or locally sourced pork for the best flavor.

  • Serve with Fresh Ingredients: Complement your Kalua Pork with fresh, vibrant ingredients, such as pineapple, cilantro, or lime, to elevate the dish.

Conclusion

This Easy Slow Cooker Hawaiian Kalua Pork recipe is a fantastic way to enjoy a taste of Hawaii without the hassle. With just a few simple ingredients and minimal prep, you can create a dish that is rich in flavor and perfect for any occasion. Whether you serve it as a traditional plate lunch, in tacos, or as part of a hearty salad, this Kalua Pork is sure to impress your family and friends. So grab your slow cooker, and get ready to savor the deliciousness of Hawaiian cuisine right in your home!
