Cooking with Seasonal Produce: Farm to Table Recipes for Every Month


As the seasons change, so do the flavors and textures of the produce available to us. Cooking with seasonal produce is not only a great way to support local farmers and reduce our carbon footprint, but it also allows us to experience the unique characteristics of each season’s bounty. In this post, we’ll explore farm-to-table recipes for every month of the year, showcasing the best of what each season has to offer.

January: Winter’s Warmth

January is a time for hearty, comforting dishes that warm the soul. One of our favorite winter recipes is Braised Short Ribs with Root Vegetables. This slow-cooked masterpiece is made with tender short ribs, caramelized onions, and a medley of root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and turnips. Serve with a side of crusty bread or over mashed potatoes for a truly satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and chopped
  • 2 turnips, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C).
  2. Season the short ribs with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the short ribs until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until caramelized, about 20 minutes.
  5. Add the garlic, carrots, parsnips, and turnips to the pot. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 20 minutes.
  6. Add the red wine, beef broth, and thyme to the pot. Stir to combine.
  7. Return the short ribs to the pot and cover with a lid.
  8. Transfer the pot to the preheated oven and braise for 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls off the bone.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

February: Love is in the Air

February is the month of love, and what better way to show our loved ones we care than with a romantic dinner? Our favorite February recipe is Pan-Seared Scallops with Lemon-Garlic Butter and Microgreens. This dish is a masterclass in simplicity, with tender scallops seared to perfection and served with a bright, citrusy sauce and a sprinkle of peppery microgreens.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large scallops
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup microgreens

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Rinse the scallops under cold water and pat dry with paper towels.
  3. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the lemon juice, white wine, and heavy cream to the saucepan. Bring to a simmer and cook until the sauce has reduced slightly, about 5 minutes.
  5. Season the scallops with salt and pepper.
  6. Heat a large skillet over high heat. Add the scallops and sear until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  7. Serve the scallops with the lemon-garlic butter sauce spooned over the top. Garnish with microgreens and serve immediately.

March: Spring Awakening

March is the month when winter finally begins to thaw, and spring starts to awaken. Our favorite March recipe is Roasted Asparagus with Lemon-Parmesan Vinaigrette. This dish is a celebration of spring’s fresh flavors, with tender asparagus roasted to perfection and served with a bright, citrusy vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Toss the asparagus with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Roast the asparagus in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice and Parmesan cheese.
  6. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the roasted asparagus and serve immediately.

April: Spring Fling

April is the month when spring is in full bloom, and the produce is at its peak. Our favorite April recipe is Grilled Strawberry Spinach Salad with Feta and Balsamic Glaze. This dish is a celebration of spring’s sweet flavors, with tender spinach, caramelized strawberries, and tangy feta cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 2 cups sliced strawberries
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the strawberries for 2-3 minutes per side, or until caramelized.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the spinach, grilled strawberries, feta cheese, and pecans.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, and honey.
  5. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the salad and serve immediately.

May: Spring’s Bounty

May is the month when spring is in full swing, and the produce is at its peak. Our favorite May recipe is Grilled Vegetable Skewers with Chimichurri Sauce. This dish is a celebration of spring’s fresh flavors, with tender vegetables grilled to perfection and served with a bright, herby sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, and onions)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup freshly chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup freshly chopped oregano
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the mixed vegetables onto skewers.
  3. Grill the skewers for 10-12 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, parsley, oregano, garlic, and red wine vinegar.
  5. Serve the grilled skewers with the chimichurri sauce spooned over the top.

June: Summer’s Sweetness

June is the month when summer officially begins, and the produce is at its peak. Our favorite June recipe is Watermelon and Feta Salad with Mint and Balsamic Glaze. This dish is a celebration of summer’s sweet flavors, with refreshing watermelon, tangy feta cheese, and bright, herby mint.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups diced watermelon
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the diced watermelon, crumbled feta cheese, and chopped fresh mint.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  3. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the salad and serve immediately.

July: Summer’s Heat

July is the month when summer is in full swing, and the produce is at its peak. Our favorite July recipe is Grilled Chicken and Peach Salad with Arugula and Prosciutto. This dish is a celebration of summer’s sweet flavors, with tender chicken, caramelized peaches, and peppery arugula.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 ripe peaches, sliced
  • 4 cups arugula leaves
  • 4 slices prosciutto, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the chicken breasts for 5-6 minutes per side, or until cooked through.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the grilled chicken, sliced peaches, arugula leaves, and chopped prosciutto.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil and balsamic vinegar.
  5. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the salad and top with crumbled blue cheese.

August: End of Summer

August is the month when summer is coming to an end, and the produce is still at its peak. Our favorite August recipe is Grilled Eggplant Parmesan with Fresh Basil and Mozzarella. This dish is a celebration of summer’s sweet flavors, with tender eggplant, creamy mozzarella, and bright, herby basil.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the eggplant slices with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the eggplant slices for 3-4 minutes per side, or until tender and slightly charred.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs and grated mozzarella cheese.
  5. Top the grilled eggplant slices with the breadcrumb mixture and a sprinkle of chopped fresh basil.

September: Fall’s Awakening

September is the month when fall is starting to awaken, and the produce is beginning to change. Our favorite September recipe is Roasted Butternut Squash Soup with Crème Fraîche and Chives. This dish is a celebration of fall’s warm flavors, with tender butternut squash, creamy crème fraîche, and bright, oniony chives.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup crème fraîche
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash cubes with butter, onion, and garlic on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the squash in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.
  4. In a blender or food processor, puree the roasted squash with heavy cream and crème fraîche until smooth.
  5. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the soup hot, garnished with chopped fresh chives.

October: Fall’s Bounty

October is the month when fall is in full swing, and the produce is at its peak. Our favorite October recipe is Braised Short Ribs with Apples and Onions. This dish is a celebration of fall’s warm flavors, with tender short ribs, caramelized apples, and sweet, caramelized onions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 apples, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C).
  2. Season the short ribs with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the short ribs until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until caramelized, about 20 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped apples to the pot and cook until tender, about 10 minutes.
  6. Add the red wine, beef broth, and thyme to the pot. Stir to combine.
  7. Return the short ribs to the pot and cover with a lid.
  8. Transfer the pot to the preheated oven and braise for 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls off the bone.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

November: Fall’s Fade

November is the month when fall is starting to fade, and the produce is beginning to change. Our favorite November recipe is Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze. This dish is a celebration of fall’s earthy flavors, with tender Brussels sprouts, crispy bacon, and tangy balsamic glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 6 slices bacon, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the Brussels sprouts in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar and honey.
  5. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the roasted Brussels sprouts and top with crispy bacon.

December: Winter’s Chill

December is the month when winter is in full swing, and the produce is at its peak. Our favorite December recipe is Braised Beef Stew with Mushrooms and Red Wine. This dish is a celebration of winter’s hearty flavors, with tender beef, earthy mushrooms, and rich red wine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C).
  2. Season the beef stew meat with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the beef until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until caramelized, about 20 minutes.
  5. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pot and cook until tender, about 10 minutes.
  6. Add the red wine, beef broth, and thyme to the pot. Stir to combine.
  7. Return the beef to the pot and cover with a lid.
  8. Transfer the pot to the preheated oven and braise for 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls off the bone.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

By cooking with seasonal produce, we can experience the unique flavors and textures of each month, while also supporting local farmers and reducing our environmental impact. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, these farm-to-table recipes are sure to inspire you to get cooking and make the most of the seasonal produce available to you.
