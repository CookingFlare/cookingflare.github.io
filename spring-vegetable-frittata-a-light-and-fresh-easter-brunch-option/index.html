Spring Vegetable Frittata: A Light and Fresh Easter Brunch Option


Spring is a time for fresh, vibrant flavors, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a delicious Spring Vegetable Frittata? This versatile dish is perfect for Easter brunch, offering a light and satisfying option that will impress your guests with its colorful presentation and delightful taste.

Ingredients

For the Frittata:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as basil, parsley, or chives)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper until well combined. Set aside.

  3. Heat the olive oil in a 10-inch ovenproof skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced red onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes, until softened.

  4. Add the asparagus to the skillet and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, until slightly tender.

  5. Stir in the cherry tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing them to soften slightly.

  6. Add the fresh spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, about 1-2 minutes.

  7. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet, gently stirring to distribute the ingredients evenly.

  8. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the top of the frittata.

  9. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the frittata is set in the center and the edges are golden brown.

  10. Remove the frittata from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Sprinkle the chopped fresh herbs over the top before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Spring Vegetable Frittata pairs beautifully with a simple mixed greens salad and a crusty baguette. For a complete Easter brunch spread, consider adding fresh fruit, yogurt, and a selection of pastries to the menu. The frittata can be served warm or at room temperature, making it a convenient option for entertaining.

Why Choose a Frittata for Easter Brunch?

Easter brunch is a time for celebrating with family and friends, and a frittata offers a host of benefits for the occasion. Not only is it easy to prepare, but it also allows for endless variations to suit your preferences and dietary needs. With its combination of eggs and fresh vegetables, the Spring Vegetable Frittata provides a wholesome and satisfying dish that will leave your guests impressed and satisfied.

Conclusion

This Spring Vegetable Frittata is a delightful addition to any Easter brunch menu. Its light and fresh flavors capture the essence of the season, making it a perfect choice for a celebratory gathering. Whether you’re hosting a small family brunch or a larger event, this frittata is sure to be a hit. Embrace the flavors of spring and treat your guests to a memorable dining experience with this delightful and versatile dish.
