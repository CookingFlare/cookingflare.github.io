Easy Seafood Recipes with Avocado


Combining the ocean’s bounty with the creamy richness of avocado can yield a variety of dishes that are as nutritious as they are delicious. Whether you’re a seafood aficionado or looking to try something new and healthful, the marriage of fresh seafood and buttery avocado is a match made in flavor heaven. Today, we’ll dive into easy seafood recipes that use avocado to elevate each bite, offering something perfect for weeknight dinners, casual lunches, or impressive gatherings.

Why Pair Seafood With Avocado?

Avocado provides a smooth texture and subtle, buttery flavor that complements the delicate, briny notes of seafood. Its healthy fats balance the lean proteins found in fish and shellfish, creating dishes that satisfy both palate and body. Plus, avocado’s natural creaminess allows for lighter recipes that don’t rely on heavy sauces or excess oil. This combination also lends itself well to vibrant, colorful presentations, bringing bright, fresh notes to your table.

1. Grilled Shrimp and Avocado Salad with Citrus Vinaigrette

This salad is a refreshing blend of smoky grilled shrimp, creamy avocado, and crisp vegetables, all tossed in a tangy citrus dressing. It’s perfect for those scorching summer days or as a light, wholesome meal anytime.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 4 cups mixed greens (arugula, spinach, romaine)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 orange, segmented
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for shrimp)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Citrus Vinaigrette:

  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • 1 tsp honey or agave syrup
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Marinate and Grill Shrimp: Toss shrimp with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Thread them onto skewers for easier grilling. Grill over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes per side until pink and opaque. Set aside to cool slightly.

  2. Make Dressing: Whisk lemon juice, orange juice, honey, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a small bowl until emulsified.

  3. Assemble Salad: In a large bowl, combine mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and orange segments. Add grilled shrimp and diced avocado gently to avoid mashing.

  4. Dress and Serve: Pour the citrus vinaigrette over the salad and toss lightly. Serve immediately.

2. Creamy Avocado and Crab Meat Sandwich

An elegant sandwich that balances the sweet, tender crab meat with luscious avocado spread. It’s simple enough for a quick lunch but fancy enough to impress guests.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz fresh crab meat, picked over for shells
  • 1 large avocado
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise (opt for light or flavored)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Fresh dill, chopped (about 1 tsp)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices whole-grain bread or sourdough
  • Optional: thin slices of cucumber or sprouts

Preparation:

  1. Prepare Avocado Spread: In a bowl, mash the avocado with lemon juice until creamy but slightly chunky. Stir in mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, dill, salt, and pepper.

  2. Mix Crab Filling: Gently fold crab meat into half of the avocado mixture.

  3. Assemble Sandwich: Spread the remaining avocado mixture on each slice of bread. Layer the crab mixture evenly onto two slices, top with cucumber or sprouts if desired, and close with the other slices.

  4. Serve: Cut the sandwiches in halves or quarters and serve immediately with a side of fresh fruit or salad.

3. Baked Salmon with Avocado Salsa

This dish highlights tender, flaky salmon topped with a refreshing avocado salsa. The salsa, packed with tomatoes, onion, and cilantro, adds bright flavors that beautifully contrast the rich fish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, quartered
  • ½ red onion, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

  1. Prepare Salmon: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Place salmon on a lined baking sheet. Rub each fillet with olive oil, sprinkle salt, and pepper.

  2. Bake Salmon: Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.

  3. Make Avocado Salsa: In a bowl, combine avocado, cherry tomatoes, red onion, jalapeño, cilantro, and lime juice. Toss gently and season with salt.

  4. Serve: Place baked salmon on plates and top generously with avocado salsa. Garnish with lime wedges if desired.

4. Spicy Tuna and Avocado Rice Bowl

Inspired by Hawaiian poke bowls and Japanese donburi, this recipe is a colorful, protein-packed meal that’s bursting with umami and creamy richness.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz sushi-grade tuna, diced
  • 1 avocado, cubed
  • 2 cups cooked sushi rice, cooled
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sriracha or chili paste (adjust to heat preference)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • Pickled ginger (optional)
  • Seaweed flakes (optional)

Preparation:

  1. Make Spicy Tuna: In a bowl, mix diced tuna, soy sauce, sesame oil, sriracha, and half of the green onions. Let marinate for 10 minutes in the fridge.

  2. Assemble Bowls: Divide sushi rice into two bowls. Top each with marinated tuna and cubed avocado.

  3. Garnish: Sprinkle with remaining green onions, sesame seeds, and optional pickled ginger or seaweed flakes.

  4. Serve: Mix before eating or enjoy each component individually.

5. Avocado and Scallop Ceviche

This light and zesty dish combines tender scallops cured in citrus juice with bursts of creamy avocado. Great as an appetizer or a light meal alongside crispy tortilla chips.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb bay scallops, cleaned and chopped
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lime juice (enough to cover scallops)
  • ½ red bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeño, minced (optional)
  • 1 handful fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Cure the Scallops: Place chopped scallops in a glass bowl. Cover with lime juice and refrigerate for 30-45 minutes until scallops become opaque and firm.

  2. Mix Ceviche: Drain about half of the lime juice from the scallops, reserving some for flavor. Add red bell pepper, onion, jalapeño, cilantro, salt, and pepper.

  3. Add Avocado: Gently fold in avocado just before serving to keep it fresh and creamy.

  4. Serve: Serve chilled with tortilla chips or on crisp lettuce leaves.

Tips for Cooking with Seafood and Avocado

  • Freshness is key: Use the freshest seafood you can find for the best flavor and texture. When buying fish or shellfish, look for firm flesh, bright eyes, and a clean ocean smell.

  • Handle avocado carefully: Avocado bruises easily, so fold it gently into recipes, especially salads and ceviches.

  • Season simply: Let the natural flavors of seafood and avocado shine. Often, a hint of citrus, salt, pepper, and fresh herbs is all you need.

  • Balance textures: Incorporate crunchy elements like fresh vegetables or toasted nuts to add contrast to the creamy avocado and tender seafood.

  • Keep it light: Avoid heavy cream-based sauces with avocado and seafood; flavor combinations with citrus, herbs, and light oils enhance the freshness.

With these easy seafood recipes featuring avocado, you can enjoy the best of both worlds—nutrient-rich, delicious dishes that come together quickly without sacrificing flavor. Whether grilled, baked, raw, or served chilled, the creamy texture of avocado brings a new dimension to classic seafood dishes. Try these at home and discover a fresh take on your favorite ocean flavors!
