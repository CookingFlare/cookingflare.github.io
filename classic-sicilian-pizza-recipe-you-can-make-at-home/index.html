Classic Sicilian Pizza Recipe You Can Make at Home


If you’re a fan of pizza, then you’re probably familiar with the classic Italian varieties like Neapolitan or Margherita. But today, let’s take a journey to the sunny island of Sicily, where pizza is made a bit differently. Sicilian pizza, also known as “sfincione,” is a unique and delightful take on the beloved dish, with a thick, airy crust and toppings that can vary widely. It’s known for its square shape, rich sauce, and flavorful toppings. If you’ve ever dreamed of making an authentic Sicilian pizza at home, you’re in the right place. In this post, I’ll guide you step-by-step on how to recreate this delicious, hearty pizza in your own kitchen.

What Makes Sicilian Pizza Unique?

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s talk about what makes Sicilian pizza stand out. Unlike the thin, round pizzas that many people are used to, Sicilian pizza is typically made with a thicker, fluffier crust. It’s baked in a rectangular pan, which gives it that signature square shape. The dough is soft and slightly airy, with a crisp exterior and a chewy interior.

Sicilian pizza also features a slightly sweeter sauce compared to other pizza types. It often incorporates onions, anchovies, and a variety of cheeses. The combination of rich toppings, a satisfying crust, and a tomato-based sauce makes it a hearty and flavorful pizza that’s perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Ingredients for Sicilian Pizza

To make a delicious Sicilian pizza at home, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour – This provides the structure and chewiness to the dough.
  • 1 teaspoon of salt – Essential for flavor.
  • 2 teaspoons of instant yeast – This helps the dough rise and gives it that light, airy texture.
  • 1 ½ cups of warm water – Make sure it’s not too hot; warm water helps activate the yeast.
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil – Adds richness to the dough.
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar – A little sugar helps feed the yeast and gives the dough a slight sweetness.

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes – The base for the sauce, providing the signature rich tomato flavor.
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil – Adds depth and richness to the sauce.
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano – A classic herb in Italian cuisine.
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder – For that subtle garlicky note.
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar – Balances out the acidity of the tomatoes.
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped – Adds sweetness and flavor to the sauce.
  • Salt and pepper to taste – Adjust the seasoning to your liking.

Toppings:

  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese – The go-to cheese for pizza, offering meltiness and flavor.
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese – Adds a salty, nutty flavor.
  • 1/2 cup of anchovies (optional) – A traditional Sicilian topping that adds a salty, umami flavor.
  • 1 cup of sliced pepperoni or sausage (optional) – For those who enjoy a bit of meat on their pizza.
  • Fresh basil leaves – For garnish and a burst of fresh flavor.

Making the Dough: The Heart of Sicilian Pizza

The key to a great Sicilian pizza starts with the dough. It’s the foundation of the pizza and plays a crucial role in achieving that perfect combination of crispy and fluffy textures. Here’s how you can make the dough from scratch:

  1. Activate the Yeast: Begin by mixing the warm water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Stir gently to dissolve, then let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy. This step ensures that the yeast is active and ready to rise.

  2. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center, and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Mix everything together with a wooden spoon until the dough begins to form.

  3. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels sticky, add a little more flour as you knead. The goal is to achieve a dough that is soft but not too sticky.

  4. Let the Dough Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap. Allow it to rise in a warm place for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Punch Down and Rest: Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any air bubbles. Let it rest for another 10 minutes before shaping it.

Making the Sauce: A Rich and Flavorful Base

While your dough is rising, it’s time to prepare the sauce. The sauce for Sicilian pizza is slightly different from the typical pizza sauce you might be used to. It’s a bit thicker and more savory, with a touch of sweetness to balance out the acidity of the tomatoes.

  1. Sauté the Onions: In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté them for 5-7 minutes, until they become soft and translucent.

  2. Add the Tomatoes and Seasonings: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, garlic powder, sugar, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken slightly and develop a rich, savory flavor.

  3. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if needed. You can add more salt, pepper, or a pinch of sugar depending on your preference.

  4. Let the Sauce Cool: Once the sauce is done, remove it from the heat and let it cool down to room temperature.

Assembling the Pizza

Now that the dough and sauce are ready, it’s time to assemble your Sicilian pizza! This step is where you get to be creative with toppings. Traditionally, Sicilian pizza is topped with mozzarella, Parmesan, and sometimes anchovies, but feel free to add your favorite ingredients.

  1. Preheat Your Oven: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Place a pizza stone or a baking sheet in the oven to heat up as well.

  2. Shape the Dough: Once the dough has rested, transfer it to an oiled rectangular baking pan (about 9x13 inches). Stretch and press the dough evenly to fit the pan. It should cover the entire surface, with a slight lip around the edges.

  3. Top the Pizza: Spread the cooled tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Then, sprinkle the shredded mozzarella and grated Parmesan cheese evenly over the sauce. If you’re using anchovies, pepperoni, or any other toppings, now is the time to add them.

  4. Bake the Pizza: Place the assembled pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Once the pizza is out of the oven, let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and flavor. Slice it up and serve while it’s still hot!

Tips for Perfecting Your Sicilian Pizza

  1. Use High-Quality Ingredients: Since Sicilian pizza relies on the dough and sauce for its flavor, using the best quality flour, tomatoes, and olive oil can make a big difference.

  2. Let the Dough Rise Slowly: For an even better texture, you can let the dough rise in the refrigerator overnight. This slow fermentation process will develop more complex flavors.

  3. Don’t Overload the Toppings: While it’s tempting to load the pizza with toppings, remember that Sicilian pizza is about balance. Too many toppings can overwhelm the crust and sauce. Keep it simple for the best results.

  4. Use a Pizza Stone: If you have a pizza stone, use it! It helps create a crispier bottom crust. Just make sure to preheat it in the oven before baking your pizza.

Variations of Sicilian Pizza

Sicilian pizza is incredibly versatile, and you can experiment with different toppings and flavors. Here are a few variations you might want to try:

  • Vegetarian Sicilian Pizza: Skip the meat and top your pizza with roasted vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, mushrooms, and olives.
  • Sausage and Peppers: For a more hearty version, add sautéed sausage and bell peppers.
  • White Sicilian Pizza: Instead of tomato sauce, use a ricotta-based sauce or simply drizzle olive oil over the dough, then top with cheese, garlic, and herbs.

Conclusion

Making classic Sicilian pizza at home is a rewarding experience that brings the taste of Italy right to your kitchen. From the thick, airy crust to the rich, flavorful sauce and the melty cheese, every bite of Sicilian pizza is a little piece of heaven. With this recipe, you’ll be able to recreate this delicious dish from scratch, impressing family and friends with your pizza-making skills.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start making your own homemade Sicilian pizza. Whether you keep it classic or add your own twist, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your pizza rotation!
