Cinnamon Roll Recipes Including Gluten-Free Options


Few things in life can beat the warm, comforting scent of freshly baked cinnamon rolls wafting through the kitchen. A perfect symphony of soft dough, aromatic cinnamon, and sweet glaze, these treats have a special place in breakfast menus and brunch spread everywhere. Whether you’re a traditionalist craving the classic recipe or someone seeking a gluten-free alternative without sacrificing flavor or texture, this guide has something for everyone.

Let’s embark on a culinary journey through various cinnamon roll recipes—from the timelessly classic version to inventive twists that will make your taste buds dance. Along the way, we’ll explore how to create fluffy gluten-free cinnamon rolls that rival their wheat-based cousins.

The Classic Cinnamon Roll: A Time-Honored Recipe

There’s a reason this recipe has endured for generations. The balance of soft, enriched dough, butter, cinnamon, and sugar is simply irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup warm whole milk (about 110°F/45°C)
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs

Filling:

  • 1 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 3 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened

Cream Cheese Glaze:

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar, sifted
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk

Instructions:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm milk and yeast. Let sit for 5-10 minutes, until frothy.

  2. Prepare dough: In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, and salt. In a separate one, whisk eggs. Gradually add yeast mixture, melted butter, and eggs to the dry ingredients, stirring until a dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  3. Rise: Place dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.

  4. Roll out dough: Punch down the dough and roll it into a 16x12 inch rectangle on a floured surface.

  5. Add filling: Spread softened butter over the rolled dough. Combine brown sugar and cinnamon, sprinkle evenly on top.

  6. Form rolls: Tightly roll up the dough from the long side, pinch edges to seal. Cut the roll into 12 even slices using a sharp knife or dental floss.

  7. Second rise: Arrange rolls on a greased baking pan or parchment-lined sheet, leaving room between them. Cover and allow to rise for 30-45 minutes.

  8. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake rolls for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.

  9. Prepare glaze: Beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Add powdered sugar, vanilla, and milk. Blend until thick and spreadable.

  10. Glaze & serve: Spread glaze generously on warm rolls. Serve fresh and enjoy!

Why You Should Try Gluten-Free Cinnamon Rolls

Gluten intolerance and celiac disease don’t mean you have to miss out on indulgent cinnamon rolls. Thanks to advances in gluten-free baking, you can enjoy a soft, flavorful treat without gluten’s structural support. The challenge is achieving the same lightness and chewiness because gluten provides elasticity and traps air.

Gluten-free flours, like rice flour, tapioca starch, and xanthan gum, combined cleverly, can mimic these properties. Let’s jump into a recipe designed to produce moist, tender, and aromatic gluten-free cinnamon rolls.

Gluten-Free Cinnamon Rolls Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose baking flour (with xanthan gum included)
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm almond milk or any milk alternative (110°F/45°C)
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted (plus extra for brushing)
  • 1 teaspoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Filling:

  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter or softened unsalted butter

Glaze (Optional):

  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon dairy-free milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare yeast mixture: Dissolve yeast in warm almond milk and let stand for 5 to 10 minutes until foamy.

  2. Mix dry ingredients: Combine gluten-free flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar in a large bowl.

  3. Wet ingredients: Whisk eggs, melted butter, vinegar, and vanilla into the yeast-milk mixture.

  4. Combine wet and dry: Slowly add wet ingredients to dry, mixing well with a wooden spoon or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment. The dough will be sticky.

  5. Rest: Cover dough and allow it to rise in a warm spot for 30-45 minutes, slightly less than traditional dough because gluten-free flour behaves differently.

  6. Roll out dough: Place dough on a lightly floured surface (gluten-free flour). Roll into a 12x9 inch rectangle.

  7. Add filling: Spread softened butter, then sprinkle cinnamon and brown sugar mixture evenly.

  8. Form rolls: Starting on the long side, roll dough tightly into a log. Cut into 9-10 slices.

  9. Proof: Arrange rolls on a greased pan and let rise 20-30 minutes.

  10. Bake: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake rolls for about 25 minutes or until lightly golden.

  11. Prepare glaze: Mix powdered sugar, milk alternative, and vanilla until smooth.

  12. Serve: Drizzle glaze over warm rolls.

Tips for Perfect Cinnamon Rolls Every Time

  • Yeast temperature: Always use warm, not hot, liquids (~110°F). Too hot and you’ll kill the yeast; too cold and it won’t activate.

  • Kneading: For classic rolls, kneading develops gluten. For gluten-free, mixing thoroughly with the right flour blend replaces this step.

  • Patience during rising: Slow, steady rising enhances flavor by allowing natural fermentation.

  • Even sizing: Use a serrated knife or unflavored floss to slice rolls for a clean cut.

  • Butter and fillings: Don’t skimp! Generous cinnamon sugar filling and butter make gooey, flavorful rolls.

  • Customization: Add nuts, raisins, or even chocolate chips to the filling for variety.

Exploring Cinnamon Roll Variations for Adventurous Bakers

The beauty of cinnamon rolls lies in their versatility. Once you master the basics, you can experiment with fillings and toppings to suit your mood and preferences.

Savory Twist

Try mixing finely chopped herbs like rosemary or thyme into cream cheese icing and replace cinnamon sugar with a blend of shredded cheese and garlic butter for a unique brunch treat.

Pumpkin Spice Cinnamon Rolls

Ideal for fall, add 1/2 cup pumpkin puree and 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice to the dough. Substitute some sugar with maple syrup in the glaze for that autumnal charm.

Vegan Cinnamon Rolls

Use plant-based milk (soy, almond, oat), substitute butter with coconut oil or vegan margarine, and replace eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed + 3 tablespoons water each). These require a bit of adjustment to achieve the perfect rise and soft texture but are wonderfully rewarding.

Storing and Reheating Cinnamon Rolls

Fresh cinnamon rolls are best enjoyed warm, but leftovers can be stored for up to 3 days in an airtight container at room temperature or up to 2 months in the freezer.

To reheat, wrap each roll loosely in foil and bake in a preheated 300°F oven for 10-15 minutes, or microwave individually for 15-20 seconds until warm. Adding a little extra glaze after reheating can revive their gooey charm.

Final Thoughts

Cinnamon rolls are the ultimate comfort food, and this guide equips you to bake both classic and gluten-free versions with ease and perfect flavor. Whether you want to stick to tradition or venture into innovative variations, these recipes promise a delicious experience every time.

Your kitchen is about to smell like heaven—grab your rolling pin and get baking!
