Saucy Selections: Sauce-Based Cooking for Two Recipes


Are you tired of the same old recipes? Looking to spice up your culinary adventures? Look no further! Saucy Selections is here to tantalize your taste buds with a variety of sauce-based cooking recipes designed specifically for two people. Whether you’re cooking for a romantic dinner or simply want to treat yourself and a friend, these saucy creations are sure to impress.

Creamy Mushroom Chicken

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken breasts to the skillet and cook until browned on both sides, about 4-5 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the sliced mushrooms and minced garlic. Cook until the mushrooms are tender and golden brown, about 5 minutes.
  5. Pour in the heavy cream and chicken broth, then add the dried thyme. Stir well to combine.
  6. Return the chicken breasts to the skillet and simmer in the sauce for another 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  7. Serve the creamy mushroom chicken over a bed of rice or pasta and enjoy!

Tangy Lemon Shrimp

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Melt the butter in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until pink and opaque, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  4. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  5. In the same skillet, add the lemon zest, lemon juice, and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes to reduce the sauce slightly.
  6. Return the shrimp to the skillet and toss to coat in the tangy lemon sauce.
  7. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve the tangy lemon shrimp with a side of steamed vegetables or crusty bread.

Spicy Tomato Pasta

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 6 ounces spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Add the diced onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in the red pepper flakes, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the cooked spaghetti to the sauce and toss to coat evenly.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve hot.
