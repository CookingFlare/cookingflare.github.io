How to Make Classic American Stuffed Shells: Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the heartwarming appeal of classic American stuffed shells. This beloved Italian-American dish combines tender pasta shells with a rich filling, all topped with a delightful marinara sauce and bubbling cheese. Perfect for a family dinner or a gathering with friends, stuffed shells are not only delicious but also surprisingly simple to make. In this post, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of creating this scrumptious dish, along with tips and tricks to make it your own.

Ingredients

To create your classic American stuffed shells, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Stuffed Shells

  • 20-25 jumbo pasta shells: Look for large shells that can hold a generous amount of filling.
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese: This creamy cheese forms the base of the filling.
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese: For a gooey, melty topping.
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese: Adds a sharp, nutty flavor to the filling.
  • 1 large egg: Helps bind the filling ingredients together.
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley: Chopped finely for added freshness.
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder: For a hint of savory flavor.
  • Salt and pepper to taste: Essential for enhancing the flavors.

For the Marinara Sauce

  • 2 cups marinara sauce: You can use store-bought or homemade.
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano: Adds a classic Italian flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil: Complements the marinara sauce beautifully.
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional): For a touch of heat.

For Topping

  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese: To sprinkle on top before baking.
  • Fresh basil leaves: For garnish (optional).

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pasta Shells

  1. Cook the Jumbo Shells: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the jumbo shells and cook according to the package instructions, typically around 9-11 minutes, until they are al dente. You want them firm enough to hold the filling without breaking apart.

  2. Drain and Rinse: Once cooked, drain the shells in a colander and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. This also makes them easier to handle.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. Combine the Filling Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, egg, parsley, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until all the ingredients are well incorporated.

  2. Taste and Adjust: Take a small spoonful of the filling and taste it. Adjust the seasoning if necessary, adding more salt, pepper, or herbs as desired.

Step 3: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

  1. Heat the Marinara Sauce: In a saucepan over medium heat, warm your marinara sauce. Stir in the dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes if you like a bit of spice. Allow the sauce to simmer for about 5 minutes to meld the flavors.

Step 4: Assemble the Stuffed Shells

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) to ensure it’s hot and ready for baking.

  2. Spread the Sauce: In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the marinara sauce on the bottom. This prevents the shells from sticking and adds flavor.

  3. Stuff the Shells: Using a spoon or a piping bag, fill each cooked jumbo shell with the ricotta mixture. Aim for a generous amount, but avoid overstuffing to prevent spillage during baking.

  4. Arrange the Shells: Place the stuffed shells seam-side up in the baking dish. Arrange them in a single layer, ensuring they are snug but not overcrowded.

  5. Top with Sauce: Pour the remaining marinara sauce over the stuffed shells, covering them evenly. Sprinkle the additional shredded mozzarella cheese on top.

Step 5: Bake the Stuffed Shells

  1. Cover and Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil to prevent the cheese from browning too quickly. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.

  2. Uncover and Brown: After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  3. Let it Rest: Remove the stuffed shells from the oven and allow them to cool for about 5-10 minutes. This resting period helps the flavors settle and makes them easier to serve.

Step 6: Serve

  1. Garnish: For a lovely presentation, garnish the stuffed shells with fresh basil leaves before serving.

  2. Enjoy: Serve the stuffed shells warm, accompanied by a side salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.

Tips for Perfect Stuffed Shells

  • Make-Ahead Option: Stuffed shells can be made ahead of time! Assemble them in the baking dish, cover tightly with foil, and refrigerate for up to 24 hours before baking. Just be sure to add a few extra minutes to the baking time if they’re coming straight from the fridge.

  • Customize Your Filling: Feel free to get creative with your filling! You can add cooked spinach, sautéed mushrooms, or even crumbled sausage for extra flavor and texture.

  • Experiment with Sauces: While marinara is classic, you can also try Alfredo sauce, pesto, or a mix of both for a different flavor profile.

  • Freezing Stuffed Shells: If you want to prepare a batch for later, you can freeze the assembled but unbaked stuffed shells. Wrap the dish tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil. When you’re ready to bake them, thaw in the refrigerator overnight and bake as directed.

Conclusion

Classic American stuffed shells are a delightful dish that brings comfort and satisfaction to any meal. With their cheesy filling and savory sauce, they are sure to become a staple in your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re making them for a family dinner or a cozy gathering, these stuffed shells are guaranteed to impress. So roll up your sleeves and get ready to enjoy the deliciousness of homemade stuffed shells! Happy cooking!
