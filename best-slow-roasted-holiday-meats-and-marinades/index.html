Best Slow Roasted Holiday Meats and Marinades


As the colder months approach, it's the perfect time to indulge in hearty, comforting dishes that showcase the rich flavors of slow-roasted meats. In this holiday season, elevate your celebration with slow-roasted meats paired with mouth-watering marinades that are sure to impress your guests and satisfy their taste buds. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a novice cook, the following recipes will help you create memorable culinary experiences around the dinner table. Start with a Stellar Brine Before diving into the recipes, it's essential to master the art of brining. A brine is a flavorful liquid that helps tenderize and enhance the flavor of your meats. To create a basic brine, combine 1 cup of kosher salt, 1/2 cup of brown sugar, and 1/4 cup of black peppercorns in a bowl. For every 4 cups of water, add 2 tablespoons of your desired flavorings - be it garlic, herbs, or citrus zest. Place the meat into the brine and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Tips for the Perfect Brine:

Use a kosher salt ratio to avoid over-salting Balance flavors according to the type of meat you are using Adjust the brining time based on the meat's size and type

Angel Hair Beef Brisket with Horseradish-Korean BBQ Marinade Slow-cooked beef is a classic holiday favorite. The addition of horseradish-Korean BBQ marinade takes it to the next level. Combine in a blender 2 cloves of garlic, 1 tablespoon of grated fresh ginger, 2 tablespoons of Gochujang, 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, 1 tablespoon of hoisin sauce, 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons of honey, 1 tablespoon of horseradish, 1 teaspoon of sesame oil, and 1 teaspoon of coarse black pepper. The perfect blend of creamy, spicy, and tangy flavors will set your Boston-style corned beef apart.

To prepare the Angel Hair Beef Brisket, combine 1 cup of chopped onion, 3 cloves of minced garlic, and 2 tablespoons of your preferred sesame oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add 1 cup of kosher salt, 1/2 cup of brown sugar, 1/4 cup of black peppercorns, 1 cup of kosher salt, and 4 cups of water to make the marinade. Place 5-6 pounds beef brisket in a roasting pan and pour the marinade over it. Transfer the pot to a low-temperature oven (275°F) for 6 hours, or when the internal temperature reaches 170°F. Remove the roast from the oven and let it rest for 30 minutes before slicing into thin strips. For the final touch, brush with your prepared marinade and sprinkle some chopped green onions for a vibrant garnish.

Flavorful Pork Chops with Cranberry-Orange Quince Glaze Rich and savory slow-cooked pork chops are always a showstopper. Combine 2 tablespoons of mixed orange juice, 1 cup of warm honey, 1/2 cup of redcurrants, 1/4 cup of brown sugar, and 2 tablespoons of white wine in a bowl to create the Cranberry-Orange Quince glaze ahead of time.

Using a whisk, adjust the glaze to the ideal consistency suitable for loose generous brushstrokes over pork. To make the key part of this glazed bake: peel and chop a butternut squash and place the peeled orange segments into a food processor or blender. Squirt with orange slices to yield an elegant-acidity filtered juice needed in soups or entirely different meals.
