Exploring Thai Breakfast: 5 Recipes to Start Your Day


Thailand, with its vibrant culture, beautiful landscapes, and rich culinary traditions, offers a breakfast experience that is both unique and flavorful. While many of us are familiar with Thai dishes like Pad Thai or Tom Yum, the countryâ€™s breakfast offerings are often overlooked, but they are just as delicious and varied. Thai breakfasts are an exciting mix of savory, sweet, and everything in between, making them perfect for anyone who wants to start the day with bold flavors and a touch of adventure.

In this post, we will explore five traditional Thai breakfast recipes that you can easily recreate at home. From rice porridge to hearty curries, these dishes will introduce you to the unique ingredients and techniques that make Thai cuisine so special.

1. Jok (Thai Rice Porridge)

What is Jok?

Jok is a Thai-style rice porridge, often enjoyed for breakfast, especially on chilly mornings or when you’re feeling under the weather. It’s warm, comforting, and highly customizable. The base is made from rice, slow-cooked in water or broth until it breaks down into a creamy consistency. Jok can be served with a variety of toppings, from minced pork to century eggs, and it often comes with a side of pickled ginger, fresh cilantro, and crispy fried garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice
  • 6 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 200g ground pork (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2-3 eggs (soft-boiled or poached)
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Fried garlic (for garnish)
  • Pickled ginger (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: Begin by washing the jasmine rice thoroughly. Add the rice to a large pot with the chicken or pork broth. Bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 45 minutes, stirring occasionally to ensure the rice breaks down and thickens.

  2. Cook the Pork: While the rice is cooking, heat a pan over medium heat. Add the ground pork and cook until browned. Add the soy sauce, fish sauce, and sesame oil to the pork, stirring well to combine. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Assemble the Jok: Once the rice has reached a creamy consistency, spoon it into bowls. Top each bowl with the cooked pork, a soft-boiled or poached egg, fresh cilantro, fried garlic, and pickled ginger (if using).

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Jok is best enjoyed warm, making it a perfect dish to kickstart your day. Pair it with a hot cup of Thai tea or coffee for the full experience.

2. Khao Tom (Thai Rice Soup)

What is Khao Tom?

Khao Tom is another traditional Thai rice soup, similar to Jok, but it’s often a little more savory and includes additional vegetables and meats like shrimp, chicken, or fish. This dish is often consumed in the morning and is favored for its light yet nourishing qualities. It’s an excellent choice for those who prefer a hearty, brothy breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jasmine rice
  • 6 cups chicken or fish broth
  • 200g shrimp or chicken breast, chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1-2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, mint) for garnish
  • Chili flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: Start by washing the rice and adding it to a pot with the broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  2. Add the Vegetables and Meat: Once the rice is softened, add the sliced onion, chopped tomatoes, and garlic to the soup. Stir and let the ingredients cook for an additional 10 minutes. Then, add your choice of shrimp or chicken and cook until tender.

  3. Season the Soup: Stir in the fish sauce and lime juice for a burst of flavor. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

  4. Serve and Garnish: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh herbs and a sprinkle of chili flakes if you enjoy a spicy kick. This dish is best enjoyed warm and offers a refreshing, nutritious way to start your day.

3. Patongo (Thai Fried Dough)

What is Patongo?

Patongo is the Thai version of fried dough, similar to Chinese youtiao. These crispy, golden, elongated dough sticks are commonly enjoyed as a breakfast snack, often dipped in sweetened condensed milk or served alongside a hot cup of Thai coffee. They’re simple to make and provide a satisfying crunch to start your day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Sweetened condensed milk (for dipping, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Gradually add warm water and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth, then cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1 hour to rise.

  2. Shape the Dough: After the dough has rested, divide it into small portions and roll each one into a long, thin strip. Pinch the ends together to form a small loop, then stretch it out into a long dough stick.

  3. Fry the Patongo: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, gently drop the dough sticks into the oil. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the hot patongo immediately with sweetened condensed milk or your favorite dipping sauce. This crispy, slightly sweet treat is perfect for those who enjoy a light yet indulgent breakfast.

4. Thai Sweet Sticky Rice with Mango (Khao Niew Mamuang)

What is Khao Niew Mamuang?

Khao Niew Mamuang is a beloved Thai dessert that is often eaten as a special breakfast or snack. Made with sticky rice, coconut milk, and ripe mango, this dish combines the creaminess of coconut with the sweetness of fresh mango, creating a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Itâ€™s a popular choice during the mango season, but it can be enjoyed year-round.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sticky rice
  • 1 1/2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Sticky Rice: Rinse the sticky rice thoroughly under cold water. Steam the rice in a traditional bamboo steamer or a rice cooker for about 20-30 minutes, until the grains are soft and sticky.

  2. Prepare the Coconut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over low heat until the sugar dissolves, but do not bring it to a boil.

  3. Mix the Rice with Coconut Milk: Once the sticky rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl. Pour the coconut milk mixture over the rice and stir gently to combine. Let the rice soak in the coconut milk for about 10 minutes.

  4. Serve with Mango: Serve the sticky rice with freshly sliced mango on the side. The sweetness of the mango pairs wonderfully with the rich coconut-flavored rice, making it a delicious and satisfying breakfast.

5. Kai Jeow (Thai Omelette)

What is Kai Jeow?

Kai Jeow is a Thai-style omelette that is often served with rice and a side of chili sauce. This omelette is light, fluffy, and savory, often made with ingredients like minced pork, shrimp, or vegetables. Itâ€™s a quick and easy breakfast option that is perfect for busy mornings when you want something filling and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 100g ground pork or shrimp (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Cooked jasmine rice (for serving)
  • Thai chili sauce (for dipping, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Eggs: In a bowl, beat the eggs and season with fish sauce, soy sauce, and white pepper. If you’re using ground pork or shrimp, cook them in a pan with a bit of oil until browned and cooked through.

  2. Fry the Omelette: Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or frying pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, pour the egg mixture into the pan. Swirl the pan to spread the eggs evenly. Let the eggs cook for about 2-3 minutes until the edges are golden and crispy.

  3. Serve the Kai Jeow: Serve the omelette on a plate, accompanied by a bowl of steaming hot jasmine rice. You can drizzle it with a bit of soy sauce or Thai chili sauce for extra flavor.
