Wholesome and Nutritious: Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Butternut Squash


Are you looking for a delicious and healthy meal that will satisfy your taste buds and nourish your body? Look no further! This Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Butternut Squash recipe is packed with wholesome ingredients and bursting with flavors that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized butternut squash
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Cut the butternut squash in half lengthwise and remove the seeds. Place the squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes, or until the squash is tender and can be easily pierced with a fork.

  3. While the squash is roasting, prepare the quinoa. In a medium-sized saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the rinsed quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is fluffy.

  4. In a separate pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they are softened and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

  5. Add the dried thyme, dried oregano, ground cumin, and paprika to the pan. Stir well to combine and let the spices toast for a minute or two, releasing their flavors.

  6. Remove the pan from the heat and add the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, and chopped pecans. Mix everything together until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Once the butternut squash is done roasting, remove it from the oven and let it cool slightly. Scoop out some of the flesh from each half, creating a cavity for the stuffing. Be careful not to puncture the skin.

  8. Fill each butternut squash half with the quinoa stuffing, pressing it down gently to ensure it fills the cavity completely.

  9. Return the stuffed squash halves to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the stuffing is heated through and slightly crispy on top.

  10. Remove from the oven, let it cool for a few minutes, and serve warm.

Serving Suggestions

This Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Butternut Squash makes for a satisfying main dish on its own. However, if you’re looking to add some extra flavors to your meal, here are a few serving suggestions:

  1. Top it with a sprinkle of fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro to add a burst of freshness.

  2. Serve it with a side of mixed greens or a simple salad for a complete and well-balanced meal.

  3. Drizzle it with a creamy tahini dressing or a tangy balsamic glaze for an extra layer of flavor.

  4. Pair it with a glass of crisp white wine or a refreshing sparkling water infused with citrus slices for a delightful dining experience.

Conclusion

With its vibrant colors, delicious flavors, and wholesome ingredients, this Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Butternut Squash is a must-try recipe for anyone looking to incorporate more nutritious and gluten-free options into their diet. Whether you’re a vegetarian, vegan, or simply someone who enjoys a good meal, this delightful dish will leave you feeling satisfied and nourished. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to indulge in a wholesome and nutritious feast that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you wanting more. Enjoy!
