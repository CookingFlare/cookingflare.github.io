Sous Vide London Broil with Rosemary and Garlic


London Broil is one of those classic dishes that never fails to earn its place on the dinner table. While it originally referred to a cooking method rather than a cut of meat, today it’s often associated with affordable cuts like flank steak or top round. But don’t let its humble origins fool you—when cooked properly, London Broil can deliver robust flavor and melt-in-your-mouth tenderness that rivals more expensive steaks. One foolproof way to achieve perfection is by using sous vide, a cooking technique that ensures your meat is tender, evenly cooked, and bursting with flavor.

This Sous Vide London Broil recipe elevates the dish with the tantalizing combination of rosemary and garlic—a pairing that enhances the beef’s richness without overwhelming it. Whether it’s for a weeknight dinner or a gathering with friends, this recipe is bound to impress. Let’s dive into the details and get you started on creating a meal where every bite tells a story.

Why Sous Vide Is Perfect for London Broil

Sous vide is a game-changer when it comes to cuts of meat like London Broil. These cuts tend to be leaner and benefit greatly from precise cooking to avoid the risk of toughness or dryness. With sous vide, you have total control over temperature and time, ensuring the meat reaches the perfect degree of doneness. Beyond precision, the sous vide technique excels at infusing flavors into the meat, allowing the rosemary and garlic to truly shine.

Traditional cooking methods often rely on guesswork, uneven heat, and continuous attention. Sous vide eliminates these variables, providing a hands-off approach that guarantees consistency and eliminates overcooking. The result? A beautifully tender London Broil that retains all its natural juiciness, complemented by fragrant herbs and aromatics.

Ingredients for Sous Vide London Broil with Rosemary and Garlic

Here’s what you’ll need to make this savory dish:

For the Beef:

  • 2 pounds of London Broil (top round or flank steak)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper (to taste)

For the Marinade:

  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • Zest of 1 lemon (optional, for brightness)

For Searing:

  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Additional minced garlic and sprigs of fresh rosemary (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Prepare the Beef

  1. Trim and Season the Meat: Start by trimming excess fat from the London Broil, if necessary. Pat the meat dry with paper towels and season generously on both sides with kosher salt and black pepper. Set it aside while you prepare the marinade.

  2. Marinate for Flavor Infusion: In a small bowl, combine minced garlic, chopped rosemary, olive oil, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, and lemon zest. Mix thoroughly to create a rich marinade. Rub this mixture all over the beef, ensuring it’s coated evenly. Place the meat in a resealable plastic bag or vacuum-seal bag, and allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes. For deeper flavor, refrigerate it overnight.

Sous Vide Cooking Method

  1. Prepare the Sous Vide Setup: Fill your sous vide container with water and set the immersion circulator to the desired temperature. For medium-rare, go with 130°F (54°C). If you prefer medium, set it to 140°F (60°C).

  2. Seal the Meat: If using a resealable plastic bag, use the water displacement method to create an airtight seal. Lower the bag into the water until only the seal remains above the surface, then close it tightly. Alternatively, use a vacuum sealer for optimum results.

  3. Cook to Perfection: Submerge the sealed London Broil into the preheated water bath. Cook it at the set temperature for 2-3 hours. Cooking sous vide offers flexibility, so don’t worry if you leave it for a little longer; the results will remain consistent.

The Searing Process

  1. Create a Gorgeous Crust: Once the cooking is complete, remove the meat from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. A dry surface is key for achieving a golden-brown crust during searing. While the beef rests momentarily, preheat a cast-iron skillet over high heat.

  2. Sear the London Broil: Add butter and olive oil to the hot skillet. For extra flavor, toss in additional minced garlic and sprigs of rosemary. Place the beef in the pan and sear on each side for 1–2 minutes, until a golden-brown crust forms. This step not only adds flavor but also enhances the presentation.

  3. Slice and Serve: Allow the meat to rest for a few minutes, then slice it thinly against the grain. This ensures maximum tenderness. Serve immediately and enjoy the mouthwatering combination of savory juices and fragrant herbs.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with Marinades: While rosemary and garlic are classic options, consider variations like thyme, balsamic vinegar, or smoked paprika for a unique flavor profile.
  • Perfect Pairings: Serve your London Broil with creamy mashed potatoes, herb-roasted vegetables, or a crisp green salad. Add a glass of red wine, and you’ve got a restaurant-worthy meal.
  • Make It a Meal Prep Dream: Sous vide London Broil can be cooked ahead of time and stored in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat it gently in a water bath set to its original cooking temperature, then sear before serving.

Why This Recipe Stands Out

The key to this recipe lies in its balance of simplicity and sophistication. London Broil may not be the most expensive cut of beef, but sous vide transforms its texture and flavor into something truly remarkable. The use of rosemary and garlic as the star aromatics perfectly complements the beef, creating a dish that feels indulgent but isn’t overly complicated. Ideal for both seasoned sous vide cooks and beginners, this recipe emphasizes precision, flavor, and heartwarming satisfaction.

Sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we approach dinner, bridging the gap between traditional and modern techniques. With this recipe, you’ll not only master the art of cooking London Broil but also elevate your kitchen skills in the eyes of family, friends, and even the most discerning foodie.

Give this Sous Vide London Broil with Rosemary and Garlic a try and discover how sous vide can maximize flavor, tenderness, and culinary creativity—all in one unforgettable dish. Happy cooking!
