Savory Meatloaf: A Family Recipe That Never Fails


Meatloaf, a classic dish that never goes out of style. It’s a staple in many families, and for good reason. It’s comforting, it’s flavorful, and it’s easy to make. But what sets a great meatloaf apart from a mediocre one? It’s all about the balance of flavors and textures. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a savory meatloaf that’s sure to become a family favorite.

The Secret to a Great Meatloaf

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s talk about what makes a great meatloaf. It’s not just about throwing some ground meat together and calling it a day. No, a great meatloaf requires a balance of flavors and textures. You want a mix of juicy meat, crispy crust, and a rich sauce. And let’s not forget about the aroma - a good meatloaf should fill your kitchen with the savory scent of roasting meat.

The Recipe

Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Now, let’s get started. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, cheese, onion, garlic, egg, ketchup, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix everything together until it’s just combined - don’t overmix! You want to leave some texture to the meat.

Shaping the Meatloaf

Once the mixture is combined, it’s time to shape the meatloaf. You can do this by hand or by using a meatloaf pan. If you’re using a pan, make sure to grease it with some cooking spray or oil. If you’re shaping it by hand, make sure to shape it into a nice, even loaf.

Baking the Meatloaf

Place the meatloaf in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C). You can check the temperature by inserting a meat thermometer into the center of the meatloaf.

Glazing the Meatloaf

While the meatloaf is baking, prepare the glaze. In a small bowl, combine the ketchup, brown sugar, and Worcestershire sauce. Once the meatloaf is done, brush the glaze all over the top of the meatloaf. Return the meatloaf to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the glaze is caramelized and sticky.

Serving the Meatloaf

Once the meatloaf is done, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. This will allow the juices to redistribute and the meat to set. Slice the meatloaf and serve with your favorite sides - we recommend roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind:

  • To add some extra flavor to your meatloaf, try adding some chopped bell peppers or mushrooms to the mixture.
  • If you want to make your meatloaf more spicy, add some diced jalapeños or red pepper flakes to the mixture.
  • To make your meatloaf more moist, try adding some chopped bacon or sausage to the mixture.
  • If you want to make your meatloaf more gluten-free, try substituting the breadcrumbs with gluten-free crumbs or crushed gluten-free crackers.

Conclusion

And there you have it - a savory meatloaf recipe that’s sure to become a family favorite. With its perfect balance of flavors and textures, this meatloaf is sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So next time you’re looking for a comforting, delicious meal, give this recipe a try. Your family will thank you!
