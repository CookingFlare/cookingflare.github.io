Decadent Chocolate Pecan Pie Recipe for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time to indulge in delicious desserts, and what better way to satisfy your sweet tooth than with a decadent chocolate pecan pie? This twist on the classic pecan pie adds a rich and velvety chocolate filling that will have your guests begging for seconds. With a buttery crust and a gooey, nutty center, this pie is sure to become a new holiday favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 3/4 cup dark corn syrup
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups pecan halves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.

  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the chocolate chips in the microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until smooth. Set aside to cool slightly.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the dark corn syrup, granulated sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  4. Slowly pour the melted chocolate into the mixture, stirring continuously until fully incorporated.

  5. Arrange the pecan halves on the bottom of the pie crust, creating a single layer.

  6. Pour the chocolate filling over the pecans, ensuring that the pecans are evenly coated.

  7. Place the pie dish on a baking sheet and carefully transfer it to the preheated oven. Bake for 50-55 minutes, or until the filling is set around the edges but slightly jiggly in the center.

  8. Remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool completely on a wire rack. The filling will continue to set as it cools.

  9. Once the pie has cooled, refrigerate it for at least 2 hours to ensure a firm and sliceable consistency.

  10. Serve chilled or at room temperature, and garnish with whipped cream or a dusting of powdered sugar, if desired.

Tips for Success:

  • Make sure to use a deep-dish pie crust to accommodate the rich chocolate filling and generous amount of pecans.

  • For an extra layer of flavor, consider adding a splash of bourbon or a teaspoon of instant coffee granules to the chocolate filling.

  • To prevent the crust from over-browning, cover the edges with foil or a pie crust shield halfway through the baking time.

  • Allow the pie to cool completely before slicing to ensure clean and neat slices.

  • If you prefer a more pronounced chocolate flavor, use bittersweet chocolate instead of semisweet.

Conclusion:

This Decadent Chocolate Pecan Pie is the perfect dessert to elevate your Thanksgiving feast. The combination of silky chocolate and crunchy pecans creates a delightful contrast of textures and flavors. Whether you serve it as the grand finale of your holiday meal or enjoy it as a sweet treat throughout the season, this pie is guaranteed to impress your friends and family. So go ahead, indulge in a slice of chocolatey goodness and savor the joy of Thanksgiving. Happy baking!
