The Best Pie Recipes for a Potluck


Few things bring people together quite like a potluck. Sharing delicious dishes is a quintessential way to forge connections and celebrate good times. If you’re looking to impress your fellow guests, pies are the perfect choice. Versatile, comforting, and downright celebratory, a good pie can easily become the centerpiece of any potluck spread. Below, we’ll dive into a collection of pie recipes that are sure to wow your crowd and leave them asking for the recipe—whether you’re going for sweet decadence or savory satisfaction.

Classic Apple Pie

No potluck is complete without a quintessential apple pie. This timeless dessert is universally adored and offers a nostalgic taste of home. A flaky, buttery crust combined with tender apples and warming spices like cinnamon makes it the standout dish on the dessert table.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 double pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, toss the sliced apples with granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
  3. Roll out your pie crust and fit it into a 9-inch pie pan. Pour the apple mixture into the crust, ensuring even distribution.
  4. Cover with the top crust, crimp the edges, and cut small slits on top for air circulation.
  5. Brush the crust with an egg wash made from the egg and milk for that golden shine.
  6. Bake for 50-60 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  7. Cool for at least 2 hours before serving for best slicing results.

Rich Chocolate Cream Pie

For chocolate lovers, this pie is a dream come true. Its velvety chocolate filling, luscious whipped cream topping, and buttery crust deliver a decadent experience that’s hard to beat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked 9-inch pie crust
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • Pinch of salt
  • 5 ounces bittersweet chocolate, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat milk just until it begins to simmer.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk sugar, cornstarch, cocoa powder, and salt. Gradually add the egg yolks, stirring until smooth.
  3. Slowly whisk the hot milk into the bowl mixture, then return everything to the saucepan. Stir continuously over medium heat until thickened.
  4. Remove from heat, add the chopped chocolate and vanilla extract, mixing until smooth.
  5. Pour the pudding mixture into the pre-baked crust and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  6. Whip the heavy cream and powdered sugar until soft peaks form. Spread over the chilled chocolate pie before serving.

Savory Chicken Pot Pie

Sometimes, a savory pie is what the table craves. Hearty, filling, and comforting all in one, chicken pot pie is the perfect savory option to bring to a potluck. This classic dish combines tender chicken and vegetables with a creamy sauce inside a flaky crust.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pre-made pie crusts
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded or diced
  • 1 cup carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup (about 2 small) potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a skillet, melt the butter and stir in the flour until smooth, forming a roux. Slowly whisk in the chicken broth and milk, bringing to a simmer until thickened. Add thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir in chicken, carrots, potatoes, and peas, ensuring they’re evenly coated with the sauce.
  4. Roll out one pie crust into a 9-inch pan, pour the filling into it, and cover with the second crust. Seal and crimp the edges, then cut several slits on top.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown. Allow to cool for 10 minutes before slicing.

Tangy Key Lime Pie

For a lighter, refreshing addition to your dessert spread, key lime pie is always a hit. Its tangy citrus flavor paired with a graham cracker crust makes it an excellent palate cleanser at a potluck.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh key lime juice
  • Zest of 2 limes
  • Whipped cream for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar in a bowl until evenly combined. Press into a 9-inch pie pan and bake for 10 minutes.
  3. Whisk egg yolks in a bowl, then add condensed milk and lime juice. Stir until thickened. Fold in lime zest.
  4. Pour the filling into the baked crust. Bake for 15 minutes, then cool completely and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  5. Garnish with whipped cream and lime slices before serving.

All-American Pecan Pie

A potluck wouldn’t be complete without a slice of pecan pie. Sweet, nutty, and buttery, pecan pie is one of the easiest and most satisfying desserts you can bring to any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 single pie crust
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups pecan halves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the corn syrup, brown sugar, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla. Mix until smooth.
  3. Add the pecans and mix to coat evenly in the filling.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust and spread out the pecans to create a uniform distribution.
  5. Bake for 50-55 minutes or until set yet slightly jiggles in the center. Cool before slicing.

Tips for Perfect Potluck Pies

To ensure your pies are the heroes of the potluck, here are a few expert tips:

  1. Use quality ingredients: Whether it’s fresh fruit or high-quality chocolate, the ingredients make all the difference.
  2. Transport smartly: Invest in sturdy pie carriers or boxes to ensure your creations arrive in perfect condition.
  3. Label your pies: Include a small sign or card detailing the flavors or any allergens to help everyone enjoy.
  4. Consider bite-sized portions: Mini pies or hand pies are easier for guests to grab and eat without utensils.

Whether you’re serving up a golden chicken pot pie or a silky chocolate cream pie, these recipes will ensure whatever dish you choose is a resounding success. When you leave the potluck, don’t be surprised to hear people raving about your pie—the real challenge is deciding which one to make. Now that you’re armed with these incredible recipes, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! Happy potlucking!
