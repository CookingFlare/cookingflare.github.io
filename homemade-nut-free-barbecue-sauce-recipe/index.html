Homemade Nut-Free Barbecue Sauce Recipe

5 min · 886 words

Are you tired of missing out on delicious barbecue sauce because of nut allergies? Look no further! This homemade nut-free barbecue sauce recipe is the perfect solution for those who want to enjoy the smoky, tangy flavors of barbecue without any nuts. Made with simple ingredients you probably already have in your pantry, this sauce is not only allergy-friendly but also incredibly flavorful. So grab your apron and let’s get saucy!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using). Stir well to combine.

  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. After 15 minutes, add the water to the saucepan and stir until well incorporated. This will help thin out the sauce and make it easier to brush onto your favorite grilled meats.

  4. Continue simmering the sauce for another 5 minutes, or until it reaches your desired consistency. If you prefer a thicker sauce, simmer for a bit longer. If you like it thinner, simmer for a shorter time.

  5. Once the sauce is ready, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly before transferring it to a jar or container. The sauce will thicken slightly as it cools.

  6. Use this nut-free barbecue sauce as a marinade, basting sauce, or dipping sauce for all your grilled favorites. It pairs perfectly with chicken, ribs, burgers, and even vegetables!

  7. Store any leftover sauce in the refrigerator for up to two weeks. Just make sure to give it a good stir before using it again.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a sweeter sauce, you can increase the amount of brown sugar in the recipe. Just adjust to taste.
  • For a smokier flavor, you can substitute the smoked paprika with liquid smoke. Start with a few drops and adjust to your preference.
  • Feel free to experiment with different spices and seasonings to customize the sauce to your liking. Add a pinch of cinnamon for a warm, spicy note, or a splash of hot sauce for an extra kick.

Enjoy the rich flavors of homemade barbecue sauce without worrying about nut allergies. This nut-free barbecue sauce recipe is so delicious, you won’t even miss the nuts. Fire up your grill and get ready for a saucy, finger-licking good time!

Remember, always check the labels of your ingredients to ensure they are nut-free, as manufacturing processes may vary. Enjoy your allergy-friendly barbecue sauce and share it with friends and family. Happy grilling!
