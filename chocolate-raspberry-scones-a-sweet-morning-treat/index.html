Chocolate Raspberry Scones: A Sweet Morning Treat


There’s nothing quite like waking up to the aroma of freshly baked scones. And when those scones are infused with the irresistible combination of chocolate and raspberries, it’s a treat that will make your taste buds dance with delight. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create Chocolate Raspberry Scones that are not only delicious but also incredibly easy to make.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Stir in the chocolate chips and raspberries, making sure they are evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the heavy cream and vanilla extract. Pour the cream mixture into the dry ingredients and gently stir until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough scones.

  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times to bring it together. Pat the dough into a circle that is about 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick.

  6. Using a sharp knife or a round cookie cutter, cut the dough into wedges or circles, depending on your preference. Place the scones onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving a few inches of space between each one.

  7. Bake the scones in the preheated oven for 15-18 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top. Remove them from the oven and allow them to cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before serving.

  8. Serve your Chocolate Raspberry Scones warm or at room temperature. They are perfect on their own, but you can also enjoy them with a dollop of clotted cream or a drizzle of melted chocolate for an extra indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a sweeter scone, you can sprinkle some coarse sugar on top of the dough before baking.
  • Feel free to substitute the dark chocolate chips with milk chocolate or white chocolate chips, depending on your preference.
  • If fresh raspberries are not available, you can use frozen raspberries. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before adding them to the dough.
  • These scones are best enjoyed on the day they are baked, but you can store them in an airtight container for up to 2 days. To reheat, simply warm them in a low oven for a few minutes.

So, the next time you’re looking for a sweet morning treat, give these Chocolate Raspberry Scones a try. With their rich chocolate flavor and bursts of juicy raspberries, they are sure to become a favorite in your household. Enjoy!
