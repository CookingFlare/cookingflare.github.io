A Sweet Treat: Gluten-Free Cherry Pie


Who doesn’t love a delicious slice of pie? The combination of a buttery crust and a sweet, fruity filling is simply irresistible. But for those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding a pie that meets their dietary restrictions can be a challenge. That’s why I’m here to share with you a mouthwatering recipe for a gluten-free cherry pie that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 4-6 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour and salt.
  2. Add the cold cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, while gently mixing the dough with a fork. Continue adding water until the dough comes together and can be formed into a ball.
  4. Divide the dough into two equal portions and flatten each portion into a disk. Wrap them in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the pitted cherries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, vanilla extract, and lemon juice. Mix well until the cherries are coated with the sugar and cornstarch mixture.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Take one of the chilled dough disks out of the fridge and roll it out on a lightly floured surface until it’s about 1/8 inch thick.
  3. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to a 9-inch pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and sides of the dish.
  4. Pour the cherry filling into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. Roll out the second dough disk and cut it into strips to create a lattice pattern on top of the cherry filling. Alternatively, you can cover the pie with a full crust, making sure to cut a few slits to allow steam to escape.
  6. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  7. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  8. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely before serving.

Serving and Storage

Once the pie has cooled, it’s ready to be enjoyed. Serve it as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to three days. Simply reheat a slice in the microwave or enjoy it cold.

Conclusion

With this gluten-free cherry pie recipe, you don’t have to compromise on taste or texture. The buttery crust and sweet cherry filling will satisfy your dessert cravings, while the gluten-free ingredients make it accessible to everyone. So go ahead and treat yourself to a slice of this delightful pie. Your taste buds will thank you!
