A Taste of Hawaii: Delicious Teriyaki Chicken Wrap Recipes


Hawaii is known for its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, and of course, its incredible food. One dish that has gained immense popularity both locally and beyond is teriyaki chicken. This savory-sweet dish, often served with rice and vegetables, has found a new home in wraps, making it a perfect on-the-go meal option. In this post, we’ll explore various delicious teriyaki chicken wrap recipes, each infused with a taste of the islands. From classic combinations to creative twists, you’ll find a wrap recipe to satisfy any craving.

The Origins of Teriyaki Chicken

Before diving into the recipes, let’s take a moment to understand the origins of teriyaki chicken. Teriyaki is a Japanese cooking technique that involves grilling or broiling meat with a glaze made from soy sauce, sake, and sugar. In Hawaii, this dish has evolved, influenced by the local cuisine and the diverse cultures that inhabit the islands. The result is a unique blend of flavors that embodies the spirit of aloha.

Essential Ingredients for Teriyaki Chicken Wraps

Creating the perfect teriyaki chicken wrap begins with selecting high-quality ingredients. Here’s a list of essentials you’ll need:

For the Teriyaki Chicken:

  • Chicken Breasts or Thighs: Boneless and skinless chicken is ideal for wraps.
  • Soy Sauce: A key ingredient in the teriyaki sauce.
  • Brown Sugar: Adds sweetness and helps create a caramelized glaze.
  • Mirin or Rice Vinegar: Provides acidity and depth of flavor.
  • Garlic and Ginger: Freshly minced for an aromatic base.
  • Sesame Oil: Adds a nutty flavor.

Wrap Components:

  • Tortillas or Wraps: Choose flour, whole wheat, or even lettuce leaves for a low-carb option.
  • Fresh Vegetables: Lettuce, cucumbers, bell peppers, and carrots add crunch and freshness.
  • Avocado: Creamy and rich, avocado enhances flavor and texture.
  • Teriyaki Sauce: Use store-bought or homemade for drizzling.
  • Sesame Seeds: For garnish and added crunch.

Recipe 1: Classic Teriyaki Chicken Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 4 large tortillas
  • 1 cup lettuce, shredded
  • 1/2 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 bell pepper, sliced thin
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, mirin, minced garlic, and ginger. Add chicken breasts and marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably overnight in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Remove chicken from the marinade, reserving the marinade. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side or until cooked through. In the last few minutes of cooking, brush the chicken with the reserved marinade for extra flavor.

  3. Assemble the Wrap: Once cooked, let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing it into strips. On each tortilla, layer the lettuce, sliced cucumbers, bell peppers, avocado, and teriyaki chicken. Drizzle with extra teriyaki sauce and sprinkle with sesame seeds.

  4. Wrap It Up: Fold in the sides of the tortilla and roll tightly. Slice in half and serve immediately, or wrap in foil for an on-the-go meal.

Recipe 2: Spicy Teriyaki Chicken Wrap

For those who love a little heat, this spicy teriyaki chicken wrap adds a kick to the classic recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 4 large spinach tortillas
  • 1 cup mixed greens
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 jalapeño, sliced (optional)
  • Cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, sriracha, rice vinegar, garlic, and ginger. Add chicken thighs and marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Grill the marinated chicken thighs for about 6-8 minutes on each side until fully cooked, brushing with the marinade during cooking.

  3. Slice and Prepare: Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before slicing it into strips.

  4. Assemble the Wrap: On each spinach tortilla, layer mixed greens, red onion, bell pepper, jalapeño slices, and spicy teriyaki chicken. Garnish with fresh cilantro and a drizzle of sriracha if desired.

  5. Wrap It Up: Fold in the sides of the tortilla and roll tightly. Enjoy this spicy twist on a Hawaiian classic!

Recipe 3: Teriyaki Chicken and Pineapple Wrap

Bring a tropical flair to your wraps with the addition of sweet pineapple.

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts, boneless and skinless
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned)
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, sliced
  • Green onions, chopped for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, honey, garlic, and ginger. Add chicken breasts and marinate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Cook the Chicken and Pineapple: Preheat a grill or skillet over medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, and in the last few minutes, add the pineapple chunks to grill until caramelized.

  3. Slice the Chicken: Once cooked, let the chicken rest before slicing into strips.

  4. Assemble the Wrap: On each tortilla, layer shredded cabbage, grilled pineapple, sliced bell pepper, and teriyaki chicken.

  5. Wrap It Up: Roll tightly and garnish with chopped green onions before serving.

Tips for the Perfect Teriyaki Chicken Wrap

  • Prep Ahead: Marinate the chicken the night before for maximum flavor. You can also chop vegetables and prepare sauces ahead of time.
  • Wrap Variations: Feel free to experiment with different types of wraps—whole wheat, spinach, or even rice paper for a fresh take.
  • Add Crunch: Incorporate ingredients like crushed nuts or crispy fried onions for an added texture.
  • Serve with Dipping Sauce: A side of extra teriyaki sauce or a spicy mayo can elevate your wrap experience.

Conclusion

Teriyaki chicken wraps are a delicious and versatile meal option that can be enjoyed any time of the day. Whether you prefer the classic recipe, crave some heat, or want to indulge in tropical flavors, there’s a wrap for everyone. These recipes not only celebrate the rich culinary heritage of Hawaii but also bring a taste of the islands to your kitchen. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to savor the delightful flavors of Hawaii with these teriyaki chicken wrap recipes! Enjoy your culinary adventure and embrace the spirit of aloha with every bite.
