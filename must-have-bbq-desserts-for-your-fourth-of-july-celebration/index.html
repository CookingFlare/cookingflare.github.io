Must-Have BBQ Desserts for Your Fourth of July Celebration


The Fourth of July is a time for family, friends, and food. If you’re hosting a BBQ, chances are you already have a delicious spread planned, from savory grilled meats to tangy sides. But no BBQ is complete without the perfect dessert to cap off the evening. This year, why not make your celebration unforgettable by serving up some mouthwatering desserts that will have your guests begging for seconds?

From classic pies to inventive treats with a patriotic twist, we’ve rounded up some must-have BBQ desserts that will steal the show at your Fourth of July celebration. Whether you’re looking for something easy to make or a showstopper to impress your guests, these recipes will take your BBQ to the next level.

1. Classic American Apple Pie

Apple pie is the epitome of an American dessert. It’s comforting, familiar, and a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. Whether served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or at room temperature with a drizzle of caramel sauce, it’s the perfect ending to a BBQ meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ pounds of Granny Smith apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 package of pie crusts (or homemade if preferred)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the apple slices with lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and flour.
  3. Roll out the bottom pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Pour the apple mixture into the crust, and dot with small pieces of butter.
  4. Roll out the top pie crust and place it over the apples. Trim any excess crust, and pinch the edges together to seal. Cut small slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake for 45–50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  6. Let cool for 15 minutes before serving. Pair with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence.

Apple pie is a dessert that screams America, and its simplicity is what makes it so beloved.

2. Red, White, and Blueberry Cheesecake Bars

These cheesecake bars are perfect for adding a patriotic pop of color to your BBQ dessert table. With layers of creamy cheesecake, a touch of sweetness from the berries, and a buttery graham cracker crust, these bars are as visually stunning as they are delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking dish to form the crust.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract and sour cream. Mix until fully combined.
  4. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the graham cracker crust and spread it out evenly.
  5. Bake for 35–40 minutes, or until the center is set and the top is lightly golden.
  6. Let the cheesecake bars cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 3 hours.
  7. Before serving, arrange the strawberries and blueberries in a star or flag pattern on top for a festive, red, white, and blue effect.

These cheesecake bars are a perfect combination of creamy and fruity, making them an ideal treat for summer.

3. S’mores Brownies

S’mores are the quintessential campfire treat, and these s’mores brownies bring all the best elements of the classic dessert into one fudgy, gooey treat. With a graham cracker crust, rich brownie layer, and toasted marshmallows on top, they’re sure to be a hit at your BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of brownie mix (plus ingredients required on the box)
  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 1 ½ cups mini marshmallows
  • 1 cup milk chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven according to the instructions on the brownie mix box.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of a greased 9x9-inch baking pan to form the crust.
  3. Prepare the brownie mix according to package directions, and pour it over the graham cracker crust.
  4. Bake according to the instructions for the brownie mix. About 5 minutes before the brownies are done, remove them from the oven and sprinkle the mini marshmallows and chocolate chips on top.
  5. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 5–7 minutes, or until the marshmallows are toasted and golden.
  6. Allow the brownies to cool before cutting into squares and serving.

S’mores brownies are an irresistible dessert that combines the flavors of a traditional s’more with the richness of a brownie.

4. Patriotic Fruit Tart

If you’re looking for a lighter dessert that still packs a punch in terms of flavor and festivity, a patriotic fruit tart is the way to go. With a buttery, flaky crust, a sweet cream filling, and fresh fruit arranged in the colors of the flag, this dessert is as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of refrigerated pie crust (or homemade)
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1 kiwi, peeled and sliced

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and press it into a 9-inch tart pan, trimming any excess dough. Poke holes in the bottom of the crust with a fork.
  3. Bake the crust for 12–15 minutes, or until golden brown. Let cool completely.
  4. In a medium bowl, beat the cream cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture until fully combined.
  5. Spread the cream filling evenly over the cooled tart crust.
  6. Arrange the strawberries, blueberries, and kiwi on top in a flag pattern, or any design you like.
  7. Serve immediately, or refrigerate until ready to serve.

This fruit tart is a refreshing and colorful addition to any BBQ spread, providing a lighter option that still feels festive.

5. No-Bake Berry Trifle

A no-bake trifle is an easy and impressive dessert that will have everyone asking for the recipe. Layered with fresh berries, whipped cream, and a delicious sponge cake, it’s a cool and creamy treat perfect for summer BBQs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 pint blueberries
  • 1 pint raspberries
  • 1 box vanilla pound cake (or homemade)
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form.
  2. Slice the pound cake into cubes. Layer the bottom of a large trifle dish with cake cubes.
  3. Spoon a layer of whipped cream over the cake cubes, followed by a layer of mixed berries.
  4. Repeat the layers until you fill the dish, finishing with a layer of whipped cream on top.
  5. Garnish with additional fresh berries for a pop of color and freshness.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

This no-bake berry trifle is a simple, yet elegant dessert that’s perfect for a hot summer day. It’s easy to prepare and sure to impress your guests with its beautiful layers and delicious flavors.

6. Red, White, and Blue Cupcakes

Cupcakes are always a favorite at any celebration, and these red, white, and blue cupcakes are ideal for your Fourth of July BBQ. With a light and fluffy vanilla cake base and patriotic frosting, they’re as fun to make as they are to eat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box vanilla cake mix (plus ingredients required on the box)
  • 1 batch of buttercream frosting (recipe below)
  • Red and blue food coloring

Buttercream Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. Prepare the vanilla cake mix according to package directions and divide the batter into three bowls.
  3. Add a few drops of red food coloring to one bowl and blue food coloring to another. Leave the third bowl of batter uncolored.
  4. Spoon alternating layers of red, white, and blue batter into each cupcake liner, filling each liner about halfway.
  5. Bake for 18–20 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let cool completely.
  6. For the frosting, beat the butter, powdered sugar, vanilla, milk, and salt together until smooth.
  7. Divide the frosting into three bowls and tint each with red, white, and blue food coloring.
  8. Frost the cupcakes with the colored frosting and decorate with sprinkles or star-shaped decorations for a festive touch.

These cupcakes are both cute and delicious, making them a perfect sweet bite for any Fourth of July celebration.

Conclusion

No matter what kind of BBQ dessert you’re looking for, these options will make your Fourth of July celebration a memorable one. From classic apple pies to showstopping trifles and cupcakes, these treats will add the perfect sweet ending to your meal. So gather your ingredients, fire up the grill, and let the festivities begin!
