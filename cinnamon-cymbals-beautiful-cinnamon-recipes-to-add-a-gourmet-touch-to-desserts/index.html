Cinnamon Cymbals: Beautiful Cinnamon Recipes to Add a Gourmet Touch to Desserts


If there is a single spice that has transcended borders and generations, it’s none other than cinnamon. With its warm, sweet, and slightly spicy flavor profile, cinnamon has an unparalleled ability to transform everyday desserts into works of art that delight the palate. Whether the goal is to breathe fresh insight into a traditional recipe or to craft truly show-stopping treats, cinnamon reigns supreme.

Let’s explore a meticulously curated selection of cinnamon-centric recipes that celebrate its versatility and ability to elevate desserts to gourmet status. You don’t need to be a seasoned pastry chef to craft these—it’s all about letting cinnamon take center stage.

The Cinnamon Whirl Tart

This tart is a visual and flavorful spectacle. A combination of buttery shortcrust pastry and a silky cinnamon-infused filling makes it a treat worthy of a special occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons chilled water
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ⅔ cup dark brown sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Crust: In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, and salt. Add the butter, rubbing it into the dry ingredients with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs. Stir in the egg yolk and water until a dough forms. Chill the dough for 30 minutes.

  2. Create the Filling: Whisk together the heavy cream, brown sugar, eggs, cinnamon, and vanilla until smooth and well-combined.

  3. Assemble the Tart: Roll out the dough and press it into a tart pan. Pour the filling into the pastry shell.

  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the tart for 40-45 minutes or until the filling is set but still slightly wobbly in the center. Cool before serving.

This tart stuns with its balance of warmth and creaminess, making it the perfect way to open a cinnamon dessert lineup.

Cinnamon Pecan Crusted Cheesecake

A cheesecake is exquisite on its own, but adding a cinnamon and pecan crust elevates it into an entirely new realm of indulgence.

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups pecans, finely chopped
  • 3 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  • 3 (8-ounce) packages of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Zest of 1 orange

Directions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 325°F (165°C) to create that perfectly smooth cheesecake texture.

  2. Prepare the Crust: Combine the pecans, granulated sugar, melted butter, and cinnamon until evenly mixed. Press firmly into the bottom of a springform pan.

  3. Make the Filling: Beat the softened cream cheese and sugar until fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla, cinnamon, and orange zest.

  4. Bake: Pour the filling over the crust and bake for 55-60 minutes or until the center is just set. Chill the cheesecake for at least 4 hours before serving.

This recipe highlights the nutty, crunchy pecan crust that’s embraced by a velvety cinnamon-spiced filling—an absolute masterpiece.

Cinnamon Rose Pavlova

A show-stopping pavlova adorned with cinnamon and a whisper of floral notes is an elegant conclusion to any gathering.

Ingredients:

Meringue:

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon

Topping:

  • 1 cup whipped cream
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon rose water
  • Ground cinnamon for dusting

Directions:

  1. Make the Meringue: Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C). Beat the egg whites in a clean bowl until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar, 1 tablespoon at a time, until stiff, glossy peaks form. Gently fold in the cinnamon and cornstarch.

  2. Bake: Spoon the meringue onto parchment paper to form a disk-like shape. Bake for 1 hour, then allow it to cool inside the oven with the door slightly ajar.

  3. Assemble: Whisk the whipped cream with honey and rose water until peaks form. Spoon this over the cooled meringue and garnish with a light dusting of cinnamon.

The blend of spiced meringue and ethereal cream topping creates a dessert that’s as light as it is decadent.

Cinnamon Churro Brownie Bars

For dessert lovers who can never choose between brownies and churros, consider this recipe the best of both worlds.

Ingredients:

Brownie Batter:

  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ⅓ cup cocoa powder
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Cinnamon-Sugar Topping:

  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Prepare the Brownie Base: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Mix the melted butter, sugar, eggs, and vanilla until smooth. Stir in the cocoa powder, flour, and salt until a thick batter is formed. Pour into a greased baking pan.

  2. Add the Churro Magic: Combine the sugar and cinnamon for the topping. Sprinkle generously over the batter before baking.

  3. Bake: Keep the bars in the oven for 25-30 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean. Allow them to cool, but not too much—these bars are divine when slightly warm.

The decadent fudginess of brownies meets the crispy sugared coating of churros, creating a dessert explosion in every bite.

Perfect Pairings: Drinks to Complement Cinnamon Desserts

Cinnamon desserts shine even brighter when paired with the right beverages. Here are some recommendations to savor every spicy, warm, and sweet bite:

  • Chai Latte: The spiced layering of chai tea perfectly echoes and complements the cinnamon in any dessert.
  • Hot Toddy: The honey, lemon, and whiskey in a classic hot toddy create a harmonious partner for cinnamon treats, particularly those with cream-based elements.
  • Mulled Wine: This festive beverage with its notes of cinnamon and citrus is a natural pairing for cinnamon desserts during colder seasons.

Cinnamon gives you the creative freedom to play with its warm, exotic tones in unexpected ways. By incorporating it into these beautiful recipes, you’ll craft desserts that evoke a sense of comfort while delivering a gourmet punch. Explore these cinnamon masterpieces, and let every bite remind you of why this timeless ingredient is a star in kitchens around the globe.
