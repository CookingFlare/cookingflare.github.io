Spicy Dishes for a Fiery Valentine's Day Celebration


Valentine's Day is just around the corner, and while many people associate this holiday with sweetness and romance, we think it's time to spice things up. If you're looking for a unique twist on the classic love-filled evening, why not add a dash of heat to your celebration? Only a careful blend of spices and seasonings can add the sultry spark you're going for. From mildly piquant to tear-inducing hot, here are some mouth-watering and sure-to-stake-your-heart recipes to tantalize your taste buds and keep things exciting on this most romantic of nights. Spicy Shrimp in a Korean-Inspired Red Curry Sauce One of the stars of the show is this deviation on the classic red curry dish. This sweet and fiery curry showcases succulent shrimp in a silky coconut-based sauce tempered with aromatic star anise and onions. Red curry paste forms the base of the sauce, paired with a sprinkling of coriander. Be bold with your additions of fresh cilantro, garlic and Thai chilies. Ingredients

2 tablespoons Red Curry paste 1 tablespoon coconut milk 1 cup water 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 onion, thinly sliced 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon sugar 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined 1 tablespoon fish sauce 1 tablespoon lemon juice Chopped cilantro for garnish

Cooking Instructions

Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat, sauté sliced onions for 7-10 minutes. Add the coriander, cumin and smoked paprika for 1 minute, and sauté. In a blender or food processor, combine red curry paste, coconut milk, water, salt, sugar, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. Blend until smooth. In a large pan over low heat, combine curry sauce, onions and shrimp. Stir gently for 7 minutes. Add fish sauce and lemon juice, then lie back, waiting for the flavors to intermingle and deepen.

Fiery Valentine's Day Celebration Valentine's Day is just around the corner, and while many people associate this holiday with sweetness and romance, we think it's time to spice things up. If you're looking for a unique twist on the classic love-filled evening, why not add a dash of heat to your celebration? Only a careful blend of spices and seasonings can add the sultry spark you're going for. From mildly piquant to tear-inducing hot, here are some mouth-watering and sure-to-stake-your-heart recipes to tantalize your taste buds and keep things exciting on this most romantic of nights. Spicy Shrimp in a Korean-Inspired Red Curry Sauce One of the stars of the show is this deviation on the classic red curry dish. This sweet and fiery curry showcases succulent shrimp in a silky coconut-based sauce tempered with aromatic star anise and onions. Red curry paste forms the base of the sauce, paired with a sprinkling of coriander. Be bold with your additions of fresh cilantro, garlic, and Thai chilies. Ingredients

2 tablespoons Red Curry paste 1 tablespoon coconut milk 1 cup water 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 onion, thinly sliced 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1 teaspoon salt 1/2 teaspoon sugar 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes 1/2 cup shrimp, peeled and deveined 1 tablespoon fish sauce 1 tablespoon lemon juice Chopped cilantro for garnish

Cooking Instructions

Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat, sauté sliced onions for 7-10 minutes. Add the coriander, cumin and smoked paprika for 1 minute, and sauté. In a blender or food processor, combine red curry paste, coconut milk, water, salt, sugar, black pepper, and red pepper flakes. Blend until smooth. In a large pan over low heat, combine curry sauce, onions and shrimp. Stir gently for 7 minutes. Add fish sauce and lemon juice, then let the flavors meld together for a truly unforgettable Valentine's Day dinner.

Molcajete Chili-Stuffed Sweet Potatoes with a Kick of Smokiness Street food lovers unite! This spicy recipe takes a sweet potato and amps up the heat with a spicy chili blend. It's perfect for a date night dinner, as it serves two and is a breeze to make. Ingredients

2 large sweet potatoes 1/4 cup chili powder 2 tablespoons tomato paste 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 medium onion, diced 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/2 teaspoon brown sugar 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 cup chopped cilantro for garnish 2 dashes hot sauce (such as sriracha or Valentina)

Cooking Instructions

Bake or microwave the sweet potatoes until cooked through. In a saucepan, heat oil over medium heat. Add garlic and onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes. Add the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, brown sugar, salt, and black pepper, and cook for another minute. Once the potatoes are cooked, cut them in half and fill the centers with the spicy chili mixture. Top with cilantro and a dash of hot sauce for an added kick.

Spicy Baja-Style Shrimp Tacos This fiery twist on traditional tacos will keep your date on their toes. The combination of spicy shrimp, crispy slaw, and cooling cilantro will keep things exciting and leave you both wanting more. Ingredients

1 pound shrimp 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon chili powder 1 teaspoon lime juice 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1 cup Mexican street corn 1/2 cup diced cilantro 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 2 tablespoons lime juice Salt and pepper, to taste 12 corn tortillas

Cooking Instructions

Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add cumin, chili powder, and smoked paprika, and cook for 1 minute. Add the shrimp and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes per side. Meanwhile, mix the corn, cilantro, and parsley. Warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds. Assemble the tacos with the spicy shrimp, corn slaw, and a squeeze of lime juice.

Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Spicy Brown Butter and Sage This unique recipe combines the comforting warmth of gnocchi with the spicy kick of chili flakes and the earthy sweetness of sage. It's perfect for a date night dinner. Ingredients

2 large sweet potatoes 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup semolina flour 1/4 cup grated Parmesan 1/4 cup unsalted butter 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon chili flakes 1/4 cup chopped fresh sage Salt and pepper, to taste

Cooking Instructions

Cook the gnocchi according to package directions. Meanwhile, melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the garlic and chili flakes, and cook for 1-2 minutes. Add the sage and cook for another minute. Toss the gnocchi with the spicy brown butter and season with salt and pepper.

Angel Hair Pasta with Shrimp and Spicy arrabbiata sauce This spicy pasta dish is a staple of Italian cuisine with a twist. The combination of succulent shrimp and spicy arrabbiata sauce will keep things exciting and leave you wanting more. Ingredients

1 pound pasta 1 pound shrimp 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup grated Parmesan 1 tablespoon chili flakes Salt and pepper, to taste

Cooking Instructions

Cook the pasta according to package directions. Heat oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the crushed tomatoes, parsley, and Parmesan, and cook for 10-15 minutes. Add the shrimp and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes per side. Toss the pasta with the spicy arrabbiata sauce and shrimp.

From these spicy dishes, pick the one that will set your taste buds ablaze. No matter which recipe you choose, you'll be sure to add some sparkle to your Valentine's Day celebration and have your partner's heart skipping a beat.
