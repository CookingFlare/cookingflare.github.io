Canning Beyond Jellies: Savory Onion Marmalades


When it comes to canning, most people think of sweet jams and jellies, but there’s a whole world of savory preserves waiting to be explored. One such gem is the savory onion marmalade, a versatile condiment that can elevate any dish with its rich, sweet, and tangy flavor profile. In this post, we’ll delve into the art of making and canning savory onion marmalades, exploring different variations and sharing tips for achieving the perfect balance of flavors.

Understanding Savory Onion Marmalades

Savory onion marmalade is a delightful concoction that balances the natural sweetness of onions with a hint of acidity and spices. It’s a perfect accompaniment to cheese platters, grilled meats, sandwiches, and even as a topping for savory tarts. The key to a great onion marmalade lies in the slow caramelization of the onions, which brings out their natural sugars and creates a deep, complex flavor.

Recipe: Classic Savory Onion Marmalade

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900g) yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) olive oil
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup (60g) brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp dried thyme

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are soft and golden brown, about 30-40 minutes.
  3. Add the balsamic vinegar, brown sugar, salt, pepper, and thyme to the caramelized onions.
  4. Continue to cook the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a jam-like consistency, about 20-30 minutes.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat and let the marmalade cool slightly before transferring it to sterilized jars for canning.

Canning Instructions

  1. Fill sterilized jars with the hot marmalade, leaving a 1/4-inch headspace.
  2. Wipe the jar rims, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands until fingertip tight.
  3. Process the jars in a boiling water canner for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove the jars from the canner and let them cool at room temperature. Check the seals before storing the jars in a cool, dark place.

Variations to Explore

Savory onion marmalades offer endless possibilities for customization. Here are a few variations to consider:

  • Red Wine Onion Marmalade: Substitute balsamic vinegar with red wine for a deeper, richer flavor.
  • Spiced Onion Marmalade: Add a hint of heat with chili flakes or smoked paprika for a spicy kick.
  • Herb-Infused Onion Marmalade: Experiment with fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, or bay leaves to add aromatic complexity.

Tips for Perfecting Your Onion Marmalade

  • Patience is Key: The slow caramelization of onions is crucial to developing the marmalade’s flavor, so resist the urge to rush this step.
  • Balancing Sweetness and Acidity: Adjust the amount of sugar and vinegar to suit your taste preferences.
  • Sterilization Matters: Properly sterilizing your jars and lids is essential for safe and long-lasting preservation.

In Conclusion

Savory onion marmalades are a delightful addition to any pantry, offering a versatile and flavorful condiment that can transform even the simplest of dishes. By mastering the art of making and canning onion marmalades, you’ll open up a world of culinary possibilities and add a touch of gourmet flair to your cooking repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, caramelize those onions, and savor the rewards of your homemade savory preserves.
