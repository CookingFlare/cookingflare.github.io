Vegetarian BBQ: Tasty and Healthy Recipes for Grilling


Summer is the perfect time for firing up the grill and enjoying delicious meals outdoors. While barbecues are often associated with meaty dishes, vegetarians can also join in on the fun and create flavorful and nutritious meals on the grill. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering vegetarian BBQ recipes that are sure to satisfy your taste buds and impress your guests.

Grilled Veggie Skewers with Balsamic Glaze

Skewers are a classic BBQ favorite, and they can easily be adapted to a vegetarian-friendly version. Start by choosing an array of colorful vegetables such as bell peppers, zucchini, onions, cherry tomatoes, and mushrooms. Cut them into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto skewers.

To add a burst of flavor, prepare a simple balsamic glaze by combining balsamic vinegar, honey, and a pinch of salt in a small saucepan. Allow the glaze to simmer over low heat until it thickens slightly.

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and brush the skewers with olive oil. Place them on the grill and cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred. During the last few minutes of grilling, brush the skewers with the balsamic glaze for a sweet and tangy finish. Serve hot and enjoy!

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers

Who says burgers are only meant for meat lovers? These grilled portobello mushroom burgers are a delicious and satisfying alternative. Start by removing the stems of the portobello mushrooms and gently brushing them with olive oil. Season with salt, pepper, and your favorite herbs or spices.

Preheat the grill to medium heat and place the mushrooms directly on the grates. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side until they are tender and juicy.

Meanwhile, prepare your burger buns and toppings. Toast the buns on the grill for a few seconds to add a nice crunch. Spread your favorite sauce or condiment on the buns and assemble the burger with lettuce, tomato slices, red onion, and any other toppings of your choice.

Once the mushrooms are cooked, place them on the prepared buns and serve immediately. The juicy and flavorful portobello mushrooms will make you forget all about meat!

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

No BBQ is complete without corn on the cob. Take this classic side dish to the next level by grilling it to perfection and adding a delicious herb butter. Start by peeling back the husks of the corn, leaving them attached at the base. Remove the silk and then pull the husks back up to cover the corn.

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and place the corn directly on the grates. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the corn kernels are tender and slightly charred.

While the corn is grilling, prepare the herb butter by combining softened butter with chopped fresh herbs such as parsley, chives, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Once the corn is cooked, carefully peel back the husks and brush the corn with the herb butter. The heat of the corn will melt the butter, creating a flavorful and indulgent treat. Serve hot and enjoy the smoky-sweet flavors of grilled corn on the cob.

Grilled Fruit Kebabs with Honey-Lime Glaze

Grilling isn’t just for savory dishes! Take advantage of the heat and add a touch of sweetness to your BBQ with grilled fruit kebabs. Choose a variety of fruits such as pineapple, peaches, watermelon, and strawberries. Cut them into chunks and thread them onto skewers.

Preheat the grill to medium heat and lightly brush the fruit skewers with olive oil. Place them on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until the fruits are caramelized and have beautiful grill marks.

While the fruit kebabs are grilling, prepare a simple honey-lime glaze by combining honey, lime juice, and a pinch of cinnamon in a small bowl.

Once the fruit is cooked, brush the kebabs with the honey-lime glaze for a burst of tangy sweetness. Serve the grilled fruit kebabs as a refreshing and healthy dessert option that will leave your guests wanting more.

Conclusion

Vegetarian BBQ doesn’t have to be boring or limited to salads. These tasty and healthy recipes for grilling will prove that vegetarian dishes can be just as delicious and satisfying as their meaty counterparts. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these recipes will surely impress your friends and family at your next BBQ gathering. So fire up the grill, embrace the flavors of summer, and enjoy the mouthwatering delights of vegetarian BBQ. Happy grilling!
