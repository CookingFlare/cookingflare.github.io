Vegan Baking: Creating Sweet Treats Without Dairy or Eggs


Vegan baking has come a long way in recent years, and it’s now easier than ever to create delicious and indulgent sweet treats without dairy or eggs. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a newcomer to the world of vegan cooking, this guide will walk you through the basics of vegan baking and provide you with a range of recipes to get you started.

Understanding Vegan Baking

Before we dive into the recipes, it’s important to understand the basics of vegan baking. Vegan baking is all about replacing traditional dairy and egg products with plant-based alternatives. This can be a bit tricky, as these products often serve multiple functions in a recipe, such as providing moisture, structure, and flavor. However, with a little creativity and experimentation, you can easily create delicious and moist vegan baked goods.

Replacing Dairy and Eggs

So, what can you use to replace dairy and eggs in your baked goods? Here are some popular alternatives:

  • Non-dairy milk: Almond milk, soy milk, coconut milk, and oat milk are all great options for replacing traditional milk in baked goods.
  • Vegetable oil: Canola oil, coconut oil, and olive oil can be used in place of butter or other dairy products.
  • Flaxseed: Ground flaxseed can be used as an egg substitute, providing a nutty flavor and a boost of omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Chia seeds: Like flaxseed, chia seeds can be used as an egg substitute, providing a nutty flavor and a boost of omega-3 fatty acids.
  • Mashed banana: Mashed banana can be used as a binding agent in place of eggs, providing moisture and natural sweetness.
  • Apple sauce: Apple sauce can be used as a binding agent in place of eggs, providing moisture and a subtle sweetness.
  • Aquafaba: The liquid from canned chickpeas, also known as aquafaba, can be used as an egg substitute, providing a light and airy texture.

Tips for Successful Vegan Baking

Here are some tips to help you achieve success with vegan baking:

  • Use high-quality ingredients: The quality of your ingredients can make a big difference in the final result of your baked goods. Choose organic and non-GMO options whenever possible.
  • Don’t overmix: Overmixing can lead to tough or dense baked goods. Mix your ingredients just until they come together in a ball, then stop mixing.
  • Don’t overbake: Vegan baked goods can be prone to drying out if they’re overbaked. Keep an eye on your baked goods and remove them from the oven as soon as they’re done.
  • Experiment with flavors: Vegan baking is all about experimentation and creativity. Don’t be afraid to try new flavors and combinations to create unique and delicious baked goods.

Recipes

Here are some delicious vegan baking recipes to get you started:

Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup non-dairy milk
  • 1/2 cup canola oil
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the non-dairy milk, canola oil, sugar, and brown sugar.
  3. Add the vanilla extract, baking soda, and salt to the wet ingredients. Whisk until well combined.
  4. Add the flour, oats, and chocolate chips to the wet ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
  5. Drop the dough by the spoonful onto the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.

Lemon Poppyseed Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup non-dairy milk
  • 1/2 cup canola oil
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 tablespoon poppyseeds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the non-dairy milk, canola oil, sugar, and lemon juice.
  3. Add the vanilla extract, baking powder, and salt to the wet ingredients. Whisk until well combined.
  4. Add the flour, oats, and poppyseeds to the wet ingredients. Mix until a batter forms.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin tin.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown.

Chocolate Avocado Cake

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1 cup non-dairy milk
  • 1/2 cup canola oil
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a blender or food processor, combine the avocados, non-dairy milk, canola oil, sugar, and cocoa powder. Blend until smooth.
  3. Add the vanilla extract, baking powder, and salt to the wet ingredients. Blend until well combined.
  4. Add the flour to the wet ingredients. Blend until a batter forms.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the cakes are cooked through.

These are just a few examples of the delicious vegan baking recipes you can create. With a little creativity and experimentation, you can make a wide range of sweet treats without dairy or eggs. Happy baking!
