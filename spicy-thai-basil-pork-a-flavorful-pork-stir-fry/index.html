Spicy Thai Basil Pork: A Flavorful Pork Stir-Fry


If you’re looking for a quick and delicious meal that packs a punch of flavor, Spicy Thai Basil Pork, or “Pad Krapow Moo,” is the perfect dish for you. This vibrant stir-fry combines the rich taste of ground pork with the aromatic freshness of Thai basil, a touch of heat from chili peppers, and savory sauces that create a delightful explosion of flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is straightforward and sure to impress.

Ingredients

To make Spicy Thai Basil Pork, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Pork Stir-Fry:

  • 500g (1 lb) ground pork: Use a blend with some fat for better flavor and moisture.
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil: For frying; you can also use peanut or canola oil.
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced: Fresh garlic adds depth to the dish.
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, finely chopped: Adjust according to your heat preference.
  • 1 medium onion, sliced: Yellow or red onion works well.
  • 1 bell pepper (red or green), sliced: For color and sweetness.
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves: Fresh basil is essential for authentic flavor.

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce: Adds umami and saltiness.
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce: For a hint of sweetness and richness.
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce: This contributes to the dish’s savory character.
  • 1 teaspoon sugar: Balances the flavors.

Optional Garnish:

  • Sliced cucumber: Freshness on the side.
  • Steamed jasmine rice: A perfect base for the stir-fry.
  • Lime wedges: A squeeze of lime adds brightness.

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you start cooking, it’s essential to prepare all your ingredients. This ensures a smooth cooking process. Mince the garlic, chop the chilies, slice the onion and bell pepper, and wash the Thai basil leaves. Having everything ready will save you time and help you avoid overcooking the pork.

Step 2: Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Mix well until the sugar is dissolved. This sauce will give your stir-fry its signature flavor, so don’t skip this step! Set the sauce aside for later.

Step 3: Cook the Pork

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and chopped chilies. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add the ground pork to the skillet, breaking it apart with a spatula. Cook until browned and fully cooked, about 5-7 minutes. Ensure that the pork is cooked through, as this is crucial for both flavor and food safety.

Step 4: Add Vegetables

Once the pork is cooked, add the sliced onion and bell pepper to the skillet. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are just tender but still crisp. This step adds a nice crunch and vibrant color to the dish.

Step 5: Incorporate the Sauce

Pour the prepared sauce over the pork and vegetables, stirring well to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. The sauce will thicken slightly, coating the pork and veggies beautifully.

Step 6: Add the Thai Basil

Finally, remove the skillet from heat and stir in the fresh Thai basil leaves. The residual heat will wilt the basil perfectly without losing its vibrant flavor. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary, adding more fish sauce or sugar to balance flavors according to your preference.

Serving Suggestions

Spicy Thai Basil Pork is best served hot, straight from the skillet. Pair it with steamed jasmine rice to soak up the delicious sauce. For an added touch, garnish with sliced cucumber and lime wedges on the side. The freshness of the cucumber and the acidity of the lime enhance the dish’s flavors, making every bite more refreshing.

Tips for Perfecting Your Spicy Thai Basil Pork

  • Choose the Right Pork: Opt for ground pork with a bit of fat for a juicier stir-fry. You can also use minced chicken or turkey if you prefer a leaner option.
  • Adjust the Spice Level: Thai bird’s eye chilies can be quite hot. If you’re sensitive to spice, start with one chili and increase as needed. Alternatively, you can use red pepper flakes for a milder version.
  • Fresh Ingredients Matter: Fresh Thai basil is integral to achieving the authentic taste of this dish. If you can’t find Thai basil, regular basil can be used, but it won’t provide the same flavor profile.
  • Make It Your Own: Feel free to add other vegetables like carrots, snap peas, or mushrooms to the stir-fry. Just ensure they are cut into bite-sized pieces for even cooking.

Storing Leftovers

If you have any leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Spicy Thai Basil Pork can be enjoyed for up to 2-3 days. Reheat in a skillet over medium heat, adding a splash of water or broth to prevent drying out.

Conclusion

Spicy Thai Basil Pork is a quintessential Thai dish that’s packed with flavor and can be made in under 30 minutes. It’s perfect for a weeknight dinner or impressing friends at your next gathering. The combination of savory pork, fresh herbs, and a touch of heat creates a delightful culinary experience that’s hard to resist. Give it a try and bring a taste of Thailand to your kitchen!

Now that you have the recipe for Spicy Thai Basil Pork, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get cooking. Enjoy this flavorful stir-fry as part of your culinary repertoire, and don’t forget to share your experience with friends and family. Happy cooking!
