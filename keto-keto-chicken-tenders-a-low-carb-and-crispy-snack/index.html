Keto Keto Chicken Tenders: A Low-Carb and Crispy Snack


If you’re on a ketogenic diet, you know how challenging it can be to find snacks that are both satisfying and compliant. Enter the Keto Chicken Tenders—a delightfully crispy, low-carb treat that will satisfy your cravings without derailing your dietary goals. These chicken tenders are not only easy to make but also incredibly versatile, allowing you to enjoy them in various ways.

Why Choose Keto Chicken Tenders?

The ketogenic diet emphasizes high-fat, low-carb meals, and these chicken tenders fit perfectly into that framework. Traditional breading methods use flour or breadcrumbs, which can quickly spike your carb intake. However, with a few simple substitutions, you can enjoy crispy, flavorful chicken tenders that won’t compromise your keto lifestyle.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To whip up these delicious keto chicken tenders, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken Tenders:

  • 1 pound of chicken breast: Cut into strips for easy handling.
  • 1 cup almond flour: A low-carb alternative to regular flour.
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese: Adds flavor and crunch.
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder: For a savory kick.
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder: Enhances the overall taste.
  • 1 teaspoon paprika: Adds a hint of smokiness.
  • Salt and pepper: Season to taste.

For the Egg Wash:

  • 2 large eggs: Beaten.
  • 1 tablespoon heavy cream: Helps the breading stick.

For Cooking:

  • Oil for frying (such as avocado oil or coconut oil): High smoke points are essential for frying.

The Preparation Process

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Begin by cutting your chicken breasts into strips. Aim for uniform sizes to ensure even cooking. Pat the chicken dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture, which helps the breading adhere better.

Step 2: Create the Breading Station

Set up a breading station with three shallow bowls:

  1. Almond flour, Parmesan cheese, and spices combined in one.
  2. Beaten eggs and heavy cream in the second.
  3. A plate or tray for the breaded chicken in the third.

Step 3: Breading the Chicken

  1. Dip each chicken strip into the egg wash, making sure it’s fully coated.
  2. Then, transfer it to the almond flour mixture, pressing gently to ensure the coating sticks well.
  3. Place the breaded chicken tenders on the plate or tray, and repeat until all chicken strips are coated.

Cooking the Chicken Tenders

Step 4: Frying

  1. In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of oil over medium-high heat. Ensure the oil is hot enough by testing it with a small piece of chicken; it should sizzle upon contact.
  2. Carefully add the breaded chicken tenders to the skillet in a single layer, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. You may need to do this in batches.
  3. Fry for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F).

Step 5: Draining

Once cooked, transfer the chicken tenders to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. This step helps maintain their crispy texture.

Serving Suggestions

Keto chicken tenders are delicious on their own, but you can elevate your snack with various dipping sauces. Here are some keto-friendly options:

  • Ranch Dressing: A classic choice that pairs perfectly with crispy chicken.
  • Buffalo Sauce: For those who love a spicy kick, toss your chicken tenders in buffalo sauce for an extra flavor boost.
  • Garlic Aioli: A creamy garlic dip that complements the crunchiness of the tenders.

Nutritional Benefits

These keto chicken tenders are not just a tasty snack; they also provide nutritional benefits that align with your dietary goals:

  • Low in Carbohydrates: With almond flour as the base, these tenders are significantly lower in carbs compared to traditional breaded chicken.
  • High in Protein: Chicken breast is a lean source of protein, which is essential for muscle maintenance and overall health.
  • Healthy Fats: Cooking in healthy oils like avocado oil adds beneficial fats to your diet.

Variations to Try

Feel free to get creative with your keto chicken tenders! Here are some variations you can experiment with:

Spicy Chicken Tenders

Add cayenne pepper or chili powder to the almond flour mixture for an extra kick.

Italian Herb Chicken Tenders

Incorporate dried Italian herbs such as oregano and basil into the breading for a Mediterranean twist.

Garlic Parmesan Chicken Tenders

Mix extra grated Parmesan cheese and minced garlic into the almond flour for an intense flavor.

Storing Leftovers

If you have any leftover chicken tenders, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. They’re best enjoyed within 3 days. To reheat, place them in an air fryer for a few minutes to regain their crispy texture or reheat them in a skillet over medium heat.

Conclusion

Keto chicken tenders are the ultimate low-carb snack that satisfies your cravings without compromising your diet. With their crispy exterior and juicy interior, they are perfect for meal prep, parties, or a quick snack. The best part is that with a few simple ingredients and steps, you can enjoy a delicious treat that aligns with your health goals.

The next time you’re looking for a satisfying snack, remember this recipe for keto chicken tenders. Not only are they a hit with keto dieters, but they also make a great option for anyone looking to enjoy a healthier version of a classic favorite. So grab your ingredients, fire up the skillet, and enjoy these crispy, flavorful chicken tenders today!
