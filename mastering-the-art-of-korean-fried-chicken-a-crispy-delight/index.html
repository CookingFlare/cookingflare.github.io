Mastering the Art of Korean Fried Chicken: A Crispy Delight


Korean fried chicken, or “KFC” as it’s lovingly referred to in Korea, has taken the culinary world by storm. What makes this crispy delight stand out from regular fried chicken is the unique method of preparation, the addictive flavor, and the unbeatable crunch. Whether it’s paired with beer in a casual setting or served as the centerpiece of a festive meal, Korean fried chicken has something for everyone.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps of mastering the art of Korean fried chicken, from the crispy batter to the mouthwatering glaze that defines this dish. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to create a dish that will leave everyone begging for more!

The Secret Behind the Crunch: Double Frying

One of the key elements that sets Korean fried chicken apart from traditional fried chicken is the method of double frying. This process creates the signature ultra-crispy texture that’s both light and airy, while also ensuring that the chicken remains juicy and tender on the inside.

Why Double Frying?

Double frying allows the chicken to cook evenly without becoming greasy or soggy. When the chicken is fried the first time, it’s cooked through but doesn’t yet develop the desired crispiness. The second fry at a higher temperature helps create that golden, crunchy exterior while keeping the interior moist and flavorful.

How to Double Fry Korean Fried Chicken:

  1. Prepare the chicken: Start by cutting the chicken into small, bite-sized pieces, such as wings or drumsticks. This ensures that the chicken cooks evenly and crisps up perfectly.

  2. First fry: Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to about 330°F (165°C). Fry the chicken in batches, cooking for about 6-8 minutes until the pieces are cooked through but not yet golden brown. The goal here is to cook the chicken without over-browning it.

  3. Rest the chicken: After the first fry, remove the chicken from the oil and let it rest for 10-15 minutes. This allows the chicken to cool down slightly and the juices to settle, which will prevent the second frying from making the chicken greasy.

  4. Second fry: Heat the oil to 375°F (190°C) and fry the chicken again for 3-5 minutes until it reaches a deep golden brown color. This is where the magic happens, giving the chicken that satisfying crispiness.

  5. Drain the chicken: Once the chicken is golden and crispy, remove it from the oil and let it drain on a wire rack or paper towels. This will prevent it from becoming soggy.

The Flavorful Glaze: The Key to Korean Fried Chicken

While the crispy texture of Korean fried chicken is a standout feature, the glaze is what truly elevates the dish. Korean fried chicken is often coated in a sweet, spicy, and tangy sauce that sticks to the crispy exterior, giving it a burst of flavor in every bite.

There are many variations of the glaze, but the most popular ones combine gochujang (Korean red chili paste), honey, soy sauce, garlic, and ginger to create a balanced flavor profile that’s both savory and sweet. Here’s how to make your own Korean fried chicken glaze.

Ingredients for the Glaze:

  • 3 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional for extra sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • 2-3 green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Directions for the Glaze:

  1. Combine the ingredients: In a small saucepan, combine the gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, and brown sugar (if using). Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.

  2. Simmer: Let the sauce simmer for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and becomes syrupy. The glaze should be smooth and glossy.

  3. Taste and adjust: Taste the glaze and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you like it spicier, add more gochujang. If you prefer it sweeter, add a little more honey or sugar.

  4. Coat the chicken: Once your chicken has finished double frying and is still hot, toss the chicken pieces in the glaze, ensuring each piece is coated generously. The heat from the chicken will help the glaze adhere and give the dish a glossy, sticky finish.

  5. Garnish: After coating the chicken in the glaze, sprinkle sesame seeds and chopped green onions on top for extra flavor and a touch of freshness.

The Best Side Dishes for Korean Fried Chicken

Korean fried chicken isn’t just a meal on its own; it’s often served with a variety of side dishes that complement its rich and spicy flavor. Here are a few popular Korean side dishes that pair perfectly with your crispy chicken.

1. Kimchi:

Kimchi, the quintessential Korean side dish, is made from fermented vegetables (usually cabbage or radish) and flavored with chili paste, garlic, and ginger. The tangy and spicy kick of kimchi contrasts beautifully with the richness of fried chicken.

2. Pickled Radish (Chpickleda):

In Korea, fried chicken is often served with a side of sweet pickled radish. The crunchy, slightly sweet, and tangy pickled radish helps cleanse the palate between bites of crispy chicken.

3. Steamed Rice:

Steamed rice is a simple, neutral side that balances out the bold flavors of the fried chicken. It also helps to soak up the extra sauce, making every bite more satisfying.

4. Korean Salad (Yangnyeom Chwi):

A fresh Korean-style salad, often made with napa cabbage, cucumber, and a light dressing, offers a refreshing contrast to the heat of the chicken and the richness of the glaze.

5. Beer (Chimaek):

In Korea, the combination of fried chicken and beer is known as “chimaek,” and it’s a beloved pairing. The light, refreshing qualities of beer help to cut through the richness of the fried chicken, making it the perfect beverage to enjoy with your meal.

Tips for Perfect Korean Fried Chicken

While the process of making Korean fried chicken may seem complicated at first, with a little practice, you’ll become an expert in no time. Here are a few helpful tips to ensure that your fried chicken turns out perfectly every time.

1. Use Cornstarch:

In addition to flour, cornstarch plays a crucial role in achieving the ultra-crispy texture. The cornstarch helps absorb moisture, making the batter crispier during the double frying process.

2. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan:

When frying, be sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the chicken in batches to ensure that each piece has enough space to cook evenly and become crispy. Overcrowding the pan can result in soggy chicken.

3. Use the Right Oil:

For the best results, use oil with a high smoke point, such as vegetable oil or canola oil. These oils can withstand the high frying temperatures without burning, ensuring a crisp exterior.

4. Let the Chicken Rest Between Frying:

Allowing the chicken to rest after the first fry ensures that it cools down slightly before the second fry. This helps prevent oil splatter and ensures that the second fry delivers the perfect crunch.

5. Serve Immediately:

Korean fried chicken is best enjoyed fresh out of the fryer when it’s at its crispiest. Serve it right away to ensure the chicken stays crunchy and doesn’t lose its texture.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of Korean fried chicken takes a little practice, but the results are well worth the effort. With its irresistible crispy texture, flavorful glaze, and mouthwatering taste, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you’ll be able to recreate this Korean comfort food at home and share it with your friends and family.

So, grab your ingredients, fire up the fryer, and prepare to enjoy a plate of crispy, flavorful, and utterly addictive Korean fried chicken. Whether you’re having it as a snack, a main dish, or a party favorite, this Korean fried chicken recipe will surely impress and satisfy all your cravings. Happy cooking!
