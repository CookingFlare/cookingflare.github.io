Savory Beef and Bean Chili: Perfect for Chilly Nights


Are you looking for a hearty and comforting meal to warm you up on those chilly nights? Look no further than this delicious and savory Beef and Bean Chili recipe. Packed with flavor, protein, and wholesome ingredients, this chili is the perfect dish to satisfy your cravings and keep you cozy. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds of beef chuck, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced (optional for extra heat)
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 ounces) of kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 ounces) of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheddar cheese, sour cream, chopped cilantro, diced avocado

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion, minced garlic, diced red and green bell peppers, and minced jalapeno pepper (if using). Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the browned beef back to the pot, along with the diced tomatoes (including their juices), kidney beans, black beans, beef broth, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together to combine.

  4. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally. The longer you simmer the chili, the more the flavors will develop.

  5. After simmering, taste and adjust the seasoning according to your preference. If you like it spicier, add more chili powder or a dash of hot sauce. If it needs more salt, sprinkle some in and stir.

  6. Serve the savory beef and bean chili hot, garnished with shredded cheddar cheese, a dollop of sour cream, chopped cilantro, and diced avocado, if desired. Pair it with some crusty bread or cornbread for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a leaner option, you can use ground beef instead of beef chuck. Simply brown the ground beef in the pot before adding the vegetables and continue with the recipe as instructed.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a tablespoon of cocoa powder or a square of dark chocolate to the chili while it simmers. The chocolate will melt and add richness to the dish.

  • If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. The flavors will meld together even more overnight, making the chili taste even better the next day.

  • Feel free to customize the toppings to your liking. You can add sliced jalapenos, diced red onions, or even a squeeze of lime for a tangy twist.

  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved depending on the number of servings you need. Adjust the ingredients accordingly and enjoy the leftovers or freeze them for later.

Now that you have this amazing Savory Beef and Bean Chili recipe in your repertoire, you’ll be ready to tackle those chilly nights with a bowl of warmth and deliciousness. So gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and let the aroma fill your kitchen. Enjoy!
