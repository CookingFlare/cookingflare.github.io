Delicious Vegan and Nut-Free Dinners for Every Night of the Week


Eating vegan and nut-free doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor, creativity, or variety at the dinner table. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or feeding a family, there are plenty of ways to create satisfying, nutritious meals that are both plant-based and nut-free. These vegan and nut-free dinner ideas are perfect for every night of the week, ensuring you can enjoy a different, exciting dish each day without worrying about common allergens.

Monday: Hearty Chickpea and Spinach Curry

Start your week with a warm, hearty chickpea and spinach curry. This dish is packed with protein and iron, thanks to the chickpeas and spinach. The combination of spices creates a rich, comforting flavor, perfect for a Monday night dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 large bunch of fresh spinach
  • 1 can of coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked rice or naan bread to serve

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until soft and translucent.
  2. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and coriander, and cook for 1-2 minutes to release the flavors.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, coconut milk, and chickpeas to the pot. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Stir in the fresh spinach and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve over rice or with naan, garnished with fresh cilantro.

This dish is not only satisfying but also full of plant-based protein and iron, making it a perfect choice for a vegan dinner.

Tuesday: Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Tacos are a fun and versatile meal that can be easily made vegan and nut-free. These sweet potato and black bean tacos are bursting with flavor and nutrients, perfect for a quick and easy dinner on a busy Tuesday.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Salsa or hot sauce, optional

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the diced sweet potatoes with olive oil, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them out on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  3. Roast the sweet potatoes for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are tender and slightly crispy.
  4. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, warm the black beans in a small pot over low heat.
  5. Heat the corn tortillas in a dry skillet for 1-2 minutes on each side until warm.
  6. Assemble the tacos by placing roasted sweet potatoes and black beans on each tortilla.
  7. Top with sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime juice, and salsa or hot sauce if desired.

These tacos are a flavorful and satisfying combination of sweetness from the sweet potatoes and earthiness from the black beans. Plus, they’re easy to make and customizable with your favorite toppings.

Wednesday: Vegan Lentil Bolognese

A classic Italian dish gets a plant-based twist with this vegan lentil bolognese. Lentils are an excellent source of protein and fiber, making this dish both filling and nutritious. Pair it with your favorite pasta for a wholesome, nut-free dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Your favorite pasta (gluten-free if necessary)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the lentils in a large pot of water for about 25-30 minutes, or until tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery, and sauté until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes to thicken and develop flavor.
  4. Add the cooked lentils to the sauce and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the lentils to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  5. Serve over your favorite pasta, garnished with fresh parsley.

This lentil bolognese is a rich, savory dish that will satisfy your pasta cravings without any animal products. The lentils provide a great texture that mimics ground meat, making it a perfect vegan alternative.

Thursday: Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

Stuffed bell peppers are a great way to incorporate a variety of vegetables into your dinner. This quinoa-stuffed bell pepper recipe is both satisfying and packed with flavor. Quinoa is a complete protein, making it an excellent choice for vegan meals.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lime wedges for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the quinoa according to the package instructions. Once cooked, fluff it with a fork.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stuff each bell pepper with the quinoa mixture, pressing down gently to pack the filling.
  5. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and drizzle with olive oil.
  6. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, until the peppers are tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice before serving.

These quinoa-stuffed bell peppers are a colorful and nutritious meal that’s perfect for any night of the week. The combination of quinoa, black beans, and corn makes them filling and full of plant-based goodness.

Friday: Vegan Mushroom Stroganoff

Rich, creamy, and comforting, this vegan mushroom stroganoff is a wonderful way to end your workweek. Made with mushrooms and a dairy-free sour cream alternative, it’s a vegan version of the classic dish that everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cups mushrooms, sliced (button, cremini, or a mix)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon flour (gluten-free if needed)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup vegan sour cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Cooked pasta or rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the mushrooms and garlic, and sauté until the mushrooms release their moisture and start to brown, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the paprika and flour, cooking for 1-2 minutes to form a roux.
  4. Gradually add the vegetable broth, stirring to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5-7 minutes, until the sauce thickens.
  5. Stir in the vegan sour cream, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the mushroom stroganoff over cooked pasta or rice, and garnish with fresh parsley.

This dish is creamy and rich without the use of dairy, making it a perfect vegan option for a cozy Friday night.

Saturday: Vegan BBQ Cauliflower Wings

Perfect for a weekend dinner or as a fun appetizer, these vegan BBQ cauliflower wings are crispy, tangy, and full of flavor. Paired with a side of coleslaw, they make for an exciting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup flour (gluten-free if needed)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 cup unsweetened plant-based milk
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • Olive oil spray for baking

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, garlic powder, paprika, and onion powder. Slowly add the plant-based milk, whisking until smooth.
  3. Dip each cauliflower floret into the batter, then coat it in breadcrumbs.
  4. Arrange the coated cauliflower on a baking sheet and spray with olive oil.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the cauliflower is crispy and golden.
  6. Toss the baked cauliflower wings in BBQ sauce and return them to the oven for an additional 5 minutes to heat the sauce.
  7. Serve with a side of coleslaw and enjoy!

These BBQ cauliflower wings are a delicious, satisfying option for your weekend dinner. They’re crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, making them a perfect nut-free vegan choice for any occasion.

Sunday: Roasted Vegetable Buddha Bowl

Wrap up your week with a nutrient-packed, vibrant Buddha bowl filled with roasted vegetables, grains, and a flavorful dressing. This meal is highly customizable, allowing you to use whatever vegetables or grains you have on hand.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa or brown rice
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Water to thin, as needed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the broccoli, sweet potato, bell pepper, and zucchini in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread them on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the vegetables for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until tender and golden.
  4. While the vegetables are roasting, cook the quinoa or rice according to package instructions.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, maple syrup, and water to create a smooth dressing.
  6. To assemble the Buddha bowls, divide the grains into bowls, top with roasted vegetables, and drizzle with tahini dressing.

This Buddha bowl is a wholesome, nourishing dinner that provides a great balance of protein, fiber, and healthy fats. It’s an easy way to enjoy a variety of roasted vegetables while keeping things simple and flavorful.

These seven delicious vegan and nut-free dinner ideas are perfect for every night of the week, offering a variety of flavors, textures, and ingredients. From hearty curries to light Buddha bowls, these recipes ensure that eating plant-based and nut-free doesn’t mean compromising on taste or nutrition. Enjoy exploring these dishes with your family and friends, and feel confident knowing you’re serving meals that are both healthy and delicious!
