Mini Lemon Meringue Pie Cakes: Light, Zesty, and Poetic


Embark on a culinary adventure with these delectable Mini Lemon Meringue Pie Cakes. A perfect balance of tangy and sweet flavors, they are as delightful to the taste buds as they are to the eye. Indulge in the delightful dance of flavors provided by these petite creations, with a gossamer crust and billowing meringue.

Ingredients

For the Shortcrust Pastry:

  • 1 and 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons ice water
  • 1 large egg yolk

For the Lemon Curd Filling:

  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

For the Meringue:

  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1/8 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions

Begin by making the Shortcrust Pastry: In a food processor, pulse together the flour, powdered sugar, and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and process until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Mix the ice water and egg yolk in a separate bowl, then pour into the food processor. Pulse until the dough begins to cling together. Press the dough into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill for at least 30 minutes or up to two days. Preheat the oven to 350°F.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough and use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out 18 circles to fit a muffin tin. Carefully press the circles into each cup, pricking the bottom with a fork. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until lightly golden. Allow the crusts to cool in the pan.

While the crusts bake, make the Lemon Curd Filling: In a small saucepan, whisk together the lemon juice, lemon zest, egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch. Cook over medium heat, constantly stirring, until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and add the butter, whisking until melted. Pass the mixture through a sieve and cool to room temperature.

When both the crusts and lemon curd are cool, prepare the Meringue: Beat the egg whites with the cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add the sugar, beating continuously until stiff, glossy peaks form. Gently fold in the vanilla extract.

Top each lemon curd-filled crust with a generous dollop of meringue, creating a swirling or artistic design as desired. Return the pies to the oven for an additional 8-12 minutes or until the meringue is lightly toasted. Cool to room temperature before serving. Enjoy the symphony of tangy lemon, indulgent buttery crust, and blushing peaks of meringue in these Mini Lemon Meringue Pie Cakes.

Cherish every bite of these luscious confections as you enjoy the harmonious balance of their sweet, tart flavors and consider experimenting with different types of lemons or even other citrus fruits to create variations of your own. These Mini Lemon Meringue Pie Cakes are the embodiment of light, zesty, and poetic, an exquisite addition to any tea party or dessert spread.
