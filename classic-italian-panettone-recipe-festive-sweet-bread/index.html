Classic Italian Panettone Recipe - Festive Sweet Bread


Panettone is a classic Italian sweet bread that is traditionally enjoyed during the festive season. With its light, fluffy texture and rich flavors, it’s no wonder that panettone has become a beloved treat around the world. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making your very own homemade panettone from scratch. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 250ml lukewarm milk
  • 100g unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 100g mixed dried fruits (such as raisins, candied orange peel, and candied lemon peel)
  • 50g chopped almonds
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in the lukewarm milk and let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, softened butter, eggs, vanilla extract, orange zest, and lemon zest. Mix well until a sticky dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook for this step.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 2 hours or until it doubles in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Add the dried fruits and chopped almonds to the dough and knead until they are evenly distributed.

  6. Shape the dough into a round ball and place it in a panettone mold or a tall, cylindrical cake pan lined with parchment paper. Cover the mold with a kitchen towel and let the dough rise for another 1-2 hours until it reaches the top of the mold.

  7. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Bake the panettone for 40-45 minutes or until it turns golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Once baked, remove the panettone from the oven and let it cool completely in the mold before transferring it to a wire rack.

  9. To serve, slice the panettone into thick slices and enjoy it with a cup of hot coffee or tea. You can also spread some butter or Nutella on the slices for an extra indulgent treat.

Tips and Variations

  • To give your panettone an extra festive touch, you can add a sprinkle of powdered sugar on top before serving.

  • If you prefer a more traditional panettone, you can omit the dried fruits and almonds and simply enjoy the bread as is.

  • Panettone can be stored in an airtight container for up to 1 week. You can also freeze it for longer storage.

  • Feel free to get creative with your choice of dried fruits and nuts. You can use cranberries, apricots, pistachios, or any other combination that you prefer.

Now that you have the recipe for making a classic Italian panettone, it’s time to roll up your sleeves and get baking! This festive sweet bread is sure to impress your family and friends, and it’s perfect for sharing during the holiday season. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and enjoy the delightful flavors of homemade panettone. Buon appetito!
