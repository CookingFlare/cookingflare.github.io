Mexican Tortilla Soup: A Spicy Fiesta in a Bowl


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine and crave a dish that is both comforting and full of flavor, then look no further than Mexican Tortilla Soup. This spicy and hearty soup is a fiesta in a bowl, packed with traditional ingredients that will transport you straight to the streets of Mexico. With its vibrant colors, rich aromas, and bold flavors, this soup is sure to delight your taste buds and warm your soul.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this delicious Mexican Tortilla Soup:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Tortilla chips, crushed

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, jalapeño pepper, and red bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  2. Stir in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. Add the shredded chicken, corn kernels, and black beans to the pot. Simmer for another 10 minutes until the soup is heated through.

  4. Remove the soup from heat and stir in the lime juice. Taste and adjust the seasonings if needed.

  5. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro, sliced avocado, and crushed tortilla chips. The cilantro adds a refreshing herbal note, while the avocado provides a creamy texture. The crushed tortilla chips add a satisfying crunch to each spoonful.

  6. Serve the Mexican Tortilla Soup hot and enjoy the explosion of flavors with every bite. The combination of the spicy broth, tender chicken, sweet corn, and earthy black beans will leave you craving for more.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, simply omit the chicken and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth. You can also add extra vegetables like zucchini or carrots for added texture and flavor.
  • If you prefer a milder soup, reduce the amount of jalapeño pepper or remove the seeds and membranes before dicing.
  • Feel free to customize your toppings based on your preferences. Sliced radishes, diced tomatoes, or a dollop of sour cream are all great options to enhance the flavor and presentation of the soup.

Final Thoughts

Mexican Tortilla Soup is a versatile and comforting dish that can be enjoyed any time of the year. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking for a satisfying weeknight meal, this soup is sure to impress. Its bold flavors and colorful presentation make it a feast for both the eyes and the taste buds. So grab a spoon, dive into a bowl of this spicy fiesta, and let the flavors of Mexico transport you to a culinary adventure like no other.
