Delicious Baked Salmon Recipes for Dinner


When it comes to dinner, few options are as versatile, nutritious, and downright delicious as baked salmon. This heart-healthy fish offers a rich flavor that pairs beautifully with a wide range of herbs, spices, and side dishes. Whether you’re looking for a simple weeknight meal or something to impress guests, baked salmon recipes can be tailored to suit any occasion. In this comprehensive guide, you’ll find an array of creative, easy-to-follow baked salmon recipes that will elevate your dinner routine.

Why Choose Baked Salmon?

Salmon is packed with beneficial nutrients such as omega-3 fatty acids, high-quality protein, vitamins (especially D and B12), and minerals like selenium and potassium. Baking is one of the best cooking methods for salmon because it preserves moisture, intensifies flavor, and requires minimal effort.

Baking doesn’t demand constant attention like pan-searing or grilling, and it allows you to infuse the fish with a variety of complementary seasonings and sauces. Plus, it’s a relatively quick cooking method that fits well into busy schedules.

Tips for Baking Perfect Salmon Every Time

Before diving into the recipes, here are some handy pointers:

  • Choose the Right Cut: Fillets and steaks both work well. Skin-on pieces help retain moisture and add texture.
  • Don’t Overcook: Salmon is done when it flakes easily with a fork but is still moist inside. Usually, 12-15 minutes at 375°F (190°C) is sufficient for fillets.
  • Preheat the Oven: This ensures even cooking and a nicely seared crust.
  • Use a Baking Sheet or Dish: Line it with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup.
  • Season Generously: Salt and pepper are essentials, but fresh herbs, citrus zest, garlic, and spices will elevate the profile.
  • Add Moisture: Whether through a marinade, a brush of olive oil, or a sauce, moisture keeps salmon from drying out.

Classic Lemon and Herb Baked Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each), skin-on
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place salmon fillets skin-side down on a lined baking sheet.
  3. In a small bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, thyme, dill, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the mixture over the salmon generously.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes, depending on thickness, until salmon flakes easily.
  6. Serve with roasted vegetables or a light green salad.

Why This Works

The bright citrus flavor balances the richness of the salmon, while the herbs add freshness without overpowering the delicate fish. Garlic introduces a subtle warmth that lingers pleasantly.

Maple-Glazed Baked Salmon with Mustard

For those who love a combination of sweet and tangy flavors, this maple-glazed salmon hits the spot.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix maple syrup, Dijon mustard, soy sauce, garlic, and ginger in a bowl.
  2. Place salmon in a baking dish and pour the glaze over, coating each fillet.
  3. Let marinate for at least 15 minutes, or up to an hour in the fridge.
  4. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Bake salmon uncovered for 12-15 minutes.
  6. Optional: for a caramelized finish, broil for the last 2 minutes, watching closely to avoid burning.
  7. Serve with steamed rice and sautéed greens.

Flavor Profile

The maple syrup adds sweetness that perfectly counters the tang of the mustard and the umami depth of the soy sauce, while ginger and garlic bring warmth and zest.

Mediterranean-Style Baked Salmon with Tomatoes and Olives

This recipe brings wonderful Mediterranean flavors to your dinner table, utilizing ingredients like sun-dried tomatoes, olives, and fresh herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup pitted Kalamata olives, halved
  • ¼ cup sun-dried tomatoes (packed in oil), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a bowl, toss tomatoes, olives, sun-dried tomatoes, olive oil, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place salmon fillets on a baking dish and spoon Mediterranean topping over each piece.
  4. Bake for 15 minutes or until salmon is cooked through.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and lemon wedges before serving.

Why You’ll Love It

The salty olives, sweet tomatoes, and herbs create a vibrant sauce that complements the fatty salmon perfectly, providing a rustic and aromatic finish.

Asian-Inspired Soy and Sesame Baked Salmon

For those craving Asian flavors, this recipe delivers savory, nutty, and slightly sweet notes that awaken the palate.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Pour the marinade over salmon in a shallow dish and refrigerate for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Place salmon in a baking tray and bake for 12-15 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle sesame seeds during the last 5 minutes of baking.
  6. Top with sliced green onions once out of the oven.
  7. Pair with jasmine rice or steamed bok choy.

Taste Highlights

This glaze offers savory umami depth with a touch of sweetness and a hint of acidity, while sesame oil adds a rich nuttiness that complements salmon beautifully.

Spicy Cajun Baked Salmon with Lime

If you enjoy bold, spicy flavors, this Cajun-style baked salmon brings heat and zest to your dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning blend
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 lime, zested and juiced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, cayenne, lime zest, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the spice mixture evenly over salmon fillets.
  4. Place salmon on a lined baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes.
  5. Drizzle lime juice over salmon before serving.
  6. Serve alongside cornbread or a chilled avocado salad.

What Makes It Special

The Cajun spices add warmth and smokiness, enhanced by the bright lime zest and juice which lighten the boldness and balance the heat.

Mediterranean Chickpea and Salmon Bake

This one-dish meal combines protein-packed chickpeas with tender salmon for a satisfying, wholesome dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small red onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, toss chickpeas, tomatoes, onion, garlic, olive oil, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread chickpea mixture evenly on a baking dish.
  4. Nestle salmon fillets on top.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes until the salmon is cooked through and chickpeas are lightly browned.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

Balanced Flavor

The earthy roasted chickpeas and spices add texture and complement the salmon’s mild richness for a well-rounded, satisfying dish.

Sweet and Smoky Paprika-Honey Salmon

A fusion of smoky and sweet flavors makes this baked salmon recipe stand out.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, honey, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the mixture generously over salmon fillets.
  4. Bake for 12-15 minutes until caramelized and flaky.
  5. Serve with roasted potatoes and steamed asparagus.

Flavor Notes

Sweet honey contrasts with smoked paprika’s warmth and slight bitterness, creating a unique glaze that seals in moisture for tender fish.

Baked Salmon in Foil with Vegetables

This all-in-one meal wraps salmon with fresh vegetables and herbs in foil packets for juicy, flavorful results.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Prepare 4 large sheets of aluminum foil.
  3. Divide zucchini, bell pepper, onion, and garlic evenly among foil sheets.
  4. Place one salmon fillet over each vegetable pile.
  5. Drizzle olive oil over everything.
  6. Top each fillet with lemon slices and thyme sprigs.
  7. Fold foil to seal packets completely.
  8. Bake for 18-20 minutes until salmon is cooked through.
  9. Serve straight from the foil for rustic presentation.

Benefits of Foil Baking

Steaming salmon and vegetables in foil traps moisture, infuses flavors, and makes cleanup a breeze.

Final Thoughts: Bringing Baked Salmon to Your Table

Baked salmon is incredibly adaptable and lends itself to endless variations. Whether you crave bold spices, sweet glazes, zesty citrus, or Mediterranean freshness, there’s a baked salmon recipe here for every palate. Serve with simple sides like roasted vegetables, salads, rice, or quinoa to round out your meal.

Experimenting with different herbs, spices, and sauces keeps dinner exciting while ensuring a nutritious protein source is always on the menu. With these easy recipes, you’re just a few steps away from a restaurant-quality, wholesome salmon dinner right at home.

Get creative, trust your taste buds, and enjoy every flavorful bite of baked salmon tonight!

What to Pair with Baked Salmon

  • Vegetables: Roasted broccoli, asparagus, green beans, or Brussels sprouts.
  • Grains: Quinoa, wild rice, or couscous infused with herbs.
  • Salads: Mixed greens with citrus vinaigrette, cucumber salad, or Mediterranean-style tabbouleh.
  • Sauces: Tzatziki, garlic yogurt sauce, honey mustard, or chimichurri.

Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or new to culinary adventures, these baked salmon recipes offer approachable ways to introduce healthy, flavorful fish dinners that the whole family will enjoy. Happy cooking!
