International Comfort Food Recipes for College Students


As a college student, finding delicious and comforting meals that remind you of home can be a challenge, especially when you’re living far away or studying abroad. However, fear not, because we’ve got you covered with some international comfort food recipes that are not only easy to make but also budget-friendly. These recipes will transport your taste buds around the world and bring a taste of home to your college kitchen. Let’s dive into the culinary journey of international comfort food!

Italian Carbonara Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz (225g) spaghetti
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup (100g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 oz (115g) pancetta or bacon, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper in a bowl until well combined.
  3. In a skillet, cook the diced pancetta or bacon over medium heat until crispy. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and return it to the pot. Immediately add the egg and cheese mixture to the hot pasta and toss quickly to coat the spaghetti evenly.
  5. Add the cooked pancetta and garlic to the pasta, tossing until everything is well combined.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and an extra sprinkle of Parmesan cheese before serving.

Japanese Chicken Katsu Curry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup (60g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup (100g) panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 cups (480ml) chicken broth
  • 2-3 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and diced
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound them to an even thickness. Season the chicken with salt and black pepper.
  2. Dredge the seasoned chicken breasts in flour, dip them in beaten eggs, and then coat them with panko breadcrumbs, pressing the breadcrumbs gently to adhere.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry the breaded chicken breasts for 5-6 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through. Transfer to a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  4. In a separate pot, combine the chicken broth and curry powder. Add the diced potatoes and carrots, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the curry thickens.
  5. Slice the chicken katsu and serve it with the curry sauce over cooked rice.

Mexican Beef Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup (100g) shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Sour cream and salsa for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the ground beef with diced onions and bell pepper until the beef is browned and the vegetables are tender. Season with chili powder, cumin, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Place a flour tortilla on a flat surface and sprinkle half of it with shredded cheddar cheese. Spoon the cooked beef mixture over the cheese and fold the tortilla in half.
  3. Heat a clean skillet over medium heat and place the folded tortilla in the skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until the tortilla is golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  4. Repeat the process with the remaining tortillas and beef mixture.
  5. Cut the quesadillas into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa for dipping.

These international comfort food recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings and bring a taste of home to your college kitchen. Whether it’s the creamy Italian carbonara, the comforting Japanese chicken katsu curry, or the flavorful Mexican beef quesadillas, these dishes are perfect for a cozy night in or a gathering with friends. So, put on your apron and get ready to embark on a global culinary adventure right in your dorm or apartment kitchen. Happy cooking!
