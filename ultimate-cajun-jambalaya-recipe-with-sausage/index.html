Ultimate Cajun Jambalaya Recipe with Sausage


Jambalaya is a rich, flavorful, and hearty dish that is a staple in Cajun and Creole cuisine, bringing the perfect balance of spice, meat, and rice. It’s one of those meals that embodies the soul of New Orleans and Louisiana’s deep culinary roots. The beauty of jambalaya lies in its versatility—though the ingredients may vary slightly, the essence of this dish remains the same. Today, we’re diving into an ultimate jambalaya recipe that features sausage, a classic ingredient in many variations of this dish. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe will guide you step by step to create a comforting and satisfying meal that will leave everyone asking for more.

Ingredients for Cajun Jambalaya with Sausage

Before we jump into the cooking instructions, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients you will need for the ultimate Cajun jambalaya:

  • 1 lb Andouille sausage (or smoked sausage, sliced into ¼-inch rounds)
  • 1 lb chicken thighs (boneless and skinless, cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper (green or red), diced
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 1 ½ cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth (preferably low-sodium)
  • 2 tsp Cajun seasoning (or to taste)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Hot sauce (optional, for an extra kick)

Garnish (optional):

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Green onions, chopped
  • Lemon wedges

How to Make the Ultimate Cajun Jambalaya with Sausage

Now that you have your ingredients ready, it’s time to get cooking. The process is straightforward, and the results are nothing short of spectacular. Follow these steps, and you’ll be able to create a pot of hearty, flavorful jambalaya that will impress your family and friends.

Step 1: Brown the Sausage and Chicken

Start by heating the vegetable oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the Andouille sausage and cook until it’s nicely browned and crisp on the edges. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes. Once browned, remove the sausage from the pot and set it aside.

Next, add the chicken thighs to the same pot. Cook them for about 5 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and cooked through. After the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside with the sausage.

Step 2: Sauté Vegetables

In the same pot, add the butter and let it melt. Once melted, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery. These three vegetables, known as the “holy trinity” of Cajun cooking, will form the aromatic base for your jambalaya. Sauté the vegetables for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they begin to soften.

Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, until the garlic is fragrant.

Step 3: Add the Tomatoes and Seasonings

Next, stir in the diced tomatoes with their juices, followed by the Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, thyme, and bay leaf. Mix everything together, allowing the tomatoes to break down and absorb the flavors of the seasoning. Let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to cook off some of the moisture and concentrate the flavors.

Step 4: Add Rice and Broth

Once the tomato mixture has simmered and thickened slightly, it’s time to add the rice. Stir the rice into the vegetable mixture, ensuring that it’s well-coated with the flavors. Pour in the chicken broth, and give everything a good stir.

Return the sausage and chicken to the pot, stirring them in with the rice and broth. Bring everything to a simmer, then cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook undisturbed for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed.

Step 5: Let the Jambalaya Rest

Once the rice is cooked, turn off the heat and let the jambalaya rest for 5-10 minutes with the lid on. This step allows the rice to fully absorb the flavors and ensures the perfect texture.

Step 6: Fluff and Serve

After the resting period, remove the bay leaf from the pot and fluff the rice with a fork. Taste the jambalaya and adjust the seasoning with salt, black pepper, or more Cajun seasoning if needed.

Serve the jambalaya in large bowls, garnished with fresh parsley and chopped green onions for a burst of color and flavor. If you like things spicy, drizzle with a few dashes of hot sauce or serve with lemon wedges for a bit of zest.

Tips for the Best Cajun Jambalaya

While this recipe is simple to follow, there are a few tips that will elevate your jambalaya to the next level.

1. Use Authentic Sausage

The Andouille sausage is a key ingredient in Cajun cooking, offering a smoky, spicy kick. If you can find it, use authentic Andouille sausage for the best flavor. If you can’t find it, smoked sausage or even kielbasa works as a substitute.

2. Don’t Skip the “Holy Trinity”

The onion, bell pepper, and celery are essential for building flavor. Don’t substitute these ingredients or skip this step, as they provide the foundation for the dish’s rich taste.

3. Let the Rice Cook Uninterrupted

Once the rice is added to the pot, resist the temptation to stir it too often. Stirring too much can cause the rice to become sticky. Instead, let it cook undisturbed on low heat to ensure fluffy rice with a great texture.

4. Adjust the Heat Level

Cajun seasoning can vary in spice level, so be sure to taste your jambalaya before adding more. If you prefer a spicier dish, add more Cajun seasoning or a few dashes of hot sauce. If you’re cooking for a crowd with varied heat preferences, you can always serve hot sauce on the side for people to adjust their own spice levels.

5. Add Shrimp or Other Proteins

If you like seafood, shrimp is a fantastic addition to jambalaya. Simply add it to the pot along with the sausage and chicken, allowing it to cook until pink and tender. You can also experiment with other proteins like ham or even turkey for different variations of the dish.

6. Let It Rest

Allowing the jambalaya to rest before serving helps all the flavors meld together and gives the rice time to absorb any remaining liquid. This step is key to achieving the perfect jambalaya texture.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make Cajun jambalaya ahead of time?

Yes! In fact, jambalaya tastes even better the next day as the flavors have time to develop. You can make it ahead and store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. When ready to serve, simply reheat on the stove over low heat, adding a splash of broth to prevent it from drying out.

Can I freeze jambalaya?

Yes, jambalaya freezes well. Let it cool completely, then transfer it to a freezer-safe container. It will keep in the freezer for up to 3 months. When ready to eat, thaw it overnight in the fridge and reheat on the stove.

Can I use brown rice instead of white rice?

Yes, you can substitute brown rice for white rice, but keep in mind that it will take longer to cook. Brown rice requires more liquid and a longer cooking time, so you may need to adjust the amount of chicken broth and the cooking time to ensure the rice cooks fully.

Final Thoughts

This ultimate Cajun jambalaya recipe with sausage is a hearty, flavorful dish that brings the taste of Louisiana to your kitchen. With smoky sausage, tender chicken, and a rich combination of spices, it’s sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or impressing guests at a gathering, this jambalaya is bound to satisfy.

Don’t forget to make the dish your own by adjusting the spice level and experimenting with different proteins. With its deep, savory flavors and satisfying texture, this Cajun jambalaya will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of New Orleans. Enjoy!
