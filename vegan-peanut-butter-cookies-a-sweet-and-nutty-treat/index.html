Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies: A Sweet and Nutty Treat


Who doesn’t love a deliciously chewy, melt-in-your-mouth cookie? And when it’s vegan and packed with the rich, nutty flavor of peanut butter, it’s hard to resist. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create the perfect vegan peanut butter cookies that are not only easy to make but also incredibly satisfying. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to explore plant-based baking, these cookies are sure to become a staple in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the creamy peanut butter, brown sugar, unsweetened applesauce, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth and well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the peanut butter mixture, stirring until a thick cookie dough forms.

  5. Using a cookie scoop or your hands, form the dough into 1-inch balls and place them on the prepared baking sheet. Use a fork to gently flatten each cookie, creating a crisscross pattern on top.

  6. Bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden. Keep in mind that the cookies will continue to firm up as they cool, so it’s okay if they seem slightly soft when you take them out of the oven.

  7. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Once cooled, store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. Though, let’s be honest, they probably won’t last that long!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, fold in a handful of dairy-free chocolate chips or chopped nuts into the cookie dough before baking.
  • If you prefer a sweeter cookie, you can increase the amount of brown sugar to 1 cup.
  • Get creative with the presentation by drizzling melted dairy-free chocolate over the cooled cookies or sandwiching a scoop of dairy-free ice cream between two cookies for an irresistible dessert sandwich.

Health Benefits of Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies

Not only are these cookies a delight for your taste buds, but they also offer some nutritional benefits. Peanut butter is a good source of plant-based protein, healthy fats, and essential vitamins and minerals. Additionally, using applesauce as a binder reduces the need for added oils or fats, making these cookies a healthier alternative to traditional peanut butter cookies.

Whether you’re baking for yourself, your family, or a gathering of friends, these vegan peanut butter cookies are a wonderful way to satisfy your sweet tooth while embracing the goodness of plant-based ingredients.

So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to savor the delectable aroma of freshly baked vegan peanut butter cookies. Happy baking!
