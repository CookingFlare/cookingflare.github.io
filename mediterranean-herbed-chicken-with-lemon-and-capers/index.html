Mediterranean Herbed Chicken with Lemon and Capers


If you’re searching for a dish that encapsulates the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean, look no further than Mediterranean Herbed Chicken with Lemon and Capers. This delightful recipe combines juicy chicken thighs marinated in a blend of aromatic herbs, zesty lemon, and briny capers, creating a meal that is both refreshing and satisfying. Whether you’re preparing a weeknight dinner or hosting a special occasion, this dish is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the details of this exquisite recipe.

Ingredients

To whip up this Mediterranean marvel, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 4 chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on for maximum flavor
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (about 1 lemon)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried oregano)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons capers, rinsed

For Cooking:

  • 1 cup chicken broth (low sodium)
  • Lemon slices for garnish
  • Additional fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, minced garlic, oregano, thyme, parsley, salt, and pepper. Whisk until well mixed.
  2. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken thighs in a resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring each piece is well coated. Add the capers into the bag or dish. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight. This allows the flavors to penetrate the chicken deeply.

Step 2: Preheat the Oven

  • While the chicken marinates, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

Step 3: Sear the Chicken

  1. Heat a Skillet: In a large oven-safe skillet, heat a small amount of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Sear the Chicken: Remove the chicken from the marinade, shaking off excess liquid. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down in the skillet. Sear for about 5-7 minutes until the skin is golden brown and crisp. Flip the chicken and sear the other side for an additional 3-4 minutes.

Step 4: Bake the Chicken

  1. Add Chicken Broth: Pour the chicken broth into the skillet, surrounding the chicken but not covering the skin.
  2. Transfer to Oven: Place the skillet in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes or until the internal temperature of the chicken reaches 165°F (75°C) and the juices run clear.
  3. Rest the Chicken: Once cooked, remove the skillet from the oven and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving. This helps keep the chicken juicy.

Step 5: Serve

  • To serve, arrange the chicken thighs on a platter, spooning some of the pan juices over the top. Garnish with fresh lemon slices and additional herbs if desired.

Pairing Suggestions

This Mediterranean Herbed Chicken with Lemon and Capers pairs beautifully with a variety of sides:

  • Roasted Vegetables: A mix of bell peppers, zucchini, and red onions drizzled with olive oil and roasted until tender complements the flavors of the chicken perfectly.
  • Couscous or Quinoa Salad: A light salad made of couscous or quinoa tossed with cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, and feta adds a refreshing touch.
  • Greek Salad: The crispness of cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, and feta cheese dressed in olive oil and lemon brings a touch of freshness to your plate.

Tips for Success

  1. Choosing the Right Chicken: Opt for bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs for richer flavor and juiciness. If you prefer chicken breasts, adjust the cooking time as they may cook quicker.
  2. Marination Time: For the best flavor, allow the chicken to marinate for at least 1 hour, but overnight marination works wonders for flavor depth.
  3. Fresh Herbs vs. Dried: Fresh herbs provide a brighter flavor, but if dried herbs are your only option, they work well too. Just use one-third of the amount.
  4. Adjusting the Acidity: Taste the marinade before adding the chicken. If you love lemon, feel free to add more zest or juice!

Health Benefits

Not only is this dish delicious, but it also brings a host of health benefits:

  • Lean Protein: Chicken is an excellent source of lean protein, essential for muscle repair and growth.
  • Heart-Healthy Fats: Olive oil is rich in monounsaturated fats, which are beneficial for heart health.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: The fresh herbs and lemon provide vitamins C and K, antioxidants, and various other nutrients.

Storing Leftovers

If you’re fortunate enough to have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. You can reheat the chicken in the oven to maintain its crispiness or in a skillet over low heat. The flavors continue to develop as the dish sits, making it even more enjoyable the next day.

Conclusion

Mediterranean Herbed Chicken with Lemon and Capers is a celebration of fresh flavors and simple cooking techniques. It’s a dish that not only nourishes but also transports you to sun-kissed shores and lush olive groves. Whether served at a family dinner or a festive gathering, it’s sure to be a favorite. So, gather your ingredients, marinate the chicken, and take a culinary journey to the heart of the Mediterranean right in your kitchen. Enjoy every bite!
