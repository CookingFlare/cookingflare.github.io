10 Delicious Korean BBQ Recipes to Try Today


Korean BBQ is a beloved cuisine that has gained popularity worldwide for its unique flavors, cooking methods, and the joy of sharing a meal with friends and family. It’s not just about grilling meat; it’s about creating an experience that’s fun, interactive, and filled with rich, savory flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned Korean BBQ enthusiast or a first-timer, these 10 delicious recipes will take your BBQ game to the next level. Let’s dive into the world of Korean BBQ with recipes that showcase the essence of this traditional yet modern culinary style.

1. Bulgogi (Korean BBQ Beef)

Bulgogi is one of the most iconic Korean BBQ dishes. The name literally translates to “fire meat,” referring to the way the beef is grilled. Thinly sliced pieces of beef are marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, sugar, and a variety of spices, giving the meat a perfectly balanced sweet, salty, and savory flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb thinly sliced beef sirloin or rib-eye
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil for grilling

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, garlic, grated onion, ginger, gochujang, and rice vinegar.
  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade and let it sit for at least 30 minutes (up to overnight) in the refrigerator.
  3. Preheat a grill or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Lightly oil the surface with vegetable oil.
  4. Grill the beef in batches for 2-3 minutes on each side, until slightly charred and cooked through.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions before serving.

Bulgogi is often served with a bowl of steamed rice and a variety of banchan (Korean side dishes) like kimchi, pickled vegetables, and sautéed spinach.

2. Samgyeopsal (Korean Pork Belly)

Samgyeopsal is one of the most popular Korean BBQ dishes, featuring thick slices of pork belly grilled right at the table. The key to this dish is the simple seasoning and the joy of dipping the grilled pork into a mixture of sesame oil, salt, and pepper.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly, sliced into thick strips
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 bunch lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1-2 green chili peppers, sliced
  • 1/2 cup ssamjang (Korean dipping sauce)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the pork belly slices for about 2-3 minutes on each side, until crispy and golden brown.
  3. While grilling, prepare the dipping sauce by mixing sesame oil, salt, and pepper together.
  4. Serve the grilled pork belly with lettuce leaves, garlic, chili peppers, and ssamjang for dipping.
  5. To eat, take a piece of grilled pork belly and wrap it in a lettuce leaf with garlic, chili, and ssamjang. Enjoy!

Samgyeopsal is often enjoyed with friends or family, making it a social and flavorful experience.

3. Galbi (Korean BBQ Short Ribs)

Galbi refers to marinated beef short ribs that are grilled until tender and juicy. The marinade consists of soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, sugar, and other traditional ingredients, creating a perfect balance of flavors that seep into the meat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef short ribs (flanken-style)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1/4 cup grated pear or apple (for tenderizing)
  • 1 tbsp rice wine
  • 2 tbsp green onions, chopped
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, grated pear or apple, and rice wine.
  2. Add the short ribs to the marinade and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight for best results).
  3. Preheat the grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the short ribs for 4-5 minutes on each side, until they are cooked through and have a nice char.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Galbi is often served with steamed rice and banchan, making it a hearty and satisfying meal.

4. Yangnyeom Chicken (Korean Fried Chicken)

Korean fried chicken, also known as yangnyeom chicken, is famous for its crispy, crunchy exterior and flavorful, sticky sauce. It’s often double-fried to ensure a perfect crunch, then coated in a spicy-sweet sauce made with gochujang, honey, and garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 10 chicken wings or drumettes
  • 1/2 cup potato starch or corn starch
  • 2 cups vegetable oil for frying

For the sauce:

  • 3 tbsp gochujang
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Coat the chicken wings or drumettes in potato starch, ensuring they are evenly covered.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
  3. Fry the chicken in batches for 8-10 minutes, or until golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate pan, combine the gochujang, honey, soy sauce, garlic, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Toss the fried chicken in the sauce, ensuring each piece is coated.
  6. Garnish with sesame seeds and serve hot.

Yangnyeom chicken is perfect as a side dish or as a main dish for a casual Korean BBQ meal.

5. Chadolbaegi (Korean Beef Brisket)

Chadolbaegi is thinly sliced beef brisket that is grilled quickly, often served with a variety of dipping sauces and accompaniments. The meat is tender and has a mild, savory flavor, making it an excellent choice for grilling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef brisket, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp pepper
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp green onions, chopped
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, pepper, and rice vinegar.
  3. Grill the brisket slices for 1-2 minutes per side, ensuring that they remain tender.
  4. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions.

Chadolbaegi is often served with a light dipping sauce made of soy sauce, sesame oil, and a little bit of garlic. It pairs wonderfully with a simple bowl of rice.

6. Jokbal (Korean Braised Pig’s Trotters)

Jokbal is a traditional Korean dish made from braised pig’s trotters, slowly simmered with soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and other spices to create a tender, flavorful meat that melts in your mouth. It’s often served as a side dish for Korean BBQ or enjoyed on its own with a dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pig’s trotters, cleaned and chopped into pieces
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 head of garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup rice wine
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp black peppercorns
  • 2-3 dried red chilies (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, add the pig’s trotters, onion, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, rice wine, brown sugar, sesame oil, and black peppercorns.
  2. Cover the ingredients with water, ensuring everything is submerged, and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook for 2-3 hours until the meat is tender.
  4. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  5. Serve the jokbal with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic.

Jokbal is a rich and savory dish that pairs well with a light kimchi or fresh vegetable side dish.

7. Dwaeji Galbi (Korean Pork Ribs)

Dwaeji galbi is a flavorful alternative to beef ribs. These marinated pork ribs are grilled until crispy on the outside but juicy and tender on the inside. The marinade typically includes a combination of gochujang, soy sauce, and other savory ingredients, giving the pork a deliciously spicy kick.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork ribs
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together soy sauce, gochujang, sesame oil, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, and rice vinegar.
  2. Marinate the pork ribs in the mixture for at least 1-2 hours.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the pork ribs for 4-5 minutes on each side until cooked through.
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.

Dwaeji galbi is delicious on its own or paired with a simple steamed rice and vegetable side.

8. Kimchi Jeon (Korean Kimchi Pancake)

Kimchi jeon is a savory pancake made with kimchi, flour, and a few simple seasonings. It’s crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, with a tangy kick from the fermented kimchi.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup kimchi, chopped
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the kimchi, flour, water, egg, soy sauce, sesame oil, and green onions.
  2. Heat a frying pan with vegetable oil over medium heat.
  3. Pour a ladle of the kimchi batter into the pan and spread it out into a thin layer.
  4. Fry for 3-4 minutes on each side until crispy and golden brown.
  5. Serve hot with soy sauce or a dipping sauce of your choice.

Kimchi jeon is a perfect side dish or snack that pairs well with any Korean BBQ meal.

9. Korean Spicy Grilled Fish (Godeungeo Gui)

Godeungeo gui is a simple yet delicious Korean dish made with mackerel or other oily fish. The fish is marinated in a spicy and tangy mixture before being grilled to perfection. The result is a smoky, flavorful fish that’s perfect for BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 2 mackerel, cleaned and gutted
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Mix the soy sauce, gochujang, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, garlic, and ginger in a bowl to make the marinade.
  2. Coat the mackerel in the marinade and let it sit for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  4. Grill the fish for 4-5 minutes on each side, basting with the remaining marinade until the fish is cooked through.
  5. Garnish with green onions and serve.

This dish is often served with steamed rice and a side of kimchi, making it a perfect addition to any Korean BBQ spread.

10. Sundubu Jjigae (Soft Tofu Stew)

Sundubu jjigae is a rich and comforting Korean stew made with silken tofu, vegetables, and often a choice of protein. It’s spicy, savory, and perfect for warming up during colder months or as a side to any Korean BBQ meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block soft tofu (sundubu)
  • 1/2 cup pork or beef (optional)
  • 1 tbsp gochujang
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a pot over medium heat and sauté the garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add the gochujang and gochugaru, stirring to combine.
  3. Add the broth and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Add the soft tofu, breaking it up into pieces.
  5. Crack an egg into the stew and let it cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Garnish with green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

This dish is a great side to accompany any grilled meats and adds a comforting, flavorful touch to your Korean BBQ meal.

Conclusion

Korean BBQ is an exciting and flavorful experience that brings people together around the grill. From tender bulgogi to crispy fried chicken, savory short ribs to spicy tofu stew, these recipes offer a wide variety of options to satisfy your craving for delicious Korean flavors. Whether you’re hosting a BBQ party or just enjoying a meal with your family, these dishes will surely elevate your Korean BBQ experience. So, fire up the grill and start cooking these mouthwatering recipes today!
