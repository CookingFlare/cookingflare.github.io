Mediterranean-Inspired Couscous Recipes for a Nutritious Meal


Couscous, a staple in Mediterranean cuisine, is a versatile and nutritious grain that can be used in a variety of dishes. Packed with flavor and easy to prepare, couscous is a perfect addition to any meal. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and healthy Mediterranean-inspired couscous recipes that will satisfy your taste buds and nourish your body.

Recipe 1: Greek-Inspired Couscous Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 cup diced cucumber
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped Kalamata olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the couscous according to the package instructions. Fluff it with a fork and let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked couscous, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, Kalamata olives, and red onion.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the dressing over the couscous mixture and toss gently to combine.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Recipe 2: Moroccan-Inspired Couscous with Roasted Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 medium eggplant, diced
  • 1 medium zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large baking sheet, toss the diced eggplant, zucchini, red and yellow bell peppers, and sliced red onion with olive oil, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast the vegetables in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. While the vegetables are roasting, cook the couscous in vegetable broth according to the package instructions.
  5. Fluff the cooked couscous with a fork and transfer it to a large serving bowl.
  6. Once the roasted vegetables are done, add them to the couscous and gently toss to combine.
  7. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro and serve warm.

Recipe 3: Italian-Inspired Couscous Stuffed Bell Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers (any color), tops removed and seeds removed
  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the couscous, cover, and remove from heat. Let it sit for 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork.
  3. In a separate skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  4. Add the cooked couscous, marinara sauce, shredded mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, chopped basil, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir until well combined and heated through.
  5. Stuff the bell peppers with the couscous mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Remove from the oven, let them cool slightly, and serve.

Conclusion

These Mediterranean-inspired couscous recipes are not only delicious but also provide a nutritious meal option. Whether you prefer a refreshing Greek-inspired salad, a flavorful Moroccan-inspired dish, or a cheesy Italian-inspired stuffed pepper, couscous is the perfect ingredient to create a wholesome and satisfying meal. Experiment with these recipes, add your own twist, and enjoy the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean right at your table. Bon appétit!
