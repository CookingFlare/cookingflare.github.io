Flaky Buttermilk Biscuits: A Classic Side Dish


There’s something truly comforting about the aroma of freshly baked flaky buttermilk biscuits wafting through the kitchen. These delightful treats, with their golden crust and tender crumb, are a staple side dish that pairs perfectly with a variety of meals. Whether served alongside a hearty breakfast, a steaming bowl of soup, or as a delightful snack on their own, flaky buttermilk biscuits are a timeless classic that never fails to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup cold buttermilk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 450°F (230°C) and lining a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.
  3. Incorporate the Butter: Add the cold cubed butter to the dry ingredients. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs with pea-sized lumps of butter.
  4. Add the Buttermilk: Make a well in the center of the mixture and pour in the cold buttermilk. Gently stir the mixture until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Shape the Biscuits: Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and pat it into a rectangle about 1-inch thick. Fold the dough in half and pat it out again to 1-inch thickness. Repeat this process two more times to create layers in the biscuits.
  6. Cut Out the Biscuits: Using a floured round cutter, cut out biscuits from the dough. Avoid twisting the cutter to ensure proper rising during baking.
  7. Bake the Biscuits: Place the cut biscuits on the prepared baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until they are golden brown on top.
  8. Serve and Enjoy: Once baked, remove the biscuits from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. These flaky buttermilk biscuits are best enjoyed warm with a slather of butter or your favorite jam.

Tips for Success:

  • Cold Ingredients: Ensure your butter and buttermilk are cold as this helps create a flaky texture in the biscuits.
  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Be gentle when mixing the dough to prevent tough biscuits. A light hand is key.
  • Layering Technique: The folding and layering technique creates those desirable flaky layers in the biscuits.
  • Proper Baking: Keep an eye on the biscuits while baking to ensure they don’t overcook. They should be beautifully golden on top.

Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting in the kitchen, mastering the art of making flaky buttermilk biscuits will surely impress your family and friends. With simple ingredients and a few handy tips, you can elevate any meal with these delicious, buttery delights. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to savor the goodness of homemade flaky buttermilk biscuits. Happy baking!
