Breakfast Bread Pudding: A Sweet Morning Indulgence


There’s something undeniably comforting and indulgent about a warm and sweet breakfast, and that’s where breakfast bread pudding comes in. This delightful dish is a perfect blend of flavors, textures, and aromas, making it an ideal treat for a leisurely weekend morning or a special occasion. Whether you’re a fan of traditional bread pudding or looking for a unique twist on a classic, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and elevate your breakfast game.

Ingredients

For the Bread Pudding:

  • 8 cups of day-old bread, cut into cubes (try using brioche, challah, or cinnamon swirl for added flavor)
  • 1 ½ cups whole milk
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

For the Topping:

  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • Optional: powdered sugar and maple syrup for serving

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. Prepare the Bread: In a large bowl, toss the bread cubes to ensure they are evenly distributed.

  3. Make the Custard: In another bowl, whisk together the milk, heavy cream, eggs, sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until well combined.

  4. Combine: Pour the custard mixture over the bread cubes, making sure all the bread is coated. Let it sit for 15-20 minutes, allowing the bread to absorb the custard.

  5. Prepare the Topping: In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, flour, and cinnamon. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  6. Assemble: Sprinkle the topping over the bread and custard mixture, evenly distributing it across the dish.

  7. Bake: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the bread pudding is set and the top is golden brown.

  8. Serve: Once done, remove the bread pudding from the oven and allow it to cool slightly. Dust with powdered sugar, drizzle with maple syrup if desired, and serve warm.

Tips for Success

  • Bread Selection: Choosing the right bread is crucial for a delicious bread pudding. Opt for bread with a sturdy texture and slightly dry, such as brioche, challah, or even leftover croissants for a decadent twist.

  • Customization: Feel free to customize your bread pudding with add-ins like raisins, chopped nuts, or even chocolate chips for an extra touch of indulgence.

  • Make-Ahead: You can prepare the bread pudding the night before, cover it with plastic wrap, and refrigerate it. In the morning, simply uncover and bake as directed for a fuss-free breakfast.

  • Serve with Love: Enhance the presentation of your breakfast bread pudding by serving it with a dollop of whipped cream, a scoop of vanilla ice cream, or fresh berries on the side.

Final Thoughts

Breakfast bread pudding is a delightful way to start your day on a sweet note. Its luscious texture, warm spices, and comforting aroma make it a standout morning treat that’s perfect for sharing with loved ones or savoring in solitude. With its simple preparation and limitless customization options, this recipe is a versatile addition to your breakfast repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a brunch gathering or simply treating yourself to a special breakfast, this sweet morning indulgence is sure to leave a lasting impression. So, embrace the joy of a homemade breakfast with this delectable bread pudding and make your mornings a little sweeter!
