Instant Pot Recipes for Beginners: Mastering the Basics


The Instant Pot is a game-changer in the kitchen. Whether you’re navigating your busy weekday schedule or looking to make meal prep easier, this versatile kitchen gadget has you covered. It combines the functionality of several appliances, including a pressure cooker, slow cooker, rice cooker, steamer, and more. But if you’ve just unboxed your Instant Pot, you might be wondering where to start. No worries—we’re here to walk you through some simple yet delicious recipes that will have you feeling like a pro in no time.

With these beginner-friendly recipes, you’ll learn the basics of cooking with the Instant Pot while mastering techniques you can use for endless culinary possibilities.

Why Use an Instant Pot?

Before diving into the recipes, let’s quickly cover why the Instant Pot is such a powerful tool. First, it’s efficient. Meals that traditionally take hours to cook, like soups, stews, and braised meats, can be completed in a fraction of the time. It’s also user-friendly, with pre-programmed settings for various dishes, making it perfect for cooks of all skill levels. Lastly, it locks in flavor and nutrients, ensuring every bite tastes as good as it is nutritious.

Recipe 1: Classic Instant Pot Rice

Rice is a foundational dish in many cuisines and a great starting point for beginners. The Instant Pot makes cooking rice effortless, with consistent results every time.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • Pinch of salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This step removes excess starch, preventing sticky rice.
  2. Add the rinsed rice, water, and salt to the Instant Pot.
  3. Secure the lid and ensure the pressure release valve is set to “Sealing.”
  4. Select the “Rice” setting or manually set the Instant Pot to cook on high pressure for 4 minutes.
  5. Once the cooking cycle is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes. Then carefully move the release valve to “Venting” to release any remaining pressure.
  6. Fluff the rice with a fork, and it’s ready to serve!

This simple recipe can be adapted to include spices, vegetables, or herbs for added flavor.

Recipe 2: Creamy Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

Who doesn’t love mac and cheese? The Instant Pot delivers creamy, cheesy goodness in a fraction of the time it would take on the stovetop.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound elbow macaroni
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions

  1. Add the macaroni, water, and salt to the Instant Pot.
  2. Secure the lid and set the pressure release valve to “Sealing.”
  3. Cook on high pressure for 4 minutes.
  4. Quick-release the pressure by carefully moving the valve to “Venting.”
  5. Stir in the milk, cheeses, and butter until creamy and smooth. If needed, keep the Instant Pot on the “Keep Warm” setting to help melt the cheese.

Serve immediately for the ultimate comfort food experience.

Recipe 3: Hearty Instant Pot Chicken Soup

Chicken soup is another staple and one of the easiest meals to prepare in your Instant Pot. Ideal for chilly days or when you’re feeling under the weather, this recipe is both nourishing and soul-warming.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup egg noodles (optional)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté.” Add the olive oil, onion, carrots, and celery, cooking for 3 to 4 minutes until softened. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Add the chicken breasts, chicken broth, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot.
  3. Secure the lid and set the pressure release valve to “Sealing.”
  4. Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  5. Remove the chicken breasts, shred them with two forks, and return the shredded chicken to the pot.
  6. (Optional) Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté,” add the egg noodles, and cook for 4 to 5 minutes until tender.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

Recipe 4: Instant Pot Hard-Boiled Eggs

One of the simplest things you can make in an Instant Pot is perfectly cooked hard-boiled eggs. No more guessing if they’re done—it’s easy and foolproof!

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. Place a steaming rack or trivet at the bottom of the Instant Pot.
  2. Add the water and arrange the eggs on the rack in a single layer.
  3. Secure the lid and set the pressure release valve to “Sealing.”
  4. Cook on high pressure for 5 minutes.
  5. Allow the pressure to release naturally for 5 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  6. Transfer the eggs to an ice bath and let them chill for 5 minutes.
  7. Peel under cold running water, and enjoy!

You’ll love how easy these eggs are to peel and how consistent the results are every time.

Recipe 5: Instant Pot Pulled Pork

Pulled pork is a crowd-pleaser, and with the Instant Pot, you can enjoy tender, flavorful meat in a fraction of the time it takes to make in a slow cooker or oven.

Ingredients

  • 3-pound pork shoulder (or pork butt), cut into large chunks
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to “Sauté” and add the olive oil. Sear the pork chunks for 2–3 minutes per side until browned. Remove the pork and set aside.
  2. Deglaze the pot with the chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
  3. Add the barbecue sauce, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Return the pork to the pot, ensuring the chunks are coated in sauce.
  5. Secure the lid and set the pressure release valve to “Sealing.”
  6. Cook on high pressure for 60 minutes. Allow the pressure to release naturally for 15 minutes before quick-releasing any remaining pressure.
  7. Shred the pork with two forks, mix it with the sauce, and serve with buns, coleslaw, or on its own.

Tips for Beginner Instant Pot Users

  1. Understand the Settings: Get familiar with your Instant Pot’s various functions, such as “Sauté,” “Pressure Cook,” and “Keep Warm.” Each serves a specific purpose and makes cooking more versatile.

  2. Use the Right Liquid Ratio: The Instant Pot requires liquid to create steam and build pressure. Make sure you follow the recipe’s instructions for the amount of liquid to avoid getting a “Burn” warning.

  3. Do a Water Test: If your Instant Pot is brand new, start with a water test to understand how it seals, builds pressure, and works overall.

  4. Don’t Overfill: Overfilling the Instant Pot can cause improper cooking and messes. Never fill it more than two-thirds full (or halfway for recipes that foam or expand, like rice or beans).

  5. Clean the Sealing Ring Regularly: The silicone sealing ring can absorb food odors, so it’s a good practice to clean or replace it as needed.

The Instant Pot is an incredible tool that simplifies cooking without compromising on flavor. These beginner-friendly recipes are the perfect way to get comfortable with your new appliance while building confidence in the kitchen. Once you’ve mastered these foundational dishes, the door to more advanced recipes will be wide open. Enjoy experimenting and happy cooking!
