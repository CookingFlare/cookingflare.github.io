Slow Cooker Cajun Shrimp Recipes for a Spicy Seafood Delight


If you’re a seafood lover and enjoy a bit of spice in your dishes, then you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’re going to explore some mouthwatering Slow Cooker Cajun Shrimp recipes that will surely satisfy your cravings. The slow cooker not only makes the cooking process easier, but it also helps infuse the flavors into the shrimp, resulting in a dish that is bursting with taste. So, let’s dive right in!

Cajun Shrimp Jambalaya

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup of long-grain rice
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes (14 ounces)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons of Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the rice, chicken broth, diced tomatoes, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.

  2. Cook on low heat for 4 hours or until the rice is cooked and the flavors have melded together.

  3. Stir in the shrimp and cook for an additional 30 minutes or until the shrimp are cooked through and pink.

  4. Serve the Cajun Shrimp Jambalaya hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Cajun Shrimp and Grits

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup of grits
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 4 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons of Cajun seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the water, grits, shredded cheddar cheese, heavy cream, bacon, onion, bell pepper, garlic, Cajun seasoning, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.

  2. Cook on low heat for 6-8 hours or until the grits are creamy and cooked through.

  3. Stir in the shrimp and cook for an additional 30 minutes or until the shrimp are cooked through and pink.

  4. Serve the Cajun Shrimp and Grits hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Cajun Shrimp Boil

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound of baby red potatoes, halved
  • 4 ears of corn, cut into thirds
  • 1 pound of smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine the potatoes, corn, smoked sausage, onion, garlic, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper, and salt. Stir well to combine.

  2. Cook on low heat for 6-8 hours or until the potatoes are tender.

  3. Stir in the shrimp and cook for an additional 30 minutes or until the shrimp are cooked through and pink.

  4. Serve the Cajun Shrimp Boil hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Conclusion

These Slow Cooker Cajun Shrimp recipes are perfect for seafood enthusiasts who crave a bit of heat. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to develop and infuse into every bite, creating a truly delightful experience. Whether you choose the Cajun Shrimp Jambalaya, Cajun Shrimp and Grits, or Cajun Shrimp Boil, you’re in for a treat. So, grab your slow cooker and get ready to indulge in a spicy seafood delight!

Remember, cooking is all about experimenting and making the dish your own. Feel free to adjust the spices and seasonings according to your taste preferences. Enjoy these Cajun Shrimp recipes with friends and family, and savor the flavors of the sea with a spicy twist.
