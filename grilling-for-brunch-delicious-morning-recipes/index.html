Grilling for Brunch: Delicious Morning Recipes


Who says grilling is only for lunch and dinner? Why not take your Sunday brunch to the next level by firing up the grill? Grilling adds a smoky flavor and a touch of outdoor adventure to your morning meals. Whether you’re hosting a brunch party or simply want to enjoy a leisurely morning with your loved ones, these delicious grilling recipes will make your brunch unforgettable. So, grab your apron and let’s get grilling!

Grilled Avocado and Egg Toast

Preparation time: 10 minutes | Grilling time: 5 minutes | Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 2 slices of bread (preferably ciabatta or sourdough)
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Cut the avocado in half and remove the pit. Slice each half into thick wedges.
  3. Drizzle the avocado wedges with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the avocado wedges directly on the grill grates and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, until grill marks appear.
  5. While the avocados are grilling, toast the bread slices on the grill until lightly charred.
  6. Remove the avocados and bread from the grill.
  7. In a small pan, fry the eggs sunny-side up until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
  8. Place the grilled avocado wedges on top of the toasted bread slices and gently mash them with a fork.
  9. Carefully place a fried egg on top of each avocado toast.
  10. Season with additional salt and pepper if desired.
  11. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Grilled Breakfast Burrito

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Grilling time: 10 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 8 slices of bacon
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Salsa, sour cream, and guacamole for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Cook the bacon in a skillet until crispy. Set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the sliced bell pepper and onion until they become tender. Set aside.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk the eggs and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Pour the beaten eggs into a greased grill-safe skillet or aluminum foil pan.
  6. Place the skillet or pan on the grill and cook the eggs, stirring occasionally, until they are scrambled and cooked through.
  7. Remove the skillet or pan from the grill and set aside.
  8. Lay out the flour tortillas on a clean surface.
  9. Divide the scrambled eggs, bacon, sautéed bell pepper, and onion evenly among the tortillas.
  10. Sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese over the fillings.
  11. Roll up the tortillas, tucking in the sides as you go.
  12. Place the burritos on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they are heated through and lightly charred.
  13. Remove the burritos from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes.
  14. Serve with salsa, sour cream, and guacamole on the side.
  15. Enjoy your delicious grilled breakfast burritos!

Grilled Fruit Skewers with Yogurt Dip

Preparation time: 15 minutes | Grilling time: 5 minutes | Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • Assorted fruits (such as pineapple, strawberries, peaches, and bananas), cut into chunks
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the fruit chunks onto the soaked wooden skewers, alternating the different fruits.
  3. Place the fruit skewers directly on the grill grates and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until grill marks appear.
  4. While the fruit skewers are grilling, prepare the yogurt dip by combining Greek yogurt, honey, and vanilla extract in a small bowl. Mix well.
  5. Remove the fruit skewers from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes.
  6. Serve the grilled fruit skewers with the yogurt dip on the side.
  7. Dip the grilled fruits into the creamy yogurt dip and savor the sweet and smoky flavors.
  8. Enjoy this refreshing and healthy grilled fruit treat!

Conclusion

Grilling isn’t just for burgers and steaks. With these delicious morning recipes, you can elevate your brunch experience and impress your guests with unique flavors. From the smoky grilled avocado and egg toast to the hearty grilled breakfast burrito, and the refreshing grilled fruit skewers, these recipes will make your brunch a memorable one. So, fire up the grill and start your day with a twist! Happy grilling and brunching!
