Scrumptious Thanksgiving Apple Pie Recipes


Welcome to our delicious collection of Thanksgiving apple pie recipes! Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, these scrumptious apple pie recipes are sure to impress your friends and family this holiday season. From classic apple pies to unique twists on the traditional recipe, we’ve got something for everyone. So put on your apron and let’s get baking!

Classic Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 store-bought pie crusts
  • 6 cups peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place one pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.
  4. Transfer the apple mixture into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. Dot the top of the apples with small pieces of butter.
  6. Place the second pie crust over the filling and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  7. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg wash.
  8. Bake for about 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Dutch Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 store-bought pie crust
  • 5 cups peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Topping:

  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.
  4. Transfer the apple mixture into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix the flour, brown sugar, and softened butter until crumbly.
  6. Sprinkle the topping mixture over the apples, covering them completely.
  7. Bake for about 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  8. Remove from the oven and let it cool for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Caramel Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 store-bought pie crusts
  • 6 cups peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Caramel Sauce:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place one pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are evenly coated.
  4. Transfer the apple mixture into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. In a saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water over medium heat. Cook until the sugar dissolves and turns golden brown.
  6. Remove the saucepan from heat and carefully add the heavy cream, butter, and vanilla extract. Stir until smooth.
  7. Pour the caramel sauce over the apple filling, covering it completely.
  8. Place the second pie crust over the filling and crimp the edges to seal. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  9. Bake for about 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  10. Remove from the oven and let it cool for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Enjoy these scrumptious apple pie recipes and make your Thanksgiving feast even more memorable! Happy baking!
