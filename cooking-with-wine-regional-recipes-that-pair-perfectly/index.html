Cooking with Wine: Regional Recipes That Pair Perfectly


When it comes to cooking with wine, many people think of rich, bold flavors and complex sauces. And while it’s true that wine can add depth and sophistication to a dish, it’s also a versatile ingredient that can be used in a wide range of recipes, from simple weeknight meals to special occasion feasts.

In this post, we’ll explore some regional recipes that pair perfectly with wine, from the classic coq au vin of France to the hearty stews of Italy. We’ll also provide some tips and tricks for using wine in your cooking, and highlight some of the best wines to use in different dishes.

French Cuisine: Coq au Vin and More

French cuisine is famous for its rich, buttery sauces, and wine is a key ingredient in many of these dishes. One of the most famous examples is coq au vin, a classic braised chicken dish that’s made with chicken cooked in red wine, mushrooms, onions, and bacon.

But coq au vin is just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to French cuisine and wine. Another classic dish is boeuf bourguignon, a hearty beef stew that’s made with beef short ribs, mushrooms, and red wine. And for a lighter option, try a simple tarte tatin, a caramelized apple tart that’s made with butter, sugar, and white wine.

Coq au Vin Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds chicken thighs, bone-in and skin-on
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine (such as Burgundy or Merlot)
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cups mushrooms (such as button or cremini), sliced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and cook until the onion is softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the red wine, chicken broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir to combine, then add the browned chicken back to the pot.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then cover the pot and transfer it to the oven. Braise the chicken for 30-40 minutes, or until it’s cooked through and the meat is tender.
  5. Remove the pot from the oven and add the butter and mushrooms to the pot. Stir to combine, then add the flour to the pot and stir to combine.
  6. Serve the chicken hot, garnished with fresh parsley and crusty bread on the side.

Italian Cuisine: Hearty Stews and Risottos

Italian cuisine is famous for its rich, comforting dishes, and wine is a key ingredient in many of these recipes. One of the most famous examples is osso buco, a classic braised veal shank dish that’s made with veal, vegetables, and white wine.

But osso buco is just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to Italian cuisine and wine. Another classic dish is braised beef with red wine and mushrooms, a hearty stew that’s made with beef short ribs, mushrooms, and red wine. And for a lighter option, try a simple risotto with white wine and asparagus, a creamy rice dish that’s made with Arborio rice, white wine, and asparagus.

Braised Beef with Red Wine and Mushrooms Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine (such as Chianti or Merlot)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups mushrooms (such as cremini or shiitake), sliced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and cook until the onion is softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the red wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir to combine, then add the browned beef back to the pot.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then cover the pot and transfer it to the oven. Braise the beef for 2-3 hours, or until it’s cooked through and the meat is tender.
  5. Remove the pot from the oven and add the mushrooms to the pot. Stir to combine, then add the flour to the pot and stir to combine.
  6. Serve the beef hot, garnished with fresh parsley and crusty bread on the side.

Tips and Tricks for Cooking with Wine

When it comes to cooking with wine, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, always use a good quality wine that you would drink on its own. Cheap wine can be overpowering and bitter, and can ruin the flavor of your dish.

Second, don’t be afraid to experiment with different types of wine. Red wine is great for braising meats, while white wine is perfect for lighter dishes like risotto and chicken.

Finally, remember that wine is a flavor enhancer, not a flavor substitute. Use it to add depth and complexity to your dish, but don’t rely on it to carry the entire flavor.

Wine Pairing Guide

Here are some general guidelines for pairing wine with different types of dishes:

  • Red wine: pair with red meat, game, and rich sauces
  • White wine: pair with white meat, fish, and lighter sauces
  • Sparkling wine: pair with appetizers and desserts
  • Fortified wine: pair with sweet and savory dishes

Conclusion

Cooking with wine is a great way to add depth and complexity to your dishes, and there are many different types of wine to choose from. Whether you’re making a classic French dish like coq au vin, or a hearty Italian stew, wine is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a wide range of recipes. By following the tips and tricks outlined in this post, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a wine-cooking expert. So the next time you’re planning a special occasion feast, don’t forget to reach for the wine!
