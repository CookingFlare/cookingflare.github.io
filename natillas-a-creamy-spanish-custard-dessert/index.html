Natillas: A Creamy Spanish Custard Dessert


Natillas is a delightful Spanish dessert that will transport you to the sunny streets of Spain with every spoonful. This creamy custard dessert is simple yet elegant, making it a perfect ending to any meal. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving something sweet, natillas is a recipe you need in your culinary repertoire. In this blog post, we will dive into the history of natillas, explore its ingredients, and guide you through the step-by-step process of making this delectable treat.

The History of Natillas

Natillas has a rich history that dates back centuries in Spain. It is believed to have originated in the Andalusian region during the Moorish rule. The Moors introduced the concept of custard to Spain, and over time, it evolved into the natillas we know and love today.

Traditionally, natillas was made with milk, sugar, eggs, and a touch of cinnamon. As the recipe spread throughout Spain, regional variations emerged, with some adding lemon zest or even a splash of brandy for an extra kick of flavor. Today, natillas remains a beloved dessert in Spanish cuisine, often served during special occasions and family gatherings.

Ingredients

To make natillas, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk and cinnamon stick. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it starts to simmer. Once simmering, remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture steep for 10 minutes to infuse the milk with the cinnamon flavor.

  2. While the milk is steeping, in a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, egg yolks, and cornstarch until well combined.

  3. After the 10 minutes have passed, remove the cinnamon stick from the milk and discard it. Return the saucepan to the heat and bring the milk back to a simmer.

  4. Slowly pour half of the simmering milk into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from curdling. This step, known as tempering, ensures a smooth custard texture.

  5. Once the milk and egg yolk mixture is well combined, pour it back into the saucepan with the remaining milk. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, until it thickens and coats the back of the spoon. This process should take about 10-15 minutes.

  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Transfer the custard into individual serving dishes or a large bowl.

  7. Let the natillas cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until chilled and set.

  8. Before serving, sprinkle ground cinnamon over the top of each natillas dish for an extra touch of flavor and presentation.

Serving Suggestions

Natillas can be enjoyed on its own, but there are many ways to elevate this classic dessert. Here are a few serving suggestions to inspire your creativity:

  • Serve natillas with a side of fresh berries or a drizzle of caramel sauce for added sweetness and texture.
  • For a traditional Spanish touch, sprinkle some crumbled Maria cookies or toasted almonds on top of each dish.
  • Pair natillas with a cup of Spanish coffee or a glass of sweet sherry for a truly authentic experience.

Conclusion

Natillas is a delightful Spanish custard dessert that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Spain. With its creamy texture and delicate flavors, it’s no wonder natillas has remained a beloved dessert in Spanish cuisine for centuries. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to indulge in something sweet, this recipe is sure to impress. So grab your saucepan and whisk, and let’s embark on a culinary adventure with natillas!
