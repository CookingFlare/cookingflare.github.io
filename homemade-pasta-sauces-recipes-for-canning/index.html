Homemade Pasta Sauces: Recipes for Canning


Are you tired of store-bought pasta sauces that lack that homemade touch? Do you crave the authentic flavors of Italy in every bite? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore a variety of homemade pasta sauce recipes that are perfect for canning. Whether you’re a seasoned canner or a beginner, these recipes will surely impress your taste buds and elevate your pasta dishes to a whole new level.

Classic Marinara Sauce

Let’s start with a classic - Marinara Sauce. This timeless recipe is a staple in Italian cuisine and is incredibly versatile. It’s perfect for topping your favorite pasta or as a base for other sauces. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 10 pounds of fresh tomatoes
  • 1 cup of chopped onions
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons of dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Begin by blanching the tomatoes in boiling water for a minute, then transfer them to an ice bath. This will make it easier to peel off the skins. Once peeled, roughly chop the tomatoes.

In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant. Add the chopped tomatoes, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for about an hour, stirring occasionally.

Once the sauce has thickened, it’s time to can it. Ladle the hot sauce into sterilized jars, leaving about a half-inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, seal the jars, and process them in a boiling water bath for 40 minutes. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Creamy Alfredo Sauce

If you prefer a rich and indulgent pasta sauce, then Alfredo Sauce is the way to go. This velvety sauce is made with butter, cream, and Parmesan cheese, resulting in a luscious dish that will impress even the most discerning palates. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the heavy cream and bring it to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and gradually whisk in the grated Parmesan cheese until it has melted and the sauce is smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

To can the Alfredo Sauce, pour the hot sauce into sterilized jars, leaving about a half-inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, seal the jars, and process them in a boiling water bath for 20 minutes. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Spicy Arrabbiata Sauce

For those who enjoy a little kick in their pasta dishes, the Spicy Arrabbiata Sauce is a must-try. This fiery sauce is made with tomatoes, garlic, and red pepper flakes, creating a bold and flavorful experience. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 6 cups of crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of red pepper flakes (adjust to your desired level of spiciness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes, and sauté until fragrant. Add the crushed tomatoes, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

To can the Spicy Arrabbiata Sauce, ladle the hot sauce into sterilized jars, leaving about a half-inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean, seal the jars, and process them in a boiling water bath for 35 minutes. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place.

Conclusion

There you have it - three delicious homemade pasta sauce recipes that are perfect for canning. Whether you prefer the classic flavors of Marinara Sauce, the indulgence of Creamy Alfredo Sauce, or the heat of Spicy Arrabbiata Sauce, these recipes will surely satisfy your cravings. So why settle for store-bought when you can have the real deal? Get canning and elevate your pasta dishes to new heights of flavor!
