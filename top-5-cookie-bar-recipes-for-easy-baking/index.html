Top 5 Cookie Bar Recipes for Easy Baking


If you’re seeking irresistible desserts that strike the perfect balance between simplicity and indulgence, cookie bars are a fantastic choice. These crowd-pleasing treats are easier to bake than traditional cookies and offer endless variation possibilities. From classic flavors to creative twists, cookie bars are a shortcut to deliciousness and a favorite for busy bakers who want maximum payoff with minimal effort. Below, you’ll find five standout cookie bar recipes that can be whipped up with ease, perfect for parties, potlucks, or just a sweet treat at home.

1. Chocolate Chip Cookie Bars

A timeless favorite, chocolate chip cookie bars bring all the comforts of classic chocolate chip cookies in a convenient slice-and-serve format. These chewy and soft bars are infused with bursts of chocolate goodness, making them an easy win for dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish or line it with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream the butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Mix in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry flour mixture to the wet ingredients and mix until fully incorporated. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  6. Spread the dough evenly into the prepared baking dish.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges turn golden brown and the center is set. Cool completely before slicing into bars.

2. Salted Caramel Cookie Bars

For a taste of indulgence that’s hard to resist, salted caramel cookie bars combine the sweet and salty magic of caramel with buttery cookie dough. Perfectly decadent, these bars make an incredible dessert or a treat to wow your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup caramel sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • Sea salt for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter, brown sugar, and white sugar together until fluffy. Add the egg and vanilla extract, mixing until well combined.
  3. Gradually incorporate the flour and salt into the mixture, blending until the dough forms.
  4. Divide the dough in half. Press half into the bottom of the baking dish to form an even layer.
  5. Spread the caramel sauce evenly over the dough. Sprinkle sea salt on top for added flavor.
  6. Crumble the remaining dough over the caramel layer, pressing gently to ensure it sticks.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown. Let the bars cool completely before cutting into squares.

3. Peanut Butter Chocolate Cookie Bars

The ultimate combo of peanut butter and chocolate shines in these cookie bars. Rich, satisfying, and packed with flavor, they’re a must-try for anyone who loves this classic pairing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl.
  3. In a mixing bowl, cream butter, peanut butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until smooth.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla extract. Slowly incorporate the dry flour mixture.
  5. Fold in the chocolate chips. Spread the batter into the baking dish evenly.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Cool completely before slicing into bars.

4. Oatmeal Raisin Cookie Bars

Oatmeal raisin cookie bars deliver a hearty and wholesome flavor, perfect for dessert enthusiasts who enjoy a bit of texture. The combination of chewy oats and sweet raisins will transport you to nostalgic childhood bites.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar (packed)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup raisins

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and brown sugar together until fluffy. Add eggs and vanilla extract, mixing until combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the flour, salt, and baking soda together. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture.
  4. Stir in rolled oats and raisins until fully incorporated. Spread the batter evenly into the prepared baking dish.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown. Let cool completely before cutting into squares.

5. S’mores Cookie Bars

Bring the nostalgic summer campfire treat indoors with these s’mores cookie bars. Featuring layers of graham crackers, marshmallows, and chocolate, these bars are sure to delight kids and adults alike.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1 cup mini marshmallows
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. Cream together the butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, mixing well.
  3. Gradually add flour, baking soda, salt, and graham cracker crumbs. Mix until fully incorporated.
  4. Press half of the dough into the bottom of the baking dish. Sprinkle with mini marshmallows and chocolate chips, then top with the remaining dough.
  5. Bake for 25-28 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center is set. Let cool before slicing into bars.

Cookie bars simplify the baking process, offering endless possibilities for creativity and flavor combinations. These five easy recipes are designed to satisfy your sweet tooth while minimizing kitchen stress. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply indulging in your favorite treats, cookie bars are guaranteed to be a hit every time.
