Mediterranean Brunch Delight: Greek Shakshuka Recipe


If you’re a fan of Mediterranean flavors and looking for a delightful brunch option, then Greek Shakshuka might just be the perfect recipe for you. This dish is a combination of poached eggs in a flavorful tomato and pepper sauce, seasoned with aromatic herbs and spices. It’s a versatile dish that can be served for brunch, lunch, or even dinner, and it’s sure to impress your family and friends with its rich and savory taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-6 large eggs
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Feta cheese (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent, then add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  2. Add the diced bell peppers to the skillet and cook until they begin to soften.

  3. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and stir in the paprika, cumin, and dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes until it thickens slightly.

  4. Using a spoon, create small wells in the sauce and carefully crack the eggs into the wells. Cover the skillet and let the eggs cook in the sauce until the whites are set but the yolks are still runny, about 5-7 minutes.

  5. Once the eggs are cooked to your liking, garnish the shakshuka with fresh parsley and crumbled feta cheese if desired.

  6. Serve the Greek shakshuka directly from the skillet, accompanied by crusty bread or pita for dipping into the flavorful sauce.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, consider adding a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the sauce.
  • If you prefer a spicier shakshuka, you can use hot paprika instead of the regular paprika.
  • Feel free to customize the dish by adding other vegetables such as zucchini, spinach, or mushrooms to the sauce.

Health Benefits

Greek shakshuka is not only a delicious and satisfying dish, but it also offers a range of health benefits. The eggs provide high-quality protein and essential nutrients, while the tomatoes and bell peppers contribute antioxidants, vitamins, and fiber. The olive oil used in the recipe is a source of heart-healthy monounsaturated fats, making this dish a wholesome choice for a balanced brunch.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Greek shakshuka is a delightful and flavorful dish that brings the vibrant tastes of the Mediterranean to your brunch table. It’s an easy-to-make recipe that is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire, and its versatility allows for endless variations to suit your taste preferences. Whether you’re hosting a brunch gathering or simply treating yourself to a special meal, Greek shakshuka is a must-try recipe that will transport you to the sunny shores of the Mediterranean with every delicious bite. Enjoy!
