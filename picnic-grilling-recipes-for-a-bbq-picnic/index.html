Picnic Grilling: Recipes for a BBQ Picnic


Are you planning a fun-filled picnic with your family or friends? Well, what better way to enjoy a sunny day than by firing up the grill and indulging in some delicious barbecue delights? In this blog post, we’ll share with you some mouthwatering picnic grilling recipes that are guaranteed to take your outdoor feast to the next level. So grab your apron, fire up the grill, and let’s get cooking!

Recipe 1: Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Cut the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces and thread them onto skewers.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the marinade over the chicken skewers, making sure to coat them evenly.
  5. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the chicken is cooked through and nicely charred.
  6. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

Recipe 2: Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili Lime Butter

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, lime zest, lime juice, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Place the corn directly on the grill grates and cook for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and slightly charred.
  4. Remove the corn from the grill and let it cool for a minute or two.
  5. Spread the chili lime butter over the grilled corn, making sure to coat it evenly.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors!

Recipe 3: Grilled Vegetable Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis, cut into thick slices
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a large bowl, toss together the zucchinis, bell peppers, red onion, cherry tomatoes, olive oil, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the vegetables onto skewers, alternating between different colors and varieties.
  4. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them cool for a minute or two before serving.

Recipe 4: Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon Sugar

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, peeled and cored
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Vanilla ice cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Cut the pineapple into thick slices.
  3. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, brown sugar, and ground cinnamon. Mix well.
  4. Brush the cinnamon sugar mixture over both sides of the pineapple slices.
  5. Place the pineapple slices on the preheated grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until they are heated through and have nice grill marks.
  6. Remove the pineapple slices from the grill and let them cool for a minute.
  7. Serve the grilled pineapple as is or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful tropical dessert.

These recipes are just a few examples of the countless delicious dishes you can prepare for a BBQ picnic. Whether you’re a meat lover, a fan of grilled veggies, or have a sweet tooth, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So gather your loved ones, head outdoors, and savor the flavors of summer with these fantastic picnic grilling recipes. Happy cooking and happy picnicking!
