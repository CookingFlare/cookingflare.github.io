Shanghai-Style Scallion Oil Noodles: A Simple and Satisfying Dish


If you’ve ever craved a quick, comforting, and utterly delicious meal, Shanghai-style scallion oil noodles are the answer. This classic Chinese dish combines the rich flavors of aromatic scallion oil with perfectly cooked noodles, resulting in a dish that’s both simple to make and incredibly satisfying to eat. Whether you’re a noodle enthusiast or just someone looking for a new culinary adventure, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your home.

Ingredients

To make Shanghai-style scallion oil noodles, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 10 ounces of fresh noodles (such as wheat noodles or egg noodles)
  • 6-8 stalks of scallions (green onions), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • Optional: 1 teaspoon of sesame oil for added flavor

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Scallion Oil

  1. Begin by heating the vegetable oil in a small pan over medium heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add the finely chopped scallions to the pan.
  3. Allow the scallions to sizzle in the oil, stirring occasionally, until they turn a golden brown color. This process should take about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Once the scallions are golden brown, remove the pan from the heat and set it aside to let the scallion oil cool.

Step 2: Cook the Noodles

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook the fresh noodles according to the package instructions. Be sure not to overcook the noodles; they should be al dente.
  2. Once the noodles are cooked, drain them and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Then, drain them again thoroughly.

Step 3: Assemble the Dish

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, sugar, and salt. Mix well until the sugar and salt are dissolved.
  2. Add the cooked noodles to the bowl and toss them in the sauce until they are evenly coated.
  3. Drizzle the scallion oil over the noodles, using as much or as little as you prefer. Toss the noodles again to distribute the scallion oil evenly.
  4. If desired, add a teaspoon of sesame oil for an extra layer of flavor.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Once the noodles are well-coated with the scallion oil and sauce, divide them into individual serving bowls.
  2. Garnish the noodles with additional chopped scallions for a pop of color and freshness.
  3. Serve the Shanghai-style scallion oil noodles immediately and enjoy this simple yet delightful dish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a touch of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a drizzle of chili oil to the noodles.
  • Feel free to customize the dish with your favorite protein, such as thinly sliced pork, chicken, or tofu.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, consider using mushroom sauce or vegetarian oyster sauce as a substitute for the soy sauce.

Final Thoughts

Shanghai-style scallion oil noodles are the epitome of comfort food with their savory, umami-packed flavors and satisfying texture. This dish is perfect for a quick and nourishing meal, whether you’re cooking for yourself or entertaining guests. The simplicity of the ingredients and the ease of preparation make these noodles a go-to option for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. Give this recipe a try, and experience the joy of savoring a bowl of Shanghai-style scallion oil noodles in the comfort of your own home.
