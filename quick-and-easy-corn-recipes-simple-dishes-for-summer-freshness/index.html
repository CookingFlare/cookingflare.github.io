Quick and Easy Corn Recipes: Simple Dishes for Summer Freshness


The sweetness of summer is in full swing, and one of the best ways to capture its essence is through the simplicity of corn. Whether boiled, grilled, or sautéed, corn is a versatile ingredient that adds a touch of warmth to any meal. In this blog post, we'll share some quick and easy corn recipes that will satisfy your cravings for the season's freshness. 4 - 6 trimmed ears of cob 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1 tablespoon minced garlic 4 sliced lemons Salt and pepper to taste Low-sodium butter or olive oil

Boil the corn for 10-12 minutes, or until slightly tender. In a separate pan, mix the parsley, minced garlic, and sliced lemons over low heat. Brush the cooked corn with butter or oil and then toss it with the mixture. Serve with a pinch of salt and pepper, and enjoy. 6-8 ears of corn 1/4 cup chopped Cotija cheese 2 tablespoons chili powder 1/4 cup chopped scallions 2 tablespoons melted butter Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the husks and silk from the corn, then brush it with butter. Sprinkle the chili powder, chopped scallions, and Cotija cheese evenly over the corn. Grill the corn for 10-12 minutes on each side, or until slightly charred. 1 cup corn kernels 2 tablespoons honey 4 slices of prosciutto 1/4 cup chopped scallions 1 tablespoon olive oil Salt and pepper to taste

Sauté the scallions in olive oil until softened, then add the corn and honey. Cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the corn is slightly tender. Meanwhile, cook the prosciutto slices until crispy, then crumble them over the corn mixture. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Conclusion Corn is an underappreciated hero in the culinary world, with its sweetness and versatility offering endless opportunities for creativity. These simple yet flavorful recipes showcase the best of the summer season, making them a perfect fit for any occasion. Whether boiled, grilled, or sautéed, corn is a great way to elevate your meals and add a touch of summer freshness.
