Cooking for Two: Easy and Quick Pasta Recipes


When it comes to cooking for two, finding recipes that are both easy to prepare and satisfying can sometimes be a challenge. Enter pasta, the culinary classic that is as versatile as it is delicious. With endless sauces, toppings, and preparation techniques, pasta is the perfect choice for weeknight dinners, lazy weekends, or even at-home date nights. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of easy and quick pasta recipes that are perfectly portioned for two people. From creamy sauces to bold Mediterranean flavors, there’s a pasta dish for every mood and craving.

1. Lemon Garlic Shrimp Pasta

This light and zesty dish comes together in under 30 minutes and offers a delightful balance of flavors. Perfect for warm evenings or when you need something quick but still impressive.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil a pot of salted water and cook the pasta according to package directions. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp, season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes, and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque.
  4. Stir in the lemon juice and zest, incorporating the reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce.
  5. Toss the drained pasta into the skillet until well-coated. Garnish with parsley and Parmesan cheese before serving.

2. Creamy Tomato Basil Pasta

When you’re in the mood for comfort food but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen, this creamy tomato basil pasta is a delightful option. It’s hearty yet light, making it perfect for casual dinners.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz penne pasta
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 6 fresh basil leaves, torn
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the penne in a pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Pour in the heavy cream and stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, and red pepper flakes. Simmer for 5 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, tossing to coat evenly. Adjust consistency with the reserved pasta water if needed.
  5. Stir in the fresh basil leaves, season with salt and pepper, and garnish with Parmesan before serving.

3. One-Pan Alfredo with Bacon and Spinach

For a no-fuss dish that minimizes cleanup while maximizing flavor, this one-pan Alfredo pasta ticks all the boxes. With crispy bacon, tender spinach, and a luscious sauce, it’s a winner every time.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz fettuccine
  • 2 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cracked black pepper for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or deep pan, cook bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove and set aside, leaving about 1 tablespoon of drippings in the pan.
  2. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute. Then pour in the chicken stock and heavy cream.
  3. Add the fettuccine to the liquid, ensuring it’s fully submerged. Cover and let simmer over medium heat for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the pasta is tender.
  4. Stir in the Parmesan cheese until the sauce thickens. Add in the spinach and cook until wilted.
  5. Return the bacon to the pan, season with salt and pepper, and garnish with cracked black pepper before serving.

4. Pesto and Grilled Chicken Pasta

Pesto is a great way to infuse big flavors with minimal effort, making this dish both quick and bold. Pair it with grilled chicken to create a satisfying and complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz bowtie or fusilli pasta
  • ½ cup fresh basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 chicken breast, grilled and sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta in salted water until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the cherry tomatoes and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Toss the cooked pasta with the pesto until evenly coated.
  4. Add the grilled chicken slices and sautéed tomatoes to the pasta, seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  5. Serve with grated Parmesan cheese sprinkled on top.

5. Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

Simple, rustic, and full of garlicky goodness, Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is the ultimate minimalist weeknight meal. Paired with crusty bread and a side salad, it’s a timeless classic that’s hard to beat.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly chopped parsley for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted water until al dente. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water and drain.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over low heat. Add the garlic slices and sauté until golden brown. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. Add the red pepper flakes and cook for an additional 30 seconds.
  4. Toss the drained spaghetti into the skillet, adding reserved pasta water as needed to create a light sauce.
  5. Garnish with parsley and optional Parmesan before serving.

Tips for Perfecting Your Pasta Nights for Two:

  • Portion Control: Measure pasta servings carefully to avoid making too little or too much. Typically, 2-3 oz of dry pasta per person works well.
  • Fresh Ingredients: Using the freshest ingredients, such as freshly grated Parmesan or homegrown basil, elevates flavors significantly, even in quick recipes.
  • Balancing Flavors: Taste your dish as you go, adjusting seasoning to ensure each bite bursts with flavor.
  • Time-Saving Techniques: Pre-chop ingredients, use pre-prepped proteins (like rotisserie chicken), or make sauces like pesto in large batches to save effort.

Cooking for two shouldn’t be complicated or time-consuming. With these easy and quick pasta recipes, dinner at home becomes a joyful culinary experience, free from stress. Whether you’re sharing a meal with a loved one, a roommate, or simply treating yourself and saving leftovers, these recipes will bring satisfaction and vibrant flavors to the table. Keep experimenting and enjoy your pasta nights!
