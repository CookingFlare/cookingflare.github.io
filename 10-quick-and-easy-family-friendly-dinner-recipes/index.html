10 Quick and Easy Family-Friendly Dinner Recipes


Finding dinner ideas that are both quick to prepare and appealing to the whole family can be a challenge. Between busy work schedules, school activities, and trying to keep everyone satisfied with healthy meals, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed. But don’t worry! We’ve got you covered with 10 family-friendly dinner recipes that are not only quick and easy but also packed with flavor. These recipes are sure to please both picky eaters and those with adventurous tastes alike. Plus, they’ll save you time in the kitchen, allowing you to enjoy more moments with your loved ones.

1. One-Pan Chicken Fajitas

This vibrant and flavorful dish is perfect for a busy weeknight. You can prepare it in just 30 minutes, and it uses only one pan for easy cleanup. The combination of tender chicken, colorful bell peppers, and onions, seasoned with fajita spices, will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into strips
  • 2 bell peppers (red, yellow, or green), sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Flour tortillas
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, salsa, guacamole, shredded cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the chicken, bell peppers, onion, olive oil, chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the mixture evenly on a baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked.
  4. Serve the fajita mixture with warm tortillas and your favorite toppings.

Pro Tip: You can also serve this with a side of rice or beans for a more filling meal.

2. Easy Beef Stir-Fry

Stir-fry is a fantastic option for busy nights since it’s quick, healthy, and customizable. This easy beef stir-fry uses tender beef strips, mixed vegetables, and a savory sauce that comes together in just 15 minutes. It’s a meal that checks all the boxes: delicious, nutritious, and kid-friendly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the beef to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes until browned.
  4. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for another 30 seconds.
  5. Toss in the vegetables and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, or until they’re tender but still crisp.
  6. Pour the sauce over the beef and vegetables, stir to coat, and cook for another 2 minutes.
  7. Serve over cooked rice.

3. Sheet Pan Salmon with Vegetables

If you’re looking for a meal that’s both healthy and simple, this sheet pan salmon is the answer. You can roast the salmon and vegetables all at once for minimal prep and cleanup. The lemon and dill add fresh, zesty flavors, while the veggies provide a perfect balance.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill
  • 2 cups baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 cup baby carrots
  • 1 cup green beans
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a large sheet pan, arrange the potatoes, carrots, and green beans. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast the vegetables in the oven for 15 minutes.
  4. While the vegetables are roasting, season the salmon fillets with salt, pepper, dill, and place lemon slices on top.
  5. After the vegetables have roasted for 15 minutes, remove the pan from the oven and push the veggies to the sides to make space for the salmon.
  6. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through.
  7. Serve immediately, with extra lemon slices if desired.

Pro Tip: You can swap the vegetables for any seasonal favorites, like Brussels sprouts, zucchini, or asparagus.

4. Cheesy Baked Ziti

A classic comfort food, baked ziti is easy to make and perfect for feeding a family. This cheesy, saucy dish is always a hit with kids and adults alike. You can even prepare it ahead of time and bake it later when you’re ready for dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ziti pasta
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the ziti pasta according to package directions and drain.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked pasta, marinara sauce, ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella, Parmesan cheese, basil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish and top with the remaining mozzarella cheese.
  5. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes.
  6. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  7. Serve hot.

Pro Tip: For added protein, you can include cooked ground beef or sausage in the mix.

5. Chicken Alfredo Pasta

A creamy, rich pasta dish that the whole family will love, chicken Alfredo is a go-to meal that’s easy to prepare. It’s comforting, filling, and only takes 30 minutes to make from start to finish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 lb fettuccine pasta
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fettuccine pasta according to package directions and set aside.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. In a large skillet, cook the chicken over medium-high heat for 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked.
  3. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, melt the butter and sauté the garlic for 1 minute.
  4. Add the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and cook until the sauce thickens, about 5 minutes.
  5. Slice the chicken into strips and add it back to the skillet with the sauce.
  6. Toss the cooked fettuccine pasta into the sauce and chicken. Stir to combine.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve.

6. Vegetarian Taco Bowls

For a meatless meal that’s hearty and satisfying, try these vegetarian taco bowls. They’re packed with protein from black beans and quinoa, plus all your favorite taco toppings. Everyone in the family can customize their own bowl with their preferred ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon taco seasoning
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat the black beans and corn over medium heat. Stir in the taco seasoning and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  2. Assemble the taco bowls by layering cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, avocado, tomatoes, cheese, and sour cream.
  3. Serve with lime wedges for a fresh squeeze of citrus.

Pro Tip: Add some shredded lettuce or a drizzle of salsa to take these taco bowls to the next level.

7. Homemade Chicken Nuggets

Forget the frozen version—these homemade chicken nuggets are a healthier alternative, and they’re incredibly easy to make. Serve them with a side of veggies or fries for a complete dinner that kids will devour.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a shallow bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
  3. Dip the chicken pieces into the beaten eggs, then coat them in the breadcrumb mixture.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and golden brown.
  6. Serve with your favorite dipping sauces.

8. Turkey and Veggie Meatballs

These turkey meatballs are a great way to sneak in extra vegetables while still offering a familiar, kid-friendly dish. Serve them with spaghetti or in a sub sandwich for a quick dinner option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 zucchini, grated
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Marinara sauce for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, grated zucchini, carrot, breadcrumbs, Parmesan, egg, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roll the mixture into small meatballs and place them on a baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until fully cooked.
  5. Serve with marinara sauce and pasta or in a sandwich roll.

9. Quick Veggie Stir-Fry with Tofu

This vegetarian stir-fry is loaded with colorful vegetables and tofu, all tossed in a savory sauce. It’s a quick and healthy dinner option that’s both filling and nutritious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add tofu cubes and cook for 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown.
  2. Add the vegetables to the skillet and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, and rice vinegar.
  4. Pour the sauce over the tofu and veggies, toss to coat, and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Serve with cooked rice.

10. Easy Shrimp Scampi

Shrimp scampi is a classic dish that’s light, garlicky, and absolutely delicious. This recipe uses shrimp, pasta, garlic, and a simple lemon butter sauce for a dish that’s ready in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package directions and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add shrimp to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes until pink and cooked through.
  4. Stir in the lemon juice and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Toss the cooked pasta into the skillet and toss to coat in the sauce.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

These 10 dinner recipes are all about making family meals both easy and enjoyable. Whether you’re craving something hearty or light, vegetarian or meaty, these dishes have something for everyone. Plus, they’re designed to save you time without compromising on flavor or nutrition. Try them out for your next weeknight dinner and watch your family’s faces light up with delight!
