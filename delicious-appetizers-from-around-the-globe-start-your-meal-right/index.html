Delicious Appetizers from Around the Globe: Start Your Meal Right


Appetizers are an essential part of any meal, setting the tone for the flavors and textures that are to come. But why settle for the same old boring options when you can explore the world and discover new, exciting flavors? In this post, we’ll take you on a culinary journey around the globe, showcasing some of the most delicious and unique appetizers from different cultures.

Spain: Patatas Bravas

Patatas bravas, or “spicy potatoes,” are a classic Spanish tapas dish that’s easy to make and always a crowd-pleaser. Thinly slice 4-6 potatoes and fry them in hot oil until crispy. Then, drain the potatoes and toss them with a spicy tomato sauce made with garlic, smoked paprika, and a splash of vinegar. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley and a dollop of aioli.

Japan: Edamame

Edamame, or boiled soybeans, are a popular appetizer in Japan, often served at sushi restaurants. Simply boil 1 cup of edamame in salted water until tender, then season with sea salt and a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve warm, accompanied by a side of soy sauce and wasabi for dipping.

India: Samosas

Samosas are crispy, savory pastries filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and onions. To make them, mix 1 cup of mashed potatoes with 1/2 cup of cooked peas, 1 onion, and 1 teaspoon of cumin. Wrap the filling in a thin layer of dough and fry until golden brown. Serve hot with a side of chutney or raita (a yogurt and cucumber sauce).

Mexico: Guacamole

Guacamole is a classic Mexican dip made with avocados, onions, and lime juice. Mash 3 ripe avocados with 1/2 red onion, 1 lime, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Stir in 1 minced garlic clove and 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro. Serve with tortilla chips or crudités.

Thailand: Spring Rolls

Spring rolls are a popular appetizer in Thailand, filled with shrimp, vegetables, and noodles. To make them, mix 1 cup of cooked and peeled shrimp with 1/2 cup of chopped cabbage, 1/2 cup of chopped carrots, and 1/4 cup of cooked rice noodles. Wrap the filling in a thin layer of dough and fry until golden brown. Serve with a sweet chili sauce.

Morocco: B’stilla

B’stilla is a savory pastry from Morocco, filled with layers of flaky dough and a mixture of onions, almonds, and spices. To make it, mix 1 cup of chopped onions with 1/2 cup of chopped almonds, 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, and 1/2 teaspoon of ground cumin. Layer the filling with flaky dough and bake until golden brown. Serve warm, garnished with powdered sugar and a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Greece: Spanakopita

Spanakopita is a classic Greek appetizer, filled with spinach, feta cheese, and herbs. To make it, mix 1 cup of chopped spinach with 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley, and 1/4 cup of chopped fresh dill. Add 1/2 teaspoon of salt and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper, then mix well. Wrap the filling in a thin layer of dough and bake until golden brown. Serve warm, garnished with a dollop of yogurt and a sprinkle of paprika.

Brazil: Pão de Queijo

Pão de queijo is a crispy, cheesy bread from Brazil, perfect for dipping in soups or olive oil. To make it, mix 2 cups of flour with 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Add 1/2 cup of warm water and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 5 minutes, then shape into small balls and fry until golden brown. Serve warm, garnished with a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.

These are just a few examples of the delicious and unique appetizers you can find around the world. Whether you’re looking for something spicy, savory, or sweet, there’s an appetizer out there to suit your taste. So why not start your meal right and explore the flavors of the globe?
