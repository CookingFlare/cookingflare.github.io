Char Siu Bao: Soft and Fluffy Chinese BBQ Pork Buns

3 min · 574 words

If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, you’ve probably encountered Char Siu Bao at dim sum restaurants or local bakeries. These delightful buns, filled with savory and sweet barbecue pork, are a staple in Cantonese cuisine and are loved by many around the world. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of making these soft and fluffy Chinese BBQ pork buns right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of active dry yeast
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound of boneless pork shoulder
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 1 tablespoon of dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine
  • 1 teaspoon of five-spice powder
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon of white pepper
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch

Instructions

Making the Filling:

  1. Start by preparing the barbecue pork filling. Cut the pork shoulder into small chunks and marinate it in a mixture of garlic, hoisin sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, honey, dark soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, five-spice powder, sesame oil, and white pepper. Let the pork marinate for at least 2 hours or preferably overnight for the best flavor.

  2. After marinating, transfer the pork and the marinade to a pot. Add water and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the pork is cooked through.

  3. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a few tablespoons of water to create a slurry. Add the slurry to the pork mixture and stir until the sauce thickens. Once thickened, remove the pork from the heat and set it aside to cool.

Making the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, and salt. In a separate bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. Add the yeast mixture and vegetable oil to the dry ingredients and mix until a dough starts to form. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  3. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  4. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Transfer it back to the floured surface and knead in the baking powder. Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.

Assembling the Buns:

  1. Take each dough ball and roll it out into a circle, about 4 inches in diameter. Place a spoonful of the barbecue pork filling in the center of each circle.

  2. Gather the edges of the dough circle and pleat them together to encase the filling, then twist and seal the top. Place each assembled bun on a small square of parchment paper.

  3. Arrange the buns in a steamer basket, leaving some space between them to allow for expansion. Cover the steamer and let the buns proof for about 15 minutes.

  4. Steam the buns over high heat for 12-15 minutes, or until they are puffed and cooked through.

Serving and Enjoying

Once steamed, these Char Siu Bao are ready to be enjoyed. The soft and fluffy buns with the juicy and flavorful barbecue pork filling are a true delight for the senses. They can be served as a snack, appetizer, or as part of a larger meal alongside other Chinese dishes.

With this recipe, you can bring the authentic flavors of Chinese cuisine into your own kitchen, impressing your family and friends with these delicious Char Siu Bao. So, roll up your sleeves, and let’s get cooking!
