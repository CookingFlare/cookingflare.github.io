Korean Cabbage Salad (Yangnyeom Geotjeori) Recipe


Korean cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors, colors, and textures, and one dish that beautifully encapsulates this diversity is Yangnyeom Geotjeori, or Korean Cabbage Salad. This refreshing salad is not only a staple in Korean households but also a delightful addition to any meal. With its crunchy cabbage, zesty dressing, and a medley of spices, Yangnyeom Geotjeori is a perfect side dish that complements a variety of main courses. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of this dish, its health benefits, and provide you with a step-by-step recipe to create your own delicious Korean Cabbage Salad at home.

The Origins of Yangnyeom Geotjeori

Yangnyeom Geotjeori is a traditional Korean salad that has been enjoyed for generations. The term “geotjeori” refers to a type of salad made with fresh vegetables, often seasoned with a spicy and tangy dressing. The word “yangnyeom” translates to “seasoned,” highlighting the dish’s flavorful profile. This salad is particularly popular during the summer months when fresh vegetables are abundant, making it a refreshing choice for hot days.

The roots of Yangnyeom Geotjeori can be traced back to the Korean countryside, where families would prepare simple yet flavorful dishes using locally sourced ingredients. Over time, this dish has evolved, incorporating various spices and seasonings that reflect the regional diversity of Korean cuisine. Today, Yangnyeom Geotjeori is enjoyed not only in Korea but also around the world, thanks to the growing popularity of Korean food.

Health Benefits of Cabbage

Cabbage, the star ingredient of Yangnyeom Geotjeori, is a powerhouse of nutrition. This cruciferous vegetable is low in calories and high in fiber, making it an excellent choice for those looking to maintain a healthy diet. Here are some of the key health benefits of cabbage:

  1. Rich in Nutrients: Cabbage is packed with vitamins C and K, as well as essential minerals like potassium and manganese. These nutrients play a vital role in maintaining overall health.

  2. Supports Digestive Health: The high fiber content in cabbage promotes healthy digestion and can help prevent constipation. Additionally, the antioxidants found in cabbage may support gut health.

  3. Anti-Inflammatory Properties: Cabbage contains compounds that have anti-inflammatory effects, which can help reduce the risk of chronic diseases.

  4. Boosts Immune System: The vitamin C in cabbage is known to enhance the immune system, helping the body fight off infections and illnesses.

  5. Weight Management: Due to its low calorie and high fiber content, cabbage can be a great addition to a weight loss diet, helping you feel full without consuming too many calories.

Ingredients for Yangnyeom Geotjeori

To make a delicious Yangnyeom Geotjeori, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Salad:

  • 1 medium head of Napa cabbage (about 1 pound)
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1 small cucumber, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (for garnish)

For the Dressing:

  • 3 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (or vegan alternative)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (or honey)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Step-by-Step Recipe for Yangnyeom Geotjeori

Step 1: Prepare the Cabbage

  1. Wash the Cabbage: Start by rinsing the Napa cabbage under cold water to remove any dirt or impurities. Shake off excess water.

  2. Chop the Cabbage: Cut the cabbage into quarters and remove the core. Then, slice the cabbage into bite-sized pieces. You can also shred it if you prefer a finer texture.

  3. Salt the Cabbage: Place the chopped cabbage in a large bowl and sprinkle with a generous amount of salt. Massage the salt into the cabbage for a few minutes until it begins to soften. This process helps to draw out excess moisture and enhances the flavor of the salad.

  4. Let it Sit: Allow the salted cabbage to sit for about 15-20 minutes. This will help it wilt slightly and become more tender.

Step 2: Prepare the Dressing

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the gochugaru, soy sauce, fish sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, minced garlic, grated ginger, and sesame oil. Adjust the seasoning to taste, adding more sugar for sweetness or more gochugaru for heat.

  2. Mix Well
