Savory Greek Spinach and Cheese Pie for Any Occasion


When it comes to the world of culinary delights, few dishes can hold a candle to the delectable experience of a traditional Greek spinach and cheese pie, also known as “Spanakopita.” This savory pastry has captured the hearts and taste buds of many, making it a beloved staple in Greek cuisine. Perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a family gathering, a holiday feast, or a casual get-together with friends, Spanakopita is sure to impress.

The Rich History of Spanakopita

Spanakopita has a rich history that dates back centuries. The Greeks have long enjoyed combining fresh greens and cheeses into flaky pastries. The origins of this dish can be traced to ancient Greece, where it was often prepared with wild greens and various types of cheese. Over the years, Spanakopita has evolved, with spinach becoming the star ingredient. This dish is not just about flavor; it embodies the essence of Mediterranean cooking, emphasizing fresh, local ingredients and simple preparation methods.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Creating a delicious Spanakopita requires a thoughtful selection of ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to whip up this savory pie:

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound fresh spinach (or 10 oz frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried dill)
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Pastry:

  • 1 package phyllo dough (16 oz), thawed
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted (or olive oil for a lighter version)

Optional:

  • Sesame seeds, for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Spinach

If you’re using fresh spinach, thoroughly wash and dry the leaves. Remove any thick stems and chop the spinach coarsely. In a large skillet over medium heat, add a tablespoon of olive oil. Once hot, sauté the chopped onion until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional minute until fragrant.

Next, add the spinach to the skillet. If using fresh spinach, you may need to do this in batches, allowing each batch to wilt before adding more. Cook until all the spinach is wilted and tender, approximately 5-7 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

Step 2: Make the Filling

In a large mixing bowl, combine the sautéed spinach and onions with the crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, chopped dill, and beaten eggs. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Be mindful of the saltiness of the feta cheese, as it can add significant flavor. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly incorporated.

Step 3: Prepare the Phyllo Dough

Phyllo dough can be delicate, so it’s essential to handle it with care. Lay out a clean kitchen towel on your work surface and cover it with a damp cloth to keep it from drying out.

Take a sheet of phyllo dough and place it on a greased baking dish, brushing it lightly with melted butter. Repeat this process, layering about 6-8 sheets, ensuring to butter each layer to create a crispy and flaky texture.

Step 4: Assemble the Pie

Once you have prepared the base with the phyllo dough, spoon the spinach and cheese filling evenly over the layered phyllo. Spread it out evenly and then fold the edges of the phyllo over the filling. For a decorative touch, you can layer additional sheets of phyllo dough on top, brushing them with butter as well.

Step 5: Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Once the oven is ready, place the assembled Spanakopita in the oven and bake for 30-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy. The aroma that fills your kitchen will be irresistible!

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Allow the Spanakopita to cool for a few minutes before slicing it into squares or wedges. This dish can be served warm or at room temperature, making it an ideal appetizer, side dish, or even a light main course. For an extra touch, sprinkle sesame seeds on top before serving.

Tips for the Perfect Spanakopita

  • Experiment with Greens: While spinach is traditional, feel free to mix in other greens like Swiss chard, kale, or even herbs for added flavor.
  • Cheese Variations: If you want to try different flavors, you can substitute some of the feta with goat cheese or mozzarella.
  • Phyllo Handling: Keep any unused phyllo dough covered with a damp cloth to prevent it from drying out while you work.
  • Freezing: You can prepare Spanakopita ahead of time and freeze it before baking. Just make sure to wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and foil. When ready to enjoy, bake from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

Pairing Suggestions

Spanakopita is incredibly versatile and pairs well with various dishes. Consider serving it alongside:

  • A fresh Greek salad with tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, and a tangy vinaigrette.
  • A creamy tzatziki sauce for a refreshing dip.
  • Roasted vegetables or a simple side of sautéed greens for a balanced meal.

Conclusion

Savory Greek spinach and cheese pie is more than just a dish; it’s a celebration of flavors, history, and culinary craftsmanship. Whether you’re hosting a festive occasion or simply looking for a delicious meal to enjoy with loved ones, Spanakopita is sure to be a crowd-pleaser. Its flaky pastry, flavorful filling, and beautiful presentation make it a wonderful addition to any table.

So why not roll up your sleeves and give this recipe a try? You might just find that it becomes a new favorite in your culinary repertoire. Happy cooking!
