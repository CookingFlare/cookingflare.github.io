Easy Nut-Free Banana Bread Recipe for Breakfast


Are you looking for a delicious and healthy breakfast option? Look no further! This easy nut-free banana bread recipe is not only packed with flavor, but it’s also perfect for those with nut allergies. Whether you enjoy it as a quick on-the-go breakfast or a tasty afternoon snack, this banana bread will surely satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed bananas, melted butter, sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring just until combined. Be careful not to overmix as it can result in a dense banana bread.

  4. Fold in the plain yogurt, ensuring it is evenly distributed throughout the batter. The addition of yogurt adds moisture to the banana bread, making it extra tender and flavorful.

  5. Pour the batter into the greased loaf pan, spreading it out evenly. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles.

  6. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 55-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The top of the banana bread should be golden brown.

  7. Once baked, remove the banana bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

  8. Slice the nut-free banana bread into thick, irresistible slices. Enjoy it on its own or spread a little butter or cream cheese for an extra indulgence.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a bit of crunch, you can add 1/2 cup of chopped walnuts or pecans to the batter. Just remember to omit this step if you want to keep it nut-free.

  • For a touch of sweetness, sprinkle some cinnamon sugar on top of the banana bread before baking. It will create a delicious caramelized crust that will leave you craving for more.

  • To store the banana bread, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil and keep it at room temperature for up to 3 days. You can also freeze it for longer storage.

  • This recipe is versatile, and you can experiment with different additions such as chocolate chips, blueberries, or even shredded coconut. Let your imagination run wild!

So, there you have it! A simple and scrumptious nut-free banana bread recipe that will surely become a breakfast favorite. With its moist texture and sweet banana flavor, it’s the perfect way to start your day. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is foolproof and guaranteed to impress. So, grab those overripe bananas and get baking!
