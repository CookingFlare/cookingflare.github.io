Paleo-Friendly Crockpot Soups and Stews for Cold Days


When the weather turns chilly, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of soup or stew to lift your spirits and satisfy your hunger. But for those following a paleo diet, finding recipes that are both hearty and compliant can sometimes be a challenge. Luckily, the crockpot (or slow cooker) comes to the rescue, making it easier than ever to prepare paleo-friendly meals that are packed with flavor and nutrition. These soups and stews are perfect for those cold days when you want something warm and filling, but don’t want to spend hours in the kitchen. Plus, they’re incredibly easy to prepare and cook.

In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best paleo-friendly crockpot soups and stews that will keep you cozy through the coldest days. Each recipe is filled with fresh, wholesome ingredients like lean meats, vegetables, herbs, and healthy fats—exactly what the paleo diet calls for. So grab your slow cooker, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into these mouthwatering recipes!

1. Paleo Beef Stew

Beef stew is a classic comfort food, but traditional versions often contain ingredients that are not paleo-friendly, like flour for thickening. Thankfully, this paleo-friendly version relies on sweet potatoes to add texture and richness, making it thick and satisfying without any grains.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat (grass-fed)
  • 4 cups beef broth (make sure it’s free from additives and gluten)
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and sear the beef on all sides until browned. Transfer the beef to the crockpot.
  2. Combine the ingredients: Add the sweet potatoes, carrots, celery, onion, and garlic to the crockpot with the beef. Pour in the beef broth and sprinkle the thyme, rosemary, bay leaf, salt, and pepper over the top.
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on low for 7-8 hours or on high for 4-5 hours, until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  4. Serve: Remove the bay leaf before serving. Ladle the stew into bowls and enjoy the comforting, hearty flavors.

This beef stew is a great option for a filling dinner. It’s packed with protein, fiber, and healthy fats, and the slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together perfectly.

2. Chicken and Vegetable Soup

This chicken and vegetable soup is perfect for anyone looking for a light but satisfying meal. It’s loaded with nutrient-dense vegetables and lean protein from the chicken, making it an excellent choice for a paleo-friendly dinner or lunch.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 6 cups chicken broth (preferably homemade or free from preservatives)
  • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the chicken: Place the chicken breasts in the bottom of the crockpot. Pour the chicken broth over the chicken and add the oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add vegetables: Add the zucchini, green beans, carrots, onion, and garlic to the crockpot. Stir everything together.
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours. About 30 minutes before serving, shred the chicken with two forks and stir it back into the soup.
  4. Serve: Remove the bay leaf before serving. Enjoy a hearty bowl of chicken and vegetable soup that’s both filling and nourishing.

This soup is a perfect option for meal prep. It stores well in the fridge for up to 4 days and tastes even better the next day when the flavors have had time to develop.

3. Paleo-Friendly Chili

Chili is a favorite for many during the colder months, but traditional chili often contains beans, which are not paleo-approved. This paleo-friendly version skips the beans but is still packed with flavor and texture. It’s hearty, warming, and full of delicious spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (grass-fed)
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups diced tomatoes (no sugar added)
  • 1 cup tomato paste (make sure it’s free from additives)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Brown the meat: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and turkey and cook, breaking up the meat as it browns. Once the meat is browned, transfer it to the crockpot.
  2. Add the vegetables: In the same skillet, sauté the onion, bell pepper, and garlic for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the sautéed vegetables to the crockpot with the meat.
  3. Add the rest of the ingredients: Pour in the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, beef broth, and all the spices. Stir to combine.
  4. Cook: Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours. The chili should be thick and flavorful.
  5. Serve: Ladle the chili into bowls and enjoy. This chili is perfect on its own or served with a side of avocado for some extra creamy texture.

This chili is ideal for a crowd and is great for leftovers. The flavors intensify as it sits, and it’s even better the next day.

4. Paleo Sweet Potato and Sausage Stew

This sweet potato and sausage stew is perfect for those who want a savory, filling meal that’s still packed with healthy, paleo-approved ingredients. The sausage adds a nice bit of flavor and spice, while the sweet potatoes bring natural sweetness and texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb paleo sausage (look for sausage with no added sugar or preservatives)
  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cups chicken broth (make sure it’s free from additives)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups spinach, chopped
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Brown the sausage: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the sausage and cook, breaking it apart as it browns. Once browned, transfer the sausage to the crockpot.
  2. Add vegetables: Add the sweet potatoes, onion, and garlic to the crockpot. Pour in the chicken broth and stir everything together.
  3. Cook: Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Add spinach: About 15 minutes before serving, stir in the chopped spinach and allow it to wilt into the stew.
  5. Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. This stew is perfect for a chilly evening and offers a balance of savory, sweet, and hearty flavors.

This stew is perfect for a hearty meal after a long day. It’s packed with vegetables, healthy fats, and lean protein, making it both nutritious and filling.

5. Paleo Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash soup is a classic fall and winter dish, but it’s also a great choice for the paleo diet due to its natural sweetness and creamy texture. This version is made entirely in the crockpot, making it an easy and delicious option for busy days.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth for a vegetarian version)
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Add the butternut squash, onion, and garlic to the crockpot. Pour in the chicken broth and stir in the ginger, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  2. Cook: Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the squash is tender.
  3. Blend: Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth and creamy. If you prefer a thicker consistency, you can leave it slightly chunky or add more broth to achieve the desired texture.
  4. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and enjoy. This soup is naturally sweet, creamy, and full of flavor.

Butternut squash soup is great for meal prepping and can be stored in the refrigerator for up to four days. It also freezes well if you want to make a larger batch to enjoy later.

Conclusion

There’s something truly special about a slow-cooked soup or stew, especially on a cold day. Whether you’re craving a rich and hearty beef stew, a light and flavorful chicken soup, or a cozy bowl of sweet potato and sausage stew, these paleo-friendly crockpot recipes are the perfect solution. The beauty of slow-cooking is that it allows the ingredients to meld together beautifully, creating rich, deep flavors with minimal effort.

These recipes offer a variety of flavors, textures, and nutrients to keep you warm and satisfied while staying true to your paleo lifestyle. They’re ideal for meal prepping, as they store well in the fridge and can even be frozen for later use. So next time you’re looking for a comforting, nutritious meal on a cold day, reach for your crockpot and try one of these delicious paleo soups and stews.

Happy cooking!
