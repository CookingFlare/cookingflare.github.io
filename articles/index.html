Vegan Peanut Butter and Jelly Bars Recipe with a Graham Cracker Crust and a Crunchy Topping

If you’re a fan of the classic peanut butter and jelly combination, then you’re in for a treat with this vegan twist on a beloved favorite. These Vegan Peanut Butter and Jelly Bars with a Graham Cracker Crust and a Crunchy Topping are the perfect blend of sweet, salty, and crunchy, making them an irresistible dessert or snack option for vegans and non-vegans alike. Ingredients For the Graham Cracker Crust: 2 cups vegan graham cracker crumbs 1/4 cup coconut sugar 1/2 cup melted coconut oil 1/4 teaspoon salt For the Peanut Butter Filling: 1 1/2 cups creamy peanut butter 1/2 cup maple syrup 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 teaspoon salt For the Jelly Layer: 1 1/2 cups of your favorite fruit jelly or jam For the Crunchy Topping: 1 cup rolled oats 1/2 cup chopped peanuts 1/4 cup maple syrup 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted 1/4 teaspoon salt Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Graham Cracker Crust Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper. In a mixing bowl, combine the vegan graham cracker crumbs, coconut sugar, melted coconut oil, and salt. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated. Press the crumb mixture firmly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside to cool. Step 2: Make the Peanut Butter Filling In a microwave-safe bowl, heat the creamy peanut butter, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth and well combined. Pour the peanut butter filling over the cooled graham cracker crust and spread it into an even layer. Step 3: Add the Jelly Layer Gently warm the fruit jelly or jam in a small saucepan over low heat until it becomes easier to spread. Carefully spread the jelly over the peanut butter layer, creating an even layer of jelly. Step 4: Prepare the Crunchy Topping In a mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, chopped peanuts, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, and salt. Stir until all the ingredients are well combined. Sprinkle the crunchy topping evenly over the jelly layer. Step 5: Bake and Cool Bake the assembled bars for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown. Remove the pan from the oven and allow the bars to cool completely at room temperature, then transfer to the refrigerator to chill for at least 2 hours before slicing. Serving and Storing Once chilled, lift the bars out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang and transfer them to a cutting board. Use a sharp knife to slice the bars into squares or rectangles. Vegan Apple Pie Recipe with a Flaky Crust and a Whipped Coconut Cream

There’s nothing quite like the aroma of a freshly baked pie wafting through the house. And when it’s a vegan apple pie with a flaky crust and a dollop of whipped coconut cream, the experience is simply divine. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a newbie in the kitchen, this recipe is a must-try for anyone looking to indulge in a delicious, plant-based dessert. Ingredients For the Crust: 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour 1 cup vegan butter, chilled and cubed 1 teaspoon salt 6-8 tablespoons ice water For the Filling: 6-7 medium-sized apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup brown sugar 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon salt 3 tablespoons cornstarch For the Whipped Coconut Cream: 1 can (14 oz) coconut cream, chilled overnight 1-2 tablespoons powdered sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Instructions Making the Crust: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the chilled vegan butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Divide the dough into two equal parts, shape each into a disc, wrap them in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Preparing the Filling: In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning. In a separate bowl, mix the granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and cornstarch. Add the sugar mixture to the apples and toss until the apples are evenly coated. Assembling the Pie: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). On a floured surface, roll out one disc of the chilled dough to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the prepared apple filling. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie. Use a sharp knife to make a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking. Optional: Brush the top crust with a little non-dairy milk and sprinkle with sugar for a golden finish. Baking the Pie: Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. If the edges of the pie start to brown too quickly, cover them with foil and continue baking. Making the Whipped Coconut Cream: Open the chilled can of coconut cream and scoop the solidified cream into a mixing bowl, leaving behind any remaining liquid. Add powdered sugar and vanilla extract to the cream. Use a hand mixer or a whisk to whip the cream until it becomes light and fluffy. Serving the Pie: Allow the pie to cool for at least 30 minutes before slicing. Serve slices of the vegan apple pie with a generous dollop of whipped coconut cream on top. Enjoy the delightful combination of sweet, spiced apples, flaky crust, and creamy coconut goodness. Final Thoughts This vegan apple pie with a flaky crust and whipped coconut cream is a true crowd-pleaser, perfect for any occasion, from holiday gatherings to casual weeknight desserts. The buttery, flaky crust pairs beautifully with the warm, cinnamon-spiced apple filling, and the whipped coconut cream adds a delightful creaminess that will leave everyone coming back for seconds. ...
