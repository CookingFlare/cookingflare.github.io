5 Classic Ice Cream Recipes Everyone Should Know


Few things delight the taste buds like a bowl of ice cream on a warm day—or any day, for that matter. Rich, creamy, and refreshingly sweet, ice cream isn’t just a dessert; it’s an experience. While there are endless varieties to explore, mastering a handful of timeless recipes can elevate your dessert repertoire and ensure there’s always something to please your guests or indulge your sweet tooth.

Below, we’ll dive into five classic ice cream recipes every home cook should know, from velvety vanilla to decadent chocolate, fruity strawberry, nutty pistachio, and dreamy mint chocolate chip. Let’s break down step-by-step how to make these favorites and explore tips for perfecting textures and flavors.

1. Timeless Vanilla Ice Cream

Vanilla ice cream is a staple of any dessert menu. Its smooth, creamy profile pairs perfectly with pies, cakes, or eaten on its own. A homemade version of this classic starts with simple ingredients, yet the quality of those ingredients is what makes all the difference.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 6 large egg yolks

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, and half the sugar over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  2. Whisk the Yolks: In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks and the remaining sugar until smooth.
  3. Temper the Yolks: Gradually add 1 cup of the hot milk mixture to the yolks while whisking vigorously. This prevents the egg yolks from curdling.
  4. Cook the Custard: Pour the tempered yolks back into the saucepan. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens to coat the back of a spoon. Remove from heat.
  5. Cool and Add Vanilla: Once cooled, stir in the vanilla extract. Cover and refrigerate for 4 hours or overnight.
  6. Churn: Pour the custard into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Freeze for 1-2 hours before serving.

This recipe is the foundation for countless variations. Add mix-ins like cookie crumbles or caramel swirls to transform it into something spectacular.

2. Rich Chocolate Ice Cream

Chocolate ice cream satisfies the deepest cravings for something rich and indulgent. Its intense flavor comes from using cocoa powder and melted dark chocolate, creating a depth that store-bought versions often lack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 ounces dark chocolate, melted
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a saucepan, mix the heavy cream, milk, sugar, and cocoa powder, whisking until smooth.
  2. Heat to Melt the Chocolate: Warm the mixture over medium heat until the cocoa dissolves. Stir in the melted dark chocolate until fully combined.
  3. Temper and Cook: Similar to the vanilla recipe, temper the egg yolks with the hot chocolate mixture, then return to the saucepan and cook until thickened.
  4. Add Vanilla and Chill: Remove from heat, stir in the vanilla extract, and allow the chocolate base to cool completely before refrigeration.
  5. Churn and Freeze: Once chilled, churn in an ice cream maker. Freeze until firm.

Chocolate ice cream can be elevated by incorporating chunks of brownie, chocolate chips, or even a swirl of marshmallow cream.

3. Fresh Strawberry Ice Cream

Strawberry ice cream is the essence of summer. Its bright, fruity flavor and creamy texture make it the perfect treat for sunny days. Fresh strawberries are key to giving this ice cream its vibrant taste.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • ¾ cup sugar, divided
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Macerate the Strawberries: Toss the sliced strawberries in ¼ cup of sugar and let them sit for 20 minutes to release their juices.
  2. Blend and Strain: Puree the strawberries in a blender until smooth. For extra smooth ice cream, strain the puree to remove seeds.
  3. Prepare the Base: Heat the heavy cream, milk, and remaining sugar in a saucepan until the sugar dissolves. Remove from heat.
  4. Combine Flavors: Stir the strawberry puree and lemon juice into the cream mixture. Chill the mixture overnight.
  5. Churn: Pour the base into your ice cream maker and churn until creamy.

Add diced strawberries or ribbons of strawberry jam during the churning process for extra bursts of flavor in every bite.

4. Classic Pistachio Ice Cream

Pistachio ice cream is a nutty, luxurious treat with a distinct flavor profile that sets it apart from other classics. Derived from roasted pistachios, this dish provides a delightful mix of creamy sweetness and earthy nuttiness.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shelled pistachios, unsalted
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 5 egg yolks
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract
  • Green food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the Pistachios: Toast the pistachios in the oven for 5 minutes at 375°F. Grind them coarsely or chop into small pieces.
  2. Prepare the Custard: Heat the cream, milk, and half the sugar in a saucepan. Meanwhile, whisk the egg yolks and remaining sugar in a separate bowl.
  3. Temper and Cook: Like the previous recipes, temper the yolks, then return the mixture to the saucepan and cook until thickened.
  4. Flavor the Ice Cream: Stir in the almond extract and pistachio pieces. Add food coloring for visual appeal if desired. Chill overnight.
  5. Churn and Serve: Churn the base in your ice cream machine and freeze until it’s ready to scoop.

Pistachio is best enjoyed topped with more crushed pistachios or alongside a drizzle of honey.

5. Mint Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

Mint chocolate chip boasts the perfect blend of refreshing mint and sweet, crunchy chocolate chips. Its light, cool flavor is a favorite for many, especially during warmer months.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • ¾ cup mini chocolate chips
  • Green food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cream Base: Heat the cream, milk, and sugar until fully dissolved.
  2. Temper and Cook: Whisk the egg yolks into the cream mixture, following the tempering method outlined earlier. Cook until it thickens slightly.
  3. Flavor with Mint: Remove from heat and stir in the peppermint extract. Add green food coloring if desired. Chill overnight.
  4. Add Chocolate Chips: During the churning process, stir in the mini chocolate chips so they are evenly distributed.
  5. Freeze: Transfer to a container and freeze until firm.

Mint chocolate chip can be customized with dark chocolate, white chocolate, or even chunks of peppermint candy for added texture.

Tips for Perfecting Homemade Ice Cream:

  1. Invest in Quality Ingredients: The taste and texture of homemade ice cream rely heavily on the quality of your milk, cream, and flavorings.
  2. Don’t Skip the Chilling: Allowing your base to chill overnight ensures a smoother, richer ice cream.
  3. Churn Properly: Avoid over-churning, which can make ice cream icy rather than creamy.
  4. Store Correctly: Use an airtight container to prevent freezer burn and maintain flavor.
  5. Get Creative: Experiment with mix-ins, swirls, and even spices to make each recipe your own.

Whether you’re entertaining guests or treating yourself, homemade ice cream is always a crowd-pleaser. These five classics are a must-know for any dessert enthusiast, offering endless opportunities for customization and joy. Once you’ve mastered the basics, there’s no limit to the delicious variations you can create—so scoop, savor, and share!
