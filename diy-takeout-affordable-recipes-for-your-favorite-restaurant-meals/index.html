DIY Takeout: Affordable Recipes for Your Favorite Restaurant Meals


There’s nothing like the joy of ordering takeout and enjoying a delicious meal in the comfort of your own home. Whether it’s the crispy chicken wings from your local pub, the creamy pasta from an Italian bistro, or the perfectly seasoned stir-fry from a beloved Asian restaurant, we all have our favorite takeout spots. However, takeout can quickly add up in cost, and sometimes it’s just not feasible to order from a restaurant as often as we’d like.

But what if you could recreate your favorite restaurant meals at home, on your own terms, and without breaking the bank? With a little creativity and some cooking know-how, it’s easier than you think to make affordable, restaurant-quality meals right in your own kitchen. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best DIY takeout recipes for your favorite dishes, giving you the satisfaction of a delicious meal and the savings that come with cooking at home.

Why DIY Takeout?

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s explore the benefits of making your favorite restaurant meals at home.

  1. Cost Savings
    Takeout can be expensive, especially when you’re feeding a family. The prices at restaurants are often inflated to cover overhead costs like rent, utilities, and staffing. By cooking at home, you can cut those costs dramatically while still enjoying the same flavorful meals.

  2. Healthier Options
    When you cook your meals, you have full control over the ingredients. This means you can opt for healthier alternatives without sacrificing flavor. You can choose to reduce sodium, control the amount of oil used, and incorporate more fresh ingredients into your dishes.

  3. Customizable Flavors
    One of the best things about making your own takeout is that you can adjust the recipes to suit your taste preferences. Do you love a dish extra spicy? Or maybe you prefer your stir-fry with less soy sauce? At home, it’s up to you to make those changes, ensuring you get exactly what you want.

  4. Satisfying Creativity
    Cooking at home can be a fun, creative activity. Experimenting with flavors, trying new ingredients, and perfecting your technique can give you a real sense of accomplishment. Plus, it’s always a bonus to wow your friends and family with a home-cooked version of a restaurant classic.

Affordable DIY Takeout Recipes

Now that we’ve covered why cooking your favorite takeout meals at home is a great idea, let’s dive into some affordable and easy-to-make recipes. From Asian-inspired dishes to classic American comfort food, these recipes will satisfy your cravings without the hefty takeout price tag.

1. Chicken Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a popular Thai street food that combines stir-fried rice noodles with a flavorful sauce, chicken, shrimp, or tofu, and a mix of fresh vegetables. While it may seem complicated, this dish is surprisingly easy to make at home.

Ingredients

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 spring onions, sliced
  • 1/4 cup peanuts, crushed (optional)

For the sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (adjust to taste)

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions, then set aside.
  2. In a large wok or skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove from the wok and set aside.
  3. In the same wok, add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Push the onion and garlic to the side of the pan, and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble them until fully cooked, then mix them with the onions and garlic.
  5. Add the cooked rice noodles to the pan and pour in the Pad Thai sauce. Toss everything together to coat the noodles evenly.
  6. Stir in the cooked chicken, bean sprouts, and spring onions. Cook for another 2 minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.
  7. Serve with crushed peanuts (if using) and a wedge of lime on the side. Enjoy!

2. Crispy Chicken Tenders

Who doesn’t love crispy chicken tenders? Whether you’re dipping them in honey mustard, BBQ sauce, or ketchup, these crispy bites are a takeout favorite. The best part? You can make them at home using affordable ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs (preferably panko)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Slice the chicken breasts into tenders (about 1-inch thick).
  2. In a shallow bowl, combine the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. In another shallow bowl, beat the eggs.
  4. Place the breadcrumbs in a third shallow bowl.
  5. Dredge each chicken tender first in the flour mixture, then dip in the egg, and finally coat in the breadcrumbs.
  6. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  7. Fry the chicken tenders in batches for 4-5 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through.
  8. Remove the chicken from the skillet and place on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  9. Serve with your favorite dipping sauces. Enjoy your homemade crispy chicken tenders!

3. Vegetable Stir-Fry with Tofu

If you’re craving a healthy, quick, and satisfying dish, a vegetable stir-fry is the perfect answer. Packed with colorful vegetables and protein-rich tofu, this dish is both nourishing and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup carrots, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (for the sauce)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

Instructions

  1. Press the tofu to remove excess water, then cut it into cubes.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of soy sauce and sesame oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the tofu cubes and sauté until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add the minced ginger and garlic, sautéing for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the broccoli, bell pepper, onion, snow peas, and carrots to the skillet. Stir-fry for about 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the remaining soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, and cornstarch (if using).
  7. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and toss everything together to coat evenly.
  8. Return the tofu to the skillet and mix well. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to heat everything through.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice. Enjoy your veggie-packed stir-fry!

4. Classic Margherita Pizza

Pizza is one of the most popular takeout items, and a classic Margherita pizza is always a crowd-pleaser. Making pizza at home allows you to control the toppings, and it’s a great way to bond with friends or family.

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough ball (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt, to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C) and place a pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven to heat.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Remove the hot pizza stone or baking sheet from the oven and transfer the dough to it.
  4. Spread the pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.
  5. Layer the mozzarella slices on top of the sauce.
  6. Drizzle olive oil over the pizza and sprinkle with salt.
  7. Bake the pizza for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  8. Remove the pizza from the oven and top with fresh basil leaves.
  9. Slice and serve with a sprinkle of red pepper flakes if you like a bit of heat. Enjoy!

Final Thoughts

Creating your favorite restaurant meals at home is not only a fun and rewarding experience, but it also saves you money and allows you to control the ingredients. Whether you’re making a flavorful stir-fry, crispy chicken tenders, or a classic pizza, DIY takeout recipes offer endless possibilities for delicious and affordable meals. So, next time you’re craving your favorite takeout dish, consider whipping it up in your own kitchen—you might just discover that homemade is the best way to go!
