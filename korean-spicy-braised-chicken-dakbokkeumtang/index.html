Korean Spicy Braised Chicken (Dakbokkeumtang)


Korean cuisine has an incredible way of blending flavors, creating unique dishes that are savory, spicy, and satisfying. One of the most popular dishes in Korean homes and restaurants is Dakbokkeumtang, a spicy, savory braised chicken dish that will awaken your taste buds. Known for its rich broth, tender chicken, and balance of spicy and savory elements, Dakbokkeumtang (닭볶음탕) is a comforting meal perfect for any occasion.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make this iconic dish at home, offering you the chance to experience a taste of Korea without leaving your kitchen. With a few simple ingredients and a little patience, you can create a meal that’s sure to impress friends and family alike.

What is Dakbokkeumtang?

Dakbokkeumtang is a traditional Korean dish made with chicken that is braised in a spicy, savory sauce. The dish is typically served with vegetables, such as potatoes, carrots, and onions, which absorb the rich flavors of the sauce during cooking. It’s a hearty, filling dish that has become a favorite in Korean households, particularly during colder months when people crave something warm and comforting.

What makes Dakbokkeumtang stand out is its ability to balance heat with savory depth. The spicy kick comes from gochujang (Korean chili paste), gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), and sometimes fresh chilies, while the umami flavor is developed from soy sauce, garlic, and other seasonings. This balance of flavors is what makes Dakbokkeumtang such a delightful dish to enjoy.

Ingredients for Korean Spicy Braised Chicken (Dakbokkeumtang)

To make Dakbokkeumtang at home, you will need the following ingredients. This recipe serves about 4 people, making it perfect for a family dinner or gathering.

Main Ingredients:

  • Whole chicken (cut into parts): 1 medium chicken (about 3-4 pounds), cut into pieces, or 8-10 chicken drumsticks or thighs
  • Potatoes: 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Carrots: 1 large carrot, peeled and sliced into rounds
  • Onion: 1 large onion, peeled and sliced
  • Green onions: 2-3 stalks, chopped for garnish
  • Garlic: 6 cloves, minced
  • Fresh ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
  • Dried red chilies: 3-4 (optional, for extra heat)

For the Braising Sauce:

  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): 2 tablespoons
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes): 1 tablespoon (adjust based on your heat preference)
  • Soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
  • Sugar: 1 tablespoon (to balance the spice)
  • Sesame oil: 1 tablespoon (for aroma and richness)
  • Rice wine: 2 tablespoons (optional, but adds depth to the flavor)
  • Water or chicken broth: 2 cups (for the braising liquid)
  • Salt and pepper: To taste

Optional Garnishes:

  • Sesame seeds: 1 teaspoon, lightly toasted
  • Chopped fresh cilantro: For extra freshness (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Dakbokkeumtang

Making Dakbokkeumtang at home is easy, but it does require some time to allow the flavors to meld. The process is simple and requires minimal prep work. Follow these steps to create a delicious, flavorful dish that will have everyone at the table asking for seconds.

1. Prepare the Chicken and Vegetables

Start by cutting the chicken into parts. If you’re using a whole chicken, cut it into quarters or smaller pieces, depending on your preference. This ensures the chicken cooks evenly and absorbs the rich flavors of the braising sauce.

Next, peel and chop the potatoes and carrots into bite-sized chunks. The potatoes will soak up the flavors of the broth and provide a creamy texture, while the carrots add a natural sweetness that complements the heat of the dish.

Slice the onion thinly and set it aside for later. You can also prepare the garlic and ginger by mincing them finely, as their aromatic presence will add richness to the dish.

2. Make the Braising Sauce

In a medium-sized bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, rice wine, and minced garlic and ginger. Stir well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated. The gochujang provides the base for the sauce, giving it its signature spice, while the gochugaru adds depth and a subtle smokiness.

Once the sauce is mixed, add in the water or chicken broth. If you want a richer flavor, use chicken broth, but water will work just as well. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to your taste.

3. Sauté the Chicken and Vegetables

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides for about 5-7 minutes. Browning the chicken helps develop a deeper flavor and creates a crispy exterior, which will contrast nicely with the tender, braised meat later.

Once the chicken is browned, add the sliced onion, carrots, and potatoes to the pot. Sauté the vegetables for a few minutes, stirring occasionally to coat them in the oil and release their aromas.

4. Braise the Chicken

Now that your chicken and vegetables are sautéed, it’s time to add the braising sauce. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, making sure everything is coated evenly. Add the dried red chilies, if using, for an extra kick.

Cover the pot and bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the vegetables are tender. During this time, the flavors will meld, and the chicken will absorb the sauce, becoming incredibly flavorful.

5. Check for Seasoning and Adjust

After 30 minutes of simmering, check the seasoning. If you find that the dish needs more salt, feel free to add a pinch at a time. If you like your Dakbokkeumtang extra spicy, you can also add more gochujang or gochugaru at this point. Adjusting the sauce is key to getting the right balance of heat, sweetness, and saltiness.

6. Garnish and Serve

Once the chicken is tender and the sauce has thickened slightly, it’s time to plate the dish. Serve the chicken and vegetables in large bowls or a serving platter. Garnish with freshly chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds for an added crunch and flavor.

If desired, sprinkle a little chopped cilantro on top for a fresh contrast to the rich and spicy dish. Serve your Dakbokkeumtang hot with steamed white rice on the side, which will help balance the bold flavors of the dish.

Tips for Making Perfect Dakbokkeumtang

  • Chicken pieces: While you can use bone-in or boneless chicken pieces, bone-in pieces tend to have more flavor and give the broth a richer taste.
  • Adjust the spice: If you’re not a fan of very spicy food, reduce the amount of gochugaru or use mild gochujang. You can always add more spice to taste later.
  • Add more vegetables: Feel free to experiment by adding other vegetables, such as mushrooms, bell peppers, or zucchini, for added texture and flavor.
  • Leftovers: Dakbokkeumtang tastes even better the next day! The flavors continue to deepen, and the chicken becomes even more tender. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

Why You’ll Love Dakbokkeumtang

Dakbokkeumtang isn’t just delicious—it’s also an incredibly comforting dish. The tender, juicy chicken paired with the rich, spicy sauce makes it a hearty, satisfying meal. The balance of spice, sweetness, and umami ensures that each bite is full of flavor, and the addition of vegetables like potatoes and carrots makes the dish even more filling and nutritious.

Whether you’re new to Korean cuisine or a seasoned fan, Dakbokkeumtang is an essential dish to try. The recipe is easy to follow and adaptable, allowing you to adjust the spice level and ingredients to suit your taste preferences.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Braised Chicken, or Dakbokkeumtang, is a delicious, comforting dish that perfectly combines spicy, savory, and umami flavors. It’s an ideal meal for family dinners or when you’re craving something bold and satisfying. With the right balance of spices and the tenderness of braised chicken, this dish will surely become a favorite in your home.

Now that you know how to make Dakbokkeumtang, try it out the next time you’re in the mood for a hearty Korean dish. The rich flavors and tender chicken will have everyone coming back for more!
