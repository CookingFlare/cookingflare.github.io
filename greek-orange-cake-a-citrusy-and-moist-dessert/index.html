Greek Orange Cake: A Citrusy and Moist Dessert


Are you craving a delightful dessert that combines the refreshing flavors of citrus with the moist texture of a cake? Look no further than Greek Orange Cake! This traditional Greek dessert is a perfect balance of tangy orange zest and sweet syrup, resulting in a truly indulgent treat that will leave you wanting more.

Ingredients

To make this delicious Greek Orange Cake, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 3 large oranges
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 cup of olive oil
  • 1/4 cup of freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon of orange zest

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a round cake pan with olive oil or butter.

  2. Wash the oranges thoroughly and place them in a pot with enough water to cover them completely. Bring the water to a boil and let the oranges simmer for about 1 hour until they become soft.

  3. Remove the oranges from the pot and let them cool. Once cooled, cut them into quarters and remove any seeds.

  4. Place the orange quarters in a blender or food processor and blend until you achieve a smooth puree.

  5. In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs and sugar together until the mixture becomes light and fluffy.

  6. Add the orange puree, flour, baking powder, olive oil, orange juice, and orange zest to the bowl. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully combined.

  7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula.

  8. Bake the cake in the preheated oven for approximately 40-45 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  9. While the cake is baking, prepare the syrup by combining the juice of one orange and two tablespoons of sugar in a small saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat until the sugar has dissolved completely.

  10. Once the cake is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer the cake to a wire rack placed over a baking tray.

  11. Pour the prepared syrup over the warm cake, allowing it to soak in. Let the cake cool completely before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Orange Cake is a versatile dessert that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with various accompaniments. Here are a few serving suggestions to enhance your culinary experience:

  • Dust the top of the cake with powdered sugar for an elegant touch.
  • Serve the cake with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a creamy contrast.
  • Garnish the cake with fresh orange segments or a sprinkle of orange zest to add a pop of color and extra citrus flavor.

Conclusion

Greek Orange Cake is a delightful dessert that showcases the vibrant flavors of oranges in a moist and citrusy cake. The combination of orange zest, juice, and syrup creates a harmonious balance of sweet and tangy, making it an irresistible treat for any occasion. So, why not indulge in this Greek delicacy and savor the taste of sunshine in every bite?

Enjoy the tantalizing flavors of Greek Orange Cake, and let its citrusy goodness transport you to the sunny shores of Greece!
