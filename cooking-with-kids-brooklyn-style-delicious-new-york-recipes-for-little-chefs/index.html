Cooking with Kids: Brooklyn-Style - Delicious New York Recipes for Little Chefs


Introducing a world of culinary exploration into the vibrant, bustling streets of Brooklyn. Get ready to have fun, stretch those creative muscles, and embark on an adventurous cooking journey with young budding chefs, inspired by the diverse, unique flavors of New York City. Forget boring cooking with kids recipes – these Brooklyn-style recipes will give your tiny taste-testing tykes an unforgettable taste of the city that never sleeps.

  1. Smoked Salmon Bagel Bites

Snag a few slices of smoked salmon and a schmear of cream cheese to kick off our Brooklyn-themed culinary adventure. These Smoked Salmon Bagel Bites will have your little chefs feeling like East Coast pro’s in no time. Plus, using mini bagels transforms the classic flavor combo into a fun bite-sized activity!

Ingredients:

  • Mini bagels (plain or everything)
  • Cream cheese
  • Smoked salmon
  • Capers and red onion (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Slice mini bagels in half and create your little slice of Brooklyn by spreading a dollop of cream cheese onto each bagel half.

  2. Top with a small piece of smoked salmon, a sprinkle of capers, and a finely chopped red onion.

  3. Serve up with a wedge of lemon, and grab a napkin – these bagel bites are a messy delight.

  4. Chicken Shawarma Pita Pocket

Experience the blend of flavors synonymous with Brooklyn’s vibrant Middle Eastern neighborhood in this Chicken Shawarma Pita Pocket recipe. This hands-on activity fuses tender chicken marinated in classic shawarma spices, avocado, and crisp bell pepper providing a taste sensation that is out of this world.

Ingredients:

  • Boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Garlic, minced
  • Shawarma spice mix
  • Whole wheat pita bread
  • Greek yogurt
  • Cucumber, diced
  • Red bell pepper, sliced
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Make the marinade by mixing olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, and shawarma spice mix in a bowl. Marinate the chicken breasts and set aside to soak up those Brooklyn flavors.

  2. While the chicken is marinating, grill or pan-fry to perfection. Shred the chicken once cooked, making it super easy for little hands to grab.

  3. Warm up pita bread in a skillet or oven. Fill each pocket with a dollop of Greek yogurt, shredded chicken, cucumber, bell pepper, avocado, and finish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley. Dig into this Brooklyn-inspired feast!

  4. Brooklyn Classic: New York-Style Cheesecake Martian Cake

Why restrict little culinary heroes to classic cheesecake when we can upgrade it with a twist of intergalactic flair? This Brooklyn Classic cheesecake has an out-of-this-world twist – we’re giving it an astro-twist by doling out a concoction that mingles the traditional New York cream cheese and graham cracker flavors with an extra space-inspired touch.

Ingredients:

  • Cream cheese
  • Sugar
  • Eggs
  • Lemon juice
  • Vanilla extract
  • Graham crackers, crushed
  • Butter
  • Chocolate chips
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Appliance: Upside-down microwave or double boiler

Instructions:

  1. Start by assembling the traditional graham cracker crust by mixing crushed graham crackers and melted butter. Press onto the bottom and sides of a cake pan.
  2. For the cheesecake batter, beat the cream cheese and sugar with an electric mixer until smooth. Add eggs, lemon juice, and mix on low speed using a whisk attachment. Stir in the vanilla extract to heighten the Brooklyn flavor.
  3. Pour the mixture over the crust and bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) until set; around 40-45 minutes. Let the cheesecake cool to room temperature before placing it in the refrigerator for a few hours.
  4. For the out-of-this-world twist, gently heat mini marshmallows and a portion of chocolate chips in the microwave or double boiler until chocolate is melted, and marshmallows are softened.
  5. Gently spread the marshmallow-chocolate mixture over the chilled cheesecake, making sure to create trails resembling a Martian landscape.
  6. Now kick back, rejoice, and relish making this scrumptious Brooklyn fusion while unleashing your kid’s creativity like an urban culinary star.

There you have it – an appetizing escape to Brooklyn’s dynamic food scene! These innovative recipes will help preserve and keep the magic of New York alive within your kitchen. Always remember, the object of cooking with kids is not about the perfect dish, but the fun memories and skills your little ones will gain, inspired by the ever-fascinating borough of Brooklyn. Happy cooking with kids!
