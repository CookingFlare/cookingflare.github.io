Kids in the Kitchen: Simple and Fun Recipes for Holiday Baking


The holiday season is the perfect time to gather your little ones in the kitchen and embark on a baking adventure together. Not only does it create beautiful memories, but it also provides an opportunity for kids to learn valuable skills and develop their creativity. In this blog post, we’ll explore some simple and fun recipes that are perfect for holiday baking with kids.

1. Festive Sugar Cookies

Who doesn’t love sugar cookies? These sweet and buttery treats are a staple during the holidays. To make them, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and baking powder. Set aside.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  6. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut out festive shapes using cookie cutters.
  7. Place the cookies on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  8. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool before decorating with icing and sprinkles.

2. Peppermint Hot Chocolate

Warm up on a chilly winter day with a cup of homemade peppermint hot chocolate. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 cups milk
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Whipped cream and crushed candy canes for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until hot but not boiling.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the cocoa powder and sugar until well combined.
  3. Slowly add the cocoa mixture to the hot milk, whisking constantly.
  4. Stir in the vanilla extract and peppermint extract.
  5. Continue to heat the mixture until it reaches your desired temperature.
  6. Pour the hot chocolate into mugs and top with whipped cream and crushed candy canes.

3. Gingerbread House

Building a gingerbread house is a classic holiday activity that kids adore. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup molasses
  • Assorted candies and icing for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the egg and molasses until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  6. Divide the dough into two equal parts and wrap each in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  7. Roll out each portion of dough on a floured surface until about ¼ inch thick.
  8. Cut out the gingerbread house pieces using templates or your own creativity.
  9. Bake the pieces for 10-12 minutes or until firm.
  10. Allow the pieces to cool completely before assembling the gingerbread house using icing as glue.
  11. Decorate the house with assorted candies and icing to your heart’s content.

These are just a few examples of the many simple and fun recipes you can try with your kids during the holiday season. Remember, the most important thing is to have fun and create lasting memories together. So put on your aprons, gather your ingredients, and let the baking adventure begin! Happy holidays!
