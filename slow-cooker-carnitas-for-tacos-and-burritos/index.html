Slow Cooker Carnitas for Tacos and Burritos


When it comes to comfort food with bold flavors and irresistibly tender meat, slow cooker carnitas rank high on the list. These delicious Mexican-style pulled pork bites are versatile, easy to make, and perfect for tacos, burritos, nachos, or even rice bowls. By using a slow cooker, you ensure the meat is cooked low and slow, soaking up rich spices and aromatics, resulting in mouthwatering carnitas that literally melt in your mouth.

Whether you’re hosting a taco night with friends, meal prepping for the week, or simply indulging in authentic Mexican cuisine at home, this recipe is guaranteed to impress. Not only does it deliver flavor-packed pork, but it also simplifies the cooking process, leaving you free to enjoy the meal with minimal effort.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make slow cooker carnitas at home, you’ll need just a few simple ingredients. Here’s what you’ll want to gather before getting started:

For the Meat:

  • Bone-in pork shoulder or pork butt (around 4-5 pounds): This cut of meat is perfect for slow cooking as the fat adds flavor and keeps the pork tender during its long cook time.

For the Seasonings:

  • Juice of two oranges: Fresh citrus brightens the meat and balances the rich pork flavor.
  • Juice of one lime: Adds additional tangy acidity that’s perfect for carnitas.
  • 3 teaspoons ground cumin: A staple spice for warmth and depth.
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika: For a subtle smoky undertone.
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder: Enhances the savory flavor effortlessly.
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder: Complements the garlic for a rounded taste.
  • 1 teaspoon oregano (Mexican oregano, if available): Brings earthy notes into the mix.
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder: Keeps the flavor bold without being overwhelmingly spicy.
  • 1 teaspoon salt: Essential for seasoning the meat.
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper: For a touch of mild spice.
  • 1 small onion, finely diced: Adds aromatic sweetness to the pork.
  • 2-3 peeled garlic cloves: Whole cloves infuse the pork with deep flavor.
  • ½ cup chicken broth or water: Keeps the meat moist as it cooks.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

Start by trimming any excess fat from the pork shoulder. While a little fat is necessary for flavor, removing large fatty chunks ensures the pork doesn’t become overly greasy.

Once the pork is prepped, pat it dry with paper towels. Drying the meat ensures the seasonings adhere well and helps create a nice caramelization during the cooking process.

Step 2: Season the Meat

In a small bowl, mix together the cumin, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture generously all over the pork, ensuring every inch is well-coated with the spices.

Place the pork in the slow cooker, and then top it with the diced onion and garlic cloves. This layer of aromatics will infuse the meat with rich flavors during the cooking process.

Step 3: Add Liquids

Pour the orange juice, lime juice, and chicken broth (or water) over the seasoned pork in the slow cooker. The citrus will tenderize the meat while creating a deliciously tangy and vibrant flavor profile.

Step 4: Slow Cook the Pork

Cover the slow cooker with its lid and set it to low heat for 8-10 hours, or high heat for 5-6 hours, depending on your schedule. Slow cooking allows the pork to become incredibly tender, so resist the temptation to lift the lid during the process. Keeping the lid closed ensures consistent heat and proper cooking.

After the cook time, the pork should easily pull apart with a fork. If it doesn’t, let it cook for another hour and check again.

Step 5: Shred the Carnitas

Once the pork is fully cooked, use tongs to transfer it to a cutting board or large bowl. It will be incredibly tender, so handle it gently. Using two forks, shred the meat until you have perfectly pulled pork strands.

Before discarding the slow cooker liquid, strain it and pour about ½ cup of this flavorful broth over the shredded pork to keep it moist and packed with flavor.

Step 6: Crisp the Carnitas (Optional but Recommended)

To achieve that coveted crispy texture so iconic to authentic carnitas, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a drizzle of oil or some reserved pork fat. Place a portion of shredded pork in the skillet, spreading it out in an even layer.

Let the pork cook undisturbed for about 2 minutes per side, allowing it to caramelize and crisp slightly on the edges. This step adds a layer of texture and smoky richness to the carnitas, perfect for tacos or burritos.

Serving Suggestions

For Tacos:

Warm small corn or flour tortillas and fill them with the carnitas. Top with fresh ingredients like diced onion, cilantro, crumbled queso fresco, and a squeeze of lime juice. Add salsa verde, guacamole, or pickled red onions for extra oomph.

For Burritos:

Use larger tortillas and layer in carnitas with rice, beans, shredded cheese, sour cream, avocado slices, and your favorite sauces. Roll tightly for handheld perfection.

Bonus Ideas:

  • Serve carnitas over crispy tortilla chips to make loaded nachos.
  • Mix them into a rice bowl with roasted vegetables, black beans, and pico de gallo.
  • Toss the pork with scrambled eggs for a hearty breakfast burrito.

Storage and Reheating Tips

One of the best things about slow cooker carnitas is how well they store! If you end up with leftovers (lucky you), keep the carnitas in an airtight container, along with a little reserved cooking liquid to prevent drying out. Refrigerate for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 3 months.

To reheat, simply microwave or rewarm the pork in a skillet. Adding a splash of chicken broth or orange juice helps revive the flavors and texture.

Expert Tips for Perfect Carnitas

  1. Choose the right cut of pork: Pork shoulder or pork butt is ideal because it has enough fat to render properly and remain tender during slow cooking.
  2. Crisping is key: Don’t skip the skillet step! A little caramelization adds depth to the flavor and an unbeatable texture.
  3. Adjust seasoning to taste: Feel free to tweak the spices depending on your taste preferences, especially if you prefer more heat or smokiness.
  4. Don’t rush: The slow cooker works its magic over time. Let the pork cook long enough to become fork-tender.

Slow cooker carnitas are a guaranteed hit for any occasion. They’re effortlessly flavorful, easy to prepare, and endlessly versatile. Whether you’re serving them at large gatherings or preparing a comforting dinner at home, their tender, juicy consistency paired with crispy edges makes carnitas nothing short of irresistible. Try this recipe, and your tacos and burritos will never be the same again!
