Italian Panna Cotta Recipe with Cream and Vanilla


Panna Cotta is a classic Italian dessert that translates to “cooked cream.” It is a silky and indulgent treat that is surprisingly easy to make. This recipe combines the richness of cream with the delicate flavor of vanilla, resulting in a dessert that is sure to impress your guests. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet treat, this Italian Panna Cotta recipe is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean, split lengthwise and seeds scraped (or 1 teaspoon vanilla extract)
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons powdered gelatin
  • 3 tablespoons cold water

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over the cold water and let it sit for about 5 minutes to bloom.

  2. In the meantime, in a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, milk, sugar, and vanilla bean seeds (or vanilla extract). Heat the mixture over medium heat until it begins to steam and small bubbles form around the edges. Do not let it boil.

  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the bloomed gelatin. Whisk until the gelatin is completely dissolved and the mixture is smooth.

  4. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a pouring jug or individual serving glasses. This step ensures a smooth and creamy texture.

  5. Allow the Panna Cotta mixture to cool slightly before transferring it to the refrigerator. Chill for at least 4 hours or until set. For best results, cover the glasses with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming on the surface.

  6. Once the Panna Cotta is set, you can serve it as is or with your favorite toppings. Fresh berries, caramel sauce, or a sprinkle of cocoa powder are all great options.

  7. To unmold the Panna Cotta, dip the bottom of the glasses in warm water for a few seconds to loosen the edges. Gently invert the glasses onto serving plates, and the Panna Cotta should slide out easily. If you’re serving it in individual glasses, you can serve it directly in them.

  8. Garnish with additional toppings if desired, and serve chilled.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different flavors by infusing the cream mixture with other ingredients such as coffee, citrus zest, or even a splash of liqueur.
  • For a lighter version, you can substitute part of the heavy cream with Greek yogurt or coconut milk. This will give the Panna Cotta a tangy twist.
  • Feel free to customize the sweetness level by adjusting the amount of sugar according to your taste.
  • To make vegan Panna Cotta, substitute the heavy cream and milk with plant-based alternatives such as coconut cream or almond milk. Use a vegan-friendly gelatin substitute, such as agar agar, following the package instructions.

Conclusion

Italian Panna Cotta is a dessert that embodies elegance and simplicity. With its velvety texture and delicate flavor, it’s no wonder that this classic Italian treat has become a favorite worldwide. Whether you serve it plain or dress it up with your favorite toppings, this Panna Cotta recipe is sure to impress. Enjoy it as the perfect ending to a meal or as a special treat any time of the day.
