Mexican Chiles en Nogada Recipe with Poblano Peppers and Walnut Sauce


If you’re looking to tantalize your taste buds with a truly authentic Mexican dish, then look no further than Chiles en Nogada. This traditional recipe combines the flavors of roasted poblano peppers, a creamy walnut sauce, and a burst of sweetness from pomegranate seeds. The result is a dish that not only looks stunning but tastes absolutely divine.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Poblano Peppers:

  • 6 large poblano peppers
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 cup white onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup almonds, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Walnut Sauce:

  • 1 cup walnuts
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup queso fresco, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt to taste

For the Garnish:

  • 1 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Start by roasting the poblano peppers. Place them directly over a gas flame or under a broiler, turning them occasionally until the skin is charred and blistered. Transfer the roasted peppers to a plastic bag and let them steam for about 10 minutes. This will make it easier to peel off the skin. Once cooled, gently remove the skin, make a small slit along the side of each pepper, and carefully remove the seeds.

  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground beef until browned. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion is translucent. Stir in the tomatoes, raisins, almonds, parsley, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.

  3. While the filling cools, prepare the walnut sauce. In a blender, combine the walnuts, milk, queso fresco, sour cream, ground cinnamon, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy. If the sauce is too thick, you can add a little more milk to achieve the desired consistency.

  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  5. Stuff each roasted poblano pepper with the ground beef mixture, making sure to gently press the filling into the pepper. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and pour the walnut sauce over them, ensuring they are well coated.

  6. Bake the stuffed peppers in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until the sauce is bubbly and lightly golden.

  7. Once out of the oven, let the stuffed peppers cool slightly before serving. Garnish with a sprinkle of pomegranate seeds and fresh parsley for a burst of color and added freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, you can substitute the ground beef with mushrooms or a combination of black beans and rice.
  • To make this dish even more authentic, you can top it with crumbled queso fresco and a drizzle of Mexican crema.
  • Chiles en Nogada is traditionally served chilled, so feel free to refrigerate the stuffed peppers for a few hours before serving.
  • This dish is often enjoyed as part of the Mexican Independence Day celebrations in September, as the colors of the dish (green, white, and red) reflect the colors of the Mexican flag.

Conclusion

Chiles en Nogada is a true culinary masterpiece that showcases the vibrant flavors and colors of Mexican cuisine. With its combination of roasted poblano peppers, savory filling, creamy walnut sauce, and sweet pomegranate seeds, this dish is a feast for both the eyes and the taste buds. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply want to treat yourself to something extraordinary, this recipe will not disappoint. So, gather your ingredients, put on some traditional Mexican music, and embark on a culinary adventure with Chiles en Nogada! Enjoy!
