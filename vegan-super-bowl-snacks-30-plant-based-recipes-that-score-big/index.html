Vegan Super Bowl Snacks: 30 Plant-Based Recipes That Score Big


Are you planning to host a Super Bowl party and want to offer your guests some delicious, plant-based snacks? No need to fret because we have a fabulous collection of 30 amazing vegan recipes that will not only satisfy your guests but also ensure your game day lives up to the hype. Get ready to score big with these mouthwatering dishes!

1. Spicy Sweet Potato Skins

Craving the classic football stadium treat but looking for a vegan twist? These Spicy Sweet Potato Skins are the perfect replacement, packed with flavored filling and an accompanying tangy dip.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup chopped onion
  • ½ cup chopped bell pepper
  • 1 jalapeno, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • ½ cup chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ¼ cup diced scallions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Poke sweet potatoes with a fork and bake for about 45-50 minutes until they’re tender.
  3. Cut sweet potatoes in half and let them cool for a while. Scoop out the flesh carefully, leaving a thin layer of sweet potato to keep the skin intact.
  4. Brush the skins with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bake for another 8-10 minutes until the skins start to crisp.
  6. In a pan, heat a bit of olive oil on medium heat, add finely chopped onion, bell pepper, and jalapeño. Saute until they’re soft.
  7. Add corn kernels and chickpeas, stir well.
  8. Carefully scoop the filling into the sweet potato skins, and bake for another 15 minutes or until the skin is crisp around the edges.
  9. Top each of them with diced scallions, serve with your favorite vegan dip.

2. Buffalo Cauliflower Bites

Who doesn’t love buffalo wings? Don’t worry, with these Buffalo Cauliflower Bites, you don’t have to miss out on the game-day grub. These are incredibly tasty and crispy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cauliflower
  • ¼ cup plant-based milk
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • ½ cup vegan buffalo sauce

Instructions:

  1. Mix the plant-based milk and 1 tbsp of white vinegar in a bowl and set it aside to curdle.
  2. Cut the cauliflower into bite-sized florets and separate them.
  3. In another bowl, mix flour, garlic powder, paprika, onion powder, smoked paprika, and salt.
  4. Dip each cauliflower floret into the curdled milk mixture, then coat with the flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  5. In a deep frying pan or bowl, mix the vegetable oil and deep-fry the coated cauliflower florets for 2-3 minutes until they turn golden brown.
  6. Remove the florets from the oil, let them cool, and toss in vegan buffalo sauce.
  7. Serve hot with your favorite vegan blue cheese or ranch dressing.

3. Jackfruit Tacos

A Super Bowl party favorite, these Jackfruit Tacos, packed with the meaty texture of pulled jackfruit, are a game-changer. Fresh, zesty, and deeply satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs jackfruit
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • ¼ cup vegan barbecue sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • 4 corn tortillas
  • ½ head shredded cabbage
  • Sliced avocado
  • Chopped red onion and cilantro for toppings

Instructions:

  1. Cut the unripe jackfruit into small pieces and toss them in soy sauce.
  2. In a pan, heat a splash of olive oil and cook jackfruit until crispy.
  3. Add barbecue sauce and toss well to coat.
  4. Cook for another 5 minutes, then add smoked paprika and garlic powder.
  5. Warm the corn tortillas, add cabbage, vegan cheese, sliced avocado, and cooked jackfruit.
  6. Top with chopped red onion and cilantro.
  7. Serve immediately.

4. Lentil and Vegetable Sliders

Bring a new flavor to the game-day table with these Lentil and Vegetable Sliders, a filling alternative to traditional burgers or meat-based sliders. Definitely crowd-pleasing!

Ingredients:

  • 1¼ cup dried lentils
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup chopped bell pepper
  • 1 cup chopped zucchini
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste

Instructions:

  1. Soak lentils overnight, then cook in 2 cups of water until soft and yielding.
  2. In a pan, sauté chopped carrots, onions, bell pepper, and zucchini until softened.
  3. Add minced garlic, cook for a minute, then mix in the lentils.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, let it cool in a mixing bowl.
  5. Add breadcrumbs and tomato paste, mix well.
  6. Form into small patties.
  7. In a pan, heat vegetable oil on medium heat, cook the patties until they’re golden brown on both sides.
  8. Serve on buns with your favorite toppings.

Your taste buds will thank you!

5. Veggie-Stuffed Mushrooms

Veggie-Stuffed Mushrooms are destined to become your go-to party appetizer. Piled high with a blend of Mediterranean flavors, you’ll can’t go wrong with these crowd-pleasers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chopped spinach
  • 1 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • ¼ cup chopped kalamata olives
  • 1 cup crumbled vegan feta cheese
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 (8oz) package of large mushrooms
  • Olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. In a bowl, mix together spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, olives, vegan feta, and cooked quinoa.
  3. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Lightly grease a baking sheet with olive oil.
  5. Remove stems from mushrooms and gently scoop out the gills.
  6. Stuff each mushroom cap with the mixture and place on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Drizzle with olive oil.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the mushrooms are soft and the stuffing is heated through.
