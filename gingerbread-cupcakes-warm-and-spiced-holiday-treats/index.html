Gingerbread Cupcakes: Warm and Spiced Holiday Treats


The holiday season is fast approaching, and what better way to embrace the festive spirit than by indulging in the warm and comforting flavors of gingerbread? While gingerbread cookies are a classic holiday treat, why not switch things up this year and delight your friends and family with delectable gingerbread cupcakes? These moist, spiced cupcakes topped with creamy frosting are sure to become a new holiday favorite. So, let’s dive into the delightful world of gingerbread cupcakes and learn how to bake up a batch of these irresistible treats.

Ingredients

For the Cupcakes:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup molasses
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup milk

For the Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 ½ cups confectioners’ sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For Garnish (optional):

  • Ground cinnamon
  • Crystallized ginger

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.

  2. Prepare the dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, ground ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.

  3. Cream the butter and sugar: In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.

  4. Add the wet ingredients: Beat in the molasses and eggs until well combined. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with the milk, and mix until just combined.

  5. Fill the cupcake liners: Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cupcake liners, filling each about two-thirds full.

  6. Bake the cupcakes: Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely before frosting.

  7. Prepare the frosting: In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add the confectioners’ sugar and vanilla extract, beating until light and fluffy.

  8. Frost the cupcakes: Once the cupcakes are completely cooled, frost them with the cream cheese frosting using a piping bag or offset spatula.

  9. Garnish (optional): Sprinkle the frosted cupcakes with a pinch of ground cinnamon and top each cupcake with a small piece of crystallized ginger for a festive touch.

Serving and Storing

These gingerbread cupcakes are best enjoyed fresh, but they can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Bring them to room temperature before serving for the best flavor and texture.

Final Thoughts

Gingerbread cupcakes are the perfect way to infuse your holiday gatherings with the cozy and aromatic flavors of the season. Whether you’re hosting a festive party or simply craving a sweet treat on a chilly evening, these cupcakes are sure to delight. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the warm and spiced goodness of homemade gingerbread cupcakes. Happy baking!
