Easy Soups for Beginners: Warm Up with These Simple Recipes


As the chill of winter settles in, there’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of soup to bring comfort and nourishment. For those new to cooking, soups can be a fantastic starting point. They are forgiving, versatile, and allow for creativity in the kitchen. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of easy soup recipes perfect for beginners. Whether you’re looking for a hearty meal or a light broth, these simple recipes will warm your heart and your home.

Why Soup is Perfect for Beginners

1. Minimal Ingredients

Most soup recipes require a handful of basic ingredients, making them economical and accessible. This simplicity allows beginners to focus on mastering cooking techniques rather than sourcing exotic components.

2. Forgiving Cooking Method

Soups are incredibly forgiving. If you accidentally add too much of one ingredient or forget to include something, chances are the soup will still turn out delicious. This flexibility encourages experimentation, making cooking more enjoyable.

3. Nutritional Benefits

Soups can be packed with nutrients. By incorporating vegetables, legumes, and lean proteins, you can create a balanced meal that warms you from the inside out.

4. Meal Prep Friendly

Soups store well, making them perfect for meal prepping. You can whip up a big batch, portion it out, and have healthy meals ready to go throughout the week.

Essential Tools for Soup Making

Before diving into the recipes, let’s go over some essential tools that every beginner should have in their kitchen:

  • Large Pot or Dutch Oven: Ideal for simmering soups and stews.
  • Cutting Board and Knife: A good knife will make chopping vegetables much easier.
  • Wooden Spoon: For stirring your soup as it cooks.
  • Measuring Cups and Spoons: To ensure you’re using the correct amounts of ingredients.
  • Ladle: Perfect for serving your delicious creations.

Classic Vegetable Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups spinach or kale (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  2. Add Garlic: Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Pour in the vegetable broth and add the diced tomatoes, green beans, thyme, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Simmer: Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for about 20-30 minutes. If using, add the spinach or kale in the last 5 minutes of cooking.

  5. Serve: Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Ladle into bowls and enjoy!

Creamy Tomato Soup

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cans (14.5 oz each) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté Onion and Garlic: In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for an additional minute.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Broth: Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. If you prefer a sweeter soup, add the sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  4. Blend the Soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, carefully transfer the soup to a blender in batches.

  5. Add Cream: Stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk, and heat through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh basil if desired and serve warm.

Chicken Noodle Soup

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 cups egg noodles
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  2. Add Garlic: Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Pour in the chicken broth and add the shredded chicken, egg noodles, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.

  4. Cook Noodles: Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the noodles are cooked through.

  5. Serve: Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Garnish with fresh parsley and enjoy!

Chunky Potato Soup

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook Vegetables: In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion and carrots, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add Potatoes and Broth: Add the diced potatoes and pour in the broth. Bring to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

  4. Blend and Add Milk: Use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup, leaving some chunks for texture. Stir in the milk or cream, and season with salt and pepper.

  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions if desired. Serve hot.

Tips for Perfecting Your Soup

  • Adjust Seasoning: Taste your soup as it cooks and adjust the seasoning accordingly. A little salt can bring out the flavors of your ingredients.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t be afraid to try different herbs and spices. Adding a splash of vinegar or a squeeze of lemon juice at the end can brighten up the flavors.

  • Garnish Creatively: Fresh herbs, croutons, or a drizzle of olive oil can elevate your soup from simple to stunning.

  • Make It Your Own: Each recipe can be customized to suit your taste. Swap out vegetables, use different proteins, or try various broths for unique flavors.

Conclusion

Soup making is an excellent way for beginners to build confidence in the kitchen while enjoying delicious, comforting meals. With these easy recipes, you have a solid foundation to explore the world of soup. Remember, the best part of cooking is the joy of experimentation. So, gather your ingredients and start simmering your way to culinary success! Happy cooking!
