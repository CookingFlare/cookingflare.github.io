How to Cook Lentils: Simple Recipes for Nutritious Meals


Lentils are a powerhouse of nutrition, offering a wealth of health benefits while being incredibly versatile in the kitchen. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice cook, learning how to cook lentils can open up a world of delicious and nutritious meals. In this post, we’ll explore the different types of lentils, how to prepare them, and share some simple yet flavorful recipes that will make lentils the star of your dining table.

Understanding Lentils

Before diving into cooking, it’s essential to understand what lentils are. Lentils are small, lens-shaped legumes that come in various colors, including green, brown, red, yellow, and black. Each type has its unique flavor and texture, making them suitable for different dishes.

Nutritional Benefits of Lentils

Lentils are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. They are an excellent source of protein, making them a fantastic meat substitute for vegetarians and vegans. Additionally, lentils are rich in dietary fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain a healthy weight. They also contain essential vitamins and minerals, including iron, folate, and magnesium.

Types of Lentils

  1. Brown Lentils: The most common variety, brown lentils have a mild, earthy flavor and hold their shape well when cooked. They are perfect for soups, stews, and salads.

  2. Green Lentils: Slightly firmer than brown lentils, green lentils have a peppery taste. They are great for salads and side dishes.

  3. Red Lentils: These lentils cook quickly and break down into a creamy texture, making them ideal for soups and purees.

  4. Black Lentils (Beluga Lentils): Named for their resemblance to caviar, black lentils have a rich, earthy flavor and hold their shape well. They are excellent in salads and as a side dish.

  5. Yellow Lentils: Similar to red lentils, yellow lentils cook quickly and are often used in Indian cuisine.

How to Cook Lentils

Cooking lentils is straightforward, but there are a few steps to ensure they turn out perfectly every time. Here’s a simple guide to cooking lentils:

Step 1: Rinse the Lentils

Before cooking, it’s essential to rinse lentils under cold water to remove any dust or debris. Place the lentils in a fine-mesh strainer and rinse them thoroughly.

Step 2: Measure the Lentils and Water

The general rule of thumb is to use three cups of water for every one cup of lentils. However, this may vary slightly depending on the type of lentils you are using.

Step 3: Cooking the Lentils

  1. Stovetop Method:

    • In a pot, combine the rinsed lentils and water.
    • Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
    • Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer.
    • Cooking times vary: brown and green lentils take about 20-30 minutes, while red and yellow lentils cook in about 15-20 minutes. Black lentils usually take around 25-30 minutes.
    • Check for doneness; lentils should be tender but not mushy.

  2. Instant Pot Method:

    • Add rinsed lentils and water to the Instant Pot.
    • Seal the lid and cook on high pressure for 9-10 minutes for brown and green lentils, 6-7 minutes for red and yellow lentils, and 10-12 minutes for black lentils.
    • Allow for a natural release of pressure for about 10 minutes before manually releasing any remaining pressure.

  3. Slow Cooker Method:

    • Combine rinsed lentils and water in the slow cooker.
    • Cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, depending on the type of lentils.

Step 4: Seasoning

Once cooked, lentils can be seasoned to taste. Adding salt during cooking can make them tough, so it’s best to season them after they are cooked. Consider adding herbs, spices, or a splash of vinegar for extra flavor.
