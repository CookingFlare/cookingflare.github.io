Butternut Squash and Goat Cheese Tart: A Savory Fall Appetizer


Fall is the perfect time to experiment with seasonal ingredients and create delightful dishes that warm the heart and soul. One such dish that embodies the essence of autumn is the Butternut Squash and Goat Cheese Tart. This savory appetizer combines the earthy sweetness of roasted butternut squash with the creamy tang of goat cheese, all nestled in a crisp and flaky pastry shell. It’s a dish that will impress your guests and leave them craving for more.

Ingredients

For the pastry:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into thin slices
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt for the pastry. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. Meanwhile, place the sliced butternut squash on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Toss to coat the squash evenly. Roast in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender and slightly caramelized.

  5. Remove the pastry from the refrigerator and roll it out on a floured surface into a circle that’s about 1/4 inch thick. Transfer the rolled-out pastry to a tart pan and press it into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough.

  6. Spread the crumbled goat cheese evenly over the bottom of the tart shell. Arrange the roasted butternut squash slices on top of the cheese, overlapping them slightly. Sprinkle with fresh thyme leaves.

  7. Bake the tart in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

  8. Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Butternut Squash and Goat Cheese Tart is a versatile dish that can be served as an appetizer, a light lunch, or even a main course when paired with a side salad. Its rich flavors and beautiful presentation make it an excellent addition to any fall gathering or holiday feast.

Conclusion

The Butternut Squash and Goat Cheese Tart is a savory fall appetizer that celebrates the bounty of autumn. With its delicate pastry, creamy goat cheese, and caramelized squash, it’s a dish that captures the flavors and colors of the season. So, don your apron, gather the ingredients, and let the aromas of fall fill your kitchen as you create this delicious and impressive tart. Your taste buds will thank you, and your guests will be asking for the recipe in no time!
