Healthy Paleo Sweet Potato and Turkey Skillet


Cooking a delicious and nutritious meal doesn’t have to be complicated. This Healthy Paleo Sweet Potato and Turkey Skillet is a perfect example of a satisfying dish that’s easy to prepare and packed with wholesome ingredients. Whether you’re following a paleo diet or simply looking to incorporate more nutrient-rich meals into your weeknight rotation, this recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Olive oil or avocado oil, for cooking

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sweet Potatoes: Start by peeling the sweet potatoes and cutting them into small, bite-sized cubes. This ensures they cook quickly and evenly in the skillet.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add a drizzle of olive oil or avocado oil. Once the oil is hot, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until the onions become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Cook the Ground Turkey: Push the onions and garlic to the side of the skillet and add the ground turkey. Use a spatula to break up the turkey into smaller pieces as it cooks. Season with salt, pepper, paprika, dried oregano, and ground cumin. Cook until the turkey is browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Add the Bell Peppers and Sweet Potatoes: Stir in the diced red and yellow bell peppers, along with the diced sweet potatoes. Continue cooking over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sweet potatoes are tender yet slightly crisp, about 10-12 minutes. Adjust the heat as needed to prevent burning.

  5. Finish and Garnish: Once the sweet potatoes are cooked through, taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Remove the skillet from heat and sprinkle with freshly chopped parsley or cilantro for added freshness and color.

  6. Serve: Divide the skillet mixture onto plates or bowls and serve hot. This dish can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a side salad for a complete meal.

Tips for Success

  • Even Cooking: Ensure that the sweet potato cubes are evenly sized to promote consistent cooking.
  • Flavor Variations: Feel free to customize the seasonings based on your preference. Add a pinch of chili powder for heat or a squeeze of lime juice for acidity.
  • Storage: This skillet meal stores well in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Nutritional Benefits

This Healthy Paleo Sweet Potato and Turkey Skillet is not only delicious but also offers a range of nutritional benefits:

  • Lean Protein: Ground turkey provides lean protein essential for muscle repair and growth.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: Sweet potatoes are rich in vitamins A and C, as well as potassium and fiber.
  • Antioxidants: Bell peppers contribute antioxidants like vitamin C, which support immune health.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or hosting guests, this Healthy Paleo Sweet Potato and Turkey Skillet is a versatile and satisfying option. It’s a great way to incorporate lean protein and nutrient-dense vegetables into your diet without sacrificing flavor or enjoyment. Plus, the vibrant colors and aromatic spices make it a feast for the senses as well as the palate.

Try this recipe for a wholesome dinner option that checks all the boxes for taste, nutrition, and ease of preparation. You’ll love how simple ingredients come together to create a meal that’s as nourishing as it is delicious. Enjoy cooking and savoring this wholesome dish!
