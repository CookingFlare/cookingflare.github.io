How to Make Paleo-Friendly Granola: 3 Easy Recipes


Granola is a breakfast and snack favorite for many, but traditional recipes often incorporate grains, refined sugars, and other ingredients that don’t align with a Paleo diet. The good news is that making Paleo-friendly granola at home is not only possible, but easy and incredibly rewarding. With simple, wholesome ingredients, you can whip up delicious, nutrient-packed granola that fits perfectly into your Paleo lifestyle. Let’s dive into three unique and simple recipes that will fill your pantry with guilt-free, crunchy goodness.

Why Choose Paleo-Friendly Granola?

For those on the Paleo diet, recreating classic favorites like granola can feel daunting at first, but it’s worth the effort. Paleo granola is typically made without grains, gluten, dairy, or refined sugars, relying instead on nutrient-dense nuts, seeds, natural sweeteners, and healthy fats. The result is a crunchy, satisfying snack or breakfast topping that’s packed with protein, fiber, and healthy fats to keep you fueled throughout the day.

Paleo granola also allows for plenty of customization. Whether you’re craving something sweet, savory, or a combination of both, you have endless opportunities to make it your own by swapping out ingredients or adjusting flavors. Below are three fool-proof recipes to get you started.

Recipe #1: Classic Paleo Nut & Seed Granola

This simple yet delicious recipe combines nutrient-dense nuts, seeds, and a touch of natural sweetness for a classic Paleo granola base. It’s incredibly versatile and works well as a snack, topping for your favorite dairy-free yogurt, or even as a quick cereal alternative with almond or coconut milk.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw almonds, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup raw pecans, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup unsweetened coconut flakes
  • ½ cup sunflower seeds
  • ½ cup pumpkin seeds
  • 3 tablespoons chia seeds
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, melted
  • 3 tablespoons raw honey or maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a large baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped almonds, pecans, coconut flakes, sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, chia seeds, cinnamon, and sea salt. Stir to mix evenly.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients: In a small bowl, mix the melted coconut oil, honey (or maple syrup), and vanilla extract. Pour this mixture over the dry ingredients and stir well to coat everything evenly.
  4. Spread & Bake: Spread the granola mixture evenly onto the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through to ensure even baking. Keep an eye on it—coconut flakes can brown quickly.
  5. Cool & Store: Remove from the oven and let the granola cool completely. It will crisp up as it cools. Store in an airtight container for up to two weeks.

Recipe #2: Chocolate Coconut Paleo Granola

This indulgent granola is perfect for when you’re craving something sweet and chocolaty but still want to keep it healthy. With raw cacao powder and creamy coconut, it’s a Paleo-friendly way to satisfy your sweet tooth while still fueling your body with natural ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cashews, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup macadamia nuts, roughly chopped
  • ½ cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 3 tablespoons raw cacao powder
  • 2 tablespoons chia seeds
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup or raw honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt
  • Optional: ¼ cup cacao nibs for extra crunch

Instructions:

  1. Preheat & Prepare: Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped cashews, macadamia nuts, shredded coconut, cacao powder, chia seeds, and sea salt.
  3. Incorporate the Wet Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Pour the mixture over the dry ingredients and stir until everything is evenly coated.
  4. Bake to Perfection: Spread the granola evenly onto the prepared baking sheet. Bake for about 18-22 minutes, stirring halfway through to avoid burning. The cacao powder can make it tricky to see browning, so keep watch.
  5. Cool & Add Cacao Nibs (Optional): Let the granola cool entirely before stirring in cacao nibs, if desired. Store in an airtight container.

Recipe #3: Savory Herbed Paleo Granola

Not all granola has to be sweet! This herb-infused savory granola is a delicious departure from the norm and makes for an irresistible topping for salads, roasted vegetables, or savory breakfast bowls.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 1 cup hazelnuts, roughly chopped
  • ½ cup pumpkin seeds
  • ½ cup sunflower seeds
  • 2 tablespoons flaxseeds
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat & Line Tray: Preheat your oven to 275°F (135°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, toss together the nuts, seeds, garlic powder, onion powder, rosemary, thyme, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Coat with Olive Oil: Drizzle the olive oil over the mixture and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Bake & Stir: Spread the granola on the baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes to prevent burning and ensure even toasting.
  5. Cool Completely: Remove from the oven and let cool fully before storing.

Tips for Perfect Paleo Granola

  • Use Raw Ingredients: Raw nuts and seeds are best for homemade granola, as they allow for better control over flavor and texture during baking.
  • Customize Sweetness: Adjust the amount of natural sweetener in your recipe to match your personal taste preferences or dietary needs.
  • Play with Spices: Add spice blends like cinnamon, nutmeg, or even cayenne for unique flavors. Don’t be afraid to experiment!
  • Batch Size: Granola can be stored for weeks in airtight containers, so consider doubling your recipe if you go through it quickly.

Final Thoughts

Making Paleo-friendly granola at home is a simple way to enjoy one of life’s favorite snacks while staying true to your healthy eating habits. These recipes not only align with Paleo principles but also offer a level of flexibility that allows you to cater to your unique taste preferences. Whether you’re enjoying the crunchy nuts and seeds in the classic recipe, indulging your chocolate cravings, or trying out savory twists, these granolas are guaranteed to become a staple in your home.

Take a bit of time to prepare, bake, and savor your homemade Paleo granola. Once you see how easy and delicious it is, you’ll never go back to store-bought options. Happy granola-making!
