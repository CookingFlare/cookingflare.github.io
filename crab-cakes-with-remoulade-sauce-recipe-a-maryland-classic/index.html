Crab Cakes with Remoulade Sauce Recipe: A Maryland Classic


Crab cakes are a beloved dish, and when it comes to this culinary delight, Maryland takes the crown. The state is renowned for its delicious crab cakes, made with fresh crab meat and a blend of seasonings that bring out the natural flavors of the sea. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating the perfect Maryland-style crab cakes, accompanied by a tangy remoulade sauce that adds a delightful kick to each bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh lump crab meat
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Remoulade Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons chopped pickles
  • 1 tablespoon capers, drained and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, gently pick through the crab meat to remove any shells or cartilage. Be careful not to break up the lumps too much, as the texture is a crucial element of the perfect crab cake.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, mayonnaise, beaten egg, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, green onions, parsley, Old Bay seasoning, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. Gently fold the crab meat into the breadcrumb mixture, being careful not to overmix. The goal is to combine the ingredients without breaking up the delicate crab meat too much.

  4. Once the crab meat is evenly distributed throughout the mixture, shape it into patties of your desired size. For a traditional Maryland-style crab cake, aim for patties that are about 3 inches in diameter and 1 inch thick.

  5. In a skillet over medium-high heat, heat a generous amount of oil. Carefully place the crab cakes in the hot oil and cook until golden brown and crispy on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side.

  6. While the crab cakes are cooking, prepare the remoulade sauce. In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, chopped pickles, capers, fresh lemon juice, hot sauce, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients thoroughly.

  7. Once the crab cakes are cooked to perfection, remove them from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  8. Serve the crab cakes hot, with a dollop of the remoulade sauce on top or on the side. The zesty remoulade sauce perfectly complements the delicate flavors of the crab cakes, adding a tangy and creamy element to each bite.

Conclusion:

Crab cakes with remoulade sauce are a true Maryland classic, capturing the essence of the state’s culinary heritage. The combination of fresh lump crab meat, seasonings, and the tangy remoulade sauce creates a dish that is rich in flavor and texture. Whether enjoyed as an appetizer, main course, or even in sandwiches, these crab cakes are sure to impress even the most discerning palates. So, gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and embark on a culinary journey to the heart of Maryland with this delicious and timeless recipe.
