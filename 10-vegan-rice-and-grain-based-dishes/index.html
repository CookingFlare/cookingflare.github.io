10 Vegan Rice and Grain-Based Dishes


Rice and grains are the backbone of countless cuisines worldwide, offering a versatile foundation for nutrient-packed dishes that are both satisfying and diverse. When it comes to vegan cooking, these staples open the door to endless flavorful possibilities. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, these 10 vegan rice and grain-based dishes will inspire you to experiment with colors, textures, and global flavors, all while staying plant-powered.

1. Lemongrass Coconut Rice Bowl

Infused with fragrant lemongrass and creamy coconut milk, this Southeast Asian-inspired dish is a delightful infusion of flavors. Start by cooking jasmine rice in coconut milk combined with water, and add finely chopped lemongrass stalks for a subtle citrus aroma. Once the rice is cooked, top it with stir-fried vegetables like bok choy, carrots, and bell peppers. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and freshly chopped cilantro for a vibrant finish.

This dish is a perfect choice for weeknight dinners when you need something simple but flavorful. Pair it with a spicy vegan sambal or peanut sauce on the side for extra zest.

2. Spanish-Style Vegan Paella

Paella traditionally celebrates Mediterranean flavors, and it’s easy to create a vegan-friendly version that is just as rich and indulgent. Begin with short-grain Spanish rice and sauté garlic, onions, and saffron in olive oil for an aromatic base. Add a colorful mix of vegetables such as artichokes, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, and green beans. Toss in smoked paprika and a splash of vegetable broth for depth, then allow the rice to simmer until tender.

To finish, incorporate chickpeas for protein and sprinkle with chopped parsley. Serve directly from the pan for a sense of authenticity that transports you straight to Spain.

3. Wild Rice Stuffed Bell Peppers

Bell peppers are a versatile vessel for stuffing, and wild rice makes the perfect filling. Begin by cooking wild rice until fluffy, then mix it with sautéed onions, garlic, mushrooms, and spinach. For added flavor, stir in chopped sun-dried tomatoes and season with Italian herbs like oregano and thyme. Cut the peppers in half, remove the seeds, and stuff them with the prepared mixture.

Bake the stuffed peppers until they’re tender and slightly caramelized, and finish with a drizzle of balsamic glaze for complexity. This dish works wonderfully for dinner parties or meal prep.

4. Kimchi Fried Rice with Tofu

A spicy, tangy fusion dish, vegan kimchi fried rice offers the bold flavors of Korean cuisine. Use day-old cooked rice for the best texture. Start by sautéing garlic and green onions in sesame oil, then mix in chopped kimchi and crumbled tofu. Stir-fry the rice and season with soy sauce, gochujang (Korean chili paste), and a touch of maple syrup for balance.

Once the fried rice is sizzling and aromatic, top it with fresh cucumber slices, roasted seaweed, and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds for garnish. This dish delivers heat and umami in every bite.

5. Quinoa Chickpea Salad with Fresh Herbs

Perfect for lunches and light dinners, this refreshing quinoa chickpea salad is packed with protein and nutrients. Cook quinoa until fluffy and let it cool. Toss it with cooked chickpeas, diced cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, and red onion. Dress the salad with a zesty combination of olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, and chopped parsley.

For an extra kick, sprinkle on sumac or smoked paprika. This salad is equally delicious as a side dish or a standalone meal. The bright flavors make it ideal for picnics or potlucks.

6. Mushroom Risotto with Arborio Rice

Creamy and comforting, vegan mushroom risotto is an Italian-inspired dish sure to impress. Start by sautéing minced shallots and garlic in olive oil, then add sliced mushrooms for an earthy flavor. Stir in Arborio rice and let it toast slightly before deglazing the pan with dry white wine. Gradually add vegetable stock, stirring continuously to achieve a creamy texture.

Enrich the risotto with nutritional yeast for a cheesy undertone and finish with freshly chopped parsley and cracked black pepper. Pair this with a crisp salad or roasted vegetables for a complete meal.

7. Freekeh Tabbouleh

Freekeh, a roasted green wheat full of nutrients, makes for a bold twist on traditional tabbouleh. Cook freekeh according to the package instructions, and let it cool. Mix it with finely chopped parsley, mint, cucumbers, and tomatoes. The dressing is simple yet effective: olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt.

This dish is wonderfully fresh and pairs beautifully with vegan falafel or pita bread. Freekeh imparts a slightly nutty flavor and chewy texture, making it a wholesome addition to your repertoire.

8. Vegan Jambalaya with Brown Rice

Inspired by the comforting flavors of Louisiana cuisine, this vegan jambalaya embraces smoky spices and hearty vegetables. Cook brown rice in advance. In a large skillet, sauté celery, bell peppers, onions, and garlic in olive oil. Add diced tomatoes, okra, and kidney beans or black-eyed peas for texture.

Season the dish with Cajun or Creole spice blends, including paprika, thyme, and cayenne pepper. Mix in the rice and let it simmer until the flavors meld. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve with a side of cornbread for an authentic experience.

9. Barley and Vegetable Casserole

For a comforting, oven-baked dish, barley makes an excellent grain. In a casserole dish, combine cooked barley with tender veggies like butternut squash, kale, and mushrooms. Layer this mixture with a creamy vegan béchamel sauce made from plant-based milk, flour, and nutritional yeast for richness.

Bake until bubbly and golden on top, and serve hot. This is an excellent make-ahead casserole that tastes even better after reheating, perfect for busy weeknights or family gatherings.

10. Sweet Rice and Coconut Porridge

Rounding out the list is a dessert-inspired rice dish that doubles as a satisfying breakfast or snack. Cook short-grain rice in a mix of coconut milk and plant-based milk, sweetening with a touch of maple syrup or brown sugar. Add cinnamon sticks and cardamom pods for warmth and fragrance.

Once the rice is creamy, remove the spices and top the porridge with fresh mango, shredded coconut, and a drizzle of almond butter. This dish is an indulgent yet nutritious way to highlight rice’s versatility beyond savory preparations.

Rice and grains are much more than humble sides—they’re the stars of these plant-powered meals. Incorporating ingredients like herbs, spices, and vegetables from various cuisines creates a world of possibilities right in your kitchen. Transform these staples into dishes filled with flavor and nutrition, delighting vegans and non-vegans alike. Now it’s time to dig in and let creativity shine!
