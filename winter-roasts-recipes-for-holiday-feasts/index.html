Winter Roasts: Recipes for Holiday Feasts


Winter is the perfect time to gather around the dinner table with loved ones and enjoy a hearty, comforting roast. Whether you’re hosting a holiday feast or just want a cozy night in, these winter roast recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings and impress your guests.

Beef Roast with Red Wine and Mushrooms

This classic beef roast is elevated by the rich flavors of red wine and earthy mushrooms. Perfect for a special occasion or a cold winter’s night.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 pound beef roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 cups mushrooms (button, cremini, or shiitake), sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Season the beef roast with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the beef roast until browned on all sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion to the Dutch oven and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  5. Add the red wine and beef broth to the Dutch oven, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Add the sliced mushrooms to the Dutch oven and cook until they release their liquid and start to brown, about 10-15 minutes.
  7. Return the beef roast to the Dutch oven and add the thyme. Cover with a lid and transfer to the preheated oven.
  8. Roast for 2-3 hours, or until the beef reaches your desired level of doneness. Let rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Pork Roast with Apple Cider Glaze

This sweet and tangy pork roast is perfect for a holiday gathering. The apple cider glaze adds a delicious twist to this classic dish.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 pound pork roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the pork roast with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the pork roast until browned on all sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion to the skillet and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the apple cider, honey, Dijon mustard, and dried sage.
  6. Brush the apple cider glaze all over the pork roast, making sure to coat evenly.
  7. Transfer the pork roast to a roasting pan and roast for 2-3 hours, or until the pork reaches your desired level of doneness. Let rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Lamb Roast with Lemon and Herbs

This bright and citrusy lamb roast is perfect for a winter evening. The combination of lemon, garlic, and herbs adds a delicious and refreshing twist to this classic dish.

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 pound lamb roast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 lemons, juiced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the lamb roast with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the lamb roast until browned on all sides, about 2-3 minutes per side. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for 1 minute.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, chopped rosemary, and chopped thyme.
  6. Brush the lemon and herb mixture all over the lamb roast, making sure to coat evenly.
  7. Transfer the lamb roast to a roasting pan and roast for 2-3 hours, or until the lamb reaches your desired level of doneness. Let rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Vegetarian Option: Eggplant and Red Pepper Roast

This flavorful and colorful vegetarian roast is perfect for a meatless holiday gathering. The combination of eggplant, red peppers, and tomatoes adds a delicious and satisfying twist to this classic dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds
  • 2 large red bell peppers, sliced into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss together the sliced eggplant, red bell peppers, and diced tomatoes.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, garlic, and dried oregano.
  4. Pour the olive oil mixture over the eggplant and vegetable mixture, tossing to coat evenly.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a roasting pan and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and lightly browned. Let rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.

These winter roast recipes are sure to become new favorites in your household. Whether you’re hosting a holiday feast or just want a cozy night in, these delicious and comforting dishes are sure to satisfy your cravings and impress your guests. So go ahead, get roasting, and enjoy the warmth and comfort of the winter season with your loved ones.
