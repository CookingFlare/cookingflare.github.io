Creamy Scalloped Potatoes: A Cheesy Side Dish for Christmas Dinner


The holiday season is a time for cherished traditions, festive decorations, and, of course, delicious food. When it comes to planning the perfect Christmas dinner, finding the ideal side dish to complement the main course is essential. One classic dish that never fails to impress is creamy scalloped potatoes. This rich and indulgent side dish is a perfect addition to any holiday feast, offering a delightful combination of tender potatoes, creamy sauce, and gooey melted cheese. Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or an intimate holiday meal, creamy scalloped potatoes are sure to be a hit with everyone at the table.

Ingredients

  • 6 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish and set it aside.

  2. Prepare the Potatoes: Peel the potatoes and slice them thinly, about 1/8 inch thick. It’s important to slice the potatoes uniformly to ensure even cooking.

  3. Make the Creamy Sauce: In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for a minute until fragrant. Sprinkle the flour over the melted butter and whisk constantly for about 2 minutes to make a roux. Slowly pour in the milk and heavy cream while whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Add the dried thyme, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. Allow the sauce to simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.

  4. Layer the Potatoes: Arrange a layer of sliced potatoes in the prepared baking dish, slightly overlapping each other. Pour a portion of the creamy sauce over the potatoes and sprinkle a handful of Gruyère cheese on top. Repeat the layering process until all the potatoes, sauce, and cheese are used, ensuring that the top layer is generously covered with cheese.

  5. Bake the Potatoes: Cover the baking dish with foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for 45 minutes, then remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the top is golden brown and bubbly.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the scalloped potatoes are done baking, remove them from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes. Garnish with freshly chopped thyme leaves and grated Parmesan cheese before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, consider adding crispy bacon or caramelized onions between the layers of potatoes.
  • If you prefer a sharper flavor, substitute some of the Gruyère cheese with aged cheddar or Swiss cheese.
  • To make this dish ahead of time, assemble the scalloped potatoes and refrigerate them before baking. Simply increase the baking time to ensure the potatoes are heated through.

Creamy scalloped potatoes are a timeless holiday favorite that brings warmth and comfort to any Christmas dinner table. The luscious combination of tender potatoes, velvety cream sauce, and melted cheese creates a dish that is both elegant and satisfying. Whether paired with roast turkey, glazed ham, or roasted vegetables, these creamy scalloped potatoes are certain to become a beloved tradition in your holiday menu. So, this Christmas, treat your loved ones to this delectable side dish and watch as it becomes a new festive staple in your home.
