Spring Vegetable Recipes for a Fresh Easter Table


As the days grow longer and the flowers begin to bloom, the arrival of spring signals a time of renewal, and what better way to celebrate than with a fresh, vibrant Easter table filled with seasonal vegetables? Spring is the season of rebirth, and its bounty offers an array of colorful vegetables that not only taste delicious but also add a burst of color to your festive table.

Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or a small, intimate Easter brunch, incorporating spring vegetables into your meal is a wonderful way to showcase the season’s flavors. These recipes are designed to be easy to prepare, healthy, and perfect for serving to your loved ones as you celebrate the holiday.

1. Spring Vegetable Medley with Lemon and Herb Butter

One of the simplest yet most flavorful ways to serve spring vegetables is in a medley with a zesty lemon and herb butter. This dish highlights the natural sweetness of spring produce like carrots, peas, asparagus, and baby potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup of baby carrots, peeled
  • 1 cup of baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 cup of fresh peas (or frozen, thawed)
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Start by boiling the baby potatoes in a large pot of salted water for about 10 minutes until tender. Add the carrots and cook for an additional 5 minutes, then toss in the asparagus. Cook everything together for 2 minutes before adding the peas. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Lemon Herb Butter: In a small saucepan, melt the butter and olive oil together over medium heat. Stir in the lemon juice, zest, thyme, and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Combine: Pour the lemon and herb butter over the drained vegetables and toss gently to coat. Serve immediately as a colorful and fragrant side dish that pairs perfectly with roasted meats or a simple Easter roast.

2. Roasted Spring Root Vegetables with Honey and Balsamic Glaze

Root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and turnips come alive in the spring, and roasting them enhances their natural sweetness. By drizzling honey and balsamic glaze over the vegetables, this dish achieves a perfect balance of savory and sweet.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium carrots, peeled and sliced into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 medium parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 2 medium turnips, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper for easy cleanup.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: Toss the carrots, parsnips, and turnips in olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them evenly on the baking sheet in a single layer.

  3. Roast: Place the baking sheet in the oven and roast the vegetables for about 30-35 minutes, or until golden and tender, tossing halfway through for even cooking.

  4. Glaze: While the vegetables are roasting, combine the honey and balsamic vinegar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir constantly and cook for about 5-7 minutes until the glaze thickens.

  5. Finish: Once the vegetables are roasted, remove them from the oven and drizzle the balsamic honey glaze over them. Toss gently to coat and garnish with freshly chopped parsley before serving.

3. Asparagus and Spring Pea Salad with Goat Cheese and Pistachios

This vibrant salad is light, refreshing, and full of spring’s freshest offerings. With the crunchy texture of asparagus and peas paired with the creamy tang of goat cheese and the rich flavor of pistachios, this salad is a perfect complement to any Easter feast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup fresh peas (or frozen, thawed)
  • 2 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup pistachios, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the Vegetables: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and add the asparagus. Cook for about 2 minutes until bright green and tender. Add the peas to the pot for the last 30 seconds of cooking. Drain the vegetables and immediately transfer them to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Drain again and set aside.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

  3. Assemble the Salad: In a large salad bowl, combine the asparagus, peas, goat cheese, and pistachios. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

4. Spring Vegetable Frittata with Spinach, Leeks, and Parmesan

A frittata is a fantastic way to make the most of spring vegetables, and it’s versatile enough to serve at any time of day. This particular frittata combines the earthy flavors of spinach and leeks with the richness of eggs and Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 leek, sliced
  • 1 cup spinach, roughly chopped
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook the Vegetables: Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the leeks and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally until softened. Add the spinach and cook for another 2-3 minutes, just until wilted. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Prepare the Frittata: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and Parmesan cheese. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet. Cook on the stove for about 2 minutes, allowing the edges to set.

  4. Bake: Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the center of the frittata is set and slightly golden. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing into wedges and serving.

5. Pea and Mint Soup

This refreshing soup brings together the sweetness of fresh peas with the cool, bright flavor of mint. It’s an elegant addition to your Easter menu, served hot or cold, depending on your preference.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups fresh peas (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Onion and Garlic: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking for 3-4 minutes until softened and fragrant.

  2. Add the Peas and Broth: Add the peas to the pot and stir for 1-2 minutes. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Lower the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the peas are tender.

  3. Blend the Soup: Remove the soup from heat and let it cool slightly. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth, or transfer it to a blender in batches. If you like a creamier texture, add a touch of cream or coconut milk at this stage.

  4. Finish: Stir in the fresh mint leaves and season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice, if desired. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with extra mint leaves.

6. Grilled Spring Vegetables with Garlic and Herb Marinade

Grilling is a wonderful way to bring out the natural flavors of spring vegetables. A simple marinade of garlic, herbs, and olive oil enhances the vegetables’ earthy taste, making them the perfect addition to any Easter meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into large strips
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme
  • 1 teaspoon fresh oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, garlic, thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper.

  2. Marinate the Vegetables: Toss the vegetables in the marinade, making sure they are evenly coated. Let them marinate for at least 15 minutes.

  3. Grill: Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the vegetables for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until tender and lightly charred.

  4. Serve: Arrange the grilled vegetables on a platter and serve immediately, drizzled with any leftover marinade.

Spring is a time for new beginnings and fresh, vibrant flavors. By incorporating these seasonal vegetable recipes into your Easter menu, you’ll create a table that celebrates both the beauty and the bounty of the season. From the richness of roasted root vegetables to the brightness of fresh pea and mint soup, these dishes are sure to be a hit with your guests, offering a perfect balance of lightness and flavor to complement your Easter feast.
