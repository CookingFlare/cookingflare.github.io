Kolokithopita: Greek Zucchini Pie Recipe


Greek cuisine is rooted deeply in its connection to fresh, seasonal ingredients, hearty flavors, and old-world techniques passed down through generations. One dish that perfectly embodies these qualities is Kolokithopita, or Greek Zucchini Pie. This savory pastry is a celebration of zucchini, fresh herbs, tangy feta cheese, and flaky phyllo dough. Whether served as an appetizer, side dish, or light main course, Kolokithopita brings the bright, sunny flavors of Greece right into your kitchen.

Here, you’ll find a detailed guide to making this beloved dish. With its combination of wholesome ingredients and satisfying textures, this recipe will quickly become a household favorite.

What Makes Kolokithopita Special?

Kolokithopita holds an honored place in Greek culinary tradition. Zucchini is a staple ingredient in Mediterranean cooking, prized for its mild, earthy flavor and versatility. Combined with ingredients such as feta cheese, fresh dill, mint, and eggs, zucchini transforms into a rich, filling pie that is as nourishing as it is delicious. This dish epitomizes the harmonious balance of flavors that defines Greek cuisine.

Another hallmark of Kolokithopita is its use of phyllo dough, the paper-thin pastry known for its delicate, flaky layers. When baked, the golden crust forms a crisp barrier that contrasts beautifully with the tender zucchini filling. This combination of textures ensures that every bite is a sensory delight.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the preparation, it’s important to gather all the necessary ingredients. Kolokithopita may look complex, but with the right components on hand, the process is straightforward and fulfilling.

For the Zucchini Filling:

  • 3 large zucchinis (approx. 5 cups grated)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 green onions, finely chopped
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional for added depth)
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh dill
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Phyllo Dough:

  • 1 package of phyllo pastry sheets (usually comes with 10-12 sheets per pack)
  • ½ cup olive oil or melted butter, for brushing

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Zucchini Filling

The first step in making Kolokithopita is preparing the zucchini filling. Grate the zucchinis using a box grater or food processor. Transfer the grated zucchini into a large bowl, sprinkle them with a pinch of salt, and let them sit for 15–20 minutes. This resting period allows the zucchini to release excess liquid.

Afterward, squeeze the zucchini to remove as much moisture as possible. You can use a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth for this step—this is crucial to avoid a soggy filling later on.

In a large skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and green onions and sauté until softened and fragrant. Combine the cooked onions with the drained zucchini in a large mixing bowl.

Add the eggs, crumbled feta cheese, Parmesan cheese (if using), olive oil, dill, mint, salt, and pepper to the bowl. Stir thoroughly until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated. Set the filling aside.

2. Prepare the Phyllo Dough

Phyllo dough can be delicate to work with, but the trick lies in keeping it covered with a damp kitchen towel while working to prevent it from drying out.

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil or butter. Choose a rectangular or circular dish, depending on your preference.

Place one sheet of phyllo dough into the dish and gently brush it with olive oil. Repeat this process for 5–6 sheets, layering them one on top of another to form the pie’s base. Allow the edges of the phyllo to hang over the sides of the dish; you’ll fold these over later.

3. Assemble the Pie

Spread the zucchini filling evenly across the phyllo base. Make sure the filling reaches all corners for even baking. Once the filling is in place, top it with additional layers of phyllo dough. Again, brush each sheet with olive oil before adding the next. Use about 5–6 sheets to cover the pie completely.

Carefully fold the overhanging edges of the bottom layers inward, creating a neat border. Brush the top and edges of the phyllo generously with olive oil to ensure a golden, crisp crust during baking.

4. Bake to Perfection

Place the Kolokithopita in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 45–50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crisp. If your oven tends to brown foods quickly, you can cover the pie lightly with aluminum foil halfway through baking to avoid over-browning.

Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool for 10–15 minutes before slicing. Kolokithopita is easiest to cut when slightly cooled and holds its shape beautifully.

Serving Kolokithopita

Kolokithopita is incredibly versatile and can be served warm or at room temperature. It makes an excellent appetizer, paired with a side of Greek yogurt or tzatziki sauce. As a main course, enjoy it alongside a fresh Greek salad with cucumbers, tomatoes, olives, and a drizzle of olive oil. For a heartier spread, serve it with roasted lamb or chicken.

This pie can also be stored in the refrigerator for up to three days, making it a convenient option for meal prep or casual gatherings with friends and family.

Tips for Perfect Kolokithopita

1. Work Quickly with Phyllo Dough: Phyllo dries out fast, so keep unused sheets covered with a damp towel while assembling the pie.

2. Drain Zucchini Thoroughly: Excess moisture is the enemy of a crispy pie. Take care to thoroughly squeeze out liquid to ensure your filling stays rich and creamy.

3. Experiment with Herbs: Dill and mint are traditional, but you can add parsley, oregano, or a touch of basil for a personal twist.

4. Use High-Quality Olive Oil: Since olive oil is central to Greek flavor, a robust extra-virgin olive oil will elevate every layer of the dish.

Why Kolokithopita Deserves a Place on Your Table

Kolokithopita is far more than just a zucchini pie—it’s a warm embrace of Greek culture, an homage to simple yet impactful flavors, and a testament to the enduring beauty of handmade cooking. The balance of fresh vegetables, salty cheese, aromatic herbs, and flaky pastry creates a dish that feels indulgent yet wholesome, traditional yet adaptable.

By making Kolokithopita, you can transport yourself and your loved ones to a taverna overlooking the Aegean Sea, all from the comfort of your own kitchen. Whether you’re exploring Greek food for the first time or returning to an old favorite, this recipe is your gateway to Mediterranean inspiration.

Kolokithopita invites creativity and care in the kitchen, making it a rewarding dish for both novice and seasoned cooks alike. Gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the flavors of Greece enchant your taste buds!
