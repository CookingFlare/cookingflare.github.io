Delicious Vegetarian Thanksgiving Main Dish Recipes


Are you looking for some mouthwatering vegetarian recipes to impress your guests this Thanksgiving? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share some delicious and satisfying vegetarian main dish recipes that will leave everyone at the table asking for seconds. These recipes are not only packed with flavor but also easy to make, allowing you to spend more time enjoying the holiday with your loved ones.

1. Stuffed Butternut Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized butternut squashes
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut the butternut squashes in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds.
  3. Place the squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up, and roast them in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes or until tender.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the quinoa according to the package instructions.
  5. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  6. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
  7. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become tender.
  8. Stir in the baby spinach and cook until wilted.
  9. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, mushroom-spinach mixture, dried cranberries, and pecans. Season with salt and pepper.
  10. Once the butternut squashes are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly.
  11. Carefully scoop out some of the flesh from each squash half, leaving about a 1/2-inch border.
  12. Mix the scooped-out flesh with the quinoa mixture.
  13. Stuff each squash half with the quinoa filling.
  14. Return the stuffed squashes to the oven and bake for an additional 15 minutes.
  15. Serve warm and enjoy!

2. Lentil and Mushroom Wellington

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green lentils
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 tablespoon non-dairy milk (for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the green lentils according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  4. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
  5. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become tender.
  6. Stir in the cooked lentils, breadcrumbs, soy sauce, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface.
  8. Spread the lentil and mushroom mixture evenly over the puff pastry, leaving a border around the edges.
  9. Carefully roll up the pastry, enclosing the filling, and seal the edges.
  10. Place the Wellington on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, seam side down.
  11. Brush the top of the Wellington with non-dairy milk.
  12. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
  13. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  14. Serve the Lentil and Mushroom Wellington with your favorite Thanksgiving sides.

3. Roasted Vegetable Tart

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the sliced eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and red onion on a baking sheet.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss the vegetables to evenly coat them with the oil and spices.
  5. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  6. Meanwhile, roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface.
  7. Place the puff pastry on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  8. Using a sharp knife, score a border around the edges of the puff pastry, about 1 inch from the edge.
  9. Prick the center of the puff pastry with a fork to prevent it from rising too much.
  10. Once the vegetables are roasted, arrange them on top of the puff pastry, staying within the scored border.
  11. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese over the vegetables.
  12. Bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes or until the puff pastry is golden brown and cooked through.
  13. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly.
  14. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

These vegetarian main dish recipes are sure to delight both vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike. They are bursting with flavor, textures, and colors that will make your Thanksgiving feast truly memorable. So, gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and get ready to impress your guests with these delicious dishes. Happy Thanksgiving!
