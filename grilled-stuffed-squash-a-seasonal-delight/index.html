Grilled Stuffed Squash: A Seasonal Delight


Autumn is in the air, which means it’s time to bring the warmth of the kitchen to the backyard grill. Grilled Stuffed Squash is not only a tasty way to welcome the fall season, but it’s also an easy and versatile dish to prepare. This vegetarian delight is sure to satisfy hearty appetites while showcasing the bounty of fall produce. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of creating this seasonal favorite.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized squash (such as acorn, delicates, or butternut)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup panko bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated mozzarella cheese

Instructions

First, preheat your grill to medium heat, approximately 350°F (175°C). While the grill is heating up, prepare the squash for stuffing. Using a spoon or small knife, scoop out and discard the seeds and fibers from the squash’s interior.

Next, create the stuffing mixture. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the olive oil, 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese, 2 minced garlic cloves, and season with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Gently fold in the chopped fresh spinach, panko bread crumbs, and 1/4 cup grated mozzarella cheese.

Now, your grill is ready for the squash. Place each stuffed squash half on the grill cut-side down. Cover and grill for approximately 25-30 minutes, until tender and golden brown.

To achieve perfectly grilled squash, avoid overcooking, as it may result in a dry, rubbery texture. Also, a word to the wise, wearing oven mitts when handling hot squash will prevent those painful, “I shouldn’t have grabbed that without gloves” moments.

Once your squash is grilled to perfection, it’s time to enjoy this tasty vegetarian delicacy that’s sure to impress your friends and family alike. Grilled Stuffed Squash is a front porch-worthy, fall gathering must-have that highlights the flavors of the harvest season.

Serving Suggestions

This seasonal delight can be enhanced with the addition of your favorite fall vegetables like butternut squash, Brussels sprouts, or pumpkin gnocchi to create a satisfying meal. For added flair, try serving your Grilled Stuffed Squash with a dollop of garlic butter or creamy herbed dip.

Indulge in the cozy flavors of the season and prepare for your family and friends to ask for seconds. Happy grilling!

Additional Tips:

  • For year-round enjoyment, substitute your favorite seasonal vegetables for a more tropical spin, using zucchini or yellow squash.
  • Prepping and measuring ingredients ahead of time will make the stuffing process smoother and less chaotic.
  • Store any leftovers in the fridge within 2 hours of preparation to prevent bacterial growth. Enjoy for up to 3 days.

Are you ready to embrace the seasonal delight of grilled stuffed squash? This versatile dish can easily become a staple in your culinary adventures this fall!
