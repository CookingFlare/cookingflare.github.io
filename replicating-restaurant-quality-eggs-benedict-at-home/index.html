Replicating Restaurant-quality Eggs Benedict at Home


Eggs Benedict is a timeless classic brunch dish that’s adored by many. The beauty of this dish lies in its simplicity - a perfectly poached egg, crispy Canadian bacon or sausage, and a creamy, tangy sauce, all on a warm, flaky English muffin. You might have enjoyed this delightful meal at various restaurants, wondering how to reproduce that restaurant-quality experience on your own. Don’t worry; I’ve got you covered! Follow this recipe to whip up a restaurant-worthy Eggs Benedict right in your kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large English muffins
  • 4 slices of thick-cut Canadian bacon or 4 links of cooked sausage
  • 4 large eggs
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup canned or fresh Hollandaise sauce
  • Fresh arugula or spinach leaves
  • Salt, to taste

Directions:

  1. Begin by setting up a draining rack near your stovetop. This helps ensure that your cooked items dry thoroughly. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C).

  2. Split your English muffins in half and toast each piece under your broiler or regular toaster until golden and crispy. Transfer the muffins to the prepped draining rack.

  3. While the muffins toast, cook your Canadian bacon or sausage. If using bacon, opt for the thick-cut variety, as it will taste best in Eggs Benedict.

  4. In a non-stick skillet over medium heat, lay the Canadian bacon or cook the sausage. Cook the Canadian bacon until it’s tender and golden, or until the sausage reaches a safe internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

  5. As the Canadian bacon or sausage cooks, prepare your poached eggs. There are two methods for poaching eggs that yield fantastic results.

Method 1: The two-stage poach:

a. Fill a medium saucepan with 2-3 inches of water. Add a dash of vinegar to help the eggs maintain their shape. Salt the water — it will enhance the flavor of the eggs once cooked.

b. Bring the water to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. This may take a few minutes so that you can adequately measure your eggs’ depth in water.

c. Lower your stove’s heat to low once the water’s simmering. Crack an egg into a cup, avoiding any high rims. You can also use a cute egg poacher if you have one.

d. Ordinary eggs will cook in about 3 minutes. To cook extra-large eggs, keep them in the water for up to 4 minutes.

e. Use a slotted spoon to gently remove your poached eggs from the water.

Method 2: The vortex poach:

a. Fill a medium saucepan with 1-2 inches of water, and add some vinegar. Heat the water until it’s just below a simmer.

b. Using your finger, create a gentle whirlpool in the water. This is your tableside theater moment, so make it count.

c. Crack an egg into the water right near the center of the whirlpool. Cook the egg in this unique whirlpool until it takes form, about 3 minutes for the regular-sized eggs.

d. Carefully remove the eggs with a slotted spoon to have a fun twist on a traditional poaching method.

  1. Now, it’s time to combine all your components in a beautiful and delightful brunch. Toasted muffin or english muffin, cooked Canadian bacon or sausage, poached eggs, freshly ground black pepper, Hollandaise sauce, and arugula or spinach leaves.

  2. Divide the cooked Canadian bacon or sausage over the two English muffin bottoms. Place one perfectly poached egg over it.

  3. Drizzle a generous helping of Hollandaise sauce over the eggs.

  4. Add a dash of freshly ground black pepper and a handful of fresh arugula or spinach leaves.

  5. Top with the toasted muffin tops, and, if desired, sprinkle some extra salt for seasoning.

Voila! Now you have delicious, restaurant-quality Eggs Benedict ready to delight both you and your guests.

When done right, Eggs Benedict can be a luscious, indulgent experience that tastes just as good with a hot cup of coffee or tea on a relaxing morning. By following this recipe, you can bring the rich flavors and textures of restaurant-quality Eggs Benedict right into your home.

Enjoy and Bon Appétit!
