A Taste of Jeju Island: Making Jeju Seafood Stew


Jeju Island, located off the southern coast of South Korea, is known for its stunning natural beauty and unique culinary traditions. One dish that captures the essence of Jeju’s coastal charm is the Jeju Seafood Stew. Packed with an array of fresh seafood, this hearty and flavorful stew is a true delight for seafood lovers. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making this delectable dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make Jeju Seafood Stew, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 500g assorted fresh seafood (such as clams, mussels, shrimp, and squid)
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 medium-sized potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 4 cups fish stock or vegetable stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the seafood. Rinse the clams and mussels under cold water to remove any sand or grit. Peel and devein the shrimp, leaving the tails intact. Clean the squid and slice it into rings. Set aside.

  2. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté until fragrant and the onions become translucent.

  3. Add the cubed potato, sliced carrot, and zucchini to the pot. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  4. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, gochujang, and sesame oil. Pour this mixture into the pot and stir well to coat the vegetables.

  5. Add the fish or vegetable stock to the pot, and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Now it’s time to add the seafood. Gently place the clams, mussels, shrimp, and squid into the simmering stew. Cover the pot and cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the seafood is cooked through.

  7. Taste the stew and season with salt and pepper according to your preference. If you like it spicier, you can add more gochujang.

  8. Finally, garnish the stew with chopped green onions. Serve hot with steamed rice or crusty bread.

The Taste of Jeju Island

Jeju Seafood Stew is more than just a dish; it’s a taste of Jeju Island itself. The combination of fresh seafood, aromatic spices, and the warmth of homemade stock creates a rich and comforting flavor that will transport you to the shores of this enchanting island.

The beauty of this stew lies in its versatility. Feel free to experiment with different types of seafood based on your preference and availability. You can also add other vegetables like mushrooms or cabbage to enhance the flavors further.

So, the next time you crave a taste of Jeju Island, try making this delightful Jeju Seafood Stew. It’s a dish that will not only satisfy your taste buds but also allow you to experience the magic of Jeju’s rich culinary heritage. Happy cooking!

Note: The recipe yields approximately 4 servings.
