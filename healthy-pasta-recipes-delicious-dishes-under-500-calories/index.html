Healthy Pasta Recipes: Delicious Dishes Under 500 Calories


Pasta is one of the most beloved comfort foods, but it often gets a bad reputation for being high in calories, especially when paired with rich sauces and cheese. However, you can still enjoy all the deliciousness of pasta without compromising on health or flavor. The key lies in selecting the right ingredients and making a few smart swaps to keep your dishes under 500 calories while still being satisfying and full of flavor.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of healthy pasta recipes that are not only low in calories but also packed with nutrients. Whether you’re trying to lose weight, maintain a healthy lifestyle, or simply want to eat lighter without sacrificing taste, these recipes are perfect for you.

1. Zucchini Noodles with Avocado Pesto

If you’re craving pasta but want to cut back on carbs, zucchini noodles (also known as “zoodles”) are an excellent alternative. Zucchini is low in calories but high in fiber, making it a great base for a healthy pasta dish. Combined with a creamy avocado pesto, this dish is both fresh and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, spiralized into noodles
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: pine nuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the avocado, basil, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice. Blend until smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the zucchini noodles over medium heat for about 3-4 minutes, just until they soften slightly. Avoid overcooking to prevent them from becoming mushy.
  3. Toss the zoodles with the avocado pesto sauce until well coated.
  4. Serve immediately, and garnish with pine nuts if desired.

This dish is incredibly refreshing, light, and creamy, with just under 300 calories per serving.

2. Whole Wheat Pasta Primavera

Whole wheat pasta is a great option for those who want the heartiness of traditional pasta but with added fiber and nutrients. Paired with a medley of colorful vegetables, this whole wheat pasta primavera is a simple yet satisfying dish that keeps the calorie count low without skimping on flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz whole wheat pasta
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the whole wheat pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and red pepper flakes (if using), sautéing for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the cherry tomatoes, broccoli, bell pepper, and zucchini to the skillet. Sauté the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, until tender but still vibrant.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the vegetables, adding salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

This pasta primavera dish is bursting with vitamins and minerals, and it comes in at approximately 450 calories per serving.

3. Shrimp Scampi with Spaghetti Squash

Spaghetti squash is another excellent low-calorie pasta alternative. When roasted, its flesh separates into strands that mimic traditional spaghetti. Paired with shrimp scampi, this dish is both light and indulgent, without the heavy calories of a classic shrimp pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and remove the seeds. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and roast the squash for 40 minutes, cut side down, until tender.
  2. While the squash roasts, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes per side until pink and cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the white wine (if using) and lemon juice, stirring to combine.
  4. Once the spaghetti squash is done, use a fork to scrape the flesh into strands. Add the squash to the skillet, tossing it with the garlic and wine mixture.
  5. Return the shrimp to the skillet, tossing everything together. Sprinkle with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

With about 400 calories per serving, this dish is light, flavorful, and perfect for a healthy weeknight dinner.

4. Lentil Pasta with Roasted Vegetables

Lentil pasta is made from lentil flour, which is high in protein and fiber while being lower in calories compared to traditional pasta. When combined with roasted vegetables and a simple balsamic glaze, this dish is a nutritious and filling meal under 500 calories.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz lentil pasta
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss the cherry tomatoes, cauliflower, and bell pepper with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes, until tender and lightly caramelized.
  2. Cook the lentil pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the balsamic vinegar with a pinch of salt and pepper. Drizzle over the roasted vegetables.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the roasted vegetables and balsamic glaze. Garnish with fresh basil.

This dish packs a punch of plant-based protein from the lentil pasta and is just under 450 calories per serving.

5. Spaghetti with Turkey Meatballs

For a leaner version of classic spaghetti and meatballs, swap traditional beef meatballs for turkey. Turkey is lower in fat and calories, but it still delivers that hearty meatball flavor. Combined with a homemade marinara sauce, this dish is both satisfying and healthy.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz spaghetti (whole wheat or gluten-free if desired)
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (whole wheat or gluten-free)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 2 cups marinara sauce (preferably homemade or low-sugar)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, breadcrumbs, egg, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, salt, and pepper. Form into 12-15 meatballs and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  2. Bake the meatballs for 20-25 minutes until golden brown and cooked through.
  3. While the meatballs bake, cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. Heat the marinara sauce in a large saucepan over low heat. Once the meatballs are done, add them to the sauce and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Serve the turkey meatballs over the pasta, garnishing with fresh parsley.

At just under 500 calories per serving, this dish is a satisfying, lean option for pasta lovers.

Conclusion

Healthy pasta dishes don’t have to be bland or boring. With the right ingredients, you can create flavorful and satisfying meals that keep the calorie count low while still delivering the comfort and taste you crave. From zucchini noodles and spaghetti squash to lentil pasta and turkey meatballs, there are endless possibilities for making pasta a nutritious part of your diet.

Each of the recipes shared in this post is designed to be easy to prepare, flavorful, and under 500 calories per serving, making them perfect for anyone looking to eat healthier without compromising on taste. So, the next time you’re craving pasta, try one of these light and delicious dishes — you won’t regret it!
