Sous Vide Caramelized Onions: Unlocking Sweet and Savory Goodness


If you’re a fan of caramelized onions, you’re in for a treat. Sous vide cooking is a game-changer when it comes to achieving the perfect caramelization, bringing out the sweet and savory goodness of this humble ingredient. In this recipe, we’ll explore how sous vide can elevate caramelized onions to a whole new level, unlocking their rich flavor and versatile uses in various dishes.

The Magic of Sous Vide

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves vacuum-sealing food in a bag and cooking it to a precise temperature in a water bath. This gentle and precise method allows for even cooking and precise control over the end result, making it perfect for caramelizing onions to perfection.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we dive into the sous vide process, let’s gather the ingredients:

  • 4 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: a sprig of thyme or a bay leaf for added flavor

Sous Vide Setup

  1. Preheat your water bath to 185°F (85°C).
  2. While the water bath is heating up, prepare the onions by thinly slicing them.
  3. Divide the onions into two vacuum-seal bags, adding 1 tablespoon of butter and 1/2 tablespoon of olive oil to each bag.
  4. Add a pinch of salt and pepper to each bag, and if desired, throw in a sprig of thyme or a bay leaf for extra flavor.
  5. Vacuum-seal the bags, ensuring the onions are spread out in a single layer to ensure even cooking.

The Sous Vide Process

Once the water bath is at the desired temperature and the onions are prepared, it’s time to start the sous vide process:

  1. Gently place the sealed bags of onions into the water bath, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  2. Cook the onions for 12-14 hours. Yes, you read that right! The slow, low-temperature cooking is what will transform them into beautifully caramelized perfection.
  3. After the cooking time is up, carefully remove the bags from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature.

Finishing Touches

The sous vide process has worked its magic, but we’re not quite done yet. Here’s how to put the finishing touches on your sous vide caramelized onions:

  1. Once the onions have cooled, carefully open the bags and transfer the contents to a skillet.
  2. Heat the skillet over medium-high heat and cook the onions, stirring occasionally, until they reach your desired level of caramelization. This should take just a few minutes.
  3. Use the caramelized onions immediately in your favorite dishes, or store them in the refrigerator for later use. They can add depth of flavor to soups, sandwiches, pizzas, and so much more.

The Sweet and Savory Result

The long, slow cooking process in the sous vide bath transforms the raw onions into a beautifully caramelized, sweet, and savory delight. The butter and olive oil infuse the onions with richness, while the low temperature ensures that they caramelize evenly without any risk of burning. The result is a versatile condiment that can elevate a wide range of dishes with its deep, complex flavor.

Conclusion

Sous vide caramelized onions are a game-changer in the kitchen, offering a hassle-free way to achieve perfect caramelization every time. By harnessing the magic of sous vide, you can unlock the sweet and savory goodness of onions, taking your culinary creations to new heights. So, fire up your sous vide setup and get ready to savor the rich, caramelized goodness of this kitchen staple.
