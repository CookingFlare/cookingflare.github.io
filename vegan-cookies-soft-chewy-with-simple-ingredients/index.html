Vegan Cookies: Soft & Chewy with Simple Ingredients


Few things are as comforting as a batch of homemade cookies. Whether you’re enjoying them fresh out of the oven or savoring them with a warm drink, vegan cookies offer a delightful way to indulge without compromising on health or values. In this post, we’ll walk you through a foolproof recipe for soft and chewy vegan cookies that comes together using simple, wholesome ingredients. This recipe is adaptable, easy to follow, and perfect for all cookie enthusiasts—whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting your vegan journey.

Why Vegan Cookies Stand Out

Vegan cookies aren’t just a dietary alternative—they’re a testament to how simple ingredient swaps can yield equally delicious results. By skipping dairy and eggs, vegan cookies become more inclusive for various dietary preferences. The absence of animal products doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or texture. In fact, these cookies are irresistibly soft, perfectly chewy, and boast a natural richness that can rival any traditional cookie.

The beauty of vegan baking lies in its simplicity. Plant-based alternatives are easy to work with, versatile, and often add extra nutrients to your baked treats. Plus, they lend themselves beautifully to experimentation—you can mix and match flavors, textures, and ingredients to suit your mood or occasion.

So, let’s dive straight into creating a batch of delectable vegan cookies that will soon become your go-to recipe.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s a list of simple ingredients to make these soft and chewy vegan cookies:

  • Flour: 2½ cups of all-purpose flour. Feel free to use whole wheat or an alternative like spelt flour for added nutrition.
  • Plant-Based Butter: 1 cup (softened) vegan butter or margarine. Look for unsalted varieties.
  • Sugar: ¾ cup granulated sugar and ½ cup brown sugar. The combination of both creates the perfect chewy texture while adding depth of flavor.
  • Non-Dairy Milk: 3 tablespoons almond milk, soy milk, or your preferred plant-based milk.
  • Vanilla Extract: 2 teaspoons for a subtle, warm flavor.
  • Baking Soda: 1 teaspoon to help with the rise and texture.
  • Salt: ½ teaspoon to balance the sweetness.
  • Mix-Ins: 1 cup of vegan-friendly chocolate chips, chopped nuts, or dried fruit. You can get creative with your additions!

Recipe Directions

Follow these simple steps to create your own batch of soft and chewy vegan cookies. The process is beginner-friendly, but even seasoned bakers will appreciate how quick and easy it is.

1. Preheat Your Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats for easy cleanup.

2. Prepare the Wet Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the softened plant-based butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar. Using a mixer or whisk, cream these ingredients together until light and fluffy. This step helps incorporate air into the mix, ensuring your cookies turn out soft and chewy.

Next, add the non-dairy milk and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.

3. Add the Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture into the wet ingredients. Stir until a cohesive dough forms, but be careful not to overmix—overworking the dough can lead to tougher cookies.

4. Fold in Your Mix-Ins

Now comes the fun part—add your favorite mix-ins! Stir in vegan chocolate chips, chopped nuts, dried cranberries, or shredded coconut. You can even divide the dough and customize each batch with different flavors.

5. Shape the Cookies

Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them 2 inches apart. For uniform cookies, gently press each portion to flatten slightly (these cookies won’t spread much during baking).

6. Bake to Perfection

Place the cookies in the preheated oven and bake for 10-12 minutes. Aim for edges that are slightly golden while the centers remain soft for the perfect chewy texture. Keep an eye on them—they can go from perfect to overdone quickly!

7. Cool Before Serving

Remove the cookies from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheet for a couple of minutes before transferring them to a wire rack. Vegan cookies tend to firm up as they cool, so don’t worry if they seem a bit soft at first.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Cookies

1. Measure Your Ingredients Accurately

Precision is key in baking—especially for vegan recipes. Use measuring cups or a digital scale to ensure your ratios are spot-on.

2. Choose High-Quality Mix-Ins

The ingredients you choose can make or break the flavor. Opt for high-quality vegan chocolate chips with a smooth melt and rich taste. Or try roasted nuts and dried fruits for added texture.

3. Chill the Dough

For extra chewy cookies, refrigerate the dough for 30 minutes before baking. Chilling firm up the fats and allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.

4. Test Various Alternatives

Don’t hesitate to swap ingredients and experiment with flavors. For example:

  • Replace all-purpose flour with almond flour for a gluten-free option.
  • Use dark chocolate chunks instead of chips for a more indulgent bite.
  • Add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg for a warm spice note.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This vegan cookie recipe checks all the boxes. It’s:

  • Easy to Make: You don’t need complex techniques or specialty tools to whip up these cookies.
  • Customizable: Perfect for tweaking based on your mood, occasion, or pantry ingredients.
  • Quick: Ready in under 30 minutes from start to finish.

Not to mention, these cookies will win over even the most skeptical taste buds. The buttery richness, melt-in-your-mouth chewiness, and slight crisp at the edges make them irresistible.

Serving Suggestions & Storage

Pairing Ideas

Enjoy these cookies with a glass of cold almond milk, a hot mug of chai tea, or a scoop of your favorite dairy-free ice cream for the ultimate treat.

Storing Your Cookies

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to one week. If you want to save some for later, freeze the baked cookies in zip-top bags or sturdy containers for up to three months. Simply thaw and enjoy whenever the craving strikes!

Final Thoughts

There’s something magical about vegan baking—it’s a chance to explore new ingredients while crafting treats that everyone can enjoy. These soft and chewy vegan cookies epitomize that magic, showing that simple swaps can deliver mouthwatering results.

Whether you’re baking for yourself, loved ones, or a crowd, this recipe is destined to be a hit. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to make vegan cookies that will have everyone asking for seconds!
