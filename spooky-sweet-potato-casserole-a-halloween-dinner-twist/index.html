Spooky Sweet Potato Casserole: A Halloween Dinner Twist


Halloween is fast approaching, and it’s time to transform your dinner table with some spooky fun! This year, bid farewell to the boring old sweet potato casserole and give in to the dark delights of the Spooky Sweet Potato Casserole. Combining the creamy taste of sweet potatoes with a touch of Halloween flair, this dish is guaranteed to leave your family in both awe and delicious anticipation.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium sweet potatoes
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon orange food coloring
  • Additional butter for greasing
  • Marshmallows and candy corn, for topping

Equipment:

  • Baking sheet
  • Instant pot or large pot, for boiling
  • 8x8 square baking dish
  • Whisk
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Sifter
  • Spatula
  • Rubber scraper
  • Mixing bowls
  • Wooden spoon
  • Electric mixer (hand mixer or stand mixer)

Instructions:

  1. Start by prepping the sweet potatoes. Poke a few holes in them using a fork and microwave them on high for about 5 minutes. Rotate them halfway through to cook evenly. Once done, let them cool down before peeling and mashing them.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed sweet potatoes, softened unsalted butter, granulated sugar, eggs, cinnamon, nutmeg, ground cloves, and salt. Use a wooden spoon or a rubber scraper to mix everything together until it forms a smooth paste. This is the base of your Spooky Sweet Potato Casserole.

  3. To make the spooky orange color, combine heavy cream, pumpkin puree, and orange food coloring in a small saucepan. Whisk everything together until the orange food coloring has completely dissolved.

  4. Stir the orange cream mixture into the sweet potato base until fully incorporated. Pour the mixture into an Instant Pot or a large pot and use a sieve to sift in the cornstarch. Mix everything together until the cornstarch has dissolved.

  5. Place a fitted lid on your Instant Pot or transfer the mixture to a large pot if using stovetop. Cook on high heat for about 20 minutes, or until the mixture starts to thicken. Take it off the heat and let it cool down slightly before whisking in the vanilla extract.

  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease an 8x8 square baking dish. Transfer the sweet potato mixture into the dish and smooth the top with a spatula.

  7. Bake the Spooky Sweet Potato Casserole in the preheated oven for about 30 minutes. When the 30 minutes are up, it’s time for the finishing touches. Top the casserole with marshmallows and candy corn to create a spine-chilling effect.

  8. Put the dish back into the oven and bake for another 10-15 minutes, or until the marshmallows have reached your desired level of toasty-brownwopiness.

  9. Remove from the oven and allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before slicing. Serve the Spooky Sweet Potato Casserole warm, and watch the hauntingly delightful reactions on your family’s faces.

There you have it – a fully madeover sweet potato casserole that’s perfect for Halloween. This Spooky Sweet Potato Casserole tribute will bring smiles to your dining table this Halloween while rekindling warm memories of the upcoming spooktacular season. So go ahead, shine a little darkness upon your dinner table, and enjoy this belly-warming, delightful, and spooky treat. Happy cooking!
