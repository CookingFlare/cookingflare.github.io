The Best Homemade Chimichurri Recipe for Grilled Steak


Chimichurri is a bold, tangy sauce that originates from Argentina, known for its vibrant green color and complex flavors. This flavorful condiment is perfect for pairing with grilled meats, particularly steak, enhancing the rich flavors of the meat with its fresh, zesty kick. If you’re looking to elevate your grilled steak experience, making chimichurri at home is the way to go. In this blog post, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about making the best homemade chimichurri recipe that pairs perfectly with grilled steak.

What is Chimichurri?

Chimichurri is a fresh herb-based sauce made with parsley, garlic, vinegar, olive oil, and a few seasonings. The sauce is typically used as a marinade or topping for grilled meats, especially steaks. Its versatility allows it to be adjusted according to your taste, with variations including the addition of red pepper flakes for a little heat or citrus zest for extra brightness. The best part? Chimichurri is incredibly easy to make and doesn’t require any fancy ingredients or complex techniques. Whether you’re grilling a juicy ribeye, tender flank steak, or even chicken, chimichurri is an excellent way to infuse your dish with vibrant, mouthwatering flavors.

Why You Should Make Chimichurri from Scratch

While store-bought chimichurri is available, nothing compares to the fresh, aromatic taste of homemade chimichurri. Making your chimichurri from scratch gives you control over the ingredients, allowing you to fine-tune the flavors to your liking. Plus, it’s easy to whip up in just a few minutes and doesn’t require much effort at all. When paired with grilled steak, the homemade version offers a deliciously fresh contrast to the rich, smoky flavors of the meat.

Another reason to make your own chimichurri is that it’s free from any preservatives or artificial additives that can be found in some commercial sauces. It’s an all-natural, gluten-free, and dairy-free option that everyone can enjoy.

The Ingredients You Need for the Best Homemade Chimichurri

To make the best homemade chimichurri, you don’t need much. Here’s a simple list of the ingredients you’ll need:

Fresh Parsley

Fresh parsley is the star of the show when it comes to chimichurri. Its vibrant green color and fresh, herbal flavor provide the foundation for this sauce. Use flat-leaf parsley for the best results. Fresh parsley helps balance the tangy vinegar and oil, making the sauce bright and lively.

Garlic

Garlic adds a robust, savory depth of flavor to chimichurri. The sharpness of fresh garlic complements the fresh herbs and cuts through the richness of the grilled steak. For the best flavor, use fresh garlic cloves and chop them finely or crush them using a garlic press.

Red Wine Vinegar

Red wine vinegar is the acidic base of chimichurri. It provides the necessary tang to balance the oil and herbs. Red wine vinegar has a mild, fruity acidity that works beautifully in chimichurri. If you don’t have red wine vinegar, you can substitute with white wine vinegar or even apple cider vinegar, though the flavor may differ slightly.

Olive Oil

A high-quality extra virgin olive oil is crucial for a flavorful chimichurri. The olive oil provides richness and helps to bring all the ingredients together into a harmonious sauce. Choose a mild olive oil to allow the other flavors to shine, but don’t opt for an overly fruity one as it might overpower the taste of the herbs.

Red Pepper Flakes

Red pepper flakes add a touch of heat to chimichurri. While the sauce is not traditionally spicy, a little kick can enhance the overall flavor profile. You can adjust the amount of red pepper flakes depending on how spicy you like your chimichurri.

Oregano

Oregano is a classic herb used in chimichurri, adding an earthy and slightly bitter flavor that complements the fresh parsley. While you can use dried oregano, fresh oregano will give a stronger, more aromatic taste.

Salt and Pepper

Salt and pepper are essential for seasoning chimichurri and helping to balance the flavors. Add them to taste, keeping in mind that salt enhances the overall flavor of the sauce, while pepper adds a subtle, savory note.

Optional Additions

  • Lemon or Lime Zest: If you want to add an extra layer of citrusy freshness, consider adding a little lemon or lime zest to the sauce.
  • Shallots or Onion: A small amount of finely chopped shallots or onion can add a slight sweetness and depth of flavor to the sauce.
  • Cilantro: If you enjoy the taste of cilantro, you can substitute some of the parsley with cilantro for a more herbal twist.

How to Make Chimichurri

Making chimichurri from scratch is incredibly simple and doesn’t require any special equipment. Here’s a step-by-step guide to making your own chimichurri:

Step 1: Prepare the Fresh Herbs

Start by washing and drying your fresh parsley and oregano. Remove the thick stems from the parsley and chop both herbs finely. The finer you chop the herbs, the more the flavors will infuse into the sauce.

Step 2: Mince the Garlic

Peel and mince the garlic cloves. You can either use a sharp knife to chop the garlic finely or use a garlic press to get a finer consistency. Make sure the garlic is minced very well, as it helps to infuse the sauce with its strong flavor.

Step 3: Mix the Ingredients

In a bowl, combine the chopped parsley, oregano, minced garlic, and red pepper flakes. Add the red wine vinegar and olive oil, and stir everything together to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste. If you’re using any optional ingredients like lemon zest or cilantro, add them now.

Step 4: Let It Rest

For the best flavor, let the chimichurri rest for at least 30 minutes before using it. This allows the flavors to meld together and develop. If possible, let it sit for a few hours or even overnight in the fridge for a more intense flavor.

Step 5: Taste and Adjust

Before serving, taste the chimichurri and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you like it tangier, add a bit more vinegar. If you prefer more heat, add additional red pepper flakes. The beauty of chimichurri is that you can tailor it to your personal preference.

How to Serve Chimichurri with Grilled Steak

Once you’ve made your chimichurri, it’s time to pair it with a perfectly grilled steak. Chimichurri is incredibly versatile and pairs well with a variety of meats. Here are some tips for serving chimichurri with grilled steak:

1. Choose the Right Steak

Chimichurri pairs wonderfully with almost any cut of steak, but it’s particularly delicious with fattier cuts like ribeye, flank steak, skirt steak, or sirloin. The rich, smoky flavors of grilled steak complement the zesty, herbaceous chimichurri.

2. Grill Your Steak to Perfection

For the perfect steak, season it with salt and pepper, and grill it over high heat for a beautifully charred exterior and a tender interior. Use a meat thermometer to ensure it’s cooked to your desired doneness. Let the steak rest for a few minutes after grilling to allow the juices to redistribute.

3. Drizzle or Serve on the Side

You can either drizzle chimichurri over your grilled steak just before serving or serve it on the side as a dipping sauce. Drizzling it on top adds a burst of flavor to each bite, while serving it on the side allows everyone to add as much or as little as they like.

4. Pair with Sides

Chimichurri is the perfect complement to grilled vegetables, roasted potatoes, or even a fresh salad. The vibrant sauce adds a bright contrast to the savory flavors of the steak and side dishes.

Tips for Storing and Using Leftover Chimichurri

If you have leftover chimichurri, store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will last for up to a week. The flavors tend to improve as it sits, so it’s a great make-ahead option for future meals.

Chimichurri isn’t just for steak – it can also be used as a marinade for chicken, lamb, or even grilled vegetables. Try using it as a dressing for salads, a topping for roasted potatoes, or a flavorful dip for crusty bread.

Final Thoughts

Homemade chimichurri is a simple and flavorful sauce that can elevate any grilled steak or meat. Its fresh, herby taste paired with a touch of acidity and heat is the perfect complement to the rich flavors of the meat. With just a few basic ingredients, you can create a vibrant and mouthwatering sauce that will make your grilled steak shine.

So, next time you’re firing up the grill, don’t forget to make a batch of chimichurri. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue or enjoying a quiet dinner at home, this homemade sauce will take your grilled steak to the next level and leave everyone asking for more.
