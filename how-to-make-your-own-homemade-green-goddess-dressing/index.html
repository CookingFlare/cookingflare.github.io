How to Make Your Own Homemade Green Goddess Dressing


Green Goddess dressing is one of those creamy, herby, and zesty condiments that can elevate any meal. Its vibrant green color and fresh, bold flavors make it the perfect addition to salads, sandwiches, roasted vegetables, and even grilled meats. The good news is, making your own homemade Green Goddess dressing is simple and far superior to store-bought versions in terms of flavor and quality. You can customize it to suit your tastes, and best of all, you know exactly what’s going into it.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through how to make Green Goddess dressing from scratch, exploring the ingredients, methods, and some tips and variations for an unforgettable dressing that will take your dishes to the next level.

What is Green Goddess Dressing?

Green Goddess dressing is a creamy, herb-infused dressing that originated in the early 1920s at the Palace Hotel in San Francisco. It was created by the hotel’s chef to honor an actor who was performing in a play called The Green Goddess. Over time, the dressing became popular for its unique blend of fresh herbs and creamy texture.

The classic Green Goddess dressing is made with a combination of mayonnaise, sour cream or yogurt, herbs like tarragon, parsley, chives, and garlic, along with a touch of vinegar, lemon juice, and anchovies. The result is a tangy, herbal, and creamy dressing that has become a staple in many kitchens.

Ingredients for Homemade Green Goddess Dressing

To make a truly delicious homemade Green Goddess dressing, you need a few simple ingredients that come together to create that signature balance of creamy, tangy, and herbaceous flavor.

Base Ingredients:

  • Mayonnaise – The creamy base of the dressing. You can use full-fat or light mayo depending on your preference.
  • Sour Cream or Greek Yogurt – This adds a rich tanginess that balances the creamy mayo and gives the dressing its signature texture. Greek yogurt is a healthier alternative if you’re looking for extra protein.
  • Fresh Herbs – Parsley, tarragon, and chives are essential for that fresh, bright flavor. Fresh herbs provide the clean, green taste that defines the dressing.
  • Anchovy Paste or Anchovies – A small amount of anchovies (or anchovy paste) adds a savory, umami flavor that deepens the dressing’s complexity. If you’re not a fan of anchovies, you can omit them, but they truly enhance the overall flavor.
  • Garlic – Fresh garlic adds a sharp, aromatic element that cuts through the richness of the mayo and sour cream.
  • Lemon Juice – Freshly squeezed lemon juice brings acidity, balancing the creamy base and brightening the flavors.
  • White Wine Vinegar or Apple Cider Vinegar – Adds a touch of tanginess, helping to preserve the dressing and enhancing its flavor.
  • Salt and Pepper – To taste, to ensure all the flavors meld together perfectly.

Optional Additions:

  • Olive Oil – Adding a bit of olive oil helps thin the dressing, making it easier to drizzle over your salad or vegetables.
  • Dijon Mustard – For a subtle sharpness and depth of flavor, Dijon mustard can be added to the dressing.
  • Avocado – If you want a richer, smoother texture and a hint of sweetness, you can add ripe avocado to the mix.
  • Cilantro – For a different herbal flavor, cilantro can replace or complement some of the parsley, especially if you like a bit of a citrusy kick.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Homemade Green Goddess Dressing

Making Green Goddess dressing at home is an easy process that takes just a few minutes. Follow these simple steps to create a dressing that will blow your mind with its freshness and flavor.

1. Prepare the Herbs

Start by washing and drying the fresh herbs. You’ll want about a half-cup of mixed parsley, tarragon, and chives. Tarragon is the star of the show, so use it generously, but parsley and chives provide a nice balance.

Once the herbs are clean, chop them finely to release their oils and flavors. If you have a food processor or blender, this step will be even easier, as you can chop the herbs and mix the dressing in one go.

2. Combine the Base Ingredients

In a medium mixing bowl or food processor, combine the following base ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream (or Greek yogurt)
  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

The mayo and sour cream (or Greek yogurt) form the rich and creamy base of the dressing, while the vinegar and lemon juice provide acidity to balance the richness.

3. Add the Anchovies and Garlic

To bring that distinct umami flavor to the dressing, add 2 anchovy fillets (or 1 teaspoon of anchovy paste) and 1 clove of garlic, minced. If you’re using a food processor, just drop everything in at once and blend until smooth. If you’re mixing by hand, you’ll need to finely chop the anchovies and garlic, then mash them into the mayo and sour cream mixture with a fork or whisk.

4. Stir in the Herbs

Add the finely chopped 1/4 cup parsley, 2 tablespoons tarragon, and 2 tablespoons chives to the mix. Stir the ingredients together until well-combined, or pulse in the food processor for a few seconds. This step infuses the dressing with that signature herbaceous flavor, and you’ll start to see the dressing take on its vibrant green hue.

5. Season the Dressing

Now it’s time to season! Add salt and freshly cracked black pepper to taste. Start with a pinch of salt and a few cracks of pepper, then taste and adjust. If you like a little more tang, add an extra squeeze of lemon juice. If you prefer a thicker dressing, you can mix in a bit more sour cream or yogurt. For extra richness, a tablespoon of olive oil can help smooth out the dressing.

6. Blend or Whisk Until Smooth

If you’re using a food processor or blender, pulse the mixture until it is smooth and well combined. If you’re mixing by hand, whisk vigorously until the dressing is completely smooth and creamy. The consistency should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, but you can adjust it by adding more sour cream, yogurt, or even a splash of water or olive oil.

7. Taste and Adjust

Once the dressing is fully blended, give it a taste. Does it need more salt or acidity? Add a little more vinegar or lemon juice if it’s too rich, or adjust the seasoning until it’s just right. Feel free to add more fresh herbs if you want a more intense herbal flavor.

8. Chill Before Serving

For the best flavor, let your Green Goddess dressing chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld together and the dressing to thicken slightly. If you’re in a hurry, you can serve it immediately, but the longer it sits, the more the flavors will develop.

How to Store Green Goddess Dressing

Green Goddess dressing can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. It will continue to thicken as it sits, so if you find it too thick after a day or two, just add a little water or olive oil to thin it out to your desired consistency.

Tips for Perfect Green Goddess Dressing

  • Fresh Herbs Are Key: The key to a great Green Goddess dressing is fresh herbs. Try not to substitute dried herbs, as they don’t provide the same bright, fresh flavor.
  • Use High-Quality Mayonnaise: Since mayo is the main ingredient, use a high-quality version for the best taste. You can even make your own mayo if you’re feeling adventurous.
  • Customize to Your Taste: Green Goddess dressing is versatile, so feel free to tweak the recipe to your liking. Add more garlic, swap out the tarragon for basil, or even include a little bit of grated Parmesan for extra depth.
  • Make It Vegan: For a vegan version, substitute the mayonnaise and sour cream with vegan alternatives, and omit the anchovies.

Ways to Use Green Goddess Dressing

Green Goddess dressing is incredibly versatile and can be used in various ways to enhance your meals. Here are some ideas to get you started:

1. As a Salad Dressing

This is the classic use for Green Goddess dressing. Simply drizzle it over a fresh salad of greens, vegetables, and protein (chicken, shrimp, or tofu). The creamy texture and bright, herbaceous flavor pair beautifully with crisp lettuce and tomatoes.

2. On Sandwiches and Wraps

Spread this dressing on sandwiches and wraps for an extra layer of flavor. It pairs well with chicken, turkey, avocado, or even roasted veggies.

3. As a Dip

Green Goddess dressing makes an excellent dip for fresh vegetables like carrots, cucumbers, and celery. You can also serve it as a dip for chips or crackers at your next party or gathering.

4. On Roasted Vegetables

Drizzle this dressing over roasted vegetables like sweet potatoes, cauliflower, or Brussels sprouts for an added burst of flavor.

5. With Grilled Meats

Green Goddess dressing also works as a sauce or drizzle over grilled meats like chicken, steak, or fish. Its creaminess complements the smoky, charred flavor of grilled dishes.

Conclusion

Making homemade Green Goddess dressing is incredibly easy, and the result is a fresh, flavorful condiment that can elevate a variety of dishes. Whether you’re using it as a dressing, a dip, or a sauce, this creamy, herby dressing is a surefire way to add a burst of flavor to your meals. With just a few simple ingredients and some quick steps, you can create a dressing that’s far superior to anything you’ll find in a store.

So why not try making your own homemade Green Goddess dressing today? The freshness and depth of flavor will leave your taste buds wanting more!
