How to Make and Can Your Own Pear Preserves


Making and canning your own pear preserves is a delightful way to capture the essence of fresh pears and enjoy their sweet, juicy flavor all year round. Whether you have a bountiful harvest from your backyard tree or you’ve picked up a bushel from the local farmer’s market, this guide will walk you through the process of creating delicious pear preserves that you can store and savor later.

Why Make Pear Preserves?

Pear preserves are not only a delicious treat but also a fantastic way to reduce food waste. When pears are in season, they can be abundant, and making preserves allows you to enjoy their flavor long after the season has ended. Additionally, homemade preserves are free from preservatives and artificial ingredients, making them a healthier choice compared to store-bought options.

Choosing the Right Pears

The first step in making pear preserves is selecting the right type of pears. Look for ripe, firm pears that are free from blemishes. Varieties such as Bartlett, Bosc, or Anjou are excellent choices for preserves due to their sweet flavor and smooth texture. Aim for pears that are slightly soft to the touch but not overly ripe, as they will break down too much during cooking.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make pear preserves, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Pears: About 4 pounds, peeled, cored, and chopped
  • Sugar: 2 to 3 cups, depending on your sweetness preference
  • Lemon juice: 1/4 cup (freshly squeezed is best)
  • Cinnamon: 1 teaspoon (optional, for added flavor)
  • Nutmeg: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for added flavor)
  • Water: 1/2 cup

Equipment Required

Before you start cooking, gather the necessary equipment:

  • Large pot or Dutch oven
  • Wooden spoon
  • Canning jars (pint or half-pint)
  • Canning lids and rings
  • Water bath canner or large stockpot
  • Jar lifter
  • Funnel
  • Ladle
  • Clean kitchen towels

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pears

  1. Wash and Peel: Start by washing the pears thoroughly. Peel the skin using a vegetable peeler, then cut the pears in half, remove the core, and chop them into small pieces. Aim for uniform sizes to ensure even cooking.

  2. Prevent Browning: To prevent the chopped pears from browning, you can toss them in a little lemon juice as you work.

Step 2: Cook the Pears

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large pot or Dutch oven, combine the chopped pears, sugar, lemon juice, and water. If you’re using cinnamon and nutmeg, add them at this stage.

  2. Heat the Mixture: Place the pot over medium heat and stir until the sugar dissolves. Once the mixture starts to bubble, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer.

  3. Cook Until Thickened: Allow the mixture to cook for about 30 to 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. You want the pears to break down and the mixture to thicken. To test the consistency, place a spoonful on a plate and let it cool for a minute. If it holds its shape, it’s ready for canning.

Step 3: Prepare for Canning

  1. Sterilize Jars: While the pear mixture is cooking, sterilize your canning jars and lids. You can do this by placing them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes or running them through a hot cycle in the dishwasher.

  2. Prepare the Water Bath: Fill your water bath canner or large stockpot with water and bring it to a simmer. You’ll need enough water to cover the jars by at least an inch during processing.

Step 4: Fill the Jars

  1. Ladle the Preserves: Once the pear preserves have thickened, remove them from the heat. Using a ladle and funnel, fill each sterilized jar with the hot pear mixture, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top.

  2. Remove Air Bubbles: Use a clean knife or a bubble remover tool to gently slide around the inside of the jar to release any trapped air bubbles.

  3. Wipe the Rims: Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean kitchen towel to remove any residue that could prevent a proper seal.

  4. Seal the Jars: Place the sterilized lids on top of the jars and screw on the metal rings until they are fingertip-tight.

Step 5: Process the Jars

  1. Submerge the Jars: Carefully place the filled jars into the simmering water bath, ensuring they are fully submerged.
