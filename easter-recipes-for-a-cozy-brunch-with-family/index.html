Easter Recipes for a Cozy Brunch with Family


Easter is the perfect time to gather with family and friends to celebrate the season of renewal. What better way to do that than with a cozy brunch filled with delicious dishes that everyone will enjoy? Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or keeping things intimate with close relatives, there are countless ways to make your Easter brunch special. From light and refreshing dishes to rich, indulgent ones, these Easter recipes are guaranteed to delight every palate at your table.

In this guide, we’ll explore a variety of Easter brunch recipes, from classic baked goods to savory treats, and everything in between. So, get ready to celebrate Easter with a feast that brings everyone together.

1. Fluffy Lemon Pancakes with Berry Compote

Start your Easter brunch with a batch of fluffy, light-as-air pancakes that capture the essence of spring. Lemon and berries make the perfect combination, bringing a burst of freshness to your table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 ½ cups buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries) for topping
  • Maple syrup for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and lemon zest.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir until combined. Be careful not to overmix; lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with butter or cooking spray.
  5. Pour about ¼ cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Serve the pancakes with a generous helping of fresh berries and maple syrup.

These lemon pancakes are bright, light, and a wonderful way to start your Easter brunch.

2. Classic Deviled Eggs with a Twist

Deviled eggs are a brunch staple, and for Easter, they make a great addition to your table. With a small twist, you can elevate this classic appetizer with a creamy and tangy filling.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh chives
  • Smoked paprika, for garnish
  • Pickled jalapeños, for an optional spicy kick

Instructions:

  1. Place the eggs in a saucepan and cover them with water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and let the eggs simmer for 10-12 minutes.
  2. After cooking, transfer the eggs to a bowl of ice water and let them cool completely.
  3. Once cooled, peel the eggs and cut them in half lengthwise.
  4. Carefully remove the yolks and place them in a bowl. Mash the yolks with a fork, then add the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, white wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Stir until smooth and creamy.
  5. Spoon or pipe the filling back into the egg whites.
  6. Garnish with chopped chives, a sprinkle of smoked paprika, and pickled jalapeños for an extra kick if desired.

These deviled eggs are sure to be a crowd-pleaser and a great way to kick off your Easter feast.

3. Spinach and Feta Quiche

Quiche is a quintessential brunch dish that can be made ahead of time, allowing you to enjoy more time with your family. The spinach and feta filling in this quiche offers a rich, savory flavor that is perfect for Easter morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade, if preferred)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (like thyme or parsley) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add the spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stir the cooled spinach mixture and feta cheese into the egg mixture.
  6. Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the center is set and the top is golden brown.
  7. Let the quiche cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Garnish with fresh herbs.

This spinach and feta quiche pairs perfectly with fresh fruit or a light salad, making it a well-rounded dish for Easter brunch.

4. Honey-Glazed Carrots with Fresh Herbs

Easter wouldn’t be complete without a side of tender, caramelized vegetables. These honey-glazed carrots are a sweet and savory addition that will brighten up your brunch spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound baby carrots, peeled
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, bring water to a boil and add the carrots. Cook for 4-5 minutes until just tender, then drain.
  2. In the same pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the honey and stir until combined.
  3. Add the carrots to the pan and toss to coat in the honey-butter mixture. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the carrots are tender and glazed.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme.
  5. Serve warm as a beautiful, vibrant side dish.

The sweetness of the honey combined with the savory thyme makes this dish an irresistible choice for your Easter brunch.

5. Baked Ham with Pineapple and Brown Sugar Glaze

For a heartier option, a beautifully glazed ham is an excellent centerpiece for your Easter brunch table. The combination of pineapple and brown sugar adds a lovely sweetness that pairs perfectly with the savory flavor of the ham.

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless ham (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 1 cup pineapple juice
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 cup pineapple rings (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Place the ham on a roasting pan and score the surface in a diamond pattern.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the pineapple juice, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, cinnamon, and cloves. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the glaze thickens.
  4. Brush the glaze generously over the ham and place it in the oven. Bake for 1-1.5 hours, basting every 20 minutes with the glaze.
  5. If desired, add pineapple rings to the pan during the last 20 minutes of baking for an extra touch of sweetness.
  6. Once the ham is fully cooked and glazed, remove from the oven and let it rest before slicing.

This honey-baked ham is an impressive dish that’s both sweet and savory, making it a perfect Easter centerpiece.

6. Hot Cross Buns

No Easter brunch is complete without a batch of freshly baked hot cross buns. These soft, spiced buns, topped with a simple cross made of icing, are a delightful treat that will fill your home with the warm scents of cinnamon and nutmeg.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¾ cup warm milk
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup currants or raisins
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 cup powdered sugar (for icing)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the warm milk, butter, and eggs. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic. Add the currants or raisins and knead until evenly distributed.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean towel, and let it rise for about 1-1.5 hours until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a bun and place them on a baking sheet.
  6. Let the buns rise for another 30 minutes, then brush with a beaten egg for an egg wash.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until the buns are golden brown.
  8. While the buns cool, make the icing by mixing the powdered sugar with a small amount of water until smooth. Drizzle over the buns in a cross shape.

These hot cross buns are perfect for serving as a sweet breakfast or dessert, and their delicate spiced flavor is a lovely nod to the season.

Conclusion

Easter brunch is an opportunity to indulge in delicious food, enjoy the company of loved ones, and celebrate the joy of the season. These recipes, from fluffy pancakes to savory quiche and honey-glazed ham, will ensure your Easter brunch is a memorable one. Whether you stick to classic dishes or add a creative twist, the key to a successful brunch is love and care in every dish.

Make this Easter extra special by spending time in the kitchen and around the table with those who matter most. Happy Easter, and happy cooking!
