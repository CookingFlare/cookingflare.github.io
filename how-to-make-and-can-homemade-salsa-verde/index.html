How to Make and Can Homemade Salsa Verde


Fresh, tangy, and bursting with vibrant flavor, salsa verde is a versatile and delicious Mexican condiment. Whether you’re serving it with tortilla chips, drizzling it over tacos, or using it as a marinade for roasted meats, homemade salsa verde brings depth and brightness to any dish. By making your own salsa verde at home, you have full control over the flavors and texture, and canning allows you to preserve it for later use, ensuring you always have a taste of summer ready to enjoy.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through every step of making and canning homemade salsa verde. It’s easier than you think and incredibly rewarding. By the end, you’ll have shelves of fresh salsa verde to enjoy year-round or share with friends and family.

Ingredients for Homemade Salsa Verde

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s review the key ingredients you’ll need:

  • Tomatillos (2-3 pounds): These are the star of salsa verde, providing the tart and fruity base for the recipe. Choose bright green tomatillos with intact husks and firm flesh for the best flavor.
  • Fresh garlic (4 cloves): Adds depth and a savory kick.
  • Onion (1 medium): White onions are traditional, but yellow or red onions can work for a unique touch.
  • Fresh cilantro (1 cup, loosely packed): Brightens up the salsa with herbaceous, citrusy notes.
  • Jalapeño peppers (2-3, to taste): For heat and a mild smokiness. Adjust the number of jalapeños depending on your spice tolerance.
  • Lime juice (2 tablespoons): A touch of acidity brings balance to the flavors.
  • Salt (to taste): Enhances the natural flavors of the ingredients.
  • Cumin (optional, ½ teaspoon): Adds earthy undertones for a warm finish.

Preparing the Tomatillos

Tomatillos are essential to salsa verde, and prepping them properly is vital to achieving that authentic flavor. Here’s how to prepare your tomatillos:

  1. Remove the Husk: Gently pull away the papery husk from each tomatillo. You’ll notice a sticky film underneath the husk.
  2. Wash Thoroughly: Rinse the tomatillos under cool water to remove the sticky residue. A gentle scrub with your hands should do the trick.
  3. Inspect and Trim: Check each tomatillo for blemishes or soft spots. Trim away imperfections and remove the stem.

Roasting for Maximum Flavor

Roasting your ingredients takes salsa verde to the next level, bringing out deep, complex flavors. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your broiler to high and position the rack about 6 inches from the heat source.
  2. Arrange Ingredients on a Baking Sheet: Place the tomatillos, peeled garlic cloves, quartered onions, and jalapeño peppers on a baking sheet. Spread them out in a single layer.
  3. Add Light Oil (Optional): You can drizzle a small amount of olive oil on the vegetables for added richness, but it’s not essential.
  4. Broil Until Charred: Roast the vegetables under the broiler for 5–7 minutes, or until the skins of the tomatillos and peppers start to blister and char slightly. Flip the ingredients halfway through for even roasting.
  5. Set Aside to Cool: Let the roasted vegetables cool slightly before blending.

Making the Salsa Verde

Once your roasted vegetables are ready, it’s time to transform them into a delicious salsa:

  1. Combine Ingredients in a Blender: Add the roasted tomatillos, onions, garlic, and jalapeños to a blender or food processor. Toss in the fresh cilantro, lime juice, salt, and optional cumin.
  2. Blend to Desired Consistency: Pulse the mixture until you reach the texture you prefer—chunky or smooth. If the salsa is too thick, add a splash of water to loosen it up.
  3. Taste and Adjust: Taste your salsa verde and adjust seasonings. Add more salt or lime juice as needed.

Preparing for Canning

Canning salsa verde not only preserves its freshness but also lets you enjoy it long after the tomatillo season has passed. Before you start, make sure you have the essential canning equipment:

  • Glass canning jars (pint size is ideal)
  • Lids and rings
  • Large stockpot or boiling water canner
  • Jar lifter and funnel

Follow these steps to prepare your jars and tools:

  1. Sterilize Jars: Wash jars with hot, soapy water and rinse well. Submerge them in boiling water for 10 minutes to sterilize.
  2. Prepare Lids and Rings: Soak lids in hot, not boiling, water to soften the seal. Wash rings in warm, soapy water and let them air dry.
  3. Heat the Salsa Verde: Pour your salsa verde into a large pot and heat it to a simmer. Keep it hot while you fill the jars.

Canning Homemade Salsa Verde

Now it’s time for the canning process. Make sure you follow these steps carefully to ensure safe preservation:

  1. Fill the Jars: Using a funnel, ladle the hot salsa verde into sterilized jars, leaving about ½ inch of headspace at the top.
  2. Remove Air Bubbles: Gently run a plastic knife or bubble remover tool along the inside edge of the jar to release trapped air bubbles.
  3. Wipe the Rim: Use a clean, damp cloth to wipe the rim of each jar to ensure a secure seal.
  4. Seal the Jars: Place the lid on top and screw the ring on tightly, but not overly tight.
  5. Process the Jars: Place the filled jars in a boiling water canner or large stockpot with a rack. Ensure the jars are fully submerged with at least 1 inch of water above them. Process pint-sized jars for 15 minutes (adjust time based on altitude).
  6. Cool and Store: Carefully remove jars from the pot using a jar lifter and place them on a towel to cool. As they cool, you’ll hear a popping sound—that’s the seal forming. Once cool, test the seals by pressing on the center of the lid; it should not flex or pop back.

Storing and Serving Salsa Verde

Properly canned salsa verde can be stored in a cool, dark pantry for up to a year. Make sure to label each jar with the date it was canned. Once opened, refrigerate and consume within a week.

To serve your salsa verde, simply spoon it out into a dish and pair it with tortilla chips, drizzle it over enchiladas, or mix it into soups for a tangy twist. Its versatility is endless!

Troubleshooting Tips

While canning salsa verde is straightforward, here are some tips to ensure success:

  • Choose Fresh Ingredients: Using fresh and high-quality produce will make all the difference in flavor. Avoid overripe or blemished tomatillos.
  • Maintain Proper Headspace: Leaving the right amount of headspace in the jars ensures a good seal.
  • Adjust for Altitude: The processing time varies by altitude. Check a canning guide to determine the correct time for your location.

Why Make Your Own Salsa Verde?

Homemade salsa verde delivers a truly unique and fresh flavor that’s hard to find in store-bought versions. You can customize the spice levels, experiment with additional ingredients like avocado or roasted poblano peppers, and take pride knowing that every jar is made from scratch. Plus, the canning process lets you savor the taste of summer even on the coldest winter days.

Making and canning salsa verde is a skill worth learning. It combines the art of cooking with the science of food preservation, resulting in a delicious, long-lasting condiment that enhances any dish. Once you experience the satisfaction of crafting your own homemade salsa verde, you’ll never look back.

Go ahead and gather those tomatillos—your next culinary adventure awaits!
