Delicious Vegan Desserts for Your Holiday Table


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to show your loved ones you care than by serving them a mouth-watering, cruelty-free dessert that's sure to impress? As the demand for plant-based options continues to rise, it's no longer a challenge to find incredible vegan desserts that satisfy any sweet tooth. Whether you're a seasoned vegan or just looking to experiment with plant-based baking, we've got you covered with these scrumptious and innovative recipes that will have your holiday table shining brighter than ever. Snowball Fight Fudge Brownies Who says vegan treats are boring? These rich, fudgy brownies are a game-changer, and they just happen to be made with the finest plant-based ingredients. Coated in a snowy blanket of coconut sugar and crushed almonds, these snowball fight fudge brownies will become a staple at any holiday gathering. Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder 1 cup sugar 1/2 cup non-dairy milk 1/2 cup canola oil 2 teaspoons vanilla extract 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips 1 cup crushed almonds 1/4 cup coconut sugar

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder, sugar, and baking powder. In a separate bowl, whisk together non-dairy milk, canola oil, and vanilla extract. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until combined. Fold in chocolate chips and crushed almonds. Press the mixture into a greased 8-inch square baking dish. Sprinkle with coconut sugar and crushed almonds.

Tips & Variations:

For a Canadian twist, add a sprinkle of maple syrup to the mix for a hint of maple flavor. If you're feeling fancy, drizzle with maple syrup or chocolate ganache before serving.

Pomegranate Apple Crisp A seasonal twist on the classic crisp, this vegan dessert is a showstopper. Featuring a hint of cinnamon, nutmeg, and star anise, this pomegranate apple crisp is the perfect blend of sweet and tangy. Ingredients:

6-8 medium-sized apples, peeled and sliced 1 cup pomegranate seeds 1/4 cup granulated sugar 2 tablespoons cornstarch 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon ground star anise 1/4 cup vegan butter or margarine 1 cup rolled oats 1/2 cup chopped almonds 1/2 cup brown sugar

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, pomegranate seeds, granulated sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and star anise. Transfer the mixture to a 9x9-inch baking dish and set aside. In a separate bowl, mix together rolled oats, almond pieces, and brown sugar. Add vegan butter or margarine to the oat mixture and mix until crumbly. Top the apple mixture with the oat mixture and bake for 40-45 minutes or until golden brown.

Tips & Variations:

Use fresh or frozen cranberries for an extra tart twist. For a unique crunch, add a handful of chopped pistachios to the oat mixture.

Chocolate Chai Cheesecake No vegan cheesecake recipe would be complete without this rich, chocolatey masterpiece. Boasting a velvety smooth texture and warming flavors of chai, this cheesecake is the perfect way to cap off any meal. Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs 1/2 cup coconut sugar 1/2 cup unsalted vegan butter or margarine, melted 2 cups non-dairy cream cheese 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1 cup unsweetened coconut whipped cream

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Press the graham cracker crumbs into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. In a blender or food processor, blend the non-dairy cream cheese, cocoa powder, and vanilla extract until smooth. Add the melted coconut sugar, cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, and nutmeg to the blender and mix until combined. Pour the chocolate mixture over the graham cracker crust and bake for 50-55 minutes or until set.

Tips & Variations:

For an Indian twist, use chai tea powder instead of ground spices. Experiment with different types of non-dairy cream cheese for varied flavor profiles.

Seasonal Fruit Tarts These picture-perfect tarts are the epitome of elegance, with a buttery crust and an assortment of seasonal fruits. From fresh berries to juicy peaches, these tarts are sure to shine on any holiday gathering. Ingredients:

1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup confectioners' sugar 1/4 cup cold unsalted vegan butter or margarine 1 egg replacement (such as flax or chia egg) 1 cup non-dairy milk 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Fresh seasonal fruits of your choice

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Press the dough into a disk and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Roll out the dough to a thickness of 1/8 inch. Cut out circles using a cookie cutter or the rim of a glass. Place the dough circles into a mini muffin tin. Press your chosen fruits onto the dough and drizzle with a little non-dairy milk.

Tips & Variations:

Experiment with various fruits, such as passionfruit or key lime, for unique flavor combinations. For a sandcastle-inspired look, sprinkle powdered sugar over the tarts before baking.

Delight your loved ones this holiday season with these mouth-watering vegan desserts that cater to any sweet tooth. Whether you're a seasoned vegan or just starting to explore plant-based baking, these innovative recipes will help you create a table that flashes festive cheer.
