Fall Salads: Recipes with Seasonal Greens and Apples


Fall is the perfect time to indulge in hearty salads that highlight the vibrant flavors of seasonal greens and crisp apples. Whether you’re looking for a light lunch or a refreshing side dish, these fall salad recipes are sure to satisfy your taste buds and keep you feeling nourished. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Harvest Kale Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch kale, stems removed and leaves torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 medium apple, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the kale, apple slices, dried cranberries, pecans, and feta cheese.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Allow the salad to sit for 10 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

2. Autumn Spinach Salad

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups baby spinach leaves
  • 1 medium apple, diced
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted and roughly chopped
  • 1/4 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the spinach leaves in a large salad bowl.
  2. Add the diced apple, walnuts, blue cheese, and dried cranberries to the bowl.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of fall flavors!

3. Roasted Beet and Apple Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium beets, roasted, peeled, and thinly sliced
  • 1 medium apple, julienned
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the roasted beet slices, julienned apple, mixed salad greens, goat cheese, and chopped walnuts.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Let the flavors meld together for a few minutes before serving.

4. Apple and Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 medium apple, diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped cucumber
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, diced apple, chopped cucumber, red onion, and fresh parsley.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Allow the salad to sit for a few minutes to let the flavors mingle before serving.

5. Butternut Squash and Apple Salad

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups cubed butternut squash
  • 1 medium apple, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. In a large salad bowl, combine the roasted butternut squash, apple slices, mixed salad greens, dried cranberries, and chopped pecans.
  4. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the apple cider vinegar, olive oil, honey, salt, and pepper.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature for a delightful fall salad experience.

Enjoy these delicious fall salads that showcase the best flavors of the season. Each salad offers a unique combination of ingredients that will tantalize your taste buds and keep you satisfied. So, grab those fresh greens and crisp apples, and let the fall salad feast begin!
