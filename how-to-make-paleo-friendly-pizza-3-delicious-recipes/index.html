How to Make Paleo-Friendly Pizza: 3 Delicious Recipes


If you’re craving pizza but want to stick to a healthier, grain-free lifestyle, then you’re in the right place. Paleo-friendly pizza can be both satisfying and delicious without compromising on taste. Gone are the days when you had to settle for a lackluster cauliflower crust. Now, there are countless ways to create a pizza that is not only compliant with the Paleo diet but also mouthwateringly good.

In this post, we’ll dive into three creative and scrumptious Paleo pizza recipes that will elevate your pizza night and satisfy your taste buds. Whether you’re new to the Paleo diet or a seasoned pro, these recipes will make sure you never have to miss out on your favorite comfort food again.

1. Cauliflower Crust Paleo Pizza

Cauliflower is the ultimate substitute when you’re looking to replace traditional flour-based crusts. It’s low-carb, full of nutrients, and gluten-free. By combining cauliflower with a few simple ingredients, you can create a perfectly crispy, chewy crust that serves as the base for all your favorite pizza toppings.

Ingredients:

For the Cauliflower Crust:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, grated or processed into fine crumbs
  • 1 egg (or egg replacement for a vegan version)
  • 1 cup of almond flour
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil (or avocado oil)

For the Toppings:

  • 1/2 cup of tomato sauce (make sure it’s free of added sugars)
  • 1 cup of fresh mozzarella (or dairy-free alternative)
  • 1/2 cup of spinach, sautéed
  • 1/4 cup of sliced mushrooms
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil (for drizzling)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cauliflower: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Begin by grating the cauliflower florets using a box grater or processing them in a food processor until you get fine crumbs. You should have around 2 cups of cauliflower rice.

  2. Cook the Cauliflower: Transfer the cauliflower to a microwave-safe bowl and microwave for about 5 minutes, or until soft. Let it cool slightly, then use a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth to squeeze out any excess moisture. This step is crucial to ensure your crust isn’t soggy.

  3. Make the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine the cauliflower, almond flour, egg, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined. The mixture should form a dough-like consistency.

  4. Shape the Crust: Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and grease it lightly with olive oil. Transfer the cauliflower dough onto the sheet and press it into a round or rectangular shape, depending on your preference. Make sure the crust is even and about 1/4 inch thick.

  5. Bake the Crust: Bake the cauliflower crust in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown and crispy around the edges. You can flip the crust halfway through baking for an even crispiness.

  6. Top the Pizza: Once the crust is done, remove it from the oven and spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on top. Add the mozzarella, spinach, mushrooms, and drizzle with a bit of olive oil.

  7. Final Bake: Return the pizza to the oven for another 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove from the oven and slice to serve.

Why You’ll Love It:

This cauliflower crust is a game-changer. It’s light yet sturdy enough to hold all your toppings without falling apart. The addition of herbs like garlic and oregano in the dough infuses the crust with flavor, making every bite satisfying. Plus, it’s an excellent way to get more vegetables into your meal while still enjoying a pizza that tastes just like the real deal!

2. Almond Flour Crust Paleo Pizza

For those who prefer a more traditional pizza texture without cauliflower, almond flour is a perfect Paleo-friendly alternative. Almond flour provides a nutty flavor and a slightly denser texture, creating a satisfying crust that closely resembles the classic pizza base.

Ingredients:

For the Almond Flour Crust:

  • 2 cups of almond flour
  • 2 tablespoons of ground flaxseed
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 1/2 cup of tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup of fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup of bell peppers, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of pepperoni or sausage (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat and Prepare the Dough: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, ground flaxseed, baking soda, garlic powder, basil, and salt. Mix well. Add the eggs and olive oil, then stir until the dough comes together. It should be slightly sticky.

  2. Shape the Dough: Roll the dough between two pieces of parchment paper to about 1/4 inch thickness. If the dough is too sticky, dust it lightly with almond flour. Transfer the shaped dough to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  3. Bake the Crust: Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until it turns golden and crispy around the edges.

  4. Add the Toppings: Remove the crust from the oven and spread a layer of tomato sauce over the base. Top with fresh mozzarella, red onion, bell peppers, and pepperoni or sausage if you’re using them.

  5. Final Bake: Return the pizza to the oven for another 10-12 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Once done, remove from the oven, slice, and serve.

Why You’ll Love It:

This almond flour crust is perfect for those who want a more hearty and filling pizza base. It’s easy to make, and the flavor profile complements a variety of toppings. You’ll love the crispy edges, and the almond flour gives the crust a rich, slightly nutty flavor that pairs wonderfully with both savory and fresh toppings.

3. Chicken Crust Paleo Pizza

Looking for a completely unique and protein-packed pizza crust? Try a chicken crust! This recipe uses ground chicken as the base for a delicious and filling pizza. It’s low in carbs, high in protein, and a perfect alternative for those on a keto or Paleo diet.

Ingredients:

For the Chicken Crust:

  • 1 pound of ground chicken (preferably dark meat for more moisture)
  • 1/4 cup of almond flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 1/2 cup of pizza sauce (Paleo-friendly)
  • 1/2 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup of spinach, sautéed
  • 1/4 cup of sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh basil, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken Dough: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a mixing bowl, combine the ground chicken, almond flour, egg, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well until everything is fully incorporated, and the mixture resembles a dough-like texture.

  2. Form the Crust: Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and lightly grease it with olive oil. Transfer the chicken mixture onto the baking sheet and press it into a round pizza shape, about 1/4 inch thick.

  3. Bake the Crust: Bake the chicken crust in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until it’s golden brown and cooked through. Make sure to check the center to ensure the chicken is fully cooked.

  4. Add the Toppings: Once the crust is done, spread a layer of pizza sauce on top. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella, sautéed spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, and fresh basil.

  5. Final Bake: Return the pizza to the oven for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Why You’ll Love It:

This chicken crust is a great way to get a dose of lean protein while still enjoying a classic pizza. It’s incredibly satisfying and works well with a variety of toppings, from veggies to meats. The texture is hearty, and it’s sure to keep you full and fueled long after you’re done eating.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re a fan of cauliflower, almond flour, or want to experiment with a protein-packed chicken crust, these Paleo-friendly pizza recipes offer a variety of delicious and healthy options to suit your cravings. Each crust provides a unique flavor and texture, making it easy to find the perfect pizza for any occasion.

With these recipes, you no longer have to miss out on pizza while maintaining a healthy, grain-free lifestyle. Enjoy experimenting with different toppings and sauces to make these pizzas truly your own. Your Paleo-friendly pizza nights just got a whole lot tastier!
