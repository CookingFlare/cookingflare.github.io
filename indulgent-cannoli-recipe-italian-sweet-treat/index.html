Indulgent Cannoli Recipe - Italian Sweet Treat

3 min · 539 words

If you’ve ever strolled the streets of Italy, you’ve likely come across the delightful aroma of freshly baked pastries wafting from local bakeries. One of the most iconic Italian sweet treats is the indulgent cannoli. These crispy, fried pastry shells filled with a creamy, sweet ricotta-based filling are a true delight for the senses. Today, I’m excited to share with you a tried and tested recipe for crafting these delectable treats in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Cannoli Shells:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/2 cup sweet Marsala wine
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Filling:

  • 2 cups whole milk ricotta cheese
  • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped dark chocolate
  • 1/4 cup candied orange peel
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions

Making the Cannoli Shells:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Add the butter to the flour mixture and use your fingers to rub it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and Marsala wine.
  4. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until the dough comes together.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it until smooth and elastic, about 5 minutes.
  6. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.
  7. After resting, roll out the dough to about 1/8 inch thickness, then use a 4-inch round cutter to cut out circles.
  8. Heat the vegetable oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot to 375°F (190°C).
  9. Carefully wrap each circle of dough around a metal cannoli tube, sealing the edges with a dab of the beaten egg to prevent them from opening during frying.
  10. Fry the shells in the hot oil until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes. Use tongs to remove the shells from the oil and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to cool.

Making the Filling:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth and creamy.
  2. Fold in the chopped dark chocolate and candied orange peel until evenly distributed.
  3. Transfer the filling to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip.

Assembling the Cannoli:

  1. Carefully remove the cannoli shells from the metal tubes.
  2. Pipe the ricotta filling into each end of the cannoli shell, filling from the center outwards.
  3. Dust the filled cannoli with confectioners’ sugar before serving.

Serving and Enjoying

These indulgent cannoli are best enjoyed fresh, as the shells maintain their delightful crispness, and the filling is at its most luscious. Serve them as a sweet ending to a special Italian-inspired dinner or as a standalone treat with a cup of espresso. The balance of creamy, sweet filling and crispy, golden shell is sure to captivate your taste buds and transport you to the bustling streets of Italy with every bite.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create these irresistible Italian sweet treats. The process may seem intricate, but the reward of biting into a homemade cannoli is truly unparalleled. Buon appetito!

With this comprehensive cannoli recipe in hand, you’re well-equipped to bring a taste of Italy into your kitchen. The combination of rich ricotta filling encased in a crispy shell makes these indulgent cannoli a true masterpiece of Italian pastry. Whether you’re a seasoned home chef or a budding culinary enthusiast, this recipe is an invitation to explore the art of Italian dessert-making and savor the delightful results.
