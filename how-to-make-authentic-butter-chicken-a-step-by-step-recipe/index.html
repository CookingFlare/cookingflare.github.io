How to Make Authentic Butter Chicken: A Step-by-Step Recipe


Butter chicken is a popular Indian dish that originated in the kitchens of the Mughal era. This rich, creamy, and aromatic dish is a staple in many Indian restaurants around the world. The dish has undergone many modifications, but the authentic version remains a closely guarded secret, passed down through generations of skilled chefs. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making authentic butter chicken, using traditional methods and ingredients. Preparation Ingredients:

1 pound boneless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces 1/2 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 medium onions, thinly sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon ginger, grated 1 tablespoon tomato puree 2 tablespoons butter 1 teaspoon garam masala powder 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt to taste Fresh cilantro, for garnish Cooked basmati rice or naan, for serving

Spice Blends Before we begin, it's essential to create two spice blends that are used in the recipe. Garam masala powder and cumin-coriander powder blend are the most important.

Garam Masala Powder: 2 whole cloves 1 stick of cinnamon 1 cardamom 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds 1/2 teaspoon coriander seeds 1/4 teaspoon black peppercorns Cumin-Coriander Powder Blend: 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds 1/2 teaspoon coriander seeds

Marinade the Chicken

In a large bowl, mix together the chicken, plain yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, garam masala powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, cayenne pepper, and salt. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Make the Spice Blends

Garam Masala Powder: Heat a small pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, black peppercorns, and cinnamon stick. Once the spices start to smell, add the cardamom and remove the pan from the heat. Allow the mixture to cool. Grind the spices into a fine powder.

Cumin-Coriander Powder Blend: Heat a small pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds and coriander seeds. Allow the seeds to sizzle and become fragrant. Then, remove the pan from the heat. Allow the mixture to cool. Grind the spices into a fine powder.

Cook the Chicken

In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade, letting any excess liquid drip off. Add the chicken to the skillet and cook until it is browned on all sides and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

Make the Tomato Sauce

In the same skillet, add the sliced onions and cook over medium heat until they are lightly browned and caramelized. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for 1 minute. Add the tomato puree and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly. Add the cumin-coriander powder blend and salt, and stir well.

Finish the Butter Chicken

Add the cooked chicken back to the skillet and stir to combine with the tomato sauce. Add 2 tablespoons of butter to the skillet and stir until melted and well combined. Add the garam masala powder and stir to combine. Simmer the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Serve

Serve the butter chicken over cooked basmati rice or with naan bread. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve immediately.

Conclusion With these simple and precise steps, you are now well on your way to making authentic butter chicken in the comfort of your own kitchen. The rich flavors of India will tantalize your taste buds, and your guests will be impressed by the bold and aromatic flavors. Whether you're looking for a quick and easy meal or want to wow your loved ones with a homemade meal, this recipe is sure to hit the mark. Timing Preparation Time: 45 minutes Cooking Time: 30 minutes Total Time: 1 hour and 15 minutes Tips and Variations

Heat control: When cooking the chicken and making the sauce, control the heat by increasing or decreasing it to prevent the chicken from burning or the sauce from reducing too quickly. Flavor: Be sure to adjust the amount of spices according to your personal taste preferences. Texture: Cook the chicken to the right doneness, and try not to overcook it. Kitchen essentials: A wok or large skillet is well-suited for searing the chicken, while a saucepan is better for cooking the sauce.

Don't hesitate to experiment with the recipe, as every delicious variation is a success!
