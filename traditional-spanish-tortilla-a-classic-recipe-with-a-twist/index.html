Traditional Spanish Tortilla: A Classic Recipe with a Twist


When it comes to iconic Spanish dishes, the traditional Spanish tortilla, also known as tortilla de patatas or Spanish omelette, holds a special place in the hearts of food enthusiasts around the world. This simple yet flavorful dish has been a staple in Spanish cuisine for centuries, and its versatility makes it an ideal canvas for culinary creativity. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the classic recipe for traditional Spanish tortilla and explore a unique twist that will elevate this beloved dish to new heights.

The Classic Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 medium potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 large eggs
  • Salt to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Heat a generous amount of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the thinly sliced potatoes and onions to the skillet, season with salt, and cook until the potatoes are tender and slightly golden. This process infuses the potatoes and onions with the rich flavor of the olive oil, creating the foundation of the tortilla.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and season with salt.
  4. Once the potatoes and onions are cooked, transfer them to the bowl with the beaten eggs. Mix gently to ensure the potatoes and onions are evenly coated with the eggs.
  5. Wipe the skillet clean and return it to the heat. Add a little more olive oil if needed.
  6. Pour the potato and egg mixture into the skillet, spreading it out evenly. Cook over medium heat until the edges start to set.
  7. Now, here comes the tricky part – flipping the tortilla. Place a large plate over the skillet and carefully invert the tortilla onto the plate. Slide it back into the skillet to cook the other side until it’s perfectly golden and set.
  8. Once the tortilla is cooked through, transfer it to a serving plate and allow it to cool slightly before slicing into wedges.

The Twist: Chorizo and Manchego Tortilla

While the classic Spanish tortilla is undeniably delicious, we can take it a step further by adding a twist that will delight the taste buds. For this variation, we’ll introduce two quintessential Spanish ingredients: chorizo and Manchego cheese.

Additional Ingredients:

  • 100g chorizo, diced
  • 50g Manchego cheese, grated

Instructions:

  1. Follow the classic recipe for traditional Spanish tortilla up to the point where the potato and egg mixture is ready to be poured into the skillet.
  2. At this stage, sprinkle the diced chorizo and grated Manchego cheese over the potato and egg mixture, distributing them evenly.
  3. Gently mix the added ingredients into the potato and egg mixture, ensuring they are well incorporated.
  4. Proceed with cooking the tortilla as per the classic recipe instructions.

Serving Suggestion

Whether you opt for the classic version or the chorizo and Manchego twist, the traditional Spanish tortilla pairs beautifully with a simple green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette. The contrast of flavors and textures makes for a delightful culinary experience.

In Conclusion

The traditional Spanish tortilla is a timeless dish that has stood the test of time, and with a simple twist, it can be reinvented to suit different palates and occasions. Whether you stick to the classic recipe or venture into creative territory with additional ingredients, the Spanish tortilla remains a beloved symbol of Spanish gastronomy.

We hope this blog post has inspired you to embark on your own culinary adventure with the traditional Spanish tortilla. Embrace the rich flavors and rustic charm of this iconic dish, and don’t be afraid to put your own spin on it. After all, the beauty of cooking lies in the freedom to experiment and create something truly unique. Buen provecho!
