Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth with Traditional Chinese Red Bean Paste Buns


Red bean paste buns, also known as “dousha bao” in Mandarin, are a delightful treat that will satisfy any sweet tooth. These traditional Chinese buns are soft, fluffy, and filled with a rich and sweet red bean paste. They are a popular snack or dessert in Chinese cuisine and can be enjoyed at any time of the day. In this recipe, we will guide you on how to make these delicious red bean paste buns from scratch.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup warm water

For the red bean paste filling:

  • 1 cup cooked red beans
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, and instant yeast. Gradually add warm water while stirring the mixture. Once the dough starts to come together, transfer it onto a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough back into the bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rest for 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

  2. Make the red bean paste filling: In a saucepan, combine the cooked red beans, sugar, and vegetable oil. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and turns into a smooth paste. Remove from heat and let it cool.

  3. Shape the buns: Once the dough has doubled in size, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and divide it into small equal-sized portions. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it with your palm. Spoon a small amount of the red bean paste filling into the center of the flattened dough. Gather the edges of the dough and pinch them together to seal the bun. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.

  4. Steam the buns: Arrange the filled buns on a steamer lined with parchment paper, leaving some space in between each bun for expansion. Cover the steamer with a lid and let the buns rest for another 15 minutes. Meanwhile, bring water to a boil in a large pot or wok. Once the water is boiling, place the steamer on top of the pot and steam the buns over high heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the buns are cooked through and the dough is fluffy.

  5. Serve and enjoy: Remove the steamer from the heat and let the buns cool for a few minutes before serving. These red bean paste buns are best enjoyed warm and fresh. Serve them as a snack or dessert, and savor the delightful combination of the soft dough and sweet red bean filling.

Conclusion

Traditional Chinese red bean paste buns are a delectable treat that will satisfy your sweet tooth. With their fluffy dough and rich, sweet red bean filling, these buns are a beloved snack or dessert in Chinese cuisine. Making them from scratch allows you to enjoy their authentic flavors and experience the joy of creating something delicious in your own kitchen. So why not give this recipe a try and indulge in the irresistible taste of red bean paste buns? Your taste buds will thank you!
