Rustic Spaghetti Aglio e Olio: A Simple and Flavorful Recipe


If you’re looking for a quick and easy pasta dish that packs a punch of flavor, look no further than Rustic Spaghetti Aglio e Olio. This classic Italian recipe is a staple in many households, and for a good reason. With just a few simple ingredients, you can create a dish that is bursting with garlic and olive oil goodness.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 6 cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water.

  2. Once the water is boiling, add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Remember to stir occasionally to prevent the pasta from sticking together.

  3. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and red pepper flakes to the skillet. Cook until the garlic turns golden brown and becomes fragrant, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.

  4. Once the pasta is cooked, carefully transfer it to the skillet using tongs or a pasta fork. Reserve some of the pasta cooking water.

  5. Toss the pasta in the garlic and olive oil mixture, ensuring that each strand is well coated. If the pasta seems dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta cooking water to loosen it up.

  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the pasta. Toss again to evenly distribute the cheese.

  7. Taste the pasta and season with salt if needed. Remember that the Parmesan cheese already adds a salty flavor, so be cautious with the amount of additional salt you add.

  8. Transfer the spaghetti aglio e olio to serving plates, and garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and added freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of flavor, you can add some sautéed mushrooms, sun-dried tomatoes, or even cooked shrimp to the skillet along with the garlic and red pepper flakes.

  • If you prefer a milder spice level, you can reduce or omit the red pepper flakes altogether. Alternatively, if you like it spicier, feel free to add more.

  • Don’t forget to reserve some of the pasta cooking water. It is essential for creating a silky sauce that coats the pasta perfectly.

  • To make this dish even more rustic, you can use whole wheat spaghetti instead of regular spaghetti. The nutty flavor of the whole wheat pasta adds a delightful twist to the dish.

  • If you’re a cheese lover, consider adding some extra grated Parmesan cheese on top of the plated pasta for an indulgent touch.

Final Thoughts

Rustic Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a classic Italian dish that is loved for its simplicity and bold flavors. This recipe is perfect for those busy weeknights when you want a satisfying meal without spending hours in the kitchen. With just a handful of ingredients, you can create a dish that will impress your family and friends. So, next time you’re craving a comforting bowl of pasta, give this recipe a try. You won’t be disappointed!
