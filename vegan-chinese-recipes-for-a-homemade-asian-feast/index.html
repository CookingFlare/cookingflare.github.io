Vegan Chinese Recipes for a Homemade Asian Feast


Are you craving the bold flavors of Chinese cuisine but want to stick to your vegan diet? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and easy-to-make vegan Chinese recipes that will satisfy your taste buds and impress your guests. From appetizers to main courses and even desserts, we have got you covered for a homemade Asian feast that will leave everyone wanting more.

Appetizers

Steamed Dumplings

Dumplings are a staple in Chinese cuisine, and this vegan version is just as tasty as the traditional ones. To make these steamed dumplings, start by preparing the filling. In a bowl, combine finely chopped vegetables like mushrooms, cabbage, and carrots. Add minced garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and a touch of sesame oil for flavor. Mix well.

Next, take store-bought dumpling wrappers or make your own using a simple dough of flour and water. Spoon a small amount of the filling onto each wrapper, fold in half, and seal the edges with water. Steam the dumplings for about 10 minutes until they are cooked through. Serve with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, rice vinegar, and a sprinkle of chili flakes.

Spring Rolls

Crispy and flavorful, vegan spring rolls are a crowd favorite. To make them, heat some oil in a pan and sauté a mixture of shredded cabbage, carrots, bean sprouts, and chopped tofu. Season with soy sauce, garlic powder, and a dash of white pepper. Once the vegetables are cooked, let the mixture cool.

Place a spoonful of the filling onto a spring roll wrapper and roll it tightly, sealing the edges with water. Deep-fry the spring rolls until golden brown and serve with a sweet chili dipping sauce.

Main Courses

General Tso’s Tofu

Craving the classic General Tso’s Chicken? Try this vegan version with tofu! Start by draining and pressing firm tofu to remove excess moisture. Cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes and coat them in cornstarch. In a separate pan, heat some oil and fry the tofu until it turns crispy and golden.

In another pan, sauté minced garlic, ginger, and red pepper flakes. Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and a touch of agave syrup for sweetness. Stir in the fried tofu and toss until it is well coated in the sauce. Serve with steamed rice and garnish with sliced scallions.

Kung Pao Cauliflower

For a spicy and savory dish, try Kung Pao Cauliflower. Start by roasting cauliflower florets in the oven until they are tender and slightly charred. In a pan, sauté chopped bell peppers, onions, and garlic until they soften.

In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, and a sprinkle of red pepper flakes. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and add roasted cashews for crunch. Toss in the roasted cauliflower and cook until everything is well combined. Serve over steamed rice and garnish with chopped green onions.

Desserts

Mango Sticky Rice

Finish off your vegan Chinese feast with a sweet and fragrant dessert – Mango Sticky Rice. Cook sticky rice according to the package instructions and let it cool. In a separate pan, heat coconut milk and sugar until the sugar dissolves. Pour the coconut milk mixture over the sticky rice and let it soak for about 30 minutes.

Serve the sticky rice with fresh slices of ripe mango and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds. The combination of the creamy coconut rice and the sweet mango will leave you wanting more.

Red Bean Soup

For a comforting and traditional dessert, try Red Bean Soup. Cook adzuki beans until they are soft and tender. In a separate pot, simmer the cooked beans with water, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Let the mixture cook until it thickens slightly and the flavors meld together.

Serve the warm red bean soup in bowls and garnish with a drizzle of coconut milk. It’s a simple yet satisfying dessert that will warm your heart.

Conclusion

Now that you have a collection of delicious vegan Chinese recipes, you can impress your loved ones with a homemade Asian feast. From steamed dumplings and spring rolls to General Tso’s Tofu and Kung Pao Cauliflower, and finishing off with Mango Sticky Rice and Red Bean Soup, these recipes will transport you to the vibrant flavors of China. So get cooking and enjoy a vegan feast that is both satisfying and compassionate.
