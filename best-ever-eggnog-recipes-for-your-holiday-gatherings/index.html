Best-Ever Eggnog Recipes for Your Holiday Gatherings


Eggnog is a traditional winter beverage that has been a staple at holiday gatherings for centuries. The rich, creamy, and sweet drink is a perfect combination of milk, cream, sugar, eggs, and spices. With its long history, eggnog has evolved over time, and various recipes have been created to cater to different tastes and dietary needs. In this article, we will explore some of the best eggnog recipes you can try at your next holiday gathering. Classic Eggnog Recipe This traditional eggnog recipe is a timeless favorite that is sure to be a hit at your holiday party. Ingredients

2 cups milk 1 cup heavy cream 1/2 cup granulated sugar 4 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

In a medium saucepan, combine milk, heavy cream, and granulated sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is hot but not boiling. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs and vanilla extract. Temper the eggs by slowly pouring the warm milk mixture into the eggs, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and reaches 160°F. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in ground nutmeg and cinnamon. Let the eggnog cool to room temperature. Chill the eggnog in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

Low-Calorie Eggnog Recipe For those watching their calorie intake, this low-calorie eggnog recipe is a perfect alternative. Ingredients

1 cup unsweetened almond milk 1/2 cup low-fat milk 1/4 cup low-fat sour cream 2 large egg yolks 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1 tablespoon sugar-free sweetener (optional)

Instructions

In a blender, combine unsweetened almond milk, low-fat milk, and sugar-free sweetener (if using). Blend until smooth. In a separate bowl, whisk together egg yolks and vanilla extract. Temper the egg yolks by slowly pouring the milk mixture into the eggs, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the blender and blend until smooth. Stir in ground nutmeg and cinnamon. Chill the low-calorie eggnog in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

Vegan Eggnog Recipe This vegan eggnog recipe is perfect for those with dietary restrictions. Ingredients

1 cup plant-based milk (such as soy milk or almond milk) 1/2 cup coconut cream 1/4 cup maple syrup 2 tablespoons cornstarch 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

In a blender, combine plant-based milk, coconut cream, and maple syrup. Blend until smooth. In a separate bowl, whisk together cornstarch and vanilla extract. Temper the cornstarch mixture by slowly pouring the milk mixture into the cornstarch, whisking constantly. Return the mixture to the blender and blend until smooth. Stir in ground cinnamon and ground nutmeg. Chill the vegan eggnog in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

Tips and Variations

Use different types of milk and cream to create a unique flavor profile, such as oat milk or coconut cream. Add a pinch of salt to balance the sweetness and combat bitterness. Experiment with various spices, such as ground ginger or ground cardamom, to create a unique flavor. For a chilled eggnog, add ice cubes to the mixture before chilling.

History of Eggnog Eggnog is a beverage that has a rich history dating back to the 17th century. The origins of eggnog are a topic of debate among historians, but most agree that it was created by European colonists in North America. Initially, eggnog was made with rum, brandy, and other spirits, which gave it a high calorie count. As time passed, eggnog became a household staple, and its recipe was adapted to include milk, cream, and eggs. A Brief History of Eggnog Risin%

17th century: Eggnog first appears in American cuisine as a frothy drink made with rum, brandy, or other spirits. 18th century: Eggnog remains a popular holiday drink, with the addition of spices and sugar. 19th century: Eggnog becomes a staple at holiday gatherings, with the introduction of dairy products and alternative milk options.

Conclusion Eggnog is a beloved holiday beverage that has been a staple for centuries. With its rich history and versatility, it's no wonder that eggnog has become a holiday classic. Whether you prefer a classic recipe or a low-calorie or vegan alternative, there's an eggnog recipe for everyone. So next time you're hosting a holiday gathering, give one of these recipes a try and enjoy the rich, creamy flavors of eggnog.
