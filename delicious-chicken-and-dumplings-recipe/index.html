Delicious Chicken and Dumplings Recipe


If you’re craving a meal that’s hearty, comforting, and packed with rich flavors, chicken and dumplings is a dish that stands at the pinnacle of home-cooked classics. It’s a recipe that warms not only the body but also the soul, making it the perfect dish for cozy evenings, family gatherings, or even a casual dinner with friends. Whether you’re an experienced cook or just diving into the culinary world, this recipe offers step-by-step guidance to craft a bowl of pure comfort and satisfaction.

Let’s dive into the details of creating your own chicken and dumplings dish. This recipe combines tender, flavorful chicken, rich broth, and pillowy-soft dumplings for a combination you’ll want to make again and again.

Ingredients for Chicken and Dumplings

The secret to great chicken and dumplings lies in using simple, fresh ingredients that come together to form complex flavors. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Chicken and Broth:

  • 3 pounds of bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (or a combination of thighs and drumsticks for added depth of flavor).
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil or unsalted butter.
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped.
  • 2–3 cloves of garlic, minced.
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and diced.
  • 3 celery stalks, diced.
  • 4 cups of chicken stock or broth (homemade is ideal for maximum flavor).
  • 1 cup of water (adjust as needed).
  • 2 bay leaves.
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme (or a few sprigs of fresh thyme).
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste.

For the Dumplings:

  • 1 ¾ cups of all-purpose flour.
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder.
  • ½ teaspoon of salt.
  • 2 tablespoons of cold unsalted butter, diced into small pieces.
  • ¾ cup of whole milk (or buttermilk for a tangier flavor).

Optional (but recommended): Fresh parsley or chives for garnish, adding a pop of color and freshness to your final dish.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Chicken and Broth

Start by heating olive oil or butter in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Season the chicken thighs generously with salt and pepper on both sides, then add them to the pot. Sear the chicken for 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown. This step locks in juices and adds incredible flavor to the broth.

Once seared, remove the chicken and set it aside on a plate. In the same pot, add the onions, garlic, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5 minutes, or until vegetables are tender and the onions are translucent. Stir frequently to prevent burning.

Add the chicken back into the pot along with the chicken stock, water, bay leaves, and thyme. Bring the liquid to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Simmer the mixture for about 25–30 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.

Remove the chicken from the pot and let it cool slightly. Discard the bay leaves and any thyme sprigs. Once the chicken is cool enough to handle, shred the meat into bite-sized pieces, discarding the skin and bones. Return the shredded chicken to the pot and give everything a good stir.

Step 2: Making the Dumpling Dough

While the chicken and broth simmer, start on the dumplings. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Add the diced cold butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs.

Pour in the milk (or buttermilk) gradually, stirring gently with a wooden spoon or spatula. Mix just until the dough comes together; do not overwork it, as this can result in dense dumplings. The dough should be slightly sticky.

Step 3: Cooking the Dumplings

Increase the heat under the pot of chicken and broth to medium so the liquid reaches a low, gentle simmer. Using a tablespoon or small ice cream scoop, drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough directly onto the surface of the simmering broth. The dumplings will expand as they cook, so make sure to leave some space between each one.

Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and let the dumplings cook undisturbed for 15–18 minutes. The steam trapped under the lid will help the dumplings cook through and become incredibly fluffy.

To check if the dumplings are done, insert a toothpick or skewer into the center of one; it should come out clean.

Step 4: Bringing It All Together

Once the dumplings are cooked, give the pot a final stir. Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, if needed. Ladle the chicken and dumplings into bowls, making sure each serving includes a generous helping of both broth and dumplings. Garnish with chopped parsley or chives for a touch of brightness.

Tips for the Perfect Chicken and Dumplings

  1. Choose Bone-In Chicken for Richer Flavor
    Bone-in chicken imparts a deeper, richer flavor to the broth. Don’t rush through the simmering process—it’s essential for developing that signature homemade taste.

  2. Don’t Overwork the Dumpling Dough
    Overmixing the dumpling batter can lead to tough, dense dumplings. Mix just until the ingredients are combined and let the dough rest for a minute before forming.

  3. Use Fresh Herbs When Possible
    Fresh herbs like thyme, parsley, or dill can take the dish to another level. While dried herbs work well, adding a sprig or two of fresh thyme to the broth is highly recommended.

  4. Homemade Broth as a Secret Weapon
    If you have time to make your own chicken stock from scratch, go for it! The depth of flavor it adds is unmatched. You can even use the chicken carcass or bones to prepare the broth in advance.

  5. Prep Ahead for Busy Weeknights
    This dish can also be partially prepped ahead of time. Cook and shred the chicken and store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Similarly, the veggie base can be pre-cooked and refrigerated for quick assembly later.

Serving Suggestions

Chicken and dumplings is a hearty, stand-alone dish, but you can pair it with a few sides to elevate the meal:

  • A light side salad with mixed greens, sliced apples, and a vinaigrette complements the richness of the dish.
  • Buttered green beans or sautéed asparagus add a bright, fresh element.
  • Homemade breadsticks or biscuits are perfect for soaking up extra broth and ensure not a single drop goes to waste.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

This recipe makes a generous batch, so leftovers are very likely—and welcome! Store chicken and dumplings in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3–4 days. Reheat gently on the stove over low heat, adding a splash of water or broth to loosen the consistency. Avoid over-mixing the dumplings when reheating to maintain their texture.

For longer storage, freeze individual portions in freezer-safe containers for up to 3 months. Let them thaw in the refrigerator overnight and reheat as needed.

Final Thoughts

Chicken and dumplings is a dish that transforms humble ingredients into a symphony of comforting flavors. It’s a recipe that speaks to tradition and nostalgia, bringing loved ones together around the table. With this step-by-step guide, you’ll master the art of crafting the perfect balance of tender chicken, flavorful broth, and fluffy dumplings.

So, why not gather your ingredients today and give this recipe a try? The reward of a bowl filled with homemade chicken and dumplings is well worth the effort. Let it become a staple in your kitchen—one that’s sure to be requested over and over again. Happy cooking!
