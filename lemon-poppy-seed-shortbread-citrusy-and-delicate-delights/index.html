Lemon Poppy Seed Shortbread: Citrusy and Delicate Delights


If you’re a fan of delicate, buttery treats with a burst of citrus flavor, then you’re in for a delightful surprise with this Lemon Poppy Seed Shortbread recipe. These crisp, crumbly cookies are perfect for a sunny afternoon tea or as a sweet addition to any dessert spread. The combination of zesty lemon and the subtle crunch of poppy seeds creates a unique and refreshing twist on the classic shortbread, making it a must-try for any baking enthusiast.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and confectioners’ sugar until light and fluffy.

  2. Add the vanilla extract and lemon zest to the butter mixture, and mix until well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, poppy seeds, and a pinch of salt.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a smooth dough forms.

  5. Divide the dough in half and shape each half into a log, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Wrap the logs in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or until firm.

  6. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  7. Once the dough has chilled, remove the plastic wrap and slice the logs into 1/4-inch thick rounds.

  8. Place the rounds onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart.

  9. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges of the shortbread are just beginning to turn golden.

  10. Allow the shortbread to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Serving Suggestions

These Lemon Poppy Seed Shortbread cookies are delightful on their own, but they also pair wonderfully with a cup of hot tea or coffee. For a more indulgent treat, consider serving them alongside a scoop of lemon sorbet or a dollop of whipped cream. The bright citrus flavor of the shortbread makes it a fantastic addition to any summer dessert platter.

Storage

To keep your Lemon Poppy Seed Shortbread fresh and flavorful, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to one week. If you’d like to enjoy them for longer, you can freeze the baked cookies for up to one month. Simply thaw them at room temperature before serving.

Final Thoughts

The delicate crumble of the shortbread, the burst of citrus, and the subtle crunch of poppy seeds make these cookies a truly special treat. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply craving a bit of sunshine in each bite, these Lemon Poppy Seed Shortbread cookies are sure to bring a smile to your face. Embrace the joy of baking and share these citrusy delights with your loved ones – they’re bound to become a new favorite in your recipe collection. Happy baking!
