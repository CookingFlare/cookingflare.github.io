The Most Popular Pie Recipes from Around the World


Pies have been a cherished part of culinary traditions for centuries, transcending borders and cultures. These delicious creations are present in almost every corner of the globe, each reflecting the ingredients, history, and flavors of the region it hails from. From savory handheld pastries to decadent dessert pies, travelers and food lovers will find countless variations to delight their tastebuds. Today, we’ll explore some of the most famous pie recipes from around the world—each one a testament to its cultural heritage.

1. American Apple Pie

An icon of comfort food, the American apple pie is synonymous with home-cooked goodness. This beloved dessert features a filling of fresh apples, cinnamon, sugar, and often a hint of nutmeg, enclosed in a flaky, buttery crust. What makes apple pie stand out is its simplicity and versatility. Granny Smith and Honeycrisp apples are commonly used for their balance of sweetness and tartness.

The origins of apple pie can be traced back to Europe, but its popularity surged in the U.S. during the Colonial era. Over the years, Americans have adopted it as their own, often serving it warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. Whether it’s for a holiday gathering or a casual weekend treat, apple pie remains a comforting classic.

2. British Steak and Kidney Pie

Savory pies hold a special place in British cuisine, and steak and kidney pie stands as one of the best-known traditional dishes. Made with tender beef steak, lamb or pork kidneys, onions, and a rich gravy of beef stock and ale, this pie offers a robust, earthy flavor. The filling is enveloped in a golden shortcrust pastry, giving it an irresistible texture.

It’s not just the taste that makes steak and kidney pie special; it’s also the nostalgic value it has for the British people. Whether served in pubs or enjoyed at home, this hearty pie is often paired with mashed potatoes and steamed vegetables, making it the ultimate comfort food.

3. French Tarte Tatin

Moving into desserts, the French tarte Tatin is a masterpiece of caramelized sweetness. This upside-down pie features apples, butter, and sugar cooked to perfection before being topped with pastry and baked. When flipped out of the pan, the fruit glistens with a deep caramelized glaze.

Legend has it that this recipe was created by accident in the 1880s at the Hôtel Tatin in France. Nevertheless, it has become a staple in French patisserie, celebrated for its rich, buttery flavors. A bite of tarte Tatin delivers a perfect balance of caramelized fruit and buttery crust, making it a decadent end to any meal.

4. Italian Ricotta Pie

Italy has its fair share of savory and dessert pies, but ricotta pie remains one of the hidden gems. This traditional pie is often served during Easter, featuring a filling of creamy ricotta cheese—sometimes blended with sugar, eggs, vanilla, and citrus zest—housed within a delicate pastry shell.

Richly textured yet light and airy, ricotta pie is a testament to the Italian love of fresh, high-quality ingredients. Variations abound throughout different regions of Italy, with some including chocolate chips or changing the crust to one made of almond flour. No matter the twist, this pie is a celebration of indulgence and tradition.

5. Australian Meat Pie

Compact, savory, and incredibly satisfying, the Australian meat pie is a national treasure and a favorite grab-and-go snack. The flaky, golden pastry crust is filled with minced beef, onions, and a deeply seasoned gravy, often thickened with flour or cornstarch.

Whether served at sporting events or at bakeries, this iconic pie is enjoyed by Australians of all ages. The portability and hearty filling make it perfect for enjoying on the move. Often paired with tomato sauce (similar to ketchup), a hot meat pie is truly an Australian comfort food classic.

6. Greek Spanakopita

Spanakopita is Greece’s take on a pie that delights with its mix of fresh, wholesome flavors. Made from layers of phyllo dough filled with spinach, feta cheese, onions, eggs, and herbs like dill or parsley, this savory pie is loved for its crisp texture and tangy taste.

While traditionally made as a large pie cut into slices, spanakopita is equally popular in small, triangular portions that are perfect for appetizers or snacks. Pair it with a Greek salad, and you’ll have a meal that captures the spirit of fresh Mediterranean cooking.

7. South African Bobotie Pie

South Africa’s bobotie pie puts a unique spin on the concept of savory pies, blending influences from Malaysian and Dutch cuisines. It features a curried meat filling—usually beef or lamb—mixed with fruits like raisins or apricots, and topped with a creamy egg custard.

The pie has a perfect balance of sweet, spicy, and savory flavors. Often served with yellow rice, chutney, and a side salad, bobotie pie exemplifies the diversity of South African culinary heritage.

8. Moroccan Pastilla

Pastilla is a Moroccan pie that beautifully combines the savory and sweet aspects of cuisine. Traditionally made with pigeon, it now uses shredded chicken or seafood baked in paper-thin warqa dough (similar to phyllo). The filling is spiced with cinnamon, saffron, and ginger, layered with scrambled eggs and ground almonds, and dusted with powdered sugar.

The harmonious blend of sweet and savory makes pastilla a favorite during weddings and other celebrations. Its incredible flavor profile offers a glimpse into Morocco’s vibrant culinary traditions.

9. Canadian Butter Tarts

Canada’s butter tarts are a simple yet irresistible dessert. The filling, made of butter, sugar, syrup, and eggs, is baked into small flaky pastry shells. This decadent treat can be made plain or with the addition of raisins, pecans, or other ingredients.

With origins in Canadian pioneer kitchens, butter tarts have grown to symbolize the country’s sweet tooth and dedication to locally sourced ingredients. Bite-sized and filled with gooey sweetness, they’re as charmingly rustic as they are delicious.

10. Argentine Empanadas

Though technically not classified as pies, empanadas deserve mention due to their close relationship with handheld pastries. Argentina is famous for these small savory pies filled with a variety of ingredients like minced beef, olives, boiled eggs, and onions. Spices like paprika and cumin lend empanadas their distinctive flavor.

Typically baked or fried, empanadas are a staple at parties, family gatherings, and roadside food stalls. Their versatility ensures there’s a filling for everyone, ranging from traditional meats to vegetarian options bursting with local produce.

11. Japanese Anko Pie

Japan’s dessert culture often highlights subtle sweetness, and anko pie is no exception. Made from flaky pastry and filled with sweet red bean paste (anko), this pie is simple yet satisfying. It’s a popular treat found in bakeries and cafes.

The natural sweetness of the red bean paste pairs beautifully with buttery pastry, creating a dessert that feels light yet indulgent. Perfect with green tea, anko pie reflects Japan’s love for desserts that honor traditional flavors.

12. Irish Shepherd’s Pie

Rounding out our exploration is Ireland’s famous shepherd’s pie. Though technically more of a casserole, its pie-like appearance has earned it a place on this list. Traditionally made with minced lamb (or beef), carrots, peas, and rich gravy, it’s topped with creamy mashed potatoes and baked to create a golden crust.

Shepherd’s pie is the epitome of hearty, home-cooked Irish food. It’s a dish often reserved for family dinners, offering warmth and comfort when served right out of the oven.

The Global Appeal of Pies

The sheer variety of pies around the world provides endless opportunities to explore cuisine through a humble yet versatile dish. Pies are more than just food—they tell stories, preserve traditions, and bring people together. Whether you’re biting into a sweet tarte Tatin or savoring a hearty steak and kidney pie, you’re diving into the heart of a culture.

So, the next time you’re planning your dinner menu or eager to try a new dessert, consider experimenting with one of these global pie recipes. It’s a delicious way to travel the world without leaving your kitchen!
