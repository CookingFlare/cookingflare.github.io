How to Make Ice Cream with Almond Milk: 5 Recipes


Ice cream is one of those universal treats that everyone loves, no matter the season. But what if you’re looking for a dairy-free alternative that’s just as creamy, indulgent, and satisfying? Enter almond milk ice cream. Almond milk, with its smooth texture and subtle nutty flavor, makes an excellent base for ice cream, providing a dairy-free, vegan-friendly option without compromising on taste or creaminess.

In this blog post, we’ll explore five unique and mouthwatering recipes to make ice cream using almond milk. Whether you’re a seasoned dairy-free enthusiast or simply trying to reduce your dairy intake, these recipes are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth without any of the guilt.

Why Choose Almond Milk for Ice Cream?

Almond milk is a fantastic dairy substitute for those who are lactose intolerant, vegan, or looking to cut back on dairy for other health reasons. Unlike cow’s milk, almond milk is lower in calories and fat, yet still rich in essential nutrients like vitamins E and D. It’s naturally creamy and smooth, making it an excellent base for homemade ice cream.

The beauty of almond milk is that it blends well with a wide variety of flavors and can take on the richness and creaminess of traditional ice cream when combined with the right ingredients. Whether you prefer a fruity, chocolatey, or nutty flavor, almond milk can easily adapt to your tastes.

Let’s dive into five creative recipes for making ice cream with almond milk.

1. Classic Almond Milk Vanilla Ice Cream

Vanilla is a timeless flavor loved by many, and it’s easy to make a dairy-free version using almond milk. This recipe is simple, creamy, and versatile — perfect for those new to making homemade ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup canned coconut milk (full-fat for extra creaminess)
  • ¾ cup maple syrup (or your preferred sweetener)
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional for thickening)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the almond milk, coconut milk, maple syrup, and salt. Stir occasionally until the mixture begins to warm up.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of cold water to create a slurry. If you’re using cornstarch for added creaminess, add it to the saucepan and whisk it into the milk mixture. Allow the mixture to come to a simmer and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until it thickens slightly.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Let the mixture cool down to room temperature.
  4. Once the base is cool, transfer it to an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  5. Transfer the churned ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 3-4 hours to firm up before serving.

This classic vanilla almond milk ice cream is simple yet satisfying. Its smooth texture and fragrant vanilla flavor make it a great base for adding mix-ins like chocolate chips, crushed cookies, or fresh berries if you like.

2. Chocolate Almond Milk Ice Cream

If you’re a chocolate lover, this rich and velvety chocolate almond milk ice cream will hit the spot. The deep cocoa flavor pairs perfectly with the creamy almond milk base, creating an indulgent treat that rivals traditional dairy ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • ½ cup cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • ½ cup maple syrup or agave syrup
  • ½ cup chocolate chips (dairy-free if necessary)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the almond milk, coconut milk, cocoa powder, and salt. Whisk together until fully combined and heated over medium heat.
  2. Stir in the maple syrup and continue to cook the mixture until it’s hot but not boiling.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and whisk in the chocolate chips until they melt completely, forming a rich chocolate base.
  4. Stir in the vanilla extract and allow the mixture to cool to room temperature.
  5. Once cooled, pour the mixture into your ice cream maker and churn as per the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. After churning, transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for 3-4 hours until it’s firm and scoopable.

This chocolate almond milk ice cream is perfect for satisfying your chocolate cravings in a healthier, dairy-free way. It’s rich, creamy, and incredibly indulgent.

3. Strawberry Almond Milk Ice Cream

Nothing beats a scoop of fresh, fruity ice cream, and strawberry almond milk ice cream is a deliciously refreshing treat, especially during the warmer months. The sweetness of ripe strawberries combined with almond milk creates a smooth and creamy dessert that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries (or frozen)
  • 1 ½ cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • ¾ cup maple syrup (or sweetener of choice)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Begin by puréeing the strawberries in a blender or food processor until smooth. If you’re using frozen strawberries, allow them to thaw slightly before blending.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the almond milk, coconut milk, maple syrup, salt, and lemon juice. Heat over medium heat until it begins to warm up, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the strawberry purée and continue to heat the mixture for a few minutes, making sure everything is combined.
  4. Remove the mixture from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Let the mixture cool completely before transferring it to an ice cream maker. Churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for 3-4 hours before serving.

This strawberry almond milk ice cream is light, fresh, and bursting with the natural sweetness of strawberries. It’s an ideal summer treat that will transport you straight to a sunny day.

4. Almond Butter Almond Milk Ice Cream

For those who love nutty flavors, this almond butter almond milk ice cream will be your new favorite. The rich and creamy almond butter base is infused with the subtle nutty flavor of almonds, making it a deliciously satisfying dessert for any nut lover.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • ½ cup almond butter (smooth)
  • ½ cup maple syrup (or sweetener of choice)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the almond milk, coconut milk, almond butter, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the almond butter is completely dissolved into the milk mixture.
  2. Add the maple syrup and continue to heat, whisking the mixture until it’s smooth and heated through.
  3. Once everything is combined, remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Allow the mixture to cool down to room temperature before transferring it to the ice cream maker.
  5. Churn the mixture according to the ice cream maker’s instructions.
  6. After churning, place the ice cream in an airtight container and freeze for 3-4 hours to firm up.

This almond butter almond milk ice cream is rich, creamy, and full of nutty goodness. It’s a great way to enjoy the flavor of almonds in a decadent frozen treat.

5. Mango Almond Milk Ice Cream

Mango is another fruit that makes an excellent addition to homemade ice cream. This tropical-inspired mango almond milk ice cream is creamy, fruity, and refreshing — perfect for a sunny day or whenever you need a bit of sunshine in your life.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe mangos (peeled and chopped)
  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • ¾ cup maple syrup or coconut sugar
  • 1 teaspoon lime juice
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Place the chopped mangoes in a blender and blend until smooth.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the almond milk, coconut milk, maple syrup, and lime juice. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once the mixture is warmed through, remove it from the heat and add the mango purée. Stir to combine and ensure the mixture is smooth.
  4. Let the mixture cool completely before transferring it to an ice cream maker.
  5. Churn the mango almond milk mixture according to your ice cream maker’s instructions.
  6. Freeze for 3-4 hours until the ice cream firms up to your desired consistency.

This mango almond milk ice cream is a tropical delight, with the natural sweetness of mango perfectly complementing the creamy almond milk base. It’s a fantastic treat for those hot days when you need something refreshing.

Final Thoughts

Making ice cream with almond milk is not only easy, but it also allows you to create delicious, creamy desserts that everyone can enjoy — whether they’re following a dairy-free, vegan, or plant-based lifestyle. With these five recipes, you can enjoy a wide variety of flavors, from rich chocolate to refreshing fruit combinations, all while staying true to your dietary preferences.

So, grab your ice cream maker, and start experimenting with almond milk-based ice cream today! Each of these recipes can be customized to suit your taste preferences — feel free to add in your favorite mix-ins, toppings, or flavors to make them your own. Enjoy every creamy, dreamy bite!
