Delicious Filipino Dishes: 6 Recipes for a Taste of the Islands


Filipino cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of flavors woven from centuries of cultural influences. With its unique blend of sweet, savory, sour, and salty elements, Filipino dishes promise an unforgettable culinary experience. Whether you’re new to Filipino food or eager to replicate your favorite island flavors at home, these six recipes offer a delicious journey through the Philippines. From hearty stews to sweet delicacies, these classics will bring authentic island vibes to your table.

1. Adobo: The Quintessential Filipino Dish

Adobo is arguably the most iconic dish in the Philippines. Known for its tangy and savory flavors, it’s a versatile recipe that varies regionally. The classic version features chicken or pork braised in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken or pork (or a mix of both)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar (white or cane vinegar is ideal)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 3 dried bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, marinate the meat in soy sauce, half the minced garlic, and black pepper for at least 1 hour (overnight if possible).
  2. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté the remaining garlic until fragrant, then add the onion (if using).
  3. Add the marinated meat, including the marinade. Cook for a few minutes, stirring to lightly brown the meat.
  4. Pour in the vinegar (do not stir once added) and allow it to boil for 2-3 minutes to mellow the acidity. Then stir gently.
  5. Add water and bay leaves. Cover and simmer on low heat until the meat is tender and the sauce has reduced to your preferred consistency.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Adobo’s magic lies in its simplicity and ability to keep well—it tastes even better the next day!

2. Sinigang: A Comforting Sour Soup

For a dish that warms the soul, look no further than sinigang. This sour tamarind-based soup incorporates pork, shrimp, or fish, along with various vegetables to create a refreshing yet comforting bowl of goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 500 g pork belly or ribs (or shrimp/fish for variation)
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 packet tamarind soup mix or 1 cup fresh tamarind pulp
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch segments
  • 1 cup water spinach (kangkong) or substitute with spinach
  • 2 green chilies (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and fish sauce to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork, onion, and tomatoes, then simmer until the pork is tender (around 1-1.5 hours). Skim off impurities as needed.
  2. Stir in the tamarind pulp or soup mix. Adjust the sourness to your liking.
  3. Add radish, string beans, and green chilies. Cook for 5 minutes or until slightly tender.
  4. Add water spinach and simmer for another 1-2 minutes.
  5. Season with fish sauce and salt to taste.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy alongside steamed rice.

The tangy and savory flavor of sinigang is a true reflection of the Filipino palate.

3. Lechon Kawali: Crispy Fried Pork Belly

A crunchy, golden-brown piece of lechon kawali is bound to steal the heart of any meat lover. Think of it as crispy pork belly perfection, a dish that pairs well with a good dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Water (enough to cover the pork)
  • Cooking oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add salt, peppercorns, garlic, and bay leaves.
  2. Carefully add the pork belly and boil until tender (around 1-1.5 hours). Remove the pork belly and let it cool completely.
  3. Once cooled, pat the pork belly dry with paper towels. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours to dry further (this ensures crispiness).
  4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or heavy pot. Fry the pork belly until golden and crispy. Be cautious of oil splatter!
  5. Let it rest for a few minutes before chopping into smaller pieces. Serve with lechon sauce or vinegar with crushed garlic as a dip.

This indulgent dish is best served as a main course or paired with an ice-cold beverage.

4. Pancit Canton: Stir-Fried Noodles with Vegetables

Noodles signify long life in Filipino culture, and Pancit Canton is a beloved choice for celebrations or everyday meals. This dish is filling, customizable, and packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 250 g egg noodles (Pancit Canton)
  • 200 g chicken or pork, sliced into strips
  • 1 cup shrimp (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup snap peas
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon slices (optional garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large frying pan. Sauté garlic and onions until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken or pork and cook until fully done. If using shrimp, add halfway through and cook until pink.
  3. Stir in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Add the vegetables and cook until tender-crisp.
  5. Add the egg noodles and toss until fully coated and heated through.
  6. Serve hot and garnish with lemon slices for a zesty finish.

This crowd-pleaser is perfect as a main dish or a shareable side.

5. Kare-Kare: A Flavorful Peanut Stew

Kare-Kare is a rich and creamy stew made with peanut sauce and oxtail, often served during special occasions. Its distinct nutty flavor is balanced by savory bagoong (fermented shrimp paste).

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg oxtail (or beef shank)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup ground toasted rice
  • 1 tablespoon annatto seeds (for color)
  • 2 cups banana blossoms, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 1/2 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 4 tablespoons bagoong (shrimp paste)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the oxtail in water until tender (slow cooking is best for this step).
  2. In a separate pan, sauté annatto seeds in oil to extract color. Discard seeds and add this oil to the stew.
  3. Mix peanut butter and ground toasted rice into a paste. Stir this into the stew and let it simmer.
  4. Add the vegetables and cook until tender.
  5. Serve hot with bagoong on the side and steamed rice.

Its creamy, nutty goodness is unforgettable!

6. Leche Flan: A Sweet Filipino Treat

End your Filipino feast with the ultimate dessert: leche flan. This silky custard topped with caramel sauce is a decadent way to conclude any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 10 egg yolks
  • 1 can (300mL) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (370mL) evaporated milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sugar (for caramel)

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt sugar over low heat until golden brown. Pour into a llanera (or baking dish) and spread thinly to coat the bottom. Let it cool and harden.
  2. In a bowl, whisk egg yolks, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla until well combined. Strain to remove bubbles and lumps.
  3. Pour the mixture into the prepared llanera.
  4. Steam for 30-40 minutes or bake in a water bath at 350°F (175°C) until set.
  5. Let it cool and chill before serving.

Its luscious texture and rich flavor make it a crowd favorite!

Filipino cuisine is a celebration of diverse flavors, textures, and traditions. By trying these six recipes, you’ll not only savor the deliciousness of the islands but also gain a warm appreciation for the country’s vibrant culinary heritage. Whether it’s the tang of sinigang or the indulgence of lechon kawali, these dishes are sure to transport your taste buds to the Philippines.
