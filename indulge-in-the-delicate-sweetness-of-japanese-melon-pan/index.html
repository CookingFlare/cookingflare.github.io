Indulge in the Delicate Sweetness of Japanese Melon Pan


If you’re a fan of sweet treats and have a soft spot for Japanese cuisine, then you’re in for a delightful treat! Today, we’re diving into the world of Japanese Melon Pan – a delightful pastry that combines the softness of bread with the sweetness of a cookie crust. Join me on this culinary adventure as we explore the origins, ingredients, and step-by-step process of making this delicate and delicious Japanese delicacy.

The Origins of Japanese Melon Pan

Melon Pan, also known as Melon Bread, is a popular sweet treat that originated in Japan during the 20th century. Despite its name, the pastry doesn’t actually contain any melon flavoring. The name “Melon Pan” comes from its unique appearance, resembling the shape of a melon. It is believed that the pastry was inspired by the European-style bread and was introduced to Japan during the Meiji era.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To make Japanese Melon Pan, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the cookie crust:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of salt

The Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Making the Bread Dough

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and instant yeast.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, softened butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until a soft dough forms.
  4. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

Step 2: Making the Cookie Crust

  1. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  2. Add the egg and mix well.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a smooth cookie dough forms.

Step 3: Assembling and Baking

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Divide the bread dough into 8 equal parts and shape each portion into a ball.
  3. Take a portion of the cookie dough and flatten it into a disc large enough to cover one ball of bread dough.
  4. Place the cookie dough disc over the bread dough ball, covering it completely.
  5. Repeat this process for the remaining bread and cookie dough portions.
  6. Place the Melon Pan on the prepared baking sheet, leaving enough space between each piece for expansion.
  7. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the cookie crust turns golden brown.
  8. Remove from the oven and let the Melon Pan cool on a wire rack before serving.

Indulge in the Delicate Sweetness

Congratulations! You’ve successfully made your own Japanese Melon Pan. Now, it’s time to indulge in the delicate sweetness of this delightful treat. The soft and fluffy bread combined with the crunchy, sweet cookie crust creates a unique texture and flavor experience that will leave your taste buds craving for more.

Whether you enjoy it with a cup of tea or as a snack on the go, Japanese Melon Pan is a versatile pastry that will satisfy your sweet tooth. So go ahead, try your hand at making this delectable treat and share it with your family and friends. Happy baking!

Note: For variations, you can add chocolate chips, matcha powder, or even fill the Melon Pan with custard or sweet bean paste. Let your creativity run wild and experiment with different flavors to create your own unique twist on this Japanese classic.
