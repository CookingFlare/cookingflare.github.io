Creamy Gratin Dauphinois: A French Potato Bake


If there’s one dish that perfectly embodies French comfort food, it’s the Creamy Gratin Dauphinois. This classic potato bake is a delicious combination of creamy, tender potatoes and rich, cheesy goodness. It’s the perfect side dish for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to elevate your weeknight meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of Yukon Gold potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups of heavy cream
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 cup of Gruyere cheese, grated
  • 1/4 cup of Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, diced
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced potatoes, minced garlic, salt, and black pepper. Toss gently to evenly distribute the seasonings.

  3. In a separate saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium-low heat until it starts to simmer. Remove from heat.

  4. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray. Arrange a single layer of potatoes in the bottom of the dish, slightly overlapping each other.

  5. Pour a generous amount of the simmering cream over the potatoes, just enough to cover them. Sprinkle a handful of Gruyere cheese and Parmesan cheese over the potatoes.

  6. Repeat step 5, layering the potatoes, cream, and cheese until all the ingredients are used. Finish with a layer of cheese on top.

  7. Dot the diced butter over the cheese.

  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 40 minutes.

  9. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the potatoes are tender when pierced with a fork.

  10. Once done, remove the dish from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes.

  11. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves for a pop of color and added flavor.

  12. Serve the Creamy Gratin Dauphinois as a side dish alongside roasted meats, grilled vegetables, or enjoy it on its own with a crisp salad.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add a layer of caramelized onions between the potato layers. Simply sauté sliced onions in butter until golden brown before layering them with the potatoes.

  • If you’re not a fan of Gruyere cheese, you can substitute it with other melting cheeses like Emmental or Fontina.

  • To make this dish vegetarian-friendly, you can use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.

  • For an extra touch of indulgence, you can add a sprinkle of truffle oil or truffle salt to the potatoes before baking.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave until heated through.

Creamy Gratin Dauphinois is a timeless French classic that never fails to impress. Its creamy, cheesy layers of potatoes are sure to melt in your mouth and leave you craving for more. So, why not bring a taste of France to your kitchen and indulge in this delectable potato bake? Bon appétit!
