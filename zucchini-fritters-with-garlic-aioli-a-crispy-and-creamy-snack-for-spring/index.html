Zucchini Fritters with Garlic Aioli: A Crispy and Creamy Snack for Spring


Spring is a season of fresh produce and vibrant flavors, and what better way to celebrate than with a delicious and easy-to-make snack? Zucchini fritters with garlic aioli are the perfect combination of crispy and creamy, making them an irresistible treat for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a spring gathering or simply looking for a tasty snack to enjoy on a sunny afternoon, these zucchini fritters are sure to satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

For the fritters:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, grated
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the garlic aioli:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by grating the zucchinis using a box grater or a food processor. Place the grated zucchinis in a colander and sprinkle with salt. Let them sit for about 10 minutes to allow the excess moisture to drain.

  2. While the zucchinis are draining, prepare the garlic aioli. In a small bowl, combine mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated. Set aside in the refrigerator to allow the flavors to meld together.

  3. After the zucchinis have drained, use your hands to squeeze out any remaining moisture. Transfer the grated zucchinis to a large mixing bowl.

  4. To the bowl of grated zucchinis, add the all-purpose flour, grated Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, black pepper, and lightly beaten eggs. Mix everything together until well combined. The mixture should be thick enough to hold its shape.

  5. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Using a tablespoon or an ice cream scoop, drop spoonfuls of the zucchini mixture into the hot skillet. Flatten each spoonful gently with the back of the spoon to form a fritter shape. Cook the fritters for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.

  6. Once the fritters are cooked, transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil. Repeat this process with the remaining zucchini mixture until all the fritters are cooked.

  7. Serve the zucchini fritters warm with a dollop of garlic aioli on top. The creamy aioli perfectly complements the crispy texture of the fritters, creating a mouthwatering combination of flavors. You can also garnish them with some freshly chopped herbs, such as parsley or dill, for an extra pop of freshness.

  8. Enjoy your zucchini fritters with garlic aioli as a snack, appetizer, or side dish. They are best served immediately while still warm and crispy. The leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days and reheated in a preheated oven for a few minutes to regain their crispiness.

Conclusion

Zucchini fritters with garlic aioli are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of spring. The crispy exterior and creamy interior of these fritters make them a satisfying snack that can be enjoyed by everyone. Whether you’re a fan of zucchini or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is a must-try. So, grab some fresh zucchinis and get ready to indulge in this crispy and creamy delight that will surely become a favorite in your spring recipe collection.
