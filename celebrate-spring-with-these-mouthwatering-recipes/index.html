Celebrate Spring with These Mouthwatering Recipes


Spring has arrived, and what better way to embrace the season than with a refreshingly delightful range of dishes? From savory starters to scrumptious desserts, these unique recipes showcase the essence of springtime flavors. Needless to say, they’re not just mouthwatering but also bursting with nutritious ingredients.

Ready to cook up a spring-inspired feast? Here are some tantalizing recipes to try.

1. Cheesy Spring Frittata

Wake up to the golden and tender embrace of a made-from-scratch frittata. Packed with fresh ingredients like spinach, cherry tomatoes, and spring onions, this simple yet elegant breakfast or brunch dish will elevate your morning routine.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped spinach
  • 1/2 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 chopped spring onion
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
  2. In an oven-safe non-stick skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add spinach, cherry tomatoes, and spring onion to the pan and sauté until soft.
  4. Pour egg mixture over vegetables.
  5. Use a spatula to gently push the cooked vegetables down into the egg mixture. Simmer until the edges are set but the center is still slightly soft.
  6. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese evenly over the top.
  7. Place skillet in the oven and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-12 minutes, or until the frittata is set.
  8. Remove from oven and top with chopped chives. Serve hot.

2. Spring Pea and Mint Risotto

A classic Italian dish gets a refreshing spring makeover. Creamy, delectable Arborio rice teams up with peas and mint for a dish that’s perfect for welcoming warmer weather.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint, tightly packed
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 1/2 cups grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, pulse peas and mint until pureed.
  2. In a saucepan, heat vegetable broth until simmering.
  3. In a large, deep skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until tender and fragrant.
  4. Stir in Arborio rice, ensuring it’s well coated with oil. Cook for a couple of minutes to toast.
  5. Gradually add white wine, stirring constantly, until absorb.
  6. Add the pea-mint puree and continue stirring until fully incorporated.
  7. Add heated vegetable broth, about 1/2 cup at a time, stirring continuously, waiting for each addition to absorb before adding more.
  8. Stir in grated Parmesan cheese, and cook for a further 2-3 minutes to blend flavors.
  9. Serve hot, garnishing with extra chopped mint.

3. Strawberry Lavender Shortcakes

Cap off your meal on a sweet note with these fruity, floral shortcakes. The buttery, crumbly biscuit base is paired with luscious strawberries and fragrant lavender to create a delightful dessert that screams spring.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup cold heavy cream
  • 1 1/2 cups sliced strawberries
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon dried lavender
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add cold butter pieces and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work butter into the flour mixture until crumbly.
  4. Add heavy cream and stir with a fork just until combined.
  5. On a floured surface, knead the dough briefly until it comes together. Pat out to about 3/4 inch thickness.
  6. Use a 2-inch biscuit cutter to cut out shortcakes. Place on prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for about 15 minutes, or until golden brown. Let cool.
  8. While the shortcakes are baking, prepare the strawberries: In a medium bowl, mix sliced strawberries, honey, and dried lavender until incorporated.
  9. To serve, place a biscuit on a plate, top with a spoonful of strawberry lavender mixture, then another biscuit. Repeat until all biscuits are used, dusting with a little powdered sugar for garnish.

Indulge in these delectable recipes this spring. They’re the perfect way to welcome warmer weather and bountiful harvests. So go ahead and spice up your springtime celebrations with mouthwatering flavors that truly celebrate the season.
