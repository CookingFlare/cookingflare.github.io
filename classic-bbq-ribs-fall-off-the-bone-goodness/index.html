Classic BBQ Ribs: Fall-Off-The-Bone Goodness


There’s nothing quite like sinking your teeth into a tender, succulent rack of classic BBQ ribs. The smoky, savory flavor combined with the satisfying sensation of the meat falling off the bone is a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a hearty meal, this classic BBQ ribs recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 racks of pork baby back ribs
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Ribs

  1. Remove Membrane: Start by preparing the ribs. Carefully remove the thin membrane from the back of the ribs. This will help the seasonings and smoke flavor to penetrate the meat more effectively.

  2. Seasoning: In a small bowl, mix the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Rub this seasoning mixture generously all over the ribs, ensuring that every inch of the meat is coated.

  3. Refrigerate: Once seasoned, cover the ribs with plastic wrap and refrigerate them for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and infuse the meat.

Step 2: Preparing the Grill

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to a temperature of 275°F (135°C) using indirect heat. If you’re using a charcoal grill, place the coals on one side and leave the other side empty. For gas grills, light one side and leave the other side unlit.

Step 3: Smoking the Ribs

  1. Wood Chips: If you’re using a charcoal grill, add soaked wood chips directly onto the coals for that delicious smoky flavor. If you’re using a gas grill, place the soaked wood chips in a smoker box or aluminum foil pouch and put it over the lit burner.

  2. Grill the Ribs: Once the grill is ready, place the seasoned ribs on the side of the grill opposite the heat source. Close the lid and let the ribs cook for about 3 hours, maintaining a consistent temperature. This slow and low cooking method is crucial for achieving that fall-off-the-bone tenderness.

Step 4: Glazing the Ribs

  1. Brush with BBQ Sauce: After 3 hours, carefully remove the ribs from the grill. Brush them generously with your favorite BBQ sauce, ensuring an even coating.

  2. Return to Grill: Place the sauced ribs back on the grill and cook for an additional 30 minutes, allowing the sauce to caramelize and create a sticky, flavorful glaze.

  3. Rest and Serve: Once done, remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in juicy, tender meat. Slice the ribs between the bones and serve hot with your favorite sides.

Conclusion

With its perfect balance of smoky, sweet, and savory flavors, these classic BBQ ribs are a true crowd-pleaser. The slow smoking process ensures that the meat is incredibly tender and practically falls off the bone with each bite. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or a novice cook, this recipe is a surefire way to impress your guests and satisfy your cravings for mouthwatering barbecue goodness. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and get ready to savor the irresistible delight of classic BBQ ribs!
