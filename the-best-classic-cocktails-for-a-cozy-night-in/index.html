The Best Classic Cocktails for a Cozy Night In


When the weather outside is chilly and the nights are long, there’s nothing quite like curling up with a good book, a warm blanket, and a perfectly crafted cocktail. Classic cocktails are a staple of any cozy night in, and for good reason. They’re familiar, comforting, and always a hit. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best classic cocktails that are sure to make your next night in a memorable one.

Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned is a timeless classic that’s been around since the late 19th century. Made with bourbon or rye whiskey, sugar, bitters, and a twist of citrus, this cocktail is a masterclass in simplicity. The combination of the rich whiskey, the sweetness of the sugar, and the bitterness of the bitters creates a complex flavor profile that’s both familiar and exciting.

To make an Old Fashioned, start by filling a mixing glass with ice. Add 2 oz of bourbon or rye whiskey, 1/2 teaspoon of sugar, and 2 dashes of bitters. Stir the mixture for about 30 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a rocks glass filled with ice, and garnish with a twist of citrus.

Manhattan

The Manhattan is another classic cocktail that’s been around for over a century. Made with whiskey, vermouth, and bitters, this cocktail is a staple of any cocktail bar. The combination of the rich whiskey, the dry vermouth, and the bitter bitters creates a complex flavor profile that’s both sophisticated and approachable.

To make a Manhattan, start by filling a mixing glass with ice. Add 2 oz of whiskey, 1/2 oz of vermouth, and 2 dashes of bitters. Stir the mixture for about 30 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a coupe glass, and garnish with a cherry.

Daiquiri

The Daiquiri is a classic cocktail that’s been around since the early 20th century. Made with rum, lime juice, and simple syrup, this cocktail is a refreshing and revitalizing choice for any night in. The combination of the sweet rum, the tart lime juice, and the simple syrup creates a flavor profile that’s both sweet and sour.

To make a Daiquiri, start by filling a shaker with ice. Add 2 oz of rum, 1 oz of lime juice, and 1/2 oz of simple syrup. Shake the mixture for about 15 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a rocks glass filled with ice, and garnish with a lime wheel.

Gin and Tonic

The Gin and Tonic is a classic cocktail that’s been around since the early 19th century. Made with gin, tonic water, and lime juice, this cocktail is a simple yet elegant choice for any night in. The combination of the herbal gin, the crisp tonic water, and the tart lime juice creates a flavor profile that’s both refreshing and complex.

To make a Gin and Tonic, start by filling a highball glass with ice. Add 1 oz of gin, 4 oz of tonic water, and a squeeze of lime juice. Stir the mixture gently to combine the ingredients, and garnish with a lime wheel.

Whiskey Sour

The Whiskey Sour is a classic cocktail that’s been around since the early 20th century. Made with whiskey, lemon juice, and simple syrup, this cocktail is a tangy and refreshing choice for any night in. The combination of the rich whiskey, the tart lemon juice, and the simple syrup creates a flavor profile that’s both sweet and sour.

To make a Whiskey Sour, start by filling a shaker with ice. Add 2 oz of whiskey, 3/4 oz of lemon juice, and 1/2 oz of simple syrup. Shake the mixture for about 15 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a rocks glass filled with ice, and garnish with a lemon wheel.

Sidecar

The Sidecar is a classic cocktail that’s been around since the early 20th century. Made with cognac, Cointreau, and lemon juice, this cocktail is a sophisticated and elegant choice for any night in. The combination of the rich cognac, the sweet Cointreau, and the tart lemon juice creates a flavor profile that’s both complex and refined.

To make a Sidecar, start by filling a shaker with ice. Add 1 oz of cognac, 1/2 oz of Cointreau, and 1/2 oz of lemon juice. Shake the mixture for about 15 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a coupe glass, and garnish with a lemon wheel.

Negroni

The Negroni is a classic cocktail that’s been around since the early 20th century. Made with gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth, this cocktail is a bitter and complex choice for any night in. The combination of the herbal gin, the bitter Campari, and the sweet vermouth creates a flavor profile that’s both bold and refined.

To make a Negroni, start by filling a mixing glass with ice. Add 1 oz of gin, 1/2 oz of Campari, and 1/2 oz of sweet vermouth. Stir the mixture for about 30 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a rocks glass filled with ice, and garnish with an orange twist.

Mai Tai

The Mai Tai is a classic cocktail that’s been around since the early 20th century. Made with rum, orange curaçao, lime juice, and orgeat syrup, this cocktail is a tropical and refreshing choice for any night in. The combination of the sweet rum, the sweet orange curaçao, the tart lime juice, and the nutty orgeat syrup creates a flavor profile that’s both complex and exotic.

To make a Mai Tai, start by filling a shaker with ice. Add 2 oz of rum, 1/2 oz of orange curaçao, 1 oz of lime juice, and 1/2 oz of orgeat syrup. Shake the mixture for about 15 seconds to combine and chill the ingredients. Strain the cocktail into a rocks glass filled with ice, and garnish with a lime wheel and a sprig of mint.

Conclusion

These classic cocktails are sure to make your next night in a memorable one. Whether you’re a seasoned cocktail enthusiast or just starting to explore the world of mixology, these timeless classics are a great place to start. With their complex flavor profiles and rich histories, they’re sure to impress your friends and family. So the next time you’re looking to cozy up with a good book and a great cocktail, give one of these classics a try. Your taste buds will thank you.
