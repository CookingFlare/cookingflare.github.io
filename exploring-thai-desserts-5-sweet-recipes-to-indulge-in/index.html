Exploring Thai Desserts: 5 Sweet Recipes to Indulge In


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic spices, and fresh ingredients. While savory dishes often steal the spotlight, Thai desserts offer a delightful array of sweet treats that are equally captivating. From creamy coconut puddings to chewy rice cakes, these desserts reflect the rich culinary heritage of Thailand. In this blog post, we will explore five irresistible Thai dessert recipes that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Bangkok and the serene beaches of Phuket. Get ready to indulge in the sweet side of Thailand!

1. Mango Sticky Rice (Khao Niew Mamuang)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • Sesame seeds or mung beans for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results. Drain the rice and steam it in a bamboo or metal steamer lined with cheesecloth for about 30-40 minutes, or until tender.

  2. Make the Coconut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Reserve about ½ cup of the coconut sauce for drizzling later.

  3. Combine Rice and Sauce: Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a mixing bowl. Pour the remaining coconut sauce over the warm rice and mix well. Let it sit for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  4. Serve: To serve, place a portion of the sticky rice on a plate, top with sliced mango, and drizzle with the reserved coconut sauce. Garnish with sesame seeds or mung beans if desired. Enjoy this classic Thai dessert warm or at room temperature!

2. Thai Coconut Pudding (Sangkhaya)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pandan leaves (optional, for flavor)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Mixture: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, sugar, and salt until well combined. Gradually add the coconut milk, cornstarch, and vanilla extract, mixing until smooth. If using pandan leaves, blend them with a little water and strain to extract the juice, then add it to the mixture for a fragrant touch.

  2. Steam the Pudding: Pour the mixture into small cups or ramekins. Prepare a steamer and steam the cups over medium heat for about 20-25 minutes, or until the pudding is set and a toothpick inserted comes out clean.

  3. Cool and Serve: Allow the puddings to cool before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or chilled. For an extra touch, serve with a drizzle of coconut cream on top.

3. Thai Banana Fritters (Kluay Tod)

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas (preferably Thai bananas)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup rice flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Oil for frying
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Batter: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the coconut milk, stirring until you achieve a smooth batter.

  2. Slice the Bananas: Peel the bananas and cut them in half lengthwise or into bite-sized pieces, depending on your preference.

  3. Fry the Fritters: Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Dip each banana piece into the batter, allowing any excess to drip off, and carefully place it in the hot oil. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes on each side. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  4. Serve: Sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired and serve warm. These crispy banana fritters are perfect for snacking or as a sweet treat after a meal.

4. Thai Coconut Ice Cream (I-Tim Kati)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Toasted coconut flakes for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the coconut milk, heavy cream, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt. Whisk until the sugar is fully dissolved.
