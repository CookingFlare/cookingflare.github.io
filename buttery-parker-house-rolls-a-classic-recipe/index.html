Buttery Parker House Rolls: A Classic Recipe


Who can resist the irresistible aroma of freshly baked rolls straight from the oven? Today, we’re diving into the world of Buttery Parker House Rolls—a classic recipe that will transport you to a place of buttery bliss. These rolls are tender, light, and packed with flavor. Get ready to impress your family and friends with this timeless favorite!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Mix well to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  2. In a small saucepan, heat the milk, water, and melted butter over low heat until the mixture reaches a temperature of 110°F (43°C). Be careful not to overheat the mixture, as it can kill the yeast.

  3. Pour the warm milk mixture into the dry ingredients. Add the eggs and mix until a soft, sticky dough forms.

  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, or until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  7. Punch down the risen dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Divide the dough into 12 equal portions and shape each portion into a ball.

  8. Place the balls of dough into the greased baking dish, leaving a small gap between each roll.

  9. Cover the baking dish with the kitchen towel and let the rolls rise for an additional 30 minutes.

  10. Bake the rolls in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they turn golden brown on top.

  11. Remove the rolls from the oven and brush the tops with melted butter for an extra touch of richness and flavor.

  12. Allow the rolls to cool slightly before serving. Serve warm and enjoy the buttery goodness!

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, try adding a sprinkle of garlic powder and grated Parmesan cheese to the melted butter before brushing it on top of the rolls.
  • These Buttery Parker House Rolls are perfect for serving with soups, stews, or as a side dish to your favorite holiday meals.
  • If you prefer a sweeter roll, you can increase the amount of sugar in the dough to your liking.
  • Leftover rolls can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. To reheat, simply place them in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for a few minutes until warmed through.

Now, it’s time to gather your ingredients and embark on a delicious baking adventure. These Buttery Parker House Rolls are sure to become a cherished addition to your recipe collection. Don’t be surprised if they disappear within moments of coming out of the oven—after all, who can resist the allure of warm, buttery goodness? Happy baking!
