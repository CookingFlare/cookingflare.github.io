The Best Wines for Pairing with Savory Pastries


Savory pastries are a delightful treat that brings comfort and sophistication to any dining occasion. From flaky quiches and buttery croissants to hearty empanadas and rich spanakopita, these dishes are versatile enough to be enjoyed at breakfast, brunch, lunch, or dinner. But what elevates these savory delights to new heights? The perfect wine pairing! In this post, we will explore the best wines to complement an array of savory pastries, enhancing both the flavors of the food and your overall dining experience.

Understanding the Basics of Wine Pairing

Before diving into specific pairings, it’s important to grasp the fundamentals of wine pairing. The goal is to create a harmonious relationship between the food and the wine, allowing both to shine. When selecting a wine, consider the following:

  • Flavor Profile: Match the intensity of the wine with the richness of the pastry. Lighter pastries pair well with crisp whites or light reds, while heartier options can stand up to full-bodied wines.
  • Acidity: Wines with higher acidity can cut through rich, buttery flavors, making them ideal companions for creamy or fatty pastries.
  • Tannins: Tannins can provide structure and balance to richer dishes. However, too much tannin can overpower delicate flavors, so choose wisely based on the pastry’s filling.
  • Sweetness: While most savory pastries are not sweet, certain wines with a hint of sweetness can complement spiced or herbaceous flavors beautifully.

With these principles in mind, let’s explore the best wines for pairing with various savory pastries.

1. Quiche and Light Whites

Wine Pairing: Chardonnay or Sauvignon Blanc

Quiche is a classic savory pastry filled with eggs, cream, cheese, and a variety of fillings such as vegetables, meats, or seafood. The richness of quiche calls for a wine that can balance its creamy texture. A lightly oaked Chardonnay offers a buttery mouthfeel that complements the pastry beautifully, while its notes of apple and pear enhance the dish’s flavors.

Sauvignon Blanc, on the other hand, with its zesty acidity and citrus notes, can brighten the rich flavors of a quiche, making it a refreshing choice. Opt for a bottle from the Loire Valley or New Zealand to experience the perfect balance.

2. Spanakopita and Dry Rosé

Wine Pairing: Dry Rosé

Spanakopita, with its flaky layers of phyllo dough and savory spinach and feta filling, pairs wonderfully with a dry rosé. The crispness of the wine cuts through the richness of the cheese and pastry, while its fruity notes of strawberry and watermelon complement the earthy flavors of the spinach. Look for a Provence-style rosé for an elegant and refreshing pairing.

3. Pies and Full-Bodied Reds

Wine Pairing: Merlot or Syrah

Savory pies, such as meat pies or pot pies, are hearty and rich, making them ideal candidates for full-bodied red wines. A smooth Merlot, with its soft tannins and fruity undertones, pairs nicely with a meat pie, enhancing the flavors without overpowering them.

Alternatively, a Syrah offers a bolder option, with its peppery notes and dark fruit flavors, which can stand up to the robust filling of a shepherd’s pie or beef pot pie. Both wines provide a comforting, velvety finish that complements the dish beautifully.

4. Empanadas and Malbec

Wine Pairing: Malbec

Empanadas are a beloved pastry in many cultures, filled with a variety of ingredients such as beef, chicken, or cheese. The savory richness of empanadas pairs excellently with a glass of Malbec. This wine, known for its bold fruit flavors and velvety tannins, complements the spices and savory notes in the filling. A bottle from Argentina, where Malbec is the star varietal, will elevate your empanada experience.

5. Cheese Straws and Sparkling Wine

Wine Pairing: Sparkling Wine or Champagne

Cheese straws are a delightful, savory snack that pairs beautifully with sparkling wine or Champagne. The effervescence of the wine cuts through the richness of the cheese, while its acidity refreshes the palate. A brut Champagne or a dry sparkling wine from regions like Cava or Prosecco will provide a delightful contrast to the salty, cheesy flavors, making it an excellent choice for parties or special occasions.

6. Sausage Rolls and Riesling

Wine Pairing: Off-Dry Riesling

Sausage rolls, with their flaky pastry and savory filling, can be a delightful treat at any gathering. An off-dry Riesling is an unexpected yet fantastic pairing. The slight sweetness of the wine balances the savory spices in the sausage, while its bright acidity cleanses the palate between bites. Look for a Riesling from Germany or the Finger Lakes region of New York for the best results.

7. Tarts and Pinot Noir

Wine Pairing: Pinot Noir

Savory tarts, such as mushroom or roasted vegetable tarts, are best enjoyed with a glass of Pinot Noir. This medium-bodied red wine, known for its elegance and complexity, complements the earthy flavors of mushrooms or roasted vegetables beautifully. The wine’s acidity also provides a lovely contrast to the buttery crust, making each bite more enjoyable.

8. Frittata and Grüner Veltliner

Wine Pairing: Grüner Veltliner

Frittatas are versatile and can be filled with a variety of ingredients, from vegetables to meats and cheeses. A vibrant Grüner Veltliner is an excellent choice for pairing with frittatas. This Austrian white wine has a refreshing acidity and notes of green apple, lime, and white pepper, which enhance the flavors of the dish while providing a refreshing finish.

9. Savory Scones and Chardonnay

Wine Pairing: Chardonnay

Savory scones, whether filled with cheese, herbs, or meats, can be beautifully paired with a glass of Chardonnay. The richness of the scone harmonizes with the buttery notes of an oaked Chardonnay, while the wine’s acidity cuts through the richness, creating a balanced palate experience. Try a Chardonnay from California or Burgundy for the best match.

10. Puff Pastry Bites and Sauvignon Blanc

Wine Pairing: Sauvignon Blanc

Puff pastry bites, filled with a variety of savory ingredients, are a delightful addition to any appetizer spread. A crisp Sauvignon Blanc is an ideal pairing, as its bright acidity and herbaceous notes cut through the buttery, flaky pastry. This combination enhances the flavors of the filling, making for a refreshing and enjoyable bite.

Conclusion

Pairing wine with savory pastries can elevate your dining experience, enhancing the flavors of both the food and the drink. By understanding the fundamentals of wine pairing and exploring the diverse range of wines available, you can create delightful combinations that will impress your guests and satisfy your palate. Whether you choose a light white, a bold red, or a sparkling option, the right wine can transform a simple savory pastry into a gourmet experience. Cheers to delicious pairings!
