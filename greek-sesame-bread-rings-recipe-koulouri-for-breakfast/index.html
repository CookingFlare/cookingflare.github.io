Greek Sesame Bread Rings Recipe (Koulouri) for Breakfast


Greek cuisine is filled with culinary treasures that speak to tradition, history, and the warmth of Mediterranean life. Among the many breakfast delights, Greek sesame bread rings, known as “koulouri,” stand out as one of the most iconic street foods in Greece. Whether enjoyed on a bustling street corner in Athens or baked in your home kitchen, koulouri is a perfect option to start your day. Its irresistible combination of soft and chewy bread coated with nutty sesame seeds makes it a favorite for locals and visitors alike. Today, we’re diving deep into how you can make your own delicious koulouri for breakfast.

What Makes Koulouri Special?

Originating as a portable, on-the-go snack for merchants and travelers since Byzantine times, koulouri has solidified its place in modern Greek breakfasts. These golden-brown bread rings exude simplicity yet reward you with unparalleled texture and flavor. Whether paired with a cup of coffee or yogurt or enjoyed plain, koulouri is a versatile baked good with a flavor profile that’s universally loved. The key is in the sesame seed coating, which provides an added layer of crunch and nutty depth to complement the soft interior.

If you’ve ever walked through Greek streets and inhaled the fresh aroma of koulouri from bakeries and vendors, you know just how captivating this breakfast staple can be. Luckily, you don’t need a plane ticket to indulge in this culinary classic. With a few basic ingredients and steps, you can recreate koulouri right in your kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make authentic koulouri, you’ll need some basic ingredients. These are common pantry staples, and their simplicity underscores the magic of turning a few items into something extraordinary.

For the Dough:

  • 500g (about 3½ cups) all-purpose flour – Provides structure for your bread.
  • 200ml (¾ cup + 2 tbsp) warm water – Crucial for activating the yeast.
  • 1 packet (7g) dry yeast – Helps the bread rise and gives it its airy texture.
  • 1 tbsp sugar – Feeds the yeast and adds a touch of sweetness.
  • 1½ tsp salt – Balances the flavors of the bread.
  • 50ml (3 tbsp) olive oil – Adds richness and a subtle Mediterranean touch.

For the Sesame Coating:

  • 100ml (a little under ½ cup) lukewarm water – Helps the sesame seeds adhere to the dough.
  • 20g (⅓ cup) sesame seeds – The star ingredient that gives koulouri its signature taste and crunch. Choose white sesame seeds for an authentic look.
  • Pinch of salt – Enhances the sesame coating and rounds out the flavor.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making koulouri requires only a few steps, but like any bread recipe, attention to detail goes a long way in ensuring the perfect result. Follow this detailed guide and bring the authentic taste of Greece into your home.

1. Prepare the Dough

Combine warm water, sugar, and yeast in a large mixing bowl. Stir gently and let the mixture sit for 5–10 minutes, or until it becomes frothy. This step ensures your yeast is active and ready.

Next, add the olive oil to the yeast mixture. Gradually incorporate the flour and salt, mixing with a wooden spoon or your hands until a rough dough begins to form. Transfer the dough to a clean surface and knead it for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, sprinkle a little extra flour, but avoid over-doing it, as this can dry out the bread.

Form the kneaded dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm area for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

2. Shape the Koulouri Rings

Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any excess air. Transfer it onto a floured surface and divide it into 8 equal portions. Roll each portion into a long rope, about 12–15 inches in length. Make sure the ropes are uniform, as this ensures your koulouri rings bake evenly.

Form each rope into a circular ring by connecting the ends firmly. You can lightly pinch them together or roll them slightly to ensure they’re securely fastened. Repeat for all portions and place the rings on a parchment-lined baking tray.

3. Coat the Rings with Sesame Seeds

In a shallow dish, mix the lukewarm water with a pinch of salt. Dip each dough ring into the water so it’s fully moistened. Then, roll it in the sesame seeds, ensuring an even coating on all sides. The sesame seeds should stick to the surface relatively easily due to the moisture. For extra flavor, toast your sesame seeds beforehand, though this step is optional.

Place the coated rings back on the baking tray, ensuring adequate space between them to allow for spreading as they bake.

4. Bake to Perfection

Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Once heated, bake the koulouri rings for 15–18 minutes, or until they turn golden brown and release a nutty aroma. Oven temperatures may vary, so keep an eye on them during the last few minutes to avoid overbaking.

Once baked, remove the koulouri from the oven and allow them to cool slightly. They’re best enjoyed warm, though they can be stored in an airtight container for up to 3 days.

Tips for Serving and Storing

Serving Suggestions:

  • Pair your koulouri with a cup of Greek coffee or a freddo cappuccino for a true Mediterranean breakfast experience.
  • Slice open the koulouri and spread some cream cheese, honey, or jam for a sweet and indulgent treat.
  • For a savory twist, stuff the rings with feta cheese, olives, or a drizzle of olive oil mixed with oregano.

Storage Tips:

While koulouri is best eaten fresh, you can store leftovers in a resealable plastic bag or airtight container. If you’d like to enjoy them warm again, simply reheat in a 180°C (350°F) oven for 5 minutes. For longer storage, freeze the koulouri for up to 1 month. Let them thaw at room temperature and reheat to restore freshness.

Why Make Koulouri at Home?

While koulouri is widely available throughout Greece, making them at home allows you to customize flavors, experiment with toppings, and capture the joy of baking. Plus, homemade koulouri ensures freshness and offers a wonderful way to immerse yourself in the rich culinary tradition of Greece.

From the soft interior to the sesame-coated crust, every bite of koulouri carries a story of shared meals, warm mornings, and bustling streets. Whether you’re reminiscing about your travels to Greece or simply exploring new recipes, baking koulouri is a delightful way to experience authentic Mediterranean flavors.

So, roll up your sleeves, heat your oven, and get ready to bring a slice of Greece to your breakfast table. Koulouri isn’t just a bread ring – it’s a cultural celebration baked to perfection.
