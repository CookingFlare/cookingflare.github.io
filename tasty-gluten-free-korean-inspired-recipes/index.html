Tasty Gluten-Free Korean-Inspired Recipes


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique ingredients, making it a favorite among food enthusiasts around the world. If you’re looking to explore the delicious world of Korean cooking while adhering to a gluten-free diet, you’re in for a treat! In this blog post, we’ll share some mouthwatering gluten-free Korean-inspired recipes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds. From savory bulgogi to spicy kimchi, these recipes are not only gluten-free but also bursting with flavor.

Kimchi Cauliflower Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, grated
  • 1 cup of kimchi, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of gluten-free tamari sauce
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the sesame oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and diced onion, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent.
  3. Add the grated cauliflower to the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Stir in the chopped kimchi and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes.
  5. Pour the gluten-free tamari sauce over the mixture and stir well.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds before serving.

This flavorful and satisfying dish is a gluten-free twist on the classic Korean fried rice, using cauliflower as a nutritious alternative to rice.

Spicy Korean BBQ Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs of boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup of gluten-free gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1/4 cup of gluten-free soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the gochujang, gluten-free soy sauce, honey, minced garlic, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and grated ginger to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated, then cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  3. Preheat the grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken cubes onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 6-8 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has a slightly charred exterior.

These spicy Korean BBQ chicken skewers are packed with flavor and are perfect for a gluten-free grilling adventure.

Bibimbap Salad Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup of shredded carrots
  • 1 cup of thinly sliced cucumber
  • 1 cup of baby spinach
  • 1 cup of bean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup of kimchi
  • 4 fried eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of gluten-free gochujang sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Divide the cooked quinoa among four serving bowls.
  2. Arrange the shredded carrots, sliced cucumber, baby spinach, and bean sprouts on top of the quinoa in a visually appealing manner.
  3. Add a spoonful of kimchi to each bowl.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the gluten-free gochujang sauce and sesame oil to create a dressing, then drizzle it over the salad bowls.
  5. Top each bowl with a fried egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.

This bibimbap-inspired salad bowl is a delightful gluten-free twist on the traditional Korean mixed rice dish, offering a vibrant and nutritious meal option.
