The Best Classic Cocktails for a Holiday Celebration


The holidays are a time to come together, share warmth, and toast to another year of cherished memories. Whether it’s an intimate gathering with close family or an extravagant party with friends, the right cocktails can elevate any celebration. Classic holiday cocktails have stood the test of time because of their balance between elegance and flavor—each sip delivering a festive note that embodies the spirit of the season.

In this guide, we’ll explore some of the most beloved classic cocktails that are perfect for a holiday celebration. From rich, warm drinks to sparkling and refreshing options, there’s a cocktail for every palate. We’ll also include tips to ensure your drinks are flawlessly executed. These recipes not only reflect the traditions of holiday festivities but also inspire you to create unforgettable moments with your loved ones.

1. The Timeless Manhattan

The Manhattan is a cocktail synonymous with sophistication. Crafted with a combination of whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters, it’s a drink that remains iconic at holiday parties.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz rye or bourbon whiskey
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Luxardo cherry and orange peel for garnish

Directions:

  1. Fill a mixing glass with ice. Add the whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters.
  2. Stir well for about 30 seconds to chill the mixture.
  3. Strain into a chilled coupe or martini glass.
  4. Garnish with a Luxardo cherry and an optional twist of orange peel for a zesty aroma.

Pro Tip: Opt for rye whiskey for a spicier profile or bourbon if you prefer a smoother, sweeter base.

2. The Festive Old Fashioned

No holiday cocktail list would be complete without the venerable Old Fashioned. With its bold simplicity, this drink is all about the interplay between a good spirit and subtle hints of sweetness and spice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz bourbon or rye whiskey
  • 1 sugar cube (or 1/2 tsp simple syrup)
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Orange peel for garnish

Directions:

  1. Place the sugar cube in a rocks glass and saturate it with bitters. Add a splash of water and muddle until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Fill the glass with a large ice cube and pour the whiskey over it.
  3. Stir gently to combine and chill, then garnish with a twist of orange peel.

Pro Tip: For an extra holiday twist, add a dash of clove or cinnamon bitters to the mix.

3. The Classic Eggnog Cocktail

Eggnog is essentially the culinary embodiment of the holiday season. Turning this creamy, rich treat into a cocktail simply enhances its festive charm.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large egg
  • 1.5 oz dark rum or bourbon
  • 1 oz heavy cream
  • 1 oz whole milk
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • Freshly grated nutmeg for garnish

Directions:

  1. Crack the egg into a shaker and dry shake (without ice) to emulsify.
  2. Add all remaining ingredients except nutmeg along with ice, and shake vigorously.
  3. Strain into a chilled glass.
  4. Garnish with freshly grated nutmeg on top.

Pro Tip: If serving a crowd, batch-brew your eggnog in advance and refrigerate until needed. Add spirits just before serving.

4. The Bright French 75

For those who love a little fizz in their drink, the French 75’s elegant sparkle fits perfectly into holiday celebrations. This cocktail is a masterful fusion of citrus and bubbly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz gin
  • 0.5 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 0.5 oz simple syrup
  • Champagne or sparkling wine
  • Lemon twist for garnish

Directions:

  1. Shake the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup with ice.
  2. Strain into a champagne flute and top with chilled champagne.
  3. Garnish with a lemon twist for added flair and aroma.

Pro Tip: Substituting gin with cognac can create a warm and spicy holiday variation of this classic drink.

5. The Warm and Cozy Hot Toddy

When the snow falls and the temperatures drop, a Hot Toddy is the ultimate winter warmer. This cocktail combines warmth, a touch of sweetness, and the soothing power of whiskey or rum.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz whiskey or spiced rum
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 0.75 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 4 oz boiling water
  • Cinnamon stick and lemon wheel for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a heatproof glass or mug, combine the honey, lemon juice, and whiskey or rum.
  2. Top with boiling water and stir until the honey dissolves.
  3. Garnish with a cinnamon stick and a lemon wheel.

Pro Tip: For a deeply spiced version, add a pinch of cloves or a dash of allspice to the mix.

6. The Timeless Mulled Wine

No winter celebration is complete without the rich aroma of mulled wine wafting through the room. Infused with warm spices and citrus, this classic holiday drink is a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle of red wine (Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon work well)
  • 1/4 cup brandy
  • 1 orange, sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cloves
  • 3 tbsp sugar or honey

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, combine all ingredients. Heat over medium-low, but do not let it boil.
  2. Simmer gently for 20–30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. Strain and serve warm in heatproof glasses or mugs.

Pro Tip: For a creative twist, add some star anise or fresh ginger slices to your mulled wine recipe.

7. The Splendid Negroni

When sophisticated bitterness is on the menu, the Negroni shines. It’s a bold, herbal drink that embodies seasonal festivity while offering unparalleled elegance.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz gin
  • 1 oz Campari
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • Orange slice or peel for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a mixing glass, combine gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth. Add ice and stir well.
  2. Strain into a rocks glass filled with a large ice cube.
  3. Garnish with a slice of orange or a twist of peel.

Pro Tip: Swap gin for bourbon to make a Boulevardier, another excellent holiday cocktail.

8. The Decadent Brandy Alexander

Creamy and dreamy, the Brandy Alexander is a dessert in a glass. Its rich flavors make it the perfect treat for cozy evenings by the fireplace.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz brandy
  • 1 oz dark crème de cacao
  • 1 oz heavy cream
  • Nutmeg for garnish

Directions:

  1. Combine brandy, crème de cacao, and heavy cream in a shaker with ice.
  2. Shake well until chilled and strain into a coupe glass.
  3. Garnish with a light dusting of nutmeg.

Pro Tip: Chill your glass beforehand to keep the drink delightfully frosty.

Adding Flair to Holiday Cocktails

No matter which classic cocktail you choose, presentation is key. Here are a few tips to add flair:

  • Garnishes: Holiday-inspired garnishes, such as sugared cranberries, rosemary sprigs, or cinnamon sticks, can elevate any cocktail.
  • Glassware: Elegant, seasonal glassware adds to the charm. Stemmed glasses for sparkling options and rustic mugs for warm cocktails set the tone beautifully.
  • Seasonal Syrups: Homemade syrups like cranberry, rosemary, or spiced cinnamon can be integrated into some recipes for a unique twist.

As you craft these classic cocktails, remember that the greatest ingredient is the love and joy you pour into making them for your guests. So set up your bar cart, light the candles, play some festive music, and raise a glass to the magic of the season.

Cheers to an unforgettable holiday celebration!
