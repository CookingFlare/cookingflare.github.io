Instant Pot Beef Wellington: A Show-Stopping Recipe


Beef Wellington is one of those dishes that turns any meal into a special occasion. The tender, flavorful beef wrapped in a golden, flaky puff pastry, combined with a savory mushroom duxelles and prosciutto, makes for a truly indulgent meal. Traditionally a labor-intensive dish, Beef Wellington is often reserved for holidays or special celebrations. However, with the help of the Instant Pot, you can create this show-stopping dish much more efficiently and without sacrificing the rich flavors that make Beef Wellington a classic.

In this blog post, we’ll walk you through how to prepare Instant Pot Beef Wellington, from selecting the right cut of beef to mastering the steps of assembling this magnificent dish. Whether you’re an experienced cook or a beginner, this recipe will allow you to bring the elegance of Beef Wellington to your dinner table in a fraction of the time.

Why Use the Instant Pot for Beef Wellington?

Traditional Beef Wellington recipes often involve multiple steps, including searing the beef, preparing the mushroom duxelles, wrapping the beef, and baking it to perfection. While this method produces fantastic results, it can also be time-consuming and complex.

The Instant Pot revolutionizes this dish by speeding up the cooking process without compromising flavor. With the pressure cooking function of the Instant Pot, the beef will cook perfectly and stay juicy while the rest of the dish comes together with ease. Plus, using the Instant Pot reduces the time spent at the stove, allowing you to focus on preparing other components of the meal, such as side dishes or a dessert to complement the Wellington.

Instant Pot Beef Wellington: What You Need

Before jumping into the recipe, let’s go over the ingredients and equipment you’ll need to prepare your Instant Pot Beef Wellington.

Ingredients:

For the Beef:

  • 2-pound center-cut beef tenderloin (also known as filet mignon)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for searing)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Duxelles:

  • 8 oz cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons dry white wine (optional, but recommended)
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Prosciutto Layer:

  • 8-10 slices of prosciutto

For the Puff Pastry:

  • 1 package of puff pastry (enough to wrap the beef)
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Equipment:

  • Instant Pot (6-quart or larger)
  • Baking sheet (for finishing in the oven)
  • Parchment paper
  • Rolling pin

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Instant Pot Beef Wellington

Now that we have everything ready, let’s dive into the steps for preparing this delicious Instant Pot Beef Wellington.

1. Searing the Beef in the Instant Pot

The first step in making Beef Wellington is searing the beef tenderloin. The Instant Pot’s sauté function is perfect for this step, as it allows you to quickly and evenly sear the beef.

  • Turn the Instant Pot to the Sauté mode and let it heat up for about 5 minutes.
  • Add 2 tablespoons of olive oil to the pot. Once the oil is hot, season the beef tenderloin generously with salt and pepper.
  • Place the beef in the Instant Pot and sear each side for 2-3 minutes, ensuring that all surfaces are browned and caramelized. This step locks in the juices and enhances the flavor of the beef.
  • Once seared, remove the beef from the Instant Pot and set it aside to cool.

2. Preparing the Mushroom Duxelles

Mushroom duxelles is a mixture of finely chopped mushrooms, onions, garlic, and herbs. It adds a rich, savory flavor to the Beef Wellington and helps keep the beef moist.

  • In the same Instant Pot, add 2 tablespoons of butter and turn the pot back to the Sauté function.
  • Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot. Sauté for about 3 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent.
  • Add the chopped mushrooms to the pot. Stir well and cook for 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms release their moisture and begin to brown.
  • Pour in the white wine (if using) and stir to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. This will add flavor to the duxelles.
  • Stir in the thyme and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the mixture is thickened and most of the liquid has evaporated.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste, then transfer the duxelles mixture to a bowl and let it cool.

3. Wrapping the Beef with Prosciutto

Once the beef is seared and the duxelles mixture has cooled, it’s time to wrap the beef with prosciutto.

  • Lay out a sheet of parchment paper on a clean surface. Lay the slices of prosciutto on the parchment paper, slightly overlapping each slice to form a rectangular layer.
  • Spread the cooled mushroom duxelles evenly over the prosciutto.
  • Place the seared beef tenderloin on top of the duxelles. Roll the prosciutto and duxelles tightly around the beef, ensuring that it is well-wrapped.
  • Once wrapped, use the parchment paper to tightly roll the beef log, making sure it holds its shape. Chill the wrapped beef in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to help it firm up.

4. Wrapping in Puff Pastry

After the beef has chilled, it’s time to wrap it in puff pastry. This will create the beautiful, golden crust that defines Beef Wellington.

  • Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface. Ensure it is large enough to completely encase the beef.
  • Unwrap the chilled beef from the parchment paper and place it in the center of the puff pastry.
  • Fold the edges of the puff pastry over the beef, sealing it tightly. Trim any excess pastry, then pinch the edges to seal.
  • Brush the top of the pastry with a beaten egg to create a golden, glossy finish when baked.

5. Pressure Cooking the Beef Wellington

Here’s where the Instant Pot truly shines. Instead of baking the Beef Wellington traditionally in the oven, we’re going to use the Instant Pot to cook the beef while still achieving that crispy pastry exterior.

  • Place a trivet in the Instant Pot and add 1 cup of water to the bottom of the pot.
  • Set the wrapped Beef Wellington on the trivet, ensuring it’s not submerged in water.
  • Close the lid of the Instant Pot and ensure the valve is set to Sealing.
  • Cook on Manual mode (or Pressure Cook) for 10 minutes. This will give the beef enough time to cook to medium-rare (adjust the time for a different level of doneness). Keep in mind that the puff pastry won’t be golden brown at this point, but that’s okay — we’re going to finish it in the oven.

6. Finishing in the Oven

Once the Beef Wellington is finished cooking in the Instant Pot, carefully remove it from the pot. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  • Place the Beef Wellington on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  • Bake it in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and crispy.
  • Allow the Beef Wellington to rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing. This helps the juices redistribute and prevents the filling from spilling out.

7. Serving Your Instant Pot Beef Wellington

Once your Beef Wellington has rested, it’s time to carve and serve this show-stopping dish. Slice the Wellington into thick, even slices, making sure each portion contains the beautiful layers of prosciutto, mushroom duxelles, and tender beef.

This Instant Pot Beef Wellington pairs wonderfully with roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a light salad. You can even drizzle a rich red wine sauce or a balsamic glaze over the top for an extra touch of sophistication.

Tips for Perfecting Your Instant Pot Beef Wellington

  • Choose the Right Cut of Beef: Beef tenderloin is the best choice for Beef Wellington due to its tenderness and ability to cook quickly. If you can’t find tenderloin, you can substitute with another cut, but keep in mind that the texture and flavor may vary.
  • Ensure the Duxelles is Dry: It’s important that the mushroom duxelles is not too wet, as excess moisture can make the pastry soggy. Be sure to cook the mixture until all the liquid has evaporated.
  • Chill Before Baking: After wrapping the beef in prosciutto and pastry, chilling it is key. This helps the Wellington hold its shape and ensures a clean, neat cut when you serve it.
  • Adjust Cooking Time: Depending on the size of your beef tenderloin and your desired doneness, you may need to adjust the pressure cooking time. For medium-rare, 10 minutes is usually perfect, but for more well-done beef, increase the time slightly.

Conclusion

Instant Pot Beef Wellington is a perfect way to impress your guests or elevate any special occasion. With this method, you can achieve the same delicious, tender beef and flaky pastry without spending hours in the kitchen. The Instant Pot helps streamline the cooking process, allowing you to focus on other aspects of your meal while still delivering a restaurant-quality dish. Try this recipe the next time you want to serve something truly extraordinary!
