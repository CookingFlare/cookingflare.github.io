Christmas Breakfast Casseroles: Easy Recipes for a Crowd


The holiday season is upon us, and with it comes the joy of gathering family and friends around the table to celebrate. One of the best parts of Christmas morning is sharing a delicious breakfast that is both hearty and satisfying, allowing everyone to start the day on a festive note. This is where Christmas breakfast casseroles come into play. They offer a fantastic way to feed a crowd with minimal fuss, leaving you free to enjoy the company of your loved ones.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of easy Christmas breakfast casserole recipes that are perfect for feeding a group. From savory to sweet, vegetarian to meaty, we have something for everyone. So roll up your sleeves, and let’s get cooking!

The Appeal of Breakfast Casseroles

Breakfast casseroles are a beloved holiday tradition for many reasons. They are:

  • Convenient: You can prepare them the night before and simply pop them in the oven in the morning.
  • Versatile: You can customize them based on dietary preferences and what you have on hand.
  • Crowd-pleasers: They are filling and can cater to various palates, making them perfect for family gatherings.

Tips for Making the Perfect Breakfast Casserole

Before diving into our delicious recipes, here are some essential tips to ensure your breakfast casseroles turn out perfectly:

  1. Choose the Right Bread: The base of most casseroles is bread. Stale or day-old bread works best as it absorbs the egg mixture without becoming soggy. Consider using brioche, sourdough, or croissants for a richer flavor.

  2. Don’t Skimp on Eggs: Eggs are the binding agent in breakfast casseroles. Ensure you have enough to provide structure and creaminess. A good rule of thumb is to use about 6-8 eggs for a standard 9x13-inch casserole.

  3. Flavor it Up: Add herbs, spices, and various cheese types to elevate the flavor profile. Fresh herbs like parsley, chives, or thyme can add a wonderful freshness.

  4. Add Some Texture: Consider adding crunchy toppings like breadcrumbs, crushed cornflakes, or even nuts for a delightful contrast to the creamy eggs.

  5. Let it Rest: Allow your casserole to rest for at least 30 minutes before baking. This gives the bread time to soak up the egg mixture fully.

Classic Sausage and Cheese Breakfast Casserole

This classic sausage and cheese breakfast casserole is sure to be a hit at your Christmas gathering. It’s hearty, flavorful, and incredibly easy to prepare.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 6 cups cubed stale bread
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess grease.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  4. In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, layer the cubed bread, cooked sausage, and shredded cheese. Pour the egg mixture over the top, ensuring everything is evenly coated. If desired, sprinkle with green onions.
  5. Cover the dish with foil and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  6. Bake, covered, for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden and the casserole is set.
  7. Let cool for a few minutes before serving.

Veggie Lovers’ Christmas Casserole

For those who prefer a meatless option, this veggie lovers’ casserole is packed with nutrients and flavor. It’s a colorful dish that will brighten up your holiday breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1 cup chopped spinach
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 6 cups cubed stale bread

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add bell peppers, onion, mushrooms, and spinach. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and paprika.
  4. In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, layer the cubed bread, sautéed vegetables, and crumbled feta. Pour the egg mixture over the top.
  5. Cover with foil and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  6. Bake, covered, for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until the top is golden and set.
  7. Allow to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Sweet Cinnamon Roll Casserole

If you have a sweet tooth, this cinnamon roll casserole will surely delight your taste buds. It combines the flavors of classic cinnamon rolls into a delightful breakfast dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans of refrigerated cinnamon rolls (with icing)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Cut the cinnamon rolls into quarters and place them in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon. Pour this mixture over the cinnamon rolls.
  4. Sprinkle chopped pecans on top if using.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the casserole is golden and set.
  6. Remove from the oven and drizzle the icing over the top before serving.

Bacon and Spinach Breakfast Casserole

This bacon and spinach breakfast casserole is perfect for those who enjoy a savory start to their day. The combination of crispy bacon and fresh spinach makes for a delightful dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound bacon, chopped
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 6 cups cubed stale bread
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Drain excess grease.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes.
  4. In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, layer the cubed bread, cooked bacon, chopped spinach, and mozzarella cheese. Pour the egg mixture over the top.
  5. Cover with foil and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  6. Bake, covered, for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, until golden and set.
  7. Let cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Crème Brûlée French Toast Casserole

For something truly indulgent, this crème brûlée French toast casserole is a showstopper. It’s creamy, sweet, and perfect for a festive brunch.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of challah or brioche bread, cut into cubes
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (for topping)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, milk, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and salt.
  3. In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, layer the bread cubes. Pour the egg mixture over the bread, ensuring all pieces are soaked.
  4. Cover with foil and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  5. Bake, covered, for 30 minutes. Remove the foil, sprinkle brown sugar on top, and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until golden.
  6. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips for Serving Your Breakfast Casseroles

Once your delicious casseroles are ready, consider the following tips for serving:

  • Garnish: Fresh herbs, whipped cream, or a dusting of powdered sugar can add a festive touch.
  • Accompaniments: Serve with fresh fruit, yogurt, or a selection of pastries for a well-rounded breakfast spread.
  • Beverages: Complement your breakfast with coffee, tea, or a festive holiday punch.

Conclusion

Christmas breakfast casseroles are the perfect way to bring everyone together around the table. With these easy recipes, you can feed a crowd and create lasting memories with your loved ones. Whether you opt for savory or sweet, these casseroles will surely add a touch of joy to your holiday celebrations. So gather your ingredients, invite your friends and family, and enjoy a delightful Christmas breakfast together! Happy cooking and Merry Christmas!
