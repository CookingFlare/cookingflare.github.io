Authentic Middle Eastern Falafel: Crispy and Flavorful


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve likely indulged in the delightful flavors of falafel. These crispy, flavorful, and aromatic chickpea fritters are a staple in Middle Eastern street food and have gained popularity worldwide for their irresistible taste and versatility. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of making authentic Middle Eastern falafel that’s crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and bursting with traditional Middle Eastern spices.

Ingredients

For the Falafel:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 3-4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For Serving:

  • Pita bread
  • Hummus
  • Tahini sauce
  • Chopped tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Pickled turnips
  • Chopped lettuce

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas: Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover them with water. Let them soak overnight. The next day, drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly.

  2. Blend the Ingredients: In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, salt, cumin, coriander, black pepper, cayenne pepper, and baking soda. Pulse the mixture until it forms a coarse paste. Be careful not to over-process the mixture, as you want to retain some texture.

  3. Chill the Mixture: Transfer the falafel mixture to a bowl, cover it, and refrigerate for 1-2 hours. Chilling the mixture will make it easier to form into balls and will help the falafel hold its shape during frying.

  4. Form the Falafel: After chilling, take small portions of the mixture and form them into small balls or patties, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. If the mixture feels too wet, add 3-4 tablespoons of all-purpose flour to help bind the falafel.

  5. Fry the Falafel: In a deep, heavy-bottomed pot, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). Carefully drop the falafel balls into the hot oil and fry in batches for 3-4 minutes, or until they are deep golden brown and crispy on the outside. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the falafel to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once all the falafel are fried, it’s time to assemble your meal. Warm the pita bread, spread a generous amount of hummus and tahini sauce, and stuff it with the crispy falafel, chopped tomatoes, sliced cucumbers, pickled turnips, and lettuce.

Tips for Perfect Falafel

  • Use dried chickpeas: Using dried chickpeas instead of canned chickpeas is essential for achieving the right texture and consistency in falafel.
  • Don’t skip the chilling: Allowing the falafel mixture to chill in the refrigerator helps it hold together and prevents it from falling apart during frying.
  • Test the oil temperature: It’s crucial to ensure that the oil is at the right temperature before frying the falafel. If the oil is too hot, the falafel will burn on the outside before cooking through, and if it’s too cold, the falafel will absorb excess oil and become greasy.

Final Thoughts

Mastering the art of making authentic Middle Eastern falafel at home is a rewarding experience that will transport you to the bustling streets of the Middle East with every bite. Whether you’re serving it as a main course, stuffing it into a sandwich, or enjoying it as a snack, this crispy and flavorful falafel is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving more. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create these irresistible nuggets of Middle Eastern goodness right in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
