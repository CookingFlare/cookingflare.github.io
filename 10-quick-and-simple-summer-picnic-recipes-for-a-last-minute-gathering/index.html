10 Quick and Simple Summer Picnic Recipes for a Last-Minute Gathering


Summer is the perfect time for picnics, especially when the sun is shining and the weather is warm. Whether you’re heading to the park, beach, or your backyard, a picnic can be a delightful way to enjoy a meal outdoors. But what happens when you find out about a last-minute gathering? Don’t worry! With these 10 quick and simple summer picnic recipes, you can whip up something delicious in no time. Each recipe is easy to prepare, bursting with fresh flavors, and sure to impress your friends and family.

1. Classic Chicken Salad Wraps

Chicken salad is a timeless picnic dish that everyone loves, and it’s incredibly easy to make. To prepare a classic chicken salad wrap, you’ll need some cooked chicken (rotisserie chicken works great for a quick option), mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, celery, red onions, and a bit of salt and pepper. Mix everything together and spread the mixture on large tortilla wraps. Add some fresh lettuce or spinach, and you’ve got a meal that’s not only filling but also refreshing on a hot summer day. These wraps are easy to pack and serve, making them ideal for a picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped celery
  • 1/4 cup red onions, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • Fresh lettuce or spinach (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, chopped celery, and red onions.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Lay out the tortillas and spread a generous amount of chicken salad on each one.
  4. Top with lettuce or spinach if desired, then roll them up tightly.
  5. Slice the wraps in half and pack them for your picnic.

2. Caprese Skewers

For a light and refreshing option, Caprese skewers are perfect for any picnic. These bite-sized appetizers combine the sweetness of cherry tomatoes, the creaminess of fresh mozzarella, and the freshness of basil leaves. Drizzle them with balsamic glaze for extra flavor, and you’ve got a no-cook dish that’s elegant yet simple to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Thread a cherry tomato, a mozzarella ball, and a basil leaf onto each skewer.
  2. Repeat the process until all ingredients are used.
  3. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and sprinkle with a little salt and pepper before serving.

3. Pasta Salad with Lemon and Parmesan

Pasta salad is a versatile dish that you can customize according to your preferences. This version with lemon and Parmesan is light and refreshing, perfect for a summer gathering. The tanginess of the lemon pairs wonderfully with the nutty, salty Parmesan, creating a simple yet flavorful salad. You can even add in some vegetables like cucumbers, tomatoes, or olives to enhance the flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz pasta (penne, fusilli, or farfalle)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • Cherry tomatoes or olives (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the cooked pasta with olive oil, lemon juice, grated Parmesan, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add optional ingredients like cherry tomatoes, olives, or fresh parsley for extra flavor.
  4. Chill in the fridge for 30 minutes before serving.

4. Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

A fruit salad is a must-have at any picnic, and this one comes with a twist. A honey-lime dressing adds a burst of sweetness and tang that elevates the natural flavors of the fresh fruit. Use whatever seasonal fruits you have on hand, such as watermelon, berries, peaches, or grapes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups watermelon, cubed
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup pineapple, cubed
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Combine the cubed watermelon, strawberries, blueberries, and pineapple in a large bowl.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey and lime juice until smooth.
  3. Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the fruit and toss gently to coat.
  4. Chill the fruit salad before serving for a refreshing treat.

5. Veggie and Hummus Pita Pockets

For a vegetarian option that’s satisfying and healthy, try these veggie and hummus pita pockets. You can fill whole-wheat pita pockets with your favorite veggies—such as cucumbers, bell peppers, and tomatoes—and a generous scoop of hummus. Add a dash of olive oil and a sprinkle of feta for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 whole-wheat pita pockets
  • 1 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese (optional)
  • Olive oil (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Slice the pita pockets in half to form pockets.
  2. Spread a generous amount of hummus inside each pita half.
  3. Fill with cucumber, bell pepper, and tomato slices.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with feta cheese if desired.
  5. Serve immediately or pack for later.

6. Grilled Shrimp Skewers

If you have access to a grill, grilled shrimp skewers are a fantastic and flavorful option for your picnic. The shrimp cook quickly and can be seasoned with a variety of spices and herbs, making them a versatile dish. You can serve these skewers as a main course or as a protein-packed appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lemon wedges (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, toss the shrimp with olive oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Thread the shrimp onto skewers and grill for 2-3 minutes on each side or until pink and opaque.
  4. Serve with fresh lemon wedges for an added zesty touch.

7. Mini Sandwiches with Deli Meat and Cheese

Mini sandwiches are an easy and crowd-pleasing option for a picnic. You can use small dinner rolls or sandwich bread and stuff them with deli meats like turkey, ham, or chicken. Add cheese, lettuce, and a bit of mustard or mayo, and you’ve got the perfect bite-sized picnic treat.

Ingredients:

  • 12 mini sandwich rolls
  • 1 lb deli meat (turkey, ham, or chicken)
  • 6 slices cheese (Swiss, cheddar, or provolone)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Mustard or mayonnaise (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cut the mini sandwich rolls in half.
  2. Layer each sandwich with a slice of deli meat, cheese, and lettuce.
  3. Add mustard or mayonnaise if desired.
  4. Close the sandwiches and pack them for your picnic.

8. No-Bake Cheesecake Jars

For a sweet treat that’s simple and impressive, no-bake cheesecake jars are a fun and creative dessert option. Using graham cracker crumbs for the crust and a cream cheese filling, you can layer everything in small mason jars for easy serving and transport. Top with fresh berries for a refreshing finish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter.
  2. Press the mixture into the bottom of mason jars to form the crust.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the cream cheese with powdered sugar and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Spoon the cream cheese filling over the graham cracker crust in each jar.
  5. Top with fresh berries and chill for at least 2 hours before serving.

9. Guacamole and Chips

You can’t go wrong with a classic guacamole and chips combination. Guacamole is a crowd favorite, and it’s so easy to prepare. Mash ripe avocados and mix with diced onions, tomatoes, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice for a refreshing dip. Serve with tortilla chips or sliced veggies for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 2 tbsp cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortilla chips or sliced veggies for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Cut the avocados in half and remove the pit. Scoop out the flesh into a bowl.
  2. Mash the avocado with a fork until smooth.
  3. Stir in the chopped onion, tomato, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Serve with tortilla chips or sliced veggies.

10. Lemonade Iced Tea

To wash down all these delicious picnic treats, a refreshing beverage is a must. Lemonade iced tea is a perfect summer drink that combines the sweetness of lemonade with the boldness of iced tea. It’s easy to prepare and can be made ahead of time.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups brewed black tea, cooled
  • 2 cups lemonade
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Combine the cooled black tea and lemonade in a large pitcher.
  2. Add ice cubes and stir to chill.
  3. Garnish with lemon slices and serve chilled.

With these 10 quick and simple summer picnic recipes, you’re ready for any last-minute gathering. Whether you’re packing wraps, skewers, or sweet treats, these dishes are sure to delight your guests and make your picnic a memorable one. So, next time you find yourself scrambling for a picnic menu, just reach for these easy-to-make options and enjoy the sunshine with great food and company!
