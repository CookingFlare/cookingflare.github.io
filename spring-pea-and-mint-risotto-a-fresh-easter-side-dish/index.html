Spring Pea and Mint Risotto: A Fresh Easter Side Dish


Risotto is a classic Italian dish that never fails to impress. With its creamy texture and rich flavors, it’s the perfect canvas for incorporating seasonal ingredients. And what better way to celebrate the arrival of spring than with a vibrant and refreshing Spring Pea and Mint Risotto? This dish is not only delicious, but it also adds a burst of color to your Easter table. So, let’s dive right in and discover how to create this delightful side dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • ½ cup white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas
  • ¼ cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  2. Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir to coat it with the oil. Toast the rice for a minute or two, stirring constantly, until it becomes slightly translucent around the edges.

  3. Pour in the white wine and stir until it is absorbed by the rice. This step helps to enhance the flavor of the risotto.

  4. Begin adding the warmed vegetable broth to the pan, one ladle at a time. Stir the rice constantly and allow the broth to be absorbed before adding the next ladle. Repeat this process until the rice is cooked al dente. This will take approximately 20-25 minutes.

  5. Meanwhile, blanch the peas in boiling water for about 2 minutes, then drain and set aside.

  6. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the blanched peas, chopped mint leaves, and grated Parmesan cheese. The peas will add a fresh burst of sweetness, while the mint will lend a vibrant and aromatic flavor to the risotto.

  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to taste the risotto along the way and adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  8. Remove the risotto from the heat and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the risotto to achieve its signature creamy consistency.

  9. Serve the Spring Pea and Mint Risotto hot, garnished with a few sprigs of fresh mint for an extra touch of freshness. Pair it with roasted lamb or grilled chicken for a complete Easter meal.

Final Thoughts

Spring Pea and Mint Risotto is a delightful side dish that captures the essence of the season. The vibrant green color and the refreshing flavors make it a perfect addition to your Easter celebration. The creaminess of the risotto, combined with the sweetness of the peas and the aromatic mint, creates a symphony of flavors that will impress your guests.

Don’t be intimidated by the process of making risotto. It’s all about patience and constant stirring. Trust me, the end result is well worth the effort. So, this Easter, surprise your loved ones with a dish that embodies the freshness of spring. Enjoy!
