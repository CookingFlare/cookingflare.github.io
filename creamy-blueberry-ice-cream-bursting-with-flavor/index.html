Creamy Blueberry Ice Cream: Bursting with Flavor


There’s nothing quite like the taste of homemade ice cream on a hot summer day. And when it comes to flavors that scream summer, blueberries take the cake. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a lusciously creamy blueberry ice cream that is bursting with flavor. Get ready to indulge your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Start by rinsing the blueberries under running water and patting them dry with a paper towel. Set aside a handful of blueberries for garnish later.

  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the remaining blueberries and sugar. Mash the blueberries slightly with a fork to release their juice. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook the blueberries until they become soft and release their juices, about 5 minutes.

  3. Once the blueberries have cooked down, remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mixture cool for a few minutes. Transfer the blueberry mixture to a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Set aside.

  4. In a separate saucepan, heat the milk and cream over medium-low heat until it begins to steam. Be careful not to let it boil. Remove from heat and set aside.

  5. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sugar, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined. Slowly pour the warm milk and cream mixture into the sugar mixture, whisking constantly until the sugar has dissolved completely.

  6. Add the blueberry puree to the milk and cream mixture and stir until well incorporated. The mixture should be a beautiful shade of purple and smell absolutely divine!

  7. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. Chilling the mixture thoroughly will ensure a smoother and creamier ice cream.

  8. Once the mixture is chilled, pour it into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes, but be sure to check the instructions for your specific machine.

  9. While the ice cream is churning, take the handful of reserved blueberries and give them a light dusting of sugar. Toss gently to coat.

  10. When the ice cream has reached a soft-serve consistency, transfer it to a lidded container, gently folding in the sugared blueberries as you go. This will create little pockets of fruity goodness in every bite.

  11. Place the container in the freezer for at least 2 hours, or until the ice cream has firmed up to your desired consistency. If you can resist the temptation, it’s best to let it freeze overnight for maximum creaminess.

  12. When you’re ready to serve, scoop the creamy blueberry ice cream into bowls or cones and garnish with a few fresh blueberries. Take a moment to admire the vibrant purple hue before diving in and savoring each spoonful.

Conclusion

Creamy blueberry ice cream is the perfect summer treat that combines the refreshing taste of blueberries with the indulgence of homemade ice cream. The creamy texture and burst of flavor will have you coming back for seconds, so make sure to prepare a double batch! Whether enjoyed on a lazy afternoon or shared with friends at a backyard barbecue, this recipe is sure to be a hit. So grab those fresh blueberries and get ready to indulge in a scoop of pure bliss!
