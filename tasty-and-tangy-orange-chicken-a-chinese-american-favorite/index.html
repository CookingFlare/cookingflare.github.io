Tasty and Tangy Orange Chicken: A Chinese-American Favorite


Orange chicken is a classic Chinese-American dish that has become a favorite in households across the globe. The combination of crispy, tender chicken pieces coated in a sweet and tangy orange sauce is simply irresistible. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to create your own restaurant-quality orange chicken at home. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds with this delightful dish!

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup of cornstarch
  • 2 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Orange Sauce:

  • 1 ½ cups of orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon of orange zest
  • ½ cup of brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup of rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • Green onions, thinly sliced for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken: In a bowl, whisk the eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper. Dip the chicken pieces into the egg mixture, then coat them evenly with cornstarch. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and cooked through. Place the fried chicken on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  2. Make the Orange Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the orange juice, orange zest, brown sugar, rice vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and ginger. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry. Slowly pour the slurry into the simmering sauce while whisking continuously. Allow the sauce to thicken, then remove it from the heat.

  3. Coat the Chicken: In a large bowl, toss the fried chicken pieces with the prepared orange sauce until they are evenly coated.

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Garnish the orange chicken with thinly sliced green onions for a pop of color and freshness. Serve the orange chicken over steamed rice or with a side of stir-fried vegetables for a complete meal.

Tips for Success

  • To achieve a crispy and light coating on the chicken, make sure the oil is hot enough before frying the chicken pieces.
  • For a healthier alternative, you can bake the coated chicken in the oven instead of frying it. Simply preheat the oven to 400°F, place the coated chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and bake for 20-25 minutes until crispy and golden.
  • Adjust the sweetness and tanginess of the orange sauce to suit your taste preferences. You can add more brown sugar for a sweeter sauce or increase the amount of rice vinegar for a tangier flavor.

Final Thoughts

Tasty and tangy orange chicken is a delightful dish that brings together the vibrant flavors of Chinese and American cuisines. With this homemade recipe, you can create a restaurant-worthy orange chicken that will impress your family and friends. Whether it’s for a special occasion or a casual weeknight dinner, this flavorful dish is sure to be a hit at the dining table. Get ready to savor every bite of this Chinese-American favorite!
