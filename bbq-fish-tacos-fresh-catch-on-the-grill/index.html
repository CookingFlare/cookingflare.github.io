BBQ Fish Tacos: Fresh Catch on the Grill


If you’re a seafood lover looking for a fresh and flavorful dish that captures the essence of summer, BBQ fish tacos are a must-try! This recipe combines the smoky goodness of the grill with the light and vibrant flavors of fish and fresh ingredients. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or simply craving a taste of coastal cuisine, these BBQ fish tacos will surely impress your taste buds and leave you wanting more.

Ingredients

For the Fish:

  • 1 pound of firm white fish fillets (such as mahi-mahi or snapper)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Marinade:

  • 1/4 cup of fresh lime juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Tacos:

  • 8 small corn or flour tortillas
  • 1 cup of shredded green cabbage
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of chopped cilantro
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, and lightly oil the grates to prevent the fish from sticking.

  2. Marinate the Fish: In a shallow dish, whisk together the olive oil, lime juice, minced garlic, cumin, chili powder, salt, and black pepper. Place the fish fillets in the marinade, turning to coat evenly. Allow the fish to marinate for 15-20 minutes while you prepare the grill.

  3. Season the Fish: In a small bowl, combine the smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Remove the fish from the marinade and sprinkle the seasoning mixture evenly on both sides of the fillets.

  4. Grill the Fish: Place the seasoned fish fillets on the preheated grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the fish is opaque and easily flakes with a fork. Remove the fish from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before flaking it into bite-sized pieces.

  5. Warm the Tortillas: While the fish is resting, warm the tortillas on the grill for about 30 seconds on each side, until they are soft and lightly charred.

  6. Assemble the Tacos: To assemble the tacos, divide the flaked fish evenly among the warm tortillas. Top each taco with shredded cabbage, sliced avocado, diced tomatoes, and a sprinkle of chopped cilantro. Serve the tacos with lime wedges on the side for a burst of citrus flavor.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Arrange the BBQ fish tacos on a platter and serve them immediately, allowing everyone to customize their tacos with their favorite toppings and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

Tips for Perfect BBQ Fish Tacos

  • Choose the Right Fish: Opt for firm white fish that holds up well on the grill, such as mahi-mahi, snapper, or cod. These varieties have a mild flavor that pairs beautifully with the smokiness of the grill.

  • Grill Carefully: Fish cooks quickly, so keep a close eye on it to prevent overcooking. Use a fish basket or a sheet of aluminum foil with small holes to grill the fish more easily.

  • Get Creative with Toppings: Feel free to customize your BBQ fish tacos with additional toppings such as salsa, pickled onions, or a drizzle of creamy cilantro-lime sauce for a burst of flavor and texture.

  • Serve with Sides: Round out your BBQ fish taco meal with a side of Mexican street corn, black bean and corn salad, or a refreshing watermelon and cucumber salad for a complete summertime feast.

Embrace the Flavors of Summer

BBQ fish tacos are a celebration of the bounty of the sea and the joy of outdoor grilling. With the smoky aroma of the grill and the zesty freshness of the toppings, these tacos are a delightful way to savor the flavors of summer. So fire up the grill, gather your favorite people, and indulge in the simple pleasure of enjoying a delicious meal together. Whether it’s a laid-back weekend cookout or a festive gathering, these BBQ fish tacos are sure to become a beloved addition to your summer recipe repertoire.
