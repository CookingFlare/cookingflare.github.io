Latvian Grey Pea Soup and More


If you’re looking for a comforting and hearty soup that will warm your soul, look no further than Latvian Grey Pea Soup. This traditional dish is a staple in Latvian cuisine and is loved for its rich flavors and nourishing qualities. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history and preparation of Latvian Grey Pea Soup, as well as share some additional Latvian recipes that you can try in your own kitchen.

Latvian Grey Pea Soup

Introduction

Latvian Grey Pea Soup, also known as Pelēkie zirņi, is a dish that has been enjoyed by generations of Latvians. It is typically made with dried grey peas, smoked bacon, onions, and a medley of herbs and spices. The long cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a thick and creamy soup that is perfect for cold winter days or anytime you crave something comforting.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make Latvian Grey Pea Soup:

  • 1 cup dried grey peas
  • 4 slices of smoked bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the dried grey peas under cold water and soak them overnight in a large bowl of water. This will help soften the peas and reduce the cooking time.

  2. In a large pot, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until it becomes crispy and renders its fat. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside for later use.

  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onions and diced carrots. Sauté them until they become soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.

  4. Drain the soaked peas and add them to the pot along with the minced garlic, bay leaf, and dried thyme. Pour in enough water to cover the peas by about an inch.

  5. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the peas are tender and start to break down. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to maintain a desired consistency.

  6. Once the peas are cooked, add the cubed potatoes to the pot and continue to simmer for another 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

  7. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the bay leaf and discard it.

  8. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with the crispy bacon and fresh parsley. Serve hot and enjoy!

Additional Latvian Recipes

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making Latvian Grey Pea Soup, why not expand your culinary horizons and try some more traditional Latvian dishes? Here are a few recipes to get you started:

  1. Pīrāgi: These Latvian bacon buns are a popular snack or appetizer. They are made with a flaky dough filled with a savory mixture of bacon and onions. Perfect for parties or a quick bite on the go.

  2. Sklandrauši: These delicious apple pastries are a sweet treat that are sure to satisfy your dessert cravings. Made with a tender dough filled with sliced apples, sugar, and cinnamon, these pastries are baked until golden and served warm.

  3. Kartupeļu pankūkas: Latvian potato pancakes are a beloved dish that can be enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Made with grated potatoes, onions, and a touch of flour, these pancakes are fried until crispy and served with sour cream or applesauce.

Conclusion

Latvian cuisine is a treasure trove of delicious and comforting dishes, and Latvian Grey Pea Soup is just the beginning. Whether you’re looking to explore the flavors of Latvia or simply want to try something new in the kitchen, these recipes are sure to inspire you. So grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and let the culinary adventure begin!
