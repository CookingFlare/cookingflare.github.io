Irresistible Indian Fusion: Tantalizing Treats from Balancing the Three Flavors


Exploring the vibrant world of Indian cuisine, we come across an array of mouth-watering flavors, textures, and spices that leave us craving for more. This fascinating culinary landscape is something extraordinary and beyond comparison. In this blog post, we will take you on a delightful journey of gastronomic exploration, presenting you with irresistible Indian fusion recipes, inspired by balancing the three classic flavors – sweet, sour, and spicy.

A bit of insight into the premise of the three flavors will provide a wonderful backdrop. The ancient Indian concept of Rasa (flavors) is based on balancing the three fundamental qualities in nature, known as the Tridosha. These elements embody the essence of Ayurveda, the traditional medicine system of India, and have long fostered the body’s harmony. This approach not only makes the food more palatable and delicious but also comprises nutritional values that enhance digestion and bring a sense of vitality to our system.

Let’s start with an appetizer that sparks curiosity while whetting your appetite. The Smoky BBQ Grilled Chicken Tikka – a blend of fiery tandoori spices, and smoky BBQ flavors, marinated into succulent pieces of chicken. Let your taste buds experience a burst of intense flavors as you bite into this succulent treat.

Smoky BBQ Grilled Chicken Tikka

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tandoori spice mix
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, olive oil, BBQ sauce, tandoori spice mix, ginger-garlic paste, and salt.
  2. Blend well to a smooth marinade, adding water if too thick.
  3. Add chicken pieces and mix until evenly coated.
  4. Marinate the chicken pieces for at least 2 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  5. Preheat a grill or grill pan on medium-high heat.
  6. Skewer the chicken pieces, alternating with onion and bell pepper pieces.
  7. Oil the skewers lightly and grill for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally until the chicken is well-cooked.
  8. Slice and serve hot with lemon wedges.

To complement the fiery, aromatic grilled chicken is an equally enticing salad, the Spicy Mango and Cucumber Salad. The sweet, juicy mango perfectly balances the spicy kick from the addition of a homemade green chili sauce.

Spicy Mango and Cucumber Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups diced ripe mango
  • 2 cups diced cucumber
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped red onion
  • 1 jalapeno, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup homemade green chili sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine diced mango, cucumber, red onion, and jalapeno.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together green chili sauce, lime juice, and honey.
  3. Pour the dressing over the mango and cucumber mixture.
  4. Toss gently to combine. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and additional honey if desired.

Next up, we explore the entrees and venture into the realm of the classic Indian protein-rich dish – the Chole Bhature.

Chole Bhature

Ingredients:

  • For the Chole (Chickpea Curry):
    • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 inch ginger, finely grated
    • 2 tomatoes, pureed
    • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1 teaspoon amchur powder (dried mango powder)
    • 1 tablespoon chickpea flour
    • Salt to taste
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh coriander, for garnish

For the Bhature (Fried Indian Bread):

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • Warm water as required

Instructions:

For the Bhature:

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Add oil, milk, and mix until the dough comes together. Add warm water gradually to make a soft, pliable dough.
  3. Let the dough rest, covered for 30 minutes.
  4. Punch down the dough and divide into small lime-sized balls.
  5. Roll each ball into a thin, oval-shaped bread.
  6. Heat oil in a pan to 350°F. Fry the bhature until golden, puffed, and cooked through.
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.

For the Chole:

  1. In a large skillet, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Cook until onions are tender, then add tomato puree and tomato paste. Cook until oil begins to separate.
  3. Stir in coriander, red chili, and garam masala powders. Add amchur powder, chickpea flour, and salt.
  4. Add cooked chickpeas and simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Add 1/2 cup water if needed to arrive at a desired consistency. Taste and adjust seasoning as per taste.
  6. Garnish with chopped coriander and serve hot with bhature.

To conclude the flavorsome journey, is a shining example of balancing sweetness with spices – the Jaggery Kulfi. A refreshing, cooling dessert that blends the richness of jaggery with the aromatic essence of cardamom, making it an irresistible dessert to round off the meal.

Jaggery Kulfi

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups milk
  • 1 cup jaggery powder
  • 10-12 green cardamoms, seeds ground
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened grated coconut
  • Pinch of saffron strands, soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk

Instructions:

  1. In a wide pan, heat milk over medium heat. Let it simmer and reduce to half.
  2. Add jaggery, ground cardamom, grated coconut, and saffron with its milk.
  3. Stir well and continue to simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Pour the mixture through a sieve into Kulfi molds or small popsicle molds.
  5. Freeze for at least 6 hours or until firm.
  6. To serve, briefly run the molds under warm water to unmold.
