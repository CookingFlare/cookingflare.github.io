Easter Baking Recipes: Make Sweet Treats with Your Kids


Easter is a time for family, celebration, and, of course, delicious food. Baking Easter treats with your kids is an exciting way to spend quality time together, create lasting memories, and indulge in some sweet goodies. Whether you’re looking to make colorful cookies, fluffy cupcakes, or adorable bunny-shaped pastries, Easter baking offers endless possibilities. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of fun and easy Easter baking recipes that you can enjoy with your little ones, helping them unleash their creativity while they learn the joys of cooking.

1. Classic Easter Sugar Cookies

Sugar cookies are a staple of Easter baking, and their versatility makes them a perfect choice for kids to decorate. These cookies can be shaped into Easter-themed cutouts like eggs, bunnies, or flowers, and then decorated with icing, sprinkles, and edible glitter. Here’s how you can make them:

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon almond extract (optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • Colorful sprinkles or icing for decorating

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, beat together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. This can take about 3-5 minutes.
  4. Add the egg, vanilla extract, and almond extract (if using) to the butter mixture and continue mixing until fully combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just incorporated. You’ll have a thick dough.
  6. Roll the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes.
  7. Once chilled, roll the dough out on a lightly floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness. Use Easter-themed cookie cutters to cut out shapes.
  8. Place the cookies onto the prepared baking sheets and bake for 8-10 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.
  9. Let the cookies cool completely before decorating. Allow your kids to go wild with colorful icing, sprinkles, and even small candy decorations.

These cookies are simple, delicious, and a great way for kids to practice their fine motor skills while getting creative.

2. Bunny-Shaped Cupcakes

Cupcakes are always a hit at Easter gatherings, and these bunny-shaped cupcakes are sure to impress. They are soft, sweet, and can be decorated to look like cute little bunnies with fluffy tails and edible ears. Here’s how you can make them:

Ingredients for Cupcakes:

  • 1 box of vanilla cake mix (or homemade recipe)
  • 3 large eggs
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Ingredients for Decoration:

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 3-4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • Pink food coloring
  • Mini marshmallows (for bunny tails)
  • Jelly beans (for bunny noses)
  • Candy eyes (or chocolate chips)
  • Pretzel sticks (for bunny ears)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake tin with cupcake liners.
  2. Prepare the cupcake batter according to the instructions on the cake mix box, or make a homemade batter if preferred.
  3. Fill the cupcake liners about 2/3 full with batter and bake for 18-22 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  4. Let the cupcakes cool completely before decorating.

Decorating the Cupcakes:

  1. To make the frosting, beat together the softened butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract in a mixing bowl. Add 1 tablespoon of milk at a time to achieve the desired consistency.
  2. Split the frosting into two bowls. Add a few drops of pink food coloring to one bowl and mix well.
  3. Frost the top of each cupcake with the white frosting.
  4. For the bunny ears, break the pretzel sticks in half and place them at the top of each cupcake. You can bend the pretzels slightly to give them a more realistic bunny ear shape.
  5. Place a mini marshmallow in the center of the cupcake for the bunny tail.
  6. Use a jelly bean for the bunny’s nose and candy eyes or chocolate chips for the eyes.
  7. Finally, pipe a little pink frosting on the edge of the marshmallow tail to give it that “fluffy” look.

These bunny-shaped cupcakes are adorable and will definitely make your Easter table look extra festive. They’re a great way for kids to get involved in decorating and add their personal touch to each bunny.

3. Easter Egg Cake Pops

Cake pops are a fun way to make Easter-themed treats with your kids. These Easter egg cake pops can be decorated with vibrant colors, and they’re easy to make, making them an excellent project for younger children to help with.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of cake mix (or homemade recipe)
  • 1 can of frosting
  • White chocolate or candy melts
  • Sprinkles, edible glitter, or colored sugar for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Bake the cake according to the package instructions and allow it to cool completely.
  2. Crumble the cake into small pieces using your hands or a fork. Add frosting to the crumbled cake a little bit at a time until the mixture is moist but not overly sticky.
  3. Roll the cake mixture into small oval-shaped balls, resembling Easter eggs.
  4. Melt the white chocolate or candy melts in the microwave according to package instructions.
  5. Dip the end of a lollipop stick into the melted chocolate, then insert the stick halfway into each cake ball to help secure it.
  6. Dip each cake pop into the melted chocolate, fully coating it. Tap off any excess chocolate.
  7. While the chocolate is still wet, decorate the cake pops with colorful sprinkles, edible glitter, or colored sugar. You can also pipe additional icing to add designs or patterns.
  8. Let the cake pops cool completely before serving.

Cake pops are a perfect combination of cake and chocolate, and their bite-sized nature makes them an excellent choice for kids. You can customize the colors and designs for each pop, making them as festive as you want.

4. Carrot-Shaped Cupcakes with Cream Cheese Frosting

For a fun and healthy twist on Easter cupcakes, try making carrot-shaped cupcakes with a delicious cream cheese frosting. These cupcakes are perfect for those who love a little more spice and a creamy frosting to complement the flavor.

Ingredients for Cupcakes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 ¼ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups grated carrots

Ingredients for Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3-4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a cupcake tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat together the sugar and oil until smooth. Add eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture and mix until combined.
  5. Fold in the grated carrots, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cupcakes cool completely.

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  1. Beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth and creamy.
  2. Gradually add the powdered sugar, 1 cup at a time, beating well after each addition.
  3. Add vanilla extract and continue mixing until the frosting is light and fluffy.

Decorating the Cupcakes:

  1. Frost the cooled cupcakes with the cream cheese frosting.
  2. To make them look like carrots, pipe the frosting in a pointed shape at the top of each cupcake, then smooth it out to resemble the shape of a carrot.
  3. Use green icing to pipe “carrot tops” on top of each cupcake.

These carrot-shaped cupcakes are flavorful and fun to make with your kids, and the cream cheese frosting adds a rich and tangy contrast to the spiced carrot cake.

Conclusion

Baking with your kids during Easter is a wonderful way to create sweet memories, engage in fun activities, and share the joy of creating something from scratch. Whether you’re making bunny cupcakes, decorating Easter cookies, or crafting cake pops, there’s something special about baking together as a family. Not only do these activities teach valuable skills like measuring, mixing, and decorating, but they also allow children to express their creativity in the kitchen. So, gather your ingredients, invite your little bakers to join in, and start creating delightful Easter treats that will be enjoyed by everyone at your table. Happy baking!
