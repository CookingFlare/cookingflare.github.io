Classic Scalloped Potatoes: The Perfect Side Dish for Easter


Easter is a time for family gatherings, celebration, and of course, delicious food. If you’re looking for a crowd-pleasing side dish that will impress your guests, look no further than classic scalloped potatoes. Creamy, cheesy, and oh-so-comforting, this dish is a true crowd favorite. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating the perfect scalloped potatoes that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about a minute, until fragrant.

  3. Add the heavy cream, dried thyme, salt, and pepper to the saucepan. Stir well and let the mixture come to a simmer. Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. Layer half of the sliced potatoes in the prepared baking dish, followed by half of the sliced onions. Sprinkle half of the shredded cheddar cheese over the top. Repeat the layers with the remaining potatoes, onions, and cheese.

  5. Give the cream mixture a good stir, then pour it evenly over the layered potatoes in the baking dish. Make sure all the potatoes are coated with the cream.

  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, until the potatoes are golden brown and fork-tender.

  7. Once the potatoes are cooked through, remove them from the oven and let them rest for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to serve.

  8. Serve your classic scalloped potatoes as a side dish alongside your Easter ham or roast. They also pair well with grilled chicken or steak for a hearty weeknight meal.

Tips for Success

  • To ensure even cooking, make sure to slice the potatoes thinly and uniformly. This will help them cook evenly and prevent any undercooked or overcooked pieces.

  • If you prefer a stronger onion flavor, you can sauté the sliced onions in a separate pan before layering them with the potatoes.

  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese. Gruyère, Parmesan, or even a blend of cheeses can add a unique twist to your scalloped potatoes.

  • For a crispy, golden top, you can broil the potatoes for a couple of minutes at the end of the cooking time. Keep a close eye on them to prevent burning.

  • Leftover scalloped potatoes can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply place them in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 15-20 minutes, until warmed through.

Conclusion

Classic scalloped potatoes are a timeless side dish that perfectly complements any Easter feast. With their creamy texture, cheesy goodness, and comforting flavors, they are sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or simply craving some comfort food, give this recipe a try and savor the deliciousness. Happy Easter and happy cooking!
