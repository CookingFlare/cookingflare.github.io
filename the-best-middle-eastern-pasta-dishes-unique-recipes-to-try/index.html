The Best Middle Eastern Pasta Dishes: Unique Recipes to Try


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, rich spices, and diverse ingredients that come together to create mouthwatering dishes. While pasta may not be native to the region, Middle Eastern cooks have cleverly incorporated it into their culinary traditions, blending it with local herbs, spices, and vegetables to create unique and unforgettable meals. If you’re looking to explore something new and exciting in your kitchen, these Middle Eastern-inspired pasta dishes are a perfect place to start.

1. Koshari – A Traditional Egyptian Comfort Food

Koshari is one of Egypt’s most beloved dishes, a hearty, satisfying combination of pasta, rice, lentils, and chickpeas topped with crispy onions and a tangy tomato sauce. This dish is a prime example of how Middle Eastern cuisine can transform simple ingredients into a flavorful and fulfilling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup elbow macaroni or small pasta shapes
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 cup brown lentils
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas (or canned chickpeas)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 tablespoon coriander
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the lentils and rice: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the lentils and cook until tender, about 20 minutes. In a separate pot, cook the rice according to package instructions.
  2. Cook the pasta: Boil the pasta in salted water until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  3. Fry the onions: In a skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. Make the tomato sauce: In the same skillet, sauté the garlic until fragrant, then add the tomato sauce, cumin, coriander, and vinegar. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until thickened. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Assemble the dish: In a large serving dish, layer the rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas. Pour the tomato sauce over the top and garnish with crispy onions and parsley. Serve warm.

Why You’ll Love It:

Koshari is a perfect comfort food that combines carbs and protein in a satisfying way. The crispy onions add a wonderful texture, while the tangy tomato sauce gives a burst of flavor that complements the dish beautifully.

2. Fatteh – Layers of Flavor

Fatteh is a traditional Middle Eastern dish that’s often served during festive occasions but makes for a fantastic weeknight dinner. This dish typically consists of layers of toasted pita bread, rice, and meat, all smothered in a garlicky yogurt sauce. In this version, we’ll incorporate pasta to give it a unique twist while still keeping the essence of the original recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pasta (such as penne or rigatoni)
  • 2 large pita breads, torn into pieces
  • 1 cup cooked chicken (or beef), shredded
  • 1 cup yogurt (Greek yogurt preferred)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the pasta: Cook the pasta according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. Toast the pita: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the torn pita pieces and toast them until golden and crispy. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Make the yogurt sauce: In a bowl, combine the yogurt, minced garlic, tahini, lemon juice, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Assemble the fatteh: In a large dish, layer the toasted pita bread, followed by the cooked pasta, and then the shredded chicken or beef. Pour the yogurt sauce over the top and drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle chopped parsley over the dish for freshness and color. Serve warm.

Why You’ll Love It:

Fatteh is a wonderful blend of flavors and textures. The creamy yogurt sauce paired with crispy pita, savory chicken, and hearty pasta makes it a dish that’s both filling and comforting. The spices like cumin and coriander add depth, while the lemon juice offers a hint of brightness.

3. Lebanese Pasta with Lamb and Cinnamon

In Lebanon, cinnamon is often used in savory dishes to add a warm, aromatic touch. This pasta dish is a perfect example of how Middle Eastern flavors can elevate classic pasta dishes. The richness of the lamb, combined with the sweetness of cinnamon, creates a beautiful contrast that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground lamb
  • 1 cup short pasta (such as fusilli or rotini)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh mint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta: Boil the pasta according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. Cook the lamb: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened. Add the ground lamb and cook until browned, breaking it up as it cooks.
  3. Add spices: Sprinkle in the cinnamon, allspice, and paprika. Stir to combine and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Simmer with tomato paste and broth: Add the tomato paste and chicken broth to the pan. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  5. Combine with pasta: Add the cooked pasta to the lamb mixture, tossing it well to coat in the sauce.
  6. Garnish and serve: Transfer the pasta to a serving dish, and garnish with fresh mint. Serve hot.

Why You’ll Love It:

This dish offers a unique flavor profile, with the sweetness of cinnamon and the savory richness of lamb coming together beautifully. The aromatic spices elevate the pasta, making it a memorable meal that’s perfect for a special dinner.

4. Sfiha-Inspired Pasta – Middle Eastern Meat Pies in Pasta Form

Sfiha is a Middle Eastern dish that’s typically made with ground lamb or beef, herbs, and spices, all baked on flatbread. We’ve taken inspiration from this delicious meat pie and turned it into a pasta dish that’s bursting with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup pasta (such as farfalle or penne)
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground sumac
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses (optional)
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • Olive oil for sautéing
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the pasta: Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. Cook the meat: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened. Add the ground meat and cook until browned.
  3. Season the meat: Sprinkle in the cumin, sumac, and season with salt and pepper. Stir in the pomegranate molasses, if using, and cook for another 5 minutes.
  4. Combine pasta and meat: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the meat mixture. Toss well to combine.
  5. Garnish and serve: Transfer to a serving dish, and garnish with toasted pine nuts and fresh parsley.

Why You’ll Love It:

This dish is packed with flavors from the aromatic spices and savory meat, while the pasta provides a perfect base to absorb all the deliciousness. The addition of pomegranate molasses adds a subtle tangy sweetness that balances the richness of the meat.

5. Spicy Harissa Pasta with Roasted Vegetables

Harissa is a fiery North African chili paste that’s made with a variety of spices and herbs. It’s often used in Middle Eastern and North African cuisine to add depth and heat to dishes. In this recipe, we’ll pair harissa with pasta and roasted vegetables for a satisfying meal that’s full of flavor and spice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta (such as spaghetti or linguine)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, zucchini, and eggplant)
  • 2 tablespoons harissa paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the vegetables: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and lightly browned.
  2. Cook the pasta: While the vegetables are roasting, cook the pasta according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  3. Make the harissa sauce: In a skillet, heat a little olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant. Stir in the harissa paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Combine pasta and vegetables: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the harissa sauce. Toss to coat the pasta in the sauce, then fold in the roasted vegetables.
  5. Garnish and serve: Transfer the pasta to a serving dish, sprinkle with crumbled feta cheese and fresh cilantro.

Why You’ll Love It:

The heat from the harissa combines beautifully with the sweetness of the roasted vegetables, creating a pasta dish that’s both bold and satisfying. The creamy feta adds a lovely contrast to the spiciness, while the fresh cilantro brings a burst of freshness.

These Middle Eastern pasta dishes offer a delicious fusion of traditional flavors with a twist on the classic pasta dishes you know and love. Whether you’re in the mood for something hearty and comforting, or light and spicy, these recipes are sure to bring exciting new flavors to your kitchen. Give them a try and let the vibrant tastes of the Middle East inspire your next meal!
