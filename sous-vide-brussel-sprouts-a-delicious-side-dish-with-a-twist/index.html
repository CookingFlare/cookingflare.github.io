Sous Vide Brussel Sprouts: A Delicious Side Dish with a Twist


Brussels sprouts, a vegetable often misunderstood and underappreciated, is a culinary delight when prepared with the right techniques. In this post, we’ll explore the world of sous vide cooking and show you how to create a delectable side dish that will impress even the most discerning palates.

The Power of Sous Vide

Sous vide, a French term that translates to “under vacuum,” is a cooking method that involves sealing food in airtight bags and then cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This technique allows for unparalleled control over the cooking process, resulting in perfectly cooked food every time. When applied to Brussels sprouts, sous vide cooking brings out the natural sweetness and tenderness of the vegetable, making it a game-changer for side dish enthusiasts.

Preparing the Brussels Sprouts

To begin, you’ll need a few simple ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup white wine (optional)

Place the Brussels sprouts in a large bowl and drizzle with olive oil. Toss to coat, then sprinkle with lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Massage the sprouts with your hands to ensure they’re evenly coated with the marinade.

Sous Vide Cooking

Next, place the marinated Brussels sprouts in a sous vide bag or a heat-safe ziplock bag. Seal the bag, making sure to remove as much air as possible before sealing. Place the bag in a large pot or container and add enough water to cover the bag by at least 2 inches.

Set the sous vide machine to 180°F (82°C) and cook the Brussels sprouts for 1 hour and 15 minutes. During the cooking process, the sprouts will become tender and slightly caramelized.

Finishing Touches

After the cooking time has elapsed, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully remove the Brussels sprouts from the bag. Place the sprouts on a plate or serving dish and dot the top with butter. If using, drizzle with chicken broth and white wine.

Serve the sous vide Brussels sprouts hot, garnished with chopped fresh herbs such as parsley or thyme. This side dish is perfect for accompanying roasted meats, poultry, or as a standalone vegetarian option.

The Benefits of Sous Vide Cooking

So why should you choose sous vide cooking for your Brussels sprouts? Here are a few benefits to consider:

  • Consistency: Sous vide cooking ensures that every Brussels sprout is cooked to the same level of doneness, resulting in a uniform texture and flavor.
  • Precision: With sous vide cooking, you can achieve a precise level of doneness, whether you prefer your Brussels sprouts tender, slightly crunchy, or somewhere in between.
  • Easy cleanup: Cooking with sous vide bags eliminates the need for pots and pans, making cleanup a breeze.
  • Flavor enhancement: The controlled environment of the sous vide machine allows for the infusion of flavors into the food, resulting in a more complex and nuanced taste experience.

In conclusion, sous vide Brussels sprouts are a game-changing side dish that’s sure to impress even the most discerning palates. With its unparalleled control, precision, and ease of cleanup, sous vide cooking is the perfect technique for elevating this often-underappreciated vegetable. Give it a try and discover a whole new world of flavor and texture in your Brussels sprouts.
