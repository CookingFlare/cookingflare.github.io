Delicious and Easy Slow Cooker Pulled Pork Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the delectable allure of slow cooker pulled pork. This classic recipe is not only mouthwatering but also simple to prepare, making it an ideal choice for busy weeknights or leisurely weekend gatherings. With its tender, juicy texture and bold flavors, this pulled pork will quickly become a family favorite. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making delicious and easy slow cooker pulled pork, along with tips and tricks to elevate your dish.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather all the ingredients you will need for this scrumptious slow cooker pulled pork recipe:

  • 3 to 4 pounds of pork butt (shoulder)
  • 1 cup of barbecue sauce (your favorite brand or homemade)
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • Buns (for serving)
  • Coleslaw (for topping, optional)

Why Use a Slow Cooker?

The beauty of using a slow cooker lies in its ability to transform tough cuts of meat into tender, flavorful dishes through low and slow cooking. The slow cooker not only allows the pork to cook evenly but also infuses it with the rich flavors of the spices and sauces. This method requires minimal hands-on time, allowing you to set it and forget it while the magic happens.

Preparing Your Pulled Pork

Step 1: Season the Pork

Start by patting the pork butt dry with paper towels. This helps ensure a nice sear if you choose to brown it beforehand, but it’s not strictly necessary. Next, mix the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper in a small bowl. Rub this spice mixture generously all over the pork, making sure to cover all sides. This seasoning blend will create a flavorful crust that enhances the overall taste of the dish.

Step 2: Searing the Meat (Optional)

For those who prefer an extra layer of flavor, consider searing the pork in a hot skillet for about 2-3 minutes on each side until it develops a golden-brown crust. This step is optional but can add depth to the final dish. Once seared, transfer the pork to the slow cooker.

Step 3: Add Liquid Ingredients

In the same skillet, you can deglaze with a bit of the chicken or vegetable broth, scraping up the brown bits from the bottom. This adds even more flavor to your pulled pork. Pour the deglazed liquid, the remaining broth, barbecue sauce, and Worcestershire sauce over the pork in the slow cooker.

Step 4: Slow Cook the Pork

Cover the slow cooker with the lid and set it to low for 8 hours or high for 4-5 hours. Cooking on low is generally recommended for the best texture. The pork is done when it is fork-tender and easily shreds apart. If you have a digital meat thermometer, aim for an internal temperature of at least 195°F to ensure the pork is perfectly cooked.

Shredding the Pork

Once the pork is cooked to perfection, remove it from the slow cooker and place it on a cutting board. Let it rest for about 10 minutes. Using two forks, shred the pork into bite-sized pieces. Be sure to discard any large pieces of fat. If desired, you can mix the shredded pork back into the sauce in the slow cooker, ensuring it’s well-coated and full of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

Classic Pulled Pork Sandwiches

One of the most popular ways to enjoy pulled pork is in a sandwich. Grab your favorite buns, pile on the shredded pork, and top it with coleslaw for a crunchy texture that perfectly complements the tender meat. You can also drizzle additional barbecue sauce on top for an extra kick.

Pulled Pork Tacos

For a fun twist, consider using the pulled pork as a filling for tacos. Warm up some corn or flour tortillas, and load them with pulled pork, diced onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime. This fresh combination adds a delightful contrast to the richness of the pork.

Pulled Pork Nachos

Transform your pulled pork into a crowd-pleasing appetizer by layering tortilla chips with shredded pork, cheese, jalapeños, and your favorite toppings. Bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly, then serve with salsa and guacamole.

Storing and Reheating Pulled Pork

If you happen to have leftovers (which is rare with this delicious dish!), you can store the pulled pork in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. To reheat, simply warm it in a skillet over medium heat or in the microwave until heated through. You can also add a splash of barbecue sauce or broth to keep it moist during reheating.

Freezing Pulled Pork

Pulled pork also freezes exceptionally well. Divide the shredded pork into meal-sized portions and store them in freezer-safe bags or containers. When you’re ready to enjoy, thaw the pork in the refrigerator overnight and reheat as mentioned above.

Tips for the Perfect Pulled Pork

  • Choose the Right Cut: Pork shoulder (also known as pork butt) is the best choice for pulled pork. It has the right amount of fat and connective tissue to become tender during cooking.

  • Don’t Rush the Cooking Process: Allow the pork to cook low and slow. This is crucial for achieving that melt-in-your-mouth texture.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to customize the spices and sauces to suit your taste. Adding a splash of apple cider vinegar or a few teaspoons of chipotle in adobo can add a unique twist to your pulled pork.

  • Serve with Sides: Pulled pork pairs beautifully with various sides, such as baked beans, cornbread, or macaroni and cheese, making for a complete meal.

Conclusion

In conclusion, making delicious and easy slow cooker pulled pork is a straightforward process that yields fantastic results. With just a few ingredients and minimal effort, you can create a dish that is sure to impress your family and friends. Whether you’re serving it in sandwiches, tacos, or nachos, the versatility of pulled pork allows for endless possibilities. So grab your slow cooker, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in a comforting, flavorful meal that will have everyone coming back for seconds! Enjoy your culinary adventure, and happy cooking!
