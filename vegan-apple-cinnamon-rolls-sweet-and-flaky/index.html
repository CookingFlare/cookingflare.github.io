Vegan Apple Cinnamon Rolls: Sweet and Flaky


There’s nothing quite as comforting as the smell of freshly baked cinnamon rolls wafting through the house. Add in the natural sweetness of apples, and you’ve got a recipe that will elevate your baking game to a whole new level. Whether you’re looking to treat yourself or impress guests at a brunch gathering, these vegan apple cinnamon rolls are an absolute winner. They’re soft, flaky, and bursting with warm cinnamon apple goodness. Plus, they’re made without any dairy or eggs, making them perfect for those following a plant-based lifestyle. In this post, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to create these irresistible rolls from scratch.

Why Choose Vegan Apple Cinnamon Rolls?

Traditional cinnamon rolls typically rely on dairy products such as butter and milk, and eggs for structure and richness. However, with a few simple swaps, we can recreate these same qualities in a plant-based version, making the rolls just as tender and flavorful. The vegan apple cinnamon rolls you’ll be making today will feature plant-based butter, non-dairy milk, and a delightful apple filling that brings an extra layer of sweetness and moisture.

These rolls are the ideal combination of textures—soft and gooey on the inside, slightly crisp on the outside, and topped with a sweet glaze that’s sure to make your taste buds dance. Plus, making them vegan means they are also suitable for anyone with lactose intolerance or egg allergies. These rolls are perfect for breakfast, dessert, or an afternoon snack with a cup of coffee.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Dough:

  • 3/4 cup non-dairy milk (such as almond, oat, or soy milk)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (use coconut sugar for a healthier alternative)
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter (melted)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (you can substitute with a gluten-free flour blend if needed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional for extra flavor)

For the Apple Filling:

  • 2 medium apples (peeled, cored, and thinly sliced)
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (or coconut sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, helps thicken the filling)
  • 2 tablespoons vegan butter

For the Glaze:

  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tablespoon non-dairy milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

Start by warming the non-dairy milk in a small saucepan or microwave. You want it to be lukewarm—too hot and it could kill the yeast, too cold and it won’t activate properly. Once the milk is at the right temperature, stir in the sugar and sprinkle the active dry yeast over the top. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes foamy. This step is essential to ensure the yeast is alive and active.

Next, in a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and cinnamon (if using). Pour in the yeast mixture along with the melted vegan butter and vanilla extract. Use a wooden spoon or dough hook to mix everything together until a dough forms.

Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Once ready, place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm area for 1-1.5 hours or until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Making the Apple Filling

While the dough is rising, you can prepare the apple filling. Start by melting the vegan butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Once the butter has melted, add the sliced apples and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally. You want the apples to soften slightly, but they shouldn’t fall apart. Stir in the brown sugar, cinnamon, and cornstarch (if using). The cornstarch will help thicken the mixture, but it’s optional if you prefer a slightly runnier filling. Continue cooking for another 3-4 minutes, then remove from heat and set aside to cool.

Step 3: Rolling and Assembling the Cinnamon Rolls

Once your dough has risen, punch it down gently to release the air. Roll the dough out on a floured surface into a large rectangle, about 14 inches by 8 inches.

Spread the apple filling evenly over the dough, leaving about 1 inch from the edges. Gently roll the dough up from the long side, creating a tight cylinder. Once rolled up, cut the dough into 8 equal pieces. You can use a sharp knife or dental floss to ensure clean cuts without squishing the rolls.

Step 4: Baking the Rolls

Arrange the cinnamon rolls in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, making sure they are snugly placed together. Cover the dish with a towel or plastic wrap and let the rolls rise for another 30 minutes, allowing them to become puffed up and airy.

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) while the rolls are rising. Once the rolls are ready, pop them into the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. The tops should be slightly crisp, and the rolls should smell heavenly as they bake.

Step 5: Adding the Glaze

While the rolls are baking, you can prepare the glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, non-dairy milk, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. If you prefer a thicker glaze, add more powdered sugar; for a thinner consistency, add more milk.

Once the rolls are done baking, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Drizzle the glaze over the warm rolls while they’re still soft, allowing it to melt into the crevices for that sweet finishing touch.

Step 6: Enjoy Your Vegan Apple Cinnamon Rolls

The hardest part is waiting for the rolls to cool just enough to take that first heavenly bite! These vegan apple cinnamon rolls are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven, but they also store well in an airtight container for a couple of days. You can even reheat them for a few seconds in the microwave for that freshly baked taste.

Tips for Perfect Vegan Apple Cinnamon Rolls

  • Use the right apples: Choose apples that hold their shape when cooked. Varieties like Fuji, Honeycrisp, or Granny Smith work wonderfully in this recipe. They add both sweetness and a touch of tartness that balances the cinnamon flavor beautifully.

  • Customize your filling: If you love nuts, add some chopped pecans or walnuts to the apple filling for a delightful crunch. You can also experiment with dried fruit like raisins or cranberries for an added burst of flavor.

  • Make it ahead: These rolls can be prepared the night before. After rolling them up and placing them in the baking dish, cover them with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight. The next morning, simply let them rise for about 30 minutes before baking.

  • Vegan butter alternatives: If you don’t have vegan butter on hand, you can substitute it with coconut oil or vegetable oil. Just be sure to melt it before using.

  • To freeze or not to freeze: These cinnamon rolls freeze really well. If you want to prepare them in advance for a special occasion, freeze the unbaked rolls after they have been assembled. On the day you want to bake them, let them thaw in the refrigerator overnight and then proceed with the final rise and baking as usual.

Vegan Apple Cinnamon Rolls: The Perfect Comfort Food

There’s something incredibly satisfying about homemade cinnamon rolls—especially when you get to enjoy them knowing that they’re completely vegan. The combination of tender dough, sweet apple filling, and warm cinnamon flavor makes these rolls an irresistible treat that everyone will love, no matter their dietary preferences.

Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or just treating yourself to a cozy breakfast, these vegan apple cinnamon rolls will surely become a favorite in your recipe rotation. They’re easy to make, taste incredible, and fill your home with an irresistible aroma. So, grab your ingredients and start baking these sweet, flaky delights today—you won’t regret it!

Happy baking!
