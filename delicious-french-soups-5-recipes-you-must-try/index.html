Delicious French Soups: 5 Recipes You Must Try


French cuisine boasts a rich tapestry of flavors and techniques, and nowhere is this more evident than in its soups. From the comforting warmth of a classic onion soup to the vibrant simplicity of a fresh vegetable bisque, French soups offer a delightful journey through the heart of France. In this post, we’ll explore five must-try French soup recipes, each brimming with authentic flavors and charm. Grab your apron and let’s dive into these delicious creations!

1. French Onion Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • Baguette slices
  • 2 cups grated Gruyère cheese

Instructions:

  1. Caramelize the Onions: In a large pot, melt the butter and olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions, salt, and sugar. Cook for about 30 minutes, stirring frequently, until the onions are deeply caramelized and golden brown.
  2. Add Garlic and Wine: Stir in the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute. Pour in the white wine, scraping the bottom of the pot to deglaze. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add Broth and Herbs: Pour in the beef broth and add thyme, pepper, and any additional salt to taste. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat and let it simmer for at least 20 minutes.
  4. Prepare Baguette Topping: While the soup simmers, preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place baguette slices on a baking sheet and toast until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Serve: Ladle the soup into oven-safe bowls, top each with a slice of toasted baguette, and generously sprinkle Gruyère cheese on top. Broil in the oven until the cheese is bubbly and golden, about 2-3 minutes. Serve immediately.

Tips:

  • For an extra depth of flavor, use a combination of beef and chicken stock.
  • Feel free to add a splash of sherry for added complexity.

2. Soupe au Pistou (Provençal Vegetable Soup)

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 potato, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup small pasta (like ditalini)
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add Remaining Vegetables: Stir in carrots, zucchinis, green beans, tomatoes, and potatoes. Cook for another 5-7 minutes, until vegetables begin to soften.
  3. Add Broth and Pasta: Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to package instructions until al dente, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Make the Pistou: In a small bowl or food processor, combine basil leaves, garlic, Parmesan, and a drizzle of olive oil. Blend until smooth, adding more olive oil if necessary.
  5. Finish and Serve: Once the pasta is cooked, stir in the pistou mixture and season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, garnished with additional Parmesan if desired.

Tips:

  • Use seasonal vegetables for the freshest flavors.
  • Pistou can be made ahead and stored in the fridge for up to a week.

3. Creamy Tomato Basil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (28 oz each) crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Add Tomatoes and Broth: Add crushed tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  3. Blend the Soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. If you prefer a chunkier texture, blend only half of it.
  4. Add Cream and Basil: Stir in heavy cream and fresh basil leaves. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Heat through before serving.
  5. Serve: Ladle into bowls and garnish with additional basil or a swirl of cream.

Tips:

  • For a smoky flavor, roast the tomatoes before adding them to the pot.
  • Pair with a grilled cheese sandwich for a classic comfort food combo.

4. Bouillabaisse (Provençal Fish Soup)

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 leeks, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 4 cups fish stock
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 pound firm white fish (like cod), cut into chunks
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 pound mussels, cleaned
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Aromatics: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, leeks, and garlic, sautéing until soft, around 5 minutes.
  2. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Stir in diced tomatoes, saffron, and fennel seeds. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add Stock and Wine: Pour in fish stock and white wine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to simmer for about 15 minutes.
  4. Add Fish and Shellfish: Add the chunks of fish, shrimp, and mussels. Cook until the mussels open and the fish is cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Serve: Ladle the bouillabaisse into bowls, garnishing with fresh parsley. Serve with a side of rouille and crusty bread.

Tips:

  • Enjoy bouillabaisse with a dollop of rouille (a garlic mayonnaise) for an authentic touch.
  • Use a variety of fish and shellfish for a more complex flavor.

5. Vichyssoise (Chilled Potato and Leek Soup)

Ingredients:

  • 3 leeks, white and light green parts only, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Leeks: In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat. Add chopped leeks and cook until soft, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add Potatoes and Broth: Stir in the diced potatoes and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes.
  3. Blend the Soup: Use an immersion blender to puree the mixture until smooth. Let it cool slightly.
  4. Add Cream and Season: Stir in heavy cream, and season with salt and pepper. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours.
  5. Serve: Ladle into bowls, garnishing with chopped chives before serving.

Tips:

  • Vichyssoise can be served warm or cold, making it versatile for any season.
  • Add a splash of lemon juice for brightness before serving.

Conclusion

French soups are a celebration of flavors and comfort, perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re warming up with a bowl of classic French onion soup or enjoying a chilled vichyssoise on a hot summer day, these recipes promise to elevate your culinary repertoire. Experiment with these dishes, adjust ingredients to your taste, and savor the essence of French cuisine in your kitchen. Bon appétit!
