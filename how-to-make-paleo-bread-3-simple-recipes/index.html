How to Make Paleo Bread: 3 Simple Recipes


Incorporating paleo bread into your diet can be a great way to enjoy baked goods while following a paleo lifestyle. These bread recipes are grain-free, gluten-free, and dairy-free, aligning with the principles of the paleo diet. Not only are these loaves healthier alternatives, but they’re also surprisingly delicious and versatile. Whether you’re looking for a basic loaf, a nutty variety, or one with a touch of sweetness, these three simple paleo bread recipes have you covered.

Recipe 1: Classic Paleo Bread

This recipe is perfect for everyday use. It’s a straightforward paleo bread that balances texture and flavor, making it ideal for sandwiches, toast, or as a side with soups and salads.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour
  • ½ cup tapioca flour (also known as tapioca starch)
  • 2 tablespoons coconut flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¼ cup unsweetened almond milk (or any non-dairy milk)
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the almond flour, tapioca flour, coconut flour, baking soda, and salt. Stir until well mixed.
  3. In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, almond milk, and apple cider vinegar.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing thoroughly until a smooth batter forms.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the bread to cool completely before slicing. Store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week, or freeze for longer storage.

This classic paleo bread strikes the perfect balance between being firm enough for toasting and soft enough for easy slicing.

Recipe 2: Nutty Paleo Seed Bread

Loaded with seeds and packed with nutritional value, this nutty paleo bread is for those who love a hearty, textured loaf. It’s dense, satisfying, and excellent as a snack or breakfast option.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • ¾ cup mixed seeds (such as sesame, sunflower, chia, and pumpkin seeds)
  • ½ cup ground flaxseed
  • ¼ cup coconut flour
  • 5 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or melted coconut oil
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. Combine the almond flour, coconut flour, baking powder, salt, ground flaxseed, and half of the mixed seeds in a large mixing bowl.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, olive oil, and apple cider vinegar.
  4. Gradually fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until combined evenly. The batter will be thick.
  5. Spoon the batter into the loaf pan and smooth down the top. Sprinkle the remaining mixed seeds on top for extra crunch and visual appeal.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes, checking for doneness with a toothpick.
  7. Let the bread cool completely before slicing to maintain its structure. Store in the refrigerator for up to five days.

This seed-packed paleo bread is fiber-rich and keeps you feeling full for hours. It pairs beautifully with avocado, nut butter, or simply a dab of grass-fed butter.

Recipe 3: Sweet Paleo Banana Bread

For a touch of natural sweetness, this paleo banana bread uses ripe bananas as the primary sweetener. It’s perfect for breakfast, dessert, or as a mid-day treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium ripe bananas, mashed
  • 2 cups almond flour
  • ¼ cup coconut flour
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup (optional for added sweetness)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon cinnamon
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease or line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Mix well to ensure no lumps remain.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mashed bananas, eggs, coconut oil, honey or maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  4. Slowly add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring constantly to form a uniform batter.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and spread it out evenly.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the loaf cool completely before slicing. To store, keep it refrigerated for up to a week, or freeze slices for quick on-the-go snacks.

This sweet paleo banana bread is wonderfully moist, naturally sweetened by the bananas, and incredibly versatile. Try adding a handful of chopped nuts, dark chocolate chips, or shredded coconut for added flavor.

Final Tips for Baking Paleo Bread

  • Let It Cool Completely: Paleo bread tends to be more delicate than traditional bread. Letting it cool fully ensures it slices properly without crumbling.
  • Experiment with Flours: Almond flour and coconut flour are widely used in paleo baking, but experimenting with flours like cassava or arrowroot can diversify your recipes.
  • Storage is Key: Always store paleo bread in the refrigerator or freezer to maintain freshness. Due to the absence of preservatives, it’s more prone to spoilage at room temperature.
  • Customize to Your Taste: Feel free to get creative! Add chopped nuts, spices, or dried fruit to make the recipes uniquely yours.

These three paleo bread recipes are simple, wholesome, and utterly satisfying. They showcase how easy it is to create delicious baked goods that align with your paleo lifestyle. Whether you’re making a classic loaf for sandwiches, a seed-heavy bread for a nutritious snack, or a sweet banana bread for dessert, there’s something to love in each recipe. Bake a loaf today and enjoy the healthy, grain-free benefits that paleo bread has to offer!
