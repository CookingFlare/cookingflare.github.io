Classic Italian Pasta Recipes for Beginner Cooks


Italian cuisine is renowned for its flavorful and comforting dishes. One of the most beloved aspects of Italian cooking is pasta. From spaghetti to lasagna, pasta dishes offer endless possibilities for creating delicious meals. If you’re a beginner cook looking to explore the world of Italian cuisine, these classic pasta recipes are a great place to start.

Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti carbonara is a classic Roman dish that combines simple ingredients to create a creamy and indulgent pasta dish. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 8 ounces of spaghetti
  • 4 slices of bacon, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley for garnish
  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pan.
  3. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper.
  5. Add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet with the bacon fat and garlic. Toss to coat the spaghetti with the flavors.
  6. Remove the skillet from heat and pour in the egg mixture, stirring quickly to prevent the eggs from scrambling. The residual heat will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce.
  7. Add the cooked bacon back into the skillet and toss to combine.
  8. Serve the spaghetti carbonara immediately, garnished with chopped parsley.

Classic Lasagna

Lasagna is a comforting and hearty Italian dish that is perfect for feeding a crowd. While it may seem intimidating, making lasagna from scratch is easier than you might think. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 9 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Chopped fresh basil for garnish
  1. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper to the skillet. Simmer the sauce for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom. Place three lasagna noodles on top of the sauce.
  6. Spread a layer of ricotta cheese on top of the noodles, followed by a layer of mozzarella cheese and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.
  7. Repeat the layers with the remaining ingredients, ending with a layer of meat sauce and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese on top.
  8. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes to allow the cheese to melt and turn golden brown.
  9. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before slicing. Garnish with chopped fresh basil before serving.

Conclusion

These classic Italian pasta recipes are perfect for beginner cooks who want to explore the world of Italian cuisine. From the creamy spaghetti carbonara to the comforting lasagna, these dishes are sure to impress your family and friends. So put on your apron, grab your pasta pot, and get ready to create delicious Italian meals in your own kitchen. Buon appetito!
