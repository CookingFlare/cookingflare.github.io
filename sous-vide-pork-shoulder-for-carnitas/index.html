Sous Vide Pork Shoulder for Carnitas


If you’re looking to make the most delicious, tender, and flavorful carnitas, then sous vide cooking is a game-changer. Sous vide allows you to cook the pork shoulder at a precise temperature for an extended period, resulting in meat that’s not only perfectly tender but also packed with flavor. Whether you’re preparing it for tacos, burritos, or just a comforting plate of carnitas, this method will ensure a juicy, melt-in-your-mouth experience every time.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique where food is vacuum-sealed in a bag and then cooked in a water bath at a low, consistent temperature for an extended period. This method preserves the flavors and juices, creating results that are hard to replicate with traditional cooking techniques. With sous vide, you get perfectly cooked meat every time, which makes it ideal for dishes like carnitas, where the texture and tenderness are key.

Why Pork Shoulder for Carnitas?

Pork shoulder, also known as pork butt, is a tough cut of meat that becomes incredibly tender and flavorful when cooked slowly. This makes it perfect for carnitas, a traditional Mexican dish that involves slow-cooking the pork until it’s so tender that it can be shredded easily with a fork.

Pork shoulder has enough fat to keep the meat juicy and flavorful as it cooks. The slow and low temperature of sous vide cooking ensures that the fat renders out gradually, keeping the meat moist and tender. The result is succulent pork that’s ideal for carnitas.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make sous vide pork shoulder for carnitas, you don’t need many ingredients. The simplicity of the recipe is one of the reasons it’s so popular. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 lbs (1.8 kg) of bone-in pork shoulder (You can also use boneless if you prefer)
  • 1 tablespoon of salt (adjust based on your taste)
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano (Mexican oregano works best)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon (optional, but adds a nice depth of flavor)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 orange, juiced (fresh juice, not from concentrate)
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1/2 cup of chicken broth or water (to keep the pork moist during sous vide cooking)
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Corn tortillas (for serving)

Optional Garnishes and Toppings:

  • Diced onions
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Salsa
  • Avocado or guacamole

Step-by-Step Instructions for Sous Vide Pork Shoulder Carnitas

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Shoulder

Start by prepping your pork shoulder. If you have a bone-in piece, it can be a bit trickier to fit it into the vacuum-seal bag, but it’s still possible. If you prefer a boneless version, it will be easier to handle. Begin by trimming any excess fat from the pork shoulder. You want to leave some fat, as it will help with the cooking process, but you don’t need an overwhelming amount.

Once the pork shoulder is prepped, rub the entire piece of meat with the dry seasoning mixture. In a small bowl, mix together the salt, black pepper, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, and cinnamon. Massage the seasoning mix into the meat, making sure it’s evenly coated.

Step 2: Vacuum Sealing

Place the seasoned pork shoulder into a vacuum-seal bag. Add the bay leaves, lime juice, and orange juice to the bag for added flavor. Once everything is in the bag, vacuum seal it tightly. The vacuum-seal process will ensure that the pork is surrounded by the seasoning and juices, which will help infuse the meat with flavor as it cooks.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking

Fill a large pot or container with water and set up your sous vide precision cooker. Preheat the water to 165°F (74°C). This temperature is perfect for pork shoulder as it allows the meat to cook slowly and become fork-tender without drying out.

Once the water has reached the desired temperature, submerge the vacuum-sealed bag with the pork shoulder into the water bath. Make sure the bag is fully submerged and that the water can circulate around the bag evenly. If you’re using a large piece of meat, it’s important to ensure there’s enough space for water flow to allow for even cooking.

Let the pork shoulder cook for 18-24 hours. The beauty of sous vide cooking is that you can set it and forget it. Whether you leave it in for 18 hours or 24 hours, the results will be deliciously tender.

Step 4: Shredding the Pork

Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the pork shoulder from the bag. It will be very tender, so handle it gently to avoid breaking it apart before you’re ready. Discard the bay leaves and any other solids from the bag.

Transfer the pork to a cutting board or large platter. Using two forks, shred the pork into bite-sized pieces. The meat should fall apart effortlessly, as it’s been cooked to perfection over the long, slow process.

Step 5: Crisping the Pork (Optional but Recommended)

For that signature carnitas texture, it’s time to crisp the shredded pork. This step will give the pork a slight crisp on the edges, adding a wonderful contrast to the tender interior.

Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of oil to the pan and once it’s hot, add the shredded pork. Let the pork cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it develops a crispy, golden-brown crust. This step is optional but really brings the dish to life.

Step 6: Serving the Carnitas

Once your carnitas are crispy and golden, it’s time to serve them! Place the carnitas on soft corn tortillas and top with your favorite garnishes. Diced onions, chopped cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and perhaps a little salsa make for the perfect toppings. You can also add avocado or guacamole for extra richness.

Additional Serving Suggestions

While carnitas are often served in tacos, there are plenty of other ways to enjoy this delicious pork. Here are some ideas:

  • Carnitas Burritos: Wrap the carnitas in a large flour tortilla along with rice, beans, and all your favorite toppings for a hearty burrito.
  • Carnitas Nachos: Spread tortilla chips on a baking sheet, top with carnitas, cheese, and your favorite nacho toppings, then bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  • Carnitas Salad: Serve the carnitas over a bed of fresh greens, topped with salsa, avocado, and a squeeze of lime for a lighter option.

Tips for the Best Sous Vide Carnitas

  • Cooking Time: The beauty of sous vide is that the cooking time is forgiving. If you’re short on time, you can cook the pork shoulder for 18 hours, but if you have more time, the full 24 hours will yield even more tender results.
  • Use the Right Cut of Pork: While pork shoulder is the traditional cut for carnitas, if you prefer a leaner option, you can substitute pork loin. However, the texture might not be as tender, so it’s worth sticking with the pork shoulder for optimal flavor and tenderness.
  • Seasoning Adjustments: Feel free to adjust the seasoning mix based on your preferences. If you like it spicier, add chili powder, cayenne pepper, or a bit of chipotle powder. You can also experiment with different herbs and spices to customize the flavor profile.
  • Leftovers: Sous vide carnitas keep well in the fridge for up to 4 days. You can also freeze the leftovers for future meals. Reheat gently in a skillet or in the microwave, and the carnitas will taste just as good as the day you made them.

Conclusion

Sous vide pork shoulder for carnitas is an easy yet incredibly flavorful way to prepare this beloved Mexican dish. The sous vide method guarantees that your pork will be tender, juicy, and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re preparing it for a family dinner, a special occasion, or a casual taco night, this recipe is sure to be a hit. The combination of slow-cooked pork and crispy edges creates the perfect balance of texture and taste, making each bite a savory delight. Enjoy it in tacos, burritos, or any other dish that calls for mouthwatering carnitas.
