Italian Tiramisu with Ladyfingers and Coffee Recipe


Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert that combines layers of creamy mascarpone cheese, ladyfingers soaked in coffee, and a dusting of cocoa powder. It’s the perfect dessert for coffee lovers and those with a sweet tooth. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create a delicious and authentic Italian tiramisu right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 24 ladyfingers
  • 1 1/2 cups strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until well combined and slightly thickened.

  2. In a small saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it reaches a simmer. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from curdling.

  3. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This will take about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool completely.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, beat the heavy cream and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. Add the mascarpone cheese and continue to beat until well combined and smooth.

  5. Gently fold the cooled egg yolk mixture into the mascarpone mixture until fully incorporated.

  6. Dip each ladyfinger into the cooled brewed coffee, making sure to coat both sides. Place the soaked ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of a 9x9-inch baking dish.

  7. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, smoothing it out with a spatula.

  8. Repeat the process with another layer of soaked ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.

  9. Sift the cocoa powder evenly over the top of the tiramisu, covering the entire surface.

  10. Cover the baking dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, to allow the flavors to meld together and the tiramisu to set.

  11. Serve chilled and enjoy this decadent and creamy Italian dessert!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick, you can add a splash of liqueur such as Marsala wine or coffee liqueur to the coffee before soaking the ladyfingers.

  • If you prefer a lighter version of tiramisu, you can use light cream cheese instead of mascarpone and substitute whipped cream for the heavy cream.

  • Experiment with different toppings such as grated chocolate, chocolate shavings, or even a drizzle of caramel sauce.

  • Tiramisu can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, but it’s best enjoyed within the first day or two for optimal flavor and texture.

Conclusion

Italian Tiramisu with Ladyfingers and Coffee is a classic dessert that never fails to impress. With its layers of creamy mascarpone cheese, delicate ladyfingers soaked in coffee, and a dusting of cocoa powder, it’s a dessert that will satisfy any sweet tooth. This recipe provides a simple and straightforward guide to creating your own homemade tiramisu that rivals those served in the finest Italian restaurants. So why not give it a try and indulge in the rich flavors of this beloved Italian delicacy? Your taste buds will thank you!
