Bacalao a la Vizcaina: A Basque Delicacy


If you’re looking to add a touch of Basque cuisine to your culinary repertoire, Bacalao a la Vizcaina is an excellent choice. This traditional dish from the Basque region of Spain features salted cod cooked in a rich and flavorful sauce, creating a dish that is both hearty and sophisticated. The dish has a long history and holds a special place in Basque culture, making it a fascinating addition to any cooking enthusiast’s repertoire.

The History of Bacalao a la Vizcaina

Bacalao a la Vizcaina, also known as Bakailaoa Bizkainiarra in Basque, has been a staple of Basque cuisine for centuries. The Basque people have a deep connection to the sea, and salted cod has been a crucial part of their diet since the Middle Ages. The tradition of salting and preserving cod allowed the Basque fishermen to stockpile their catch and bring it back to the inland regions, where fresh fish was not as readily available.

Over time, the Basques developed a variety of recipes for preparing salted cod, and Bacalao a la Vizcaina emerged as one of the most beloved. The dish is named after the Biscay (Vizcaya) region of the Basque Country, where it originated. It reflects the Basque people’s resourcefulness in creating delicious and satisfying meals from preserved ingredients.

Ingredients

To prepare Bacalao a la Vizcaina, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound (450g) salted cod
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 red bell peppers, roasted, peeled, and diced
  • 3 tomatoes, peeled, seeded, and chopped
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) olive oil
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) white wine
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup (60g) pitted black olives
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced

Preparation

  1. Begin by desalting the cod. Place the cod in a large bowl of water and refrigerate, changing the water every 8 hours for 24-48 hours, depending on the thickness of the fish. Once the cod has been desalted, drain it and pat it dry with paper towels.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic and sauté until they are soft and translucent.

  3. Add the red peppers and tomatoes to the skillet, and continue to cook for another 5 minutes.

  4. Pour in the white wine and chicken or vegetable broth, then add the black pepper and paprika. Stir the mixture well and let it simmer for 10 minutes.

  5. Gently place the cod in the skillet, making sure it is covered by the sauce. Cover and cook over low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the cod is tender and flakes easily with a fork.

  6. Once the cod is cooked, add the olives to the skillet and let them heat through for a few minutes.

  7. To serve, place the cod on a platter and spoon the sauce over the top. Garnish with the sliced hard-boiled eggs and chopped parsley.

Serving Suggestions

Bacalao a la Vizcaina is often served with crusty bread or steamed rice to soak up the flavorful sauce. A fresh green salad makes for a refreshing accompaniment, providing a contrast to the rich and savory flavors of the dish.

Conclusion

Bacalao a la Vizcaina is a dish that encapsulates the rich culinary heritage of the Basque people. Its robust flavors and historical significance make it a delightful addition to any cooking repertoire. Whether you’re an experienced chef or a curious home cook, exploring the world of Basque cuisine through this classic dish is sure to be a rewarding experience. Enjoy the process of preparing and savoring this Basque delicacy, and let it transport you to the picturesque landscapes and vibrant culture of the Basque Country.
