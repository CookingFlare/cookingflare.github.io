One-Pot Wonders: 5 Healthy and Light Recipes for Easy Cleanup


Cooking can be a joyous experience, but the cleanup afterward can sometimes be a daunting task. That’s where one-pot wonders come to the rescue! These recipes not only save time and effort but also result in delicious and healthy meals. In this blog post, we will share five easy one-pot recipes that are not only light on your stomach but also light on your cleaning duties.

1. Quinoa and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, etc.)
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water and drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the quinoa and water to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce over the cooked quinoa and vegetables. Stir well to combine.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Lemon Herb Chicken with Roasted Vegetables

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (potatoes, carrots, Brussels sprouts, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large oven-safe pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and half of the lemon zest.
  4. Sear the chicken breasts in the pot until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  6. In the same pot, add the mixed vegetables, garlic, dried thyme, dried rosemary, and the remaining lemon zest. Toss to coat the vegetables in the oil and herbs.
  7. Place the seared chicken breasts on top of the vegetables.
  8. Drizzle the lemon juice over the chicken and vegetables.
  9. Cover the pot and transfer it to the preheated oven. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy!

3. Lentil and Vegetable Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (zucchini, bell peppers, cauliflower, etc.)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the lentils under cold water and drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  3. Add the curry powder, cumin, and turmeric to the pot. Stir well to coat the onion and garlic in the spices.
  4. Add the lentils, vegetable broth, diced tomatoes, and mixed vegetables to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Simmer for 25-30 minutes or until the lentils are cooked and the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan bread.

4. Shrimp and Vegetable Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the bell pepper, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, and shrimp to the pot. Cook for 5-7 minutes or until the shrimp is pink and cooked through.
  4. Add the cooked spaghetti to the pot and toss to combine.
  5. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the pasta and vegetables. Stir well to coat.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil and serve hot.

5. Moroccan Chickpea Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, sweet potatoes, kale, etc.)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the ground cumin, ground coriander, and ground cinnamon to the pot. Stir well to coat the onion and garlic in the spices.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, mixed vegetables, and chickpeas to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Simmer for 25-30 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot with couscous or crusty bread.

One-pot wonders not only make cooking a breeze but also make cleaning up a breeze. These five healthy and light recipes are perfect for those busy weeknights when you want a delicious meal without the hassle of multiple pots and pans. Give them a try and enjoy the simplicity and flavor they bring to your table. Happy cooking!
