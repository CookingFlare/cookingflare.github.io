Chinese Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken Recipe


Looking to add a little spice to your dinner routine? Look no further than this Chinese Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken recipe! With its bold flavors and fiery kick, it’s sure to satisfy your cravings for a delicious and authentic Chinese dish. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make this popular Szechuan dish from scratch, using simple ingredients that you can find in your pantry. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup unsalted peanuts
  • 3-4 dried red chilies, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 3 green onions, chopped

For the marinade:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

For the sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the chicken with the marinade ingredients: soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cornstarch. Mix well and set aside for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated chicken and cook until it turns golden brown and is cooked through. This should take approximately 5-6 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the peanuts and dried red chilies. Stir-fry them for about 2 minutes until they become fragrant and slightly toasted. Be careful not to burn them.

  4. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the skillet and sauté for another minute, until they release their aroma.

  5. Now, it’s time to add the diced bell peppers to the skillet. Stir-fry them for 2-3 minutes until they become slightly tender but still retain their crunch.

  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the sauce ingredients: soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and chicken broth. Pour the sauce into the skillet and bring it to a simmer.

  7. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and toss it with the sauce and vegetables. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is evenly coated and heated through.

  8. Finally, sprinkle the chopped green onions over the top of the dish and give it a quick stir to incorporate them.

  9. Serve the Chinese Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken hot over steamed rice or noodles. Enjoy the combination of flavors and textures in this mouthwatering dish!

Conclusion

There you have it – a simple and flavorful Chinese Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken recipe that you can easily recreate at home. The combination of tender chicken, crunchy peanuts, and spicy chilies is a true delight for your taste buds. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or looking to try something new, this dish is sure to impress. Give it a go and let us know how it turns out!

Remember, feel free to adjust the spice level to your preference by adding more or fewer dried chilies. Don’t be afraid to experiment and make this recipe your own. Happy cooking!
