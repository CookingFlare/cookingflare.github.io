Instant Pot Spaghetti and Meatballs: A Family Favorite


When you think of comfort food, few dishes hold a candle to the timeless combination of spaghetti and meatballs. It’s a meal that brings families together, fills your kitchen with savory aromas, and satisfies the heart and soul. But let’s face it: cooking the perfect spaghetti and meatballs can be time-consuming, especially if you’re juggling a busy schedule. That’s where the Instant Pot comes in. With its speed, convenience, and ability to create rich, flavorful dishes in a fraction of the time, the Instant Pot has revolutionized home cooking, making it easier than ever to prepare this classic dish.

In this blog post, we’re going to dive into a simple and delicious Instant Pot spaghetti and meatballs recipe that will quickly become a family favorite. With just a few ingredients, some basic kitchen tools, and a little bit of time, you’ll be able to serve up a homemade, hearty meal that everyone will love.

Why Instant Pot?

Before we jump into the recipe, let’s talk about why the Instant Pot is the ideal tool for this dish. The Instant Pot is a pressure cooker, slow cooker, rice cooker, steamer, sauté pan, and yogurt maker all in one. For busy families, this means you can quickly get dinner on the table with minimal hands-on time.

Using the Instant Pot for spaghetti and meatballs allows you to cook both the meatballs and the pasta in one pot. The pressure cooking method infuses the meatballs with incredible flavor, making them tender and juicy. Meanwhile, the spaghetti cooks to perfection without turning mushy, and all the flavors meld together beautifully in a fraction of the time it would take using traditional methods.

The Ingredients

To create a mouthwatering Instant Pot spaghetti and meatballs dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Meatballs:

  • 1 lb ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork for extra flavor)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried parsley)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sauce:

  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce (or homemade tomato sauce)
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or water (for pressure cooking)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional, for sautéing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano

For the Spaghetti:

  • 12 oz dry spaghetti (you can use gluten-free or whole wheat spaghetti if desired)

Optional Toppings:

  • Fresh basil or parsley, chopped
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

The Prep Work: Making the Meatballs

Step 1: Prepare the Meatball Mixture

Start by preparing the meatball mixture. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, garlic, parsley, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Use your hands or a spoon to mix everything together until well combined.

Step 2: Form the Meatballs

Once your meatball mixture is ready, use your hands to roll the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. You should get around 16-18 meatballs, depending on the size you make them. Be sure to roll them tightly but not too tightly, as this will ensure they stay moist while cooking.

Step 3: Sear the Meatballs (Optional)

While not essential, you can choose to sear the meatballs for a deeper, caramelized flavor. Turn your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function and add a little olive oil. Once the oil is hot, add the meatballs in batches, searing each side for about 2-3 minutes. This step enhances the flavor, but if you’re pressed for time, you can skip it and add the raw meatballs directly to the pot.

Cooking in the Instant Pot

Now that your meatballs are ready, it’s time to cook everything in the Instant Pot.

Step 4: Add the Sauce and Broth

Once the meatballs are ready (whether seared or raw), place them in the bottom of the Instant Pot. Pour the marinara sauce over the meatballs, followed by the beef broth or water. Add the dried basil, oregano, and red pepper flakes (if using), along with the teaspoon of sugar to help balance the acidity of the tomato sauce. Stir gently to combine.

Step 5: Layer the Spaghetti

Next, break the dry spaghetti in half and layer it on top of the sauce and meatballs. It’s important to stagger the pasta so that it cooks evenly and doesn’t clump together. Don’t worry if the pasta isn’t fully submerged— the pressure cooking will ensure that it cooks evenly and absorbs all the wonderful flavors.

Step 6: Pressure Cook

Secure the lid of the Instant Pot and set the valve to “Sealing.” Select the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” function and set the time to 8 minutes on high pressure. Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to naturally release for about 5 minutes before switching the valve to “Venting” to release the remaining pressure.

Step 7: Stir and Serve

When you open the Instant Pot, you’ll be greeted by a savory, delicious-smelling dish. Gently stir the spaghetti to break up any pieces that may have clumped together. The meatballs will be tender, the pasta perfectly cooked, and the sauce rich and flavorful. If you like, you can add a little more Parmesan cheese on top or sprinkle with fresh herbs for extra flavor and color.

Tips for Perfect Instant Pot Spaghetti and Meatballs

1. Don’t Overcrowd the Meatballs

It’s tempting to pack all the meatballs in at once, but overcrowding can cause them to cook unevenly. If your Instant Pot is too small to fit all the meatballs in a single layer, cook them in batches.

2. Use Quality Marinara Sauce

The marinara sauce you use plays a big role in the overall flavor of the dish. Opt for a high-quality store-bought sauce, or even better, make your own from fresh tomatoes, garlic, and herbs.

3. Adjust the Pasta for Different Sizes

If you’re making a larger or smaller batch, adjust the amount of pasta accordingly. As a general rule, the ratio should be 1 cup of pasta for every 2 cups of sauce.

4. Avoid Overcooking the Pasta

The Instant Pot cooks pasta quickly, so it’s important not to overcook it. Always use the “natural release” method for pressure cooking pasta to ensure it doesn’t become too mushy.

5. Customize Your Meatballs

Feel free to get creative with your meatballs. You can add grated vegetables like zucchini or carrots for extra nutrition, or switch up the seasonings for a different flavor profile. Adding a bit of mozzarella cheese in the center of each meatball can create a delicious surprise.

6. Make It Ahead

If you’re prepping for a busy week, you can make the meatballs ahead of time and freeze them. When you’re ready to cook, just add them directly to the Instant Pot along with the sauce, and proceed with the recipe as usual.

Why This Recipe is a Game-Changer

The beauty of this Instant Pot spaghetti and meatballs recipe is that it combines convenience with flavor. It’s the perfect solution for busy families who still want to enjoy a home-cooked meal without spending hours in the kitchen. The Instant Pot locks in the flavors of the meatballs, sauce, and pasta, ensuring that every bite is juicy and full of flavor. It also saves you from dirtying multiple pots and pans, as everything cooks in one single pot.

Serving Suggestions

  • Garlic Bread: A warm, buttery garlic bread is the perfect side to soak up the delicious sauce.
  • Salad: A light green salad with balsamic vinaigrette offers a refreshing contrast to the richness of the spaghetti and meatballs.
  • Roasted Vegetables: Consider serving roasted vegetables like broccoli, zucchini, or bell peppers for a healthy side dish.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a quick, easy, and delicious meal that the whole family will love, this Instant Pot spaghetti and meatballs recipe is the answer. With minimal prep time, the help of your trusty Instant Pot, and a handful of basic ingredients, you can create a comforting, flavorful dish that will make everyone feel right at home. Whether it’s a weeknight dinner or a family gathering, this recipe is sure to impress.

So, gather your ingredients, fire up the Instant Pot, and get ready to enjoy a classic Italian favorite that’s been simplified for modern-day cooks. Your family is going to love it!
