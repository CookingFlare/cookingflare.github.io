How to Make Vegan Doughnuts: Soft and Sweet


Doughnuts are a beloved treat, often associated with delightful mornings and indulgent moments. The good news is that you can enjoy these sweet, soft delights without any animal products. In this guide, we’ll delve into the art of making vegan doughnuts that are not only soft but also incredibly sweet. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, this recipe will guide you through every step to achieve that perfect doughnut.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before we dive into the method, let’s gather the ingredients. This recipe yields about 12 delicious vegan doughnuts.

For the Doughnut Batter:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour: This will be the base of your doughnuts, creating that fluffy texture.
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar: Sweetness is key! You can substitute with coconut sugar for a healthier option.
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder: This will help your doughnuts rise and become light and fluffy.
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda: A little boost to ensure your doughnuts are perfectly airy.
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt: Balances the sweetness and enhances flavor.
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce: Acts as an egg replacement, adding moisture and sweetness.
  • 1/2 cup non-dairy milk: Almond, soy, or oat milk works wonderfully.
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil: For that rich, moist texture.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: A must-have for flavor enhancement.

For the Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar: To achieve that sweet, glossy finish.
  • 2 tablespoons non-dairy milk: Adjust for desired consistency.
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract: For a lovely aromatic touch.

Equipment Needed

To make your vegan doughnuts, you will need the following equipment:

  • Mixing bowls: For combining your dry and wet ingredients.
  • Whisk: To blend the ingredients smoothly.
  • Doughnut pan: A silicone or metal pan specifically designed for doughnuts.
  • Cooling rack: Essential for letting your doughnuts cool evenly after baking.
  • Spoon or piping bag: For filling the doughnut pan.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This temperature is ideal for baking doughnuts, allowing them to rise perfectly without becoming dry.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk these ingredients together until they are well combined. This ensures that your leavening agents are evenly distributed throughout the flour.

Step 3: Combine the Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, mix the unsweetened applesauce, non-dairy milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Whisk these ingredients until smooth. The applesauce not only serves as an egg substitute but also contributes a wonderful sweetness and moisture to the doughnuts.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients. Gently fold the mixture using a spatula or a wooden spoon until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are perfectly fine. Overmixing can lead to dense doughnuts.

Step 5: Fill the Doughnut Pan

Using a spoon or a piping bag, carefully fill each cavity of the doughnut pan about three-quarters full. This allows room for the doughnuts to rise without overflowing. Smooth the tops with a spatula if necessary.

Step 6: Bake the Doughnuts

Place the filled doughnut pan in the preheated oven and bake for 10-12 minutes. The doughnuts are ready when they spring back when lightly touched and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 7: Cool the Doughnuts

Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and let it cool for about 5 minutes. Then, gently transfer the doughnuts to a cooling rack. Allow them to cool completely before glazing.

Making the Glaze

Step 8: Prepare the Glaze

While your doughnuts are cooling, it’s time to make the glaze. In a small bowl, combine the powdered sugar, non-dairy milk, and vanilla extract. Whisk until smooth. If the glaze is too thick, add more non-dairy milk a teaspoon at a time until you reach your desired consistency.

Step 9: Glaze the Doughnuts

Once the doughnuts are completely cool, dip the tops into the glaze, allowing any excess to drip off. You can also drizzle the glaze over the doughnuts using a spoon for a more artistic presentation.

Optional Toppings

To elevate your vegan doughnuts, consider adding some toppings. Here are a few ideas:

  • Coconut flakes: For a tropical touch.
  • Chopped nuts: Almonds, walnuts, or pecans for added crunch.
  • Sprinkles: Fun and colorful, perfect for celebrations.
  • Cinnamon sugar: A mix of sugar and cinnamon for a classic finish.

Storage Tips

To keep your vegan doughnuts fresh, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. If you want to keep them longer, you can freeze them for up to 3 months. Just let them thaw at room temperature before enjoying, and feel free to re-glaze if desired.

Conclusion

Now you have a delightful recipe for soft and sweet vegan doughnuts that will impress your friends and family. The joy of baking these treats lies not only in the delicious end result but also in the process itself. Don’t hesitate to experiment with flavors, such as adding cocoa powder for chocolate doughnuts or incorporating spices like nutmeg or cardamom for a unique twist.

Baking vegan doughnuts is a fun and rewarding experience that allows you to indulge in a classic favorite without compromising your dietary preferences. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy the soft, sweet goodness of homemade vegan doughnuts! Happy baking!
