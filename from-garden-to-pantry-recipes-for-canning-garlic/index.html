From Garden to Pantry: Recipes for Canning Garlic


Garlic, with its pungent aroma and distinct flavor, is a staple ingredient in many cuisines around the world. If you’re a garlic lover like me, you know that having a steady supply of fresh garlic is essential in the kitchen. That’s where canning garlic comes in handy. By canning garlic, you can preserve its freshness and ensure that you always have this flavorful ingredient at your fingertips. In this blog post, I’ll share with you some delicious and easy recipes for canning garlic that will elevate your culinary creations.

Why Can Garlic?

Canning garlic offers numerous benefits. Firstly, it allows you to extend the shelf life of garlic, ensuring that you have a ready supply throughout the year, even when it’s out of season. Secondly, canning garlic allows you to preserve its flavor and aroma, so you can enjoy the true essence of fresh garlic in your dishes. Lastly, canning garlic is a great way to reduce food waste, as you can use up any excess garlic from your garden or pantry.

Recipe 1: Pickled Garlic

Pickled garlic is a tangy and slightly sweet treat that adds a burst of flavor to salads, sandwiches, and even cocktails. To make pickled garlic, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound of garlic cloves
  • 2 cups of white vinegar
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black peppercorns
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional)

Here’s how to make it:

  1. Peel the garlic cloves and set them aside.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the vinegar, water, sugar, salt, peppercorns, and red pepper flakes (if using). Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar and salt dissolve.
  3. Add the garlic cloves to the boiling mixture and let them simmer for about 5 minutes.
  4. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the garlic cloves to sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  5. Pour the pickling liquid over the garlic cloves, ensuring that they are fully submerged.
  6. Wipe the rims of the jars, seal them tightly, and process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place for at least 2 weeks to allow the flavors to develop.

Recipe 2: Garlic Paste

Garlic paste is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of dishes, from marinades to dips. To make garlic paste, you’ll need:

  • 1 cup of peeled garlic cloves
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

Here’s how to make it:

  1. Place the garlic cloves in a food processor or blender.
  2. Pulse the garlic cloves until they are finely chopped.
  3. Gradually add the olive oil while continuing to blend the garlic until it forms a smooth paste.
  4. Season with salt to taste.
  5. Transfer the garlic paste to sterilized jars, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace.
  6. Wipe the rims of the jars, seal them tightly, and process the jars in a boiling water bath for 15 minutes.
  7. Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in the refrigerator for up to 6 months.

Recipe 3: Roasted Garlic in Olive Oil

Roasted garlic in olive oil is a delicious and fragrant addition to pasta, bread, and roasted vegetables. To make roasted garlic in olive oil, you’ll need:

  • 1 head of garlic
  • Olive oil, enough to cover the garlic

Here’s how to make it:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Cut off the top of the garlic head to expose the cloves.
  3. Place the garlic head in a small baking dish and drizzle it with olive oil, ensuring that all the cloves are coated.
  4. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and roast the garlic for about 45 minutes, or until the cloves are soft and golden.
  5. Allow the roasted garlic to cool completely.
  6. Squeeze the cloves out of the garlic head and transfer them to sterilized jars.
  7. Pour enough olive oil over the garlic cloves to fully cover them.
  8. Wipe the rims of the jars, seal them tightly, and store them in the refrigerator for up to 2 months.

Conclusion

Canning garlic is a simple and rewarding process that allows you to enjoy the flavors of fresh garlic all year round. Whether you choose to pickle garlic, make garlic paste, or roast garlic in olive oil, these recipes will add depth and richness to your culinary creations. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your garlic, and embark on a journey from the garden to your pantry. Happy canning!

Note: These canning recipes have not been tested for long-term storage. Follow proper canning procedures and guidelines to ensure the safety of your preserved garlic.
