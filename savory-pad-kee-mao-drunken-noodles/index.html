Savory Pad Kee Mao (Drunken Noodles)


If you’re looking to add a burst of flavor to your dinner routine, Savory Pad Kee Mao, also known as Drunken Noodles, is a dish you must try. This popular Thai street food is a delightful mix of wide rice noodles, fresh vegetables, aromatic herbs, and savory sauces that come together to create a mouthwatering experience. Let’s dive into the details of how you can recreate this delicious dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz wide rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 Thai bird’s eye chilies, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 lb protein of your choice (chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu)
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Handful of Thai basil leaves
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions until they are al dente. Drain and set aside.

  2. Stir-Fry the Aromatics: In a wok or large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced Thai chilies. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Add Protein and Vegetables: Push the aromatics to the side of the wok and add the protein of your choice. Cook until the protein is almost done, then add the bell pepper, broccoli, and snap peas. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  4. Create the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix together the oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar. Pour this sauce over the stir-fry mixture.

  5. Incorporate Noodles and Basil: Add the cooked rice noodles to the wok. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce. Throw in a handful of Thai basil leaves and give it a final stir.

  6. Serve: Transfer the Pad Kee Mao to a serving dish and garnish with additional basil leaves. Serve hot with lime wedges on the side for a burst of citrusy freshness.

Tips for Success:

  • Protein Choice: Feel free to customize this dish with your preferred protein. Chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu all work wonderfully in this recipe.
  • Adjusting Spice Levels: If you prefer a milder dish, you can reduce the amount of Thai chilies or remove the seeds before slicing.
  • Fresh Ingredients: For the best flavors, use fresh vegetables and herbs. Thai basil adds a unique aroma to the dish that enhances its authenticity.
  • Serving Suggestions: Pad Kee Mao pairs beautifully with a side of jasmine rice or a simple cucumber salad to balance out the flavors.

Dive into the tantalizing world of Thai cuisine with this delectable Savory Pad Kee Mao recipe. Whether you’re a fan of bold flavors or looking to explore new taste sensations, this dish is sure to impress your taste buds. Enjoy the medley of textures, aromas, and tastes that make Pad Kee Mao a beloved classic in Thai culinary tradition.
