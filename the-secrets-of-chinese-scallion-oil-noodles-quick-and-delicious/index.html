The Secrets of Chinese Scallion Oil Noodles: Quick and Delicious


Chinese scallion oil noodles, also known as Cong You Ban Mian, are a popular Chinese dish that has gained worldwide recognition for its bold flavors and aromatic aroma. This simple yet flavorful dish is a staple in many Chinese restaurants and households, and for good reason. In this post, we will delve into the secrets of making the perfect Chinese scallion oil noodles, and share the techniques and ingredients needed to create this mouthwatering dish.

The Essential Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients needed to make Chinese scallion oil noodles.

  • 200g of noodles (preferably fresh or high-quality dried noodles)
  • 2 tablespoons of scallion oil (see recipe below for homemade scallion oil)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 scallions, thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped cilantro or scallions for garnish (optional)

Homemade Scallion Oil Recipe

Before we proceed with the noodle recipe, let’s start with the homemade scallion oil recipe. This is the key component that sets Chinese scallion oil noodles apart from other noodle dishes.

  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup of thinly sliced scallions
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the thinly sliced scallions and minced garlic to the oil, and stir-fry until the scallions are translucent and fragrant.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Strain the oil through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl, discarding the solids.
  5. Store the homemade scallion oil in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

Cooking the Noodles

Now that we have our homemade scallion oil, let’s cook the noodles.

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, and cook the noodles according to the package instructions.
  2. Drain the noodles and set them aside.

Preparing the Sauce

Next, let’s prepare the sauce.

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch until smooth.
  2. Add the vegetable oil, minced garlic, and thinly sliced scallions to the bowl, and stir until well combined.
  3. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

Assembling the Dish

Now that we have our noodles and sauce prepared, let’s assemble the dish.

  1. In a large wok or frying pan, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the cooked noodles to the wok or frying pan, and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes, until the noodles are well coated with the oil and slightly caramelized.
  3. Add the sauce to the noodles, and stir-fry for another minute, until the sauce is well combined with the noodles.
  4. Taste the noodles and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  5. Garnish with chopped cilantro or scallions, if desired.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to help you improve your Chinese scallion oil noodles:

  • Use high-quality noodles: Fresh or high-quality dried noodles will yield a better texture and flavor.
  • Adjust the amount of scallion oil: If you prefer a stronger scallion flavor, you can add more scallion oil to the noodles.
  • Add protein: You can add cooked chicken, beef, or tofu to the noodles for added protein.
  • Experiment with spices: You can add red pepper flakes or Szechuan peppercorns to the noodles for added heat or flavor.

Conclusion

Chinese scallion oil noodles are a simple yet flavorful dish that can be made with just a few ingredients. By following these steps and using high-quality ingredients, you can create a dish that is both delicious and aromatic. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.
