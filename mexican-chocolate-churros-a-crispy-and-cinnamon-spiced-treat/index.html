Mexican Chocolate Churros: A Crispy and Cinnamon-Spiced Treat


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine and have a sweet tooth, then you’re in for a treat with this delightful recipe for Mexican Chocolate Churros. These crispy, cinnamon-spiced delights are the perfect combination of indulgent flavors and satisfying textures. Whether you’re hosting a fiesta or simply craving a special dessert, these churros are sure to impress with their rich chocolate dipping sauce and irresistible aroma of cinnamon.

Ingredients

For the Churros:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

For the Cinnamon Sugar Coating:

  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Chocolate Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for a hint of spice)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chocolate Sauce

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer.
  2. Remove the pan from the heat and add the chocolate chips, stirring until the chocolate is fully melted and the mixture is smooth.
  3. Stir in the ground cinnamon and cayenne pepper (if using), then set the chocolate sauce aside to cool slightly.

Step 2: Make the Churro Dough

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the water, granulated sugar, salt, and 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the mixture reaches a rolling boil, remove the pan from the heat and stir in the all-purpose flour until a smooth dough forms.

Step 3: Fry the Churros

  1. Heat 2 quarts of vegetable oil in a deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat until it reaches 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spoon the churro dough into a piping bag fitted with a large star tip.
  3. Carefully pipe 6-inch lengths of dough directly into the hot oil, using kitchen scissors to cut the dough from the piping tip.
  4. Fry the churros for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy.
  5. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the churros to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 4: Coat the Churros in Cinnamon Sugar

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the granulated sugar and ground cinnamon for the coating.
  2. While the churros are still warm, roll each one in the cinnamon sugar mixture until they are evenly coated.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!

  1. Arrange the freshly coated churros on a serving platter alongside the warm chocolate sauce.
  2. Encourage your guests to dip the churros into the decadent chocolate sauce, savoring each bite of this delightful treat.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, sprinkle the churros with a dusting of powdered sugar before serving.
  • Experiment with different flavors by adding a touch of orange zest or a sprinkle of espresso powder to the chocolate sauce.
  • To make mini churro bites, use a small star tip to pipe the dough into bite-sized pieces before frying.

In Conclusion

Mexican Chocolate Churros are a beloved dessert that combines the warmth of cinnamon, the richness of chocolate, and the irresistible crunch of fried dough. Whether you’re preparing them for a special occasion or simply looking to treat yourself, these churros are a delightful way to indulge in the flavors of Mexico. From their crispy exterior to their decadent chocolate dipping sauce, these churros are sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire. Enjoy the process of making these delightful treats and savor every bite of this delightful, cinnamon-spiced creation.
