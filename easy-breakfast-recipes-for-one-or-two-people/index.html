Easy Breakfast Recipes for One or Two People


Starting your day with a delicious and satisfying breakfast sets the tone for everything that follows. Whether you’re getting ready for a busy workday or having a relaxing weekend morning, a homemade breakfast can give you that energy boost you need. But what if you’re cooking for just one or two people? That doesn’t mean you have to settle for something boring or overly complicated. In fact, cooking breakfast for one or two can be an opportunity to enjoy quick, easy, and healthy dishes with minimal effort.

In this post, we’re going to explore a variety of simple yet tasty breakfast ideas for one or two people. From savory to sweet, these recipes are perfect for a leisurely morning or when you’re pressed for time. Whether you prefer eggs, oatmeal, smoothies, or something a little different, we have something for you!

1. Classic Scrambled Eggs with Toast

Scrambled eggs are a timeless breakfast choice that’s both quick and versatile. You can easily adjust the flavor to match your mood, adding herbs, cheese, or even vegetables. Here’s a simple recipe to start your day right.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1-2 slices of bread (your choice of type)
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, fresh herbs (such as chives or parsley), or a splash of milk for creaminess

Instructions:

  1. Prep the toast: Start by toasting your slices of bread. While the toast is browning, move on to the eggs.
  2. Whisk the eggs: Crack the eggs into a bowl, add a pinch of salt and pepper, and whisk until smooth. If you prefer creamier scrambled eggs, add a teaspoon of milk or cream to the mix.
  3. Cook the eggs: Heat the butter in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Once it’s melted, pour in the eggs. Stir constantly with a spatula, scraping the sides and bottom to prevent sticking. Keep cooking until the eggs are just set but still moist.
  4. Serve: Plate your scrambled eggs next to your freshly toasted bread. Add optional toppings like cheese or herbs for extra flavor.

This dish is filling, nutritious, and easy to make in under 10 minutes.

2. Avocado Toast with Poached Egg

Avocado toast is a modern classic that’s both simple and satisfying. Pairing it with a poached egg adds a touch of elegance and extra protein. It’s perfect for breakfast when you’re looking to feel nourished but not weighed down.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 slices of bread (sourdough or multigrain are great options)
  • Salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes, to taste
  • Optional: lemon juice, olive oil, or garlic powder for added flavor

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread: Start by toasting your bread to your preferred level of crispiness.
  2. Prepare the avocado: While the bread is toasting, cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Mash with a fork until smooth. Add a pinch of salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes for seasoning. For an extra burst of flavor, squeeze in a bit of lemon juice or drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Poach the egg: In a small saucepan, bring water to a gentle simmer. Add a splash of vinegar to help the egg hold its shape. Crack the egg into a small cup or bowl and gently slide it into the simmering water. Let it cook for about 3-4 minutes for a soft, runny yolk. Remove with a slotted spoon.
  4. Assemble: Spread the mashed avocado evenly on the toasted bread. Top with the poached egg, season with more salt and pepper, and add any extra toppings like chili flakes, herbs, or a drizzle of olive oil.

This dish is both nutritious and indulgent, with healthy fats from the avocado and a satisfying protein punch from the egg.

3. Oatmeal with Fresh Fruit and Nuts

Oatmeal is a great option for a filling breakfast that can be customized in many different ways. It’s hearty, warming, and packed with nutrients. If you’re looking for a healthy breakfast that’s also easy to prepare, this oatmeal recipe is perfect.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1 cup milk or water (or a combination)
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • Fresh fruit (like berries, banana, or apple)
  • A handful of nuts or seeds (such as almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, or flaxseeds)
  • A sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the oats: Combine the oats and milk (or water) in a small pot and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oats are soft and have absorbed most of the liquid.
  2. Sweeten the oatmeal: Stir in the honey or maple syrup for a touch of sweetness. If you prefer a more savory oatmeal, you can skip the sweetener and add a pinch of salt instead.
  3. Top the oatmeal: Once the oats are cooked, spoon them into a bowl. Top with your choice of fresh fruit, nuts, and a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg for extra flavor.

Oatmeal is a fantastic breakfast because it’s highly customizable. Feel free to experiment with different toppings like dried fruit, yogurt, or even nut butter.

4. Greek Yogurt Parfait

If you prefer something lighter but still filling, a Greek yogurt parfait is an ideal choice. It combines the creamy richness of yogurt with the crunch of granola and the sweetness of fresh fruit. This is an easy breakfast that’s perfect for a quick bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt (plain or flavored)
  • 1/2 cup granola
  • 1/2 cup fresh fruit (such as strawberries, blueberries, or kiwi)
  • Honey or maple syrup, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Layer the yogurt: In a bowl or glass, start by adding a few spoonfuls of Greek yogurt to the bottom.
  2. Add granola: Sprinkle a layer of granola on top of the yogurt.
  3. Top with fruit: Add a layer of fresh fruit to the parfait. You can mix and match fruits based on what you have available or what’s in season.
  4. Finish with sweetener: Drizzle a bit of honey or maple syrup on top to add some sweetness.

This parfait is not only delicious but also packed with protein from the yogurt and fiber from the fruit and granola.

5. Smoothie Bowl

A smoothie bowl is a fun and refreshing breakfast that’s packed with nutrients. It’s similar to a smoothie, but thicker and served in a bowl with toppings. You can get creative with flavors, fruits, and textures for a breakfast that’s as nutritious as it is visually appealing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 banana (frozen for a thicker texture)
  • 1/2 cup frozen berries (blueberries, strawberries, or mixed berries)
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt or almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon nut butter (peanut butter, almond butter, or cashew butter)
  • Toppings: granola, chia seeds, sliced fruit, coconut flakes, nuts, or honey

Instructions:

  1. Blend the base: In a blender, combine the frozen banana, frozen berries, Greek yogurt (or almond milk), and nut butter. Blend until smooth and creamy. If it’s too thick, add a little more almond milk to reach your desired consistency.
  2. Assemble the bowl: Pour the smoothie into a bowl and top with your choice of granola, fresh fruit, nuts, seeds, and a drizzle of honey.

A smoothie bowl is a great option for a healthy breakfast packed with antioxidants, protein, and fiber.

6. Veggie Omelette

An omelette is a great way to use up leftovers and can be customized with a variety of vegetables, cheeses, and herbs. Whether you’re craving something savory or looking to use up what’s in your fridge, a veggie omelette is always a winning choice.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • A handful of spinach or kale
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or feta work well)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a little olive oil or butter. Add the diced bell peppers, onions, and spinach (or any other veggies you like). Sauté until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Cook the eggs: In a bowl, whisk the eggs with a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the eggs over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet, making sure the eggs spread evenly.
  3. Add cheese: Once the eggs start to set, sprinkle cheese on top. Let it cook for a few more minutes until the eggs are fully set but still tender. Fold the omelette in half and serve immediately.

This veggie omelette is packed with nutrients and can be made with whatever vegetables you have on hand, making it a versatile breakfast option.

7. Pancakes for Two

Who says you can’t enjoy pancakes for breakfast when cooking for just one or two people? These fluffy pancakes are easy to make and can be topped with anything from syrup to fresh fruit or whipped cream.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Optional toppings: syrup, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or nuts

Instructions:

  1. Make the batter: In a bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the milk, egg, and melted butter, and mix until smooth.
  2. Cook the pancakes: Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with butter or oil. Pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown on the other side.
  3. Serve: Stack the pancakes and serve with your favorite toppings, such as maple syrup, fresh berries, or a dollop of whipped cream.

These pancakes are light, fluffy, and a perfect treat for a weekend breakfast or brunch.

Conclusion

Breakfast is an essential meal that helps fuel your day, and with these easy recipes, you can make something delicious for yourself or a partner without spending too much time in the kitchen. Whether you prefer something savory like scrambled eggs or want a light and refreshing smoothie bowl, there are plenty of options to suit your taste and dietary preferences. So, the next time you find yourself cooking for one or two, try one of these simple breakfast ideas and start your day off right!
