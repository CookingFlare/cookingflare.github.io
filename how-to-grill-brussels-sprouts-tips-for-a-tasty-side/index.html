How to Grill Brussels Sprouts: Tips for a Tasty Side


Grilled Brussels sprouts are an incredible way to enjoy this often-underestimated vegetable. The smoky char from the grill, paired with the natural sweetness and mild bitterness of Brussels sprouts, creates a perfectly balanced dish. Whether you’re a Brussels sprout enthusiast or someone who is just starting to appreciate this vegetable, grilling them can unlock a new level of flavor. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the best techniques to grill Brussels sprouts to perfection, ensuring they turn out crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Why Grill Brussels Sprouts?

Before diving into the steps, it’s worth understanding why grilling Brussels sprouts is such a fantastic cooking method. When you grill Brussels sprouts, the high heat caramelizes their natural sugars, creating a crispy, delicious outer layer. The inside of the Brussels sprouts remains tender, resulting in a perfect contrast of textures. The grill also adds a smoky flavor that complements their earthy taste, transforming what is often considered a humble vegetable into a standout side dish for any meal.

Selecting the Best Brussels Sprouts for Grilling

The first step to making sure your grilled Brussels sprouts turn out great is to choose the right sprouts. Freshness is key to a flavorful and tender dish. Here are a few tips to help you pick the best Brussels sprouts:

Look for Firm, Compact Sprouts

Choose Brussels sprouts that are small to medium-sized, as they are typically more tender and flavorful. Larger sprouts may be tougher and take longer to cook. Look for Brussels sprouts that are tightly closed and firm to the touch. Avoid any that are yellowing or have black spots, as this can be an indication of age or spoilage.

Check the Color

Fresh Brussels sprouts should be bright green. While some slight discoloration is normal, particularly at the outer leaves, you want to avoid sprouts that appear brown or overly wilted. The fresher the Brussels sprouts, the better the final dish will taste.

Trim the Ends

When you’re ready to prepare your Brussels sprouts for grilling, trim off the tough stem ends. This will help them cook more evenly and prevent them from burning on the grill. You can also remove any outer leaves that look yellowed or damaged.

Preparing Brussels Sprouts for the Grill

Once you’ve selected and cleaned your Brussels sprouts, it’s time to prepare them for grilling. There are several methods to get your sprouts ready, each offering a slightly different result. Here’s a basic guide to prepping Brussels sprouts for the grill:

Halve or Skewer the Brussels Sprouts

While it’s certainly possible to grill whole Brussels sprouts, cutting them in half or skewering them will help them cook more evenly. Halving them allows for more surface area to be exposed to the grill, which gives you more opportunities for that delicious char and smoky flavor.

To halve the Brussels sprouts, simply use a sharp knife to cut them from top to bottom. Be sure to remove any outer leaves that may fall off during grilling. If you prefer, you can skewer the Brussels sprouts on long metal or wooden skewers. This method makes them easier to flip on the grill and helps prevent them from falling through the grill grates.

Soak Wooden Skewers (If Using)

If you choose to use wooden skewers to grill your Brussels sprouts, be sure to soak them in water for about 30 minutes before grilling. This helps prevent the skewers from burning during cooking.

Seasoning for Grilled Brussels Sprouts

Now comes the fun part—seasoning your Brussels sprouts! Grilled Brussels sprouts are incredibly versatile, and you can go with a variety of flavors depending on your taste preferences. Here’s a simple seasoning blend to get you started:

  1. Olive Oil: Toss your Brussels sprouts in a light coating of olive oil. This helps them brown and crisp up on the grill. It also ensures the seasoning sticks well.
  2. Salt and Pepper: Season with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. These simple ingredients highlight the natural flavor of the sprouts.
  3. Garlic Powder: A touch of garlic powder adds depth and savory richness.
  4. Lemon Juice: A squeeze of fresh lemon juice before serving brightens the flavor and adds a bit of zest to your dish.

If you want to get more creative, here are a few additional seasoning ideas to try:

  • Balsamic Vinegar: A drizzle of balsamic vinegar after grilling brings out a tangy, slightly sweet flavor that pairs beautifully with the smokiness of the sprouts.
  • Bacon Bits: If you’re a fan of bacon, crumble some crispy bacon on top of your Brussels sprouts before serving for a savory crunch.
  • Parmesan Cheese: Grated Parmesan cheese adds a salty, umami kick to your Brussels sprouts and melts slightly when added to the warm grilled vegetables.
  • Chili Flakes: For a spicy kick, sprinkle red chili flakes on your Brussels sprouts after grilling.

Marinate for Extra Flavor

If you have extra time, marinating your Brussels sprouts before grilling can infuse them with even more flavor. Simply combine olive oil, vinegar (such as balsamic or apple cider), garlic, salt, and pepper in a bowl, then toss the Brussels sprouts in the mixture. Let them marinate in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to an hour before grilling.

Grilling Brussels Sprouts: The Perfect Technique

Grilling Brussels sprouts is straightforward, but there are a few important techniques to ensure they turn out perfect every time. Whether you’re using a gas or charcoal grill, these tips will help you achieve that crispy, smoky flavor.

Preheat the Grill

Before placing your Brussels sprouts on the grill, make sure to preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a charcoal grill, let the coals burn down until they’re covered in a layer of white ash. This provides consistent heat for grilling your Brussels sprouts.

Use a Grill Basket or Directly on the Grates

One of the best tools for grilling Brussels sprouts is a grill basket. The basket keeps the sprouts from falling through the grates while allowing the smoke and heat to circulate around them. If you don’t have a grill basket, you can place the Brussels sprouts directly on the grill grates, but be sure to turn them regularly to prevent burning.

Grill Over Medium Heat

Once your grill is preheated, place your Brussels sprouts on the grill over medium heat. If you’re grilling whole sprouts, cook them for about 6-8 minutes per side, turning occasionally. For halved Brussels sprouts, they will likely cook in about 4-5 minutes per side. The goal is to get a nice char on the outside while cooking them through on the inside.

Monitor the Temperature

Grilled Brussels sprouts can cook quickly, so it’s important to monitor their temperature. You want them to be tender when pierced with a fork but not overcooked to the point of becoming mushy. Aim for a nice crisp exterior with a soft, flavorful interior.

Keep the Lid Closed

When grilling Brussels sprouts, it’s helpful to keep the lid closed for most of the cooking time. This helps maintain a consistent cooking temperature and allows the Brussels sprouts to cook more evenly. Just be sure to check on them occasionally to prevent burning.

How to Serve Grilled Brussels Sprouts

Grilled Brussels sprouts are a versatile side dish that can complement a wide range of main courses. They pair well with:

  • Grilled meats: Whether you’re grilling chicken, steak, or pork, Brussels sprouts make a perfect accompaniment. The smoky flavor of the sprouts meshes wonderfully with grilled meats.
  • Roasted vegetables: For a complete meal, pair your Brussels sprouts with other roasted vegetables such as carrots, sweet potatoes, or cauliflower.
  • Salads: Grilled Brussels sprouts can be a great addition to salads, either as the base or as a topping. They provide an earthy contrast to lighter greens like arugula or spinach.
  • Rice or quinoa: Serve grilled Brussels sprouts over a bed of rice or quinoa for a hearty and satisfying side dish.

Garnishing Your Grilled Brussels Sprouts

To elevate the dish further, consider garnishing your grilled Brussels sprouts with a drizzle of balsamic glaze, a sprinkle of fresh herbs like parsley or thyme, or a bit of crumbled feta or goat cheese. These garnishes add extra flavor and visual appeal to the dish.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Grilling Brussels sprouts can be tricky if you’re not familiar with the process, but don’t worry—here are some common issues and how to solve them:

Issue: Brussels Sprouts Are Too Bitter

Brussels sprouts can sometimes have a slightly bitter taste, especially if they’re overcooked. To reduce bitterness, be sure to cook your Brussels sprouts just until they’re tender but still bright green. The caramelization from grilling will also balance out the bitterness.

Issue: Brussels Sprouts Are Too Dry

If your Brussels sprouts are turning out dry, it’s likely that they’re overcooking on the grill. Make sure to monitor the temperature and turn them regularly. You can also brush them with a bit more olive oil before grilling to keep them moist.

Issue: Brussels Sprouts Are Soggy

Soggy Brussels sprouts are usually a result of over-marinating or overcrowding on the grill. Ensure that there’s enough space between each Brussels sprout for air circulation and even cooking. If you’re marinating them, avoid leaving them in the marinade for too long.

Final Thoughts

Grilled Brussels sprouts are a game-changing way to enjoy this nutritious vegetable. With the right technique and seasoning, they can become a delicious and versatile side dish that will impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. Whether you enjoy them as a simple, crispy treat or as part of a more elaborate meal, grilled Brussels sprouts are bound to become a staple in your grilling repertoire. Happy grilling!
