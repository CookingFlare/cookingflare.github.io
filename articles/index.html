10 Flavorful Spring Soup Recipes to Warm Your Soul

Spring is a season of renewal, where nature awakens and brings forth a bounty of fresh ingredients. As the weather begins to warm up, there’s nothing quite like a comforting bowl of soup to celebrate the flavors of the season. Whether you’re looking for something light and refreshing or hearty and satisfying, these ten spring soup recipes are sure to warm your soul and tantalize your taste buds. 1. Asparagus and Pea Soup Ingredients: 1 lb fresh asparagus, trimmed and chopped 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen) 1 medium onion, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup heavy cream (optional) Salt and pepper to taste Fresh mint leaves for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic in a drizzle of olive oil until translucent. Add the chopped asparagus and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Add the peas and cook for an additional 5 minutes until tender. Using an immersion blender, puree the soup until smooth. If you prefer a creamier texture, stir in the heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper and garnish with fresh mint leaves before serving. 2. Spring Vegetable Soup Ingredients: 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 cup carrots, diced 1 cup celery, diced 1 cup zucchini, diced 1 cup green beans, chopped 1 cup baby spinach 6 cups vegetable broth 1 teaspoon thyme Salt and pepper to taste Lemon wedges for serving Instructions: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the carrots and celery, sautéing until softened. Add the zucchini and green beans, cooking for another 5 minutes. Pour in the vegetable broth and stir in the thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes. Stir in the baby spinach and cook until wilted. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for added brightness. 3. Creamy Spinach and Potato Soup Ingredients: 3 cups fresh spinach, chopped 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup milk or plant-based alternative 2 tablespoons butter Salt and pepper to taste Grated Parmesan for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant. Add the diced potatoes and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until the potatoes are tender. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. Use an immersion blender to puree until smooth. Stir in the milk and season with salt and pepper. Serve topped with grated Parmesan. 4. Carrot and Ginger Soup Ingredients: 1 lb carrots, peeled and chopped 1 onion, diced 2 tablespoons fresh ginger, grated 4 cups vegetable broth 1 tablespoon olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Coconut cream for drizzling Instructions: Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until soft. Stir in the carrots and ginger, cooking for about 5 minutes. Add the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Lower heat and simmer until the carrots are tender. Blend the soup until smooth and season with salt and pepper. Serve with a drizzle of coconut cream for an extra touch of flavor. 5. Tomato Basil Soup Ingredients: 2 cans (14 oz each) diced tomatoes 1 cup fresh basil leaves 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 cups vegetable broth 1 tablespoon olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Croutons for serving Instructions: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened. Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes. Add the fresh basil and blend until smooth. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, topped with croutons for added crunch. 6. Lemon Chicken Orzo Soup Ingredients: 1 lb chicken breast, cooked and shredded 1 cup orzo pasta 1 onion, diced 2 carrots, diced 2 celery stalks, diced 6 cups chicken broth 1 lemon, juiced and zested 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Fresh dill for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until tender. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Stir in the orzo and cook according to package instructions. Once the orzo is cooked, add the shredded chicken, lemon juice, and zest. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, garnished with fresh dill for a burst of flavor. 7. Creamy Mushroom Soup Ingredients: 1 lb mushrooms, sliced 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup heavy cream 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Fresh parsley for garnish Instructions: Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until soft. Stir in the mushrooms and cook until they release their juices and become tender. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cook for 10 minutes. Blend the soup until smooth, then stir in the heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley. 8. Spicy Lentil Soup Ingredients: 1 cup red lentils, rinsed 1 onion, chopped 2 carrots, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 teaspoon cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1 tablespoon olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Fresh cilantro for garnish Instructions: In a pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and carrots, sautéing until soft. Stir in the cumin and paprika, cooking for an additional minute. Add the lentils and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes until lentils are tender. Blend the soup if desired for a smoother texture. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. 9. Pea and Mint Soup Ingredients: 4 cups fresh or frozen peas 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup fresh mint leaves 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Crème fraîche for serving (optional) Instructions: Heat olive oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until soft. Add the peas and vegetable broth, bringing to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Stir in the fresh mint leaves and blend until smooth. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot with a dollop of crème fraîche for creaminess. 10. Roasted Red Pepper Soup Ingredients: 4 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 4 cups vegetable broth 1 cup heavy cream (optional) 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Fresh basil for garnish Instructions: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until soft. Add the roasted red peppers and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Blend until smooth and stir in the heavy cream if desired. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil. From the earthy notes of asparagus and peas to the refreshing brightness of lemon and herbs, these soups are not just meals; they are a warm embrace for your soul. Enjoy cooking and sharing these soups with loved ones as you welcome the beauty of spring into your kitchen! ...
