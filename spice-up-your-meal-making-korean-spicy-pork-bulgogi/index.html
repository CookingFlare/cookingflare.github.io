Spice Up Your Meal: Making Korean Spicy Pork Bulgogi


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and spicy kick. One of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Korean cooking is Bulgogi, a sweet and savory marinated beef dish that has gained worldwide popularity. However, today we’re going to spice things up by giving Bulgogi a bold twist with the addition of spicy Korean chili flakes, known as gochugaru.

What is Bulgogi?

Bulgogi is a traditional Korean dish that literally translates to “fire meat.” It’s a classic Korean marinade made with a sweet and savory mixture of soy sauce, sugar, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, and black pepper. The marinade is applied to thinly sliced beef, typically ribeye or sirloin, which is then grilled or stir-fried to perfection.

The Spicy Twist: Adding Gochugaru

Gochugaru, also known as Korean chili flakes, is a staple ingredient in Korean cooking. It adds a depth of heat and a burst of flavor to any dish. For this recipe, we’re going to add gochugaru to the marinade to give the dish a spicy kick.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork shoulder or pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together soy sauce, gochugaru, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and black pepper until well combined.
  2. Add the sliced pork to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Remove the pork from the marinade, letting any excess liquid drip off.
  4. Add the pork to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until browned and cooked through.
  5. Remove the pork from the skillet and transfer to a serving platter. Garnish with sesame seeds, green onions, and cilantro.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations:

  • For an extra crispy texture, try pan-frying the pork after grilling or stir-frying.
  • Substitute pork with beef or chicken for a different twist.
  • Add some crunch by sprinkling toasted sesame seeds on top of the dish.
  • For a spicier version, add more gochugaru to the marinade or serve with a side of gochujang sauce.
  • Experiment with different types of chili flakes, such as Korean chili paste (gochujang) or Szechuan peppercorns, for a unique flavor profile.

Conclusion:

Korean Spicy Pork Bulgogi is a flavorful and fiery twist on the classic Korean dish. The addition of gochugaru gives the dish a bold and spicy kick that’s sure to tantalize your taste buds. With its sweet and savory marinade, crispy texture, and vibrant colors, this dish is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So, go ahead and spice up your meal with this Korean-inspired recipe!
