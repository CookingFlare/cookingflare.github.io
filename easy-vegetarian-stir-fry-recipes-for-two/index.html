Easy Vegetarian Stir-Fry Recipes for Two


When life gets busy, there’s nothing like a quick and easy stir-fry to save dinner time while keeping things nutritious and delicious. Stir-frying is a fantastic cooking method—it’s fast, versatile, and bursts with vibrant colors and flavors. If you’re cooking for two and want simple vegetarian meals that don’t skimp on taste, these easy stir-fry recipes will quickly become staples in your recipe box.

Why Choose Vegetarian Stir-Fries?

Vegetarian stir-fries are not just meatless; they’re power-packed with fresh vegetables, aromatic herbs, and an array of plant-based proteins. Vegetables such as bell peppers, snap peas, mushrooms, and broccoli become tender yet retain their crunch. Moreover, the short cooking time preserves nutrients and yields vibrant, colorful dishes. These recipes are perfect for intimate dinners, weeknight meals, or when you want to impress without the fuss.

Essential Tips for Perfect Stir-Fries

Before diving into the recipes, here are a few tips to elevate your stir-fry game:

  1. Prep Everything First: Stir-frying is rapid, so chop all your veggies, prepare sauces, and have your protein ready beforehand.
  2. High Heat is Key: Use a wok or a large skillet on high heat to sear vegetables quickly, locking in their flavor and texture.
  3. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Cook in batches if necessary. Overcrowding lowers the pan’s temperature and causes steaming instead of frying.
  4. Balance Flavors: A good stir-fry strikes a balance between salty, sweet, tangy, and umami. Ingredients like soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and sesame oil help achieve that delicious harmony.
  5. Choose the Right Protein: Tofu, tempeh, seitan, or edamame are excellent vegetarian options that complement vegetables well.

Recipe 1: Tempeh and Vegetable Stir-Fry with Hoisin-Ginger Sauce

Ingredients

  • 200g tempeh, cut into thin strips
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced thin
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat.
  2. Add tempeh strips and stir-fry until golden and crispy on the edges, about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add garlic and ginger to the pan; stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Toss in bell peppers, snap peas, carrot, and green onions. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until veggies are tender-crisp.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil together.
  6. Return tempeh to the pan and pour the sauce over it. Stir well to coat everything evenly.
  7. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  8. Serve over steamed jasmine rice and garnish with sesame seeds.

Recipe 2: Spicy Tofu and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients

  • 200g firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red chili, sliced thin (deseeded for less heat)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tsp chili paste (optional for extra heat)
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or honey
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • Cooked brown rice or noodles, to serve

Instructions

  1. Press tofu to remove excess moisture, then cube it.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Add tofu cubes, cook without stirring for 3 minutes for a crispy crust, then turn and cook other sides until golden.
  3. Remove tofu and set aside.
  4. To the same pan, add garlic, ginger, and chili. Stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  5. Add broccoli and a splash of water to create steam. Cover and cook for 3 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp.
  6. In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, hoisin sauce, chili paste, maple syrup, and sesame oil.
  7. Return tofu to the pan and pour sauce over the vegetables and tofu. Toss gently to combine.
  8. Cook for 2 more minutes, allowing flavors to meld.
  9. Serve hot over brown rice or noodles.

Recipe 3: Sweet and Sour Mushroom Stir-Fry with Cashews

Ingredients

  • 1 cup button mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or peanut)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp pineapple juice
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup roasted cashews
  • Cooked quinoa or rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and ginger; sauté for 2 minutes until onions soften.
  3. Toss in mushrooms, cauliflower, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 5 minutes until veggies are just tender.
  4. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, pineapple juice, rice vinegar, and brown sugar; stir until sugar dissolves.
  5. Pour sauce into the pan; stir well to coat vegetables.
  6. Cook a further 2 minutes as sauce thickens.
  7. Remove from heat, sprinkle cashews over the top.
  8. Serve alongside cooked quinoa or rice.

Bonus: Simple Stir-Fry Sauce You Can Whisk Up Anytime

If you enjoy customizing your stir-fries, keep this versatile stir-fry sauce handy for mixing and matching with any vegetable or protein combo.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup or honey
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger

Instructions

  1. Combine all ingredients in a small bowl.
  2. Whisk until well blended.
  3. Add to vegetables and protein during final stages of cooking.
  4. Stir until sauce thickens and coats everything evenly.

Pair Your Stir-Fry with These Sides

  • Steamed Jasmine or Brown Rice: A classic base that soaks up sauce.
  • Noodles: Egg-free rice noodles or soba noodles work beautifully.
  • Fresh Herbs: Cilantro, basil, or green onion add a fresh burst.
  • Pickled Vegetables: Add crunch and acidity to contrast rich stir-fry flavors.

Wrapping Up: Quick and Nutritious for Two

Preparing a vibrant vegetarian stir-fry for two doesn’t need to be time-consuming or complicated. With fresh ingredients, simple sauces, and quick cooking techniques, dining at home becomes a delightful and healthful experience. Each recipe can be easily adapted to your pantry’s contents or seasonal produce, making stir-fry a flexible go-to option. Plus, leftovers keep well and make fantastic next-day lunches.

Next time you’re craving something flavorful and easy, try these vegetarian stir-fry recipes. Your taste buds—and your schedule—will thank you.
