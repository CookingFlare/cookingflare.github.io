Dominican Republic Cooking: 10 Easy Recipes for Beginners


The Dominican Republic boasts a rich culinary tradition that reflects its diverse cultural influences, blending African, Spanish, and Taino flavors to create dishes full of vibrant colors, hearty flavors, and aromatic spices. Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or someone looking to expand your culinary horizons, Dominican cooking offers a fantastic starting point. From savory stews to delicious rice dishes, you don’t need to be a professional chef to enjoy the flavors of the Dominican Republic. In this post, we’ll guide you through ten easy-to-make recipes that will bring the taste of the island straight to your table.

1. Mangu (Mashed Plantains)

A quintessential Dominican breakfast, mangu is a simple yet flavorful dish made from mashed green plantains. It’s commonly served with fried eggs, salami, and cheese, making it a filling and satisfying start to the day.

Ingredients:

  • 3 green plantains
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Peel the plantains and cut them into 2-inch pieces. Place them in a pot of water and bring to a boil.
  2. Cook for about 15 minutes or until the plantains are soft and tender.
  3. Drain the plantains and place them in a bowl. Add the butter and mash with a fork or potato masher until smooth. If the mixture feels too thick, you can add water, a little at a time, until you reach the desired consistency.
  4. Add salt to taste and serve hot with your choice of sides, like fried eggs, salami, or cheese.

This dish is so easy to make, and the creamy, slightly sweet plantains pair wonderfully with savory sides.

2. Arroz con Pollo (Rice with Chicken)

No Dominican meal is complete without arroz con pollo. This dish is a Dominican classic, featuring tender chicken cooked with seasoned rice. The secret to a flavorful arroz con pollo lies in the spices, which include garlic, cilantro, and a hint of saffron for that signature golden color.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs, bone-in, skinless
  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads (or turmeric for a budget-friendly option)
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chicken thighs, seasoning them with salt and pepper, and cook until browned on all sides. Remove from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onions, garlic, and bell pepper until soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the rice and mix it with the sautéed vegetables. Stir in the saffron or turmeric, and cook for another minute.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Add the chicken thighs back into the pot, along with the peas and carrots.
  5. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot.

Arroz con pollo is a perfect one-pot meal that’s both comforting and full of flavor.

3. Sancocho (Dominican Stew)

Sancocho is a beloved Dominican stew that combines different meats, vegetables, and spices for a rich, hearty dish. It’s perfect for large gatherings and can be customized with your choice of meat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef stew meat
  • 1 lb chicken (bone-in)
  • 2 ears of corn, cut into thirds
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 yuca roots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1/2 pound plantains, peeled and sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat. Brown the beef stew meat and chicken pieces, then remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté the onions and garlic until fragrant. Add the tomatoes and cook until they soften, then stir in the tomato paste, oregano, and thyme.
  3. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Return the meat to the pot, along with the corn, potatoes, yuca, and plantains.
  4. Lower the heat and simmer for 1-2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve hot.

Sancocho is a deliciously filling meal that is perfect for sharing with family and friends. The combination of meats and vegetables makes it a comforting, well-rounded dish.

4. Mofongo (Mashed Plantains with Garlic)

Another Dominican favorite, mofongo is made by mashing fried plantains with garlic, olive oil, and pork cracklings (chicharrón). It’s traditionally served with a flavorful broth or meat.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe plantains
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chicharrón (fried pork skin)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup chicken broth (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice the plantains into 1-inch pieces. Fry them in hot oil until golden brown.
  2. Once fried, drain the plantains and place them in a mortar and pestle or a bowl.
  3. Add the garlic, chicharrón, and olive oil to the plantains. Mash everything together until it becomes a smooth, slightly chunky paste.
  4. Season with salt to taste, and if desired, serve with a side of chicken broth for dipping.

Mofongo is a versatile dish that can be served as a side or as a main course, often paired with seafood or stewed meats.

5. Tostones (Fried Green Plantains)

Tostones are a crunchy and savory snack made from green plantains that are twice fried. They are a popular appetizer or side dish, perfect for dipping in garlic sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 green plantains
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (for garlic sauce)

Instructions:

  1. Peel the plantains and slice them into 1-inch rounds.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the plantain slices until golden, about 3 minutes per side.
  3. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  4. Use the back of a spoon to flatten each piece into a disk shape. Return the flattened plantains to the oil and fry again until they are crispy, about 2 minutes per side.
  5. In a separate pan, heat olive oil and sauté the garlic until fragrant. Pour over the tostones and sprinkle with salt.

Tostones are the perfect combination of crispy and savory, and they are ideal when paired with a garlicky dipping sauce.

6. Pescado Frito (Fried Fish)

Dominican cuisine features a variety of seafood dishes, and pescado frito is one of the most popular. This dish consists of whole fish fried to perfection, often served with a tangy sauce and accompanied by rice and beans.

Ingredients:

  • 2 whole fish (snapper, tilapia, or any firm white fish)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Clean and gut the fish, if not already done by your fishmonger. Rub the fish with garlic, lime juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Fry the fish for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until golden and crispy.
  3. Serve with rice and beans, and garnish with a fresh lime wedge.

Fried fish is a simple yet flavorful dish that brings out the natural sweetness of the fish, making it a perfect main course for any meal.

7. Chicharrón de Pollo (Fried Chicken Chunks)

This dish consists of crispy, golden chicken chunks that are marinated in a delicious blend of Dominican spices before being deep-fried. It’s typically served as a snack or appetizer at parties or casual gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, marinate the chicken pieces with garlic, vinegar, oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper. Let sit for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Fry the chicken pieces until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels before serving.

Chicharrón de pollo is a tasty, crispy delight that’s sure to be a hit with anyone who enjoys fried foods.

8. Habichuelas Guisadas (Stewed Beans)

A classic Dominican side dish, habichuelas guisadas are stewed red beans cooked with garlic, onions, and bell peppers. This hearty, flavorful dish is often served with rice and meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried red beans (or 2 cans of cooked beans)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. If using dried beans, soak them overnight and cook them until tender. If using canned beans, drain and rinse them.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot and sauté the onions, bell pepper, and garlic until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, cumin, oregano, and cooked beans. Add chicken broth and bring to a simmer.
  4. Let the beans simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot with rice.

Habichuelas guisadas are a deliciously comforting dish that pairs perfectly with a variety of Dominican mains.

9. Yuca con Mojo (Yuca with Garlic Sauce)

Yuca con mojo is a traditional Dominican dish made with yuca, a starchy root vegetable, boiled and served with a garlic sauce. This side dish is simple but full of flavor, making it a popular accompaniment to meats and stews.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb yuca, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the yuca in a pot of salted water for 20-30 minutes, or until tender.
  2. While the yuca cooks, prepare the garlic sauce by heating olive oil in a small pan and sautéing the minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. Add lime juice and salt to the garlic, stirring to combine.
  4. Drain the yuca and serve with the garlic sauce drizzled over the top.

Yuca con mojo is a tasty, flavorful side that brings out the natural sweetness of the yuca and complements any main course.

10. Pastelón (Dominican Sweet Plantain Casserole)

A pastelón is a savory-sweet casserole made with layers of sweet plantains, ground beef, and cheese. It’s similar to a lasagna, but with a tropical twist!

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe plantains, peeled and sliced
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (mozzarella or cheddar)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Fry the sliced plantains in hot oil until golden, then drain them on paper towels.
  2. In a separate pan, cook the ground beef with onions, bell peppers, and garlic. Add the tomato sauce, oregano, and season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a baking dish, layer the fried plantains, followed by the beef mixture, and then cheese.
  4. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of cheese on top.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F for 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Pastelón is a hearty and satisfying dish that beautifully combines the sweetness of plantains with the savory meat filling, making it perfect for dinner.

With these ten easy recipes, you can start bringing the flavors of the Dominican Republic into your kitchen. From the comforting and filling sancocho to the savory sweetness of pastelón, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or feeding a crowd, these dishes are sure to impress and bring a taste of the Caribbean to your home. Happy cooking!
