10 Delicious Ways to Use Sweet Potatoes This Fall


As the crisp air and vibrant colors of fall settle in, there’s no better time to enjoy the hearty, comforting flavor of sweet potatoes. With their naturally sweet taste and versatility, sweet potatoes can easily be incorporated into your fall meals. Whether you’re preparing a savory side dish, a sweet dessert, or something in between, sweet potatoes offer endless possibilities to enhance your autumn table.

In this post, we’ll explore 10 delicious and creative ways to use sweet potatoes this fall. These recipes will not only keep you warm as the weather cools down but also offer a nutritious and flavorful twist on your usual fall dishes. From roasted to mashed, baked to grilled, there’s a perfect sweet potato recipe for every craving.

1. Roasted Sweet Potatoes with Maple and Cinnamon

If you’re looking for a simple yet flavorful side dish, roasted sweet potatoes with maple syrup and cinnamon are a perfect choice. Roasting sweet potatoes brings out their natural sweetness while adding a rich caramelized flavor. To make this dish:

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed sweet potatoes in olive oil, maple syrup, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the sweet potatoes in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until they are tender and golden brown.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy the sweet, spicy goodness!

This dish works wonderfully as a side for roast chicken, pork, or a fall salad. The combination of maple syrup and cinnamon creates a warming, comforting flavor perfect for the fall season.

2. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Tacos are a favorite meal year-round, and sweet potatoes make a great filling for a fall twist on this Mexican classic. The sweetness of the roasted sweet potatoes pairs perfectly with the savory black beans and the crunch of fresh toppings. Here’s how to make them:

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, cilantro, lime wedges, salsa, or cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss the diced sweet potatoes in olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them evenly on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are soft and slightly caramelized.
  4. Warm the black beans in a saucepan over medium heat.
  5. To assemble, warm the corn tortillas and fill them with the roasted sweet potatoes and black beans. Top with your favorite toppings and serve with lime wedges for an extra burst of freshness.

These tacos are perfect for a casual fall dinner and are an excellent way to enjoy sweet potatoes in a savory, satisfying dish.

3. Sweet Potato Soup with Coconut Milk

A warm bowl of soup is ideal for chilly fall evenings, and this creamy sweet potato soup made with coconut milk is both comforting and nourishing. The coconut milk adds a velvety texture that complements the natural sweetness of the potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can coconut milk (14 oz)
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until softened.
  2. Add the cubed sweet potatoes, vegetable broth, and ginger. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  3. Add the coconut milk and stir to combine. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth and creamy (or carefully transfer to a blender in batches).
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh herbs or a drizzle of coconut milk.

This soup is a deliciously warming fall meal that’s perfect for a cozy evening. It’s rich, creamy, and full of flavor, making it a great way to use sweet potatoes in a comforting dish.

4. Sweet Potato and Kale Salad

For a lighter yet satisfying fall dish, a sweet potato and kale salad is a great choice. The bitterness of the kale balances perfectly with the sweetness of the roasted sweet potatoes, and a tangy vinaigrette ties everything together.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 bunch kale, stems removed and torn into pieces
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup toasted pecans or walnuts (optional)
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the sweet potato cubes in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender.
  3. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, prepare the vinaigrette by whisking together olive oil, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  4. Massage the kale with a little bit of olive oil and salt to soften the leaves.
  5. Once the sweet potatoes are done, toss them with the kale and vinaigrette. Top with toasted pecans and dried cranberries for added texture and flavor.

This salad is a fantastic side dish or light main course that brings together the best flavors of fall.

5. Sweet Potato Casserole with Marshmallows

A classic fall dish, sweet potato casserole with marshmallows is always a crowd-pleaser. It’s rich, sweet, and indulgent, making it the perfect comfort food for a Thanksgiving dinner or a cozy family meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 cups mini marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil the cubed sweet potatoes in a pot of water until tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain and mash them with a potato masher.
  3. Add melted butter, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla extract, and milk to the mashed sweet potatoes. Stir until smooth and well combined.
  4. Transfer the sweet potato mixture to a baking dish and top with mini marshmallows.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the marshmallows are golden brown and the casserole is hot.

This classic casserole is a perfect balance of sweet and savory, with a fluffy, marshmallow-topped finish that’s sure to be a hit at any fall gathering.

6. Sweet Potato Hash with Eggs

For a hearty breakfast or brunch, sweet potato hash with eggs is an easy, satisfying dish. The sweet potatoes are sautéed with onions, bell peppers, and spices, then topped with fried eggs for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced sweet potatoes and cook for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally until they start to soften.
  2. Add the bell pepper and onion to the skillet, along with paprika, salt, and pepper. Continue cooking for another 5-7 minutes until the sweet potatoes are fully tender and the vegetables are softened.
  3. In a separate skillet, fry the eggs to your desired doneness.
  4. Serve the sweet potato hash with a fried egg on top.

This dish is a great way to start your fall mornings with a warm, filling meal that’s packed with flavor.

7. Sweet Potato Pie

Sweet potato pie is a fall favorite that rivals pumpkin pie in flavor and richness. It’s a sweet and spiced dessert that pairs beautifully with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and boiled
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 pre-made pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mash the boiled sweet potatoes and place them in a large mixing bowl.
  3. Add sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, vanilla extract, evaporated milk, and eggs. Mix until smooth and well combined.
  4. Pour the sweet potato mixture into the pie crust and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and a knife inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Let the pie cool before serving with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of ice cream.

Sweet potato pie is a beloved dessert that’s perfect for fall gatherings, offering a rich, spiced flavor with a buttery, flaky crust.

8. Grilled Sweet Potato Fries

If you’re in the mood for a healthier alternative to regular fries, grilled sweet potato fries are a fantastic option. They have a smoky flavor from the grill and are perfect for pairing with your favorite dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into fries
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the sweet potato fries in olive oil, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the fries for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and slightly charred.
  4. Serve with your favorite dipping sauce, such as ketchup or aioli.

Grilled sweet potato fries are a great side dish for burgers, grilled meats, or as a tasty snack.

9. Sweet Potato Pancakes

Sweet potato pancakes are a delicious and unique twist on the classic breakfast favorite. The sweet potatoes add a rich flavor and soft texture, making these pancakes the perfect way to start your fall mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mashed sweet potatoes
  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the mashed sweet potatoes, flour, baking powder, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the milk, eggs, and melted butter.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until smooth.
  4. Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Pour about 1/4 cup of the pancake batter onto the griddle and cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for another 2-3 minutes until golden brown.
  5. Serve with maple syrup and butter.

These sweet potato pancakes are perfect for a cozy fall breakfast or brunch, offering a deliciously sweet and spiced flavor.

10. Sweet Potato Smoothie

For a healthy and filling breakfast or snack, a sweet potato smoothie is a great way to incorporate this nutrient-packed vegetable into your day. It’s a creamy, flavorful drink that’s perfect for fall.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup cooked and cooled sweet potato
  • 1/2 banana
  • 1/2 cup almond milk (or your preferred milk)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ice cubes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine all the ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth.
  2. Pour into a glass and enjoy!

This smoothie is a nutritious way to start your fall day or enjoy as an afternoon snack.

Sweet potatoes are a versatile, nutrient-rich ingredient that can be used in so many different ways. Whether you prefer them sweet or savory, roasted, mashed, or in soups, these 10 recipes will help you enjoy the best of sweet potatoes this fall.
