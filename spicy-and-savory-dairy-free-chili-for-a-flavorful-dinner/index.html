Spicy and Savory Dairy-Free Chili for a Flavorful Dinner


If you’re looking for a delicious and dairy-free option for dinner tonight, look no further than this Spicy and Savory Dairy-Free Chili recipe. Packed with bold flavors and hearty ingredients, this chili is sure to satisfy your cravings while keeping you warm and cozy. Plus, it’s incredibly easy to make!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1 pound ground beef (or substitute with ground turkey for a leaner option)
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (14 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, red bell pepper, green bell pepper, and jalapeño pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.

  2. Push the vegetables to one side of the pot and add the ground beef (or ground turkey) to the other side. Cook the meat until browned and cooked through, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.

  3. Once the meat is cooked, mix it together with the vegetables in the pot. Add the chili powder, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir well to coat everything evenly.

  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, kidney beans, and black beans. Stir to combine all the ingredients. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Bring the chili to a simmer and let it cook uncovered for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the chili to thicken slightly.

  6. After 30 minutes, taste and adjust the seasonings if needed. If you prefer a spicier chili, you can add more cayenne pepper at this point.

  7. Serve the spicy and savory dairy-free chili hot, garnished with your favorite toppings such as chopped fresh cilantro, diced avocado, or a dollop of dairy-free sour cream. This chili pairs perfectly with warm cornbread or crusty bread.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, omit the ground beef or turkey and add an extra can of beans or your favorite meat substitute.
  • If you prefer a milder chili, reduce the amount of chili powder and cayenne pepper.
  • Feel free to customize your chili with additional vegetables such as corn, zucchini, or carrots.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for future meals.

So there you have it – a delicious and dairy-free chili recipe that will surely become a favorite for your flavorful dinner nights. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a comforting meal, this spicy and savory chili is a winner. Enjoy the robust flavors, the satisfying textures, and the warmth it brings to your table.
