Chinese Mapo Tofu: Spicy and Silky Soft Tofu Recipes


Mapo Tofu is a classic Sichuan dish that has gained popularity all over the world. With its bold and spicy flavors, combined with the delicate and silky texture of the tofu, it’s no wonder that Mapo Tofu has become a favorite among both vegetarians and meat lovers alike. In this recipe, we will explore how to make an authentic and mouthwatering Mapo Tofu dish that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 block of soft tofu
  • 200 grams of ground pork (substitute with mushrooms for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of doubanjiang (fermented broad bean paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of chili bean paste
  • 1 tablespoon of Sichuan peppercorns
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, minced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of cornstarch, mixed with 2 tablespoons of water
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the tofu by cutting it into small cubes and set aside. If you prefer a firmer texture, you can blanch the tofu in boiling water for a few minutes before using.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns, and sauté until fragrant.

  3. Add the ground pork (or mushrooms) to the pan and cook until browned. Make sure to break up any clumps of meat or mushrooms with a spatula.

  4. Once the meat is cooked, add the doubanjiang and chili bean paste to the pan and stir well to combine. These two ingredients are the key to the spicy and flavorful base of Mapo Tofu.

  5. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, followed by the soy sauce and sugar. Stir everything together and let the mixture simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  6. Gently add the tofu cubes to the pan, being careful not to break them. Simmer the tofu in the sauce for about 5-7 minutes, or until the tofu is heated through.

  7. Finally, stir in the cornstarch mixture to thicken the sauce. Continue to cook for another minute or two until the sauce has thickened to your desired consistency.

  8. Taste the Mapo Tofu and adjust the seasoning with salt if needed. Remember that the doubanjiang and chili bean paste add a lot of flavor, so be cautious with the salt.

  9. Remove the Mapo Tofu from the heat and garnish with chopped green onions.

Serving Suggestions

Mapo Tofu is traditionally served with steamed rice, which helps balance out the spiciness of the dish. You can also serve it with noodles or enjoy it on its own as a flavorful and satisfying main course.

Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the ground pork with mushrooms or textured vegetable protein (TVP).
  • If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of doubanjiang and chili bean paste.
  • For an extra kick, sprinkle some crushed Sichuan peppercorns on top before serving.

Conclusion

Chinese Mapo Tofu is a dish that embodies the bold and vibrant flavors of Sichuan cuisine. Its spicy and silky soft tofu is a true delight for the taste buds. With this recipe, you can recreate the authentic flavors of Mapo Tofu in the comfort of your own kitchen. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or just looking to expand your culinary horizons, give this recipe a try and experience the magic of Mapo Tofu for yourself.
