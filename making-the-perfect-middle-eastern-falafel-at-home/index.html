Making the Perfect Middle Eastern Falafel at Home


Falafel is a beloved Middle Eastern dish that is enjoyed by people all over the world. These crispy, flavorful chickpea fritters are a staple of Middle Eastern cuisine and can be enjoyed on their own, stuffed into pita bread, or added to salads. While you can easily find falafel at restaurants or street food stalls, there’s something truly special about making them at home. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making the perfect Middle Eastern falafel in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas
  • 1/2 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

Step 1: Soak the Chickpeas

Start by soaking the dried chickpeas in cold water for at least 12 hours or overnight. This will help soften the chickpeas and make them easier to blend.

Step 2: Drain and Rinse

After soaking, drain and rinse the chickpeas thoroughly. This ensures that any impurities are removed and helps create a smoother texture for the falafel.

Step 3: Blend the Ingredients

In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, onion, parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and baking powder. Pulse the ingredients until they are well combined and form a coarse mixture. Be careful not to over-blend, as we want the falafel to have some texture.

Step 4: Rest the Mixture

Transfer the mixture into a bowl, cover it, and refrigerate it for at least 1 hour. This resting time allows the flavors to meld together and makes it easier to shape the falafel.

Step 5: Shape the Falafel

Take the chilled falafel mixture out of the refrigerator and shape it into small patties, about 1 inch in diameter. You can use your hands or a falafel scoop for this step. Ensure that the patties are compact, so they hold their shape during frying.

Step 6: Fry the Falafel

In a deep pan or pot, heat vegetable oil to about 350°F (175°C). Carefully place the shaped falafel patties into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the falafel for about 3-4 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside. Flip them halfway through to ensure even cooking.

Step 7: Drain and Serve

Once the falafel are cooked, remove them from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain them on a paper towel-lined plate. This helps remove any excess oil.

Serve the falafel warm with tahini sauce, hummus, or tzatziki. You can also stuff them into pita bread with fresh vegetables, pickles, and a drizzle of your favorite sauce for a delicious falafel sandwich.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have dried chickpeas, you can use canned chickpeas instead. Just make sure to drain and rinse them thoroughly before using.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of spices and herbs according to your taste preferences. Add more cayenne pepper if you like it spicy, or omit it altogether if you prefer a milder flavor.
  • For a healthier alternative, you can bake the falafel instead of frying them. Simply preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C), place the shaped falafel on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and bake for about 20-25 minutes, flipping them halfway through.

Making the perfect Middle Eastern falafel at home is a rewarding experience that allows you to savor the authentic flavors of this beloved dish. With this recipe, you’ll be able to create crispy, flavorful falafel that will impress your family and friends. So why not give it a try and bring a taste of the Middle East to your kitchen? Enjoy!
