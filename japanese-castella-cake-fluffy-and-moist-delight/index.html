Japanese Castella Cake: Fluffy and Moist Delight


If you’re a fan of light, fluffy, and moist cakes, then you’re in for a treat with this Japanese Castella Cake recipe. Originating from the Nagasaki region of Japan, this sponge cake has a unique texture and flavor that sets it apart from other traditional cakes. The Castella Cake is known for its simplicity and delightful taste, making it a popular choice for tea time or dessert. Let’s dive into the steps to create this delightful Japanese delicacy in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 100ml milk
  • 100g cake flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Begin by preheating your oven to 320°F (160°C). Line the bottom of an 8-inch (20cm) square cake pan with parchment paper and grease the sides with butter.

  2. Egg Mixture: In a heatproof bowl, combine the eggs, granulated sugar, and honey. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and whisk the mixture until it reaches a temperature of 104°F (40°C). This step is crucial as it helps create a stable foam for the cake.

  3. Whipping the Eggs: Once the egg mixture reaches the desired temperature, remove it from the heat and continue to whisk using a hand mixer or stand mixer until the mixture becomes pale, thick, and forms ribbons when the whisk is lifted.

  4. Sifting and Mixing: Sift the cake flour, baking powder, and salt into the egg mixture in small portions, gently folding in each addition with a spatula. Be careful not to deflate the air incorporated into the batter during the whisking process.

  5. Incorporating Milk and Vanilla: Warm the milk in the microwave for about 20 seconds and add it to the batter, followed by the vanilla extract. Gently fold the mixture until the milk is fully incorporated.

  6. Pouring into the Pan: Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and tap it gently on the counter to release any large air bubbles.

  7. Baking: Place the cake pan in the preheated oven and bake for 10 minutes. After 10 minutes, decrease the temperature to 280°F (140°C) and bake for an additional 50-60 minutes, or until the cake is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Cooling: Once the cake is baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 5 minutes. Then, transfer the cake to a wire rack to cool completely.

  9. Serving: Once the Castella Cake has cooled, slice it into squares or rectangles and serve. The cake is best enjoyed with a cup of green tea or as a sweet ending to a meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a citrus twist, you can add lemon or orange zest to the batter.
  • Some variations include using matcha powder for a green tea flavor or cocoa powder for a chocolate Castella Cake.
  • To achieve an even fluffier texture, you can separate the egg yolks and whites, and beat the egg whites to stiff peaks before folding them into the batter.

Now that you have the key steps and ingredients to make this delightful Japanese Castella Cake, it’s time to put your baking skills to the test. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress with its airy texture and delightful taste. Enjoy the process of creating this fluffy and moist Japanese treat, and share the joy of homemade goodness with your loved ones. Happy baking!
