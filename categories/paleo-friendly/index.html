Delightful Paleo Coconut Shrimp Recipe

If you’re a fan of seafood and looking for a delicious and healthy recipe to try, then this delightful Paleo Coconut Shrimp recipe is perfect for you! Packed with flavor and easy to make, this dish will leave your taste buds satisfied and your body nourished. The best part? It’s completely gluten-free, grain-free, and dairy-free, making it an excellent choice for those following a paleo or clean eating lifestyle. Ingredients 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined 1 cup of unsweetened shredded coconut 1/2 cup of almond flour 2 eggs 1 teaspoon of garlic powder 1 teaspoon of paprika 1/2 teaspoon of sea salt 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper Cooking oil of your choice (coconut oil recommended) Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs until well beaten. In a separate bowl, combine the shredded coconut, almond flour, garlic powder, paprika, sea salt, and black pepper. Mix well. Dip each shrimp into the beaten eggs, making sure to coat both sides. Next, roll the shrimp in the coconut mixture, pressing gently to ensure the coating sticks. Place the coated shrimp onto the prepared baking sheet, making sure to leave space between each piece. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and add enough cooking oil to cover the bottom of the pan. Carefully transfer the coated shrimp to the hot skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Once cooked, remove the shrimp from the skillet and place them back onto the baking sheet. Bake the shrimp in the preheated oven for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until they are cooked through and crispy. Serve the delightful Paleo Coconut Shrimp hot, with your favorite dipping sauce or alongside a fresh salad. Why Choose Paleo Coconut Shrimp? Not only is this recipe incredibly delicious, but it also offers numerous health benefits. By using almond flour and shredded coconut as the coating, you’re avoiding refined grains and opting for nutrient-rich alternatives. Almond flour is a great source of healthy fats, protein, and fiber, while shredded coconut provides essential minerals like iron and manganese. ...
