Grilled Skewers: Creative Combos for Every Taste Bud


Grilled skewers are a staple of summer gatherings and outdoor barbecues, offering a convenient and flavorful way to feed a crowd. But why settle for the same old combination of chicken and vegetables when you can get creative with your skewers? In this post, we’ll explore some unique and delicious combinations to satisfy every taste bud.

Meat Lovers Unite

For those who can’t get enough of a good meat, we’ve got three mouth-watering combinations that are sure to please.

  • Korean-Style BBQ Beef and Pineapple: Marinate thinly sliced beef in a mixture of soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and sesame oil, then thread onto skewers with chunks of fresh pineapple. Grill until the beef is caramelized and the pineapple is slightly charred.
  • Greek-Inspired Lamb and Feta: Alternate chunks of lamb with crumbles of feta cheese, cherry tomatoes, and fresh oregano on skewers. Brush with olive oil and grill until the lamb is cooked to your liking.
  • Spicy Chorizo and Manchego: Mix spicy chorizo sausage with crumbles of Manchego cheese, red bell peppers, and onions. Grill until the sausage is cooked through and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Veggie Delights

For our vegetarian and vegan friends, don’t worry – we haven’t forgotten about you! Here are three tasty combinations that showcase the best of plant-based ingredients.

  • Roasted Veggie Medley: Alternate chunks of zucchini, yellow squash, red bell peppers, and onions on skewers. Brush with olive oil and grill until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  • Tropical Fruit Skewers: Thread chunks of fresh pineapple, mango, and kiwi onto skewers. Brush with a mixture of honey and lime juice, then grill until the fruit is caramelized and slightly softened.
  • Mushroom and Spinach: Alternate slices of cremini mushrooms with fresh spinach leaves and chunks of red onion on skewers. Brush with olive oil and grill until the mushrooms are tender and the spinach is wilted.

Seafood Options

For those who love seafood, here are three combinations that are sure to impress.

  • Shrimp and Pineapple Skewers: Alternate chunks of shrimp with pineapple chunks and red bell peppers on skewers. Brush with a mixture of soy sauce, brown sugar, and rum, then grill until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.
  • Salmon and Asparagus: Thread chunks of salmon fillet with asparagus spears and cherry tomatoes onto skewers. Brush with olive oil and grill until the salmon is cooked through and the asparagus is tender.
  • Scallop and Mango: Alternate chunks of scallops with chunks of fresh mango and red onion on skewers. Brush with a mixture of honey and lime juice, then grill until the scallops are cooked through and the mango is caramelized.

Dessert Skewers

Why save dessert for last? Here are three sweet combinations that are perfect for a summer dessert party.

  • Fruit and Cream: Alternate chunks of fresh strawberries, blueberries, and pineapple with dollops of whipped cream on skewers. Drizzle with honey and serve chilled.
  • Chocolate-Dipped Fruit: Dip fresh fruit like strawberries, bananas, and grapes in melted chocolate, then thread onto skewers. Chill until the chocolate is set.
  • Cinnamon Sugar Donut Holes: Thread mini donut holes onto skewers, then drizzle with a mixture of melted butter, cinnamon, and sugar. Grill until the donut holes are caramelized and slightly puffed.

With these creative combinations, you’ll never have to worry about running out of ideas for your grilled skewers. Whether you’re a meat lover, a veggie enthusiast, or a seafood fan, there’s something for everyone on this list. So go ahead, get creative, and fire up those skewers!
