Cooking for Two: 5 Easy Recipes for Weeknight Dinners


Cooking for two can sometimes feel like a chore, especially on busy weeknights when time and energy levels are low. However, the right recipes can make all the difference, transforming your dinners from bland to brilliant in no time. The good news? You don’t have to spend hours in the kitchen or rely on takeout to enjoy delicious, home-cooked meals. Whether you’re planning a cozy meal for you and your partner or sharing dinner with a friend, these five easy recipes are perfect for weeknight dinners. Let’s dive right in!

1. Honey Garlic Glazed Salmon with Roasted Veggies

This flavorful salmon recipe combines sweet and savory notes and is ready in just about 30 minutes. Pair it with sheet pan roasted vegetables, and you’ve got a nutritious and satisfying weeknight meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the salmon fillets on a lined baking sheet. In a small bowl, whisk honey, minced garlic, soy sauce, and olive oil until combined. Brush the mixture over the salmon generously.
  3. On the same baking sheet, spread out the zucchini, red bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes. Drizzle the vegetables with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast everything in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and the veggies are tender.
  5. Serve the salmon and veggies warm, and enjoy a nutritious, no-fuss dinner.

2. Creamy One-Pot Chicken Alfredo

Who doesn’t love a creamy pasta dish? This one-pot chicken Alfredo comes together effortlessly and requires minimal cleanup, making it ideal for busy evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt, pepper, and parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or sauté pan over medium heat. Add the diced chicken and cook until browned and cooked through. Set the chicken aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the garlic and sauté until fragrant. Pour in the chicken broth and heavy cream. Bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the fettuccine pasta to the liquid and let it cook, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. This should take about 10-12 minutes.
  4. Once the pasta is tender, stir in the Parmesan cheese and add the cooked chicken back to the pan. Toss everything together until the sauce is creamy and coats the pasta evenly.
  5. Garnish with parsley and freshly cracked black pepper before serving.

3. Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms with Spinach and Cheese

Portobello mushrooms are hearty and satisfying, making them a wonderful, low-carb dinner option. This recipe is quick to assemble and packed with cheesy goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Portobello mushrooms, stems removed
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Brush the Portobello mushrooms with olive oil and place them on a lined baking tray, cap-side down. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a skillet, sauté the minced garlic until fragrant. Add the spinach and cook until wilted.
  4. Remove the skillet from heat and mix the spinach with ricotta, Parmesan, and half the shredded mozzarella.
  5. Stuff the mushroom caps with the spinach and cheese mixture. Top with the remaining mozzarella cheese.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  7. Serve as a main dish or pair with a simple side salad for a balanced meal.

4. Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Stir-fries are the ultimate quick dinner solution, and this beef and broccoli recipe is no exception. It’s fast, flavorful, and perfect for two!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, oyster sauce, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the sliced beef and cook until browned. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add sesame oil and sauté the garlic until fragrant. Add the broccoli and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until tender-crisp.
  4. Return the beef to the skillet and pour in the sauce mixture. Stir well to coat everything evenly and cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the sauce thickens slightly.
  5. Serve the stir-fry over a bed of steamed rice and enjoy.

5. Caprese Flatbread Pizza

When you’re craving pizza but don’t want to wait for delivery, this quick and flavorful flatbread pizza is an excellent option. It’s fresh, simple, and takes less than 20 minutes to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 flatbreads or naan
  • 1/4 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 cup fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Place the flatbreads on a baking sheet and spread marinara sauce over each.
  3. Top with slices of fresh mozzarella and scatter the cherry tomatoes across the flatbreads.
  4. Bake in the oven for 8-10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Remove from the oven, add fresh basil leaves, and drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze.
  6. Slice and serve immediately.

Tips for Cooking for Two:

  • Plan Ahead: Keep your pantry stocked with versatile ingredients, such as rice, pasta, and canned goods, to make meal prep easier.
  • Smart Portioning: Adjust recipes to avoid leftovers unless you plan to pack them for lunch the next day.
  • Invest in the Right Tools: A smaller skillet or saucepan can help prevent overcooking or burning when making smaller portions.
  • Experiment Together: If you’re cooking with a partner, turn dinner prep into a fun activity to experiment with flavors and new recipes.

Weeknight dinners don’t have to be mundane or overly complicated. With these five easy recipes, you can create flavorful, well-balanced meals for two without the stress. Whether you’re in the mood for creamy pasta, hearty stir-fry, or a quick pizza, these recipes are sure to make dinnertime more enjoyable. Start experimenting and enjoy the process of creating simple, delicious meals that suit both your tastes and schedule.
