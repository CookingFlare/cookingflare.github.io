Valentine's Day Treats for Kids: Fun Recipes


Valentine’s Day is a time to celebrate love, and what better way to do that than by making sweet treats with your little ones? Whether you’re hosting a Valentine’s Day party or simply looking to create some memorable moments in the kitchen, these kid-friendly recipes are sure to bring a smile to everyone’s face. From colorful cookies to creatively crafted snacks, these ideas are perfect for engaging kids in something fun, festive, and delicious.

1. Heart-Shaped Strawberry Pancake Pops

Start your Valentine’s Day with a sweet breakfast treat that’s as cute as it is delicious. These heart-shaped strawberry pancake pops are a fun way to bring a festive touch to the morning. Kids will love making them just as much as they’ll love eating them!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pancake mix
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh strawberries
  • Red food coloring (optional)
  • Wooden popsicle sticks
  • Butter or cooking oil, for greasing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat a non-stick griddle or frying pan to medium heat.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the pancake mix, milk, and egg until smooth. Stir in a couple of drops of red food coloring to give the pancakes a pink tint, if desired.
  3. Fold in the chopped strawberries.
  4. Grease a heart-shaped cookie cutter lightly with butter or oil and place it on the hot griddle.
  5. Pour a small amount of pancake batter into the cutter, filling it halfway, and insert a popsicle stick into the batter.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges are firm, then carefully remove the cookie cutter and flip the pancake to cook the other side.
  7. Repeat until all the batter is used. Serve warm with syrup or a dusting of powdered sugar.

2. Cupid’s Arrow Fruit Skewers

A healthy, colorful Valentine’s Day snack is always a hit, and these Cupid’s Arrow Fruit Skewers fit the bill perfectly. They’re easy to assemble, visually appealing, and fun for kids to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • Strawberries
  • Watermelon
  • Grapes
  • Pineapple
  • Skewer sticks
  • Small heart-shaped cookie cutter

Instructions:

  1. Cut the watermelon and pineapple into thick slices. Use the heart-shaped cookie cutter to cut heart pieces from the fruit slices.
  2. Wash and prepare the strawberries by removing the stems, and leave the grapes whole.
  3. Slide the fruits onto the skewers in a pattern – for example, start with a watermelon heart, then add a grape, followed by a strawberry. Get creative!
  4. Arrange the skewers on a platter for a striking and kid-friendly treat.

3. Valentine’s Day Rice Krispie Hearts

Transform regular Rice Krispie treats into festive Valentine’s Day hearts by adding colorful toppings and using a heart cookie cutter. These are quick to make, and kids can customize their treats with sprinkles, candy, and icing.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 cups mini marshmallows
  • 6 cups crispy rice cereal
  • Red and pink sprinkles
  • Melted white or milk chocolate
  • Heart-shaped cookie cutter

Instructions:

  1. Melt the butter in a large pot over low heat. Add the marshmallows and stir until melted and smooth.
  2. Remove the pot from heat and gently stir in the rice cereal until fully coated.
  3. Spread the mixture into a greased baking pan and press firmly to create an even layer. Let cool for 10 minutes.
  4. Use the heart-shaped cookie cutter to cut out hearts from the Rice Krispie mixture.
  5. Dip the top of each heart into melted chocolate, then decorate with sprinkles. Let the treats set at room temperature before serving.

4. Pink Lemonade Valentine’s Floats

Looking for an easy Valentine’s Day beverage that kids will love? These pink lemonade floats are a fizzy and fun delight, perfect for celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter pink lemonade
  • 1 quart vanilla ice cream
  • Strawberry slices for garnishing
  • Whipped cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Pour pink lemonade into tall glasses, filling each about halfway.
  2. Top with two scoops of vanilla ice cream. The lemonade will fizz and foam as the ice cream melts.
  3. Garnish with fresh strawberry slices and a dollop of whipped cream.
  4. Serve with festive straws and enjoy!

5. Chocolate-Covered Pretzel Wands

Kids will have a blast making and decorating these colorful pretzel wands. With their combination of sweet, salty, and crunchy, these treats are irresistible and perfect for Valentine’s Day.

Ingredients:

  • Large pretzel rods
  • 1 cup melted white chocolate
  • 1 cup melted milk or dark chocolate
  • Red, pink, and white sprinkles
  • Crushed candies or mini chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Dip each pretzel rod halfway into the melted chocolate, letting any excess drip off.
  3. Place the coated pretzel on the parchment paper and immediately decorate with sprinkles or crushed candies before the chocolate hardens.
  4. Repeat with all the pretzel rods and let them set at room temperature or in the refrigerator for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Once hardened, arrange the pretzel wands in a jar or on a tray for serving.

6. No-Bake Chocolate Raspberry Tarts

These miniature chocolate tarts are an elegant yet easy Valentine’s Day treat that kids can help assemble. With their no-bake crust and rich chocolate filling, they’re an indulgence everyone will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup crushed graham crackers
  • 4 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh raspberries for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, mix the crushed graham crackers, melted butter, and sugar until combined.
  2. Press the mixture into small tart molds or muffin tins to create crusts. Chill in the refrigerator for 30 minutes.
  3. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan until hot, but not boiling. Pour it over the chocolate chips and stir until smooth and glossy.
  4. Spoon the chocolate filling into the prepared crusts and top each tart with fresh raspberries.
  5. Chill for another 30 minutes to allow the filling to set before serving.

7. Love Bug Sugar Cookies

Decorating sugar cookies is always a fun activity for kids, and these “love bug” cookies are a Valentine’s Day favorite. With their cute designs and vibrant colors, they’re almost too adorable to eat!

Ingredients:

  • 1 batch of sugar cookie dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Roll-out cookie cutters
  • Assorted colored icing or frosting
  • Candy eyes, edible glitter, and sprinkles

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven and roll out the sugar cookie dough on a floured surface.
  2. Use cookie cutters to cut heart shapes and other fun designs. Bake according to recipe instructions and let them cool completely.
  3. Decorate the cookies by adding icing or frosting, and use candy eyes and sprinkles to create “love bug” faces and designs.
  4. Allow the frosting to set before serving or packaging them up as treats.

Valentine’s Day is the perfect occasion to get creative in the kitchen with your kids. Baking and decorating these festive treats not only creates lasting memories but also makes the day extra special. By using simple ingredients and fun designs, these recipes ensure smiles all around. Whether it’s heart-shaped pancake pops, chocolate raspberry tarts, or colorful sugar cookies, you’re sure to find a recipe that your family will enjoy making and sharing this Valentine’s Day!
