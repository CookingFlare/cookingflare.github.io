Savory Roman-Style Gnocchi Recipe - Italian Potato Perfection


If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, you’re probably no stranger to the delight of gnocchi. These pillowy-soft potato dumplings have been a staple of Italian cooking for centuries, and there are countless regional variations to savor. One such variation is the Roman-style gnocchi, which offers a unique twist on this classic dish. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create these delectable morsels and elevate your culinary skills to new heights.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of russet potatoes
  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • Salt, to taste
  • Nutmeg, freshly grated
  • Black pepper, freshly ground

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes

Start by washing and scrubbing the potatoes thoroughly. Then, place them in a large pot of salted water and bring it to a boil. Cook the potatoes until they are tender when pierced with a fork, usually for about 45 minutes to 1 hour.

Step 2: Rice the Potatoes

Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them and allow them to cool slightly. Then, peel the potatoes and rice them using a potato ricer or a fine-mesh sieve. This step is crucial for achieving the light and airy texture that makes Roman-style gnocchi so delightful.

Step 3: Form the Dough

Transfer the riced potatoes to a clean work surface, forming a mound with a well in the center. Add the flour, egg, salt, nutmeg, and black pepper to the well. Gently knead the mixture until the dough comes together, being careful not to overwork it. The goal is to achieve a smooth and pliable dough.

Step 4: Shape the Gnocchi

Divide the dough into manageable portions and roll each portion into a long rope, about 3/4 inch in diameter. Using a sharp knife, cut the rope into 1-inch pieces. To create the classic gnocchi shape, gently roll each piece along the tines of a fork or a gnocchi board to create ridges that will help the sauce cling to the gnocchi.

Step 5: Cook the Gnocchi

Bring a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil. Carefully drop the gnocchi into the water in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Once the gnocchi rise to the surface, allow them to cook for an additional 1-2 minutes before removing them with a slotted spoon.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Once the gnocchi are cooked, you can serve them with your favorite sauce. Whether it’s a rich ragù, a simple tomato sauce, or a brown butter and sage sauce, the delicate flavor and texture of the Roman-style gnocchi will complement a wide range of accompaniments.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of creating Roman-style gnocchi is a rewarding endeavor that will surely impress your family and friends. The process may seem intricate at first, but with a bit of practice and patience, you’ll soon be crafting these delectable potato dumplings with ease. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure that celebrates the rich tradition of Italian cooking. Buon appetito!
