How to Cook Spanish Chorizo: 5 Delicious Recipes


Spanish chorizo is a culinary treasure renowned for its bold flavors, smoky aroma, and versatile nature. This cured sausage, made primarily from coarsely chopped pork and a blend of spices including paprika, garlic, and chili, can elevate any dish with a burst of savory goodness. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen beginner, learning how to cook with Spanish chorizo opens up a world of rich, hearty, and satisfying meals.

In this guide, we’ll explore five mouthwatering recipes that showcase Spanish chorizo in different ways, from traditional tapas to hearty stews. Follow along to discover new ideas for incorporating this flavorful ingredient into your cooking repertoire.

Understanding Spanish Chorizo: The Basics

Before diving into recipes, it’s helpful to understand the types of Spanish chorizo and how they affect cooking. Spanish chorizo is typically cured and smoked, available in two main varieties:

  • Picante: Spicy, with red pepper flakes and smoked paprika that add heat.
  • Dulce: Mild and sweet, featuring paprika without much heat.

Both types add deep, smoky flavor to dishes, but the choice depends on your heat preference. When cooking, chorizo can be sliced and eaten as is if it’s cured, or broken apart and cooked if raw.

1. Spanish Chorizo and Potato Tortilla

A traditional Spanish tortilla is an ideal place to start your chorizo adventure. This recipe blends tender potatoes, eggs, and chorizo, resulting in a hearty dish perfect for breakfast, lunch, or tapas.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Spanish chorizo (sliced or diced)
  • 4 medium potatoes (peeled and thinly sliced)
  • 1 medium onion (thinly sliced)
  • 6 large eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the potatoes and onion: Heat olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add potatoes and onions, cooking gently until soft but not browned, about 15 minutes.

  2. Cook the chorizo: In a separate pan, cook chorizo slices until they release their oils and become slightly crispy, about 4-5 minutes.

  3. Combine and beat eggs: Drain excess oil from potatoes and onions, then add to a large bowl. Mix in the cooked chorizo. Beat the eggs with salt and pepper, then pour into the bowl and mix well.

  4. Cook the tortilla: Wipe the skillet clean and add a little olive oil. Pour the egg mixture back into the pan over medium heat. Cook gently until edges set, using a spatula to shape the sides.

  5. Flip and finish cooking: Once the bottom is golden, carefully flip the tortilla onto a plate and slide it back into the pan to cook the other side until fully set.

Serve warm or at room temperature. This tortilla pairs perfectly with crusty bread and a fresh salad.

2. Chorizo and Chickpea Stew

Warm and hearty, this chorizo and chickpea stew is a comforting meal that combines smoky sausage with protein-rich legumes and robust seasonings.

Ingredients:

  • 250g Spanish chorizo (sliced)
  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 red bell pepper (chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté vegetables and chorizo: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and bell pepper, cooking until softened. Add the chorizo slices and fry until browned and fragrant.

  2. Add spices and tomatoes: Stir in smoked paprika and cumin to the pot, followed by the fresh tomatoes. Cook until tomatoes break down, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add chickpeas and broth: Pour in chickpeas and broth, stirring to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes to develop flavors.

  4. Season and serve: Taste and season with salt and pepper. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve hot, accompanied by crusty bread to soak up the rich stew.

This stew captures the essence of rustic Spanish cuisine with every spoonful.

3. Grilled Chorizo and Vegetable Skewers

For a smoky summer meal, grilled chorizo skewers offer a simple yet flavor-packed option, combining juicy sausage pieces with colorful vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Spanish chorizo (cut into chunks)
  • 1 red onion (quartered)
  • 1 zucchini (sliced thick)
  • 1 red bell pepper (cut into pieces)
  • 1 yellow bell pepper (cut into pieces)
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary sprigs
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the skewers: Thread pieces of chorizo, onion, zucchini, and bell peppers alternately onto soaked wooden skewers or metal skewers.

  2. Season and oil: Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice over the skewers, sprinkle with salt, pepper, and fresh herbs.

  3. Grill to perfection: Heat a grill or grill pan to medium-high. Cook the skewers for 5-7 minutes per side or until vegetables are tender and chorizo has crisped edges.

  4. Serve immediately: These skewers are great served with a chilled glass of white wine or alongside a bright green salad.

The combination of smoky chorizo with charred vegetables makes this a crowd-pleaser at any barbecue.

4. Chorizo-Stuffed Mushrooms

An elegant appetizer that packs a punch, chorizo-stuffed mushrooms deliver a delightful burst of flavor in every bite. Perfect for dinner parties or cozy nights in.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large button mushrooms (stems removed)
  • 150g Spanish chorizo (finely chopped)
  • 1 small onion (finely diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Manchego cheese or Parmesan
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven: Set oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook filling: Heat olive oil in a pan. Sauté onions and garlic until translucent. Add chopped chorizo and cook until browned and fragrant. Remove from heat and mix in breadcrumbs, cheese, and parsley. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Stuff mushrooms: Place mushroom caps on a baking tray. Fill each mushroom with spoonfuls of the chorizo mixture.

  4. Bake: Drizzle mushrooms lightly with olive oil and bake for 15-20 minutes or until mushrooms are tender and the stuffing is golden.

These bite-sized treats pair wonderfully with a chilled rosé or sparkling wine.

5. Spanish Chorizo Paella

Paella is Spain’s most iconic dish, and adding chorizo gives it an extra layer of smoky richness. This recipe brings the flavors of the Mediterranean into your kitchen with ease.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Spanish chorizo (sliced)
  • 1 cup Arborio or bomba rice
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 red bell pepper (chopped)
  • 1 tomato (grated)
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable stock (warmed)
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté base ingredients: In a large paella pan or wide skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and bell pepper and cook until softened.

  2. Cook chorizo and rice: Add sliced chorizo and fry until slightly crisp. Stir in rice, coating grains with oil for about 2 minutes.

  3. Add tomato and stock: Stir in grated tomato and smoked paprika, then pour in the warm stock. Bring to a simmer without stirring.

  4. Simmer and add peas: Cook on medium-low heat for about 20 minutes or until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Add peas during the last 5 minutes.

  5. Rest and garnish: Remove from heat, cover with a clean towel, and let rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and lemon wedges.

Serve this paella alongside a chilled glass of sangria for an authentic Spanish experience.

Final Thoughts

Spanish chorizo is a versatile and flavorful ingredient that can transform simple dishes into memorable meals. From traditional tortillas to hearty stews and elegant appetizers, experimenting with chorizo in your cooking will reward you with a spectrum of smoky, spicy, and savory flavors.

As you try these recipes, don’t hesitate to adjust spice levels, pair with your favorite sides, and create your own chorizo-inspired dishes. Whether you’re cooking for friends, family, or yourself, Spanish chorizo is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser that deserves a spot in every kitchen.

Ready to embrace the vibrant taste of Spain? Grab a chorizo, fire up your stove, and start cooking!
