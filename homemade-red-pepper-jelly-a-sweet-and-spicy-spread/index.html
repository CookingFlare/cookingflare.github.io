Homemade Red Pepper Jelly: A Sweet and Spicy Spread


Are you a fan of both sweetness and spiciness? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a delicious Homemade Red Pepper Jelly that strikes the perfect balance between sweet and spicy. Whether you want to add some zing to your cheese and crackers or elevate your favorite sandwich, this versatile spread is sure to become a pantry staple.

Ingredients

  • 4 red bell peppers
  • 1 jalapeno pepper
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 package (85g) powdered pectin
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Start by sterilizing your canning jars and lids. Wash them in hot, soapy water, rinse thoroughly, and place them in a large pot of boiling water for 10 minutes. Remove from the water and let them air dry upside down on a clean towel.

  2. Prep the Peppers: Remove the seeds and stems from the red bell peppers and jalapeno pepper. Roughly chop them and transfer to a food processor or blender. Pulse until finely minced, but not pureed. You want some texture to remain.

  3. Cooking the Peppers: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, combine the minced peppers, sugar, apple cider vinegar, and salt. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil over medium-high heat, stirring frequently.

  4. Adding the Pectin: Sprinkle the powdered pectin evenly over the boiling pepper mixture while continuing to stir. Boil for an additional minute, stirring constantly to ensure the pectin is fully dissolved.

  5. Testing for Gel: To check if the jelly has reached the desired consistency, place a small plate in the freezer for a few minutes. Once chilled, spoon a small amount of the jelly onto the plate and let it sit for a few seconds. If it wrinkles when you push it with your finger, it’s ready. If not, continue boiling for another minute and repeat the test.

  6. Canning the Jelly: Carefully ladle the hot jelly into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace. Wipe the rims clean with a damp cloth, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands until fingertip tight.

  7. Processing the Jars: Place the filled jars back into the large pot of boiling water. Make sure the water covers the jars by at least an inch. Process for 10 minutes, then carefully remove the jars and let them cool on a towel-lined countertop. As they cool, you’ll hear the satisfying “pop” of the lids sealing.

  8. Enjoying the Red Pepper Jelly: Allow the jars to cool completely before storing them in a cool, dark place. The jelly will continue to thicken as it cools and sets. Once opened, refrigerate and use within a month.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the Homemade Red Pepper Jelly with a variety of cheeses and crackers for an easy and elegant appetizer.
  • Use it as a glaze for roasted meats, such as chicken or pork.
  • Spread it on a sandwich or burger for a burst of flavor.
  • Add a dollop to your favorite salad dressing for a tangy twist.

Conclusion

Homemade Red Pepper Jelly is a delightful combination of sweet and spicy that adds a unique flavor profile to any dish. With just a few simple ingredients and easy steps, you can create a versatile spread that will impress your family and friends. So, why not give this recipe a try and elevate your culinary creations? Enjoy!
