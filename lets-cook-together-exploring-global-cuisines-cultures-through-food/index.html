Let's Cook Together: Exploring Global Cuisines & Cultures through Food


Embarking on a culinary journey around the world is as delightful as it sounds. Through cooking, we can experience the distinctive flavors, unique ingredients, and rich histories of various cultures. Let’s dive into the vibrant world of global cuisines and uncover what makes each one truly special!

1. Argentina: Argentine Asado

Argentina is a land of wide-open spaces and bustling cities, and the country’s culinary tradition centers around the “asado” - the Argentine barbecue. This simple yet hearty dining experience brings people together and lets the smoky, savory meat shine.

Ingredients:

  • Chuck roast (800g)
  • Rib eye steaks (600g)
  • Chorizo sausages (500g)
  • Saltena potatoes (6 pieces)
  • Inca:-ligera salad (as per the recipe below)
  • Chimichurri sauce (as per the recipe below)

Instructions:

  1. Start by preheating your grill or oven to a high temperature. Be patient, for Argentinians believe in slow and low cooking to attain that tender, juicy meat.
  2. Season the meats generously with salt and pepper. Let them rest for a bit to absorb all that flavor.
  3. Cook the meats evenly over medium to low heat, allowing them to slowly cook through. Pile the green wood alongside the meats to add a smoky flavor.
  4. While the meat is cooking, grill or saut√© the Saltena potatoes until they receive a desirable crispy exterior.
  5. Prepare the chimichurri sauce and Inca ligera salad to accompany this feast.

Sauce & Salad Recipes:

Chimichurri Sauce:

  • Fresh parsley (1 cup, chopped)
  • Fresh oregano (1/4 cup, finely chopped)
  • Red chili flakes (1/2 tsp)
  • Garlic cloves (5, minced)
  • Extra virgin olive oil (1/2 cup)
  • Red wine vinegar (1/4 cup)
  • Salt (to taste)

Combine all ingredients in a blender/nutri bullet and blend until smooth. Set aside.

Inca-ligera Salad:

  • Cherry tomatoes (1 cup, halved)
  • Red onion (3/4 cup, thinly sliced)
  • Red bell pepper (1, thinly sliced)
  • Black olives (1/2 cup)
  • Fresh parsley (1/4 cup, chopped)
  • Extra virgin olive oil (2 tbsp)
  • Red wine vinegar (1 tbsp)
  • Salt (to taste)

Combine all ingredients in a large bowl, mix gently, and drizzle olive oil and vinegar on top.

2. Vietnam: Vietnamese Spring Rolls

Discover the art of Vietnamese cuisine through its refreshing spring rolls, packed with vibrant vegetables, savory meats, and immersed in a tangy peanut dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • Rice vermicelli (200g)
  • Shrimp (200g, peeled and deveined)
  • Pork belly (100g, thinly sliced)
  • Wood ear mushrooms (100g, rehydrated and drained)
  • Bean sprouts (200g)
  • Cucumber (1, julienned)
  • Lettuce leaves (8, large)
  • Rice paper (16 sheets)
  • Hoisin sauce (for serving)
  • Fresh mint (1 bunch, leaves only)
  • Crushed peanuts (1/2 cup, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Fill a large bowl with warm water and soften the rice paper one at a time, about 30 seconds, or until pliable.
  2. Place a rice paper sheet in a flat plate or bowl, ensuring that some of the sheet overlaps the rim. This will make rolling easier.
  3. Start by arranging a few vermicelli noodles in the center of the rice wrapper. Add some shrimp, pork belly, wood ear mushrooms, bean sprouts, cucumber, and lettuce on top.
  4. Fold the sides of the wrapper over the filling, followed by the bottom part of the wrapper over the filling, tucking it in firmly to prevent it from unwrapping while rolling. Proceed to roll the rice paper, making sure that the edges are neatly tucked in.
  5. Repeat the process with all the remaining rice paper sheets and filling components.

Peanut Dipping Sauce:

  • Peanut butter (1/4 cup, smooth)
  • Fish sauce (2 tbsp)
  • Rice vinegar (1 tbsp)
  • Sriracha sauce (1 tbsp)
  • Water (2-3 tbsp)
  • Sugar (1 1/2 tbsp)
  • Crushed peanuts (2 tbsp, for garnish)
  • Fresh mint leaves (10, for garnish)

Mix all ingredients in a small bowl and whisk well. Add water if needed to adjust the consistency.

Serve the spring rolls with the peanut dipping sauce, and garnish with crushed peanuts and a sprig of fresh mint.

3. Morocco: Moroccan Lamb Tagine

Experience the tantalizing, aromatic world of Moroccan cuisine through its traditional lamb tagine. A blend of spices, tender meat, raisins, and almonds come together in this unforgettable, heart-warming dish.

Ingredients:

  • Bone-in lamb shoulder (750g)
  • Onions (1.5 cups, thinly sliced)
  • Garlic cloves (3, minced)
  • Olive oil (2 tbsp)
  • Fresh ginger (2 inch piece, grated)
  • Cinnamon sticks (2, ground)
  • Ground cumin (1 1/2 tsp)
  • Ground ginger (1 1/2 tsp)
  • Paprika (1 tsp)
  • Turmeric (1 tsp)
  • Ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Dried apricots (1/2 cup, roughly chopped)
  • Raisins (1/4 cup)
  • Saffron (pinch)
  • Fresh cilantro (1/2 cup, chopped, for garnish)
  • Fresh lemon juice (2 tbsp, for garnish)
  • Almonds (1/4 cup, slivered, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat, and saut√© onions until they turn golden brown. Add garlic, ginger, cinnamon, cumin, ginger, paprika, turmeric, pepper, and salt, mixing well.
  2. Add the lamb, trying to brown each side for additional flavor.
  3. Pour in enough water or lamb stock to cover the meat by 1 cm. Bring to a gentle simmer, covering the pot with a lid.
  4. Cook the lamb slowly, allowing the flavors to meld, for around 2 hours. Test to ensure the meat is tender; if not, cook longer until desired tenderness is achieved.
  5. Add the dried apricots and raisins, checking the seasoning as needed, and simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  6. Just before serving, stir in the saffron and garnish with cilantro, lemon juice, and slivered almonds.

Conclusion:

Exploring global cuisines offers a unique glimpse into various cultures and their tastes. From the sizzling embers of barbecued Argentine meats to the delicate Vietnamese spring rolls and the sumptuous Moroccan tagine, each dish speaks volumes about the heart and soul behind each palate-captivating creation. Cooking is a means of preserving memories, forging relationships, and sharing experiences. So, venture across geographical and cultural borders through food, let your tastebuds be your guide, and create unforgettable memories. Let’s cook together, and let the world’s native flavors dance across your tongue.
