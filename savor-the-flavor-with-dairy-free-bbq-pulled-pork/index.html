Savor the Flavor with Dairy-Free BBQ Pulled Pork


Are you ready to take your barbecue game to the next level? Look no further than this dairy-free BBQ pulled pork recipe that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Whether you follow a dairy-free diet or simply want to try a new twist on a classic favorite, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Let’s dive in and savor the flavor of this mouthwatering dish.

Ingredients

For the Pulled Pork:

  • 4 pounds pork shoulder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 cup vegetable broth

For the BBQ Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons molasses
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Dairy-free hamburger buns or sandwich rolls
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pork: Start by seasoning the pork shoulder with salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the pork shoulder on all sides until browned.

  2. Slow Cook the Pork: Transfer the seared pork shoulder to a slow cooker. Pour the vegetable broth over the pork, cover, and cook on low for 8-10 hours, or on high for 4-5 hours, until the meat is tender and easily shreds with a fork.

  3. Make the BBQ Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, molasses, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.

  4. Shred the Pork: Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and transfer it to a cutting board. Use two forks to shred the pork into bite-sized pieces.

  5. Combine the Pork and BBQ Sauce: Place the shredded pork back into the slow cooker and pour the prepared BBQ sauce over the meat. Stir to coat the pork evenly with the sauce.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Toast the dairy-free hamburger buns or sandwich rolls. Spoon the dairy-free BBQ pulled pork onto the buns, and top with coleslaw if desired. Serve hot and savor the irresistible flavors!

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra smoky flavor, add a few drops of liquid smoke to the BBQ sauce.
  • If you’re short on time, you can also cook the pork in a pressure cooker for about 90 minutes.
  • Leftover pulled pork can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days, or frozen for up to 3 months.

Conclusion

There you have it – a delectable dairy-free BBQ pulled pork recipe that will undoubtedly become a family favorite. With its tender, flavorful pork and the rich, tangy BBQ sauce, this dish is perfect for any occasion, from casual gatherings to weekend family meals. So, fire up the grill or dust off your slow cooker, and get ready to savor the mouthwatering flavors of this dairy-free BBQ pulled pork. Happy cooking!
