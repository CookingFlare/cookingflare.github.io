Thai Mango Sticky Rice Recipe with Coconut Milk


Mango sticky rice is a classic Thai dessert that combines the sweetness of ripe mangoes with the rich creaminess of coconut milk. This delightful treat is not only delicious but also incredibly easy to make. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create an authentic and mouthwatering Thai mango sticky rice that will transport your taste buds to the streets of Bangkok.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup glutinous rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes

Instructions

  1. Soak the glutinous rice in water for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. This will soften the rice and ensure a perfect texture when cooked.

  2. After soaking, drain the rice and rinse it thoroughly until the water runs clear. This step removes excess starch from the rice, resulting in a fluffier and less sticky final product.

  3. In a steamer, cook the rice for approximately 20-25 minutes or until it becomes tender and fully cooked. Make sure to line the steamer basket with a cheesecloth or banana leaves to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom.

  4. While the rice is cooking, prepare the coconut sauce by combining coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a saucepan. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar has dissolved completely. Remove from heat and set aside.

  5. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a mixing bowl and pour half of the coconut sauce over it. Gently fold the sauce into the rice until it is well-coated. Let the rice sit for 10-15 minutes to allow it to absorb the flavors.

  6. While the rice is resting, peel and slice the mangoes into thin strips or bite-sized pieces.

  7. To serve, take a scoop of the coconut-infused sticky rice and place it on a plate. Top it with a few slices of fresh mango. Drizzle some more of the coconut sauce over the mango and rice. Repeat this process for each serving.

  8. Garnish with a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds or a sprig of fresh mint for an extra touch of flavor and presentation.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a pandan leaf to the rice while it’s cooking. The pandan leaf gives a subtle fragrance that complements the sweetness of the mangoes.

  • If you prefer a sweeter coconut sauce, you can increase the amount of sugar to your taste.

  • Experiment with different types of mangoes for a unique twist. While the traditional Thai mangoes are best, you can use any ripe and sweet variety available to you.

  • If you don’t have a steamer, you can cook the rice in a rice cooker or on the stovetop. Just make sure to follow the cooking instructions specific to your chosen method.

  • Leftover mango sticky rice can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Simply reheat the rice before serving, and it will taste just as delicious as when it was freshly made.

Conclusion

Indulge in the lusciousness of Thai mango sticky rice with coconut milk and experience the perfect balance of sweetness and creaminess. This traditional Thai dessert is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Whether you’re a fan of Thai cuisine or simply looking to explore new flavors, this recipe is a must-try. So gather the ingredients, follow the instructions, and transport yourself to the vibrant streets of Thailand with every delightful spoonful. Enjoy!
