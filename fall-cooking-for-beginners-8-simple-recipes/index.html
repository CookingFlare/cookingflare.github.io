Fall Cooking for Beginners: 8 Simple Recipes


As the air turns crisp and the leaves begin to change color, it’s the perfect time to start exploring the world of fall cooking. Fall offers an abundance of seasonal ingredients—sweet potatoes, apples, squash, and hearty greens—that lend themselves beautifully to comforting, delicious meals. If you’re new to cooking or just looking for some easy and satisfying recipes to try, this blog post will guide you through 8 simple fall dishes that are sure to warm your kitchen and your soul.

1. Butternut Squash Soup

There’s nothing more comforting on a chilly fall day than a bowl of homemade butternut squash soup. This creamy, velvety soup is perfect for beginners because it requires minimal ingredients and is incredibly easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the diced butternut squash to the pot and stir for another minute.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 20 minutes or until the squash is fork-tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth (alternatively, you can transfer it in batches to a regular blender).
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg if desired. Stir well and serve hot.

This soup is a cozy fall favorite that pairs wonderfully with a slice of crusty bread.

2. Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal

Fall is the season of apples, and this warm apple cinnamon oatmeal is an easy and nutritious breakfast option for those crisp mornings.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 cups milk (or water for a dairy-free option)
  • 1 medium apple, diced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • A pinch of salt
  • Optional toppings: nuts, raisins, or a dollop of yogurt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the milk (or water) to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add the rolled oats and a pinch of salt. Stir occasionally and cook for about 5-7 minutes until the oats are tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  3. While the oatmeal cooks, heat a small pan over medium heat. Add the diced apple and cook for 3-4 minutes until they soften. Sprinkle the apple with cinnamon as it cooks.
  4. Once the oats are cooked, stir in the cooked apples, honey, and additional cinnamon to taste. Mix everything together and serve hot.
  5. Add your favorite toppings like chopped nuts, raisins, or a spoonful of yogurt for extra flavor and texture.

This oatmeal will keep you full and energized all morning long, making it an ideal breakfast during the fall months.

3. Roasted Brussels Sprouts and Sweet Potatoes

Roasting vegetables is one of the simplest and most delicious ways to prepare fall produce. Brussels sprouts and sweet potatoes are a match made in heaven and make for an easy side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the halved Brussels sprouts and cubed sweet potatoes with olive oil, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and thyme.
  3. Spread the vegetables in an even layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are golden and crispy on the edges, tossing halfway through.
  5. Remove from the oven and serve hot as a side dish to any fall meal.

This dish is a fantastic way to enjoy two of fall’s best vegetables, and it pairs well with roasted meats or a hearty grain dish.

4. Pumpkin Pancakes

No fall recipe list would be complete without pumpkin. These fluffy pumpkin pancakes are a delightful way to enjoy the flavors of the season for breakfast or brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, egg, milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined (do not overmix).
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it with butter or cooking spray.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface of the pancake, then flip and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Serve the pancakes warm with maple syrup and a dusting of cinnamon.

These pancakes are not only easy to make, but they also capture the essence of fall with their warm spices and pumpkin flavor.

5. Spaghetti Squash with Marinara Sauce

If you’re looking for a low-carb, fall-friendly meal, spaghetti squash is an excellent choice. Its delicate texture makes it an ideal stand-in for pasta, and it pairs wonderfully with marinara sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 2 cups marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Slice the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and remove the seeds.
  3. Drizzle the flesh of the squash with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until the squash is tender and can be easily shredded with a fork.
  5. While the squash roasts, heat the marinara sauce in a small saucepan over low heat.
  6. Once the squash is done, use a fork to scrape the flesh into strands. Top with marinara sauce and a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese.

This dish is an excellent way to enjoy the flavors of fall while keeping things light and healthy.

6. Crispy Baked Apple Chips

If you’re craving a sweet snack that feels fall-inspired, these baked apple chips are the perfect solution. They’re simple to make and require only a few ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium apples (preferably a firm variety like Fuji or Honeycrisp)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 225°F (107°C).
  2. Slice the apples into thin rounds using a mandoline or sharp knife. Remove any seeds.
  3. Lay the apple slices in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Sprinkle the apples with cinnamon and sugar (if using).
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 1.5 to 2 hours, flipping the apple slices halfway through. The chips should be crisp and golden when done.
  6. Let them cool before enjoying. Store any leftovers in an airtight container.

These baked apple chips are a great alternative to store-bought snacks and capture the natural sweetness of apples.

7. Beef and Vegetable Stew

A hearty beef and vegetable stew is a fall classic, packed with tender chunks of beef and seasonal vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and parsnips. This dish is ideal for beginner cooks because it’s mostly hands-off once the ingredients are in the pot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef stew meat, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 large carrots, chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 1 cup parsnips, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and brown on all sides.
  2. Once the beef is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside.
  3. In the same pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the beef back to the pot along with the beef broth, carrots, potatoes, parsnips, rosemary, and thyme.
  5. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Let it simmer for 1.5-2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve hot.

This stew is the epitome of fall comfort food, offering a warm, satisfying meal for any night of the week.

8. Chili with Pumpkin

For a fun twist on classic chili, adding pumpkin is a fantastic way to bring some fall flavor into the mix. This pumpkin chili is rich, hearty, and full of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground meat over medium heat until browned. Remove any excess fat.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot, and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in the pumpkin puree, diced tomatoes, kidney beans, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add the broth and bring the chili to a simmer. Cook for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld together.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite chili toppings, such as sour cream, cheese, or chopped green onions.

This pumpkin chili offers a delightful blend of savory and slightly sweet flavors, making it a must-try for fall.

These 8 simple fall recipes are perfect for beginners looking to embrace the flavors of the season. From hearty soups to satisfying side dishes and sweet treats, fall cooking has something for everyone. Whether you’re preparing a cozy dinner or looking for a warm breakfast to start your day, these dishes will help you bring the essence of autumn into your kitchen with ease.
