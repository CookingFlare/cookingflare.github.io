One-Pot Holiday Side Dish Recipes


The hustle and bustle of holiday cooking often leaves home chefs scrambling to prepare a feast that not only feels festive but satisfies the crowd without creating a mountain of dishes. One-pot holiday side dish recipes offer a perfect solution to cut down on clutter while still delivering bold flavors worthy of any celebration. Combining simplicity with taste, these recipes emphasize comfort food vibes with just the right amount of flair to impress your guests. Let’s dive into some irresistible options that fit the season like a hug.

Creamy Garlic Parmesan Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are an undeniable holiday classic, but elevating them with rich garlic and parmesan takes them to another level. The beauty of this recipe lies in its one-pot preparation. No more boiling potatoes and dirtying separate pans for mix-ins!

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs russet or Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup milk (whole or 2%)
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 stick (1/2 cup) unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, add potatoes and cover them with water, seasoning the water generously with salt. Bring to a boil and cook until the potatoes are fork-tender (about 15 minutes).
  2. Drain the potatoes directly in the same pot and return it to low heat.
  3. Add the butter, garlic, milk, and cream to the drained potatoes. Using a potato masher or an electric hand mixer, mash everything together until smooth and creamy.
  4. Stir in the parmesan cheese and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and serve warm.

Pro Tip: For an added layer of decadence, sprinkle your mashed potatoes with crispy bacon bits or caramelized onions before serving.

Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Brussels sprouts during the holidays are always a crowd-pleaser, and when cooked with bacon and topped with a sweet maple glaze, they’ll be the side dish everyone fights over. This toss-and-cook recipe keeps things simple while bringing festive charm to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 6 strips of thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 3 tbsp maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large Dutch oven or deep skillet over medium heat. Add chopped bacon and cook until crispy, then transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain. Leave about 1-2 tbsp of bacon grease in the pan.
  2. Add olive oil to the pot, followed by the Brussels sprouts. Sauté until they start to caramelize and turn golden brown (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. Reduce heat slightly and pour the maple syrup and Dijon mustard over the sprouts, tossing until coated. Cook for another 2-3 minutes to let the glaze thicken and coat evenly.
  4. Stir crispy bacon back into the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve warm as a deliciously sweet and savory holiday side.

Pro Tip: Add dried cranberries or a handful of toasted pecans for added texture and color.

Buttery Herb Rice Pilaf

Forget boxed mixes—this one-pot rice pilaf will deliver a buttery, herbaceous flavor with a fraction of the effort and cleanup. Perfect for pairing with hearty mains, it balances richness with delicate notes of fresh herbs for a versatile holiday must-have.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice or basmati rice
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large, deep saucepan over medium heat and melt butter. Add onion and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
  2. Stir in the rice until it’s coated in the butter and slightly toasted.
  3. Pour the broth into the pot and add thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle boil.
  4. Reduce heat, cover the pot, and simmer for about 18-20 minutes until the rice absorbs all the liquid and is tender.
  5. Fluff the rice with a fork and stir in the fresh parsley before serving.

Pro Tip: For a nuttier flavor, substitute part of the rice for wild rice or add toasted almonds during the final steps.

Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel

Sweet potatoes make their annual debut on holiday tables with good reason—they’re the pinnacle of cozy, seasonal comfort. This recipe streamlines the process into a single pot, saving time while still delivering the sweet-savory goodness everyone loves.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter (for streusel)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, add sweet potatoes and cover them with water. Bring to a boil and cook until tender, about 10 minutes. Drain and return to the pot.
  2. Mash sweet potatoes while warm and mix in butter, brown sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg until smooth.
  3. In a small bowl, combine pecans, flour, and the additional butter to form a crumbly streusel topping.
  4. Spread the mashed sweet potatoes evenly in the pot, topping them with the streusel mixture.
  5. Place the pot in an oven-safe position and bake in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 15 minutes, or until the topping crisps slightly.

Pro Tip: To add an indulgent twist, top the casserole with mini marshmallows during the last 5 minutes of baking.

Cheesy Broccoli and Rice Casserole

This one-pot wonder combines two holiday staples—broccoli and creamy rice—into a dish that’s comforting, cheesy, and a cinch to prepare while maintaining maximum flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup rice (long-grain white or brown)
  • 2 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter, melted
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine rice, broth, and milk in a large pot over medium heat. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for about 15 minutes until the rice is almost tender.
  2. Stir in broccoli florets, cover, and cook for another 5 minutes until the broccoli is bright green and tender.
  3. Remove from heat and mix in melted butter, paprika, and most of the cheddar cheese (reserving about 1/2 cup for topping).
  4. Spread the mixture evenly in the pot, top with the remaining cheese, and place under the broiler for 3-4 minutes to melt and brown the cheese slightly.
  5. Serve warm and watch it disappear from the table.

Pro Tip: Add a pinch of garlic powder or chili flakes for a bit of extra punch.

Holidays are busy and exciting, so whether you’re hosting family for dinner or preparing dishes to bring to a potluck, one-pot holiday side dishes ensure you spend less time cleaning and more time savoring the season. With these recipes in your arsenal, you can create versatile and crowd-pleasing sides that bring joy to every plate. Happy cooking!
