Delicious Vegan Tacos for Taco Night


Taco night is a beloved tradition in many households, bringing together friends and family over a spread of vibrant flavors and textures. Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, or simply looking to explore plant-based alternatives, vegan tacos can be just as satisfying and delicious as their meat-filled counterparts. Packed with wholesome ingredients and topped with fresh salsas, creamy guacamole, and a variety of toppings, these vegan tacos are sure to be a hit at your next taco night.

In this post, we will guide you through a variety of vegan taco recipes that are easy to make, full of flavor, and perfect for customizing to suit your tastes. From savory jackfruit and seasoned lentils to smoky roasted vegetables and fresh salsa, vegan tacos can be as diverse and creative as you want them to be.

The Basics of Vegan Taco Night

The beauty of taco night lies in its versatility. Each taco is like a blank canvas, ready to be filled with an endless combination of ingredients. For a vegan version, we replace the usual meat options with plant-based alternatives that still deliver on flavor, texture, and satisfaction.

Start by preparing a variety of fillings. You can go with simple, hearty beans, roasted veggies, or mock meats like jackfruit or tempeh. Then, add all the taco toppings that you love, such as guacamole, salsa, shredded lettuce, fresh cilantro, and lime wedges.

Here are some essential ingredients to get you started:

  • Taco Shells: You can use soft corn tortillas, flour tortillas, or even crispy taco shells. Corn tortillas work particularly well for vegan tacos as they have a slightly earthy flavor and hold up well with moist fillings.
  • Base Fillings: These can range from spiced beans, lentils, roasted veggies, tempeh, or jackfruit. Feel free to mix and match.
  • Toppings: Toppings make tacos truly unique and exciting. From creamy guacamole and spicy salsa to tangy pickled onions and crispy lettuce, the possibilities are endless.

Now that you know the basics, let’s dive into some mouthwatering vegan taco recipes that are sure to elevate your next taco night.

Recipe 1: Smoky Roasted Vegetable Tacos

These smoky roasted vegetable tacos are a fantastic way to enjoy seasonal vegetables in a vibrant, flavorful way. Roasting vegetables at a high temperature caramelizes their natural sugars, making them irresistibly sweet and savory.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cauliflower florets
  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas or soft flour tortillas

Toppings:

  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Lime wedges
  • Vegan sour cream or cashew cream
  • Sliced avocado
  • Salsa or pico de gallo

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cauliflower, sweet potato, bell pepper, and red onion. Drizzle with olive oil and toss the vegetables to coat evenly.
  3. Sprinkle the smoked paprika, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper over the vegetables. Toss again to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.
  4. Spread the vegetables in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
  5. While the vegetables are roasting, warm your tortillas on a dry skillet over medium heat until they’re soft and slightly charred.
  6. Once the vegetables are done, assemble your tacos by spooning the roasted vegetables onto each tortilla.
  7. Top with fresh cilantro, a squeeze of lime, sliced avocado, vegan sour cream, and a spoonful of salsa. Serve immediately and enjoy!

These roasted vegetable tacos are a perfect combination of smokiness, earthiness, and a touch of sweetness from the roasted sweet potato. They’re also highly customizable, so feel free to switch out the vegetables based on what you have on hand.

Recipe 2: Jackfruit Tacos with Avocado Crema

Jackfruit is an incredibly versatile fruit that, when cooked, has a texture similar to pulled pork or shredded chicken. This makes it a fantastic meat substitute for tacos. In this recipe, jackfruit is simmered in a savory, smoky sauce, then topped with a creamy avocado crema for a delicious and satisfying taco.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned young green jackfruit, drained and shredded
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas or soft flour tortillas

Avocado Crema:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/4 cup coconut yogurt or vegan sour cream
  • Juice of 1/2 lime
  • Salt to taste

Toppings:

  • Fresh cilantro
  • Sliced red onion
  • Pickled jalapeños
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
  3. Add the shredded jackfruit, cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, vegetable broth, soy sauce, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Simmer the mixture over low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the jackfruit is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  5. While the jackfruit is cooking, make the avocado crema. In a blender or food processor, combine the avocado, coconut yogurt (or vegan sour cream), lime juice, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  6. Warm your tortillas on a skillet or griddle until soft and pliable.
  7. To assemble the tacos, spoon the jackfruit mixture onto each tortilla. Top with a dollop of avocado crema, fresh cilantro, sliced red onion, pickled jalapeños, and a squeeze of lime juice.

The jackfruit in these tacos provides a hearty, satisfying texture, while the avocado crema adds a smooth, rich contrast. This recipe is a great option for taco night when you want something comforting but with a unique twist.

Recipe 3: Spicy Lentil Tacos with Mango Salsa

Lentils are a great source of plant-based protein and work wonderfully as a filling for tacos. In this recipe, lentils are cooked in a spicy tomato-based sauce and paired with a sweet and tangy mango salsa for an explosion of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Corn tortillas or soft flour tortillas

Mango Salsa:

  • 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Toppings:

  • Fresh cilantro
  • Sliced avocado
  • Vegan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, cook the lentils in vegetable broth according to package instructions until tender, about 20-25 minutes. Drain any excess liquid.
  2. While the lentils are cooking, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute, until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, cumin, chili powder, turmeric, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, and pepper. Let the mixture simmer for 5 minutes, then add the cooked lentils to the pan. Stir to combine and cook for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. To make the mango salsa, combine the diced mango, red onion, bell pepper, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Stir well and set aside.
  6. Warm the tortillas on a skillet or in the microwave.
  7. To assemble the tacos, spoon the spicy lentil mixture onto each tortilla. Top with a generous helping of mango salsa, sliced avocado, and fresh cilantro. If you like, add some vegan cheese for extra creaminess.

These spicy lentil tacos with mango salsa offer the perfect balance of heat, sweetness, and freshness. The mango salsa adds a pop of color and flavor that contrasts beautifully with the savory lentils.

Conclusion

Vegan tacos are not only delicious but also versatile, offering endless opportunities for creativity. Whether you’re roasting vegetables, simmering jackfruit in smoky spices, or making a hearty lentil filling, these plant-based tacos will satisfy even the most discerning taco lover. Taco night is a chance to get creative, so don’t hesitate to experiment with different fillings, toppings, and salsas to make each taco unique.

So, gather your friends and family, set up a taco bar with all your favorite toppings, and enjoy these tasty vegan tacos at your next taco night!
