Sous Vide BBQ Pulled Jackfruit (Vegan Recipe)


When it comes to plant-based recipes, jackfruit has taken the world by storm as a versatile meat substitute. Its ability to mimic the texture of pulled pork, chicken, and even beef, while absorbing bold and savory flavors, has made it a go-to ingredient for many vegan and vegetarian dishes. One of the most beloved ways to enjoy jackfruit is in a BBQ pulled form, and when combined with sous vide cooking, it elevates the dish to a whole new level of deliciousness.

In this recipe, we’ll dive into how to prepare mouth-watering, tender, and flavorful Sous Vide BBQ Pulled Jackfruit. This dish will wow your taste buds, whether you’re a dedicated vegan or simply looking to try something new and exciting. Let’s get started!

What Is Jackfruit?

Jackfruit is a large tropical fruit native to Southwest India. While its fruit is sweet and typically used in desserts, the young, green jackfruit is prized for its meat-like texture. When cooked, jackfruit shreds into fibrous, tender pieces that resemble pulled meat, making it an ideal plant-based substitute for traditional BBQ recipes.

Rich in fiber, low in calories, and packed with vitamins and minerals, jackfruit is a nutritional powerhouse. It has gained immense popularity in vegan and vegetarian cooking due to its ability to absorb a variety of flavors, making it a perfect choice for BBQ dishes.

Why Sous Vide?

Sous vide cooking is a method that involves cooking food in a vacuum-sealed bag submerged in a temperature-controlled water bath for an extended period. This technique is known for producing incredibly tender, juicy, and evenly cooked food. It ensures that the ingredients retain their full flavor while achieving the perfect texture.

For pulled jackfruit, sous vide cooking is the ideal technique. It allows the jackfruit to slowly absorb the BBQ sauce, breaking down its fibers while keeping it incredibly moist and flavorful. The result is a tender, melt-in-your-mouth pulled jackfruit that will impress even the most skeptical carnivores.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this delicious Sous Vide BBQ Pulled Jackfruit, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Jackfruit:

  • 2 cans of young green jackfruit in brine (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the BBQ Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup (preferably organic)
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 2 tablespoons mustard (yellow or Dijon, depending on your preference)
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari (for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon liquid smoke (optional but adds depth of flavor)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Burger buns (for serving)
  • Pickles (optional but adds a tangy crunch)
  • Coleslaw (optional)

Equipment Needed

  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • Large pot or container for the water bath
  • Vacuum-seal bags or resealable freezer bags (with the water displacement method)
  • A sauté pan for browning the jackfruit
  • A large bowl for tossing the jackfruit in the BBQ sauce

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep the Jackfruit

Start by preparing the jackfruit. Drain the canned jackfruit and rinse it thoroughly to remove any excess brine. Depending on the brand, you might find that some pieces are too large, so cut them into smaller chunks that are easier to work with. You can use a fork or your hands to shred the jackfruit into bite-sized pieces.

Step 2: Sauté the Jackfruit

Heat olive oil in a sauté pan over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and garlic, cooking until fragrant and softened, about 3-4 minutes. Then, add the shredded jackfruit to the pan, stirring it well to combine with the onions and garlic.

Next, add the smoked paprika, ground cumin, chili powder, and apple cider vinegar. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and cook for another 2-3 minutes. This step allows the jackfruit to absorb the spices and flavors, setting the foundation for a rich and savory BBQ flavor.

Step 3: Prepare the BBQ Sauce

While the jackfruit is cooking, prepare the BBQ sauce. In a medium saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, mustard, smoked paprika, onion powder, garlic powder, soy sauce, and liquid smoke (if using). Stir the mixture well, bringing it to a simmer over medium heat. Let it cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Once it’s done, remove it from the heat and set it aside.

Step 4: Vacuum Seal the Jackfruit

Now that your jackfruit is seasoned and the BBQ sauce is prepared, it’s time to bring in the sous vide magic. Transfer the seasoned jackfruit into a vacuum-seal bag or a resealable freezer bag if you don’t have a vacuum sealer.

Pour the BBQ sauce over the jackfruit, ensuring that it’s evenly coated. If you’re using a vacuum-seal bag, seal it tightly. For a resealable bag, use the water displacement method: submerge the bag in water, pressing out as much air as possible before sealing the bag completely.

Step 5: Sous Vide Cooking

Fill a large pot or container with water and attach your sous vide precision cooker. Set the temperature to 185°F (85°C). Once the water has reached the desired temperature, submerge the bag with the jackfruit and cook it for 2-3 hours. The long cooking time ensures that the jackfruit becomes tender and fully absorbs the BBQ flavors.

Step 6: Shred the Jackfruit

After the sous vide cooking time is complete, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Open the bag and transfer the jackfruit to a large bowl. Use two forks to shred the jackfruit further, pulling it apart into tender, stringy pieces. The BBQ sauce will already be infused into the jackfruit, adding to its delicious flavor.

Step 7: Sauté for Extra Flavor (Optional)

If you prefer a bit of crispy texture and caramelization on your jackfruit, you can take it a step further. Heat a small amount of olive oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Add the shredded jackfruit and sauté for 5-7 minutes until some of the edges become crispy and browned. This step adds extra texture and flavor but is optional.

Step 8: Assemble Your BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches

To serve, toast your burger buns for a nice crispness. Pile the pulled jackfruit high onto the bottom half of the bun, and top it with pickles, coleslaw, or any other toppings you desire. Place the top bun on and serve immediately.

Tips for the Perfect BBQ Pulled Jackfruit

  • Using Fresh Jackfruit: If you can find fresh jackfruit, feel free to use it instead of canned jackfruit. Just make sure you use the young, unripe variety, as ripe jackfruit is too sweet for savory dishes.

  • Flavor Variations: Feel free to adjust the BBQ sauce to your taste. You can add more heat by incorporating cayenne pepper, or add a touch of sweetness with brown sugar or molasses.

  • Serving Suggestions: In addition to sandwiches, BBQ pulled jackfruit is excellent served with mashed potatoes, in tacos, or as a topping for baked potatoes. The possibilities are endless!

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This Sous Vide BBQ Pulled Jackfruit recipe is a game-changer. The sous vide method ensures that the jackfruit retains moisture, absorbs flavors deeply, and becomes melt-in-your-mouth tender. The homemade BBQ sauce adds a perfect balance of tanginess, smokiness, and sweetness that complements the jackfruit beautifully.

Whether you’re serving it to friends at a summer BBQ, enjoying it as a weeknight meal, or bringing it to a potluck, this dish will impress everyone. It’s satisfying, comforting, and packed with flavor, offering a plant-based alternative to traditional pulled pork that everyone can enjoy.

Health Benefits of Jackfruit

Jackfruit is a nutrient-dense fruit that offers numerous health benefits:

  • High in fiber: Jackfruit is an excellent source of dietary fiber, promoting healthy digestion and supporting weight management.
  • Rich in antioxidants: Packed with vitamin C, jackfruit helps boost the immune system and protect against oxidative stress.
  • Low in calories: A perfect option for those looking to enjoy a hearty meal without the extra calories.
  • Loaded with vitamins and minerals: Jackfruit contains potassium, magnesium, and folate, supporting overall health and well-being.

Final Thoughts

Sous Vide BBQ Pulled Jackfruit is a perfect addition to any plant-based menu. It’s flavorful, tender, and easy to prepare, making it a go-to recipe for vegan BBQ lovers. The sous vide technique takes it to the next level, ensuring that each bite is bursting with smoky, tangy, and savory goodness.

Whether you’re new to cooking with jackfruit or an experienced vegan chef, this recipe will become a staple in your kitchen. Give it a try, and let the magic of sous vide and BBQ flavors transform your next meal!
