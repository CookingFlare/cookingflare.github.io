Classic British Fish and Chips: 5 Recipes for a Pub Night


Are you craving a hearty and satisfying meal that will transport you to the bustling pubs of Britain? Look no further than the classic British fish and chips! This iconic dish, consisting of crispy battered fish and golden chips, is a beloved favorite that has stood the test of time. In this blog post, we will explore five different recipes for a perfect pub night at home. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let’s dive into the world of classic British fish and chips!

1. Traditional Beer-Battered Fish and Chips

Ingredients:

  • Fresh white fish fillets (such as cod or haddock)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Salt and pepper
  • Beer (lager or ale)
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Potatoes, cut into thick chips
  • Malt vinegar, for serving
  • Tartar sauce or mushy peas, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Gradually whisk in the beer until a smooth batter forms.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 180°C (350°F).
  3. Dip each fish fillet into the batter, ensuring it is evenly coated. Carefully lower the battered fish into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crisp. Remove and drain on a paper towel.
  4. In the same oil, fry the potato chips until golden and crispy. Remove and drain on a paper towel.
  5. Serve the fish and chips piping hot with a sprinkle of salt, malt vinegar, and your choice of tartar sauce or mushy peas. Enjoy!

2. Panko-Crusted Fish and Sweet Potato Wedges

Ingredients:

  • Fresh white fish fillets
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper
  • Sweet potatoes, cut into wedges
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Season the fish fillets with salt and pepper. Dip each fillet into the beaten eggs, then coat with panko breadcrumbs, pressing lightly to adhere.
  3. Place the breaded fish fillets onto the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil.
  4. Toss the sweet potato wedges with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them on the same baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the sweet potato wedges are golden and crispy.
  6. Serve with lemon wedges for a zesty touch.

3. Oven-Baked Fish and Chips with a Twist

Ingredients:

  • Fresh white fish fillets
  • All-purpose flour
  • Paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder
  • Salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil
  • Potatoes, cut into thick chips
  • Malt vinegar, for serving
  • Tartar sauce or lemon wedges, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Lightly coat each fish fillet in the seasoned flour mixture, shaking off any excess.
  4. Place the floured fish fillets onto the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle with vegetable oil.
  5. Toss the potato chips with vegetable oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them on the same baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the chips are crispy and golden.
  7. Serve with a sprinkle of salt, malt vinegar, and your choice of tartar sauce or lemon wedges.

4. Gluten-Free Fish and Chips

Ingredients:

  • Fresh white fish fillets
  • Gluten-free flour blend
  • Salt and pepper
  • Sparkling water
  • Vegetable oil
  • Potatoes, cut into thick chips
  • Malt vinegar, for serving
  • Tartar sauce or lemon wedges, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend, salt, and pepper. Gradually whisk in the sparkling water until a smooth batter forms.
  3. Dip each fish fillet into the gluten-free batter, ensuring it is evenly coated. Place on the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle with vegetable oil.
  4. Toss the potato chips with vegetable oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them on the same baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the chips are crispy and golden.
  6. Serve with a sprinkle of salt, malt vinegar, and your choice of tartar sauce or lemon wedges.

5. Baked Fish and Veggie Chips

Ingredients:

  • Fresh white fish fillets
  • Bread crumbs
  • Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper
  • Zucchini, cut into thin strips
  • Carrots, cut into thin strips
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine the bread crumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper.
  3. Lightly coat each fish fillet in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing lightly to adhere.
  4. Place the breaded fish fillets onto the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil.
  5. Toss the zucchini and carrot strips with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them on the same baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the veggie chips are crispy.
  7. Serve with lemon wedges for a refreshing twist.

There you have it – five delicious recipes for classic British fish and chips that will make your pub night at home truly special. Whether you prefer a traditional beer batter or a unique twist, there’s a recipe here to suit every taste. So, gather your loved ones, recreate the cozy atmosphere of a British pub, and indulge in this timeless culinary delight. Cheers and enjoy your pub night feast!
