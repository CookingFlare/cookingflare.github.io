Spaghetti with Clams and White Wine Sauce (Spaghetti alle Vongole)


Few dishes embody the essence of Italian coastal cuisine better than Spaghetti alle Vongole, or Spaghetti with Clams and White Wine Sauce. This timeless recipe showcases the simplicity and elegance of Italian cooking, where fresh, high-quality ingredients do all the talking. The combination of sweet, briny clams, al dente spaghetti, garlic, chili, and white wine creates a harmonious meal that transports your taste buds straight to the Mediterranean. Whether it’s a casual family dinner, a date night indulgence, or a celebratory gathering, this dish is sure to impress.

Below, you’ll find a step-by-step guide to making beautifully fragrant Spaghetti alle Vongole, along with tips to ensure you master this iconic pasta dish.

Ingredients

To make Spaghetti alle Vongole, you’ll need just a handful of simple ingredients. Choose the freshest clams and natural, quality components for the most authentic flavor experience. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the dish:

  • 12 oz (340 g) spaghetti – Opt for high-quality durum wheat pasta for perfect texture.
  • 2 lbs (900 g) fresh clams – Littleneck, Manila, or cockles work beautifully.
  • ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil – Adds richness and depth.
  • 4 garlic cloves – Thinly sliced for aromatic goodness.
  • 1 small red chili (or red pepper flakes) – For a gentle kick of heat (adjust to preference).
  • ½ cup dry white wine – Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio complement the clams wonderfully.
  • Handful of fresh parsley – Chopped, for garnish and freshness.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper – To taste.

For prepping the clams (optional but recommended):

  • 2–3 tbsp coarse sea salt – To purge the clams.
  • Cold water – For soaking.

Preparing the Clams

Fresh clams are the hero of this dish, and it’s important to prep them properly. If your clams are already clean and purged, you can skip this step. Otherwise, follow these instructions:

  1. Purge the sand: Place your clams in a large bowl filled with cold water. Add 2–3 tablespoons of coarse sea salt and let them soak for at least 30 minutes, or up to 1 hour. During this time, the clams will expel any sand or grit they may contain.

  2. Rinse thoroughly: After soaking, drain the clams and rinse them under cold running water. Scrub any shells with a brush to ensure they’re clean. Discard any clams that are cracked, broken, or remain open when gently tapped.

Cooking the Pasta

While preparing the clams and sauce, you’ll multitask by cooking your pasta. Follow this method to ensure perfectly al dente spaghetti every time:

  1. Boil water: Fill a large pot with water and add a generous pinch of salt. Bring it to a rolling boil.

  2. Cook the spaghetti: Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions. Aim for al dente texture—firm yet tender. This is key for absorbing the sauce later.

  3. Reserve pasta water: Before draining the spaghetti, scoop out ½ cup of the starchy cooking water. This can be used to adjust the consistency of the final dish.

  4. Drain and set aside: Drain the pasta and set it aside while you prepare the sauce. Tossing the spaghetti in a little olive oil can prevent sticking.

Making the Sauce

Now that the clams are prepped and the pasta is ready, it’s time to build the dish’s signature savory sauce. Timing is essential here, as you want to keep things quick to preserve the clams’ delicate flavor.

  1. Heat the olive oil: In a large skillet or sauté pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.

  2. Add garlic and chili: Toss in the thinly sliced garlic and your chopped red chili (or a pinch of pepper flakes). Sauté gently for 1–2 minutes, just until fragrant. Be cautious not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

  3. Cook the clams: Add the cleaned clams to the skillet and pour in the white wine. Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid and increase the heat to medium-high. Allow the clams to steam for 5–7 minutes, or until they open. Discard any clams that remain closed, as they’re not safe to eat.

  4. Season the sauce: Once the clams are cooked, taste the sauce and season with salt and black pepper, if needed. Keep in mind that clams are naturally briny, so go easy on the salt.

  5. Add the pasta: Transfer the drained spaghetti to the skillet with the clams and sauce. Toss gently to combine, allowing the pasta to absorb the flavors. If the sauce seems dry, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it.

Serving Your Spaghetti alle Vongole

Presentation matters, especially for a dish as beautiful as Spaghetti alle Vongole. Here’s how to plate and serve it like a pro:

  1. Garnish generously: Once plated, sprinkle the chopped parsley over the spaghetti. Fresh parsley adds a bright, herbaceous contrast to the dish.

  2. Drizzle with olive oil: A final drizzle of high-quality extra virgin olive oil enhances the aroma and richness.

  3. Serve immediately: Spaghetti alle Vongole is best enjoyed fresh and hot. Pair it with crusty bread to soak up the sauce and a glass of chilled white wine for the full Italian experience.

Tips & Tricks for Perfect Spaghetti alle Vongole

For an irresistible rendition of this dish, keep the following tips in mind:

  1. Choose the right wine: Dry white wine is essential for balancing the sweetness of the clams. Avoid overly sweet varieties; instead, opt for crisp and acidic options.

  2. Timing is key: Spaghetti alle Vongole comes together quickly. Ensure all your ingredients are prepped and ready before you start cooking.

  3. Avoid overcooking the clams: Steam clams until they open, then immediately remove them from heat. Overcooking can turn their texture rubbery.

  4. Get creative: For an extra layer of flavor, you can add freshly grated lemon zest or a squeeze of lemon juice when garnishing the finished dish.

A Feast for the Senses

Spaghetti alle Vongole is more than just a meal; it’s a celebration of simple ingredients coming together in perfect harmony. With every bite, the sweetness of the clams dances with the bright acidity of the wine and the subtle heat of the chili, while the olive oil ties it all together with velvety richness. This classic Italian recipe proves that elegance doesn’t require complexity, but rather an appreciation for the natural flavors of each element.

By following this guide, you’ll be able to create restaurant-quality Spaghetti alle Vongole right in your own kitchen—a dish that’s bound to leave a lasting impression on anyone fortunate enough to share it with you.

So next time you’re looking to elevate your dinner game, dive into this coastal Italian treasure. The sea-infused aroma, the glistening strands of pasta coated in golden sauce, and the joy of scooping out tender clams from their shells await you. Buon appetito!
