Savory Easter Quiche Recipes: Perfect for Brunch


Easter brunch is one of those occasions where the food is just as important as the company, and a savory quiche is a perfect dish to grace the table. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or looking for a delicious way to celebrate the season, these savory Easter quiche recipes will elevate your brunch game. Quiche is not only versatile and easy to make, but it can also be prepared in advance, saving you time on the day of your event.

Quiche is a dish that combines a rich, flavorful filling with a buttery, flaky crust, making it a fantastic choice for a brunch spread. From classic combinations to more adventurous flavors, there is a savory quiche recipe for every taste. Let’s dive into some creative and delectable quiche options that will impress your guests this Easter.

The Classic Quiche Lorraine

Let’s start with the classic. Quiche Lorraine is a timeless favorite that pairs crispy bacon with creamy custard, all nestled in a flaky pie crust. This traditional French dish has stood the test of time for a reason — it’s utterly delicious and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade if you prefer)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup grated Gruyère cheese
  • 1/2 cup cooked bacon, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish, and press it gently into the sides. Set aside.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, milk, salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  4. Stir in the grated Gruyère cheese, cooked bacon, and onions (if using).
  5. Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and golden brown on top. Let it cool for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

The combination of crispy bacon and creamy custard creates a rich and savory flavor that’s always a hit at any brunch. It’s simple, elegant, and sure to please.

Spinach and Mushroom Quiche

For a lighter option that still packs a punch of flavor, try a spinach and mushroom quiche. This vegetarian delight is perfect for those who prefer a meatless dish but don’t want to sacrifice on taste. Earthy mushrooms and fresh spinach make for a delightful filling that pairs perfectly with a rich egg custard.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup fresh mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and onions, cooking until softened and the mushrooms release their moisture, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the spinach and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, heavy cream, mozzarella, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stir in the sautéed mushroom and spinach mixture.
  6. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown and the center is set.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

This spinach and mushroom quiche is rich in flavor and provides a savory option for those looking for a vegetarian dish. It’s light enough to enjoy with a refreshing salad or as part of a larger brunch spread.

Ham and Asparagus Quiche

Asparagus is synonymous with spring, and its fresh, grassy flavor makes it the perfect addition to an Easter quiche. Combined with hearty ham and a rich, savory custard, this quiche is a springtime delight. It’s a wonderful choice for brunch, as it brings a seasonal flair that celebrates the beauty of the season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup cooked ham, diced
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté the asparagus until tender, about 4-5 minutes.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, heavy cream, cheddar cheese, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add the diced ham and sautéed asparagus to the egg mixture and stir to combine.
  5. Pour the filling into the prepared pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and golden brown on top.
  6. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing.

This ham and asparagus quiche is perfect for Easter brunch as it features seasonal vegetables and hearty, flavorful ham. It’s a crowd-pleaser that showcases the best of spring’s bounty.

Mediterranean Quiche with Feta and Olives

If you’re looking for something a little more exotic, a Mediterranean-inspired quiche is a great way to introduce new flavors into your Easter brunch spread. This quiche combines briny olives, creamy feta cheese, and aromatic herbs, creating a savory pie that transports you to the sunny Mediterranean coast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, heavy cream, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir in the crumbled feta, olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and oregano.
  4. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is golden brown and the filling is set.
  5. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

The combination of feta cheese, olives, and sun-dried tomatoes gives this quiche a tangy and savory flavor that pairs wonderfully with a light salad or roasted vegetables.

Tips for Perfect Quiche Every Time

While quiche is relatively simple to prepare, there are a few tips that can ensure your dish comes out perfectly every time.

1. Use Fresh Eggs

The foundation of a good quiche is fresh eggs. Fresh eggs produce a smoother, creamier texture and help the quiche set properly. When you crack an egg, the yolk should be firm and bright yellow, and the whites should be thick and clear.

2. Don’t Overmix the Custard

Whisk the eggs and cream together gently, just enough to combine. Overmixing can result in a custard that is too airy, leading to a less-than-ideal texture in the finished quiche.

3. Let the Quiche Cool Slightly

After baking, let your quiche cool for 10-15 minutes before slicing. This allows the filling to set and prevents it from being too runny.

4. Blind Bake the Crust

For a perfectly crisp pie crust, blind bake it before adding the filling. This step involves pre-baking the crust for 10-15 minutes before filling it. This ensures the crust stays crispy and doesn’t become soggy under the custard.

5. Customize Your Fillings

One of the best things about quiche is that it’s highly customizable. Feel free to swap out ingredients depending on what you have on hand or your personal preferences. Add roasted vegetables, different cheeses, or meats to create your own signature quiche.

Conclusion

Savory quiche is the perfect dish for Easter brunch, offering a rich and satisfying meal that can be made ahead of time and served with minimal fuss. Whether you opt for a classic quiche Lorraine, a spring-inspired ham and asparagus version, or something more adventurous like a Mediterranean-style quiche, there’s no doubt that these dishes will impress your guests and make your brunch unforgettable.

Experiment with different flavors, and don’t be afraid to make your quiche your own. The versatility of quiche makes it a go-to dish for any occasion, and Easter brunch is the perfect time to showcase your culinary skills and create lasting memories with friends and family.

So gather your ingredients, preheat the oven, and get ready to indulge in the savory goodness of a homemade quiche this Easter.
