Buttery and Delicious Raspberry Crumble Bars: A Perfect Summer Dessert


There’s something magical about the combination of buttery crust, sweet raspberry filling, and crumbly topping that makes Raspberry Crumble Bars the ultimate summer dessert. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a sweet treat, these bars are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of the season. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating these delectable bars, from preparing the buttery crust to baking them to golden perfection.

Ingredients

For the Crust and Crumble Topping:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Raspberry Filling:

  • 3 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust and Crumble Topping

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang for easy removal.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and lemon zest.
  3. Add the cold butter cubes to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and vanilla extract. Pour this mixture into the flour-butter mixture and stir until the dough comes together.
  5. Set aside 3/4 cup of the mixture for the crumble topping, then press the remaining dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan.

Step 2: Make the Raspberry Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the raspberries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and the raspberries break down, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Remove the raspberry filling from the heat and let it cool slightly.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Pour the raspberry filling over the prepared crust, spreading it out evenly.
  2. Sprinkle the reserved crumble topping over the raspberry filling.
  3. Place the baking pan in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown.
  4. Allow the raspberry crumble bars to cool completely in the pan before using the parchment paper overhang to lift them out and transfer to a cutting board.
  5. Cut into squares and serve at room temperature.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra layer of flavor, consider adding a handful of fresh basil leaves to the raspberry filling for a hint of herbal freshness.
  • If raspberries are not in season, you can use frozen raspberries for the filling. Simply thaw and drain them before using.

In Conclusion

These Buttery and Delicious Raspberry Crumble Bars are a delightful way to enjoy the abundance of summer raspberries. The buttery crust, sweet-tart raspberry filling, and crumbly topping come together to create a harmonious balance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re making them for a summer gathering or simply to enjoy with a cup of tea, these bars are sure to become a favorite in your dessert repertoire. Embrace the flavors of the season and indulge in the simple pleasure of homemade treats with these delightful Raspberry Crumble Bars.
