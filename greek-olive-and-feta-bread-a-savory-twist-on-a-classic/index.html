Greek Olive and Feta Bread: A Savory Twist on a Classic


When it comes to traditional Greek cuisine, there are a few staple ingredients that immediately come to mind: olives, feta cheese, and bread. While each of these components can be enjoyed on its own, combining them in a single dish can result in something truly special. In this recipe, we’ll be exploring the perfect union of Greek olive and feta bread, a savory twist on a classic that’s sure to please even the most discerning palates.

The Bread

The foundation of our Greek olive and feta bread is, of course, the bread itself. For this recipe, we’ll be using a classic Greek bread recipe, with a few tweaks to incorporate the star ingredients. Begin by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of warm water, 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes, or until the yeast is activated and frothy.

Next, add in 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 tablespoon of sugar. Mix the dough until it forms a sticky ball, then turn it out onto a floured surface and knead for 5-10 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic.

Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

The Filling

While the dough is rising, it’s time to prepare the filling. In a small bowl, crumble 1/2 cup of feta cheese into small pieces. Add in 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley and 2 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil, mixing until the cheese is well coated.

Assembly and Baking

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a long, thin rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick.

Spread the feta cheese mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Sprinkle with 1/4 cup of chopped Kalamata olives and 1/4 cup of crumbled feta cheese.

Roll the dough into a tight log, starting from one of the long edges. Place the log seam-side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

Bake the bread for 35-40 minutes, or until it’s golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Serving

Once the bread is cool enough to handle, slice it into thick rounds and serve. You can enjoy it on its own, or paired with a variety of accompaniments such as olive oil, hummus, or grilled meats.

Tips and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor to your bread, try sprinkling some chopped fresh oregano or thyme on top of the feta cheese mixture before rolling the dough.
  • For a more intense olive flavor, use a higher-quality olive oil or add in some chopped green olives to the filling.
  • If you prefer a crisper crust, try baking the bread for an additional 5-10 minutes.

Conclusion

Greek olive and feta bread is a classic recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your household. With its crispy crust, tangy feta cheese, and briny olives, it’s the perfect accompaniment to a variety of meals and snacks. So next time you’re looking for a savory twist on a classic, give this recipe a try – your taste buds will thank you!
