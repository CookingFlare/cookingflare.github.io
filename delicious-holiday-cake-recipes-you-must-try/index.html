Delicious Holiday Cake Recipes You Must Try


The holiday season is the perfect time to gather with family and friends, create lasting memories, and indulge in delicious food. Among the various desserts that make their way to the table, cakes often take center stage. From decadent chocolate creations to fruity delights, holiday cakes add a sweet touch to your celebrations. Whether you’re planning a Christmas party, a New Year’s gathering, or simply want to bake something special for the season, these holiday cake recipes will impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of cakes you can bake for the holidays, each bursting with festive flavors and textures. These recipes are perfect for beginners and seasoned bakers alike, providing options that cater to different tastes and preferences. So, let’s dive into these delicious holiday cake recipes that you must try this season!

1. Classic Christmas Fruitcake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed dried fruits (raisins, currants, chopped dates)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup dark brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground allspice
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • ½ cup brandy or orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • ½ cup chopped candied ginger
  • ½ cup candied orange peel (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan. Line the bottom with parchment paper for easy removal.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, spices, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the butter mixture, mixing until just combined.
  5. Stir in the dried fruits, nuts, ginger, candied peel, and orange zest.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Bake for 1 ½ to 2 hours, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Once baked, allow the fruitcake to cool completely in the pan. For an extra festive touch, wrap the cooled cake in cheesecloth soaked with brandy or orange juice. Let it sit for a few days before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

This classic fruitcake is rich in flavor, with a beautiful balance of spices and sweet fruits. It’s a holiday tradition that never goes out of style!

2. Spiced Gingerbread Cake

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup molasses
  • 1 cup hot water
  • 1 tbsp freshly grated ginger (optional)
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9x13-inch baking pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, spices, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. Stir in the molasses and grated ginger (if using).
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with the hot water, and mix until smooth.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Let the gingerbread cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, dust with powdered sugar for a festive touch.

This spiced gingerbread cake is full of warm, aromatic flavors. It’s perfect for the holiday season and pairs wonderfully with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

3. Eggnog Pound Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup eggnog
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C) and grease and flour a 10-inch bundt pan.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy and pale.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. In a separate bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, spices, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, alternating with the eggnog, and mix until smooth.
  6. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared bundt pan and bake for 1 hour to 1 hour and 10 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

This eggnog pound cake is rich and moist, with the comforting flavors of nutmeg and cinnamon that remind you of the holidays. It’s a perfect centerpiece for your dessert table!

4. Chocolate Peppermint Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1 cup crushed peppermint candies (for garnish)
  • 1 ½ cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp peppermint extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Add the sugar and mix well. Add the eggs, milk, oil, and vanilla extract. Beat until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the boiling water to the batter, mixing until well combined. The batter will be thin, but that’s normal.
  5. Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  7. For the frosting, beat the heavy cream with the powdered sugar and peppermint extract until stiff peaks form.
  8. Once the cakes are cooled, frost the first layer and sprinkle with crushed peppermint candies. Top with the second layer and frost the top of the cake. Garnish with more crushed peppermint.

This chocolate peppermint cake is a delightful combination of rich chocolate and refreshing mint. It’s a perfect cake for holiday gatherings, offering a refreshing twist on traditional chocolate desserts.

5. Lemon Poppy Seed Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 stick unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp poppy seeds
  • ¾ cup buttermilk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. Stir in the vanilla extract, lemon zest, and poppy seeds.
  5. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients, alternating with the buttermilk, and mix until just combined. Stir in the lemon juice.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Dust the cooled cake with powdered sugar for a festive touch.

This lemon poppy seed cake is bright and zesty, with a light and tender crumb. It’s perfect for those who enjoy a refreshing, citrus-flavored dessert during the holiday season.

Conclusion

Holiday cakes bring warmth, joy, and a sense of tradition to your celebrations. Whether you’re making a rich, spiced fruitcake, a refreshing lemon poppy seed cake, or a decadent chocolate peppermint cake, these recipes will surely become favorites in your holiday baking repertoire. So, grab your mixing bowls, preheat the oven, and get ready to bake up some sweet holiday memories with these delightful cakes!
