How to Make Homemade Chimichurri Sauce


Chimichurri sauce is a vibrant and zesty condiment that originates from Argentina and Uruguay, commonly served alongside grilled meats. It’s a versatile sauce that not only elevates grilled steaks but also complements chicken, seafood, vegetables, and even rice. With its fresh ingredients and simple preparation, chimichurri is easy to make at home and can instantly add a burst of flavor to any dish.

In this guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about how to make homemade chimichurri sauce, including the ingredients, the steps, and various ways to use it in your cooking. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner, this recipe will quickly become a staple in your kitchen.

What Is Chimichurri Sauce?

Chimichurri is a uncooked, herb-based sauce made from a combination of parsley, garlic, vinegar, oil, and various spices. It’s known for its tangy, garlicky, and slightly spicy flavor, which complements grilled meats wonderfully. In many ways, chimichurri acts like an Argentine barbecue sauce, offering a fresh and vibrant contrast to rich, smoky grilled dishes.

Traditionally, chimichurri is used as a marinade or as a topping for grilled meats, such as steaks, sausages, and ribs. It’s also commonly served alongside empanadas, grilled vegetables, or roasted potatoes.

The Essential Ingredients for Homemade Chimichurri Sauce

The beauty of chimichurri lies in its simplicity. With only a few ingredients, this sauce is packed with flavors that make it an unforgettable addition to your meals. The basic ingredients you’ll need are:

1. Fresh Parsley

Parsley is the foundation of chimichurri sauce. It adds a bright, herbaceous flavor that balances the richness of grilled meats. While flat-leaf parsley is the most common choice, curly parsley can also be used depending on your preference.

2. Garlic

Garlic brings a sharp, aromatic punch to the sauce. It’s important to finely mince the garlic so that it blends well with the other ingredients, giving the sauce a smooth, evenly distributed flavor.

3. Red Wine Vinegar

The acidity of red wine vinegar is crucial to the sauce. It adds a tangy note that cuts through the richness of grilled meats and balances the other ingredients. You can substitute with white wine vinegar, but red wine vinegar offers a deeper, more complex flavor.

4. Olive Oil

Extra virgin olive oil is the preferred oil for chimichurri. It adds richness and smoothness to the sauce and helps to emulsify the ingredients together. You can experiment with different olive oils for a slightly different flavor profile, but a high-quality oil is always recommended.

5. Oregano

Oregano is another key flavor in chimichurri. It’s slightly earthy and aromatic, providing a savory base note that complements the other fresh herbs. You can use either fresh or dried oregano depending on what you have available.

6. Red Pepper Flakes

To add some heat to the sauce, red pepper flakes are essential. The level of spice is entirely up to you—use a pinch for a mild heat or more if you prefer a spicier version.

7. Salt and Black Pepper

Seasoning with salt and black pepper helps to balance the flavors. Be sure to taste the sauce as you go, adjusting the seasoning to suit your preferences.

How to Make Homemade Chimichurri Sauce: Step-by-Step Instructions

Making chimichurri sauce at home is quick and straightforward. Follow these simple steps to create a delicious and aromatic sauce that will elevate any dish.

Step 1: Prepare the Herbs

Start by thoroughly washing the fresh parsley and oregano to remove any dirt or impurities. Once clean, pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. Next, finely chop the parsley and oregano. You want the herbs to be small enough so they blend well into the sauce, but not so finely chopped that they become a paste.

Step 2: Mince the Garlic

Peel the garlic cloves and mince them finely. If you want a smoother sauce, you can use a garlic press to crush the garlic, but finely chopping it will also work well. Make sure the garlic is as small as possible to distribute its flavor evenly throughout the sauce.

Step 3: Mix the Ingredients

In a medium-sized mixing bowl, combine the chopped parsley, oregano, and minced garlic. Add the red pepper flakes and season with salt and black pepper to taste. Then, pour in the red wine vinegar and olive oil. Mix everything together until well-combined.

Step 4: Let It Rest

Once all the ingredients are mixed, let the chimichurri sauce sit for at least 15 minutes before serving. This resting period allows the flavors to meld together and become even more vibrant. For best results, you can let it sit for up to an hour, or even refrigerate it overnight. The longer it rests, the more flavorful it becomes.

Step 5: Taste and Adjust

Before serving, taste the chimichurri and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a more tangy sauce, add a bit more vinegar. For a spicier kick, add more red pepper flakes. Don’t be afraid to tweak the recipe until it’s exactly how you like it!

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Once your chimichurri sauce is ready, it’s time to enjoy it! Pour it over your favorite grilled meats, vegetables, or seafood. You can also serve it as a dipping sauce or drizzle it over roasted potatoes, rice, or even bread.

Tips for Making the Best Homemade Chimichurri Sauce

While chimichurri sauce is simple to make, there are a few tips and tricks that can help you perfect the recipe:

1. Use Fresh Herbs

Fresh herbs make a world of difference when it comes to chimichurri. Try to use fresh parsley and oregano whenever possible, as they provide the bright and aromatic flavors that are characteristic of the sauce. Dried herbs can be used in a pinch, but they won’t offer the same vibrant taste.

2. Adjust the Consistency

Chimichurri sauce can vary in consistency depending on personal preference. If you like a chunkier sauce, leave the herbs and garlic a bit more coarsely chopped. For a smoother version, you can pulse the ingredients in a food processor or blender to create a finer texture.

3. Customize the Heat Level

Chimichurri is traditionally made with red pepper flakes for a mild heat, but feel free to adjust the spice level based on your preference. For a milder version, use fewer red pepper flakes or omit them altogether. For more heat, consider adding a finely chopped fresh chili or extra red pepper flakes.

4. Try Different Vinegars

While red wine vinegar is the most traditional choice, you can experiment with different types of vinegar to create variations in flavor. White wine vinegar, apple cider vinegar, or even balsamic vinegar can each add their own unique twist to the sauce.

5. Rest the Sauce

Letting the sauce rest is key to allowing the flavors to develop. While you can use it immediately, giving it time to sit will ensure the herbs infuse the oil and vinegar, making it even more flavorful.

6. Make It Ahead

Chimichurri sauce can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator. In fact, it often tastes even better the next day, as the flavors have had more time to develop. Store the sauce in an airtight container for up to a week. Just make sure to stir it well before using, as the ingredients may separate over time.

How to Use Chimichurri Sauce

While chimichurri is most often associated with grilled meats, there are so many ways to use this sauce in your meals:

1. Grilled Meats and Seafood

The most classic use for chimichurri is as a topping for grilled steaks, sausages, chicken, or seafood. It provides a refreshing contrast to the rich, smoky flavors of the grill.

2. Grilled Vegetables

Chimichurri is a fantastic accompaniment to grilled vegetables. Pour it over grilled bell peppers, zucchini, eggplant, or even corn on the cob for a burst of flavor.

3. Sandwiches and Wraps

Use chimichurri as a spread for sandwiches or wraps. It adds a refreshing flavor and a bit of heat, making it perfect for a variety of sandwiches, from grilled cheese to chicken wraps.

4. Rice and Potatoes

Drizzle chimichurri over a bowl of rice or roasted potatoes for a quick and flavorful side dish. It also pairs beautifully with mashed potatoes or even fries for an easy upgrade.

5. Salads

Chimichurri can be used as a dressing for salads. Simply toss it with your favorite greens, or use it as a dip for a salad of roasted vegetables.

Conclusion

Homemade chimichurri sauce is an incredibly versatile and flavorful addition to any meal. With its bright herbaceous flavor, garlic kick, and tangy vinegar base, it can transform even the simplest of dishes into something extraordinary. Whether you’re grilling meat, drizzling it over vegetables, or adding it to a salad, chimichurri is a sauce that will never disappoint.

The recipe is simple, quick, and easily adaptable to suit your personal taste preferences. So go ahead and give it a try—your taste buds will thank you!
