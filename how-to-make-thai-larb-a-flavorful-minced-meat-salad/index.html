How to Make Thai Larb: A Flavorful Minced Meat Salad


Thai Larb, also known as Larb Moo when made with pork, is a traditional dish from Laos that has gained immense popularity in Thailand and beyond. This vibrant and aromatic minced meat salad is not only a feast for the senses but also a healthy option packed with protein and fresh herbs. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of Larb, its key ingredients, and a step-by-step guide on how to make this delicious dish at home.

The Origins of Larb

Larb is considered the national dish of Laos and is often served at special occasions and gatherings. The dish is believed to have originated from the Laotian people and has since been embraced by Thai cuisine, particularly in the northeastern region known as Isaan. Traditionally, Larb is made with minced meat, which can include chicken, pork, beef, or even fish, and is flavored with a unique blend of herbs, spices, and lime juice. The dish is typically served with sticky rice and fresh vegetables, making it a complete meal that is both satisfying and refreshing.

Key Ingredients for Thai Larb

To create an authentic Thai Larb, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Meat

  • Minced Meat: You can use pork, chicken, beef, or even tofu for a vegetarian version. The choice of meat will influence the flavor and texture of the dish.
  • Fish Sauce: This salty and umami-rich sauce is a staple in Thai cooking and adds depth to the dish.
  • Lime Juice: Freshly squeezed lime juice provides acidity and brightness, balancing the richness of the meat.

For the Seasoning

  • Shallots: Thinly sliced shallots add sweetness and a mild onion flavor.
  • Chili Flakes: Adjust the amount based on your spice tolerance. Thai bird’s eye chilies can also be used for a more authentic kick.
  • Roasted Rice Powder: This unique ingredient adds a nutty flavor and a slight crunch to the salad. It is made by toasting glutinous rice and grinding it into a powder.

For the Fresh Herbs

  • Mint Leaves: Fresh mint adds a refreshing note and is essential for an authentic Larb.
  • Cilantro: Chopped cilantro enhances the flavor and adds a burst of color.
  • Green Onions: Sliced green onions provide a mild onion flavor and a pop of color.

For Serving

  • Lettuce Leaves: Use butter lettuce or romaine leaves to serve the Larb as a wrap.
  • Sticky Rice: This is a traditional accompaniment that complements the flavors of the salad.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Thai Larb

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Before you start cooking, gather all your ingredients and prepare them. Mince the meat if you are not using pre-minced meat, slice the shallots, chop the herbs, and prepare the lime juice. If you are making roasted rice powder, toast about 1/4 cup of glutinous rice in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden brown, then grind it into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.

Step 2: Cook the Meat

  1. Heat a Pan: In a large skillet or frying pan, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the Minced Meat: Once the oil is hot, add the minced meat to the pan. Cook, stirring frequently, until the meat is fully cooked and no longer pink. This should take about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Season the Meat: Once the meat is cooked, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked meat with fish sauce, lime juice, and chili flakes. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

Step 3: Mix in the Fresh Ingredients

  1. Add Shallots: Stir in the thinly sliced shallots, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the meat mixture.
  2. Incorporate the Herbs: Gently fold in the chopped mint, cilantro, and green onions. The fresh herbs are what make Larb truly special, so don’t skimp on them!

Step 4: Add Roasted Rice Powder

  1. Incorporate the Rice Powder: Sprinkle in the roasted rice powder and mix well. This ingredient not only adds flavor but also gives the dish a unique texture.
  2. Taste and Adjust: Taste the Larb and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You may want to add more lime juice or fish sauce depending on your preference.

Step 5: Serve the Larb

  1. Prepare the Serving Dish: Transfer the Larb to a serving platter or individual bowls.
  2. Garnish: Garnish with additional mint and cilantro leaves for a fresh presentation.
