Spicy and Tangy: Indian Butter Chicken Recipe


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine, you’ve likely indulged in the rich, creamy flavors of butter chicken. This iconic dish is a perfect balance of spicy and tangy, with tender pieces of chicken bathed in a luscious tomato-based sauce. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating an authentic and mouthwatering Indian butter chicken dish that will impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 1.5 pounds (700g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt to taste

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 1/2 cups tomato puree
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Marinating the Chicken

  1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, ground cumin, ground coriander, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix until well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight for the flavors to meld.

Step 2: Cooking the Chicken

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the marinated chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through.
  2. Once done, set the chicken aside while you prepare the sauce.

Step 3: Making the Sauce

  1. In a large pan, melt the butter with oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the tomato puree, ground cumin, garam masala, paprika, sugar, and salt. Cook the sauce for 10-15 minutes, allowing it to thicken and the flavors to develop.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream and cooked chicken, stirring gently to coat the chicken with the sauce. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 4: Serving

  1. Transfer the butter chicken to a serving dish and garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.
  2. Serve the butter chicken hot, accompanied by steamed basmati rice or warm naan bread.

Conclusion

The aroma of the spices, the creaminess of the sauce, and the tender chicken will make your homemade Indian butter chicken a culinary triumph. Embrace the vibrant flavors of Indian cuisine and treat yourself to this delightful dish that beautifully balances spiciness and tanginess. Whether it’s for a special occasion or a cozy night in, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Enjoy the rich and complex flavors of this Indian classic, and savor every bite of this spicy and tangy butter chicken masterpiece.
