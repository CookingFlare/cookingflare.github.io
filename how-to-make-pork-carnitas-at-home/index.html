How to Make Pork Carnitas at Home


Pork carnitas, a beloved Mexican dish, translates to “little meats” and refers to a flavorful and tender braised pork that’s been slow-cooked to perfection. Traditionally, pork carnitas is cooked until it reaches a crispy, golden exterior while staying juicy and tender on the inside. The result is an irresistible filling for tacos, burritos, or any dish where you want to add rich, savory flavor.

Making pork carnitas at home is simpler than you might think. Whether you use a slow cooker, Instant Pot, or stovetop, this recipe guides you through the steps to achieve the most tender and flavorful carnitas. In this post, we’ll cover everything from selecting the right cut of pork to how to cook it to perfection.

Ingredients for Pork Carnitas

To make delicious pork carnitas, you’ll need just a few essential ingredients that you might already have in your pantry. Here’s the full list:

  • 4 pounds of pork shoulder (also called pork butt) – This is the best cut of meat for carnitas. The fat marbling and connective tissue will break down as it cooks, creating a tender, juicy texture.
  • 1 onion – A whole onion adds sweetness and depth of flavor to the carnitas.
  • 4 cloves garlic – Garlic infuses the pork with a rich, aromatic flavor.
  • 1 orange – The juice and zest from an orange add citrusy brightness and balance to the rich pork.
  • 1 lime – Lime juice enhances the freshness of the carnitas, balancing the richness of the meat.
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin – A key spice for carnitas, cumin adds warmth and earthiness.
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder – For a hint of heat and smokiness.
  • 1 teaspoon ground oregano – Adds an herby, aromatic flavor to the carnitas.
  • 1 teaspoon paprika – This mild spice deepens the flavor profile.
  • 1 bay leaf – Bay leaves add subtle, herbal notes as the pork cooks.
  • 1 cup chicken broth – Helps create the braising liquid and ensures the meat stays moist.
  • Salt and pepper – Season to taste.

Once you have your ingredients ready, it’s time to get started!

Preparing the Pork Shoulder

Start by preparing your pork shoulder. First, trim any large pieces of excess fat from the meat. While you want some fat for flavor and tenderness, you don’t want to be left with an overwhelming amount. Cut the pork shoulder into large chunks, about 2 to 3 inches in size. These chunks will ensure the meat braises evenly and cook more quickly.

Season the pork with salt, pepper, and the spices (cumin, chili powder, oregano, and paprika). You want to coat the meat generously, ensuring each piece is well-seasoned. For additional flavor, you can rub the spices in with your hands to make sure everything is evenly distributed.

Searing the Pork (Optional Step)

This step is optional but highly recommended, as it enhances the flavor of the carnitas. Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add a little oil and sear the pork chunks on all sides until they develop a nice golden-brown crust. This caramelization helps to deepen the flavors and create a more flavorful carnitas.

Once the pork is seared, transfer the chunks into your slow cooker or pot. If you don’t have a Dutch oven, you can skip this step and move straight to slow cooking.

Slow Cooking the Pork Carnitas

Option 1: Slow Cooker

The slow cooker is the most popular method for making carnitas. It’s low-maintenance, allowing you to set it and forget it while the pork braises.

  • Step 1: Add the seared pork chunks to the slow cooker.
  • Step 2: Pour in the chicken broth, followed by the juice and zest of the orange and lime. Place the bay leaf in the slow cooker and scatter the garlic and onion around the pork.
  • Step 3: Cover and cook on low for 7 to 8 hours or high for 4 to 5 hours. The pork should be fork-tender, easily shreddable with minimal effort.
  • Step 4: Once done, remove the pork from the slow cooker. Discard the bay leaf, and use two forks to shred the meat. The pork should fall apart easily.

Option 2: Stovetop (Dutch Oven)

If you prefer to make pork carnitas on the stovetop, a heavy-duty Dutch oven is ideal for this method.

  • Step 1: Preheat your Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add a little oil, then sear the pork chunks on all sides until brown and crispy.
  • Step 2: Once seared, add the chicken broth, orange juice, lime juice, garlic, and onion to the pot. Stir everything together, ensuring the pork is submerged in the liquid.
  • Step 3: Cover and lower the heat to a simmer. Let it cook for about 2.5 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally. Keep the lid on while the pork braises to trap moisture, ensuring a tender result.
  • Step 4: After 2.5 to 3 hours, check for tenderness. The pork should easily shred with a fork. Remove the meat from the pot, discard the bay leaf, and shred the pork.

Option 3: Instant Pot

For a quicker version of carnitas, the Instant Pot is a great tool. It’ll yield tender, flavorful pork in a fraction of the time.

  • Step 1: Set your Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add oil and sear the pork pieces on all sides.
  • Step 2: Once seared, add the chicken broth, orange juice, lime juice, garlic, and onion to the pot. Stir to combine.
  • Step 3: Close the lid and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 45 minutes. After the cooking time is up, let the pressure release naturally for about 10 minutes before performing a quick release.
  • Step 4: Remove the pork and shred it using two forks.

Crisping the Carnitas

While the pork is perfectly tender after slow cooking, the next step to elevate your carnitas is to crisp up the edges. This step adds texture and extra flavor, and it’s what makes carnitas so irresistible.

  • Step 1: Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  • Step 2: Spread the shredded pork out in an even layer on a baking sheet. You want the pork to be spread out thinly to get as much crispy surface area as possible.
  • Step 3: Place the baking sheet in the oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the edges of the pork are golden and crispy.

Alternatively, if you prefer not to use the oven, you can crisp the carnitas in a skillet. Simply heat a little oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat and add the shredded pork. Let it sit for a few minutes before stirring to get those crispy bits. Continue cooking until the pork is crispy to your liking.

Serving Pork Carnitas

Now that your carnitas are crispy and ready to go, it’s time to serve them up! Pork carnitas are incredibly versatile and can be used in many dishes. Here are a few ways to enjoy them:

  • Tacos: Fill soft corn or flour tortillas with the carnitas and top with diced onions, cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and your favorite salsa.
  • Burritos: Stuff a large flour tortilla with carnitas, rice, beans, guacamole, sour cream, and your favorite salsa for a satisfying meal.
  • Tostadas: Spread refried beans on a crispy tostada shell, top with carnitas, and finish with lettuce, cheese, and salsa.
  • Nachos: Layer tortilla chips with carnitas, cheese, jalapeños, and sour cream for a crowd-pleasing snack.
  • Rice Bowls: Serve the carnitas over cilantro lime rice, beans, and your favorite toppings for a simple and delicious meal.

Storing Leftover Carnitas

Pork carnitas are perfect for meal prep. If you have any leftovers, they store well in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. To reheat, you can microwave the carnitas or heat them in a skillet to get them crispy again. Leftover carnitas also freeze beautifully for up to 3 months. To freeze, allow the pork to cool completely, then transfer it to an airtight container or freezer bag. When you’re ready to eat, defrost in the refrigerator overnight and reheat as desired.

Final Thoughts

Making homemade pork carnitas is a fun and rewarding cooking project that doesn’t require much effort. With the right ingredients, a few hours of cooking, and a little patience, you can enjoy this delicious, versatile dish that’s perfect for a family dinner or a party. Whether you’re making tacos, burritos, or nachos, pork carnitas will undoubtedly be the star of the meal.

So go ahead and try your hand at making carnitas at home! The tender, flavorful pork with crispy bits is sure to impress your friends and family, and you’ll be amazed at how easy it is to make.
