Paleo-Friendly Mexican Seafood Recipes: Spicy and Flavorful


Looking to add some heat and flavor to your paleo-friendly diet? Look no further than these delicious Mexican seafood recipes that are both spicy and full of flavor. From tangy ceviche to smoky grilled shrimp, these dishes will transport your taste buds south of the border. Get ready to spice up your paleo menu with these mouthwatering recipes!

1. Spicy Shrimp Tacos

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 paleo-friendly tortillas
  • Sliced avocado, chopped cilantro, and lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, chili powder, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss to coat them in the spice mixture.
  4. Thread the shrimp onto skewers and grill for 2-3 minutes per side, until they are pink and opaque.
  5. Warm the tortillas on the grill for a minute on each side.
  6. Serve the grilled shrimp in the warm tortillas, topped with sliced avocado, chopped cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.

2. Tangy Ceviche

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of white fish fillets, such as halibut or snapper, diced
  • 1/2 cup of freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/4 cup of freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced avocado and lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the diced fish, lime juice, orange juice, red onion, jalapeno pepper, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stir well to combine, making sure the fish is fully coated in the citrus juices.
  3. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the fish is opaque and firm.
  4. Serve the ceviche chilled, garnished with sliced avocado and lime wedges.

3. Spicy Grilled Salmon

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced avocado and lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, chili powder, cumin, paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the spice mixture onto the salmon fillets, coating them evenly.
  4. Place the salmon fillets on the grill, skin-side down, and grill for 4-5 minutes per side, or until the salmon flakes easily with a fork.
  5. Serve the grilled salmon with sliced avocado and lime wedges.

4. Spicy Guacamole with Shrimp

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1/2 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup of finely chopped red onion
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grilled shrimp for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mash the avocados with a fork until they reach your desired consistency.
  2. Add the diced tomatoes, red onion, jalapeno pepper, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  4. Serve the guacamole with grilled shrimp on top for a spicy and flavorful appetizer.

Conclusion

These paleo-friendly Mexican seafood recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings for spicy and flavorful dishes. Whether you’re grilling shrimp, marinating fish in tangy ceviche, or enjoying a spicy guacamole with shrimp, these recipes will bring the vibrant flavors of Mexico to your paleo menu. So go ahead, spice things up and enjoy a taste of Mexico with these delicious recipes!
