10 Cheap and Easy-to-Make Green Smoothie Recipes


Green smoothies have become incredibly popular in recent years due to their numerous health benefits, including increased energy levels, improved digestion, and even weight loss. However, many people are put off by the high cost of green smoothie recipes, which often feature expensive ingredients like kale, spinach, and avocado. But fear not, dear readers! Today, we’ll be sharing 10 cheap and easy-to-make green smoothie recipes that won’t break the bank.

Recipe 1: Simple Spinach and Banana Smoothie

  • 1 cup frozen spinach
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

Combine all the ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth. This smoothie is a great way to get your daily dose of spinach without breaking the bank.

Recipe 2: Avocado-Free Green Smoothie

  • 2 cups frozen collard greens
  • 1/2 cup frozen pineapple
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup coconut water
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

This smoothie is a game-changer for those who don’t like the taste of avocado. The collard greens provide a rich source of vitamins and minerals, while the pineapple adds a touch of sweetness.

Recipe 3: Spinach and Pear Smoothie

  • 1 cup frozen spinach
  • 1 ripe pear
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

The sweetness of the pear pairs perfectly with the earthiness of the spinach, making this smoothie a delicious and refreshing treat.

Recipe 4: Green Goddess Smoothie

  • 2 cups frozen kale
  • 1/2 cup frozen mango
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup coconut water
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

This smoothie is packed with nutrients and is perfect for those who want to boost their immune system. The kale provides a rich source of vitamins and minerals, while the mango adds a touch of sweetness.

Recipe 5: Spinach and Banana Boost Smoothie

  • 1 cup frozen spinach
  • 1 ripe banana
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 scoop vanilla protein powder
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

This smoothie is a great way to get a boost of energy and protein. The spinach provides a rich source of iron, while the banana adds natural sweetness.

Recipe 6: Green Tea Smoothie

  • 1 cup frozen green tea leaves
  • 1/2 cup frozen pineapple
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup coconut water
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

This smoothie is perfect for those who love the taste of green tea. The green tea leaves provide a rich source of antioxidants, while the pineapple adds a touch of sweetness.

Recipe 7: Spinach and Avocado-Free Green Smoothie

  • 2 cups frozen collard greens
  • 1/2 cup frozen banana
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup coconut water
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Ice cubes (as needed)

This smoothie is a great alternative to traditional green smoothies that feature avocado. The collard greens provide a rich source of vitamins and minerals, while the banana adds natural sweetness.
