Indulge in the Creaminess of Middle Eastern Labneh Cheesecake


If you’re a fan of cheesecake and looking to add a unique twist to your dessert repertoire, then you’re in for a treat! Middle Eastern cuisine is renowned for its rich and flavorful dishes, and one particular ingredient that stands out is labneh. Labneh, a strained yogurt cheese, brings a delightful creaminess and tangy flavor to this indulgent Labneh Cheesecake.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the Filling:

  • 24 oz labneh cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 orange

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, melted butter, and cinnamon until well combined.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, creating an even layer.
  4. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and set aside to cool.

Step 2: Making the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, beat the labneh cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  3. Mix in the vanilla extract, flour, lemon zest, and orange zest until fully incorporated.
  4. Pour the filling over the cooled crust and smooth the top with a spatula.

Step 3: Baking the Cheesecake

  1. Place the springform pan on a baking sheet to catch any drips, then bake the cheesecake for 50-60 minutes until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  2. Turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside with the door slightly ajar for 1 hour to cool gradually.
  3. Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool to room temperature before refrigerating for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

Step 4: Adding the Topping

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the sour cream, sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  2. Spread the topping over the chilled cheesecake and return it to the refrigerator for another hour to set.

Serving and Enjoying

Slice the Labneh Cheesecake into wedges and serve chilled. The creamy, tangy flavor of the labneh combined with the sweetness of the filling and the buttery graham cracker crust creates a delightful fusion of flavors that will captivate your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a special dinner or simply craving a luxurious dessert, this Middle Eastern-inspired cheesecake is sure to impress.

With its velvety texture and unique flavor profile, Middle Eastern Labneh Cheesecake offers a delightful departure from traditional cheesecake recipes. The incorporation of labneh introduces a tangy richness that elevates this dessert to a whole new level of indulgence. Whether you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine or simply seeking a new dessert adventure, this Labneh Cheesecake is a must-try. So, grab your apron and embark on a culinary journey that will leave you and your guests wanting more!
