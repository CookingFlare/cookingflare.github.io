Delicious Raspberry Crumble Bars: A Perfect Summer Treat


There’s something about summer that calls for fruity, tangy treats, and these delicious raspberry crumble bars perfectly fit the bill. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, planning a picnic, or simply craving a delightful dessert, these bars are sure to be a hit. With a buttery crumble crust, a sweet and tart raspberry filling, and a golden crumb topping, they are a symphony of flavors and textures that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

For the Crust and Crumble

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of one lemon
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 egg

For the Raspberry Filling

  • 4 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Juice of one lemon

Instructions

Making the Crust and Crumble

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13 inch (23x33 cm) baking pan and line it with parchment paper, leaving an overhang for easy removal.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and lemon zest. Add the cold butter cubes and the egg. Using a pastry cutter or your hands, work the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Set aside about 1 1/2 cups of the crumb mixture for the topping. Press the remaining mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan, creating an even layer. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until lightly golden.

Making the Raspberry Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the raspberries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring gently, until the mixture thickens, and the raspberries break down, about 10-12 minutes. Remove from the heat and let it cool slightly.

Assembling the Bars

  1. Spread the raspberry filling over the partially baked crust. Sprinkle the reserved crumb mixture evenly over the top.

  2. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown.

  3. Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan before using the parchment overhang to lift them out. Transfer to a cutting board and slice into bars.

Serving and Storing

These raspberry crumble bars are best served at room temperature. They can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week. If you’re making them ahead, they also freeze well for up to 3 months. Simply thaw at room temperature before serving.

Enjoy the Fruity Bliss!

These raspberry crumble bars are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of summer. The buttery crust, the luscious raspberry filling, and the crumbly topping come together to create a mouthwatering dessert that will have everyone asking for seconds. Whether you’re enjoying them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream, these bars are a perfect way to indulge in the season’s bounty of fresh raspberries. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and get ready to savor the taste of summer!
