A French Comfort Food: Beef Bourguignon Stew


When it comes to classic French comfort food, Beef Bourguignon stew holds a special place in the hearts and stomachs of many. This hearty dish, originating from the Burgundy region of France, is a perfect blend of tender beef, aromatic vegetables, and a rich, flavorful sauce. Its slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a dish that is nothing short of comforting and indulgent.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of stewing beef, cut into chunks
  • 4 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 bottle of red wine
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound of mushrooms, quartered
  • 1/4 cup of butter
  • 3 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef: Start by patting the beef dry with paper towels to ensure it browns properly. Then, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches and brown on all sides. Transfer the browned beef to a plate and set it aside.

  2. Saute the Aromatics: In the same Dutch oven, add the chopped bacon and cook until it’s browned and crisp. Add the carrots, onion, and garlic, and cook until the vegetables are tender, stirring occasionally.

  3. Deglaze with Wine: Return the browned beef to the pot and pour in the entire bottle of red wine. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then add the beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Stir well and bring to a gentle boil.

  4. Simmer and Cook: Once the stew reaches a gentle boil, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the sauce has thickened.

  5. Cook the Mushrooms: In a separate skillet, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil and the butter over medium heat. Add the quartered mushrooms and cook until they are golden brown and all the liquid has evaporated. Sprinkle in the flour and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly.

  6. Finish the Stew: Add the cooked mushrooms to the beef stew and let it simmer for an additional 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Remove the bay leaf from the stew and discard it. To serve, ladle the Beef Bourguignon into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh parsley. This stew pairs wonderfully with a crusty baguette or creamy mashed potatoes.

Tips and Tricks

  • For the best flavor, marinate the beef in red wine and aromatics overnight before cooking.
  • Use a good quality, full-bodied red wine such as a Burgundy or Pinot Noir for the stew.
  • Don’t rush the cooking process; allowing the stew to simmer and develop flavors over time is key to achieving a rich and delicious result.

Beef Bourguignon stew is a labor of love, but the rewards are well worth the effort. Whether you’re serving it at a special gathering or simply craving a taste of French comfort at home, this timeless dish is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, pour yourself a glass of red wine, and embark on a culinary journey to the heart of Burgundy with this delightful Beef Bourguignon stew. Bon appétit!
