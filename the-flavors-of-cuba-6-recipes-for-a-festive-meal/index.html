The Flavors of Cuba: 6 Recipes for a Festive Meal


Cuban cuisine is a unique blend of Spanish, African, and Caribbean flavors, showcasing the island's rich cultural heritage. From the spicy kick of Afro-Cuban dishes to the refreshment of citrus-infused drinks, every bite and sip takes you on a culinary journey through the vibrant streets of Havana. In this post, we'll explore six recipes that will transport your taste buds to the colorful island nation and leave you craving for more.

  1. Mojo Marinated Pork Shoulder with Arroz Moros y Cristianos Serve as the centerpiece of your Cuban feast with this mouth-watering Mojo Marinated Pork Shoulder, paired with a traditional side of Arroz Moros y Cristianos. Mojo Marinated Pork Shoulder: Ingredients:

2 tablespoons brown sugar 1 tablespoon olive oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar 2 tablespoons orange juice 2 tablespoons cilantro, chopped 1 (2-pound) pork shoulder

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). In a blender or food processor, combine brown sugar, olive oil, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Process until smooth. Place the pork shoulder in a large resealable plastic bag and add the marinade. Seal and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Place the pork in a large Dutch oven and add the orange juice and chopped cilantro. Cover with a lid and transfer to the preheated oven. Baste with marinade every 30 minutes. After 3 hours, remove the pork from the oven and let it rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Arroz Moros y Cristianos: Ingredients:

1 cup uncooked white rice 1 cup uncooked black beans, drained and rinsed 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 small onion, finely chopped 1 garlic clove, minced 1 cup chicken broth 1 tablespoon white wine Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the rice and cook, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes. Add the chicken broth, white wine, and black beans. Stir to combine, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and cook for 20-25 minutes or until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  1. Cuban Citrus and Avocado Salad Elevate your sides with this refreshing Cuban Citrus and Avocado Salad, featuring a vibrant medley of citrus segments and creamy avocado. Ingredients:

2 ripe avocados, diced 1 cup freshly squeezed orange juice 1 cup freshly squeezed lime juice 2 tablespoons olive oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1 cup chopped red onion 1 cup chopped cilantro 2 cups mixed citrus segments (orange, grapefruit, and lemon)

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the avocado, orange juice, lime juice, olive oil, garlic, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir until smooth. Add the chopped red onion and cilantro, and stir to combine. Gently fold in the citrus segments until well coated. Serve chilled, garnished with additional citrus segments, if desired.

  1. Half-Moon Empanadas Turn a classic Cuban snack into a main course with these crispy Half-Moon Empanadas, packed with a savory beef and onion filling. Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon baking powder 1/4 cup shortening 1 egg, beaten 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese 1 cup beef, cooked and shredded 1 small onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder. Gradually add the shortening and beat until a dough forms. Gradually add the beaten egg and mix until smooth. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until elastic and smooth. Divide the dough into 8 equal wedges and roll each wedge into a thin circle. Brush with water and fold each circle in half to form a half-moon shape. Place a tablespoon of filling in the center of each half-moon and fold to seal the edges. Brush with egg and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.

  1. Plátanos Fritos Elevate your snack game with these crispy Plátanos Fritos, a classic Cuban treat that's sure to become a favorite. Ingredients:

4 ripe plantains, peeled 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 1 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 cup milk

Instructions:

Heat the oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Slice the plantains into 1-inch slices and fry until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side. Drain the plantains on paper towels and set aside. In a shallow dish, mix together sugar, flour, salt, and baking powder. Dip each fried plantain in the sugar mixture, then roll in milk to coat. Return the coated plantains to the oil and fry until golden brown.

  1. Croquetas de Carne (Beef Croquettes) Add some fun to your party with these bite-sized Croquetas de Carne, made with shredded beef, onions, and spices. Ingredients:

1 pound beef, cooked and shredded 1 onion, finely chopped 1 egg, beaten 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup grated Manchego cheese 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1 cup breadcrumbs

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the shredded beef, onion, beaten egg, flour, Manchego cheese, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Mix until a dough forms. Shape into small balls and roll in breadcrumbs to coat. Fry in hot oil until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

  1. Café con Leche with Cinnamon Whipped Cream Bring your Cuban feast to a sweet close with this rich Café con Leche and Cinnamon Whipped Cream, a nod to the island's English-speaking ancestors. Ingredients:

1 cup heavy cream 2 tablespoons granulated sugar 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1 cup strong brewed coffee 1 cup milk 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the heavy cream, granulated sugar, and ground cinnamon. Beat until stiff peaks form. Brew a strong cup of coffee and pour into a separate bowl. In a small saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until hot but not boiling. In a large coffee cup, combine the coffee, milk, and unsalted butter. Stir until smooth. Top with whipped cream and sprinkle with cinnamon, if desired.

Whether you're celebrating the vibrant streets of Havana or simply looking to spice up your weeknight meals, these Cuban recipes are sure to bring the island's rich flavors to your table. With each bite, you'll be transported to the colorful streets of Cuba, where Caribbean rhythms and African drums beat to the rhythm of rich, bold flavors.
