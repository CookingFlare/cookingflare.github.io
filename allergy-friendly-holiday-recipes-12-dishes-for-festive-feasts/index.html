Allergy-Friendly Holiday Recipes: 12 Dishes for Festive Feasts


The holiday season is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and, of course, delicious food. However, for those with food allergies or intolerances, navigating holiday meals can be a daunting task. Fortunately, there are plenty of allergy-friendly recipes that can bring joy to your festive feasts without compromising on flavor or tradition. In this post, we’ll explore 12 delightful dishes that cater to various dietary restrictions, ensuring everyone at your table can enjoy the festivities.

1. Herb-Roasted Turkey

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, rosemary, thyme, sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the herb mixture all over the turkey, including under the skin.
  4. Place the turkey in a roasting pan and pour the vegetable broth into the bottom of the pan.
  5. Roast the turkey for about 3-4 hours, basting every 30 minutes, until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).
  6. Let the turkey rest for 20 minutes before carving.

This herb-roasted turkey is not only gluten-free but also dairy-free, making it a perfect centerpiece for your holiday table.

2. Cranberry Orange Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • Zest of 1 orange

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine cranberries, orange juice, maple syrup, and orange zest.
  2. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce to a simmer.
  3. Cook for about 10-15 minutes, until the cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.
  4. Allow to cool before serving.

This vibrant cranberry orange sauce is a delightful addition to your holiday spread, and it’s free from common allergens like gluten and dairy.

3. Garlic Mashed Cauliflower

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, chopped into florets
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup almond milk (or any non-dairy milk)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Steam the cauliflower florets until tender, about 10-12 minutes.
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.
  3. In a food processor, combine steamed cauliflower, sautéed garlic, almond milk, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  4. Serve warm.

This creamy garlic mashed cauliflower is a fantastic alternative to traditional mashed potatoes, perfect for those avoiding dairy and gluten.

4. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread them on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 20-25 minutes until crispy and golden.
  4. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar before serving.

These roasted Brussels sprouts are not only allergy-friendly but also packed with flavor, making them a perfect side dish for any holiday meal.

5. Quinoa Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring vegetable broth to a boil. Add quinoa, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes until liquid is absorbed.
  2. In a skillet, sauté onion, celery, and carrot until tender. Add thyme, sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Combine the sautéed vegetables with cooked quinoa and mix well.

This quinoa stuffing is a nutritious and gluten-free alternative to traditional bread stuffing, making it suitable for various dietary needs.
