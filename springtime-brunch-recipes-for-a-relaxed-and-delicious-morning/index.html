Springtime Brunch: Recipes for a Relaxed and Delicious Morning


As the last wisps of winter’s chill dissipate, the promise of spring’s arrival brings with it a sense of renewal and rebirth. And what better way to celebrate this season of renewal than with a leisurely brunch, surrounded by loved ones and savoring the sweet flavors of the season?

In this post, we’ll take you on a culinary journey through the best of springtime brunch recipes, from classic comfort dishes to innovative twists on traditional favorites. Whether you’re hosting a gathering for friends and family or simply want to treat yourself to a relaxing morning at home, these recipes are sure to delight.

Springtime Brunch Menu

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s take a look at the menu we’ll be featuring today. Our springtime brunch menu is designed to showcase the freshest flavors and ingredients of the season, with dishes that are both easy to make and impressive to serve.

  • Fresh Fruit Salad: A colorful medley of seasonal fruits, including strawberries, blueberries, and pineapple, tossed with a hint of citrus and a drizzle of honey.
  • Spring Vegetable Frittata: A hearty, egg-based dish packed with sautéed spring vegetables, including asparagus, bell peppers, and onions, and topped with a crispy, golden-brown crust.
  • Herb and Cheese Quiche: A classic quiche recipe infused with the bright, fresh flavors of spring herbs like chives and parsley, and paired with a rich, creamy cheese filling.
  • Lemon Poppyseed Muffins: Moist, buttery muffins infused with the bright, citrusy flavor of lemon zest and topped with a crunchy, poppyseed-studded streusel topping.
  • Iced Tea with Fresh Mint: A refreshing, thirst-quenching beverage that pairs perfectly with the sweet, floral flavors of springtime brunch.

Fresh Fruit Salad Recipe

** Servings:** 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed fresh fruit (strawberries, blueberries, pineapple, kiwi, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mixed fresh fruit.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the orange juice and honey until well combined.
  3. Pour the juice mixture over the fruit and toss to coat.
  4. Sprinkle the chopped fresh mint leaves over the top of the fruit salad and toss gently to combine.
  5. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Spring Vegetable Frittata Recipe

Servings: 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 6 eggs
  • 1 cup diced spring vegetables (asparagus, bell peppers, onions, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the diced spring vegetables and cook until tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the cooked vegetables in the skillet.
  6. Sprinkle the grated cheddar and Parmesan cheese over the top of the eggs.
  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the top is golden brown.
  8. Sprinkle the chopped fresh parsley over the top of the frittata and serve hot.

Herb and Cheese Quiche Recipe

Servings: 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust
  • 2 cups grated cheese (cheddar, Swiss, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh herbs (chives, parsley, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place in a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the grated cheese, chopped fresh herbs, heavy cream, and eggs.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the pie crust.
  5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the crust is golden brown.
  6. Garnish with chopped fresh chives and serve hot.

Lemon Poppyseed Muffins Recipe

Servings: 12

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup poppy seeds
  • Confectioners’ sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, buttermilk, eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Fold in the poppy seeds.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  8. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until the muffins are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  9. Dust with confectioners’ sugar, if desired. Serve warm.

Iced Tea with Fresh Mint Recipe

Servings: 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 1 quart brewed tea
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves
  • Ice cubes
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the brewed tea, lemon juice, and sugar.
  2. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  3. Add the chopped fresh mint leaves to the pitcher and stir gently.
  4. Chill the tea in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Serve the tea over ice cubes and garnish with fresh mint leaves.
