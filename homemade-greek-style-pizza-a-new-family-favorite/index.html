Homemade Greek Style Pizza: A New Family Favorite


When you think of pizza, your mind might drift to the classic Italian flavors of pepperoni and mozzarella. But as the culinary world continues to evolve, it’s time to broaden those horizons and introduce a delightful twist: the Greek style pizza. This homemade version embodies the vibrant and fresh ingredients characteristic of Mediterranean cuisine, promising to become a new family favorite. In this post, we’ll explore how to create this delicious treat from scratch, including the dough, sauce, toppings, and baking techniques. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to impress your family with a pizza that bursts with flavor and character!

The Magic of Greek Ingredients

Greek cuisine is known for its use of fresh vegetables, herbs, and quality cheeses. When making Greek style pizza, the combination of these ingredients creates a delightful experience. Imagine tangy feta cheese, juicy olives, fresh tomatoes, and a medley of herbs all coming together on a warm, crispy crust. Each bite is a celebration of flavor, transporting you to the sun-soaked shores of Greece.

Making the Perfect Pizza Dough

Ingredients for the Dough

To start your culinary adventure, you’ll need to prepare the dough. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 ¼ cups warm water (about 110°F or 43°C)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions for the Dough

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This step is crucial as it ensures that the yeast is active.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a well in the center, and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil.

  3. Knead the Dough: Using your hands or a wooden spoon, mix until a shaggy dough forms. Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Let it Rise: Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Divide and Shape: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions. Shape each piece into a ball and let them rest for an additional 15 minutes.

Crafting the Greek Pizza Sauce

Ingredients for the Sauce

The sauce is a crucial component of any pizza, and for Greek style pizza, we’ll keep it simple yet flavorful. Gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions for the Sauce

  1. Sauté the Garlic: In a saucepan over medium heat, add the olive oil. Once hot, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant but not browned.

  2. Combine Ingredients: Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir well and let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Cool and Blend: Remove the sauce from heat and let it cool slightly. For a smoother texture, you can blend it using an immersion blender or standard blender, but this step is optional.

Choosing the Toppings

One of the beautiful aspects of Greek style pizza is the variety of toppings you can choose from. Here are some classic options to consider:

  • Feta Cheese: Crumbled for that tangy flavor.
  • Kalamata Olives: Pitted and sliced for a briny kick.
  • Spinach: Fresh or sautéed, adding a vibrant green element.
  • Red Onion: Thinly sliced for a bit of sweetness and crunch.
  • Bell Peppers: Colorful slices that add both sweetness and crunch.
  • Artichoke Hearts: Chopped, they introduce a lovely texture and flavor.

Feel free to get creative with your toppings! You can add grilled chicken, sun-dried tomatoes, or even a sprinkle of fresh dill for an extra flavor boost.

Assembling Your Greek Style Pizza

Preheat the Oven

It’s time to preheat your oven to a sizzling 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up as well. The high temperature will help achieve that perfect crispy crust.

Stretching the Dough

  1. Shape the Dough: Take one dough ball and place it on a floured surface. Using your fingers, gently press down the center, pushing the dough outward to form a circle. You can also use a rolling pin if you prefer a more uniform shape.

  2. Create the Crust: Once you have a circle about 12 inches in diameter, pinch the edges to create a crust. This will help hold in all the delicious toppings.

Adding Sauce and Toppings

  1. Spread the Sauce: Using a ladle or the back of a spoon, spread a generous amount of sauce over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.

  2. Layer the Toppings: Start with a sprinkle of mozzarella cheese for a base, then add your desired toppings—feta cheese, olives, spinach, red onion, and bell peppers. Remember not to overdo it; you want a balanced pizza that cooks evenly.

  3. Finish with Cheese: Top it all off with more mozzarella and a sprinkle of dried oregano for added flavor.

Baking the Pizza

  1. Transfer to Oven: If you’re using a pizza stone, carefully transfer your pizza onto it. If not, place the pizza on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  2. Bake: Let the pizza bake for about 12-15 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling.

  3. Cool and Slice: Once baked, remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. This will make slicing easier. Use a pizza cutter to slice it into wedges.

Serving Suggestions

As you serve your homemade Greek style pizza, consider pairing it with a fresh Greek salad. A mix of cucumbers, tomatoes, red onion, olives, and feta drizzled with olive oil and oregano creates a refreshing side that complements the pizza perfectly. You can also offer tzatziki sauce for dipping, adding an authentic Greek touch.

Storing Leftovers

If you have any leftovers (which is rare, but it happens), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or air fryer for a quick and delicious meal.

Conclusion

Homemade Greek style pizza is not just a dish; it’s an experience. With its crispy crust, vibrant toppings, and rich flavors, it’s bound to become a new family favorite. Enjoy the process, experiment with different ingredients, and most importantly, gather your loved ones around the table to share this delightful creation. So, what are you waiting for? Get started on your Greek culinary adventure today!
