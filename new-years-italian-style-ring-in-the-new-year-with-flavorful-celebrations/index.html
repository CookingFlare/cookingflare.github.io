New Years, Italian Style: Ring in the New Year with Flavorful Celebrations


As the year draws to a close and we eagerly await the arrival of 2022, there’s no better way to bid adieu to the previous year and welcome the new one than with a feast that brings together friends, family, and a world of complex flavors in every bite. This New Year’s Eve, let’s set the table with a spread that celebrates Italian cuisine at its finest. We’ll guide you through a curated menu, from sumptuous appetizers to traditional sweets, that will leave your dinner guests marveling and your taste buds singing with joy.

Start with a Spectacular Start: Antipasti Platter

Begin the evening right with an antipasti platter laden with cured meats, hunks of cheese, and briny pickled vegetables. This classic Italian appetizer showcases an array of artisanal products and sums up the Italian approach to food in one magnificent dish. You’ll find that most Italians love to savor the flavors and textures of their antipasti, making it the perfect way to kick off a memorable occasion.

Ingredients:

  • Prosciutto di Parma
  • Salami
  • Mortadella
  • Pecorino Romano and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • Marinated artichoke hearts
  • Marinated mushrooms
  • Marinated olives
  • Crackers or crusty bread

Arrange these ingredients on a large platter and let each guest craft their own combination of flavors. Join in by sharing recommendations and stories behind the ingredients—this will bring to life the traditions surrounding antipasti platters.

Entice with a Zesty First Course: Caprese Salad

The first course of any Italian feast cannot go without a green, leafy salad, and today, we have a classic: Caprese salad. This dish is a perfect representation of Italian cuisine, boasting three simple yet exquisite elements: tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil.

Ingredients:

  • Heirloom tomatoes
  • Fresh mozzarella
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic vinegar
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt (optional)

Slice the tomatoes and mozzarella, drizzle the balsamic and olive oil, and finish with a pinch of black pepper. This salad bursts with flavor and brightness, setting the stage for the meal ahead.

Indulge in a Hearty Second Course: Orecchiette with Broccoli Rabe and Sausage

We say it’s just as enjoyable to be familiar with Italian cuisine as it is be introduced to it for the first time. Let’s dive into a classic pasta dish that marries an abundance of flavors and textures: orecchiette with broccoli rabe and sausage.

Ingredients:

  • 1lb orecchiette pasta
  • 1lb Italian sausage
  • 1lb broccoli rabe
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Salt and pepper
  • Grated Pecorino Romano cheese

Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, slice the sausage, sauté broccoli rabe with garlic and red pepper flakes, and combine everything for a dish that’s perfect for sharing. The slightly bitter broccoli rabe and spicy sausage create a harmonious balance of flavors that is both satisfying and memorable.

Satisfy Your Cravings with a Polenta Pie

Transitioning to the main event, how about serving up an Italian-inspired polenta pie? The possibilities are endless, and with a warm and comforting polenta base, everyone will surely be feeling the amore.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups instant polenta
  • 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/3 cup aged balsamic vinegar
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • 3 cups grated mozzarella cheese
  • 2 cups sautéed mushrooms
  • ½ pound ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Fresh herbs, such as thyme or rosemary

Instructions:

  1. Cook the polenta in the chicken or vegetable broth, stirring frequently to avoid lumps.
  2. Once cooked and set, pour it into a greased pie dish, spreading it evenly and allowing it to cool slightly.
  3. Add a layer of marinara sauce, followed by a layer of sautéed mushrooms, ground beef or sausage, and garlic.
  4. Sprinkle fresh herbs and a generous amount of grated mozzarella cheese on top, and finish with a drizzle of aged balsamic vinegar.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly, and allow to cool slightly before serving.

Conclude with a Sweet Touch: Italian Cannoli

It would not be complete without a pastry that stirs up sweet memories of Italy. Introducing Italian cannoli, a classic dessert that’ll leave your guests craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can pre-made cannoli shells
  • 1 (14oz) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 (8oz) pkg. cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Powdered sugar
  • Chopped chocolate or mini chocolate chips
  • Crushed pistachios or candied fruit peel (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Beat together the condensed milk, cream cheese, and mascarpone cheese until smooth.
  2. Add the vanilla, then whisk in the cocoa powder, and set aside.
  3. Fill the cannoli shells with the sweet and creamy filling, chill, and then dust with powdered sugar. If desired, finish by topping with chopped chocolate or crushed pistachios.
  4. These mouthwatering treats should be the final jewel in your Italian New Year’s celebration crown.

With a menu like this, you’re bound to create an atmosphere of warmth, love, and community. Embrace the fresh start the new year brings with every bite, as you nourish both body and soul by savoring the flavors of Italy. Buon Apetito!
