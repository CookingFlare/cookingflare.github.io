Delicious and Juicy: Grilled Ribeye Steak Recipe


There’s nothing quite like sinking your teeth into a perfectly grilled ribeye steak. The tender, juicy meat, combined with the smoky flavors from the grill, creates a dining experience that is truly unforgettable. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the steps to achieve a delicious and juicy grilled ribeye steak that will leave your taste buds wanting more.

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye steaks (1 1/2 inches thick)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 sprig of rosemary, finely chopped
  • Optional: your favorite steak seasoning blend

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. This will ensure that your steaks cook evenly and develop a beautiful sear on the outside.

  2. Season your ribeye steaks generously with salt and pepper. If you prefer, you can also use your favorite steak seasoning blend to add an extra layer of flavor. Allow the steaks to sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes before grilling. This will help them cook more evenly.

  3. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, and chopped rosemary. Mix well to create a flavorful marinade.

  4. Brush both sides of the steaks with the marinade, making sure to coat them evenly. This will not only infuse the steaks with delicious flavors but also help prevent them from sticking to the grill.

  5. Place the steaks on the preheated grill and cook for approximately 4-5 minutes per side for medium-rare doneness. Adjust the cooking time according to your preferred level of doneness. Remember, the internal temperature of the steaks will continue to rise slightly after they are removed from the grill.

  6. Once the steaks are cooked to your liking, remove them from the grill and let them rest for at least 5 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a more tender and flavorful steak.

  7. After the resting period, slice the ribeye steaks against the grain to ensure maximum tenderness. Serve immediately and enjoy the juicy, mouthwatering goodness of your grilled ribeye steak.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra flavor boost, you can marinate the steaks for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator. Simply place the seasoned steaks in a resealable plastic bag with the marinade and let them sit. Remember to bring them back to room temperature before grilling.

  • To achieve those beautiful grill marks, make sure your grill grates are clean and well-oiled before placing the steaks on them. This will prevent the meat from sticking and give you those coveted grill marks.

  • If you’re unsure about the doneness of your steak, use an instant-read meat thermometer. The internal temperature should reach 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare, 145°F (63°C) for medium, and 160°F (71°C) for well-done.

  • Pair your grilled ribeye steak with your favorite side dishes, such as grilled vegetables, a refreshing salad, or some creamy mashed potatoes. The possibilities are endless!

Now that you have this delicious and juicy grilled ribeye steak recipe in your arsenal, it’s time to fire up the grill and impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. Enjoy every bite of this mouthwatering masterpiece and savor the flavors that only a perfectly grilled ribeye can deliver. Happy grilling!
