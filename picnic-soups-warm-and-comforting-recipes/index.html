Picnic Soups: Warm and Comforting Recipes


When it comes to picnics, we often think of sandwiches, salads, and other portable finger foods. But why not bring some warmth and comfort to your outdoor dining experience with a delicious homemade soup? Picnic soups are not only easy to prepare but also a great way to add variety and flavor to your outdoor spread. In this blog post, we’ll share three warm and comforting picnic soup recipes that are sure to impress your friends and family.

1. Creamy Tomato Basil Soup

Nothing beats the classic combination of ripe tomatoes and fragrant basil. To make this creamy tomato basil soup, start by sautéing finely chopped onions and garlic in a large pot with a drizzle of olive oil. Once the onions turn translucent, add in diced tomatoes (fresh or canned) and a handful of fresh basil leaves. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar to balance the acidity. Let the soup simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Next, using an immersion blender or a regular blender, blend the soup until smooth and creamy. If using a regular blender, be sure to cool the soup slightly before blending to avoid any accidents. Return the soup to the pot and stir in a generous splash of heavy cream for that luxurious, velvety texture. Heat the soup gently until warmed through, and it’s ready to be packed for your picnic adventure.

2. Hearty Chicken Noodle Soup

A classic comfort food, chicken noodle soup is not just for sick days. It’s also a fantastic option for a picnic, providing warmth and nourishment. To prepare this hearty soup, start by sautéing diced onions, carrots, and celery in a large pot with a drizzle of olive oil. Once the vegetables have softened, add in diced chicken breast and cook until browned.

Next, pour in chicken broth and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and add in dried noodles of your choice. Cook the noodles until al dente, following the instructions on the package. Season the soup with salt, pepper, and dried herbs like thyme or rosemary for added flavor. Let the soup simmer for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Serve the chicken noodle soup hot in thermos containers or insulated jars for your picnic. Don’t forget to pack some crusty bread or crackers on the side for a complete and satisfying meal.

3. Spicy Corn Chowder

If you’re looking for a soup with a little kick, this spicy corn chowder is the perfect choice. Start by sautéing diced onions, bell peppers, and jalapeños in a large pot with a drizzle of olive oil. Once the vegetables have softened, add in diced potatoes and frozen corn kernels. Cook for a few minutes until the corn is thawed.

Next, pour in vegetable or chicken broth and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer until the potatoes are tender. In a separate bowl, whisk together milk and flour until smooth, then add it to the pot. Stir well to combine, and let the soup thicken for a few more minutes.

To add a burst of freshness and color, garnish the spicy corn chowder with chopped fresh cilantro or spring onions. Pack it in a thermos or insulated container, and you’re ready to enjoy a flavorful and satisfying soup during your picnic.

Conclusion

Picnic soups are a delightful addition to any outdoor gathering. Whether you prefer the classic flavors of tomato basil soup, the heartiness of chicken noodle soup, or the spicy kick of corn chowder, these recipes are sure to warm both your body and soul. So, next time you plan a picnic, don’t forget to bring along a comforting and delicious soup. Your taste buds will thank you!
