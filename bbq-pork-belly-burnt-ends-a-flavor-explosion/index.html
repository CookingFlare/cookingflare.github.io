BBQ Pork Belly Burnt Ends: A Flavor Explosion


The summer sun is shining, the grill is hot, and the neighborhood is filled with the tantalizing aroma of slow-cooked, caramelized deliciousness - that can only mean one thing - BBQ Pork Belly Burnt Ends have taken center stage! With the perfect balance of succulent meat, smoky depth, and an irresistible, almost candy-like exterior, this scrumptious dish will leave your friends and family clamoring for more.

To create this phenomenal crowd-pleaser, follow this simple, yet flavorful recipe that will elevate your summer BBQs to new heights.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. pork belly, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup applewood chips, soaked in water for 30 minutes
  • 2 tbsp coarse kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp celery seeds
  • 1/2 tsp ground mustard
  • 1/4 cup liquid honey
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

The Artistry of the BBQ Pork Belly Burnt Ends:

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Belly Pat-dry the cubed pork belly with paper towels to remove excess moisture. This will allow the seasonings to adhere better to the meat during the slow-cooking process.

Step 2: Seasoning the Pork Belly In a small bowl, mix together the coarse kosher salt, brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, cayenne pepper, celery seeds, and ground mustard. Rub this seasoning blend generously onto the pork belly cubes, ensuring each piece is evenly coated.

Step 3: Preparing the Grill While seasoning the pork, soak the applewood chips in water for 30 minutes. Once the pork belly is seasoned, drain and lightly distribute the soaked applewood chips across the hot coal grate of your grill.

Step 4: Slow-Cooking the Pork Belly Place the seasoned pork belly cubes on the grill grates above the applewood chips, close the lid, and cook over low heat for about 3 hours. Every hour, rotate the pork belly cubes for even cooking and to prevent any one side from charring too much.

Why settles on low heat? The low heat allows for a slow and steady cook, resulting in tender and succulent pork belly. It’s also the perfect setting for kettle grill aficionados.

Step 5: The Final Touches After the pork belly has been cooking for about 2 hours and 45 minutes, remove it from the grill. Brush the remaining applewood chips off the grate and discard them. Turn the heat to high and tr ack the pork belly cubes straight onto the smoking-hot grate. This high-heat Searing process forms a delicious, caramelized exterior and adds a touch of smoky, charred depth to the pork belly cubes.

Step 6: The Finishing Touch - Sauce While the burnt ends are still on the grill, prepare your favorite barbecue sauce or a mix of liquid honey and apple cider vinegar. When the pork belly cubes have that perfect burnt end look and feel, remove them from the grill and toss them in your sauce, creating a sweet and tangy glaze.

Step 7: Cool-down and Serve Allow the pork belly burnt ends to cool just enough for easy handling. Serve these tantalizing morsels on their own or atop a bed of coleslaw, slow-cooked beans, or even mashed potatoes - the possibilities are endless!

End your summer BBQs on a high note by adding BBQ Pork Belly Burnt Ends to your menu. no matter how many times anyone suggests moving away from this mouthwatering, more-ish flavor explosion. Enjoy!
