Comforting Baked Potato Soup with Bacon


When the chill sets in and you crave something both indulgent and nourishing, few dishes hit the spot like a rich, creamy baked potato soup. It’s a bowl brimming with velvety potatoes, smoky bacon, sharp cheddar, and a touch of freshness—everything you want to wrap your hands around on a cold day. This baked potato soup recipe brings that cozy warmth and satisfying flavor straight to your table, offering a comforting meal that feels like a hug in a bowl.

Why Baked Potato Soup?

Unlike your average potato soup, baked potato soup takes the humble potato to a new level by roasting them first. Roasting intensifies the potato’s natural sweetness and adds a subtle, caramelized depth that makes every spoonful more flavorful. When combined with crispy bacon and a creamy base, the result is decadently comforting without being overly heavy.

Plus, the versatility of baked potato soup means you can easily customize it. Whether you prefer it extra cheesy, loaded with veggies, or with an herbaceous kick, this soup adapts effortlessly to your taste.

Ingredients Overview

To build a great baked potato soup with bacon, these ingredients are the stars:

  • Russet potatoes: Perfect for baking, they have a fluffy interior that turns silky when pureed.
  • Bacon: Crispy and savory, adds that essential smoky crunch.
  • Onions and garlic: A classic flavor base that infuses the soup with aromatic depth.
  • Chicken or vegetable broth: Forms the soup’s light yet flavorful liquid foundation.
  • Heavy cream or half-and-half: Adds richness and creaminess without overwhelming the flavors.
  • Cheddar cheese: Sharp and melty, it complements the potatoes and bacon perfectly.
  • Chives or green onions: Freshness to balance the richness.
  • Butter: Helps build that luxurious texture and mouthfeel.
  • Salt, pepper, and optional seasonings: To enhance and elevate the natural flavors.

Optional add-ins like sour cream, additional veggies (like celery or corn), or even a dash of smoked paprika can give it a unique twist.

Step-by-Step Cooking Guide

1. Bake the Potatoes

Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Scrub four large russet potatoes, prick them with a fork a few times, and place them directly on the oven rack or a baking sheet. Bake for about 45-60 minutes until tender. Once cool enough to handle, scoop out the insides and set aside.

Tip: Baking the potatoes whole first gives that authentic baked flavor and slightly dry texture inside, which helps the soup thicken nicely.

2. Cook the Bacon

While the potatoes bake, cook 6–8 strips of bacon in a large soup pot over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and drain on paper towels. Reserve about 1-2 tablespoons of bacon fat in the pot for sautéing. Crumble the bacon once cooled.

3. Sauté the Aromatics

Add 2 tablespoons of butter to the bacon fat in the pot. Sauté one diced onion until translucent and lightly caramelized (around 5-7 minutes). Add 3 minced garlic cloves and cook for another minute until fragrant.

4. Build the Soup Base

Pour in 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth, pulling up any browned bits stuck to the pan to maximize flavor. Stir in the reserved baked potato flesh and bring the mixture to a simmer.

5. Blend for Creaminess

Using an immersion blender right in the pot, blend the soup until mostly smooth but still retaining some small chunks for texture. Alternatively, carefully blend half the soup in batches in a regular blender and return it to the pot.

6. Add Dairy and Cheese

Reduce the heat to low, then stir in 1 cup of heavy cream (or half-and-half for a lighter option) and 1.5 cups of shredded sharp cheddar cheese. Stir gently until the cheese melts fully and the soup is silky smooth.

7. Season and Serve

Season with salt, freshly ground black pepper, and if you like, a pinch of smoked paprika or cayenne for warmth. Ladle the soup into bowls and top each serving generously with crumbled bacon, chopped chives, and even a dollop of sour cream or extra cheese.

Tips for Perfecting Your Baked Potato Soup

  • Choose the right potato: Russet potatoes are best because their starchy quality yields a creamy texture without being gluey.
  • Don’t skip roasting: Roasting the potatoes adds a natural sweetness and nuttiness you won’t get by just boiling.
  • Balance the salt: Bacon is salty, so add salt gradually and taste as you go.
  • Texture control: If you like a chunkier soup, blend only half or a third of it; for ultra silky, blend it all.
  • Customize the toppings: Alongside bacon and chives, try crispy fried onions, crumbled blue cheese, or crunchy toasted nuts for variety.

Pairing Suggestions

Baked potato soup is a meal in itself, but you can round out the experience with:

  • A simple mixed greens salad with a tangy vinaigrette to cut through the richness
  • Crusty artisan bread or garlic toast for dipping
  • A light white wine such as a Sauvignon Blanc or a malty ale for beer lovers

Storing and Reheating

This soup reheats wonderfully, making it perfect for leftovers or batch cooking. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to four days. Reheat gently over low heat on the stove, stirring occasionally. If the soup thickens too much, add a splash of broth or cream to smooth it out again.

Variations for Every Taste

  • Vegetarian friendly: Skip the bacon and use smoked paprika or liquid smoke to infuse that smoky flavor. Add extra veggies like roasted carrots or celery.
  • Cheese lovers: Mix in other cheeses like Gruyere, Monterey Jack, or Parmesan for complex depth.
  • Spicy kick: Stir in diced jalapeños or a dash of hot sauce to energize the traditional flavors.
  • Chunky loaded: Fold in diced ham, corn kernels, or even cooked sausage for an extra hearty meal.
  • Dairy-free: Substitute cream with coconut milk or a cashew cream for a vegan-friendly version.

Final Thoughts: Why This Soup Endures

There’s something timelessly comforting about baked potato soup, especially when paired with the quintessential bacon garnish. It’s a marriage of textures and flavors—the creamy potato base, the sharp tang of cheddar, the luscious creaminess, and the smoky crunch of bacon—that makes every mouthful deeply satisfying.

No matter where you are or what kind of day you’ve had, a warm bowl of baked potato soup invites you to slow down, savor, and reconnect with simple pleasures. So go ahead, try this recipe on the next cold evening. Your taste buds—and your soul—will thank you.
