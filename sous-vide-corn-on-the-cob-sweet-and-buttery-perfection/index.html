Sous Vide Corn on the Cob: Sweet and Buttery Perfection


Corn on the cob is a classic summer staple, but have you ever tried cooking it using the sous vide method? Sous vide cooking involves vacuum-sealing food in a bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature for an extended period. This technique ensures that the corn retains its natural sweetness and juiciness, resulting in a truly delectable dish. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps to achieve perfectly cooked sous vide corn on the cob that is sweet and buttery, bringing out the best flavors of this beloved vegetable.

Ingredients

  • 4 ears of fresh corn
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 183°F (84°C).

  2. Husk the corn and remove the silk, ensuring that each ear is clean and free from any debris.

  3. In a small bowl, combine the melted butter and honey. Stir until well mixed.

  4. Take four large pieces of plastic wrap or vacuum-seal bags, enough to wrap each ear of corn individually.

  5. Place one ear of corn on each piece of plastic wrap. Drizzle the butter and honey mixture evenly over each ear, ensuring that they are well-coated. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Tightly wrap each ear of corn in the plastic wrap, ensuring that no air can escape. If using vacuum-seal bags, seal them according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  7. Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, carefully place the wrapped corn into the water bath. Ensure that the corn is fully submerged.

  8. Cook the corn for 60 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the corn to become tender and juicy.

  9. After the cooking time is complete, carefully remove the corn from the water bath. Unwrap the plastic wrap or remove from the vacuum-seal bags.

  10. Serve the sous vide corn on the cob immediately, optionally garnishing with fresh herbs for added flavor and visual appeal.

  11. Enjoy the sweet and buttery perfection of sous vide corn on the cob!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice to the butter and honey mixture before drizzling it over the corn.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, sprinkle a pinch of chili powder or paprika over the corn before wrapping it in the plastic wrap.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to create your own unique flavor combinations. Thyme, rosemary, or even a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese can take this dish to the next level.

Sous vide corn on the cob is a delightful side dish that pairs well with grilled meats, seafood, or even as a standalone snack. Its tender texture and sweet, buttery taste will surely impress your family and friends. Give this recipe a try, and elevate your corn on the cob experience to new heights of perfection!
