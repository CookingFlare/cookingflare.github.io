Vegan and Vegetarian Quiche Recipes for a Savory Meal


Quiche is a versatile, savory dish that has long been a favorite for breakfast, brunch, or lunch. Traditionally made with eggs and cream in a buttery, flaky pastry shell, quiche can easily be adapted to suit different dietary needs. For those following a plant-based or vegetarian diet, vegan and vegetarian quiche recipes offer a delicious and nutritious alternative, without compromising on flavor or texture. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan chef or just beginning to experiment with plant-based meals, these recipes will inspire you to create something hearty, satisfying, and full of fresh ingredients.

Introduction to Vegan and Vegetarian Quiche

Quiche’s comforting nature lies in its creamy filling encased in a delicate pastry, and fortunately, there are numerous ways to achieve this same texture and taste without using animal products. Whether you’re looking for a vegan quiche made with tofu or a vegetarian quiche with eggs and dairy alternatives, the options are endless. By using plant-based ingredients, you can create a dish that suits everyone at the table, including those with dairy allergies or vegans who avoid animal-based products entirely.

In this post, we will explore several vegan and vegetarian quiche recipes that are bursting with flavor, texture, and fresh ingredients. From classic flavors like spinach and mushroom to more creative combinations like roasted red pepper and chickpea, these quiches will keep you coming back for more.

Essential Ingredients for Vegan and Vegetarian Quiche

To craft the perfect vegan or vegetarian quiche, you’ll need to have a few key ingredients. The core components of any quiche are the filling and the crust. Below, we’ll break down both components and provide some useful ingredient substitutions and additions.

Crust

A traditional quiche crust is made from butter, flour, and water, but when making a vegan or vegetarian version, there are some simple substitutions.

  1. For a vegan crust: Use plant-based butter or coconut oil in place of regular butter. You can also make a gluten-free crust using a combination of gluten-free flour and a vegan fat source.

  2. For a healthier option: You might choose to use a whole wheat crust or even a cauliflower crust, which adds extra vegetables and reduces the amount of refined flour.

  3. Pre-made crusts: If you’re short on time, you can opt for pre-made vegan or vegetarian pie crusts found in most grocery stores.

Filling

The filling of a quiche is where the magic happens! The rich, creamy texture is often created with eggs and cream, but there are plenty of plant-based options that replicate this.

  1. For vegan quiche: Tofu is the primary ingredient used for the filling, offering a similar consistency to eggs. Silken tofu, in particular, blends into a smooth, creamy texture that serves as a base for the quiche. You can also use cashews, nutritional yeast, and various plant-based milk (such as almond or soy) for added creaminess.

  2. For vegetarian quiche: Eggs remain a primary ingredient for the filling, but plant-based milks like almond, oat, or soy can be used in place of dairy cream to achieve a similar richness.

  3. Flavorings and seasonings: A variety of herbs and spices such as garlic, onion, turmeric, paprika, and nutritional yeast can be used to elevate the flavor profile of your quiche. Fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley work wonderfully in both vegan and vegetarian versions.

  4. Vegetables: A wide range of vegetables can be used in quiche, including spinach, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, leeks, tomatoes, zucchini, and more. For a protein-packed vegan quiche, consider adding legumes like chickpeas, lentils, or beans.

  5. Cheese: Vegan cheese options such as cashew cheese, dairy-free mozzarella, or nutritional yeast can be used in place of regular cheese in vegan quiches. For vegetarian versions, you can opt for goat cheese, feta, or other non-vegan cheeses.

Vegan Quiche Recipes

1. Vegan Spinach and Mushroom Quiche

A vegan spin on the classic spinach and mushroom quiche, this recipe features tofu as the base for a creamy, savory filling. The mushrooms and spinach add earthiness and color, making this dish a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made vegan pie crust
  • 1 block silken tofu, drained and blended
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat and sauté onions and garlic until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the mushrooms release their moisture and shrink down.
  4. Stir in the chopped spinach and cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. In a blender, combine the silken tofu, nutritional yeast, turmeric, garlic powder, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  6. In a large mixing bowl, combine the tofu mixture with the sautéed vegetables and stir to combine.
  7. Pour the mixture into the pre-made vegan pie crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
  8. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the quiche is firm and lightly golden on top.
  9. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

2. Vegan Roasted Red Pepper and Chickpea Quiche

This quiche brings a touch of Mediterranean flavor with roasted red peppers and chickpeas, creating a hearty and filling dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made vegan pie crust
  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and mashed
  • 1 cup roasted red pepper, diced
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas (or one can, drained and rinsed)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the mashed tofu, roasted red pepper, chickpeas, nutritional yeast, tahini, lemon juice, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  3. Gently fold in the fresh parsley.
  4. Pour the mixture into the pre-made vegan pie crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, until the top is golden and the quiche is firm.
  6. Allow the quiche to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Vegetarian Quiche Recipes

1. Vegetarian Broccoli and Cheddar Quiche

Broccoli and cheddar is a classic flavor combination, and this vegetarian quiche showcases these two ingredients perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 cup broccoli florets, steamed
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese (or a vegan alternative)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (or oat milk for a lighter option)
  • 1/2 cup milk (or soy milk)
  • 1/2 small onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Steam the broccoli florets until tender, then chop into smaller pieces.
  3. In a pan, sauté the onion and garlic in olive oil until soft, about 5 minutes.
  4. In a mixing bowl, whisk the eggs, cream, and milk together. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Stir in the steamed broccoli, sautéed onions, and garlic, followed by the shredded cheddar cheese.
  6. Pour the mixture into the prepared pie crust and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the quiche is set and lightly browned on top.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

2. Vegetarian Tomato and Basil Quiche

For a lighter, fresher option, try this tomato and basil quiche. The juicy tomatoes and fragrant basil make it an ideal summer dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 2 large tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded (or a dairy-free alternative)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk (or almond milk)
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat the olive oil in a pan over medium heat and sauté the tomatoes for 2-3 minutes, until softened.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk the eggs and milk together. Add salt, pepper, and chopped basil.
  4. Layer the sautéed tomatoes and mozzarella in the pie crust.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the tomatoes and cheese.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the quiche is golden and set in the center.
  7. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Tips for Perfect Vegan and Vegetarian Quiches

  • Experiment with fillings: The beauty of quiche lies in its versatility. Feel free to experiment with your favorite vegetables, beans, and spices to create new and exciting combinations.
  • Don’t overfill the crust: While it’s tempting to load up your quiche with lots of filling, be sure not to overfill the crust, as this can cause the quiche to spill over during baking.
  • Let it cool: Allow the quiche to cool slightly before slicing to ensure the filling sets properly and doesn’t become too runny.

Conclusion

Vegan and vegetarian quiches are a wonderful way to enjoy a savory, comforting meal while incorporating a variety of fresh vegetables, plant-based proteins, and dairy-free alternatives. These recipes prove that quiche can be both delicious and inclusive, catering to different dietary needs without compromising on taste or texture. Whether you’re making a vegan spinach and mushroom quiche or a vegetarian tomato and basil quiche, these savory meals are sure to satisfy your hunger and impress your guests.

Explore these quiche recipes, and don’t be afraid to get creative in the kitchen by experimenting with your favorite ingredients. Happy cooking!
