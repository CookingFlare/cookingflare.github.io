The Secrets of Chinese Eggplant: Delicious Recipes to Try


If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine or simply looking to add some variety to your cooking repertoire, Chinese eggplant is a vegetable you shouldn’t overlook. With its unique texture and ability to absorb flavors, Chinese eggplant can be transformed into a variety of mouthwatering dishes that will delight your taste buds. In this blog post, we’ll explore the secrets of Chinese eggplant and share some delicious recipes for you to try at home.

The Versatility of Chinese Eggplant

Chinese eggplant, also known as Asian eggplant or aubergine, is longer and thinner than the more common globe eggplant. It has a sweeter and more delicate flavor, making it a popular ingredient in Chinese cooking. Chinese eggplant’s versatility allows it to be used in a wide range of dishes, from stir-fries to braises and even grilled or roasted.

Recipe 1: Mapo Tofu with Chinese Eggplant

Mapo Tofu is a classic Sichuan dish that combines the creaminess of tofu with the bold flavors of chili bean paste and Sichuan peppercorns. Adding Chinese eggplant to this dish provides a delightful contrast in texture and adds a subtle sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 Chinese eggplants, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 block of soft tofu, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili bean paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns, crushed
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons of water
  • Green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and crushed Sichuan peppercorns and cook until fragrant.
  2. Add the chili bean paste and stir-fry for a minute until well combined.
  3. Add the Chinese eggplant and stir-fry for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  4. Add the tofu cubes and gently stir to combine.
  5. Pour in the vegetable broth and soy sauce. Cover and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the eggplant is tender.
  6. Stir in the cornstarch mixture to thicken the sauce.
  7. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

Recipe 2: Chinese Eggplant with Garlic Sauce

This simple yet flavorful dish showcases the natural sweetness of Chinese eggplant and the aromatic qualities of garlic. It makes for a quick and satisfying vegetarian meal when served with steamed rice or noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 2 Chinese eggplants, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons of water
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  2. Add the sliced Chinese eggplant and stir-fry for a few minutes until slightly softened.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and cornstarch mixture.
  4. Pour the sauce over the eggplant and stir-fry until the sauce thickens and coats the eggplant evenly.
  5. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve hot.

Recipe 3: Chinese Eggplant and Ground Pork Stir-Fry

For those who enjoy a meatier dish, this Chinese eggplant and ground pork stir-fry is sure to satisfy. The combination of savory ground pork and tender eggplant creates a harmonious blend of flavors that is hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 2 Chinese eggplants, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 200g ground pork
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine or dry sherry
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons of water
  • Green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  2. Add the ground pork and stir-fry until cooked through and lightly browned.
  3. Add the sliced Chinese eggplant and continue stir-frying for a few minutes until the eggplant is slightly softened.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, Shaoxing wine, sugar, and cornstarch mixture.
  5. Pour the sauce over the eggplant and pork mixture. Stir-fry until the sauce thickens and coats the ingredients evenly.
  6. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

Conclusion

Chinese eggplant is a versatile and delicious ingredient that can elevate your cooking to new heights. Whether you prefer spicy Sichuan flavors or the simplicity of garlic sauce, there’s a Chinese eggplant recipe for every palate. So go ahead and explore the secrets of Chinese eggplant in your kitchen, and let your taste buds embark on a delightful culinary journey. Happy cooking!
