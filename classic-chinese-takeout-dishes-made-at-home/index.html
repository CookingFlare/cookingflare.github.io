Classic Chinese Takeout Dishes Made at Home


Chinese takeout is a popular choice for many people when it comes to quick and delicious meals. However, with a few key ingredients and some simple techniques, you can recreate these classic dishes in the comfort of your own home. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most beloved Chinese takeout dishes and provide easy-to-follow recipes that will satisfy your cravings and impress your friends and family.

1. General Tso’s Chicken

General Tso’s Chicken is a staple in Chinese-American cuisine, known for its crispy chicken and tangy sauce. To make this dish at home, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, sliced

To prepare General Tso’s Chicken, follow these steps:

  1. In a bowl, toss the chicken pieces with cornstarch until well coated.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, honey, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes.
  5. In a clean pan, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  6. Pour the sauce mixture into the pan and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  7. Add the fried chicken to the pan and toss until well coated.
  8. Garnish with green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.

2. Mongolian Beef

Mongolian Beef is a classic Chinese dish that features tender strips of beef cooked in a rich and savory sauce. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Sesame seeds for garnish (optional)

To make Mongolian Beef, follow these steps:

  1. In a bowl, toss the beef slices with cornstarch until well coated.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan or wok over high heat.
  3. Fry the beef in batches until browned. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, and beef broth.
  5. In the same pan, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  6. Pour the sauce mixture into the pan and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  7. Add the fried beef to the pan and toss until well coated.
  8. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds, if desired. Serve hot with steamed rice.

3. Kung Pao Chicken

Kung Pao Chicken is a spicy and flavorful dish that combines tender chicken, peanuts, and vegetables in a savory sauce. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup unsalted peanuts
  • 2 green onions, sliced

To prepare Kung Pao Chicken, follow these steps:

  1. In a bowl, toss the chicken pieces with cornstarch until well coated.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, honey, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes.
  5. In the same pan, heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add garlic, ginger, and red bell pepper, and sauté until tender.
  6. Pour the sauce mixture into the pan and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  7. Add the fried chicken, peanuts, and green onions to the pan and toss until well coated.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Conclusion

With these easy-to-follow recipes, you can recreate your favorite classic Chinese takeout dishes right in your own kitchen. Whether it’s General Tso’s Chicken, Mongolian Beef, or Kung Pao Chicken, these homemade versions are sure to impress your taste buds. So grab your wok and get cooking!

Remember, cooking is all about experimentation and adding your own personal touch. Feel free to adjust the ingredients and seasonings according to your taste preferences. Enjoy your homemade Chinese takeout feast and savor the flavors of the East!
