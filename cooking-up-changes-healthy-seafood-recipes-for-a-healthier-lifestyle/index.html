Cooking Up Changes: Healthy Seafood Recipes for a Healthier Lifestyle


Embrace the ocean’s bounty and elevate your wellbeing with these scrumptious seafood recipes that are loaded with nutrients and sumptuous flavors. Seafood doesn’t have to be dull or unhealthy - here are some mouth-watering options to inspire your cooking, helping you embark on a healthier route with vibrant taste sensations.

Lightened-Up Shrimp Scampi with Whole Wheat Pasta

Revamp the classic Shrimp Scampi by incorporatingWhole Wheat Pasta and a zesty garlic-lemon sauce. This heart-healthy meal offers a burst of flavors with a fraction of the calories of its traditional counterpart. Whole Wheat Pasta Whole Wheat Pasta, packed with fiber, provides a filling and nutritious base to your dish. Unlike regular pasta, it still recreates that satisfying bite and texture you, and your palate, crave.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz Whole Wheat Spaghetti
  • 2 lbs Medium Shrimp, shelled and deveined
  • 4 cloves Fresh Garlic, minced
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • Red Pepper Flakes (optional)
  • 1/4 cup Butter
  • 1 Lemon, juiced and zested
  • 1/3 cup Fresh Parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • Olive Oil, for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Season shrimp with salt and pepper, and cook them in a large skillet over medium heat until pink, about 3-4 minutes. Transfer the shrimp to a plate.
  2. In the same skillet, melt the butter and add garlic. Cook until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the shrimp and add lemon juice and zest, red pepper flakes (optional), and parsley. Simmer for another 2 minutes.
  4. Toss in the cooked Whole Wheat Spaghetti, combining it gently with the sauce.
  5. Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese and give the dish a final mix to make sure all ingredients are well incorporated.
  6. Drizzle with olive oil, and voil√†! Serve this protein-rich delight on a bed of steamed green vegetables for a gratifying and nourishing meal.

Omega-3 Rich Grilled Salmon with Quinoa Salad

Boost your heart health with thisOmega-3 packed Grilled Salmonrecipe entwined with a refreshing Quinoa Salad. Salmon is one of the fish highest in omega-3 fatty acids, essential for brain, heart, and joint health. This recipe offers a balanced and nutritious dish perfect for any health-conscious foodie.

Ingredients:

For the Quinoa Salad:

  • 1 cup Cooked Quinoa
  • 1 small Cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 Red Onion, finely chopped
  • 1 Cherry Tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata Olives, pitted and halved
  • 3 Tbsp Fresh Dill, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp Fresh Lemon Juice
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste
  • Olive Oil, for drizzling

For the Grilled Salmon:

  • 2 (6 oz) Salmon Fillets
  • Salt, Pepper, and Dried Thyme, to taste
  • 2 Tbsp Olive Oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Quinoa according to package directions. Set it aside to cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, cucumber, red onion, halved olives, cherry tomato, and dill.
  3. Drizzle the lemon juice over the salad, add a pinch of salt and pepper, and lightly drizzle with olive oil. Stir well to combine.
  4. For the salmon, preheat a grill or grill pan to medium-high heat.
  5. Season the salmon fillets with salt, pepper, and a pinch of dried thyme. Drizzle the olive oil over the fish, making sure to cover the entire surface.
  6. Cook the salmon for about 4-5 minutes per side, depending on the desired level of doneness.
  7. Plate the Quinoa Salad and top it with the mouth-watering grilled salmon fillets. This protein and fiber-packed dish promises to leave you feeling satisfied while fostering a healthier lifestyle.

Soothing Seared Tuna with Edamame Sesame Slaw

Entice your taste buds with this refreshing Seared Tuna meal combined with a lively Edamame Sesame Slaw. Tuna is a leaner protein source while also supplying antioxidants and vitamins. The crisp Edamame Sesame Slaw adds a delightful crunch while complementing the richness of the seared tuna. Let’s bring this dish to life and infuse some color and zest into your plate!

Ingredients:

For the Tuna:

  • 2 (6 oz) Ahi Tuna Steaks
  • Sesame Oil, for drizzling
  • Salt and Pepper, to taste

For the Edamame Sesame Slaw:

  • 2 cups Cooked Edamame
  • 1/2 Red Cabbage, shredded
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper, julienned
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Mint Leaves, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp Coconut Aminos
  • 1 Tbsp Sesame Oil
  • 1 Tbsp Rice Vinegar
  • 1 Tsp Honey
  • 1/2 Tsp Red Pepper Flakes
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season both sides of the tuna steaks with salt and pepper, and drizzle with sesame oil.
  2. In a hot skillet over medium-high heat, sear the tuna steaks for 2-3 minutes per side, or to your desired doneness.
  3. In a large bowl, mix the cooked edamame, shredded red cabbage, julienned red bell pepper, chopped cilantro, and mint leaves.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut aminos, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, and red pepper flakes. Season with salt and set aside.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the slaw, tossing well to evenly coat.
  6. Slice the seared tuna and arrange it atop the vibrant Edamame Sesame Slaw for a harmonious blend of flavors and textures.
