How to Make an Easy Lemon Cake from Scratch


Lemon cake is the epitome of sunshine on your plate. Its tangy citrus flavor perfectly balances the sweetness, creating a light and refreshing dessert suitable for any occasion. Whether you’re entertaining guests or simply craving a personal treat, crafting a lemon cake from scratch is surprisingly simple. In this guide, we’ll walk you step by step through the process, ensuring your cake is moist, flavorful, and irresistibly good.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For any recipe, starting with the right ingredients is key. Here’s what you’ll need to whip up a delicious lemon cake from scratch:

For the Cake:

  • 2 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon of baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  • Zest of 2 lemons (preferably organic)
  • ½ cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 4-5 lemons)
  • ¾ cup of whole milk

For the Lemon Glaze:

  • 1 ½ cups of powdered sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Equipment You’ll Need

To prepare a lemon cake, it’s helpful to have the following equipment on hand:

  • Stand mixer or hand mixer
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Medium mixing bowl
  • Whisk
  • Citrus zester
  • Lemon juicer
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Rubber spatula
  • 9x13-inch cake pan or two 9-inch round cake pans
  • Parchment paper (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare Your Workspace

Before diving into the recipe, ensure your oven is preheated to 350°F (175°C). Grease your chosen cake pan(s) with either butter or nonstick spray, and if desired, line the bottom with parchment paper for easy removal. Set aside.

2. Combine Dry Ingredients

In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. This step ensures even distribution of leavening agents, which contributes to a uniform and fluffy cake texture.

3. Cream the Butter and Sugar

In a large mixing bowl, use a stand mixer or hand mixer to cream the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This step is crucial in incorporating air into the batter, which ensures a tender crumb. Be patient—this may take 3-4 minutes.

4. Add Eggs and Flavorings

Add one egg at a time to the creamed mixture, beating well after each addition. Once all eggs are incorporated, mix in the vanilla extract and lemon zest. The lemon zest carries the aromatic oils of the lemon and delivers that bright citrus flavor.

5. Incorporate Lemon Juice and Milk

Reduce the mixer speed to low and gradually add the freshly squeezed lemon juice alongside the whole milk. Don’t worry if the mixture appears curdled—this is completely normal and will come together once the dry ingredients are added.

6. Fold in the Dry Ingredients

With the mixer still on low, slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture in three increments. After each addition, mix just until incorporated. Be careful not to overmix, as this could result in a dense cake. Use a rubber spatula to scrape the sides and bottom of the bowl to ensure everything is well combined.

7. Pour and Bake

Transfer the batter to your prepared cake pan(s), spreading it evenly. Smooth the surface with the spatula if needed. Place the pan(s) in the preheated oven and bake for 30-36 minutes (for round pans) or 40-45 minutes (for a 9x13-inch pan), or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

8. Cool the Cake

Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Afterward, transfer the cake to a cooling rack to cool completely before adding the glaze. Cooling ensures the glaze doesn’t slide off and properly adheres to the cake.

9. Prepare the Lemon Glaze

While the cake is cooling, prepare your lemon glaze. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, freshly squeezed lemon juice, and zest of one lemon until smooth. Adjust the consistency by adding more powdered sugar (for a thicker glaze) or lemon juice (for a thinner glaze). The glaze should be pourable but not overly runny.

10. Glaze the Cake

Once the cake has cooled, drizzle the lemon glaze over the top, allowing it to gently cascade down the sides. If you’re feeling creative, use a spoon to spread the glaze evenly or let gravity work its magic for a rustic look. Allow the glaze to set for at least 10 minutes before serving.

Tips for Success

- Use Fresh Lemons

The flavor of your lemon cake rests heavily on the quality of lemons you use. Freshly squeezed lemon juice and vibrant lemon zest will deliver the bright, tangy flavor that makes this cake shine.

- Room Temperature Ingredients

Ensuring your butter, eggs, and milk are at room temperature helps everything mix smoothly, preventing curdling and uneven textures.

- Don’t Overmix

Once you add the dry ingredients, be careful not to overmix your batter. Overmixing can activate too much gluten in the flour, resulting in a dense and chewy cake.

- Test for Doneness

Every oven operates slightly differently, so keep a close eye on your cake during the baking process. Use the toothpick test to confirm it’s baked through before removing it.

Serving Suggestions

Lemon cake is a versatile treat that can be enjoyed in countless ways. Serve it as-is for a simple weekday dessert or pair it with a dollop of whipped cream and fresh berries for a zest-filled celebration. For special occasions, consider garnishing it with candied lemon slices; they add an elegant flair and even more citrusy goodness.

Storing Your Lemon Cake

If you have leftovers (though this cake is likely to disappear quickly!), store them properly to keep the cake fresh. Cover tightly with plastic wrap or place in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. If you need to store it longer, move the cake to the fridge, where it will stay fresh for up to a week. Bring refrigerated cake to room temperature before serving for the best texture and flavor.

Why Make Lemon Cake from Scratch?

While boxed cake mixes might be convenient, nothing beats the flavor of a homemade lemon cake. Every ingredient adds to your cake’s taste and texture, giving it depth and freshness you simply can’t achieve with pre-packaged options. Plus, making dessert from scratch allows you to adjust sweetness, tartness, and other elements to suit your preferences.

Final Thoughts

A freshly baked lemon cake is an expression of warmth and creativity. By following these simple steps, you can confidently create a dessert that showcases harmony in flavor and texture—all with the satisfaction of knowing you made it yourself. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this easy lemon cake recipe is bound to impress. So grab your lemons and get baking! Your kitchen is moments away from being filled with the irresistible scent of homemade citrus bliss.
