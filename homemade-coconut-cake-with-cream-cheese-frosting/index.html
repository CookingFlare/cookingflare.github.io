Homemade Coconut Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting


Coconut cake is one of those desserts that brings a sense of nostalgia and comfort to anyone who’s ever enjoyed a slice. With its delicate, moist layers and creamy, tangy frosting, it’s the perfect treat for any occasion, whether you’re celebrating a special event or just looking to satisfy your sweet tooth. This homemade coconut cake with cream cheese frosting is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser, combining the subtle sweetness of coconut with the rich, velvety smoothness of cream cheese frosting. It’s an easy-to-make cake that can impress even the most discerning dessert lovers. So, if you’re ready to bake something delicious, here’s everything you need to know about making a perfect coconut cake from scratch.

Ingredients for Coconut Cake

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups buttermilk (room temperature)
  • 1 ½ cups unsweetened shredded coconut (plus extra for garnish)
  • ½ cup coconut milk (full-fat, optional for added coconut flavor)

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese (softened)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream (or more, depending on consistency)

Optional Garnish:

  • Additional shredded coconut for coating the outside of the cake
  • Toasted coconut flakes for extra texture and flavor

Preparing the Coconut Cake

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans, ensuring that the cake comes out easily after baking.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set this bowl aside while you prepare the wet ingredients. The dry mixture is essential for creating the structure of the cake and helping it rise evenly.

Step 3: Cream the Butter and Sugar

In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar together using an electric mixer on medium speed. Beat until the mixture is light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes. This step is crucial for creating a light, airy texture in the cake.

Step 4: Add the Eggs

Add the eggs, one at a time, to the butter-sugar mixture, beating well after each addition. This helps incorporate air into the batter, which will give your cake that soft, fluffy texture. Don’t forget to scrape down the sides of the bowl to ensure everything is well combined.

Step 5: Add the Vanilla Extract and Coconut Milk

Add the vanilla extract and coconut milk to the mixture, and beat until smooth. Coconut milk will enhance the coconut flavor of the cake, adding richness and depth.

Step 6: Alternately Add the Dry Ingredients and Buttermilk

Now it’s time to alternate adding the dry ingredients and buttermilk to the batter. Start by adding one-third of the dry ingredients to the mixture, mixing until combined, then add half of the buttermilk. Repeat this process, finishing with the dry ingredients. This helps prevent overmixing, which can result in a dense cake.

Step 7: Fold in the Shredded Coconut

Once the batter is smooth and well combined, fold in the shredded coconut. You can also add coconut milk at this point to enhance the flavor. Gently fold the coconut into the batter using a spatula, being careful not to deflate the mixture.

Step 8: Bake the Cake

Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared cake pans. Smooth the tops with a spatula for an even bake. Place the pans in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean. Be careful not to overbake; you want the cakes to remain moist and soft.

Step 9: Cool the Cakes

Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes before removing them and transferring them to wire racks to cool completely. This step is crucial to prevent the cakes from breaking apart when you frost them.

Preparing the Cream Cheese Frosting

Step 1: Beat the Cream Cheese and Butter

While the cakes are cooling, it’s time to prepare the cream cheese frosting. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together using an electric mixer on medium speed until smooth and creamy. This creates the base for your frosting.

Step 2: Add the Powdered Sugar

Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, beating well after each addition. This will create a thick, sweet frosting. Continue mixing until the frosting is smooth and fluffy. Adjust the consistency by adding a little heavy cream if the frosting seems too thick.

Step 3: Add the Vanilla Extract

Finally, add the vanilla extract and mix to combine. Vanilla enhances the cream cheese flavor and adds a hint of sweetness to balance out the tanginess of the cream cheese.

Assembling the Coconut Cake

Step 1: Level the Cakes (Optional)

Once your cakes have cooled completely, you may want to level them by trimming the tops with a serrated knife. This step is optional but helps ensure an even, smooth surface for the frosting.

Step 2: Frost the Cake

Place the first cake layer on a serving platter. Spread a generous amount of cream cheese frosting over the top, smoothing it out with a spatula. Place the second cake layer on top and gently press it down to ensure it’s sitting evenly.

Step 3: Coat the Cake with Frosting

Use the remaining cream cheese frosting to cover the entire cake, including the sides. Apply a thick layer to achieve a smooth finish. If you like a rustic look, you can apply the frosting more loosely for a textured appearance.

Step 4: Garnish with Shredded Coconut

Once the cake is fully frosted, garnish the sides and top with shredded coconut. You can use sweetened or unsweetened coconut, depending on your preference. Toasted coconut flakes can also be sprinkled on top for a crunchy, golden touch.

Step 5: Chill the Cake

For the best results, allow the cake to chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving. This gives the frosting a chance to firm up and the flavors to meld together.

Tips for a Perfect Coconut Cake

Use Fresh Coconut

If possible, use fresh coconut for the best flavor and texture. Shredded coconut found in stores can sometimes be dry and lacking in freshness. Fresh coconut adds a moist, tender crumb to the cake and a richer, more natural coconut flavor.

Experiment with Coconut Extract

If you love the taste of coconut, you can add a little coconut extract to both the cake batter and the frosting. This will intensify the coconut flavor and make the cake even more aromatic.

Make It Ahead of Time

Coconut cake actually gets better with time, so feel free to make it a day ahead of when you plan to serve it. The flavors will have a chance to develop, and the frosting will become even creamier.

Try a Layered Design

For a more elegant presentation, you can stack the cake into three or four layers. This not only looks impressive but also gives more surface area for frosting, ensuring that every bite is as creamy and coconutty as possible.

Why This Coconut Cake is a Must-Try

This coconut cake with cream cheese frosting is more than just a dessert—it’s a celebration of flavor, texture, and balance. The light, fluffy cake combined with the tangy, creamy frosting creates a perfect harmony that will leave your taste buds begging for more. Whether you’re making it for a birthday, holiday, or simply as a sweet indulgence, this homemade coconut cake will never disappoint.

The best part? It’s a versatile recipe. You can adapt it by adding other ingredients like pineapple for a tropical twist, or a bit of lime zest for a citrusy touch. No matter how you customize it, this cake remains a timeless favorite for coconut lovers everywhere.

So next time you’re looking for a show-stopping dessert, turn to this coconut cake with cream cheese frosting. It’s a treat that’s as delightful to make as it is to eat, and it will surely be a hit at any gathering. Enjoy every bite and savor the delicious, homemade goodness!
