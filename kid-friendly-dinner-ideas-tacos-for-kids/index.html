Kid-Friendly Dinner Ideas: Tacos for Kids


Tacos are a classic favorite among kids, and for good reason. They’re fun, customizable, and can be filled with a variety of delicious ingredients. In this post, we’ll explore some kid-friendly dinner ideas that are sure to be a hit with your little ones.

The Benefits of Tacos for Kids

Tacos offer a number of benefits for kids, including:

  • Customization: Let’s face it, kids can be picky eaters. Tacos allow them to choose their own fillings, which means they’re more likely to eat what’s in front of them.
  • Nutrition: Tacos can be filled with a variety of nutritious ingredients, such as lean protein, whole grains, and plenty of vegetables.
  • Fun: Tacos are a fun and interactive meal option that can help to create a positive dining experience for kids.

Kid-Friendly Taco Fillings

When it comes to filling tacos for kids, the options are endless. Here are a few kid-friendly ideas:

  • Ground Beef: A classic taco filling, ground beef is a crowd-pleaser among kids. You can season it with taco seasoning for added flavor.
  • Shredded Chicken: Shredded chicken is a great option for kids who don’t like ground beef. You can season it with fajita seasoning for added flavor.
  • Black Beans: Black beans are a great source of protein and fiber, making them a nutritious option for kids. You can season them with cumin and chili powder for added flavor.
  • Cheese: Let’s face it, kids love cheese. You can use shredded cheese or sliced cheese to add some extra flavor to your tacos.
  • Veggies: Tacos are a great way to get kids to eat more vegetables. You can use shredded carrots, diced bell peppers, and diced tomatoes to add some extra nutrition to your tacos.

How to Make Tacos for Kids

Making tacos for kids is easy and fun. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Use store-bought taco seasoning: Taco seasoning is a great way to add flavor to your tacos without a lot of fuss. You can find it in most grocery stores.
  • Use pre-cooked ground beef: Pre-cooked ground beef is a great time-saver when it comes to making tacos for kids. You can find it in most grocery stores.
  • Use whole wheat tortillas: Whole wheat tortillas are a great way to add some extra nutrition to your tacos. You can find them in most grocery stores.
  • Let kids customize their own tacos: Letting kids customize their own tacos is a great way to get them to eat what’s in front of them. You can offer a variety of toppings, such as shredded cheese, diced tomatoes, and shredded lettuce.

5 Kid-Friendly Taco Recipes

Here are 5 kid-friendly taco recipes that your little ones are sure to love:

Recipe 1: Classic Ground Beef Tacos

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 packet of taco seasoning
  • 8-10 whole wheat tortillas
  • Shredded cheese, diced tomatoes, and shredded lettuce for toppings

Brown the ground beef in a large skillet, then add the taco seasoning and cook according to package instructions. Serve the ground beef in the whole wheat tortillas with your choice of toppings.

Recipe 2: Shredded Chicken Tacos

  • 1 pound cooked chicken breast
  • 1 packet of fajita seasoning
  • 8-10 whole wheat tortillas
  • Shredded cheese, diced bell peppers, and shredded lettuce for toppings

Shred the cooked chicken breast and season with fajita seasoning. Serve the shredded chicken in the whole wheat tortillas with your choice of toppings.

Recipe 3: Black Bean and Cheese Tacos

  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
  • 8-10 whole wheat tortillas
  • Diced tomatoes and shredded lettuce for toppings

Heat the black beans in a large skillet and season with cumin and chili powder. Serve the black beans in the whole wheat tortillas with shredded cheese and your choice of toppings.

Recipe 4: Veggie Tacos

  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 8-10 whole wheat tortillas
  • Shredded cheese and shredded lettuce for toppings

Heat the shredded carrots, diced bell peppers, and diced tomatoes in a large skillet and season with cumin and chili powder. Serve the vegetables in the whole wheat tortillas with shredded cheese and your choice of toppings.

Recipe 5: Breakfast Tacos

  • 1 cup scrambled eggs
  • 1 cup cooked sausage
  • 8-10 whole wheat tortillas
  • Shredded cheese and diced bell peppers for toppings

Scramble the eggs and cook the sausage in a large skillet. Serve the scrambled eggs and cooked sausage in the whole wheat tortillas with shredded cheese and your choice of toppings.

Conclusion

Tacos are a fun and interactive meal option that can be customized to suit your kids’ tastes. With a variety of filling options and toppings, you’re sure to find a recipe that your little ones will love. Whether you’re looking for a quick and easy meal or a fun and interactive way to get your kids to eat more vegetables, tacos are a great option. So next time you’re planning dinner, consider making some kid-friendly tacos for your little ones.
