Fall Appetizers: Recipes with Seasonal Squash


As the leaves start to change and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to embrace the flavors of fall. One of the most versatile and delicious ingredients of the season is squash. From butternut to acorn, squash varieties offer a rich and earthy taste that can be transformed into delectable appetizers that will impress any guest. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering fall appetizer recipes featuring seasonal squash that are perfect for any autumn gathering.

1. Butternut Squash Crostini

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 baguette, sliced
  • 1 clove garlic, halved
  • 4 ounces goat cheese
  • Fresh thyme leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.
  3. While the squash is roasting, brush the baguette slices with olive oil and toast in the oven until golden brown.
  4. Rub the toasted baguette slices with the halved garlic clove.
  5. Spread a layer of goat cheese on each crostini and top with a spoonful of roasted butternut squash.
  6. Garnish with fresh thyme leaves and serve.

2. Acorn Squash Soup Shooters

Ingredients:

  • 2 small acorn squash, halved and seeded
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 45-50 minutes, or until tender.
  3. In a large pot, melt the butter and sauté the chopped onion and garlic until soft.
  4. Scoop the roasted squash flesh into the pot and add the vegetable broth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth.
  6. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Pour the soup into shot glasses or small cups, garnish with fresh chives, and serve.

3. Spaghetti Squash Fritters

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash, cooked and shredded
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded spaghetti squash, Parmesan cheese, flour, green onions, beaten egg, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Heat a thin layer of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  3. Form the squash mixture into small patties and carefully place them in the hot skillet.
  4. Fry the fritters for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden and crisp.
  5. Transfer the fritters to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  6. Serve the spaghetti squash fritters warm with a side of sour cream or marinara sauce for dipping.

These delightful fall appetizers celebrate the bountiful flavors of the season and are sure to be a hit at any autumn gathering. Whether you’re hosting a cozy dinner party or simply want to savor the tastes of fall, these recipes with seasonal squash will elevate your appetizer game and leave your guests craving more. So, embrace the autumn harvest and get creative with these delicious squash-inspired appetizers!
