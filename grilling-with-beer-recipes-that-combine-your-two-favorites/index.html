Grilling with Beer: Recipes That Combine Your Two Favorites


Who doesn’t love a good barbecue? The sizzle of the grill, the aroma of charred meat, and the promise of delicious flavors all come together to create the ultimate outdoor dining experience. Now, imagine taking that experience to the next level by incorporating one of your other favorite things - beer! Yes, you heard that right. Grilling with beer can add a whole new dimension to your favorite dishes, infusing them with rich, complex flavors that will leave your taste buds tingling. In this blog post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering recipes that combine the best of grilling and the rich, malty goodness of beer.

Beer-Marinated Grilled Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into chunks
  • 1 bottle of your favorite beer (pale ale or lager works well)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the beer, minced garlic, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken chunks to the marinade, ensuring they are fully coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight for maximum flavor infusion.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken chunks onto the soaked skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a delicious charred exterior.

The beer marinade not only tenderizes the chicken but also imparts a subtle malty flavor that takes this classic grilled dish to a whole new level.

Beer Can Burgers

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices of cheddar cheese
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 slices of bacon
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 oz beer (any variety)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions and shape them into large patties.
  3. Season the patties with salt and pepper.
  4. Make a small indentation in the center of each patty and place a slice of cheddar cheese, some sliced onions, and minced garlic into the indentation.
  5. Wrap each patty with a slice of bacon and secure it with a toothpick.
  6. Open a can of beer and drink or discard half of it.
  7. Carefully place the beer can upright on the grill and slide the prepared burger patties over the beer can, forming a cup shape.
  8. Grill the burgers for 20-25 minutes, or until the beef is cooked through and the bacon is crispy.

The beer in the can steams the burgers from the inside, keeping them juicy and flavorful. Plus, the infusion of beer flavors takes the humble burger to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Beer-Braised BBQ Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs pork ribs
  • 2 bottles of dark beer (stout or porter)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the pork ribs with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large roasting pan, combine the beer, chopped onion, and minced garlic.
  3. Place the seasoned ribs in the pan, ensuring they are fully submerged in the beer mixture.
  4. Cover the pan with foil and place it on the grill over indirect heat.
  5. Braise the ribs for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and juicy.
  6. Remove the ribs from the pan and generously brush them with BBQ sauce.
  7. Return the ribs to the grill and cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, basting with more BBQ sauce as they cook.

The slow braising process infuses the ribs with the rich flavors of the beer, resulting in tender, succulent meat that practically falls off the bone.

Conclusion

Grilling with beer opens up a world of tantalizing flavors and creative possibilities. Whether you’re marinating, steaming, or braising, beer can add depth and complexity to your favorite grilled dishes. So, the next time you fire up the grill, don’t forget to crack open a cold one and let the magic of beer elevate your barbecue game to new heights. Cheers to delicious grilling with beer!
