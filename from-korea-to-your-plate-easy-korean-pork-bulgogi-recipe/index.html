From Korea to Your Plate: Easy Korean Pork Bulgogi Recipe


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or simply love exploring new flavors, then this Easy Korean Pork Bulgogi recipe is a must-try! Bursting with bold and savory flavors, this dish will transport your taste buds straight to the streets of Korea. The best part? It’s incredibly simple to make, requiring just a few basic ingredients and minimal preparation time. So, let’s dive right in and learn how to create this delicious Korean delight!

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of pork belly, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine (mirin)
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for cooking)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the soy sauce, brown sugar, minced garlic, sesame oil, grated ginger, rice wine, and toasted sesame seeds. Mix well until the sugar has dissolved and the marinade is well combined.

  2. Add the thinly sliced pork belly to the marinade, ensuring that each slice is well coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork slices, reserving the marinade for later use.

  4. Stir-fry the pork slices for 5-7 minutes, or until they are cooked through and slightly caramelized. Make sure to flip them occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and pour in the reserved marinade. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken slightly.

  6. Remove the skillet from the heat and garnish the pork bulgogi with thinly sliced green onions.

  7. Serve the Easy Korean Pork Bulgogi hot with steamed rice and your favorite Korean side dishes for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips and Tricks

  • To achieve perfectly thin slices of pork belly, try freezing the meat for about 1 hour before slicing. This will make it easier to achieve the desired thickness.

  • If you prefer a spicier version of this dish, feel free to add a teaspoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste) or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes to the marinade.

  • For an extra burst of freshness, serve the pork bulgogi with a side of crisp lettuce leaves. Simply wrap a slice of the pork in a lettuce leaf, add a dollop of ssamjang (Korean dipping sauce), and enjoy!

Conclusion

With just a handful of ingredients and a few simple steps, you can now bring the vibrant flavors of Korea to your own plate. This Easy Korean Pork Bulgogi recipe is a fantastic introduction to Korean cuisine, showcasing the perfect balance of sweet, savory, and umami flavors. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and get ready to enjoy a truly delicious meal that will transport you straight to the heart of Korea.
