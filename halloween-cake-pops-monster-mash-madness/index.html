Halloween Cake Pops: Monster Mash Madness


Ah, October is finally here! You can smell the crisp air, hear the howling winds, and feel the excitement for Halloween coming our way. Before you usher in the frightful night with your spooky costume and ghoulish gatherings, how about baking some delectable Halloween Cake Pops? This year, let’s bring a twist to the classic cake ball confection with our monstrous and enticing recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 18.25 oz Devil’s Food Cake Mix (plus required oil and water)
  • 1/2 cup prepared Espresso, at room temperature
  • 2 cups Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted Butter
  • 2 tablespoons Vegetable Shortening
  • 1 teaspoon Green Gel Food Coloring
  • White Nonpareils
  • Black Sugar Pearls
  • Black fondant for monster eyes

Instructions:

Step 1: Bake & Cool the Cake

Start by baking the devil’s food cake in a 9x13 inch baking pan, according to the package instructions. Let it cool for about 15 minutes in the pan, and then carefully take it out. Allow the cake to cool completely, preferably overnight.

Step 2: Crumble the Cake

Once the cake is entirely cooled, crumble it into a large mixing bowl, breaking up any clumps as you go.

Step 3: Add Espresso

Pour in the room-temperature espresso and mix until the cake has absorbed the liquid. The mixture should resemble a thick muddy texture; this will give it an added depth of flavor.

Step 4: Combine Ingredients

Now, it’s time to mix in the softened cream cheese one cup at a time, continuing to blend until both ingredients are evenly incorporated.

Step 5: Form the Cake Pops

With your hands, scoop out a two-inch ball of the mixture, roll it into shape, and place it on a tray lined with parchment paper. Repeat this process until all the mixture is used up, resulting in about 24 balls.

Step 6: Prepare the Chocolate Coating

In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the semi-sweet chocolate chips with the butter and vegetable shortening, stirring every 30 seconds to prevent overheating. Once fully melted and smooth, set aside to cool slightly.

Step 7: Dip Cake Pops

By now, the chocolate coating should have thickened slightly. Take a lollipop stick and insert it into the center of a cake ball. Adjust the thickness of the coating so that it doesn’t drip, and dip the cake pop into the chocolate. Slowly rotate the pop to ensure it’s fully coated.

Step 8: Decorate the Cake Pops

Sprinkle with green nonpareils for hair and black sugar pearls for eyes (you can add more dots for pupils if desired). Then, using the black fondant, roll small balls for additional decorations and attach them to the cake pops to complete your monster’s appearance.

Step 9: Settle the Freaks

Chill your latter-day monstrous cake pops in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the coating to set securely and keep their shape.

Step 10: Serve & Enjoy

Your Halloween Cake Pops are now ready to dazzle your guests. Keep the monster treats in the fridge and bring them out to have everyone gawking and giggling in your monstrous bash.

So gather your friends and family members, serve these ghoulish delights, and have a hauntingly unforgettable Halloween!

Enjoy the Monster Mash Madness, and happy baking!
