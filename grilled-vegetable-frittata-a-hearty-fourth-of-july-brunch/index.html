Grilled Vegetable Frittata: A Hearty Fourth of July Brunch


Summer is here, and what better way to celebrate the Fourth of July than with a delicious and hearty brunch? This Grilled Vegetable Frittata is not only packed with flavor but also perfect for feeding a crowd. The best part? It’s easy to make and showcases the vibrant colors of seasonal vegetables. So fire up the grill, gather your friends and family, and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 8-10 asparagus spears, trimmed
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (such as parsley or basil) for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, prepare the vegetables by slicing them into thin, even strips.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper until well combined. Set aside.

  3. Brush the sliced vegetables with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place them on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly charred. Make sure to turn them occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  4. Once the vegetables are grilled to perfection, remove them from the grill and let them cool slightly. Then, chop them into smaller pieces, if desired.

  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Heat a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat and lightly coat it with cooking spray or a drizzle of olive oil.

  6. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet and let it cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the edges start to set.

  7. Gently arrange the grilled vegetables on top of the partially cooked eggs, distributing them evenly. Press them down slightly into the eggs.

  8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the frittata is set and slightly golden on top.

  9. Remove the frittata from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh herbs, if desired.

  10. Slice the frittata into wedges and serve warm or at room temperature. It pairs perfectly with a side salad or some crusty bread.

The Perfect Brunch Dish

The Grilled Vegetable Frittata is not only a delicious and satisfying dish, but it also makes for a stunning centerpiece on your brunch table. The smoky and charred flavors from the grilled vegetables add depth to the dish, while the creamy and fluffy egg mixture brings everything together.

This frittata is incredibly versatile, allowing you to customize it with your favorite vegetables and herbs. You can also add some crumbled feta or goat cheese for an extra layer of richness.

Whether you’re hosting a Fourth of July brunch or simply looking for a crowd-pleasing dish to enjoy with friends and family, this Grilled Vegetable Frittata is the perfect choice. It’s a celebration of summer flavors and a wholesome meal that will keep everyone coming back for seconds.

So gather your loved ones, fire up the grill, and make this hearty frittata the star of your Fourth of July brunch. It’s a dish that embodies the spirit of the season and will leave everyone satisfied and impressed. Happy cooking and happy Fourth of July!
