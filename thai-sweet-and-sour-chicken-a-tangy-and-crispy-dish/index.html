Thai Sweet and Sour Chicken: A Tangy and Crispy Dish


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this delicious recipe for Thai Sweet and Sour Chicken. This tangy and crispy dish is a crowd-pleaser that combines the perfect balance of flavors. The succulent chicken is coated in a crispy batter, then smothered in a sweet and tangy sauce that will leave your taste buds tingling. Best of all, it’s easy to make at home!

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup cold water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup water

For the Stir-fry:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 green bell pepper, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 carrot, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and black pepper. Gradually add the cold water, whisking until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.

  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat. Dip each piece of chicken into the batter, allowing any excess to drip off, then carefully place it into the hot oil. Fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes per batch. Transfer the cooked chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the ketchup, rice vinegar, soy sauce, honey, oyster sauce, cornstarch, and water until well combined and smooth. Set aside.

  4. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant. Add the sliced onion, bell peppers, and carrot to the skillet and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.

  5. Pour the prepared sauce into the skillet with the vegetables. Stir continuously until the sauce thickens and coats the vegetables, about 2-3 minutes. Add the fried chicken pieces to the skillet and toss until they are well coated with the sauce.

  6. Serve the Thai Sweet and Sour Chicken hot with steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with sliced green onions or sesame seeds for an extra touch of flavor and presentation.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add some chopped red chili peppers or a dash of chili sauce to the sauce mixture.
  • Feel free to customize the stir-fry vegetables based on your preference. You can add pineapple chunks, snow peas, or baby corn for additional flavors and textures.
  • If you prefer a healthier option, you can bake the chicken instead of frying it. Place the battered chicken on a greased baking sheet and bake at 200°C (400°F) for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown and cooked through.

Enjoy this tantalizing Thai Sweet and Sour Chicken dish with its perfect blend of tangy and crispy flavors. It’s a fantastic choice for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion. Impress your family and friends with this easy-to-make, restaurant-quality dish that will transport them straight to the streets of Thailand. Happy cooking!
