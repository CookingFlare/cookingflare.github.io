How to Make Stuffed Pizza: A Delicious Recipe


Pizza is a beloved dish worldwide, but sometimes, we crave a little more than the traditional flat crust and toppings. Enter stuffed pizza—an indulgent, hearty twist on the classic, with a thick, doughy crust encasing a generous filling of cheese, meats, vegetables, and savory sauces. Making stuffed pizza at home allows you to customize it exactly to your tastes while impressing friends and family with your culinary skills.

This recipe walks you through every step of crafting your own stuffed pizza, from preparing the dough to baking the perfect golden crust. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, this stuffed pizza recipe is easy to follow and guarantees a satisfying, flavorful meal. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to create a pizza that’s as fun to make as it is to eat!

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s gather the necessary ingredients for both the dough and the filling. For stuffed pizza, the dough is thick and fluffy, so it’s important to get the texture right. The filling is entirely up to you—whether you prefer a meaty, vegetarian, or even a combination of both, you can make it as creative as you want!

For the Dough:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (about 110°F or 45°C)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornmeal (optional, for dusting)

For the Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup cooked sausage (or ground beef, turkey, or any meat of your choice)
  • 1/2 cup sliced pepperoni or any preferred deli meat
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup black olives, sliced (optional)
  • 1 cup pizza sauce (or marinara sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional for heat)

To Brush the Crust:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

The dough is the foundation of a great stuffed pizza. It needs to be soft, stretchy, and able to hold up the heavy filling.

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes. The mixture should become frothy as the yeast activates. If it doesn’t foam, the yeast may be expired, and you should try a new packet.

  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Create a well in the center to pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil.

  3. Knead the dough: Gradually mix the wet ingredients into the flour. Once the dough begins to come together, turn it out onto a floured surface. Knead the dough for about 7-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour.

  4. Let the dough rise: Once the dough is kneaded, shape it into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Allow it to rise in a warm place for 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until it doubles in size.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the dough is rising, you can prepare the fillings for your stuffed pizza. The ingredients are customizable, but this combination of cheeses, meats, and vegetables creates a deliciously balanced flavor.

  1. Cook the sausage: In a skillet over medium heat, cook the sausage (or ground meat of your choice). Use a spoon to break the meat into small pieces. Once browned and cooked through, set the meat aside to cool.

  2. Prepare the vegetables: In the same skillet, sauté the mushrooms, bell peppers, and onions until they’re softened and slightly caramelized. This usually takes about 5 minutes. Remove them from the heat and set aside.

  3. Mix the cheese: In a medium-sized bowl, combine the shredded mozzarella, ricotta, and Parmesan cheeses. Add the oregano, garlic powder, and red pepper flakes (if using) for extra flavor.

  4. Assemble the filling: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked sausage, sautéed vegetables, and cheese mixture. Stir everything together and set it aside.

Step 3: Assemble the Stuffed Pizza

Now that your dough has risen and your filling is ready, it’s time to assemble the pizza. This step is where the magic happens, as you’ll be layering everything into a crust that forms a pocket of cheesy, savory goodness.

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). You want the oven to be hot to give the crust that perfect golden, crispy finish.

  2. Prepare the baking pan: Grease a 9-inch round or 10-inch springform pan with olive oil. You can also sprinkle some cornmeal on the bottom to prevent sticking and add a nice texture to the bottom crust.

  3. Roll out the dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Transfer the dough to a floured surface and roll it out into a large circle. The circle should be about 12 inches in diameter, allowing enough dough to cover the bottom and sides of the pan, with extra to fold over the filling.

  4. Add the filling: Place the rolled-out dough into the prepared pan, pressing it gently into the bottom and up the sides. Spoon the filling into the center of the dough, spreading it out evenly.

  5. Seal the pizza: Carefully fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pinching the dough together to seal it. Be sure that the pizza is fully enclosed to prevent any filling from leaking out during baking.

  6. Top the pizza: Brush the top of the dough with olive oil and sprinkle a pinch of salt and garlic powder. This gives the crust a beautiful, flavorful finish. You can also drizzle a bit of pizza sauce over the top if desired, or simply leave it as is for a more traditional appearance.

Step 4: Bake the Stuffed Pizza

With your pizza fully assembled, it’s time to bake it.

  1. Bake the pizza: Place the stuffed pizza in the preheated oven and bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy. The cheese inside should be bubbling and the crust should be cooked through. If you notice the top is browning too quickly, cover it loosely with foil and continue baking until done.

  2. Cool before serving: Once the pizza is finished baking, remove it from the oven and let it sit for about 5 minutes. This helps the filling set and makes it easier to slice. If you cut into it too soon, the cheesy filling might spill out.

Step 5: Slice and Serve

After the pizza has cooled slightly, carefully slice it into wedges. Serve it with a side of marinara sauce for dipping, or enjoy it as is. The stuffed pizza’s crust is thick, so it’s incredibly filling, making it a perfect meal for family gatherings, parties, or just a cozy night in.

Tips for the Perfect Stuffed Pizza

  1. Don’t overload the filling: While it’s tempting to pile on a lot of ingredients, too much filling can cause the pizza to overflow or make the dough too soggy. Stick to a balanced amount of cheese, meats, and vegetables.

  2. Use fresh dough: If you’re in a rush, you can use store-bought pizza dough, but nothing beats the taste of freshly made dough. Plus, it’s surprisingly simple to make!

  3. Experiment with fillings: Stuffed pizza is versatile, so feel free to get creative with your fillings. Add different vegetables, like spinach or zucchini, or switch up the cheeses based on your preferences. Want a spicy kick? Add jalapeños or spicy sausage.

  4. Let the pizza rest: After baking, let your pizza sit for a few minutes before slicing. This allows the cheese to set and prevents a messy, gooey mess when you cut into it.

  5. Serve with a salad: A side of crisp, fresh salad can help balance out the richness of the stuffed pizza. Try a simple arugula and cherry tomato salad with balsamic vinegar.

Conclusion

Making stuffed pizza at home is an incredibly rewarding experience. The crispy, golden crust encases a rich, cheesy filling that’s sure to satisfy any pizza lover. This stuffed pizza recipe is easy to follow and adaptable to various flavor combinations, making it a great option for casual family dinners or special occasions. With a little bit of patience and a few key ingredients, you can create a mouthwatering pizza that rivals any pizzeria.

So next time you’re craving something more substantial than your usual slice of pizza, try making this stuffed pizza recipe. You’ll be amazed at how simple it is to create such a delicious, comforting dish right in your own kitchen!
