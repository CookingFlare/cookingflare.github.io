Canning Beyond Fruits and Vegetables: Unexpected Recipes


Canning is a fantastic way to preserve the flavors of your favorite fruits and vegetables. From homemade jams to pickled cucumbers, the possibilities are endless. But did you know that canning can go beyond just fruits and veggies? In this blog post, we’ll explore some unexpected recipes that you can can, taking your culinary adventures to a whole new level!

1. Canned Salsa Verde

Salsa verde is a tangy and flavorful Mexican condiment that adds a burst of freshness to any dish. By canning salsa verde, you can enjoy its vibrant flavors all year round. Gather fresh tomatillos, jalapenos, onions, garlic, and cilantro. Blend them together with lime juice and salt until smooth. Transfer the mixture to sterilized jars and process them in a water bath canner. The result? A zesty and versatile salsa that can be used as a dip, sauce, or marinade.

2. Canned Pumpkin Butter

Pumpkin butter is a delightful autumn treat that can elevate your breakfast or dessert. To make canned pumpkin butter, start by roasting fresh pumpkin until tender. Puree the roasted pumpkin with spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. Add a sweetener of your choice, such as maple syrup or brown sugar. Cook the mixture over low heat until it thickens. Finally, pour the pumpkin butter into sterilized jars, seal, and process in a water bath canner. Spread this velvety goodness on toast or swirl it into yogurt for a taste of fall, any time of the year.

3. Canned Spicy Pickled Eggs

Pickled eggs are a classic bar snack, but have you ever considered canning them? This unexpected twist on a traditional recipe allows you to enjoy pickled eggs whenever you desire. Hard-boil and peel a dozen eggs. In a saucepan, combine vinegar, water, sugar, salt, and spices like chili flakes and peppercorns. Bring the mixture to a boil and pour it over the eggs in sterilized jars. Seal the jars and process in a water bath canner. Let the eggs sit for a few days to develop their tangy flavor. Slice them and serve on salads, sandwiches, or as a unique addition to your charcuterie board.

4. Canned Chocolate Hazelnut Spread

Who doesn’t love a good chocolate hazelnut spread? Instead of buying a jar from the store, why not make your own and can it for later? Start by roasting hazelnuts until golden brown and remove their skins. Blend the roasted hazelnuts with cocoa powder, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Slowly add oil until the mixture reaches a smooth and creamy consistency. Pour the chocolate hazelnut spread into sterilized jars, seal tightly, and process in a water bath canner. Spread this decadent treat on toast, pancakes, or simply enjoy it by the spoonful.

5. Canned Herb-infused Oils

Herb-infused oils can bring a burst of flavor to your cooking creations. By canning these oils, you can have them readily available in your pantry. Choose your favorite herbs like rosemary, thyme, or basil. Heat olive oil in a saucepan and add the herbs. Let the mixture simmer for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse. Strain the oil and pour it into sterilized jars. Seal and process the jars in a water bath canner. These herb-infused oils are perfect for drizzling over salads, dipping bread, or adding a touch of aromatic goodness to your dishes.

Conclusion

Canning is not limited to fruits and vegetables alone. With a little creativity and experimentation, you can preserve a wide variety of unexpected recipes. From tangy salsas to velvety spreads and flavorful oils, canning allows you to enjoy these culinary delights long after their seasonal availability. So, grab your canning jars and embark on a journey of preserving flavors that will surprise and delight your taste buds. Happy canning!
