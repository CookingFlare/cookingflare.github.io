Asian eats: Scary sushi roll for Hallow's Evening snacking


As the leaves turn yellow and the air turns crisp, it’s safe to say that Halloween is just around the corner. Decorations are going up, costumes are being planned, and festivities are beginning. A Halloween tradition for some is indulging in spooky food, and considering the world of sushi possibilities, there are plenty of eerie options to explore. One such option is the Scary Sushi Roll, created specifically for Hallow’s evening snacking. Let’s dive into the ghastly goodness of this frightful roll.

Ingredients

To make the Scary Sushi Roll, you’ll need traditional sushi ingredients like rice, seaweed sheets, raw fish (optional), cucumber, avocado, cream cheese, and spicy mayo sauce. Additionally, you’ll need some creepy toppings like cooked shrimp (for spider legs), crab meat (for spider eyes), sliced almonds (for spider fangs), and sesame seeds (for mouth texture). You also have the option of adding some black food coloring to some of the ingredients for an extra spooky twist.

Instructions

First, let’s start by preparing the sushi rice. Cook the rice in a rice cooker or on the stove and mix with seasoned rice vinegar. Spread the rice evenly on a large tray and let it cool. While the rice is cooling, prepare the “spiders” for the roll.

  • Spider Legs (cooked shrimp)
    • Uncooked deveined shrimp with tails attached for authenticity, you can also use cooked shrimp or shrimp paste.
    • For added spookiness, paint a mix of sesame seeds and black food coloring onto the shrimp to create a more convincing spider leg.
    • In a saucepan, lightly cook the spiced shrimp in oil until they turn pink. Let them cool down before assembling your sushi roll.
  • Spider Eyes (crab meat)
    • Mix some black food coloring into the crab meat to give it an eerie appearance.
    • Using a fine tube or small round cutter, create small crab eye balls.
  • Spider Fangs (sliced almonds)
    • Blanched almonds are perfect as spider fangs to add a wicked bite to the sushi roll.
    • Slice the almonds lengthwise using a sharp knife to create an intimidating fang.
  • Mouth Texture (sesame seeds)
    • Mix with a little black food coloring to form scary-looking mouth textures.

Next, it’s time to assemble our frightful creation:

  1. Place a sheet of seaweed on a bamboo sushi mat and coat it with sushi rice evenly, leaving 1 inch of seaweed uncovered at the top.
  2. Place a thin layer of cream cheese at the bottom of the seaweed to create a fang base.
  3. Stack your ingredients in a line: cucumber, avocado, and raw fish (if desired).
  4. Arrange the “spider legs” at the bottom of the avocado piece, reaching out towards the bottom edge of the roll to create a scary look.
  5. Place the “spider eyes” near the top of the avocado piece, and “spider fangs” on top.
  6. With the bamboo sushi mat, start rolling the sushi away from you while gently pressing but not too tight, so the ingredients don’tsqueeze out.
  7. Once the roll is complete, use moistened fingers to shape the seaweed at the top into a rounded or even creepy “bug-like” shape.
  8. For added spookiness, sprinkle some black sesame seed “mouth texture” on the top edge of the sushi roll.

Using a sharp, wet knife, slice the sushi roll into bite-sized pieces, and repeat the steps until all of the roll is used.

Serve the Spooky Sushi Roll with spicy mayo sauce on the side for dipping. Enjoy your Hallow’s evening treat without putting a scare in your diet.

Conclusion

Embrace the spirit of Halloween by crafting these spooooky sushi rolls! It’s a playful twist on a beloved Japanese dish that’s sure to delight Even your little ghost or goblin. These scary sushi rolls will make the perfect addition to your Halloween feast, and let’s face it, who said spooky-food meant unhealthy? Happy Sushi Rolling!
