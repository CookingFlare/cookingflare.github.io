Spring Pasta: Recipes for Fresh and Vibrant Sauces


Spring is a time of renewal, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a plate of fresh and vibrant pasta? With the arrival of warmer weather, our palates crave lighter, brighter flavors that showcase the bounty of spring produce. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of spring pasta recipes that feature delicious sauces bursting with flavor. From creamy pesto to zesty lemon, these recipes will awaken your taste buds and leave you craving more.

1. Creamy Pesto Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound pasta of your choice

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the basil leaves, pine nuts, garlic, and Parmesan cheese. Pulse until coarsely chopped.
  2. Gradually add the olive oil while continuing to pulse until the mixture reaches a smooth and creamy consistency.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and reserve a cup of the cooking water.
  5. In a large skillet, heat the pesto sauce over medium heat. Add the cooked pasta and toss to coat, adding some of the reserved cooking water to loosen the sauce if needed.
  6. Serve immediately and garnish with additional Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves.

2. Lemon Garlic Shrimp Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound pasta of your choice
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Add the shrimp to the bowl and toss to coat in the marinade. Let it sit for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  3. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and reserve a cup of the cooking water.
  4. In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the marinated shrimp and cook until pink and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss to coat, adding some of the reserved cooking water to loosen the sauce if needed.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh parsley.

3. Pea and Mint Pesto Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pound pasta of your choice

Instructions:

  1. If using frozen peas, blanch them in boiling water for a few minutes until tender. If using fresh peas, steam them until tender.
  2. In a food processor, combine the peas, mint leaves, Parmesan cheese, pine nuts, garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Pulse until smooth and well combined.
  3. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and reserve a cup of the cooking water.
  4. In a large skillet, heat the pea and mint pesto sauce over medium heat. Add the cooked pasta and toss to coat, adding some of the reserved cooking water to loosen the sauce if needed.
  5. Serve immediately and garnish with additional Parmesan cheese and fresh mint leaves.

Conclusion

Spring is the perfect time to experiment with fresh and vibrant pasta sauces. Whether you prefer the classic flavors of basil and pine nuts in a creamy pesto, the zesty tang of lemon and garlic with shrimp, or the unique combination of sweet peas and refreshing mint, these recipes will bring a burst of flavor to your spring table. Get creative, embrace the season’s produce, and enjoy the delightful flavors of spring with these delicious pasta dishes.
