Delicious Pumpkin Recipes to Celebrate Fall


Fall is here, and with it comes cooler weather, vibrant foliage, and the warm, comforting flavors that define the season. One of the undisputed stars of fall cooking is pumpkin. From sweet treats to savory dishes, this versatile ingredient can play a starring role in countless recipes. Whether you’re looking for hearty dinner options, satisfying snacks, or indulgent desserts, pumpkin is the perfect canvas for your culinary creativity. Let’s dive into some mouthwatering pumpkin recipes that will elevate your fall celebrations and make the most of this seasonal favorite.

Pumpkin Soup with Roasted Garlic and Thyme

A warm bowl of pumpkin soup is an autumn classic, and adding roasted garlic and thyme gives it a flavorful twist. This creamy dish is both comforting and elegant, making it ideal for a chilly evening.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh pumpkin puree (or canned pumpkin puree)
  • 1 head of garlic, roasted
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or coconut milk for a dairy-free option)
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Slice off the top of the garlic head to expose the cloves, drizzle with olive oil, wrap in foil, and roast for 35 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté the diced onion until translucent.
  3. Add the pumpkin puree and broth to the pot and stir well. Squeeze the roasted garlic into the mixture and discard the skins.
  4. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce heat to simmer. Stir in cream and thyme leaves, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Let it simmer for about 15 minutes, then use an immersion blender to puree it to a smooth consistency.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh thyme or a drizzle of cream.

Pumpkin Risotto with Parmesan and Sage

Pumpkin risotto is a luxurious dish that’s packed with autumnal flavor. The creaminess of the rice pairs beautifully with the earthiness of sage and the nuttiness of Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 1 cup fresh pumpkin puree
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1/3 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable stock, warmed
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 5 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft.
  2. Add the arborio rice and stir for 1-2 minutes to lightly toast the grains.
  3. Pour in the white wine and cook until it evaporates.
  4. Gradually add the warm vegetable stock, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing the rice to absorb the liquid before adding more.
  5. After 20 minutes of cooking, stir in the pumpkin puree and continue to cook until the risotto is creamy and the rice is tender.
  6. Add the Parmesan cheese and stir well. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Garnish with fresh sage leaves and serve immediately.

Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Start your morning off right with these fluffy pumpkin spice pancakes. Brimming with cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger, they’re like autumn on a plate.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 3/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/3 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, combine the buttermilk, pumpkin puree, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  4. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot surface for each pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown.
  6. Serve warm with a drizzle of maple syrup.

Savory Pumpkin Hummus

This creamy and flavorful pumpkin hummus is an excellent fall appetizer or snack. Pair it with pita bread, crackers, or fresh veggies for a party-perfect platter.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup canned chickpeas (or cooked chickpeas)
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, pumpkin puree, olive oil, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, cumin, and smoked paprika.
  2. Blend until smooth, stopping occasionally to scrape down the sides.
  3. Add salt and pepper to taste, and adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl and drizzle with olive oil, paprika, or pumpkin seeds for garnish.
  5. Serve with dippers of your choice.

Pumpkin Bread with a Cinnamon Streusel Topping

Pumpkin bread is a quintessential fall favorite, but adding a cinnamon-sugar streusel topping elevates this classic to irresistible levels.

Ingredients:

For the Bread:

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup milk

For the Streusel Topping:

  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 3 tbsp butter, melted

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, sugars, spices, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine the eggs, pumpkin puree, oil, and milk. Pour the wet mixture into the dry mixture, stirring until just combined.
  4. Make the topping by combining flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and melted butter. Mix until crumbly.
  5. Pour the batter into the greased loaf pan and sprinkle the streusel evenly on top.
  6. Bake for 55–60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Slice and enjoy!

These recipes will infuse your kitchen with the nostalgic smells and flavors of autumn, while dazzling your taste buds and those of your family and friends. Pumpkin truly shines in both savory and sweet dishes, making it one of the most versatile ingredients to experiment with this season. So grab a pumpkin and start cooking—you’ll be amazed by what you can create!
