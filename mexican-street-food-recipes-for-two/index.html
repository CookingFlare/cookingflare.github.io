Mexican Street Food: Recipes for Two


Mexican street food is known for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and mouthwatering aromas. From tacos to quesadillas, the street food scene in Mexico is a culinary experience like no other. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and easy-to-make Mexican street food recipes that are perfect for two people. So grab your apron and get ready to embark on a flavorful journey!

1. Tacos al Pastor

Tacos al Pastor is a classic Mexican street food dish that combines marinated pork with pineapple, creating a symphony of flavors. Here’s how you can make it:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons of achiote paste
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Sliced pineapple
  • Chopped fresh cilantro
  • Diced onions
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the pineapple juice, achiote paste, minced garlic, dried oregano, ground cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the thinly sliced pork shoulder to the marinade and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the marinated pork until it is cooked through and slightly crispy.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas on a separate skillet until they are pliable.
  5. Assemble your tacos by placing a few slices of the cooked pork on each tortilla.
  6. Top with sliced pineapple, fresh cilantro, diced onions, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  7. Serve and enjoy!

2. Quesadillas de Huitlacoche

Quesadillas de Huitlacoche are a unique and delicious Mexican street food specialty made with a filling of huitlacoche, a type of corn fungus. Don’t let the name scare you away, as huitlacoche adds an earthy and savory flavor to these quesadillas.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of huitlacoche
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 1/4 cup of diced onion
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheese (preferably Oaxaca or Monterey Jack)
  • Corn tortillas
  • Sliced avocado
  • Mexican crema

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion and jalapeño to the skillet and cook until they are softened.
  3. Add the huitlacoche to the skillet and cook for about 5 minutes, until heated through.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Warm the corn tortillas on a separate skillet until they are pliable.
  6. Spread a layer of shredded cheese on one half of each tortilla.
  7. Spoon the huitlacoche mixture on top of the cheese.
  8. Fold the tortilla in half to enclose the filling.
  9. Cook the quesadillas on a skillet until the cheese is melted and the tortillas are crispy.
  10. Serve the quesadillas with sliced avocado and a drizzle of Mexican crema.

3. Elote (Mexican Street Corn)

No Mexican street food experience is complete without elote, or Mexican street corn. This deliciously messy treat is a must-try, and it’s perfect for two people.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ears of corn, husked
  • Mayonnaise
  • Crumbled cotija cheese
  • Chili powder
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Grill the corn, turning occasionally, until it’s slightly charred and cooked through.
  3. Spread a thin layer of mayonnaise all over the grilled corn.
  4. Sprinkle crumbled cotija cheese generously over the mayo.
  5. Dust the corn with chili powder to add a kick of heat.
  6. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the elote for a burst of citrus flavor.
  7. Enjoy your messy and delicious elote!

Final Thoughts

These Mexican street food recipes for two are a fantastic way to bring the vibrant flavors of Mexico into your home. Whether you’re craving the savory goodness of tacos al pastor, the unique taste of quesadillas de huitlacoche, or the messy delight of elote, these recipes are sure to satisfy your taste buds. So gather your ingredients, get cooking, and enjoy the fiesta of flavors that Mexican street food has to offer!
