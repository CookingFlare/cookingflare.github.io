Taste of the Orient: 6 Delicious Recipes from Malaysia


Malaysia, a country renowned for its rich cultural diversity, is also home to an incredible array of mouthwatering dishes. The nation’s cuisine is a harmonious fusion of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous influences, offering a truly unique experience for food lovers. From vibrant street food to hearty family meals, the flavors of Malaysia will take you on an unforgettable culinary journey. If you’ve ever dreamed of bringing the taste of the Orient to your kitchen, then look no further. We’re about to explore six classic Malaysian recipes that capture the essence of this beautiful country.

1. Nasi Lemak – The National Dish of Malaysia

Often considered the national dish of Malaysia, Nasi Lemak is a beloved breakfast favorite, though it can be enjoyed any time of the day. The name “Nasi Lemak” translates to “rich rice,” referring to the creamy, fragrant coconut milk that infuses the rice. This dish is a celebration of flavors and textures, with each ingredient playing a pivotal role.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 pandan leaf (optional, but recommended for an authentic taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • ½ cup roasted peanuts
  • ½ cup sambal (spicy chili paste)
  • 2 to 3 cucumber slices
  • 1 tablespoon oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice: Rinse the jasmine rice thoroughly under cold water to remove excess starch. Combine the rice with coconut milk, water, salt, and the pandan leaf (if using) in a rice cooker. Cook the rice as you normally would, ensuring it absorbs the fragrant coconut milk.

  2. Prepare the Sambal: In a small pan, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add the sambal and stir-fry for a few minutes until fragrant. You can adjust the level of spice based on your preference.

  3. Prepare the Garnishes: While the rice is cooking, fry the peanuts and slice the cucumber and boiled eggs.

  4. Assemble the Dish: Once the rice is ready, plate it and top with the sambal, fried peanuts, cucumber slices, and a halved boiled egg.

Nasi Lemak is a perfectly balanced dish with the richness of the coconut rice, the heat of the sambal, and the crunch of the peanuts and cucumber. It’s the ultimate comfort food!

2. Hainanese Chicken Rice – A Malaysian Favorite with Chinese Roots

Hainanese Chicken Rice is a staple in Malaysian cuisine, especially popular in both street food stalls and restaurants. The dish consists of tender chicken served with fragrant rice, accompanied by flavorful dipping sauces. Originating from Hainan, China, it has been embraced and adapted by Malaysians, making it one of the most iconic dishes in the country.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1.5 kg)
  • 5 cups jasmine rice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • Salt to taste

For the Dipping Sauces:

  • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger paste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chicken: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the whole chicken and cook it for about 40 minutes or until the chicken is fully cooked. Remove the chicken, and once cooled, rub it with a little salt. Chop the chicken into bite-sized pieces.

  2. Cook the Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly. In a separate pot, heat the vegetable oil, and sauté the minced garlic and ginger until fragrant. Add the rinsed rice and stir-fry for 2 minutes. Then, add 5 cups of water and cook the rice as you would in a rice cooker. For extra flavor, you can cook the rice in the chicken broth from boiling the chicken.

  3. Prepare the Sauces: For the dipping sauce, mix soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, garlic, chili, and ginger paste in a small bowl. Stir well and set aside.

  4. Assemble the Dish: Serve the cooked rice alongside the chicken, garnished with sliced cucumber. Drizzle the sesame oil over the chicken and rice for extra flavor. Serve with the dipping sauce.

Hainanese Chicken Rice is comfort food at its finest—delicate, fragrant, and bursting with flavors. Perfect for any occasion!

3. Char Kway Teow – Stir-fried Noodles with a Malaysian Twist

Char Kway Teow is a popular stir-fried noodle dish that originated in the Chinese community of Malaysia. With its smoky aroma and vibrant flavors, this dish is a favorite for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. It’s traditionally made with flat rice noodles, shrimp, Chinese sausage, and eggs, all cooked together in a wok for that perfect “wok hei” (charred aroma).

Ingredients:

  • 400g flat rice noodles (Kway Teow)
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 Chinese sausages, thinly sliced
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce (for color)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • Chili flakes to taste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles: If using fresh Kway Teow, loosen them by soaking in warm water for about 10 minutes. Drain well. If using dried noodles, follow the package instructions to cook them, then drain and set aside.

  2. Stir-fry the Ingredients: Heat oil in a large wok over high heat. Add the garlic and stir-fry until fragrant. Then, add the shrimp and Chinese sausage, cooking until the shrimp turns pink.

  3. Cook the Noodles: Push the shrimp and sausage to the side of the wok and crack the eggs into the wok. Scramble the eggs, then toss in the noodles. Stir everything together, making sure the noodles absorb the flavors.

  4. Add the Seasonings: Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and dark soy sauce. Stir-fry the mixture for 2-3 minutes until the noodles are evenly coated with the sauces.

  5. Finish the Dish: Add the bean sprouts and spring onions. Stir-fry for another minute, ensuring everything is well-mixed. If you like it spicy, sprinkle with chili flakes to taste.

Char Kway Teow is all about that delicious smoky flavor and the interplay of textures—from the tender shrimp to the crispy bean sprouts. It’s the ultimate comfort food that’s quick, easy, and so flavorful.

4. Satay – Grilled Skewers with Peanut Sauce

Satay is one of Malaysia’s most famous street foods. Tender pieces of marinated meat (often chicken or beef) are skewered and grilled to perfection, then served with a rich and flavorful peanut dipping sauce. This dish is perfect for gatherings or a fun weeknight meal.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast or beef, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

For the Peanut Sauce:

  • 1 cup peanut butter
  • ½ cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1-2 teaspoons chili paste (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Meat: In a bowl, combine the vegetable oil, soy sauce, brown sugar, ground coriander, turmeric powder, garlic, and lemon juice. Add the chicken or beef pieces and toss to coat evenly. Cover and marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours.

  2. Prepare the Peanut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the peanut butter, coconut milk, soy sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, and chili paste. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sauce becomes smooth and slightly thickened. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Grill the Satay: Preheat a grill or griddle pan over medium-high heat. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers, then grill for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until cooked through and slightly charred.

  4. Serve: Serve the satay with the peanut dipping sauce on the side. For extra flavor, garnish with chopped cucumber and onions.

Satay is a delightful treat that’s full of smoky, savory, and sweet flavors. Whether you serve it as an appetizer or a main dish, it’s always a crowd-pleaser.

5. Laksa – A Spicy Noodle Soup

Laksa is a classic Malaysian dish that combines a rich, spicy broth with noodles, vegetables, and protein. The two most popular types of Laksa in Malaysia are Asam Laksa (with a tangy tamarind-based broth) and Curry Laksa (with a coconut milk-based broth). In this recipe, we’ll focus on the creamy, spicy, and aromatic Curry Laksa.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles or egg noodles
  • 300g chicken breast, cooked and shredded
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 boiled eggs, halved
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • 4 cups coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 2 tablespoons sambal oelek (or chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Salt to taste

For the Laksa Paste:

  • 3 dried red chilies, soaked
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger
  • 1 lemongrass stalk, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Laksa Paste: Blend all the laksa paste ingredients in a food processor or mortar and pestle until smooth. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the Soup: In a large pot, heat a little oil and sauté the laksa paste until fragrant. Add the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Add curry powder, sambal oelek, soy sauce, and salt. Stir well and let the broth simmer for about 10 minutes.

  3. Assemble the Noodles: Cook the rice or egg noodles according to package instructions. Divide the cooked noodles into bowls.

  4. Add the Toppings: To each bowl of noodles, add shredded chicken, shrimp, bean sprouts, boiled egg halves, and cucumber.

  5. Pour the Broth: Ladle the hot, fragrant broth over the noodles and toppings.

  6. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle with chopped spring onions and serve with extra sambal for added spice.

Laksa is a comforting, hearty dish that brings together the warmth of the coconut milk and the heat of the chili paste. It’s the perfect dish for a cozy evening.

6. Roti Canai – The Malaysian Flatbread

Roti Canai is a type of flatbread that’s crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and perfect for dipping in dhal (lentil curry) or other side dishes. It’s often eaten for breakfast or as a snack but can also be served as part of a larger meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • ¾ cup water
  • Ghee or oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Gradually add water and knead until a smooth dough forms. Divide the dough into small balls and coat them with a little ghee. Allow the dough to rest for about 30 minutes.

  2. Roll and Stretch the Dough: Take one dough ball and flatten it out with your hands. Stretch the dough as thin as possible, almost to the point of transparency. Fold it into a square shape and set aside.

  3. Cook the Roti Canai: Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add a little ghee or oil, then place the dough squares on the pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden and crispy.

  4. Serve: Roti Canai is best served hot with dhal curry, sugar, or even a side of sambal.

Conclusion

Exploring Malaysian cuisine is like taking a journey through the country’s rich history and diverse cultures. From the iconic Nasi Lemak to the deliciously smoky Char Kway Teow, every dish offers a unique experience of flavors and textures. These six recipes represent the very best of what Malaysian food has to offer, bringing together spices, herbs, and fresh ingredients in exciting and satisfying ways. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, these dishes will transport you straight to the heart of Malaysia, allowing you to savor the taste of the Orient in the comfort of your own home.
