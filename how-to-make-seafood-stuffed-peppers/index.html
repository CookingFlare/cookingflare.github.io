How to Make Seafood Stuffed Peppers


Seafood stuffed peppers are a wonderful dish that marries the vibrant flavors of the sea with the hearty sweetness of bell peppers. Perfect for impressing guests or treating yourself to a special meal, this dish presents an eye-catching, nutrient-rich option that’s as satisfying as it is stunning. Today, we’ll take a deep dive into crafting seafood stuffed peppers that combine fresh ingredients, aromatic herbs, and a variety of seafood for a dish bursting with flavor.

Why Choose Seafood Stuffed Peppers?

Bell peppers, with their natural sweetness and crisp texture, serve as an ideal vessel for seafood fillings. Combining shrimp, crab, scallops, or even fish with rice, herbs, and seasonings creates a perfect balance. The peppers not only encapsulate the filling beautifully but also become tender and fragrant after baking, enhancing every bite.

Seafood is naturally rich in omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins, and minerals, making this dish a nutritious choice. Plus, it offers versatility in terms of ingredient substitutions, accommodating everything from budget-friendly canned crab to freshly caught scallops.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The core of great seafood stuffed peppers lies in choosing fresh, quality ingredients that complement one another. Here’s a comprehensive list to guide you:

For the Seafood Filling:

  • 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled, deveined, and chopped
  • 1/2 pound lump crab meat (fresh or canned, drained)
  • 1/2 pound sea scallops, finely chopped (optional for extra luxury)
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cooked white rice (or quinoa for a healthier alternative)
  • 1/2 cup celery, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup red bell pepper, finely chopped (adds extra crunch)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and black pepper to season

For the Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers (red, yellow, orange, or green for color variety)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella or Monterey Jack cheese (optional for topping)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Step-by-Step Preparation

1. Preparing the Peppers

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). While the oven warms, wash the bell peppers thoroughly. Slice off the tops and carefully remove the seeds and membranes. Set the hollowed-out peppers aside. To soften them slightly before stuffing, you can parboil them in boiling water for 3 minutes, then drain and place them upside down on a paper towel. Alternatively, brushing the insides with olive oil and baking them empty for 10 minutes softens the flesh for a more tender finish.

2. Preparing the Seafood Filling

In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, and red bell pepper, sautéing until they’re tender but still vibrant, about 4 to 5 minutes. Toss in the garlic and cook for an additional 30 seconds until fragrant.

Next, stir in the chopped shrimp and scallops. Cook just until the seafood is opaque—roughly 2 to 3 minutes—to avoid overcooking. Remove the skillet from heat and let the mixture cool slightly.

3. Combining the Ingredients

Transfer the seafood and vegetable mix to a large bowl. Add the cooked rice, crab meat, parsley, lemon juice, smoked paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Fold everything gently until fully incorporated. Taste your filling and adjust seasoning if necessary, adding a sprinkle of cayenne or a dash more lemon juice for brightness if desired.

4. Stuffing the Peppers

Carefully fill each bell pepper cavity with the seafood mixture, packing it to the brim without overflowing. Arrange the stuffed peppers upright in a baking dish. If you have leftover filling, you can bake it separately in a small casserole dish.

5. Adding Cheese (Optional)

If you enjoy a melty, cheesy top, sprinkle the shredded mozzarella or Monterey Jack cheese generously over each stuffed pepper before baking. This adds a golden, bubbly crust that pairs beautifully with the tender seafood.

6. Baking

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 to 15 minutes or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is golden brown. The baking time may vary depending on your oven and the pepper size, so keep an eye on them to avoid drying out the filling.

Serving Suggestions

Seafood stuffed peppers make for a fantastic main course. Serve them warm, garnished with extra fresh parsley or a wedge of lemon for squeezing at the table. Accompany the dish with a crisp green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette or roasted seasonal vegetables to complete your meal.

For a finer dining experience, pair the stuffed peppers with a chilled white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay, which complements seafood’s delicate flavors beautifully.

Tips for Perfect Seafood Stuffed Peppers Every Time

  • Freshness matters: Using fresh seafood significantly elevates the dish, but quality canned crab meat can be a budget-friendly and tasty substitute.
  • Control the heat: The cayenne adds a nice subtle kick but can be adjusted or omitted for a milder flavor.
  • Rice alternatives: Quinoa or couscous work well in place of white rice, adding different textures and nutritional benefits.
  • Make-ahead friendly: Prepare the filling ahead of time and refrigerate. Stuff and bake the peppers on the day you plan to serve them.
  • Avoid overcooking: Overdone seafood turns rubbery. Keep cooking time short once the filling hits the heat to maintain tenderness.

Variations to Explore

  • Creamy seafood stuffed peppers: Mix in a spoonful of sour cream or cream cheese into the filling for added richness.
  • Spicy stuffed peppers: Elevate the heat by adding diced jalapeños or hot sauce to the filling.
  • Mediterranean twist: Incorporate olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and feta cheese into the mix for a Mediterranean flair.
  • Low-carb option: Skip the rice and bulk up the filling with extra veggies, chopped nuts, or cauliflower rice.

Conclusion

Seafood stuffed peppers are a remarkably versatile and delightful dish that offers both visual appeal and mouthwatering flavor. Whether you’re looking to impress at a dinner party or enjoy a nourishing solo meal, this recipe offers a perfect combination of freshness, texture, and taste.

By following these steps, you’ll create a sumptuous dish that highlights the best of land and sea in one colorful package. Dive into this culinary adventure and enjoy every bite of your perfectly crafted seafood stuffed peppers!
