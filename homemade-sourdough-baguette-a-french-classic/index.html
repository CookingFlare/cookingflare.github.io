Homemade Sourdough Baguette: A French Classic


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through your kitchen. And when it comes to classic French bread, the sourdough baguette takes the cake. With its crispy crust and light, airy interior, this iconic bread is a staple in French cuisine. But why settle for store-bought when you can make your own homemade sourdough baguette? In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating this French classic right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 500g bread flour
  • 350g water
  • 100g sourdough starter
  • 10g salt

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Before you begin, make sure your sourdough starter is active and bubbly. If not, feed it a few hours before starting the recipe to ensure its vitality.

  2. Mixing the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, water, and sourdough starter. Mix until the ingredients are well incorporated and a shaggy dough forms. Cover the bowl with a kitchen towel and let it rest for 30 minutes. This resting period allows the flour to hydrate and the gluten to develop.

  3. Autolyse: After the resting period, add the salt to the dough. Use your hands or a dough scraper to incorporate the salt evenly. Then, perform an autolyse by letting the dough rest for another 30 minutes. This step improves the dough’s extensibility and overall texture.

  4. Stretch and Fold: To strengthen the gluten, perform a series of stretch and folds. Wet your hands and gently grab one side of the dough, stretching it up and folding it over the rest of the dough. Rotate the bowl and repeat this process for a total of 4 folds. Cover the bowl and let it rest for another 30 minutes.

  5. Bulk Fermentation: Place the dough in a warm spot, ideally around 25°C (77°F), and let it ferment for 3 to 4 hours. During this time, the dough will rise and develop its characteristic sourdough flavor. Perform a set of stretch and folds every hour to further strengthen the dough’s structure.

  6. Shaping: Once the dough has completed its bulk fermentation, lightly flour your work surface. Gently transfer the dough onto the floured surface, being careful not to deflate it too much. Divide the dough into three equal portions and shape each into a baguette. To shape a baguette, take one portion of dough and flatten it slightly. Fold the top third of the dough over itself, then fold the bottom third up to meet the top fold. Roll the dough away from you, starting from the top, to create a cylinder shape. Repeat this process for the remaining portions.

  7. Final Proofing: Place the shaped baguettes onto a well-floured couche or a baguette pan, seam side up. Cover them with a kitchen towel and let them proof at room temperature for 1 to 2 hours, or until they have increased in size by about 50%.

  8. Baking: Preheat your oven to 230°C (450°F) and place a baking stone or an inverted baking sheet inside to preheat as well. Carefully transfer the proofed baguettes onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, leaving enough space between each loaf. Score the tops of the baguettes with a sharp knife or a razor blade to allow for proper expansion. Place the baking sheet with the baguettes on the preheated baking stone or baking sheet and quickly spritz the oven with water to create steam. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the baguettes are golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  9. Cooling: Once the baguettes are baked, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Although it might be tempting to cut into them right away, allowing them to cool ensures the interior finishes cooking and the crust becomes crisp.

Congratulations! You’ve just made your own homemade sourdough baguettes. Now it’s time to enjoy the fruits of your labor. Serve them warm with some butter or use them as the base for your favorite sandwiches. The possibilities are endless, and your kitchen will forever have that unmistakable aroma of fresh-baked bread. Bon appétit!
