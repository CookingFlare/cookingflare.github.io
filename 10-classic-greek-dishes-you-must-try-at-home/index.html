10 Classic Greek Dishes You Must Try at Home


Greek cuisine is a delightful tapestry of flavors, aromas, and traditions that reflect the rich history and culture of Greece. With its emphasis on fresh ingredients, herbs, and olive oil, Greek dishes are not only delicious but also healthy. In this post, we’ll explore ten classic Greek dishes that you can easily recreate at home, each offering a unique taste of this Mediterranean paradise.

1. Moussaka: A Layered Delight

Moussaka is perhaps one of the most iconic Greek dishes. This baked casserole features layers of eggplant, ground meat (typically lamb or beef), and a creamy béchamel sauce. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced
  • Salt
  • 500g ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g canned tomatoes, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon oregano
  • Olive oil
  • 3 cups milk
  • 4 tablespoons flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 eggs
  • Grated cheese (Parmesan or Kefalotyri)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Eggplants: Sprinkle salt on the eggplant slices and let them sit for about 30 minutes to draw out bitterness. Rinse and pat dry.
  2. Cook the Meat: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant. Add ground meat, cooking until browned. Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, cinnamon, and oregano. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Make the Béchamel Sauce: In a saucepan, melt butter and add flour, whisking to form a roux. Gradually add milk while whisking to avoid lumps. Cook until thickened, then remove from heat and stir in beaten eggs and cheese.
  4. Assemble the Moussaka: In a greased baking dish, layer eggplant, meat mixture, and béchamel sauce. Repeat layers and top with remaining béchamel and cheese.
  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) and bake for 45 minutes or until golden brown. Let it cool for 30 minutes before serving.

2. Souvlaki: The Ultimate Street Food

Souvlaki, skewered and grilled meat, is a beloved Greek street food that is simple yet flavorful. Traditionally made with pork, chicken, or lamb, it’s perfect for a quick meal or backyard barbecue.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork or chicken, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper
  • Pita bread (for serving)
  • Tzatziki sauce (for dipping)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Meat: In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add meat cubes and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Skewer the Meat: Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  3. Grill: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until cooked through.
  4. Serve: Serve with warm pita and tzatziki sauce for dipping.

3. Spanakopita: A Flaky Spinach Pie

Spanakopita is a savory pie filled with spinach and feta cheese, wrapped in layers of crispy phyllo pastry. This dish is perfect as an appetizer or a light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fresh spinach, chopped
  • 200g feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon dill
  • 1 pack of phyllo pastry
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a skillet, sauté the onion in olive oil until translucent. Add spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool. Stir in feta, eggs, dill, salt, and pepper.
  2. Assemble the Spanakopita: Brush a baking dish with olive oil. Layer 4 sheets of phyllo, brushing each with oil. Add the spinach filling and cover with more phyllo, brushing each layer with oil.
  3. Bake: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) and bake for about 30-40 minutes, or until golden and crispy. Cut into squares and serve warm.

4. Greek Salad: A Fresh Classic

A Greek salad is a vibrant mix of vegetables, olives, and feta cheese, dressed simply with olive oil and lemon. It’s a staple in Greek cuisine and perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 200g feta cheese, cubed
  • A handful of Kalamata olives
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Oregano
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Combine Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, and onion.
  2. Add Cheese and Olives: Toss in the feta cheese and olives.
  3. Dress the Salad: Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice. Sprinkle with oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to combine and serve immediately.

5. Dolmades: Stuffed Grape Leaves

Dolmades are grape leaves stuffed with a mixture of rice, herbs, and sometimes meat. They are a popular appetizer in Greek cuisine and are often served with a side of yogurt or lemon.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar of grape leaves (about 200g)
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: In a skillet, sauté the onion in olive oil until soft. Add rice and cook for 2 minutes. Stir in parsley, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat and let cool.
  2. Roll the Dolmades: Rinse grape leaves and lay them flat. Place a tablespoon of filling on each leaf, fold the sides over, and roll tightly.
  3. Cook the Dolmades: Place the rolled dolmades in a pot, cover with water, and simmer for about 30 minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature.

6. Tzatziki: The Perfect Dip

Tzatziki is a creamy yogurt-based dip that is essential in Greek cuisine. Made with cucumber, garlic, and dill, it pairs beautifully with grilled meats and bread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 cucumber, grated and drained
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • Fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cucumber: Grate the cucumber and squeeze out excess moisture.
  2. Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, combine Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, garlic, olive oil, vinegar, dill, salt, and pepper.
  3. Chill and Serve: Let the tzatziki chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes before serving with pita or as a dip for vegetables.

7. Fasolada: A Hearty Bean Soup

Fasolada is a traditional Greek bean soup, often referred to as the national dish of Greece. It’s a hearty and nutritious meal, perfect for colder months.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup white beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Beans: Drain and rinse the soaked beans. In a pot, cover them with water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until tender, about 1 hour.
  2. Sauté Vegetables: In a separate pan, heat olive oil and sauté onion, carrots, and celery until soft. Add tomatoes and tomato paste, cooking for another 5 minutes.
  3. Combine and Simmer: Add the sautéed vegetables to the beans, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for another 30 minutes.
  4. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and a drizzle of olive oil before serving.

8. Karpatka: A Sweet Pastry

Karpatka is a traditional Greek dessert made from layers of pastry filled with custard. It’s a delightful treat that’s perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 cup water
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Make the Pastry: In a saucepan, bring water and butter to a boil. Add flour and stir until a dough forms. Remove from heat and let cool slightly. Beat in eggs one at a time until smooth.
  2. Bake the Layers: Divide the dough into two parts and spread each on a baking sheet.
