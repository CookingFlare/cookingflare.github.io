Savory Spinach and Feta Tart: A Flavorful Twist


As the seasons change and the weather cools, our taste buds begin to crave heartier, more comforting dishes. One such classic comfort food is the savory tart, a dish that combines the richness of buttery pastry with the savory flavors of cheese, herbs, and vegetables. In this recipe, we’ll take the traditional tart to new heights by incorporating the bold flavors of spinach and feta cheese.

The Pastry

To begin, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter, and 1/4 cup of ice-cold water. Use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the gluten to relax.

The Filling

While the pastry is chilling, let’s prepare the filling. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 small onion, finely chopped, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for an additional minute. Then, add 1 package of fresh spinach leaves, chopped, and cook until wilted, about 3-4 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.

Assembly and Baking

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry to a thickness of about 1/8 inch (3 mm). Transfer the pastry to a 9-inch (23 cm) tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges and press the pastry into the corners of the pan. Fill the pastry with the spinach and onion mixture, leaving a 1-inch (2.5 cm) border around the edges.

Crumble 1/2 cup of feta cheese over the filling, followed by a sprinkle of salt and pepper. Fold the edges of the pastry over the filling, pressing gently to seal. Brush the pastry with a beaten egg and sprinkle with a pinch of paprika.

Bake the tart for 35-40 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the filling is hot and bubbly. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • To add an extra layer of flavor, sprinkle a handful of chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley or thyme, over the filling before baking.
  • For a more indulgent twist, add 1/4 cup of crumbled cooked bacon or pancetta to the filling.
  • Experiment with different types of cheese, such as goat cheese or ricotta, for a unique flavor profile.

Conclusion

The savory spinach and feta tart is a perfect dish for a chilly evening, combining the comforting flavors of pastry, cheese, and vegetables with the boldness of spinach and feta. Whether served as a main course or as a side dish, this tart is sure to please even the most discerning palates. So go ahead, give it a try, and discover a flavorful twist on a classic comfort food.
