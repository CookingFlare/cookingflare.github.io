Vegan Banana Bread: Moist and Delicious


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked banana bread wafting through the kitchen. And when that banana bread is vegan, moist, and delicious, it’s even better! Whether you follow a vegan lifestyle or simply want to try a plant-based twist on a classic recipe, this Vegan Banana Bread recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with coconut oil or line it with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed bananas, applesauce, melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking soda, salt, ground cinnamon, and nutmeg (if using).

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense and tough banana bread.

  5. If desired, fold in the chopped walnuts for an added crunch and nutty flavor.

  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it evenly.

  7. Bake for approximately 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The baking time may vary, so keep an eye on your banana bread as it bakes.

  8. Once baked, remove the banana bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Tips and Variations

  • For a gluten-free version, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend. Ensure that the blend contains xanthan gum or another binding agent to maintain the bread’s structure.

  • If you prefer a sweeter banana bread, you can increase the amount of coconut sugar or add a tablespoon or two of pure maple syrup.

  • Feel free to get creative with mix-ins! Try adding vegan chocolate chips, dried fruit, or even a swirl of peanut butter for a decadent twist.

  • To keep your banana bread moist, store it in an airtight container at room temperature. It will stay fresh for up to 3-4 days. You can also freeze individual slices for longer storage.

Final Thoughts

This Vegan Banana Bread recipe is a delightful treat that proves you don’t need eggs or dairy to create a moist and delicious baked good. The ripe bananas and applesauce provide natural sweetness and moisture, while the coconut oil adds richness to the bread. The warm spices of cinnamon and nutmeg complement the banana flavor beautifully. Whether enjoyed as a snack, breakfast, or dessert, this vegan banana bread is a true crowd-pleaser. So grab those ripe bananas and let’s get baking!
