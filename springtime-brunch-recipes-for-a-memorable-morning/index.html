Springtime Brunch: Recipes for a Memorable Morning


As the last wisps of winter’s chill dissipate, the sweet scent of spring flowers begins to waft through the air, signaling the arrival of a new season. With longer days and warmer temperatures, there’s no better time to gather with loved ones and celebrate the beauty of spring. And what better way to do so than with a delicious springtime brunch?

In this post, we’ll explore three mouth-watering recipes that are sure to impress your guests and leave them feeling refreshed and rejuvenated. From sweet treats to savory delights, we’ve got you covered with a range of options to suit every taste.

Recipe 1: Lemon Lavender Waffles

There’s something special about waffles that makes them the perfect brunch food. Maybe it’s the crispy edges and fluffy centers, or the way they can be topped with a multitude of sweet and savory ingredients. Whatever the reason, our Lemon Lavender Waffles are sure to become a new favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon dried lavender buds
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • Fresh lavender buds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your waffle iron according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, lemon juice, and melted butter.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix.
  5. Fold in the dried lavender buds.
  6. Pour approximately 1/4 cup of the batter onto the center of the waffle iron and cook for 3-5 minutes, or until the waffles are golden brown and crispy.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter, greasing the waffle iron with cooking spray as needed.
  8. Serve the waffles warm, garnished with fresh lavender buds and your choice of toppings.

Recipe 2: Asparagus and Prosciutto Frittata

Frittatas are a staple of Italian cuisine, and for good reason. They’re easy to make, versatile, and can be filled with a wide range of ingredients to suit any taste. Our Asparagus and Prosciutto Frittata is a perfect example of this versatility, combining the sweetness of asparagus with the salty, savory flavor of prosciutto.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup diced prosciutto
  • 1/2 cup diced fresh asparagus
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and a pinch of salt.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a 10-inch oven-safe skillet over medium heat.
  4. Add the diced prosciutto and cook until crispy, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the diced asparagus and cook until tender, stirring occasionally.
  6. Pour the eggs over the prosciutto and asparagus mixture.
  7. Sprinkle the Parmesan cheese over the top.
  8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the frittata is golden brown.
  9. Remove the skillet from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  10. Slice the frittata into wedges and serve warm, garnished with fresh parsley.

Recipe 3: Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp

There’s something special about the combination of sweet and tart flavors, and our Strawberry Rhubarb Crisp is the perfect example of this. With its crunchy oat topping and sweet, tangy filling, this dessert is sure to be a hit at your springtime brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup fresh rhubarb, sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and rhubarb.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Add the sugar mixture to the strawberry and rhubarb mixture and stir until everything is well combined.
  5. Transfer the mixture to a 9x9-inch baking dish and set aside.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix together the rolled oats, brown sugar, chopped pecans, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  7. Add the melted butter to the oat mixture and stir until everything is well combined.
  8. Spread the oat mixture evenly over the top of the strawberry and rhubarb mixture.
  9. Bake the crisp for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the filling is bubbly and tender.
  10. Remove the crisp from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  11. Serve warm, topped with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream if desired.

These three recipes are sure to make your springtime brunch a memorable one. Whether you’re in the mood for something sweet and indulgent or savory and satisfying, we’ve got you covered. So go ahead, gather your loved ones, and enjoy the beauty of spring with a delicious meal that’s sure to impress.
