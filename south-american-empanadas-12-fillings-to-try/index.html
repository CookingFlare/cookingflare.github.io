South American Empanadas: 12 Fillings to Try


Empanadas are one of South America’s signature dishes—a delicious hand-pie traditionally baked or fried, packed with fillings that reflect the heart of its culinary diversity. These beloved pockets of flavor are enjoyed across the continent, showcasing local ingredients, cooking techniques, and cultural traditions. Whether you’re looking to indulge your adventurous taste buds or searching for comfort food that feels familiar, empanadas are the perfect choice.

Here, we explore twelve unique and mouthwatering fillings, taking inspiration from various South American cultures. From savory classics to adventurous twists, these combinations will guarantee your empanada experience is both satisfying and unforgettable.

1. Argentinian Beef Empanadas

Argentina is home to some of the most iconic empanadas, and the beef version reigns supreme. Filled with ground beef, onions, paprika, cumin, and sometimes hard-boiled eggs or raisins, this filling perfectly balances savory and slightly sweet notes. A buttery or flaky dough encases the flavorful filling, giving this empanada its beloved classic taste. Many variations include olives for a touch of saltiness.

2. Chilean Seafood Empanadas

Coastal Chile brings fresh seafood to the forefront of its empanada traditions. Stuffed with crab meat, shrimp, or fish, these empanadas often feature garlic, onions, cilantro, and chili peppers for a burst of flavor. The dough is typically thicker to hold the rich filling, perfect for deep-frying or baking. A squeeze of lemon on top takes this filling to the next level.

3. Peruvian Chicken Empanadas

Peru embraces empanadas with tangy chicken-based fillings. Shredded chicken is often mixed with ají amarillo (Peruvian yellow chili), onions, garlic, and vibrant spices like turmeric and cumin. Some recipes incorporate cheese to add creaminess, while others include boiled eggs for added texture. It’s the ideal filling for those who want a mild yet flavorful twist to their empanada repertoire.

4. Colombian Cheese & Guava Empanadas

In Colombia, empanadas are not limited to savory pleasures—sweet variations like the cheese-and-guava filling are equally popular. Guava paste pairs beautifully with melty white cheese, creating a combination of tangy and creamy flavors. This filling is often nestled in tender dough, baked for a warm and gooey dessert-like treat or fried for added crunch.

5. Venezuelan Black Beans & Plantains Empanadas

Venezuela’s empanadas embrace local ingredients and bold flavors. One popular filling includes black beans and ripe plantains, often seasoned with garlic, cumin, and sometimes queso fresco. This vegetarian-friendly option hits all the right notes, blending sweetness, earthiness, and creaminess into one bite.

6. Ecuadorian Pork & Peanut Sauce Empanadas

Ecuador offers a unique twist with its pork and peanut sauce empanadas. Slow-cooked pulled pork is combined with a rich peanut and spicy ají sauce, delivering an unparalleled savory experience. This distinctive filling captures the depth of Ecuadorian cuisine, blending textures and flavors in harmony.

7. Bolivian Salteñas

Though technically a type of empanada, Bolivian salteñas are so unique they deserve their own mention. These empanadas are known for their juicy, stew-like fillings made from chicken or beef mixed with potatoes, carrots, peas, and spices. The dough is slightly sweet and seals the rich filling perfectly, making every bite a burst of flavorful soup.

8. Uruguayan Spinach & Ricotta Empanadas

From Uruguay comes this light yet satisfying filling. Fresh spinach and creamy ricotta cheese team up to create a rich, earthy balance. Garlic, nutmeg, and a hint of Parmesan are often added for extra dimension. These empanadas are perfect for vegetarians or anyone searching for a lighter indulgence.

9. Paraguayan Cornmeal & Cheese Empanadas

Paraguayan cuisine shines with hearty, cheese-filled empanadas made with cornmeal dough. The smooth cheese filling melts beautifully as it bakes or fries, offering incredible texture and flavor. To elevate flavor further, many recipes incorporate onions or fresh herbs like parsley. The use of cornmeal gives these empanadas their unique, slightly sweet taste.

10. Brazilian Hearts of Palm Empanadas

A classic filling in Brazil is hearts of palm, often diced and mixed with onions, tomatoes, and cream cheese. This vegetarian sensation is light, refreshing, and indulgent at the same time. The creamy filling contrasts beautifully with the crisp outer shell, making it a favorite among locals. Adding olives or capers can give this filling a bold twist.

11. Patagonia Lamb Empanadas

In Patagonia, the cool climate and rich culinary heritage bring lamb to the forefront of empanadas. Ground or shredded lamb is cooked with onions, garlic, herbs like thyme or rosemary, and a touch of wine for depth. The result is a filling that’s hearty, aromatic, and unmistakably South American.

12. Wine Braised Mushroom Empanadas

As South America embraces plant-based lifestyles, creative empanada fillings are on the rise. Braised mushrooms simmered in red wine, garlic, and thyme result in an earthy and robust vegetarian filling. Combined with caramelized onions or creamy cheese, this filling feels luxurious and satisfying, yet light enough for any occasion.

Tips for Perfect Empanadas

Empanadas may come in countless varieties, but achieving perfection depends on mastering a few key elements.

  1. Dough Selection: Different countries use varying dough recipes depending on whether empanadas are baked or fried. Experiment with flakier doughs for baking, or sturdier ones if frying.

  2. Cooking Method: Baking creates tender empanadas with a flaky exterior, while frying delivers crispy, golden results.

  3. Sealing Techniques: Properly sealing your empanadas ensures no filling spills out during cooking. Use a fork to crimp the edges for a decorative finish, or twist the borders for a more traditional look.

  4. Season Carefully: South American recipes often layer spices to build complexity. Taste your filling as you go to achieve the bold flavors these cuisines are known for.

  5. Try Regional Variations: Empanadas vary greatly within each country—think triangular shapes, circular sliders, or larger, oval versions. Experiment with shapes and sizes to appreciate the diversity of styles across South America.

Beyond the Fillings

Part of the magic of empanadas lies in their versatility. They’re equally perfect for grab-and-go snacks, elegant appetizers, or full meals. Pair them with traditional sauces like chimichurri, ají criollo, or a simple tomato salsa for added flavor. Hosting a party? Serve a mix of fillings to impress your guests and show off South America’s culinary charm.

Empanadas have become a global favorite, but exploring their roots and regional variations reveals fascinating cultural stories baked into every bite. It’s time to roll out your dough, gather ingredients, and unleash your creativity in the kitchen. Which filling will you try first?
