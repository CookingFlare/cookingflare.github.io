Gluten-Free Quiche and Frittata Recipes for Brunch


Brunch is the perfect blend of breakfast and lunch, and it’s no wonder that it’s one of the most popular meals to enjoy on weekends. Need an excuse to sleep in on Saturdays or Sundays? How about preparing a delightful gluten-free quiche or frittata to impress your family and friends? It’s time to take your brunch game to the next level.

Gluten-Free Quiche Recipe

Quiche is a fantastic way to make an elegant and versatile brunch dish without any hassle. This gluten-free quiche recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen.

IngredientsQuantity
Gluten-free pie crust1 (9-inch)
3 large eggs3
1 1/2 cups half-and-half1 1/2 cups
1/2 cup chopped onion1/2 cup
3/4 cup chopped spinach3/4 cup
2 slices cooked bacon, crumbled2
1/2 cup shredded Swiss cheese1/2 cup
Salt and ground black pepperto taste
1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted1 tablespoon
1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese1 tablespoon

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

Roll out the gluten-free pie crust and place it into a 9-inch pie dish, trimming any excess dough. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and half-and-half. Stir in the onion, spinach, bacon, and Swiss cheese. Season with salt and pepper.

Pour the egg mixture into the unbaked crust and bake for 35-40 minutes or until the center is set. Let the quiche cool for a few moments before slicing and serving.

Gluten-Free Frittata Recipe

Frittatas are a fantastic breakfast or brunch option, as they are easy to prepare and customizable to your taste preferences. This gluten-free frittata recipe is a perfect start.

IngredientsQuantity
6 large eggs6
2 tablespoons olive oil2
1/2 cup chopped onion1/2 cup
3/4 cup chopped spinach3/4 cup
2 slices cooked bacon, crumbled2
1/4 cup shredded Swiss cheese1/4 cup
Salt and ground black pepperto taste
Fresh thyme or basil for garnishas desired

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Heat the olive oil in a 10-inch skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and spinach and cook until tender, about 5-7 minutes. Stir in the bacon and Swiss cheese.

In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and season with salt and pepper. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet, covering the veggies and bacon. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the center is set. Garnish with fresh thyme or basil and serve hot or room temperature.

Making Brunch a Breeze

Both quiche and frittata recipes are simple to prepare and allow for variations in ingredients. You can incorporate your go-to veggies, meats, and cheeses to create mouth-watering, gluten-free dishes that will impress your brunch crowd. Happy cooking!
