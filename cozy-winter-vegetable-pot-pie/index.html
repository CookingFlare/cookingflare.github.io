Cozy Winter Vegetable Pot Pie


As the winter chill sets in, there’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting meal to bring joy to the soul. This Cozy Winter Vegetable Pot Pie is the perfect dish to fill your home with delicious aromas and gather your loved ones around the table for a heartwarming feast. Packed with a medley of hearty vegetables and a flaky, golden crust, this pot pie is a celebration of winter flavors that will leave everyone craving for more.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2/3 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 6-7 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 cups potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any non-dairy milk)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing gently until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  3. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap them in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  2. Add the carrots, celery, potatoes, and mushrooms to the skillet. Sauté for 8-10 minutes, until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Cook for a couple of minutes to get rid of the raw flour taste.

  4. Gradually pour in the vegetable broth and almond milk, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Add the dried thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Allow the mixture to simmer and thicken for about 5-7 minutes.

  5. Stir in the green peas, then remove the skillet from the heat and let the filling cool slightly.

Assembling the Pot Pie:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Roll out one of the chilled dough disks on a floured surface to fit the size of your pie dish. Carefully transfer the rolled out dough to the bottom of the pie dish.

  3. Pour the cooled vegetable filling into the pie crust.

  4. Roll out the second dough disk and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  5. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  6. Bake the pot pie in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  7. Let the pot pie rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Cozy Winter Vegetable Pot Pie pairs beautifully with a fresh green salad dressed with a light vinaigrette. You can also serve it with a side of creamy mashed potatoes or roasted winter vegetables for a truly satisfying meal.

Conclusion

With its wholesome ingredients and comforting flavors, this Cozy Winter Vegetable Pot Pie is a delightful way to embrace the season and enjoy a cozy evening at home. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a nourishing meal, this pot pie is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. So, gather your loved ones, savor the warmth of winter, and indulge in the goodness of this irresistible dish.
