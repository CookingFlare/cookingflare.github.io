Savory and Spicy Jalapeno Cornbread: A Southwestern Favorite


If you’re a fan of Southwestern cuisine and love a little kick in your cornbread, then this recipe is for you. Savory and spicy jalapeno cornbread is a perfect accompaniment to a bowl of chili, a hearty stew, or simply enjoyed on its own. The combination of the sweet cornmeal, tangy buttermilk, and fiery jalapenos creates a flavor explosion that will leave your taste buds begging for more. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup canned corn kernels, drained
  • 2-3 jalapeno peppers, seeded and finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a 9-inch square baking dish.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, salt, and baking soda. Mix well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, melted butter, and eggs until well combined.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense cornbread.

  5. Gently fold in the corn kernels and chopped jalapenos, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  6. Pour the batter into the greased baking dish and spread it out evenly.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  8. Once baked, remove the cornbread from the oven and allow it to cool in the baking dish for a few minutes.

  9. Cut the cornbread into squares or wedges and serve warm.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add shredded cheese, such as cheddar or pepper jack, to the batter before baking.

  • If you prefer a milder heat, remove the seeds and membranes from the jalapeno peppers before chopping.

  • This cornbread pairs well with a dollop of butter or a drizzle of honey on top.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the microwave or oven before serving.

  • Feel free to experiment with other additions, such as diced onions, bacon bits, or even roasted red peppers, to customize the cornbread to your liking.

Final Thoughts

Savory and spicy jalapeno cornbread is a staple in Southwestern cuisine, and for good reason. Its bold flavors and moist texture make it a crowd-pleaser at any gathering or as a comforting treat at home. Whether you’re a spice lover or just looking to spruce up your cornbread recipe, give this Southwestern favorite a try. Your taste buds will thank you!

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in the mouthwatering goodness of savory and spicy jalapeno cornbread. Enjoy!
