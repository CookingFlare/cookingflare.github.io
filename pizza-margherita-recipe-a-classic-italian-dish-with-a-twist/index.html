Pizza Margherita Recipe: A Classic Italian Dish with a Twist


Pizza Margherita, a classic Italian dish that has captured the hearts of foodies around the world. This simple yet elegant recipe has been a staple in Italian cuisine for decades, and for good reason. The combination of flavorful ingredients, delicate preparation, and presentation makes it a true masterpiece. In this recipe, we’ll explore the traditional Pizza Margherita, and add a twist to give it a modern spin.

The Origins of Pizza Margherita

Pizza Margherita is said to have originated in Naples, Italy in the late 19th century. According to legend, Queen Margherita of Savoy visited Naples in 1889, and was served a pizza that resembled the colors of the Italian flag: red (tomato sauce), white (mozzarella cheese), and green (fresh basil leaves). The queen was so impressed with the dish that she asked the chef to name it after her, and the rest is history.

The Traditional Recipe

To make a traditional Pizza Margherita, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped

First, combine the warm water and yeast in a large mixing bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until the yeast is activated and frothy. Add the flour, salt, and olive oil, and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until it has doubled in size.

Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Punch down the dough and shape it into a circle or rectangle, depending on your preference. Place the dough on a greased pizza pan or baking sheet. Spread the tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a 1/2 inch border around the edges. Top with the sliced mozzarella cheese and chopped basil leaves.

Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

The Twist: Adding a Modern Spin

While the traditional Pizza Margherita is a classic, we can add a modern twist to give it a fresh spin. Here are a few ideas:

  • Prosciutto and Arugula: Add some crispy prosciutto and peppery arugula leaves on top of the pizza for a salty and refreshing flavor combination.
  • Burrata Cheese: Replace the mozzarella cheese with burrata cheese, which is made from mozzarella and cream. The creamy center adds a rich and indulgent texture to the pizza.
  • Caramelized Onions: Caramelize some onions before adding them to the pizza for a sweet and savory flavor. This pairs well with the salty prosciutto and arugula.
  • Balsamic Glaze: Drizzle some balsamic glaze over the pizza before serving for a tangy and sweet flavor. This pairs well with the creamy burrata cheese.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind:

  • Use high-quality ingredients: Fresh and high-quality ingredients make a big difference in the taste and texture of the pizza.
  • Experiment with toppings: Don’t be afraid to experiment with different toppings to find your favorite combination.
  • Try different cheeses: While mozzarella is traditional, you can try using other cheeses like parmesan or gorgonzola for a different flavor profile.
  • Make it ahead: You can make the dough ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours.

In conclusion, Pizza Margherita is a classic Italian dish that is easy to make and delicious to eat. With a few simple ingredients and some creative twists, you can elevate this dish to a whole new level. Whether you’re a pizza lover or just looking for a new recipe to try, Pizza Margherita is a must-try. So go ahead, get creative, and make it your own!
