Creamy Nut-Free Butternut Squash Soup Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, a steaming bowl of creamy butternut squash soup is hard to beat. With its rich, velvety texture and warm, earthy flavor, this soup is the perfect dish for a cozy night in or a festive gathering. And if you or your loved ones have nut allergies, fear not - this nut-free version of the classic butternut squash soup will satisfy your cravings without compromising on taste or creaminess.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Squash: Start by peeling, seeding, and dicing the butternut squash. This can be a bit of a chore, but the sweet, vibrant flesh of the squash is well worth the effort.

  2. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they turn soft and fragrant.

  3. Cook the Squash: Add the diced butternut squash to the pot, along with the vegetable broth, ground cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the squash is tender.

  4. Blend to Creamy Perfection: Once the squash is soft, remove the pot from the heat and allow the mixture to cool slightly. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, carefully puree the soup until it reaches a smooth, creamy consistency.

  5. Add Creaminess: Return the pureed soup to the pot and stir in the coconut milk. The coconut milk not only adds a luxurious creaminess to the soup but also complements the natural sweetness of the butternut squash.

  6. Season to Taste: Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste, adjusting the seasoning until it reaches the perfect balance of flavors.

  7. Serve and Garnish: Ladle the soup into bowls, and for a touch of freshness and color, sprinkle some freshly chopped parsley on top before serving.

Tips and Tricks:

  • For a more complex flavor profile, roast the diced butternut squash in the oven before adding it to the soup. The caramelization from roasting will add depth and richness to the soup.
  • If you prefer a thinner consistency, simply add more vegetable broth or coconut milk until the soup reaches your desired thickness.
  • This soup can be made ahead of time and refrigerated for a few days, making it a convenient option for meal prepping or entertaining.

There you have it - a delectable, nut-free butternut squash soup that’s sure to warm both body and soul. Whether you’re craving a comforting bowl of soup on a chilly evening or looking for a crowd-pleasing starter for your next dinner party, this recipe is a winner. So, roll up your sleeves, grab a butternut squash, and get ready to savor the creamy deliciousness of this delightful soup. Cheers to good food, good company, and good times!
