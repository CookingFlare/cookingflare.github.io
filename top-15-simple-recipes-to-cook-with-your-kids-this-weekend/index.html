Top 15 Simple Recipes to Cook with Your Kids This Weekend


Cooking with your kids is a wonderful way to spend quality time together, teach them valuable life skills, and create lasting memories. Weekends provide the perfect opportunity for parents to bond with their little ones over fun and easy recipes. Whether your child is a beginner in the kitchen or already loves experimenting with flavors, there are plenty of simple and delicious recipes that both adults and kids can enjoy making together.

In this blog post, we’ll explore 15 simple recipes that are perfect for a family cooking session. From sweet treats to savory snacks, these recipes are kid-friendly, easy to follow, and require minimal prep time. So, roll up your sleeves, grab your ingredients, and let’s get cooking!

1. Mini Pizzas

Mini pizzas are a great way for kids to get involved in the kitchen, as they can customize their own toppings. Use store-bought pizza dough or English muffins as the base. Set up a mini pizza bar with various toppings like tomato sauce, cheese, pepperoni, veggies, and more. Kids can then assemble their pizzas and watch them bake to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • Pizza dough or English muffins
  • Tomato sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Toppings: pepperoni, olives, bell peppers, mushrooms, etc.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. If using pizza dough, roll it out into small circles. If using English muffins, split them in half.
  3. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on each base.
  4. Sprinkle cheese and let your kids add their favorite toppings.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.

2. Banana Pancakes

Pancakes are a breakfast favorite that kids love to make. Add some mashed bananas to the batter for extra flavor and nutrition. This recipe is easy, quick, and perfect for kids to get involved in pouring, mixing, and flipping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • Butter or oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the mashed banana, milk, egg, and vanilla extract.
  3. Combine both mixtures and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Heat a pan over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  5. Pour small portions of batter onto the pan and cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook until golden brown on both sides.

3. Cheesy Quesadillas

Quesadillas are an easy and fun way to introduce kids to cooking. They can help by adding cheese and other fillings into the tortilla. After the quesadilla is assembled, it just needs a quick cook in a skillet, and it’s ready to serve.

Ingredients:

  • Flour tortillas
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a blend)
  • Optional fillings: cooked chicken, beans, bell peppers, onions, or spinach

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place a tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle cheese evenly over the surface.
  3. Add any additional fillings your kids like, then top with another tortilla.
  4. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until the cheese is melted and the tortilla is golden brown.
  5. Slice into wedges and serve with salsa or guacamole.

4. Veggie Fritters

Getting kids to eat their vegetables can be tricky, but veggie fritters are a fun and tasty way to sneak in some greens. Grate carrots, zucchini, or sweet potatoes, mix them with eggs and flour, and then fry them up into crispy, golden fritters. Serve with a side of yogurt dip for an extra treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium zucchini, grated
  • 1 large carrot, grated
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Greek yogurt for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Grate the zucchini and carrot and squeeze out any excess moisture with a paper towel.
  2. In a bowl, mix the grated veggies with flour, egg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  4. Scoop spoonfuls of the veggie mixture and shape into small patties.
  5. Cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden and crispy.
  6. Serve with a side of Greek yogurt for dipping.

5. Homemade Granola Bars

Granola bars are a healthy, portable snack, and they’re easy to make with your kids. Simply mix together oats, honey, peanut butter, and a variety of add-ins like chocolate chips, nuts, and dried fruit, then bake and slice into bars. Your kids will love the hands-on process of mixing and pressing the ingredients into a pan.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup chocolate chips (optional)
  • 1/4 cup raisins or dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup nuts or seeds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, mix together oats, honey, peanut butter, and any add-ins your kids like.
  3. Press the mixture into a greased baking dish.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown.
  5. Let the bars cool before slicing into squares.

6. Fruit Popsicles

Making homemade popsicles is a fun activity that kids can enjoy on a hot day. Blend fresh fruit like berries, mango, or banana with a little juice or yogurt, pour the mixture into molds, and freeze. Kids will love watching their creations turn into delicious frozen treats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup fresh fruit (berries, mango, banana, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt or fruit juice

Instructions:

  1. Blend the fruit and yogurt or juice until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into popsicle molds.
  3. Insert sticks and freeze for 4-6 hours or until solid.
  4. To release the popsicles, run warm water over the outside of the mold.

7. Egg Muffins

Egg muffins are a fun and healthy way to start the day. These little savory bites can be customized with different fillings like spinach, cheese, bacon, or even leftover vegetables. They’re easy to make in a muffin tin, and kids will love cracking the eggs and adding the ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 6 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional fillings: cheese, spinach, bell peppers, bacon, or ham

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk with milk, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the egg mixture into the muffin tin, filling each cup halfway.
  4. Add any desired fillings to each cup.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes, until the eggs are set and slightly golden.

8. Smoothie Bowls

Smoothie bowls are a fun, colorful, and healthy way to enjoy breakfast. Blend fruits like banana, berries, and mango with a bit of milk or yogurt, and top with granola, nuts, and seeds. Your kids can get creative with the toppings and even arrange them in fun patterns.

Ingredients:

  • 1 banana, frozen
  • 1/2 cup berries (strawberries, blueberries, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt or milk
  • Granola, nuts, seeds, and fresh fruit for toppings

Instructions:

  1. Blend the frozen banana, berries, and yogurt until smooth.
  2. Pour the smoothie into bowls.
  3. Let your kids top the bowls with their favorite toppings, creating fun designs.

9. Mini Meatballs

Mini meatballs are great for little hands and can be made with a variety of meats like beef, turkey, or chicken. They’re simple to prepare, and kids will enjoy rolling the meatballs and watching them cook. Serve them with marinara sauce for dipping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground meat (beef, turkey, chicken)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese
  • Salt, pepper, and garlic powder to taste
  • Marinara sauce for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix the ground meat, breadcrumbs, egg, cheese, and seasonings.
  3. Roll the mixture into small meatballs and place them on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, until the meatballs are cooked through.
  5. Serve with marinara sauce for dipping.

10. Homemade Soft Pretzels

Making homemade pretzels is a fun and interactive activity. Kids can shape the dough into twists, brush them with butter, and sprinkle them with salt before baking. These soft pretzels are a great snack for family movie night.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • Coarse salt for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for 5 minutes until bubbly.
  2. Mix the flour and salt in another bowl, then add the yeast mixture and melted butter. Knead until smooth.
  3. Let the dough rise for 1 hour in a warm place.
  4. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  5. Roll the dough into long ropes and shape into pretzels.
  6. Boil the pretzels in water for 1-2 minutes before baking on a greased baking sheet for 10-12 minutes.

11. Apple Nachos

Apple nachos are a fun, healthy snack that’s easy to assemble. Slice apples into thin rounds, drizzle with peanut butter or caramel, and sprinkle with toppings like granola, chocolate chips, or nuts. It’s a sweet treat that kids will love making and eating.

Ingredients:

  • 2 apples, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter or caramel
  • Toppings: granola, chocolate chips, mini marshmallows, nuts

Instructions:

  1. Slice the apples into thin rounds and arrange them on a plate.
  2. Drizzle with peanut butter or caramel sauce.
  3. Top with your favorite toppings, such as granola, chocolate chips, and nuts.

12. Taco Cups

Taco cups are a fun way to serve tacos. Use muffin tins to bake small tortilla shells and fill them with seasoned ground beef or chicken, cheese, lettuce, and salsa. Your kids will enjoy assembling these little taco cups and eating them with their hands.

Ingredients:

  • 4 flour tortillas
  • 1 lb ground beef or chicken
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • Shredded cheese, lettuce, salsa, and sour cream for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a muffin tin.
  2. Cut the tortillas into small circles and press them into the muffin cups.
  3. Cook the ground beef or chicken with taco seasoning.
  4. Fill each taco cup with the meat mixture and top with cheese.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes, until the cheese is melted and the tortillas are crispy.
  6. Top with lettuce, salsa, and sour cream.

13. Zucchini Noodles with Marinara Sauce

If you’re looking for a healthy and fun meal, zucchini noodles (or zoodles) are a great alternative to traditional pasta. Kids will love using a spiralizer to create the noodles, and they can help sauté them in a pan before topping with marinara sauce and cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis
  • 1 cup marinara sauce
  • Grated parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Use a spiralizer to turn the zucchinis into noodles.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté the zucchini noodles for 2-3 minutes until tender.
  3. Add marinara sauce and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Serve with grated parmesan cheese.

14. Rice Krispies Treats

Rice Krispies treats are a classic no-bake treat that kids can help with from start to finish. They’ll love stirring the melted marshmallow mixture into the Rice Krispies cereal and pressing it into a pan.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups Rice Krispies cereal
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1 package (10 oz) marshmallows

Instructions:

  1. Melt the butter in a large pan over low heat.
  2. Add the marshmallows and stir until completely melted.
  3. Stir in the Rice Krispies cereal until well coated.
  4. Press the mixture into a greased pan and let it cool before cutting into squares.

15. Homemade Fruit Leather

Making fruit leather is a simple and healthy way to enjoy dried fruit. Blend up fresh fruit, spread it on a baking sheet, and dry it in the oven for several hours. It’s a fun activity that your kids will love and can take as a snack to school.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of fresh fruit (berries, mango, or apples)
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 170°F (75°C).
  2. Blend the fruit until smooth, then stir in honey if desired.
  3. Pour the mixture onto a parchment-lined baking sheet and spread it into an even layer.
  4. Bake for 4-6 hours, until the fruit leather is firm and pliable.
  5. Let it cool before cutting into strips.

These simple and fun recipes are perfect for cooking with your kids this weekend. Not only will they learn valuable kitchen skills, but they’ll also get to enjoy delicious food that they helped create. Happy cooking!
