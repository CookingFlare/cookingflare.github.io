Southern Biscuits and Gravy: Breakfast Comfort in Every Bite


There’s nothing quite like waking up to the aroma of freshly baked biscuits and the rich, savory scent of homemade gravy. Southern Biscuits and Gravy is a classic breakfast dish that brings comfort and satisfaction to every bite. Today, I’m going to share with you my tried and true recipe for this beloved Southern favorite. So, roll up your sleeves and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

For the Biscuits:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk

For the Gravy:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Biscuits

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients. Using your fingertips or a pastry cutter, cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Pour in the buttermilk and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough biscuits.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thickness.
  6. Using a round biscuit cutter, cut out the biscuits and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake the biscuits for 12-15 minutes or until they are golden brown on top. Remove from the oven and set aside.

Step 2: Making the Gravy

  1. In a large skillet, cook the breakfast sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Use a wooden spoon to break the sausage into smaller pieces as it cooks.
  2. Once the sausage is cooked, sprinkle the flour over it and stir well to coat the sausage evenly.
  3. Slowly pour in the milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Continue cooking and stirring until the gravy thickens to your desired consistency. This usually takes about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Season the gravy with salt and pepper to taste. Remember to taste as you go and adjust the seasoning accordingly.

Step 3: Serving

  1. Split the warm biscuits in half and place them on individual plates.
  2. Generously ladle the hot sausage gravy over each biscuit half, allowing it to seep into the layers.
  3. Serve immediately and enjoy the warm, comforting flavors of Southern Biscuits and Gravy.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have buttermilk on hand, you can make a quick substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar to 1 cup of milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using.
  • For a twist on the traditional recipe, try adding sautéed onions, bell peppers, or even a dash of hot sauce to the gravy.
  • If you’re looking for a vegetarian option, replace the breakfast sausage with crumbled tofu or vegetarian sausage.

So there you have it, a classic Southern recipe that will bring warmth and comfort to your breakfast table. Southern Biscuits and Gravy is the perfect way to start your day or indulge in a cozy weekend brunch. With its flaky biscuits and creamy, flavorful gravy, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. So go ahead, give it a try, and savor the taste of true Southern comfort.
