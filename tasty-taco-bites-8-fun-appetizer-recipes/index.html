Tasty Taco Bites: 8 Fun Appetizer Recipes


Tacos are a staple in many cuisines around the world, and their popularity shows no signs of waning. But what happens when you want to serve tacos at a party or gathering, but don’t want to deal with the hassle of individual tacos or the mess of messy fillings? Enter the humble taco bite, a delicious and easy-to-eat snack that’s perfect for any occasion.

In this post, we’ll explore 8 fun and tasty taco bite recipes that are sure to impress your guests. From classic beef and cheese to more adventurous options like Korean BBQ and vegan black bean, there’s something for everyone in this collection. So grab your apron and let’s get started!

Recipe 1: Classic Beef and Cheese Taco Bites

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Preheat the oil in a deep frying pan to 350°F. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, chili powder, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook until the beef is browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Drain the excess fat and stir in the shredded cheese and chopped cilantro. Meanwhile, warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds. Assemble the taco bites by spooning the beef and cheese mixture onto a tortilla, folding the tortilla in half to form a triangle, and sealing the edges with a toothpick. Fry the taco bites in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Recipe 2: Korean BBQ Taco Bites

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 tablespoons Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Preheat the oil in a deep frying pan to 350°F. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, Gochujang, soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Cook until the beef is browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks. Drain the excess fat and stir in the chopped green onions. Meanwhile, warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds. Assemble the taco bites by spooning the beef and sauce mixture onto a tortilla, folding the tortilla in half to form a triangle, and sealing the edges with a toothpick. Fry the taco bites in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Recipe 3: Vegan Black Bean Taco Bites

  • 1 cup cooked black beans
  • 1/4 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped yellow onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Preheat the oil in a deep frying pan to 350°F. In a large bowl, combine the black beans, red bell pepper, yellow onion, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes. Drain the excess liquid and stir in the chopped cilantro. Meanwhile, warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds. Assemble the taco bites by spooning the bean and vegetable mixture onto a tortilla, folding the tortilla in half to form a triangle, and sealing the edges with a toothpick. Fry the taco bites in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Recipe 4: Chicken Tinga Taco Bites

  • 1 pound cooked chicken breast
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Preheat the oil in a deep frying pan to 350°F. In a large bowl, combine the cooked chicken, red onion, cilantro, garlic, lime juice, cumin, and cayenne pepper. Cook until the mixture is heated through, about 2-3 minutes. Meanwhile, warm the tortillas by wrapping them in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 20-30 seconds. Assemble the taco bites by spooning the chicken and sauce mixture onto a tortilla, folding the tortilla in half to form a triangle, and sealing the edges with a toothpick. Fry the taco bites in batches until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.
