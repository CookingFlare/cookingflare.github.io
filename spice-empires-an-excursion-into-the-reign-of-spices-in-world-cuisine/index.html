Spice Empires: An Excursion into the Reign of Spices in World Cuisine


The allure of spices has captivated people across the globe for centuries, deriving from not only their tantalizing flavors but also their ability unlock global tastes and cultures. From aromatic Indian curries to zesty North African tagines, spices hold a special place in the rich tapestry of world cuisines. In this exploration of spice empires, we delve into the intoxicating world of spices and uncover the unique and enchanting dishes they bring to our tables.

Embark on a Culinary Journey:

Let’s set sail on a captivating adventure through history, trade, and the infusion of spice into the very fabric of world cuisines. Starting in the bustling streets of medieval Asia and traveling across continents, we’ll learn how the spice trade redefined the culinary landscape for centuries to come.

1. The African Spice: Aside from everyday spices, one will find the use of exotic ingredients that demonstrate the rich culinary traditions of Africa. Morocco, in particular, boasts a cuisine that’s a harmonious blend of Berber, Arab, Moorish, and Mediterranean influences.

Spiced Chicken Tagine:

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs or leg quarters (skinless)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Handful of mixed spices (cinnamon, coriander, ginger, cumin, paprika, and cayenne)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 preserved lemons, rind only, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of green olives
  • 1 cup of stock or water
  • Fresh coriander to garnish

Instructions:

  • Prepare the spices by grinding them using a traditional Moroccan mortar and pestle or a spice grinder.
  • In a large pot, heat some oil on medium-high heat. Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper, ensuring to pat them dry.
  • Place the chicken in the pot, and sear until golden brown on all sides. Transfer the chicken to a plate and set it aside.
  • Over the same pot, sauté the sliced onion until slightly caramelized. Add the spice mixture and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  • Return the chicken to the pot, along with the preserved lemon and olives. Pour the stock or water into the pot, ensuring the chicken is mostly submerged.
  • Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, cover with a lid, and let it cook for around 40-50 minutes until the chicken is tender and the sauce is, thickened.
  • Once done, crumble in a small pinch of saffron and stir in a few drops of honey, tasting the dish and adjusting the seasonings as necessary.
  • Serve the tagine with a sprinkling of fresh coriander on top, and accompanying bread or couscous to soak up that rich, fragrant sauce.

2. The Indian Spice: The rich colors and complex flavors of Indian cuisine have long captured culinary enthusiasts across the globe, as evidenced by the love affair with curries, biryanis, and chutneys that our taste buds share.

Tandoori Chicken:

Ingredients:

  • 6 chicken thighs or leg quarters (skinless)
  • 3/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons of garam masala (a blend including cloves, cardamom, black pepper, and cumin)
  • Salt to taste
  • A handful of fresh cilantro and mint leaves, chopped

Instructions:

  • In a large bowl, mix together yogurt, garlic, ginger, and garam masala to create the marinade.
  • Pat the chicken pieces dry using paper towels and season them with salt. Add the chicken to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well coated. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours (overnight is optimal).
  • Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and place a cast-iron skillet or baking dish in the oven as it heats. Once heated, remove the skillet carefully from the oven and place it on the stove over medium heat.
  • Pour oil into the heated skillet, making sure it evenly coats the surface. Place the chicken pieces in the skillet, spacing them to avoid overcrowding.
  • Lower the heat to medium-low and cook the chicken pieces until they develop a nice crust on one side (approximately 12-15 minutes).
  • Once the chicken has developed a nice crust, transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for around 25-30 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  • Remove the chicken from the oven and allow it to rest for approximately 5 minutes before garnishing it with cilantro and mint.
  • Serve the tandoori chicken warm, accompanied by rice, flatbreads, or a refreshing salad.

3. The Medieval European Spice: A trip through the annals of history reveals that during the Middle Ages, trade routes opened up European cuisines to a world of intrigue and flavor. It’s during this period that spices like black pepper, nutmeg, and others seeped into every aspect of daily life, spicing up dishes in an era of hearty stews and roasts.

Spiced Pear and Goat Cheese Tart:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made or homemade pie crust
  • 2 pears, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup port wine or white wine
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 4 oz goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup candied pecans, roughly chopped

Instructions:

  • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Roll out the pie crust and transfer it to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Prick the base with a fork and set it aside.
  • In a small bowl, mix together wine, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Arrange the pear slices in a pattern on the pie crust, drizzling the spiced wine mixture over the pears.
  • Bake for about 20 minutes or until the crust is golden and the pears are tender.
  • While the tart is baking, prepare the candied pecans. In a small saucepan, heat 1/4 cup sugar and 1/4 cup water on medium heat, bring to a simmer to dissolve sugar. Add the pecans and let them simmer for about 5 minutes, then remove from the heat and let them cool.
  • Upon removing the tart from the oven, crumble the goat cheese over the top and sprinkle with the chopped candied pecans.
  • Allow the tart to cool before serving.

While traditional recipes are fiercely guarded secrets and passed down from generations, unearthing the stories and flavors of the past gives us a glimpse into the spice empires that forever transformed the elements that make up the present. As every region shaped its culinary arts with the flavors of transported spices, memories of these spice empires continue to nourish our souls through our sense of taste.

Embark on your own culinary exploration and spice up your cooking with a dash of history. The vast range of ingredients at your fingertips begs you to unleash your own creativity and dazzle your dinner table with a feast fit for every discerning palate! Happy cooking!
