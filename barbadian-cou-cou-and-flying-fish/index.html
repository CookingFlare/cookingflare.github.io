Barbadian Cou-Cou and Flying Fish


Barbadian cuisine is a delightful fusion of flavors influenced by the island’s rich history and diverse cultural heritage. One of the most iconic dishes that encapsulates the essence of Barbadian cooking is Cou-Cou and Flying Fish. This traditional dish is a harmonious blend of creamy cornmeal and tender flying fish, flavored with a medley of herbs and spices. Let’s embark on a culinary journey to discover the secrets of preparing this beloved Barbadian delicacy.

Ingredients

For the Cou-Cou:

  • 2 cups of cornmeal
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1 cup of okra, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of fish or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Flying Fish:

  • 4 fresh flying fish fillets
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

Instructions

Preparing the Cou-Cou:

  1. In a large saucepan, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Gradually add the cornmeal to the boiling water, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and continue to stir the mixture until it thickens and begins to pull away from the sides of the pan.
  4. In a separate pan, sauté the onions and garlic until they are soft and translucent.
  5. Add the chopped okra to the pan and cook for a few minutes until tender.
  6. Gradually incorporate the sautéed vegetables into the thickened cornmeal mixture, stirring well to combine.
  7. Pour in the fish or vegetable broth, and continue to cook the Cou-Cou over low heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a smooth and creamy consistency.
  8. Season the Cou-Cou with fresh thyme, parsley, salt, and pepper, adjusting the seasoning to taste.

Cooking the Flying Fish:

  1. Rinse the flying fish fillets under cold water and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. In a bowl, combine the lime juice, minced garlic, chopped thyme, salt, and pepper to create a flavorful marinade.
  3. Place the flying fish fillets in the marinade, ensuring they are well coated, and let them marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Gently place the marinated flying fish fillets in the skillet and cook them for 3-4 minutes on each side until they are golden brown and cooked through.

Serving the Dish:

To serve, scoop a generous portion of the creamy Cou-Cou onto a plate and place a perfectly seared flying fish fillet on top. Garnish with a sprig of fresh parsley and a wedge of lime for a burst of citrus flavor.

Conclusion

Barbadian Cou-Cou and Flying Fish is a dish that reflects the vibrant spirit and culinary prowess of the island. The velvety texture of the Cou-Cou combined with the delicate flavor of the flying fish creates a symphony of taste that is sure to captivate your senses. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or an adventurous home cook, this recipe offers a tantalizing glimpse into the heart of Barbadian gastronomy. Embrace the flavors of the Caribbean and savor each bite of this time-honored delicacy.

So, gather your ingredients, let your kitchen be filled with the aroma of thyme and garlic, and transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed shores of Barbados with this delectable Cou-Cou and Flying Fish recipe. Cheers to a culinary escapade like no other!
