Christmas Recipes from Around the World: A Global Feast


Christmas brings with it a sense of joy, togetherness, and celebration like no other season. One of the most cherished traditions of the holiday is gathering around the table to share a festive feast. From rich desserts to savory roasts and hearty side dishes, the flavors of Christmas offer a glimpse into the culinary customs of cultures far and wide. Travel along with us on this journey of global flavors as we explore traditional Christmas recipes from around the world. Whether you’re looking to try something new this holiday season or simply curious about festive cuisines in other countries, this culinary adventure promises to be deliciously rewarding.

Germany: Christstollen – A Classic Christmas Fruit Bread

Germany is known for its deeply rooted holiday traditions, and nothing says Christmas quite like Christstollen. This dense, fruit-laden bread is packed with dried fruits, nuts, candied citrus peel, and an aromatic blend of spices—all wrapped in a generous coating of powdered sugar. Often referred to simply as “stollen,” this treat is perfect for enjoying with a hot cup of tea or coffee during the cold winter months.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 cup lukewarm milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup raisins
  • ½ cup chopped almonds
  • ½ cup candied citrus peel
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Method:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in lukewarm milk and let it sit for 10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt, cardamom, and cinnamon. Add the yeast mixture, eggs, and butter. Knead until the dough is smooth and elastic, about 10 minutes.
  3. Gradually work in the raisins, nuts, and candied peel. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  4. Shape the dough into an oval loaf and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Let it rise again for 30 minutes.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 40-50 minutes or until golden brown.
  6. Once cooled, dust the loaf generously with powdered sugar. Wrap in aluminum foil and let it sit for a day or two—Christstollen is best when allowed to mature.

Italy: Panettone – A Tall and Sweet Celebration Cake

In Italy, no Christmas is complete without panettone. This iconic Milanese dessert is a tall, dome-shaped cake with a light, fluffy texture and a burst of festive flavors. Packed with dried fruits and often spiked with citrus zest, panettone is a symbol of Italian Christmas cheer.

Ingredients:

  • 4⅓ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • ½ cup warm water
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 orange and 1 lemon
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup raisins
  • ½ cup candied orange peel

Method:

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water and let it activate for 10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, salt, zest, and vanilla. Add the yeast mixture, eggs, and butter, then knead until smooth.
  3. Incorporate raisins and orange peel, ensuring they’re evenly distributed. Cover the dough and let it rise for 2 hours.
  4. Shape the dough and place it into a high-sided, traditional panettone mold (or substitute with a tall round baking pan). Let it rise again until it doubles in size, about 1 hour.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 35-40 minutes. Cool completely before slicing.

Mexico: Tamales – A Flavorful Christmas Tradition

Mexican Christmas celebrations often feature the beloved tamales, a comforting dish of masa harina dough stuffed with various fillings, wrapped in corn husks, and steamed to perfection. They’re both versatile and packed with flavor, as families customize the fillings to suit their tastes—pork, chicken, cheese, and even sweet versions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups masa harina (corn flour)
  • 1½ cups chicken broth
  • ½ cup lard or vegetable shortening
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken (or pork)
  • 1 cup red chili sauce
  • Dried corn husks, soaked in warm water

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, mix masa harina, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add chicken broth while stirring.
  2. Whip in the lard or shortening until the dough is light and fluffy.
  3. Toss chicken with red chili sauce to create a flavorful filling.
  4. Spread a thin layer of masa dough onto a corn husk. Add a spoonful of chicken filling in the center, then fold and secure the husk.
  5. Steam tamales in a large pot for 1-1½ hours, ensuring they’re cooked through. Serve hot with additional chili sauce.

Sweden: Lussekatter – Saffron-Infused Christmas Buns

Swedish Christmas mornings are often accompanied by Lussekatter, or saffron buns. These golden, softly spiced rolls are shaped into intricate designs, such as spirals or figure eights, and are traditionally enjoyed during Lucia celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads, crushed
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • Raisins, for garnish

Method:

  1. Dissolve saffron in warm milk and let it steep for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Add saffron-infused milk, butter, and eggs, kneading until elastic.
  3. Cover and let the dough rise for 1½ hours.
  4. Divide the dough into portions and shape into buns. Press raisins into the dough for garnish.
  5. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 10-12 minutes until golden.

Japan: Christmas Fried Chicken

In Japan, Christmas is synonymous with fried chicken thanks to clever marketing campaigns by fast-food restaurants. Many families now associate the holiday with crispy chicken served alongside vibrant salads and desserts.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds chicken drumsticks or thighs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • Oil for frying

Method:

  1. Marinate chicken in buttermilk for 1 hour.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix flour, garlic powder, paprika, and pepper.
  3. Coat each piece of chicken in the flour mixture.
  4. Heat oil in a deep fryer or pot to 350°F (175°C). Fry chicken until golden and cooked through, about 12-15 minutes. Drain on paper towels and serve immediately.

South Africa: Malva Pudding – A Decadent Dessert

South Africa’s Christmas tables often feature Malva Pudding, a sticky, caramelized dessert best served warm with custard or cream. Its sweetness and richness perfectly complement the jovial holiday spirit.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 large egg
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon apricot jam
  • 1 cup milk
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ¼ cup butter
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Mix flour and baking soda. In another bowl, beat egg, sugar, and apricot jam until smooth.
  3. Alternate adding dry ingredients and milk to the wet mixture. Pour batter into a greased baking dish.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes. Meanwhile, heat cream, butter, and sugar in a saucepan to create a sauce.
  5. Pour hot sauce over the baked pudding and allow to soak. Serve warm.

Bringing Global Flavors to Your Table

These Christmas recipes from around the world reflect the diversity of holiday traditions. Whether it’s the comforting richness of Christstollen, the savory satisfaction of Mexican tamales, or the sweet celebration of Malva Pudding, each dish tells a unique story. By incorporating these global recipes into your holiday spread, you can create a feast that transcends borders and brings loved ones closer through the shared joy of culinary exploration. Let this Christmas be an invitation to embrace flavors far and wide while celebrating the season’s warmth and togetherness.
