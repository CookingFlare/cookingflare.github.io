Comfort Food Classics: Homemade Recipes to Cure Homesickness


There’s something magical about the aroma of a home-cooked meal that instantly transports us back to the comforting embrace of our childhood. Whether it’s the hearty flavors of a stew, the buttery goodness of mashed potatoes, or the sweet indulgence of a warm apple pie, comfort foods have a way of soothing our souls and curing homesickness. In this blog post, we’ll explore some timeless comfort food classics and share homemade recipes that will not only fill your belly but also warm your heart.

1. Chicken Pot Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of diced carrots
  • 1 cup of peas
  • 1/2 cup of diced onions
  • 1/3 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Store-bought or homemade pie crust

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pot, poach the chicken breasts in the chicken broth until fully cooked. Remove the chicken, let it cool, and then shred it.
  3. In the same pot, sauté the carrots, peas, and onions until they are slightly tender.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat.
  5. Gradually pour in the milk, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  6. Add the shredded chicken back into the pot and stir to combine.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Pour the chicken mixture into a pie dish lined with the pie crust.
  9. Cover the pie with the remaining pie crust, crimp the edges, and make a few slits on top for ventilation.
  10. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the crust is golden brown.

2. Macaroni and Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 2 cups of shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: breadcrumbs for topping

Instructions:

  1. Cook the macaroni according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes until it forms a paste.
  4. Gradually whisk in the milk and cook until the mixture thickens.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the shredded cheddar cheese until melted and smooth.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir to combine.
  8. Optional: Transfer the mac and cheese to a baking dish, sprinkle with breadcrumbs, and bake for 20-25 minutes at 350°F (175°C) for a crispy topping.

3. Beef Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds of stew beef, cut into cubes
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of diced potatoes
  • 1 cup of diced carrots
  • 1 cup of diced celery
  • 1 cup of diced onions
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the stew beef over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the onions and garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for a few minutes.
  4. Pour in the beef broth and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Add the potatoes, carrots, celery, and thyme.
  6. Cover and simmer for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

4. Apple Crisp

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups of peeled and sliced apples
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup of butter, softened

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice, granulated sugar, and cinnamon.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, oats, and brown sugar. Cut in the softened butter until the mixture is crumbly.
  4. Place the apple mixture in a greased baking dish.
