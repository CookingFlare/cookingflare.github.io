Classic Russian Borscht: 5 Recipes for a Hearty Soup


Borscht, a beloved staple of Eastern European cuisine, especially Russian and Ukrainian, is more than just a soup; it’s a comforting embrace on a cold day. This vibrant dish, primarily made from beets, is known for its rich flavor, deep crimson color, and nutritional benefits. Whether served hot or cold, borscht can be enjoyed as a main course or a side, making it a versatile option for any meal. In this blog post, we will explore five different recipes for classic Russian borscht, each with its unique twist while keeping the heart and soul of this traditional dish intact.

Understanding Borscht

Before diving into the recipes, it’s important to understand what makes borscht so special. At its core, borscht revolves around beets, which impart a sweet, earthy flavor and a stunning color. The soup can be made with various ingredients, including vegetables, meat, and herbs, and is often served with a dollop of sour cream and fresh dill. The beauty of borscht lies in its regional variations and personal adaptations, allowing each cook to leave their mark on this classic dish.

The Base Ingredients of Borscht

While borscht recipes can vary, several ingredients commonly recur:

  • Beets: The star of the dish, beets provide sweetness and color.
  • Cabbage: Adds texture and a slight crunch.
  • Carrots: For sweetness and depth of flavor.
  • Potatoes: Make the soup hearty and filling.
  • Onions and Garlic: Essential aromatics that enhance the overall flavor.
  • Tomatoes: Fresh or canned, they add acidity and brightness.
  • Broth: Vegetable, beef, or chicken broth forms the soup’s foundation.
  • Dill and Parsley: Fresh herbs for garnish and flavor.

Recipe 1: Traditional Russian Borscht

Ingredients

  • 3 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1/4 head of cabbage, shredded
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 6 cups of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill for garnish
  • Sour cream for serving

Instructions

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until translucent. Stir in grated beets and carrots, cooking for about 5-7 minutes until softened.

  2. Combine Ingredients: Add diced potatoes, shredded cabbage, and tomato paste to the pot. Mix well.

  3. Add Broth: Pour in the beef broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  4. Finish Off: Stir in vinegar, salt, and pepper. Adjust seasoning to taste. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes.

  5. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls, top with a generous dollop of sour cream and garnish with fresh dill.

Recipe 2: Vegetarian Borscht

Ingredients

  • 3 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1/4 head of cabbage, shredded
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 6 cups of vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Vegan sour cream for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Add grated beets and carrots, cooking for about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Combine Ingredients: Add diced potatoes, shredded cabbage, and tomato paste. Stir to combine.

  3. Add Broth: Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30-35 minutes.

  4. Finish Off: Stir in lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for an additional 5 minutes.

  5. Serve: Dish into bowls and top with fresh parsley and vegan sour cream, if desired.

Recipe 3: Beef and Beet Borscht

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef shank or chuck, cut into cubes
  • 3 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1/4 head of cabbage, shredded
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 cups of beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill for garnish
  • Sour cream for serving

Instructions

  1. Brown the Beef: In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add beef cubes and brown on all sides. Remove from the pot and set aside.

  2. Sauté Vegetables: In the same pot, add onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Add grated beets and carrots, cooking for about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Return the beef to the pot, add diced potatoes, shredded cabbage, and tomato paste. Mix well.

  4. Add Broth: Pour in the beef broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 1-1.5 hours until the beef is tender.

  5. Finish Off: Stir in cider vinegar, salt, and pepper. Adjust seasoning to taste and simmer for another 10 minutes.

  6. Serve: Ladle into bowls, topping with sour cream and fresh dill.

Recipe 4: Cold Borscht (Okroshka)

Ingredients

  • 3 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 1 medium cucumber, diced
  • 1 medium radish, diced
  • 1/4 head of cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 6 cups of cold vegetable broth or kefir
  • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill for garnish
  • Boiled eggs for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine Vegetables: In a large bowl, mix grated beets, diced cucumber, diced radish, and shredded cabbage.

  2. Prepare Broth: In another bowl, combine cold vegetable broth or kefir with vinegar, salt, and pepper.

  3. Mix Together: Pour the broth mixture over the vegetables and stir until well combined.

  4. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

  5. Serve: Ladle into bowls and garnish with fresh dill and a boiled egg if desired.

Recipe 5: Borscht with Mushrooms

Ingredients

  • 3 medium beets, peeled and grated
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced (button or shiitake)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot, grated
  • 1 medium potato, diced
  • 1/4 head of cabbage, shredded
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 6 cups of vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish
  • Sour cream for serving

Instructions

  1. Sauté Mushrooms: In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add sliced mushrooms and sauté until browned. Remove and set aside.

  2. Sauté Vegetables: In the same pot, add onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant. Add grated beets and carrots, cooking for about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Return the mushrooms to the pot, add diced potatoes, shredded cabbage, and tomato paste. Stir to combine.

  4. Add Broth: Pour in the broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 30-35 minutes.

  5. Finish Off: Stir in white wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for another 5 minutes.

  6. Serve: Ladle into bowls, garnishing with fresh thyme and sour cream.

Conclusion

Borscht is a timeless dish that caters to a wide range of palates and dietary preferences. With these five recipes — from the traditional to the vegetarian, and even a refreshing cold version — you can experience the heartiness and rich flavors of this classic Russian soup in various ways. Each recipe offers a unique take while preserving the essence of what makes borscht such a beloved meal.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aromas of beets, garlic, and herbs fill your kitchen. Whether you choose to enjoy it on a chilly evening or as a refreshing summer dish, borscht is sure to warm your heart and satisfy your hunger, making it a cherished addition to your recipe collection. Enjoy your cooking adventure!
