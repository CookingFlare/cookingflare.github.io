Elevated Chicken Fried Rice: Comfort Food Perfected


There’s something special about a comforting bowl of chicken fried rice. It’s a dish that brings together the warmth of home-cooked meals with the exotic flavors of the Far East. In this recipe, we’re going to take the traditional chicken fried rice and elevate it to new heights, adding a touch of sophistication and a burst of flavor that will leave you craving for more. Get ready to embark on a culinary journey as we delve into the world of elevated chicken fried rice.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

For the Fried Rice:

  • 4 cups cooked and chilled jasmine rice
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots, thawed
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced

Instructions

Marinating the Chicken:

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, cornstarch, and black pepper.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and toss to coat evenly.
  3. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.

Cooking the Fried Rice:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the beaten eggs and scramble until just set. Remove the eggs from the wok and set aside.
  3. In the same wok, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over high heat.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and stir-fry until fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the wok and set aside.
  5. In the same wok, add the diced onion and cook until translucent.
  6. Stir in the minced garlic, peas, and carrots, and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add the chilled jasmine rice to the wok, breaking up any clumps, and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.
  8. Drizzle the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil over the rice, and sprinkle with white pepper.
  9. Return the cooked chicken and scrambled eggs to the wok, tossing everything together until heated through.
  10. Stir in the sliced green onions and cook for an additional minute.
  11. Remove the wok from the heat and transfer the elevated chicken fried rice to a serving dish.

Serving Suggestions

This elevated chicken fried rice is a complete meal on its own, but you can always pair it with some traditional accompaniments. Serve it alongside some crispy spring rolls, a refreshing cucumber salad, or a hot bowl of wonton soup for a truly delightful dining experience.

Final Thoughts

Elevated chicken fried rice takes a beloved classic and infuses it with new flavors and textures, creating a dish that’s both familiar and exciting. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply satisfying your own cravings, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. So, grab your wok and get ready to elevate your comfort food game with this mouthwatering chicken fried rice recipe!
