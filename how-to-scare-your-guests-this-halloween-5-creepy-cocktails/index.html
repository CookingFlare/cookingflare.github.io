How To Scare Your Guests This Halloween: 5 Creepy Cocktails


Looking to up the ante at your Halloween party this year? Bring spine-chilling frights to your guests with these five creepy cocktails. Each concoction draws on various spooky elements and themes to give your gathering an unforgettable edge. 1. Crimson Chiller

Serving up a cold glass of your own “bloody wine” is the perfect way to kick off the Halloween frights at your event. Swoop in with a splash of red food coloring and frozen berries for an extra jolt of scares.

Ingredients

  • 1 bottle red wine, chilled
  • Red food coloring
  • 1 pint fresh or frozen raspberries or cranberries
  • Lemon slices and mint leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Pour the red wine into a punch bowl or large serving pitcher.
  2. Mix in a few drops of red food coloring, adjusting to your desired “bloody” effect.
  3. Add the frozen berries and stir gently so as not to crush them, allowing their color to infuse slightly into the wine.
  4. Serve over ice, garnished with a lemon slice and sprig of mint.

2. Laughing Jack’s Mojito

Create a deviously delightful twist on the classic mojito with the mascaraded grin of the infamous trickster, Jack. With the addition of lime wheels and maraschino cherries, the “Smiling Mojito” will have guests questioning their trust in what’s in their glass.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 1/2 oz white rum
  • 1/2 oz triple sec
  • Splash of soda water
  • 1 cup ice
  • Lime wheels and maraschino cherries for garnish

Instructions

  1. Muddle lime juice and mint leaves together in the bottom of a glass.
  2. Add in white rum and triple sec, followed by a cup of ice.
  3. Gently mix, then top off with a splash of soda water for that eerie effervescence.
  4. Garnish with a lime wheel and cherry arranged to resemble a sinister smile.

3. Ghostly Glimmer Gimlet

Nothing screams Fright Fest quite like Bohemian zombies, but if you want to liven up the undead neighborhood theme, let the “Ghostly Glimmer Gimlet” be your haunting addition to your party.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 oz gin
  • 3/4 oz lime juice
  • 1/4 oz simple syrup
  • Edible glitter
  • Lemon wheel for garnish

Instructions

  1. Combine gin, lime juice, and simple syrup into a cocktail shaker filled with ice.
  2. Shake until well-chilled.
  3. Strain the drink into a chilled glass.
  4. Sprinkle edible glitter to bring out the ghostly sheen.

4. Cucumber Creature Cooler

Summon forth the essence of the eerie in this “Cucumber Creature Cooler” that’ll slither its way into your guests’ glasses. The cucumber’s coolness pairs perfectly with the tartness of the gin to transport your Halloween party right into a forest of frights.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz gin
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 3/4 oz honey syrup
  • [数] oz cucumber infusion
  • Cucumber ribbon and fresh lavender for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start with a cucumber infusion by muddling two slices in the bottom of a cocktail shaker.
  2. Toss in 2 oz of gin, 1 oz fresh lemon juice, and 3/4 oz honey syrup followed by a handful of ice.
  3. Shake until well-blended and chilled.
  4. Strain into a glass filled with fresh ice.
  5. Garnish with a cucumber ribbon and sprig of lavender to bring the eerie forest to life.

5. Bloodied Bramble

Bringing the nightmare fuel to life with a “Bloodied Bramble,” this twist on the classic gin-based cocktail invokes red-tinged madness. Using blackberries and a crimson drizzle, the “Bloodied Bramble” brings a shocking splash of fright to any Halloween soirée.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz gin
  • 3/4 oz lemon juice
  • 1 1/2 oz blackberry simple syrup
  • Blackberries and red food coloring for garnish

Instructions

  1. Gently muddle blackberries in the bottom of a cocktail shaker.
  2. Add in gin, lemon juice, and blackberry simple syrup.
  3. Fill the shaker with ice and shake until well-chilled.
  4. Strain into a chilled glass, leaving behind the blackberry residue for that “bloodied” touch.
  5. For a twist, drizzle red food coloring on top, allowing it to sink into the sweet blackberry mixture.
