Chinese Cuisine Week: From Breakfast to Dinner


Chinese cuisine is renowned worldwide for its diverse flavors, rich history, and mouthwatering dishes. This week, we’ll be embarking on a culinary journey through the traditional Chinese meals, exploring authentic recipes that make up a balanced and delicious Chinese diet. From a sizzling breakfast to a sumptuous dinner, let’s dive into the fascinating world of Chinese cuisine.

Ming-Tin Dumplings (Dim Sum) –Authentic Breakfast Delight

Begin your day with a nourishing and satisfying breakfast by preparing some classic Ming-Tin dumplings, also known as dumplings from the Ming Dynasty. This renowned comfort food is not only a staple in Chinese homes but much-loved by people worldwide. Transport your taste buds to China’s bustling streets by whipping up a batch of these delightful treats.

Ingredients:

  • 200g ground pork
  • ½ cup finely chopped green onion
  • 1 small head of bok choy (chopped)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil, or to taste
  • Salt and white pepper
  • 1 package of round dumpling wrappers
  • Water for sealing

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling by combining pork, green onion, bok choy, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper in a bowl. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed.
  2. Lay out the dumpling wrappers on a flat surface and place a spoonful of filling in the center of each. Wet the edges with water, then fold the wrappers into your desired dumpling shape.
  3. Steam the dumplings for about 15 minutes or pan-fry until golden brown on the bottom, and the filling is thoroughly cooked.
  4. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as soy sauce or a chili oil mixture.

Deliciously Tangy Dan-Dan Noodles For Lunch

Inspired by the Sichuan Province’s mishmash of flavors, Dan-Dan noodles are a zesty, spicy, and satisfying dish that is perfect for lunch. Loaded with Sichuan pepper and a hearty assortment of proteins and vegetables, this dish is sure to keep you energized throughout the afternoon.

Ingredients:

  • 4 servings of fresh rice noodles (or stir-fry noodles)
  • 200g ground pork
  • 2 tbsp chili bean paste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp black vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame paste (or tahini)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch, mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorn, ground
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cups of mixed vegetables (spinach, bean sprouts, and zucchini)

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium heat. Add the pork and cook until browned. Then, add the chili bean paste and garlic, and stir-fry until fragrant.
  2. Add soy sauce, vinegar, sesame paste, cornstarch mix, and ground Sichuan peppercorn to the wok. Simmer the mixture for five minutes until it thickens.
  3. Meanwhile, cook the noodles according to package instructions, then drain. If you choose to use fresh noodles, slice them into shorter strips.
  4. Combine the noodles with the sauce and toss well to combine. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for another minute or until they are just wilted.
  5. Top the Dan-Dan noodles with sliced green onions and enjoy your fiery, tangy, and delightful lunch.

Comforting Beef and Tofu Stir-Fry –The Perfect Dinner

A well-deserved dinner calls for a bowl of tender beef and firm tofu, cooked to perfection with a tapestry of vegetables. This classic stir-fry dish combines savoury sauces, succulent beef, and silky tofu; making it an irresistible ensemble of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 300g thin beef slices
  • 3 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, and green beans)
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Green onions, sliced for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Season the beef with salt and pepper, and set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a wok or frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil and toss in the tofu cubes. Cook until golden brown on all sides.
  4. In a separate skillet, heat the remaining oil over medium-high heat. Add garlic, ginger, and mixed vegetables. Stir-fry for about three minutes until they are slightly tender.
  5. Add the soy sauce and oyster sauce to the vegetables. Then pour the cornstarch mix into the skillet and let it simmer until it thickens.
  6. Combine the cooked tofu, beef, and vegetables, stirring gently to coat everything with the sauce.
  7. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve immediately with rice for a delicious and heartwarming dinner.

The only hard part about exploring the beautiful world of Chinese cuisine is deciding what to prepare next! Each dish is filled with a memorable experience, making it nearly impossible to choose just one. But one thing’s for sure, this week’s focus on Chinese cuisine has undoubtedly shown that you cannot go wrong in the kitchen, as long as you embrace the bold and authentic flavors that China has to offer. Happy cooking!
