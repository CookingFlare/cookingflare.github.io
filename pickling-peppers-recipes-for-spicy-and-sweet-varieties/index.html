Pickling Peppers: Recipes for Spicy and Sweet Varieties


Pickling peppers is a fantastic way to preserve the vibrant flavors of these versatile vegetables. Whether you prefer a spicy kick or a hint of sweetness, pickled peppers can add a burst of flavor to any dish. In this blog post, we’ll explore different recipes for pickling peppers, offering a variety of options to suit your taste buds. So, let’s dive right in!

Spicy Pickled Peppers

If you’re a fan of heat and enjoy a spicy kick in your meals, these recipes are sure to tantalize your taste buds. Here are three different methods to pickle peppers with a fiery twist:

1. Jalapeno and Habanero Medley

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jalapeno peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup habanero peppers, sliced
  • 2 cups white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the white vinegar, water, sugar, salt, garlic cloves, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the sliced jalapeno and habanero peppers to the saucepan. Stir well to ensure the peppers are coated with the pickling liquid.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Transfer the pickled peppers and the liquid to sterilized jars, ensuring that the peppers are fully submerged.
  5. Seal the jars tightly and store them in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours before enjoying your spicy pickled peppers.

2. Sriracha Pickled Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed hot peppers (such as serrano, cayenne, and Thai chilies), sliced
  • 1 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, sugar, sriracha sauce, minced garlic, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Add the sliced hot peppers to the saucepan and cook for another 2-3 minutes, until the peppers soften slightly.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Transfer the pickled peppers and the liquid to sterilized jars, making sure the peppers are fully covered with the pickling liquid.
  5. Seal the jars tightly and refrigerate them for at least 48 hours for the flavors to develop fully.

3. Ghost Pepper Inferno

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ghost peppers, sliced
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the apple cider vinegar, white vinegar, sugar, salt, mustard seeds, coriander seeds, and cumin seeds. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Add the sliced ghost peppers to the saucepan and cook for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Transfer the pickled peppers and the liquid to sterilized jars, ensuring that the peppers are completely covered with the pickling liquid.
  5. Seal the jars tightly and store them in a cool, dark place for at least two weeks to allow the flavors to meld together. Caution: Ghost peppers are extremely hot, so handle them with care!

Sweet Pickled Peppers

If you prefer a milder, sweeter flavor, these pickled pepper recipes are perfect for you. Here are three delightful options to satisfy your sweet tooth:

1. Honey-Spiced Pickled Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed sweet peppers (such as bell peppers and banana peppers), sliced
  • 1 1/2 cups white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon whole cloves
  • 1 tablespoon whole allspice berries
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the white vinegar, honey, whole cloves, allspice berries, cinnamon stick, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the honey has dissolved.
  2. Add the sliced sweet peppers to the saucepan and cook for 3-4 minutes, until the peppers are slightly softened.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Transfer the pickled peppers and the liquid to sterilized jars, making sure the peppers are fully covered with the pickling liquid.
  5. Seal the jars tightly and refrigerate them for at least 24 hours before indulging in your honey-spiced pickled peppers.

2. Sweet and Tangy Pepper Relish

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed sweet peppers (such as red, orange, and yellow bell peppers), diced
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, combine the apple cider vinegar, white vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, celery seeds, turmeric, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Add the diced sweet peppers to the saucepan and cook for 10-15 minutes, until the peppers are tender-crisp.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Transfer the sweet and tangy pepper relish and the liquid to sterilized jars, making sure the peppers are fully covered with the pickling liquid.
  5. Seal the jars tightly and store them in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

3. Balsamic Pepper Chutney

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups mixed sweet peppers (such as bell peppers and pimento peppers), chopped
  • 1 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar, sugar, raisins, red onion, grated ginger, mustard seeds, chili flakes, and salt. Bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Add the chopped sweet peppers to the saucepan and cook for 15-20 minutes, until the peppers are soft and the chutney thickens.
  3. Remove the saucepan from heat and let it cool to room temperature.
  4. Transfer the balsamic pepper chutney and the liquid to sterilized jars, ensuring that the peppers are fully covered with the chutney.
  5. Seal the jars tightly and refrigerate them for at least 24 hours before enjoying your delightful balsamic pepper chutney.

Conclusion

Pickling peppers opens up a world of flavor possibilities, from fiery heat to delightful sweetness. Experiment with different pepper varieties and pickling methods to find your perfect combination. These recipes are just a starting point, so feel free to adjust the ingredients and spice levels to suit your taste. Get creative and enjoy the flavorful journey of pickling peppers!
