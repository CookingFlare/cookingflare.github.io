10 Easy Fall Recipes for Busy Families


As the crisp autumn air begins to settle in, the fall season brings an abundance of rich flavors, comforting aromas, and hearty ingredients perfect for busy families looking to enjoy delicious home-cooked meals. Whether it’s the sweetness of pumpkin, the earthiness of roasted root vegetables, or the warmth of spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, fall offers a wide variety of recipes that can make weeknight dinners easier and more enjoyable. These 10 fall-inspired recipes are not only simple but also versatile, making them ideal for busy families who want to bring the cozy vibes of autumn to the dinner table without spending hours in the kitchen.

1. Slow Cooker Butternut Squash Soup

Fall is the perfect time for a bowl of warm, velvety soup, and this slow-cooker butternut squash soup is both easy and comforting. The best part? It practically makes itself in the slow cooker, giving you more time to focus on other tasks.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional for extra richness)

Instructions:

  1. Place the butternut squash, onion, garlic, vegetable broth, ginger, cinnamon, salt, and pepper into the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  2. Cook on low for 6–7 hours or on high for 3–4 hours until the squash is tender.
  3. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth (or transfer to a regular blender in batches).
  4. Stir in the heavy cream for a creamier texture, and adjust the seasoning to taste before serving.

This soup is a great option for meal prepping and can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for future use. Serve with a slice of crusty bread for a complete meal.

2. One-Pan Roasted Chicken with Root Vegetables

This one-pan roasted chicken recipe is perfect for families who need a filling, nutritious meal with minimal cleanup. The combination of tender chicken and sweet root vegetables like carrots, sweet potatoes, and parsnips makes it a perfect fall dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 large sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 2 parsnips, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a large baking sheet, arrange the chicken thighs in the center. Surround them with the cubed sweet potatoes, carrots, and parsnips.
  3. Drizzle the olive oil over the chicken and vegetables. Sprinkle the thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper evenly over everything.
  4. Roast for 35-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  5. Serve the chicken and roasted vegetables together, drizzling the pan juices over the top for extra flavor.

This dish is not only easy but also highly customizable. Feel free to swap out any root vegetables for others you have on hand, like turnips or rutabaga.

3. Pumpkin Chili

Chili is a fall favorite, and this pumpkin chili recipe is a fantastic twist on a classic. Packed with protein from beans and ground turkey, this hearty dish has the added richness of pumpkin, which brings a subtly sweet flavor that pairs beautifully with chili spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the ground turkey and cook until browned, breaking it up into crumbles as it cooks.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in the pumpkin puree, kidney beans, black beans, diced tomatoes, chicken broth, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the chili to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve with your favorite chili toppings like sour cream, shredded cheese, or chopped green onions.

This chili is both filling and nutritious, making it perfect for a busy family dinner. You can also double the recipe and freeze individual portions for future meals.

4. Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal

Oatmeal is a breakfast staple, and when fall rolls around, apple cinnamon oatmeal is the perfect way to start the day. It’s quick to make, hearty, and can be customized with toppings like nuts, dried fruit, or a dollop of yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 2 cups milk or water
  • 1 apple, peeled, cored, and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the oats, milk or water, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oats are soft and the liquid is absorbed.
  3. In the last 2 minutes of cooking, add the chopped apple and cinnamon to the oats. Stir until the apples soften.
  4. Remove from heat and drizzle with maple syrup. Stir to combine, then serve.

This oatmeal is great for busy mornings and can be made in advance. Store leftovers in the fridge and reheat with a splash of milk.

5. Stuffed Acorn Squash

Acorn squash is a fall favorite, and this stuffed version is the perfect combination of savory and sweet. The filling of quinoa, cranberries, and pecans offers a hearty dish that works as a main or a side.

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squashes, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast for 35-40 minutes, or until the squash is tender.
  4. While the squash is roasting, combine the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, and maple syrup (if using) in a bowl.
  5. Once the squash is done, remove from the oven and fill each half with the quinoa mixture.
  6. Serve warm, and enjoy the delightful combination of textures and flavors.

This dish is a great way to make use of seasonal squash and offers a satisfying meal that’s both nutritious and flavorful.

6. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Tacos are always a hit, and these sweet potato and black bean tacos are a perfect fall variation. Sweet potatoes provide a comforting base, while black beans add protein and heartiness, making these tacos a filling and nutritious option for busy families.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, cilantro, lime, salsa

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the sweet potato cubes in olive oil, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and slightly crispy on the edges.
  4. Warm the tortillas in a dry skillet or the oven.
  5. Assemble the tacos by placing the roasted sweet potatoes and black beans on the tortillas. Top with avocado, cilantro, a squeeze of lime, and salsa.
  6. Serve and enjoy!

These tacos are full of flavor, and they come together quickly, making them ideal for a weeknight dinner.

7. Baked Ziti with Roasted Vegetables

Baked ziti is a classic comfort food, and this version with roasted vegetables adds a fall twist. The sweetness of roasted squash, zucchini, and bell peppers pairs wonderfully with the cheesy pasta, creating a dish that’s both satisfying and easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ziti pasta
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup roasted vegetables (such as squash, zucchini, and bell peppers)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the ziti pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta, marinara sauce, ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and roasted vegetables. Mix well.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish and top with Parmesan cheese.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Serve warm and enjoy!

This dish is a great way to incorporate more vegetables into your family’s diet while keeping things simple and delicious.

8. Maple Glazed Brussels Sprouts

Brussels sprouts are a fall favorite, and this maple glaze elevates them to a new level of deliciousness. The sweet and savory combination makes them irresistible, and they can be served as a side dish to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the Brussels sprouts are golden brown and crispy on the edges.
  5. Serve warm and enjoy the sweet and savory flavors.

This side dish is simple but bursting with flavor and a great way to enjoy fall’s vegetable offerings.

9. Crockpot Apple Crisp

Apple crisp is a quintessential fall dessert, and using a slow cooker makes it even easier to prepare. The apples become tender and juicy, while the topping becomes golden and crisp, creating the perfect dessert to enjoy after a busy day.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup butter, cubed
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Place the sliced apples in the bottom of the slow cooker.
  2. In a bowl, combine the oats, brown sugar, flour, butter, cinnamon, and salt. Use your fingers or a pastry cutter to combine the ingredients until crumbly.
  3. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples in the slow cooker.
  4. Cook on low for 3-4 hours or until the apples are tender and the topping is golden and crisp.
  5. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a perfect fall dessert.

10. Autumn Quinoa Salad

Quinoa salads are perfect for fall because they can be served warm or cold and are packed with hearty ingredients that make for a filling meal. This autumn quinoa salad combines roasted root vegetables, cranberries, and walnuts for a healthy and flavorful dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup roasted butternut squash, cubed
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted squash, cranberries, and walnuts.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, then toss to combine.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, and serve.

This salad can be served as a side dish or as a light main course, making it a versatile option for busy families.

These 10 easy fall recipes are perfect for busy families who want to enjoy delicious, comforting meals without spending all day in the kitchen. From hearty soups and stews to sweet and savory dishes, these recipes incorporate the best of fall’s flavors and can be prepared quickly, leaving you with more time to enjoy the season with your loved ones. Whether you’re looking for a simple dinner, a cozy breakfast, or a festive dessert, these recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings and keep your family full and happy throughout the season.
