How to Make a Homemade Mustard Vinaigrette


When it comes to elevating a simple salad or bringing a burst of flavor to your dishes, a homemade mustard vinaigrette is an excellent choice. Not only is it quick and easy to make, but it also adds a tangy, savory kick that complements a wide range of ingredients. Whether you’re preparing a crisp green salad, a grain bowl, or roasted vegetables, this mustard vinaigrette will take your dish to the next level.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to make a delicious homemade mustard vinaigrette, explore variations, and share some tips on how to customize it according to your taste preferences.

What is Mustard Vinaigrette?

Mustard vinaigrette is a classic French-style salad dressing that combines the sharpness of mustard with the acidity of vinegar, balanced by the richness of oil. It’s an incredibly versatile dressing that’s perfect for any occasion. The base of the dressing is made with three main ingredients: mustard, vinegar, and oil. From there, you can add sweeteners, herbs, and seasonings to create a variety of flavor profiles.

Mustard vinaigrette is most commonly used on fresh salads but can also be drizzled over roasted vegetables, used as a marinade for meats, or even as a dipping sauce for crispy bread.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of a mustard vinaigrette lies in its simplicity. To get started, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Base Vinaigrette:

  • Mustard – Dijon mustard is most commonly used in vinaigrettes for its smooth texture and mild tang. However, you can use other varieties like whole grain mustard or yellow mustard for different flavors.
  • Vinegar – Apple cider vinegar, white wine vinegar, or balsamic vinegar are all excellent choices. Each type of vinegar imparts a unique flavor, so choose one that complements your dish.
  • Olive Oil – A high-quality extra virgin olive oil is ideal for the dressing as it provides richness and depth. You can also experiment with other oils like avocado oil, depending on your preferences.
  • Salt and Pepper – These basic seasonings help to balance the acidity and enhance the flavors.

Optional Flavor Additions:

  • Honey or Maple Syrup – A small amount of honey or maple syrup can add a subtle sweetness to balance the tanginess of the mustard and vinegar.
  • Garlic – Fresh minced garlic or garlic powder can add an extra layer of depth to the vinaigrette.
  • Herbs – Fresh or dried herbs such as thyme, rosemary, oregano, or tarragon can enhance the flavor profile of the dressing.
  • Shallots or Red Onion – Finely minced shallots or red onion can introduce a mild, savory bite to the vinaigrette.
  • Lemon Juice – For an added layer of brightness, a squeeze of fresh lemon juice can work wonders.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Make Mustard Vinaigrette

1. Combine the Mustard and Vinegar

Start by placing 1 to 2 tablespoons of mustard in a mixing bowl. Dijon mustard works well here due to its smooth texture and mild heat. Add 2 tablespoons of vinegar of your choice, such as apple cider vinegar, white wine vinegar, or balsamic vinegar.

Whisk the mustard and vinegar together until they are fully combined and emulsified. The mustard acts as an emulsifier, helping the oil to blend smoothly with the vinegar.

2. Gradually Add the Oil

Next, it’s time to add the oil. Begin by slowly pouring 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil into the mustard-vinegar mixture while whisking constantly. This gradual incorporation helps the dressing emulsify, creating a smooth and silky texture. If you prefer a thicker vinaigrette, you can add more mustard to help stabilize the dressing.

If you want a more pronounced oil flavor, you can use a more flavorful oil like walnut or avocado oil. Alternatively, you can mix oils, such as using 2 tablespoons of olive oil and 2 tablespoons of avocado oil, to balance richness and flavor.

3. Season to Taste

Once the oil is fully incorporated, taste the vinaigrette. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. A pinch of salt will help to balance the acidity and bring out the flavors, while pepper will add a touch of heat and complexity.

If you like a sweeter vinaigrette, add a teaspoon of honey, maple syrup, or even a pinch of brown sugar. Whisk it in well until fully dissolved. This subtle sweetness helps to round out the tanginess of the mustard and vinegar.

4. Add Any Additional Flavorings

If you want to further enhance the flavor, consider adding minced garlic, finely chopped shallots, or a dash of fresh lemon juice. Fresh herbs like parsley, thyme, or tarragon can also be added for a fragrant touch. Herbs work wonderfully in a mustard vinaigrette, especially when paired with roasted vegetables or grilled chicken.

For a tangy twist, you might experiment with a dash of Worcestershire sauce or a small amount of grated Parmesan cheese to add umami depth.

5. Taste and Adjust

Once all the ingredients are mixed together, give the dressing another taste. Adjust the balance by adding more vinegar for acidity, more oil for richness, or a bit more mustard if you want to increase the tanginess. If the dressing is too tart, a little more honey or maple syrup can help mellow out the acidity.

Storing Your Mustard Vinaigrette

Homemade mustard vinaigrette is best when fresh, but it can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. The oil may solidify when chilled, but you can easily re-emulsify the dressing by letting it come to room temperature and giving it a good shake or whisk before using.

Variations on Mustard Vinaigrette

While the basic mustard vinaigrette is delicious on its own, you can create many variations to suit different tastes. Here are a few ideas to customize your dressing:

1. Whole Grain Mustard Vinaigrette

Swap out Dijon mustard for whole grain mustard to give your vinaigrette extra texture and a more robust flavor. Whole grain mustard has a slightly coarser consistency and a stronger tang, which makes it perfect for adding a bit of crunch and an extra punch of flavor.

2. Balsamic Mustard Vinaigrette

For a sweeter, richer twist, try using balsamic vinegar as the base for your vinaigrette. The sweet and complex notes of balsamic vinegar complement the mustard’s tanginess, making it a great option for drizzling over roasted vegetables or a Caprese salad.

3. Herb-Infused Mustard Vinaigrette

Infuse your vinaigrette with fresh or dried herbs for added flavor. Thyme, rosemary, tarragon, or oregano work well with mustard-based dressings. Simply add the herbs to the vinaigrette and let it sit for a few hours before using to allow the flavors to develop.

4. Garlic and Shallot Mustard Vinaigrette

If you love garlic and shallots, you can enhance the flavor of your mustard vinaigrette by adding finely minced garlic or shallots. These ingredients infuse the dressing with savory depth and pair well with hearty salads or roasted meats.

5. Spicy Mustard Vinaigrette

For those who love a little heat, add a small amount of hot sauce, red pepper flakes, or even a bit of freshly grated horseradish to the mustard vinaigrette. This will create a spicy vinaigrette that adds an exciting kick to your dishes.

How to Use Mustard Vinaigrette

Mustard vinaigrette is incredibly versatile and can be used in a variety of ways:

  • As a Salad Dressing: The most obvious use for mustard vinaigrette is as a salad dressing. Toss it with mixed greens, arugula, spinach, or any salad of your choice.
  • As a Marinade: Use the vinaigrette as a marinade for chicken, pork, or fish. The tangy mustard and vinegar help tenderize the meat while infusing it with flavor.
  • On Roasted Vegetables: Drizzle the vinaigrette over roasted vegetables like Brussels sprouts, carrots, or sweet potatoes. It adds a delicious contrast to the earthy flavors of the vegetables.
  • In Grain Bowls: A mustard vinaigrette pairs perfectly with grain bowls, such as quinoa or farro. It can add a refreshing tang to the hearty grains and vegetables.
  • As a Dipping Sauce: Use it as a tangy dipping sauce for crusty bread, roasted potatoes, or crispy appetizers like chicken wings or fried cauliflower.

Conclusion

Making your own mustard vinaigrette is an easy and rewarding way to enhance the flavors of your dishes. The simple combination of mustard, vinegar, and oil can be easily customized with herbs, spices, and sweeteners to suit your taste. Whether you’re looking for a tangy dressing for salads, a marinade for meats, or a dip for appetizers, mustard vinaigrette is a must-have in your culinary arsenal. By following this simple recipe and experimenting with variations, you’ll create a dressing that brings bold and vibrant flavors to every meal.

So the next time you’re preparing a salad or looking for a quick way to dress up your meal, reach for this easy-to-make homemade mustard vinaigrette, and let its tangy, savory goodness work its magic.
