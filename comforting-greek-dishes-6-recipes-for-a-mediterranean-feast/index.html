Comforting Greek Dishes: 6 Recipes for a Mediterranean Feast


When the sun sets, and the temperature drops, you crave comfort food that warms your heart and belly. For those times, there's no better destination than the Mediterranean. Greece, in particular, is renowned for its delicious, comforting dishes that transport you to a world of warm summers, bustling markets, and genuine hospitality. In this post, we will explore six mouth-watering recipes that showcase the rich flavors and ingredients of Greek cuisine. Olive Oil Herb Roasted Chicken Gather the ingredients:

1 whole chicken (2-3 lbs) 1/2 cup olive oil 4 cloves of garlic, minced 1 tsp dried oregano 1 tsp dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste 2 lemons, sliced 1 cup of mixed herbs (such as rosemary and thyme)

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper. Rub the marinade all over the chicken, then place it in a roasting pan. Roast for 45-50 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. After that, garnish with a skewered lemon slice and herbs. The aroma of roasting herbs will instantly transport you to a Greek household where food and family take precedence. Spanakopita: A Harvest Feta Delight Ingredients for the Filling:

1 package frozen spinach, thawed and drained 1 cup crumbled feta cheese 1/2 medium onion, finely chopped 4 cloves of garlic, minced 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1 tsp nutmeg 1 tsp salt 1/4 tsp black pepper 2 tbsp olive oil

Ingredients for the Pastry:

1 package filo pastry, thawed 1/4 cup olive oil

For the filling, combine the spinach, feta, onion, garlic, parsley, nutmeg, salt, and pepper in a bowl. For the pastry, brush the first sheet of filo with olive oil. Spoon a quarter of the spinach mixture along the sheet's length and roll it up. Repeat with the remaining sheets. Brush the entire pastry with olive oil and bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 35-40 minutes or until golden. Gigantes: Creamy Eggplant and Lentil Stew Ingredients:

2 eggplants, cut into 1 inch cubes 2 medium onions, chopped 4 cloves of garlic, minced 2 cups cooked lentils 1 can of diced tomatoes, 14.5 oz 2 cups vegetable broth 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme 1 tsp cumin 1 tsp smoked paprika Salt and pepper to taste 2 tbsp olive oil 2 tbsp tomato paste

Saute the eggplant and onion in olive oil. Then, add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Stir in thyme, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the lentils, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes. Finally, stir in tomato paste for a depth of flavor. Stifado: Braised Beef with Onions and Port Ingredients:

2 lbs beef, cut into 1-inch cubes 4 cloves of garlic, minced 2 medium onions, thinly sliced 2 tbsp tomato paste 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1 tsp ground allspice 1 tsp ground cloves 1 tsp cayenne pepper Salt and pepper to taste 2 tbsp olive oil

Pour olive oil in a Dutch oven and saute the onions until golden brown. Add the garlic and saute for an extra minute. Add the beef, tomato paste, cinnamon, allspice, cloves, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Mix well and let it braise for 2-3 hours or until the beef is tender. Kourambiedes: Caramelized Almond Cookies Ingredients:

1 cup confectioner's sugar 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened 1/2 cup ground almonds 1/2 tsp baking powder 1/2 tsp salt 2 egg yolks 1 vanilla extract

Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Whisk together confectioner's sugar and ground almonds in a bowl. Then add the softened butter until crumbly. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks and vanilla extract. Add the egg yolk mixture to the dry mixture and mix until combined. Scoop spoonfuls of the dough into a greased baking sheet and flatten with a fork. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until caramelized. Greek Yogurt Cheesecake with Lemon Syrup Ingredients for the Crust:

1 cup graham cracker crumbs 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

Ingredients for the Filling:

16 oz Greek yogurt 12 oz cream cheese, softened 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 large eggs 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the crust, combine graham cracker crumbs, granulated sugar, and melted butter. Press the mixture into a 9 inch springform pan. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until lightly browned. For the filling, beat the Greek yogurt, cream cheese, sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract in a bowl. Pour the mixture into the prepared crust and bake for 45-50 minutes or until set. Let it cool completely, then drizzle with lemon syrup and refrigerate for 2 hours. The recipes in this post evoke the very essence of Greece: its rich flavors, color, and authenticity. Comforting Greek dishes evoke memories of grandparents, Sunday lunches, and school holidays that are steeped in taste. Add these recipes to your collection and I assure you, you'll be whisked away to a culinary world where love, hospitality, and warmth are alive and tangible.
