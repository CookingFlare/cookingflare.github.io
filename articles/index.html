Classic Beef and Vegetable Soup: Nourishing and Delicious

As the seasons change, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of soup to nourish both body and soul. Among the many varieties of soup, classic beef and vegetable soup is a timeless favorite that never goes out of style. In this recipe, we’ll explore the rich flavors and textures that make this soup a staple in many households. Ingredients 1 pound beef stew meat (such as chuck or round) 2 medium onions, chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped 2 medium potatoes, peeled and chopped 1 large celery stalk, chopped 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes 4 cups beef broth 1 teaspoon dried thyme 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 2 tablespoons butter Instructions Brown the Beef: In a large Dutch oven or pot, heat 1 tablespoon of butter over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove the browned beef from the pot and set it aside. Soften the Onions: Reduce the heat to medium and add the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter to the pot. Add the chopped onions and cook until they’re softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the Aromatics: Add the minced garlic, chopped carrots, chopped potatoes, and chopped celery to the pot. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are slightly tender. Add the Tomato Paste and Broth: Add the canned diced tomatoes, beef broth, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir to combine, then bring the mixture to a boil. Return the Beef: Add the browned beef back to the pot, making sure it’s covered with the soup mixture. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, covered, for 2 1/2 hours, or until the beef is tender. Thicken the Soup: In a small bowl, mix the flour and 2 tablespoons of cold water until smooth. Stir the flour mixture into the soup and continue to simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the soup has thickened to your liking. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh herbs or a sprinkle of paprika, if desired. Tips and Variations For a heartier soup, add 1 cup of cooked noodles, such as egg noodles or macaroni, to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking. To add some extra flavor, sauté 1/4 cup of chopped mushrooms or 1/4 cup of chopped bell peppers along with the onions and garlic. For a clearer broth, skim off any excess fat that rises to the surface during cooking. Conclusion Classic beef and vegetable soup is a comforting and nourishing dish that’s perfect for any occasion. With its rich flavors and tender texture, it’s a recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your household. Whether you’re looking for a warm and comforting meal or a delicious and satisfying snack, this soup is sure to hit the spot. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and get cooking! ...
