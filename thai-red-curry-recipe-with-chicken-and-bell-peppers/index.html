Thai Red Curry Recipe with Chicken and Bell Peppers


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this delectable Thai Red Curry recipe featuring tender chicken and vibrant bell peppers. This rich and aromatic dish is a perfect balance of flavors, combining the creaminess of coconut milk with the warmth of red curry paste and the freshness of bell peppers. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a cooking novice, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Start by slicing the chicken breasts, bell peppers, and onion thinly to ensure quick and even cooking. Also, have your coconut milk, chicken broth, red curry paste, and other ingredients measured and ready to go.

  2. Sear the Chicken: In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 3-4 minutes. Once the chicken is cooked through, remove it from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. Create the Flavor Base: In the same skillet, add the red curry paste and cook for about a minute to release its aromatic flavors. Then, slowly pour in the coconut milk while stirring to combine it with the curry paste.

  4. Simmer with Aromatics: Once the coconut milk and curry paste are well combined, add the chicken broth, fish sauce, and brown sugar. Stir the mixture well and let it simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Add Vegetables and Chicken: After the curry base has simmered, add the sliced bell peppers, onion, and the seared chicken back into the skillet. Stir everything together and let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and the chicken is fully cooked.

  6. Finish with Fresh Basil: Just before serving, stir in the fresh basil leaves for a burst of herbal aroma and flavor.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the Thai red curry over a bed of cooked jasmine rice, allowing the fragrant sauce to soak into the rice for a truly satisfying experience.

Tips for Success

  • Customize the Heat: Adjust the amount of red curry paste to suit your preferred level of spiciness. You can also include a few slices of red chili for an extra kick.

  • Vegetarian Option: For a vegetarian twist, substitute the chicken with tofu and the chicken broth with vegetable broth.

  • Prep Ahead: This dish can be prepped ahead of time, making it a convenient option for busy weeknights or entertaining guests.

  • Storage and Reheating: Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply gently warm the curry in a saucepan over medium heat, adding a splash of water or broth if needed to maintain the desired consistency.

Final Thoughts

This Thai Red Curry recipe with chicken and bell peppers is a delightful way to bring the exotic flavors of Thailand into your home kitchen. With its vibrant colors, aromatic spices, and delightful creaminess, this dish is a true crowd-pleaser that will leave everyone asking for seconds. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary journey to Thailand right in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
