5 Easy Rice Desserts You Can Make at Home


Rice isn’t just for savory dishes – it’s also the perfect ingredient for a variety of sweet treats. With its neutral flavor and versatile texture, rice can easily transform into delicious, comforting desserts. Whether you’re craving something creamy, fruity, or spiced, there’s a rice-based dessert that can satisfy your sweet tooth. Here are five easy and delightful rice desserts you can make at home.

1. Rice Pudding

Rice pudding is one of the most beloved rice-based desserts around the world. This creamy, comforting treat is perfect for chilly evenings or when you need a simple yet satisfying dessert. There are many variations of rice pudding, but the classic version is simple to make and always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of short-grain rice (or arborio rice)
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water to remove excess starch. This will help the pudding achieve a creamy texture.
  2. In a large saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, milk, sugar, and salt. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring frequently to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom.
  3. Reduce the heat to low once the milk begins to simmer. Let the rice cook for about 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally until the rice is soft and the mixture has thickened to a creamy consistency.
  4. Once the rice pudding reaches your desired consistency, remove the saucepan from the heat. Stir in the vanilla extract and spices (if using).
  5. Serve the rice pudding warm or chilled. Top it with a sprinkle of cinnamon or some fresh fruit for an extra burst of flavor.

Rice pudding is not only easy to make but also a great dessert to enjoy with family and friends. The comforting combination of creamy milk and soft rice will have everyone coming back for seconds.

2. Coconut Rice Pudding

If you’re a fan of tropical flavors, coconut rice pudding is a must-try! By replacing regular milk with coconut milk, you give this dessert a rich and exotic twist. The creamy texture, combined with the subtle sweetness of coconut, makes this rice pudding irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of jasmine rice (or any medium-grain rice)
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • Fresh fruit or toasted coconut flakes (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water to remove excess starch.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice, coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. Once the coconut milk is simmering, reduce the heat to low and let the rice cook for about 20-25 minutes, stirring often to ensure it doesn’t stick.
  4. When the rice is fully cooked and the pudding has thickened, remove it from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Serve the coconut rice pudding warm or chilled. Garnish with fresh fruit like mango or pineapple, or sprinkle some toasted coconut flakes on top for added texture and flavor.

This coconut rice pudding is a great dessert for those who love rich, creamy treats with a tropical flair. The coconut milk makes it a unique variation of the classic rice pudding.

3. Rice Krispies Treats

Rice Krispies treats are a childhood favorite, and they’re incredibly easy to make. While they’re typically made with Rice Krispies cereal, you can also make them with cooked rice for a chewy and crispy dessert. These treats are perfect for a quick snack or for parties and celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of cooked rice (preferably short-grain or medium-grain)
  • 4 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 bag (10 oz) of marshmallows
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preparing the cooked rice if you don’t already have some on hand. Use freshly cooked rice and let it cool for a few minutes before starting the recipe.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the marshmallows and stir until they are completely melted and smooth.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt.
  4. Gradually add the cooked rice to the melted marshmallow mixture. Stir carefully to coat all the rice evenly with the marshmallow mixture.
  5. Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan with butter or cooking spray, then press the rice mixture into the pan, making sure it’s spread evenly.
  6. Allow the Rice Krispies treats to cool for about 30 minutes before cutting them into squares.

These homemade rice-based treats are crispy, chewy, and sweet, making them a fantastic dessert for kids and adults alike. Feel free to customize them by adding chocolate chips, dried fruits, or nuts to suit your preferences.

4. Sweet Rice with Mango (Khao Niew Mamuang)

This Thai-inspired dessert, also known as “Khao Niew Mamuang,” is a popular treat in Southeast Asia. It pairs sticky rice with ripe, juicy mango and a rich coconut sauce, creating a balanced dessert that’s sweet, creamy, and utterly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of glutinous rice (sticky rice)
  • 1 can (14 oz) of coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • A pinch of salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes (peeled and sliced)
  • Sesame seeds or mung beans (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Begin by soaking the sticky rice in water for at least 1 hour, or overnight if you have the time. This will help the rice become soft and sticky when cooked.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and place it in a steamer basket lined with cheesecloth. Steam the rice over boiling water for about 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and tender.
  3. While the rice is steaming, prepare the coconut sauce. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Heat over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves. Do not bring it to a boil – you just want to warm it through.
  4. Once the rice is done, transfer it to a bowl and pour the coconut milk mixture over it. Stir gently to ensure the rice absorbs the creamy coconut sauce.
  5. To serve, place a portion of the coconut sticky rice on a plate, then top it with sliced mango. Garnish with sesame seeds or mung beans for a bit of crunch.

This sweet rice with mango is a refreshing and exotic dessert that combines the tropical flavors of coconut and mango. It’s a perfect way to enjoy the delicious sweetness of ripe fruit with a creamy base.

5. Rice Flour Cake

Rice flour isn’t just for savory dishes; it can also be used to make a wonderfully soft and spongy cake. Rice flour cakes are popular in many cultures, including in Chinese and Japanese cuisines. This simple recipe yields a light, fluffy cake that can be enjoyed with tea or as a dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of rice flour
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and then add the vegetable oil, milk, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until you have a smooth batter.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  6. Bake the cake for about 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the cake to cool before removing it from the pan. You can enjoy it as is or dust it with powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness.

Rice flour cake has a unique texture and is a great option for those who prefer gluten-free desserts. Its simple flavor pairs well with a variety of toppings, such as whipped cream, berries, or even a drizzle of honey.

These five easy rice-based desserts are perfect for anyone looking to add a little sweetness to their day. From comforting rice pudding to tropical sticky rice with mango, there’s something for every taste. Give them a try and discover how versatile rice can be in the world of desserts!
