Authentic Instant Pot Butter Chicken Recipe for Indian Cuisine Lovers


Butter chicken, a popular Indian dish, is a staple in many Indian restaurants and households. The rich, creamy tomato sauce infused with butter, spices, and herbs, served with tender and juicy chicken, is a treat for the taste buds. In this recipe, we will show you how to make authentic butter chicken using an Instant Pot, a pressure cooker that simplifies the cooking process and helps to retain the flavors and nutrients.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons tomato puree
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

To start, heat the Instant Pot by pressing the “Saute” button. Once the pot is hot, add the ghee or oil and let it melt. Add the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and cayenne pepper (if using) and let them sizzle for a few seconds.

Add the diced onion and sauté until it’s translucent and starting to brown. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute, until fragrant.

Add the chicken pieces and cook until they are browned on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside.

Add the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, heavy cream, butter, tomato puree, garam masala powder, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine.

Return the chicken to the pot and close the lid. Make sure the valve is set to “Sealing”. Press the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” button and set the cooking time to 10 minutes at high pressure.

When the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.

Open the lid and check that the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened. If the sauce is too thin, press the “Saute” button and simmer it for a few minutes until it reaches the desired consistency.

Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with basmati rice or naan bread.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the dish more authentic, use a mixture of spices like cumin, coriander, garam masala, and cayenne pepper to give it a distinct Indian flavor.
  • Adjust the amount of cayenne pepper to suit your desired level of spiciness.
  • Use Greek yogurt or sour cream instead of heavy cream for a lighter version of the sauce.
  • Add some potatoes, bell peppers, or cauliflower to make it a hearty and filling meal.
  • Experiment with different types of chicken, such as bone-in or boneless, or try using turkey or shrimp for a variation.

Conclusion

Butter chicken is a classic Indian dish that is easy to make and delicious to eat. By using an Instant Pot, you can simplify the cooking process and retain the flavors and nutrients of the dish. This recipe is a great starting point for those who want to try their hand at making authentic Indian cuisine at home. With its rich, creamy sauce and tender chicken, this dish is sure to become a favorite in your household.
