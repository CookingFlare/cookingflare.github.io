10 Delicious Easter Recipes for a Memorable Feast


Easter is a time for family gatherings, springtime celebrations, and, of course, an unforgettable feast. If you’re planning a special meal this Easter, you’ll want a selection of dishes that are both delicious and seasonal. From traditional classics to modern twists, these 10 Easter recipes will help you create a memorable spread that will have your guests talking long after the last bite.

1. Honey Glazed Ham

No Easter feast is complete without a show-stopping centerpiece, and a honey glazed ham fits the bill perfectly. The sweetness of honey, combined with a tangy hint of mustard and a touch of brown sugar, creates a caramelized crust that’s absolutely irresistible. This dish is easy to prepare, yet it looks like something you spent all day on.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in ham (6-8 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • Whole cloves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern and stud with cloves.
  3. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine honey, Dijon mustard, brown sugar, and apple cider vinegar. Stir until the sugar dissolves and the glaze thickens.
  4. Place the ham on a roasting rack and brush generously with the glaze.
  5. Roast the ham for about 1.5-2 hours, basting every 30 minutes until the ham reaches an internal temperature of 140°F (60°C).
  6. Once done, let the ham rest for 10-15 minutes before carving and serving.

2. Classic Deviled Eggs

Deviled eggs are a staple at Easter brunches and family gatherings. They’re simple to make but incredibly satisfying. The creamy yolk filling, with a touch of mustard and paprika, provides the perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Hard boil the eggs by placing them in a pot of water, bringing it to a boil, then reducing the heat and simmering for 10 minutes. Let the eggs cool and peel them.
  2. Cut the eggs in half lengthwise and scoop the yolks into a bowl.
  3. Mash the yolks and mix in mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  4. Spoon or pipe the yolk mixture back into the egg whites.
  5. Sprinkle with paprika for garnish and serve chilled.

3. Roasted Spring Vegetables

Spring vegetables are at their peak during Easter, making them the perfect side dish. Roasted asparagus, carrots, and baby potatoes, with a drizzle of olive oil and fresh herbs, will add color and flavor to your spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 4-5 medium carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 1 pound baby potatoes, halved
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the vegetables in olive oil, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 25-30 minutes, turning halfway through, until tender and golden brown.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh herbs.

4. Garlic Parmesan Crusted Lamb Chops

Lamb is a traditional Easter protein, and these garlic Parmesan crusted lamb chops are a decadent treat. The rich flavor of the lamb is perfectly complemented by the crispy, savory crust.

Ingredients:

  • 8 lamb chops
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Mix the Parmesan, garlic, rosemary, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  3. Coat the lamb chops with the Parmesan mixture, pressing down to form a crust.
  4. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and sear the lamb chops for 2-3 minutes per side until browned.
  5. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 10-12 minutes for medium-rare, or longer for your desired doneness.
  6. Let the lamb rest for a few minutes before serving.

5. Easter-Specific Hot Cross Buns

These soft, spiced buns are a delightful treat for Easter morning. Topped with a cross made of icing, hot cross buns are both symbolic and delicious. Serve them warm for an irresistible breakfast or dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast
  • 1 cup milk, warmed
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup currants or raisins
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar (for icing)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and yeast.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the warm milk, butter, eggs, and salt.
  3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and knead the dough until smooth.
  4. Mix in the currants or raisins and let the dough rise for about 1-2 hours.
  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Divide the dough into 12 equal portions and shape them into buns. Place them on a baking sheet and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  7. Bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown.
  8. For the icing, mix confectioners’ sugar with a small amount of water and pipe a cross over each bun.
  9. Serve warm for a perfect Easter breakfast.

6. Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is a beloved Easter dessert. The combination of moist cake and tangy cream cheese frosting makes it a perfect choice for your holiday celebration. Top it with walnuts for added texture and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups finely grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. In another bowl, whisk eggs, oil, sugar, and vanilla until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until smooth.
  5. Stir in the grated carrots and chopped walnuts.
  6. Pour the batter into greased and floured cake pans and bake for 30-35 minutes.
  7. While the cake is cooling, prepare the frosting by beating cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla together until smooth.
  8. Once the cake has cooled, frost with the cream cheese mixture and decorate with extra walnuts if desired.

7. Spring Pea Risotto

A creamy and vibrant spring pea risotto is a wonderful side dish for Easter. The fresh peas add a pop of color and sweetness, balancing the richness of the creamy rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 cup fresh peas (or frozen, thawed)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the broth over low heat.
  2. In a large pan, melt the butter and sauté the onion and garlic until soft.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Pour in the wine and let it absorb, stirring frequently.
  5. Gradually add the warm broth, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing the liquid to absorb before adding more.
  6. Once the rice is tender and creamy (about 18-20 minutes), stir in the peas and Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve immediately as a vibrant side to any Easter main course.

8. Lemon Blueberry Cheesecake Bars

These lemon blueberry cheesecake bars are a refreshing and tangy dessert, perfect for an Easter gathering. The zesty lemon and sweet blueberries complement the creamy cheesecake filling beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup blueberry jam or fresh blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter in a bowl. Press the mixture into the bottom of a baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the cream cheese, sour cream, and sugar until smooth.
  4. Add the eggs, lemon zest, and lemon juice, mixing until fully incorporated.
  5. Pour the cheesecake mixture over the crust and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until set.
  6. Let the bars cool completely, then top with blueberry jam or fresh blueberries.
  7. Chill for at least 2 hours before serving.

9. Asparagus and Prosciutto Bundles

These elegant asparagus and prosciutto bundles are a quick and delicious side dish that will impress your guests. The salty prosciutto pairs wonderfully with the tender asparagus for a savory treat.

Ingredients:

  • 12 asparagus spears, trimmed
  • 6 slices prosciutto
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Divide the asparagus into bundles of 2-3 spears each.
  3. Wrap each bundle with a slice of prosciutto and place on a baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Roast for 10-15 minutes until the asparagus is tender and the prosciutto is crispy.

10. Mango and Avocado Salad

This bright and refreshing mango and avocado salad is a perfect addition to your Easter meal. The creamy avocado and sweet mango create a vibrant combination that pairs wonderfully with your main courses.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 large mango, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the diced avocado, mango, red onion, and cilantro.
  2. Drizzle with lime juice and gently toss to combine.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve chilled for a refreshing and colorful side dish.

These 10 delicious Easter recipes will bring a mix of traditional flavors and modern twists to your holiday table. Whether you’re making a show-stopping ham, creamy risotto, or decadent desserts, these dishes are sure to make your Easter feast unforgettable.
