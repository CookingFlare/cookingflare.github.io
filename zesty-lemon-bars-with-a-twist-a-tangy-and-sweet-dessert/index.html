Zesty Lemon Bars with a Twist: A Tangy and Sweet Dessert


If you’re a fan of tangy and sweet desserts, then you’re in for a treat! These zesty lemon bars with a twist are the perfect balance of tartness and sweetness, making them a delightful treat for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a summer party or simply craving a refreshing dessert, these lemon bars will surely satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup of powdered sugar
  • ¾ cup of unsalted butter, softened

For the filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups of granulated sugar
  • ½ cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon zest
  • ¼ cup of all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon of baking powder
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper.

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and powdered sugar for the crust. Cut in the softened butter using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Press the crust mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake for about 15 minutes or until lightly golden brown. Remove from the oven and set aside.

  4. In another mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and granulated sugar until well combined and slightly frothy. Add in the freshly squeezed lemon juice and lemon zest, mixing until smooth.

  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry mixture to the wet mixture, whisking until well incorporated and no lumps remain.

  6. Pour the lemon filling over the pre-baked crust, spreading it evenly with a spatula. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.

  7. Once done, remove the lemon bars from the oven and allow them to cool completely in the pan. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the bars to firm up.

  8. When ready to serve, remove the lemon bars from the refrigerator and cut them into squares. Dust with powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra twist, you can add a layer of raspberry jam on top of the pre-baked crust before pouring the lemon filling. This will create a delicious raspberry lemon combo that’s sure to impress your guests.

  • If you prefer a more intense lemon flavor, you can add a few drops of lemon extract to the filling mixture. This will give the bars an extra zing!

  • To make the bars even more indulgent, you can drizzle them with a homemade lemon glaze. Simply mix powdered sugar with freshly squeezed lemon juice until you achieve a smooth and pourable consistency. Drizzle over the cooled bars before serving.

  • These lemon bars can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. However, they are best enjoyed within the first couple of days for optimal freshness.

So there you have it – zesty lemon bars with a twist that are sure to brighten up any dessert table. The combination of tangy lemon and sweet crust is simply irresistible. Give this recipe a try and let your taste buds dance with joy! Enjoy!
