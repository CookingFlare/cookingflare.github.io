Farmhouse Flavors: Rustic Beer Pairings with Farm Fresh Ingredients


Ever wanted to elevate your home brewing game with the best brews and farm-to-table cuisine? Pairing beer with fresh farmhouse ingredients is an ideal way to enjoy mouthwatering flavors and delicate aromas. Here’s a roundup of traditional favorites and innovative blends for a rustic yet delicious beer and food experience.

Rustic Pairings for your Brewery

Beer-Soaked Bread

It doesn’t get more down-home than beer-soaked bread. For a rustic twist, try this recipe featuring fresh Farmhouse Ale or Lambic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup artisan farmhouse ale or lambic
  • 2 slices of bread, crusts removed
  • 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup of fresh apple slices, peeled and cored
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
  • 1/2 cup of mixed nuts
  • Cooking spray

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a shallow dish, combine ale or lambic, melted butter, and cinnamon. Soak bread slices with the mixture, and place them into a greased baking dish.
  3. In a small bowl, combine honey and thyme. Drizzle over the apple slices and toss to coat.
  4. Spread the apple slices over the bread slices, and then sprinkle nuts over the top.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes or until the bread is golden brown and the apples are tender. Serve warm.

Beer-Can Chicken

Beer-Can chicken makes a scrumptious centerpiece for your farmhouse-themed meal. This classic barbecue recipe brings together herbs, spices, beer, and a whole chicken for an unforgettable taste experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 1 can of Farmhouse Ale or Pilsner
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped herbs (parsley, cilantro, or dill)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Remove the chicken’s insides and rinse it under cold water. Pat dry with paper towels.
  3. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  4. Rub the mixture all over the chicken, taking care to generously season the inside of the bird.
  5. Pour the Farmhouse Ale or Pilsner into the center of a beer can, and then add a teaspoon or two of your favorite barbecue sauce.
  6. Place the can in a shallow dish and lay the chicken atop the can on the grill.
  7. Cover the grill, and let it cook for 30-50 minutes, or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 180°F.
  8. After removing the chicken from the grill, let it rest for about ten minutes before carving. Garnish with herbs for an aromatic finish.

Cheese and Brew Fondue

Fondue is a delightful way to share flavors while enjoying time with friends and family. This hearty, zesty recipe pairs Farmhouse cheese with your favorite seasonal brew for a creamy, savory dip perfect for dunking crusty bread or your choice of vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound Farmhouse cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 cup of brewing ale or lager
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • Chopped herbs ( parsley, chives, dill )
  • Garlic bread or vegetable slices

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the shredded farmhouse cheese with the flour and cornstarch. Set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the brewing ale or lager with the heavy cream over medium heat.
  3. Slowly whisk in the shredded cheese mixture, stirring continuously, until the dip thickens and reaches a gentle simmer.
  4. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Transfer the cheese dip to a fondue pot and melt 2 tablespoons of butter in the pot. Stir the melted butter into the dip.
  6. Serve the cheese dip with sides of garlic bread or vegetable slices. Dip your choice of bread or vegetables into the fondue and enjoy!

Explore the World of Beer and Farm-to-Table Pairings

With this collection of rustic beer pairings using farm fresh ingredients, your next gathering or family dinner will be a satisfying taste adventure. Whether you enjoy beer-soaked bread, beer-can chicken, or a delicious cheese fondue, you’ll appreciate the unmistakable charm of farm-to-table flavor combinations.
