Savory Pumpkin Ricotta Pasta for Fall Comfort


As the cool breeze of autumn sweeps in, bringing with it vibrant leaves and the scent of crisp air, we start to crave warm, comforting meals that fill both our bellies and our hearts. There’s something magical about fall dishes—rich in flavor, cozy, and satisfying. One of the standout ingredients that embodies the essence of the season is pumpkin. From soups to pies, pumpkin adds a touch of sweetness and earthiness to any dish, but have you ever considered incorporating it into pasta?

This savory pumpkin ricotta pasta recipe combines the creamy richness of ricotta with the natural sweetness of pumpkin, creating a comforting meal that’s perfect for chilly fall evenings. Whether you’re hosting a cozy dinner party, meal prepping for the week, or simply indulging in a homemade treat, this dish is sure to become a fall favorite.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the steps of this delicious pumpkin ricotta pasta, let’s take a look at what you’ll need to make it happen. You might be surprised by how simple and accessible the ingredients are, yet the resulting dish is extraordinary.

For the Pumpkin Ricotta Sauce:

  • 1 cup of pureed pumpkin (freshly roasted or canned)
  • 1/2 cup of ricotta cheese (whole milk or part-skim for a lighter option)
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil (for sautéing)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth (or chicken broth for added depth)
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese (optional for extra flavor)
  • Fresh thyme leaves (for garnish)

For the Pasta:

  • 12 oz of pasta (rigatoni, penne, or fettuccine works beautifully)
  • Salt (for boiling the pasta)

Optional Toppings:

  • Toasted pumpkin seeds
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Extra Parmesan for sprinkling

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Pumpkin Puree

If you’re using fresh pumpkin, start by roasting it. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut a small pumpkin in half, scoop out the seeds, and place the halves face down on a baking sheet. Roast for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the flesh is soft and easily pierced with a fork. Once done, scoop out the flesh and mash it with a fork or blend it in a food processor until smooth.

For a quicker option, you can use canned pureed pumpkin. Just make sure it’s 100% pure pumpkin and not pumpkin pie filling, which contains added sugars and spices.

Step 2: Cooking the Pasta

While the pumpkin is roasting, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add your pasta of choice and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about a cup of pasta water before draining, as it will be useful for thinning the sauce later.

Once the pasta is cooked, drain and set it aside. Do not rinse the pasta, as the starch will help the sauce adhere to it.

Step 3: Making the Pumpkin Ricotta Sauce

Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until it becomes translucent and soft. The sweetness of the onion will complement the pumpkin, adding depth to the sauce.

Next, add the minced garlic and sauté for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Garlic brings an aromatic warmth to the dish that pairs beautifully with the earthy pumpkin.

Add the pureed pumpkin to the skillet, followed by the ricotta cheese. Stir well to combine, creating a creamy, luscious sauce. The ricotta adds a velvety richness to the pumpkin, making the sauce incredibly smooth and comforting.

Season the sauce with cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. These spices are quintessential to fall and enhance the natural sweetness of the pumpkin. Stir in the vegetable broth to loosen the sauce to your desired consistency—if you like it thicker, use less broth, and if you prefer a lighter sauce, add a bit more.

Let the sauce simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. If you feel the sauce needs a little extra richness, add a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese at this stage.

Step 4: Combining the Pasta and Sauce

Once the sauce is ready, add your cooked pasta to the skillet and toss it gently, ensuring every piece is coated with the creamy pumpkin ricotta sauce. If the sauce is too thick, gradually add the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, until the sauce reaches your preferred consistency.

At this point, give everything a final taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you’d like to add a bit of brightness to the dish, a small squeeze of lemon juice can elevate the flavors and provide a subtle contrast to the richness of the sauce.

Step 5: Serving the Dish

To serve, plate your pasta and garnish it with fresh thyme leaves, which add an aromatic touch, and toasted pumpkin seeds for a delightful crunch. If you love cheese, feel free to sprinkle extra Parmesan over the top, along with a few freshly cracked black peppercorns.

This savory pumpkin ricotta pasta pairs wonderfully with a light green salad, perhaps with a tangy vinaigrette, to balance the richness of the dish. A glass of crisp white wine, such as Chardonnay or Sauvignon Blanc, would also be an excellent complement.

Why This Pumpkin Ricotta Pasta is Perfect for Fall

Fall is synonymous with comfort food. As the days grow shorter and the temperatures drop, we long for meals that provide warmth and nourishment. Pumpkin, one of the season’s signature ingredients, naturally embodies these qualities. When combined with the creamy texture of ricotta cheese, the result is a rich, indulgent sauce that coats the pasta in a way that feels like a warm hug on a cool evening.

Additionally, the blend of fall spices like cinnamon and nutmeg brings a cozy, aromatic quality to the dish that’s irresistible. The simplicity of the ingredients ensures that the pumpkin is the star of the show, while the other components, like the ricotta and Parmesan, add just the right amount of creaminess and depth.

Another bonus is that this recipe is versatile. You can make it your own by adding other seasonal ingredients like roasted squash, spinach, or even crumbled sausage for a heartier meal. The beauty of this dish lies in its adaptability, allowing you to make it as simple or as complex as you prefer.

Tips for the Best Savory Pumpkin Ricotta Pasta

  • Use Fresh Pumpkin: If you have the time, roasting fresh pumpkin will yield the best flavor. The natural sweetness and richness of freshly roasted pumpkin make the sauce taste even more vibrant.

  • Creamy Ricotta: Ricotta is key to the creamy texture of the sauce, but make sure you’re using a good quality ricotta. Opt for whole milk ricotta for the richest flavor and creaminess.

  • Adjust the Seasoning: Taste as you go and feel free to adjust the seasoning to your liking. Add a pinch of chili flakes if you prefer a bit of heat or a touch of honey for extra sweetness.

  • Add Protein: For a more filling dish, consider adding cooked chicken, turkey, or even crispy pancetta to the pasta. The saltiness of the meat pairs wonderfully with the sweet pumpkin.

  • Garnish for Texture: The addition of toasted pumpkin seeds or crushed walnuts on top adds a satisfying crunch that contrasts nicely with the creamy sauce.

Final Thoughts

Savory pumpkin ricotta pasta is a celebration of fall flavors, combining the natural sweetness of pumpkin with the richness of ricotta and the warmth of seasonal spices. Whether you’re making it for a cozy weeknight dinner or a festive gathering, this dish is sure to become a beloved fall favorite.

The beauty of this recipe lies in its simplicity—it’s easy to make, but the result is an indulgent, comforting meal that feels special. So, the next time you’re craving something hearty and seasonal, give this savory pumpkin ricotta pasta a try. It’s the perfect way to embrace the flavors of autumn and enjoy the cozy comfort that fall cuisine is all about.
