Korean Spicy Braised Tofu: A Fiery and Flavorful Vegan Delight


Are you ready to embark on a culinary adventure that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more? Look no further than Korean Spicy Braised Tofu. Bursting with bold flavors and packed with plant-based goodness, this dish is a vegan delight that will impress even the most discerning palates. Get ready to experience a fiery and flavorful journey through Korean cuisine!

Ingredients

To create this mouthwatering dish, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Sesame seeds and chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tofu. Drain the tofu and gently press it between paper towels or kitchen towels to remove excess moisture. Cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes and set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the red bell pepper and chopped green onions to the skillet, and continue to cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, sesame oil, and vegetable broth until well combined. Pour the sauce mixture into the skillet and stir to coat the vegetables evenly.

  5. Gently add the tofu cubes to the skillet, making sure they are submerged in the sauce. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the tofu simmer in the spicy sauce for about 15-20 minutes, or until the tofu absorbs the flavors and becomes tender.

  6. Once the tofu is cooked, remove the skillet from the heat and let it cool slightly. Serve the Korean Spicy Braised Tofu over steamed rice or enjoy it on its own. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an extra burst of flavor and visual appeal.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a milder version of this dish, you can reduce the amount of gochujang or substitute it with a milder chili paste.
  • Feel free to experiment with different vegetables such as mushrooms, zucchini, or carrots to add more texture and color to the dish.
  • For an extra protein boost, you can add some cooked chickpeas or edamame to the skillet along with the tofu.
  • If you like your food extra spicy, you can sprinkle some additional red pepper flakes or drizzle some hot sauce over the finished dish.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Braised Tofu is a delightful fusion of fiery flavors and wholesome ingredients. It showcases the versatility of tofu and the vibrant essence of Korean cuisine. Whether you are a vegan, a tofu enthusiast, or simply someone looking to explore new culinary horizons, this dish is a must-try. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the magic of Korean Spicy Braised Tofu take your taste buds on a memorable journey. Enjoy!
