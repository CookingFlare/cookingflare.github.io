Butternut Squash Risotto: A Creamy and Comforting Holiday Dish


When it comes to holiday cooking, few dishes evoke the feeling of warmth and comfort quite like a creamy butternut squash risotto. This classic Italian dish is a perfect balance of rich, velvety texture and the earthy sweetness of butternut squash, making it an ideal addition to any holiday gathering. Whether you’re hosting a festive dinner or simply want to treat your family to a special meal, this Butternut Squash Risotto recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh sage leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Butternut Squash: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the diced butternut squash on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender and lightly caramelized. Set aside.

  2. Simmer the Broth: In a saucepan, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a gentle simmer. Keep it warm over low heat.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until softened and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Toast the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the skillet and stir to coat it with the oil. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the rice becomes slightly translucent around the edges.

  5. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the white wine and stir until it’s absorbed by the rice. This step adds a delightful depth of flavor to the risotto.

  6. Add the Squash: Gently fold the roasted butternut squash into the rice mixture, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  7. Start Adding the Broth: Using a ladle, add a couple of scoops of the warm broth to the skillet. Stir gently and allow the rice to absorb the liquid. Continue adding the broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently, until the rice is creamy and al dente, about 20-25 minutes.

  8. Finish with Butter and Cheese: Once the risotto reaches the desired consistency, remove it from the heat. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and butter until melted and well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  9. Serve and Garnish: Spoon the Butternut Squash Risotto into individual serving dishes, garnish with fresh sage leaves, and offer additional Parmesan at the table for those who desire an extra sprinkle of cheese.

The Perfect Holiday Dish

This Butternut Squash Risotto is a delightful marriage of flavors and textures, with the creamy rice serving as the perfect backdrop for the sweet, nutty notes of the roasted squash. The addition of aromatic sage adds a festive touch, making it a fitting choice for any holiday celebration.

Not only is this dish a showstopper in terms of flavor, but it’s also a testament to the comfort and joy that holiday cooking can bring. The process of slowly stirring the risotto to creamy perfection can be a meditative and rewarding experience, allowing you to savor the anticipation of the upcoming festivities.

So, this holiday season, consider adding Butternut Squash Risotto to your menu. Whether it’s a cozy family dinner or a larger gathering of friends and loved ones, this creamy and comforting dish is sure to become a new holiday tradition. Enjoy the warmth and joy it brings to your table!
