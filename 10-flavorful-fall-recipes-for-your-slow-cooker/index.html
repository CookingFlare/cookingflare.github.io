10 Flavorful Fall Recipes for Your Slow Cooker


Fall is the season of rich flavors, cozy comfort food, and the warmth of home-cooked meals. There’s something magical about the combination of crisp air outside and the delicious aromas wafting from the kitchen. One of the best ways to take advantage of this season’s abundance of ingredients is by utilizing your slow cooker. These handy kitchen devices allow you to prepare hearty, flavorful dishes with minimal effort, making them perfect for busy fall evenings or for when you want to set it and forget it.

In this post, we’ll explore 10 mouthwatering fall recipes that are made even easier and more flavorful when prepared in your slow cooker. From soups and stews to sweet desserts, these recipes will help you embrace the best flavors of the season.

1. Slow Cooker Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash is the quintessential fall vegetable, and when paired with a creamy broth, it becomes a comforting soup that will warm you from the inside out. This slow cooker recipe is perfect for a busy weeknight or a weekend gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk or cream
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add the butternut squash, onion, and garlic to the slow cooker.
  2. Pour in the vegetable broth and season with cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the squash is tender.
  4. Once cooked, use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth.
  5. Stir in coconut milk or cream for a velvety texture.
  6. Garnish with fresh thyme and serve.

This soup is a true fall favorite, offering the perfect balance of sweetness from the squash and warmth from the spices. It’s ideal as a starter or a main dish when paired with crusty bread.

2. Slow Cooker Chicken and Apple Stew

Apples aren’t just for desserts! In this hearty chicken stew, the natural sweetness of apples pairs wonderfully with the savory chicken and vegetables, creating a comforting fall dish that’s packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 medium apples, peeled, cored, and chopped
  • 3 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts at the bottom of the slow cooker.
  2. Add the apples, carrots, celery, onion, and garlic.
  3. Pour the chicken broth over the ingredients and season with thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  5. Shred the chicken using two forks and stir everything together before serving.

This dish is a perfect balance of savory and sweet, with the apples adding a delicious twist to the traditional chicken stew.

3. Slow Cooker Beef Stew with Root Vegetables

A classic beef stew is always a fall favorite, and when made in the slow cooker, it becomes even more tender and flavorful. Root vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and parsnips add a rustic touch to this hearty meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat, cubed
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the beef stew meat, potatoes, carrots, parsnips, onion, and garlic in the slow cooker.
  2. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using), and season with rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cook on low for 7-8 hours or high for 4-5 hours until the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.
  4. Adjust seasoning if needed before serving.

This beef stew is the ultimate fall comfort food, full of tender beef and earthy vegetables. It’s perfect for a cozy family meal or for entertaining guests on a chilly evening.

4. Slow Cooker Pumpkin Chili

What could be more fitting for fall than a chili made with pumpkin? This hearty dish is perfect for cozy nights and is packed with flavor, thanks to the combination of beans, pumpkin, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, brown the ground meat over medium heat and then add it to the slow cooker.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, pumpkin puree, black beans, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker.
  3. Stir to combine, then cook on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours.
  4. Taste and adjust seasonings if necessary before serving.

This pumpkin chili is the perfect blend of savory and sweet, with the pumpkin giving it a rich, creamy texture that’s unlike any other chili you’ve tasted. Serve it with sour cream, shredded cheese, and tortilla chips for a complete meal.

5. Slow Cooker Maple Balsamic Pork Tenderloin

Pork tenderloin is tender, juicy, and easy to cook in the slow cooker, and when paired with a maple-balsamic glaze, it becomes a deliciously flavorful dish. This recipe is simple yet elegant, making it ideal for a family dinner or a special occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pork tenderloins
  • 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the pork tenderloins with salt and pepper, then place them in the slow cooker.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, and garlic.
  3. Pour the maple balsamic glaze over the pork tenderloins.
  4. Cook on low for 6-7 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the pork is cooked through and tender.
  5. Once cooked, remove the pork from the slow cooker and slice. Serve with the remaining glaze.

This maple balsamic pork tenderloin is a great combination of savory, sweet, and tangy flavors, making it a perfect fall meal to serve with roasted vegetables or mashed potatoes.

6. Slow Cooker Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Tacos are always a crowd-pleaser, and these slow cooker sweet potato and black bean tacos are a flavorful, vegetarian option perfect for fall. The sweet potatoes add a deliciously hearty base, while the black beans bring protein and a savory element.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Toppings: avocado, salsa, cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Add the sweet potatoes, black beans, onion, bell pepper, and garlic to the slow cooker.
  2. Season with chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper, then stir to combine.
  3. Cook on low for 6-7 hours or high for 3-4 hours until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Serve the mixture in corn tortillas and top with avocado, salsa, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

These tacos are a delicious way to enjoy fall flavors in a plant-based, light meal that’s filling and satisfying.

7. Slow Cooker Apple Crisp

Fall desserts are some of the most comforting treats, and apple crisp is a classic. This slow cooker version allows you to enjoy a warm, gooey dessert without heating up your oven, leaving more space for other dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup butter, softened
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Vanilla ice cream for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the sliced apples in the bottom of the slow cooker.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the oats, brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Cut the butter into small pieces and add it to the oat mixture, then use your hands or a pastry cutter to combine until it forms a crumbly topping.
  4. Sprinkle the topping over the apples.
  5. Cook on low for 4-5 hours or high for 2-3 hours until the apples are tender and the topping is golden brown.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream, if desired.

This slow cooker apple crisp is the ultimate fall dessert, combining the sweetness of apples with a crunchy topping that’s perfect for a cozy night in.

8. Slow Cooker Risotto with Mushrooms and Sage

Risotto is a rich and creamy dish that can be difficult to make on the stovetop, but in a slow cooker, it’s effortless. This version, made with earthy mushrooms and fragrant sage, is the ideal fall comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the Arborio rice, onion, mushrooms, and vegetable broth to the slow cooker.
  2. If using, add the white wine and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook on low for 3-4 hours, stirring occasionally, until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  4. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, butter, and fresh sage.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with additional sage if desired.

This creamy, comforting risotto is perfect for fall dinners, offering the rich flavor of mushrooms and the aromatic taste of sage.

9. Slow Cooker Maple Pecan Oatmeal

Breakfast in the fall should be warm and satisfying, and this slow cooker oatmeal is just the thing. The maple and pecans bring a touch of sweetness and crunch to your morning routine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups steel-cut oats
  • 4 cups water or milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Add the oats, water or milk, maple syrup, cinnamon, and salt to the slow cooker.
  2. Stir well and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours.
  3. Stir in the chopped pecans before serving.

This maple pecan oatmeal is a warm and hearty way to start your fall mornings, and the slow cooker makes it so easy to enjoy a comforting breakfast with minimal effort.

10. Slow Cooker Cranberry Orange Sauce

This cranberry sauce is a holiday staple, but it’s also perfect for fall dinners. The combination of tart cranberries and the citrusy brightness of oranges makes it a refreshing accompaniment to roasted meats and poultry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bag (12 oz) fresh cranberries
  • 1 orange, zested and juiced
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Combine the cranberries, orange zest and juice, sugar, water, and cinnamon in the slow cooker.
  2. Cook on low for 4-5 hours or high for 2-3 hours, until the cranberries have burst and the sauce has thickened.
  3. Stir well before serving.

This cranberry orange sauce is a perfect addition to any fall meal, adding a burst of flavor and a touch of brightness.

These 10 flavorful fall recipes are perfect for your slow cooker, bringing warmth, comfort, and the best flavors of the season right into your kitchen. Whether you’re preparing a hearty stew, a cozy dessert, or a simple breakfast, these dishes will make your fall meals easy, delicious, and memorable. So fire up your slow cooker and embrace the season with these comforting recipes!
