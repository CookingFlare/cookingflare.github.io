Seafood Recipes Perfect for Meal Prep


When it comes to meal prep, seafood offers an unbeatable combination of nutrition, flavor, and versatility that can transform weeknight dinners and quick lunches into gourmet experiences. Rich in omega-3 fatty acids, high-quality protein, and essential vitamins, seafood makes a delicious and healthy foundation for your weekly meals. Plus, many seafood dishes hold up beautifully in the fridge or freezer, maintaining their texture and taste.

Whether you’re a fan of flaky white fish, tender shellfish, or robust, meaty seafood, this guide will walk you through some fantastic recipes designed specifically for meal prep. You’ll find tips on storage, reheating, ingredient swaps, and flavor boosters to keep your meals fresh and exciting.

Why Choose Seafood for Meal Prep?

Seafood is a fantastic option for meal prep because:

  • Quick cooking times: Most seafood only takes a few minutes to cook, which means you can whip up a week’s worth of meals in no time.
  • Nutrient-dense: Packed with protein, vitamins D and B12, selenium, and omega-3s.
  • Low in calories and fat: Ideal for those mindful of their dietary intake.
  • Versatility: From grilled salmon and shrimp stir-fries to seafood pasta and stews.
  • Great for freezing: Many seafood dishes freeze well and can be defrosted without losing quality.

Meal Prep Tips for Seafood

Before we dive into recipes, here are some essential tips to ensure your seafood meal prep is successful:

1. Select Fresh, High-Quality Seafood

Choose wild-caught or responsibly farmed seafood when possible. Fresh fish should smell like the ocean, not fishy, and have firm, translucent flesh.

2. Don’t Overcook

Seafood cooks fast and continues cooking slightly after removal from heat. Overcooking makes it rubbery or dry, which doesn’t reheat well.

3. Store Properly

After cooking, cool seafood to room temperature, then refrigerate in airtight containers. For longer storage, freeze portions individually.

4. Reheat Gently

Avoid the microwave’s drying effect by reheating seafood gently on the stove or in the oven wrapped in foil, or enjoy it cold in salads.

5. Use Flavorful Marinades and Sauces

Marinades not only add flavor but can help keep seafood moist during cooking and storage. Prepare sauces separately for topping as needed.

1. Mediterranean Baked Cod with Olives & Tomatoes

This baked cod recipe is an easy, no-fuss meal prep winner. With bright Mediterranean flavors and plenty of bulk from olives and cherry tomatoes, it’s filling, fresh, and fridge-friendly.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cod fillets (about 6 oz each)
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. In a baking dish, toss cherry tomatoes, olives, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.

  3. Place cod fillets on top of the tomato-olive mixture.

  4. Bake uncovered for 12-15 minutes or until fish is opaque and flakes with a fork.

  5. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving or storing.

Meal Prep Notes:

  • Store cod and sauce together in airtight containers.
  • Reheat gently to preserve moisture, or enjoy chilled with a side of quinoa or couscous.
  • This dish freezes well and can be portioned out for lunches.

2. Spicy Garlic Shrimp with Brown Rice and Vegetables

Shrimp cooks fast and absorbs flavors beautifully. This spicy garlic shrimp pairs perfectly with fiber-rich brown rice and crisp vegetables, making a balanced and emboldened meal prep choice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb peeled and deveined shrimp
  • 4 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp chili flakes (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.

  2. Add garlic and chili flakes, sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Add shrimp and cook 2-3 minutes per side until pink and cooked through.

  4. Toss in bell pepper and snap peas, cooking 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp.

  5. Stir in soy sauce and lime juice.

  6. Season with salt and pepper, and garnish with cilantro.

Meal Prep Notes:

  • Divide shrimp and vegetable mixture over cooked brown rice in containers.
  • Reheat in the microwave for 1-2 minutes or on the stovetop until warmed.
  • The flavors intensify after a day, making it even tastier.

3. Thai Coconut Curry Salmon Bowls

Rich, creamy coconut milk and vibrant Thai spices complement tender salmon in this meal prep-friendly bowl. Loaded with vegetables and jasmine rice, it’s exotic comfort food that’s easy to reheat and enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 4 cups cooked jasmine rice
  • Fresh basil and lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, whisk together coconut milk, red curry paste, fish sauce, and brown sugar. Simmer on low for 5 minutes.

  2. Add bell pepper, broccoli, and snap peas; cook until vegetables are tender (about 6 minutes).

  3. While vegetables cook, pan-sear salmon fillets over medium heat for about 4-5 minutes on each side or until cooked through.

  4. Divide rice into four bowls, top with vegetables and salmon, and ladle curry sauce over the top.

  5. Garnish with basil leaves and lime wedges.

Meal Prep Notes:

  • Store salmon, vegetables, and sauce together or separate if preferred.
  • Reheat gently in the microwave or on the stove.
  • Keeps well refrigerated for up to 4 days and freezes nicely.

4. Lemon Herb Tuna Salad Wraps

If you’re looking for a no-cook seafood meal prep option, this tuna salad is perfect. It’s fresh, bright, and easily turned into lunch wraps, salads, or sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (5 oz each) tuna in water, drained
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise (optional)
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 stalk celery, diced
  • 1 small red onion, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole wheat wraps or lettuce leaves

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix tuna, Greek yogurt, mayonnaise, lemon juice, dill, celery, and red onion.

  2. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Spoon mixture into wraps or use as a salad topper.

Meal Prep Notes:

  • Portion tuna salad into containers and keep wraps separate until ready to eat.
  • Keeps fresh in the fridge for 3-4 days.
  • Add sliced avocado or tomato at serving time for extra freshness.

5. Seafood Paella for the Week

Paella is a classic Spanish rice dish rich in flavors, colorful vegetables, and a variety of seafood. Making a large batch means you can divide it into containers for multiple meals.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb mixed seafood (shrimp, mussels, clams, squid)
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio or short-grain rice
  • 4 cups seafood broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp saffron threads (optional but authentic)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet or paella pan. Sauté onions, garlic, and bell pepper until soft.

  2. Add tomatoes, smoked paprika, saffron, salt, and pepper. Cook 3-4 minutes.

  3. Stir in rice until coated with the spices.

  4. Pour in seafood broth and bring to a simmer.

  5. Cook uncovered over medium heat until rice is almost tender, about 15 minutes.

  6. Nestle mixed seafood into the rice, cover with foil or lid, and cook 5-7 minutes more until seafood is cooked and rice is tender.

  7. Garnish with parsley and lemon wedges.

Meal Prep Notes:

  • Cool before portioning into airtight containers.
  • Reheat with a splash of broth or water to loosen the rice.
  • Freezes well but shrimp and mussels might lose some texture, so best enjoyed within a few days.

6. Asian-Inspired Sesame Glazed Mackerel with Stir-fried Greens

Mackerel is a rich, oily fish that holds flavor beautifully. When glazed with a sweet-and-salty sesame sauce and paired with quick stir-fried bok choy or kale, it turns into a nutrient-packed meal prep hero.

Ingredients:

  • 4 mackerel fillets
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 4 cups stir-fry greens (bok choy, kale, or spinach)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Sesame seeds and scallions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, ginger, and garlic.

  2. Marinate mackerel fillets in the sauce for 15-20 minutes.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat, cook mackerel skin-side down for 4 minutes, then flip and cook another 3 minutes until caramelized and cooked through.

  4. In the same pan, stir-fry greens for 2-3 minutes.

  5. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped scallions.

Meal Prep Notes:

  • Store fish and greens side by side for balanced containers.
  • Reheat quickly on the stovetop or enjoy cold over rice or noodles.
  • Mackerel freezes well, but best to prepare fresh for optimal flavor.

Final Thoughts

Seafood offers a fantastic way to elevate your meal prep game with minimal effort and maximum nutritional payoff. From simple baked fish and quick shrimp dishes to soulful stews and hearty paellas, these recipes bring variety and excitement to your weekly meals without sacrificing convenience.

Keep experimenting with flavors like Mediterranean herbs, Asian spices, or Caribbean zest to keep your meal prep enjoyable. Using fresh herbs, citrus, and vibrant vegetables ensures that every bite stays bright and satisfying.

Enjoy your cooking adventure—and happy meal prepping!
