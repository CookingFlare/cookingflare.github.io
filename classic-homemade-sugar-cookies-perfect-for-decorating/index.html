Classic Homemade Sugar Cookies: Perfect for Decorating


If there’s one thing that can transport you back to childhood, it’s the smell of freshly baked sugar cookies. These classic treats are not only delicious but also a canvas for your creativity. From simple shapes to intricate designs, sugar cookies are perfect for decorating and adding a personal touch to any occasion. Whether you’re a baking enthusiast or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe will guide you through the process of creating the perfect sugar cookies for decorating.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.

  2. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.

  3. Add the Egg and Vanilla: Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.

  4. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt.

  5. Mix the Dough: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, mixing until a dough forms. Be careful not to overmix.

  6. Chill the Dough: Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disk, wrap in plastic wrap, and chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour.

  7. Roll and Cut: Once chilled, roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch thickness. Use cookie cutters to cut out desired shapes.

  8. Bake: Place the cut-out cookies onto the prepared baking sheets and bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are just beginning to turn golden.

  9. Cool and Decorate: Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, the fun part begins – decorating your sugar cookies with icing, sprinkles, and edible decorations of your choice.

Tips for Decorating

  • Royal Icing: For a smooth and professional finish, consider using royal icing, which hardens to a beautiful sheen, making it perfect for intricate designs.

  • Coloring Icing: Divide the royal icing into separate bowls and add food coloring for vibrant hues. Use piping bags and small tips for detailed decorating.

  • Sprinkles and Edible Decorations: Let your creativity shine by adding sprinkles, edible pearls, or even edible glitter to make your sugar cookies truly stand out.

Storage and Serving

Once decorated, allow the icing to set completely before storing the cookies in an airtight container. These cookies can last up to a week, making them ideal for gifting or preparing ahead for special events.

Whether it’s a holiday, birthday, or just a moment of creativity in the kitchen, classic homemade sugar cookies are a delightful way to spread joy and share your artistic flair. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your decorating supplies, and let your imagination run wild as you create your own edible masterpieces.

Remember, the beauty of sugar cookies lies not only in their taste but also in the joy of decorating them. So, grab your favorite cookie cutters and get ready to embark on a delicious and artistic adventure in your kitchen!
