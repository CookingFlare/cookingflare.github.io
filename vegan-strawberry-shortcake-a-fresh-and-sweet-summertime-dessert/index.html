Vegan Strawberry Shortcake: A Fresh and Sweet Summertime Dessert


Summer is the perfect time to indulge in refreshing and vibrant desserts. And what better way to satisfy your sweet tooth than with a delicious vegan strawberry shortcake? This classic dessert gets a cruelty-free makeover without compromising on flavor. With its light and fluffy layers, juicy strawberries, and creamy whipped topping, this vegan version of strawberry shortcake is sure to impress everyone, whether they follow a vegan diet or not.

Ingredients

For the shortcake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the strawberries:

  • 4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup

For the whipped topping:

  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk, chilled overnight
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.

  3. Add the cold cubed vegan butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Pour in the almond milk and vanilla extract. Stir until just combined, being careful not to overmix. The dough should be slightly sticky.

  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick rectangle.

  6. Using a round biscuit cutter or a glass, cut out 6-8 shortcakes from the dough. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  7. Bake the shortcakes in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown.

  8. While the shortcakes are cooling, prepare the strawberries. In a bowl, combine the sliced strawberries and maple syrup. Toss gently to coat the strawberries and let them macerate for about 10 minutes.

  9. To make the whipped topping, carefully open the chilled can of coconut milk. Scoop out the solid coconut cream that has risen to the top and place it in a mixing bowl. Discard the liquid or save it for another use.

  10. Using an electric mixer, beat the coconut cream on high speed until it becomes fluffy and resembles whipped cream. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to beat for another minute or until well combined.

  11. To assemble the strawberry shortcakes, slice each cooled shortcake in half horizontally. Place the bottom half on a plate, spoon some macerated strawberries over it, and top with a dollop of whipped coconut cream. Place the other half of the shortcake on top and repeat the process.

  12. Serve immediately and enjoy the fresh, sweet flavors of this vegan strawberry shortcake.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, try adding a sprinkle of lemon zest to the macerated strawberries.
  • If you prefer a sweeter shortcake, you can dust the tops with powdered sugar before serving.
  • Feel free to experiment with different fruits like blueberries or peaches in place of strawberries.
  • If you don’t have almond milk on hand, you can substitute it with any other non-dairy milk of your choice.

Now that you have this amazing vegan strawberry shortcake recipe, you can treat yourself and your loved ones to a delightful summertime dessert. Enjoy the vibrant colors, the light and fluffy shortcakes, and the burst of juicy strawberries in every bite. This vegan version of a classic favorite will surely become a staple in your dessert repertoire. So go ahead, whip up this sweet treat, and savor the taste of summer!
