Satisfying Eggplant Parmesan: A Vegetarian Delight


If you’re looking for a hearty and satisfying vegetarian dish, look no further than Eggplant Parmesan. This classic Italian dish is a crowd-pleaser, even for the most dedicated meat lovers. The crispy, golden-brown eggplant slices, layered with rich marinara sauce and creamy mozzarella cheese, create a mouthwatering combination that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Whether you’re a vegetarian, trying to incorporate more meatless meals into your diet, or simply looking for a delicious new recipe to try, this Eggplant Parmesan is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Eggplant:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

For the Marinara Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Assembly:

  • 1 pound fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Eggplant:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
    • Place the flour, beaten eggs, and panko breadcrumbs mixed with grated Parmesan cheese into three separate shallow dishes.
    • Season the eggplant slices with salt and pepper. Dredge each slice in the flour, then dip into the beaten eggs, and finally coat with the panko mixture, pressing gently to adhere.
    • In a large skillet, heat a generous amount of olive oil over medium-high heat. Working in batches, fry the eggplant slices until golden brown on both sides, adding more oil as needed. Transfer the fried slices to the prepared baking sheet.

  2. Make the Marinara Sauce:

    • In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
    • Pour in the crushed tomatoes and add the dried oregano and basil. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.

  3. Assemble the Eggplant Parmesan:

    • Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices on top of the sauce.
    • Spoon more marinara sauce over the eggplant, then layer with slices of fresh mozzarella cheese. Repeat the layers, finishing with a final layer of marinara sauce.
    • Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the top of the dish.

  4. Bake and Serve:

    • Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, until the cheese is melted and the sauce is bubbly.
    • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the top is golden and crispy.
    • Let the Eggplant Parmesan rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves.

Final Thoughts

Eggplant Parmesan is a versatile dish that can be served as a main course with a side of pasta or a green salad, or as a delicious appetizer for a gathering of family and friends. The crispy texture of the eggplant, combined with the savory marinara sauce and gooey melted cheese, makes every bite a delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to add more meatless options to your weekly menu, this Eggplant Parmesan recipe is a must-try. Enjoy the satisfaction of creating a comforting and flavorful dish that will have everyone asking for more!
