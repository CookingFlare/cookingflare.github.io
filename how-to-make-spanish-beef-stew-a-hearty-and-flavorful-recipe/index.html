How to Make Spanish Beef Stew: A Hearty and Flavorful Recipe


Spanish beef stew, or “Estofado de Carne,” is one of the most beloved comfort foods in Spain. Known for its deep, rich flavors and tender beef, this dish is perfect for those chilly evenings when you need something hearty to warm you up. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or looking to impress your guests with a flavorful meal, Spanish beef stew is a recipe that will definitely do the trick.

In this post, we’ll explore how to make Spanish beef stew from scratch. From choosing the right cuts of beef to mastering the cooking techniques that will bring out the flavors of this dish, you’ll find everything you need to recreate this classic Spanish recipe right in your own kitchen. So, let’s dive into the world of Spanish cuisine and learn how to make this irresistible beef stew.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before you start cooking, gather all the ingredients you’ll need. Spanish beef stew is traditionally made with a variety of vegetables, herbs, and spices, along with rich beef that can withstand slow cooking. Here’s what you’ll need to make a delicious Spanish beef stew:

  • 2 pounds of beef stew meat (chuck roast or brisket are excellent choices)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth (or more if needed)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • 1 tablespoon flour (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (optional, to balance the acidity of the tomatoes)

Beef Stew Meat

The beef is the star of the dish, so selecting the right cut is crucial. In Spain, “Estofado de Carne” often uses cuts of beef that are well-marbled and can withstand long, slow cooking. Common cuts used include chuck roast or brisket. These cuts are flavorful, and they become incredibly tender when cooked slowly.

Make sure the meat is cut into cubes of about 1 to 1.5 inches. If you prefer leaner cuts, you can opt for round steak, but for the best stew, go for meat that has a little fat to help keep it moist during the cooking process.

Vegetables

The vegetables in this stew not only add texture and flavor but also contribute to the hearty nature of the dish. You’ll need onions, garlic, carrots, potatoes, and red bell peppers. These vegetables meld perfectly with the beef, absorbing the juices and spices, and creating a comforting base for the stew.

Herbs and Spices

Smoked paprika is a signature ingredient in Spanish cuisine, and it will lend your beef stew a smoky depth of flavor. Ground cumin adds a subtle warmth, while bay leaves enhance the overall aroma of the dish. You may also choose to add a touch of sugar to balance the acidity of the tomatoes, which is common in some Spanish recipes.

Wine and Broth

A good dry red wine is a key component of this recipe. The wine will deglaze the pan after browning the beef, releasing all the delicious caramelized bits that will enrich the stew. Choose a wine that you enjoy drinking, as its flavors will infuse the stew. For the broth, beef broth is ideal, but you can use chicken broth if necessary.

How to Prepare Spanish Beef Stew

Now that you have all the ingredients, it’s time to begin the cooking process. Follow these steps for a slow-cooked, flavorful Spanish beef stew.

Step 1: Brown the Beef

Start by heating the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the beef cubes in batches. Don’t overcrowd the pot to ensure that the beef browns properly. Brown the beef on all sides, which will take about 5–7 minutes per batch. This step is important because it enhances the flavor of the stew and adds depth to the dish.

Once the beef is browned, transfer it to a plate and set it aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, add a little more olive oil if needed, then add the chopped onions. Cook the onions for about 4-5 minutes, or until they are soft and translucent. Add the minced garlic and sauté for another minute until fragrant.

Now, add the chopped carrots, potatoes, and red bell pepper. Stir the vegetables and let them cook for a few minutes. This helps them to absorb the flavors left in the pot from the beef.

Step 3: Add the Spices and Tomatoes

Next, sprinkle in the smoked paprika and ground cumin. Stir the mixture to evenly coat the vegetables with the spices. This is where the magic happens as the spices infuse the stew with their rich, earthy flavors.

Add the crushed tomatoes and stir well. The tomatoes provide a tangy base for the stew, balancing out the richness of the beef. Let the mixture simmer for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 4: Deglaze with Red Wine

Pour in the red wine, and with a wooden spoon, scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. These bits are packed with flavor, so make sure you don’t miss any. Allow the wine to cook off for about 5-7 minutes, reducing slightly. This helps concentrate the flavors and ensures that the wine’s acidity will not overpower the dish.

Step 5: Add the Broth and Bay Leaves

Once the wine has reduced, return the browned beef to the pot. Add the beef broth to the pot, ensuring the beef is covered. If needed, add a little more broth or water to make sure everything is submerged.

Add the bay leaf, then bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Once it begins to boil, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer. The stew should cook low and slow for about 2–3 hours, or until the beef becomes fork-tender.

Step 6: Thicken the Stew (Optional)

If you prefer a thicker stew, you can make a slurry with 1 tablespoon of flour and a little cold water. Stir the flour mixture into the stew during the last 30 minutes of cooking. This will give the stew a velvety texture. Alternatively, if you don’t mind a more brothy consistency, you can skip this step.

Step 7: Final Seasoning and Garnish

After 2–3 hours, the beef should be incredibly tender, and the vegetables should be soft. Taste the stew and season with salt and black pepper to your liking. You can also adjust the amount of smoked paprika or cumin for a bolder flavor if desired.

Once the stew is ready, remove the bay leaves and discard them. Ladle the stew into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley to add a pop of color and freshness.

Serving Spanish Beef Stew

Spanish beef stew is best served with crusty bread to soak up the rich sauce. A side of steamed rice or mashed potatoes is also an excellent pairing, allowing you to enjoy the savory broth.

If you want to create a complete Spanish meal, serve the stew with a side salad of mixed greens, dressed with olive oil, vinegar, and a sprinkle of sea salt. A glass of red wine, preferably a Spanish Rioja or Tempranillo, will elevate the flavors and make the meal even more authentic.

Tips for the Best Spanish Beef Stew

  1. Slow Cooking is Key: To achieve the most tender beef, the stew needs to cook slowly. Don’t rush the process; let the beef simmer gently for a couple of hours so it becomes melt-in-your-mouth tender.

  2. Use Quality Beef: The better the beef, the more flavorful your stew will be. Choose well-marbled cuts like chuck roast or brisket for the best results.

  3. Customize the Vegetables: While carrots, potatoes, and bell peppers are common in Spanish beef stew, you can add other vegetables like peas, green beans, or even mushrooms to suit your preferences.

  4. Make Ahead and Let it Rest: Like many stews, Spanish beef stew often tastes even better the next day after the flavors have had time to meld. If you have the time, make the stew a day in advance and let it sit in the refrigerator overnight.

  5. Freezing: This stew freezes wonderfully! If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 3 months. Just reheat thoroughly before serving.

Conclusion

Spanish beef stew is a comforting and hearty dish that combines tender beef, flavorful vegetables, and a rich, smoky broth. Whether you’re serving it for a family dinner or a special occasion, this dish is sure to impress. By following this simple recipe, you can bring the authentic flavors of Spain into your own kitchen. With the perfect balance of spices and slow-cooked beef, it’s a meal that’s bound to become a favorite in your home.

So, next time you’re craving a filling and flavorful meal, consider making this Spanish beef stew. It’s a perfect way to enjoy the warmth of Spanish cuisine, and you’ll be sure to enjoy every savory bite!
