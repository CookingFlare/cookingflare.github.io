The Best Recipes for Holiday Pies and Tarts


The holiday season is the perfect time to gather in the kitchen and craft delicious, aromatic treats that bring family and friends together. Few desserts capture the spirit of the holidays quite like pies and tarts. Whether you’re hosting a festive celebration or looking for the perfect dessert to accompany a cozy night by the fireplace, these recipes will undoubtedly bring joy to your table. From classic favorites to modern twists, there’s something here for every taste and skill level. Let’s dive into a selection of recipes that are sure to impress during the holidays.

Classic Apple Pie

Apple pie is a quintessential holiday dessert that radiates warmth and comfort. This timeless favorite never fails to delight. Here’s how you can make the perfect classic apple pie:

Ingredients:

  • 6–8 medium apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work best), peeled, cored, and sliced thin
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 pre-made or homemade pie crusts
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (optional)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the apple slices, granulated sugar, brown sugar, flour, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Toss until the apples are fully coated.
  3. Roll out one of the pie crusts and fit it into a 9-inch pie dish. Ensure the crust covers the bottom and sides evenly.
  4. Pour the apple mixture into the pie crust. Dot with unsalted butter for an extra layer of richness, if desired.
  5. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the apples. You can leave it whole or cut it into strips for a lattice design. Seal the edges and cut slits on the top to release steam.
  6. Brush the top crust with beaten egg to achieve a golden finish.
  7. Bake for 50–60 minutes until the crust is golden brown and the apple filling bubbles.
  8. Cool for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the filling to set.

Pumpkin Tart with a Ginger Snap Crust

Looking to switch things up from the traditional pumpkin pie? A pumpkin tart with a ginger snap crust is a sophisticated alternative that packs bold flavor with every bite. The spiced crust complements the creamy pumpkin filling perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • For the Crust:
    • 2 cups crushed ginger snap cookies
    • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • For the Filling:
    • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
    • 3/4 cup heavy cream
    • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
    • 1/4 cup brown sugar
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix the crushed ginger snap cookies with melted butter. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom and up the sides of a tart pan. Bake the crust for 10 minutes. Allow it to cool slightly before adding the filling.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, heavy cream, sugars, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg until smooth.
  4. Pour the filling into the prepared crust and spread evenly.
  5. Bake for 45–50 minutes or until the filling is set and slightly firm to the touch.
  6. Cool on a wire rack. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

Rich Chocolate Pecan Pie

Pecan pie is a holiday staple, but adding chocolate creates a decadently rich and irresistible variation. This recipe combines the nutty flavor of pecans with the smooth allure of chocolate for the perfect indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 1 unbaked pie crust
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 2 cups pecan halves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, corn syrup, beaten eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Stir in the chocolate chips and pecan halves until evenly distributed.
  4. Pour the mixture into the unbaked pie crust, spreading it out evenly.
  5. Bake for 50–55 minutes, or until the filling is set around the edges but slightly jiggly in the center.
  6. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving. Pair with whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for added decadence.

Cranberry Almond Tart

For those who crave something tart and nutty, this cranberry almond tart is a bright addition to the holiday dessert table. The ruby-red cranberries shine against the nutty almond filling, creating a dessert that’s as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients:

  • For the Crust:
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 2 tablespoons cold water
  • For the Filling:
    • 1 cup almond flour
    • 3/4 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 teaspoon almond extract
    • 2 cups fresh cranberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. To make the crust, pulse flour, sugar, and butter in a food processor until crumbly. Add egg yolk and cold water, pulsing until a dough forms. Press the dough into a tart pan and chill for 20 minutes.
  3. To make the filling, beat almond flour, powdered sugar, butter, eggs, and almond extract in a medium bowl until combined.
  4. Spread the almond filling into the chilled crust. Scatter cranberries across the filling in an even layer.
  5. Bake for 40–45 minutes, until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  6. Cool completely before slicing.

Tips for Perfect Holiday Pies and Tarts

Mastering pies and tarts requires a few best practices. Here are some tips to help you achieve stellar results:

  • Homemade Crusts: Start with a high-quality crust. Whether you’re rolling out your own dough or using a store-bought version, ensure it’s fresh and flaky to complement your filling.
  • Temperature Matters: When baking pies and tarts, the oven temperature plays a crucial role. Preheating ensures crisp crusts and evenly baked fillings.
  • Chill Time: Allow pies and tarts to cool before cutting. This prevents the filling from spilling out and gives it a cleaner look.
  • Seasonal Ingredients: Choose fruits and spices that are in season for maximum flavor—like fresh cranberries, apples, and pumpkin. Seasonal produce brings the best taste to your creations.

Holiday baking isn’t just about crafting delicious desserts—it’s about making memories and embracing the joy of the season. These recipes for pies and tarts are perfect for any gathering, big or small, and will surely bring smiles to the faces of everyone you share them with. So roll up your sleeves, get out the rolling pin, and let your baking spread holiday cheer all season long!
