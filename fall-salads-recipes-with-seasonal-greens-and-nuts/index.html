Fall Salads: Recipes with Seasonal Greens and Nuts


As the leaves start to change and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to enjoy the bountiful flavors of fall in a refreshing and nutritious salad. Fall salads are not only a feast for the taste buds but also an opportunity to incorporate seasonal greens and nuts that are packed with essential nutrients. Whether you’re looking for a light lunch, a vibrant side dish, or a colorful starter for a fall gathering, these fall salad recipes will elevate your autumn dining experience.

Harvest Kale Salad with Maple Glazed Pecans

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of fresh kale
  • 1 cup pecans
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinaigrette
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash and de-stem the kale, then tear it into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a skillet over medium heat, toast the pecans until fragrant.
  3. Add the maple syrup to the skillet and stir until the pecans are evenly coated. Remove from heat and let them cool.
  4. In a large bowl, massage the kale with the balsamic vinaigrette for a few minutes to soften the leaves.
  5. Add the maple glazed pecans, dried cranberries, and crumbled feta cheese to the kale. Toss gently to combine.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste before serving.

This vibrant salad celebrates the earthy flavors of kale, complemented by the sweetness of maple glazed pecans and the tartness of dried cranberries. It’s a delightful blend of textures and tastes that captures the essence of fall on a plate.

Apple Walnut Spinach Salad with Honey Dijon Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 apples, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, honey, and Dijon mustard to create the dressing. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large salad bowl, combine the fresh spinach, sliced apples, toasted walnuts, red onion, and crumbled goat cheese.
  3. Drizzle the honey Dijon dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat all the ingredients.

This salad is a celebration of autumn’s bounty, with the sweetness of crisp apples, the earthy richness of walnuts, and the subtle tang of goat cheese. The honey Dijon dressing ties all the flavors together in a delightful medley.

Roasted Butternut Squash and Quinoa Salad with Toasted Almonds

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar and honey to create the dressing.
  4. In a large serving bowl, combine the roasted butternut squash, cooked quinoa, toasted almonds, and dried cranberries.
  5. Drizzle the balsamic honey dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine all the ingredients.

This hearty salad brings together the robust flavors of roasted butternut squash, nutty quinoa, and crunchy almonds, accented by the sweet pops of dried cranberries. It’s a wholesome and satisfying dish that embodies the essence of fall.

Conclusion

These fall salad recipes are not only a celebration of seasonal produce but also a delightful way to nourish your body with the wholesome goodness of greens and nuts. Embrace the flavors of fall with these vibrant and nutritious salads that are sure to become favorites at your table. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply enjoying a quiet meal at home, these recipes will add a burst of autumnal joy to your dining experience. Try them out and savor the taste of fall in every forkful!
