How to Can and Preserve Fresh Citrus for Year-Round Use


As the seasons change, fresh citrus fruits like lemons, oranges, and grapefruits become treasured staples in the kitchen. These versatile fruits bring brightness to both savory dishes and sweet treats. But what if you could savor their tangy goodness all year long by preserving them? Canning citrus is an excellent way to make the most of fresh produce when it’s in peak season, ensuring you have a pantry stocked with sunshine for every recipe that comes to mind.

In this guide, we’ll explore how to can and preserve fresh citrus for year-round use. From choosing the right fruits to mastering techniques that lock in their flavors, you’ll be equipped to build your stash of vibrant citrus preserves, marmalades, and more.

Why Preserve Fresh Citrus?

Preserving citrus goes far beyond squeezing out a bit of juice and freezing it. When canned properly, citrus fruits retain their natural zing, tartness, and sweetness, opening up a world of kitchen possibilities. Whether you want to add fresh-tasting lemon juice to soups, spoon tangy marmalade over baked goods, or enjoy candied peel in holiday recipes, canning citrus allows you to use every part of the fruit and extend its lifespan significantly.

Additionally, canning citrus reduces waste and saves money. The peak season for citrus runs during winter and early spring, when these fruits are plentiful and affordable. Preserving them allows you to purchase citrus in bulk and save the excess for future use.

Tools You’ll Need for Canning Citrus

Investing in the proper tools ensures a smooth and safe canning process. Let’s go over the must-haves you’ll need:

  • Canning Jars and Lids: Select jars designed specifically for canning. They come in various sizes; pint and half-pint jars are popular choices for citrus.
  • Water Bath Canner or Large Pot: Essential for processing jars and sealing them properly.
  • Jar Lifter: Makes handling hot jars safe and easy.
  • Canning Funnel: Ensures clean pouring and packing of citrus into jars.
  • Ladle or Skimmer: Useful for transferring liquids or whole fruit into jars.
  • Citrus Juicer/Zester: A juicer and zester are invaluable tools for creating juice, zest, and peel-based preserves.
  • Cutting Board and Knife: Use these to prepare the fruit for canning.
  • Kitchen Towels: Prevent burns and keep surfaces clean during the process.

With your tools at hand, you’re ready to dive into the world of citrus preservation.

Step 1: Choosing the Right Citrus

When selecting citrus for canning, always go for fresh, firm, and vibrant fruits. Look for fruits that feel heavy for their size, as this typically means they’re juicy. Avoid any citrus with blemishes, soft spots, or mold—these can compromise the canning process and affect the final product.

The type of citrus you choose depends on what you’d like to preserve. Here are some popular options:

  • Oranges: Perfect for marmalades or preserving in slices for desserts and beverages.
  • Lemons and Limes: Excellent for canning juice, making lemonade concentrate, or creating preserved slices.
  • Grapefruits: Great for marmalade, segment preserves, or tart juice that complements cocktails.
  • Tangelos and Tangerines: Known for their sweetness, these fruits shine in marmalades or syrupy preserves.

Once your fruit is selected, be sure to wash the citrus thoroughly with warm water to remove any wax or residue before getting started.

Step 2: Preparing Citrus for Canning

Proper preparation is essential for retaining the best texture, flavor, and aroma of citrus. Depending on what you’re making, you’ll need to prepare the fruit as follows:

Juicing Citrus

If you’re planning to preserve juice or make lemonade concentrate, use a high-quality juicer to extract the juices efficiently. Strain the juice through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove pulp and seeds, leaving behind smooth, clear liquid.

Segmenting Citrus

When canning citrus segments, you’ll first need to remove the peel and pith. Using a sharp knife, cut away the peel and white pith, then carefully slice the fruit along the membranes to extract clean, juicy segments.

Zesting and Peeling

For candied citrus and marmalades, you’ll want to save every bit of zest or peel from your citrus. A microplane zester creates fine zest, while a vegetable peeler allows you to remove strips of peel without the bitter pith. You can blanch peels in boiling water to soften them before canning.

Step 3: Canning Methods and Recipes for Citrus

Let’s dive into some of the most popular and practical ways to can and preserve citrus. Each method brings out unique flavors and textures, giving you limitless options for enjoying citrus throughout the year.

Marmalade: A Classic Citrus Preserve

Marmalade is made from citrus fruits, sugar, and water, resulting in a slightly tangy, slightly sweet spread bursting with zest and flavor. It’s a wonderful topping for toast, scones, and cakes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of oranges
  • 1 lemon
  • 4 cups of water
  • 4 cups of sugar

Instructions:

  1. Slice the oranges and lemon thinly, removing seeds as you go.
  2. In a large pot, combine the sliced fruit with water and soak for 12-24 hours to soften the rinds.
  3. The next day, bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer until the rinds are tender.
  4. Add sugar, stirring constantly until dissolved. Cook until the mixture gels when tested on a cold plate.
  5. Ladle the marmalade into sterilized jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace. Process jars in a water bath canner for 10 minutes.

Preserved Lemons

Preserved lemons are widely used in North African and Mediterranean cuisines, bringing a tangy depth to stews, salads, and sauces.

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 organic lemons
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • Additional lemon juice (if needed)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the lemons and cut them into quarters, leaving the ends attached so they remain whole.
  2. Pack the lemons into sterilized jars, sprinkling layers of salt between wedges. Compress them to release juices.
  3. Cover the lemons completely with their juice (add extra if necessary).
  4. Seal the jars and leave them at room temperature for 3-4 weeks, flipping occasionally, before refrigerating.

Candied Citrus Peels

Candied peels are versatile for garnishing desserts, snacking, or using in baked goods.

Ingredients:

  • Citrus peels from 6 fruits
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Cut the peels into strips and boil them in water for 10 minutes. Drain and rinse to remove bitterness.
  2. Combine sugar and water in a pot and bring to a boil to create a syrup.
  3. Add the peels and simmer until translucent. Remove and dry on parchment paper for several hours.

Step 4: Storing and Using Your Citrus Preserves

Once your jars have been processed and cooled, check the seals by pressing the center of each lid—there should be no give. Properly sealed jars can be stored in a cool, dark place for up to a year. Always label jars with the contents and date for future reference.

When you’re ready to enjoy your preserves, the possibilities are endless. Spread marmalade on toast, add a preserved lemon to your favorite tagine, or chop up candied peels for holiday baking. Citrus juice can be used in cocktails, marinades, and vinaigrettes to brighten any dish.

Final Thoughts

Canning fresh citrus is a satisfying and rewarding process that keeps the flavors of the season alive year-round. Whether you prefer tangy marmalade, tart juice, or sweet candied peels, these methods will ensure you’re never without a burst of citrus brightness in your cooking. By following this guide, you can fill your pantry with jars of sunshine that capture the essence of the season and elevate every meal. So gather your citrus, roll up your sleeves, and embark on the delightful journey of preserving nature’s bounty!
