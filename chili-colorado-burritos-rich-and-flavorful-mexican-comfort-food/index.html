Chili Colorado Burritos: Rich and Flavorful Mexican Comfort Food


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Chili Colorado Burritos. This dish is a delightful combination of tender, flavorful beef, smothered in a rich and spicy red chili sauce, all wrapped up in a warm flour tortilla. It’s a hearty and comforting meal that’s perfect for a cozy night in or for entertaining guests. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the process of creating this mouthwatering dish step by step.

Ingredients

For the Chili Colorado Sauce:

  • 3 dried ancho chilies
  • 3 dried guajillo chilies
  • 2 dried pasilla chilies
  • 1 onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 4 cups beef stock
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

For the Beef Filling:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into chunks
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 cup beef stock

Additional:

  • Large flour tortillas
  • Shredded cheese
  • Chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chili Colorado Sauce

  1. Begin by removing the stems and seeds from the dried chilies. Place the chilies in a dry skillet over medium heat and toast for a few seconds on each side until they become fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
  2. Transfer the toasted chilies to a bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for about 20 minutes until they become soft.
  3. In a blender, combine the soaked chilies, onion, garlic, ground cumin, dried oregano, and beef stock. Blend until smooth.
  4. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Pour in the chili puree and bring it to a simmer. Cook for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens. Season with salt to taste.

Step 2: Prepare the Beef Filling

  1. Season the beef chunks with salt and black pepper.
  2. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef and sear on all sides until browned. Remove the beef from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and cook until softened. Stir in the minced garlic, ground cumin, and dried oregano, cooking for an additional minute.
  4. Return the seared beef to the skillet and add the bay leaf and beef stock. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the liquid has reduced, stirring occasionally.

Step 3: Assemble the Chili Colorado Burritos

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Warm the flour tortillas in a dry skillet or directly over the flame of a gas burner for a few seconds on each side.
  3. Place a generous amount of the beef filling in the center of each tortilla. Roll up the tortillas, tucking in the sides as you go, to form burritos.
  4. Arrange the burritos in a baking dish and ladle the chili colorado sauce over the top. Sprinkle with shredded cheese.
  5. Bake the burritos in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Step 4: Garnish and Serve

  1. Remove the burritos from the oven and garnish with chopped fresh cilantro. Serve with lime wedges on the side for a burst of citrus flavor.
  2. These Chili Colorado Burritos pair wonderfully with Mexican rice, refried beans, or a crisp green salad.

Conclusion

With its robust flavors and comforting appeal, Chili Colorado Burritos are sure to become a favorite in your recipe repertoire. This dish is perfect for a cozy family dinner or for impressing guests at your next gathering. The rich and savory chili colorado sauce, combined with the tender beef filling, makes for a truly satisfying meal. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to the delightful experience of creating and savoring these delicious burritos. Enjoy!
