Swirls of Flavor: Discover the Magic of Middle Eastern Fusion


Middle Eastern cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, rich history, and mesmerizing fusion of culinary delights. With ingredients ranging from luscious lemons to fragrant spices and succulent meats, Middle Eastern fare can instantly transport you to another world. As the gateway to a fascinating gastronomic experience, here, you’ll discover a tantalizing array of Middle Eastern fusion recipes that will truly entice your taste buds.

  1. sumac-spiced lamb kebabs with tahini sauce

The humble lamb kebab is elevated to new heights with the pungent aroma of sumac, delivering a burst of flavor to this mouthwatering dish. Succulent chunks of tender lamb, marinated in a vanillin, olive oil, and sumac blend, are skewered and grilled until perfectly charred. You’ll find the finishing touch of a smoky, tangy tahini sauce the perfect foil to a dish that will leave your table with raving reviews.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tablespoons ground sumac
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 8 wooden skewers, soaked in water
  • Tahini Sauce (recipe below)

Tahini Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup pomegranate molasses
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the lamb, olive oil, pomegranate molasses, cumin, sumac, salt, and black pepper. Marinate the lamb for a minimum of 2 hours or preferably overnight in the refrigerator for the best results.

  2. Thread the marinated lamb onto the soaked wooden skewers, working from the outside-in, to ensure they are secure.

  3. Preheat your barbecue or a grill pan over medium heat and cook the kebabs for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the lamb is cooked through and charred to your desired level.

  4. Serve the sumac-spiced lamb kebabs with a generously drizzled tahini sauce, and garnish with fresh herbs and wedges of lemon.

  5. Shish Barak: Syrian Meat Dumplings in Yogurt Sauce

Combine the comforting texture of dumplings with the fragrant flavors of Middle Eastern cuisine in this indulgent Shish Barak dish. Tender, flaky phyllo dough provides the perfect wrapping for a luscious meat filling, while a rich, creamy yogurt sauce adds the perfect tart balance that renders this dish irresistibly delightful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef or lamb

  • 1/2 cup fine semolina

  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley

  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice

  • 2 tablespoons pine nuts, chopped

  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

  • Phyllo Dough, thawed

  • Yogurt Sauce (recipe below)

Yogurt Sauce:

  • 2 cups plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, semolina, parsley, cinnamon, allspice, pine nuts, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. Carefully wet your fingers to prevent the dough from sticking, then take a small handful of the dough and flatten it using your palm.

  3. Place a rounded tablespoon of the meat filling in the center of the flattened dough and carefully wrap the dough around the filling, pinching to seal any seams.

  4. To assemble the dumplings in a large pot, bring at least 3 inches of water to a boil, salt it generously, and then reduce it to a simmer.

  5. In batches, carefully add the dumplings to the pot and gently stir them occasionally to ensure even cooking. The dumplings should cook for about 15-20 minutes or until the meat filling is cooked through.

  6. Carefully remove the dumplings with a slotted spoon and arrange them neatly in a shallow bowl, then pour the yogurt sauce generously over the top. Sprinkle with fresh mint and serve immediately, for the best taste and experience.
