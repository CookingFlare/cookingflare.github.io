A Beginner's Guide to Making Macarons at Home


As a beginner with a zeal for baking, there is no better introduction to the exquisite world of French confections than macarons. These delicate meringue-based treats, combining sweetness and finesse, are not just the epitome of French gastronomy but also an ideal foundation for your culinary journey.

To demystify the process of making these enchanting pastries, we sat down with Chef Sophie, a renowned pâtissière in Paris renowned for her artisanal macarons. She kindly gave us insights on creating the perfect macaron shells, as well as advice on matcha and strawberry ganache fillings. No more pre-made macaron kits or enticing macaron shops – let us embark on this empowering adventure in the kitchens across the world.

Step 1: Gather the necessary ingredients

Before diving headfirst into the world of macarons, ensure you have the following ingredients:

  • 1½ cups of powdered sugar
  • 3/4 cup of finely ground almond flour
  • 2 large room-temperature eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 40g unsalted butter
  • A dash of pure vanilla extract
  • Green tea powder (for matcha ganache) or food coloring (for strawberry)

Step 2: Prepare the macaron shells

To create that perfect macaron shell - smooth, glossy, and with feet - start by making a dry mixture of powdered sugar and almond flour. Sift this mixture at least three times, then set it aside.

Next, whip egg whites in a stand mixer while gradually adding granulated sugar until stiff peaks form. Then, gently fold the dry mixture into the meringue with a rubber spatula. Halfway through this folding process, add a bit of vanilla extract to impart pleasant flavors.

French pastry chefs famously say, ‘age, not heat determines the quality of macarons.’ To replicate such perfection, use your weight and age instead of the oven’s temperature. By gently controlling your kitchen environment for 2-3 hours, you’ll achieve the best macaron shell with firm, easy-to-peel exteriors.

Step 3: Create the ganache filling

Construct the ganache filling by mixing cooked (mandatory!) chopped strawberries and blueberries with heated cream, and let it infuse. Once cooled, opt for a German or a French ganache by whisking chocolate of your choice into the fruit-infused cream.

For a delicious matcha green tea-ganache, incorporate matcha powder into your warmed-up cream and incorporate it into your melted chocolate.

Step 4: Assembling and serving

Carefully pipe the ganache onto the fresh macarons, matching size to ensure even filling distribution. Allow these masterpieces to mature for at least 24 hours to form a texturally blissful, unforgettable dessert.

And there you have it – the simplest of steps to creating macarons at home. As a seasoned amateur baker, there’s no topping the satisfaction that these treats bring, both in terms of taste and creating a beautiful culinary art piece. So now, all you need is some creativity, determination, and Chef Sophie’s guiding words to become a master macaron maker. Bon appétit!
