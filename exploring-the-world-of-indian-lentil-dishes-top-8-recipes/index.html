Exploring the World of Indian Lentil Dishes: Top 8 Recipes


Indian cuisine is known for its rich variety and diversity, and one of the staples that feature prominently in most Indian dishes is lentils. Whether it's the humble dal makhani, the creamyoolently flavored dal tadka, or the spicy grilled lentil skewers, lentils are an integral part of Indian cooking. In this post, we'll explore the fascinating world of Indian lentil dishes and take a closer look at the top 8 recipes that showcase the versatility and complexity of these tiny powerhouses.

  1. Dal Makhani Dal makhani, or dal makhni, is a popular Punjabi dish that originated in the city of Amritsar. This slow-cooked lentil curry is made with kidney beans, black lentils, and a robust blend of spices, including cumin, coriander, and chili peppers. The result is a hearty, comforting dish that is both flavorful and nutritious. To make dal makhani, you'll need:

1 cup kidney beans, soaked overnight and drained 1 cup black lentils, rinsed and drained 2 tablespoons mustard oil or ghee 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1 teaspoon garam masala powder Salt, to taste 2 cups water

Instructions:

Heat the mustard oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until it is lightly caramelized. Add the garlic, cumin, coriander, and garam masala powder, and cook for another minute. Add the lentils and kidney beans, and pour in the water. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils and kidney beans are tender. Season with salt and serve.

  1. Toor Dal Fry Toor dal fry is a popular South Indian dish made with split yellow lentils, also known as yellow gram. This flavorful and fragrant dal is typically made with a simple tempering of spices, including cumin, coriander, and chili peppers. To make toor dal fry, you'll need:

1 cup split yellow lentils 2 cups water 1 tablespoon ghee or oil 1 onion, finely chopped 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon coriander seeds 1 teaspoon chili peppers, crushed Salt, to taste

Instructions:

Rinse the lentils and soak them in water for 30 minutes. Drain the lentils and place them in a blender or food processor. Add 2 cups of water to the blender and blend the mixture until it is smooth. Heat the ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, and crushed chili peppers, and let them sizzle for a few seconds. Add the blended lentil mixture to the pan and stir well. Cook the dal for 10-15 minutes, or until it thickens slightly. Season with salt and serve.

  1. Mysore Masala Dosa Mysore masala dosa is a popular South Indian dish made with rice and lentil batter, which is fermented overnight to give it a sour taste. The dosa is stuffed with a spicy filling made with potatoes, onions, and chutneys. To make Mysore masala dosa, you'll need:

1 cup split black lentils 1 cup rice 1/4 cup fermented batter 1 onion, finely chopped 2 potatoes, boiled and mashed 1 tablespoon butter 1 teaspoon cumin powder Salt, to taste

Instructions:

Rinse the lentils and soak them in water for 30 minutes. Drain the lentils and place them in a blender or food processor. Add the rice, fermented batter, and salt to the blender and blend the mixture until it is smooth. Heat the butter in a pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until it is lightly caramelized. Add the mashed potatoes, cumin powder, and salt, and mix well. Stuff the dosa with the filling and serve.
