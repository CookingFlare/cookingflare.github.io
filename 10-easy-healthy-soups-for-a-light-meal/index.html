10 Easy Healthy Soups for a Light Meal


When it comes to preparing a light meal, soups are often the unsung heroes of the culinary world. Not only are they comforting and satisfying, but they can also be incredibly healthy and quick to prepare. Whether you’re looking for a warm bowl to enjoy on a chilly evening or a fresh option for a sunny afternoon, these 10 easy healthy soups will keep you nourished and energized.

1. Classic Tomato Basil Soup

This timeless favorite is simple to make and rich in flavor. The fresh basil adds a delightful aroma and taste that elevates the humble tomato soup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (14.5 ounces each) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté until translucent.
  2. Stir in the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the fresh basil. Use an immersion blender to blend until smooth.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot.

2. Hearty Vegetable Soup

Packed with nutrients, this vegetable soup is a rainbow of flavors and textures. It’s a great way to use up leftover vegetables!

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 ounces) white beans, drained and rinsed
  • Salt, pepper, and herbs to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, cooking until softened.
  2. Stir in the zucchini and bell pepper, cooking for an additional 5 minutes.
  3. Add the vegetable broth and white beans. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 25 minutes.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and your choice of herbs. Serve warm.

3. Creamy Broccoli Soup

This creamy broccoli soup is so delicious, you won’t believe it’s dairy-free! It’s smooth, velvety, and full of nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups broccoli florets
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add the broccoli and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and blend until smooth with an immersion blender. Stir in almond milk and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with extra broccoli florets if desired.

4. Spicy Lentil Soup

This spicy lentil soup is not only filling but also packed with protein and fiber. It’s perfect for those who enjoy a little heat in their meals.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Stir in lentils, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Add cumin and chili powder.
  3. Bring to a boil, reduce to a simmer, and cook for 30-35 minutes until lentils are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot, optionally garnished with fresh cilantro.

5. Chicken and Quinoa Soup

This nourishing soup combines lean chicken and quinoa for a satisfying meal that’s perfect any time of the year.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 pound chicken breast, cooked and shredded
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery, cooking until softened.
  2. Stir in the shredded chicken, quinoa, chicken broth, and thyme.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 25-30 minutes until quinoa is tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Serve warm.

6. Sweet Potato and Coconut Soup

This sweet potato and coconut soup is not only delicious but also packed with vitamins and minerals. The coconut milk adds a creamy texture without dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add sweet potatoes, vegetable broth, and ginger. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 20-25 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender.
  3. Blend until smooth. Stir in coconut milk and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro or a sprinkle of chili flakes.

7. Curried Carrot Soup

This curried carrot soup is bright, flavorful, and incredibly easy to make. The curry powder adds a warm spice that perfectly complements the sweetness of the carrots.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 pound carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and sauté until soft.
  2. Add chopped carrots, vegetable broth, and curry powder. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 25 minutes until carrots are tender.
  3. Blend until smooth and season with salt and pepper. Serve warm.

8. Zucchini and Corn Soup

This light and refreshing zucchini and corn soup is perfect for summer. It’s bursting with fresh flavors and is incredibly quick to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 zucchinis, chopped
  • 2 cups corn (fresh or frozen)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened.
  2. Stir in zucchini and corn, cooking for about 5 minutes.
  3. Add vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Blend until smooth and season with salt and pepper. Serve hot or cold.

9. Mushroom Barley Soup

This hearty mushroom barley soup is perfect for a filling yet light meal. The barley adds a chewy texture and is a great source of fiber.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 cup barley
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • Thyme and bay leaf for seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and mushrooms, sautéing until softened.
  2. Stir in carrots and barley, cooking for an additional 2 minutes.
  3. Add vegetable broth, thyme, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 30-35 minutes until barley is tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Remove bay leaf before serving.

10. Spinach and White Bean Soup

This spinach and white bean soup is nutrient-dense and flavorful, making it a perfect option for a light meal. It’s quick to prepare and can be made in under 30 minutes!

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups spinach, fresh or frozen
  • 1 can (15 ounces) white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
  2. Stir in spinach and white beans, cooking until spinach wilts.
  3. Add vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Serve warm.

Conclusion

These 10 easy healthy soups are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re looking for a quick lunch, a light dinner, or a comforting bowl on a chilly day. With a variety of ingredients and flavors, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So grab your pot and get cooking! Your taste buds and your body will thank you.
