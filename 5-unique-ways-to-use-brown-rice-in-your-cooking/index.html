5 Unique Ways to Use Brown Rice in Your Cooking


Brown rice is a versatile, nutritious, and flavorful ingredient that can be used in various dishes. It is not only a great substitute for white rice but also a fantastic base for meals that require a bit more texture and depth. Whether you are looking to create savory sides, wholesome main dishes, or even breakfast options, brown rice can enhance the flavor and nutritional value of your meals.

If you’ve been cooking the same old way, it’s time to get creative with brown rice. Here are five unique and exciting ways to use brown rice in your cooking that will elevate your culinary skills and satisfy your taste buds.

1. Brown Rice Stir-Fry with a Twist

When you think of stir-fry, your mind might immediately go to white rice, but brown rice adds a whole new level of texture and health benefits to the dish. Its nutty flavor and chewy texture make it an ideal base for stir-fries. To make a brown rice stir-fry, all you need are some fresh vegetables, a protein of your choice, and a flavorful sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cooked brown rice
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/2 cup cooked chicken, beef, or tofu (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Brown Rice: If you haven’t done so already, cook the brown rice according to package instructions. Brown rice typically takes longer to cook than white rice, so plan accordingly.

  2. Cook the Vegetables: In a large skillet or wok, heat sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 2 minutes until soft. Then, add the garlic, bell pepper, carrot, zucchini, and snap peas. Stir-fry the vegetables for 4–5 minutes, ensuring they remain slightly crisp.

  3. Add the Protein: If you’re using a protein, add it now and stir to combine. You can use leftover chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu for a plant-based option.

  4. Combine with Rice: Add the cooked brown rice to the skillet. Stir well to mix the rice with the vegetables and protein.

  5. Flavor the Stir-Fry: In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), ginger, and rice vinegar. Pour this sauce over the rice and stir to evenly coat the mixture.

  6. Finish and Serve: Sprinkle sesame seeds and fresh cilantro over the stir-fry just before serving. This dish is perfect with a side of pickled vegetables or a light soup.

Brown rice stir-fry is a perfect way to use up leftover rice and vegetables, and you can customize it according to your taste and dietary preferences. Whether it’s a quick weekday dinner or a meal prep favorite, this dish is sure to be a hit.

2. Brown Rice and Bean Burrito Bowls

Burrito bowls are a fun and customizable dish that’s easy to prepare and packed with nutrients. The brown rice serves as the hearty base, and you can pile on your favorite toppings, including beans, avocado, and salsa. These burrito bowls are a great option for a filling, healthy lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup black beans (or any beans you prefer), drained and rinsed
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes or salsa
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: shredded cheese, sour cream, or Greek yogurt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Brown Rice: Cook the brown rice and set it aside to cool slightly. Brown rice can take longer to cook, so it’s best to prepare it ahead of time.

  2. Prepare the Beans and Vegetables: In a small saucepan, heat the beans over low heat, adding cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir occasionally until warmed through. You can also season the corn with a little bit of salt and pepper, and a squeeze of lime for extra flavor.

  3. Assemble the Bowl: In a large bowl, start with a base of brown rice. Then, layer on the black beans, corn, and fresh vegetables. Top with sliced avocado, diced tomatoes or salsa, and a sprinkle of chopped cilantro.

  4. Finish and Serve: Squeeze fresh lime juice over the entire bowl, and add any extra toppings you like, such as shredded cheese, sour cream, or Greek yogurt. Mix everything together or enjoy it in layers for a burst of flavors with each bite.

This brown rice and bean burrito bowl is not only vegetarian but also packed with fiber, protein, and healthy fats. You can switch up the ingredients according to your taste preferences, adding grilled chicken or shrimp for extra protein.

3. Brown Rice Pudding for Dessert

When most people think of rice pudding, they imagine using white rice, but brown rice can give this classic dessert a nutty and hearty twist. The chewy texture of brown rice complements the creaminess of the milk, creating a rich and comforting dish that’s perfect for dessert or a sweet breakfast treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 2 1/2 cups whole milk (or almond milk for a dairy-free option)
  • 1/4 cup sugar (or honey for a natural sweetener)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt
  • Optional toppings: raisins, chopped nuts, fresh berries

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Brown Rice: If you don’t already have cooked brown rice, prepare it ahead of time. Leftover rice works great for this recipe.

  2. Combine the Ingredients: In a saucepan, combine the cooked brown rice, milk, sugar, cinnamon, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.

  3. Simmer and Stir: Let the rice pudding cook for about 25-30 minutes, stirring frequently until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy. The rice should remain slightly chewy, but it will absorb the milk and become tender.

  4. Finish and Serve: Once the pudding reaches your desired consistency, remove it from the heat. You can serve it warm or chilled. Top with raisins, chopped nuts, or fresh berries for added texture and sweetness.

This brown rice pudding is a healthier version of the traditional dessert. It’s a great way to use leftover rice and enjoy a comforting dish with fewer calories than its traditional counterpart.

4. Brown Rice Veggie Patties

Brown rice can be transformed into delicious veggie patties that are perfect for a light lunch or dinner. Packed with vegetables and grains, these patties are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them an excellent vegetarian option or a fun alternative to traditional meat patties.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1/2 cup grated carrot
  • 1/2 cup chopped spinach or kale
  • 1/4 cup diced onion
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (use gluten-free if preferred)
  • 1 egg (or flax egg for vegan)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Brown Rice: Make sure your brown rice is cooked and cooled. Using slightly chilled rice makes it easier to form patties.

  2. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked brown rice, grated carrot, chopped spinach, diced onion, breadcrumbs, egg, soy sauce, garlic powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix everything until it’s well combined and forms a thick dough-like consistency.

  3. Form the Patties: Take small portions of the rice mixture and shape them into patties. Press down gently to form a round shape.

  4. Cook the Patties: Heat a little olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the patties for 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: Serve these veggie patties with a side of fresh salad or in a bun as a burger alternative. You can also top them with a dollop of yogurt or a squeeze of lemon for extra flavor.

These brown rice veggie patties are a delicious and nutritious alternative to store-bought veggie burgers. They are customizable with your favorite vegetables, and they make a great meal prep option too.

5. Brown Rice Salad with Roasted Vegetables

Brown rice salads are a great way to incorporate whole grains into your diet while enjoying fresh, roasted vegetables. This salad can be served warm or cold and is perfect for meal prepping for the week. The earthy flavor of the brown rice pairs wonderfully with the roasted sweetness of the vegetables and a tangy dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes (or other root vegetables)
  • 1/2 cup roasted Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese (optional)
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Vegetables: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the sweet potatoes and Brussels sprouts in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and roast for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until tender and caramelized.

  2. Prepare the Rice: While the vegetables are roasting, cook the brown rice according to package instructions. Let it cool slightly before adding it to the salad.

  3. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.

  4. Assemble the Salad: In a large bowl, combine the cooked brown rice, roasted vegetables, red onion, and feta cheese. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Top with fresh parsley and serve warm or chilled.

This brown rice salad is a fantastic way to enjoy hearty, healthy ingredients that are both satisfying and flavorful. You can also add protein, like grilled chicken or chickpeas, to make the salad a more complete meal.

These five unique ways to use brown rice in your cooking prove that this versatile ingredient can be the star of any meal. Whether you are preparing a stir-fry, a hearty salad, or a comforting dessert, brown rice provides a wholesome and delicious base. Try incorporating these ideas into your meal planning for the week, and enjoy the benefits of this nutrient-packed whole grain!
