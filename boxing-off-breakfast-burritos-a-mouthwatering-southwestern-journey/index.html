Boxing Off Breakfast Burritos: A Mouthwatering Southwestern Journey


There’s something magical about rolling up your breakfast in a tortilla and diving into a flavorful adventure with every bite. Let’s journey into a scrumptious Southwestern sensory experience! From creamy avocado to spicy salsa, we’ll boxer off our breakfast with a punch of vibrant flavors in these breakfast burritos.

Don’t be fooled by their size – these breakfast burritos are a compact powerhouse that’ll keep you energized and smiling until lunchtime. The beauty of this recipe lies in its versatility. You can customize it to your taste and dietary preferences. Let’s start boxing off our breakfast!

What You’ll Need

  • 6 large tortillas (you can choose between flour or corn)
  • 1 pound cooked breakfast sausage
  • 2 ripe avocados, cubed
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 small jalapeno, seeded and minced
  • 2 large eggs, whisked
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • ½ cup shredded cheddar or pepper jack cheese
  • ½ cup salsa (choose your desired heat level)
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, cilantro, green onions

The Bomb-astic Preparation

  1. In a non-stick skillet over medium heat, cook the breakfast sausage, breaking it apart, until no longer pink. Season with a dash of salt and pepper.
  2. Simultaneously, in another non-stick skillet, whisk and scramble the eggs over medium heat. Don’t overcook the eggs – just until they’re set.
  3. While the sausage and eggs cook, prepare the vegetables. Gently dice the avocado, onion, and jalapeno. Remember to wear gloves when handling the jalapeno to prevent burning your hands.
  4. Assembly line time! Lay out the tortillas on a clean surface. Evenly divide the sausage, egg, avocado, onion, jalapeno, and cheese among the tortillas.
  5. Mix the salsa and remaining salt and pepper; drizzle it judiciously over your tortilla contents.
  6. Here comes the fun part! Box each burrito using the following method: a. Place a quarter of the filling about an inch from the bottom edge of the tortilla. b. Fold the bottom of the tortilla up to cover the filling. c. Take the left edge of the tortilla and fold it over the filling. d. Finally, roll the rest of the tortilla closed like a burrito. e. Repeat this process for all tortillas, making sure to keep your burritos snug and secure.
  7. Your burritos can be eaten immediately or stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

Wrapping Up

As the sun peeks over the horizon, you can pack your day with an extra bounce thanks to these Southwestern boxing off breakfast burritos. Don’t be afraid to mix it up with other ingredients like shredded chicken, black beans, or sautéed veggies. And remember, although the competition’s fierce, this isn’t the boxing ring – so have fun experimenting in the kitchen!

Now you’ve got your breakfast burrito game on point, we can’t wonder where the day will take you. After this culinary journey across the Southwest, start your next adventure with a satisfying and invigorating meal. So, go ahead – it’s breakfast burrito time!
