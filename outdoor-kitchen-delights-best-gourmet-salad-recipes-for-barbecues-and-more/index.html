Outdoor Kitchen Delights: Best Gourmet Salad Recipes for Barbecues and More


Ah, summertime! The perfect excuse to break out your grilling tools and invite friends and family over for a backyard barbecue. What could be more refreshing than a delicious gourmet salad in the warm weather, right? Nothing, we say! As much as we love grilled meats, you’d be surprised at the versatility of your outdoor kitchen. Elevate your summer gatherings with this selection of salad recipes that are sure to impress your guests.

  1. Grilled Watermelon and Halloumi Salad

Summer salads are all about bringing out the natural flavors of fresh vegetables and fruits. This grilled watermelon and halloumi salad does just that! The sweetness of the watermelon pairs perfectly with the salty, crispy texture of halloumi cheese, while the zesty lemon dressing pulls it all together.

Ingredients: For the Salad:

  • 1 seedless watermelon, sliced into 1-inch wedges
  • 8 oz halloumi cheese, sliced into 1/2-inch thick planks
  • 1/2 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cucumber, sliced
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves For the Dressing:
  • 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. Brush the watermelon and halloumi slices with the olive oil, then season with salt and pepper.

  3. Grill the halloumi for 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown, and the watermelon for about 2 minutes on each side until slightly charred.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, minced garlic, remaining olive oil, salt, pepper, and honey to create your lemon dressing.

  5. Arrange the grilled watermelon and halloumi on a large serving platter, with the cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and fresh herbs.

  6. Drizzle the lemon dressing generously over the salad and serve immediately.

  7. Quinoa Edamame Salad

Quinoa is a go-to ingredient for a hearty summer salad, as it’s high in protein and fiber, and extremely versatile. This protein-packed quinoa and edamame salad works well as a main dish or side, and is filled with healthy, fresh ingredients for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed and cooked according to package instructions
  • 1 cup shelled edamame, cooked according to package instructions
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, edamame, chickpeas, bell pepper, cucumber, red onion, pumpkin seeds, and cilantro.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lime juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper to create your lime dressing.

  3. Pour the lime dressing over the salad and mix thoroughly to combine.

  4. Adjust seasoning to taste and refrigerate until ready to serve.

  5. Spinach and Strawberry Salad with Crispy Prosciutto

This crispy prosciutto spinach and strawberry salad will add a touch of gourmet flair to your barbecue spread. The salty prosciutto is a perfect complement to the sweetness of the strawberries, while the baby spinach provides a fresh, healthy base.

Ingredients: For the Salad:

  • 6 cups baby spinach
  • 1 cup strawberries, sliced
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese crumbles
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/4 cup thinly sliced almonds
  • Crispy prosciutto (recipe below) For the Dressing:
  • 3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Crispy Prosciutto:

  • 8 thin slices prosciutto
  • 1/ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. To make the crispy prosciutto, first, season the flour with salt and pepper. Coat the prosciutto slices in the seasoned flour, then dip them in the beaten eggs, and finally coat them in the panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Place the coated prosciutto on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, then bake for 10-12 minutes or until crispy.
  4. While the prosciutto is baking, in a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, honey, olive oil, salt, and pepper to create your balsamic dressing.
  5. In a large serving bowl, combine the baby spinach, strawberries, goat cheese, red onion, and almonds.
  6. Drizzle the balsamic dressing over the salad, then top with the crispy prosciutto before serving.

In conclusion, outdoor kitchens are not just limited to your traditional barbecue fare. These gourmet salad recipes will showcase the range and versatility of your outdoor kitchen, and they’ll make a delightful addition to your summer gatherings. So, dust off the grill, heat up that outdoor pizza oven, and get ready to impress your friends and family with these tantalizing salad recipes. Bon appétit!
