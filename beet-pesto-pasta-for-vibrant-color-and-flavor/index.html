Beet Pesto Pasta for Vibrant Color and Flavor


If you’re looking for a dish that’s bursting with color, flavor, and nutrients, look no further than beet pesto pasta. This stunning recipe takes the familiar pesto pasta and elevates it with the earthy sweetness of beets, creating a visually captivating and deliciously unique experience. Packed with healthy ingredients, it’s a perfect option for lunch or dinner that’s both comforting and nourishing.

Whether you’re a fan of vibrant dishes or simply looking to try something new, beet pesto pasta is sure to impress. The bold flavor of roasted beets combined with the richness of nuts, olive oil, and parmesan creates a creamy, irresistible pesto sauce. Plus, it’s incredibly versatile—serve it with your favorite pasta, or even use it as a dip for fresh veggies. Let’s dive into the recipe, and explore the simple process of making this one-of-a-kind dish.

What Makes Beet Pesto Pasta So Special?

Beets are often known for their deep purple color, but they offer more than just aesthetics. Beets are rich in vitamins and minerals, including folate, manganese, and potassium. These root vegetables also boast antioxidants, which can help reduce inflammation and promote heart health. Roasting the beets enhances their natural sweetness, making them a perfect complement to the savory flavors of the pesto sauce.

Pesto, traditionally made with basil, garlic, pine nuts, parmesan, and olive oil, gets a makeover here. The bright beetroot replaces the basil, while still keeping that familiar pesto texture and consistency. The earthiness of beets blends beautifully with the richness of the cheese and nuts, while the garlic and olive oil add depth and flavor.

Not only is beet pesto pasta packed with nutrition, but it also adds a beautiful pop of color to your plate. The vibrant pinkish-red hue is sure to make this dish a showstopper at any dinner table.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Beet Pesto:

  • 2 medium-sized beets, roasted and peeled
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts (or walnuts, if preferred)
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup fresh herbs like parsley or arugula for added flavor

For the Pasta:

  • 12 oz of your favorite pasta (spaghetti, fettuccine, or penne work great)
  • Salt for the pasta water

Garnishes:

  • Extra grated parmesan cheese
  • Fresh herbs (parsley, basil, or arugula)
  • A drizzle of olive oil

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Roasting the Beets

Before you start on the pesto sauce, it’s essential to prepare the beets. Roasting them will bring out their natural sweetness and create a smooth texture for the pesto. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Wash and peel the beets. To make peeling easier after roasting, trim off the beet greens (saving them for another use) and leave the root ends intact.
  3. Wrap each beet in aluminum foil and place them on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast the beets in the oven for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until they are tender when pierced with a fork.
  5. Let the beets cool for a few minutes, then peel off the skins. The skin should come off easily, revealing the bright, vibrant root underneath.

Once the beets are roasted and peeled, you can set them aside while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.

Step 2: Preparing the Beet Pesto Sauce

Now that the beets are ready, it’s time to make the pesto sauce. This step is simple and takes only a few minutes:

  1. In a food processor, combine the roasted beets, pine nuts, parmesan, garlic, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  2. Pulse a few times until everything is finely chopped.
  3. With the food processor running, slowly drizzle in the olive oil until the pesto reaches a smooth, creamy consistency. If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can pulse the ingredients less to keep some texture.
  4. Taste the pesto and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or lemon juice, depending on your preference.

Feel free to experiment with the ingredients! If you like a more herbal flavor, you can add a handful of fresh herbs like parsley or arugula into the pesto for added depth.

Step 3: Cooking the Pasta

While you’re making the pesto, it’s time to cook the pasta:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add your pasta and cook according to the package instructions.
  2. Once the pasta is cooked al dente, reserve a small cup of pasta water to help thin the pesto if necessary. Then, drain the pasta.

Step 4: Combining the Pasta and Beet Pesto

Now comes the fun part: mixing everything together!

  1. In a large bowl, toss the cooked pasta with the beet pesto. If the sauce is too thick, add a little bit of the reserved pasta water, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired consistency. The starchy pasta water will help the sauce cling to the pasta and make it silky smooth.
  2. Continue tossing until the pasta is evenly coated with the vibrant beet pesto.

Step 5: Serving the Dish

Plate the pasta and garnish with extra parmesan cheese and fresh herbs. A drizzle of olive oil on top can add an extra layer of richness.

The bright pink hue of the pesto against the pasta creates a stunning visual effect, making this dish a showstopper at any gathering. Serve it with a side of garlic bread or a crisp salad for a complete meal.

Why You’ll Love Beet Pesto Pasta

1. Vibrant and Unique Flavor

The combination of earthy, sweet beets and the rich, nutty flavors of pesto creates a one-of-a-kind taste. The lemon juice brightens the flavors, while the garlic and parmesan add depth and complexity. Every bite is a harmonious balance of sweet, savory, and slightly tangy flavors.

2. Packed with Nutrients

Beets are full of essential vitamins and minerals like folate, potassium, and iron. When combined with the healthy fats in olive oil and the protein in parmesan cheese, this dish becomes a nutritious powerhouse that will leave you feeling satisfied and energized.

3. Simple to Make

While it might look fancy, beet pesto pasta is surprisingly easy to prepare. Roasting the beets takes some time, but the rest of the process is quick and straightforward. You can make the pesto ahead of time and store it in the fridge, saving even more time when you’re ready to eat.

4. Customizable

Feel free to experiment with different variations of the pesto. If you’re not a fan of pine nuts, walnuts or cashews can be used for a different flavor. You can also add a touch of balsamic vinegar to the pesto for an extra layer of complexity. The pasta itself is flexible too—choose a gluten-free option, whole wheat, or even zucchini noodles for a low-carb alternative.

5. Beautiful Presentation

The bold, rich color of the beet pesto makes this dish stand out. It’s perfect for impressing guests at a dinner party, or simply for brightening up your everyday meals. The vibrant pink hue is not only visually appealing but also an indicator of all the antioxidants packed into each bite.

Variations and Tips for Beet Pesto Pasta

  • Vegan Version: For a vegan-friendly option, simply omit the parmesan cheese and substitute nutritional yeast to add a cheesy, savory flavor to the pesto.
  • Add Protein: If you want to boost the protein content, consider adding grilled chicken, chickpeas, or roasted tofu to the pasta. This will make it a more filling meal, perfect for a hearty lunch or dinner.
  • Spicy Kick: If you enjoy a little heat, add some red pepper flakes or a fresh chili to the pesto for a spicy twist. The spiciness pairs wonderfully with the sweetness of the beets.
  • Herb Variations: Feel free to switch up the herbs. Fresh basil, mint, or dill can offer a different flavor profile, making each version of the dish unique.

Storing Leftovers

If you happen to have any leftovers, they can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The pasta and pesto will keep their flavors, though the vibrant pink color may fade slightly. When reheating, you can add a little extra olive oil or a splash of pasta water to bring the dish back to life.

Final Thoughts

Beet pesto pasta is a fantastic way to bring some color, flavor, and health benefits to your dinner table. The natural sweetness of the roasted beets combined with the rich pesto creates a dish that’s as nourishing as it is delicious. Whether you’re a beet lover or just looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen.

Give this vibrant, flavorful dish a try, and you’ll see just how easy it is to elevate your pasta game with a twist of beet pesto.
