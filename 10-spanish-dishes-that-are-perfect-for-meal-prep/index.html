10 Spanish Dishes That Are Perfect for Meal Prep


Meal prepping has become a go-to strategy for those looking to save time, stay organized, and maintain a balanced diet during the week. If you’re a fan of bold flavors, vibrant colors, and dishes that transport you to the sunny coasts of Spain, incorporating Spanish cuisine into your meal prep routine is a winning move. Known for its use of fresh produce, fragrant spices, and mouthwatering combinations, Spanish food is as versatile as it is delicious.

Below, you’ll find 10 Spanish dishes that not only satisfy those culinary cravings but are perfect for preparing in advance. These recipes are ideal for busy lifestyles and provide a welcome burst of flavor to your weekly meal plan.

1. Spanish Tortilla

The Spanish tortilla, or “tortilla de patatas,” is a simple yet deeply flavorful dish made with eggs, potatoes, and onions. This versatile meal can be served warm or cold, making it a fantastic addition to your meal prep rotation.

To prepare a Spanish tortilla, thinly slice potatoes and onions, sauté them in olive oil until tender, and mix with beaten eggs. Cook the mixture in a skillet until it’s golden and firm. Once cooled, slice into wedges for easy grab-and-go portions. It works as breakfast, lunch, dinner, or even a snack. Pair with a fresh salad or roasted veggies to complete your meal.

2. Paella with Chicken and Vegetables

A classic Spanish paella is a showstopper, but it can also be adapted for meal prep. Chicken-based paella (sometimes referred to as “paella de pollo”) combines saffron-infused rice with chicken, garlic, and a colorful medley of vegetables.

Prepare a large batch, and once it has cooled, divide the portions into individual containers. The vibrant flavors from the spices and ingredients deepen over time, making this dish taste even better on subsequent days. Use airtight containers to preserve the aroma and texture, and simply reheat when ready to enjoy.

3. Albondigas (Spanish Meatballs)

Spanish albondigas are succulent meatballs simmered in a rich tomato sauce. These bite-sized morsels are packed with paprika, garlic, and parsley, delivering a flavorful punch in every bite.

For meal prep, cook a large batch of albondigas and separate them into containers with plenty of sauce to keep them moist. Pair these meatballs with a side of roasted potatoes, steamed rice, or even sautéed spinach to create a well-rounded meal. They freeze beautifully, which makes them a great option for stocking up on quick lunches or dinners.

4. Gazpacho

This chilled tomato-based soup is a Spanish delicacy that’s refreshing and satisfying, especially during warmer months. Made with ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, bell peppers, and a touch of olive oil, gazpacho is both nutritious and bursting with freshness.

Gazpacho is one of the easiest dishes to include in your meal prep routine. Blend a large batch, store it in airtight jars or containers, and simply shake or stir before serving. This dish pairs well with crusty bread or a side of roasted vegetables, and its flavor only enhances as it chills.

5. Empanadas

Empanadas are flaky pastry pockets stuffed with savory fillings such as seasoned beef, chicken, or vegetables. These portable pies are perfect for lunches on the go and can easily withstand refrigeration or freezing without losing their texture.

For meal prep, whip up a variety of empanadas and store them to enjoy throughout the week. They’re incredibly versatile and can be paired with side salads, roasted peppers, or eaten solo when you need a quick bite. Pop them into the oven or microwave to refresh them, and they’re good as new.

6. Spanish Lentil Stew (Lentejas)

Lentejas, or Spanish lentil stew, is a hardy, comforting dish made with brown lentils, carrots, onions, potatoes, and spicy chorizo sausage. With its smoky flavors and satisfying texture, this dish is perfect for meal prep and freezes beautifully.

Cook a large pot of lentejas, divide it into containers, and refrigerate or freeze portions for easy reheating later. This dish is ideal for cold days when you crave something hearty and nourishing. Serve it with crusty bread or enjoy it on its own — it’s a complete meal in every spoonful.

7. Gambas al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp)

Gambas al ajillo is a simple yet elegant dish made with shrimp, garlic, chili, and olive oil. The rich, garlicky flavor makes it a standout in Spanish cuisine, and thankfully, it’s also incredibly easy to prepare in bulk.

Prepare the shrimp, let it cool, and portion it out alongside quinoa, rice, or a medley of roasted vegetables. The olive oil and garlic sauce keep everything moist and flavorful, making it perfect for reheat-and-eat situations. This dish brings a touch of sophistication to your weekly meals and is perfect for days when you need something light but flavorful.

8. Patatas Bravas

Patatas bravas is Spain’s most well-loved tapa, featuring crispy potatoes served with a tangy, spicy tomato-based sauce. These bite-sized delights are versatile enough to pair with almost any main dish or other sides, making them perfect for meal prep.

Roast or fry the potatoes in advance and store them alongside small jars of the bravas sauce in your containers. Reheat the potatoes in the oven or toaster for maximum crispness before serving. You can enjoy them as a side, snack, or even as a base for pairing with grilled proteins.

9. Pollo al Ajillo (Garlic Chicken)

Pollo al ajillo is a quintessential Spanish chicken dish where chicken pieces are infused with garlic and fragrant herbs, then seared and simmered for deep, savory flavor. The simplicity of this recipe makes it an excellent meal prep candidate.

Cook the chicken in batches, allow it to cool, and divide the portions into your preferred serving sizes. This dish pairs beautifully with roasted vegetables, rice, or a fresh salad. The garlic-infused olive oil sauce keeps the chicken juicy and delicious, even after reheating.

10. Espinacas con Garbanzos (Spinach and Chickpeas)

Espinacas con garbanzos is a healthy and flavorful dish combining sautéed spinach with tender chickpeas, garlic, and a touch of paprika. Often served as a tapa in Spain, it has quickly become a staple for those seeking vegetarian-friendly Spanish cuisine.

Make a large batch and package it into individual containers for quick and nutritious meals throughout the week. This dish can be eaten solo or paired with grains like farro, bulgur, or rice for added substance. Not only does this recipe pack in protein and vitamins, but it also tastes even better as the flavors meld over time.

Spanish cuisine is brimming with bold spices, fresh ingredients, and versatile dishes that work beautifully for meal prepping. Whether you’re in the mood for hearty comfort food or light, refreshing flavors, there’s something on this list to suit every craving. With these recipes, you can enjoy a taste of Spain all week long — no passport required!
