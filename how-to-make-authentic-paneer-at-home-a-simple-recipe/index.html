How to Make Authentic Paneer at Home: A Simple Recipe


Paneer, the staple of Indian cuisine, is a delightful addition to various dishes, from classic curries to creamy sauces. However, store-bought paneer often lacks the rich flavor and texture that homemade paneer can provide. Making paneer at home is a simple and rewarding process that requires minimal ingredients and effort. Preparation Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes Total Time: 35 minutes Servings: 1 (approximately 200g of paneer) Ingredients:

1 liter full-fat milk 1 tablespoon lemon juice or/or 1/4 teaspoon vinegar Cheesecloth or a clean, thin cotton cloth A large bowl or container with a spout or a strainer

Making Paneer: A Simple Step-by-Step Guide Paneer making involves curdling milk with lemon juice or vinegar and then separating the curds from the whey using cheesecloth or a clean cotton cloth. Spin a large bowl under cold running water for 30 seconds to sharpen its edges, then place a cheesecloth or a clean, thin cotton cloth into the bowl. Boil the full-fat milk in a saucepan over medium heat, constantly stirring until the milk reaches its boiling point. Remove the milk from the heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. Gently stir the milk every 30 seconds until it has cooled down slightly, between 110°F to 120°F (43°C to 49°C). Slowly add the lemon juice or vinegar to the milk while gently stirring the mixture in a slow, continuous circular motion with a spoon for about 2 minutes. Use a spatula to gently fold the curds over on themselves, keeping the motion slow and smooth. Gently flip the edges of the cheesecloth or cotton cloth over the curds, allowing the excess liquid to flow into the bowl. Slowly gather the edges of the cheesecloth or cotton cloth, bringing them together to form a bundle, then twist the edges around the paneer to create a compact shape. Squeeze out as much liquid from the cloth as possible to remove excess whey. Tie the cheesecloth or cloth tightly around the paneer and place it in the bowl with the spout or under a strainer. You can also weigh down the cloth with a plate or a weight to eliminate the whey. Allow the paneer to press under its own weight, in a cool, dry place for at least 30 minutes or until most of the whey has been removed. Once the paneer has pressed, carefully remove the cloth, and the homemade paneer is ready to use in your favorite recipes. Tips for Making Perfect Paneer

Use full-fat milk for a richer flavor and a better texture. Handle the paneer gently to avoid breaking the curds or losing too much whey. Make sure the milk has cooled down before adding the lemon juice or vinegar to minimize the formation of a skin on the surface. Don't over-squeeze the cloth or handle the paneer too much, as this can cause it to break apart.

Conclusion Creating authentic paneer at home is a straightforward process that requires minimal ingredients and effort. By following the above steps and tips, you can enjoy homemade paneer with the rich flavor and texture that store-bought can only imitate.
