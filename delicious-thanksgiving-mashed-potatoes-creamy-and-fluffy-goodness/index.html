Delicious Thanksgiving Mashed Potatoes: Creamy and Fluffy Goodness


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in a feast of delectable dishes. And what’s a Thanksgiving feast without a generous serving of creamy and fluffy mashed potatoes? This classic side dish is a crowd-pleaser that never fails to bring comfort and joy to the table. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create the most delicious Thanksgiving mashed potatoes that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds of Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • Fresh chives, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into evenly sized chunks. Rinse the potatoes under cold water to remove any excess starch.

  2. In a large pot, add the potatoes and cover them with cold water. Bring the water to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to medium-low and let the potatoes simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until they are fork-tender.

  3. While the potatoes are cooking, melt the butter in a small saucepan over low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. Set aside.

  4. Once the potatoes are cooked, drain them well and return them to the pot. Use a potato masher or a fork to mash the potatoes until they are smooth and creamy.

  5. Gradually pour the melted butter and garlic mixture into the mashed potatoes, stirring well to incorporate. This will add a rich and savory flavor to the dish.

  6. In a separate saucepan, heat the heavy cream over low heat until warm. Gradually pour the warm cream into the mashed potatoes, stirring constantly. The cream will make the mashed potatoes extra creamy and luscious.

  7. Season the mashed potatoes with salt and black pepper, adjusting the seasoning to taste. Give it a final stir to ensure that all the flavors are well combined.

  8. Transfer the mashed potatoes to a serving dish and garnish with freshly chopped chives. The vibrant green color and mild onion flavor of the chives will add a beautiful finishing touch to your dish.

  9. Serve the mashed potatoes immediately while they’re still warm. They pair perfectly with roasted turkey, gravy, and all the other Thanksgiving fixings.

Tips and Variations

  • For extra richness, you can substitute half of the heavy cream with sour cream or cream cheese. This will give the mashed potatoes a tangy and decadent twist.

  • If you prefer a more rustic texture, you can leave some small chunks of potato in the mash. This will add a delightful contrast in every bite.

  • To make these mashed potatoes ahead of time, simply prepare them as instructed and transfer them to a baking dish. Cover with foil and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to serve, reheat in the oven at 350°F for about 30 minutes, or until heated through.

  • Feel free to get creative with your toppings! Crumbled bacon, shredded cheese, or even a dollop of sour cream can take your mashed potatoes to the next level.

There you have it – a foolproof recipe for the most delicious Thanksgiving mashed potatoes. The creamy and fluffy goodness of this classic dish will have your guests raving and coming back for more. So, whip up a batch of these heavenly mashed potatoes and get ready to make your Thanksgiving feast truly unforgettable. Enjoy!
