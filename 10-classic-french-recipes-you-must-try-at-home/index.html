10 Classic French Recipes You Must Try at Home


French cuisine is often celebrated for its rich flavors, elegant presentations, and time-honored techniques. While dining at a fine French restaurant can be a delightful experience, there’s something incredibly satisfying about recreating these classic dishes in your own kitchen. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a culinary novice, this guide will walk you through ten timeless French recipes that you can easily try at home. From savory entrees to decadent desserts, you’ll discover the heart of French cooking in each of these delectable dishes.

1. Coq au Vin

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 750ml red wine (preferably Burgundy)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 150g bacon lardons
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 250g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with the red wine, bay leaves, and thyme. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

  2. Sear the Bacon: In a large Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the bacon lardons and cook until crispy. Remove and set aside.

  3. Brown the Chicken: Drain the chicken, reserving the marinade. In the same pot, add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides. Remove and set aside.

  4. Sauté Vegetables: Add the onions, carrots, and garlic to the pot, cooking until softened. Stir in the mushrooms and cook for a few more minutes.

  5. Make the Sauce: Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables, stirring to combine. Add the tomato paste, browned chicken, and reserved marinade. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil.

  6. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 1.5 hours, or until the chicken is tender.

  7. Serve: Remove the bay leaves before serving. Enjoy this dish with a side of crusty French bread or creamy mashed potatoes.

2. Ratatouille

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Sprinkle the diced eggplant with salt and let it sit for 30 minutes to draw out moisture. Rinse and pat dry.

  2. Sauté the Onions and Garlic: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.

  3. Cook the Vegetables: Add the eggplant, zucchinis, and bell pepper to the skillet. Cook for about 10 minutes until softened.

  4. Add Tomatoes: Stir in the diced tomatoes, seasoning with salt and pepper. Cook for an additional 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Remove from heat and let it cool slightly. Garnish with fresh basil before serving. Ratatouille can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

3. Bouillabaisse

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg mixed fish (such as snapper, mussels, and shrimp)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 leeks, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 liter fish stock
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Base Ingredients: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, leeks, and garlic, cooking until softened.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Stock: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste, cooking for a few minutes. Pour in the fish stock and bring to a simmer.

  3. Incorporate Saffron and Orange Zest: Add the saffron and orange zest, seasoning with salt and pepper. Simmer for 15 minutes.

  4. Add Fish: Gently add the mixed fish and seafood to the pot, cooking for an additional 10-15 minutes until the fish is cooked through.

  5. Serve: Ladle the bouillabaisse into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve with crusty bread and a side of rouille for an authentic touch.

4. Tarte Tatin

Ingredients:

  • 6 apples (such as Granny Smith), peeled, cored, and halved
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 150g sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry
  • Whipped cream, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Caramelize the Apples: In a skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sugar and cook until it turns golden brown. Stir in the apples and vanilla extract, cooking until the apples are caramelized.

  2. Prepare the Pastry: Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F). Roll out the puff pastry to fit your skillet over the apples.

  3. Bake: Cover the apples with the pastry, tucking the edges down into the skillet. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown.

  4. Invert and Serve: Allow the tart to cool for a few minutes, then carefully invert onto a serving plate. Serve warm with whipped cream.

5. Quiche Lorraine

Ingredients:

  • 1 pie crust
  • 150g bacon, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 large eggs
  • 300ml heavy cream
  • 100g grated Gruyère cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pre-bake the Pie Crust: Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F). Place the pie crust in a pie dish and pre-bake for 10 minutes until lightly golden.

  2. Cook the Bacon and Onion: In a skillet, cook the bacon until crispy. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent. Remove from heat and let cool.

  3. Prepare the Egg Mixture: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and heavy cream. Stir in the cheese, bacon, and onion. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Fill the Crust: Pour the egg mixture into the pre-baked crust, spreading it evenly.

  5. Bake: Bake for 35-40 minutes until the quiche is set and golden on top.

  6. Garnish and Serve: Let cool slightly before slicing. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm or at room temperature.

6. Crème Brûlée

Ingredients:

  • 500ml heavy cream
  • 100g sugar
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Extra sugar for caramelizing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 150°C (300°F).

  2. Heat the Cream: In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  3. Whisk Egg Yolks and Sugar: In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy. Gradually pour in the warm cream, whisking continuously. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  4. Bake: Pour the mixture into ramekins and place them in a baking dish. Fill the dish with hot water until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until set.

  5. Chill: Remove from the oven and let cool. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.

  6. Caramelize the Top: Before serving, sprinkle a thin layer of sugar on top of each crème brûlée and use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until golden brown.

7. Beef Bourguignon

Ingredients:

  • 1.5kg beef chuck, cut into cubes
  • 750ml red wine (Burgundy preferred)
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 150g bacon, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 250g pearl onions
  • 250g mushrooms, quartered
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Beef: In a large bowl, combine beef cubes with red wine, bay leaves, and thyme. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

  2. Sear the Bacon: In a large Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Cook the bacon until crispy, then remove and set aside.

  3. Brown the Beef: Drain the beef, reserving the marinade. In the same pot, brown the beef cubes on all sides. Remove and set aside.

  4. Sauté Vegetables: Add the onions, carrots, and garlic to the pot, cooking until softened. Stir in the flour and cook for an additional minute.

  5. Simmer: Add the reserved marinade, beef broth, and browned beef back into the pot.
