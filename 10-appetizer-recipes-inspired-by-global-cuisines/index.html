10 Appetizer Recipes Inspired by Global Cuisines


When it comes to cooking, one of the most exciting aspects is exploring different cuisines from around the world. Each culture has its own unique flavors, ingredients, and techniques that can add a new dimension to your cooking. In this post, we’ll take you on a culinary journey, exploring 10 appetizer recipes inspired by global cuisines.

1. Korean-Style Fried Chicken Wings

Inspired by the popular Korean dish, “Yangnyeom Chicken,” these crispy fried chicken wings are marinated in a sweet and spicy sauce made with gochujang, soy sauce, garlic, and brown sugar. Serve them with a side of kimchi slaw for a flavorful and spicy kick.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken wings
  • 1/2 cup gochujang
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp crushed black pepper
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, garlic, brown sugar, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and black pepper.
  2. Add the chicken wings to the marinade and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  3. Preheat the oil to 350°F.
  4. Remove the chicken wings from the marinade and coat with flour.
  5. Fry the chicken wings until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Drain the chicken wings on paper towels and serve hot.

2. Indian-Style Samosas with Spiced Potatoes and Peas

Inspired by the popular Indian snack, “samosas,” these crispy fried pastries are filled with a flavorful mixture of spiced potatoes and peas. Serve them with a side of tamarind chutney for a tangy and sweet contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup lukewarm water
  • Filling ingredients:
    • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
    • 1 cup peas
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tsp ground cumin
    • 1 tsp ground coriander
    • 1/2 tsp turmeric
    • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
    • Salt, to taste
    • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.
  2. Add the ghee or oil and mix until the dough comes together.
  3. Gradually add the lukewarm water and knead the dough for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into 6-8 equal portions.
  5. Roll out each portion into a thin circle.
  6. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of each circle.
  7. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges to seal.
  8. Fry the samosas until golden brown, about 3-5 minutes.
  9. Drain the samosas on paper towels and serve hot.

3. Japanese-Style Edamame Dip

Inspired by the popular Japanese snack, “edamame,” this creamy dip is made with boiled edamame, garlic, ginger, and soy sauce. Serve it with crudités or pita chips for a healthy and flavorful snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup edamame, boiled and shelled
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine edamame, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, tahini, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy, adding water as needed.
  3. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  4. Serve the dip with crudités or pita chips.

4. Moroccan-Style B’stilla with Spinach and Feta

Inspired by the popular Moroccan dish, “b’stilla,” this savory pie is made with layers of flaky pastry, spinach, feta cheese, and spices. Serve it as an appetizer or snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Roll out the puff pastry to a thickness of about 1/8 inch.
  3. In a bowl, combine spinach, feta cheese, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread the spinach mixture over the center of the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  5. Fold the edges of the pastry over the filling, pressing gently to seal.
  6. Brush the pastry with olive oil and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Serve the b’stilla warm or at room temperature.

5. Mexican-Style Guacamole with Roasted Tomatoes

Inspired by the popular Mexican dip, “guacamole,” this creamy dip is made with roasted tomatoes, avocados, lime juice, and spices. Serve it with tortilla chips or crudités for a flavorful and healthy snack.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe avocados, halved and pitted
  • 2 cups roasted tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 lime, juiced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine avocados, roasted tomatoes, lime juice, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy, adding water as needed.
  3. Stir in cilantro and jalapeño pepper.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  5. Serve the guacamole with tortilla chips or crudités.

6. Thai-Style Spring Rolls with Shrimp and Vegetables

Inspired by the popular Thai snack, “spring rolls,” these crispy fried rolls are filled with a flavorful mixture of shrimp, vegetables, and spices. Serve them with a side of sweet chili sauce for a tangy and sweet contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package spring roll wrappers
  • 1/2 cup cooked shrimp, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped mixed vegetables (such as carrots, zucchini, and cabbage)
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine shrimp, mixed vegetables, scallions, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, turmeric, salt, and pepper.
  2. Lay a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface.
  3. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the bottom half of the wrapper over the filling, then fold in the sides and roll up the wrapper to form a tight cylinder.
  5. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  6. Fry the spring rolls until golden brown, about 3-5 minutes.
  7. Drain the spring rolls on paper towels and serve hot.

7. Middle Eastern-Style Falafel with Spiced Chickpeas

Inspired by the popular Middle Eastern street food, “falafel,” these crispy fried chickpea patties are flavored with spices and herbs. Serve them with a side of tahini sauce for a creamy and tangy contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Tahini sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine chickpeas, parsley, cilantro, scallions, garlic, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
  2. Using your hands, shape the mixture into small patties.
  3. Coat the patties with flour, shaking off any excess.
  4. Fry the falafel until golden brown, about 3-5 minutes.
  5. Drain the falafel on paper towels and serve with tahini sauce.

8. Italian-Style Bruschetta with Tomato and Basil

Inspired by the popular Italian appetizer, “bruschetta,” this toasted bread is topped with a flavorful mixture of tomatoes, basil, garlic, and mozzarella cheese. Serve it as an appetizer or snack.

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 slices baguette
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
  2. Slice the baguette into 1-inch thick slices.
  3. Place the slices on a baking sheet and toast until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. In a bowl, combine tomatoes, basil, garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Spoon the tomato mixture over the toasted bread slices.
  6. Top with mozzarella cheese and serve warm.

9. Chinese-Style Dumplings with Pork and Vegetables

Inspired by the popular Chinese dish, “dumplings,” these steamed dumplings are filled with a flavorful mixture of pork, vegetables, and spices. Serve them with a side of soy sauce for a savory and umami contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package dumpling wrappers
  • 1/2 cup ground pork
  • 1/2 cup chopped mixed vegetables (such as cabbage, carrots, and scallions)
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine pork, mixed vegetables, scallions, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  2. Lay a dumpling wrapper on a flat surface.
  3. Place a tablespoon of the filling in the center of the wrapper.
  4. Fold the wrapper over the filling, pressing gently to seal.
  5. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.
  6. Steam the dumplings until cooked through, about 10-12 minutes.
  7. Serve the dumplings with soy sauce.

10. Peruvian-Style Ceviche with Sea Bass and Lime Juice

Inspired by the popular Peruvian dish, “ceviche,” this refreshing appetizer is made with raw sea bass, lime juice, and spices. Serve it with a side of crispy corn kernels for a crunchy and tangy contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound sea bass, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup crispy corn kernels

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine sea bass, lime juice, red onion, cilantro, parsley, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stir gently to combine, being careful not to break up the fish.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the fish to “cook” in the lime juice.
  4. Just before serving, stir in the crispy corn kernels.
  5. Serve the ceviche chilled, garnished with additional cilantro and lime wedges if desired.

These 10 appetizer recipes inspired by global cuisines offer a diverse range of flavors, ingredients, and techniques to add to your cooking repertoire. From the spicy kick of Korean-style fried chicken wings to the refreshing tang of Peruvian-style ceviche, each dish is sure to delight your taste buds and leave you wanting more. So go ahead, get cooking, and explore the world of global cuisine!
