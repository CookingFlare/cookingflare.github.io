Ginger Garlic Shrimp Stir Fry: A Quick and Tasty Seafood Delight


There’s nothing quite like a delicious stir fry to satisfy your cravings for a quick and flavorful meal. And when it comes to seafood, shrimp is a popular choice that never fails to deliver. In this recipe, we’ll be combining the bold flavors of ginger and garlic with succulent shrimp to create a mouthwatering dish that is both easy to make and incredibly tasty. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 medium-sized onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and add the vegetable oil. Allow it to heat for a minute or two.

  2. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the skillet and stir-fry for about 30 seconds, or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

  3. Add the sliced bell peppers and onion to the skillet and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes, or until they start to soften.

  4. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and add the shrimp to the other side. Cook the shrimp for about 2 minutes on each side, until they turn pink and opaque.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and honey. Pour the sauce over the shrimp and vegetables, and stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes, ensuring everything is well coated.

  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and give it a final toss to combine all the flavors.

  7. Remove the skillet from heat and garnish with chopped green onions.

  8. Serve the ginger garlic shrimp stir fry hot with steamed rice or noodles.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier stir fry, you can add some red pepper flakes or a few dashes of hot sauce to the sauce mixture.

  • Feel free to add other vegetables of your choice, such as snap peas, carrots, or mushrooms, to enhance the nutritional value and texture of the dish.

  • For a gluten-free version, you can substitute tamari sauce for the soy sauce.

  • To make the dish even more aromatic, sprinkle some sesame seeds or drizzle sesame oil over the stir fry before serving.

Final Thoughts

Ginger garlic shrimp stir fry is a fantastic dish that brings together the vibrant flavors of ginger, garlic, and succulent shrimp. It’s a quick and easy recipe that can be whipped up in no time, making it perfect for busy weeknight dinners or when you’re craving a delicious seafood delight. The combination of the crisp bell peppers, tender shrimp, and savory sauce creates a harmonious blend of flavors that will leave your taste buds wanting more. So why not give this recipe a try and experience the joy of cooking a delightful stir fry in the comfort of your own kitchen? Enjoy!
