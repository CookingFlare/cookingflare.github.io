Easy Apple Cider Donuts Recipe for Autumn Indulgence


Autumn is the season of cozy warmth, vibrant colors, and the unmistakable aroma of fresh apples. As the leaves change and the air grows crisp, our taste buds crave the comforting flavors of the harvest season. And what better way to indulge in the autumnal spirit than with a warm, gooey, and utterly delicious apple cider donut?

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of creating these mouthwatering treats, using the finest ingredients and a dash of creativity. So, grab your apron, preheat your oven, and let’s get started!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting
  • Apple cider glaze (recipe below)
  • Fresh apple slices, for garnish

Apple Cider Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Proof the yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm milk (about 105°F to 115°F) and yeast. Stir to dissolve, then let it sit for 5 to 10 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy and bubbly.
  2. Mix the dough: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. Add the melted butter, egg, and vanilla extract, and mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Add the yeast mixture: Gradually pour the yeast mixture into the dough, mixing until a smooth, elastic batter forms.
  4. Knead the dough: Using a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment or a wooden spoon, knead the dough for 5 to 7 minutes, until it becomes smooth and slightly sticky.
  5. Let it rise: Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.
  6. Punch it down: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead it for 1 to 2 minutes, until it becomes smooth and pliable.
  7. Cut out the donuts: Use a donut cutter or a glass to cut out the donuts. You should be able to get about 6 to 8 donuts.
  8. Fry the donuts: Heat the oil in a deep frying pan or a deep fryer to 350°F. Fry the donuts in batches for 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  9. Drain and cool: Use a slotted spoon to remove the donuts from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Let them cool for 5 to 10 minutes before glazing.
  10. Glaze the donuts: Dip the cooled donuts in the apple cider glaze, or drizzle it over the top using a spoon or piping bag.
  11. Dust with confectioners’ sugar: Dust the glazed donuts with confectioners’ sugar for an extra touch of sweetness and texture.
  12. Garnish with fresh apple slices: Top the donuts with fresh apple slices for a pop of color and a nod to the autumn season.

Tips and Variations:

  • For an extra boost of apple flavor, use apple cider-infused oil instead of regular oil for frying.
  • Experiment with different types of sugar, such as brown sugar or turbinado sugar, for a unique flavor profile.
  • Add a sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg to the dough for an extra warm, spicy flavor.
  • Make a batch of apple cider donut holes by using a cookie scoop or a small ice cream scoop to portion out the dough.

As the autumn leaves rustle and the air grows crisp, these easy apple cider donuts are the perfect treat to indulge in. With their tender, fluffy texture and sweet, sticky glaze, they’re sure to become a new fall favorite. So go ahead, get baking, and let the autumnal magic begin!
