Easy College Recipes You Can Make with Only 5 Ingredients


College life often comes with a busy schedule, late-night study sessions, and a tight budget. Yet, eating well doesn’t have to be difficult or expensive. If you’re trying to keep things simple, nutritious, and affordable, this post is for you. We’ve compiled a list of easy college recipes that you can make with only five ingredients, perfect for students who need quick meals without spending hours in the kitchen.

These recipes are ideal for anyone living in a dorm, apartment, or student housing with limited access to a full kitchen. Plus, each recipe uses minimal ingredients but still packs plenty of flavor, so you can enjoy a home-cooked meal in no time. Let’s dive in!

1. One-Pot Garlic Parmesan Pasta

Ingredients:

  • Spaghetti or your favorite pasta
  • Garlic
  • Butter
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil and add your pasta. Cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, melt butter in a pan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Drain the cooked pasta, reserving a bit of the pasta water.
  4. Toss the drained pasta into the pan with garlic butter, adding a bit of the pasta water to help coat the noodles.
  5. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve warm, topped with extra Parmesan if desired.

This easy pasta dish is not only satisfying but also flavorful, combining the rich taste of garlic with the sharpness of Parmesan.

2. Avocado Toast with Egg

Ingredients:

  • Avocado
  • Eggs
  • Bread
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Instructions:

  1. Toast a slice of your favorite bread until crispy.
  2. While the bread is toasting, heat a non-stick skillet and cook an egg to your liking (fried, scrambled, or poached).
  3. Slice the avocado in half, remove the pit, and mash it with a fork. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread the mashed avocado generously on the toasted bread.
  5. Top with your cooked egg and sprinkle a little more salt and pepper for extra flavor.

This classic breakfast or lunch option is quick, healthy, and packed with protein, healthy fats, and fiber.

3. Peanut Butter Banana Smoothie

Ingredients:

  • Banana
  • Peanut butter
  • Milk (any type)
  • Ice cubes
  • Honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place a ripe banana, 1-2 tablespoons of peanut butter, a cup of milk, and a handful of ice cubes into a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth, adding honey for sweetness if desired.
  3. Pour into a glass and enjoy a delicious, protein-packed smoothie that’s perfect for breakfast or an afternoon snack.

This smoothie is filling, nutritious, and provides a good balance of carbs, protein, and healthy fats.

4. Cheesy Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • Flour tortillas
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar or your favorite kind)
  • Butter or oil
  • Salsa (optional)
  • Sour cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a pan over medium heat and add a small amount of butter or oil.
  2. Place one tortilla in the pan and sprinkle a generous amount of shredded cheese on top.
  3. Cover with another tortilla and cook for about 2 minutes on each side until golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  4. Remove from the pan and cut into wedges. Serve with salsa or sour cream for dipping.

Quesadillas are a great snack or meal because they’re so customizable—add beans, chicken, or veggies if you like, but this simple version is just as delicious.

5. Tomato and Mozzarella Caprese Salad

Ingredients:

  • Fresh tomatoes
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil
  • Balsamic vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Slice the tomatoes and mozzarella into thick rounds.
  2. Arrange them in alternating layers on a plate.
  3. Tuck fresh basil leaves between the tomato and mozzarella slices.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Enjoy this simple, refreshing salad that’s perfect as a side dish or light meal.

This salad is not only delicious but also looks impressive—making it a great option for potlucks, gatherings, or just a quick snack.

6. Pasta with Pesto Sauce

Ingredients:

  • Pasta
  • Pesto sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Olive oil
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Pine nuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package instructions. Drain, reserving a bit of the cooking water.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat some pesto sauce in a pan, adding a little olive oil to thin it out.
  3. Toss the cooked pasta in the pesto sauce, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if necessary to help coat the noodles.
  4. Top with Parmesan cheese and pine nuts for a crunchy texture.

This simple pasta dish is packed with flavor thanks to the basil and garlic in the pesto sauce.

7. Egg Salad Sandwich

Ingredients:

  • Eggs
  • Mayonnaise
  • Mustard
  • Bread
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Boil the eggs until hard boiled, about 10 minutes.
  2. Peel the eggs, chop them up, and place them in a bowl.
  3. Add a tablespoon of mayonnaise and a teaspoon of mustard, then stir until combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread the egg salad onto slices of bread to make a sandwich.

This egg salad sandwich is perfect for lunch and requires only a few ingredients, making it a cost-effective choice.

8. Baked Potatoes with Toppings

Ingredients:

  • Potatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Sour cream
  • Shredded cheese
  • Green onions (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Scrub the potatoes clean and prick them a few times with a fork.
  3. Rub the potatoes with olive oil and season with salt.
  4. Place them directly on the oven rack and bake for 45-60 minutes until tender.
  5. Slice open the potatoes and top with sour cream, shredded cheese, and chopped green onions.

Baked potatoes are a perfect canvas for your favorite toppings, and they make for a comforting meal any time of day.

9. Crispy Chickpeas Snack

Ingredients:

  • Canned chickpeas
  • Olive oil
  • Paprika
  • Garlic powder
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Drain and rinse the canned chickpeas, then pat them dry with a paper towel.
  3. Toss the chickpeas with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Spread them on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through, until crispy.
  5. Enjoy this healthy, crunchy snack that’s full of protein.

This crunchy snack is both healthy and satisfying, perfect for when you need a quick bite between classes.

10. Tuna Salad Lettuce Wraps

Ingredients:

  • Canned tuna
  • Mayonnaise
  • Celery (optional)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Drain the canned tuna and place it in a bowl.
  2. Add a tablespoon of mayonnaise and chopped celery if desired. Stir until everything is combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spoon the tuna salad into large lettuce leaves and wrap them up.

This low-carb option is great for lunch or dinner and is a refreshing alternative to a sandwich.

11. Garlic Butter Shrimp

Ingredients:

  • Shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • Butter
  • Garlic
  • Lemon
  • Parsley (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side until pink and opaque.
  4. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the shrimp and sprinkle with chopped parsley for extra flavor.

This flavorful dish is fast, simple, and perfect for any seafood lover.

12. Simple Veggie Stir Fry

Ingredients:

  • Frozen mixed vegetables
  • Soy sauce
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Rice (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add the frozen mixed vegetables and cook until heated through and slightly crispy.
  4. Stir in soy sauce and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve over rice if desired.

This veggie stir fry is a great way to get your daily serving of vegetables and can be customized with any veggies you have on hand.

These easy, five-ingredient recipes are the perfect solution for busy college students looking to save time, money, and effort while eating delicious and nutritious meals. From hearty pasta dishes to light salads, there’s something for everyone on this list. Remember, the key to great meals in college is creativity—don’t be afraid to experiment and use whatever ingredients you have on hand. Happy cooking!
