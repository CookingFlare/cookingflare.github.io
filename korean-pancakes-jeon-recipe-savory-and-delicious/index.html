Korean Pancakes (Jeon) Recipe: Savory and Delicious


If you’ve ever dined at a Korean restaurant or explored Korean home-cooking, you’ve likely come across Jeon, a traditional Korean pancake. These savory, crispy treats are not only a beloved dish in Korean cuisine but also a versatile recipe that can be customized with various ingredients. Whether enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or main dish, Jeon offers a delightful burst of flavors and textures. Today, we’ll dive into the world of Korean Pancakes and explore how to make your own Jeon at home.

What is Jeon?

Jeon (전) refers to a category of Korean pancakes made from a variety of ingredients, such as vegetables, seafood, and meat, mixed into a simple flour batter. They are pan-fried to crispy perfection and served with a dipping sauce on the side. Jeon is an integral part of Korean cuisine, often served during special occasions like holidays, family gatherings, and celebrations. It’s also a popular dish for casual meals and a great way to use up leftover ingredients.

In Korea, there are several variations of Jeon, each with its own distinctive ingredients and flavors. Some of the most well-known types include:

  • Pajeon: A savory pancake made with scallions (green onions).
  • Kimchijeon: A pancake made with kimchi, often with a spicy and tangy flavor.
  • Buchujeon: A pancake made with chives, offering a mild and aromatic flavor.

Today, we’ll focus on a classic version of Jeon that can be easily customized based on your preferences and available ingredients.

Ingredients for Korean Pancakes (Jeon)

The beauty of Jeon lies in its flexibility. You can tailor the ingredients to suit your taste, whether you prefer vegetarian options, seafood, or meat-filled versions. The basic ingredients needed for a standard Jeon recipe are simple and easy to find at your local grocery store. Here’s what you’ll need:

Basic Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup potato starch or cornstarch (optional, for extra crispiness)
  • 1 cup cold water
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Vegetables and Fillings:

  • 1 medium zucchini (julienned)
  • 1 small carrot (julienned)
  • 1/2 onion (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms (sliced) – optional, but adds great texture
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions (green onions)
  • 1/2 cup cooked shrimp or beef – optional for added protein

Dipping Sauce (Optional but Recommended):

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed garlic
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green onions

How to Make Korean Pancakes (Jeon)

Now that we’ve gathered all the necessary ingredients, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making delicious Korean pancakes.

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables and Fillings

The first step is to prepare the vegetables and any other ingredients you plan to include in your Jeon. Start by julienning the zucchini and carrot into thin strips. This will help them cook evenly and give your pancakes a beautiful texture.

Next, thinly slice the onion and chop the scallions into bite-sized pieces. If you’re using mushrooms, slice them thinly as well. If you’re adding protein, such as shrimp or cooked beef, chop them into small pieces.

Once everything is prepared, set it aside in a large mixing bowl.

Step 2: Make the Batter

In a separate bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, potato starch (if using), and a pinch of salt. The addition of potato starch helps give the pancake a slightly crispier texture, but it’s completely optional.

To the dry ingredients, add the cold water and egg. Whisk everything together until you have a smooth, lump-free batter. The batter should be somewhat thin, but not runny. You want it to be able to coat the vegetables and fillings lightly.

Step 3: Combine the Vegetables with the Batter

Now it’s time to combine the prepared vegetables and fillings with the batter. Add the zucchini, carrot, onion, scallions, and mushrooms (if using) into the batter. Mix gently so that all the ingredients are coated in the batter. If you’re using shrimp or beef, add them now as well.

You should have a nice mixture that will hold together when frying but not be overly dense.

Step 4: Fry the Pancakes

Heat a large non-stick skillet or frying pan over medium heat. Add a generous amount of vegetable oil to coat the bottom of the pan. It’s important to use enough oil to ensure the pancakes crisp up nicely.

Once the oil is hot, scoop a portion of the vegetable-batter mixture and carefully place it into the skillet. Use the back of the spoon to spread the mixture into a thin, even layer, making sure it covers the pan evenly. You can make one large pancake or several smaller ones, depending on your preference.

Fry the pancake for about 3 to 4 minutes on the first side, or until the edges are golden brown and crispy. Flip it carefully using a spatula and cook for an additional 2 to 3 minutes on the other side. The pancake should be crispy on both sides, with the vegetables tender inside.

Remove the pancake from the skillet and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 5: Make the Dipping Sauce

While the pancakes are cooking, you can prepare the dipping sauce. In a small bowl, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, crushed garlic, and toasted sesame seeds. Mix well until the sugar dissolves. Taste the sauce and adjust the flavors as needed. If you like it spicier, you can add a little gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes).

Once the pancakes are ready, serve them hot with the dipping sauce on the side.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Now that your Jeon is perfectly fried and paired with a delicious dipping sauce, it’s time to serve! Korean pancakes are typically cut into bite-sized pieces and served family-style, so everyone can dig in. Enjoy them as a main dish with steamed rice, or serve them as an appetizer or side dish to complement other Korean dishes.

Tips for Perfect Korean Pancakes

Making the perfect Jeon requires a little practice, but once you master it, you’ll be able to whip up these savory pancakes in no time. Here are some tips to ensure your pancakes turn out crispy, flavorful, and satisfying every time:

1. Use Cold Water for the Batter

Using cold water in the batter helps keep the pancakes crispier. Cold water slows down the gluten formation in the flour, resulting in a lighter and crunchier texture. It’s a small detail that makes a big difference in the final product.

2. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan

When frying Jeon, avoid overcrowding the pan. If you place too much batter in the pan at once, the pancakes will become soggy and difficult to flip. Fry the pancakes in batches to ensure they cook evenly and get crispy.

3. Adjust the Thickness of the Batter

The consistency of the batter is crucial. If it’s too thick, your pancakes will be dense and heavy. If it’s too thin, the vegetables might fall apart. Aim for a batter that’s thick enough to coat the vegetables, but not so thick that it forms clumps.

4. Experiment with Fillings

While the classic Jeon recipe calls for zucchini, carrots, onions, and scallions, feel free to experiment with other vegetables like bell peppers, spinach, or cabbage. You can also try adding proteins like pork, chicken, or tofu for a more substantial meal.

5. Serve Immediately

Korean pancakes are best enjoyed fresh and hot from the pan. The crispy texture and savory flavors are at their peak when the pancakes are served immediately. If you need to make them ahead of time, you can reheat them in a hot skillet for a couple of minutes to restore their crispiness.

Conclusion

Korean pancakes, or Jeon, are a delightful dish that combines the flavors of crispy vegetables, savory fillings, and a flavorful dipping sauce. With their versatility and ease of preparation, Jeon can be enjoyed on any occasion – from casual family meals to festive gatherings. By following this simple recipe and experimenting with different fillings, you’ll be able to create your own perfect version of Jeon.

So, next time you’re craving something savory and satisfying, skip the takeout and make your own Korean pancakes at home. With a few fresh ingredients, you can enjoy this traditional dish in the comfort of your kitchen. Happy cooking!
