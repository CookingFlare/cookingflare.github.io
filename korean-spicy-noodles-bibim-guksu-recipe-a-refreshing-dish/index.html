Korean Spicy Noodles (Bibim Guksu) Recipe: A Refreshing Dish


Korean cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and diverse textures. Among the many delightful dishes, Bibim Guksu stands out as a refreshing and spicy noodle dish that is perfect for warm weather or any time you crave something light yet satisfying. This dish combines chewy noodles with a medley of fresh vegetables and a spicy, tangy sauce that tantalizes the taste buds. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of Bibim Guksu, the ingredients needed, and a step-by-step guide to making this delicious dish at home.

What is Bibim Guksu?

Bibim Guksu, which translates to “mixed noodles,” is a popular Korean dish that features cold wheat noodles tossed in a spicy sauce. It is often enjoyed during the summer months as a refreshing meal. The dish is characterized by its vibrant colors, thanks to the variety of vegetables used, and its unique flavor profile, which balances spiciness, sweetness, and tanginess. Bibim Guksu is not only delicious but also highly customizable, allowing you to add your favorite ingredients or adjust the spice level to suit your taste.

The Origins of Bibim Guksu

Bibim Guksu has its roots in Korean street food culture, where vendors would serve quick and satisfying meals to busy city dwellers. The dish has evolved over the years, with various regional variations emerging. Traditionally, Bibim Guksu is made with thin wheat noodles, but some variations may use buckwheat noodles or even rice noodles. Regardless of the type of noodles used, the key to a great Bibim Guksu lies in the sauce, which is typically made from gochujang (Korean red chili paste), soy sauce, sesame oil, and other flavorful ingredients.

Ingredients for Bibim Guksu

To make a delicious bowl of Bibim Guksu, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Noodles:

  • 200g of thin wheat noodles (or your preferred noodle type)
  • Water for boiling

For the Spicy Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of honey or sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon of minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds (for garnish)

For the Vegetables:

  • 1 small cucumber, julienned
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup of bean sprouts (optional)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • A handful of fresh spinach or lettuce leaves (for garnish)

Optional Toppings:

  • Hard-boiled eggs, halved
  • Sliced avocado
  • Toasted seaweed strips

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Bibim Guksu

Step 1: Cook the Noodles

  1. Boil Water: In a large pot, bring water to a rolling boil. Add a pinch of salt to enhance the flavor of the noodles.

  2. Cook the Noodles: Add the thin wheat noodles to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions, usually around 4-6 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  3. Drain and Rinse: Once cooked, drain the noodles in a colander and rinse them under cold running water to stop the cooking process and cool them down. This step is crucial for achieving the refreshing quality of Bibim Guksu.

Step 2: Prepare the Spicy Sauce

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey (or sugar), and minced garlic. Whisk the ingredients together until smooth and well combined.

  2. Taste and Adjust: Taste the sauce and adjust the sweetness or spiciness according to your preference. If you like it spicier, add more gochugaru or gochujang.

Step 3: Prepare the Vegetables

  1. Julienne the Vegetables: While the noodles are cooking, prepare the vegetables. Julienne the cucumber and carrot, and thinly slice the red bell pepper. If using bean sprouts, rinse them under cold water.

  2. Chop the Green Onions: Chop the green onions and set them aside for garnish.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

  1. Combine Noodles and Sauce: In a large mixing bowl, add the cooled noodles and pour the spicy sauce over them. Toss the noodles gently to ensure they are evenly coated with the sauce.
