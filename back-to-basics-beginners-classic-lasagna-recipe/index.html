Back to Basics: Beginner's Classic Lasagna Recipe


Welcome to our cooking blog! Today, we’re going back to the basics with a classic lasagna recipe that’s perfect for beginners. Lasagna is a hearty and comforting dish that never fails to impress. With layers of pasta, meat sauce, and cheese, it’s a crowd-pleaser that will leave everyone asking for seconds. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 package (16 ounces) lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is translucent.

  2. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. While the sauce is simmering, cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  5. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles on top, slightly overlapping each other.

  6. Spoon about 1/3 of the ricotta cheese over the noodles, spreading it evenly. Sprinkle a handful of shredded mozzarella cheese on top.

  7. Repeat the layers: meat sauce, noodles, ricotta cheese, and mozzarella cheese. You should have enough ingredients for about three layers.

  8. For the final layer, spread the remaining meat sauce over the noodles and top with the grated Parmesan cheese.

  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  10. Once the lasagna is out of the oven, let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian lasagna, you can substitute the ground beef with a mixture of sautéed vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, and mushrooms.

  • Feel free to add your favorite herbs and spices to the meat sauce for extra flavor. Some popular choices include thyme, rosemary, and red pepper flakes.

  • To make ahead, assemble the lasagna but don’t bake it. Cover tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to serve, remove the plastic wrap and bake as directed.

  • Leftover lasagna can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the oven or microwave until heated through.

Now that you have the recipe for a delicious and comforting classic lasagna, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get cooking! Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or a seasoned chef, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. So roll up your sleeves and enjoy the process of creating a homemade lasagna that will impress your family and friends. Bon appétit!
