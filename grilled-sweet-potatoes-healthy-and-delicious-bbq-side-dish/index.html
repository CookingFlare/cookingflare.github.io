Grilled Sweet Potatoes: Healthy and Delicious BBQ Side Dish


Looking for a healthy and delicious side dish to complement your next BBQ? Look no further than grilled sweet potatoes! Not only are they packed with nutrients, but they also add a burst of flavor to any outdoor gathering. Whether you’re a meat lover or a vegetarian, grilled sweet potatoes are a versatile and satisfying addition to your menu. In this blog post, we’ll explore different ways to prepare and serve grilled sweet potatoes, guaranteeing a crowd-pleasing dish that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Why Choose Sweet Potatoes?

Sweet potatoes are not only tasty but also highly nutritious. They are rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making them a perfect choice for a healthy side dish. Their natural sweetness pairs well with smoky flavors from the grill, creating a delightful contrast that will leave your taste buds craving more. Additionally, sweet potatoes are low in calories and fat, making them an excellent option for those watching their waistline. So, let’s dive into the world of grilled sweet potatoes and discover some mouthwatering recipes!

Grilling Techniques

1. Classic Grilled Sweet Potatoes

Start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. Wash and scrub the sweet potatoes, leaving the skin intact for added texture and nutrients. Slice them into uniform rounds or wedges, approximately half an inch thick. Toss the sweet potato slices with olive oil, salt, and your favorite herbs and spices.

Place the sweet potato slices directly on the grill grates and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they develop beautiful grill marks and are tender when pierced with a fork. Remove from the grill and let them cool slightly before serving.

2. Sweet Potato Skewers

For a fun twist, try making sweet potato skewers. Cut the sweet potatoes into cubes and thread them onto skewers, alternating with pieces of bell peppers, onions, or your favorite vegetables. This not only adds color to your dish but also infuses the sweet potatoes with the flavors of the other grilled veggies.

Brush the skewers with a marinade of olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, and your preferred seasonings. Grill the skewers over medium heat for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the sweet potatoes are tender and slightly charred. Serve them as a vibrant and flavorful side dish.

3. Sweet Potato Fries

Who can resist a plate of crispy sweet potato fries? To achieve that perfect crunch, start by preheating your grill to medium-high heat. Cut the sweet potatoes into thin strips, resembling fries. Toss them with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, and salt.

Place the sweet potato fries on a grilling tray or a foil-lined baking sheet and grill for approximately 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy. Serve them alongside your favorite dipping sauce for a satisfying BBQ treat.

Serving Suggestions

Grilled sweet potatoes can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to spark your culinary creativity:

  • Top the grilled sweet potato rounds with a dollop of Greek yogurt and a sprinkle of fresh herbs for an elegant appetizer.
  • Add grilled sweet potato cubes to a mixed green salad for a hearty and nutritious meal.
  • Create a flavorful sweet potato and black bean burger by mashing grilled sweet potatoes with black beans, breadcrumbs, and spices. Serve on a bun with your favorite toppings.
  • Pair grilled sweet potato fries with a tangy aioli or homemade ketchup for a guilt-free indulgence.

Conclusion

Grilled sweet potatoes are a versatile and nutritious addition to any BBQ menu. Their natural sweetness and smoky flavor make them a crowd favorite, appealing to both meat lovers and vegetarians alike. Whether you choose to grill them in classic rounds, skewers, or as crispy fries, these healthy and delicious side dishes are sure to impress your guests. So fire up the grill, grab some sweet potatoes, and get ready to elevate your outdoor cooking game with this delectable BBQ side dish!
