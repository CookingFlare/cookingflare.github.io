How to Make Healthy Oatmeal Bread at Home


If you’re looking for a delicious, wholesome, and nutritious bread recipe, look no further than homemade oatmeal bread. This bread is not only easy to make but also packed with essential nutrients. Oatmeal bread combines the benefits of oats with the heartiness of traditional bread, resulting in a loaf that’s rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals. Whether you want to make a healthier version of your favorite sandwich bread or you’re simply looking for a comforting loaf to serve with soups and salads, oatmeal bread is a great choice.

In this guide, we’ll show you step-by-step how to make healthy oatmeal bread at home, using simple ingredients and easy-to-follow instructions. Let’s dive in!

Why Choose Oatmeal Bread?

Before we get into the recipe, let’s take a moment to discuss why oatmeal bread is such a great choice. Oats are naturally high in fiber, especially soluble fiber, which can help lower cholesterol levels and promote digestive health. Oats also provide a steady source of energy, making oatmeal bread a filling and satisfying option for breakfast or a snack. The addition of whole wheat flour in this recipe further boosts the nutritional profile, providing additional fiber and important vitamins.

Another benefit of oatmeal bread is that it’s incredibly versatile. You can use it to make sandwiches, toast it with some butter and jam, or enjoy it with a warm bowl of soup. It also keeps well for several days and can be frozen for later use.

Now, let’s get started with the recipe!

Ingredients

To make a delicious loaf of oatmeal bread, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup warm water (about 110°F)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or melted coconut oil
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (or use more whole wheat flour for a denser loaf)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon ground flaxseeds (optional, for extra fiber and omega-3s)
  • 1 tablespoon chia seeds (optional, for extra texture and nutrients)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional, for a hint of spice)
  • ½ cup non-fat milk or dairy-free alternative (like almond milk)

These ingredients come together to create a soft, slightly sweet, and wholesome bread with the unique texture of oats.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preparing the Oats

Start by placing your rolled oats in a medium-sized bowl. Pour 1 cup of warm water over the oats, ensuring they are all submerged. Stir the mixture gently and let the oats soak for about 10 minutes. Soaking the oats helps soften them, allowing them to blend more easily into the dough and giving the bread its characteristic texture.

2. Activating the Yeast

While your oats are soaking, you can activate the yeast. In a separate bowl, combine 1 cup of warm water with 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast. Add a small pinch of sugar or honey to help activate the yeast, and let it sit for about 5 minutes. You’ll know the yeast is ready when it becomes foamy and bubbly. If this doesn’t happen, the yeast may be expired, and it’s a good idea to use fresh yeast for the best results.

3. Mixing the Wet Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the soaked oats, activated yeast mixture, olive oil (or coconut oil), honey or maple syrup (if using), and milk. Stir everything together until well combined. The mixture should be somewhat smooth and slightly thick, with a nice balance of wet and dry ingredients.

4. Adding the Dry Ingredients

Now it’s time to add the dry ingredients. Add the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, salt, ground flaxseeds, chia seeds, and ground cinnamon (if using) to the wet ingredients. Mix everything until it forms a sticky dough. The dough might be a little tacky, but it shouldn’t be overly wet. If it’s too sticky, add a little extra flour, one tablespoon at a time, until it reaches the right consistency.

5. Kneading the Dough

Once your dough is mixed, it’s time to knead. Transfer the dough to a floured surface, and knead it for about 8-10 minutes. Use your palms to push the dough away from you and then fold it back over itself. Repeat this motion, turning the dough as you go, until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment if you prefer.

6. First Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly greased bowl, and cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it has doubled in size. You can speed up this process by placing the dough in a slightly warm oven (about 80°F) or near a heater.

7. Shaping the Dough

Once the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release any air bubbles. Transfer it to a floured surface, and shape it into a loaf. You can do this by rolling the dough out into a rectangle and then rolling it up into a tight log. Tuck the ends under to ensure a smooth top and place the shaped dough into a greased loaf pan.

8. Second Rise

Cover the dough with a kitchen towel again, and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes. This second rise allows the dough to become airy and soft, giving your bread a light and fluffy texture. While the dough is rising, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

9. Baking the Bread

Once the dough has risen to your satisfaction, place the loaf pan in the preheated oven. Bake the bread for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown, and the bread sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. If you have a thermometer, the internal temperature of the bread should be about 190°F (88°C).

10. Cooling and Slicing

Remove the oatmeal bread from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for about 5 minutes. Then, transfer the loaf to a wire rack to cool completely. This cooling process is crucial as it helps set the texture of the bread. While the bread is cooling, resist the temptation to slice it right away, as the interior can be a little soft and crumbly when it’s still hot.

Once the bread has cooled, slice it to your desired thickness and enjoy! You’ll notice that this oatmeal bread is slightly denser than regular white bread but with a delightful chewiness and nutty flavor.

Tips for Perfect Oatmeal Bread

  • Use fresh yeast: Old yeast can lead to a dough that doesn’t rise properly. Always check the expiration date on your yeast packet.
  • Soak the oats: Soaking the oats beforehand softens them and helps integrate them better into the dough.
  • Add-ins: Feel free to add extra ingredients like raisins, sunflower seeds, or walnuts to the dough for more flavor and texture.
  • Make it dairy-free: If you need to make the bread dairy-free, simply use a plant-based milk alternative like almond or oat milk.
  • Storage: Store your oatmeal bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, you can slice and freeze the bread for up to 3 months.

Health Benefits of Oatmeal Bread

Oatmeal bread is more than just a tasty treat. It’s packed with numerous health benefits that make it an excellent addition to any diet.

1. Rich in Fiber

Oats are an excellent source of soluble fiber, particularly beta-glucan, which has been shown to help reduce cholesterol levels, improve blood sugar control, and promote heart health. The whole wheat flour in this recipe adds additional fiber, making oatmeal bread a high-fiber option for those looking to improve their digestive health.

2. Helps Regulate Blood Sugar

The combination of oats and whole wheat flour in this bread can help regulate blood sugar levels. The complex carbohydrates in oats are digested more slowly, providing a steady source of energy and helping to prevent blood sugar spikes and crashes.

3. Promotes Heart Health

The high fiber content of oatmeal bread can support heart health by helping lower cholesterol levels. The flaxseeds and chia seeds in the recipe also provide heart-healthy omega-3 fatty acids.

4. Provides Lasting Energy

Oatmeal bread is a good source of complex carbohydrates, which provide sustained energy throughout the day. This makes it an excellent option for breakfast or a mid-day snack.

Conclusion

Making homemade oatmeal bread is a simple and rewarding process that results in a nutritious and delicious loaf. With its high fiber content, heart-healthy benefits, and rich flavor, oatmeal bread is a fantastic alternative to traditional white bread. Plus, you can easily customize it with your favorite add-ins to make it uniquely yours.

So, why not try baking a loaf of oatmeal bread today? With just a few basic ingredients and some patience, you’ll have a wholesome, homemade bread that’s perfect for any meal. Enjoy!
