Eerie Eyeball Cake Pops: Food-Decorating Halloween Recipe


Dive into the world of Halloween festivities by whipping up some semi-homemade, eerie eyeball cake pops! And guess what? You don’t even need to be a master chef. Following this recipe, you’ll surely create the perfect spooky themed treats that will make your Halloween celebration even more spectacular.

Serves: Approximately 24 cake pops

Ingredients:

  • Cake mix (your favorite flavor) and ingredients to bake per package instructions
  • 1 tub of frosting (preferably black)
  • 4 oz of cream cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup of white chocolate wafers
  • Chocolate eggs for the “eyeball” center (look for them in the Halloween section of your store)
  • Small candy eyes
  • 1 cup of finely crushed Oreos or graham crackers (optional, for coating)

Instructions:

  1. Bake the Cake: Follow the instructions on the cake mix package to bake a 9-inch cake. Allow the cake to cool for a few minutes before moving it to a wire rack. Once cooled, crumble it into a large bowl using your hands or a fork.

  2. Mix the Frosting: In a separate bowl, mix the frosting, cream cheese, and melted butter until well-blended. This mixture will serve as the base for your cake pops.

  3. Combine Cake and Frosting: Add the cake crumbles to the frosting mixture and stir until you achieve a moldable consistency. You might need to adjust by adding more frosting or cake crumbs as needed.

  4. Create the Cake Balls: Shape a spoonful of the mixture into balls and place them on a tray. Once your tray is filled with cake balls, place them in the freezer for about 15 minutes or until they’re firm.

  5. Melt Chocolate: In a microwave-safe bowl, melt about half of the white chocolate wafers in 30-second intervals, stirring constantly, until smooth.

  6. Coat Cake Balls: Remove the cake balls from the freezer and dip each one into the melted white chocolate, covering the entire surface. Let the excess chocolate drip off, then place them on a lined tray and let them harden in the freezer for at least 10 minutes.

  7. Prepare Eyeballs: Insert a chocolate egg and candy eyes into the softened chocolate “eyeball” centers. Place these in the freezer for another 5 minutes or until the chocolate hardens again.

  8. Dip Cake Pops: Meanwhile, melt the remaining white chocolate in the same manner as before. Using a spoon, drizzle the previously hardened cake pops with a thin layer of white chocolate. Gently place the eyeball centers, then cover the remaining white chocolate completely with crushed Oreos or graham crackers (if using) to achieve a soil-like texture. This is optional.

  9. Final Touches: Let the newly coated cake pops set for another 15 minutes, then serve and enjoy your eerily delightful creations!

Tip: To carry these cake pops conveniently to a party or any Halloween event, transfer them in a clear cylinder cake pop jar.

The Eerie Eyeball Cake Pops are fantastic treat options for any Halloween gathering. With their uniquely creepy and charming appearance, as well as their delightful cake pop texture, they will undoubtedly leave your guests in awe. Experiment with different toppings or make them even more spooky by adding finger-friendly food coloring and create blood dripping effects. Don’t forget to share your creations on social media and give your friends a taste of something otherworldly this Halloween!
