Greek Spanakopita Recipe with Spinach and Feta Cheese


Spanakopita is a delicious Greek dish that combines the flavors of spinach and feta cheese, wrapped in crispy layers of phyllo pastry. This savory pie is a crowd-pleaser and makes for a perfect appetizer or main course. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making homemade spanakopita that will transport your taste buds straight to the Mediterranean.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh spinach, washed and chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • ½ cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 package phyllo pastry sheets, thawed
  • ½ cup melted butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted, stirring occasionally. This should take about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool.

  4. In a mixing bowl, combine the crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, chopped dill, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  5. Once the spinach has cooled, squeeze out any excess moisture using a clean kitchen towel or paper towels. Add the spinach to the cheese mixture and stir until well combined.

  6. Lay out one sheet of phyllo pastry on a clean, dry surface. Brush it lightly with melted butter. Place another sheet on top and brush with butter again. Repeat this process until you have a stack of 5-6 sheets.

  7. Spoon a generous amount of the spinach and cheese filling along the shorter edge of the phyllo stack, leaving a small border. Roll the phyllo tightly, tucking in the sides as you go. Place the roll seam-side down in the greased baking dish.

  8. Repeat steps 6 and 7 with the remaining phyllo sheets and filling until all the ingredients are used.

  9. Brush the top of the spanakopita with melted butter, ensuring it is evenly coated. This will give the pastry a beautiful golden color when baked.

  10. Using a sharp knife, score the top of the spanakopita into individual portions. This will make it easier to serve later.

  11. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the phyllo is golden brown and crisp.

  12. Once done, remove the spanakopita from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the filling to set and make it easier to slice.

  13. Serve warm as an appetizer, side dish, or even as a main course with a fresh Greek salad on the side.

Enjoy the delightful combination of flavors in this Greek Spanakopita recipe! The crispy phyllo pastry, paired with the creamy spinach and tangy feta cheese, creates a dish that is both comforting and satisfying. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of Greece, this recipe is sure to impress. Give it a try and let your taste buds embark on a Mediterranean adventure.

Note: Spanakopita is best enjoyed fresh, but any leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat in the oven at a low temperature until warmed through.
