Delightful Sri Lankan Hoppers: 5 Recipes for a Unique Breakfast


When it comes to breakfast, many of us are accustomed to the usual suspects: cereal, toast, or pancakes. But if you’re looking to switch things up and add a burst of flavor to your mornings, Sri Lankan hoppers are the perfect choice. These bowl-shaped, crispy-edged pancakes are a staple in Sri Lankan cuisine and can be enjoyed in various forms. Here, we present five delightful Sri Lankan hopper recipes that will take your breakfast game to a whole new level.

Classic Plain Hopper

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp active dry yeast
  • Warm water
  • Coconut oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, coconut milk, sugar, salt, and yeast.
  2. Gradually add warm water to form a smooth, lump-free batter. The batter should be slightly thicker than a crepe batter.
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and allow the batter to ferment for 2-3 hours, or until it doubles in volume.
  4. Heat a hopper pan over medium heat and grease it lightly with coconut oil.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter into the pan, swirling it around to evenly coat the bottom and create a bowl shape.
  6. Cover the pan and cook the hopper for 2-3 minutes, or until the edges are crispy and the bottom is golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter, adding more oil to the pan as needed.

Egg Hopper

Ingredients:

  • Prepared hopper batter
  • Eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped onions, optional
  • Chopped green chilies, optional
  • Chopped coriander leaves, optional

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the hopper batter as per the instructions for the Classic Plain Hopper.
  2. Once the hopper is set in the pan, crack an egg into the bowl-shaped center.
  3. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, onions, green chilies, and coriander leaves, if using.
  4. Cover the pan and cook until the egg is set to your liking. The yolk can be runny or fully cooked, depending on your preference.

String Hopper

Ingredients:

  • String hopper press
  • Rice flour
  • Warm water
  • Salt
  • Coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the rice flour, salt, and enough warm water to form a soft, pliable dough.
  2. Fill the string hopper press with the dough and press it onto small, round individual mats.
  3. Steam the string hoppers for 3-5 minutes, or until they are cooked through and firm to the touch.
  4. Serve the string hoppers with your choice of accompaniments, such as dhal curry, coconut sambol, or a spicy fish curry.

Sweet Jaggery Hopper

Ingredients:

  • Prepared hopper batter
  • Jaggery (palm sugar) or brown sugar
  • Grated coconut
  • Cardamom powder
  • Ghee or butter

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the hopper batter as per the instructions for the Classic Plain Hopper.
  2. Sprinkle some grated coconut and a pinch of cardamom powder into the bowl-shaped hopper.
  3. Place a small piece of jaggery or brown sugar in the center.
  4. Cover the pan and cook until the edges are crispy and the jaggery has melted.
  5. Fold the sides of the hopper towards the center to create a parcel and serve with a drizzle of ghee or butter.

Savory Hopper with Katta Sambol

Ingredients:

  • Prepared hopper batter
  • Katta Sambol (spicy coconut relish)
  • Coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the hopper batter as per the instructions for the Classic Plain Hopper.
  2. Once the hopper is set in the pan, spread a generous spoonful of katta sambol over the center.
  3. Cover the pan and cook until the edges are crispy and the sambol is heated through.
  4. Fold the sides of the hopper towards the center to create a semi-circle and serve hot.

Sri Lankan hoppers are a delightful and versatile breakfast option that can be enjoyed in myriad ways. Whether you prefer the simplicity of a plain hopper or the bold flavors of a savory variation, these recipes are sure to elevate your breakfast experience. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your hopper pan, and treat yourself to a taste of Sri Lanka right in your own kitchen.
