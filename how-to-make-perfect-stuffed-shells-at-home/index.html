How to Make Perfect Stuffed Shells at Home


Stuffed shells are a classic Italian-American dish that brings comfort and satisfaction to any dining table. These pasta shells are not just filled with a delicious mixture, but they are also enrobed in marinara sauce and topped with gooey cheese, making them irresistible. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to create perfect stuffed shells right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Stuffed Shells

  • 12-14 jumbo pasta shells (about 8 ounces)
  • 15 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese (plus more for topping)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Marinara Sauce

  • 2 cups marinara sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil

Optional Toppings

  • Extra shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Cook the Pasta Shells

Start by boiling a large pot of salted water. Once boiling, add the jumbo pasta shells and cook them according to the package instructions until they are al dente. This usually takes about 8-10 minutes. Be careful not to overcook them, as they will continue to soften in the oven.

Once cooked, drain the shells and rinse them under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set them aside on a clean kitchen towel to cool completely.

Step 2: Prepare the Filling

While the shells are cooling, it’s time to prepare the delicious filling. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, egg, chopped parsley, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix everything until well combined.

Taste the filling and adjust the seasoning if necessary. The filling should be flavorful, as it’s the heart of your stuffed shells.

Step 3: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures that your stuffed shells will cook evenly and get that nice golden finish.

Step 4: Prepare the Marinara Sauce

In a saucepan over medium heat, warm the marinara sauce. Stir in the dried oregano and basil, allowing the flavors to meld together. This simple step elevates the sauce and gives it a homemade touch.

Step 5: Assemble the Stuffed Shells

Now comes the fun part—stuffing the shells! Take one shell at a time and use a spoon to fill it generously with the ricotta mixture. Don’t be shy; pack it in! Place the filled shell seam-side up in a baking dish. Repeat this process until all the shells are filled and placed in the dish.

Step 6: Add the Sauce

Once your shells are arranged snugly in the baking dish, pour the warmed marinara sauce evenly over the top. Make sure each shell is coated with sauce to keep them moist during baking. For an extra cheesy touch, sprinkle additional shredded mozzarella on top of the sauce.

Step 7: Bake

Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil to prevent the cheese from burning. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. After that, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

Step 8: Let it Rest

Once removed from the oven, let the stuffed shells rest for about 10 minutes. This allows the filling to set and makes serving easier.

Step 9: Garnish and Serve

Garnish your stuffed shells with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and added flavor. Serve them warm with extra marinara sauce on the side, and enjoy the comforting goodness of this classic dish.

Tips for Perfect Stuffed Shells

  • Use Quality Ingredients: The flavor of your stuffed shells heavily relies on the quality of your ingredients, especially the cheese and the marinara sauce. Opt for fresh, high-quality products whenever possible.

  • Don’t Overfill: While it’s tempting to stuff the shells to the brim, overfilling can cause them to burst during baking. Aim for a generous yet manageable filling.

  • Experiment with Fillings: Feel free to customize the filling by adding cooked spinach, sautéed mushrooms, or ground meat. The possibilities are endless!

  • Make Ahead: Stuffed shells can be made ahead of time. Prepare them up to the baking stage, cover, and refrigerate. When ready to serve, bake them straight from the fridge, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

  • Leftover Storage: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the oven for best results.

Conclusion

Making perfect stuffed shells at home is not only easy but also incredibly satisfying. This dish is perfect for family dinners, gatherings, or even meal prep for the week ahead. With its delicious filling, rich marinara sauce, and cheesy topping, stuffed shells are sure to become a favorite in your household. Dive into this culinary adventure and impress your family and friends with your homemade Italian delight!
