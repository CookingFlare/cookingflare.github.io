Korean Spicy Cold Noodles: A Chilled Delight for Hot Summer Days


When the scorching heat of summer arrives, nothing beats a refreshing and satisfying meal that can cool you down. Korean Spicy Cold Noodles, also known as Mul Naengmyeon, is a traditional Korean dish that perfectly fits the bill. This chilled delight is not only a popular choice in Korea but has also gained popularity worldwide for its unique flavors and refreshing qualities.

The Origins of Korean Spicy Cold Noodles

Korean Spicy Cold Noodles have their roots in North Korea, where the dish was initially created to combat the sweltering summers. It later spread to South Korea and became a beloved staple in Korean cuisine. Traditionally, the noodles were made from buckwheat flour, giving them a distinct texture and nutty flavor. However, nowadays, a combination of buckwheat and sweet potato starch is commonly used to create the perfect balance of chewiness and lightness.

The Art of Preparation

To make Korean Spicy Cold Noodles, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 bundles of Korean buckwheat noodles
  • 2 cups of beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili pepper flakes)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 2 radishes, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Now, follow these simple steps to create your own Korean Spicy Cold Noodles:

  1. Cook the buckwheat noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, rinse them under cold water and drain well.

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the beef or vegetable broth, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, and gochugaru. Stir well until the sugar is dissolved and the flavors are fully combined.

  3. Divide the cold noodles into individual serving bowls. Pour the spicy broth mixture over the noodles, ensuring they are well-coated.

  4. Arrange the sliced hard-boiled eggs, julienned cucumber, sliced radishes, and chopped green onions on top of the noodles.

  5. Sprinkle sesame seeds over the dish for added flavor and visual appeal.

  6. Serve the Korean Spicy Cold Noodles immediately and enjoy!

Customizing Your Cold Noodles

Korean Spicy Cold Noodles can be customized to suit your taste preferences. If you prefer a milder version, adjust the amount of gochugaru accordingly. Additionally, you can add other toppings such as shredded chicken, pickled vegetables, or even a dollop of kimchi for an extra kick.

The Perfect Pairing

To complement the flavors of Korean Spicy Cold Noodles, serve them with a side of tangy kimchi and a refreshing glass of iced barley tea. The combination of the spicy noodles, crunchy kimchi, and cooling tea will make for a truly satisfying and well-rounded meal.

Beat the Heat with Korean Spicy Cold Noodles

Korean Spicy Cold Noodles are not only a delicious and refreshing dish, but they also offer a unique culinary experience. With their spicy and tangy flavors, chewy noodles, and vibrant toppings, they are the perfect way to beat the heat during the hot summer days. So, give this delightful Korean dish a try and let it transport you to a culinary adventure like no other. Stay cool and enjoy!
