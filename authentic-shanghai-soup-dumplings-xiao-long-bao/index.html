Authentic Shanghai Soup Dumplings (Xiao Long Bao)


Shanghai Soup Dumplings, or Xiao Long Bao (小笼包), are an iconic delicacy that has captured the hearts and appetites of food lovers around the world. Originating from the bustling streets of Shanghai, these dumplings combine tender dough, flavorful filling, and a rich, savory broth that bursts in your mouth with each bite. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make authentic Xiao Long Bao at home, from the intricate dough-making process to preparing the perfect filling and broth that are the heart and soul of these dumplings.

Whether you’re an experienced chef or a beginner, this step-by-step guide will help you recreate this beloved dish from the comfort of your kitchen. Let’s dive right in!

What Are Xiao Long Bao?

Xiao Long Bao translates to “small basket buns” in Mandarin, which refers to the traditional bamboo baskets in which these dumplings are steamed. The dumplings themselves are made from a thin dough that encases a delicious mixture of ground meat, usually pork, along with a hot, savory broth that turns into jelly when cooled. When steamed, the broth liquefies inside the dumpling, creating an explosion of flavor in every bite.

The key to making the perfect Xiao Long Bao is mastering the balance between a delicate, thin dough and a flavorful filling that holds the broth without leaking. The process may seem challenging at first, but with patience and the right ingredients, you can make these restaurant-quality dumplings at home.

The Essential Ingredients for Xiao Long Bao

Before you begin, ensure you have all the necessary ingredients. While the recipe may seem intricate, the core components are simple and easy to find.

For the Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

For the Broth (Aspic):

  • 2 cups pork skin (or pork bone) stock, homemade or store-bought
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (Shaoxing wine)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons gelatin powder

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound ground pork (preferably with some fat content for juiciness)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger, finely minced
  • 2 tablespoons scallions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper

For Steaming:

  • Bamboo steamer (or any steamer suitable for dumplings)
  • Parchment paper or cabbage leaves (to line the steamer)

Making the Aspic (Soup Gelatin)

The key feature of Xiao Long Bao is the liquid broth that sits inside the dumplings. To achieve this, we need to create an aspic (gelatinous broth) that solidifies when cooled and melts back into liquid when steamed. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Prepare the Pork Stock: If you have homemade pork stock, great! If not, you can use store-bought pork bone broth. The stock should be rich and flavorful. Bring the pork stock to a boil and then reduce to a simmer for 5 minutes.

  2. Flavor the Stock: Add soy sauce, rice wine, salt, and sugar to the stock. Stir until everything is dissolved and combined. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. You want the broth to be well-seasoned, as it will be the base of your filling’s flavor.

  3. Add Gelatin: Sprinkle the gelatin powder into the hot stock and stir to dissolve it completely. You can use powdered gelatin or agar-agar if you prefer a vegetarian alternative. Let the mixture cool slightly before transferring it into a shallow dish.

  4. Set the Aspic: Once the gelatin is dissolved, pour the broth into a shallow dish and refrigerate it for about 4 hours, or until it has fully set. Once set, cut the aspic into small cubes. These cubes will be added to your filling to create the signature soup-filled center of your Xiao Long Bao.

Preparing the Dough

The dough for Xiao Long Bao needs to be delicate yet strong enough to hold the filling. This requires a special technique to make the dough pliable and smooth. Follow these steps:

  1. Combine Flour and Salt: In a large mixing bowl, add the flour and salt. Mix well.

  2. Add Warm Water: Slowly pour in warm water, a little at a time, while stirring with your hand or a wooden spoon. Once the dough starts to come together, knead it into a smooth ball.

  3. Rest the Dough: Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes. This resting time allows the gluten to relax, making the dough easier to roll out later.

  4. Knead and Roll: After resting, knead the dough again until it’s soft and smooth. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a long cylinder. Cut the cylinder into small, even pieces (about 20 pieces per dough ball). Roll each piece into a small ball.

  5. Roll Out the Dough: Using a rolling pin, flatten each ball into a small, round wrapper. You want the edges of the wrapper to be thinner than the center, so make sure to roll out the edges slightly thinner than the center for the perfect dumpling wrapper.

Preparing the Filling

Now, let’s create the flavorful filling for the Xiao Long Bao.

  1. Combine Pork and Seasonings: In a mixing bowl, combine the ground pork with ginger, scallions, soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, salt, and white pepper. Stir the mixture in one direction to incorporate air into the filling, which will make the filling tender.

  2. Add the Aspic: Gently fold in the small cubes of aspic that you prepared earlier. The cubes should be evenly distributed in the filling. This will ensure that the broth inside the dumpling stays intact during the steaming process.

Assembling the Dumplings

Once your dough, filling, and aspic are ready, it’s time to assemble your Xiao Long Bao. This part requires a bit of practice, but with patience, you’ll be able to master it.

  1. Place the Dough Wrapper: Take one of the dough wrappers you rolled out earlier and place it in the palm of your hand.

  2. Add the Filling: Spoon a generous portion of the filling onto the center of the wrapper. The filling should not be too much, or it will be difficult to seal the dumpling.

  3. Pinch the Edges: Carefully pleat the edges of the dough wrapper by folding the dough around the filling. It’s essential to pinch and twist the top of the dumpling to seal it completely. Try to create pleats as you go around the dumpling, ensuring that the soup inside doesn’t leak out.

  4. Shape the Dumpling: Once the edges are pinched together, give the dumpling a slight twist to seal it tightly. The dumpling should look like a little purse with pleats at the top.

Steaming the Xiao Long Bao

The final step is to steam your Xiao Long Bao to perfection. Steaming them correctly ensures the broth inside stays intact, while the dough becomes tender and soft.

  1. Prepare the Steamer: Line a bamboo steamer basket with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent the dumplings from sticking. If you’re using parchment paper, you can punch small holes in it to allow steam to circulate.

  2. Arrange the Dumplings: Place the Xiao Long Bao in the steamer, ensuring they aren’t touching each other. Leave enough space between the dumplings for them to expand while steaming.

  3. Steam: Bring a pot of water to a gentle boil. Place the bamboo steamer over the pot and cover it with a lid. Steam the dumplings over medium-high heat for about 8 to 10 minutes, or until the dough is cooked through and the filling is hot.

  4. Serve: Once the dumplings are steamed, remove them from the steamer and serve immediately. Xiao Long Bao is best enjoyed hot, so be sure to serve them right away.

Tips for the Perfect Xiao Long Bao

  1. Don’t Overstuff: The key to a successful Xiao Long Bao is balance. Too much filling can cause the dumplings to burst open during steaming. Stick to a moderate amount of filling.

  2. Use a Steamer Liner: To prevent the dumplings from sticking to the steamer, always line the steamer with parchment paper or cabbage leaves. You can also oil the liner lightly.

  3. Be Gentle When Steaming: Don’t overcrowd the steamer basket, and avoid using high heat. Steaming on medium-high heat for the recommended time will yield the best results.

  4. Practice Makes Perfect: If you’re new to folding dumplings, don’t worry if your first few aren’t perfect. With time and practice, you’ll get the hang of it. The most important thing is to enjoy the process!

Enjoying Xiao Long Bao

To eat Xiao Long Bao properly, it’s essential to be gentle, as the broth inside can be quite hot. Here’s the ideal way to savor them:

  1. Pick Up the Dumpling: Use chopsticks to pick up a Xiao Long Bao by the pleated top. Be careful not to puncture the dumpling.

  2. Place It on a Spoon: Gently place the dumpling on a soup spoon. This will catch any broth that might escape.

  3. Take a Small Bite: Carefully take a small bite from the side to release the hot broth into the spoon.

  4. Sip the Soup: Drink the delicious broth from the spoon before finishing the rest of the dumpling. Finally, eat the remaining dumpling, enjoying the tender filling and perfectly cooked dough.

Conclusion

Making authentic Xiao Long Bao may require a bit of practice, but the end result is well worth the effort. The delicate, juicy dumplings filled with savory broth are a true testament to Shanghai’s rich culinary heritage. Whether you’re making these dumplings for a special occasion or just a fun cooking project, you’ll be able to impress your friends and family with your homemade version of this iconic dish. Happy cooking, and enjoy your delicious Xiao Long Bao!
