Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse: A Decadent Dessert


When it comes to creating luxurious desserts that are guaranteed to impress, few can compete with the smooth, velvety goodness of a chocolate mousse. While many traditional methods of making chocolate mousse involve a combination of whipped cream, egg whites, and chocolate, there’s a modern twist that can elevate the experience even further: Sous vide cooking. If you’re unfamiliar with sous vide, it’s a technique where food is cooked in a vacuum-sealed bag submerged in warm water at precise temperatures. This method allows you to achieve the most tender, evenly cooked results possible—and it works beautifully with desserts too.

Sous vide chocolate mousse is a game-changer. The combination of rich, melted chocolate, velvety texture, and the slight hint of vanilla creates an unforgettable treat. This method not only guarantees perfect texture but also simplifies the cooking process, allowing you to focus on other aspects of your meal. If you’ve ever tried to make mousse and struggled with temperature control or texture, this recipe is for you.

What Makes Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse Different?

Sous vide cooking takes traditional chocolate mousse and transforms it into something even more special. The reason sous vide works so well for chocolate mousse is that the controlled water temperature ensures even cooking, preventing any overcooking or curdling. By cooking the mousse in a precisely regulated water bath, you avoid the common pitfalls of using a double boiler or stovetop method, like overheating the chocolate or scrambling the eggs.

This technique also allows for a more consistent texture throughout the dessert. The gentle, slow cooking process results in a mousse that is incredibly smooth, creamy, and stable. The beauty of sous vide is that it doesn’t just cook your ingredients; it brings out the full potential of each one, blending them together in perfect harmony.

Why Sous Vide?

  • Precision Cooking: Sous vide cooking uses water at a precise, low temperature. For chocolate mousse, this is crucial. You won’t risk overheating or burning the chocolate or overcooking the eggs.
  • Even Texture: Sous vide cooking results in uniform texture throughout the mousse, eliminating the risk of separation or curdling.
  • Hands-Off Cooking: Once the ingredients are in the water bath, the sous vide process requires little attention, freeing up time for other preparations.
  • Rich Flavor Development: Cooking at low temperatures allows the chocolate and vanilla to meld beautifully, creating a more sophisticated flavor profile.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse

To make this decadent dessert, you’ll need a few basic ingredients. Choose high-quality ingredients for the best result:

  • 8 oz (about 225g) of high-quality bittersweet or semisweet chocolate (70% cacao works best)
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) of heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) of whole milk
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup (50g) of granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp of vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Optional Garnishes:

  • Whipped cream
  • Shaved chocolate or cocoa powder
  • Fresh berries
  • Mint leaves

Equipment Needed

  • Sous Vide Precision Cooker: This is the heart of the process. You’ll need a sous vide machine that can maintain a precise temperature.
  • Vacuum Sealer or Zipper-Top Bags: Sous vide cooking requires sealing your ingredients in a bag to ensure that they cook evenly and don’t come into direct contact with water.
  • Immersion Circulator: This is another name for the sous vide precision cooker, which helps maintain the correct temperature.
  • Mixing Bowls: For whisking the egg yolks and sugar together.
  • Heatproof Container for Water Bath: A large pot or container that will hold the water for sous vide cooking.
  • Microwave-Safe or Heatproof Bowl for Melting Chocolate: You’ll melt the chocolate before combining it with the egg mixture.

How to Make Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse

Step 1: Prepare the Chocolate

Start by melting the chocolate. You can do this by placing the chocolate in a heatproof bowl and microwaving it in short bursts of 20-30 seconds, stirring in between. Alternatively, you can melt it over a double boiler. The goal is to get the chocolate smooth and fully melted without overheating it.

Once the chocolate is melted, let it cool slightly. The next step is to mix the other ingredients into the chocolate, so it’s important that the chocolate is no longer scalding hot but still warm.

Step 2: Whisk the Egg Yolks and Sugar

While the chocolate is cooling, whisk the egg yolks and sugar together in a separate mixing bowl. Whisk until the mixture turns pale and slightly thickened. This is a crucial step to create the right texture for the mousse.

Step 3: Mix Cream, Milk, and Vanilla

In a small saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and a pinch of salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Be sure to keep an eye on it to prevent boiling. Once it’s warm, remove it from the heat and add the vanilla extract.

Slowly pour this cream mixture into the egg and sugar mixture while whisking constantly. The gradual incorporation of the warm cream into the egg yolks helps avoid scrambling the eggs.

Step 4: Combine the Chocolate and Egg Mixture

Now that both the chocolate and the egg mixture are prepared, it’s time to combine them. Slowly pour the egg and cream mixture into the melted chocolate while whisking to ensure everything is well incorporated. The result should be a smooth, silky chocolate mixture that forms the base of your mousse.

Step 5: Seal and Bag the Mixture

Once the mixture is ready, it’s time to seal it for sous vide cooking. Pour the mousse mixture into a vacuum-seal bag or a zipper-top bag, being careful to remove as much air as possible. If using a zipper-top bag, you can use the water displacement method to remove air by submerging the bag in water and sealing it just before it’s fully submerged.

Step 6: Sous Vide Cooking

Set your sous vide machine to 175°F (80°C) and place the bag with the mousse mixture into the water bath. Cook for 45 minutes to 1 hour. During this time, the chocolate mousse will gently thicken and set, creating the perfect texture. The sous vide method ensures that the mousse doesn’t become overcooked or separated, maintaining a creamy, stable consistency.

Step 7: Chill the Mousse

Once the mousse has finished cooking, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Let the bag cool for a few minutes before transferring the mousse into individual serving cups or bowls. The mousse should be served chilled, so place it in the refrigerator for at least 2-3 hours, or overnight if you prefer.

Step 8: Garnish and Serve

Once the mousse has chilled and set, it’s time to serve. Top it with a dollop of freshly whipped cream, a sprinkle of shaved chocolate or cocoa powder, and perhaps a few fresh berries for a burst of color and flavor. A sprig of mint can also add a refreshing touch. Serve the mousse on its own or as a luxurious accompaniment to your favorite cakes or pastries.

Tips for the Perfect Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse

  1. Choose High-Quality Chocolate: The quality of the chocolate plays a huge role in the flavor of your mousse. Opt for a good-quality bittersweet or semisweet chocolate with at least 60% cacao for the best results.
  2. Control the Temperature: Sous vide cooking is all about precision. Ensure that your water bath is at the correct temperature and doesn’t fluctuate during the cooking process.
  3. Cool Before Serving: The mousse will be too soft to serve immediately after sous vide cooking. Give it time to chill and set, as this enhances the texture and flavor.
  4. Experiment with Flavors: While the traditional chocolate mousse is incredible on its own, feel free to experiment with flavors by adding a touch of orange zest, coffee, or a splash of liqueur like Grand Marnier or Bailey’s Irish Cream.
  5. Use the Right Equipment: Make sure your vacuum-seal bags or zipper bags are sealed tightly to prevent water from seeping in during the cooking process. This ensures the mousse stays perfectly smooth.

Why Sous Vide Chocolate Mousse is Worth the Effort

Making chocolate mousse using the sous vide method takes the dessert to an entirely new level of sophistication. The sous vide process guarantees that every bite is perfectly smooth, creamy, and rich, while also preserving the natural flavors of the chocolate. The ability to control the temperature with precision ensures that the mousse turns out perfectly every time—no more worrying about curdled eggs or chocolate that seizes up.

Additionally, sous vide chocolate mousse is incredibly versatile. You can customize the flavor by adding different extracts, spices, or even a splash of your favorite liquor. Plus, because the mousse needs time to chill, it’s a great make-ahead dessert for special occasions, dinner parties, or holiday gatherings. Just prepare it in advance, let it set in the fridge, and serve it when you’re ready for a show-stopping dessert.

Whether you’re a novice home cook or a seasoned chef, this sous vide chocolate mousse is an absolute game-changer. Its smooth texture and decadent chocolate flavor are sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who tastes it.

Happy cooking!
