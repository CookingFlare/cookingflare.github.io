Vegetarian BBQ Recipes That Even Meat Lovers Will Enjoy


When the grill fires up, the smell of smoky, sizzling food fills the air, sparking appetites of all kinds. But what if the selection is mostly meat-based? Luckily, vegetarian BBQ options have evolved far beyond simple veggie burgers or plain grilled corn. Today, the world of plant-based grilling offers mouthwatering dishes packed with flavor, texture, and the smoky charm that makes outdoor cooking irresistible—raising the bar even for die-hard carnivores.

In this post, you’ll discover a collection of vegetarian BBQ recipes designed to impress every guest at your next cookout, meat lovers included. From charred skewers bursting with umami to smoky, hearty mains that satisfy big appetites, these recipes showcase the diverse potential of vegetables, legumes, and even some clever plant-based swaps.

Why Go Vegetarian on the Grill?

Before diving into the recipes, it’s worth reflecting on why incorporating vegetarian dishes at a barbecue is such a game changer. First, grilling vegetables elevates their natural sweetness and enhances robust flavors with a smoky touch. Second, vegetarian options add variety, ensuring not everyone is eating the same thing and keeping the menu vibrant. Finally, for those reducing meat intake or supporting a sustainable lifestyle, this approach is humane and earth-friendly without sacrificing taste or experience.

Let’s take a look at compelling vegetarian BBQ recipes that celebrate the grill’s magic with bold, textured, and satisfying dishes that appeal to every palate.

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Steaks with Balsamic Glaze

Portobello mushrooms, often called the “steak of the vegetable world,” deliver an impressively meaty texture thanks to their dense, thick caps. When grilled directly over high heat, their juices caramelize, creating rich, deep flavors that mimic the essence of a juicy steak.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushroom caps, stems removed
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, olive oil, garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate the mushroom caps in the mixture for at least 30 minutes, flipping occasionally.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill mushrooms for about 5–7 minutes on each side, basting occasionally with the leftover marinade.
  5. Remove from heat and let rest for a few minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve as a main or hearty side.

The smoky balsamic glaze paired with the robust mushroom makes this dish a perfect centerpiece that even the most devoted meat fans tend to enjoy.

Spicy Grilled Cauliflower Steaks with Chimichurri

Cauliflower, with its sturdy texture and mild flavor, absorbs bold marinades brilliantly while grilling. When sliced into thick “steaks,” it offers an intriguing alternative to traditional BBQ fare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Chimichurri Sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Remove the leaves and trim the stem of the cauliflower, then slice into 3/4-inch thick steaks.
  2. Mix olive oil, smoked chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Brush this mixture over both sides of the cauliflower steaks.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium.
  4. Grill the steaks for 6–8 minutes each side, flipping carefully to avoid crumbling.
  5. While grilling, prepare the chimichurri by combining parsley, cilantro, vinegar, garlic, crushed red pepper, and olive oil in a blender or food processor until smooth. Season with salt.
  6. Serve the cauliflower steaks topped with a generous drizzle of chimichurri.

The spicy, herbal chimichurri cuts through the char perfectly, creating a vibrant, layered flavor profile that’s anything but boring.

BBQ Jackfruit Sliders with Tangy Coleslaw

Jackfruit is a superstar for its shredded, meat-like texture, making it ideal for recreating the satisfying mouthfeel of pulled pork or chicken. This recipe transforms canned green jackfruit into luscious, barbecue-flavored sliders that meld sweet, tangy, and savory notes beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans young green jackfruit in water or brine, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetarian BBQ sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Slider buns

Tangy Coleslaw:

  • 2 cups shredded cabbage (mix green and purple)
  • 1 carrot, shredded
  • 1/4 cup vegan mayonnaise or Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Shred the jackfruit by hand or with forks, removing seeds and tough core pieces.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onion and garlic; sauté until translucent.
  3. Add shredded jackfruit and cook for 8–10 minutes, stirring often.
  4. Pour BBQ sauce over the jackfruit and simmer for another 10 minutes, letting it thicken and absorb flavors.
  5. Mix coleslaw ingredients together in a bowl, then season to balance tang and creaminess.
  6. Serve jackfruit piled high on slider buns topped with a scoop of coleslaw.

These sliders are juicy, smoky, and have just the right amount of bite and sweetness—hard to resist even for those who normally prefer pork.

Grilled Halloumi and Vegetable Skewers with Lemon-Herb Marinade

Halloumi cheese’s firm texture and high melting point make it perfect for kebabs. Combined with colorful vegetables, these skewers offer a pleasing contrast of smoky, salty, and fresh flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 250g halloumi, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, thickly sliced
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 8 cherry tomatoes

Marinade:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together all marinade ingredients.
  2. Toss halloumi and vegetables in marinade and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Thread halloumi and vegetables onto skewers, alternating for an even mix.
  4. Preheat the grill to medium-high and grill the skewers for 10–12 minutes, turning occasionally until the cheese is golden and veggies are tender.
  5. Serve immediately with a sprinkle of fresh herbs.

These skewers are a vibrant, flavorful addition to any barbecue table, with the salty halloumi offering a unique twist many meat lovers appreciate.

Smoky BBQ Tempeh Bites with Pineapple Salsa

Tempeh’s firm texture soaks up marinades like a sponge, making it an ideal candidate for smoky, sweet, and spicy barbecue dishes. Paired with a fresh pineapple salsa, it offers a balanced yet exciting bite.

Ingredients:

  • 250g tempeh, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce or tamari
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper

Pineapple Salsa:

  • 1 cup fresh pineapple, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, maple syrup, smoked paprika, olive oil, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Marinate tempeh cubes in mixture for at least 1 hour.
  3. Preheat grill to medium.
  4. Thread tempeh onto skewers or place in a grill basket.
  5. Grill 3–4 minutes per side until crispy and caramelized.
  6. Combine salsa ingredients in a bowl and season accordingly.
  7. Serve tempeh bites topped with pineapple salsa for a refreshing contrast.

The sweet and smoky tempeh paired with zesty, juicy salsa delivers an unforgettable flavor explosion.

Grilled Sweet Potato Wedges with Avocado Crema

Sweet potatoes, with their natural sweetness and dense flesh, are a knock-out on the BBQ. When grilled until tender and slightly charred, they develop a caramelized exterior complemented perfectly by a cool avocado creme.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper

Avocado Crema:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/2 cup vegan or regular sour cream
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss sweet potato wedges in olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill over medium heat for 10–15 minutes, turning occasionally until tender and grill-marked.
  3. Blend avocado, sour cream, lime juice, garlic, and salt until smooth.
  4. Serve wedges with a generous dollop of avocado crema.

These wedges make a crowd-pleasing side or snack with contrasting smoky and creamy notes.

Tips for Perfect Vegetarian BBQ

  • Marinate generously: Vegetables and plant proteins benefit from bold marinades to infuse flavor.
  • Use a charcoal grill or add wood chips: They add authentic smoke that elevates each dish.
  • Mind cooking times: Vegetables vary widely in density. Cut them uniformly and grill according to their firmness.
  • Embrace spice and acid: A balance of heat, tang, and sweetness prevents vegetarian dishes from tasting flat.
  • Get creative with textures: Mix crispy, creamy, and juicy elements for a satisfying meal.

Vegetarian BBQ doesn’t mean boring or bland. With fresh ingredients, clever marinades, and smart grilling techniques, you can create dishes that stand proudly next to any rich cut of meat. These recipes prove that smoky, charred, and flavorful fare can be vegetable-forward and crowd-pleasing at the same time—perfect for those warm, social days around the grill. Whether you’re a vegetarian or a dedicated meat eater, these bites offer something remarkable for everyone’s plate.

Fire up the grill and let these vegetarian stars shine at your next barbecue!
