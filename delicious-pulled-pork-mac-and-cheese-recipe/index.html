Delicious Pulled Pork Mac and Cheese Recipe


If you’re looking for a dish that combines bold flavors, creamy textures, and the ultimate comfort food experience, it’s hard to beat pulled pork mac and cheese. This irresistible recipe is perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or just treating yourself to something incredible. From tender, smoky pulled pork to rich, gooey cheese sauce mixed with perfectly cooked pasta, every bite is packed with flavor. In this blog post, you’ll find everything you need to create your own pulled pork mac and cheese masterpiece—from helpful tips to step-by-step instructions.

Why Pulled Pork Mac and Cheese Is Your Next Must-Try Recipe

Pulled pork mac and cheese brings together two classic dishes that are already crowd favorites: slow-cooked pulled pork and cheesy, creamy macaroni. The smoky savoriness of the pork melds perfectly with the rich and indulgent mac and cheese, creating a dish that’s hearty yet sophisticated. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or craving something indulgent for a cozy night in, this recipe combines the best of both worlds.

What sets this recipe apart is the attention to every detail, ensuring that each element is perfectly balanced. From selecting the right cut of pork for tender, juicy results to choosing the perfect blend of cheeses to complement the smoky meat, this recipe is designed to impress both your palate and your guests.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to gather all your ingredients. Below is a breakdown of everything you need for the pulled pork and mac and cheese components.

Pulled Pork Ingredients:

  • Pork shoulder or pork butt (3–4 lbs): These cuts are ideal for slow cooking due to their marbling and flavor.
  • Barbecue dry rub: A mix of paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, chili powder, and black pepper provides a savory-sweet base for your pork.
  • Liquid smoke (optional): Adds an extra smoky kick if you’re not smoking the pork traditionally.
  • Chicken or beef broth (2 cups): Helps keep the pork moist during the cooking process.
  • Barbecue sauce (1 cup): Choose a tangy or spicy sauce to complement the richness of mac and cheese.

Mac and Cheese Ingredients:

  • Elbow macaroni (1 lb): Classic pasta choice for mac and cheese, but you can opt for shells or cavatappi for added texture.
  • Butter (4 tbsp): Forms the base for the cheese sauce.
  • All-purpose flour (4 tbsp): Helps thicken the sauce.
  • Milk (3 cups): Use whole milk for a creamy consistency.
  • Heavy cream (1 cup): Enhances the richness of the cheese sauce.
  • Sharp cheddar cheese (2 cups, shredded): A classic choice for mac and cheese with bold flavor.
  • Monterey Jack cheese (1 cup, shredded): Adds creaminess to the cheese sauce.
  • Parmesan cheese (1 cup, shredded): Brings a nutty, salty finish to the dish.
  • Salt and pepper: To season the cheese sauce.
  • Breadcrumbs (optional, for topping): Toasted breadcrumbs add a delightful crunch when baked.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Pulled Pork

  1. Season the Pork: Rub the pork shoulder generously with your barbecue dry rub, ensuring all sides are coated evenly. For best results, let the pork rest in the refrigerator overnight to allow the flavors to penetrate.

  2. Slow-Cook the Pork: Place the seasoned pork in a slow cooker or Dutch oven. Add the chicken or beef broth and liquid smoke (if using), then cook on low heat for 8–10 hours or until the pork is tender and easily pulls apart with a fork.

  3. Shred the Pork: Once cooked, remove the pork from the pot and shred it using two forks. Mix the shredded meat with your favorite barbecue sauce and set aside.

Making the Creamy Mac and Cheese

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the macaroni according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1–2 minutes, whisking continuously to create a roux. Slowly add the milk and heavy cream, whisking until smooth.

  3. Add the Cheese: Reduce the heat to low and gradually stir in the shredded cheddar, Monterey Jack, and Parmesan cheeses. Stir until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce is thick and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Fold the cooked macaroni into the cheese sauce, ensuring every piece of pasta is coated in the creamy mixture.

Assembling the Pulled Pork Mac and Cheese

  1. Layer the Dish: In a large baking dish, layer the mac and cheese first, followed by a generous helping of pulled pork on top. If you’d like, you can gently mix the layers together or leave them layered for contrast.

  2. Optional Topping: Sprinkle breadcrumbs over the dish for an extra crunchy texture. You can also add a little more shredded cheese on top for a golden, cheesy finish.

  3. Bake It: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the dish for 15–20 minutes, or until the breadcrumbs are golden and the cheese is bubbly.

Tips for the Best Pulled Pork Mac and Cheese

Achieving pulled pork mac and cheese perfection takes a little know-how. Here are some pro tips:

  1. Use Fresh-Cooked Pulled Pork: While it’s tempting to use store-bought pulled pork, freshly made pork brings an undeniable depth of flavor to the dish.

  2. Experiment with Cheese Blends: Don’t be afraid to get creative with your cheese choices! Gouda, Gruyère, or even smoked cheddar can take your mac and cheese to new heights.

  3. Pair with the Right Sauce: The barbecue sauce you use for the pulled pork makes a big difference. Opt for sauces with bold flavors, such as smoky, spicy, or tangy varieties, to enhance the mac and cheese.

  4. Add a Pop: For an extra burst of flavor and texture, consider adding crispy bacon bits, caramelized onions, or a splash of hot sauce when serving.

Serving Suggestions

Pulled pork mac and cheese is a meal in itself, but it pairs beautifully with fresh sides. Consider serving it alongside a crisp green salad, roasted vegetables, or a loaf of warm, buttery cornbread. For beverages, a cold glass of iced tea, craft beer, or even a bold red wine can round out the flavors of this dish.

Leftovers and Storage

If you find yourself with leftovers (though it’s rare with a dish this good), store the pulled pork mac and cheese in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Gently reheat in the oven or microwave, adding a splash of milk to restore the creamy texture.

Final Thoughts

Pulled pork mac and cheese is a dish that feels indulgent yet comforting—a perfect combination of flavors and textures. Whether you’re making it for a special occasion or to savor on a quiet night, this recipe is guaranteed to be a hit. With its smoky pulled pork, luscious cheese sauce, and tender pasta, it’s a culinary creation you’ll want to make again and again.

Take your cooking to the next level with this pulled pork mac and cheese recipe, and prepare for rave reviews from everyone lucky enough to taste it. Give it a try, and let the magic happen!
