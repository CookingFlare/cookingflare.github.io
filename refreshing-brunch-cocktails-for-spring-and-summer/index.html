Refreshing Brunch Cocktails for Spring and Summer


As the days grow warmer and the sun shines brighter, gatherings filled with laughter and good company come alive. Whether you’re hosting a laid-back brunch on the patio or crafting a chic weekend spread indoors, spring and summer are the perfect seasons to elevate the experience with refreshing cocktails. Using fresh, seasonal ingredients, these recipes blend vibrant flavors that awaken the palate and complement a lush brunch menu.

Here’s a curated selection of six imaginative brunch cocktails that will bring a touch of class—and a burst of flavor—to your next sunny gathering.

1. Classic Grapefruit Mimosa

When you think of brunch cocktails, mimosas are often the first to come to mind. This citrusy twist on the traditional mimosa dresses up the classic pairing of champagne and orange juice for a more complex flavor profile.

Ingredients:

  • 3 oz freshly squeezed pink grapefruit juice
  • 3 oz chilled champagne or Prosecco
  • 1 tsp elderflower liqueur (optional, for extra floral notes)
  • Grapefruit wedge or twist for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Chill your champagne flute in the freezer for 5-10 minutes.
  2. Pour chilled grapefruit juice into the flute, followed by elderflower liqueur if desired.
  3. Slowly top with champagne and gently stir to combine without losing the bubbles.
  4. Garnish with a grapefruit wedge or twist, and serve immediately.

Pair this light and refreshing cocktail with a tangy arugula salad, smoked salmon bagels, or buttery croissants for an idyllic brunch experience.

2. Watermelon Basil Spritz

When summer hits its stride, ripe watermelon is a quintessential seasonal fruit. Juicy and hydrating, watermelon pairs beautifully with fragrant basil in this subtly sweet spritz cocktail, perfect for those seeking a crisp and revitalizing drink.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups watermelon chunks, fresh or chilled
  • 3-4 fresh basil leaves
  • 2 oz vodka or gin
  • 1 oz lime juice
  • 1 oz honey or agave syrup
  • Soda water, to top
  • Ice cubes
  • Basil sprig or lime wheel for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Puree watermelon chunks until smooth, then strain into a shaker to remove pulp.
  2. Tear basil leaves slightly to release oils, and add them to the shaker along with vodka or gin, lime juice, and honey or agave syrup.
  3. Add ice cubes, cover, and shake well.
  4. Fill a glass with ice, pour the strained mixture, and top with soda water for effervescence.
  5. Garnish with a sprig of basil or a lime wheel, and enjoy!

Pair this cocktail with juicy fruit salads, savory breakfast sandwiches, or grilled vegetable platters.

3. Peach Bellini with Thyme Infusion

This sophisticated bellini recipe brings seasonal peaches to the forefront while adding an herbal touch with thyme. The combination of fruity and earthy flavors makes it a standout accompaniment to brunch spreads.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe peaches, peeled and sliced
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup (1:1 sugar-to-water ratio)
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 3 oz chilled Prosecco or sparkling wine
  • Sprig of thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Blend peach slices until smooth, then strain to create a fine puree.
  2. In a shaker, mix peach puree with lemon juice, simple syrup, and thyme leaves. Shake gently to infuse the flavors.
  3. Pour the mixture into a champagne flute. Top with Prosecco, stirring gently to combine.
  4. Garnish with a sprig of thyme or a slice of peach.

Pair this bellini with creamy goat cheese spreads, peach tarts, or prosciutto-wrapped fresh fruits.

4. Sparkling Cucumber Mint Cooler

If you’re looking for a cocktail that’s equal parts invigorating and refreshing, this cucumber mint cooler is a delicious option. Its light, bright flavors make it an ideal choice for outdoor brunch parties or casual gatherings under the sun.

Ingredients:

  • 5 slices of English cucumber
  • 4-5 fresh mint leaves
  • 2 oz gin or tequila
  • 1 oz lime juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • Soda water, to top
  • Ice cubes or crushed ice
  • Mint sprig or cucumber ribbon for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Muddle cucumber slices and mint leaves in the bottom of a shaker until aromatic.
  2. Add gin or tequila, lime juice, and simple syrup to the shaker with ice. Shake vigorously.
  3. Strain into a glass filled with crushed ice. Top with soda water for bubbles and stir.
  4. Garnish with a mint sprig or a decorative cucumber ribbon.

Pair this cocktail with light bites like avocado toast, zucchini fritters, or chilled soups.

5. Strawberry Frosé Slush

Chilled to perfection, this frozen rosé cocktail is a vibrant, crowd-pleasing drink that combines ripe strawberries with a refreshing rosé wine base. This icy delight is perfect for outdoor brunches where the sun is shining bright.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups frozen strawberries
  • 1 cup chilled rosé wine
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • 1 oz honey or agave syrup
  • Fresh strawberry slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add frozen strawberries, rosé, lemon juice, and honey or agave syrup to a blender. Blend until smooth and slushy.
  2. Pour into chilled glasses and garnish with strawberry slices.

Pair this refreshing frosé with fluffy buttermilk pancakes, grilled peach salads, or citrus-glazed pastries.

6. Iced Lavender Latte Martini

For a coffee-flavored option with a spring twist, this iced lavender latte martini strikes the perfect balance between bold coffee and floral notes. It’s ideal for those brunch-goers who crave that energizing jolt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz espresso or strongly brewed coffee, chilled
  • 1 oz vodka
  • 1 oz coffee liqueur
  • 1 oz lavender-infused simple syrup
  • Ice cubes
  • Dried lavender for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine espresso, vodka, coffee liqueur, and lavender simple syrup in a shaker filled with ice. Shake vigorously.
  2. Strain into a chilled martini glass or coupe.
  3. Sprinkle dried lavender or use a lavender sprig for garnish.

Pair this cocktail with nutty biscotti, almond croissants, or savory sausage quiche.

Pro Brunch Tips

  1. Fresh is Best: Always opt for fresh, seasonal fruits and herbs to maximize flavor and presentation. Spring and summer offer endless possibilities—from berries and citrus to melons and stone fruits.
  2. Serve Chilled: To keep cocktails cool, store glasses and mixers in the freezer before assembly. For outdoor brunches, stationed ice buckets can keep bottles and garnishes cold.
  3. Make Ahead: Preparing cocktail bases like syrups, infusions, or purees in advance saves time during busy brunch prep.
  4. Balance Your Menu: These vibrant cocktails sing when paired with dishes that highlight complementary flavors. Think fresh salads, flaky pastries, and savory eggs to anchor your meal.

Elevate your brunch game with these thoughtfully crafted cocktails that embrace the vibrancy of spring and summer. Whether you’re sipping on a sparkling cucumber mint cooler or indulging in a decadent lavender latte martini, these beverages will keep spirits high and taste buds delighted. Celebrate warmer days with drinks that fuse creativity and refreshment in every sip! Cheers to unforgettable brunch moments.
