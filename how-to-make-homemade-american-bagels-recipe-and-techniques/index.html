How to Make Homemade American Bagels: Recipe and Techniques


Bagels, with their delightful chewy texture and glossy crust, are a breakfast staple that can be easily made at home. Making homemade American bagels not only fills your kitchen with a mouthwatering aroma but also allows you to customize flavors and toppings to your liking. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the process of creating perfect bagels from scratch, including expert techniques and tips to achieve that authentic New York-style bagel.

The History of Bagels

Bagels have a rich history that dates back to the 16th century in Poland, where they were originally made as a way to celebrate the birth of a child. These boiled and baked dough rings gradually made their way to America with Jewish immigrants in the early 20th century. Today, bagels are enjoyed worldwide, often served with cream cheese, lox, or simply toasted with butter. Understanding the origins of bagels adds a layer of appreciation to this culinary craft.

Ingredients You Will Need

For the Dough

  • 4 cups bread flour: Bread flour has a higher protein content, resulting in a chewier texture.
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast: Instant yeast allows for quicker rising and produces a consistent result.
  • 1 tablespoon salt: Salt enhances flavor and controls yeast activity.
  • 1 tablespoon sugar: Sugar feeds the yeast, helping the dough rise.
  • 1 ½ cups warm water: The ideal temperature is around 110°F (43°C) for activating the yeast.

For the Boiling Water

  • 2 quarts water: This will be used to boil the bagels before baking.
  • 1 tablespoon baking soda: Baking soda gives the bagels their signature shiny crust.
  • 1 tablespoon malt syrup or honey: This is optional but adds flavor and enhances the browning during baking.

Toppings (Optional)

  • Sesame seeds
  • Poppy seeds
  • Everything bagel seasoning
  • Onion flakes
  • Garlic powder

Equipment You Will Need

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Wooden spoon or dough scraper
  • Clean surface for kneading
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper
  • Large pot for boiling
  • Slotted spoon
  • Cooling rack

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, instant yeast, salt, and sugar. Mix well to ensure the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. Add Warm Water: Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the warm water. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms.

  3. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface. Knead for about 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. If the dough is sticky, add a little more flour as needed.

  4. First Rise: Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl, covering it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap. Allow it to rise in a warm place for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.

Step 2: Shape the Bagels

  1. Divide the Dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Turn it out onto a floured surface and divide it into 8 equal pieces for large bagels or 12 pieces for smaller ones.

  2. Shape Each Bagel: Take one piece of dough and roll it into a ball. Use your finger to poke a hole in the center, then gently stretch the hole to form a ring. Make sure the hole is wide enough, as it will shrink during boiling and baking. Repeat this process with all pieces of dough.

  3. Second Rise: Place the shaped bagels on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Cover them with a towel and let them rise for another 20-30 minutes. This short rise allows them to relax and become less dense.

Step 3: Boil the Bagels

  1. Prepare the Boiling Water: In a large pot, bring 2 quarts of water to a boil. Add the baking soda and malt syrup or honey if using. This mixture will help create that signature chewy texture.

  2. Boil the Bagels: Carefully drop a few bagels into the boiling water, being cautious not to overcrowd the pot. Boil for about 30 seconds on each side. Use a slotted spoon to remove the bagels and place them back on the baking sheet. This boiling step is crucial for achieving that classic bagel texture.

Step 4: Add Toppings

  1. Preheat the Oven: While you are boiling the bagels, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Apply Toppings: If you wish to add toppings, now is the time. Brush the boiled bagels with water and sprinkle your desired toppings over the surface. Be generous, as the toppings will enhance the flavor of your bagels.

Step 5: Bake the Bagels

  1. Bake: Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes or until the bagels are golden brown. The longer you bake, the more crusty they will become.

  2. Cool: Once baked, transfer the bagels to a cooling rack. Allow them to cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing. This will help retain their moisture and enhance their flavor.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Bagels

  • Use Bread Flour: For that chewy texture, bread flour is essential. All-purpose flour may result in a softer bagel.
  • Don’t Skip the Boiling: Boiling the bagels is a key step that distinguishes bagels from other types of bread. It sets the crust and gives them their unique chewiness.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to mix in various ingredients into the dough. Chopped herbs, cheese, or spices can create deliciously unique bagels.
  • Storage: Homemade bagels can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for a day or two. For longer storage, freeze them and toast directly from the freezer when needed.

Serving Suggestions

Homemade bagels can be enjoyed in numerous ways. Here are a few serving suggestions:

  • Classic Cream Cheese: Spread a generous layer of cream cheese on a toasted bagel for a traditional breakfast.
  • Lox and Capers: For a New York-style experience, top your bagel with smoked salmon, capers, red onion, and a squeeze of lemon.
  • Avocado Toast: Smash some avocado, season it with salt and pepper, and spread it on your bagel for a trendy twist.
  • Breakfast Sandwich: Layer a fried egg, bacon, or sausage on your bagel for a hearty breakfast sandwich.

Conclusion

Making homemade American bagels is a rewarding culinary project that yields delicious results. With a simple set of ingredients and a few techniques, you can create bagels that rival your favorite bakery. Experiment with flavors, toppings, and serving styles to make them your own. Whether you enjoy them plain, toasted, or piled high with your favorite spreads, homemade bagels are sure to become a cherished part of your breakfast routine. So roll up your sleeves, embrace the process, and enjoy the delightful journey of bagel-making!
