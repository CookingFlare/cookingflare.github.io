How to Throw a Christmas Cookie Decorating Party


The holiday season is a magical time filled with joy, laughter, and, of course, delicious treats. One of the most delightful ways to celebrate this festive season is by throwing a Christmas cookie decorating party. Gather your friends and family, roll up your sleeves, and get ready for an evening of creativity and sweet indulgence. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to host a memorable cookie decorating party, from planning and preparation to decorating techniques and party ideas.

Planning Your Cookie Decorating Party

Setting the Date and Time

Choose a date that works for you and your guests. Weekends in December are popular, but a weekday evening can also be a great option. Aim for around 2-3 hours of decorating time, which will give everyone enough time to create their masterpieces without feeling rushed.

Creating Your Guest List

Decide on the number of guests you want to invite. A smaller group can foster a more intimate atmosphere, while a larger gathering can bring a festive buzz. Make sure to reach out to your guests in advance so they can save the date.

Sending Out Invitations

Send out invitations that reflect the festive spirit of the party. You can create traditional paper invitations or use digital ones through email or social media platforms. Include the date, time, location, and any special instructions, like bringing an apron or a favorite cookie recipe to share.

Preparing for the Party

Choosing the Right Cookies

To ensure everyone has a fun decorating experience, select a few types of cookies that are easy to decorate and taste delicious. Classic options include:

  • Sugar Cookies: These are versatile and can be cut into various shapes using cookie cutters.
  • Gingerbread Cookies: A holiday favorite that can be decorated with royal icing.
  • Shortbread Cookies: Simple and buttery, these can be adorned with chocolate or sprinkles.

Baking the Cookies

Bake your cookies in advance so they have time to cool before the party. Here’s a quick guide for each cookie type:

Sugar Cookies

  • Ingredients: 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon baking soda, ½ teaspoon baking powder, 1 cup unsalted butter, 1 ½ cups white sugar, 1 egg, 1 teaspoon vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt.
  • Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a bowl, stir together flour, baking soda, and baking powder. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until smooth. Beat in the egg and vanilla. Gradually blend in the dry ingredients. Roll rounded teaspoonfuls of dough into balls and place onto ungreased pans. Flatten each ball with the bottom of a glass. Bake 8 to 10 minutes or until golden.

Gingerbread Cookies

  • Ingredients: 3 cups all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon baking soda, 1 tablespoon ground ginger, 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon, ¼ teaspoon ground cloves, ½ teaspoon salt, ¾ cup unsalted butter, ¾ cup brown sugar, 1 egg, and 1 cup unsulfured molasses.
  • Instructions: In a bowl, mix flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. In a separate bowl, cream the butter and brown sugar until fluffy. Beat in the egg and molasses. Gradually add the flour mixture. Chill the dough for at least two hours. Roll out and cut into shapes, then bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes.

Shortbread Cookies

  • Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 cup unsalted butter, ½ cup powdered sugar, and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract.
  • Instructions: Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Cream together the butter and sugar until smooth. Stir in the vanilla. Gradually mix in the flour until well blended. Roll the dough into balls and flatten them on a baking sheet. Bake for 18-20 minutes.

Preparing the Decorating Station

Create a designated decorating area in your home where guests can sit and get creative. Here’s how to set it up:

  • Tables and Chairs: Arrange tables and chairs to accommodate your guests comfortably.
  • Table Coverings: Use disposable tablecloths or craft paper to protect the surfaces from icing and sprinkles.
  • Decorating Supplies: Set out various decorating supplies, including:
    • Icing: Prepare different colors of royal icing or buttercream in piping bags with various tips.
    • Sprinkles and Edible Decorations: Stock up on a variety of sprinkles, colored sugars, chocolate chips, and candies.
    • Tools: Provide spatulas, offset spatulas, and cake decorating tools to help with the decorating process.

Drinks and Snacks

To keep the festive spirit alive, offer a selection of drinks and light snacks. Consider these options:

  • Hot Chocolate Bar: Provide various toppings such as whipped cream, marshmallows, and peppermint sticks.
  • Festive Punch: Create a non-alcoholic punch with cranberry juice, ginger ale, and fresh fruit.
  • Savory Snacks: Serve a variety of appetizers like cheese platters, veggie trays, and holiday-themed snacks to keep energy levels up.

Hosting the Party

Welcoming Your Guests

As your guests arrive, greet them warmly and show them to the decorating station. Offer them a drink and encourage them to mingle and chat while waiting for everyone to arrive.

Demonstrating Decorating Techniques

Once all guests have arrived, gather everyone around and demonstrate a few basic decorating techniques. Show them how to pipe outlines, fill in colors, and use different sprinkles. Encourage guests to unleash their creativity and try new ideas.

Decorating Time

Let the decorating begin! Encourage guests to take their time and enjoy the process. Play some festive music in the background to create a joyful atmosphere. Walk around, offering tips and encouragement, and take photos to capture the fun moments.

Cookie Contest (Optional)

To add a competitive edge to the party, consider hosting a cookie decorating contest. Create categories such as “Most Creative,” “Best Holiday Theme,” and “Funniest Cookie.” Provide small prizes for the winners, which could be festive-themed items or baking supplies.

Wrapping Up the Party

Displaying the Cookies

Once everyone has finished decorating, set up a display table to showcase all the beautifully decorated cookies. This creates a fun photo opportunity and allows guests to admire each other’s work.

Packing Up Cookies

Provide guests with boxes or bags to take their cookies home. You can even include a little card with the recipe for the cookies they decorated. This way, they can recreate the fun at home.

Thanking Your Guests

As the party comes to a close, thank your guests for coming and making the evening special. Consider sending follow-up messages or emails to express your gratitude and share any photos taken during the event.

Final Tips for a Successful Cookie Decorating Party

  • Set Up Ahead of Time: Prepare the decorating station and bake the cookies in advance to minimize stress on the day of the party.
  • Be Flexible: Allow guests to express their creativity without strict guidelines. The goal is to have fun and enjoy each other’s company.
  • Encourage Teamwork: If space allows, consider pairing guests up for decorating, fostering collaboration and shared laughter.
  • Document the Experience: Capture memories by taking photos during the party, and share them with your guests afterward.

Throwing a Christmas cookie decorating party is a wonderful way to celebrate the holiday season with loved ones. With thoughtful planning, delicious cookies, and a sprinkle of creativity, you’ll create an unforgettable experience filled with laughter, joy, and festive spirit. So gather your supplies, invite your friends, and get ready to spread holiday cheer one decorated cookie at a time!
