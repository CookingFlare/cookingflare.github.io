The Perfect Thai Sweet Chili Sauce Recipe

4 min · 655 words

Thai cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors and unique combinations of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy. One staple ingredient that adds a burst of flavor to many Thai dishes is the Thai sweet chili sauce. This versatile condiment can be used as a dipping sauce, marinade, or glaze, and it’s incredibly easy to make at home. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create the perfect Thai sweet chili sauce that will elevate your favorite dishes to a whole new level.

Ingredients

  • 10-12 red chili peppers (Thai bird’s eye chilies are best)
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the chili peppers: Start by removing the stems from the chili peppers and roughly chop them. If you prefer a milder sauce, you can remove the seeds as well. Remember to handle the chili peppers with caution and avoid touching your face or eyes.

  2. Blend the chili peppers and garlic: In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped chili peppers and garlic cloves. Blend until you have a smooth paste. You can adjust the spiciness of the sauce by adding more or fewer chili peppers.

  3. Cook the chili paste: In a medium saucepan, combine the chili paste, white sugar, water, rice vinegar, and salt. Stir well to dissolve the sugar. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken slightly.

  4. Thicken the sauce: In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water to create a slurry. Add the slurry to the saucepan and stir well. Continue to cook the sauce for another 2-3 minutes until it thickens to your desired consistency.

  5. Cool and store: Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the sauce cool completely. Once cooled, transfer the sauce to a sterilized jar or airtight container. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to two weeks.

Serving Suggestions

Now that you have the perfect Thai sweet chili sauce, here are a few ways to enjoy it:

  1. Dipping Sauce: Serve the Thai sweet chili sauce alongside crispy spring rolls, grilled chicken satay, or vegetable tempura. Its sweet and spicy flavors will complement the crispy textures perfectly.

  2. Marinade: Use the Thai sweet chili sauce as a marinade for chicken, shrimp, or tofu. Let the protein marinate for at least 30 minutes before grilling or baking. The sauce will infuse the dish with a delightful balance of flavors.

  3. Glaze: Brush the Thai sweet chili sauce onto grilled meats, such as pork chops or salmon fillets, during the last few minutes of cooking. The heat will help caramelize the sugars in the sauce, creating a sticky and delicious glaze.

  4. Stir-Fry: Add a spoonful or two of the Thai sweet chili sauce to your favorite stir-fry dishes. It will add a touch of sweetness and spice, elevating the overall flavor profile.

Get Creative

Feel free to experiment with the Thai sweet chili sauce recipe to suit your taste preferences. You can add a squeeze of lime juice for extra tanginess, or even a splash of fish sauce for a savory twist. The possibilities are endless!

So, the next time you’re craving the flavors of Thailand, whip up a batch of this homemade Thai sweet chili sauce. It’s easy to make, bursting with flavor, and will take your dishes to new culinary heights. Enjoy!

Note: This recipe yields approximately 2 cups of Thai sweet chili sauce. You can easily double or halve the recipe depending on your needs.
