Rich and Creamy Pumpkin Bread Pudding Recipe for a Sweet Ending


There’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting dessert to bring a satisfying end to a delicious meal. And when it comes to fall flavors, pumpkin takes the cake. Or in this case, the bread pudding. This rich and creamy pumpkin bread pudding recipe is the ultimate indulgence for pumpkin lovers, combining the flavors of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves with the softness of bread and the richness of a custard. So grab your apron and let’s dive into this heavenly dessert!

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of day-old bread, preferably brioche or challah, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of canned pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the milk, cream, pumpkin puree, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and salt until well combined. Set aside.
  3. Place the bread cubes in the prepared baking dish, making sure to distribute them evenly.
  4. Pour the pumpkin mixture over the bread cubes, pressing down gently to ensure all the bread is soaked.
  5. Allow the bread to soak for about 15-20 minutes, or until it has absorbed most of the liquid.
  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the bread pudding is set and golden brown on top.
  7. Remove the bread pudding from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.
  8. Serve the pumpkin bread pudding warm, either on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra touch of decadence.

Tips and Variations

  • For added texture and flavor, you can sprinkle some chopped nuts, such as pecans or walnuts, on top of the bread pudding before baking.
  • If you prefer a more intense pumpkin flavor, you can increase the amount of pumpkin puree to 1 1/2 cups.
  • Don’t have any day-old bread on hand? You can quickly dry out fresh bread by toasting it in the oven for a few minutes until it becomes slightly crispy.
  • This pumpkin bread pudding can also be made ahead of time and reheated before serving. Simply cover the baking dish with foil and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to serve, remove the foil and bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until warmed through.

Conclusion

Indulge your taste buds with this rich and creamy pumpkin bread pudding that embodies the essence of fall. The combination of warm spices and velvety custard, paired with the softness of the bread, creates a dessert that is both comforting and decadent. Whether served as a sweet ending to a holiday feast or as a special treat on a cozy evening, this pumpkin bread pudding is sure to delight your senses and leave you craving more. So go ahead, give this recipe a try and enjoy the flavors of autumn in every blissful bite!
