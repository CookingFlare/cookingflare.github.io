Classic Coconut Cake with a Twist: Think Pink!


The classic coconut cake, a beloved dessert throughout the ages, is getting a fresh, modern twist. This time, pink is the new white! Say goodbye to the traditional snowy-white frosting and hello to a vibrant burst of pink, sure to delight the eyes and taste buds of your family and friends alike. This tantalizing, yet relatively simple recipe will enhance your basic coconut cake experience and bring you a new favorite treat – a Classic Coconut Cake with a Twist: Think Pink!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sweetened shredded coconut
  • 1 tablespoon boiling water
  • 2 cups sifted all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups buttermilk
  • 3/4 cup raspberry puree (from fresh raspberries - see instructions below for preparation)
  • Pink food coloring (optional)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup raspberry puree (from fresh raspberries)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2-3 cups shredded coconut, for topping
  • Toothpicks or cake skewers (for decorating)

Raspberry Puree Preparation:

Before preparing the coconut cake, it’s essential to make raspberry puree. To achieve a vibrant pink color, you’ll need around 1 1/2 cups of fresh raspberries.

  1. Wash the raspberries thoroughly and remove any stems or leaves.
  2. Place the raspberries in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth, creating a puree. Be mindful that freshly pureed raspberries may tend to be on the thicker side, which is crucial for this recipe.

Now that the raspberry puree is prepared, it’s time to dive into the actual cake-making process!

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans and set them aside.
  2. In a small bowl, combine sweetened shredded coconut and boiling water. Set aside for later.
  3. In a separate medium-sized bowl, whisk together sifted all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. This mixture forms the foundation of our cake base, requiring gentle yet thorough incorporation. Set aside as well.
  4. In a large, mixing bowl with an electric mixer, cream together softened unsalted butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy, approximately 3-4 minutes.
  5. Beat in two eggs, one at a time, followed by the addition of the vanilla extract. Ensure to blend well after each addition.
  6. Gradually mix in the sifted flour mixture into the wet ingredients alternately with the buttermilk. When the dry and wet mixtures have been combined, gently fold in the coconut that has been soaking in boiling water, being careful not to overmix.
  7. To create two separate batters, evenly divide the coconut cake batter between two bowls. Add in the raspberry puree to one bowl and, if desired, a couple of drops of pink food coloring to achieve an even more vibrant pink hue. Gently mix the raspberries and food coloring into the batter until well combined and a pretty shade of pink.
  8. Pour the pink batter into one prepared cake pan and the white batter into the other.
  9. Bake both layers in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. When done, remove from the oven and let them cool in the pans for a few minutes before transferring onto wire racks to fully cool.
  10. While your cakes are cooling, prepare the frosting by combining softened unsalted butter, powdered sugar, raspberry puree, and vanilla extract in a mixing bowl. Mix until smooth and creamy, adjusting the consistency as needed by adding more powdered sugar for thickness or raspberry puree for thinning.
  11. Once the cakes have fully cooled, place the pink cake layer onto a plate or cake stand, and top it with approximately a third of the raspberry buttercream frosting. Carefully place the white cake layer on top, pressing down gently to ensure it is stable.
  12. Apply the remaining frosting evenly on top and outer edges of the stacked layers, ensuring a smooth and appealing finish. For decoration, gently press shredded coconut into the frosted cake, going all the way around to create a snowy appearance.
  13. To finish off this delightful coconut cake with a twist, use a few toothpicks or cake skewers to create a whimsical stem effect, piercing the top of the cake gently.

Be ready to delight everyone as they experience the combination of classic coconut and the whimsical touch of pink raspberry goodness. The tartness of the raspberries beautifully complements the sweetness of the cake and frosting, creating an unforgettable dessert experience.

Conclusion:

Enjoy your Classic Coconut Cake with a Twist: Think Pink and share with your friends and family. This dish not only satisfies your sweet cravings but also delivers a striking, visually stunning presentation. Don’t be surprised if this becomes the crown jewel of your dessert repertoire! Happy baking, and remember to keep experimenting and refining your culinary skills. The world of baking knows no bounds when you’re open to trying new flavors and techniques.
