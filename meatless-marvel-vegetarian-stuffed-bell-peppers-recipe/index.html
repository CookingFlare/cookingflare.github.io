Meatless Marvel: Vegetarian Stuffed Bell Peppers Recipe


Are you looking for a delicious and nutritious meatless recipe that will tantalize your taste buds? Look no further than these vegetarian stuffed bell peppers! Packed with wholesome ingredients and bursting with flavor, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your meal rotation. Whether you’re a dedicated vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these stuffed bell peppers are a delightful way to enjoy a meatless marvel.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color will do!)
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (optional, for topping)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Bell Peppers: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place the peppers in a baking dish, cut side up, and set aside.

  2. Cook the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  3. Prepare the Filling: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic, and sauté until softened. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes, corn, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and let the mixture cook for 5-7 minutes.

  4. Combine Quinoa and Filling: Once the quinoa is ready, add it to the skillet with the filling mixture. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Stuff the Peppers: Spoon the quinoa and vegetable mixture into the prepared bell peppers, filling them to the top. If desired, sprinkle shredded cheese on top of each stuffed pepper.

  6. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake the stuffed peppers in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the stuffed peppers are cooked, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve them warm and savor the delightful flavors!

Nutritional Benefits

These vegetarian stuffed bell peppers are not only a treat for your taste buds but also a nutritional powerhouse. Bell peppers are rich in vitamin C, while quinoa provides a good dose of protein and fiber. The black beans offer additional protein and fiber, making this dish a satisfying and wholesome option for vegetarians and non-vegetarians alike.

Conclusion

With their vibrant colors and delectable flavors, these vegetarian stuffed bell peppers are a feast for the senses. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply preparing a weeknight meal, this recipe is a versatile and crowd-pleasing choice. Embrace the meatless marvel of these stuffed bell peppers and enjoy a hearty, nutritious, and utterly satisfying dining experience.
