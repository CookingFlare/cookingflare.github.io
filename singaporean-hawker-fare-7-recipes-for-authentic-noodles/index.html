Singaporean Hawker Fare: 7 Recipes for Authentic Noodles


Singapore, often called the Lion City, is renowned globally for its rich tapestry of culinary traditions. A melting pot of Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Peranakan influences, this bustling city-state is where flavors unite to create something truly unique. Among its standout offerings, the hawker fare is perhaps the most iconic. Situated in bustling hawker centers, these dishes tell the story of generations, mixing bold flavors and humble origins.

In any discussion of Singaporean hawker cuisine, noodles demand their own spotlight. These versatile, comforting, and flavor-packed creations are a testament to the vibrant food culture of Singapore. Whether stir-fried, in a broth, or tossed in a savory sauce, Singaporean noodle dishes are a feast for the senses. Below, we uncover seven mouthwatering noodle recipes inspired by authentic hawker treasures, so you can bring a slice of Singapore’s bustling food culture into your kitchen.

1. Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow, literally meaning “stir-fried rice noodles,” is a quintessential Singaporean street food favorite. It’s smoky, savory, and slightly sweet, with a mix of textures that make every bite a delight.

Ingredients:

  • 300g kway teow (flat rice noodles)
  • 100g yellow noodles
  • 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 100g prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 100g bean sprouts
  • 2 Chinese sausages (lap cheong), sliced
  • Chopped chives, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Mix dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar in a small bowl to create the seasoning.
  2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat. Add garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  3. Toss in the prawns and sausages, cooking until prawns turn pink.
  4. Push everything to one side and scramble the beaten eggs in the wok.
  5. Add kway teow, yellow noodles, and the seasoning mixture. Stir-fry briskly, ensuring everything is well-coated.
  6. Add bean sprouts and stir for another 1-2 minutes.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with fresh chives.

2. Laksa

This spicy, creamy noodle soup is a star of Peranakan cuisine, a fusion of Chinese and Malay influences. Its rich and aromatic coconut-based broth paired with vermicelli noodles creates a bowl of indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 200g thick rice vermicelli noodles
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 600ml chicken stock
  • 100g laksa paste (store-bought or homemade)
  • 150g prawns, peeled
  • 100g fish cakes, sliced
  • 1 boiled egg, halved
  • 50g bean sprouts
  • 1 stalk Vietnamese coriander (laksa leaf), chopped
  • Lime wedges, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions, then set aside.
  2. In a pot, heat a tablespoon of oil and sauté the laksa paste until fragrant.
  3. Add coconut milk and chicken stock, stirring to combine. Bring to a gentle boil.
  4. Add prawns, fish cakes, and bean sprouts to the pot. Cook until the prawns are pink and cooked through.
  5. Place noodles in serving bowls and ladle the hot laksa broth over them.
  6. Top with boiled egg halves, Vietnamese coriander, and a wedge of lime.

3. Mee Goreng

This spicy noodle dish of Indian-Muslim heritage packs bold flavors and a touch of fiery heat. The combination of chewy yellow noodles, vegetables, egg, and chili paste makes it a hawker center classic.

Ingredients:

  • 250g yellow noodles
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 100g tofu, cubed and fried
  • 100g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • A handful of green vegetables (e.g. spinach or choy sum)
  • Lime wedges and fried shallots for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok and sauté garlic, onion, and chili paste until fragrant.
  2. Add tofu, shrimp, and vegetables, stir-frying until cooked.
  3. Push the mixture aside and pour in the eggs. Scramble gently until set.
  4. Add the yellow noodles, soy sauce, and ketchup. Stir-fry until the noodles are evenly coated and slightly caramelized.
  5. Serve with fried shallots and a squeeze of lime.

4. Hokkien Prawn Mee

Hokkien Prawn Mee is a flavorful stir-fry combining yellow noodles and rice vermicelli with a robust prawn stock, making it a savory noodle dish with rich umami undertones.

Ingredients:

  • 150g yellow noodles
  • 150g rice vermicelli
  • 200g prawns, peeled and heads reserved
  • 500ml chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 100g squid, sliced
  • Lime wedges and sambal chili, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Simmer prawn heads in chicken stock for 15 minutes to create a rich broth. Strain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a wok and stir-fry garlic until fragrant. Add prawns and squid, cooking briefly.
  3. Push the seafood aside, pour in the beaten egg, and scramble lightly.
  4. Add yellow noodles and rice vermicelli to the wok along with the prawn stock and soy sauce. Stir-fry until most of the liquid evaporates.
  5. Serve with lime wedges and sambal chili on the side.

5. Wanton Mee

Wanton Mee is a beloved noodle dish served dry or in soup, featuring springy egg noodles, savory pork wantons, and char siu (roast pork). The dry version is especially popular in Singapore.

Ingredients:

  • 250g egg noodles
  • 150g char siu, sliced
  • 8 pork wantons (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Blanched leafy greens, such as kailan or bok choy
  • Chili paste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook egg noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and toss with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil.
  2. Boil the wantons until they float, then remove and drain.
  3. Serve noodles with a side of char siu, boiled wantons, and greens. Add chili paste if desired.

6. Bee Hoon Goreng

Bee Hoon Goreng, meaning “fried rice vermicelli,” is a Malay-influenced dish with a smoky, fiery kick. It’s light, flavorful, and quick to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice vermicelli
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 shallot, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 100g shredded chicken or thinly sliced beef
  • 50g bean sprouts
  • 1 egg, fried and sliced for topping

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice vermicelli in warm water until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a wok and sauté garlic, shallot, and chili paste until fragrant.
  3. Add carrots and chicken or beef, cooking until done.
  4. Stir in the rice vermicelli, soy sauce, and bean sprouts. Mix well and stir-fry evenly.
  5. Serve topped with fried egg slices.

7. Fish Ball Noodle Soup

Comforting and light, Fish Ball Noodle Soup is a heartwarming hawker dish featuring springy noodles, handmade fish balls, and a clear, savory broth.

Ingredients:

  • 250g thin egg noodles or rice noodles
  • 400ml chicken or fish stock
  • 12 fish balls
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • A handful of choy sum or bok choy
  • Light soy sauce and white pepper, to taste
  • Fried garlic and scallions, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Bring the stock to a boil in a pot. Add minced garlic, fish balls, and vegetables. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Season with soy sauce and white pepper.
  4. Divide noodles into bowls and ladle the broth, fish balls, and vegetables over them. Garnish with fried garlic and scallions.

These seven authentic noodle dishes bring the vibrant flavors of Singapore’s hawker fare into your kitchen. With a bit of preparation and a willingness to experiment, you can recreate these iconic dishes to savor the soul of Singapore in every bite. From smoky stir-fries to comforting soups, these recipes celebrate the diverse culinary heritage of this gastronomic paradise. Dive in, and let your taste buds take a memorable trip to the hawker centers of Singapore!
