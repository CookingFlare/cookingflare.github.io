Tantalizing Bourbon Glazed Ham for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for families to gather, share stories, and indulge in a feast that celebrates the season’s bounty. While turkey often steals the spotlight on this occasion, there’s something undeniably enchanting about a beautifully glazed ham that can elevate your holiday dinner to new heights. If you’re looking for a show-stopping centerpiece that marries sweet, savory, and smoky flavors, this Tantalizing Bourbon Glazed Ham recipe is exactly what you need.

Why Choose Ham?

Ham is a versatile protein that pairs well with various flavors, making it an excellent choice for Thanksgiving. Its rich, savory profile can stand up to bold seasonings, and when glazed with a bourbon-infused sauce, it transforms into a dish that will have your guests raving. Plus, ham can often be prepared ahead of time, allowing you to enjoy more quality moments with your family on the big day.

The Perfect Cut

When selecting your ham, consider the type and cut that best suits your gathering. There are two primary types of ham you can choose from:

  1. Bone-In Ham: This cut generally has more flavor and moisture. The bone adds depth to the overall taste and can also be used to create a delicious broth for soups after the holiday.

  2. Boneless Ham: This is easier to slice and serve, making it ideal for larger gatherings where ease of serving is a priority.

Opt for a high-quality, fully cooked ham, which will save you time and effort. A weight of 8 to 12 pounds is generally recommended for a gathering of 10-12 people.

Ingredients

For the Ham

  • 1 (8-12 lb) fully cooked bone-in or boneless ham
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup bourbon
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Whole cloves for studding the ham (optional)

For Garnish

  • Fresh herbs (such as thyme or rosemary)
  • Orange slices
  • Pomegranate seeds

Preparing the Ham

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (165°C). This moderate temperature allows the ham to heat through without drying out, ensuring a juicy final product.

Step 2: Prepare the Ham

Remove the ham from its packaging and pat it dry with paper towels. If you’re using a bone-in ham, you can score the surface in a diamond pattern, cutting about 1/4 inch deep. This not only helps the glaze penetrate but also creates an attractive presentation.

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can stud the ham with whole cloves, inserting them into the intersections of the diamond pattern. This adds a subtle spice to the finished dish.

Step 3: Make the Bourbon Glaze

In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine the brown sugar, honey, bourbon, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, stirring constantly until the sugar has dissolved. Allow it to simmer for about 5-7 minutes until it thickens slightly.

Step 4: Glazing the Ham

Place your ham in a roasting pan, cut side down. Brush a generous amount of the bourbon glaze over the surface of the ham, ensuring it seeps into the scored areas. Reserve some of the glaze for later basting.

Step 5: Bake the Ham

Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for approximately 15 minutes per pound. This means a 10-pound ham will take about 2.5 hours. Basting the ham with the reserved glaze every 30 minutes will help build a beautiful, caramelized crust.

Step 6: Add the Final Touch

During the last 30 minutes of cooking, remove the foil to allow the glaze to caramelize further. Keep an eye on it to prevent burning. Once the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C), remove the ham from the oven. Let it rest for at least 15 minutes before slicing.

Serving Suggestions

Once your tantalizing bourbon glazed ham has rested, it’s time to serve! Slice the ham into thin or thick pieces, depending on your preference. Arrange it on a platter and drizzle any remaining glaze over the top. For a festive touch, garnish with fresh herbs, orange slices, and pomegranate seeds.

Pair your ham with traditional Thanksgiving sides such as creamy mashed potatoes, roasted Brussels sprouts, or a refreshing cranberry salad. The sweet and smoky flavors of the ham complement these dishes beautifully, creating a balanced and satisfying meal.

Storing Leftovers

If you happen to have any leftover ham (which is unlikely, given how delicious this dish is), store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. You can also freeze sliced ham for up to 6 months. Just make sure to wrap it tightly to prevent freezer burn.

Creative Uses for Leftover Ham

Don’t let any leftover ham go to waste! Here are a few ideas to repurpose your delicious ham into new meals:

  • Ham and Cheese Omelette: Dice up your leftover ham and add it to a fluffy omelet with your favorite cheeses and vegetables for a hearty breakfast.
  • Ham Sandwiches: Layer slices of ham with cheese, lettuce, and mustard on your favorite bread for a quick and tasty lunch.
  • Ham and Bean Soup: Use the leftover ham bone to create a comforting bean soup, simmering it with vegetables and spices for a warming meal.
  • Quiche: Incorporate diced ham into a savory quiche with eggs, cheese, and seasonal vegetables for a delightful brunch option.

Conclusion

This Tantalizing Bourbon Glazed Ham is bound to become a cherished tradition at your Thanksgiving table. Not only does it provide a stunning centerpiece, but its rich, sweet, and smoky flavors will leave your guests coming back for seconds. With its straightforward preparation and the ability to be made ahead of time, it allows you to enjoy the holiday without stressing in the kitchen.

So this Thanksgiving, step away from the turkey and embrace the magic of a bourbon-glazed ham. It’s a dish that exemplifies the spirit of togetherness and gratitude that the holiday represents, making it a perfect addition to your festive celebration. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
