Exploring the Sweet Side: Middle Eastern Ashta (Clotted Cream)


When it comes to indulging in the sweet pleasures of Middle Eastern cuisine, one cannot ignore the rich and creamy delight that is Ashta, also known as clotted cream. Ashta is a luxurious dairy product that has been adored for centuries in the Middle East, particularly in Lebanon, Syria, and other Levantine countries. Its velvety texture and delicate sweetness make it a versatile ingredient for both traditional and modern desserts. In this blog post, we will delve into the fascinating world of Ashta, exploring its history, culinary uses, and, of course, some delectable recipes that showcase its heavenly flavor.

The History of Ashta

Ashta holds a significant place in Middle Eastern culinary traditions, with its origins dating back to ancient times. The process of making Ashta involves simmering milk over low heat for an extended period, allowing the cream to naturally rise to the surface and form a thick, luscious layer. This meticulous technique has been passed down through generations, and the result is a luxurious dairy product that embodies the essence of indulgence.

Culinary Uses of Ashta

The versatility of Ashta in the kitchen is truly remarkable. Its creamy texture and subtly sweet taste make it a perfect accompaniment to a wide array of desserts. From traditional pastries to modern creations, Ashta elevates the flavor and texture of any sweet dish it graces. It can be used as a filling for pastries such as qatayef, a popular Middle Eastern dessert that is enjoyed during the holy month of Ramadan. Ashta is also a key component in ice creams, mouhalabieh (milk pudding), and a variety of other confections. Its ability to add richness and depth to desserts makes it a beloved ingredient in Middle Eastern sweet treats.

Ashta Recipes to Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth

Now, let’s explore some delightful recipes that highlight the exquisite flavor of Ashta:

Ashta-filled Qatayef

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 2 cups Ashta
  • 1 cup finely chopped pistachios
  • Simple syrup for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, sugar, and water to form a smooth batter.
  2. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and pour 1 tablespoon of the batter to form small pancakes.
  3. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Once the pancakes are cool, fill each with a spoonful of Ashta, fold in half, and pinch the edges to seal.
  5. Heat some oil in a pan and fry the filled qatayef until golden brown.
  6. Drizzle with simple syrup and sprinkle with chopped pistachios before serving.

Ashta Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup Ashta
  • 1 cup condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  2. Gently fold in the Ashta, condensed milk, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. Pour the mixture into a freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 6 hours.
  4. Scoop and serve the luscious Ashta ice cream, topped with your favorite nuts or fruits.

In Conclusion

Ashta, the heavenly clotted cream of the Middle East, is a testament to the region’s rich culinary heritage. Its velvety texture and delicate sweetness make it a cherished ingredient in a wide range of desserts, from traditional delicacies to modern creations. Whether enjoyed in a qatayef during Ramadan or churned into a luxurious ice cream, Ashta never fails to captivate the senses and leave a lasting impression of indulgence. So, the next time you’re craving a taste of the Middle East, consider incorporating the delightful essence of Ashta into your sweet creations.
