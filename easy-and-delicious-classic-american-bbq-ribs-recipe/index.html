Easy and Delicious Classic American BBQ Ribs Recipe


There’s nothing quite like sinking your teeth into tender, juicy, and flavorful BBQ ribs. The smoky aroma, the caramelized glaze, and the savory meat falling off the bone – it’s the epitome of classic American comfort food. Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout, planning a family dinner, or simply craving some finger-licking goodness, this easy and delicious Classic American BBQ Ribs recipe is a surefire crowd-pleaser. So, roll up your sleeves, fire up the grill, and get ready to embark on a mouthwatering culinary adventure!

Ingredients

For the Ribs:

  • 2 racks of pork baby back ribs
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the BBQ Sauce:

  • 1 1/2 cups ketchup
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Ribs

  1. Preheat the Grill: Preheat your grill to 275°F (135°C) using indirect heat. If you’re using a gas grill, turn off one of the burners to create an indirect cooking zone.

  2. Remove the Membrane: To ensure tender ribs, remove the thin membrane from the back of each rack. Use a small knife to lift the edge of the membrane, then grip it with a paper towel and pull it off.

  3. Season the Ribs: In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Rub this seasoning mixture evenly over both sides of the ribs.

Step 2: Smoking the Ribs

  1. Place on the Grill: Place the seasoned ribs on the grill, away from direct heat. If you’re using wood chips for extra smokiness, now is the time to add them to the grill.

  2. Slow Cook: Close the lid and let the ribs cook for 3 to 4 hours. Maintain a consistent temperature, and resist the temptation to peek too often – we want to keep that smoky goodness inside!

Step 3: Making the BBQ Sauce

  1. Combine Ingredients: While the ribs are cooking, it’s time to whip up the BBQ sauce. In a saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, honey, molasses, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper.

  2. Simmer: Bring the sauce to a simmer over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once it has thickened slightly, remove it from the heat.

Step 4: Glazing the Ribs

  1. Brush with Sauce: After the ribs have cooked for 3 to 4 hours, it’s time to work some magic with the BBQ sauce. Brush the ribs generously with the homemade sauce, ensuring a tantalizing glaze covers every inch.

  2. Caramelize: Increase the grill temperature to 300°F (150°C) and place the sauced ribs back on the grill for 15-20 minutes, allowing the sauce to caramelize and create that irresistible crust.

  3. Rest and Serve: Once the ribs are done, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. Then, it’s time to slice, serve, and watch the smiles light up around the table.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra kick of flavor, consider adding a dash of hot sauce or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the BBQ sauce.
  • If you don’t have a grill, you can achieve similar results by using a smoker or even your oven – simply adjust the cooking times and temperatures accordingly.
  • Don’t rush the cooking process – low and slow is the secret to perfectly succulent ribs.

In Conclusion

With its winning combination of smoky, sweet, and savory flavors, this Classic American BBQ Ribs recipe is a testament to the timeless appeal of barbecue cuisine. Whether you’re a seasoned pitmaster or a novice griller, this recipe is designed to deliver mouthwatering results with minimal effort. So, gather your loved ones, savor the anticipation as the aroma wafts through the air, and get ready to savor every delectable bite of these irresistible ribs. Happy grilling!
