How to Make Homemade Bagels for Brunch


Bagels hold a cherished spot in the hearts of breakfast and brunch lovers everywhere. Their shiny, chewy crust and soft, doughy interior create a delightful textural contrast that’s hard to resist. While store-bought bagels might be convenient, crafting your own at home elevates the experience into something truly special. In this guide, we’ll explore the art and science of making homemade bagels, so you can impress friends and family with a brunch that bursts with flavor and freshness.

Why Make Bagels at Home?

There’s something magical about biting into a freshly baked bagel steaming straight out of your oven. Homemade bagels allow complete control over ingredients, texture, and flavors. You can customize toppings, experiment with dough variations, and avoid preservatives commonly found in commercial bagels. Plus, the process of making bagels is deeply rewarding—mixing, shaping, boiling, and baking each to golden perfection.

The Foundations: Ingredients for Perfect Bagels

Creating the ideal bagel starts with fundamental ingredients that work harmoniously:

  • Flour: Bread flour with a higher protein content (11-13%) is best to develop the strong gluten structure that gives bagels their chewiness.
  • Water: Lukewarm water activates yeast and hydrates flour. Precise measurements affect dough consistency.
  • Yeast: Active dry yeast or instant yeast helps the dough rise and develop flavor.
  • Salt: Enhances flavor and tightens gluten structure.
  • Sweetener: Malt syrup or honey is traditional, helping browning during baking and lending a subtle sweetness.
  • Optional toppings: Sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything bagel mix, coarse salt, or even herbs.

Step-by-Step: Making the Dough

1. Activate the Yeast

Begin by dissolving your yeast into lukewarm water. This wakes up the yeast cells, especially if you’re using active dry yeast. After about 5 minutes, you should notice froth forming, indicating the yeast is alive and ready.

2. Mix Ingredients

Combine the flour, salt, and sweetener in a large mixing bowl. Slowly add yeast water and stir until the dough begins to come together. You can use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment or knead by hand.

3. Kneading

Knead the dough for about 10 minutes or until it becomes smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky (not sticky). Kneading develops gluten, which forms the network responsible for the bagel’s chew and structure.

4. First Rise

Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm spot until doubled in size, typically 1 to 1.5 hours.

Shaping the Bagels

Once your dough has risen, punch it down gently to release trapped air. Divide the dough into equal portions—usually 8 to 10 pieces depending on size preference.

Traditional Ring Shape

Roll each piece into a smooth ball. Then, using your thumb, poke a hole through the middle and gently stretch it out to about 2-3 inches in diameter. The hole will shrink during boiling and baking, so a generous opening helps maintain the classic bagel shape.

Pro tip: Keep the surface of the dough lightly floured but don’t overdo it, as excess flour can affect the final texture.

Boiling: The Unique Step That Sets Bagels Apart

Bagels are boiled before baking, which gelatinizes the starch on the surface, giving bagels their signature chewy crust. This step is what makes bagels different from regular bread rolls.

  • Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  • Add a tablespoon of malt syrup, honey, or baking soda. This additives alters the water chemistry, encouraging a glossy crust and helping the surface brown.
  • Boil bagels in batches, about 1 to 2 minutes per side.
  • Using a slotted spoon, remove them and drain on a wire rack or clean kitchen towel.

Baking to Golden Perfection

Preheat your oven to about 425°F (220°C). After boiling, bagels are ready for toppings and baking.

  • Place bagels on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  • Brush lightly with egg wash (1 beaten egg + a tablespoon water) for shine and helping toppings adhere.
  • Sprinkle your desired toppings.
  • Bake for 20-25 minutes until the crust is golden brown and crisp.

Creative Flavor Variations for Every Palate

Once you master plain bagels, feel free to explore variations that excite your taste buds:

  • Cinnamon Raisin Bagels: Add cinnamon and soaked raisins to the dough.
  • Onion Bagels: Incorporate sautéed onions inside the dough or sprinkle chopped onions on top.
  • Everything Bagels: Mix poppy seeds, sesame seeds, dried garlic, onion flakes, and coarse salt as classic toppings.
  • Cheese-Stuffed Bagels: Stuff with cream cheese or cheddar before boiling for a gooey surprise inside.

Serving Suggestions to Elevate Your Brunch

Homemade bagels are incredibly versatile. Serve sliced with:

  • Cream cheese (plain or flavored)
  • Smoked salmon and capers
  • Avocado and poached eggs
  • Nut butters and jam
  • Fresh vegetables and sprouts for a light sandwich

Pair with fresh fruit, coffee, or a mimosa for the full brunch vibe.

Tips and Troubleshooting

  • If dough is too sticky: Add flour sparingly while kneading, but avoid making it dry.
  • Bagels flattening: Make sure you boil for sufficient time and ensure the hole is large enough.
  • Crust not crisp: Use malt syrup or baking soda in boiling water; avoid steaming in the oven.
  • Dough rising too fast or slow: Adjust environment temperature. Yeast thrives around 75-80°F (24-27°C).

Final Thoughts

Making homemade bagels for brunch is both an enjoyable culinary adventure and a delicious way to impress your guests. The process—from the velvety dough to the shimmering crust—combines tradition and technique, transforming humble ingredients into an unforgettable treat. Once you master the basics, you’re free to customize endlessly, from sweet to savory, classic to adventurous. So fire up your kitchen, roll up your sleeves, and bring the bakery experience right into your home.

Ready to elevate your weekend brunch? Start with this recipe, and you’ll soon discover that homemade bagels are a labor of love well worth every bite.
