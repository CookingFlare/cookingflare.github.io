BBQ Mac and Cheese: Comfort Food with a Grilled Twist


As the weather starts to warm up, our cravings for comforting, creamy dishes begin to shift towards lighter, fresher options. But what if we told you that you don’t have to sacrifice the richness of mac and cheese for a grilled twist? Introducing BBQ Mac and Cheese, a game-changing recipe that combines the best of both worlds.

The Classic Comfort Food

Macaroni and cheese is a staple comfort food that evokes memories of childhood meals and cozy nights in. A perfectly cooked al dente pasta, smothered in a velvety cheese sauce, is the epitome of comfort. However, the classic recipe can be a bit…predictable. That’s where the BBQ twist comes in.

The Grilled Element

Adding a grilled element to your mac and cheese might seem unconventional, but trust us, it’s a revelation. The smoky, caramelized flavors of the grill perfectly complement the creamy cheese sauce, creating a depth of flavor that will leave you wanting more. And the best part? It’s surprisingly easy to incorporate into your recipe.

The Recipe

To make this BBQ Mac and Cheese, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups grated cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (optional)
  • 1/4 cup crumbled cooked bacon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Cook the macaroni according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk together to form a roux, cooking for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Slowly pour in the heavy cream, whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove the sauce from the heat and stir in the cheddar and mozzarella cheese until melted and smooth. Season with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the cooked macaroni, cheese sauce, and chopped cilantro (if using). Mix until the pasta is well coated.
  6. Transfer the macaroni mixture to a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  7. Grill the macaroni dish over medium heat for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the top is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.
  8. Remove the dish from the grill and sprinkle with crumbled bacon (if using). Serve hot and enjoy!

The Verdict

BBQ Mac and Cheese is a bold, comforting twist on a classic dish. The addition of grilled elements elevates the recipe to new heights, making it perfect for a summer dinner party or a cozy night in. Give it a try and experience the magic for yourself.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra crispy top, sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on top of the macaroni before grilling.
  • Substitute the heavy cream with half-and-half or whole milk for a lighter sauce.
  • Add some diced jalapeños or red pepper flakes for an extra kick of heat.
  • Use different types of cheese, such as Gruyère or Gouda, for a unique flavor profile.

With BBQ Mac and Cheese, you’ll never have to choose between comfort food and a grilled twist again. It’s the perfect recipe for anyone looking to mix things up and impress their friends and family. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the magic of grilled comfort food.
