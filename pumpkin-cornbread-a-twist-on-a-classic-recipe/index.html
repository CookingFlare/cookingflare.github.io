Pumpkin Cornbread: A Twist on a Classic Recipe


Are you looking to add a little twist to your classic cornbread recipe? Look no further than this delightful Pumpkin Cornbread! With its warm and comforting flavors, this recipe is perfect for autumn gatherings or cozy nights at home. The addition of pumpkin gives this cornbread a subtle sweetness and a lovely orange hue that will wow your taste buds and impress your guests. So, grab your apron and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9-inch square baking dish.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and cloves. Mix well until all the dry ingredients are evenly combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until well combined. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking well after each addition. Stir in the pumpkin puree and milk until the mixture is smooth and creamy.

  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense cornbread.

  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and smooth the top with a spatula.

  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The top should be golden brown and the edges slightly crisp.

  7. Remove from the oven and let the cornbread cool in the baking dish for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Pumpkin Cornbread is delicious on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it with some whipped honey butter or maple butter. The sweetness of the butter complements the subtle sweetness of the cornbread perfectly. You can also serve it alongside a warm bowl of chili or as a side dish for Thanksgiving dinner.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier kick, add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the batter.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of flour, such as whole wheat or gluten-free flour, to make this recipe suitable for dietary restrictions.
  • Add some texture to your cornbread by stirring in a handful of chopped nuts or dried cranberries before baking.
  • If you prefer a sweeter cornbread, increase the amount of sugar to your taste.

Final Thoughts

Pumpkin Cornbread is a delightful twist on a classic recipe that will impress your family and friends with its unique flavors and beautiful presentation. The combination of the warm spices and the subtle sweetness of the pumpkin creates a cornbread that is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a fall brunch or simply craving a comforting treat, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, go ahead and give it a try – you won’t be disappointed!
