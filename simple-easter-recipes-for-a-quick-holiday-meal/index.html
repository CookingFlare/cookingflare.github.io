Simple Easter Recipes for a Quick Holiday Meal


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and delicious food. However, not everyone has hours to spend in the kitchen preparing an elaborate feast. Fortunately, you can create a memorable holiday meal with simple, quick recipes that bring festive flavors to your table without the fuss. Whether you’re hosting a small brunch or a family dinner, these easy Easter recipes will help you enjoy the day without the stress.

Starter: Spring Pea and Mint Soup

Start your Easter meal with a light and refreshing Spring Pea and Mint Soup. This vibrant green soup is quick to prepare and makes a perfect seasonal appetizer. It highlights fresh peas, which are at their peak in spring, and the subtle coolness of mint complements the natural sweetness beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A dollop of Greek yogurt or crème fraîche (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent.
  2. Add peas and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in fresh mint leaves, then use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, blend in batches in a blender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve warm with a spoonful of Greek yogurt or crème fraîche on top.

Main Course: Honey-Glazed Roasted Ham

No Easter meal feels complete without ham, yet roasting a full-size ham can be time-consuming. This honey-glazed roasted ham recipe simplifies the process while delivering tender, flavorful meat with a beautiful golden crust. It’s great for smaller gatherings or when you want a no-fuss centerpiece.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-cooked spiral ham (about 5 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Fresh rosemary for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. Place the ham on a roasting pan, cut side down.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, and ground cloves.
  4. Brush the glaze generously over the ham.
  5. Roast the ham in the oven for about 1 to 1.5 hours, brushing with remaining glaze every 20 minutes.
  6. Once heated through and caramelized on the edges, remove from the oven and let rest for 10 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh rosemary before serving.

Side Dish: Cheesy Scalloped Potatoes with Spinach

Potatoes are Easter classics, and scalloped potatoes creamy with cheese can be a guaranteed crowd-pleaser. Adding spinach introduces a fresh, nutritious element that brightens the dish and adds color. This side dish can be assembled ahead of time, then baked just before serving.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large Yukon gold potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Butter a medium-sized baking dish.
  2. Layer half of the sliced potatoes into the dish.
  3. Spread the chopped spinach over the potatoes.
  4. Sprinkle 1 cup of shredded cheddar over the spinach.
  5. Add the remaining potato slices on top.
  6. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream, minced garlic, butter, salt, pepper, and nutmeg just until the butter melts and the mixture is warm.
  7. Pour the cream mixture evenly over the potatoes.
  8. Top with the remaining cheddar cheese.
  9. Cover with foil and bake for 40 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 20 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  10. Let the dish rest for 5 minutes before serving.

Fresh Salad: Citrus and Arugula Salad with Pecans

Create a bright and crunchy contrast to the richness of the ham and potatoes with a Citrus and Arugula Salad. The peppery arugula combined with the sweetness of orange and grapefruit slices and toasted pecans makes a refreshing salad that energizes the palate for the rest of the meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh arugula
  • 1 orange, peeled and segmented
  • 1 grapefruit, peeled and segmented
  • 1/2 cup toasted pecans
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine arugula, orange segments, grapefruit segments, and toasted pecans.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk olive oil, white wine vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently.
  4. Sprinkle with crumbled feta cheese if desired and serve immediately.

Dessert: Lemon Blueberry Pound Cake

For dessert, a Lemon Blueberry Pound Cake brings together fresh, fruity flavors with a moist, tender crumb. It’s perfect for spring, made in a single pan, and can be prepared the night before, freeing up your time on Easter day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a loaf pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then add lemon zest, lemon juice, and vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture.
  6. Gently fold in blueberries to avoid breaking them.
  7. Pour batter into the loaf pan and smooth the top.
  8. Bake for 55-60 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  9. Cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
  10. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Beverages: Sparkling Lavender Lemonade

Complement your meal with a refreshing Sparkling Lavender Lemonade. Its floral undertone and zesty citrus flavor make it a delightful accompaniment to Easter dishes, and it’s incredibly quick to put together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 3 cups sparkling water
  • 1/2 cup honey or simple syrup (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon dried culinary lavender
  • Ice cubes
  • Lemon slices and lavender sprigs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, bring 1/2 cup water to a boil. Remove from heat, add lavender, and steep for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain out lavender and stir honey into the warm lavender water until dissolved.
  3. In a large pitcher, combine lemon juice, lavender syrup, and sparkling water.
  4. Add ice cubes and stir gently.
  5. Garnish with lemon slices and fresh lavender sprigs.

Final Thoughts

With these simple Easter recipes, you can celebrate the holiday with minimal stress and maximum flavor. From the vibrant soup to the classic honey-glazed ham and fruity dessert, each dish captures the spirit of spring and togetherness. By focusing on fresh ingredients and straightforward techniques, you ensure that your Easter meal is both enjoyable to prepare and delightful to eat.

These recipes not only save time but also bring a cohesive, seasonal menu that can be scaled to fit any number of guests. You’ll have more time to enjoy your company—and maybe even sneak in an Easter egg hunt or two. Happy cooking and happy Easter!
