Slow Cooker Lentil Soup for a Nutritious Meal


Are you looking for a comforting and nutritious meal that's both easy to prepare and packed with protein and fiber? Look no further than this delicious and highly customizable slow cooker lentil soup recipe. This comforting potpourri of flavors and nutrients is perfect for a chilly evening or a weeknight meal solution, making it a great addition to your slow cooker repertoire. What You'll Need

1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed and drained 4 cups vegetable broth 2 medium-sized onions, chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 carrots, chopped 2 stalks celery, chopped 1 can (28 oz) diced tomatoes 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional) 1 teaspoon salt 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 bay leaf

Instructions Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients Begin by rinsing the lentils and placing them in the slow cooker. Add the vegetable broth, salt, cumin, oregano, smoked paprika, and bay leaf. Stir to combine, then add the chopped onions, garlic, carrots, and celery. Stir to combine. Step 2: Cook the Soup Place the lid on the slow cooker and cook the lentil mixture on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-5 hours. In the meantime, heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the diced tomatoes and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Step 3: Finish the Soup Once the cooking time is up, remove the bay leaf and stir in the cooked tomato mixture, cayenne pepper (if using), and chopped parsley. Use an immersion blender or a regular blender to puree some or all of the soup to your desired consistency. Serving and Variations This soup is incredibly versatile, making it perfect for meal prep or adjusting to your taste. Some ideas to consider:

For a creamier soup, stir in some heavy cream or Greek yogurt before serving. For a spicier soup, add more cayenne pepper or introduce other spices like cumin or chili powder. Experiment with different spices or herbs, like cinnamon, thyme, or basil, to give the soup a unique twist. Use a combination of vegetable broth and diced potatoes for a heartier soup.

Nutritional Benefits of the Slow Cooker Lentil Soup This slow cooker lentil soup recipe is an exemplary representation of the benefits of a plant-based diet:

Lentils are an excellent source of protein, fiber, and essential minerals like iron and zinc. Tomatoes add a boost of vitamin C, potassium, and lycopene, an antioxidant pigment. Brown or green lentils are higher in fiber and nutrients compared to red or yellow lentils. This recipe is vegan-friendly, dairy-free, and low in calories.

The Benefits of Using a Slow Cooker Investing in a slow cooker has numerous benefits, including:

Convenience: This recipe can simmer for hours without requiring attention, yet reap excellent results. Nutrient preservation: The slow cooking process ensures the breakdown of fiber, protein, and vitamins, making the ingredients more digestible and nutrient-dense. Versatility: Use the slow cooker for a range of recipes, from comforting stews and soups to hearty stews and flavorful vegetables.

Final Tips and Variations

Stir the soup before serving to redistribute the flavors and spices. For an exotic twist, swap the cumin with coriander or cumin substitutes, such as orajmeal or mountain rosemary for a more aromatic soup. If using other ingredients, blend with a fork or potato masher to break them down evenly before serving.

With this mouthwatering slow cooker lentil soup recipe, you'll enjoy a warm, nourishing, and delicious meal that blends comforting flavors with key nutrients for your body and soul.
