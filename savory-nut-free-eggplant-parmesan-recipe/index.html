Savory Nut-Free Eggplant Parmesan Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious and nut-free twist on a classic Italian dish, this savory eggplant parmesan recipe is sure to become a new favorite in your home. It’s a comforting and flavorful dish that’s perfect for a family dinner or a gathering with friends. The best part? It’s nut-free, making it a safe and tasty option for those with nut allergies.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium eggplants, sliced into 1/2 inch rounds
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Prep the Eggplant: Start by slicing the eggplants into 1/2 inch rounds. Place the slices in a colander and sprinkle them with salt. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to draw out the bitterness. After 30 minutes, rinse the eggplant slices under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.

  2. Set Up Your Breading Station: In three separate shallow dishes, prepare your breading station. In the first dish, place the all-purpose flour. In the second dish, beat the eggs. In the third dish, combine the breadcrumbs, garlic powder, dried oregano, dried basil, and a generous pinch of salt and pepper.

  3. Bread the Eggplant: Dip each eggplant slice into the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat the eggplant in the seasoned breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere. Place the breaded eggplant slices on a baking sheet.

  4. Fry the Eggplant: In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, fry the breaded eggplant slices in batches until they are golden brown on both sides. Transfer the fried eggplant to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  5. Assemble the Eggplant Parmesan: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a 9x13 inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce. Arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices on top of the sauce. Sprinkle a portion of the grated Parmesan and shredded mozzarella over the eggplant. Repeat the layers, finishing with a generous layer of marinara sauce and the remaining cheeses on top.

  6. Bake and Serve: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden. Let the eggplant parmesan rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

This nut-free eggplant parmesan is a wonderful dish that’s packed with flavor and satisfying textures. It’s a versatile recipe that can be served as a main course with a side of pasta or a fresh salad, or even as a delicious appetizer for a gathering. Whether you’re catering to nut allergies or simply looking for a new take on a beloved classic, this savory eggplant parmesan is sure to impress. Enjoy!
