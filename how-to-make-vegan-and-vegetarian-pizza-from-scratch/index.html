How to Make Vegan and Vegetarian Pizza from Scratch


Making your own pizza from scratch is not only rewarding but also a fun way to control what goes into your meal. Whether you are a vegan, vegetarian, or simply trying to eat more plant-based foods, creating a delicious homemade pizza that caters to your dietary preferences is easier than you think. In this post, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to make both vegan and vegetarian pizzas from scratch. We’ll cover the pizza dough, sauces, toppings, and even baking tips, ensuring that every bite is packed with flavor.

Ingredients for Pizza Dough

The foundation of any great pizza is the dough. To get a light, crispy crust, you’ll need simple ingredients that you can find in most kitchens. Here’s what you need:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour (or bread flour for a chewier texture)
  • 1 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (around 110°F or 43°C)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (extra virgin for a rich flavor)
  • 1 tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, mix the warm water with the sugar and sprinkle the active dry yeast over it. Stir gently and let it sit for 5–10 minutes, or until the mixture becomes frothy. This step ensures that the yeast is alive and will help your dough rise.

  2. Combine ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Mix with a spoon until the dough begins to come together.

  3. Knead the dough: Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 5–7 minutes. The dough should become smooth and elastic. If it’s too sticky, add a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much, as it could make the dough tough.

  4. Let the dough rise: Lightly oil a bowl and place the dough inside. Cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let it rise in a warm place for 1–2 hours or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Preheat the oven: While the dough is rising, preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up. A pizza stone can give your crust a professional, crispy texture.

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two portions if you’re making multiple pizzas. You can either roll the dough out with a rolling pin or stretch it by hand to your desired thickness.

Vegan Pizza Sauce Recipe

A good pizza sauce is crucial for adding depth and richness to your pizza. For a vegan-friendly sauce, the key is to use fresh tomatoes and herbs that bring out a natural sweetness and savory flavor. Here’s a simple recipe for vegan pizza sauce:

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes (or fresh tomatoes, blended)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste (optional for a thicker, richer sauce)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the garlic: In a medium saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.

  2. Add the tomatoes and seasonings: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, onion powder, and tomato paste. Bring the sauce to a simmer, and cook for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Season: Taste the sauce and season with salt and pepper to your liking. If you prefer a sweeter sauce, add a small pinch of sugar. For a spicier kick, consider adding red pepper flakes.

Once the sauce has thickened slightly, remove from heat and set it aside. The sauce is now ready to spread on your pizza dough.

Vegetarian Pizza Toppings

When it comes to vegetarian pizza, you have an abundance of tasty options to choose from. Here are some of the best vegetarian pizza toppings that will not only enhance the flavor but also create a colorful and vibrant pizza.

Vegetables:

  • Bell peppers: Red, yellow, and green bell peppers add a sweet crunch.
  • Mushrooms: Cremini, portobello, and button mushrooms bring an earthy, savory flavor.
  • Red onions: Sweet and tangy, they add a burst of color and taste.
  • Spinach: Fresh spinach leaves provide a mild, slightly peppery flavor that pairs well with other veggies.
  • Olives: Black or green olives are a great choice for adding briny saltiness.
  • Tomatoes: Fresh, sliced tomatoes enhance the pizza’s juiciness and add a bit of tang.
  • Zucchini: Thinly sliced zucchini adds a fresh and light touch.
  • Artichokes: These are an excellent choice for a Mediterranean flair, offering a unique, slightly tangy taste.

Cheese Alternatives:

For a vegan pizza, you’ll need a dairy-free cheese alternative. There are many great options available:

  • Vegan mozzarella: Most vegan mozzarella cheeses melt well and provide a creamy texture.
  • Cashew cheese: Cashew-based cheeses are creamy and can add a tangy flavor.
  • Vegan parmesan: A sprinkle of vegan parmesan will add some nutty sharpness.

For vegetarian pizzas, you can use traditional mozzarella or a combination of mozzarella and goat cheese for an extra layer of richness.

Vegan Pizza Toppings

Vegan pizzas are all about creativity! You can still enjoy a delicious pizza without any animal products. Here’s a list of tasty and wholesome toppings that can transform your pizza into a vegan masterpiece:

  • Vegan sausage: Many plant-based brands offer vegan sausage that mimics the texture and flavor of traditional sausage.
  • Vegan bacon: If you’re craving something smoky and savory, look for plant-based bacon or tempeh bacon.
  • Fresh herbs: Basil, arugula, and thyme are great herbs to add to your pizza once it’s out of the oven for a burst of freshness.
  • Vegan pesto: Spread a thin layer of vegan pesto as your base for a rich, herby flavor.
  • Sun-dried tomatoes: These add a tangy, slightly sweet flavor that pairs wonderfully with vegan mozzarella.

Assembling the Pizza

Once you’ve prepared your dough, sauce, and toppings, it’s time to assemble your pizza. Here’s how:

  1. Roll or stretch the dough: On a floured surface, roll out your dough to your desired thickness. If you prefer a thin crust, roll it out thinly. If you like a thicker, chewy crust, leave the dough slightly thicker. Transfer the dough onto a parchment paper-lined baking sheet or pizza peel if you’re using a pizza stone.

  2. Spread the sauce: Using a spoon, spread a generous layer of pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.

  3. Add toppings: Start layering your toppings. For vegan pizzas, make sure to use dairy-free cheese and plant-based options like vegan sausage, olives, and vegetables. For vegetarian pizzas, you can use traditional cheese, but feel free to include a wide range of vegetables and herbs.

  4. Bake the pizza: Place your pizza in the preheated oven. If you’re using a pizza stone, transfer the pizza onto the stone carefully. Bake for about 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese has melted. If you’re using a baking sheet, it may take slightly longer. Keep an eye on the pizza, as ovens vary.

Tips for Perfect Pizza

  • Preheat your oven: Make sure your oven is fully preheated before you bake your pizza. This ensures that your pizza cooks evenly and the crust gets crispy.
  • Use a pizza stone: If you have one, a pizza stone is ideal for achieving a crispy crust. It retains and evenly distributes heat, mimicking the conditions of a brick oven.
  • Don’t overload with toppings: Too many toppings can weigh down the dough and make the pizza soggy. Keep it simple and let the flavors shine.
  • Let the pizza cool: Once your pizza is baked, let it cool for a few minutes before slicing. This helps the cheese set and prevents the toppings from sliding off.

Conclusion

Making vegan and vegetarian pizzas from scratch is a fun, creative, and delicious way to enjoy pizza while sticking to your dietary preferences. With a simple dough recipe, flavorful vegan and vegetarian toppings, and a few tips for perfecting your pizza, you’ll be able to create a pizza that rivals your favorite pizzeria. Whether you’re hosting a pizza night or simply craving a homemade meal, this pizza recipe will surely impress your friends and family. Enjoy the process, experiment with different toppings, and most importantly—have fun making your perfect plant-based pizza!
