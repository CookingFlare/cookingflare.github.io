10 Christmas Side Dishes You Need to Try


The holiday season brings with it a flurry of celebrations, warmth, and of course, hearty feasts. While the centerpiece of a Christmas dinner often steals the spotlight—think succulent roast turkey, glazed ham, or prime rib—side dishes are where creativity shines and flavors meld to perfection. This year, why not elevate your Christmas table with a collection of side dishes that complement every main course, tantalize taste buds, and invite guests to eat seconds (or thirds)?

Whether you appreciate savory, sweet, or a bit of both, this lineup of ten Christmas side dishes offers a variety of textures, colors, and festive flavors. These recipes blend traditional classics with inventive twists, ensuring your holiday spread feels both comforting and novel.

1. Maple-Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Pecans

Brussels sprouts often earn a bad rap, but roasting them with a hint of sweetness and crunch changes the game entirely. Toss halved Brussels sprouts with olive oil, a touch of maple syrup, salt, and pepper, then roast until crispy on the edges. Meanwhile, fry crispy bacon and toast pecans in a dry skillet. Once the sprouts are golden, toss everything together for a side dish bursting with smoky, sweet, and nutty notes.

Why this works: The caramelized edges and the umami from bacon balance the natural bitterness of Brussels sprouts. Pecans add a delightful crunch that makes every bite exciting.

2. Creamy Garlic Mashed Potatoes with Chives

Mashed potatoes are a holiday staple, but you can make yours stand out by infusing them with roasted garlic and finishing them with a generous handful of fresh chives. Use Yukon Gold potatoes for their buttery texture, boil until tender, and mash with warm cream, butter, and roasted garlic cloves. The result is a luscious, velvety side dish fragrant with garlic and dotted with vibrant green chives.

Why this works: Roasted garlic mellows the harshness of raw garlic, adding depth without overpowering. Fresh chives bring a mild onion flavor and color contrast.

3. Cranberry Orange Relish with Pomegranate Seeds

Instead of the traditional canned cranberry sauce, whip up a zesty homemade relish. Combine fresh cranberries, orange zest, and juice, a splash of maple syrup, and a handful of pomegranate seeds for crunch. Pulse briefly in a food processor or chop finely—this relish offers a bright pop of flavor that cuts through rich holiday meats.

Why this works: The tartness of cranberries and pomegranate, combined with sweet orange notes, refreshes the palate and visually enlivens any plate.

4. Herb-Roasted Root Vegetables with Balsamic Glaze

Winter offers a bounty of root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and beets, perfect for roasting. Cube or chunk them generously, toss with olive oil, salt, pepper, and fresh herbs such as thyme and rosemary. Roast until tender and caramelized, then drizzle with a tangy balsamic reduction to add brightness and a glossy finish.

Why this works: Slow roasting brings out natural sweetness, while herbs and balsamic provide aromatic complexity and acidity.

5. Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel

Sweet potatoes lend themselves to casual coziness, especially in casserole form. Mash cooked sweet potatoes with brown sugar, cinnamon, a bit of butter, and vanilla extract. Top with a crumbly streusel made from chopped pecans, brown sugar, flour, and butter, then bake until bubbly and golden.

Why this works: The creamy sweet potatoes paired with the crunchy, sweet topping create a satisfying textural contrast beloved by many.

6. Sauteed Green Beans with Shallots and Almonds

Looking for something light, crisp, and elegant? Green beans quickly sauteed in butter with caramelized shallots and topped with toasted slivered almonds offer a refined and refreshing side dish. Finish with a sprinkle of flaky sea salt and a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness.

Why this works: The crispness and clean flavor of green beans complement the savory shallots and the nutty crunch of almonds, creating well-rounded mouthfeel and taste.

7. Wild Rice Pilaf with Mushrooms and Cranberries

Wild rice adds a chewy, nutty dimension that’s one step above regular steamed rice. For a festive pilaf, cook wild rice with vegetable or chicken broth, then stir in sautéed wild mushrooms, toasted walnuts, sweet dried cranberries, and finely chopped parsley. This dish is both hearty and vibrant, pairing well with many meat choices.

Why this works: Mushrooms provide earthiness, the cranberries add sweet tartness, and walnuts bring richness and crunch, making each forkful a festive celebration.

8. Parmesan and Herb Stuffed Mushrooms

Take bite-sized mushrooms and pop off their stems. Make a filling by mixing cream cheese, shredded Parmesan, minced garlic, chopped fresh herbs (parsley, thyme), and a pinch of black pepper. Fill mushroom caps generously, then bake until bubbly and golden brown on top.

Why this works: These savory morsels deliver an umami punch and creamy texture, perfect as a side or an appetizer that can steal the show.

9. Spiced Carrot and Ginger Soup Shots

Inject some warmth early in the meal with velvety carrot and ginger soup served in small glasses as shots or mini bowls. Simmer diced carrots with fresh ginger, onion, and garlic, then blend with vegetable broth until smooth. Finish with a touch of cream and garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley.

Why this works: The natural sweetness of carrot pairs beautifully with spicy ginger, preparing the palate for heavier courses while adding a splash of color.

10. Pecan and Cranberry Couscous Salad

For a cold salad option with a holiday twist, fluff couscous with vegetable or chicken broth, then toss with toasted pecans, dried cranberries, chopped green onions, and fresh herbs like mint and parsley. Dress with a citrus vinaigrette made from orange juice, olive oil, honey, and Dijon mustard.

Why this works: This salad balances wholesome grains with tart and nutty accents and a vibrant, tangy dressing, making a refreshing counterpart on a plate full of roasted or rich dishes.

Bringing It All Together: Perfect Pairings and Tips

Each of these side dishes has its personality, designed to either complement or contrast main courses traditionally served at Christmas. For instance, the creamy garlic mashed potatoes and herb-roasted root vegetables pair beautifully with roasted turkey or ham, while the wild rice pilaf and stuffed mushrooms match well with beef or lamb.

Tips for Success This Christmas:

  • Plan Ahead: Several dishes like mashed potatoes, casseroles, and relishes can be prepared or partially prepped a day or two in advance, saving precious time on the big day.
  • Balance Flavors: Aim to include sweet, savory, and acidic elements across your side dishes to prevent the meal from feeling too heavy or monotonous.
  • Vary Textures: Contrast creamy, crunchy, roasted, and fresh to keep every bite interesting.
  • Presentation Matters: Use colorful ingredients like cranberries, herbs, pomegranate seeds, and vibrant vegetables to make your table visually appealing.

Final Thoughts

Christmas side dishes are far more than mere accompaniments to the main event. They add hearty, sweet, savory, and tart layers to your holiday feast, making every meal memorable and inviting. Whether you embrace the comforting familiarity of mashed potatoes or venture into new territory with a wild rice pilaf or carrot ginger soup shots, these ten recipes will have you looking forward to the side dishes nearly as much as the turkey or ham.

Consider mixing and matching from this list to create a versatile, balanced, and festive menu that will impress friends and family alike. This holiday season, let your side dishes tell their own delicious story wrapped in the warmth and joy that Christmas brings.
