Healthy and Delicious Quinoa Bread: Gluten-Free Option


Are you looking for a healthy and delicious bread recipe that is also gluten-free? Look no further! This quinoa bread recipe is packed with nutrients and flavor, making it the perfect choice for those with dietary restrictions or anyone who wants to incorporate more nutritious ingredients into their diet.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup quinoa flour
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup tapioca flour
  • 1/4 cup ground flaxseed
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a loaf pan with olive oil or line it with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, quinoa flour, almond flour, tapioca flour, ground flaxseed, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well until all the dry ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, olive oil, honey, almond milk, and apple cider vinegar. Make sure the wet ingredients are well combined.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until a thick batter forms. Be careful not to overmix.

  5. Transfer the batter into the greased loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.

  6. Bake the bread in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  7. Once baked, remove the bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have quinoa flour, you can make your own by grinding quinoa seeds in a blender or food processor until they reach a fine flour-like consistency.

  • Feel free to add your favorite mix-ins to the batter, such as chopped nuts, dried fruits, or seeds. Be creative and experiment with different flavors!

  • To make the bread vegan, you can substitute the eggs with flax eggs (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons of water for each egg) and use maple syrup instead of honey.

  • This quinoa bread is delicious on its own, but it also pairs well with various spreads and toppings. Try it with avocado, nut butter, or your favorite jam for a tasty and nutritious snack.

Why Quinoa Bread?

Quinoa is a nutritious grain that is naturally gluten-free and packed with essential nutrients. It is a great source of protein, fiber, and various vitamins and minerals. By using quinoa flour and cooked quinoa in this bread recipe, you are adding an extra dose of nutrition to your diet.

Moreover, quinoa flour provides a unique flavor and texture to the bread, making it moist and slightly nutty. It also helps to bind the ingredients together, resulting in a bread that holds its shape well.

Whether you are following a gluten-free diet or simply looking for a healthier alternative to traditional bread, this quinoa bread is sure to satisfy your cravings while nourishing your body.

So why not give this healthy and delicious quinoa bread recipe a try? Your taste buds and body will thank you! Enjoy!
