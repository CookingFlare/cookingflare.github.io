How to Make Authentic Butter Chicken Curry at Home


If you’re a fan of Indian cuisine, you’ve likely indulged in the rich, creamy flavors of butter chicken curry. This iconic dish, also known as “murgh makhani,” hails from the streets of Delhi and has captured the hearts and taste buds of food enthusiasts worldwide. While it may seem daunting to recreate the magic of this dish at home, I’m here to guide you through the process of making an authentic butter chicken curry that will rival any restaurant version.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 1.5 pounds (700g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 can (14 oz) tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces with the yogurt, ginger garlic paste, ground cumin, garam masala, ground turmeric, chili powder, ground coriander, and salt. Mix well, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight for the best flavor.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the marinated chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred around the edges. Set aside.

  3. Prepare the Sauce: In a large skillet, melt the butter and vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for another 2 minutes. Add the ground turmeric, cumin, coriander, chili powder, and garam masala. Cook the spices for a minute until fragrant.

  4. Simmer the Sauce: Pour in the tomato puree and simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld together and the sauce thickens. Stir in the heavy cream, sugar, and salt. Let the sauce simmer for an additional 10 minutes.

  5. Finish the Dish: Add the cooked chicken to the sauce, stirring gently to coat the chicken pieces with the rich, creamy sauce. Allow the butter chicken curry to simmer for 10 minutes, ensuring the flavors meld together beautifully.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Garnish the butter chicken curry with fresh cilantro and serve it hot, accompanied by steamed basmati rice or warm naan bread. The velvety texture and aromatic flavors of this dish are sure to captivate your senses and transport you to the vibrant streets of India.

Conclusion

With this recipe, you can bring the tantalizing flavors of butter chicken curry from the bustling kitchens of India straight to your own dining table. The combination of aromatic spices, creamy tomato sauce, and tender chicken creates a dish that is truly a celebration of flavor. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of something extraordinary, this authentic butter chicken curry is a culinary masterpiece that will leave a lasting impression on anyone who has the pleasure of savoring it. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that will elevate your home cooking to new, delicious heights!
