Delicious Shawarma Recipe with Chicken and Lamb


Shawarma is a beloved dish enjoyed by millions around the world. Its rich flavors, tender meat, and savory spices make it an iconic street food that has gained popularity in various cultures. Traditionally, shawarma is made by stacking marinated meat (usually lamb, chicken, or beef) on a vertical rotisserie, where it is slow-cooked to perfection. However, making shawarma at home can be just as rewarding, and you don’t need a vertical rotisserie to enjoy this delicious dish.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through preparing a mouth-watering chicken and lamb shawarma with homemade spices, juicy meat, and all the toppings that will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of the Middle East. Get ready to enjoy an unforgettable shawarma experience right in the comfort of your kitchen!

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 2 large chicken breasts or thighs (boneless and skinless)
  • 4 tablespoons of plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of ground paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of ground allspice
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Lamb Marinade:

  • 1 lb (450g) lamb shoulder or leg, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 tablespoons of plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon of ground paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of ground allspice
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Shawarma Toppings and Sauces:

  • 4 pita or flatbreads
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Tomato, thinly sliced
  • Cucumber, thinly sliced
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Pickled vegetables (optional)
  • Tahini sauce or garlic sauce (for drizzling)

Instructions

1. Marinate the Chicken and Lamb

The key to an amazing shawarma is the marinade. Start by marinating both the chicken and lamb separately so each meat can soak in its own blend of spices and flavors.

For the Chicken Marinade:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, olive oil, minced garlic, cumin, paprika, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, allspice, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Stir well to create a smooth marinade.
  2. Cut the chicken breasts or thighs into bite-sized pieces, ensuring they will cook evenly.
  3. Add the chicken to the marinade, tossing to coat all the pieces thoroughly. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for the best flavor infusion.

For the Lamb Marinade:

  1. In another bowl, mix together the yogurt, olive oil, minced garlic, cumin, paprika, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, allspice, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Cut the lamb into bite-sized chunks, trimming any excess fat.
  3. Add the lamb to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for optimal flavor development.

2. Prepare the Grill or Pan

Shawarma is traditionally cooked on a vertical rotisserie, but don’t worry if you don’t have one at home. You can achieve similar results using a grill, grill pan, or skillet.

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. If using a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil to prevent the meat from sticking.
  2. Ensure the cooking surface is hot before adding the marinated meat. This will help lock in the flavors and create that signature shawarma sear.

3. Cook the Chicken and Lamb

Cooking the Chicken:

  1. Place the marinated chicken pieces onto the grill or in the skillet in a single layer. Cook for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is fully cooked through and has a nice golden-brown exterior.
  2. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the heat and set it aside to rest for a few minutes. This will help the juices redistribute, keeping the meat moist.

Cooking the Lamb:

  1. Similarly, place the marinated lamb pieces on the grill or in the skillet. Cook for 5-8 minutes, depending on the size of the pieces, until they are tender and slightly charred on the outside. Lamb cooks quickly, so keep an eye on it to avoid overcooking.
  2. Let the lamb rest for a few minutes before serving.

4. Assemble the Shawarma

Now that the meats are cooked, it’s time to assemble your shawarma!

  1. Warm the pita or flatbreads by placing them on the grill for a minute or two, or wrap them in foil and heat them in the oven.
  2. Spread a generous amount of tahini sauce or garlic sauce on the center of each pita. If you prefer a tangy alternative, you can use hummus as a spread.
  3. Begin layering the chicken and lamb on top of the sauce. You can alternate between the two meats for an extra burst of flavor.
  4. Add a handful of fresh parsley for a burst of color and freshness. Top with thin slices of tomato, cucumber, and red onion. Feel free to add pickled vegetables if you like an extra tangy kick.
  5. Drizzle a little more tahini or garlic sauce over the top to bring all the flavors together.

5. Serve and Enjoy

Once your shawarma is assembled, fold the pita or flatbread around the filling, creating a pocket or wrap. You can serve the shawarma as a handheld street-food snack, or you can plate it with a side of fries, salad, or couscous for a more substantial meal.

Tips for the Perfect Shawarma

  • Marinate the Meat Overnight: While marinating for an hour will still give you delicious results, marinating the chicken and lamb overnight allows the spices to infuse deeper into the meat, making it even more flavorful.
  • Use Bone-In Cuts for Extra Flavor: If you have the option, try using bone-in chicken thighs or lamb shoulder. The bone adds extra flavor during the cooking process, resulting in even juicier meat.
  • Customize Your Spices: Shawarma spices can be adjusted based on your preferences. If you like more heat, you can add chili powder or cayenne pepper to the marinade. Alternatively, add extra cinnamon or allspice for a more aromatic taste.
  • Grill the Vegetables: If you like a smoky flavor, try grilling your tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers for an added depth of taste before assembling your shawarma.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

While the chicken and lamb combination is a classic choice, you can experiment with other meats or even make a vegetarian version.

Vegetarian Shawarma:

  • Use grilled mushrooms, eggplant, or cauliflower as the base for a meatless shawarma. These vegetables soak up the spices beautifully and make for a satisfying plant-based alternative.
  • Try adding some falafel for extra crunch and flavor.

Shawarma Platter:

  • If you’re serving a crowd or prefer to eat shawarma as a more formal meal, serve the chicken and lamb shawarma on a platter with sides like tabbouleh, fattoush, hummus, baba ganoush, and warm pita bread. This way, everyone can build their own shawarma with a variety of toppings.

Shawarma Salad:

  • For a healthier twist, you can skip the pita and serve the shawarma filling over a bed of mixed greens or romaine lettuce. Add your favorite vegetables, such as cucumbers, tomatoes, and red onions, and drizzle with a simple dressing made of olive oil and lemon juice.

Final Thoughts

Making shawarma at home is a fun and rewarding experience that brings the authentic taste of the Middle East right to your kitchen. With the combination of tender marinated chicken and lamb, aromatic spices, and fresh toppings, you’re sure to create a dish that’s as delicious as it is satisfying.

Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or hosting a gathering, this shawarma recipe is guaranteed to be a hit. By following these steps and tips, you’ll master the art of shawarma in no time. Enjoy every bite of this flavorful and juicy dish, and let it become a staple in your weekly meal rotation.
