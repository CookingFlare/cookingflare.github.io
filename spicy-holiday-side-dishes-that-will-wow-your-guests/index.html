Spicy Holiday Side Dishes That Will Wow Your Guests


As the holiday season approaches, hosts and hostesses are eager to impress their guests with delicious and memorable side dishes. This year, why not try something new and exciting? Spicy side dishes are a great way to add some heat to your holiday spread and leave a lasting impression on your loved ones. In this article, we'll explore some mouth-watering and easy-to-make spicy side dishes that are sure to wow your guests. Spicy Sweet Potato Casserole with Crispy Pepitas This twist on the classic sweet potato casserole is a game-changer. The addition of crispy pepitas and a hint of cayenne pepper adds a delightful crunch and a burst of heat. To make this dish, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, combine 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced, 1/2 cup of brown sugar, 1/4 cup of granulated sugar, 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon, and a pinch of cayenne pepper. In a separate bowl, mix together 1/2 cup of heavy cream, 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, and 2 large eggs. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until well combined. Transfer the mixture to a 9x13-inch baking dish and top with 1 cup of crispy pepitas. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until the sweet potatoes are tender and the top is golden brown. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Harissa and Pine Nuts This recipe is a symphony of flavors and textures that will leave your guests wanting more. The spicy kick from the harissa pairs perfectly with the nutty flavor of the pine nuts and the crunch of the Brussels sprouts. To make this dish, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss 1 pound of Brussels sprouts with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 1 tablespoon of harissa, and 1 tablespoon of pine nuts. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until the Brussels sprouts are tender and caramelized. Spicy Green Bean Almondine This side dish is a masterclass in balance and contrast. The spicy kick from the red pepper flakes pairs perfectly with the nutty flavor of the almonds and the sweetness of the green beans. To make this dish, blanch 1 pound of green beans in boiling water for 3-5 minutes. In a separate pan, sauté 1/4 cup of sliced almonds and 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter until fragrant. Add 1 tablespoon of red pepper flakes and cook for 1-2 minutes. Toss the green beans with the almond mixture and serve hot. Jalapeño Popper Stuffed Mushrooms These bite-sized appetizers are sure to be a hit at any holiday gathering. The creamy filling and spicy kick from the jalapeños will leave your guests begging for more. To make this dish, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a bowl, mix together 1 cup of cream cheese, 1/2 cup of shredded cheddar cheese, 1/4 cup of chopped cilantro, and 1 diced jalapeño pepper. Stuff each mushroom cap with the filling and bake for 12-15 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Spicy Maple-Glazed Carrots These carrots are a masterclass in sweet and spicy flavors. The hint of maple syrup pairs perfectly with the kick of the sriracha and the crunch of the sesame seeds. To make this dish, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss 1 pound of carrots with 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 1 tablespoon of maple syrup, and 1 tablespoon of sriracha. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes or until the carrots are tender and caramelized. Pecan-Crusted Sweet Potato Wedges with Chipotle Aioli This side dish is a game-changer. The spicy kick from the chipotle peppers in adobo sauce pairs perfectly with the crunch of the pecans and the sweetness of the sweet potatoes. To make this dish, preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a bowl, mix together 1 cup of pecans, 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and 1/4 cup of all-purpose flour. Toss 2 large sweet potatoes, cut into wedges, with the pecan mixture and bake for 20-25 minutes or until crispy. Meanwhile, mix together 1/2 cup of mayonnaise, 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, and 1 tablespoon of lime juice. Serve the sweet potato wedges with the chipotle aioli for dipping. Spicy Holiday Side Dishes That Will Wow Your Guests Spicy side dishes are the perfect way to add some excitement to your holiday spread. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a novice cook, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and leave them wanting more. With their unique flavor combinations and spice levels, these dishes are sure to be a hit at any holiday gathering. So, why not try something new and exciting this year? Cookbook these recipes and get ready to impress your friends and family with your culinary skills! Tips and Variations

To add an extra kick to your dishes, experiment with different types of hot sauce or hot peppers. For a milder flavor, reduce or omit the amount of hot peppers or hot sauce. To make these dishes vegan or gluten-free, substitute traditional cheese with dairy-free alternatives or gluten-free flours. To add some crunch to your dishes, top with toasted nuts or seeds.

Whether you're a seasoned chef or a novice cook, these recipes are easy to make and packed with flavor. So, why not try something new and exciting this year? Cookbook these recipes and get ready to wow your guests with your culinary skills!
