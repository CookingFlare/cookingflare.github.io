Crust Goals: Crafting the Perfect Gluten-Free, Vegan, or Keto Pizza Crust for Dietary Accommodations


Are you tired of feeling excluded from the pizza party because of dietary restrictions? Fear not! In this blog post, we will explore the art of crafting the perfect gluten-free, vegan, or keto pizza crust. Each of these cravings holds its unique characteristics, challenges, and benefits. But with a little innovation and the right ingredients, creating these amazing crusts is entirely achievable for even the most novice of home bakers. So, roll up your sleeves, put on your apron, and let’s get started!

Exploring Gluten-Free Pizza Crust

If you’re following a gluten-free diet for health or dietary reasons, fret not – you’re in for a delightful surprise! Gluten-free pizza crusts can be just as delicious and satisfying as their gluten-filled counterparts. To craft the perfect gluten-free pizza crust, we urge you to consider the following recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 2 c. gluten-free flour blend (ensure it contains xanthan gum if included in your blend)
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1/2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1 tbsp. honey
  • 1 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 c. warm water
  • 1 tsp. active dry yeast
  • 1 egg (or a flaxseed egg for vegan version – 1 tbsp. ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp. water)

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to 425°F (220°C) and gathering all the ingredients.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend, salt, and baking powder.
  3. Add honey and olive oil to the dry ingredients and slowly mix until well incorporated.
  4. Dissolve the active dry yeast in the warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes.
  5. Pour the yeast mixture into the large mixing bowl and mix well until a smooth dough forms.
  6. Crack the egg (or add the flaxseed egg if making the vegan version) and mix in until fully combined.
  7. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and gently spread the dough over the sheet, leaving it slightly thicker around the edges.
  8. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and firm.
  9. Allow the crust to cool slightly before adding your toppings and baking for another 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese (if using) is melted and bubbly.

Focusing on Vegan Pizza Crust

For those living a vegan lifestyle or have dairy allergies, fear not - a delicious vegan pizza crust is entirely achievable with plant-based alternatives. Here’s a quick recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 c. whole wheat flour
  • 1 c. almond flour
  • 1/2 c. tapioca flour
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 1 tbsp. sugar
  • 1 tbsp. baking powder
  • 1/2 c. warm water
  • 1/4 c. olive oil
  • 1 tbsp. apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. As before, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Combine the whole wheat flour, almond flour, tapioca flour, salt, sugar, and baking powder in a large mixing bowl.
  3. Mix in the warm water, olive oil, and apple cider vinegar until the dough becomes smooth.
  4. Transfer the dough to a well-floured surface and knead gently for about 5 minutes.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball and allow it to rest for 10 minutes.
  6. Roll out the dough and either transfer to a greased pizza plate or line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 10 minutes, then add your toppings and bake for another 15-20 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and the toppings are cooked.

Discovering Keto Pizza Crust

A keto-friendly lifestyle can sometimes seem restricting; however, fear not! You can indulge in a delicious pizza crust without throwing off your macros. Here’s a simple recipe to help you craft the perfect keto-friendly crust:

Ingredients:

  • 3 c. almond flour
  • 1/4 c. psyllium husk powder
  • 1 tsp. baking soda
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 6 eggs
  • 1/4 c. olive oil
  • 1 tsp. dried herbs (basil or oregano)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine the almond flour, psyllium husk powder, baking soda, and salt in a mixing bowl.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, olive oil, and dried herbs until combined.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix well to form a smooth dough.
  5. Grease your hands and scoop the dough onto the prepared baking sheet, flattening it into a circular shape.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the crust is brown and crispy.
  7. Allow the crust to cool before adding your toppings and baking for another 10-15 minutes or until the cheese, if using, is melted and bubbly.

Summing it Up

Dietary accommodations don’t have to mean denying yourself the delicious pleasure of a piping-hot pizza straight from the oven. With a bit of creativity and a willingness to experiment with different ingredients, crafting the perfect gluten-free, vegan, or keto pizza crust has never been more enjoyable. Happy baking!
