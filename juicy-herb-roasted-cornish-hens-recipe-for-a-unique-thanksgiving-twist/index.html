Juicy Herb-Roasted Cornish Hens Recipe for a Unique Thanksgiving Twist


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with loved ones, express gratitude, and indulge in a delicious feast. While turkey is the traditional centerpiece of the Thanksgiving table, why not shake things up this year with a unique twist? Introducing the Juicy Herb-Roasted Cornish Hens, a mouthwatering alternative that is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving for more.

Ingredients

  • 4 Cornish hens
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 1 cup chicken broth

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place a roasting rack inside a large roasting pan and set aside.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, minced garlic, chopped thyme, rosemary, and sage. Mix well until all the ingredients are incorporated.

  3. Rinse the Cornish hens under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Season the hens generously with salt and pepper, both inside and out.

  4. Gently loosen the skin of each hen by carefully sliding your fingers between the skin and the meat. Be careful not to tear the skin.

  5. Divide the herb butter mixture equally among the hens, spreading it evenly under the loosened skin. This will help infuse the meat with flavor and keep it moist during roasting.

  6. Stuff each hen cavity with a few slices of lemon, adding a burst of citrusy freshness to the dish.

  7. Place the hens on the prepared roasting rack, breast side up. Pour the chicken broth into the bottom of the roasting pan, ensuring it covers the bottom but doesn’t touch the hens.

  8. Roast the hens in the preheated oven for about 60-75 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) when measured with a meat thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the thigh, without touching the bone.

  9. Baste the hens with the pan juices every 20-30 minutes to ensure they stay moist and flavorful.

  10. Once cooked, remove the hens from the oven and let them rest for 10 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in tender and juicy meat.

  11. Serve the Juicy Herb-Roasted Cornish Hens as the star of your Thanksgiving table. Garnish with fresh herbs and lemon slices for an elegant touch.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider marinating the hens overnight in the herb butter mixture. This will infuse the meat with even more deliciousness.
  • If you prefer a crispy skin, increase the oven temperature to 425°F (220°C) during the last 10-15 minutes of roasting.
  • Feel free to customize the herb blend to suit your taste. Experiment with other fresh herbs like tarragon or oregano for a unique twist.
  • Pair the Cornish hens with your favorite Thanksgiving side dishes, such as creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or cranberry sauce.

So, this Thanksgiving, surprise your loved ones with the Juicy Herb-Roasted Cornish Hens. With its tender and flavorful meat, this unique twist on the traditional Thanksgiving feast is guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser. Get ready to create new traditions and make lasting memories around the dinner table. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
