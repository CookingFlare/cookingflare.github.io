Make-ahead Summer Picnic Recipes for a Relaxing Day Outdoors


As the days grow longer and the sun shines brighter, there’s no better way to enjoy the season than by packing a delicious picnic and spending the day outdoors. Whether you’re heading to the beach, a park, or your own backyard, a well-packed picnic can make all the difference in ensuring you have a fun, stress-free experience. However, there’s one key to a successful picnic: preparation. The best summer picnic recipes are the ones you can make ahead of time, so you’re not scrambling at the last minute. In this guide, we’ve curated a selection of make-ahead summer picnic recipes that are not only easy to prepare but also perfect for a relaxing day outdoors.

1. Classic Potato Salad

No summer picnic is complete without a creamy and flavorful potato salad. This classic dish is simple to prepare, and you can make it the day before your outing. The key to an excellent potato salad is using waxy potatoes, as they hold their shape well and don’t turn mushy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds small waxy potatoes (such as Yukon Gold)
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped celery
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the potatoes thoroughly and place them in a large pot. Fill the pot with cold water and add a pinch of salt. Bring the water to a boil and cook the potatoes until tender (about 15-20 minutes).
  2. Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly before cutting them into bite-sized cubes.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and olive oil until smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Add the cooled potatoes, celery, red onion, and chopped eggs to the bowl. Gently toss everything together until evenly coated.
  5. Cover the potato salad and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

2. Mediterranean Quinoa Salad

This colorful, nutritious salad is a great make-ahead option because quinoa holds up well when refrigerated. Packed with fresh vegetables and a tangy lemon dressing, this salad will add a Mediterranean touch to your picnic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water. In a medium saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the quinoa and reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the water has been absorbed.
  2. Remove the quinoa from the heat and fluff it with a fork. Let it cool completely.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooled quinoa, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, olives, feta cheese, and parsley.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  6. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld.

3. Caprese Skewers

Caprese skewers are a fresh and elegant addition to any picnic. These bite-sized skewers combine juicy tomatoes, creamy mozzarella, and fragrant basil, making them the perfect no-cook, make-ahead appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1 package fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini or ciliegine)
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Thread a cherry tomato, a mozzarella ball, and a basil leaf onto small skewers or toothpicks. Repeat until all ingredients are used.
  2. Arrange the skewers on a platter and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Drizzle with balsamic glaze just before serving.

4. Hummus and Veggie Wraps

For a light, healthy, and portable picnic lunch, hummus and veggie wraps are an excellent choice. You can easily prepare these the night before and store them in the fridge until youâ€™re ready to pack up and go.

Ingredients:

  • 4 whole wheat tortillas
  • 1 cup hummus (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup spinach or arugula
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Lay a tortilla flat on a clean surface. Spread a generous layer of hummus over the entire surface.
  2. Layer the veggies on top of the hummus: bell pepper, cucumber, carrot, avocado, and spinach.
  3. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Roll the tortilla tightly and slice into smaller pieces if desired.
  5. Wrap each roll tightly in plastic wrap and refrigerate until you’re ready to pack for your picnic.

5. Grilled Chicken Skewers with Lemon Herb Marinade

Grilled chicken skewers are a savory and satisfying option that you can prepare the night before your picnic. The marinade infuses the chicken with bright, fresh flavors, and the skewers are perfect for serving outdoors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the bowl and toss to coat evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers.
  4. Grill the chicken skewers for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is fully cooked and golden brown.
  5. Allow the skewers to cool slightly before packing them for your picnic.

6. Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

A refreshing fruit salad is the perfect way to end a picnic meal. This fruit salad features a mix of vibrant seasonal fruits, and the honey-lime dressing adds a zesty, sweet finish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1 cup watermelon, cubed
  • 1 cup pineapple, cubed
  • 1 orange, peeled and segmented
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Zest of 1 lime

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all of the fruit.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, lime juice, and lime zest.
  3. Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the fruit and gently toss to coat.
  4. Cover and refrigerate until you’re ready to serve.

7. Mini Pita Pockets with Tuna Salad

These mini pita pockets are the perfect combination of protein and convenience. Filled with a flavorful tuna salad, these bite-sized pockets make a satisfying picnic snack.

Ingredients:

  • 4 mini whole wheat pitas
  • 2 cans of tuna in water, drained
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon pickle relish
  • 1/4 cup celery, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the tuna, mayonnaise, mustard, relish, and celery. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Cut the mini pitas in half to create pockets.
  3. Spoon the tuna salad into each pita pocket and pack them for your picnic.

8. Lemonade Iced Tea

To wash it all down, a refreshing homemade lemonade iced tea is a perfect picnic drink. This easy-to-make beverage is both refreshing and thirst-quenching, with the tang of lemonade and the mellow taste of iced tea.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 black tea bags
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup honey or sugar (optional)
  • Lemon slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan. Remove from heat and steep the tea bags for 5-7 minutes.
  2. In a pitcher, combine the tea with the remaining 2 cups of cold water.
  3. Stir in the fresh lemon juice and honey (if desired) until the sweetener dissolves.
  4. Refrigerate the iced tea until chilled. Serve over ice and garnish with lemon slices.

Conclusion

With these make-ahead summer picnic recipes, you can spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying the outdoors. From refreshing salads to hearty wraps and savory skewers, each dish is designed to be prepared ahead of time, ensuring your picnic is easy to pack, easy to serve, and packed with flavor. Donâ€™t forget to prepare a few drinks to keep everyone refreshed and hydrated! These recipes not only make your summer outings more enjoyable but also take the stress out of planning, leaving you to savor the simple pleasures of a relaxed day in the sun. So grab your picnic basket, get cooking, and enjoy a memorable summer day outdoors.
