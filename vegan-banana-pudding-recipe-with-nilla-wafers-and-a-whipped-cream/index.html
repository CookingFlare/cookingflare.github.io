Vegan Banana Pudding Recipe with Nilla Wafers and a Whipped Cream


If you’re a fan of bananas and creamy desserts, then you’re in for a treat with this vegan banana pudding recipe. Not only is it incredibly delicious, but it’s also dairy-free, egg-free, and full of wholesome ingredients. This recipe puts a vegan twist on the classic banana pudding, incorporating the beloved Nilla wafers and a luscious coconut whipped cream. So, let’s dive into this delightful vegan dessert that will surely impress both vegans and non-vegans alike.

Ingredients

For the Pudding:

  • 3 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

For the Assembly:

  • Vegan Nilla wafers (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh banana slices
  • Fresh strawberries (optional)

For the Whipped Cream:

  • 2 cans of full-fat coconut milk, refrigerated overnight
  • 2-3 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pudding

  1. In a saucepan, whisk together the coconut sugar and cornstarch.
  2. Gradually whisk in the almond milk until smooth.
  3. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a gentle boil, stirring constantly.
  4. Once the mixture has thickened, remove it from the heat.
  5. Stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt.
  6. Let the pudding cool for a few minutes.

Step 2: Assemble the Pudding

  1. In a glass dish or individual serving glasses, layer the sliced bananas and Nilla wafers.
  2. Pour the prepared pudding over the bananas and wafers, ensuring an even layer.
  3. Add another layer of sliced bananas and Nilla wafers on top of the pudding.

Step 3: Make the Whipped Cream

  1. Open the refrigerated coconut milk cans and scoop out the solid coconut cream that has risen to the top.
  2. Place the cream in a large mixing bowl and beat it with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy.
  3. Add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, and continue to beat until well combined.

Step 4: Final Assembly

  1. Dollop the coconut whipped cream over the layered pudding.
  2. Garnish with additional Nilla wafers, fresh banana slices, and optional sliced strawberries.
  3. Chill the pudding in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the vegan banana pudding is thoroughly chilled, it’s ready to be served and enjoyed. The combination of the sweet, creamy pudding, the nostalgic crunch of Nilla wafers, and the airy coconut whipped cream creates a symphony of flavors and textures that will delight your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting dessert, this vegan banana pudding is a crowd-pleaser that’s sure to leave everyone asking for seconds.

Conclusion

This vegan banana pudding recipe with Nilla wafers and whipped cream is a testament to the fact that plant-based desserts can be just as indulgent and satisfying as their traditional counterparts. By using simple, natural ingredients, this recipe delivers a dessert that is not only delicious but also cruelty-free and environmentally friendly. So, whip up a batch of this delightful vegan banana pudding and savor every spoonful of this guilt-free pleasure.
