Baking with Yeast: No-Knead Bread Rolls that Wow


No-knead bread rolls are an excellent choice for beginner and experienced bakers alike. With little effort, you’ll have perfect, golden brown rolls that are soft and warm on the inside, ready to delight your taste buds. As a bonus, these rolls can be stored for days and enjoyed as needed. The time you invest in making these tempting treats will surely pay off!

To get started, gather your ingredients:

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 4 cups of warm water (around 120°F)
  • 2 tablespoons of active dry yeast

Before diving into the dough, preheat your oven to 450°F (232°C). Heating the oven at this temperature helps provide a hot surface that will result in crispier rolls.

Next, in a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and a bit of the sugar. The warm water is important because it allows the yeast to activate and begin eating away at the sugar, releasing carbon dioxide and other gases, giving the bread its characteristic texture.

Let the water and yeast mixture sit for about 5 minutes, until the yeast dissolves, and a frothy mixture forms. This foam acts as a sign that the yeast is active and ready to go. Afterward, add half of the sifted flour and give it a quick mix.

Allow the flour to sit for another 5 minutes to help the flour hydrate and the yeast to become more active. Meanwhile, mix the salt with the remaining flour. Gradually, add the remaining flour mixture to the yeast mixture, and combine until the dough forms.

With such a simple procedure, you can immediately start enjoying homemade dough for your no-knead bread rolls. If you’re intrigued by the potential of no-knead bread, consider exploring other flavors, such as adding Italian herbs, garlic, or rosemary.

If you’re in need of a visual guide, head to this video for a helpful demonstration.

Handy hint: If desired, you can even include additional ingredients now, such as sun-dried tomatoes or olives, to adjust the flavors and create your own signature rolls.

To create well-rounded rolls, scoop out the dough using a spoon or your hands, and shape them into small, round shapes. Sprinkle a little cornmeal on your countertop or baking sheets for an added texture, and then let them rest for a few minutes.

In less than 20 minutes, your rolls will have risen to their peak. Place them in the preheated oven and bake until they are golden brown, around 15–20 minutes. Finalizing the rolls with a little butter or a sprinkling of garlic powder will elevate their taste even more.

For best results, remove the rolls immediately after baking and serve them warm. Store the extra rolls in airtight containers or plastic bags at room temperature. You may even try re-crisping your rolls on a baking sheet at 350°F (175°C) for a few minutes prior to serving if you need to store and serve at a later time.

The smell of fresh baked bread rolls alone will have your family and friends coming over to feast. Essentially, these no-knead rolls offer the blissful opportunity to create scrumptious bread – simple yet incredibly rewarding. So fire up the oven, gather your ingredients, and roll up your sleeves.
