Thai Sweet Coconut Pudding Recipe


When it comes to desserts that embody the sweet, creamy richness of Southeast Asia, Thai sweet coconut pudding stands out as one truly unforgettable treat. This traditional Thai dessert perfectly balances the lusciousness of coconut milk with the delicate sweetness and subtle textures that make it genuinely special. Whether you’re a seasoned fan of Thai cuisine or a curious home cook eager to explore new flavors, this Thai sweet coconut pudding recipe invites you on a culinary journey to savor a comforting, melt-in-your-mouth experience.

What Is Thai Sweet Coconut Pudding?

Thai sweet coconut pudding, often known locally as khanom thuai, is a beloved dessert that combines creamy coconut milk with a lightly sweetened rice or tapioca base. The pudding is typically served in small containers or bowls, topped with a rich coconut cream layer and sometimes sprinkled with toasted sesame seeds or mung beans for added texture. Traditionally, it’s an integral part of Thai street food culture and festive occasions, cherished for its simplicity and depth of flavor.

This dessert is arguably one of the easiest ways to enjoy the authentic taste of Thai coconut, and its naturally gluten-free and dairy-free profile makes it appealing to a wide array of dietary preferences.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To bring this Thai coconut pudding to life, you need just a handful of staple ingredients, most of which you can find at an Asian grocery store or even well-stocked supermarkets. Here’s the list:

  • Coconut milk: This is the star of the show, providing the creamy, tropical essence. Use full-fat coconut milk for the best richness.
  • Sugar: Both white granulated sugar and palm sugar work well. Palm sugar adds a subtle caramel note characteristic of traditional Thai desserts.
  • Rice flour or tapioca flour: Acts as the thickening agent. Tapioca produces a slightly chewier texture, while rice flour yields a smoother pudding.
  • Water: To create the perfect consistency.
  • Salt: Just a pinch to balance the sweetness and enhance flavor.
  • Optional toppings: Toasted sesame seeds, mung beans, or a drizzle of extra thick coconut cream.

By sourcing quality coconut milk and balancing your sweetness, you craft a pudding that captures the heart of Thai culinary tradition.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Perfect Thai Sweet Coconut Pudding

Step 1: Prepare the Coconut Milk Mixture

Start by shaking your can of coconut milk well. Pour 1 cup of coconut milk into a saucepan and add 3 to 4 tablespoons of sugar (adjust according to your preference) along with a pinch of salt. Stir gently over medium heat, dissolving the sugar completely without bringing the mixture to a boil. This gentle heating helps meld the flavors together.

Step 2: Make the Flour Mixture

In a separate bowl, combine 1/4 cup rice flour or tapioca flour with 1 cup of water. Stir until smooth and lump-free. This mixture is your pudding base.

Step 3: Combine and Cook

Slowly pour the coconut milk mixture into the flour mixture, whisking continually to ensure no lumps form. Transfer everything back to the saucepan and cook over low heat. Stir constantly using a wooden spoon or silicone spatula, scraping the bottom and sides of the pan to prevent sticking.

The pudding mixture will gradually thicken. This process usually takes around 10-15 minutes. When the pudding becomes thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, it is ready.

Step 4: Pour into Serving Containers

Ladle the thickened pudding into small ceramic cups or dessert bowls. Allow them to cool to room temperature before chilling in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour. The pudding will set further and develop a beautiful, soft texture.

Step 5: Prepare the Coconut Cream Topping

While the pudding chills, prepare the topping. Use about 1/2 cup of coconut milk (preferably the thick cream from the top of the can) mixed with a pinch of salt and a teaspoon of sugar. Warm slightly to dissolve the sugar, then let cool.

Before serving, pour a thin layer of this coconut cream over each pudding cup. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds or mung beans if desired.

Pro Tips for Making the Best Thai Sweet Coconut Pudding

  • Flour choice impacts texture: Tapioca flour yields a slightly bouncy, chewy texture popular in some Thai regions, while rice flour provides a silky, smooth consistency favored in others. Experiment to find your ideal mouthfeel.
  • Control sweetness: Traditional recipes lean towards subtle sweetness rather than overpowering sugariness. Taste as you go to suit your palate.
  • Use quality coconut milk: Full-fat, creamy coconut milk with no additives enhances authenticity. Avoid watered-down or flavored versions.
  • Constant stirring: This keeps your pudding smooth and prevents lumps or scorching.
  • Presentation matters: Serving in small, elegant bowls or even banana leaf cups adds a touch of Thai street food charm.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

Thai sweet coconut pudding is versatile and lends itself well to fun variations:

  • Mango Coconut Pudding: Add fresh mango slices alongside or as a topping for a tropical twist.
  • Pandan Infusion: Incorporate pandan extract or pandan leaf-infused coconut milk for a floral aroma and bright green color.
  • Black Bean or Mung Bean: Layer the pudding with lightly sweetened black beans or mung beans for an extra texture boost.
  • Chilled vs. Warm: While commonly served chilled, some enjoy this dessert warm for a comforting treat in cooler weather.

For serving, enjoy your Thai pudding as a light dessert after a spicy Thai meal, or savor it as a sweet snack alongside tea or coffee.

Why This Recipe Is a Must-Try

Thai sweet coconut pudding is more than just a dessert — it’s a taste story of Thailand’s tropical bounty and culinary creativity. It showcases how simple, accessible ingredients can transform into a delicacy that delights every sense. Making this pudding at home bridges kitchen cultures, bringing the street food spirit and authentic flavors to your table.

Whether you’re looking to impress guests with an exotic yet comforting sweet, or simply to indulge in a luscious coconut treat, this recipe offers an easy, authentic approach to an iconic Thai dessert.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Pudding too runny: Continue cooking and stirring until it thickens, or add a little more flour dissolved in water.
  • Lumpy texture: Whisk the flour mixture thoroughly before combining and ensure constant stirring on heat.
  • Pudding too sweet: Adjust sugar amounts next time, or balance with an unsweetened coconut cream topping.
  • Coconut milk separating: Stir well before using, and avoid boiling coconut milk at high heat to prevent curdling.
