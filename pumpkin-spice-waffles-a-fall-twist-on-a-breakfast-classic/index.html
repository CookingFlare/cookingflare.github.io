Pumpkin Spice Waffles: A Fall Twist on a Breakfast Classic


There’s something magical about the arrival of fall—the crisp air, the vibrant colors, and, of course, the delicious flavors that come with the season. And what better way to celebrate autumn than with a warm and comforting breakfast? These Pumpkin Spice Waffles are the perfect fall twist on a breakfast classic, combining the rich flavors of pumpkin and warm spices into a fluffy and indulgent treat.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons pumpkin spice blend
  • 1 3/4 cups buttermilk
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your waffle iron according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and pumpkin spice blend until well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, pumpkin puree, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth and well incorporated.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold them together with a spatula until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense waffles.

  5. Lightly grease the hot waffle iron with cooking spray or a small amount of melted butter. Spoon the batter onto the iron, using about 1/2 cup of batter for each waffle. Close the lid and cook until the waffles are golden brown and crisp, according to your waffle iron’s instructions.

  6. Once cooked, carefully remove the waffles from the iron and transfer them to a wire rack to cool slightly. This will help them retain their crispiness.

  7. Serve the Pumpkin Spice Waffles warm, topped with your favorite fall-inspired toppings. Consider options such as whipped cream, maple syrup, caramel sauce, chopped pecans, or a dusting of powdered sugar. Get creative and experiment with different flavors to make this breakfast truly your own.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have buttermilk on hand, you can make a simple substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or white vinegar to 1 3/4 cups of milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using.

  • If you prefer a lighter and fluffier waffle, separate the egg yolks from the whites. Whisk the yolks into the wet ingredients and beat the egg whites separately until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the batter just before cooking.

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a handful of chocolate chips or chopped nuts to the batter before cooking.

  • These Pumpkin Spice Waffles also make for a great dessert. Serve them with a scoop of vanilla ice cream and drizzle with warm caramel sauce for an indulgent treat.

  • Leftover waffles can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat them in a toaster or oven before enjoying.

Conclusion

Pumpkin Spice Waffles are the ultimate fall breakfast indulgence. With their warm and comforting flavors, they are sure to become a favorite in your home. Whether you’re enjoying them on a cozy weekend morning or serving them as a special treat for guests, these waffles are a delicious way to embrace the flavors of the season. So grab your waffle iron, whip up a batch of these Pumpkin Spice Waffles, and savor the taste of fall. Happy cooking!
