Soft and Fluffy Hawaiian Rolls Recipe


Welcome to a mouth-watering journey as we explore the world of homemade Hawaiian rolls – soft, fluffy, and irresistible! The Hawaiian roll is a versatile bread that makes an ideal sandwich bread, dinner roll, or toast. These rolls are perfect for family dinners, parties, or simply enjoying as a snack.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 1/3 cups warm water
  • 4 eggs, room temperature

Equipment:

  • Large bowl
  • Mixer
  • Rubber spatula
  • Pajd cocktail stick
  • Mixing bowl
  • Plastic wrap
  • Cooling rack

Preparation:

  1. In a large bowl, combine 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup sugar, 2 1/4 teaspoons of active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon salt. Mix these ingredients well using a mixer. This mixture will serve as the base for our Hawaiian rolls.

  2. In a separate bowl, melt 1/4 cup of unsalted butter until it becomes soft. Set aside to cool.

  3. In a mixing bowl, beat 4 eggs, room temperature until they become frothy.

  4. Slowly add the warm water to the eggs while mixing continuously.

  5. Mix the melted butter into the egg mixture.

  6. Gradually pour the egg mixture into the flour mixture. Mix well until you obtain a smooth, homogenous dough.

  7. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes on a lightly floured surface until it becomes elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour as needed.

Dough Balls:

Divide the prepared dough into equal portions to form balls. The size of the balls depends on your preference and the number of rolls you want to create. You can use a measuring cup or the scale to have uniformly-sized dough balls.

Rising:

Place your dough balls on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spacing them evenly and covering them with plastic wrap. Let them rise for about an hour in a warm spot. Ensure the room is cool enough so the warmth encourages the dough to rise but not too hot to burn the dough.

Final Proof:

Once the dough has risen, it is essential to let it rest for 15-20 minutes before baking. Avoid disturbing the dough during this final proofing period for the best possible texture.

Oven and Preheat:

Preheat your oven t to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius).

Scoring:

Before baking, use a pair of sharp scissors or a sharp knife to score the dough into individual rolls. Cut about 3/4 of the way through the layer, leaving some dough connected to keep the shape of the roll.

Baking:

Place your baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for about 15-20 minutes or until golden brown. Let the rolls cool on a cooling rack for about 10-15 minutes before serving.

Enjoy:

Soft and fluffy Hawaiian rolls are now ready! Serve them hot or at room temperature as an appetizer, side dish, or in your favorite sandwiches. These sweet rolls can also be stored in an airtight container for three to five days, or freeze them for up to two months for later enjoyment.

Indulge in the soft, buttery goodness of homemade Hawaiian rolls and delight in the melt-in-your-mouth texture that makes them a crowd favorite. Do share your loved ones your newfound baking skills and spread the joy of homemade bread!
