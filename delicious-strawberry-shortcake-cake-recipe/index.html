Delicious Strawberry Shortcake Cake Recipe


When it comes to decadent and crowd-pleasing desserts, strawberry shortcake cake tops the list as a timeless favorite. It’s the kind of dessert that has everything: the juicy sweetness of strawberries, the soft, fluffy layers of cake, and the luscious richness of whipped cream. There’s something about the harmonious combination of these flavors and textures that makes strawberry shortcake cake an irresistible treat for any occasion. Whether you’re entertaining guests or simply indulging your sweet tooth, this recipe will bring smiles all around.

Why Strawberry Shortcake Cake Is the Ultimate Dessert

Strawberry shortcake cake offers a slightly more refined and layered experience than traditional strawberry shortcake. Instead of piling ingredients on a biscuit or sponge, this version transforms the concept into a proper stacked cake—layered with fresh fruit, creamy filling, and tender crumb. As strawberries are naturally vibrant, juicy, and flavorful, they shine beautifully in this recipe while complementing the butteriness of the cake and the creaminess of the whipped layer.

Better yet, strawberry shortcake cake is surprisingly simple to make. With a balance of effort and elegance, it’s the perfect dessert for spring and summer gatherings, afternoon tea parties, or a casual weekend indulgence. Let’s get started with the detailed recipe steps below, so you can craft your own slice of perfection right in your kitchen.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Cake Layers:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 ½ teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¾ cup buttermilk
  • ¾ cup sour cream

For the Strawberry Filling:

  • 1 lb fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice

For the Whipped Cream Frosting:

  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream, chilled
  • ¼ cup confectioner’s sugar (powdered sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Optional Garnish:

  • Whole fresh strawberries
  • Mint leaves for decoration

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cake Layers

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line two 9-inch round cake pans with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

  2. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside. These dry ingredients are the foundation for a light and fluffy cake.

  3. Using a mixer, beat the softened butter and granulated sugar in a large bowl until the mixture becomes pale and creamy. This should take around 3 to 5 minutes. Butter and sugar creaming makes the cake moist and airy.

  4. Add the eggs to the butter mixture one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract to enhance flavor.

  5. Reduce the mixer’s speed to low, and alternate adding the dry ingredients and buttermilk into the mixer, starting and ending with the dry mixture. Finally, mix in the sour cream gently for extra moisture.

  6. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans. Smooth the tops with a spatula, then bake the cakes for about 25 to 30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Cool the cakes completely in the pans before transferring them to a wire rack.

Step 2: Make the Strawberry Filling

  1. Combine the sliced strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a bowl. Toss gently to coat the berries. The sugar will draw out the berries’ natural juices, creating a luscious syrup.

  2. Let the mixture sit for about 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This will create a flavorful strawberry filling with a slightly thickened juice.

Step 3: Whip the Cream for Frosting

  1. In a chilled bowl, combine heavy whipping cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Using a hand mixer or standing mixer, beat on medium-high speed until soft peaks form. Be careful not to overmix, as the cream can become too stiff or grainy.

  2. Store the whipped cream frosting in the refrigerator until it’s time to assemble the cake.

Step 4: Assemble the Strawberry Shortcake Cake

  1. Place the first cake layer on a serving plate or cake stand. Spread an even layer of whipped cream frosting across the surface.

  2. Spoon half of the macerated strawberries with their syrup over the whipped cream, spreading them gently to cover the layer. Be sure to keep some juice aside for drizzling later.

  3. Add the second cake layer on top and repeat the process: spread whipped cream and top with the remaining strawberries. If desired, drizzle some of the reserved strawberry syrup over the top to amplify the fruity flavor.

  4. Cover the entire cake with a thin layer of whipped cream frosting, smoothing it out for a clean look. For decorative flair, pipe extra whipped cream around the edges or swirl it on top.

  5. Garnish the cake with whole strawberries and fresh mint leaves for a vibrant finishing touch.

Tips for Success

  • Freshness Matters: Be sure to use ripe, juicy strawberries for the best flavor and natural sweetness. If fresh berries aren’t available, frozen strawberries can work as a substitute for the filling (just thaw them beforehand).

  • Cake Storage: If you’re preparing the cake ahead of time, store it in the refrigerator to keep the whipped cream frosting stable. Bring it to room temperature for about 20 minutes before serving.

  • Customization: Feel free to add twists such as grated lemon zest in the cake batter, almond extract in the whipped cream, or a thin layer of strawberry jam between the cake layers for concentrated fruity flavor.

Serving and Enjoying

Strawberry shortcake cake is best served chilled or at room temperature. Its refreshing nature pairs wonderfully with a cup of coffee, iced tea, or even a glass of champagne for those special celebrations. This dessert is guaranteed to impress and delight at birthday parties, anniversaries, or casual gatherings.

The balance of flavors and textures in this recipe is what makes it truly spectacular. With every forkful, you’ll experience the delicate softness of the cake, the light creaminess of whipped frosting, and the juicy brightness of fresh strawberries—all blending into a perfect bite.

No matter the occasion, this strawberry shortcake cake recipe is bound to become one of your favorites. The simplicity, elegance, and unmistakable flavors make it a dessert worth savoring and sharing. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to wow yourself and anyone lucky enough to enjoy this beautiful treat!
