Classic American Chicken Fried Rice Recipe for Easy Dinner


When it comes to quick and satisfying meals, few dishes can compete with the comfort of fried rice. This Classic American Chicken Fried Rice recipe is a beloved choice for families looking for an easy dinner that doesn’t compromise on flavor. Not only is this dish incredibly simple to prepare, but it also allows you to utilize leftover chicken and vegetables, making it a versatile option that can be tailored to your pantry staples. In this post, we’ll explore the step-by-step process of creating this delicious meal, along with tips for achieving the perfect fried rice texture and flavor.

Ingredients

To make this Classic American Chicken Fried Rice, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old for the best texture)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, diced (rotisserie chicken works great)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (frozen peas and carrots are ideal)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce (optional but recommended)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (canola or sunflower oil)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Optional Add-Ins

  • Protein: Shrimp, tofu, or pork.
  • Vegetables: Bell peppers, broccoli, or baby corn.
  • Spices: Sesame oil or a dash of Sriracha for heat.

The Importance of Day-Old Rice

One of the secrets to achieving the perfect fried rice lies in the choice of rice. Freshly cooked rice tends to be too moist and sticky, resulting in a clumpy texture. Day-old rice, on the other hand, has had time to dry out in the refrigerator, making it ideal for frying. If you don’t have day-old rice, you can spread freshly cooked rice on a baking sheet and let it cool in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes before using it.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you start cooking, it’s essential to have all your ingredients prepped and ready to go. Chop the green onions, mince the garlic, and dice the cooked chicken. If you’re using fresh vegetables, chop them into small, bite-sized pieces. Preparing everything in advance ensures a smooth cooking process.

Step 2: Heat the Oil

In a large skillet or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Make sure the oil is hot before adding the ingredients to achieve that coveted fried rice texture. You can test the heat by adding a small piece of garlic; if it sizzles immediately, the oil is ready.

Step 3: Cook the Eggs

Crack the eggs directly into the skillet and scramble them until they are just set. This should take about 1-2 minutes. Once cooked, transfer the scrambled eggs to a plate and set aside. This step adds a rich flavor and protein to your fried rice.

Step 4: Sauté the Aromatics

In the same skillet, add a little more oil if necessary, and toss in the minced garlic. Sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant—be careful not to burn it, as burnt garlic can turn bitter. Next, add the mixed vegetables and cook for another 2-3 minutes until they are heated through and slightly tender.

Step 5: Add the Chicken and Rice

Now it’s time to bring everything together. Add the diced chicken to the skillet, stirring to incorporate it with the vegetables. Once the chicken is heated through, add the day-old rice, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir everything together, ensuring that the rice is evenly mixed with the chicken and vegetables.

Step 6: Season the Fried Rice

Pour in the soy sauce and oyster sauce (if using) over the rice mixture. Stir well to ensure that every grain of rice is coated with the savory sauces. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Remember, the soy sauce adds saltiness, so taste before adding more salt.

Step 7: Finish with Green Onions and Eggs

Once everything is well combined and heated through, add the chopped green onions and the scrambled eggs back into the skillet. Give everything a final stir, allowing the flavors to meld together. The green onions add a fresh crunch and vibrant color to the dish.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Remove the skillet from heat and transfer the chicken fried rice to a serving platter. You can garnish it with additional green onions or a drizzle of sesame oil for added flavor if desired. Serve hot and enjoy a hearty meal that’s sure to please the whole family.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Fried Rice

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: If you’re making a larger batch, consider cooking in batches to avoid steaming the rice.
  • Let the Rice Cool: If using freshly cooked rice, ensure it cools down before frying to achieve the right texture.
  • Customize It: Feel free to get creative with the ingredients based on what you have on hand. Fried rice is a fantastic way to use up leftovers.

Nutritional Information

This Classic American Chicken Fried Rice is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here’s a general breakdown of the nutritional content per serving (based on 4 servings):

  • Calories: 350
  • Protein: 22g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Carbohydrates: 40g
  • Fiber: 2g

The combination of chicken, vegetables, and rice provides a balanced meal that is both filling and nutritious.

Conclusion

In summary, this Classic American Chicken Fried Rice recipe is a fantastic option for an easy dinner that doesn’t skimp on flavor. With its simple preparation process and adaptability, it’s a meal that can easily become a family favorite. Whether you’re using leftover chicken or experimenting with different vegetables, this fried rice is sure to satisfy your hunger and bring joy to your dining table. So, the next time you’re in a rush, remember this recipe for a quick, delicious, and fulfilling meal that everyone will love.

Enjoy cooking and savor every bite of your homemade chicken fried rice!
