Pumpkin Ravioli with Brown Butter Sage Sauce: A Halloween Dinner Recipe


As the crisp autumn air settles in and the leaves transform into vibrant hues of orange and gold, it’s time to embrace the seasonal flavors of fall. One of the quintessential dishes that perfectly encapsulates the spirit of Halloween is Pumpkin Ravioli with Brown Butter Sage Sauce. This delightful recipe not only brings the comforting flavors of pumpkin to your table, but it also embodies the warmth and coziness of the season. Whether you’re hosting a Halloween dinner party or simply indulging in a romantic meal for two, this dish will surely impress and satisfy.

Ingredients

For the Ravioli Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional)

For the Pumpkin Filling:

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (fresh or canned)
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg yolk (for binding)

For the Brown Butter Sage Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 10-12 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving
  • Crushed walnuts or pecans (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Make the Ravioli Dough

  1. Mix the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and crack the eggs into it. If you’re using olive oil, add it now. Using a fork, beat the eggs gently, then gradually incorporate the flour from the edges of the well until a rough dough forms.

  2. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. Wrap it in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. This resting period allows the gluten to relax, making it easier to roll out later.

Step 2: Prepare the Pumpkin Filling

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, ricotta cheese, nutmeg, cinnamon, salt, pepper, Parmesan cheese, and egg yolk. Mix well until the ingredients are fully incorporated. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Set aside while you roll out the dough.

Step 3: Roll Out the Dough

  1. Divide and Roll: After the dough has rested, divide it into four equal portions. Keep the portions you are not currently using covered to prevent them from drying out. Roll out one portion on a floured surface or use a pasta machine, rolling it to about 1/16 inch thick.

  2. Cut the Dough: Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out circles from the rolled dough. Aim for about 3 inches in diameter.

Step 4: Assemble the Ravioli

  1. Fill the Ravioli: Place a teaspoon of the pumpkin filling in the center of each dough circle. Be careful not to overfill, as this can cause the ravioli to burst during cooking.

  2. Seal the Ravioli: Moisten the edges of each dough circle with a little water. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape. Press the edges firmly to seal, ensuring there are no air pockets. Use a fork to crimp the edges for an extra seal and decorative touch.

  3. Repeat: Continue this process until all the dough and filling are used, placing the finished ravioli on a floured baking sheet.

Step 5: Cook the Ravioli

  1. Boil Water: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Carefully drop the ravioli into the boiling water in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pot.

  2. Cook the Ravioli: Boil for about 3-4 minutes, or until the ravioli float to the surface and are cooked through. Fresh ravioli cooks much quicker than dried.

  3. Drain the Ravioli: Using a slotted spoon, gently remove the ravioli from the pot and transfer them to a plate. Drizzle with a little olive oil to prevent sticking while you prepare the sauce.

Step 6: Prepare the Brown Butter Sage Sauce

  1. Melt the Butter: In a large skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. Allow it to cook for about 5-7 minutes, swirling the pan occasionally until the butter turns a golden brown and develops a nutty aroma. Be careful not to burn it.

  2. Add the Sage: Once the butter is browned, add the fresh sage leaves. They will sizzle and crisp up, infusing the butter with their earthy flavor. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.

  3. Season: Remove the skillet from the heat and season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 7: Serve the Dish

  1. Combine: Gently toss the cooked ravioli in the brown butter sage sauce, ensuring each piece is well coated.

  2. Plating: Serve the ravioli on warm plates. Sprinkle with additional grated Parmesan cheese and garnish with crushed walnuts or pecans for a delightful crunch.

  3. Enjoy: Sit back, savor the first bite, and relish the flavors of fall in every mouthful.

Tips for the Perfect Pumpkin Ravioli

  • Pumpkin Options: If using fresh pumpkin, roast it until tender, scoop out the flesh, and puree it for the filling. This process enhances the flavor and freshness of your ravioli.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the ravioli ahead of time and freeze them before cooking. Just ensure they are laid out on a floured baking sheet until frozen solid, then transfer to a freezer bag for storage.
  • Sauce Variations: Feel free to customize your sauce. A dash of lemon juice or zest can add brightness, or you could incorporate some cream for a richer sauce.

Conclusion

Pumpkin Ravioli with Brown Butter Sage Sauce is more than just a dish; it’s a celebration of the flavors of fall, an experience that brings warmth and joy to the table. Perfect for Halloween gatherings or cozy dinners, this recipe is sure to be a hit. As you gather around with friends and family, share stories and laughter over this delightful meal, embracing the spirit of the season. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to impress with this deliciously festive dish! Happy cooking!
