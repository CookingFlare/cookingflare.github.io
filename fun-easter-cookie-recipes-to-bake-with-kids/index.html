Fun Easter Cookie Recipes to Bake with Kids


Easter is the perfect occasion to gather the family in the kitchen and create some delicious, festive treats together. Baking cookies with kids not only ignites creativity but also provides a wonderful bonding experience. Whether it’s the joy of mixing dough or the excitement of decorating, these fun Easter cookie recipes will fill your home with sweet smells and even sweeter memories. Below, you’ll find mouthwatering recipes tailored for baking enthusiasts of all ages, from classic sugar cookies to charming bunny-shaped delights.

Why Bake Easter Cookies with Kids?

Baking with children is a fantastic way to encourage teamwork, teach kitchen skills, and foster creativity. Kids love the hands-on nature of rolling dough, using cookie cutters, and decorating with colorful icing and sprinkles. Plus, there’s something magical in seeing their eyes light up as they transform simple ingredients into charming treats. These recipes are designed to be simple, safe, and full of fun.

1. Classic Easter Sugar Cookies

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon milk

Directions

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, use an electric mixer to beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add Wet Ingredients: Beat in the egg, vanilla extract, and milk until combined.
  4. Combine: Gradually add dry ingredients to the wet mixture, blending on low speed until dough forms.
  5. Chill Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic and refrigerate for at least one hour to make rolling easier.
  6. Roll and Cut: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll dough out on a floured surface to about ¼ inch thick. Use Easter-themed cookie cutters (bunnies, eggs, chicks) to cut shapes.
  7. Bake: Place cookies on a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes or until edges start to turn golden. Cool completely before decorating.

Decorating Idea:

Set up a decorating station with brightly colored royal icing, assorted sprinkles, edible glitter, and small candies. Let the kids unleash their creativity decorating bunnies with carrot-orange icing or eggs bursting with pastel colors.

2. Chewy Carrot Cake Cookies

If you want to combine the essence of Easter and healthy veggies, carrot cake cookies are a fantastic choice—and kids love them!

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup finely grated carrots
  • ½ cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)
  • ½ cup raisins (optional)
  • ½ cup cream cheese frosting for topping (optional)

Directions

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: Sift together flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  2. Cream Butter and Sugar: Beat butter and brown sugar until fluffy.
  3. Add Egg and Vanilla: Mix in the egg and vanilla until smooth.
  4. Incorporate Carrots: Stir in grated carrots.
  5. Mix Dry and Wet Ingredients: Gradually add dry ingredients to wet. Stir in nuts and raisins if using.
  6. Scoop: Drop dough by spoonfuls onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake: Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-12 minutes, until edges are golden.
  8. Optional Topping: Once cooled, spread cream cheese frosting on top and decorate with small Easter-themed candies.

This recipe delivers moist, flavorful cookies full of texture—perfect for an Easter picnic or classroom treat.

3. Bunny Butt Cutout Cookies

One cookie shape kids adore making is the hilarious bunny butt. These quirky cookies are just as fun to bake as they are to eat.

Ingredients

  • Use the basic sugar cookie dough recipe from above.
  • Mini marshmallows (for bunny tails)
  • White and pink royal icing
  • Edible black gel for eyes

Directions

  1. Roll and Cut: Using a bunny-shaped cookie cutter, cut out the dough.
  2. Bake: Follow the sugar cookie recipe baking times.
  3. Decorate: Use white icing as the base. Attach a mini marshmallow at the back of the cookie with a dab of icing to create the fluffy bunny tail.
  4. Add Details: Use pink icing to make cheeks and ears, and black gel to craft cute eyes on the bunny’s face.

This playful design invites giggles and encourages kids to experiment with details and colors.

4. Easter Egg Marbled Cookies

A striking visual effect, the marbled Easter egg cookies are easy and elegant. They’re great for older kids who can get hands-on with the rolling and swirling.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Food coloring in pastel shades (pink, blue, yellow, green)

Directions

  1. Make Basic Dough: Combine dry ingredients in one bowl, cream butter and sugar in another.
  2. Add Egg and Vanilla: Beat in egg and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Form Base Dough: Mix dry ingredients into wet.
  4. Divide Dough: Split dough evenly into small bowls, add different pastel food coloring to each, mixing well.
  5. Create Marble: Take small pieces from each colored dough and gently knead them together just a few times to create a marbled effect.
  6. Roll and Cut: Roll out the marbled dough and use an egg-shaped cookie cutter.
  7. Bake: At 350°F (175°C) for around 9-11 minutes. Cool before serving.

These cookies are delightful eye candy and taste as good as they look, with soft, buttery texture.

Tips to Make Baking with Kids Even More Enjoyable

  • Prep Ahead: Measure all ingredients before starting to keep things smooth and fun.
  • Use Child-Friendly Tools: Smaller rolling pins, safe cookie cutters, and kid-sized mixing spoons make it easier for little hands.
  • Encourage Creativity: Let kids pick their own colors and designs for decorating.
  • Embrace the Mess: Flour on the counter and icing on the floor are all part of the fun! Just have extra towels ready.
  • Story Telling: Share Easter traditions or invent stories about the cookies you bake to add an imaginative twist.

Baking these fun Easter cookie recipes with your children can create lifelong traditions and memories. These treats are not just desserts—they’re moments of joy shared around the kitchen counter. So gather your ingredients, invite your little ones into the kitchen, and let the Easter magic unfold, one cookie at a time. Happy baking!
