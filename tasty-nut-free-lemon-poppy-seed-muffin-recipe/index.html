Tasty Nut-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffin Recipe


Are you a fan of muffins but have a nut allergy? Look no further! I have the perfect recipe for you - Nut-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins. These muffins are bursting with tangy lemon flavor and a delightful crunch from the poppy seeds. And the best part? They are completely safe for those with nut allergies. So let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons poppy seeds

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes with an electric mixer.

  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next one. Add the vanilla extract, lemon zest, and lemon juice, and mix until well combined.

  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk. Start and finish with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined, being careful not to overmix.

  6. Gently fold in the poppy seeds, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  7. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about two-thirds full.

  8. Bake for approximately 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

  9. Remove the muffins from the oven and let them cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  10. Once cooled, these Nut-Free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins are ready to be enjoyed! They make the perfect breakfast treat or afternoon snack. Serve them with a cup of tea or coffee for an extra special treat.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of lemon flavor, you can make a simple lemon glaze by combining powdered sugar and lemon juice. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled muffins for an added touch of sweetness.

  • If you don’t have poppy seeds on hand, you can substitute them with chia seeds or omit them altogether. The muffins will still be delicious!

  • These muffins can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. You can also freeze them for longer storage. Just make sure to thaw them before enjoying.

  • Feel free to experiment with different citrus flavors. You can try orange or lime zest and juice instead of lemon for a unique twist on this classic recipe.

So there you have it - a delicious and nut-free Lemon Poppy Seed Muffin recipe that is sure to please everyone, regardless of dietary restrictions. Give it a try and let me know what you think. Happy baking!
