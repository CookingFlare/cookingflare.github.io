Cooking with Gochujang: Korean Chili Paste Recipes


When it comes to Korean cuisine, one ingredient that stands out for its unique flavor and versatility is Gochujang. This fermented Korean chili paste is a staple in Korean cooking and adds a depth of flavor and spice to a wide variety of dishes. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious and creative ways to use Gochujang in your cooking. From spicy marinades to savory stews, Gochujang can elevate your dishes to new heights.

Gochujang Marinated Chicken Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of Gochujang
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine Gochujang, soy sauce, honey, minced garlic, and sesame oil to make the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken cubes onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers for 6-8 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.

These Gochujang marinated chicken skewers are a perfect combination of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet flavors. They make for a fantastic appetizer or main course at your next barbecue.

Spicy Gochujang Ramen

Ingredients:

  • 2 packs of ramen noodles
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of Gochujang
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Sliced scallions, for garnish
  • Soft-boiled eggs, for garnish
  • Kimchi, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the broth to a boil and add the Gochujang, soy sauce, and sesame oil.
  2. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions and drain.
  3. Divide the cooked noodles into serving bowls and pour the hot broth over them.
  4. Garnish with sliced scallions, soft-boiled eggs, and kimchi.

This spicy Gochujang ramen is a comforting and flavorful dish that’s perfect for a cozy night in. The combination of the rich broth, chewy noodles, and spicy Gochujang creates a bowl of pure bliss.

Gochujang Glazed Tofu Stir-fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 3 tablespoons of Gochujang
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • Mixed vegetables of your choice (bell peppers, broccoli, snap peas, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together Gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and brown sugar to make the glaze.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the cubed tofu to the pan and cook until golden and crispy on all sides.
  4. Push the tofu to one side of the pan and add the mixed vegetables to the other side.
  5. Stir-fry the vegetables until they are tender-crisp.
  6. Pour the Gochujang glaze over the tofu and vegetables, and toss to coat evenly.

This Gochujang glazed tofu stir-fry is a delightful and wholesome dish that’s packed with flavor and nutrients. It’s a great way to incorporate Gochujang into a vegetarian or vegan meal.

In conclusion, Gochujang is a versatile ingredient that can add a delightful kick to a wide range of dishes. Whether you’re grilling, simmering, or stir-frying, the bold flavor of Gochujang is sure to impress your taste buds. Experiment with these recipes and unleash the spicy magic of Gochujang in your kitchen!
