Quick and Tasty Vegetarian Stir Fry Recipes for Meat-Free Mondays


If you’re looking to add some excitement and flavor to your Meat-Free Mondays, then look no further! Stir fry recipes are a fantastic way to incorporate a variety of vegetables into your meals while keeping things quick and tasty. In this blog post, we’ll explore three delicious vegetarian stir fry recipes that are sure to become favorites in your kitchen. So grab your wok and let’s get cooking!

1. Spicy Sesame Tofu Stir Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat one tablespoon of sesame oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the wok and set aside.
  2. In the same wok, add the remaining sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. Stir fry for about a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the bell peppers and sugar snap peas to the wok. Stir fry for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. Return the tofu to the wok and add the soy sauce, sriracha sauce, sesame seeds, salt, and pepper. Stir fry for an additional 2-3 minutes, ensuring everything is well coated in the sauce.
  5. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

2. Teriyaki Vegetable Stir Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and carrots, and stir fry for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
  2. Add the bell peppers, broccoli, snow peas, and mushrooms to the wok. Stir fry for another 4-5 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the teriyaki sauce, honey, rice vinegar, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Pour the mixture over the vegetables in the wok and stir fry for an additional 2-3 minutes until the sauce has thickened.
  4. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

3. Thai Basil Eggplant Stir Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 medium eggplants, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili pepper, sliced (adjust to taste)
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the eggplants and stir fry for 5-6 minutes until they begin to soften and turn golden brown.
  2. Add the garlic and red chili pepper to the wok. Stir fry for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the bell pepper, Thai basil leaves, soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), brown sugar, salt, and pepper to the wok. Stir fry for an additional 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are cooked through and well coated in the sauce.
  4. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

Conclusion

There you have it, three quick and tasty vegetarian stir fry recipes that are perfect for Meat-Free Mondays or any day of the week! Whether you’re a tofu lover, a fan of teriyaki, or enjoy the flavors of Thai cuisine, there’s something here to satisfy every palate. So grab your wok and start cooking up these delicious meat-free meals. Your taste buds will thank you!
