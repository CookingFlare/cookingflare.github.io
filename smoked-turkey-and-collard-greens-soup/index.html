Smoked Turkey and Collard Greens Soup


As the weather turns crisp and cozy meals call us to the kitchen, few dishes embrace the warmth and richness of autumn better than a bowl of Smoked Turkey and Collard Greens Soup. This dish beautifully combines the savory depth of smoked turkey with the earthy, nutrient-packed goodness of collard greens, creating a robust and satisfying soup that will quickly become a household favorite. Perfect for family dinners, meal prep, or even holiday gatherings, this recipe elevates traditional Southern flavors into a heartwarming culinary experience.

Why Smoked Turkey and Collard Greens Are a Match Made in Food Heaven

Smoked turkey is undeniably one of the most versatile ingredients in rustic cooking, infusing a subtle smoky aroma into every dish it graces. It pairs seamlessly with collard greens, a Southern staple that boasts vibrant color, remarkable flavor, and exceptional nutritional benefits. When simmered together in a harmonious broth, the tender turkey imparts richness while the greens provide an earthy balance that’s both comforting and refreshing.

Beyond the exquisite flavor, Smoked Turkey and Collard Greens Soup is a powerhouse of nutrients. Collard greens are loaded with vitamins K, A, and C, while smoked turkey contributes lean protein and essential minerals. Together, they create a balanced one-pot meal that warms both the heart and body.

Let’s dive into this delicious recipe that’s sure to impress on your dinner table!

Smoked Turkey and Collard Greens Soup Recipe

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need for a pot of soulful Smoked Turkey and Collard Greens Soup:

For the Base:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and diced

For the Stock:

  • 8 cups chicken or turkey broth (preferably homemade for added richness)
  • 1 smoked turkey leg or wings (around 1 to 1½ pounds)

For the Greens:

  • 4 cups fresh collard greens, stemmed and chopped (washed thoroughly to remove grit)

For the Seasoning:

  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional, for a touch of heat)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

Optional Toppings:

  • Chopped parsley or chives for garnish
  • A dash of hot sauce

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Base

In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onions, garlic, celery, and carrots. Sauté the vegetables until they’re softened and aromatic, about 5-7 minutes. This creates the foundation of flavor for your soup, building layers of deliciousness right at the start.

Step 2: Add the Smoked Turkey

Place the smoked turkey leg or wings into the pot, nestled among the softened vegetables. Pour in the chicken or turkey broth, ensuring the turkey is fully submerged. If you’re using homemade broth, this is an excellent opportunity to unlock deeper flavor notes. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil.

Step 3: Season and Simmer

Reduce the heat to low and add the bay leaf, smoked paprika, ground black pepper, and crushed red pepper flakes (if using). Let the soup simmer slowly for about 45 minutes to 1 hour. This step is crucial as the smoky richness from the turkey infuses into the broth, creating the soulful depth that makes this dish shine.

As the soup simmers, periodically skim off any foam or excess fat that may collect at the surface to keep the broth clear and clean.

Step 4: Prepare the Collard Greens

While the soup is simmering, wash the collard greens thoroughly and remove any tough stems. Slice them into bite-sized ribbons. Collard greens can sometimes carry grit, so ensure they’re properly rinsed under cold water before adding them to your soup.

Step 5: Incorporate the Greens

Once the turkey has tenderized and the broth is beautifully flavored, carefully remove the smoked turkey leg or wings from the pot and set them aside briefly. Stir the collard greens into the soup and let them simmer for an additional 20 minutes, or until tender.

Step 6: Shred the Turkey

Using a fork, shred the smoked turkey meat into bite-sized pieces, discarding any bones and skin. Return the shredded turkey to the pot, stirring it into the greens-laced broth. Taste the soup and adjust seasonings as needed. A splash of apple cider vinegar rounds out the flavors, adding brightness to the smoky and savory notes.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Ladle the hot soup into bowls, and if desired, garnish with freshly chopped parsley or chives. A drizzle of hot sauce can also amplify the bold flavors, perfect for anyone who loves a touch of spice. Serve immediately with cornbread, crusty sourdough, or crackers on the side.

Cooking Tips and Variations

Broth Recommendations

While store-bought broth works in a pinch, using homemade chicken or turkey stock takes this soup to incredible heights. Achieving that “from-scratch” depth of flavor is well worth the effort.

Alternative Greens

If collard greens aren’t readily available, you can substitute them with kale, mustard greens, or even Swiss chard. Each type of green brings its own nuances to the dish, so feel free to experiment based on your preference.

Add Starch for Heftier Soup

For a heartier meal, consider adding cooked rice, quinoa, or diced potatoes directly to the pot during the final simmer. These additional carbs will make the soup substantial enough to satisfy even the hungriest eaters.

Spice Tweaks

Feel free to adjust the spice levels to suit your taste. Crushed red pepper flakes pack heat, but for a milder version, you can leave them out entirely.
