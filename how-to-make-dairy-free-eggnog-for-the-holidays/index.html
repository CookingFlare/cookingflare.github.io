How to Make Dairy-Free Eggnog for the Holidays


The holiday season brings with it a plethora of comforting traditions, and one of the most-loved beverages to emerge during this festive time is eggnog. Rich, creamy, and delightfully spiced, eggnog is synonymous with holiday cheer. But for those who are lactose intolerant, vegan, or simply opting for a dairy-free lifestyle, traditional eggnog can be off the table. Luckily, creating a delicious dairy-free eggnog at home is easier than you might think—and it’s every bit as luscious as the classic version.

Whether you’re hosting a holiday gathering or simply cozying up by the fireplace, this dairy-free eggnog recipe is guaranteed to elevate your festivities. And with high-quality plant-based ingredients and the right blend of spices, you won’t miss traditional dairy one bit. Let’s dive into how to create your very own glass of dairy-free magic!

Ingredients for Dairy-Free Eggnog

The hallmark of any holiday eggnog is its creamy base, rich flavor, and hint of warming spices. In this dairy-free version, traditional milk and cream are swapped out for plant-based alternatives, all without sacrificing texture or taste. Here’s what you’ll need:

Liquid Base

  • Unsweetened almond milk (or any plant-based milk of your choice): Almond milk lends a smooth, nutty flavor, but oat milk, coconut milk, or cashew milk work just as well.
  • Full-fat canned coconut milk: This adds richness and a velvety texture to mimic the traditional cream in eggnog.

Flavor Enhancements

  • Maple syrup or agave nectar: A natural sweetener that complements the other flavors wonderfully.
  • Vanilla extract: For that extra depth and classic holiday aroma.
  • Ground cinnamon: A signature spice in eggnog that provides warmth.
  • Ground nutmeg: Adds a distinctive seasonal touch.
  • Clove or allspice (optional): For an extra burst of holiday flavor.

Egg Substitute

  • Unsweetened cashew butter or silken tofu: These help replicate the creamy consistency of traditional eggnog while maintaining an egg-free profile.
  • Black salt (Kala Namak) (optional): Adds a subtle, egg-like flavor for those who miss the traditional eggnog essence.

For a Boozy Twist

  • Dark rum, bourbon, or whiskey: A splash of spirits elevates the eggnog into an indulgent holiday cocktail. Use about 1-2 ounces per serving (optional).

Tools You’ll Need

Here are the simple tools you’ll need to whip up dairy-free eggnog:

  • Blender: Achieving a creamy consistency is key, and a blender makes this effortless.
  • Whisk: Perfect for blending spices smoothly.
  • Saucepan: For heating the eggnog mixture and allowing the flavors to meld.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Dairy-Free Eggnog

Now that you have your ingredients and tools ready, let’s walk through the step-by-step process of making this delightful holiday beverage.

Step 1: Create the Creamy Base

Begin by combining your plant-based milk (such as almond milk) and canned coconut milk in your blender. The coconut milk adds richness, while the almond milk balances out the flavor perfectly. Blend on medium speed until smooth and well-integrated. If using cashew butter or silken tofu as an egg substitute, add it to the blender at this stage to achieve that traditional creamy texture.

Step 2: Sweeten and Flavor

Next, add your sweetener of choice—you only need about 2-3 tablespoons of maple syrup or agave nectar to keep the eggnog lightly sweet. Follow this with vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of cloves or allspice for depth. Blend again to incorporate the spices evenly. If you prefer an egg-like taste, a dash of black salt can go a long way in mimicking that profile.

Step 3: Heat and Infuse

Pour the blended mixture into a saucepan and place it over medium heat. Slowly whisk the eggnog to keep it smooth and prevent clumping. Heating the mixture allows the spices to bloom and meld beautifully, creating a fragrant and comforting base.

Cook for about 5-7 minutes, but be cautious not to boil the mixture. You’re looking for gently heated eggnog with an enticing aroma filling your kitchen. If you’re adding alcohol later and want to ensure a non-alcoholic version for kids or non-drinkers, go ahead and heat the mixture thoroughly to finalize it before serving.

Step 4: Chill or Serve Fresh

Once the eggnog has reached the desired temperature, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly if serving warm, or refrigerate it for at least four hours if you prefer chilled eggnog. Eggnog tends to thicken as it cools, giving it that desired luxurious consistency.

When serving, pour into festive glasses or mugs and give it a gentle stir or shake to ensure the texture remains smooth. If you plan to serve it later, store the eggnog in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days.

Optional Additions and Garnishes

To really make your dairy-free eggnog stand out, consider a few finishing touches:

  • Freshly grated nutmeg or cinnamon stick: Sprinkle nutmeg on top or add a cinnamon stick to each glass for a charming garnish.
  • Coconut whipped cream: Dollop some creamy coconut whipped topping over the eggnog for extra indulgence.
  • Splash of spirits: If you’re serving adult-only eggnog, stir in a shot of dark rum, bourbon, or whiskey right before serving. The warm notes of these spirits pair beautifully with the spiced flavors.
  • Dusting of cocoa powder: For a festive twist, sprinkle cocoa powder or serve with a chocolate wafer.

Why Make Dairy-Free Eggnog at Home?

Making your own dairy-free eggnog offers plenty of benefits. Not only do you avoid the additives commonly found in store-bought versions, but you also control the level of sweetness, richness, and flavors to suit your preferences. Customization is key—whether you prefer it nutty with almond milk or extra luscious with coconut milk, you’re in charge. Plus, sharing homemade eggnog with family and friends shows a little extra love during the holiday season.

Common Questions About Dairy-Free Eggnog

Can you make it sugar-free?

Yes! If you’re looking to lower sugar content, opt for monk fruit sweetener or stevia in place of maple syrup.

I don’t like coconut milk—are there alternatives?

Absolutely. While coconut milk provides the creamiest texture, oat milk, cashew milk, and even soy milk are excellent substitutes. Choose what works best for your flavor preferences!

How do I make it frothy?

For a frothier consistency, blend the eggnog mixture for a few extra seconds right before serving or use a milk frother on the final drink.

Bring Joy with Every Sip

This holiday season, there’s no reason to miss out on the classic comfort of eggnog simply because you’re living dairy-free. Rich, delicious, and packed with holiday flavors, this vegan-friendly eggnog is a seasonal treasure. Whether enjoyed warm or chilled, spiked or sober, each sip is a celebration of festive cheer. So gather your loved ones, raise a glass of dairy-free eggnog, and toast to the magical moments of the holidays ahead. Cheers!
