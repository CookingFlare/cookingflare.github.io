Satisfying Beef and Vegetable Casserole: Winter's Wholesome Comfort


As the winter chill sets in, there’s nothing quite like a hearty and comforting casserole to warm the soul. This satisfying beef and vegetable casserole recipe is a celebration of wholesome ingredients and rich flavors that come together to create a dish that is perfect for cozy nights in and family gatherings. Packed with tender beef, vibrant vegetables, and a luscious sauce, this casserole is a true winter comfort food that will leave everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900g) beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup (240ml) beef broth
  • 1 cup (240ml) red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups (480ml) baby potatoes, halved
  • 2 cups (480ml) green beans, trimmed
  • 1 cup (240ml) frozen peas
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).

  2. In a large oven-safe pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.

  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté until the onion is soft and translucent.

  4. Add the carrots and celery to the pot, and cook for a few minutes until they begin to soften.

  5. Pour in the beef broth and red wine, and stir in the tomato paste. Add the bay leaves, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. Return the browned beef to the pot and stir to combine everything.

  6. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then cover the pot and transfer it to the preheated oven. Let the casserole cook for 1.5 hours, or until the beef is tender.

  7. Remove the pot from the oven and stir in the halved baby potatoes, green beans, and frozen peas. Return the pot to the oven and continue cooking for another 30-45 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

  8. Once the casserole is ready, remove the bay leaves and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Sprinkle the chopped parsley over the top for a pop of freshness.

  9. Serve the beef and vegetable casserole hot, spooning the tender beef and vegetables into bowls and ladling the flavorsome sauce over the top.

Why This Recipe Works

The slow cooking process in the oven allows the beef to become incredibly tender, while infusing the vegetables with rich, savory flavors. The combination of beef broth and red wine creates a deeply flavorful sauce that coats every ingredient in the casserole. The addition of baby potatoes, green beans, and peas not only adds color and vibrancy to the dish but also provides a variety of textures and tastes in every bite.

This beef and vegetable casserole is perfect for feeding a crowd or for making ahead of time, as the flavors only improve with time. It’s a versatile dish that can be served on its own, over a bed of fluffy mashed potatoes, or alongside crusty bread to soak up every last drop of the delicious sauce.

So, when the cold weather has you yearning for a wholesome and satisfying meal, this beef and vegetable casserole will be your go-to recipe for comfort, flavor, and warmth. Enjoy the simple pleasure of a homemade dish that brings everyone together around the dinner table, creating memories that will last long after the last bite has been savored.
