Brunch Baking: Delicious Pastry Recipes for Your Morning Table


Brunch is more than just a meal; it’s an experience. Whether you’re hosting friends, enjoying a quiet weekend with family, or treating yourself to a special morning, brunch has a unique charm. It blends the best elements of breakfast and lunch, often featuring delicious, indulgent pastries that set the tone for the entire day. Baking pastries for brunch allows you to get creative and elevate your table with a variety of flavors, textures, and colors.

In this post, we’ll explore a range of pastry recipes that will bring joy to your morning table. From sweet and fruity to savory and cheesy, these brunch-friendly pastries offer something for everyone. Let’s dive into the world of brunch baking!

The Appeal of Brunch Pastries

What makes pastries so irresistible at brunch? Pastries are versatile, easy to make in batches, and always a crowd-pleaser. Their flaky layers, tender interiors, and the combination of sweet or savory fillings create the perfect balance of flavors and textures. Plus, many pastry recipes can be prepped ahead of time, making them perfect for busy mornings when you want to serve something special without too much effort.

The key to great brunch pastries lies in the balance of flavor, freshness, and convenience. Whether it’s a buttery croissant, a warm cinnamon roll, or a savory quiche, there are countless options to choose from. Let’s explore some of the most delicious and easy-to-make pastries you can add to your brunch repertoire.

1. Flaky Butter Croissants

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 ¼ cups cold unsalted butter
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a bowl, combine the warm water and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast on top and let it sit for about 5 minutes, or until it becomes frothy.

  2. Prepare the Dough: In a stand mixer, combine the flour, salt, and activated yeast mixture. Mix on low speed until the dough comes together, then knead on medium for 5 minutes until smooth.

  3. Chill the Dough: Shape the dough into a ball and wrap it in plastic wrap. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour.

  4. Butter Layering: Roll the dough into a large rectangle. Place the cold butter between two sheets of parchment paper and pound it out to form a square. Place the butter in the center of the dough rectangle, folding the edges over it. Roll the dough into a long rectangle and fold it into thirds like a letter. Chill the dough for 30 minutes, then repeat the folding process twice.

  5. Shape and Bake: After the final fold, roll out the dough into a rectangle and cut it into triangles. Roll each triangle tightly, starting from the wide end. Place them on a baking sheet, brush with egg wash, and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 15-18 minutes, or until golden brown.

These buttery croissants are the epitome of a perfect brunch pastry. Their flaky layers and melt-in-your-mouth texture are sure to impress your guests.

2. Cinnamon Rolls with Cream Cheese Frosting

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • ¾ cup warm milk
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup butter, melted
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 3 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/3 cup butter, softened

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp milk

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy. Add in the butter, salt, eggs, and flour. Mix until a dough forms and knead for about 5-6 minutes until smooth. Let the dough rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a small bowl, mix the brown sugar and cinnamon. Set aside.

  3. Roll the Dough: After the dough has risen, roll it out into a large rectangle. Spread the softened butter evenly across the dough, then sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over the top.

  4. Shape the Rolls: Starting from the long edge of the rectangle, roll the dough tightly into a log. Slice it into 12 even pieces and place them in a greased baking dish.

  5. Bake the Rolls: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Let the rolls rise for another 30 minutes. Once they’ve risen, bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.

  6. Make the Frosting: While the rolls bake, beat together the cream cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and milk until smooth. Once the rolls are done baking, spread the frosting over them while they’re still warm.

These cinnamon rolls are the perfect sweet treat to accompany your brunch. The soft, fluffy dough and gooey cinnamon filling, paired with the rich cream cheese frosting, make these rolls an irresistible favorite.

3. Savory Cheese and Herb Scones

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh rosemary
  • ¾ cup buttermilk

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the cold butter to the flour mixture and cut it in using a pastry cutter or your hands until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add the Cheese and Herbs: Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and chopped rosemary.

  4. Make the Dough: Pour in the buttermilk and stir gently until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  5. Shape and Bake: Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently pat it into a 1-inch thick circle. Cut the dough into 8 wedges and place them on a baking sheet. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

These savory scones are the perfect complement to your brunch spread. The sharp cheddar cheese and aromatic rosemary create a wonderfully savory flavor that pairs well with eggs, salad, or just on their own.

4. Fruit-Filled Danish Pastries

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 ½ tsp instant yeast
  • ¾ cup warm milk
  • 1/3 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 egg

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup mixed berries (such as raspberries, blueberries, and strawberries)
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Add the warm milk, butter, and egg. Mix and knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, until smooth. Cover the dough and let it rise for about 1 hour.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a small saucepan, combine the mixed berries, sugar, lemon zest, and cornstarch. Cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens and the berries break down. Let it cool.

  3. Shape the Pastries: After the dough has risen, divide it into 8 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a circle, then fold the edges inward to create a small well in the center. Spoon some of the berry filling into each pastry.

  4. Bake the Pastries: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the edges of the pastries with egg wash and bake for 15-18 minutes, or until golden brown.

These fruit-filled Danish pastries are a delightful treat for a morning brunch. The combination of flaky dough and sweet, tangy berry filling makes them a refreshing option for those who prefer fruity flavors at brunch.

5. Mini Quiche Lorraine

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 4-6 tbsp ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup cooked bacon, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the Crust: In a food processor, pulse together the flour, salt, and cold butter until it forms a crumbly mixture. Slowly add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and chill for 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and heavy cream. Stir in the bacon, Gruyère cheese, thyme, salt, and pepper.

  3. Assemble the Quiches: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough and cut it into circles to fit into muffin tins. Press the dough into the tins and fill each crust with the egg mixture.

  4. Bake the Quiches: Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the quiches are set and golden brown on top. Let them cool slightly before serving.

These mini Quiche Lorraine are savory, rich, and satisfying. Perfect for a brunch gathering, they are easy to make and can be customized with different fillings like spinach, mushrooms, or ham.

Conclusion

Brunch baking opens up a world of culinary possibilities, from sweet pastries like cinnamon rolls and croissants to savory options like cheese scones and mini quiches. These recipes will allow you to indulge in the art of pastry-making and create something truly special for your morning table. Whether you’re a novice baker or an experienced pro, there’s a pastry recipe for everyone. Enjoy the process, get creative with your fillings and flavors, and most importantly, share these delightful treats with the people you love. Happy baking!
