Sweet Potato Dinner Rolls: A Unique Twist


When it comes to dinner rolls, we usually think of tender, pillowy clouds of dough, ready to soak up all the flavors of our favorite dishes. But what if we could enhance that comforting staple with a touch of sweetness? Meet the star of your holiday table - sweet potato dinner rolls! This unique spin on a classic adds an aromatic, wholesome twist that will have everyone asking for seconds.

The beauty of these rolls lies in their simplicity. Grounded in a sturdy yet tender dough made with whole wheat flour, our little sweet potato nuggets of joy bring the benefits of this nutritious and flavorful root vegetable to your favorite meals.

Before we dive into the recipe, a few tips and tricks to ensure your sweet potato dinner rolls are perfect:

  1. Opt for a ripe sweet potato with deep-orange color, as overripe sweet potatoes tend to be soft and too sugary.

  2. Cook the sweet potato before mashing it, as this will prevent it from making the dough too moist. You can bake it, microwave it, or boil it – whatever method you prefer.

  3. Use active dry yeast for the best results. This ensures that the dough rises to its full potential, resulting in soft and delicious rolls.

  4. Resist the urge to handle the dough too much. Just a gentle kneading is needed for these rolls. Over-handling can lead to dense rolls.

Now let’s dive into this comforting recipe that has been a hit at my table and is sure to be a crowd pleaser at yours!

Ingredients

  • 1 ⅓ cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup mashed cooked sweet potato
  • Optional: pumpkin seeds or sunflower seeds for added texture

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm water and sugar until dissolved. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5 minutes.

  2. Meanwhile, in a separate bowl, mix together both types of flour and the salt.

  3. Once the yeast mixture is frothy, pour it into the dry mix, followed by the egg, and vegetable oil.

  4. Fold in the mashed sweet potato until a soft dough forms.

  5. Dust a clean surface with a bit of flour and start kneading the dough. Try not to over-knead. The consistency should be smooth and elastic, but not too stretchy.

  6. Form the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a tea towel and let it rise in a warm place for about 45 minutes or until doubled in size.

  7. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  8. Sprinkle the optional seeds on a plate and roll the risen dough into them to coat on both sides. Then, divide the dough into equal parts.

  9. Shape each part into smooth round rolls and place them on the prepared baking sheet.

  10. Cover the rolls with the tea towel and let them rest and rise for another 10 minutes.

  11. Bake for around 20-25 minutes or until the rolls are golden brown.

  12. Remove the rolls from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

It’s hard to say goodbye to these lovelies once they’re gone, but you can bring them back time and time again by freezing the dough for a future meal.

Incorporate these irresistible sweet potato dinner rolls into your holiday table or as an impressive appetizer at your next gathering. Made with wholesome ingredients, bursting with sweet potato flavor, and as versatile as they are easy to make, these treats scream comfort and add a unique twist to your culinary sessions.
