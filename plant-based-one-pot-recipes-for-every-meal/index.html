Plant-Based One-Pot Recipes for Every Meal


Cooking can often feel overwhelming, especially when you’re juggling a busy schedule or are looking to reduce your time in the kitchen. The good news is that plant-based cooking doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. One-pot meals are a game-changer, providing an easy, convenient way to create delicious, nutritious dishes that require minimal clean-up.

In this post, we’re exploring a variety of plant-based one-pot recipes that can be enjoyed at any time of the day. Whether you’re looking for a hearty breakfast, a filling lunch, or a satisfying dinner, these recipes offer something for every occasion. Let’s dive in and discover how you can make the most out of a single pot for every meal.

1. Savory Breakfast Scramble

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 1/2 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup firm tofu, crumbled
  • 1 tablespoon nutritional yeast
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onions and bell peppers, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Toss in the spinach and mushrooms and cook for another 3-4 minutes, allowing the spinach to wilt and the mushrooms to release their moisture.
  4. Add the crumbled tofu to the pot, breaking it up into small chunks.
  5. Sprinkle in the turmeric, garlic powder, nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper, stirring everything together to combine evenly.
  6. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally until the tofu is lightly browned and heated through.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve warm.

This tofu scramble is a fantastic plant-based breakfast option, full of protein and flavor. The tofu mimics scrambled eggs, while the turmeric adds a vibrant yellow color and an earthy flavor. You can switch up the veggies to suit your taste and enjoy a customizable breakfast every time.

2. Vibrant Vegetable and Chickpea Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 zucchinis, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes, until soft.
  2. Add the carrots, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes to the pot. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Stir in the chickpeas, vegetable broth, and diced tomatoes. Season with cumin, paprika, cinnamon, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Adjust seasoning if necessary and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This stew is perfect for any meal of the day. The combination of chickpeas and vegetables makes it both satisfying and nutritious. The spices, especially cinnamon and cumin, add warmth and depth, making this dish both comforting and exciting.

3. Lentil and Sweet Potato Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup dried lentils (red or green)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and grated ginger, cooking for an additional 2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Toss in the sweet potato cubes and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the lentils, vegetable broth, coconut milk, curry powder, turmeric, and cinnamon. Stir well to combine.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for 30-35 minutes, or until the lentils and sweet potatoes are tender.
  6. Season with salt and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This hearty and flavorful curry is rich in plant-based protein and fiber from the lentils and sweet potatoes. The coconut milk creates a creamy texture, while the curry powder and turmeric give it a comforting, aromatic flavor profile. This dish is a great way to enjoy a nourishing meal that is also easy to prepare.

4. One-Pot Vegan Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until the onion is soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the red bell pepper and cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in the kidney beans, black beans, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and vegetable broth. Season with chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the chili to thicken.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve hot with your favorite toppings like avocado, cilantro, or vegan cheese.

This one-pot vegan chili is packed with protein from the beans and is full of rich, smoky flavors from the spices. It’s a great option for a filling, satisfying meal that can easily be stored and reheated throughout the week.

5. Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced (such as cremini or button)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 3 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 1/4 cup white wine (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the mushrooms and cook for 5-7 minutes, until they release their moisture and begin to brown.
  2. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking for another 3 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice, letting it cook for 1-2 minutes until the rice is lightly toasted.
  4. Pour in the white wine (if using) and let it cook off, about 2 minutes.
  5. Gradually add the warm vegetable broth, one cup at a time, stirring constantly. Wait until most of the liquid is absorbed before adding more broth.
  6. Continue adding broth and stirring for about 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is creamy and tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper and garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

This creamy mushroom risotto is a comforting dish that showcases the depth of flavor mushrooms bring to the table. It’s a wonderful plant-based dish that doesn’t compromise on taste or texture, making it a perfect option for a cozy dinner.

6. One-Pot Vegan Pasta Primavera

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 12 oz whole-wheat pasta (or any pasta of your choice)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the cherry tomatoes, zucchini, and bell pepper. Sauté for 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are softened and the tomatoes have released their juices.
  2. Stir in the spinach and cook for an additional 2 minutes until wilted.
  3. Add the pasta, vegetable broth, nutritional yeast, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low.
  4. Cover and let simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the pasta is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary, then serve hot.

This one-pot vegan pasta primavera is a colorful and flavorful dish, filled with fresh vegetables and a cheesy flavor from the nutritional yeast. It’s a quick and easy way to enjoy a satisfying pasta dish without the hassle of multiple pots and pans.

Conclusion

Plant-based one-pot meals are an excellent way to create wholesome, delicious dishes that are both simple and satisfying. Whether you’re preparing a hearty breakfast, a nourishing lunch, or a filling dinner, these recipes show just how versatile and easy it can be to cook plant-based meals in a single pot.

These recipes can be adapted to suit your personal taste, and the variety of vegetables, grains, and legumes used ensures that you’re getting a balanced and nutritious meal every time. Embrace the simplicity of one-pot cooking and enjoy the ease of having fewer dishes to clean while still making incredible, flavorful meals. Happy cooking!
