5 Delicious and Healthy Rice Casserole Recipes


Rice casseroles are a classic comfort food, perfect for family dinners, meal prepping, or even bringing to a potluck. They are versatile, easy to prepare, and can be made ahead of time, which makes them a go-to dish for many home cooks. The best part? You can load them with nutritious ingredients and enjoy a wholesome, balanced meal in a single dish. Whether you prefer a meat-based casserole, a vegetarian option, or a lighter version, there’s a rice casserole for everyone.

Here, we’ll explore five delicious and healthy rice casserole recipes that are not only packed with flavor but also good for your body. Each recipe incorporates whole, nutritious ingredients, so you can feel good about what you’re serving to your loved ones.

1. Chicken and Vegetable Rice Casserole

This chicken and vegetable rice casserole is a protein-packed meal that combines tender chicken, brown rice, and a variety of colorful vegetables. It’s the perfect way to sneak in some extra servings of veggies while satisfying your hunger.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt (for creaminess)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced chicken breasts and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.

  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same skillet, add the diced onion, bell pepper, and zucchini. Sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the peas and cook for another 2 minutes.

  4. Prepare the Casserole Mix: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked rice, sautéed vegetables, chicken, chicken broth, Greek yogurt, garlic powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.

  5. Bake the Casserole: Transfer the mixture into a greased baking dish. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese on top.

  6. Bake: Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Serve: Let the casserole cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish is great on its own or served with a simple salad for extra greens.

2. Spinach and Mushroom Rice Casserole

If you’re craving a vegetarian option, this spinach and mushroom rice casserole is an excellent choice. Loaded with hearty vegetables and earthy mushrooms, this casserole is not only flavorful but also packed with nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked brown rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup low-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Add the mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and become golden brown, about 5 minutes. Stir in the spinach and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes.

  3. Prepare the Casserole Mix: In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked rice, sautéed vegetables, milk, Parmesan cheese, ricotta cheese, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Stir until the mixture is well combined.

  4. Transfer to Baking Dish: Spoon the rice mixture into a greased baking dish and spread it out evenly.

  5. Bake: Cover the dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. After 20 minutes, remove the foil and bake for another 10 minutes to allow the top to brown slightly.

  6. Serve: Let the casserole rest for a few minutes before serving. This dish pairs wonderfully with a side of roasted vegetables or a fresh tomato salad.

3. Turkey and Sweet Potato Rice Casserole

This turkey and sweet potato rice casserole is a perfect fall dish that combines lean turkey, nutrient-packed sweet potatoes, and hearty rice. It’s filling, healthy, and full of flavor, making it an ideal meal for a cozy evening.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked quinoa or brown rice
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cook the Sweet Potatoes: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the cubed sweet potatoes and cook until tender, about 10 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  3. Brown the Turkey: In a large skillet, heat a little olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground turkey and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon as it cooks.

  4. Sauté the Aromatics: Add the diced onion and garlic to the skillet and sauté until softened, about 3 minutes. Stir in the smoked paprika, cumin, and cinnamon, and cook for another minute.

  5. Assemble the Casserole: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa or rice, ground turkey mixture, cooked sweet potatoes, chicken broth, cilantro, salt, and pepper. Stir everything together.

  6. Bake the Casserole: Transfer the mixture into a greased baking dish and spread it evenly. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes to allow the top to brown.

  7. Serve: Let the casserole cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish pairs nicely with a side of greens or a refreshing cucumber salad.

4. Broccoli and Cheddar Rice Casserole

This classic broccoli and cheddar rice casserole is a comforting and wholesome dish that will please even the pickiest eaters. It’s filled with the goodness of broccoli and the creamy richness of cheddar, making it a family favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup low-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Cook the Broccoli: Steam or blanch the broccoli florets until they are just tender, about 3 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  3. Prepare the Casserole Mix: In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked rice, steamed broccoli, shredded cheddar cheese, milk, Greek yogurt, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir until well combined.

  4. Assemble the Casserole: Transfer the rice mixture into a greased baking dish. If using breadcrumbs, sprinkle them over the top for added crunch.

  5. Bake: Cover the casserole with foil and bake for 20 minutes. After 20 minutes, remove the foil and bake for another 10 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  6. Serve: Let the casserole sit for a few minutes before serving. It’s the perfect side dish for any meal or a hearty main dish when paired with a fresh salad.

5. Tuna and Rice Casserole

For a quick and satisfying meal, this tuna and rice casserole is a great option. It’s packed with protein from the tuna and is made creamy with a combination of low-fat sour cream and cheese. Plus, it’s a one-pan meal that’s ready in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked white or brown rice
  • 2 cans (5 oz each) tuna in water, drained
  • 1 cup low-fat sour cream
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/4 cup chopped onion
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Casserole Mix: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked rice, drained tuna, sour cream, peas, chopped onion, garlic powder, shredded cheddar cheese, salt, and pepper. Stir until the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. Assemble the Casserole: Transfer the mixture into a greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  4. Bake: Cover the dish with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, until the top is golden and bubbly.

  5. Serve: Allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before serving. It’s great on its own or paired with a side of steamed vegetables.

These five healthy rice casserole recipes offer a variety of flavors, textures, and nutrients to suit every taste and dietary need. Whether you’re in the mood for something hearty, light, or vegetarian, there’s a casserole recipe here that will satisfy your cravings while nourishing your body. Try them out today and enjoy the comfort of a homemade, wholesome meal!
