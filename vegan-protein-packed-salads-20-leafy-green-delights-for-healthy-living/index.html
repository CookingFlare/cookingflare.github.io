Vegan Protein-Packed Salads: 20 Leafy Green Delights for Healthy Living


Do you want to enjoy a delicious and nutritious salad that will make your taste buds dance while providing your body with the protein it needs? Embracing plant-based protein sources is the way to go, especially if you’re adhering to a vegan lifestyle. At the same time, indulging in a healthy dose of leafy greens ensures that your body gets the nutrients it requires while dropping the pounds. Check out this list of 20 leafy green delights that will satisfy your tastebuds and fuel your overall well-being:

  1. Quinoa, Avocado, and Spinach Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, uncooked
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 ripe avocado, cubed
  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the quinoa and 2 cups of water to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked. Fluff with a fork and let it cool.

  2. In a large salad bowl, mix the cooked quinoa, chickpeas, avocado, and baby spinach.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

  4. Pour the dressing over the salad and mix well to incorporate the flavors.

  5. Escarole and White Bean Salad

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups chopped escarole
  • 1 can white kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes (oil-packed)
  • 1/2 cup sliced pitted ripe olives
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine escarole, white kidney beans, sun-dried tomatoes, and olives.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, garlic, salt, and pepper.

  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and mix well.

  4. Agedashi Tofu Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup plus 2 tbsp potato starch
  • 1 cup plus 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 2 cups thinly sliced napa cabbage
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red cabbage
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red bell pepper
  • 1 cup finely chopped seaweed
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green onions

Directions:

  1. To prepare the tofu, drain the water from the tofu block and press the tofu with a weighted plate for at least 30 minutes. After pressing, cut the tofu block into 1-inch cubes.

  2. In a shallow dish, mix potato starch and 1 cup of water to create a thin batter. Dip each tofu cube into the batter, ensuring an even coating.

  3. In another shallow dish, mix 2 tbsp potato starch and the remaining 2 tbsp water to create a thicker batter. Carefully coat the tofu cubes with the thick batter and set them aside.

  4. In a saucepan over medium heat, heat 1 inch of oil. Fry the tofu cubes until golden brown. Drain the tofu on a paper towel-lined plate.

  5. In a small saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Cook for 2-3 minutes over medium heat.

  6. In a large bowl, mix the sliced napa cabbage, red cabbage, bell pepper, seaweed, and green onions.

  7. Arrange the tofu on top of the salad greens, spoon the sauce over the tofu, and enjoy.

  8. Vietnamese Rice Paper Salad

Ingredients:

  • 8 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, julienned
  • 2 medium zucchini, julienned
  • 1 cup shelled edamame
  • 2 cups shredded unripe papaya or jicama
  • 2 cups fresh bean sprouts
  • 1 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro leaves

Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder

Directions:

  1. Fill a large bowl with warm water. Submerge one rice paper wrapper at a time, allowing it to soften for about 10 seconds.
  2. Carefully remove the wrapper and place it on a clean, dry surface. In the center of the wrapper, arrange some carrot, red bell pepper, green bell pepper, zucchini, edamame, papaya or jicama, bean sprouts, and herbs.
  3. Fold the bottom of the wrapper over the filling, then fold in the sides and continue rolling until completely sealed.
  4. Repeat the process with the remaining rice paper wrappers and fillings.
  5. For the dressing, whisk together rice vinegar, lime juice, soy sauce, sugar, and garlic powder in a medium-sized bowl.
  6. Pour the dressing over the salad, and toss well to evenly coat all the ingredients.
