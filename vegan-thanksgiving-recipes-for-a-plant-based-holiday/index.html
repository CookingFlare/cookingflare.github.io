Vegan Thanksgiving Recipes for a Plant-Based Holiday


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with loved ones, celebrate the season, and share delicious food. For those embracing a vegan lifestyle, there’s no need to miss out on the comforting flavors and festive dishes traditionally associated with this holiday. A plant-based Thanksgiving offers an abundance of creative possibilities that showcase seasonal ingredients, vibrant flavors, and wholesome cooking techniques. Whether you’re hosting a full vegan feast or incorporating plant-based options into your menu, these recipes will impress your guests and satisfy every palate.

Appetizers That Set the Tone

Start your Thanksgiving celebration with appetizers that highlight fall flavors and get everyone excited for the meal ahead.

Savory Pumpkin Hummus

Pumpkin and Thanksgiving go hand-in-hand, making this savory pumpkin hummus an ideal starter. The creamy texture and rich, earthy flavor pair perfectly with veggies or seeded crackers.

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 cup cooked pumpkin puree
    • 1 can chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
    • 2 tbsp tahini
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • 1 garlic clove
    • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
    • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. In a food processor, combine pumpkin puree, chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, smoked paprika, and cumin.
    2. Blend until smooth, adding water a tablespoon at a time if the mixture is too thick.
    3. Season with salt and pepper, then transfer to a serving bowl.
    4. Garnish with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of smoked paprika.

Stuffed Mushrooms with Walnut Pesto

These bite-sized stuffed mushrooms are bursting with flavor, thanks to a rich walnut and herb pesto filling.

  • Ingredients:

    • 16 large button mushrooms
    • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
    • 1/2 cup walnuts (toasted)
    • 2 garlic cloves
    • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • Salt and pepper

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Remove mushroom stems and hollow out the caps slightly.
    2. Blend basil, walnuts, garlic, nutritional yeast, olive oil, salt, and pepper into a paste.
    3. Spoon the walnut pesto into each mushroom cap.
    4. Arrange mushrooms on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, until tender.

Show-Stopping Mains

Impress your guests with main courses that highlight the bounty of autumn, incorporating hearty and satisfying ingredients.

Lentil and Mushroom Loaf with Maple Glaze

This lentil and mushroom loaf is the ultimate comfort dish. Topped with a maple glaze, it sets the bar high for plant-based main courses.

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 cup cooked lentils
    • 2 cups sautéed mushrooms
    • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
    • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
    • 1 cup breadcrumbs
    • 2 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 5 tbsp water (vegan egg substitute)
    • 1 tsp thyme
    • Salt and pepper

    For the glaze:

    • 2 tbsp maple syrup
    • 1 tbsp tomato paste
    • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
    2. Combine lentils, mushrooms, onion, garlic, breadcrumbs, flaxseed mixture, thyme, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Mash and mix thoroughly.
    3. Press the mixture into the loaf pan and smooth out the top.
    4. Whisk together maple syrup, tomato paste, and apple cider vinegar for the glaze, then spread it evenly over the loaf.
    5. Bake for 40-50 minutes, allowing the glaze to caramelize. Let cool for 15 minutes before serving.

Stuffed Acorn Squash with Quinoa and Cranberries

This festive stuffed acorn squash is a colorful and nutrient-packed centerpiece perfect for Thanksgiving.

  • Ingredients:

    • 3 acorn squashes (halved and seeds removed)
    • 1 cup cooked quinoa
    • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
    • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
    • 1 small apple (diced)
    • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
    • Olive oil, salt, and pepper

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush squash halves with olive oil, sprinkle with salt and pepper, and bake cut-side down for 25 minutes.
    2. While the squash is baking, mix quinoa with cranberries, pecans, apple, and cinnamon in a bowl.
    3. Flip squash halves, fill each with the quinoa mixture, and bake for an additional 15 minutes.

Sides to Complete the Feast

No Thanksgiving table is complete without an array of sides. From creamy classics to fresh and vibrant dishes, these recipes complement your main courses beautifully.

Garlic Mashed Cauliflower

A lighter, plant-based twist on traditional mashed potatoes, this garlic mashed cauliflower is just as creamy and satisfying.

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 large head of cauliflower (chopped into florets)
    • 2 garlic cloves
    • 2 tbsp vegan butter
    • Salt and pepper

  • Instructions:

    1. Steam cauliflower and garlic until tender, about 10 minutes.
    2. Blend cauliflower and garlic with vegan butter, salt, and pepper until smooth.

Maple-Glazed Roasted Brussels Sprouts

These Brussels sprouts caramelize beautifully in the oven, becoming sweet and crispy.

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 lb Brussels sprouts (halved)
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 tbsp maple syrup
    • Salt and pepper

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, maple syrup, salt, and pepper.
    2. Spread sprouts on a baking sheet and roast for 25 minutes, stirring halfway through.

Decadent Desserts

After a hearty meal, treat your guests to desserts that hit the right balance of indulgence and seasonal flavor.

Vegan Pumpkin Pie

A Thanksgiving staple, this vegan pumpkin pie is made with a rich coconut milk filling and a crisp crust.

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 pre-made vegan pie crust
    • 1 can pumpkin puree
    • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
    • 2/3 cup brown sugar
    • 1/4 cup cornstarch
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
    • 1/4 tsp cloves

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Whisk together all filling ingredients until smooth.
    2. Pour filling into pie crust and smooth out the top.
    3. Bake for 45 minutes, until the filling is firm at the edges but slightly jiggles in the center. Cool before serving.

Apple Crisp with Oatmeal Crumble

Warm, spiced apples topped with a golden oatmeal crumble make this dessert a crowd-pleaser.

  • Ingredients:

    • 6 apples (peeled, cored, and sliced)
    • 1 tbsp lemon juice
    • 1 tsp cinnamon
    • 1/2 cup old-fashioned oats
    • 1/2 cup flour
    • 1/3 cup brown sugar
    • 1/4 cup vegan butter (melted)

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Toss apples with lemon juice and cinnamon in a baking dish.
    2. Mix oats, flour, sugar, and melted butter until crumbly.
    3. Spread the crumble topping over the apples and bake for 35 minutes, until golden brown.

Hosting Tips for a Memorable Vegan Thanksgiving

Beyond the recipes, there are some thoughtful ways to ensure your Thanksgiving celebration is a success:

  1. Plan the Menu Early: Decide on your recipes ahead of time to avoid last-minute stress.
  2. Incorporate Seasonal Décor: Use pumpkins, gourds, and autumn leaves to create a cozy atmosphere.
  3. Serve Family-Style or Buffet-Style: Lay out dishes so everyone can help themselves to the array of options.
  4. Accommodate Food Allergies: Double-check for nuts, gluten, or other allergens and offer alternatives if needed.

A plant-based Thanksgiving isn’t about compromise; it’s about celebrating the ingredients and flavors that honor the season. Whether you’re new to vegan cooking or a seasoned pro, these recipes deliver the spirit of Thanksgiving with every bite. So gather around the table, express gratitude, and enjoy a feast that’s kind to animals, the planet, and your taste buds.
