Traditional Mexican Ceviche de Pescado


If you’re a fan of fresh and vibrant flavors, then you’re in for a treat with this traditional Mexican Ceviche de Pescado recipe. Ceviche is a popular dish in Mexico, known for its zesty and refreshing taste, making it a perfect choice for a light and flavorful meal. This dish is made with raw fish that is marinated in citrus juices, which “cook” the fish, along with a mix of fresh vegetables and herbs. It’s a dish that’s bursting with color, flavor, and a hint of spice, making it a delightful addition to any summertime gathering or a refreshing appetizer for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh white fish fillets, such as tilapia or red snapper, cut into small cubes
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, seeded and diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortilla chips or tostadas, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a glass or ceramic bowl, combine the fish, lime juice, and lemon juice. Ensure that the fish is completely submerged in the citrus juices. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or until the fish is opaque and “cooked” through.

  2. Once the fish is ready, drain off the excess citrus juices. Add the chopped red onion, tomatoes, cucumber, cilantro, and jalapeño to the fish. Gently toss the mixture until the ingredients are well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Cover the ceviche and refrigerate for an additional 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. Serve the ceviche chilled, either in individual bowls or as a topping for tostadas or alongside tortilla chips. Garnish with extra cilantro and slices of lime for an extra burst of freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, consider adding a splash of hot sauce or a sprinkle of chili powder to the ceviche.
  • If you prefer a milder onion flavor, you can soak the chopped red onion in cold water for 10 minutes before adding it to the ceviche.
  • While traditional ceviche is made with white fish, you can also experiment with shrimp or mixed seafood for a different twist on this classic dish.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of jalapeño to suit your spice preference. You can leave the seeds in for a spicier kick or remove them for a milder flavor.

The Beauty of Ceviche

Ceviche is not just a dish; it’s a celebration of fresh and vibrant flavors. The tangy citrus juices combined with the freshness of the fish and the crispness of the vegetables create a symphony of tastes and textures that is simply irresistible. This traditional Mexican Ceviche de Pescado recipe is a wonderful way to experience the essence of Mexican cuisine and delight your taste buds with a burst of sunshine in every bite.

So, whether you’re hosting a gathering with friends or simply craving a light and flavorful dish, consider whipping up a batch of Ceviche de Pescado. It’s a dish that embodies the spirit of Mexico, where every bite tells a story of tradition, flavor, and the joy of good food. Enjoy!
