Homemade Strawberry Shortcake Ice Cream


Are you ready to elevate your ice cream game with a delicious homemade treat that combines the classic flavors of strawberry shortcake with the creamy goodness of ice cream? Look no further! This homemade strawberry shortcake ice cream recipe is a delightful fusion of sweet, tangy, and creamy flavors that will leave your taste buds craving for more.

Ingredients

For the Strawberry Swirl:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice

For the Shortcake Crumble:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Ice Cream Base:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

Strawberry Swirl:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the sliced strawberries, granulated sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the strawberries have softened and the mixture has thickened, about 10-15 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, blend the mixture until smooth. Set aside.

Shortcake Crumble:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold cubed butter and using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the dry ingredients until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk the egg and vanilla extract together, then add it to the flour mixture. Stir until the dough comes together.
  5. Spread the dough onto the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown. Let it cool completely, then break into small crumbles.

Ice Cream Base:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and vanilla extract until the sugar has dissolved.
  2. Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  3. Once the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency, gently fold in the shortcake crumbles.
  4. Transfer half of the ice cream into a freezer-safe container, then drizzle half of the strawberry swirl on top. Repeat with the remaining ice cream and strawberry swirl, swirling gently with a knife or skewer to create a marbled effect.
  5. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

Serving

Scoop the homemade strawberry shortcake ice cream into bowls or cones and enjoy the delightful combination of creamy ice cream, tangy strawberry swirl, and buttery shortcake crumbles. This delightful treat is perfect for hot summer days or any time you’re craving a taste of nostalgia with a unique twist.

Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply treating yourself to a special dessert, this homemade strawberry shortcake ice cream is sure to impress. Get ready to savor every spoonful of this delightful frozen delight that captures the essence of a classic strawberry shortcake in every bite.

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients, put on your apron, and get ready to create a homemade strawberry shortcake ice cream that will be the highlight of any occasion!
