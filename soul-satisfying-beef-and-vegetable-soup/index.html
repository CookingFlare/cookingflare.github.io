Soul-Satisfying Beef and Vegetable Soup


There’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of homemade soup to comfort and nourish the soul. And when it comes to comfort food, few things can rival the hearty goodness of a delicious beef and vegetable soup. Packed with tender chunks of beef, vibrant vegetables, and flavorful broth, this soul-satisfying soup is perfect for those chilly evenings or when you simply need a comforting pick-me-up. In this recipe, I’ll share with you my tried and tested method for creating a bowl of pure comfort.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup corn kernels, fresh or frozen
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. This will help develop a rich flavor in the soup. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced carrots, celery, green beans, and corn to the pot. Stir to combine and cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the vegetables to soften slightly.
  4. Return the browned beef to the pot and pour in the beef broth and water. Add the bay leaf and dried thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer uncovered for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.
  6. Once the soup is ready, remove the bay leaf and discard. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  7. Ladle the soul-satisfying beef and vegetable soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley for a touch of brightness and freshness.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy the comforting flavors that will warm your heart and soul.

Tips and Variations

  • For added depth of flavor, you can brown the beef stew meat in batches instead of all at once. This will ensure each piece gets a nice sear and develops a rich, caramelized crust.
  • Feel free to customize this soup with your favorite vegetables. You can add in some diced potatoes, peas, or even mushrooms for extra texture and flavor.
  • If you prefer a thicker soup, you can mix a tablespoon of cornstarch with a little water and stir it into the soup during the last few minutes of cooking. This will help thicken the broth slightly.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat on the stovetop or in the microwave for a quick and comforting meal.

Conclusion

There’s something truly special about a bowl of soul-satisfying beef and vegetable soup. It warms you from the inside out, nourishes your body, and brings a sense of comfort and contentment. With this recipe, you can easily create your own pot of goodness, packed with tender beef, vibrant vegetables, and a flavorful broth. So grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to experience the magic of this comforting soup. Your taste buds and soul will thank you. Enjoy!
