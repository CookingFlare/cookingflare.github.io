Homemade Ketchup Recipe: A Tangy Twist for Your Fries


Who doesn’t love a good, tangy ketchup to dip their fries in? While store-bought ketchup is convenient, there’s something special about making your own homemade ketchup. Not only can you customize the flavors to suit your taste, but you also know exactly what goes into it. So, if you’re ready to elevate your fries to a whole new level, let’s dive into this homemade ketchup recipe that will surely become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tomatoes: Begin by draining the whole peeled tomatoes and reserving the liquid. Then, roughly chop the tomatoes and set them aside.

  2. Cook the Onions: In a large saucepan, cook the chopped onions over medium heat until they become translucent, about 5 minutes.

  3. Add the Tomatoes and Simmer: Add the chopped tomatoes to the saucepan with the onions. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. The tomatoes should start to break down and become saucy.

  4. Create the Flavor Base: Pour in the reserved tomato liquid and apple cider vinegar. Add the brown sugar, salt, black pepper, ground cloves, ground allspice, celery seeds, ground mustard, and cayenne pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Simmer and Stir: Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for 1 to 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally. The ketchup will thicken, and the flavors will meld together beautifully.

  6. Blend the Mixture: Once the ketchup has thickened, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly. Then, using a blender or food processor, blend the mixture until smooth. Be cautious as the mixture will be hot.

  7. Strain (Optional): If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can strain the ketchup through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any remaining seeds or skin.

  8. Cool and Store: Allow the homemade ketchup to cool completely before transferring it to airtight containers. Store the ketchup in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

The Perfect Pairing

This homemade ketchup is not just for fries! It adds a zesty kick to burgers, chicken tenders, and even meatloaf. You can also use it as a base for barbecue sauces or cocktail sauce. Get creative and let this tangy condiment elevate your favorite dishes.

Final Thoughts

Making your own homemade ketchup is a rewarding process that allows you to savor the flavors of fresh, quality ingredients. Plus, the satisfaction of knowing that you crafted this tangy condiment from scratch is truly unbeatable. So, the next time you’re craving some fries, whip up a batch of this homemade ketchup and take your dipping experience to a whole new level.
