Baking Bliss: How to Make French Baguettes at Home


Welcome to Baking Bliss, where we explore the art of baking and bring you delightful recipes to try at home. In today’s post, we’re going to dive into the world of French baguettes. There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked bread, and with this recipe, you can recreate that experience in your own kitchen. So put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 500g strong bread flour
  • 10g salt
  • 10g sugar
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 350ml warm water

Instructions

  1. Preparation: Begin by preheating your oven to 230°C (450°F) and placing a baking stone or an overturned baking sheet on the middle rack. This will help create that classic crunchy crust.

  2. Mixing the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Make a well in the center and gradually pour in the warm water. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a shaggy dough forms.

  3. Kneading: Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. This step is crucial as it helps develop the gluten, giving the baguette its characteristic chewy texture.

  4. First Rise: Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm spot for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Shaping the Baguettes: Once the dough has risen, gently deflate it by pushing down with your fingertips. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into three equal portions. Shape each portion into a long, thin loaf, about 35cm (14 inches) in length. Place the shaped baguettes onto a lightly greased or parchment-lined baking sheet.

  6. Second Rise: Cover the shaped baguettes with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30 minutes to an hour. During this time, they will puff up slightly and develop more flavor.

  7. Scoring the Baguettes: Just before baking, take a sharp knife or a bread lame and make three to five diagonal slashes on the surface of each baguette. This not only adds an aesthetic touch but also allows the bread to expand and rise properly in the oven.

  8. Baking: Carefully transfer the baking sheet with the baguettes onto the preheated baking stone or baking sheet in the oven. Quickly spritz some water onto the sides of the oven or place a tray of ice cubes on the bottom to create steam. This steam will help create a crusty exterior. Bake the baguettes for about 20-25 minutes, or until they turn golden brown.

  9. Cooling: Once baked, remove the baguettes from the oven and transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. This step is essential as it allows the bread to properly set and develop that satisfying crunch in the crust.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve successfully made your own French baguettes at home. Now, all that’s left to do is to slice into that crusty exterior and enjoy the soft, chewy interior. These baguettes are perfect for sandwiches, to accompany soups, or even just to savor on their own. So, go ahead and impress your family and friends with your newfound baking skills. Happy baking!

Remember, the art of baking takes practice, so don’t be discouraged if your first attempt isn’t perfect. Keep experimenting and tweaking the recipe to find your own personal baguette bliss. Stay tuned for more baking adventures here at Baking Bliss!

Note: This recipe yields three baguettes, but feel free to adjust the quantities if you prefer to make fewer or more.
