Exploring Korean Cuisine: 7 Must-Try Dishes for Beginners


Korean cuisine offers a vibrant and diverse range of flavors, textures, and cooking techniques that can be both exciting and intimidating for beginners. If you are new to Korean food, or if you want to expand your culinary horizons, exploring the traditional dishes of Korea is a fantastic place to start. From the savory richness of stews to the bold spice of kimchi, Korean dishes bring together a perfect balance of salty, sour, sweet, and umami flavors.

In this guide, we’ll walk through seven iconic dishes from Korea that are not only delicious but also showcase the essence of the country’s food culture. Whether you’re at a local Korean restaurant, trying your hand at cooking at home, or simply curious about what to try next time you order takeout, these dishes should be on your radar.

1. Kimchi (김치) – The Heart of Korean Cuisine

No discussion of Korean food is complete without kimchi. Kimchi is a fermented vegetable dish, most commonly made with napa cabbage or radishes, that is often spiced with gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), garlic, ginger, and salted seafood, such as fermented shrimp or fish sauce. The fermentation process gives kimchi its distinctive tangy flavor, and the spiciness adds a nice kick.

Kimchi is served as a side dish, or banchan, at nearly every Korean meal, and its presence is almost symbolic of Korean cuisine. You can find it served with grilled meats, rice, or even in soups and stews. Kimchi can be eaten fresh, but it’s also common to let it ferment over time to develop a deeper flavor profile.

For beginners, the most approachable version to try is baechu kimchi (napa cabbage kimchi), which is crunchy, spicy, and a little bit sour. It’s often the gateway to more advanced kimchi variations.

How to Enjoy Kimchi:

  • As a side dish: Enjoy kimchi alongside grilled meat, rice, or stir-fried dishes.
  • In a stew: Try kimchi jjigae, a hearty kimchi stew made with tofu, pork, and vegetables.
  • Kimchi fried rice: Stir-fry leftover rice with kimchi, vegetables, and a fried egg for a quick and flavorful meal.

2. Bibimbap (비빔밥) – The Colorful Mixed Rice Bowl

Bibimbap is another iconic Korean dish known for its vibrant colors and variety of ingredients. It’s a bowl of steamed rice topped with a variety of sautéed vegetables, sliced meat (usually beef), a fried egg, and a dollop of gochujang (Korean chili paste). The beauty of bibimbap lies in its customization—each topping brings its own texture and flavor to the dish.

The word “bibimbap” translates to “mixed rice,” and traditionally, the dish is meant to be mixed thoroughly before eating, combining all the elements into a deliciously satisfying meal. The hot stone version, dolsot bibimbap, adds an extra element with a crispy layer of rice that forms at the bottom of the stone bowl.

How to Enjoy Bibimbap:

  • As a complete meal: It’s a balanced dish with carbs, protein, and vegetables. It’s perfect for lunch or dinner.
  • Try different variations: Opt for vegetarian bibimbap or bibimbap with bulgogi for different flavors.

3. Bulgogi (불고기) – Marinated Beef Barbecue

Bulgogi is one of Korea’s most beloved dishes, made with thinly sliced beef marinated in a savory-sweet mixture of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and pepper. This marinade tenderizes the beef, giving it a slightly caramelized exterior when grilled. It’s often served with a bowl of steamed rice and a side of banchan.

The key to great bulgogi is the balance of flavors in the marinade, which should be both sweet and savory with a hint of umami from the soy sauce. While bulgogi can be grilled, it is often cooked on a stovetop or even in a hotpot, especially when cooking at home.

How to Enjoy Bulgogi:

  • With rice: Serve it over rice for a simple yet satisfying meal.
  • In lettuce wraps: For a more interactive meal, wrap bulgogi in fresh lettuce leaves with some garlic, green onion, and gochujang.
  • In a sandwich: For a fusion twist, try bulgogi as a filling in a sandwich or a Korean-style burger.

4. Tteokbokki (떡볶이) – Spicy Rice Cakes

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food that is both comforting and spicy. It features chewy rice cakes, or tteok, that are simmered in a sweet and spicy sauce made from gochujang, sugar, and other seasonings. Often, fish cakes (odeng) are added to the dish, and it’s served with a sprinkling of sesame seeds and green onions.

The texture of the rice cakes, which are soft and chewy, perfectly contrasts with the heat of the sauce. This dish is loved by both children and adults in Korea and is a common comfort food for late-night snacks.

How to Enjoy Tteokbokki:

  • As a snack: Ideal for munching on when you’re craving something savory and spicy.
  • With fish cakes: Add odeng to your tteokbokki for a fuller, heartier meal.
  • Pair with a cold drink: Due to its spiciness, pairing tteokbokki with a refreshing cold beverage like iced tea or soda is a great choice.

5. Jjigae (찌개) – Hearty Korean Stews

Korean stews, or jjigae, are perfect for colder months and provide a warming and comforting meal. There are many different types of jjigae, but one of the most popular is kimchi jjigae, made with fermented kimchi, tofu, vegetables, and pork or beef. Another famous jjigae is doenjang jjigae, made with fermented soybean paste (doenjang) and often served with tofu, vegetables, and sometimes meat.

These stews are hearty, flavorful, and perfect for sharing with family or friends. They are typically enjoyed with steamed rice and banchan on the side.

How to Enjoy Jjigae:

  • With rice: Jjigae is always enjoyed with a bowl of rice, which helps to balance the richness of the stew.
  • Mix it up: Try different jjigae varieties to explore the diverse flavors that Korean cuisine has to offer.
  • As a side dish: Jjigae can be served as a shared dish at the center of the table for a family-style meal.

6. Samgyeopsal (삼겹살) – Grilled Pork Belly

Samgyeopsal is one of the most beloved Korean BBQ dishes. It consists of thick slices of pork belly, which are grilled at the table and then eaten with a variety of dipping sauces, fresh vegetables, and ssamjang (a thick, spicy paste). The grilled pork is usually served with lettuce leaves, which you use to wrap the meat along with garlic, green onions, and a bit of kimchi.

The key to samgyeopsal is the interactive grilling process. It’s a social meal, often enjoyed with a group of friends or family. The crispy edges of the grilled pork belly are irresistible, and the combination of flavors from the various side dishes adds an extra layer of depth.

How to Enjoy Samgyeopsal:

  • Grill it yourself: Samgyeopsal is typically cooked on a grill, making it a fun, interactive meal.
  • Create wraps: Use lettuce leaves to wrap the grilled pork with garlic, green onions, and ssamjang for a delicious bite.
  • With soju or beer: Samgyeopsal pairs well with traditional Korean alcoholic drinks like soju or beer.

7. Hwe (회) – Korean-Style Sashimi

For those who enjoy raw fish, hwe (Korean sashimi) is a must-try dish. Similar to Japanese sashimi, hwe consists of thinly sliced fresh fish, typically served with a dipping sauce made of gochujang, soy sauce, and vinegar. The fish is usually served alongside vegetables like sliced radishes and sometimes garnished with sesame seeds.

In Korea, hwe is often enjoyed with a glass of makgeolli, a traditional Korean rice wine, which complements the freshness of the fish. While hwe is not as widely known outside of Korea as sushi, it holds an important place in Korean culinary traditions.

How to Enjoy Hwe:

  • With dipping sauce: Dip the fish slices in a flavorful sauce for a zesty and spicy bite.
  • Pair with rice: Hwe is often enjoyed with a bowl of steamed rice to balance the freshness of the fish.
  • As a shared dish: Hwe is typically shared among a group, allowing everyone to sample different types of fish.

Final Thoughts

Korean cuisine is an exciting adventure into a world of bold flavors, diverse ingredients, and unique cooking techniques. Whether you’re savoring the richness of bulgogi, enjoying the heat of kimchi, or diving into the heartiness of jjigae, Korean food offers something for everyone. Each dish reflects Korea’s long history, cultural heritage, and the harmonious balance of flavors that the country is famous for.

By trying these seven dishes, you’ll have a solid foundation in Korean cuisine. So, whether you’re cooking at home or dining out, don’t hesitate to explore these classic dishes and discover the joy of Korean food for yourself. Happy eating!
