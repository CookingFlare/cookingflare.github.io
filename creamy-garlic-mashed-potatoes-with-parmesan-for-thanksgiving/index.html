Creamy Garlic Mashed Potatoes with Parmesan for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with family, sharing stories, and, of course, indulging in delicious food. Among the array of dishes that grace the holiday table, mashed potatoes often hold a special place in the hearts of many. This year, elevate your Thanksgiving feast with a creamy garlic mashed potato recipe that incorporates the rich flavor of Parmesan cheese. These mashed potatoes are not only creamy and comforting but also infused with a delightful garlicky kick that will have your guests coming back for more.

The Perfect Thanksgiving Side Dish

Mashed potatoes are a classic side dish that pairs perfectly with turkey, gravy, and all the other traditional Thanksgiving offerings. Their creamy texture and buttery flavor make them a staple on nearly every holiday table. However, if you want to take your mashed potatoes to the next level, adding roasted garlic and Parmesan cheese is a game changer. This combination brings depth of flavor and richness, making them irresistible.

Why Choose Garlic Mashed Potatoes?

Garlic mashed potatoes are an excellent choice for Thanksgiving for several reasons:

  1. Flavor Profile: The addition of garlic not only enhances the flavor but also adds a savory note that pairs beautifully with turkey and stuffing.
  2. Creamy Texture: The creamy nature of mashed potatoes provides a comforting element to the meal, making it feel warm and inviting.
  3. Versatility: These mashed potatoes can be served alongside any main dish, whether it’s a traditional turkey, ham, or a vegetarian option.
  4. Make-Ahead Option: This dish can be prepared in advance and reheated, allowing you to spend more time with your loved ones on the big day.

Ingredients

To create these creamy garlic mashed potatoes with Parmesan, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 3 pounds of russet or Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh chives or parsley, finely chopped (for garnish)

Choosing the Right Potatoes

When it comes to making the best mashed potatoes, the type of potato you choose is crucial. Russet potatoes are starchy and yield a fluffy texture, while Yukon Gold potatoes are creamier and have a buttery flavor. For the ultimate creamy garlic mashed potatoes, a combination of both is ideal. This blend provides the perfect balance of fluffiness and creaminess, making each bite a delight.

Preparing the Garlic

Roasting garlic elevates its flavor, transforming it from sharp and pungent to sweet and creamy. Here’s how to roast garlic for this recipe:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Slice the top off the head of garlic, exposing the cloves.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and wrap in aluminum foil.
  4. Roast in the oven for about 30-35 minutes, or until the cloves are soft and golden brown.
  5. Allow to cool slightly before squeezing the roasted garlic out of the skins.

Cooking the Potatoes

Once the garlic is roasted, it’s time to prepare the potatoes:

  1. Peel and Cut: Peel the potatoes and cut them into even-sized chunks. This helps them cook uniformly.
  2. Boil the Potatoes: Place the chopped potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold water. Add a generous pinch of salt. Bring to a boil and cook for about 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.
  3. Drain and Dry: Once cooked, drain the potatoes in a colander and return them to the pot. Allow them to steam for a couple of minutes to remove excess moisture.

Making the Mashed Potatoes

With the potatoes ready and the garlic roasted, it’s time to create the creamy mixture:

  1. Mash the Potatoes: Using a potato masher or ricer, mash the potatoes until they reach your desired consistency. For ultra-creamy potatoes, a ricer is recommended.
  2. Add the Butter and Cream: In a saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the heavy cream and warm it slightly. Pour this mixture over the mashed potatoes.
  3. Incorporate the Garlic: Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves into the potatoes, discarding the skins. Mix until well combined.
  4. Add Parmesan: Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese, ensuring it melts into the potatoes. This adds a rich, savory flavor that complements the garlic perfectly.
  5. Season: Taste your mashed potatoes and season with salt and pepper to your liking. Remember, Parmesan can add some saltiness, so adjust accordingly.

Serving Suggestions

To serve your creamy garlic mashed potatoes with Parmesan, transfer them to a large serving bowl. For an extra touch, consider the following garnishes:

  • Fresh Herbs: Sprinkle with chopped chives or parsley for a pop of color and freshness.
  • Additional Parmesan: Grate extra Parmesan on top for added flavor and presentation.
  • Drizzle of Olive Oil: A light drizzle of high-quality olive oil can enhance the richness and add a beautiful sheen.

Make-Ahead Tips

To make your Thanksgiving preparation easier, consider preparing the creamy garlic mashed potatoes in advance. Here’s how:

  1. Prepare Up to a Day Before: Follow the recipe up to the point of mixing in the cream, butter, and cheese. Allow the mashed potatoes to cool, then store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator.
  2. Reheat on Thanksgiving: On the day of your gathering, transfer the potatoes to a pot and reheat gently over low heat. You may need to add a splash of milk or cream to restore the creamy texture. Stir frequently to prevent sticking.
  3. Final Touches: Once heated through, mix in any additional seasonings, garnish, and serve warm.

Pairing Ideas

Creamy garlic mashed potatoes with Parmesan pair beautifully with a variety of Thanksgiving dishes. Consider serving them alongside:

  • Roast Turkey: The creamy potatoes complement the savory flavors of the turkey.
  • Gravy: A rich turkey or mushroom gravy poured over the mashed potatoes adds an extra layer of flavor.
  • Stuffing: The contrast of textures between the soft mashed potatoes and hearty stuffing creates a delightful experience.
  • Vegetable Sides: Roasted Brussels sprouts, green bean casserole, or honey-glazed carrots all make excellent companions.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, impress your family and friends with these creamy garlic mashed potatoes with Parmesan. They are a comforting, delicious, and easy-to-prepare side dish that will steal the show on your holiday table. With their rich flavor and creamy texture, these mashed potatoes are sure to become a new family favorite, creating memories with every bite. So grab your ingredients, gather your loved ones in the kitchen, and savor the joy of cooking together this holiday season. Happy Thanksgiving!
