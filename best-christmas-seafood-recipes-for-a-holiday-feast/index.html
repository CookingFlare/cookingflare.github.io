Best Christmas Seafood Recipes for a Holiday Feast


The magic of Christmas isn’t complete without a delicious feast that brings everyone together. This year, why not bring the ocean’s bounty to your holiday table? Seafood offers a luxurious, flavorful alternative to traditional Christmas fare, creating an unforgettable dining experience. From the delicate sweetness of scallops to the rich taste of lobster, these dishes combine classic and innovative techniques to impress your guests.

In this expansive guide, you’ll find a diverse selection of Christmas seafood recipes, perfect for starters, mains, and sides. Each recipe has been carefully selected for its festive appeal, ease of preparation, and outstanding flavors. Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or an intimate dinner, these dishes will elevate your holiday feast.

Elegance on a Plate: Starter Recipes to Awaken the Palate

1. Herb-Crusted Baked Scallops with Lemon Butter

Scallops are tender, subtly sweet, and quick to cook, making them an ideal holiday starter. This herb crust adds a crispy texture, while lemon butter imparts bright citrus notes.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large sea scallops
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp freshly chopped parsley
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Zest of one lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for garnish

Method:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pat the scallops dry and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Combine panko, parsley, garlic powder, and lemon zest in a bowl.
  4. Brush each scallop with melted butter, then coat with the breadcrumb mixture.
  5. Place scallops on a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, until golden and cooked through.
  6. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.

This dish pairs wonderfully with a crisp Sauvignon Blanc or a sparkling Prosecco, setting the seasonal tone right from the start.

2. Smoked Salmon and Avocado Canapés

A sophisticated yet simple appetizer, these canapés can be assembled ahead of time, perfect for busyholiday entertaining.

Ingredients:

  • 200g smoked salmon slices
  • 2 ripe avocados, mashed
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped dill
  • Crackers or small slices of rye bread
  • Capers and thinly sliced red onion for garnish
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Method:

  1. Mix mashed avocado with lemon juice, dill, and black pepper.
  2. Spread a generous layer on each cracker or bread slice.
  3. Top with a folded slice of smoked salmon.
  4. Garnish with capers and a sliver of red onion.
  5. Keep chilled until serving.

The creamy avocado balances the smoky salmon perfectly, offering a bite that’s both fresh and indulgent.

Main Course Marvels: Seafood Dishes to Steal the Show

3. Classic Lobster Thermidor

For a Christmas centerpiece dish that screams luxury, Lobster Thermidor is a timeless choice. Rich, creamy, and flamed to perfection, it brings French elegance to your holiday table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 live lobsters (1 ½ lbs each)
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 shallot, finely diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • ½ cup grated Gruyère cheese
  • Salt and cayenne pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Method:

  1. Boil lobsters for about 8 minutes until partially cooked. Remove meat from shells, reserving the shells.
  2. Chop lobster meat and set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, melt butter and sauté shallots & garlic until translucent.
  4. Add white wine and reduce by half.
  5. Stir in mustard and cream, simmer gentle until thickened.
  6. Add lobster meat, salt, and a pinch of cayenne pepper.
  7. Spoon the mixture back into the lobster shells, top with Gruyère.
  8. Place under a broiler or in a hot oven until cheese bubbles and browns.
  9. Garnish with parsley and serve hot.

An opulent main dish like this pairs beautifully with lightly oaked Chardonnay or a dry Champagne to cut through the richness.

4. Mediterranean Baked Cod with Cherry Tomatoes and Olives

If you want a lighter but still deeply flavorful main course, baked cod with Mediterranean flavors makes a stunning statement.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cod fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano, chopped
  • Juice of one lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Lay cod fillets in a baking dish. Scatter tomatoes, olives, and garlic around.
  3. Drizzle olive oil and lemon juice on top, season with salt, pepper, and oregano.
  4. Bake uncovered for 20-25 minutes, until fish is opaque and flakes easily.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

The acidity of the tomatoes and lemon lifts the tender white fish, while olives add a punchy depth. This dish complements a crisp Vermentino or a citrusy Grüner Veltliner.

5. Spiced Prawn and Coconut Curry

Bring a comforting warmth and touch of the exotic to the table with this vibrant prawn curry. The coconut milk softens the spices and creates a luscious sauce perfect for serving over jasmine rice or crusty bread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large prawns, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tbsp curry powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 fresh red chili, sliced (optional)
  • Juice of half a lime
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Method:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and spices, cooking until fragrant.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add prawns and cook for 4-5 minutes or until pink and cooked through.
  5. Stir in lime juice and adjust seasoning with salt.
  6. Garnish with cilantro and chili slices.

This aromatic curry adds a festive warmth to your meal and pairs wonderfully with a Riesling or Gewürztraminer with a hint of sweetness to balance the spices.

Celebratory Sides to Complement Your Seafood Feast

6. Garlic Butter Roasted Asparagus

Asparagus is a classic holiday vegetable, and here it’s roasted with garlic butter to bring out nuttiness and fragrance.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Zest of one lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss asparagus with melted butter and garlic.
  3. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  4. Roast for 12-15 minutes until tender and slightly caramelized.
  5. Sprinkle lemon zest, salt, and pepper before serving.

This side adds vibrant color and a fresh note to your richer seafood mains.

7. Creamy Parmesan Risotto

Risotto is an indulgent side dish that pairs expertly with seafood’s texture and flavors, forming a comforting base without overpowering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, warmed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Method:

  1. Heat olive oil over medium heat, sauté onion until translucent.
  2. Add Arborio rice, stir to coat grains for 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in white wine, stirring until absorbed.
  4. Add broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently until absorbed before adding more.
  5. Continue until rice is creamy and al dente (about 18 minutes).
  6. Remove from heat and stir in butter and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Season with salt and pepper.

Serve spooned beside your lobster or cod for a complete, festive plate.

Decadent Desserts to Complete the Celebration

8. Lemon and White Chocolate Mousse

Light, airy, and smooth, this mousse is a perfect conclusion after a seafood-rich meal. The citrus cuts through the creaminess for a refreshing touch.

Ingredients:

  • 150g white chocolate, chopped
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 200ml double cream
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp sugar

Method:

  1. Melt the white chocolate gently over a double boiler.
  2. Whip cream to soft peaks.
  3. Beat egg whites with sugar until stiff peaks form.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk egg yolks with lemon juice and zest.
  5. Combine melted chocolate with yolk mixture.
  6. Fold in whipped cream, then gently fold in egg whites.
  7. Spoon into serving glasses and refrigerate at least 3 hours.

Top with fresh berries or a sprig of mint for added festivity.

Final Thoughts

Integrating seafood into your Christmas feast invites a range of textures and flavors that evoke celebration and warmth. The recipes outlined here—from elegant starters to show-stopping mains and indulgent desserts—offer something for every palate and skill level. The ocean’s gifts add a touch of luxury and refinement to your holiday, making it a feast your guests will remember for years to come.

Celebrate this Christmas with these seafood dishes that combine tradition and creativity, ensuring your holiday table dazzles with flavor and style. Cheers to a joyous and delicious holiday season!
