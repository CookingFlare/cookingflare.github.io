Dinner Harvest: Farm to Table Recipes for Evening Feasts

Welcome to Dinner Harvest, where we celebrate the bounty of the farm with recipes designed for unforgettable evening feasts. Our farm-to-table approach ensures that every dish bursts with the freshness and flavor of seasonal produce. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a wholesome meal, our collection of recipes will inspire you to create delicious dishes straight from the heart of the farm. Rustic Roasted Vegetable Tart Ingredients: 1 sheet of puff pastry 1 small eggplant, sliced 1 zucchini, sliced 1 red bell pepper, sliced 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced 1 red onion, sliced 2 garlic cloves, minced 2 tbsp olive oil 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese Fresh basil leaves for garnish Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the puff pastry sheet on a baking tray. In a bowl, toss the eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and red onion with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Arrange the seasoned vegetables on the puff pastry, leaving a border around the edges. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese over the vegetables. Fold the edges of the puff pastry over the vegetables to create a rustic tart. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown and the vegetables are tender. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving. Savory Stuffed Acorn Squash Ingredients: 2 acorn squashes, halved and seeded 1 cup quinoa, cooked 1/2 cup dried cranberries 1/2 cup pecans, chopped 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 2 tbsp maple syrup 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar 1 tsp cinnamon Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the acorn squash halves on a baking tray, cut side up. In a bowl, mix the cooked quinoa, dried cranberries, chopped pecans, parsley, maple syrup, balsamic vinegar, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Divide the quinoa mixture among the acorn squash halves, pressing gently to pack the filling. Cover the tray with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until the squash is tender and the filling is golden. Serve the stuffed acorn squash warm, garnished with additional parsley if desired. Harvest Chicken and Butternut Squash Skillet Ingredients: 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts 2 cups butternut squash, cubed 1 red onion, sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/2 cup chicken broth 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar 1 tbsp honey 1 tsp dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste Fresh parsley for garnish Instructions: Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, and dried thyme. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breasts to the skillet and cook for 6-8 minutes on each side until golden and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the butternut squash, red onion, and minced garlic. Cook for 5-6 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Pour in the chicken broth, balsamic vinegar, and honey, stirring to combine. Return the chicken to the skillet, nestling it among the vegetables. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until the flavors meld together. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.
