Simple Korean Braised Potatoes (Gamja Jorim) Recipe


Gamja Jorim (감자조림) is a traditional Korean side dish that features tender, flavorful potatoes braised in a savory-sweet sauce. This dish is commonly served as a banchan (side dish) at Korean meals, often accompanying rice and other vegetables. The best part? It’s incredibly easy to make, requires only a few simple ingredients, and comes together in no time at all. Whether you’re new to Korean cuisine or looking to expand your cooking repertoire, Gamja Jorim is a perfect dish to try.

In this post, I will walk you through a simple yet delicious recipe for Korean braised potatoes that you can recreate at home. Let’s dive into the ingredients, step-by-step instructions, tips, and variations for making this tasty side dish.

What is Gamja Jorim?

Gamja Jorim (감자조림) translates directly to “braised potatoes” in Korean. The dish typically involves simmering potatoes in a flavorful sauce made with soy sauce, sugar, garlic, and sesame oil. The potatoes absorb the sauce, becoming soft and tender while still holding their shape. The result is a harmonious balance of salty, sweet, and savory flavors.

Although Gamja Jorim is most often enjoyed as a banchan in Korean meals, it also works well as a side dish with grilled meats, barbecued foods, or any meal that needs a hearty, flavorful accompaniment.

Ingredients

For this recipe, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 3 medium potatoes (about 600 grams)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (preferably brown sugar for depth of flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • 2 green onions, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Ingredient Substitutes:

  • Potatoes: Russet potatoes or Yukon Gold potatoes work best for this dish, as they are starchy and hold their shape when braised. If you’re looking for a healthier option, you can also use sweet potatoes for a slightly different texture and flavor.

  • Soy Sauce: Use low-sodium soy sauce if you prefer a lighter sodium option, but regular soy sauce provides a more robust flavor.

  • Sesame Oil: The toasted sesame oil adds a nutty, aromatic flavor to the dish. Don’t skip it, as it is essential for creating the signature taste of Gamja Jorim.

  • Sugar: Brown sugar adds a deeper flavor, but white sugar can also be used. Some variations of this dish use honey for an extra layer of sweetness.

  • Water: If you want a thicker sauce, you can reduce the amount of water and allow the sauce to simmer until it thickens to your liking.

How to Make Simple Korean Braised Potatoes (Gamja Jorim)

Now that we have all our ingredients ready, let’s jump into the cooking process. The steps for making this simple and flavorful side dish are straightforward, but the key to great Gamja Jorim is in the braising technique and balancing the flavors.

Step 1: Peel and Slice the Potatoes

Start by peeling the potatoes using a vegetable peeler or knife. After peeling, cut the potatoes into bite-sized chunks. Aim for pieces that are roughly 1 to 1.5 inches in size, as this will allow them to cook evenly while absorbing the flavor of the sauce.

Step 2: Heat the Oil and Sauté Garlic

In a medium-sized skillet or frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and sauté it for about 1-2 minutes, or until it becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

Step 3: Brown the Potatoes

Add the chopped potatoes to the skillet with the garlic. Toss them gently to coat with the oil and garlic. Allow the potatoes to brown slightly, stirring occasionally. This step is essential as it adds depth of flavor to the dish. You want the potatoes to get a little crispy on the edges.

Step 4: Add the Braising Sauce

Once the potatoes are browned, add the soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir everything together until the potatoes are evenly coated in the sauce.

Step 5: Simmer the Potatoes

Add the water to the skillet and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Once it starts to bubble, reduce the heat to low. Cover the skillet with a lid and let the potatoes simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until they are fork-tender. Make sure to check the potatoes occasionally to ensure the sauce doesn’t reduce too much. If it does, you can add a little more water.

Step 6: Adjust the Sauce and Seasoning

After the potatoes are cooked through, remove the lid and let the sauce cook down a little if it’s still too watery. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if needed, adding a little more soy sauce, sugar, or salt to balance the flavors. The sauce should be rich, slightly sweet, and savory.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Once the potatoes are tender and the sauce has thickened, turn off the heat. Garnish the dish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for an extra touch of freshness and crunch. Serve the Gamja Jorim warm as part of a Korean meal or as a side dish with your favorite protein.

Tips for Making Perfect Gamja Jorim

  • Choose the Right Potatoes: As mentioned earlier, starchy potatoes like Russet or Yukon Gold are ideal for braising. They hold their shape well during the cooking process and absorb the sauce beautifully.

  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: If you’re making a large batch, it’s better to cook the potatoes in batches. Overcrowding the pan can prevent the potatoes from browning properly.

  • Simmer Gently: Be patient when braising the potatoes. Cooking them over low heat helps them absorb the flavors of the sauce and become tender without falling apart.

  • Adjust Sweetness and Saltiness: Depending on your taste, you can adjust the sugar and soy sauce to achieve the perfect balance of sweet and savory. Some people prefer a sweeter sauce, while others prefer a more savory profile.

  • Serve with Rice: Gamja Jorim pairs beautifully with steamed white rice. The sauce soaks into the rice, making each bite more flavorful.

Variations of Gamja Jorim

While the basic recipe for Gamja Jorim is simple, there are several variations that you can try depending on your taste preferences or the ingredients you have available.

Spicy Gamja Jorim

For a spicy version of this dish, you can add a tablespoon of gochujang (Korean red chili paste) or some red pepper flakes to the sauce. The heat from the spice complements the sweetness of the sauce and adds an extra layer of flavor.

Vegan Gamja Jorim

To make a vegan version of Gamja Jorim, simply swap out the sugar for a vegan alternative, such as maple syrup or agave nectar. Make sure to use plant-based sesame oil and soy sauce, which are typically vegan-friendly.

Gamja Jorim with Vegetables

You can also add other vegetables to your Gamja Jorim, such as carrots, onions, or bell peppers. These vegetables will absorb the flavors of the braising sauce and add more texture and color to the dish.

How to Store and Reheat Gamja Jorim

Gamja Jorim can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors often improve after a day or two, as the potatoes continue to soak up the braising sauce.

To reheat, simply heat the potatoes in a skillet over low heat, adding a splash of water if the sauce has thickened too much. You can also microwave the dish in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until heated through.

Conclusion

Gamja Jorim is a comforting and flavorful dish that showcases the essence of Korean home cooking. Its combination of savory, sweet, and nutty flavors makes it a perfect addition to any meal, and it’s so simple to prepare that you can easily make it on a busy weeknight. The tender potatoes, infused with the aromatic braising sauce, are sure to be a hit with your family and friends.

Give this recipe a try, and enjoy the delicious simplicity of this traditional Korean side dish. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, Gamja Jorim is a dish that will surely bring a little taste of Korea to your kitchen.
