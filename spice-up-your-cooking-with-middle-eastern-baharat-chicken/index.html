Spice Up Your Cooking with Middle Eastern Baharat Chicken


Are you tired of the same old chicken recipes? Looking to add a burst of flavor to your meals? Look no further than Middle Eastern Baharat Chicken! This aromatic and delicious dish will elevate your cooking to a whole new level. With a blend of spices that will awaken your taste buds, Baharat Chicken is a must-try for any food lover.

Ingredients

To make this tantalizing dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons baharat spice blend
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the chicken breasts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, baharat spice blend, salt, black pepper, cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Mix well to form a thick paste.

  3. Rub the spice paste all over the chicken breasts, making sure to coat them evenly. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight in the refrigerator for maximum flavor.

  4. Place the marinated chicken breasts in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

  5. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in tender and juicy meat.

  6. Serve the Baharat Chicken with a sprinkle of fresh parsley for a pop of color and added freshness. This dish pairs well with rice pilaf, couscous, or a fresh salad.

Why Baharat Chicken?

What sets Baharat Chicken apart from other chicken recipes is the unique blend of spices used in the marinade. Baharat, which translates to “spice” in Arabic, is a popular spice blend in Middle Eastern cuisine. It adds a rich and aromatic flavor to dishes, making them truly special.

The combination of warm spices like cumin, coriander, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg creates a symphony of flavors that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of the Middle East. The addition of paprika and lemon juice adds a tangy and vibrant element to the dish, balancing out the richness of the spices.

Tips for Success

  • Don’t be afraid to adjust the spice levels to suit your taste. If you prefer a milder flavor, reduce the amount of baharat spice blend. On the other hand, if you like it spicy, feel free to add a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili powder.

  • For the best results, allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes. This allows the flavors to penetrate the meat, resulting in a more flavorful and tender dish.

  • If you don’t have baharat spice blend on hand, you can make your own by combining equal parts of ground cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg. This homemade blend will still give you the authentic Middle Eastern flavors.

Conclusion

Spice up your cooking with Middle Eastern Baharat Chicken and take your taste buds on a flavorful journey. This dish is sure to impress your family and friends with its exotic flavors and aromatic spices. So, why wait? Gather your ingredients, unleash your culinary creativity, and enjoy the magic of Baharat Chicken in your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
