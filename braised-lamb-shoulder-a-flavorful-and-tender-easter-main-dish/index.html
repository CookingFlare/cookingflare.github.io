Braised Lamb Shoulder: A Flavorful and Tender Easter Main Dish


Easter is a time for family gatherings and delicious feasts. If you’re looking for a show-stopping main dish that will impress your guests, look no further than braised lamb shoulder. This flavorful and tender dish is sure to be the highlight of your Easter celebration. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create a succulent lamb shoulder that will melt in your mouth.

Ingredients

  • 1 lamb shoulder, bone-in (approximately 5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 sprigs of rosemary
  • 2 sprigs of thyme
  • 2 cups red wine
  • 2 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F.

  2. Season the lamb shoulder generously with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a large, oven-safe pot over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the lamb shoulder and sear on all sides until nicely browned. This will help to develop a rich flavor.

  3. Remove the lamb shoulder from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the garlic, onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant.

  4. Return the lamb shoulder to the pot, along with the rosemary and thyme sprigs. Pour in the red wine and broth, making sure the liquid covers about two-thirds of the lamb shoulder. This will ensure a moist and tender result.

  5. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Allow the lamb shoulder to braise for 3 to 4 hours, or until it is fork-tender. Baste the lamb shoulder with the cooking liquid every hour to keep it moist and flavorful.

  6. Once the lamb shoulder is cooked to perfection, remove it from the pot and let it rest for about 15 minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more tender and flavorful meat.

  7. While the lamb shoulder is resting, strain the cooking liquid from the pot and skim off any excess fat. You can use this liquid as a flavorful sauce to accompany the lamb.

  8. To serve, carve the lamb shoulder into thick slices and drizzle with the cooking liquid. Pair it with your favorite sides, such as roasted potatoes or steamed vegetables, for a complete and satisfying Easter feast.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, you can add additional herbs and spices to the braising liquid. Consider adding a bay leaf, a cinnamon stick, or a pinch of cumin for a unique twist.

  • If you prefer a richer sauce, you can reduce the strained cooking liquid on the stovetop until it thickens. This will concentrate the flavors and create a luxurious sauce to drizzle over the lamb.

  • Leftovers can be refrigerated and enjoyed the next day. The flavors will continue to develop, making the lamb even more delicious.

  • This recipe can easily be adapted for a larger or smaller lamb shoulder. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly, as a larger shoulder will require more time to become tender.

Braised lamb shoulder is a classic Easter main dish that never fails to impress. Its melt-in-your-mouth tenderness and rich flavors make it a standout centerpiece for any festive occasion. So this Easter, gather your loved ones around the table and savor the deliciousness of this flavorful and tender lamb dish.
