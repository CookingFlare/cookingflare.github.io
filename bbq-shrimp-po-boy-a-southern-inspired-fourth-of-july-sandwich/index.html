BBQ Shrimp Po' Boy: A Southern-Inspired Fourth of July Sandwich


Who doesn’t love a good sandwich, especially on the Fourth of July? This year, why not take your celebration to the next level with a Southern-inspired BBQ Shrimp Po’ Boy? This iconic Louisiana sandwich is a perfect blend of bold flavors, crispy textures, and a touch of smoky sweetness. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply craving a taste of the South, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and your guests.

Ingredients

For the BBQ Shrimp:

  • 1 pound fresh large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Remoulade Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Creole mustard
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Additional Ingredients:

  • 4 French baguettes or sub rolls
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced dill pickles
  • Sliced red onions
  • Butter for toasting the bread

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the BBQ Shrimp

  1. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, BBQ sauce, Worcestershire sauce, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create the marinade.
  2. Add the peeled and deveined shrimp to the marinade and toss to coat evenly. Let the shrimp marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

Step 2: Make the Remoulade Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Creole mustard, hot sauce, lemon juice, minced garlic, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to your taste and refrigerate until ready to use.

Step 3: Cook the BBQ Shrimp

  1. Heat a grill or a skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the marinated shrimp in a single layer and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they turn pink and slightly charred. Be careful not to overcook the shrimp to maintain their tenderness and juiciness.

Step 4: Assemble the Po’ Boy Sandwiches

  1. Slice the French baguettes or sub rolls lengthwise, but not all the way through. Spread a generous amount of the prepared remoulade sauce on both sides of the bread.
  2. Layer the shredded lettuce, sliced tomatoes, BBQ shrimp, dill pickles, and red onions on the bottom half of each baguette.
  3. Close the sandwiches and lightly butter the outside of the bread.

Step 5: Toast the Sandwiches

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat and place the assembled sandwiches, buttered side down, onto the skillet. Toast for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the bread is golden brown and crispy, and the filling is warmed through.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Once the sandwiches are toasted to perfection, remove them from the skillet and cut them in half diagonally. Serve immediately with a side of coleslaw, potato salad, or your favorite Fourth of July sides.

Conclusion

There you have it, a mouthwatering BBQ Shrimp Po’ Boy that’s perfect for your Fourth of July celebration. This sandwich is packed with vibrant flavors, from the smoky BBQ shrimp to the zesty remoulade sauce, all nestled in a crispy baguette. Whether you’re in the heart of the South or miles away, this recipe will transport you to the lively streets of New Orleans with every bite. So, fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the taste of summer with this Southern-inspired delight. Happy Fourth of July!
