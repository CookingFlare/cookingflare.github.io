Asian-Inspired Family Dinners: Recipes to Spice Up Your Menu


When it comes to family dinners, finding recipes that are both delicious and exciting can be a challenge. If your family loves Asian cuisine or you’re looking to add some new flavors to your dinner rotation, we’ve got you covered. These Asian-inspired family dinner recipes are not only packed with flavor, but they are also easy to make and perfect for sharing with the ones you love. From savory stir-fries to comforting noodle dishes, these recipes will take your family dinners to a whole new level.

Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken pieces and stir-fry until they are cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the remaining vegetable oil and sauté the garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  4. Add the broccoli and bell peppers to the skillet and stir-fry until they are tender-crisp.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and pour the teriyaki sauce over the ingredients. Stir to coat everything evenly.
  6. Serve the teriyaki chicken stir-fry over cooked rice and garnish with sesame seeds, if desired.

Thai Coconut Curry Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into strips
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • Cooked rice noodles, for serving
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth and coconut milk to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add the red curry paste, fish sauce, and brown sugar to the pot and stir until the ingredients are well combined.
  3. Add the chicken, red bell pepper, mushrooms, and snap peas to the pot and simmer until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  4. Serve the Thai coconut curry soup over cooked rice noodles and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Vegetable Lo Mein

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces lo mein noodles
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the lo mein noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, and sesame oil.
  3. In a large skillet or wok, heat some oil over medium-high heat and sauté the garlic until fragrant.
  4. Add the shredded cabbage, julienned carrot, snow peas, and green onions to the skillet and stir-fry until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Add the cooked lo mein noodles to the skillet and pour the sauce over the ingredients. Toss everything together until the noodles and vegetables are evenly coated.
  6. Serve the vegetable lo mein garnished with sesame seeds.

Conclusion

These Asian-inspired family dinner recipes are sure to add a dash of excitement to your dinner table. Whether you’re a fan of bold and spicy flavors or prefer something more comforting and mild, these recipes offer a variety of options to suit different tastes. So, gather your loved ones around the table and savor the delicious flavors of these Asian-inspired dishes. Happy cooking and happy eating!
