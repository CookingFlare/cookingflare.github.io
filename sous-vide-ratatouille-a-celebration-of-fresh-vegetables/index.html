Sous Vide Ratatouille: A Celebration of Fresh Vegetables


Ratatouille is a classic French dish that celebrates the vibrant flavors of fresh vegetables. Traditionally, it is made by sautéing the vegetables together, but today, we are going to take it up a notch and prepare it using the sous vide method. Sous vide cooking allows us to retain the natural colors, textures, and flavors of the vegetables, resulting in a Ratatouille that is not only visually stunning but also incredibly delicious. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

  • 1 eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 orange bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide cooker to 185°F (85°C).

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, orange bell pepper, red onion, minced garlic, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, olive oil, dried thyme, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss everything together until well coated.

  3. Transfer the vegetable mixture into a vacuum-sealable bag or a ziplock bag. Remove as much air as possible and seal the bag.

  4. Place the sealed bag into the preheated water bath of your sous vide cooker. Cook for 2 hours, ensuring that the vegetables are fully submerged in the water.

  5. After 2 hours, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and let it rest for a few minutes.

  6. Open the bag and transfer the cooked vegetables to a serving dish. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for added freshness and aroma.

  7. Serve the sous vide Ratatouille as a side dish or as a main course, accompanied by crusty bread or over a bed of cooked quinoa or rice.

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize your sous vide Ratatouille by adding other vegetables like mushrooms, cherry tomatoes, or even artichoke hearts. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • For a more intense flavor, you can roast the garlic before adding it to the vegetable mixture. Roasting brings out the sweetness and complexity of the garlic.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Ratatouille tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld together.

  • If you don’t have a sous vide cooker, you can still make Ratatouille using the traditional stovetop method. Simply sauté the vegetables in a large skillet with some olive oil until tender.

  • Ratatouille pairs well with a variety of proteins such as grilled chicken, fish, or tofu. It can also be enjoyed on its own as a light and healthy meal.

Final Thoughts

Sous vide Ratatouille is a delightful dish that captures the essence of summer with its vibrant colors and fresh flavors. By cooking the vegetables sous vide, we ensure that they retain their natural textures and flavors, resulting in a Ratatouille that is both visually appealing and incredibly tasty. Whether you’re a vegetarian, a vegetable lover, or simply looking to add more plant-based dishes to your repertoire, this recipe is a must-try. So, gather your freshest vegetables and let the sous vide magic happen in your kitchen. Bon appétit!
