Fourth of July Blueberry Pie: A Classic and Delicious Dessert


There’s something magical about the Fourth of July. The warm summer breeze, the sound of fireworks crackling in the night sky, and the delicious scent of grilled burgers and hot dogs filling the air. But no Fourth of July celebration is complete without a classic and delicious dessert: Blueberry Pie.

Ingredients

To make this mouthwatering blueberry pie, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup of ice water
  • 6 cups of fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process of making this delectable Fourth of July Blueberry Pie:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to blend it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, starting with 1/4 cup, and mix until the dough comes together. Add more ice water if needed, but be careful not to overmix. The dough should hold together when squeezed but still be slightly crumbly.

  4. Divide the dough in half and shape each half into a disk. Wrap them in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  5. In another bowl, combine the blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract. Gently toss everything together until the blueberries are coated in the mixture.

  6. Take one chilled dough disk out of the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface until it’s about 12 inches in diameter. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides.

  7. Pour the blueberry filling into the prepared pie crust, spreading it evenly.

  8. Roll out the second chilled dough disk to the same size as the first one. You can either place it on top of the pie as a full crust or cut it into strips to create a lattice pattern. If using a full crust, make sure to cut a few vents on top to allow steam to escape during baking.

  9. Brush the top crust or lattice with the beaten egg to give the pie a beautiful golden color when baked.

  10. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for 45 to 50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the blueberry filling is bubbling.

  11. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set and make for easier slicing.

Enjoy the Classic Blueberry Pie!

There you have it – a classic Fourth of July Blueberry Pie that is sure to impress your friends and family. The flaky crust, bursting blueberries, and hint of tangy lemon make it the perfect summer dessert.

Serve it on its own, or top it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence. Either way, this pie is bound to be the star of your Fourth of July celebration.

So, fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and savor every delicious bite of this classic and mouthwatering blueberry pie. Happy Fourth of July!
