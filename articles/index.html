Vegan Brunch Waffles for a Delicious Weekend Morning

There’s something special about a leisurely weekend morning, and nothing quite sets the tone like a delicious brunch. If you’re in the mood for a delightful vegan option, look no further than these scrumptious Vegan Brunch Waffles. They’re fluffy, flavorful, and perfect for a relaxed weekend morning at home. Let’s dive into the recipe and get ready to elevate your next brunch experience! Ingredients 2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 cup cornstarch 2 tablespoons sugar 1 tablespoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1 3/4 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk of your choice) 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Vegan butter or coconut oil for greasing the waffle iron Instructions Start by preheating your waffle iron according to the manufacturer’s instructions. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In a separate bowl, combine the almond milk, applesauce, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay. Once the waffle iron is heated, lightly grease it with vegan butter or coconut oil. Pour the appropriate amount of batter onto the waffle iron, close the lid, and cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions, or until the waffles are golden brown and crisp. Carefully remove the waffles from the iron and place them on a wire rack to maintain crispness while you finish cooking the remaining batter. Serve the waffles warm with your favorite toppings, such as fresh fruit, maple syrup, coconut whipped cream, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar. Tips for Perfect Vegan Brunch Waffles Fluffy Texture: The combination of all-purpose flour and cornstarch helps achieve a light and fluffy texture in these waffles. Customization: Feel free to add a dash of cinnamon, a sprinkle of nutmeg, or a handful of dairy-free chocolate chips to the batter for a flavor twist. Make-Ahead: If you’re planning to serve a crowd, you can keep the cooked waffles warm in a 200°F oven while you finish cooking the rest. Freezing: These waffles freeze beautifully! Once cooled, store them in a freezer-safe bag or container, and reheat in a toaster for a quick weekday breakfast. The Perfect Brunch Atmosphere Imagine the inviting aroma of freshly cooked waffles filling your kitchen, the sound of sizzling batter, and the anticipation of a delightful brunch spread. Delightful Vegan Blueberry Muffins for Breakfast

Who doesn’t love a warm and delicious blueberry muffin in the morning? The combination of juicy blueberries and soft, fluffy muffin batter is simply irresistible. And guess what? You don’t need eggs or dairy to achieve that perfect texture and flavor. Today, I’m going to share with you my recipe for delightful vegan blueberry muffins that will make your breakfasts a whole lot happier! Ingredients 2 cups all-purpose flour 1 cup granulated sugar 1 tablespoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1 cup unsweetened almond milk 1/4 cup vegetable oil 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 1/2 cups fresh blueberries Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly with cooking spray. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Make sure they are well combined. In a separate bowl, mix the almond milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract until well incorporated. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix as it can result in dense muffins. Gently fold in the fresh blueberries, making sure they are evenly distributed throughout the batter. Using a spoon or an ice cream scoop, divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each one about 2/3 full. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean. Remove the muffins from the oven and let them cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
