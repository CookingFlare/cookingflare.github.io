Easter Meatless Mains: Flavorful Vegetarian Options


When it comes to Easter, many people associate the holiday with indulgent meat dishes like ham or lamb. However, for those who follow a vegetarian diet or simply want to explore new flavors, there are plenty of delicious and satisfying options to enjoy. In this blog post, we will explore some flavorful vegetarian mains that are perfect for Easter, whether you’re hosting a vegetarian feast or simply looking for a meatless option to add to your holiday spread.

Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells

Preparation time: 30 minutes Cooking time: 45 minutes Servings: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups of ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of marinara sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.
  2. Cook the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, chopped spinach, Parmesan cheese, egg, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Mix well until fully combined.
  4. Spoon the ricotta mixture into each cooked pasta shell and arrange them in the greased baking dish.
  5. Pour the marinara sauce over the stuffed shells, covering them evenly.
  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 15 minutes until the sauce is bubbly and the cheese is golden brown.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired.

Lentil and Vegetable Shepherd’s Pie

Preparation time: 30 minutes Cooking time: 1 hour Servings: 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked green or brown lentils
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups of mashed potatoes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the diced onion, carrots, and celery over medium heat until they begin to soften.
  3. Add the cooked lentils, frozen peas, vegetable broth, tomato paste, soy sauce, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  4. Cook the mixture for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Transfer the lentil and vegetable mixture to the greased baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  6. Spoon the mashed potatoes on top of the lentil mixture and spread it out into an even layer.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-40 minutes until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  8. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving.

Mushroom and Leek Quiche

Preparation time: 20 minutes Cooking time: 40 minutes Servings: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 leeks, thinly sliced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup of shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and place the pre-made pie crust in a greased pie dish.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced leeks and mushrooms and sauté until they are tender and lightly browned.
  3. Spread the sautéed leeks and mushrooms evenly over the pie crust. Sprinkle the shredded Gruyere cheese on top.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, and pepper. Pour the mixture over the leeks, mushrooms, and cheese in the pie crust.
  5. Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.
  6. Remove from the oven and let cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley.

These Easter meatless mains offer a variety of flavors and textures that will satisfy both vegetarians and meat lovers alike. Whether you choose to serve the spinach and ricotta stuffed shells, lentil and vegetable shepherd’s pie, or mushroom and leek quiche, you can be sure that your Easter feast will be both delicious and memorable. Enjoy the celebration of spring with these flavorful vegetarian options!

Note: Remember to adjust the serving sizes and ingredients according to your specific needs and preferences.
