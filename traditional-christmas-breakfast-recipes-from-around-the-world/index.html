Traditional Christmas Breakfast Recipes from Around the World


Christmas morning is a magical time when families and friends come together to celebrate the festive season with delicious food, laughter, and joy. Around the globe, cultures have developed unique and mouthwatering breakfast traditions to mark this special day. From sweet treats to savory classics, these recipes reflect the local flavors and customs that make each region’s Christmas breakfast unique. Whether you want to explore new culinary traditions or add an international twist to your holiday table, this guide will inspire you to savor Christmas morning in a whole new way.

1. The Classic English Christmas Breakfast

In England, Christmas morning often begins with a satisfying breakfast filled with warm, comforting dishes that set the tone for the rest of the day. The English Christmas breakfast typically includes items like scrambled eggs, bacon, sausages, grilled tomatoes, baked beans, and toast. However, the festive version adds a little extra indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 8 slices of bacon
  • 6 pork sausages
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 1 can baked beans
  • 4 slices of bread
  • Butter for spreading

Directions:

  1. Fry bacon slices in a skillet until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  2. Cook sausages until browned and cooked through, then grill halved tomatoes on medium heat.
  3. Scramble eggs with a bit of milk, salt, and pepper for a creamy texture.
  4. Heat baked beans in a saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  5. Toast the bread until golden and spread butter generously before serving.
  6. Arrange everything on a platter for a full English breakfast experience!

The rich flavors and hearty texture of the English breakfast are a crowd-pleaser, ensuring that everyone is energized for holiday festivities.

2. Stollen and Coffee in Germany

In Germany, Christmas breakfast is incomplete without “Stollen,” a sweet bread filled with dried fruits, nuts, and marzipan—a festive favorite. Typically served with strong coffee, Stollen embodies the rich, celebratory spirit of the German holiday tradition.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup candied fruits
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (almonds or walnuts)
  • 1/2 cup marzipan
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1 packet dry yeast

Directions:

  1. Mix flour, sugar, and baking powder in a large bowl. Dissolve yeast in the warm milk and let sit for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the milk mixture to the dry ingredients along with melted butter. Knead into a soft dough.
  3. Fold in candied fruits and nuts, rolling marzipan into small pieces before blending into the dough.
  4. Shape into a loaf, place on a baking tray, and allow to rise for an hour.
  5. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 35–40 minutes until golden brown.
  6. Dust with powdered sugar before serving with hot coffee.

Stollen carries a sense of nostalgia and tradition that makes Christmas morning extra special for German families.

3. Swedish Lussekatter (Saffron Buns)

In Sweden, Christmas morning often revolves around warm and fragrant saffron buns known as “Lussekatter.” Their golden hue and slightly sweet flavor make them a perfect start to the holiday celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups bread flour
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons dry yeast
  • 1/3 cup melted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • Raisins for decoration

Directions:

  1. Dissolve saffron threads in the warm milk and let sit for 10 minutes to infuse.
  2. Combine flour, sugar, and yeast in a bowl. Gradually add saffron milk and melted butter, kneading until smooth.
  3. Incorporate eggs into the dough for richness, then allow it to rise for 1 hour in a warm place.
  4. Divide the dough into small pieces, rolling them into S-shaped buns. Garnish with raisins on top.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 10–12 minutes until golden brown. Serve warm.

Pair Lussekatter with a steaming mug of tea or coffee, bringing the warmth and beauty of Swedish tradition to your home.

4. Mexican Tamales and Atole

In Mexico, Christmas morning breakfast is a vibrant affair with traditional tamales served alongside atole, a thick, comforting beverage made with corn masa and milk. Tamales, made of masa dough stuffed with fillings, are wrapped in corn husks and steamed to perfection.

Ingredients for Tamales:

  • 3 cups masa harina
  • 1 cup lard or vegetable shortening
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 cups warm chicken broth
  • Corn husks (soaked in warm water)
  • Filling of choice (shredded chicken, beef, or cheese)

Ingredients for Atole:

  • 1/3 cup masa harina
  • 3 cups milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. For tamales, mix masa harina, lard, and baking powder together. Gradually add chicken broth until the dough is smooth and spreadable.
  2. Spread dough onto soaked corn husks, add filling, and wrap tightly. Steam tamales for 2 hours.
  3. For atole, whisk masa harina into milk over medium heat. Add cinnamon stick, sugar, and vanilla. Simmer until thickened.
  4. Serve tamales with a mug of hot atole for a truly festive breakfast.

The warmth and flavor of tamales and atole create a cozy and welcoming atmosphere, ideal for Christmas morning.

5. Australian Pavlova Pancakes

In Australia, Christmas falls during summer, which means breakfasts include light, refreshing dishes. Pavlova pancakes are a modern twist on the iconic Australian dessert and are perfect for enjoying seasonal fruits and flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 eggs
  • Fresh fruits (berries, mango, kiwi)
  • Whipped cream

Directions:

  1. Stir flour, baking powder, and salt together in a bowl. Whisk eggs into milk and combine with dry ingredients until smooth.
  2. Cook pancakes on a non-stick skillet until golden on both sides.
  3. Assemble by layering pancakes with whipped cream and fresh fruits on top for a Pavlova-inspired look.
  4. Serve immediately for a summery Christmas morning treat.

Colorful and delightful, this dish captures the vibrant and light-hearted spirit of an Australian Christmas.

6. Polish Pierogi for Breakfast

In Poland, pierogi (dumplings) are often enjoyed during Christmastime. While savory pierogi are the norm, sweet variations filled with fruit or cheese make a delicious breakfast option for Christmas morning.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Filling options: Sweetened farmer’s cheese, cherry preserves, or applesauce

Directions:

  1. Mix flour, salt, sour cream, and water into a soft dough. Knead until smooth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll the dough out thin and cut into small circles. Add filling to the center, fold into half-moon shapes, and seal edges.
  3. Boil pierogi in salted water until they float to the top, then fry lightly in butter for added texture.
  4. Serve warm, dusted with powdered sugar or drizzled with honey.

Sweet pierogi are the perfect blend of Polish heritage and festive indulgence.

These traditional Christmas breakfast recipes from around the world highlight the beauty and diversity of holiday traditions through food. Whether you choose to recreate one of these delicious dishes or explore them all, they’re sure to add an international flair to your Christmas morning. By embracing new flavors and customs, you can enrich your holiday experience and bring family and friends closer together through the universal joy of sharing a meal.
