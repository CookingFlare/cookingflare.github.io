Korean BBQ at Home: 8 Recipes for a Flavorful Experience


Korean BBQ, known for its vibrant flavors and communal dining experience, has gained immense popularity worldwide. The sizzling sounds of marinated meats grilling over an open flame, paired with an array of side dishes, create an unforgettable culinary experience. If you’re looking to bring the excitement of Korean BBQ into your home, you’re in the right place. In this post, we’ll explore eight delicious recipes that will elevate your home dining experience and impress your family and friends.

1. Galbi (Korean BBQ Short Ribs)

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of beef short ribs
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup minced ginger
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a large bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, minced garlic, minced ginger, green onions, sesame seeds, and black pepper. Mix well until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Marinate the Ribs: Place the short ribs in a large zip-top bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the ribs, ensuring they are well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Grill the Ribs: Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the ribs from the marinade and let excess marinade drip off. Grill the ribs for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until they are nicely charred and cooked to your desired doneness.
  4. Serve: Let the ribs rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with additional green onions and sesame seeds if desired.

2. Bulgogi (Marinated Beef)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of thinly sliced beef (ribeye or sirloin)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 1/4 cup grated pear (or apple)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Make the Marinade: In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, minced garlic, minced ginger, grated pear, chopped green onions, and black pepper.
  2. Marinate the Beef: Add the sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for more flavor.
  3. Cook the Bulgogi: Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Add the marinated beef in a single layer and cook for about 3-4 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the beef is cooked through and caramelized.
  4. Serve: Serve the bulgogi with steamed rice and lettuce leaves for wrapping.

3. Samgyeopsal (Grilled Pork Belly)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of pork belly, sliced into 1/4 inch thick pieces
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lettuce leaves for wrapping
  • Ssamjang (Korean dipping sauce)
  • Sliced garlic and green peppers for grilling

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Grill: Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the Pork Belly: Season the pork belly slices with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Grill the Pork Belly: Place the pork belly on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until crispy and golden brown.
  4. Serve: Serve the grilled pork belly with lettuce leaves, ssamjang, and grilled garlic and green peppers. Wrap the pork in lettuce with a dollop of ssamjang for a delicious bite.
