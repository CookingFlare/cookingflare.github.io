Homemade Cinnamon Raisin Bread: Perfect for Toast


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked cinnamon raisin bread wafting through your kitchen. The tantalizing scent fills the air, beckoning you to grab a slice while it’s still warm. And when that toasted slice hits your tongue, the flavors of cinnamon and plump raisins dance in harmony, creating a breakfast experience that’s simply irresistible.

Today, I’m going to share with you my tried-and-true recipe for homemade cinnamon raisin bread. This recipe is perfect for those lazy Sunday mornings when you want to treat yourself to something special. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup raisins

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Give it a quick mix to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Pour this mixture into the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a floured surface and roll it out into a rectangular shape, about 1/2 inch thick.

  6. In a small bowl, mix together the ground cinnamon and raisins. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the dough, making sure to reach all the edges.

  7. Starting from one of the shorter sides, tightly roll up the dough into a log. Pinch the seam to seal it and tuck the ends underneath.

  8. Place the rolled dough into a greased loaf pan, cover it with the kitchen towel, and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until it has risen just above the rim of the pan.

  9. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Once the dough has finished its final rise, bake it in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped.

  10. Remove the bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Now that your homemade cinnamon raisin bread is ready, it’s time to enjoy it in all its toasty glory. Slice it up, pop it into the toaster, and watch as the cinnamon swirls and raisins plump up before your eyes. Spread a little butter or cream cheese on top, and savor each bite as the flavors meld together in perfect harmony.

This delightful bread is not only perfect for breakfast but also makes a wonderful snack or even a dessert. It’s versatile, delicious, and sure to become a favorite in your household.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for something warm, comforting, and full of flavor, remember this homemade cinnamon raisin bread recipe. It’s a simple pleasure that will bring joy to your mornings and make every day feel a little bit more special.
