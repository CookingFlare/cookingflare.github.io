Ultimate BBQ Ribs Recipe: Fall-Off-The-Bone Perfection


Have you ever craving a succulent rack of ribs that you could just sink your teeth into? Look no further! Our ultimate BBQ ribs recipe is designed to give you fall-off-the-bone perfection with the perfect balance of smoky flavor and tangy BBQ sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 slab of baby-back ribs (about 5-6 pounds)
  • 2 cups of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1 cup of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup of your preferred BBQ rub
  • 1 ½ cups of water
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Equipment

  • Large cooking pot (for boiling)
  • Grill to grill-smoke ribs

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the ribs

Using a sharp knife, cut the slab of ribs down to individual servings, saving a portion of the backbone and carnage to make an impromptu “chicken” stock. Rinse each rib plate in cold water to remove any residue and pat it dry with paper towels.

Step 2: Boil the ribs

You’re going to cook the ribs in a big pot on top of the stove first to tenderize them. To do that, fill the large cooking pot with enough water to cover the ribs. Add a portion of the backbone, which now you’ve saved, a little bit of salt, onion, garlic, celery, and bay leaf. Boil the ribs for about 1 hour. Keep an eye on the pot and remove any scum that rises to the surface. Make sure to skim occasionally.

Step 3: Season the ribs

Remove the ribs from the boiling pot and let them cool for a few minutes. Now the ribs have to be seasoned. Rub the ribs all over with a flavorful BBQ rub – we suggest using paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, salt, and freshly ground black pepper. It’s okay to leave some meat exposed for a more authentic look.

Step 4: Smoke the ribs

After the ribs have been seasoned, it’s time to trust your BBQ to the grill. Preheat the grill for indirect grilling, positioning the ribs over a pile of hot coals with just the opposite end of the grate directly over the heat. This way, you are smoking them rather than searing them. Grill each side for approximately 20 minutes. Then, move to the other side of the grill to smoke the ribs with a subtle touch of applewood for approximately 45 minutes.

Step 5: Baste and finish the ribs

After the ribs have spent some time getting smoky on both sides, apply a generous portion of homemade BBQ sauce to the ribs. Grill the ribs for an additional 10-15 minutes, brushing with additional sauce every 5 minutes until desired glaze is achieved. Make sure not to overcook the ribs as this will dry them out.

Step 6: Rest and Serve

When the ribs stretch has reached perfection, remove them from the grill and allow them to set for 10 minutes before serving. During this resting period is when the ribs truly tenderize. The tenderness of these ribs is fall-off-the-bone perfection! Memories made around the grill are sure to last a lifetime.

Now, gather some friends and family and enjoy the hard-earned rewards of your labor. Even the most discerning rib-lovers should be thrilled with this ultimate BBQ ribs recipe!
