The Best Spanish Meat Dishes for Meat Lovers


Spain, a country renowned for its rich culture and culinary diversity, offers an impressive array of meat dishes that tantalize the taste buds of meat lovers around the world. From succulent pork to flavorful beef, the Spanish cuisine showcases a variety of meats prepared with a flair that reflects the country’s regional traditions and vibrant ingredients. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best Spanish meat dishes, delve into their origins, and provide you with delicious recipes to recreate these mouthwatering meals in your own kitchen.

1. Cochinillo Asado (Roast Suckling Pig)

One of Spain’s most iconic dishes, Cochinillo Asado, hails from the region of Segovia. This traditional roast suckling pig is known for its crispy skin and tender, juicy meat. The preparation is simple yet requires patience, as the pig is slow-roasted to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 suckling pig (about 10-12 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons coarse sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup white wine
  • Fresh rosemary (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preparation: Preheat your oven to 250°F (120°C).
  2. Seasoning: Rub the pig inside and out with olive oil, sea salt, and black pepper. Optionally, place fresh rosemary inside the cavity for added flavor.
  3. Roasting: Place the pig on a roasting rack in a large roasting pan. Pour the white wine into the bottom of the pan, which will help keep the meat moist.
  4. Cooking: Roast the pig for about 4-5 hours, basting every hour. The skin should be golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serving: Allow the pig to rest for about 30 minutes before carving. Serve with roasted potatoes and a simple salad.

2. Fabada Asturiana (Asturian Bean Stew with Chorizo)

Fabada Asturiana is a hearty bean stew from the northern region of Asturias, characterized by its rich flavors and comforting texture. This dish typically features various meats, including chorizo and morcilla (blood sausage).

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fabes (Asturian white beans) or cannellini beans
  • 1 chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 morcilla (blood sausage), sliced
  • 1 pound pork shoulder, cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water or chicken broth

Instructions:

  1. Soaking Beans: Soak the beans overnight in water. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sautéing: In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until translucent. Add the pork shoulder and cook until browned.
  3. Adding Ingredients: Stir in the chorizo, morcilla, paprika, and bay leaf. Add the soaked beans and enough water or broth to cover the ingredients.
  4. Cooking: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 1.5-2 hours, or until the beans are tender. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serving: Serve hot with crusty bread on the side.

3. Pisto con Carne (Spanish Ratatouille with Meat)

Pisto is a traditional Spanish dish, similar to the French ratatouille, made with tomatoes, peppers, and zucchini. When combined with meat, it becomes a delicious and filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef or pork
  • 2 medium zucchinis, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cooking Meat: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground meat and cook until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. Sautéing Vegetables: In the same skillet, add more olive oil if necessary and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant. Then add the bell pepper and zucchini, cooking until softened.
  3. Combining: Add the tomatoes and cooked meat back into the skillet. Season with salt and pepper. Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Serving: Garnish with fresh basil or parsley before serving. Enjoy with crusty bread or over rice.

4. Cordero Asado (Roast Lamb)

Cordero Asado, or roast lamb, is a staple dish in Spanish cuisine, particularly in the regions of Castilla y León and Aragón. The lamb is often marinated with herbs and spices, resulting in tender, flavorful meat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole leg of lamb (about 5-7 pounds)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon thyme, chopped
  • 1 cup red wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinating: In a bowl, mix garlic, olive oil, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Rub this mixture all over the lamb and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  2. Preheating Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  3. Roasting: Place the lamb in a roasting pan and pour red wine over it. Roast for about 2-3 hours, or until the meat reaches your desired doneness. Baste occasionally with the pan juices.
  4. Resting: Let the lamb rest for 20-30 minutes before carving. Serve with roasted vegetables or potatoes.

5. Albondigas (Spanish Meatballs)

Albondigas are a beloved Spanish dish made from ground meat, typically served in a rich tomato sauce. They can be made with beef, pork, or a combination, making them versatile and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground meat (beef, pork, or a mix)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 jar marinara sauce (or homemade tomato sauce)
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Mixing Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine ground meat, breadcrumbs, egg, garlic, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  2. Forming Meatballs: Shape the mixture into meatballs, about the size of a golf ball.
  3. Frying: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the meatballs and cook until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. Cooking Sauce: In the same skillet, pour in the marinara sauce and bring to a simmer. Return the meatballs to the skillet and cook for an additional 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Serving: Serve the albondigas with crusty bread or over rice or pasta.

6. Chuleton (Spanish Ribeye Steak)

Chuleton refers to a thick-cut ribeye steak, often enjoyed in northern Spain, especially in the Basque Country. This dish is all about simplicity, showcasing the quality of the meat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ribeye steaks (about 2 inches thick)
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh herbs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Steak: Remove the steaks from the fridge and let them come to room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  2. Grilling: Preheat a grill or cast-iron skillet over high heat. Drizzle olive oil over the steaks and sprinkle with coarse sea salt.
  3. Cooking: Grill the steaks for about 4-5 minutes on each side for medium-rare, adjusting the time based on your preferred doneness.
  4. Resting: Allow the steaks to rest for about 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.

7. Rabo de Toro (Oxtail Stew)

Rabo de Toro, or oxtail stew, is a traditional dish from Andalusia that embodies the essence of Spanish comfort food. The slow-cooked oxtail becomes incredibly tender and is packed with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Browning the Oxtail: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown the oxtail pieces on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  2. Sautéing Vegetables: In the same pot, sauté the onions, carrots, and garlic until softened.
  3. Adding Ingredients: Stir in the diced tomatoes, red wine, beef broth, bay leaf, and the browned oxtail. Bring to a boil.
  4. Simmering: Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 2-3 hours, or until the oxtail is fork-tender. Season with salt and pepper.
