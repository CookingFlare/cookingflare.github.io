Maple Glazed Roasted Acorn Squash: A Simple and Tasty Side


When it comes to fall and winter produce, acorn squash stands out as a versatile and delicious ingredient. Its sweet, nutty flavor lends itself well to a variety of cooking methods, making it a popular choice for comforting side dishes. One of the best ways to prepare acorn squash is by roasting it, and when you add a delectable maple glaze, the result is a dish that’s sure to impress. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through the steps to create Maple Glazed Roasted Acorn Squash - a simple and tasty side that pairs perfectly with a wide range of main courses.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium acorn squash
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 tablespoons pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). This will ensure that it’s nice and hot when it’s time to roast the acorn squash.

  2. Prepare the Squash: Wash the acorn squash and pat them dry with a paper towel. Then, carefully slice each squash in half vertically, using a sharp knife. Be sure to exercise caution while cutting, as acorn squash can be quite tough.

  3. Remove the Seeds: Once the squash is halved, use a spoon to scoop out the seeds and stringy pulp from the center of each half. You can discard the seeds or save them for roasting as a snack later.

  4. Slice the Squash: With the seeds removed, slice each squash half into 1-inch thick wedges. This will create the perfect size for roasting and ensure that the maple glaze can coat each piece evenly.

  5. Prepare the Glaze: In a small bowl, combine the melted butter, maple syrup, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until they are well combined, creating a fragrant and flavorful glaze.

  6. Coat the Squash: Place the squash wedges on a large baking sheet, ensuring that they are in a single layer. Drizzle the maple glaze over the squash, using a brush or spoon to evenly coat each piece.

  7. Roast to Perfection: Transfer the baking sheet to the preheated oven and roast the squash for 30-35 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the squash is tender when pierced with a fork.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Once the acorn squash is roasted to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Then, transfer the squash to a serving platter and drizzle any remaining glaze over the top. This dish is best served warm, alongside your favorite main course.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of flavor, consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper to the maple glaze.
  • Sprinkle chopped fresh herbs, such as thyme or rosemary, over the squash before roasting for an aromatic twist.
  • Experiment with different types of squash, such as butternut or delicata, for a unique variation on this recipe.

Conclusion

Maple Glazed Roasted Acorn Squash is a delightful side dish that embodies the comforting flavors of the fall and winter seasons. The natural sweetness of the squash pairs beautifully with the rich, aromatic maple glaze, creating a dish that’s both simple to prepare and incredibly satisfying to eat. Whether you’re serving it alongside a holiday feast or adding it to your weeknight dinner rotation, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. So, the next time you’re craving a delicious and wholesome side dish, give this Maple Glazed Roasted Acorn Squash recipe a try - you won’t be disappointed!
