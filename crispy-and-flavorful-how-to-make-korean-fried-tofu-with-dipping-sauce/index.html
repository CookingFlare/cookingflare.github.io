Crispy and Flavorful: How to Make Korean Fried Tofu with Dipping Sauce


There’s something undeniably satisfying about biting into a piece of perfectly crispy and flavorful Korean fried tofu. The combination of the crunchy exterior and the soft, silky interior, paired with a tangy dipping sauce, makes for a truly delightful dish. Whether you’re a tofu aficionado or simply looking to expand your culinary horizons, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving more.

Ingredients

For the Korean Fried Tofu:

  • 1 block of firm tofu
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Tofu

  1. Start by pressing the block of tofu to remove excess moisture. Place the block of tofu between paper towels and set a heavy object, such as a cast-iron skillet, on top. Let it sit for about 30 minutes.
  2. Once the tofu is pressed, cut it into bite-sized cubes or rectangles, whichever shape you prefer.

Step 2: Coating the Tofu

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Roll each piece of tofu in the cornstarch mixture, ensuring that it’s evenly coated on all sides.

Step 3: Frying the Tofu

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the coated tofu pieces in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until golden brown and crispy, turning the pieces occasionally to ensure even frying. This should take about 4-5 minutes per batch.
  3. Once the tofu is crispy and golden, transfer it to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Step 4: Making the Dipping Sauce

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, and sesame seeds until well combined.

Step 5: Serving and Enjoying

  1. Serve the Korean fried tofu hot with the dipping sauce on the side. Garnish with additional sesame seeds or sliced green onions if desired.
  2. Dip the crispy tofu into the tangy sauce and savor the explosion of flavors and textures with each bite.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the dipping sauce.
  • To make the tofu extra crispy, you can double coat it by dipping the cornstarch-coated tofu in a mixture of water and cornstarch before frying.
  • Experiment with different shapes and sizes of tofu pieces to find the perfect bite for you.

Conclusion

With its irresistible crunch and delightful flavors, Korean fried tofu with dipping sauce is a dish that’s sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Whether you’re serving it as an appetizer, snack, or part of a larger meal, this recipe is bound to impress and satisfy. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure that will leave you craving more of this crispy and flavorful delight. Enjoy!
