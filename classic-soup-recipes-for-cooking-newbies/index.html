Classic Soup Recipes for Cooking Newbies


Are you a cooking newbie looking to impress your friends and family with delicious and comforting soups? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll explore some classic soup recipes that are perfect for beginners. These recipes are easy to follow, require minimal ingredients, and will leave you with a warm and satisfied feeling. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Creamy Tomato Basil Soup

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 30 minutes Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup of fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.
  2. Add the diced tomatoes (with their juice) to the pot, along with the vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20 minutes.
  3. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth. If using a regular blender, be sure to let the soup cool slightly before blending and blend in batches if necessary.
  4. Return the soup to the pot and stir in the heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Simmer the soup for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Stir in the chopped basil leaves just before serving.
  6. Ladle the creamy tomato basil soup into bowls and garnish with additional fresh basil leaves if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

Chicken Noodle Soup

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 45 minutes Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and shredded
  • 8 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 ounces of egg noodles

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery, and sauté until they start to soften.
  2. Pour in the chicken broth and add the bay leaf and dried thyme. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20 minutes.
  3. Add the shredded chicken to the pot and season with salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Meanwhile, cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  5. Just before serving, stir the cooked egg noodles into the soup. Remove the bay leaf.
  6. Ladle the chicken noodle soup into bowls and serve hot. Enjoy the comforting flavors!

Minestrone Soup

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 40 minutes Servings: 6-8

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup of canned kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup of elbow macaroni
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery, and sauté until they start to soften.
  2. Pour in the diced tomatoes (with their juice) and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20 minutes.
  3. Add the zucchini, kidney beans, elbow macaroni, dried basil, and dried oregano to the pot. Simmer for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the pasta is cooked al dente.
  4. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. If desired, sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on top before serving.
  5. Ladle the minestrone soup into bowls and serve hot. Enjoy the hearty and comforting flavors!

Conclusion

With these classic soup recipes in your cooking arsenal, you’ll be able to create delicious and satisfying meals for yourself and your loved ones. Don’t be intimidated by the kitchen; embrace your inner chef and let your creativity shine. Remember, cooking is all about experimentation and having fun. So, grab a ladle and start cooking these classic soups today. Happy cooking!
