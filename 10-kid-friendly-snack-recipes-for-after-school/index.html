10 Kid-Friendly Snack Recipes for After School


After a long day at school, kids deserve a snack that’s not only delicious but also easy to make and fun to eat. As a parent, it’s essential to provide your little ones with healthy and nutritious options that will keep them energized and focused for the rest of the day. Here are 10 kid-friendly snack recipes that are perfect for after school:

1. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

These moist and flavorful muffins are a great way to get your kids to eat their daily dose of fruits. Simply mix together 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 cup of granulated sugar, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, 1 large egg, 1/2 cup of milk, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and 1/2 cup of diced apples. Spoon the batter into a lined muffin tin and bake at 375°F for 20-25 minutes. While the muffins are still warm, mix together 2 tablespoons of cinnamon and 2 tablespoons of sugar. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture on top of the muffins and serve.

2. Yogurt Parfait Bar

Set up a yogurt parfait bar with your kids and let them create their own snacks. Layer Greek yogurt, granola, fresh berries, and a drizzle of honey for a delicious and healthy treat. You can also add other toppings like banana slices, chopped nuts, or shredded coconut.

3. Cheesy Pretzel Bites

Who doesn’t love a good pretzel? These cheesy pretzel bites are easy to make and fun to eat. Simply mix together 2 cups of warm water, 1 tablespoon of sugar, and 2 tablespoons of active dry yeast. Add 3 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/4 cup of unsalted butter. Roll out the dough and cut into bite-sized pieces. Place the pretzels on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake at 400°F for 12-15 minutes. While the pretzels are still warm, sprinkle with shredded cheddar cheese.

4. Banana Oat Bites

These bite-sized treats are perfect for snacking on the go. Simply mix together 2 ripe bananas, 1/2 cup of rolled oats, 1/4 cup of honey, and 1/4 cup of chopped walnuts. Roll the mixture into small balls and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Serve chilled.

5. Hummus and Veggie Sticks

Hummus is a great dip for kids, and pairing it with veggie sticks is a healthy and tasty snack. Simply mix together 1 cup of cooked chickpeas, 1/4 cup of tahini, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, and 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder. Serve with carrot sticks, celery sticks, and cucumber slices.

6. Mini Quiches

These individual quiches are perfect for snacking on the go. Simply mix together 1 cup of grated cheddar cheese, 1/2 cup of diced ham, and 1/2 cup of chopped bell peppers. Add 2 large eggs and 1/2 cup of milk. Pour the mixture into mini muffin tins and bake at 375°F for 15-20 minutes.

7. Cinnamon Sugar Toast

This sweet and crunchy snack is a kid-friendly favorite. Simply toast 4 slices of bread and mix together 2 tablespoons of granulated sugar and 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture on top of the toast and serve.

8. Energy Balls

These no-bake energy balls are a great snack for kids. Simply mix together 2 cups of rolled oats, 1 cup of peanut butter, 1/2 cup of honey, and 1/4 cup of chopped chocolate chips. Roll the mixture into small balls and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Serve chilled.

9. Mini Pita Pizzas

Who doesn’t love pizza? These mini pita pizzas are easy to make and fun to eat. Simply spread 1 tablespoon of pizza sauce on each pita bread, followed by 1/4 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese and 1/4 cup of chopped pepperoni. Bake at 400°F for 10-12 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

10. Rice Krispy Treats

These classic treats are a kid-friendly favorite. Simply melt 2 tablespoons of butter and 2 cups of marshmallows in a microwave-safe bowl. Add 2 cups of Rice Krispies cereal and stir until the cereal is evenly coated. Press the mixture into a lined baking dish and let cool. Cut into bars and serve.

These 10 kid-friendly snack recipes are perfect for after school. They’re easy to make, fun to eat, and provide a healthy and nutritious option for your little ones. Whether you’re a busy parent or a stay-at-home parent, these recipes are sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So go ahead, get creative, and have fun making these delicious snacks with your kids!
