Mouthwatering Lemon Meringue Pie: A Tangy and Sweet Dessert


Are you ready to indulge in a tangy and sweet dessert that will leave your taste buds begging for more? Look no further than this mouthwatering Lemon Meringue Pie recipe! With its delicate balance of tart lemon custard and fluffy meringue topping, this classic dessert is sure to impress both friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 3 large egg yolks, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the pre-baked pie crust on a baking sheet and set it aside.

  2. In a medium-sized saucepan, whisk together 1 cup of granulated sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually add water and lemon juice, stirring until the mixture is smooth. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.

  3. Once the mixture has boiled, reduce the heat to low. Take a small amount of the hot mixture and whisk it into the beaten egg yolks. This step, known as tempering, will prevent the eggs from curdling when added to the hot mixture.

  4. Slowly pour the tempered egg yolks into the saucepan, whisking constantly. Cook the mixture for an additional 2 minutes, stirring continuously. Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the butter and lemon zest until well combined.

  5. Pour the lemon custard into the pre-baked pie crust, spreading it evenly. Set the pie aside while you prepare the meringue topping.

  6. In a large mixing bowl, beat the egg whites and cream of tartar on medium speed until frothy. Gradually add the remaining 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, continuing to beat until stiff peaks form. The meringue should hold its shape when the beaters are lifted.

  7. Spoon the meringue onto the lemon custard, starting from the edges and working your way towards the center. Be sure to seal the edges of the pie crust to prevent the meringue from shrinking during baking.

  8. Use the back of a spoon to create peaks and swirls on the meringue, adding a touch of whimsy to the pie’s appearance.

  9. Place the pie on the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown. Keep a close eye on the pie to prevent it from burning.

  10. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. This will allow the meringue to set and the flavors to meld together.

  11. Once cooled, slice the pie into wedges and serve. The tangy lemon custard paired with the sweet meringue creates a delightful harmony of flavors that will leave your guests craving another slice.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of citrus flavor, try adding a teaspoon of lemon extract to the lemon custard mixture.
  • If you prefer a thicker meringue, increase the cream of tartar to 1/2 teaspoon.
  • To achieve a perfectly golden meringue, sprinkle a pinch of granulated sugar over the top before baking.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use a store-bought pie crust instead of making your own.

Conclusion

There you have it, a tantalizing recipe for a Mouthwatering Lemon Meringue Pie! Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a sweet delight, this dessert is sure to impress. The tangy lemon custard combined with the fluffy meringue topping creates a symphony of flavors that will leave everyone craving more. So, why wait? Gather your ingredients, preheat that oven, and get ready to savor every blissful bite of this classic dessert. Happy baking!
