Easy Middle Eastern Chicken and Rice Recipe with Yogurt


When you’re craving a dish that is both comforting and packed with flavor, nothing beats a hearty plate of Middle Eastern chicken and rice with yogurt. This vibrant and aromatic meal combines the tender succulence of seasoned chicken, the richness of long-grain rice, and the creamy tang of yogurt. It’s the kind of dish that transports your taste buds to the heart of the Middle East, where spices and fresh ingredients come together to create something truly special.

Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or preparing for a gathering, this recipe delivers both simplicity and sophistication. You don’t need to be an expert in Middle Eastern cuisine to make this dish; with just a few ingredients and easy steps, you can prepare a flavorful meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

The Essential Ingredients for Middle Eastern Chicken and Rice with Yogurt

To create this Middle Eastern-inspired chicken and rice dish, you will need a few key ingredients that bring together a rich combination of flavors. The good news is that these ingredients are easy to find at most grocery stores. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

For the Chicken:

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs: The bone and skin provide extra flavor and juiciness. You can also use boneless, skinless chicken if you prefer, but thighs are the best option for tenderness and flavor.
  • Olive oil: Adds richness and helps to crisp up the skin.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds depth and an aromatic base for the chicken.
  • Ground cumin: A key spice in Middle Eastern cuisine, cumin provides a warm, earthy flavor.
  • Ground coriander: This spice brings a citrusy, slightly floral note that complements the cumin.
  • Ground cinnamon: Adds a hint of sweetness and warmth.
  • Ground turmeric: Gives the dish a beautiful golden color and a slightly bitter flavor that balances the richness.
  • Salt and black pepper: Essential for seasoning the chicken.
  • Lemon juice: Adds acidity and freshness, balancing out the spices.

For the Rice:

  • Basmati rice: This long-grain rice is known for its fragrance and fluffy texture. It absorbs flavors well, making it the perfect base for this dish.
  • Butter: Adds richness and a subtle flavor to the rice.
  • Onion: A small onion, finely chopped, creates a savory base flavor.
  • Cinnamon stick: Adds a sweet and aromatic flavor to the rice as it cooks.
  • Cardamom pods: This spice has a sweet, floral taste that complements the richness of the rice.
  • Chicken broth: For cooking the rice and infusing it with flavor. You can use vegetable broth for a lighter option.

For the Yogurt Sauce:

  • Plain yogurt: Greek yogurt works particularly well, as it is thick and creamy, offering a nice balance of tartness.
  • Garlic: Minced garlic adds a punch of flavor to the yogurt.
  • Cucumber: Finely grated cucumber gives the sauce a refreshing and cooling element.
  • Fresh dill: A perfect herb to complement the tangy yogurt and cucumber. You can also use mint for a more traditional touch.
  • Lemon juice: A squeeze of lemon adds brightness and enhances the yogurt’s flavor.
  • Salt and black pepper: For seasoning the sauce.

Preparing the Chicken

The first step in making your Middle Eastern chicken and rice with yogurt is to prepare the chicken. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, ground cumin, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, salt, and black pepper. Add the chicken thighs to the bowl and coat them well with the marinade. For the best flavor, let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours if you have the time. If you want to take it a step further, you can marinate the chicken overnight in the refrigerator.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add a little olive oil to the pan, and once it’s hot, add the chicken thighs, skin-side down. Sear the chicken for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the skin is golden and crispy.

Once the chicken is browned, reduce the heat to medium-low and cover the pan. Let the chicken cook for an additional 20-25 minutes until it is fully cooked through and tender. The internal temperature of the chicken should reach 165°F (74°C).

Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside while you cook the rice.

Cooking the Rice

The rice is just as important as the chicken, as it absorbs all the delicious flavors from the spices and the chicken broth. Here’s how to make the perfect rice:

Step 1: Sauté the Onion and Spices

In the same pan where you cooked the chicken, add a bit more olive oil or butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 3-4 minutes, or until it becomes soft and translucent. Add the cinnamon stick and cardamom pods, and cook for another minute to release their aromatic oils.

Step 2: Add the Rice and Broth

Next, add the basmati rice to the pan and stir it into the onion mixture, making sure each grain of rice is coated with the spices and oil. Pour in the chicken broth and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the rice simmer for 15-20 minutes. The rice should be tender and the liquid absorbed by the time it’s done.

If you find the rice isn’t fully cooked but the liquid has been absorbed, simply add a little more chicken broth and cook for an additional few minutes.

Making the Yogurt Sauce

While the chicken and rice are cooking, it’s time to prepare the yogurt sauce. This cooling sauce provides a perfect contrast to the spiced chicken and rice.

Step 1: Combine the Ingredients

In a bowl, mix the yogurt with the grated cucumber, minced garlic, fresh dill, and lemon juice. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. The combination of tangy yogurt, refreshing cucumber, and aromatic dill will elevate the dish and bring balance to the richness of the chicken and rice.

You can refrigerate the sauce for about 10 minutes to let the flavors meld, but it’s not strictly necessary. It’s delicious served immediately.

Assembling the Dish

Once everything is ready, it’s time to assemble the Middle Eastern chicken and rice with yogurt.

  1. Fluff the cooked rice with a fork to separate the grains. Place a generous portion of rice onto each plate.
  2. Arrange one or two chicken thighs on top of the rice.
  3. Spoon a generous amount of the creamy yogurt sauce over the chicken and rice.
  4. Garnish with fresh herbs, such as cilantro or parsley, and a wedge of lemon on the side for added freshness.

Tips for Serving

  • Side Dishes: This dish pairs beautifully with a simple side salad made with fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onions, dressed with olive oil and lemon juice. You could also serve it with a warm pita bread or flatbread to scoop up the yogurt sauce.
  • Make it Spicy: If you like a little heat, consider adding a sprinkle of chili flakes or a few slices of fresh chili to the yogurt sauce or as a garnish.
  • Leftovers: This dish makes for fantastic leftovers. The chicken and rice can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, and the yogurt sauce can be stored separately for freshness.

Why This Recipe Works

This Middle Eastern chicken and rice with yogurt recipe works because of the balance of flavors and textures. The chicken is rich, juicy, and flavorful, thanks to the spices, while the rice is fragrant and perfectly cooked, providing a hearty base. The yogurt sauce brings everything together with its creamy, tangy profile, cooling down the heat from the spices and adding a refreshing element to the dish.

Additionally, this recipe is versatile and easy to adjust to your taste preferences. You can use boneless chicken, swap in different herbs or spices, and even modify the yogurt sauce by adding more garlic or dill. The simplicity of the ingredients makes this dish a great choice for both beginner and experienced cooks.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying meal that combines the bold flavors of the Middle East with the comfort of a hearty dinner, this easy Middle Eastern chicken and rice with yogurt recipe is sure to impress. Whether you’re making it for a special occasion or just a weeknight dinner, it’s a dish that will have your family and friends coming back for seconds. With tender chicken, aromatic rice, and a creamy yogurt sauce, this recipe brings together all the best elements of Middle Eastern cuisine in one easy-to-make meal.
