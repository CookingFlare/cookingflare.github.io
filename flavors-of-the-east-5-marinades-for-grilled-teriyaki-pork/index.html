Flavors of the East: 5 Marinades for Grilled Teriyaki Pork


Are you looking to add some mouthwatering flavors to your grilled teriyaki pork? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore five delicious and easy-to-make marinades inspired by the flavors of the East. These marinades will take your grilled teriyaki pork to a whole new level, leaving your taste buds craving for more.

1. Classic Teriyaki Marinade

Let’s start with the classic teriyaki marinade, a staple in Japanese cuisine. This marinade combines the perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors, resulting in a tender and juicy grilled teriyaki pork.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, mirin, rice vinegar, minced garlic, and grated ginger.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it. Ensure that the pork is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Grill the marinated pork over medium-high heat until cooked through, usually about 8-10 minutes per side.

2. Spicy Ginger-Sesame Marinade

If you’re a fan of bold flavors, this spicy ginger-sesame marinade is perfect for you. The combination of ginger, sesame oil, and chili flakes adds a kick to the grilled teriyaki pork, making it a delightful dish for those who enjoy a little heat.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (adjust to taste)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, and chili flakes.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it. Ensure that the pork is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Grill the marinated pork over medium-high heat until cooked through, approximately 8-10 minutes per side.

3. Citrus Soy Marinade

For a refreshing and tangy twist to your grilled teriyaki pork, try this citrus soy marinade. The combination of citrus flavors and soy sauce creates a delightful balance that will leave you wanting more.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, orange juice, lemon juice, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it. Ensure that the pork is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Grill the marinated pork over medium-high heat until cooked through, approximately 8-10 minutes per side.

4. Hoisin Glaze Marinade

If you’re a fan of rich, sweet, and savory flavors, this hoisin glaze marinade is a must-try. The combination of hoisin sauce, soy sauce, and garlic creates a sticky and flavorful glaze that will elevate your grilled teriyaki pork to new heights.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the hoisin sauce, soy sauce, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it. Ensure that the pork is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Grill the marinated pork over medium-high heat until cooked through, approximately 8-10 minutes per side.

5. Pineapple Brown Sugar Marinade

For a tropical twist to your grilled teriyaki pork, try this pineapple brown sugar marinade. The combination of sweet pineapple and brown sugar creates a caramelized glaze that perfectly complements the flavors of the grilled pork.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, pineapple juice, brown sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger.
  2. Place the pork in a resealable bag and pour the marinade over it. Ensure that the pork is fully coated.
  3. Seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Grill the marinated pork over medium-high heat until cooked through, approximately 8-10 minutes per side.

Now that you have five tantalizing marinades for grilled teriyaki pork, it’s time to fire up the grill and enjoy the flavors of the East. Whether you prefer the classic teriyaki marinade or want to spice things up with the spicy ginger-sesame marinade, these recipes are sure to impress your friends and family. So go ahead, experiment with these marinades, and let your taste buds embark on a culinary journey they won’t forget. Happy grilling!
