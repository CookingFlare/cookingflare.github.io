Cooking with Korean Rice Cakes: Quick and Easy Recipes


Korean rice cakes, also known as tteok or ttuk, are a staple ingredient in Korean cuisine. Made from glutinous rice flour, they can be boiled, steamed, or pan-fried to create a variety of dishes. In this post, we’ll explore some quick and easy recipes that showcase the versatility of Korean rice cakes.

Boiled Rice Cakes with Vegetables

This simple recipe is a great way to get started with Korean rice cakes. Boiling the rice cakes with some vegetables and seasonings creates a delicious and healthy snack or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Korean rice cakes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as zucchini, carrots, and bell peppers)
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon Gochujang (Korean chili paste)

Instructions:

  1. Cut the rice cakes into small pieces and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until they start to soften.
  4. Add the rice cakes and water to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the rice cakes are cooked through.
  5. Season with salt, black pepper, soy sauce, and Gochujang (if using). Serve hot.

Pan-Fried Rice Cakes with Spicy Gochujang Sauce

This recipe adds a spicy kick to the boiled rice cakes with a gochujang sauce. It’s a great appetizer or side dish for a Korean-inspired meal.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Korean rice cakes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the rice cakes into small pieces and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Heat one tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the rice cakes and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they’re golden brown and crispy.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the garlic, gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and red pepper flakes.
  4. Add the sauce to the skillet with the rice cakes and stir to combine. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  5. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped green onions (if using). Serve hot.

Steamed Rice Cakes with Sesame Oil and Sesame Seeds

This recipe is a great way to showcase the texture and flavor of Korean rice cakes. The sesame oil and sesame seeds add a nutty and aromatic flavor to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Korean rice cakes
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped scallions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the rice cakes into small pieces and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Place the rice cakes in a steamer basket and steam for 10-15 minutes, or until they’re cooked through.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the sesame oil and sesame seeds.
  4. Remove the rice cakes from the steamer and toss with the sesame oil and sesame seeds. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Garnish with chopped scallions (if using). Serve hot.

Rice Cakes with Spicy Pork and Kimchi

This recipe combines the Korean rice cakes with spicy pork and kimchi for a flavorful and filling meal. It’s a great option for a weeknight dinner or lunch.

Ingredients:

  • 200g Korean rice cakes
  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup kimchi, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the rice cakes into small pieces and rinse them under cold water.
  2. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground pork and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  3. Add the onion and garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent.
  4. Add the kimchi, gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, salt, and black pepper to the skillet. Stir to combine.
  5. Add the rice cakes to the skillet and stir to combine with the pork and kimchi mixture. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the rice cakes are heated through.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with chopped green onions (if using).

These recipes showcase the versatility of Korean rice cakes and can be easily adapted to suit your taste preferences. Whether you’re looking for a quick snack or a filling meal, Korean rice cakes are a great ingredient to work with.
