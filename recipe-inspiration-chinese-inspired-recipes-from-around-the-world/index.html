Recipe Inspiration: Chinese-Inspired Recipes from Around the World


Chinese cuisine is one of the oldest and most diverse in the world, with influence spreading from East Asia to various parts of the globe. As globalization continues to blur lines between cultures, we see fusion versions of classic Chinese recipes that incorporate different regional flavors and ingredients. In this blog post, we’ll explore some creative Chinese-inspired recipes from around the world.

1. Japanese-Style Kung Pao Chicken

If you love the intensity of kung pao chicken, the Japanese twist on this quintessential Chinese dish may surprise you. The dish gets a makeover with tender chicken, crispy veggies, and a savory-sweet sauce, but the pièce de résistance is the addition of Kewpie mayonnaise. This recipe creates a creamy texture that contrasts well with the crunch in each bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup julienned carrots
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup kewpie mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces, and cook until golden brown and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the carrots, snap peas, and red bell pepper. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, hoisin sauce, chili sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, salt, and pepper. Pour the mixture over the cooked vegetables and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and stir well to combine. Cook for another 3-5 minutes, or until the sauce thickens slightly and the chicken is heated through.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice or your favorite side dish.

2. Indian Butter Chicken with Chinese Egg Noodles

This fusion dish is a mouthwatering combination of the popular Indian butter chicken and Chinese egg noodles. The rich, spiced tomato gravy and tender chicken goes perfectly with the chewy noodles, offering a delightful twist on two beloved dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped garlic
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 10 ounces Chinese egg noodles, cooked according to package instructions
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil and butter in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Once the butter has melted, add the garlic, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the chopped onions and cook until soft and translucent. Then add the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute.
  3. Stir in the cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Cook for another 30 seconds to release the flavors of the spices.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook for about 10 minutes or until the tomatoes have broken down and the gravy has thickened.
  5. Add the chicken pieces to the gravy and cook until the chicken is cooked through and well coated with the sauce.
  6. Stir in the cooked Chinese egg noodles, ensuring they’re evenly coated with the sauce. Cook for a couple of more minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  7. Salt and pepper to taste, then garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

3. Vietnamese Beef and Broccoli Stir-fry with Oyster Sauce

This Vietnamese twist on the traditional Chinese beef and broccoli stir-fry delivers a fresh, zesty flavor that pairs perfectly with the tender beef and crisp-tender broccoli. The addition of oyster sauce and fish sauce adds depth and a unique savory-umami taste.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound flank steak, sliced thinly against the grain
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 green onions, sliced (separate the white and green parts)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the sliced flank steak, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Toss to ensure the steak is evenly coated. Set aside.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add half of the beef slices and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the cooked beef from the skillet and set it aside. Repeat the process with the remaining beef.
  3. In the same skillet, add the remaining 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil. Add the garlic, ginger, and white parts of the green onions. Cook until fragrant.
  4. Add the broccoli florets and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes, until they are tender but still crisp.
  5. In a bowl, whisk together the oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, and honey. Pour the mixture over the broccoli and cook for another minute.
  6. Return the cooked beef to the skillet and toss well to coat with the sauce. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, until heated through.
  7. Garnish with the sliced green parts of the green onions before serving.

These globally-inspired recipes showcase just how versatile traditional Chinese dishes can be. They honor the origins of these classic flavors while infusing them with new influences - inviting you to experiment with foods from various cultures and create dishes that are unique and unforgettable.

Enjoy exploring and discovering new flavors, as the culinary world is a vast playground waiting to be explored. Happy cooking!
