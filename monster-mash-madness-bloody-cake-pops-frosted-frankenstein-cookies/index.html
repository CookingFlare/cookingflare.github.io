Monster Mash Madness: Bloody Cake Pops & Frosted Frankenstein Cookies


What’s spookier than a ghost? A little twist to your traditional sweets! Feast your eyes and taste buds on two monstrous dishes that blend the worlds of Halloween and Horror with the sugary goodness of your favorite treats. Prepare to embark on a delectable journey with our Bloody Cake Pops and Frosted Frankenstein Cookies.

Bloody Cake Pops: A Twist on a Classic

Blood-red cake in a hand-sculpted form makes this sweet treat spooky and spooktacular! Ready to fright the crowd with these bite-sized monsters? Let’s get started.

Ingredients:

  • 1 box red velvet cake mix
  • 1 (16 oz) can of frosting (red velvet flavor for extra impact)
  • 2 cups cake pop mix-ins, such as crushed Oreos, gummy brains, or candy eyeballs
  • 12+ oz red candy melts or coating
  • 6-8 red food coloring gel
  • Lollipop sticks

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the red velvet cake mix according to the package instructions, swapping water with the same amount of red velvet frosting to enhance the color and flavor. Allow it to cool down completely.
  2. Once cooled, crumble the cake until it resembles fine crumbs.
  3. Mix in cake pop mix-ins until well combined.
  4. Grease hands with cooking spray or roll the crumbled cake in your hands to form 1-inch balls.
  5. Gently insert lollipop sticks into the cake balls and place them into the freezer for 15-30 minutes or until firm.
  6. Prepare the candy melts by melting them in a microwave-safe bowl for 30 seconds, then stirring, and repeating as necessary until fully melted. Add four drops of red food coloring gel and continue mixing until you achieve the desired blood-red color.
  7. One by one, dip each cake pop into the candy melts, ensuring they are fully coated.
  8. Before the coating hardens, gently roll the cake pop in crushed Oreo or your preferred mix-in. Then, place them in a cupcake liner for serving. Add a gummy brain or candy eye for a finishing touch.

Frosted Frankenstein Cookies: Baked with Love for Horror Lovers

Like the famous monster, these Frosted Frankenstein Cookies are part monster and part human. An ode to the classic horror character, let’s get the gears turning and bake up some monstrous treats!

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 cups unsifted powdered sugar
  • 4-6 tablespoons heavy cream or milk
  • Green gel food coloring
  • Yellow gel food coloring
  • White chocolate chips or white frosting

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  2. In another bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy, then add the egg and vanilla extract and continue mixing.
  3. Slowly incorporate the dry ingredients while continuously mixing until combined.
  4. Roll the dough into small balls and place them onto a lightly greased baking sheet. Flatten the cookies slightly using a spoon or the bottom of a glass. Bake at 350℉ or 180℃ for 9-11 minutes or until golden.
  5. While the cookies cool, prepare the frosting by mixing powdered sugar, heavy cream or milk, and green and yellow gel food coloring in a separate bowl. Adjust the consistency and colors as desired.
  6. Once the cookies have cooled, spread green frosting on half of the cookies (these will be the green parts of Frankenstein), and yellow frosting on the other half (the means the breadcrumbs were made from human remains!).
  7. Lastly, attach the two cookies with white chocolate chips or white frosting in a zigzag pattern to create the bolts on the neck.

And just like that, your reign of terror and concoction of frightful fun begin! Playing with your food has surely never been this entertaining before. So, get ready to channel your inner monster baker and bring this thrills and chills theme to life through these dishes.

Remember, not only do these treats look horrifyingly delicious, but they are surprisingly simple to make at home. The perfect combination of gooey cake pops infused with gory details and frosty Frankenstein-inspired cookies will make monster mash madness a hit at your next Halloween gathering. Don’t forget to include vampire punch, werewolf punch, and ghostly green macarons for a full frightful affair.

Get baking, and may the monstrous madness begin!
