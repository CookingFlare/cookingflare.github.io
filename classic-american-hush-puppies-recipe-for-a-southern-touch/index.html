Classic American Hush Puppies Recipe for a Southern Touch


If you’re looking to bring a taste of the American South into your kitchen, then this Classic Hush Puppies recipe is just what you need. These little balls of deep-fried cornmeal batter are a beloved Southern staple, often served as a side dish alongside fried fish or barbecued meats. With their crispy exterior and soft, flavorful interior, hush puppies are a delightful treat that will transport you to the heart of Southern comfort food. Let’s dive into the recipe and bring a touch of the South to your table.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow cornmeal
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onion
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, onion powder, garlic powder, and cayenne pepper. Mix well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, whole milk, and beaten egg. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring gently to combine. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough hush puppies.

  3. Fold in the finely chopped onion until it’s evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  4. In a heavy-bottomed pot or deep fryer, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil to 375°F (190°C).

  5. Using a spoon or small ice cream scoop, carefully drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil. Fry the hush puppies in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Fry for 2-3 minutes, or until they are golden brown on all sides, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.

  6. Using a slotted spoon, remove the hush puppies from the oil and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

  7. Serve the hush puppies warm and enjoy the crispy, golden goodness of this Southern classic.

Serving Suggestions

Classic American Hush Puppies are incredibly versatile and can be served in various ways. They make a fantastic side dish for fried fish, shrimp, or any seafood dish. They also pair well with barbecued meats, providing a delightful contrast to the smoky flavors. For a fun twist, consider serving them with a drizzle of honey or a side of remoulade sauce for dipping.

Tips and Tricks

  • To ensure a light and airy texture, avoid overmixing the batter. A few lumps are perfectly fine.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, feel free to increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the batter.
  • For a touch of sweetness, you can add a tablespoon of sugar to the batter.

Whether you’re hosting a Southern-themed gathering or simply craving a taste of the South, this Classic American Hush Puppies recipe is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, get your oil hot, and let the aroma of these golden nuggets fill your kitchen. Embrace the spirit of Southern hospitality and indulge in the irresistible charm of hush puppies.
