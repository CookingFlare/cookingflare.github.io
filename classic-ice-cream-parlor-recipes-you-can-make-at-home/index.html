Classic Ice Cream Parlor Recipes You Can Make at Home


As the warm sun shines down and summer beckons, there’s nothing quite like the joy of a classic ice cream treat. The nostalgic charm of an ice cream parlor, with its vibrant flavors and creamy textures, can easily be recreated in the comfort of your own kitchen. This guide will take you through some classic ice cream parlor recipes that are not only easy to make but will also transport you back to those delightful days of indulging in sweet, frozen delights. Get ready to scoop, swirl, and savor the flavors of homemade ice cream!

1. Vanilla Bean Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 2 teaspoons vanilla extract)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 3/4 cup egg yolks (about 5 large yolks)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vanilla Bean: If using a vanilla bean, split it lengthwise and scrape the seeds into a saucepan. Add the empty pod, heavy cream, and milk to the saucepan. If using vanilla extract, add it later in the process.

  2. Heat the Mixture: Over medium heat, whisk the mixture until it starts to steam, but do not let it boil. Remove from heat and let it steep for 30 minutes to infuse the vanilla flavor.

  3. Whisk Egg Yolks: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and salt until pale and creamy.

  4. Temper the Egg Yolks: Gradually pour the warm cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to avoid scrambling the eggs.

  5. Cook the Custard: Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly until the custard thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon (about 170°F or 77°C).

  6. Cool and Chill: Remove from heat, strain the mixture to remove the vanilla pod, and stir in the vanilla extract if using. Let it cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until completely chilled, preferably overnight.

  7. Churn: Pour the chilled custard into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  8. Freeze: Transfer the ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Tips:

  • For an extra indulgent treat, fold in mini chocolate chips or crushed cookies during the last few minutes of churning.

2. Chocolate Fudge Brownie Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 1 batch of vanilla bean ice cream (see recipe above)
  • 1 cup brownie pieces (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup chocolate fudge sauce

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: Start by making your vanilla bean ice cream as per the recipe above.

  2. Fold in Brownies: Once the vanilla ice cream is churned and at a soft-serve consistency, gently fold in the brownie pieces and chocolate fudge sauce until evenly distributed.

  3. Freeze: Transfer the mixture to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours until firm.

  4. Serve: Scoop into bowls or cones and drizzle with additional fudge sauce if desired.

Tips:

  • For added texture, sprinkle crushed nuts or chocolate chips on top before freezing.

3. Strawberry Swirl Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (divided)
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Make the Strawberry Sauce: In a saucepan, combine strawberries and 1/4 cup of sugar. Cook over medium heat until the strawberries break down and release their juices (about 10 minutes). Remove from heat and let cool.

  2. Prepare the Ice Cream Base: In a separate bowl, whisk together heavy cream, milk, remaining sugar, vanilla extract, and salt until the sugar dissolves.

  3. Chill the Base: Refrigerate the mixture for at least 1 hour or until completely chilled.

  4. Churn: Pour the chilled mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  5. Swirl in Strawberry Sauce: Transfer half of the ice cream to an airtight container. Spoon half of the strawberry sauce over it, then layer with the remaining ice cream and the rest of the sauce. Use a knife to swirl together gently.

  6. Freeze: Freeze for at least 4 hours until firm.

Tips:

  • For a more intense strawberry flavor, use a combination of fresh and frozen strawberries.

4. Mint Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips
  • Green food coloring (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: In a bowl, whisk together heavy cream, milk, sugar, peppermint extract, and salt until the sugar is dissolved.

  2. Chill the Mixture: Refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or until thoroughly chilled.

  3. Churn: Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn until it reaches a soft-serve consistency. If desired, add a few drops of green food coloring during the last few minutes of churning.

  4. Add Chocolate Chips: Fold in the mini chocolate chips before transferring the ice cream to an airtight container.

  5. Freeze: Freeze for at least 4 hours until firm.

Tips:

  • For an even more refreshing treat, garnish with fresh mint leaves when serving.

5. Cookies and Cream Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup crushed chocolate sandwich cookies
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a bowl, whisk together heavy cream, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt until the sugar dissolves.

  2. Chill the Mixture: Refrigerate for at least 1 hour or until thoroughly chilled.

  3. Churn: Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  4. Fold in Cookies: Gently fold in the crushed chocolate sandwich cookies.

  5. Freeze: Transfer to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours until firm.

Tips:

  • For a more intense flavor, use double-stuffed sandwich cookies.

6. Coffee Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brewed coffee (strong)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Mix Ingredients: In a bowl, combine heavy cream, whole milk, granulated sugar, brewed coffee, vanilla extract, and salt. Whisk until the sugar is dissolved.

  2. Chill the Mixture: Refrigerate for at least 1 hour or until thoroughly chilled.

  3. Churn: Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  4. Freeze: Transfer to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours until firm.

Tips:

  • For a mocha twist, add chocolate syrup during the last few minutes of churning.

7. No-Churn Ice Cream Variations

If you don’t have an ice cream maker, fear not! You can still enjoy homemade ice cream with these simple no-churn recipes.

No-Churn Vanilla Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Whip the Cream: In a large bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.

  2. Fold in Condensed Milk: Gently fold the sweetened condensed milk and vanilla extract into the whipped cream until well combined.

  3. Freeze: Transfer to an airtight container and freeze for at least 6 hours until firm.

No-Churn Chocolate Ice Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Whip the Cream: Whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form.

  2. Combine Ingredients: Fold in the sweetened condensed milk, cocoa powder, and vanilla extract until smooth.

  3. Freeze: Transfer to an airtight container and freeze for at least 6 hours until firm.

Conclusion

Creating classic ice cream parlor recipes at home is a delightful way to enjoy sweet treats all summer long. With simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can whip up delicious flavors that will impress your family and friends. Whether you prefer the rich and creamy texture of vanilla bean, the indulgence of chocolate fudge brownie, or the refreshing notes of mint chocolate chip, these recipes provide a fantastic base for your ice cream creations. So gather your ingredients, grab your ice cream maker (or not!), and get ready to scoop up some happiness in a bowl. Happy churning!
