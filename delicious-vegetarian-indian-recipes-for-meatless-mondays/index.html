Delicious Vegetarian Indian Recipes for Meatless Mondays


Are you looking to add some flavor to your Meatless Mondays? Indian cuisine offers a rich variety of vegetarian dishes that are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients and bold flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out on your culinary journey, these vegetarian Indian recipes are sure to impress your taste buds and keep you coming back for more.

1. Chana Masala

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chickpeas
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the chickpeas overnight and cook until tender.
  2. In a pan, heat some oil and add the cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add the onions, garlic, ginger, and green chili. Saute until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add the pureed tomatoes, coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  4. Add the cooked chickpeas and some water if needed. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan.

2. Palak Paneer

Ingredients:

  • 2 bunches of spinach, blanched and pureed
  • 200g paneer, cubed
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat some oil and add the cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add the onions, garlic, and ginger. Saute until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add the pureed tomatoes, garam masala, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  3. Add the pureed spinach and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Gently add the cubed paneer and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and serve hot with roti or naan.

3. Aloo Gobi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, heat some oil and add the cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add the onions, garlic, and ginger. Saute until the onions turn golden brown.
  2. Add the pureed tomatoes, coriander powder, turmeric, garam masala, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  3. Add the cauliflower and potatoes, and stir to coat them with the masala.
  4. Cover and cook on low heat until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with steamed rice or roti.

Conclusion

These vegetarian Indian recipes are not only perfect for Meatless Mondays but can be enjoyed any day of the week. They offer a burst of exotic flavors and wholesome ingredients that will leave you craving more. So, go ahead, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to India right in your own kitchen. Your taste buds will thank you!
