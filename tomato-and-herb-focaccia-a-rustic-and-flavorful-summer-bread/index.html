Tomato and Herb Focaccia: A Rustic and Flavorful Summer Bread


Summer is the perfect time to indulge in fresh, flavorful breads that complement the season’s bounty. Among the many delights of the Mediterranean diet, focaccia is a staple that never fails to impress. In this recipe, we’ll combine the classic Italian flatbread with the sweetness of tomatoes and the brightness of herbs, creating a rustic and aromatic summer bread that’s sure to become a favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chopped fresh tomatoes (such as cherry or grape tomatoes)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh oregano
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • Coarse sea salt, for topping

Instructions

  1. Proof the yeast: In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Let it sit for 5-7 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy and bubbly.
  2. Mix the dough: Add the olive oil, salt, and 2 cups of flour to the bowl. Mix until a shaggy dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough: Gradually add the remaining cup of flour, kneading the dough for 10-12 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment or knead by hand.
  4. Let the dough rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours until it has doubled in size.
  5. Prepare the topping: In a small bowl, combine the chopped tomatoes, basil, oregano, and garlic.
  6. Shape the dough: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and shape it into a rectangle or oval shape, about 1/2-inch thick.
  7. Top the dough: Spread the tomato and herb mixture evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  8. Drizzle with olive oil: Drizzle the extra-virgin olive oil over the toppings.
  9. Season with salt: Sprinkle coarse sea salt over the top.
  10. Bake the focaccia: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place the focaccia on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 20-25 minutes until the bread is golden brown and the toppings are caramelized.
  11. Cool and serve: Let the focaccia cool for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense tomato flavor, use 2 cups of chopped tomatoes and reduce the amount of fresh herbs.
  • Substitute the basil and oregano with other herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley for a different flavor profile.
  • Add some crumbled feta cheese or grated Parmesan for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Use this recipe as a base and experiment with different toppings, such as roasted vegetables, caramelized onions, or sun-dried tomatoes.

Conclusion

Tomato and herb focaccia is a rustic and flavorful summer bread that’s perfect for picnics, barbecues, or simply enjoying with a glass of wine. The combination of fresh tomatoes and herbs creates a bright, refreshing flavor that’s sure to become a staple in your summer repertoire. So go ahead, get creative with your toppings, and enjoy the warmth of the season with a delicious homemade bread.
