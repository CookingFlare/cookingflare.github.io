Sous Vide Squash Soup: Fall Comfort Food


As the crisp air of autumn settles in, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of soup to make you feel cozy and satisfied. Squash soup, especially made with the sous vide method, elevates a classic fall favorite to a whole new level. The sous vide cooking technique locks in the flavors, creating a rich, velvety texture that will have you reaching for seconds.

In this post, we will explore everything you need to know to make the perfect sous vide squash soup, including tips on selecting the best squash, seasoning, and how sous vide cooking can transform this humble dish into an extraordinary comfort food.

Why Choose Sous Vide for Squash Soup?

Sous vide, a French cooking technique that means “under vacuum,” involves sealing ingredients in a bag and cooking them slowly in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method ensures that your food cooks evenly, retaining moisture, nutrients, and flavor.

When it comes to squash soup, sous vide cooking is an absolute game-changer. Here’s why:

  • Enhanced Flavor: Sous vide allows the squash to release its natural sweetness and rich flavors, making the soup taste more intense and delicious.
  • Perfect Texture: The slow cooking process preserves the squash’s natural texture, resulting in a creamy, smooth, and velvety soup without the need for added cream or butter.
  • Hands-off Cooking: Once the squash is sealed in the bag and submerged in the water bath, you can leave it to cook without constantly checking it, giving you more time to focus on other tasks.

Let’s dive into the step-by-step process for making a comforting batch of sous vide squash soup.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Squash Soup

Before you start, gather the following ingredients:

For the Soup:

  • 2 medium-sized butternut squash (or your preferred type of squash like acorn or pumpkin)
  • 1 large onion, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (you can substitute chicken broth if you prefer)
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Optional Garnishes:

  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • Croutons or toasted pumpkin seeds
  • Fresh herbs (like thyme or parsley)
  • A drizzle of olive oil or a swirl of coconut milk for a dairy-free version

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Squash

Start by peeling the butternut squash and cutting it into chunks. You can also use other types of squash like acorn or kabocha for slightly different flavors. The beauty of sous vide cooking is that you can adjust the type of squash based on your preferences. Butternut squash tends to have a sweet, nutty flavor, making it perfect for fall soups.

Once the squash is peeled and cut, place the chunks into a large vacuum-seal bag or a resealable ziplock bag. Add the chopped onion, garlic, olive oil, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt and pepper.

2. Vacuum Seal or Seal the Bag

If you have a vacuum sealer, now is the time to use it. Vacuum sealing helps to ensure that the squash and aromatics are tightly sealed in the bag, maximizing flavor infusion. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use the water displacement method with a ziplock bag. Simply seal the bag almost all the way and slowly lower it into the water, allowing the pressure to push the air out before sealing it completely.

3. Sous Vide Cooking

Set your sous vide machine to 185°F (85°C). This temperature is ideal for achieving a perfectly tender and flavorful squash. Once the water bath reaches the desired temperature, submerge the sealed bag into the water. Let it cook for 1.5 to 2 hours. The beauty of sous vide is that you don’t have to worry about overcooking—your squash will cook perfectly in this time window, and the flavors will meld beautifully.

4. Blending the Soup

After the squash and aromatics have cooked, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Pour the contents of the bag into a blender or food processor. At this point, the squash should be incredibly soft, and the flavors should have developed into a deep, fragrant base for your soup.

Blend the soup on high until it reaches a smooth, velvety texture. If you prefer a thinner consistency, you can add vegetable broth, one tablespoon at a time, until it’s the desired thickness. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or spices as needed.

5. Final Touches

Transfer the blended soup to a pot and heat it over medium heat, stirring occasionally. This step ensures that the soup is nice and hot before serving. You can also adjust the consistency here by adding more broth if you like a lighter soup, or let it simmer for a few minutes if you want it a little thicker.

Once your soup is heated through, serve it in bowls, garnishing with a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt, a sprinkle of croutons or toasted pumpkin seeds, and a drizzle of olive oil or coconut milk for a bit of richness. A sprinkle of fresh herbs like thyme or parsley can also brighten up the dish.

Variations and Tips for Perfecting Your Soup

1. Different Squash Varieties

While butternut squash is a popular choice, feel free to experiment with other varieties to create unique flavor profiles. For example:

  • Acorn squash adds a slightly earthy, sweeter flavor.
  • Kabocha squash gives the soup a rich, creamy texture with a mild sweetness.
  • Delicata squash has a thin skin, making it easy to prep and cook.

2. Spice Variations

If you want to play around with the flavors, try adding other spices like cumin, coriander, or allspice. A pinch of chili flakes can give your soup a subtle kick, while a splash of apple cider vinegar or a squeeze of lemon juice can add brightness.

3. For a Creamier Soup

While the sous vide method produces a naturally creamy texture, you can enhance the richness by adding a splash of heavy cream, coconut milk, or half-and-half once the soup is blended. For a dairy-free option, coconut milk is a great substitute, offering a smooth texture and a touch of sweetness that pairs wonderfully with the squash.

4. Topping Ideas

A sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds adds a delightful crunch to the soup, while croutons provide an extra layer of texture. For a fun twist, try drizzling the soup with a little balsamic glaze or a dash of smoked paprika for a smoky finish.

5. Make-Ahead and Storage Tips

Sous vide squash soup is an excellent make-ahead dish. You can prepare it a day or two in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The soup can also be frozen for up to 3 months—just be sure to let it cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container. To reheat, simply warm it over low heat on the stovetop, adding a bit of broth if necessary to reach the desired consistency.

The Perfect Fall Comfort Food

Sous vide squash soup is the epitome of fall comfort food. Whether you’re serving it for a family dinner, a cozy evening in, or as part of your Thanksgiving spread, this soup will quickly become a seasonal favorite. The sous vide technique not only enhances the flavors but also makes the process simple and stress-free, leaving you more time to enjoy the autumn season.

With its creamy texture, rich flavors, and endless variations, this squash soup is sure to warm both your body and soul. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the sous vide machine, and enjoy the ultimate fall comfort food.
