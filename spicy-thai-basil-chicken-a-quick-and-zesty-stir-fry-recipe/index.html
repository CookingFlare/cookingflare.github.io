Spicy Thai Basil Chicken: A Quick and Zesty Stir-Fry Recipe


If you’re craving a dish that bursts with flavor and can be whipped up in no time, look no further than Spicy Thai Basil Chicken. This delectable stir-fry is not only easy to prepare, but it also brings a delightful mix of spices and fresh ingredients that will tantalize your taste buds. Whether you’re a novice cook or a seasoned chef, this recipe is sure to impress.

What is Spicy Thai Basil Chicken?

Spicy Thai Basil Chicken, known as Pad Krapow Gai in Thailand, is a popular street food that features ground chicken or diced chicken stir-fried with Thai basil leaves, garlic, and fresh chilies. The dish is renowned for its savory and spicy profile, making it a favorite among those who enjoy a kick in their meal. The fragrant basil adds a unique aromatic touch that pairs beautifully with the umami flavors of soy sauce and fish sauce.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this mouthwatering dish, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken Stir-Fry:

  • 1 pound ground chicken (or diced boneless chicken breast)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any neutral oil)
  • 5 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 Thai bird chilies (sliced thinly, adjust to taste)
  • 1 red bell pepper (sliced)
  • 1 cup Thai basil leaves (fresh, packed)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice (freshly squeezed)

For Serving:

  • Cooked jasmine rice (or rice of your choice)
  • Lime wedges
  • Extra Thai basil leaves (for garnish)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Before you start cooking, it’s essential to prepare all your ingredients. This includes mincing the garlic, slicing the chilies and bell pepper, and washing the Thai basil leaves. Having everything ready will make the cooking process smoother and faster.

Step 2: Heat the Oil

In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. This step is crucial as it ensures that the chicken will fry properly and develop a nice texture.

Step 3: Sauté the Aromatics

Add the minced garlic and sliced chilies to the hot oil. Stir-fry them for about 30 seconds or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken

Next, add the ground chicken to the skillet. Break it apart with a spatula and stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and no longer pink. If you’re using diced chicken breast, it may take a bit longer, so ensure it’s cooked thoroughly.

Step 5: Add the Vegetables

Once the chicken is cooked, toss in the sliced red bell pepper. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the peppers are slightly softened but still crisp. The vibrant color of the bell peppers will add a delightful crunch to the dish.

Step 6: Season the Stir-Fry

Now it’s time to add the sauces! Pour in the soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Stir everything together, ensuring the chicken and vegetables are well-coated in the sauce. Allow it to cook for an additional 1-2 minutes to let the flavors meld.

Step 7: Add the Thai Basil

Finally, remove the skillet from heat and fold in the fresh Thai basil leaves. The residual heat will wilt the basil just enough to release its fragrant aroma without losing its bright green color.

Step 8: Finish with Lime Juice

For a zesty finish, squeeze fresh lime juice over the stir-fry. This will elevate the dish, balancing the richness of the sauces with a refreshing tang.

Serving Suggestions

To serve your Spicy Thai Basil Chicken, spoon the stir-fry over a generous portion of fluffy jasmine rice. The rice will soak up the flavorful juices, making each bite delightful. Garnish with additional Thai basil leaves and lime wedges on the side for an extra pop of flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Dish

  1. Adjust the Heat: If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the number of chilies or remove the seeds. Conversely, for those who love heat, feel free to add more chilies or even a splash of chili sauce.

  2. Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh Thai basil makes a significant difference in flavor. If you can’t find Thai basil, regular basil can be used, but the taste will be slightly different.

  3. Customize Your Protein: While ground chicken is traditional, you can substitute it with ground pork, beef, or tofu for a vegetarian option.

  4. Meal Prep Friendly: This dish stores well in the fridge for up to three days, making it perfect for meal prep. Simply reheat in a skillet and serve over fresh rice.

  5. Pair with Sides: Consider pairing this dish with a light cucumber salad or spring rolls for a complete Thai-inspired meal.

Nutritional Information

While the specific nutritional values can vary based on ingredient choices, a standard serving of Spicy Thai Basil Chicken is a great source of protein and packed with vitamins from the fresh vegetables and herbs. It’s a well-rounded dish that provides energy and satisfies cravings without being overly heavy.

Conclusion

Spicy Thai Basil Chicken is a fantastic addition to your weeknight dinner rotation. Its vibrant flavors, quick preparation, and versatility make it a go-to recipe for those busy evenings when you want something quick yet impressive. Whether you’re serving it for yourself or entertaining guests, this dish is sure to bring the essence of Thailand into your home kitchen.

So, grab your ingredients and get ready to enjoy a quick and zesty meal that will transport you straight to the bustling streets of Bangkok. Happy cooking!
