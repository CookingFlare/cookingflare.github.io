Grilled Eggplant Parmesan: A Vegetarian Favorite


You’re probably familiar with the classic eggplant Parmesan that’s baked in the oven, but have you tried grilling your eggplant before layering it with cheese and tomato sauce? Grilled Eggplant Parmesan adds a smoky, firefighterscale grilled flavor to a favorite Italian dish. It’s an excellent vegetarian main course that’s light enough for summer and hearty enough for winter.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups of panko bread crumbs
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 2 cups of grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1½ cups of pasta sauce
  • ½ cup of grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preparing the eggplants. Slice them into ½-inch thick rounds. Then, salt the slices and place them in a colander to drain for 30 minutes.

  2. After salting, rinse the eggplant slices to remove the excess salt, squeeze out the excess water, and pat them dry with a paper towel.

  3. Next, set up the breading station. Place the flour in a shallow dish and set aside. In another dish, beat the eggs with a little water to make an egg wash. Put the panko bread crumbs in a third dish.

  4. Dip each eggplant slice first into the flour, then into the egg wash, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat the slice in the panko bread crumbs, pressing gently to adhere.

  5. Heat a grill pan or grill over medium-high heat. Add a small amount of olive oil and brush it evenly over the surface.

  6. Carefully place the breaded eggplant slices on the oiled surface. Grill for 3-4 minutes on each side, until they’re tender and golden-brown.

  7. While the eggplant slices are cooking, preheat your oven to 350°F.

  8. Time to assemble the parmigiana. Arrange half of the grilled slices in a single layer in a greased baking dish.

  9. Sprinkle half of the mozzarella cheese and Parmesan over the eggplants, then layer on the pasta sauce, reserving a small amount for the top.

  10. Repeat the layering process using the remainder of the eggplant slices, mozzarella cheese, Parmesan, and pasta sauce.

  11. If you’d like, sprinkle some additional Parmesan over the top.

  12. Place the baking dish in the oven and bake for 20-30 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and melted, and the dish is heated through.

  13. Remove the baking dish from the oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes. Slice the eggplant into portions, garnish with your favorite herb, and serve it with warm garlic bread, pasta salad, or a fresh green salad.

Variations:

Why not mix it up and try some of these variations to make grilled eggplant Parmesan your own:

  • Replace the panko bread crumbs with crushed chips, crackers, or pretzels.
  • Add a little spicy heat with some blackened seasoning, red pepper flakes, or jalapenos.
  • Spruce up the pasta sauce by adding roasted garlic, sun-dried tomatoes, or spinach.
  • Swap out the mozzarella cheese for provolone, Asiago, or even ricotta cheese.

For cooks looking to elevate their cooking skills or vegetarians desiring a delectable main course, grilled Eggplant Parmesan hits the spot. With tweaks and swaps, it’s an adaptable meal that will become a regular in your culinary repertoire. Enjoy!
