Thai Cuisine Made Easy: 7 Popular Street Food Recipes


Thai cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and intricate balance of sweet, salty, sour, and spicy elements. The street food culture in Thailand is an essential part of daily life, offering a wide variety of dishes that showcase the richness of Thai culinary traditions. Whether you’re strolling through the bustling streets of Bangkok or enjoying a quiet evening at home, Thai street food provides a delicious and authentic experience.

1. Pad Thai: The Iconic Thai Stir-Fry Noodles

Pad Thai is one of the most famous dishes of Thai street food and a beloved favorite around the world. It’s a quick stir-fry made with rice noodles, eggs, tofu or shrimp, and a tangy sauce that brings together the perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 150g shrimp or tofu (optional)
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, chopped
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • 1/4 tsp chili flakes (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together tamarind paste, fish sauce, sugar, and lime juice to make the sauce.
  3. Heat a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add a little oil and stir-fry the garlic for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the shrimp or tofu and cook until fully cooked through. Push to one side of the pan.
  5. Crack the eggs into the pan and scramble them until cooked through.
  6. Add the noodles to the pan and pour the sauce over the noodles. Toss everything together, making sure the noodles are well coated.
  7. Stir in the bean sprouts and spring onions, and cook for another minute.
  8. Serve hot with a sprinkle of chopped peanuts, fresh coriander, and chili flakes on top for added flavor.

Pad Thai is best enjoyed immediately, and it pairs wonderfully with a cold Thai iced tea for a complete street food experience.

2. Som Tum: Spicy Green Papaya Salad

Som Tum is a refreshing and spicy salad that is perfect for hot days. This iconic dish combines shredded green papaya with chilies, garlic, peanuts, and a zesty lime dressing. It’s sweet, sour, spicy, and utterly addictive.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2-3 bird’s eye chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 10 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces

Instructions:

  1. Using a mortar and pestle, pound the garlic and chilies together until they’re smashed into a paste.
  2. Add the palm sugar, fish sauce, and lime juice, and continue to pound until everything is well mixed.
  3. Add the shredded papaya, green beans, and tomatoes to the mortar. Use the pestle to gently pound and mix everything together until well combined.
  4. Stir in the peanuts and adjust the seasoning to taste, adding more lime juice or fish sauce if necessary.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with more peanuts and lime wedges on the side.

Som Tum is the perfect combination of crunchy, spicy, and refreshing flavors. It’s a great appetizer or side dish that pairs well with grilled meats or sticky rice.

3. Moo Ping: Grilled Pork Skewers

Moo Ping is a popular street food in Thailand, known for its flavorful grilled pork skewers. The pork is marinated in a savory-sweet mixture and then grilled to perfection, making it a tasty and satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork shoulder or pork belly, sliced into thin strips
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the garlic, black pepper, soy sauce, fish sauce, palm sugar, oyster sauce, and coconut milk to create the marinade.
  2. Add the pork strips to the bowl and mix well, making sure the meat is fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for the best flavor.
  3. Thread the marinated pork onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
  4. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the pork is cooked through and has a nice char.
  5. Serve with sticky rice and a side of sweet chili dipping sauce.

Moo Ping is a great dish to serve at barbecues or as part of a larger Thai-inspired meal. Its smoky, sweet, and savory flavor is sure to impress your guests.

4. Satay Gai: Thai Chicken Skewers

Satay Gai is a Thai variation of chicken satay, a dish that is incredibly popular in Southeast Asia. The chicken is marinated in a fragrant mixture of coconut milk, turmeric, and spices, then grilled and served with a creamy peanut sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast or thighs, cut into thin strips
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

For the peanut sauce:

  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tsp chili paste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the coconut milk, soy sauce, fish sauce, lime juice, turmeric powder, garlic, and brown sugar to create the marinade.
  2. Add the chicken strips to the bowl and mix until well coated. Marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Thread the marinated chicken onto the soaked bamboo skewers.
  4. Preheat the grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Grill the chicken for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until fully cooked and slightly charred.
  5. While the chicken cooks, prepare the peanut sauce by whisking together peanut butter, soy sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, warm water, and chili paste in a bowl until smooth.
  6. Serve the chicken skewers with the peanut sauce on the side for dipping.

Satay Gai is a crowd-pleasing dish thatâ€™s perfect for appetizers or a main course. The rich peanut sauce adds an irresistible creamy texture that pairs wonderfully with the grilled chicken.

5. Khao Man Gai: Thai-Style Hainanese Chicken Rice

Khao Man Gai is a simple yet satisfying dish that consists of tender poached chicken served with flavorful rice cooked in chicken fat. This Thai version of Hainanese chicken rice is commonly served as a street food dish, especially in busy food markets.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 1.5kg)
  • 2 cups jasmine rice
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Cucumber slices, for garnish

For the dipping sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1-2 birdâ€™s eye chilies, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and add the whole chicken. Poach the chicken for about 45 minutes, or until the meat is tender and fully cooked.
  2. Remove the chicken from the pot, saving the broth for later. Let the chicken cool, then chop it into pieces.
  3. In a separate pan, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, cooking for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the rice to the pan and stir for 2-3 minutes, ensuring the rice is well coated in the garlic-ginger mixture.
  5. Add the chicken broth to the rice and cook according to package instructions.
  6. To make the dipping sauce, combine soy sauce, sugar, lime juice, and chopped chilies in a small bowl. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  7. Serve the chicken over the fragrant rice, garnished with cucumber slices, and drizzle with the dipping sauce.

Khao Man Gai is a comforting dish thatâ€™s both hearty and refreshing. Itâ€™s perfect for a quick lunch or dinner, and the dipping sauce adds a delightful kick to every bite.

6. Thai Fish Cakes: Tod Mun Pla

Tod Mun Pla are crispy, flavorful fish cakes commonly served as a street snack in Thailand. Made with minced fish, red curry paste, and fresh herbs, these fish cakes are deep-fried to golden perfection and served with a sweet and spicy dipping sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 500g white fish fillets (like tilapia or snapper), minced
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/4 cup green beans, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup kaffir lime leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the dipping sauce:

  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 small red chili, sliced

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the minced fish, red curry paste, fish sauce, chopped green beans, kaffir lime leaves, egg, and rice flour until well combined.
  2. Shape the mixture into small patties or cakes, about 2-3 inches in diameter.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium heat. Fry the fish cakes in batches for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden and crispy.
  4. While the fish cakes are frying, prepare the dipping sauce by dissolving the sugar in water and vinegar over low heat. Stir in the fish sauce and chili slices.
  5. Serve the fish cakes with the dipping sauce on the side.

Tod Mun Pla are a delicious and crispy snack that pairs well with a cold drink, and they make a perfect appetizer or party food.

7. Khao Pad: Thai Fried Rice

Khao Pad is a classic Thai fried rice dish thatâ€™s simple to make and bursting with flavor. Made with leftover rice, vegetables, and protein, it’s a great way to use up ingredients while enjoying the flavors of Thai cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup diced chicken, shrimp, or tofu
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and onion and sautÃ© for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken, shrimp, or tofu, and cook until fully cooked through.
  3. Push the ingredients to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the pan. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir everything together. Add soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar, and continue to stir-fry for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Stir in the peas and carrots and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Serve with lime wedges on the side for a fresh burst of flavor.

Khao Pad is a quick and satisfying dish that is perfect for a weeknight dinner or as a side dish to accompany other Thai dishes.
