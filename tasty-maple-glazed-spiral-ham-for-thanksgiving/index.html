Tasty Maple Glazed Spiral Ham for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. One centerpiece dish that never fails to impress is the glazed spiral ham. This recipe for Tasty Maple Glazed Spiral Ham combines the sweetness of maple syrup with warm spices, creating a flavor profile that will have your guests coming back for seconds. With its tender, juicy slices and beautiful presentation, this dish is sure to become a favorite at your Thanksgiving table.

Why Choose Spiral Ham?

Spiral ham is not only aesthetically pleasing, but it also offers convenience. Pre-sliced and easy to serve, it eliminates the hassle of carving. Each slice is infused with flavor, ensuring that every bite is as delicious as the last. Moreover, spiral hams are typically pre-cooked, which means that your primary task is to heat and glaze it, making it a perfect choice for busy holiday cooks.

Ingredients for Maple Glazed Spiral Ham

To create this mouthwatering dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Ham

  • 1 fully cooked spiral ham (about 8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • Freshly cracked black pepper to taste

Optional Garnish

  • Fresh rosemary or thyme sprigs
  • Sliced oranges or apples for a festive touch

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 325°F (165°C). This temperature is ideal for heating the ham while allowing the glaze to caramelize beautifully.

Step 2: Prepare the Ham

Remove the spiral ham from its packaging and place it in a large roasting pan, cut side down. This position helps retain moisture during cooking. If your ham comes with a plastic disk on the bottom, make sure to remove that as well.

Step 3: Make the Maple Glaze

In a medium saucepan, combine the maple syrup, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, cinnamon, cloves, nutmeg, and black pepper. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely and the glaze comes to a gentle simmer. This should take about 5 minutes.

Step 4: Glaze the Ham

Using a basting brush, generously apply the maple glaze over the surface of the ham. Make sure to get the glaze into the spiral cuts for maximum flavor. Reserve some of the glaze for later basting.

Step 5: Bake the Ham

Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil to prevent it from drying out. Place the ham in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 15 minutes per pound. For an 8-pound ham, this will take about 2 hours.

Step 6: Baste for Flavor

Every 30 minutes, remove the foil and baste the ham with the reserved glaze. This step is essential for achieving that glossy, caramelized finish. After about 1 hour of cooking, remove the foil to allow the glaze to develop a rich color.

Step 7: Check for Doneness

To ensure your ham is heated through, use a meat thermometer. The internal temperature should reach 140°F (60°C). If you don’t have a thermometer, check that the juices run clear and the meat is hot throughout.

Step 8: Rest and Serve

Once the ham is done, remove it from the oven, and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more succulent ham.

Step 9: Slice and Enjoy

Slice the ham and arrange it on a platter. Drizzle any remaining glaze over the top, and garnish with fresh herbs and fruit slices for a pop of color. Your Tasty Maple Glazed Spiral Ham is now ready to be served!

Tips for the Perfect Spiral Ham

  1. Choose Quality Ham: Select a high-quality, bone-in spiral ham for the best flavor and texture. Look for options that are labeled “natural” or “hormone-free” for a healthier choice.

  2. Glaze Variations: Feel free to get creative with your glaze! Add orange juice or zest for a citrus twist, or incorporate bourbon for a deeper flavor.

  3. Leftovers: Don’t let any ham go to waste. Use leftovers in sandwiches, omelets, or even casseroles. The sweet and savory flavors will elevate any dish.

  4. Serving Suggestions: Pair your maple glazed ham with classic Thanksgiving sides like roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or cranberry sauce for a well-rounded meal.

  5. Storage: Store any leftover ham in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will last up to 5 days. For longer storage, you can freeze sliced ham for up to 2 months.

Conclusion

Tasty Maple Glazed Spiral Ham is not just a dish; it’s an experience that brings families together during the Thanksgiving season. The sweet, sticky glaze paired with the savory flavors of ham creates a harmonious balance that will delight your taste buds. This recipe is simple enough for even novice cooks, yet impressive enough to wow your holiday guests.

As you gather around the table this Thanksgiving, let the aroma of this delicious ham fill your home and hearts with warmth and joy. Celebrate the season of gratitude with the perfect centerpiece for your feast. Enjoy every bite and the cherished moments spent with loved ones!
