Indulge in Middle Eastern Moghrabieh: A Hearty Couscous Dish


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine or just looking to try something new and exciting, then Moghrabieh is the perfect dish for you. This hearty couscous dish hails from the Levant region and is packed with flavors and textures that will leave you craving for more. Whether you’re a vegetarian or a meat lover, Moghrabieh has something to offer everyone. So, let’s dive right into this delicious recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Moghrabieh (Lebanese couscous)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 2 cups of vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup of cooked chicken or beef, shredded (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they turn golden brown and fragrant.

  2. Add the ground cumin, ground coriander, ground cinnamon, paprika, and turmeric to the pot. Stir well to coat the onions and garlic with the spices. Let them cook for a minute or two to release their flavors.

  3. Add the Moghrabieh to the pot and stir it into the onion and spice mixture. Toast the Moghrabieh for a few minutes until it turns slightly golden brown.

  4. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the Moghrabieh simmer for about 20 minutes or until it becomes tender and absorbs most of the broth.

  5. If you’re adding cooked chicken or beef to your Moghrabieh, now is the time to do so. Simply shred the cooked meat and mix it into the pot along with the drained and rinsed chickpeas. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

  6. Season the dish with salt and pepper according to your taste preferences. Remember to add just a little at a time, tasting as you go, to avoid over-seasoning.

  7. Once everything is well combined and seasoned, cover the pot again and let the Moghrabieh cook for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  8. When the Moghrabieh is ready, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit for a few minutes to slightly cool down. This will allow the dish to settle and the flavors to develop further.

  9. Serve the Moghrabieh hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley. It pairs perfectly with a side of yogurt or a refreshing salad.

Conclusion

Moghrabieh is a dish that truly embodies the rich and diverse flavors of Middle Eastern cuisine. With its hearty couscous, aromatic spices, and the option to add meat or keep it vegetarian, it’s a versatile dish that can be enjoyed by everyone. So, why not indulge in this Middle Eastern delight and experience a taste of the Levant in your own kitchen? Give this recipe a try and prepare to be transported to a world of culinary delight. Enjoy!
