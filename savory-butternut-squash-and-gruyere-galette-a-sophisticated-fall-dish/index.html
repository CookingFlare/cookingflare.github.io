Savory Butternut Squash and Gruyere Galette: A Sophisticated Fall Dish


As the leaves start to turn golden and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to indulge in the rich and comforting flavors of fall. One dish that embodies the essence of the season is the savory butternut squash and Gruyere galette. This sophisticated yet approachable dish combines the sweetness of roasted butternut squash with the nuttiness of Gruyere cheese, all encased in a flaky, buttery crust. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a special meal, this galette is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Additional fresh thyme leaves for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, a few tablespoons at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Roast the Butternut Squash

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the sliced butternut squash on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, then toss to coat.
  3. Roast the butternut squash for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized. Remove from the oven and allow to cool slightly.

Step 3: Assemble the Galette

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a large circle, about 14 inches in diameter. Transfer the dough to a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Leaving a 2-inch border, sprinkle half of the Gruyere cheese over the dough. Arrange the roasted butternut squash slices over the cheese, then sprinkle with the remaining Gruyere and fresh thyme leaves.
  3. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pleating as necessary to create a rustic, free-form shape. Brush the edges of the dough with the beaten egg.
  4. Bake the galette for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Allow the galette to cool for a few minutes before garnishing with additional fresh thyme leaves. Slice and serve warm, either as a main dish or as a delightful accompaniment to a seasonal salad.

Why This Recipe Works

The combination of sweet butternut squash, nutty Gruyere cheese, and aromatic thyme creates a symphony of flavors that perfectly captures the essence of fall. The buttery, flaky crust provides the ideal canvas for these delicious ingredients, making every bite a delight for the taste buds. This recipe is not only a feast for the senses but also a beautiful addition to any fall table, making it a must-try for anyone looking to elevate their seasonal cooking.

Conclusion

The savory butternut squash and Gruyere galette is a sophisticated yet approachable dish that celebrates the flavors of fall in a truly delicious way. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this recipe is a wonderful opportunity to explore the rich and comforting tastes of the season. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the warmth and elegance of fall with this exquisite galette.
