Phyllo-Wrapped Cheese Triangles: An Easy Tiropita Recipe


If you’ve ever fallen in love with traditional Greek cuisine, there’s a good chance you’ve heard of tiropita. This savory pastry packs layers of crispy phyllo dough and creamy cheese filling into bite-sized wonders that are perfect for appetizers, parties, or simply satisfying your snack cravings. While tiropita may seem intimidating to make at first glance due to the delicate phyllo dough, this easy recipe will walk you through each step, ensuring a flawless batch of phyllo-wrapped cheese triangles every time.

The beauty of tiropita lies in its simplicity—a harmonious blend of flavors created using simple ingredients. Whether you’re hosting a Mediterranean-themed dinner or looking to impress with something unique, these cheese triangles will elevate any occasion.

What Makes Tiropita So Incredible?

Tiropita is a timeless dish rooted in Greek culinary traditions. The crispy, golden phyllo pastry provides the perfect vessel for a warm, comforting cheese filling made from a combination of feta, ricotta, and grated hard cheese. This dish is more than just a snack; it’s a taste of history, blending rich textures and flavors passed down through generations.

If you’re unfamiliar with working with phyllo dough, don’t worry. Despite its delicate reputation, phyllo is surprisingly forgiving when you follow the right techniques, and this recipe will guide you to success. With just a few basic ingredients, you’ll be creating layers of flaky perfection that rival your favorite bakery.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s what you’ll need to make these irresistible phyllo-wrapped cheese triangles:

For the Filling:

  • 8 oz feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese or cottage cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan or Kefalotyri cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Pinch of nutmeg

For the Phyllo Pastry:

  • 1 pack of phyllo dough, thawed according to package instructions
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted (or olive oil, if preferred)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, for light brushing (optional)

Kitchen Tools:

  • A pastry brush
  • Mixing bowls
  • Baking sheet lined with parchment paper
  • Clean kitchen towel to cover phyllo sheets and prevent drying

Step-By-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Cheese Filling

Start by preparing the heart of your tiropita—the cheese filling. In a mixing bowl, combine the crumbled feta, ricotta cheese, and grated hard cheese. Stir well to create a smooth and creamy texture. Add the eggs, black pepper, nutmeg, and parsley (if using). Mix again until fully blended. The filling should be thick enough to spoon but still soft and creamy. Set aside.

2. Prepare Your Phyllo Dough

Phyllo dough is notoriously delicate and dries out quickly, so handle it with care. Lay out a clean, dry surface and unroll your phyllo dough. Cover with a damp (not wet) kitchen towel to keep it moist while you work. Phyllo sheets are thin, so don’t be alarmed if they tear occasionally—imperfect triangles are still delicious.

3. Assemble the Cheese Triangles

Now, the fun begins!

  1. Take one sheet of phyllo dough and place it on the work surface. Brush it lightly with melted butter or olive oil to give it moisture and that signature flakiness.
  2. Fold the sheet lengthwise into thirds, creating a long, narrow strip.
  3. Spoon a small amount of the cheese mixture onto one end of the strip, about a tablespoon. Be careful not to overfill.
  4. Fold the strip diagonally over the cheese filling, creating a triangle. Continue folding over and over, maintaining that triangular shape, until you reach the end of the strip.
  5. Seal the edges with a bit of melted butter or a touch of water.
  6. Place the finished triangle on the prepared baking sheet and repeat the process until you’ve used up all the filling.

4. Bake to Golden Perfection

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Once all your triangles are assembled and arranged on the baking sheet, brush the tops with extra melted butter for added richness. Bake for approximately 20–25 minutes, or until the phyllo is crisp and golden brown. Keep an eye on them; you’ll want to avoid burning the delicate pastry edges.

Serving Suggestions

When your phyllo-wrapped cheese triangles are freshly baked, they’re best enjoyed warm. Serve them as:

  • Appetizers: Pair them with olives, hummus, and a fresh green salad for a complete Mediterranean platter.
  • Party Snacks: Display them on a platter for guests to grab and munch—a surefire crowd-pleaser.
  • Brunch Treat: Combine with fresh fruit and coffee for a slightly indulgent morning delight.

While traditional recipes keep it simple, you can elevate your tiropita by adding other flavor-enhancing ingredients to the cheese filling, like chopped spinach, green onions, or sun-dried tomatoes.

Storage and Make-Ahead Tips

One of the great things about phyllo-wrapped cheese triangles is their versatility. If you’re planning ahead, you’re in luck:

To Freeze Unbaked: Arrange assembled triangles in a single layer on a baking sheet and freeze until firm. Once frozen, transfer them to an airtight container or zip-top bag. Bake directly from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time.

To Store Cooked Tiropita: Allow the triangles to cool completely, then store them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to three days. Reheat in a 350°F (175°C) oven for about 10 minutes to restore their crispiness.

Pro Tips for Perfect Phyllo Pastry

  1. Work Quickly but Calmly: Phyllo dough dries out in seconds, so always keep it covered with a damp towel as you work.
  2. Don’t Skimp on Butter: Generously brushing butter (or olive oil) between layers ensures maximum flakiness and flavor.
  3. Customizable Shapes: If triangles feel too complicated, phyllo can also be shaped into mini squares or rolled into spirals.
  4. Room Temperature Ingredients: When preparing the cheese filling, make sure cheeses and eggs are at room temperature to blend more easily.

A Heartwarming Culinary Tradition

Phyllo-wrapped cheese triangles—or tiropita—aren’t just a recipe. They represent a piece of Greek culture, where every bite tells a story of shared meals and cherished moments. Whether you’re crafting them for a special celebration, a casual afternoon nibble, or something in between, these savory pastries bring a taste of authenticity and joy to your table.

Try making a batch today, and watch as they disappear in no time. Perfectly golden, irresistibly flaky, and filled with creamy goodness—they’re guaranteed to become a household favorite.
