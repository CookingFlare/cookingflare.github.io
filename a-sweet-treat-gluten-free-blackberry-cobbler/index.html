A Sweet Treat: Gluten-Free Blackberry Cobbler


Are you looking for a delightful gluten-free dessert that celebrates the flavors of summer? Look no further! This gluten-free blackberry cobbler is the perfect way to enjoy the abundance of juicy blackberries during the warmer months. Whether you follow a gluten-free diet or simply appreciate a delicious homemade dessert, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 5 cups fresh blackberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, gently toss together the blackberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract until the blackberries are evenly coated. Transfer the blackberry mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out into an even layer.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, almond flour, granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold pieces of butter to the flour mixture and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. In a small bowl, combine the milk and vanilla extract. Pour the milk mixture over the flour mixture and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  5. Drop spoonfuls of the topping over the blackberry filling, covering as much of the surface as possible. It’s okay if some of the blackberry filling peeks through.

  6. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the blackberry filling is bubbly.

  7. Remove the cobbler from the oven and allow it to cool for about 15 minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This gluten-free blackberry cobbler is best enjoyed warm, either on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The combination of sweet, juicy blackberries and buttery, golden topping is a match made in dessert heaven. Share it with family and friends at summer gatherings, or savor a cozy evening with a serving of this delightful treat.

Pro Tip: Feel free to experiment with different types of berries or a combination of fruits for the filling. Raspberries, blueberries, or a mix of berries can all be used to create your own unique cobbler variation.

Conclusion

There’s something special about the simplicity of a fruit cobbler, and this gluten-free blackberry cobbler is no exception. With its buttery, tender topping and luscious blackberry filling, it’s a dessert that captures the essence of summer in every bite. Whether you’re accommodating dietary restrictions or simply want to indulge in a delicious homemade treat, this cobbler is a wonderful addition to any dessert repertoire. Try this recipe and savor the taste of summer in every forkful!
