One Pot Beef and Vegetable Stir Fry


If you’re looking for a delicious and convenient meal that’s packed with flavor and nutrition, then look no further than this one pot beef and vegetable stir fry. This recipe is a perfect balance of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, making it a wholesome choice for a family dinner or a quick weeknight meal. The best part? It’s all made in just one pot, minimizing the cleanup and maximizing the flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the thinly sliced flank steak with soy sauce, oyster sauce, and cornstarch. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes while you prepare the vegetables.

  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large pot or wok over medium-high heat. Add the marinated beef slices and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  3. In the same pot, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  4. Add the sliced onion, red bell pepper, and green bell pepper to the pot. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.

  5. Toss in the broccoli florets and snap peas, and continue stir-frying for an additional 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are vibrant and tender-crisp.

  6. Return the cooked beef to the pot and toss everything together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Serve the beef and vegetable stir fry over cooked rice or noodles, and enjoy the delightful flavors and textures of this one pot meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicy kick, add some red pepper flakes or a dash of sriracha sauce to the stir fry.
  • You can customize the vegetable selection based on your preferences or what you have on hand. Sliced carrots, mushrooms, or baby corn are great additions to this versatile dish.
  • To make this recipe gluten-free, use tamari instead of soy sauce and ensure that the oyster sauce is also gluten-free.

Nutritional Benefits

This one pot beef and vegetable stir fry is not only a flavorful and satisfying meal, but it also offers a range of nutritional benefits. The lean flank steak provides high-quality protein, while the colorful array of vegetables contributes essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber. The balanced combination of protein and fiber helps to keep you feeling full and energized.

Conclusion

The one pot beef and vegetable stir fry is a fantastic addition to your recipe repertoire, offering a harmonious blend of savory flavors and vibrant textures. It’s a versatile dish that can be tailored to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs, making it a go-to option for busy weeknights or leisurely family dinners. With minimal cleanup and maximum taste, this one pot meal is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, grab your pot and get ready to stir-fry your way to a delicious and nutritious meal!
