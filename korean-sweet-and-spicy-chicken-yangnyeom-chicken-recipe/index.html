Korean Sweet and Spicy Chicken (Yangnyeom Chicken) Recipe


Known for its irresistible flavor combination of sweet, spicy, and savory, Korean Sweet and Spicy Chicken, or Yangnyeom Chicken, is a dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of food enthusiasts worldwide. This crispy fried chicken coated in a sticky, flavorful sauce is a staple of Korean cuisine, often served as a communal dish at gatherings or as a satisfying indulgence on its own. The recipe may seem complex at first glance, but with the right ingredients, preparation steps, and techniques, you’ll be serving up a homemade version of this street-food favorite in no time.

Ingredients You Will Need

The beauty of Yangnyeom Chicken lies in its combination of crispy fried chicken with a perfectly balanced sauce. Below is a detailed list of the ingredients you’ll need for both the chicken and the sauce.

For the Chicken:

  • 1 pound (500g) chicken drumsticks or wings – Bone-in, skin-on pieces work best for crispy results.
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch – Helps achieve the signature crunch when frying.
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour – Provides structure to the fried coating.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt – To season the chicken before frying.
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper – Enhances the seasoning of the chicken.
  • 1 egg – Acts as a binding agent.
  • Oil for frying – Vegetable, canola, or any neutral oil with a high smoke point.

For the Yangnyeom Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean red chili paste) – The key ingredient for authentic Korean heat.
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup – Adds sweetness and a tangy note to balance the spice.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce – Contributes savory umami to the sauce.
  • 2 tablespoons honey or corn syrup – Creates the sticky and sweet texture.
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar – Adds depth to the sweetness.
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic – A favorite in Korean cooking for its bold flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger – Brings warmth and complexity to the sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar – Balances the flavor with a subtle tang.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil – Offers a nutty, aromatic finish.
  • Sesame seeds and chopped green onions – For garnishing.

Now that we’ve gathered everything, let’s dive into the preparation and cooking process.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Before frying, it’s important to properly prep your chicken. If you’re using drumsticks or wings, give them a quick rinse under cold water, then pat them dry thoroughly using paper towels. Removing excess moisture helps the coating stick better and achieves a crispier texture when frying. Season the chicken with salt and black pepper, ensuring each piece is evenly coated.

Step 2: Create the Coating Mixture

In a large bowl, mix the cornstarch, all-purpose flour, and any additional seasonings (if desired). In a separate bowl, beat the egg. Dip each piece of chicken into the egg, ensuring it’s fully coated, then dredge it in the flour mixture, pressing slightly to help the coating adhere. Repeat for all the chicken pieces and set them aside on a tray or plate.

Step 3: Fry the Chicken

To achieve that signature crunch, double-frying is the technique of choice. Heat enough oil in a deep pan or fryer to fully submerge the chicken pieces, maintaining a temperature of around 340°F (170°C). Carefully lower the chicken into the hot oil in batches, ensuring not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for 8-10 minutes, or until the chicken starts to turn lightly golden. Remove the chicken from the oil and let it rest on a wire rack for 5 minutes.

Once the first fry is complete, increase the oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Fry the chicken a second time for 4-6 minutes, or until the pieces turn a deep golden brown and achieve maximum crispiness. The double frying process not only removes excess moisture but ensures the chicken stays crunchy even when coated in sauce.

Step 4: Prepare the Yangnyeom Sauce

While the chicken is resting, it’s time to make the sauce. In a medium saucepan over low heat, combine gochujang, ketchup, soy sauce, honey (or corn syrup), brown sugar, minced garlic, grated ginger, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Stir the mixture until well combined and cook gently for 3-5 minutes. The sauce should thicken slightly and develop a deep, aromatic flavor.

Taste the sauce and adjust as needed. If you prefer it spicier, add a little more gochujang. For extra sweetness, increase the honey or sugar slightly.

Step 5: Coat the Chicken

When the sauce is ready, transfer the fried chicken into the saucepan, tossing to coat each piece generously. Work quickly while the sauce is still warm to ensure an even coating.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the sauced chicken to a serving plate and sprinkle generously with sesame seeds. For freshness and added flavor, top with chopped green onions. Serve immediately while the chicken is hot, crispy, and coated in its glossy, flavorful sauce.

Pro Tips for Success

  1. Don’t Skip the Double Frying: This technique is a game-changer for crispy chicken. Even if it seems like extra effort, it guarantees the crunch we all crave.
  2. Temperature Control: Use a kitchen thermometer to monitor oil temperature. Maintaining consistent heat is key to evenly cooked, golden-brown chicken.
  3. Use Fresh Garlic and Ginger: While pre-minced options save time, fresh ingredients deliver maximum flavor.
  4. Prep in Advance: You can make the sauce ahead of time and store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week, making weeknight cooking more convenient.
  5. Customizable Heat Levels: Adjust the gochujang quantity to suit your preferred spice level. For a milder sauce, reduce gochujang and add more ketchup or honey.

The Cultural Significance of Yangnyeom Chicken

Yangnyeom Chicken is more than just food—it’s a cultural phenomenon in Korea. Often enjoyed alongside beer (known as “chimaek,” a combination of chicken and maekju, which means beer), it’s a go-to snack for social gatherings, late-night cravings, and celebratory moments. Its popularity has transcended borders, with Korean fried chicken establishments gaining loyal followings around the globe.

What sets Korean fried chicken apart is its unique preparation methods and sauces, such as the Yangnyeom variety. This style of chicken perfectly embodies the harmony of flavors in Korean cuisine, where sweetness, spiciness, and savory notes exist in perfect balance.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you somehow end up with leftovers (a rare occurrence!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. To reheat, avoid the microwave, as it will make the chicken soggy. Instead, place the chicken in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes to regain its crispiness.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned fan of its bold, flavorful dishes, Yangnyeom Chicken is a must-try recipe that’s sure to impress. The combination of crispy, juicy chicken coated in a sticky sauce will leave you and anyone lucky enough to share it utterly satisfied. Best of all, it’s a dish that brings warmth, comfort, and a sense of celebration to any table.

Give this recipe a try, and let it transport you to the lively street markets and vibrant dining scenes of Korea. With every bite, you’ll be reminded of the joy that good food brings. Happy cooking!
