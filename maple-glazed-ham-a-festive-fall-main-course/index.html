Maple Glazed Ham: A Festive Fall Main Course


Fall is the perfect time to gather with loved ones and enjoy a delicious meal together. And what better way to celebrate the season than with a Maple Glazed Ham? This mouthwatering dish is a classic main course that is sure to impress your guests. The combination of savory and sweet flavors will leave everyone craving for more. So, without further ado, let’s dive into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 fully cooked ham (bone-in or boneless)
  • 1 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).

  2. Place the ham in a roasting pan and score the surface in a crisscross pattern. This will allow the glaze to penetrate the meat and add a beautiful texture to the finished dish.

  3. In a small saucepan, combine the maple syrup, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, ground cloves, and ground cinnamon. Stir well to combine.

  4. Place the saucepan over medium heat and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the glaze thickens slightly.

  5. Pour half of the glaze over the scored ham, making sure to coat it evenly. Reserve the remaining glaze for basting later.

  6. Place the ham in the preheated oven and roast for about 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C). Remember to baste the ham with the remaining glaze every 30 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful.

  7. Once the ham is fully cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a tender and juicy ham.

  8. Carve the ham into slices and serve it with your favorite fall side dishes. Whether it’s roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh salad, the Maple Glazed Ham will be the star of the show.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of fall flavor, you can add a tablespoon of maple syrup to your side dishes. It will complement the sweetness of the ham and tie the whole meal together.

  • If you prefer a spicier glaze, you can add a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce to the mixture. This will give your Maple Glazed Ham a little kick that will surely impress your guests.

  • Leftover ham can be used in a variety of delicious recipes. From sandwiches and omelets to soups and stews, the possibilities are endless. Be sure to store the leftover ham in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

  • If you’re hosting a larger gathering, you can double or triple the recipe to accommodate more guests. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly.

In Conclusion

Maple Glazed Ham is a festive fall main course that will make any gathering special. The combination of sweet and savory flavors, along with the tender texture of the ham, is simply irresistible. Whether you’re hosting a holiday dinner or simply want to enjoy a delicious meal with your loved ones, this recipe is a surefire hit. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and get ready to impress your guests with this mouthwatering dish. Happy cooking!
