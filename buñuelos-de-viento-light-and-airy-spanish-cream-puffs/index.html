Buñuelos de Viento: Light and Airy Spanish Cream Puffs


Who can resist the heavenly combination of light and airy cream puffs filled with delectable cream? Buñuelos de Viento, a traditional Spanish dessert, is sure to leave you craving for more. These delicate treats are perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a cozy family gathering or a fancy dinner party. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create these delightful cream puffs that will impress your guests and satisfy your sweet tooth. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the choux pastry:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs

For the cream filling:

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the sugar coating:

  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a medium saucepan, combine water and butter over medium heat. Bring to a boil and stir until the butter has melted completely.

  3. Reduce the heat to low and add the flour all at once. Stir vigorously until the mixture forms a ball and pulls away from the sides of the pan. This process will ensure that the choux pastry is light and airy.

  4. Transfer the dough to a mixing bowl and let it cool slightly for a few minutes.

  5. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. The dough should be smooth and glossy.

  6. Using a piping bag fitted with a round tip, pipe small mounds of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each puff.

  7. Bake the puffs in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown and puffed up. Avoid opening the oven door while they are baking, as this can cause them to deflate.

  8. While the puffs are baking, prepare the cream filling. In a saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until it starts to steam. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, cornstarch, and egg yolks until well combined.

  9. Slowly pour the hot milk into the egg mixture, whisking constantly to prevent curdling. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and comes to a boil.

  10. Remove the cream filling from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Transfer it to a bowl and cover the surface with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming. Let it cool completely in the refrigerator.

  11. Once the cream puffs have cooled, use a sharp knife to make a small slit in the side of each puff. Fill a piping bag fitted with a small tip with the cream filling and pipe it into each puff until they are nicely filled.

  12. Dust the filled puffs with powdered sugar, giving them a beautiful finishing touch.

  13. Serve the Buñuelos de Viento immediately and enjoy their lightness and creamy goodness.

Conclusion

Buñuelos de Viento are a true delight for dessert lovers. Their delicate choux pastry and luscious cream filling make them a perfect treat for any occasion. Whether you’re looking to impress your guests or satisfy your own sweet cravings, these Spanish cream puffs won’t disappoint. So why not give this recipe a try and indulge in the heavenly flavors of Buñuelos de Viento? You won’t be able to resist their airy goodness!
