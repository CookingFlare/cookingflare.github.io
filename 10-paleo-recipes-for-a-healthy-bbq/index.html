10 Paleo Recipes for a Healthy BBQ


Barbecues are a staple of summer dining, with the delicious smell of grilled meats, vegetables, and smoky flavors filling the air. But if you’re following the Paleo diet, which emphasizes whole, nutrient-dense foods, you might find it challenging to balance your cravings for BBQ with your health goals. Fear not! We’ve got a collection of mouthwatering, Paleo-friendly BBQ recipes that will keep you feeling satisfied without straying from your clean-eating lifestyle. From savory meats to vibrant vegetable sides, these dishes prove that Paleo BBQ can be both delicious and healthy.

1. Paleo BBQ Ribs

Nothing says BBQ like a tender rack of ribs. This Paleo BBQ rib recipe uses a homemade spice rub and a natural, refined sugar-free BBQ sauce to create a truly flavorful dish. The slow cooking method allows the ribs to become melt-in-your-mouth tender, and the smokiness from the grill adds the perfect touch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 rack of baby back ribs
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup of Paleo-friendly BBQ sauce (look for one with no added sugar)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-low heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Rub the spice mixture all over the ribs, ensuring an even coating.
  4. Place the ribs on the grill, bone-side down. Cook for 2.5 to 3 hours, turning every 30 minutes.
  5. During the last 15 minutes of cooking, brush the ribs with your Paleo BBQ sauce, allowing the sauce to caramelize slightly.
  6. Remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.

2. Grilled Chicken Skewers with Lemon and Herb Marinade

These juicy grilled chicken skewers are packed with flavor thanks to a zesty lemon and herb marinade. Perfect for any BBQ gathering, they’re easy to make, quick to cook, and incredibly versatile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice and zest, garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  2. Add the chicken cubes to the bowl, making sure they are well coated with the marinade. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers, ensuring an even distribution.
  5. Grill the chicken skewers for 6-8 minutes, turning every 2 minutes until they are golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve with a side of Paleo-friendly dipping sauce or your favorite grilled veggies.

3. Grilled Shrimp and Avocado Salad

If you’re looking for something light and refreshing, this grilled shrimp and avocado salad is a perfect BBQ side dish. The combination of tender shrimp, creamy avocado, and a tangy lime dressing makes this a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss the shrimp with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on each side until they are pink and opaque.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the diced avocados, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and cilantro.
  5. Once the shrimp are cooked, add them to the salad mixture.
  6. Drizzle with lime juice and gently toss to combine.
  7. Serve immediately as a refreshing side or light main dish.

4. Paleo Grilled Vegetables

Grilled vegetables are a must-have side dish at any BBQ, and they’re incredibly easy to make while still being full of flavor. This simple recipe lets the natural sweetness and smokiness of the veggies shine.

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into strips
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss all the vegetables in olive oil, salt, and pepper until well coated.
  3. Place the vegetables on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, until they’re tender and lightly charred.
  4. Remove the vegetables from the grill and garnish with fresh basil or parsley.
  5. Serve as a side dish or as part of a veggie-packed BBQ platter.

5. Paleo Grilled Pork Tenderloin with Apple and Rosemary

Pork tenderloin is lean, flavorful, and perfect for the grill. This recipe combines sweet apples with fragrant rosemary for a delicious, well-balanced dish that’s perfect for your next BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin (about 1 lb)
  • 1 apple, sliced thinly
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub the pork tenderloin with olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the tenderloin on the grill and cook for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally, until the internal temperature reaches 145°F.
  4. During the last 5 minutes of grilling, add the apple slices to the grill and cook until they are soft and caramelized.
  5. Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  6. Slice the pork and serve with the grilled apple slices for a sweet and savory combination.

6. Grilled Portobello Mushrooms with Balsamic Glaze

Portobello mushrooms are a great meat substitute for those following a plant-based Paleo diet. This recipe uses a balsamic glaze to bring out the deep umami flavors of the mushrooms while adding a tangy sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large Portobello mushrooms, stems removed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the mushrooms with olive oil and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme.
  3. Place the mushrooms on the grill, gill side down, and cook for 4-5 minutes per side.
  4. While the mushrooms are grilling, mix the balsamic vinegar in a small bowl. Drizzle the vinegar over the mushrooms during the last minute of cooking.
  5. Serve immediately with additional fresh thyme or a drizzle of extra balsamic glaze.

7. Grilled Salmon with Avocado Salsa

Salmon is an excellent source of healthy fats, and when paired with a fresh, zesty avocado salsa, it becomes a crowd-pleasing BBQ dish. The creamy avocado balances the rich flavor of the salmon for a perfect bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the salmon fillets with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the salmon on the grill, skin side down, and cook for 4-5 minutes per side until the fish is opaque and flakes easily.
  4. While the salmon is grilling, prepare the salsa by mixing the diced avocado, red onion, cilantro, and lime juice in a bowl. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Once the salmon is cooked, remove it from the grill and top each fillet with the avocado salsa before serving.

8. Paleo Grilled Stuffed Peppers

Stuffed peppers are a great way to load up on veggies while keeping things exciting on the grill. These Paleo-friendly stuffed peppers are filled with a savory mixture of ground meat and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté the onions and zucchini for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the ground meat to the skillet and cook until browned. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Stuff the peppers with the meat and veggie mixture, packing tightly.
  6. Place the stuffed peppers on the grill and cook for 15-20 minutes until the peppers are tender and slightly charred.
  7. Serve hot with a drizzle of olive oil or fresh herbs.

9. Paleo Guacamole with Plantain Chips

Guacamole is always a favorite at BBQs, and when paired with crunchy plantain chips, it becomes an irresistible snack. This Paleo-friendly version skips the processed tortilla chips and uses naturally crispy plantains.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • 1 jalapeño, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 plantains, peeled and sliced into chips
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil, melted

Directions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a large bowl, mash the avocados and mix in the onion, lime juice, jalapeño, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss the plantain slices in melted coconut oil and season with salt.
  4. Grill the plantain slices for 3-4 minutes on each side until they are crispy and golden.
  5. Serve the guacamole with the crispy plantain chips for dipping.

10. Paleo Grilled Steak with Chimichurri Sauce

A perfectly cooked steak is a BBQ classic, and when paired with a fresh chimichurri sauce, it takes on a vibrant burst of flavor. This recipe is simple but packs a punch with the tangy, herb-filled chimichurri.

Ingredients:

  • 2 steaks (ribeye or sirloin)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp dried oregano

Directions:

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat.
  2. Season the steaks with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the steaks for 4-5 minutes per side, depending on your preferred level of doneness.
  4. While the steaks are grilling, prepare the chimichurri sauce by mixing the parsley, olive oil, vinegar, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Once the steaks are done, let them rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving with a generous drizzle of chimichurri sauce.

These 10 Paleo BBQ recipes are guaranteed to keep your grill sizzling and your taste buds satisfied. Whether you’re grilling up tender meats, juicy seafood, or vibrant veggies, you can enjoy a healthier BBQ without sacrificing flavor. Happy grilling!
