Taco Pizza: Combining Two Beloved Dishes


If you’re a fan of both tacos and pizza, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to show you how to make a delicious and unique dish that combines the best of both worlds: Taco Pizza. This creative fusion is sure to be a hit at your next gathering or family dinner. So, let’s dive right in and get cooking!

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the taco topping:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup refried beans
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

For the pizza sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and sugar. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  3. Pour the yeast mixture and olive oil into the flour mixture. Mix until the dough comes together and knead it on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  5. While the dough is rising, cook the ground beef in a skillet over medium heat until it’s browned and cooked through. Drain any excess grease, then add the taco seasoning and water. Simmer for about 5 minutes until the mixture thickens.

  6. In a small bowl, mix together the tomato sauce, garlic powder, onion powder, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Set aside.

  7. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions. Roll out each portion into a circle, about 12 inches in diameter.

  8. Place the rolled-out dough onto greased or parchment-lined baking sheets. Spread a thin layer of refried beans onto each pizza crust, leaving a small border around the edges.

  9. Divide the cooked ground beef evenly between the two pizza crusts, spreading it on top of the refried beans.

  10. Drizzle the pizza sauce over the beef, followed by the shredded cheddar cheese, diced tomatoes, diced red onion, and chopped fresh cilantro.

  11. Bake in the preheated oven for about 15-18 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  12. Remove the taco pizzas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. You can garnish with additional cilantro, diced tomatoes, and a dollop of sour cream if desired.

Enjoy this unique and flavorful Taco Pizza that brings together the best elements of tacos and pizza. The crispy crust, savory taco filling, and fresh toppings create a mouthwatering combination that will leave your taste buds begging for more. It’s a recipe that’s sure to impress your family and friends, and it’s perfect for any occasion. So, grab your ingredients and get ready to indulge in this delicious fusion dish!
