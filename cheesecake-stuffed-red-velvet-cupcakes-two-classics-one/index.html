Cheesecake Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcakes: Two Classics, One


Red velvet and cheesecake, two beloved American treats, have finally met and created a remarkable union - the Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcakes. This delightful and decadent dessert provides an unforgettable experience, awash in rich, moist red velvet cake draped in velvety cheesecake filling. What’s not to love?

The History of Red Velvet and Cheesecake

Believed to be a British dessert, red velvet cake gained its popularity in the United States in the 20th century when bakers began experimenting with adding cocoa and vinegar to the batter to give it a distinct taste and a deep red hue. Its original name, “Red Devil’s Food Cake,” comes from the cocoa’s color and intense chocolate flavor, reminiscent of the devil’s food cake, but much tamer and without the high levels of cocoa.

As for cheesecake, its roots can be traced back to ancient Greece. The earliest recorded recipes for cheesecake appeared in both Roman and Greek writings. The Romans perfected the sweet version of cheesecake, and it has been enjoyed by many cultures since. This classic dessert is made up of a soft, creamy filling on a crunchy crust, providing the perfect balance of textures.

The Birth of the Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcakes

Enter the Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcake, the union of two great desserts. This ambitious culinary creation combines the iconic texture of red velvet cake with the smooth creaminess of classic New York-style cheesecake. Every bite features a delightful hidden core of luscious, tangy cheesecake nestled in rich, soft, and velvety red velvet cake.

The Perfect Ingredients for Success

Before we dive into the delectable depths of creating these impressive cupcakes, let’s first gather our essential ingredients. You will need:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • 1 tablespoon white distilled vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup boiling water
  • Red food coloring (optional)
  • 16 oz. cream cheese (room temperature)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (room temperature)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Cupcake liners
  • Piping bag and tip of your choice

The Culinary Dance: Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcakes Recipe

For the Red Velvet Cupcake Batter:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and prepare your cupcake tin with cupcake liners. This recipe will yield 12-15 cupcakes.
  2. In a medium bowl, sift together the flour, cocoa powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the oil, eggs, buttermilk, vinegar, and vanilla extract, and mix until well incorporated.
  4. Gently fold in the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients in three additions, alternating with the boiling water and food coloring (if using) in two additions. Be careful not to overmix the batter; it should be smooth and silky.
  5. Fill each cupcake liner about 2/3rds full with the red velvet batter, and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely.

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  1. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the cheesecake filling. In a large bowl, beat together the cream cheese, sugar, sour cream, butter, eggs, and vanilla extract until completely smooth, scraping down the sides of the bowl as necessary.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the flour and cornstarch. Gradually fold the flour mixture into the cream cheese mixture until well combined; avoid overmixing.

Assembling the Masterpiece

  1. Create the perfect little pocket in your cooled red velvet cupcakes for the cheesecake filling. Taking a paring knife or apple corer, gently remove a small portion from the center of each cupcake, making sure not to puncture the bottom. Make sure to save the removed pieces for later.
  2. Pipe the cheesecake filling into the center of the red velvet cupcake pocket, making sure to use enough to create a generous core without oozing out from the top. You may have some filling left over, which is perfectly okay. It can be frozen for another use.
  3. Once filled, take the reserved red velvet cake scraps and press them gently onto the top of the cheesecake filling, covering the filling completely. This step will not only cover the filling but also create an even surface for topping with frosting or decorating the cupcake.

The Final Touches

Now that your Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcakes are assembled, you have a few options for decorating:

  • Frost with a smooth cream cheese frosting, or simply dust with powdered sugar.
  • Add more visual flair with colorful frosting swirls or a decorative drizzle.
  • Dress it up with ruby red or golden sprinkles, or even tiny chocolate chips

Then, serve and savor! Your Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcakes will create a wholesome sensation that’s perfect for any occasion.

Enjoy the luscious fusion of two timeless classics - the Cheesecake-Stuffed Red Velvet Cupcake. This delightful twist is bound to leave your taste buds singing in sweet satisfaction.
