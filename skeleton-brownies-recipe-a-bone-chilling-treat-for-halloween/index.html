Skeleton Brownies Recipe: A Bone-Chilling Treat for Halloween


Just in time for Halloween, prepare for a spine-chilling and delectable dessert that guarantees jaws to drop in more ways than one. Say hello to the creepy yet delightful Skeleton Brownies! This eerie yet good-looking treat is everything one would expect from a Halloween classic.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup salted butter
  • 6 oz semi-sweet chocolate
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 large eggs (room temperature)
  • 1/4 cup vanilla or pistachio protein powder (optional, for extra flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • Blue candy melts (with gel food coloring, optional)
  • White candy melts (optional)
  • Black sprinkles (optional)

Instructions:

Part 1: Chocolate Brownie Base

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and line a square baking dish with parchment paper.

  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter and chocolate in 30-second intervals, stirring between each. You can also do this on a double boiler, but trust me, the microwave is easier and just as efficient!

  3. Whisk in the sugar, cocoa powder, eggs, protein powder, vanilla extract, baking powder, and salt until well combined.

  4. Pour the batter into your prepared baking dish and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. The edges should be just starting to pull away from the sides of the pan, for that perfect fudgy texture.

  5. Allow your brownies to cool completely in the pan before proceeding to the next step.

Part 2: The Skeleton

  1. Once your brownies have cooled, it’s time to get creative!

  2. Start by melting your blue candy melts according to the package’s instructions. If you want to add a deeper hue, mix in a few drops of gel food coloring and stir until you achieve a corpse-like appearance.

  3. Snip a small corner of a plastic bag, and carefully pipe the blue melted candy to create skeletal finger bones on the brownies. To achieve pointed tips, apply pressure on the bag while piping. Let the candy set to form a firm base layer.

  4. Melt your white candy melts according to the package’s instructions. Snip another small corner from a second plastic bag and pipe the outline of the finger bones and the skeletons hands in the white candy. Allow this layer to set.

  5. Once the white candy base is set, you can add the finishing touches: black sprinkles would make the perfect “skeleto-choclate” bones look more authentic.

And There You Have It!

Your chilling skeleton brownies are ready to serve and cater your Halloween parties. Draped in eerie and cute designs with an appealing twist to the classic brownie, everyone will be begging you for the recipe. So lurk no more, and prepare to indulge in these bone-chilling delicious treats.

Enjoy your Halloween and the making of these Skeleton Brownies! The memories will certainly have you craving these annually, on All Hallows’ Eve. Happy haunting!
