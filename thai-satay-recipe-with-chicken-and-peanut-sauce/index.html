Thai Satay Recipe with Chicken and Peanut Sauce


Are you craving a taste of Thailand? Look no further! In this recipe, we’ll be making a delicious Thai satay with juicy chicken skewers and a creamy peanut sauce. This dish is perfect for a gathering with friends or a cozy dinner at home. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the chicken satay:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

For the peanut sauce:

  • 1 cup unsalted peanuts, roasted
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 small shallot, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili paste (optional)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk

Instructions

  1. Start by marinating the chicken. Cut the chicken breasts into thin, long strips and place them in a bowl. In a separate bowl, mix together the soy sauce, fish sauce, vegetable oil, curry powder, brown sugar, and ground turmeric. Pour the marinade over the chicken and toss until it is well coated. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  2. While the chicken is marinating, let’s prepare the peanut sauce. In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted peanuts, garlic, shallot, soy sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, and chili paste (if using). Blend until you have a smooth paste. If the mixture is too thick, add coconut milk gradually until you reach your desired consistency. Transfer the sauce to a serving bowl and set aside.

  3. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken strips onto the soaked bamboo skewers, making sure to leave some space between each piece. This will allow for even cooking and prevent the chicken from sticking together.

  4. Place the chicken skewers on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are cooked through and slightly charred. Make sure to baste the skewers with any remaining marinade for extra flavor.

  5. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. Serve the chicken satay with the peanut sauce on the side for dipping. You can also garnish with some fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have a grill or grill pan, you can also cook the chicken satay in a preheated oven at 200°C (400°F) for about 15-20 minutes, turning halfway through.
  • Feel free to customize the marinade by adding other spices or herbs like lemongrass or ginger for an extra kick of flavor.
  • If you prefer a milder peanut sauce, you can reduce the amount of chili paste or omit it altogether.
  • This Thai satay recipe also works well with other proteins like beef, pork, or tofu. Just adjust the cooking time accordingly.

Now that you have the recipe for this mouthwatering Thai satay with chicken and peanut sauce, it’s time to gather your ingredients and start cooking. The combination of tender chicken skewers and creamy peanut sauce is sure to transport you to the vibrant streets of Thailand. So, fire up the grill and get ready to indulge in a taste of Southeast Asia. Enjoy!
