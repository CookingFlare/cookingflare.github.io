Satisfying BBQ Recipes for a Memorable Picnic Experience


Summer is the perfect time for picnics, and what better way to enjoy the outdoors than with some delicious BBQ dishes? BBQ is an art and a science that brings people together. I’m excited to share some mouth-watering BBQ recipes that will elevate your picnic experience and delight your taste buds. From spicy to smoky and everything in between, these recipes will hit the spot.

BBQ Chicken Drumsticks

Who doesn’t love crispy chicken drumsticks? Here’s a simple, yet delicious BBQ chicken drumsticks recipe that will tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Papa Spuds BBQ Rub
  • 2 lbs chicken drumsticks
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the BBQ rub with the water until it forms a paste.
  2. Place chicken drumsticks in a large resealable plastic bag and add the rub mixture.
  3. To infuse more flavor, allow the chicken drumsticks to marinate for at least 2 hours in the fridge.
  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  5. Remove drumsticks from the marinade and discard the extra marinade.
  6. Cook the chicken drumsticks on the grill for 20-25 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  7. Let them cool slightly before serving, and enjoy!

Sweet Barbecue Pork Ribs

Pork ribs are another staple of any barbecue picnic. Here’s a recipe for sweet barbecue pork ribs that your guests will love.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs pork spare ribs
  • 1 cup Papa Spuds BBQ Rub
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 cup liquid smoke
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to low heat.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the BBQ rub, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, liquid smoke, and water.
  3. Rub the ribs with the mixture, making sure to cover both sides evenly.
  4. Place your seasoned ribs on the grill and cook for 1 hour at low heat.
  5. After an hour, crank up the heat to medium-high and cook the ribs for an additional 10 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  6. Ensure the ribs reach an internal temperature of 190°F.
  7. Allow the ribs to rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

BBQ Alabama White Sauce

Add a twist to your barbecue experience with this Southern-inspired BBQ Alabama White Sauce. Once you’ve tried it, you’ll understand why Alabamians won’t barbecue without it!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup Buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon NSF Salt
  • 1 tablespoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon paprika

Instructions:

  1. Shake all the ingredients in a mason jar or container until combined.
  2. Store the sauce in an airtight container in the fridge for up to two weeks.
  3. Use the BBQ Alabama White Sauce as a marinade or a dipping sauce for your favorite BBQ dishes.
