Christmas Baking Recipes: 10 Treats to Fill Your Home with Cheer


The holiday season is a wonderful time for gathering with family and friends, exchanging gifts, and, of course, indulging in delicious delights fresh from the oven. Christmas baking is a tradition that brings warmth and sweet smells into the home, filling hearts with holiday cheer. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just learning the ropes, there’s something magical about creating festive treats that everyone can enjoy. From timeless classics to modern twists, these recipes will surely inspire you to get creative in the kitchen and impress your loved ones.

1. Classic Gingerbread Cookies

Gingerbread cookies are a quintessential Christmas treat. Their rich, spiced flavor and delightful shapes make them a favorite among all ages.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Whisk together flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  3. Cream butter and sugar in a separate bowl until light and fluffy. Add molasses and egg; mix well.
  4. Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients into the wet mixture.
  5. Roll out the dough, cut into festive shapes, and bake for 8-10 minutes.
  6. Decorate as desired!

These spiced cookies are perfect for placing on festive platters or even hanging on the Christmas tree.

2. Peppermint Bark

Simple and irresistible, peppermint bark is a quick dessert to whip up and makes an excellent gift.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate, melted
  • 12 ounces white chocolate, melted
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • Crushed candy canes for topping

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Spread the melted dark chocolate on the parchment and allow it to set.
  3. Mix the peppermint extract into the white chocolate and spread over the dark chocolate.
  4. Sprinkle crushed candy canes on top and let it fully cool and harden.
  5. Break into pieces and enjoy!

Make sure to save a few extra pieces for yourself before gifting – you’ll love how festive this sweet snack is.

3. Eggnog Cheesecake Bars

Combine the creamy richness of cheesecake with the flavor of eggnog for a dessert that truly sings “Merry Christmas!”

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup eggnog
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Mix graham cracker crumbs and melted butter. Press into a baking pan to form the crust.
  2. Beat cream cheese and sugar until smooth, then add eggnog, eggs, vanilla extract, and nutmeg.
  3. Pour the filling over the crust and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 30-35 minutes.
  4. Allow the bars to cool before slicing and serving.

The creamy filling combined with the hint of nutmeg will keep guests coming back for more.

4. Cranberry Orange Bread

Moist, flavorful, and bursting with tart cranberries and zesty orange, this bread is perfect for Christmas breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup orange juice
  • Zest of one orange
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Combine dry ingredients in one bowl and wet ingredients in another.
  2. Slowly blend the wet ingredients into the dry, folding in the cranberries.
  3. Pour the batter into a greased loaf pan.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 50-60 minutes.

The citrusy aroma of this loaf will brighten any winter morning.

5. Snowball Cookies

These buttery cookies, dusted with powdered sugar, are as magical as fresh-fallen snow.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup finely chopped nuts (pecans or walnuts recommended)

Instructions:

  1. Cream butter and powdered sugar, then stir in vanilla extract.
  2. Gradually mix in flour and nuts until a dough forms.
  3. Shape dough into small balls and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-12 minutes.
  4. Roll warm cookies in powdered sugar.

Perfectly crumbly and sweet, these cookies practically melt in your mouth.

6. Chocolate Yule Log

This French classic transforms dessert into a holiday centerpiece.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Filling and Frosting:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Chocolate buttercream frosting

Instructions:

  1. Whisk eggs and sugar until pale and thick. Fold in cocoa powder, flour, and vanilla extract.
  2. Bake in a jelly roll pan at 375°F (190°C) for 10 minutes.
  3. Once cool, spread whipped cream on the cake and roll tightly.
  4. Frost with chocolate buttercream and decorate with powdered sugar or fresh berries.

Your guests will marvel at the elegant presentation of this decadent dessert.

7. Spiced Apple Doughnuts

Pair these flavorful doughnuts with a steaming cup of cider or coffee for a cozy holiday treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups applesauce
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 1/4 cups sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder

Instructions:

  1. Mix applesauce, eggs, butter, and sugar.
  2. Gradually add dry ingredients until a smooth batter forms.
  3. Pour into donut molds and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 12-15 minutes.

Cinnamon sugar coating optional—but highly recommended!

8. Thumbprint Cookies with Jam

These delicate cookies filled with a dollop of fruity jam are as delightful to make as they are to eat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • Your choice of jam (raspberry, apricot, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Cream butter and sugar, and add egg yolks and vanilla extract.
  2. Fold in flour to form a soft dough.
  3. Shape dough into balls, then press your thumb into the center of each.
  4. Fill the indentation with jam and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10-12 minutes.

Their vibrant colors make these cookies a festive addition to any holiday spread.

9. Stained Glass Window Shortbread

Combine buttery shortbread with the beauty of candy “stained glass” for a treat that’s almost too pretty to eat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • Hard candies, crushed

Instructions:

  1. Cream butter and sugar, then mix in flour to form dough.
  2. Cut shapes from the dough and remove the centers to fill with crushed candy.
  3. Bake at 325°F (163°C) for 12-15 minutes.

Display these cookies in a festive jar or serve alongside a warm beverage.

10. Holiday Walnut Fudge

Rich, creamy, and deeply indulgent, there’s no better way to end your holiday feast.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 14-ounce can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts

Instructions:

  1. Melt chocolate chips and condensed milk together in a saucepan.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla and walnuts.
  3. Pour the mixture into a prepared pan, cool, and cut into squares.

You’ll love how quickly this fudge comes together—and disappears!

These 10 Christmas baking recipes are sure to become cherished traditions in your home. Whether you’re baking for a party, gifting, or simply keeping the family’s holiday spirit alive, these festive ideas will sweeten your season with joy. Grab your apron, turn on your favorite Christmas playlist, and bake your way to holiday magic!
