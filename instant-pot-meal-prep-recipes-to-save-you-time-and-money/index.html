Instant Pot Meal Prep: Recipes to Save You Time and Money


In today’s fast-paced world, meal prep has become a game-changer for people looking to save time, reduce stress, and eat healthier. One of the best tools in any kitchen to streamline meal prep is the Instant Pot. This multifunctional appliance does more than just pressure cook; it can sauté, slow cook, steam, and even make yogurt. With the Instant Pot, you can cut down your cooking time significantly while making delicious, nutritious meals that will last the whole week.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of Instant Pot meal prep recipes that not only save you time but also help you save money. These recipes are perfect for busy professionals, parents, or anyone looking to simplify their weekly meals. Let’s dive in!

Why Instant Pot Meal Prep?

Before jumping into the recipes, let’s talk about why meal prepping with an Instant Pot is such a fantastic idea.

1. Time-Saving

The Instant Pot can cut down cooking times dramatically. Traditional slow-cooking meals that might take hours can now be made in a fraction of the time. Whether you’re cooking grains, meats, or stews, the Instant Pot offers quick, efficient cooking with minimal effort.

2. Easy to Use

Unlike traditional stovetops or ovens, the Instant Pot does most of the work for you. With just a few ingredients and the touch of a button, you can prepare complex meals that would normally require hours of attention.

3. One-Pot Cooking

Meal prep can be complicated, especially when you’re juggling multiple dishes. The Instant Pot allows you to cook your entire meal in one pot, reducing the time spent cleaning multiple pans and utensils.

4. Batch Cooking for the Week

Meal prep is all about preparing meals in advance so you have them ready to go during the week. The Instant Pot is perfect for cooking large batches of food that can be portioned out into individual servings for the week. From soups and stews to grains and proteins, this kitchen gadget can handle it all.

5. Healthier Meals

Using an Instant Pot allows you to control the ingredients and portion sizes, leading to healthier meal choices. You can easily incorporate vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains into your meals, making it simple to stick to a nutritious diet.

Instant Pot Meal Prep Recipes

Now, let’s dive into some delicious and easy Instant Pot meal prep recipes that will save you time and money while keeping your meals flavorful and nutritious.

1. Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry

This chicken and vegetable stir-fry recipe is a great way to prepare a balanced meal with lean protein and plenty of vegetables. It’s perfect for lunch or dinner and can be stored in the fridge for a few days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté function and heat olive oil. Add the chicken pieces, season with salt and pepper, and cook for 4-5 minutes until browned.
  2. Add the garlic and onion, sautéing for another 2 minutes.
  3. Add the bell peppers, broccoli, and snap peas, stirring to combine.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and sesame oil.
  5. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, stirring to coat.
  6. Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 3 minutes.
  7. Once the cooking time is up, quickly release the pressure and remove the lid.
  8. Serve over brown rice or quinoa for a complete meal.

This recipe yields multiple servings, making it perfect for meal prep. The chicken and veggies can be stored in individual containers for the week.

2. Beef and Sweet Potato Chili

Chili is a fantastic meal for meal prep because it tastes even better the next day! This beef and sweet potato chili is hearty, flavorful, and packed with protein and fiber, making it a great choice for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (or turkey for a leaner option)
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups beef or vegetable broth

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode and brown the ground beef for 5-7 minutes, breaking it up as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add the onions, bell pepper, and garlic to the Instant Pot, sautéing for 3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the sweet potatoes, beans, tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, pepper, and broth. Stir everything together.
  4. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 15 minutes.
  5. After the cooking time is complete, allow for a natural release of pressure for about 10 minutes, then release the remaining pressure.
  6. Stir the chili and check the seasoning. Adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  7. Divide the chili into individual containers, and it’s ready to eat throughout the week.

This chili is perfect for meal prep, and it freezes well, so you can make a double batch and save some for later.

3. Lentil Soup with Spinach and Carrots

This lentil soup is full of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. It’s a comforting, nutritious option for meal prep, and the Instant Pot does all the heavy lifting, making it a quick and easy meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups spinach, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode and heat the olive oil. Add the onions, carrots, and garlic, sautéing for 4 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the cumin, turmeric, coriander, salt, and pepper, stirring to combine.
  3. Add the lentils, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth, and stir everything together.
  4. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 15 minutes.
  5. Once the cooking time is complete, allow for a natural release of pressure.
  6. Open the lid, stir in the chopped spinach, and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  7. Portion the soup into containers for meal prep.

This lentil soup is an excellent plant-based meal that can be enjoyed throughout the week and even frozen for future use.

4. Quinoa and Chickpea Salad

For a lighter, refreshing option, try this quinoa and chickpea salad. It’s full of plant-based protein, fiber, and healthy fats, making it a perfect lunch for meal prep.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water and add it to the Instant Pot with 1 1/2 cups water.
  2. Close the lid, set the valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 1 minute.
  3. Once the cooking time is up, allow for a natural release of pressure for about 10 minutes, then release the remaining pressure.
  4. Fluff the quinoa with a fork and let it cool slightly.
  5. In a large bowl, combine the quinoa, chickpeas, cucumber, bell pepper, and red onion.
  6. Drizzle with olive oil, lemon juice, and season with oregano, salt, and pepper.
  7. Toss everything together and divide it into containers for meal prep.

This quinoa and chickpea salad is a refreshing, light option that holds up well in the fridge for a few days, making it perfect for meal prep.

Tips for Successful Instant Pot Meal Prep

  • Plan Ahead: Choose a few recipes that can be easily doubled to make extra servings for the week. This will save you time on busy days.
  • Use Containers: Invest in good-quality meal prep containers that are microwave and freezer safe. Glass containers are ideal because they don’t absorb odors like plastic.
  • Batch Cook: Cook large quantities of grains (quinoa, rice, or oats) or beans in the Instant Pot, and store them in the fridge to be used throughout the week.
  • Keep It Simple: You don’t need elaborate meals for meal prep. Simple recipes with fresh ingredients are often the best.

Conclusion

The Instant Pot is a meal prep game-changer that can make your life easier and more efficient. With just a little bit of planning, you can create a variety of delicious, nutritious meals that will keep you fueled throughout the week. From chicken stir-fries to comforting soups and fresh salads, the Instant Pot can help you save time, money, and stress in the kitchen. Start meal prepping today, and enjoy delicious homemade meals all week long!
