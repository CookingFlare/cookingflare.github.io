Seafood Cioppino Recipe: A San Francisco-Style Fish Stew


Are you a seafood lover? Do you enjoy hearty, flavorful stews? If so, then you’re in for a treat! Today, I’ll be sharing with you an authentic recipe for Seafood Cioppino, a San Francisco-style fish stew that will transport your taste buds to the shores of the Bay Area. This mouthwatering dish is a perfect combination of fresh seafood, aromatic herbs, and rich tomato broth. So, let’s dive right in and get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 fennel bulb, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup seafood stock
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 pound firm white fish fillets, cut into chunks
  • 1 pound clams, scrubbed
  • 1 pound mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and diced bell peppers. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the thinly sliced fennel bulb to the pot and continue cooking for another 3-4 minutes until it begins to soften.

  3. Stir in the dried oregano, dried thyme, red pepper flakes, and bay leaf. Allow the herbs and spices to release their flavors for about a minute.

  4. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, seafood stock, and dry white wine. Give everything a good stir and bring the mixture to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Once the broth has simmered and developed a rich flavor, it’s time to add the seafood. Gently place the white fish chunks, clams, mussels, and shrimp into the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover the pot and let the seafood cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the clams and mussels have opened and the shrimp are pink and opaque.

  6. Remove the pot from the heat and discard any clams or mussels that did not open during cooking. Give the stew a gentle stir to combine all the ingredients.

  7. Ladle the Seafood Cioppino into bowls, making sure to include a generous amount of the flavorful broth, fish, and shellfish in each serving. Sprinkle with fresh parsley for a pop of color and added freshness.

  8. Serve the Seafood Cioppino with crusty bread or garlic toast to soak up the delicious broth. Enjoy this San Francisco-inspired fish stew with your loved ones and savor the taste of the ocean!

Final Thoughts

Seafood Cioppino is a true culinary delight that brings the flavors of the sea right to your table. This San Francisco-style fish stew is perfect for a cozy dinner with family and friends, or even for impressing guests at a dinner party. The combination of fresh seafood, aromatic herbs, and rich tomato broth creates a symphony of flavors that will leave you craving for more.

Don’t be afraid to experiment with different types of seafood in your Cioppino. Feel free to add scallops, squid, or any other seafood that tickles your taste buds. The beauty of this recipe lies in its versatility, allowing you to customize it to your liking.

So, what are you waiting for? Head to your nearest fish market, gather the freshest ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to San Francisco with this delicious Seafood Cioppino recipe. Your taste buds will thank you!
