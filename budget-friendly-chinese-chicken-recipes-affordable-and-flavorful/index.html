Budget-Friendly Chinese Chicken Recipes: Affordable and Flavorful


Chinese cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and affordability. When it comes to cooking Chinese dishes, chicken is a staple protein that can be prepared in a multitude of ways to suit any taste or budget. In this post, we’ll explore some budget-friendly Chinese chicken recipes that are not only delicious but also easy on the wallet.

Stir-Fried Chicken with Ginger and Garlic

This classic Chinese stir-fry is a staple for a reason. It’s quick, easy, and packed with flavor. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 inches fresh ginger, peeled and grated
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, broccoli, etc.)

Heat the oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Add the ginger, garlic, soy sauce, and oyster sauce (if using) and stir-fry for another minute. Add the mixed vegetables and cook until they’re tender-crisp. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve over rice or noodles.

Chicken and Mushroom Wonton Soup

This comforting soup is a staple of Chinese cuisine and can be made with minimal ingredients. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake), sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 package wonton wrappers (about 20-24 wrappers)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Scallions, chopped (optional)

Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Add the mushrooms and cook until they’re tender. In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, soy sauce, and cornstarch. Add the mixture to the pot and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes. Meanwhile, cook the wonton wrappers according to the package instructions. Add the cooked wontons to the pot and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes. Season with sesame oil, salt, and pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped scallions, if desired.

Spicy Chicken and Vegetable Lo Mein

This spicy lo mein is a staple of Chinese-American cuisine and can be made with minimal ingredients. To make this dish, you’ll need:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (bean sprouts, cabbage, carrots, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 package lo mein noodles
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Scallions, chopped (optional)

Heat the oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5 minutes. Add the mixed vegetables and cook until they’re tender-crisp. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and hoisin sauce. Add the mixture to the wok or skillet and stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes. Add the cooked lo mein noodles and stir-fry until they’re well coated with the sauce. Season with sesame oil, salt, and pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped scallions, if desired.

Conclusion

Chinese cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and affordability. With these budget-friendly Chinese chicken recipes, you can enjoy delicious and flavorful meals without breaking the bank. From classic stir-fries to comforting soups and spicy noodles, there’s something for everyone in this collection of recipes. So go ahead, get cooking, and experience the flavors of China without the hefty price tag!
