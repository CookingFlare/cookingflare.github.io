Easy Fermentation Basics: Dip Your Toes Into the Pickling Pool


Fermentation, a process that involves the conversion of sugars into acids or alcohol, is a key part of many traditional culinary techniques. From sourdough bread to cheese, sauerkraut, and pickles – fermentation adds unique flavors and creates new textures. If you’ve always wanted to try your hand at fermentation, now is the perfect time! Let’s dive into the basics, so you can confidently start your fermentation journey.

Understanding the process

Fermentation begins with the transformation of sugars by microorganisms into desired end-products, such as lactic acid or alcohol. These organisms – yeasts, bacteria, and molds – thrive in oxygen-deprived environments, which can be achieved through anaerobic preservation techniques such as brining, canning, or vacuum sealing.

For pickling, lactic acid bacteria (LABs) are our primary culprits. When submerged in a brine solution (a saltwater bath), these bacteria convert sugars into lactic acid. This process retains the texture and crunch of the vegetable while imparting a tangy, zesty flavor.

Tools and equipment

To embark on your pickling journey, you’ll need some basic tools and equipment:

  1. Non-reactive containers: Avoid using metal containers, as they can react with acidic solutions over time. Instead, opt for glass jars, crocks, or food-grade plastic containers.
  2. Sterilizing equipment: Before beginning fermentation, you must sterilize your equipment to prevent any unwanted bacteria or yeasts from contaminating your pickles.
  3. Airlocks: These aerators provide a bubble for gases to escape while preventing the introduction of outside contaminants.
  4. Weights: Pressing vegetables down into the brine ensures even pickling and encourages the desired Lacto-fermentation.
  5. Thermometers: Monitoring temperature is crucial for successful fermentation, as each organism has its optimal range.

Pickling process step-by-step

  1. Selection of vegetables: Fresh, firm vegetables are best for pickling, as they retain their texture and crunch. Popular pickling vegetables include cucumbers, carrots, cauliflower, and beets.
  2. Sterilization: Thoroughly clean and sterilize your containers, weights, and airlocks to prevent contamination. You can achieve this by washing items in hot, soapy water and then boiling them for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Brining: Prepare a saturated brine solution using non-iodized salt and water. Typically, 5-10% salt by volume is used, although certain vegetables may require adjustments to flavor balance.
  4. Fermentation: Add vegetables to the brine solution, ensuring they are fully submerged. Attach weights and airlocks to containers, and let the magic happen! You’ll want to maintain a stable temperature of 70-75°F (21-24°C) for optimal LAB activity. The fermentation process can take anywhere from 1 day to a few weeks, depending on your desired level of tanginess.
  5. Storage: Once pickling is complete, your ferments can be stored for extended periods using airtight methods such as canning, refrigeration, or vacuum sealing.

Diversify your pickle palate

The pickling possibilities are endless! Why not try some unconventional pickling combinations? Consider:

  1. Fermented citrus, like grapefruit or oranges, for a refreshing change of pace.
  2. Experiment with different types of salt, such as kosher or flaky, to modify the brine strength.
  3. Spice up your pickles with unique combinations of herbs and spices, like dill and cumin or fennel and coriander.
  4. Try pickling fruits like berries or apples for an extraordinary dessert treat!

However, remember that the process of fermentation is sensitive and should follow good hygiene practices to avoid unwanted contaminants. Start small and gradually allow the process to evolve to your preference. As you familiarize yourself with the basics, you can expand your fermentation horizons and create unique, flavorful pickles perfect for any occasion. So, dip your toes into the pickling pool and enjoy the rewarding outcomes of your fermentation journey!
