Thai Drunken Noodles: A Fiery and Flavorful Dish


If you’re a fan of bold and spicy flavors, then Thai Drunken Noodles should definitely be on your culinary radar. This fiery and flavorful dish is a staple in Thai cuisine, known for its unique combination of heat, sweetness, and tang. In this recipe, we’ll explore the secrets behind creating an authentic and mouthwatering plate of Thai Drunken Noodles.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces of wide rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 Thai chili pepper, sliced (adjust according to your spice tolerance)
  • 1/2 cup of sliced chicken, beef, or shrimp (or a combination)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, and broccoli)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Begin by soaking the rice noodles in warm water for about 15 minutes or until they become soft and pliable. Drain and set aside.

  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced Thai chili pepper, and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Add the sliced chicken, beef, or shrimp to the wok and stir-fry until cooked through. If using a combination of meats, ensure each is cooked separately before combining them in the wok.

  4. Push the cooked meat to one side of the wok, creating space for the beaten egg. Pour the egg into the empty space and scramble it until fully cooked.

  5. Add the mixed vegetables to the wok and stir-fry for a few minutes until they are slightly tender but still crisp.

  6. Now it’s time to add the star of the show – the rice noodles! Toss them into the wok, ensuring they are well-coated with the flavors from the other ingredients.

  7. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Pour this sauce over the noodles and stir-fry everything together until the noodles are evenly coated and heated through.

  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you prefer a spicier dish, feel free to add more sliced Thai chili pepper at this stage.

  9. Finally, garnish the Thai Drunken Noodles with fresh basil leaves for an extra burst of flavor and aroma.

Serving Suggestions

Thai Drunken Noodles can be enjoyed as a standalone dish or paired with a side of steamed jasmine rice. To add some additional freshness, serve it with a side of cucumber slices or a Thai-inspired salad. This dish also goes well with a squeeze of lime juice for an added tang.

In Conclusion

Thai Drunken Noodles are a delightful blend of fiery spices, umami-rich sauces, and fresh ingredients. This dish is not only bursting with flavor but also offers a satisfying balance of textures. With this recipe, you can bring the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine right into your own kitchen. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Thailand. Enjoy!
