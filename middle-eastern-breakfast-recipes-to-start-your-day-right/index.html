Middle Eastern Breakfast Recipes to Start Your Day Right


The Middle East is a region known for its rich, diverse, and flavorful culinary traditions. From the spices and fresh herbs to the variety of grains, legumes, and vegetables, Middle Eastern cuisine offers an endless range of dishes that are not only delicious but also nourishing. If you’re looking for a way to start your day right, Middle Eastern breakfast recipes offer a combination of satisfying, nutrient-packed meals that can fuel you for the day ahead.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of Middle Eastern breakfast dishes, each with its unique blend of ingredients, textures, and flavors. Whether you’re craving something light and fresh or hearty and filling, these recipes will introduce you to the vibrant tastes of the Middle East, making your morning meal a delightful experience.

1. Shakshuka: A Traditional Tomato and Egg Dish

Shakshuka is a quintessential Middle Eastern breakfast that has gained popularity worldwide. This dish features poached eggs simmered in a flavorful tomato sauce made with onions, garlic, peppers, and spices like cumin, paprika, and coriander. It’s a simple yet deeply satisfying breakfast that pairs perfectly with crusty bread for scooping up the sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 large eggs
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Crusty bread (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large, deep skillet or cast-iron pan over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, cumin, paprika, coriander, salt, and pepper. Simmer the mixture for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Create four small wells in the tomato sauce, and gently crack an egg into each well.
  5. Cover the pan and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the eggs reach your desired level of doneness (runny yolks are traditional).
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley and serve with crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

2. Fatteh: A Layered Delight of Bread, Yogurt, and Chickpeas

Fatteh is a popular breakfast in many Middle Eastern countries, often enjoyed in Lebanon, Syria, and Jordan. The dish is made by layering torn pieces of pita bread with a savory yogurt sauce, warm chickpeas, and crispy fried nuts. It’s a filling and comforting dish that’s perfect for a leisurely breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pieces of pita bread
  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts or slivered almonds, toasted
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Tear the pita bread into pieces and toast or bake them until crispy and golden.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chickpeas and sauté for 5 minutes. Season with cumin, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, tahini, garlic, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Mix well until smooth.
  4. To assemble the fatteh, layer the toasted pita pieces at the bottom of a serving dish. Spoon the warm chickpeas over the bread, followed by the yogurt sauce.
  5. Garnish with toasted pine nuts or slivered almonds and fresh parsley.
  6. Serve with lemon wedges for a burst of fresh citrus.

3. Manakish: Middle Eastern Flatbread with Za’atar and Cheese

Manakish is a popular street food in Lebanon and Syria, often eaten for breakfast. This flatbread is topped with a variety of toppings, but the most traditional is za’atar – a Middle Eastern spice blend made from thyme, sesame seeds, sumac, and salt. For a heartier version, cheese is often added to the topping.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (for topping)
  • 2 tablespoons za’atar
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese or Akkawi cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Add the olive oil and warm water and knead until a smooth dough forms. Let the dough rise for 1-1.5 hours or until doubled in size.
  2. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up.
  3. Once the dough has risen, divide it into 4 equal portions. Roll each portion into a round flatbread shape, about 8 inches in diameter.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil and za’atar. Brush the mixture evenly over each flatbread.
  5. Optionally, sprinkle with crumbled feta or Akkawi cheese.
  6. Bake the manakish for 7-10 minutes, or until golden and crispy.
  7. Serve warm, straight out of the oven.

4. Labneh with Olive Oil and Herbs

Labneh is a strained yogurt cheese that is a staple in many Middle Eastern households. It’s thick, creamy, and tangy, and it pairs wonderfully with olive oil, herbs, and a side of fresh vegetables. This dish is not only incredibly easy to make, but it’s also a nutritious and refreshing option to start your day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon dried mint (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cucumbers, tomatoes, and olives (for serving)
  • Pita bread (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. If you don’t have store-bought labneh, strain the Greek yogurt through a cheesecloth or fine sieve for several hours until it reaches a thick consistency.
  2. Spread the labneh onto a serving plate and drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Sprinkle with dried mint and a pinch of salt.
  4. Serve with fresh cucumbers, tomatoes, and olives, along with warm pita bread for dipping.

5. Hummus with Fresh Vegetables

While hummus is typically a dip served at lunch or dinner, it can also be a wonderful and satisfying breakfast option. Made from chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, hummus is high in protein and fiber, making it a great way to start your day. Pair it with fresh vegetables like cucumber, radish, and carrots for a balanced breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh vegetables, sliced (cucumber, radishes, carrots)

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy. If needed, add a little water to achieve the desired consistency.
  2. Transfer the hummus to a serving dish and drizzle with extra olive oil.
  3. Serve with sliced vegetables for dipping.

6. Arabic Coffee and Dates

In many Middle Eastern cultures, no breakfast is complete without a cup of strong Arabic coffee paired with dates. Arabic coffee is often spiced with cardamom and served in small cups. The rich and aromatic flavor of the coffee, combined with the natural sweetness of dates, provides a simple yet satisfying way to start the day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon Arabic coffee (or ground coffee)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 cup water
  • Dates (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. In a small pot, combine the coffee and cardamom with the water.
  2. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Pour the coffee into small cups and serve with dates on the side.

7. Falafel with Hummus

Falafel is another iconic Middle Eastern dish that’s perfect for breakfast. These crispy, deep-fried chickpea patties are packed with flavor and typically served with pita bread, tahini, and hummus. While falafel is often eaten for lunch or dinner, it also makes for a hearty and filling breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup flour (or chickpea flour)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is well combined but still slightly chunky.
  2. Add the flour and pulse again until the mixture holds together. If it’s too sticky, add more flour.
  3. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. Heat the oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serve with hummus, pita bread, and fresh vegetables.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern breakfasts are a delightful combination of savory, hearty, and refreshing dishes that are perfect for starting your day with flavor and energy. Whether you enjoy the rich spices of shakshuka, the creamy texture of labneh, or the crispy crunch of falafel, there’s something for every taste. Incorporating these Middle Eastern breakfast recipes into your morning routine will not only provide you with a satisfying meal but also introduce you to the diverse and exciting flavors of this culinary tradition. Try them out, and enjoy a breakfast that’s both delicious and nourishing!
