The Best Thai Soups: Recipes for Every Season


Thai cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and the perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy. Among the many culinary delights that Thailand has to offer, soups hold a special place. They are not only comforting but also versatile, making them suitable for any season. In this blog post, we will explore some of the best Thai soup recipes that you can enjoy year-round, each tailored to the unique characteristics of the seasons.

Spring: Tom Yum Goong

Ingredients:

  • 500g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 5-6 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3-4 slices galangal (or ginger if unavailable)
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Optional: Thai chili paste (Nam Prik Pao)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a boil.
  2. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Let it simmer for about 5 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  3. Add the mushrooms and shrimp to the pot. Cook until the shrimp turns pink and the mushrooms are tender.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce, lime juice, and bird’s eye chilies. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. For an extra kick, add a spoonful of Thai chili paste.

Why It’s Perfect for Spring:

Tom Yum Goong is a refreshing soup that embodies the essence of spring with its bright flavors and aromatic herbs. The lightness of the shrimp and the zing of lime make it an ideal dish as the weather begins to warm up.

Summer: Tom Kha Gai

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast, sliced thinly
  • 4 cups coconut milk
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into 2-inch pieces and smashed
  • 5-6 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 3-4 slices galangal
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2-3 Thai bird’s eye chilies, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro and sliced red chili for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine coconut milk and chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  2. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Let it simmer for about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken and mushrooms, cooking until the chicken is fully cooked.
  4. Stir in the fish sauce, lime juice, and bird’s eye chilies. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and sliced red chili.

Why It’s Perfect for Summer:

Tom Kha Gai is a creamy and aromatic soup that provides a cooling effect during the hot summer months. The coconut milk adds richness, while the lime and herbs keep it light and refreshing.

Fall: Pumpkin Curry Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium pumpkin, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Optional: lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the red curry paste and cook for another minute.
  3. Stir in the pumpkin cubes and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the pumpkin is tender.
  4. Blend the soup until smooth, then return to the pot. Stir in the coconut milk, fish sauce, and brown sugar. Heat through.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and lime wedges.

Why It’s Perfect for Fall:

This pumpkin curry soup is a warm and comforting dish that captures the essence of fall. The sweetness of the pumpkin combined with the spiciness of the curry paste creates a delightful balance that is perfect for cooler weather.
