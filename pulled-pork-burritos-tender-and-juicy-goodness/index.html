Pulled Pork Burritos: Tender and Juicy Goodness


If you’re craving a meal that’s bursting with flavor and texture, look no further than pulled pork burritos. This dish combines the succulent tenderness of slow-cooked pork with fresh, vibrant ingredients, all wrapped up in a warm tortilla. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or just want to treat yourself to something delicious, these burritos are sure to impress. In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making pulled pork burritos, from the perfect pork preparation to assembling your masterpiece. Let’s dive in!

The Heart of the Burrito: Perfect Pulled Pork

Ingredients

To start, let’s focus on the star of the show: the pulled pork. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 pounds of pork shoulder (also known as pork butt)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup barbecue sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

  1. Trim the Fat: Start by trimming excess fat from the pork shoulder. While some fat is necessary for flavor, too much can make the final dish greasy.

  2. Season the Pork: In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, chili powder, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Rub this spice mixture all over the pork, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes, or ideally, refrigerate it overnight for deeper flavor.

Step 2: Slow Cooking

  1. Sear the Pork (Optional): For an extra layer of flavor, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the pork on all sides until browned (about 2-3 minutes per side). This step is optional but recommended.

  2. Transfer to Slow Cooker: Place the seasoned pork in a slow cooker. Pour the chicken broth, apple cider vinegar, and Worcestershire sauce over the top. If you love barbecue sauce, feel free to add it now.

  3. Cook Low and Slow: Cover and cook on low for 8-10 hours, or until the pork is fork-tender and easily pulls apart.

Step 3: Pulling the Pork

Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and place it on a cutting board. Let it rest for a few minutes before shredding it with two forks. Discard any large pieces of fat. If desired, stir the shredded pork back into the juices left in the slow cooker for added moisture and flavor.

Building Your Burrito: Essential Ingredients

Now that you have your mouthwatering pulled pork ready, it’s time to assemble your burrito. Here’s what you’ll need:

Burrito Essentials

  • Large flour tortillas (10-12 inches)
  • 1 cup cooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1 cup canned black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • 1 cup salsa or pico de gallo
  • 1 avocado, sliced or guacamole
  • 1 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Assembly Instructions

Step 1: Warm the Tortillas

To make your burritos easier to roll, warm the flour tortillas. You can do this in a microwave for 10-15 seconds or on a skillet over medium heat for about 30 seconds per side.

Step 2: Layering the Ingredients

  1. Start with Rice: Place a generous scoop of cooked rice in the center of the tortilla.

  2. Add Pulled Pork: Top the rice with a hearty portion of pulled pork, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Add Beans and Corn: Sprinkle black beans and corn over the pork for added texture and nutrition.

  4. Cheese and Salsa: Add shredded cheese and a spoonful of salsa or pico de gallo for fresh, zesty flavor.

  5. Avocado and Sour Cream: For creaminess, add slices of avocado or a dollop of guacamole and top it off with sour cream or Greek yogurt.

  6. Fresh Herbs: If you like, sprinkle some fresh cilantro for a burst of herbal freshness.

Step 3: Wrapping the Burrito

To wrap your burrito, fold in the sides of the tortilla and then roll it up from the bottom, tucking in the filling as you go. Make sure it’s tightly wrapped to keep all that delicious goodness inside.

Enjoying Your Pulled Pork Burritos

Now that you’ve created your pulled pork burrito masterpiece, it’s time to enjoy it! You can serve these burritos with a side of extra salsa, chips, or a light salad. The beauty of this dish is its versatility—feel free to customize it with your favorite toppings and sides.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

If you have any leftovers (which is rare, but possible!), store the pulled pork in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to four days. You can reheat it in the microwave or on the stovetop, adding a splash of chicken broth if it seems dry. Burritos can be wrapped in foil and frozen for up to three months. When you’re ready to eat, simply thaw and reheat in the oven or microwave.

Variations and Customizations

While traditional pulled pork burritos are delicious on their own, there are plenty of variations you can try to keep things interesting.

Spicy Pulled Pork Burrito

For those who love heat, add sliced jalapeños or hot sauce to your burrito. You can also mix diced chipotle peppers in adobo sauce into the pulled pork for a smoky, spicy kick.

Vegetarian Option

For a vegetarian twist, swap the pulled pork for sautéed mushrooms or jackfruit, seasoned with similar spices. This will provide a satisfying texture and flavor, making it a great alternative for non-meat eaters.

Breakfast Burrito

Transform your pulled pork burrito into a breakfast delight by adding scrambled eggs, diced potatoes, and a sprinkle of cheese. Serve it with a side of salsa verde for a morning treat that’s both hearty and delicious.

Pairing Suggestions

To round out your meal, consider pairing your pulled pork burritos with:

  • Mexican Street Corn (Elote): Grilled corn on the cob topped with mayo, cheese, chili powder, and lime.
  • Fresh Guacamole and Tortilla Chips: A classic appetizer or side that complements the burritos beautifully.
  • Margaritas or Horchata: Refreshing drinks that bring a festive touch to your meal.

Conclusion: A Dish to Remember

Pulled pork burritos are more than just a meal; they’re an experience. The combination of tender, juicy pork, vibrant toppings, and soft tortillas creates a symphony of flavors that will leave you craving more. Whether you’re enjoying them at a family gathering or indulging in a cozy night in, these burritos are sure to become a favorite in your household.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to make the ultimate pulled pork burritos. The deliciousness awaits!
