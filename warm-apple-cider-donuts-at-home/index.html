Warm Apple Cider Donuts at Home


Ever yearned for doughnuts that are freshly made, warm, and melting in your mouth? Achieving that feeling at home is a lot easier than you might think! Homemade apple cider doughnuts are delectable, fragrant, and simply scrumptious. Let’s dive into the world of doughnuts and create a fantastic treat in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup apple cider
  • 1/4 cup apple cider concentrate
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. In another bowl, beat the egg and mix it with the vegetable oil. The next step is to combine the wet and dry ingredients, incorporating the egg mixture into the dry ingredients to form a smooth batter.

  2. Add apple cider: Pour in the apple cider and mix it in thoroughly, followed by the apple cider concentrate and the vanilla extract. Stir everything well until it is all smooth, making sure all the ingredients have combined thoroughly. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let the dough rest for an hour.

  3. Fry the doughnuts: By now, your dough should be on the thicker side. Pour oil about 2 inches deep into a large pot (preferably cast iron) and heat it to about 375°F. Once the oil is hot enough, you can start dropping bits of dough into it with a spoon. Be careful not to overcrowd the oil, as it may lead to uneven cooking.

  4. Shape the doughnuts: The most interesting part - the shape! Various tools can come into play, such as a funnel or a small ice cream scoop. Another more traditional route is using a squeeze bottle or fingers to drop the dough into the oil. Once the dough lands into the oil, it swiftly starts to float as it forms a doughnut shape.

  5. Cook evenly: Ensure that you carefully flip the doughnuts as needed so that they cook evenly on all sides. Fry them for about two minutes or until they float to the surface and are golden brown in color. Drain the cooked doughnuts on a paper towel-lined baking tray and let them cool.

  6. Toss in cinnamon sugar: While still warm, toss the doughnuts into a bowl with a mix of granulated sugar and cinnamon. This step will add a delightful sweetness and cinnamon kick to your freshly-made apple cider doughnuts.

  7. Serve Freündeesly: Allow your doughnuts to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy warm apple cider doughnuts with your friends and family while sipping on a delicious apple cider for a complete experience.

And, that’s it! A homemade treat that is perfect for any occasion. Your friends will thank you, and you can call yourself a doughnut master. Retrieve your secret recipe carefully from your brain and start preparing today. Don’t forget to share your results with the folks you care about. Have a doughnut-making adventure and let us know how it goes. Buon appetito!
