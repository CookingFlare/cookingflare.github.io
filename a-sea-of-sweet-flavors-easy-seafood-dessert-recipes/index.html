A Sea of Sweet Flavors: Easy Seafood Dessert Recipes

Prepare to be tempted by the allure of the sea as well as the sweetness that lingers on the tongue.

  1. Shrimp and Mango Sorbet

A fruity and vibrant twist on a classic sorbet, this refreshing seafood dessert unites sweet and savory in perfect harmony. It’s a delightful conversation starter as the sweetness of ripe mangoes dances with the subtle, delicate brine of plump and succulent shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups peeled and diced mangoes
  • 1 cup cooked and peeled shrimp
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups water
  • Sugar syrup

Instructions:

  1. Prepare a simple sugar syrup by combining water and sugar in a saucepan. Heat over medium heat until sugar dissolves, and then let it cool.

  2. In a blender, puree the diced mangoes and lemon juice until smooth.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the cooked shrimp, honey, salt, and cooled sugar syrup. Toss well to coat.

  4. Combine the mango puree and shrimp mixture in a blender. Blend until smooth. Taste and adjust sweetness if necessary.

  5. Pour the sorbet mixture into a shallow, wide container and place it in the freezer.

  6. Every 30 minutes, stir the mixture with a fork to break up any ice crystals. Do this until the sorbet reaches a soft-serve consistency.

  7. Serve immediately for a soft-serve texture, or transfer the sorbet to a ziplock back, shape into portions, and freeze until firm.

  8. Lobster Tiramisu

Oh, the decadence of tiramisu! And now, imagine it with a twist - a luxurious, creamy delight infused with tender chunks of succulent, sweet lobster meat. Divine. Impress your dinner guests with this upscale dessert that combines the exquisite flavors of spaghetti lovers and coffee lovers alike.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup strong espresso or coffee
  • 2 shots of rum
  • 8 ladyfinger biscuits
  • 2 cups mascarpone cheese
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 lobster tails
  • 1/4 cup sifted cocoa powder

Instructions:

  1. Fill a large pot with water, add a pinch of salt, and bring to a boil. Carefully lower the lobster tails into the pot, cover, and steam until they are just cooked through, about 7-10 minutes. Allow them to cool, then carefully remove the meat from the shells.

  2. In a bowl, mix the espresso, rum, and a tablespoon of sugar.

  3. In another bowl, beat the egg yolks with 2 tablespoons of sugar until light and fluffy. Gradually whisk in the warm mascarpone cheese until smooth.

  4. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form, and gently fold them into the mascarpone mixture.

  5. Dunk each ladyfinger in the coffee mixture, and then place in a single layer at the bottom of a dish. Spread half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers, and then arrange half the lobster meat on top.

  6. Repeat the process with another layer of soaked ladyfingers, the remainder of the mascarpone mixture, and finally, the last of the lobster meat.

  7. Dust the top with cocoa powder. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.

  8. Salmon and Lavender Panna Cotta

Venture beyond the garden as you marry the soothing floral bouquet of lavender with the luscious ocean flavor of salmon in this exquisite dessert. Delicate and dreamy, this creamy, aromatic panna cotta will transport you to another world - an ethereal realm where the sea meets the land, and love is in the air.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon lavender buds
  • 1/2 teaspoon gelatin
  • 4 ounces cooked salmon, flaked
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the cream and honey until it just starts to simmer. Remove from heat and allow the lavender buds to steep in the cream for 10 minutes. Strain out the buds.
  2. Soften the gelatin in cold water for a few minutes, then gently heat it in a microwave until completely dissolved.
  3. Add the dissolved gelatin to the cream and stir well. Cover and refrigerate until the mixture begins to thicken.
  4. Gently fold in the flaked salmon and season with a pinch of salt.
  5. Pour the mixture into ramekins or a large souffl√© dish, and refrigerate until set.
  6. When ready to serve, run a knife around the edge of each ramekin to loosen the panna cotta. Invert onto a plate, garnish with a sprig of lavender or a Sakura in season, and serve.
