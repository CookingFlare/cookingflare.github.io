Fermented Pickled Watermelon Rind: A Sweet and Tangy Treat


If you’re looking for a unique and delicious way to use up those watermelon rinds, look no further than fermented pickled watermelon rind! This sweet and tangy treat is not only a great way to reduce food waste but also a delightful addition to any meal or snack. In this post, we’ll walk you through the simple steps to create your own batch of fermented pickled watermelon rind.

Ingredients

To make fermented pickled watermelon rind, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • Watermelon rind: Make sure to remove the green outer skin and any remaining red flesh. Cut the rind into small, bite-sized pieces.
  • Salt: Use a high-quality, non-iodized salt for the best flavor and fermentation results.
  • Water: Filtered water is ideal to avoid any chlorine or other chemicals that may interfere with the fermentation process.
  • Spices: Feel free to experiment with spices such as peppercorns, coriander seeds, or chili flakes to add extra flavor to your pickles.

Instructions

  1. Start by thoroughly washing the watermelon rind to remove any dirt or debris.
  2. Cut the watermelon rind into small, bite-sized pieces. You can remove the green outer skin and any remaining red flesh, as they won’t be used in this recipe.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the watermelon rind pieces with salt. Use about 1 tablespoon of salt for every cup of watermelon rind. The salt will help draw out moisture from the rind and create a brine for fermentation.
  4. Massage the salt into the watermelon rind pieces until they start to release their juices. This will help create the brine.
  5. Transfer the salted watermelon rind and its juices into a clean jar. Make sure the jar is large enough to leave some headspace at the top.
  6. Pour enough filtered water into the jar to cover the watermelon rind completely. Leave about an inch of headspace to allow for fermentation.
  7. Add your desired spices to the jar. This is where you can get creative and experiment with different flavors. You can add a few peppercorns, coriander seeds, or even some chili flakes for a spicy kick.
  8. Secure the lid on the jar tightly.
  9. Leave the jar at room temperature for about 3-5 days, depending on your desired level of fermentation. During this time, the watermelon rind will start to ferment and develop its tangy flavor.
  10. After the fermentation period, taste the pickled watermelon rind. If it has reached your desired level of tanginess, transfer the jar to the refrigerator to slow down the fermentation process. If you prefer a stronger tang, you can leave it at room temperature for a few more days.
  11. Your fermented pickled watermelon rind is now ready to enjoy! Serve it as a side dish, add it to salads or sandwiches, or simply snack on it straight out of the jar.

Conclusion

Fermented pickled watermelon rind is a delightful and unexpected treat that not only reduces food waste but also adds a sweet and tangy flavor to your meals. With just a few simple ingredients and some patience, you can create your own batch of these delicious pickles. So, the next time you find yourself with leftover watermelon rinds, don’t throw them away. Give them a second life by fermenting them into a unique and tasty treat. Enjoy!
