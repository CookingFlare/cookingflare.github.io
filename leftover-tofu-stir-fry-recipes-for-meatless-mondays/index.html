Leftover Tofu Stir-Fry Recipes for Meatless Mondays


Nothing brightens up a dreary Monday like a vibrant, flavorful meal that’s both nutritious and easy to prepare. Enter the world of tofu stir-fries—a perfect way to reinvent leftover tofu and turn it into a dazzling centerpiece for your dinner table. Whether you have firm, silken, or marinated tofu remnants, these recipes transform your leftovers into mouthwatering dishes that will make Meatless Mondays the highlight of your week.

The Magic of Leftover Tofu

Tofu is incredibly versatile. Its mild flavor readily absorbs any seasoning, marinade, or sauce, allowing you to customize each stir-fry to your liking. Leftover tofu, in particular, comes pre-seasoned or partially cooked, which means it can step into the skillet and soak up flavors quickly, cutting down overall preparation time. Repurposing tofu also cuts down food waste and saves money, making it an economical and eco-friendly choice.

Preparing Your Leftover Tofu for Stir-Frying

Before we dive into recipe ideas, here are some tips to prepare your tofu properly, ensuring the best texture and flavor:

  • Drain thoroughly: Leftover tofu might be a bit wetter than when first opened. Press it between paper towels or a clean kitchen towel to remove excess moisture.
  • Cut uniformly: Whether cubes, strips, or triangles, even pieces ensure even cooking.
  • Boost texture: For firm or extra-firm tofu, consider giving the edges a quick sear for added crispness. For softer tofu, gentle handling helps keep it from breaking apart during stir-frying.
  • Reheat smartly: If your tofu was frozen, thaw it completely before cooking for the best texture.

The Flavor Foundations: Sauces and Seasonings

When working with leftover tofu, sauces are your best friend. The right sauce not only enhances flavor but also ties all the ingredients together. Here are some popular flavor bases to keep handy:

  • Soy-Ginger Garlic Sauce: A classic blend that adds sharpness and warmth. Mix low-sodium soy sauce with minced garlic, grated fresh ginger, a dash of sesame oil, and a splash of rice vinegar.
  • Spicy Peanut Sauce: Creamy, nutty, and with a kick. Combine peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, chili flakes, and a hint of honey or maple syrup.
  • Sweet Teriyaki Glaze: Combine soy sauce, mirin or rice wine, brown sugar, and a bit of cornstarch slurry for a sticky, sweet glaze perfect for thicker tofu pieces.
  • Thai Basil Sauce: Use Thai basil leaves, lime juice, fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian), garlic, and chili for a fresh and aromatic punch.

Feel free to experiment with fresh herbs, juices, and spices to make your stir-fry pop.

Leftover Tofu Stir-Fry Recipes to Try Tonight

1. Ginger-Garlic Tofu and Broccoli Stir-Fry

This simple, aromatic stir-fry is a comforting classic, perfect for a quick weekday dinner.

Ingredients:

  • Leftover firm tofu, cubed
  • 2 cups fresh broccoli florets
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water (optional for thickening)
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add ginger and garlic; sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Toss broccoli and bell pepper into the skillet. Stir-fry for about 3-4 minutes until vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. Add the leftover tofu to the pan and drizzle with soy sauce and sesame oil. Toss gently to combine.
  5. If you want a thicker sauce, add the cornstarch slurry and stir until the sauce thickens, about 1-2 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat and garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions.

Serve over steamed jasmine rice or chewy brown rice for a satisfying meal.

2. Spicy Peanut Tofu Stir-Fry with Snap Peas

This dish merges creamy peanut flavors with crispy snap peas for a delightful texture and a satisfying spicy finish.

Ingredients:

  • Leftover tofu, sliced into strips
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1/4 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp chili flakes or sriracha
  • 2 tbsp water (to thin sauce)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, chili flakes, and water until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet and add tofu strips, cooking until golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add snap peas and carrot to the skillet; stir-fry until just tender, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Return tofu to the skillet and pour the peanut sauce over everything. Toss gently to coat and heat through.
  5. Sprinkle fresh cilantro before serving over cooked rice noodles or quinoa.

3. Sweet Teriyaki Tofu Stir-Fry with Mushrooms and Baby Corn

A sweet and savory delight with an umami-packed mix of mushrooms and crunchy baby corn.

Ingredients:

  • Leftover tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms (shiitake, cremini, button), sliced
  • 1/2 cup baby corn, halved
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin or rice wine
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Sesame seeds to garnish

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, mirin, brown sugar, and cornstarch slurry. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan, sauté garlic briefly until fragrant.
  3. Add mushrooms and baby corn, stir-fry for 5 minutes until mushrooms release their juices and baby corn softens slightly.
  4. Add tofu cubes and pour in the sauce mixture. Cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring gently as the sauce thickens and glazes the ingredients.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds and pair with steamed white rice or sticky rice.

4. Thai Basil Tofu Stir-Fry

Brimming with the bold flavors of Thai basil, chili, and tangy lime, this recipe offers freshness and heat that dance on your palate.

Ingredients:

  • Leftover tofu, cubed or torn into chunks
  • 1 cup green beans or snap peas
  • 1 red chili, thinly sliced (adjust to taste)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp sugar or palm sugar
  • 1 cup fresh Thai basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a wok, add garlic and chili, and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  2. Add green beans or snap peas, cook for 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  3. Toss in tofu and pour in soy sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Stir-fry for another 2 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and immediately stir in fresh basil leaves; let the heat wilt them slightly.
  5. Serve this vibrant dish with jasmine rice or rice noodles.

Tips for Elevating Your Tofu Stir-Fries

  • Add crunch: Sprinkle roasted peanuts, cashews, or toasted sesame seeds on top for a delightful texture contrast.
  • Use fresh veggies: Incorporate whatever fresh vegetables you have—carrots, zucchini, baby corn, mushrooms, snap peas all work beautifully.
  • Balance your flavors: Play with sour, salty, sweet, and spicy elements to achieve harmony in your dish. A splash of citrus or a pinch of sugar can lift an ordinary stir-fry to extraordinary.
  • Experiment with noodles and grains: Swap rice for soba noodles, quinoa, farro or cauliflower rice to keep things exciting.
  • Keep tofu interesting: Adding a quick pan-fry step before stir-frying can transform soft leftover tofu into crispy nuggets that contrast well with saucy vegetables.

Wrapping Up

Leftover tofu stir-fry recipes are a treasure trove for weeknight dinner creativity. By combining fresh vegetables, complementary sauces, and carefully prepared tofu, you’ll turn what’s left in your fridge into vibrant, nourishing meals. Meatless Mondays have never been so delicious and guilt-free.

So next time you see leftover tofu staring back at you from the fridge, grab a wok, some veggies, and a handful of simple seasonings and let your imagination run wild. Your taste buds—and your wallet—will thank you.

Happy cooking!
