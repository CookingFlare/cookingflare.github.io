Korean Braised Short Ribs: A Tender and Flavorful Delight


If you’re looking to elevate your cooking game and impress your guests with a dish that’s bursting with flavor, Korean braised short ribs, also known as Galbi jjim, is the perfect choice. This traditional Korean dish features succulent beef short ribs slow-cooked in a rich, savory sauce until they are fall-off-the-bone tender. The result is a mouthwatering delight that will leave everyone at the table craving for more.

Ingredients

To create this delectable dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 3 pounds of beef short ribs
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of rice wine
  • 1/4 cup of sesame oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds
  • 2 cups of water
  • 3 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 6 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 4 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Short Ribs

Start by soaking the short ribs in cold water for about 1 hour to draw out the excess blood. After soaking, drain the water and pat the ribs dry with paper towels.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

In a bowl, mix together the soy sauce, brown sugar, rice wine, sesame oil, onion, garlic, ginger, and sesame seeds. This aromatic marinade will infuse the ribs with layers of umami flavor.

Step 3: Marinate the Ribs

Place the short ribs in a large, shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, ensuring that the ribs are evenly coated. Cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and penetrate the meat.

Step 4: Braise the Ribs

Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C). In a large, oven-safe pot, arrange the marinated short ribs along with any remaining marinade. Add water, carrots, potatoes, dried shiitake mushrooms, and green onions to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Cover the pot and place it in the oven. Allow the ribs to braise for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and almost falling off the bone. The slow cooking process will create an incredibly flavorful and tender dish that’s worth the wait.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the ribs are done, carefully remove them from the pot and arrange them on a serving platter. Spoon the vegetables and sauce over the ribs, and garnish with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

Pair your Korean braised short ribs with steamed rice and kimchi for a complete and satisfying meal that will transport your taste buds to Korea.

Conclusion

Korean braised short ribs are a true testament to the art of slow cooking, resulting in a dish that’s tender, flavorful, and deeply satisfying. The combination of savory soy sauce, sweet brown sugar, and aromatic spices creates a harmony of flavors that will delight anyone who has the pleasure of tasting this classic Korean dish. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to create this unforgettable and mouthwatering delight. Your guests will thank you, and your taste buds will rejoice in the flavors of Korea.
