Flavors of the Middle East: 4 Marinades for Grilled Kebabs


If you’re looking to add some excitement to your next barbecue, why not explore the vibrant flavors of the Middle East? Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its bold spices and aromatic herbs, which can transform simple grilled kebabs into a culinary delight. In this blog post, we’ll explore four mouthwatering marinades that will take your grilled kebabs to the next level. So, fire up your grill and get ready to tantalize your taste buds with these Middle Eastern flavors!

1. Za’atar and Lemon Marinade

Za’atar is a fragrant Middle Eastern spice blend made with dried thyme, sumac, sesame seeds, and salt. Combined with the tanginess of lemon juice, this marinade adds a zesty kick to your kebabs. To make this marinade, mix together 2 tablespoons of za’atar, the juice of 2 lemons, 1/4 cup of olive oil, 3 cloves of minced garlic, and a pinch of salt. Marinate your choice of protein (such as chicken, beef, or lamb) for at least 4 hours or overnight for maximum flavor. Grill the marinated kebabs to perfection, and serve with a side of creamy tzatziki sauce for a truly Middle Eastern feast.

2. Harissa and Mint Marinade

Harissa is a spicy chili paste commonly used in North African and Middle Eastern cuisines. It adds a fiery kick to your kebabs, while the freshness of mint helps balance the heat. To make this marinade, combine 2 tablespoons of harissa paste, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh mint leaves, the juice of 1 lemon, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Coat your choice of protein with the marinade and let it sit for at least 2 hours. Grill the kebabs until they are cooked through, and serve with a cooling cucumber and yogurt salad for a delicious contrast of flavors.

3. Pomegranate and Molasses Marinade

Pomegranate molasses is a thick, tangy syrup made from reduced pomegranate juice. It adds a sweet and sour note to your kebabs, creating a delightful flavor profile. To make this marinade, whisk together 1/4 cup of pomegranate molasses, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 cloves of minced garlic, 1 teaspoon of ground cumin, and a pinch of salt. Marinate your choice of protein for at least 3 hours, allowing the flavors to penetrate the meat. Grill the kebabs to perfection, and serve with a side of fluffy couscous or a fresh green salad for a complete Middle Eastern meal.

4. Yogurt and Spice Marinade

Yogurt-based marinades are a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine, as they help tenderize the meat and infuse it with flavor. For this marinade, combine 1 cup of plain yogurt, 2 tablespoons of ground cumin, 2 tablespoons of ground coriander, 1 tablespoon of paprika, 2 cloves of minced garlic, and a pinch of salt. Mix well and coat your choice of protein with the marinade. Let it marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for a more intense flavor. Grill the kebabs until they are cooked through and serve with warm pita bread and a dollop of tangy tahini sauce for a truly authentic Middle Eastern experience.

With these four tantalizing marinades, you can bring the flavors of the Middle East to your backyard barbecue. Each marinade offers a unique combination of spices and seasonings that will transport your taste buds to exotic lands. So, gather your friends and family, fire up the grill, and embark on a culinary journey through the Middle East. Happy grilling!
