Southern Comfort: Homemade Chicken and Waffles Recipe


When it comes to Southern comfort food, few dishes can rival the delightful combination of crispy fried chicken and fluffy waffles. Whether you’re looking for a hearty weekend brunch or a satisfying dinner, this homemade chicken and waffles recipe is sure to hit the spot. The contrast of savory and sweet, crispy and tender, makes this dish a true Southern classic.

Ingredients

For the Chicken:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Waffles:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Maple Butter:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

Marinating the Chicken:

  1. Place the chicken breasts in a bowl and pour the buttermilk over them. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

Making the Waffle Batter:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, beat the eggs and then stir in the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

Preparing the Maple Butter:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, maple syrup, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt. Mix until well combined. Set aside.

Frying the Chicken:

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
  2. Remove the chicken from the buttermilk, allowing any excess to drip off. Dredge the chicken in the seasoned flour, ensuring it is evenly coated.
  3. In a large skillet, heat about an inch of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, carefully place the chicken in the oil and fry for 6-8 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through. Transfer to a wire rack to drain any excess oil.

Cooking the Waffles:

  1. Preheat your waffle iron and lightly grease it if necessary.
  2. Pour the waffle batter onto the hot iron and cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions, until golden and crispy.

Assembling the Dish:

  1. Place a waffle on a plate, top with a piece of fried chicken, and finish with a generous dollop of the maple butter.
  2. Serve immediately and enjoy the delicious contrast of flavors and textures.

Conclusion

This homemade chicken and waffles recipe captures the essence of Southern comfort food, bringing together the best of both savory and sweet worlds. The crispy, flavorful chicken pairs perfectly with the light, fluffy waffles, and the maple butter adds a touch of indulgence. Whether you’re hosting a brunch with friends or simply treating yourself to a special meal, this dish is sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, get cooking, and savor the delightful flavors of this Southern classic.
