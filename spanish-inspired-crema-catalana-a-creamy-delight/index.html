Spanish Inspired Crema Catalana: A Creamy Delight


If you’re a fan of creamy desserts with a hint of citrus, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re diving into the world of Spanish cuisine to bring you a recipe that will leave your taste buds dancing with delight. Say hello to Crema Catalana, a traditional Spanish dessert that is sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

To make this creamy delight, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 lemon, zest only
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Brown sugar, for caramelizing

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat. Add the cinnamon stick and lemon zest, and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Let it infuse for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  2. While the milk is simmering, in a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla extract until well combined. This will be the base of our creamy custard.

  3. Once the milk has infused, remove the cinnamon stick and lemon zest, and gradually pour the warm milk into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to prevent the eggs from curdling. Make sure to pour slowly and whisk vigorously to ensure a smooth custard.

  4. Return the custard mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 10-15 minutes. Be patient, as slow cooking ensures a velvety texture.

  5. Once the custard has thickened, remove it from the heat and pour it into individual ramekins or serving dishes. Allow the custard to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or until set.

  6. Just before serving, sprinkle a thin layer of brown sugar over the chilled custard. Using a kitchen torch, carefully caramelize the sugar until it forms a golden crust. If you don’t have a kitchen torch, you can also place the ramekins under a broiler for a few minutes, keeping a close eye on them to prevent burning.

  7. Serve the Crema Catalana immediately, delighting in the contrast between the creamy custard and the crisp caramelized sugar. The result is a dessert that is both rich and refreshing, with a perfect balance of flavors.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of indulgence, you can add a splash of liqueur, such as Grand Marnier or Cointreau, to the custard mixture before cooking.

  • If you’re not a fan of cinnamon, you can omit the cinnamon stick and replace it with a vanilla bean for a more classic flavor.

  • To make this dessert even more special, you can garnish each serving with fresh berries or a sprinkle of ground cinnamon.

  • Crema Catalana is best served chilled, so make sure to allow enough time for the custard to set in the refrigerator before serving.

  • This recipe makes approximately 6 servings, but you can easily double or halve the ingredients to suit your needs.

Conclusion

Crema Catalana is a Spanish dessert that brings together the richness of custard and the sweetness of caramelized sugar. Its creamy texture and delicate flavors make it a true delight for the senses. So why not transport yourself to the sunny shores of Spain and indulge in this luscious treat? Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of something special, Crema Catalana is sure to impress. Give it a try and let the creamy delight melt in your mouth!
