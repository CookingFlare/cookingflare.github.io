Instant Pot Veggie Burgers: A Plant-Based Meal in Minutes


Veggie burgers have become a staple in plant-based diets, offering a delicious and healthy alternative to traditional meat patties. Preparing them at home guarantees fresh ingredients and gives you complete control over flavors and textures. But what if you could make mouthwatering veggie burgers in less time without sacrificing taste or quality? Enter the Instant Pot! This versatile kitchen appliance can streamline the process and help you whip up a batch of hearty, nutritious veggie burgers in no time. Let’s dive into how you can create these impressive plant-based burgers with ease.

Why Veggie Burgers Made in an Instant Pot?

Conventional methods of preparing veggie burgers often involve multiple steps, from cooking grains and legumes separately to blending everything together and then pan-frying or baking. The Instant Pot revolutionizes this process by allowing you to cook the core ingredients in one pot without compromising on flavor or consistency. The pressure-cooking capability ensures that ingredients like lentils, chickpeas, or quinoa are tender, while the sauté function lets you infuse aromatics and spices for a perfect taste profile.

Additionally, homemade veggie burgers are budget-friendly and free from unwanted fillers or preservatives. They’re fully customizable—swap out ingredients based on dietary preferences or simply use what you have in your pantry.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Here’s a breakdown of ingredients for straightforward yet flavor-packed veggie burgers. Feel free to adjust based on taste:

For the Base:

  • 1 cup of dried lentils or chickpeas (soaked for a minimum of 6 hours if using chickpeas)
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats for binding
  • 1/2 cup cooked quinoa or brown rice for added texture

Vegetables:

  • 1/2 cup finely diced onions
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped mushrooms (optional, for a “meaty” texture)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley or cilantro

Spices and Seasonings:

  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder or red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Optional Flavor Boosters:

  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast for a umami punch
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard

Additional Binding Agents:

  • 2 tablespoons ground flaxseeds mixed with 6 tablespoons water (flax egg substitute)
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs (use gluten-free if necessary)

For Cooking:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or avocado oil for sautéing
  • Water or vegetable stock for pressure cooking

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Rinse the lentils or chickpeas thoroughly and set aside. Assemble all other ingredients within easy reach. This saves time and ensures you don’t overlook anything while cooking.

Step 2: Cook the Legumes and Grains in the Instant Pot

Turn the Instant Pot on and select the sauté function. Add some olive oil and sauté the onions and garlic until fragrant, about 2 minutes. Mix in the grated carrots and mushrooms, cooking for another minute. Add the rinsed lentils or chickpeas along with quinoa or rice and pour in enough water or vegetable stock to cover the ingredients by about half an inch.

Cancel the sauté function, secure the lid, and select the pressure cooking feature. Cook on high pressure for 15 minutes for lentils or 25 minutes for chickpeas. Once done, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then carefully vent any remaining pressure.

Step 3: Flavor and Mash

Open the lid, and check the texture of your legumes—they should be tender but not overly mushy. Drain any excess liquid if necessary, leaving just enough to keep the mixture moist.

Add all your spices, parsley or cilantro, soy sauce, nutritional yeast, and mustard. Using a potato masher or the back of a spoon, mash the ingredients until they come together. You’re aiming for a consistency that’s chunky yet cohesive.

Step 4: Shape and Bind

Add the flaxseed mixture and panko breadcrumbs to the mashed mixture. Stir thoroughly until everything combines into a uniform “dough.” You can adjust the consistency by adding more breadcrumbs if the mixture feels too wet or some water if it’s dry.

Shape the mixture into burger patties of your desired size—typically about 1/2 to 3/4 inches thick to ensure even cooking.

Step 5: Sauté for Crispness

Clean out the Instant Pot or use a skillet instead. Select the sauté function on the Instant Pot or heat a pan over medium heat, adding a little oil. Cook each veggie burger for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until a golden crust forms. This step isn’t just about aesthetics—it enhances the flavor and adds a satisfying texture.

Serving Suggestions

Freshly made Instant Pot veggie burgers pair beautifully with both classic and creative accompaniments. Here are some ideas to inspire your presentation:

  1. Classic Burger: Place the patty on a toasted whole-grain bun and top with sliced avocado, lettuce, tomatoes, onions, and your favorite condiments such as vegan mayo, barbecue sauce, or mustard.

  2. Bowl Style: Serve the patty over a bed of greens, roasted vegetables, and quinoa or rice, drizzling with tahini dressing or a zesty vinaigrette for a healthier alternative.

  3. Wrap It Up: Use a tortilla or lettuce leaf to create a veggie burger wrap, packed with crunchy slaw, hummus, and pickled onions.

  4. Top It Off: Crumble the patty over a baked potato or salad for a hearty meal.

Storage and Meal Prep Tips

The beauty of these Instant Pot veggie burgers is that they’re perfect for meal prep! Here are a few storage tips:

  1. Refrigeration: Store uncooked patties in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Cooked patties can last up to 4-5 days.

  2. Freezing: Wrap each uncooked patty individually in parchment paper, place them in a freezer-safe bag, and store for up to 3 months. When ready to cook, defrost overnight in the fridge and sauté or bake.

  3. Batch Cooking: Double the recipe to make extras for the week. These patties are versatile—you can transform them into sliders, crumble them into soups, or serve as a protein-packed side.

Customization Ideas

One of the best things about veggie burgers is their adaptability. Here are some customization ideas to keep your recipe fresh and exciting:

  • Swap the Legumes: Try black beans, kidney beans, or even split peas for a new flavor profile.
  • Sneak in Veggies: Add grated zucchini, chopped spinach, or roasted sweet potatoes to the mix.
  • Spice It Up: Experiment with different spice combinations, like curry powder, herbes de Provence, or garam masala.

Why Homemade Beats Store-Bought

Store-bought veggie burgers often contain processed ingredients or additives that can lessen their nutritional value. When making them yourself, you have complete control over the flavors, nutrition, and quality. Plus, nothing beats the satisfaction of knowing your meal was crafted by hand using wholesome, unprocessed ingredients.

Final Thoughts

Instant Pot veggie burgers not only save time but also elevate plant-based dining to a whole new level. They’re nutritious, customizable, and, most importantly, brimming with flavor. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, this recipe is an excellent addition to your repertoire. Once you experience how easy it is to create homemade veggie burgers in the Instant Pot, you may never return to store-bought options again. So, dust off your Instant Pot, gather your ingredients, and get cooking—you’re about to make veggie burger magic in minutes!
