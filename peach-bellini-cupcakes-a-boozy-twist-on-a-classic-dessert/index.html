Peach Bellini Cupcakes: A Boozy Twist on a Classic Dessert


Indulge in the delightful combination of peaches and champagne with these Peach Bellini Cupcakes. This boozy twist on a classic dessert is sure to impress your guests and leave them craving for more. Perfect for special occasions or simply when you want to treat yourself, these cupcakes are a must-try.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup peach puree (made from fresh or frozen peaches)
  • 1/4 cup champagne
  • 1/4 cup milk

For the Peach Bellini Frosting

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup peach puree
  • 1/4 cup champagne

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the peach puree, champagne, and milk. Gradually add this mixture to the butter mixture, alternating with the dry ingredients. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing well after each addition.

  5. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners, filling them about two-thirds full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean.

  6. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the Peach Bellini frosting. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, one cup at a time, until fully incorporated. Mix in the peach puree and champagne until smooth and fluffy.

  7. Once the cupcakes have completely cooled, frost them with the Peach Bellini frosting. You can do this using a piping bag or simply spreading the frosting with a knife.

  8. Optional: Garnish each cupcake with a slice of fresh peach or a sprinkle of edible gold dust for an extra touch of elegance.

  9. Serve and enjoy! These Peach Bellini Cupcakes are best enjoyed fresh, but can be stored in an airtight container for up to three days.

Tips and Variations

  • For a non-alcoholic version, you can substitute the champagne with sparkling white grape juice or peach nectar.
  • Feel free to experiment with different fruit purees. Mango or strawberry puree would also work well in this recipe.
  • If you prefer a lighter frosting, you can reduce the amount of powdered sugar to your liking.
  • These cupcakes can be a great addition to bridal showers, baby showers, or any other special occasion. You can even turn them into a centerpiece by arranging them on a tiered cupcake stand.

Now, go ahead and give these Peach Bellini Cupcakes a try. With their delicate peach flavor and hint of champagne, they are sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Cheers to a sweet and boozy twist on a classic dessert!
