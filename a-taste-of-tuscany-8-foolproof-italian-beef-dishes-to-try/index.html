A Taste of Tuscany: 8 Foolproof Italian Beef Dishes to Try


Tuscany, known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and exquisite cuisine, is a region that delights the senses. One characteristic of Tuscany that leaves a lasting impression is their love for beef dishes. Juicy, tender, and packed with flavor, Tuscany’s beef dishes are simple to prepare and taste phenomenal. Let’s dive into 8 foolproof Italian beef recipes to bring that authentic Tuscan taste to your table.

1. Florentine-Style Beef Stew (Ragù alla Fiorentina)

Ragù alla Fiorentina, a hearty stew originating from Florence, is made with guanciale (cured pork cheek) and tomatoes. It goes perfectly with creamy polenta or your favorite pasta. To make this dish at home, start by sautéeing chopped onion, celery, and carrots in olive oil. Next, add the ground beef and cook until browned. Lastly, add canned tomatoes, Guanciale, and broth, and simmer for hours. Enjoy this mouth-watering stew!

2. Chianina-Style Grilled Steaks (Bistecca alla Chianina)

Chianina is an ancient breed of cattle found in central Italy. Their meat is known for tenderness, juiciness, and flavor. To achieve that Chianina-style grilled steak, marinate the meat with olive oil, garlic, rosemary, and lemon juice for a few hours. Preheat your grill, sear the steaks, and let them rest before slicing and serving.

3. Tuscan Beef Ragu with Ricotta (Ragù di Manzo alla Toscana)

Although similar to the Florentine-style beef stew, the Tuscan version adds ricotta cheese for an extra layer of creaminess. Add cooked ground beef and grated Parmesan to a saucepan with canned tomatoes, red wine, and herbs. Simmer for hours before adding ricotta and serving with pasta or crusty bread.

4. Beef Osso Buco (Osso Buco alla Tosca)

Beef Osso Buco is a famous Tuscan dish featuring braised veal shanks. To replicate this flavorful yet simple dish, braise the meat with vegetables, browned beef, and tomatoes. Simmer for hours until the meat is tender and fall-off-the-bone delicious.

5. Beef and Red Wine Braised Shoulder (Braccolino al Vino Rosso)

Slow-cooked beef shoulder is an excellent comforting meal that highlights the robust flavor of red wine. Marinate the beef shoulder with garlic, rosemary, and red wine before slow-cooking it in a casserole dish. Serve this tender and flavorful dish with potatoes or crusty bread.

6. T-bone Steak Florentine-Style (Steak alla Fiorentina)

T-bone steaks are another delightful Italian beef option with Tuscan flair. To prepare these steaks, season them with rosemary, garlic, salt, and pepper before cooking on a hot grill. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar before serving.

7. Tuscan Boiled Beef (Bollito di Manzo alla Toscana)

Tuscan boiled beef is both simple and substantial. The art is in the timing of adding various ingredients to the pot. Begin by boiling the beef with carrots, celery, and onion before adding herbs, spices, and red wine for an exquisite twist.

8. Beef Stew with Porcini Mushrooms (Ragù di Manzo con Porcini)

Lastly, end with a hearty beef stew that highlights the exquisite taste of porcini mushrooms. To create this dish, cook chopped onion, celery, and carrots in olive oil before adding the ground beef. Add dried porcini mushrooms, their soaking liquid, and canned tomatoes to create a delectable stew.

By cooking these authentic Italian beef dishes, you will transport your taste buds straight to the Tuscan countryside. The combination of simple ingredients, robust flavors, and traditional techniques makes each dish a genuine Tuscan experience. Buon appetito!
