Paleo Baking: 5 Delicious Recipes for Grain-Free Treats


Are you following a paleo diet and craving something sweet? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share five delicious grain-free treats that are perfect for paleo baking. These recipes are not only healthy but also incredibly tasty. So, let’s get started and satisfy that sweet tooth!

1. Almond Flour Chocolate Chip Cookies

Who doesn’t love a classic chocolate chip cookie? With this paleo-friendly recipe, you can enjoy the taste of a traditional cookie without the guilt. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the almond flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, baking soda, and salt. Mix until well combined.
  3. Fold in the dark chocolate chips.
  4. Using a cookie scoop or spoon, drop rounded tablespoons of cookie dough onto the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until lightly golden around the edges.
  6. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Coconut Flour Banana Bread

Banana bread is a classic comfort food, and this paleo version will not disappoint. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the mashed bananas, eggs, coconut flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt until well combined.
  3. Pour the batter into the greased loaf pan.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Pumpkin Spice Muffins

With fall just around the corner, it’s time to embrace the flavors of pumpkin spice. These paleo pumpkin spice muffins are the perfect treat for a cozy morning. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the almond flour, coconut flour, pumpkin puree, maple syrup, eggs, vanilla extract, pumpkin spice, baking soda, and salt until well combined.
  3. Divide the batter evenly among the paper liners, filling each about 3/4 full.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

4. Apple Cinnamon Crumble Bars

These apple cinnamon crumble bars are the perfect combination of sweet and tart. They make a great snack or dessert option. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups diced apples

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the almond flour, coconut flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt. Mix until well combined.
  3. Press two-thirds of the mixture into the bottom of the prepared baking dish to form the crust.
  4. Spread the diced apples evenly over the crust.
  5. Sprinkle the remaining crumble mixture over the apples.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the top is golden brown.
  7. Allow the bars to cool in the dish for 10 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

5. Chocolate Avocado Pudding

If you’re looking for a rich and creamy dessert, this chocolate avocado pudding is a must-try. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor or blender, combine the avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, coconut milk, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Transfer the pudding to individual serving dishes or jars.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.
  4. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Conclusion

Paleo baking doesn’t mean sacrificing taste. With these five delicious grain-free treats, you can indulge in sweet treats while staying true to your paleo lifestyle. Give these recipes a try, and let us know your favorite! Happy baking!
