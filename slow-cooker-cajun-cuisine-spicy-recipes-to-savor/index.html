Slow Cooker Cajun Cuisine: Spicy Recipes to Savor


If you’re a fan of bold flavors and hearty meals, then Cajun cuisine is right up your alley. With its roots in Louisiana, Cajun cooking is known for its spiciness, depth of flavor, and the use of a wide variety of ingredients. And what better way to bring out those flavors than by using a slow cooker? In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and easy slow cooker Cajun recipes that will transport your taste buds straight to the bayous of Louisiana.

1. Slow Cooker Jambalaya

Jambalaya, a quintessential Cajun dish, is a flavorful one-pot wonder that combines rice, meat, and vegetables. By using a slow cooker, you can let the flavors meld together beautifully, resulting in a dish that is both comforting and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 pound chicken thighs, boneless and skinless, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups long grain rice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, brown the sausage and chicken until cooked through. Transfer to the slow cooker.
  2. Add the onion, bell pepper, celery, and garlic to the skillet and saut√© until softened. Transfer to the slow cooker.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  4. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours.
  5. Stir in the rice and cook on high for an additional 1-2 hours, or until the rice is tender.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

2. Slow Cooker Shrimp Etouffee

Etouffee, meaning “smothered” in French, is a rich and flavorful dish that showcases the beauty of Cajun cooking. Traditionally made with crawfish, this slow cooker version features shrimp, making it an excellent choice for seafood lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In the slow cooker, combine the shrimp, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, diced tomatoes, chicken broth, tomato paste, Cajun seasoning, thyme, oregano, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir to combine.
  2. Cook on low for 4-5 hours or on high for 2-3 hours.
  3. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour to make a roux. Cook, stirring continuously, until the roux turns a dark golden brown color.
  4. Slowly whisk the roux into the slow cooker to thicken the sauce.
  5. Cook for an additional 30 minutes on high, or until the sauce has thickened.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the shrimp etouffee over cooked rice, garnished with fresh parsley.

3. Slow Cooker Red Beans and Rice

No Cajun meal is complete without a serving of red beans and rice. This classic dish is a staple in Louisiana households, often enjoyed on Mondays as a way to use up leftover Sunday ham. By using a slow cooker, you can achieve the same rich and smoky flavors with minimal effort.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound dried red kidney beans, soaked overnight and drained
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 ham hock or ham bone
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In the slow cooker, combine the soaked and drained kidney beans, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, smoked sausage, ham hock or bone, chicken broth, bay leaves, thyme, oregano, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir to combine.
  2. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or on high for 4-6 hours, or until the beans are tender.
  3. Remove the ham hock or bone from the slow cooker and shred the meat. Return the meat to the slow cooker.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the red beans and sausage over cooked rice, garnished with chopped green onions.

Conclusion

By using your slow cooker to prepare these delicious Cajun dishes, you can enjoy the bold and spicy flavors of Louisiana without spending hours in the kitchen. From Jambalaya to Shrimp Etouffee and Red Beans and Rice, these recipes will transport you to the heart of Cajun country. So dust off your slow cooker, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the taste of the bayou. Happy cooking!
