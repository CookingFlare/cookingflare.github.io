Budget-Friendly Greek Salad Recipes for a Fresh Meal


When it comes to healthy and refreshing meals, Greek salads are a go-to choice for many. Packed with fresh vegetables, tangy feta cheese, and a flavorful dressing, Greek salads are not only delicious but also budget-friendly. Whether you’re looking for a light lunch, a side dish for a barbecue, or a quick and easy meal, these Greek salad recipes will surely satisfy your cravings without breaking the bank.

Classic Greek Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives
  • 4 ounces feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and bell pepper.
  2. Add the Kalamata olives and feta cheese to the bowl.
  3. In a small jar, combine the olive oil, red wine vinegar, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Shake well to emulsify the dressing.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  5. Serve immediately or refrigerate for later. This classic Greek salad is perfect as a standalone meal or a side dish for grilled meats.

Quinoa Greek Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup pitted Kalamata olives
  • 4 ounces crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and let it cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, Kalamata olives, feta cheese, and parsley.
  3. In a small jar, combine the olive oil, red wine vinegar, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Shake well to emulsify the dressing.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the quinoa salad and toss gently to combine.
  5. Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes before serving. This quinoa Greek salad is a perfect make-ahead dish for picnics or potlucks.

Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, chopped
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup pitted Kalamata olives
  • 4 ounces crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, Kalamata olives, feta cheese, and mint.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, ground cumin, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the chickpea salad and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Let the salad marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before serving. This Mediterranean chickpea salad is a protein-packed option for a light and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

With these budget-friendly Greek salad recipes, you can enjoy the fresh and vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean without spending a fortune. Whether you prefer the classic combination of tomatoes, cucumbers, and feta, or you want to add a twist with quinoa or chickpeas, these recipes are versatile, nutritious, and perfect for any occasion. Embrace the simplicity and goodness of Greek cuisine with these satisfying salads that won’t disappoint your taste buds or your wallet.
