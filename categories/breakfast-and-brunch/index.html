Sweet and Savory Dutch Baby Pancake Recipes for Brunch

Looking for a delicious and impressive dish to impress your guests at brunch? Look no further than sweet and savory Dutch baby pancakes! These puffy, oven-baked pancakes are incredibly versatile and can be customized to suit any taste. In this blog post, we’ll explore two mouthwatering recipes – one sweet and one savory – that are sure to elevate your brunch game. Sweet Dutch Baby Pancake Ingredients: 1 cup all-purpose flour 3 large eggs 1 cup milk 2 tablespoons granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 teaspoon salt 2 tablespoons unsalted butter Powdered sugar, for dusting Fresh berries, for topping Maple syrup, for drizzling Instructions: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a blender or mixing bowl, combine the flour, eggs, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and salt. Blend or whisk until smooth and well combined. Place a 10-inch cast-iron skillet or oven-safe frying pan in the preheated oven for about 5 minutes to heat it up. Carefully remove the hot skillet from the oven and add the butter, swirling it around to coat the bottom and sides. Pour the pancake batter into the skillet and return it to the oven. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pancake is puffed up and golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. The pancake will deflate slightly as it cools. Dust the pancake with powdered sugar and top with fresh berries. Drizzle with maple syrup and serve immediately. Savory Dutch Baby Pancake Ingredients: 1 cup all-purpose flour 3 large eggs 1 cup milk 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme 1/4 teaspoon dried rosemary 2 tablespoons unsalted butter 4 slices bacon, cooked and crumbled 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese Chopped fresh herbs, for garnish (optional) Instructions: Follow steps 1-5 from the sweet Dutch baby pancake recipe. In a small bowl, whisk together the salt, black pepper, garlic powder, dried thyme, and dried rosemary. Sprinkle the herb mixture evenly over the pancake batter in the skillet. Sprinkle the crumbled bacon and shredded cheddar cheese over the top. Return the skillet to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the pancake is puffed up and golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs, if desired. Slice into wedges and serve warm. Conclusion Sweet or savory, these Dutch baby pancake recipes are a fantastic addition to any brunch menu. Their unique presentation and delicious flavors are sure to impress your guests. Whether you opt for the sweet version with powdered sugar and fresh berries or the savory variation with bacon and cheese, these pancakes are guaranteed to be a hit. Deliciously Creamy Breakfast Polenta Recipes

Polenta is a versatile and delicious dish that is often enjoyed as a savory side or main course. But have you ever thought about using polenta in your breakfast recipes? It may sound unconventional, but trust me, once you try these deliciously creamy breakfast polenta recipes, you’ll be hooked! Creamy Blueberry Polenta Bowl Ingredients: 1 cup of polenta 2 cups of water 2 cups of milk (dairy or plant-based) 1 tablespoon of honey 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract 1 cup of fresh blueberries 2 tablespoons of sliced almonds Instructions: In a medium saucepan, bring the water and milk to a boil. Gradually whisk in the polenta, honey, and vanilla extract. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the polenta is thick and creamy. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Divide the creamy polenta into bowls and top each with fresh blueberries and sliced almonds. Serve warm and enjoy! Savory Mushroom and Spinach Polenta Skillet Ingredients: 1 cup of polenta 2 cups of vegetable broth 1 cup of milk (dairy or plant-based) 1 tablespoon of olive oil 1 small onion, diced 2 cloves of garlic, minced 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced 2 cups of fresh spinach Salt and pepper to taste Grated Parmesan cheese (optional) Instructions: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and golden brown. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they release their moisture and become tender. In a separate saucepan, bring the vegetable broth and milk to a boil. Gradually whisk in the polenta and reduce the heat to low. Cook the polenta, stirring constantly, until it thickens and becomes creamy. Add the sautéed mushrooms and fresh spinach to the skillet, and cook until the spinach wilts. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the creamy polenta mixture in bowls, and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Creamy Banana and Cinnamon Polenta Porridge Ingredients: 1 cup of polenta 2 cups of water 2 cups of milk (dairy or plant-based) 2 ripe bananas, mashed 2 tablespoons of honey 1 teaspoon of cinnamon Chopped walnuts for garnish Instructions: In a medium saucepan, bring the water and milk to a boil. Gradually whisk in the polenta, mashed bananas, honey, and cinnamon. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the polenta is thick and creamy. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Divide the creamy polenta porridge into bowls and garnish with chopped walnuts. Serve warm and enjoy the comforting flavors of banana and cinnamon. These deliciously creamy breakfast polenta recipes are a delightful twist on traditional breakfast dishes. Whether you prefer sweet or savory flavors, there’s a polenta recipe here to suit your taste buds. Give them a try and start your day with a creamy and satisfying breakfast that will keep you energized until lunchtime. Enjoy! ...
