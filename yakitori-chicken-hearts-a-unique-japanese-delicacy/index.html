Yakitori Chicken Hearts: A Unique Japanese Delicacy


Yakitori, a popular Japanese dish, is known for its skewered and grilled meats. While chicken thighs and breasts are commonly used, there is one ingredient that stands out as a unique delicacy - chicken hearts. Yes, you heard it right! Chicken hearts are not only delicious but also packed with flavor. In this blog post, we will explore the art of preparing Yakitori Chicken Hearts and how you can recreate this authentic Japanese dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

To make Yakitori Chicken Hearts, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500 grams of chicken hearts
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

Instructions

  1. Start by cleaning the chicken hearts thoroughly. Remove any excess fat or connective tissue and rinse them under cold water. Pat them dry using a paper towel.

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger. This marinade will add a burst of flavor to the chicken hearts.

  3. Place the cleaned chicken hearts in a Ziploc bag or a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Make sure all the hearts are coated evenly. Close the bag or cover the dish and let them marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. For best results, leave them overnight to allow the flavors to infuse.

  4. While the chicken hearts are marinating, prepare your grill. If you don’t have access to a grill, you can use a stovetop grill pan or even an oven broiler.

  5. Once the hearts have marinated, remove them from the bag or dish and thread them onto the soaked bamboo skewers. Skewering them will make them easier to handle on the grill.

  6. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a grill pan or oven broiler, preheat them accordingly. Make sure the surface is hot before placing the skewered chicken hearts on it.

  7. Grill the chicken hearts for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Be careful not to overcook them as they can become tough.

  8. Once cooked, remove the skewers from the grill and let the chicken hearts rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute, resulting in tender and juicy morsels.

  9. Serve the Yakitori Chicken Hearts hot, either as an appetizer or as part of a main course. They pair wonderfully with a side of steamed rice and a fresh salad.

Conclusion

Yakitori Chicken Hearts may sound unusual, but they are truly a unique Japanese delicacy that shouldn’t be overlooked. The combination of the tender chicken hearts and the flavorful marinade creates a dish that is both satisfying and adventurous. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, embrace the culinary delights of Japan, and enjoy the bold flavors of Yakitori Chicken Hearts.
