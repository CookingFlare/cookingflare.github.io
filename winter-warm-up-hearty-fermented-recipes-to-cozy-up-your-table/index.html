Winter Warm-Up: Hearty Fermented Recipes to Cozy Up Your Table


When the temperatures drop, there’s nothing like curling up with a warm meal, accompanied by the inviting aromas of hearty fermented dishes. This winter, consider incorporating more probiotics into your diet with these delightful recipes, perfect for bringing loved ones together at your cozy dining table.

1. Fermented Vegetable Medley

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cups white cabbage, shredded
  • 1 cup daikon radish, sliced
  • 1 cup beet slices
  • 2 teaspoons sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine sliced vegetables, mixing them evenly.
  2. Sprinkle sea salt and black pepper over the vegetables, ensuring an even distribution.
  3. Let the vegetables sit for 30 minutes so that the salt can draw out excess water.
  4. Rinse the vegetables gently under cold water, ensuring that no excess salt remains.
  5. Dissolve 2 tablespoons of sea salt in 1 quart of water to create an immersion brine.
  6. In a non-reactive vessel, layer the vegetables with garlic cloves, then pour the brine over the vegetables.
  7. Seal your container and place it in a cool, dark spot for 3-7 days, depending on your preferred level of fermentation.
  8. Every day, release any built-up gases by opening your container and pressing down on the vegetables.
  9. After 7 days, transfer the fermented vegetables to the refrigerator to slow the fermentation process and maintain flavor for 2-3 weeks.

2. Classic Kimchi

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized head of napa cabbage
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 cups of scallions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of glutinous rice flour
  • 1 cup of fish sauce
  • 1 cup of water
  • 6 tablespoons of light brown sugar or honey
  • 1 teaspoon of roasted sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of Korean (Gochugaru) chili flakes
  • 1 teaspoon of coarse sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Rinse and gently dry the napa cabbage, then slice it thickly.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix minced garlic, ginger, scallions, glutinous rice flour, fish sauce, water, brown sugar, roasted sesame seeds, chili flakes, and sea salt.
  3. Toss the sliced cabbage in the mixture, ensuring even distribution.
  4. Press the mixture into a clean, airtight container, packing it down tightly.
  5. Allow the kimchi to rest at room temperature for 1-2 days until the fermentation process starts.
  6. Once the fermentation begins, transfer the kimchi to a cooler location and release built-up gases each day by opening the container and pressing down on the cabbage.
  7. Taste your kimchi frequently, and once it reaches your desired level of flavor and fermentation, transfer it to the refrigerator to slow the fermentation process further.

3. Multigrain Sourdough

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of active sourdough starter
  • 1 ½ cups of lukewarm water
  • 2 cups of a mixture of whole grain flours – rye, spelt, emmer, einkorn
  • ½ cup of bread flour
  • 1 tablespoon of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine sourdough starter and lukewarm water, mixing well.
  2. Gradually incorporate the whole grain flours, bread flour, and sea salt, stirring gently to avoid overmixing.
  3. Once the dough is well-combined, allow it to rest for 5 minutes before kneading.
  4. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Form the dough into a round shape and place it in a floured b unsigned container.
  6. Leave the dough to ferment for about 4-6 hours in a cool spot, allowing the sourdough to develop.
  7. Once the dough has risen, preheat the oven to 450°F (232°C) and prepare to bake.
  8. Enjoy your freshly baked multigrain sourdough with some cultured butter or a drizzle of olive oil.

Reap the benefits of seasonal warmth with these delicious fermented recipes, tailored to indulge your senses and nourish your body. Share your passion for fermenting with friends and family by inviting them to participate in the culinary experience of creating these probiotic-rich dishes. Happy cooking!
