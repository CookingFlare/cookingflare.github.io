Greek Stuffed Grape Leaves Recipe with Rice and Herbs


When it comes to Greek cuisine, one dish that stands out is the flavorful and aromatic Greek Stuffed Grape Leaves. Known as Dolmades, these small parcels are made with tender grape leaves that are stuffed with a delicious filling of rice and herbs. They are not only a delight to the taste buds but also a feast for the eyes with their vibrant green color. In this recipe, we will take you through the process of making these delectable Greek Stuffed Grape Leaves step by step.

Ingredients

  • 1 jar of grape leaves (approximately 60 leaves)
  • 1 cup of long-grain rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Firstly, prepare the grape leaves by rinsing them under cold water to remove any brine or excess salt. Then, place them in a large bowl filled with warm water and let them soak for about 15 minutes. This will soften the leaves, making them easier to work with.

  2. While the grape leaves are soaking, prepare the filling. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the rice to the saucepan and stir it well to coat it with the oil. Cook the rice for a few minutes, stirring occasionally, until it becomes slightly toasted. This will give the dish a nutty flavor.

  4. Next, add the chopped dill, mint, and parsley to the saucepan. These fresh herbs will infuse the filling with their delightful aroma and taste. Stir everything together to combine the flavors.

  5. Pour in enough water to cover the rice and herbs completely. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid.

  6. While the filling is cooking, prepare the grape leaves for stuffing. Drain the soaked leaves and pat them dry using a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.

  7. Take one grape leaf and place it shiny side down on a clean surface. Trim off the stem if necessary. Spoon a small amount of the rice filling onto the center of the leaf, near the stem end. Fold the sides of the leaf over the filling, then roll it up tightly, tucking in the sides as you go. Repeat this process with the remaining grape leaves and filling.

  8. Once all the grape leaves are stuffed, arrange them in a large saucepan or Dutch oven, placing them snugly together to prevent unravelling during cooking. Drizzle the lemon juice and a bit of olive oil over the stuffed grape leaves.

  9. Pour enough water into the saucepan to cover the grape leaves. Place a heatproof plate over the stuffed grape leaves to keep them submerged in the liquid. This will help them cook evenly.

  10. Cover the saucepan with a lid and bring it to a gentle boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat to low and let the stuffed grape leaves simmer for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the leaves are tender and the flavors have melded together.

  11. Once cooked, remove the stuffed grape leaves from the saucepan and let them cool slightly before serving. They can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Stuffed Grape Leaves can be served as an appetizer or a main dish. They are often enjoyed with a dollop of creamy tzatziki sauce or a drizzle of lemon-infused olive oil. They pair beautifully with a fresh Greek salad and some crusty bread.

Conclusion

The Greek Stuffed Grape Leaves Recipe with Rice and Herbs is a classic dish that showcases the rich flavors and culinary traditions of Greece. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to expand your culinary repertoire, these delightful parcels are sure to impress. So, why not give them a try and transport yourself to the sunny shores of Greece with every bite?
