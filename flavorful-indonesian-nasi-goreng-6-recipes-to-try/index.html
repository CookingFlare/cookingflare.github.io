Flavorful Indonesian Nasi Goreng: 6 Recipes to Try


Indonesian Nasi Goreng, translating to ‘fried rice,’ is a beloved dish that holds a special place in the hearts of many food enthusiasts around the world. Known for its vibrant flavors and fragrant aromas, this dish is a staple in Indonesian cuisine and is often enjoyed as a quick meal or a late-night snack. What makes Nasi Goreng so appealing is its versatility; it can be customized with various proteins, vegetables, and spices, allowing each cook to put their unique twist on it. In this post, we will explore six delectable Nasi Goreng recipes that are sure to tantalize your taste buds and bring the essence of Indonesia into your kitchen.

1. Classic Nasi Goreng

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 2 red chilies, sliced (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, and corn)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Crispy fried shallots (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Rice: If you’re using freshly cooked rice, spread it out on a tray and let it cool to prevent mushiness. Day-old rice works best as it’s firmer and less sticky.

  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or frying pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped shallots, stirring until fragrant and slightly golden.

  3. Add Chilies: Incorporate the sliced chilies to the mix, allowing their heat to infuse into the oil.

  4. Scramble the Eggs: Push the aromatics to the side of the pan, crack the eggs into the cleared space, and scramble until just set.

  5. Stir in Rice: Add the cooled rice, breaking apart any clumps. Stir to combine with the eggs and aromatics, ensuring an even distribution.

  6. Season: Pour in the sweet soy sauce and regular soy sauce. Toss the rice until it’s evenly coated, then add the mixed vegetables. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Serve: Remove from heat, garnish with chopped green onions and crispy fried shallots, and enjoy your classic Nasi Goreng!

2. Nasi Goreng Kampung (Village Fried Rice)

Ingredients

  • 3 cups day-old rice
  • 3 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste (terasi), toasted
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup bok choy, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (sambal oelek)
  • 2 eggs
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Heat Oil: In a large wok, heat coconut oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.

  2. Incorporate Shrimp Paste: Stir in the toasted shrimp paste and fry for about a minute until it becomes aromatic.

  3. Add Chicken and Bok Choy: Introduce the shredded chicken and chopped bok choy, cooking until the bok choy wilts.

  4. Mix in Rice: Add the day-old rice to the pan, breaking apart any clumps. Stir until everything is well combined.

  5. Flavor with Chili Sauce: Drizzle the chili sauce over the rice, mixing thoroughly to ensure an even coating.

  6. Scramble Eggs: Push the rice mixture to one side of the pan and scramble the eggs on the other side until cooked. Fold them into the rice.

  7. Serve: Adjust seasoning with salt if necessary. Serve hot with fresh cucumber and tomato slices on the side for a refreshing crunch.

3. Seafood Nasi Goreng

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked jasmine rice
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g squid, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce
  • 2 eggs
  • Green peas (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Heat Sesame Oil: In a wok, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.

  2. Cook Seafood: Add shrimp and squid to the wok. Stir-fry until the shrimp turns pink and the squid is cooked through.

  3. Add Rice and Sauces: Incorporate the cooked rice, oyster sauce, soy sauce, and chili sauce. Toss everything together until the rice is heated through.

  4. Scramble Eggs: Push the rice to one side and scramble the eggs on the other side of the pan. Fold the scrambled eggs into the rice.

  5. Serve: Garnish with green peas and serve with lime wedges for a zesty finish.

4. Vegetarian Nasi Goreng

Ingredients

  • 3 cups day-old rice
  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce
  • 2 eggs (omit for vegan)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Sauté Vegetables: Heat peanut oil in a wok over medium heat. Add garlic and onion, sautéing until soft. Add carrot, bell pepper, and green beans, cooking until tender.

  2. Incorporate Rice: Add the day-old rice, breaking up any clumps. Stir well to combine with the vegetables.

  3. Season: Drizzle soy sauce and sweet soy sauce over the rice. Stir to evenly coat. Season with salt and pepper.

  4. Scramble Eggs: If using, push the rice mixture to one side and scramble the eggs on the other side. Fold them into the rice when cooked.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot as a delightful vegetarian dish.

5. Nasi Goreng Ayam (Chicken Fried Rice)

Ingredients

  • 3 cups day-old rice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 300g chicken breast, diced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sweet soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas)
  • 2 eggs
  • Cucumber and tomato slices (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook Chicken: In a wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add garlic and onion, sauté until soft, then add the diced chicken. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink.

  2. Add Rice: Stir in the cooked rice, breaking apart any clumps. Mix well with the chicken and aromatics.

  3. Flavoring: Add soy sauce and sweet soy sauce, tossing to ensure the rice is evenly coated. Stir in mixed vegetables.

  4. Scramble Eggs: Push the rice aside and scramble the eggs in the pan until cooked. Mix them into the rice.

  5. Serve: Plate the Nasi Goreng Ayam and garnish with cucumber and tomato slices.

6. Spicy Nasi Goreng

Ingredients

  • 3 cups day-old rice
  • 3 tablespoons chili oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 200g beef, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons sambal oelek (chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 eggs
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat Chili Oil: In a wok, heat chili oil over medium heat. Sauté minced garlic and onion until fragrant.

  2. Add Beef: Introduce the sliced beef and cook until browned.

  3. Incorporate Rice and Sauces: Add the day-old rice, sambal oelek, and soy sauce. Toss everything together until well combined.

  4. Scramble Eggs: Push the rice to one side and scramble the eggs on the other side until cooked. Fold them into the rice.

  5. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve hot for a fiery kick.

Nasi Goreng is more than just a dish; it’s a canvas for culinary creativity, allowing you to experiment with various ingredients and flavors. Each recipe captures the essence of Indonesia while providing a delightful experience for your palate. Whether you prefer the classic version or a spicy twist, Nasi Goreng is a beloved meal that you can enjoy any time of the day. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a flavorful journey through the vibrant world of Indonesian cuisine!
