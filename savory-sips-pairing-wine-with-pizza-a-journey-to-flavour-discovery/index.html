Savory Sips: Pairing Wine with Pizza: A Journey to Flavour Discovery


Picture this: the smell of freshly baked crust, the sight of perfectly melted cheese, and the delightful crunch of an expertly tossed pizza crust. Now imagine enhancing that experience with the perfect glass of wine, carefully selected to complement the flavors and elevate your culinary journey to new heights. This is what savory sips and pizza pairings are all about. So grab your trusty pizza peel, preheat that oven, and let’s dive into a world of flavor discovery.

The Basic Foundations of Flavors

Before we start pairing, let’s brush up on some basic pizza terminology and foundations of flavors:

  1. Dough: Made primarily from flour, water, yeast, and salt, the dough can vary in texture and density from recipe to recipe. Interestingly enough, the ancient Greeks were one of the first civilizations to take note of the versatility of pizza when they came up with flatbread baked with toppings centuries ago!

  2. Sauce: Traditionally tomato-based, the tomato sauce is the foundation that unites the ingredients of any pizza variety. However, some recipes may use a white or olive oil base, opening up a wide range of pairing possibilities.

  3. Cheese: The cheese on your pizza is key when it comes to balancing flavors. Classic cheese options include mozzarella (and its variations like fior di latte), parmesan, and cheddar while adventurous chefs may opt to incorporate blue cheeses or even goat cheese.

  4. Toppings: Toppings range from savory meats to fresh vegetables and gourmet additions such as truffle oil. Each topping on a pizza plays a crucial role in determining which wine it will best pair with.

The Zipcode Theory of Wine Pairing

When it comes to what wines pair well with your pizza, the rules are somewhat flexible! Here’s a useful guideline known as the “zipcode theory.” In this fascinating food and wine pairing theory, the wine’s zipcode is used to give an approximate idea of the vintage of the wine. The concept being, that wines made from the same region and same year (i.e., with the same zipcode) will often have comparable flavor profiles, with subtle differences tied to weather variations. It is always crucial to keep this in mind when you’re pairing a savory pizza with a glass of wine!

With that said, let’s explore some irresistible pizza-wine pairing combinations for your next culinary adventure:

  1. Margherita Pizza & Pinot Noir: As the most classic pizza option, a Margherita with its trinity of ingredients - tomato, mozzarella, and basil - is the perfect match for a delicious Pinot Noir. The slight earthiness of the wine complements the freshness of the basil and the tanginess of the tomato sauce.

  2. Pepperoni & Cheddar Pizza & Tempranillo: This bold pairing is bound to impress. A spicy pepperoni pizza with a cheddar crust is best paired with a robust Tempranillo from Spain. The wine’s fruit flavors and gentle tannins will stand up to the spiciness of the pepperoni while the oak aging harmoniously complements the cheddar.

  3. Prosciutto & Fig Pizza & Sauvignon Blanc: Thinking about an unconventional gourmet pizza? Prosciutto and figwork remarkably well together on a pizza, and pair perfectly with a fresh, crisp Sauvignon Blanc. The wine’s acidity cuts right through the saltiness of the prosciutto and the sweetness of the figs, delivering a deliciously balanced experience.

  4. BBQ Chicken & Pineapple & viognier: The sweet and tangy combination of barbecue chicken and pineapple slices screams for a wine with similar qualities. Enter the Australian Viognier, a white wine with hints of apricot and peach, along with a good refreshing acidity which balances the sweetness of the pineapple.

  5. Quattro Formaggi & Chardonnay: For those who can’t decide which cheese to choose, there’s always the option to go with Quattro Formaggi, which features a combination of four different cheeses. A full-bodied Chardonnay with hints of butter and vanilla is the perfect complement to cut through the richness of the cheese layers.

Conclusion

Remember, there are no strict rules when it comes to pairing wines with your pizza. Experiment, taste, and have fun while exploring the fantastic world of savory sips and pizza. The journey of discovery may begin with a classic Margherita and a Pinot Noir, but there’s a whole universe of combinations awaiting to be discovered.

Now that you have this guide in hand, it’s time to preheat that oven, roll out the dough, and select that perfect wine to pair with your creation. Cheers to flavor discovery!
