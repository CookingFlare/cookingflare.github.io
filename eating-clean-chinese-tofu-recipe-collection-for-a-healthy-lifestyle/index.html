Eating Clean: Chinese Tofu Recipe Collection for a Healthy Lifestyle


Who said eating clean had to be boring? Transform your plate into an explosion of flavors and textures with this array of mouth-watering Chinese tofu recipes. In this whirlwind tour of culinary delights, you’ll discover how this humble ingredient can be the star of the show, steeped in rich Chinese tradition. No more bland, flavorless meals - it’s time to savor the potential of tofu and invigorate your taste buds with exciting and authentic flavors.

  1. Fragrant Sizzling Tofu with Peanuts

Ingredients:

  • 1 block (14 oz.) of firm tofu
  • 1/4 cup of shelled, roasted peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger, finely chopped
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • A pinch of crushed red pepper flakes
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  • First, press the tofu between two plates for 20-30 minutes to remove excess moisture. Then, cut the tofu into cubes, making sure they are evenly sized.

  • Next, heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once heated, add the tofu cubes, in batches, if necessary, and fry until golden brown on all sides. Be careful not to overcrowd the pan to achieve a crispy texture. Remove the fried tofu and set aside.

  • In the same skillet, add the peanuts and toast until lightly browned. Carefully remove nuts and set aside.

  • In the same skillet, add minced garlic and ginger, stirring until fragrant. Add the green onions and cook briefly.

  • Lower heat to medium-low, then pour in the soy sauce, rice wine vinegar, sugar, and crushed red pepper flakes. Stir to combine.

  • Return the tofu cubes, crispy peanuts, and any accumulated juices to the skillet. Gently toss the ingredients together so that each piece of tofu is well-coated with the sauce. Adjust seasoning with salt and let flavors meld for about a minute. Serve immediately with steamed rice or crusty bread to soak up the delicious sauce.

  1. Scallion Cups filled with Tofu and Mushrooms

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of water

  • 3 tablespoons of active dry yeast

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour

  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil

  • 1/4 cup of chopped scallions

  • 1 block (14 oz.) of firm tofu

  • 1 cup of shiitake mushrooms, finely chopped

  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced

  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil

  • 1 teaspoon of sugar

  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  • Begin by making the scallion cups. In a bowl, combine water and yeast. Let the yeast bloom for about 10 minutes, then add flour and sesame oil, and knead until a smooth dough is formed. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 30-45 minutes.

  • Once the dough has risen, divide it into smaller pieces, roll each piece into balls, and place on a lined or greased baking sheet. Using your thumb, press down in the center of each ball to create a cup shape. Brush each cup with a small amount of sesame oil and let rise for another 15 minutes.

  • Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit (190 degrees Celsius) and bake scallion cups for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown. Set aside and let cool.

  • For the filling, press the tofu to remove excess moisture, then crumble into smaller pieces. In a large skillet, saut√© the tofu with the minced garlic, and chopped shiitakes over medium heat until tofu starts to brown and mushrooms release their moisture.

  • Add soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar, then cook for a few more minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  • Once both the scallion cups and filling have cooled completely, fill each cup with the tofu and mushroom mixture. Finish with a sprinkle of chopped scallions for added flavor and texture.

  1. Spicy Tofu and Cabbage Stir-fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 block (14 oz.) of firm tofu
  • 1 head of napa cabbage
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, finely grated
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  • Begin this dish by pressing the tofu to remove excess moisture, and then cut into cubes. Heat a large skillet over medium heat, and fry cubes in batches until golden brown on all sides. Carefully remove tofu and set aside.

  • In the same skillet, add tempered red pepper flakes, minced garlic, and grated ginger, stirring until aromatic. Slice the napa cabbage, and add it to the skillet, cooking until slightly softened.

  • Return the fried tofu cubes to the skillet, followed by soy sauce, rice vinegar, vegetable broth, and salt. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until cabbage is tender and flavors are well combined. Taste and adjust seasoning as necessary.

Conclusion

Now that you’ve discovered these tantalizing tofu recipes, your palate is ready to embark on a flavorful journey. Refined yet rustic, these Chinese dishes will impress friends and family alike. So, say goodbye to bland meals and embrace the joy of eating clean - the Chinese tofu way.
