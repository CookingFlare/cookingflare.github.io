Easy Apple Turnovers: A Flaky and Delicious Dessert


Looking for a delightful dessert that’s easy to make and sure to impress? Look no further than these easy apple turnovers. With their flaky, buttery crust and sweet, cinnamon-spiced apple filling, these turnovers are the perfect treat for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a homemade dessert, this recipe will become a fast favorite in your kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 4 large apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • Additional granulated sugar for sprinkling

Instructions

Making the Pastry:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the chilled butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Slowly add the ice water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the diced apples, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the apples are tender, about 8-10 minutes.

  2. Sprinkle the flour over the apple mixture and stir until the juices thicken. Remove from heat and let the filling cool to room temperature.

Assembling the Turnovers:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry dough to about 1/8 inch thickness. Using a sharp knife or a pizza cutter, cut the dough into 6-inch squares.

  3. Spoon a generous amount of the apple filling onto one half of each square, leaving a border around the edges. Fold the other half of the pastry over the filling to form a triangle. Use a fork to crimp the edges and seal the turnovers.

  4. Place the turnovers on the prepared baking sheet. In a small bowl, mix the beaten egg with water. Brush the tops of the turnovers with the egg wash and sprinkle with granulated sugar.

  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the turnovers are golden brown and flaky. Allow them to cool slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

These easy apple turnovers are delicious on their own, but you can take them to the next level by serving them warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. They also pair beautifully with a hot cup of coffee or a glass of cold milk.

Final Thoughts

With their golden, flaky crust and warm, gooey apple filling, these easy apple turnovers are a true delight to the senses. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your collection. So go ahead, give it a try, and treat yourself to a taste of homemade goodness!
