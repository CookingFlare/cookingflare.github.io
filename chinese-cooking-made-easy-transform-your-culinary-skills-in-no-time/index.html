Chinese Cooking Made Easy: Transform Your Culinary Skills in No Time


Chinese cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and delicate balance of taste. In this article, I’ll introduce you to a range of simple yet delicious Chinese recipes that will have you transforming your culinary skills in no time! Let’s dive right in and explore the wonderfully diverse world of Chinese cuisine.

Feng Zhou Tang (Honey-Glazed Pork Stir-Fry)

This mouthwatering pork dish offers a fantastic combination of sweet and savory flavors. Simple and quick to prepare, it’s perfect for beginners looking to master the almighty Chinese stir-fry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pork tenderloin, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • ½ cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine pork with soy sauce, wine, cornstarch, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well, ensuring the pork is evenly coated.
  2. In a smaller bowl, whisk together honey, orange juice, and minced garlic. Set aside.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Add half of the pork and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, until the meat is browned. With a slotted spoon, transfer cooked pork to a separate plate and set aside.
  5. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the wok. Add the rest of the pork and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, until browned. Return the first batch of cooked pork to the wok.
  6. Pour the honey mixture over the pork and toss well to coat. Add red pepper flakes if desired.
  7. Stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes, until the sauce becomes thick and glossy. Serve your Feng Zhou Tang over steamed rice or noodles for a truly satisfying meal.

Gong Bao Chicken (Kung Pao Chicken)

Gong Bao Chicken is a classic Chinese dish known for its bold flavors and crunchy veggies. This street food staple is sure to become a go-to in your Chinese cooking repertoire.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • ¼ cup peanut oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup unsalted roasted peanuts
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound Chinese eggplant or 1 large regular eggplant, cut into 1-inch lengths
  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • ½ cup unsalted cashews
  • ¼ cup spicy bean sauce (look for it in well-stocked grocery stores)
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced, for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine chicken with soy sauce, wine, cornstarch, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well, ensuring the chicken is evenly coated.
  2. Place the egg in a small bowl and beat until well combined. Dredge chicken pieces through the egg, then coat with roasted peanuts, pressing the peanuts onto the chicken to adhere.
  3. In a wok or large skillet, heat 3 tablespoons of peanut oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Add half of the peanut-coated chicken and fry until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove from the wok and set on a paper towel-lined plate to drain. Repeat with the remaining chicken.
  5. Allow wok to return to a high heat, then add the remaining 1 tablespoon of peanut oil. Add garlic, eggplant, and red bell pepper, stirring constantly for 3-4 minutes or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together spicy bean sauce, rice vinegar, and sugar until well combined. Add mixture to the wok and stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken and coat the vegetables.
  7. Return the fried chicken to the wok, along with the roasted cashews. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes, until everything is well mixed and heated through. Season with additional salt and pepper if needed. Serve your Gong Bao Chicken over steamed rice and garnish with thinly sliced green onions.

How to Master Chinese Stir-Fry

Stir-frying is the cornerstone of Chinese cooking, bringing together a zesty mix of flavors, textures, and colors. The secret to mastering this technique is a well-preheated wok and a confident hand to ensure swift cooking. So next time you’re in the kitchen, unlock the delightful world of Chinese cooking and enjoy the nourishing, flavorful meals by applying these essential stir-frying tips:

  • Preheat your wok or skillet over high heat before adding ingredients. The high heat helps the dish cook quickly while maintaining the natural texture and nutrients of the ingredients, and helps create the desired crispy exterior.
  • Have all ingredients prepped and ready to go before you start stir-frying, as the cooking process is rapid and should be done in quick succession.
  • Use a generous amount of oil to prevent food from sticking. Remember, stir-frying is not about fat reduction!
  • Remember to have all the ingredients correctly seasoned before stir-frying, as the cooking times are short, and you won’t have an opportunity to adjust the flavors further in the cooking process.
  • Keep the wok moving! Combination woks, made of both flat and rounded surfaces, allow you to add ingredients directly to the fire, providing even heat distribution. Whether you’re using a traditional wok or a standard skillet, never leave ingredients in one spot for too long; constant motion is the name of the game.

By mastering stir-frying techniques, it’s easier to incorporate Chinese cooking into your repertoire. Along the way, you’ll craft a unique culinary identity, infusing classic dishes with your personal flair and taste buds. Start engaging with these delightful recipes, and soon you’ll be creating an authentic Chinese culinary experience right in your very own kitchen!

Remember, the melting pot that is Chinese cuisine offers limitless possibilities. Now it’s time for you to explore and find your own culinary passions, one delicious bite at a time. Happy cooking!
