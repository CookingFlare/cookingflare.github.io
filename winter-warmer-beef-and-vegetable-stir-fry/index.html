Winter Warmer Beef and Vegetable Stir-Fry


Winter is the perfect time to indulge in hearty and comforting meals, and what better way to warm up than with a delicious beef and vegetable stir-fry? Packed with nutritious ingredients and bursting with flavor, this dish is sure to satisfy your cravings and keep you cozy throughout the cold season.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef steak, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: chili flakes, for added heat

Instructions

  1. In a large wok or skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté for about a minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the sliced beef to the wok and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the wok and set aside.

  3. In the same wok, add the sliced onion, julienned carrots, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  4. Next, add the sliced zucchini, mushrooms, broccoli florets, and snow peas to the wok. Continue to stir-fry for another 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Pour the sauce over the vegetables in the wok and stir to coat evenly.

  6. Return the cooked beef to the wok and mix well with the vegetables and sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Taste the stir-fry and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you like it spicy, sprinkle some chili flakes on top for an extra kick.

  8. Serve the winter warmer beef and vegetable stir-fry over steamed rice or noodles. Garnish with some fresh cilantro or green onions for a pop of color and added freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a healthier twist, you can use lean cuts of beef or substitute it with chicken or tofu for a vegetarian option.
  • Feel free to experiment with other vegetables based on your preference or what you have on hand. Some great additions include snap peas, baby corn, or even thinly sliced cabbage.
  • If you like your stir-fry to have a bit of crunch, you can blanch the broccoli florets and snow peas in boiling water for a minute before adding them to the wok.
  • To make the dish gluten-free, use tamari sauce instead of soy sauce.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in a skillet or microwave before enjoying.

So there you have it, a delicious and satisfying winter warmer beef and vegetable stir-fry recipe to add to your repertoire. This hearty dish is not only packed with flavor but also provides a healthy dose of nutrients to keep you energized during the colder months. Give it a try and let this comforting stir-fry warm you up from the inside out!
