Rustic Valentine's Day Dinner: Coq au Vin Recipe for Two


Valentine’s Day is the perfect occasion to create a special and memorable meal for your loved one. If you’re looking to impress your partner with a rustic and flavorful dish, look no further than Coq au Vin. This classic French dish combines tender chicken, rich red wine, and aromatic herbs to create a truly indulgent dining experience. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make Coq au Vin for two, ensuring that you and your significant other have a romantic and delicious Valentine’s Day dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken leg quarters
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 8 ounces button mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 shallots, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Season the chicken leg quarters generously with salt and pepper on both sides. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 8-10 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  2. In the same skillet, add the chopped bacon and cook until crispy. Once the bacon is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.

  3. Add the sliced mushrooms, chopped shallots, and minced garlic to the skillet. Sauté until the mushrooms are golden brown and the shallots are translucent, about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Return the chicken leg quarters to the skillet, along with any juices that have accumulated. Pour in the red wine and chicken broth, ensuring that the chicken is mostly submerged. Add the fresh thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf to the skillet.

  5. Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the skillet with a lid. Simmer for 45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.

  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set aside. Increase the heat to medium-high and simmer the liquid until it has reduced by half, about 10-15 minutes.

  7. Remove the thyme sprigs, rosemary sprigs, and bay leaf from the skillet. Stir in the butter until melted and well combined, creating a velvety sauce.

  8. To serve, place the chicken leg quarters on a plate and spoon the sauce over the top. Sprinkle with the crispy bacon and chopped fresh parsley for garnish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a heartier meal, serve Coq au Vin with mashed potatoes, crusty bread, or buttered noodles.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water and add it to the skillet during the last few minutes of simmering.
  • Feel free to customize the recipe by adding your favorite vegetables, such as carrots or pearl onions, to the skillet along with the mushrooms and shallots.
  • Coq au Vin can be made ahead of time and reheated before serving, making it a convenient option for a romantic dinner.

Conclusion

Coq au Vin is a classic dish that is sure to impress your loved one on Valentine’s Day. With its rich flavors and tender chicken, this rustic French recipe embodies the essence of romance and indulgence. By following this recipe, you can create a memorable and delicious meal for two, ensuring that your Valentine’s Day dinner is truly special. So light some candles, pour a glass of red wine, and enjoy the cozy and intimate atmosphere as you savor every bite of this exquisite Coq au Vin. Happy Valentine’s Day!
