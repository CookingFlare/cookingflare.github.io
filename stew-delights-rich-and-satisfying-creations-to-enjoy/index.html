Stew Delights: Rich and Satisfying Creations to Enjoy


When it comes to comfort food, stews rank high on the list. These rich and satisfying creations are perfect for chilly days, bringing warmth to both the body and soul. Stews can be made with an array of ingredients, such as meats, vegetables, and immune-boosting broths. Let’s dive into some delightful stew recipes that can be prepared in no time!

Classic Beef Stew

A classic beef stew is a favorite for a reason—it’s simple to make and delivers mouth-watering flavors. Gather the following:

  • 2 lbs. beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp. kosher salt
  • 1 tsp. ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 2 large carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 tbsp. tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves

Method:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the beef cubes, flour, salt, and pepper. Coat well.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot on medium-high heat. Add half of the beef and cook until browned, about 3 minutes per side. Remove and set aside. Repeat with the other half of the beef.
  3. Add carrots, celery, onion, and garlic to the pot and cook until soft, around 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the browned beef, beef broth, red wine, tomato paste, and bay leaves. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat, cover the pot, and let simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  6. Serve alongside crusty bread for a cozy meal!

Vegetable Stew

For a delicious plant-based option, this vegetable stew is filling and nourishing, perfect for vegan, vegetarian, and omnivores alike:

  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 1 cup chopped potatoes
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped green beans
  • 1 cup chopped green cabbage
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp. thyme leaves
  • 1 tsp. rosemary leaves
  • Salt and ground black pepper

Method:

  1. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, cooking until softened.
  2. Add potatoes, carrots, green beans, cabbage, vegetable broth, thyme, and rosemary. Stir well.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer for 30 to 40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Serve as is or with a side of crusty bread for an extra treat.

Slow-Cooker Lamb Stew

For a special occasion or a lazy Sunday afternoon, prepare this lamb stew that cooks itself while you relax:

  • 2 lbs. lamb shoulder, cut into 2-inch chunks
  • 3 cups lamb broth
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 tbsp. tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp. flour
  • 1 tsp. Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp. redcurrant jelly
  • Salt and ground black pepper
  • Parsley, chopped (optional)

Method:

  1. In a slow cooker, combine lamb chunks, lamb broth, onion, carrots, celery, and tomato paste.
  2. Season with salt, pepper, and Worcestershire sauce.
  3. Cover and cook on low heat for 6 to 8 hours, until the meat is tender and pulls apart easily.
  4. Stir in the flour and redcurrant jelly. Cook for an additional 10 minutes.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Serve garnished with parsley, if desired.

Prep a stew this weekend, and you’ll have a tantalizing meal that’s hearty, satisfying, and bursting with flavor. Enjoy!
