Zesty Thai Mango Sticky Rice: A Sweet Thai Dessert


In the heart of Southeast Asia, Thailand is renowned for its vibrant street food scene, aromatic spices, and delectable desserts. One of the most beloved Thai desserts is Mango Sticky Rice, a sweet and refreshing treat that is both easy to make and utterly delicious. In this recipe, we’ll introduce you to a zesty twist on this classic dessert, incorporating the sweetness of mango and the creaminess of coconut milk.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of Thai glutinous rice (also known as “sweet rice”)
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of coconut milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of pandan extract
  • 2 ripe mangos, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of toasted sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • 1/4 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cardamom

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sticky Rice: Rinse the glutinous rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 4 hours or overnight. Drain the rice and cook it in a medium saucepan with 2 cups of water, sugar, and salt. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the saucepan with a tight-fitting lid. Simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  2. Prepare the Coconut Milk Sauce: In a small saucepan, combine the coconut milk, pandan extract, and a pinch of salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and reduces slightly. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. Assemble the Dessert: In a large serving dish, arrange the cooked sticky rice in a layer. Drizzle the coconut milk sauce over the rice, followed by a layer of sliced mango. Repeat this process until all the ingredients have been used, ending with a layer of sticky rice on top.
  4. Add the Toppings: Sprinkle the toasted sesame seeds and chopped fresh mint leaves over the top of the dessert. Drizzle the melted butter, honey, grated ginger, ground cinnamon, and ground cardamom over the top.
  5. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the Zesty Thai Mango Sticky Rice warm or at room temperature. This dessert is best consumed within a few hours of preparation, as the sticky rice tends to dry out over time.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added burst of flavor, substitute the pandan extract with a few drops of rosewater or orange blossom water.
  • If you prefer a creamier dessert, add an additional 1/4 cup of coconut milk to the sauce.
  • Substitute the mango with other sweet fruits, such as pineapple or banana, for a unique twist on the dessert.
  • For a more intense flavor, add a pinch of salt to the sticky rice while it’s cooking.

Conclusion

Zesty Thai Mango Sticky Rice is a sweet and refreshing dessert that is perfect for warm weather or special occasions. With its creamy coconut milk sauce, sweet sticky rice, and ripe mango, this dessert is sure to delight even the most discerning palates. Whether you’re a fan of Thai cuisine or just looking to try something new, this recipe is a must-try. So go ahead, get creative, and indulge in the sweet and zesty flavors of Thailand!
