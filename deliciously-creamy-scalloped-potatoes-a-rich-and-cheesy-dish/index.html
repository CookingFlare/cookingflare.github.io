Deliciously Creamy Scalloped Potatoes: A Rich and Cheesy Dish


Scalloped potatoes are a classic comfort food that never fails to impress. With their creamy texture and cheesy goodness, they make the perfect side dish for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving some indulgence, these deliciously creamy scalloped potatoes are sure to satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of Yukon gold potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 cups of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the heavy cream, milk, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring occasionally.

  3. Once the mixture is simmering, remove it from the heat and set it aside.

  4. Arrange a layer of thinly sliced potatoes in the greased baking dish, slightly overlapping them. Pour a portion of the cream mixture over the potatoes, making sure to cover them evenly.

  5. Sprinkle a layer of shredded cheddar cheese over the potatoes and cream mixture. Repeat this process, layering the potatoes, cream mixture, and cheese until all the ingredients are used. Make sure to finish with a layer of cheese on top.

  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes.

  7. After 45 minutes, remove the foil and sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the top layer of cheese. Return the dish to the oven and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the cheese is golden and bubbly.

  8. Once cooked, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the dish to set slightly.

  9. Serve the deliciously creamy scalloped potatoes as a side dish alongside your favorite main course. They pair well with roasted chicken, grilled steak, or even a simple salad for a vegetarian option.

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add thinly sliced onions or cooked bacon between the layers of potatoes for extra flavor.
  • If you prefer a lighter version, you can substitute part of the heavy cream with chicken or vegetable broth.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of cheese for a unique flavor profile. Gruyere, fontina, or even blue cheese can add a delicious twist to this dish.
  • To make ahead, assemble the scalloped potatoes but don’t bake them. Cover the dish tightly with foil and refrigerate for up to 24 hours. When ready to serve, simply bake as directed, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time if needed.

Enjoy the creamy, cheesy goodness of these scalloped potatoes. They are sure to become a favorite in your household and leave your guests asking for seconds. Happy cooking!
