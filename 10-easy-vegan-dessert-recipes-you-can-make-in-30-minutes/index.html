10 Easy Vegan Dessert Recipes You Can Make in 30 Minutes


Desserts are a joy to make, especially when they’re effortless and don’t require hours of preparation. Whether you’re fully plant-based or simply trying to incorporate more vegan-friendly recipes into your life, these 10 easy vegan dessert recipes will have you in and out of the kitchen in under 30 minutes. Each recipe guarantees maximum flavor with minimal effort – perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth in no time. Let’s dive into the kitchen and start whisking!

1. No-Bake Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Rich, creamy, and decadent, this chocolate avocado mousse is a showstopper that takes just minutes to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 4 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Scoop out the flesh of the avocados and place it in a food processor.
  2. Add the cocoa powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and sea salt.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  4. Transfer to serving bowls and refrigerate for 10 minutes to chill slightly.
  5. Garnish with fresh berries, coconut flakes, or chopped nuts before serving.

2. Vegan Coconut Macaroons

These bite-sized treats are naturally sweetened, coconutty, and perfect for a quick dessert fix.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shredded unsweetened coconut
  • 1/3 cup almond flour
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine shredded coconut, almond flour, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Mix until evenly combined.
  3. Form small balls with the mixture and place them on the baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 10-12 minutes until golden brown.
  5. Let cool before devouring.

3. Quick Peanut Butter Energy Bites

This is the ultimate guilt-free dessert that delivers bursts of flavor and energy in every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup natural peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix until fully combined.
  2. Roll the mixture into small balls.
  3. Place them on a tray and refrigerate for about 15 minutes to firm up.
  4. Store them in an airtight container for snacking throughout the week.

4. 5-Minute Banana Ice Cream

Who needs dairy when frozen bananas can be transformed into the creamiest ice cream in minutes?

Ingredients:

  • 4 frozen bananas
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Splash of almond milk (if needed)

Instructions:

  1. Chop the frozen bananas into smaller pieces.
  2. Place them in a high-speed blender and blend until smooth and creamy. Add a splash of almond milk if the mixture is too thick.
  3. Stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Scoop into bowls and add your favorite toppings, such as crushed nuts, chocolate chips, or fresh fruit.

5. Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

Craving cookies in a hurry? These vegan chocolate chip cookies are chewy, gooey, and come together in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1/2 cup vegan chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the flour, coconut sugar, and baking powder in a large bowl.
  3. Add the melted coconut oil and almond milk, stirring until combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  4. Scoop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes, then cool slightly before enjoying.

6. Simple Vegan Berry Crumble

Warm, gooey berries topped with a crumbly, nutty mixture make for an irresistible dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon coconut sugar
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 2 tablespoons melted coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the berries with coconut sugar and place them in a small baking dish.
  3. In a bowl, combine oats, almond flour, and melted coconut oil until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle the crumble mixture over the berries.
  5. Bake for 15 minutes or until bubbly and golden brown.

7. Vegan Chocolate Pudding

This silky chocolate pudding is rich, indulgent, and surprisingly easy to whip up.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/3 cup maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together all ingredients in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir continuously until the mixture thickens to a pudding-like consistency.
  3. Pour into bowls and refrigerate for 10 minutes to cool slightly.
  4. Top with vegan whipped cream or fresh fruits before serving.

8. 3-Ingredient Chia Seed Pudding

This creamy chia seed pudding is a versatile base for any flavor or topping you desire.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups almond milk
  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Mix almond milk, chia seeds, and maple syrup in a bowl. Whisk well.
  2. Let the mixture sit for 5 minutes, then whisk again to prevent clumping.
  3. Refrigerate for 20 minutes or until thickened into a pudding consistency.
  4. Serve with fresh fruit, nuts, or granola for added texture.

9. Vegan Lemon Bars

Bright, tangy, and perfectly sweet, these lemon bars are the ideal pick-me-up dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 3 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup (for the crust)
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup (for the filling)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and grease a small baking dish.
  2. Mix almond flour, melted coconut oil, and maple syrup for the crust, then press into the dish.
  3. Bake for 10 minutes. In the meantime, whisk together lemon juice, cornstarch, and maple syrup for the filling.
  4. Pour the filling over the crust and bake for another 10 minutes.
  5. Cool completely before cutting into bars.

10. Vegan Raspberry Chocolate Bark

A dessert that’s as elegant as it is quick, this chocolate bark combines fruit and rich cacao beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup vegan chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup freeze-dried raspberries
  • 1 tablespoon coconut flakes

Instructions:

  1. Melt the vegan chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl, stirring every 30 seconds until smooth.
  2. Spread the melted chocolate on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Sprinkle freeze-dried raspberries and coconut flakes evenly over the chocolate.
  4. Refrigerate for 20 minutes until solidified.
  5. Break into pieces and enjoy!

Whether you’re looking for something rich and chocolatey or light and fruity, these 10 recipes ensure you’ll always have a quick and satisfying vegan dessert in your arsenal. From creamy puddings to crunchy cookies, the simplicity of these desserts proves that plant-based treats can be every bit as delicious as traditional ones. Choose your favorite and let your creativity shine in the kitchen!
