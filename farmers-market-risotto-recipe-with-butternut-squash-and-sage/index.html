Farmers' Market Risotto Recipe with Butternut Squash and Sage


As the leaves begin to change and the air turns crisp, farmers’ markets come alive with the vibrant colors and rich flavors of autumn produce. One standout ingredient that perfectly encapsulates the season is butternut squash. Its sweet, nutty flavor and creamy texture make it an ideal addition to a classic dish: risotto. In this blog post, we will dive into a delectable Farmers’ Market Risotto Recipe featuring butternut squash and sage, highlighting how to create this comforting dish from scratch.

What is Risotto?

Risotto is a traditional Italian dish known for its creamy texture, achieved by slowly cooking rice in broth while stirring frequently. The most commonly used rice for risotto is Arborio, which is short-grained and high in starch, lending the dish its signature creaminess. This versatile base allows for endless variations, making risotto a perfect canvas for seasonal vegetables and flavors.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create a delicious Farmers’ Market Risotto with butternut squash and sage, gather the following ingredients:

For the Risotto

  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (preferably homemade)
  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh sage leaves, chopped (about 2 tablespoons)
  • Chopped parsley for garnish

Equipment Needed

  • A large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven
  • A wooden spoon
  • A ladle for broth
  • A cutting board and knife

Choosing the Best Ingredients

When creating a dish that relies on fresh produce, sourcing quality ingredients is essential. Visit your local farmers’ market to select the best butternut squash and herbs. Look for a squash that is firm and heavy for its size, with a smooth skin. Fresh sage should be bright green and aromatic. Supporting local farmers not only enhances the flavor of your dish but also promotes sustainable agriculture.

Preparing the Butternut Squash

Start by preparing the butternut squash. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Peel: Use a sharp vegetable peeler to remove the skin. This may take some effort, but it’s worth it for the creamy texture it adds.
  2. Seed: Cut the squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds with a spoon.
  3. Dice: Cut the squash into small, even cubes, about ½ inch in size. This ensures even cooking and perfect tenderness.

Making the Risotto

Step 1: Roasting the Squash

Roasting the butternut squash enhances its natural sweetness and brings out its flavor.

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the diced squash with a tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread it evenly on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for about 25-30 minutes or until tender and caramelized, flipping halfway through for even cooking.

Step 2: Cooking the Aromatics

While the squash is roasting, prepare the base for your risotto.

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the remaining tablespoon of olive oil and one tablespoon of butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, being careful not to let it burn.

Step 3: Toasting the Rice

  1. Add the Arborio rice to the pot, stirring to coat the grains with the oil and butter.
  2. Toast the rice for about 2-3 minutes, until the edges become slightly translucent. This step enhances the nutty flavor of the rice.

Step 4: Deglazing with Wine

If using wine, add it to the pot and stir continuously until it has mostly evaporated. This adds depth and acidity to balance the sweetness of the butternut squash.

Step 5: Adding the Broth

  1. Begin adding the warm vegetable broth one ladle at a time, stirring continuously.
  2. Allow the rice to absorb most of the liquid before adding the next ladle. This slow cooking process is crucial for achieving the creamy texture characteristic of risotto.
  3. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes, until the rice is al dente and creamy.

Step 6: Incorporating the Roasted Squash and Sage

  1. Once the risotto is cooked, fold in the roasted butternut squash and chopped sage.
  2. Stir in the remaining tablespoon of butter and the grated Parmesan cheese, mixing until everything is well combined and creamy.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 7: Serving

Serve the risotto hot, garnished with additional chopped sage and parsley for a burst of freshness. For an extra touch, drizzle a bit of high-quality olive oil over each bowl before serving.

Pairing Suggestions

This Farmers’ Market Risotto pairs wonderfully with a crisp salad featuring seasonal greens or a side of roasted vegetables. For a complete meal, consider serving it alongside grilled chicken or fish, which can complement the rich flavors of the risotto.

Wine Pairing

If you enjoy wine, consider pairing your risotto with a glass of Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio. The acidity in these wines balances the creaminess of the dish and enhances the flavors of the squash and sage.

Storage and Reheating Tips

If you have leftovers, store the risotto in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. When reheating, add a splash of broth or water to loosen the rice and restore its creamy texture.

Final Thoughts

Creating a Farmers’ Market Risotto with butternut squash and sage is not only a celebration of seasonal ingredients but also a comforting dish perfect for cozy gatherings. By following this recipe, you can enjoy the warm, creamy goodness of risotto while embracing the flavors of autumn. So, head to your local farmers’ market, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor every delicious bite of this risotto masterpiece.

Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or a simple weeknight dinner, this risotto will surely impress. Happy cooking!
