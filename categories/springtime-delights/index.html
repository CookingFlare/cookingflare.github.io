Savor the Season: Grilled Spring Vegetable Salad with Balsamic Glaze

Spring is nature’s way of saying, “Let’s party!” And what better way to celebrate the vibrant flavors of the season than with a delightful Grilled Spring Vegetable Salad with Balsamic Glaze? This recipe is a celebration of fresh, tender vegetables kissed by the grill and drizzled with a luscious balsamic glaze. It’s a dish that embodies the essence of spring – light, colorful, and bursting with flavor. Ingredients 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed 1 zucchini, sliced lengthwise 1 yellow squash, sliced lengthwise 1 red bell pepper, halved and seeded 1 red onion, cut into thick rings 2 tablespoons olive oil Salt and pepper to taste 1/2 cup balsamic vinegar 2 tablespoons honey Mixed salad greens Crumbled feta cheese (optional) Toasted pine nuts (optional) Grilling the Vegetables Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. In a large bowl, toss the asparagus, zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, and red onion with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place the vegetables on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side until they have beautiful grill marks and are tender-crisp. Once grilled, transfer the vegetables to a cutting board and let them cool slightly. Making the Balsamic Glaze In a small saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar and honey. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the glaze coats the back of a spoon. Remove from heat and let it cool. Assembling the Salad Arrange the mixed salad greens on a large platter. Cut the grilled vegetables into bite-sized pieces and arrange them on top of the greens. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the vegetables. If desired, sprinkle the salad with crumbled feta cheese and toasted pine nuts for an extra layer of flavor and texture. Serving Suggestions This Grilled Spring Vegetable Salad with Balsamic Glaze is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed in various ways. Serve it as a light and refreshing lunch, a colorful side dish at a springtime gathering, or as a vibrant accompaniment to grilled meats or seafood. It pairs beautifully with a crisp white wine or a citrusy IPA, making it a perfect addition to any springtime meal. ...
