Slow Cooker Chicken Shawarma: A Middle Eastern Delight


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this Slow Cooker Chicken Shawarma recipe. This dish is bursting with aromatic flavors, tender chicken, and a medley of Middle Eastern spices that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Beirut or the vibrant markets of Istanbul. The best part? You can achieve all of this culinary magic right in your own kitchen, thanks to the wonders of slow cooking. So, let’s roll up our sleeves, gather our spices, and embark on a flavor-packed journey to create this Middle Eastern delight.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground paprika
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 tsp ground allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Pita bread, for serving
  • Toppings: diced tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions, and tahini sauce

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine the sliced red onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, paprika, turmeric, coriander, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, allspice, salt, pepper, plain yogurt, olive oil, and lemon juice. Mix well to form a marinade.

  2. Place the chicken thighs in the slow cooker and pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.

  3. Once the chicken has marinated, set the slow cooker to low and cook for 4-5 hours, or until the chicken is tender and easily shreddable with a fork.

  4. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded chicken to the slow cooker and toss it in the flavorful juices.

  5. To serve, warm the pita bread and spoon the succulent chicken shawarma onto the bread. Top with diced tomatoes, cucumbers, red onions, and a generous drizzle of tahini sauce.

  6. Roll up the pita bread, tucking in the sides to form a wrap, and enjoy the explosion of Middle Eastern flavors with every bite.

Tips for Success

  • For an extra depth of flavor, consider toasting the whole spices (cumin, coriander, and allspice) in a dry pan before grinding them for the marinade.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the marinade to suit your taste.
  • Don’t be afraid to customize the toppings to your preference. Pickles, pickled turnips, or even a sprinkle of sumac can add another layer of complexity to your shawarma.

Final Thoughts

Slow Cooker Chicken Shawarma is a dish that embodies the soul of Middle Eastern cuisine. The warm blend of spices, the succulent chicken, and the vibrant toppings come together to create a culinary experience that is truly unforgettable. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of the exotic, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy. So, embrace the flavors of the Middle East and savor every bite of this tantalizing, aromatic delight.
