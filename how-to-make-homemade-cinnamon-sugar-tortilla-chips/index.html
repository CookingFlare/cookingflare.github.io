How to Make Homemade Cinnamon Sugar Tortilla Chips


If you’re craving a sweet and crunchy snack, homemade cinnamon sugar tortilla chips are the perfect treat. Whether you want something quick to whip up for movie night, a fun addition to a party spread, or a simple indulgence for yourself, these chips are sure to hit the spot. The beauty of this recipe lies in its simplicity. With just a few basic ingredients, you can make your own homemade cinnamon sugar tortilla chips in no time.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through step-by-step instructions to create crispy, sweet, and perfectly spiced chips. From the essential ingredients to tips for achieving the best crunch, this recipe will help you make a snack that tastes far superior to anything you can buy from a store. Let’s dive right into how to make these delicious treats!

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we get started with the recipe, let’s gather the ingredients. Fortunately, you probably already have most of these in your pantry:

  • Flour tortillas: You can use store-bought flour tortillas, or if you’re feeling adventurous, you can even make your own.
  • Butter: Melted butter is essential to make the cinnamon sugar stick to the chips and add a rich flavor.
  • Cinnamon: The star of the show! Ground cinnamon adds that warm, sweet spice that’s just perfect with sugar.
  • Sugar: Granulated white sugar will be combined with cinnamon to create the sweet coating.
  • Salt: A pinch of salt balances the sweetness and enhances the overall flavor of the chips.

Optional:

  • Vanilla extract: A teaspoon of vanilla extract can add a little extra depth to the cinnamon flavor, though it’s not necessary.

Equipment You’ll Need

Before you begin cooking, make sure you have all the tools you’ll need to prepare these crispy, cinnamon-sugar-covered tortilla chips:

  • Baking sheet: This is where you’ll bake the tortilla chips until they’re crispy and golden brown.
  • Sharp knife or pizza cutter: To slice the tortillas into triangles.
  • Brush or spoon: To spread melted butter over the tortilla pieces.
  • Small bowl: For mixing the cinnamon-sugar coating.
  • Cooling rack (optional): For letting your tortilla chips cool and stay crispy after baking.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that the chips will bake evenly and crisp up perfectly without burning.

2. Slice the Tortillas

Once your oven is preheated, it’s time to prepare the tortillas. Lay your flour tortillas flat on a cutting board. Using a sharp knife or a pizza cutter, slice each tortilla into 6 to 8 triangles. The exact number of slices depends on the size of your tortillas, but the goal is to have even pieces so they bake uniformly. If you prefer smaller or larger chips, feel free to adjust the size accordingly.

3. Prepare the Cinnamon-Sugar Mixture

In a small bowl, combine the cinnamon and sugar. The general ratio is 1 part cinnamon to 4 parts sugar. For example, if you’re using 1 tablespoon of cinnamon, you’ll need 4 tablespoons of sugar. Stir the mixture well until the cinnamon is evenly distributed throughout the sugar. If you’d like to experiment with the flavor a bit, you can add a pinch of salt to balance out the sweetness or a splash of vanilla extract for a hint of richness.

4. Brush the Tortillas with Butter

Next, melt about 4 tablespoons of butter in the microwave or on the stovetop. Once the butter is melted and slightly cooled, use a pastry brush or a spoon to generously coat each tortilla triangle with butter. Make sure the butter is applied evenly to the surface of each triangle, as this will help the cinnamon-sugar mixture adhere and create that crispy, golden coating.

5. Coat the Tortillas with Cinnamon Sugar

Once the tortilla triangles are coated in butter, sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over them. You can use your hands or a spoon to gently sprinkle the mixture, making sure each chip gets a good, even coating. If you want a heavier cinnamon flavor, feel free to add more of the cinnamon-sugar mix, but be careful not to overdo it so that it doesn’t overpower the chips’ natural flavor.

6. Arrange the Chips on a Baking Sheet

Place the buttered and sugar-coated tortilla triangles in a single layer on a baking sheet. It’s important not to overcrowd the chips to ensure they bake evenly. If you need to use two baking sheets, feel free to divide the chips between them.

7. Bake the Chips

Now it’s time to bake the chips. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for about 10 to 15 minutes, or until the chips are golden brown and crispy. Keep a close eye on them during the last few minutes of baking, as they can go from perfectly crispy to slightly burnt quickly.

You may want to flip the chips halfway through the baking time to ensure even crisping on both sides, but this step is optional depending on your preference. If you do flip them, be gentle as the cinnamon sugar can get a little sticky.

8. Cool and Serve

Once the chips are golden brown and crispy, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes on the baking sheet. If you have a cooling rack, transferring the chips to it will help them cool faster and stay crispy for longer.

Let the chips cool for at least 5 minutes before serving so that the cinnamon sugar can set. This also ensures that they are cool enough to enjoy without burning your fingers. The chips will be warm, crunchy, and sweet — perfect for snacking!

Tips for the Perfect Cinnamon Sugar Tortilla Chips

  • Even Coating: The key to achieving the perfect balance of flavor is ensuring that each tortilla chip has an even layer of melted butter and cinnamon sugar. If you find that the mixture isn’t sticking well, try brushing the chips with a little more butter.
  • Watch the Oven: Cinnamon sugar chips can go from golden brown to burnt in a flash, so keep an eye on them during the last few minutes of baking.
  • Experiment with Flavors: While cinnamon and sugar is the classic combination, feel free to get creative. Try adding a bit of nutmeg or cardamom to the cinnamon-sugar mix for a unique twist. You could even try using brown sugar instead of white sugar for a deeper, richer flavor.
  • Storage: These cinnamon sugar tortilla chips are best eaten fresh out of the oven, but you can store any leftovers in an airtight container for a few days. If the chips lose their crunch, simply pop them back in the oven for a few minutes to crisp up again.

Variations You Can Try

While the traditional cinnamon-sugar combination is a fan favorite, you can easily customize the flavor to suit your taste. Here are a few variations you might want to try:

  • Chocolate Cinnamon Sugar Chips: Add a tablespoon of cocoa powder to the cinnamon-sugar mixture for a chocolatey twist. This will give your chips a rich, decadent flavor that pairs perfectly with a cold glass of milk.
  • Apple Pie Chips: Add a teaspoon of ground ginger and a dash of ground nutmeg to the cinnamon-sugar mixture for an apple pie-inspired flavor. This makes for a great autumn snack or a fun twist on a holiday treat.
  • Spicy Cinnamon Chips: If you like a bit of heat with your sweetness, try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or chili powder to the cinnamon-sugar mix. The subtle spice will balance out the sweetness beautifully.
  • Honey Drizzle: For an extra indulgent treat, drizzle some honey over the chips after baking. The sweetness from the honey will complement the cinnamon sugar and add a sticky, delicious finish.

Pairing Suggestions

Homemade cinnamon sugar tortilla chips are a versatile snack that pairs well with a variety of dips and beverages. Here are some fun ideas for what to serve alongside your chips:

  • Chocolate Sauce: For an extra sweet treat, dip your cinnamon chips into warm chocolate sauce.
  • Whipped Cream or Ice Cream: These chips are excellent with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. They add a wonderful crunch to a creamy dessert.
  • Caramel Dip: The rich flavor of caramel works wonderfully with cinnamon chips. You can either use store-bought caramel sauce or make your own.
  • Fresh Fruit Salsa: For a refreshing, fruity contrast to the sweet and spicy chips, try making a fresh fruit salsa with ingredients like strawberries, mango, and lime.

Final Thoughts

Making homemade cinnamon sugar tortilla chips is a simple and satisfying way to enjoy a sweet, crunchy treat. With just a few ingredients and minimal effort, you can create a delicious snack that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re indulging by yourself, sharing with friends, or adding a sweet element to a party spread, these chips are sure to become a favorite in your snack rotation.

So, what are you waiting for? Grab your tortillas, fire up the oven, and make these delicious homemade cinnamon sugar tortilla chips today! Enjoy the crispy, sweet goodness that will leave everyone coming back for more.
