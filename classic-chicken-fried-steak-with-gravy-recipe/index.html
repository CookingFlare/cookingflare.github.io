Classic Chicken Fried Steak with Gravy Recipe


Chicken fried steak is a beloved Southern classic that brings comfort and nostalgia to the dinner table. This dish features tenderized beef steak, breaded and fried to golden perfection, then smothered in a rich, creamy gravy. Whether served for a hearty breakfast or a satisfying dinner, chicken fried steak is sure to please. In this post, we’ll explore the history of this iconic dish, the secrets to making it perfectly, and a step-by-step recipe that will have you serving up this comfort food favorite in no time.

The History of Chicken Fried Steak

The origins of chicken fried steak are somewhat murky, but it is widely believed to have roots in the German immigrants who settled in Texas in the 19th century. They brought with them the tradition of Wiener schnitzel, a breaded and fried veal cutlet. Over time, this dish evolved into what we now know as chicken fried steak, using beef instead of veal. The name itself reflects the cooking method, as the steak is prepared in a manner similar to fried chicken.

Chicken fried steak has become a staple in Southern cuisine, often served with creamy gravy and sides like mashed potatoes or collard greens. Its popularity has spread beyond the South, making it a favorite comfort food across the United States.

Ingredients for Chicken Fried Steak

To make classic chicken fried steak with gravy, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken Fried Steak:

  • 4 beef cube steaks (about 1/2 inch thick)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Gravy:

  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (reserved from the breading)
  • 2 cups milk
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Steak

  1. Tenderize the Steak: If your cube steaks are not already tenderized, use a meat mallet to pound them to about 1/2 inch thickness. This helps to break down the fibers and ensures a tender bite.

  2. Set Up Breading Station: In one shallow dish, combine the flour, salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder. In another dish, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined.

  3. Bread the Steaks: Dip each steak into the flour mixture, coating both sides thoroughly. Shake off any excess flour, then dip the steak into the egg mixture, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, return the steak to the flour mixture for a second coating. This double breading creates a crispy crust.

Frying the Steak

  1. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet, pour in enough vegetable oil to cover the bottom of the pan (about 1/4 inch deep). Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it shimmers.

  2. Fry the Steaks: Carefully place the breaded steaks into the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 4-6 minutes on each side, or until the coating is golden brown and the steak reaches an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C). Use tongs to flip the steaks only once to maintain the crispy coating.

  3. Drain the Steaks: Once cooked, transfer the steaks to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Keep them warm in a low oven while you prepare the gravy.

Making the Gravy

  1. Prepare the Gravy Base: After removing the steaks, pour off all but about 2 tablespoons of oil from the skillet. Leave the browned bits in the pan, as they add flavor to the gravy.

  2. Make the Roux: Sprinkle the reserved flour over the oil in the skillet. Whisk continuously over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes, until the flour turns a light golden brown. This step is crucial for developing the flavor of the gravy.

  3. Add Milk: Gradually whisk in the milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Continue to cook and whisk until the gravy thickens, about 5-10 minutes. If the gravy becomes too thick, add a little more milk until you reach your desired consistency.

  4. Season the Gravy: Taste the gravy and season with salt and pepper as needed. For a little extra kick, consider adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or a dash of hot sauce.

Serving

  1. Plate the Dish: Serve the chicken fried steak hot, generously topped with the creamy gravy. This dish pairs beautifully with sides like mashed potatoes, green beans, or a simple salad.

  2. Enjoy: Dig in and savor the comforting flavors of this classic dish. The crispy steak combined with the rich gravy is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Fried Steak

  • Choose the Right Cut: Cube steak is the traditional choice for chicken fried steak, but you can also use other cuts like sirloin or round steak. Just be sure to tenderize them well.

  • Don’t Rush the Breading: Take your time with the breading process. A thorough coating will ensure a crispy exterior.

  • Maintain Oil Temperature: If the oil is too hot, the coating may burn before the steak is cooked through. If it’s too cool, the steak will absorb too much oil and become greasy. Use a thermometer to monitor the oil temperature, aiming for around 350°F (175°C).

  • Make It Ahead: You can prepare the breaded steaks in advance and refrigerate them until you’re ready to fry. This can help the coating adhere better.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to customize the seasoning in the flour mixture or the gravy. Adding herbs like thyme or rosemary can elevate the dish.

Conclusion

Classic chicken fried steak with gravy is more than just a meal; it’s a comforting experience that brings people together. With its crispy exterior and creamy gravy, this dish is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a family dinner or a weekend brunch. By following this recipe and tips, you can create a delicious chicken fried steak that rivals any restaurant. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a true Southern classic that will have everyone asking for seconds!
