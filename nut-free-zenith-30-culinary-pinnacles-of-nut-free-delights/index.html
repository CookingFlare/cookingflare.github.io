Nut-Free Zenith: 30 Culinary Pinnacles of Nut-Free Delights


When YOU utter the words “no nuts,” pastry pantries shudder. No longer. This rich, velvety cake demands a spotlight all its own. Prepare for a rabid following, because once you experience it, there’s no turning back.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 oz unsweetened chocolate, melted & cooled
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 3 large eggs, separated
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups unsweetened cocoa
  • 3/4 cup almond flour (don’t panic, it’s just a trick!)
  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Whipped cream & fresh berries for garnishing

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease an 8-inch springform pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, beat the egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale. Add then add melted chocolate and oil, stirring until smooth.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together almond flour, all-purpose flour, cocoa, baking powder, and salt. Gradually stir into the chocolate mixture until fully combined.
  4. In a separate, clean bowl beat your egg whites until they form soft peaks. Gently fold them into the batter.
  5. Pour the batter into your prepared pan and bake for about 25 minutes, or until it has risen and is set on the outside but still, slightly gooey in the middle.
  6. Allow to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
  7. When cool, dollop whipped cream on top and adorn with your choice of berries.

2. Quinoa Salad with Lemon-Herb Dressing

This recipes is a playful twist on the traditional grain salad with an exciting twist – the star of the bowl is quinoa, not the pesto. The plus side: no nuts, just pure flavors!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 cups diced cucumber
  • 1 and 1/2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 3/4 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Bring water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add quinoa and stir to combine. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until quinoa is tender and water has been absorbed, about 15 minutes. Set aside to cool.
  2. In a large bowl combine cooled quinoa with cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and parsley.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Pour over quinoa mixture and toss to combine. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

3. Dairy-Free Coconut Chai Latte

Desire something warm and comforting? Skip the coffee shop and learn to craft this creamy, aromatic blend at home! Chai without nuts is a breeze – and so much more delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 green cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, peeled and sliced
  • 3 black peppercorns
  • 2 black tea bags (such as Assam or Ceylon)
  • 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk
  • Sweetener of your choice (such as honey or maple syrup), to taste
  • Steamed milk of choice (optional)

Directions:

  1. Combine cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, and peppercorns in a small saucepan, and use a spoon to roughly crush them together.
  2. Add water to cover the spices and heat to a simmer. As the water heats, the aroma will fill your home.
  3. Add tea bags and simmer for 5 minutes to allow the spices to infuse.
  4. Remove from heat and add the coconut milk, gently stirring to combine. Steep for another 5 minutes.
  5. Strain and sweeten to taste. If you prefer a creamier latte, top with steamed milk of choice.

4. Sindhi Kadhi - Yogurt Based Curry Made Without Nuts

In Indian cuisine, nuts are often an afterthought - a sprinkle atop a curry, not the foundation. Here, the star is the tangy, golden pool of flavor – our Sindhi Kadhi. A yogurt-based curry that’s flavorful and divine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • Few curry leaves
  • 1 green chili, slit

Directions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and when they splutter, add curry leaves and green chili.
  2. Add ginger paste and cook for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix chickpea flour with some water to make a smooth paste, then pour into the pan and let it cook for a while.
  4. Add yogurt, turmeric, salt, and mix well. Gradually add water until the desired consistency is reached.
  5. Keep on simmer, stirring occasionally, for 10-12 minutes until the kadhi thickens.
  6. Add the boiled and cubed potatoes, give a gentle stir and cook for another 3-4 minutes.

Sindhi Kadhi is excellent with steamed rice or rotis.

5. Vegan Spaghetti Squash Alfredo

Traditional alfredo contains nuts in the form of butter, making it non-vegan. With a few tweaks, this version captures that delicate and rich flavor while being vegan. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

  • 1 spaghetti squash
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 cup melted vegan butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut your spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds.
  2. Brush the inside with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place cut-side down on a lined baking sheet and bake until tender, about 50 minutes.
  3. When squash is done, carefully flip the halves over and let it cool slightly. Using a fork, scrape the spaghetti-like strands into a serving dish. Set aside.
  4. In a small saucepan, warm the almond milk over medium-low heat. Add the garlic and let it simmer gently for a couple of minutes.
  5. Whisk in nutritional yeast and vegan butter until smooth and creamy. Season to taste with additional salt and pepper.
  6. Drizzle sauce over spaghetti squash and give a gentle toss. Garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

6. Vegan Avocado Breakfast Sandwich

What are mornings without a satisfying breakfast sandwich? This gluten-free, vegan take on the classic offers a plant-powered twist that’ll make your mornings brighter. Oh, it’s delightful, indeed!

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of gluten-free bread (or english muffin)
  • 1 avocado, mashed
  • 2 Vegan “eggs” (or tofu scramble Replace “vegan eggs”, “tofu scramble” with other words like Chickpea flour omelette)
  • 1/4 cup chickpea flour, mixed with 1/2 cup water, to make a pourable batter
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: vegan cheese, lettuce, tomato, etc.

Directions:

  1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat. Pour the batter evenly, making a small panneque.
  2. Cook until set and crispy on the bottom, flip and cook the other side. Once cooked, transfer to a plate and set aside.
  3. Spread a portion of mashed avocado on one slice of bread. Place the cooked Chickpea flour omelette on top, then top with chopped cilantro, salt and pepper.
  4. Place the other slice of bread on top, and enjoy! For additional layers or texture, consider adding vegan cheese or extra veggies.

7. Urad Dal Soup - A protein packed comforting soup

A perfect comfort food that’ll tease your taste buds with flavor beyond compare. The Urad Dal Soup is unique and super easy to prepare, with common pantry ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup split urad dal (black gram)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida powder
  • 2 cloves Garlic, chopped
  • 1/2 inch ginger, chopped
  • 2 Green Chillies
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Directions:

  1. Rinse the urad dal thoroughly until water runs clear. Bring water to a boil in a medium-sized saucepan.
  2. Add the dal and stir so it doesn’t stick to the bottom. Lower the flame and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes or until the lentils are softened.
  3. In a small pan heat little oil/ghee, add asafoetida, then add the chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until the raw odor goes away.
  4. Pour this tempering over the urad dal, add turmeric powder and salt, stir well.
  5. Let it simmer for 2-3 more minutes, then serve hot garnished with chopped coriander leaves.

8. Glossy Roasted Root Vegetables

Hearty and healthy, these glossy roasted root vegetables literally have that wow factor! Stubborn nuts, get ready to be outshined!

Ingredients:

  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 large parsnips, peeled and sliced
  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 large red onion, sliced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon Maple syrup

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine sliced carrots, parsnips, cubed sweet potatoes, and sliced red onion.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and maple syrup, tossing to combine.
  4. Spread arranged on a lined baking sheet and roast for 35-45 minutes, flipping halfway through, until vegetables are tender and edges are golden.
  5. Serve immediately, and enjoy the glossy, caramelized stars.

9. Matcha Smoothie with Avocado and Banana

Combine the health benefits of matcha with the creaminess of ripe avocado, natural sweetness of banana, and flaxseeds in this icy, green, smoothie bowl. Each sip tastes like a blissful thumbs up to nut-free joy!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 teaspoon matcha powder
  • 1/2 ripe banana
  • 1/2 ripe avocado
  • 1 tablespoon flaxseeds
  • A handful of spinach
  • 1/2 cup ice cubes
  • Sweetener of choice, to taste

Directions:

  1. Add almond milk, matcha powder, banana, avocado, flaxseeds, spinach, ice cubes, and sweetener of choice in the blender.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour the smoothie into a bowl or glass, add toppings, and indulge!

10. Dairy-Free Coconut Frozen Yogurt

Turn aged-old frozen yogurt dreams into a reality with a delectable dairy-free version bursting with coconut goodness. Dairy-avoiding never tasted so sumptuous!

Ingredients:

  • 2 (14 oz) cans coconut cream, chilled
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Directions:

  1. Remove the cans of coconut cream from the refrigerator and scoop out the solid part into a mixing bowl, leaving the liquid behind.
  2. Add maple syrup, vanilla, almond milk, and salt to the bowl holding the coconut cream.
  3. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer with the whisk attachment, whisk until soft peaks start to form.
  4. Transfer to an airtight container, cover, and freeze for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  5. Scoop into bowls and serve with preferred toppings.
