Pies from Around the World: Global Recipes to Try


Pies are a beloved dish that transcends cultures and continents. They come in countless varieties, each with its own unique twist on flavors, crusts, and fillings. From savory to sweet, pies symbolize comfort, warmth, and hospitality. In this post, we will take you on a culinary journey, exploring pies from around the globe that you can recreate in your own kitchen. Prepare to dive into an array of delicious recipes that celebrate the diversity and creativity of pie-making!

1. American Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water
  • 6 to 8 apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp), peeled and sliced
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon butter, diced
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar. Cut in butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in ice water, a tablespoon at a time, until the mixture forms a ball. Wrap in plastic and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Make the filling: In another bowl, toss the sliced apples with sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out half the dough on a floured surface and fit it into a pie pan. Fill with apple mixture and dot with butter. Roll out the second half of the dough and place over the filling. Seal edges and cut slits in the top to allow steam to escape.

  4. Bake: Brush the top with beaten egg. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 35-45 minutes, or until apples are soft and crust is golden brown. Cool before serving.

2. French Tarte Tatin

Ingredients:

  • 6 to 8 apples (preferably Braeburn or Honeycrisp)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Caramelize the apples: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a cast-iron skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add sugar and cook until it turns golden. Peel and quarter the apples, then place them in the skillet, cut side up. Cook for about 15 minutes until they start to caramelize.

  2. Cover with pastry: Once the apples are softened, sprinkle with vanilla extract. Roll out the puff pastry and cover the apples, tucking the edges in.

  3. Bake: Place the skillet in the oven and bake for about 25-30 minutes until the pastry is golden brown. Allow to cool for a few minutes, then carefully invert onto a serving plate.

3. British Shepherd’s Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds ground lamb (or beef)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup peas
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 4 cups mashed potatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook the filling: Heat olive oil in a large skillet. Add onion, carrots, and garlic, cooking until softened. Add the ground meat, cooking until browned. Stir in peas, tomato paste, broth, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 10 minutes.

  2. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the meat mixture in a baking dish and top with mashed potatoes, smoothing the surface.

  3. Bake: Bake for 20-25 minutes until the potatoes are golden brown. Let cool slightly before serving.

4. Italian Ricotta Pie (Torta di Ricotta)

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups ricotta cheese
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, combine ricotta, sugar, eggs, vanilla, and lemon zest, mixing until smooth. Add flour, baking powder, and salt, stirring until well combined.

  2. Assemble the pie: Fit the pie crust into a pie pan and pour in the ricotta mixture.

  3. Bake: Bake for 40-45 minutes or until the center is set and lightly golden. Allow to cool before slicing.

5. Japanese Meat Pie (Niku Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 sheet puff pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the filling: In a pan, sauté onions and garlic until translucent. Add ground beef, cooking until browned. Stir in soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, salt, and pepper. Let cool.

  2. Assemble the pie: Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out the puff pastry and cut into squares. Place a spoonful of filling on one half and fold over, sealing the edges with a fork. Brush with egg wash.

  3. Bake: Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown. Serve warm.

6. Brazilian Empadão (Savory Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 egg
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup shredded chicken (or beef)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup olives, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make the crust: In a bowl, mix flour, butter, egg, baking powder, and salt until combined. Form into a dough and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the filling: In a pan, sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper until soft. Add shredded chicken, olives, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes and let cool.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out half the dough and fit into a pie pan. Fill with the chicken mixture and cover with the remaining dough, sealing the edges.

  4. Bake: Bake for about 40 minutes until golden brown. Serve warm.

7. Austrian Apfelstrudel

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 6 to 8 apples, peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup raisins (optional)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough: In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar. Gradually add warm water and olive oil, mixing until a dough forms. Knead for 10 minutes, then let rest for 30 minutes.

  2. Make the filling: In a separate bowl, mix apples, sugar, cinnamon, breadcrumbs, and raisins.

  3. Assemble the strudel: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the dough as thin as possible on a floured surface. Spread the apple mixture evenly over the dough, then roll it up tightly.

  4. Bake: Place on a baking sheet and bake for 30-40 minutes until golden brown. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

8. Greek Spanakopita (Spinach Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo pastry
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, chopped (or frozen)
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a pan, sauté onion until translucent. Add spinach and cook until wilted. In a bowl, mix spinach, ricotta, feta, eggs, salt, and pepper.

  2. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Layer phyllo sheets in a greased baking dish, brushing each layer with olive oil. Spread the filling over the phyllo and fold the edges over. Top with more phyllo, brushing with oil.

  3. Bake: Bake for 30-40 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
