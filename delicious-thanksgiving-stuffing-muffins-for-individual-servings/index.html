Delicious Thanksgiving Stuffing Muffins for Individual Servings


As the Thanksgiving season approaches, it’s time to start planning the perfect menu for this special occasion. While traditional dishes like turkey and mashed potatoes often steal the spotlight, it’s the side dishes that truly bring the feast together. If you’re looking to add a creative twist to your Thanksgiving spread, these delicious Thanksgiving stuffing muffins are the perfect choice for individual servings.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of crusty bread, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup diced onions
  • 1 cup diced celery
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 1/2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease each cup with butter.

  2. Spread the bread cubes on a baking sheet and toast them in the oven for about 15 minutes, or until they are golden and crisp. Transfer the toasted bread cubes to a large mixing bowl.

  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced onions, celery, and garlic, and sauté until the vegetables are soft and translucent.

  4. Add the dried sage, thyme, rosemary, salt, and black pepper to the skillet, and cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Pour the vegetable and herb mixture over the toasted bread cubes in the mixing bowl. Toss to combine, ensuring the bread cubes are well-coated with the seasoned butter.

  6. In a separate bowl, whisk together the chicken or vegetable broth and beaten eggs. Pour this mixture over the bread cubes and stir until everything is evenly moistened.

  7. Gently fold in the dried cranberries and chopped pecans, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the stuffing mixture.

  8. Spoon the stuffing mixture into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup to the top and pressing down gently to pack the mixture.

  9. Bake the stuffing muffins in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until they are golden brown and set.

  10. Allow the stuffing muffins to cool in the muffin tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Serving Suggestions

These delightful Thanksgiving stuffing muffins can be served as a unique and convenient side dish for your holiday feast. They make for perfect individual servings, ensuring that each guest gets a taste of the delicious flavors in every bite.

Pair these stuffing muffins with your favorite Thanksgiving dishes, such as roasted turkey, cranberry sauce, and creamy mashed potatoes, for a truly memorable meal.

Conclusion

With their flavorful combination of savory herbs, hearty bread, and delightful mix-ins, these Thanksgiving stuffing muffins are sure to become a new holiday favorite. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying an intimate dinner, these individual servings add a special touch to your Thanksgiving table. Give this recipe a try, and watch as your guests delight in these delectable stuffing muffins during the holiday season. Happy Thanksgiving!
