Easy Spanish Rice Recipes for Quick Weeknight Dinners


Spanish rice is a vibrant, comforting dish that can transform a weeknight dinner into a lively, flavorful experience. With its rich tomato base, fragrant spices, and versatile ingredients, Spanish rice not only acts as a perfect side but can also shine as a main course. If you’re looking for quick, simple, and satisfying recipes, this guide has got you covered. From the classic versions that have stood the test of time to exciting modern twists, these easy Spanish rice recipes will be your go-to solution during busy evenings. Let’s dive in!

What Makes Spanish Rice So Special?

Spanish rice, often called Mexican rice or arroz rojo, is more than just cooked grains. It’s about layering flavors and textures to create a dish that’s both hearty and bright. Key components include:

  • Rice – Typically long grain white rice is used, toasted before cooking to add nuttiness.
  • Tomato – From tomato sauce or fresh tomatoes to bring a natural acidity and sweetness.
  • Aromatics and Spices – Garlic, onions, cumin, and smoked paprika add complexity.
  • Broth – Chicken or vegetable broth enriches the rice, infusing it with depth.
  • Add-ins – Peas, corn, bell peppers, or even proteins like chicken or shrimp can elevate the dish.

The simplicity lies in how these ingredients come together, melding into a cozy, vibrant plate that requires little hands-on time.

Classic Spanish Rice: The Foundation Recipe

Let’s start with the classic recipe, perfect as a hearty side to grilled meats, tacos, or roasted vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 ¾ cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth for vegetarian)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)
  • ½ cup frozen peas (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the Rice: Heat olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the rice and cook, stirring frequently, until it turns golden brown and smells nutty (about 3-4 minutes).
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Add onion and garlic to the rice, cooking until softened and fragrant (around 2 minutes).
  3. Add Tomato and Spices: Stir in tomato sauce, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Pour in Broth: Add broth to the pan and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until rice absorbs the liquid.
  5. Add Peas: Five minutes before the rice is done, stir in peas, cover, and continue cooking.
  6. Fluff and Serve: Remove from heat and let the rice sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and garnish with cilantro if desired.

Spanish Rice with Chicken and Bell Peppers

Adding chicken and bell peppers turns this staple into a filling, protein-packed dinner. It’s a fantastic choice for busy weeknights when you want a full meal in one pot.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 1 ¾ cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook Chicken: Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season chicken with salt and pepper and cook until browned and nearly cooked through (5-6 minutes). Remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté Veggies: Add remaining olive oil to the pan. Sauté onion, garlic, and bell peppers until soft (about 5 minutes).
  3. Toast Rice: Stir in the rice and cook, stirring frequently for 2-3 minutes to toast.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Spices: Add diced tomatoes, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  5. Add Broth and Chicken: Pour in chicken broth and return chicken pieces to the pan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  6. Simmer Covered: Cover and simmer for 20 minutes, or until rice is tender and liquid absorbed.
  7. Rest and Garnish: Let the dish rest for 5 minutes before fluffing and garnishing with parsley or cilantro.

Vegetarian Spanish Rice with Corn and Black Beans

For a meat-free option bursting with color and nutrition, corn and black beans are ideal additions. This recipe is both hearty and fiber-rich, perfect for flexitarians or anyone seeking plant-based comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long grain white rice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup tomato puree or sauce
  • 1 ¾ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1 cup cooked or canned black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lime wedges and chopped cilantro for serving

Instructions:

  1. Toast the Rice and Sauté Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add rice and cook until golden, then add onion and garlic, cooking until fragrant.
  2. Add Tomato and Spices: Pour in tomato puree, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper, stirring to combine.
  3. Simmer Rice: Add vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cover, reduce heat to low, and cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Add Corn and Beans: Stir in corn and black beans, cover again, and cook for another 8-10 minutes until liquid is absorbed and rice is tender.
  5. Serve: Squeeze fresh lime juice over the dish and sprinkle with cilantro before serving.

Tips for Perfect Spanish Rice Every Time

Achieving fluffy, flavorful Spanish rice isn’t complicated, but a few tricks can elevate your dish:

  • Toast the rice first: This step creates a nutty flavor and prevents the rice from becoming mushy.
  • Use good broth: Quality chicken or vegetable broth adds a depth that water can’t match.
  • Don’t peek too often: Keep the lid on during cooking so the rice can steam properly.
  • Rest before fluffing: Letting the rice sit covered for 5-10 minutes helps the grains firm up.
  • Customize add-ins: Feel free to experiment with vegetables like diced carrots, zucchini, or olives for added texture and flavor.

Quick Spanish Rice Stir-Fry: A Weeknight Hack

If you have leftover rice and want to whip up dinner in a flash, a Spanish rice stir-fry can save the day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked Spanish rice (preferably chilled leftover)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu (optional)
  • ½ cup chopped bell peppers
  • ½ cup corn or peas
  • 2 tablespoons tomato sauce or salsa
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Sauté onion, garlic, and bell peppers until softened.
  3. Add in your choice of protein, cooking until warmed through.
  4. Stir in cooked Spanish rice, corn or peas, tomato sauce, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Cook, stirring often, until everything is heated well, about 5 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve immediately.

Wrapping It Up

Spanish rice is a timeless dish that’s flexible, flavorful, and perfect for quick weeknight dinners. Whether you stick to the classic tomato-based rice, add in proteins and vegetables, or get creative with leftovers, these easy recipes can fit your schedule and satisfy your taste buds effortlessly.

Experiment with spices, swap in fresh ingredients, and tailor each recipe to your preferences. With just a handful of pantry staples, you can have a comforting, crowd-pleasing meal on the table in under 30 minutes. Try out these recipes tonight and watch Spanish rice become a staple in your dinner rotation!
