BBQ Hacks: How to Make Grilling Easier and More Delicious


Grilling is one of the most enjoyable ways to cook, especially during the warmer months. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue with friends and family or just looking to enjoy some delicious grilled food, there’s something special about cooking over an open flame. But grilling can sometimes feel daunting, especially if you’re trying to get everything just right. Fear not! In this guide, we’ll share some tried-and-tested BBQ hacks that will not only make grilling easier but also elevate the flavor of your food.

1. Preheat Your Grill for Perfect Results

The first step to successful grilling is preheating your grill. It might sound basic, but many people overlook this essential step. Preheating your grill ensures that the heat is evenly distributed, preventing food from sticking or cooking unevenly. Whether you’re using a gas or charcoal grill, allow it to heat up for at least 10-15 minutes before you start cooking.

For charcoal grills, ensure the coals are glowing red before you place your food on the grill. This will give you that signature smoky flavor that is often associated with grilled food.

2. Oil Your Grill Grates

One of the most common grilling challenges is food sticking to the grill. To avoid this issue, oil your grill grates before cooking. You can use a paper towel soaked in vegetable oil or a grill brush with a bit of oil on it to coat the grates. This simple step creates a non-stick surface, making it easier to flip your food and preventing it from falling apart.

Be sure to let the grill grates cool down before applying oil. It’s a good idea to do this during the preheating phase, so you’re all set by the time you’re ready to cook.

3. The Importance of a Meat Thermometer

When grilling meat, it’s crucial to cook it to the right internal temperature. Using a meat thermometer is one of the best hacks for ensuring that your meat is cooked perfectly every time. You can easily check if your steaks, burgers, or chicken are cooked to your liking with a simple thermometer.

For example, steak should be at least 145°F (62.8°C) for medium-rare, while chicken should reach 165°F (73.8°C) to ensure it’s fully cooked. Having a thermometer on hand removes the guesswork and ensures that your food is safe to eat while maintaining its juiciness and tenderness.

4. Invest in Grill Mats

Grill mats are an absolute game-changer, especially if you’re grilling delicate foods like fish or vegetables. These non-stick mats provide an easy way to cook food on the grill without the risk of it falling through the grates or sticking to them. Grill mats are heat-resistant and can be reused multiple times, making them both eco-friendly and budget-friendly.

Simply lay the mat over the grill grates, and you can place your food on top. The mat helps distribute the heat evenly, ensuring that your food cooks evenly without direct exposure to the flames. Grill mats are especially great for cooking small items like shrimp, mushrooms, and thin cuts of meat.

5. Use Indirect Heat for Large Cuts of Meat

When grilling larger cuts of meat, such as roasts or whole chickens, direct heat can sometimes be too harsh, resulting in burnt exteriors and raw interiors. A great BBQ hack is to use indirect heat for these larger cuts of meat. This method involves placing the meat on one side of the grill and leaving the other side empty or with coals that are not directly under the food.

Indirect heat allows the meat to cook slowly and evenly, ensuring that it stays juicy and tender. If you’re using a gas grill, simply turn on only one side of the burners. For a charcoal grill, you can create a two-zone setup by pushing the coals to one side. This method works wonderfully for whole chickens, pork shoulders, and ribs.

6. Try the Reverse Sear Method for Perfect Steaks

The reverse sear method is a fantastic BBQ hack for cooking the perfect steak. It involves cooking the steak on indirect heat first and then finishing it off with a quick sear on direct heat. The result is a perfectly cooked steak with a crispy exterior and juicy interior.

To use the reverse sear method, start by cooking your steak on indirect heat, either on a cooler side of the grill or by turning on only one burner on a gas grill. Once the steak has reached your desired internal temperature (use that thermometer!), move it to the direct heat side for a quick sear, about 1-2 minutes per side, until you get a beautiful crust.

This technique works especially well for thicker cuts of steak, like ribeye or porterhouse, and ensures that your steak is cooked to perfection.

7. Marinate, But Don’t Overdo It

Marinating is an essential BBQ hack to infuse your food with flavor. However, there’s a fine line between marinating just enough and marinating too long. While marinating for a few hours or overnight is ideal for most meats, don’t overdo it, especially with acidic marinades (those with vinegar or citrus). Over-marinating can lead to mushy meat, which no one wants.

Aim for 30 minutes to 4 hours of marination, depending on the type of meat. For example, chicken and pork can benefit from a few hours in a marinade, while beef steaks only need about 30 minutes to an hour. If you’re using an acidic marinade, it’s best not to go beyond that to maintain the texture of the meat.

8. Use a BBQ Basting Brush for Extra Flavor

One simple yet effective BBQ hack is using a basting brush to apply sauces, glazes, or marinades to your meat while it’s grilling. A basting brush allows you to evenly coat your food with your favorite BBQ sauce or glaze, giving it an extra layer of flavor.

Try basting your food about 10-15 minutes before it’s done cooking. This allows the sauce to caramelize and form a delicious, sticky coating. For an added touch, experiment with making your own BBQ sauce or glaze at home for a unique flavor profile.

9. Keep a Spray Bottle of Water Handy

Grilling over an open flame can sometimes lead to flare-ups, especially when cooking fatty meats like burgers or sausages. Flare-ups can burn your food or cause it to cook unevenly. A simple BBQ hack to prevent this is to keep a spray bottle of water near the grill. If you notice a flare-up, give the flames a quick spritz with the water to calm them down without having to move your food.

Be careful not to over-spray, as too much water can put out the coals or disturb the heat balance of your grill. Use this hack sparingly for those occasional flare-ups that can happen when grilling fatty foods.

10. Let the Meat Rest After Grilling

After all the hard work of grilling your meat, don’t forget to let it rest for a few minutes before serving. This step is crucial for ensuring that your meat stays juicy and tender. When you grill meat, the juices inside move towards the center of the meat. Allowing it to rest helps redistribute those juices back into the meat, resulting in a more flavorful bite.

For steaks, allow them to rest for about 5-10 minutes. For larger cuts like roasts or chicken, let them rest for about 15 minutes. This simple step can make a world of difference in the texture and flavor of your BBQ creations.

11. Experiment with Wood Chips for Smoky Flavor

If you love that smoky flavor that comes with grilling, why not take it up a notch by using wood chips? Wood chips are a great way to infuse your food with an extra layer of smokiness, and they can be used on both gas and charcoal grills.

Soak your wood chips in water for at least 30 minutes before using them. Once soaked, place the chips in a smoker box or wrap them in aluminum foil with holes poked in it. For charcoal grills, you can simply add the soaked chips directly to the coals. For gas grills, use a smoker box or place the chips in a foil packet, and place it directly on the burners. Wood chips come in a variety of flavors, such as hickory, applewood, or mesquite, so you can experiment with different profiles to enhance your grilled meats.

12. Grill Vegetables for an Easy and Healthy Side

Grilled vegetables are an excellent addition to any BBQ. Not only are they healthy, but they also add color and flavor to your plate. To grill vegetables, simply toss them in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and place them directly on the grill. Some vegetables, like corn on the cob, bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms, cook beautifully on the grill and take on a rich, smoky flavor.

If you have a grill basket, use it for smaller vegetables like asparagus or diced potatoes. This allows you to stir and flip the vegetables easily while still getting those nice grill marks. Grilled veggies are a great way to complement your meats and add more variety to your BBQ spread.

13. Clean Your Grill After Each Use

To maintain your grill’s performance and longevity, it’s important to clean it after every use. While this may seem like a chore, a clean grill ensures that your food cooks properly and that the flavors from previous meals don’t affect the taste of your current cookout.

After you’re done grilling, let the grill cool slightly, then use a grill brush to remove food residue from the grates. For a deeper clean, consider removing the grates and cleaning them with warm, soapy water. You can also wipe down the exterior of the grill to keep it looking new. Regular maintenance keeps your grill in top shape and ready for the next BBQ!

With these BBQ hacks, you’ll be grilling like a pro in no time. From ensuring your food doesn’t stick to the grill to trying out new techniques like the reverse sear, these tips will help you take your grilling game to the next level. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned griller, these hacks will make grilling easier, faster, and, most importantly, more delicious. Happy grilling!
