Best Easter Recipes for a Classic Family Dinner


Easter is a wonderful occasion that brings families together, offering the perfect excuse to whip up a spectacular feast full of warmth, tradition, and fresh flavors. Whether you’re hosting your loved ones or joining a family gathering, choosing the right dishes is essential to crafting a memorable meal. In this guide, you’ll find classic Easter recipes that strike a beautiful balance between timeless tradition and modern flair. From elegant mains to delectable sides and irresistible desserts, this collection ensures your Easter dinner will be a showstopper.

Main Course Ideas

The centerpiece of any Easter dinner often revolves around hearty, flavorful dishes that appeal to a variety of tastes. Here are some main courses that have stood the test of time, each bringing a unique appeal to the holiday table.

Herb-Crusted Leg of Lamb

A traditional Easter star, leg of lamb embodies celebration with its rich flavor and tender texture. This herb-crusted variant is fragrant and elegant, perfect for impressing guests while maintaining simplicity in preparation.

Ingredients:

  • 1 leg of lamb (5-6 pounds)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

Preparation:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix garlic, rosemary, thyme, mustard, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  3. Rub this herb mixture generously all over the leg of lamb.
  4. Place the lamb on a roasting rack in a pan, and roast for approximately 1 hour 30 minutes for medium rare (internal temperature 135°F), or adjust time to your preferred doneness.
  5. Let the lamb rest for 15-20 minutes before carving to retain juices.

This recipe pairs beautifully with mint sauce or a mild horseradish cream, offering that classic combo of flavors that elevate lamb.

Honey-Glazed Ham with Clove and Orange

For those who prefer a sweet glaze and a bit of zest, a honey-glazed ham infused with cloves and orange is quintessentially festive and delightfully aromatic.

Ingredients:

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (7-8 pounds)
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange
  • 20 whole cloves
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard

Preparation:

  1. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern and stud with cloves.
  2. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  3. In a saucepan, combine honey, brown sugar, orange zest, and juice, heating gently until smooth.
  4. Brush the ham with mustard, then apply half the glaze.
  5. Bake the ham in a roasting pan, basting every 20 minutes with remaining glaze, for about 2 hours or until heated through and caramelized on the outside.

Serve sliced ham with the pan juices and a side of roasted vegetables for a hearty Easter feast.

Classic Roasted Chicken with Lemon and Thyme

For a lighter alternative that’s equally satisfying, a perfectly roasted whole chicken seasoned with lemon and thyme brings rustic charm and comforting flavors to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (4-5 pounds)
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • Several sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Stuff the chicken cavity with lemon halves, thyme, and garlic.
  3. Rub the outside with olive oil, then season with salt and pepper.
  4. Roast in a roasting pan for about 1 hour 20 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Let rest for 10 minutes before carving.

The citrus and herbs infuse the chicken with bright, classic flavors that appeal to every palate.

Side Dishes

No Easter dinner is complete without a selection of satisfying sides that complement the main course and introduce fresh spring ingredients.

Creamy Scalloped Potatoes

A luxurious accompaniment, scalloped potatoes bring creamy indulgence with a golden, bubbling crust.

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds russet potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Gruyère cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a saucepan, heat cream with garlic until simmering.
  3. Layer potatoes in a buttered casserole dish, seasoning each layer with salt, pepper, and cheese.
  4. Pour warm cream over the layered potatoes.
  5. Top with additional cheese.
  6. Bake for 1 hour or until potatoes are tender and the top is golden brown.
  7. Garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

Roasted Asparagus with Lemon and Parmesan

Simple to prepare but bursting with fresh, vibrant flavor, roasted asparagus is a springtime favorite and perfectly balances richer dishes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 12-15 minutes.
  4. Remove from oven and sprinkle with lemon zest and Parmesan.
  5. Serve immediately as a crisp, delicious side.

Spring Pea and Mint Salad

A refreshing salad adds a bright, herbaceous note to the meal and celebrates the season’s best fresh peas.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas (thawed)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation:

  1. Combine peas, mint, and red onion in a bowl.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice.
  3. Toss gently, then season with salt and pepper.
  4. Chill briefly before serving.

This vibrant salad refreshes the palate and balances heavier dishes.

Desserts

Here’s where the magic culminates in delightful sweets that convey the joy and renewal of Easter.

Classic Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake’s moist texture, warm spices, and creamy frosting epitomize homemade goodness.

Ingredients (Cake):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Ingredients (Frosting):

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. Add eggs, oil, and vanilla extract; mix until smooth.
  4. Fold in grated carrots and nuts.
  5. Pour batter evenly into the pans; bake 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Cool completely on wire racks.
  7. For frosting, beat cream cheese and butter until fluffy. Gradually add powdered sugar, then vanilla.
  8. Frost the cooled cake layers and assemble.

This classic dessert delights with spiced flavors and rich texture.

Lemon Poppy Seed Bundt Cake

Citrusy, moist, and slightly nutty, this lemon poppy seed cake offers a lighter sweet option that’s ideal after a filling meal.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/3 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 3 tablespoons poppy seeds

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a Bundt pan.
  2. In a bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream butter and sugar until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, then lemon zest.
  4. Alternately add flour mixture and sour cream to the butter mixture, beginning and ending with flour.
  5. Stir in lemon juice and poppy seeds.
  6. Pour batter into Bundt pan and bake 50-60 minutes, until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  7. Let cool before serving. Optionally drizzle with a simple lemon glaze.

This cake offers a fresh, spring-forward note that’s irresistible.

Tips for a Successful Easter Dinner

  • Plan Ahead: Many parts of the meal, such as carrot cake or ham glaze, can be made a day before to save stress.
  • Balance Flavors and Textures: Combine rich mains with crisp, fresh sides to keep the meal exciting.
  • Add Personal Touches: Customize seasoning or presentation to highlight family favorites or dietary preferences.
  • Set the Mood: A beautifully set table with simple spring blooms can enhance the dining atmosphere.

Whether you opt for the robust flavors of herb-crusted lamb or the comforting taste of honey-glazed ham, paired with fresh spring sides and classic desserts, these recipes arm you with all you need for a delightful Easter family dinner. Celebrate the season with heartwarming dishes that honor tradition and bring everyone around the table together in joy.

Enjoy your cooking and happy Easter!
