Paleo Zucchini Noodle Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


In recent years, the paleo diet has gained immense popularity due to its emphasis on whole, unprocessed foods and its potential health benefits. One of the most exciting aspects of the paleo diet is the variety of creative and delicious recipes that can be made using zucchini noodles, also known as “zoodles.” In this article, we will explore 5 paleo zucchini noodle recipes that are not only grain-free but also packed with flavor and nutrients.

Recipe 1: Paleo Spaghetti Bolognese

This recipe is a classic take on the Italian staple, spaghetti bolognese. Instead of using traditional spaghetti, we’ll use zucchini noodles to create a grain-free and paleo-friendly version. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up into small pieces as it cooks.
  4. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until the onion is translucent.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, dried basil, and dried oregano to the skillet. Stir to combine.
  6. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add the zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss to combine with the sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the zoodles are tender.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot.

Recipe 2: Paleo Creamy Mushroom Zoodles

This recipe is a creamy and savory take on traditional zucchini noodles. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms (such as cremini, shiitake, and button), sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown.
  4. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes or until fragrant.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and dried thyme. Pour the cream mixture over the mushrooms and stir to combine.
  6. Add the zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss to combine with the mushroom cream sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the zoodles are tender.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot. If desired, sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese before serving.

Recipe 3: Paleo Pesto Zoodles with Cherry Tomatoes

This recipe is a refreshing and flavorful take on traditional zucchini noodles. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1/2 cup freshly made pesto sauce (see below for recipe)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Pesto Sauce Recipe:

  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the pesto sauce over medium heat.
  4. Add the cherry tomatoes to the skillet and cook until they start to release their juices.
  5. Add the zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss to combine with the pesto sauce and cherry tomatoes. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the zoodles are tender.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot. If desired, sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese before serving.

Recipe 4: Paleo Shrimp and Bacon Zoodles

This recipe is a savory and indulgent take on traditional zucchini noodles. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 6 slices of bacon, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the diced bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  4. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until pink and cooked through. Remove the shrimp from the skillet with a slotted spoon and set aside with the bacon.
  5. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional 1 minute or until fragrant.
  6. Add the unsalted butter to the skillet and stir to combine with the garlic.
  7. Add the zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss to combine with the garlic butter sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the zoodles are tender.
  8. Add the cooked shrimp and bacon back into the skillet and toss to combine with the zoodles. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot.

Recipe 5: Paleo Lemon Chicken Zoodles

This recipe is a bright and citrusy take on traditional zucchini noodles. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 medium zucchinis
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Spiralize the zucchinis into noodles and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned, about 5-6 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and cook for an additional 1 minute or until fragrant.
  5. Add the lemon juice to the skillet and stir to combine with the garlic.
  6. Add the zucchini noodles to the skillet and toss to combine with the lemon garlic sauce. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until the zoodles are tender.
  7. Add the cooked chicken back into the skillet and toss to combine with the zoodles. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot.

Conclusion

These 5 paleo zucchini noodle recipes offer a variety of delicious and grain-free options for anyone looking to incorporate more paleo-friendly meals into their diet. From classic spaghetti bolognese to creamy mushroom zoodles, there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re a seasoned paleo enthusiast or just starting out, these recipes are sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So go ahead, get creative, and start cooking up some paleo zucchini noodles today!
