Crispy Baked Coconut Shrimp: A Tropical Sensation


If you’re looking to add a taste of the tropics to your dinner table, look no further than this delicious and easy-to-make recipe for Crispy Baked Coconut Shrimp. With its golden, crunchy exterior and juicy, succulent interior, this dish is sure to transport your taste buds to a sunny beachside paradise. And the best part? It’s baked, not fried, making it a healthier alternative without sacrificing any of the flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound jumbo shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Cooking spray

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs. In a separate bowl, combine the shredded coconut, panko breadcrumbs, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper.

  3. Dip each shrimp into the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat the shrimp in the coconut breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere. Place the coated shrimp onto the prepared baking sheet.

  4. Repeat the dipping and coating process with the remaining shrimp.

  5. Lightly spray the coated shrimp with cooking spray. This will help them achieve a crispy texture while baking.

  6. Bake the shrimp for 12-15 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Be sure to flip them halfway through the cooking time to ensure even browning.

  7. Remove the shrimp from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the crust to become even crispier.

  8. Serve the Crispy Baked Coconut Shrimp with your favorite dipping sauce, such as sweet chili sauce or a tangy pineapple salsa. The combination of the crunchy shrimp and the tropical flavors of the dipping sauce is simply irresistible.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or a squeeze of lime juice to the coconut breadcrumb mixture.
  • If you prefer a more intense coconut flavor, toast the shredded coconut in a dry skillet over medium heat until golden brown before using it in the recipe.
  • Experiment with different dipping sauces to find your favorite flavor combination. Mango chutney, Thai peanut sauce, or even a zesty mango salsa would all be delicious choices.
  • Serve the Crispy Baked Coconut Shrimp as an appetizer, a main course, or even as part of a tropical-themed party spread. It’s versatile and always a crowd-pleaser.

So, the next time you’re craving a taste of the tropics, whip up a batch of Crispy Baked Coconut Shrimp. Not only is it easy to make, but it’s also a healthier alternative to the traditional fried version. With its crispy exterior and tender interior, this tropical sensation is sure to become a favorite in your household. Enjoy!
