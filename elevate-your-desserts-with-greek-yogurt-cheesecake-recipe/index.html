Elevate Your Desserts with Greek Yogurt Cheesecake Recipe


If you’re a fan of creamy, indulgent desserts but also want to keep things on the healthier side, then this Greek yogurt cheesecake recipe is just what you need. This delightful dessert combines the rich, velvety texture of traditional cheesecake with the tangy goodness of Greek yogurt, resulting in a treat that’s not only delicious but also a bit lighter than its classic counterpart. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special occasion, or simply craving a sweet indulgence, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Filling:

  • 24 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt

For the Topping (optional):

  • Fresh berries, such as strawberries, raspberries, or blueberries
  • Whipped cream

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).

  2. Prepare the Crust: In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are well coated with the butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, forming an even layer. Place the pan in the refrigerator while you prepare the filling.

  3. Make the Filling: In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy. Add the vanilla extract and mix until well combined. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Finally, gently fold in the Greek yogurt until just combined.

  4. Assemble and Bake: Pour the filling over the prepared crust in the springform pan. Smooth the top with a spatula. Place the pan on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.

  5. Chill and Serve: Once the cheesecake is done baking, turn off the oven and crack the oven door open. Allow the cheesecake to cool in the oven for 1 hour, then transfer it to the refrigerator to chill for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. When ready to serve, carefully remove the sides of the springform pan. If desired, top the cheesecake with fresh berries and a dollop of whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • For a citrusy twist, add the zest of one lemon or orange to the filling mixture.
  • Experiment with different types of crusts, such as chocolate cookie crumbs or crushed nuts.
  • To make a gluten-free version, use gluten-free graham cracker crumbs or a gluten-free cookie crust.

Final Thoughts

This Greek yogurt cheesecake is a delightful way to enjoy the lusciousness of a classic cheesecake while introducing the tangy and creamy notes of Greek yogurt. With its smooth texture and rich flavor, this dessert is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to the world of cheesecakes, this recipe is a must-try. So go ahead, elevate your desserts with this delightful Greek yogurt cheesecake, and treat yourself and your loved ones to a slice of pure indulgence!
