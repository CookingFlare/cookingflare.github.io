Creamy Homemade White BBQ Sauce


Barbecue lovers across the globe know that a great sauce can elevate a grilled dish to a whole new level of flavor. While many people are familiar with the tangy, smoky taste of traditional red barbecue sauces, there’s another sauce that’s equally deserving of the spotlight: creamy white BBQ sauce. Originating from Alabama, this unique, tangy, and creamy sauce has become a beloved staple in BBQ kitchens everywhere.

In this blog post, we’re going to show you how to make a delicious, homemade creamy white BBQ sauce that is perfect for grilling season and will have your guests coming back for more. Whether you’re looking to elevate chicken, pork, or even grilled vegetables, this sauce adds a fresh, creamy twist to any dish.

What is White BBQ Sauce?

White BBQ sauce is a southern specialty with a flavor profile that’s completely different from the more traditional tomato-based barbecue sauces. Unlike the rich red sauces packed with vinegar and tomatoes, white BBQ sauce is creamy and tangy, often made with mayonnaise, vinegar, and mustard. It’s a versatile sauce that’s not just limited to barbecue; it can also be used as a salad dressing, dipping sauce, or marinade.

This sauce originated in Alabama and is most commonly paired with grilled or smoked chicken. It’s a favorite because of its balance of sharp, acidic notes and creamy texture, making it an ideal complement to charred meats.

Ingredients for Creamy White BBQ Sauce

Creating a flavorful white BBQ sauce at home requires just a few basic ingredients that you probably already have in your pantry. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup mayonnaise: The base of the sauce, giving it its creamy consistency.
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar: This adds acidity and helps balance the richness of the mayo.
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard: Mustard gives the sauce an extra zing, contributing to the tangy flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice: Adds freshness and enhances the tanginess of the sauce.
  • 2 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce: A little umami goes a long way to deepen the sauce’s flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder: A key ingredient for that savory undertone.
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder: Adds depth and complements the garlic powder.
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper: For a little kick and seasoning.
  • 1 teaspoon salt: To bring all the flavors together.
  • Optional: 1-2 teaspoons hot sauce: If you like your white BBQ sauce with a bit of heat, add a couple of dashes of your favorite hot sauce.

Why Make Your Own White BBQ Sauce?

While store-bought versions of white BBQ sauce are readily available, homemade sauces offer far more control over flavor and consistency. By making your own, you can adjust the acidity, tanginess, and spice levels to match your preferences. Plus, making BBQ sauce at home ensures that it’s free from any unnecessary preservatives or artificial ingredients that might be lurking in pre-made bottles.

Another benefit of homemade white BBQ sauce is that it can be made ahead of time and stored in the fridge. The flavors only improve as it sits, allowing all the ingredients to meld together. This sauce is also incredibly versatile — you can use it as a dipping sauce, drizzle it over grilled meats, or toss it with vegetables.

How to Make Creamy Homemade White BBQ Sauce

Step 1: Gather Your Ingredients

Before you start, make sure all your ingredients are on hand. This recipe is incredibly simple and doesn’t require any cooking, so having everything pre-measured and ready to go makes the process even easier.

Step 2: Mix the Base Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, combine the mayonnaise, apple cider vinegar, and Dijon mustard. Whisk them together until the mixture is smooth and well-combined. The mayonnaise provides the creamy base, while the vinegar and mustard bring acidity and tang to the sauce.

Step 3: Add Seasonings

Next, add the garlic powder, onion powder, Worcestershire sauce, lemon juice, and black pepper to the bowl. Continue whisking until everything is well-blended. Taste the sauce at this point and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you want it tangier, feel free to add a little more vinegar or lemon juice. For more depth, a bit more Worcestershire sauce will do the trick.

Step 4: Add Optional Heat

If you prefer a bit of heat in your white BBQ sauce, this is the time to add hot sauce. Start with one teaspoon, mix it in, and taste it. Add more if you like your sauce on the spicier side. If you’re cooking for a crowd with varying spice preferences, it might be best to leave the hot sauce out and let everyone add it individually to their taste.

Step 5: Chill the Sauce

Once the ingredients are thoroughly mixed, cover the bowl and place it in the refrigerator for at least an hour. Chilling the sauce allows the flavors to meld together and become even more delicious. If you have time, making the sauce the night before is a great option, as it will have plenty of time to develop flavor.

Tips for the Perfect White BBQ Sauce

  1. Adjust the Consistency: If you find the sauce too thick, you can thin it out with a little water or additional vinegar, depending on your flavor preferences. Thin it just enough to drizzle over your grilled meats, but don’t make it too runny.

  2. Customize the Flavor: Don’t be afraid to adjust the recipe to suit your taste. You can add a bit more mustard if you prefer a sharper sauce or more lemon juice for an extra tang. Experiment with different types of mustard for unique flavor twists.

  3. Add Fresh Herbs: For a more complex flavor, add finely chopped fresh herbs like parsley, chives, or thyme. Fresh herbs give the sauce a burst of color and added freshness.

  4. Storage: Store your homemade white BBQ sauce in an airtight container in the fridge for up to a week. Be sure to give it a good stir before using, as some separation may occur over time.

How to Use White BBQ Sauce

While white BBQ sauce is traditionally paired with grilled or smoked chicken, it’s a highly versatile condiment that can be used in a variety of ways. Here are a few ideas for how to incorporate this creamy sauce into your meals:

1. Grilled Chicken

The most classic pairing for white BBQ sauce is grilled chicken. Whether you’re grilling wings, thighs, or a whole chicken, this sauce is the perfect complement. Brush it on the chicken while it’s cooking, or serve it as a dipping sauce on the side.

2. Pulled Pork

Pulled pork sandwiches are delicious with a dollop of creamy white BBQ sauce. The tangy sauce cuts through the richness of the pork, creating a balanced and satisfying bite.

3. Grilled Vegetables

If you’re grilling vegetables, especially hearty ones like cauliflower, zucchini, or mushrooms, try using white BBQ sauce as a marinade or a finishing drizzle. It adds flavor and richness to your vegetables.

4. As a Salad Dressing

Transform your creamy white BBQ sauce into a tangy dressing by thinning it out with a bit of water or buttermilk. Toss it with fresh greens, grilled chicken, or even a potato salad for a unique take on your usual salad dressings.

5. Dipping Sauce for Fries or Onion Rings

Use the sauce as a creamy dip for crispy fries, onion rings, or even fried chicken tenders. The tangy, savory flavor is the perfect dipper for all your crispy snacks.

Conclusion

Homemade white BBQ sauce is a game-changer when it comes to grilling and outdoor cooking. With its creamy, tangy, and slightly spicy flavor, it complements a variety of meats, vegetables, and sides. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, cooking for the family, or just looking to switch up your usual condiment game, this sauce is a must-try.

By making this white BBQ sauce at home, you can control the flavor, customize it to your liking, and avoid unnecessary preservatives found in store-bought options. With just a few ingredients, you’ll have a versatile, flavorful sauce that’s sure to become a favorite in your cooking repertoire. So, fire up the grill, and get ready to enjoy the best creamy homemade white BBQ sauce you’ve ever tasted!
