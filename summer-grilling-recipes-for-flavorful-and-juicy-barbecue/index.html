Summer Grilling: Recipes for Flavorful and Juicy Barbecue


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy some delicious barbecue with family and friends. As the weather heats up, the smell of sizzling meat and savory spices wafts through the air, tantalizing everyone’s taste buds. But with so many barbecue recipes out there, it can be overwhelming to decide which ones to try. In this post, we’ll explore some mouth-watering summer grilling recipes that will elevate your barbecue game and impress your loved ones.

Classic BBQ Favorites

Before diving into some innovative recipes, let’s start with some classic BBQ favorites that never go out of style.

Grilled Ribeye Steaks with Garlic Butter

  • 1.5 lbs ribeye steaks
  • 1/4 cup garlic butter (softened)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Season the ribeye steaks with paprika, salt, and pepper. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Meanwhile, mix the garlic butter with a fork until it’s smooth. Brush the garlic butter onto the steaks during the last minute of grilling. Let the steaks rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving.

Smoky BBQ Pulled Pork

  • 2 lbs boneless pork shoulder
  • 1/4 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

Preheat your grill to low heat. Season the pork shoulder with smoked paprika, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. Place the pork shoulder on the grill and close the lid. Smoke for 8-10 hours, or until the pork is tender and falls apart easily. Shred the pork with two forks and toss with BBQ sauce.

Global Inspirations

Now that we’ve covered some classic BBQ favorites, let’s explore some global inspirations to add some excitement to your summer grilling.

Korean-Style BBQ Beef Tacos

  • 1 lb beef (such as flank steak or skirt steak)
  • 1/4 cup Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 8 tacos shells
  • Sliced green onions, cilantro, and kimchi (optional)

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Marinate the beef in a mixture of Gochujang, soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic powder, and sesame oil for at least 30 minutes. Grill the beef for 3-4 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired level of doneness. Slice the beef thinly and serve in tacos shells with your choice of toppings.

Indian-Style BBQ Chicken Tikka

  • 1 lb boneless chicken breast or thighs
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (optional)

Preheat your grill to medium heat. Marinate the chicken in a mixture of yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper for at least 30 minutes. Grill the chicken for 5-6 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Serve with chopped cilantro, if desired.

Refreshing Sides

No BBQ is complete without some delicious sides to complement the main event. Here are some refreshing summer sides that pair perfectly with your grilled favorites.

Cucumber and Feta Salad

  • 2 large cucumbers, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Combine the cucumber slices, feta cheese, and chopped dill in a bowl. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Grilled Corn with Herbs

  • 4 ears of corn, husked and silked
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat your grill to medium heat. Brush the corn with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, parsley, and basil. Grill for 10-12 minutes, turning every 2-3 minutes, or until the corn is lightly charred and tender.

Conclusion

Summer grilling is all about experimenting with new flavors and techniques to create unforgettable BBQ experiences. Whether you’re a classic BBQ fan or looking to try something new, these recipes will inspire you to fire up the grill and fire up your taste buds. So go ahead, get grilling, and make this summer one to remember!
