Pennsylvania Dutch Shoofly Pie Recipe


Shoofly pie is an iconic dessert that hails from the Pennsylvania Dutch community, bringing a rich history and delightful flavor to the table. This molasses-based pie is beloved for its sweet, slightly tangy filling, complemented by a crumbly topping that creates the perfect balance of textures. Whether you’re preparing it for a family gathering, a holiday feast, or just craving something sweet, Pennsylvania Dutch Shoofly Pie will undoubtedly be a crowd-pleaser.

In this post, we’ll explore the origins of Shoofly Pie, walk you through the steps to make this delicious pie from scratch, and offer tips for ensuring your pie turns out perfectly every time.

The Origins of Shoofly Pie

Shoofly Pie has deep roots in the Amish and Mennonite communities of Pennsylvania, dating back to the 19th century. It is believed to have been made by the early settlers who combined simple, locally available ingredients like molasses, flour, and sugar into a flavorful dessert. The name “shoofly” is thought to refer to the sweet molasses filling attracting flies, with the “shoof” (a colloquial term for “shoo”) suggesting a playful effort to keep them away.

While the pie may have originated in the Pennsylvania Dutch region, it has spread throughout the United States and remains a beloved part of Pennsylvania Dutch cuisine. Over time, variations of the traditional recipe have emerged, but the essence of the pie — its molasses filling and crumbly, buttery topping — has remained unchanged.

Ingredients for Pennsylvania Dutch Shoofly Pie

To create a traditional Shoofly Pie, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Pie Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small cubes
  • 3-4 tablespoons cold water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup molasses (unsulfured)
  • 3/4 cup hot water
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves

For the Crumb Topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Shoofly Pie

Preparing the Pie Crust

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: Start by mixing the flour and salt in a large bowl. This forms the base for your pie crust.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs. The key is to keep the butter cold, which will give the crust a flakey texture once baked.

  3. Add Cold Water: Gradually add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough begins to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough — just mix it enough to form a cohesive ball.

  4. Chill the Dough: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and place it in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough helps prevent shrinkage during baking and makes rolling it out much easier.

  5. Roll Out the Dough: After chilling, roll the dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Press the dough into the pie dish, trimming any excess along the edges. If desired, crimp the edges of the crust for a decorative touch. Set aside while you prepare the filling and crumb topping.

Making the Shoofly Pie Filling

  1. Dissolve Baking Soda in Water: In a small bowl, dissolve the baking soda in hot water. Stir well and set aside. The baking soda helps to create a slightly lighter texture in the filling.

  2. Combine Molasses, Egg, and Spices: In a separate large bowl, whisk together the molasses, egg, vanilla extract, cinnamon, ginger, and cloves. This forms the sweet and aromatic base of your pie’s filling.

  3. Add the Baking Soda Mixture: Pour the dissolved baking soda and water mixture into the molasses mixture. Stir until the ingredients are well combined. This mixture will become the rich filling for your Shoofly Pie.

Preparing the Crumb Topping

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon for the topping.

  2. Cut in the Butter: Add the softened butter to the dry ingredients and, using a fork or pastry cutter, cut the butter into the mixture until it forms small, pea-sized crumbs. The butter should be well incorporated but still create a crumbly texture that will top the pie.

Assembling the Pie

  1. Layer the Filling and Topping: Pour the molasses filling into the prepared pie crust, spreading it out evenly. Then, sprinkle the crumb topping generously over the filling, ensuring it covers the entire surface.

  2. Bake the Pie: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the pie for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. The center of the pie should be firm, but slightly jiggle when gently shaken. If the top begins to brown too quickly, you can cover it with aluminum foil to prevent over-browning.

  3. Cool and Serve: Once the pie is baked, allow it to cool completely on a wire rack. This helps the filling to firm up and makes it easier to slice. Shoofly pie can be served warm or at room temperature, but many people find it tastes best after it has been allowed to cool and set.

Tips for Perfect Shoofly Pie

  • Chill the Dough: Make sure your dough is chilled before rolling it out. This not only helps the crust hold its shape during baking but also results in a flakier texture.

  • Use Unsulfured Molasses: For the best flavor, opt for unsulfured molasses. It’s sweeter and less bitter than sulfured molasses, which results in a smoother, more balanced flavor in the pie filling.

  • Adjust the Spice Levels: While cinnamon, ginger, and cloves are traditional in Shoofly Pie, feel free to adjust the spice amounts to suit your preferences. Some people like a more intense flavor, while others prefer a milder touch.

  • Keep the Crumb Topping Chunky: The crumb topping is the star of the show, so don’t worry about making it too fine. The larger crumbs will bake into a beautiful, buttery, crunchy layer that contrasts perfectly with the soft, sweet filling.

  • Don’t Overbake: Keep a close eye on the pie as it bakes. If you overbake it, the filling can become too firm or dry. The perfect Shoofly Pie should have a slightly soft center that’s firm enough to hold its shape when sliced.

Variations of Pennsylvania Dutch Shoofly Pie

While the classic Shoofly Pie is a favorite, there are some variations that you might want to try. Here are a few ideas to put your spin on this traditional dessert:

1. Sour Cream Shoofly Pie

Adding sour cream to the filling mixture creates a richer, slightly tangy flavor that balances out the sweetness of the molasses. This variation is popular among some Pennsylvania Dutch families, offering a unique twist on the classic recipe.

2. Creamy Shoofly Pie

If you prefer a creamier filling, you can add a small amount of heavy cream or evaporated milk to the molasses mixture. This creates a smooth, custard-like filling that still retains the traditional molasses flavor.

3. Adding Nuts

Some versions of Shoofly Pie include chopped walnuts or pecans in the crumb topping. The nuts add a pleasant crunch and depth of flavor to the pie, making it even more satisfying.

4. Using a Pre-made Crust

For a shortcut, you can use a pre-made pie crust to save time. While the homemade crust offers the best flavor and texture, a store-bought crust can still make a great base if you’re in a pinch.

Serving Shoofly Pie

Shoofly Pie is perfect for a variety of occasions. It pairs wonderfully with a hot cup of coffee or tea and makes a delicious dessert for any gathering. Here are a few ideas for serving your Shoofly Pie:

  • With Whipped Cream or Ice Cream: A dollop of fresh whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream makes for a decadent treat when served with Shoofly Pie. The richness of the cream complements the pie’s molasses filling beautifully.

  • As a Breakfast Treat: In some Pennsylvania Dutch homes, Shoofly Pie is enjoyed as a breakfast or brunch item. Its sweet, comforting nature makes it a perfect addition to a morning spread.

  • With a Glass of Milk: Shoofly Pie pairs wonderfully with a cold glass of milk, creating the ultimate indulgent experience.

Conclusion

Shoofly Pie is more than just a dessert — it’s a piece of Pennsylvania Dutch history, and it’s a delightful treat that brings people together. With its rich molasses filling, buttery crumb topping, and flakey pie crust, it’s no wonder that this beloved dessert has stood the test of time. By following this simple yet delicious recipe, you can create your very own Pennsylvania Dutch Shoofly Pie and share a taste of tradition with family and friends.

So, next time you’re looking to bake something truly special, reach for the molasses and flour, and enjoy the warm, comforting flavors of Shoofly Pie. Happy baking!
