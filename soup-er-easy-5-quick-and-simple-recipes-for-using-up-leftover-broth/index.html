Soup-er Easy: 5 Quick and Simple Recipes for Using Up Leftover Broth


Are you tired of letting that leftover broth go to waste? Well, worry no more! In this blog post, we have gathered five quick and simple recipes that will help you make the most out of that delicious broth. From comforting soups to flavorful stews, these recipes are guaranteed to satisfy your taste buds and make your leftovers shine.

1. Hearty Vegetable Soup

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 30 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup canned diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the leftover broth over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, sliced carrots, chopped celery, and diced zucchini. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the canned diced tomatoes and frozen peas. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Simmer for another 10 minutes, or until all the flavors have melded together.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Chicken and Rice Casserole

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 35 minutes Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover broth
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the leftover broth, cooked chicken, cooked rice, and frozen mixed vegetables.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese on top.
  4. Bake for 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

3. Beef and Barley Stew

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 1 hour 30 minutes Serves: 8

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups leftover broth
  • 1 pound beef stew meat, cubed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 cup pearl barley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, brown the beef stew meat over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, sliced carrots, chopped celery, minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the leftover broth and pearl barley. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1 hour, or until the beef is tender and the barley is cooked.
  4. Serve hot and enjoy the rich flavors of this hearty stew.

4. Creamy Mushroom Risotto

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 30 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 pound mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • ½ cup white wine
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion and sliced mushrooms. Cook until the mushrooms are golden brown and the onion is translucent, about 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for another 2 minutes, or until the rice is well coated.
  4. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer until it evaporates.
  5. Gradually add the leftover broth, ½ cup at a time, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed before adding more.
  6. Continue this process until the rice is tender and creamy, about 20 minutes.
  7. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve hot and savor the rich and comforting flavors of this creamy mushroom risotto.

5. Spicy Tomato and Lentil Soup

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 40 minutes Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups leftover broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion, diced carrots, and chopped celery until they are tender, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the red lentils, diced tomatoes, ground cumin, paprika, cayenne pepper (if desired), salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine.
  3. Pour in the leftover broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 30 minutes, or until the lentils are cooked and the flavors have melded together.
  4. Serve hot and enjoy the spicy kick of this comforting tomato and lentil soup.

There you have it - five quick and simple recipes to make the most out of your leftover broth. These dishes are not only delicious but also a great way to reduce food waste and save money. Give them a try and let us know which one becomes your new favorite. Enjoy cooking and happy eating!
