Elegant and Easy: Spanish Marinated Olives


If you’re looking for a simple yet elegant appetizer that will impress your guests, look no further than Spanish marinated olives. This classic dish is not only easy to make but also a versatile addition to any gathering, from casual get-togethers to formal dinner parties. The beauty of marinated olives lies in their simplicity and the depth of flavor they bring to the table. Here’s a delightful recipe that will elevate your entertaining game and leave your guests asking for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups assorted olives (such as Manzanilla, Gordal, or Arbequina)
  • 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 2-3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2-3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Olives: Rinse the olives under cold water to remove excess brine or oil. If using olives with pits, carefully pit them using a sharp knife.

  2. Infuse the Olive Oil: In a small saucepan, combine the extra virgin olive oil, garlic, lemon zest, orange zest, fennel seeds, coriander seeds, red pepper flakes, thyme, and rosemary. Warm the mixture over low heat for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to infuse into the oil. Be careful not to let the oil come to a boil.

  3. Marinate the Olives: Place the prepared olives in a glass jar or airtight container. Pour the infused oil mixture over the olives, ensuring they are completely submerged. Seal the jar or container and gently shake to distribute the flavors.

  4. Let the Flavors Develop: Allow the olives to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours to allow the flavors to fully develop. For best results, let them marinate for 2-3 days, shaking the jar occasionally to redistribute the marinade.

  5. Serve and Enjoy: When ready to serve, bring the marinated olives to room temperature. Transfer them to a serving bowl or dish, allowing the fragrant oil to accompany the olives. Garnish with fresh herbs or citrus zest if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to create your own signature blend of marinated olives.
  • Add a splash of balsamic vinegar to the marinade for a slightly tangy twist.
  • Serve the marinated olives alongside a selection of cured meats, cheeses, and crusty bread for a delightful antipasto platter.

The Art of Entertaining with Spanish Marinated Olives

Spanish marinated olives are not just a tasty treat; they are a conversation starter, a symbol of hospitality, and a reflection of the art of Mediterranean entertaining. The rich, briny flavor of the olives combined with the aromatic infusion of citrus, herbs, and spices creates a sensory experience that is both sophisticated and approachable.

When serving marinated olives, consider pairing them with a crisp white wine or a refreshing Spanish cava to enhance the flavors and elevate the overall dining experience. The simplicity of this dish allows the quality of the ingredients to shine, making it a perfect addition to a tapas spread or a charcuterie board.

Whether you’re hosting a summertime soiree, a cozy winter gathering, or a casual Sunday brunch, Spanish marinated olives are a timeless crowd-pleaser that will leave a lasting impression on your guests. The beauty of this dish lies in its ability to effortlessly elevate any occasion, turning a simple gathering into a memorable culinary experience.

So, the next time you’re planning a gathering, consider adding a touch of elegance and flavor with Spanish marinated olives. With just a few simple ingredients and a bit of patience, you can create a delightful appetizer that embodies the spirit of Mediterranean hospitality and the art of effortless entertaining. Cheers to good food, good company, and the simple pleasures that bring us together!
