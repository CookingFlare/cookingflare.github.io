Classic German Dishes: 6 Hearty Recipes for Oktoberfest


As the leaves change colors and the air grows crisp, Germans everywhere begin to prepare for their favorite celebration: Oktoberfest. For five weeks, Germany's breweries and taverns, or Weinstuben, transform into vibrant beer halls, filled with the sounds of laughter, music, and the clinking of steins. And at the heart of this festive atmosphere are the classic German dishes that have been delighting locals and visitors alike for centuries. In this post, we'll explore six traditional German recipes that are sure to become staples at any Oktoberfest celebration. From hearty stews to rich, comforting casseroles, these classic dishes showcase the rich culinary heritage of Germany's various regions.

  1. Sauerbraten mit Kartoffeln und Rotkohl (Pot Roast with Potatoes and Red Cabbage) Sauerbraten, a classic pot roast from southwest Germany, has been a staple in German cuisine for over 700 years. This slow-cooked beef dish is marinated in a mixture of vinegar, water, and spices, resulting in tender, flavorful meat that falls apart with ease. Serve with boiled potatoes and red cabbage, a traditional accompaniment, for a satisfying, comforting meal. Ingredients:

3 lbs beef pot roast 1 cup vinegar 1 cup water 2 tbsp brown sugar 1 tsp salt 1 tsp black pepper 2 carrots 2 potatoes 1 large red cabbage

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine beef, vinegar, water, brown sugar, salt, and pepper. Mix well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Remove from refrigerator and let come to room temperature. Preheat oven to 300°F (150°C). Transfer beef to a large Dutch oven and add carrots and potatoes. Roast, covered, for 2 1/2 to 3 hours or until meat is tender. Remove from oven and stir in chopped red cabbage. Serve hot, garnished with parsley.

  1. Kartoffelpuffer mit Apfelmus (Potato Pancakes with Apple Sauce) In eastern Germany, a region known as Ostpreussen, lies a hidden gem of German cuisine: Kartoffelpuffer, or potato pancakes. These crispy, golden brown treats are a staple side dish during the Oktoberfest season. Serve them with a side of sweet, tangy Apfelmus, or apple sauce, for a delightful contrast in flavors. 2 large potatoes 1 onion, finely chopped 1 egg 1/4 cup all-purpose flour Vegetable oil, for frying Salt, to taste Apple sauce, for serving

Instructions:

Peel and grate potatoes. Squeeze out excess moisture and transfer to a large bowl. Add onion, egg, and flour. Mix well. Using a 1/4 cup measuring cup, scoop mixture into patties. Schweinshaxe mit Kartoffeln und Rotkohl (Pork Knuckle with Potatoes and Red Cabbage) In Germany's south, particularly in the state of Bavaria, pork knuckle, or Schweinshaxe, is a celebratory dish that brings people together during Oktoberfest. This hearty, comforting dish is sure to become a favorite among your family and friends. Ingredients:

2 pork knuckles 1 cup potatoes, boiled 1 large red cabbage, chopped 2 tbsp brown sugar 1 tsp salt 1 tsp black pepper

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Rinse pork knuckles and pat dry with paper towels. Rub skin with salt, pepper, and brown sugar. Bake, skin side up, for 20-25 minutes or until crispy. Remove from oven and let rest for 5 minutes. Serve with boiled potatoes and chopped red cabbage.

  1. Eintopf mit Käse und Kräutern (Cheesy Soup with Herbs) On chilly fall evenings, there's nothing more comforting than a warm bowl of cheese soup. This simple, yet satisfying dish, known as Eintopf, is a staple in many German households, especially during Oktoberfest. Ingredients:

2 cups chicken broth 1 cup grated cheddar cheese 1 tsp dried thyme 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional) Salt and pepper, to taste Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

In a large pot, combine chicken broth, cheddar cheese, and thyme. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Reduce heat to low and let cook, stirring occasionally, for 10-15 minutes or until cheese is melted. Stir in heavy cream, if using. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley, if desired.

  1. Spätzle mit Zwiebeln und Paprika (Spätzle with Onions and Paprika) Spätzle, a traditional German noodle, is a staple in many German households, especially during Oktoberfest. This simple dish is a must-make during the festive season. Ingredients:

1 cup spätzle noodles 2 tbsp butter 1 large onion, finely chopped 1 large paprika pepper Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spätzle noodles, 3-5 minutes or until tender. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add paprika pepper and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until slightly softened. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  1. Apfelstrudel mit Vanilleeis (Apple Strudel with Vanilla Ice Cream) For a sweet and satisfying finish to a festive meal, try making your own Apple Strudel. This classic German dessert is sure to impress your friends and family. Ingredients:

2 cups apples, peeled and sliced 1/2 cup sugar 2 tbsp flour 1 package puff pastry 1 tsp vanilla 1 cup vanilla ice cream

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, sugar, and flour. Mix well. Roll out puff pastry and spread apple mixture evenly over the dough. Roll up dough, starting from the long side. Brush with vanilla and place on a baking sheet. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown. Let cool slightly. Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.
