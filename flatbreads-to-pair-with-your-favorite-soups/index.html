Flatbreads to Pair with Your Favorite Soups


When the weather turns chilly and the desire for a comforting bowl of soup arises, nothing complements it better than a warm, hearty flatbread. Whether you’re enjoying a velvety tomato bisque, a savory lentil soup, or a spicy bowl of Moroccan harira, the addition of flatbread can elevate the entire dining experience. Flatbreads, with their soft, pillowy texture or crispy, slightly charred surface, provide a perfect balance of flavor and texture to complement any soup. Let’s explore various types of flatbreads that pair wonderfully with your favorite soups and offer tips on how to make them at home.

1. Classic Indian Naan Bread

Naan is a beloved flatbread from India that is traditionally cooked in a tandoor, giving it a soft and slightly smoky flavor. Its subtle buttery taste and pillowy texture make it an ideal partner for a wide range of soups, particularly those with rich, spiced broths like dal or butter chicken soup. The best part about naan is its versatility; it can be flavored with garlic, herbs, or even stuffed with cheese, making it a perfect match for the deep, complex flavors found in many Indian-inspired soups.

How to Make Naan at Home

Making naan at home might sound intimidating, but it’s actually quite simple. You’ll need all-purpose flour, yogurt, warm water, baking powder, and a bit of sugar. The dough is mixed and allowed to rest until it’s soft and pliable. Once rested, divide it into small balls and roll them out into oval shapes. They’re then cooked on a hot griddle or cast-iron skillet, and brushed with butter for a soft, delicious finish.

Soup Pairings:

  • Dal Tadka: The spiced yellow lentil soup pairs wonderfully with naan, as the bread helps soak up the flavorful dal.
  • Chicken Korma Soup: The richness of the chicken korma’s creamy, coconut-based broth is balanced perfectly with naan’s airy texture.

2. Turkish Pide

Pide is a Turkish flatbread that’s often referred to as Turkish pizza due to its boat-like shape and varied toppings. While it can be enjoyed as a meal on its own, its mild, soft texture is an excellent complement to soups, particularly those with Mediterranean influences. The dough is made with flour, water, salt, and yeast, which gives it a slightly chewy texture with a crisp outer crust when baked.

How to Make Pide at Home

To make pide, begin by preparing the dough with a combination of warm water, yeast, and flour. Let the dough rise until it’s doubled in size. After dividing it into portions, roll each piece out into an oval shape and fold the edges slightly to form a boat shape. Top with your favorite ingredients like feta cheese, olives, or minced lamb before baking until golden brown.

Soup Pairings:

  • Lentil Soup: The mild, earthy flavors of Turkish lentil soup are enhanced by the texture of pide, making each bite satisfying.
  • Tomato and Red Pepper Soup: Pide’s soft, chewy texture pairs beautifully with the acidity and richness of tomato-based soups.

3. Middle Eastern Khubz

Khubz is a staple flatbread in many Middle Eastern countries, and its round, slightly puffed shape makes it perfect for dipping. Unlike naan, khubz is more airy and light, offering a gentle chew. It’s traditionally baked in a very hot oven, which helps it puff up perfectly. Khubz’s subtle flavor makes it ideal for soups that are delicate or feature vegetables as the star ingredient.

How to Make Khubz at Home

The dough for khubz is fairly simple, made with flour, water, yeast, and a pinch of salt. The dough is allowed to rise and then shaped into small rounds. After being placed on a hot, preheated surface (like a baking stone or a hot oven), the bread bakes quickly, puffing up as it cooks. Serve fresh out of the oven for the best texture.

Soup Pairings:

  • Vegetable Soup: The mild flavor of khubz is a perfect match for vegetable soups like minestrone or a simple roasted carrot and ginger soup.
  • Chicken Shawarma Soup: The tender, spiced chicken in this soup works wonderfully with khubz, as the bread can easily scoop up both the broth and the tender pieces of chicken.

4. Mediterranean Flatbread (Laffa)

Laffa, a soft and slightly chewy flatbread, hails from the Levantine region and is known for its deliciously soft texture. It’s perfect for wrapping around fresh ingredients, but it also serves as a fantastic companion to soups, especially those with Mediterranean flavors. Laffa can be slightly thicker than some other flatbreads, allowing it to hold up to denser soups while still remaining soft.

How to Make Laffa at Home

Laffa dough is made with flour, salt, water, and a touch of olive oil. The dough is mixed, rested, and rolled out into thick rounds. It’s then baked on a hot, flat surface like a griddle or baking stone to achieve the right balance of chew and tenderness. Laffa is often served warm with a light brush of olive oil or za’atar for extra flavor.

Soup Pairings:

  • Harira: This rich Moroccan soup with chickpeas, tomatoes, and lamb pairs beautifully with laffa, as the bread helps scoop up the hearty, thick soup.
  • Fattoush Soup: A light and tangy Lebanese soup made with vegetables and lemon, laffa’s dense yet soft texture complements the fresh, vibrant ingredients.

5. Rustic Focaccia

Focaccia, a popular flatbread in Italy, is known for its fluffy interior and crispy, olive oil-drenched crust. It can be flavored with rosemary, garlic, or other herbs, making it a fantastic pairing for heartier soups. Focaccia’s texture holds up well to soups that are chunky or include a lot of vegetables, like minestrone or Italian wedding soup.

How to Make Focaccia at Home

Making focaccia requires a bit more patience, but the result is well worth it. Begin by making a dough with flour, yeast, salt, and olive oil. After kneading and allowing it to rise, the dough is spread out in a baking pan. It’s then dimpled with your fingers and topped with olive oil, salt, and any herbs or toppings you desire. Bake until golden brown, and serve it warm.

Soup Pairings:

  • Minestrone: The hearty, vegetable-packed soup is perfectly complemented by the rich, herby flavors of focaccia.
  • Italian Wedding Soup: The light meatballs and spinach in this soup pair beautifully with focaccia, which can be used to soak up the broth.

6. Simple Flatbread (Anadama)

Anadama bread is a New England classic made with cornmeal, giving it a distinct flavor and texture. Its slightly sweet, crumbly exterior and dense, soft center make it an excellent choice to pair with soups that have a rich or creamy base, such as potato leek soup or clam chowder.

How to Make Anadama Bread at Home

To make anadama bread, mix cornmeal with flour, salt, yeast, and a bit of molasses. Let the dough rise until it’s doubled in size. It’s then shaped into rounds and baked until golden brown. The cornmeal gives it a unique texture, which makes it stand out from traditional flatbreads.

Soup Pairings:

  • Clam Chowder: The slight sweetness and dense texture of anadama bread are perfect for dipping into creamy clam chowder.
  • Potato Leek Soup: The hearty and comforting flavors of potato leek soup are well complemented by the subtle sweetness of anadama bread.

7. Afghan Bolani

Bolani is a stuffed flatbread popular in Afghan cuisine. It’s typically filled with a variety of vegetables or potatoes, creating a delightful savory treat that complements the spices and textures of soups. Bolani is often pan-fried, which adds a crispy outer layer to the soft, flavorful filling inside.

How to Make Bolani at Home

The dough for bolani is made with flour, water, and a pinch of salt. The filling, which could include mashed potatoes, spinach, leeks, or pumpkin, is placed between two thin layers of dough. Once assembled, the bolani is fried in a hot pan until golden and crispy on both sides.

Soup Pairings:

  • Pumpkin Soup: The earthy, sweet flavor of pumpkin soup pairs beautifully with the savory pumpkin filling of bolani.
  • Spicy Lamb Soup: The crispy, savory bolani can be a perfect complement to a hearty, spicy lamb soup.

Conclusion

Flatbreads are more than just an accompaniment to your favorite soups; they bring an added dimension of texture, flavor, and tradition to the table. Whether you prefer the light, fluffy textures of naan, the chewy softness of laffa, or the crispy, herby goodness of focaccia, there’s a flatbread to suit every type of soup. Experiment with different combinations, and enjoy the satisfying feeling that comes from dipping a warm, flavorful flatbread into a bowl of your favorite soup. The possibilities are endless, and the results are always comforting and delicious. So next time you’re in the mood for soup, don’t forget to bake up a fresh batch of flatbread to go with it!
