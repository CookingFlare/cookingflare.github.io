How to Grill Zucchini and Summer Squash


When summer arrives, the bounty of fresh, vibrant vegetables beckons us to embrace outdoor cooking. Among the most delightful and versatile are zucchini and summer squash. Their mild flavor, satisfying texture, and adaptability make them perfect candidates for grilling. Whether you’re a seasoned grill master or just starting out, learning how to grill zucchini and summer squash perfectly can elevate your meals and add a smoky, caramelized twist to your vegetable repertoire.

In this detailed guide, you’ll uncover everything you need to know—from selecting the best produce, prepping your veggies, mastering different grilling techniques, to experimenting with flavors and serving ideas. Let’s dig into the nuances of grilling these summer staples.

Choosing the Best Zucchini and Summer Squash

The journey to the perfect grilled vegetable begins at the market or garden. Selecting fresh, firm zucchini and summer squash guarantees the best texture and flavor after grilling.

  • Size matters: Opt for medium-sized squash, about 6-8 inches long. These tend to be tender with fewer seeds. Oversized squash often have a tougher skin and spongier flesh, which can affect grilling results.
  • Look for blemish-free skin: Smooth, glossy skin without bruises or discoloration indicates freshness.
  • Check firmness: The squash should feel firm to the touch, not soft or mushy.
  • Varieties: While green zucchini and yellow summer squash are the most common, pattypan squash or other heirloom varieties also grill beautifully.

Preparing Zucchini and Summer Squash for Grilling

How you prepare the squash influences both cooking time and flavor absorption.

1. Washing and Drying

After washing your squash thoroughly under cold running water, pat them dry using a clean towel. Dry squash will hold oil and seasoning better, preventing sticking on the grill.

2. Cutting Techniques

There is no one “correct” way to cut your squash for grilling; it varies depending on the final texture and presentation you want.

  • Long slices or planks: Cutting lengthwise into ¼-inch thick slices creates a classic look with grill marks and tender flesh.
  • Rounds: Cutting into ½-inch thick rounds makes for bite-sized pieces perfect for skewers or mixing into salads.
  • Halves or quarters: Leaving the squash in larger chunks allows a slightly smoky, juicy bite but requires longer cooking.
  • Batons: Thick sticks (about ½ inch by ½ inch) work great for grilling and dipping.

Whichever cut you choose, aim for uniform thickness to ensure even cooking.

3. Salting (Optional)

Sprinkling salt on cut zucchini and squash and letting them rest for 10-15 minutes draws out excess moisture. Afterward, wipe off the moisture with a paper towel. This step helps prevent sogginess and enhances caramelization on the grill.

Seasoning and Marinating: Elevate Your Flavor Profile

Plain grilled zucchini can be lovely, but the right seasoning or marinade can transform the taste entirely.

Simple seasoning ideas:

  • Olive oil, salt, and black pepper — a timeless classic.
  • Garlic powder or minced fresh garlic for an aromatic burst.
  • Smoked paprika or chili flakes for heat and smokiness.
  • Fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, or oregano to complement the summer vibes.
  • Lemon zest or a squeeze of fresh lemon to add brightness.

Marinating tips:

A good marinade helps tenderize squash and infuses it with flavor. Because zucchini and summer squash absorb liquid quickly, a short marinade time (15-30 minutes) is sufficient.

Try these marinade blends:

  • Mediterranean: Olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, and black pepper.
  • Asian-inspired: Soy sauce, sesame oil, ginger, and a touch of honey.
  • Herbaceous: Olive oil, balsamic vinegar, fresh rosemary, and thyme.

Avoid soaking for too long to prevent mushiness.

Preparing the Grill for Perfect Results

Grilling zucchini and summer squash calls for a properly prepared grill to achieve those signature char marks and prevent sticking.

1. Clean the grill grates

Use a grill brush to scrape off any residue from previous cooking sessions.

2. Preheat the grill

Aim for medium-high heat (around 400°F / 200°C). This temperature cooks the squash quickly, caramelizing the exterior while keeping inside tender.

3. Oil the grill grates

Lightly brush the grill racks with high-smoke-point oil (like canola or grapeseed) using a folded paper towel and tongs. This step helps foods release naturally and avoid sticking.

Grilling Techniques for Zucchini and Summer Squash

Now it’s time to bring your prepped, seasoned squash to the fire. The approach depends on the thickness of your cuts and your preferred textural outcome.

Direct grilling

Place the squash slices or chunks directly over the heat source. Grill for about 3-5 minutes per side, until tender and charred. Frequent turning is not necessary; flipping once or twice will do.

Indirect grilling

For larger or stuffed squash pieces, use indirect heat by placing them on the cooler part of the grill. This gentler heat prevents burning while allowing thorough cooking.

Skewers

Thread rounds or batons onto metal or soaked wooden skewers for easy handling. Grill over direct heat, rotating every few minutes until cooked through.

Tips to Avoid Common Grilling Mistakes

  • Overcooking: Squash can quickly become mushy if left too long. Keep an eye and test tenderness by piercing with a fork.
  • Under-seasoning: Vegetables absorb oil and seasoning differently than meats, so don’t be shy with flavor.
  • Skipping oil: Without brushing oil directly on squash, it can stick to grates and tear apart.
  • Uneven cuts: Thin slices burn faster than thick ones, so uniformity helps consistency.
  • Ignoring resting time: Letting grilled squash rest off heat for a few minutes allows juices to redistribute, resulting in moister bites.

Creative Serving Suggestions

Grilled zucchini and summer squash offer endless versatility beyond a simple side dish.

  • Salads: Toss slices with fresh greens, feta cheese, cherry tomatoes, and balsamic vinaigrette.
  • Pasta: Dice grilled squash into your favorite pasta or grain bowl for an added smoky element.
  • Sandwiches & wraps: Layer onto sandwiches with hummus, grilled chicken, or roasted red peppers.
  • Toppings: Add over homemade pizza or flatbreads for vegetable variety.
  • Dips: Pair with tzatziki, baba ganoush, or herb aioli for a delicious appetizer.

Storing and Reheating Grilled Zucchini and Summer Squash

If you have leftovers (which can happen fast!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, warm gently in a pan over medium heat or briefly on the grill. Avoid the microwave to retain texture.

Final Thoughts

Grilling zucchini and summer squash is a straightforward yet rewarding culinary adventure. Their natural sweetness amplifies with smoky char, while their tender flesh offers a pleasant bite that complements countless dishes. By paying attention to selection, preparation, seasoning, and grilling technique, you’ll consistently nail this summer favorite.

Next time you fire up the grill, treat yourself to perfectly grilled zucchini or summer squash. Experiment with herbs, spices, and serving ideas until you find your signature style. Your taste buds—and guests—will thank you for it.

Bonus Recipe Idea: Grilled Summer Squash with Lemon-Herb Vinaigrette

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchini, cut lengthwise into ¼-inch slices
  • 2 medium yellow summer squash, cut lengthwise into ¼-inch slices
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced

Instructions:

  1. Preheat grill to medium-high.
  2. Toss zucchini and summer squash slices in olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill slices for 3-4 minutes per side until tender with grill marks.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, garlic, parsley, and thyme.
  5. Remove squash from grill, drizzle vinaigrette over top, and serve warm or at room temperature.

Enjoy the fresh, zesty flavors alongside grilled meats or your favorite grain bowl!

Dive in, get creative, and savor the smoky goodness of grilled zucchini and summer squash all summer long!
