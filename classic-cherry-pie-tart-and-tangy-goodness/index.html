Classic Cherry Pie: Tart and Tangy Goodness


There’s something timeless and comforting about a classic cherry pie. The combination of tart cherries and flaky pastry creates a dessert that is simply irresistible. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this classic cherry pie recipe will guide you through the process, resulting in a masterpiece that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Cherry Filling:

  • 4 cups fresh or frozen cherries, pitted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon (optional)

For Assembly:

  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Coarse sugar for sprinkling (optional)

Instructions

Pie Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Add the cold cubed butter to the flour mixture.
  3. Use a pastry cutter or your hands to work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough until it holds together.
  5. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Cherry Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the pitted cherries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, almond extract, lemon juice, and cinnamon (if using).
  2. Gently toss the ingredients together until the cherries are evenly coated. Set aside.

Assembly:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of the chilled pie dough into a circle large enough to fit into a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. Transfer the rolled-out dough to the pie dish, gently press it into the bottom and sides, and trim any excess dough.
  4. Pour the cherry filling into the prepared pie crust.
  5. Roll out the second disc of pie dough and place it over the cherry filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal.
  6. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  7. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with coarse sugar, if desired.

Baking:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any drips and bake in the preheated oven for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  2. If the edges of the crust are browning too quickly, cover them with foil halfway through baking.
  3. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a golden, flaky crust, make sure all the pie crust ingredients are cold and handle the dough as little as possible.
  • If using frozen cherries, be sure to thaw and drain them before using in the filling.
  • Serve the cherry pie with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful treat.

Conclusion

Classic cherry pie is a dessert that brings joy to any occasion. With its tangy cherry filling and buttery crust, it’s a true crowd-pleaser. This recipe allows you to create a homemade cherry pie that rivals any bakery offering. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a baking adventure that will result in a delicious, timeless classic.
