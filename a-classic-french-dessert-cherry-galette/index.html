A Classic French Dessert: Cherry Galette


When it comes to classic French desserts, one cannot overlook the delightful Cherry Galette. With its flaky crust, sweet cherries, and a touch of almond, this rustic dessert is a perfect way to showcase the natural beauty and flavors of the cherries. In this blog post, we will explore the step-by-step process of creating this mouthwatering treat. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 cups fresh cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add the cold butter cubes to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, a few tablespoons at a time, and mix until the dough comes together.
  4. Divide the dough into two equal portions, shape into disks, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Step 2: Make the Filling

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the cherries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and almond extract. Gently toss until the cherries are coated evenly. Set aside.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. On a floured surface, roll out one disk of the chilled dough into a circle, about 12 inches in diameter. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  3. Spoon the cherry filling onto the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border around the edges.
  4. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling, pleating as you go, to create a rustic look.
  5. Repeat steps 2-4 with the second disk of dough and remaining cherry filling.
  6. Brush the edges of the galettes with an egg wash (1 egg beaten with 1 tablespoon of water) to give them a golden finish.
  7. Bake the galettes in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  8. Remove from the oven and let the galettes cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the cherry galette warm or at room temperature.
  • Dust with powdered sugar for an added touch of elegance.
  • Pair with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for a delightful treat.

Conclusion

The Cherry Galette is a classic French dessert that never fails to impress. With its buttery crust, sweet cherries, and hint of almond, it’s a true delight for the taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving something sweet, this galette is sure to please. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and enjoy creating this timeless dessert in your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
