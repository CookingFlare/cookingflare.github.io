Fall Flavors: 5 Unique Squash Recipes You Need to Try


As the crisp autumn air settles in and the leaves change color, there’s nothing quite like embracing the cozy, comforting flavors of fall. Among the season’s bounty, squash takes center stage with its versatility, rich flavors, and hearty texture. Whether you’re a fan of sweet, savory, or a bit of both, squash is an ingredient that can be transformed into a variety of dishes that are perfect for chilly evenings and fall gatherings. In this post, we’ll explore five unique and delicious squash recipes that will warm you up and showcase the best of autumn’s harvest.

1. Butternut Squash Soup with Sage and Brown Butter

There’s nothing more comforting than a warm bowl of creamy soup, and butternut squash is the perfect base for a fall-inspired dish. The rich, sweet flavor of butternut squash is balanced by the savory notes of sage and brown butter, creating a soup that’s both luxurious and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (optional for extra creaminess)
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 6 fresh sage leaves, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Squash: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the cubed butternut squash on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt, pepper, and ground ginger. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender and slightly caramelized.

  2. Prepare the Soup Base: In a large pot, heat a bit of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Add the roasted squash, cinnamon, and nutmeg, and cook for another minute to enhance the spices.

  3. Blend the Soup: Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 10 minutes. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth. Return to the pot if necessary, and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. Add heavy cream for extra creaminess if desired.

  4. Make Brown Butter: In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the fresh sage leaves and cook for 2-3 minutes until the butter turns golden brown and becomes fragrant. Drizzle the brown butter over the soup just before serving.

This rich, velvety soup is perfect as a starter for any fall meal. The brown butter and sage elevate the natural sweetness of the butternut squash, making each spoonful a true autumn delight.

2. Spaghetti Squash Carbonara

Spaghetti squash is a fantastic alternative to traditional pasta, especially for those looking to cut down on carbs or add more vegetables to their diet. In this recipe, spaghetti squash is transformed into a delicious, light version of carbonara, making it a perfect fall comfort dish with a twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Spaghetti Squash: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut the spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and remove the seeds. Drizzle the inside with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place the squash halves, cut side down, on a baking sheet and roast for 40-45 minutes, or until the strands can be easily pulled away with a fork.

  2. Cook the Bacon: While the squash is roasting, heat a skillet over medium heat and cook the chopped bacon until crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the bacon from the pan and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the skillet.

  3. Make the Carbonara Sauce: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan cheese, heavy cream, garlic, salt, and pepper until well combined. Set aside.

  4. Combine the Squash and Bacon: Once the spaghetti squash is roasted, use a fork to scrape the flesh into long strands. Add the squash strands to the skillet with the bacon fat, tossing to coat. Pour the carbonara sauce over the squash, stirring quickly to combine. The residual heat from the squash will cook the eggs, creating a creamy, luscious sauce. Stir in the crispy bacon.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and additional Parmesan cheese before serving.

This spaghetti squash carbonara is a rich and flavorful dish that’s surprisingly light but satisfying. It’s perfect for a cozy fall dinner or as a fun twist on a classic Italian recipe.

3. Acorn Squash Stuffed with Quinoa and Cranberries

Acorn squash is a delightful variety that’s often overlooked but makes for an excellent vessel for stuffing. This recipe features a savory-sweet stuffing made with quinoa, cranberries, and pecans, creating a perfect balance of flavors that are ideal for fall. It’s a fantastic side dish or even a vegetarian main course.

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squash, halved and seeded
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Squash: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Drizzle the cut sides of the acorn squash with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place the squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side down, and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and golden brown.

  2. Prepare the Quinoa Stuffing: While the squash roasts, cook the quinoa in vegetable broth according to package instructions. Once the quinoa is cooked, fluff it with a fork and transfer it to a large mixing bowl. Stir in the dried cranberries, chopped pecans, chopped onion, cinnamon, ginger, salt, and pepper.

  3. Stuff the Squash: Once the acorn squash is roasted and tender, flip the halves over and stuff each one with the quinoa mixture. Return the stuffed squash to the oven for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Serve: Serve the stuffed squash warm, garnished with extra pecans or herbs if desired.

This acorn squash dish is a hearty, flavorful option that’s both visually stunning and delicious. The combination of nutty quinoa, tart cranberries, and crunchy pecans adds complexity to the sweet squash, making it a crowd-pleaser for any fall celebration.

4. Kabocha Squash Curry

Kabocha squash, often referred to as Japanese pumpkin, is perfect for curries. Its dense, sweet flesh holds up beautifully in rich, spiced sauces. This recipe features a fragrant coconut milk-based curry that highlights the natural sweetness of the squash while complementing it with warm spices like turmeric, cumin, and coriander.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium kabocha squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and grated ginger, sautéing for about 5 minutes until the onion becomes soft and translucent.

  2. Add the Spices: Stir in the curry powder, cumin, turmeric, and coriander, cooking for another 1-2 minutes until the spices become fragrant.

  3. Simmer the Squash: Add the cubed kabocha squash to the pot and stir to coat it with the spices. Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender and the curry has thickened.

  4. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with rice or naan bread for a complete meal.

This kabocha squash curry is a warm, comforting dish with a perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors. It’s perfect for a fall dinner and a great way to incorporate squash into a comforting, flavorful curry.

5. Delicata Squash Fries with Garlic Aioli

Delicata squash, with its tender skin and sweet, nutty flavor, makes for a perfect alternative to traditional fries. This recipe takes advantage of its natural sweetness and combines it with a crispy coating for a fun and healthy fall snack or side dish. Paired with a tangy garlic aioli, these squash fries are sure to be a hit.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium delicata squashes, sliced into fries
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Squash Fries: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Slice the delicata squashes into fries, leaving the skin on for added texture. Toss the fries in olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until evenly coated.

  2. Bake the Fries: Spread the seasoned squash fries in a single layer on a baking sheet. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the fries are golden brown and crispy on the edges.

  3. Make the Garlic Aioli: While the fries bake, mix the mayonnaise, lemon juice, and minced garlic in a small bowl. Stir well and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Serve: Once the fries are baked and crispy, serve them hot with the garlic aioli for dipping.

These delicata squash fries are a fun and flavorful fall snack, with the sweetness of the squash perfectly balanced by the smoky paprika and tangy garlic aioli. They’re a perfect addition to any fall gathering or as a healthy alternative to regular fries.

With these five unique squash recipes, you’ll be able to make the most of the fall season and explore a variety of flavors and textures that squash has to offer. Whether you’re roasting, stuffing, or blending, these recipes are sure to become staples in your autumn meal rotation. Enjoy the warmth and comfort of fall, and let these delicious squash dishes become part of your culinary tradition.
