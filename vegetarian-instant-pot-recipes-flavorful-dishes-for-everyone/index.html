Vegetarian Instant Pot Recipes: Flavorful Dishes for Everyone


The Instant Pot has revolutionized home cooking, making meal preparation faster, easier, and more convenient without sacrificing flavor. For vegetarians, the Instant Pot is a dream come true, offering a variety of cooking methods like sautéing, pressure cooking, and slow cooking, all in one appliance. Whether you’re a seasoned vegetarian or just trying to incorporate more plant-based meals into your routine, the Instant Pot allows you to create wholesome, delicious dishes in no time. In this post, we’ll explore a range of vegetarian Instant Pot recipes that will satisfy everyone, from hearty meals to light snacks, and everything in between.

Why Choose Vegetarian Instant Pot Recipes?

Before diving into the recipes, it’s worth mentioning why Instant Pot cooking is ideal for vegetarian meals. The Instant Pot retains nutrients in food better than many other cooking methods, making it a great choice for preserving the vitamins and minerals in vegetables, legumes, and grains. Additionally, the pressure cooking function infuses flavors, so you get a depth of taste in every dish. Whether you’re cooking lentils, beans, or stews, the Instant Pot ensures that everything is cooked evenly and to perfection. It’s not only faster than traditional stovetop methods but also incredibly hands-off, allowing you to focus on other tasks or relax while your meal cooks.

Now, let’s take a look at some fantastic vegetarian Instant Pot recipes that will elevate your plant-based cooking game.

1. Instant Pot Lentil Soup

Lentils are a nutritious and protein-packed ingredient that cooks quickly and easily in the Instant Pot. This lentil soup is rich in flavor, hearty, and comforting – perfect for a chilly day or a quick weeknight meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” setting. Add olive oil and allow it to heat up.
  2. Once hot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the diced carrots and celery to the pot. Sauté for another 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, cumin, turmeric, salt, and pepper to the Instant Pot.
  5. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook on high pressure for 15 minutes.
  6. After cooking, let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes before performing a quick release.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot.

2. Instant Pot Vegetable Biryani

This aromatic and flavorful vegetable biryani is a great dish for feeding a crowd or as a filling meal for yourself. The Instant Pot ensures that the rice is perfectly cooked while the spices infuse every bite with rich flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice, rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or olive oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes, bell peppers)
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to the “Sauté” setting and heat the ghee or olive oil.
  2. Add the cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom pods. Sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onion and sauté until golden brown, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and mixed vegetables, and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Stir in the turmeric, garam masala, ground coriander, and ground cumin. Cook for another 1 minute to let the spices bloom.
  6. Add the rinsed basmati rice, vegetable broth, and salt. Stir to combine.
  7. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook on high pressure for 6 minutes.
  8. Once done, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release.
  9. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

3. Instant Pot Chickpea and Spinach Curry

This hearty and flavorful chickpea and spinach curry is a perfect weeknight dinner. The creamy coconut milk and spices create a luscious, comforting sauce that pairs beautifully with rice or naan.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (or 1 can, drained and rinsed)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to the “Sauté” setting and heat the olive oil.
  2. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened and fragrant.
  3. Stir in the ground coriander, cumin, turmeric, and garam masala. Sauté for another minute to toast the spices.
  4. Add the chickpeas, coconut milk, diced tomatoes, and salt. Stir to combine.
  5. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes.
  6. Once the cooking time is complete, perform a quick release of pressure.
  7. Open the lid and stir in the chopped spinach. The residual heat will wilt the spinach.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with rice or naan.

4. Instant Pot Stuffed Bell Peppers

Stuffed bell peppers are a classic comfort food, and making them in the Instant Pot cuts down on the cooking time while keeping them perfectly tender. These peppers are stuffed with a flavorful mixture of quinoa, beans, and vegetables for a filling, satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bell peppers, tops cut off and seeds removed
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (optional, for topping)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Stuff each bell pepper with the quinoa mixture, pressing down gently to pack the filling.
  3. Pour the vegetable broth into the bottom of the Instant Pot. Place the stuffed peppers in the pot, standing upright.
  4. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook on high pressure for 8 minutes.
  5. Once done, perform a quick release of pressure.
  6. If desired, sprinkle shredded cheese on top of the peppers and set the Instant Pot to “Sauté” for a couple of minutes to melt the cheese.
  7. Serve the stuffed peppers hot, garnished with fresh herbs or a squeeze of lime juice.

5. Instant Pot Sweet Potato and Black Bean Chili

This sweet potato and black bean chili is a comforting, nutritious dish perfect for a cold evening. Packed with sweet potatoes, black beans, and a blend of spices, this chili is hearty and satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to the “Sauté” setting and heat a tablespoon of olive oil.
  2. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, and salt. Sauté for another minute to bloom the spices.
  4. Add the diced sweet potatoes, black beans, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Stir to combine.
  5. Close the Instant Pot lid, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook on high pressure for 10 minutes.
  6. Once done, perform a quick release of pressure.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve.

Conclusion

The Instant Pot is a fantastic tool for making vegetarian dishes that are not only quick and easy but also bursting with flavor. From comforting lentil soup to vibrant vegetable biryani, these recipes offer a range of delicious, wholesome meals that will satisfy everyone, whether they follow a plant-based diet or not. With the Instant Pot’s ability to infuse flavors and retain nutrients, vegetarian cooking has never been more accessible or enjoyable. Try these recipes for a delightful, nutritious experience in the kitchen!
