Vegan Pie and Tart Recipes: Delicious Plant-Based Alternatives


In recent years, the popularity of plant-based diets has skyrocketed, and for good reason. Not only are these diets beneficial for our health, but they also have a positive impact on the environment. If you’re looking to add some delicious and satisfying vegan recipes to your repertoire, look no further than these amazing vegan pie and tart recipes. From sweet to savory, these plant-based alternatives will have your taste buds dancing with joy.

1. Classic Vegan Apple Pie

Nothing says comfort like a warm slice of apple pie. This vegan version is just as delicious as the traditional recipe, if not better! The secret lies in the flaky crust and the perfectly spiced apple filling. Serve it with a scoop of vegan vanilla ice cream for an indulgent treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of vegan butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 4-5 tablespoons of ice-cold water
  • 6-7 large apples, peeled and thinly sliced
  • ½ cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold vegan butter and use your fingertips to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. In another bowl, toss the sliced apples with sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice until well coated.
  5. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to fit your pie dish. Transfer the dough to the dish and trim any excess.
  6. Fill the pie crust with the apple mixture, spreading it evenly.
  7. Roll out the remaining dough to create a lattice pattern or cover the entire pie. Press the edges to seal.
  8. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  9. Allow the pie to cool for at least 1 hour before serving.

2. Savory Vegan Mushroom Tart

If you’re in the mood for something savory, this vegan mushroom tart is sure to satisfy your cravings. With a buttery crust and a flavorful mushroom filling, this tart is perfect for a cozy dinner or a weekend brunch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of vegan butter, cold and cubed
  • 4-5 tablespoons of ice-cold water
  • 3 cups of mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • ½ teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup of vegan cream cheese
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold vegan butter and use your fingertips to rub it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  4. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms, onion, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper. Sauté until the mushrooms are browned and the onions are translucent.
  5. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface and transfer it to a tart pan. Press the dough into the pan and trim any excess.
  6. Spread the vegan cream cheese evenly over the tart crust.
  7. Spoon the mushroom mixture over the cream cheese, spreading it evenly.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.
  9. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Conclusion

These vegan pie and tart recipes prove that plant-based alternatives can be just as delicious and satisfying as their non-vegan counterparts. Whether you’re craving a sweet apple pie or a savory mushroom tart, these recipes will delight your taste buds and leave you feeling nourished. So, grab your apron and get ready to create some plant-based magic in the kitchen. Enjoy!
