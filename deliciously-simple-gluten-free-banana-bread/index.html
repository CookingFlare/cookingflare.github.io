Deliciously Simple: Gluten-Free Banana Bread


If you’re a fan of banana bread but need to follow a gluten-free diet, you’re in luck! This recipe for Deliciously Simple Gluten-Free Banana Bread is not only easy to make, but it’s also incredibly delicious. With just a few simple ingredients, you can enjoy a moist and flavorful banana bread that is perfect for breakfast, snacking, or dessert.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 3/4 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with coconut oil or line it with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mashed bananas, melted coconut oil, honey or maple syrup, eggs, and vanilla extract. Stir well until all the ingredients are well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking soda, salt, and ground cinnamon.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense and dry banana bread.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  6. Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 55-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Once baked, remove the banana bread from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, consider adding a handful of chocolate chips, chopped nuts, or dried fruits to the batter.
  • If you prefer a sweeter banana bread, you can increase the amount of honey or maple syrup to suit your taste.
  • This recipe can easily be made vegan by substituting the eggs with flax eggs or applesauce. Simply mix 2 tablespoons of ground flaxseed with 6 tablespoons of water and let it sit for a few minutes to thicken.
  • Leftover banana bread can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze individual slices for longer storage.

Enjoy Your Gluten-Free Banana Bread!

There you have it – a deliciously simple recipe for gluten-free banana bread that is sure to satisfy your cravings. Whether you follow a gluten-free diet or just want to try something new, this recipe is a winner. So grab those ripe bananas and get baking – you won’t be disappointed!

Remember, the key to a successful banana bread is using ripe, spotty bananas. They not only add sweetness but also provide moisture and flavor. So don’t throw away those overripe bananas – turn them into a delightful treat instead.

We hope you enjoy making and devouring this gluten-free banana bread. Share it with friends, family, or keep it all to yourself – we won’t judge! Happy baking!
