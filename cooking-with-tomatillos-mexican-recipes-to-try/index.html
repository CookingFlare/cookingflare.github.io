Cooking with Tomatillos: Mexican Recipes to Try


Tomatillos, those small, green, papery-skinned fruits, are a staple in Mexican cuisine. Often misunderstood and underutilized, tomatillos bring a unique flavor and texture to a variety of dishes. In this post, we'll explore the world of cooking with tomatillos and provide you with authentic Mexican recipes to try at home. Understanding Tomatillos Before we dive into the recipes, it's essential to understand the different types of tomatillos and how to use them. There are two primary types of tomatillos: green and purple. Green tomatillos are more common and have a slightly sweeter taste than their purple counterparts. They can be used in a range of dishes, from salsas to soups, and can be substituted for tomatoes in many recipes. Selecting and Preparing Tomatillos When selecting tomatillos, look for fruit that's firm and has no signs of mold or bruising. You can store tomatillos in the refrigerator for up to five days. Before using, rinse the tomatillos under cold water, removing any debris or stickers. Remove the papery skin and stems, and rinse again. Roasted Tomatillos Roasting brings out the natural sweetness in tomatillos, making them an excellent addition to soups, stews, and sauces. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Rinse 4-6 tomatillos, removing the papery skin and stems. Place the tomatillos on a baking sheet, drizzle with 1-2 tablespoons of olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast for 10-15 minutes or until the tomatillos are tender and lightly caramelized. Tomatillo Salsa Tomatillo salsa, also known as salsa de tomate, is a staple in Mexican cuisine. It's made with roasted tomatillos, onions, garlic, and jalapeños. To make salsa, combine the roasted tomatillos with 1 diced onion, 3 cloves of minced garlic, and 1 or 2 diced jalapeños. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lime juice. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Substituting Tomatillos in Recipes Tomatillos can be substituted for tomatoes in many recipes. If a recipe calls for 2 cups of chopped tomatoes, you can use 1 cup of chopped tomatillos and 1 cup of chopped onions or other aromatics. Tomatillos have a more intense flavor than tomatoes, so adjust the seasoning accordingly. Tomatillo Recipes to Try Here are five authentic Mexican recipes that feature tomatillos as the main ingredient: Tomatillo and Chicken Tacos

2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces 4-6 tomatillos, roasted and chopped 1 diced onion 2 cloves of minced garlic 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro Salt and pepper to taste 8-10 corn tortillas Sliced radishes, lime wedges, and sour cream for toppings

Cook the chicken with the roasted tomatillos, onion, and garlic. Season with salt and pepper. Serve the chicken in tacos with radishes, lime wedges, and sour cream. Tomatillo and Black Bean Soup

4-6 tomatillos, roasted and chopped 1 can of diced black beans 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth 1 diced onion 2 cloves of minced garlic 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro Salt and pepper to taste 1/4 cup of chopped scallions for garnish

Combine the roasted tomatillos with the black beans, broth, onion, and garlic in a large pot. Simmer until the soup is hot and the flavors have melded together. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve the soup with chopped cilantro and scallions. Grilled Fish with Tomatillo Salsa

4-6 tomatillos, roasted and chopped 2 cloves of minced garlic 1 diced onion 1 or 2 diced jalapeños 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro Salt and pepper to taste 4-6 blackened or grilled fish fillets Lime wedges for serving

Combine the roasted tomatillos with the garlic, onion, jalapeños, and cilantro in a food processor. Pulse until the mixture is smooth. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Grill or pan-fry the fish and serve with the tomatillo salsa. Tomatillo and Chimichurri Skewers

4-6 tomatillos, roasted and chopped 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley 2 cloves of minced garlic 1/4 cup of chopped fresh oregano 1/4 cup of olive oil Salt and pepper to taste 4-6 wooden skewers 1/4 cup of chopped red onion for garnish

Combine the roasted tomatillos with the parsley, garlic, and oregano in a food processor. Pulse until the mixture is smooth. Mix in the olive oil and season with salt and pepper to taste. Thread the mixture onto the skewers with cherry tomatoes and red onion. Enchiladas with Roasted Tomatillos

4-6 tomatillos, roasted and chopped 1 can of diced tomatoes 1 cup of shredded cheese (Monterey Jack or Cheddar work well) 8-10 corn tortillas 2 cups of chopped cooked chicken or beef Salt and pepper to taste 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Combine the roasted tomatillos with the diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, and chopped cooked chicken or beef. Simmer the mixture over low heat until the cheese has melted. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Assemble the enchiladas by dipping each tortilla in the mixture and rolling them up. Serve the enchiladas with cilantro and sour cream. Conclusion Tomatillos are a versatile ingredient that add a unique flavor and texture to a variety of dishes. With these recipes, you'll be well on your way to mastering the art of cooking with tomatillos. Experiment with different types of tomatillos and recipes to find your favorite ways to use them in the kitchen.
