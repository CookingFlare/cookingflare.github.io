Seattle-Style Teriyaki Chicken Bowls Guide


Seattle-style Teriyaki chicken bowls are a beloved comfort food that perfectly blends the savory flavors of traditional Japanese teriyaki with the fresh ingredients of the Pacific Northwest. Known for their tangy, sweet, and savory sauce, these bowls have become a go-to dish for food lovers across Seattle and beyond. In this guide, we’ll take you through the steps to make this mouthwatering dish at home, bringing the taste of Seattle straight to your kitchen.

What Makes Seattle-Style Teriyaki Chicken Bowls Special?

Seattle-style teriyaki is unique in that it combines the deep flavors of a sweet soy sauce-based marinade with the freshness of local, seasonal produce. Unlike traditional teriyaki, Seattle’s version is often characterized by a slightly thicker sauce that has a rich, syrupy consistency. The grilled chicken is typically served over a bed of rice, accompanied by a variety of vegetables, and finished off with a sprinkle of sesame seeds or green onions for extra texture and flavor.

One of the distinguishing features of this bowl is the balance between the tender, grilled chicken and the savory sauce that infuses every bite. While teriyaki has roots in Japan, Seattle has made this dish its own, adding an American twist with locally sourced ingredients. This Seattle-style twist makes for a satisfying and vibrant meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make your Seattle-style teriyaki chicken bowls, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Teriyaki Marinade:

  • 1/2 cup soy sauce (low-sodium if preferred)
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds (optional, for garnish)

For the Chicken:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts (thighs recommended for juiciness)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or vegetable oil)

For the Bowl:

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (or your preferred rice variety)
  • 1 cup broccoli florets (steamed or blanched)
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1 small cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (for garnish)

Optional Toppings:

  • Sriracha or chili paste (for a spicy kick)
  • Pickled ginger
  • Seaweed salad
  • Avocado slices

How to Make Seattle-Style Teriyaki Chicken Bowls

Step 1: Prepare the Teriyaki Sauce

The key to the deliciousness of this dish lies in the homemade teriyaki sauce. Begin by combining the soy sauce, mirin, honey or brown sugar, rice vinegar, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger in a small saucepan. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally to help dissolve the sugar and mix the ingredients.

Once the sauce begins to simmer, reduce the heat and allow it to cook for an additional 5-7 minutes. The sauce will begin to thicken slightly as the sugar and soy sauce reduce. If you prefer a thicker consistency, you can mix the cornstarch with water to create a slurry and add it to the sauce. Continue to cook for another 2 minutes until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency. Remove from heat and set aside to cool.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

While the sauce is cooling, it’s time to prepare the chicken. If you’re using chicken thighs, they provide a tender, juicy bite that pairs beautifully with the teriyaki sauce. Chicken breasts can also be used if you prefer a leaner option.

Place the chicken pieces in a shallow dish and pour about half of the prepared teriyaki sauce over the chicken. Make sure the chicken is well-coated in the sauce. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 30 minutes, but for optimal flavor, let it marinate for 1-2 hours in the refrigerator.

Step 3: Cook the Chicken

Once the chicken is marinated, heat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat and lightly coat it with oil. Place the chicken on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). During the cooking process, you can brush the chicken with additional teriyaki sauce for an extra layer of flavor.

For an alternative method, you can also cook the chicken in a skillet or oven. If using a skillet, heat oil over medium-high heat and sear the chicken for about 5 minutes per side. If baking in the oven, preheat it to 375°F (190°C) and bake the chicken for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.

Once cooked, let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before slicing it into thin strips against the grain. This step ensures the chicken remains tender and juicy.

Step 4: Assemble the Bowls

To assemble your Seattle-style teriyaki chicken bowls, start by dividing the cooked jasmine rice into bowls as the base. You can use other types of rice, like brown rice or sushi rice, depending on your preferences. The rice should be hot, as it helps melt any remaining sauce from the chicken and adds a comforting base.

Next, arrange the sliced teriyaki chicken on top of the rice. Add your steamed or blanched broccoli, julienned carrots, and thinly sliced cucumber to the bowl. These vegetables provide a fresh, crunchy contrast to the rich teriyaki chicken.

Garnish your bowl with a sprinkle of sesame seeds, chopped green onions, and any other optional toppings such as avocado, pickled ginger, or a drizzle of sriracha for heat.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once everything is assembled, drizzle the remaining teriyaki sauce over the top of the bowl. The sauce ties all the ingredients together, infusing the rice, chicken, and vegetables with its sweet and savory flavor. Serve your Seattle-style teriyaki chicken bowls immediately while everything is fresh and warm.

Tips for Making the Perfect Seattle-Style Teriyaki Chicken Bowls

  • Use Chicken Thighs for Tenderness: While chicken breasts are a great option, chicken thighs are much juicier and will soak up the teriyaki sauce more, making them the preferred choice for this recipe.

  • Thicken the Sauce for Extra Flavor: If you like your teriyaki sauce to be a bit thicker, adding a cornstarch slurry (cornstarch mixed with water) will help create a richer consistency.

  • Customize the Veggies: Feel free to get creative with the vegetables in your bowl. You can add bell peppers, snap peas, or even sautéed mushrooms for a more complex flavor profile.

  • Make It Spicy: If you like heat, don’t hesitate to add some sriracha or chili paste to your sauce or as a topping. A little spice will contrast wonderfully with the sweetness of the teriyaki.

  • Try Different Types of Rice: While jasmine rice is the most common choice, you can experiment with other varieties such as brown rice, sushi rice, or even quinoa for a different texture and flavor.

  • Garnish for Extra Crunch: Adding a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds, chopped cilantro, or a handful of crunchy seaweed can elevate the flavor and texture of your bowl.

Why Seattle-Style Teriyaki is a Local Favorite

Seattle’s love for teriyaki chicken bowls can be traced back to the 1970s when Japanese immigrants began opening small teriyaki shops in the area. These early versions of teriyaki chicken were a hit with locals due to their simple yet bold flavors, quick preparation time, and satisfying nature. Over time, Seattle’s diverse food culture allowed the dish to evolve and take on unique twists, incorporating local ingredients and flavors.

Today, Seattle-style teriyaki bowls are a staple of the city’s food scene, with restaurants and food trucks serving their own variations. The bowls are a convenient, healthy, and delicious meal option, making them perfect for lunch, dinner, or even a satisfying takeout option after a long day.

Whether you’re a Seattle native or someone looking to recreate the flavors of this iconic dish at home, the Seattle-style teriyaki chicken bowl is an easy and rewarding dish to master.

Conclusion

Seattle-style teriyaki chicken bowls are a delightful and comforting meal that combines bold, savory flavors with fresh, healthy ingredients. With a few simple steps, you can create a flavorful teriyaki sauce, marinate juicy chicken, and assemble a bowl full of vibrant vegetables and rice. It’s the perfect dish for any occasion, whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or an impressive meal to serve at a gathering.

By following this guide and customizing your toppings and ingredients, you can make the perfect Seattle-style teriyaki chicken bowls that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Enjoy the unique flavors of Seattle in the comfort of your own home!
