Authentic Italian Pasta Recipes You Need to Try


Italy is renowned for its culinary traditions, and pasta is at the heart of this rich gastronomic heritage. From the bustling streets of Rome to the serene countryside of Tuscany, every region in Italy boasts unique pasta dishes that reflect local ingredients and age-old recipes. If you’re looking to bring a taste of Italy into your kitchen, here are some authentic Italian pasta recipes that you absolutely need to try.

1. Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g guanciale (pork cheek) or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Cook the Spaghetti: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package instructions.
  2. Prepare the Guanciale: In a large skillet, cook the guanciale or pancetta over medium heat until it becomes crispy. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. Mix the Eggs and Cheese: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Combine Everything: When the spaghetti is ready, reserve a cup of the pasta cooking water and drain the rest. Add the hot spaghetti to the skillet with the guanciale. Quickly pour in the egg mixture, stirring vigorously to create a creamy sauce. If the mixture is too thick, add the reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach the desired consistency.
  5. Serve Immediately: Plate the carbonara and serve with additional Pecorino Romano and black pepper on top.

2. Penne all’Arrabbiata

Ingredients

  • 400g penne pasta
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-2 red chili peppers, chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 500g canned tomatoes, crushed
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt
  • Pecorino Romano cheese, grated (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Penne: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the penne and cook until al dente according to package instructions.
  2. Make the Sauce: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped chili peppers, sautéing until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. Add the Tomatoes: Pour in the crushed tomatoes, season with salt, and let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes until it thickens.
  4. Combine and Serve: Drain the penne and add it to the sauce, tossing to coat the pasta evenly. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and, if desired, sprinkle with Pecorino Romano cheese before serving.

3. Tagliatelle al Ragù Bolognese

Ingredients

  • 400g tagliatelle pasta
  • 300g ground beef
  • 200g ground pork
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200ml red wine
  • 500g canned tomatoes, crushed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 500ml beef broth
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sofrito: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped carrot, celery, and onion, cooking until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Cook the Meat: Add the ground beef and pork to the pot, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned.
  3. Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the red wine and let it simmer until the alcohol evaporates.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Broth: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and beef broth. Season with salt and pepper. Reduce the heat and let the sauce simmer for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  5. Cook the Tagliatelle: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the tagliatelle and cook until al dente.
  6. Combine and Serve: Drain the tagliatelle and mix it with the ragù sauce. Serve with a generous helping of grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese.

4. Spaghetti alle Vongole

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 1kg fresh clams, cleaned
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small chili pepper, chopped (optional)
  • 200ml dry white wine
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Cook the Spaghetti: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente according to package instructions.
  2. Prepare the Clams: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chili pepper, cooking until fragrant. Pour in the white wine and bring to a simmer.
  3. Cook the Clams: Add the cleaned clams to the skillet, cover with a lid, and cook until the clams open (about 5-7 minutes). Discard any clams that do not open.
  4. Combine and Serve: Drain the spaghetti and add it to the skillet with the clams, tossing to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

5. Fettuccine Alfredo

Ingredients

  • 400g fettuccine pasta
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • 100g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Fettuccine: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine and cook until al dente.
  2. Make the Sauce: In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir in the grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese until the sauce is smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Combine and Serve: Drain the fettuccine and add it to the skillet with the Alfredo sauce. Toss to coat the pasta evenly. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired and serve immediately.

6. Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa (Orecchiette with Broccoli Rabe)

Ingredients

  • 400g orecchiette pasta
  • 1 bunch broccoli rabe (cime di rapa), trimmed and chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 anchovy fillets, chopped
  • 1 small chili pepper, chopped
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pecorino cheese, grated (optional)

Instructions

  1. Blanch the Broccoli Rabe: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the broccoli rabe and blanch for 2-3 minutes. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside. Use the same water to cook the orecchiette until al dente.
  2. Sauté the Garlic and Anchovies: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, chopped anchovy fillets, and chili pepper. Sauté until the garlic is fragrant and the anchovies have dissolved.
  3. Combine Everything: Add the blanched broccoli rabe to the skillet and toss to combine. Drain the orecchiette and add it to the skillet, mixing everything thoroughly. Season with salt to taste.
  4. Serve: Plate the orecchiette and broccoli rabe and, if desired, sprinkle with grated Pecorino cheese before serving.

7. Lasagna alla Bolognese

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles, cooked according to package instructions
  • 300g ground beef
  • 200g ground pork
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 200ml red wine
  • 500g canned tomatoes, crushed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 500ml béchamel sauce
  • 200g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 100g Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, grated
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ragù: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped carrot, celery, and onion, cooking until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Add the ground beef and pork, cooking until browned. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer until the alcohol evaporates. Add the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste, season with salt and pepper, and let the sauce simmer for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  2. Assemble the Lasagna: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Spread a layer of ragù in the bottom of a baking dish. Cover with a layer of cooked lasagna noodles. Spread a layer of béchamel sauce over the noodles, followed by a layer of shredded mozzarella. Repeat these layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of ragù topped with béchamel sauce and grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese.
  3. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes until the top is golden and bubbly.
  4. Serve: Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

Exploring the diverse world of Italian pasta dishes can be a delightful journey through Italy’s rich culinary landscape. These authentic recipes capture the essence of Italian cuisine, emphasizing fresh ingredients, simple techniques, and bold flavors. Whether you’re savoring the creamy richness of Fettuccine Alfredo or the spicy kick of Penne all’Arrabbiata, each dish offers a unique taste of Italy. So, gather your ingredients, don your apron, and start cooking these delicious Italian pasta recipes. Buon appetito!
