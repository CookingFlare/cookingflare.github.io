Wine Pairings for Mexican Quesadillas: Adding a Twist to Traditional Fillings


When it comes to Mexican cuisine, we often think of bold flavors, vibrant spices, and mouthwatering dishes that make our taste buds dance. One popular and versatile Mexican dish that never fails to satisfy is the quesadilla. Traditionally filled with cheese and served with salsa or guacamole, quesadillas are a delicious treat that can be enjoyed as an appetizer, snack, or even a main course. But have you ever thought about pairing your quesadillas with wine?

Yes, you read that right! Wine can be a fantastic complement to the flavors found in Mexican quesadillas, elevating the dining experience to a whole new level. In this blog post, we will explore some exciting and unexpected wine pairings that will add a twist to your traditional quesadilla fillings.

1. Spinach and Mushroom Quesadillas

Let’s start with a classic vegetarian option - spinach and mushroom quesadillas. The earthy flavors of sautéed mushrooms and the freshness of spinach make for a delightful combination. To enhance these flavors, we recommend pairing this quesadilla with a light-bodied and fruity red wine, such as a Pinot Noir. The subtle notes of cherries and berries in the wine will complement the earthiness of the mushrooms and the greenness of the spinach.

2. Chicken and Poblano Quesadillas

For those who prefer a bit of heat in their quesadillas, the combination of chicken and poblano peppers is a match made in heaven. The smoky and slightly spicy flavor of the poblano peppers can be beautifully balanced with a crisp and refreshing white wine. A Sauvignon Blanc or a Verdejo would be an excellent choice, as their citrusy and herbaceous notes will complement the flavors of the chicken and peppers.

3. Shrimp and Mango Quesadillas

If you’re looking for a quesadilla that is both sweet and savory, then shrimp and mango is the way to go. The succulent shrimp and the juicy sweetness of the mango create a burst of flavors in every bite. To enhance this tropical combination, consider pairing it with a slightly off-dry Riesling. The hint of sweetness in the wine will complement the sweetness of the mango while balancing the savory notes from the shrimp.

4. Beef and Caramelized Onion Quesadillas

For those who crave a rich and hearty filling, beef and caramelized onion quesadillas are a must-try. The tender beef and the sweet and savory onions create a symphony of flavors that can be elevated with a full-bodied and robust red wine. Opt for a Malbec or a Cabernet Sauvignon, as their bold tannins and dark fruit flavors will stand up to the richness of the beef and the sweetness of the onions.

5. Black Bean and Corn Quesadillas

Last but not least, let’s not forget our vegetarian friends who enjoy the combination of black beans and corn in their quesadillas. The smoky and slightly sweet flavors of the black beans and the sweet crunchiness of corn deserve a wine that can complement their unique taste. A medium-bodied and fruity red wine, like a Grenache or a Barbera, would be an excellent choice. The bright fruit flavors in these wines will enhance the flavors of the black beans and corn, creating a harmonious pairing.

Now that you have some exciting wine pairing ideas for your Mexican quesadillas, it’s time to get creative in the kitchen and explore the endless possibilities. Remember, wine pairing is all about experimentation and finding what works best for your palate. So, grab your favorite bottle of wine, whip up some delicious quesadillas, and prepare to indulge in a truly unforgettable culinary experience!
