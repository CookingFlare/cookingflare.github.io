Wholesome and Delicious: Homemade Oat Bars


As the saying goes, “an apple a day keeps the doctor away.” But what about a delicious, wholesome oat bar a day? Oat bars are a staple in many households, and for good reason. They’re easy to make, packed with nutritious ingredients, and can be enjoyed as a snack or dessert. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the world of homemade oat bars and explore the benefits of making them from scratch.

The Benefits of Homemade Oat Bars

When you make oat bars from scratch, you have complete control over the ingredients. This means you can choose the type of oats, nuts, and dried fruits that go into your bars. You can also customize the recipe to suit your dietary needs and preferences. For example, you can make gluten-free oat bars by using gluten-free oats, or vegan oat bars by substituting dairy products with plant-based alternatives.

Another benefit of homemade oat bars is that they’re fresher and more flavorful than store-bought options. Many commercial oat bars contain preservatives and additives that can affect their taste and texture. When you make oat bars at home, you can use fresh ingredients and avoid these unwanted additives.

The Recipe

Here’s a simple recipe for homemade oat bars that yields a delicious and wholesome snack. This recipe makes 12-15 bars, depending on the size you prefer.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (such as almonds, walnuts, or pecans)
  • 1 cup dried fruit (such as cranberries, raisins, or cherries)
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup chopped dark chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line an 8x8 inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the oats, nuts, and dried fruit.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the honey, brown sugar, and vegetable oil. Stir until well combined.
  4. Add the vanilla extract, salt, baking powder, and cinnamon to the wet ingredients and stir until well combined.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients and stir until everything is well combined.
  6. Press the mixture into the prepared baking dish.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  8. Remove the baking dish from the oven and let it cool for 10-15 minutes.
  9. If using chocolate chips, melt them in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth. Pour the melted chocolate over the oat mixture and spread evenly.
  10. Let the bars cool completely before cutting them into bars.

Tips and Variations

  • To make the oat bars more crunchy, bake them for an additional 5-10 minutes.
  • To make the oat bars more chewy, bake them for 20-25 minutes.
  • You can customize the recipe by adding or substituting different ingredients. Some ideas include:
    • Coconut flakes or shredded coconut for added texture and flavor
    • Seeds such as chia, flax, or hemp for added nutrition
    • Cinnamon or nutmeg for added spice
    • Diced apples or bananas for added natural sweetness
  • You can also make these oat bars in a slow cooker. Simply mix all the ingredients together and cook on low for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally.

Conclusion

Homemade oat bars are a delicious and wholesome snack that can be customized to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. By making them from scratch, you can avoid unwanted additives and preservatives and ensure that your bars are fresh and flavorful. With this simple recipe, you can make a batch of oat bars that will keep you satisfied and energized all day long. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the benefits of homemade oat bars!
