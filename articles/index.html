Flavorful Dairy-Free Chicken Curry Recipe

Curry is a popular dish that brings together a harmonious blend of spices and flavors. However, for those who are lactose intolerant or follow a dairy-free diet, finding a delicious and creamy curry recipe can be a challenge. But fear not! In this blog post, we will guide you through a flavorful dairy-free chicken curry recipe that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more. Ingredients 500g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces 1 tablespoon vegetable oil 1 onion, finely chopped 3 garlic cloves, minced 1-inch piece of ginger, grated 2 teaspoons curry powder 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust according to your spice preference) 1 can (400ml) coconut milk 1 cup chicken broth 2 tablespoons tomato paste 1 tablespoon soy sauce (gluten-free if required) 1 tablespoon lime juice Salt, to taste Fresh cilantro, for garnish Instructions Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan and sauté for another minute, until fragrant. In a small bowl, combine the curry powder, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric powder, and chili powder. Mix well to create a spice blend. Push the onion, garlic, and ginger mixture to one side of the pan and add the spice blend to the cleared area. Toast the spices for about 30 seconds, stirring constantly to prevent burning. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and cook until they are browned on all sides. This will take approximately 5-7 minutes. Pour in the coconut milk, chicken broth, tomato paste, soy sauce, and lime juice. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Reduce the heat to low and let the curry simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Taste the curry and season with salt according to your preference. Once the chicken is cooked and the flavors have melded together, remove the pan from the heat. Serve the dairy-free chicken curry over steamed rice or with warm naan bread. Garnish with fresh cilantro for an added burst of freshness. Tips and Variations To make this dish even more indulgent, you can add a splash of coconut cream or cashew cream towards the end of cooking for a richer, creamier texture. Feel free to customize the spice level of the curry by adjusting the amount of chili powder. If you prefer a milder curry, reduce the amount, or if you like it spicy, increase it accordingly. You can also add vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, or peas to the curry for added color and nutrition. Simply sauté them along with the onions in the initial step. If you prefer a different protein, such as tofu or shrimp, you can easily substitute them for the chicken in this recipe. Adjust the cooking time accordingly to ensure they are fully cooked. Conclusion This flavorful dairy-free chicken curry recipe is a delightful alternative for those who cannot consume dairy products. The combination of aromatic spices, creamy coconut milk, and tender chicken creates a curry that is both satisfying and nourishing. So, whether you are lactose intolerant or simply looking for a dairy-free option, give this recipe a try and let your taste buds dance with joy! Delicious Dairy-Free Raspberry Chia Pudding

Looking for a delicious and healthy breakfast or dessert option? Look no further! This dairy-free raspberry chia pudding is not only easy to make, but it’s also packed with nutrients and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re following a dairy-free diet or simply looking to incorporate more plant-based foods into your meals, this recipe is perfect for you. Ingredients 1 cup almond milk (or any other dairy-free milk of your choice) 1/4 cup chia seeds 1/2 cup fresh or frozen raspberries 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract Optional toppings: fresh raspberries, sliced almonds, shredded coconut Instructions In a medium-sized bowl, combine the almond milk, chia seeds, maple syrup (or honey), and vanilla extract. Stir well to ensure the chia seeds are evenly distributed throughout the mixture. Let the mixture sit for about 5 minutes, then give it another stir to prevent clumping. This will allow the chia seeds to absorb the liquid and create a pudding-like consistency. Add the raspberries to the bowl and gently mash them with a fork. This will release their natural juices and give the pudding a vibrant pink color. Stir the mixture once more, making sure the raspberries are well incorporated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results. This will give the chia seeds enough time to fully absorb the liquid and thicken the pudding. Once the pudding has set, give it a good stir to break up any clumps that may have formed. You can adjust the consistency by adding more almond milk if desired. Divide the pudding into serving bowls or jars and top with fresh raspberries, sliced almonds, or shredded coconut for added texture and flavor. Enjoy your delicious dairy-free raspberry chia pudding straight away, or refrigerate for later consumption.
