The Art of Leftover Sauces: 8 Creative Ways to Use Up Extra Gravy


We’ve all been there: you make a delicious roast or a savory stew, and you end up with a surplus of gravy. Instead of letting it go to waste, why not get creative and find new ways to incorporate that leftover sauce into your meals? In this post, we’ll explore eight innovative and mouthwatering ways to use up that extra gravy. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Gravy-Stuffed Mashed Potato Balls

Transform your leftover gravy into a delightful surprise by stuffing it into mashed potato balls. Simply mix your gravy with mashed potatoes, form small balls, and then bake or fry them until golden brown. The result? A crispy-on-the-outside, creamy-on-the-inside treat that will have your taste buds dancing.

2. Gravy-Smothered Meatloaf

Give your meatloaf a flavor boost by smothering it in leftover gravy. Pour the gravy over the meatloaf before baking, and let it infuse the dish with its rich, savory goodness. The result is a moist and flavorful meatloaf that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

3. Gravy Poutine

Take your poutine game to the next level by using leftover gravy instead of traditional gravy. Layer crispy fries, cheese curds, and your reheated gravy, then watch as it all melts together into a heavenly combination of flavors. This indulgent treat is perfect for when you’re craving something comforting and satisfying.

4. Gravy-Stuffed Biscuits

Elevate your biscuits to a whole new level by stuffing them with leftover gravy. Simply make your favorite biscuit dough, flatten it, spoon some gravy onto each piece, and fold it over to seal. Bake until golden brown, and you’ll have a batch of savory, gravy-filled biscuits that are perfect for breakfast or as a side dish.

5. Gravy Shepherd’s Pie

Give your classic shepherd’s pie a twist by using leftover gravy as the base instead of the usual sauce. Layer your gravy with cooked ground meat, vegetables, and mashed potatoes, then bake until golden and bubbly. The result is a comforting and hearty dish that will warm your soul.

6. Gravy-Stuffed Omelette

Take your breakfast game up a notch with a gravy-stuffed omelette. Whisk your eggs, pour them into a heated pan, and when they start to set, spoon some reheated gravy onto one half. Fold the other half over the gravy, cook until the eggs are fully set, and enjoy a fluffy omelette with a burst of savory flavor.

7. Gravy-Stuffed Mushrooms

Impress your guests with these elegant and flavorful appetizers. Remove the stems from button mushrooms, fill the caps with leftover gravy, and bake until the mushrooms are tender. The combination of the earthy mushrooms and the rich gravy creates a mouthwatering bite-sized treat.

8. Gravy-Stuffed Pasta Shells

Get creative with pasta by stuffing cooked shells with leftover gravy. Mix the gravy with your choice of cheese, herbs, and cooked ground meat, then fill each shell with the mixture. Bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and you’ll have a unique and delicious pasta dish that will leave everyone asking for the recipe.

So, the next time you find yourself with leftover gravy, don’t let it go to waste. Put your culinary skills to the test and try out these creative and tasty ways to use up that extra sauce. From savory stuffed biscuits to indulgent poutine, there’s no limit to what you can create with leftover gravy. Happy cooking!
