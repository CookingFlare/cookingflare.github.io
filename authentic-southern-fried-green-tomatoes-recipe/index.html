Authentic Southern Fried Green Tomatoes Recipe


Fried green tomatoes are a classic Southern dish that embodies the rich culinary heritage of the region. This dish showcases the tangy flavor of unripe tomatoes enveloped in a crispy, golden-brown crust. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a kitchen novice, this authentic Southern fried green tomatoes recipe will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of the South.

The History of Fried Green Tomatoes

Fried green tomatoes have a storied history that dates back to the late 19th century. They became popular in the Southern United States, particularly in Georgia and Alabama. The dish is often associated with rural Southern cuisine, where resourcefulness and creativity in the kitchen were essential. During the summer months, when tomatoes were plentiful, unripe tomatoes were picked and fried to create a delicious side dish or snack.

The dish gained national prominence with the publication of the novel Fried Green Tomatoes at the Whistle Stop Cafe by Fannie Flagg in 1987, which was later adapted into a film. This cultural moment introduced fried green tomatoes to a wider audience, solidifying their status as a beloved Southern delicacy.

Selecting the Perfect Green Tomatoes

The key to making authentic Southern fried green tomatoes lies in selecting the right tomatoes. Look for firm, unripe tomatoes that are bright green in color. They should be slightly hard to the touch, indicating that they are not overripe. Avoid tomatoes with blemishes or soft spots, as these may not hold up well during frying.

Some popular varieties for frying include:

  • Green Zebras: Known for their unique striped appearance, these tomatoes have a sweet-tart flavor.
  • Cherokee Green: A heritage variety with a rich, tangy taste that holds its shape well when fried.
  • Early Girl: While typically a red variety, the unripe green version is perfect for frying.

Ingredients You Will Need

For the best results, gather the following ingredients:

For the Fried Green Tomatoes

  • 4 medium green tomatoes
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornmeal (preferably stone-ground for texture)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (smoked or regular)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Remoulade Sauce (Optional)

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Tomatoes

  1. Slice the Tomatoes: Begin by washing the tomatoes thoroughly. Remove the stems and slice them into 1/4-inch thick rounds. Lay the slices on a paper towel and sprinkle a little salt over them. This will help draw out excess moisture and enhance the flavor. Let them sit for about 10-15 minutes.

  2. Set Up Your Breading Station: In one shallow dish, add the all-purpose flour. In a second dish, combine the cornmeal, paprika, black pepper, and cayenne pepper. In a third bowl, whisk together the eggs and buttermilk until well combined.

Step 2: Bread the Tomatoes

  1. Dredging Process: Take each tomato slice and first dip it into the flour, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Shake off any excess flour.

  2. Egg Wash: Next, dip the floured tomato slice into the egg and buttermilk mixture, allowing any excess to drip off.

  3. Coat with Cornmeal Mixture: Finally, press the tomato slice into the cornmeal mixture, making sure it’s fully coated. Repeat this process with all the tomato slices.

Step 3: Fry the Tomatoes

  1. Heat the Oil: In a heavy skillet (cast iron works best), pour enough vegetable oil to cover the bottom of the pan by about 1/4 inch. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C). You can test the oil by dropping a small piece of bread into it; if it sizzles immediately, the oil is ready.

  2. Fry in Batches: Carefully place a few breaded tomato slices into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until they are golden brown and crispy.

  3. Drain and Keep Warm: Once cooked, transfer the fried green tomatoes to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. If needed, you can keep them warm in a preheated oven while you fry the remaining batches.

Step 4: Prepare the Remoulade Sauce (Optional)

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, and chopped parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well until smooth and creamy.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Plate the fried green tomatoes while they are still warm. Serve them as a side dish, appetizer, or even as a topping on a Southern-style burger. Drizzle with the remoulade sauce or serve it on the side for dipping.

Tips for Perfect Fried Green Tomatoes

  • Temperature Control: Maintaining the right oil temperature is crucial for achieving that perfect crispy texture. If the oil is too hot, the outside may burn before the inside is cooked. If it’s too cool, the tomatoes may absorb too much oil and become greasy.

  • Experiment with Seasoning: Feel free to customize the seasoning in your cornmeal mixture. Adding garlic powder, onion powder, or even a pinch of sugar can give your fried green tomatoes a unique flavor profile.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the breaded tomatoes ahead of time and refrigerate them. Just remember to let them come to room temperature before frying to ensure even cooking.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Fried green tomatoes pair wonderfully with a range of dishes. Consider serving them alongside fried catfish, grilled meats, or a fresh salad for a complete Southern feast.

Conclusion

Fried green tomatoes are more than just a dish; they are a celebration of Southern tradition and flavor. With their crispy exterior and tangy interior, they are sure to delight your family and friends. Whether you’re enjoying them at a backyard barbecue, a Sunday dinner, or as a comforting snack, this authentic Southern fried green tomatoes recipe is bound to become a favorite in your kitchen.

Remember, cooking is all about experimenting and finding what works best for you. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a taste of the South right at home!
