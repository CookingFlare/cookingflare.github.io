Delicious Winter Frittata Recipes for a Savory Breakfast


As the cold winter months set in, there’s nothing quite like a warm and hearty breakfast to start the day off right. Frittatas are the perfect dish for a cozy winter morning – they’re easy to make, versatile, and packed with delicious flavors. In this post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering winter frittata recipes that will bring comfort and warmth to your breakfast table.

Spinach and Feta Frittata

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 25 minutes

Servings: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Heat the olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the diced red bell pepper and red onion, and sauté until they are softened.
  4. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until it wilts.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet.
  6. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese evenly over the top of the frittata.
  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the frittata is set in the center.
  8. Once done, remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Potato and Bacon Frittata

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Servings: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 6 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, beat the eggs and season with salt and pepper.
  3. In an oven-safe skillet, cook the chopped bacon until it’s crispy. Remove the bacon from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Add the sliced potatoes to the skillet with the bacon fat and cook until they are tender and slightly golden.
  5. Remove the potatoes from the skillet and set aside.
  6. Melt the butter in the skillet over medium heat. Add the beaten eggs, cooked potatoes, bacon, cheddar cheese, and chopped chives.
  7. Cook for a few minutes until the edges start to set.
  8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the frittata is golden and puffed.
  9. Let the frittata cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Mushroom and Swiss Chard Frittata

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 25 minutes

Servings: 4-6

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 2 cups chopped Swiss chard
  • 1/2 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped shallots
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your broiler.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, salt, pepper, and dried thyme.
  3. Heat the olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they are golden brown.
  4. Add the chopped Swiss chard and shallots to the skillet, and sauté until the chard wilts.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet.
  6. Sprinkle the shredded Swiss cheese evenly over the top of the frittata.
  7. Place the skillet under the broiler for 3-5 minutes, or until the frittata is set and golden on top.
  8. Once done, remove from the broiler and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

These delicious winter frittata recipes are sure to warm your soul and satisfy your cravings for a savory breakfast. Whether you prefer the earthy flavors of mushrooms and Swiss chard or the classic combination of potatoes and bacon, there’s a frittata recipe here to suit every palate. So, embrace the winter mornings with these delightful frittatas and enjoy a comforting start to your day!
