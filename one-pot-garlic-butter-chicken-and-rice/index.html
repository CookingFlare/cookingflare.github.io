One Pot Garlic Butter Chicken and Rice


If you’re looking for a delicious and hassle-free dinner option, then this One Pot Garlic Butter Chicken and Rice recipe is for you. With just a few simple ingredients and minimal cleanup, you can have a flavorful and satisfying meal on the table in no time. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup of long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the butter and let it melt.

  2. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then add them to the skillet. Cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until browned. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic, diced onion, and diced red bell pepper. Cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables are softened.

  4. Add the rice to the skillet and stir to coat it with the butter and vegetables. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the rice is slightly toasted.

  5. Pour in the chicken broth and add the dried thyme. Give everything a good stir, then bring the mixture to a boil.

  6. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and place the chicken thighs on top of the rice. Cover the skillet with a lid and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.

  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and let it sit, covered, for about 5 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the rice to absorb any remaining liquid.

  8. Serve the One Pot Garlic Butter Chicken and Rice hot, garnished with fresh parsley. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • You can customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables, such as peas, carrots, or mushrooms. Simply sauté them with the onion and bell pepper before adding the rice.

  • For extra flavor, you can marinate the chicken thighs in a mixture of garlic, butter, and herbs before cooking them. This will infuse even more deliciousness into the dish.

  • If you prefer a creamier texture, you can stir in some heavy cream or grated Parmesan cheese at the end of cooking. This will add a rich and velvety touch to the dish.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop for a quick and easy meal.

Final Thoughts

One Pot Garlic Butter Chicken and Rice is a versatile and satisfying dish that is perfect for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. With its simple preparation and delicious flavors, it’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Give it a try and enjoy the convenience of a complete meal cooked in just one pot. Happy cooking!
