Satisfying Salisbury Steak: A Classic Comfort Food Recipe


There’s something timeless and comforting about a classic Salisbury steak. This hearty dish has been a staple in American homes for generations, and for good reason. The combination of seasoned ground beef, flavorful gravy, and savory onions creates a dish that never fails to satisfy. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or simply craving some old-fashioned comfort food, Salisbury steak is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/3 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 egg
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Salisbury Steaks: In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, chopped onion, egg, garlic, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix the ingredients together until well combined. Shape the mixture into oval patties, about 1 inch thick.

  2. Brown the Steaks: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the Salisbury steaks and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until browned. Remove the steaks from the skillet and set aside.

  3. Saute the Onions and Mushrooms: In the same skillet, add the sliced onions and mushrooms. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the onions are softened and the mushrooms are golden brown.

  4. Prepare the Gravy: In a small bowl, mix the flour and water until smooth to create a slurry. Pour the beef broth into the skillet with the onions and mushrooms. Stir in the flour slurry and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook until the gravy has thickened.

  5. Simmer the Steaks in Gravy: Return the Salisbury steaks to the skillet, nestling them into the gravy. Simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the steaks to absorb the flavors of the gravy.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once the steaks are cooked through and the flavors have melded, it’s time to serve. Spoon the rich gravy over the steaks and pair them with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes or buttered green beans.

Tips and Variations

  • For a richer flavor, you can add a splash of red wine to the gravy.
  • If you prefer a thicker gravy, increase the amount of flour used for the slurry.
  • Feel free to customize the seasoning of the Salisbury steaks to suit your taste. Add a dash of paprika or dried herbs for extra flavor.

Conclusion

Satisfying Salisbury steak is a classic comfort food that never goes out of style. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a taste of nostalgia, this recipe delivers on flavor and satisfaction. With its simple ingredients and rich, savory gravy, Salisbury steak is a timeless dish that deserves a place in every home cook’s repertoire. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and treat yourself to a hearty helping of this beloved comfort food classic.
