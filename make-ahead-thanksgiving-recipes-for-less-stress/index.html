Make-Ahead Thanksgiving Recipes for Less Stress


Thanksgiving is a time to show gratitude and share delicious meals with family and friends. However, juggling multiple dishes on the big day can be overwhelming. To ease the stress, include make-ahead recipes in your menu planning. These dishes can be prepared ahead of time, allowing you to enjoy the holiday without spending all your time in the kitchen.

1. Make-Ahead Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a staple at every Thanksgiving dinner. Make them ahead of time and store them in the refrigerator until you’re ready to serve.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped chives, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes in a large pot until soft, about 15-20 minutes.
  2. Drain and return the potatoes to the pot.
  3. Add the milk and butter, then mash until smooth.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Refrigerate the mashed potatoes until ready to serve.
  6. Reheat in the microwave or oven before serving and garnish with chopped chives.

2. Bread Stuffing

This classic bread stuffing can be made ahead and frozen, taking the pressure off on Thanksgiving day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf stale bread, cubed
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped celery
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups grated Granny Smith apples

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Toast the cubed bread in the oven until golden brown, about 10-15 minutes. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the onions and celery in the butter over medium heat until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the parsley, chicken broth, eggs, and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Mix in the toasted bread and apples.
  6. Transfer the stuffing mixture to a greased baking dish.
  7. Cover and refrigerate until ready to bake.
  8. Reheat the stuffing in a 350°F (175°C) oven for about 30 minutes before serving.

3. Cranberry Sauce

This homemade cranberry sauce adds a burst of flavor to your Thanksgiving table. Make it ahead of time for easy assembly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 (12-ounce) bag fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/4 cup chopped orange
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh rosemary

Instructions:

  1. In a large Saucepan bring the water and sugar to a boil, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Add the cranberries, and cook until they burst open, about 10 minutes.
  3. Stir in the orange juice, orange zest, and chopped orange.
  4. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Remove from the heat and stir in the rosemary.
  6. Let the cranberry sauce cool and then refrigerate until ready to serve.

4. Make-Ahead Gravy

No need to worry about making gravy on the day of; prepare it ahead of time and store it in the fridge.

Ingredients:

  • 1 quart turkey broth
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped celery
  • 2 cups chopped mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the onions, celery, and mushrooms, cooking until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir constantly to prevent any lump formation.
  4. Gradually stir in the turkey broth.
  5. Cook until the gravy has thickened, about 10 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Refrigerate the gravy until ready to serve, then reheat before use.

With these make-ahead recipes, your Thanksgiving meal will have less stress and more room for gratitude. Enjoy the day with family and friends without worrying about last-minute preparations in the kitchen.
