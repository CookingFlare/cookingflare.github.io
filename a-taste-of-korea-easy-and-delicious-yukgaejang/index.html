A Taste of Korea: Easy and Delicious Yukgaejang


Yukgaejang, a traditional Korean beef soup, is a staple dish in Korean cuisine. With its rich broth and tender beef, it’s a comforting and nourishing meal that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a delicious and easy-to-make yukgaejang that’s perfect for a chilly evening or a special occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef brisket or beef shank, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 tablespoon of Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup of toasted sesame seeds
  • Salt, to taste
  • Noodles or rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the beef to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes until browned. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.
  3. Add the diced onion to the pot and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Add the beef broth, water, Korean chili flakes, black pepper, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sugar to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 1 hour.
  5. Add the browned beef back to the pot and simmer for an additional 30 minutes, or until the beef is tender.
  6. Season the soup with salt to taste.
  7. Serve the yukgaejang hot, garnished with thinly sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds. You can also serve it with noodles or rice.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a spicier soup, add more Korean chili flakes or use hot sauce to taste.
  • For a clearer broth, skim off any excess fat that rises to the surface before serving.
  • You can also add other ingredients to the soup, such as sliced carrots, diced potatoes, or chopped zucchini, to add more texture and flavor.
  • To make the soup more substantial, add noodles or rice to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking.

The Story Behind Yukgaejang:

Yukgaejang has a long history in Korea, dating back to the Joseon Dynasty. It was originally a peasant dish, made with scraps of beef and vegetables to feed the working class. Over time, the dish became popular among all social classes, and its recipe was passed down from generation to generation. Today, yukgaejang remains a beloved comfort food in Korea, and its rich flavors and hearty texture continue to warm the hearts and bellies of Koreans and international foodies alike.

Conclusion:

Yukgaejang is a delicious and easy-to-make Korean beef soup that’s perfect for any occasion. With its rich broth and tender beef, it’s a comforting and nourishing meal that’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is easy to follow and requires minimal ingredients. So why not give it a try and experience the warm and comforting flavors of Korea for yourself?
