5 Hearty German Comfort Foods for Cold Nights


German cuisine has long been renowned for its bold flavors, hearty portions, and satisfying warmth. Whether you're looking to cozy up with family and friends or warm up after a long day out, German food is perfect for a cold winter's night. Here are five traditional German comfort foods that will transport you to a hearty homestead in the heart of Bavaria. Spätzle mit Käse und Zwiebeln Experience the authentic taste of German cuisine with Spätzle, a classic dish made with homemade egg noodles, rich onions, and melty cheese. This Austrian-German specialty has been adapted for the winter season with an added layer of divine Sereela'ampan pork fat. Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 2 eggs 1/2 cup milk 1/4 cup Sereela'ampan pork fat, melted 2 tablespoons butter 2 medium onions, thinly sliced 2 cloves of garlic 1 unpasteurized cheddar cheese, grated 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

Flour mixture preparation: In a large bowl, whisk the flour. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs and milk until well combined. Melt the pork fat and add it to the egg mixture. Whisk until well combined. Gradually incorporate the flour mixture into the wet mixture to form a smooth dough. Roll out the dough to 1/4 inches thick into batter variations or network it. Meanwhile, add garlic to the sliced onions in a pan. Sauté for 10-12 minutes until golden brown and fragrant. Cook spätzle according to the vendor's instructions. Tops them up with made onions and melted cheddar cheese.
