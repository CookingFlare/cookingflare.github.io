Spanish Flan: A Creamy Delight


If you’re a fan of creamy desserts, then Spanish Flan is a must-try! This classic dessert is known for its smooth texture and rich caramel flavor. Made with simple ingredients, this recipe is surprisingly easy to prepare, making it a perfect choice for both novice and experienced bakers alike.

Ingredients

To make this delectable Spanish Flan, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup granulated sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While the oven is preheating, let’s get started on the caramel sauce.

  2. In a small saucepan, melt the granulated sugar over medium heat. Stir constantly until the sugar melts and turns a golden brown color. Be careful not to let it burn.

  3. Once the caramel is ready, quickly pour it into a 9-inch round baking dish, tilting the dish to ensure the caramel spreads evenly on the bottom. Set aside to cool.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs until well combined. Add the sweetened condensed milk, whole milk, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth and creamy.

  5. Pour the custard mixture into the caramel-coated baking dish. Smooth out the top with a spatula if needed.

  6. Place the baking dish in a larger roasting pan. Pour hot water into the roasting pan, creating a water bath that surrounds the flan dish halfway up the sides.

  7. Carefully transfer the roasting pan with the flan into the preheated oven. Bake for approximately 45-50 minutes or until the flan is set around the edges but still slightly jiggly in the center.

  8. Once baked, remove the flan from the oven and let it cool completely in the water bath. Then, transfer it to the refrigerator to chill for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  9. When you’re ready to serve, run a knife around the edges of the flan to loosen it from the dish. Place a serving plate on top of the dish and invert the flan onto the plate. The caramel sauce will flow over the top, creating a beautiful presentation.

  10. Slice the flan into individual servings and enjoy the creamy, caramel goodness!

Tips for Success

  • To achieve a smooth texture, strain the custard mixture before pouring it into the baking dish. This will remove any lumps and ensure a silky flan.

  • Let the caramel cool slightly before pouring it into the dish. This will prevent the custard from curdling when it comes into contact with the hot caramel.

  • For a twist on the classic recipe, you can add a splash of rum or a sprinkle of cinnamon to the custard mixture. This will add a subtle depth of flavor to your Spanish Flan.

  • Serve the flan chilled for the best taste and texture. The coolness enhances the creamy richness and allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.

Conclusion

Spanish Flan is a dessert that never fails to impress. Its velvety smoothness, combined with the sweet caramel sauce, creates a heavenly treat that will leave your taste buds dancing with delight. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a sweet indulgence, this recipe is sure to satisfy. So why not give it a try and experience the magic of Spanish Flan for yourself?
