Sous Vide Scallops in Garlic Herb Butter


If you’re craving a refined, melt-in-your-mouth seafood experience, look no further than sous vide scallops in garlic herb butter. This dish is not only simple to prepare but also packed with flavors that will impress your guests or elevate a special meal at home. By using the sous vide method, you can achieve perfect scallops every time, with an unparalleled level of tenderness.

In this post, we will walk you through the process of making sous vide scallops in garlic herb butter, step by step. We’ll discuss why sous vide is the ultimate cooking method for scallops, provide a detailed recipe, and share some tips to ensure your dish turns out perfectly. So, let’s get started!

What Is Sous Vide?

Sous vide, a French term meaning “under vacuum,” refers to the process of cooking food in a vacuum-sealed bag submerged in a water bath at a precise, controlled temperature. The sous vide method allows for even cooking, preserving the natural flavors, textures, and moisture of ingredients, all while infusing them with any accompanying seasonings or flavors.

When it comes to seafood like scallops, the sous vide technique is a game-changer. Scallops are delicate, and cooking them at a high temperature can easily result in rubbery, overcooked bites. Sous vide eliminates this problem, ensuring your scallops are tender and juicy every time.

Why Scallops?

Scallops are among the most exquisite and versatile seafood options, prized for their sweet, delicate flavor and buttery texture. They are perfect for a gourmet dinner, whether served as an appetizer or as a main course. Scallops cook quickly, which makes them ideal for sous vide preparation, where precise temperature control is crucial.

In this recipe, we’re pairing the tender scallops with a rich garlic herb butter sauce that complements their natural sweetness. The butter sauce adds layers of flavor, and the garlic infuses each bite with a savory punch. Together, these ingredients create a truly indulgent dish that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients for Sous Vide Scallops in Garlic Herb Butter

Before diving into the cooking process, gather the following ingredients to make this dish:

For the Scallops:

  • 1 lb (about 450g) of fresh sea scallops (preferably dry-packed)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for searing)

For the Garlic Herb Butter:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (1 stick), softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, finely chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Equipment:

  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • Vacuum-seal bags or resealable freezer bags (using the water displacement method)
  • Large pot or container for sous vide cooking
  • Skillet or cast-iron pan for searing

How to Cook Scallops Using Sous Vide

The key to perfectly cooked scallops lies in the precise temperature control of sous vide cooking. Let’s break down the process into easy steps.

Step 1: Preparing the Scallops

Before placing the scallops in the sous vide bag, you’ll need to clean and prepare them. Start by patting the scallops dry with paper towels. This step is essential because excess moisture can prevent the scallops from searing properly later.

If your scallops have the tough side muscle attached, carefully remove it with your fingers or a small knife. The muscle is edible, but it’s slightly chewy, so it’s best to remove it for a more tender result.

Step 2: Seasoning the Scallops

Once the scallops are prepped, season them generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper. Since you’ll be infusing them with garlic herb butter later, you don’t need to overdo it with seasoning at this stage. Just a light coating will do.

Step 3: Vacuum Sealing the Scallops

Place the seasoned scallops into a vacuum-seal bag, ensuring they are arranged in a single layer to ensure even cooking. If you don’t have a vacuum-sealer, you can use a resealable freezer bag and employ the water displacement method. To do this, slowly lower the bag with the scallops into a pot of water, letting the pressure push out the air before sealing it shut.

Step 4: Setting Up the Sous Vide Cooker

Fill a large pot or container with water and attach your sous vide precision cooker. Set the temperature to 122°F (50°C) for tender, medium-rare scallops. For a slightly firmer texture, you can increase the temperature to 130°F (54°C), but keep in mind that scallops cook quickly, and anything above 130°F can result in a tougher texture.

Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, carefully submerge the sealed bag with the scallops into the water. Clip the bag to the side of the pot to keep it fully submerged. Let the scallops cook for 30-45 minutes.

Step 5: Preparing the Garlic Herb Butter

While your scallops are cooking, it’s time to prepare the garlic herb butter. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter with minced garlic, fresh parsley, thyme, lemon zest, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Stir until everything is well incorporated. Season the butter with salt and pepper to taste. Once it’s ready, set it aside.

Step 6: Searing the Scallops

After the scallops have finished cooking in the sous vide bath, it’s time to give them a quick sear. This step is optional, but searing will add a delicious crispy exterior while maintaining the tender, juicy interior.

Heat a skillet or cast-iron pan over high heat and add a tablespoon of olive oil. Once the oil is hot and shimmering, carefully remove the scallops from the bag and add them to the skillet. Sear the scallops for about 30-60 seconds per side, just enough to develop a golden-brown crust. Be sure not to overcook them at this stage, as the scallops have already been perfectly cooked in the sous vide bath.

Step 7: Finishing with Garlic Herb Butter

Once your scallops are seared, transfer them to a serving plate. Immediately spoon the prepared garlic herb butter over the top of each scallop, allowing it to melt beautifully. The butter will infuse the scallops with rich flavor, while the herbs and garlic provide a fragrant, savory finish. You can also garnish with extra chopped parsley or a spritz of lemon juice if desired.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy!

Sous vide scallops in garlic herb butter are ready to be served! Pair them with a side of roasted vegetables, a fresh salad, or even over a bed of creamy risotto for a luxurious meal. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or treating yourself to an indulgent weeknight dinner, this dish is sure to impress.

Tips for Perfect Sous Vide Scallops

  • Fresh is Best: Always use fresh, high-quality scallops for the best flavor. Dry-packed scallops, which have not been treated with water or chemicals, are ideal as they won’t release excess moisture during cooking.

  • Don’t Overcook: Scallops cook quickly, even at low temperatures. While sous vide cooking offers more leeway, it’s important not to exceed the recommended cook times to avoid overcooking and losing the delicate texture.

  • Sealing the Bag: For optimal results, ensure that the vacuum-sealed bag is tightly sealed. If using the water displacement method, make sure the bag is as airtight as possible to prevent any water from getting in.

  • Garlic Butter Variations: While garlic herb butter is a classic choice, you can easily customize the butter to suit your preferences. Try adding a little parmesan cheese, chili flakes for heat, or even a splash of white wine for extra depth.

Pairing Suggestions

Sous vide scallops in garlic herb butter can be paired with various sides and beverages to create a well-rounded meal. Here are a few ideas:

  • Sides: Serve with roasted asparagus, mashed potatoes, sautéed spinach, or a fresh green salad with a citrus vinaigrette.

  • Wine Pairing: A crisp white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay complements the delicate flavor of the scallops. For a more decadent pairing, try a sparkling wine like Champagne to add an element of celebration to your meal.

Conclusion

Sous vide scallops in garlic herb butter are an elegant and foolproof way to enjoy this delicious seafood at home. The sous vide method ensures that your scallops turn out perfectly cooked every time, while the garlic herb butter adds an indulgent richness that elevates the dish to gourmet status. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or impressing guests, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

So, get your sous vide equipment ready, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to a dish that’s both simple and sophisticated. Happy cooking!
