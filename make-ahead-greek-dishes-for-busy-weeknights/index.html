Make Ahead Greek Dishes for Busy Weeknights


In the whirlwind of modern life, finding time to prepare healthy and delicious meals can feel like a luxury. Busy weeknights often leave us scrambling for quick solutions that might not always be the best for our health or our wallets. However, with a bit of planning and some delightful Greek-inspired recipes, you can make ahead meals that are not only simple to prepare but also packed with flavor and nutrition. This post will provide you with a selection of make-ahead Greek dishes perfect for those hectic evenings.

Why Greek Cuisine?

Greek cuisine is renowned for its fresh ingredients, vibrant flavors, and health benefits. It emphasizes vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, and healthy fats, making it an excellent choice for nutritious meals. The Mediterranean diet, which forms the basis of Greek cooking, is associated with numerous health benefits, including heart health, weight management, and a lower risk of chronic diseases. By incorporating these dishes into your weekly meal prep, you can enjoy the taste of Greece while supporting your well-being.

Tips for Meal Prep Success

Before diving into the recipes, here are some essential tips to ensure your meal prep is successful:

  1. Plan Your Week: Take some time at the beginning of the week to outline your meals. Choose recipes that can be made in bulk and stored easily.

  2. Use High-Quality Containers: Invest in good-quality glass or BPA-free plastic containers that can be used for storage. This helps keep your food fresh and makes reheating a breeze.

  3. Batch Cooking: Prepare larger quantities of staples like grains, proteins, and roasted vegetables that can be mixed and matched throughout the week.

  4. Storage: Label your containers with the date and contents to keep track of freshness.

  5. Freezer-Friendly: Identify which dishes can be frozen for later use. Many Greek dishes hold up well in the freezer.

Make Ahead Greek Dishes

1. Greek Quinoa Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse quinoa under cold water and drain.
  2. In a saucepan, combine quinoa and water (or broth). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until quinoa is fluffy and water is absorbed.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa with cherry tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, red onion, olives, feta cheese, and parsley.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper. Pour over the salad and toss to combine.
  5. Store in airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to five days. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

2. Spanakopita (Spinach Pie)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound phyllo dough, thawed
  • 1 pound fresh spinach, chopped (or 2 cups frozen, thawed and drained)
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent, then add spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine spinach mixture, ricotta, feta, and beaten eggs. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Lay one sheet of phyllo dough in a greased baking dish, brushing with olive oil. Layer another sheet on top and repeat until you have about 6 layers.
  5. Spread the spinach mixture evenly over the phyllo. Cover with more sheets of phyllo, brushing each layer with olive oil, until you have at least 6 layers on top.
  6. Score the top layer into squares for easy serving, then bake for 30-40 minutes, until golden brown.
  7. Allow to cool before slicing and storing in the refrigerator for up to a week. Reheat in the oven before serving.

3. Greek Chicken Souvlaki

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (soaked in water if wooden)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, salt, and pepper. Add chicken cubes and marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight for best flavor.
  2. Preheat the grill or grill pan over medium-high heat.
  3. Thread marinated chicken onto skewers and grill for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. Allow to cool before storing in airtight containers. These souvlaki can be enjoyed cold in salads or reheated for wraps throughout the week.

4. Moussaka

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced into rounds
  • 1 pound ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 2 cups béchamel sauce (see instructions below)
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Béchamel Sauce Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Salt the eggplant slices and let them sit for about 30 minutes to draw out moisture. Rinse and pat dry.
  3. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Fry the eggplant slices until golden and soft. Set aside on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  4. In the same skillet, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant. Add ground meat and cook until browned. Stir in crushed tomatoes, cinnamon, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. For the béchamel sauce, melt butter in a saucepan. Whisk in flour and cook for 1 minute. Gradually whisk in milk, stirring until thickened. Season with nutmeg and salt.
  6. In a baking dish, layer half of the eggplant, followed by the meat mixture, and then the remaining eggplant. Pour béchamel sauce on top.
  7. Bake for 45 minutes until the top is golden. Allow to cool before slicing and storing in the refrigerator. Moussaka can be reheated in the oven or microwave.

5. Greek Yogurt Parfait

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups Greek yogurt
  • 2 cups granola
  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • Honey or maple syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a jar or container, layer Greek yogurt, granola, and mixed berries. Repeat layers until containers are full.
  2. Drizzle with honey or maple syrup if desired.
  3. Store in the refrigerator for up to five days. These parfaits make for a quick breakfast or snack option.

Conclusion

With these make-ahead Greek dishes, busy weeknights can be transformed from chaotic to culinary bliss. By preparing these meals in advance, you not only save time but also ensure that you and your loved ones enjoy wholesome and flavorful food throughout the week. So gather your ingredients, set aside a couple of hours for meal prep, and savor the taste of Greece without the stress. Enjoy your culinary journey!
