How to Make a Deep-Dish Pizza: An Easy Recipe


Deep-dish pizza is a beloved style of pizza that hails from the Windy City of Chicago. It’s characterized by its thick, buttery crust, a hearty layer of cheese, savory meat or vegetable toppings, and a chunky tomato sauce on top. If you’re craving a slice of this iconic pizza but don’t want to deal with the hassle of ordering out, you’re in the right place. In this easy recipe, I’ll guide you step-by-step on how to create your own homemade deep-dish pizza that will rival your local pizzeria.

Ingredients for the Perfect Deep-Dish Pizza

Before you begin assembling your deep-dish pizza, you need to gather all the ingredients. Here’s a breakdown of everything you’ll need:

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (about 110°F/43°C)
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornmeal (optional, for dusting)

For the Pizza Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese (preferably whole milk)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 pound Italian sausage (mild or spicy, your choice)
  • 1 cup sliced pepperoni (optional)
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 cup sliced black olives (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)

Tools and Equipment You’ll Need:

  • 12-inch deep-dish pizza pan or a cast-iron skillet
  • Rolling pin
  • Large mixing bowls
  • Wooden spoon or spatula
  • Knife and chopping board
  • Measuring cups and spoons

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

A good deep-dish pizza starts with a solid dough base. The dough for this style of pizza is unique in that it’s slightly thicker and has a buttery texture compared to traditional pizza dough. Follow these steps for the best results:

  1. Activate the Yeast:

    • In a small bowl, combine warm water, yeast, and sugar. Stir until the yeast dissolves, then let it sit for about 5 minutes, or until the mixture becomes foamy. This step ensures that your dough will rise properly.

  2. Mix the Dough:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Once the yeast mixture is ready, add the olive oil to the flour mixture, followed by the yeast mixture. Stir using a wooden spoon until the dough comes together.

  3. Knead the Dough:

    • Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 8 minutes, or until it’s smooth and elastic. Add small amounts of flour if the dough is too sticky, but be careful not to add too much. You want the dough to be soft but not overly dry.

  4. Let the Dough Rise:

    • Lightly oil a large bowl and place the dough inside. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size. This is the time when the dough will develop its airy texture, ready for a perfect deep-dish crust.

Step 2: Make the Pizza Sauce

While the dough is rising, it’s a great time to prepare your pizza sauce. A rich and flavorful sauce is key to a delicious deep-dish pizza. Here’s how to make it:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables:

    • Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for about 5 minutes, until it softens and becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds, or until fragrant.

  2. Prepare the Sauce:

    • Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, and sugar to the saucepan. Stir everything together, then bring the sauce to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Once the sauce thickens and the flavors meld together, remove it from the heat and set it aside to cool.

Step 3: Brown the Sausage

The sausage is one of the star ingredients in your deep-dish pizza. It adds a rich, savory flavor that complements the cheese and sauce perfectly. Here’s how to prepare it:

  1. Cook the Sausage:

    • In a large skillet, heat a little olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the sausage and cook, breaking it apart with a spoon, until it’s fully browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the sausage from the skillet and set it aside on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess fat.

  2. Optional: Add Vegetables or Other Meats:

    • If you want to add additional toppings, now is the time. You can sauté sliced bell peppers, onions, or mushrooms in the same skillet for a few minutes until they soften. You can also add pepperoni, ground beef, or other meats to the mix, depending on your preference.

Step 4: Assemble the Deep-Dish Pizza

Now comes the fun part! Assembling the deep-dish pizza requires careful layering to ensure each slice is packed with flavor and holds up to the generous toppings.

  1. Preheat the Oven:

    • Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). Make sure your oven is fully preheated before you bake the pizza.

  2. Prepare the Pan:

    • Generously grease your 12-inch deep-dish pizza pan or cast-iron skillet with olive oil. You can also sprinkle a little cornmeal on the bottom for extra texture and flavor. This helps prevent the crust from sticking and adds a subtle crunch to the base.

  3. Roll Out the Dough:

    • Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and roll it out into a circle that’s slightly larger than your pizza pan. The dough should be about 1/4-inch thick.

  4. Fit the Dough into the Pan:

    • Carefully transfer the dough into the greased pizza pan, pressing it along the edges and up the sides of the pan. This will create a high crust that can hold all of the delicious fillings. If the dough tears slightly, don’t worry—just patch it up.

  5. Layer the Ingredients:

    • Start by adding a layer of shredded mozzarella cheese at the bottom of the dough. This will create a gooey, melty base that holds everything together. Next, spread an even layer of the cooked sausage, followed by any other optional toppings like pepperoni, bell peppers, or olives.

    • Pour the prepared pizza sauce over the top, spreading it evenly to cover all the ingredients. Finally, sprinkle a generous amount of grated Parmesan cheese over the sauce.

Step 5: Bake the Pizza

The deep-dish pizza needs to bake for a longer time than a regular pizza due to its thick crust and layers of toppings. Here’s how to bake it perfectly:

  1. Bake the Pizza:

    • Place the assembled pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbling and melted. You may want to check the pizza halfway through the baking time to make sure the crust isn’t browning too quickly. If necessary, cover the edges with aluminum foil to prevent over-browning.

  2. Cool Before Slicing:

    • Once the pizza is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool for about 5 minutes. This allows the cheese and sauce to set, making it easier to slice and serve.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Now it’s time to enjoy the fruits of your labor. Slice the deep-dish pizza into wedges, and serve hot with a side salad or your favorite drink. The pizza will be packed with layers of flavor, from the rich crust to the gooey cheese and hearty sauce. This deep-dish pizza recipe is perfect for sharing with family and friends, and it’s sure to be a hit at any gathering.

Tips for Making the Best Deep-Dish Pizza:

  • Use Quality Cheese: The cheese is a key ingredient in deep-dish pizza. Opt for high-quality mozzarella and Parmesan for the best melt and flavor.
  • Let the Dough Rise Slowly: For a dough that’s extra tender, let it rise slowly in the fridge overnight. This can help develop more complex flavors and a better texture.
  • Don’t Overload the Toppings: While it’s tempting to pile on extra toppings, remember that deep-dish pizza is all about balance. Too many toppings can make the pizza soggy and difficult to slice.
  • Experiment with Toppings: While the traditional deep-dish pizza is made with sausage and pepperoni, you can get creative with your toppings. Try adding mushrooms, spinach, or even fresh basil for a twist on the classic.

Conclusion

Making a deep-dish pizza at home is easier than you might think, and it’s a fun way to enjoy a comforting, indulgent meal without having to leave your kitchen. By following these simple steps, you can create a delicious homemade deep-dish pizza with a buttery, flaky crust, flavorful toppings, and a rich tomato sauce that will impress everyone at the table. Whether you’re a seasoned pizza maker or a beginner, this recipe guarantees a satisfying and hearty meal every time. Enjoy the process and, most importantly, enjoy your pizza!
