Healthy Slow Cooker Vegetable and Lentil Stew Recipe


When it comes to hearty, nutritious meals, few dishes can compete with a slow cooker vegetable and lentil stew. This recipe is not only easy to prepare but also packed with flavor and health benefits. Perfect for busy weeknights or meal prep, this stew is a delightful way to incorporate more vegetables and plant-based protein into your diet. Let’s dive into the details of this wholesome dish!

Why Choose Lentils?

Lentils are a powerhouse of nutrition. They are rich in protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, making them an excellent choice for anyone looking to maintain a healthy diet. They are low in calories and high in iron, which is crucial for energy production and overall health. Additionally, lentils are versatile and can absorb flavors beautifully, making them a perfect base for stews and soups.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this delicious slow cooker vegetable and lentil stew, gather the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils (green or brown)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (low-sodium preferred)
  • 2 medium carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium potato, diced (you can leave the skin on for added nutrients)
  • 1 cup butternut squash, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Start by rinsing the lentils under cold water to remove any debris. Chop all your vegetables into bite-sized pieces. This not only helps with even cooking but also makes for a more appealing presentation.

Step 2: Combine Ingredients in the Slow Cooker

In your slow cooker, add the rinsed lentils, vegetable broth, diced carrots, celery, onion, garlic, potato, butternut squash, green beans, and diced tomatoes. Sprinkle in the dried thyme, oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the olive oil over the top to enhance the flavor.

Step 3: Cooking Time

Set your slow cooker to low and let it cook for 7-8 hours. If you’re short on time, you can set it to high for 3-4 hours. The longer cooking time allows the flavors to meld beautifully, resulting in a rich and hearty stew.

Step 4: Final Touches

Once the cooking time is up, give the stew a good stir. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. If you prefer a thicker stew, you can mash some of the lentils and potatoes against the side of the slow cooker with a fork.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Ladle the stew into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley if desired. This stew pairs wonderfully with crusty bread or a simple side salad for a complete meal.

Health Benefits of This Stew

This vegetable and lentil stew is not just delicious; it’s also incredibly healthy. Here are some of the benefits:

  • High in Fiber: The combination of lentils and vegetables provides a significant amount of dietary fiber, which aids digestion and helps maintain a healthy weight.
  • Rich in Vitamins and Minerals: The variety of vegetables ensures you’re getting a wide range of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin A, vitamin C, potassium, and iron.
  • Low in Fat: This stew is low in fat, making it a heart-healthy option that can fit into various dietary plans.
  • Vegan and Gluten-Free: This recipe is suitable for vegans and those with gluten sensitivities, making it a versatile dish for any gathering.

Tips for Customization

One of the best things about this stew is its versatility. Here are some ideas for customizing the recipe to suit your taste:

  • Add More Vegetables: Feel free to throw in any vegetables you have on hand, such as zucchini, bell peppers, or spinach. Just be mindful of the cooking times, as some vegetables may cook faster than others.
  • Spice It Up: If you enjoy a bit of heat, consider adding a pinch of red pepper flakes or a diced jalapeño to the mix.
  • Herbs and Spices: Experiment with different herbs and spices. Fresh herbs like basil or cilantro can add a fresh flavor, while spices like cumin or coriander can give the stew an earthy depth.
  • Protein Boost: For an extra protein kick, consider adding cooked quinoa or chickpeas to the stew during the last hour of cooking.

Storing and Reheating

This stew is perfect for meal prep! You can store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. It also freezes well, so feel free to make a double batch and freeze portions for later. When reheating, you may need to add a splash of vegetable broth or water to loosen the stew, as it may thicken in the fridge or freezer.

Conclusion

This healthy slow cooker vegetable and lentil stew is a fantastic addition to your recipe collection. It’s easy to make, packed with nutrients, and incredibly satisfying. Whether you’re looking for a comforting meal on a chilly evening or a nutritious option for meal prep, this stew has you covered. Enjoy the warmth and goodness of this delightful dish, and feel free to share it with family and friends!
