Mouthwatering Dairy-Free Chicken Tikka Masala


  1. In a large bowl, combine the dairy-free yogurt, lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, ground cumin, ground coriander, ground turmeric, paprika, cayenne pepper, and salt. Mix well to form a marinade.

  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and coat them thoroughly. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or overnight for maximum flavor.

  3. Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat. If using a grill, thread the marinated chicken onto skewers. If using an oven, place the chicken pieces on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  4. Cook the chicken for about 10-12 minutes on each side, or until it is cooked through and slightly charred. If using an oven, cook the chicken at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until done.

  5. While the chicken is cooking, prepare the tikka masala sauce. In a large pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the tomato puree and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  6. Stir in the coconut milk and simmer for another 5 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly.

  7. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the grill or oven and transfer it to the pan with the tikka masala sauce. Stir to coat the chicken pieces evenly.

  8. Simmer the chicken in the sauce for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  9. Serve the mouthwatering dairy-free chicken tikka masala over rice or with naan bread. Garnish with fresh cilantro for added freshness and flavor.

Enjoy this flavorful and dairy-free twist on a classic Indian dish. The tender chicken, marinated in a spiced yogurt mixture, pairs perfectly with the creamy and aromatic tikka masala sauce. This recipe is sure to impress your family and friends, and they won’t even miss the dairy. So go ahead, indulge in the rich flavors of this mouthwatering chicken tikka masala, guilt-free!

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier version, increase the amount of cayenne pepper in the marinade or sauce.
  • If you prefer a milder flavor, reduce the amount of cayenne pepper or omit it altogether.
  • If you don’t have dairy-free yogurt, you can use a dairy-free alternative like coconut milk yogurt or cashew yogurt.
  • Customize the recipe by adding vegetables like bell peppers, onions, or even green peas to the tikka masala sauce.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop or in the microwave before serving.
