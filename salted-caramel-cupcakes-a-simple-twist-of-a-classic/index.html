Salted Caramel Cupcakes: A Simple Twist of a Classic


Cupcakes are timeless. They manage to put a smile on your face, and taking that first bite is always filled with pure joy. Usually, they are standalone desserts that come in different flavors, but what if you could elevate the classic vanilla cupcake to another level? Introducing Salted Caramel Cupcakes – a fusion of simplicity with a desirable twist that tantalize your taste buds. This delightful dessert is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser that’ll have everyone coming back for more.

Ingredients:

For the Vanilla Cupcakes:

  1. 1 and 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 1 teaspoon baking powder
  3. 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  4. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  5. 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  6. 1 cup granulated sugar
  7. 2 large eggs
  8. 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  9. 3/4 cup milk
  10. Water

For the Salted Caramel Buttercream:

  1. 3 sticks unsalted butter, softened
  2. 3 cups confectioners’ sugar
  3. 1/2 cup salted caramel sauce
  4. 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  5. A pinch of salt

For the Salted Caramel:

  1. 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  2. 3 tablespoons water
  3. 1/4 cup heavy cream
  4. 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  5. 2 tablespoons fleur de sel or kosher salt

Instructions:

Making the Vanilla Cupcakes:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (177°C) and line a 12-cup muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Using a stand or hand mixer, cream the butter and sugar together until light and fluffy.
  4. Gradually add in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  5. Slowly incorporate the flour mixture and milk alternately into the wet ingredients, beginning and ending with the flour mixture.
  6. Fill each cupcake liner with an equal amount of batter, about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cupcakes to cool for a few minutes in the pan before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Making the Salted Caramel:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the sugar and water over medium heat.
  2. Allow the sugar to dissolve completely without stirring, but swirling the pan occasionally.
  3. As the sugar reaches a boiling point, reduce the heat to low and simmer until it turns a deep amber color.
  4. Remove from heat and carefully whisk in the heavy cream and butter.
  5. Add the salt and stir until smooth, then allow the caramel to cool.

Making the Salted Caramel Buttercream:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, beat the butter until creamy and light using an electric mixer.
  2. Gradually add the confectioners’ sugar, salted caramel sauce, and vanilla extract, beating until smooth.
  3. If the frosting is too stiff, add a little more salted caramel sauce or a tablespoon of heavy cream to achieve the desired consistency.

Assembling the Salted Caramel Cupcakes:

  1. Once the cupcakes have completely cooled, use a small pairing knife to cut out a small cone-shaped piece from the center of each cupcake, being careful not to cut all the way to the bottom.
  2. Fill the hole with a small amount of salted caramel sauce.
  3. Scoop a generous amount of salted caramel buttercream onto the top of each cupcake, covering the hole and the edges.
  4. Drizzle additional salted caramel sauce over the frosting for added flavor and visual appeal. You could also sprinkle some finely chopped sea salt or fleur de sel on top for an extra twist.

Now that you have your Salted Caramel Cupcakes, enjoy these heavenly treats with every bite uncovering a delicious fusion of sweet and salty flavors. Bake these for friends or family, and you’ll soon become the talk of the town. Happy baking!
