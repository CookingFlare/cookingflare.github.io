10 Delicious Fall Recipes for Your Grill


Fall is the perfect time to fire up the grill and indulge in hearty, flavorful dishes that showcase the best of the season’s harvest. The crisp air, cooler evenings, and vibrant colors provide the ideal backdrop for grilling a variety of dishes that highlight the rich flavors of autumn. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or simply enjoying a quiet evening at home, these 10 delicious fall recipes for your grill will bring comfort, flavor, and warmth to your table.

1. Grilled Apple and Sausage Skewers

Sausage and apples may not be the first combination that comes to mind when you think of grilling, but this dish is a fall favorite for a reason. The smoky flavor from the grill perfectly complements the sweetness of the apples and the savory, spiced sausages. To make these skewers, cut apples into large chunks and alternate them with pieces of sausage on skewers. Season with cinnamon, thyme, and a little bit of honey for an extra touch of sweetness. Grill over medium heat, turning occasionally, until the sausage is cooked through and the apples have caramelized slightly.

Pro Tip: Try using different types of sausage—apple, bratwurst, or even chorizo—for unique flavor combinations.

2. Grilled Butternut Squash Steaks

Butternut squash is a quintessential fall vegetable, and grilling it enhances its natural sweetness while adding a delightful smoky flavor. Slice the squash into thick steaks, then brush them with olive oil, and season with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of smoked paprika. Grill the squash on medium heat for 5-6 minutes per side, until it’s tender and has grill marks. To take it to the next level, drizzle the grilled squash with a balsamic reduction or top with crumbled feta and toasted pumpkin seeds.

Pro Tip: To speed up the grilling process, you can precook the squash in the microwave or oven for a few minutes before grilling to soften it up.

3. Grilled Sweet Potato Wedges

Sweet potatoes are a fall staple and grilling them brings out their natural sweetness. Cut the sweet potatoes into wedges and toss them with olive oil, salt, pepper, and a pinch of cayenne for a bit of heat. Grill the wedges over medium heat, turning them occasionally, for about 10-15 minutes, until they are tender on the inside and slightly crispy on the outside. Serve them with a creamy dipping sauce, such as garlic aioli or a tangy yogurt-based dip, for a perfect fall side dish.

Pro Tip: For added depth of flavor, sprinkle the grilled sweet potato wedges with smoked salt or a touch of cinnamon.

4. Grilled Autumn Veggie Medley

A colorful medley of autumn vegetables is a great way to add variety to your grilled meals. This fall recipe features a mix of hearty vegetables like Brussels sprouts, carrots, parsnips, and bell peppers. Cut the veggies into uniform sizes to ensure even cooking, and toss them with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, rosemary, and thyme. Grill over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the veggies are tender and have a smoky char. This dish works wonderfully as a side or can be enjoyed as a vegetarian main course.

Pro Tip: For extra flavor, sprinkle some Parmesan cheese over the grilled vegetables just before serving.

5. Grilled Pork Chops with Maple Mustard Glaze

Pork chops are perfect for grilling in the fall, and they pair beautifully with the rich flavors of maple and mustard. For this recipe, season the pork chops with salt, pepper, and a dash of garlic powder. While the pork chops are grilling, prepare a glaze by combining maple syrup, Dijon mustard, and a splash of apple cider vinegar. Brush the glaze onto the pork chops during the last few minutes of grilling, allowing it to caramelize slightly. The result is a savory-sweet dish with a beautiful balance of flavors.

Pro Tip: Let the grilled pork chops rest for a few minutes before serving to retain their juiciness.

6. Grilled Chicken Thighs with Cranberry Salsa

Chicken thighs are juicy, flavorful, and perfect for grilling. For this recipe, marinate the chicken thighs in a mixture of olive oil, garlic, thyme, and a splash of apple cider vinegar for at least 30 minutes. Grill the chicken over medium-high heat, turning occasionally until it’s fully cooked. While the chicken is grilling, prepare a refreshing cranberry salsa by combining fresh cranberries, diced red onion, cilantro, lime juice, and a touch of honey. The tangy, sweet salsa complements the rich flavor of the grilled chicken perfectly.

Pro Tip: If you want to make the salsa ahead of time, it will stay fresh for up to two days in the fridge.

7. Grilled Pumpkin Burgers

Pumpkin isn’t just for pie—it can also be a fantastic base for burgers! To make these grilled pumpkin burgers, combine cooked and mashed pumpkin with breadcrumbs, an egg, garlic, and your favorite spices (such as cinnamon, nutmeg, and cumin). Form the mixture into patties and grill them over medium heat for about 5-6 minutes per side until golden brown. Serve the burgers on toasted buns with a dollop of creamy goat cheese or your favorite fall-inspired condiments.

Pro Tip: These burgers can easily be made vegetarian or vegan by swapping out the egg and breadcrumbs for plant-based alternatives.

8. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili Lime Butter

Grilled corn is a summer classic, but it’s just as delicious in the fall. To make this dish, husk the corn and brush it with olive oil, then place it directly on the grill over medium heat. Grill the corn, turning occasionally, for 10-15 minutes until the kernels are lightly charred. In a small bowl, mix together butter, chili powder, lime zest, and a squeeze of lime juice. After grilling, slather the corn with the spicy, tangy butter for a zesty twist on a beloved classic.

Pro Tip: For an even more indulgent version, add crumbled cotija cheese on top of the buttered corn for a creamy, salty finish.

9. Grilled Pear and Gorgonzola Salad

Pears are one of fall’s most delightful fruits, and grilling them intensifies their sweetness and adds a smoky edge. Slice ripe pears in half and remove the core. Brush them with a little bit of olive oil and grill them cut-side down for about 4-5 minutes until grill marks appear and they soften. To assemble the salad, toss mixed greens with candied pecans, crumbled Gorgonzola cheese, and the grilled pear halves. Drizzle with a balsamic glaze or vinaigrette for a refreshing and elegant fall salad.

Pro Tip: Add thinly sliced prosciutto for an extra layer of savory flavor that pairs wonderfully with the sweetness of the pears.

10. Grilled Caramelized Onion and Brie Sandwiches

Grilled cheese sandwiches are a comfort food classic, but adding caramelized onions and creamy Brie makes for an elevated fall version. Start by caramelizing onions in a pan over low heat until they’re golden and sweet. Spread butter on two slices of your favorite hearty bread and layer them with a generous amount of Brie and the caramelized onions. Grill the sandwiches over medium heat until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melting. The result is a gooey, savory sandwich with the perfect balance of rich flavors.

Pro Tip: For an extra touch, drizzle the sandwich with a bit of honey before serving to enhance the sweetness of the caramelized onions.

Final Thoughts

These 10 fall recipes for your grill are designed to bring the comforting flavors of autumn to your outdoor cooking experience. Whether you’re grilling vegetables, meats, or even fruit, the smoky, charred flavors from the grill are the perfect complement to the season’s rich harvest. From savory dishes like grilled sausage skewers and maple-glazed pork chops to sweet treats like grilled pears and pumpkin burgers, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

As the weather cools and the leaves change, embrace the season’s best flavors with these easy-to-make, crowd-pleasing recipes. So, fire up your grill, gather your loved ones, and savor the essence of fall with these delicious recipes.
