Budget-Friendly Mexican Soup Recipes: Hearty and Affordable


When it comes to comfort food, few things can beat a warm and hearty bowl of soup. With the rich and vibrant flavors of Mexican cuisine, you can create delicious and budget-friendly soups that will satisfy your cravings and fill you up. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering Mexican soup recipes that are not only easy on the wallet but also bursting with flavor. So, grab your pots and get ready to simmer up some culinary magic!

1. Chicken Tortilla Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (4 ounces) chopped green chilies
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortilla chips, avocado, and lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  2. Stir in the cumin and chili powder, and cook for another minute to toast the spices.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, green chilies, and chicken broth. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Stir in the shredded chicken and black beans. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Ladle the soup into bowls and serve with crushed tortilla chips, sliced avocado, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

2. Beef and Bean Chili

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) beef broth
  • 1 can (15 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Shredded cheese and sour cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook the ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until the beef is no longer pink.
  2. Stir in the chili powder and cumin, and cook for another minute.
  3. Add the tomato sauce and beef broth. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Stir in the kidney beans. Simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Ladle the chili into bowls and top with shredded cheese and a dollop of sour cream.

3. Vegetarian Black Bean Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 cans (15 ounces each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro and lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  2. Stir in the cumin and oregano, and cook for another minute.
  3. Add 2 cans of black beans, vegetable broth, and diced tomatoes. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Using a blender or immersion blender, puree the soup until smooth.
  5. Stir in the remaining can of black beans and frozen corn. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.

These budget-friendly Mexican soup recipes are not only easy on the wallet but also perfect for feeding a hungry crowd. Whether you’re craving the warmth of a classic chicken tortilla soup, the heartiness of beef and bean chili, or the wholesome goodness of vegetarian black bean soup, these recipes have got you covered. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the aromas of Mexico fill your kitchen. Happy cooking!
