Family-Friendly Holiday Recipes Everyone Will Enjoy


The holiday season is a time for coming together with loved ones, creating lasting memories, and enjoying delicious meals that bring everyone together. If you're looking for new and exciting recipes to add to your holiday table, you've come to the right place. In this post, we'll share some mouth-watering family-friendly holiday recipes that are sure to be a hit with both kids and adults. Recipe 1: Cranberry Brie Baked Brie Start your holiday celebration with this easy and impressive appetizer. This Cranberry Brie Baked Brie is perfect for a dinner party or a holiday gathering. Ingredients:

1 (8 oz) wheel of brie cheese 1/4 cup of fresh or frozen cranberries 2 tablespoons of honey 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh thyme 1 tablespoon of chopped pecans (optional) 1 tablespoon of balsamic glaze (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Remove the brie cheese from its packaging and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. In a small saucepan, combine the cranberries, honey, and thyme. Heat over low heat until the cranberries have popped and the mixture is syrupy. Place the brie cheese in the oven and bake for 5-7 minutes, or until it's soft and runny. Top the brie cheese with the cranberry mixture, chopped pecans, and balsamic glaze (if using). Serve warm with crackers or bread.

Recipe 2: Chocolate Peppermint Mousse Who says chocolate mousse is only for adults? This Chocolate Peppermint Mousse is a fun and easy dessert that kids will love. Ingredients:

8 ounces of high-quality dark chocolate, broken into small pieces 1 cup of heavy cream 1/2 cup of granulated sugar 1/4 cup of crushed candy canes (optional) 2 tablespoons of peppermint extract

Instructions:

In a medium-sized bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks. Set aside. In a separate bowl, melt the chocolate in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth. In a large bowl, combine the melted chocolate, granulated sugar, and peppermint extract. Stir until well combined. Fold in the whipped cream until no white streaks remain. Spoon the mousse into individual serving cups or a large serving dish. Top with crushed candy canes (if using) and serve chilled.

Recipe 3: Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Pomegranate and Pecans This delicious side dish is a game-changer for Brussels sprouts haters. The sweetness of the pomegranate and the crunch of the pecans make this dish a hit with adults and kids alike. Ingredients:

1 pound of Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved 2 tablespoons of olive oil 1/4 cup of pomegranate seeds 1/4 cup of chopped pecans 2 tablespoons of honey Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet. Roast the Brussels sprouts in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and caramelized. In a small bowl, mix together the pomegranate seeds and chopped pecans. Remove the Brussels sprouts from the oven and toss with the pomegranate-pecan mixture and honey. Serve hot and enjoy.

Recipe 4: Maple Bacon Chicken Casserole This comforting casserole is a twist on traditional chicken recipes. The sweetness of the maple syrup and the smokiness of the bacon make this dish a crowd-pleaser for kids and adults. Ingredients:

1 1/2 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled 1/4 cup of maple syrup 1/4 cup of grated cheddar cheese 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley 1 cup of pasta (such as penne or bowtie)

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside. In a large skillet, cook the chicken with the crumbled bacon until the chicken is cooked through. In a separate bowl, whisk together the maple syrup and grated cheddar cheese. In a large casserole dish, combine the cooked pasta, chicken-bacon mixture, and maple syrup-cheese mixture. Top with chopped parsley and serve hot.

Recipe 5: Apple Cider Donuts Who says donuts are only for special occasions? These Apple Cider Donuts are a sweet and indulgent treat that's perfect for the holiday season. Ingredients:

2 cups of all-purpose flour 1/2 cup of granulated sugar 1/2 cup of whole milk 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast 1/4 cup of apple cider 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted Confectioners' sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and yeast. In a small bowl, whisk together the milk, apple cider, and melted butter. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until a smooth dough forms. Let the dough rise for 1 hour, or until double in size. Preheat your oil to 350°F (180°C). using a cookie scoop or a piping bag, drop the dough into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Fry the donuts for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Drain the donuts on paper towels and dust with confectioners' sugar (if using). Serve warm and enjoy.

Tips and Variations

To make these recipes more kid-friendly, consider using fun shapes and colors, such as using cookie cutters to cut out shapes from the dough or using food coloring to dye the mousse pink or red. To add an extra layer of flavor to these recipes, consider using nuts, seeds, or dried fruit to add texture and interest. To make these recipes more convenient, consider making them ahead of time and freezing or refrigerating until serving.

Conclusion These family-friendly holiday recipes are sure to be a hit with both kids and adults. With a little creativity and experimentation, you can create a menu that's both delicious and pleasing to the eye. So why wait? Gather your family and friends around the table, and get ready to make some holiday magic. Happy cooking!
