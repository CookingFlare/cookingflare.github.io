How to Make and Can Homemade Plum Jam


Plum jam is a delicious, sweet, and tangy preserve that can transform your mornings, snacks, or desserts. Whether you’re spreading it on toast, dolloping it over ice cream, or pairing it with cheese, plum jam is versatile, simple to make, and a great way to capture the flavors of summer. Making and canning your own homemade plum jam not only allows you to control the ingredients but also lets you savor the delicious taste of fresh, seasonal plums all year long. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know about making and canning your own homemade plum jam from scratch.

What You’ll Need

Before you begin, ensure you have all the necessary ingredients and tools on hand. Here’s what you’ll need to make a batch of homemade plum jam:

Ingredients:

  • Plums: The star of the show! You’ll need around 4 pounds of ripe plums. Choose plums that are ripe but not overripe to ensure the jam has the best texture and flavor.
  • Sugar: About 4 cups of sugar will help preserve the jam and provide the sweet contrast to the plums’ tartness.
  • Lemon Juice: The acid in lemon juice helps to balance the sweetness and ensures the jam gels properly. You’ll need the juice of one lemon.
  • Pectin: This natural gelling agent is essential for helping the jam set. You can use either liquid or powdered pectin, but make sure it’s designed for jam-making.
  • Water: A small amount of water is used to help dissolve the sugar and create the right consistency for your jam.

Tools:

  • Large Pot or Jam Pot: You’ll need a heavy-bottomed pot to cook the jam. A large pot ensures even heat distribution and helps prevent scorching.
  • Jar Lifter: This tool is essential for safely removing hot jars from the boiling water bath.
  • Canning Jars: Ensure you have enough clean, sterilized jars to store your jam. Mason jars are a popular choice for canning.
  • Lid and Bands: Make sure your canning lids and bands are free of rust and damage. You’ll need these to seal your jars.
  • Canning Funnel: A funnel makes transferring the jam into jars much cleaner and easier.
  • Candy Thermometer: This will help you monitor the temperature of the jam, ensuring that it reaches the right consistency.
  • Tongs: Tongs are useful for placing jars into the boiling water bath.

Preparing the Plums

Start by preparing your plums. Wash them thoroughly under cold water. If your plums have pits, you’ll need to remove them. The easiest way to do this is to cut the plums in half, remove the pit, and then cut the flesh into smaller chunks. If you prefer a smoother jam, you can purée the plums after removing the pits. However, leaving them in chunks gives the jam a more rustic texture.

Once your plums are prepared, you can move on to the next step: cooking the jam.

Making the Jam

Step 1: Combine the Ingredients

In a large pot, combine the plums, sugar, and lemon juice. Stir to combine the ingredients thoroughly. The sugar will help draw out the juices from the plums, making it easier to cook down the mixture into a thick jam.

Step 2: Cook the Plum Mixture

Place the pot over medium heat and cook the plum mixture. Stir frequently to prevent the sugar from burning on the bottom of the pot. As the plums start to soften, you’ll notice the mixture becoming more liquid. Continue cooking, occasionally stirring, until the plums break down and the jam starts to thicken.

Step 3: Add Pectin

Once the plums have softened and released their juices, it’s time to add the pectin. This is the key ingredient to making sure your jam sets properly. Follow the instructions on the pectin package, as different brands may have different requirements. Usually, powdered pectin is added after the mixture has come to a boil. If you’re using liquid pectin, add it at the end of the cooking process, just before you remove the jam from the heat.

Step 4: Boil and Test for the Right Consistency

Now that you’ve added the pectin, increase the heat to bring the mixture to a boil. Let it cook for about 10–15 minutes. As the jam cooks, it will thicken. To test whether the jam is ready, use the cold plate test: Place a small plate in the freezer before you start cooking. Once the jam has boiled for the recommended time, drop a spoonful onto the cold plate and wait for a few seconds. Run your finger through the jam—if it wrinkles and holds its shape, your jam is ready. If not, continue boiling for another few minutes and test again.

Step 5: Skim the Foam

As the jam cooks, it may produce foam on the surface. Skim this off with a spoon and discard it. This helps give your jam a smooth, clear texture. While this step is optional, it’s recommended for a professional-looking final product.

Canning the Jam

Once your plum jam has reached the right consistency, it’s time to preserve it for long-term storage. Canning is a great way to store your homemade plum jam and enjoy it months down the line.

Step 1: Sterilize the Jars

Before you start canning, you need to sterilize your jars. This ensures that no harmful bacteria can spoil your jam. You can sterilize your jars by placing them in the dishwasher on a hot cycle or by placing them in a pot of boiling water for 10 minutes. Don’t forget to sterilize the lids and bands as well.

Step 2: Fill the Jars

Using a ladle or canning funnel, carefully fill each sterilized jar with hot jam. Leave about 1/4-inch of headspace at the top of the jar to allow for expansion. Be careful not to spill any jam on the rims, as this can interfere with the sealing process. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue.

Step 3: Seal the Jars

Place the sterilized lids onto the jars and screw the bands on tightly, but not too tight. You want to ensure a good seal, but don’t overtighten, as air needs to escape during the canning process.

Step 4: Process the Jars in a Boiling Water Bath

Once your jars are filled and sealed, it’s time to process them in a boiling water bath. Place the jars in a large pot filled with hot water, ensuring that the water covers the jars by at least an inch. Bring the water to a boil and process the jars for 10-15 minutes. This step ensures that the jars are sealed properly and the jam is preserved.

Step 5: Cool and Store

After processing, carefully remove the jars from the water bath using a jar lifter and set them on a clean towel or cooling rack. Let the jars cool completely before storing them. You’ll hear a satisfying “pop” sound as the jars seal, indicating that your plum jam is securely preserved.

Once cooled, check the seals by pressing down on the center of each lid. If it doesn’t pop back, the jar is sealed. If it does pop, store the jar in the fridge and use it within a few weeks. Properly sealed jars can be stored in a cool, dark pantry for up to a year.

Troubleshooting Common Problems

Making homemade plum jam is a straightforward process, but sometimes things don’t go as planned. Here are some common problems and how to fix them:

Jam Is Too Runny

If your jam didn’t set properly and is too runny, you can reprocess it. Simply return the jam to a pot, add a little more pectin (or use the liquid pectin method), and boil for a few more minutes. Then, re-can the jam.

Jam Is Too Thick

If the jam is too thick and difficult to spread, it may have been overcooked. In this case, you can loosen it up by stirring in a bit of water or fruit juice until you reach the desired consistency.

Cloudy or Grainy Texture

Sometimes, the sugar doesn’t dissolve properly or the fruit hasn’t broken down enough. If this happens, you can try straining the jam through a fine sieve to remove any leftover fruit particles, or process it a little longer on low heat.

Enjoying Your Homemade Plum Jam

Once your plum jam is made and canned, you’ll have a sweet, fruity treat to enjoy throughout the year. Use it as a topping for toast, pancakes, or yogurt, or pair it with cheese and crackers for an easy appetizer. It also makes a fantastic gift, so consider sharing your homemade jam with friends and family.

Storing and Using Plum Jam

Store your sealed jars in a cool, dark pantry, and once opened, refrigerate the jam. It will last for several weeks in the fridge after opening. If you have leftover jam, you can also freeze it in smaller portions to extend its shelf life.

Homemade plum jam is a simple and rewarding project that allows you to capture the essence of summer in a jar. Whether you’re new to canning or a seasoned pro, this recipe will help you create a batch of delicious plum jam that’s sure to become a family favorite.
