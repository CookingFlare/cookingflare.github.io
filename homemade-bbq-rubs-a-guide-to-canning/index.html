Homemade BBQ Rubs: A Guide to Canning


BBQ rubs are a staple in many cuisines, adding depth and complexity to grilled meats, vegetables, and even fruits. While store-bought rubs can be convenient, making your own from scratch allows you to customize the flavor profile to your liking. But what happens when you want to preserve your homemade BBQ rubs for future use? That’s where canning comes in.

Why Canning is a Game-Changer

Canning is a great way to preserve your homemade BBQ rubs for several reasons. For one, it allows you to make a large batch of rub at once, which can be a huge time-saver. Additionally, canning helps to prevent spoilage and contamination, ensuring your rub stays fresh for months to come. And let’s be real, who doesn’t love having a stash of homemade BBQ rubs on hand for impromptu grilling sessions?

The Basics of Canning

Before we dive into the specifics of canning homemade BBQ rubs, it’s essential to understand the basics. Canning is a process that involves heating food to a high temperature, creating a vacuum seal, and then storing it in a sterilized container. This process kills off any bacteria or other microorganisms that could cause spoilage or illness.

Choosing the Right Container

When it comes to canning homemade BBQ rubs, you’ll want to use glass jars with lids and bands. Mason jars are a popular choice, but any glass jar with a tight-fitting lid will work. Make sure to choose jars that are specifically designed for canning, as they are made to withstand the high temperatures and pressures involved in the process.

Preparing the Rub

Before canning your homemade BBQ rub, you’ll need to prepare it for preservation. This involves mixing together your dry ingredients, such as spices, herbs, and other seasonings, and then grinding them into a fine powder. You can use a spice grinder or mortar and pestle to achieve the desired consistency.

Canning the Rub

Once your rub is prepared, it’s time to can it. Here’s a step-by-step guide to follow:

  1. Sterilize your jars and lids by washing them in hot soapy water and then rinsing thoroughly.
  2. Fill the hot, sterilized jars with the prepared BBQ rub, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top.
  3. Use a clean utensil to remove any air bubbles and ensure the rub is evenly distributed.
  4. Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any excess rub.
  5. Place the sterilized lids and bands on the jars, making sure they are securely tightened.
  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10-15 minutes, adjusting for altitude as necessary.
  7. Remove the jars from the water bath and let them cool on a wire rack.

Storing and Using Your Canned BBQ Rubs

Once your jars have cooled, you can store them in a cool, dark place for up to 6 months. When you’re ready to use your canned BBQ rub, simply twist off the lid and scoop out the desired amount. You can use it to season meats, vegetables, and even fruits before grilling or roasting.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to keep in mind when making and canning your own BBQ rubs:

  • Experiment with different spice blends and seasonings to create unique flavor profiles.
  • Add a bit of oil or vinegar to the rub to help it adhere to meats and vegetables.
  • Use your canned BBQ rub as a base and add other ingredients, such as garlic or onion powder, to create a customized flavor.
  • Consider making a smaller batch of rub and canning it in smaller jars for easier storage and transport.

Conclusion

Canning homemade BBQ rubs is a great way to preserve your favorite flavors and have them on hand for future use. By following these simple steps and tips, you can create a delicious and customized BBQ rub that will elevate your grilled meats and vegetables to the next level. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the fruits of your labor!
