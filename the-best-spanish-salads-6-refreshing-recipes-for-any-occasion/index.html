The Best Spanish Salads: 6 Refreshing Recipes for Any Occasion


Spain is renowned for its vibrant culinary heritage, where bold flavors and fresh ingredients take center stage. While tapas, paella, and gazpacho often steal the spotlight, Spanish salads deserve their own moment of glory. Rooted in regional specialties and seasonal produce, these dishes are a celebration of simplicity, taste, and color. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering, need a light lunch, or want to bring something unique to the table, these six Spanish salad recipes are guaranteed to impress.

1. Ensalada Mixta: The Classic Spanish Mixed Salad

Ensalada Mixta is a staple at any Spanish home or restaurant. It’s a versatile salad that adapitates to regional customs, but the base remains delightfully simple. This vibrant dish often serves as a starter or a side on sunny afternoons.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of lettuce (Romaine or iceberg)
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 small red onion, finely sliced
  • 1 boiled egg, quartered
  • 1 can of tuna in olive oil, drained
  • Handful of olives (green or black)
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash the lettuce thoroughly and tear it into bite-sized pieces, arranging it as the base of the salad.
  2. Layer the tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion over the lettuce.
  3. Scatter the pieces of boiled egg and tuna across the vegetables.
  4. Add the olives as a finishing touch.
  5. Drizzle generously with olive oil and a splash of vinegar. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Mix gently just before serving to ensure all flavors meld together. Pair this salad with a crusty baguette to savor every drop of the dressing.

2. Salpicon de Mariscos: A Coastal Delight

Brimming with fresh seafood, Salpicon de Mariscos is perfect for seafood lovers. Found along Spain’s sun-drenched coasts, this dish balances the sweetness of shellfish with zippy citrus and crunchy vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 200g cooked shrimp, peeled
  • 200g cooked octopus or squid, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons parsley, finely chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the shrimp and octopus (or squid) in a large bowl.
  2. Add the diced peppers, onion, and tomatoes to the seafood.
  3. Sprinkle with parsley for added color and freshness.
  4. Dress the mixture with lemon juice and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, adjusting to taste.
  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to develop. Serve cold for the ultimate refreshing bite.

3. Ensalada de Naranja y Bacalao: Orange and Salt Cod Salad

This unique and beautifully balanced salad hails from Andalusia. The sweetness of oranges contrasts perfectly with the saltiness of cod, creating a harmonious blend of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 300g salt cod (desalted and flaked)
  • 2 medium oranges, peeled and sliced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup black olives
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • A pinch of smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt (if needed)

Instructions:

  1. Begin by desalting the cod. Soak it in cold water for 24-48 hours, changing the water every 6-8 hours. Once desalinated, flake the cod with your hands.
  2. On a large serving plate, arrange orange slices in a single layer.
  3. Scatter the cod, onion slices, and olives over the oranges.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and a light pinch of smoked paprika. Adjust salt carefully as needed.
  5. Serve this salad at room temperature for a truly Andalusian experience.

4. Pipirrana: A Refreshing Tomato-Based Salad

A specialty of southern Spain, particularly in Jaén, Pipirrana showcases the Mediterranean triad of tomatoes, peppers, and olive oil. Often served as a side or a light meal, it’s a prime example of the country’s focus on quality ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Sherry vinegar
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the tomatoes, bell pepper, cucumber, and onion in a large bowl.
  2. Using a mortar and pestle, crush the garlic with a pinch of salt to form a paste. Add this to the vegetables.
  3. Drizzle generously with olive oil and add a splash of sherry vinegar.
  4. Toss to combine, ensuring the vegetables are well-coated. Let it rest for 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Serve chilled or at room temperature with rustic bread for mopping up the dressing.

5. Ensaladilla Rusa: Spain’s Take on Russian Salad

Though its name points to Russian origins, Ensaladilla Rusa has become a staple in Spain, particularly as a tapa. Creamy, tangy, and satisfying, this salad is hard to resist.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 150g frozen peas
  • 2 boiled eggs, diced
  • 1 can tuna in olive oil, drained
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes, carrots, and peas until tender but firm. Allow them to cool completely.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the vegetables, eggs, and tuna.
  3. Gently fold in the mayonnaise until everything is coated. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least an hour to achieve the best flavor. Serve cold as an appetizer or side dish.

6. Escalivada: Grilled Vegetable Salad

Originating from Catalonia, Escalivada is a smoky, grilled vegetable salad that highlights eggplant, peppers, and onions. The dish is as visually appealing as it is flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 medium onions
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: Anchovies or fresh herbs (like parsley) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Roast the vegetables either in the oven at high heat or over a grill until their skins are charred and the flesh is tender.
  2. Allow the vegetables to cool slightly, then peel away the skins and remove any seeds.
  3. Tear the vegetables into strips and arrange them on a serving platter.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt. For an extra kick, garnish with anchovies or fresh herbs.
  5. Serve warm or at room temperature with crusty bread.

From the comforting simplicity of Ensalada Mixta to the smoky sophistication of Escalivada, these Spanish salads are a testament to the country’s love for fresh ingredients and bold flavors. Each dish brings its own slice of Spain to the table, offering a light yet satisfying taste of the Mediterranean sunshine. Whether you’re enjoying a lazy summer day, planning a dinner party, or simply craving something unique, these salads are sure to delight your palate.
