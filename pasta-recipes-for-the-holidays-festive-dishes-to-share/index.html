Pasta Recipes for the Holidays: Festive Dishes to Share


The holiday season is the perfect time to gather around the table with friends and family, celebrate tradition, and create new memories over delicious meals. One of the most beloved foods for these occasions is pasta. Whether it’s for a cozy dinner by the fireplace or a large holiday feast, pasta offers comfort, variety, and satisfaction. From creamy indulgence to savory, fresh flavors, there are countless ways to elevate your holiday meals with pasta.

In this post, we’ll explore a collection of pasta recipes that are perfect for the holiday season. These dishes will impress your guests, spark conversation, and leave everyone craving more. Whether you’re preparing a small intimate dinner or a festive buffet, there’s a pasta recipe to suit every occasion. Let’s dive into some delicious and creative ways to bring pasta to your holiday table!

1. Creamy Garlic Parmesan Pasta with Roasted Vegetables

If you’re looking for a comforting and creamy pasta dish to serve during the holidays, this Garlic Parmesan Pasta with Roasted Vegetables will surely be a hit. Roasted vegetables like Brussels sprouts, carrots, and butternut squash add a caramelized sweetness that perfectly complements the richness of the creamy garlic sauce. This dish is not only satisfying but also visually stunning, making it a perfect centerpiece for any holiday meal.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1 cup cubed butternut squash
  • 1 carrot, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the Brussels sprouts, butternut squash, and carrots with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the vegetables are tender and golden.
  2. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream, and bring to a simmer. Stir in the Parmesan cheese until the sauce becomes smooth and creamy. Season with salt, pepper, and fresh thyme.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, tossing to coat in the sauce. If needed, add the reserved pasta water to reach the desired sauce consistency.
  6. Gently fold in the roasted vegetables and stir to combine. Top with chopped fresh parsley and serve immediately.

This dish combines earthy vegetables and a rich, cheesy sauce, making it the perfect holiday side or main course.

2. Pasta Primavera with a Lemon Basil Pesto

For a lighter, yet vibrant holiday pasta dish, consider a Pasta Primavera with Lemon Basil Pesto. This dish showcases the best of fresh, seasonal vegetables and is drizzled with a bright, zesty pesto that captures the essence of spring even in the middle of winter. It’s a fresh and flavorful option that will brighten up your holiday spread.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh peas
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Lemon Basil Pesto:

  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (or walnuts)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain, reserving 1/4 cup of pasta water, and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the zucchini, bell pepper, and peas. Sauté until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant, about 5-7 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Prepare the pesto by blending basil, pine nuts, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, and pepper in a food processor until smooth and creamy.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the sautéed vegetables and lemon basil pesto. Add the reserved pasta water if necessary to help coat the pasta evenly.
  5. Gently fold in the cherry tomatoes and serve immediately.

This dish brings an irresistible balance of fresh, seasonal vegetables and a bright, citrusy pesto that’s perfect for any holiday gathering.

3. Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells with Marinara Sauce

If you want to serve a hearty, indulgent pasta dish that feels festive, look no further than Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells. These jumbo pasta shells are stuffed with a creamy spinach and ricotta filling, baked in marinara sauce, and topped with melted mozzarella cheese. It’s a comforting, crowd-pleasing dish that’s perfect for feeding a crowd during the holidays.

Ingredients:

  • 20 jumbo pasta shells
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, sautéed and chopped
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the pasta shells according to package instructions. Drain and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, sautéed spinach, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Stir until fully combined.
  3. Stuff each pasta shell with the spinach and ricotta mixture, then place the stuffed shells in a baking dish. Pour marinara sauce over the shells, ensuring each one is coated.
  4. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with extra Parmesan cheese and fresh basil.

This dish is a true showstopper with its layers of cheese, sauce, and perfectly stuffed pasta shells. It’s a comforting, satisfying option that will steal the show at any holiday meal.

4. Butternut Squash Ravioli in Sage Butter Sauce

Butternut squash ravioli is a quintessential holiday dish that’s both elegant and flavorful. The sweet, creamy filling of the ravioli pairs beautifully with a rich sage butter sauce, making it a luxurious addition to your holiday menu. This dish is perfect as a main course or as an appetizer to set the stage for a more substantial meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of butternut squash ravioli (or homemade, if desired)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 10 fresh sage leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • A pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the ravioli according to the package instructions. Drain, reserving some pasta water.
  2. In a large skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add the fresh sage leaves and cook for 2-3 minutes, until the butter begins to brown slightly and the sage becomes crisp.
  3. Add the cooked ravioli to the skillet and toss gently to coat in the sage butter sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a small amount of reserved pasta water to thin it out.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg, if using.
  5. Garnish with grated Parmesan cheese and serve immediately.

This dish is rich and flavorful, with the buttery sauce bringing out the sweetness of the squash. It’s an ideal holiday dish that combines comfort and sophistication in every bite.

5. Baked Ziti with Sausage and Ricotta

Baked ziti is a classic Italian-American dish that’s perfect for large holiday gatherings. Packed with hearty sausage, ricotta cheese, and a rich marinara sauce, this dish is baked to perfection and topped with bubbly mozzarella cheese. It’s a satisfying and comforting option that will please even the pickiest eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz ziti pasta
  • 1 lb Italian sausage (mild or spicy)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the ziti according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and crumbled. Remove from heat and stir in marinara sauce.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, half of the mozzarella cheese, and half of the Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. In a large baking dish, layer the cooked ziti, sausage and marinara mixture, and ricotta cheese mixture. Repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of sauce and cheese on top.
  5. Bake, covered with foil, for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes, until the cheese is melted and golden.
  6. Garnish with fresh basil and serve hot.

This baked ziti recipe combines savory sausage, rich cheese, and hearty pasta for a satisfying meal that’s perfect for large holiday gatherings.

Conclusion

The holidays are a time for indulgence, celebration, and, of course, food. Pasta is a versatile and beloved ingredient that can be transformed into countless festive dishes to delight your guests. Whether you’re looking for something creamy and comforting, light and fresh, or hearty and filling, there’s a holiday pasta recipe that will suit every taste and occasion.

These recipes offer a mix of flavors and ingredients that will enhance your holiday menu and bring people together around the table. From creamy garlic Parmesan pasta to butternut squash ravioli, each dish offers a unique and delicious way to celebrate the season.

So gather your loved ones, put on your apron, and get ready to share the joy of festive pasta dishes this holiday season!
