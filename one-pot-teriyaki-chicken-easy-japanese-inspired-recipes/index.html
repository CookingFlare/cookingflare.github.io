One-Pot Teriyaki Chicken: Easy Japanese-Inspired Recipes


Cooking a delicious and flavorful meal doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. This One-Pot Teriyaki Chicken recipe is the perfect example of a simple yet incredibly tasty dish that brings the flavors of Japan right to your kitchen. With a harmonious blend of sweet and savory teriyaki sauce coating tender chicken pieces, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Plus, the best part is that it can be prepared in just one pot, minimizing the cleanup afterwards!

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Optional garnish: sliced green onions, sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Chicken: In a large pot or deep pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken pieces and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a bowl, mix together soy sauce, water, brown sugar, garlic, ginger, and honey. Pour the sauce over the chicken in the pot.

  3. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Let the chicken simmer in the sauce for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.

  4. Thicken the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix cornstarch with water to create a slurry. Stir the slurry into the pot with the chicken and sauce. Continue to simmer for another 5 minutes until the sauce has reached your desired thickness.

  5. Serve: Once the sauce has thickened to your liking and the chicken is fully cooked, remove the pot from the heat. Serve the One-Pot Teriyaki Chicken hot over steamed rice, garnished with sliced green onions and sesame seeds if desired.

Tips and Variations:

  • Vegetable Addition: Feel free to add your favorite vegetables like bell peppers, broccoli, or snap peas to the dish for added color and nutrition.

  • Spicy Twist: For those who enjoy a bit of heat, sprinkle red pepper flakes or drizzle some sriracha over the chicken before serving.

  • Marinate Ahead: To enhance the flavor, marinate the chicken pieces in a portion of the teriyaki sauce for a few hours before cooking.

  • Rice Alternatives: If you prefer, serve the One-Pot Teriyaki Chicken over noodles or quinoa instead of rice for a different twist.

This One-Pot Teriyaki Chicken recipe is a fantastic way to enjoy the bold and delicious flavors of Japanese cuisine without spending hours in the kitchen. It’s a versatile dish that can be easily customized to suit your preferences and is perfect for both busy weeknights and special occasions. Give it a try and bring a taste of Japan to your dinner table tonight!

Remember, cooking should be a fun and enjoyable experience, so don’t be afraid to get creative and make this recipe your own. Happy cooking!
