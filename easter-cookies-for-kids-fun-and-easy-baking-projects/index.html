Easter Cookies for Kids: Fun and Easy Baking Projects


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and of course, delicious treats! If you’re looking for a fun and easy baking project to do with your kids this Easter, then look no further. In this blog post, we’ll be sharing some exciting Easter cookie recipes that are not only delicious but also a blast to make. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Bunny Sugar Cookies

These adorable bunny sugar cookies are a perfect way to get into the Easter spirit. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Bunny-shaped cookie cutter

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and baking powder.
  2. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing well after each addition.
  5. Divide the dough into two equal portions and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  6. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  7. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness.
  8. Use the bunny-shaped cookie cutter to cut out cookies and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  9. Bake for 8-10 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.
  10. Allow the cookies to cool completely before decorating with icing, sprinkles, or candy eyes.

2. Egg Nest Cookies

These egg nest cookies are not only adorable but also a delicious treat for Easter. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 ½ cups crispy rice cereal
  • 1 ½ cups milk chocolate chips
  • ½ cup peanut butter
  • Mini chocolate eggs

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the crispy rice cereal, milk chocolate chips, and peanut butter until well combined.
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Use a spoon or cookie scoop to drop small mounds of the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to create a small well in the center of each mound, resembling a nest.
  5. Place a few mini chocolate eggs in each nest.
  6. Refrigerate for about 30 minutes or until the chocolate has set.

3. Carrot Patch Cookies

These carrot patch cookies are not only adorable but also a great way to incorporate some healthy ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Orange food coloring
  • Chocolate sandwich cookies
  • Orange candy melts
  • Green candy melts

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing well after each addition.
  5. Add a few drops of orange food coloring to the dough and mix until the desired color is achieved.
  6. Roll the dough into small balls and place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  7. Flatten each ball slightly with the back of a spoon to resemble a carrot.
  8. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 10-12 minutes or until set.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool completely.
  10. Meanwhile, melt the orange and green candy melts separately according to the package instructions.
  11. Dip the chocolate sandwich cookies into the melted green candy melts to create the carrot tops.
  12. Place a dollop of melted orange candy melts on top of each cooled cookie and attach the chocolate sandwich cookie on top.
  13. Allow the candy melts to set before serving.

These Easter cookie recipes are not only delicious but also a fantastic way to bond with your kids and create lasting memories. Whether you choose to make bunny sugar cookies, egg nest cookies, or carrot patch cookies, the joy and excitement of baking together will make this Easter extra special. So, put on your aprons, gather your ingredients, and let the baking fun begin! Happy Easter!

Remember to always supervise children in the kitchen and have fun exploring your creativity. Enjoy these delightful Easter cookies with your loved ones, and may your Easter be filled with joy, love, and lots of sweet treats!
