Heavenly Butterscotch Pudding: A Smooth and Creamy Treat


Welcome to our culinary adventure where we explore the delightful world of desserts. Today, we’re diving into the velvety goodness of butterscotch pudding. If you’re a fan of rich, creamy desserts with a hint of caramel, then this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. Let’s not waste a single moment and get right into the indulgent world of Heavenly Butterscotch Pudding.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Preparation: In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and set them aside. This will ensure they are at room temperature when you add them to the pudding later.

  2. Combine Milk and Cream: In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, combine the whole milk and heavy cream. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it just begins to simmer. Remove the saucepan from the heat and set it aside.

  3. Prepare Butterscotch Mixture: In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the dark brown sugar and granulated sugar. Allow the sugars to melt and caramelize, stirring constantly to prevent burning. Once the mixture turns a rich amber color and the sugar has completely dissolved, carefully pour in the warm milk and cream mixture. Stir until the caramel is fully dissolved into the liquid.

  4. Thicken the Pudding: In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a few tablespoons of the butterscotch milk mixture to create a smooth slurry. Pour the slurry back into the saucepan with the butterscotch milk mixture. Return the saucepan to medium heat and cook, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon.

  5. Temper the Eggs: Once the butterscotch milk mixture has thickened, slowly pour a ladleful of the hot liquid into the bowl of egg yolks, whisking constantly. This will temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling when added to the hot mixture. Gradually add the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan, stirring vigorously.

  6. Final Touches: Cook the pudding for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until it reaches a pudding-like consistency. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract and salt.

  7. Chill and Serve: Pour the butterscotch pudding into individual serving dishes or a large bowl. Cover the surface of the pudding with plastic wrap to prevent a skin from forming. Refrigerate the pudding for at least 2 hours, or until it is completely chilled and set.

Serving Suggestions

You can enjoy this butterscotch pudding as is or take it to the next level with some creative toppings. A dollop of freshly whipped cream, a sprinkle of sea salt, or a drizzle of caramel sauce can elevate the flavors and add a touch of elegance to this classic dessert.

Conclusion

Heavenly Butterscotch Pudding is a timeless treat that offers a perfect balance of sweetness and warmth. Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion or simply indulging in a well-deserved dessert, this recipe promises to deliver a luscious and satisfying experience. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a journey to create a dessert that will undoubtedly leave a lasting impression on your taste buds.
