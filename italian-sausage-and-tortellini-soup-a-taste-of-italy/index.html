Italian Sausage and Tortellini Soup: A Taste of Italy


As the crisp autumn air sets in, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of soup to soothe the soul. And what better way to do so than with a classic Italian dish that combines the rich flavors of sausage and cheese with the tender bite of fresh pasta? In this recipe, we’ll explore the wonders of Italian Sausage and Tortellini Soup, a hearty and satisfying meal that’s sure to become a staple in your household.

The Magic of Italian Sausage

Italian sausage, or sausage made with pork and fennel, is a staple in many Italian kitchens. Its rich, meaty flavor is the perfect base for our soup, and its slightly sweet and anise notes will complement the other ingredients beautifully. For this recipe, we’ll use sweet Italian sausage, which has a milder flavor than hot Italian sausage. You can find Italian sausage at most grocery stores, usually in the meat or international section.

Tortellini: The Star of the Show

Tortellini, those delightful stuffed pasta rings, are the crowning glory of our soup. Made with a mixture of cheese, meat, and herbs, they add a delightful texture and flavor to the dish. For this recipe, we’ll use cheese-filled tortellini, which will melt beautifully in the soup and add a creamy richness. You can use fresh or frozen tortellini, whichever is readily available to you.

The Soup Itself

Now that we have our main ingredients, let’s move on to the soup itself. In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 onion, diced, and cook until it’s translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes. Add 2 cloves of garlic, minced, and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Next, add 1 pound of sweet Italian sausage, casings removed, and cook until it’s browned and cooked through, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. This will take about 5-7 minutes.

Adding the Liquid and Flavorings

Once the sausage is cooked, add 4 cups of chicken broth, 1 can of diced tomatoes, and 1 cup of water to the pot. Stir to combine, then bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the flavors have melded together and the soup has thickened slightly.

Assembling the Soup

Now it’s time to add our star of the show: the tortellini. Add 1 package of cheese-filled tortellini to the pot and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until they’re heated through and the cheese is melted.

Finishing Touches

To finish the soup, add 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese and 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh parsley. Stir to combine, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

Serving and Enjoying

Ladle the soup into bowls and serve hot, garnished with additional Parmesan cheese and parsley if desired. This soup is perfect for a chilly fall or winter evening, and it’s sure to become a family favorite.

Variations and Tips

  • For an added layer of flavor, add 1-2 cups of sliced mushrooms or diced bell peppers to the pot along with the onion and garlic.
  • If you prefer a creamier soup, add 1/2 cup of heavy cream or half-and-half towards the end of cooking time.
  • To make the soup more substantial, add 1 cup of diced cooked chicken or turkey to the pot along with the sausage.
  • You can also add a sprinkle of red pepper flakes to the soup for a spicy kick.

Conclusion

Italian Sausage and Tortellini Soup is a hearty and satisfying meal that’s sure to become a staple in your household. With its rich, meaty flavor and tender pasta, it’s the perfect comfort food for a chilly evening. Try it out and enjoy the taste of Italy in your own home!
