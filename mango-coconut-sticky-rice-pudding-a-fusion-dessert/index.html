Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding: A Fusion Dessert


Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding is a delightful fusion dessert that brings together the tropical flavors of mango and coconut with the comforting texture of sticky rice. This dessert is not only a treat for the taste buds but also a feast for the senses, combining fragrant jasmine rice, creamy coconut milk, and sweet, ripe mangoes. Let’s dive into this exotic recipe and explore how you can recreate this delicious dish at home.

Ingredients

To make Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Sticky Rice:

  • 1 cup of glutinous (sticky) rice
  • Water (for soaking and steaming)
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt

For the Coconut Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup of coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons of water (optional, for thickening)

For the Mango Topping:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced

Garnish:

  • Toasted sesame seeds or mung beans

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Sticky Rice

  1. Soak the Rice: Begin by rinsing the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too mushy. After rinsing, soak the rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight for the best results.

  2. Steam the Rice: Drain the soaked rice and place it in a steamer lined with cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel. Steam the rice over boiling water for 20-30 minutes until the grains are tender and translucent. You can check the doneness by tasting a few grainsâ€”if they are soft and chewy, the rice is ready.

Step 2: Make the Coconut Mixture

  1. Heat the Coconut Milk: In a small saucepan, combine 1 cup of coconut milk, 1/4 cup of sugar, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar is completely dissolved. Do not let the mixture boil.

  2. Mix with the Rice: Once the rice is steamed, transfer it to a large mixing bowl. Pour the warm coconut milk mixture over the rice and stir gently to combine. Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap and let it sit for about 20 minutes to allow the rice to absorb the coconut milk fully.

Step 3: Prepare the Coconut Sauce

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a small saucepan, combine 1/2 cup of coconut milk, 2 tablespoons of sugar, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar is dissolved.

  2. Thicken the Sauce: If you prefer a thicker sauce, mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to create a slurry. Slowly add the slurry to the coconut milk mixture, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency. Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 4: Assemble the Dessert

  1. Plate the Rice: Spoon the coconut sticky rice onto individual serving plates, shaping it into neat mounds or using a mold for a more uniform appearance.

  2. Add Mango Slices: Arrange the mango slices around or on top of the sticky rice. The bright yellow mango adds a pop of color and a burst of fresh flavor that complements the creamy rice.

  3. Drizzle with Coconut Sauce: Generously drizzle the coconut sauce over the sticky rice and mango slices. The sauce adds an extra layer of richness and ties all the flavors together.

  4. Garnish: Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds or mung beans over the top for added texture and a touch of nuttiness.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Serve the Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding immediately while the rice is still slightly warm, or refrigerate it for a chilled version. This dessert is perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a family gathering, a dinner party, or simply a sweet treat for yourself.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing the Right Rice: It’s essential to use glutinous (sticky) rice for this recipe. Regular rice won’t give you the same chewy texture that’s characteristic of traditional sticky rice pudding.

  • Mango Selection: Choose ripe, sweet mangoes for the best flavor. Ataulfo (honey) mangoes are often recommended due to their creamy texture and sweetness.

  • Coconut Milk Quality: Use high-quality, full-fat coconut milk for a rich and creamy result. Avoid using light coconut milk as it may not provide the same depth of flavor.

  • Adjusting Sweetness: The sweetness of the pudding can be adjusted to your taste. Feel free to add more or less sugar depending on your preference and the sweetness of the mangoes.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the sticky rice and coconut sauce ahead of time and store them separately in the refrigerator. When ready to serve, gently reheat the rice and sauce before assembling the dessert.

Variations and Additions

Pandan Flavor

For an aromatic twist, add a few pandan leaves to the coconut milk mixture while heating. Pandan leaves impart a unique fragrance that pairs wonderfully with coconut and mango.

Black Sticky Rice

Black sticky rice can be used instead of white glutinous rice for a striking visual contrast and a slightly nuttier flavor. The preparation method remains the same, though black sticky rice might require a bit longer cooking time.

Tropical Fruit Medley

While mango is the classic choice, you can experiment with other tropical fruits like pineapple, kiwi, or papaya for a different flavor profile. A medley of fruits can make the dessert even more vibrant and exciting.

Nutty Crunch

Add a crunchy element by sprinkling toasted coconut flakes, chopped peanuts, or cashews over the top. These additions not only enhance the texture but also add an extra layer of flavor.

Vegan Option

This recipe is naturally vegan if you use plant-based coconut milk. Ensure that the sugar you use is also vegan-friendly (some white sugars are processed with bone char).

Nutritional Information

Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding is a rich and indulgent dessert, but it’s also packed with nutrients:

  • Mangoes: Rich in vitamins A and C, mangoes are also a good source of dietary fiber and antioxidants.
  • Coconut Milk: Provides healthy fats, including medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs), which can offer various health benefits.
  • Sticky Rice: While glutinous rice is starchy, it provides a good amount of energy and is filling, making it a satisfying dessert option.

Conclusion

Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding is a harmonious blend of sweet, creamy, and fruity elements that come together to create a truly unforgettable dessert. Its origins might be rooted in traditional Thai cuisine, but its appeal is universal. Whether you’re serving it at a special event or enjoying it as a personal treat, this fusion dessert is sure to delight and impress.

By following this detailed guide, you can easily make this dessert at home and bring a taste of the tropics to your table. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and indulge in the delectable flavors of Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Pudding. Your taste buds will thank you!
