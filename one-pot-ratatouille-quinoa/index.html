One Pot Ratatouille Quinoa


Ratatouille is a classic French dish that combines the flavors of various vegetables in a rich and hearty stew. Traditionally, it is cooked slowly on the stovetop, but today we’re going to give it a modern twist by turning it into a one-pot wonder with the addition of quinoa. This One Pot Ratatouille Quinoa recipe is not only delicious and satisfying, but it’s also incredibly easy to make. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 3 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  2. Add the diced eggplant, zucchinis, red bell pepper, and yellow bell pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine and cook for about 5 minutes, until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Add the diced tomatoes, dried thyme, dried oregano, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir everything together and let it cook for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness. Add the rinsed quinoa to the pot, followed by the vegetable broth. Stir well to combine.

  5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid.

  6. Once the quinoa is cooked, remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for a few minutes to allow the flavors to develop.

  7. Serve the One Pot Ratatouille Quinoa hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves. Enjoy this wholesome and flavorful dish on its own or as a side to your favorite protein.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  • Easy One-Pot Preparation: With just one pot, you can create a delicious and nutritious meal without the hassle of multiple dishes to clean up afterward.

  • Versatile and Customizable: Feel free to substitute or add your favorite vegetables to make this recipe your own. It’s a great way to use up any veggies you have in your fridge.

  • Healthy and Nutritious: This recipe is packed with vitamins, minerals, and fiber from the various vegetables and quinoa. It’s a wholesome and balanced meal that will leave you feeling satisfied.

  • Flavorful and Comforting: The combination of herbs and vegetables in this dish creates a rich and hearty flavor profile that is both comforting and satisfying.

Final Thoughts

One Pot Ratatouille Quinoa is a versatile and delicious dish that can be enjoyed all year round. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or feeding a crowd, this recipe is sure to impress with its vibrant colors, robust flavors, and healthy ingredients. Give it a try and let us know how you liked it! Happy cooking!
