The Best Vegan Cupcake Recipes You Need to Try


Cupcakes are a classic treat, loved by people of all ages. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply indulging in something sweet, cupcakes are versatile and delightful. However, for those following a plant-based lifestyle or avoiding dairy and eggs, vegan cupcakes offer a wonderful alternative. These cupcakes are just as fluffy, moist, and delicious as their non-vegan counterparts—if not better!

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of vegan cupcake recipes, from simple classics to creative flavor combinations. These recipes prove that going vegan doesn’t mean compromising on taste or texture. So, let’s dive into the world of plant-based baking and discover the best vegan cupcake recipes you need to try!

What Makes Vegan Cupcakes Different?

Before we dive into the recipes, it’s helpful to understand what makes vegan cupcakes stand out. Traditional cupcakes rely on dairy products and eggs for moisture, binding, and structure. In vegan cupcakes, we replace these ingredients with plant-based alternatives. Here’s a quick rundown of the key substitutions:

  • Eggs: Eggs help provide structure and moisture. In vegan baking, common substitutes include flaxseed meal, chia seeds, applesauce, silken tofu, or even mashed bananas.
  • Dairy Milk: Instead of cow’s milk, use any plant-based milk such as almond milk, soy milk, oat milk, or coconut milk.
  • Butter: Vegan butter or oils like coconut oil or vegetable oil are used to replace dairy butter.

These swaps ensure that vegan cupcakes are just as satisfying and tasty as traditional ones. Let’s take a look at some mouthwatering recipes to prove it!

Classic Vegan Vanilla Cupcakes

Nothing beats a classic vanilla cupcake, and this vegan version will quickly become your go-to recipe. Light, airy, and perfectly sweet, these cupcakes can be topped with your favorite vegan frosting for an extra indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar (use cane sugar or any plant-based alternative)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk (or your choice of plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or melted coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper cupcake liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the almond milk, oil, vanilla extract, and apple cider vinegar. Stir well.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Spoon the batter evenly into the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the cupcakes cool completely before frosting with your favorite vegan frosting.

These cupcakes are fluffy, slightly sweet, and perfect for any occasion. You can also add sprinkles, fresh berries, or even a drizzle of vegan chocolate ganache for an extra touch!

Vegan Chocolate Cupcakes

Chocolate lovers, rejoice! These vegan chocolate cupcakes are rich, moist, and packed with deep chocolate flavor. They’re the ultimate treat for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup cocoa powder (preferably unsweetened)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any other plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup boiling water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper cupcake liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking soda, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the almond milk, oil, vanilla extract, and apple cider vinegar.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Slowly add the boiling water to the batter, mixing until smooth. The batter will be thin, but that’s okay—this is what makes the cupcakes moist.
  6. Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely before frosting with vegan chocolate frosting.

These chocolate cupcakes are rich and decadent, with a wonderfully moist texture. For an extra treat, you can fill them with vegan chocolate ganache or top them with crushed nuts or chocolate shavings.

Vegan Lemon Cupcakes with Blueberry Frosting

Looking for a refreshing and fruity twist on the traditional cupcake? These vegan lemon cupcakes paired with tangy blueberry frosting are a perfect spring or summer treat. The light lemon flavor combined with the sweetness of the blueberry frosting creates a perfect balance.

Ingredients for Lemon Cupcakes:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Ingredients for Blueberry Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil or vegan butter
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper cupcake liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine the almond milk, oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and apple cider vinegar.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Spoon the batter evenly into the cupcake liners and bake for 18-20 minutes.
  6. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the blueberry frosting. Heat the blueberries in a small saucepan over medium heat until they release their juice. Mash them with a fork and let the mixture cool.
  7. In a bowl, beat together the coconut oil or vegan butter with powdered sugar. Once smooth, add the mashed blueberries and vanilla extract, mixing until combined.
  8. Frost the cooled cupcakes with the blueberry frosting.

These cupcakes offer a delightful mix of tart and sweet, making them a perfect option for those who love fruit-based desserts.

Vegan Carrot Cupcakes with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cupcakes are a classic favorite that you can easily make vegan. The combination of spiced carrot and creamy vegan frosting makes this dessert perfect for any occasion, from birthdays to holiday gatherings.

Ingredients for Carrot Cupcakes:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup grated carrots (about 2 medium-sized carrots)
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts or raisins (optional)

Ingredients for Vegan Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 1/4 cup vegan cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper cupcake liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and sugar.
  3. In another bowl, combine the oil, applesauce, and vanilla extract.
  4. Stir in the grated carrots (and walnuts or raisins, if using).
  5. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners and bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  7. While the cupcakes are cooling, prepare the cream cheese frosting. Beat together the vegan cream cheese and vegan butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, mixing until fluffy.
  8. Frost the cooled cupcakes with the cream cheese frosting.

These carrot cupcakes are spiced and tender, with a creamy frosting that brings everything together. They’re perfect for anyone who loves a moist, flavorful cupcake.

Vegan Strawberry Cupcakes

When fresh strawberries are in season, why not incorporate them into your cupcakes? These vegan strawberry cupcakes are bursting with fruity flavor and topped with a delicious vegan strawberry frosting.

Ingredients for Strawberry Cupcakes:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pureed fresh strawberries
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Ingredients for Strawberry Frosting:

  • 1/2 cup pureed fresh strawberries
  • 1/2 cup vegan butter
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper cupcake liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, combine the almond milk, oil, vanilla extract, and pureed strawberries.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Spoon the batter into the cupcake liners and bake for 18-20 minutes.
  6. To make the frosting, beat together the pureed strawberries, vegan butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth and fluffy.
  7. Frost the cooled cupcakes with the strawberry frosting.

These cupcakes are the ultimate treat for strawberry lovers, offering a burst of fresh fruit flavor in every bite.

Conclusion

Vegan cupcakes are a fantastic way to enjoy a sweet treat without compromising your dietary choices. With so many flavor options, you can create the perfect cupcake for any occasion. Whether you prefer classic vanilla, rich chocolate, or fruity lemon and strawberry, there’s a vegan cupcake recipe for everyone. Plus, the substitutions for eggs and dairy ensure these cupcakes are just as delicious and moist as traditional ones.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for something sweet, try one of these vegan cupcake recipes. Your taste buds (and the planet) will thank you! Happy baking!
