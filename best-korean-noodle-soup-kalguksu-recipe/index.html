Best Korean Noodle Soup (Kalguksu) Recipe


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors, comforting textures, and the balance of heat, sweetness, and savory elements. Among the many dishes that define Korean comfort food, one of the most beloved and wholesome is Kalguksu – a rich and satisfying noodle soup that’s perfect for any season, but especially ideal during colder months. This dish, which literally translates to “knife-cut noodles,” features delicate handmade noodles in a savory, clear broth that is a perfect combination of simplicity and complexity. Whether you’re craving a light meal or a soul-warming dish, Kalguksu offers the ultimate comfort in a bowl.

What is Kalguksu?

Kalguksu is a traditional Korean noodle soup that consists of broad, chewy noodles served in a savory, lightly seasoned broth. The noodles are made from wheat flour and cut by hand (hence the name “knife noodles”), which gives them a unique texture. The broth is typically made from chicken, beef, or anchovies, and the soup is often accompanied by a variety of vegetables and sometimes meat for added depth of flavor.

This humble soup is a staple in Korean households and is often enjoyed as a hearty, satisfying meal during family gatherings, special occasions, or simply when you’re in need of some comfort food. The beauty of Kalguksu lies in its versatility – while the basic ingredients remain the same, regional variations and personal preferences allow for a wide range of delicious interpretations of the dish.

In this post, we will walk you through the process of making the perfect Kalguksu, covering everything from how to prepare the broth and noodles to tips for achieving that signature chewy texture. Whether you’re an experienced home cook or a beginner looking to try something new, this recipe will guide you step-by-step in creating a delicious and authentic Korean noodle soup.

Ingredients

For the Noodles:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the Broth:

  • 1 lb (450g) chicken thighs or beef short ribs (for a richer flavor, chicken is preferred)
  • 10 cups water
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2-3 dried anchovies (optional, for umami)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or fish sauce, for a more authentic flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper (optional)

Vegetables and Garnish:

  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and thinly sliced (optional but recommended for added texture)
  • 1/2 cup napa cabbage, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • A handful of fresh cilantro (optional)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for spice)

Preparation

Step 1: Making the Noodles

Making homemade noodles for Kalguksu is one of the most exciting and rewarding aspects of the dish. While store-bought noodles can be used in a pinch, nothing beats the chewy texture and satisfying bite of fresh, handmade noodles.

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Gradually add water to the flour mixture, stirring with a wooden spoon or chopsticks. Keep adding water until the dough begins to come together. The dough should be firm but not too dry or too wet.

  2. Knead the Dough: Turn the dough onto a clean, lightly floured surface. Knead for about 5-10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a bit more flour; if it’s too dry, add a few drops of water. Once kneaded, wrap the dough in a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  3. Roll and Cut the Noodles: After resting, roll out the dough into a thin sheet, about 1/8-inch thick. Use a sharp knife to cut the dough into broad, thick strips, about 1/4-inch wide. These are your noodles. Try to keep them as uniform in size as possible for even cooking.

Step 2: Preparing the Broth

The broth is the heart of Kalguksu, and it is essential to build deep, savory flavors. A well-made broth can elevate the dish from good to great, so take your time with this step.

  1. Cook the Meat: If using chicken, place the chicken thighs or drumsticks in a large pot and cover them with 10 cups of water. Bring the pot to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes. Skim off any impurities or foam that rise to the surface during this time.

  2. Add the Aromatics: After simmering the chicken, add the onion, garlic, ginger, and dried anchovies (if using). Continue simmering for another 30-40 minutes. The broth should be flavorful and slightly rich in color at this point. If you prefer a clearer broth, strain the broth at this stage to remove the solids.

  3. Season the Broth: Once the broth has simmered, season it with soy sauce (or fish sauce), sesame oil, salt, and pepper to taste. The soup should have a delicate but savory flavor that complements the richness of the noodles.

  4. Strain the Broth (Optional): For an extra-clear broth, you can strain out all the solids using a fine-mesh sieve. This step is optional but results in a cleaner, more refined final dish.

Step 3: Cooking the Noodles and Assembling the Soup

Now that the broth is ready and the noodles are prepared, it’s time to assemble your Kalguksu. This is the most straightforward part of the recipe, but it requires careful timing to get everything just right.

  1. Cook the Noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil and add the fresh noodles. Cook the noodles for 2-3 minutes or until they float to the top. Fresh noodles cook very quickly, so be careful not to overcook them.

  2. Add the Vegetables: While the noodles are cooking, add the thinly sliced vegetables – zucchini, potato, and napa cabbage – into the pot with the broth. These vegetables will cook quickly, so after adding them, let the broth simmer for about 5-7 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Combine Noodles and Broth: Once the noodles are cooked, transfer them to the pot with the simmering broth and vegetables. Stir gently to combine everything. Let the soup simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 4: Serving the Kalguksu

Now that your Kalguksu is ready, it’s time to serve! Ladle the noodles and broth into individual bowls. Garnish with green onions, a sprinkle of sesame seeds, and fresh cilantro. You can also add a pinch of red pepper flakes for a bit of spice if you like.

Enjoy your warm and satisfying bowl of Kalguksu with a side of kimchi or a light salad for a complete meal. This dish is perfect for sharing with family and friends or enjoying on your own when you need something soothing and filling.

Tips for the Best Kalguksu

  • Homemade Noodles: If you’re short on time, you can always use store-bought Korean noodles or udon noodles as a substitute for the homemade noodles. However, homemade noodles add a special touch to the dish and are highly recommended if you’re looking for authenticity.
  • Broth Variations: While chicken broth is the most traditional, you can also make a beef-based broth for a richer flavor or use anchovy-based broth for a more umami-packed version.
  • Varying the Vegetables: Kalguksu is very customizable when it comes to vegetables. Feel free to add mushrooms, carrots, or any other vegetables you enjoy. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time for each vegetable to ensure they remain tender but not overcooked.
  • Spiciness: If you like your soup with a bit of heat, you can add a spoonful of gochujang (Korean chili paste) or sprinkle some red pepper flakes into your bowl. However, the traditional version of Kalguksu is usually served mild.
  • Storing Leftovers: If you have any leftover Kalguksu, store the broth, noodles, and vegetables separately in airtight containers. When reheating, add a bit of water or broth to the noodles as they will soak up the liquid.

Conclusion

Kalguksu is one of those dishes that can evoke nostalgia and bring comfort with every bite. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner, a special occasion, or simply to enjoy a comforting bowl of soup, this Korean noodle soup is guaranteed to please. With its hand-cut noodles, savory broth, and tender vegetables, Kalguksu is a dish that is as heartwarming as it is delicious.

We hope this recipe has inspired you to try making this classic Korean noodle soup at home. With a little patience and love, you can recreate this beloved dish and enjoy its rich, satisfying flavors with friends and family.
