Mouthwatering Nut-Free Apple Pie Recipe


As the seasons change and the air grows crisp, there’s nothing quite like the warm, comforting aroma of freshly baked apple pie wafting from the oven. But for those with nut allergies or dietary restrictions, traditional recipes can be a challenge. Fear not, dear bakers! Today, we’re sharing a mouthwatering nut-free apple pie recipe that’s sure to become a new family favorite.

The Secret to a Flaky Crust

Before we dive into the filling, let’s talk about the foundation of a great apple pie: the crust. A nut-free crust can be a bit more finicky, but with the right combination of ingredients and techniques, you’ll be rewarded with a flaky, buttery masterpiece. Here’s the secret: use a combination of all-purpose flour, cold unsalted butter, and ice-cold water.

In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter. Use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add 1/4 cup of ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a shaggy mass.

The Filling: A Symphony of Apples

Now it’s time to bring together the stars of the show: the apples. For this recipe, we’re using a mix of sweet and tart varieties to create a beautifully balanced flavor profile. Peel, core, and slice 6-8 apples (a combination of Granny Smith, Fuji, and Gala works beautifully) and set them aside.

In a separate bowl, combine 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, 2 tablespoons of cornstarch, and 1 teaspoon of cinnamon. Add the sliced apples to the bowl and toss until they’re evenly coated with the dry ingredients.

Assembly and Baking

Roll out the chilled pie dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Place the dough into a 9-inch pie dish, trimming any excess from the edges. Fill the pie crust with the apple mixture, mounding it slightly in the center.

Roll out the remaining pie dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Use a pastry cutter or a knife to create a decorative edge, then place the rolled-out dough on top of the apple filling. Crimp the edges to seal the pie and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.

Bake the pie in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apples are tender. Let the pie cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra-flaky crust, be sure to keep the butter and water cold. If the dough starts to warm up, pop it in the fridge for 10-15 minutes to chill.
  • Want to add a little extra flavor to your pie? Try using a combination of apple cider and water when mixing the filling.
  • If you’re looking for a gluten-free crust option, substitute the all-purpose flour with a 1:1 ratio of gluten-free flour blend.

There you have it, folks: a mouthwatering nut-free apple pie recipe that’s sure to become a new favorite. Whether you’re serving it at a family gathering or enjoying it with a warm cup of coffee on a crisp autumn morning, this pie is sure to bring joy and satisfaction to all who taste it. Happy baking!
