Worms in Dirt Pudding Cups: A Fun and Frightening Treat


Welcome to our spooky and delicious recipe for Worms in Dirt Pudding Cups! This creepy-crawly dessert is perfect for Halloween parties, kids’ birthdays, or any time you want to add a dash of fun and fright to your table. With its rich chocolate flavor, creamy texture, and gummy worm surprise, it’s sure to delight both kids and kids at heart. So, let’s dive into this delightfully eerie recipe and get ready to make some deliciously spooky treats!

Ingredients

For the pudding:

  • 2 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 4 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

For the “dirt” and assembly:

  • 15-20 chocolate sandwich cookies
  • 8-10 gummy worms

Instructions

Making the Pudding

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the milk, sugar, cocoa powder, cornstarch, and salt. Place the pan over medium heat and cook, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.

  2. Once the mixture has reached a boil, reduce the heat to low and cook for an additional 2 minutes, continuing to whisk.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Gradually whisk in about 1 cup of the hot milk mixture to temper the eggs. Then, pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the rest of the milk mixture.

  4. Cook the pudding over low heat, whisking constantly, until it thickens, about 2 minutes.

  5. Remove the pan from the heat and add the chopped chocolate, butter, and vanilla extract. Stir until the chocolate and butter are melted and the pudding is smooth.

  6. Strain the pudding through a fine-mesh sieve into a bowl, cover the surface of the pudding with plastic wrap, and refrigerate until chilled, about 2 hours.

Assembling the Worms in Dirt Pudding Cups

  1. Place the chocolate sandwich cookies in a large resealable plastic bag and crush them with a rolling pin or the back of a spoon until they resemble coarse crumbs.

  2. Spoon a layer of the chocolate cookie crumbs into the bottom of each serving cup to create the “dirt” layer.

  3. Once the pudding is chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and give it a good stir to loosen it up.

  4. Spoon the pudding on top of the cookie crumbs in each cup, creating a smooth layer.

  5. Place the gummy worms on top of the pudding, letting them peek out of the “dirt” for a fun and spooky effect.

  6. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra spooky touch, you can place the cups in the freezer for 10-15 minutes before serving to create a chilly, graveyard-like effect.
  • Experiment with different gummy worm colors and sizes for a more diverse and eye-catching presentation.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use instant chocolate pudding mix as a quick alternative to homemade pudding.

Conclusion

There you have it – a simple and delightful recipe for Worms in Dirt Pudding Cups! Whether you’re hosting a Halloween bash or just want to surprise your family with a playful and delicious dessert, these pudding cups are sure to be a hit. The rich, chocolatey pudding combined with the fun and quirky gummy worms make for a treat that’s as delightful to eat as it is to look at. So, go ahead and whip up a batch of these eerie delights for your next gathering, and watch as they disappear in a spooktacular fashion! Happy cooking, and enjoy your frightfully tasty treats!
