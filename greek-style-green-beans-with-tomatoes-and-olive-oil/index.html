Greek Style Green Beans with Tomatoes and Olive Oil


Greek cuisine is renowned for its vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and healthy cooking methods. One dish that perfectly embodies these qualities is Greek Style Green Beans with Tomatoes and Olive Oil. This simple yet flavorful recipe highlights the natural sweetness of fresh green beans, the acidity of ripe tomatoes, and the richness of high-quality olive oil. It’s a delightful side dish that can complement a variety of meals or even stand alone as a light vegetarian option.

The Essence of Greek Cooking

At its core, Greek cooking focuses on the quality of ingredients. Olive oil, fresh vegetables, herbs, and spices play vital roles in creating dishes that are both satisfying and nourishing. The Mediterranean diet, which emphasizes these elements, is celebrated not only for its delicious flavors but also for its health benefits. Greek Style Green Beans with Tomatoes and Olive Oil is a perfect example of this culinary philosophy, showcasing fresh ingredients and straightforward preparation.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh green beans: Look for vibrant, firm green beans. Trim the ends and wash them thoroughly.
  • 2 cups ripe tomatoes: You can use fresh tomatoes or canned tomatoes, depending on the season. If using fresh, consider varieties like Roma or vine-ripened for their sweetness.
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil: The quality of olive oil is crucial. Opt for a good quality extra virgin olive oil for the best flavor.
  • 1 medium onion: Thinly sliced. Sweet onions, such as Vidalia, work beautifully in this dish.
  • 3 cloves garlic: Minced. Garlic adds depth and aroma to the dish.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste. Seasoning is essential to enhance the flavors of the ingredients.
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill or parsley: Chopped, for garnish. Fresh herbs add a burst of flavor and color.

Preparation Steps

1. Prepare the Green Beans

Begin by preparing the green beans. Rinse them under cold water and trim the ends. If you prefer, you can cut them into smaller pieces for easier eating. Blanch the beans in boiling salted water for about 2-3 minutes until they are bright green and slightly tender but still crisp. Drain them and immediately transfer to an ice bath to stop the cooking process. This technique helps to preserve their vibrant color and crunch.

2. Cook the Aromatics

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook until they become translucent, about 5-7 minutes. The goal is to soften the onions without browning them. Next, add the minced garlic and sauté for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can become bitter.

3. Incorporate the Tomatoes

If using fresh tomatoes, peel and chop them into small pieces. If you are using canned tomatoes, simply crush them with your hands or a fork. Add the tomatoes to the skillet with the onions and garlic. Stir to combine and let the mixture simmer for about 10-15 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the tomatoes to break down into a sauce.

4. Combine Everything

Once the tomato mixture has thickened slightly, add the blanched green beans to the skillet. Stir gently to coat the beans in the tomato sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Allow the mixture to cook together for an additional 5-7 minutes, so the green beans absorb some of the flavors from the sauce while remaining tender-crisp.

5. Garnish and Serve

Once the green beans are cooked to your liking, remove the skillet from the heat. Sprinkle the dish with freshly chopped dill or parsley for a burst of color and flavor. Serve warm or at room temperature, drizzled with a little extra olive oil if desired.

Tips for Perfect Greek Style Green Beans

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Whenever possible, opt for fresh green beans and ripe tomatoes. The quality of your ingredients will significantly affect the final dish.
  • Experiment with Herbs: While dill and parsley are traditional, feel free to experiment with other herbs like basil or mint to give the dish a personal touch.
  • Add Protein: For a heartier meal, consider adding cooked chickpeas or grilled chicken to the dish.
  • Make it Ahead: This recipe can be made in advance and stored in the refrigerator. The flavors deepen as it sits, making it an excellent make-ahead option.

Nutritional Benefits

This Greek Style Green Beans with Tomatoes and Olive Oil dish is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Green beans are rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as folate and fiber. Tomatoes are an excellent source of antioxidants, particularly lycopene, which is known for its heart health benefits. The use of extra virgin olive oil adds healthy monounsaturated fats to the dish, contributing to a balanced diet.

Serving Suggestions

Greek Style Green Beans with Tomatoes and Olive Oil makes a wonderful addition to any meal. Here are some serving suggestions:

  • As a Side Dish: Serve alongside grilled meats, roasted chicken, or fish for a complete meal.
  • In a Salad: Toss the green beans with some feta cheese and olives for a refreshing salad.
  • On a Mezze Platter: Include this dish as part of a larger spread of Greek mezze, alongside hummus, tzatziki, and pita bread.

Final Thoughts

Greek Style Green Beans with Tomatoes and Olive Oil is a timeless recipe that captures the essence of Greek cuisine. Its simplicity and wholesome ingredients make it a go-to dish for any occasion. Whether you’re preparing a family dinner or entertaining guests, this vibrant side dish is sure to impress. Try it today and bring a taste of the Mediterranean to your table!
