Picnic-Style Holidays: Outdoor-Inspired Recipes for Al Fresco Celebrations


As the warm breeze of summer beckons and the days stretch longer, there’s no better way to celebrate than with a picnic-style holiday feast. Whether you’re planning a Fourth of July gathering, a Labor Day barbecue, or simply a weekend escape into nature, these outdoor-inspired recipes are perfect for al fresco dining under the open sky. From refreshing salads to hearty mains and delectable desserts, here’s how you can create a memorable picnic spread that combines flavor, freshness, and the joy of outdoor festivities.

Appetizers: Mediterranean Mezze Platter

Kick off your picnic-style holiday with a vibrant Mediterranean mezze platter that’s as colorful as it is delicious. This assortment of small bites offers a delightful array of flavors and textures, perfect for grazing under the shade of a sprawling tree or by the beachside.

Ingredients:

  • Hummus: Creamy and smooth, made with chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic.
  • Baba Ganoush: Smoky eggplant dip with tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil.
  • Tzatziki: Refreshing yogurt dip with cucumber, garlic, dill, and lemon juice.
  • Tabbouleh: Fresh parsley and bulgur wheat salad with tomatoes, cucumbers, mint, and lemon dressing.
  • Marinated Olives: Mixed variety marinated in olive oil, herbs, and citrus zest.
  • Pita Bread or Crackers: Sliced for easy dipping and scooping.

Instructions:

  1. Arrange the hummus, baba ganoush, tzatziki, tabbouleh, and marinated olives on a large platter.
  2. Garnish with fresh herbs like parsley and mint for a burst of color.
  3. Serve with warmed pita bread or assorted crackers for dipping and scooping.

Salad: Grilled Peach and Burrata Salad

Celebrate the flavors of summer with a refreshing grilled peach and burrata salad that combines sweetness with creaminess and a hint of smokiness from the grill. This salad is a delightful blend of textures and flavors that will brighten up any picnic table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 8 oz burrata cheese
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 1/4 cup balsamic glaze
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the peach halves with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the peaches cut-side down for 3-4 minutes until grill marks appear.
  4. Flip and grill for another 2-3 minutes until tender.
  5. Arrange arugula on a serving platter.
  6. Place grilled peaches and torn burrata over the arugula.
  7. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and olive oil.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Main Course: BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwiches

No picnic-style holiday feast is complete without a hearty main course that satisfies appetites and brings everyone together. These BBQ pulled pork sandwiches are a crowd-pleasing favorite, packed with smoky flavors and tender meat that’s perfect for serving outdoors.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs pork shoulder or butt
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 8 sandwich buns

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Rub the spice mixture all over the pork shoulder.
  3. In a slow cooker or Instant Pot, combine apple cider vinegar and 1/2 cup BBQ sauce.
  4. Add the seasoned pork shoulder and cook on low for 8 hours in a slow cooker or on high pressure for 90 minutes in an Instant Pot.
  5. Once cooked, shred the pork using two forks.
  6. Mix in the remaining BBQ sauce until well combined.
  7. Serve the pulled pork on sandwich buns with coleslaw on the side for a refreshing crunch.

Side Dish: Quinoa and Vegetable Salad

For a nutritious and filling side dish that’s perfect for outdoor dining, try this quinoa and vegetable salad. Packed with protein, fiber, and a medley of fresh veggies, this salad is light yet satisfying, ideal for pairing with grilled meats or enjoying on its own.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Rinse quinoa under cold water.
  2. In a medium saucepan, bring water or vegetable broth to a boil.
  3. Add quinoa, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until liquid is absorbed and quinoa is tender.
  4. Fluff quinoa with a fork and let cool to room temperature.
  5. In a large bowl, combine quinoa, cucumber, bell pepper, cherry tomatoes, red onion, parsley, and mint.
  6. Drizzle with lemon juice and olive oil.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Toss gently to combine.

Dessert: Lemon Blueberry Bars

End your picnic-style holiday feast on a sweet note with these lemon blueberry bars that are bursting with fresh flavors. Tangy lemon combined with sweet blueberries atop a buttery crust makes these bars a delightful treat for outdoor celebrations.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
    • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/4 tsp salt
  • For the filling:
    • 4 large eggs
    • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • Zest and juice of 3 lemons
    • 1 cup fresh blueberries
    • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, cream together softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Gradually add flour and salt, mixing until crumbly dough forms.
  4. Press dough evenly into the bottom of a greased 9x13-inch baking pan.
  5. Bake crust for 15-20 minutes until lightly golden brown.
  6. In another bowl, whisk together eggs, granulated sugar, flour, lemon zest, and lemon juice until smooth.
  7. Gently fold in blueberries.
  8. Pour filling over the baked crust.
  9. Return to the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes until filling is set and edges are golden brown.
  10. Let cool completely in the pan on a wire rack.
  11. Dust with powdered sugar before cutting into bars.

Conclusion

Embrace the beauty of outdoor dining and celebrate your picnic-style holiday with these flavorful recipes designed to be enjoyed under the sun or stars. From Mediterranean mezze to BBQ pulled pork sandwiches, quinoa and vegetable salad, and lemon blueberry bars, these dishes offer something for everyone to savor amidst the natural splendor of your chosen outdoor setting. Create lasting memories with loved ones as you indulge in delicious food and the joy of al fresco celebrations. Cheers to picnics, holidays, and the simple pleasures of good food enjoyed outdoors!
