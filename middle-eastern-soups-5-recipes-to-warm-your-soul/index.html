Middle Eastern Soups: 5 Recipes to Warm Your Soul


When the weather turns chilly or you simply crave comfort food, nothing warms the heart and soul quite like a bowl of soup. Middle Eastern cuisine, known for its rich flavors and aromatic spices, offers a variety of soups that are not only delicious but also nourishing. In this post, we will explore five traditional Middle Eastern soup recipes that are sure to warm you up and tantalize your taste buds.

1. Harira: The Moroccan Classic

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lentils
  • 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 6 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Base: In a large pot, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until the onion becomes translucent.

  2. Add Spices: Stir in the cumin, cinnamon, turmeric, and paprika. Cook for another minute until the spices are fragrant.

  3. Incorporate Lentils and Chickpeas: Add the lentils and soaked chickpeas to the pot, followed by the diced tomatoes and broth. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Simmer: Reduce the heat and let the soup simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils and chickpeas are tender.

  5. Finish with Herbs: Stir in the chopped parsley and cilantro. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and serve with lemon wedges on the side for a zesty finish.

Harira is traditionally served during Ramadan, but its hearty nature makes it perfect for any occasion. The combination of lentils and chickpeas provides a satisfying protein boost, while the spices create a warm, inviting aroma.

2. Shorbat Adas: Lentil Soup from the Levant

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon juice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, cooking until softened.

  2. Add Garlic and Spices: Stir in the garlic, cumin, coriander, and turmeric, cooking for an additional minute.

  3. Combine Lentils and Broth: Add the rinsed lentils and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.

  4. Blend (Optional): For a creamier texture, use an immersion blender to puree the soup to your desired consistency.

  5. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper, and serve with a splash of lemon juice for brightness.

Shorbat Adas is a staple in many Middle Eastern households, known for its simplicity and nutritional value. The red lentils provide a beautiful color and a rich, earthy flavor that pairs perfectly with the aromatic spices.

3. Chicken and Rice Soup (Shorbat Dajaj)

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup rice (preferably basmati)
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 8 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Chicken: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and brown on all sides. Remove the chicken and set aside.

  2. Sauté Vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté until softened, then add the garlic, cinnamon, and allspice.

  3. Simmer: Return the chicken to the pot, add the chicken broth, and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes.
