Flavorful Stuffed Bell Peppers: A Festive Vegetarian Main Course


Bell peppers are a staple in many cuisines around the world, and for good reason. Not only are they a great source of vitamins and antioxidants, but they’re also incredibly versatile and can be used in a wide range of dishes. In this recipe, we’ll be stuffing bell peppers with a flavorful mixture of grains, vegetables, and cheese, creating a delicious and satisfying vegetarian main course that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, any color
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup black beans, cooked
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup chopped red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh scallions, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cut off the tops of the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place them in a baking dish.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, quinoa, black beans, corn kernels, red onion, cilantro, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Stuff each bell pepper with the rice mixture, filling them as full as possible.
  5. Drizzle the olive oil over the peppers and sprinkle with lime juice.
  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  7. Remove the foil and top each pepper with shredded cheddar cheese and crumbled feta cheese.
  8. Return the peppers to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Remove the peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.
  10. Garnish with chopped scallions and serve warm.

Tips and Variations:

  • To add some heat to your stuffed peppers, you can add diced jalapeños or serrano peppers to the rice mixture.
  • If you prefer a creamier stuffing, you can add some grated Parmesan cheese or sour cream to the mixture.
  • You can also customize the recipe by adding your favorite vegetables, such as diced bell peppers, mushrooms, or zucchini.
  • Stuffed bell peppers make a great make-ahead dish. Simply prepare the filling and stuff the peppers, then refrigerate or freeze until ready to bake.

Nutrition Information:

Per serving (1 stuffed bell pepper):

  • Calories: 420
  • Protein: 20g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Saturated fat: 8g
  • Carbohydrates: 40g
  • Fiber: 10g
  • Sugar: 10g
  • Sodium: 400mg
  • Cholesterol: 30mg

This recipe is not only delicious, but it’s also packed with nutrients and can be adapted to suit your dietary needs. The quinoa and black beans provide a good source of protein and fiber, while the cheese adds a creamy and indulgent touch. The smoked paprika adds a smoky flavor that pairs perfectly with the sweetness of the bell peppers. This recipe is perfect for a special occasion or a weeknight dinner, and can be easily scaled up or down to feed a crowd. Give it a try and enjoy the flavors of this festive vegetarian main course!
