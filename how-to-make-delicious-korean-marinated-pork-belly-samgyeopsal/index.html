How to Make Delicious Korean Marinated Pork Belly (Samgyeopsal)


Korean cuisine offers a wide array of delectable dishes, each with its own distinct flavors and techniques. Among the many popular dishes that have captured the hearts and appetites of food lovers worldwide, Korean marinated pork belly stands out as a flavorful, tender, and satisfying meal. Known as Samgyeopsal in Korean, this dish features thick, juicy slices of pork belly that are grilled to perfection, often served with an array of fresh vegetables and savory dipping sauces. While the grilled version is already a favorite, marinating the pork before grilling it takes the flavor to a whole new level. In this blog post, we will explore how to make delicious Korean marinated pork belly, step by step, so you can recreate this mouth-watering dish at home.

What is Samgyeopsal?

Samgyeopsal (삼겹살) translates to “three-layered meat” in Korean, referring to the three distinct layers found in pork belly: skin, fat, and meat. This cut of pork is revered in Korean cuisine for its tender texture and rich flavor, making it a go-to choice for grilling. While the unseasoned version of Samgyeopsal is often grilled over charcoal and served with side dishes, marinating the pork belly before grilling can infuse the meat with extra layers of flavor, making it an even more enjoyable experience.

Ingredients for Korean Marinated Pork Belly (Samgyeopsal)

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s gather the essential ingredients needed for this delicious recipe.

For the Pork Belly Marinade:

  • 1 lb (450g) pork belly (preferably with skin)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic (about 3-4 cloves)
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (or mirin)
  • 1 tablespoon onion, finely minced
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon green onions, finely chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice (optional, for a zesty finish)

For Grilling:

  • Lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
  • Garlic cloves (thinly sliced, optional)
  • Ssamjang (fermented bean paste sauce)
  • Kimchi (optional, but highly recommended as a side)
  • Pickled vegetables or any preferred side dishes

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Make Korean Marinated Pork Belly (Samgyeopsal)

Step 1: Preparing the Pork Belly

Start by selecting fresh pork belly with a nice balance of fat and meat. While you can use boneless or bone-in pork belly, boneless is typically more manageable for grilling. Slice the pork belly into 1/2-inch thick pieces, ensuring each slice has a bit of skin and fat. This will allow the pork belly to remain juicy and flavorful when grilled. The thicker slices ensure that the pork stays moist during the grilling process, rather than drying out.

Step 2: Preparing the Marinade

To create the marinade, combine the following ingredients in a medium-sized bowl:

  • Soy Sauce: Adds saltiness and umami flavor to the pork belly.
  • Sesame Oil: Gives the dish a nutty aroma and richness.
  • Honey or Brown Sugar: Balances the savory flavors with a touch of sweetness.
  • Rice Vinegar: Offers a mild acidity to help balance the fat of the pork.
  • Minced Garlic: Brings a depth of flavor with its aromatic, savory punch.
  • Grated Ginger: Adds a fragrant, slightly spicy kick to the marinade.
  • Gochujang: A key ingredient in Korean cuisine, this chili paste brings a smoky, spicy depth to the pork.
  • Sesame Seeds: These add texture and a mild nutty flavor.
  • Rice Wine: Adds a bit of sweetness and helps tenderize the pork.
  • Onion: Minced onion adds a subtle sweetness and depth of flavor.
  • Black Pepper: For seasoning and enhancing the overall flavors.
  • Lemon Juice: Optional, but it brightens the marinade and adds a zesty finish.

Whisk all these ingredients together until the honey or brown sugar has fully dissolved. The mixture should have a balanced flavor profile—slightly sweet, savory, with a hint of spiciness from the gochujang and a kick from the garlic and ginger.

Step 3: Marinating the Pork Belly

Once your marinade is prepared, place the sliced pork belly in a resealable plastic bag or a shallow container. Pour the marinade over the pork belly, ensuring that all pieces are well-coated. Seal the bag or cover the container, and refrigerate the pork belly for at least 2 hours, but preferably overnight. The longer the pork belly marinates, the more tender and flavorful it will be. Be sure to toss the meat occasionally while marinating to allow the flavors to penetrate evenly.

Step 4: Preparing the Grill

While the pork belly is marinating, prepare your grill. If you have access to a charcoal grill, this is the ideal choice for cooking Samgyeopsal, as the smoky flavor complements the richness of the pork belly. However, if you don’t have a charcoal grill, a gas grill or a stovetop grill pan works just as well.

  • For a charcoal grill, allow the coals to burn down to a medium heat. You want to ensure a nice even heat without flames, which will allow for slow, even cooking.
  • For a gas grill, preheat to medium-high heat.
  • If using a stovetop grill pan, preheat the pan over medium-high heat and brush it lightly with oil to prevent sticking.

Step 5: Grilling the Pork Belly

Now that the pork belly is marinated and the grill is ready, it’s time to cook! Take the marinated pork belly slices out of the fridge and allow them to come to room temperature for about 10-15 minutes. This will help them cook evenly.

Place the pork belly slices directly on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes per side or until they achieve a crispy, caramelized exterior while remaining tender and juicy inside. Keep a close eye on the pork to avoid overcooking, as pork belly can dry out if left on the grill for too long. If you like a more charred flavor, leave the pork belly on the grill for an extra minute or two, but be careful not to burn the marinade.

Step 6: Serving the Korean Marinated Pork Belly

Once the pork belly is grilled to your preferred level of doneness, it’s time to serve it! Traditional Samgyeopsal is served with lettuce leaves for wrapping the grilled pork, along with ssamjang, a flavorful dipping sauce made from fermented bean paste, sesame oil, and garlic. You can also serve it with thinly sliced garlic, grilled peppers, and kimchi for added flavor and texture.

To eat, simply take a piece of the grilled pork belly and place it in a lettuce leaf. Add a dollop of ssamjang, some garlic slices, and a piece of kimchi if desired. Wrap it up like a small parcel and take a bite—this combination of flavors and textures will leave your taste buds dancing.

Tips for the Perfect Korean Marinated Pork Belly

  1. Use Fresh Pork Belly: The best pork belly has a good balance of fat and meat. Opt for fresh, high-quality pork for the best results.
  2. Marinate Longer for Better Flavor: If possible, marinate the pork belly overnight. The longer the pork sits in the marinade, the more flavor it will absorb.
  3. Grill Over Medium Heat: Pork belly should be grilled over medium heat to ensure it cooks evenly without burning. Too high a heat might char the outside before the inside is cooked through.
  4. Serve with Traditional Sides: In addition to the lettuce wraps and ssamjang, you can serve Korean marinated pork belly with pickled radish, kimchi, and a variety of banchan (Korean side dishes).

Why You’ll Love Korean Marinated Pork Belly

Korean marinated pork belly offers a perfect combination of savory, spicy, and sweet flavors, making it an irresistible dish for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a BBQ or simply craving a delicious Korean meal, this recipe is sure to impress. The marinated pork belly becomes tender, juicy, and full of umami, while the grilling process adds a delightful smoky aroma. The interactive nature of eating Samgyeopsal, where everyone gathers around the grill and assembles their own wraps, also makes it a fun and communal experience.

Final Thoughts

Making Korean marinated pork belly at home may seem like an elaborate process, but with the right ingredients and a little patience, you can easily recreate this flavorful dish. Whether you’re looking to enjoy a Korean BBQ night or simply want to experience the deliciousness of Samgyeopsal, this recipe will take your grilling game to the next level. So, fire up your grill, marinate your pork belly, and get ready to indulge in one of the best dishes Korea has to offer.
