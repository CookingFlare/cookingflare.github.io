Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso and Whipped Cream: A Valentine's Day Dessert Recipe


Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso and Whipped Cream: A Valentine’s Day Dessert Recipe

Valentine’s Day is a time to celebrate love and indulge in sweet treats with your significant other. And what better way to do so than with a decadent and romantic dessert like Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake? This delightful cake combines the classic flavors of tiramisu with a twist, making it a perfect choice for a Valentine’s Day celebration.

Ingredients

For the cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 cup boiling water

For the filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the espresso syrup:

  • 1/2 cup strong brewed coffee
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)

For the ladyfingers:

  • 24 ladyfingers

For the whipped cream:

  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.

  3. Add the milk, vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Beat on medium speed for 2 minutes.

  4. Stir in the boiling water. The batter will be thin, but that’s okay. It will result in a moist and tender cake.

  5. Pour the batter evenly into the prepared pans and bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.

  6. While the cakes are cooling, prepare the filling. In a mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. Set aside.

  7. Make the espresso syrup by combining the strong brewed coffee and sugar in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is dissolved. Remove from heat and stir in the coffee liqueur, if using.

  8. Once the cakes have cooled, carefully remove them from the pans. Using a serrated knife, slice each cake horizontally into two equal layers.

  9. Take one layer and place it on a serving platter. Brush the top with the espresso syrup, making sure to moisten it evenly.

  10. Spread a layer of the whipped cream filling over the moistened cake layer.

  11. Place a layer of ladyfingers on top of the whipped cream filling, arranging them in a single layer.

  12. Repeat steps 9 to 11 with the remaining cake layers, espresso syrup, whipped cream filling, and ladyfingers.

  13. For the final layer, brush the top of the ladyfingers with the remaining espresso syrup.

  14. In a separate mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until stiff peaks form. This will be the whipped cream topping for the cake.

  15. Spread the whipped cream topping over the top and sides of the cake, creating a smooth and even layer.

  16. Optional: You can decorate the cake with chocolate shavings, cocoa powder, or fresh berries to add an extra touch of elegance.

  17. Refrigerate the cake for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld together and the cake to set.

  18. When ready to serve, slice the Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake into generous pieces and enjoy with your loved one.

Conclusion

The Till Death Do Us Part Tiramisu Cake with Ladyfingers and Espresso and Whipped Cream is a show-stopping dessert that will impress your significant other on Valentine’s Day. The combination of moist chocolate cake, espresso-infused ladyfingers, and fluffy whipped cream filling creates a tantalizing symphony of flavors that is sure to delight. So go ahead, indulge in this romantic dessert and create a sweet memory that will last a lifetime. Happy Valentine’s Day!
