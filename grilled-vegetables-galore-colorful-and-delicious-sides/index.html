Grilled Vegetables Galore: Colorful and Delicious Sides


Grilled vegetables are a fantastic addition to any summer meal. Not only do they add a burst of color to your plate, but they also bring a deliciously smoky and charred flavor that is hard to resist. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply looking to elevate your weeknight dinner, these grilled vegetable recipes are sure to impress. So fire up that grill and get ready to enjoy some vibrant and flavorful sides!

Grilled Asparagus with Lemon and Parmesan

Asparagus is a springtime favorite, and grilling it takes its natural sweetness to a whole new level. Start by trimming the tough ends of the asparagus spears, then toss them in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place them on a preheated grill over medium heat and cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and lightly charred.

Once the asparagus is grilled to perfection, squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese. The tangy lemon and salty Parmesan perfectly complement the smoky flavor of the grilled asparagus. Serve it as a side dish or enjoy it as a light and healthy appetizer.

Grilled Bell Peppers with Balsamic Glaze

If you’re looking for a burst of vibrant colors on your plate, grilled bell peppers are the way to go. Start by slicing the bell peppers into thick strips and removing the seeds and membranes. Drizzle them with olive oil, then season with salt and pepper. Place the peppers on a preheated grill over medium-high heat and cook for about 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are charred and tender.

Once the peppers are grilled to perfection, transfer them to a serving platter and drizzle with a balsamic glaze. The sweet and tangy glaze adds a delightful depth of flavor to the smoky peppers. Sprinkle some fresh basil leaves on top for a pop of freshness. These grilled bell peppers make a great side dish or a colorful addition to salads and sandwiches.

Grilled Zucchini and Yellow Squash Skewers

Zucchini and yellow squash are abundant during the summer months, and grilling them on skewers is a fun and delicious way to enjoy them. Start by slicing the zucchini and yellow squash into thick rounds. Thread them onto skewers, alternating between the two vegetables. Drizzle the skewers with olive oil, then season with salt, pepper, and dried Italian seasoning.

Place the skewers on a preheated grill over medium heat and cook for about 6-8 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred. The skewers can be served as is or brushed with a garlic and herb butter for an extra burst of flavor. These grilled zucchini and yellow squash skewers make a fantastic side dish for any grilled meat or fish.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili Lime Butter

Corn on the cob is a summer staple, and grilling it brings out its natural sweetness and adds a delightful smoky flavor. Start by husking the corn and removing the silk. Brush each ear of corn with melted butter, then season with salt, pepper, and chili powder.

Place the corn on a preheated grill over medium-high heat and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and lightly charred. Once the corn is grilled to perfection, brush it with a mixture of melted butter, lime zest, and lime juice. The tangy and slightly spicy chili lime butter takes the grilled corn to a whole new level of deliciousness.

Grilled Eggplant with Tahini Sauce

Eggplant is a versatile vegetable that becomes incredibly tender and flavorful when grilled. Start by slicing the eggplant into thick rounds and brushing them with olive oil. Season with salt, pepper, and dried oregano.

Place the eggplant slices on a preheated grill over medium heat and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and have beautiful grill marks. Once the eggplant is grilled to perfection, serve it with a drizzle of tahini sauce and a sprinkle of fresh parsley. The creamy and nutty tahini sauce complements the smoky flavor of the grilled eggplant perfectly.

Conclusion

Grilled vegetables are a fantastic way to elevate your summer meals. From asparagus and bell peppers to zucchini and corn on the cob, these colorful and delicious sides are sure to impress your family and friends. So dust off that grill, gather your favorite vegetables, and get ready to enjoy the smoky and charred flavors of grilled vegetables galore!
