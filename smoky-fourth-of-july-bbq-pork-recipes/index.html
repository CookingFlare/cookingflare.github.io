Smoky Fourth of July BBQ Pork Recipes


The Fourth of July is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than with a smoky, flavorful BBQ pork feast? Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or just enjoying a meal with your family, these mouthwatering pork recipes will surely make your Fourth of July celebration one to remember. So, grab your apron and let’s fire up the grill!

1. Smoky BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwiches

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 6-8 hours | Total Time: 6 hours 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless pork shoulder (about 4-5 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce
  • Hamburger buns

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper to make a dry rub.
  2. Rub the dry rub all over the pork shoulder, making sure to coat it evenly.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Place the pork shoulder on the grill and cook for about 2-3 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 160°F (71°C).
  5. Wrap the pork shoulder tightly in aluminum foil and return it to the grill. Cook for an additional 4-5 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 195°F (90°C).
  6. Remove the pork shoulder from the grill and let it rest for about 15 minutes.
  7. Using two forks, shred the pork shoulder into bite-sized pieces.
  8. In a large bowl, mix the shredded pork with your favorite BBQ sauce.
  9. Serve the BBQ pulled pork on hamburger buns and enjoy!

2. Smoky BBQ Pork Ribs

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 3-4 hours | Total Time: 3 hours 10 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 racks of pork ribs
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • BBQ sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, salt, and black pepper to make a dry rub.
  3. Rub the dry rub all over the pork ribs, making sure to coat them evenly.
  4. Place the ribs on the grill and cook for about 3-4 hours, or until the meat is tender and pulls away from the bone easily.
  5. During the last 30 minutes of cooking, baste the ribs with your favorite BBQ sauce.
  6. Remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.
  7. Cut the ribs into individual portions and serve with extra BBQ sauce on the side.

3. Smoky BBQ Pork Skewers

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 10-12 minutes | Total Time: 32 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pork tenderloin, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the olive oil, soy sauce, honey, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Add the pork cubes to the bowl and toss to coat them in the marinade. Let them marinate for at least 15 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the marinated pork cubes onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 10-12 minutes, turning them occasionally, until the pork is cooked through and slightly charred.
  6. Remove the skewers from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.
  7. Serve the smoky BBQ pork skewers as an appetizer or alongside your favorite side dishes.

Conclusion

These smoky BBQ pork recipes are sure to impress your family and friends on the Fourth of July. From pulled pork sandwiches to tender ribs and flavorful skewers, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, fire up the grill, savor the smoky aroma, and indulge in these delicious pork dishes. Happy Fourth of July!
