Best Korean Beef Stew (Galbitang) Recipe


Korean cuisine is renowned for its hearty, flavorful dishes that offer a perfect balance of comfort and sophistication, and Galbitang is no exception. This traditional Korean beef stew is a soul-warming delicacy made with tender beef short ribs, rich broth, and a medley of classic Korean spices. Whether you’re looking to explore the depths of Korean culinary heritage or simply craving a more substantial soup for dinner, this Galbitang recipe is perfect to savor.

What Makes Galbitang Special?

Galbitang is the epitome of Korean comfort food. Unlike spicy stews that dominate Korean cuisine, this dish features a clear, savory broth that showcases the natural flavors of beef and aromatics. The slow cooking process ensures the beef ribs become tender while drawing out their rich, meaty essence. With its mild yet flavorful profile, Galbitang appeals to all palates, making it a great introduction to Korean soup cuisine.

It’s versatile enough to serve as a cozy family meal or an impressive centerpiece for special gatherings. Traditionally enjoyed with steamed rice and a side of kimchi, this dish embodies the essence of Korean home cooking.

Now, let’s dive into how you can create this hearty masterpiece in your own kitchen.

Ingredients for Galbitang

To create authentic Galbitang, youâ€™ll need simple yet essential ingredients that can be sourced from your local Asian market or specialty products section.

Main Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef short ribs: The star of the dish. Choose high-quality ribs with a good ratio of meat and fat.
  • 10 cups of water: Used to create the broth that’s infused with the flavors of beef and aromatics.
  • 1 large onion: Cut into quarters, this adds depth and sweetness to the soup.
  • 1 large Korean radish (mu): Peeled and sliced into large chunks for its mild, earthy flavor.
  • 6-8 garlic cloves: Smashed for bold aromatics.

Seasonings and Enhancements

  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce: A touch of umami elevates the broth’s complexity.
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil: For nutty warmth in the soup.
  • Salt and black pepper: Adjust for taste.
  • Green onion slices (garnish): A fresh, bright topping.

Optional Additions

  • Glass noodles (dangmyeon) for texture: These chewy noodles soak up the stew’s flavor.
  • Egg garnish: Thinly fried and sliced strips add elegance to the presentation.

Preparing Galbitang

Step 1: Prepping the Beef Short Ribs

Good preparation is key to preventing a greasy stew. Begin by trimming excess fat from the beef short ribs. Then, soak the ribs in cold water for 1-2 hours to draw out impurities. This step is essential for achieving a clean and clear broth.

After soaking, rinse the ribs under cold running water to remove residual blood and debris.

Step 2: Blanching the Ribs

Place the cleaned short ribs in a pot of boiling water and cook for 5 minutes. This preliminary blanching helps further eliminate any remaining impurities, ensuring a robust yet refined broth.

Drain the ribs and rinse them once more. Set aside.

Step 3: Creating the Base Broth

Fill a large pot with 10 cups of water. Add the blanched short ribs, quartered onion, Korean radish chunks, and smashed garlic cloves. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Simmer for about 1-2 hours, skimming off foam and impurities from the surface as needed. This ensures a clear broth while allowing flavors to intensify.

Step 4: Seasoning the Broth

Once the beef short ribs are tender and the vegetables have softened, strain the broth to remove the aromatics. Reintroduce the ribs to the pot, discarding the onion and garlic. For the radish, you can return pieces to the stew depending on your preference for texture.

At this stage, add soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Allow the stew to simmer for an additional 20 minutes to meld the flavors fully.

Step 5: Optional Enhancements

If you prefer, soak glass noodles in warm water until softened, then add them to the soup during the last 10 minutes of cooking. Additionally, prepare a fried egg garnish by whisking an egg, frying it into a thin sheet, and slicing strips to place atop the finished stew.

Serving Galbitang

Once the stew is ready, ladle it into serving bowls and garnish with sliced green onions for a burst of freshness. Pair it with steamed white rice and a small variety of kimchi to complete the meal.

Galbitang is best enjoyed hot, and the comforting flavors are sure to win the hearts of everyone at the table. The tenderness of the beef paired with the delicate, fragrant broth creates a balance that is uniquely satisfying.

Storing and Reheating Leftovers

This stew is a great make-ahead dish, as the flavors deepen over time. Allow any leftovers to cool completely before transferring them to an airtight container. Store in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or freeze for longer storage.

To reheat, place the stew in a pot over medium heat until thoroughly warmed. Adding a bit of fresh green onion right before serving enhances the reheated dish’s appearance and taste.

Tips for Perfect Galbitang

  1. Skim diligently: A clear broth is key to authentic Galbitang. Skim off foam and fat consistently during cooking.
  2. Use quality short ribs: The beef is the centerpiece, so investing in good-quality ribs will make the dish more memorable.
  3. Donâ€™t rush the process: This dish relies on slow cooking to extract maximum flavor and achieve fall-off-the-bone tenderness.
