Lemon Blueberry Scones: A Buttery and Tangy Treat


Scones, with their crumbly yet tender texture, are a delightful addition to any breakfast or afternoon tea. Today, we’re diving into the world of Lemon Blueberry Scones—a perfect blend of buttery richness and tangy freshness that will elevate your baking repertoire.

Ingredients

To start, gather these key ingredients for your Lemon Blueberry Scones:

  • Dry Ingredients:

    • 3 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • Wet Ingredients:

    • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
    • 1 cup fresh blueberries
    • Zest of 1 lemon
    • 1/2 cup buttermilk (plus more for brushing)
    • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • For Glaze (optional):

    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 2-3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
    • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions

1. Preparing the Dough

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Mix the dry ingredients in a large bowl: flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  3. Cut in the butter using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. The butter should be pea-sized.

  4. Add the lemon zest and blueberries to the flour mixture, gently tossing to coat the blueberries.

  5. Combine the wet ingredients separately: buttermilk, lemon juice, and vanilla extract.

  6. Slowly add the wet ingredients to the flour mixture, stirring gently until the dough just comes together. Be careful not to overmix; the dough should be slightly sticky.

2. Shaping and Baking

  1. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and pat it into a circle about 1 inch thick.

  2. Cut the dough into 8 wedges and place them on the prepared baking sheet, leaving space between each scone.

  3. Brush the tops of the scones with buttermilk to encourage browning.

  4. Bake for 15-18 minutes or until the scones are golden brown and cooked through. Remove from the oven and let cool on a wire rack.

3. Adding the Glaze (Optional)

  1. Mix powdered sugar, lemon juice, and lemon zest in a small bowl until smooth and drizzleable.

  2. Drizzle the glaze over the cooled scones and allow it to set for a few minutes before serving.

Tips for Success

  • Cold butter: Ensure your butter is cold and cubed before incorporating it into the flour mixture. This helps create flaky layers in the scones.

  • Gentle mixing: Avoid overmixing the dough to prevent tough scones. Mix until just combined for a tender texture.

  • Fresh ingredients: Use fresh blueberries and lemon zest for vibrant flavors that shine through in every bite.

  • Customization: Feel free to customize your scones with different fruits or even add a touch of cinnamon or nutmeg to the dough for added warmth.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your Lemon Blueberry Scones warm or at room temperature with a dollop of clotted cream and a spread of lemon curd. Pair them with a cup of freshly brewed tea or coffee for a delightful breakfast or afternoon treat.

Conclusion

Lemon Blueberry Scones are not just a baked good; they’re a culinary experience. With their buttery richness, bursts of juicy blueberries, and refreshing lemon zest, these scones are sure to brighten any day. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is a wonderful way to indulge in homemade goodness. Enjoy the process of baking these scones and savor the rewards—they’re worth every bite!
