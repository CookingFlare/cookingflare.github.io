Spanish Cooking for Beginners: 5 Easy Recipes to Start With


For those drawn to the flavors, aromas, and vibrant colors of Spanish cuisine, starting your cooking journey can feel both exciting and a bit daunting. Spanish cooking is a celebration of fresh ingredients, bold seasonings, and centuries-old traditions that vary from region to region. However, you don’t need to be an experienced chef or have access to a specialty pantry to delve into the heart of Spain’s culinary treasures. This guide introduces five simple and authentic Spanish recipes perfect for beginners, helping you bring the flavors of Spain into your kitchen one dish at a time.

1. Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

The Spanish tortilla is a classic dish that highlights the beauty of simplicity. Made with just a handful of ingredients—potatoes, eggs, olive oil, and a bit of seasoning—this recipe is a perfect starting point for beginners.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced (optional)
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a nonstick frying pan over medium heat. Add the sliced potatoes and onions, and season lightly with salt. Cook gently, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are soft but not browned.
  2. In a large bowl, beat the eggs and season with a pinch of salt. Drain the cooked potatoes and onions, letting the excess oil drip off, and then fold them into the eggs.
  3. Heat a small drizzle of the reserved oil in the same frying pan over medium heat. Pour the egg and potato mixture into the pan, spreading it gently into an even layer. Cook for 6–8 minutes until the edges are set and the bottom is golden.
  4. Carefully flip the tortilla onto a plate, then slide it back into the pan to cook the other side for 3–5 minutes. Once fully set and golden, transfer it to a serving plate.
  5. Serve warm or at room temperature, sliced into wedges. Tortilla Española is delicious on its own, with a side of crusty bread, or accompanied by a fresh green salad.

2. Pan con Tomate (Tomato Bread)

Pan con tomate is a quintessential Spanish breakfast or snack that takes no more than five minutes to prepare. Simple, rustic, and utterly delicious, it’s an effortless way to savor the Mediterranean spirit.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of crusty bread, toasted
  • 2 ripe tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, peeled
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Grate the ripe tomatoes using a box grater, discarding the skins, and set the pulp aside in a small bowl.
  2. While the bread is still warm, rub each slice with the garlic clove.
  3. Spoon the freshly grated tomato over each piece of bread. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
  4. Serve immediately as a standalone snack or alongside Spanish cheeses and cured meats like jamón serrano.

3. Gazpacho Andaluz (Cold Tomato Soup)

Gazpacho is a refreshing cold soup that originates from Andalusia, a southern region of Spain. This dish is a light and satisfying option for warm days and a great way for beginners to practice blending simple flavors with fresh ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, coarsely chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and diced
  • 1 small green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup cold water
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the tomatoes, cucumber, green bell pepper, and garlic in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  2. Add the olive oil, red wine vinegar, and cold water to the mixture. Season with salt and pepper, then blend again until well combined.
  3. Taste and adjust the seasoning, if needed. For a smoother texture, strain the gazpacho through a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Chill the soup in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours before serving. Serve cold, garnished with diced veggies, croutons, or a drizzle of olive oil.

4. Patatas Bravas (Spicy Potatoes)

Patatas bravas are a popular Spanish tapa, featuring crispy potatoes topped with a mildly spicy tomato-based sauce. They’re easy to make at home and perfect for sharing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil (for frying)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 1 can (14 oz) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss the potato cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread them in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crispy.
  2. For the sauce, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onion and garlic until soften. Add the smoked paprika and cayenne pepper, stirring for 1 minute.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes and sugar, then simmer the mixture for 10–15 minutes until slightly thickened. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread the bravas sauce over the crispy potatoes. Garnish with fresh parsley, and serve immediately.

5. Churros con Chocolate

For a sweet finish, churros con chocolate is a beloved Spanish dessert and a perfect introduction to Spanish baking. Crisp on the outside and soft on the inside, these fried pastries are served with a thick, velvety chocolate dipping sauce.

Ingredients for Churros:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Sugar and cinnamon for coating

Ingredients for Chocolate Sauce:

  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 oz bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

Instructions for Churros:

  1. In a saucepan, bring water, butter, sugar, and salt to a boil. Remove from heat and stir in flour until it forms a dough. Let it cool slightly, then transfer to a piping bag fitted with a star nozzle.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C). Pipe 4–6-inch strips of dough directly into the hot oil, cutting them with scissors. Fry until golden brown and crisp.
  3. Drain on paper towels, then roll in sugar-cinnamon mixture while still warm.

Instructions for Chocolate Sauce:

  1. In a small saucepan, whisk together milk, cornstarch, and sugar. Heat over medium heat until the mixture begins to thicken.
  2. Stir in the chocolate until melted and smooth. Cook for an additional 1–2 minutes to thicken further.
  3. Serve alongside the warm churros for dipping.

These five recipes offer a flavorful and approachable introduction to Spanish cuisine. From savory classics like tortilla Española to the indulgent churros con chocolate, each dish provides an opportunity to embrace the rich culinary heritage of Spain. As you prepare these dishes, you’ll not only familiarize yourself with staple ingredients like olive oil and smoked paprika but also deepen your appreciation for the warmth and vibrancy that define Spanish cooking. Happy cooking, or as the Spaniards say, ¡Buen provecho!
