Grilled Fruit Fiesta: 4 Marinades for a Summertime Treat


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy the great outdoors. And what better way to indulge in the season’s bountiful produce than by grilling some delicious fruits? Grilled fruits are not only a healthy alternative to traditional desserts, but they also add a burst of flavor and a touch of smokiness that takes your taste buds on a delightful journey. In this post, we’ll explore four mouthwatering marinades that will elevate your grilled fruit fiesta to new heights. Get ready to tantalize your senses and impress your guests with these summertime treats!

1. Citrus Splash

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated orange zest
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the orange juice, lemon juice, lime juice, honey, grated orange zest, grated lemon zest, and salt until well combined.
  2. Place your choice of fruits, such as pineapple slices, peach halves, and watermelon wedges, in a large ziplock bag.
  3. Pour the citrus splash marinade over the fruits, seal the bag, and gently massage to evenly coat the fruits.
  4. Allow the fruits to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a more intense flavor.
  5. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the fruits from the marinade, discarding any excess liquid.
  7. Grill the fruits for 2-3 minutes per side, or until grill marks appear and the fruits are tender.
  8. Serve the grilled fruits as a refreshing dessert or alongside grilled meats for a burst of citrusy goodness.

2. Honey Cinnamon Delight

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, lemon juice, ground cinnamon, and salt until well combined.
  2. Place your choice of fruits, such as apple slices, pear halves, and banana halves, in a large ziplock bag.
  3. Pour the honey cinnamon delight marinade over the fruits, seal the bag, and gently massage to evenly coat the fruits.
  4. Allow the fruits to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a more intense flavor.
  5. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the fruits from the marinade, discarding any excess liquid.
  7. Grill the fruits for 2-3 minutes per side, or until grill marks appear and the fruits are tender.
  8. Sprinkle with a dash of cinnamon before serving for an extra touch of flavor.

3. Coconut Lime Zing

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated lime zest
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, lime juice, honey, grated lime zest, and salt until well combined.
  2. Place your choice of fruits, such as mango slices, kiwi halves, and pineapple chunks, in a large ziplock bag.
  3. Pour the coconut lime zing marinade over the fruits, seal the bag, and gently massage to evenly coat the fruits.
  4. Allow the fruits to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a more intense flavor.
  5. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the fruits from the marinade, discarding any excess liquid.
  7. Grill the fruits for 2-3 minutes per side, or until grill marks appear and the fruits are tender.
  8. Serve the grilled fruits with a drizzle of coconut milk and a sprinkle of lime zest for an exotic twist.

4. Spicy Maple Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, soy sauce, hot sauce, ground cumin, and salt until well combined.
  2. Place your choice of fruits, such as peach halves, plum halves, and nectarine slices, in a large ziplock bag.
  3. Pour the spicy maple glaze marinade over the fruits, seal the bag, and gently massage to evenly coat the fruits.
  4. Allow the fruits to marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a more intense flavor.
  5. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  6. Remove the fruits from the marinade, discarding any excess liquid.
  7. Grill the fruits for 2-3 minutes per side, or until grill marks appear and the fruits are tender.
  8. Sprinkle with a pinch of cumin before serving for a spicy kick.

Conclusion

Grilled fruits are a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of summer. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply looking for a healthy dessert option, these four marinades will transform your ordinary fruits into a fiesta of taste sensations. From the refreshing citrus splash to the sweet and spicy maple glaze, there’s a marinade to suit every palate. So fire up the grill, grab your favorite fruits, and let the grilling adventure begin!
