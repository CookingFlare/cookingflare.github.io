How to Make and Can Your Own Cranberry Sauce


Cranberry sauce is a quintessential part of many holiday meals, especially during Thanksgiving and Christmas. While store-bought options are convenient, nothing compares to the rich, tangy flavor of homemade cranberry sauce. Not only is it easy to make, but canning your own cranberry sauce allows you to enjoy this delightful condiment year-round. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of making and canning your own cranberry sauce, ensuring you have a delicious and shelf-stable product to savor.

Why Make Your Own Cranberry Sauce?

Making your own cranberry sauce offers several advantages:

  1. Flavor: Homemade cranberry sauce can be tailored to your taste preferences. You can adjust the sweetness, acidity, and spice levels to create a sauce that perfectly complements your meals.

  2. Quality Ingredients: When you make your own sauce, you have complete control over the ingredients. You can choose organic cranberries, natural sweeteners, and fresh spices, ensuring a high-quality product.

  3. Preservation: Canning your cranberry sauce allows you to preserve it for months, making it a convenient option for future meals.

  4. Sustainability: By making your own sauce, you reduce packaging waste and support local farmers if you choose to buy fresh cranberries from a nearby source.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make and can your own cranberry sauce, gather the following ingredients:

  • Fresh Cranberries: 12 ounces (about 3 cups)
  • Granulated Sugar: 1 cup (adjust to taste)
  • Water: 1 cup
  • Zest of 1 Orange: Optional, for added flavor
  • Cinnamon Stick: Optional, for a warm spice note
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon (for acidity)

Equipment Needed

Before you start, ensure you have the following equipment:

  • Large Pot: For cooking the cranberry sauce.
  • Canning Jars: Use pint or half-pint jars, depending on your preference.
  • Canning Lids and Rings: Ensure they are new and in good condition.
  • Water Bath Canner: For processing the jars.
  • Jar Lifter: To safely remove hot jars from the canner.
  • Funnel: To help fill jars without spilling.
  • Ladle: For transferring the sauce into jars.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cranberries

  1. Rinse the Cranberries: Place the fresh cranberries in a colander and rinse them under cold water. Remove any stems, leaves, or damaged berries.

  2. Inspect the Berries: Discard any soft or shriveled cranberries, as they can affect the quality of your sauce.

Step 2: Cook the Cranberry Sauce

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large pot, combine the rinsed cranberries, sugar, water, and optional orange zest and cinnamon stick.

  2. Heat the Mixture: Place the pot over medium heat and stir until the sugar dissolves. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for about 10-15 minutes. You will notice the cranberries bursting and releasing their juices.

  4. Add Lemon Juice: Stir in the lemon juice for added brightness and acidity. If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender to puree the mixture to your desired consistency.

  5. Remove from Heat: Once the sauce has thickened to your liking, remove it from the heat. If you used a cinnamon stick, be sure to remove it at this stage.

Step 3: Prepare for Canning

  1. Sterilize Jars: Before canning, sterilize your jars and lids. You can do this by placing them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes or running them through a dishwasher cycle.

  2. Fill the Jars: Using a funnel, ladle the hot cranberry sauce into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top.

  3. Wipe the Rims: Use a clean cloth to wipe the rims of the jars to ensure a proper seal.

  4. Seal the Jars: Place the sterilized lids on top of the jars and screw on the rings until they are fingertip-tight.

Step 4: Process the Jars

  1. Prepare the Canner: Fill your water bath canner with enough water to cover the jars by at least an inch. Bring the water to a simmer.

  2. Process the Jars: Carefully place the filled jars into the canner using a jar lifter. Ensure they are not touching each other. Cover the canner with a lid and bring the water to a rolling boil.
