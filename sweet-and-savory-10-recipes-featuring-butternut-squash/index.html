Sweet and Savory: 10 Recipes Featuring Butternut Squash


Butternut squash, with its vibrant orange hue and sweet, nutty flavor, has become a staple in kitchens across the world. This versatile vegetable can be roasted, mashed, or even pureed into soups, making it an essential ingredient for both sweet and savory dishes. Whether you’re looking to add a comforting side dish to your holiday meals or trying to experiment with something new for dinner, butternut squash is your go-to ingredient. In this post, we’ll explore ten creative recipes that highlight the unique flavor and texture of butternut squash in both sweet and savory ways.

1. Roasted Butternut Squash Soup

Nothing is quite as comforting as a warm bowl of homemade soup, especially when it features the rich, earthy sweetness of roasted butternut squash. Roasting the squash before blending it into a smooth soup intensifies its natural flavor. This velvety soup pairs perfectly with a crusty loaf of bread or a light salad.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk (optional, for extra creaminess)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the chopped butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread it on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  3. Roast for 30-35 minutes, until the squash is tender and lightly caramelized.
  4. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until soft, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the roasted squash, vegetable broth, cumin, and cinnamon. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
  6. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth (or transfer in batches to a regular blender).
  7. Stir in coconut milk for extra creaminess if desired. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  8. Serve hot with a drizzle of olive oil or a sprinkle of fresh herbs.

2. Butternut Squash and Quinoa Salad

A colorful salad packed with protein and fiber, this butternut squash and quinoa salad is a nutritious option for a light lunch or a side dish at dinner. The quinoa provides a hearty base, while the roasted squash adds a sweet and savory contrast. Tossed with a tangy lemon vinaigrette, this salad is as satisfying as it is refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  3. While the squash is roasting, cook the quinoa. Bring 2 cups of water or vegetable broth to a boil, add the quinoa, cover, and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for about 15 minutes, until the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is tender.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted butternut squash, feta, pumpkin seeds, and cranberries.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently.
  6. Serve warm or chilled.

3. Butternut Squash and Spinach Lasagna

Lasagna gets a seasonal twist with the addition of roasted butternut squash and spinach. This dish combines the creamy richness of cheese with the sweetness of the squash, making it a comforting and hearty meal perfect for cooler months.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 3 cups fresh spinach, wilted
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper, then roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  3. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of mozzarella, Parmesan, wilted spinach, salt, and pepper. Stir in the roasted butternut squash and thyme.
  5. In a baking dish, layer the lasagna starting with a spoonful of marinara sauce, then a layer of noodles, followed by the cheese and squash mixture. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used up.
  6. Top with the remaining mozzarella cheese and bake for 25-30 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  7. Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

4. Butternut Squash Breakfast Muffins

These butternut squash breakfast muffins are a perfect way to start your day with a healthy and flavorful twist. The subtle sweetness of the squash pairs beautifully with the warmth of cinnamon, making these muffins a great addition to your morning routine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup pureed butternut squash
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix the pureed butternut squash, honey, eggs, coconut oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Combine the wet ingredients with the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  6. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the muffins to cool before enjoying.

5. Butternut Squash Risotto

This creamy butternut squash risotto is the epitome of comfort food. The rice is cooked slowly with a bit of white wine and vegetable broth, absorbing all the flavors from the roasted squash. The final result is a creamy, dreamy dish that’s perfect for a special dinner or a weeknight meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and toss the diced butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  2. In a large pan, sauté the onion and garlic in a bit of olive oil until soft.
  3. Add the Arborio rice and stir to coat the grains in the oil.
  4. Pour in the white wine and stir until absorbed.
  5. Gradually add the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Allow the liquid to absorb before adding more.
  6. Continue this process until the rice is cooked al dente and creamy (about 20 minutes).
  7. Stir in the roasted butternut squash and Parmesan cheese.
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve warm.

6. Butternut Squash Tacos

Butternut squash tacos offer a fantastic balance of sweetness and spice. The roasted squash becomes caramelized and slightly crispy, making it the perfect filling for your favorite taco shells. Add a little avocado, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, and you’ve got a delicious, plant-based meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  3. Heat the tortillas in a dry pan or microwave.
  4. Assemble the tacos by filling each tortilla with roasted squash, avocado slices, and a sprinkle of cilantro.
  5. Squeeze fresh lime juice over the top before serving.

7. Butternut Squash Pizza with Arugula

Take your pizza game to the next level with a butternut squash topping. Roasted squash pairs beautifully with the peppery bite of arugula and the creaminess of goat cheese, creating a pizza that’s both savory and sweet.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and sliced into thin rounds
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pizza dough
  • 1/2 cup goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1 cup arugula
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic glaze

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C).
  2. Toss the sliced butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes until tender.
  3. Roll out your pizza dough on a baking sheet or pizza stone.
  4. Top with roasted squash and goat cheese, then bake for 10-12 minutes until the crust is golden and crispy.
  5. Once the pizza is out of the oven, top with fresh arugula and drizzle with balsamic glaze.

8. Butternut Squash and Sage Pasta

Butternut squash and sage create a heavenly combination in this simple yet sophisticated pasta dish. The sweetness of the squash complements the savory notes of the crispy sage leaves, and the creamy sauce brings everything together for a dish that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 12 oz pasta (your choice)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup fresh sage leaves
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and toss the diced butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 25-30 minutes.
  2. Cook your pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, melt the butter over medium heat and add the sage leaves. Cook until crispy.
  4. Add the roasted squash to the pan and mash lightly with a fork to create a creamy texture.
  5. Toss in the cooked pasta and Parmesan, and stir until well coated.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning before serving.

9. Butternut Squash and Apple Casserole

This casserole brings together the sweetness of apples and butternut squash for a deliciously comforting dish. The combination of tender squash, juicy apples, and a crunchy topping makes this the perfect side dish for holiday gatherings or a cozy dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a greased casserole dish, layer the butternut squash and apples.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the breadcrumbs, brown sugar, cinnamon, and melted butter. Sprinkle this mixture evenly over the top of the squash and apples.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes, until the squash is tender and the topping is golden and crunchy.

10. Butternut Squash and Lentil Curry

This flavorful curry is a hearty and nourishing dish, perfect for a weeknight dinner. The butternut squash adds a natural sweetness, which pairs wonderfully with the earthy lentils and fragrant spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup lentils
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot and sauté the onion and garlic until soft.
  2. Add the spices and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  3. Add the butternut squash, lentils, coconut milk, and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  4. Cook for 25-30 minutes, until the squash and lentils are tender and the curry has thickened.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper before serving.

Butternut squash truly shines in both sweet and savory dishes. From soups and salads to casseroles and curries, this versatile vegetable can be incorporated into your meals in so many delicious ways. Whether you’re looking for comforting dishes to warm you up or light, nutritious meals to enjoy year-round, these recipes will help you make the most of this seasonal favorite. Happy cooking!
