Irresistible Pecan Pie: A Southern Christmas Classic


Pecan pie is a beloved Southern dessert that has become a staple during the Christmas season. The rich, buttery crust combined with the sweet and nutty filling creates a truly irresistible treat. In this recipe, I’ll share with you the secrets to making the perfect pecan pie that will leave your guests begging for more.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 2-3 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup light corn syrup
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups pecan halves

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt for the crust. Add in the cold, cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, corn syrup, melted butter, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.

  5. Take the chilled dough out of the refrigerator and roll it out on a lightly floured surface. Transfer the rolled dough into a 9-inch pie dish and gently press it into the bottom and sides.

  6. Arrange the pecan halves evenly over the bottom of the pie crust. Pour the filling mixture over the pecans, making sure they are well coated.

  7. Place the pie dish on a baking sheet and carefully transfer it to the preheated oven. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.

  8. Once the pie is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack before serving. This will allow the filling to set properly.

  9. Serve slices of the irresistible pecan pie on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence. Enjoy the sweet and nutty flavors that are characteristic of this Southern Christmas classic.

Tips and Variations

  • For added flavor, you can toast the pecans before adding them to the pie. Simply spread the pecan halves on a baking sheet and toast them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes, or until fragrant. Let them cool before using.

  • If you prefer a gooey filling, reduce the baking time by 5-10 minutes. Keep an eye on the pie towards the end of the baking time to ensure it doesn’t get overcooked.

  • Experiment with different types of nuts, such as walnuts or almonds, to create your own unique variation of this classic pecan pie.

  • To make the pie ahead of time, you can prepare the crust and filling separately, then assemble and bake the pie on the day you plan to serve it. This will save you time and ensure the pie is as fresh as possible.

Conclusion

Pecan pie is a timeless dessert that brings warmth and joy to any holiday gathering. With this recipe, you can easily recreate the flavors of the South in your own kitchen. Whether it’s Christmas or any other time of the year, this irresistible pecan pie is sure to be a hit with your family and friends. So go ahead, indulge in a slice of this Southern classic and savor every sweet and nutty bite.
