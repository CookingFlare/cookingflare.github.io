Delicious Flatbreads to Pair with Your Favorite Dips


Flatbreads have become a staple in kitchens all over the world. Their versatility, deliciousness, and ability to complement a wide range of dishes make them a go-to option for pairing with dips. Whether you’re hosting a casual get-together or just looking for a tasty snack, flatbreads can elevate the flavor experience of your dips to new heights. From classic hummus to savory cheese spreads, flatbreads serve as the perfect vehicle for scooping, dipping, and savoring.

In this post, we’ll explore several different types of flatbreads, how they’re made, and how they can be paired with your favorite dips. Whether you prefer a soft, pillowy texture or a crispy crunch, there’s a flatbread here for everyone.

What is Flatbread?

Flatbread is a type of bread that is made with simple ingredients—usually flour, water, and salt—and is rolled or stretched into thin, flat shapes before being baked, grilled, or cooked. Unlike traditional leavened bread, flatbreads are either unleavened or only lightly leavened, making them denser and easier to cook quickly.

Flatbreads come in various forms and have been a staple of many cuisines for thousands of years. They are often associated with Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and Indian cuisine, but they can be found in cultures worldwide, each with its own variation. These versatile breads are enjoyed in countless forms, from soft and chewy to crispy and crunchy.

Now that we know what flatbreads are, let’s dive into some mouthwatering varieties of flatbreads that pair wonderfully with your favorite dips.

1. Classic Pita Bread

Pita bread is a classic flatbread with a soft, pillowy texture that’s perfect for dipping. It’s known for its signature “pocket” that forms when the dough puffs up during baking. This feature makes pita a favorite for stuffing with meats, vegetables, or spreads, but it’s also a great choice for dipping into various dips like hummus, baba ganoush, and tzatziki.

How to Make Pita Bread

Making pita bread at home is relatively simple. You’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Combine the yeast, sugar, and warm water in a bowl. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the yeast becomes frothy.
  2. Add the flour and salt to the bowl and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C) and place a baking stone or inverted baking sheet inside to heat.
  6. Punch down the dough and divide it into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle about 6 inches in diameter.
  7. Bake the pitas for 5-7 minutes, or until they puff up and develop golden brown spots.
  8. Remove from the oven and let them cool before cutting them into wedges for dipping.

Best Dips for Pita Bread

Pita bread pairs wonderfully with a variety of dips, including:

  • Hummus – This Middle Eastern dip made from pureed chickpeas, tahini, garlic, and olive oil is the ultimate match for pita.
  • Baba Ganoush – Smoky and creamy eggplant dip with tahini, garlic, and lemon juice.
  • Tzatziki – A refreshing cucumber-yogurt dip with dill and garlic.
  • Spinach and Artichoke Dip – A creamy, cheesy dip loaded with spinach and artichokes.

2. Naan Bread

Naan is a popular Indian flatbread that’s soft, chewy, and often flavored with yogurt and garlic. Traditionally baked in a tandoor oven, naan is a versatile flatbread that works beautifully as a side dish to curries or as a base for dipping into rich and flavorful dips.

How to Make Naan Bread

Here’s a simple naan bread recipe to try at home:

  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 garlic clove, minced (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix the yeast, sugar, and warm water in a bowl. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture, olive oil, yogurt, and melted butter. Stir to form a dough.
  3. Knead the dough for about 8 minutes until smooth. Cover and let it rise for 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  4. Preheat a large skillet or tandoor oven. Roll the dough into small balls, then flatten them into circles.
  5. Cook each piece of dough in the skillet over medium-high heat for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown and slightly charred.
  6. Brush with melted butter and minced garlic for extra flavor, if desired.

Best Dips for Naan Bread

Naan pairs exceptionally well with:

  • Mango Chutney – A sweet and tangy dip made with mangoes, vinegar, and sugar.
  • Raita – A yogurt-based dip with cucumber, mint, and spices.
  • Curry Dips – Spicy, creamy curry dips that are perfect for naan to scoop up.
  • Lentil Dip – A savory dip made with spiced lentils and a touch of lemon.

3. Lavash

Lavash is a traditional Armenian flatbread that’s thin and soft with a slight crispiness around the edges. It’s often used to wrap up grilled meats and vegetables, but it also makes a fantastic dipper for spreads, dips, and cheeses.

How to Make Lavash

To make lavash, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast

Instructions:

  1. Mix the yeast, sugar, and warm water in a bowl and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. Combine the flour and salt in a large bowl. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover the dough and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  5. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C).
  6. Roll the dough into thin rounds, about 10 inches in diameter, and place them onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for about 5-7 minutes, or until the lavash is crisp and lightly browned on the edges.

Best Dips for Lavash

Lavash is perfect for pairing with:

  • Pesto – A rich, herbaceous dip made with basil, garlic, pine nuts, and Parmesan.
  • Cheese Dip – A smooth, melty cheese dip with spices and herbs.
  • Muhammara – A Syrian dip made from roasted red peppers, walnuts, and olive oil.
  • Olives and Olive Oil – Simple, yet delicious, you can dip your lavash in olive oil and garnish it with herbs and spices.

4. Focaccia

Focaccia is a thick, Italian flatbread that’s soft on the inside with a crispy crust. While it’s often served as a side bread or sandwich bread, it also works wonderfully for dipping. Focaccia is typically flavored with olive oil, herbs, and sea salt, giving it a rich, savory flavor that complements various dips.

How to Make Focaccia

To prepare focaccia, you will need:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 ½ cups warm water
  • ¼ cup olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar in a bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir to form a dough.
  3. Knead the dough for 10 minutes, then cover and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  4. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  5. After the dough has risen, punch it down and spread it onto a greased baking sheet. Use your fingers to create dimples in the dough.
  6. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with sea salt and herbs.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown on top.

Best Dips for Focaccia

Focaccia pairs excellently with:

  • Balsamic Vinegar and Olive Oil – A classic combination of tangy balsamic vinegar and rich olive oil for dipping.
  • Tomato and Basil Dip – A fresh, tangy dip made with tomatoes, garlic, and fresh basil.
  • Garlic Butter – Melted garlic butter for a rich and indulgent dipping experience.
  • Ricotta with Honey and Lemon – A sweet and savory dip with creamy ricotta, honey, and a touch of lemon.

Conclusion

Flatbreads are incredibly versatile and delicious, making them a perfect choice to pair with a wide range of dips. Whether you’re making pita, naan, lavash, or focaccia, each type of flatbread has its unique texture and flavor profile, allowing you to experiment with different combinations of dips to find your favorite pairing.

The next time you’re preparing a snack, appetizer, or meal, consider baking one of these flavorful flatbreads to accompany your favorite dips. With so many varieties to choose from, there’s a perfect flatbread waiting for you to enjoy!
