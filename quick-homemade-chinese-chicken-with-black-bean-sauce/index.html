Quick Homemade Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce


Chinese cuisine is a beautiful blend of flavors, textures, and colors, offering a diverse array of dishes that cater to every palate. Among the vast variety, chicken with black bean sauce stands out for its unique taste profile. The bold umami of fermented black beans combined with savory, tender chicken makes for a quick and satisfying meal.

If you love the rich flavors of Chinese food but don’t always have the time to cook it, then this quick homemade Chinese chicken with black bean sauce recipe is perfect for you. In this blog post, we will dive deep into making this iconic dish in your own kitchen, offering tips, variations, and a step-by-step guide to ensure a successful and delightful meal every time.

What is Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce?

Chinese chicken with black bean sauce is a stir-fry dish often found in Chinese restaurants, especially in the Cantonese style of cooking. The main ingredients are tender chicken, fermented black beans, garlic, ginger, and other seasonings. The black beans used in this dish are typically fermented soybeans that have been salted. They lend a savory, slightly tangy, and deep umami flavor to the dish.

The sauce itself is made by combining fermented black beans with soy sauce, rice wine, and sugar, then simmering the mixture until it forms a rich, flavorful base that coats the chicken perfectly. Vegetables like bell peppers, onions, or even chilies are often added to enhance the flavor and provide texture.

This dish is perfect served over steamed rice or noodles, as the sauce pairs beautifully with the mildness of the rice or pasta, allowing the bold flavors to shine.

Ingredients for Homemade Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce

To create this dish at home, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken:

  • 500g boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Black Bean Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons fermented black beans (available at Asian grocery stores)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce (optional for extra depth)
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock (or water if you don’t have chicken stock)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (for extra flavor)

Vegetables and Aromatics:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for stir-frying)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 small chili (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • 2 tablespoons spring onions (green onions), sliced (for garnish)

How to Make Quick Homemade Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by marinating the chicken to infuse it with flavor. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of rice wine (if using), and 1 teaspoon of cornstarch. Toss everything together so that the chicken is evenly coated with the marinade. Allow it to sit for about 10 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.

Step 2: Prepare the Black Bean Sauce

In a separate small bowl, combine the fermented black beans, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce (if using), chicken stock, and sesame oil. Stir everything together and set aside. The fermented black beans add a salty, umami-packed element to the sauce, so don’t skip this key ingredient. If you’re using dried black beans, you might want to soak them in warm water for a few minutes before using.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Chicken

In a wok or large frying pan, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken pieces to the pan in a single layer. Let the chicken cook undisturbed for about 3-4 minutes until it’s golden brown on one side. Flip the chicken pieces and cook for another 2-3 minutes until fully cooked and no longer pink in the center. Once done, remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Vegetables and Aromatics

In the same pan, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil if needed. Throw in the minced garlic and ginger and stir-fry them for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Be careful not to burn them, as garlic can turn bitter quickly.

Next, add the sliced red bell pepper, onion, and chili (if using). Stir-fry the vegetables for about 2-3 minutes, ensuring they remain crisp and colorful. If you prefer softer vegetables, you can cook them a little longer.

Step 5: Add the Black Bean Sauce

Now it’s time to bring everything together. Pour the pre-made black bean sauce into the pan with the vegetables. Stir to combine and let the sauce simmer for 1-2 minutes so the flavors meld together.

Step 6: Combine the Chicken with the Sauce

Return the cooked chicken to the pan and stir to coat it in the black bean sauce. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken and glaze the chicken. You can adjust the thickness of the sauce by adding a bit more chicken stock or water if necessary. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning with more soy sauce or sugar to your liking.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Once the chicken is fully coated in the black bean sauce, remove the pan from the heat. Transfer the dish to a serving platter and garnish with fresh spring onions. This dish is best served immediately with steamed rice or noodles to soak up all that delicious sauce.

Tips for Perfecting Your Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce

  1. Use Chicken Thighs for Extra Juiciness: While chicken breasts work fine, using boneless, skinless chicken thighs will give you a juicier and more tender result. The thighs also hold up better during stir-frying.

  2. Fermented Black Beans Are Key: Fermented black beans are the backbone of this dish, offering a unique flavor that you can’t substitute easily. If you can’t find them, look for other types of fermented beans or consider using miso paste as a base, though the flavor won’t be quite the same.

  3. Customize the Vegetables: You can customize the vegetables according to your preferences. Snap peas, baby corn, or mushrooms are great additions. Feel free to experiment with what you have in your kitchen!

  4. Adjust the Spice Level: If you like heat, add more chili or even a dash of chili paste to the sauce. For a milder version, omit the chili and go easy on the ginger.

  5. Don’t Overcook the Vegetables: For that perfect crunch, make sure you don’t overcook the vegetables. They should still have some bite when you serve the dish.

Pairing Ideas for Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce

1. Steamed Jasmine Rice

Jasmine rice, with its delicate fragrance, pairs perfectly with the bold flavors of this dish. The soft, fluffy rice absorbs the sauce beautifully, offering a perfect contrast to the savory chicken.

2. Noodles

If you’re in the mood for noodles, this chicken with black bean sauce can be served over stir-fried or plain noodles. Choose thin egg noodles or even rice noodles for an authentic touch.

3. Asian Greens

Sautéed or steamed Asian greens like bok choy or Chinese broccoli are a fantastic side dish. Their slightly bitter flavor provides a nice balance to the richness of the chicken.

4. Egg Rolls or Dumplings

For a more substantial meal, consider serving your chicken with crispy egg rolls or dumplings. The crispy textures will contrast nicely with the saucy chicken.

Variations of Chinese Chicken with Black Bean Sauce

1. Chicken with Black Bean Sauce and Tofu

If you’re looking for a vegetarian alternative, swap the chicken for tofu. Firm tofu works best in stir-fries, as it absorbs the flavors of the sauce while holding its shape. Simply follow the same steps, stir-frying the tofu first before adding the sauce.

2. Add More Protein

Feel free to experiment with other proteins like beef, shrimp, or pork in place of chicken. Beef tends to pair wonderfully with black bean sauce, and shrimp will give the dish a lighter, seafood flavor.

3. Spicy Black Bean Sauce

If you love heat, you can add more chili or even a tablespoon of chili paste or chili oil to the sauce. This version will have a spicy kick that adds excitement to every bite.

4. Vegetable-Only Black Bean Sauce Stir-Fry

For a lighter option, you can make a vegetable-only stir-fry using the black bean sauce. Vegetables like mushrooms, zucchini, carrots, and bell peppers work wonderfully in this version, offering a deliciously savory meal.

Final Thoughts

Making Chinese chicken with black bean sauce at home is a rewarding experience, and with a few key ingredients, you can recreate this popular restaurant dish in the comfort of your kitchen. Not only is it quick and easy, but it’s also highly customizable to suit your tastes. The savory black bean sauce, tender chicken, and crisp vegetables come together to create a meal that’s satisfying, flavorful, and full of depth.

Give this recipe a try the next time you’re craving Chinese food, and you’ll be amazed at how simple it is to prepare. With the right ingredients and a little practice, you can enjoy a restaurant-quality meal right at home, all without the need to leave your kitchen!
