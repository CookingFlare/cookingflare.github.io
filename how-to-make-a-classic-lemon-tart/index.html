How to Make a Classic Lemon Tart


When it comes to desserts that strike the perfect balance between indulgence and freshness, the classic lemon tart sits confidently at the top of the list. With its smooth, tangy filling and buttery, flaky crust, this dessert is both elegant and bursting with zesty flavor. Whether you’re preparing it for a dinner party or simply to treat yourself, a well-made lemon tart is guaranteed to impress. Follow along as we break down the process step-by-step to help you create a flawless dessert.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Crust:

  • 1 ¼ cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • ⅓ cup (40g) powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1-2 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • ½ cup (120ml) freshly squeezed lemon juice (about 4-5 lemons)
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • ½ cup (120ml) heavy cream
  • Pinch of salt

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

The crust, known as pâte brisée in French baking, forms the foundation of the tart. A buttery crust offers the perfect contrast to the tanginess of the filling, so nailing this step is key.

Combine the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour and powdered sugar. This ensures a fine texture and prevents lumps in the crust.

Incorporate the Butter

Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients. Use your fingertips or a pastry cutter to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Cold butter helps create a flaky texture as it melts during baking.

Add the Wet Ingredients

Mix in the egg yolk and gradually add ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix; you want the crust to remain tender and not overly dense.

Chill the Dough

Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the dough allows the butter to firm up and makes it easier to roll out later.

Step 2: Roll Out and Bake the Crust

Once the dough is sufficiently chilled, it’s time to roll it out and blind-bake it to ensure a crisp and golden base for the lemon filling.

Preheat Your Oven

Set your oven to 375°F (190°C) to create an even, hot environment for pie crust baking.

Roll the Dough

Take the chilled dough out of the fridge and place it on a lightly floured surface. Roll it out into a circle about 12 inches in diameter and ⅛ inch thick. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan, pressing it gently into the edges. Trim off any excess dough around the rim.

Blind-Bake the Crust

To prevent the crust from puffing up, place a sheet of parchment paper over it and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove the parchment and weights, then bake for another 5-7 minutes until the crust is lightly golden. Set it aside to cool completely.

Step 3: Make the Lemon Filling

The filling is the heart and soul of a lemon tart. It’s soft and luscious with a citrus-forward flavor that’s wonderfully refreshing yet rich.

Whisk Together the Eggs and Sugar

In a medium-sized mixing bowl, beat the eggs and granulated sugar until pale and creamy. This ensures a smooth, custard-like texture for the filling.

Incorporate the Lemon Juice and Zest

Add the freshly squeezed lemon juice and zest, stirring well to combine. The zest intensifies the lemon flavor while the juice brings the essential tangy bite.

Add the Cream and Salt

Pour in the heavy cream and add a pinch of salt to enrich the filling and balance the sweetness. Stir until the mixture is fully combined.

Step 4: Assemble and Bake

Pour the Filling into the Crust

Once the crust has cooled completely, pour the lemon filling into it. Smooth the surface with a spatula if needed to create an even layer.

Bake the Tart

Reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (163°C). Bake the tart for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set but still slightly wobbly in the center. Avoid overbaking, as this can cause cracks.

Cool and Chill

Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool completely at room temperature. Once cooled, refrigerate it for at least two hours to allow the filling to firm up before serving.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

To present your lemon tart in all its glory, dust the surface lightly with powdered sugar or decorate with fresh berries and mint leaves. Slice into wedges using a sharp knife and serve chilled or at room temperature.

The combination of buttery crust and tangy filling creates a harmonious flavor profile that’s perfectly complemented by each bite. Whether paired with a cup of coffee or served with a dollop of whipped cream, this dessert truly shines.

Tips for Success

Don’t Skip Blind-Baking

Blind-baking prevents the crust from becoming soggy when the filling is added. It’s a crucial step for achieving the perfect texture.

Use Fresh Lemons

Freshly squeezed lemon juice is non-negotiable. Bottled juice lacks the vibrancy and fragrance of real lemons, which are essential for a classic lemon tart.

Work Quickly with the Dough

To maintain the crust’s flakiness, avoid handling the dough excessively or allowing the butter to melt before baking.

Check for Doneness

The filling should have a slight jiggle when you gently shake the tart pan. This indicates that it’s perfectly cooked and will firm up as it cools.

Why Make This at Home?

Making a classic lemon tart from scratch allows you to control every element for a truly tailored dessert experience. From the crisp homemade crust to the tangy filling bursting with natural flavor, each bite reflects the effort you put into creating it. Plus, it’s a rewarding way to engage with timeless baking techniques.

For dessert lovers and aspiring bakers alike, few recipes are as satisfying as crafting a classic lemon tart that looks stunning and tastes even better. So roll up your sleeves, grab some fresh lemons, and start baking—the sweet rewards await!
