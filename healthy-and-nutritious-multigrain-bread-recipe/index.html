Healthy and Nutritious Multigrain Bread Recipe


If you’re a bread lover looking for a healthier alternative, this multigrain bread recipe is perfect for you. Packed with wholesome grains and seeds, this bread is not only delicious but also a great source of nutrition. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, this recipe is simple enough for anyone to follow. Let’s dive into the details and get baking!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup flaxseeds
  • 1/4 cup sunflower seeds
  • 1/4 cup pumpkin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons chia seeds
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, honey, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.

  2. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, rolled oats, flaxseeds, sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, chia seeds, and salt.

  3. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the frothy yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a shaggy dough forms.

  4. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until the dough becomes smooth and elastic.

  5. First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

  6. Shape the Loaf: Once the dough has risen, punch it down and shape it into a loaf. Place the loaf in a greased loaf pan, cover it, and let it rise for another 30-45 minutes.

  7. Preheat the Oven: While the dough is rising, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  8. Bake the Bread: Once the dough has risen in the pan, place it in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  9. Cool and Enjoy: Remove the bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and enjoy with your favorite spreads or as a side to your meals.

Nutritional Benefits

This multigrain bread is not only a treat for your taste buds but also a nutritional powerhouse. Whole wheat flour provides fiber, while the various seeds and oats contribute healthy fats, protein, and essential minerals. The combination of these ingredients makes this bread a great addition to a balanced diet.

Conclusion

Baking your own multigrain bread allows you to control the ingredients, ensuring that you and your family enjoy a wholesome, nutritious loaf. Once you try this recipe, you’ll never look back at store-bought bread the same way. Experiment with different seeds and grains to customize the recipe to your liking, and savor the satisfaction of creating a delicious and healthy loaf from scratch. Happy baking!
