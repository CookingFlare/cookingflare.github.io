Easter Recipes for a Farm Fresh Feast


Easter is a time to celebrate renewal, family, and the bounty of spring, making it the perfect occasion to gather loved ones and share a meal bursting with fresh, seasonal ingredients. With longer days and warmer weather, the Easter table is an opportunity to embrace vibrant flavors, light textures, and farm-fresh produce. This guide features a complete farm-to-table Easter menu filled with delicious, thoughtfully curated dishes that will infuse your celebration with warmth, tradition, and flavor.

Appetizers: A Fresh Start to Your Easter Feast

Spring Pea and Mint Crostini

Nothing says spring quite like the vibrant green of freshly picked peas. This simple yet elegant appetizer pairs lightly sweet peas with aromatic mint, creating a perfect balance of flavors. Serve it atop crispy crostini for a satisfying crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup freshly shelled peas (or frozen peas, thawed)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Baguette slices, toasted

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, blanch peas in boiling water for 2-3 minutes before transferring to an ice bath to preserve their bright color.
  2. Blend the peas, mint, lemon juice, and olive oil into a coarse mixture using a food processor or blender. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Spoon the pea mixture onto toasted baguette slices, garnish with additional mint, and serve immediately.

Deviled Eggs with Farm Fresh Eggs and Herbs

Deviled eggs are a timeless Easter favorite, but using high-quality farm eggs takes this dish to the next level. Fresh herbs like dill, chives, and parsley add a garden-fresh touch.

Ingredients:

  • 6 hard-boiled eggs
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • Fresh chives, dill, and parsley, chopped
  • Paprika for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel the eggs and cut them lengthwise. Remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
  2. Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and a pinch of salt and pepper until smooth.
  3. Fold in half of the chopped herbs. Spoon or pipe the mixture back into the eggs, then top with remaining herbs and a sprinkle of paprika.

Main Course: A Celebration of Fresh Flavors

Herb-Crusted Leg of Lamb

For many, lamb is the centerpiece of Easter dinner. A leg of lamb seasoned with fresh rosemary, thyme, and garlic creates a beautifully aromatic roast that pairs perfectly with the fresh flavors of spring.

Ingredients:

  • 1 boneless leg of lamb (about 4–5 pounds)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of one lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, combine garlic, rosemary, thyme, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper to form a paste.
  3. Rub the mixture generously all over the lamb, ensuring it is evenly coated.
  4. Place the lamb on a roasting rack in a pan and roast in the oven until the internal temperature reaches 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare (about 1.5–2 hours).
  5. Let the lamb rest for 15 minutes before carving and serving.

Honey-Glazed Carrots

Bright, sweet, and simple, roasted honey-glazed carrots are the ideal side dish. Their natural sweetness pairs beautifully with the savory lamb.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound carrots, peeled and trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange the carrots on a baking sheet. Drizzle with honey, olive oil, and season with thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast for 20–25 minutes, turning halfway through, until the carrots are tender and caramelized.

Sides: Fresh and Vibrant Accents to the Meal

Spring Asparagus Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

Asparagus is one of spring’s first gifts, making it a must-have at any Easter feast. A light lemon dressing amplifies its natural flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 cups mixed spring greens
  • 1/4 cup toasted almonds
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • Juice of one lemon
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the asparagus in boiling water for 2 minutes, then immediately transfer to an ice bath.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper to create a vinaigrette.
  3. Toss the asparagus and mixed greens with the vinaigrette, and top with almonds and goat cheese before serving.

Cheddar and Chive Buttermilk Biscuits

No feast is complete without warm, flaky biscuits. Adding sharp cheddar cheese and fresh chives makes these buttermilk delights a flavorful addition.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk, cold
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Cut the cold butter into the dry ingredients using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Stir in buttermilk, cheddar, and chives, mixing just until combined.
  5. Roll out the dough to 1-inch thickness, cut with a biscuit cutter, and place on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 12–15 minutes or until golden brown.

Dessert: A Sweet Finale

Lemon Ricotta Cake

Light, tangy, and moist, this lemon ricotta cake is the perfect way to end your Easter meal. The combination of zesty lemon and creamy ricotta is a match made in springtime heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • Zest of two lemons
  • Juice of one lemon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, mix flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk ricotta, sugar, eggs, lemon zest, and lemon juice until smooth.
  4. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, then gently mix in the melted butter.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake for 40–45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  6. Cool before serving, and garnish with powdered sugar or fresh berries if desired.

Wrapping Up Your Easter Feast

Easter is a time of renewal and joy, and your feast should reflect just that. By using farm-fresh produce and seasonal ingredients, you’ll create a menu that is not only delightful but also celebrates the spirit of the holiday. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a large family dinner, these recipes are sure to bring comfort, flavor, and memories to your Easter celebration. So, roll up your sleeves, set the table, and toast to the arrival of spring with a menu that honors the freshness of the season!
