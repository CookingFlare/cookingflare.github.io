Instant Pot Butter Chicken: A Rich and Creamy Indian Classic


Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is one of the most beloved dishes in Indian cuisine. It’s a rich, flavorful, and creamy chicken curry that is perfect for any occasion. The unique combination of spices, tomatoes, cream, and butter creates a dish that is indulgent, comforting, and absolutely delicious. Traditionally, butter chicken is slow-cooked, but with the convenience of the Instant Pot, you can recreate this classic in less than an hour, without compromising on flavor.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to make Instant Pot Butter Chicken from scratch, using simple ingredients that you may already have in your pantry. By using the Instant Pot, you’ll save time while still achieving the same luxurious, melt-in-your-mouth texture that makes butter chicken so irresistible.

Ingredients for Instant Pot Butter Chicken

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 ½ pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste

For the Butter Chicken Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Optional:

  • 1 tablespoon sugar (if you prefer a slightly sweeter taste)
  • Cooked rice or naan for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Instant Pot Butter Chicken

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

The key to tender and flavorful butter chicken is in the marinade. Start by cutting the chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces and placing them in a large bowl. Add the yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, cumin, coriander, paprika, garam masala, chili powder, and salt to the chicken. Mix well until the chicken is evenly coated in the marinade.

Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes, although marinating for a few hours or overnight in the fridge will yield even better results. The longer the chicken sits in the marinade, the more the flavors will penetrate the meat.

Step 2: Sauté the Onions and Aromatics

Once the chicken is marinated, set your Instant Pot to the “Sauté” function and add the unsalted butter. Let the butter melt, then add the finely chopped onion to the pot. Sauté the onions for about 5 minutes, or until they become soft and translucent. Stir occasionally to ensure that they don’t burn.

Next, add the ginger-garlic paste to the onions. Sauté the mixture for another 2 minutes, allowing the ginger and garlic to become fragrant. This step enhances the overall depth of flavor in the sauce, giving it a rich, savory aroma.

Step 3: Add the Tomato Paste and Spices

Add the tomato paste to the Instant Pot and cook it with the onion mixture for 2-3 minutes. The paste will caramelize slightly, deepening the flavor of the sauce. Once the tomato paste has darkened, add the crushed tomatoes, ground cumin, ground coriander, chili powder, garam masala, and ground cinnamon to the pot. Stir everything together to combine the spices with the tomato mixture.

The addition of the cinnamon adds a subtle warmth to the dish, which balances the heat from the chili powder and the richness of the butter and cream.

Step 4: Add the Marinated Chicken

Once the spices and tomato mixture have come together, add the marinated chicken to the pot. Stir to combine the chicken with the sauce, making sure the chicken is evenly coated in the spices and tomato mixture. At this stage, the Instant Pot should have a lovely, fragrant base that will infuse the chicken with flavor.

Close the lid of the Instant Pot and set the valve to the “Sealing” position. Cook the chicken on high pressure for 6 minutes. While this may seem like a short amount of time, the Instant Pot’s pressure cooking function ensures that the chicken cooks quickly and remains tender.

Step 5: Quick Release and Finish the Sauce

Once the Instant Pot has finished cooking, carefully perform a quick release by turning the valve to “Venting.” This will release the built-up steam, and once the pin drops, you can open the lid.

At this point, the chicken should be cooked through and tender. The sauce will likely appear a bit thin, but that’s perfectly fine—this is where the magic happens. Add the heavy cream to the sauce and stir well. The cream will instantly transform the sauce into a velvety, rich consistency. If you prefer a slightly sweeter butter chicken, this is the time to add the sugar. Stir until it’s fully dissolved, and taste the sauce for seasoning. Add salt if needed.

If the sauce is too thin for your liking, you can use the “Sauté” function again to simmer the sauce for a few minutes, allowing it to thicken up. Stir occasionally to prevent it from burning.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Once the butter chicken is ready, it’s time to serve! Spoon the chicken and sauce over a bed of hot, fluffy rice or alongside warm naan for the ultimate experience. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro for a burst of color and freshness. The bright, herbal notes of cilantro complement the rich, creamy sauce perfectly.

Tips for the Best Instant Pot Butter Chicken

  1. Use Chicken Thighs for Tenderness: While chicken breast can be used in a pinch, chicken thighs are the best choice for butter chicken. They stay moist and tender even after cooking under pressure, and they absorb the spices beautifully.

  2. Adjust the Spice Level: The amount of chili powder you use can be adjusted to suit your taste. If you prefer a milder dish, reduce the chili powder or use a mild variety. If you like your butter chicken with more of a kick, increase the chili powder or even add a chopped green chili during the sautéing stage.

  3. Marinate for Maximum Flavor: The longer you marinate the chicken, the more flavorful it will be. If you have the time, let the chicken sit in the marinade for several hours or overnight.

  4. Add More Butter for Extra Richness: If you want a more decadent butter chicken, feel free to add a little more butter when making the sauce. You can’t go wrong with extra butter in this dish!

  5. Make it Ahead of Time: Butter chicken tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to meld. You can make it in advance and store it in the fridge for up to 3 days. Just reheat it on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

Conclusion

Instant Pot Butter Chicken is an easy way to enjoy this classic Indian dish in the comfort of your own home without spending hours in the kitchen. The Instant Pot cuts down on cooking time while still delivering a dish that is rich, creamy, and full of flavor. The tender chicken, fragrant spices, and velvety sauce make this a meal that everyone will love.

Serve it with rice, naan, or even roasted vegetables for a complete meal. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, meal prepping for the week, or just craving something indulgent, this Instant Pot Butter Chicken is sure to satisfy your taste buds and leave you coming back for more.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for an Indian-inspired feast, skip the takeout and try making this delicious butter chicken at home. Your Instant Pot will do all the work, and you’ll have a rich and creamy dish that tastes just as good as what you’d get at your favorite Indian restaurant. Happy cooking!
