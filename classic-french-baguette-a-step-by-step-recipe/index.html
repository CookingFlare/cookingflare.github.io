Classic French Baguette: A Step-by-Step Recipe


Welcome to our kitchen, where we’re about to embark on a journey to create the quintessential French bread - the classic French baguette. With its crispy golden crust and soft, airy interior, the baguette is a staple in French cuisine, and now you can bring a taste of France to your own table. So, roll up your sleeves, dust off your apron, and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

To create the perfect baguette, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 500g of bread flour
  • 10g of salt
  • 5g of sugar
  • 7g of instant yeast
  • 350ml of lukewarm water

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, and sugar. Mix well to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  2. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the instant yeast in lukewarm water. Let it sit for a few minutes until the yeast becomes active and frothy.

  3. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the activated yeast mixture. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, gradually incorporate the flour into the liquid. Continue mixing until a sticky dough forms.

  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead vigorously for about 10 minutes. This step is crucial for developing the gluten, which will give our baguette its characteristic chewiness.

  5. Once the dough becomes smooth and elastic, shape it into a ball and place it back into the mixing bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free area for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  6. After the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles that may have formed. Transfer the dough back onto the floured surface and divide it into three equal portions.

  7. Taking one portion at a time, flatten the dough into a rectangular shape using your hands. Starting from one end, tightly roll the dough, applying pressure with your fingertips as you go. Pinch the seam to seal it.

  8. Place the rolled dough onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Repeat the process with the remaining portions of dough, leaving enough space between each baguette to allow for expansion.

  9. Cover the baguettes with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let them rise for an additional 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).

  10. Just before baking, make shallow diagonal cuts along the top of each baguette with a sharp knife or razor blade. This step, known as scoring, allows the bread to expand and gives it that distinctive baguette appearance.

  11. Carefully transfer the baguettes into the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until they turn golden brown and emit a hollow sound when tapped on the bottom.

  12. Once baked, remove the baguettes from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack. It may be tempting to dive right in, but allowing the baguettes to cool completely will ensure a crisp crust and a light, airy interior.

Congratulations! You’ve successfully created your very own classic French baguettes. Now, all that’s left to do is slice, slather with butter or your favorite spread, and savor the taste of France in every bite.

Conclusion

Baking a classic French baguette may seem intimidating, but with this step-by-step recipe, you’ll have the confidence to tackle it head-on. From mixing the ingredients to scoring the dough, each step plays a crucial role in crafting the perfect baguette. So, why not gather your ingredients and give it a try? Your kitchen will soon be filled with the delightful aroma of freshly baked bread, and your taste buds will thank you for it. Bon appétit!
