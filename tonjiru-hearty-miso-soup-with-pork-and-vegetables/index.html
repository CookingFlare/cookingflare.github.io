Tonjiru: Hearty Miso Soup with Pork and Vegetables


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you’ve probably experienced the comforting warmth and umami flavors of miso soup. But have you tried Tonjiru? Tonjiru is a hearty miso soup that takes the classic miso soup to the next level by adding pork and a variety of vegetables. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making Tonjiru from scratch, and delve into the rich flavors and nutritional benefits of this beloved Japanese dish.

Ingredients:

  • 200g of thinly sliced pork belly
  • 3 tablespoons of miso paste (white or red, according to your preference)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium carrot, cut into thin slices
  • 1 medium potato, cut into small cubes
  • 1/2 block of konnyaku (konjac) or shirataki noodles, cut into small pieces
  • 4 cups of dashi stock (you can use bonito flakes or kombu to make the stock)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:

    • Thinly slice the pork belly and set it aside.
    • Cut the onion, carrot, and potato into the desired shapes and sizes.
    • If you’re using konnyaku or shirataki noodles, cut them into small pieces and rinse them thoroughly.

  2. Sauté the Pork:

    • In a pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
    • Add the sliced pork belly and cook until it’s no longer pink.

  3. Add the Vegetables:

    • Add the sliced onion, carrot, and potato to the pot with the pork. Sauté the vegetables until they start to soften.

  4. Pour in the Dashi Stock:

    • Once the vegetables have softened, pour in the dashi stock. Bring the soup to a gentle simmer.

  5. Season the Soup:

    • Add the miso paste, soy sauce, and sugar to the pot. Stir gently to dissolve the miso paste and distribute the flavors.

  6. Incorporate the Konnyaku or Shirataki:

    • Add the konnyaku or shirataki noodles to the soup. Let the soup simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Adjust the Seasoning:

    • Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.

  8. Finish and Serve:

    • Once the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded, turn off the heat.
    • Garnish the Tonjiru with chopped green onions before serving.

The Art of Tonjiru:

Tonjiru is not just a soup; it’s a reflection of Japanese culinary tradition and the essence of umami. The combination of savory miso, tender pork, and an assortment of vegetables creates a symphony of flavors that warms the soul. The addition of konnyaku or shirataki noodles provides a delightful textural element to the soup, making each spoonful a journey of taste and sensation.

Nutritional Benefits:

Beyond its incredible taste, Tonjiru offers a host of nutritional benefits. Miso paste is rich in essential minerals and probiotics, making it beneficial for gut health. The addition of pork provides a good source of protein, while the array of vegetables offers vitamins and fiber. This makes Tonjiru a nourishing and wholesome dish that’s perfect for any occasion.

Final Thoughts:

Tonjiru is a celebration of balance and harmony in Japanese cuisine. Its robust flavors and nourishing qualities make it an ideal comfort food that excites the palate and nurtures the body. Whether you’re seeking warmth on a chilly day or a taste of authentic Japanese flavors, Tonjiru is a dish that invites you to savor each spoonful and appreciate the art of culinary craftsmanship. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure to create your own bowl of delightful Tonjiru.
