Elevate Your Brunch with These Bubbly Champagne Cocktails


Who doesn’t love a good brunch? It’s the perfect time to gather with friends or family, indulge in delicious food, and sip on a refreshing beverage. And what better way to take your brunch to the next level than by adding a touch of bubbly champagne to your cocktails? In this post, we’ll explore some mouthwatering champagne cocktail recipes that are sure to impress your guests and elevate your brunch experience.

1. Classic Mimosa

Let’s start with a classic that needs no introduction – the Mimosa. This simple yet elegant cocktail is a staple at brunches around the world. To make a Mimosa, you’ll need:

  • Champagne
  • Orange juice

To prepare the Mimosa, fill a champagne flute halfway with chilled champagne. Then, top it off with an equal amount of freshly squeezed orange juice. Give it a gentle stir, and voila! You have a delightful Mimosa ready to be enjoyed.

2. Bellini

Originating from Venice, Italy, the Bellini is a fruity and vibrant cocktail that will transport you to the sunny shores of the Mediterranean. For this cocktail, you’ll need:

  • Champagne
  • Peach puree

To make a Bellini, start by adding a spoonful of peach puree to a champagne flute. Fill the rest of the glass with chilled champagne, and gently stir to combine the flavors. Sip on this refreshing cocktail and imagine yourself lounging by the canals of Venice.

3. French 75

If you’re looking for a cocktail with a little more kick, the French 75 is the perfect choice. This classic gin-based cocktail gets its name from the powerful French 75mm field gun used during World War I. To make a French 75, gather the following ingredients:

  • Champagne
  • Gin
  • Fresh lemon juice
  • Simple syrup

In a shaker filled with ice, combine 2 ounces of gin, 1 ounce of fresh lemon juice, and half an ounce of simple syrup. Shake well, then strain the mixture into a champagne flute. Top it off with chilled champagne, and garnish with a lemon twist. The French 75 is a sophisticated cocktail that adds a touch of elegance to any brunch.

4. Raspberry Champagne Cocktail

For those with a sweet tooth, the Raspberry Champagne Cocktail is a must-try. The combination of tart raspberries and bubbly champagne creates a delightful flavor profile that will leave you craving more. To make this cocktail, you’ll need:

  • Champagne
  • Raspberry liqueur
  • Fresh raspberries

Start by adding a spoonful of raspberry liqueur to a champagne flute. Drop a few fresh raspberries into the glass, and then carefully fill it with chilled champagne. The vibrant red color of the raspberries adds a pop of visual appeal to this already delicious cocktail.

5. Elderflower Fizz

Last but not least, we have the Elderflower Fizz – a light and floral cocktail that is perfect for a brunch in spring or summer. To make this refreshing drink, gather the following ingredients:

  • Champagne
  • Elderflower liqueur
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Lime juice

In a glass, muddle a few fresh mint leaves with a splash of lime juice. Add a spoonful of elderflower liqueur and gently stir to combine the flavors. Fill the glass with chilled champagne and garnish with a sprig of mint. The Elderflower Fizz is a fantastic way to add a touch of elegance and sophistication to your brunch.

As you can see, champagne cocktails can truly elevate your brunch experience. Whether you prefer a classic Mimosa or want to try something more adventurous like the French 75, these bubbly concoctions are sure to impress. So next time you’re hosting a brunch, don’t forget to add a touch of champagne to your cocktails and toast to good times and great company. Cheers!
