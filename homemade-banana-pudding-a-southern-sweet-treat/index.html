Homemade Banana Pudding: A Southern Sweet Treat


There’s nothing quite like the sweet, creamy goodness of homemade banana pudding. This classic Southern dessert is a crowd-pleaser that brings back memories of childhood and family gatherings. The combination of ripe bananas, luscious vanilla pudding, and layers of fluffy whipped cream and cookies creates a dessert that is simply irresistible. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make your own homemade banana pudding from scratch, step by step.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe bananas
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 box (11 ounces) vanilla wafers

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk and heavy cream. Heat over medium heat until it begins to simmer, then remove from heat and set aside.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, flour, and salt. Add the egg yolks and vanilla extract, and whisk until well combined.

  3. Slowly pour the milk mixture into the bowl with the sugar and egg mixture, whisking continuously to avoid any lumps.

  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  5. Remove from heat and let the pudding cool for a few minutes. While the pudding is cooling, slice the bananas into thin rounds.

  6. In a large glass dish, start assembling the banana pudding. Begin with a layer of vanilla wafers, followed by a layer of sliced bananas, and then a layer of pudding. Repeat this process until you run out of ingredients, ending with a layer of pudding on top.

  7. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the pudding to set.

  8. Just before serving, crush a few vanilla wafers and sprinkle them on top of the pudding for added crunch and texture.

  9. Serve chilled and enjoy the creamy, dreamy goodness of homemade banana pudding!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, you can add a layer of whipped cream on top of the pudding before serving. Simply whip some heavy cream with a little powdered sugar until stiff peaks form, then spread it over the pudding.

  • If you prefer a stronger banana flavor, you can add some mashed bananas to the pudding mixture before cooking it. This will give the pudding a more intense banana taste.

  • To make individual servings, layer the ingredients in small dessert cups or mason jars. This is a great option for parties or gatherings.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, you can experiment with different types of cookies or biscuits in place of the vanilla wafers. Graham crackers or shortbread cookies would also work well.

  • Don’t be afraid to get creative with garnishes! You can sprinkle some cocoa powder, chocolate shavings, or even crushed nuts on top of the pudding for added flavor and visual appeal.

Homemade banana pudding is a timeless dessert that never fails to impress. With its creamy texture, sweet banana flavor, and layers of cookies, it’s a true Southern classic. Whether you’re serving it at a family gathering or simply indulging in a sweet treat for yourself, this homemade banana pudding recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. Give it a try and discover the joy of this delightful Southern dessert!
