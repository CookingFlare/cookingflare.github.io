Farm-to-Table Quiche Recipe with Fresh Herbs and Cheese


Quiche, a classic French dish, is a staple in many households around the world. Its versatility and ease of preparation make it a perfect option for breakfast, lunch, or dinner. In this recipe, we’ll take the traditional quiche to the next level by incorporating fresh herbs and cheese, giving it a farm-to-table twist.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water

For the filling:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

Make the Crust

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Prepare the Filling

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, cheddar cheese, Parmesan cheese, salt, and black pepper until well combined. Stir in the chopped fresh herbs.

Assemble and Bake the Quiche

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges and press the dough into the corners of the pan.

Pour the filling mixture into the crust and smooth the top. Dot the top with the melted butter.

Bake the quiche for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is set. Remove from the oven and let cool for 10 minutes.

Serve and Enjoy

Remove the quiche from the tart pan and transfer it to a serving plate. Slice and serve warm, garnished with additional fresh herbs if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Use any combination of fresh herbs you like or have on hand. Basil, thyme, and tarragon are all great options.
  • Add some diced ham, bacon, or cooked vegetables to the filling for added flavor and texture.
  • For a gluten-free crust, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend and add xanthan gum if necessary.
  • Quiche is a great make-ahead dish. Prepare the filling and crust separately, then assemble and bake the quiche just before serving.

Conclusion

This farm-to-table quiche recipe is a delicious and satisfying way to enjoy the freshness of the season. The combination of fresh herbs and cheese adds a bright and tangy flavor to the classic quiche. Whether you’re serving it for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, this quiche is sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So go ahead, get creative, and make this quiche your own!
