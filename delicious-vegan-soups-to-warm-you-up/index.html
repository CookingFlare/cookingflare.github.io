Delicious Vegan Soups to Warm You Up


When the temperatures drop and the days get shorter, there’s nothing more comforting than a hot bowl of soup. Vegan soups, packed with rich flavors, wholesome vegetables, and satisfying textures, are the perfect way to warm up during chilly days. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to try something new, these plant-based soups will keep you cozy while nourishing your body.

From creamy, smooth purees to hearty, chunky stews, the variety of vegan soups is endless. Each recipe is not only delicious but also easy to make, bringing together wholesome ingredients that are good for you and the planet. Here are some of the best vegan soups to enjoy when you need a comforting, warm meal.

1. Classic Vegan Tomato Soup

Tomato soup is a beloved comfort food, and when made vegan, it becomes an even healthier, plant-based option that’s just as satisfying. The natural sweetness of tomatoes combined with a touch of garlic, onion, and herbs creates a flavor-packed soup. To make this soup extra creamy without dairy, blend in some coconut milk or cashews.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 2 cans (14 oz) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk or cashew cream (optional for creaminess)
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the garlic and carrot, cooking for an additional 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  4. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, you can transfer the soup to a blender in batches.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk or cashew cream for extra creaminess if desired. Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves if desired.

This vegan tomato soup is perfect with a slice of toasted bread or a vegan grilled cheese sandwich for a comforting meal.

2. Creamy Vegan Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash soup is a classic fall and winter favorite, offering a velvety texture and a naturally sweet, rich flavor. This vegan version skips the dairy, but still maintains the smooth, creamy consistency we all crave in a comforting soup. You can also add a touch of coconut milk for extra creaminess.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk (optional)
  • Fresh parsley or thyme for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the cubed butternut squash on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the squash is tender and caramelized.
  2. While the squash roasts, heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Once the squash is roasted, add it to the pot along with the vegetable broth, ginger, turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Use an immersion blender or transfer the soup to a blender to puree until smooth and creamy.
  5. Stir in the coconut milk for extra richness, if desired, and adjust the seasoning.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley or thyme.

This soup is not only delicious, but it’s also packed with vitamins and antioxidants, making it a healthy and hearty option for any meal.

3. Vegan Lentil Soup

Lentils are a powerhouse of plant-based protein, and in this hearty soup, they create a thick, flavorful base. Combined with vegetables and aromatic herbs, this vegan lentil soup is satisfying enough to be a meal on its own.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups spinach or kale, chopped (optional)
  • Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add the lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a boil.
  3. Lower the heat and let the soup simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  4. If using, stir in the spinach or kale during the last few minutes of cooking, allowing it to wilt.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Serve the soup hot with a squeeze of lemon juice for extra brightness.

This vegan lentil soup is a perfect option for a filling, protein-rich meal that will keep you full for hours.

4. Spicy Vegan Black Bean Soup

If you love a bit of spice, this vegan black bean soup will hit the spot. The combination of black beans, tomatoes, and spices creates a hearty, flavorful soup with a kick. It’s a great way to enjoy a satisfying, protein-packed meal with a bit of heat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeds removed and chopped (optional for extra heat)
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and jalapeño (if using). Cook until the onion is softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, cumin, chili powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil.
  3. Lower the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend together.
  4. Use an immersion blender to blend part of the soup for a thicker consistency, or leave it chunky if you prefer.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice.

This spicy black bean soup is a crowd-pleaser, especially for those who love bold, vibrant flavors.

5. Vegan Potato Leek Soup

Potato leek soup is a classic French dish, but this vegan version keeps all the creamy comfort without any dairy. The combination of earthy leeks and starchy potatoes creates a smooth, satisfying soup that’s perfect for a cozy evening.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 leeks, cleaned and chopped
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk or almond milk (optional)
  • Fresh chives for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the leeks, onion, and garlic, and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the potatoes, vegetable broth, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.
  3. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth, or blend it in batches using a regular blender.
  4. Stir in the coconut or almond milk for extra creaminess, if desired.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  6. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh chives.

This vegan potato leek soup is a comforting, creamy dish that is simple yet full of flavor.

6. Vegan Miso Soup with Tofu

Miso soup is a classic Japanese dish that’s light yet packed with umami flavor. This vegan version uses tofu and seaweed to add texture, while the miso paste brings depth and savory goodness. It’s the perfect light soup for a quick meal or as a starter.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste (white or red)
  • 1 block firm tofu, cubed
  • 1/4 cup dried wakame seaweed
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Soy sauce, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium pot, bring the vegetable broth to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk the miso paste with a small amount of warm broth until smooth.
  3. Add the miso mixture to the pot, stirring to combine.
  4. Add the tofu cubes, wakame seaweed, and green onions. Simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine and the seaweed to rehydrate.
  5. Stir in the sesame oil and soy sauce to taste.
  6. Serve the soup hot.

This vegan miso soup is light but full of flavor, making it a great option for a light meal or a refreshing addition to any vegan menu.

Whether you’re in the mood for something creamy, spicy, or hearty, these vegan soups are sure to warm you up and satisfy your taste buds. Easy to make and full of wholesome ingredients, these soups offer a variety of flavors and textures to suit any palate. Enjoy them with a side of crusty bread or a fresh salad for a complete and nutritious meal.

So, when the cold weather hits, don’t hesitate to whip up a batch of these comforting soups to keep you cozy all season long. Happy cooking!
