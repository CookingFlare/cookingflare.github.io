Sous Vide Sweet Corn Soup with Basil


Soup is a timeless comfort food, offering warmth and satisfaction with every spoonful. But have you ever tried making it with sous vide? Sous vide is a cooking technique that involves vacuum-sealing ingredients and cooking them in a precisely controlled water bath, ensuring that the food is cooked evenly and retains all of its natural flavors. When combined with the sweet, rich taste of fresh corn and the aromatic freshness of basil, sous vide cooking elevates this soup to a new level.

In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of making a delicious sous vide sweet corn soup with basil. This recipe is easy to follow, yielding a creamy, flavorful, and slightly sweet soup that’s perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a light starter for a fancy dinner or a comforting meal on a cold day.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this sous vide sweet corn soup, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large ears of fresh corn, husked and kernels removed (or about 4 cups of frozen corn if fresh isn’t available)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced (this helps to thicken the soup and create a creamy texture)
  • 2 cups vegetable stock (or chicken stock for a non-vegetarian version)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter (for sautéing)
  • 1 cup heavy cream (for richness)
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves (about 1 large handful)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: A squeeze of fresh lemon juice or a pinch of cayenne pepper for extra flavor

Preparing Your Ingredients

The first step in making sous vide sweet corn soup with basil is to prepare your ingredients. Start by husking your corn and removing the kernels. You can use a sharp knife to cut the kernels off the cob, but be careful not to waste any of that delicious corn juice. If fresh corn is not available, you can use frozen corn—just make sure it’s thawed before cooking.

Next, chop your onion and garlic. These two ingredients will be sautéed in oil or butter to bring out their natural sweetness and depth of flavor. Peel and dice the potato into small cubes. The potato acts as a thickening agent in this soup, providing a velvety, creamy texture without the need for added flour or cornstarch.

Once your ingredients are prepped, you’re ready to start cooking!

Sous Vide Cooking Method: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Set Up the Sous Vide Water Bath

Fill your sous vide container with water and set the temperature to 185°F (85°C). This is the perfect temperature for cooking the sweet corn soup, ensuring the corn cooks thoroughly while preserving its natural sweetness and texture. If you don’t have a sous vide container, you can use any large pot or vessel and attach an immersion circulator to control the water temperature.

Step 2: Prepare the Ingredients for Sous Vide

Place the fresh corn kernels, chopped onion, garlic, and diced potato in a large vacuum-seal bag. If you prefer, you can divide the ingredients between two smaller bags—just make sure not to overfill the bags so that the ingredients are evenly distributed and can cook thoroughly.

Add a tablespoon of olive oil or butter to the bag. This will enhance the flavor and ensure a rich, silky soup. You can also add a pinch of salt and pepper for seasoning, but keep in mind that you’ll be seasoning the soup again later, so go light on the initial salt.

Seal the bags using a vacuum sealer, making sure to remove as much air as possible. Proper sealing ensures that the flavors meld together perfectly during cooking, and it helps to keep the ingredients from floating to the top during the sous vide process.

Step 3: Sous Vide Cooking

Submerge the vacuum-sealed bag(s) into the preheated water bath. Let the ingredients cook for about 1 to 1.5 hours. The slow and steady cooking process ensures that each kernel of corn is cooked to perfection, and the flavors are able to infuse and intensify.

While the corn and vegetables are cooking, you can take a moment to prepare the basil. Wash and pat dry the fresh basil leaves. You’ll use these at the end to finish the soup and add a burst of fresh flavor.

Step 4: Finalizing the Soup

After the sous vide cooking time has passed, carefully remove the bags from the water bath. Open the bag and transfer the cooked ingredients into a blender or food processor. Be cautious, as the contents will be very hot. Blend until the mixture reaches a smooth, creamy consistency. If you prefer a thicker texture, you can blend the soup in batches, adding more stock to reach your desired consistency.

Next, pour the blended soup back into a pot on the stove over low heat. Slowly stir in the heavy cream to add richness and creaminess to the soup. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. If you like a slightly tangy note, a squeeze of fresh lemon juice can brighten up the soup and balance the sweetness of the corn.

Step 5: Adding the Fresh Basil

Once the soup has reached the perfect texture and flavor, it’s time to add the fresh basil. Stir in the basil leaves, allowing them to wilt and infuse the soup with their vibrant, aromatic flavor. If you want a more intense basil flavor, you can blend the basil into the soup as well.

If desired, you can garnish the soup with additional fresh basil leaves, a drizzle of cream, or a sprinkle of roasted corn kernels for texture and color. The final touch can also include a pinch of cayenne pepper for a subtle heat or a dash of smoked paprika for a smoky twist.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy!

Your sous vide sweet corn soup with basil is now ready to serve! Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with your choice of toppings. This soup pairs wonderfully with crusty bread or a light salad for a complete meal. Whether you’re enjoying it on a chilly evening or serving it at a gathering, this soup is sure to impress with its rich flavor and velvety texture.

Why Sous Vide Cooking Makes a Difference

Sous vide cooking is a game-changer when it comes to making soups like this one. The precision of sous vide ensures that each ingredient is cooked to perfection, allowing the corn to retain its sweetness and the vegetables to soften evenly. By cooking everything in a sealed bag, the natural flavors of the ingredients are concentrated, and nothing escapes into the air or water. The result is a soup that’s bursting with vibrant, fresh taste.

Additionally, sous vide cooking is a hands-off process, which means you can set it and forget it while the magic happens. There’s no need to constantly stir the soup or worry about overcooking. Once the cooking time is done, simply blend the ingredients together for a smooth, consistent texture that’s hard to achieve with traditional stovetop methods.

Variations and Tips

While this recipe is a great starting point, there are many ways you can customize your sous vide sweet corn soup with basil to suit your taste:

  • Add Spices: Experiment with adding other herbs and spices like thyme, rosemary, or bay leaves during the sous vide process to enhance the depth of flavor.
  • Make it Spicy: Add a small amount of fresh chili pepper or cayenne to the soup for a little heat. This pairs nicely with the sweetness of the corn.
  • Make it Vegan: For a dairy-free version, simply replace the heavy cream with coconut milk or a plant-based cream. The coconut milk will add a lovely richness to the soup.
  • Use Different Garnishes: Top the soup with crumbled feta, a sprinkle of toasted sunflower seeds, or a drizzle of truffle oil for a more gourmet touch.

The Final Word

Sous vide sweet corn soup with basil is a truly delightful dish that combines the natural sweetness of corn with the freshness of basil, all brought together in a velvety, creamy base. Using the sous vide method takes this soup to the next level, ensuring that each bite is full of flavor and smooth texture. Whether you’re a seasoned sous vide pro or a newcomer to this innovative cooking technique, this recipe will quickly become a favorite in your kitchen.

Try it for yourself, and enjoy the delicious, comforting taste of this easy-to-make, yet impressively elegant soup.
