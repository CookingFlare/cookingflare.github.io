Succulent Honey Glazed Ham: A Crowd-Pleasing Easter Classic


Easter is a time for gathering with loved ones, celebrating new beginnings, and indulging in delicious food. And what better way to delight your guests than with a succulent honey glazed ham? This crowd-pleasing Easter classic is a showstopper that will have everyone coming back for seconds. In this recipe, I’ll share with you my secrets to achieving the perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors, along with some tips to make your glazed ham extra succulent. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 (10-pound) fully cooked spiral-cut ham
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Place the ham in a large roasting pan, cut side down.

  2. In a small saucepan, combine the honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, and ground cloves. Heat over medium-low heat until the mixture is smooth and well combined.

  3. Pour half of the glaze over the ham, making sure to brush it evenly all over the surface. Reserve the remaining glaze for later.

  4. Cover the roasting pan tightly with foil, ensuring that it’s sealed well. This will help keep the moisture in and prevent the ham from drying out.

  5. Place the ham in the preheated oven and bake for about 10 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C). Remember to baste the ham with the drippings every 30 minutes or so to keep it moist and flavorful.

  6. About 15 minutes before the ham is done, remove the foil and brush the remaining glaze over the surface. Increase the oven temperature to 400°F (204°C) and continue baking uncovered until the glaze is caramelized and sticky. This will give the ham that irresistible golden-brown color.

  7. Once the ham is cooked to perfection, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 15 minutes before carving. This will allow the juices to redistribute and ensure a tender, juicy result.

  8. Serve the succulent honey glazed ham as the centerpiece of your Easter feast. The sweet and tangy glaze will complement the rich and savory flavors of the ham, creating a mouthwatering combination that will have your guests raving.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing the right ham: Look for a fully cooked spiral-cut ham for convenience and ease. The spiral cuts not only make it easier to carve, but they also help the glaze penetrate the meat, resulting in a more flavorful ham.

  • Basting is key: Regularly basting the ham with the drippings and glaze throughout the cooking process is essential to keep it moist and flavorful. Don’t skip this step!

  • Get creative with leftovers: If you have any leftovers, don’t let them go to waste. Use the succulent honey glazed ham in sandwiches, salads, or even omelets for a tasty post-Easter treat.

There you have it, a foolproof recipe for a succulent honey glazed ham that will be the star of your Easter table. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or an intimate family brunch, this classic dish is sure to impress. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the smiles of satisfaction from your loved ones. Happy Easter!
