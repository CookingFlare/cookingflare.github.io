A Taste of the Coast: Provençal Seafood Pasta


If you’re looking for a taste of the Mediterranean coast right in your own kitchen, this Provençal seafood pasta recipe is just what you need. Bursting with the flavors of the sea and the vibrant herbs of the Provençal region, this dish is a celebration of fresh seafood and aromatic ingredients. Let’s dive into the world of Provençal cuisine and create a mouthwatering seafood pasta that will transport you to the sunny shores of the French Riviera.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of linguine or spaghetti
  • 1 pound of mixed seafood (shrimp, scallops, mussels, and/or clams)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup of pitted Kalamata olives, halved
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh basil, chiffonade
  • 1 teaspoon of Herbes de Provence
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for a hint of heat)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Seafood

Start by preparing the seafood. If you’re using shellfish, make sure to scrub the shells thoroughly and remove any beards from the mussels. Pat the seafood dry with paper towels and season lightly with salt and pepper.

Step 2: Cook the Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a cup of the pasta water, and set it aside.

Step 3: Sauté the Aromatics

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped shallot, and sauté for a minute until fragrant. Be careful not to let the garlic brown.

Step 4: Add the Seafood and Tomatoes

Increase the heat to medium-high and add the mixed seafood to the skillet. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the seafood is just cooked through. Add the halved cherry tomatoes and Kalamata olives to the skillet, and sauté for another minute to let the flavors meld.

Step 5: Deglaze with White Wine

Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for a minute, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet. This step will infuse the sauce with the delicate flavors of the wine.

Step 6: Bring It All Together

Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the seafood and tomatoes. Toss everything together, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce. The starchy pasta water will help create a silky, emulsified sauce that clings to the pasta.

Step 7: Finish with Herbs and Seasoning

Sprinkle in the chopped parsley, chiffonade of basil, and Herbes de Provence. Toss everything one last time to distribute the herbs evenly throughout the dish. Taste the pasta and adjust the seasoning with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if you like a bit of heat.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Divide the Provençal seafood pasta among serving plates or a large platter. Garnish with additional fresh herbs and a drizzle of olive oil if desired. Serve this delightful dish with a crisp glass of white wine and some crusty bread to soak up the flavorful juices.

The Taste of Provençal Coast

With each bite of this Provençal seafood pasta, you’ll experience the briny essence of the sea, the robust flavors of garlic and herbs, and the sweetness of ripe cherry tomatoes. The Kalamata olives add a touch of saltiness, while the white wine brings a bright acidity that ties everything together. It’s a dish that embodies the sun-drenched flavors of the Mediterranean coast and invites you to savor the simple pleasures of life.

Final Thoughts

This Provençal seafood pasta is a delightful representation of the coastal cuisine that has captivated food lovers for generations. The recipe’s simplicity allows the quality of the ingredients to shine, making it a perfect choice for a special dinner or a relaxed weekend meal. So, channel your inner Provençal chef, gather the freshest seafood and herbs you can find, and embark on a culinary journey to the sun-soaked shores of the French coast right from your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
