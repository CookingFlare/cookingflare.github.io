Crunchy Coconut Shrimp with Sweet Chili Dipping Sauce


Looking to add a little crunch to your seafood cravings? Look no further than this delicious recipe for Crunchy Coconut Shrimp with Sweet Chili Dipping Sauce. These golden, crispy shrimp are coated in a flavorful mixture of shredded coconut and panko breadcrumbs, resulting in a satisfyingly crunchy texture. And the accompanying sweet chili dipping sauce adds just the right amount of heat and tang to complement the shrimp perfectly. Whether you’re hosting a party or simply treating yourself to a special meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

For the shrimp:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the sweet chili dipping sauce:

  • 1/2 cup sweet chili sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey

Instructions

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the shredded coconut, panko breadcrumbs, salt, and black pepper. Mix well.

  2. Set up a breading station with three bowls - one with the flour, one with the beaten eggs, and one with the coconut-panko mixture.

  3. Take a shrimp and dip it into the flour, making sure it’s coated evenly. Shake off any excess flour.

  4. Next, dip the shrimp into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess egg to drip off.

  5. Finally, coat the shrimp in the coconut-panko mixture, pressing gently to ensure the coating adheres well. Repeat the process with the remaining shrimp.

  6. Place the coated shrimp on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and chill for about 15 minutes. This will help the coating set and stay intact during frying.

  7. In a deep pot or skillet, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (175°C). It’s important to have enough oil to fully submerge the shrimp.

  8. Carefully add the chilled shrimp to the hot oil, working in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until they turn golden brown and crispy.

  9. Remove the shrimp from the oil using a slotted spoon or tongs and transfer them to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

  10. While the shrimp are draining, prepare the sweet chili dipping sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together the sweet chili sauce, lime juice, soy sauce, and honey until well combined.

  11. Serve the Crunchy Coconut Shrimp with the Sweet Chili Dipping Sauce on the side, and watch as your guests dig in with delight!

Tips and Tricks

  • To make peeling and deveining the shrimp easier, leave the tails intact. This will give you something to hold onto while peeling.

  • For an extra crispy coating, you can double-dip the shrimp by repeating steps 4 and 5 before chilling and frying.

  • If you prefer a spicier dipping sauce, you can add a teaspoon of sriracha or red pepper flakes to the sweet chili sauce mixture.

  • To keep the shrimp warm and crispy, you can place them on a wire rack set over a baking sheet in a low-temperature oven (around 200°F or 93°C) while you fry the remaining batches.

Final Thoughts

Crunchy Coconut Shrimp with Sweet Chili Dipping Sauce is a crowd-pleasing dish that brings together the flavors of the tropics with a hint of heat. The combination of the crispy coconut coating and the tangy sweetness of the dipping sauce is simply irresistible. Whether you’re enjoying these shrimp as an appetizer, main course, or party snack, they are sure to be a hit. So give this recipe a try and get ready to savor every crunchy bite!
