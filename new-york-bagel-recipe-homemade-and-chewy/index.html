New York Bagel Recipe: Homemade and Chewy


Bagels are more than just a food; they’re an experience. From their rich history to the distinct chewy texture, New York-style bagels are iconic and beloved around the world. If you’ve ever tasted an authentic bagel in the heart of New York City, you know there’s something special about that crispy, golden crust and the soft, airy inside. But did you know you can recreate this delicious treat in the comfort of your own kitchen?

In this post, we’ll guide you through every step of making homemade New York-style bagels that are perfectly chewy, with a shiny, golden exterior and a soft, airy interior. Whether you’re a beginner or have experience baking bread, this recipe is accessible and guarantees amazing results.

What Makes New York Bagels Special?

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a moment to understand what makes New York bagels stand out. The key differences lie in the dough’s ingredients, the boiling process, and the baking method.

1. The Dough: Chewy and Soft

The secret to a great bagel lies in the dough. New York bagels are made with high-gluten bread flour, which helps create the chewy texture. Additionally, the dough is allowed to rise slowly, which gives it time to develop flavor and structure. This slow fermentation process contributes to the iconic New York bagel taste that is slightly tangy and robust.

2. The Boiling Step: Important for the Crust

Before bagels are baked, they’re boiled in a water bath. This is what gives bagels their distinctive shiny crust. The water is typically mixed with a bit of malt syrup or honey to help with the browning process, ensuring that each bagel gets a perfectly crisp, golden crust once it’s baked.

3. The Baking: High Heat for Perfect Texture

To get that perfect bagel texture, a high oven temperature is key. Bagels are traditionally baked at temperatures between 450°F (232°C) and 500°F (260°C), ensuring a quick and even bake that results in a crisp outer layer and a soft, chewy interior.

Ingredients for Homemade New York Bagels

For a batch of about 8 bagels, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups (480g) high-gluten bread flour (or all-purpose flour, but high-gluten gives a better chew)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1 ½ teaspoons sugar
  • 1 ½ cups (360ml) warm water (about 110°F / 45°C)
  • 1 tablespoon malt syrup (optional, but it adds sweetness and a golden color to the crust)

For Boiling:

  • 2 quarts (2 liters) of water
  • 1 tablespoon malt syrup or honey (optional)

For the Topping (optional):

  • Sesame seeds
  • Poppy seeds
  • Onion flakes
  • Garlic flakes

Step-by-Step Instructions for New York Bagels

Making homemade bagels is a bit of a labor of love, but the results are so worth it. Let’s break down each step of the process.

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by activating your yeast in warm water. Pour the warm water into a bowl, sprinkle the yeast and sugar over the top, and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until the mixture becomes foamy. This means the yeast is active and ready to go. If the mixture doesn’t foam, your yeast might be expired, and you’ll need to try with fresh yeast.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Create a small well in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the activated yeast mixture. Stir the ingredients together until the dough begins to come together. You may need to use your hands to knead it into a rough ball.

Once you have a shaggy dough, turn it out onto a floured surface and begin kneading for about 8-10 minutes, until the dough is smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky but not sticky. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour, a tablespoon at a time, but be careful not to add too much.

Step 3: First Rise

Form the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it doubles in size. This step is crucial for developing the flavor and texture of the bagels.

Step 4: Shape the Bagels

Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and divide it into 8 equal pieces. Roll each piece of dough into a ball, then use your finger to create a hole in the center of each ball. Stretch the hole out to about 2-3 inches wide to form the classic bagel shape.

If you prefer a different shape, you can also use a bagel mold or form the dough into mini loaves, but the traditional round shape is what we’re after here.

Step 5: Second Rise

Place the shaped bagels onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, cover them with a damp towel, and allow them to rise for another 30 minutes. This will ensure that the bagels stay light and airy during baking.

Step 6: Boil the Bagels

While the bagels are rising, preheat your oven to 475°F (246°C) and bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add in the malt syrup or honey, if using. When the water is boiling, carefully drop in a few bagels at a time. Let them boil for about 1-2 minutes on each side. The bagels should puff up and float to the top as they cook. Use a slotted spoon to remove them from the water and transfer them back to the baking sheet.

Step 7: Add Toppings

At this point, you can get creative with toppings. If you want sesame, poppy seeds, or onion flakes on your bagels, gently press the top of each bagel into the toppings before baking.

Step 8: Bake the Bagels

Transfer the boiled bagels to your preheated oven and bake them for 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown and the crust is crisp. For an extra-crispy bagel, you can leave them in for a couple more minutes. Keep an eye on them so they don’t burn.

Step 9: Cool and Enjoy

Once your bagels are baked to perfection, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool on a wire rack for a few minutes before slicing and serving. The bagels will be perfectly chewy on the inside with that desirable crispy, golden exterior.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Bagels

  • Use High-Gluten Flour: While you can substitute all-purpose flour, high-gluten bread flour is what gives New York-style bagels their signature chewy texture. If you can’t find high-gluten flour, bread flour is the next best option.

  • Boiling Matters: Don’t skip the boiling step! Boiling helps the bagels develop their signature shiny, chewy crust. You can also add a bit of baking soda to the boiling water to further enhance the crust’s texture.

  • Experiment with Toppings: Classic New York bagels are often topped with sesame or poppy seeds, but you can get creative with your toppings. Try everything from garlic flakes and onion to everything bagel seasoning or even just a simple sprinkle of sea salt.

  • Storage: Bagels are best enjoyed fresh, but you can store them in an airtight container for up to 2-3 days. To keep them fresh longer, slice them and freeze the bagels. When you’re ready to eat them, just toast them for a few minutes, and they’ll taste almost as fresh as the day they were baked.

The Perfect Pairings for Your Bagels

Homemade bagels are fantastic on their own, but they’re also perfect when paired with a variety of spreads and toppings. Here are a few ideas for what you can top your bagels with:

  • Cream Cheese: A classic pairing. You can’t go wrong with a generous spread of plain or flavored cream cheese. Try adding smoked salmon, capers, and fresh dill for a traditional bagel experience.

  • Avocado: For a more modern twist, mash up some avocado and add a sprinkle of salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes for a simple and healthy topping.

  • Peanut Butter and Jelly: For a sweet and savory breakfast, go with a spread of peanut butter and your favorite jam.

  • Egg and Bacon: Create a hearty breakfast sandwich by adding a fried egg and crispy bacon to your bagel.

Conclusion

There’s no denying that New York-style bagels are a classic. They’re chewy, flavorful, and incredibly satisfying. With this homemade recipe, you can now recreate the magic of a New York bagel in your own kitchen. Although the process takes a little time and patience, the results are well worth it. With the right ingredients and techniques, you’ll be able to enjoy fresh, chewy, and golden bagels that rival those found in any bakery.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start baking! The world of homemade bagels awaits you.
