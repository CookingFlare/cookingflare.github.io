Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Truffles: A No-Bake Vegan Treat


If you’re a fan of cookie dough and chocolate chip cookies, then you’re in for a treat! Today, I’m going to share with you a delicious and easy recipe for Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Truffles. These truffles are not only vegan but also require no baking, making them a convenient and indulgent dessert option. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup of vegan butter, softened
  • ¾ cup of brown sugar
  • ¼ cup of plant-based milk (such as almond or soy milk)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of vegan chocolate chips
  • 1 cup of dairy-free chocolate, melted (for coating)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, cream together the softened vegan butter and brown sugar until well combined and fluffy.

  2. Add the plant-based milk and vanilla extract to the butter mixture, and mix until smooth.

  3. Gradually add the all-purpose flour and salt to the mixture, stirring until a soft cookie dough forms.

  4. Fold in the vegan chocolate chips, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.

  5. Using a small cookie scoop or a tablespoon, portion out the cookie dough and roll it into small balls. Place the dough balls onto a parchment-lined baking sheet.

  6. Once all the dough has been rolled into balls, place the baking sheet in the freezer for about 30 minutes to firm up the truffles.

  7. While the truffles are chilling, melt the dairy-free chocolate in a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second intervals, stirring in between, until smooth and melted.

  8. Remove the truffles from the freezer and dip each one into the melted chocolate, ensuring they are fully coated. Use a fork or a dipping tool to remove any excess chocolate, allowing it to drip back into the bowl.

  9. Place the coated truffles back onto the parchment-lined baking sheet and repeat the process until all the truffles are coated.

  10. Once all the truffles are coated, return them to the refrigerator to allow the chocolate to set. This will take about 1 hour.

  11. Once the chocolate has set, your Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Truffles are ready to be enjoyed! Serve them chilled and store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Tips and Variations

  • For a fun twist, you can roll the truffles in crushed nuts, shredded coconut, or cocoa powder before chilling them.
  • If you prefer a sweeter truffle, you can use semisweet or dark chocolate chips instead of vegan chocolate chips in the cookie dough.
  • Feel free to get creative with the shape of your truffles. You can make them into squares, hearts, or any other shape you desire.
  • If you’re not a fan of chocolate, you can omit the chocolate coating and enjoy the cookie dough truffles as they are.
  • These truffles make a great homemade gift for birthdays, holidays, or special occasions. Just package them in a cute box or jar and tie it with a ribbon.

So, there you have it—a delightful recipe for Chocolate Chip Cookie Dough Truffles that are vegan, no-bake, and absolutely scrumptious. Whether you’re a vegan or simply looking for a delectable treat, these truffles are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth. Give them a try and let me know how they turn out. Happy cooking!
