Irresistible Nut-Free Cheesecake Recipe


Are you a cheesecake lover but have a nut allergy? Fear not! This irresistible nut-free cheesecake recipe is here to satisfy your cravings without compromising on flavor or texture. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special occasion, or simply treating yourself, this cheesecake is sure to impress. Let’s dive into the delightful world of nut-free cheesecake baking!

Ingredients

Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted

Filling:

  • 24 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour

Topping:

  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

Crust:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly moistened.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, using the back of a spoon to create an even layer.
  4. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and let it cool while preparing the filling.

Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy.
  2. Add the vanilla extract and beat until well combined.
  3. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  4. Add the sour cream and flour, and beat until just combined, being careful not to overmix.
  5. Pour the filling over the cooled crust and spread it into an even layer.

Baking:

  1. Place the cheesecake in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly.
  2. Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool for 10 minutes while preparing the topping.

Topping:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the sour cream, sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  2. Gently spread the topping over the warm cheesecake, being careful not to disturb the surface.
  3. Return the cheesecake to the oven and bake for an additional 5 minutes.

Chilling:

  1. Once baked, remove the cheesecake from the oven and run a knife around the edge of the pan to loosen the cheesecake.
  2. Allow the cheesecake to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to set.

Serving

Before serving, remove the cheesecake from the springform pan and place it on a serving platter. Slice and serve chilled for the perfect nut-free cheesecake experience.

Conclusion

This nut-free cheesecake is a testament to the fact that dietary restrictions should never stand in the way of indulging in delightful desserts. With its creamy texture, rich flavor, and buttery graham cracker crust, this cheesecake is a crowd-pleaser for all occasions. Whether you have a nut allergy or not, this recipe is a must-try for anyone who appreciates a good cheesecake. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and let’s bake up a slice of pure cheesecake bliss!
