The Boulevardier 2.0: A Modern Twist on a Classic Whiskey Cocktail


The Boulevardier, a whiskey-based cocktail that’s often overshadowed by its more popular cousin, the Negroni. Yet, this elegant drink has a rich history and a unique flavor profile that’s worth exploring. In this post, we’ll take a closer look at the classic Boulevardier recipe and then give it a modern twist to make it more appealing to today’s palate.

The Classic Boulevardier Recipe

The Boulevardier is a variation of the Negroni, substituting sweet vermouth with Campari instead of gin. The recipe is simple:

  • 1 1/2 oz whiskey (bourbon or rye work well)
  • 1/2 oz Campari
  • 1/2 oz sweet vermouth
  • Orange peel (optional, for garnish)

Combine the whiskey, Campari, and sweet vermouth in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir for about 30 seconds to chill and dilute the ingredients. Strain into a chilled coupe or cocktail glass and garnish with an orange peel, if desired.

The Problem with the Classic Recipe

While the Boulevardier is a well-balanced drink, it can be a bit too bitter and sweet for some palates. The Campari, in particular, can overpower the other flavors, making it difficult to appreciate the whiskey and vermouth. Additionally, the drink can be too strong and overwhelming, especially for those who prefer a more subtle cocktail.

Introducing the Boulevardier 2.0

To modernize the Boulevardier, we can make a few tweaks to the recipe. Here’s a revised version that incorporates some new ingredients and techniques:

  • 1 1/2 oz bourbon
  • 1/2 oz Campari
  • 1/2 oz Amaro Nonino
  • 1/2 oz honey syrup (1:1 honey and water, dissolved)
  • 2 dashes of Angostura bitters
  • Orange peel (optional, for garnish)

The key changes in this recipe are the addition of Amaro Nonino and honey syrup. Amaro Nonino is a sweet and herbal Italian liqueur that complements the bourbon and Campari nicely, while the honey syrup adds a touch of sweetness without making the drink too cloying. The Angostura bitters also help to balance out the flavors and add a subtle complexity to the drink.

How to Make the Boulevardier 2.0

To make the Boulevardier 2.0, combine the bourbon, Campari, Amaro Nonino, and honey syrup in a mixing glass filled with ice. Stir for about 30 seconds to chill and dilute the ingredients. Add the Angostura bitters and stir for another 10-15 seconds. Strain into a chilled coupe or cocktail glass and garnish with an orange peel, if desired.

Tasting Notes

The Boulevardier 2.0 is a more refined and complex drink than its classic counterpart. The bourbon provides a rich and smooth base, while the Campari and Amaro Nonino add a subtle bitterness and herbal flavor. The honey syrup and Angostura bitters help to balance out the sweetness and bitterness, making the drink more approachable and easy to drink. Overall, the Boulevardier 2.0 is a modern twist on a classic cocktail that’s sure to please even the most discerning palates.

Conclusion

The Boulevardier is a classic cocktail that deserves more attention and experimentation. By incorporating new ingredients and techniques, we can create a more modern and appealing version of this whiskey-based drink. The Boulevardier 2.0 is a great example of how to modernize a classic cocktail without sacrificing its original character. So next time you’re looking for a new cocktail to try, give the Boulevardier 2.0 a shot – you won’t be disappointed!
