Korean Soy-Glazed Salmon with Vegetables: A Flavorful and Healthy Delight


Are you tired of the same old boring salmon recipes? Look no further! In this recipe, we will take your taste buds on a journey to Korea with a deliciously flavorful and healthy twist on a classic dish. Get ready to impress your family and friends with this Korean Soy-Glazed Salmon with Vegetables recipe!

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean red pepper paste)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as broccoli, bell peppers, and carrots)
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, gochujang, and rice vinegar. This flavorful marinade will be the star of the dish!

  3. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper on both sides. Place the fillets on the prepared baking sheet and brush them generously with the marinade, reserving some for later use.

  4. In a separate bowl, toss the mixed vegetables with a drizzle of sesame oil and a pinch of salt. Arrange the vegetables around the salmon fillets on the baking sheet.

  5. Roast the salmon and vegetables in the preheated oven for about 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. The vegetables should be tender-crisp and lightly charred.

  6. While the salmon and vegetables are roasting, transfer the remaining marinade to a small saucepan and bring it to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 5 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly. This will be a delicious glaze for the salmon!

  7. Once the salmon is cooked, remove it from the oven and brush it with the glaze. Sprinkle the green onions and sesame seeds on top for added flavor and presentation.

  8. Serve the Korean Soy-Glazed Salmon with Vegetables hot, alongside steamed rice or quinoa for a complete and satisfying meal. Enjoy the burst of flavors as the tender salmon combines with the savory glaze and perfectly roasted vegetables!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier glaze, add more gochujang to the marinade. Adjust the amount according to your taste preferences.

  • Feel free to customize the vegetables based on what you have on hand or what you enjoy. Try adding zucchini, mushrooms, or snap peas for extra crunch and color.

  • For a vegetarian or vegan option, substitute the salmon with tofu or tempeh. Marinate and bake them the same way, adjusting the cooking time as needed.

  • Don’t forget to garnish your dish with fresh cilantro or sliced red chilies for an extra pop of color and flavor!

Final Thoughts

This Korean Soy-Glazed Salmon with Vegetables recipe is a delightful combination of flavors, textures, and colors. It brings together the richness of salmon, the umami of soy sauce, the sweetness of honey, and the heat of gochujang. The roasted vegetables add a refreshing crunch and provide a perfect balance to the dish. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or just looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to impress. So, put on your apron and get ready to create a delicious and healthy delight that will leave you craving for more!
