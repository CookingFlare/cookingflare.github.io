Seafood Chowder Recipe with Cod, Potatoes, and Cream


There’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of seafood chowder on a chilly day. This Cod, Potatoes, and Cream Seafood Chowder Recipe is a hearty, flavorful dish that’s sure to become a family favorite. With the rich taste of cod, the creamy texture of potatoes, and the velvety smoothness of cream, this chowder is a true delight. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the simple process of preparing this delicious seafood chowder, using fresh and accessible ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound cod fillets, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 cups fish stock
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Cod

In a large pot, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the cod pieces and cook until they’re opaque and flake easily with a fork, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the cod from the pot and set it aside.

Step 2: Cook the Potatoes and Onion

Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter to the pot. Add the diced potatoes and chopped onion, and cook until the potatoes are slightly tender, about 5 minutes.

Step 3: Add the Garlic and Flour

Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant. Sprinkle the all-purpose flour over the potatoes and onion, and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.

Step 4: Add the Fish Stock and Cream

Gradually add the fish stock to the pot, whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 5 minutes. Stir in the heavy cream and whole milk, and let the chowder simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the flavors have melded together.

Step 5: Add the Cod and Season

Add the cooked cod back to the pot, and stir gently to combine. Season the chowder with salt, pepper, and dried thyme. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Step 6: Serve

Ladle the chowder into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra rich and creamy chowder, add 1/4 cup of grated cheddar cheese to the pot during the last 2 minutes of cooking.
  • Substitute other types of fish, such as haddock or halibut, for the cod if desired.
  • Add some diced bell peppers or carrots to the pot for extra flavor and nutrients.
  • Make the chowder ahead of time and refrigerate or freeze it for later use. Simply reheat it over low heat, whisking occasionally, until warmed through.

Conclusion

This Cod, Potatoes, and Cream Seafood Chowder Recipe is a delicious and satisfying dish that’s perfect for a chilly evening. With its rich flavors and creamy texture, it’s sure to become a family favorite. Try it out and enjoy the warm, comforting taste of the sea!
