Instant Pot Side Dishes: Perfect Pairings for Any Meal


When it comes to preparing meals that are both delicious and time-efficient, the Instant Pot is a true kitchen game changer. This magical appliance has revolutionized the way we cook, making it easier than ever to create flavorful side dishes that complement any meal. Whether you’re serving a hearty roast, grilled chicken, or a simple vegetarian entrée, the right side dish can elevate your meal to new culinary heights. Below, we’ll explore a variety of Instant Pot side dishes, ranging from classics to creative twists, that are sure to impress your family, friends, and even your toughest food critics.

Why Choose the Instant Pot for Side Dishes?

The Instant Pot is more than just a trend; it’s a solution for busy home cooks. Its versatility allows it to sauté, pressure cook, steam, and slow cook. This means you can make everything from creamy mashed potatoes to perfectly cooked rice with minimal effort. The precision and speed of the Instant Pot help you eliminate guesswork and reduce cooking times, making it ideal for whipping up side dishes that taste as though they’ve been lovingly prepared over hours. Plus, the one-pot approach saves time on clean-up.

Irresistible Instant Pot Side Dishes to Try

1. Classic Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a staple in many homes, but they can often be a time-consuming endeavor. The Instant Pot simplifies this process without sacrificing flavor. For this recipe, all you’ll need are potatoes, garlic, butter, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt.

How to Prepare:

  1. Prep the Potatoes: Peel and chop potatoes into small chunks. Place them in the Instant Pot.
  2. Add Garlic: Toss in a few peeled garlic cloves for an extra layer of flavor.
  3. Pressure Cook: Add water to cover the potatoes, secure the lid, and cook on high pressure for 8 minutes.
  4. Mash and Season: Once the pressure releases naturally, drain the water, then mash the potatoes with butter and heavy cream. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

These mashed potatoes are creamy, buttery perfection that pairs beautifully with steak, roast chicken, or even a Thanksgiving feast.

2. Fluffy Instant Pot Rice Pilaf

Rice pilaf is the ultimate versatile side dish—it works with everything from Mediterranean-inspired menus to Indian curries. Cooking rice in the Instant Pot ensures a fluffy texture every time.

How to Prepare:

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Use the sauté function on the Instant Pot to cook diced onions and minced garlic in olive oil until fragrant.
  2. Add Rice and Broth: Add long-grain white rice and stir to coat it with the oil. Pour in chicken or vegetable broth for added depth of flavor.
  3. Cook: Pressure cook on high for 4 minutes, then let the pressure release naturally.
  4. Finish: Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with fresh parsley or toasted almonds for an added crunch.

This light and flavorful pilaf is the perfect side for grilled fish, kabobs, or roasted vegetables.

3. Creamy Cheddar Broccoli

If you’re looking for a comforting, cheesy side dish, Instant Pot cheddar broccoli is a must-try. It’s a crowd-pleaser that turns simple vegetables into something rich and satisfying.

How to Prepare:

  1. Cook the Broccoli: Add fresh or frozen broccoli with a cup of water to the Instant Pot and pressure cook on high for 0 minutes (yes, zero minutes!).
  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: Drain the broccoli, then switch to sauté mode. Add heavy cream, grated cheddar cheese, and a sprinkle of garlic powder. Stir until the cheese is fully melted.
  3. Serve: Mix the cheese sauce with the broccoli and serve warm.

This creamy side works wonderfully with baked chicken, pork chops, or even as a topping for baked potatoes.

4. Instant Pot Mac and Cheese

Macaroni and cheese isn’t just for kids anymore—it’s a side dish that can elevate a variety of meals. The Instant Pot makes an ultra-creamy, cheesy mac and cheese in a fraction of the time.

How to Prepare:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Place uncooked elbow macaroni, water, butter, and a pinch of salt in the Instant Pot. Pressure cook on high for 4 minutes, then quick release.
  2. Add the Cheese Sauce: Stir in milk or heavy cream, grated cheddar, and Parmesan cheese until smooth and creamy.
  3. Customize: Optional add-ins like crumbled bacon or diced jalapeños can take this dish to the next level.

Pair this rich and indulgent side dish with barbecued meats, burgers, or even a crisp green salad for balance.

5. Herbed Quinoa

Quinoa is a superfood that deserves a spot on your dinner table, and the Instant Pot simplifies its preparation. This herbed quinoa recipe is light, fluffy, and packed with nutrients.

How to Prepare:

  1. Rinse the Quinoa: Thoroughly rinse quinoa to remove its natural bitterness.
  2. Pressure Cook: Add quinoa, water or broth, and a pinch of salt to the Instant Pot. Cook on high pressure for 1 minute, then let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes.
  3. Season: Toss the cooked quinoa with fresh herbs like parsley, dill, or cilantro, a squeeze of lemon juice, and a drizzle of olive oil.

Herbed quinoa is a versatile side dish that pairs well with grilled salmon, roasted vegetables, or as a base for grain bowls.

6. Sweet and Savory Instant Pot Carrots

Carrots are often an underrated side dish, but with the Instant Pot, they can shine as a sweet and savory highlight of your meal.

How to Prepare:

  1. Prep the Carrots: Peel and slice fresh carrots into even pieces.
  2. Cook: Add water or broth to the Instant Pot and pressure cook the carrots for 2 minutes. Quick release the pressure.
  3. Glaze: Drain the liquid, then switch to sauté mode. Add a mix of butter, honey, and a splash of balsamic vinegar to glaze the carrots.

These caramelized carrots are the perfect side dish for roast turkey, ham, or a vegetarian main.

7. Instant Pot Lentil Salad

If you’re looking to add a protein-packed, plant-based side dish to your meal, an Instant Pot lentil salad fits the bill. It’s wholesome, hearty, and full of bold flavors.

How to Prepare:

  1. Cook the Lentils: Rinse lentils and add them to the Instant Pot with water or broth. Pressure cook on high for 9 minutes, followed by a quick release.
  2. Mix the Salad: Combine cooked lentils with diced cucumbers, cherry tomatoes, red onions, and a simple vinaigrette made with olive oil, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard.
  3. Finish with Herbs: Toss in fresh herbs like parsley or mint for an aromatic touch.

This lentil salad is an excellent partner for grilled chicken, lamb, or simply enjoyed on its own.

Final Thoughts

The Instant Pot opens up a world of possibilities for side dishes that are both quick to prepare and restaurant-worthy. From creamy mashed potatoes to vibrant quinoa salads, there’s an option for every palate and every meal. With the Instant Pot, you can spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying a delicious meal with your loved ones.

So, the next time you’re planning a meal, let your Instant Pot be your secret weapon for creating mouthwatering side dishes that complete the dining experience. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or crafting a weeknight family meal, these side dishes will not only save you time but also earn you rave reviews. Happy cooking!
