Slow Cooker Meatballs in Marinara Sauce: Perfect for Pasta Night


There’s nothing quite like the comforting aroma of a delicious, home-cooked meal filling your kitchen. And when it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the classic combination of meatballs and marinara sauce. This recipe for slow cooker meatballs in marinara sauce is not only incredibly easy to prepare, but it’s also the perfect addition to any pasta night. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just want to indulge in a cozy dinner for two, these slow cooker meatballs are sure to become a staple in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

For the Meatballs:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Marinara Sauce:

  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Use your hands to mix the ingredients until well combined.

  2. Shape the meat mixture into meatballs, approximately 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. This recipe should yield about 20 meatballs.

  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, diced onions, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper.

  4. Pour a thin layer of the marinara sauce into the bottom of the slow cooker.

  5. Carefully arrange the meatballs in the slow cooker, ensuring they are evenly spaced and not stacked on top of each other.

  6. Pour the remaining marinara sauce over the meatballs, ensuring they are completely covered.

  7. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 6-8 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours. The meatballs are ready when they are cooked through and the marinara sauce has thickened slightly.

  8. Once the meatballs are ready, serve them hot over a bed of your favorite cooked pasta. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a burst of color and flavor.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a leaner option, you can use ground turkey or chicken instead of ground beef.
  • If you prefer a finer texture, you can pulse the diced onions and minced garlic in a food processor before adding them to the marinara sauce.
  • These meatballs can also be served as a hearty sub sandwich, topped with melted mozzarella cheese.

Final Thoughts

This slow cooker meatballs in marinara sauce recipe is a true crowd-pleaser. The slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in tender, juicy meatballs and a rich, savory marinara sauce. Whether you’re hosting a family dinner or simply craving a comforting meal, this recipe is a must-try for any pasta lover. So, next time you’re planning a pasta night, consider letting your slow cooker do the work and enjoy the delightful aroma of these simmering meatballs as you prepare the rest of your meal. Buon appetito!
