How to Make Korean Sweet Potato Soup (Goguma Juk)


Korean cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors, textures, and comforting dishes, and one of the most beloved comfort foods is Goguma Juk, or Korean Sweet Potato Soup. This dish is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, making it a perfect choice for a cozy meal or a nourishing snack. In this blog post, we will explore the history of Goguma Juk, its health benefits, and provide you with a step-by-step guide to making this delightful soup at home.

The History of Goguma Juk

Goguma Juk has its roots in traditional Korean cooking, where it has been enjoyed for centuries. Sweet potatoes, or “goguma,” have been a staple in Korean diets due to their availability and versatility. Historically, this dish was often prepared during the colder months, providing warmth and sustenance. It is also a popular choice for those recovering from illness, as it is easy to digest and gentle on the stomach.

Health Benefits of Goguma Juk

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a moment to appreciate the health benefits of Goguma Juk. Sweet potatoes are rich in vitamins A and C, fiber, and antioxidants. They are known to support immune function, promote healthy skin, and aid digestion. Additionally, the warm, soothing nature of the soup makes it an excellent choice for those looking to nourish their bodies and souls.

Ingredients for Goguma Juk

To make a delicious pot of Goguma Juk, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 medium-sized Korean sweet potatoes (or regular sweet potatoes if unavailable)
  • 4 cups of water (or vegetable broth for added flavor)
  • 1/2 cup of glutinous rice flour (or sweet rice flour)
  • 1/4 cup of sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional, for added flavor)
  • Chopped nuts or seeds (for garnish, optional)
  • Coconut milk or cream (for a richer texture, optional)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Goguma Juk

Step 1: Prepare the Sweet Potatoes

Start by washing the sweet potatoes thoroughly to remove any dirt. If you prefer a smoother texture, you can peel the sweet potatoes, but leaving the skin on adds extra nutrients and fiber. Cut the sweet potatoes into small cubes to ensure they cook evenly.

Step 2: Cook the Sweet Potatoes

In a medium-sized pot, add the cubed sweet potatoes and pour in the water or vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender and easily pierced with a fork.

Step 3: Blend the Sweet Potatoes

Once the sweet potatoes are cooked, remove the pot from the heat. Using a potato masher or an immersion blender, mash the sweet potatoes until smooth. If you prefer a chunkier texture, you can leave some pieces intact. If you used an immersion blender, be careful not to over-blend, as you want to maintain some texture.

Step 4: Prepare the Rice Flour Mixture

In a separate bowl, combine the glutinous rice flour with 1 cup of water. Stir until the mixture is smooth and free of lumps. This will help thicken the soup and give it a creamy consistency.

Step 5: Combine the Mixtures

Return the pot with the mashed sweet potatoes to the stove over low heat. Gradually add the rice flour mixture to the sweet potato puree, stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming. Continue to cook for an additional 5-10 minutes, allowing the soup to thicken. If the soup becomes too thick, you can add more water or broth to reach your desired consistency.

Step 6: Season the Soup

Once the soup has thickened, add the sugar and salt to taste. Stir well to combine. If you’re using vanilla extract, add it at this stage for an extra layer of flavor. Taste the soup and adjust the sweetness or saltiness according to your preference.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Ladle the Goguma Juk into bowls and garnish with chopped nuts or seeds for added texture and flavor. If you want a creamier soup, drizzle some coconut milk or cream on top. Serve the soup warm, and enjoy the comforting flavors of this traditional Korean dish.

Tips for Making the Perfect Goguma Juk

  • Choosing Sweet Potatoes: For the best flavor, opt for Korean sweet potatoes, which are sweeter and creamier than regular sweet potatoes. If you can’t find them, any variety of sweet potato will work.
  • Adjusting Consistency: The thickness of Goguma Juk can be adjusted based on your preference.
