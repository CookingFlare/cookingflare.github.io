How to Make Your Own Baking Powder and Baking Soda


As a home baker, you may have noticed that store-bought baking powder and baking soda can be quite expensive. Moreover, some recipes may require specific types of baking powder or soda that are hard to find in stores. In this post, we will show you how to make your own baking powder and baking soda at home, saving you money and ensuring that you always have the right ingredients on hand.

Why Make Your Own Baking Powder and Baking Soda?

Store-bought baking powder and baking soda are often mixed with additives and fillers that can affect the taste and texture of your baked goods. By making your own, you can control the ingredients and avoid any unwanted additives. Additionally, homemade baking powder and baking soda can be customized to your specific needs, allowing you to create the perfect blend for your recipes.

Making Your Own Baking Powder

Baking powder is a mixture of baking soda and an acid (usually cream of tartar) that is designed to release carbon dioxide gas slowly over time. This slow release is what gives baked goods their light and airy texture. To make your own baking powder, you will need:

  • 2 tablespoons baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon cream of tartar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Combine the baking soda and cream of tartar in a small bowl. Add the cornstarch and stir until the mixture is well combined. Sift the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any lumps.

Tips for Using Your Homemade Baking Powder

When using your homemade baking powder, keep the following tips in mind:

  • Use it within 6 months of making it, as the mixture can lose potency over time.
  • Store it in an airtight container to keep it fresh.
  • Use it in recipes that call for baking powder, substituting it 1:1 for store-bought baking powder.

Making Your Own Baking Soda

Baking soda, also known as sodium bicarbonate, is a natural mineral that is commonly used in baking. To make your own baking soda, you will need:

  • 1 cup washing soda (sodium carbonate)
  • 1 cup citric acid (such as lemon juice or vinegar)
  • 1 cup water

Combine the washing soda and citric acid in a small saucepan. Add the water and stir until the mixture is well combined. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the mixture has thickened and turned into a paste.

Tips for Using Your Homemade Baking Soda

When using your homemade baking soda, keep the following tips in mind:

  • Use it within 6 months of making it, as the mixture can lose potency over time.
  • Store it in an airtight container to keep it fresh.
  • Use it in recipes that call for baking soda, substituting it 1:1 for store-bought baking soda.

Conclusion

Making your own baking powder and baking soda is a simple and cost-effective way to ensure that you always have the right ingredients on hand. By following these recipes and tips, you can enjoy the benefits of homemade baking powder and baking soda in your baked goods. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, making your own baking powder and baking soda is a great way to take your baking to the next level.
