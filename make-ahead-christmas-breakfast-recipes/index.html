Make-Ahead Christmas Breakfast Recipes


The magic of Christmas morning is often wrapped up in the warmth of shared moments, twinkling lights, and the scent of festive delights. However, the joy can quickly turn to chaos when everyone is trying to whip up breakfast while eagerly awaiting gift unwrapping. That’s where make-ahead Christmas breakfast recipes come to the rescue, allowing you to prepare delicious, satisfying dishes in advance so you can focus on family time instead of the kitchen frenzy.

Below, you’ll find an extensive collection of recipes that range from comforting casseroles to sweet treats and savory bites. Each is designed to be prepped ahead of time and simply baked or reheated on the big morning. Whether your Christmas is a casual brunch or a gourmet gala, these recipes will help you create memorable moments around the breakfast table without burning out.

Why Choose Make-Ahead Breakfast Recipes for Christmas?

The holiday season is packed with festivities, often leaving little time for elaborate morning meals. Preparation efforts can be overwhelming with so many hands to hold, presents to open, and other meals to plan throughout the day. By choosing make-ahead recipes, you can:

  • Save time: Prepare dishes the day before or earlier, reducing last-minute cooking.
  • Minimize stress: No scrambling to cook everything fresh while kids and guests wait.
  • Enhance flavor: Some casseroles and baked goods taste even better after resting or chilling.
  • Offer variety: Prepare both sweet and savory options in advance to satisfy every palate.
  • Simplify clean-up: Use one or two baking dishes, making cleaning more manageable post-meal.

Savory Make-Ahead Breakfast Casseroles

1. Classic Sausage and Cheese Breakfast Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb breakfast sausage, cooked and crumbled
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 cups cubed French bread (day-old works great)
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Assemble the night before: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish.
  2. Spread the bread cubes evenly in the dish.
  3. Distribute the cooked sausage over the bread.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, mustard, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  5. Pour the egg mixture evenly over the bread and sausage.
  6. Sprinkle shredded cheddar cheese on top.
  7. Cover tightly with foil and refrigerate overnight.
  8. Bake on Christmas morning: Remove from the fridge 30 minutes before baking. Bake covered for 30 minutes, then uncover and bake an additional 15 minutes or until the top is golden and the casserole is set. Garnish with parsley, if desired.

Why it works: The bread soaks up the egg custard overnight, creating a perfect texture with savory sausage and melted cheese. This casserole is incredibly comforting without fuss.

2. Vegetable and Herb Frittata Bake

Ingredients:

  • 10 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup chopped spinach (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly oil a baking dish.
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté onions, bell pepper, garlic until softened — about 5 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk eggs and milk. Add sautéed vegetables, spinach, cherry tomatoes, feta, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the mixture into the baking dish.
  5. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  6. Remove from refrigerator 20 minutes before baking.
  7. Bake uncovered for 35-40 minutes or until eggs are set and golden.

Pro tip: This vegetable-rich frittata is flexible; swap out spinach or peppers for seasonal favorites or what’s available. It’s colorful, healthy, and can cater to vegetarians.

3. Cheesy Ham and Potato Strata

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups cubed bread (rustic or sourdough recommended)
  • 3 cups peeled and diced potatoes, parboiled
  • 2 cups diced cooked ham
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 tsp dry mustard powder
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine bread cubes, diced potatoes, ham, and cheese in a large bowl.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk eggs, milk, mustard powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Mix wet ingredients into the bread mixture until fully coated.
  4. Transfer to a greased casserole dish, cover tightly with foil, and refrigerate up to 24 hours.
  5. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45-50 minutes, uncovering halfway through to allow the top to brown.

Why this recipe shines: The potatoes add extra heartiness, while ham and Swiss cheese bring familiar, comforting flavors. Parboiling potatoes ensures they’re tender without raw spots after baking.

Sweet Make-Ahead Christmas Breakfast Ideas

1. Cinnamon Roll French Toast Bake

Ingredients:

  • 1 package (12 count) refrigerated cinnamon rolls with icing
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish.
  2. Cut cinnamon rolls into quarters and spread evenly in the dish.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, milk, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Pour egg mixture over the rolls, pressing gently to soak.
  5. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  6. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 40-45 minutes or until the custard is set and the top is golden.
  7. Drizzle with included icing before serving.

Benefits: This recipe delights kids and adults alike with a sweet, gooey breakfast that requires just a few minutes of assembly before chilling.

2. Make-Ahead Berry and Cream Cheese Danish Bake

Ingredients:

  • 1 package refrigerated crescent roll dough or puff pastry sheets
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mixed fresh or frozen berries
  • 1 egg, beaten for egg wash
  • Powdered sugar for dusting (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Spread dough out into a greased 9x13 pan, pressing seams to create an even layer.
  2. In a bowl, mix cream cheese, sugar, lemon zest, and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Spread cream cheese mixture evenly over dough, leaving a small border.
  4. Sprinkle berries over the cream cheese layer.
  5. Fold dough edges over slightly to create a rustic border.
  6. Brush edges with beaten egg.
  7. Cover tightly and refrigerate overnight.
  8. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25-30 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  9. Cool slightly and dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Why it’s a winner: This Danish bake tastes like a bakery creation but requires minimal prep. The tangy cream cheese and sweet berries create a luscious contrast.

3. Overnight Apple Cinnamon Oatmeal Bake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or pecans (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Lightly grease a 9x13 baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine oats, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk milk, yogurt, maple syrup, eggs, and melted butter.
  4. Pour wet ingredients into dry, stir until combined.
  5. Fold in half the apple slices and nuts.
  6. Transfer mixture to the baking dish. Arrange remaining apple slices on top.
  7. Cover with foil and refrigerate overnight.
  8. In the morning, bake at 350°F (175°C) for 40-45 minutes until set and golden.

Why you’ll love this: This bake is a wholesome way to incorporate fruit and whole grains. It’s slightly sweet with a hint of cinnamon and crisp apple, perfect for a warming start.

Tips for Success with Make-Ahead Christmas Breakfast Recipes

  • Prep lifts yourself: Tackle chopping, cooking sausage, or pre-cooking potatoes a day or two ahead during less busy times.
  • Use quality containers: Glass or ceramic baking dishes work best for even baking and reheating.
  • Temperature timing: Let refrigerated casseroles sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes before baking to promote even cooking.
  • Label ingredients: If you prepare multiple dishes, labeling can prevent confusion and speed up morning cooking.
  • Coordinate ovens: If baking multiple dishes, plan baking times and rack positions for optimal use without overcrowding.
  • Customize flavors: Many casseroles and bakes are forgiving. Swap cheeses, meats, or veggies based on family preferences or dietary needs.

Accompaniments to Complete Your Christmas Breakfast Spread

While these make-ahead dishes are often the centerpiece, complementing them with fresh fruits, warm beverages, and simple sides can elevate your meal.

  • Fresh fruit platter: Including citrus segments, grapes, and berries adds color and brightness.
  • Yogurt and granola parfaits: Layered and served in jars for an elegant touch.
  • Breakfast meats: Crisp bacon or sausage links can be quickly cooked in the oven.
  • Freshly brewed coffee and festive teas: Add cinnamon sticks, cloves, or orange peel for holiday flair.
  • Homemade or store-bought muffins or scones: Serve alongside or as a snack later in the day.

Wrapping Up the Perfect Christmas Morning

Make-ahead Christmas breakfast recipes turn the rush and mess of holiday mornings into calm, joyous celebrations. By preparing casseroles, bakes, and sweet treats ahead, you gift yourself extra time savoring the things that matter the most: laughter, stories, and moments with loved ones.

Whether your style leans toward hearty savory casseroles or sweet, indulgent baked goodies, the options above offer something for every taste and family tradition. Next Christmas, take the pressure off your kitchen duties and enjoy a breakfast that feels like a warm hug – prepared ahead so you can truly relax and revel in the day’s magic.

Happy holidays and happy cooking! If you try any of these recipes or have your own make-ahead breakfast favorites, don’t hesitate to share the joy by passing those recipes along!
