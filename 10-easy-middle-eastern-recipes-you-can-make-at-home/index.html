10 Easy Middle Eastern Recipes You Can Make at Home


Middle Eastern cuisine offers a delightful array of flavors, textures, and aromas that are sure to tempt your taste buds. From rich stews to light salads, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant culinary tradition. Whether you’re craving a quick snack or a full-course meal, you can easily recreate these iconic dishes in your own kitchen.

Here are 10 easy Middle Eastern recipes that you can prepare at home. These dishes are perfect for anyone wanting to explore the exotic tastes of the region or simply add some variety to their meals. Let’s dive into these delicious and simple recipes!

1. Hummus – The Ultimate Middle Eastern Dip

Hummus is one of the most beloved dishes from the Middle East, and it’s incredibly easy to make at home. This creamy, smooth dip is made from simple ingredients like chickpeas, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil. It’s the perfect snack served with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or even as a spread for sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of chickpeas (or 1 ½ cups cooked chickpeas)
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed for consistency)

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, and garlic.
  2. Blend until smooth. If the consistency is too thick, add a bit of water, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach a creamy texture.
  3. Season with salt to taste.
  4. Drizzle with extra olive oil and garnish with paprika or parsley, if desired.

This simple yet delicious hummus recipe is perfect for anyone looking to add a bit of Middle Eastern flavor to their meals.

2. Falafel – Crispy Chickpea Fritters

Falafel is a deep-fried, golden-brown chickpea fritter that has become a popular snack or street food across the world. These crispy balls are packed with flavor from herbs and spices, making them a perfect addition to a meal or served as a snack with tahini or yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained (or 1 ½ cups cooked chickpeas)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 4-5 tablespoons flour (more if needed)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, cayenne, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture is well combined but still slightly chunky.
  2. Add baking powder and flour, then pulse until the mixture starts to come together and form a dough. If the mixture is too wet, add more flour.
  3. Refrigerate the mixture for at least 30 minutes to allow it to firm up.
  4. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Fry the falafel until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve with tahini or yogurt.

Falafel is best enjoyed with a side of fresh vegetables and pita bread for a complete meal.

3. Tabbouleh – Fresh Parsley Salad

Tabbouleh is a vibrant, fresh salad that is made primarily with parsley, tomatoes, and bulgur wheat. It’s the perfect balance of healthy, light ingredients with an amazing refreshing taste. This Middle Eastern salad can be served as a side dish or as a starter.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 1 ½ cups fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ½ cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small cucumber, diced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the bulgur in a bowl and cover it with boiling water. Let it sit for about 10 minutes, then drain any excess water and fluff the bulgur with a fork.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the parsley, mint, tomatoes, cucumber, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  3. Add the bulgur to the salad and mix well.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Let the tabbouleh sit for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.

Tabbouleh is the perfect light, healthy dish to accompany a rich main course or as a standalone meal.

4. Baba Ganoush – Smoky Eggplant Dip

Baba ganoush is a smoky, savory eggplant dip that’s similar to hummus but with a different flavor profile. It’s creamy and slightly tangy, making it an excellent spread for pita or a topping for grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium eggplants
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Smoked paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pierce the eggplants with a fork and place them on a baking sheet. Roast for 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until the eggplants are soft and the skin is charred.
  3. Let the eggplants cool for a few minutes, then peel off the skin and scoop out the flesh.
  4. In a food processor, combine the eggplant flesh, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and olive oil. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  5. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with smoked paprika for garnish.

This rich and smoky baba ganoush is a fantastic alternative to hummus.

5. Shakshuka – Poached Eggs in Tomato Sauce

Shakshuka is a hearty, comforting dish made by poaching eggs in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce. It’s perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even a light dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 4 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, and sauté for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken.
  4. Create small wells in the sauce and crack the eggs into them. Cover the skillet and cook until the eggs are poached to your liking.
  5. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with warm pita or crusty bread.

Shakshuka is a wonderfully filling dish that brings the bold flavors of the Middle East to your breakfast table.

6. Fattoush – Crispy Bread Salad

Fattoush is a fresh and tangy salad that includes crispy pieces of toasted pita bread, giving it a satisfying crunch. This colorful dish is packed with fresh vegetables and a zesty dressing, making it a perfect side or light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large pita bread
  • 2 cups romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Tear the pita into pieces and toast it in the oven for 5-7 minutes until crispy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, onion, parsley, and mint.
  3. Add the toasted pita pieces.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, pomegranate molasses (if using), salt, and pepper.
  5. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.

Fattoush is a delightful blend of fresh and crispy textures with a tangy dressing that pairs wonderfully with grilled meats or as a standalone dish.

7. Lentil Soup – Hearty and Nourishing

Lentil soup is a staple in Middle Eastern kitchens, offering a warm and comforting meal full of protein and fiber. This simple yet flavorful soup is made with lentils, vegetables, and a mix of spices, creating a hearty dish that can be served on its own or with some warm pita.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic, and sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  2. Add cumin, turmeric, and coriander, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the lentils and vegetable broth, then bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This lentil soup is nourishing, flavorful, and perfect for chilly evenings or as a light lunch.

8. Kebabs – Grilled Meat Skewers

Middle Eastern kebabs are skewered pieces of marinated meat (typically lamb, beef, or chicken) that are grilled to perfection. The marinade often includes a mix of yogurt, garlic, and spices, giving the meat a rich, tender texture and bold flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken or lamb, cut into chunks
  • ½ cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix the yogurt, olive oil, cumin, paprika, coriander, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add the meat to the marinade, cover, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for more flavor.
  3. Thread the marinated meat onto skewers.
  4. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Grill the kebabs for 5-7 minutes per side, until the meat is cooked through.
  5. Serve with rice, flatbread, or a salad.

These juicy and flavorful kebabs are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a BBQ or enjoying a casual meal at home.

9. Manakish – Middle Eastern Flatbread

Manakish is a type of Middle Eastern flatbread often topped with za’atar (a blend of thyme, sesame seeds, and sumac) and baked until crispy. It’s an excellent snack or breakfast option and can be served with fresh vegetables or yogurt.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ¼ cup za’atar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil for brushing

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt, and yeast. Add the warm water and olive oil, then mix until a dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until smooth, then cover and let it rise for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  4. Punch down the dough and divide it into smaller portions. Roll each portion into a thin round.
  5. Sprinkle the za’atar over the dough and drizzle with olive oil.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown.

These delicious flatbreads are perfect as a snack or paired with a fresh salad.

10. Baklava – Sweet Pastry Dessert

Baklava is a rich, sweet pastry made from layers of filo dough, chopped nuts, and sweet syrup. This famous dessert is perfect for special occasions and brings a satisfying sweetness to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package filo dough
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (walnuts, pistachios, or almonds), chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup honey
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, combine the chopped nuts and cinnamon.
  3. Brush a baking dish with melted butter and layer 5 sheets of filo dough, brushing each sheet with butter before adding the next.
  4. Sprinkle a thin layer of the nut mixture over the dough.
  5. Repeat the process, layering filo dough and nuts, until all the nuts are used.
  6. Top with several more sheets of filo dough, brushing each with butter.
  7. Using a sharp knife, cut the baklava into diamond or square shapes.
  8. Bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown and crispy.
  9. Meanwhile, in a small saucepan, combine honey, sugar, and vanilla extract. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer for 10 minutes.
  10. Pour the syrup over the baklava while it’s still hot. Allow it to cool before serving.

This sweet and nutty dessert is the perfect way to end any Middle Eastern meal.

By preparing these 10 easy Middle Eastern recipes, you can bring the delicious and diverse flavors of the region right into your home kitchen. Whether you’re making a savory dip, grilling kebabs, or indulging in a sweet pastry, these dishes are simple enough for beginners yet rich in flavor.
