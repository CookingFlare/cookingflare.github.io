Apple and Cheddar Stuffed Pork Chops: A Savory Fall Recipe


As the air turns crisp and the leaves begin to change, it’s the perfect time to indulge in the rich, comforting flavors of fall. And what better way to do so than with a hearty, savory dish that combines the sweetness of apples with the richness of cheddar cheese? This Apple and Cheddar Stuffed Pork Chops recipe is a true celebration of the season, bringing together the best of autumn’s bounty in a single, satisfying dish. Whether you’re hosting a cozy dinner party or simply craving a comforting meal, this recipe is sure to impress and delight.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in pork chops
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 large apple, diced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Using a sharp knife, carefully cut a pocket into each pork chop, creating a cavity for the stuffing. Be sure not to cut all the way through the chop.

  3. In a skillet over medium heat, warm the olive oil. Season the pork chops with salt and pepper, then sear them in the skillet until golden brown on both sides. Remove the chops from the skillet and set them aside.

  4. In the same skillet, add the diced apple, onion, and garlic. Sauté until the onions are translucent and the apples are slightly softened.

  5. Stir in the thyme and cinnamon, then add the shredded cheddar cheese to the skillet, stirring until it begins to melt and combine with the apple and onion mixture.

  6. Carefully stuff each pork chop with the apple and cheddar mixture, dividing it evenly among the chops.

  7. Place the stuffed pork chops in a baking dish and pour the chicken broth around them. Dot the tops of the chops with butter.

  8. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pork reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C).

  9. Once cooked, remove the foil and return the pork chops to the oven to broil for a few minutes, allowing the tops to caramelize and brown.

  10. Serve the Apple and Cheddar Stuffed Pork Chops hot, accompanied by your favorite fall sides such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a crisp green salad.

Tips for Success

  • Choose bone-in pork chops for the best flavor and juiciness.
  • Feel free to experiment with different apple varieties, such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp, to find your favorite flavor combination.
  • If you prefer a sharper cheese flavor, consider using aged cheddar in place of the standard variety.

The combination of sweet, tender apple, sharp cheddar cheese, and succulent pork creates a symphony of flavors that perfectly captures the essence of fall. With a hint of warming spices and a touch of richness from the cheese, these Apple and Cheddar Stuffed Pork Chops are a delightful way to savor the season’s harvest.

Whether served for a special occasion or as a comforting weeknight meal, this recipe is a wonderful way to embrace the flavors of fall and create lasting memories around the dinner table.
