The Best Fruit Desserts for a Summer BBQ


When the sun shines bright and the days stretch long, summer BBQs become the centerpiece of our social lives. There’s something undeniably satisfying about firing up the grill and enjoying flavorful meals outdoors. But what sets a memorable BBQ apart from the rest? A delightful array of fruit desserts that beautifully complement the smoky, savory flavors of grilled meats. In this post, we’ll explore the best fruit desserts for a summer BBQ that will not only tantalize your taste buds but also impress your guests. Why Fruit Desserts? Fruit desserts are the perfect ending to any summer meal for several reasons. They are refreshing, often light, and full of natural sweetness. Additionally, fruit desserts can be made in advance, allowing you to enjoy your time with friends and family instead of staying tied to the kitchen during the festivities. Finally, they are versatile, accommodating various dietary preferences and restrictions while being packed with vitamins and fiber. Now, let’s dive into some scrumptious fruit dessert ideas that will highlight your summer BBQ menu!

  1. Grilled Peaches with Honey and Balsamic Glaze Ingredients:

4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted 2 tablespoons olive oil 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar 2 tablespoons honey 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Salt and pepper to taste Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

Heat your grill to medium-high. Brush the peach halves with olive oil, then sprinkle with salt and pepper. Place the peaches cut side down on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes or until grill marks form. In a small saucepan, combine the balsamic vinegar, honey, and vanilla extract. Bring to a gentle simmer and reduce by half, about 5-7 minutes. Once the peaches are nicely grilled, remove them from the heat and drizzle the balsamic glaze over them. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve warm.

Grilled peaches offer a rich, caramelized flavor that pairs beautifully with the tanginess of balsamic glaze. This dish is not just about flavor; it’s about the visual appeal as well, making it a fantastic centerpiece. 2. No-Bake Berry Cheesecake Parfaits Ingredients:

2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries) 1 cup cottage cheese 1 cup cream cheese, softened 1/2 cup powdered sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/2 cup graham cracker crumbs Fresh mint for garnish

Instructions:

In a mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese, cottage cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth and creamy. In clear glasses or parfait dishes, layer the graham cracker crumbs, cheese mixture, and mixed berries. Repeat the layers until the glasses are filled, finishing with a mix of berries on top. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour to firm up the parfaits. Garnish with fresh mint before serving.

These no-bake berry cheesecake parfaits are not only simple to make but also incredibly refreshing. The creamy cheese base pairs flawlessly with the tangy sweetness of the berries, creating a dessert that feels indulgent without being overly heavy. 3. Watermelon Fruit Salad with Lime and Mint Ingredients:

1 small seedless watermelon, cubed 1 cup strawberries, hulled and halved 1 cup blueberries 1 cup pineapple, diced Juice of 2 limes 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped

Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine watermelon, strawberries, blueberries, and pineapple. Squeeze the lime juice over the fruit mixture and sprinkle chopped mint. Toss gently to combine, being careful not to bruise the fruit. Chill in the refrigerator for 30 minutes before serving.

This vibrant watermelon fruit salad not only adds a pop of color to your BBQ spread but is also incredibly hydrating—a crucial factor for those hot summer days. The zest of lime combined with fresh mint elevates the natural sweetness of the fruit. 4. Tropical Fruit Skewers with Coconut Cream Dip Ingredients:

1 pineapple, cut into chunks 2 ripe kiwis, peeled and sliced 2 bananas, cut into thick slices 1 cup strawberries, hulled For the Dip: 1 cup coconut cream 2 tablespoons powdered sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

Preheat the grill to medium heat (or you can serve these fresh without grilling). Thread chunks of pineapple, kiwi slices, banana pieces, and strawberries onto skewers. If grilling, place the skewers on the grill for about 2-3 minutes, turning periodically until slightly charred. In a small mixing bowl, combine coconut cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth. Serve the skewers warm or cold with the coconut cream dip on the side.

Fruit skewers are a fun, interactive dessert option perfect for BBQs and gatherings. They’re easy to customize with your favorite fruits and dietary needs, plus the addition of a creamy coconut dip takes this dessert to another level. 5. Strawberry Shortcake Trifle Ingredients:

4 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced 1 cup sugar 3 cups whipped cream (store-bought or homemade) 1 pound sponge cake or angel food cake, cubed Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

In a medium bowl, combine sliced strawberries and sugar. Let sit for about 30 minutes for the flavors to meld. In a trifle dish or individual cups, layer the cubed cake, strawberries, and whipped cream. Repeat the layers until the dish is full, finishing with whipped cream on top. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

Strawberry shortcake is a classic summer dessert that never goes out of style. This trifle version is not only visually stunning but also incredibly satisfying, combining the sweetness of strawberries with the creaminess of whipped cream and the softness of sponge cake. 6. Blueberry Lemon Bars Ingredients:

For the crust: 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened 1/3 cup powdered sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt

For the filling: 2 large eggs 1 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup all-purpose flour Zest of 1 lemon 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice 1 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking dish. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, butter, powdered sugar, and salt. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared dish to form the crust. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until lightly golden. Let cool slightly. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, flour, lemon zest, and lemon juice until smooth. Fold in the blueberries. Pour the filling over the cooled crust and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes until set. Allow to cool completely before slicing into squares.

Blueberry lemon bars offer a delightful balance of tart and sweet. The refreshing citrus notes make them an ideal choice for summer gatherings, while the blueberries add a juicy burst of flavor. 7. Mango Sorbet Ingredients:

4 ripe mangoes, peeled and chopped 1/2 cup coconut milk 1/4 cup lime juice 1/2 cup sugar (adjust based on sweetness preference)

Instructions:

In a blender, combine mangoes, coconut milk, lime juice, and sugar. Blend until smooth. Pour the mixture into an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours, stirring every hour to break up any ice crystals. Once frozen, scoop out the sorbet and serve in chilled bowls.

This vibrant mango sorbet is a refreshing treat that’s perfect for combating the summer heat. It’s naturally dairy-free and can be enjoyed as a light dessert or a palate cleanser. 8. Cherry Almond Galette Ingredients:

For the crust: 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup almond flour 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed 4-5 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the filling: 2 cups fresh cherries, pitted and halved 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1 tablespoon cornstarch 1 teaspoon almond extract

Instructions:

To make the crust, combine flours and salt in a mixing bowl. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add water, mixing until the dough comes together. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a bowl, combine cherries, sugar, cornstarch, and almond extract. Allow to sit for 10 minutes. Roll out the chilled dough into a large circle on a floured surface. Transfer to the prepared baking sheet. Spoon the cherry mixture into the center, leaving about 2 inches of crust around the edges. Fold the edges over the filling, pleating as necessary. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cherries are bubbling.

This cherry almond galette is an elegant yet approachable dessert that combines flaky crust with juicy cherries. The almond flavor adds an unexpected twist, making it an unforgettable addition to your BBQ. 9. Fruit Pizza Ingredients:

For the crust: 1 package refrigerated sugar cookie dough

For the cream cheese frosting: 8 ounces cream cheese, softened 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the toppings: Assorted fresh fruits (kiwi, strawberries, blueberries, mandarin oranges)

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a pizza pan. Press the sugar cookie dough onto the pan to form a crust, shaping it into a round, even layer. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden. Let cool completely. In a bowl, beat together cream cheese, sugar, and vanilla until smooth. Spread the cream cheese frosting over the cooled cookie crust. Arrange an array of fresh fruits on top in a colorful pattern. Slice into wedges and serve.

Fruit pizza brings a playful twist to dessert tables. The combination of a sweet cookie crust, tangy cream cheese frosting, and vibrant fruits creates a delightful experience for everyone. 10. Frozen Fruit Pops Ingredients:

2 cups mixed berries (e.g., strawberries, blueberries, raspberries) 1 cup coconut water or juice 2 tablespoons honey or agave syrup (optional)

Instructions:

In a blender, combine mixed berries, coconut water, and honey (if using). Blend until smooth. Pour the mixture into ice pop molds, leaving a little space at the top for expansion. Insert sticks and freeze for at least 4-6 hours or until solid. To release, run warm water over the outside of the molds for a few seconds.

These frozen fruit pops are an excellent way to cool off on a hot summer day. They are customizable, delicious, and make a fun treat for both kids and adults alike. Final Thoughts As you prepare to host your summer BBQ, remember that desserts hold the power to elevate your gathering and leave lasting memories. Fruit-based desserts not only offer bursts of flavor but also celebrate the vibrant produce of the season. Explore these recipes, put your spin on them, and enjoy the smiles that come when your guests take that first bite. Whether you choose to grill juicy peaches, whip up glamorous berry parfaits, or serve chilled fruit pops, your summer BBQ will be a hit with these delightful fruit desserts. So gather your friends, fire up the grill, and enjoy the delicious tastes of summer!
