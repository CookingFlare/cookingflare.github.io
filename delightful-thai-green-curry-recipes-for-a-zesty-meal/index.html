Delightful Thai Green Curry Recipes for a Zesty Meal


Are you craving a zesty and flavorful meal that will transport you to the vibrant streets of Thailand? Look no further than these delightful Thai Green Curry recipes. Bursting with aromatic herbs, spices, and the perfect balance of heat, these recipes will satisfy your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

1. Classic Thai Green Curry

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 pound boneless chicken breasts or tofu, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, drained
  • 1 cup bell peppers, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and halved
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (omit for vegetarian/vegan option)
  • 2 teaspoons palm sugar or brown sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan or wok, heat the green curry paste over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth, stirring well to combine.
  3. Increase the heat to medium-high and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Add the sliced chicken or tofu, bamboo shoots, bell peppers, and green beans to the pan, stirring gently to coat them in the curry sauce.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the curry simmer for 15-20 minutes until the chicken is cooked through or the tofu is heated.
  6. Stir in the fish sauce (if using) and palm sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  7. Serve the Thai green curry over steamed jasmine rice, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

2. Vegetarian Thai Green Curry with Eggplant

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 medium eggplant, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, drained
  • 1 cup bell peppers, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons palm sugar or brown sugar
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the green curry paste in a large saucepan or wok over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk and vegetable broth, stirring well to combine.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the eggplant, bamboo shoots, bell peppers, and snow peas to the pan, stirring gently to coat them in the curry sauce.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the curry simmer for 15-20 minutes until the eggplant is tender.
  6. Stir in the soy sauce and palm sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  7. Serve the vegetarian Thai green curry over steamed jasmine rice, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

3. Thai Green Curry Soup with Shrimp

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can (14 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup baby corn, halved
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 2 teaspoons palm sugar or brown sugar
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the green curry paste in a large saucepan or wok over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk and chicken or vegetable broth, stirring well to combine.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the shrimp, mushrooms, baby corn, and cherry tomatoes to the pan, stirring gently to coat them in the curry sauce.
  5. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the curry simmer for 5-8 minutes until the shrimp is cooked through.
  6. Stir in the fish sauce and palm sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  7. Serve the Thai green curry soup hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

Final Thoughts

These delightful Thai Green Curry recipes are a testament to the rich and bold flavors of Thai cuisine. Whether you prefer chicken, tofu, or seafood, there’s a recipe here to suit your taste. So why not bring a taste of Thailand into your kitchen and indulge in a zesty and satisfying meal? Give these recipes a try and let your taste buds embark on a culinary adventure. Enjoy!
