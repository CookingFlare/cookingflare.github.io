Spring Brunch Recipes Using Seasonal Produce


Spring brings a refreshing palette of vibrant and fresh produce that can transform any brunch into a delightful celebration of the season. The arrival of tender asparagus, sweet peas, radishes, strawberries, ramps, and herbs like mint and chives inspire creative dishes that are light, colorful, and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re hosting a casual weekend gathering or looking to impress with a leisurely brunch spread, incorporating spring’s bounty elevates every plate.

Here’s a collection of fabulous spring brunch recipes that highlight the best seasonal ingredients. These dishes balance textures and flavors, from crunchy and crisp to creamy and rich, all showcasing the natural sweetness and freshness of spring veggies and fruits.

Asparagus and Pea Frittata with Mint

This frittata captures the essence of spring with tender asparagus spears, sweet garden peas, and a hint of fresh mint. It’s an easy-to-make, delightful option for brunch, suitable for serving warm or at room temperature.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup whole milk or cream
  • 1 cup fresh asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup fresh or thawed peas
  • 2 tablespoons butter or olive oil
  • 1/3 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat butter or olive oil in an ovenproof skillet over medium heat. Add asparagus and peas, sautéing for 3-4 minutes until just tender.
  3. In a bowl, whisk eggs and milk together, seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet, sprinkle feta cheese and chopped mint on top.
  5. Cook without stirring for about 3-4 minutes until the edges start to set.
  6. Transfer skillet to oven and bake for 10-12 minutes or until the frittata is puffed, golden, and fully set.
  7. Remove from oven, let cool slightly, then slice and serve.

Pro tip: Pair with a light green salad dressed in lemon vinaigrette for a complete springtime meal.

Strawberry and Rhubarb Compote with Vanilla Yogurt

Rhubarb and strawberries are the quintessential duo of spring. This warm compote made with fresh rhubarb and strawberries is perfect spooned over vanilla yogurt, pancakes, or waffles for a refreshing, fruity brunch addition.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 2 cups rhubarb stalks, chopped into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Vanilla yogurt or Greek yogurt, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine strawberries, rhubarb, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the fruit breaks down and the mixture thickens slightly, about 10-12 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat, stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Serve warm or chilled over bowls of vanilla yogurt or as a topping for your favorite breakfast breads.

Enhancement: Add a sprinkle of toasted oats or chopped nuts on top for added crunch and texture.

Radish and Herb Butter Tartines on Rustic Bread

Radishes are gloriously crisp and peppery, perfect for springtime. Paired with creamy herb butter and hearty rustic bread, these open-faced sandwiches (tartines) are elegant yet simple.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf rustic bread, sliced and toasted
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 bunch radishes, thinly sliced
  • Coarse sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: microgreens or pea shoots for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine softened butter, chives, dill, and lemon zest. Mix well.
  2. Spread a generous layer of herb butter over toasted bread slices.
  3. Arrange radish slices over the butter, overlapping slightly.
  4. Sprinkle with coarse sea salt, black pepper, and garnish with microgreens.
  5. Serve immediately as a crunchy, creamy springtime snack or small bite.

Serving suggestion: These tartines pair beautifully with a crisp white wine or sparkling water infused with cucumber.

Spring Vegetable Quinoa Salad with Lemon-Dill Dressing

Bright, colorful, and packed with nutrients, this quinoa salad is a stellar option for brunch, showcasing roasted spring vegetables tossed with a zesty lemon-dill dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup asparagus, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas, trimmed and halved
  • 1 small bunch radishes, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup baby spinach or arugula
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup toasted slivered almonds

Lemon-Dill Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa in water or broth according to package instructions. Fluff and let cool.
  2. Blanch asparagus and sugar snap peas in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then plunge into ice water to retain crispness and color.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooled quinoa, blanched vegetables, radishes, greens, and dill.
  4. Whisk together dressing ingredients, then toss with the salad.
  5. Sprinkle toasted almonds on top before serving to add a satisfying crunch.

This salad can be made ahead and tastes even better once flavors have melded for a few hours.

Lemon-Poppy Seed Pancakes with Warm Berry Sauce

Welcome spring mornings with fluffy lemon-poppy seed pancakes paired with a warm, medley berry sauce made from seasonal strawberries and blueberries. These pancakes deliver a bright, citrusy punch, perfect for a decadent but fresh brunch.

Pancakes Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Warm Berry Sauce:

  • 1 cup strawberries, chopped
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, lemon zest, and poppy seeds.
  2. In another bowl, combine buttermilk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  3. Pour wet ingredients into dry and stir gently until just combined (do not overmix).
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake, cook until bubbles form on top, then flip and cook until golden.
  6. For the berry sauce, combine strawberries, blueberries, syrup, and lemon juice in a small saucepan. Warm on low until berries soften and sauce thickens slightly, about 5 minutes.
  7. Serve pancakes hot, drizzled generously with warm berry sauce.

Tip: A dollop of whipped cream or mascarpone adds a creamy counterpoint to the tangy berries and citrus.

Ramp and Goat Cheese Crostini

Ramps are a treasured spring ingredient with their garlicky, oniony flavor. These crostini are fast to prepare and make a stunning appetizer or brunch nibble rich with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 baguette, sliced diagonally and toasted
  • 1 cup fresh ramps, cleaned and roughly chopped
  • 4 oz soft goat cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet and sauté ramps for 3-4 minutes until tender but still vibrant green.
  2. Spread goat cheese on toasted baguette slices.
  3. Top with sautéed ramps, seasoning lightly with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve immediately.

Variation: Add a drizzle of honey or balsamic glaze for a sweet note balancing the earthiness.

Fresh Herb and Lemon Ricotta Toast with Pea Shoots

Ricotta toast is a decadent yet simple way to enjoy spring flavors. The addition of lemon and fresh herbs along with crunchy pea shoots brings brightness and texture to this easy brunch option.

Ingredients:

  • Whole grain or sourdough bread, toasted
  • 1 cup fresh ricotta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped mixed fresh herbs (chives, parsley, basil)
  • 1 cup fresh pea shoots
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix ricotta, lemon zest, lemon juice, herbs, salt, and pepper until creamy and smooth.
  2. Spread the lemon-herb ricotta liberally over toasted bread.
  3. Top with fresh pea shoots, drizzle with olive oil.
  4. Serve immediately.

The lightness of pea shoots and the creaminess of ricotta make this an inviting starter or side to any brunch spread.

Bringing Spring to Your Brunch Table

These spring brunch recipes emphasize vibrant, seasonal produce to celebrate the best this time of year can offer. From hand-picked herbs to freshly harvested asparagus and strawberries, each recipe is designed to let the quality of the ingredients shine.

When planning your next weekend brunch, consider featuring these dishes as a showcase of spring’s bounty. They’re fresh, flavorful, and satisfying without being heavy—a perfect balance after the long winter months.

Enjoy experimenting with these recipes and savor the bright, crisp flavors that make spring brunch truly special!
