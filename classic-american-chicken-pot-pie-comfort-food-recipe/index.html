Classic American Chicken Pot Pie: Comfort Food Recipe


When it comes to classic American comfort food, few dishes evoke as much nostalgia as chicken pot pie. This savory pie, filled with tender chicken, hearty vegetables, and a creamy sauce, wrapped in a flaky crust, is the embodiment of warmth and home-cooked love. Whether it’s a chilly autumn evening or a casual family gathering, chicken pot pie is the ultimate dish that brings everyone to the table.

The History of Chicken Pot Pie

The origins of chicken pot pie can be traced back to ancient civilizations. The Greeks and Romans created early versions of meat-filled pastry dishes, but the modern American chicken pot pie has its roots in the 19th century. It became a popular dish among American settlers, who adapted the recipe to include local ingredients like chicken and seasonal vegetables. Over the years, chicken pot pie has evolved, but its comforting essence remains the same.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This classic American chicken pot pie recipe combines simple ingredients with rich flavors, making it a go-to dish for any occasion. Here are a few reasons why you’ll fall in love with it:

  1. Versatile Ingredients: You can customize the filling with your favorite vegetables and seasonings.
  2. Make-Ahead Friendly: It’s perfect for meal prep and can be frozen for later enjoyment.
  3. Crowd-Pleaser: This dish is a hit with both kids and adults alike, making it ideal for family dinners.

Ingredients

To make a delicious chicken pot pie, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded: You can use rotisserie chicken for convenience.
  • 1 cup carrots, diced: Fresh or frozen carrots work well.
  • 1 cup peas: Frozen peas are a great option.
  • 1 cup potatoes, diced: Use Yukon Gold or russet potatoes for the best texture.
  • 1/2 cup celery, diced: Adds a lovely crunch to the filling.
  • 1/2 cup onion, diced: Sweet or yellow onions enhance the flavor.
  • 3 tablespoons butter: For sautéing the vegetables.
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour: To thicken the sauce.
  • 2 cups chicken broth: Homemade or store-bought broth is fine.
  • 1 cup milk: For creaminess.
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme: Adds an aromatic flavor.
  • Salt and pepper to taste: Essential for seasoning.

For the Crust:

  • 1 package refrigerated pie crusts: This saves time, but you can make your own if preferred.
  • 1 egg, beaten: For egg wash to give the crust a beautiful golden color.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the diced onions, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

  2. Add the Flour: Sprinkle the flour over the sautéed vegetables, stirring to combine. Cook for an additional minute to eliminate the raw flour taste.

  3. Create the Sauce: Gradually whisk in the chicken broth and milk, stirring continuously. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, about 5 minutes.

  4. Mix in Chicken and Seasoning: Add the shredded chicken, peas, potatoes, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine. Remove from heat and let it cool while you prepare the crust.

Step 2: Assemble the Pie

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Prepare the Pie Dish: Unroll one pie crust and place it in a 9-inch pie dish, pressing it gently against the bottom and sides.

  3. Fill the Pie: Spoon the chicken filling into the pie crust, making sure to distribute it evenly.

  4. Top with Crust: Unroll the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Trim any excess crust hanging over the edges. Pinch the edges of the crusts together to seal and cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  5. Egg Wash: Brush the beaten egg over the top crust for a golden finish.

Step 3: Bake the Pie

  1. Bake: Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  2. Cool Before Serving: Once out of the oven, let the pie cool for about 10-15 minutes. This allows the filling to set, making it easier to slice.

Tips for the Perfect Chicken Pot Pie

  • Use Leftover Chicken: This recipe is perfect for using up leftover rotisserie chicken or turkey.
  • Add Your Favorite Vegetables: Feel free to customize the filling with vegetables like corn, green beans, or even mushrooms for added flavor.
  • Homemade Crust: If you prefer a homemade crust, use a simple pastry dough recipe, and chill before rolling it out.
  • Make it Ahead: You can prepare the filling and store it in the fridge for up to two days before assembling the pie.

Storage and Reheating

To store leftover chicken pot pie, cover it tightly with plastic wrap or aluminum foil and place it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. You can also freeze the unbaked pie for up to 3 months. When ready to bake, simply thaw it overnight in the refrigerator and bake as directed.

To reheat, place slices in the microwave or reheat the entire pie in a 350°F (175°C) oven until warmed through.

Conclusion

Chicken pot pie is a timeless classic that brings comfort and joy to any meal. With its rich filling and flaky crust, it’s no wonder this dish holds a special place in the hearts of many. Whether it’s a weeknight dinner or a special occasion, this chicken pot pie recipe is sure to impress. Gather your loved ones, serve up a slice of this delicious comfort food, and enjoy the warmth and happiness it brings to your table. Happy cooking!
