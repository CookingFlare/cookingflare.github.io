Savory Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry Recipe


Beef and broccoli stir-fry is a flavorful and nutritious dish that has captured the hearts of many food enthusiasts worldwide. Whether you’re looking to prepare a quick dinner on a busy weeknight or want to impress your guests with a delicious and healthy homemade meal, this savory beef and broccoli stir-fry recipe is the perfect choice. With tender beef, vibrant broccoli, and a rich, savory sauce, this dish is an irresistible combination of taste and texture.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of preparing this dish at home, from the ingredients you’ll need to the methods that will ensure your stir-fry turns out perfect every time. Let’s dive into the world of savory stir-fries!

Ingredients for Savory Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Before you get started, it’s important to gather all of the necessary ingredients. These include fresh, simple ingredients that come together to create a delicious, hearty meal.

For the Beef and Marinade:

  • 1 lb (450g) of flank steak or sirloin beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for a gluten-free version)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 2 cups of fresh broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any neutral oil of your choice)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, freshly grated
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup beef broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch (to thicken the sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes (optional, for some heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving (optional)

Preparation: Marinating the Beef

To get that tender and flavorful beef, marinating is a crucial step. This allows the beef to absorb the flavors and results in a more succulent stir-fry. Begin by preparing your beef. Choose cuts like flank steak or sirloin for the best results in stir-frying; they’re tender and quick to cook, making them ideal for this dish.

  1. Slice the Beef: Thinly slice the beef against the grain. The thinner the slices, the more quickly they’ll cook and the tenderer they’ll be.
  2. Marinate the Beef: In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, cornstarch, sesame oil, rice vinegar, brown sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Place the sliced beef in a bowl and pour the marinade over it. Toss to coat the beef evenly and let it marinate for at least 15 minutes. For optimal flavor, marinate it for 30 minutes to 1 hour in the refrigerator.

While the beef marinates, you can prepare the vegetables and the stir-fry sauce.

Preparing the Vegetables

In stir-fry recipes, fresh vegetables not only add flavor but also contribute texture, color, and nutrition. For this dish, the key vegetable is broccoli, but adding bell pepper and onion enhances the taste and presentation of the meal.

  1. Prepare the Broccoli: Cut the broccoli into bite-sized florets, making sure they’re all approximately the same size so they cook evenly.
  2. Slice the Bell Pepper: Remove the seeds and slice the red bell pepper into thin strips.
  3. Prepare the Onion: Peel the onion and slice it thinly, making sure to separate the rings for even cooking.

Set these vegetables aside while you prepare the sauce.

Making the Savory Stir-Fry Sauce

The sauce is what brings everything together in a stir-fry, and it’s important to get the right balance of salty, sweet, and savory. This sauce is the key to the dish’s irresistible flavor.

  1. Prepare the Stir-Fry Sauce: In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, and beef broth (or water). Stir well to mix the ingredients.
  2. Cornstarch Slurry: In another small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a couple of tablespoons of water to create a slurry. This will help thicken the sauce during cooking.

Now that your beef is marinated, your vegetables are prepped, and your sauce is ready, you’re all set for the stir-fry!

Stir-Frying the Beef and Vegetables

Stir-frying is a fast cooking method that ensures the beef and vegetables retain their texture and flavor. It’s important to work quickly and efficiently, so make sure all your ingredients are within arm’s reach.

  1. Heat the Pan: Heat a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and swirl it around to coat the pan.
  2. Cook the Beef: Once the pan is hot, add the marinated beef in a single layer. Let it sear for about 1-2 minutes, undisturbed, to get a nice caramelization. Stir the beef to cook all sides evenly, about 2-3 more minutes. Once cooked, remove the beef from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Stir-Fry the Vegetables: In the same pan, add another tablespoon of vegetable oil. Add the sliced onion, bell pepper, and broccoli florets. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, until the vegetables begin to soften but still have a nice crunch. Add the garlic and ginger to the pan and stir for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add the Sauce: Return the beef to the pan along with the prepared stir-fry sauce. Pour in the cornstarch slurry and stir everything together. Let the sauce simmer for 2-3 minutes until it thickens and coats the beef and vegetables nicely.
  5. Taste and Adjust: Taste the stir-fry and adjust the seasoning as needed with salt, pepper, and chili flakes if you like a bit of heat.

Once everything is well combined and the sauce is glossy and thick, your savory beef and broccoli stir-fry is ready to serve.

Serving Your Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

This savory dish can be served in a variety of ways. For a complete meal, serve it over a bed of steamed rice, quinoa, or noodles. The rice helps soak up the savory sauce and balances out the dish. Garnish the stir-fry with sesame seeds, chopped green onions, or cilantro for added flavor and visual appeal.

If you want to make the dish even more filling, try adding other vegetables like carrots, snap peas, or mushrooms. You can also experiment with protein by substituting beef with chicken, shrimp, or tofu, depending on your preference.

Additional Tips for the Perfect Stir-Fry

  • Use a High-Heat Oil: Stir-frying requires cooking at high temperatures, so it’s important to use a high-heat oil such as vegetable oil, grapeseed oil, or peanut oil.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: For a crispy texture, avoid overcrowding the pan. If necessary, cook the beef in batches to ensure it sears properly.
  • Fresh Ingredients: Fresh garlic, ginger, and vegetables make a big difference in flavor, so don’t cut corners when it comes to the quality of your ingredients.
  • Make Ahead: This stir-fry is great for meal prep! You can cook the beef and vegetables ahead of time and store them in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. When you’re ready to eat, simply reheat the stir-fry in a pan or microwave and enjoy.

Why You’ll Love This Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry Recipe

This savory beef and broccoli stir-fry recipe is a perfect combination of convenience, flavor, and nutrition. It’s packed with lean protein, fiber-rich vegetables, and a mouthwatering sauce that brings it all together. Whether you’re preparing a quick meal for your family or hosting a dinner party, this stir-fry is sure to please everyone.

Plus, it’s versatile! You can make it as spicy or mild as you like, and experiment with different vegetables or proteins to suit your tastes. It’s a well-balanced dish that’s not only delicious but also easy to make in less than 30 minutes. What’s not to love?

So, the next time you’re in the mood for something savory, wholesome, and satisfying, give this beef and broccoli stir-fry a try. It’s a guaranteed crowd-pleaser that you’ll want to make again and again!

Enjoy your homemade stir-fry, and happy cooking!
