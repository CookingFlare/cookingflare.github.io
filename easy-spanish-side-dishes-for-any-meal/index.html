Easy Spanish Side Dishes for Any Meal


When it comes to Spanish cuisine, the flavor profile is as rich as its history, offering an array of tantalizing side dishes that can elevate any meal. Whether you’re planning a casual family dinner or a festive gathering with friends, these easy-to-prepare Spanish side dishes will undoubtedly impress your guests and bring a touch of Spain to your table. Let’s dive into some delightful recipes that are not only simple to make but also bursting with flavor.

1. Patatas Bravas

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt, to taste
  • For the sauce:
    • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
    • 2 tablespoons ketchup
    • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional)
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Potatoes: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add the cubed potatoes and cook for about 10-15 minutes until just tender. Drain and set aside.

  2. Fry the Potatoes: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the drained potatoes and season with smoked paprika and salt. Fry until golden and crispy on the outside, about 10 minutes.

  3. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, combine mayonnaise, ketchup, hot sauce, and garlic powder. Mix well until smooth.

  4. Serve: Serve the crispy potatoes hot, drizzled with the spicy sauce. Patatas Bravas are perfect as a side or a tapas dish for sharing.

2. Spanish Rice (Arroz)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  2. Toast the Rice: Add the rice to the saucepan, stirring well to coat with the oil and onion mixture. Toast the rice for about 2-3 minutes until slightly golden.

  3. Add Broth and Tomatoes: Pour in the broth and add the diced tomatoes, saffron, and paprika. Stir to combine and season with salt and pepper.

  4. Cook the Rice: Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  5. Fluff and Serve: Remove from heat and let sit covered for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

3. Pan con Tomate (Bread with Tomato)

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of rustic bread (like ciabatta or sourdough)
  • 2 ripe tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toast the Bread: Lightly toast the slices of bread until golden and crisp. You can use a grill or a toaster for this.

  2. Prepare the Tomatoes: Cut the tomatoes in half and grate the flesh into a bowl, discarding the skins. If the tomatoes are very juicy, you may want to drain some of the excess liquid.

  3. Garlic Rub: Once the bread is toasted, rub a garlic clove over each slice for added flavor.

  4. Assemble: Spoon the grated tomato onto each slice of bread. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil and sprinkle with salt. Add fresh basil or parsley if desired.

  5. Serve: Serve immediately as a refreshing side or appetizer.

4. Spanish Chickpea Salad (Ensalada de Garbanzos)

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, red onion, bell pepper, cucumber, and parsley.

  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, cumin, salt, and pepper.

  3. Dress the Salad: Pour the dressing over the chickpea mixture and stir gently to coat everything evenly.

  4. Chill and Serve: Let the salad chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

5. Escalivada (Roasted Vegetables)

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 green bell pepper
  • 1 eggplant
  • 1 zucchini
  • 1 onion
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil or oregano (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Vegetables: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the whole bell peppers, eggplant, zucchini, and onion on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.

  2. Bake: Roast in the oven for about 30-40 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred.

  3. Peel and Slice: Once roasted, let the vegetables cool slightly. Peel the skin off the peppers and eggplant, and slice them into strips.

  4. Serve: Arrange the roasted vegetables on a platter, drizzle with more olive oil, and garnish with fresh herbs.

6. Spanish Potato Salad (Ensaladilla Rusa)

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • Olives for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Boil the Vegetables: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the cubed potatoes and diced carrots. Cook until tender, about 10-15 minutes. Add the frozen peas in the last 2 minutes of cooking. Drain and let cool.

  2. Mix the Salad: In a large bowl, combine the cooled potatoes, carrots, and peas. Add mayonnaise, mustard, salt, and pepper, mixing gently until well combined.

  3. Add Eggs: Fold in the chopped hard-boiled eggs, reserving some for garnish if desired.

  4. Chill and Serve: Refrigerate for at least an hour before serving. Garnish with olives if using.

7. Grilled Asparagus with Romesco Sauce

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For the Romesco sauce:
    • 1 red bell pepper, roasted and peeled
    • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted
    • 2 cloves garlic
    • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
    • 1 teaspoon paprika
    • Olive oil, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Asparagus: Preheat the grill to medium-high. Toss asparagus with olive oil, salt, and pepper.

  2. Grill: Grill asparagus for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and slightly charred.

  3. Make the Romesco Sauce: In a food processor, combine roasted red pepper, almonds, garlic, vinegar, and paprika. Blend until smooth, adding olive oil to achieve desired consistency. Season with salt to taste.

  4. Serve: Serve the grilled asparagus drizzled with the Romesco sauce for a vibrant and flavorful side dish.

Conclusion

These easy Spanish side dishes are perfect for any meal, whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a quiet evening at home. With their vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and simple preparation methods, these recipes will undoubtedly become staples in your cooking repertoire. So gather your ingredients, channel your inner Spanish chef, and get ready to impress your family and friends with these delightful side dishes! Enjoy your culinary journey through Spain without leaving your kitchen.
