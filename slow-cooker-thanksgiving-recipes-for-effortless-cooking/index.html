Slow Cooker Thanksgiving Recipes for Effortless Cooking


Thanksgiving is a celebration of gratitude, family, and indulgent meals that warm both the heart and the home. For many, creating the perfect Thanksgiving feast can be equal parts rewarding and overwhelming—there’s nothing quite like juggling oven timings, stovetop burners, and last-minute side dishes all at once. For those looking to simplify their holiday cooking without compromising flavor, the slow cooker is an unsung hero that deserves a place in the spotlight.

With the ability to effortlessly handle everything from appetizers to entrées and even desserts, slow cooker recipes are ideal for hosting the ultimate Thanksgiving meal without stressing over constant supervision. Let your ingredients simmer to perfection while you focus on decorating the dinner table or enjoying time with loved ones.

Below, you’ll find a collection of slow cooker Thanksgiving recipes that promise to deliver mouthwatering results with minimal effort—the perfect fusion of comfort and convenience.

Classic Slow Cooker Turkey Breast

No Thanksgiving meal feels complete without turkey at the center of the table. Preparing a turkey breast in a slow cooker results in tender, juicy meat that’s perfectly seasoned and easy to carve. Plus, it frees up oven space for other dishes!

Ingredients:

  • 4–5 lbs turkey breast (bone-in or boneless)
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth or turkey stock
  • 1 lemon, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves, peeled

Instructions:

  1. Gently pat the turkey breast dry with paper towels.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the butter with garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, paprika, salt, and black pepper until well combined.
  3. Rub the mixture generously over the turkey breast, ensuring every inch is coated with seasoning.
  4. Place the lemon quarters and garlic cloves at the bottom of the slow cooker. Pour the broth over them.
  5. Place the turkey breast on top and cover the slow cooker with the lid.
  6. Cook on low heat for 6–7 hours or on high heat for 3–4 hours, until the turkey reaches an internal temperature of 165°F.
  7. Allow the turkey to rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with gravy or cranberry sauce for maximum flavor.

Buttery Slow Cooker Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are an irresistible side dish, but they often require stovetop boiling that eats up space and attention. This simplified version lets the slow cooker do all the work, producing dreamy mashed potatoes that melt in your mouth.

Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs Yukon Gold or Russet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Add the cubed potatoes to the slow cooker and sprinkle them with 1 teaspoon of salt.
  2. Place the butter cubes over the potatoes. Cover and cook on high heat for 4–5 hours until the potatoes are soft and fork-tender.
  3. Drain any excess liquid if necessary.
  4. Mash the potatoes with a potato masher or immersion blender.
  5. Gradually stir in the heavy cream, adjusting quantity to achieve your desired consistency.
  6. Season with garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Keep warm in the slow cooker until ready to serve.

Savory Slow Cooker Stuffing

Getting stuffing just right can be tricky, but using a slow cooker ensures it stays moist, flavorful, and perfectly cooked. This classic stuffing recipe complements turkey and gravy effortlessly.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of bread, cubed and slightly dried (about 10 cups)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 ½ cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 tbsp dried sage
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Melt the butter in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onion and celery until they’re soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the bread cubes, cooked vegetables, sage, thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix until evenly distributed.
  3. Whisk together the broth and eggs, then pour the mixture over the bread cubes. Toss gently to coat.
  4. Transfer the mixture to the slow cooker and cover. Cook on low heat for 4 hours. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Adjust seasonings if needed. Serve warm alongside turkey and gravy.

Slow Cooker Green Bean Casserole

A beloved Thanksgiving staple, green bean casserole can easily be adapted for slow cooker convenience. Tender green beans coated in a creamy sauce and topped with crispy fried onions make this dish a must-have.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ lbs fresh or frozen green beans, trimmed
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup fried onions

Instructions:

  1. Combine the green beans, cream of mushroom soup, milk, soy sauce, salt, and pepper in the slow cooker. Mix well.
  2. Cover and cook on low heat for 4 hours or high heat for 2 hours. Stir occasionally.
  3. Before serving, sprinkle the fried onions on top. Allow them to heat slightly before serving warm.

Slow Cooker Pumpkin Pie Pudding

No Thanksgiving table is complete without dessert, and this slow cooker pumpkin pie pudding offers all the flavors of traditional pie in a hassle-free format.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 1 can (12 oz) evaporated milk
  • 3 eggs
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, evaporated milk, eggs, sugar, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into the slow cooker.
  3. Cover and cook on low heat for 5 hours or high heat for 3 hours, until the pudding sets and the edges begin to pull away from the sides.
  4. Cool slightly before serving with whipped cream.

Holiday Cooking Made Easier

Thanksgiving is a time to create memories, savor loved ones’ company, and enjoy delicious food that brings everyone together. Slow cooker recipes offer a stress-free solution to crafting the perfect holiday meal, ensuring you spend less time in the kitchen and more time at the table.

With these effortless slow cooker Thanksgiving recipes, you’ll have everything you need to serve a feast that’s flavorful, comforting, and truly special. Whether you’re cooking for a gathering of many or a cozy dinner for a few, the slow cooker is your trusted ally this holiday season.
