Mastering the Art of Making Korean Seafood Pancakes


Korean seafood pancakes, known as Haemul Pajeon, are a delicious and savory treat that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re craving a light and crispy snack or a delightful appetizer to accompany a meal, mastering the art of making Korean seafood pancakes will open up a world of culinary delights. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of Haemul Pajeon, the key ingredients, and step-by-step instructions to create this delectable dish in the comfort of your own kitchen.

The History of Haemul Pajeon

Haemul Pajeon has a rich history deeply rooted in Korean culinary tradition. Dating back to the Joseon Dynasty, this savory pancake was initially created as a way to make the most of the available ingredients, especially seafood. Over the years, it has evolved into a popular dish enjoyed by many, both in Korea and around the world.

Key Ingredients

For the Pancake Batter:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups cold water
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup assorted seafood (shrimp, squid, and/or clams), chopped
  • 1 cup scallions, chopped
  • 1/2 cup onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup carrot, julienned

Dipping Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (gochugaru)

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Pancake Batter

In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, cold water, egg, salt, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Whisk the ingredients together until you have a smooth and slightly thick batter. Set the batter aside while you prepare the filling.

2. Prepare the Filling

In a separate bowl, mix together the assorted seafood, scallions, onion, and carrot. Ensure that the seafood and vegetables are evenly distributed to create a balanced filling for the pancakes.

3. Combine the Batter and Filling

Gently fold the prepared filling into the pancake batter, ensuring that the seafood and vegetables are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

4. Cook the Pancakes

Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a generous amount of vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, pour a ladleful of the pancake batter into the skillet, spreading it out evenly to form a round pancake. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the pancake is golden brown and crispy.

5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce

While the pancakes are cooking, prepare the dipping sauce by combining soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sesame seeds, and red pepper flakes in a small bowl. Mix well to ensure the flavors are fully blended.

6. Serve and Enjoy

Once the pancakes are cooked to perfection, transfer them to a serving plate and cut them into manageable slices. Serve the pancakes warm, accompanied by the prepared dipping sauce for an extra burst of flavor.

In Conclusion

Mastering the art of making Korean seafood pancakes is a delightful journey that culminates in a mouthwatering dish that’s perfect for sharing with family and friends. With a perfect balance of seafood, vegetables, and a crispy exterior, Haemul Pajeon is a true delight for the senses. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure that will transport you to the heart of Korean cuisine. Enjoy the process, relish the flavors, and savor every bite of these delectable seafood pancakes.
