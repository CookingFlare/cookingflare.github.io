Heavenly Hummingbird Cake Recipe: A Southern Classic with a Twist


If you’re a fan of classic Southern desserts, then you’re in for a treat with this Heavenly Hummingbird Cake recipe. This beloved cake is a staple in Southern kitchens, known for its moist, flavorful crumb and delightful combination of tropical ingredients. Traditionally, a Hummingbird Cake features bananas, pineapples, and pecans, all coming together to create a truly indulgent dessert. However, this recipe takes the classic to new heights with a twist that will surprise and delight your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup crushed pineapple, drained
  • 2 cups mashed ripe bananas
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1 cup chopped pecans
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour three 9-inch round cake pans.

  2. In a large bowl, sift together the flour, sugar, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.

  3. In a separate bowl, combine the beaten eggs, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.

  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.

  5. Fold in the crushed pineapple, mashed bananas, shredded coconut, and chopped pecans.

  6. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared cake pans.

  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.

  8. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to wire racks to cool completely.

  9. While the cakes are cooling, prepare the cream cheese frosting. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, beating until the frosting is creamy and smooth.

  10. Once the cakes are completely cooled, spread a layer of cream cheese frosting between each layer, stacking the cakes on top of each other.

  11. Use the remaining frosting to generously cover the top and sides of the cake.

  12. Optional: garnish with additional chopped pecans or a sprinkle of shredded coconut for added flair.

The Twist

Now, for the twist that sets this Heavenly Hummingbird Cake apart from the traditional version. Instead of the usual cream cheese frosting, consider infusing the frosting with a hint of rum or coconut for a tropical flavor profile. This unexpected addition adds a layer of sophistication and a delightful twist to the classic recipe, elevating it to a whole new level of indulgence.

Serving Suggestions

This Heavenly Hummingbird Cake is perfect for any occasion, from casual gatherings to formal celebrations. Pair a slice of this decadent cake with a cup of freshly brewed coffee for a delightful afternoon treat, or serve it as the grand finale to a special dinner party. No matter the occasion, this cake is sure to impress and satisfy your guests.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Heavenly Hummingbird Cake with a twist is a delightful homage to the classic Southern dessert. Its combination of tropical flavors and unexpected twists makes it a true standout, sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or new to the world of cake baking, this recipe is accessible and rewarding, offering a slice of Southern charm with a modern twist. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the heavenly flavors of this delightful cake.
