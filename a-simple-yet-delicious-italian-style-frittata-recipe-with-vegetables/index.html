A Simple yet Delicious Italian-Style Frittata Recipe with Vegetables


Are you craving a wholesome and flavorful dish that’s quick and easy to prepare? Look no further than this delightful Italian-style frittata recipe with vegetables. Bursting with vibrant colors and rich flavors, this dish is perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even a light dinner. Plus, it’s a great way to use up any leftover vegetables you have in your fridge. So let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs, such as basil or parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk until well combined. Set aside.

  3. Heat the olive oil in a non-stick, oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent, about 2-3 minutes.

  4. Add the sliced bell pepper and zucchini to the skillet, and cook for another 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables have softened slightly.

  5. Gently pour the egg mixture over the sautéed vegetables in the skillet. Make sure the vegetables are evenly distributed throughout the frittata.

  6. Arrange the cherry tomato halves on top of the frittata, cut side up. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the frittata is set and slightly golden on top.

  8. Remove the skillet from the oven and let the frittata cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh herbs, if desired.

  9. Slice the frittata into wedges and serve warm. It’s delicious on its own or accompanied by a side salad or crusty bread.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize your frittata by adding your favorite vegetables. Spinach, mushrooms, asparagus, or even leftover roasted potatoes work wonderfully in this recipe.
  • If you prefer a cheesy frittata, sprinkle some grated Parmesan or crumbled feta cheese on top before baking.
  • For a heartier meal, you can add cooked bacon, ham, or sausage to the vegetable mixture before pouring in the egg mixture.
  • Leftover frittata can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it in the microwave or enjoy it cold as a tasty and nutritious snack.

Final Thoughts

This Italian-style frittata recipe with vegetables is a versatile and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed any time of the day. With its colorful medley of fresh vegetables and fluffy eggs, it’s sure to be a hit with both veggie lovers and meat enthusiasts alike. So why not give it a try and savor the flavors of Italy in the comfort of your own kitchen? Buon appetito!
