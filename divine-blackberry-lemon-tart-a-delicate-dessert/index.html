Divine Blackberry Lemon Tart: A Delicate Dessert


There’s nothing quite as delightful as a perfectly balanced dessert that combines the tartness of lemons with the sweetness of blackberries. If you’re a fan of citrusy flavors and crave a dessert that’s both refreshing and indulgent, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to dive into the world of Divine Blackberry Lemon Tart. This delicate dessert is a feast for the senses, with its vibrant colors, tangy lemon filling, and juicy blackberries. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get ready to create a masterpiece!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tablespoon of ice water

For the lemon filling:

  • 3/4 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon zest
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, cubed

For the blackberry topping:

  • 2 cups of fresh blackberries
  • 1/4 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. In a food processor, combine the flour, sugar, and salt. Pulse a few times to mix.
  2. Add the cold cubed butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and ice water. Pour it into the food processor and pulse until the dough comes together.
  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and shape it into a disk. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Rolling out the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle slightly larger than your tart pan.
  3. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to your tart pan, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough.
  4. Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork and place it in the freezer for 15 minutes.
  5. Once chilled, line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans.
  6. Bake the crust for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper. Bake for an additional 10 minutes or until golden brown. Set aside to cool.

Step 3: Making the Lemon Filling

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the lemon juice, lemon zest, sugar, cornstarch, and egg yolks.
  2. Place the saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the cubed butter. Stir until melted and well combined.
  4. Pour the lemon filling into the cooled tart crust and spread it evenly.

Step 4: Creating the Blackberry Topping

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the blackberries, sugar, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the blackberries release their juices and the mixture thickens slightly.
  3. Remove from heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
  4. Carefully spoon the blackberry topping over the lemon filling, spreading it evenly.

Step 5: Chilling and Serving

  1. Place the tart in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or until set.
  2. Once chilled, remove from the refrigerator and garnish with additional fresh blackberries, if desired.
  3. Slice into wedges and serve this divine dessert to your lucky guests!

Conclusion

The Divine Blackberry Lemon Tart is a delicate dessert that combines the tangy goodness of lemons with the sweet burst of blackberries. The buttery crust provides the perfect foundation for the luscious lemon filling, while the blackberry topping adds a beautiful pop of color and flavor. This refreshing and indulgent dessert is guaranteed to impress your family and friends at any gathering. So, channel your inner pastry chef and savor every bite of this exquisite treat. Happy baking!
