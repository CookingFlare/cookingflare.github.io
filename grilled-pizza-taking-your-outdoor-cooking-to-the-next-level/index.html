Grilled Pizza: Taking Your Outdoor Cooking to the Next Level


When it comes to outdoor cooking, few dishes can rival the excitement and satisfaction of grilled pizza. The combination of a crispy crust, gooey cheese, and savory toppings, all cooked to perfection over an open flame, is a culinary experience like no other. In this post, we’ll explore the art of grilled pizza, from the basics of dough preparation to the secrets of achieving the perfect char.

The Importance of Dough

While it’s possible to use store-bought pizza dough for grilled pizza, the real magic happens when you make your own. With a few simple ingredients – flour, yeast, water, salt, and olive oil – you can create a crust that’s both crispy and chewy. The key is to use a high-gluten flour, such as bread flour or all-purpose flour with a high protein content, which will give your crust its signature texture.

To make your own dough, combine 2 cups of flour, 1 teaspoon of yeast, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large mixing bowl. Gradually add 1 cup of warm water, stirring until a shaggy dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Preparing the Grill

While your dough is rising, it’s time to prepare your grill. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat, or around 425°F (220°C). If you have a gas grill, make sure to clean the grates with a wire brush to prevent sticking. If you have a charcoal grill, make sure to spread the coals evenly and adjust the vents to maintain a consistent temperature.

Shaping the Dough

Once your dough has risen, it’s time to shape it into a ball. Gently punch down the dough and divide it into 2-4 equal portions, depending on the size of pizza you prefer. Roll out each portion into a thin circle, about 1/4 inch thick. Place the dough on a piece of parchment paper or a lightly floured pizza peel, if you have one.

Toppings and Assembly

The toppings are where the magic happens. Choose your favorite combinations of cheese, meats, vegetables, and herbs to create a pizza that’s truly unique. Some popular toppings include mozzarella cheese, pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, bell peppers, and olives. Don’t be afraid to get creative and add your own favorite ingredients to the mix.

Once you’ve assembled your toppings, place the dough on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the crust starts to char and the cheese starts to melt. Rotate the pizza 90 degrees to ensure even cooking, and continue cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Tips and Variations

Here are a few tips and variations to take your grilled pizza to the next level:

  • Use a pizza stone: A pizza stone can help to distribute heat evenly and prevent the crust from burning.
  • Try different cheeses: While mozzarella is a classic choice, other cheeses like parmesan, gorgonzola, and feta can add unique flavors and textures to your pizza.
  • Add some spice: A pinch of red pepper flakes or a sprinkle of oregano can add a burst of flavor to your pizza.
  • Get creative with toppings: Don’t be afraid to try new and unusual toppings, such as pineapple, prosciutto, or arugula.

Conclusion

Grilled pizza is a culinary experience that’s sure to impress. With a few simple ingredients and some basic cooking techniques, you can create a pizza that’s truly unforgettable. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, the art of grilled pizza is a journey that’s worth taking. So fire up your grill, gather your friends and family, and get ready to take your outdoor cooking to the next level.
