Cozy Winter Ham and Potato Chowder


As the chilly winter weather sets in, there’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting bowl of chowder to lift your spirits. This Cozy Winter Ham and Potato Chowder recipe is the perfect blend of hearty ingredients and rich flavors, making it an ideal dish for cozy nights in or festive gatherings. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just getting started in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress and satisfy your cravings for a delicious, soul-warming meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of cooked ham, diced
  • 4 cups of potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup of chopped yellow onion
  • 1 cup of chopped celery
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced ham, chopped onion, and celery. Sauté for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

  2. Sprinkle the flour over the ham and vegetables, stirring to coat everything evenly. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to allow the flour to cook slightly.

  3. Gradually pour in the chicken broth while stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Add the cubed potatoes, minced garlic, dried thyme, and paprika. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

  4. Bring the chowder to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the chowder has thickened.

  5. Once the potatoes are cooked through, stir in the whole milk and simmer for an additional 5 minutes to heat through. Adjust the seasoning if needed.

  6. Remove the chowder from the heat and let it sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Ladle the Cozy Winter Ham and Potato Chowder into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh parsley. Serve hot and enjoy the heartwarming flavors of this comforting dish.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a creamier chowder, you can use half-and-half or heavy cream instead of whole milk.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables such as corn, carrots, or peas to the chowder for added color and texture.
  • If you prefer a smokier flavor, you can use smoked ham or add a touch of liquid smoke to the chowder.
  • Leftover chowder can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it gently on the stovetop or in the microwave before serving.

There you have it – a delightful Cozy Winter Ham and Potato Chowder recipe to warm you up on those cold winter days. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a comforting meal for yourself, this chowder is a fantastic choice that will fill your home with cozy aromas and bring smiles to the faces of your loved ones. Enjoy every spoonful of this soul-soothing dish and embrace the joy of winter comfort food!
