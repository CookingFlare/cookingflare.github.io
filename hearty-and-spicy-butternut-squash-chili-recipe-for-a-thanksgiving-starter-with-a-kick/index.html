Hearty and Spicy Butternut Squash Chili Recipe for a Thanksgiving Starter with a Kick


In the midst of the bustling holiday season, it’s easy to get caught up in the whirlwind of preparation and planning. But, let’s not forget the importance of a delicious and memorable Thanksgiving starter. This year, why not shake things up with a twist on the classic chili recipe? Introducing our Hearty and Spicy Butternut Squash Chili Recipe, a flavorful and satisfying dish that’s sure to delight your guests.

The Inspiration

As the leaves begin to change and the crisp autumn air sets in, our taste buds crave something warm, comforting, and filling. Butternut squash, with its sweet and nutty flavor, is the perfect ingredient to add depth and complexity to our chili. By combining it with the bold flavors of chipotle peppers, cumin, and smoked paprika, we create a dish that’s both familiar and excitingly new.

The Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash (about 2 lbs), peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 oz) red kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (16 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced green chilies
  • 1 tsp chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • 1/4 cup crumbled queso fresco, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the butternut squash with 1 tbsp olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper until well coated. Spread the squash on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until tender and caramelized.
  3. In a large Dutch oven or pot, heat the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until translucent, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Add the roasted butternut squash, red bell pepper, diced tomatoes, red kidney beans, black beans, diced green chilies, chipotle peppers, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir to combine.
  5. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let cook for 20-25 minutes, or until the flavors have melded together and the chili has thickened slightly.
  6. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  7. Serve the chili hot, garnished with chopped cilantro and crumbled queso fresco, if desired.

The Result

This Hearty and Spicy Butternut Squash Chili Recipe is a game-changer for your Thanksgiving starter. The roasted butternut squash adds a sweet and comforting element, while the chipotle peppers provide a bold and spicy kick. The cumin, smoked paprika, and cinnamon add a depth of flavor that’s both familiar and excitingly new. This dish is sure to become a new family favorite, and its versatility makes it perfect for a crowd.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, add 1-2 cups of cooked ground turkey or beef to the chili.
  • Substitute the red bell pepper with diced jalapeño or Anaheim pepper for an extra spicy kick.
  • Add a splash of apple cider vinegar or lime juice to brighten the flavors.
  • Serve the chili with a dollop of sour cream, shredded cheese, or crumbled tortilla chips for added texture and flavor.

In this busy holiday season, it’s the little things that count. A delicious and memorable starter can make all the difference in setting the tone for a wonderful Thanksgiving celebration. This Hearty and Spicy Butternut Squash Chili Recipe is sure to be a hit, and its unique flavors will leave your guests talking long after the holiday is over. So, go ahead and give it a try – your taste buds will thank you!
