Delicious Nut-Free Raspberry Crumble Bars Recipe


When it comes to baking, there’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of creating a dish that’s both delicious and visually appealing. And that’s exactly what you get with these nut-free raspberry crumble bars. Made with fresh raspberries, a crunchy oat and brown sugar topping, and a hint of vanilla, these bars are the perfect treat for any occasion.

Ingredients:

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water

For the filling:

  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the crumble topping:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup chopped dried cranberries (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line an 8-inch square baking dish with parchment paper or aluminum foil, leaving some overhang for easy removal.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball.
  3. Press the dough evenly into the prepared baking dish.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the raspberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and salt. Let it sit for about 15 minutes, until the raspberries start to release their juice and the mixture becomes syrupy.
  5. Pour the raspberry mixture over the crust, spreading it out evenly.
  6. In another bowl, mix together the rolled oats, brown sugar, and cold butter until crumbly. If using dried cranberries, stir them in at this point.
  7. Spread the crumble topping evenly over the raspberry filling.
  8. Bake the bars for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the topping is crispy.
  9. Remove the bars from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan. Then, lift them out of the pan using the parchment paper or foil and cut them into squares.

Tips and Variations:

  • To make these bars more festive, you can add a few drops of red food coloring to the raspberry mixture to give it a pink hue.
  • If you prefer a less sweet bar, you can reduce the amount of granulated sugar in the filling to 1/4 cup.
  • You can also substitute the raspberries with other berries, such as blueberries or strawberries, for a different flavor profile.
  • To make individual servings, simply cut the bars into smaller squares and serve them on a plate or in a cup.

Nutrition Information:

Per serving (1 bar):

  • Calories: 220
  • Fat: 12g
  • Saturated fat: 8g
  • Cholesterol: 20mg
  • Sodium: 100mg
  • Carbohydrates: 25g
  • Fiber: 2g
  • Sugar: 15g
  • Protein: 2g

These nut-free raspberry crumble bars are perfect for anyone with nut allergies or intolerances. The crunchy oat and brown sugar topping provides a satisfying texture without the need for nuts, and the fresh raspberries add a burst of flavor and color. Whether you’re looking for a sweet treat to enjoy on your own or a dessert to share with friends and family, these bars are sure to please. So go ahead, give them a try, and indulge in the sweet and tangy goodness of these delicious nut-free raspberry crumble bars!
