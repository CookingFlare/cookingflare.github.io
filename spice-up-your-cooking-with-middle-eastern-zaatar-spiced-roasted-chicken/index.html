Spice Up Your Cooking with Middle Eastern Za'atar-Spiced Roasted Chicken


If you’re looking to add a burst of flavor to your traditional roasted chicken, look no further than the Middle Eastern spice blend called Za’atar. This aromatic and versatile blend of herbs and spices will take your roasted chicken to a whole new level. In this recipe, we will explore how to create a delicious Za’atar-spiced roasted chicken that will wow your taste buds and impress your guests.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (about 4 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons Za’atar spice blend
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. Clean the chicken thoroughly, removing any giblets or excess fat. Pat it dry with paper towels.

  3. In a small bowl, combine the Za’atar spice blend, olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Mix well to form a paste.

  4. Rub the Za’atar paste all over the chicken, making sure to get it under the skin and in the cavity.

  5. Place the sliced lemon inside the chicken cavity for added flavor.

  6. Transfer the seasoned chicken to a roasting pan, breast-side up.

  7. Roast the chicken in the preheated oven for about 1 hour and 15 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).

  8. Baste the chicken with the pan juices every 20 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful.

  9. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes before carving.

  10. Serve the Za’atar-spiced roasted chicken with your favorite side dishes and enjoy!

Why Za’atar?

Za’atar is a traditional Middle Eastern spice blend that adds a unique and vibrant flavor to any dish. It typically consists of dried herbs such as thyme, oregano, and marjoram, combined with sesame seeds, sumac, and salt. The combination of these ingredients creates a savory and tangy flavor profile that pairs exceptionally well with roasted chicken.

The Za’atar spice blend not only adds incredible taste to your dish but also brings a range of health benefits. Thyme, one of the main ingredients in Za’atar, is rich in antioxidants and has antimicrobial properties. Sumac, another key ingredient, is packed with vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C and potassium.

Serving Suggestions

The Za’atar-spiced roasted chicken is a versatile dish that can be served with a variety of side dishes. Here are a few suggestions to complement the flavors of the chicken:

  • Roasted vegetables: Serve the chicken with a medley of roasted vegetables, such as carrots, potatoes, and zucchini. The earthy flavors of the vegetables will complement the Za’atar spice blend beautifully.

  • Couscous: Prepare a fluffy bowl of couscous seasoned with herbs and spices. The light and fluffy texture of couscous pairs well with the robust flavors of the Za’atar-spiced chicken.

  • Greek salad: Create a refreshing Greek salad with cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, and feta cheese. The tangy flavors of the salad will balance the savory notes of the chicken.

Conclusion

Spice up your cooking repertoire with this delicious and aromatic Za’atar-spiced roasted chicken. The combination of the Middle Eastern flavors and the succulent roasted chicken will transport your taste buds to a whole new culinary experience. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to elevate your weeknight meals, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and embark on a flavorful journey with Za’atar-spiced roasted chicken.
