Homemade Focaccia Bread: Soft and Aromatic


Welcome to another delicious recipe on our cooking blog! Today, we’re going to explore the wonderful world of homemade focaccia bread. This soft and aromatic bread is a staple in Italian cuisine and is sure to impress your family and friends. So, let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 350ml warm water
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, active dry yeast, sugar, and salt. Mix well to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  2. Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the warm water. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a sticky dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Add more flour if the dough is too sticky.

  4. Place the kneaded dough back into the mixing bowl and drizzle a generous amount of olive oil over it. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 to 2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Grease a baking sheet or line it with parchment paper.

  6. Transfer the risen dough onto the prepared baking sheet and gently press it down with your fingertips, spreading it evenly to cover the entire sheet. If desired, dimple the surface of the dough with your fingers to create small pockets.

  7. Drizzle more olive oil over the surface of the dough, making sure it gets into the dimples. Sprinkle coarse sea salt over the top, allowing it to enhance the flavor.

  8. If desired, garnish the focaccia with fresh rosemary sprigs for added aroma and visual appeal.

  9. Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for about 20 to 25 minutes, or until it turns golden brown on top.

  10. Once baked, remove the focaccia from the oven and let it cool slightly before slicing and serving. Enjoy it warm with your favorite dips, soups, or simply on its own.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more flavored focaccia, you can add garlic powder, dried oregano, or grated Parmesan cheese to the dough before kneading.

  • If you prefer a softer crust, brush the baked focaccia with melted butter as soon as it comes out of the oven.

  • Get creative with toppings! Besides rosemary and sea salt, you can experiment with cherry tomatoes, olives, caramelized onions, or even thinly sliced potatoes.

  • Leftover focaccia can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. To reheat, simply place it in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for a few minutes until warmed through.

So there you have it - a simple and delicious recipe for homemade focaccia bread. With its soft texture and aromatic flavors, this bread is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re serving it as an appetizer, a side dish, or a snack, it’s sure to be a hit. So why not give it a try and let your taste buds indulge in the wonderful flavors of homemade focaccia bread? Enjoy!
