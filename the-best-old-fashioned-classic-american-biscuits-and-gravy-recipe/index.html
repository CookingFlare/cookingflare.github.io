The Best Old-Fashioned Classic American Biscuits and Gravy Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes evoke the warmth of home like an old-fashioned plate of biscuits and gravy. This beloved American classic is a staple in Southern kitchens and breakfast tables across the nation. Rich, creamy, and utterly satisfying, biscuits and gravy can transform any morning into a special occasion. In this post, we’ll dive deep into the history, ingredients, and step-by-step instructions to create the best old-fashioned classic American biscuits and gravy that will have your taste buds singing.

A Brief History of Biscuits and Gravy

The origins of biscuits and gravy date back to the early 19th century in the Southern United States. This dish was born out of necessity during the post-Civil War era when food was scarce. Biscuits, a quick bread made with flour, baking powder, and fat, were easy to prepare and could be made in bulk. The gravy, typically made from sausage drippings and milk, provided a hearty topping to accompany the biscuits.

Over the years, biscuits and gravy have become a breakfast favorite, especially in the South, where they are often served with a side of eggs and crispy bacon. The dish has evolved into various regional variations, but the core elements remain the same: flaky biscuits smothered in a savory, creamy gravy.

The Perfect Biscuit: Ingredients and Techniques

To create the best old-fashioned biscuits, you’ll need the right ingredients and techniques. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients for Biscuits

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour: This forms the base of your biscuits.
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder: This leavening agent helps the biscuits rise.
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda: A little extra lift.
  • 1 teaspoon salt: Enhances the flavor of the biscuits.
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter: Cold and cut into small cubes. Butter adds richness and flakiness.
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk: For moisture and a tangy flavor. If you don’t have buttermilk, you can make a substitute by adding one tablespoon of vinegar or lemon juice to regular milk and letting it sit for about 10 minutes.

Tips for Flaky Biscuits

  1. Use Cold Ingredients: The key to flaky biscuits is to keep your butter and buttermilk cold. This helps create steam when baking, which results in a lighter texture.
  2. Don’t Overwork the Dough: Mix the ingredients until just combined. Overworking the dough can lead to dense biscuits.
  3. Use a Light Touch: When rolling out the dough, be gentle. The less you handle it, the fluffier your biscuits will be.

Step-by-Step Biscuit Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). A hot oven is crucial for achieving that perfect rise.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.

Step 3: Cut in the Butter

Add the cold butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter, fork, or your fingers, cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. It’s okay to have some small pieces of butter visible; they’ll create those delicious flaky layers.

Step 4: Add Buttermilk

Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and pour in the buttermilk. Gently stir with a spatula or wooden spoon until the dough begins to come together.

Step 5: Turn Out the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Using your hands, gently bring the dough together and pat it into a rectangle about 1-inch thick.

Step 6: Fold and Roll

To create layers, fold the dough in thirds like a letter. Gently roll it out again to about 1-inch thick. Repeat the folding and rolling process two more times. This technique will enhance the flakiness of your biscuits.

Step 7: Cut the Biscuits

Using a floured biscuit cutter or a glass, cut out biscuits from the dough. Press straight down without twisting to ensure they rise properly. Place the biscuits on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving a little space between each one.

Step 8: Bake

Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown on top. Remove from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving.

The Creamy Sausage Gravy: Ingredients and Techniques

No biscuits are complete without a generous ladle of sausage gravy. The gravy is rich, savory, and perfectly complements the flaky biscuits. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients for Sausage Gravy

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage: You can use mild or spicy, depending on your preference.
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour: This will help thicken the gravy.
  • 2 cups milk: Whole milk is ideal for a creamy texture.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • Optional: A pinch of cayenne pepper for added heat.

Tips for Perfect Gravy

  1. Brown the Sausage: Cook the sausage until it’s well browned. This adds depth of flavor to your gravy.
  2. Create a Roux: The flour needs to cook slightly to remove the raw taste. Stir it in with the sausage before adding the milk.
  3. Whisk Constantly: This will help prevent lumps and ensure a smooth gravy.

Step-by-Step Gravy Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Sausage

In a large skillet over medium heat, add the breakfast sausage. Cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.

Step 2: Add Flour

Once the sausage is cooked, sprinkle the flour over the sausage. Stir to combine, cooking for an additional 1-2 minutes. This creates a roux that will help thicken the gravy.

Step 3: Add Milk

Slowly pour in the milk while whisking continuously to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring frequently, until the gravy thickens to your desired consistency, about 5-10 minutes.

Step 4: Season

Taste the gravy and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and cayenne pepper if desired. The gravy should be creamy, savory, and packed with flavor.

Serving Your Biscuits and Gravy

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making both biscuits and gravy, it’s time to bring it all together. Split the warm biscuits in half and generously spoon the sausage gravy over the top. For an extra touch, you can garnish with chopped fresh parsley or a sprinkle of paprika for color.

Pairing Suggestions

While biscuits and gravy are delicious on their own, you can elevate your breakfast with some sides. Consider serving with:

  • Scrambled or fried eggs
  • Crispy bacon or sausage links
  • Fresh fruit for a refreshing contrast

Storing Leftovers

If you find yourself with leftover biscuits and gravy (though it’s unlikely!), here’s how to store them:

  • Biscuits: Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. To reheat, wrap in foil and warm in a 350°F (175°C) oven until heated through.
  • Gravy: Refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat on the stovetop over low heat, adding a splash of milk to restore creaminess.

Conclusion

There you have it: the best old-fashioned classic American biscuits and gravy recipe that’s sure to bring a smile to your face and warmth to your belly. This dish is not just a meal; it’s a tradition, a comfort, and a reminder of home. Whether enjoyed on a lazy Sunday morning or as a hearty breakfast for dinner, biscuits and gravy never go out of style. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and enjoy the delightful process of creating this quintessential American dish. Happy cooking!
