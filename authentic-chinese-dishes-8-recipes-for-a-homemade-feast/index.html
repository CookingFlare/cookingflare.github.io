Authentic Chinese Dishes: 8 Recipes for a Homemade Feast


Chinese cuisine, with its rich history and diverse regional flavors, has captivated food lovers around the world. From the bold and spicy dishes of Sichuan to the delicate and flavorful meals of Cantonese cooking, Chinese food offers an incredible range of tastes, textures, and aromas. If you’re craving an authentic homemade Chinese feast, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll explore 8 timeless Chinese recipes that are sure to bring the flavors of China right into your kitchen.

1. Kung Pao Chicken

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 6 dried red chilies
  • 2 teaspoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1/2 cup roasted peanuts
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, combine soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, and sugar. Add the chicken cubes and let them marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat the oil: In a large wok or skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Cook the aromatics: Add the dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns to the oil. Stir-fry them until fragrant.
  4. Cook the chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the wok. Stir-fry until golden and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add vegetables and peanuts: Add the garlic, ginger, onion, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes. Toss in the roasted peanuts for added crunch.
  6. Final touches: Season with salt and pepper, then serve immediately. Enjoy the combination of tender chicken, savory sauce, and crunchy peanuts.

2. Mapo Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 400g soft tofu, cut into cubes
  • 200g ground pork (optional for a meatier dish)
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon doubanjiang (fermented bean paste)
  • 2 tablespoons chili paste
  • 2 teaspoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Toast the peppercorns: In a dry pan, toast the Sichuan peppercorns over medium heat until fragrant, then grind them using a mortar and pestle or spice grinder.
  2. Cook the pork: Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or skillet. Add the ground pork and stir-fry until browned.
  3. Add the aromatics: Add the garlic and ginger to the pan and cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the seasonings: Add the doubanjiang and chili paste, stirring to combine. Let it cook for a minute.
  5. Add the tofu: Gently add the tofu cubes into the wok, being careful not to break them. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  6. Thicken the sauce: In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a bit of water to create a slurry. Stir this into the wok to thicken the sauce.
  7. Final touches: Sprinkle the ground Sichuan peppercorns and chopped green onions over the dish. Serve with steamed rice.

3. Peking Duck

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole duck (about 2-3 kg)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 20-25 thin Chinese pancakes (or tortillas as a substitute)
  • Cucumber, julienned
  • Spring onions, cut into strips

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the duck: Start by washing the duck and patting it dry. Rub the entire duck with five-spice powder, making sure to coat it evenly.
  2. Glaze the duck: Mix honey, soy sauce, and rice vinegar in a small bowl. Brush this mixture generously over the duck.
  3. Roast the duck: Place the duck on a rack in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) and roast for about 1.5-2 hours, basting with the honey sauce every 30 minutes.
  4. Serve the duck: Once the duck is golden and crispy, carve it into thin slices. Serve the slices with the hoisin sauce, julienned cucumber, spring onions, and pancakes.
  5. Assemble: Place a few slices of duck on the pancake, add a bit of hoisin sauce, and top with cucumber and spring onions. Roll it up and enjoy!

4. Sweet and Sour Pork

Ingredients:

  • 400g pork tenderloin, cut into bite-sized cubes
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1/2 cup pineapple chunks
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Coat the pork: Toss the pork cubes in cornstarch, making sure they are fully coated.
  2. Fry the pork: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat. Fry the pork cubes until golden and crispy. Remove and set aside.
  3. Prepare the sauce: In a bowl, mix the rice vinegar, ketchup, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Cook the vegetables: In the same pan, sauté the garlic, onion, and bell pepper for about 2-3 minutes until soft.
  5. Combine and simmer: Add the pineapple chunks and the sauce. Stir to combine, bringing it to a simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Finish the dish: Add the fried pork back into the pan and toss to coat in the sauce. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined. Serve hot over steamed rice.

5. Chow Mein (Stir-Fried Noodles)

Ingredients:

  • 200g fresh egg noodles (or dry noodles, cooked according to package instructions)
  • 150g chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 100g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup carrots, julienned
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the noodles: If you’re using dry noodles, cook them according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Cook the protein: Heat vegetable oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and shrimp, stir-frying until cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  3. Stir-fry the vegetables: In the same wok, add the garlic, onion, and carrots, stir-frying until tender.
  4. Combine the noodles: Add the cooked noodles, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Stir well to combine.
  5. Finish the dish: Add the cooked chicken, shrimp, cabbage, and bean sprouts. Toss everything together and cook for another 2 minutes. Serve hot and enjoy!

6. Hot and Sour Soup

Ingredients:

  • 300g shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 200g tofu, sliced into thin strips
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil. Add the soy sauce, rice vinegar, chili paste, and sugar.
  2. Add the mushrooms and bamboo shoots: Stir in the mushrooms and bamboo shoots, and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Thicken the soup: Mix the cornstarch with a little water to create a slurry, then stir it into the broth to thicken the soup.
  4. Add the tofu: Gently add the tofu and let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Finish the soup: Pour the beaten egg into the soup while stirring gently to create egg ribbons. Drizzle with sesame oil and garnish with green onions. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

7. Dim Sum (Pork Dumplings)

Ingredients:

  • 250g ground pork
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice wine
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 package of dumpling wrappers
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil for steaming

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, combine ground pork, chopped cabbage, soy sauce, rice wine, ginger, and sugar. Mix well to combine.
  2. Assemble the dumplings: Place a teaspoon of the filling onto the center of each dumpling wrapper. Fold and seal the edges, pleating as necessary.
  3. Steam the dumplings: Heat a steamer over medium-high heat. Place the dumplings in the steamer basket and steam for 10-12 minutes, until the filling is cooked through.
  4. Serve: Serve the dumplings hot with soy sauce for dipping.

8. Egg Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably cold, day-old rice works best)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil for stir-frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the rice: If using freshly cooked rice, spread it on a tray and let it cool for a few hours or refrigerate overnight.
  2. Cook the eggs: Heat vegetable oil in a wok over medium heat. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked, then remove them from the wok.
  3. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add more oil to the wok and stir-fry the peas, carrots, and onions until tender.
  4. Add the rice: Add the cold rice to the wok, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until heated through.
  5. Season and serve: Add soy sauce, sesame oil, and scrambled eggs back into the wok. Toss everything together, then garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot.

Conclusion

Chinese cuisine is an exciting culinary adventure with endless possibilities for flavor and variety. Whether you enjoy the spicy heat of Sichuan dishes or the savory richness of Cantonese meals, there is always something to satisfy every palate. These 8 recipes will bring the authenticity of Chinese cooking into your home, allowing you to enjoy the delicious tastes of China with your loved ones. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and start cooking today!
