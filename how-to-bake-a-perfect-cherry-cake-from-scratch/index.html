How to Bake a Perfect Cherry Cake from Scratch


Baking a cherry cake from scratch can be an incredibly satisfying and rewarding experience. The combination of tender, moist cake and the sweet burst of juicy cherries creates a delightful dessert that’s perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re baking for a special celebration or simply treating yourself, a homemade cherry cake is sure to impress. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of baking a perfect cherry cake, from choosing the right ingredients to mastering each step of the baking process.

Ingredients You Will Need

Before you begin, ensure you have the following ingredients on hand. These items will form the base for your cherry cake:

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (softened)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sour cream (or Greek yogurt for a tangier twist)
  • 1 ½ cups fresh or frozen cherries (pitted)

For the Cherry Filling (optional but recommended):

  • 1 cup cherries (pitted and chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Topping (optional):

  • Powdered sugar (for dusting)

Tools You Will Need:

  • 9-inch round cake pan (or any preferred shape)
  • Mixing bowls
  • Hand mixer or stand mixer
  • Spatula
  • Parchment paper
  • Cooling rack

Step 1: Preparing the Cherry Filling (Optional)

If you’re aiming for an extra burst of cherry flavor in your cake, it’s a great idea to prepare a cherry filling. This step is optional, but it enhances the flavor of the cake and helps to keep the cherries from sinking to the bottom during baking.

Start by combining your chopped cherries with sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice in a small saucepan. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly. As the cherries cook, they will release their juices, and the cornstarch will thicken the mixture, turning it into a syrupy filling. Allow it to simmer for 5-7 minutes until it reaches a slightly thickened consistency. Once done, remove the pan from the heat and set it aside to cool.

Step 2: Preheat the Oven and Prepare Your Pan

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease your 9-inch round cake pan with butter and lightly dust it with flour to prevent the cake from sticking. Alternatively, you can line the bottom with parchment paper for easy removal later.

Step 3: Mixing the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized mixing bowl, sift together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Sifting these ingredients will ensure they’re evenly distributed throughout the cake batter, resulting in a lighter, fluffier texture.

Step 4: Creaming the Butter and Sugar

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar using a hand mixer or stand mixer. Start on a low speed to prevent the sugar from flying out, then gradually increase the speed. Beat the butter and sugar for 3-4 minutes, or until the mixture is light and fluffy. This step is crucial as it helps to incorporate air into the batter, giving the cake a soft, tender crumb.

Step 5: Adding the Eggs and Vanilla Extract

Next, add the eggs, one at a time, to the creamed butter and sugar mixture. Beat well after each addition to ensure the eggs are fully incorporated. After the eggs, add the vanilla extract, mixing it in until well combined.

Step 6: Incorporating the Sour Cream (or Yogurt)

Now, add the sour cream (or Greek yogurt) to the wet mixture. This ingredient is key to achieving a moist, flavorful cake. It also adds a slight tang, which balances the sweetness of the cherries. Mix until smooth and fully combined.

Step 7: Combining the Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing at a low speed to avoid flour spills. Alternate between adding dry ingredients and the sour cream mixture. Start with a small amount of the dry ingredients, followed by some of the sour cream, and repeat until all the ingredients are combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this could result in a dense cake.

Step 8: Folding in the Cherries

Once the batter is fully mixed, fold in the cherries gently using a spatula. If you’re using fresh cherries, make sure they’re pitted and roughly chopped into small chunks. If you’re using frozen cherries, allow them to thaw and drain the excess liquid to prevent the cake batter from becoming too runny. Carefully fold the cherries into the batter, ensuring they’re evenly distributed but without breaking them apart too much.

Step 9: Pouring the Batter into the Pan

Pour the cherry-laden batter into the prepared cake pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. If you made a cherry filling earlier, you can swirl this through the batter at this point. Use a spoon to gently drop spoonfuls of the filling over the batter, then use a knife or skewer to swirl it throughout the cake, creating a marbled effect.

Step 10: Baking the Cherry Cake

Place the cake pan in the preheated oven and bake for 30-40 minutes. The exact time may vary depending on your oven and the size of your pan, so it’s important to check the cake regularly. After 30 minutes, test the cake by inserting a toothpick or cake tester into the center. If it comes out clean or with a few moist crumbs, the cake is done. If not, continue baking for an additional 5-10 minutes and test again.

While baking, you may notice the cherry cake starting to brown on top. This is a good sign, indicating that the cake is cooking evenly. However, if the top begins to brown too quickly, you can cover it loosely with foil to prevent over-browning.

Step 11: Cooling the Cake

Once the cake is fully baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10-15 minutes. Afterward, carefully remove the cake from the pan and place it on a cooling rack to cool completely. Allow the cake to cool fully before adding any toppings or serving it.

Step 12: Adding the Finishing Touches

Once the cake has cooled, you can dust the top with powdered sugar for an elegant finishing touch. The powdered sugar adds a subtle sweetness and a lovely appearance. If you have any leftover cherry filling, you can drizzle it on top or serve it on the side for added flavor.

Step 13: Serving the Cherry Cake

Slice the cake into wedges and serve it on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The cherry cake is incredibly versatile and pairs well with both sweet and savory accompaniments.

This cherry cake is perfect for birthdays, family gatherings, or any time you want a delicious, homemade treat. The burst of cherries in every bite adds a fresh and vibrant flavor to the moist, fluffy cake. Whether you choose to bake it as a single-layer cake or double it for a layered version, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your baking repertoire.

Tips for Success

  • Use Fresh or Frozen Cherries: Fresh cherries are always a great option if they’re in season. If using frozen cherries, ensure they’re thawed and drained well to avoid excess moisture in the batter.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to a dense and tough cake. Mix just until the ingredients are combined for a lighter texture.
  • Check for Doneness: Always check your cake with a toothpick to make sure it’s fully cooked through before removing it from the oven.
  • Chill the Filling: If using a cherry filling, make sure it’s fully cooled before swirling it into the batter to avoid disturbing the texture of the cake.

Conclusion

Baking a cherry cake from scratch is a wonderful way to enjoy the fresh, tart flavors of cherries in a delightful dessert. The process is relatively simple and results in a moist, flavorful cake that can be enjoyed by anyone with a sweet tooth. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, this step-by-step guide ensures you can bake the perfect cherry cake every time. Give this recipe a try for your next celebration or when you want a homemade treat that will impress friends and family. Happy baking!
