Picnic Quiches: Savory and Portable Options


quiche, a versatile and delicious dish, is a perfect fit for a picnic. its savory flavors and flaky crust make it a great option for a outdoor gathering. in this post, we will explore different savory and portable quiche options that are perfect for a picnic.

classic quiche lorraine

quiche lorraine is a classic french dish that consists of eggs, cream, and lardons (small pieces of bacon) baked in a pie crust. it’s a simple yet flavorful dish that is easy to make and transport. to make quiche lorraine, you will need:

  • 1 pie crust
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 6 slices of bacon, diced
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (such as cheddar or parmesan)

preheat your oven to 375°f (190°c). roll out the pie crust and place it in a pie dish. in a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, cream, salt, and pepper. add the diced bacon and grated cheese to the egg mixture and stir until well combined. pour the egg mixture into the pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the crust is golden brown.

spinach and feta quiche

for a vegetarian option, you can make a spinach and feta quiche. this quiche is a great way to use up any leftover spinach and feta cheese you may have on hand. to make spinach and feta quiche, you will need:

  • 1 pie crust
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

preheat your oven to 375°f (190°c). roll out the pie crust and place it in a pie dish. in a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, cream, salt, and pepper. add the chopped spinach and crumbled feta cheese to the egg mixture and stir until well combined. pour the egg mixture into the pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the crust is golden brown.

mushroom and gruyère quiche

for a savory and earthy option, you can make a mushroom and gruyère quiche. this quiche is a great way to use up any leftover mushrooms and gruyère cheese you may have on hand. to make mushroom and gruyère quiche, you will need:

  • 1 pie crust
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup grated gruyère cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

preheat your oven to 375°f (190°c). roll out the pie crust and place it in a pie dish. in a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, cream, salt, and pepper. add the sliced mushrooms and grated gruyère cheese to the egg mixture and stir until well combined. pour the egg mixture into the pie crust and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the quiche is set and the crust is golden brown.

tips and variations

  • to make quiches more portable, you can cut them into wedges or slices before serving.
  • you can also add other ingredients to your quiche, such as diced ham or bell peppers, to make it more flavorful.
  • if you don’t have heavy cream, you can substitute it with whole milk or half-and-half.
  • quiches can be made ahead of time and refrigerated or frozen for later use.
  • you can also make individual quiches in muffin tin cups for a more bite-sized option.

conclusion

quiche is a versatile and delicious dish that is perfect for a picnic. with its savory flavors and flaky crust, it’s a great option for a outdoor gathering. by following the recipes and tips outlined in this post, you can make a variety of savory and portable quiches that are sure to please.
