10 Simple Pickling Recipes to Elevate Your Home-cooked Meals


Making your own pickles can elevate your home-cooked meals significantly. Not only do homemade pickles provide a burst of freshness to your dishes, but they also give you full control over the quality and taste. Let’s dive into ten simple and versatile pickling recipes to make your culinary game even more exciting.

  1. Quick Dill Pickles

Dill pickles are a classic and versatile pickling recipe. To make these, combine 1/2 cup of apple cider vinegar with 1 tablespoon of salt and 1 teaspoon of black peppercorns per pint jar. Fill the jar with sliced cucumbers and fresh dill before boiling a water bath for 15 minutes.

  1. Spicy Kimchi

Kimchi is a Korean staple and offers a delicious punch of flavor. Mix equal parts cabbage and radish, along with 2 tablespoons of salt and 2 tablespoons of minced ginger. Then, add 1 tablespoon of fermented chili paste, and let this mixture sit at room temperature for 1-3 days. Refrigerate for at least a week.

  1. Sweet Pickled Red Onions

Give a Mexican touch to your dishes with these sweet pickled red onions. Combine 1 pint of sliced red onions with 1/4 cup of white vinegar, 1/4 cup of brown sugar, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1 teaspoon of black peppercorns. Let the mixture sit in a sealed container for 4-6 hours before enjoying.

  1. Garlic Pickled Beets

Beets have an inherent earthy taste, and pickling them with garlic makes for a flavorful side dish. Boil 1/2 cup of apple cider vinegar and 1 teaspoon of salt for 10 minutes before adding sliced beets and minced garlic. Let the mixture sit in a sealed container for 2-3 days before enjoying.

  1. Easy Bread and Butter Pickles

Originating in the Midwestern United States, these pickles provide a sweet and tangy flavor. Combine sliced cucumbers with equal parts sugar and apple cider vinegar. Add a pinch of turmeric, celery seeds, and mustard seeds, along with 1 teaspoon of salt. Let this mixture sit for at least a week before enjoying.

  1. Pickled Golden Raisins

Pickled raisins can add a unique, sweet and tangy flavor to salads or sandwiches. Soak the raisins in boiling water before filling a sealed container with equal parts golden raisins, apple cider vinegar, and honey. Let the mixture sit for 1-2 weeks before eating.

  1. Southwestern Corn Relish

Corn relish provides a zesty and unique flavor that can elevate any meal. Mix diced tomatoes, chopped bell peppers, and frozen corn with white sugar, white vinegar, and apple cider vinegar. Add celery seeds and mustard seeds before storing the mixture in the refrigerator for at least a day.

  1. Dilled Cherries

Dilled cherches are a perfect addition to both meats and salads. Combine halved cherries with equal parts apple cider vinegar and water. Add 2 tablespoons of dill pickling spice, along with 1 teaspoon of salt. Let the cherries sit for about 2 weeks before indulging.

  1. Pickled Jalapenos

These spicy pickles provide a kick to sandwiches and salads alike. Fill a pint jar with sliced jalapenos before adding white sugar, white vinegar, and a pinch of salt. Boil a water bath for 10 minutes for a tangible spicy kick.

  1. Quick Spiced Carrots

Spiced carrots can provide a tangy and unique element to any dish. Combine sliced carrots with white vinegar, honey, and 1 teaspoon of salt. Let the mixture steep in a sealed container for 4-6 hours before enjoying.

These ten simple recipes showcase the versatility of pickling. From cucumbers to cherries, few ingredients remain untouchable when transformed into homemade pickles. Discover the world of unique flavors at home and enjoy creating your signature pickling recipes.
