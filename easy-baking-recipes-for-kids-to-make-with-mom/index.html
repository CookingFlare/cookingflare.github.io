Easy Baking Recipes for Kids to Make with Mom


Baking can be a fun and educational activity for kids. Not only does it allow them to tap into their creativity, but it also helps them develop important skills like reading, measuring, and following instructions. Plus, baking together with mom creates priceless memories that will last a lifetime. In this blog post, we will explore some easy baking recipes that kids can make with their moms.

1. Chocolate Chip Cookies

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 10-12 minutes
Total Time: 25-27 minutes

Nothing beats the classic chocolate chip cookie. It’s a crowd-pleaser and a perfect recipe for beginners. To make these delicious cookies, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 cup of softened butter
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 cups of chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until well combined.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, mixing well after each addition.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto ungreased baking sheets.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to wire racks to cool completely.

2. Mini Pizzas

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 10-12 minutes
Total Time: 25-27 minutes

Kids love pizza, so why not let them make their own mini pizzas from scratch? Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of active dry yeast
  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Tomato sauce
  • Shredded cheese
  • Desired toppings (e.g., pepperoni, diced bell peppers, sliced olives)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes frothy.
  3. Pour the yeast mixture and olive oil into the flour mixture.
  4. Stir until the dough comes together, then knead it on a lightly floured surface for about 5 minutes.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls and let them rest for 10 minutes.
  6. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C).
  7. Roll out each dough ball into mini pizza crusts.
  8. Spread tomato sauce over the crusts and sprinkle with shredded cheese.
  9. Add desired toppings.
  10. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.

3. Banana Bread

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 55-60 minutes
Total Time: 70-75 minutes

Banana bread is a great way to use up overripe bananas and teach kids about food waste reduction. Gather these ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 4 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar.
  4. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the mashed bananas, milk, and vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the banana mixture, mixing well after each addition.
  6. Pour the batter into a greased 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  7. Bake for 55-60 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Conclusion

Baking together with mom is not only a great bonding activity but also an opportunity for kids to learn valuable skills. These easy baking recipes are a perfect starting point for kids who are eager to explore the world of baking. So put on your aprons, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create delicious treats that will be enjoyed by the whole family. Happy baking!
