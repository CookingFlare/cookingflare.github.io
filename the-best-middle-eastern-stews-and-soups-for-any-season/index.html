The Best Middle Eastern Stews and Soups for Any Season


Middle Eastern cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, aromatic spices, and hearty dishes that warm the soul. From the sweltering heat of the Arabian Peninsula to the frigid winters atop the mountains of Lebanon, the region’s stews and soups reflect its deep culinary traditions and seasonal adaptability. These dishes aren’t just meals; they’re stories told through complex layers of spices, wholesome ingredients, and centuries-old cultural heritage. No matter the time of year, Middle Eastern stews and soups offer comfort, nourishment, and a burst of bold flavors that will leave your palate begging for more.

Here are some of the best Middle Eastern stews and soups to savor, whether you’re preparing for a chilly winter’s night or a light summer meal. Each recipe showcases unique ingredients, cooking techniques, and cultural nuances that make this cuisine one-of-a-kind.

1. Shorbat Adas (Lentil Soup)

Lentil soup is an iconic dish across the Middle Eastern region, cherished for its simplicity, nutrition, and heartwarming qualities. Popular in countries like Lebanon, Jordan, and Palestine, Shorbat Adas is a must-have during autumn and winter, although it’s equally enjoyed year-round.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Olive oil for sautéing

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat, and sauté the onions until translucent. Add the garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Stir in the spices: cumin, turmeric, and coriander. Let them bloom to release their fragrant aromas.
  3. Add the lentils, carrots, and broth. Bring everything to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 25 minutes until the lentils are tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to achieve your preferred texture—whether smooth or slightly chunky.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, then finish with a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness.

This lentil soup pairs beautifully with warm flatbread or a drizzle of olive oil for extra indulgence.

2. Fesenjan (Persian Pomegranate and Walnut Stew)

Hailing from Iran, Fesenjan is a luxurious stew that combines sweet, tangy, and nutty flavors to create a dish that’s irresistible in any season. Traditionally made with chicken or duck, it’s just as delectable with vegetarian substitutions.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups ground walnuts
  • 1½ cups pomegranate molasses
  • 1 lb chicken thighs or tofu
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Start by toasting the ground walnuts in a dry skillet over low heat, stirring frequently until aromatic. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot and sauté the onion until golden. Add the chicken or tofu and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Stir in cinnamon and nutmeg to enhance the dish’s warm, earthy notes.
  4. Gradually add the toasted walnuts, pomegranate molasses, and broth. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 45–60 minutes, or until the stew thickens and the flavors meld beautifully.
  5. Adjust the seasoning, adding salt and pepper as needed.

Serve Fesenjan over saffron-infused rice for a meal that feels as decadent as it tastes.

3. Harira (Moroccan Tomato and Chickpea Soup)

Traditionally enjoyed during Ramadan to break the fast, Harira is a Moroccan favorite filled with nourishing ingredients. The balance of protein-packed legumes, spices, and fresh herbs makes it a fantastic choice for a hearty yet light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 1 cup cooked lentils
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, blended into a purée
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • ½ cup chopped parsley
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Juice of one lemon
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat olive oil and sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  2. Add the tomato purée, cilantro, parsley, saffron, paprika, and ground ginger. Cook for 5 minutes to bring out the full flavor of the spices.
  3. Next, stir in the chickpeas, lentils, and broth. Simmer for 25–30 minutes until the soup has thickened slightly.
  4. Finish with a squeeze of lemon juice for a touch of acidity and balance.

Harira is best served with a fresh loaf of bread, such as a crusty baguette or traditional Moroccan khobz.

4. Marak Temani (Yemeni Beef Stew)

This robust stew from Yemen is richly flavored and packed with tender meat, root vegetables, and warm spices. Known for its use of hulbah, a fenugreek-based condiment, Marak Temani is ideal for chilly weather and special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb beef chunks
  • 1 diced onion
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon hawaij spice mix (Yemeni curry blend)
  • 4 cups water or beef stock
  • 1 tablespoon hulbah paste (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, brown the beef chunks in oil, then remove them and set aside.
  2. Sauté onions and garlic in the same pot until fragrant.
  3. Add carrots, potatoes, hawaij, and hulbah if available. Stir everything well before returning the beef to the pot.
  4. Pour in the stock and let the stew simmer for 1–1.5 hours, or until the meat and vegetables are tender and the flavors have harmonized.
  5. Taste and adjust with salt and pepper.

Marak Temani pairs beautifully with a side of warm pita or steamed rice.

5. Kubbeh Hamusta (Tangy Vegetable and Dumpling Soup)

Kubbeh Hamusta is a delightful Iraqi and Levantine dish featuring semolina dumplings (kubbeh) simmered in a tangy vegetable broth. This vibrant soup is perfect for spring and summer when light, bright flavors are most appreciated.

Ingredients:

  • For the kubbeh dumplings:
    • 1 cup fine semolina
    • 1 cup ground meat (optional)
    • ¼ cup water
    • Pinch of salt
  • For the soup:
    • 1 bunch Swiss chard or spinach, chopped
    • 2 zucchini, sliced
    • 1 leek, diced
    • 8 cups vegetable broth
    • Juice of one lemon
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix semolina, water, and salt together in a bowl to make the kubbeh dough. Form small balls and stuff them with ground meat, if desired.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil and sauté the leek and garlic until tender.
  3. Add Swiss chard, zucchini, and broth. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Gently drop the kubbeh dumplings into the soup and cook for an additional 10–15 minutes.
  5. Stir in the lemon juice and season with salt and pepper.

Kubbeh Hamusta embodies the perfect balance of tartness and earthiness, making it an excellent addition to any recipe repertoire.

These Middle Eastern stews and soups are steeped in tradition yet versatile enough for modern kitchens. Their reliance on wholesome ingredients and aromatic spices ensures meals that are both nourishing and unforgettable. Whether you’re in search of comfort foods for cold days, vibrant dishes for warmer evenings, or new flavors to explore, these recipes will undoubtedly elevate your cooking game.

Invite the diverse and delicious world of Middle Eastern cuisine into your kitchen today—your taste buds will thank you!
