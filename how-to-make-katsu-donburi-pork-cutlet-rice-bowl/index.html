How to Make Katsu Donburi: Pork Cutlet Rice Bowl


Katsu Donburi, often affectionately referred to as "Katsu Don," is a comforting and satisfying Japanese rice bowl. This dish features a crispy pork cutlet (tonkatsu) served on a bed of rice, topped with a savory sauce and a delicate layer of egg. Not only is Katsu Don delicious, but it also embodies the essence of home-cooked comfort food, making it a favorite in households and restaurants across Japan and abroad. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll delve into the steps needed to create this delightful dish, covering everything from ingredient selection to cooking techniques. With a dash of patience and a sprinkle of love, you can impress your family and friends with your culinary skills. Let’s get started! Ingredients For the Pork Cutlet

2 boneless pork loin chops (about 150-200g each) Salt and pepper, to taste 1/2 cup all-purpose flour 1 large egg, beaten 1 cup panko breadcrumbs Vegetable oil (for frying)

For the Donburi Sauce

1/2 cup dashi broth (you can use instant dashi powder or make your own) 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon mirin 1 tablespoon sugar

For the Topping

2 large eggs 2 green onions, thinly sliced (optional for garnish)

For Serving

Cooked Japanese short-grain rice

Cooking Tools

Large frying pan or deep skillet Mixing bowls Whisk or chopsticks for beating eggs Cooking thermometer (optional) Ladle or spoon for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions Step 1: Preparing the Pork Cutlet

Pound the Pork: If your pork chops are thick, place them between two pieces of plastic wrap or parchment paper. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, gently pound the pork to an even thickness of about 1/2 inch. This helps them cook evenly and tenderizes the meat.

Season the Pork: Generously season both sides of each pork chop with salt and pepper. The seasoning is essential to add flavor to the cutlet.

Step 2: Breading the Cutlet

Set Up the Breading Station: In one bowl, place the all-purpose flour. In a second bowl, beat the egg. In a third bowl, pour the panko breadcrumbs.

Coat the Pork: Dredge each pork chop in flour, shaking off any excess. Next, dip it into the beaten egg, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, press the chop into the panko breadcrumbs, ensuring an even coating. Gently press down to help the breadcrumbs adhere.

Let It Rest: After breading, let the cutlets rest for about 10 minutes. This allows the breading to set, creating a crispier texture when fried.

Step 3: Frying the Cutlet

Heat the Oil: In a large frying pan or skillet, pour enough vegetable oil to cover the bottom of the pan by about 1/4 inch. Heat the oil over medium-high heat until it's hot enough for frying. You can test the temperature by dropping a few breadcrumbs into the oil; they should sizzle immediately.

Fry the Cutlets: Carefully place the breaded pork cutlets into the hot oil. Fry each side for about 4-5 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through. Adjust the heat as necessary to avoid burning the breadcrumbs.

Remove and Drain: Once the cutlets are golden brown, remove them from the pan and place them on a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Step 4: Making the Donburi Sauce

Combine Ingredients: In a small saucepan, combine the dashi broth, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Stir well to dissolve the sugar.

Heat the Sauce: Place the saucepan over medium heat, allowing the mixture to come to a gentle simmer. Keep it warm while you prepare the eggs.

Step 5: Cooking the Eggs

Beat the Eggs: In a mixing bowl, crack the two eggs and beat them lightly with a whisk or chopsticks. You want to break the yolks and incorporate the whites but don’t overbeat; some streaks of yolk are fine.

Combine with Donburi Sauce: When the sauce is simmering, carefully pour the beaten eggs into the saucepan. Gently stir with a spatula, ensuring the eggs are distributed evenly throughout the sauce. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the eggs are just set but still slightly runny.

Step 6: Assembling the Katsu Donburi

Prepare the Rice: While the eggs are cooking, ensure that your Japanese short-grain rice is ready. The rice should be fluffy and hot.

Slice the Pork Cutlet: Using a sharp knife, slice the fried pork cutlet into strips. The size of the strips is up to your preference, but aim for about 1-inch pieces for easy eating.

Layer the Ingredients: In a large bowl, serve a generous portion of rice. Arrange the sliced pork cutlet on top, then pour the egg and sauce mixture over the top.

Garnish: If desired, sprinkle with sliced green onions for an added burst of flavor and color.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy Serve your Katsu Donburi immediately while it’s hot. It pairs beautifully with a simple side salad or pickled vegetables to complement the richness of the dish. Gather around the table and enjoy this exquisite rice bowl that brings a taste of Japan to your home. Tips for Perfect Katsu Don

Use High-Quality Meat: For the best flavor and texture, choose fresh pork loins from a reputable butcher. Spending a little more on quality cuts makes a noticeable difference.

Crispy Panko: Panko breadcrumbs create an exceptionally crispy crust. Avoid traditional breadcrumbs for this recipe, as they tend to be denser and may not yield the desired crunch.

Adjust the Sauce: Depending on your taste, you can adjust the ingredients in the donburi sauce. Increase the sugar for sweetness or add a dash of chili oil if you prefer some heat.

Cooking Eggs Gently: Avoid overcooking the eggs; they should be soft and creamy, almost like a custard. The residual heat will continue to cook them slightly even after you remove them from the heat.

Experiment with Variations: While this recipe focuses on pork, you can easily substitute chicken for a chicken katsu don or use vegetables for a vegetarian version.

Conclusion Making Katsu Donburi is an engaging culinary adventure that transports you to the vibrant streets of Japan with its rich flavors and comforting textures. Whether you're preparing it for a special occasion or simply as a treat for yourself, this dish is sure to impress. The balance of crispy, savory pork, fluffy rice, and luscious egg creates a delightful harmony in every bite. Now that you have all the steps to create this delectable dish, it’s time to don your apron and start cooking. Enjoy your journey into the heart of Japanese comfort food!
