Easy Korean Mushroom Stir-Fry Recipe (Beoseot Bokkeum)


Korean cuisine is rich in flavor and tradition, offering an array of delightful dishes that are both comforting and exciting to prepare. One such dish that is a must-try for both seasoned cooks and beginners is Beoseot Bokkeum, a delicious Korean mushroom stir-fry. This easy recipe showcases the simplicity and depth of Korean cooking, where mushrooms are stir-fried in a savory, umami-packed sauce. Perfect as a side dish or even as a main course, Beoseot Bokkeum brings a touch of Korean comfort food to your table with minimal effort.

Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight meal or something to impress your guests, this dish delivers both in terms of flavor and ease of preparation. Let’s dive into how to make this tasty Korean mushroom stir-fry.

What Is Beoseot Bokkeum?

Beoseot Bokkeum (버섯볶음) is a classic Korean stir-fried dish made primarily with mushrooms and seasoned with garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sometimes a touch of sugar. The name “Beoseot” (버섯) means “mushroom,” and “Bokkeum” (볶음) refers to the stir-frying technique. This simple yet flavorful dish is incredibly versatile, and you can use a variety of mushrooms to suit your taste. It pairs wonderfully with steamed rice and is often served as a side dish in Korean meals.

The beauty of Beoseot Bokkeum lies in its simplicity, allowing the mushrooms to shine in their natural flavors. The dish highlights the delicate umami of the mushrooms, complemented by the savory, slightly sweet, and nutty flavors of the sauce.

Ingredients for Korean Mushroom Stir-Fry (Beoseot Bokkeum)

Main Ingredients:

  • Mushrooms: Any variety of mushrooms can be used, but shiitake, oyster, or button mushrooms are commonly used in this dish. A mix of different types of mushrooms will add depth to the dish.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic adds a fragrant aroma and depth of flavor to the stir-fry.
  • Soy Sauce: Traditional Korean soy sauce or all-purpose soy sauce works well for the savory base.
  • Sesame Oil: For that signature Korean flavor, sesame oil is a key ingredient in this stir-fry. It adds a rich, nutty aroma to the dish.
  • Sugar: A small amount of sugar balances out the saltiness of the soy sauce and gives the dish a subtle sweetness.
  • Green Onions: Finely chopped green onions provide a fresh, slightly spicy kick and make for a great garnish.
  • Sesame Seeds: Toasted sesame seeds are often used as a finishing touch to enhance the dish with a crunch and extra nutty flavor.

Optional Ingredients:

  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes): If you want a bit of heat, add a pinch of gochugaru to spice things up.
  • Carrots: Thinly sliced carrots add color and a touch of sweetness to the dish.
  • Onion: Some variations of Beoseot Bokkeum include sliced onions for added texture and flavor.

How to Make Korean Mushroom Stir-Fry (Beoseot Bokkeum)

Step 1: Prepare the Mushrooms

Begin by cleaning the mushrooms. Mushrooms tend to absorb moisture easily, so it’s best to wipe them with a damp paper towel rather than rinsing them under water. This helps retain their texture and flavor. Once cleaned, slice the mushrooms into thin strips or bite-sized pieces, depending on your preference.

For a variety of textures and flavors, you can mix different types of mushrooms. Shiitake mushrooms provide a robust umami flavor, while oyster mushrooms add a delicate chew. Button mushrooms, on the other hand, bring a mild taste to balance the stronger mushrooms.

Step 2: Prep the Other Ingredients

While the mushrooms are being prepared, chop the garlic finely. Slice the green onions into small pieces, separating the white and green parts. The white parts will be used for stir-frying, while the green parts will be saved for garnish. If you are using carrots or onions, slice them thinly to ensure even cooking.

Step 3: Heat the Pan

Place a large skillet or wok over medium heat and add a tablespoon of sesame oil. Once the oil is hot and begins to shimmer, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter quickly.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Mushrooms

Add the sliced mushrooms to the pan. Stir-fry them for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms have released their moisture and become tender. If you’re using a mix of mushrooms, keep in mind that different types may cook at slightly different rates, so make sure to stir frequently to ensure everything cooks evenly.

As the mushrooms cook, they will shrink and release a lot of moisture, which will evaporate as they cook. This allows them to caramelize slightly, bringing out their natural sweetness and umami flavors.

Step 5: Add the Seasoning

Once the mushrooms are tender and have reduced in size, it’s time to add the seasonings. Pour in 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 teaspoon of sugar, and a pinch of freshly ground black pepper. Stir everything together, allowing the mushrooms to absorb the flavors of the sauce. Continue cooking for another 2-3 minutes, ensuring the mushrooms are well-coated with the sauce.

If you’re adding gochugaru for some heat, sprinkle it in now. The chili flakes will add a subtle spice to the dish without overpowering the delicate flavors of the mushrooms.

Step 6: Add the Green Onions

Add the white parts of the green onions to the pan and stir them in. Let them cook for another 1-2 minutes. This adds a bit of sharpness and crunch to the dish.

Step 7: Finish the Dish

Remove the pan from the heat and drizzle in a little more sesame oil for added fragrance and richness. Sprinkle the toasted sesame seeds on top of the dish, followed by the green parts of the green onions for garnish.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Serve the Beoseot Bokkeum hot with a side of steamed rice. The mushrooms are tender, savory, and slightly sweet with a perfect balance of flavors from the sesame oil, soy sauce, and sugar. The sesame seeds add a nice crunch, while the green onions provide a burst of freshness.

This dish can be enjoyed as a side to complement a variety of Korean meals or served as a light vegetarian main course. Pair it with kimchi, bulgogi, or a simple Korean soup for a complete and satisfying meal.

Tips for Perfect Korean Mushroom Stir-Fry

  • Mushroom Choice: Different mushrooms bring unique flavors and textures to this dish. While shiitake and oyster mushrooms are popular choices, feel free to experiment with other varieties like enoki, maitake, or cremini mushrooms for varied tastes.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: When stir-frying, it’s essential not to overcrowd the pan, as this will cause the mushrooms to steam instead of stir-fry. If necessary, cook the mushrooms in batches to allow for proper caramelization.
  • Adjust Seasoning to Taste: If you like a saltier dish, you can add a bit more soy sauce. For those who prefer a sweeter version, increase the sugar slightly. This recipe is versatile, so feel free to adjust the seasoning based on your personal taste.
  • Add Protein: To make the dish heartier, consider adding tofu, chicken, or beef. Tofu, especially, works wonderfully in this dish as it absorbs the flavors of the sauce and provides a satisfying texture.
  • Storage: Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in a pan or microwave before serving.

Why You Should Try Beoseot Bokkeum

Beoseot Bokkeum is a fantastic dish for anyone looking to add more plant-based meals to their diet without compromising on flavor. Mushrooms are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, such as vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making this dish both healthy and tasty. It’s a great way to incorporate more vegetables into your meals, and the stir-fry method ensures that you retain all the nutrients from the mushrooms while creating a satisfying dish.

The beauty of this recipe lies in its simplicity. You don’t need to be an expert cook to create a flavorful, satisfying dish. With just a few ingredients and a little bit of time, you can prepare a delicious, authentic Korean mushroom stir-fry that will impress your friends and family.

Conclusion

Whether you are an experienced cook or a beginner, Beoseot Bokkeum is a wonderful dish to try. Its combination of savory, sweet, and nutty flavors will appeal to your taste buds, and it pairs perfectly with a bowl of warm rice. The recipe is adaptable, so feel free to make it your own by adding different types of mushrooms or tweaking the seasoning. This Korean mushroom stir-fry is not only easy to make but also a flavorful addition to your weeknight dinner rotation. Enjoy!
