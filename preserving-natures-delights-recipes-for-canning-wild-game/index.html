Preserving Nature's Delights: Recipes for Canning Wild Game


Canning wild game is not only a practical way to preserve your harvest, but it also lets you enjoy the unique flavors of nature throughout the year. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or simply appreciate the taste of wild meats, learning to can these delicacies opens up a world of culinary possibilities. This guide will walk you through the fundamentals of canning wild game, provide you with delicious recipes, and offer tips to make your canning experience as smooth as possible.

Understanding the Basics of Canning

Before diving into specific recipes, it’s crucial to understand the basics of canning. Canning is a method of preserving food in jars by heating them to kill bacteria and seal them for long-term storage. There are two primary methods of canning: water bath canning and pressure canning.

  • Water Bath Canning is best for high-acid foods such as fruits, pickles, and tomatoes. However, it is not suitable for low-acid foods like wild game.

  • Pressure Canning is the method of choice for low-acid foods, including meats, vegetables, and most wild game. This method ensures that the temperature inside the canner reaches levels high enough to kill harmful bacteria.

Essential Equipment for Canning Wild Game

To get started with canning wild game, gather the following equipment:

  • Pressure Canner: A must for safely canning meats.
  • Mason Jars: Quart or pint jars are ideal for storing wild game.
  • Lids and Bands: Ensure they are new and in good condition for an airtight seal.
  • Canning Utensils: A jar lifter, funnel, and bubble remover will make the process easier.
  • Thermometer: To ensure your pressure canner maintains the correct temperature.
  • Large Pot: For preparing your meat or stock.

Safety First

Safety is paramount when canning. Always follow tested recipes and guidelines to ensure that your canned goods are safe to consume. The USDA and the National Center for Home Food Preservation provide excellent resources for safe canning practices.

Recipes for Canning Wild Game

1. Canned Venison Stew

Ingredients:

  • 4 pounds venison, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth (or homemade venison broth)
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Meat: Brown the venison cubes in a large pot over medium heat. This step adds flavor and helps seal in the juices.

  2. Add Vegetables: Once the meat is browned, add the onions, garlic, carrots, potatoes, and celery. Cook for about 5 minutes until the onions are translucent.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Stir in the broth, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, thyme, and rosemary. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Fill Jars: Pack the hot stew into sterilized jars, leaving 1-inch headspace at the top. Remove any air bubbles with a bubble remover.

  5. Seal and Process: Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean cloth, place the lids on, and screw on the bands until they’re fingertip-tight. Process in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 75 minutes for quarts and 60 minutes for pints.

2. Canned Wild Game Chili

Ingredients:

  • 3 pounds ground wild game (deer, elk, or bear)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef or venison broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust for spice preference)

Instructions:

  1. Brown the Meat: In a large pot, brown the ground meat over medium heat. Drain excess fat if necessary.

  2. Sauté Vegetables: Add the onion and garlic to the pot. Cook until the onion is soft.

  3. Combine Remaining Ingredients: Stir in the beans, crushed tomatoes, broth, and spices. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer for about 30 minutes.

  4. Fill Jars: Ladle the hot chili into sterilized jars, leaving 1-inch headspace.

  5. Seal and Process: Wipe the rims, seal with lids, and process in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 75 minutes for quarts and 60 minutes for pints.

3. Canned Wild Game Sausage

Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds ground wild game (deer, moose, or elk)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Mix Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the ground game with all spices and water. Mix thoroughly.

  2. Cook the Sausage: Form the mixture into patties or crumble it into a skillet. Cook over medium heat until browned and cooked through.

  3. Fill Jars: Pack the hot sausage into sterilized jars, leaving 1-inch headspace.

  4. Seal and Process: Wipe the rims, seal with lids, and process in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 75 minutes for quarts and 60 minutes for pints.

4. Canned Wild Game Stock

Ingredients:

  • Bones from wild game (deer, elk, or fowl)
  • 2 onions, quartered
  • 4 carrots, chopped
  • 4 celery stalks, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 bunch fresh herbs (thyme, parsley, bay leaves)
  • 12 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Stock: Place all ingredients in a large stockpot. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer. Cook for 4-6 hours to extract flavors.

  2. Strain the Stock: Once the stock has simmered, strain out the solids, retaining the liquid.

  3. Fill Jars: Pour the hot stock into sterilized jars, leaving 1-inch headspace.

  4. Seal and Process: Wipe the rims, seal with lids, and process in a pressure canner at 10 pounds of pressure for 20 minutes for quarts and 15 minutes for pints.

Tips for Successful Canning

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Always use fresh and high-quality ingredients for the best flavor and safety.
  • Sterilize Jars: Ensure your jars are sterilized before filling. This can be done by placing them in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  • Don’t Overfill: Leave the appropriate headspace in jars to allow for expansion during processing.
  • Check Seals: After processing, check that the lids have sealed properly. They should not flex up and down when pressed.
  • Label Jars: Don’t forget to label your jars with the contents and the date to keep track of your pantry.

Storing Your Canned Wild Game

Once your jars are sealed and cooled, store them in a cool, dark place. A pantry or cellar is ideal. Canned wild game can generally be stored for up to a year, but for the best quality, consume it within six months. Always check for signs of spoilage before consuming, such as bulging lids or unusual odors.

Conclusion

Canning wild game is a rewarding process that not only helps preserve your harvest but also allows you to savor the flavors of the wilderness all year round. With these recipes and tips, you can confidently embark on your canning journey, knowing that you are making the most of nature’s bountiful offerings. Embrace the tradition of preserving food and enjoy the satisfaction of creating delicious meals from your wild game harvest. Happy canning!
