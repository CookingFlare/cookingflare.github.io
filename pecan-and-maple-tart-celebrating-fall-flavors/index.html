Pecan and Maple Tart: Celebrating Fall Flavors


As the leaves start to change color and the air becomes crisp, it’s the perfect time to embrace the flavors of fall. One delicious way to do so is by indulging in a homemade Pecan and Maple Tart. This delightful dessert captures the essence of autumn with its rich, nutty flavor and sweet, comforting notes of maple. Whether you’re hosting a festive gathering or simply craving a taste of the season, this recipe is sure to be a hit.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup pure maple syrup
  • 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups pecan halves

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and sugar. Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to combine the mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and ice water. Drizzle this mixture over the flour and butter mixture, and stir with a fork until the dough begins to come together.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently a few times to bring it together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Place a baking sheet in the oven to preheat as well.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, brown sugar, melted butter, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt until well combined.
  3. Stir in the pecan halves until they are evenly coated with the mixture.

Assembling and Baking:

  1. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom, pressing it gently into the pan and trimming any excess dough.
  2. Pour the pecan filling into the prepared crust.
  3. Place the tart pan on the preheated baking sheet in the oven and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.
  4. Allow the tart to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Pecan and Maple Tart is best enjoyed slightly warm or at room temperature. Serve it as a delightful ending to a fall-inspired meal, or pair it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat.

Conclusion

With its buttery crust, sweet maple filling, and crunchy pecans, this Pecan and Maple Tart is a celebration of the flavors that make autumn so special. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply treating yourself to a taste of the season, this recipe is a wonderful way to welcome fall into your kitchen. Embrace the warmth and comfort of this delightful dessert, and savor the delicious essence of fall with each and every bite.
