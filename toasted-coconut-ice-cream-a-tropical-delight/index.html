Toasted Coconut Ice Cream: A Tropical Delight


If you’re craving a taste of the tropics, look no further than this creamy and indulgent Toasted Coconut Ice Cream recipe. With its rich coconut flavor and the delightful crunch of toasted coconut flakes, this frozen treat is sure to transport your taste buds to a sunny paradise. Whether you’re hosting a summer get-together or simply treating yourself to a refreshing dessert, this homemade ice cream is guaranteed to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (28 ounces) coconut milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Begin by toasting the shredded coconut. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C) and spread the shredded coconut in an even layer on a baking sheet. Toast the coconut for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it turns a golden brown color and becomes fragrant. Keep a close eye on it to prevent burning. Once toasted, set the coconut aside to cool.

  2. In a large saucepan, combine the coconut milk, heavy cream, sugar, and salt. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook the mixture, stirring frequently, until it reaches a gentle simmer.

  3. Once the mixture is simmering, remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature.

  4. Once the mixture has cooled, cover the saucepan and refrigerate it for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. Chilling the mixture thoroughly will ensure a smooth and creamy ice cream texture.

  5. After the mixture has chilled, pour it into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This typically takes about 20-25 minutes, or until the ice cream reaches a thick and creamy consistency.

  6. During the last few minutes of churning, add the toasted coconut to the ice cream and allow it to mix in evenly.

  7. Once the ice cream is ready, transfer it to a freezer-safe container, smoothing the top with a spatula. Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap and freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until it reaches the desired firmness.

  8. When you’re ready to serve, scoop the Toasted Coconut Ice Cream into bowls or cones and garnish with additional toasted coconut flakes for an extra crunch and burst of coconut flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra layer of flavor, consider adding a splash of coconut rum to the ice cream mixture before churning.
  • To make this recipe dairy-free, simply replace the heavy cream with full-fat coconut milk and ensure that the ice cream maker bowl is properly frozen before churning.
  • Experiment with different toppings such as chopped macadamia nuts, a drizzle of chocolate sauce, or a sprinkle of fresh mango chunks for a tropical twist.

Indulge in the luscious creaminess and tropical essence of this Toasted Coconut Ice Cream, and savor every spoonful of this delightful homemade dessert. Whether you’re a coconut enthusiast or simply seeking a refreshing treat, this recipe is bound to become a staple in your dessert repertoire. Embrace the flavors of the tropics and treat yourself to a scoop of paradise in every bite.
