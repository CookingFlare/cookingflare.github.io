Delightful Thanksgiving Pancake Casserole Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. While traditional Thanksgiving dishes like turkey and stuffing often take center stage, why not switch things up this year with a delightful Thanksgiving pancake casserole? This unique twist on a classic breakfast favorite is sure to impress your guests and add a touch of creativity to your holiday spread. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering pancake casserole recipes that are perfect for Thanksgiving brunch or even as a decadent dessert. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Pumpkin Spice Pancake Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups buttermilk
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, pumpkin pie spice, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, pumpkin puree, eggs, and melted butter.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the pancake casserole cool for a few minutes before slicing it into squares.
  8. Serve with a drizzle of maple syrup and enjoy!

2. Apple Cinnamon Pancake Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 cups buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 2 medium apples, peeled and thinly sliced
  • Cinnamon sugar, for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and ground cinnamon.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, applesauce, eggs, and melted butter.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined.
  5. Pour half of the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.
  6. Layer the sliced apples on top of the batter.
  7. Pour the remaining batter over the apples, spreading it evenly.
  8. Sprinkle cinnamon sugar over the top.
  9. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. Allow the pancake casserole to cool slightly before serving.

3. Pecan Pie Pancake Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 1 1/2 cups buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Whipped cream, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and chopped pecans.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, maple syrup, eggs, and melted butter.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the pancake casserole cool for a few minutes before serving.
  8. Top with a dollop of whipped cream and enjoy the delicious pecan pie flavors!

These delightful Thanksgiving pancake casserole recipes are a fantastic way to add some flair to your holiday menu. Whether you opt for the warm spices of the pumpkin spice pancake casserole, the comforting combination of apples and cinnamon in the apple cinnamon pancake casserole, or the indulgent pecan pie pancake casserole, your taste buds will thank you. So why not try something different this Thanksgiving and serve up a delightful pancake casserole that will have everyone asking for seconds? Happy cooking and have a wonderful Thanksgiving!
