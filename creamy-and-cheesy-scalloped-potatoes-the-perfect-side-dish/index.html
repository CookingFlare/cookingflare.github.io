Creamy and Cheesy Scalloped Potatoes: The Perfect Side Dish


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the rich and indulgent flavors of creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes. This classic side dish, known for its velvety texture and savory appeal, is a staple at family gatherings, holiday feasts, and cozy dinners alike. Whether you’re serving it alongside a succulent roast, grilled meats, or a hearty salad, scalloped potatoes elevate any meal with their comforting embrace.

In this blog post, we will dive deep into the world of creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes, exploring its history, perfecting the recipe, and offering tips and tricks to ensure your dish turns out restaurant-quality every time. Prepare to impress your family and friends with this delightful side that is sure to become a favorite!

A Brief History of Scalloped Potatoes

Scalloped potatoes have been a beloved dish for centuries, with roots tracing back to the early 19th century. The term “scalloped” refers to a cooking technique where thinly sliced ingredients are layered and baked in a creamy sauce. Originally, scalloped potatoes were made using a simple white sauce, but over time, cheese became a common addition, enhancing the dish’s flavor profile and making it even more irresistible.

This dish gained widespread popularity in the United States during the mid-20th century, particularly with the rise of convenience foods and casseroles. Today, creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are synonymous with comfort and celebration, appearing on tables during holidays like Thanksgiving, Christmas, and Easter.

Ingredients for Creamy and Cheesy Scalloped Potatoes

To create the ultimate creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes, you will need the following ingredients:

For the potatoes:

  • 2 pounds of russet potatoes: These potatoes have a high starch content and are perfect for achieving that creamy texture.
  • 1 medium onion: Adds a subtle sweetness and depth of flavor.
  • 2 cloves of garlic: Freshly minced for a fragrant kick.

For the creamy cheese sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter: For richness and flavor.
  • 4 tablespoons of all-purpose flour: To thicken the sauce.
  • 2 cups of heavy cream: The key to creaminess—don’t skimp on this!
  • 1 cup of whole milk: To balance the richness of the cream.
  • 2 cups of shredded cheese: A blend of sharp cheddar and Gruyère works beautifully for a perfect melt and flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon of salt: To enhance all the flavors.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper: For a touch of heat.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of paprika: Optional, but it adds a lovely color and flavor.
  • Fresh parsley or chives: For garnish, adding a pop of color and freshness.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Potatoes

  1. Preheat your oven: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures that your scalloped potatoes will bake evenly.

  2. Wash and peel the potatoes: Thoroughly wash the russet potatoes under cold water, then peel them using a vegetable peeler.

  3. Slice the potatoes: Using a sharp knife or a mandoline slicer, cut the potatoes into thin, even slices (about 1/8 inch thick). Consistent thickness ensures even cooking.

  4. Soak the potatoes: To prevent browning, soak the sliced potatoes in a bowl of cold water while you prepare the other ingredients. This also helps to remove excess starch.

Step 2: Making the Cheese Sauce

  1. Sauté the onion and garlic: In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 4-5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  2. Create a roux: Sprinkle the flour over the onion and garlic mixture. Stir continuously for about 1-2 minutes, allowing the flour to cook and form a roux. This step is essential for thickening the sauce.

  3. Add the cream and milk: Gradually pour in the heavy cream and whole milk while whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer, allowing it to thicken for about 5 minutes.

  4. Incorporate the cheese: Reduce the heat to low and add the shredded cheese to the sauce, stirring until melted and smooth. Season with salt, black pepper, and paprika, adjusting to taste.

Step 3: Assembling the Dish

  1. Drain the potatoes: After soaking, drain the sliced potatoes and pat them dry with a kitchen towel to remove excess moisture.

  2. Layer the potatoes: In a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, start layering the sliced potatoes. Begin with a layer of potatoes, then pour a portion of the cheese sauce over them. Repeat this process until all the potatoes and sauce are used, finishing with a layer of cheese sauce on top.

  3. Cover and bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for 45 minutes.

  4. Uncover and brown: After 45 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbly.

Step 4: Serving Your Scalloped Potatoes

  1. Let it rest: Once out of the oven, allow the scalloped potatoes to rest for about 10-15 minutes. This resting period helps the sauce thicken and makes serving easier.

  2. Garnish and serve: Before serving, sprinkle fresh parsley or chives on top for a burst of color and flavor. Serve your creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes as a delightful side dish that complements any main course.

Tips for Perfect Scalloped Potatoes

  • Choosing the right potatoes: While russet potatoes are ideal for this recipe, you can also use Yukon Gold potatoes for a slightly different flavor and texture. They hold their shape well and provide a buttery taste.

  • Experiment with cheeses: While sharp cheddar and Gruyère are classic choices, feel free to experiment with other cheeses like mozzarella, fontina, or even blue cheese for a unique twist.

  • Add some veggies: For added nutrition, consider layering in some cooked vegetables like spinach, broccoli, or mushrooms between the layers of potatoes.

  • Make it ahead: Scalloped potatoes can be prepared a day in advance. Simply assemble the dish, cover, and refrigerate. When ready to bake, increase the baking time by 10-15 minutes to account for the chill.

  • Leftovers: If you have any leftovers (which is rare!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the oven or microwave, adding a splash of milk to keep them creamy.

Conclusion

Creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are more than just a side dish; they are a celebration of flavor, texture, and comfort. With their rich, velvety cheese sauce and tender layers of potatoes, they are sure to impress at any gathering. Whether it’s a holiday feast or a simple weeknight dinner, this dish is a crowd-pleaser that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

So why not treat yourself and your loved ones to this deliciously indulgent recipe? With easy-to-follow steps and the ability to customize to your taste, creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are destined to become a cherished part of your culinary repertoire. Embrace the comfort, share the love, and enjoy every bite of this classic dish!
