Spice Up Your Fall: 8 Must-Have Recipes with Cinnamon


As the crisp air of fall settles in and the leaves begin to change color, there’s no better way to embrace the season than by filling your kitchen with the warm, comforting aroma of cinnamon. This versatile spice is a fall favorite, known for its ability to enhance both sweet and savory dishes. Whether you’re baking a classic dessert, preparing a warming drink, or adding depth to a savory meal, cinnamon offers endless possibilities for elevating your fall recipes.

In this blog post, we’ve curated 8 must-have recipes featuring cinnamon. From fluffy pancakes to spiced roasted vegetables, these dishes will have your taste buds celebrating the season.

1. Cinnamon Apple Pancakes

Nothing says fall like the combination of cinnamon and apples. These cinnamon apple pancakes are the perfect way to start your day, combining fluffy, spiced pancakes with tender apple chunks for a truly indulgent breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1 ¼ cups buttermilk
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 cup diced apples (preferably Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the egg, buttermilk, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; it’s okay if the batter is a little lumpy.
  4. Gently fold in the diced apples and vanilla extract.
  5. Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat and lightly grease it.
  6. Pour about ¼ cup of batter onto the hot griddle for each pancake. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until bubbles begin to form on the surface.
  7. Flip the pancake and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  8. Serve warm with maple syrup and a dusting of cinnamon.

These pancakes are soft, fluffy, and packed with the warmth of cinnamon and apples. A perfect fall breakfast!

2. Cinnamon-Spiced Roasted Carrots

Roasted vegetables are always a great side dish, but adding cinnamon takes them to a whole new level. The sweetness of the carrots combined with the warmth of cinnamon creates an irresistible savory-sweet flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrot sticks with olive oil, cinnamon, cumin, salt, and pepper until well coated.
  3. Spread the carrots evenly on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the carrots are tender and caramelized.
  5. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

This simple yet flavorful dish pairs beautifully with roasted meats or can be enjoyed on its own as a light fall meal.

3. Pumpkin Cinnamon Rolls

No fall recipe collection is complete without a pumpkin-inspired treat, and these pumpkin cinnamon rolls are the perfect way to indulge your sweet tooth. They’re soft, gooey, and bursting with the rich flavor of pumpkin and cinnamon.

Ingredients for the Dough:

  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup melted butter

Ingredients for the Filling:

  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp cinnamon
  • ¼ cup melted butter

Ingredients for the Icing:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tbsp milk
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm milk with sugar. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until bubbly.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the yeast mixture, cinnamon, salt, egg, pumpkin puree, and melted butter. Gradually add the flour, one cup at a time, until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 5-7 minutes, then place it in a greased bowl and cover with a damp cloth. Let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  4. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Roll out the dough into a large rectangle on a floured surface. Brush the dough with melted butter, then sprinkle with brown sugar and cinnamon.
  6. Roll up the dough tightly and slice into 12 rolls.
  7. Place the rolls on a greased baking pan and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  9. While the rolls are baking, make the icing by whisking together powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract.
  10. Once the rolls are done, drizzle them with the icing and serve warm.

These cinnamon rolls are a fall favorite, perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even dessert!

4. Cinnamon-Spiced Sweet Potato Soup

Warm, creamy, and flavorful, this cinnamon-spiced sweet potato soup is the ideal fall dish. The earthy sweetness of the sweet potatoes pairs perfectly with the aromatic warmth of cinnamon, creating a comforting bowl of goodness.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup coconut milk (or heavy cream)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened.
  2. Add the cubed sweet potatoes, vegetable broth, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper to the pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are tender.
  3. Use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth and creamy. Alternatively, transfer the soup to a blender in batches.
  4. Stir in the coconut milk and cook for another 5 minutes, then taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

This soup is rich, velvety, and the perfect comfort food to enjoy on a chilly fall evening.

5. Cinnamon-Infused Hot Chocolate

Hot chocolate is a cozy beverage that’s perfect for fall, and adding a touch of cinnamon transforms this classic drink into something special. This cinnamon-infused hot chocolate is rich, creamy, and incredibly satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 4 oz semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • Whipped cream or marshmallows for topping

Directions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the milk and heavy cream. Heat over medium heat until warmed through, but not boiling.
  2. Add the chopped chocolate and cinnamon, stirring until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth.
  3. Taste and adjust sweetness by adding sugar, if desired.
  4. Pour the hot chocolate into mugs and top with whipped cream or marshmallows.

This spiced hot chocolate is a comforting drink to enjoy while relaxing by the fire or after a day of outdoor activities.

6. Cinnamon Pear Crisp

For a delicious fall dessert, look no further than this cinnamon pear crisp. It’s the perfect balance of sweet, tart, and spiced, with a buttery crumble topping that takes it to the next level.

Ingredients for the Filling:

  • 6 ripe pears, peeled and sliced
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Ingredients for the Topping:

  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp cold butter, cubed

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced pears with brown sugar, cinnamon, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Pour the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Add the cold butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Sprinkle the oat topping evenly over the pears.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes until the topping is golden and the pears are tender.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

This cinnamon pear crisp is the perfect fall dessert—sweet, spiced, and comforting.

7. Cinnamon-Braised Chicken with Apples

This savory dish combines the rich flavors of cinnamon and apples to create a warm, hearty meal. The cinnamon infuses the chicken and apples, creating a tender, flavorful dish that’s perfect for a fall dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 apples, cored and sliced
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then brown them in the skillet for 3-4 minutes per side.
  2. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add the sliced onion and sauté for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Add the apples, cinnamon, and chicken broth to the skillet, stirring to combine.
  4. Return the chicken to the skillet, nestling it among the apples. Cover the skillet and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the apples are tender.
  5. Garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

This dish pairs beautifully with mashed potatoes or roasted vegetables for a satisfying fall meal.

8. Cinnamon-Spiced Granola

For a crunchy and flavorful fall snack, this homemade cinnamon-spiced granola is perfect. Packed with oats, nuts, and dried fruit, it’s sweetened with honey and spiced with cinnamon for a satisfying treat.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts, or pecans)
  • ½ cup honey
  • ¼ cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup dried cranberries or raisins

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the oats and chopped nuts.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together honey, coconut oil, cinnamon, and vanilla extract. Pour this mixture over the oats and nuts, stirring until evenly coated.
  4. Spread the mixture in an even layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until golden brown and crisp.
  6. Let the granola cool completely, then stir in the dried cranberries or raisins.
  7. Store in an airtight container for up to two weeks.

This cinnamon-spiced granola is perfect for breakfast, a snack, or as a topping for yogurt or oatmeal.

Cinnamon is truly a fall essential, and these 8 recipes are just the beginning of what this warm spice can do in the kitchen. From breakfast to dinner to dessert, cinnamon can be used to create comforting, flavorful dishes that capture the essence of the season. So, grab a jar of cinnamon, get cooking, and embrace the cozy flavors of fall!
