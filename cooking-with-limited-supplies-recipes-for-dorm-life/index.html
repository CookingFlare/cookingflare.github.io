Cooking with Limited Supplies: Recipes for Dorm Life


Living in a dorm can present unique challenges, especially when it comes to cooking. With limited space, equipment, and ingredients, it’s easy to feel like your culinary options are limited. However, with a bit of creativity and resourcefulness, you can whip up delicious and satisfying meals right in your dorm room. In this blog post, we’ll explore some simple yet flavorful recipes that are perfect for dorm life. Whether you’re a college student or someone living in a small space, these recipes will help you make the most out of your limited supplies.

Quick and Easy Microwave Omelette

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional add-ins: diced vegetables, shredded cheese, cooked bacon or ham

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper.
  2. Add in any desired add-ins, such as diced vegetables, cheese, or cooked bacon.
  3. Microwave the mixture on high for 1 minute, then stir.
  4. Microwave for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until the omelette is set.
  5. Let it cool for a minute and enjoy your quick and easy microwave omelette.

One-Pot Pasta Primavera

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pasta
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • ½ cup diced vegetables (bell peppers, cherry tomatoes, zucchini, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a medium-sized microwave-safe bowl, combine the pasta, water, diced vegetables, olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Microwave the mixture on high for 8-10 minutes, stirring every 2 minutes, until the pasta is cooked and the water is absorbed.
  3. Let it cool for a minute, then sprinkle with grated parmesan cheese if desired.

DIY Microwave Nachos

Ingredients:

  • Tortilla chips
  • Shredded cheese
  • Canned black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Salsa
  • Sour cream
  • Chopped green onions (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Arrange a single layer of tortilla chips on a microwave-safe plate.
  2. Sprinkle the chips with shredded cheese and black beans.
  3. Microwave on high for 1-2 minutes, or until the cheese is melted.
  4. Top with salsa, sour cream, and chopped green onions.

No-Bake Energy Bites

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup peanut butter
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • ½ cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the rolled oats, peanut butter, honey, chocolate chips, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  2. Roll the mixture into small balls and place them on a parchment-lined plate.
  3. Chill in the refrigerator for 30 minutes, then enjoy your no-bake energy bites.

Microwave Mug Brownie

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons water
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe mug, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, and salt.
  2. Add the vegetable oil and water, and mix until smooth.
  3. Microwave on high for 1-2 minutes, or until the brownie is set.
  4. Allow it to cool for a minute, then dig into your warm and gooey microwave mug brownie.

Conclusion

Cooking in a dorm doesn’t have to be bland or boring. With a few simple ingredients and a microwave, you can create a variety of delicious and satisfying meals. These recipes are not only easy to make, but they also require minimal cleanup, making them perfect for dorm life. So, the next time you’re craving a homemade meal, don’t let your limited supplies hold you back. Get creative in the kitchen and enjoy the flavors of home right in your dorm room!
