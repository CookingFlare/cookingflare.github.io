Easy and Flavorful BBQ Ribs for Your Summer Picnic

3 min · 506 words

Are you planning a summer picnic and looking for a delicious and easy recipe to impress your guests? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share with you a mouthwatering recipe for easy and flavorful BBQ ribs that will leave everyone wanting more. So, roll up your sleeves and get ready to fire up that grill!

Ingredients

To make these delectable BBQ ribs, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 racks of baby back ribs
  • 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (optional, for some extra heat)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. While the grill is heating up, let’s prepare the ribs.

  2. Start by removing the membrane from the back of the ribs. This will help the flavors penetrate the meat better and make the ribs more tender. To do this, simply slide a butter knife under the membrane and lift it off. Use a paper towel to grip the membrane and pull it off completely.

  3. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if desired). This will be our flavorful dry rub for the ribs.

  4. Sprinkle the dry rub generously over both sides of the ribs, making sure to massage it into the meat. Let the ribs sit for about 15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Once the grill is hot, place the ribs on the grill, bone-side down. Close the lid and let the ribs cook for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and starts to pull away from the bones. Make sure to turn the ribs every 30 minutes or so to ensure even cooking.

  6. During the last 15 minutes of cooking, brush the BBQ sauce onto the ribs, coating them evenly. This will give them a beautiful glaze and add even more flavor.

  7. Remove the ribs from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute, making the ribs even more succulent. Cut the ribs into individual portions and serve with extra BBQ sauce on the side.

Tips and Variations

  • For a smokier flavor, you can use a smoker instead of a grill. Simply follow the same instructions, but adjust the cooking time according to your smoker’s instructions.

  • If you prefer a different type of rib, such as spare ribs or St. Louis-style ribs, feel free to use them instead. Just keep in mind that the cooking time may vary.

  • If you don’t have access to a grill or smoker, you can also cook the ribs in the oven. Preheat the oven to 275°F (135°C) and place the ribs on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil. Cook for about 2.5 to 3 hours, or until tender, then finish them off under the broiler for a few minutes to get that delicious caramelized BBQ sauce.

  • Serve these BBQ ribs with your favorite picnic sides, such as coleslaw, cornbread, baked beans, or potato salad. The possibilities are endless!

Conclusion

There you have it - an easy and flavorful BBQ ribs recipe that will make your summer picnic a memorable one. The combination of the savory dry rub and the tangy BBQ sauce will have your taste buds dancing with joy. So, fire up that grill, invite your friends and family, and get ready to indulge in these mouthwatering ribs. Happy grilling!
