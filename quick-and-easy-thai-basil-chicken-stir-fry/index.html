Quick and Easy Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry


Thai cuisine is known for its bold flavors, fragrant herbs, and perfectly balanced spices. Among the many beloved dishes, Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry, also known as Gai Pad Krapow, stands out as a quick and easy favorite. This dish combines tender chicken, aromatic Thai basil, and a savory sauce that packs a punch. It’s a one-pan meal that’s perfect for busy weeknights or when you’re craving something flavorful without spending hours in the kitchen.

In this post, we’ll walk you through how to make Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry in no time, using simple ingredients that you can find at most grocery stores. The dish is naturally gluten-free and can easily be made with a variety of proteins, but we’ll focus on chicken here for its quick cooking time and versatility.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry

Before diving into the cooking process, it’s important to gather all the necessary ingredients. The great thing about this recipe is that it uses simple, fresh ingredients that you likely already have in your kitchen. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Stir Fry:

  • Chicken breast or thighs – 1 lb (boneless, skinless, and thinly sliced)
  • Thai basil leaves – 1 cup (fresh and fragrant, you can substitute with regular basil if needed, but Thai basil is ideal for this dish)
  • Red bell pepper – 1, thinly sliced (optional, for added sweetness and crunch)
  • Jalapeño – 1, thinly sliced (optional, depending on your spice preference)
  • Garlic – 4 cloves, minced
  • Onion – 1 small, finely chopped (optional)
  • Vegetable oil – 2 tablespoons (for frying)

For the Sauce:

  • Fish sauce – 3 tablespoons (this gives the dish its signature salty umami flavor)
  • Soy sauce – 2 tablespoons (you can use tamari for a gluten-free version)
  • Oyster sauce – 1 tablespoon (adds richness and depth)
  • Sugar – 1 teaspoon (balance out the savory flavors)
  • Lime juice – 1 tablespoon (for a zesty, fresh contrast)

How to Make Quick and Easy Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry

Making this Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry is simple and doesn’t require any special equipment. All you need is a wok or a large skillet, and you’ll be enjoying a delicious meal in no time. Let’s break it down step by step.

Step 1: Prep Your Ingredients

Start by slicing the chicken into thin strips. You can use chicken breasts or thighs—both work well, but thighs tend to be more flavorful and juicy. If you’re in a rush, you can use pre-sliced chicken from the store.

Next, chop the garlic, slice the onion (if you’re using it), and prepare the bell pepper and jalapeño. The basil leaves should be picked from the stem and roughly chopped. Make sure to have all your ingredients ready because this stir fry cooks fast!

Step 2: Make the Stir Fry Sauce

In a small bowl, mix together the fish sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and lime juice. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely. This sauce is the heart of the dish, bringing all the signature Thai flavors of salty, sweet, and tangy together.

Step 3: Heat Your Pan

Heat a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the vegetable oil and allow it to warm up. You want the oil to be hot but not smoking, as you’ll be cooking the chicken quickly and you don’t want to burn the garlic.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken

Once the oil is hot, add the thinly sliced chicken to the pan. Stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and slightly golden on the outside. It’s important not to overcrowd the pan, as this will prevent the chicken from browning properly. If you need to, cook the chicken in batches.

Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pan and set it aside.

Step 5: Sauté the Aromatics

In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Toss in the garlic and onion and stir-fry for about 1-2 minutes until the garlic is fragrant and the onion is slightly softened. If you’re adding bell pepper or jalapeño, now is the time to throw them in. Stir-fry everything for another minute until the vegetables begin to soften but still have a nice crunch.

Step 6: Combine the Chicken and Sauce

Return the cooked chicken to the pan and pour the sauce mixture over everything. Stir well to coat the chicken and vegetables in the sauce. Allow the mixture to cook for another 2-3 minutes, letting the flavors meld together and the sauce thicken slightly.

Step 7: Add the Basil

Finally, add the fresh Thai basil leaves to the pan. Stir everything together, and cook for an additional 30 seconds, just until the basil is wilted. The heat from the stir fry will release the aromatic oils from the basil, infusing the dish with its unmistakable fragrance and flavor.

Step 8: Serve and Enjoy

Once everything is combined and the basil is wilted, your Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry is ready to serve. This dish pairs perfectly with steamed jasmine rice or sticky rice. You can also garnish it with extra basil leaves or a squeeze of lime juice for an added burst of freshness.

Tips for Making the Best Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry

  • Use fresh Thai basil: While regular basil will work in a pinch, nothing beats the authentic taste of Thai basil. Its slightly peppery, anise-like flavor adds a unique layer to the dish.
  • Adjust the spice level: If you like it spicy, feel free to add extra jalapeño or even some Thai bird’s eye chilies. For a milder version, skip the peppers altogether or use fewer slices of jalapeño.
  • Customize with vegetables: Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry is quite versatile. You can add other vegetables like carrots, zucchini, or snap peas if you want to increase the veggie content of the dish.
  • Make it ahead: This stir fry keeps well in the fridge for 1-2 days. Just reheat it in a pan before serving, and you’re good to go.
  • Use lean chicken or substitute with another protein: If you prefer a leaner cut of meat, you can use chicken breast, but chicken thighs tend to be juicier and more flavorful. Alternatively, this recipe works great with ground chicken, turkey, or even tofu for a vegetarian version.

Why Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry is a Favorite

There’s something irresistible about the combination of savory, salty, and slightly sweet flavors in Gai Pad Krapow. The sauce is both simple and complex, with fish sauce giving it depth, soy sauce adding umami, and lime juice brightening everything up. The Thai basil brings a beautiful fragrance that elevates the dish to another level.

Not only is Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry packed with flavor, but it’s also incredibly quick and easy to make. The stir-fry method ensures that the ingredients stay fresh, tender, and flavorful. Plus, it’s a one-pan meal, meaning less mess and cleanup, making it ideal for a weeknight dinner or a weekend lunch when you want something satisfying without a lot of effort.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a quick and easy Thai stir fry that’s bursting with flavor, Thai Basil Chicken Stir Fry is the answer. With its savory sauce, fresh herbs, and perfectly cooked chicken, it’s a dish that’s sure to please everyone at the table. Whether you’re a Thai food enthusiast or new to the cuisine, this recipe is a must-try. It’s fast, simple, and a great introduction to the bold and aromatic flavors of Thailand.

Give it a try today and enjoy the delicious taste of Thai street food from the comfort of your own home. Happy cooking!
