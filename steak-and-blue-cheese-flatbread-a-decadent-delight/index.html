Steak and Blue Cheese Flatbread: A Decadent Delight


If you’re a fan of the rich, savory flavors of steak and the bold tang of blue cheese, then this Steak and Blue Cheese Flatbread recipe is a must-try for you. This dish combines the succulence of perfectly cooked steak with the creamy, pungent notes of blue cheese, all nestled on a crispy flatbread crust. Whether you’re looking to impress guests at a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a gourmet meal at home, this flatbread is sure to elevate your culinary experience.

Ingredients

For the Flatbread:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • 1 pound sirloin steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 1/2 cup caramelized onions
  • Fresh arugula for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Flatbread Dough

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. Gradually add the lukewarm water and olive oil, mixing until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 5-7 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rest for 1 hour.

Step 2: Cook the Steak

  1. Season the thinly sliced sirloin steak with salt and pepper.
  2. In a skillet over medium-high heat, warm the olive oil.
  3. Add the steak slices and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness.
  4. Remove the steak from the skillet and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

Step 3: Assemble the Flatbread

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Divide the rested dough into two equal portions and roll each portion into a thin, oval-shaped flatbread.
  3. Transfer the flatbread to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Sprinkle half of the crumbled blue cheese over each flatbread, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Distribute the caramelized onions evenly over the blue cheese.
  6. Arrange the cooked steak strips on top of the onions.

Step 4: Bake and Garnish

  1. Place the assembled flatbreads in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  2. Remove the flatbreads from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes.
  3. Garnish with fresh arugula before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

This Steak and Blue Cheese Flatbread pairs beautifully with a crisp, green salad dressed in a light vinaigrette. For a complete meal, consider serving it alongside a glass of robust red wine, such as a Cabernet Sauvignon or Malbec.

Final Thoughts

The combination of tender steak, piquant blue cheese, and caramelized onions on a crispy flatbread creates a symphony of flavors that is nothing short of indulgent. Whether you’re preparing this dish for a special occasion or a quiet evening at home, the Steak and Blue Cheese Flatbread is a decadent delight that is sure to leave a lasting impression on your taste buds. Try this recipe today and savor the luxurious flavors of this gourmet flatbread creation.
