Japanese Home Cooking: 7 Easy and Delicious Recipes


Japanese cuisine is renowned for its emphasis on fresh ingredients, simple preparation methods, and harmonious balance of flavors. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, Japanese home cooking offers a world of exciting possibilities. In this post, we'll explore 7 easy and delicious recipes that showcase the beauty of Japanese cuisine, using ingredients readily available in most supermarkets and with minimal specialized equipment.

  1. Tonkatsu Bento Box Tonkatsu, the breaded and fried pork cutlet, is a beloved dish in Japanese home cooking. It's easy to prepare and versatile enough to be served with various sides, making it ideal for a bento box lunch. Ingredients:

1 lb boneless pork loin 1 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup breadcrumbs 1 egg, beaten 1/4 cup dashi (see note), vegetable oil for frying Tonkatsu sauce Steamed rice assorted vegetables

Instructions:

Cut the pork into thin slices, pound to 1/4 inch thickness. Dip each slice in flour, then the beaten egg, and finally in breadcrumbs. Fry in hot oil until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side. Serve with steamed rice, tonkatsu sauce, and assorted vegetables.

  1. Miso Soup A staple in Japanese cuisine, miso soup is a comforting and easy to make dish. It's a must-try for beginners, showcasing the use of dashi, the fundamental base in Japanese cooking. Ingredients:

2 cups dashi 2 tablespoons miso paste 1 inch sliced green onion 1 cube tofu, cut into small pieces Wakame seaweed

Instructions:

Combine dashi and miso paste in a saucepan and whisk until smooth. Add green onion and tofu, then cook until the tofu is slightly browned. Add wakame seaweed and cook for a few minutes.

  1. Teriyaki Chicken with Stir-Fried Vegetables Teriyaki sauce is a versatile condiment used extensively in Japanese cooking. Its sweet and tangy flavor pairs well with a variety of dishes, especially those grilled or sashimi. Ingredients:

1 lb chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce 2 tablespoons vegetable oil Assorted vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, bok choy)

Instructions:

Marinate the chicken in teriyaki sauce for at least 30 minutes. Grill or pan-fry the chicken until cooked through. Stir-fry the vegetables in oil until tender.

  1. Hiyayakko Tofu with Ginger and Soy Sauce Hiyayakko is a simple tofu dish often served in Japanese households, especially during hot summer days. It's a perfect comfort food for a light meal. Ingredients:

1 block firm tofu, drained and cut into wedges 2 inches sliced ginger 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet Japanese cooking wine)

Instructions:

Pan-fry the tofu until golden brown on both sides. Mix soy sauce and mirin in a saucepan. Add ginger and cook for a few minutes. Pour the sauce over the tofu and serve immediately.

  1. Green Tea Ice Cream Green tea ice cream, also called matcha ice cream, is a unique dessert in Japanese cuisine. It's a must-try for ice cream lovers. Ingredients:

1 cup heavy cream 1/2 cup granulated sugar 2 teaspoons matcha powder 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

Combinecream, sugar, matcha powder, and vanilla extract in a saucepan and heat until beads of importantly sugar are formed. Let cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until chilled. Freeze in an ice cream maker.

  1. Pan-Seared Salmon Sashimi Sashimi is a classic Japanese dish, and pan-seared salmon sashimi is a variation of the traditional recipe. It offers a rich and elegant experience for those looking to expand their cooking repertoire. Ingredients:

2 lbs salmon fillets 2 tablespoons sesame oil 2 tablespoons soy sauce 2 tablespoons wasabi Sesame seeds

Instructions:

Pan-sear the salmon until the skin is crispy and the flesh is medium-rare. Slice the pan-seared salmon into sashimi pieces. Serve with sesame oil, soy sauce, and wasabi.

  1. Fried Udon Noodles with Teriyaki Chicken and Vegetables Udon noodles, made from wheat flour, are a staple in Japanese cuisine. Pairing them with teriyaki chicken and various vegetables creates a hearty and filling meal. Ingredients:

1 cup udon noodles 1 lb chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces 1 cup assorted stir-fried vegetables 1/2 cup teriyaki sauce

Instructions:

Cook the udon noodles according to the package instructions. Stir-fry the chicken and vegetables in a pan until cooked. Serve with cooked udon noodles, teriyaki sauce, and your choice of toppings.

Starting your culinary journey with authentic Japanese dishes is easy with these recipes. Each dish not only showcases the wonderful taste of Japan but also offers a backdrop to explore more complex and intricate recipes.
