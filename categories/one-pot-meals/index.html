Savory One Pot Lentil and Vegetable Curry

There’s something magical about one-pot meals. They save time, reduce cleanup, and allow flavors to meld together beautifully. This Savory One Pot Lentil and Vegetable Curry is no exception. Packed with protein-rich lentils and an array of vibrant vegetables, it’s a hearty and nutritious dish that will satisfy even the pickiest eaters. Ingredients 1 cup dried lentils 1 onion, finely chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1-inch piece of ginger, grated 1 tablespoon curry powder 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1 teaspoon turmeric powder 1 teaspoon paprika 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust according to your spice preference) 1 can (400 grams) diced tomatoes 2 cups vegetable broth 2 medium carrots, diced 1 bell pepper, diced 1 zucchini, diced 1 cup cauliflower florets 1 cup coconut milk Salt and pepper to taste Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish Instructions Rinse the lentils thoroughly under cold water and set aside. In a large, deep pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pot and cook for another minute, until fragrant. Add all the spices - curry powder, cumin, coriander, turmeric powder, paprika, and chili powder. Stir well to coat the onions, garlic, and ginger with spices. Pour in the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, and rinsed lentils. Stir everything together and bring to a simmer. Add the diced carrots, bell pepper, zucchini, and cauliflower florets to the pot. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover the pot and let the curry simmer gently for about 30 minutes, or until the lentils and vegetables are tender. Stir in the coconut milk and cook for an additional 5 minutes, until heated through. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Serve the Savory One Pot Lentil and Vegetable Curry hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves. It pairs perfectly with steamed rice or warm naan bread. Tips and Variations Feel free to experiment with different vegetables based on what you have on hand. You can add spinach, peas, sweet potatoes, or even mushrooms for added texture and flavor. For a creamier curry, you can puree a portion of the cooked lentils and vegetables before adding the coconut milk. If you prefer a spicier curry, increase the amount of chili powder or add some chopped fresh chili peppers. Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will develop even further, making it even more delicious the next day. With just one pot and a handful of simple ingredients, you can create a wholesome and flavorful meal that will please everyone at the table. This Savory One Pot Lentil and Vegetable Curry is not only delicious but also packed with essential nutrients. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds will thank you! ...
