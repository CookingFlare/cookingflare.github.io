Panang Curry: A Creamy and Savory Thai Curry


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, then you’re in for a treat with this mouthwatering Panang Curry recipe. This dish is known for its rich, creamy texture and bold flavors that will transport you straight to the streets of Thailand. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress both your taste buds and your dinner guests.

Ingredients

To make this delectable Panang Curry, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 1 pound of boneless chicken, thinly sliced
  • 1 can (13.5 ounces) of coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons of Panang curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup of green beans, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions

Now that we have our ingredients ready, let’s dive into the cooking process:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the sliced chicken and cook until it’s no longer pink in the center. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

  2. In the same pan, add the Panang curry paste and cook for a minute or two until it becomes fragrant.

  3. Pour in the coconut milk and stir well to combine it with the curry paste. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.

  4. Add the fish sauce and palm sugar to the pan, stirring until the sugar has dissolved.

  5. Now it’s time to add the sliced red bell pepper and green beans. Stir everything together and let it simmer for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  6. Return the cooked chicken to the pan and stir it into the curry, allowing it to heat through.

  7. Tear the kaffir lime leaves into small pieces and add them to the pan. These leaves will infuse the curry with a refreshing citrusy aroma.

  8. Give the curry a final stir and let it simmer for a few more minutes to allow all the flavors to meld together.

  9. Remove the pan from the heat and garnish the curry with fresh cilantro.

  10. Serve the Panang Curry hot with steamed rice or fragrant jasmine rice. The creamy and savory flavors of this Thai curry will leave your taste buds craving more.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a vegetarian version, simply omit the chicken and add your favorite vegetables such as tofu, mushrooms, or broccoli.
  • For an extra kick of heat, you can add a few sliced Thai bird’s eye chilies to the curry.
  • If you can’t find Panang curry paste, you can substitute it with red curry paste or even yellow curry paste for a slightly different flavor profile.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of fish sauce and palm sugar to suit your taste preferences. The balance of sweet, salty, and savory flavors is key to a delicious Panang Curry.

Now that you have this tantalizing Panang Curry recipe, it’s time to put on your apron and start cooking. Enjoy the creamy and savory flavors of this Thai curry, and don’t forget to share it with your friends and family. Happy cooking!
