Delicious Dessert Recipes for a Tea Party


A well-planned tea party is a delightful way to gather friends and family for a charming and elegant afternoon. While the tea itself is essential, no tea party is complete without a variety of delicious desserts that satisfy every sweet tooth. Whether you’re hosting a formal event or a more relaxed gathering, these dessert recipes will elevate your tea party and leave your guests raving about your culinary skills. From classic scones to contemporary sweet treats, there’s something for everyone. Let’s dive into some mouthwatering dessert ideas that will make your tea party truly memorable.

1. Classic Scones with Jam and Clotted Cream

No tea party is complete without freshly baked scones. These little beauties are a quintessential part of the British tea tradition, and they’re surprisingly easy to make. Pair them with jam and clotted cream for the perfect combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp cold unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 large egg (for brushing)
  • Jam and clotted cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Gradually add the milk and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  5. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and gently roll it out to about 1-inch thickness.
  6. Use a round cookie cutter or a glass to cut out the scones and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Brush the tops of the scones with the beaten egg for a golden finish.
  8. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Serve warm with your favorite jam and a dollop of clotted cream.

These scones will melt in your mouth and are the perfect accompaniment to a steaming cup of tea.

2. Lemon Drizzle Cake

For something a little zesty and refreshing, a lemon drizzle cake is a fantastic option. The bright, tangy lemon flavor pairs wonderfully with the rich, buttery cake, making it an irresistible treat for any tea party.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

For the drizzle:

  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and line a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream the butter and eggs until light and fluffy. Add the milk, lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract, mixing until well combined.
  4. Gradually fold in the dry ingredients until the batter is smooth.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. While the cake is baking, prepare the drizzle by mixing the lemon juice and powdered sugar in a small bowl.
  7. When the cake is done, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for 10 minutes. Then, drizzle the lemon mixture over the cake while it is still warm.
  8. Let the cake cool completely before slicing and serving.

The tangy sweetness of the lemon drizzle adds a vibrant touch to your tea table.

3. Mini Fruit Tarts

If you want to impress your guests with a sophisticated dessert, mini fruit tarts are the way to go. These individual-sized pastries are filled with smooth custard and topped with a variety of fresh fruits, making them a visually stunning and flavorful choice for your tea party.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package pre-made tart shells (or make your own with shortcrust pastry)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries (blueberries, raspberries, strawberries) and sliced kiwi for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the tart shells according to the package instructions. Once they are golden and crisp, remove them from the oven and let them cool completely.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the cream, milk, and sugar. Heat over medium heat until the mixture is warm but not boiling.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and cornstarch until smooth.
  4. Slowly pour the warm cream mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to avoid curdling.
  5. Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over low heat, whisking constantly until it thickens to a custard consistency (about 5-7 minutes).
  6. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the custard to cool slightly.
  7. Spoon the custard into the cooled tart shells and top with an assortment of fresh fruit.
  8. Chill the tarts in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving.

These mini fruit tarts are not only delicious but also add a colorful touch to your tea party spread.

4. Chocolate Truffles

For chocolate lovers, chocolate truffles are a decadent and bite-sized indulgence. These rich, creamy treats are the perfect way to satisfy a sweet tooth after a cup of tea.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz semi-sweet or dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Cocoa powder, chopped nuts, or sprinkles for coating

Instructions:

  1. Place the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl.
  2. In a small saucepan, heat the cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer.
  3. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and let it sit for a minute to melt the chocolate.
  4. Stir until the mixture is smooth and glossy, then add the vanilla extract.
  5. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate it for 1-2 hours or until firm enough to scoop.
  6. Once firm, use a spoon or melon baller to scoop out small portions of the chocolate mixture and roll them into balls.
  7. Roll the truffles in cocoa powder, chopped nuts, or sprinkles, depending on your preference.
  8. Store the truffles in an airtight container in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

These melt-in-your-mouth chocolate truffles will be a crowd favorite at your tea party.

5. Raspberry Fool

A raspberry fool is a light, airy, and refreshing dessert that combines whipped cream with fresh fruit. This simple yet elegant treat is perfect for a summer tea party and is incredibly easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until the raspberries break down and form a thick sauce.
  2. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool. Once cooled, strain out the seeds if desired.
  3. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream with the powdered sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form.
  4. Gently fold the raspberry sauce into the whipped cream until just combined. You want to keep the mixture light and airy.
  5. Spoon the raspberry fool into individual serving dishes and refrigerate for at least an hour before serving.

This creamy, fruity dessert is a great way to balance out the richer desserts on your tea party menu.

6. Chai-Spiced Biscotti

For a crunchy, spiced treat, chai-spiced biscotti is an excellent addition to your tea party. These twice-baked cookies are perfect for dipping into your tea and are full of warm, aromatic spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped almonds (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, ginger, cardamom, cloves, salt, and sugar.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until a dough forms. If desired, fold in the chopped almonds.
  5. Divide the dough into two equal logs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the logs are firm and golden.
  7. Remove from the oven and allow them to cool for 10 minutes before slicing them into 1-inch pieces.
  8. Arrange the slices back on the baking sheet and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes to crisp them up.
  9. Let the biscotti cool completely before serving.

These spiced biscotti offer a satisfying crunch and a burst of warmth that pairs beautifully with a hot cup of tea.

Conclusion

A tea party isn’t just about the tea; it’s about creating a memorable experience for your guests. With these delicious dessert recipes, you can offer a variety of sweet treats that will cater to every taste and preference. From the classic scone to the decadent chocolate truffle, each of these desserts is sure to leave a lasting impression. Whether you’re hosting an intimate gathering or a larger celebration, these desserts will bring elegance, flavor, and joy to your tea party. So, gather your favorite teas, prepare these delightful treats, and enjoy a truly special occasion with friends and family.
