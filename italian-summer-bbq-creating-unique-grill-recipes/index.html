Italian Summer BBQ: Creating Unique Grill Recipes


Welcome to the sunny days of summer, where the warmth calls for an outdoor gathering and the aroma of smoked wood and grilled meats tantalizes our taste buds. Traditional barbecue may be all about the beef and the booze, but when the mercado abounds with fresh veggies, aromatics, and an array of olives and cheeses, the Italian spirit beckons us to create a fusion of flavors rooted in our culinary past. Here are five regional Italian dishes and their twists for your summer grilling repertoire.

  1. Grigliata di Pesce Spada alla Griglia (Grilled Swordfish Skewers)

When the sun sets after a day of beachfront volleyball, nothing says “summer in Italy” more than a zesty marinade of fresh oregano, garlic, and lemon zest. Layer slices of succulent Mediterranean sea bass or swordfish on skewers before marinating with olive oil, a splash of red wine, a dash of oregano, and a tire nutshell’s worth of garlic. Grill these skewers to medium-rare (optimal for swordfish) directly over a charcoal fire, offering stellar smokey contrasts alongside a summer mixture of heirloom tomatoes, basil, and crusty artichokes.

  1. Spiced-up Risotto alla Milanese (Italian-inspired BBQ Risotto)

Experience the marvels of a wood-fired grill with this intriguing and unconventional take on the traditional Italian risotto. Yes, risotto on the grill! By assemblaging Arborio rice with red wine, saffron, and parmesan, you can forge a creamy, Italian-burnished risotto favorable to the unicorn of grilling. Make use of a non-stick grilling pan for supreme smokey spills into the dish.

  1. Grilled Caprese Salad al Forno (Grilled Caprese Salad with a Twist)

Stay with us on this tangy, juicy course. Taking inspiration from the famed Caprese salad of tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil, re-imagine this trio al forno (in the oven) atop the grill. Layer quartered heirloom tomatoes and mozzarella slices in a lightly greased cast iron skillet and broil with fresh basil leaves. Drizzle balsamic glaze and olive oil to conclude this elegant, smoky delight.

  1. Grilled Pici con Sugo al Polo Selvaggio (Grilled Pici with Wild Game and Tomato Sauce)

Tuscany’s most undiscovered treasure is Pici, similar to spaghetti, yet rolled by hand. Grilled pici, however, will shatter your expectations. The smoky charring of the pici absorbs the nuanced flavors of a wild game and tomato sauce redolent with carrots, celery, garlic, and olive oil. Grill the pici before tossing them in this sauce, infusing another level of gratifying textures.

  1. Grilled Tiramisu al Forno (Grilled Tiramisu Turnovers)

End your meal in the most delightfully unexpected way by grilling the universally adored Tiramisu dessert. Incorporating this Italian summer BBQ recipe replaces the ordinary with infrared. Sweet and smoky notes intertwine as you fold warmed puff pastry around mascarpone cheese and cocoa-dusted ladyfingers. Allow your senses to breathe in the latent side of Tiramisu.

With the generous bounty of an Italian summer BBQ under your belt, you now have accessibility to a newfound fusion of unforgettable regional dishes ─ always a testament to Italy’s nourishing simplicity and earthy goodness. However, no matter the recipes you choose, create delightfully spirited culinary concoctions with respect for your ingredients and a boundless palette of marinades, glazes, and sauces embracing tradition. Enjoy your newly-created repertoire!
