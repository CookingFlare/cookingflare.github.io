Kid-Approved: Homemade Chicken Nuggets Recipe


Who doesn’t love chicken nuggets? They are a classic kid-friendly meal that even adults can’t resist. But have you ever considered making them at home? Not only does it give you control over the ingredients, but it can also be a fun and educational activity to involve your kids in. In this recipe, we’ll walk you through how to make delicious, crispy homemade chicken nuggets that are sure to be a hit with the whole family.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Start by cutting the chicken breasts into bite-sized pieces. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, ensuring that each piece is well-coated.

  2. Set Up Your Dredging Station: In three separate bowls, prepare the following:

    • Bowl 1: All-purpose flour mixed with garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika.
    • Bowl 2: Whisked eggs.
    • Bowl 3: Breadcrumbs.

  3. Coat the Chicken: Take each piece of seasoned chicken and coat it in the flour mixture, then dip it into the whisked eggs, and finally coat it with breadcrumbs. Ensure each piece is evenly coated at every step.

  4. Heat the Oil: In a large skillet, heat the cooking oil over medium-high heat. Make sure the oil is hot enough for frying.

  5. Fry the Nuggets: Carefully place the coated chicken pieces into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the skillet. Fry the nuggets for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. You may need to fry them in batches.

  6. Drain and Serve: Once the nuggets are cooked, remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Serve the homemade chicken nuggets with your favorite dipping sauce and enjoy!

Tips for Success

  • Keep it Crispy: For extra crispy nuggets, you can double coat the chicken by dipping it back into the egg and breadcrumbs after the first coating.

  • Bake Instead of Fry: If you prefer a healthier option, you can bake the coated chicken nuggets in a preheated oven at 400°F for 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.

  • Get Creative with Seasonings: Feel free to experiment with different seasonings and spices to customize the flavor of your chicken nuggets. You can try adding parmesan cheese to the breadcrumbs for a cheesy twist.

  • Make it a Family Activity: Involving your kids in the preparation process can make mealtime more enjoyable. Let them help with coating the chicken or choosing the dipping sauces.

Final Thoughts

Homemade chicken nuggets are a versatile and crowd-pleasing dish that can be enjoyed by both kids and adults. By making them from scratch, you can ensure that your family is enjoying a healthier version of this beloved classic. Whether you fry or bake them, these homemade chicken nuggets are guaranteed to be a hit at your dinner table. So, roll up your sleeves, get the kids involved, and enjoy the process of creating delicious, golden nuggets of joy right in your own kitchen!
