Thai Chicken Wrap: A Burst of Exotic Flavors


If you’re looking for a delightful dish that combines the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine with the convenience of a handheld meal, then look no further than the Thai Chicken Wrap. This recipe is a fusion of fresh ingredients, aromatic herbs, and a zesty sauce, all wrapped up in a soft tortilla. The result is a burst of exotic flavors that will transport your taste buds to the bustling streets of Bangkok.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

For the Wrap:

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves

For the Spicy Peanut Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 2-4 tablespoons warm water (to thin the sauce)

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, fish sauce, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, lime juice, and sesame oil. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and massage the chicken to ensure it’s well coated. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 4 hours for maximum flavor.

  2. Grill the Chicken: Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and discard the excess marinade. Grill the chicken for 6-7 minutes on each side, or until cooked through and nicely charred. Once cooked, transfer the chicken to a cutting board and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

  3. Prepare the Spicy Peanut Sauce: In a bowl, whisk together the creamy peanut butter, soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, and red pepper flakes. Add warm water, a tablespoon at a time, until the sauce reaches your desired consistency. Set the sauce aside.

  4. Assemble the Wraps: Lay out the tortillas and divide the shredded purple cabbage, julienned carrots, sliced red bell pepper, cucumber, cilantro, and mint leaves among them. Top each with the grilled chicken strips. Drizzle the spicy peanut sauce over the fillings.

  5. Wrap and Serve: To wrap the tortillas, fold in the sides and then roll them up tightly, tucking the fillings as you go. Cut the wraps in half diagonally and serve immediately.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, swap the chicken for tofu or tempeh and adjust the grilling time accordingly.
  • Add a sprinkle of chopped roasted peanuts for extra crunch and flavor.
  • Feel free to customize the wrap with additional vegetables such as bean sprouts, avocado, or even mango slices for a touch of sweetness.

Enjoy the Exotic Flavors

The Thai Chicken Wrap is a versatile and satisfying dish that’s perfect for a quick lunch, a picnic, or a flavorful dinner. With its colorful array of vegetables, succulent grilled chicken, and the rich, creamy peanut sauce, this wrap is a celebration of Thai cuisine that you can enjoy anytime, anywhere. So why not bring a taste of Thailand into your kitchen and treat yourself to the exotic flavors of this delightful dish?
