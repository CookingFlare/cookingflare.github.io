Christmas Recipes for the Instant Pot: Quick and Delicious Meals


The festive season is a time of joy, laughter, and, of course, great food. As we gather around the table with loved ones, we often find ourselves seeking ways to make holiday cooking faster and less stressful, without skimping on flavor and quality. Enter the Instant Pot, the versatile kitchen appliance that’s become a favorite for home cooks everywhere. During Christmas, it transforms into a culinary magic wand, helping you whip up delectable dishes with ease. From savory mains to indulgent desserts, this useful tool lets you spend more time with family and friends rather than in the kitchen.

Below, discover an array of Christmas-inspired recipes all made in the Instant Pot. Whether you’re hosting a dinner or looking for side dishes to impress, these recipes are designed to be quick, delicious, and perfect for the season.

Main Dishes

Instant Pot Cranberry Glazed Ham

Transform your holiday ham into a sweet and tangy masterpiece with this recipe. The Instant Pot ensures a moist, evenly cooked ham without hours of baking or basting.

Ingredients:

  • 1 spiral-cut ham (5–6 lbs)
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Pour the water into the bottom of the Instant Pot and place the trivet inside. Lay the ham on the trivet, cut side down.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine cranberries, orange juice, honey, brown sugar, mustard, and cinnamon. Blend until smooth or keep it chunky, depending on your preference.
  3. Pour the glaze over the ham, ensuring it coats the top and sides well.
  4. Seal the Instant Pot and set to Pressure Cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Quick-release the pressure once done, and remove the ham. Optional: broil for 5 minutes to caramelize the top.
  6. Serve warm, garnished with extra cranberries or orange slices.

Instant Pot Herb-Infused Prime Rib

A tender prime rib roast is the highlight of many Christmas dinners. This Instant Pot recipe captures the flavors of a slow roast in a fraction of the time.

Ingredients:

  • 4–5 lbs prime rib roast
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 onion, quartered

Instructions:

  1. Rub the prime rib with olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Turn the Instant Pot to Sauté mode and brown the roast on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add the beef broth and onion to the pot. Place the trivet in and set the prime rib on top.
  4. Seal the Instant Pot and set to Pressure Cook for 20 minutes for medium-rare (adjust cooking time based on desired doneness).
  5. Allow a natural pressure release for 15 minutes before manually releasing remaining pressure.
  6. Let the meat rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
  7. Serve with au jus or your preferred sauce.

Side Dishes

Instant Pot Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Mashed potatoes are a Christmas classic, and using the Instant Pot to make them means creamy, perfectly cooked potatoes in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 2½ lbs Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes and garlic in the Instant Pot with chicken broth. Seal and set to Pressure Cook for 10 minutes.
  2. Perform a quick release, then drain the liquid (reserving some for later, if needed).
  3. Mash the potatoes and garlic together. Stir in butter and cream until smooth.
  4. Adjust consistency with reserved broth if necessary. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve warm with gravy or as a stand-alone side dish.

Instant Pot Bacon Brussels Sprouts

Give your Brussels sprouts an upgrade with crispy bacon and caramelized flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 strips bacon, chopped
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to Sauté and cook bacon until crispy. Remove and set aside.
  2. Add Brussels sprouts to the pot and sear for 3–4 minutes.
  3. Pour in balsamic vinegar, honey, and chicken broth. Stir well.
  4. Seal the Instant Pot and set to Pressure Cook for 3 minutes.
  5. Perform a quick release, then mix in the crispy bacon. Adjust seasoning.
  6. Serve immediately as a flavorful vegetable side dish.

Desserts

Instant Pot Gingerbread Cheesecake

Nothing says Christmas quite like the warm, spicy aroma of gingerbread. Combined with creamy cheesecake, this dessert is guaranteed to delight your guests.

Ingredients:

Crust:

  • 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar

Filling:

  • 16 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Mix graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and brown sugar to form the crust. Press into the bottom of a 7-inch springform pan.
  2. In a bowl, beat the cream cheese, brown sugar, molasses, ginger, cinnamon, and nutmeg until smooth. Add eggs one at a time.
  3. Pour filling over the crust. Cover pan tightly with foil.
  4. Place 1 cup of water in the Instant Pot, insert the trivet, and set the pan on top.
  5. Seal and set to Pressure Cook for 35 minutes. Allow natural pressure release.
  6. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours before serving.

Instant Pot Eggnog Rice Pudding

End your meal on a cozy note with this creamy eggnog rice pudding. It’s a luxurious twist on a classic dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 2 cups eggnog
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Combine rice, eggnog, water, sugar, and nutmeg in the Instant Pot.
  2. Seal and set to Pressure Cook for 10 minutes.
  3. Perform a quick release and stir in vanilla extract. Let sit for 5 minutes to thicken.
  4. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with ground nutmeg or whipped cream.

Final Thoughts

Christmas is a time for creating memories, and food plays a vital role in those moments. Using an Instant Pot to craft holiday dishes ensures you spend more time savoring traditions instead of juggling kitchen timers. These recipes bring out the best of the holiday table—comforting, flavorful dishes that are ready in a fraction of the time. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or preparing an intimate meal, these Instant Pot Christmas recipes can simplify your dinner preparation while keeping the magic of the season alive.

Take the stress out of holiday cooking this year and let your Instant Pot do the work. From appetizers to desserts, celebrate Christmas with quick, delicious meals that will keep your guests coming back for seconds.
