Spicy Creole Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya: A Southern Classic


Jambalaya is a classic Southern dish that brings together the bold flavors of Creole cuisine. This Spicy Creole Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya recipe is a delicious and hearty one-pot meal that is perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers throughout the week. With its rich blend of spices, succulent shrimp, and savory sausage, this jambalaya is sure to become a favorite in your household.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 2 tablespoons Creole seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Green onions, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sausage and cook until browned. Remove the sausage from the pot and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the onion, bell pepper, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, Creole seasoning, paprika, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Add the rice to the pot and stir to combine. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the liquid.

  5. While the rice is cooking, season the shrimp with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of Creole seasoning. Set aside.

  6. After the rice has cooked for 20 minutes, add the cooked sausage and seasoned shrimp to the pot. Stir gently to combine.

  7. Cover the pot again and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.

  8. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  9. Serve the Spicy Creole Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya hot, garnished with sliced green onions.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a few dashes of hot sauce or sprinkle some crushed red pepper flakes before serving.
  • Feel free to experiment with different types of sausage, such as Andouille or chorizo, to add more depth of flavor.
  • If you prefer a milder version, reduce the amount of cayenne pepper or omit it altogether.
  • Leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 3 days and reheated in the microwave or on the stovetop.
  • This jambalaya pairs well with a side of cornbread or a fresh green salad.

Enjoy the vibrant flavors and comforting aromas of this Spicy Creole Shrimp and Sausage Jambalaya. It’s a true Southern classic that will transport you to the heart of Louisiana with every bite. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a taste of the South, this jambalaya recipe is sure to satisfy your culinary desires.
