Homemade Pumpkin Pie Recipe for a Classic Thanksgiving Dessert


As the leaves turn to shades of red and gold and the air becomes crisp, it’s time to embrace the warmth of the holiday season. And what better way to do so than with a classic Thanksgiving dessert - homemade pumpkin pie. This iconic dish is a staple on many Thanksgiving tables, and for good reason. Its rich, spiced filling and buttery crust evoke feelings of comfort and joy, making it the perfect finale to a festive meal. In this blog post, I’ll walk you through a step-by-step recipe for creating your own homemade pumpkin pie, from scratch. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get ready to bake up a storm!

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup chilled vegetable shortening
  • 4-5 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 can (12 ounces) evaporated milk

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Cut in the vegetable shortening with a pastry blender or fork until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Sprinkle 1 tablespoon of ice water at a time over the mixture, tossing with a fork until the dough holds together when pressed.
  4. Shape the dough into a ball, then flatten it into a disk. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

Step 2: Roll Out the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Transfer the dough to the pie plate and trim the overhanging edges.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, sugar, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and salt.
  2. Add the eggs and beat until well combined.
  3. Gradually stir in the evaporated milk until fully incorporated.

Step 4: Assemble the Pie

  1. Pour the filling into the prepared pie crust.
  2. Cover the edges of the crust with foil to prevent over-browning.
  3. Bake for 15 minutes, then reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for 40-50 minutes, or until a knife inserted near the center comes out clean.

Step 5: Cool and Serve

  1. Allow the pie to cool on a wire rack for 2 hours.
  2. Serve at room temperature or chilled, topped with whipped cream if desired.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a decorative touch, use cookie cutters to create shapes from the leftover pie crust dough and arrange them on top of the pie before baking.
  • To prevent the crust from becoming soggy, pre-bake it for 8-10 minutes before adding the filling.
  • Experiment with different spice blends such as nutmeg or allspice to customize the flavor of your pumpkin pie.

Conclusion

There you have it – a foolproof recipe for homemade pumpkin pie that is sure to be a hit at your Thanksgiving gathering. From the flaky, buttery crust to the velvety, spiced filling, this dessert is a true celebration of the season. So, gather your loved ones, preheat your oven, and let the aroma of cinnamon and pumpkin fill your home. Happy baking, and happy Thanksgiving!
