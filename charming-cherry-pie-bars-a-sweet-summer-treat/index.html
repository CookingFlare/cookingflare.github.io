Charming Cherry Pie Bars: A Sweet Summer Treat


Summer is here, and with it comes the warmth and sweetness of juicy cherries. There’s nothing like a refreshing slice of cherry pie to beat the heat, but sometimes a traditional pie can be too rich and overwhelming. That’s why we’ve come up with a delightful solution: Charming Cherry Pie Bars! These bite-sized treats are the perfect way to enjoy the flavors of summer without the fuss of a full-sized pie.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup cherry jam or preserves
  • 1/4 cup cherry juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cherries
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans or walnuts (optional)

Instructions

To make the crust, preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Line an 8-inch square baking dish with parchment paper or aluminum foil, leaving some overhang on the sides for easy removal.

In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, granulated sugar, and confectioners’ sugar. Add the softened butter and mix until the dough comes together in a ball. Press the dough into the prepared baking dish, making sure to evenly cover the bottom.

Bake the crust for 20-25 minutes, or until it’s lightly golden brown. Remove it from the oven and let it cool completely.

For the filling, in a large bowl, whisk together the cherry jam, cherry juice, chopped cherries, baking powder, salt, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg.

Pour the filling over the cooled crust and smooth the top. If using nuts, sprinkle them over the filling.

Bake the bars for an additional 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is lightly golden brown.

Remove the bars from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan. Once cooled, refrigerate them for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Tips and Variations

  • Use fresh or frozen cherries for the filling, depending on what’s available in your area. If using frozen cherries, thaw them first and pat dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  • Substitute other types of jam or preserves, such as strawberry or blueberry, for a different flavor profile.
  • Add a sprinkle of confectioners’ sugar on top of the bars before baking for an extra touch of sweetness.
  • Make ahead: Prepare the crust and filling separately, then assemble and bake the bars just before serving.
  • Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

Conclusion

Charming Cherry Pie Bars are the perfect summer treat to enjoy with family and friends. With their sweet and tangy filling, crumbly crust, and crunchy nuts (if using), these bars are sure to become a new favorite. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or just need a sweet pick-me-up, these bars are the perfect solution. So go ahead, indulge in a slice (or two, or three!) and savor the flavors of summer.
