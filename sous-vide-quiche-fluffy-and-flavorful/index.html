Sous Vide Quiche: Fluffy and Flavorful


Quiche is a classic dish that has stood the test of time, and for good reason. Its creamy, custard-like filling combined with a flaky crust makes it a favorite for breakfast, brunch, or even a light dinner. In this blog post, we’ll explore a unique method for making quiche that elevates its texture and flavor to a whole new level: sous vide cooking. By using the sous vide technique, we can achieve a quiche that is unbelievably fluffy and bursting with flavor. So, let’s dive into the world of sous vide quiche and discover how to create this delightful dish.

The Sous Vide Advantage

Sous vide, which translates to “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking method that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precisely controlled temperature. This technique allows for even cooking throughout the food, resulting in a perfectly tender and flavorful outcome. When applied to quiche, sous vide ensures that the custard filling sets uniformly and the flavors meld together beautifully.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1 large egg, beaten (for egg wash)

For the filling:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (Gruyère, Swiss, or cheddar work well)
  • 1/2 cup cooked and crumbled bacon
  • 1/2 cup chopped spinach
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the ice water, a little at a time, and mix the dough until it just comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.
  3. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Pre-cook the Crust

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 190°F (88°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface and line a 9-inch pie dish with it. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges. Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork.
  3. Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans to prevent it from puffing up during pre-cooking.
  4. Place the pie dish in a large resealable bag and seal it, removing as much air as possible.
  5. Submerge the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 20 minutes.

Step 3: Prepare the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.
  2. Stir in the shredded cheese, cooked bacon, and chopped spinach.

Step 4: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 167°F (75°C).
  2. Pour the prepared filling into the pre-cooked crust.
  3. Place the quiche in a large resealable bag, seal it, and remove as much air as possible.
  4. Submerge the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 1 hour.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

  1. Carefully remove the quiche from the water bath and the bag. Allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serving Suggestions

Sous vide quiche is incredibly versatile and pairs well with a variety of sides. Consider serving it with a fresh green salad, roasted vegetables, or a side of fruit for a well-rounded meal. It also makes a delightful addition to a brunch spread or a picnic.

Conclusion

Sous vide quiche offers a delightful departure from the traditional baking method, resulting in a quiche that is velvety smooth, perfectly set, and full of flavor. This sous vide technique ensures that every bite is as light as a cloud, making it a standout dish for any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned sous vide enthusiast or new to the technique, this fluffy and flavorful quiche is sure to impress your family and friends. So, fire up your sous vide machine and embark on a culinary adventure with this exceptional sous vide quiche recipe.
