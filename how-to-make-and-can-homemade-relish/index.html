How to Make and Can Homemade Relish


Homemade relish is the perfect way to preserve the flavors of fresh vegetables while adding a tangy, zesty punch to your meals. Whether you’re a fan of backyard barbecues, deli sandwiches, or simple crackers with cheese, relish can elevate your dishes to new heights. If you’ve ever considered making your own relish from scratch, this is your perfect guide. The process is simpler than you think, and the outcome is not only satisfying but cost-effective. You retain complete control over the flavors, quality, and freshness. Plus, with proper canning methods, you can stock jars of relish to enjoy or gift year-round.

Understanding Relish: Flavorful and Versatile

At its core, relish is a finely chopped condiment made from vegetables or fruits pickled in a tangy, spiced vinegar mixture. Classic variations include cucumber relish (often associated with hot dogs) and green tomato relish, but the possibilities are vast. The appeal of relish lies in its versatility. You can tailor it to be sweet, spicy, tart, or mild, depending on your taste preferences.

Making and canning homemade relish might require a bit of time, but the steps are straightforward and highly rewarding. Here’s how you can craft your own jar of deliciousness.

Ingredients for Homemade Relish

This recipe focuses on a classic sweet-and-tangy relish made from cucumbers, bell peppers, and onions. However, don’t feel limited—this recipe can be adapted to incorporate zucchini, green tomatoes, or even apples for a sweeter twist. Here’s what you’ll need:

Vegetables:

  • 4 cups diced cucumbers (about 4 medium cucumbers)
  • 2 cups diced onions (about 2 medium onions)
  • 2 cups diced green or red bell peppers (or a mix)

Other Components:

  • 1/4 cup kosher salt (for drawing out excess moisture)
  • 2 1/2 cups white distilled vinegar (5% acidity)
  • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon celery seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Homemade Relish

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

Start by washing all the vegetables thoroughly under cold water. Chop them finely by hand or use a food processor, ensuring the pieces are consistent in size for even texture. Once chopped, combine the cucumbers, onions, and bell peppers in a large mixing bowl.

Step 2: Salt the Vegetables

Sprinkle the kosher salt over the diced vegetables, tossing well to coat evenly. Transfer the mixture to a colander set over a bowl or sink and let it sit for at least 2 to 3 hours. The salt will draw out excess moisture from the vegetables, ensuring your relish maintains the perfect texture without being watery. After the resting period, rinse the vegetables thoroughly to remove the excess salt and pat them dry with a clean kitchen towel.

Step 3: Prepare the Pickling Mixture

In a large stainless-steel or non-reactive pot, combine the vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, celery seeds, turmeric powder, crushed red pepper flakes (if using), and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar. This spiced vinegar mix is the heart of the relish—it’s where all the flavors come together.

Step 4: Cook the Relish

Carefully add the prepared vegetables to the pot with the pickling mixture, stirring well to combine. Let everything simmer together for about 10 to 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. The vegetables will soften slightly but should still retain a bit of crunch for a pleasant texture.

Step 5: Taste and Adjust

Once simmered, taste your relish and adjust the seasonings if needed. You can add a little more sugar for sweetness, vinegar for tanginess, or spices for a bolder kick. Remember, the flavors will deepen as the relish sits, so don’t overdo any adjustments.

How to Can Your Homemade Relish Safely

For long-term storage, canning is the best way to safely preserve your relish. Proper canning prevents spoilage and ensures your relish stays fresh for months.

Step 1: Prepare Your Canning Jars

Sterilize your jars, lids, and rings by boiling them in hot water for at least 10 minutes. Keep the jars warm until ready to use. This ensures a contamination-free environment for your relish.

Step 2: Fill the Jars

Using a ladle and canning funnel, carefully fill the sterilized jars with the hot relish, leaving about 1/2 inch of headspace at the top. Remove any air bubbles by running a skewer or knife along the interior edge of the jar.

Step 3: Seal the Jars

Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue. Place the lids and screw the bands on until they’re just fingertip-tight—not over-tightened.

Step 4: Process in a Water Bath

In a large stockpot or canner, bring enough water to cover the jars by at least 1 inch to a rolling boil. Place the filled jars in the boiling water using a jar lifter. Process the jars in the boiling water bath for 10 minutes (adjust for altitude if necessary).

Step 5: Cool and Check Seals

After processing, carefully remove the jars and let them cool on a towel undisturbed for 12 to 24 hours. Check the seals by pressing the centers of the lids—if they don’t pop back, the jars are properly sealed. Store any unsealed jars in the refrigerator and consume within a few weeks.

Tips for the Best Homemade Relish

  1. Choose Fresh Ingredients: Use the freshest vegetables possible for a bright, vibrant flavor.
  2. Experiment with Add-Ins: Try adding jalapeños for heat, chopped garlic for depth, or even a handful of grated ginger for a spicy-sweet twist.
  3. Use a Food Processor: If you want a finer texture, pulse the veggies in a food processor until they reach your desired consistency.
  4. Label Your Jars: Be sure to label and date your jars so you know when they were prepared.
  5. Follow Canning Protocols: Always use tested and approved canning methods to ensure safety.

Delicious Ways to Use Your Homemade Relish

Now that you have jars of homemade relish ready to enhance your meals, the possibilities are endless. Here are some ideas:

  • Spread it on burgers, hot dogs, and bratwurst.
  • Mix with mayonnaise for a quick tartar sauce.
  • Spoon over grilled chicken or pork for a zesty kick.
  • Stir into potato, pasta, or chicken salads for added depth.
  • Serve alongside a cheese platter or charcuterie board.
  • Pair with crackers and cream cheese for an easy appetizer.

Making and canning homemade relish is a delightful way to preserve the garden’s bounty and enhance your meals with a personal touch. Whether you’re gifting jars to loved ones or savoring your own creation, this time-honored recipe will bring joy and flavor to every bite. Once you’ve mastered this basic process, you’ll find yourself experimenting with flavors, savoring the creativity that homemade condiments offer. So, roll up your sleeves and start crafting your very own relish masterpiece today!
