Delicious Winter Quiche Recipes for Brunch Lovers


As the winter season approaches, our taste buds crave warm, comforting, and savory dishes that can be easily prepared for a cozy brunch gathering. Quiche, a classic French dish, is an excellent option for a delicious and satisfying winter brunch. In this article, we will explore some mouth-watering winter quiche recipes that will surely impress your guests.

Winter Quiche Recipes

1. Spinach, Feta, and Sun-dried Tomato Quiche

This quiche is a perfect combination of flavors and textures, making it a great option for a winter brunch. The sweetness of the sun-dried tomatoes pairs well with the tanginess of the feta cheese, while the spinach adds a boost of nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch tart pan.
  3. In a large skillet, sauté the chopped spinach and garlic until wilted.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked spinach, feta cheese, sun-dried tomatoes, and cheddar cheese to the egg mixture. Stir until well combined.
  6. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and smooth the top.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and golden brown.
  8. Garnish with chopped parsley, if desired.

2. Mushroom, Leek, and Goat Cheese Quiche

This quiche is a savory and earthy delight, perfect for a cold winter morning. The combination of sautéed mushrooms and leeks with the creaminess of goat cheese is a match made in heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust
  • 1 cup mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, shiitake), sliced
  • 2 medium leeks, sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch tart pan.
  3. In a large skillet, sauté the sliced mushrooms and leeks in butter until tender and fragrant.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked mushroom and leek mixture, goat cheese, and cheddar cheese to the egg mixture. Stir until well combined.
  6. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and smooth the top.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and golden brown.
  8. Garnish with chopped thyme, if desired.

3. Caramelized Onion, Bacon, and Cheddar Quiche

This quiche is a hearty and comforting option for a winter brunch. The caramelized onions and crispy bacon add a depth of flavor that is sure to please.

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pie crust and place it in a 9-inch tart pan.
  3. In a large skillet, caramelize the sliced onions over medium-low heat until golden brown.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked bacon, caramelized onions, cheddar cheese, and mozzarella cheese to the egg mixture. Stir until well combined.
  6. Pour the mixture into the pie crust and smooth the top.
  7. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and golden brown.
  8. Garnish with chopped parsley, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Use leftover vegetables or meats to add variety to your quiche.
  • Experiment with different cheeses, such as Parmesan or Gruyère, for a unique flavor.
  • Add some spice to your quiche by incorporating diced jalapeños or red pepper flakes.
  • Serve your quiche with a side salad or roasted vegetables for a well-rounded meal.

Conclusion

Winter quiche recipes are a great way to warm up on a cold winter morning. With these three delicious recipes, you’ll be sure to impress your guests with your culinary skills. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced cook, these quiche recipes are easy to make and can be customized to suit your taste preferences. So go ahead, get cooking, and enjoy the cozy warmth of a winter brunch with your loved ones.
