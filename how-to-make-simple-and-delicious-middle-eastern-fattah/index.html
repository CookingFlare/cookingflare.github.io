How to Make Simple and Delicious Middle Eastern Fattah


Fattah, a traditional Middle Eastern dish, is a comforting and flavorful recipe that’s perfect for any occasion. Known for its rich layers of flavor and texture, Fattah brings together crispy bread, savory meat, and a delicious yogurt sauce. This dish is a beloved staple in countries such as Egypt, Lebanon, and Syria, and it’s often prepared during special events or family gatherings. In this post, we’ll show you how to make a simple yet mouthwatering version of Middle Eastern Fattah.

What is Fattah?

Fattah is a Middle Eastern dish that combines layers of toasted or fried bread with rice, meat, and a garlicky yogurt sauce. It’s a versatile dish that can be made with different types of meat, such as lamb, beef, or chicken. The bread soaks up the savory meat juices and the yogurt sauce, creating a deliciously satisfying meal that’s perfect for lunch or dinner. The beauty of Fattah lies in its simplicity – it uses basic ingredients but packs a punch in terms of flavor.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

  1. Easy to Make: While it might sound complex, making Fattah at home is actually quite simple. Most of the ingredients are easy to find in your local grocery store, and the process can be completed in less than an hour.

  2. Full of Flavor: The combination of crispy bread, tender meat, and creamy yogurt sauce creates a delightful contrast in textures and flavors.

  3. Customizable: You can easily adjust the recipe to suit your preferences. Whether you prefer chicken over beef or want to add extra spices, Fattah is a versatile dish that allows for plenty of creativity.

  4. Perfect for Gatherings: Fattah is a great dish to serve when hosting guests. Its generous portions and rich flavors are sure to impress anyone who tries it.

Ingredients You’ll Need for Fattah

For the Meat:

  • 1 lb (450g) lamb, beef, or chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups beef or chicken broth

For the Bread Layer:

  • 4 pita bread or flatbreads, cut into strips or squares
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for frying or toasting)

For the Yogurt Sauce:

  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon tahini (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Garnishes:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Pine nuts or almonds, toasted (optional)
  • Pomegranate seeds (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Fattah

Step 1: Preparing the Meat

Start by heating 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent, which should take about 5 minutes. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute until fragrant.

Next, add the pieces of meat (lamb, beef, or chicken) to the pan. Cook the meat until browned on all sides. This should take around 8-10 minutes, depending on the size of the meat pieces.

Once the meat is browned, add the cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Stir well to coat the meat evenly with the spices. These warm spices are essential to creating the signature flavor of Fattah.

Pour in the broth and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cover the pan and let it cook for about 30 minutes if you’re using lamb or beef, or around 20 minutes for chicken. The meat should become tender, and the broth should reduce slightly. If needed, add a little more broth during cooking to ensure the meat stays moist.

Step 2: Preparing the Bread Layer

While the meat is cooking, it’s time to prepare the bread layer. Cut the pita bread or flatbreads into squares or strips, depending on your preference.

You can either fry or toast the bread. To fry, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a separate pan over medium heat. Add the bread pieces and fry until golden brown and crispy. This should take about 3-5 minutes. If you prefer to toast the bread, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the bread pieces on a baking sheet for 10-12 minutes, or until they are crispy.

Once the bread is crispy, set it aside on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.

Step 3: Preparing the Yogurt Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together the plain yogurt, tahini (if using), lemon juice, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. This creamy yogurt sauce adds a refreshing contrast to the rich flavors of the meat and the crispy bread. If the sauce is too thick, you can thin it out with a little bit of water.

Step 4: Assembling the Fattah

Once all the components are prepared, it’s time to assemble your Fattah.

Start by layering the crispy bread at the bottom of a large serving dish or individual bowls. Spoon some of the cooked meat and its broth over the bread, ensuring the bread soaks up the flavorful juices.

Next, drizzle a generous amount of the yogurt sauce over the meat. This will add creaminess and tang to each bite. If you like, you can repeat the layers, adding more bread, meat, and yogurt sauce until you’ve used up all the ingredients.

Step 5: Garnishing and Serving

Top your Fattah with fresh chopped parsley for a burst of color and freshness. For an added touch of elegance, you can sprinkle toasted pine nuts or almonds on top. If you enjoy a sweet and tangy flavor, pomegranate seeds make a beautiful and tasty garnish.

Fattah is best served warm, and it pairs wonderfully with a side of Arabic salad, tabbouleh, or pickled vegetables. This dish is sure to be a hit at your next meal, whether you’re serving it for a casual family dinner or a special gathering.

Tips for Making the Perfect Fattah

  1. Choose the Right Meat: While lamb is a popular choice for traditional Fattah, beef and chicken also work wonderfully. If you prefer a lighter dish, chicken is an excellent option.

  2. Adjust the Spices: The beauty of Fattah lies in the spices, so feel free to adjust the amounts according to your taste. If you love extra warmth, add a pinch of ground cloves or nutmeg.

  3. Use Fresh Bread: Using fresh, soft pita bread will yield the best results. Stale bread can become too soggy when layered with the meat and sauce.

  4. Make It Ahead: Fattah can be made ahead of time. You can prepare the meat, bread, and yogurt sauce separately and assemble the dish when ready to serve. This makes it a great option for meal prepping.

  5. Experiment with Garnishes: Garnishes such as crispy fried onions, chopped tomatoes, or even a drizzle of olive oil can add extra flavor and texture to the dish.

Why Fattah is a Favorite in Middle Eastern Cuisine

Fattah is more than just a meal; it’s a dish that brings people together. It has been enjoyed for generations in Middle Eastern families and continues to be a favorite for its comforting nature and ability to be easily shared. Whether it’s for a festive occasion or a simple family dinner, Fattah never fails to bring joy to the table.

The combination of tender meat, crispy bread, and the creamy yogurt sauce creates a unique flavor profile that’s both hearty and satisfying. It’s a dish that reflects the rich culinary traditions of the Middle East, offering a taste of history with every bite.

Final Thoughts

Making Fattah at home is easier than you might think, and it’s a fantastic way to explore the flavors of Middle Eastern cuisine. The dish is both indulgent and nourishing, with layers of flavor and texture that will delight your taste buds. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a delicious comfort food meal, Fattah is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen.

Next time you’re looking for a hearty dish that combines savory meat, crispy bread, and a creamy yogurt sauce, give this Middle Eastern Fattah recipe a try. It’s simple to make, full of flavor, and guaranteed to impress your family and friends. Enjoy!
