How to Make the Best Cinnamon Rolls from Scratch


There’s nothing quite like the smell of freshly baked cinnamon rolls wafting through your home. Warm, gooey, and cinnamon-sweet, these rolls are the ultimate comfort food. Whether you’re making them for a special breakfast, a holiday brunch, or just as a treat to enjoy with your morning coffee, homemade cinnamon rolls made from scratch are a real crowd-pleaser.

While store-bought cinnamon rolls are convenient, they don’t come close to the taste and texture of cinnamon rolls made from scratch. If you’re looking to master the art of cinnamon roll making, you’ve come to the right place. In this post, we’ll guide you step-by-step on how to create the most delicious cinnamon rolls from scratch. You’ll learn about the dough, the filling, the icing, and everything you need to know to create the perfect cinnamon rolls every time.

Ingredients for the Best Cinnamon Rolls

Before you begin, let’s gather the ingredients you’ll need for both the dough and the filling. For the cinnamon rolls, we will keep things simple, but these simple ingredients make a huge difference in the final product.

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk (about 110°F/45°C)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Cinnamon Filling:

  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional, but it enhances the flavor)

For the Icing:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons milk (you can use heavy cream for a richer taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Dough

Start by activating the yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk and sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves, then add the yeast. Let the mixture sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This is a sign that the yeast is active and ready to work its magic.

Next, in a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, the melted butter, and the eggs. Using a wooden spoon or a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment, mix everything together until it forms a soft dough. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour, one tablespoon at a time, until it becomes smooth and elastic.

Once your dough comes together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes. The dough should be smooth and slightly tacky but not sticky. If you’re using a stand mixer, you can let it knead the dough for you, which will take about 4-5 minutes.

Once the dough is kneaded, place it in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot for about 1 to 1 1/2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 2: Preparing the Cinnamon Filling

While your dough is rising, prepare the cinnamon filling. In a medium bowl, mix together the brown sugar, ground cinnamon, and salt. Set this aside until the dough is ready to be rolled out.

Step 3: Rolling Out the Dough

After the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and roll it into a large rectangle, about 16 inches long by 12 inches wide. Try to keep the edges as straight as possible for an even roll.

Spread the softened butter evenly over the surface of the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar mixture generously over the buttered dough, making sure to cover it completely.

Step 4: Rolling the Dough

Starting at one of the longer edges of the rectangle, carefully roll the dough into a tight log. Be gentle but firm, making sure the cinnamon filling doesn’t spill out. Once the dough is rolled up, pinch the seam to seal it and then cut the log into 12 even pieces using a sharp knife or a piece of dental floss.

Step 5: Second Rise

Arrange the sliced cinnamon rolls in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish, leaving a little space between each roll. Cover the pan loosely with a clean towel and let the rolls rise for another 30 minutes to 1 hour. They should puff up and fill the dish.

Step 6: Baking the Cinnamon Rolls

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). When the rolls have finished their second rise, place them in the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown on top. You can check for doneness by gently pressing on the center of one roll—it should spring back. The edges will also become slightly crisp and golden.

Step 7: Making the Icing

While the cinnamon rolls are baking, prepare the icing. In a small bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar, softened butter, milk, and vanilla extract. Continue whisking until the icing is smooth and creamy. If you want a thicker icing, you can add more powdered sugar. If you prefer a thinner consistency, add a bit more milk.

Step 8: Glazing the Cinnamon Rolls

As soon as the cinnamon rolls come out of the oven, let them cool for about 5 minutes. This gives the rolls time to firm up slightly while still being warm enough to absorb the delicious icing.

Drizzle the icing generously over the warm cinnamon rolls. You want the icing to melt slightly, soaking into the cinnamon rolls, making each bite sweet and decadent.

Step 9: Enjoying Your Perfect Cinnamon Rolls

Now that you’ve made the best cinnamon rolls from scratch, it’s time to enjoy them! Serve them warm with a cup of coffee, tea, or hot chocolate for the ultimate indulgence. They’re perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even as a treat any time of day.

Tips for the Best Cinnamon Rolls

Making cinnamon rolls from scratch might seem like a big task, but with a little patience and attention to detail, you’ll be rewarded with the best cinnamon rolls you’ve ever had. Here are a few tips to make sure your cinnamon rolls turn out perfectly:

Use Fresh Yeast

For the best rise and texture, make sure you’re using fresh active dry yeast. Old or expired yeast won’t work as well and could leave your cinnamon rolls dense instead of light and fluffy.

Don’t Overwork the Dough

When kneading the dough, be careful not to overwork it. Over-kneading can make the dough tough, and we want our cinnamon rolls to be soft and pillowy. Just knead it until it’s smooth and elastic.

Allow Time for Rising

Patience is key when it comes to the dough’s rise. Don’t rush the process. Allow the dough to double in size during the first rise, and give the rolls a good 30 minutes to an hour for the second rise before baking. This ensures the rolls will be light and airy.

Customize Your Cinnamon Rolls

Feel free to get creative with your cinnamon rolls. You can add nuts, such as chopped pecans or walnuts, to the filling for extra texture. For a different flavor twist, add raisins, chocolate chips, or even a bit of orange zest.

Storing Cinnamon Rolls

If you happen to have any leftover cinnamon rolls (although that’s unlikely!), you can store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. To keep them soft and fresh, you can microwave them for about 15-20 seconds before eating.

For longer storage, you can freeze cinnamon rolls before baking. Simply place the unbaked rolls on a baking sheet to freeze individually, then transfer them to a zip-top bag once frozen. When you’re ready to bake, let them thaw in the fridge overnight, then proceed with the second rise and baking as usual.

Conclusion

There’s no better way to satisfy your cinnamon roll cravings than by making them from scratch. With a soft, fluffy dough, a rich cinnamon filling, and a sweet, creamy glaze, homemade cinnamon rolls are always a hit. They might take a little time and patience, but the end result is worth every second. Whether you’re baking them for a special occasion or simply for the joy of indulging in a warm, freshly baked treat, these cinnamon rolls will be a crowd favorite.

Happy baking!
