Meatless Marvels: 5 Marinades for Flavorful Grilled Vegetable Skewers


Grilling season is in full swing, and it’s not just about burgers and steaks anymore. Vegetables are taking center stage on the grill, and for good reason. Grilled vegetables offer a smoky, charred flavor that can’t be beaten. But to take your grilled veggies to the next level, you need some fantastic marinades. Today, we’re sharing five marinades that will transform your grilled vegetable skewers into meatless marvels. So fire up the grill and get ready to tantalize your taste buds!

1. Tangy Balsamic Glaze

Balsamic glaze adds a tangy sweetness to grilled vegetables, complementing their natural flavors. To make this marinade, you’ll need:

  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, honey, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Pour the marinade over your skewered vegetables and let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a more intense flavor. Grill the skewers over medium-high heat until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred. Drizzle any remaining marinade over the grilled vegetables before serving.

2. Spicy Thai Peanut Sauce

If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, this marinade will be a game-changer for your grilled vegetable skewers. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1/4 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sriracha sauce (adjust to your desired level of spiciness)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk
  • Salt to taste

In a blender, combine the peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, sriracha sauce, minced garlic, coconut milk, and salt. Blend until smooth and creamy. Pour the sauce over your skewered vegetables and let them marinate for at least 30 minutes. Grill the skewers over medium heat until the vegetables are tender and have grill marks. Serve with a sprinkle of chopped peanuts and fresh cilantro.

3. Mediterranean Herb Infusion

For a burst of Mediterranean flavors, try this herb-infused marinade. Gather the following ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh basil
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped basil, chopped parsley, chopped oregano, salt, and pepper. Whisk until well blended. Place your skewered vegetables in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Let the vegetables marinate for at least 1 hour, turning them occasionally. Grill the skewers over medium-high heat until the vegetables are tender and have a nice char. Sprinkle with some crumbled feta cheese before serving for an extra touch of Mediterranean goodness.

4. Smoky Chipotle Lime

If you like a bit of heat and smokiness, this chipotle lime marinade will hit the spot. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Juice of 2 limes
  • Zest of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a bowl, combine the lime juice, lime zest, olive oil, minced chipotle peppers, minced garlic, honey, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Whisk until well combined. Place your skewered vegetables in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Let the vegetables marinate for at least 30 minutes or up to 2 hours for a bolder flavor. Grill the skewers over medium heat until the vegetables are tender and slightly charred. Serve with a squeeze of fresh lime juice for an extra zing.

5. Teriyaki Glaze

For an Asian-inspired twist, this teriyaki glaze is a winner. Gather the following ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water

In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. In a separate bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water until smooth, then add it to the saucepan. Cook, stirring constantly, until the glaze thickens. Remove from heat and let it cool. Pour the teriyaki glaze over your skewered vegetables and let them marinate for at least 1 hour. Grill the skewers over medium-high heat until the vegetables are tender and caramelized. Serve with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

Get Your Grill On!

Now that you have these mouthwatering marinades in your arsenal, it’s time to fire up the grill and get cooking. Experiment with different combinations of vegetables, and don’t be afraid to get creative with your skewers. Whether you’re hosting a summer cookout or simply enjoying a quiet evening at home, these meatless marvels are sure to impress. So grab your tongs, embrace the sizzle, and indulge in the flavors of summer! Happy grilling!
