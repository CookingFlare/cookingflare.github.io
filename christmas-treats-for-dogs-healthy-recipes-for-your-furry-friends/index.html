Christmas Treats for Dogs: Healthy Recipes for Your Furry Friends


The holiday season is all about spreading joy and celebrating with loved ones, including our furry family members. As you prepare platters of festive meals and snacks for your human companions, why not whip up some wholesome Christmas treats for your dog as well? Homemade treats are a delicious and healthy way to show your pup how much you care, while also ensuring they stay safe and avoid harmful ingredients.

In this post, we’ll share a variety of holiday-inspired recipes for dogs that are both festive and nutritious. These treats are sure to have tails wagging and spirits soaring throughout the season!

Why Make Homemade Dog Treats for Christmas?

Store-bought dog snacks can sometimes contain fillers, preservatives, and artificial ingredients, potentially upsetting your dog’s health or digestion. By making treats yourself, you have full control over the ingredient list and can cater to your dog’s unique preferences or dietary needs.

Homemade Christmas dog treats are also a way to bond with your pet. Plus, they make thoughtful gifts for other pet-owners or are perfect for a doggie stocking stuffer!

Holiday Ingredients Your Dog Will Love

Before diving into the recipes, it’s important to choose ingredients that are safe and nutritious for dogs. Here are some festive flavors that your pup will enjoy:

  • Pumpkin: Rich in fiber, Vitamin A, and antioxidants, pumpkin is a great base for dog-friendly treats.
  • Peanut Butter (Unsalted, Unsweetened): A flavor dogs adore, packed with protein and healthy fats.
  • Carrots: Naturally sweet and rich in beta-carotene.
  • Apples (No Seeds): A juicy, fiber-packed treat for dogs.
  • Sweet Potatoes: Filled with vitamins and sweet goodness, they make an excellent ingredient for holiday treats.
  • Coconut: Offers healthy fats and adds a tropical twist to festive snacks.
  • Turkey (Cooked, Plain): A holiday staple and great protein source.
  • Oats: A gentle grain that’s easy on digestion.

Always ensure the ingredients you choose are free of toxic additives like artificial sweeteners, particularly xylitol, which is dangerous for dogs. Avoid nutmeg, raisins, chocolate, and any onion or garlic products.

Festive Christmas Treat Recipes for Dogs

1. Peppermint Pup Cookies

These peppermint-inspired cookies are crunchy, refreshing, and holiday-perfect—minus the actual mint, which dogs shouldn’t eat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups oat flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened coconut flakes
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon crushed dried parsley (for the peppermint look!)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix all ingredients together until a dough forms.
  3. Roll the dough out on a floured surface and use cookie cutters to create festive shapes like stars or candy canes.
  4. Place cookies on the baking sheet and bake for 15 minutes or until lightly golden.
  5. Allow to cool completely before serving.

2. Sweet Potato Christmas Stars

Sweet potato lends itself beautifully to a festive recipe that’s both healthy and easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium sweet potato (peeled and mashed)
  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons plain Greek yogurt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Combine mashed sweet potato, oat flour, egg, and yogurt in a bowl. Mix until the dough is slightly sticky but workable.
  3. Roll out dough and use star-shaped cookie cutters to form cut-outs.
  4. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until firm.
  5. Cool completely before serving or storing in an airtight container.

3. Turkey & Pumpkin Holiday Bites

Packed with protein and fiber, these soft bites are perfect for Christmas dinner with your pup.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup cooked ground turkey (plain)
  • 1/4 cup pure pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 1 egg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C) and grease a mini muffin tray.
  2. Mix all ingredients in a bowl until a wet, sticky dough forms.
  3. Spoon the mixture into mini muffin cups, filling each about halfway.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes or until firm to the touch.
  5. Let cool, then pop out from the tin and store in the fridge for up to a week.

4. Frozen Coconut Snowballs

If you live in a warmer climate or want an easy no-bake treat, these snowballs are perfect for your pup.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsweetened coconut flakes
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter (unsweetened and unsalted)
  • 1 tablespoon unsweetened applesauce

Instructions:

  1. Combine coconut flakes, peanut butter, and applesauce in a bowl. Mix until fully incorporated.
  2. Take small spoonfuls of the mixture and roll them into bite-sized balls.
  3. Freeze balls for at least 1 hour or until solid.
  4. Serve directly from the freezer for a cool treat!

5. Carrot & Apple Crunchies

These crunchy snacks are a simple, festive option for dogs who enjoy crispy treats.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped apples (seeded and cored)
  • 1 cup oat flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup water (as needed)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Combine all ingredients in a bowl, adding water gradually to form a firm dough.
  3. Roll out the dough and use cookie cutters to create shapes.
  4. Bake on a parchment-lined tray for 20-25 minutes or until hard and golden.
  5. Cool before serving.

Tips for Making Christmas Treats for Dogs

  • Portion Sizes Matter: Keep treat sizes small to avoid overfeeding your dog and disrupting their regular diet.
  • Store Properly: Refrigerate soft treats to extend their shelf life, or freeze batches for long-term storage.
  • Introduce Gradually: If your dog is trying a new ingredient for the first time, offer a small amount to ensure no allergies or adverse reactions.
  • Personalize: Feel free to swap out ingredients based on your dog’s preferences or dietary needs, but always check first to ensure they’re dog-safe.

Create a Christmas Tradition with Your Dog

Making holiday treats for your dog can become a cherished tradition. Whether you’re baking biscuits, crafting edible snowballs, or preparing turkey bites, your furry friend will relish the extra attention. It’s a chance to involve them in the festivities and create a stronger bond between you.

Remember, the greatest gift you can offer your dog this Christmas is your care and love. By choosing healthy ingredients and handmade snacks, you’re ensuring their well-being while also making them feel like part of the family celebrations.

So, tie on your apron, grab those cookie cutters, and start creating treats that will leave your pup woofing with delight. Happy Holidays!
