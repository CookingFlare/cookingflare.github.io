How to Make Spanish Bean Stew: Fabada Asturiana


Fabada Asturiana is a traditional Spanish bean stew that hails from the northern region of Asturias. Renowned for its rich flavors and hearty ingredients, this dish has won the hearts of many around the world. In this blog post, we’ll explore the origins of Fabada Asturiana, delve into its ingredients, and guide you through the cooking process step-by-step. By the end, you’ll be well-equipped to prepare this comforting dish in your own kitchen.

The Origins of Fabada Asturiana

Fabada Asturiana has deep-rooted cultural significance in Asturias, where it was originally crafted as a sustenance dish for farmers and laborers. With its origins tracing back to the 16th century, it was designed to be hearty and filling, perfect for providing energy after a long day’s work. Traditionally, the dish features fabes, a specific type of white bean that is larger and creamier than standard varieties, making it a perfect base for the stew.

Over the years, Fabada Asturiana has evolved but remains a staple in Asturian cuisine. It is often served during festive occasions and family gatherings, embodying the warmth and camaraderie of communal meals.

Ingredients for Fabada Asturiana

To create an authentic Fabada Asturiana, you’ll need several key ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

Main Ingredients:

  • Fabes de la Granja (Asturian white beans): 500 grams
  • Chorizo: 200 grams
  • Morcilla (blood sausage): 200 grams
  • Pancetta or lard: 150 grams
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced
  • Saffron: a pinch (optional)
  • Paprika: 1 tablespoon (preferably smoked)
  • Bay leaves: 2
  • Salt and pepper: to taste
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Water or broth: 1.5 liters

Optional Ingredients:

  • Carrots: 1 large, diced
  • Celery: 1 stalk, chopped
  • Fresh parsley: for garnish

With the ingredients gathered, let’s dive into how to make this delectable stew.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Soaking the Beans

The first step to achieving a perfect Fabada Asturiana is to soak the fabes. Place the beans in a large bowl and cover them with cold water. Allow them to soak overnight, or for at least 8 hours. This process softens the beans and reduces cooking time.

Step 2: Preparing the Ingredients

Once the beans have soaked, it’s time to prepare the other ingredients.

  1. Chop the Pancetta: Cut the pancetta into small cubes. If you’re using lard, you can simply chop it into small pieces.
  2. Slice the Sausages: Cut the chorizo and morcilla into thick slices. This will help them maintain their shape during cooking while infusing the stew with their flavors.
  3. Chop the Vegetables: Finely chop the onion and mince the garlic. If you’re using carrots and celery, chop them into small pieces as well.

Step 3: Cooking the Base

In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Once hot, add the pancetta or lard. Cook until it begins to render its fat and becomes crispy, about 3-4 minutes.

Next, add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, around 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and continue to cook for another minute, stirring frequently to avoid burning.

Step 4: Adding the Sausages

Once the onion and garlic are ready, add the slices of chorizo and morcilla to the pot. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sausages are browned and have released their flavors.

Step 5: Incorporating the Spices

Sprinkle in the paprika and saffron (if using), and stir well to combine. The spices should coat the sausage and vegetables, creating a fragrant base for the stew.

Step 6: Adding the Beans and Liquid

After the spices have been incorporated, drain the soaked beans and add them to the pot. Pour in 1.5 liters of water or broth. Add the bay leaves, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 7: Simmering the Stew

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the stew simmer gently for 1.5 to 2 hours. Stir occasionally, and add more water if necessary to keep the beans submerged. The goal is to cook the beans until they are tender and infused with the flavors of the meat and spices.

Step 8: Adjusting Seasoning and Serving

Once the beans are cooked to your desired tenderness, taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Remove the bay leaves before serving.

Step 9: Garnishing

For a beautiful presentation, serve the Fabada Asturiana in individual bowls, garnished with freshly chopped parsley. This adds a pop of color and a hint of freshness to the dish.

Additional Tips for the Perfect Fabada Asturiana

  • Slow Cooking: For an even richer flavor, consider cooking the stew in a slow cooker. This allows the flavors to meld beautifully over a longer period.
  • Serving Suggestions: Fabada Asturiana is best served with crusty bread for dipping. A side salad can also complement the dish well and add freshness.
  • Storing Leftovers: If you have any leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. The flavors continue to develop overnight, making the stew even tastier the next day.

Conclusion

Fabada Asturiana is more than just a bean stew; it’s a celebration of Asturian culture and a dish that brings people together around the table. Its rich flavors and hearty ingredients make it a perfect choice for family gatherings, cold winter nights, or anytime you crave something comforting. With this guide, you’re well on your way to creating an authentic Fabada Asturiana that will impress your friends and family.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy the warm, inviting flavors of this traditional Spanish dish. Happy cooking!
