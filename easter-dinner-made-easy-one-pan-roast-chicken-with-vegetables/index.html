Easter Dinner Made Easy: One-Pan Roast Chicken with Vegetables


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and of course, delicious food. If you’re looking for a hassle-free yet impressive meal to serve your loved ones this Easter, look no further than this One-Pan Roast Chicken with Vegetables recipe. Not only is it incredibly easy to prepare, but it also yields succulent, flavorful chicken and perfectly roasted vegetables, all cooked in just one pan. Get ready to impress your guests with minimal effort!

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken (approximately 4 pounds)
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 medium potatoes, cut into quarters
  • 1 large onion, cut into wedges
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of dried rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place a large roasting pan in the oven while it preheats.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, olive oil, dried rosemary, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

  3. Pat the chicken dry with paper towels. Rub the garlic and herb mixture all over the chicken, making sure to get it under the skin as well. This will infuse the chicken with incredible flavor.

  4. Remove the preheated roasting pan from the oven and carefully place the chicken in the center. Arrange the carrots, potatoes, and onion around the chicken, ensuring they are evenly distributed.

  5. Drizzle any remaining garlic and herb mixture over the vegetables. Season the vegetables with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Place the roasting pan back in the oven and roast for approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender. To ensure the chicken is cooked, insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the thigh. It should read 165°F (74°C).

  7. Once cooked, remove the pan from the oven and let the chicken rest for about 10 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful chicken.

  8. Serve the One-Pan Roast Chicken with Vegetables on a platter, garnished with fresh herbs if desired. Accompany it with a side of gravy or cranberry sauce, and enjoy a stress-free Easter dinner that will impress your guests.

Tips and Tricks

  • To add more variety to your meal, feel free to include other vegetables such as Brussels sprouts, parsnips, or butternut squash. Just make sure to adjust the cooking time accordingly.

  • For extra crispy skin, you can brush the chicken with melted butter or sprinkle it with paprika before roasting.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Use them in sandwiches, salads, or soups for a delicious post-Easter meal.

  • Don’t forget to save the chicken carcass to make a flavorful homemade chicken broth. Simply simmer it with water, vegetables, and herbs for a few hours, then strain and store the broth for future use.

Conclusion

With this One-Pan Roast Chicken with Vegetables recipe, Easter dinner just got a whole lot easier. By combining all the ingredients in one pan, you’ll save time on prep and cleanup, without compromising on flavor or presentation. The succulent chicken paired with perfectly roasted vegetables will create a memorable meal that will have your guests coming back for seconds. So, this Easter, sit back, relax, and enjoy the festivities, knowing that your delicious dinner is taken care of. Happy Easter and happy cooking!
