This dish is excellent with chicken, shrimp, or tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 300g chicken (or tofu), thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)
  • Cooked jasmine rice (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the chicken or tofu: Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the chicken or tofu and stir-fry until cooked through and lightly browned.

  2. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add the onion, red bell pepper, and zucchini to the pan. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still crisp.

  3. Add the green curry paste: Stir in the green curry paste and cook for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

  4. Make the sauce: Add the coconut milk, fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir well to combine, and bring to a simmer.

  5. Finish and serve: Once the sauce has thickened slightly, garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve the green curry stir-fry over jasmine rice.

Conclusion

Thai stir-fries offer an endless variety of flavors and ingredients to play with, making them a fantastic option for quick and satisfying meals. From the iconic Pad Thai to spicy Thai basil chicken and aromatic green curry stir-fry, these dishes allow you to bring the vibrant flavors of Thailand right to your kitchen. By adjusting the ingredients and level of heat, you can create a personalized stir-fry experience that suits your preferences perfectly.

The beauty of Thai stir-fry recipes is that they are flexible, quick, and easy to make, yet full of complex flavors that will elevate your cooking. Whether you’re making a dish for a weeknight dinner or a gathering with friends and family, these Thai stir-fries are sure to impress. So, get ready to explore the diverse world of Thai stir-fry and start cooking these delicious, quick, and flavorful meals today!
