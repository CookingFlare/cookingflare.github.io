Maryland Crab Cakes with Homemade Tartar Sauce


Crab cakes are a classic dish in Maryland, where the sweet, delicate taste of fresh blue crab shines through in every bite. The state’s coastal proximity ensures that the crab used in these cakes is often harvested the same day, ensuring unparalleled freshness. The preparation of Maryland crab cakes is simple yet requires attention to detail to balance the delicate flavor of the crab with the right blend of seasonings. Paired with homemade tartar sauce, this dish is a true celebration of the sea’s bounty.

Why Maryland Crab Cakes Are Special

Maryland crab cakes are known for their high crab-to-filler ratio. Unlike some recipes where fillers like breadcrumbs and mayonnaise dominate, Maryland crab cakes highlight the sweetness of the crab. The cakes are lightly bound together with just enough filler to keep the delicate meat intact while maintaining its natural flavors. The secret to the best crab cakes lies in using fresh, quality crab meat, minimal binding ingredients, and a light, crispy exterior.

While crab cakes are often fried, you can also bake them to achieve a lighter texture. Regardless of how they are cooked, Maryland crab cakes remain a go-to comfort food for many seafood lovers, whether as an appetizer, a main dish, or even a sandwich filling.

In this recipe, we’ll take you through the process of making the most flavorful Maryland crab cakes, along with a tangy homemade tartar sauce that perfectly complements the crab’s sweetness.

Ingredients for Maryland Crab Cakes

For the Crab Cakes:

  • 1 lb fresh lump crab meat (preferably blue crab)
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tsp Old Bay seasoning (or to taste)
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped green onions (or chives)
  • 1/2 cup crushed saltine crackers (or breadcrumbs)
  • 1-2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp butter (for cooking)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For the Homemade Tartar Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh dill
  • 2 tbsp finely chopped pickles or relish
  • 1 tsp capers, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

How to Make Maryland Crab Cakes

Step 1: Prepare the Crab Meat

The first step in creating perfect Maryland crab cakes is selecting high-quality crab meat. Fresh lump crab meat is ideal because it provides the perfect balance of flavor and texture. Be sure to pick through the crab meat carefully to remove any remaining shell fragments.

Once your crab meat is clean and ready, set it aside in a large bowl.

Step 2: Make the Crab Cake Mixture

In a separate bowl, combine the mayonnaise, egg, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, Old Bay seasoning, parsley, green onions, and a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Whisk these ingredients together until fully combined. The egg and mayonnaise provide binding, while the mustard and Worcestershire sauce add depth and complexity to the flavor.

Next, gently fold in the crab meat. Be careful not to break up the lumps of crab too much; you want to retain those delicate chunks. Gradually fold in the crushed saltine crackers (or breadcrumbs) to absorb excess moisture and help bind the mixture.

Taste the mixture and season with salt, pepper, and more Old Bay seasoning as needed. The crab mixture should hold together when formed into a patty but should still feel light and not overly dense.

Step 3: Shape the Crab Cakes

Once the crab mixture is ready, form it into patties. Use your hands or a spoon to create small, even cakes about 3 inches in diameter. Aim for a thickness of around 1 inch to ensure they cook through properly while maintaining a crisp exterior.

After shaping, place the crab cakes on a parchment-lined baking sheet and refrigerate them for at least 30 minutes. Chilling the crab cakes helps them hold together during cooking and ensures they don’t fall apart.

Step 4: Cook the Crab Cakes

You can either pan-fry or bake your crab cakes, depending on your preference.

Pan-Frying:

To pan-fry, heat a large skillet over medium heat and add the butter. Once the butter is melted and sizzling, add the crab cakes to the skillet. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. The goal is to cook the cakes until the exterior is crispy and golden, while the inside remains moist and tender.

Baking:

If you prefer a lighter, less greasy option, you can bake the crab cakes. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the crab cakes on a baking sheet and brush them lightly with melted butter. Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the cakes are golden brown and crispy on the outside.

Step 5: Serve the Crab Cakes

Once the crab cakes are cooked, serve them immediately. They are perfect on their own or served with a side of tangy homemade tartar sauce, a wedge of lemon, and a few sprigs of parsley for garnish.

Homemade Tartar Sauce Recipe

Tartar sauce is a must-have when serving crab cakes. Its creamy, tangy profile complements the sweetness of the crab and balances out the richness of the dish. Here’s how to make a simple, flavorful tartar sauce from scratch.

Step 1: Combine the Ingredients

In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, and lemon juice. The mustard adds a bit of tang, while the lemon juice provides acidity to balance the creaminess of the mayonnaise.

Step 2: Add Flavorful Additions

Next, stir in the chopped fresh dill, pickles (or relish), and capers (if using). Dill is the classic herb used in tartar sauce, giving it a fresh, herby note. The pickles or relish add a crunchy texture and sweetness, while the capers bring a touch of briny flavor.

Step 3: Adjust Seasoning

Taste the tartar sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and freshly ground black pepper. If you prefer a spicier sauce, you can add a dash of hot sauce or a pinch of cayenne pepper.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

Refrigerate the tartar sauce for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together. The longer it sits, the better the flavors will develop.

Serve the crab cakes with a generous side of this tartar sauce for the perfect flavor pairing. The creamy, tangy sauce will elevate the sweet crab cakes to another level of deliciousness.

Tips for the Best Maryland Crab Cakes

  1. Use Fresh Crab Meat: Always opt for fresh crab meat, ideally lump or jumbo lump, to ensure the best texture and flavor. Avoid canned crab, as it doesn’t have the same sweetness or consistency.

  2. Minimal Filler: The key to great Maryland crab cakes is using just enough filler to hold the cakes together. Too much filler will make the cakes heavy and detract from the delicate crab flavor. Stick with simple ingredients like crackers or breadcrumbs, which allow the crab to shine.

  3. Chill Before Cooking: Don’t skip the chilling step. Refrigerating the crab cakes helps them set and prevents them from falling apart during cooking.

  4. Cook on Medium Heat: Whether frying or baking, always cook your crab cakes over medium heat to ensure that the outside is golden and crispy while the inside stays moist and tender.

  5. Customize the Flavor: While Old Bay seasoning is a classic choice for Maryland crab cakes, feel free to adjust the seasoning to your taste. A pinch of cayenne pepper or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice can add a little extra kick.

Conclusion

Maryland crab cakes are a beloved regional dish that showcases the sweet, delicate flavor of fresh crab. With just the right balance of seasoning, minimal filler, and a crispy exterior, these crab cakes are sure to be a hit at any meal. Paired with a homemade tartar sauce made from scratch, you can create a dish that is both comforting and sophisticated.

By following these simple steps and tips, you can enjoy an authentic Maryland crab cake experience right in your own kitchen. Whether served as an appetizer, a main course, or a sandwich, this dish is guaranteed to satisfy any seafood lover’s cravings. So, gather your ingredients, get cooking, and enjoy a taste of Maryland at home!
