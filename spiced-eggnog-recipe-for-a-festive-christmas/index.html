Spiced Eggnog Recipe for a Festive Christmas


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to embrace the festive cheer than with a rich and creamy cup of spiced eggnog? This classic Christmas drink has been a beloved tradition for centuries, and it’s no wonder why – the combination of creamy milk, aromatic spices, and a hint of boozy warmth makes for the perfect indulgence during the chilly winter months. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making spiced eggnog from scratch, infusing it with the warmth and flavors of the season.

Ingredients

To create the perfect spiced eggnog, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup bourbon (optional)
  • Whipped cream, for garnish
  • Ground cinnamon, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks and sugar together until the mixture is smooth and creamy.

  2. In a saucepan, combine the whole milk, heavy cream, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and ground cloves. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a simmer. Remove the saucepan from the heat.

  3. Slowly pour the hot milk mixture into the bowl with the egg yolks and sugar, whisking constantly to temper the eggs.

  4. Pour the mixture back into the saucepan and return it to the stove over low heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon.

  5. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract. If you’d like to add a boozy kick to your eggnog, stir in the bourbon at this point.

  6. Strain the eggnog through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container to remove any lumps or bits of cooked egg.

  7. Cover the eggnog and refrigerate it for at least 4 hours, or until it’s thoroughly chilled.

  8. Once the eggnog is chilled, give it a good stir before serving to ensure the spices are well distributed.

  9. To serve, pour the spiced eggnog into glasses and top each serving with a dollop of whipped cream. Sprinkle a pinch of ground cinnamon on top for a festive finish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a non-alcoholic version, simply omit the bourbon from the recipe.
  • To make a lighter version, you can use low-fat milk and light cream instead of whole milk and heavy cream.
  • Experiment with different spices, such as cardamom or allspice, to create your own unique spiced eggnog blend.

Conclusion

There’s something truly magical about sipping on a glass of homemade spiced eggnog as you gather with loved ones during the holiday season. Whether you choose to enjoy it by the crackling fire or as a delightful accompaniment to your Christmas feast, this spiced eggnog recipe is sure to bring warmth and joy to your festive celebrations. Embrace the tradition of crafting this timeless drink from scratch, and revel in the heartwarming flavors that define the spirit of Christmas. Cheers to a joyous and delicious holiday season!
