Sibling Rivalry- Chinese Chefs Share their One-of-a-Kind Recipes


In the world of Chinese cuisine, sibling rivalry has driven many chefs to create masterpieces that leave unforgettable impressions on every palate. The competitive spirit between Chef Zhu and Chef Wang, for example, resulted in two exquisite culinary creations that showcase China’s diverse and rich culinary landscape. In this blog post, we’ll feature their unique recipes and delve into the tales that inspired them.

Chef Zhu – Shanghai Fried Dumplings with Spicy Sesame Sauce

Hailing from Shanghai, Chef Zhu takes the traditional dumpling recipe to a whole new level with his fiery and aromatic take on the dish. In this family recipe, ingredients like ground pork, shrimp, and Chinese chives are combined to make the perfect dough wrapper. Each dumpling is stuffed with a juicy and savory filling that’s sure to ignite your taste buds.

To make the dish, first prepare the dough by mixing flour and boiling water. Next, knead the dough until it becomes soft and pliable, then divide it into smaller portions. You’ll also need to prepare filling for the dumplings, finely chopping up ingredients and combining them with your chosen protein.

Once you have the filling and dough ready, assemble the dumplings by wrapping each portion of filling in the dough, making sure to seal it well. In a large pan over high heat, add some oil and fry the dumplings until they achieve a beautiful golden-brown color. With a spatula, transfer them to a plate with a little bit of space between each dumpling.

Now, it’s time to prepare the spicy sesame sauce. Just mix hoisin sauce, chili paste, sesame oil, and a few drops of rice vinegar to create a uniform and delicious sauce. Drizzle it over the fried dumplings, ensuring that each dumpling is coated in the sauce. Finally, garnish with chopped scallions and sesame seeds, and serve the dumplings immediately.

Chef Wang – Sichuan Braised Beef with Tofu Puffs

Coming from Sichuan province, Chef Wang’s passion for bold and spicy flavors is evident in his signature dish—braised beef with tofu puffs. This rich and hearty dish has played a significant role in showcasing China’s vibrant regional cuisines to the world.

Chef Wang’s recipe begins with cutting beef into pieces and marinating it in salt, pepper, and a small amount of Sichuan peppercorns to help tenderize the meat. While the beef is marinating, become acquainted with Sichuan cuisine by sautéing chopped garlic, ginger, and green onions in a pot with oil. Add the marinated beef to the pot, frying until each piece becomes dark reddish-brown and fragrant.

Once the beef is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside. Next, prepare your Sichuan-style broth by boiling water with star anise, cinnamon, bay leaves, and other spices until it’s flavorful and aromatic. Add a splash of soy sauce and rice wine, then return the beef to the pot, letting it simmer and absorb all of those incredible flavors.

While the beef is simmering, chop up some fresh tofu puffs and add them to the pot. Tofu puffs, with their tender yet chewy texture, absorb the sauce beautifully, perfectly complementing the bold flavors of the braised beef. Cook everything together until the tofu puffs are soft and delicious, and your Sichuan braised beef with tofu puffs is ready to serve.

Conclusion

These signature dishes by Chef Zhu and Chef Wang not only display their passion and rivalry for culinary excellence but also tell tales of their regional heritage. Each dish showcases distinct ingredients and bold flavors that are rooted in traditional techniques and transformed by the creativity of master chefs. Sibling rivalry has certainly brought about a beautiful and delectable result in the world of Chinese cuisine, and we can’t wait to see what dish these talented chefs create next.
