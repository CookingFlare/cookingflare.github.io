Mediterranean-Inspired Stuffed Peppers: A Colorful Delight

As the weather warms up, our taste buds crave lighter, fresher flavors that evoke the sun-kissed Mediterranean coast. One dish that perfectly captures the essence of this region is the humble stuffed pepper. By filling bell peppers with a vibrant mix of ingredients, you’ll create a dish that’s not only visually stunning but also packed with nutrients and flavor. Ingredients 4 large bell peppers, any color 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 cup cooked brown rice 1 cup black beans, cooked 1 cup diced tomatoes 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 2 tbsp olive oil 1 tsp smoked paprika Salt and pepper, to taste 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint (optional) Instructions Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut off the tops of the peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place them in a baking dish. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant. Stir in the cooked brown rice, black beans, diced tomatoes, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until the mixture is well combined. Stuff each pepper with the rice mixture, filling them as full as possible. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and top each pepper with crumbled feta cheese and chopped parsley. Return the peppers to the oven and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Remove the peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve warm, garnished with chopped fresh mint if desired. The Magic of Mediterranean Flavors The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its emphasis on whole grains, fresh vegetables, and healthy fats. By incorporating these ingredients into your stuffed peppers, you’ll create a dish that’s both nutritious and flavorful. The smoked paprika adds a subtle smokiness, while the feta cheese provides a tangy, creamy element. The fresh parsley and mint add a bright, herbaceous note that complements the sweetness of the bell peppers. ...

Wholesome Mediterranean Vegetarian Recipes

The Mediterranean diet is renowned for its health benefits, and it’s no wonder why. Bursting with fresh vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and healthy fats, this diet is not only nutritious but also delicious. If you’re looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these wholesome Mediterranean vegetarian recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and nourish your body. 1. Greek Spinach and Feta Pie Ingredients: 1 package of phyllo dough 2 lbs of fresh spinach 1 cup of crumbled feta cheese 1 onion, finely chopped 3 cloves of garlic, minced 1/4 cup of olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until fragrant. Add the fresh spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, squeeze out any excess liquid from the spinach and transfer it to a large bowl. Add the crumbled feta cheese to the spinach mixture and season with salt and pepper. Mix until well combined. Lay out a sheet of phyllo dough and brush it lightly with olive oil. Repeat with 5 more sheets, layering them on top of each other. Place the spinach and feta mixture on top of the phyllo dough and spread it out evenly. Layer 6 more sheets of phyllo dough on top of the spinach mixture, brushing each sheet with olive oil. Tuck in the edges of the phyllo dough to form a neat pie. Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the pie is golden brown. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. 2. Mediterranean Quinoa Salad Ingredients: 1 cup of quinoa 1 cucumber, diced 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved 1/2 cup of Kalamata olives, pitted and sliced 1/4 cup of red onion, finely chopped 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped 1/4 cup of fresh mint, chopped Juice of 1 lemon 3 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Rinse the quinoa under cold water and cook according to package instructions. Once cooked, fluff it with a fork and let it cool. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, diced cucumber, cherry tomatoes, Kalamata olives, red onion, parsley, and mint. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil, salt, and pepper. Pour the dressing over the quinoa salad and toss until everything is well coated. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld. 3. Stuffed Bell Peppers with Mediterranean Couscous Ingredients: 4 large bell peppers 1 cup of couscous 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1/2 cup of sun-dried tomatoes, chopped 1/4 cup of pine nuts 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 teaspoon of dried oregano 1 teaspoon of dried basil Salt and pepper to taste 2 tablespoons of olive oil Instructions: Preheat the oven to 375°F. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. In a large bowl, combine the couscous, chickpeas, sun-dried tomatoes, pine nuts, garlic, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Stuff the mixture into the bell peppers and place them in a baking dish. Drizzle the stuffed bell peppers with olive oil and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
