Grilled Side Dishes to Serve with Your 4th of July BBQ


The 4th of July is a time to celebrate with family, friends, and of course, delicious food. A BBQ is often the centerpiece of the holiday, with juicy burgers, sizzling hot dogs, and perfectly grilled steaks stealing the spotlight. But while the main dishes are essential, the side dishes are what truly elevate the meal. What better way to complement your BBQ than with a selection of grilled side dishes that will bring out the smoky flavors and the essence of summer?

Grilling side dishes allows you to infuse that irresistible charred flavor while keeping the preparation light and fun. Whether you’re looking for something fresh and crisp or rich and comforting, we’ve compiled a list of the best grilled side dishes to make your 4th of July BBQ a memorable feast.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Herb Butter

Corn on the cob is a classic BBQ side, and grilling it brings out its natural sweetness while adding a smoky flavor. The addition of herb butter makes it even more irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush each ear of corn with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the corn on the grill, turning occasionally, for about 10-12 minutes, until the kernels are charred and tender.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter with the parsley, chives, garlic powder, and paprika.
  5. Once the corn is cooked, remove it from the grill and slather the herb butter over each ear. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Grilled corn on the cob with herb butter is a simple yet flavorful side that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Grilled Mediterranean Vegetable Platter

A medley of fresh vegetables, grilled to perfection, makes for an ideal BBQ side. The smoky flavors of grilled zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, and red onions paired with a zesty vinaigrette bring Mediterranean vibes to your BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 2 zucchinis, sliced into rounds
  • 1 eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 2 red bell peppers, cut into quarters
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss the vegetables in olive oil, balsamic vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, and red onion for about 4-5 minutes per side, until they are tender and slightly charred.
  4. Arrange the grilled vegetables on a platter and garnish with fresh basil.
  5. Drizzle a little extra olive oil and balsamic vinegar over the top before serving.

This grilled Mediterranean vegetable platter not only adds color to your BBQ spread but also provides a healthy and delicious option for your guests.

Grilled Sweet Potato Wedges with Lime Crema

Sweet potatoes are naturally sweet and earthy, and grilling them intensifies their flavor. Paired with a zesty lime crema, these grilled sweet potato wedges become a show-stopping side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss the sweet potato wedges in olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the wedges for 10-12 minutes per side, until they are tender and crispy on the edges.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the sour cream, mayonnaise, lime zest, lime juice, and chopped cilantro to make the lime crema.
  5. Serve the grilled sweet potatoes with a generous dollop of lime crema on the side.

These grilled sweet potato wedges with lime crema are a crowd-pleasing side that adds a smoky, sweet, and tangy flavor to your BBQ.

Grilled Asparagus with Parmesan and Lemon

Grilled asparagus is a light, yet flavorful side that complements a variety of grilled meats. The addition of Parmesan and a squeeze of fresh lemon adds a burst of flavor that makes it irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch of asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the asparagus in olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the asparagus for 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until tender and slightly charred.
  4. Once grilled, transfer the asparagus to a serving platter. Sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese and drizzle with fresh lemon juice and zest.
  5. Serve immediately, and enjoy the vibrant and tangy flavors.

Grilled asparagus with Parmesan and lemon is a simple yet elegant side that adds a burst of freshness to your BBQ spread.

Grilled Caprese Salad Skewers

Caprese salad is a beloved Italian dish, and grilling the ingredients enhances the flavors, especially the mozzarella. These skewers are easy to assemble and make a perfect appetizer or side for your BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 12 cherry tomatoes
  • 12 small mozzarella balls (bocconcini or ciliegine)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Assemble the skewers by alternating the cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, and fresh basil leaves.
  3. Brush the skewers with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Grill the skewers for about 2-3 minutes, just enough to get grill marks on the tomatoes and melt the cheese slightly.
  5. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and serve immediately.

Grilled Caprese salad skewers bring all the delicious flavors of a classic Caprese salad into a smoky, grilled form, making them a perfect BBQ side dish.

Grilled Garlic Mushrooms

Garlic mushrooms are an easy, flavorful side that pairs beautifully with grilled meats. The mushrooms soak up the smoky flavors from the grill, and the garlic adds a wonderful aroma.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups button mushrooms, cleaned and trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Toss the mushrooms in olive oil, minced garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Grill the mushrooms for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are tender and slightly charred.
  4. Serve immediately as a savory side that perfectly complements your grilled meats.

These grilled garlic mushrooms are savory, aromatic, and simple to prepare, making them an excellent addition to your BBQ.

Grilled Caesar Salad

For a refreshing side that brings a twist to the traditional Caesar salad, try grilling the romaine lettuce. The charred edges of the lettuce add a smoky flavor that pairs beautifully with creamy Caesar dressing and crunchy croutons.

Ingredients:

  • 2 heads of romaine lettuce, halved lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup Caesar dressing
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Croutons for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the cut sides of the romaine lettuce halves with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill the lettuce for 1-2 minutes, just long enough to get grill marks and a slight char.
  4. Drizzle with Caesar dressing, sprinkle with grated Parmesan, and top with croutons.
  5. Serve immediately, and enjoy this grilled version of a classic salad.

A grilled Caesar salad adds a smoky and crisp element to your BBQ, making it an exciting twist on the traditional dish.

Grilled Panzanella Salad

Panzanella is an Italian bread salad that is perfect for summer. Grilling the bread and vegetables before tossing them together adds a delicious depth of flavor to this refreshing side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of crusty bread, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the bread cubes with olive oil and grill them for 4-5 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are crispy and charred.
  3. Grill the cherry tomatoes and cucumber slices for about 2-3 minutes, just enough to get grill marks.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the grilled bread, vegetables, red onion, and fresh basil.
  5. Drizzle with red wine vinegar, season with salt and pepper, and toss to combine.
  6. Serve immediately for a refreshing and smoky salad.

Grilled panzanella salad brings a burst of fresh flavors and texture to your BBQ table.

Final Thoughts

Grilled side dishes are a fantastic way to elevate your 4th of July BBQ, offering a variety of flavors and textures that complement your main dishes. From smoky grilled corn on the cob to savory grilled vegetables and creamy grilled potato salad, there’s something for every palate. These sides are easy to prepare and packed with flavor, making them perfect for your summer celebrations. So fire up the grill and start cooking these delicious side dishes that will impress your guests and make your BBQ a hit!
