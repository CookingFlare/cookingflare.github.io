Loaded Baked Potato Soup: A Comfort Food Favorite


Nothing warms the soul quite like a bowl of hearty potato soup. And when it comes to comfort food, it doesn’t get much better than loaded baked potato soup. This creamy, flavorful dish is the perfect combination of rich potatoes, savory bacon, and gooey cheese. Whether you’re looking for a cozy weeknight dinner or a crowd-pleasing party appetizer, loaded baked potato soup is sure to hit the spot.

Ingredients

  • 6 large russet potatoes, washed and cubed
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1 cup of sour cream
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, cook the bacon until crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside, leaving the bacon grease in the pot.

  2. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the cubed potatoes to the pot and pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the potatoes are tender.

  4. Using a potato masher or immersion blender, partially mash the potatoes in the pot. This will help thicken the soup and give it a creamy texture.

  5. Stir in the milk, sour cream, and shredded cheddar cheese. Continue to cook the soup on low heat until the cheese is melted and the soup is heated through.

  6. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. Be sure to taste as you go and adjust the seasoning accordingly.

  7. Ladle the loaded baked potato soup into bowls and garnish with the crumbled bacon and chopped green onions.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the loaded baked potato soup as a main dish with a side of crusty bread or a fresh green salad.

  • For a heartier meal, top the soup with additional shredded cheese, sour cream, and chopped chives.

  • If you’re looking to add some extra protein, stir in cooked chicken or crispy fried tofu.

  • This soup also makes a fantastic appetizer for parties. Serve it in shot glasses or small bowls for a bite-sized treat.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a smoother soup, use an immersion blender to puree the potatoes completely.

  • If you prefer a chunkier soup, reserve some of the cubed potatoes before mashing.

  • Don’t skip the garnishes! The crumbled bacon and chopped green onions add a burst of flavor and a nice crunch to the soup.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat the soup on the stovetop over low heat, stirring occasionally.

  • Feel free to customize the toppings to suit your taste. Try adding a dollop of hot sauce, a sprinkle of paprika, or a drizzle of truffle oil for an extra touch of flavor.

Conclusion

Loaded baked potato soup is the ultimate comfort food. It’s creamy, flavorful, and packed with all the classic toppings you love. Whether you’re enjoying a bowl on a chilly winter night or serving it at a gathering with friends, this soup is sure to be a hit. So grab your apron and get ready to indulge in a bowl of pure comfort.
