The French 75: A Classic Champagne Cocktail with a Twist


The French 75 is a classic cocktail that has stood the test of time. With its refreshing and bubbly nature, it’s the perfect drink to elevate any occasion. In this recipe, we’ll be putting a unique twist on the traditional French 75 by adding some unexpected flavors that will take your taste buds on a delightful journey. So, let’s dive in and discover how to make this enticing cocktail!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 ounces gin
  • 3/4 ounce freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 ounce simple syrup
  • Champagne
  • Lemon twist, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a cocktail shaker, combine the gin, lemon juice, and simple syrup.
  2. Fill the shaker with ice and shake vigorously for about 15 seconds to ensure the ingredients are well mixed and chilled.
  3. Strain the mixture into a chilled Champagne flute, leaving some room at the top for the Champagne.
  4. Top off the cocktail with Champagne, pouring slowly to avoid excessive fizzing.
  5. Gently stir the cocktail to incorporate the Champagne with the other ingredients.
  6. Garnish with a lemon twist for an added touch of elegance.

The Twist: Adding a Floral Note

To give this classic cocktail a unique twist, we’ll be adding a floral note using lavender syrup. Lavender brings a delicate and aromatic quality to the French 75, making it even more enchanting. Here’s how to make the lavender syrup:

Lavender Syrup Ingredients

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons dried lavender flowers

Lavender Syrup Instructions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the water and granulated sugar.
  2. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has completely dissolved.
  3. Add the dried lavender flowers to the saucepan and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the saucepan from heat and let the syrup cool completely.
  5. Strain the syrup through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the lavender flowers.
  6. Transfer the lavender syrup to a clean bottle or jar and store it in the refrigerator until ready to use.

To incorporate the lavender twist into the French 75:

  1. Replace the traditional simple syrup with 1/2 ounce of lavender syrup in the cocktail shaker.
  2. Follow the same instructions as before, shaking the gin, lemon juice, and lavender syrup with ice.
  3. Strain the mixture into a Champagne flute and top it off with Champagne.
  4. Stir gently to combine the flavors and garnish with a lemon twist.

The Perfect Pairing

The French 75 is a versatile cocktail that pairs well with a variety of dishes. Its bright and effervescent nature makes it an excellent companion to both savory and sweet flavors. Here are a few pairing suggestions to enhance your culinary experience:

  • Savory Pairing: Serve the French 75 alongside a plate of oysters on the half shell. The cocktail’s citrusy notes and bubbles complement the brininess of the oysters, creating a harmonious balance of flavors.

  • Sweet Pairing: Indulge your sweet tooth by pairing the French 75 with a decadent slice of lemon tart. The tangy lemon flavors in both the cocktail and the dessert will elevate each other, resulting in a delightful symphony of taste.

Conclusion

The French 75 is a timeless classic that never fails to impress. By adding a twist of lavender, you can elevate this cocktail to new heights, enticing your guests with its unique flavors and aromas. Whether you’re celebrating a special occasion or simply looking to enjoy a sophisticated drink, the French 75 with a twist is the perfect choice. So, gather your ingredients, mix up this delightful concoction, and savor the moment with every sip. Cheers!
