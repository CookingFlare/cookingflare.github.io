Savory Spinach and Feta Quiche: A Crowd-Pleasing Easter Dish


Easter is a time of renewal and celebration, often marked by gatherings with family and friends. One of the most delightful ways to celebrate this festive season is through the joy of cooking. If you’re looking for a dish that is not only delicious but also visually appealing, look no further than a savory spinach and feta quiche. This recipe is perfect for brunch, lunch, or even a light dinner, making it a versatile addition to your Easter menu.

The Appeal of Quiche

Quiche is a classic French dish that has gained popularity worldwide. Its rich, creamy filling encased in a flaky pastry crust makes it an irresistible option for any meal. The beauty of quiche lies in its versatility; you can mix and match ingredients to suit your tastes and dietary preferences.

Spinach and feta quiche, in particular, is a crowd-pleaser. The combination of fresh spinach, tangy feta cheese, and creamy egg custard creates a harmonious flavor profile that delights the palate. Plus, it’s an excellent way to sneak in some greens, making it a relatively healthy choice.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a scrumptious spinach and feta quiche, gather the following ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 4-5 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg (optional)

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh herbs (dill or parsley)
  • Additional feta cheese for topping

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making the Crust

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
    • Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  2. Chill the Dough:

    • Form the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. This step helps to relax the gluten, resulting in a flakier crust.

  3. Roll Out the Dough:

    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Transfer the dough to the pie plate, pressing it gently into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess dough hanging over the edges.

  4. Blind Bake:

    • Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment. Bake for an additional 10 minutes or until golden brown. Set aside to cool.

Preparing the Filling

  1. Sauté the Vegetables:

    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté for about 5 minutes, or until translucent.
    • Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant. Add the chopped spinach and cook until wilted, about 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.

  2. Mix the Egg Mixture:

    • In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg (if using).

  3. Combine the Ingredients:

    • Stir in the sautéed spinach mixture and crumbled feta cheese into the egg mixture, ensuring everything is evenly distributed.

Assembling the Quiche

  1. Pour the Filling:

    • Pour the spinach and feta filling into the pre-baked crust, spreading it evenly.

  2. Bake the Quiche:

    • Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-45 minutes, or until the center is set and lightly browned on top. A knife inserted into the center should come out clean.

  3. Cool and Serve:

    • Once baked, remove the quiche from the oven and let it cool for about 10-15 minutes before slicing. This allows the filling to set further, making it easier to cut clean slices.

Serving Suggestions

This savory spinach and feta quiche can be enjoyed warm, at room temperature, or even cold. Serve it with a simple side salad for a light meal or pair it with roasted potatoes for something heartier. Consider garnishing with fresh herbs or additional feta for an extra touch of flavor.

Storage Tips

If you have leftovers (though they are unlikely to last long!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. You can also freeze slices of quiche for up to 2 months. To reheat, simply thaw overnight in the fridge and warm in the oven at 350°F (175°C) until heated through.

Why This Quiche is Perfect for Easter

Easter brunch is all about celebrating with loved ones and enjoying seasonal ingredients. Spinach is at its peak during spring, making this quiche not only delicious but also timely. The bright green spinach, combined with the white feta and golden crust, creates a visually stunning dish that is sure to impress your guests.

Additionally, this quiche can easily be made in advance, allowing you to spend more time with your family and friends rather than slaving away in the kitchen. Its versatility means it can be served alongside other Easter favorites like glazed ham, roasted vegetables, and a variety of fresh breads.

Conclusion

A savory spinach and feta quiche is an ideal centerpiece for your Easter celebration. With its rich flavors, beautiful presentation, and the ability to be made ahead of time, it checks all the boxes for a successful holiday dish. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying a quiet meal with family, this quiche is sure to be a hit.

So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and prepare to impress your loved ones with this delightful quiche. Happy cooking and Happy Easter!
