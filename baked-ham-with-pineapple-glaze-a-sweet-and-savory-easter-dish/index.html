Baked Ham with Pineapple Glaze: A Sweet and Savory Easter Dish


Easter is a time for celebration, family gatherings, and of course, delicious food. One dish that has become a staple at Easter feasts is the classic baked ham with pineapple glaze. This sweet and savory dish is a crowd-pleaser and is sure to impress your guests. In this blog post, we’ll take a closer look at how to prepare and bake a mouthwatering ham with a delectable pineapple glaze that will have everyone asking for seconds.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to make this delightful baked ham with pineapple glaze:

  • 1 spiral-cut, bone-in ham
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 can pineapple rings
  • Maraschino cherries for garnish
  • Cloves for studding the ham (optional)

Preparation

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Place the ham in a roasting pan and score the surface with shallow diagonal cuts, creating a diamond pattern. If desired, you can stud the ham with whole cloves at the intersections of the cuts for added flavor and a decorative touch.
  3. In a small saucepan, combine the brown sugar, pineapple juice, Dijon mustard, and ground cloves. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the glaze is smooth and slightly thickened.
  4. Arrange the pineapple rings on top of the ham, securing them with toothpicks, and place a cherry in the center of each pineapple ring.
  5. Pour the pineapple glaze over the ham, making sure to coat the entire surface evenly.

Baking

Once you’ve prepared the ham with the pineapple glaze, it’s time to bake it to perfection:

  1. Cover the ham with aluminum foil and bake for 1 hour in the preheated oven.
  2. After 1 hour, remove the foil and baste the ham with the pan drippings. Return the ham to the oven and continue baking for an additional 30-45 minutes, basting every 15 minutes, until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).
  3. Once the ham is fully cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing.

Serving

Serving the baked ham with pineapple glaze is just as important as preparing it. Here are a few tips for a stunning presentation:

  • Arrange the sliced ham on a platter, and if desired, garnish with additional pineapple rings and cherries.
  • Serve the remaining pineapple glaze on the side as a delicious dipping sauce.
  • Pair the ham with your favorite Easter sides, such as scalloped potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh salad, for a well-rounded and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Baked ham with pineapple glaze is a timeless Easter dish that never fails to delight. The combination of sweet and savory flavors, along with the tender juiciness of the ham, makes it a standout centerpiece for any Easter feast. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or having an intimate family dinner, this recipe is sure to be a hit. So, this Easter, treat your loved ones to a memorable and mouthwatering meal with this classic baked ham with pineapple glaze recipe.
