5 Simple and Affordable Grain Side Dish Recipes


Looking for some delicious and budget-friendly grain side dish recipes? Look no further! In this post, we’ll be sharing five simple and affordable recipes that are sure to please your taste buds. From quinoa to rice, these grain-based dishes are packed with flavor and will complement any main course. So, let’s get started!

1. Garlic Butter Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of white rice
  • 2 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium-sized saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the rice to the saucepan and stir to coat it evenly with the butter and garlic.
  4. Pour in the water, season with salt and pepper, and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan, and let the rice simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until all the water is absorbed and the rice is cooked through.
  6. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving. Enjoy!

2. Lemon Herb Quinoa

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of quinoa
  • 2 cups of vegetable broth
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh herbs (such as parsley or basil), chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  4. Remove from heat and let it sit for a few minutes.
  5. Fluff the quinoa with a fork, then add the lemon juice and fresh herbs.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, and mix well. Serve warm.

3. Mexican-Style Couscous

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of couscous
  • 1 ½ cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 can of black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup of corn kernels
  • 1 small red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and chopped (optional)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil.
  2. Stir in the couscous, cover, and remove from heat. Let it sit for about 5 minutes.
  3. Fluff the couscous with a fork, then transfer it to a large bowl.
  4. Add the black beans, corn, red bell pepper, red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, and cilantro.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and toss everything together until well combined.
  6. Serve chilled or at room temperature. Enjoy the vibrant flavors of this Mexican-inspired dish!

4. Herbed Farro Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of farro
  • 2 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • ¼ cup of red onion, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the farro and vegetable broth.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the farro is tender.
  3. Drain any excess liquid and transfer the farro to a large bowl.
  4. Add the cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, feta cheese, dill, parsley, lemon juice, and olive oil.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, and toss gently to combine.
  6. Let the salad sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve chilled or at room temperature.

5. Curry Coconut Quinoa

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of quinoa
  • 1 ½ cups of coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon of curry powder
  • 1 cup of mixed vegetables (such as peas, carrots, and bell peppers)
  • ¼ cup of cashews, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the quinoa under cold water to remove any bitterness.
  2. In a saucepan, combine the quinoa, coconut milk, and curry powder.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  4. In a separate pan, sauté the mixed vegetables until tender.
  5. Fluff the quinoa with a fork, then stir in the sautéed vegetables and chopped cashews.
  6. Season with salt and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve warm and enjoy the aromatic flavors of this dish!

There you have it – five simple and affordable grain side dish recipes that are sure to elevate any meal. Whether you’re looking for a light and refreshing quinoa salad or a comforting bowl of garlic butter rice, these recipes are easy to make and will satisfy your cravings without breaking the bank. Give them a try and let your taste buds thank you!
