Creative Ways to Use Cheap Cuts of Meat in Recipes


Cooking with cheap cuts of meat is a fantastic way to save money while still preparing delicious, flavorful meals. Many of these cuts are often overlooked in favor of pricier options, but they can be just as tender and satisfying with the right preparation. In this blog post, we will explore creative ways to use inexpensive cuts of meat in your recipes, offering ideas that range from simple comfort foods to more adventurous dishes.

Why Use Cheap Cuts of Meat?

Cheap cuts of meat are often more affordable, but that doesn’t mean they lack in taste or potential. Cuts like beef chuck, pork shoulder, and chicken thighs can deliver fantastic flavor and texture when prepared properly. Plus, using these cuts is a sustainable choice. They come from parts of the animal that require longer cooking times to become tender, making them ideal for slow cooking, braising, and simmering.

The key to transforming these cheaper cuts into mouth-watering dishes is to use the right techniques. With patience and creativity, you’ll discover that inexpensive meat can be just as enjoyable as its more expensive counterparts. Let’s explore some inventive ways to incorporate these cuts into your cooking.

1. Slow-Cooked Beef Chuck for Shredded Tacos or Sandwiches

Beef chuck is a hearty, inexpensive cut of beef that becomes tender and flavorful when slow-cooked. One of the best ways to use beef chuck is in slow-cooked recipes where the meat can simmer for hours, allowing it to break down and absorb all the seasonings.

For a tasty taco filling, place a piece of beef chuck into your slow cooker with spices like cumin, chili powder, garlic, onion, and a bit of lime juice. Let it cook on low for 8 hours until the meat is fork-tender and easy to shred. This beef can also be used as a filling for sandwiches or even atop a hearty salad. The result is a satisfying meal that feels indulgent but is budget-friendly.

2. Pork Shoulder for Pulled Pork

Pork shoulder, also known as pork butt, is another cheap cut that becomes wonderfully tender when slow-cooked. This cut is perfect for making pulled pork, whether you’re craving sandwiches, tacos, or even a topping for pizza.

To make pulled pork, rub the pork shoulder with a mixture of spices, including brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Slow cook the pork until it’s fall-apart tender, then shred it with two forks. Combine with your favorite barbecue sauce or use it in a variety of different recipes like tacos, sliders, or even on top of mac and cheese.

The beauty of pork shoulder is its versatility – you can make it your own by experimenting with different seasoning blends and sauces. Whether you like it smoky, spicy, or tangy, there’s a way to make this cheap cut of meat work for any palate.

3. Braised Chicken Thighs with Herbs and Vegetables

Chicken thighs are an affordable alternative to chicken breasts and are packed with flavor and moisture. They’re perfect for braising, where the chicken slowly cooks in a flavorful liquid, absorbing the taste of the seasonings and remaining juicy.

To make a simple braised chicken thigh dish, sear the thighs in a hot pan to create a crispy skin. Then, add aromatics such as garlic, onion, and fresh herbs like rosemary and thyme. Pour in chicken broth, white wine, or even a splash of cider, then cover and let the chicken cook for an hour or more. The result is incredibly tender, flavorful chicken thighs with a savory sauce.

Serve the braised chicken thighs with mashed potatoes, rice, or crusty bread to soak up the rich sauce. Braising chicken thighs with vegetables like carrots, celery, and potatoes creates a well-rounded, one-pot meal that is both affordable and satisfying.

4. Beef Brisket for Smoked or Oven-Baked Recipes

Beef brisket is often associated with barbecue, but it can also be prepared at home without the need for a smoker. This cut of meat requires slow cooking, but the results are well worth the wait.

To prepare brisket, first rub the meat with a blend of spices like paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. You can either smoke the brisket over a low fire for several hours or bake it slowly in the oven. If you don’t have access to a smoker, simply wrap the brisket in foil and slow roast it at a low temperature for 4-5 hours, adding a few sprigs of thyme or rosemary for added flavor.

Once it’s tender and juicy, slice the brisket thinly and serve it with your favorite sides, such as coleslaw, cornbread, or roasted vegetables. This method allows you to enjoy the rich, smoky flavors of brisket without needing a fancy smoker.

5. Oxtail Stew for a Rich, Hearty Meal

Oxtail is a flavorful but often overlooked cut that, when cooked low and slow, becomes a rich, succulent stew. Oxtail has a lot of connective tissue, which, when braised for hours, breaks down into a silky broth that infuses the meat with intense flavor.

To make oxtail stew, brown the oxtails in a large pot, then remove them and sauté onions, garlic, carrots, and celery in the same pot. Add the oxtails back in, along with beef broth, tomato paste, and a bouquet of herbs (like bay leaves, thyme, and parsley). Cover and let the stew simmer for 3-4 hours, until the meat is tender and the broth has thickened.

This dish is perfect for a cozy dinner and can be served with rice, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread to absorb the flavorful broth. The richness of oxtail makes it a perfect comfort food for cold evenings, and its deep flavor will make you forget that it’s a cheap cut of meat.

6. Stews and Soups with Lamb Shank

Lamb shank is a lesser-known cut that can be a real game-changer in hearty stews and soups. Lamb shank contains a good amount of collagen, which breaks down into a rich, velvety broth during long cooking.

For a simple lamb shank stew, sear the lamb shanks in a large pot until browned on all sides. Then, add aromatics like onions, garlic, carrots, and celery, and deglaze the pot with wine or broth. Add your seasonings – cumin, rosemary, thyme, and a dash of cinnamon work beautifully with lamb – and let the stew simmer for hours until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.

Serve the stew with couscous, mashed potatoes, or flatbread to complement the rich flavors of the lamb. This dish is a great way to make use of a cheaper cut of lamb while still creating a luxurious, indulgent meal.

7. Ground Meats for Meatballs, Burgers, or Meatloaf

When it comes to ground meats, you don’t have to use premium cuts to create tasty meals. Ground beef, pork, or even lamb can be used in a variety of recipes that range from meatballs to meatloaf. The key is to use a mix of lean and fatty cuts, which provide flavor and juiciness without breaking the bank.

Ground meat can be the foundation for a wide range of dishes. Combine it with breadcrumbs, eggs, and your favorite seasonings to form meatballs for spaghetti, or create burger patties and serve them with your favorite toppings. For a more filling option, try making meatloaf, where the ground meat is mixed with vegetables, herbs, and a simple glaze.

Ground meats are also excellent in casseroles, stuffed peppers, and chili, making them one of the most versatile and budget-friendly ingredients in your kitchen.

8. Beef Short Ribs for Rich, Flavorful Meals

Beef short ribs are another cut that benefits from slow cooking. When braised or slow-roasted, these ribs become incredibly tender, with the meat practically falling off the bone. Short ribs have a lot of connective tissue, which, when cooked low and slow, creates a rich, gelatinous broth.

To prepare beef short ribs, sear the ribs in a hot pan, then add onions, garlic, and herbs like thyme and rosemary. Pour in beef broth or red wine, cover, and let the ribs cook for 3-4 hours until they are melt-in-your-mouth tender. You can also add vegetables like carrots, potatoes, or mushrooms to make it a complete meal.

Short ribs are perfect for a special occasion dinner, and they’re relatively affordable compared to other premium beef cuts. The rich, savory flavors will impress your guests while keeping your budget in check.

Conclusion: Affordable, Flavorful Meals

Using cheap cuts of meat doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice flavor. By applying the right cooking techniques and getting creative with your recipes, you can transform these cuts into hearty, satisfying dishes that rival anything you would make with more expensive options. Whether you’re slow cooking pork shoulder for pulled pork, braising chicken thighs with vegetables, or simmering oxtail stew, these inexpensive cuts are the key to affordable and delicious meals.

Next time you’re at the butcher’s or grocery store, don’t shy away from the cheaper cuts. With a little patience and creativity, you can make incredible meals that are both budget-friendly and full of flavor.
