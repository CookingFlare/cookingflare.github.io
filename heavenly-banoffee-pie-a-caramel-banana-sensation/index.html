Heavenly Banoffee Pie: A Caramel Banana Sensation


If you’ve ever craved a dessert that can make your taste buds dance with joy, look no further than the heavenly banoffee pie. This delectable treat combines the rich flavors of caramel, ripe bananas, and whipped cream atop a buttery biscuit base, creating a dessert that is nothing short of divine. Banoffee pie is not just a dessert; it’s an experience, a celebration of flavors that come together in perfect harmony. In this blog post, we will delve into the history of banoffee pie, its components, and how to make this beloved dessert from scratch. So, grab your apron and let’s embark on a culinary adventure!

The Origin of Banoffee Pie

Banoffee pie is a relatively modern dessert, believed to have been created in the 1970s in England. The name “banoffee” is a delightful combination of the words “banana” and “toffee,” which are the two star ingredients in this delightful pie. Legend has it that the pie first appeared on the menu at the Hungry Monk restaurant in East Sussex, where it quickly gained popularity for its irresistible flavor profile.

The original recipe called for a graham cracker crust, sweetened condensed milk to create the luscious caramel, fresh bananas, and a generous dollop of whipped cream on top. Over the years, variations have emerged, incorporating different types of biscuits, chocolate, and even coffee. However, the classic banoffee pie remains a favorite among dessert lovers all over the world.

Ingredients You Will Need

To create a banoffee pie that will impress your friends and family, gather the following ingredients:

For the Base:

  • 200g digestive biscuits (or graham crackers)
  • 100g unsalted butter, melted

For the Caramel:

  • 1 can (397g) sweetened condensed milk
  • 100g unsalted butter
  • 100g brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon golden syrup (or corn syrup)

For the Filling:

  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 300ml heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons icing sugar (powdered sugar)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Grated chocolate or cocoa powder (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Biscuit Base

  1. Crush the Biscuits: Begin by placing the digestive biscuits in a food processor and blitz until they resemble fine crumbs. If you don’t have a food processor, you can place the biscuits in a ziplock bag and crush them with a rolling pin.

  2. Mix with Butter: In a mixing bowl, combine the crushed biscuits with the melted butter. Stir until the mixture is well combined and resembles wet sand.

  3. Form the Base: Press the biscuit mixture firmly into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch (23cm) loose-bottom tart tin. Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to ensure an even layer.

  4. Chill: Place the tart tin in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the base to set.

Step 2: Make the Caramel

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the sweetened condensed milk, unsalted butter, brown sugar, and golden syrup.

  2. Cook the Mixture: Place the saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, and continue to cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until it thickens and turns a golden caramel color.

  3. Cool the Caramel: Once the caramel is ready, remove it from the heat and let it cool slightly before pouring it into the chilled biscuit base. Spread it out evenly with a spatula.

Step 3: Assemble the Banoffee Pie

  1. Slice the Bananas: Peel the ripe bananas and slice them into thin rounds. Evenly distribute the banana slices over the caramel layer in the pie.

  2. Whip the Cream: In a mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream with the icing sugar and vanilla extract until soft peaks form. Be careful not to over-whip, as you want the cream to be light and fluffy.

  3. Top the Pie: Spoon the whipped cream over the banana layer, spreading it out gently. You can use a spatula or the back of a spoon for a smooth finish.

  4. Garnish: For an extra touch of decadence, sprinkle grated chocolate or cocoa powder on top of the whipped cream.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

  1. Chill the Pie: Place the assembled banoffee pie back in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld and the pie to set.

  2. Slice and Enjoy: When you’re ready to serve, carefully remove the pie from the tart tin. Cut it into wedges and serve immediately. Enjoy the delightful combination of caramel, banana, and whipped cream in every bite!

Tips for the Perfect Banoffee Pie

  • Choose Ripe Bananas: The bananas should be perfectly ripe, not overripe or under-ripe. They should have a few brown spots on the skin, indicating sweetness and flavor.

  • Make Your Own Caramel: If you want to take it a step further, consider making your own caramel from scratch using granulated sugar. This can add a depth of flavor, but requires careful attention to avoid burning.

  • Chill for Best Texture: Allow the pie to chill for a sufficient amount of time, as this helps the flavors meld together and makes for a more enjoyable texture.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to experiment by adding a hint of coffee or chocolate to the whipped cream for a twist on the classic recipe.

Why You’ll Love Banoffee Pie

Banoffee pie is a dessert that brings joy to any occasion. Whether it’s a birthday celebration, a family gathering, or a simple weeknight treat, this pie is sure to impress. The combination of the crunchy biscuit base, luscious caramel, sweet bananas, and fluffy cream creates a symphony of flavors and textures that is absolutely irresistible.

Moreover, banoffee pie is incredibly versatile. You can easily adjust the sweetness, incorporate chocolate, or even add a hint of sea salt to enhance the caramel flavor. This dessert encourages creativity and experimentation in the kitchen, making it a favorite among both novice and experienced bakers.

Storing Your Banoffee Pie

If you have leftovers (which is rare!), you can store them in the refrigerator for up to 2-3 days. However, keep in mind that the bananas may brown over time, and the whipped cream may lose its fluffiness. To maintain the best texture and flavor, it’s recommended to enjoy the pie fresh.

Final Thoughts

Heavenly banoffee pie is a dessert that everyone should try at least once in their lifetime. Its unique combination of flavors and textures creates a delightful experience that is hard to forget. Whether you stick to the classic recipe or put your own spin on it, this dessert is bound to become a beloved favorite in your household.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for something sweet, remember this caramel banana sensation and treat yourself to a slice of bliss. Happy baking!
