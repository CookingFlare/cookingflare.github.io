Delicious Greek Honey Rice Pudding in Just 20 Minutes


Greek cuisine is renowned for transforming simple ingredients into dishes packed with flavor and comforting nostalgia. Among its many delightful desserts, rice pudding, or rizogalo as it’s known in Greek, holds a special place. This creamy, fragrant, and subtly sweet dessert is a favorite across generations, offering a slice of Mediterranean happiness with each spoonful. Today, we’re bringing you an easy and quick recipe to enjoy this classic dish: Greek honey rice pudding, ready in just 20 minutes.

This no-fuss recipe combines creamy rice, warm spices, and the natural sweetness of honey, creating a rich, satisfying dessert for every occasion. Whether you’re preparing it for a cozy family night or as an elegant ending to a dinner party, this Greek honey rice pudding will impress with its comforting texture and heavenly flavor.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this delightful dessert, you’ll need just a handful of pantry staples and a bit of preparation. Here’s everything required to make this Greek rice pudding:

For the Pudding

  • ½ cup short-grain rice: Preferably arborio or another starchy variety for a creamy texture.
  • 2 cups whole milk: Whole milk works best for richness, but you can opt for a plant-based alternative like almond or oat milk.
  • 1 cup water: To cook the rice to fluffy perfection.
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract: For a fragrant, aromatic touch.
  • 1 cinnamon stick: Enhances the warmth of the dish.
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg: Optional, but it adds depth.
  • 3-4 tablespoons Greek honey: Adjust based on how sweet you’d like your pudding.
  • Pinch of salt: To balance the flavors.

For Garnishing

  • A drizzle of Greek honey
  • Ground cinnamon
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds (optional)
  • Fresh mint leaves (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Rinse Your Rice

Start by thoroughly rinsing the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This step helps remove excess starch and ensures the rice cooks evenly without clumping. Set the rinsed rice aside.

Step 2: Cook the Rice

In a medium-sized saucepan, combine the rinsed rice and one cup of water. Add a pinch of salt and bring to a simmer over medium heat. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the water is mostly absorbed.

Step 3: Add Milk and Spices

Reduce the heat to low and pour in the milk, stirring slowly. Add the cinnamon stick and a sprinkle of ground nutmeg. If nutmeg isn’t your favorite spice, feel free to omit it and let the cinnamon shine on its own. Stir the mixture gently, ensuring the rice doesn’t stick to the bottom of the pan.

Step 4: Sweeten with Honey

As the rice absorbs the milk and begins to soften, swirl in the Greek honey. The natural sweetness of the honey pairs beautifully with the earthy spices, creating an irresistible flavor. Adjust the amount of honey based on your preferences—taste as you go and add more if needed.

Step 5: Cook Until Creamy

Continue cooking the rice over low heat, stirring frequently, until it reaches a thick and creamy consistency, about 12-15 minutes. If the mixture becomes too thick before the rice is fully cooked, simply add a splash more milk to loosen it.

Step 6: Add Vanilla and Remove Spices

Once the rice is tender and the pudding is rich and creamy, stir in the vanilla extract. Remove the cinnamon stick from the mixture. Give everything a final stir to ensure the flavors are well combined.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Spoon the warm rice pudding into serving bowls. For that iconic Greek touch, drizzle a little extra honey over the top and dust with ground cinnamon. If desired, sprinkle with chopped pistachios or almonds for added crunch and garnish with a fresh mint leaf for a pop of color.

Tips for the Perfect Greek Rice Pudding

Choosing the Right Rice

The key to achieving the signature creamy texture of rice pudding lies in the choice of rice. Short-grain or medium-grain rice varieties, such as arborio or carnaroli, work wonders due to their high starch content. Avoid using long-grain rice, as it tends to be less starchy, resulting in a less creamy pudding.

Adjust Sweetness to Taste

Greek honey is wonderfully aromatic and sweet, but sweetness levels can vary depending on the brand or type of honey you use. Always taste-test your pudding as it cooks and adjust the honey to get the perfect balance of flavor.

Experiment with Aromatics

While cinnamon and nutmeg are traditional in many rice pudding recipes, feel free to experiment with other spices or flavorings that reflect your personal taste. A touch of orange or lemon zest can add a refreshing citrus note to your pudding, while a splash of rosewater or orange blossom water could impart a floral dimension.

Serve Warm or Cold

This rice pudding is equally delicious served warm as a cozy treat on a cool evening or chilled for a refreshing dessert on a hot summer’s day. If you’re planning to serve it cold, let it cool to room temperature before chilling in the refrigerator for at least an hour.

A Quick History of Greek Rizogalo

The roots of rizogalo can be traced back to the simple culinary traditions of Greece, where rice and milk were staple ingredients. Historically, it was prepared as a comforting dish for children and the elderly, as its soft, creamy texture made it easy to enjoy. Today, it has transcended generations and cultural boundaries, finding a place on dessert menus worldwide. The addition of honey not only adds sweetness but also ties the dish to its Mediterranean heritage, where honey has been cherished since ancient times.

This recipe is quick—ready in just 20 minutes—yet it manages to deliver a dessert that feels indulgent and luxurious. This quick and easy recipe ensures that you can enjoy this beloved dessert with minimal effort while achieving maximum satisfaction. Perfectly creamy, delicately spiced, and naturally sweetened with honey, it’s a dish that celebrates the beauty of humble ingredients.
