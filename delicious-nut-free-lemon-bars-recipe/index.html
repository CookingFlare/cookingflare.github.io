Delicious Nut-Free Lemon Bars Recipe


If you’re a fan of tangy desserts, you’re in for a treat with this delicious nut-free lemon bars recipe. These zesty treats are perfect for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a summer picnic or simply craving a refreshing citrusy dessert. The best part? They’re nut-free, making them suitable for those with allergies or dietary restrictions. So let’s dive right in and whip up a batch of these delectable lemon bars!

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ⅓ cup powdered sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

For the filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • ½ cup fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper. This will make it easier to remove the bars once they’re done.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, softened butter, powdered sugar, and salt for the crust. Mix everything together until it resembles coarse crumbs. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan.

  3. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for about 15-18 minutes, or until it turns a light golden brown. Remove it from the oven and set it aside to cool slightly.

  4. While the crust is cooling, prepare the filling. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, lemon juice, flour, and lemon zest until well combined. Make sure there are no lumps of flour remaining.

  5. Pour the filling over the slightly cooled crust, spreading it evenly. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.

  6. Once the lemon bars are baked, remove them from the oven and let them cool completely in the pan. This will allow the filling to firm up before cutting.

  7. Once cooled, carefully lift the bars out of the pan using the parchment paper. Transfer them to a cutting board and use a sharp knife to cut them into squares or rectangles.

  8. Dust the bars with powdered sugar just before serving to add a touch of sweetness and a beautiful finishing touch.

Serving Suggestions

These nut-free lemon bars are delightful on their own, but you can also serve them with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. They pair perfectly with a cup of hot tea or a refreshing glass of lemonade on a warm summer day.

Storage Instructions

If you have any leftovers (although it’s hard to resist eating them all!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3-4 days. The bars will still taste delicious even after being chilled.

Conclusion

These nut-free lemon bars are a true crowd-pleaser. With their vibrant lemon flavor, buttery crust, and easy preparation, they’re sure to become a favorite in your dessert rotation. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to a batch of these tangy and irresistible lemon bars. Enjoy!

I hope you find this recipe for nut-free lemon bars delightful and easy to follow. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting out, these bars are a wonderful addition to your dessert repertoire. Their zesty flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture are bound to impress your family and friends. So give this recipe a try and let the refreshing taste of lemon brighten up your day!
