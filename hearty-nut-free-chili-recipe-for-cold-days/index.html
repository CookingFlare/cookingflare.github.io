Hearty Nut-Free Chili Recipe for Cold Days


When temperatures drop and a chill settles in the air, there’s nothing quite like a comforting bowl of chili to warm you up from the inside out. This nut-free chili recipe is not only hearty and delicious but also perfect for those with nut allergies or sensitivities. Packed with wholesome ingredients and robust flavors, it’s sure to become a favorite on cold days and cozy evenings alike.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this hearty nut-free chili:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, finely chopped (optional for heat)
  • 1 pound ground beef (or ground turkey for a lighter option)
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (15 ounces) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: Heat the olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the diced onion, garlic, bell peppers, carrots, celery, and jalapeno (if using). Sauté for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables have softened.

  2. Brown the Meat: Push the sautéed vegetables to the side of the pot and add the ground beef (or turkey) to the center. Cook, breaking up the meat with a spatula, until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, kidney beans, black beans, and broth. Add the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring the chili to a simmer.

  4. Simmer and Cook: Reduce the heat to low and let the chili simmer, uncovered, for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally. This allows the flavors to meld together and the chili to thicken to your desired consistency.

  5. Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or spices if needed.

  6. Serve: Ladle the chili into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips for the Perfect Nut-Free Chili

  • Variations: Feel free to customize this chili recipe to suit your taste. You can add more vegetables like corn or zucchini, or adjust the level of spiciness by adding more or less chili powder and jalapeno.

  • Make Ahead and Storage: Chili often tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to develop. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days or freeze for longer storage.

  • Serve with: Cornbread, tortilla chips, or a dollop of sour cream and shredded cheese are classic accompaniments to chili and add extra texture and flavor.

  • Nutritional Benefits: This chili is rich in protein from the ground beef and beans, and packed with vitamins and fiber from the vegetables. It’s a wholesome meal that satisfies both hunger and nutritional needs.

Why Nut-Free Matters

For individuals with nut allergies, finding nut-free recipes that are both safe and delicious can be a challenge. This nut-free chili ensures that everyone can enjoy a hearty and comforting meal without any worry about allergens.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re hosting a cozy dinner at home or preparing a meal for a crowd, this nut-free chili recipe is a versatile and satisfying option. It’s easy to make, packed with wholesome ingredients, and guaranteed to warm you up on even the coldest of days. So, gather your ingredients, fire up the stove, and enjoy the comforting aromas that will fill your kitchen as you cook up a pot of delicious homemade chili. Cheers to good food and warm memories!
