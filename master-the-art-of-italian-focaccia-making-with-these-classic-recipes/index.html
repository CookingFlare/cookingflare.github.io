Master the Art of Italian Focaccia Making with These Classic Recipes


Focaccia, a classic Italian bread, is loved for its pillowy texture, savory flavor, and endless possibilities for toppings. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, mastering the art of Italian focaccia making is a skill that will impress your family and friends. In this blog post, we’ll explore three classic focaccia recipes that will transport you to the streets of Italy. So roll up your sleeves, dust off your apron, and let’s get started!

Classic Olive Focaccia

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 7g active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 350ml lukewarm water
  • 100g pitted olives (black or green)
  • Extra virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt flakes
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in lukewarm water. Let it sit for 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Stir until the dough starts to come together.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

  5. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Grease a baking sheet with olive oil.

  6. Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to the prepared baking sheet. Gently press the dough with your fingertips to create dimples.

  7. Scatter the olives evenly over the dough, pressing them gently into the surface. Drizzle the top with olive oil and sprinkle sea salt flakes and fresh rosemary sprigs.

  8. Bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes or until it turns golden brown.

  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Cheesy Tomato Focaccia

Ingredients:

  • 500g bread flour
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 350ml lukewarm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 200g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 150g mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Sea salt flakes

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Make a well in the center and pour in the lukewarm water and olive oil. Stir until the dough starts to come together.

  2. Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  3. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

  4. Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Grease a baking sheet with olive oil.

  5. Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to the prepared baking sheet. Gently press the dough with your fingertips to create dimples.

  6. Arrange the cherry tomato halves evenly over the dough, pressing them gently into the surface. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese over the top, followed by fresh basil leaves and sea salt flakes.

  7. Bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes or until it turns golden brown and the cheese melts.

  8. Remove from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Rosemary and Garlic Focaccia

Ingredients:

  • 500g strong white bread flour
  • 7g instant yeast
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 350ml lukewarm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • Sea salt flakes

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Make a well in the center and pour in the lukewarm water and olive oil. Stir until the dough starts to come together.

  2. Transfer the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  3. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour or until it doubles in size.

  4. Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). Grease a baking sheet with olive oil.

  5. Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to the prepared baking sheet. Gently press the dough with your fingertips to create dimples.

  6. Sprinkle minced garlic and fresh rosemary leaves evenly over the dough, pressing them gently into the surface. Drizzle the top with olive oil and sprinkle sea salt flakes.

  7. Bake the focaccia for 20-25 minutes or until it turns golden brown.

  8. Remove from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

Congratulations! You’ve now mastered the art of Italian focaccia making with these classic recipes. Whether you prefer the savory flavors of olive focaccia, the cheesy goodness of tomato focaccia, or the aromatic combination of rosemary and garlic, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds. Experiment with different toppings and flavors to create your own signature focaccia masterpiece. So go ahead, share the love of freshly baked bread with your loved ones and enjoy the satisfaction of homemade goodness. Buon appetito!
