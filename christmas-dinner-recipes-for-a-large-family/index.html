Christmas Dinner Recipes for a Large Family


Gathering a large family for Christmas dinner is a joyous occasion, filled with laughter, stories, and the comforting aroma of home-cooked food. Creating a menu that pleases varied palates and can feed numerous hungry mouths is no small feat. This guide is your companion to crafting a festive, delicious, and memorable Christmas dinner – complete with recipes that scale beautifully for large groups without sacrificing flavor or festivity.

Planning the Perfect Christmas Dinner Menu

When cooking for a large family, balance is key. You want dishes that complement each other, offer variety, and can be prepared without overwhelming your kitchen or your schedule. Consider a central protein, hearty side dishes that showcase seasonal ingredients, and a show-stopping dessert that caps the meal with sweetness.

Start with:

  • A succulent main course that caters to traditional tastes but allows for a few twists.
  • Side dishes rich in festive flavors and textures that provide warmth and comfort.
  • Bread or rolls that add a soft, buttery touch to every plate.
  • A dessert that brings nostalgia and indulgence.

Set aside ample time for preparation and enlist help where possible – cooking for a crowd is teamwork!

Main Course: Herb-Crusted Prime Rib Roast

The centerpiece of many Christmas dinners, prime rib offers rich flavor and a tender bite that soaks in the spirit of celebration.

Ingredients

  • 1 prime rib roast (bone-in, about 8-10 pounds)
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup beef broth

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Roast: Remove the roast from the fridge an hour before cooking to reach room temperature.
  2. Seasoning: In a small bowl, mix garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper with olive oil to form a paste. Rub this generously over the roast.
  3. Roasting: Preheat your oven to 450°F (232°C). Place the roast on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast for 20 minutes to create a crust.
  4. Reduce Heat: Lower the oven temperature to 325°F (163°C) and continue roasting for about 15 minutes per pound for medium rare. Use a meat thermometer; aim for an internal temperature of 130°F (54°C).
  5. Rest: Let the roast rest for 20 minutes before carving. This allows juices to redistribute, ensuring tenderness.
  6. Sauce: Pour the drippings into a saucepan, add beef broth, and reduce slightly over medium heat for a flavorful jus.

Tips for Scaling

For larger groups, consider two smaller prime rib roasts rather than one massive cut; cooking times remain manageable, and it allows variety if desired (such as one plain and one herb-crusted).

Sides to Complement the Feast

Garlic Mashed Potatoes with Chives

creamy, garlicky mashed potatoes are the ultimate comfort food, perfect alongside the rich prime rib.

Ingredients

  • 8 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 8 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup fresh chives, chopped

Instructions

  1. Boil potatoes with garlic cloves until tender, about 20 minutes.
  2. Drain and mash with butter and warm heavy cream.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, stir in fresh chives before serving.

Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Cranberries

A holiday favorite that adds crispness and a pop of color.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1 pound bacon, chopped
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (204°C).
  2. Toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 20 minutes until crisp.
  4. Fry bacon separately until crispy, then toss with roasted Brussels sprouts and cranberries.

Honey-Glazed Carrots with Thyme

Sweet and aromatic, these carrots bring balance to your plate.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds carrots, cut into sticks
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil carrots until just tender, about 8 minutes.
  2. Melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add carrots, honey, and thyme; cook until glazed and slightly caramelized, about 5 more minutes.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.

Classic Dinner Rolls

Soft rolls are a must-have for sopping up gravy and making family meals feel homey.

Ingredients

  • 8 cups all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 packets active dry yeast (about 4 ½ tsp)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm water with a pinch of sugar, let stand 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt.
  3. Add milk, yeast mixture, melted butter, and eggs to dry ingredients.
  4. Knead dough for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Allow to rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down dough, form rolls, and place on greased baking sheets.
  7. Let rise 30 minutes, then bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15 minutes or until golden brown.

Dessert: Classic Buche de Noel (Yule Log Cake)

This traditional French Christmas dessert is a showstopper both in terms of flavor and visual appeal, perfect for ending your feast on a sweet note.

Ingredients (serves around 15)

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • ½ cup powdered sugar
  • 7 oz bittersweet chocolate, melted and cooled

Instructions

  1. Preheat: Oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 15x10 inch jelly roll pan with parchment paper.
  2. Make Sponge: Beat eggs and sugar until thick and pale. Sift flour and cocoa powder together; fold gently into eggs along with vanilla extract.
  3. Bake: Spread batter evenly in pan. Bake 12-15 minutes.
  4. Roll Cake: While warm, turn cake onto a clean kitchen towel dusted with cocoa powder. Peel off parchment and roll cake with the towel, set aside to cool.
  5. Whip Cream: Beat heavy cream with powdered sugar until stiff peaks. Fold in melted chocolate.
  6. Assemble: Unroll cake, spread chocolate cream filling, and reroll tightly. Chill before serving.
  7. Decorate: Dust with powdered sugar and garnish with fresh berries or sprigs of holly.

Tips for a Seamless Christmas Dinner Experience

  • Prepare in advance: Many side dishes and desserts can be prepped or fully made the day before.
  • Use a cooking schedule: Map out timing to have everything hot and fresh without stress.
  • Delegate: Don’t hesitate to have family help with simpler tasks like stirring, setting the table, or carving.
  • Serve buffet-style: For large families, allowing everyone to serve themselves can keep the flow smooth.

Final Thoughts: Bringing It All Together

A Christmas dinner for a large family is more than just a meal – it’s an experience filled with warmth, love, and shared memories. With the right recipes and a bit of organization, you can host a dinner that reflects tradition while making everyone feel right at home.

This herb-crusted prime rib paired with flavorful sides and a stunning Yule Log dessert is designed to celebrate in style and taste. May your holiday table be bountiful, and your family’s laughter echo long after the plates are cleared.

Celebrate big, cook with joy, and most importantly, savor every moment together!
