The Classic Condiment: Homemade Ketchup Recipe


Ketchup is a staple condiment that adds tangy sweetness to a variety of dishes. While store-bought ketchup is convenient, making your own homemade version allows you to customize the flavors and avoid any unnecessary additives. In this recipe, we will guide you through the steps of creating a classic homemade ketchup that will elevate your meals to a whole new level.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds ripe tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tomatoes. Wash them thoroughly and remove the stems. Cut them into quarters and remove the seeds. Chop the tomatoes into small pieces.

  2. In a large saucepan, combine the chopped tomatoes and finely chopped onion. Cook over medium heat until the mixture begins to simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Once the tomatoes and onions have softened, use a blender or immersion blender to puree the mixture until smooth. Be careful when blending hot liquids, as they can splatter. If using a blender, blend in batches if necessary.

  4. Return the pureed mixture to the saucepan and add the apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, salt, garlic powder, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, and ground allspice. Stir well to combine.

  5. Increase the heat to medium-low and let the mixture simmer for another 45 minutes to an hour, stirring occasionally. The ketchup will gradually thicken and develop a rich, deep flavor.

  6. Once the ketchup has reached your desired consistency, remove it from the heat and let it cool completely. As it cools, it will thicken slightly more.

  7. Transfer the homemade ketchup to sterilized jars or bottles, making sure to leave some headspace at the top. Seal the containers tightly and refrigerate.

  8. Allow the ketchup to sit in the refrigerator for at least 24 hours before using. This resting period will allow the flavors to meld together and intensify.

Serving Suggestions

Homemade ketchup is incredibly versatile and can be used in a variety of ways. Here are some ideas to get you started:

  • Serve it alongside classic comfort foods like french fries, burgers, and hot dogs.
  • Use it as a base for barbecue sauces or marinades.
  • Mix it with mayonnaise to create a tangy dip for vegetables or chicken nuggets.
  • Add a dollop to your favorite meatloaf or meatball recipes for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Use it as a condiment for breakfast sandwiches or omelets.

Final Thoughts

Making your own homemade ketchup not only allows you to control the ingredients but also adds a personal touch to your meals. This classic condiment will become a staple in your kitchen, and you’ll never want to go back to store-bought versions again. So grab some ripe tomatoes and give this homemade ketchup recipe a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
