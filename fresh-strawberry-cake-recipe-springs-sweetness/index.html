Fresh Strawberry Cake Recipe: Spring's Sweetness


Ah, spring - that magical time of year when everything seems to bloom in vibrant shades of pink and red. A time of new beginnings, rejuvenation, and even a bit of sweetness. As nature bursts with her fresh fruit bounty, let’s bring the essence of spring into our kitchens, with a delightful Fresh Strawberry Cake Recipe!

This delightfully moist cake loaded with strawberries is not just a feast for the eyes, but for the taste buds too. It is an absolute pleasure to bake and to eat, making it a splendid choice for your springtime gatherings, or simply for treating yourself to some home-baked goodness.

Ingredients:

  1. For the Cake:

2 cups all-purpose flour 2 teaspoons baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1 cup unsalted butter, softened 2 cups granulated sugar 1/2 cup vegetable oil 4 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 3/4 cup buttermilk 1 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, roughly chopped

  1. For the Strawberry Glaze:

2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped 1/4 cup granulated sugar 2 tablespoons strawberry jam 1 1/2 cups confectioners’ sugar 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat and Prepare: Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch round cake pans.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.

  3. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar together using an electric mixer until it’s light and fluffy. This should take about 3-4 minutes. Incorporate the vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla extract, mixing well after each addition.

  4. Alternate Adding Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually blend the dry ingredients into the butter mixture, alternating with the buttermilk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients.

  5. Add Fresh Strawberries: Gently fold in the roughly chopped strawberries.

  6. Bake: Evenly distribute the batter between the two prepared cake pans. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cakes comes out clean.

  7. Cool and Layer Remove from the oven and allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, before turning them out onto a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Prepare the Strawberry Glaze: While the cake is cooling, prepare the glaze. Blend the strawberries, sugar, and jam in a food processor until smooth. Strain the puree to remove seeds. Stir in the confectioners’ sugar and lemon juice to achieve a thick but pourable consistency.

  9. Assemble: Once the cakes have cooled, place one layer on a cake stand or serving plate. Brush the top of the cake with some of the glaze. Place the second layer on top and brush it too, following up with the sides of the cake. Allow the glaze to soak in.

  10. Final Glaze: Pour the remaining glaze gently over the top of the cake, allowing it to drip down the sides. At this point, you can decide to either serve the cake straight away or refrigerate it for 30 minutes to an hour to allow the glaze to set.

Your Fresh Strawberry Cake is now ready to be shared with your friends and family, offering a delightful flavor of spring. Share the love and relish the sweetness!

Remember, baking is both an art and a science, and you can always experiment with the recipe at your own risk. Let your culinary adventures take flight! Happy Baking!

With its laid-back approach to baking, scrumptious taste, and magnificent color, this dessert is sure to be a hit at any gathering or simply a special treat for a lazy spring afternoon.
