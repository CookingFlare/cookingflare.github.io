The Best Holiday Gluten-Free Baking Recipes


The holidays are just around the corner, and for those with dietary restrictions, finding the perfect holiday treats can be a daunting task. The good news is that with a little creativity and the right ingredients, gluten-free holiday baking has never been easier. In this post, we'll explore the best gluten-free baking recipes to make this season truly unforgettable. Classic Holiday Sugar Cookies Start with a tried-and-true recipe that the entire family will love. These classic holiday sugar cookies are not only delicious but are also forgiving if you have a little too much fun sprinkling colors and decorations on top. To make these mouthwatering treats, you'll need:

  • 1 1/2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour (almond flour or rice flour work perfectly)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Colored sprinkles or decorations of your choice To make the dough, combine the gluten-free flour and granulated sugar in a large bowl. Add the unsalted butter and vanilla extract and mix until a cohesive dough forms. Roll the dough into balls, flatten into disks, and cut with a glass or cookie cutter to create the perfect shape. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes. Allow cookies to cool before decorating. Holiday Gluten-Free Baking Recipes for a Memorable Season The holiday season is back, and with it, the craving for sweet and delicious treats. For those with gluten intolerance or preferences, finding the perfect holiday cookies or pastries can be a daunting task. The good news is that with some creativity and the right ingredients, gluten-free baking has never been easier. In this article, we'll explore the best gluten-free holiday recipes to make this season truly unforgettable. Classic Gluten-Free Sugar Cookies Start with a tried-and-true recipe that the entire family will love. These classic gluten-free sugar cookies are not only delicious but are also forgiving if you have a little too much fun sprinkling colors and decorations on top. To make these mouthwatering treats, you'll need:

1 1/2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour (almond flour or rice flour work perfectly) 1/2 cup unsalted butter 1 cup granulated sugar 1 egg 1 teaspoon vanilla extract Colored sprinkles or decorations of your choice

Combine the gluten-free flour and granulated sugar in a large bowl. Add the unsalted butter and vanilla extract and mix until a cohesive dough forms. Roll the dough into balls, flatten into disks, and cut with a glass or cookie cutter to create the perfect shape. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 8-10 minutes. Allow cookies to cool before decorating. Tea-Riffic Victorian Scones Logically named after the drink for which they're perfect accompaniment, these Victorian-style scones are sure to wow your holiday guests. Light, fluffy, and indulgent, they're the perfect treat to serve at a holiday gathering. To make the Victorian scones, you'll need:

2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour 2 tablespoons sugar 1/4 cup cold unsalted butter 3/4 cup heavy cream 1 egg 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Combine the gluten-free flour and sugar in a large bowl. Add the cold unsalted butter and use a pastry blender or your fingers to work the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs. In a separate bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and egg. Add the vanilla extract and mix well. Add the cream mixture to the flour mixture and mix until a dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead a few times until it comes together. Pat the dough into a circle that's about 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick. Use a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out scones. Gather the scraps, re-form the dough, and cut out additional scones. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the scones are golden brown. Gingerbread Men Make your holiday season merry and bright with these classic gingerbread men. They're perfect for decorating with icing, candy, and sprinkles, making them a fun activity for kids and adults alike. To make the gingerbread men, you'll need:

2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour 2 teaspoons baking soda 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 cup unsalted butter 3/4 cup white granulated sugar 1/4 cup light brown sugar 2 large eggs Decorations of your choice

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking soda, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and salt. In a separate bowl, cream together the unsalted butter and sugars until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs until well combined. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until a dough forms. Roll out the dough on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch (6 mm) thickness. Use a gingerbread man cookie cutter or a knife to cut out the men. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are set. Peppermint Bark Make your holiday treats even more decadent with this delicious peppermint bark. Perfect for gift-giving or snacking, it's easy to make and always a crowd-pleaser. To make the peppermint bark, you'll need:

1 cup gluten-free chocolate chips 1/2 cup white chocolate chips 1/4 cup crushed candy canes 1 teaspoon peppermint extract

Melt the gluten-free chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth. Stir in the peppermint extract. Pour the melted chocolate onto a parchment-lined baking sheet. Melt the white chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth. Drizzle the melted white chocolate over the dark chocolate. Sprinkle with crushed candy canes. Allow the chocolate to set before breaking it into pieces. These gluten-free holiday recipes are sure to make this season truly unforgettable.
