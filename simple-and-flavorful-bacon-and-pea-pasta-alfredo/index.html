Simple and Flavorful Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo


When you’re craving a rich, creamy, and utterly satisfying pasta dish, look no further than this Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo. Combining the smoky goodness of crispy bacon with the delicate sweetness of peas, all enveloped in a luxurious alfredo sauce, this dish is a comforting crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re cooking for a busy weeknight dinner or preparing a delightful meal for guests, this recipe is sure to deliver on both flavor and simplicity.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo, you’ll need a few essential ingredients that come together to create the perfect balance of creamy, savory, and sweet flavors.

For the Pasta:

  • 8 oz fettuccine (or any pasta you prefer like penne, rigatoni, or spaghetti)
  • Salt (for seasoning the pasta water)

For the Bacon and Sauce:

  • 4 strips of bacon, chopped into small pieces
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional, if needed for cooking the bacon)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk (or you can use half-and-half for a richer sauce)
  • 1 cup frozen peas, thawed (you can also use fresh peas if preferred)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter (for extra creaminess)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning (optional for an added depth of flavor)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How to Make Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo

Making this dish is easier than you might think. It’s all about creating a creamy sauce and adding in the right combination of ingredients to achieve the ultimate balance of flavors. Let’s break down the process step by step.

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Once boiling, add in your pasta and cook according to the package instructions. You want your pasta to be al dente, which usually takes about 8-10 minutes, depending on the type you’ve chosen.

Once the pasta is done, drain it, reserving about a cup of pasta water to help thin the sauce later if needed. Set the pasta aside while you prepare the rest of the dish.

Step 2: Cook the Bacon

While your pasta is cooking, take a large skillet and heat it over medium heat. Add the chopped bacon and cook, stirring occasionally, until it becomes crispy and golden brown. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

Once the bacon is crispy, use a slotted spoon to remove it from the skillet and set it aside on a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess grease.

If there’s too much bacon grease left in the skillet, drain some of it off, leaving about a tablespoon in the pan. This will help infuse your sauce with that delicious bacon flavor.

Step 3: Make the Alfredo Sauce

In the same skillet with the bacon grease, add the butter and let it melt over medium heat. Once the butter is melted, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, or until the garlic becomes fragrant. Be careful not to burn it!

Now, pour in the heavy cream and whole milk. Stir everything together, making sure the cream and milk combine seamlessly. Bring the mixture to a simmer, but not a boil, and let it cook for about 3-4 minutes to thicken slightly.

Add in the grated Parmesan cheese, stirring constantly to create a smooth, creamy sauce. Continue to simmer the sauce for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the cheese to melt and the sauce to thicken to your desired consistency.

Step 4: Add the Peas and Bacon

Once your Alfredo sauce has thickened to perfection, it’s time to add the peas and cooked bacon to the skillet. Stir in the peas, letting them heat through. You’ll love the way the sweet peas contrast with the rich, savory sauce.

Add the crispy bacon back into the sauce, giving everything a good mix. Taste the sauce and season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning (if using) to your liking. If the sauce is too thick, you can thin it out by adding some reserved pasta water, one tablespoon at a time, until you reach your desired sauce consistency.

Step 5: Combine the Pasta and Sauce

Now, it’s time to bring everything together. Add the cooked pasta directly into the skillet with the Alfredo sauce, tossing gently to coat each strand or piece of pasta with the creamy sauce. Let it sit in the pan for a minute or two so the pasta can absorb some of the sauce and flavors.

Step 6: Garnish and Serve

Once your pasta is coated with the delicious Alfredo sauce, transfer it to a serving dish. Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley on top for a burst of color and freshness. The green parsley not only looks beautiful but also adds a touch of brightness to the rich, creamy dish.

Serve immediately, and watch how quickly this Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo disappears. It’s the perfect combination of comfort food with a bit of elegance, making it ideal for any occasion.

Tips for the Perfect Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo

  • Pasta Variety: While fettuccine is traditional for Alfredo dishes, feel free to use any pasta you prefer. Penne, spaghetti, or rigatoni work wonderfully too. Just make sure to cook the pasta al dente to ensure it holds up well in the creamy sauce.

  • Bacon Choices: For extra smokiness, consider using smoked bacon or pancetta in place of regular bacon. It’ll infuse the sauce with an even richer flavor.

  • Creaminess: If you want your Alfredo sauce extra creamy, consider using a combination of heavy cream and half-and-half instead of all milk. This will make the sauce thicker and even more indulgent.

  • Peas Substitutes: While peas are a classic addition to this dish, you could also swap them out for other vegetables like spinach, broccoli, or even mushrooms for a different twist.

  • Cheese: Use freshly grated Parmesan cheese instead of pre-shredded for the best melt and flavor. Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents, which can affect the smoothness of the sauce.

  • Make It Ahead: This dish is best enjoyed fresh, but if you want to make it ahead of time, store the pasta and sauce separately. Reheat the sauce gently on the stovetop, adding a bit of pasta water to loosen it up before tossing it with the pasta.

Why This Recipe Works

There are a few key reasons why this Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo is such a standout recipe. First, the combination of crispy bacon and sweet peas adds layers of flavor that make each bite a delight. The smokiness from the bacon pairs perfectly with the richness of the creamy Alfredo sauce, while the peas provide a fresh and sweet contrast.

Second, the simplicity of the ingredients makes this recipe accessible, even for beginner cooks. You don’t need any fancy techniques or hard-to-find ingredients to create a dish that feels luxurious. With just a few pantry staples and some fresh elements, you can whip up a restaurant-quality meal in no time.

Lastly, this Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo is versatile. It can be easily adapted to suit your taste preferences or dietary needs. For example, you could swap in gluten-free pasta, or add extra veggies for a healthier take on the dish. The recipe is also flexible when it comes to the sauce—you can adjust the richness of the cream and cheese to suit your preferences.

Conclusion

This Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo is the perfect dish to serve when you’re craving something creamy, flavorful, and satisfying. The combination of crispy bacon, sweet peas, and a luscious Alfredo sauce will surely become a favorite in your recipe rotation. Whether you’re feeding your family or hosting a dinner party, this dish is guaranteed to impress. It’s simple, yet indulgent, and sure to please everyone at the table. So, next time you’re in the mood for a comforting pasta dish, give this Bacon and Pea Pasta Alfredo a try—you won’t be disappointed!
