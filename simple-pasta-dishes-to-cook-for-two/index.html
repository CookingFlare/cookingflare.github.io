Simple Pasta Dishes to Cook for Two


Cooking for two can often be a challenge—too much food goes to waste, or you end up ordering takeout out of convenience. But pasta offers an excellent solution! It’s versatile, quick to prepare, and easy to portion out for a cozy meal for two. Plus, with a few simple ingredients and techniques, you can elevate humble pasta dishes into delightful culinary experiences.

In this blog post, you’ll discover several simple pasta recipes specifically tailored for two people. Each recipe strikes a balance between flavor, ease of preparation, and minimal cleanup. Whether you’re planning a romantic dinner, a casual meal, or a special occasion, these pasta dishes will satisfy your appetite without overwhelming your kitchen or your schedule.

1. Classic Garlic and Olive Oil Spaghetti (Spaghetti Aglio e Olio)

This Italian staple is as simple as it sounds but rich with flavor. It relies on high-quality olive oil, fresh garlic, chili flakes, and parsley to create a comforting yet elegant dish.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz spaghetti (about half a standard package)
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente, approximately 8-9 minutes. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the sliced garlic and cook gently, stirring frequently, until it’s golden and fragrant but not burnt (about 2-3 minutes).
  3. Stir in the red pepper flakes.
  4. Add the drained spaghetti to the pan, tossing to coat in the garlic oil.
  5. Slowly add the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, to create a silky sauce that clings to the noodles.
  6. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  7. Finish with chopped parsley and a sprinkle of Parmesan if desired.
  8. Serve immediately with a simple side salad or crusty bread.

This dish is a perfect example of how just a few pantry staples come together beautifully in a minimalist yet satisfying meal.

2. Creamy Lemon Pesto Penne

Lemon pesto infuses traditional pesto with a citrusy brightness that pairs wonderfully with the creamy sauce. This simple penne recipe is fresh, flavorful, and easy to whip up.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz penne pasta
  • 1/4 cup prepared basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the penne according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a skillet over medium heat, warm olive oil.
  3. Stir in lemon zest, lemon juice, pesto, and heavy cream. Cook gently for 2-3 minutes until the sauce is warmed through and slightly thickened.
  4. Add the cooked pasta and toss to coat evenly.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Top with grated Parmesan and garnish with fresh basil.
  7. Serve with a crisp white wine for a light, summery dinner.

This pasta is bright and luscious, ideal for spring or summer, yet comforting enough for cooler nights.

3. Spaghetti Carbonara for Two

This Roman classic comes together in minutes but delivers deep, savory satisfaction with just eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz spaghetti
  • 3 oz pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, sparingly, for pasta water

Instructions:

  1. Boil salted water and cook spaghetti until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, cook pancetta in a pan over medium heat until crisp.
  3. In a bowl, whisk eggs and cheese together with a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Drain pasta, reserving about 1/2 cup of pasta water.
  5. Quickly toss hot pasta with pancetta, then immediately mix with egg and cheese mixture, stirring vigorously to create a smooth sauce. Add reserved pasta water if necessary to loosen the sauce.
  6. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper.

Carbonara is a super comforting meal with luxurious texture and simple ingredients—perfect for impressing a dinner guest or anyone you want to feel special.

4. Roasted Tomato and Basil Linguine

Roasting tomatoes intensifies their sweetness and flavor, making this simple pasta a standout dish.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz linguine
  • 12 cherry or grape tomatoes, halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • Grated Parmesan or Pecorino (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss tomatoes with 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20 minutes until soft and starting to caramelize.
  3. Cook linguine in salted boiling water until al dente.
  4. Drain pasta, reserving some pasta water.
  5. Mix pasta with roasted tomatoes, remaining olive oil, a splash of pasta water, and red pepper flakes.
  6. Toss gently with torn fresh basil.
  7. Serve with a sprinkle of cheese if preferred.

This dish illustrates how technique—like roasting—makes simple ingredients extraordinary.

5. Simple Tuna and Capers Fusilli

A quick pantry-friendly pasta celebrating Mediterranean flavors with savory tuna and the tangy brininess of capers.

Ingredients:

  • 6 oz fusilli pasta
  • 1 small can (3-4 oz) tuna packed in olive oil or water, drained
  • 1 tbsp capers, drained
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook fusilli until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan. Add garlic and cook gently until fragrant.
  3. Add tuna and capers, breaking up tuna with a fork.
  4. Stir in lemon juice and season to taste.
  5. Toss pasta into the tuna mixture and combine well.
  6. Garnish with parsley and serve immediately.

This straightforward recipe transforms canned tuna into an elegant, satisfying meal with minimal effort.

Tips for Cooking Pasta for Two

Cooking pasta for two can sometimes be tricky when package sizes are usually enough for 4+ servings. Here are some quick tips:

  • Portion control: Use about 2 to 3 ounces of dry pasta per person for a main course.
  • Save pasta water: Always reserve a bit of pasta water before draining. That starchy liquid helps bind sauces to noodles seamlessly.
  • Mix textures: Consider adding a crunchy element (toasted breadcrumbs, nuts, or fresh greens) to create contrast in simple pasta dishes.
  • Fresh herbs: A sprinkle of fresh parsley, basil, or mint can brighten flavors and add color that makes the dish pop visually.
  • Leftovers: If you do end up with more than two servings, pasta dishes often taste great reheated with a little splash of water or extra sauce.

Creative Add-ons and Variations

Feel free to personalize these pasta dishes with your favorite ingredients:

  • Proteins: Grilled chicken, shrimp, or sautéed mushrooms add a heartier element.
  • Cheeses: Try crumble feta, burrata, or pecorino romano for different flavor profiles.
  • Vegetables: Roasted bell peppers, spinach, zucchini ribbons, or sun-dried tomatoes work beautifully.
  • Nuts: Pine nuts, toasted walnuts, or almonds introduce richness and texture.

Experimentation makes pasta an endlessly rewarding canvas for creativity.

Final Thoughts

Pasta for two doesn’t have to be complicated or time-consuming. These simple pasta recipes allow you to enjoy flavorful, fresh meals tailored to smaller portions without fuss or waste. From light lemon pesto penne to indulgent carbonara, each dish is designed to bring comfort, balance, and delight to your dinner table.

So next time you want a quick, satisfying meal for two, reach for pasta and one of these wholesome recipes. You’ll be surprised how easy it is to create wonderful meals that taste like a special occasion—even on a busy weeknight.

Happy cooking and buon appetito!
