Stir Fry Udon Noodles with Vegetables and Tofu


Stir fry udon noodles with vegetables and tofu is an irresistible dish that combines the chewy texture of udon noodles with the vibrant flavors of fresh vegetables and the hearty, protein-packed goodness of tofu. This meal is perfect for anyone seeking a quick, healthy, and filling dinner that bursts with umami and satisfying textures. Whether you are a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe offers a simple yet impressive way to enjoy Asian-inspired cuisine at home.

Why Choose Stir Fry Udon Noodles?

Udon noodles are thick, white wheat noodles originating from Japan, revered for their soft and slightly chewy texture. They provide an excellent base for stir fry dishes because they hold sauces well and complement a variety of ingredients perfectly. Pairing them with tofu, a versatile, plant-based protein rich in essential amino acids, elevates the dish’s nutritional profile, making it an excellent option for vegetarians, vegans, or anyone wanting to reduce meat consumption.

The addition of colorful vegetables not only enhances the visual appeal of the dish but also infuses it with vitamins, minerals, and fiber. This balance of carbohydrates, protein, and micronutrients makes stir fry udon noodles a wholesome choice that satisfies both taste buds and dietary needs.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To whip up this delicious stir fry, gather the following ingredients:

For the Stir Fry:

  • 12 oz (340 g) fresh or frozen udon noodles
  • 14 oz (400 g) firm tofu, pressed and cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any neutral oil)
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned or thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), thinly sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas, trimmed
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, minced

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (use vegetarian oyster sauce to keep it vegan)
  • 1 tablespoon mirin or rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons sugar or maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes or Sriracha (optional, to add heat)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Tofu

  1. Press the tofu. Place the tofu block between two plates lined with paper towels or clean kitchen towels. Set a weight (like a heavy pan or canned goods) on top to press out excess moisture for at least 15-20 minutes. This ensures the tofu crisps nicely when cooked.

  2. Cut into cubes. After pressing, cut the tofu into roughly 1-inch cubes.

  3. Cook the tofu. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes and fry until all sides are golden and crispy, about 8 minutes total. Remove tofu from pan and set aside.

Cooking the Vegetables

  1. Heat remaining oil. In the same pan, add the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil. Sauté garlic and ginger over medium heat until fragrant, about 30 seconds to a minute.

  2. Add vegetables. Toss in carrots, bell pepper, broccoli, and snap peas. Stir fry for about 4-5 minutes or until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Keep them vibrant and fresh by not overcooking.

Bringing it all together

  1. Add noodles. If using fresh or frozen udon noodles, separate them gently and add directly to the pan. If using dried noodles, cook beforehand according to package instructions, then drain and add.

  2. Mix in sauce. Pour in the soy sauce mixture including soy sauce, oyster sauce, mirin, sesame oil, sugar, and optional chili flakes. Toss everything well to coat the noodles, veggies, and tofu in a glossy, flavorful sauce.

  3. Add tofu back. Return tofu to the pan and gently toss to combine everything evenly.

  4. Finish with green onions. Sprinkle chopped green onions on top for freshness and a hint of sharpness.

Tips for the Perfect Stir Fry Udon Noodles

  • Choosing tofu: Firm or extra-firm tofu works best for stir frying because it holds its shape better during cooking.
  • Pressing tofu: Don’t skip pressing your tofu to avoid soggy texture.
  • Noodle selection: Fresh or frozen udon noodles give the best chewy, soft texture; if you use dried, cook al dente to avoid mushiness after stir frying.
  • Sauce balance: Adjust soy sauce and sugar levels to your taste; if you like it sweeter or saltier, feel free to tweak.
  • Vegetable variety: Feel free to swap or add other vegetables such as mushrooms, baby corn, bok choy, or zucchini to keep the dish colorful and nutritious.
  • Protein options: For variety, you can add shrimp, chicken, or beef instead of tofu.
  • Garnishes: Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds or drizzle with extra sesame oil just before serving for a nutty finish.

Serving Suggestions

This stir fry udon noodles recipe serves beautifully as a standalone meal because it includes ample protein, carbs, and vegetables. However, you might want to round out the dining experience with:

  • A simple miso soup or clear broth soup before the main course.
  • A fresh cucumber salad tossed with rice vinegar and sesame seeds for a cooling contrast.
  • Pickled ginger or kimchi on the side to add tang and spice.

Pair the meal with hot jasmine or green tea for an authentic Japanese dining vibe.

Health Benefits of Udon Noodles with Vegetables and Tofu

  • Protein power: Tofu is a fantastic plant-based protein source, rich in calcium, iron, and magnesium, which supports muscle health and energy production.
  • Vegetable boost: Broccoli, snap peas, and bell peppers provide fiber, antioxidants, and vitamins like C and A, which enhance immunity and skin health.
  • Balanced carbs: Udon noodles offer slow-digesting carbohydrates for sustained energy without spiking blood sugar levels drastically.
  • Low in saturated fat: Using vegetable oil and including tofu instead of animal fats helps keep saturated fat intake low, supporting heart health.

Storing and Reheating Tips

  • Storage: Store leftover stir fry udon noodles in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
  • Reheating: Reheat in a skillet over medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even heating. Avoid microwaving if possible, as it may make the noodles gummy.
  • Freezing: Freezing is not recommended, as the texture of tofu and noodles can deteriorate upon thawing.

Final Thoughts

Stir fry udon noodles with vegetables and tofu is a vibrant, satisfying dish that perfectly balances flavor and nutrition. Its quick preparation time makes it ideal for busy weeknights, and its adaptability means you can easily customize it to suit your taste preferences and dietary requirements.

By mastering this recipe, you gain a versatile skill that opens doors to endless variations in the realm of Asian-inspired cooking. Why not try it tonight and enjoy a delightful meal full of chewy noodles, crunchy veggies, and crispy tofu?
