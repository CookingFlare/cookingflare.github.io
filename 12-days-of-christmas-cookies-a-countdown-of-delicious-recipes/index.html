12 Days of Christmas Cookies: A Countdown of Delicious Recipes


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to celebrate than with a delightful array of Christmas cookies? From classic favorites to innovative twists, this countdown of 12 days of Christmas cookies will not only satisfy your sweet tooth but also spread holiday cheer to your friends and family. Get your aprons ready, because we’re diving into a world of scrumptious flavors, festive decorations, and heartwarming traditions.

Day 1: Classic Sugar Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 ½ cups white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons milk
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Sprinkles or colored sugar for decoration

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a small bowl, stir together flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until smooth. Beat in the egg and vanilla.
  4. Gradually blend in the dry ingredients. If the dough is too crumbly, add milk one tablespoon at a time until it holds together.
  5. Roll rounded teaspoonfuls of dough into balls, and place them onto ungreased cookie sheets. Flatten each ball slightly with the bottom of a glass dipped in sugar.
  6. Bake 8 to 10 minutes in the preheated oven. Let cool on wire racks.

These classic sugar cookies are a versatile base for holiday decorating. Use icing and sprinkles to create festive designs that will wow your guests!

Day 2: Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ¾ cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • ½ cup molasses

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, and salt. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and brown sugar until smooth. Beat in the egg and molasses.
  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until well combined.
  4. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about ¼-inch thickness.
  6. Use cookie cutters to create festive shapes and place them on baking sheets. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes. Cool before decorating.

The aroma of gingerbread cookies baking in the oven is a quintessential part of the holiday season. Decorate them with icing and candy for a whimsical touch!

Day 3: Peppermint Bark Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup crushed peppermint candies
  • 1 cup white chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing well.
  4. Stir in the crushed peppermint and white chocolate chips.
  5. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. Allow to cool on wire racks.

These peppermint bark cookies are a delightful fusion of flavors that embody the spirit of Christmas. The festive red and white colors make them perfect for holiday gatherings!

Day 4: Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease cookie sheets or line them with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together cocoa powder, granulated sugar, and vegetable oil. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  3. In another bowl, combine flour, baking powder, and salt. Gradually add to the cocoa mixture, mixing until just combined.
  4. Chill the dough for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  5. When ready to bake, roll the dough into balls and coat each ball in powdered sugar. Place them on the prepared baking sheets.
  6. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. Let cool before serving.

Chocolate crinkle cookies are not only delicious but also visually stunning with their powdered sugar dusting. These cookies are sure to impress anyone who lays eyes on them!

Day 5: Eggnog Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ½ cup eggnog

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix together flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, nutmeg, and cinnamon. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla and eggnog.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until well combined.
  5. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. Cool on wire racks.

These eggnog cookies capture the essence of the holiday season in every bite. Their rich flavor pairs perfectly with a glass of eggnog!

Day 6: Snickerdoodles

Ingredients:

  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup vegetable shortening
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Grease cookie sheets.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together butter, shortening, and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs.
  3. In another bowl, combine flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add to the creamed mixture, mixing until well blended.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together sugar and cinnamon. Roll dough into balls and coat them in the sugar-cinnamon mixture.
  5. Place on the prepared cookie sheets and bake for 8 to 10 minutes. Cool before serving.

Snickerdoodles are a classic favorite that never goes out of style. Their cinnamon-sugar coating adds a warm, cozy touch to the holiday festivities.

Day 7: Cranberry Orange Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon orange zest
  • 1 tablespoon fresh orange juice
  • 1 cup dried cranberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy. Beat in the egg, orange zest, and orange juice.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until well combined. Fold in the dried cranberries.
  5. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheets. Bake for 12 to 15 minutes. Allow to cool before serving.

Cranberry orange cookies offer a refreshing twist that balances sweetness with tanginess, making them a delightful addition to your holiday cookie platter.
