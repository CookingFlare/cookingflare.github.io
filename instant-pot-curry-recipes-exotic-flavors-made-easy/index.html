Instant Pot Curry Recipes: Exotic Flavors Made Easy


Curry is the epitome of comfort food for millions around the world. Fragrant, flavorful, and endlessly versatile, curry has a unique way of bringing people together over a shared love of spice, aroma, and warmth. Whether it’s creamy butter chicken, aromatic Thai green curry, or hearty vegetarian dal, there’s a curry to suit every taste and dietary preference. However, making curry from scratch can sometimes feel like a daunting task, especially if you’re short on time or unfamiliar with complex spices.

Enter the Instant Pot: this workhorse of a kitchen gadget takes the intimidation out of traditional curry-making. With its pressure-cooking powers and ability to blend flavors seamlessly, the Instant Pot turns what could take hours into a process that’s fast, simple, and fail-proof. If you’re ready to embark on a culinary journey full of exotic flavors but without the hassle, these Instant Pot curry recipes will become your go-to solutions for weeknight dinners or weekend indulgence.

Why Make Curry in the Instant Pot?

Before diving into recipes, it’s worth highlighting why the Instant Pot is a game-changer for curry enthusiasts:

  1. Time-Saving Convenience: Traditional curries often require simmering for extended periods to develop deep, rich flavors. The Instant Pot significantly reduces the cooking time without compromising on taste.

  2. Effortless One-Pot Cooking: With an Instant Pot, there’s no need to juggle multiple pots and pans. You can sauté, pressure cook, and even keep your curry warm—all in one pot.

  3. Flavor Infusion: Curries made in the Instant Pot allow pressure to intensify the blend of spices, resulting in a dish that tastes as though it’s been simmering for hours.

  4. Perfectly Cooked Ingredients: From tender chunks of meat to perfectly cooked lentils, the pressure cooking function ensures consistent results every time.

Recipe 1: Classic Butter Chicken

Butter chicken is perhaps the most iconic curry dish, characterized by its rich, tomato-based sauce and tender chicken. This Instant Pot version delivers all the creamy decadence of the traditional recipe in a fraction of the time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • ½ tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro (for garnish)

Directions:

  1. Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode and melt the butter. Add the onions, garlic, and ginger, and cook until fragrant and softened (about 3 minutes).
  2. Stir in the spices—garam masala, turmeric, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper—cooking for 1-2 minutes until they release their aroma.
  3. Add the chicken pieces, stirring to coat them in the spice mixture. Cook until they’re lightly browned on all sides.
  4. Pour in the tomato puree and give everything a good stir. Cancel sauté mode, secure the lid, and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 8 minutes.
  5. Once the cooking is complete, allow the pressure to naturally release for 10 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
  6. Stir in the heavy cream and set the Instant Pot to sauté for 2-3 minutes, letting the sauce thicken slightly.
  7. Garnish with chopped cilantro. Serve with steamed basmati rice or naan bread, and enjoy this restaurant-quality meal at home.

Recipe 2: Thai Green Curry with Vegetables

For those who prefer a lighter yet equally flavorful curry option, this Thai green curry is a must-try. The Instant Pot ensures that the veggies are perfectly tender without being mushy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp green curry paste
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 medium carrot, sliced
  • ½ cup snap peas
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh Thai basil leaves (for garnish)

Directions:

  1. Turn the Instant Pot to sauté mode and melt the coconut oil. Add the green curry paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes to release its flavors.
  2. Stir in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, ensuring the curry paste is well incorporated.
  3. Add all the vegetables—bell pepper, broccoli, zucchini, carrot, and snap peas. Stir gently to coat the vegetables in the liquid.
  4. Add the soy sauce and sugar. Cancel sauté mode, seal the lid, and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 2 minutes.
  5. Allow the pressure to naturally release for 5 minutes before performing a quick release.
  6. Stir in the lime juice and garnish with fresh Thai basil leaves. Serve hot with jasmine rice or rice noodles for a vibrant, satisfying meal.

Recipe 3: Hearty Lentil Dal

Lentil dal is a vegan-friendly curry option that’s both nutritious and budget-friendly. The Instant Pot takes the guesswork out of cooking lentils, making this dish incredibly easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp chili powder
  • 3 cups water or vegetable stock
  • ½ tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro (for garnish)

Directions:

  1. On sauté mode, heat the oil and cook the onions, garlic, and ginger until softened.
  2. Add the spices—coriander, cumin, turmeric, and chili powder—and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
  3. Mix in the chopped tomato and cook until softened (around 3 minutes).
  4. Add the rinsed lentils, water or vegetable stock, and salt. Cancel sauté mode, secure the lid, and set the Instant Pot to pressure cook on high for 8 minutes.
  5. Perform a natural release after cooking, then stir in the lemon juice for a touch of brightness.
  6. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with warm roti or rice. This simple yet flavorful dish is perfect for a comforting, plant-based dinner.

Tips for Perfect Instant Pot Curries

  • Bloom Your Spices: Always sauté your spices for a minute or two before pressure cooking. This step releases their essential oils, giving your curry a deeper and more complex flavor.
  • Adjust the Heat: If you love spicy food, feel free to increase the amount of chili or cayenne pepper in your recipes. If you’re cooking for a milder palate, reduce the spice or omit it altogether.
  • Balance the Flavors: A good curry strikes a balance between spice, acidity, and sweetness. Don’t hesitate to adjust the seasoning with lime juice, sugar, or salt to achieve your desired taste.
  • Layer the Ingredients: To prevent the dreaded “Burn” warning on your Instant Pot, avoid mixing thick ingredients (like tomato puree) with the bottom layer. Instead, layer liquids first and give the pot a small stir before sealing.

With the Instant Pot, creating exotic curries at home has never been simpler or more rewarding. These recipes bring vibrant global flavors to your table with minimal effort, making them ideal for busy weeknights or leisurely meals with loved ones. Whether you’re in the mood for creamy butter chicken, zesty green curry, or hearty lentil dal, these dishes are sure to impress even the choosiest eaters. Grab your Instant Pot, stock up on fragrant spices, and get ready to enjoy some of the best homemade curries you’ve ever tasted!
