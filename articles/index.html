10 Easy Vegan Stir-Fry Recipes for Busy Weeknights

If you’re someone who craves a quick, nutritious, and delicious meal after a long day, vegan stir-fries are the perfect solution. They are versatile, quick to prepare, and can be packed with a variety of vegetables and proteins, making them a fantastic option for busy weeknights. Here, we present ten easy vegan stir-fry recipes that will keep your weeknight dinners exciting and healthy. 1. Classic Vegetable Stir-Fry Ingredients: 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snap peas) 1 block firm tofu, cubed 3 tablespoons soy sauce 2 tablespoons sesame oil 1 tablespoon ginger, minced 2 cloves garlic, minced Cooked rice or quinoa, for serving Instructions: Prepare the Tofu: Press the tofu to remove excess moisture, then cut it into cubes. Sauté the Tofu: In a large pan, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove and set aside. Cook the Vegetables: In the same pan, add ginger and garlic. Sauté for 1 minute, then add the mixed vegetables. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes until tender-crisp. Combine: Return the tofu to the pan, pour in the soy sauce, and toss everything together. Serve over rice or quinoa. 2. Spicy Szechuan Noodles Ingredients: 8 oz. rice noodles 2 cups broccoli florets 1 bell pepper, sliced 1 cup snap peas 3 tablespoons Szechuan sauce 2 tablespoons vegetable oil Green onions, for garnish Instructions: Cook the Noodles: Prepare the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside. Stir-Fry the Veggies: In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add broccoli, bell pepper, and snap peas, and sauté for 4-5 minutes. Combine with Noodles: Add the cooked noodles and Szechuan sauce to the pan, tossing until everything is well-coated. Cook for an additional 2 minutes. Serve: Garnish with sliced green onions before serving. 3. Chickpea and Spinach Stir-Fry Ingredients: 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed 3 cups fresh spinach 1 onion, sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon lemon juice Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Sauté Onion and Garlic: Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, cooking until translucent. Add Chickpeas: Stir in the chickpeas and cook for 3-4 minutes until heated through. Incorporate Spinach: Add spinach to the pan, cooking until wilted. Season with lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Serve: Serve warm over rice or in a wrap. 4. Peanut Butter Tofu Stir-Fry Ingredients: 1 block firm tofu, cubed 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, zucchini) 3 tablespoons peanut butter 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon maple syrup 1 tablespoon vegetable oil Instructions: Cook Tofu: In a skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add cubed tofu and cook until golden. Prepare Sauce: In a bowl, mix peanut butter, soy sauce, and maple syrup until smooth. Stir-Fry Vegetables: Add mixed vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for 5 minutes. Combine with Sauce: Pour the peanut sauce over the tofu and vegetables, mixing until well combined. Serve hot. 5. Sweet and Sour Tempeh Stir-Fry Ingredients: 1 block tempeh, cubed 1 cup pineapple chunks 1 bell pepper, sliced 1 onion, sliced 3 tablespoons sweet and sour sauce 2 tablespoons vegetable oil Instructions: Cook Tempeh: Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add tempeh and cook until browned. Add Vegetables: Stir in onion and bell pepper, cooking for about 5 minutes. Incorporate Pineapple: Add pineapple chunks and sweet and sour sauce, cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes. Serve: Serve over rice or quinoa for a filling meal. 6. Garlic Mushroom and Asparagus Stir-Fry Ingredients: 2 cups mushrooms, sliced 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut 3 cloves garlic, minced 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Sauté Garlic: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for 1 minute. Cook Mushrooms: Add sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and start to brown. Add Asparagus: Toss in the asparagus and cook for another 5 minutes. Season with balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper. Serve: Serve as a side dish or over grains for a complete meal. 7. Coconut Curry Vegetable Stir-Fry Ingredients: 1 can coconut milk 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, cauliflower) 2 tablespoons curry powder 1 tablespoon vegetable oil Fresh cilantro, for garnish Instructions: Cook Vegetables: Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for 5 minutes. Add Coconut Milk: Pour in the coconut milk and stir in curry powder. Simmer for 10 minutes until the vegetables are tender. Garnish: Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. 8. Lemon Basil Stir-Fry Ingredients: 2 cups broccoli florets 1 bell pepper, sliced 1 cup snap peas 1 tablespoon lemon juice Fresh basil, chopped 2 tablespoons olive oil Instructions: Sauté Vegetables: In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add broccoli, bell pepper, and snap peas. Sauté for 5 minutes. Add Lemon Juice: Stir in lemon juice and fresh basil, cooking for an additional 2 minutes. Serve: This stir-fry pairs beautifully with quinoa or rice. 9. Miso Eggplant Stir-Fry Ingredients: 2 eggplants, cubed 1 bell pepper, sliced 2 tablespoons miso paste 2 tablespoons vegetable oil 3 tablespoons water Green onions, for garnish Instructions: Cook Eggplant: Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add cubed eggplant and sauté until soft. Add Bell Pepper: Stir in bell pepper and cook for another 3-4 minutes. Make Miso Sauce: In a bowl, mix miso paste with water until smooth. Pour over the eggplant and bell pepper, cooking for an additional 2 minutes. Serve: Garnish with green onions and serve over rice. 10. Zucchini Noodle Stir-Fry Ingredients: 2 medium zucchinis, spiralized 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved 1 bell pepper, sliced 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 tablespoon pesto Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Sauté Vegetables: In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add cherry tomatoes and bell pepper, cooking for 3-4 minutes. Add Zucchini Noodles: Toss in the spiralized zucchini and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until just tender. Finish with Pesto: Stir in pesto, seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve immediately. Flavorful Vegan Coconut Chickpea Curry

This Flavorful Vegan Coconut Chickpea Curry is a delightful and satisfying dish that will please both vegans and non-vegans alike. Packed with aromatic spices, creamy coconut milk, and protein-rich chickpeas, this curry is a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or new to vegan cooking, this recipe is easy to follow and will surely impress your taste buds. Ingredients 1 tablespoon coconut oil 1 onion, finely chopped 3 cloves of garlic, minced 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, grated 1 tablespoon curry powder 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (adjust according to your spice preference) 1 can (400 ml) coconut milk 1 can (400 g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed 1 red bell pepper, diced 1 zucchini, diced 1 cup baby spinach Salt and pepper, to taste Fresh cilantro, for garnish Instructions Heat the coconut oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent burning. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the pan. Cook for another 2 minutes, stirring constantly to release their flavors. In a small bowl, combine the curry powder, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric powder, and chili flakes. Mix well to create a fragrant spice blend. Add the spice blend to the pan and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to coat the onion, garlic, and ginger with the spices. Pour in the coconut milk and stir well to combine with the spice mixture. Bring the curry to a gentle simmer. Add the drained and rinsed chickpeas, diced red bell pepper, and diced zucchini to the pan. Stir to coat the vegetables with the curry sauce. Cover the pan and let the curry simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still have a slight crunch. Stir in the baby spinach and cook for an additional 2 minutes, until wilted. Season the curry with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the spice level by adding more chili flakes if desired. Serve the Flavorful Vegan Coconut Chickpea Curry hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. It pairs perfectly with steamed rice or warm naan bread. Why You’ll Love This Recipe Flavorful: The combination of aromatic spices like curry powder, cumin, coriander, and turmeric creates a rich and complex flavor profile that will tantalize your taste buds. Creamy and Coconutty: The addition of coconut milk adds a creamy and luscious texture to the curry, while also imparting a subtle coconut flavor that enhances the overall taste. Protein-Packed: Chickpeas are a great source of plant-based protein, making this curry a satisfying and nutritious meal option. Versatile: Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables or adjusting the spice level to suit your taste preferences. You can also experiment with different garnishes, such as chopped cashews or a squeeze of fresh lime juice. Vegan Party Snacks to Impress Your Guests

Throwing a party and want to impress your guests with delicious vegan snacks? Look no further! Stuffed Mini Peppers Ingredients: 12 mini sweet peppers 1 cup cooked quinoa 1/2 cup black beans, rinsed and drained 1/2 cup corn (fresh or frozen) 1/4 cup diced red onion 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped 1 tablespoon lime juice Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Slice the mini sweet peppers in half lengthwise and remove the seeds. In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, red onion, cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Stuff each mini pepper half with the quinoa mixture and place them on a baking sheet. Bake for 15-20 minutes until the peppers are tender. Serve warm or at room temperature for a colorful, healthy snack. 2. Avocado and Chickpea Salad Toast Ingredients: 1 ripe avocado 1 can chickpeas, rinsed and drained 1 tablespoon tahini 1 tablespoon lemon juice Salt and pepper to taste 4 slices of whole-grain bread Cherry tomatoes and microgreens for topping Instructions: In a bowl, mash the avocado with a fork. Add chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined but still chunky. Toast the whole-grain bread slices until golden brown. Spread the avocado and chickpea mixture generously on each slice. Top with halved cherry tomatoes and a sprinkle of microgreens for a fresh touch. 3. Crispy Baked Cauliflower Wings Ingredients: 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets 1 cup almond flour 1 cup plant-based milk 1 cup breadcrumbs 1 teaspoon garlic powder 1 teaspoon smoked paprika Salt and pepper to taste 1 cup buffalo sauce Instructions: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. In a bowl, combine almond flour, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Dip each cauliflower floret into the plant-based milk, then coat with the almond flour mixture, followed by breadcrumbs. Place the coated florets on the baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes until golden and crispy. Toss the baked cauliflower wings in buffalo sauce and return to the oven for an additional 5 minutes. Serve with celery sticks and a vegan ranch dip for an irresistible appetizer. 4. Spinach and Artichoke Dip Ingredients: 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained 1 can artichoke hearts, drained and chopped 1 cup cashews, soaked in water for 2 hours 1/2 cup nutritional yeast 1/4 cup plant-based milk 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 cloves garlic Salt and pepper to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a blender, combine soaked cashews, nutritional yeast, plant-based milk, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy. In a mixing bowl, combine the spinach, artichoke hearts, and cashew mixture. Stir well to combine. Transfer the dip to a baking dish and bake for 20-25 minutes until bubbly and golden on top. Zucchini and Corn Fritters Ingredients: 2 medium zucchinis, grated 1 cup corn (fresh or frozen) 1/2 cup chickpea flour 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water (flax egg) 2 green onions, chopped Salt and pepper to taste Olive oil for frying Instructions: Squeeze excess moisture from the grated zucchini using a clean kitchen towel. In a mixing bowl, combine zucchini, corn, chickpea flour, flax egg, green onions, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Drop spoonfuls of the mixture into the skillet and flatten slightly. Cook for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden brown and crispy. Serve warm with a vegan yogurt dip or salsa. 9. Roasted Chickpeas Ingredients: 1 can chickpeas, rinsed and drained 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 teaspoon smoked paprika 1 teaspoon garlic powder Salt to taste Instructions: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Pat the chickpeas dry with a paper towel and place them in a bowl. Drizzle with olive oil, add smoked paprika, garlic powder, and salt. Toss to coat evenly. Spread the chickpeas in a single layer on the baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through until they are golden and crunchy.
