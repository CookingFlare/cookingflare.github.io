The Best Thai Desserts: Sweet Recipes to Satisfy Your Cravings


Thailand is known for its vibrant food scene, rich in flavors that span the savory and sweet. Thai desserts, often marked by their perfect balance of sweet, salty, and creamy, are an essential part of the culinary culture. Whether you’re craving something refreshing or indulgent, Thai sweets offer something for every taste. This guide will take you through some of the most beloved Thai desserts, each one a treat worth savoring. From sticky rice with mango to coconut-flavored delicacies, these recipes will bring the essence of Thailand’s sweet tooth into your kitchen.

1. Mango Sticky Rice (Khao Niew Mamuang)

Perhaps the most famous Thai dessert internationally, Mango Sticky Rice, or Khao Niew Mamuang, combines the best of two worlds: sweet, juicy mango and sticky, coconut-infused rice. This dessert is as satisfying to the eyes as it is to the palate, with its bright, golden mango slices perched on top of the sticky rice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (sticky rice)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes (peeled, pitted, and sliced)
  • Sesame seeds or mung beans for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sticky Rice: Wash the sticky rice thoroughly, and soak it in water for at least 1 hour. Once soaked, steam it for about 20-30 minutes until tender.
  2. Make the Coconut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Stir over low heat until the sugar dissolves completely. Be careful not to boil the mixture.
  3. Combine Rice and Coconut Sauce: Once the sticky rice is cooked, transfer it to a bowl. Pour the coconut milk mixture over the rice, stirring gently to ensure every grain is coated. Let the rice absorb the coconut sauce for about 20 minutes.
  4. Serve: Arrange the sticky rice on a plate, topping it with fresh mango slices. Garnish with sesame seeds or mung beans for a little extra texture and flavor.

This simple yet divine dessert is perfect for any occasion, but it’s especially popular during the mango season, which typically peaks during the summer months in Thailand.

2. Thai Coconut Custard (Sangkhaya)

Another favorite, Sangkhaya is a smooth, fragrant coconut custard that has a rich, creamy texture. It’s a popular dessert served in a variety of ways, but the most common is in small cups or bowls made from pandan leaves, giving it a unique touch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 pandan leaves (optional)
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Steamer: In a steamer, bring water to a boil. If you’re using pandan leaves, tie them in a knot and place them in the steamer for extra flavor.
  2. Mix the Custard: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, coconut milk, palm sugar, and salt until well combined. Make sure the sugar is completely dissolved. If you want a more aromatic custard, you can steep the pandan leaves in the coconut milk beforehand.
  3. Steam the Custard: Pour the custard mixture into small heatproof bowls or cups. Place them in the steamer and cook for 15-20 minutes, or until the custard is set and firm to the touch.
  4. Cool and Serve: Let the custard cool before serving. It can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a dollop of sweetened coconut cream.

The silky custard paired with the fragrant coconut and subtle sweetness from the palm sugar makes this dessert irresistible.

3. Thai Coconut Ice Cream (I-Scream)

Thai coconut ice cream, also known as I-Scream, is creamy, refreshing, and packed with the rich taste of coconut. Often served in a coconut shell with an array of toppings, this frozen treat is ideal for cooling down on a hot day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or coconut cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Crushed peanuts, sweet corn, or chocolate chips for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Ice Cream Base: In a bowl, whisk together the coconut milk, sugar, heavy cream, salt, and vanilla extract. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Freeze the Mixture: Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. If you don’t have an ice cream maker, pour the mixture into a shallow pan and place it in the freezer, stirring every 30 minutes to break up the ice crystals.
  3. Serve: Once the ice cream is firm, scoop it into serving bowls or coconut shells. Top with your favorite toppings, such as crushed peanuts, sweet corn, or chocolate chips for added texture.

This tropical dessert brings a creamy indulgence with every bite and is perfect for hot summer days.

4. Thai Fried Bananas (Kluay Tod)

Kluay Tod is a deep-fried banana treat that is both crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. It’s a street food staple in Thailand, typically served with a dusting of sesame seeds or a drizzle of honey.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas (small variety, like the Thai “Kluay Namwa”)
  • 1/2 cup rice flour
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Sesame seeds or honey for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bananas: Peel the bananas and slice them into small pieces, about 2 inches long.
  2. Make the Batter: In a bowl, whisk together the rice flour, all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Gradually add cold water to form a smooth batter.
  3. Fry the Bananas: Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat. Dip the banana pieces into the batter, ensuring they are evenly coated. Carefully fry them in the hot oil until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes. Be sure to fry in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan.
  4. Drain and Serve: Remove the fried bananas and drain excess oil on paper towels. Garnish with sesame seeds or drizzle with honey for added sweetness and flavor.

These sweet, crispy banana bites make a great snack or dessert to enjoy at any time of day.

5. Thai Sticky Rice with Durian (Khao Niew Durian)

Durian, often called the “king of fruits” in Southeast Asia, has a distinctive aroma and flavor that is both loved and loathed by many. For those who appreciate its unique taste, Khao Niew Durian is a delightful dessert. It combines the creamy, custard-like texture of durian with the soft, sticky rice, creating a perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sticky rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2-3 ripe durian pods (or durian paste if fresh durian is unavailable)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Sticky Rice: Soak the sticky rice in water for at least 1 hour, then steam it until tender.
  2. Make the Coconut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat gently, stirring until the sugar dissolves completely.
  3. Combine Rice and Coconut Sauce: Pour the coconut mixture over the steamed sticky rice, mixing well so that the rice absorbs the coconut flavor.
  4. Serve with Durian: Once the sticky rice is ready, serve it topped with fresh durian flesh. If you’re using durian paste, simply spoon it onto the rice.

This dessert is perfect for durian lovers and offers a creamy, coconut-infused experience.

6. Thai Palm Sugar Jelly (Saku Pandan)

Saku Pandan is a vibrant green jelly made from pandan leaves and palm sugar. It’s refreshing, slightly chewy, and has an earthy, sweet flavor. Often served chilled, this dessert is perfect for those who enjoy light, non-creamy treats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pandan juice (or pandan extract)
  • 1/2 cup palm sugar
  • 1/2 cup tapioca flour
  • 2 cups water
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Pandan Jelly Mixture: In a saucepan, combine pandan juice, palm sugar, and water. Heat the mixture over medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Add the Tapioca Flour: Gradually add the tapioca flour, whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until the mixture thickens.
  3. Mold the Jelly: Pour the mixture into small molds or an ice cube tray, and allow it to cool. Once set, refrigerate the jelly for at least an hour to firm up.
  4. Serve: Serve the pandan jelly chilled as a refreshing treat.

This bright green jelly is light, chewy, and full of aromatic pandan flavor, making it a refreshing end to a Thai meal.

Conclusion

Thai desserts offer an enticing variety of flavors and textures, with each dish providing a unique sensory experience. Whether you’re indulging in the creaminess of coconut, the sweetness of ripe mangoes, or the aromatic notes of pandan, there’s a Thai sweet treat to suit every craving. Try your hand at making these desserts at home, and you’ll soon understand why Thailand’s sweet offerings are so cherished around the world. Happy cooking!
