How to Make Korean Spicy Pork (Daeji Bulgogi) at Home


Korean cuisine is known for its bold and vibrant flavors, with dishes that are both savory and spicy. One of the most popular and beloved dishes in Korean households is Daeji Bulgogi, or Korean spicy pork. This dish features tender slices of pork marinated in a rich, spicy, and slightly sweet sauce, which is then grilled or pan-fried to perfection. If you love the flavors of Korean barbecue, this recipe will bring that authentic experience straight to your kitchen.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of making Daeji Bulgogi at home, including tips on selecting the best cuts of pork, preparing the marinade, and cooking it just like they do in Korea. Whether you’re a fan of Korean food or new to it, this recipe is simple to follow, and the result is absolutely delicious. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s talk about the ingredients. Daeji Bulgogi requires some specific elements to capture its signature flavor profile, but most of them are easily available at your local grocery store or an Asian market.

For the Marinade:

  • 500g (1 lb) pork shoulder or pork belly – Thinly sliced
  • 3 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste) – This provides the spicy, umami-rich base of the marinade.
  • 2 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) – Adds another layer of spice and texture to the dish.
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce – Brings saltiness and depth of flavor.
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil – Imparts a nutty aroma and flavor.
  • 2 tbsp sugar – Balances out the heat from the gochujang and gochugaru.
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar – Adds a touch of acidity for balance.
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic – Fresh garlic for that aromatic punch.
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger – Gives a warm, slightly spicy note to the marinade.
  • 2 tbsp grated pear or apple – Korean cuisine often uses fruit to tenderize meat, and pear is commonly used in Bulgogi recipes.
  • 2 tbsp chopped green onions – Adds a fresh, mild onion flavor.
  • Black pepper – To taste, typically a pinch or two.

For the Garnish and Cooking:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil – For frying.
  • Sliced onions – Optional, for added sweetness and texture.
  • Sesame seeds – To sprinkle on top.
  • Sliced green onions – For garnish.
  • Rice – Steamed white rice, for serving.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Daeji Bulgogi

1. Prepare the Pork

For Daeji Bulgogi, it’s crucial to choose the right cut of pork. Pork shoulder or pork belly works best because of its tenderness and natural fat content, which makes the meat juicy and flavorful when grilled or pan-fried. You want to slice the pork thinly so that it absorbs the marinade well and cooks quickly.

Use a sharp knife to slice the pork thinly against the grain. If you find it hard to slice the pork thinly, you can place the meat in the freezer for about 30 minutes before cutting—it will firm up, making it easier to slice.

2. Make the Marinade

Now that your pork is prepped, it’s time to make the marinade. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the gochujang (Korean chili paste), gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, rice vinegar, minced garlic, minced ginger, grated pear or apple, and chopped green onions. Mix everything together until you get a smooth and evenly combined sauce.

Taste the marinade and adjust the seasoning if needed. You may want to add a little more sugar for sweetness or more gochugaru for extra heat. The balance of flavors in the marinade is key to achieving the perfect Daeji Bulgogi, so don’t be afraid to tweak it to your liking.

3. Marinate the Pork

Once the marinade is prepared, add the thinly sliced pork to the bowl. Toss the meat well, making sure every piece is fully coated with the marinade. For the best results, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. If you have the time, marinating the pork for 4-6 hours (or even overnight) will allow the flavors to fully penetrate the meat, making it more flavorful and tender.

4. Cooking the Daeji Bulgogi

Pan-Frying Method:

If you don’t have a grill or prefer cooking indoors, the pan-frying method is an excellent option. Heat a tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated pork in a single layer. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure the pork cooks evenly.

Cook the pork for 3-4 minutes on each side until it’s crispy around the edges and fully cooked through. Make sure to stir occasionally to ensure the meat cooks evenly and gets nice caramelization.

Grilling Method:

For that signature smoky flavor that you get at Korean BBQ restaurants, grilling is the way to go. Preheat your grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. Once it’s hot, place the marinated pork on the grill and cook it for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the meat is cooked through and has beautiful grill marks.

Regardless of the method you choose, the pork should be tender, slightly charred, and rich in flavor from the marinade. The slightly crispy edges will give it that irresistible texture that’s characteristic of Daeji Bulgogi.

5. Garnishing and Serving

Once the Daeji Bulgogi is cooked, it’s time to plate it up. Serve the spicy pork hot, garnished with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and freshly chopped green onions. You can also serve it with some grilled onions or lettuce leaves for wrapping.

Daeji Bulgogi is traditionally served with steamed rice on the side, and you can complement the dish with some Korean banchan (side dishes), such as kimchi, pickled radish, or stir-fried vegetables. The heat from the pork pairs beautifully with the coolness of the rice and the tanginess of the sides.

Tips for the Perfect Daeji Bulgogi

  • Choose the Right Pork: As mentioned, pork shoulder or pork belly is ideal for this dish. It has the perfect balance of fat and tenderness, ensuring a juicy and flavorful outcome.
  • Adjust the Spice: Korean spicy pork is meant to have a good amount of heat, but you can adjust the spice level by controlling the amount of gochujang and gochugaru you use. If you prefer a milder version, use less chili paste and flakes.
  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh garlic, ginger, and pear or apple really make a difference in the flavor of the marinade. Don’t use substitutes if you can avoid it.
  • Let It Marinate: The longer you marinate the pork, the more flavorful it will be. At a minimum, marinate it for an hour, but if possible, leave it in the fridge for a few hours or overnight to let the flavors meld together.
  • Sear for Crispiness: Whether you’re pan-frying or grilling, make sure to sear the pork for a few minutes on each side to get that slightly crispy texture that makes the dish so special.

Why You’ll Love Daeji Bulgogi

Daeji Bulgogi is one of those dishes that’s packed with flavor and perfect for any occasion. It combines the heat from gochujang and gochugaru, the savory umami from soy sauce, and the slight sweetness from pear or apple. This balance of flavors makes the dish irresistible, and whether you’re grilling it up for a family dinner or serving it at a gathering, it’s sure to be a hit.

The best part about making Daeji Bulgogi at home is that you can adjust the spice level to suit your taste, and you have complete control over the quality of the ingredients. Plus, it’s an easy and fun recipe to make for anyone who loves Korean food or is curious about exploring new cuisines.

Conclusion

Making Daeji Bulgogi at home is a wonderful way to bring the flavors of Korean cuisine into your kitchen. With its perfect balance of heat, sweetness, and savory umami, this dish is a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or a group of friends, this Korean spicy pork recipe is sure to impress.

So, next time you’re craving a delicious and authentic Korean dish, try making Daeji Bulgogi. It’s a simple yet flavorful meal that will leave everyone coming back for more!
