A Culinary Love Story: Valentine's Day Dinner Recipes to Remember


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and with it comes the pressure to create a romantic dinner that will leave your loved one weak in the knees. But don’t worry, we’ve got you covered. In this post, we’ll take you on a culinary journey that will make your Valentine’s Day dinner one to remember.

Amuse Bouche: Seared Scallops with Garlic Butter

Start your meal off right with these succulent seared scallops, served with a rich garlic butter sauce. This bite-sized appetizer is sure to set the tone for a romantic evening.

  • 12 large scallops
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Rinse the scallops under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels. Season with salt and pepper. Heat the butter in a skillet over medium-high heat and add the garlic. Sear the scallops for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they’re cooked through. Serve with a dollop of garlic butter sauce, made by mixing the white wine and heavy cream with the garlic butter.

Appetizer: Heirloom Tomato Salad with Burrata Cheese

This fresh and flavorful salad is the perfect palate cleanser before the main course. The creamy burrata cheese pairs perfectly with the sweet heirloom tomatoes.

  • 4 heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 ball burrata cheese
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped (optional)

Arrange the tomato slices on a platter and top with the burrata cheese. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic glaze. Sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with chopped fresh basil leaves, if desired.

Main Course: Filet Mignon with Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes and Broccolini

This decadent filet mignon is cooked to perfection and served with creamy roasted garlic mashed potatoes and a side of steamed broccolini.

  • 4 filet mignon steaks, 6 ounces each
  • 2 heads of garlic, separated into individual cloves
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cups mashed potatoes
  • 4 cups broccolini florets
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roast the garlic cloves in the oven for 30-40 minutes, or until they’re tender and caramelized. Mash the roasted garlic with the butter and heavy cream to make the mashed potatoes. Season the filet mignon steaks with salt and pepper. Grill or pan-fry the steaks to desired doneness. Serve with the roasted garlic mashed potatoes and steamed broccolini.

Dessert: Chocolate Lava Cake with Vanilla Ice Cream

End your meal with a rich and decadent chocolate lava cake, served with a scoop of creamy vanilla ice cream.

  • 2 ounces high-quality dark chocolate, broken into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 scoop vanilla ice cream

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, and vanilla extract. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and stir until just combined. Melt the chocolate in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth. Fold the chocolate into the batter until well combined. Pour the batter into 4 ramekins or small cups. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are set and the centers are still slightly jiggly. Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Beverage Pairing: Champagne Cocktail

Pair your meal with a glass of bubbly champagne, mixed with a splash of citrus and a touch of sweetness.

  • 1 bottle champagne
  • 1/2 cup triple sec
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1/4 cup simple syrup
  • Slices of orange, for garnish

In a champagne flute, combine 1 ounce of champagne, 1/2 ounce of triple sec, 1/2 ounce of orange juice, and 1/4 ounce of simple syrup. Stir gently and garnish with a slice of orange.

And there you have it, a culinary love story that will make your Valentine’s Day dinner one to remember. From the amuse bouche to the main course and dessert, this menu is sure to impress your loved one. So go ahead, get cooking, and make this Valentine’s Day one to cherish.
