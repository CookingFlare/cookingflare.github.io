Savory Sausage and Sage Stuffing Recipe for Christmas Dinner


Christmas dinner is a time for indulgence, delicious flavors, and creating lasting memories with loved ones. One dish that never fails to impress is a classic savory sausage and sage stuffing. This hearty and flavorful side dish is the perfect accompaniment to your roast turkey or ham, adding a touch of warmth and comfort to your festive feast. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a mouthwatering sausage and sage stuffing that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of day-old bread, cut into small cubes
  • 1 pound of ground sausage (choose your favorite variety)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 stalks of celery, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a large skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Use a wooden spoon to break up the sausage into small crumbles. Remove the cooked sausage from the skillet and set aside.

  3. In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced onion, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread cubes, cooked sausage, sautéed vegetables, chopped sage, and parsley. Toss everything together until well combined.

  5. Gradually pour the chicken or vegetable broth over the bread mixture, stirring gently to moisten the bread evenly. Add salt and pepper to taste. Be careful not to oversaturate the stuffing; it should be moist but not soggy.

  6. Transfer the stuffing mixture into a greased baking dish, spreading it out evenly. Cover the dish with aluminum foil.

  7. Bake the stuffing covered for 30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.

  8. Once the stuffing is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This savory sausage and sage stuffing pairs perfectly with your Christmas turkey or ham, adding an extra layer of flavor to your festive meal. Serve it alongside your main course and watch as your guests savor every bite. For a complete holiday feast, complement the stuffing with classic sides like roasted Brussels sprouts, creamy mashed potatoes, and tangy cranberry sauce.

Conclusion

This savory sausage and sage stuffing recipe is a tried and true classic that will elevate your Christmas dinner to new heights. The combination of flavorful sausage, aromatic sage, and tender bread cubes creates a stuffing that is both comforting and delicious. Prepare this dish ahead of time to save yourself some stress on the big day, and enjoy the compliments from your loved ones as they savor the rich flavors. Merry Christmas and happy cooking!
