Homemade Mixed Berry Crumb Cake: A Delicious and Fruity Treat


Looking for a delightful dessert that combines the sweetness of mixed berries with the comforting texture of a crumbly cake? Look no further! This Homemade Mixed Berry Crumb Cake recipe is the perfect balance of fruity goodness and indulgent satisfaction. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

For the cake batter:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk

For the mixed berry filling:

  • 2 cups mixed berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch

For the crumb topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch square baking pan.

  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt for the cake batter. Add the softened butter and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, vanilla extract, and buttermilk. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

  4. In another bowl, toss the mixed berries with sugar and cornstarch to create the filling. Make sure the berries are evenly coated.

  5. Pour half of the cake batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly. Top with the mixed berry filling, spreading it out to cover the entire surface. Pour the remaining cake batter over the berries, spreading it carefully to create an even layer.

  6. In a small bowl, combine the flour and sugar for the crumb topping. Pour in the melted butter and mix until crumbly. Sprinkle the crumb topping evenly over the cake batter.

  7. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Once the cake has cooled, slice it into squares and serve. You can enjoy this Mixed Berry Crumb Cake on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different combinations of berries. You can use fresh or frozen berries, depending on what’s available.
  • Add a hint of citrus by incorporating lemon zest into the cake batter or drizzling lemon glaze over the finished cake.
  • If you prefer a more pronounced crumb topping, increase the amount of flour, sugar, and butter accordingly.

This Homemade Mixed Berry Crumb Cake is a versatile treat that can be enjoyed year-round. Whether you serve it as a dessert for a special occasion or enjoy it as a sweet indulgence with your afternoon tea, this fruity delight is sure to impress. So grab your mixing bowl and get ready to savor the flavors of summer in every bite!
