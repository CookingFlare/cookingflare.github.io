Slow Cooker Beef Stew: Easy and Delicious


There’s nothing quite like coming home to the aroma of a hearty beef stew simmering away in the slow cooker. This classic dish is not only comforting and delicious but also incredibly easy to prepare. Whether you’re a busy professional, a parent juggling multiple responsibilities, or simply someone who loves a good home-cooked meal, this slow cooker beef stew recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Start by preparing all the ingredients. Cut the beef stew meat into bite-sized pieces, slice the carrots, cube the potatoes, chop the onion, and mince the garlic.

  2. Coat the Beef: In a bowl, mix the flour, salt, and pepper. Toss the beef pieces in the flour mixture until they are evenly coated.

  3. Sear the Beef: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the coated beef pieces until they are browned on all sides. This step adds depth of flavor to the stew.

  4. Assemble the Stew: Place the browned beef, carrots, potatoes, onion, and garlic in the slow cooker. Add the bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). Stir in the tomato paste until it’s well incorporated.

  5. Cook the Stew: Cover the slow cooker and cook the beef stew on low for 7-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours. The long, slow cooking time allows the flavors to meld together, resulting in a rich and savory stew.

  6. Thicken the Stew (Optional): If you prefer a thicker stew, mix the flour and water in a small bowl to create a slurry. Stir the slurry into the stew during the last 30 minutes of cooking, and allow the stew to thicken.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once the beef is tender and the vegetables are cooked through, it’s time to serve the stew. Remove the bay leaves and season with additional salt and pepper if needed.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra depth of flavor, you can add a splash of Worcestershire sauce or balsamic vinegar to the stew.
  • Feel free to customize the vegetables to your liking. Mushrooms, celery, and parsnips are all great additions to beef stew.
  • Leftover stew can be refrigerated and enjoyed for several days, and the flavors often improve with time.

In Conclusion

This slow cooker beef stew recipe is a perfect example of “set it and forget it” cooking. It’s a simple, no-fuss way to enjoy a wholesome and satisfying meal without spending hours in the kitchen. The tender beef, flavorful broth, and hearty vegetables come together to create a dish that’s both comforting and nourishing. Whether it’s a cozy family dinner or a gathering with friends, this easy and delicious beef stew is sure to be a hit. So dust off that slow cooker and give this recipe a try – you won’t be disappointed!
