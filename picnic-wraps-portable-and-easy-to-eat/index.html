Picnic Wraps: Portable and Easy to Eat


When it comes to planning a picnic, one of the most important considerations is what to bring to eat. You want something that’s easy to transport, can be eaten on the go, and is delicious, of course. That’s where picnic wraps come in. These tasty, portable meals are perfect for a day out in the park or a backyard BBQ.

What is a Picnic Wrap?

A picnic wrap is a type of sandwich wrap that’s designed to be easy to eat on the go. It’s typically made with a large flour tortilla or flatbread that’s filled with a variety of ingredients, such as meats, cheeses, vegetables, and condiments. The wrap is then rolled up and secured with a toothpick or twist tie, making it easy to hold and eat.

Benefits of Picnic Wraps

There are many benefits to using picnic wraps for your outdoor gatherings. For one, they’re incredibly easy to make. Simply fill the wrap with your desired ingredients, roll it up, and you’re good to go. They’re also very portable, making them perfect for picnics, camping trips, or outdoor festivals. And, because they’re self-contained, you don’t have to worry about making a mess or carrying around multiple dishes.

Another benefit of picnic wraps is that they’re highly customizable. You can fill them with a wide variety of ingredients to suit your tastes and dietary needs. Plus, they’re a great way to use up leftover ingredients and reduce food waste.

Ingredients for Picnic Wraps

The ingredients you use for your picnic wraps will depend on your personal preferences and the type of gathering you’re hosting. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Meats: turkey, ham, roast beef, chicken, or vegetarian options like hummus or avocado
  • Cheeses: cheddar, Swiss, mozzarella, or feta
  • Vegetables: lettuce, tomato, cucumber, bell peppers, or sprouts
  • Condiments: mustard, mayonnaise, or ranch dressing
  • Spreads: hummus, guacamole, or tzatziki sauce
  • Fruits: sliced apples or grapes

Recipes for Picnic Wraps

Here are a few recipes for picnic wraps that you might enjoy:

Turkey and Avocado Wrap

  • 1 large flour tortilla
  • 2 slices of turkey breast
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of mixed greens
  • 1/4 cup of crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of mayonnaise
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Roll up the wrap and secure with a toothpick.

Veggie Wrap

  • 1 large flour tortilla
  • 1/2 cup of hummus
  • 1/2 cup of sliced bell peppers
  • 1/2 cup of sliced cucumber
  • 1/4 cup of sprouts
  • 1/4 cup of shredded carrot
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Roll up the wrap and secure with a toothpick.

Chicken Caesar Wrap

  • 1 large flour tortilla
  • 2 slices of chicken breast
  • 1/4 cup of Caesar dressing
  • 1/2 cup of romaine lettuce
  • 1/4 cup of crumbled parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Roll up the wrap and secure with a toothpick.

Tips for Making Picnic Wraps

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when making picnic wraps:

  • Use a large flour tortilla or flatbread to make the wrap easier to roll up.
  • Choose ingredients that complement each other in terms of flavor and texture.
  • Don’t overfill the wrap, as this can make it difficult to roll up and can cause ingredients to fall out.
  • Use a toothpick or twist tie to secure the wrap and prevent it from unraveling.
  • Consider packing a few extra ingredients, such as napkins and condiments, in a small bag or container.

Conclusion

Picnic wraps are a delicious and convenient option for your next outdoor gathering. They’re easy to make, portable, and highly customizable. With a few simple ingredients and a bit of creativity, you can create a wrap that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters. So next time you’re planning a picnic or outdoor event, consider giving picnic wraps a try. Your guests are sure to thank you!
