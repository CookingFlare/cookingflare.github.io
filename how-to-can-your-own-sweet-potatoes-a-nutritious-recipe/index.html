How to Can Your Own Sweet Potatoes: A Nutritious Recipe


Sweet potatoes are not only delicious but also packed with essential nutrients such as fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants. Canning sweet potatoes at home is a fantastic way to preserve them for future use while maintaining their natural flavors and health benefits. Whether you want to stock up for the chilly winter months or keep a supply of ready-to-eat foods for busy days, this guide will walk you step-by-step through the process of canning your own sweet potatoes. Let’s dive into this rewarding kitchen project!

Why Can Sweet Potatoes at Home?

Canning sweet potatoes is both practical and economical. Here are some reasons to consider doing it at home:

  1. Long Shelf Life: When canned properly, sweet potatoes can stay fresh and ready to use for up to 12–18 months.
  2. Nutritional Retention: Canning preserves the rich nutrients in sweet potatoes, like beta-carotene, keeping them as flavorful and healthful as the day you cooked them.
  3. Convenience: With canned sweet potatoes in your pantry, you’ll always have a quick meal or side dish ingredient on hand.
  4. Customizable Flavor: When you can sweet potatoes at home, you control the ingredients. Add natural sweeteners, spices, or keep them plain—it’s up to you.

Tools and Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you begin, gather the essential tools and ingredients to ensure a smooth canning process.

Tools:

  • A pressure canner (not a water bath canner—this is necessary for low-acid foods like sweet potatoes).
  • Mason jars (preferably pint or quart size), lids, and rings.
  • A jar lifter.
  • A large pot for boiling water.
  • A vegetable peeler or paring knife.
  • A cutting board and sharp kitchen knife.
  • A clean cloth or paper towel.
  • A bowl for preparing the sweet potatoes.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh, firm sweet potatoes.
  • Boiling water (you can also use a light syrup or broth instead, depending on your flavor preference).
  • Optional: cinnamon, nutmeg, or brown sugar for added flavor during the canning process.

Step-by-Step Instructions to Can Sweet Potatoes

1. Select and Prepare Your Sweet Potatoes

Choose fresh, unbruised sweet potatoes. Opt for medium-sized ones as they are easier to peel and evenly cook. Wash the sweet potatoes thoroughly under running water to remove dirt or debris.

2. Peel and Slice

Using a vegetable peeler or paring knife, remove the peels from the sweet potatoes. After peeling, cut the sweet potatoes into chunks or cubes, about 1.5 to 2 inches in size. Make sure the pieces are uniform so they cook evenly.

3. Cook the Sweet Potatoes

Place the sweet potato chunks into a large pot and cover with water. Bring the water to a boil and allow the potatoes to simmer for 2–3 minutes. Do not fully cook them—this step is just to soften them slightly to ensure they will hold up well during the canning process.

4. Prepare Your Canning Jars

While the sweet potatoes are simmering, prepare your jars. Wash your canning jars, lids, and rings in hot, soapy water and rinse thoroughly. Sterilize the jars by placing them in boiling water for 10 minutes, or simply run them through the hottest cycle of your dishwasher.

Keep the jars warm while you work, as sudden temperature changes can cause glass jars to crack.

5. Fill the Jars with Sweet Potatoes

Using a slotted spoon, carefully fill the sterilized jars with the sweet potato chunks. Leave about 1 inch of headspace at the top of each jar to allow for expansion during canning.

6. Add Liquid

Pour boiling water, light syrup, or broth over the sweet potatoes in the jars. Make sure the potatoes are completely submerged. Leave 1 inch of headspace at the top to prevent overflows during the pressure canning process.

If you’d like to add warmth and flavor, sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg into each jar before sealing. For a slightly sweeter twist, stir in a teaspoon of brown sugar.

7. Remove Air Bubbles

Use a bubble remover tool or a clean, non-metal spatula to remove any air bubbles trapped in the jars. Simply slide the tool down the inside edges of the jar, moving the contents around slightly to release trapped air.

8. Wipe and Seal the Jars

With a clean, damp cloth or paper towel, wipe the rims of each jar to remove any residue. This step is essential to ensure a proper seal. Place the sterilized canning lids on top of the jars, and screw on the rings until they are just fingertip-tight—do not overtighten.

9. Load the Pressure Canner

Fill your pressure canner with the recommended amount of water as per its manufacturer’s instructions. Carefully place the filled jars inside the canner, using a jar lifter to avoid burns or spills.

10. Process the Jars

Seal the pressure canner and heat it according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Process the jars at 10 PSI (pounds per square inch) for the appropriate amount of time based on your altitude:

  • Pint Jars: Process for 65 minutes.
  • Quart Jars: Process for 90 minutes.

If you live at an altitude above 1,000 feet, consult your pressure canner manual to adjust the PSI accordingly.

11. Cool and Check Seals

Once the canning time has elapsed, turn off the heat and allow the pressure canner to cool completely before opening. This may take 30–60 minutes. Once safe, remove the jars using your jar lifter and place them on a towel or heatproof surface to cool overnight.

As the jars cool, you’ll hear a popping sound—this is a sign that the lids are sealing properly. After 12–24 hours, test the seals by pressing down on the center of each lid. If the lid doesn’t flex or move, the jar is sealed. If a jar didn’t seal, refrigerate and use it within a week.

Storing Your Canned Sweet Potatoes

Label each jar with the date of canning to keep track of freshness. Store the jars in a cool, dark place, such as a pantry or basement. Properly sealed jars can last up to a year, but for the best quality, aim to use them within 12 months.

Tips for Using Your Canned Sweet Potatoes

Canned sweet potatoes are a versatile pantry staple. Here are a few ways to use them:

  • Mashed Sweet Potatoes: Drain the liquid, mash the potatoes, and season with butter, salt, and a touch of cinnamon.
  • Sweet Potato Casserole: Use them in a classic casserole topped with marshmallows and pecans.
  • Smoothies: Add a creamy, nutrient-packed element to your breakfast smoothie by blending some sweet potato chunks with almond milk and spices.
  • Soups and Stews: Toss them into your favorite soups or stews for added flavor and texture.

Final Thoughts

Canning your own sweet potatoes is a rewarding way to preserve the nutrition and taste of this versatile vegetable. With a little preparation, you can enjoy the convenience of having ready-to-use sweet potatoes at your fingertips year-round. Whether you’re meal prepping, stocking up for the winter, or simply enjoying the satisfaction of homemade goods, this process is worth every effort.

Take some time this season to explore the art of canning, and let sweet potatoes be your muse. Happy canning!
