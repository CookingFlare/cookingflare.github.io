Scrumptious Thanksgiving Meatball Recipes for a Crowd


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones and indulging in delicious food. If you’re hosting a Thanksgiving feast this year and want to impress your guests with a unique and flavorful dish, look no further than these scrumptious Thanksgiving meatball recipes. Perfect for feeding a crowd, these meatball recipes are sure to satisfy everyone’s taste buds.

1. Cranberry-Glazed Turkey Meatballs

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 40 minutes
Servings: 8-10

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground turkey, breadcrumbs, onion, parsley, egg, thyme, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into 1-inch meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. In a small saucepan, combine cranberry sauce, maple syrup, and Dijon mustard. Cook over medium heat until the sauce is smooth and heated through.
  5. Brush the meatballs with the cranberry glaze, reserving some for serving.
  6. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until cooked through.
  7. Serve the meatballs with the remaining cranberry glaze on the side.

2. Apple-Sage Pork Meatballs

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 50 minutes
Servings: 8-10

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground pork
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated apple
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup applesauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground pork, breadcrumbs, grated apple, sage, Parmesan cheese, milk, egg, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into 1-inch meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. In a small saucepan, combine applesauce, honey, and apple cider vinegar. Cook over medium heat until the sauce is heated through.
  5. Brush the meatballs with the applesauce glaze, reserving some for serving.
  6. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until cooked through.
  7. Serve the meatballs with the remaining applesauce glaze on the side.

3. Sweet and Spicy Cranberry-Glazed Meatballs

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Total Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 8-10

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cranberry sauce
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, onion, parsley, egg, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Shape the mixture into 1-inch meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. In a small saucepan, combine cranberry sauce, hot sauce, brown sugar, and soy sauce. Cook over medium heat until the sauce is smooth and heated through.
  5. Brush the meatballs with the cranberry glaze, reserving some for serving.
  6. Bake the meatballs in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until cooked through.
  7. Serve the meatballs with the remaining cranberry glaze on the side.

Final Thoughts

These scrumptious Thanksgiving meatball recipes are a fantastic addition to your Thanksgiving feast. Whether you prefer turkey, pork, or beef, these flavorful meatballs are sure to please a crowd. The tangy cranberry glaze in the first and third recipes adds a festive touch, while the apple-sage pork meatballs offer a delightful blend of flavors. Give these recipes a try and watch as your guests rave about your culinary skills this Thanksgiving!
