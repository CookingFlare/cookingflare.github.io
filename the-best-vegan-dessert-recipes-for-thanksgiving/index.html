The Best Vegan Dessert Recipes for Thanksgiving


Thanksgiving is a time for gratitude, family gatherings, and, of course, indulgent feasts. While traditional Thanksgiving desserts often center around pumpkin pie, pecan pie, and apple crisp, there’s a world of delicious vegan dessert options that can satisfy even the most discerning palate. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best vegan dessert recipes that will not only delight your taste buds but also impress your guests. These recipes are perfect for creating a festive atmosphere while ensuring that everyone at your table enjoys a sweet treat, regardless of their dietary preferences.

1. Decadent Vegan Pumpkin Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made vegan pie crust
  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • 3/4 cup coconut milk
  • 3/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 2 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, coconut milk, maple syrup, cornstarch, pumpkin pie spice, vanilla extract, and salt until smooth.
  3. Pour the filling into the pre-made vegan pie crust and spread it evenly.
  4. Bake for 60 minutes or until the filling is set and slightly firm to the touch.
  5. Allow the pie to cool completely before refrigerating for at least 2 hours. Serve with coconut whipped cream for an extra treat!

Why This Recipe Works

This vegan pumpkin pie captures the essence of traditional flavors while being completely plant-based. The coconut milk adds creaminess, while the maple syrup provides natural sweetness. It’s a delightful way to celebrate the season, and your guests won’t even realize it’s vegan!

2. Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. In a food processor, combine the avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, almond milk, vanilla extract, and salt.
  2. Blend until the mixture is completely smooth and creamy.
  3. Taste and adjust sweetness if necessary by adding more maple syrup.
  4. Spoon the mousse into individual serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Why This Recipe Works

This rich and creamy chocolate mousse is a decadent dessert that feels indulgent without being heavy. The avocados provide a silky texture, while the cocoa powder delivers a rich chocolate flavor. This recipe is not only easy to make, but it also packs in healthy fats, making it a guilt-free treat!

3. Vegan Apple Crisp

Ingredients

  • 6 cups sliced apples (such as Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt. Spread the mixture evenly in a greased baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, combine the rolled oats, almond flour, brown sugar, melted coconut oil, and a pinch of salt. Mix until crumbly.
  4. Sprinkle the oat mixture over the apples, covering them evenly.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the topping is golden brown and the apples are tender.
  6. Serve warm, optionally with a scoop of dairy-free ice cream.

Why This Recipe Works

This vegan apple crisp is a warm, comforting dessert that evokes the spirit of fall. The combination of apples and spices makes for a fragrant and flavorful dish, while the oat topping provides a delightful crunch. It’s a perfect way to highlight seasonal produce and can easily be made ahead of time.

4. Vegan Pecan Pie Bars

Ingredients

  • For the crust:

    • 1 cup almond flour
    • 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
    • 2 tbsp maple syrup
    • 1/4 tsp salt

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup pecans, chopped
    • 1 cup maple syrup
    • 1/4 cup coconut cream
    • 2 tbsp cornstarch
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking dish with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the almond flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and salt until a dough forms. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  3. Bake the crust for 10-12 minutes, until lightly golden.
  4. In another bowl, combine the chopped pecans, maple syrup, coconut cream, cornstarch, vanilla extract, and salt. Stir until well mixed.
  5. Pour the pecan mixture over the crust and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set.
  7. Allow to cool before cutting into bars.

Why This Recipe Works

These vegan pecan pie bars capture the rich, nutty flavor of traditional pecan pie, but in a more portable form. The almond flour crust adds a nice texture, while the filling is sweet and satisfying. They are perfect for serving at a Thanksgiving gathering, and they pack well for leftovers.

5. Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup almond milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine the almond flour, rolled oats, coconut sugar, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix together the melted coconut oil, almond milk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Scoop tablespoon-sized amounts of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, until the edges are golden. Allow to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Why This Recipe Works

These vegan chocolate chip cookies are soft, chewy, and utterly delicious. They are a crowd-pleaser that everyone can enjoy. The use of almond flour and rolled oats gives them a unique texture and flavor while keeping them entirely plant-based.

6. Vegan Cranberry Orange Cake

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries (or frozen)
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1/4 cup fresh orange juice
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch round cake pan.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the almond milk, melted coconut oil, orange juice, and orange zest.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, mixing until just incorporated. Gently fold in the cranberries.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Allow to cool before serving. This cake can be topped with a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and orange juice.

Why This Recipe Works

This cranberry orange cake is a stunning centerpiece for your Thanksgiving dessert table. The tartness of the cranberries balances perfectly with the sweetness of the orange, creating a refreshing and vibrant cake. It’s a wonderful way to incorporate seasonal flavors into your celebration.

Conclusion

Thanksgiving is all about sharing and enjoying delicious food with loved ones. These vegan dessert recipes offer a variety of flavors and textures that are sure to please everyone at your table. Whether you choose to make the classic pumpkin pie, the rich chocolate mousse, or the refreshing cranberry orange cake, you can feel good knowing that these desserts are not only delicious but also completely plant-based. So, this Thanksgiving, embrace the spirit of gratitude and enjoy these delightful vegan treats that everyone can savor together!
