Taste of Thailand: Authentic Pad Thai with Shrimp

If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’ve probably indulged in the rich and vibrant flavors of Pad Thai. This iconic dish is a symphony of sweet, sour, and savory notes, all coming together in perfect harmony. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of creating an authentic Pad Thai with succulent shrimp, transporting your taste buds to the bustling streets of Thailand. Ingredients: 8 oz of rice noodles 1/2 lb of fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 shallot, finely chopped 2 eggs 1/4 cup of firm tofu, cubed 1/4 cup of dried shrimp 1 cup of bean sprouts 2 tbsp of crushed roasted peanuts 3 tbsp of fish sauce 3 tbsp of tamarind paste 2 tbsp of palm sugar 2 tbsp of vegetable oil 1/4 tsp of chili flakes Lime wedges for serving Chopped cilantro for garnish Instructions: Step 1: Prepare the Noodles Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes until they are soft but still slightly firm. Drain and set aside. Step 2: Create the Sauce In a small bowl, mix the fish sauce, tamarind paste, and palm sugar together until the sugar dissolves. Set the sauce aside. Step 3: Cook the Shrimp Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp from the pan and set them aside. Step 4: Saute the Aromatics and Tofu In the same wok, add another tablespoon of oil. Toss in the minced garlic and chopped shallot, and sauté until fragrant. Add the cubed tofu and dried shrimp, stirring gently until the tofu begins to turn golden. Step 5: Scramble the Eggs Push the aromatics and tofu to one side of the wok and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are just set, then mix them with the rest of the ingredients in the wok. Step 6: Stir-Fry the Noodles Add the softened rice noodles to the wok, using tongs to toss and coat the noodles with the other ingredients. Pour the prepared sauce over the noodles, and continue to stir-fry until everything is well combined. Step 7: Add the Final Touches Toss in the bean sprouts and cooked shrimp, stirring for another minute or so until the bean sprouts just begin to wilt. Sprinkle the chili flakes and half of the crushed peanuts over the Pad Thai, giving it a final toss to distribute the ingredients evenly. Step 8: Serve and Garnish Transfer the Pad Thai to serving plates, and sprinkle the remaining crushed peanuts on top. Garnish with a wedge of lime and a sprinkle of chopped cilantro for a burst of freshness. The Taste of Thailand on Your Plate This Authentic Pad Thai with Shrimp recipe captures the essence of Thai street food, bringing the vibrant flavors and textures into your home kitchen. The marriage of sweet, tangy, and umami flavors combined with the toothsome rice noodles and succulent shrimp creates a culinary experience that’s truly unforgettable. ...
