Red Velvet Cupcakes with Cream Cheese Frosting: Valentine's Day Must-Have


Does the thought of savoring velvety rich red velvet cupcakes with a luscious dollop of cream cheese frosting on Valentine’s Day get you excited? I’ll share my tried-and-true recipe to make these heartwarming treats that are perfect for your celebration. This recipe is not only a delight to the taste buds but also contains a delightful secret ingredient that sets these cupcakes apart. So grab your apron and get ready to indulge in a scrumptious appetizer for your Valentine’s Day feast.

Ingredients:

  1. 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 2 teaspoons baking powder
  3. 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  4. 1/4 teaspoon salt
  5. 1/2 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
  6. 1 cup granulated sugar
  7. 2 large eggs
  8. 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  9. 2 tablespoons red food coloring
  10. 1/2 cup buttermilk
  11. 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  12. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  13. Cream Cheese Frosting:
  14. 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  15. 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  16. 2 cups powdered sugar
  17. 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit and line a muffin tin with 12 cupcake liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

  3. In another medium-sized bowl, cream together the softened unsalted butter and granulated sugar using a hand mixer until light and fluffy.

  4. Beat in two large eggs, one at a time, ensuring each egg is well incorporated before adding the next.

  5. Add cocoa powder and red food coloring to the creamed butter mixture, blending it evenly to result in a deep, luscious shade of red.

  6. Drop in the vanilla extract and mix until combined.

  7. Gradually alternate adding the flour mixture and buttermilk to the batter, starting and ending with the flour. Stir the batter until it is smooth with a thin consistency. Be careful not to overmix.

  8. In a separate,small bowl, combine the white vinegar and buttermilk. Add this mixture to the red velvet batter and give it a quick stir to blend.

  9. Divide the red velvet batter evenly among the 12 cupcake liners. Press a chunk of white vinegar-drenched paper towel to the bottom of each liner. This secret ingredient offers a touch of tanginess to the cupcakes, mimicking the taste of fresh buttermilk to a certain extent.

  10. Bake at 350 degrees Fahrenheit for 18 to 20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Allow the cupcakes to cool completely on a wire rack before frosting.

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  1. Beat together softened cream cheese and unsalted butter until creamy in a large bowl. You can use a hand mixer or an electric stand mixer for this task.

  2. Gradually sift in the powdered sugar while continuing to beat the mixture until smooth.

  3. Stir in the vanilla extract, and you are all set to add this delightful frosting to your red velvet cupcakes.

  4. Simply pipe or spread the cream cheese frosting evenly over the cooled cupcakes, and voila! They make a wonderful Valentine’s Day taste temptation!

As you prepare to indulge in these scrumptious red velvet cupcakes, remember they are also an expression of our love and affection towards those we hold dear. Share this delightful recipe with your friends and family, whether you choose to celebrate Valentine’s Day with a lively party or a cozy night in. No matter who you share them with, your delectable creation will bring smiles and a taste of pure joy on Valentine’s Day and beyond. Happy baking!
