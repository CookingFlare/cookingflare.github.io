Sweet and Savory Breakfast Muffin Recipes for Kids

Are you tired of the same old breakfast routine for your kids? Do you want to add some excitement and variety to their morning meals? Look no further than these delicious and easy-to-make sweet and savory breakfast muffin recipes. Not only are they packed with flavor, but they are also a great way to sneak in some extra nutrients. Let’s dive right in! 1. Blueberry Banana Breakfast Muffins Ingredients: 2 ripe bananas, mashed 1 cup blueberries 1/4 cup honey 1/4 cup melted butter 1/2 cup milk 2 cups flour 1/2 cup oats 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 1/2 teaspoon salt Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners. In a large bowl, combine the mashed bananas, blueberries, honey, melted butter, and milk. Mix well. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, oats, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about three-quarters full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. 2. Ham and Cheese Breakfast Muffins Ingredients: 1 cup diced ham 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1/4 cup chopped green onions 2 cups flour 1/4 cup sugar 1 tablespoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1 cup milk 1/4 cup melted butter 2 eggs Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners. In a large bowl, combine the diced ham, shredded cheddar cheese, and chopped green onions. Mix well. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, melted butter, and eggs until well combined. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Fold in the ham, cheese, and green onion mixture. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about three-quarters full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. 3. Apple Cinnamon Breakfast Muffins Ingredients: 2 cups diced apples 1/2 cup sugar 1/4 cup melted butter 1/4 cup milk 2 cups flour 1 teaspoon baking powder 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon salt Instructions: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners. In a large bowl, combine the diced apples, sugar, melted butter, and milk. Mix well. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about three-quarters full. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. These sweet and savory breakfast muffins are sure to become a hit with your kids. They are perfect for busy mornings or as a grab-and-go snack. Feel free to experiment with different flavors and ingredients to suit your family’s preferences.
