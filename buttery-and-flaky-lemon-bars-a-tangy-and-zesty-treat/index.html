Buttery and Flaky Lemon Bars: A Tangy and Zesty Treat


There’s something undeniably refreshing and delightful about the combination of tangy lemon and buttery, flaky crust. Lemon bars are a classic dessert that never fails to brighten up any occasion, from afternoon tea to a summer picnic. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create the perfect balance of sweet and tart in every bite of these irresistible lemon bars.

Ingredients:

For the Crust:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • A pinch of salt

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2/3 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal later.
  2. In a mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Gradually add the flour and salt to the butter mixture, mixing until a crumbly dough forms.
  4. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is lightly golden.

Step 2: Making the Lemon Filling

  1. While the crust is baking, prepare the lemon filling. In a separate bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar and flour.
  2. Add the eggs, lemon juice, and lemon zest to the sugar mixture, and whisk until smooth and well combined.
  3. Once the crust is done baking, reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (160°C).
  4. Pour the lemon filling over the hot crust and return the pan to the oven. Bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set and the edges are lightly golden.

Step 3: Cooling and Serving

  1. Allow the lemon bars to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Once cooled, refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow the filling to set.
  2. Once chilled, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the pan and place them on a cutting board.
  3. Dust the top of the bars with powdered sugar and cut into squares using a sharp knife.
  4. Serve the lemon bars chilled or at room temperature, and enjoy the tangy, zesty flavors!

Tips for Success:

  • For an extra burst of citrus flavor, consider adding a few drops of lemon extract to the filling.
  • Ensure that the lemon juice is freshly squeezed for the best and brightest flavor.
  • If you prefer a thicker crust, you can double the crust ingredients and press the dough more heavily into the pan.

Conclusion:

These buttery and flaky lemon bars are a delightful balance of sweet and tangy, making them the perfect treat for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a refreshing dessert, these lemon bars are sure to impress. With their buttery, flaky crust and vibrant lemon filling, they’re a true delight for the senses. Try this recipe for a taste of sunshine in every bite!
