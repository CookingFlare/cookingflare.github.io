How to Make BBQ Chicken Pizza on the Grill


Grilling pizza is an adventure that brings smoky, charred flavors right to your backyard. One of the most irresistible creations you can grill is BBQ chicken pizza: a perfect blend of tangy barbecue sauce, tender chicken, melting cheese, and crisp crust cooked over glowing coals. Imagine biting into slices with just the right balance of sweet, savory, and smoky notes—all made easily on your grill. This detailed guide walks you through the entire process, from preparing the ingredients to the art of grilling pizza dough, ensuring your next BBQ chicken pizza is nothing short of spectacular.

Why Grill Pizza?

Grilling pizza introduces a unique flavor dimension impossible to replicate with indoor ovens alone. The open flame crisps the dough beautifully, creating a slightly charred base with a tender interior. The heat from the grill caramelizes the barbecue sauce and melts the cheese to perfection, while infusing everything with subtle smoky undertones. Plus, grilling is a perfect outdoor cooking activity that pairs well with summer gatherings, game day cookouts, or casual weekend dinners.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Dough

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons (7 g) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water (around 110°F/45°C)
  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the Toppings

  • 2 cups cooked chicken breast, shredded or chopped (grilled chicken preferred for extra smokiness)
  • ½ cup barbecue sauce (choose your favorite sweet or smoky variety)
  • 1 ½ cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup shredded smoked gouda or cheddar cheese (optional for depth of flavor)
  • ¼ cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro or parsley (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing the dough)
  • Optional: sliced jalapeños or bell peppers for extra kick

Preparing the Dough

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5 to 10 minutes until it foams. This indicates your yeast is alive and kicking.

  2. Mix the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together flour and salt, then make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to bring the dough together until it becomes a shaggy ball.

  3. Knead: Transfer dough onto a floured surface. Knead for 8 to 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If it’s sticky, sprinkle in a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much as that can make the dough tough.

  4. Let It Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it doubles in size.

Preparing the Chicken and Toppings

While the dough rises, prep your chicken and toppings:

  • If the chicken isn’t cooked, grill or pan-sear chicken breasts seasoned with salt and pepper. Let it rest, then shred or dice.
  • Toss the shredded chicken in about half a cup of your chosen barbecue sauce to coat evenly.
  • Slice red onions thinly and keep other toppings at the ready.
  • Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (around 450°F/232°C). If using charcoal, allow coals to burn down until they’re glowing red with a thin layer of ash.

Rolling Out the Dough and Prepping for the Grill

  1. Divide and Roll: Punch down the risen dough to release air bubbles. For a medium-sized pizza, divide the dough into two equal pieces to manage easier grilling. Lightly dust your surface and rolling pin with flour and roll each dough ball into a 12-inch circle about ¼ inch thick.

  2. Prepare for the Grill: Brush one side of the rolled dough lightly with olive oil. This prevents sticking and helps create a crispy crust.

Grilling the Pizza Crust

  1. Grill Dough, Oiled Side Down: Place the dough on the hot grill with the oiled side touching the grates. Close the lid and cook for 2 to 3 minutes until grill marks appear and the dough firms up.

  2. Brush Top Side with Olive Oil: While the bottom cooks, brush the uncooked side with olive oil to prevent dryness.

  3. Flip the Dough: Using tongs or a spatula, carefully flip the dough over to the uncooked side.

Adding Toppings and Final Cooking

  1. Build Your BBQ Chicken Pizza: Immediately after flipping, quickly spread a thin layer of barbecue sauce over the cooked side. Add shredded chicken evenly.

  2. Add Cheese and Onions: Sprinkle mozzarella generously, then add a mix of smoked gouda or cheddar if desired. Scatter thinly sliced red onions on top.

  3. Close the Lid: Close the grill cover to allow cheese to melt, and toppings to heat, which usually takes about 4 to 6 minutes. Keep an eye on the pizza to prevent burning.

  4. Check for Doneness: The crust should be golden with slight char, cheese should be bubbly and melty.

Serving and Garnishing

  • Remove the pizza carefully from the grill using a large spatula or pizza peel.
  • Let the pizza cool for a couple of minutes; this makes slicing easier.
  • Sprinkle chopped cilantro or parsley atop for a fresh, herbal touch.
  • Optionally add sliced jalapeños for those who appreciate some heat or scatter thinly sliced scallions to brighten flavors further.

Tips for Perfect BBQ Chicken Pizza on the Grill

  • Temperature Control: The secret to successful grilled pizza is managing heat. If the grill is too hot, the crust will burn before toppings cook; too low, and the crust becomes tough. Aim for a medium-high steady temperature.
  • Use a Pizza Stone or Cast Iron: If you want more even cooking and easier handling, try grilling on a preheated pizza stone or cast iron pan.
  • Prep Everything Before You Fire Up: Grilling pizza requires quick choreography; have all your toppings, utensils, and plates ready before the dough hits the grill.
  • Experiment with Sauce: While classic barbecue sauce works beautifully, mixing in a splash of hot sauce or honey can add unique twists.
  • Try Different Cheeses: Combining mozzarella with smoked cheeses or even blue cheese crumbles adds complexity to every bite.

Variations to Try Next Time

  • Spicy BBQ Chicken Pizza: Add chopped jalapeños, smoked paprika in the sauce, and a drizzle of hot honey to balance spice and sweetness.
  • White BBQ Chicken Pizza: Use a white garlic sauce or ranch base instead of traditional red BBQ sauce, topped with grilled chicken and a garlic herb cheese blend.
  • Vegetarian BBQ Pizza: Replace chicken with grilled portobello mushrooms, zucchini, or caramelized onions for a smoky vegetarian option.
  • Buffalo BBQ Chicken Pizza: Mix BBQ sauce with buffalo sauce for a tangy heat punch and top with blue cheese crumbles.

Conclusion

Grilled BBQ chicken pizza is a delightful fusion of smoky grill flavors with the comforting goodness of pizza. From preparing homemade dough to mastering toppings and heat control, this recipe takes your outdoor cooking to the next level. Whether for a casual weeknight meal or a lively weekend gathering, nothing beats the aroma and taste of pizza made on an open flame. Grab your grill, fire it up, and enjoy crafting a memorable meal that’s sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Feel free to experiment and personalize this recipe to suit your tastes—grilled pizza is all about creativity and good company. Enjoy every smoky, cheesy bite!
