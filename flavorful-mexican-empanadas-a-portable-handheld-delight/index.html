Flavorful Mexican Empanadas: A Portable Handheld Delight


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of empanadas. These delicious hand pies are a portable delight that can be filled with a variety of flavorful ingredients. Whether you’re hosting a party, preparing a quick meal, or looking for a tasty snack, Mexican empanadas are a fantastic choice. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of making flavorful Mexican empanadas from scratch.

Ingredients

To make these tasty empanadas, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water
  • 1 lb ground beef or chicken
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the cold, cubed butter and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Form the dough into a ball, cover it with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  3. In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and jalapeno pepper. Cook until the onion is translucent and the peppers are soft.

  4. Add the ground beef or chicken to the skillet and cook until it’s browned and no longer pink. Drain any excess fat.

  5. Stir in the ground cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the spices are evenly distributed.

  6. Remove the dough from the refrigerator and divide it into small portions. Roll each portion into a circle, about 5 inches in diameter.

  7. Place a spoonful of the meat mixture onto one half of the dough circle. Sprinkle some shredded cheddar cheese on top of the filling.

  8. Fold the other half of the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Press the edges together to seal the empanada.

  9. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or pot for frying. Carefully place the empanadas into the hot oil and fry until golden brown on both sides.

  10. Remove the empanadas from the oil and drain them on a paper towel-lined plate to remove excess oil.

  11. Serve the empanadas hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as salsa or guacamole.

Conclusion

Flavorful Mexican empanadas are a versatile and delicious handheld delight that can be enjoyed on various occasions. With a flaky crust and a savory filling, these portable treats are sure to wow your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a party or simply craving a satisfying snack, give these homemade empanadas a try. The combination of spices, meat, and cheese will transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the flavors of Mexico with these tasty empanadas.
