Tasty and Colorful Rice and Beans Enchiladas


Who doesn’t love a delicious and colorful plate of enchiladas? This recipe for Rice and Beans Enchiladas will tantalize your taste buds with its vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients. Whether you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine or simply looking for a new and exciting dish to try, these enchiladas are sure to become a staple in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

For the Enchiladas:

  • 1 cup of cooked rice
  • 1 can of black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • 1 ½ cups of enchilada sauce
  • 1 ½ cups of shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)

For the Garnish:

  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Avocado slices
  • Sour cream
  • Sliced jalapeños (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked rice, black beans, diced bell peppers, red onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. Warm the corn tortillas in the microwave for about 30 seconds to make them pliable. Spoon a generous portion of the rice and bean mixture onto each tortilla, roll it up, and place it seam-side down in the prepared baking dish. Repeat this process for the remaining tortillas.

  4. Once all the enchiladas are assembled, pour the enchilada sauce over the top, making sure to coat each enchilada evenly. Sprinkle the shredded cheese over the saucy enchiladas.

  5. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  6. Remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped cilantro, avocado slices, sour cream, and sliced jalapeños if you like a bit of heat.

  7. Serve the enchiladas warm and enjoy the burst of flavors in every mouthful!

Why You’ll Love These Enchiladas

These Rice and Beans Enchiladas are a delightful fusion of Mexican and Tex-Mex flavors. The combination of tender rice, hearty black beans, and colorful bell peppers creates a satisfying filling that’s both nutritious and delicious. The warm spices like cumin and chili powder add a depth of flavor that will excite your taste buds.

Moreover, this recipe is incredibly versatile. You can customize the filling by adding other vegetables like corn or zucchini, or even incorporating shredded chicken or tofu for a protein boost. The options are endless, allowing you to tailor the enchiladas to your personal preferences and dietary needs.

Not only are these enchiladas a feast for the senses, but they are also a fantastic make-ahead meal. Prepare a batch over the weekend and enjoy the convenience of ready-to-bake enchiladas during busy weekdays. They also freeze well, making them an excellent option for meal prepping.

In conclusion, these Tasty and Colorful Rice and Beans Enchiladas are a celebration of vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply treating yourself to a flavorful dinner, these enchiladas are guaranteed to bring joy to your table. Try this recipe today and embark on a culinary journey filled with color, taste, and satisfaction.
