Flavorful and Satisfying Mushroom and Spinach Stuffed Shells


If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying vegetarian dish that’s packed with flavor, then look no further! These mushroom and spinach stuffed shells are sure to be a hit at your next dinner party or family gathering. Not only are they easy to make, but they also provide a wholesome and hearty option for those who prefer a meatless meal. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 1 box of jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups of ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of marinara sauce

Instructions:

  1. Cook the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions. Make sure to cook them al dente, as they will continue to cook in the oven later. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.

  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook until they have released their moisture and are lightly browned. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

  4. Stir in the chopped spinach and cook until wilted. Season with dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.

  5. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella cheese, and grated Parmesan cheese. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  6. Add the cooked mushroom and spinach mixture to the cheese mixture and stir until well combined.

  7. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  8. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.

  9. Stuff each cooked jumbo pasta shell with a generous spoonful of the mushroom and spinach filling. Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish, arranging them in a single layer.

  10. Pour the remaining marinara sauce over the stuffed shells, covering them completely.

  11. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the sauce is bubbly and the cheese is melted.

  12. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 5 minutes to allow the cheese to brown slightly.

  13. Remove from the oven and let the stuffed shells cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving Suggestions:

These mushroom and spinach stuffed shells can be served as a main dish on their own, accompanied by a fresh salad or garlic bread. The flavors of the mushrooms, spinach, and cheese blend together perfectly, creating a dish that is both creamy and savory.

Conclusion:

Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to incorporate more meatless meals into your diet, these flavorful and satisfying mushroom and spinach stuffed shells are sure to please. The combination of earthy mushrooms, vibrant spinach, and creamy cheese makes this dish a winner for any occasion. So, gather your ingredients, follow the simple steps, and enjoy a delicious and wholesome meal that will leave you and your loved ones asking for seconds!
