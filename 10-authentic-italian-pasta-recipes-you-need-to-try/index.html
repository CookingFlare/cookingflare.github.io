10 Authentic Italian Pasta Recipes You Need to Try


Italian cuisine is known for its rich flavors, fresh ingredients, and incredible variety. One of the stars of Italian cooking is pasta. Whether it’s a simple spaghetti dish or a more intricate stuffed pasta, the world of pasta is as diverse as it is delicious. If you’re a pasta lover or someone who wants to explore the deep tradition of Italian cooking, here are 10 authentic Italian pasta recipes that you absolutely need to try. These recipes range from classics to lesser-known gems, all bursting with flavor and tradition.

1. Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Spaghetti alla Carbonara is one of the most beloved pasta dishes in Italy, and for good reason. This simple yet rich dish combines just a few ingredients, but when done right, the results are nothing short of spectacular. The main ingredients are spaghetti, guanciale (cured pork cheek), eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and black pepper.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g guanciale (or pancetta)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 100g Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, dice the guanciale and cook it in a pan over medium heat until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve a cup of pasta water and drain the rest. Quickly toss the hot pasta with the guanciale and some of the reserved pasta water.
  5. Remove from heat and slowly add the egg mixture, tossing continuously to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Serve immediately, topped with extra Pecorino Romano and black pepper.

2. Pesto Genovese with Trofie

Pesto Genovese is a classic Italian sauce from the region of Liguria. Made from fresh basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmesan cheese, and extra virgin olive oil, pesto is perfect for pairing with pasta. Trofie, a traditional Ligurian pasta, is twisted and holds the pesto sauce beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 400g trofie pasta
  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1/3 cup pine nuts
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the trofie pasta in salted water until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, prepare the pesto by blending the basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmesan, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a food processor.
  3. Once the pasta is cooked, reserve a bit of pasta water and drain the rest.
  4. Toss the trofie with the pesto sauce, adding a little pasta water to help the sauce coat the pasta.
  5. Serve immediately with a sprinkle of Parmesan.

3. Lasagna alla Bolognese

Lasagna is a quintessential Italian dish, and the Bolognese variation, or Lasagna alla Bolognese, is a staple. This version features layers of fresh pasta, a rich Bolognese sauce made from beef, pork, tomatoes, and béchamel sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 12 sheets of lasagna noodles
  • 500g ground beef
  • 250g ground pork
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Prepare the Bolognese sauce by browning the ground beef and pork in a large pan. Add the onion, carrot, celery, and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes and red wine, simmering for 30 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. For the béchamel sauce, melt the butter in a pan, then whisk in the flour. Gradually add the milk, stirring constantly until the sauce thickens.
  5. In a baking dish, layer the lasagna noodles, Bolognese sauce, béchamel, and grated Parmesan. Repeat the layers and top with cheese.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes, then uncover and bake for an additional 15 minutes until golden and bubbly.

4. Cacio e Pepe

Cacio e Pepe is a Roman classic that is incredibly simple but packed with flavor. The name translates to “cheese and pepper,” which is exactly what this dish is all about. Made with Pecorino Romano cheese and freshly cracked black pepper, it’s a dish that showcases the beauty of minimalism.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly cracked black pepper
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted water until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, toast the black pepper in a dry pan to release its aroma.
  3. Once the pasta is ready, reserve some pasta water and drain the rest.
  4. Toss the hot pasta with the toasted pepper and grated Pecorino Romano, adding reserved pasta water to help create a creamy sauce.
  5. Serve immediately with extra cheese and pepper.

5. Fettuccine Alfredo

Although widely known in the United States, Fettuccine Alfredo is actually an Italian classic, especially in Rome. It’s known for its rich, creamy sauce made from butter, cream, and Parmesan cheese. The creamy sauce perfectly coats the broad ribbons of fettuccine.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fettuccine
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the fettuccine in salted water until al dente.
  2. In a separate pan, melt the butter and cream over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  3. Once the sauce thickens slightly, stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until it melts and the sauce becomes smooth.
  4. Toss the pasta in the sauce, adding a little reserved pasta water to achieve the desired consistency.
  5. Season with salt and pepper and serve hot.

6. Pappardelle al Ragù di Cinghiale (Wild Boar Ragù)

A dish for those who want to experience traditional Tuscan flavors, Pappardelle al Ragù di Cinghiale is a rich and savory dish made with wild boar meat. The slow-cooked ragù is robust, and the wide pappardelle pasta provides the perfect base to soak up all the flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 400g pappardelle
  • 500g wild boar meat, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef stock
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Brown the wild boar in olive oil until seared on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, sauté the onion, carrot, celery, and garlic until softened.
  3. Add the wild boar back into the pan and deglaze with red wine. Add the crushed tomatoes and beef stock, then simmer for 2-3 hours until the ragù is rich and tender.
  4. Cook the pappardelle in salted water until al dente and toss it with the ragù.
  5. Serve with freshly grated Parmesan.

7. Orecchiette with Broccoli Rabe and Sausage

This dish is a true representation of the southern Italian region of Puglia. Orecchiette, a small, ear-shaped pasta, pairs beautifully with the bitterness of broccoli rabe and the savory flavors of sausage. It’s simple yet delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 400g orecchiette
  • 200g Italian sausage, crumbled
  • 2 cups broccoli rabe, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the orecchiette in salted water until al dente. During the last 3 minutes of cooking, add the broccoli rabe to the pot.
  2. Meanwhile, cook the sausage in a pan with olive oil and garlic until browned.
  3. Once the pasta and broccoli rabe are done, drain them and toss them with the sausage mixture.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and serve immediately.

8. Ravioli al Burro e Salvia (Ravioli with Butter and Sage)

A classic from the Emilia-Romagna region, ravioli al burro e salvia is a simple yet refined dish. The homemade ravioli is filled with ricotta and spinach, and the butter and sage sauce elevates the flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 400g fresh ravioli (filled with ricotta and spinach)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • Fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the ravioli in salted water until they float to the surface, indicating they are done.
  2. While the ravioli cooks, melt the butter in a pan and add the sage leaves. Cook until the butter becomes golden and aromatic.
  3. Drain the ravioli and toss them gently in the butter and sage sauce.
  4. Serve immediately, garnished with extra sage and Parmesan.

9. Bucatini all’Amatriciana

Bucatini all’Amatriciana is a traditional pasta dish from the town of Amatrice in Lazio. It features bucatini, a thick pasta with a hole running through the center, and is paired with a sauce made from guanciale, tomatoes, and Pecorino Romano cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 400g bucatini
  • 150g guanciale, sliced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the bucatini in salted water until al dente.
  2. Meanwhile, cook the guanciale in a pan until it becomes crispy.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes and simmer for 20 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Toss the pasta with the sauce and top with grated Pecorino Romano.

10. Gnocchi alla Sorrentina

Gnocchi alla Sorrentina is a comforting dish from the region of Campania. Soft potato gnocchi are baked in a tomato sauce with mozzarella and Parmesan, creating a cheesy, flavorful masterpiece.

Ingredients:

  • 500g gnocchi
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 cup mozzarella, shredded
  • 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil

Instructions:

  1. Cook the gnocchi in salted water until they float to the surface.
  2. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  3. In a baking dish, layer the gnocchi with marinara sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan.
  4. Bake for 20 minutes, or until bubbly and golden.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

These 10 authentic Italian pasta recipes represent the essence of Italy’s culinary traditions. From creamy sauces to hearty ragùs, each dish offers something unique and unforgettable. Whether you’re in the mood for something quick or a slow-cooked masterpiece, these recipes will bring the flavors of Italy right into your kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a taste of Italy in every bite. Buon appetito!
