Authentic Mexican Birria Tacos: A Flavorful Meat Fiesta

4 min · 820 words

If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine, you’ve probably heard of birria tacos. These mouthwatering delights are a popular street food in Mexico, known for their rich and savory flavors. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make authentic Mexican birria tacos that will transport you straight to the bustling streets of Mexico.

Ingredients

For the Birria:

  • 2 pounds of beef (such as chuck roast or beef shanks)
  • 4 dried guajillo chilies
  • 2 dried ancho chilies
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 4 cups of beef broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tacos:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Diced onions
  • Lime wedges
  • Sliced radishes

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the dried chilies. Remove the stems and seeds, then toast the chilies in a dry skillet over medium heat for a few seconds on each side. This will help enhance their flavors.

  2. In a bowl, cover the toasted chilies with hot water and let them soak for about 15 minutes until they soften.

  3. While the chilies are soaking, season the beef with salt and pepper. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Brown the beef on all sides until it develops a nice crust. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  4. In the same pot, add the quartered onion and garlic cloves. Sauté them until they become fragrant and slightly caramelized.

  5. Drain the soaked chilies and add them to a blender along with the sautéed onion and garlic. Blend until you have a smooth paste.

  6. Return the beef to the pot and pour the chili paste over it. Add the dried oregano, ground cumin, ground cloves, and beef broth. Give everything a good stir to combine.

  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the birria simmer for about 3-4 hours until the beef becomes tender and easily shreds.

  8. Once the beef is cooked, remove it from the pot and shred it using two forks. Set the shredded beef aside.

  9. Strain the remaining liquid from the pot and discard any solids. This flavorful liquid will be used for dipping the tacos later.

  10. To assemble the tacos, warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or on a griddle until they become pliable. Fill each tortilla with a generous amount of the shredded beef.

  11. Heat a separate skillet over medium-high heat and place the filled tortillas onto the skillet. Cook each side for about a minute until the tortillas become slightly crispy.

  12. Serve the birria tacos hot with a side of the flavorful birria dipping liquid. Garnish with fresh cilantro, diced onions, lime wedges, and sliced radishes.

Conclusion

Authentic Mexican birria tacos are a true fiesta for your taste buds. The rich and savory flavors of the tender beef combined with the aromatic spices and the freshness of the garnishes create a culinary experience like no other. Whether you’re hosting a Mexican-themed party or simply craving a delicious meal, these birria tacos are sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, follow this recipe, and embark on a flavorful journey to Mexico without leaving your kitchen. ¡Buen provecho!
