Grilled Calzones: Italian Flavors on the BBQ


There’s something undeniably satisfying about biting into a crispy, golden brown calzone, oozing with gooey cheese and savory Italian flavors. Now, imagine taking that experience to the next level by grilling your calzones to perfection on the BBQ. The result? A delightful fusion of smoky, charred goodness with the classic Italian comfort food. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the art of creating grilled calzones, exploring various fillings, dough options, and grilling techniques to elevate your outdoor cooking game.

The Dough: Homemade vs. Store-Bought

The foundation of any great calzone lies in the dough. While you can certainly opt for store-bought pizza dough for convenience, there’s something special about making your own. The process of kneading, proofing, and shaping the dough adds a personal touch to your culinary creation. If you’re feeling adventurous, try experimenting with whole wheat or herbed dough for an extra flavor boost.

Recipe: Homemade Pizza Dough

  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 3 1/2 - 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 1/2 cups of flour, salt, and sugar.
  3. Create a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  4. Stir the ingredients until a soft dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, gradually add more flour.
  5. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until it’s smooth and elastic.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until doubled in size.

The Fillings: A Symphony of Flavors

Now, let’s talk about the filling. Traditional calzone fillings often feature a combination of mozzarella cheese, ricotta, and Italian meats such as pepperoni or salami. However, the beauty of grilled calzones lies in the endless possibilities for customization. From fresh vegetables to gourmet cheeses, the choice is yours to create a flavor symphony that suits your palate.

Recipe: Classic Calzone Filling

  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced pepperoni
  • 1/4 cup marinara sauce
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. In a bowl, mix together the mozzarella, ricotta, Parmesan, pepperoni, marinara sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper.

Feel free to experiment with different combinations such as spinach and feta, grilled vegetables with goat cheese, or even a dessert calzone with Nutella and bananas.

Grilling Techniques: Achieving the Perfect Char

Grilling calzones requires a delicate balance of heat and timing. Preparing your grill for indirect heat is essential to prevent burning the delicate dough. Aim for a medium-high temperature and oil the grates to avoid sticking. Place the assembled calzones on the grill and cook them for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they develop a beautiful, golden crust.

Serving and Enjoying

Once your grilled calzones are golden brown and the cheese is bubbling, carefully remove them from the grill and let them cool for a few minutes. Slice into wedges and serve with a side of warm marinara sauce for dipping. The combination of the smoky grilled exterior and the melty, cheesy interior is sure to delight your taste buds and those of your fortunate dining companions.

Conclusion

Grilled calzones offer a delightful twist on the classic Italian dish, infusing it with the smoky, charred flavor that only a BBQ can provide. From crafting the perfect dough to experimenting with diverse fillings, the process of creating and grilling calzones is a true culinary adventure that promises a delicious reward. So, fire up the grill, gather your favorite ingredients, and embark on a journey of flavor as you savor the joy of grilled calzones. Buon appetito!
