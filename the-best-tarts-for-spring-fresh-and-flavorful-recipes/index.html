The Best Tarts for Spring: Fresh and Flavorful Recipes


Spring is the season of renewal, and it brings with it an incredible bounty of fresh fruits, vegetables, and herbs. With the chill of winter finally behind us, it’s time to embrace the vibrant colors, delicate flavors, and aromas that define this rejuvenating time of year. Among the many ways to celebrate spring’s culinary harvest, tarts are the perfect choice. They offer versatility, sophistication, and the chance to showcase seasonal ingredients in a deliciously beautiful format.

Below, you will find a collection of tart recipes that are perfect for spring. Each recipe combines fresh, flavorful ingredients with simple techniques to create dishes that are both elegant and approachable. Whether you’re hosting a gathering, enjoying a quiet brunch, or simply indulging in seasonal flavors, these tarts will elevate any occasion.

Lemon Lavender Tart

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (cold and cubed)
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • 1 egg yolk
  • ¼ cup cold water
  • 4 large eggs
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup fresh lemon juice
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • ¾ cup heavy cream
  • 1 tsp dried lavender (culinary grade)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the crust. Combine the flour, powdered sugar, and butter in a food processor. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add the egg yolk and water, and pulse until the dough comes together. Wrap in plastic wrap and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface and carefully press it into a tart pan. Trim the edges and prick the bottom with a fork to prevent puffing. Bake for 15 minutes, then cool completely.
  3. For the filling, whisk together the eggs, sugar, lemon juice, and zest in a mixing bowl. Add ¾ cup heavy cream and dried lavender, ensuring everything is well combined. Pour the mixture into the cooled tart shell.
  4. Bake at 325°F (165°C) for 30-35 minutes, or until the filling is set but still slightly jiggly in the center. Cool to room temperature before serving. For an elegant touch, garnish with edible flowers or fresh mint leaves.

The lemon-lavender combination offers a delicate balance of citrus brightness and floral sweetness, making this tart a quintessential ode to spring.

Strawberry Basil Tart

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made puff pastry sheet
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ¼ cup powdered sugar
  • ½ tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 lb fresh strawberries, sliced
  • 2 tbsp strawberry jam
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Roll out the puff pastry and place it on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Fold the edges to create a small border. Prick the center with a fork to prevent bubbling. Bake at 400°F (205°C) for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown. Cool completely.
  2. In a small bowl, beat the cream cheese, powdered sugar, and vanilla extract until smooth. Spread this mixture over the cooled puff pastry crust.
  3. Arrange the sliced strawberries on top in an overlapping pattern. Warm the strawberry jam slightly and brush it over the strawberries for a glossy finish.
  4. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a fragrant burst of flavor before serving.

The combination of sweet strawberries and aromatic basil creates an unexpected yet delightful pairing, making this tart perfect for warm spring afternoons.

Asparagus and Goat Cheese Tart

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • ½ cup goat cheese, softened
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 bunch fresh asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon zest, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the puff pastry on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Fold the edges to form a border, and prick the center with a fork. Bake for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and cool slightly.
  2. Whisk together the goat cheese, sour cream, olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper. Spread this creamy mixture over the center of the baked puff pastry.
  3. Arrange the asparagus spears in a single layer on top of the cheese mixture. Return the tart to the oven and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender and the edges are golden brown.
  4. Garnish the tart with fresh lemon zest for a bright, tangy finish.

This savory tart is a fantastic way to showcase crisp spring asparagus, and the creamy goat cheese base adds a touch of richness to every bite.

Raspberry Almond Tart

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • ½ tsp almond extract
  • 1 cup fresh raspberries
  • ¼ cup raspberry jam
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Combine the almond flour, sugar, melted butter, egg, and almond extract in a bowl. Mix until a dough forms. Press the dough into a tart pan, ensuring an even layer on the bottom and sides.
  2. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then let it cool slightly.
  3. Spread raspberry jam evenly over the crust, then arrange fresh raspberries on top. Press them gently to ensure they stick.
  4. Bake for another 10 minutes, allowing the raspberries to soften. Cool completely before dusting with powdered sugar and serving.

This gluten-free tart celebrates the bright, tart flavors of fresh raspberries and complements them with the subtle nuttiness of almond flour, making it a crowd-pleaser for spring gatherings.

Peach and Thyme Tart

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 4 fresh peaches, sliced
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • ¼ cup mascarpone cheese
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp turbinado sugar

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the pie crust and press it into a tart pan. Trim the edges and chill for 10 minutes before baking for 15 minutes. Let cool completely.
  2. In a small bowl, mix mascarpone cheese, honey, and lemon juice until smooth. Spread this mixture evenly over the cooled crust.
  3. Arrange the peach slices in concentric circles over the mascarpone mixture. Sprinkle fresh thyme leaves and turbinado sugar on top.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the peaches are tender and slightly caramelized. Cool to room temperature before serving.

The combination of sweet, juicy peaches with earthy thyme creates a unique flavor profile that’s perfect for embracing spring’s warmer days.

Tarts are more than just a dessert or a snack—they’re a celebration of seasonal abundance. These recipes highlight the best that spring has to offer and turn simple ingredients into works of culinary art. Whether you prefer something sweet, savory, or a little bit in between, there’s a tart here to match every palate and occasion.

Celebrate the season with these vibrant recipes and bring the flavors of spring to your kitchen!
