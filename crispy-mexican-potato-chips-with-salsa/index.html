Crispy Mexican Potato Chips with Salsa


When it comes to snacking, few things can compete with the delightful crunch and flavor of crispy potato chips. Add a zesty salsa to the mix, and you’ve got a snack that’s not only satisfying but also bursting with authentic Mexican flavor. In this post, we’ll take you through the steps to create your own crispy Mexican potato chips paired with a vibrant and refreshing salsa. Get ready for a culinary adventure that will elevate your snack game!

Ingredients

For the Crispy Mexican Potato Chips

  • 4 large russet potatoes (or any starchy variety)
  • Oil for frying (vegetable or canola)
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Fresh lime juice (optional, for serving)

For the Salsa

  • 3 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 medium red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced (adjust for heat)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 avocado, diced (optional, for creaminess)

Equipment Needed

  • Mandoline slicer or sharp knife
  • Deep frying pan or Dutch oven
  • Slotted spoon
  • Paper towels
  • Mixing bowl

Preparing the Crispy Mexican Potato Chips

Step 1: Slice the Potatoes

To achieve the perfect crispy texture, start by washing and peeling the russet potatoes. Using a mandoline slicer or a sharp knife, cut the potatoes into thin, even slices – about 1/8 inch thick. Thinner slices will result in crispier chips, so take your time with this step!

Step 2: Soak the Potato Slices

After slicing the potatoes, place them in a large bowl of cold water. Soaking the potato slices for at least 30 minutes helps remove excess starch, which is crucial for achieving that coveted crispiness. Feel free to soak them longer if you have time; some people find that a few hours is ideal.

Step 3: Drain and Dry

Once the soaking time is up, drain the potato slices and lay them out on paper towels. Pat them dry thoroughly to remove as much moisture as possible. This step is essential, as excess moisture can lead to soggy chips.

Step 4: Heat the Oil

In a deep frying pan or Dutch oven, heat about 2 inches of oil over medium heat. You can test if the oil is ready by dropping a small piece of potato into it; if it sizzles right away, you’re good to go. Alternatively, a cooking thermometer should read around 350°F (175°C).

Step 5: Fry the Potato Slices

Carefully add a handful of potato slices to the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them in batches, turning occasionally, until they turn golden brown and crispy, approximately 3-4 minutes per batch. Use a slotted spoon to remove the chips from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.

Step 6: Season the Chips

While the chips are still hot, sprinkle them with sea salt, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, and paprika. Toss them gently to ensure each chip is evenly coated. For an extra zing, squeeze some fresh lime juice over the chips just before serving.

Making the Salsa

Step 1: Combine the Ingredients

In a medium mixing bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, finely chopped red onion, minced jalapeño, and chopped cilantro. Mix well to ensure an even distribution of flavors.

Step 2: Add Lime Juice and Season

Squeeze the juice of one lime over the salsa mixture and season with salt to taste. If you’re adding avocado for creaminess, gently fold it in at this point to avoid mashing it too much.

Step 3: Let It Marinate

For the best flavor, let the salsa sit for at least 20 minutes before serving. This allows the ingredients to meld together beautifully, creating a fresh and zesty dip that pairs perfectly with your crispy potato chips.

Serving Your Crispy Mexican Potato Chips with Salsa

Once your chips are fried and your salsa is ready, it’s time to serve! Arrange your crispy Mexican potato chips on a platter and place the salsa in a bowl at the center. For an added touch, consider garnishing the salsa with a few extra sprigs of cilantro or lime wedges on the side.

Enjoying Your Snack

These crispy chips and zesty salsa make for an excellent appetizer, party snack, or game day treat. The flavor combination is sure to impress friends and family alike, and you’ll love the satisfaction of having made everything from scratch.

Tips for Perfect Crispy Chips

  1. Choose the Right Potatoes: Starchy potatoes like russets yield the best results. Waxy potatoes will not crisp up as well.

  2. Control the Oil Temperature: Maintaining the right oil temperature is crucial for crispy chips. If the oil is too hot, the chips may burn; if too cool, they will absorb more oil and become greasy.

  3. Experiment with Seasonings: Feel free to experiment with different seasonings to suit your taste preferences. Add cayenne pepper for heat, or try a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese for a cheesy twist.

  4. Store Leftover Chips: If you have leftover chips (though they are unlikely to last long!), store them in an airtight container at room temperature. Re-crisp them in the oven if they lose their crunch.

  5. Make It a Meal: These chips and salsa can easily be transformed into a meal. Top the chips with shredded chicken, cheese, and a drizzle of sour cream for a delicious nacho platter!

Conclusion

Creating crispy Mexican potato chips with a fresh salsa is a rewarding culinary experience that brings bold flavors and textures to your table. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply indulging in a snack at home, this recipe guarantees satisfaction. With a bit of preparation and the right techniques, you’ll find yourself reaching for these homemade chips time and time again. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy some of the best chips and salsa you’ve ever tasted!
