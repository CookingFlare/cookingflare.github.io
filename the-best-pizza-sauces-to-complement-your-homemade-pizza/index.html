The Best Pizza Sauces to Complement Your Homemade Pizza


When crafting the perfect homemade pizza, it’s easy to get caught up in choosing toppings, cheese blends, or the thickness of your crust. Yet, one element often underestimated is the true star of any pizza: the sauce. Whether you prefer a classic marinara or crave something unconventional, the sauce provides the foundation for the flavors that follow and can make or break your masterpiece. In this post, we’ll delve deep into some of the best pizza sauces, their characteristics, and how you can create them at home. By the end, you’ll be equipped to transform your pizza-making skills and impress everyone gathered around your dinner table.

1. Classic Marinara Pizza Sauce

A quintessential choice for pizza lovers worldwide, marinara pizza sauce embodies simplicity and balance. Made from ripe tomatoes, olive oil, garlic, and herbs, this sauce packs a tangy flavor that pairs beautifully with almost any topping.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of canned San Marzano tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prep Your Tomatoes: Begin by blending the tomatoes until smooth if you prefer a silky texture, or crush them gently by hand for a chunkier finish.
  2. Cook the Garlic: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat, then add garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Combine: Pour the tomatoes into the skillet, then mix in oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Let simmer for about 15 minutes to thicken.
  4. Cool Before Use: Once the sauce has cooled, spread it over your pizza dough before adding toppings.

This sauce is a foolproof classic that works for traditional pizzas like margherita, pepperoni, or even veggie-packed pizzas. The simplicity of this blend lets the crust and toppings shine without overshadowing them.

2. Creamy White Garlic Sauce

If you love a rich, savory pizza base, a creamy white garlic sauce is an exceptional option. This sauce pairs well with toppings like grilled chicken, artichokes, spinach, and mushrooms, making it an excellent choice for white pizzas.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 3 garlic cloves (finely minced)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Make a Roux: Start by melting butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to remove the raw flavor.
  2. Add Milk: Gradually whisk in milk, ensuring no lumps form.
  3. Infuse Garlic Flavor: Add minced garlic and cook until the sauce thickens.
  4. Incorporate Cheese: Mix in Parmesan cheese, stirring to melt and combine evenly.
  5. Season to Taste: Finish with salt and pepper before letting the sauce cool.

This luxurious sauce is perfect for specialty pizzas like chicken alfredo pizza or spinach artichoke pizza. Its creamy texture and robust flavors elevate your pizza offering to restaurant-quality standards.

3. Spicy Arrabbiata Sauce

If you prefer bold and fiery flavors, an arrabbiata pizza sauce will bring heat and depth to your pie. Its spicy profile pairs wonderfully with toppings like spicy sausage, jalapeños, or roasted vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the Oil: Warm olive oil in a pan over medium heat, then sauté garlic and chili flakes until aromatic.
  2. Add Tomatoes: Stir in crushed tomatoes and smoked paprika.
  3. Simmer: Allow the sauce to cook on low heat for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Season: Taste and adjust with salt and pepper.
  5. Finish: Garnish with fresh parsley before spreading on your pizza base.

This sauce is tailor-made for adventuresome eaters and adds a punch of spice to your pizza party.

4. Pesto Sauce

For those who like to break away from tradition, pesto sauce offers a fresh and herbaceous twist to your homemade pizzas. Its light yet flavorful nature pairs beautifully with toppings like fresh mozzarella, cherry tomatoes, and grilled zucchini.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Blend Ingredients: In a food processor, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, and Parmesan cheese.
  2. Add Oil Gradually: While blending, drizzle in olive oil until the mixture reaches your desired consistency.
  3. Season: Add salt and pepper, then give the sauce one final mix.

While pesto is traditionally green and vibrant, you can experiment with other leafy greens like spinach or arugula for a slight variation. Spread the pesto lightly to avoid overpowering your crust and toppings.

5. BBQ Sauce

For fans of smoky and tangy flavors, BBQ sauce offers a delicious option for your pizza base. Pair it with shredded chicken, bacon, caramelized onions, and cheddar cheese for a hearty barbecue chicken pizza.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of store-bought or homemade BBQ sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Dash of cayenne pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix Ingredients: Combine BBQ sauce, Worcestershire, honey, smoked paprika, and cayenne in a bowl.
  2. Heat and Thicken: Warm the mixture on low heat for a few minutes.
  3. Spread: Once cooled slightly, layer the sauce generously over your pizza base.

BBQ sauce is a versatile choice for non-traditional pizzas, delivering tangy, smoky, and sweet flavors all at once.

6. Buffalo Wing Sauce

If you’re a fan of buffalo wings, this fiery sauce is perfect for a buffalo chicken pizza. Its vibrant and tangy notes pair perfectly with shredded chicken, crumbled blue cheese, and red onions.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup hot sauce (Frank’s RedHot works well)
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder

Instructions:

  1. Combine Butter and Hot Sauce: Melt butter in a saucepan and stir in hot sauce until smooth.
  2. Add Vinegar and Garlic Powder: Mix in vinegar and garlic powder for flavor complexity.
  3. Cool: Allow the sauce to cool before spreading it on your crust.

Use this sauce for a pizza that packs a perfect punch, ideal for game nights or casual gatherings.

Tips for Success with Pizza Sauces

The best way to achieve delicious pizza results every time is to prioritize quality and freshness in your ingredients. Opt for ripe tomatoes, aromatic herbs, and robust flavors to make your sauces shine. Additionally, consider the following:

  • Balance: Ensure that your sauce complements your toppings and crust without overwhelming them.
  • Consistency: Adjust the thickness of your sauce to suit your crust. Thinner sauces work best for thin-crust pizzas, while thicker sauces suit deep-dish styles.
  • Experimentation: Don’t be afraid to combine flavors or try unconventional ingredients to give your pizza a unique spin.

The Final Slice

Creating the perfect pizza sauce elevates your homemade pizzas from good to unforgettable. Whether you gravitate toward classic marinara, creamy garlic sauces, or zesty buffalo blends, these recipes allow you to explore diverse flavors and personalize your pizza experience. Armed with these options, you can embrace your creativity, crafting each pizza as a culinary work of art. So, roll out your dough, choose your sauce, and let the magic begin!
