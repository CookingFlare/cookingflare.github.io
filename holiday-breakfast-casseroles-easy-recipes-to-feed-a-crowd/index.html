Holiday Breakfast Casseroles: Easy Recipes to Feed a Crowd


Are you tired of scrambling to make breakfast for your family and friends during the holidays? Do you struggle with cooking for a large group, only to find yourself stuck in the kitchen while everyone else is having fun? Don't worry; we've got you covered with these delicious and easy-to-make holiday breakfast casseroles. These mouthwatering recipes are perfect for Christmas morning, Easter brunch, or any special occasion when you need to feed a crowd. Simple Holiday Breakfast Casserole Ingredients:

1 lb sausage, cooked and crumbled 6 eggs 1 cup milk 2 cups milk 2 cups cubed bread 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese 1/2 cup chopped onion 1/2 cup chopped bell pepper 1 tsp salt 1 tsp black pepper 2 tbsp butter

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter. In a large skillet, cook the sausage until browned, breaking it into smaller pieces as it cooks. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, milk, and salt. Season with black pepper to taste. Stir the cooked sausage, bread, shredded cheese, onion, and bell pepper into the egg mixture. Pour the mixture into the prepared 9x13-inch baking dish and smooth the top. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the eggs are set. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs and serve.

Twist on the Classic Strata Ingredients:

6 eggs 1/4 cup milk 2 cups milk 4 cups torn bread 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1 cup diced ham 1/2 cup chopped bell pepper 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms 2 tbsp butter 1 tsp dried thyme Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Butter a 9x13-inch baking dish and set aside. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper. Stir in the shredded cheese and diced ham, chopped bell pepper, and sliced mushrooms. Arrange half of the torn bread in the prepared baking dish, then pour half of the egg mixture over the bread. Add the remaining bread, the remaining egg mixture, and the cheese on top. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for 10-15 minutes or until golden brown.

Make-Ahead Sausage and Sausage Breakfast Casserole Ingredients:

1 lb sausage, cooked and crumbled 6 eggs 1 cup milk 2 cups milk 2 cups cubed bread 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese 1/2 cup chopped onion 1/2 cup chopped bell pepper 1 tsp salt 1 tsp black pepper 2 tbsp butter 1 cup frozen hash browns

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter. In a large skillet, cook the sausage until browned, breaking it into smaller pieces as it cooks. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, milk, and salt. Season with black pepper to taste. Stir the cooked sausage, bread, shredded cheese, onion, and bell pepper into the egg mixture. Pour the mixture into the prepared 9x13-inch baking dish. Add frozen hash browns on top of the egg mixture. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the eggs are set. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs and serve.

Tips and Tricks These delicious holiday breakfast casseroles are easy to make, and with minor tweaks to the ingredients and preparation time, you can create them to cater to your group's taste preferences. Prepare your egg mixture the night before to speed up the process in the morning. Grease your baking dish with butter, butterscotch, or even non-stick cooking spray for easy cleanup. Keep it Lighter To keep your breakfast casseroles healthier, make a few changes:

Switch from regular cheese to reduced-fat cheese. This modification will reduce the saturated fat and calorie content of your dish. Choose whole grain bread or other whole-grain products to boost the fiber content. Load up on colorful vegetables to provide your guests with essential vitamins and minerals.

Different Holiday Casseroles Whether you're planning a Christmas, Easter, or New Year's Eve breakfast, these delicious breakfast casseroles are sure to please your guests. Use various ingredients to create an Easter bunny stuffed breakfast casserole, complete with baby carrots and sweet potatoes. For Christmas morning, add some chopped cranberries and cherry flavors for a sweet twist on the classic Strata.
