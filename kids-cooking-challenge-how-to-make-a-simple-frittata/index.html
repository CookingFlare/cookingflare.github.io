Kids' Cooking Challenge: How to Make a Simple Frittata


Are you looking for a fun and interactive cooking activity to do with your kids? Look no further! In this cooking challenge, we’ll be making a delicious and simple frittata that your little ones can easily help with. Frittatas are versatile and customizable, making them the perfect dish for kids to get creative in the kitchen. So gather your ingredients and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup diced cooked ham
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place a 10-inch oven-safe skillet on the stovetop over medium heat.

  2. In a medium-sized bowl, crack the eggs and whisk them together with the milk. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Set aside.

  3. Heat the olive oil in the skillet and add the diced onion and bell pepper. Sauté until they become tender and slightly caramelized, about 5 minutes.

  4. Add the diced ham to the skillet and stir until it’s heated through, about 2 minutes.

  5. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet, making sure it covers all the ingredients evenly. Let it cook undisturbed for about 2 minutes, until the edges start to set.

  6. Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the top of the frittata. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the eggs are set and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Remove the skillet from the oven using oven mitts, as the handle will be hot. Let it cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Get Creative!

The beauty of frittatas is that you can add any ingredients you and your kids love. Encourage your little ones to pick their favorite veggies or meats to include in the frittata. You can also experiment with different herbs and spices to add a burst of flavor.

Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Spinach and feta: Sauté some fresh spinach and crumble feta cheese on top for a Mediterranean twist.
  • Mushroom and Swiss: Sliced mushrooms paired with creamy Swiss cheese make for a rich and earthy combination.
  • Tomato and basil: Add some sliced tomatoes and fresh basil leaves for a simple yet refreshing frittata.

Remember, cooking with kids is all about having fun and letting their imaginations run wild. Don’t worry about making it perfect; it’s the process that matters most. Let them whisk, chop, and sprinkle their way to a delicious meal.

Enjoy and Share!

Once your frittata is ready, gather around the table and enjoy the fruits of your labor. Slice it into wedges and serve it with a side salad or some crusty bread. The leftovers, if there are any, can be refrigerated and enjoyed the next day.

Feel free to take pictures of your delicious creation and share them with friends and family. Let them know about the fun cooking challenge you and your kids took on. Who knows, maybe they’ll be inspired to try it too!

Conclusion

Cooking with kids is a wonderful way to bond and create lasting memories. This simple frittata recipe is the perfect starting point for a kids’ cooking challenge. It’s easy to make, customizable, and most importantly, delicious. So gather your little chefs and embark on this culinary adventure together. Happy cooking!
