Greek Tiropita Recipe with Feta Cheese and Spinach


If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or simply love experimenting with new flavors, then you’re in for a treat with this delicious Greek Tiropita recipe. Tiropita, a traditional Greek pastry, is a savory delight that combines the richness of feta cheese, the earthy goodness of spinach, and the flaky, buttery layers of phyllo dough. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this recipe is sure to impress your taste buds and those of your guests.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of phyllo dough
  • 1 pound of feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 pound of fresh spinach, chopped
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup of ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for sautéing

Instructions

Preparing the Filling

  1. Begin by sautéing the chopped onion and minced garlic in a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat until they turn translucent and aromatic.
  2. Add the chopped spinach to the pan and cook until it wilts, then remove from the heat and allow it to cool.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the crumbled feta cheese, ricotta cheese, eggs, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  4. Once the spinach mixture has cooled, add it to the cheese mixture and stir until evenly distributed.

Assembling the Tiropita

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish with a bit of melted butter.
  2. Carefully unroll the phyllo dough and place it on a clean, flat surface. Using a pastry brush, lightly coat a sheet of phyllo dough with melted butter. Place another sheet of phyllo on top and repeat the process until you have a stack of 6-8 sheets.
  3. Spoon a generous amount of the cheese and spinach mixture along one edge of the phyllo stack. Carefully roll the phyllo over the filling to create a log-like shape.
  4. Continue rolling the phyllo and filling until you have a neat, compact log. Transfer the log to the greased baking dish and tuck the edges underneath to prevent unraveling.
  5. Brush the top of the Tiropita with more melted butter to ensure a golden, flaky crust.

Baking and Serving

  1. Place the assembled Tiropita in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the pastry turns a beautiful golden brown.
  2. Once baked, remove the Tiropita from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes before slicing it into individual portions.
  3. Serve the Tiropita warm and enjoy the delightful combination of creamy cheese, earthy spinach, and crispy phyllo pastry.

Final Thoughts

This Greek Tiropita recipe with feta cheese and spinach is a delightful blend of flavors and textures that will transport you to the sun-kissed shores of Greece with every bite. Whether you’re preparing it for a special occasion or simply craving a taste of the Mediterranean, this recipe is a surefire way to impress your palate and those of your loved ones. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to discover the tantalizing charm of Greek cuisine. Opa!
