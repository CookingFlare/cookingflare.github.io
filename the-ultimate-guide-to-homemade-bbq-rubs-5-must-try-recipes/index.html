The Ultimate Guide to Homemade BBQ Rubs: 5 Must-Try Recipes


When it comes to BBQ, the rub is often the unsung hero. A good BBQ rub can elevate a dish from ordinary to extraordinary, adding depth, complexity, and a whole lot of flavor. But store-bought rubs can be lacking, and that’s where homemade BBQ rubs come in. With just a few simple ingredients and some careful blending, you can create your own unique flavors that will make your friends and family beg for the recipe.

In this post, we’ll explore the world of homemade BBQ rubs, covering the basics of what makes a great rub, and sharing five must-try recipes to get you started. Whether you’re a seasoned pitmaster or a BBQ newbie, these recipes will help you take your grilling game to the next level.

What Makes a Great BBQ Rub?

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s talk about what makes a great BBQ rub. A good rub should have a balance of sweet, salty, smoky, and savory flavors, with a texture that’s neither too fine nor too coarse. Here are the essential characteristics of a great BBQ rub:

  • Sweetness: A hint of sweetness to balance out the savory flavors
  • Savory: A depth of savory flavor from ingredients like garlic, onion, and paprika
  • Smokiness: A smoky flavor from ingredients like chipotle peppers, liquid smoke, or smoked paprika
  • Texture: A blend of fine and coarse textures to add depth and interest
  • Flavor Profile: A unique flavor profile that’s both bold and subtle

Recipe 1: Classic Southern-Style BBQ Rub

This classic Southern-style BBQ rub is a staple for a reason. With its perfect balance of sweet and savory flavors, it’s a versatile rub that works well on everything from ribs to chicken to pork.

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon cayenne pepper

Mix all ingredients together until well combined. Store in an airtight container for up to 6 months.

Recipe 2: Spicy Tex-Mex BBQ Rub

This spicy Tex-Mex BBQ rub is perfect for those who like a little heat. With its bold blend of chili peppers and spices, it’s a great rub for grilled meats, vegetables, and even tofu.

  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chipotle peppers in adobo sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime zest

Mix all ingredients together until well combined. Store in an airtight container for up to 6 months.

Recipe 3: Mediterranean-Style BBQ Rub

This Mediterranean-style BBQ rub is a flavorful and aromatic blend that’s perfect for grilled meats, vegetables, and even seafood. With its bold blend of herbs and spices, it’s a great rub for those who like a little bit of everything.

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon crushed red pepper flakes

Mix all ingredients together until well combined. Store in an airtight container for up to 6 months.

Recipe 4: Asian-Inspired BBQ Rub

This Asian-inspired BBQ rub is a unique and flavorful blend that’s perfect for grilled meats, vegetables, and even tofu. With its bold blend of soy sauce and ginger, it’s a great rub for those who like a little bit of Asian flair.

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Mix all ingredients together until well combined. Store in an airtight container for up to 6 months.

Recipe 5: Sweet and Smoky BBQ Rub

This sweet and smoky BBQ rub is a classic blend that’s perfect for grilled meats, vegetables, and even desserts. With its bold blend of brown sugar and smoked paprika, it’s a great rub for those who like a little bit of sweetness.

  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon molasses

Mix all ingredients together until well combined. Store in an airtight container for up to 6 months.

Tips and Tricks for Making Your Own BBQ Rubs

  • Experiment with different ingredients: Don’t be afraid to try new ingredients and flavor combinations to create your own unique BBQ rubs.
  • Use a variety of textures: A good BBQ rub should have a mix of fine and coarse textures to add depth and interest.
  • Store your rubs properly: Store your homemade BBQ rubs in airtight containers to keep them fresh and flavorful.
  • Use your rubs wisely: Don’t be afraid to use your homemade BBQ rubs on a variety of foods, from meats to vegetables to even desserts.

With these five must-try recipes and a few simple tips and tricks, you’re well on your way to creating your own unique BBQ rubs that will elevate your grilling game to the next level. Happy grilling!
