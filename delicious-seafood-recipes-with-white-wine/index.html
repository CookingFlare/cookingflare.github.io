Delicious Seafood Recipes with White Wine


Seafood and white wine have been a classic pairing for centuries, blending the freshwater crispness and slight acidity of the wine with the fresh, briny flavors of the ocean’s bounty. Whether you prefer the delicate sweetness of shrimp, the tender bite of scallops, or the rich meatiness of fish, cooking with white wine unlocks depths of flavor that elevate everyday seafood dishes into something truly special.

This guide explores an array of delicious seafood recipes infused with white wine, each carefully crafted to bring out the best in both ingredients. From simple sautés to elegant casseroles, every recipe invites you to enjoy the culinary synergy that white wine imparts.

Why Cook Seafood with White Wine?

Before we dive into the recipes, it’s worth understanding what makes white wine a perfect companion for seafood cooking. Unlike red wines, white wines tend to have brighter acidity and less tannin, which helps to balance the natural oils and sweetness of seafood. The wine works as a deglazing agent in pans, a flavorful cooking liquid, and a subtle seasoning enhancer, adding complexity without overwhelming the delicate textures.

Common types of white wines used in cooking seafood include Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Grigio, Chardonnay, and Riesling. Each brings its own aromatic profile—from citrus and green apple to tropical fruit and floral notes—that can highlight different flavor dimensions of the dish.

1. White Wine and Garlic Butter Shrimp

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup dry white wine (Sauvignon Blanc works well)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned.
  2. Add shrimp to the pan in a single layer. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook shrimp for 2 minutes per side until pink and opaque.
  4. Pour in the white wine and lemon juice, stirring to deglaze the pan and scrape up any browned bits.
  5. Reduce the heat and simmer for 2 minutes, letting the wine reduce slightly.
  6. Swirl in the butter until melted and the sauce is silky.
  7. Garnish with lemon zest and chopped parsley. Serve immediately over rice or crusty bread.

This recipe embodies simplicity and elegance, delivering a bright, buttery sauce enriched with fresh herbs.

2. Creamy White Wine Mussels

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds fresh mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1 cup dry white wine (such as Pinot Grigio)
  • 2 shallots, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Fresh thyme
  • Crusty bread for serving

Directions

  1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add shallots and garlic and cook until translucent.
  2. Pour in the white wine and bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the mussels and fresh thyme, cover with a lid, and steam for 5-7 minutes or until the mussels have opened.
  4. Remove mussels with a slotted spoon and discard any unopened.
  5. Stir the heavy cream into the pot’s cooking liquid and simmer to thicken slightly.
  6. Return the mussels to the pot and toss gently in the creamy sauce.
  7. Serve hot with warm crusty bread to soak up the flavorful broth.

The creaminess balances the briny mussels perfectly, while white wine provides a subtle acidic lift.

3. Baked Cod in White Wine and Herb Sauce

Ingredients

  • 4 cod fillets (6 oz each)
  • 1 cup dry white wine (Chardonnay is excellent here)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  3. Add white wine, lemon juice, dill, and parsley. Simmer for 3-5 minutes to meld flavors.
  4. Place cod fillets in a baking dish, season with salt and pepper.
  5. Pour the white wine and herb sauce evenly over the fish.
  6. Bake uncovered for 15-20 minutes or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.
  7. Serve with roasted vegetables or a fresh salad.

This dish highlights how white wine can tenderize and enhance the natural essence of flaky fish.

4. Scallops Poached in White Wine with Lemon and Capers

Ingredients

  • 1 pound sea scallops, cleaned and patted dry
  • 1 cup dry white wine (Riesling adds a lovely floral sweetness)
  • 2 tablespoons capers, drained
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • Fresh chives, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Directions

  1. In a skillet, combine white wine, lemon juice, zest, and capers. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  2. Add scallops to the poaching liquid, cover, and cook gently for 4-5 minutes or until scallops are opaque.
  3. Carefully remove scallops and set aside.
  4. Increase heat and reduce the poaching liquid to intensify flavor.
  5. Whisk in butter to create a smooth sauce.
  6. Return scallops to the skillet briefly to warm through.
  7. Garnish with fresh chives and serve immediately.

The gentle poaching keeps scallops tender while infusing a delicate citrus and caper brightness.

5. Seafood Risotto with White Wine

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups seafood stock (or vegetable stock with fish sauce added)
  • 1 cup dry white wine (any dry variety)
  • 1 pound mixed seafood (shrimp, scallops, calamari rings)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a deep skillet or saucepan. Sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  2. Add Arborio rice and stir for 2-3 minutes until rice starts to look translucent at edges.
  3. Pour in white wine and stir until mostly absorbed.
  4. Add stock one ladle at a time, stirring continuously, allowing rice to absorb liquid before adding more. This process should take around 18-20 minutes.
  5. When the rice is creamy and almost al dente, stir in the mixed seafood and cook for 3-5 minutes, until seafood is cooked through.
  6. Remove from heat, stir in butter and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve hot.

Risotto is a labor of love, and the white wine adds that signature tang that makes this dish shine.

A Few Tips for Cooking Seafood with White Wine

  • Choose a wine you enjoy drinking. Cooking with wine concentrates its flavors, so the better your wine tastes on its own, the better your dish will be.
  • Avoid overly oaked or sweet wines unless the recipe explicitly calls for it. Dry, crisp whites complement seafood better.
  • Use fresh seafood when possible. The freshness makes all the difference, especially with delicate cooking methods like poaching and steaming.
  • Don’t overcook seafood. It becomes rubbery and loses flavor. Pay attention to the cooking times above and watch carefully.
  • Balance acidity with richness. White wine adds acidity, and pairing it with butter, cream, or olive oil creates harmony in the dish.

Wrapping Up

White wine brings a beautiful balance of acidity, aroma, and depth to seafood dishes, turning simple ingredients into something worthy of celebration. Whether you want a quick garlic shrimp or a luxurious seafood risotto, there’s a white wine-infused recipe for every occasion.

Next time you plan a seafood dinner, invite a bottle of crisp dry white wine into your kitchen—not just for drinking, but for cooking. The resulting dishes will be lighter, more aromatic, and utterly delicious.

Happy cooking—and bon appétit!
