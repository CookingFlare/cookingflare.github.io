Crema de Calabaza: Spanish-Style Pumpkin Soup


As the weather starts to cool down, there’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting bowl of soup to soothe the soul. And what better way to celebrate the changing of the seasons than with a traditional Spanish-Style Pumpkin Soup, or Crema de Calabaza? This rich and creamy soup is a staple of Spanish cuisine, and for good reason. Made with roasted pumpkin, onions, garlic, and a touch of heavy cream, it’s a flavor combination that’s sure to become a new favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium-sized pumpkin (about 2 lbs), peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Roast the Pumpkin

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the cubed pumpkin on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast the pumpkin in the oven for about 30-40 minutes, or until it’s tender and caramelized.

Step 2: Sauté the Onions and Garlic

In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it’s translucent and starting to caramelize, about 8-10 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Step 3: Add the Spices and Pumpkin

Add the cumin, smoked paprika (if using), salt, and pepper to the pot and stir to combine. Add the roasted pumpkin to the pot and stir to combine with the onion and garlic mixture.

Step 4: Add the Chicken Broth and Cream

Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the soup has thickened slightly. Stir in the heavy cream and continue to simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the soup has reached the desired consistency.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with chopped fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot, and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an added depth of flavor, use chicken or vegetable stock instead of chicken broth.
  • If you prefer a thicker soup, add more heavy cream or simmer the soup for a longer period of time.
  • Experiment with different spices, such as ground cinnamon or nutmeg, to add a unique twist to the soup.
  • Serve the soup with a swirl of crème fraîche or a sprinkle of chopped fresh herbs for added flavor and texture.

Conclusion

Crema de Calabaza is a Spanish-Style Pumpkin Soup that’s sure to become a new favorite. With its rich and creamy texture, and flavor combination of roasted pumpkin, onions, garlic, and spices, it’s the perfect soup to warm up with on a chilly fall or winter day. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the comfort and warmth of this traditional Spanish-Style Pumpkin Soup.
