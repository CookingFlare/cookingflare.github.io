Spanish Potato Recipes: 8 Comforting Dishes to Try


Potatoes are a staple ingredient in Spanish cuisine, cherished for their versatility, heartiness, and comforting qualities. From rustic tapas to hearty main dishes, potatoes have a unique ability to absorb flavors while adding a satisfying texture to any meal. In Spain, these humble tubers are transformed into iconic recipes that showcase the country’s rich culinary heritage. This post explores eight delicious Spanish potato recipes, perfect for cozying up your table and delighting friends and family.

1. Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

No discussion about Spanish potato dishes is complete without the legendary Tortilla Española, a fluffy and savory potato omelette that’s as rustic as it is elegant.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 large eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Preparation:

Begin by frying the potatoes and onions slowly in plenty of olive oil over medium heat. The goal is to cook them gently until they are soft but not browned—a process that usually takes about 25-30 minutes. Once tender, drain the potatoes and onions from excess oil.

In a large bowl, whisk the eggs with a pinch of salt. Combine the eggs with the cooled potatoes and onions. Heat a little oil in a non-stick pan, pour in the mixture, and cook over low heat. As the edges start to set, gently shake the pan to prevent sticking.

Once the bottom is golden and the top is mostly set but still a little runny, use a large plate to flip the tortilla and cook the other side for another 4-5 minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips: Tortilla Española is a great meal any time of day—perfect for breakfast, lunch, or a light dinner. Pair with a crisp green salad and crusty bread for an authentic Spanish experience.

2. Patatas Bravas

For those who love a bit of spice, Patatas Bravas is the ultimate tapas choice. It’s a crowd-pleaser featuring crispy fried potatoes smothered in a piquant tomato sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Salt

Bravas Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (pimentón)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper or chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (400g)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Start by frying the potatoes in hot olive oil until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels and season with salt.

To make the sauce, sauté garlic and onion in olive oil until translucent. Add the smoked paprika and cayenne pepper, stirring for about 30 seconds to release their aroma. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, sugar, and salt; simmer the sauce gently for 15-20 minutes until thickened.

Serve the crispy potatoes topped generously with the spicy bravas sauce. This dish is often accompanied by a garlicky aioli to cool the heat.

3. Patatas a lo Pobre (Poor Man’s Potatoes)

Simple yet profoundly satisfying, Patatas a lo Pobre is a traditional Andalusian dish featuring potatoes cooked with green peppers, onions, and garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, sliced into thick rounds
  • 2 green bell peppers, sliced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Preparation:

Heat plenty of olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the potato slices and cook slowly, allowing them to soften without browning too much. Add the onions, green peppers, garlic, salt, and pepper.

Continue cooking on low heat, stirring occasionally, until all vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized. The slow cooking helps develop deep flavors.

Serve warm, garnished with fresh parsley. This dish is excellent alongside grilled meats or as a vegetarian main.

4. Papas Gratinadas (Spanish Potato Gratin)

If you crave cheesy comfort food with a Spanish twist, Papas Gratinadas is the dish for you. This rich gratin is infused with garlic and Spanish cheeses like Manchego.

Ingredients:

  • 5 large potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup Manchego cheese, grated (or any hard Spanish cheese)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh thyme (optional)

Preparation:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil. Arrange a layer of potato slices on the bottom. Season with salt, pepper, and a sprinkle of minced garlic. Pour a little cream over, and scatter some grated cheese.

Repeat layering until all potatoes, cream, and cheese are used, finishing with a generous cheese topping. Add thyme leaves if desired.

Bake for about 45-50 minutes, until the potatoes are tender and the top is golden and bubbling. Let it rest before serving.

5. Spanish Potato and Chorizo Skillet

This rustic skillet combines the smoky richness of chorizo sausage with tender potatoes, creating an unbeatable combination of flavors and textures.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 200g chorizo, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Cook the chorizo slices in a large skillet over medium heat until they start to release their oils and brown slightly. Remove the chorizo and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pan.

Add the diced potatoes to the pan and cook, stirring frequently, until they start to soften and brown. Add the onion and garlic, season with salt and pepper, and continue cooking until the potatoes are fully tender.

Return the chorizo to the pan, stir to combine and heat through. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.

6. Ensalada Rusa (Russian Salad)

Though known internationally as Russian Salad, this potato salad has a strong Spanish identity, especially as a popular tapa in bars across Spain.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 cup peas, fresh or frozen
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Boil the potatoes, carrots, and peas until tender. Drain and cool the vegetables.

In a large bowl, mix the cooled vegetables with chopped eggs and mayonnaise. Add olive oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper to taste, stirring gently.

Chill the salad for an hour before serving. This creamy, tangy, and colorful dish works wonderfully as a refreshing side or as part of a tapas spread.

7. Papas con Mojo (Potatoes with Mojo Sauce)

Originally from the Canary Islands, Papas con Mojo features small boiled potatoes doused in a vibrant and spicy garlic sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg small new potatoes
  • Mojo Verde Sauce:
    • 1 cup fresh cilantro or parsley
    • 2 cloves garlic
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1/2 cup olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
    • Salt and pepper

Preparation:

Boil the small potatoes in salted water until just tender. Drain and set aside.

Prepare the mojo verde by blending cilantro or parsley, garlic, cumin seeds, olive oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper until smooth.

Pour the sauce over the warm potatoes before serving, allowing the flavors to meld. This bright, bold dish pairs beautifully with seafood or grilled meats.

8. Patatas Revolconas (Twisted Potatoes)

A traditional dish from Extremadura, Patatas Revolconas features mashed potatoes mixed with smoky paprika and crispy fried bacon or chorizo.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 100g bacon or chorizo, diced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Preparation:

Boil the potatoes in salted water until soft, then mash until smooth.

In a skillet, cook the diced bacon or chorizo until crisp. Add garlic and paprika, cooking briefly to release the flavors.

Mix the paprika and bacon mixture into the mashed potatoes, stirring well. Adjust salt and drizzle with olive oil before serving.

Bringing Spanish Comfort to Your Kitchen

Each of these treasured potato dishes reflects Spain’s diverse regions and culinary history, bringing warmth and flavor to your table. Whether it’s the iconic Tortilla Española, fiery Patatas Bravas, or the creamy Papas Gratinadas, these recipes celebrate the potato’s central role in Spanish cooking.

Experimenting with these recipes allows you to savor authentic tastes — from smoky paprika and chorizo to garlicky mojo sauces and creamy mayonnaise dressings. These comforting dishes are designed not just to satisfy hunger but to offer moments of shared joy and tradition.

Gather your ingredients, pick a recipe, and immerse yourself in the comforting embrace of Spanish potato dishes. Your taste buds—and your loved ones—will thank you!
