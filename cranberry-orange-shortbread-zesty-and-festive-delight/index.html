Cranberry Orange Shortbread: Zesty and Festive Delight


If you’re looking for a delightful treat that perfectly captures the essence of the holiday season, look no further than Cranberry Orange Shortbread. This zesty and festive delight is a perfect balance of buttery richness, tangy cranberries, and vibrant orange zest. Whether you’re planning a cozy gathering with loved ones or simply want to indulge in some seasonal baking, this recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, cream the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. Add the vanilla extract and mix until well combined. Gradually add the flour and salt, mixing until a soft dough forms. Fold in the dried cranberries and orange zest, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.

  2. Shape the Shortbread: Divide the dough in half and shape each portion into a log, approximately 1 1/2 inches in diameter. Wrap the logs in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or until firm.

  3. Preheat the Oven: While the dough chills, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  4. Slice and Bake: Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and unwrap the logs. Using a sharp knife, slice the logs into 1/4-inch rounds and place them on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 1 inch apart. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.

  5. Cool and Enjoy: Allow the Cranberry Orange Shortbread to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, these delightful treats are ready to be enjoyed with a cup of hot tea or coffee.

Serving Suggestions

Cranberry Orange Shortbread is a versatile treat that can be enjoyed in various settings. Whether you’re hosting a holiday gathering or simply want to savor a moment of tranquility, consider pairing these delightful shortbread cookies with a steaming cup of Earl Grey tea or a creamy latte. The buttery richness and zesty flavors of the cranberries and orange zest are perfectly complemented by the subtle notes of the tea or coffee, creating a harmonious sensory experience.

Storage Tips

To preserve the freshness of your Cranberry Orange Shortbread, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. If you plan to make them ahead of time, the dough can also be wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, then stored in the freezer for up to three months. When ready to enjoy, simply slice and bake the frozen dough as directed, adding an extra minute or two to the baking time.

Conclusion

Cranberry Orange Shortbread is a delightful addition to any holiday baking repertoire. Its vibrant flavors and buttery texture make it a standout treat that will impress friends and family alike. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe offers a perfect balance of simplicity and sophistication, making it a joy to create and share. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to savor the zesty and festive delight of Cranberry Orange Shortbread!
