Quick and Easy Homemade Pizza for College Students


Hey there, college students! Are you tired of the same old cafeteria food and takeout? It’s time to take matters into your own hands and whip up a delicious homemade pizza right in your dorm room. Yes, you heard that right – homemade pizza that’s quick, easy, and oh-so-satisfying. Whether you’re craving a classic margherita, a meat lover’s delight, or a veggie-packed masterpiece, this recipe has got you covered. So, let’s roll up our sleeves and get ready to create some mouthwatering magic!

Ingredients

Pizza Dough

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Pizza Sauce

  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Toppings

  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Your choice of toppings (pepperoni, bell peppers, mushrooms, onions, olives, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough

    • In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar. In a separate bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
    • Pour the yeast mixture and olive oil into the flour mixture. Stir until a dough forms.
    • Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
    • Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour.

  2. Make the Sauce

    • In a saucepan, combine the crushed tomatoes, garlic, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer over low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.

  3. Preheat and Assemble

    • Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well.
    • Roll out the dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness and shape.
    • Transfer the dough to a parchment-lined baking sheet or a preheated pizza stone.
    • Spread the sauce over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust. Sprinkle a generous amount of mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
    • Add your favorite toppings on top of the cheese.

  4. Bake and Enjoy

    • Place the pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
    • Carefully remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
    • Slice, serve, and savor every delicious bite of your homemade pizza creation!

Tips and Tricks

  • If you don’t have instant yeast, you can use active dry yeast. Just dissolve it in the warm water with a pinch of sugar and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it froths.
  • Get creative with your toppings! Experiment with different combinations of meats, veggies, and even fruits for a unique flavor explosion.
  • Don’t have a pizza stone? No problem! A baking sheet works just fine for a crispy crust.

So there you have it, a simple yet sensational homemade pizza recipe that’s perfect for busy college students. It’s a great way to bond with your roommates, impress your friends, or simply treat yourself to a delicious meal without breaking the bank. With a little bit of effort and a whole lot of flavor, you can enjoy the satisfaction of creating your very own pizza masterpiece right in your dorm room. Bon appétit!
