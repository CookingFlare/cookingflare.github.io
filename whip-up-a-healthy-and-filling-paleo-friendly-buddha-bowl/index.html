Whip Up a Healthy and Filling Paleo-Friendly Buddha Bowl


Are you looking for a delicious and nutritious meal that is easy to prepare and perfect for a healthy lifestyle? Look no further! In this recipe blog post, we will show you how to whip up a mouthwatering Paleo-friendly Buddha Bowl that will leave you feeling satisfied and energized. This versatile dish is packed with nutrient-dense ingredients and can be customized to suit your taste preferences. So let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup of roasted sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup of steamed broccoli florets
  • 1 cup of sliced cucumber
  • 1 cup of shredded carrots
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup of sliced avocado
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup of toasted sesame seeds

Dressing

  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of tamari sauce (gluten-free soy sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon of honey (optional for added sweetness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the quinoa according to the package instructions. Once cooked, set it aside to cool.

  2. Next, roast the sweet potatoes in the oven until they are tender and slightly crispy. This will take approximately 25-30 minutes at 400°F (200°C). Once roasted, allow them to cool.

  3. While the sweet potatoes are roasting, steam the broccoli florets until they are bright green and tender. This will take about 5-7 minutes. Once cooked, remove them from the heat and let them cool.

  4. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, roasted sweet potatoes, steamed broccoli, sliced cucumber, shredded carrots, cherry tomatoes, sliced avocado, and chopped fresh cilantro.

  5. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the extra virgin olive oil, fresh lemon juice, tamari sauce, honey (if using), ground cumin, ground turmeric, salt, and pepper. This will be the dressing for your Buddha Bowl.

  6. Pour the dressing over the bowl of ingredients and toss everything together until well coated.

  7. Sprinkle the toasted sesame seeds on top for an added crunch and extra flavor.

  8. Serve your Paleo-friendly Buddha Bowl immediately and enjoy!

Customization Options

  • Protein: Feel free to add your choice of protein to make this Buddha Bowl even more filling. Grilled chicken, salmon, tofu, or chickpeas are all great options.
  • Greens: If you’re a fan of leafy greens, add a handful of fresh spinach or kale to your bowl for an extra boost of vitamins and minerals.
  • Sauces: Experiment with different sauces or dressings to suit your taste. Tahini, almond butter, or a zesty citrus dressing would all work well with this dish.
  • Toppings: Get creative with additional toppings such as sliced almonds, sunflower seeds, or even a sprinkle of nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor.

Conclusion

There you have it – a simple and satisfying Paleo-friendly Buddha Bowl recipe that will nourish your body and delight your taste buds. This dish is not only visually appealing but also packed with wholesome ingredients that will keep you feeling energized throughout the day. Remember, the key to a successful Buddha Bowl is to be creative and customize it to your liking. So go ahead, gather your favorite ingredients, and whip up a bowl of goodness that will leave you feeling satisfied and nourished. Enjoy!
