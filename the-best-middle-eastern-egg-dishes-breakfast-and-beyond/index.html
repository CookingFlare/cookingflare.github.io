The Best Middle Eastern Egg Dishes: Breakfast and Beyond


Eggs are a universal ingredient, cherished across different cultures for their versatility, flavor, and nutritional value. In the Middle Eastern culinary tradition, eggs have long been a central component of breakfast, lunch, and dinner dishes. Rich in history and flavor, Middle Eastern egg dishes blend savory ingredients, aromatic spices, and fresh herbs to create mouthwatering meals. Whether you’re enjoying a leisurely breakfast or preparing a hearty dinner, these egg dishes bring a unique blend of flavors to the table.

In this blog post, we will explore some of the best and most iconic Middle Eastern egg dishes, offering insights into their cultural significance, the ingredients that make them special, and how to recreate them at home. From classic Shakshuka to lesser-known regional favorites, these egg dishes are sure to spice up your culinary repertoire. Let’s dive into the delightful world of Middle Eastern eggs!

1. Shakshuka: The Iconic North African-Middle Eastern Dish

Shakshuka is arguably the most famous Middle Eastern egg dish, loved by many for its simplicity, hearty flavor, and versatility. This dish is a true breakfast staple in countries like Israel, Tunisia, and Morocco, though its roots trace back to North Africa. The core of shakshuka consists of eggs poached in a rich and aromatic tomato sauce spiced with cumin, paprika, garlic, and chili. The sauce often includes onions, bell peppers, and a variety of fresh herbs, making it a satisfying meal any time of the day.

How to Make Shakshuka

To make a delicious shakshuka at home, follow these easy steps:

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4-6 eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Feta cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and bell peppers and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Stir in the garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes with their juices and season with salt and pepper. Simmer the sauce for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken.
  4. Create small wells in the sauce and carefully crack the eggs into each well.
  5. Cover the skillet and cook for 6-8 minutes, or until the eggs are cooked to your liking.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs and feta cheese if desired. Serve with warm pita or crusty bread for dipping.

Shakshuka is often served straight from the pan and enjoyed with family or friends, making it the perfect communal breakfast or brunch.

2. Frittata with Za’atar: A Middle Eastern Twist on the Classic

Frittata is an Italian-style omelette that has become a beloved dish in many parts of the world, including the Middle East. This version of the frittata is infused with the earthy, aromatic flavors of za’atar, a spice blend consisting of thyme, oregano, sumac, sesame seeds, and salt. Za’atar brings a bright, herby flavor that complements the richness of eggs, creating a fusion dish that’s perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

How to Make Frittata with Za’atar

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tbsp za’atar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a large, oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and zucchini and cook until soft, about 7-10 minutes.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, za’atar, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the egg mixture into the skillet with the cooked vegetables. Stir gently to combine.
  5. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese over the top.
  6. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the eggs are fully set and golden brown on top.
  7. Remove from the oven and garnish with fresh mint leaves.
  8. Serve the frittata warm or at room temperature, ideal for a light meal or as a side dish.

This za’atar-infused frittata is a perfect blend of Middle Eastern and Mediterranean flavors, offering a unique twist on a classic favorite.

3. Eggs in Kibbeh: A Traditional Lebanese Dish

Kibbeh, a traditional Lebanese dish, is typically made from bulgur wheat, ground meat (usually lamb or beef), and spices. While kibbeh is often prepared as patties or a baked casserole, a lesser-known variation incorporates eggs into the mix. Known as kibbeh bel laban or kibbeh bil bayd, this version of kibbeh features eggs stuffed inside the kibbeh mixture, creating a delicious surprise when sliced open.

How to Make Eggs in Kibbeh

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fine bulgur wheat
  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 large eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • Olive oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the bulgur wheat in water for about 30 minutes, then drain and squeeze out any excess moisture.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bulgur, ground meat, onion, cinnamon, allspice, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well until the ingredients are evenly combined.
  3. Divide the kibbeh mixture into portions, then flatten each portion into a disc.
  4. Place a hard-boiled egg in the center of each disc and mold the kibbeh mixture around the egg, sealing it completely.
  5. Heat olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Fry the kibbeh until golden brown and crispy on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
  6. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  7. Serve warm, and enjoy this savory, comforting dish.

Eggs in kibbeh offer a delicious contrast between the tender meat mixture and the creamy, yolky surprise inside.

4. Menemen: A Turkish Breakfast Favorite

Menemen is a beloved Turkish egg dish, often served for breakfast, but it can also be enjoyed at any time of day. Similar to shakshuka, it consists of eggs cooked in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce, but with subtle differences in seasoning and texture. The dish typically uses a mix of green peppers, tomatoes, and onions, along with Turkish spices like red pepper flakes and paprika.

How to Make Menemen

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green bell peppers, diced
  • 3 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 4 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Crusty bread (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and peppers and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the tomatoes and red pepper flakes, and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes have softened and the sauce has thickened.
  3. Make small wells in the sauce and crack the eggs into each well. Cover the skillet and cook for 5-8 minutes, or until the eggs are set but still slightly runny.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and garnish with fresh parsley.
  5. Serve with warm crusty bread for dipping.

Menemen is a perfect combination of tangy tomatoes, savory peppers, and soft eggs, making it an irresistible breakfast or brunch dish.

5. Eggplant and Egg Casserole: A Hearty Middle Eastern Delight

Eggplant is a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine, known for its rich, smoky flavor and tender texture. One of the best ways to enjoy eggplant is in a casserole dish with eggs. This dish, often referred to as baba ghanoush with eggs or moutabal with eggs, combines the creamy, roasted eggplant with the comforting richness of eggs, creating a hearty and satisfying meal.

How to Make Eggplant and Egg Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, peeled and diced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the diced eggplant with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread it evenly on a baking sheet. Roast for 25-30 minutes, turning halfway through, until the eggplant is tender and golden.
  3. Heat some olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant and soft.
  4. Add the tomatoes to the skillet and cook for another 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and form a sauce.
  5. Stir in the roasted eggplant and cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  6. Create small wells in the mixture and crack the eggs into each well. Cover and cook until the eggs are done to your liking.
  7. Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley and serve hot with pita bread or rice.

This eggplant and egg casserole is a filling and flavorful dish, perfect for any meal of the day.

Conclusion: The Magic of Middle Eastern Eggs

Middle Eastern cuisine offers an abundance of egg-based dishes, each one reflecting the region’s rich history, diverse cultures, and unique blend of spices and herbs. From the comforting shakshuka to the flavorful menemen and the savory kibbeh, these dishes showcase the versatility and deliciousness of eggs in Middle Eastern cooking.

Whether you’re preparing breakfast for yourself or hosting a brunch gathering, these egg dishes will transport you to the heart of the Middle East with every bite. The next time you’re looking to explore new flavors and culinary traditions, try one of these Middle Eastern egg recipes and experience the magic of eggs in a whole new way.
