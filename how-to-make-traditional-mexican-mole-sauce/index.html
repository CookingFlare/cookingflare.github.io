How to Make Traditional Mexican Mole Sauce


Mole sauce, a staple of Mexican cuisine, is a rich, complex, and flavorful condiment that has been a part of the country’s gastronomic heritage for centuries. This iconic sauce is a blend of chocolate, spices, nuts, and chilies, which come together to create a sauce that is both sweet and savory. In this recipe, we will explore the traditional method of making mole sauce, a process that requires patience, dedication, and attention to detail.

The Origins of Mole Sauce

Mole sauce has its roots in the indigenous cuisine of Mexico, dating back to the Aztec and Mayan civilizations. The word “mole” comes from the Nahuatl language, meaning “mix” or “blend.” Over time, Spanish conquistadors and African slaves introduced new ingredients and techniques, which were incorporated into the traditional recipe. Today, mole sauce is a symbol of Mexican gastronomy, with different regions and families having their own unique variations.

Ingredients

To make traditional Mexican mole sauce, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 mulato chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 pasilla chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 1/4 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 2 tablespoons dark chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons lard or vegetable oil

Preparing the Chilies

To start, you will need to rehydrate the dried chilies. Place the ancho, mulato, and pasilla chilies in a large bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for about 30 minutes, or until they are soft and pliable. Drain the chilies and remove the stems and seeds. Cut the chilies into small pieces and set them aside.

Roasting the Nuts

Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C). Spread the almonds on a baking sheet and roast them in the oven for about 10 minutes, or until they are fragrant and lightly browned. Let the almonds cool and then chop them into small pieces.

Grinding the Spices

In a spice grinder or mortar and pestle, grind the cinnamon, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper into a fine powder. Set the spice blend aside.

Making the Mole Sauce

In a large saucepan, heat the oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for about 1 minute, or until fragrant. Add the rehydrated chilies, roasted almonds, ground spices, and chocolate chips. Cook for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture is well combined.

Add the chicken broth, water, tomato paste, and flour to the saucepan. Whisk the mixture until it is smooth and free of lumps. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.

Finishing the Mole Sauce

Stir in the lard or vegetable oil and continue to simmer the sauce for another 5 minutes. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let the mole sauce cool to room temperature.

Using the Mole Sauce

Mole sauce is a versatile condiment that can be used in a variety of dishes, from enchiladas and tacos to chicken and pork. You can also use it as a dip for tortilla chips or as a topping for grilled meats and vegetables.

Tips and Variations

  • To make a creamier mole sauce, add a tablespoon or two of heavy cream or sour cream.
  • To make a spicier mole sauce, add more cayenne pepper or use hotter peppers like habaneros or ghost peppers.
  • To make a vegan mole sauce, replace the lard or vegetable oil with a plant-based oil and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • To make a gluten-free mole sauce, use gluten-free flour or omit the flour altogether.

Conclusion

Traditional Mexican mole sauce is a complex and flavorful condiment that requires patience, dedication, and attention to detail. By following this recipe, you can create a rich and savory sauce that is perfect for a variety of dishes. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.
