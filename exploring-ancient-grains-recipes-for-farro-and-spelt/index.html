Exploring Ancient Grains: Recipes for Farro and Spelt


Ancient grains have been enjoying a well-deserved resurgence in popularity. With their rich histories, distinct flavors, and impressive nutritional profiles, they offer a welcome respite from more common grains like rice or wheat. Among this esteemed group, farro and spelt stand out as versatile and hearty grains that have fed civilizations for centuries. In today’s kitchen, they are more than just food staples—they are a way to reconnect with culinary traditions while keeping meals wholesome and satisfying. This post dives into the culinary journeys of farro and spelt and presents creative recipes to help you incorporate these ancient grains into your diet.

What Are Farro and Spelt?

Before jumping into recipes, let’s uncover what makes farro and spelt unique.

Farro is a type of wheat grain with roots tracing back to Mesopotamia. Believed to be one of the earliest cultivated grains, it is known for its nutty, earthy flavor and chewy texture. Commonly mistaken for barley, farro stands apart due to its firm bite that holds up perfectly in soups, salads, or even as a hearty side. It’s available in three forms—whole, semi-pearled, and pearled—the pearled version cooking the quickest due to the removal of some bran layers.

Spelt, another ancient grain of the wheat family, was a staple in European diets over 8,000 years ago. While closely related to modern wheat, it contains a slightly milder, slightly sweeter flavor and lighter texture. It has a slightly firmer hull than traditional wheat, preserving its nutrients while creating a versatile grain that works well in everything from bread and pasta to pilafs and breakfast porridges.

Both of these grains provide an impressive array of nutrients. They are rich in fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals like magnesium and zinc. They also contain less gluten than modern wheat, making them more digestible for those sensitive to gluten—but not suitable for those with celiac disease.

Cooking Basics for Farro and Spelt

Cooking with ancient grains might seem intimidating at first, but once you understand their properties, they’re incredibly approachable.

How to Cook Farro

  1. Rinse and Soak: Like most grains, rinsing farro under cool water is essential. For whole grains, soaking overnight reduces cooking time, though semi-pearled and pearled varieties can be cooked without soaking.
  2. Cooking Ratio: Use a ratio of 1 cup of farro to 2-3 cups of water or broth.
  3. Cook Time: Pearled farro cooks in 15-20 minutes, semi-pearled in 25-30 minutes, and whole farro in 40-50 minutes, depending on soaking.

How to Cook Spelt

  1. Preparation: Rinse spelt well to remove impurities before cooking.
  2. Cooking Ratio: Use the same ratio as farro: 1 cup of spelt to 2-3 cups cooking liquid.
  3. Simmer Times: Like farro, whole spelt benefits from soaking overnight and takes 50 minutes to cook. Pearled spelt is quicker, requiring around 20-25 minutes.

Now that you know the basics, let’s explore some creative, delicious ways to incorporate farro and spelt into your meals.

Recipes to Elevate Your Ancient Grain Game

Hearty Farro and Roasted Vegetable Salad

Kickstart your exploration of farro with a robust salad that’s packed with flavor, color, and nutrition. This recipe is perfect as a main dish or side for any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pearled farro
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 2 cups kale, chopped
  • ½ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/3 cup toasted pine nuts
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread zucchini, bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with salt and pepper, and roast for 20-25 minutes until tender.
  2. In the meantime, cook farro in vegetable broth according to package instructions and let it cool slightly.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Combine cooked farro, roasted vegetables, kale, feta, and toasted pine nuts in a large bowl. Toss with the dressing and serve warm or chilled.

Creamy Spelt Risotto with Mushrooms

Trade in your arborio rice for spelt in this earthy twist on a classic risotto. The nutty flavor of spelt pairs beautifully with mushrooms and Parmesan cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pearled spelt
  • 3 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups mixed mushrooms, sliced
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 2 tbsp parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the broth in a saucepan and keep warm.
  2. In a deep skillet, heat olive oil and 1 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant.
  3. Stir in mushrooms and cook for 5-7 minutes until they release their juices. Add spelt and toast for 2 minutes.
  4. Deglaze the pan with white wine, stirring until absorbed.
  5. Gradually ladle in warm broth, ½ cup at a time, stirring frequently. Allow each addition to be absorbed before adding more. Continue until spelt is al dente, about 25 minutes.
  6. Stir in the remaining butter, Parmesan cheese, and parsley. Season with salt and pepper to taste and serve warm.

Breakfast Spelt Porridge with Cinnamon and Bananas

Start your morning with this comforting, nutrient-packed spelt porridge that’s simple to prepare and endlessly customizable.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked spelt
  • 1 ½ cups milk or plant-based milk
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 banana, sliced
  • 2 tbsp chopped walnuts or pecans

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine cooked spelt, milk, honey, and cinnamon. Heat over medium until warmed through, stirring occasionally.
  2. Serve in bowls topped with banana slices and chopped nuts. Add an extra drizzle of honey, if desired.

Why Choose Ancient Grains?

Including farro and spelt in your meals doesn’t just elevate flavor and texture—it also boosts your diet’s nutritional diversity. They are exceptional sources of plant-based protein, making them staple ingredients for vegetarians or anyone seeking to reduce meat consumption. Additionally, their robust, chewy textures make meals feel hearty and satisfying, ensuring you stay full longer.

The Revival of Ancient Grains in Your Kitchen

From hearty salads to creamy risottos and comforting breakfast bowls, farro and spelt are the perfect way to embrace ancient grains in modern cooking. Their versatility allows them to seamlessly replace more common grains, adding dimension and depth to your favorite dishes. Whether you’re motivated by nutrition, culinary curiosity, or a desire to connect with ancient food traditions, farro and spelt have something to offer every cook. Start experimenting today and experience the rich flavors and benefits that these celebrated grains bring to your table.
