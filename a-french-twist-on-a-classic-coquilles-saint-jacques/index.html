A French Twist on a Classic: Coquilles Saint-Jacques


Coquilles Saint-Jacques, also known as scallops in white wine sauce, is a classic French dish that has been a staple of fine dining for centuries. Originating from the city of Saint-Jacques-de-la-Blande, this dish is a masterclass in simplicity and elegance. In this recipe, we will put a French twist on this classic by incorporating a few unexpected ingredients and techniques, elevating it to new heights.

Ingredients

  • 12 large scallops
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • Fresh thyme, chopped (optional)

Instructions

Preparation

  1. Rinse the scallops under cold water and pat them dry with paper towels.
  2. In a large skillet, melt 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute, until fragrant.
  4. Add the white wine to the skillet, scraping the bottom to release any browned bits. Bring the wine to a simmer and cook until it has reduced by half, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the heavy cream, Dijon mustard, and paprika to the skillet. Stir to combine and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  6. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

Cooking the Scallops

  1. Add the scallops to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, until they are cooked through and golden brown.
  2. Remove the scallops from the skillet and set them aside on a plate.
  3. Strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean saucepan, discarding the solids.
  4. Simmer the sauce over low heat until it has thickened slightly, about 5 minutes.

Assembly and Serving

  1. To assemble the dish, place a cooked scallop in each of 4 shells or ramekins.
  2. Spoon the warm sauce over the scallops, dividing it evenly among the shells.
  3. Sprinkle a pinch of chopped parsley and thyme over the top of each shell, if desired.
  4. Serve the Coquilles Saint-Jacques immediately, garnished with additional parsley and thyme if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For an added layer of flavor, add a few sprigs of fresh thyme to the saucepan with the sauce and simmer for an additional 5 minutes.
  • To make the dish more substantial, serve it with a side of garlic mashed potatoes or steamed asparagus.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the scallops with sliced portobello mushrooms or eggplant.

Conclusion

Coquilles Saint-Jacques is a dish that is sure to impress even the most discerning palate. With its rich, creamy sauce and perfectly cooked scallops, it is a true masterpiece of French cuisine. By incorporating a few unexpected ingredients and techniques, we have put a French twist on this classic, elevating it to new heights. Whether you are a seasoned chef or a culinary novice, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your repertoire. Bon appétit!
