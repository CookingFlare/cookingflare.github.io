How to Make a Classic Italian Pizza in Your Oven


Pizza is one of the most beloved foods across the globe, and it’s no wonder why. The combination of a crispy crust, rich tomato sauce, gooey cheese, and your choice of toppings is both comforting and satisfying. But while you can always order in, making pizza from scratch at home brings a sense of accomplishment and allows you to tailor every aspect of the pizza to your liking.

If you’ve ever wondered how to make a classic Italian pizza at home, the good news is that it’s easier than you might think. With a few essential ingredients, a bit of patience, and the right technique, you can make a pizza that rivals any pizzeria, all from the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients for Classic Italian Pizza

To make a traditional Italian pizza, you’ll need a handful of fresh, high-quality ingredients. The key to an authentic Italian pizza is simplicity—fewer ingredients mean each one has to shine. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Dough:

  • 500 grams (about 4 cups) of all-purpose flour: It’s best to use high-quality flour, ideally ‘00’ pizza flour if you want to get the most authentic texture.
  • 325 ml (about 1 1/3 cups) of lukewarm water: Water that’s too hot or too cold will hinder the dough from rising properly.
  • 2 teaspoons of salt: Salt enhances the flavor of the dough.
  • 7 grams (1 packet) of active dry yeast: Fresh yeast is a great option, but active dry yeast works well for home recipes.
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil: Olive oil helps in creating a soft, supple dough.
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar: The sugar helps activate the yeast and aids in the rise of the dough.

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 1 can (400 grams) of whole peeled tomatoes: Fresh tomatoes can also be used, but canned tomatoes are the preferred option for their flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil: Olive oil adds richness to the sauce.
  • 1 garlic clove, minced: Garlic adds depth of flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano: Oregano is an essential herb in Italian cooking.
  • A pinch of salt: To season the sauce.
  • A pinch of sugar: To balance the acidity of the tomatoes.

For the Toppings:

  • 250 grams (about 9 ounces) of mozzarella cheese: Fresh mozzarella is best for that gooey, melty effect.
  • Fresh basil leaves: Basil is a classic topping and adds a burst of freshness to the pizza.
  • Extra virgin olive oil: For drizzling on top of the pizza before baking.
  • Grated Parmesan cheese: Optional, but it adds an extra layer of flavor.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Classic Italian Pizza

1. Preparing the Pizza Dough

Start by preparing your pizza dough. A good dough is the foundation of any great pizza, so don’t rush this step.

Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the lukewarm water with sugar and the dry yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes. If the water is the right temperature, the yeast will bubble and foam up, signaling that it’s activated and ready to go.

Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, mix together the flour and salt. Make a well in the center of the flour, and pour in the activated yeast mixture along with the olive oil.

Form the Dough: Mix the ingredients together with a spoon or your hands until a dough begins to form. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic.

Let the Dough Rise: Place the dough in a large, lightly oiled bowl. Cover it with a damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rise for 1-2 hours at room temperature, or until it has doubled in size. The dough needs to rest and rise to develop its flavor and texture, so be patient!

2. Preparing the Tomato Sauce

While the dough is rising, you can prepare the sauce. It’s best to make the sauce fresh to get that authentic, fresh-tasting flavor.

Cook the Garlic: Heat the olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until it becomes fragrant but not browned.

Add the Tomatoes: Crush the whole peeled tomatoes with your hands or a spoon, and add them to the saucepan with the garlic. Stir to combine.

Season the Sauce: Add the oregano, salt, and sugar. Allow the sauce to simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken slightly, and the flavors will meld together beautifully. Once it’s done, taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Let it Cool: Once the sauce is ready, set it aside to cool. It should not be hot when spreading it on the pizza dough.

3. Shaping the Pizza Dough

Once your dough has risen, it’s time to shape it into pizza form. Preheat your oven to its highest setting, usually around 250°C (475°F), and place a pizza stone or a baking sheet inside to heat up.

Punch Down the Dough: Punch down the dough to release any air bubbles that have formed. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface.

Shape the Dough: Using your hands, gently stretch the dough out into a round shape. You can use a rolling pin, but it’s better to use your hands for an authentic, airy crust. Aim for a thin, even layer but don’t worry if the edges are a little thicker—they will puff up beautifully in the oven.

If you’re using a pizza stone, carefully transfer the shaped dough onto a piece of parchment paper to make it easier to slide onto the stone. If you’re using a baking sheet, lightly grease it with olive oil before placing the dough on top.

4. Assembling the Pizza

Now it’s time to bring everything together. This is where you can get creative, but for a classic Italian pizza, we’ll stick to the basics.

Spread the Sauce: Spread a thin layer of your prepared tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. You don’t need too much sauce, just enough to cover the surface. Remember, less is more!

Add the Cheese: Tear the fresh mozzarella into small chunks and scatter it evenly over the sauce. The cheese should melt beautifully during baking, creating the gooey, melt-in-your-mouth texture that makes pizza so irresistible.

Add the Fresh Basil: Tear a few fresh basil leaves and scatter them over the top of the pizza. The basil will wilt slightly in the oven, releasing its sweet aroma and enhancing the flavor of the pizza.

5. Baking the Pizza

Place your pizza in the preheated oven, either on the hot pizza stone or baking sheet. Bake for about 7-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and crispy, and the cheese has melted and begun to bubble.

Keep an eye on the pizza as it bakes—every oven is different, and you may need to adjust the baking time slightly. If you want an extra crispy crust, you can even broil the pizza for the last minute of baking.

6. Finishing Touches

Once the pizza is out of the oven, drizzle a bit of extra virgin olive oil over the top for added richness. You can also sprinkle a little grated Parmesan cheese over the pizza for an extra boost of flavor.

If you like, add a few more fresh basil leaves for a burst of color and flavor. The beauty of a classic Italian pizza is that it’s simple and unpretentious, but every detail counts.

7. Serving and Enjoying

Slice the pizza with a sharp knife or pizza cutter and serve immediately while it’s still hot. Pair it with a glass of red wine or a refreshing Italian soda for the perfect meal.

Tips for the Perfect Italian Pizza

  • Use a pizza stone: A pizza stone helps distribute heat evenly, creating that perfectly crisp crust that is a hallmark of Italian pizzas.
  • Stretch the dough by hand: Avoid using a rolling pin for the dough. Stretching it by hand gives the pizza that airy, chewy texture.
  • Keep the toppings simple: Authentic Italian pizzas often have just a few ingredients, and the emphasis is on quality rather than quantity. Too many toppings can weigh down the pizza.
  • Preheat your oven: A hot oven is crucial to achieving a crisp crust. Preheat the oven for at least 30 minutes before baking the pizza.
  • Let the dough rise slowly: If you have the time, let the dough rise slowly in the fridge for 24 hours. This slow fermentation develops more flavor in the dough.

Conclusion

Making a classic Italian pizza in your oven at home is an incredibly rewarding experience. With just a few fresh ingredients, some patience, and the right techniques, you can create a pizza that rivals any pizzeria. Whether you’re making it for a family dinner, a casual get-together, or just a cozy night in, your homemade pizza will be the star of the show.

So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and start making your own slice of Italy! Enjoy every step of the process, from the dough to the final slice, and savor the flavor of an authentic Italian pizza made with love and care.
