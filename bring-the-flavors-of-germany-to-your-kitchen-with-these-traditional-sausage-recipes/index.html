Bring the Flavors of Germany to Your Kitchen with These Traditional Sausage Recipes


Germany is renowned for its rich culinary heritage, and one of the most iconic and beloved aspects of German cuisine is its sausage. Whether you’re a seasoned foodie or just looking to spice up your mealtime routine, German sausages are sure to delight. In this article, we’ll explore the world of traditional German sausages and share some mouth-watering recipes to bring the flavors of Germany to your kitchen.

Bratwurst

Bratwurst is one of Germany’s most famous sausages, and for good reason. These plump, juicy sausages are typically made from a combination of pork, beef, and spices, then grilled or pan-fried to perfection. To make a classic Bratwurst, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound pork shoulder, finely ground
  • 1/2 pound beef, finely ground
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted

Combine the ground pork, beef, breadcrumbs, egg, salt, black pepper, nutmeg, coriander, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Add the water, milk, and melted butter, and mix until a sticky dough forms. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Form into 4-6 patties, depending on desired size. Grill or pan-fry until browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes per side.

Weisswurst

Weisswurst is a traditional Bavarian sausage made from veal and pork, and is typically served with sweet mustard and a soft pretzel. To make Weisswurst, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound veal, finely ground
  • 1/2 pound pork, finely ground
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup sweet mustard

Combine the ground veal, pork, breadcrumbs, egg, salt, black pepper, nutmeg, coriander, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Add the water, milk, and melted butter, and mix until a sticky dough forms. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Form into 4-6 patties, depending on desired size. Grill or pan-fry until browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes per side. Serve with sweet mustard and a soft pretzel.

Currywurst

Currywurst is a popular German street food made from a grilled sausage covered in a spicy tomato-based sauce and served with fries or a roll. To make Currywurst, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound pork sausage, such as Bratwurst or Knockwurst
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Fries or roll for serving

Grill the sausage according to package instructions. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, curry powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly, about 10 minutes. Brush the grilled sausage with the curry sauce and serve with fries or a roll, garnished with parsley.

Knockwurst

Knockwurst is a short, plump sausage made from a combination of pork and veal, and is typically served with sauerkraut and potatoes. To make Knockwurst, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound pork, finely ground
  • 1/2 pound veal, finely ground
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup sauerkraut, drained and chopped
  • 2-3 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced

Combine the ground pork, veal, breadcrumbs, egg, salt, black pepper, nutmeg, coriander, and cayenne pepper in a large bowl. Mix well with your hands until just combined. Add the water, milk, and melted butter, and mix until a sticky dough forms. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Form into 4-6 patties, depending on desired size. Grill or pan-fry until browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes per side. Serve with sauerkraut and potatoes.

These traditional German sausage recipes are sure to bring a taste of Germany to your kitchen. From the classic Bratwurst to the spicy Currywurst, each sausage offers a unique flavor profile that is sure to delight. So why not give them a try and experience the rich culinary heritage of Germany for yourself?
