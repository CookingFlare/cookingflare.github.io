Greek Red Lentil Soup with Olive Bread


Greek cuisine is celebrated for its rustic flavors, simple preparations, and emphasis on wholesome ingredients. At its heart is a love for dishes that bring people together, create a sense of home, and highlight the beauty of fresh, nourishing food. Today, we’re exploring a classic pairing that embodies the Greek spirit: red lentil soup served with warm olive bread. This hearty combination is not only satisfying but also brimming with Mediterranean flavors. It’s perfect for a cozy meal on any day of the week.

A Harmony of Flavors: Why Greek Red Lentil Soup and Olive Bread Work So Well Together

The creamy texture and earthy flavor of red lentil soup make it an ideal companion for the salty, briny notes of olive bread. Together, they create a symphony of complementary flavors and textures. The soup delivers warmth and comfort with a rich infusion of herbs and spices, while the bread provides structure with its slightly chewy texture and bursts of olive goodness. Pairing the two is not just a culinary delight—it’s an experience of Greek-inspired comfort food that transports you straight to the sun-soaked Mediterranean coast.

Ingredients for Greek Red Lentil Soup

Before diving into the preparation steps, let’s take a look at what you’ll need to create this nourishing soup. The beauty of this dish is that it relies on pantry staples, keeping the recipe budget-friendly and accessible.

For the Soup:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes (or fresh tomatoes, pureed)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Optional Toppings:

  • Crumbled feta cheese
  • A drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil

Ingredients for Olive Bread

This bread is a rustic loaf flavored with Greek olives, bringing bursts of salty, briny flavor with every bite.

For the Bread:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • ½ cup pitted Kalamata olives, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Preparation Instructions

Greek Red Lentil Soup

  1. Prepare the Base:
    Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 2-3 minutes. Stir in the garlic, carrot, and celery, cooking for another 5 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.

  2. Add the Spices:
    Sprinkle in the oregano, cumin, smoked paprika, and bay leaf. Let the flavors bloom in the hot oil for 30 seconds. This step enhances the aroma and brings depth to the soup.

  3. Incorporate the Lentils:
    Add the red lentils to the pot and stir to coat them in the fragrant base. Pour in the vegetable broth or water, ensuring the lentils are fully submerged.

  4. Simmer the Soup:
    Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes. The lentils will cook down and thicken the soup, creating a creamy texture.

  5. Add Tomatoes:
    Stir in the crushed tomatoes and continue cooking for another 10 minutes, allowing all the flavors to meld beautifully.

  6. Finish with Lemon Juice:
    Remove the bay leaf and stir in the lemon juice for a bright hint of acidity. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper.

  7. Serve and Garnish:
    Ladle the soup into bowls, garnish with chopped parsley, and optionally top with crumbled feta cheese or a drizzle of olive oil.

Olive Bread

  1. Activate Yeast:
    In a small bowl, mix the yeast with ¼ cup of lukewarm water and sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. Mix the Dough:
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture, ¾ cup lukewarm water, olive oil, and oregano. Knead until a smooth dough forms. You can do this by hand or with a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook.

  3. Add the Olives:
    Work the chopped Kalamata olives into the dough, ensuring they are evenly distributed.

  4. Let It Rise:
    Form the dough into a ball and place it in an oiled bowl. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rise for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

  5. Shape and Bake:
    Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch down the dough and shape it into a loaf or round. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.

  6. Cool and Serve:
    Allow the bread to cool slightly before slicing. Serve alongside the soup for dipping or simply enjoy it warm with butter or olive oil.

Serving Tips

When serving Greek red lentil soup with olive bread, keep the table inviting with a Mediterranean touch. Pair the meal with a crisp Greek white wine or a refreshing cucumber salad. For an authentic twist, create a small platter of sides that include extra olives, creamy hummus, and tangy tzatziki.

For dipping, slice the olive bread into thick, hearty pieces. The crusty exterior contrasts beautifully with the rich, velvety soup. A sprinkle of fresh herbs or crumbled feta on top of the soup adds a pop of color and even more flavor.

Health Benefits

Both components of this dish are as nutritious as they are delicious. Red lentils are packed with plant-based protein, fiber, and essential vitamins such as folate and iron. They’re also naturally low in fat, making them an excellent choice for heart-healthy meals.

Olive bread brings healthy fats from Kalamata olives, which are loaded with antioxidants and anti-inflammatory properties. Paired together, these two dishes deliver sustained energy and nourishment—a wholesome combination that feels indulgent without compromising on health.

Make It Your Own

Greek red lentil soup and olive bread are delightfully versatile, allowing you to customize the flavors to suit your preferences. Here are a few ideas to tweak the recipe:

  • Spice Boost: Add a pinch of cayenne for heat or a dash of cinnamon for a sweeter, more aromatic profile.
  • Extra Vegetables: Zucchini, spinach, or bell peppers can be added to the soup for additional nutrients and texture.
  • Bread Variations: Experiment with different herbs, such as rosemary or thyme, for a unique twist on the olive bread.

The Ultimate Comfort Food Duo

Greek red lentil soup with olive bread is more than just a meal; it’s a celebration of Mediterranean tradition and flavors. Whether you’re serving it for a family gathering, a quiet dinner, or meal prepping for the week, this perfect pairing brings satisfaction, nourishment, and comfort. The heartiness of the soup combined with the rustic charm of olive bread embodies everything we love about classic Greek cuisine—a true ode to the simple joys of cooking and eating well.

Dive into this warm bowl of soup and enjoy a slice of fresh olive bread. Let this meal transport you to sunny Greek landscapes as you savor the authentic flavors of the Mediterranean.
