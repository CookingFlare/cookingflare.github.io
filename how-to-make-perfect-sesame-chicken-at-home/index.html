How to Make Perfect Sesame Chicken at Home


Sesame chicken is one of the most beloved dishes in Chinese cuisine, known for its crisp coating, tender chicken, and the rich, savory sweetness of its sesame sauce. Whether you’re enjoying a takeout order from your favorite restaurant or making this delicious dish from scratch at home, the flavor of sesame chicken is simply irresistible. The crispy fried chicken, drenched in a sticky-sweet sesame sauce, provides the perfect balance of texture and flavor. But how can you recreate that authentic taste at home?

Making sesame chicken from scratch may seem like a daunting task, but with the right ingredients and a bit of practice, you can easily master this dish in your own kitchen. This detailed guide will walk you through each step of the process, ensuring that you achieve a restaurant-quality sesame chicken every time.

Ingredients for Perfect Sesame Chicken

Before you start cooking, it’s important to gather all the necessary ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need to make a satisfying and flavorful sesame chicken dish:

For the Chicken:

  • Boneless, skinless chicken breast (or thighs) – cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • Cornstarch – for coating the chicken and giving it that crispy texture.
  • Eggs – used to create a light batter for the chicken.
  • Flour – a key ingredient in the crispy coating.
  • Salt – enhances the flavor of the chicken.
  • Pepper – for seasoning.
  • Vegetable oil – for frying the chicken to golden perfection.

For the Sesame Sauce:

  • Soy sauce – provides the base for the savory flavor.
  • Honey – adds the perfect balance of sweetness.
  • Rice vinegar – gives the sauce a tangy kick.
  • Sesame oil – brings out the authentic nutty flavor.
  • Garlic – finely minced, for aromatic flavor.
  • Ginger – fresh ginger adds a zesty warmth.
  • Brown sugar – deepens the sweetness of the sauce.
  • Water – to adjust the sauce’s consistency.
  • Sesame seeds – toasted for topping and adding texture to the final dish.

For Garnishing:

  • Green onions – finely sliced for a fresh, crisp topping.

Preparing the Chicken

To make sesame chicken, start by preparing the chicken pieces. First, cut the chicken breasts (or thighs) into bite-sized chunks, aiming for pieces that are roughly uniform in size for even cooking.

Step 1: Make the Chicken Coating

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the flour and cornstarch. The combination of flour and cornstarch is key to getting that extra crispy exterior on the chicken. Season the flour mixture with a pinch of salt and pepper to ensure your chicken pieces are well-seasoned throughout.

Next, in a separate bowl, lightly beat the eggs. The eggs will act as a binding agent to hold the coating on the chicken. Dip each chicken piece into the egg mixture, making sure it is fully coated. Then, roll the chicken piece in the flour and cornstarch mixture, ensuring a generous coating. Shake off any excess flour mixture to prevent clumping during frying.

Step 2: Fry the Chicken

In a deep skillet or wok, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. You want enough oil to submerge the chicken pieces halfway, ensuring they fry evenly and get that golden, crispy coating.

Once the oil is hot (around 350°F or 175°C), carefully drop the coated chicken pieces into the oil, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding. Fry the chicken for about 3-4 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through. Use a slotted spoon to remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Repeat this process until all the chicken pieces are fried.

Preparing the Sesame Sauce

Now that your chicken is perfectly crispy, it’s time to make the sesame sauce that will coat your crispy chicken. This sauce is what brings the dish together, infusing the chicken with savory, sweet, and tangy flavors.

Step 3: Cook the Sauce

In a saucepan over medium heat, add a tablespoon of sesame oil and sauté the minced garlic and ginger for about 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it can turn bitter.

Next, add in the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and water. Stir the mixture well to combine, and bring it to a simmer. Allow the sauce to simmer for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can mix a teaspoon of cornstarch with a tablespoon of water and add it to the sauce while it’s simmering to help thicken it further.

Once the sauce has thickened to your liking, taste and adjust the seasoning. If you prefer a sweeter sauce, add more honey. If you want more of a savory kick, add a bit more soy sauce.

Step 4: Toss the Chicken in the Sauce

When your sesame sauce is ready, remove it from the heat and carefully toss the fried chicken pieces in the sauce until they are fully coated. The warm, sticky sauce will cling to the crispy chicken, creating the perfect balance of textures.

Step 5: Add Sesame Seeds

To finish off the dish, sprinkle toasted sesame seeds generously over the chicken. The sesame seeds add a delightful crunch and nutty flavor that is essential for an authentic sesame chicken dish.

For an extra touch of freshness and color, you can garnish the sesame chicken with sliced green onions before serving.

Serving Suggestions

Sesame chicken is best served immediately after being coated in the sauce while it’s still crispy. Here are some great ways to enjoy this dish:

  • Over Rice: Serve the sesame chicken over a bed of steamed white or brown rice to absorb the sauce and create a hearty meal.
  • With Vegetables: Pair the sesame chicken with sautéed vegetables like broccoli, bell peppers, or snap peas to add a healthy touch to the dish.
  • With Noodles: If you’re craving noodles, serve sesame chicken over some chow mein or rice noodles for a satisfying twist.

If you’re hosting a dinner party or preparing a family meal, sesame chicken also pairs wonderfully with other popular Chinese-inspired dishes like sweet and sour chicken, Kung Pao chicken, or fried rice.

Tips for the Best Sesame Chicken

  • Use high-quality ingredients: The flavor of the sesame sauce relies heavily on the quality of soy sauce and sesame oil. Opt for a good, flavorful soy sauce and pure toasted sesame oil for the best taste.
  • Ensure the oil is hot enough: When frying the chicken, make sure the oil is at the right temperature. If the oil is too cold, the chicken will absorb too much oil and become greasy. If it’s too hot, the coating may burn before the chicken is fully cooked.
  • Crispy chicken: If you want extra crispy chicken, double-coat the chicken by dipping it back into the egg and flour mixture after the first coating. This will give you an even thicker and crispier crust.
  • Adjust the sauce: The beauty of sesame chicken lies in the sauce, so feel free to adjust the sweetness or saltiness to your preference. If you prefer a spicier dish, you can add a bit of chili paste or red pepper flakes to the sauce.
  • Toasted sesame seeds: Toasting the sesame seeds before sprinkling them on top brings out their nutty flavor, enhancing the dish’s overall taste. You can toast them in a dry skillet over low heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently to avoid burning.

Conclusion

Making sesame chicken at home is a rewarding experience that allows you to enjoy this Chinese restaurant favorite with all the flavors and textures you love. The combination of crispy chicken, savory sesame sauce, and the added crunch of sesame seeds creates a dish that’s both comforting and indulgent. By following these steps and tips, you’ll be able to recreate the perfect sesame chicken every time. So gather your ingredients, heat up the wok, and get ready to enjoy a delicious, homemade meal that rivals any takeout order.

Enjoy your sesame chicken, and don’t forget to share it with family and friends—because this dish is always a crowd-pleaser!
