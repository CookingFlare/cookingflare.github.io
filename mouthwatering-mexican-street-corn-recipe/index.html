Mouthwatering Mexican Street Corn Recipe


Who doesn’t love the charm of Mexican cuisine? The rich flavors, variety of textures, and vibrant colors are everything a food aficionado would desire. One of the stars of the Mexican culinary landscape is Mexican street corn, also known as elotes. This popular street food has found its way into kitchen kitchens worldwide.

Elotes is a popular summertime snack in Mexico. Vibrant and color-filled, this dish showcases an unusual, yet exciting pairing of flavors. The real magic of this dish happens on the grill and can be easily replicated at home.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh corn kernels (about 4 ears of corn)
  • 2 large red bell peppers, sliced (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chilli powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/2 cup cotija cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons jalapeño, diced (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Start by preparing your corn. Husk your corn, and remove as much of the silks as you can. The goal here is to leave as much of the corn itself intact.

  2. Bak thte corn after removing its silk which will make grilling simpler.

  3. Grill your corn, turning often, until it’s tender and has nice, charred lines, for about 20 minutes.

  4. Next, prepare your seasoning. Mix together the oil, garlic, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Then, spread the mixture evenly on the corn.

  5. Continue grilling the corn until really tender, and your seasonings are fragrant. This should take around 10-15 mins.

  6. Once done, remove your corn from the grill and let it cool slightly. Be very careful not to burn yourself.

  7. Drizzle each ear of corn with lime juice. Then top the corn with crumbled cotija cheese and chopped cilantro.

  8. If you’re so inclined, add a dash of hot sauce or diced jalapeño.

  9. To finish, let your vej grilled Mexican street corn rest another few minutes to meld the flavors before serving. Enjoy your burst of Mexican flavors!

The subtle smokiness of the grill, combined with the refreshing tang from the lime juice, and the creaminess of the cotija cheese, makes for a truly scrumptious burst of Mexican flavors that will have your taste buds craving for more.

Conclusion:

Elotes is the latest in the trend of globalized street food, making its way into our plates from Mexico with its delectable, grilled, and cheesy goodness. Making it is simple, testy and gratifying. Give this flavorful delight a try the next time you want a barbecue flavor bomb that combines sweet, smoky, spicy flavors in one simple dish. Its unique blend of flavors would be sure to add a gloriously bold layer to any menu.

In essence, this dish has universal appeal – you can’t help but love it.

I hope you enjoy making and enjoying this Mexican street corn recipe as much as we do! Remember, it tastes even better when shared with family and friends, so don’t be shy in inviting them over!

Happy Cooking!
