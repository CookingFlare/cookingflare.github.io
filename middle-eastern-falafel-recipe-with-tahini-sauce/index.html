Middle Eastern Falafel Recipe with Tahini Sauce


Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish that has gained immense popularity worldwide. These crispy and flavorful chickpea fritters are not only delicious but also packed with protein and nutrients. Paired with a creamy tahini sauce, they make for a perfect appetizer or a satisfying main course. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making traditional Middle Eastern falafel with tahini sauce from scratch.

Ingredients

For the falafel:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the tahini sauce:

  • 1/2 cup tahini paste
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preparing the Chickpeas: Start by soaking the dried chickpeas in water overnight. The next day, drain and rinse them thoroughly. This step helps to soften the chickpeas and ensures a creamy texture for the falafel.

  2. Blending the Ingredients: In a food processor, combine the soaked chickpeas, chopped onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, ground cumin, ground coriander, baking powder, all-purpose flour, salt, and pepper. Pulse the mixture until it forms a coarse paste. Be careful not to over-process, as the texture should still have some texture and not be completely smooth.

  3. Shaping the Falafel: Scoop out a tablespoon of the falafel mixture and shape it into a small patty using your hands. Repeat this process until all the mixture is used. You can make them into round or oval shapes, whichever you prefer.

  4. Frying the Falafel: In a deep pan or skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Carefully place the shaped falafel patties into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry them for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until they turn golden brown and crispy. Once cooked, remove them from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  5. Preparing the Tahini Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini paste, water, lemon juice, minced garlic, and salt until smooth and well combined. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.

  6. Serving the Falafel: Serve the falafel hot with a side of tahini sauce. You can also serve them in pita bread or wrap them in lettuce leaves for a refreshing and healthy twist. Garnish with additional parsley or cilantro for added freshness.

Enjoy the Middle Eastern falafel with tahini sauce as a delicious appetizer, snack, or even a main course. The crispy exterior and soft, flavorful interior of the falafel, combined with the creamy and tangy tahini sauce, create a culinary experience that will transport you to the streets of the Middle East. Dive into the world of Middle Eastern cuisine and savor the authentic flavors of falafel today!
