Herb-Infused Nut Butters: Delicious Spreads to Try


Nut butters are a beloved staple in many kitchens, known for their creamy texture, rich flavor, and versatility. While traditional peanut butter or almond butter is delightful on its own, there’s a world of flavor waiting to be explored when you infuse these spreads with herbs. Herb-infused nut butters can elevate your breakfast toast, enhance your smoothies, and even serve as a gourmet ingredient in savory dishes. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the art of creating herb-infused nut butters, featuring a variety of recipes, tips, and ideas to inspire your culinary adventures.

The Basics of Nut Butters

Nut butters are made by grinding nuts into a creamy paste. This process releases the healthy fats within the nuts, resulting in a spread that is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Common nuts used for nut butters include:

  • Almonds: Rich in vitamin E, magnesium, and healthy fats.
  • Cashews: Creamy and slightly sweet, high in iron and zinc.
  • Peanuts: A good source of protein, folate, and niacin.
  • Hazelnuts: Nutty and sweet, rich in antioxidants.
  • Pecans: Delicate flavor with a high content of healthy fats.

When you infuse these nut butters with fresh herbs, you introduce new flavor profiles and health benefits. Herbs are not only delicious but also packed with antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals.

Choosing Your Herbs

The first step in creating herb-infused nut butters is selecting the right herbs. Here are some popular options to consider:

  • Basil: Adds a fresh, sweet, and slightly peppery flavor.
  • Rosemary: Offers a fragrant, woody aroma with a hint of pine.
  • Thyme: Delivers an earthy flavor with floral notes.
  • Cilantro: Provides a bright, citrusy flavor that can lift any dish.
  • Mint: Adds a refreshing, cool taste that pairs well with sweet nut butters.

You can experiment with both fresh and dried herbs, although fresh herbs usually provide a more vibrant flavor.

Basic Herb-Infused Nut Butter Recipe

Before diving into specific herb-infused recipes, let’s start with a simple base recipe for a classic almond butter, which can be infused with your chosen herbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw almonds
  • 1-2 tablespoons of your chosen fresh herb, finely chopped (e.g., basil, rosemary, cilantro)
  • 1-2 tablespoons of coconut oil (optional for creaminess)
  • Salt to taste
  • Honey or maple syrup (optional for sweetness)

Instructions

  1. Toast the Almonds: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Spread the almonds on a baking sheet and toast them for about 10 minutes, or until they are fragrant and slightly golden. This step enhances the nutty flavor of the almonds.

  2. Blend the Nuts: Allow the almonds to cool for a few minutes, then transfer them to a food processor. Blend on high until the almonds break down into a flour-like consistency, scraping down the sides as needed.

  3. Add the Herbs: Once the almonds have turned into a creamy paste, add the finely chopped herbs. If using, also add the coconut oil, salt, and sweetener at this stage. Blend again until the herbs are well incorporated, and the butter is smooth.

  4. Taste and Adjust: Taste your nut butter and adjust the seasoning or sweetness as desired. You can add more herbs for a stronger flavor or additional sweetener if you prefer a sweeter spread.

  5. Store: Transfer the herb-infused nut butter to a clean jar and store it in the refrigerator. It will keep for about two weeks.

Flavor Variations

Now that you have a base recipe, let’s explore some exciting flavor combinations to try. Each of these variations can be made by following the basic recipe and simply changing the herbs or adding other complementary ingredients.

1. Garlic and Rosemary Almond Butter

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw almonds
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic, roasted and mashed
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Follow the basic recipe, adding the garlic and rosemary in step 3. This savory nut butter pairs well with crackers, cheese, or as a sandwich spread.

2. Cilantro and Lime Cashew Butter

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw cashews
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Blend the cashews until creamy, then add cilantro, lime zest, lime juice, and salt. This bright and zesty butter is perfect for drizzling over tacos or spreading on grilled chicken.

3. Mint Chocolate Hazelnut Butter

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw hazelnuts
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Follow the basic recipe for hazelnuts, adding the mint, cocoa powder, and sweetener in step 3. This decadent spread is great on toast, pancakes, or as a dip for fruits.

4. Spicy Thai Peanut Butter

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1-2 tablespoons sriracha (to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Blend peanuts until creamy, then add cilantro, sriracha, lime juice, and salt. This spicy spread is fantastic on sandwiches or as a dip for fresh veggies.

Serving Suggestions

Herb-infused nut butters can be used in a variety of ways to enhance your meals. Here are some delicious serving ideas:

  • Spread on Toast: Slather your herb-infused nut butter on whole-grain bread or bagels for a flavorful breakfast.
  • In Smoothies: Add a spoonful of your favorite nut butter to smoothies for an extra boost of flavor and creaminess.
  • As a Dip: Serve nut butters with fresh vegetables, apple slices, or crackers for a nutritious snack.
  • In Dressings: Whisk your favorite nut butter into salad dressings for a creamy texture and unique flavor.
  • With Meats: Use herb-infused nut butters as marinades or glazes for grilled meats, adding depth to your dishes.

Storing Your Herb-Infused Nut Butters

To ensure the longevity of your herb-infused nut butters, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. Separation may occur, so give it a good stir before each use. If you notice any off smells or mold, it’s best to discard the nut butter.

Conclusion

Herb-infused nut butters are a delightful way to elevate your spread game and introduce exciting new flavors to your meals. With just a few simple ingredients and a little creativity, you can transform a traditional nut butter into a gourmet spread that tantalizes your taste buds. Whether you enjoy it sweet or savory, the possibilities are endless. So gather your favorite nuts and herbs, and start experimenting with these delicious spreads today. Your toast, smoothies, and snacks will never be the same!
