Spice Up Your Life: Top 10 Mouthwatering North African & Middle Eastern Fusion Recipes


Welcome, adventurous foodie, to an extraordinary culinary journey. Today, we’re diving headfirst into the world of delectable North African and Middle Eastern fusion cuisine, bringing you an irresistible selection of dishes that will awaken your senses and tantalize your taste buds. Think exotic spices, vibrant colors, and satisfying flavors that tell a thousand stories. So, grab your apron, sharpen those knives, and let’s get cooking!

  1. Moroccan-Style Lamb Meatballs with Harissa and Lemon

A twist on the classic, these succulent lamb meatballs swim in a tangy harissa sauce, seasoned with North African spices. Having the taste of Morocco in your own kitchen is as easy as 1, 2, 3!

Ingredients:

  • 500g ground lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp harissa paste
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 2 lemons, juiced

Directions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine lamb, onion, garlic, parsley, cumin, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Mix well and form into meatballs.

  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Brown meatballs on all sides, about 5 minutes.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together harissa and chicken broth. Pour over meatballs.

  4. Simmer for 10 minutes, then stir in lemon juice. Serve with couscous or crusty bread.

  5. Levantine Chicken Shawarma Wrap

Lockdown has taken a tasty turn with this crave-worthy chicken shawarma wrap, loaded with ripe tomatoes, cucumbers, and a creamy tahini sauce. Don’t forget the flaming hot sauce!

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, butterflied
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander
  • 1 tbsp ground allspice
  • 1 tsp ground sumac
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Tahini sauce, for serving
  • Hot sauce, for serving
  • Flatbread, sliced tomatoes, sliced cucumbers, lettuce, parsley, for serving

Directions:

  1. In a large, shallow dish, combine onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, allspice, sumac, salt, and pepper. Coat chicken well and marinate for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  2. Preheat oven to 425°F (221°C). Place chicken and onions in a roasting pan and pour yogurt mixture over the top.

  3. Bake for 45-50 minutes, basting occasionally, until chicken is cooked through.

  4. Serve the shawarma on warm flatbreads with tahini, hot sauce, tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce, and parsley.

  5. Egyptian Ful Medames (Stewed Fava Beans)

This popular Egyptian breakfast is a protein-packed, fiber-rich dish that’s as healthful as it is delicious. A staple in Egyptian households, ful medames is a hearty flavor experience you don’t want to miss.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried fava beans
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp cumin, for serving
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, chopped, for serving
  • Sliced green onions, for serving

Directions:

  1. Soak fava beans in water for at least 8 hours, then drain and rinse.

  2. In a large pot, boil beans in water for 15 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  3. In a separate pan, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté garlic until golden. Remove from heat and mix in lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  4. Combine garlic mixture with fava beans and mash mixture to your desired consistency.

  5. Serve with cumin and a sprinkle of parsley and green onions.

  6. Iraqi-Style Masgouf (Grilled Fish)

A true taste of Iraq, the masgouf is a delicately spiced fish that’s commonly cooked over an open fire. With its smoky, succulent flavor, this dish is a crowd pleaser – and no, you don’t need an open fire, a grill will do!

Ingredients:

  • 5 lbs carp fish, gutted and scaled
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 lemons, sliced

Directions:

  1. Make several deep slashes in the fish, season inside and out with cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper.

  2. Place fish on a grill basket, and layer with sliced onions. Brush with olive oil and grill over medium heat for 20-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.

  3. Squeeze lemon juice over the fish and serve with lemon slices, fresh herbs, and rice.

  4. Persian Lamb Stew (Khoresht Ghormeh)

A flavorsome, deliciously aromatic lamb stew, khoresht ghormeh is a satisfying Iranian dish loaded with fragrant herbs and hearty chickpeas. It ain’t fancy, but it’s a pleasure to behold – and even more so to eat!

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless leg of lamb, cubed
  • 2 onions, 1 sliced and 1 chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dried kidney beans
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh chives, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • Basmati rice, for serving

Directions:

  1. Soak kidney beans overnight, then drain and rinse.

  2. Sauté sliced onion in a large pot over medium heat until golden. Add lamb and cook until browned.

  3. Add chopped onion, garlic, beans, and tomato paste, and stir well.

  4. Pour in 6 cups of water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 1 hour.

  5. Season with salt and pepper, then stir in cilantro, parsley, chives, and dill. Serve with basmati rice.

  6. Lebanese Stuffed Eggplant (Moussaka)

Another floor-show apart, the Lebanese moussaka brings together tender eggplant slices with a vibrant, layered filling. Topped off with a creamy béchamel sauce, it’s about as comfort food as it gets, folks.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs eggplants, sliced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 lbs ground lamb
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 potato, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup béchamel sauce
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for serving

Directions:

  1. Season eggplant slices with salt and pepper, then layer with half the onion and garlic in a large baking dish.

  2. In a separate pan, cook ground lamb over medium heat until browned. Add the remaining onion, garlic, tomato paste, and tomatoes, then simmer for 20 minutes.

  3. Layer this meaty tomato sauce over the eggplant, followed by a layer of sliced potato.

  4. Bake in a preheated 350°F (175°C) oven for 1 hour. Remove from oven, pour over béchamel sauce, and return to oven for another 30 minutes, until golden.

  5. Serve with a generous scattering of parsley and enjoy!

  6. Turkish Stuffed Grape Leaves (Yaprak Sarma)

A scrumptious staple of Turkish table, these grape leaves brimming with savory rice, ground meat, and aromatic herbs are a delightful little package of flavors that’ll awaken your senses. You’ll be rolling them with joy!

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar grape leaves, rinsed
  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 lb ground lamb or beef
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted

Directions:

  1. Place rice and water in a small saucepan, bring to a boil, then remove from heat and let sit.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine cooked rice, onion, meat, salt, pepper, parsley, and mint. Mix well.

  3. Lay grape leaves shiny side down, place a tablespoon of meat mixture at the stem end, and roll tightly.

  4. Place stuffed grape leaves in a large baking dish, seam-side down, and layer with pine nuts on top.

  5. Pour 1/2 cup water around the grape leaves, cover with aluminum foil, and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 45 minutes.

  6. Tunisian Chicken Tagine with Apricots and Honey

Tagines, named after the clay pot used in cooking and the Moroccan tradition, are flavorful stew dishes designed to savor. Rich in history and depth, the Tunisian offering combines tender chicken, sweet apricots, and zesty fresh herbs, making for a match made in Heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp coriander
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped
  • 1/2 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 1 cup warm chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Directions:

  1. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and half the onion. Set aside.

  2. In a large pot, sauté the remaining onion in olive oil over medium heat until golden.

  3. Add garlic, cumin, coriander, chicken, pecans, apricots, and chicken broth. Simmer for 45 minutes.

  4. Stir in cilantro and honey, continue simmering for an additional 10 minutes.

  5. Serve over a bed of crusty bread and savor the enticing flavors of the North African and Middle Eastern worlds.

  6. Algerian-Style Couscous Royal

A culinary treasure of Algeria, this couscous royal is a celebratory feast of tender slow-cooked meat, succulent vegetables, and fragrant spices, all tucked upon a bed of pillowy, steamed couscous. The preparation might be a little time-consuming, but trust us, the results make every second worth it.

  • 2 lbs bone-in lamb shoulder
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1 parsnip, sliced
  • 1 potato, sliced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 cup green olives, pitted
  • 1 lb multicolored peppers, chopped
  • 1 cup chickpeas, cooked
  • 1 1/2 cups couscous
  • 2 1/4 cups warm water
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup almonds, chopped
  • Paprika, for garnish
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for serving

Directions:

  1. Season lamb with salt, pepper, and 1 sliced onion. Place in a slow cooker with carrot, parsnip, and potato.
  2. Pour in 1 1/2 cups water, cover, and cook on low for 6-7 hours, or until meat is tender and falling off the bone.

10. Bahraini Biryani Rice

Last but definitely not least, let's get our exotic spice on with this decadent Biryani Rice from the shores of Bahrain. A symphony of vibrant spices, tender lamb, and aromatic basmati rice, this distinctive and sumptuous feast promises a delightful taste adventure you'll want to embark on again and again.

Ingredients:
- 2 lbs boneless lamb, cut into cubes
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 onion, sliced
- 1/4 cup yogurt
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 1/2 cup fried onions
- 2 cups basmati rice
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 lemon, juiced
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 1/2 cup cashews, chopped
- Fresh cilantro and mint, chopped, for serving
- Saffron, for serving

Directions:
1. Season lamb with salt, pepper, and half the sliced onion. Set aside.
2. In a large pot, sauté remaining onion in olive oil, then add yogurt, tomatoes, and fried onions. Stir well.
3. Add lamb to the pot and coat in sauce. Cook for 10 minutes, then add rice, bay leaves, cinnamon sticks, and lemon juice.
4. Pour in 4 cups of water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30 minutes, covered.
5. Remove from heat and let stand for 10 minutes. Fluff rice with a fork, top with cashews, cilantro, and mint. Add a pinch of saffron for that restaurant-worthy aesthetics.

Bonus Recipe: Yemenite Spiced Coffee

If you've got a coffee routine that runs from the snooze button to sip, give it a flavor upgrade with this fingers-crossed magic from the Land of Sheba – Yemenite Spiced Coffee. The blend of cardamom, saffron, and ginger in this fragrant coffee infusion deliver an experience you didn't know was missing from your mornings.

Ingredients:
- 1 cup hot water
- 2 tbsp coffee grounds
- 1 tsp cardamom
- A pinch of saffron
- 1/2 tsp ginger
- 2 tbsp sugar, to taste

Directions:
1. In a coffee maker or French press, combine water, coffee grounds, cardamom, saffron, ginger, and sugar. Stir well.
2. Brew coffee as you usually would, using the same method for your coffee beans or press.
3. Pour, savor, and let your day get a taste of Yemen!
