Paleo Turkey Meatball Recipes: 5 Delicious and Grain-Free Options


If you’re following a Paleo diet or simply looking for a healthier alternative to traditional meatballs, look no further! In this post, we’ll be sharing five delicious and grain-free Paleo turkey meatball recipes that are sure to satisfy your taste buds and keep you on track with your dietary goals. These recipes are easy to make, packed with flavor, and perfect for meal prepping or family dinners. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Classic Italian Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce (sugar-free)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, almond flour, onion, garlic, parsley, oregano, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Shape the mixture into golf ball-sized meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the tomato sauce and olive oil. Brush the mixture over the meatballs.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through and golden brown.
  6. Serve hot with your favorite Paleo-friendly sauce or over zucchini noodles.

2. Asian-Inspired Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup coconut aminos
  • 2 tablespoons almond flour
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, coconut aminos, almond flour, ginger, garlic, green onion, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle the sesame oil over the meatballs.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through and slightly browned.
  6. Serve hot with a side of cauliflower rice and steamed vegetables.

3. Spicy Buffalo Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce (sugar-free)
  • 1 tablespoon melted ghee or coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, almond flour, hot sauce, melted ghee or coconut oil, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through and crispy.
  5. Serve hot with a side of celery sticks and ranch dressing for dipping.

4. Greek-Inspired Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped red onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, almond flour, red onion, garlic, dill, parsley, oregano, salt, black pepper, and lemon juice. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle the olive oil over the meatballs.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through and slightly browned.
  6. Serve hot with a side of Greek salad or tzatziki sauce.

5. Tex-Mex Turkey Meatballs

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon avocado oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey, almond flour, bell pepper, garlic, cilantro, chili powder, cumin, salt, black pepper, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Shape the mixture into meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle the avocado oil over the meatballs.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until the meatballs are cooked through and slightly crispy.
  6. Serve hot with a side of guacamole and salsa.

Enjoy these delicious and grain-free Paleo turkey meatball recipes as part of your healthy eating journey. They are versatile, flavorful, and perfect for any occasion. Experiment with different sauces and side dishes to create your own unique culinary experience. Bon appétit!
