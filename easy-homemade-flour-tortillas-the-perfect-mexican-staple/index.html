Easy Homemade Flour Tortillas: The Perfect Mexican Staple


Flour tortillas are an integral part of Mexican cuisine, and yet, they’re often overlooked as a simple, yet crucial ingredient. With a few basic ingredients and some simple steps, you can create your own homemade flour tortillas that will elevate your Mexican cooking to the next level. In this post, we’ll explore the art of making easy homemade flour tortillas that will become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the process, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 cup of warm water
  • Vegetable oil for brushing

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making flour tortillas is a straightforward process that requires some patience and practice. Here’s a step-by-step guide to get you started:

Step 1: Combine Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, sugar, and baking powder. Whisk the dry ingredients together until they’re well combined.

Step 2: Add Warm Water

Gradually add the warm water to the dry ingredients, stirring with a fork until the dough starts to form. You may not need to use the entire 1/2 cup of water, so add it gradually and be prepared to stop adding water when the dough comes together.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. You can also use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment to knead the dough.

Step 4: Rest the Dough

Cover the dough with plastic wrap or a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes. This will allow the gluten in the dough to relax, making it easier to roll out.

Step 5: Roll Out the Dough

Divide the dough into 8-10 equal pieces and roll each piece into a ball. Using a rolling pin, flatten each ball of dough into a thin circle, about 1/8 inch thick.

Step 6: Cook the Tortillas

Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Cook each tortilla for 30-45 seconds on each side, until they’re lightly browned and slightly puffed. Brush with vegetable oil to prevent sticking.

Step 7: Store the Tortillas

Once the tortillas are cooked, wrap them in a clean cloth or plastic wrap to keep them warm and pliable. You can store them at room temperature for up to 2 days or freeze them for up to 2 months.

Tips and Variations

Here are some tips and variations to help you take your homemade flour tortillas to the next level:

  • Use a cast iron skillet or comal to cook the tortillas, as they retain heat well and can cook the tortillas evenly.
  • Add a pinch of baking soda to the dough to help the tortillas puff up more.
  • Use different types of flour, such as whole wheat or corn flour, to create different flavor profiles.
  • Add some herbs or spices to the dough, such as cumin or oregano, for extra flavor.
  • Use a tortilla press to flatten the dough, especially if you’re making large quantities.

Conclusion

Making homemade flour tortillas is a simple process that requires some patience and practice. With these easy steps and tips, you’ll be well on your way to creating delicious, homemade flour tortillas that will elevate your Mexican cooking. Whether you’re making tacos, burritos, or quesadillas, homemade flour tortillas are the perfect addition to any meal. So next time you’re in the mood for some authentic Mexican flavors, give homemade flour tortillas a try!
