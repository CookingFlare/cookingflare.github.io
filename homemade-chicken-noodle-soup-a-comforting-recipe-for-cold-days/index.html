Homemade Chicken Noodle Soup: A Comforting Recipe for Cold Days


There’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of homemade chicken noodle soup on a cold winter day. The comforting aroma fills the kitchen, and every spoonful warms your soul. This classic recipe is not only delicious but also packed with nutritious ingredients that will give your immune system a boost. In this blog post, we will guide you through the step-by-step process of making your own homemade chicken noodle soup, ensuring a flavorful and satisfying meal that will keep you cozy all winter long.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, about 3-4 pounds
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 cups of chicken broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 8 ounces of egg noodles

Instructions

  1. In a large stockpot, place the whole chicken and cover it with water. Bring the water to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to low and let the chicken simmer for about 1 hour, or until the meat is tender and easily falls off the bones.

  2. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside to cool. Once cool enough to handle, remove the meat from the bones and shred it into bite-sized pieces. Discard the bones and set the shredded chicken aside.

  3. In the same stockpot, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, sliced carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables for about 5 minutes, or until they are tender.

  4. Pour in the chicken broth, bay leaves, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Add the shredded chicken back into the pot, along with the egg noodles. Cook for an additional 8-10 minutes, or until the noodles are tender but still slightly firm to the bite.

  6. Remove the bay leaves from the soup and discard them. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, if needed.

  7. Ladle the homemade chicken noodle soup into bowls and serve hot. You can garnish each bowl with a sprinkle of fresh parsley or a squeeze of lemon juice for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • To save time, you can use pre-cooked rotisserie chicken instead of boiling a whole chicken. Simply shred the rotisserie chicken and add it to the sautéed vegetables in step 4.

  • Feel free to customize your soup by adding other vegetables such as peas, corn, or diced potatoes. You can also incorporate herbs like thyme, rosemary, or parsley to enhance the flavor profile.

  • If you prefer a thicker soup, you can make a roux by melting butter in a separate saucepan, whisking in flour until it forms a paste, and then slowly adding it to the soup, stirring constantly until it thickens.

  • Leftover soup can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. It can also be frozen for future meals. Just be sure to allow the soup to cool completely before freezing.

Conclusion

Homemade chicken noodle soup is the ultimate comfort food, perfect for warming up on chilly days or whenever you need a soothing meal. With simple ingredients and easy-to-follow steps, you can create a flavorful and hearty soup that will satisfy both your taste buds and your soul. So, gather your ingredients, get into the kitchen, and let the aroma of this homemade chicken noodle soup fill your home with warmth and comfort.
