Cinnamon Spice & All Things Nice: Halloween Cookies & Gravestones


As the leaves change color and crisp air fills our lungs, we know that Halloween is just around the corner. This spooky season is a perfect time to get creative in the kitchen. This year, why not treat your friends, family, and neighbors to a batch of delectable Halloween cookies - also known as “gravestones”? littlepasijans is a recipe for the ultimate Halloween cookies, featuring the warm and comforting flavors of cinnamon, spice, and everything delicious. Let’s uncover the secrets to these sugary masterpieces of terror together.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup sugar, for rolling
  • Black food coloring (gel or liquid)
  • Black frosting, or gel (optional)

Instructions

  1. Start by preheating the oven to 350°F (175°C) and lining two baking sheets with parchment paper. Prepare yourself for the sweet magic that’s about to unfold in your kitchen!

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Give it a quick whisk or stir, making sure all the ingredients are well-incorporated. Then, set this dry mixture aside for a moment.

  3. Next, cream the softened butter and granulated sugar in a separate large mixing bowl using either an electric hand mixer or a stand mixer. Beat the mixture until it’s smooth, fluffy, and light in color - almost like a cloud!

  4. While continuing to beat the butter and sugar mixture, add in the large egg and the vanilla extract. Mix until everything’s well combined and even more irresistible!

  5. Gradually add the dry mixture to your wet ingredients, stirring with a wooden spoon or mixing until a dough starts to form.

  6. Now let the magic unfurl! Take a handful of dough and gather it between your hands. Roll it around until it forms the shape of a gravestone, or cookie, roughly one-and-a-half inches long. Carefully pinch both ends to create the appearance of a weathered stone.

  7. Use the black food coloring to create a simulated “gravestone post” by drawing a simple design or ‘Tombstone’ inscription on one end of the cookie with a fine-tipped brush or piping gel.

  8. Once your gravestones are shaped, it’s time to roll them in the cinnamon sugar mixture. This will give them a unique and flavorful coat as well as leaving your kitchen smelling like an autumn wonderland.

  9. Carefully place each cookie evenly spaced on the parchment-lined baking sheets.

  10. Bake for 10-12 minutes, rotating the baking sheets halfway through to ensure even cooking. The cookies will be golden and slightly crisp, but remember that they will continue baking as they cool.

  11. Remove the gravestones from the oven and allow them to cool on the baking sheets for five minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

  12. While the gravestones are cooling, you have the option of further embellishing them with black frosting or gel to imitate moss or grass at the base of the tombstones. This step is optional, but it creates an enchanting effect that’s sure to delight Halloween revelers of all ages.

Presentation and Enjoyment

Arrange your congregations of gravestones on a fancy platter, and watch the ghouls and goblins gather ‘round in appreciation of your spooky culinary skills. Pair these delightful treats with warm cider or hot cocoa for a taste of Halloween that’ll leave them craving more. Don’t forget to keep the traditional “spooky spices” like cloves, nutmeg, and ginger in mind as you sip and savor this ensnaringly enchanting experience. Happy haunting, my friends!
