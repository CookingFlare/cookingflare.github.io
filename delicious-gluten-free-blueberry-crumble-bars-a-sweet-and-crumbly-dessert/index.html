Delicious Gluten-Free Blueberry Crumble Bars: A Sweet and Crumbly Dessert


If you’re looking for a delectable gluten-free dessert that’s bursting with the flavors of juicy blueberries and a crumbly, buttery crust, then you’re in for a treat! These gluten-free blueberry crumble bars are the perfect balance of sweet and tart, with a satisfyingly crumbly texture that will have you reaching for seconds. Whether you’re gluten intolerant or simply looking to explore a new delicious dessert, these blueberry crumble bars are sure to become a new favorite in your baking repertoire.

Ingredients

For the Crust and Crumble Topping:

  • 2 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small cubes

For the Blueberry Filling:

  • 4 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan and line it with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides to easily lift out the bars later.

  2. Prepare the Crust and Crumble Topping: In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour, rolled oats, brown sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Reserve 1 1/2 cups of the mixture for the crumble topping, and press the rest evenly into the bottom of the prepared pan.

  3. Make the Blueberry Filling: In a separate bowl, gently toss the blueberries with the granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract until the blueberries are evenly coated.

  4. Assemble and Bake: Spread the blueberry filling over the crust in the pan. Sprinkle the reserved crumble mixture evenly over the blueberry layer.

  5. Bake the Bars: Place the pan in the preheated oven and bake for 45 to 50 minutes, or until the blueberry filling is bubbly and the crumble topping is golden brown. Allow the bars to cool completely in the pan set on a wire rack.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Once cooled, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the pan and transfer them to a cutting board. Cut into squares and serve these delicious gluten-free blueberry crumble bars to your family and friends.

Tips and Variations

  • Storage: Store the blueberry crumble bars in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, place them in the refrigerator for up to a week.
  • Gluten-Free Flour: Ensure that the gluten-free all-purpose flour blend you use contains xanthan gum or add 1/2 teaspoon of xanthan gum to the recipe if your flour blend doesn’t include it.
  • Frozen Blueberries: If fresh blueberries are out of season, you can use frozen blueberries. Toss them with an additional tablespoon of cornstarch to absorb the extra moisture.

These gluten-free blueberry crumble bars are a delightful combination of fruity sweetness and buttery crumble that is sure to please everyone’s taste buds. Whether you’re serving them at a summer picnic, a brunch gathering, or simply as a sweet treat with your afternoon tea, these bars are a crowd-pleaser that will have everyone asking for the recipe. So, go ahead, gather your ingredients, and bake up a batch of these delightful blueberry bars for a taste of the sweet and crumbly goodness!
