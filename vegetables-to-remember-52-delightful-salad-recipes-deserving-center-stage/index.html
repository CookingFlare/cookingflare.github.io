Vegetables to Remember: 52 Delightful Salad Recipes Deserving Center Stage


There’s a world of creativity waiting to be unleashed when you look at salad recipes. The humble salad can easily be overlooked or taken for granted, rarely playing center stage in our minds when it comes to glorious meals. Yet there’s an entire universe of flavor, texture, and color that is just waiting for you to explore. For this reason, we’ve compiled a list of 52 salad recipes – one for every week of the year - that will ensure that those veggies will always be front and center on your plate. So, let’s dig into this delightful culinary journey!

1. Roasted Rainbow Carrot Salad

Start your year with a burst of vitamin A from these sweet, tender roasted carrots. Roasting them brings out their natural sugars, making them all the more irresistible. Top the roasted carrots with tangy goats’ cheese, earthy walnuts, fresh mint, and a zesty lemon vinaigrette for a salad that’s truly a feast for the senses!

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb rainbow carrots, peeled
  • ½ cup walnut pieces
  • 4 oz goat cheese
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss the carrots in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread them on a baking sheet.

  2. Roast for 20-25 minutes, until tender and slightly caramelized.

  3. Meanwhile, toast the walnuts in a dry pan over medium heat for a few minutes until aromatic.

  4. Combine the roasted carrots, toasted walnuts, crumbled goat cheese, and fresh mint leaves in a bowl. Drizzle with the lemon vinaigrette, toss to combine, and adjust seasoning to taste.

2. Quinoa and Pomegranate Salad

This salad is a delightful medley of textures and flavors. Quinoa, an excellent source of protein and fiber, is combined with juicy pomegranate seeds, crunchy arugula, and crispy roasted chickpeas. A generous splash of lemon juice and olive oil ties it all together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and dried
  • 1 pomegranate
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the quinoa according to package instructions and let it cool.

  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and toss the chickpeas with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 30-40 minutes, stirring halfway through, until crispy.

  3. Seed the pomegranate, reserving the seeds.

  4. Combine the cooled quinoa, roasted chickpeas, pomegranate seeds, and arugula in a large bowl.

  5. Drizzle with olive oil and lemon juice, toss to combine, and season to taste.

3. Summery Butterbean Salad

Nothing screams summer like a delicious butterbean salad. This low and slow, gently braised bean salad is a must-try, especially when served with fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, and a zingy yogurt dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb butterbeans, cooked
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the cooked butterbeans.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the yogurt, olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper until combined.

  3. Gently fold in the butterbeans, cucumber, tomatoes, red onion, and parsley.

  4. Chill the salad in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

4. Zucchini Noodle and Tofu Rice Paper Salad

A refreshing twist on the classic Vietnamese rice paper roll, this salad combines sautéed zucchini noodles with crispy tofu and a spicy peanut sauce for a mouth-watering dish that’s bursting with texture and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchini
  • 8 oz firm tofu
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • ½ cup fresh basil leaves
  • 8 rice paper wraps
  • 1 cup peanut sauce

Instructions:

  1. Slice the zucchini into thin noodles using a spiralizer or grater.

  2. Cut the tofu into thin strips and fry until crispy. Drain on paper towels.

  3. Soak the rice paper wraps in warm water for about 20 seconds until pliable. Carefully lay them on a flat surface and follow the pictures below.

  4. Layer the zucchini noodles, tofu, bell pepper, bean sprouts, and basil leaves onto the middle of the rice paper wrap. Fold the sides in, roll, and continue rolling until you reach the end.

  5. Slice in half, serve with peanut sauce, and enjoy!

5. Asparagus and Avocado Salad with Citrus Vinaigrette

A fantastic salad to celebrate spring, this Asparagus and Avocado Salad feature fresh, locally grown asparagus grilled to perfection, topped with crunchy almonds, creamy avocado, and fragrant cilantro. A tangy citrus vinaigrette ties everything together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh asparagus
  • 2 avocados
  • 1/3 cup sliced almonds
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill or grill pan to high heat. Grill the asparagus until tender and slightly charred, approximately 4-5 minutes.

  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, lime juice, salt, and pepper.

  3. Combine the grilled asparagus, sliced avocado, toasted almonds, and fresh cilantro in a serving bowl.

  4. Drizzle the citrus vinaigrette over the salad, gently toss, and adjust seasoning to taste.

6. Shaved Fennel, Orange, and Arugula Salad

A classic blend with a twist, this salad brings together crisp shaved fennel, juicy oranges, peppery arugula, and shallots, all bathed in a light, bright lemon vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large fennel bulb
  • 2 oranges
  • 4 cups arugula
  • 2 tablespoons shallots, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Using a mandoline or sharp knife, shave the fennel into thin slices.

  2. Peel and segment the oranges and chop any remaining orange membranes.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  4. Combine the fennel, oranges, arugula, shallots, and vinaigrette in a large bowl. Toss gently to combine and adjust seasoning to taste.

7. Heirloom Tomato and Mozzarella Caprese Salad

A timeless classic with never-ending charm, the traditional Caprese salad finds fresh basil, creamy mozzarella, and ripe tomatoes once again showcasing their perfect harmony.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 lb fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Layer the sliced tomatoes and mozzarella on a plate, alternating between them.

  2. Tightly roll up two or three basil leaves and thinly slice them into strips.

  3. Drizzle with olive oil and balsamic vinegar, and season with salt and pepper.

  4. Garnish with additional fresh basil leaves, and enjoy!

As you can see, salads have the ability to be a mix of textures and flavors that’ll make your taste buds dance. We hope these 52 salad recipes inspire you to enjoy more fresh vegetables and carnivorous delights. No matter your taste preferences, you’ll never be far from a scrumptious creation that showcases the natural goodness of vegetables and pays tribute to their rightful place in the kitchen: center stage. Happy cooking!
