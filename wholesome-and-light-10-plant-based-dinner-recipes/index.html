Wholesome and Light: 10 Plant-Based Dinner Recipes


Eating a plant-based diet doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or satisfaction. In fact, plant-based meals can be vibrant, delicious, and incredibly nourishing. If you’re looking to add some wholesome and light dinners to your weekly rotation, you’ve come to the right place. Here, we present ten tantalizing plant-based dinner recipes that are not only easy to prepare but also packed with nutrients. Let’s dive in!

1. Quinoa and Black Bean Stuffed Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes or until liquid is absorbed.
  3. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove seeds. Place them upright in a baking dish.
  4. In a large bowl, mix cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper.
  5. Stuff each bell pepper with the quinoa mixture and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until peppers are tender.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

2. Creamy Vegan Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and garlic; sauté until translucent.
  2. Add mushrooms and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in Arborio rice and toast for 2 minutes. If using, add white wine and let it evaporate.
  4. Gradually add warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring frequently until absorbed. Repeat until rice is creamy and al dente.
  5. Stir in nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

3. Zucchini Noodles with Avocado Pesto

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cherry tomatoes for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine avocado, basil, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  2. In a large skillet, lightly sauté the zucchini noodles for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and toss with the avocado pesto.
  4. Serve topped with cherry tomatoes.

4. Sweet Potato and Chickpea Buddha Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 sweet potatoes, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups kale, chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked brown rice or quinoa for serving
  • Tahini dressing (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss sweet potatoes and chickpeas with olive oil, paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes.
  3. In the last 5 minutes of roasting, add kale to the baking sheet until wilted.
  4. Serve in bowls over cooked brown rice or quinoa, drizzled with tahini dressing if desired.

5. Mediterranean Lentil Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine lentils and water. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Drain and cool.
  2. In a large bowl, combine cooled lentils, cucumber, tomatoes, onion, and parsley.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Pour over the salad and toss to combine.

6. Cauliflower Tacos with Lime Crema

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • 1/2 cup cashews, soaked and blended for crema
  • Juice of 1 lime

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss cauliflower florets with olive oil, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 25 minutes until golden.
  3. For the lime crema, blend soaked cashews with lime juice, water, and a pinch of salt until smooth.
  4. Serve roasted cauliflower in corn tortillas, drizzled with lime crema.

7. Thai Coconut Curry with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, snap peas)
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté cubed tofu until golden. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add mixed vegetables and sauté for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Stir in coconut milk, red curry paste, soy sauce, and lime juice. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Add tofu back into the pot and cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

8. Spinach and Chickpea Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

Ingredients:

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté garlic until fragrant. Add spinach and cook until wilted.
  3. In a bowl, mash chickpeas with a fork and mix with sautéed spinach, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  4. Remove stems from portobello mushrooms and stuff with the chickpea mixture.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes until mushrooms are tender.

9. Baked Ratatouille

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large baking dish, layer eggplant, zucchini, bell pepper, tomatoes, and onion.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with garlic, salt, and pepper.
  4. Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil before serving.

10. Lemon Garlic Pasta with Asparagus

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz whole wheat pasta
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated vegan cheese for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package instructions. In the last 3 minutes, add asparagus to the pot.
  2. Drain and return to the pot. Add garlic, lemon juice, zest, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Toss to combine.
  3. Serve warm, topped with grated vegan cheese if desired.

These ten plant-based dinner recipes are not only wholesome and light but also bursting with flavor and nutrients. Incorporating more plant-based meals into your diet can lead to numerous health benefits while being kind to the environment. Enjoy exploring these recipes and feel free to mix and match ingredients according to your taste preferences. Happy cooking!
