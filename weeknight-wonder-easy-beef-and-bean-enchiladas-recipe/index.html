Weeknight Wonder: Easy Beef and Bean Enchiladas Recipe


When weeknight dinners start to feel like a chore, it’s time to shake things up with a hearty and satisfying meal that comes together in no time. Enter Easy Beef and Bean Enchiladas—a delicious recipe that is not only simple but also loaded with flavor and nutrition. Whether you’re cooking for your family or entertaining friends, these enchiladas are sure to impress.

Why Choose Beef and Bean Enchiladas?

Enchiladas are a classic Mexican dish that can be easily customized according to your tastes. The combination of seasoned beef, creamy beans, and savory sauce wrapped in soft tortillas makes for a comforting meal. This recipe is a perfect weeknight wonder because it:

  • Is quick to prepare and cook
  • Utilizes accessible ingredients
  • Provides a balanced meal with protein and fiber
  • Can be made ahead and frozen for future meals

Ingredients You’ll Need

To whip up these scrumptious enchiladas, gather the following ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 1 pound ground beef (or turkey for a lighter option)
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)

For the Enchiladas:

  • 8 small flour or corn tortillas
  • 2 cups enchilada sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Additional cheese for topping (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt (for serving)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. Cook the Beef: In a large skillet over medium heat, add the ground beef. Cook until browned, breaking it up with a spatula as it cooks. Drain any excess fat.

  2. Sauté Onion and Garlic: Add the chopped onion to the skillet and sauté for about 3-4 minutes until translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  3. Season the Mixture: Sprinkle in the chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the beef is evenly seasoned.

  4. Add Beans: Stir in the black beans and half of the shredded cheese. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the mixture is heated through. Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 2: Assemble the Enchiladas

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Tortillas: If using corn tortillas, you may want to warm them in a dry skillet or microwave for about 30 seconds to make them pliable. This will help prevent them from cracking when rolled.

  3. Fill the Tortillas: Spread a spoonful of the beef and bean mixture onto each tortilla. Roll them up tightly and place them seam-side down in a greased 9x13 inch baking dish.

  4. Add Sauce: Pour the enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas, ensuring they are well covered. This will keep them moist and flavorful during baking.

  5. Top with Cheese: Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese over the top of the enchiladas. Feel free to add extra cheese for a cheesier finish!

Step 3: Bake

  1. Bake the Enchiladas: Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  2. Let It Rest: Once done, remove the enchiladas from the oven and let them sit for about 5 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld together and makes serving easier.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Garnish and Enjoy: Top the enchiladas with chopped cilantro and serve with sour cream or Greek yogurt on the side. These enchiladas pair beautifully with a simple side salad or some Mexican rice.

Tips for Perfect Enchiladas

  • Make it Vegetarian: Swap out the beef for sautéed vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, and mushrooms, or use lentils for a protein-packed vegetarian option.
  • Spice it Up: If you enjoy heat, add diced jalapeños to the filling or use a spicier enchilada sauce.
  • Make-Ahead: Prepare the enchiladas in advance and refrigerate them for up to 24 hours before baking. Alternatively, you can assemble and freeze them unbaked. Just add an extra 10-15 minutes to the baking time if cooking from frozen.
  • Experiment with Cheese: Try using different types of cheese, such as pepper jack for a spicy kick, or a combination of cheeses for a delightful flavor profile.

Nutritional Benefits of Beef and Bean Enchiladas

This dish is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here’s a breakdown of the health benefits:

  • Protein: The ground beef provides a great source of protein, essential for muscle repair and growth. Black beans also contribute protein and fiber.
  • Fiber: The combination of beans and whole-grain tortillas (if you choose whole wheat) ensures a good intake of dietary fiber, which aids digestion and keeps you feeling full longer.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: Ingredients like onions, garlic, and herbs add essential vitamins and antioxidants, supporting overall health.

Conclusion: A Weeknight Favorite

Easy Beef and Bean Enchiladas are truly a weeknight wonder! They embody the essence of comfort food while being quick and effortless to prepare. Their versatility allows for countless variations, making them a staple in any kitchen. Whether you’re enjoying them fresh out of the oven or reheating leftovers for lunch, these enchiladas are bound to become a family favorite.

So next time you’re in need of a quick and satisfying meal, remember this easy recipe. Give it a try, and turn your weeknight dinner into a fiesta of flavor!
