Instant Pot Meal Prep: 5 Recipes to Save Time All Week


Meal prepping is an essential part of a busy lifestyle, especially when you want to eat healthy, save time, and reduce stress during the week. The Instant Pot has become a game-changer for many home cooks, enabling them to prepare meals quickly and efficiently without sacrificing flavor or nutrition. If you’re looking to save time and effort while still enjoying delicious and wholesome meals, these five Instant Pot recipes are perfect for you. Each of these recipes is designed to be meal-prep-friendly, making it easy to cook in bulk and store for the week ahead. Whether you’re planning lunches, dinners, or snacks, these recipes will ensure that you stay on track with your goals.

1. Instant Pot Chicken Burrito Bowls

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 1 cup brown rice (washed)
  • 1 can black beans (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 cup corn kernels (frozen or fresh)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup chicken broth or water
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Begin by pressing the “Sauté” button on your Instant Pot. Add olive oil and sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic for about 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant.
  2. Season the Chicken: Season the chicken breasts with chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the seasoned chicken to the Instant Pot and sauté for another 3-4 minutes, allowing it to brown slightly on both sides.
  3. Add Rice and Liquids: Layer the rice, black beans, corn, and diced tomatoes on top of the chicken. Pour the chicken broth (or water) over everything. Do not stir the ingredients together.
  4. Pressure Cook: Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Select “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” and set the timer for 10 minutes on high pressure.
  5. Release and Shred Chicken: Once the cooking time is complete, allow the Instant Pot to naturally release for 5 minutes, then perform a quick release. Open the lid and use two forks to shred the chicken inside the pot. Stir everything together, ensuring the rice, beans, and chicken are mixed well.
  6. Meal Prep and Serve: Divide the chicken burrito bowl mixture into containers for meal prep. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with lime wedges for extra zest.

These chicken burrito bowls are perfect for lunch or dinner. The combination of rice, beans, corn, and chicken provides a balanced meal with fiber, protein, and essential nutrients. Store these bowls in the fridge for up to 4 days, or freeze for later use.

2. Instant Pot Beef Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef stew meat (cut into cubes)
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional, can substitute with more broth)
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Beef: Press the “Sauté” button on your Instant Pot and heat up a tablespoon of oil. Once hot, add the beef stew meat in batches to avoid overcrowding, and sear each side until browned (about 4 minutes). Set the beef aside once seared.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics: Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot and sauté for 2-3 minutes, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. This will help develop rich flavor.
  3. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the red wine (if using) or additional broth to deglaze the pot. Stir in the tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, and bay leaves. Return the beef to the pot along with the diced potatoes and carrots.
  4. Pressure Cook: Add the beef broth, ensuring the ingredients are just covered. Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Select “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” on high pressure for 35 minutes.
  5. Natural Release and Thicken (Optional): After the cooking time is up, let the Instant Pot naturally release pressure for about 10 minutes, then do a quick release. If you prefer a thicker stew, mix 2 tablespoons of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water and stir it into the stew. Use the “Sauté” function to simmer for a few minutes until the stew thickens to your liking.
  6. Meal Prep and Serve: Store the beef stew in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat thoroughly before serving.

This hearty beef stew is rich in flavor and perfect for meal prepping during the colder months. It’s packed with vegetables, tender beef, and a savory broth, making it a comforting and nutritious meal that can be easily reheated throughout the week.

3. Instant Pot Quinoa & Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa (rinsed)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced thinly
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce or tamari (for gluten-free)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tbsp sesame seeds (optional)
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Quinoa: Place the quinoa and vegetable broth in the Instant Pot. Close the lid, set the valve to “Sealing,” and cook on high pressure for 1 minute. Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables: While the quinoa cooks, press the “Sauté” button and add sesame oil to the Instant Pot. Add the garlic and ginger, and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the Vegetables: Add the diced bell pepper, zucchini, carrots, and broccoli to the pot. Stir-fry the vegetables for 4-5 minutes, just until they’re tender yet still vibrant.
  4. Combine Quinoa and Vegetables: Add the cooked quinoa to the Instant Pot and stir to combine with the vegetables. Drizzle the soy sauce and rice vinegar over the mixture and toss everything together.
  5. Serve and Garnish: Sprinkle sesame seeds and chopped green onions over the stir-fry before serving. This quinoa and vegetable stir-fry can be stored in meal prep containers for up to 4 days.

This vibrant and nutritious quinoa stir-fry is perfect for meal prep, as it’s quick to make, filled with colorful vegetables, and rich in protein from the quinoa. It’s an excellent option for a light lunch or dinner.

4. Instant Pot Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups dried lentils (rinsed)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups fresh spinach (optional, for added nutrition)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Turn on the “Sauté” function on your Instant Pot. Add a bit of oil and sauté the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic for 3-4 minutes, until softened.
  2. Add Spices: Stir in the cumin, turmeric, coriander, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Sauté for another 1-2 minutes to toast the spices.
  3. Add Lentils and Liquids: Add the lentils, diced tomatoes (with juice), and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir to combine, ensuring that the lentils are submerged.
  4. Pressure Cook: Close the Instant Pot lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Set the pot to “Pressure Cook” on high for 15 minutes.
  5. Natural Release: After cooking, allow the pressure to release naturally for about 10 minutes, then quick release any remaining pressure.
  6. Add Spinach (Optional): Stir in fresh spinach for added color and nutrients. Let the soup sit for a few minutes for the spinach to wilt before serving.
  7. Meal Prep and Serve: Store in airtight containers for up to 5 days in the fridge. This lentil soup can also be frozen for up to 3 months.

This hearty and nourishing lentil soup is perfect for a meat-free meal that’s rich in protein and fiber. It’s simple to prepare, and it’s great for batch cooking to enjoy throughout the week.

5. Instant Pot Sweet Potato & Chickpea Curry

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can chickpeas (drained and rinsed)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 can coconut milk (13.5 oz)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Press the “Sauté” function on your Instant Pot. Add a tablespoon of oil and sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger for 3-4 minutes, until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add Spices: Stir in the curry powder, turmeric, cumin, salt, and pepper. Sauté for 1-2 minutes to allow the spices to bloom.
  3. Add Sweet Potatoes and Chickpeas: Add the cubed sweet potatoes, chickpeas, tomato paste, coconut milk, and vegetable broth to the Instant Pot. Stir everything together.
  4. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and set the valve to “Sealing.” Cook on high pressure for 10 minutes.
  5. Quick Release and Serve: After cooking, perform a quick release. Stir the curry and adjust the seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve.
  6. Meal Prep and Store: This curry can be stored in the fridge for up to 5 days or frozen for up to 3 months.

This vegan and gluten-free sweet potato and chickpea curry is bursting with flavor and nutrients. It’s perfect for meal prep, offering a delicious, comforting, and filling meal that’s both satisfying and healthy.

By incorporating these five Instant Pot recipes into your weekly meal prep, you can save time, reduce stress, and enjoy a variety of delicious, nutritious meals throughout the week. Each recipe is designed for ease of cooking, whether you’re a beginner or an experienced Instant Pot user. So, embrace the convenience and power of your Instant Pot to transform your meal prep game!
