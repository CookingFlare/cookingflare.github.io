The Ultimate Korean Dumplings: Kimchi Mandu Recipe


Kimchi mandu, a staple in Korean cuisine, is a type of dumpling that is both flavorful and versatile. These spicy, kimchi-filled dumplings are a perfect snack or appetizer for any occasion. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making kimchi mandu from scratch, using a combination of traditional Korean ingredients and modern cooking techniques.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup kimchi, chopped (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup toasted sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup grated ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions

To make the dumpling wrappers, combine the flour and salt in a large mixing bowl. Gradually add the warm water to the bowl, stirring with a wooden spoon until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 5-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a small egg. Roll out each ball into a thin circle, about 1/16 inch thick. Place a tablespoon of the kimchi filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape, and press the edges together to seal the dumpling.

Repeat the process with the remaining dough and filling. You should end up with around 20-25 dumplings.

Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the dumplings to the skillet, being careful not to overcrowd it. Cook the dumplings for 2-3 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and crispy.

In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, Gochujang, and 2 tablespoons of water. Pour the sauce over the dumplings and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, until the sauce has thickened and the dumplings are coated.

Serve the kimchi mandu hot, garnished with chopped cilantro if desired. You can also serve them with a dipping sauce made from equal parts soy sauce and Gochujang.

Tips and Variations

  • To freeze the dumplings, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze until solid. Then, transfer them to an airtight container or freezer bag and store for up to 3 months.
  • To cook the dumplings in advance, cook them for 2-3 minutes on each side, then let them cool completely. Store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. Reheat them in a skillet with a little oil before serving.
  • For a spicy kick, add some diced jalapenos or Korean chili flakes to the kimchi filling.
  • For a vegetarian version, substitute the kimchi with a mixture of sautéed mushrooms, spinach, and garlic.

Conclusion

Kimchi mandu is a delicious and versatile Korean dish that can be enjoyed at any time of the year. With its spicy kimchi filling and crispy dumpling wrapper, it’s a perfect snack or appetizer for any occasion. By following this recipe, you can make kimchi mandu from scratch and enjoy the authentic flavors of Korea in the comfort of your own home.
