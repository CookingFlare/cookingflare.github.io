Tonkotsu Ramen: Rich and Creamy Pork Broth


Tonkotsu ramen is a beloved Japanese dish known for its rich and creamy pork broth. This flavorful soup, paired with tender noodles, is a comfort food that warms the soul. In this blog post, we will explore the art of making tonkotsu ramen from scratch, including tips and tricks to achieve that perfect creamy broth.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of pork bones (such as trotters, neck bones, and/or ribs)
  • 1 onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 8 cups of water
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1 cup of sake
  • 2 tablespoons of miso paste
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • Salt, to taste
  • 4 servings of ramen noodles
  • Toppings of your choice (such as sliced pork belly, soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, and bamboo shoots)

Instructions

  1. Begin by preparing the pork bones. Rinse them under cold water to remove any impurities. Then, blanch the bones in boiling water for 5-10 minutes. This step helps to remove excess fat and any remaining impurities.

  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced ginger. Sauté until fragrant and the onion becomes translucent.

  3. Add the blanched pork bones to the pot and stir well to combine with the aromatics. Cook for a few minutes, allowing the bones to release their flavors.

  4. Pour in the water and chicken stock, ensuring the bones are fully submerged. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for at least 6 hours, or until the pork bones are tender and the broth has become rich and creamy.

  5. While the broth simmers, prepare the toppings and noodles. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Rinse them under cold water and set aside.

  6. In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, sake, miso paste, and sugar. Heat the mixture over low heat, stirring until the miso paste has fully dissolved. Set aside.

  7. Once the broth is ready, strain it through a fine-mesh sieve, discarding the solids. Return the broth to the pot and season with salt to taste. Keep the broth warm over low heat.

  8. To assemble the tonkotsu ramen bowls, divide the cooked noodles among serving bowls. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles, ensuring each bowl is generously filled. Drizzle the miso sauce over the broth, adjusting the amount to your taste.

  9. Garnish the ramen with your desired toppings, such as sliced pork belly, soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, and bamboo shoots. Get creative and add your own unique touches!

  10. Serve the tonkotsu ramen immediately while it’s piping hot. Enjoy the rich and creamy pork broth, the tender noodles, and the delightful combination of flavors.

Conclusion

Tonkotsu ramen is a labor of love, requiring time and patience to create that perfect, rich and creamy pork broth. With this recipe, you can now embark on your own culinary adventure and savor the flavors of this beloved Japanese dish. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aromatic broth and tender noodles transport you to the streets of Japan. Happy cooking!
