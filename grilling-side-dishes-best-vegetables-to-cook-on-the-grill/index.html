Grilling Side Dishes: Best Vegetables to Cook on the Grill


When it comes to grilling, the focus is often on the main course – whether it’s juicy steaks, tender chicken, or perfectly grilled fish. However, the side dishes are what can truly elevate the entire meal. Grilled vegetables, in particular, offer a delicious and healthy way to add variety and flavor to any outdoor cooking experience.

Whether you’re preparing a summer barbecue, a casual weeknight dinner, or a special gathering, grilling vegetables can provide that perfect balance of smoky flavor and caramelized goodness. Let’s dive into some of the best vegetables to cook on the grill and explore how to make the most of their unique textures and tastes.

Why Grill Vegetables?

Grilling vegetables is not only a healthy option, but it also brings out their natural sweetness and deepens their flavor. The high heat from the grill creates a beautiful char while retaining the vegetable’s nutrients. Grilled vegetables can be a perfect side dish on their own or complement a wide range of main courses. Grilling is versatile – you can choose to cook them directly on the grates, use skewers, or even opt for a grilling basket to prevent smaller items from falling through.

Besides the obvious flavor benefits, grilling vegetables is a quick process. Once prepared and seasoned, they only need a few minutes on the grill, making them an ideal choice when you need to prepare a side dish fast.

Key Tips for Grilling Vegetables

Before we dive into the specific vegetables, here are a few tips that will help you achieve perfectly grilled vegetables:

  • Cut Consistently: To ensure even cooking, try to cut vegetables into uniform pieces. Larger vegetables like peppers or eggplants should be cut into thicker slices, while smaller vegetables like zucchini or mushrooms should be halved or quartered.
  • Preheat the Grill: Always preheat the grill to medium-high heat before adding the vegetables. This ensures that the vegetables cook evenly and get a nice sear.
  • Use Oil: Toss vegetables in olive oil or a flavored oil to prevent them from sticking to the grill. Oil also helps them caramelize and adds richness.
  • Seasoning: While salt and pepper are the basics, consider experimenting with garlic, herbs, balsamic vinegar, lemon zest, or your favorite spice mixes for added flavor.
  • Watch the Time: Different vegetables cook at different rates. Keep an eye on them and flip them as needed to avoid burning. Vegetables with a high water content (like zucchini) tend to cook faster than denser vegetables like potatoes.

1. Grilled Corn on the Cob

Corn on the cob is a classic and highly popular vegetable to grill. The smoky flavor from the grill enhances its natural sweetness, making it the perfect complement to many grilled meats.

How to Grill Corn on the Cob:

  • Prep: You can grill corn either in the husk or without it. Grilling corn with the husk on will steam the kernels and preserve their moisture. For a charred effect, remove the husks and grill the corn directly on the grates.
  • Seasoning: Before grilling, brush the corn with melted butter or olive oil. You can also sprinkle some salt, pepper, paprika, or chili powder to give it an extra kick.
  • Grill Time: Grill the corn for about 10–15 minutes, turning every 3–4 minutes to ensure even charring. The kernels should be tender and slightly caramelized when done.

2. Grilled Asparagus

Grilled asparagus is a quick and easy side dish that pairs wonderfully with almost anything. Its tender, crispy texture and slightly smoky flavor make it a great addition to any meal.

How to Grill Asparagus:

  • Prep: Trim the tough ends of the asparagus stalks. If the stalks are thick, consider slicing them lengthwise to ensure they cook more evenly.
  • Seasoning: Drizzle with olive oil, salt, and pepper. You can also add a squeeze of lemon juice or a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese after grilling.
  • Grill Time: Grill asparagus for 4-6 minutes, turning them occasionally until they are tender and have grill marks.

3. Grilled Bell Peppers

Bell peppers are naturally sweet, and grilling them brings out their rich flavor. They also add vibrant color to your grill plate, making your meal even more inviting.

How to Grill Bell Peppers:

  • Prep: Cut the bell peppers in half, remove the seeds, and brush the cut sides with olive oil.
  • Seasoning: For extra flavor, sprinkle with salt, pepper, garlic powder, or smoked paprika.
  • Grill Time: Place the peppers cut side down on the grill. Grill for about 6–8 minutes, turning occasionally until the skin is charred and the flesh is tender.

4. Grilled Zucchini and Summer Squash

Zucchini and summer squash are mild-flavored vegetables that take on a wonderfully smoky taste when grilled. Their tender texture and vibrant color make them perfect for summer cookouts.

How to Grill Zucchini and Summer Squash:

  • Prep: Slice the zucchini and squash into thick rounds or long strips to ensure they don’t fall apart on the grill.
  • Seasoning: Toss the slices with olive oil, salt, pepper, and a dash of Italian seasoning or fresh basil.
  • Grill Time: Grill for 3-5 minutes per side, flipping until both sides are nicely charred.

5. Grilled Eggplant

Eggplant has a meaty texture, making it an excellent vegetable for grilling. When cooked properly, eggplant becomes tender, juicy, and infused with smoky flavors, making it a hearty side dish.

How to Grill Eggplant:

  • Prep: Slice the eggplant into rounds or lengthwise. Salt the slices and let them sit for about 20 minutes to draw out excess moisture. Pat them dry before grilling.
  • Seasoning: Brush with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic. You can also drizzle balsamic vinegar over the slices after grilling for an extra layer of flavor.
  • Grill Time: Grill for 4-6 minutes on each side, until the eggplant is soft and lightly charred.

6. Grilled Sweet Potatoes

Sweet potatoes are a fantastic option for grilling. Their natural sweetness intensifies when grilled, and their firm texture allows them to hold up well on the grates.

How to Grill Sweet Potatoes:

  • Prep: Slice the sweet potatoes into thick rounds or wedges. Parboiling the slices for 5–10 minutes before grilling can help ensure they cook evenly and become tender inside while still crisp on the outside.
  • Seasoning: Brush with olive oil, salt, and pepper, and consider adding cinnamon, paprika, or cayenne pepper for a sweet-and-spicy twist.
  • Grill Time: Grill for 5–7 minutes per side until the sweet potatoes are tender and lightly caramelized.

7. Grilled Mushrooms

Mushrooms are one of the easiest vegetables to grill, and they’re packed with umami flavor. They grill quickly and develop a savory, smoky profile that’s irresistible.

How to Grill Mushrooms:

  • Prep: Use whole mushrooms or large mushroom caps for grilling. If you’re grilling small mushrooms like cremini or button mushrooms, consider skewering them to prevent them from falling through the grill grates.
  • Seasoning: Toss the mushrooms with olive oil, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  • Grill Time: Grill for 4–5 minutes per side or until tender and juicy.

8. Grilled Onions

Grilled onions become sweet and tender as they caramelize, adding a great flavor dimension to any dish. The slight char enhances their natural sweetness, making them a great complement to meats or as a standalone side dish.

How to Grill Onions:

  • Prep: Slice onions into thick rings or wedges. If using large onions, you can even skewer them to make flipping easier.
  • Seasoning: Brush with olive oil and sprinkle with salt, pepper, and a pinch of brown sugar to help with caramelization.
  • Grill Time: Grill the onion slices for 6–8 minutes on each side until they’re soft and charred.

9. Grilled Broccoli

Grilled broccoli offers a different way to enjoy this nutrient-packed vegetable. When grilled, broccoli becomes crispy and slightly smoky, enhancing its flavor.

How to Grill Broccoli:

  • Prep: Cut broccoli into florets. If they’re small, you might want to use a grilling basket or skewer them to prevent them from falling through the grates.
  • Seasoning: Toss the broccoli florets in olive oil, salt, pepper, and a bit of lemon juice for freshness.
  • Grill Time: Grill for 4–6 minutes, turning frequently until the broccoli is tender and lightly charred.

10. Grilled Cauliflower

Cauliflower is a versatile vegetable that can be transformed into a satisfying grilled dish. Grilling cauliflower brings out its mild, nutty flavor while giving it a crispy texture on the outside.

How to Grill Cauliflower:

  • Prep: Cut the cauliflower into thick steaks or large florets. If using steaks, make sure they’re thick enough to hold together on the grill.
  • Seasoning: Brush with olive oil, season with salt, pepper, and a touch of turmeric or curry powder for added flavor.
  • Grill Time: Grill for 4–5 minutes per side until the cauliflower is tender and has a golden-brown crust.

Conclusion

Grilling vegetables is one of the best ways to bring out their natural flavors, create complex textures, and add variety to your grilling repertoire. Whether you’re using zucchini, eggplant, peppers, or corn, these vegetables are simple to prepare and incredibly versatile. With the right seasoning and grilling techniques, you can turn any vegetable into a mouthwatering side dish that perfectly complements your grilled meats and other favorites.

Next time you fire up the grill, be sure to give these vegetables a try. They’ll not only add color and flavor to your plate but also make your meal healthier and more satisfying. Happy grilling!
