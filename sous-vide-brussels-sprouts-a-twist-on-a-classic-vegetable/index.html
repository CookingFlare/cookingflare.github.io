Sous Vide Brussels Sprouts: A Twist on a Classic Vegetable


Brussels sprouts have long been a popular side dish on dinner tables around the world. With their distinctive flavor and versatility, they have become a staple in many households. But have you ever considered taking this classic vegetable to the next level by cooking them sous vide? In this recipe, we will explore how to prepare perfectly tender and flavorful sous vide Brussels sprouts that will elevate your dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your water bath to 185°F (85°C).

  2. While the water bath is heating up, prepare your Brussels sprouts by trimming off any excess stems and removing any loose outer leaves.

  3. In a large bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Make sure each sprout is evenly coated with the mixture.

  4. Transfer the seasoned Brussels sprouts to a vacuum-sealed bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a ziplock bag and remove as much air as possible by using the water displacement method.

  5. Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, carefully place the bag of Brussels sprouts into the water. Ensure that the bag is fully submerged.

  6. Allow the Brussels sprouts to cook in the water bath for 45 minutes to 1 hour. This longer cooking time will ensure that the sprouts become tender and develop a rich flavor.

  7. After the cooking time has elapsed, carefully remove the bag from the water bath and transfer the Brussels sprouts to a serving dish.

  8. For an optional finishing touch, you can sear the cooked Brussels sprouts in a hot skillet with a little olive oil for a crispy exterior. This step adds an extra layer of texture and flavor to the dish.

  9. Serve the sous vide Brussels sprouts immediately as a delightful side dish to complement your main course. They pair well with roasted meats, grilled fish, or even as a standalone vegetarian option.

Tips and Variations

  • For a burst of citrus flavor, add the zest of one lemon to the olive oil and garlic mixture before tossing it with the Brussels sprouts.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to create unique flavor profiles. Try adding smoked paprika, cumin, or thyme for a twist on the classic recipe.
  • If you prefer a bit of sweetness, drizzle the cooked Brussels sprouts with a balsamic glaze or honey before serving.
  • To save time, you can prepare the seasoned Brussels sprouts in advance and store them in the refrigerator until you’re ready to cook them sous vide.

Conclusion

By cooking Brussels sprouts sous vide, you unlock a whole new level of tenderness and flavor that is sure to impress even the most discerning palates. The precise temperature control and extended cooking time ensure that the sprouts are perfectly cooked throughout, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture. So, give this twist on a classic vegetable a try and elevate your dining experience with sous vide Brussels sprouts. Your taste buds will thank you!
