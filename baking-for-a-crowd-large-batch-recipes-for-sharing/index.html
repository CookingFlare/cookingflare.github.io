Baking for a Crowd: Large Batch Recipes for Sharing


When it comes to baking, everyone loves to share the love! Whether it’s a birthday party, holiday gathering, or just a casual get-together, it’s always a delight to have mouth-watering treats to enjoy and share with others. But how do you bake for a crowd? Fear not! We have gathered a few large batch recipes to bring everyone together through the magic of baking.

1. Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

What is a crowd gathering without the ultimate comfort food – chocolate chip cookies? Here is a simple, but delightful recipe that serves 24.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons chia seed
  • 4 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven 375°F (190°C). Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, salt, and chia seed.
  3. In another larger bowl, cream the butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and vanilla extract until light and fluffy.
  4. Gradually stir in the dry ingredients until combined. Then fold in the chocolate chips.
  5. Drop generous cookies scoop balls of dough onto prepared baking sheets.
  6. Bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes until golden brown and soft in the centers.
  7. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheets for at least 5 minutes before transferring to wire racks to cool completely. Now you’re ready to share your delicious creation!

2. Spiced Sweet Potato Blondies

If you’re looking for an easy and tasty crowd-pleaser, these spiced sweet potato blondies are sure to do the trick. They serve a generous 30 treat sizes!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 cups mashed cooked sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup chopped toasted pecans

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 10 x 15-inch jelly roll pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, beat the butter and brown sugar together until creamy. Add the eggs and vanilla extract, and continue to mix.
  4. Stir in the sweet potatoes and pecans. Then, blend in the dry ingredients.
  5. Spread the batter evenly into the prepared pan.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the blondies to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before slicing into bars. Cool completely on wire racks before serving.

3. Cheesy Pull-Apart Bread

If you love melty, cheesy goodness, check out this crowd-pleasing cheesy pull-apart bread. The large batch serves around 20 people.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups warm water
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 weight active dry yeast
  • 3 1/2 to 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit for about 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix in the flour, salt, and olive oil. Then add the grated cheddar and Parmesan cheese.
  3. Flour a work surface, and turn the dough out onto it. Pat the dough into a 12-inch circle.
  4. Cut the dough circle into 20 wedges.
  5. On a parchment-lined baking sheet, make a liberal mixture of melted butter and yeast. Brush the butter over the dough wedges.
  6. Roll up each wedge, starting at the wider end, and place it into the prepared pan, pointy-end down.
  7. Cover loosely with a tea towel and let rise in a warm place for about 30 minutes, until doubled in size.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  9. Bake the bread for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  10. Allow the bread to cool for at least 10 minutes before pulling apart and serving.

These recipes will make your gatherings even merrier, and as you share them with friends and family, the love will only grow stronger. So, gather your supplies and whisk up some magic in the kitchen today!
