How to Make a Sausage and Pepper Pizza from Scratch


When it comes to homemade pizza, few combinations are as satisfying as the hearty and flavorful duo of sausage and peppers. This sausage and pepper pizza brings together savory, spicy sausage, sweet peppers, and melty cheese, all nestled on a perfect crust. Best of all, making it from scratch allows you to control the quality of ingredients, and the result is a pizza that’s better than anything you could get from a delivery service.

If you’ve been looking for a way to elevate your pizza-making game, making sausage and pepper pizza from scratch is a fantastic choice. Whether you’re a seasoned pizza maker or trying it for the first time, this recipe offers a simple yet satisfying way to make a delicious, homemade pizza.

Ingredients for the Sausage and Pepper Pizza

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for rolling)
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (about 2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (around 110°F)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (plus more for greasing)
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • A pinch of red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)

For the Toppings:

  • 2 Italian sausages (preferably mild or spicy, depending on your preference)
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (for cooking the vegetables)
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)
  • A drizzle of olive oil (for finishing)

Step 1: Preparing the Pizza Dough

The first step to creating your sausage and pepper pizza is preparing the dough. While it may take some time to rise, making pizza dough from scratch is quite simple and yields a light, airy crust that will form the perfect base for your toppings.

  1. Activate the Yeast
    In a small bowl, combine the warm water and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast on top and stir to dissolve. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy. If the yeast doesn’t foam, it may be expired, so it’s important to start with fresh yeast for best results.

  2. Mix the Dough
    In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture along with the olive oil. Using a spoon or your hands, stir until the mixture begins to come together into a sticky dough.

  3. Knead the Dough
    Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, adding flour as necessary, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour on the surface and continue kneading.

  4. Let the Dough Rise
    Lightly oil the mixing bowl and place the dough back inside. Cover the bowl with a damp towel or plastic wrap. Let the dough rise in a warm spot for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until it has doubled in size. This step is crucial for getting a soft, airy crust.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

While the dough is rising, it’s a great time to prepare the pizza sauce. Homemade pizza sauce adds freshness and depth to the pizza, and it’s super simple to make.

  1. Sauté Garlic and Oil
    In a saucepan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.

  2. Add Tomatoes and Seasonings
    Stir in the crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. Simmer the sauce on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

  3. Blend the Sauce (Optional)
    For a smoother texture, you can blend the sauce with an immersion blender or transfer it to a regular blender. This step is optional, but it will give you a more uniform sauce for your pizza.

  4. Set Aside
    Once the sauce has thickened and the flavors have developed, remove it from heat and set it aside. You want to let it cool slightly before spreading it on the dough.

Step 3: Prepare the Toppings

Now that the dough is rising and the sauce is simmering, you can prepare the toppings. Freshly sautéed vegetables and sausage will bring your pizza to life.

  1. Cook the Sausage
    Remove the sausage from its casing and crumble it into a skillet. Cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes, breaking it apart with a spoon, until it’s fully cooked and browned. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. Sauté the Peppers and Onions
    In the same skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the sliced bell peppers and onions and sauté for about 5-7 minutes, or until they begin to soften and caramelize. You want them to be tender but not mushy. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.

Step 4: Assembling the Sausage and Pepper Pizza

Once the dough has risen and your toppings are ready, it’s time to assemble the pizza. This is the fun part where you get to be creative and build the pizza to your liking.

  1. Preheat the Oven
    Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat as well. A pizza stone helps to create a crispier crust, but a baking sheet will work just fine if you don’t have one.

  2. Shape the Dough
    Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a floured surface. Roll the dough out with a rolling pin until it’s about 12 inches in diameter. If you prefer a thicker crust, you can make it slightly smaller. If you like a thinner crust, roll it out to a larger size.

  3. Add the Sauce
    Spread a thin layer of the homemade pizza sauce onto the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust. Use a spoon or the back of a ladle to spread the sauce evenly.

  4. Layer the Toppings
    Begin by sprinkling a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce. Next, distribute the crumbled sausage evenly over the cheese. Follow with the sautéed peppers and onions, arranging them in an even layer.

  5. Finish with Cheese
    Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese on top of the other toppings. This adds a nice salty and nutty flavor to the pizza.

  6. Bake the Pizza
    Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (using a pizza stone or a baking sheet) and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the sausage and pepper pizza is out of the oven, give it a few minutes to cool slightly. Then, cut it into slices and serve. If you like, garnish with a few fresh basil leaves and drizzle a bit of olive oil over the top for extra flavor.

The combination of the spicy sausage, sweet peppers, and melted cheese on the crisp homemade crust is absolutely irresistible. Each bite brings a burst of savory goodness, and it’s sure to become a favorite in your pizza-making repertoire.

Tips for the Perfect Sausage and Pepper Pizza

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: The key to a flavorful pizza is using fresh, high-quality ingredients. Fresh mozzarella, homemade sauce, and quality sausage make a noticeable difference in the taste.
  • Sausage Choices: You can choose between mild or spicy sausage depending on your personal preference. If you like a bit of heat, opt for spicy Italian sausage or add a few red pepper flakes to the sauce.
  • Vegetable Variations: While bell peppers are traditional, feel free to experiment with other vegetables such as mushrooms, zucchini, or even spinach. The versatility of this recipe makes it easy to adjust to your tastes.
  • Make Ahead: You can make the pizza dough and sauce ahead of time and store them in the fridge for up to 3 days. This allows you to save time on the day you want to make pizza.
  • Crust Variations: If you prefer a gluten-free option, you can use a gluten-free pizza dough, which can be found at most grocery stores.

Final Thoughts

Making a sausage and pepper pizza from scratch is a rewarding experience that not only fills your kitchen with delightful aromas but also offers a satisfying meal for family and friends. Whether you’re enjoying a quiet evening at home or hosting a pizza party, this recipe will be a hit every time. The combination of rich flavors, fresh ingredients, and the satisfaction of creating your own pizza from scratch is something that can’t be beat.

Happy pizza making!
