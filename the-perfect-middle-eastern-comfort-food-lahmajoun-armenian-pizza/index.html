The Perfect Middle Eastern Comfort Food: Lahmajoun (Armenian Pizza)


If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying comfort food, look no further than Lahmajoun, also known as Armenian Pizza. This Middle Eastern dish is a perfect combination of flavors, spices, and textures that will leave you wanting more. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of Lahmajoun, the ingredients you’ll need, and a step-by-step guide to making this mouthwatering dish.

History of Lahmajoun

Lahmajoun has a rich history that dates back centuries. It originated in the Middle East and quickly spread throughout the region, becoming a beloved staple in Armenian and Turkish cuisines. The word “Lahmajoun” comes from the Arabic word “lahm bi’ajin,” which translates to “meat with dough.” This dish traditionally consists of a thin, crispy dough topped with a flavorful mixture of ground meat, vegetables, and aromatic spices.

Ingredients

To make the perfect Lahmajoun, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground lamb or beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground lamb or beef, chopped onion, diced tomatoes, red bell pepper, minced garlic, tomato paste, olive oil, ground cumin, ground paprika, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

  3. Divide the dough into small, golf ball-sized portions. On a lightly floured surface, roll out each portion into a thin circle, about 6-8 inches in diameter. Place the rolled dough onto the prepared baking sheet.

  4. Spoon a generous amount of the meat mixture onto each dough circle, spreading it evenly to the edges. Make sure not to overload the dough, as it may become soggy during baking.

  5. Bake the Lahmajoun in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and crispy.

  6. Remove the Lahmajoun from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side.

Serving Suggestions

Lahmajoun is traditionally enjoyed as a finger food, perfect for sharing with family and friends. You can serve it as a main dish or as an appetizer alongside a refreshing salad or a side of tzatziki sauce. The tangy flavor of the lemon wedges complements the rich and savory Lahmajoun perfectly.

Conclusion

Lahmajoun, the Middle Eastern comfort food also known as Armenian Pizza, is a delightful dish that brings together the flavors of the region in a unique and satisfying way. Whether you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine or looking to try something new, Lahmajoun is sure to impress. So gather your ingredients, roll out the dough, and enjoy the deliciousness of this beloved dish.
