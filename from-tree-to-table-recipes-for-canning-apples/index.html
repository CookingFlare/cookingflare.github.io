From Tree to Table: Recipes for Canning Apples


Are you a fan of the delicious, crisp taste of apples? Do you find yourself overwhelmed with the abundance of apples from your backyard tree? Well, look no further. In this blog post, we will explore the process of canning apples and provide you with some mouthwatering recipes to enjoy throughout the year. So grab your apron and let’s get started!

Why Can Apples?

Canning apples is a fantastic way to preserve their freshness and flavor. By canning, you can enjoy the taste of apples long after the harvest season is over. It also allows you to have a ready supply of apples for pies, sauces, and snacks, whenever you desire. Plus, it’s a great way to reduce food waste and make the most of your apple harvest.

The Canning Process

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s go over the basic steps of canning apples. Here’s a quick overview:

  1. Preparation: Start by selecting ripe, firm apples. Wash them thoroughly and remove any blemishes or bruises. Peel, core, and slice the apples according to your recipe.

  2. Sanitization: It’s crucial to ensure your canning equipment, including jars, lids, and utensils, are clean and sterile. Boil the jars and lids to sanitize them properly.

  3. Cooking: In a large pot, combine the prepared apples with water, sugar, and any desired spices or flavorings. Cook the mixture until the apples are tender but not mushy.

  4. Filling the Jars: Carefully pack the hot apple mixture into the sterilized jars, leaving some headspace. Wipe the jar rims, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands.

  5. Processing: Place the sealed jars in a canning pot filled with boiling water. Process them according to the recipe’s instructions for the recommended time. This step ensures the jars are properly sealed and the contents are safe for long-term storage.

  6. Cooling and Storage: Once the processing time is complete, carefully remove the jars from the canning pot and place them on a towel. Allow them to cool completely before checking the seals and storing them in a cool, dark place.

Recipes for Canning Apples

1. Classic Applesauce

Ingredients:

  • 6 pounds of peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the sliced apples, water, sugar, and cinnamon.
  2. Cook the mixture over medium heat until the apples are soft and easily mashed.
  3. Using a potato masher or immersion blender, puree the cooked apples until smooth.
  4. Ladle the hot applesauce into sterilized jars, leaving 1/2 inch of headspace.
  5. Remove any air bubbles by running a knife or spatula along the sides of the jars.
  6. Wipe the jar rims, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands.
  7. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 20 minutes.
  8. Remove the jars from the canning pot and let them cool completely before storing.

2. Spiced Apple Pie Filling

Ingredients:

  • 10 pounds of peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 4 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground cloves
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the sliced apples, sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook the mixture over medium heat until the apples are tender and the sauce has thickened.
  3. Ladle the hot apple pie filling into sterilized jars, leaving 1 inch of headspace.
  4. Remove any air bubbles by running a knife or spatula along the sides of the jars.
  5. Wipe the jar rims, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands.
  6. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 25 minutes.
  7. Remove the jars from the canning pot and let them cool completely before storing.

3. Caramel Apple Butter

Ingredients:

  • 8 pounds of peeled, cored, and sliced apples
  • 2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice.
  2. Cook the mixture over medium heat until the apples are very tender and can be easily mashed.
  3. Using a potato masher or immersion blender, puree the cooked apples until smooth.
  4. Return the mixture to the pot and simmer over low heat, stirring frequently, until the apple butter thickens.
  5. Ladle the hot apple butter into sterilized jars, leaving 1/4 inch of headspace.
  6. Remove any air bubbles by running a knife or spatula along the sides of the jars.
  7. Wipe the jar rims, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands.
  8. Process the jars in a boiling water bath for 10 minutes.
  9. Remove the jars from the canning pot and let them cool completely before storing.

Enjoy the Fruits of Your Labor!

Congratulations! You’ve successfully canned a variety of apple recipes that will keep you satisfied throughout the year. Whether you enjoy the classic applesauce, the flavorful apple pie filling, or the indulgent caramel apple butter, these canned delights will bring the taste of fresh apples to your table whenever you desire.

Remember to label your jars with the recipe and date, and store them in a cool, dark place. Enjoy the fruits of your labor and share your delicious creations with family and friends. Happy canning!

Note: Canning safety is essential. Always follow reputable canning recipes and guidelines to ensure food safety.
