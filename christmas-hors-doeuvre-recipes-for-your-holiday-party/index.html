Christmas Hors d'Oeuvre Recipes for Your Holiday Party


The holiday season is finally here, and with it comes the joyful hustle of party planning, especially the food. While the centerpiece meals get their due attention, it’s the hors d’oeuvres that truly set the festive tone, offer variety, and keep guests mingling happily with a bite in hand. Whether you’re hosting a Christmas soiree or a cozy gathering, a well-curated selection of appetizers can elevate your event and leave your guests impressed.

Below is an assortment of creative, flavorful, and easy-to-make Christmas hors d’oeuvre recipes, each with a festive twist. These recipes balance classic flavors with holiday flair, perfect for any palate. Let’s dive into deliciousness and get your holiday party started on the right foot.

1. Cranberry Brie Puff Pastry Bites

A blend of sweet, tart cranberries paired with creamy brie, wrapped in buttery puff pastry, makes for a heavenly bite-sized appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 6 oz brie cheese, cut into small cubes
  • ½ cup cranberry sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • Fresh rosemary, finely chopped (optional)
  • One egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and cut into small squares (about 2x2 inches).
  3. Place a cube of brie in the center of each square.
  4. Add a dollop of cranberry sauce on top of the brie.
  5. Sprinkle finely chopped rosemary if using for a subtle festive aroma.
  6. Fold the corners of the pastry squares toward the center and gently press to seal.
  7. Brush the tops with the beaten egg to achieve a golden finish.
  8. Arrange on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, and bake for 15-20 minutes or until puffed and golden.

Serve warm to enjoy the melted brie with the burst of cranberry sweetness. These are great crowd-pleasers and perfect for guests to nibble as they arrive.

2. Prosciutto-Wrapped Asparagus with Garlic Aioli

This is a simple yet elegant hors d’oeuvre that combines crunchy asparagus wrapped in savory prosciutto, served alongside a creamy garlic aioli dip.

Ingredients:

  • 24 asparagus spears, trimmed
  • 12 slices prosciutto, halved lengthwise
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Garlic Aioli:

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (205°C).
  2. Toss asparagus in olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Wrap each asparagus spear with a half-slice of prosciutto.
  4. Place on a baking tray and roast for 12-15 minutes until prosciutto is crispy and asparagus tender.
  5. Meanwhile, whisk together garlic, mayonnaise, lemon juice, and salt to make the aioli.
  6. Serve the prosciutto-wrapped asparagus with a small bowl of garlic aioli for dipping.

This dish offers a wonderful balance of smoky, salty, and creamy that enhances the natural freshness of asparagus—a great way to add something green to your spread.

3. Mini Beef Wellington Bites

Miniature renditions of the classic Beef Wellington, these elegant bites combine tender beef, mushroom duxelles, and puff pastry into perfect party-sized portions.

Ingredients:

  • ½ lb (225g) beef tenderloin, cut into 24 small cubes
  • 1 package puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 cup cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten for egg wash

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt butter and sauté shallots until softened.
  2. Add mushrooms and cook until moisture evaporates completely; season with salt and pepper. Allow to cool.
  3. Season beef cubes with salt and pepper, sear in a hot pan for 30 seconds on each side just to brown the exterior but keep the inside rare. Let cool.
  4. Roll out puff pastry and cut into 24 squares.
  5. Spread a small amount of mushroom duxelles on each square, place a beef cube on top, brush with Dijon mustard.
  6. Wrap pastry around the beef tightly, sealing the edges, then place seam-side down on a baking sheet.
  7. Brush with egg wash and bake at 400°F (205°C) for 12-15 minutes or until golden.

Serve these warm with a dab of horseradish cream or a red wine reduction for an elevated flavor profile. They’re guaranteed to impress the meat lovers at your party.

4. Caprese Skewers with Balsamic Glaze

Colorful, fresh, and vibrant, these bite-sized Caprese skewers combine sweet cherry tomatoes, fresh mozzarella balls, and fragrant basil.

Ingredients:

  • 24 cherry tomatoes
  • 24 small fresh mozzarella balls (bocconcini)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • Balsamic glaze (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. Assemble each skewer by threading a cherry tomato, a basil leaf, and a mozzarella ball.
  2. Arrange on a platter, sprinkle lightly with salt and pepper.
  3. Drizzle balsamic glaze over just before serving.

This appetizer is not just delicious but visually appealing with festive reds, greens, and whites. It’s refreshing palate-friendly fare amid richer dishes.

5. Spiced Pumpkin Hummus with Pita Chips

Give your appetizer spread a seasonal spin with creamy pumpkin hummus infused with warm spices, perfect with homemade crispy pita chips.

Ingredients:

For Pumpkin Hummus:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ½ cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Pita Chips:

  • 3 pita breads, cut into triangles
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Toss pita triangles with olive oil, smoked paprika, and salt; spread on a baking tray.
  3. Bake for 10-12 minutes until golden and crisp.
  4. For hummus, combine chickpeas, pumpkin puree, tahini, garlic, cumin, cinnamon, nutmeg, olive oil, and lemon juice in a food processor.
  5. Blend until smooth, season with salt and pepper.
  6. Transfer to a serving bowl and drizzle with a little olive oil.

Serve these colorful chips and the spiced pumpkin hummus side-by-side for a festive, nutritious snack that adds a cozy autumn-winter vibe.

6. Smoked Salmon and Dill Cucumber Bites

These delicate bites offer a fresh, light alternative to heavier appetizers, featuring silky smoked salmon atop crunchy cucumber slices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large cucumber, sliced into ¼ inch rounds
  • 6 oz smoked salmon, cut into small pieces
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix cream cheese, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread a dollop of the cream cheese mixture onto each cucumber slice.
  3. Top with a folded piece of smoked salmon.
  4. Garnish with a small sprig of dill or a lemon zest twist.

These bite-sized morsels are as pleasing to the eye as they are to the palate, perfect for guests who appreciate freshness and sophistication at any holiday gathering.

7. Stuffed Mushrooms with Sausage and Cheese

Rich mushrooms stuffed with savory sausage, herbs, and melted cheese embody a warm, inviting flavor perfect for cold weather celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 24 button mushrooms, stems removed and reserved
  • 8 oz Italian sausage, casing removed
  • ½ cup cream cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Finely chop reserved mushroom stems.
  3. In a skillet, cook sausage until browned, breaking it apart.
  4. Add garlic and chopped mushroom stems; cook until soft.
  5. Stir in cream cheese, Parmesan, parsley, salt, and pepper. Remove from heat.
  6. Spoon mixture into mushroom caps.
  7. Bake for 15-18 minutes until browned on top and mushrooms are tender.

These savory bites are hearty and satisfying, appealing especially to those who enjoy robust, meaty appetizers with a hint of creaminess.

Party Planning Tips for Hors d’Oeuvres

  • Mix textures and flavors: Combine crunchy, creamy, salty, and sweet bites to keep your guests intrigued.
  • Balance vegetarian and meat options: Make sure everyone has choices, whether they prefer plant-based or carnivorous treats.
  • Prep ahead: Many hors d’oeuvres can be prepared partially or fully in advance, making party day less hectic.
  • Presentation matters: Use festive platters, garnishes like fresh herbs or pomegranate seeds, and arrange items neatly to bring a holiday sparkle.
  • Consider portion size: Keep hors d’oeuvres bite-sized so guests can sample many without spoiling their appetite.

Hosting a Christmas party and creating an array of appetizing hors d’oeuvres is an art and a joy. From flaky puff pastry treats to fresh vegetable bites, this collection ensures variety, flavor, and festive appeal. Enjoy crafting these recipes and savor the happy smiles and compliments from your guests—it’s the perfect way to spread holiday cheer.

Happy holidays and happy cooking!
