Easy Recipes for Cooking with Friends in College


College life is a whirlwind of classes, late-night study sessions, and unforgettable memories. Amidst the chaos, cooking can be a fun and rewarding way to bond with friends, unwind, and enjoy delicious meals together. Whether you’re a novice in the kitchen or a seasoned chef, these easy recipes are perfect for whipping up with your roommates or friends. Let’s dive into some delightful dishes that will not only satisfy your hunger but also create lasting memories in your college years!

1. One-Pot Pasta Wonder

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz. spaghetti or your favorite pasta
  • 1 can (14.5 oz.) diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups fresh spinach (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the pasta, diced tomatoes (with juice), sliced onion, minced garlic, broth, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a simmer. Stir occasionally and cook until the pasta is al dente and the liquid is mostly absorbed, about 10-12 minutes.
  3. If using, add the fresh spinach and stir until wilted.
  4. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese.

Why It’s Great:

This one-pot wonder is perfect for a group because it requires minimal cleanup and allows everyone to dig in right from the pot. Plus, you can customize it with whatever vegetables or proteins you have on hand!

2. Taco Night Extravaganza

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. ground beef, chicken, or turkey
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 12 taco shells (hard or soft)
  • 1 can refried beans (optional)
  • Toppings: shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, sour cream, guacamole, salsa, and jalapeños

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the ground meat over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Stir in the taco seasoning and a splash of water, cooking until thickened.
  3. Heat the taco shells according to package instructions.
  4. Set up a taco bar with all the toppings and let everyone assemble their own tacos!

Why It’s Great:

Taco night is a classic college favorite! It’s interactive, allowing everyone to customize their meal, and it’s a great excuse to gather and enjoy each other’s company over good food.

3. Breakfast-for-Dinner Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup diced vegetables (bell peppers, onions, spinach, etc.)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, feta, or your choice)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: cooked bacon or sausage

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs and milk. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a large oven-safe skillet, sauté the diced vegetables until tender. If using, add cooked bacon or sausage.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables, then sprinkle cheese on top.
  5. Cook on the stovetop for 5 minutes until the edges start to set. Then transfer to the oven and bake for 20-25 minutes until fully set and golden on top.
  6. Slice and serve warm.

Why It’s Great:

Frittatas are incredibly versatile and can accommodate whatever leftovers you have in the fridge. They also make for a hearty meal that’s perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner!

4. DIY Pizza Party

Ingredients:

  • 2 pre-made pizza crusts or dough
  • 1 cup pizza sauce
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Toppings: pepperoni, sliced bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, olives, etc.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven according to the pizza crust package instructions.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough (if using) on a floured surface and transfer to a baking sheet or pizza stone.
  3. Spread a layer of pizza sauce over the crust, followed by a generous amount of cheese.
  4. Let everyone add their favorite toppings to the pizza.
  5. Bake according to package instructions until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

Why It’s Great:

A DIY pizza night is not only delicious but also a fantastic way to unleash your creativity in the kitchen. Everyone can make their own unique pizza, making it a fun and personalized dining experience.

5. Cheesy Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend)
  • Optional fillings: cooked chicken, beans, corn, diced bell peppers, or jalapeños
  • Salsa or guacamole for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place one tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle half of the cheese evenly over it. Add any desired fillings, then top with another tortilla.
  3. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until the bottom is golden brown, then carefully flip and cook the other side until golden and the cheese is melted.
  4. Remove from the skillet, let cool slightly, then slice into wedges. Serve with salsa or guacamole.

Why It’s Great:

Quesadillas are quick to make and incredibly satisfying. They’re perfect for a late-night snack or a casual dinner, plus you can customize them based on what everyone likes.

6. Colorful Buddha Bowls

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa or brown rice
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups assorted vegetables (carrots, cucumbers, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup hummus or tahini dressing
  • Fresh herbs (parsley, cilantro) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa or brown rice according to package instructions.
  2. While the grain is cooking, prepare the vegetables by slicing and dicing them into bite-sized pieces.
  3. In bowls, layer cooked quinoa, chickpeas, fresh veggies, and avocado.
  4. Drizzle with hummus or tahini dressing and garnish with fresh herbs.

Why It’s Great:

Buddha bowls are healthy, colorful, and packed with nutrients. They allow for plenty of customization based on personal preferences, making them ideal for a group of friends with varying tastes.

7. Sweet Treat: No-Bake Chocolate Oatmeal Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine sugar, milk, cocoa powder, and butter. Stir until the mixture reaches a boil.
  2. Boil for 1 minute, then remove from heat. Stir in peanut butter and vanilla until smooth.
  3. Mix in the rolled oats until well combined.
  4. Drop spoonfuls onto wax paper and let cool until set.

Why It’s Great:

These no-bake cookies are a quick and easy dessert that requires minimal effort. They’re also a great way to satisfy your sweet tooth without turning on the oven!

Conclusion

Cooking with friends in college is not just about the food; it’s about creating memories, sharing laughs, and enjoying each other’s company. These easy recipes provide a variety of options that cater to different tastes and dietary preferences, allowing everyone to get involved in the cooking process. So gather your friends, make a shopping list, and spend some quality time in the kitchen. You’ll not only enjoy delicious meals but also forge bonds that will last a lifetime. Happy cooking!
