How to Cook Chicken: Easy Recipes for Beginners


Cooking chicken is one of the most versatile and rewarding skills in the kitchen. Whether you’re a novice cook or simply looking to elevate your skills, there are a variety of easy chicken recipes that can satisfy your taste buds and help you feel more confident in your cooking. Chicken is a lean, affordable, and delicious protein that can be prepared in countless ways, making it perfect for any occasion, from weeknight dinners to weekend feasts.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through different cooking techniques, along with some beginner-friendly chicken recipes that are both tasty and simple to make. So, let’s dive in and learn how to cook chicken in ways that anyone can master.

1. Preparing Chicken for Cooking

Before you dive into cooking your chicken, it’s important to properly prepare it. Whether you’re working with chicken breasts, thighs, wings, or a whole chicken, a little prep work will go a long way.

A. Choosing the Right Cut of Chicken

Chicken comes in a variety of cuts, and each one has its own unique characteristics. As a beginner, it’s helpful to know which cut to choose for different recipes:

  • Chicken Breasts: These are lean cuts that cook quickly and are great for grilling, baking, or pan-searing. They’re versatile and can be used in many recipes, from chicken sandwiches to casseroles.
  • Chicken Thighs: Thighs are flavorful and juicy because they contain more fat than breasts. They’re great for slow cooking or roasting.
  • Chicken Wings: Perfect for appetizers or casual meals, wings can be baked, grilled, or fried.
  • Whole Chicken: Cooking a whole chicken may seem intimidating, but it’s actually a great way to get tender meat and flavorful broth.

B. Defrosting Chicken

If you’re using frozen chicken, make sure it’s properly thawed before cooking. The best way to defrost chicken is by placing it in the fridge for 24 hours. If you’re short on time, you can also use the defrost setting on your microwave.

C. Seasoning Your Chicken

Chicken on its own can be somewhat bland, but with the right seasoning, it becomes a mouthwatering dish. Always season your chicken with salt and pepper, at the very least. If you have more time, marinate the chicken for extra flavor. A basic marinade might include olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and herbs like rosemary or thyme.

2. Popular Cooking Methods for Chicken

There are several ways to cook chicken, and each method offers a different flavor and texture. Here are some beginner-friendly techniques:

A. Baking Chicken

Baking chicken is a simple and healthy way to cook your chicken. It requires minimal effort and gives you juicy, tender meat with a crispy exterior. Here’s a basic baked chicken recipe:

Easy Baked Chicken Breasts

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried herbs (such as thyme, rosemary, or oregano)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the chicken breasts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Drizzle olive oil over the chicken breasts, then season with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and dried herbs.
  4. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  5. Let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving.

This recipe is a great base for customizing with your favorite seasonings or marinades.

B. Grilling Chicken

Grilling is a fantastic way to infuse chicken with smoky flavor. While it’s often associated with summer, grilling chicken is great year-round.

Simple Grilled Chicken Thighs

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Rub the chicken thighs with olive oil, lemon juice, paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the chicken on the grill and cook for 6-8 minutes on each side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

Grilling gives chicken an irresistible flavor and is an easy way to cook in large batches.

C. Pan-Seared Chicken

If you don’t have a grill or oven, pan-searing chicken is another quick and easy method. The key to a perfect pan-seared chicken breast is getting a nice golden crust without overcooking the meat.

Pan-Seared Chicken Breasts

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Fresh herbs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Place the chicken breasts in the skillet and cook for 5-6 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  4. Add butter and minced garlic to the skillet, and cook for an additional minute, spooning the butter over the chicken as it melts.
  5. Remove the chicken from the skillet and let it rest before serving.

Pan-searing allows you to cook chicken quickly and ensures a delicious, crispy exterior.

D. Slow Cooking Chicken

If you prefer a “set it and forget it” method, slow cooking is the way to go. This method works particularly well for chicken thighs or a whole chicken.

Slow Cooker Chicken Stew

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skinless chicken thighs
  • 3 medium carrots, chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken thighs, carrots, potatoes, onion, and garlic in the slow cooker.
  2. Pour in the chicken broth and season with thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Cover and cook on low for 6-7 hours, or until the chicken is tender and fully cooked.
  4. Remove the chicken from the slow cooker, shred it, and return it to the pot. Stir to combine and serve.

This comforting dish is perfect for busy days when you need a hearty meal with minimal effort.

E. Stir-Frying Chicken

Stir-frying is a fast and flavorful way to cook chicken. It’s an excellent method for creating quick weeknight meals, especially when paired with vegetables and a savory sauce.

Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked.
  3. Add the bell pepper, onion, and broccoli, and stir-fry for an additional 3-4 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, ginger, and garlic. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, and stir well to combine.
  5. Cook for another 2 minutes until everything is coated in the sauce, then serve over rice or noodles.

Stir-fry is a great way to incorporate a variety of vegetables into your chicken dishes while keeping things quick and easy.

3. Tips for Cooking Chicken

A. Ensure Proper Cooking Temperature

Chicken should always be cooked to an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) to ensure it is safe to eat. A meat thermometer is an invaluable tool for beginners and ensures that your chicken is cooked perfectly every time.

B. Don’t Overcook Your Chicken

One of the most common mistakes when cooking chicken is overcooking it, which leads to dry and tough meat. To avoid this, monitor the cooking time carefully and always use a thermometer to check for doneness.

C. Let the Chicken Rest

Allowing the chicken to rest for a few minutes after cooking helps retain its juices. This simple step will make your chicken more flavorful and tender.

D. Experiment with Flavors

Chicken is incredibly versatile, so don’t hesitate to experiment with different herbs, spices, marinades, and sauces. A little creativity can turn a simple chicken recipe into something extraordinary.

4. Conclusion

Learning how to cook chicken is a fantastic starting point for beginners in the kitchen. Whether you prefer baking, grilling, stir-frying, or slow-cooking, there’s a method that fits your taste and schedule. With the recipes and tips provided, you can create flavorful chicken dishes that will impress your family and friends while honing your cooking skills.

Don’t be afraid to get creative and try different flavor combinations. Chicken is a blank canvas, and the possibilities are endless. Enjoy your cooking journey and happy eating!
