Fall Flavor Trends: 7 Recipes to Try This Year


As the leaves begin to change and the air takes on a crisp chill, fall brings with it an exciting opportunity to embrace rich, comforting flavors that perfectly match the season’s cozy vibes. Fall is a time of harvest, with an abundance of root vegetables, hearty grains, and warming spices taking center stage. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to bring more autumnal flair to your everyday meals, there’s no shortage of flavors to explore. In this blog post, we dive into seven must-try recipes that highlight the season’s most delicious ingredients.

1. Butternut Squash and Sage Risotto

Nothing embodies the essence of fall quite like the creamy, comforting texture of risotto. This butternut squash and sage version is a perfect way to welcome the cooler months. The sweetness of roasted butternut squash pairs beautifully with the earthy, aromatic flavor of fresh sage.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 ½ cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (or chicken broth)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • 2 tbsp butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the cubed butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread in a single layer on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.

  2. In a large saucepan, bring the broth to a simmer. Keep warm over low heat.

  3. In another large pan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, sautéing until soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  4. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes until the rice is lightly toasted.

  5. Add the white wine and cook until it’s absorbed by the rice. Begin adding the warm broth, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly. Allow the liquid to be absorbed before adding the next ladleful.

  6. Continue this process until the rice is cooked to al dente texture, about 18-20 minutes.

  7. Stir in the roasted butternut squash, Parmesan cheese, fresh sage, and butter. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste. Serve warm and enjoy the comforting flavors of fall.

2. Apple Cider Glazed Pork Tenderloin

Pork and apples are a classic fall combination, and the addition of apple cider glaze takes this dish to a whole new level of deliciousness. The sweetness of the cider and the savory notes of roasted pork make this recipe perfect for a weeknight dinner or a festive occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs pork tenderloin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup apple cider
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tbsp butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Season the pork tenderloin generously with salt and pepper.

  2. Heat the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the pork on all sides until golden brown, about 3-4 minutes per side.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the apple cider, Dijon mustard, maple syrup, and thyme.

  4. Once the pork is seared, pour the apple cider mixture over the tenderloin. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the pork reaches 145°F (63°C).

  5. Remove the pork from the oven and let it rest for 5 minutes. Meanwhile, transfer the sauce to a small saucepan and simmer until it reduces by half, about 5 minutes. Stir in butter until the sauce is smooth and glossy.

  6. Slice the pork and drizzle the cider glaze over the top before serving.

3. Roasted Root Vegetables with Maple-Balsamic Drizzle

Fall is synonymous with hearty root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes. Roasting them enhances their natural sweetness and brings out deep, caramelized flavors. A drizzle of maple-balsamic glaze makes this dish even more irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large carrots, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 parsnips, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into cubes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Fresh thyme sprigs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Arrange them in a single layer on a baking sheet.

  2. Roast the vegetables for 25-30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until they are tender and golden brown.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, balsamic vinegar, and Dijon mustard. Drizzle the glaze over the roasted vegetables and toss to coat.

  4. Garnish with fresh thyme sprigs before serving.

4. Pumpkin Spice Latte Cupcakes

Pumpkin spice is a quintessential fall flavor, and what better way to indulge in its comforting warmth than with a batch of pumpkin spice latte cupcakes? These treats combine the flavors of pumpkin, cinnamon, and nutmeg with the richness of coffee for the ultimate autumn dessert.

Ingredients:

For the cupcakes:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ½ cup strong brewed coffee (cooled)

For the frosting:

  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp strong brewed coffee (cooled)
  • Pinch of cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt.

  3. In a large bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, eggs, brown sugar, granulated sugar, oil, vanilla extract, and coffee. Mix until well combined.

  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring just until combined.

  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. While the cupcakes are cooling, make the frosting. Beat the cream cheese and butter together until light and fluffy. Gradually add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, coffee, and cinnamon, beating until smooth and creamy.

  7. Once the cupcakes are completely cooled, frost them generously with the coffee-flavored cream cheese frosting. Serve and enjoy the cozy flavors of fall in every bite.

5. Caramelized Onion and Apple Tart

For a savory fall appetizer or light meal, this caramelized onion and apple tart is a delicious option. The sweetness of the apples combined with the richness of caramelized onions makes for a perfect bite, and the flakey puff pastry ties everything together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 apple, thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tbsp honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and place it on a baking sheet.

  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and cook slowly, stirring occasionally, for about 15-20 minutes until the onions are caramelized and tender.

  3. Stir in the balsamic vinegar and season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat.

  4. Arrange the caramelized onions evenly over the puff pastry, leaving a small border around the edges. Top with apple slices and sprinkle with fresh thyme.

  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the puff pastry is golden and crisp.

  6. Drizzle honey over the tart before serving for an added layer of sweetness.

6. Sweet Potato and Kale Salad with Tahini Dressing

This sweet potato and kale salad is a hearty, nourishing dish that highlights two of the season’s most popular ingredients. Roasted sweet potatoes provide natural sweetness, while kale offers a slightly bitter contrast. The creamy tahini dressing adds the perfect finishing touch.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 cups kale, torn into bite-sized pieces
  • ¼ cup pumpkin seeds
  • ¼ cup dried cranberries
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the sweet potato cubes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes until tender.

  2. In a large bowl, massage the kale with olive oil until softened. Add the roasted sweet potatoes, pumpkin seeds, and dried cranberries.

  3. In a small bowl, whisk together tahini, lemon juice, and maple syrup. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.

  4. Serve immediately, or refrigerate for an hour for a cold salad.

7. Pear and Gorgonzola Salad with Candied Walnuts

The combination of ripe pears, tangy Gorgonzola, and sweet candied walnuts makes for a perfect fall salad. The flavor balance between the sweet, salty, and savory components is what makes this dish a standout.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pears, sliced
  • 2 cups arugula
  • ½ cup crumbled Gorgonzola cheese
  • ¼ cup candied walnuts
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the arugula, pear slices, Gorgonzola, and candied walnuts.

  2. Drizzle the balsamic vinegar and olive oil over the salad. Toss gently to combine.

  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately.

As the season shifts into fall, these recipes are sure to bring the flavors of the harvest right to your table. Whether you’re craving the comforting richness of risotto or the bold tang of a savory tart, fall offers an abundance of ingredients and flavors that are perfect for any occasion. Happy cooking!
