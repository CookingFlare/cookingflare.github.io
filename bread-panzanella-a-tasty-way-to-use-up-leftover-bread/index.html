Bread Panzanella: a Tasty Way to Use Up Leftover Bread


There’s nothing quite as satisfying as finding a scrumptious and inventive way to put those pesky leftover bread slices to good use. One such delicious dinner option is Bread Panzanella, an Italian bread salad that brings together a mouth-watering mixture of tomatoes, basil, red onions, garlic and fresh bread. It’s an excellent choice for a healthy, easy-to-make meal, and lends itself perfectly to a wide variety of flavor variations, making it ideal for any daylight table.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups of 1/2-inch cubed leftover bread
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, bruised
  • 2 cups of small-diced, seeded tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup small-diced red onion
  • 1/2 cup small-diced, fresh mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup coarsely chopped fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh bread crumbs
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Step 6: Once the bread is cool enough to handle, cut it into smaller, bite-sized pieces and add it to the large bowl with the vegetables.

Step 7: Toss the ingredients until they are evenly combined.

Step 8: Note that bread panzanella is traditionally served at room temperature, so it’s best to let the salad rest for about 30-40 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 9: Serve the bread panzanella garnished with additional fresh basil leaves and a sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper.
