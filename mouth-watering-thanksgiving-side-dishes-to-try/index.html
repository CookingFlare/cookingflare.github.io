Mouth-Watering Thanksgiving Side Dishes to Try


Thanksgiving is a time of indulgence, gratitude, and of course, delicious food! While the turkey may take center stage on the table, the side dishes are what truly elevate the meal to a feast. This Thanksgiving, why not impress your guests with some mouth-watering side dishes that will have everyone asking for seconds? Here are some delectable recipes to add a flavorful twist to your traditional Thanksgiving spread:

1. Savory Herb Stuffing

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of day-old bread, cubed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon each of dried sage, rosemary, and thyme
  • 2 cups of vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, sauté the onions, celery, and garlic until softened.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the cubed bread, sautéed vegetables, herbs, and seasoning.
  3. Slowly add the vegetable broth to the mixture until well combined.
  4. Transfer the stuffing to a baking dish and bake at 350°F for 30-40 minutes or until golden brown.

2. Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1 head of roasted garlic
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes until fork-tender, then drain.
  2. In a pot, heat the butter and milk until the butter is melted.
  3. Mash the potatoes with the butter and milk mixture until smooth.
  4. Squeeze the roasted garlic cloves into the mashed potatoes, season with salt and pepper, and mix well.
  5. Garnish with chopped chives before serving.

3. Maple Glazed Carrots

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1/4 cup of maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt the butter and add the sliced carrots.
  2. Sauté the carrots until slightly tender.
  3. Drizzle the maple syrup over the carrots and continue to cook until glazed.
  4. Season with salt and pepper, then garnish with fresh thyme before serving.

4. Balsamic Roasted Brussels Sprouts

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated parmesan cheese for topping

Instructions:

  1. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  2. Roast in the oven at 400°F for 25-30 minutes or until crispy.
  3. Sprinkle with grated parmesan cheese before serving.

Conclusion

This Thanksgiving, elevate your dinner table with these mouth-watering side dishes that are sure to impress your family and friends. From classic stuffing to modern twists on traditional vegetables, these recipes will add a burst of flavor and color to your holiday feast. Remember, the key to a memorable Thanksgiving meal is cooking with love and creativity. Enjoy the process and savor every bite. Happy Thanksgiving!
