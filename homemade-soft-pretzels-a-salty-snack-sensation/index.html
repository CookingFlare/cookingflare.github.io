Homemade Soft Pretzels: A Salty Snack Sensation


Soft pretzels are a classic favorite that brings back memories of amusement parks, sporting events, and street food vendors. There’s something undeniably satisfying about sinking your teeth into a warm and chewy pretzel, generously sprinkled with coarse salt. Now, imagine being able to recreate that same experience right in your own kitchen. With this homemade soft pretzels recipe, you can!

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (110°F/43°C)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 ¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • Cooking spray
  • 10 cups water
  • ⅔ cup baking soda
  • Coarse salt, for topping

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the yeast becomes frothy.

  2. Add the salt and flour to the yeast mixture. Stir until a sticky dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough to a well-floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour, one tablespoon at a time.

  4. Grease a large mixing bowl with cooking spray. Place the dough in the bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel. Allow the dough to rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  6. In a large pot, bring 10 cups of water to a boil. Once boiling, add the baking soda and reduce the heat to medium-high. Be careful as the mixture may bubble up.

  7. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a rope about 20 inches long. Twist each rope into a pretzel shape and place it on the prepared baking sheet.

  8. Carefully drop each pretzel into the boiling water mixture, one at a time. Boil for about 30 seconds, then remove with a slotted spoon and return to the baking sheet.

  9. Sprinkle each pretzel with coarse salt, ensuring it sticks to the surface. This will give the pretzels their signature salty flavor.

  10. Bake the pretzels in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until they turn golden brown.

  11. Remove the pretzels from the oven and let them cool slightly before enjoying. Serve with your favorite mustard, cheese sauce, or even butter.

Tips and Variations

  • For a sweeter twist, brush the pretzels with melted butter and sprinkle them with cinnamon sugar before baking.
  • Experiment with different toppings, such as sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or even grated cheese.
  • Don’t be afraid to get creative with the shape of your pretzels. Try forming them into letters, hearts, or even pretzel bites for a fun and bite-sized snack.
  • Soft pretzels are best enjoyed fresh out of the oven, but you can store any leftovers in an airtight container for up to two days. To reheat, simply pop them in a preheated oven for a few minutes until warmed through.

Now that you have the recipe for homemade soft pretzels, it’s time to get baking! Gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and prepare to delight your taste buds with this salty snack sensation. Whether you’re hosting a party, having a movie night, or simply craving a comforting treat, these homemade soft pretzels are sure to hit the spot. Enjoy!
