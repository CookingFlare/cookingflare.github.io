Homemade Nut-Free Enchilada Sauce Recipe


Enchilada sauce, a staple in Mexican cuisine, is often a crucial component in many traditional dishes. However, for those with nut allergies or intolerances, store-bought options may be off-limits. Fear not, dear cooks! Today, we’ll embark on a journey to create a homemade nut-free enchilada sauce recipe that’s not only delicious but also safe for everyone to enjoy.

The Importance of Nut-Free Enchilada Sauce

For those with nut allergies, it’s crucial to avoid any products that may contain tree nuts, peanuts, or other types of nuts. Unfortunately, many store-bought enchilada sauces contain nuts or are processed in facilities that also handle nuts. This can lead to cross-contamination and pose a significant risk to those with nut allergies.

The Secret to a Delicious Nut-Free Enchilada Sauce

So, what makes a great enchilada sauce? It’s all about the balance of flavors. A good enchilada sauce should be rich, slightly sweet, and have a depth of flavor that comes from a combination of spices and ingredients. To achieve this, we’ll use a blend of aromatic spices, including cumin, oregano, and chili powder. We’ll also add a splash of acidity from a splash of vinegar and a pinch of salt to bring out the flavors.

The Recipe

Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Stir in the cumin, oregano, chili powder, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing the spices to bloom.
  5. Add the diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, and apple cider vinegar. Stir to combine.
  6. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  7. Season the sauce with salt to taste.
  8. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
  9. Use immediately or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 1 week or in the freezer for up to 3 months.

Tips and Variations

  • For a smokier flavor, add 1-2 tablespoons of chipotle peppers in adobo sauce to the sauce.
  • To add a bit of heat, increase the amount of cayenne pepper or add diced jalapeños to the sauce.
  • Experiment with different types of tomatoes, such as fire-roasted or crushed tomatoes, for a unique flavor profile.
  • Use this sauce as a base for other recipes, such as chili con carne or fajita sauce.

Conclusion

Homemade nut-free enchilada sauce is a game-changer for those with nut allergies or intolerances. This recipe is not only delicious but also safe and easy to make. With its rich, slightly sweet flavor and depth of spice, it’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So, go ahead and give it a try – your taste buds and those with nut allergies will thank you!
