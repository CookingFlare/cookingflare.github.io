Quick and Easy Holiday Cookies: Delicious Recipes for Festive Fun


The holiday season is upon us, and what better way to get into the spirit than with a plate of delicious homemade cookies? Whether you're hosting a party or just want to treat your family and friends to a sweet surprise, these quick and easy holiday cookie recipes are sure to impress. In this post, we'll explore a variety of flavors and decorating ideas to make your holiday baking experience fun and stress-free. Classic Sugar Cookies with a Twist These buttery, crinkled treats are a staple of the holiday season. Here's a recipe that adds a hint of cinnamon and a touch of magic to the classic recipe. Ingredients:

2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1 tsp baking powder 1 tsp baking soda 1 tsp ground cinnamon 1 tsp vanilla extract 1 cup unsalted butter (at room temperature) 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar 1 egg Colored sugar or sprinkles for decoration (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and cinnamon in a medium bowl. In a large bowl, cream together butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients until a dough forms. Scoop the dough into balls, about 1 tablespoon each. Place on the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. If desired, decorate with colored sugar or sprinkles before they cool.

Cranberry White Chocolate Chip Cookies These chewy cookies combine the sweetness of white chocolate with the tart flavor of cranberries, perfect for the holiday season. Ingredients:

2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1 tsp baking powder 1 tsp baking soda 1 tsp salt 1 tsp vanilla extract 1 cup unsalted butter (at room temperature) 3/4 cup granulated sugar 1 cup white chocolate chips 1 cup chopped fresh or frozen cranberries

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a medium bowl. In a large bowl, cream together butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the vanilla extract. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients until a dough forms. Fold in the white chocolate chips and cranberries. Scoop the dough into balls, about 1 tablespoon each. Place on the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Snickerdoodle Stars These cinnamon-sugar cookies are a classic holiday treat. This recipe uses a combination of all-purpose flour and cornstarch to create a tender, chewy texture. Ingredients:

2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1 cup cornstarch 1 tsp baking soda 1 tsp cream of tartar 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg 1/4 tsp salt 1 cup unsalted butter (at room temperature) 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar 2 large eggs 2 tsp vanilla extract Colored sugar for decoration (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Whisk together flour, cornstarch, baking soda, cream of tartar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt in a medium bowl. In a large bowl, cream together butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs and vanilla extract. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients until a dough forms. Roll the dough into balls, about 1 tablespoon each. Roll in colored sugar to coat, if desired. Place the balls onto the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Royal Icing for Decorating Royal icing is a must-have for decorating holiday cookies. This simple recipe is perfect for making intricate designs and patterns on your cookies. Ingredients:

1 cup powdered sugar 2 tbsp meringue powder 2 tbsp water Food coloring (optional)

Instructions:

In a large bowl, whisk together powdered sugar and meringue powder. Gradually add in water, whisking until smooth and stiff. Add food coloring, if desired.

Tips and Tricks for Baking Holiday Cookies Before you start baking, here are a few tips and tricks to keep in mind:

Use room temperature butter for the best baking results. Don't overmix the dough, as this can lead to tough cookies. Chill the dough for 30 minutes to an hour before baking for crisper cookies. Use parchment paper to prevent cookies from sticking to the baking sheet. Chill the cookies on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool.

Conclusion With these quick and easy holiday cookie recipes, you'll be the talk of the town this holiday season. From classic sugar cookies to snickerdoodle stars and cranberry white chocolate chip cookies, there's something for everyone. Decorate your cookies with royal icing and a pinch of festive fun to make them truly unforgettable. Whether you're baking for a party or just want to treat your loved ones, these recipes are sure to bring joy and cheer to your holiday season.
