Farm-to-Table Spring Vegetable Risotto Recipe


As the snow begins to melt and the earth awakens from its winter slumber, spring brings with it a vibrant tapestry of colors and flavors to the kitchen. There’s nothing quite like embracing the freshness of the season with a hearty, comforting risotto recipe that showcases the best of what spring has to offer. In this recipe, we’ll be celebrating the arrival of spring with a farm-to-table spring vegetable risotto that’s sure to become a new favorite.

###Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, warmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed spring vegetables (such as asparagus, bell peppers, carrots, and zucchini)
  • 1/2 cup white wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

###Instructions

In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until it’s translucent and starting to caramelize, about 8-10 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Next, add the Arborio rice to the pot and cook, stirring constantly, for 1-2 minutes. The rice should be coated in oil and slightly toasted. If using wine, add it to the pot and cook until the liquid is almost completely absorbed, stirring constantly, about 2-3 minutes.

Warm the vegetable broth in a separate pot and keep it hot. Add 1/2 cup of the warmed broth to the rice and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is almost completely absorbed. Repeat this process, adding the broth in 1/2 cup increments and waiting for it to be absorbed before adding more, until the rice is cooked and creamy, about 20-25 minutes.

While the rice is cooking, prepare the mixed spring vegetables. Cut them into bite-sized pieces and set them aside. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the butter and Parmesan cheese until melted and well combined. Add the mixed spring vegetables to the pot and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

###Tips and Variations

  • For an added pop of color and flavor, try adding some fresh edible flowers like pansies or violas to the risotto towards the end of cooking.
  • If you’re short on time, you can use frozen or canned spring vegetables instead of fresh.
  • To make this recipe more substantial, try adding some cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu to the risotto along with the spring vegetables.
  • For a vegan version, substitute the Parmesan cheese with a dairy-free alternative and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.

###Conclusion

This farm-to-table spring vegetable risotto recipe is the perfect way to celebrate the arrival of spring in the kitchen. With its vibrant colors, fresh flavors, and comforting texture, it’s sure to become a new favorite in your household. So go ahead, get creative with your mixed spring vegetables and enjoy the simple pleasures of a warm, satisfying meal on a chilly spring evening.
