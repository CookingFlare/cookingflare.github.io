Spanish Inspired Caramelized Onion and Manchego Tarts Recipe


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine, you’re in for a treat with this Spanish-inspired caramelized onion and Manchego tarts recipe. These savory tarts are bursting with the rich flavors of caramelized onions and the nutty goodness of Manchego cheese, all encased in a buttery, flaky pastry. Whether you’re hosting a tapas night or simply craving a taste of Spain, these tarts are sure to impress your taste buds and your guests.

Ingredients:

For the pastry:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup grated Manchego cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the pastry:

    • In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cubed butter and use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • Gradually add the ice water, a little at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overwork the dough. Shape the dough into a disc, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Caramelize the onions:

    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and cook, stirring occasionally, until they are soft and golden brown, about 30-40 minutes.
    • Stir in the balsamic vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper, and continue to cook for another 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let the onions cool slightly.

  3. Assemble the tarts:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the chilled pastry on a lightly floured surface to about 1/4 inch thick. Cut the pastry into rounds or squares, depending on your preference.
    • Place the pastry rounds or squares on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Spoon a generous amount of the caramelized onions onto each pastry, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle the grated Manchego cheese over the onions.

  4. Bake the tarts:

    • Bake the tarts in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the pastry is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned. Remove from the oven and let the tarts cool for a few minutes. Sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

These Spanish-inspired caramelized onion and Manchego tarts are best enjoyed warm, either as a delightful appetizer or as a part of a tapas spread. Serve them alongside a glass of Spanish red wine for an authentic experience.

Conclusion:

With the irresistible combination of sweet caramelized onions and rich Manchego cheese, these tarts are a celebration of Spanish flavors that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Spain. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just beginning your culinary journey, this recipe is a delightful way to bring a taste of Spain into your kitchen. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure with these Spanish-inspired tarts. Buen provecho!
