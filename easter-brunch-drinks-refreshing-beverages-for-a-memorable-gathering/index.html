Easter Brunch Drinks: Refreshing Beverages for a Memorable Gathering


Easter brunch is a time-honored tradition for many families and friends, and it’s an occasion that requires a special touch. While the food is certainly an important aspect of the celebration, the drinks can make or break the atmosphere. In this post, we’ll explore some refreshing Easter brunch drinks that are sure to make your gathering a memorable one.

Spring-inspired Mimosas

Mimosas are a classic Easter brunch drink, and for good reason. The combination of champagne and orange juice is a timeless favorite that’s easy to make and always a hit. To give your mimosas a spring-inspired twist, try adding a splash of lemon-lime soda and a sprig of fresh mint. This adds a bright, citrusy flavor that’s perfect for the season.

Recipe:

  • 1 cup champagne
  • 1 cup orange juice
  • 1/2 cup lemon-lime soda
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Combine the champagne and orange juice in a large pitcher. Stir in the lemon-lime soda and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Serve over ice in champagne flutes and garnish with a sprig of fresh mint.

Fresh Berry Spritzers

Spring is the perfect time to get creative with your drinks, and fresh berries are a great way to add some color and flavor to your Easter brunch. Try making a fresh berry spritzer by mixing sparkling wine with a mixture of fresh berries and a splash of lemon-lime soda.

Recipe:

  • 1 cup sparkling wine
  • 1 cup mixed fresh berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)
  • 1/2 cup lemon-lime soda
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Muddle the fresh berries in a large pitcher to release their juice and flavor. Add the sparkling wine and lemon-lime soda, and stir to combine. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Serve over ice in wine glasses and garnish with a sprig of fresh mint.

Cucumber Lime Refresher

Cucumber is a refreshing and light ingredient that’s perfect for spring, and when combined with lime juice and sparkling water, it makes for a refreshing Easter brunch drink. Try making a cucumber lime refresher by muddling sliced cucumber in a pitcher with lime juice and then topping with sparkling water.

Recipe:

  • 2 cups sliced cucumber
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 cup sparkling water
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Muddle the sliced cucumber in a large pitcher to release their juice and flavor. Add the lime juice and stir to combine. Top with sparkling water and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Serve over ice in tall glasses and garnish with a sprig of fresh mint.

Iced Tea with Fresh Fruit

Iced tea is a classic Easter brunch drink that’s easy to make and always a crowd-pleaser. To give your iced tea a fresh twist, try adding some fresh fruit to the pitcher. Try using strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries to add a sweet and fruity flavor to your tea.

Recipe:

  • 1 quart brewed tea
  • 1 cup fresh fruit (such as strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries)
  • 1 cup sparkling water
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Combine the brewed tea and fresh fruit in a large pitcher. Stir to combine and chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. Serve over ice in tall glasses and top with sparkling water. Garnish with a sprig of fresh mint.

Conclusion

Easter brunch is a time to come together with friends and family and celebrate the season. With these refreshing Easter brunch drinks, you’ll be sure to make your gathering a memorable one. Whether you’re looking for a classic mimosa or something a little more unique, these recipes are sure to please. So why not get creative and try something new this Easter? Your guests will thank you!
