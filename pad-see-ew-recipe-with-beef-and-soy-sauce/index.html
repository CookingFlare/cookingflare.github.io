Pad See-Ew Recipe with Beef and Soy Sauce


If you’re a fan of Thai cuisine, you’ve probably indulged in the delightful flavors of Pad See-Ew. This classic stir-fried noodle dish is a favorite among food lovers for its rich, savory taste and comforting aroma. While Pad See-Ew is traditionally made with chicken or pork, this recipe puts a delectable twist on the dish by incorporating tender strips of beef, infusing the dish with a hearty and satisfying flavor. The combination of succulent beef, wide rice noodles, and a savory soy sauce-based marinade creates a dish that is sure to tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon dark soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper

For the Pad See-Ew:

  • 12 ounces wide rice noodles
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup Chinese broccoli or regular broccoli, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Beef:

    • In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, brown sugar, minced garlic, and black pepper. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring that the meat is well-coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  2. Prepare the Rice Noodles:

    • Soak the wide rice noodles in warm water for about 20-30 minutes until they are pliable but still firm to the touch. Once soaked, drain the noodles and set them aside.

  3. Stir-Fry the Ingredients:

    • Heat a wok or a large skillet over high heat. Add 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil and swirl to coat the pan. Add the marinated beef and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the beef is cooked to your desired doneness. Remove the beef from the wok and set it aside.

  4. Cook the Eggs and Vegetables:

    • In the same wok, heat the remaining tablespoon of oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant. Crack the eggs into the wok and scramble them until they are just set. Add the Chinese broccoli, carrots, and bean sprouts, and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.

  5. Combine the Ingredients:

    • Return the cooked beef to the wok with the vegetable and egg mixture. Add the drained rice noodles, soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, and brown sugar. Toss everything together gently, ensuring that the noodles are evenly coated with the sauce and the ingredients are well combined.

  6. Finish and Serve:

    • Add the green onions to the wok and continue to stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes until everything is heated through. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Transfer the Pad See-Ew with Beef to a serving platter and serve hot.

  7. Enjoy Your Homemade Pad See-Ew:

    • Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice for an extra burst of flavor. Serve the Pad See-Ew with beef and soy sauce alongside a side of pickled Thai chili peppers for a touch of heat, and enjoy the delightful medley of flavors and textures in every bite.

Conclusion

This Pad See-Ew recipe with beef and soy sauce offers a delightful fusion of Thai and beef flavors that is guaranteed to impress your family and friends. With its tender beef, chewy rice noodles, and vibrant assortment of vegetables, this dish is a satisfying and savory addition to any meal. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is a great way to explore the rich and diverse world of Thai cuisine while indulging in a comforting and delicious meal. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure with this tantalizing Pad See-Ew recipe. Your taste buds will thank you!
