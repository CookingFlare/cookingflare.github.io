Classic Roast Turkey Recipe for a Perfect Thanksgiving Feast


Are you ready to bring the classic flavors of Thanksgiving to your dining table? Look no further! This Classic Roast Turkey recipe is a foolproof way to create a mouthwatering centerpiece for your holiday feast. With simple ingredients and step-by-step instructions, this recipe will guide you through the process of roasting the perfect turkey that will leave your guests in awe.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (12-14 pounds)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon dried rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 1 lemon, halved

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Make sure the turkey is completely thawed before you begin.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, olive oil, dried thyme, dried rosemary, dried sage, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are incorporated.

  3. Carefully loosen the skin of the turkey by gently sliding your fingers underneath, being careful not to tear the skin. Rub half of the butter mixture under the skin, spreading it evenly over the breast and thighs.

  4. Stuff the cavity of the turkey with the onion, garlic cloves, and lemon halves. This will infuse the bird with aromatic flavors as it roasts.

  5. Place the turkey on a rack in a roasting pan, breast side up. Tuck the wings underneath the bird and tie the legs together with kitchen twine.

  6. Brush the remaining butter mixture all over the outside of the turkey, making sure to cover every inch.

  7. Insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the thigh, being careful not to touch the bone. This will help you monitor the internal temperature of the turkey throughout the cooking process.

  8. Place the turkey in the preheated oven and roast for about 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C). Baste the turkey with the pan juices every 30 minutes to keep it moist and flavorful.

  9. Once the turkey reaches the desired temperature, remove it from the oven and tent it loosely with aluminum foil. Allow it to rest for at least 30 minutes before carving. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a tender and juicy turkey.

  10. Carve the turkey and serve it alongside your favorite Thanksgiving side dishes. Enjoy the delicious flavors and the joy of sharing a memorable meal with your loved ones.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a crispy skin, increase the oven temperature to 425°F (220°C) during the last 30 minutes of roasting. This will help to achieve a beautifully golden and crispy exterior.

  • To add extra flavor to the pan juices, you can pour in a cup of chicken or turkey broth before roasting the turkey. This will create a rich and flavorful base for making gravy.

  • Feel free to customize the seasoning to your liking. You can add herbs like thyme, rosemary, sage, or even experiment with spices like paprika or garlic powder.

  • Don’t forget to save the turkey carcass after carving! It can be used to make a delicious homemade turkey stock or soup.

Now that you have the recipe for a classic roast turkey, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get ready for a Thanksgiving feast that will be remembered for years to come. Enjoy the process of preparing this iconic dish and savor the flavors of the holiday season. Happy Thanksgiving!
