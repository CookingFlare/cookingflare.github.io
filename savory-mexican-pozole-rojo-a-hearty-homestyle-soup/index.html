Savory Mexican Pozole Rojo: A Hearty Homestyle Soup


Pozole Rojo is a beloved Mexican dish that is perfect for those colder days when you crave something warm, comforting, and bursting with flavor. This homestyle soup is made with tender pork, hearty hominy, and a rich red chili broth that will transport your taste buds straight to Mexico. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply want to enjoy a delicious bowl of soup, this Savory Mexican Pozole Rojo recipe is sure to impress. Let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork shoulder, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cans (29 ounces each) hominy, drained and rinsed
  • 4 dried guajillo chilies
  • 2 dried ancho chilies
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Toppings: sliced radishes, shredded cabbage, chopped cilantro, lime wedges, and diced avocado

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, add the dried chilies and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat and let them soak for 15-20 minutes until softened. Drain the chilies, remove the stems and seeds, and set aside.

  2. In the same pot, heat some oil over medium-high heat. Add the pork pieces and brown them on all sides until they develop a nice crust. This step will add depth of flavor to the soup.

  3. Once the pork is browned, remove it from the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  4. While the onion and garlic are cooking, place the soaked chilies in a blender along with a cup of water. Blend until smooth, creating a vibrant red chili paste.

  5. Add the chili paste to the pot with the onions and garlic. Stir in the dried oregano and ground cumin. Cook for a couple of minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. Return the browned pork to the pot and add enough water to cover the ingredients. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, covered, for about 1 hour and 30 minutes, or until the pork is tender.

  7. Once the pork is tender, add the drained hominy to the pot. Simmer for an additional 30 minutes to allow the flavors to marry together.

  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning as needed to suit your preferences.

  9. Ladle the Pozole Rojo into bowls and garnish with your favorite toppings. Sliced radishes, shredded cabbage, chopped cilantro, lime wedges, and diced avocado are all traditional and delicious options.

  10. Serve the Savory Mexican Pozole Rojo piping hot and enjoy the explosion of flavors in every spoonful. Don’t forget to squeeze some fresh lime juice over the top for that extra zing!

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a spicier Pozole Rojo, you can add some chopped jalapeños or serrano peppers along with the onions and garlic.
  • For a vegetarian version, replace the pork with your favorite vegetables such as zucchini, bell peppers, and carrots. You can also use vegetable broth instead of water for a richer flavor.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The flavors will continue to develop, making the soup even more delicious with each passing day.

Now that you have the recipe for this Savory Mexican Pozole Rojo, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get cooking. This homestyle soup will warm your soul and transport you to the vibrant streets of Mexico. Enjoy this traditional dish with your loved ones and savor every spoonful of this hearty and delicious Pozole Rojo!
