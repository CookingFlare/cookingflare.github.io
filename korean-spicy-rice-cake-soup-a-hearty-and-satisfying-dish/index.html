Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup: A Hearty and Satisfying Dish


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or looking to expand your culinary horizons, then Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup, also known as Tteokbokki, is a dish that you absolutely must try. This delectable and hearty soup is a staple in Korean cuisine and is beloved for its rich flavors and satisfying texture. In this blog post, we’ll explore the origins of this dish, its cultural significance, and of course, provide you with a mouth-watering recipe to recreate this Korean classic in your own kitchen.

The Origins and Cultural Significance

Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup has a rich history that dates back centuries. It is believed to have originated during the Joseon Dynasty in Korea, where it was enjoyed by the royal court as well as the common people. Over time, it has become a beloved street food and comfort dish in Korea, with numerous variations and regional twists adding to its popularity.

In Korean culture, Tteokbokki holds a special place as a symbol of celebration and togetherness. It is often enjoyed during holidays and special occasions, bringing families and friends together around the dining table. Its vibrant red color and spicy aroma evoke a sense of warmth and conviviality, making it a cherished part of Korean culinary tradition.

The Recipe: How to Make Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup

Now, let’s dive into the exciting part - making your own Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup at home. This recipe serves 4 and is perfect for a cozy dinner or a gathering with loved ones.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of cylinder-shaped rice cakes (tteok)
  • 4 cups of anchovy or beef broth
  • 3 1/2 tablespoons of Korean red chili pepper paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 3 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs
  • 150g of fish cakes, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, bring the broth to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the red chili pepper paste, soy sauce, and sugar to the broth, stirring until they are fully dissolved.
  3. Add the rice cakes and fish cakes to the broth, and let them cook for about 5 minutes until they are tender.
  4. Stir in the green onions and cabbage, cooking for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Once the soup is ready, remove it from the heat and drizzle with sesame oil.
  6. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with sliced hard-boiled eggs and sesame seeds.

Serving Suggestion:

Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup is best enjoyed hot and fresh. Pair it with a side of kimchi and some pickled radishes for a complete and satisfying meal.

In Conclusion

Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup is a delightful and comforting dish that offers a perfect balance of spice, umami, and heartiness. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or looking to explore new flavors, this soup is sure to impress your taste buds and warm your soul. From its rich history to its vibrant flavors, Tteokbokki is a true embodiment of the warmth and joy of Korean culture. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor the delightful flavors of Korean Spicy Rice Cake Soup right in your own kitchen.
