Healthy Mediterranean Quinoa Bowls for Meal Prep


When it comes to meal prep, many of us often turn to familiar favorites like brown rice or pasta, but there's a healthier alternative that deserves some attention: quinoa. This protein-rich, gluten-free grain is a staple in the Mediterranean diet, and for good reason. Packed with fiber, vitamins, and minerals, quinoa makes for a filling and nutritious base for a week's worth of meals. In this recipe, we'll show you how to create a flavorful and nutritious Mediterranean quinoa bowl with a medley of juicy vegetables, savory spices, and a tangy tzatziki sauce. Preparation Time: 30 minutes Cook Time: 25 minutes Servings: 5-6 Calories per serving: 400-450 Ingredients:

1 cup quinoa 2 cups water or vegetable broth 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 medium onion, diced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 medium red bell pepper, diced 1 medium zucchini, diced 1 medium cucumber, sliced 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice 1 teaspoon dried oregano Salt and pepper, to taste Tzatziki sauce (see below for recipe)

Tzatziki Sauce Ingredients:

1 cup Greek yogurt 1/2 cup diced cucumber 1/4 cup diced red onion 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon lemon juice Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Cook the quinoa: Rinse the quinoa in a fine mesh strainer, then add it to a medium saucepan and use a fine-mesh strainer again, drain the quinoa thoroughly, discard the water, and cook the quinoa with 2 cups of water or vegetable broth over medium heat until the water has been absorbed and the quinoa is tender. Allow the quinoa to rest for a few minutes, then fluff it with a fork. Roast the vegetables: In a separate bowl, toss the diced onion, minced garlic, red bell pepper, and zucchini with olive oil, salt, and pepper until they are evenly coated. Spread the mixture on a baking sheet and roast in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and lightly browned. Make the tzatziki sauce: Combine the yogurt, diced cucumber, red onion, garlic, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl and mix well. Refrigerate until ready to use. Assemble the bowls: Divide the cooked quinoa between five or six bowls, then top each one with a medley of roasted vegetables, cherry tomatoes, fresh parsley, mint, and a dollop of tzatziki sauce.

Mix-and-Match Tips: Feel free to customize the bowls to your taste by choosing your favorite Mediterranean flavors. Here are a few ideas:

Substitute chickpeas or feta cheese for added protein and creaminess. Add a sprinkle of hummus or a drizzle of tahini sauce for extra flavor. Exchange the cucumber for thinly sliced eggplant or zucchini for added texture. Experiment with different herbs like oregano, thyme, or parsley for a unique twist.

Why Quinoa Makes an Exceptional Base for Meal Prep Quinoa is a nutritional superfood that offers a range of benefits that makes it an excellent base for meal prep. Here are a few reasons why:

High in fiber: Quinoa is high in both soluble and insoluble fiber, which helps manage digestion and supports healthy blood sugar levels. Protein-rich: Quinoa is a complete protein that contains all nine essential amino acids that the body cannot produce on its own, making it a perfect choice for vegetarians and vegans. Versatile: Quinoa absorbs and cools down the flavors of the surrounding ingredients, making it a base that pairs well with a variety of flavors and textures. Gluten-free: Quinoa is a gluten-free grain that is easy to digest, making it an excellent option for those with gluten intolerance or sensitivity.

How to Store and Reheat Quinoa Bowls To prepare meal prep on a larger scale, consider storing the cooked quinoa, tzatziki sauce, and roasted vegetables separately and assembling the bowls as you go. Here are a few storage and reheating tips:

Quinoa: Store cooked quinoa in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze for up to 3 months. Tzatziki sauce: Store the sauce in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Roasted vegetables: Store the roasted vegetables in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat: Refrigerated or frozen quinoa and vegetables can be reheated in the microwave or in a saucepan over low heat. Tzatziki sauce can be reheated in the microwave or at room temperature.

With its boost of protein, fiber, and healthy fats, these Mediterranean quinoa bowls offer a nutritious twist on traditional meal prep. By incorporating these bowls into your daily routine, you'll be well on your way to adopting healthier eating habits and achieving your health goals.
