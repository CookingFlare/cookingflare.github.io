Deliciously Nutty Pecan Sandies: A Classic Cookie


If you’re a fan of classic cookies, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for deliciously nutty Pecan Sandies. These melt-in-your-mouth cookies are the perfect combination of buttery goodness and nutty flavor. Whether you’re enjoying them with a cup of coffee or sharing them with friends, Pecan Sandies are sure to please even the most discerning cookie connoisseur.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup finely chopped pecans

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. This should take about 2-3 minutes.

  3. Add the vanilla extract to the butter mixture and mix until well combined.

  4. Gradually add the flour to the butter mixture, mixing well after each addition. The dough should come together and be slightly crumbly.

  5. Gently fold in the finely chopped pecans, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.

  6. Take about a tablespoon of dough and roll it into a ball. Place the ball onto the prepared baking sheet and flatten it slightly with the bottom of a glass or the palm of your hand. Repeat this process with the remaining dough, spacing the cookies about 2 inches apart.

  7. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until they are lightly golden around the edges.

  8. Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. Then, transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  9. Once the cookies have cooled, store them in an airtight container at room temperature. They will stay fresh for up to a week, although they probably won’t last that long!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of sweetness, you can dust the cooled cookies with powdered sugar before serving.

  • If you prefer a more pronounced nutty flavor, you can toast the pecans before chopping them. Simply spread the pecans on a baking sheet and toast them in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 5-7 minutes, or until they become fragrant. Let them cool before chopping and adding them to the dough.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, you can add a teaspoon of cinnamon or a pinch of nutmeg to the dough for a subtle hint of warm spice.

  • Pecan Sandies are also delicious when dipped in melted chocolate. Simply melt your favorite chocolate and dip half of each cookie into the melted chocolate. Allow the chocolate to set before serving.

Enjoy these delightful Pecan Sandies with a glass of milk or a hot cup of tea. Their buttery texture and nutty flavor will keep you coming back for more. Whether you’re baking them for yourself or sharing them with loved ones, these classic cookies are sure to be a hit. Happy baking!
