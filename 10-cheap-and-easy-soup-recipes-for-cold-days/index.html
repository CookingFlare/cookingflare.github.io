10 Cheap and Easy Soup Recipes for Cold Days


There’s nothing quite as comforting as a warm bowl of soup on a chilly day. Not only do soups provide a cozy reprieve from the cold, but they’re also budget-friendly, versatile, and simple to make. Preparing your own soup at home is an easy way to use up pantry staples, reduce food waste, and feed your family without breaking the bank. Below, we’ve compiled ten affordable and easy soup recipes perfect for cold days. These options are hearty, delicious, and designed to keep you warm through the season.

1. Classic Lentil Soup

Lentils are the hero of budget-friendly cooking. Packed with protein and fiber, they’re not only filling but also highly nutritious. This lentil soup recipe requires minimal ingredients, most of which you might already have in your pantry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic in a large pot with olive oil until softened.
  2. Add the lentils, broth, cumin, and paprika. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for about 30–35 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Blend a portion of the soup if you prefer creaminess. Serve warm.

2. Potato and Leek Soup

This creamy potato and leek soup is the ultimate winter warmer. It’s simple to whip up yet loaded with flavor, thanks to the fragrant leeks.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 leeks, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • ½ cup milk or cream
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the leeks and onion in a pot with a bit of butter or oil until softened.
  2. Add potatoes and stock. Bring to a boil, then simmer until the potatoes are tender.
  3. Blend until smooth, then stir in the milk or cream. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

3. Vegetarian Minestrone

This hearty Italian soup is healthy, easy to make, and perfect for using up any leftover veggies or beans.

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 celery sticks, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup spinach or kale leaves
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can white beans or kidney beans, drained
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup small pasta (e.g., ditalini)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onion and celery in olive oil until softened.
  2. Add zucchini, tomatoes, beans, and broth. Bring to a boil.
  3. Stir in pasta and cook until tender. Add spinach or kale at the end and season with oregano. Serve hot.

4. Cabbage Soup

Cabbage is inexpensive and nutrient-rich, making it a great choice for a light yet hearty soup.

Ingredients:

  • ½ head green cabbage, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme

Instructions:

  1. Cook the onion, garlic, and carrots in a large pot with some oil until fragrant.
  2. Add the cabbage, tomatoes, broth, and thyme. Simmer for 30–40 minutes.
  3. Adjust seasoning to taste, and enjoy with crusty bread.

5. Chicken and Rice Soup

This comforting classic is a go-to for staying warm and satiated on cold nights.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked chicken, shredded
  • ½ cup uncooked rice (white or brown)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the onion and carrots in olive oil until softened.
  2. Add the chicken broth and rice. Simmer until the rice is tender.
  3. Stir in the shredded chicken and parsley. Heat through and serve.

6. Tomato Basil Soup

This creamy soup is as simple as it gets, combining pantry staples with fresh basil for a burst of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 cups vegetable stock
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil, chopped
  • ½ cup milk or cream

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil. Add the tomatoes and vegetable stock.
  2. Simmer for 20 minutes. Add fresh basil, then purée the soup until smooth.
  3. Stir in cream, season to taste, and serve warm.

7. Sweet Corn Soup

This creamy sweet corn soup is light yet filling and very affordable to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups frozen or fresh sweetcorn
  • 1 potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • ½ cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Cook onion and potato in a bit of oil or butter until potatoes start softening.
  2. Add corn and vegetable broth. Simmer until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Blend the soup until smooth, stir in the milk, and warm through before serving.

8. Split Pea Soup

Split peas provide a rich, creamy texture without the need for cream or other thickeners. This old-fashioned recipe is a classic for frugal eating.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried split peas, rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion, carrot, and celery in a large pot until softened.
  2. Add split peas, thyme, and broth. Simmer for 1 hour or until peas are tender.
  3. Mash or blend part of the soup for added creaminess. Season to taste before serving.

9. Egg Drop Soup

This simple Chinese-inspired soup is quick, light, and perfect for when you need something warm and soothing.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with a little water

Instructions:

  1. Bring the broth to a boil, then stir in the soy sauce and cornstarch slurry.
  2. Slowly drizzle the beaten eggs into the boiling broth while stirring to create ribbons.
  3. Serve immediately with green onion or sesame seeds on top.

10. Spicy Black Bean Soup

This soup adds a kick to cold days, with hearty black beans as the star ingredient.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder

Instructions:

  1. Cook onion, garlic, and bell pepper in olive oil until softened.
  2. Add black beans, broth, and chili powder. Simmer for 20–25 minutes.
  3. Blend half the soup for a thicker texture. Adjust seasoning and serve with a squeeze of lime.

Soups are a lifesaver when it comes to staying frugal and fueled during colder months. Each of these recipes highlights easy techniques and affordable ingredients, ensuring that you won’t need to compromise on taste or nutrition. Whether you’re looking for a creamy, hearty option or something brothy and light, these soups are sure to warm you from the inside out.
