How to Make Middle Eastern Chicken Shawarma at Home


Chicken Shawarma is a beloved Middle Eastern dish that has captured the hearts of food enthusiasts worldwide. With its tender, juicy meat, aromatic spices, and vibrant accompaniments, Shawarma is the perfect street food that you can now recreate in the comfort of your kitchen. This recipe will guide you step by step to make restaurant-quality Chicken Shawarma at home. From marinating the chicken to creating your own tangy garlic sauce and assembling the wrap, you’ll experience the authentic taste of this Middle Eastern classic.

What Is Chicken Shawarma?

Originating from the Levantine region, Shawarma is traditionally made with various meats such as lamb, beef, or chicken. The meat is marinated with a variety of spices, skewered, and then slow-cooked on a vertical rotisserie. Once cooked, it’s shaved thin and served in a pita or flatbread, topped with vegetables, pickles, and sauces.

For home cooks, the vertical rotisserie may not be an option, but don’t worry! This recipe simplifies the process by using a grill pan or oven, allowing you to achieve the same delicious, juicy result with a fraction of the effort.

Ingredients for Chicken Shawarma

To prepare Chicken Shawarma, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs (you can use chicken breasts, but thighs offer a juicier result)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

For the Garlic Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Shawarma Wrap:

  • Pita bread or flatbread
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Pickled vegetables (optional)

Step 1: Marinating the Chicken

The key to a flavorful Chicken Shawarma lies in the marinade. The blend of spices used not only tenderizes the meat but also infuses it with the signature deep, savory taste. To begin, cut the chicken thighs into strips or leave them whole, depending on your preference. Thighs are preferred because they stay tender and juicy, but you can use chicken breasts for a leaner option.

In a bowl, mix the minced garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, cinnamon, allspice, cloves, black pepper, and salt. Whisk until the ingredients are well combined into a smooth marinade.

Add the chicken to the bowl, ensuring that each piece is thoroughly coated in the marinade. Once mixed, cover the bowl and place it in the refrigerator. Allow the chicken to marinate for at least 2 hours, but ideally overnight, to maximize flavor. The longer the chicken marinates, the more intense the flavor will be.

Step 2: Preparing the Garlic Sauce

While the chicken is marinating, it’s time to prepare the garlic sauce. This creamy sauce is a delicious accompaniment to the Shawarma and adds a rich, tangy flavor to the wrap. In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, Greek yogurt, minced garlic, and lemon juice. Stir until everything is well mixed.

Taste the sauce and add salt and pepper to your liking. If you prefer a more intense garlic flavor, you can add more minced garlic. Once the sauce is ready, refrigerate it until you’re ready to serve.

Step 3: Cooking the Chicken

Method 1: Grilling the Chicken (Preferred Method)

For an authentic smoky flavor, grilling the chicken is the best option. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. If you’re using a grill pan, lightly grease it with olive oil to prevent sticking.

Place the marinated chicken on the grill and cook for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until the chicken is fully cooked through with an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C). The chicken should be golden brown with a slight char for that signature Shawarma taste.

If you’re cooking the chicken whole, be sure to flip it every few minutes to ensure even cooking. Once done, remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing it into thin strips.

Method 2: Baking the Chicken (Alternative Method)

If you don’t have a grill, baking the chicken is a great alternative. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the marinated chicken on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a lightly greased rack. Bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked through. To get a crispy texture, you can broil the chicken for the last 3-4 minutes of cooking.

Once cooked, remove the chicken from the oven, let it rest, and slice it into thin strips.

Step 4: Assembling the Shawarma Wrap

Now that your chicken is cooked and sliced, it’s time to assemble the Shawarma wrap. Take a warm pita or flatbread and place it on a clean surface. Add a generous amount of the garlic sauce in the center of the bread, followed by a few slices of the grilled chicken. Then, layer on the fresh parsley, sliced tomatoes, cucumbers, and pickled vegetables (if using).

You can also add some extra sauce on top for more flavor. Once all the ingredients are added, fold the sides of the bread inwards and roll it up tightly to secure everything inside.

Step 5: Serving and Enjoying

Your homemade Chicken Shawarma is now ready to be enjoyed! Serve it immediately with a side of fries, a salad, or some hummus. Shawarma is often served with a refreshing drink like Ayran, a yogurt-based beverage, or a glass of fresh lemonade.

Tips for the Best Chicken Shawarma

  1. Use Chicken Thighs: As mentioned, chicken thighs are the best cut for Shawarma due to their tender and juicy texture. If you choose chicken breasts, be mindful not to overcook them, as they can become dry.

  2. Marinate Overnight: For the most flavorful chicken, marinate it for as long as possible, preferably overnight. This allows the spices to penetrate the meat and ensures maximum flavor.

  3. Experiment with Spices: Shawarma is versatile, and you can adjust the spices based on your preferences. Feel free to play around with additional spices like cardamom, paprika, or sumac to tailor the flavor to your liking.

  4. Serve with Fresh Vegetables: The combination of fresh vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, and lettuce balances the richness of the chicken and garlic sauce, making each bite refreshing.

  5. Add Extra Toppings: While pickled vegetables are common, you can add other toppings like feta cheese, olives, or tahini for a richer taste.

Variations of Chicken Shawarma

Shawarma Salad Bowl

If you’re looking for a lighter, low-carb alternative, turn your Chicken Shawarma into a salad bowl. Instead of wrapping the chicken in pita, place the sliced chicken over a bed of mixed greens, tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onions. Drizzle the garlic sauce over the top and enjoy a fresh, healthy take on this classic dish.

Shawarma Skewers

For a fun twist, you can skewer the marinated chicken pieces and grill them like kebabs. This method is perfect for BBQs or family gatherings. Simply thread the marinated chicken onto skewers and grill them until cooked through.

Shawarma in a Bowl

Another variation is to serve your Chicken Shawarma as a rice bowl. Place the grilled chicken on a bed of fluffy rice, and top it with your favorite Shawarma accompaniments like tomatoes, cucumbers, parsley, and a drizzle of garlic sauce.

Conclusion

Making Chicken Shawarma at home is not only easy but incredibly rewarding. With the right combination of spices, a simple marinade, and the right cooking method, you can replicate the flavors of your favorite Middle Eastern street food right in your own kitchen. Whether you prefer it wrapped in pita or served in a bowl, this Chicken Shawarma recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings and impress your guests. Happy cooking!
