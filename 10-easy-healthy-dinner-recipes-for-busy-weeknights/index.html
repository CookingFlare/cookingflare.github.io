10 Easy Healthy Dinner Recipes for Busy Weeknights


As the day winds down and the evening approaches, the last thing on your mind is probably cooking a healthy dinner. With work, family, and social commitments, it’s easy to get caught up in the hustle and bustle and opt for a quick, yet unhealthy, fix. However, with a little planning and prep, you can whip up a delicious and nutritious meal in no time.

Here are 10 easy healthy dinner recipes that are perfect for busy weeknights:

1. One-Pot Pasta with Tomatoes and Basil

  • 1 pound pasta
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped (optional)

Cook pasta, cherry tomatoes, onion, and garlic in a large pot with vegetable broth and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve with chopped fresh basil leaves, if desired.

2. Baked Salmon with Lemon and Herbs

  • 4 salmon fillets, 6 ounces each
  • 2 lemons, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place salmon fillets on the baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, lemon slices, parsley, and dill. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until cooked through.

3. Black Bean and Sweet Potato Tacos

  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 taco shells
  • Optional toppings: shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, salsa, avocado

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss sweet potato with olive oil, cumin, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Cook black beans with onion and garlic in a skillet. Warm taco shells according to package instructions. Assemble tacos with roasted sweet potato, black beans, and desired toppings.

4. Spaghetti Squash with Meatballs

  • 2 medium spaghetti squash
  • 1 pound ground turkey
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut spaghetti squash in half lengthwise and scoop out seeds. Place squash on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 30-40 minutes or until tender. Meanwhile, cook ground turkey with onion and garlic in a skillet. Mix with egg, breadcrumbs, salt, and pepper. Form into meatballs and bake for 15-20 minutes or until cooked through. Serve meatballs on top of roasted spaghetti squash.

5. Chicken and Vegetable Stir-Fry

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as broccoli, bell peppers, carrots)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Cook chicken and mixed vegetables in a wok or large skillet with vegetable oil and garlic. Stir in soy sauce and season with salt and pepper to taste.

6. Lentil Soup

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Cook lentils, vegetable broth, onion, garlic, carrot, and celery in a large pot. Stir in diced tomatoes and thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

7. Quesadillas with Chicken and Cheese

  • 1 pound cooked chicken breast, shredded
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (such as cheddar or Monterey Jack)
  • 2 large tortillas
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional toppings: diced tomatoes, shredded lettuce, diced avocado, sour cream

Heat olive oil in a large skillet. Place one tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle with shredded cheese and cooked chicken. Fold tortilla in half and cook for 2-3 minutes or until cheese is melted and tortilla is crispy. Flip and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes or until crispy and golden. Repeat with remaining ingredients.

8. Roasted Chicken Thighs with Potatoes and Carrots

  • 4 chicken thighs
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper. Place chicken thighs, potatoes, and carrots on the baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper to taste. Roast for 25-30 minutes or until chicken is cooked through and vegetables are tender.

9. Grilled Chicken and Vegetable Skewers

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as cherry tomatoes, mushrooms, bell peppers)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Thread chicken and mixed vegetables onto skewers. Brush with olive oil and season with garlic, salt, and pepper. Grill for 10-15 minutes or until chicken is cooked through and vegetables are tender.

10. Breakfast for Dinner: Scrambled Eggs and Spinach with Whole Wheat Toast

  • 4 eggs
  • 1 cup fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 slices whole wheat bread

Cook scrambled eggs in a skillet with butter and salt and pepper to taste. Add fresh spinach leaves and cook until wilted. Serve with whole wheat toast.

These easy and healthy dinner recipes are perfect for busy weeknights. With a little planning and prep, you can whip up a delicious and nutritious meal in no time. Whether you’re in the mood for pasta, seafood, or stir-fry, there’s something on this list for everyone. So go ahead, get cooking, and enjoy a stress-free dinner with your loved ones.
