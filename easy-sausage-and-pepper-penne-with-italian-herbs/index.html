Easy Sausage and Pepper Penne with Italian Herbs


Looking for a hearty, one-pan dish that combines the bold flavors of Italian-inspired comfort food with the simplicity of weeknight cooking? This Easy Sausage and Pepper Penne with Italian Herbs is a meal that brings savory, colorful, and wholesome ingredients together in perfect harmony. Bursting with flavors from aromatic herbs, tender pasta, juicy sausage, and vibrant bell peppers, this recipe is an effortless choice when you’re short on time but crave something satisfying.

Whether you’re cooking for your family, hosting a casual dinner party, or meal prepping for the week, this dish will quickly become a go-to favorite. With minimal prep, a short cook time, and ingredients you can easily find at your local grocery store, you’ll be left with a beautiful, crowd-pleasing dish that’s as flavorful as it is simple.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we dive into the steps, gather everything you need for this delicious sausage and pepper penne. Keeping your ingredients prepped and organized ensures a smooth cooking process.

For the Pasta:

  • 12 oz (340 g) penne pasta: Penne is ideal for this recipe as its shape catches the sauce and ingredients beautifully. Feel free to substitute with rigatoni or ziti if needed.
  • Salt: For salting your pasta water, which helps to enhance the flavor of the noodles.

For the Sausage and Peppers:

  • 1 lb (450 g) Italian sausage: Use a mix of sweet and spicy Italian sausage, or stick to just one variety based on your preference. Both casings-on and ground sausage work here.
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil: For sautéing and adding extra depth of flavor.
  • 1 red bell pepper: Sliced thin for a pop of sweetness and color.
  • 1 yellow bell pepper: Brings a slightly milder sweetness, balancing the dish.
  • 1 green bell pepper: Adds earthiness and vibrant contrast.
  • 1 medium yellow onion: Thinly sliced to create caramel-like flavor when cooked.
  • 4 cloves garlic: Minced or finely chopped for savory, aromatic goodness.

For the Sauce:

  • 1 (14 oz) can of crushed tomatoes: This forms the base for your flavorful sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste: Enhances the tomato flavor and thickens the sauce.
  • ½ cup chicken broth: Adds moisture and richness to the sauce.
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning: A blend of basil, oregano, thyme, and rosemary that ties the dish together.
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes: Optional, for a hint of heat.
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika: Elevates the smokiness of the sausage.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper: Season to taste.
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar: Balances the acidity of the tomatoes.

Optional For Garnish:

  • Fresh basil leaves: Chopped or torn for a fresh, vibrant touch.
  • Grated Parmesan cheese: For an umami-packed finish.

Instructions: Step-by-Step Guide

Let’s get to cooking! This recipe comes together quickly, so follow along with these easy steps to make your Sausage and Pepper Penne with Italian Herbs.

1. Cook the Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add your penne pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. This should take about 9–11 minutes. Once cooked, reserve ½ cup of the starchy pasta water and set it aside. Drain the pasta and toss it with a drizzle of olive oil to prevent sticking, then set aside.

2. Sauté the Sausage

Heat a large skillet or sauté pan over medium heat. Add 1 tablespoon of olive oil, followed by the sausage. If you’re using sausage links, remove the casings beforehand. Break up the sausage into crumbles with a wooden spoon as it cooks, allowing it to brown evenly.

Cook for about 6–7 minutes, or until the sausage is fully cooked and no longer pink. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the cooked sausage to a plate, and leave the rendered fat in the pan for added flavor.

3. Cook the Peppers and Onion

In the same skillet, add the sliced bell peppers and onion. Sauté over medium heat, tossing occasionally, until the vegetables soften and begin to caramelize. This should take about 5–7 minutes. Add a pinch of salt to help draw out the moisture.

When the vegetables are tender and lightly golden, add the minced garlic. Sauté for 30 seconds to 1 minute, just until fragrant.

4. Build the Sauce

To the skillet with the vegetables, add the tomato paste and cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly. This helps deepen the tomato flavor. Deglaze the pan with chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet.

Stir in the crushed tomatoes, Italian seasoning, red pepper flakes, smoked paprika, sugar, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 8–10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

5. Combine the Ingredients

Return the cooked sausage to the skillet, stirring to combine it with the sauce and vegetables. Allow everything to cook together for 2–3 minutes, letting the sausage infuse the sauce with its savory flavor.

Add your cooked penne pasta to the skillet, tossing to coat every piece evenly in the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, use the reserved pasta water, adding a splash at a time until the dish reaches your desired consistency.

6. Taste and Serve

Taste the pasta and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or red pepper flakes as needed. Transfer the sausage and pepper penne to serving plates or a large family-style bowl.

Garnish with freshly chopped basil leaves and a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips for Success

  • Pick your sausage wisely: Sweet and spicy Italian sausages each bring a unique flavor profile. Use a mix of both for variety or stick to your personal favorite.
  • Cook the pasta al dente: Slightly undercooked pasta retains its texture once it’s tossed in the sauce, preventing it from becoming mushy.
  • Don’t skip the garlic: Garlic adds essential depth and authenticity to this Italian-inspired dish. Fresh garlic is preferable, but garlic powder can work in a pinch.
  • Make it your own: Add extra veggies like mushrooms, zucchini, or cherry tomatoes to increase the nutritional value and bulk up the dish further.

Make-Ahead and Storage

This Sausage and Pepper Penne is not only quick to prepare but also perfect for leftovers. Here are a few storage tips to keep your dish fresh:

  • Refrigerator: Store any leftover pasta in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3–4 days. Reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop with a splash of water or chicken broth to loosen the sauce.
  • Freezer: While the pasta itself is best enjoyed fresh, you can prepare the sausage, pepper, and sauce mixture ahead of time. Cool it completely, then freeze in a sealed container for up to 2 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating and combining it with freshly cooked pasta.

Bringing It All Together

This Easy Sausage and Pepper Penne with Italian Herbs is proof that you don’t need a long list of complicated ingredients or hours in the kitchen to create a dish that’s bursting with flavor. Its combination of tender pasta, bold sausage, sweet peppers, and savory herbs makes it the kind of recipe you’ll find yourself coming back to again and again.

Next time you’re planning a meal, give this recipe a try. Pair it with a crisp green salad and warm garlic bread for a complete Italian-inspired dinner that will leave everyone at the table asking for seconds. Happy cooking!
