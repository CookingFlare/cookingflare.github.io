Spice it Up: 5 Cajun-Inspired Marinades for Blackened Fish


If you’re a seafood lover looking to add some bold flavors to your blackened fish, look no further! In this post, we’ll explore five delicious Cajun-inspired marinades that will take your blackened fish to a whole new level. These marinades are packed with spices, herbs, and other ingredients that will infuse your fish with incredible flavors. Whether you prefer a tangy, spicy, or smoky taste, there’s a marinade here for everyone. So, let’s dive in and spice up your next fish dish!

1. Classic Cajun Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, thyme, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Add olive oil and lemon juice to the spice mixture, and whisk until well combined.
  3. Place your fish fillets in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them.
  4. Ensure the fish is evenly coated with the marinade, and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least one hour, or preferably overnight.
  5. When ready to cook, remove the fish from the marinade and pat dry with paper towels before blackening it in a hot skillet or grill.

2. Spicy Cajun Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine Cajun seasoning, hot sauce, Worcestershire sauce, minced garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  2. Stir well until all the ingredients are thoroughly mixed.
  3. Place your fish fillets in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them.
  4. Make sure the fish is coated with the marinade, and refrigerate for at least one hour, or overnight.
  5. Remove the fish from the marinade and cook it in a hot skillet or grill until blackened and cooked through.

3. Smoky Cajun Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon dried thyme
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine smoked paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, oregano, cayenne pepper, and salt.
  2. Add olive oil and apple cider vinegar to the spice mixture, and whisk until well blended.
  3. Place your fish fillets in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them.
  4. Ensure the fish is evenly coated with the marinade, and refrigerate for at least one hour, or overnight.
  5. Remove the fish from the marinade, and cook it in a hot skillet or grill until blackened and cooked to perfection.

4. Tangy Cajun Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons Creole mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together Creole mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, minced garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice.
  2. Make sure all the ingredients are well combined.
  3. Place your fish fillets in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them.
  4. Coat the fish with the marinade, and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least one hour, or overnight.
  5. Remove the fish from the marinade, and cook it in a hot skillet or grill until blackened and cooked through.

5. Herb-infused Cajun Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup lime juice

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine Cajun seasoning, chopped parsley, thyme, basil, oregano, minced garlic, olive oil, and lime juice.
  2. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly incorporated.
  3. Place your fish fillets in a ziplock bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them.
  4. Make sure the fish is coated with the marinade, and refrigerate for at least one hour, or overnight.
  5. Remove the fish from the marinade, and cook it in a hot skillet or grill until blackened and cooked to perfection.

That concludes our exploration of five Cajun-inspired marinades for blackened fish. These flavor-packed marinades are sure to bring a taste of Louisiana to your dinner table. Experiment with different combinations of spices and herbs to create your own signature marinade. Whether you prefer it spicy, tangy, or smoky, these marinades will elevate your blackened fish to new heights. Enjoy the bold flavors, and happy cooking!
