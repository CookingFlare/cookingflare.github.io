Brazilian BBQ Recipes for Your Next Cookout


Are you ready to take your cookout to the next level? Look no further than Brazilian BBQ recipes! Known as churrasco, Brazilian BBQ is a feast for the senses, combining smoky flavors, tender meats, and vibrant marinades. Whether you’re hosting a backyard gathering or simply craving a taste of Brazil, these recipes will transport you to the sunny shores of Rio de Janeiro. Get your grill fired up and let’s dive into the world of Brazilian BBQ!

1. Picanha (Brazilian Steak)

Let’s start with the star of the show - Picanha! This juicy and flavorful cut of beef is a staple in Brazilian BBQ. To prepare it, start by seasoning the picanha with coarse salt and pepper. Let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

Next, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the picanha directly on the grill, fat-side down. Cook for about 5-6 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches your desired level of doneness. For medium-rare, aim for an internal temperature of 130°F (54°C).

Once cooked, remove the picanha from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing. Serve it with chimichurri sauce, a traditional Argentinean condiment made with parsley, garlic, olive oil, vinegar, and red pepper flakes. The combination of the tender meat and tangy sauce will have your guests coming back for seconds!

2. Frango Piri-Piri (Spicy Grilled Chicken)

If you’re looking for a flavorful alternative to beef, try Frango Piri-Piri. This spicy grilled chicken dish is a crowd-pleaser, perfect for those who enjoy a bit of heat. To make it, start by marinating chicken pieces in a mixture of olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Let the chicken marinate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for maximum flavor.

When you’re ready to grill, preheat your grill to medium heat. Place the chicken on the grill and cook for about 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C). Baste the chicken with the marinade occasionally to keep it moist and flavorful.

Once cooked, remove the chicken from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with a side of rice or a crisp salad. The spicy kick of the chicken combined with the smoky flavors from the grill will have your taste buds dancing!

3. Linguiça (Brazilian Sausage)

No Brazilian BBQ is complete without Linguiça, a traditional Brazilian sausage bursting with flavor. To prepare it, start by soaking the sausage casings in warm water for about 30 minutes. This will make them easier to handle and prevent them from bursting during grilling.

Next, mix ground pork with garlic, paprika, salt, black pepper, and a pinch of red pepper flakes. Stuff the mixture into the sausage casings, twisting them at regular intervals to form individual sausages.

Preheat your grill to medium heat and grill the Linguiça for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until they are browned and cooked through.

Serve the Linguiça with a side of farofa, a traditional Brazilian side dish made from toasted cassava flour. The combination of the smoky sausage and the crunchy texture of the farofa will transport you straight to Brazil!

Conclusion

Brazilian BBQ recipes are a true celebration of flavors and a fantastic addition to your next cookout. From juicy Picanha to spicy Frango Piri-Piri and flavorful Linguiça, these dishes will impress your guests and leave them craving more. So fire up the grill, gather your friends and family, and embark on a culinary journey to Brazil. Bom apetite!
