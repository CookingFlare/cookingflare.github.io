Tex-Mex Queso Dip: A Cheesy and Spicy Appetizer


If you’re a fan of Tex-Mex cuisine and love a good cheese dip, then you’re in for a treat! Today, we’re going to whip up a delicious Tex-Mex Queso Dip that is sure to satisfy your cravings. This cheesy and spicy appetizer is perfect for parties, game nights, or even just a cozy night in. So grab your tortilla chips and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (10 ounces) diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup milk
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened and cubed
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a medium-sized saucepan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and jalapeno pepper, and sauté until they become soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.

  3. Add the diced tomatoes with green chilies to the saucepan, along with the cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Stir well to combine all the flavors.

  4. Pour in the milk and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Gradually add the cream cheese to the saucepan, stirring constantly until it has melted and incorporated into the mixture.

  6. Reduce the heat to low and slowly add the shredded cheddar cheese, stirring continuously until it melts and creates a smooth and creamy consistency.

  7. Remove the queso dip from the heat and stir in the chopped cilantro for a burst of fresh flavor.

  8. Transfer the Tex-Mex Queso Dip to a serving bowl and garnish with additional chopped cilantro if desired.

  9. Serve the dip warm with a side of tortilla chips, and watch it disappear within minutes!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of spice, you can leave the seeds in the jalapeno pepper or add a dash of cayenne pepper to the dip.
  • If you prefer a milder flavor, you can substitute the diced tomatoes with green chilies for a can of regular diced tomatoes.
  • Feel free to customize your Tex-Mex Queso Dip by adding your favorite toppings such as diced tomatoes, green onions, or even cooked ground beef for a heartier version.
  • Leftover dip can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it in a saucepan over low heat, stirring occasionally, until warmed through.

Final Thoughts

Tex-Mex Queso Dip is a classic appetizer that combines the creamy richness of cheese with the bold flavors of Tex-Mex spices. This crowd-pleasing dish is the perfect way to kick off any gathering or even enjoy as a snack on a lazy Sunday afternoon. So go ahead, give this recipe a try, and get ready to indulge in a cheesy and spicy delight that will leave your taste buds craving for more. Enjoy!
