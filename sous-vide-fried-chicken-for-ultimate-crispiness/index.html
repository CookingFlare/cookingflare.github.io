Sous Vide Fried Chicken for Ultimate Crispiness


When it comes to fried chicken, everyone has their own preference—some like it juicy and tender on the inside, while others crave that perfect crispy crunch on the outside. For many, achieving the ultimate fried chicken texture seems elusive, but with the wonders of sous vide cooking, you can achieve a level of tenderness and crispiness that’s hard to beat. This sous vide fried chicken recipe is the answer to your chicken cravings, combining the precision of sous vide cooking with the classic frying technique for the ultimate crispy fried chicken experience.

What is Sous Vide Cooking?

Sous vide is a French term that translates to “under vacuum.” It refers to a cooking method in which food is sealed in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooked in a temperature-controlled water bath. This technique allows for precise control over the cooking temperature, ensuring that your food cooks evenly throughout and retains its moisture.

While sous vide is often associated with meats like steak and fish, it works wonders for chicken as well. When chicken is cooked sous vide, the result is incredibly tender and juicy, as the meat retains all of its natural flavors and moisture. This is the foundation for our sous vide fried chicken recipe, which combines the perfect sous vide cooking method with a crispy, golden crust that will make your taste buds sing.

Why Sous Vide for Fried Chicken?

Traditionally, fried chicken can sometimes be dry on the inside or unevenly cooked, but sous vide eliminates these issues by ensuring the chicken is cooked to the exact level of doneness you desire before you even begin frying. The sous vide method allows the chicken to cook slowly at a consistent temperature, which means the meat is perfectly juicy and tender when it’s time to fry. The frying process, which follows sous vide cooking, is solely focused on achieving that crispy, golden coating, so you never have to worry about overcooking or undercooking the chicken.

In short, sous vide cooking allows you to focus on the crispy, fried exterior, while the sous vide method takes care of the tender, moist interior. It’s a game-changer for anyone looking to take their fried chicken game to the next level.

The Ultimate Sous Vide Fried Chicken Recipe

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process to make your very own sous vide fried chicken. This recipe includes tips for both sous vide cooking and frying, ensuring you get the crispiest, juiciest fried chicken you’ve ever had.

Ingredients

For the sous vide chicken:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs (you can also use bone-in chicken if you prefer)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika (for a touch of smoky flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed (optional)

For the breading:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for extra crunch)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk (for a tangy, crispy coating)

For frying:

  • 4 cups vegetable oil or peanut oil (enough to submerge the chicken in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot)

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken for Sous Vide Cooking

Start by prepping your chicken for sous vide cooking. You can use chicken breasts or thighs, depending on your preference, or even opt for bone-in pieces for added flavor. The beauty of sous vide is that it works with all cuts of chicken, though boneless thighs or breasts are often preferred for their tenderness and quick cooking time.

  1. Seasoning: In a small bowl, combine salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, and dried thyme. Rub this seasoning blend all over the chicken pieces. Make sure to coat every inch of the meat to ensure maximum flavor.

  2. Vacuum Sealing: Place each seasoned chicken piece into a vacuum-seal bag. If you’re using a sous vide bag, make sure to seal it tightly according to your device’s instructions. You can also use a Ziploc bag and employ the water displacement method to remove excess air from the bag.

  3. Add Aromatics (Optional): For added flavor, you can include smashed garlic cloves inside the bag with the chicken. This will infuse the chicken with subtle garlic undertones during cooking.

Step 2: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Set Up the Sous Vide Bath: Fill your sous vide container with water and set your sous vide immersion circulator to 140°F (60°C) for chicken breasts or 165°F (73.8°C) for chicken thighs. If you prefer your chicken a little more tender and juicy, you can lower the temperature to 145°F (63.8°C) for breasts.

  2. Cook the Chicken: Once the water has reached the desired temperature, place the vacuum-sealed bag into the water bath. Make sure the chicken is fully submerged and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours. Sous vide cooking is very forgiving, so even if you leave it in the water bath for a little longer, the chicken will remain juicy and tender.

  3. Remove and Dry the Chicken: After the chicken has cooked, carefully remove the bags from the water. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes, and then take it out of the bag. Use paper towels to pat the chicken dry thoroughly. This step is crucial to ensure that the breading sticks well and the chicken doesn’t become soggy when frying.

Step 3: Prepare the Breading

While the chicken is cooking, you can prepare your breading mixture. This crispy coating is key to getting that irresistible crunch.

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, and cayenne pepper. The cornstarch is important for creating a crispier coating that won’t get soggy after frying.

  2. Whisk the Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, beat the egg and buttermilk together until well combined. The buttermilk will help the breading adhere to the chicken and also add a nice tang to the crust.

  3. Dredge the Chicken: Once the chicken is dry, dip each piece into the wet mixture, making sure it’s fully coated. Then, coat the chicken in the dry flour mixture, pressing lightly to ensure an even coating. Shake off any excess flour, and repeat for all the chicken pieces.

Step 4: Fry the Chicken

Now that your chicken is prepped and breaded, it’s time to fry! The goal is to achieve that perfect crispy crust without overcooking the chicken.

  1. Heat the Oil: In a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot, heat the vegetable or peanut oil to 350°F (175°C). You’ll need enough oil to fully submerge the chicken. A thermometer is crucial for maintaining the correct frying temperature.

  2. Fry the Chicken: Gently place the breaded chicken pieces into the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the chicken for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. The chicken is already cooked through from the sous vide process, so you’re just looking for that perfect, crispy exterior.

  3. Drain and Rest: Once fried, use tongs to remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving.

Step 5: Serving Your Sous Vide Fried Chicken

Your sous vide fried chicken is now ready to be enjoyed! Serve it with your favorite sides, such as mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or cornbread, for a classic Southern-style meal. The crispy exterior and juicy, tender interior will be the highlight of any dinner table.

Tips for the Best Sous Vide Fried Chicken

  • Use Bone-In Chicken for Extra Flavor: While boneless chicken breasts or thighs are quick and easy, using bone-in pieces can add more flavor. Bone-in chicken retains moisture and flavor better during the sous vide process.
  • Let the Chicken Rest After Frying: Don’t skip the resting step after frying. Allowing the chicken to rest helps the juices redistribute and ensures that every bite is as juicy as possible.
  • Experiment with Seasonings: Feel free to get creative with your seasoning blends. You can add some heat with hot sauce, chili powder, or cayenne pepper, or go for a more herbal flavor with rosemary or sage.
  • Use Peanut Oil for Frying: Peanut oil has a high smoking point, making it ideal for frying. It also adds a subtle nutty flavor that complements the chicken perfectly.
  • Double Fry for Extra Crispiness: If you want an even crispier crust, you can double fry the chicken. After the first fry, let the chicken rest for a few minutes, then fry it again for 1-2 minutes until extra crispy.

Conclusion

Sous vide fried chicken is a game-changing technique that combines the best of both worlds—the tenderness of sous vide and the crispy exterior of traditional fried chicken. With the perfect blend of flavors, textures, and seasonings, this method will elevate your fried chicken to a whole new level. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or simply craving a delicious meal, sous vide fried chicken will never disappoint. Give it a try and experience the ultimate in fried chicken perfection!
