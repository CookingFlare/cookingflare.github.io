Satisfyingly Sweet Plum Clafoutis: A Rustic Delight


There’s something undeniably charming about rustic desserts. They exude a sense of simplicity and comfort, yet never fail to impress with their delightful flavors and textures. If you’re looking to add a touch of old-world elegance to your dessert repertoire, look no further than the delightful and satisfyingly sweet Plum Clafoutis.

Embracing the Rustic Charm

Originating from the Limousin region of France, clafoutis is a classic dessert that beautifully showcases seasonal fruits in a custard-like batter. Traditionally made with cherries, this versatile dish can be adapted to highlight a variety of fruits, making it a perfect choice for showcasing the bounties of each season. In this recipe, we’ll be celebrating the juicy sweetness of plums, which bring a delightful tartness to the dish.

Ingredients

For the Clafoutis Batter:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of one lemon

For the Plums:

  • 4 ripe plums, pitted and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, for greasing the baking dish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and generously butter a 9-inch pie dish or cast-iron skillet.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, cream, vanilla extract, and lemon zest until well combined. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, whisking until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.

  3. Arrange the sliced plums in the buttered dish, ensuring they are evenly distributed. Sprinkle the plums with 2 tablespoons of sugar, allowing them to macerate slightly.

  4. Pour the prepared batter over the plums, ensuring that they are evenly covered.

  5. Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the clafoutis is puffed and golden brown on the edges, and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Once baked, allow the clafoutis to cool slightly before serving. It can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature, dusted with a light snowfall of powdered sugar.

Serving Suggestions

This Plum Clafoutis is a delightful treat on its own, but it can be elevated with a dollop of lightly whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The contrast between the warm, custardy clafoutis and the cool, creamy accompaniment is truly a match made in dessert heaven.

Embracing the Sweet Simplicity

The beauty of a Plum Clafoutis lies in its simplicity and rustic charm. The tender, juicy plums nestled in a creamy, vanilla-scented custard create a dessert that is both visually stunning and irresistibly delicious. Whether you’re entertaining guests or simply treating yourself to a cozy night in, this delightful dessert is sure to leave a lasting impression.

So, the next time you find yourself with an abundance of ripe plums, consider whipping up this satisfyingly sweet Plum Clafoutis. It’s a rustic delight that perfectly captures the essence of seasonal indulgence and simple, heartwarming pleasures.
