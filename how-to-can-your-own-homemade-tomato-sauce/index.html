How to Can Your Own Homemade Tomato Sauce


Making your own homemade tomato sauce is a rewarding experience. Not only does it allow you to control the flavor, but it also gives you the satisfaction of preserving the summer harvest to enjoy throughout the year. If you’ve ever thought about canning your own tomato sauce, this guide will take you step-by-step through the process. From choosing the best tomatoes to canning them properly, you’ll learn how to create a delicious homemade sauce that can be stored for months.

Why Can Your Own Tomato Sauce?

There are several benefits to making your own tomato sauce rather than buying it from the store. For starters, homemade tomato sauce is fresher, with more vibrant flavors. Store-bought sauces often contain preservatives and artificial ingredients, whereas when you make your own, you get to decide exactly what goes into the pot.

Additionally, canning your own sauce is an excellent way to preserve the abundance of fresh tomatoes when they are in season. By canning, you can enjoy the taste of summer all year long, without relying on store-bought varieties that might not live up to the flavors you’re craving.

What You Need to Get Started

Before you dive into the world of canning, it’s essential to gather the necessary tools and ingredients. Here’s a quick rundown of what you’ll need to make homemade tomato sauce and preserve it for future use.

Ingredients

  • Fresh tomatoes (Roma, plum, or heirloom are ideal for making sauce)
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic (optional, for added flavor)
  • Salt
  • Sugar (optional, to balance the acidity)
  • Lemon juice (to acidify the sauce for safe canning)
  • Fresh herbs like basil or oregano (optional, for a fragrant touch)

Equipment

  • Canning jars (pint or quart-sized)
  • Canning lids and rings
  • A large pot for blanching tomatoes
  • A food mill or sieve (to remove skins and seeds from the tomatoes)
  • A large saucepan (for cooking the sauce)
  • A canning pot or water bath canner
  • Tongs and jar lifters (for handling hot jars)
  • A funnel (for easy pouring into jars)

Picking the Right Tomatoes

Choosing the right tomatoes is crucial to making a good tomato sauce. The best tomatoes for sauce are those that have a firm flesh, fewer seeds, and a low moisture content. Roma tomatoes, also known as plum tomatoes, are widely regarded as the best choice for canning because they have a meaty texture that holds up well when cooked into a sauce.

However, you can use almost any tomato variety for making sauce. Heirloom varieties will add unique flavors, and vine-ripened tomatoes will give you that fresh, summery taste. If you’re using larger tomatoes like beefsteak or brandywine, be sure to remove the excess water and seeds for a thicker, more concentrated sauce.

Step-by-Step Guide to Canning Tomato Sauce

1. Prepare Your Tomatoes

The first step is to prepare the tomatoes. Start by washing them thoroughly to remove any dirt or chemicals. Next, you’ll need to blanch the tomatoes to make it easier to remove the skins.

How to Blanch Tomatoes:

  • Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
  • While the water is heating, score an “X” at the bottom of each tomato with a sharp knife.
  • Once the water is boiling, carefully place the tomatoes into the water using a slotted spoon. Do this in batches if necessary.
  • Let the tomatoes sit in the boiling water for about 30 seconds, or until you see the skin beginning to peel back.
  • Quickly transfer the tomatoes to an ice bath to stop the cooking process and cool them down.
  • Once cooled, peel off the skins and discard them.

Blanching is a crucial step because it helps loosen the skins, making them easy to remove without losing any of the tomato’s precious juice.

2. Remove Seeds and Purée the Tomatoes

Once the skins are removed, cut the tomatoes in half and scoop out the seeds. You can use your hands or a spoon to remove them. If you want a completely smooth sauce, you can also use a food mill or sieve to remove the seeds while puréeing the tomatoes.

Use a blender or food processor to purée the tomatoes until smooth or chunkier, depending on your texture preference. If you like a thicker sauce, cook the tomato purée down for a longer period to reduce the water content and concentrate the flavor.

3. Cook the Tomato Sauce

Now that you have your tomato purée ready, it’s time to cook the sauce. Pour the tomato purée into a large saucepan or stockpot. This is when you can start adding flavor to your sauce.

  • Add olive oil to the pan to create a rich base for the sauce.
  • Chop a few cloves of garlic and sauté them in the olive oil to release their fragrance, if desired.
  • Season with salt, and add a pinch of sugar if the sauce tastes too acidic. Stir to combine.
  • You can also add fresh herbs like basil or oregano for extra flavor. Simmer for about 30-45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened to your liking.

Be sure to taste your sauce throughout the cooking process, adjusting the seasoning as needed. A pinch of salt and sugar can make all the difference, and you can always add more herbs for a more aromatic sauce.

4. Prepare for Canning

Once your sauce has thickened to the desired consistency, it’s time to prepare your jars for canning. Before you start, ensure that your jars, lids, and rings are clean and sterilized.

How to Sterilize Jars:

  • Wash the jars with hot, soapy water or run them through the dishwasher.
  • Place the jars in a large pot of water and bring it to a boil. Boil the jars for about 10 minutes to sterilize them.
  • Alternatively, you can place the jars in the oven at 225°F (107°C) for about 20 minutes to sterilize them.

Once sterilized, place the jars on a clean towel or cooling rack and set aside.

5. Fill the Jars

Using a funnel, carefully ladle the hot tomato sauce into the prepared jars. Leave about 1/2-inch of headspace at the top of the jar to allow for expansion during the canning process.

After filling the jars, wipe the rims with a clean cloth to remove any sauce that may have spilled. This ensures that the lids will seal properly.

Place the lids on the jars and screw on the rings until they are finger-tight. Be careful not to overtighten, as this can affect the seal.

6. Process the Jars in a Water Bath

The next step in the canning process is to seal the jars by processing them in a water bath canner. This ensures that the tomato sauce is preserved safely and can be stored for months.

  • Fill your canning pot or large stockpot with water, making sure the water level is at least 1-2 inches above the top of the jars.
  • Bring the water to a boil.
  • Once boiling, carefully lower the jars into the water using jar lifters.
  • Process the jars for about 35-45 minutes. The exact time depends on your altitude and the size of the jars. Consult a canning guide for specific processing times if needed.
  • Once the processing time is complete, remove the jars from the water and place them on a clean towel or cooling rack.

7. Check the Seal

After the jars have cooled for several hours, check the seals. The lids should be concave, and when pressed in the center, they shouldn’t pop back. If a jar didn’t seal properly, refrigerate it and use the sauce within a few days.

The sealed jars can be stored in a cool, dark place for up to a year. Label the jars with the date so you know when they were canned.

8. Enjoy Your Homemade Tomato Sauce

Now that you’ve successfully canned your homemade tomato sauce, it’s time to enjoy it! Use it as a base for pasta dishes, pizza, soups, or stews. The possibilities are endless, and you’ll appreciate the depth of flavor that comes with your homemade creation.

Tips for Success

  • If you prefer a smooth sauce, be sure to strain out any seeds and skin during the puréeing process.
  • Adding herbs like basil, thyme, or oregano during the cooking process can enhance the flavor.
  • Always use fresh tomatoes for the best flavor. Canned tomatoes will not give you the same results.
  • When canning, always check the seal on the lids after processing to ensure your sauce stays preserved properly.

Final Thoughts

Canning homemade tomato sauce is a great way to enjoy the fruits of your labor throughout the year. With the right tomatoes and a bit of patience, you can make a sauce that is rich, flavorful, and free of preservatives. Whether you’re making it to enjoy now or to store for later, homemade tomato sauce will always be a staple in your kitchen.
