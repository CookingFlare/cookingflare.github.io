Grilled Chicken Recipes for Summer BBQs


As the sun shines brighter and the days grow longer, it’s the perfect time to fire up the grill and gather with friends and family for a delicious summer BBQ. Among the many options you can toss on the grill, grilled chicken remains one of the most popular and versatile choices. Whether you’re a beginner or a seasoned BBQ master, there’s always a way to elevate your grilled chicken recipes to suit your tastes and impress your guests.

In this blog post, we will walk you through a variety of grilled chicken recipes, from the basics to more adventurous and flavorful twists. We’ll also share tips and tricks for grilling the perfect chicken every time, ensuring that your summer BBQs are filled with mouthwatering dishes.

The Basics of Grilling Chicken

Before diving into specific recipes, it’s important to understand the basics of grilling chicken. Whether you’re grilling chicken breasts, thighs, wings, or whole chickens, there are some fundamental steps that will ensure your chicken comes out tender, juicy, and full of flavor.

1. Choose the Right Chicken Cuts

The first step in making great grilled chicken is selecting the right cuts. Here are some popular options for the grill:

  • Chicken Breasts: Skinless, boneless chicken breasts are lean and quick to cook but can dry out if overcooked.
  • Chicken Thighs: Thighs are fattier and more forgiving on the grill, resulting in juicier and more flavorful meat.
  • Chicken Wings: Perfect for finger food, wings are easy to marinate and grill. Their small size makes them quick to cook.
  • Whole Chicken: If you’re looking to feed a crowd, grilling a whole chicken is an impressive and delicious option. It can be cooked using a method called spatchcocking, which helps it cook evenly.

2. Marinate or Season the Chicken

Marinades and rubs are key to infusing chicken with flavor. A basic marinade usually consists of oil, acid (like lemon juice or vinegar), and seasonings. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes (or up to overnight) to maximize flavor. For rubs, mix spices and herbs to create a dry seasoning blend that coats the chicken for an aromatic finish.

3. Preheat the Grill

Make sure your grill is preheated to the right temperature before cooking. For direct grilling, aim for medium-high heat, while indirect grilling (such as for larger cuts like whole chickens) requires a lower heat to allow for longer cooking times without burning.

4. Cook the Chicken

Grilling chicken requires attention to avoid drying it out or burning it. For bone-in cuts, ensure the heat is not too high, as it will burn the outside before the inside is fully cooked. For boneless cuts, you can use a higher heat for faster cooking. Make sure to turn the chicken regularly to ensure even cooking and avoid flare-ups. Always use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature – it should reach 165°F (75°C) for safe consumption.

5. Rest the Chicken

Once the chicken is done, let it rest for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, keeping it tender and juicy.

Grilled Chicken Recipes to Try This Summer

Now that you’ve got the basics down, let’s dive into some delicious grilled chicken recipes that will take your BBQ to the next level. Whether you’re looking for a classic dish or something with a bit more flair, these recipes will satisfy everyone at your summer cookout.

1. Classic Grilled Lemon Herb Chicken

This recipe is a timeless favorite that never goes out of style. The fresh flavors of lemon, garlic, and herbs are perfect for chicken, and it’s incredibly easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 lemons (zested and juiced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, olive oil, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the chicken breasts in a resealable bag or shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Seal and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (or up to 4 hours).
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the chicken for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Remove from the grill and let the chicken rest for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh lemon slices and extra herbs.

2. Spicy BBQ Grilled Chicken

For those who love a little heat, this spicy BBQ chicken recipe is perfect. The combination of sweet and spicy BBQ sauce, along with a hint of smokiness from the grill, makes this dish irresistible.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the BBQ sauce, hot sauce, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper.
  2. Pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels and season them with salt and pepper.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down on the grill and cook for 7-8 minutes per side.
  4. Brush the chicken with the BBQ sauce mixture during the last 5 minutes of grilling.
  5. Once the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.

3. Grilled Honey Mustard Chicken Skewers

If you’re looking for a fun and easy way to serve grilled chicken, skewers are the way to go. These honey mustard chicken skewers are sweet, tangy, and perfect for any BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (cut into 1-inch cubes)
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Thread the chicken cubes onto skewers, alternating with vegetables like bell peppers or onions if desired.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Brush the chicken skewers with the honey mustard marinade.
  5. Grill the skewers for 5-7 minutes per side, until the chicken is cooked through and the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  6. Serve with extra honey mustard sauce for dipping.

4. Grilled Mediterranean Chicken

This Mediterranean-inspired recipe combines bold flavors like garlic, lemon, oregano, and olives, creating a zesty grilled chicken dish that’s perfect for summer.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken breasts
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  2. Coat the chicken breasts in the marinade, ensuring they are evenly covered. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the chicken for 6-8 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  5. Once the chicken is cooked, top with chopped Kalamata olives and serve with a side of grilled vegetables or a Greek salad.

5. Grilled Chicken with Mango Salsa

For a tropical twist, this grilled chicken recipe paired with a fresh mango salsa will transport your taste buds straight to paradise. The sweetness of the mango complements the smoky grilled chicken perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 ripe mangoes (peeled, pitted, and diced)
  • 1/2 red onion (finely chopped)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • 1 jalapeño (seeded and minced)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.
  2. Grill the chicken for 6-8 minutes per side, until fully cooked and the internal temperature reaches 165°F.
  3. While the chicken is grilling, prepare the mango salsa by combining the diced mango, red onion, cilantro, jalapeño, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Once the chicken is cooked, serve it with a generous topping of mango salsa.

Tips for Grilling Chicken Perfectly

  • Avoid Overcooking: One of the most common mistakes when grilling chicken is overcooking it, which can lead to dry meat. Always use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature.
  • Resting Time is Key: Let the chicken rest after grilling to allow the juices to redistribute.
  • Use Indirect Heat for Larger Cuts: If you’re grilling a whole chicken or large pieces, use indirect heat to cook it more evenly without burning the outside.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t be afraid to get creative with your marinades and rubs. The possibilities are endless, from sweet and tangy to spicy and savory.

Conclusion

Grilled chicken is the star of any summer BBQ, and with the right recipes and techniques, you can create a variety of delicious dishes that will impress your guests. From classic lemon herb chicken to spicy BBQ and tropical mango salsa, there’s a grilled chicken recipe for every taste. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and enjoy the best of summer with these flavorful and easy-to-make grilled chicken recipes. Happy grilling!
