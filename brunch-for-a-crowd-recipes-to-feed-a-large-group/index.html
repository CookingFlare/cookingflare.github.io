Brunch for a Crowd: Recipes to Feed a Large Group


When you’re planning a brunch for a large group, you need to strike the perfect balance between delicious food, easy preparation, and crowd-pleasing appeal. Brunch, a combination of breakfast and lunch, offers a relaxed yet refined atmosphere, making it the perfect occasion to enjoy a leisurely meal with friends and family. Whether you’re hosting a special celebration or just gathering for a weekend feast, you’ll want recipes that satisfy a variety of tastes and dietary preferences. From savory to sweet, we’ve rounded up an array of recipes that can feed a crowd with ease and style. These recipes are sure to elevate your brunch spread and keep everyone coming back for seconds.

1. Savory Strata: A Hearty Breakfast Casserole

A savory strata is the ultimate brunch dish for feeding a large group. This baked casserole is a combination of eggs, bread, cheese, and vegetables, all layered and soaked in a rich custard mixture before being baked to perfection. You can easily customize it with a variety of ingredients, making it perfect for different tastes and dietary preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of day-old French bread, cut into cubes
  • 10 large eggs
  • 2 cups of whole milk
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup of cooked sausage or bacon (optional)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 cup of spinach or kale (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, pepper, and thyme.
  3. Layer the bread cubes in the prepared baking dish, then sprinkle with cooked sausage or bacon, onion, bell pepper, spinach, and cheese.
  4. Pour the egg mixture evenly over the layers, pressing down slightly to ensure the bread soaks up the custard.
  5. Cover with foil and refrigerate for at least one hour, or overnight for the best results.
  6. When ready to bake, remove the foil and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the top is golden and the eggs are set. Let cool slightly before slicing and serving.

2. Eggs Benedict: A Classic Brunch Favorite

Eggs Benedict is a quintessential brunch dish that is sure to impress your guests. While it may seem complicated at first, it’s actually quite easy to make in large batches, especially if you use a muffin tin to assemble individual portions.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 6 English muffins, split and toasted
  • 1 lb of Canadian bacon or ham
  • 2 cups of hollandaise sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Directions:

  1. Poach the eggs: Bring a large pot of water to a simmer. Add a splash of vinegar to help the eggs set. Crack each egg into a small bowl and gently slide into the water, cooking for 2-3 minutes, or until the whites are set and the yolks are still runny. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  2. Assemble the Benedict: Place a slice of Canadian bacon on each toasted English muffin half. Top with a poached egg.
  3. Drizzle with hollandaise sauce and garnish with fresh parsley.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy the richness of this indulgent brunch dish.

3. Build-Your-Own Pancake Bar

Pancakes are always a hit at brunch, but when you’re feeding a large group, it’s best to take it up a notch with a build-your-own pancake bar. This interactive setup allows guests to customize their pancakes with a variety of toppings and syrups.

Ingredients for Pancakes:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 4 tablespoons of melted butter

Topping Ideas:

  • Fresh berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries)
  • Sliced bananas
  • Whipped cream
  • Maple syrup
  • Nutella or chocolate sauce
  • Crushed nuts (pecans, walnuts)
  • Chopped chocolate
  • Powdered sugar

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients: eggs, milk, vanilla, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or cooking spray.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter onto the griddle for each pancake and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown.
  6. Set up a pancake station with all of the toppings and syrups, allowing guests to build their perfect pancake stack.

4. Bacon-Wrapped Avocado Toast

Avocado toast has become a brunch staple, and when you wrap it in crispy bacon, it’s taken to a whole new level. This dish combines creamy avocado with the smoky, salty flavor of bacon, all piled on top of toasted bread.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe avocados
  • 8 slices of bacon
  • 4 slices of hearty bread (sourdough or whole grain works best)
  • Lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Directions:

  1. Cook the bacon: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Lay the bacon slices on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until crispy. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  2. Prepare the avocado: Cut the avocados in half, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Mash the avocado with a fork and season with lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toast the bread: Toast the slices of bread in a toaster or on a grill pan until golden brown.
  4. Assemble: Spread a generous layer of mashed avocado on each piece of toast. Place a slice of crispy bacon on top of each toast. Optionally, sprinkle with red pepper flakes for some extra heat.
  5. Serve immediately, and enjoy the savory goodness of this brunch treat.

5. Fresh Fruit Salad with Honey-Lime Dressing

For a refreshing and healthy option, a fresh fruit salad with a tangy honey-lime dressing is a great addition to any brunch spread. This dish can be made in advance, allowing you more time to focus on the other elements of the meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of diced watermelon
  • 2 cups of diced pineapple
  • 1 cup of sliced strawberries
  • 1 cup of blueberries
  • 1 cup of grapes, halved
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine all of the fruit.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey and lime juice.
  3. Drizzle the honey-lime dressing over the fruit and toss gently to combine.
  4. Garnish with fresh mint leaves and serve chilled.

6. Mimosas and Bloody Marys Bar

No brunch is complete without a drink station to complement your meal. A mimosa and Bloody Mary bar lets guests mix their drinks to their liking. Set out different types of juice, sparkling wine, and garnishes to make it a fun and interactive experience.

Ingredients for Mimosas:

  • Orange juice
  • Cranberry juice
  • Pineapple juice
  • Sparkling wine or champagne

Ingredients for Bloody Marys:

  • Tomato juice
  • Vodka
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Hot sauce
  • Celery, olives, lemon wedges, pickles, and other garnishes

Directions:

  1. Set up a station with all the ingredients for mimosas and Bloody Marys.
  2. For mimosas, offer a variety of juices (orange, cranberry, and pineapple) along with champagne or sparkling wine.
  3. For Bloody Marys, provide tomato juice, vodka, and a selection of garnishes, including celery, olives, and lemon wedges.
  4. Allow guests to create their perfect brunch drink, and enjoy the fun atmosphere that these beverages bring.

7. Mini Quiches: Bite-Sized Savory Delights

Mini quiches are the perfect brunch appetizer or side dish. These bite-sized savory treats are packed with flavor and can be easily made in large batches. You can customize them with a variety of fillings like spinach, mushrooms, cheese, and bacon.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or several, depending on the number of quiches)
  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup of cream
  • 1 cup of shredded cheese (cheddar, Swiss, or a mix)
  • 1 cup of cooked spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of cooked bacon, crumbled
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a mini muffin tin or quiche pan.
  2. Roll out the pie crust and cut into small circles to fit into the muffin tin. Press down gently.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, cream, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add the cheese, spinach, and bacon to the egg mixture, stirring to combine.
  5. Pour the egg mixture into each pie crust until just about full.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the quiches are set and lightly browned on top.
  7. Serve warm or at room temperature.

8. Cinnamon Rolls with Cream Cheese Icing

For a sweet touch, homemade cinnamon rolls with cream cheese icing are the perfect way to end your brunch spread. They’re warm, gooey, and covered in a rich, tangy icing that will have everyone swooning.

Ingredients for Cinnamon Rolls:

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 packet of active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of cinnamon

For the Icing:

  • 8 oz of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup of powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the milk and butter until the butter is melted. Let it cool to lukewarm.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and yeast. Add the milk mixture, eggs, and vanilla extract. Knead the dough until smooth, then cover and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  3. Roll out the dough into a large rectangle. Spread the butter on top and sprinkle with brown sugar and cinnamon.
  4. Roll the dough up tightly and slice it into individual rolls. Place them on a greased baking sheet and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 20-25 minutes.
  6. While baking, prepare the icing by mixing cream cheese, powdered sugar, butter, and vanilla in a bowl until smooth.
  7. Once the rolls are done, drizzle with the cream cheese icing and serve warm.

Conclusion

Brunch for a crowd doesn’t have to be stressful. With these delicious and easy-to-make recipes, you can create a spread that satisfies everyone and creates a fun, relaxed atmosphere. From savory dishes like strata and eggs Benedict to sweet treats like cinnamon rolls and fruit salad, these recipes are sure to leave a lasting impression. So gather your ingredients, invite your friends and family, and get ready to host the best brunch ever!
