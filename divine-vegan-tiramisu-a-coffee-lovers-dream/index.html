Divine Vegan Tiramisu: A Coffee Lover's Dream


Are you a coffee lover with a sweet tooth? If so, you’re in for a treat! Today, I’ll be sharing with you a recipe for Divine Vegan Tiramisu, a dessert that will satisfy your cravings and leave you wanting more. This vegan twist on the classic Italian dessert is not only delicious, but it’s also cruelty-free and perfect for those following a plant-based lifestyle. So grab your apron and let’s get started!

Ingredients

For the coffee-soaked ladyfingers:

  • 1 cup of strong brewed coffee
  • 1 tablespoon of instant coffee granules
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 package of vegan ladyfingers

For the vegan mascarpone cream:

  • 1 ½ cups of raw cashews, soaked overnight and drained
  • ½ cup of coconut cream
  • ¼ cup of maple syrup
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

For the cocoa dusting:

  • 2 tablespoons of unsweetened cocoa powder

Instructions

  1. In a shallow dish, combine the brewed coffee, instant coffee granules, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Stir well to combine.

  2. Take each vegan ladyfinger and quickly dip it into the coffee mixture, making sure to coat both sides. Be careful not to soak them for too long, as they can become mushy. Place the soaked ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of an 8x8-inch baking dish. Repeat this process until all the ladyfingers are used.

  3. In a blender or food processor, combine the soaked cashews, coconut cream, maple syrup, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed. This will be your vegan mascarpone cream.

  4. Spoon half of the mascarpone cream over the layer of soaked ladyfingers in the baking dish. Smooth it out with a spatula to create an even layer.

  5. Repeat the process with another layer of soaked ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone cream. Smooth the top layer of cream with a spatula.

  6. Sift the unsweetened cocoa powder evenly over the top of the tiramisu, creating a dusting of cocoa.

  7. Cover the baking dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the tiramisu to set.

  8. When you’re ready to serve, slice the Divine Vegan Tiramisu into squares and plate each piece. You can garnish with a sprinkle of cocoa powder or a dollop of vegan whipped cream, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe gluten-free, simply use gluten-free vegan ladyfingers or substitute them with gluten-free almond cookies.

  • If you’re not a fan of coffee, you can use a combination of cocoa powder and almond milk to soak the ladyfingers instead.

  • Feel free to get creative with the toppings. You can add shaved vegan chocolate, crushed nuts, or even a drizzle of vegan caramel sauce.

  • This Divine Vegan Tiramisu can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, making it the perfect make-ahead dessert for dinner parties or special occasions.

There you have it, a recipe for Divine Vegan Tiramisu that will undoubtedly impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more. With its rich coffee flavor, creamy mascarpone cream, and cocoa dusting, this dessert is the perfect indulgence for any coffee lover. So go ahead, give it a try, and let me know how you like it. Enjoy!
