Vegan Mac & Cheeze: 15 Hearty & Scrumptious Plant-Based Recipes


Indulge in the creamy goodness of mac & cheeze without compromising your vegan beliefs. These 15 hearty and scrumptious plant-based recipes will satisfy your cravings while nourishing your body, proving that traditional comfort food can be equally delicious and healthy. Get ready to whip up some unforgettable flavors and enjoy every mouthful of this vegan delight!

1. Classic Cashew Cheeze Mac

Give your mac & cheeze a luxurious twist by including cashews in the recipe. These healthy nuts make the sauce rich, creamy, and flavorful with a hint of garlic and nutritional yeast. This classic recipe allows you to relish your childhood favorite while nourishing your body with wholesome ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw cashews, soaked for a minimum of 2 hours and drained
  • 3 cups plant-based unsweetened milk (preferably almond)
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 cup organic unsweetened apple sauce
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 packages (12 oz each) elbow macaroni
  • Optional: chopped chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F.
  2. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Add macaroni and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain, reserving 1 cup of cooking water.
  3. In a blender, combine drained cashews, unsweetened milk, nutritional yeast, apple sauce, olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Blend until completely smooth.
  4. Pour the sauce into a large mixing bowl, and additive in the cooked macaroni with reserved cooking water. Stir everything together to coat the macaroni evenly.
  5. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish, pour in the macaroni & cheeze, and bake uncovered for 35 minutes.
  6. Garnish with chopped chives and serve immediately.

2. Butternut Squash Cheeze Mac

Warm up this winter with a hint of seasonal butternut squash in your mac & cheeze. Substitute regular noodles for spiralized zucchini or sweet potato noodles for additional health benefits, and pair it with the smooth, natural sweetness of butternut squash. A winning combination for any vegan dinner party.

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, seeded and chopped (about 5 cups)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 3/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/2 cup raw cashews, soaked for at least 2 hours and drained
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1 cup finely chopped kale
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 1 package (12 oz) spiralized zucchini noodles, or any desired type of noodles

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Roast butternut squash, onion, and garlic on a lined or greased baking sheet for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  2. In a high-speed blender, blend roasted butternut squash mixture, almond milk, nutritional yeast, soaked cashews, olive oil, chopped kale, salt, pepper, and basil leaves until creamy and smooth.
  3. In a large pot, cook noodles according to the package instructions, then drain.
  4. Pour the butternut squash cheeze sauce over the drained noodles and mix until fully coated.
  5. Serve warm with additional fresh basil if desired.

3. Sazole Mac & Cheeze

Upgrade your traditional mac & cheeze with the earthy flavors of caramelized onions and roasted garlic in a vibrant sun-dried tomato sauce. Hearty and full of flavor, this Sazole Mac & Cheeze will win the hearts of both vegans and non-vegans alike.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups raw cashews, soaked for at least 2 hours and drained
  • 1 1/2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 8-10 sun-dried tomatoes, chopped, with 2 tablespoons of water
  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2-4 cloves roasted garlic (add more if desired)
  • 2-3 large caramelized onions (about 2 cups)
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 16 oz uncooked dry elbow macaroni
  • Chopped fresh basil or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a high-speed blender, combine cashews, almond milk, nutritional yeast, sun-dried tomatoes, water, olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, roasted garlic, caramelized onions, and black pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Add macaroni and cook for about 10 minutes or according to package instructions.
  3. Drain the cooking water, reserving 1/2 cup.
  4. Transfer macaroni to a large serving bowl, add the blended cheeze sauce and mix well, adding cooking water if a thinner consistency is desired.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley and serve immediately.

4. Broccoli Cheddar Mac & Cheeze

Not to be confused with the traditional cheese involved, this Broccoli Cheddar Mac & Cheeze is a vegan sensation. Protein-rich broccoli florets are paired with a deliciously savory sauce for a dish that’s sure to satisfy.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw cashews, soaked for a minimum of 2 hours and drained
  • 4 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 3/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cups shredded carrot or zucchini for the cheddar-like texture
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 package (12 oz) elbow macaroni
  • Chopped fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Toss broccoli and shredded carrot (or zucchini) on a lined or greased baking sheet. Roast for 25 minutes, stirring halfway through.
  3. Bring a large pot of water to boil. Add macaroni and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain, reserving 1 cup of cooking water.
  4. In a high-speed blender, combine cashews, almond milk, nutritional yeast, cornstarch, apple cider vinegar, roasted broccoli and carrot (or zucchini), garlic powder, onion powder, turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  5. Pour sauce over cooked macaroni and mix gently, adding reserved cooking water for desired consistency.
  6. Serve warm and garnish with chives.

By experimenting with a variety of recipes and flavors, you’ll discover your newfound favorite vegan comfort foods and make healthier choices without ever feeling deprived. These hearty and scrumptious vegan mac & cheeze recipes cater to everyone’s taste buds and are sure to make you fall in love with plant-based cuisine all over again.
