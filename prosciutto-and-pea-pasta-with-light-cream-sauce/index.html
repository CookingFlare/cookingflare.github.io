Prosciutto and Pea Pasta with Light Cream Sauce


When comfort meets elegance, you get a dish like prosciutto and pea pasta with light cream sauce. This recipe combines the salty richness of thinly sliced prosciutto with the natural sweetness of fresh peas, all brought together by a silky cream sauce that’s both indulgent and understated. Whether you’re preparing a weeknight dinner or hosting a dinner party, this dish never fails to impress with its simplicity and sublime flavors.

Choosing the right ingredients is key to bringing out the best in this recipe. By focusing on fresh vegetables, quality prosciutto, and al dente pasta, you can create a meal that feels gourmet without requiring hours of preparation. Let’s delve into how you can make this delightful dish at home.

Ingredients for Prosciutto and Pea Pasta

Before diving into the cooking process, gathering your ingredients is an essential step. Here’s a list of what you’ll need to craft the perfect prosciutto and pea pasta:

Pasta:

  • 12 ounces of fettuccine or tagliatelle – You want long, flat noodles that can hold the cream sauce and mix harmoniously with the peas and prosciutto.

Meat and Vegetables:

  • 4 ounces of thinly sliced prosciutto – Ideally purchase high-quality prosciutto with a nice salt balance and thin ribbons that are easy to tear.
  • 1 cup of fresh or frozen peas – Fresh is better if they’re in season, but frozen peas work wonderfully for this dish.

Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter – Butter is a subtle base for starting your cream sauce.
  • 2 cloves of garlic, finely minced – Garlic provides a depth of flavor without overpowering.
  • 1 cup heavy cream – For the light cream sauce, this is essential.
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese – The tangy sharpness of Parmesan balances the creaminess of the sauce.
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste – Use sparingly, as prosciutto is slightly salty.

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh chopped parsley or basil – Bright, fresh herbs for a pop of color and flavor.
  • Crushed red pepper flakes – For those who enjoy a bit of heat.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Cooking Prosciutto and Pea Pasta

Follow these simple steps to bring the flavors of Italy into your kitchen.

1. Cook the Pasta

Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, add a generous pinch of salt to season the water. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, ensuring it’s al dente. This is crucial, as the noodles will absorb some of the sauce later. Drain the pasta, but reserve about 1 cup of the pasta water—it will be helpful for adjusting the sauce consistency later.

2. Prepare the Prosciutto

While the pasta cooks, cut the prosciutto into thin strips or tear it into small bite-sized pieces. Prosciutto is delicate and works beautifully in small portions, so no need to over-complicate this step.

3. Sauté the Garlic

In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. Once melted, add the minced garlic, stirring gently for about 1 minute until fragrant. Garlic burns quickly, so be careful not to let it become overly browned, as that can turn the flavor bitter.

4. Create the Cream Sauce

Lower the heat slightly and pour in the heavy cream. Slowly stir while the cream warms up, allowing it to meld with the garlicky butter base. Gradually add the grated Parmesan cheese, whisking to ensure it melts completely into the cream. Season the sauce with freshly cracked black pepper and a tiny pinch of salt, keeping in mind that prosciutto will naturally add saltiness.

5. Add the Peas

Toss the peas into the skillet with the cream sauce. If using frozen peas, you won’t need to pre-cook them—they’ll soften as they simmer in the sauce. If using fresh peas, blanch them briefly in a pot of salted water before adding them to ensure they’re tender.

6. Incorporate the Prosciutto

Gently fold the strips of prosciutto into the sauce. The heat will lightly render the fat in the prosciutto, enhancing its flavor and making it mingle beautifully with the cream and peas.

7. Toss with the Pasta

Add cooked pasta directly into the skillet and toss to coat the noodles evenly with the sauce. Use a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce if it seems too thick—start with a couple of tablespoons at a time until you reach your desired consistency.

Tips for Elevating Your Prosciutto and Pea Pasta

  • Choose the Right Pasta Shape: While fettuccine or tagliatelle are ideal, feel free to experiment with other shapes like farfalle or rigatoni. These shapes work particularly well if you prefer bite-sized pasta pieces over long strands.

  • Experiment with Vegetables: While peas are the perfect sweet counterpart to prosciutto, other vegetables like asparagus or zucchini can be substituted or added for variation.

  • Use Fresh Parmesan: For the most authentic flavor, skip pre-grated cheese and grate your Parmesan straight from the wedge. The freshly grated cheese melts more smoothly into sauces and has a superior taste.

  • Add Lemon Zest: For a burst of citrusy freshness, sprinkle the dish with lemon zest before serving. This adds a subtle acidity that cuts through the richness of the cream sauce.

  • Pair with Wine: Serve this dish with a crisp white wine like Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc to complement the creamy texture and salty notes of prosciutto.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

This prosciutto and pea pasta recipe strikes the perfect balance between refinement and ease. It’s a testament to the magic that happens when a few simple ingredients come together to create something extraordinary. The light cream sauce coats the pasta just enough to make it indulgent without being overly heavy, and the interplay of sweet peas and salty prosciutto makes every bite a delight.

In just one skillet and one pot, you can transform pantry staples into a restaurant-worthy meal. Best of all, this dish is versatile—you can dress it up for a formal gathering or enjoy it as an everyday comfort food. With minimal prep and cooking time, it’s the kind of recipe that keeps busy home cooks coming back for more.

Final Touches

To serve, plate the pasta in elegant swirls and garnish with chopped parsley or basil for a touch of color. Add a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese across the top or a dash of crushed red pepper flakes for a bit of spice. Serve warm, and take in the moment as you watch your family or guests marvel at the symphony of flavors packed into one plate.

Prosciutto and pea pasta with light cream sauce might soon claim a spot in your recipe rotation—it’s classic, comforting, and endlessly versatile. Try it out for yourself, and discover how simplicity can sometimes yield the most delicious results.
