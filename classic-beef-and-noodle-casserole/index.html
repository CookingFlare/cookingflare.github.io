Classic Beef and Noodle Casserole


Beef and noodle casserole is the perfect comfort food, offering a satisfying balance of flavors and textures that make it a beloved dish in many households. Whether you’re preparing it for a weeknight dinner or a family gathering, this classic casserole recipe is a crowd-pleaser. The rich, savory beef combined with tender noodles, creamy sauce, and melted cheese creates a dish that is both filling and incredibly delicious.

In this recipe, we’ll walk you through each step to prepare a hearty beef and noodle casserole that’s sure to become a family favorite. From the ingredients to the baking instructions, you’ll find everything you need to create a comforting meal that will warm you from the inside out.

Ingredients for Beef and Noodle Casserole

Before you begin, gather all the ingredients you’ll need. This recipe is designed to serve 6-8 people, so feel free to adjust the quantities if you’re cooking for a larger or smaller group.

Meat and Vegetables:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef – Opt for lean ground beef to keep the casserole moist without being overly greasy.
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped – Adds flavor and aroma to the casserole.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced – For a subtle kick of flavor.
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced – Optional, but they enhance the richness of the dish.
  • 1 cup bell pepper, chopped – A pop of color and sweetness that complements the savory beef.

Casserole Base:

  • 12 oz (340g) egg noodles – The best noodles for casseroles as they absorb the flavors without becoming mushy.
  • 2 cups beef broth – Adds depth to the casserole sauce.
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup – A classic ingredient that provides creaminess and binds the casserole together.
  • 1/2 cup sour cream – Adds richness and a tangy flavor to the dish.
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce – Gives the casserole a savory umami boost.
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme – For a fragrant herbal note.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt – To enhance the flavor.
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper – For a mild spiciness.

Topping:

  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar cheese – A generous amount of cheese that will melt into a gooey, golden topping.
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs – For a crispy, crunchy finish.

Preparing the Beef and Noodle Casserole

Step 1: Cook the Egg Noodles

Start by boiling a large pot of water on the stove. Once the water is at a rolling boil, add a pinch of salt and the egg noodles. Cook according to the package instructions, usually for about 7-8 minutes until they are tender but still firm to the bite. Drain the noodles and set them aside, allowing them to cool slightly.

Step 2: Brown the Ground Beef

While the noodles are cooking, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the ground beef to the pan and cook, breaking it up with a spatula as it browns. Stir occasionally to ensure the meat cooks evenly. Once the beef is browned and no longer pink, remove any excess fat by draining it through a colander or using a spoon to remove it from the pan.

Step 3: Sauté the Vegetables

To the same skillet, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, mushrooms, and bell pepper. Cook them over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables have softened and the onions have become translucent. This step infuses the casserole with flavor, enhancing the overall dish with a combination of savory and sweet notes.

Step 4: Combine the Ingredients

Once the vegetables have softened, return the browned ground beef to the skillet. Stir in the beef broth, cream of mushroom soup, Worcestershire sauce, sour cream, dried thyme, salt, and black pepper. Mix everything together until it is well combined. Let this mixture simmer on low heat for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly.

Step 5: Add the Noodles

Once the sauce has thickened, gently fold in the cooked egg noodles. Stir the mixture carefully so that the noodles are coated with the sauce. Be sure the noodles are evenly distributed throughout the beef and vegetable mixture.

Step 6: Assemble the Casserole

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch casserole dish with butter or non-stick spray. Transfer the beef and noodle mixture into the casserole dish, spreading it out evenly. Use a spatula to smooth the top of the casserole.

Step 7: Add the Toppings

Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese generously over the top of the casserole. Follow this with a light sprinkle of breadcrumbs to create a crispy topping. The cheese will melt beautifully as it bakes, and the breadcrumbs will turn golden and crunchy, adding texture to the dish.

Step 8: Bake

Place the casserole in the preheated oven and bake for about 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown. If you like your cheese extra crispy, you can broil the casserole for an additional 1-2 minutes at the end of the baking time, but be sure to keep an eye on it so it doesn’t burn.

Step 9: Serve and Enjoy

Once the casserole is done, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. The layers of tender beef, noodles, and creamy sauce will come together beautifully in each bite. Serve with a side salad or steamed vegetables to complete the meal.

Tips for Making the Best Beef and Noodle Casserole

  • Use lean ground beef: While fattier cuts of ground beef can be used, lean beef ensures that your casserole won’t be greasy and helps the flavors of the other ingredients shine through.
  • Add more vegetables: If you want to make your casserole even heartier and more nutritious, feel free to add more vegetables like carrots, peas, or zucchini.
  • Make it spicy: For those who enjoy a bit of heat, add some crushed red pepper flakes or diced jalapeños to the beef mixture.
  • Swap the cheese: While cheddar cheese is classic, you can experiment with different types of cheese. Try using mozzarella for a gooey, melty texture, or a sharp blue cheese for a bold flavor.
  • Make ahead: This casserole can be assembled ahead of time and stored in the fridge for up to 24 hours before baking. If you’re planning to freeze it, prepare the casserole, but skip the breadcrumbs and cheese topping. When you’re ready to bake, thaw it in the fridge overnight, then top it with cheese and breadcrumbs before baking as directed.
  • Use homemade soup: If you’re not a fan of canned cream of mushroom soup, you can easily make your own by combining heavy cream, butter, flour, and some sautéed mushrooms.

Variations of Beef and Noodle Casserole

While the classic beef and noodle casserole is delicious on its own, there are plenty of ways to switch up the recipe based on your personal preferences or dietary restrictions.

Ground Turkey or Chicken

For a leaner version of this casserole, swap the ground beef with ground turkey or chicken. These meats will absorb the flavors of the casserole just as well, creating a lighter alternative without sacrificing flavor.

Vegetarian Option

If you’re looking to make this casserole vegetarian, consider substituting the beef with plant-based protein like lentils, tempeh, or meatless crumbles. These options provide a satisfying texture and flavor that pairs perfectly with the creamy sauce.

Gluten-Free Version

To make this casserole gluten-free, simply swap the egg noodles with a gluten-free pasta of your choice. Be sure to check the ingredients on the cream of mushroom soup or make a homemade version to ensure it’s gluten-free.

Why Beef and Noodle Casserole is a Great Family Meal

Beef and noodle casserole has earned its place as a staple in many kitchens, and for good reason. It’s a one-dish meal that’s easy to prepare, easy to clean up, and loved by nearly everyone. The savory beef, tender noodles, creamy sauce, and crispy topping create a perfect balance of flavors that will leave your family asking for seconds.

Moreover, this casserole is versatile enough to accommodate different tastes and dietary preferences. You can add in extra vegetables, switch up the protein, or even adjust the seasoning to make it your own. It’s the kind of dish that can be made ahead of time, so it’s a great option for busy nights or when you’re entertaining guests.

Whether you enjoy it with a fresh salad, some crusty bread, or on its own, this classic beef and noodle casserole is sure to become a favorite in your home. Its rich flavor and comforting texture make it a dish worth repeating.
