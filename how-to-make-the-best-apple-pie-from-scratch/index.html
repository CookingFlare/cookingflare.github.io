How to Make the Best Apple Pie from Scratch


Who doesn’t love the aroma of a freshly baked apple pie wafting through the house? The combination of buttery, flaky crust and sweet, cinnamon-spiced apple filling is a timeless classic that never fails to delight. If you’ve always wanted to master the art of making the perfect apple pie from scratch, you’ve come to the right place. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through every step of the process, from selecting the right apples to achieving that coveted golden brown crust. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let’s dive into the world of apple pie perfection.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 8 cups of apples, peeled, cored, and thinly sliced (use a mix of Granny Smith and Honeycrisp for the best flavor and texture)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch

Instructions

Making the Crust:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar together.

  2. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix the dough with a fork until it starts to come together. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  4. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, and wrap them in plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

Preparing the Filling:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, brown sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and cornstarch. Toss until the apples are evenly coated. Set aside.

Assembling the Pie:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one disc of the chilled dough into a circle large enough to line your pie dish. Carefully transfer the dough to the pie dish and gently press it into the bottom and sides.

  3. Pour the prepared apple filling into the pie crust.

  4. Roll out the second disc of dough and place it over the filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  5. Use a sharp knife to make several small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.

  6. Optional: brush the top crust with a beaten egg and sprinkle with a little sugar for a beautiful, golden finish.

Baking the Pie:

  1. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake in the preheated oven for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  2. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with strips of aluminum foil.

  3. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before slicing.

Tips and Tricks

  • For an extra flaky crust, make sure all your ingredients are cold, and handle the dough as little as possible.

  • If you prefer a sweeter filling, you can increase the amount of sugar or use sweeter apple varieties.

  • Serve your apple pie warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a truly indulgent treat.

Conclusion

Congratulations, you’ve just mastered the art of making the best apple pie from scratch! Whether it’s for a family gathering, a holiday celebration, or just a cozy night in, this homemade apple pie is sure to steal the show. With its buttery crust, tender apples, and warm spices, it’s a dessert that never goes out of style. So, go ahead, slice into that flaky perfection, and savor the fruits of your labor. Happy baking!
