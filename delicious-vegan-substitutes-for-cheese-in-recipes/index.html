Delicious Vegan Substitutes for Cheese in Recipes


For anyone who’s adopted a plant-based lifestyle or simply looking to reduce dairy intake, cheese can often be one of the trickiest food items to replace. However, with a little creativity and some pantry staples, there are plenty of delicious and satisfying vegan alternatives for cheese that will elevate your favorite dishes without compromising on flavor. Whether you’re looking to make the creamiest vegan mac and cheese or need a cheese replacement for pizza or sandwiches, this guide will show you how to make the most of plant-based ingredients to create the perfect substitutes for cheese in your recipes.

The Case for Vegan Cheese

The rise in popularity of vegan cheese has coincided with a growing awareness of the environmental and ethical concerns related to dairy production. Dairy farming often has a significant environmental impact, with high water usage and greenhouse gas emissions, not to mention the ethical issues surrounding the treatment of animals. Vegan cheese provides a delicious, cruelty-free, and often lower-calorie alternative to dairy cheese, and it can be made from various plant-based ingredients like nuts, soy, coconut, and root vegetables.

Vegan cheeses come in many different forms: soft and creamy, hard and aged, and even processed cheese-style slices. With a little guidance, you’ll be able to recreate your favorite cheesy dishes without the use of animal products.

Best Vegan Cheese Substitutes and How to Use Them

1. Cashew-Based Cheese

Cashews are one of the most versatile nuts used in vegan cheese-making. When blended, cashews form a creamy base that can be enhanced with various flavorings to mimic the richness and texture of dairy cheese. Cashew cheese can be used in a variety of applications, from spreadable cheeses to creamy sauces.

How to Make Cashew Cheese:

  • Soak 1 cup of raw cashews in water for at least 4 hours, or overnight if possible.
  • Drain the cashews and blend them with 1/4 cup of water, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1 tablespoon of nutritional yeast, and a pinch of salt.
  • Blend until smooth and creamy. If you like a tangier flavor, you can add a bit of apple cider vinegar or a tablespoon of miso paste for depth.

Cashew cheese is fantastic for spreading on crackers, mixing into pasta dishes, or even using as a creamy base for vegan pizzas. Its rich and creamy texture makes it a great substitute for cream cheese in bagels and dips as well.

2. Nutritional Yeast

Nutritional yeast is often referred to as “nooch” in the plant-based world, and for good reason. It’s a flaky, yellow powder that has a cheesy, umami flavor that makes it an excellent addition to any recipe in need of a cheesy kick. Nutritional yeast is packed with B vitamins, making it a nutritious addition to your meals.

How to Use Nutritional Yeast:

  • Sprinkle nutritional yeast directly over popcorn, pasta, or roasted vegetables for an instant cheesy flavor.
  • Mix it into vegan cheese sauces to create a velvety texture.
  • Use it in dressings or dips for added richness and flavor.

Nutritional yeast shines in simple dishes like vegan mac and cheese or as a topping for your favorite veggie burger. It’s often combined with other ingredients, such as cashews or tofu, to create a more complex cheese substitute.

3. Vegan Cream Cheese

Vegan cream cheese is another delicious alternative to its dairy counterpart. It’s usually made from a base of cashews or soy, sometimes with added coconut oil for extra creaminess. The tangy and slightly sweet flavor profile of vegan cream cheese makes it perfect for bagels, dips, or vegan cheesecakes.

How to Make Vegan Cream Cheese:

  • Soak 1 cup of raw cashews for 4 hours and drain.
  • Blend with 1/4 cup of water, 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, and a pinch of salt.
  • Blend until smooth. For a creamier texture, add 1 tablespoon of coconut oil.

This rich, spreadable cheese is great for sandwiches, crackers, and as a dip base for veggies. You can even use it in vegan frosting recipes for cakes or cupcakes!

4. Tofu-Based Cheese

Tofu is an incredibly versatile ingredient and is commonly used to replace cheese in a variety of ways. Silken tofu, in particular, makes for a fantastic base for creamy cheese sauces, while firm tofu can be grated or crumbled to mimic the texture of cheese curds.

How to Make Tofu Cheese:

  • For a cream cheese alternative, blend 1 block of silken tofu with 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, 1 tablespoon of nutritional yeast, and salt to taste.
  • For a firmer cheese, press a block of firm tofu to remove excess water, then crumble or shred it for use in tacos, pizza, or sandwiches.

Tofu-based cheese works wonderfully as a base for vegan ricotta or mozzarella. It’s also great in dishes like vegan lasagna or even grilled cheese sandwiches. The beauty of tofu is its adaptability—you can flavor it with a variety of herbs and spices to achieve different cheese-like profiles.

5. Coconut-Based Cheese

Coconut milk or coconut cream is another great ingredient for creating rich and creamy vegan cheese. Though it may not have the same flavor profile as dairy cheese, coconut-based cheeses can be incredibly creamy and tangy. These cheeses are often found in the form of blocks, spreadable options, or even slices for sandwiches.

How to Make Coconut Cheese:

  • In a blender, combine 1 cup of full-fat coconut milk with 1/4 cup of agar agar powder (a plant-based gelling agent), 1 tablespoon of nutritional yeast, and a pinch of salt.
  • Heat the mixture on the stove until it thickens, then pour it into a mold and refrigerate until set.

Coconut-based cheeses are perfect for melting on pizza, using as a dip, or creating the perfect cheese for a vegan grilled cheese sandwich. The mild flavor pairs well with a variety of dishes, including curries, pasta, and nachos.

6. Almond-Based Cheese

Almonds can also be used to make vegan cheese, and they offer a slightly different flavor and texture than cashews. Almond-based cheese tends to have a firmer, more crumbly texture, which makes it great for grating over salads, pastas, or pizza.

How to Make Almond Cheese:

  • Blend 1 cup of soaked almonds with 1/4 cup of nutritional yeast, 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil until smooth.
  • For a firmer texture, add agar agar and refrigerate the mixture until it sets.

Almond cheese is fantastic for creating crumbled cheese-like textures for salads or tacos. It’s also delicious when used in combination with other vegan cheeses to create complex flavors.

7. Vegan Parmesan

If you’re a fan of grated parmesan but have made the switch to a plant-based diet, you don’t have to miss out on that savory, salty flavor. Vegan parmesan is often made by blending nuts, seeds, and nutritional yeast into a coarse, crumbly texture that is perfect for sprinkling on pasta, pizza, and salads.

How to Make Vegan Parmesan:

  • Blend 1/2 cup of raw cashews or almonds, 2 tablespoons of nutritional yeast, 1 tablespoon of garlic powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt in a food processor until finely ground.
  • Store in an airtight container for up to a month.

Vegan parmesan adds a burst of flavor to any dish, especially pasta. Its crunchy texture and salty taste make it a great finishing touch for soups, roasted vegetables, and even popcorn.

8. Store-Bought Vegan Cheese

If you don’t have the time or inclination to make your own vegan cheese, there are plenty of store-bought options available. Many brands now offer a variety of plant-based cheeses, from shredded mozzarella to slices of cheddar and even vegan cream cheese. These products are made from ingredients like nuts, coconut oil, and starches, and they provide a convenient way to enjoy vegan cheese without any extra effort.

Look for brands that use high-quality, non-GMO ingredients and avoid those with excessive added sugars or preservatives. Whether you’re looking for cheese to melt on a pizza, spread on a sandwich, or sprinkle over a salad, there’s sure to be a product that suits your needs.

How to Use Vegan Cheese in Recipes

Now that you have a variety of vegan cheese substitutes to choose from, let’s dive into some delicious ways to incorporate them into your meals.

Vegan Pizza

Pizza is one of the most popular dishes that people miss when they transition to a plant-based diet. Fortunately, with the right vegan cheese, you can recreate your favorite pizza with ease. Whether you opt for a creamy cashew-based mozzarella or a coconut-based cheese, both provide the gooey, melty texture you love. Add your favorite toppings like fresh vegetables, vegan sausage, or olives, and you’ve got a pizza that’s just as satisfying as the traditional version.

Vegan Mac and Cheese

Mac and cheese is a classic comfort food, and with a little vegan creativity, it can still be a part of your meal rotation. Cashew-based cheese sauce is a popular choice, as it’s both rich and creamy, making it a perfect base for pasta. Add some nutritional yeast for a cheesy flavor and blend in a little mustard or turmeric for color and tang. You’ll have a vegan mac and cheese that’s just as indulgent as the dairy version.

Vegan Grilled Cheese Sandwich

For a classic grilled cheese sandwich, tofu-based cheese or store-bought vegan cheese slices work perfectly. Layer your favorite bread with cheese, grill it until golden and crispy, and you’ve got a satisfying sandwich that’s melty on the inside and crispy on the outside. Add some tomatoes, vegan bacon, or avocado for extra flavor.

Vegan Lasagna

When making lasagna, tofu-based ricotta or a cashew-based mozzarella are great substitutes for dairy cheese. Layer these with your favorite pasta, tomato sauce, and roasted vegetables for a rich and satisfying meal. Don’t forget to sprinkle some vegan parmesan on top for added flavor!

Vegan Queso Dip

For a fun appetizer, whip up a creamy vegan queso dip using cashews, nutritional yeast, and spices like chili powder and cumin. Blend it until smooth and serve with tortilla chips, veggies, or tacos. You can even make a spicy version with diced jalapeños and smoked paprika for an extra kick.

Final Thoughts

Vegan substitutes for cheese can be just as flavorful and satisfying as the dairy versions, and they open up a whole new world of possibilities for your plant-based cooking. Whether you’re creating creamy sauces, cheesy dips, or melty pizza toppings, there’s a plant-based cheese substitute for every dish. With the right ingredients and a bit of practice, you’ll be able to craft delicious meals that are entirely cruelty-free and full of flavor.

As plant-based options continue to grow in popularity, the availability of vegan cheese substitutes has expanded, making it easier than ever to enjoy cheesy dishes without the dairy. So get creative, experiment with different recipes, and discover the delicious world of vegan cheese alternatives!
