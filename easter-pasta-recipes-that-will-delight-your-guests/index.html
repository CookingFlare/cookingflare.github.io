Easter Pasta Recipes That Will Delight Your Guests


Easter is a time of renewal, celebration, and gathering with loved ones. It’s also the perfect occasion to serve dishes that are both comforting and festive. Pasta, with its versatility and timeless appeal, makes a fantastic centerpiece or side for any Easter meal. Whether you’re hosting a casual brunch or a formal dinner, these Easter pasta recipes will not only satisfy your guests but also add a touch of creativity to your holiday table.

In this blog post, you’ll discover a variety of pasta dishes inspired by the flavors and spirit of spring. From rich and creamy sauces to vibrant vegetable medleys, these recipes leverage fresh ingredients and classic techniques, all designed to make your Easter feast memorable.

1. Spring Vegetable Primavera with Lemon and Parmesan

Overview

Primavera means “spring” in Italian, and this pasta dish perfectly showcases the season’s bounty. Bright vegetables, tender pasta, and a hint of citrus combine to create a fresh, mouthwatering plate.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz fettuccine or linguine
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup fresh peas or sugar snap peas
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced into half-moons
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest and juice of 1 large lemon
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  3. Add asparagus, peas, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes. Cook until vegetables are tender but still crisp, about 4–5 minutes.
  4. Stir in lemon zest and juice. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss everything together.
  5. If the mixture seems dry, add reserved pasta water a little at a time until you reach your desired consistency.
  6. Remove from heat, stir in the Parmesan cheese and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve immediately with an extra sprinkle of Parmesan.

Why This Recipe Works for Easter

The bright colors and fresh ingredients capture the essence of spring perfectly. It’s light enough to pair with ham or roast lamb yet satisfying on its own.

2. Creamy Pesto Tortellini with Roasted Cherry Tomatoes and Pine Nuts

Overview

Pesto and tortellini make a match made in heaven, and adding roasted cherry tomatoes and toasted pine nuts gives this dish a festive and rich twist suitable for Easter dining.

Ingredients

  • 1 package (9 oz) refrigerated cheese tortellini
  • 2 cups cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup prepared basil pesto (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • Fresh basil for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss cherry tomatoes in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast in the oven for 15 minutes or until the tomatoes begin to burst.
  3. Cook tortellini according to package directions and drain.
  4. In a large skillet over medium heat, combine pesto and heavy cream, stirring until smooth and heated through.
  5. Add the cooked tortellini and roasted tomatoes to the skillet. Toss gently to combine and warm everything for 2 minutes.
  6. Plate the tortellini and sprinkle with toasted pine nuts, extra basil, and Parmesan.
  7. Serve warm for a decadent and colorful Easter main or side.

Easter Appeal

This dish embodies indulgence and freshness, balancing creamy pesto with the sweetness of roasted tomatoes. It makes a great centerpiece for an Easter buffet.

3. Easter Lamb Ragù with Pappardelle

Overview

For a heartier, rustic dish, lamb ragù is ideal — a slow-cooked, fragrant sauce that’s perfect with broad pappardelle noodles. Lamb is a traditional Easter protein, and this ragù incorporates it in a comforting yet elegant way.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground lamb
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup beef or chicken broth
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper
  • 12 oz pappardelle pasta
  • Freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery, cooking until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add ground lamb, breaking it apart and browning evenly.
  4. Pour in red wine, scraping any browned bits from the bottom, and simmer until reduced by half.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, broth, rosemary, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer gently for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally, until thickened and rich.
  6. Remove rosemary and thyme. Season ragù with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Meanwhile, cook pappardelle until al dente. Drain and toss with the ragù.
  8. Serve topped generously with Pecorino Romano.

Why This Works for Easter

This ragù brings tradition to the plate with lamb and herbs reminiscent of spring. The rich sauce paired with wide pasta ribbons creates an impressive dish for holiday gatherings.

4. Deviled Egg Carbonara – A Fun Easter Twist

Overview

Carbonara is an Italian classic, usually made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. For Easter, it’s fun to incorporate deviled eggs for a playful, thematic touch that guests will love.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 4 slices pancetta or bacon, chopped
  • 3 deviled eggs (prepared with yolks mixed with mustard, mayo, and paprika)
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook spaghetti in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water and drain.
  2. In a skillet, cook pancetta until crispy; remove from heat.
  3. In a bowl, blend the yolk of one fresh egg with cheese and black pepper.
  4. Roughly chop deviled eggs and add them along with the pancetta to the pasta.
  5. Toss pasta quickly with the egg mixture, adding reserved pasta water a little at a time to create a creamy sauce without scrambling the eggs.
  6. Season with more pepper and sprinkle with extra cheese.
  7. Serve immediately, surprising your guests with this playful Easter homage to carbonara.

Easter Fun

Integrating deviled eggs into pasta is quirky and clever, giving your holiday meal an extra festive edge while honoring traditional flavors.

5. Roasted Butternut Squash and Sage Lasagna Roll-Ups

Overview

Lasagna roll-ups offer individual portions of comfort food with room for creativity. Roasting butternut squash enhances its natural sweetness, while sage brings aromatic warmth perfect for early spring celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 8 lasagna noodles
  • 3 cups diced butternut squash
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried sage (or 1 tbsp fresh, chopped)
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan
  • 2 cups béchamel sauce (white sauce made with butter, flour, and milk)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh sage for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss butternut squash with olive oil, sage, salt, and pepper. Roast for 20 minutes until tender and caramelized.
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and lay flat to cool.
  3. In a bowl, mix ricotta with roasted squash, half the mozzarella, Parmesan, and seasoning.
  4. Spread a thin layer of béchamel sauce on each noodle, then place a generous spread of the squash and cheese mixture. Roll up tightly.
  5. Place roll-ups seam-side down in a baking dish, cover with remaining béchamel and mozzarella.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  7. Garnish with fresh sage before serving.

Why This Recipe Shines

This dish feels cozy and elegant, perfect for a special occasion like Easter. The flavors bring balance between sweetness, creaminess, and herbaceous notes.

Final Thoughts

Easter pasta recipes offer a wonderful opportunity to embrace seasonal ingredients and celebrate togetherness around the table. Whether you prefer light vegetable-forward dishes or rich, meaty sauces, pasta’s adaptability shines through in spring cooking.

These recipes go beyond the usual to bring freshness, tradition, and creativity into your Easter feast. Try one — or mix several — to delight your guests with dishes that look beautiful and taste even better.
