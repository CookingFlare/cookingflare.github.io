Sous Vide Garlic Butter Lobster Tails: A Decadent Seafood Delight

If you’re looking for a luxurious and mouth-watering seafood dish that will impress your guests, look no further than Sous Vide Garlic Butter Lobster Tails. This recipe takes succulent lobster tails to the next level, infusing them with the rich flavors of garlic and butter. The sous vide cooking technique ensures perfectly cooked lobster every time, with a tender and juicy texture that will leave you craving more. Ingredients 4 lobster tails 4 tablespoons unsalted butter 4 garlic cloves, minced 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped Salt and pepper, to taste Instructions Preheat your water bath to 135°F (57°C). This temperature will give you a tender and buttery lobster texture. While the water bath is heating up, prepare the lobster tails. Use kitchen shears to cut through the top shell of each tail, stopping at the base of the tail. Gently lift the meat from the shell, keeping it attached at the base. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 2-3 minutes, until fragrant. Remove from heat and set aside. Season the lobster tails with salt and pepper, to taste. Brush the garlic butter mixture over the exposed meat, making sure to coat it evenly. Place each lobster tail into a separate vacuum-sealed bag. If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use the water displacement method by placing the lobster tails in a zip-top bag and slowly submerging it in water, allowing the pressure to push the air out. Seal the bag tightly. Once the water bath has reached the desired temperature, gently place the sealed bags into the water bath. Ensure that the lobster tails are fully submerged. Cook the lobster tails for 45 minutes to 1 hour, depending on their size. This will ensure that they are cooked to perfection. After the cooking time has elapsed, remove the lobster tails from the water bath and carefully remove them from the bags. Pat them dry with a paper towel. Preheat a grill or a cast-iron skillet over high heat. Once hot, sear the lobster tails for 1-2 minutes on each side, until they develop a beautiful golden color. Remove the lobster tails from the heat and sprinkle them with fresh parsley. Serve immediately with lemon wedges, if desired. Tips and Variations For an extra flavor boost, you can add a squeeze of lemon juice to the garlic butter mixture. If you prefer a spicier kick, you can add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the garlic butter mixture. Serve the lobster tails with a side of drawn butter or a lemon garlic aioli for dipping. This recipe can be easily doubled or tripled to accommodate larger gatherings. Conclusion Sous Vide Garlic Butter Lobster Tails are a decadent seafood delight that will elevate any special occasion. The sous vide technique ensures that the lobster tails are perfectly cooked, while the garlic butter adds a rich and luxurious flavor. Impress your guests with this show-stopping dish that is surprisingly simple to prepare. Serve it as the star of an elegant dinner party or as a romantic meal for two. Either way, your taste buds will thank you for this indulgent seafood experience. ...
