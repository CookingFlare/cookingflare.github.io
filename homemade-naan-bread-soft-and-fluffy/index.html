Homemade Naan Bread: Soft and Fluffy


Naan bread is a popular Indian flatbread that is traditionally cooked in a tandoor, a clay oven. Its soft and fluffy texture, along with its slightly charred exterior, makes it a perfect accompaniment to curries and other Indian dishes. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of making homemade naan bread that is just as delicious as the one you get in your favorite Indian restaurant.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Additional flour for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, salt, and baking powder. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, yogurt, and vegetable oil.

  3. Mix the ingredients until they come together to form a soft dough. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour. If it’s too dry, add a splash of warm water. Knead the dough for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

  4. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased bowl. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rise in a warm place for about 2 hours until it doubles in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release the air. Divide the dough into 4 equal portions and shape each portion into a ball. Place the balls on a lightly floured surface and cover them with a damp cloth. Let them rest for about 15 minutes.

  6. Preheat a non-stick skillet or a griddle over medium-high heat.

  7. Take one dough ball and roll it out into an oval shape, about 1/4 inch thick. If the dough starts to stick, dust it with a little flour.

  8. Place the rolled naan bread onto the preheated skillet and cook for about 1 minute, or until bubbles start to form on the surface. Flip the naan bread and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until it puffs up and develops a golden brown color.

  9. Remove the cooked naan bread from the skillet and brush it with melted butter. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

  10. Serve the homemade naan bread warm with your favorite curry or dip. It’s best enjoyed fresh, but you can also store it in an airtight container for up to 2 days.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a garlic naan variation, simply mix 2 cloves of minced garlic with the melted butter and brush it onto the cooked naan bread.
  • You can also add chopped cilantro or nigella seeds to the dough for added flavor.
  • If you don’t have instant yeast, you can use active dry yeast instead. Just make sure to activate it in warm milk before adding it to the dough.
  • To achieve a slightly smoky flavor, you can cook the naan bread on a grill or barbecue instead of a skillet.

Now that you know how to make soft and fluffy homemade naan bread, it’s time to get creative in the kitchen. Experiment with different flavors and toppings to make this Indian classic your own. Enjoy the process of making this delicious bread from scratch, and savor the satisfaction of watching it rise and puff up in the skillet. Serve it alongside your favorite Indian dishes or enjoy it on its own as a tasty snack. Happy cooking!
