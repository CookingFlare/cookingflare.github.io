Vegan Fruit Tart with Cashew Cream Filling


When it comes to desserts that are as stunning to look at as they are to eat, few options rival the vibrant charm of a fruit tart. This vegan fruit tart with cashew cream filling is not just visually appealing; it’s a treat that guarantees creamy decadence paired with the natural sweetness of fresh fruit. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving something delightful, this recipe is ideal for everyone—vegans and non-vegans alike.

Creating this masterpiece involves three key components: the crust, the cashew cream filling, and the colorful medley of fruits. Each element is simple yet flavorful, working in harmony to create a dessert that is as wholesome as it is indulgent. Let’s dive into the details and make this irresistible vegan fruit tart from scratch.

Simple Ingredients for a Vegan Delight

For the Tart Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups almond flour
  • ½ cup rolled oats
  • ¼ cup coconut oil (softened)
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

For the Cashew Cream Filling:

  • 1 ½ cups raw cashews (soaked for 4-6 hours or boiled for 20 minutes)
  • ½ cup unsweetened plant milk (such as almond or oat milk)
  • ¼ cup pure maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon melted coconut oil

For the Fruit Topping:

  • Any combination of fresh fruit you love! Here are some great options:
    • Strawberries
    • Blueberries
    • Kiwi
    • Mango
    • Raspberries
    • Blackberries
    • Peaches

Step-by-Step Instructions

Make the Crust:

The crust serves as the foundation for this tart, and getting it right is key. It has a subtly nutty flavor and a crispy texture that perfectly complements the creamy filling.

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a food processor, combine the almond flour, rolled oats, and sea salt. Pulse until the oats are finely ground and evenly distributed.
  3. Add the softened coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract to the mixture. Pulse again until the ingredients come together to form a crumbly dough.
  4. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Press the dough evenly into the bottom and up the sides to form a crust. Make sure there are no gaps or cracks.
  5. Poke a few holes in the base of the crust with a fork to prevent bubbling during baking.
  6. Bake the crust for 12-15 minutes, or until it turns lightly golden. Remove it from the oven and allow it to cool completely.

Prepare the Cashew Cream Filling:

This filling is the heart of the vegan fruit tart, offering a silky-smooth texture and a rich flavor profile. The cashew cream complements the sweetness of the fruit topping beautifully.

  1. Drain the soaked cashews and place them in a high-speed blender.
  2. Add the plant milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and melted coconut oil to the blender.
  3. Blend on high until the mixture becomes completely smooth and creamy. Stop occasionally to scrape down the sides of the blender for even consistency.
  4. Taste the cream and adjust sweetness or tartness as needed. Add a touch more maple syrup if you prefer it sweeter or an extra dash of lemon juice for brightness.
  5. Once ready, transfer the cashew cream filling to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before assembling the tart. This step helps the filling firm up slightly.

Assemble the Tart:

Now comes the fun part—assembling and decorating your fruit tart. Get creative with your fruit placements to make it as eye-catching as possible!

  1. Spread the chilled cashew cream filling over the cooled tart crust. Use a spatula to spread it evenly across the base.
  2. Arrange your chosen fruits in a visually striking pattern atop the cream. You can go for concentric circles, rows, or a freeform design depending on your artistic vision.
  3. For extra shine and sweetness, lightly brush the fruits with a mix of maple syrup and lemon juice to create a natural glaze.

Serve and Enjoy:

Refrigerate the assembled tart for at least an hour before serving. This helps the flavors meld together and ensures a refreshing bite. When it’s time to indulge, slice the tart and admire its gorgeous layers!

Tips for Success

Customize Your Tart:

This recipe is highly adaptable. For the crust, you can swap almond flour for hazelnut or pecan flour if you prefer a different flavor profile. For the filling, experiment with cinnamon or nutmeg for a spiced variation. When it comes to the fruit, choose what’s in season for maximum freshness.

Soaking Cashews Is Key:

The creaminess of the filling largely depends on properly soaked cashews. If you’re short on time, boiling cashews for 20 minutes will do the trick. Just make sure to rinse and drain them thoroughly before blending.

Don’t Skip the Glaze:

A simple glaze made with maple syrup or apricot preserves thinned with water adds a professional touch to your tart. It enhances the shine of the fruit and gives the dessert a polished, bakery-style presentation.

Why Choose a Vegan Fruit Tart?

Opting for a vegan fruit tart isn’t just about accommodating dietary preferences; it’s about crafting a dessert that’s nourishing and full of flavor. The absence of dairy and eggs opens the door to lighter, plant-based ingredients that highlight the natural richness of nuts, fruits, and grains. Plus, the vibrant colors of the fresh fruit make it a feast for the eyes. This dessert is not only delicious, but it also celebrates the beauty and bounty of plant-based eating.

The next time you’re in search of a dessert that combines elegance and wholesome ingredients, give this vegan fruit tart with cashew cream filling a try. Packed with flavor, texture, and freshness, it’s a culinary creation that invites you to savor every bite. Whether it’s for special occasions or a simple treat for yourself, this tart is bound to become a favorite in your recipe collection. Happy baking!
