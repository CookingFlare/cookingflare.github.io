How to Make a Delicious BBQ Pulled Pork Sandwich


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the mouthwatering allure of a BBQ pulled pork sandwich. This classic American dish combines tender, flavorful pork with a tangy barbecue sauce, all nestled between two soft buns. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, a family gathering, or simply craving a hearty meal, mastering the art of pulled pork is a skill worth acquiring. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the entire process, from selecting the right cut of meat to serving up the perfect sandwich.

Understanding Pulled Pork

Pulled pork is traditionally made from pork shoulder, also known as pork butt. This cut is ideal for slow cooking because it contains a good amount of fat and connective tissue, which break down during the cooking process, resulting in tender, juicy meat. The key to great pulled pork lies in low and slow cooking, allowing the flavors to develop and the meat to become fork-tender.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create a delicious BBQ pulled pork sandwich, gather the following ingredients:

For the Pulled Pork:

  • 4-5 pounds pork shoulder (pork butt)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup chicken broth or apple cider

For the BBQ Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Soft hamburger buns
  • Coleslaw (optional)
  • Pickles (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pork

  1. Trim the Pork Shoulder: Start by trimming excess fat from the pork shoulder. While some fat is necessary for flavor and moisture, too much can make the dish greasy.

  2. Make the Rub: In a small bowl, combine the smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. This spice rub will infuse the meat with flavor.

  3. Season the Meat: Rub the olive oil all over the pork shoulder, then generously apply the spice rub, ensuring every inch of the meat is coated. For best results, let the seasoned pork sit in the refrigerator for at least an hour, or overnight if time allows.

Step 2: Cook the Pork

Slow Cooker Method

  1. Add to Slow Cooker: Place the seasoned pork shoulder in the slow cooker. Pour the chicken broth or apple cider around the meat to keep it moist during cooking.

  2. Cook Low and Slow: Cover and cook on low for 8-10 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily pulls apart with a fork. The low temperature allows the collagen in the meat to break down, resulting in a succulent texture.

Oven Method

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C).

  2. Sear the Meat: In a large oven-safe pot or Dutch oven, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the pork shoulder on all sides until browned, about 3-4 minutes per side.

  3. Add Liquid: Once seared, remove the pot from heat and add the chicken broth or apple cider. Cover the pot with a lid or aluminum foil.

  4. Bake: Place the pot in the preheated oven and bake for 4-5 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender.

Step 3: Make the BBQ Sauce

While the pork is cooking, you can prepare the BBQ sauce. This homemade sauce is simple yet packed with flavor.

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, brown sugar, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

  2. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  3. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a sweeter sauce, add more brown sugar; for a tangier flavor, increase the vinegar.

Step 4: Shred the Pork

Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the slow cooker or oven and let it rest for about 15 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat.

  1. Shred the Meat: Using two forks, shred the pork into bite-sized pieces.
