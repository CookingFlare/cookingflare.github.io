Satisfy Your Cravings with Greek Spinach and Feta Pie Recipe


As the aroma of freshly baked pie wafts through the air, our senses are transported to a world of comfort and indulgence. The combination of crispy crust, savory filling, and melted cheese is a match made in heaven. In this recipe, we’ll take you on a journey to the Mediterranean, where the tangy flavors of Greek spinach and feta cheese come together in a heavenly union.

The Perfect Pie Crust

Before we dive into the filling, let’s start with the foundation of our pie: the crust. A flaky, buttery crust is essential to any pie, and we’ll use a simple recipe to achieve it. In a large bowl, combine 2 1/4 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 tsp of salt, and 1/2 cup of cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces. Use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until it resembles coarse crumbs.

The Filling: A Symphony of Flavors

Now that our crust is ready, it’s time to create the filling. In a large skillet, heat 2 tbsp of olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 large onion, finely chopped, and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

Next, add 1 package of frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained, and cook until heated through. Stir in 1/2 cup of crumbled feta cheese, 1/2 cup of grated Parmesan cheese, and 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley.

Assembling the Pie

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). On a lightly floured surface, roll out the pie crust to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the crust to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom.

Fill the crust with the spinach and feta mixture, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges. Fold the edges of the crust over the filling, pressing gently to seal.

The Finishing Touches

Brush the crust with 1 tbsp of beaten egg and sprinkle with 1/4 cup of grated Parmesan cheese. Bake the pie for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is hot and bubbly.

Serving Suggestions

This Greek spinach and feta pie is perfect for a variety of occasions. Serve it as a main course, accompanied by a simple green salad or roasted vegetables. Alternatively, slice it into wedges and serve it as an appetizer or snack.

Tips and Variations

  • To add some extra flavor, sprinkle a pinch of dried oregano or thyme over the filling before baking.
  • For a vegetarian option, substitute the feta cheese with a crumbled goat cheese or ricotta cheese.
  • Experiment with different types of cheese, such as cheddar or mozzarella, to create a unique flavor profile.

Conclusion

In this recipe, we’ve combined the rich flavors of Greece with the comfort of a warm, flaky pie crust. The result is a dish that’s sure to satisfy your cravings and leave you wanting more. Whether you’re a fan of spinach, feta, or just a good pie, this recipe is sure to become a new favorite. So go ahead, give it a try, and indulge in the delicious world of Greek cuisine!
