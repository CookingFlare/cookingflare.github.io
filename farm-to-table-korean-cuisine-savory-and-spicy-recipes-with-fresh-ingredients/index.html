Farm to Table Korean Cuisine: Savory and Spicy Recipes with Fresh Ingredients


Korean cuisine has gained immense popularity all over the world for its bold flavors, unique ingredients, and healthful qualities. Farm to table Korean cooking takes this rich culinary tradition to the next level by emphasizing the use of fresh, locally-sourced ingredients, resulting in dishes that are not only delicious but also nourishing. In this blog post, we’ll explore some savory and spicy Korean recipes that celebrate the beauty of farm to table cooking.

Kimchi Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 1 cup kimchi, chopped
  • 1/2 cup kimchi juice
  • 1/2 cup pork belly or bacon, diced
  • 1/2 cup onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Fried eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the pork belly or bacon and cook until browned.
  2. Add the onion and garlic to the skillet and sauté until the onion is translucent.
  3. Stir in the kimchi and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the rice to the skillet and stir to combine with the other ingredients.
  5. Pour in the kimchi juice, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar. Stir well to evenly distribute the flavors.
  6. Cook the fried rice for an additional 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and garnish the fried rice with sliced green onions and sesame seeds.
  8. Serve the kimchi fried rice with a fried egg on top, if desired.

Spicy Tofu Stew (Sundubu Jjigae)

Ingredients:

  • 14 oz soft tofu
  • 1/2 cup clams or seafood mix (optional)
  • 1/2 cup kimchi, chopped
  • 1/4 cup onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 green onion, sliced
  • 1 red chili pepper, sliced
  • 2 cups anchovy or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon Korean chili flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 egg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring the broth to a gentle boil over medium heat.
  2. Add the clams or seafood mix, if using, and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Gently add the tofu to the pot, breaking it into large chunks with a spoon.
  4. Stir in the kimchi, onion, garlic, green onion, and chili pepper.
  5. Season the stew with Korean chili flakes, soy sauce, sesame oil, and salt. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste.
  6. Crack an egg into the center of the stew and let it cook for a few minutes, if desired.
  7. Serve the spicy tofu stew hot with a bowl of steamed rice.

Bibimbap

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 1 cup assorted vegetables (spinach, bean sprouts, carrots, zucchini, shiitake mushrooms)
  • 1/2 cup soybean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the assorted vegetables by blanching or sautéing them individually until tender. Season with a bit of salt and sesame oil.
  2. Cook the beef in a hot skillet with a bit of vegetable oil until browned and cooked through.
  3. Divide the cooked rice into four serving bowls.
  4. Arrange the assorted vegetables and beef on top of the rice in a visually appealing manner.
  5. Fry the eggs sunny-side up or over-easy and place one on each serving bowl.
  6. Serve the bibimbap with a dollop of gochujang and a drizzle of sesame oil. Sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving.

These farm to table Korean recipes showcase the vibrant flavors and wholesome nature of traditional Korean cuisine. By using fresh, locally-sourced ingredients, you can elevate the taste and nutritional value of these dishes while also supporting local farmers and producers. Whether you’re a fan of spicy stews, hearty fried rice, or colorful rice bowls, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and bring the essence of Korean farm to table cooking into your home. Enjoy the journey of creating these delightful dishes and savor the rich flavors of Korean cuisine!
