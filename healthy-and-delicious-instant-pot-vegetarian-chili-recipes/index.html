Healthy and Delicious Instant Pot Vegetarian Chili Recipes


Chili is a beloved dish that transcends borders and brings warmth to the soul, perfect for cozy evenings and gatherings. When it comes to vegetarian options, the Instant Pot makes it easier than ever to whip up a healthy and delicious chili that’s packed with flavor and nutrients. In this post, we will explore a variety of Instant Pot vegetarian chili recipes that are not only easy to prepare but also bursting with taste. Whether you’re a seasoned vegetarian or just looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into your diet, these recipes are sure to become staples in your kitchen.

Why Choose Instant Pot for Chili?

The Instant Pot revolutionizes cooking by combining the functions of multiple kitchen appliances. It allows you to sauté, pressure cook, and slow cook, all in one device. This versatility is particularly beneficial for making chili, as you can develop rich flavors quickly while retaining the nutrients of the ingredients. Additionally, the pressure cooking feature reduces cooking time significantly, making it easier to enjoy a hearty meal even on busy weeknights.

Key Ingredients for Vegetarian Chili

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s take a look at some of the key ingredients that make vegetarian chili so wholesome and satisfying:

  • Beans: A great source of protein and fiber, beans are the backbone of any chili. Consider using a mix of black beans, kidney beans, and pinto beans for a variety of textures and flavors.
  • Vegetables: Bell peppers, onions, garlic, and tomatoes add depth and freshness to the chili. You can also experiment with zucchini, corn, or carrots for added sweetness and nutrition.
  • Spices: Chili powder, cumin, and smoked paprika are essential for creating that signature chili flavor. Don’t shy away from experimenting with other spices like oregano or cayenne pepper for a kick.
  • Broth: Vegetable broth or stock enhances the chili’s flavor while keeping it vegetarian. You can also use a homemade broth for an extra layer of taste.
  • Acid: A splash of lime juice or a dash of vinegar at the end of cooking brightens the dish and balances the flavors.

Classic Vegetarian Chili Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced (any color)
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: avocado, cilantro, cheese, or sour cream

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: Turn the Instant Pot to the sauté setting. Add olive oil and let it heat up. Add the diced onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Sauté for about 3-5 minutes until the vegetables are softened.

  2. Add Remaining Ingredients: Stir in the carrots, zucchini, black beans, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine all ingredients.

  3. Pressure Cook: Close the lid of the Instant Pot and ensure the valve is set to sealing. Select the ‘Pressure Cook’ setting and set the timer for 15 minutes.

  4. Natural Release: Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for about 10 minutes, then carefully switch the valve to venting to release any remaining pressure.

  5. Serve: Open the Instant Pot, stir the chili, and taste for seasoning. Serve hot, garnished with your choice of toppings.

Spicy Sweet Potato and Black Bean Chili

For those who love a touch of sweetness in their chili, this recipe combines sweet potatoes and black beans with a spicy kick.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño, minced (adjust to taste)
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Set the Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add coconut oil, onion, garlic, and jalapeño. Cook for 3-5 minutes until soft.

  2. Add Sweet Potatoes: Stir in the diced sweet potatoes, black beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.

  3. Pressure Cook: Secure the lid and set to pressure cook for 12 minutes.

  4. Release Pressure: Allow natural release for 10 minutes, then switch to venting.

  5. Final Touches: Stir well and adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve hot with toppings like avocado or chopped cilantro.

Lentil and Vegetable Chili

This hearty lentil chili is not only filling but also packed with protein and fiber, making it a nutritious choice for any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes (with juice)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Sauté: In the Instant Pot, heat olive oil on sauté mode. Add onion, garlic, carrots, and bell pepper. Cook for 5 minutes until softened.

  2. Combine Ingredients: Add lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, chili powder, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.

  3. Pressure Cook: Close the lid and set to pressure cook for 15 minutes.

  4. Natural Release: Let the pressure release naturally for 10 minutes, then vent.

  5. Serve: Stir well and adjust seasoning. Garnish with fresh herbs and serve.

Tips for Perfect Instant Pot Chili

  • Layering Flavors: Sautéing the onions and garlic first builds a flavor base that enhances the overall dish.
  • Bean Variations: Feel free to mix and match beans based on your preferences. White beans, garbanzo beans, or lentils work well.
  • Vegetable Additions: Experiment with seasonal vegetables like butternut squash or bell peppers to keep things interesting.
  • Storage: Chili stores well in the fridge for up to a week and can also be frozen for later meals. Reheat gently on the stove or in the microwave.

Conclusion

Making healthy and delicious vegetarian chili in your Instant Pot is an easy and satisfying way to enjoy a wholesome meal. With these recipes, you can tailor your chili to your taste preferences while reaping the benefits of plant-based eating. So gather your ingredients, fire up that Instant Pot, and indulge in a bowl of warmth and flavor that will nourish both body and soul. Whether you opt for the classic, the spicy sweet potato version, or the lentil-packed delight, each bite will remind you of the joy of cooking and the comfort of a well-made chili. Enjoy!
