One-Pot Cioppino: A Hearty and Flavorful Seafood Stew


If you’re a seafood lover looking for a comforting and flavorful dish to warm your soul, look no further than One-Pot Cioppino. This hearty and aromatic seafood stew originates from the Italian-American community in San Francisco and has become a beloved classic in many kitchens around the world. With a rich tomato-based broth, a variety of fresh seafood, and a hint of spice, this dish is a celebration of the ocean’s bounty. The best part? It’s easy to make and perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Ingredients

To create the perfect One-Pot Cioppino, you’ll need a selection of fresh and flavorful ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cups fish or seafood stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 pound mussels, scrubbed and debearded
  • 1 pound clams, scrubbed
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 pound firm white fish (such as halibut or cod), cut into chunks
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Now that you have your ingredients ready, it’s time to bring this delightful dish to life. Follow these simple steps to create your very own One-Pot Cioppino:

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and green bell pepper. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  2. Season and Simmer: Stir in the dried oregano, basil, and red pepper flakes, allowing the flavors to meld for a minute. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for a couple of minutes until slightly reduced.

  3. Add the Base: Add the crushed tomatoes, fish or seafood stock, and bay leaf to the pot. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes, allowing the flavors to develop and the liquid to reduce slightly.

  4. Add the Seafood: Gently add the mussels, clams, shrimp, and chunks of white fish to the pot. Cover with a lid and let the seafood cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the mussels and clams have opened, and the shrimp and fish are opaque and cooked through. Discard any unopened mussels or clams.

  5. Season and Serve: Season the One-Pot Cioppino with salt and pepper to taste. Ladle the stew into bowls, making sure to distribute the seafood evenly. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for a burst of color and freshness.

  6. Enjoy!: Serve the One-Pot Cioppino with crusty bread or over a bed of cooked linguine for a truly satisfying meal. Don’t forget to savor each spoonful and enjoy the rich flavors of the sea.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to customize your One-Pot Cioppino with your favorite seafood, such as scallops, squid, or crab legs. The key is to use the freshest seafood available for the best flavor.
  • For a richer broth, you can add a splash of heavy cream or a dollop of rouille, a French sauce made with olive oil, breadcrumbs, garlic, saffron, and chili peppers.
  • If you prefer a spicier kick, increase the amount of red pepper flakes or add a dash of hot sauce to the stew.

Final Thoughts

One-Pot Cioppino is a culinary masterpiece that brings the essence of the sea to your table. Its vibrant colors, robust flavors, and heartwarming aroma make it a perfect choice for special occasions or a cozy night in. Whether you’re an experienced cook or a novice in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress and delight your taste buds. So, gather your ingredients, don your apron, and embark on a culinary adventure with One-Pot Cioppino. Your senses and your loved ones will thank you!
