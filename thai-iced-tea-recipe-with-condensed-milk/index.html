Thai Iced Tea Recipe with Condensed Milk


If you’ve ever dined at a Thai restaurant, chances are you’ve come across the delightful and refreshing Thai iced tea. This sweet and creamy beverage is a perfect blend of strong black tea, aromatic spices, and silky condensed milk. The combination of flavors and textures makes it a popular choice for those looking to indulge in a unique and satisfying drink. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating your own homemade Thai iced tea with condensed milk, allowing you to savor this delightful drink in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of water
  • 4 Thai tea bags (you can find these at Asian grocery stores or online)
  • 1 star anise
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup of evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup of sweetened condensed milk
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  1. Brew the Tea: In a medium-sized saucepan, bring the water to a boil. Once boiling, add the Thai tea bags, star anise, cardamom pods, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 5 minutes, ensuring that the flavors infuse thoroughly. After 5 minutes, remove the saucepan from the heat and let the tea steep for an additional 15 minutes.

  2. Strain the Tea: Using a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth, strain the tea into a pitcher, ensuring that the spices and tea bags are removed. This will leave you with a rich, spiced tea base that forms the foundation of the Thai iced tea.

  3. Sweeten the Tea: While the tea is still warm, stir in the sugar until it completely dissolves. The warmth of the tea will help the sugar dissolve seamlessly, infusing the tea with a delightful sweetness.

  4. Chill the Tea: Place the pitcher of sweetened tea in the refrigerator and allow it to chill for at least 2 hours. Chilling the tea will enhance its refreshing nature and ensure that it’s ready to be transformed into a cool, invigorating beverage.

  5. Prepare the Serving Glasses: Fill glasses with ice cubes, leaving enough room for the creamy layer of condensed milk.

  6. Assemble the Thai Iced Tea: Once the tea has chilled, pour it over the ice-filled glasses, leaving some space at the top. Slowly drizzle in the evaporated milk, allowing it to cascade through the tea, creating a beautiful marbled effect. Then, finish off by adding a generous swirl of sweetened condensed milk on top of each glass. The condensed milk not only adds a creamy richness but also beautifully balances the bold flavors of the tea.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: With the glasses beautifully layered with tea, evaporated milk, and condensed milk, your homemade Thai iced tea with condensed milk is ready to be enjoyed. Stir gently before sipping to blend the flavors, and revel in the delightful combination of creamy sweetness and spiced tea.

This authentic Thai iced tea recipe with condensed milk offers a delightful escape to the bustling streets of Thailand, allowing you to savor the flavors of this beloved beverage in the comfort of your own home. Whether you’re entertaining guests or simply looking to treat yourself, this recipe is sure to impress with its unique blend of flavors and its visually stunning presentation. So, why not embark on a culinary journey and create your own batch of Thai iced tea with condensed milk today? Cheers to the sweet and creamy indulgence that is Thai iced tea!
