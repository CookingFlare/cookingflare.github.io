Pollo al Chilindrón: A Flavorful Spanish Chicken Stew


As the weather starts to get chilly, there’s nothing like a hearty and flavorful stew to warm up the soul. In this recipe, we’ll take you on a culinary journey to the sun-kissed Mediterranean, where the warmth of the Spanish cuisine will envelop you in its rich aromas and bold flavors. Say hello to Pollo al Chilindron, a mouthwatering Spanish chicken stew that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

The Story Behind the Dish

Pollo al Chilindron is a traditional Spanish dish that originated in the Andalusia region, where the warm climate and rich soil give rise to a plethora of fresh vegetables and herbs. The name “Chilindron” itself is derived from the Arabic word “al-chilindron,” meaning “garlic,” which is a key ingredient in this recipe. Over time, the dish has evolved and spread throughout Spain, with each region adding its own unique twist and flair.

The Recipe

** Servings: 4-6 people**

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 large bell peppers (any color), chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine (optional)
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.
  2. Reduce the heat to medium and add the minced garlic. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the garlic is softened and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped onion, bell peppers, and smoked paprika to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, cumin, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir to combine and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the browned chicken back to the pot, along with the white wine (if using) and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and cook, covered, for 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Stir in the chopped parsley, cilantro, and feta cheese (if using). Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  7. Serve the Pollo al Chilindron hot, garnished with additional parsley and cilantro if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more intense flavor, use homemade chicken broth instead of store-bought.
  • Add some heat to the dish by incorporating diced jalapeños or red pepper flakes.
  • Substitute the feta cheese with crumbled goat cheese or grated Parmesan for a different flavor profile.
  • Serve the stew with crusty bread or over rice for a comforting meal.

Conclusion

Pollo al Chilindron is a true celebration of Spanish cuisine, with its rich flavors and aromas transporting you to the sun-drenched Mediterranean. With its ease of preparation and flexibility, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and let the warm and comforting flavors of Pollo al Chilindron envelop you in its culinary embrace.
