Spice Up Your Meal: Making Korean Spicy Chicken Bulgogi


If you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or simply love the bold flavors of Asian cooking, then Korean Spicy Chicken Bulgogi is a dish that should definitely be on your radar. This spicy, savory, and slightly sweet chicken dish is a staple in Korean cuisine and is sure to elevate your home-cooked meals to a whole new level.

Ingredients

Before we delve into the cooking process, let’s take a look at the key ingredients you’ll need to prepare this delectable dish:

  • 1.5 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Optional: thinly sliced carrots, onions, and mushrooms for added vegetables

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by slicing the boneless, skinless chicken thighs into thin, bite-sized pieces. This will allow the chicken to absorb the flavors of the marinade and cook quickly and evenly.

Step 2: Make the Marinade

In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, gochujang, honey, sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and black pepper. Whisk the ingredients together until the marinade is well combined and the honey has dissolved.

Step 3: Marinate the Chicken

Place the sliced chicken in a large resealable plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over the chicken, ensuring that each piece is well coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, then refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse the chicken.

Step 4: Cook the Chicken

Heat a tablespoon of cooking oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken to the skillet and cook for 6-8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and caramelized on the edges.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the chicken is cooked, sprinkle the sliced green onions and toasted sesame seeds over the chicken, and stir to combine. Serve the Korean Spicy Chicken Bulgogi over steamed white rice and garnish with additional sesame seeds and sliced green onions for an extra pop of color and flavor.

Optional: Add Vegetables

If you’d like to add vegetables to your dish, you can stir-fry thinly sliced carrots, onions, and mushrooms in the same skillet before cooking the marinated chicken. This will not only add vibrant colors to your dish but also introduce a delightful crunch and extra nutrients.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Chicken Bulgogi is a fantastic way to spice up your mealtime routine with its bold and tantalizing flavors. Whether you’re a fan of spicy food or just looking to explore new culinary horizons, this dish is sure to impress. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to embark on a flavorful journey through the vibrant world of Korean cuisine. Enjoy!

Now that you have the recipe for Korean Spicy Chicken Bulgogi, it’s time to bring a taste of Korea to your kitchen. Happy cooking!
