Quick and Easy Chicken Alfredo Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious and satisfying meal that you can whip up in no time, then this quick and easy chicken alfredo recipe is just what you need. Creamy, flavorful, and oh-so-comforting, this dish is perfect for a busy weeknight or a cozy weekend dinner. The best part? You don’t need to be a master chef to pull this off – it’s simple, straightforward, and guaranteed to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fettuccine pasta
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. While the water is heating up, slice the chicken breasts into thin strips and season with salt and pepper. Also, grate the Parmesan cheese, mince the garlic, and chop the fresh parsley for garnish.

  2. Cook the Pasta: Once the water is boiling, add the fettuccine pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. This usually takes about 10-12 minutes. When the pasta is done, drain it and set it aside.

  3. Sauté the Chicken: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the seasoned chicken strips and cook until they are golden brown and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes per side. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set it aside.

  4. Make the Alfredo Sauce: In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and sauté for about a minute until fragrant. Reduce the heat to medium and add the heavy cream and butter. Stir the mixture continuously until the butter has melted and the cream is heated through. Be careful not to let the cream boil.

  5. Combine and Simmer: Once the cream and butter are well combined, add the grated Parmesan cheese to the skillet. Stir the sauce continuously until the cheese has melted and the sauce has thickened slightly. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. Bring It All Together: Add the cooked fettuccine pasta to the skillet with the alfredo sauce, tossing to coat the pasta evenly. Then, add the cooked chicken back to the skillet and gently toss everything together until the chicken is heated through.

  7. Serve and Enjoy: Once everything is well combined, transfer the chicken alfredo to a serving dish. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness. Serve hot and enjoy the creamy goodness!

Tips and Tricks

  • For a twist, you can add some sautéed mushrooms or steamed broccoli to the chicken alfredo for extra flavor and texture.
  • If you prefer a thinner sauce, you can add a splash of chicken broth or pasta cooking water to the skillet while making the alfredo sauce.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop, adding a splash of cream to revive the sauce if needed.

There you have it – a quick and easy chicken alfredo recipe that’s sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or just craving a comforting meal for yourself, this dish delivers on both flavor and convenience. Give it a try and let the creamy, dreamy goodness speak for itself!
