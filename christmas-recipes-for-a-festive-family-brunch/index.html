Christmas Recipes for a Festive Family Brunch


The holidays bring an air of magic, joy, and togetherness, and what better way to celebrate Christmas morning than with a delightful family brunch? This festive meal is the perfect opportunity to gather around and savor seasonal flavors while enjoying the warmth of shared moments. From indulgent mains to comforting sides and sweet treats, let’s explore a collection of recipes that are sure to create lasting memories on Christmas Day.

Savory Christmas Morning Casserole

Few dishes capture the festive essence of a family brunch like a hearty breakfast casserole. Packed with eggs, sausage, cheese, and seasonal flavors, it’s the ultimate one-dish meal that delivers satisfaction with every bite.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk (or your preferred alternative, such as almond or oat milk)
  • 1 pound crumbled breakfast sausage (or plant-based sausage for a vegetarian twist)
  • 4 cups cubed bread (preferably day-old sourdough or brioche)
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced bell peppers (red and green for a festive touch)
  • 1/3 cup diced onion
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with butter or cooking spray.
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until thoroughly browned. Set aside to cool.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. To the greased baking dish, layer the bread cubes, cooked sausage, bell peppers, onions, and cheese. Pour the egg mixture evenly over the top, ensuring it’s fully absorbed into the bread.
  5. Cover with foil and let it rest in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Alternatively, prepare the dish the night before for ease in the morning.
  6. Bake covered for 30 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the eggs are set.
  7. Let cool for five minutes, then serve slices with a fresh green salad for balance.

Cranberry Orange Mimosa

No brunch is complete without a festive drink, and this cranberry orange mimosa combines sweet, tart, and citrusy notes that make it ideal for Christmas morning. Beautifully vibrant, it’s refreshing and celebratory while remaining easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cranberry juice (100% juice for a natural flavor)
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1 bottle chilled champagne or sparkling wine (substitute sparkling water or non-alcoholic cider for a kid-friendly version)
  • Whole cranberries and orange slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Chill your juice and champagne ahead of time to ensure the drink maintains its crispness.
  2. In a champagne flute or a tall glass, pour equal parts cranberry juice and orange juice.
  3. Top with champagne, sparkling wine, or your non-alcoholic substitute.
  4. Garnish with a couple of cranberries and a thin orange slice for a festive presentation.

Gingerbread Pancakes with Maple Whipped Cream

Spice up your brunch table with gingerbread pancakes that taste like Christmas in every bite. They’re fluffy, aromatic, and pair perfectly with a dollop of maple whipped cream.

Ingredients for Pancakes:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

Ingredients for Maple Whipped Cream:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, sift together the dry ingredients: flour, baking powder, baking soda, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, and sugar.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the wet ingredients: molasses, eggs, milk, and melted butter.
  3. Combine the wet and dry ingredients until just mixed. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Heat a griddle or non-stick skillet over medium heat. Lightly grease with butter, then pour 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden and cooked through.
  5. For the whipped cream, beat heavy cream and maple syrup in a chilled bowl until soft peaks form. Serve alongside pancakes for dipping or as a topping.

Smoked Salmon and Dill Croissant Sandwiches

Add a touch of elegance to your brunch menu with smoked salmon croissant sandwiches. They’re simple yet sophisticated and ideal for seafood lovers.

Ingredients:

  • 6 fresh croissants, halved horizontally
  • 8 ounces smoked salmon
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • Thinly sliced cucumber for added crunch

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix the cream cheese, dill, lemon juice, and zest until smooth and creamy.
  2. Spread the cream cheese mixture generously onto the bottom half of each croissant.
  3. Layer smoked salmon and cucumber slices before placing the top half of the croissant.
  4. Serve on a platter surrounded by fresh greens for a classy presentation.

Mini Sweet Potato Hash Cups

This bite-sized dish packs bold flavors and seasonal appeal. These mini sweet potato hash cups are an appealing finger food loaded with warmth and wholesomeness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and grated
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional toppings: diced avocado, fresh parsley, or chili flakes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a bowl, combine grated sweet potatoes, Parmesan cheese, eggs, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spoon the mixture into the muffin tin, pressing down lightly to create small “cups.”
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes or until crisp and golden on the edges.
  5. Cool slightly before removing from the tin and topping with avocado slices, parsley, or a sprinkle of chili flakes.

Peppermint Chocolate Bark

End your brunch on a sweet note with peppermint chocolate bark. This festive dessert is easy to whip up and doubles as a thoughtful homemade gift for guests.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate chips
  • 6 ounces white chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup crushed candy canes
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract

Instructions:

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Melt the dark chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl, stirring every 30 seconds until smooth. Add peppermint extract and stir until combined.
  3. Pour the melted dark chocolate onto the parchment sheet and spread evenly using a spatula.
  4. Melt the white chocolate chips in a separate bowl. Drizzle the white chocolate over the dark base and use a skewer to create a swirling pattern.
  5. Sprinkle crushed candy canes over the bark. Chill in the refrigerator for 1 hour or until fully hardened.
  6. Break into pieces and serve alongside coffee or tea.

Conclusion

A festive family brunch is much more than a meal—it’s an opportunity to create cherished memories while celebrating the season. Whether you opt for savory staples like casseroles and sandwiches or sweet indulgences like pancakes and peppermint bark, each recipe brings a touch of Christmas spirit to your table. As your loved ones gather, let this carefully curated menu spark joy and conversation, making Christmas morning truly magical.
