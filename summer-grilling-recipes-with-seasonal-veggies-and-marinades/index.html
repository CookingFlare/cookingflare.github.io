Summer Grilling: Recipes with Seasonal Veggies and Marinades


Summer is the perfect time to fire up the grill and enjoy the great outdoors. And what better way to make the most of the season than by incorporating fresh, seasonal vegetables into your grilling recipes? In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious and nutritious recipes that highlight the flavors of summer and combine them with mouthwatering marinades.

Grilled Zucchini Skewers with Lemon-Herb Marinade

Zucchini is a versatile vegetable that takes on the flavors of marinades beautifully. For this recipe, you’ll need:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced into rounds
  • 1 lemon, juiced and zested
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

To prepare the marinade, combine the lemon juice, lemon zest, olive oil, minced garlic, fresh thyme, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Toss the zucchini rounds in the marinade, making sure they are evenly coated. Let the zucchini marinate for at least 30 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

While the zucchini is marinating, preheat your grill to medium heat. Once hot, place the zucchini rounds on skewers and grill for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and have grill marks. Serve these tasty grilled zucchini skewers as a side dish or as a healthy addition to your favorite summer salads.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Spicy Chipotle Butter

Nothing says summer like fresh corn on the cob. Elevate this classic barbecue staple with a spicy chipotle butter that will leave your taste buds craving more. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 ears of sweet corn, husks removed
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lime, juiced
  • Salt to taste

To prepare the chipotle butter, mix the softened butter, minced chipotle pepper, chopped cilantro, lime juice, and salt in a small bowl. Set aside.

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Place the corn directly on the grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the kernels are tender and slightly charred. Remove the corn from the grill and generously spread the chipotle butter over each ear. Serve hot and enjoy the smoky, spicy flavors of this delicious summer side dish.

Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burgers with Balsamic Glaze

For those who prefer a meatless option, grilled portobello mushroom burgers are a tasty and satisfying choice. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 large portobello mushroom caps
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Place the mushroom caps in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, ensuring they are well coated. Let the mushrooms marinate for at least 20 minutes.

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill for about 5 minutes on each side, or until they are tender and juicy. Baste the mushrooms with the remaining marinade while grilling to enhance the flavors.

Serve the grilled portobello mushroom burgers on toasted buns with your favorite toppings, such as lettuce, tomato, and avocado. The rich umami flavors of the mushrooms combined with the tangy balsamic glaze create a truly satisfying vegetarian burger experience.

Conclusion

Summer grilling is all about embracing the flavors of the season, and what better way to do that than with fresh, seasonal vegetables and mouthwatering marinades? These recipes are just a starting point for your culinary adventures, so feel free to experiment with different veggies and marinades to create your own summer grilling masterpieces. Happy grilling!
