Roasted Acorn Squash with Maple Glaze: A Fall Delight


As the leaves begin to change colors and the crisp autumn air sets in, our taste buds crave the warm, comforting flavors of the season. One of the most iconic and delicious fall ingredients is the humble acorn squash. Sweet, nutty, and utterly satisfying, roasted acorn squash is a staple of the season. But why settle for plain roasted squash when you can elevate it to new heights with a rich, sticky maple glaze? In this recipe, we’ll show you how to bring out the full potential of this autumnal superstar.

The Perfect Squash for the Job

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s talk about the importance of choosing the right squash. Acorn squash is a type of winter squash that’s specifically designed for roasting. Its sweet, slightly nutty flavor and dense, meaty texture make it the perfect candidate for a maple glaze. Look for squash that’s heavy for its size, with a hard, smooth skin and no signs of soft spots or bruising.

The Magic of Maple Glaze

The real magic happens when we add a sweet and sticky maple glaze to our roasted squash. This simple mixture of pure maple syrup, apple cider vinegar, and spices adds a depth of flavor and a caramelized crust that elevates the dish to new heights. The key is to not overpower the natural sweetness of the squash, but rather to enhance it with a subtle, balanced flavor. Our maple glaze achieves this perfect balance, making it the perfect accompaniment to our roasted acorn squash.

The Recipe

Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 large acorn squash (about 2 lbs)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut the squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds and pulp. Place the squash on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt, black pepper, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Roast the squash in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and caramelized.

While the squash is roasting, prepare the maple glaze. In a small saucepan, combine the maple syrup, apple cider vinegar, and water. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the glaze has thickened slightly. Remove from heat and set aside.

Once the squash is done roasting, remove it from the oven and brush the maple glaze all over the flesh. Return the squash to the oven and roast for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the glaze is caramelized and sticky.

Serve and Enjoy

Roasted acorn squash with maple glaze is a versatile dish that can be served as a side, a main course, or even as a topping for salads or soups. Try pairing it with a hearty green salad, crumbled goat cheese, and a drizzle of balsamic glaze for a satisfying fall lunch. Or, serve it alongside a roasted chicken or pork tenderloin for a comforting fall dinner.

Conclusion

Roasted acorn squash with maple glaze is a true fall delight, a dish that combines the natural sweetness of the season with the comforting warmth of a rich, sticky glaze. With its simple ingredients and easy preparation, this recipe is a must-try for anyone looking to elevate their fall cooking game. So go ahead, give it a try, and taste the magic of the season for yourself.
