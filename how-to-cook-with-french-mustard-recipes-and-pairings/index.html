How to Cook with French Mustard: Recipes and Pairings


French mustard, also known as Dijon mustard, has been a staple condiment in French cuisine for centuries. Its unique flavor profile, with its characteristic blend of sharpness and tanginess, makes it a versatile ingredient in a variety of dishes, from classic sauces and marinades to salad dressings and dips. In this post, we'll delve into the world of French mustard, exploring its history, uses, and pairings, as well as providing you with some mouth-watering recipes to get you started. A Brief History of French Mustard French mustard, specifically Dijon mustard, originated in the town of Dijon in eastern France in the 13th century. The mustard seeds used in its production were carefully selected and ground into a fine paste to create a distinctive flavor profile. Dijon mustard quickly became a prized condiment among the French aristocracy, who used it to enhance the flavor of various dishes, from meats to sauces. Over the centuries, French mustard remained a closely guarded secret, with only a select few in the region knowing the precise ratio of mustard seeds to vinegar, salt, and spices that gave it its signature taste. Today, the art of Dijon mustard-making continues, with producers in France adhering to traditional techniques while innovating new flavors and textures. Using French Mustard in Your Cooking French mustard offers a unique flavor profile that complements a wide range of ingredients, making it a staple condiment in many French and international dishes. From classic deviled eggs to salad dressings, dips, and marinades, French mustard adds a tangy, slightly sweet flavor that elevates the taste of any dish. Its versatility lies in its ability to balance the flavors of acidic ingredients, while its strong mustard flavor adds a bold, pungent taste. The Essentials of French Mustard Before diving into recipes, it's essential to understand the basic characteristics of French mustard, which will guide your choice of recipe:

Phytagoras: mustard packs a strong flavor, so it's often used in small amounts to balance out dishes. Texture: French mustard can be smooth or coarse, with the former tending towards a more neutral flavor and the latter adding a biting kick. Spices: Certain spices, such as coriander, cumin, and cayenne, are often added to create new and refreshing flavor profiles.

Recipe Ideas for French Mustard Now that we have explored the concept of French mustard and its secrets, let's dive into some original and different cooking recipes: Recipe 1: Classic French Deviled Ham Salad

6 ounces of ham, diced 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard 1 tablespoon of chopped parsley 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh chives 1/2 cup of fresh bread crumbs Salt and pepper to taste 1 tablespoon of chopped olive tapenade (optional)

Combine diced ham, Dijon mustard, chopped parsley, chives, and salt and pepper to taste. Serve on buttered bread or crackers, topped with a drizzle of olive oil. If you like, add the French classics to the Deviled Eggs of French cuisine. Recipe 2: Creamy French Potato Salad

1 pound of cooked potatoes, diced 1 tablespoon of French mustard 1 cup of grated cheddar cheese 1 cup of heavy cream Salt and pepper to taste 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh parsley

Combine cooked potatoes, French mustard, grated cheddar cheese, heavy cream, and chopped fresh parsley. Serve chilled or at room temperature. A pesto-based potato salad is a delicious flatbread curry for leftovers. Recipe 3: Salad with French Vinaigerons

1 head of lettuce 1 cup of chopped cucumber 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved 1 cup of chopped watermelon 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh dill Salt and pepper to taste 2 tablespoons of chopped pesto

Combine chopped lettuce, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and chopped watermelon in a bowl. In a separate bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, whipped heavy cream, salt and pepper, and chopped fresh dill. Drizzle the creamy mustard mixture over the salad, garnished with chopped pesto and fresh chives. Recipe 4: Crispy French Mustard Meatballs

2 pounds of ground meat 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh parsley 1/2 cup of panko 1 egg Salt and pepper to taste

Combine ground meat, Dijon mustard, chopped parsley, panko, and egg in a bowl. Mix well and form meatballs. Serve on toothpicks or serve them as a family standby side dish, offering friends and family a treat to remember. Recipe 5: French Mustard-Crusted Chicken Cutlets

1 pound of chicken cutlets 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard 1 cup of panko 1 egg 1/4 cup of grated cheddar

Combine chicken cutlets, Dijon mustard, panko, egg, salt and pepper, and grated cheddar cheese. Mix gently and add a pinch of pink pigment or Mexican mozzarella for garnish.
