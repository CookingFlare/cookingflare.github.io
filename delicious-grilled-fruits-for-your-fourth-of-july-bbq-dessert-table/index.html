Delicious Grilled Fruits for Your Fourth of July BBQ Dessert Table


As the summer heat kicks in, the Fourth of July arrives with all its patriotic fervor, bringing family, friends, and food together in perfect harmony. If you’re planning to host a BBQ for the occasion, why not elevate your dessert table with some unique and delicious grilled fruits? Grilling fruits brings out their natural sweetness and enhances their flavors, making them the perfect sweet finish to a savory BBQ meal.

Grilled fruits are not only a delicious treat, but they also bring a beautiful smoky flavor and caramelized texture to the table. These simple-to-make desserts are the perfect balance of refreshing and indulgent, showcasing the best that summer has to offer. Whether you’re serving them as individual servings or incorporating them into a grand dessert spread, grilled fruits can add a fun and festive touch to your Fourth of July BBQ.

Why Grilled Fruits Are Perfect for the Fourth of July BBQ

Grilling fruits is a brilliant way to make your dessert stand out while keeping it light and refreshing. Here’s why you should consider grilling fruits at your BBQ this year:

  1. Healthier Alternative to Traditional Desserts
    Grilled fruits are a great way to offer a lighter dessert option without compromising on flavor. While traditional sugary treats can be heavy, grilled fruits provide natural sweetness with fewer added sugars and more nutrients.

  2. Easy to Prepare
    Most fruits require little prep work before grilling. Just slice, season, and throw them on the grill for a few minutes. They cook quickly, which makes them ideal for large gatherings.

  3. Versatile Flavors
    You can experiment with different fruits and seasoning combinations to create diverse flavors. Whether you prefer tangy citrus or sweet berries, grilled fruits can cater to every taste.

  4. Perfect for Outdoor BBQs
    There’s something magical about grilling during a summer BBQ. Grilled fruits offer a fun, interactive dessert that adds to the overall ambiance and keeps your guests engaged.

  5. Compliment Savory BBQ Flavors
    The smoky char from grilling complements the savory flavors of BBQ meats and grilled vegetables, providing a balanced and satisfying meal.

Let’s dive into some of the best grilled fruit recipes that will steal the show at your Fourth of July BBQ dessert table.

Grilled Peaches with Honey and Cinnamon

Peaches are a classic summer fruit that becomes even more delicious when grilled. Grilling caramelizes their natural sugars, creating a soft, juicy texture that is perfect for a BBQ dessert. A drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of cinnamon turn these simple grilled peaches into a sophisticated treat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of sea salt
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the peach halves with olive oil to prevent them from sticking to the grill.
  3. Place the peaches, cut side down, on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until grill marks appear and the fruit starts to soften.
  4. Flip the peaches and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes on the other side.
  5. Remove from the grill and drizzle with honey, then sprinkle with cinnamon and a pinch of sea salt.
  6. Garnish with fresh mint leaves for a pop of color and freshness.
  7. Serve warm as is or top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent touch.

Grilled Pineapple with Coconut Flakes

Grilled pineapple is a tropical treat that can transport your guests to an island paradise. The natural sugars in the pineapple caramelize beautifully on the grill, while the addition of toasted coconut flakes adds a delightful crunch. This simple yet indulgent dessert will definitely be a crowd favorite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole pineapple, peeled, cored, and cut into rings
  • 2 tablespoons melted coconut oil or butter
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Brush the pineapple rings with melted coconut oil or butter to prevent sticking.
  3. Sprinkle brown sugar over both sides of the pineapple slices for extra caramelization.
  4. Place the pineapple rings on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, until golden grill marks form and the pineapple softens.
  5. While the pineapple is grilling, toast the shredded coconut flakes in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly until golden brown.
  6. Once the pineapple is done, remove it from the grill and sprinkle with the toasted coconut flakes.
  7. Serve immediately, perhaps with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of coconut ice cream.

Grilled Watermelon with Feta and Balsamic Glaze

Watermelon is one of the most refreshing fruits of summer, and grilling it enhances its sweetness while adding a smoky complexity. Pairing grilled watermelon with salty feta cheese and a drizzle of balsamic glaze creates an irresistible flavor combination. This unique dessert is sure to be a conversation starter at your BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small seedless watermelon, cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the watermelon wedges with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the watermelon wedges on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes per side, until grill marks form and the watermelon softens slightly.
  4. Remove from the grill and top each wedge with crumbled feta cheese.
  5. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and garnish with fresh basil leaves.
  6. Serve immediately for a refreshing and savory-sweet treat that’s perfect for a summer BBQ.

Grilled Apples with Brown Sugar and Walnuts

Grilled apples are another fantastic option for a Fourth of July dessert. The heat of the grill softens the apples, while the brown sugar and walnuts add warmth and crunch. These grilled apples make for a delightful, comforting dessert that’s perfect for an outdoor BBQ.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium apples, cored and halved
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush the cut sides of the apple halves with melted butter.
  3. Sprinkle the brown sugar, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt evenly over the apples.
  4. Place the apple halves cut side down on the grill and cook for 4-5 minutes, until they start to soften and develop grill marks.
  5. Flip the apples and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes, until tender.
  6. Remove from the grill and top with chopped walnuts for a crunchy finish.
  7. Serve warm, perhaps with a scoop of cinnamon ice cream for a comforting end to your BBQ feast.

Grilled Banana Boats with Chocolate and Marshmallows

For a truly decadent and indulgent dessert, try grilled banana boats. Grilled bananas, stuffed with chocolate and marshmallows, melt together into gooey goodness, making for a fun, interactive dessert that both kids and adults will love.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup mini marshmallows
  • 1 tablespoon chopped nuts (optional)
  • Aluminum foil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Slice each banana lengthwise down the center, leaving the peel intact. Be careful not to slice all the way through.
  3. Gently open the banana along the slit and stuff with chocolate chips, mini marshmallows, and chopped nuts, if using.
  4. Wrap each stuffed banana in aluminum foil and place them on the grill.
  5. Grill the banana boats for 5-7 minutes, until the chocolate and marshmallows have melted and the banana is soft.
  6. Carefully remove from the foil and serve immediately for a gooey, warm treat that’s perfect for the occasion.

Grilled Strawberries with Balsamic and Mint

Grilled strawberries are a surprising and delightful addition to any BBQ dessert table. The heat intensifies their natural sweetness, and when paired with a balsamic reduction and fresh mint, they create an unforgettable flavor profile. These grilled strawberries are ideal for a lighter yet flavorful dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint fresh strawberries, hulled and halved
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Toss the strawberries in olive oil and sprinkle with brown sugar.
  3. Grill the strawberries for 2-3 minutes per side, until they soften and develop grill marks.
  4. While the strawberries are grilling, reduce the balsamic vinegar by simmering it in a small saucepan over medium heat for about 5 minutes, until it thickens.
  5. Once the strawberries are grilled, drizzle them with the balsamic reduction and garnish with fresh mint leaves.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature for a refreshing, tangy-sweet dessert.

Final Thoughts

Grilled fruits are a creative and delicious way to wrap up your Fourth of July BBQ. From the rich and smoky flavors of grilled peaches to the sweet, caramelized perfection of grilled pineapple, there’s a grilled fruit dessert for everyone. With minimal preparation and quick grilling times, you can create a dessert table that’s both impressive and easy to execute.

Grilling fruits is a great way to experiment with new flavors, textures, and combinations. By using seasonal summer fruits and simple ingredients like honey, cinnamon, and balsamic vinegar, you can transform everyday fruits into decadent, crowd-pleasing desserts. So, fire up the grill and let your creativity shine as you celebrate Independence Day with these delicious grilled fruit recipes.
