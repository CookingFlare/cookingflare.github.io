Farm to Table Cooking: 7 Recipes for a Cozy Autumn Evening


There’s something inherently comforting about autumn — the cooler breezes, golden hues on every tree, and the crisp air that heralds the start of hearty, home-cooked meals. For those who cherish the idea of seasonal eating and supporting local farmers, fall provides the perfect opportunity to bring fresh, in-season ingredients straight from the farm to your table. This is the season of abundance, where squash, root vegetables, hearty greens, and apples take center stage, and every dish tells a story of the earth’s bounty.

Below are seven soul-warming farm-to-table recipes tailored for an idyllic autumn evening. From rustic soups to indulgent desserts, these recipes celebrate the season in all its glory.

1. Butternut Squash and Apple Soup

A bowl of warm soup is like a hug from the inside, and this butternut squash and apple soup strikes the perfect balance between sweet and savory. With ingredients sourced fresh from a nearby farmers market, this dish sets the tone for a cozy autumn supper.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash (peeled, seeded, and cubed)
  • 2 medium apples (peeled, cored, and chopped)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk or heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Place the cubed squash and apples on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt. Roast for 25–30 minutes or until tender.
  2. In a large pot, sauté the chopped onion in olive oil over medium heat until translucent.
  3. Add the roasted squash, apples, broth, cinnamon, and nutmeg to the pot. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Using an immersion blender, purée the soup until smooth. Stir in the coconut milk or cream, adjust the seasoning, and serve with crusty bread.

2. Autumn Harvest Salad with Maple Vinaigrette

A salad might not feel seasonal at first glance, but with roasted root vegetables, toasted nuts, and a luscious maple vinaigrette, this dish offers all the fall flavors while still keeping things light and fresh.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed greens (such as arugula, spinach, and kale)
  • 1 medium sweet potato, cubed
  • 1 cup peeled and cubed beets
  • ½ cup crumbled goat cheese or feta
  • ¼ cup dried cranberries
  • ¼ cup toasted pecans or walnuts

For the vinaigrette:

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Toss the sweet potato and beet cubes in olive oil, season with salt and pepper, and roast for 25–30 minutes until tender.
  2. Assemble the salad by layering the greens, roasted vegetables, goat cheese, cranberries, and nuts.
  3. Whisk together the vinaigrette ingredients and drizzle over the salad just before serving.

3. Lentil and Mushroom Shepherd’s Pie

This vegetarian twist on a traditional shepherd’s pie will satisfy even the most devoted meat lovers. The umami-rich flavor of mushrooms combined with earthy lentils makes for a deeply comforting dish.

Ingredients:

For the filling:

  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2 cups mushrooms (sliced)
  • 2 medium carrots (diced)
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the topping:

  • 4 medium potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • ¼ cup milk (or a non-dairy substitute)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Cook the lentils in the vegetable broth as per package instructions and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil and sauté the onion, garlic, and mushrooms until softened. Add the carrots, tomato paste, thyme, and cooked lentils. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, boil the potatoes until fork-tender. Mash them with butter, milk, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spread the lentil and mushroom mixture in a baking dish and top with the mashed potatoes. Bake for 20 minutes or until lightly golden.

4. Savory Pumpkin Risotto

Pumpkin plays a starring role in this creamy, dreamy risotto, which is the epitome of fall comfort food. Serve alongside a simple green salad for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup pumpkin purée
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sage (fresh or dried)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, heat the vegetable broth and keep it warm over low heat.
  2. In a large pan, heat olive oil and sauté the onion until translucent. Add the rice and cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Slowly add broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently and allowing the liquid to absorb before adding more. Continue until the rice is creamy and tender.
  4. Stir in the pumpkin purée, Parmesan, butter, sage, salt, and pepper. Serve immediately.

5. Stuffed Acorn Squash with Wild Rice and Cranberries

Perfect as a main or side dish, these stuffed acorn squash are as beautiful as they are delicious. They also make a wonderful plant-based option for holiday get-togethers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium acorn squash (halved and seeds removed)
  • 1 cup cooked wild rice
  • ½ cup dried cranberries
  • ¼ cup toasted pumpkin seeds
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Brush the cut sides of the squash with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Place them cut side up on a baking sheet and roast for 35–40 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, sauté the onion in olive oil until softened. Combine with cooked wild rice, cranberries, pumpkin seeds, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Fill the squash halves with the rice mixture and return to the oven for 10 minutes. Serve warm.

6. Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

A simple yet irresistible side dish, these Brussels sprouts provide the perfect mix of caramelized sweetness and tangy glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts (halved)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread them on a baking sheet in a single layer and roast for 20–25 minutes, stirring halfway through.
  3. Drizzle with balsamic vinegar and honey before serving.

7. Spiced Apple Crisp with Oat Topping

No autumn meal is complete without a dessert, and this spiced apple crisp is the ultimate seasonal treat. It’s easy to make and showcases the best of fall’s apple harvest.

Ingredients:

  • 6 medium apples (peeled, cored, and sliced)
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup butter (softened)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Toss the apple slices with brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Spread them in a baking dish.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix oats, flour, and butter together until crumbly. Sprinkle over the apples.
  3. Bake for 30–35 minutes or until the topping is golden and the apples are tender. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream if desired.

These seven recipes bring together vibrant autumn ingredients in ways that honor their natural flavors and textures. By prioritizing locally sourced produce, you not only enhance the taste and nutrition of your meals, but you also support your local farming community — a true win-win.

So, light a few candles, pour yourself a warm drink, and enjoy the cozy comfort that these farm-to-table creations bring to your table.
