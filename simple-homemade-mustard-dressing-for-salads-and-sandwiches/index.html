Simple Homemade Mustard Dressing for Salads and Sandwiches


When it comes to dressing your salads or spreading on sandwiches, a homemade mustard dressing can make all the difference. Tangy, zesty, and incredibly versatile, mustard dressing is a must-have in any kitchen. Not only does it elevate your dishes, but it’s also easy to prepare with ingredients you likely already have at home.

In this post, we will dive into how to make a simple, flavorful mustard dressing that pairs beautifully with a variety of salads and sandwiches. Plus, we’ll explore different variations and tips on how to customize the dressing to suit your taste.

Why Choose Mustard Dressing?

Mustard dressing is a fantastic alternative to heavy creamy dressings, offering a lighter, more refreshing option. It’s packed with flavor and brings a delightful sharpness that can cut through the richness of your meals. Whether you’re tossing a fresh leafy salad or adding some zing to your sandwich, mustard dressing brings the perfect balance of acidity, sweetness, and spice.

Here are a few reasons why mustard dressing is a great choice for your next meal:

  • Quick and Easy: It takes just minutes to whip up.
  • Low in Calories: Unlike creamy dressings, mustard is typically low in fat and calories.
  • Vegan-Friendly: With the right ingredients, mustard dressing can be completely plant-based.
  • Versatile: Works with a wide range of dishes including salads, roasted vegetables, and even grilled meats.

Let’s get started with the recipe.

The Basic Mustard Dressing Recipe

This mustard dressing recipe is incredibly easy to make and uses just a few staple ingredients. With its tangy base and slightly sweet undertones, it pairs perfectly with a wide variety of salads and sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard: This type of mustard gives the dressing a smooth texture and sharp flavor. You can also experiment with yellow mustard if you prefer something milder.
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar: The vinegar adds acidity, balancing out the richness of the mustard.
  • 1 teaspoon honey: A little honey helps to mellow out the sharpness of the mustard and vinegar, creating a well-rounded flavor.
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil: This will help emulsify the dressing, giving it a smooth and creamy texture.
  • Salt and pepper: Season to taste.
  • Optional: 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder or minced garlic: If you want a bit of extra flavor, garlic adds a nice kick.

Instructions:

  1. Whisk the Ingredients Together: In a small bowl, combine the Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, honey, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Whisk them together until the ingredients are well combined.

  2. Add Olive Oil: Gradually add the olive oil while continuously whisking. The oil will slowly emulsify into the mustard mixture, creating a smooth and creamy dressing. This process helps prevent the oil from separating.

  3. Taste and Adjust: Taste the dressing and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you like a little more sweetness, add an extra drizzle of honey. If you want a bit more acidity, feel free to add a splash more vinegar.

  4. Serve: Once the dressing is smooth and well-balanced, it’s ready to serve. Drizzle it over a fresh salad, toss with roasted vegetables, or spread it on a sandwich for an added burst of flavor.

Storage:

If you have leftovers, store the dressing in an airtight container in the refrigerator. It will stay fresh for up to one week. Before using it again, just give it a quick stir or shake as the ingredients may separate over time.

Variations to Try

While the basic mustard dressing is delicious on its own, there are many ways you can customize it to suit your personal taste or the dish you’re preparing. Here are a few variations to consider:

1. Creamy Mustard Dressing

If you prefer a creamier dressing, you can add a dollop of plain yogurt, sour cream, or mayonnaise to the mustard base. This will create a smoother, richer texture, while still maintaining the tangy flavor of the mustard.

  • Add 2 tablespoons of mayonnaise or 2 tablespoons of Greek yogurt after whisking the mustard and vinegar together. This will give you a thicker, creamier dressing that works great on sandwiches or as a dip.

2. Herbed Mustard Dressing

If you enjoy a burst of fresh flavors, try incorporating herbs into your mustard dressing. Fresh or dried herbs can elevate the taste and add a fragrant aroma.

  • Add 1 tablespoon of finely chopped fresh herbs like parsley, thyme, dill, or chives. These herbs will bring a refreshing element to the dressing.
  • Alternatively, you can use 1 teaspoon of dried herbs if you prefer a more subtle herb flavor.

3. Spicy Mustard Dressing

For those who love a bit of heat, a spicy mustard dressing is a great option. You can add chili flakes or a dash of hot sauce to your basic recipe.

  • Add 1/4 teaspoon of chili flakes or a few dashes of hot sauce to the mustard and vinegar mixture. This will create a spicy kick that will liven up your salad or sandwich.

4. Lemon Mustard Dressing

If you’re looking for a citrusy twist, lemon is a wonderful addition to mustard dressing. It enhances the tanginess and gives the dressing a fresh, zesty flavor.

  • Add 1 tablespoon of freshly squeezed lemon juice along with the vinegar. The lemon will brighten up the dressing and provide a more vibrant flavor profile.

5. Maple Mustard Dressing

If you like a sweet and savory contrast, swapping honey for maple syrup can create a unique twist on the classic mustard dressing.

  • Substitute the honey with maple syrup in equal amounts. This will add a subtle sweetness and a hint of caramel flavor, perfect for roasted vegetables or as a glaze for grilled meats.

How to Use Mustard Dressing

The beauty of mustard dressing lies in its versatility. Here are some ways to use it to enhance your meals:

1. Salads

A simple mustard dressing can take your salad to the next level. Whether you’re making a classic green salad, a grain-based salad like quinoa or farro, or a simple mixed greens salad, this dressing adds flavor and balance. Toss your greens in a bit of mustard dressing and enjoy the delicious tangy flavor that elevates your salad.

2. Sandwiches and Wraps

Mustard dressing works wonderfully as a spread for sandwiches and wraps. It’s a great alternative to mayonnaise and can be paired with a variety of sandwich fillings, such as turkey, chicken, or roasted vegetables. The mustard dressing adds a light but bold flavor that complements the other ingredients.

3. Roasted Vegetables

Drizzle the mustard dressing over roasted vegetables like Brussels sprouts, carrots, or cauliflower for an added punch. The acidity of the dressing helps cut through the richness of the roasted veggies and enhances their natural sweetness.

4. Grilled Meats

Grilled meats such as chicken, pork, or steak pair wonderfully with mustard dressing. Use it as a marinade before grilling, or serve it as a tangy sauce on the side. The mustard’s sharpness complements the smoky, charred flavors of grilled meats.

5. Grain Bowls

For a hearty meal, drizzle the mustard dressing over grain bowls. These bowls typically feature a base of quinoa, brown rice, or couscous, topped with veggies, protein, and sometimes cheese or nuts. The mustard dressing ties all the ingredients together and adds a punch of flavor.

Tips for Perfecting Your Mustard Dressing

To ensure your mustard dressing is always a success, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Balance the flavors: Taste your dressing as you go and adjust the vinegar, mustard, and sweetness to achieve a balanced flavor. You want the dressing to have a good balance of tangy, sweet, and savory notes.
  • Use good-quality olive oil: Since olive oil makes up a significant portion of the dressing, using a high-quality extra virgin olive oil will improve the overall taste.
  • Whisk well: To achieve the right consistency, make sure you whisk the dressing thoroughly, especially when incorporating the olive oil. This ensures a smooth, emulsified dressing.
  • Allow it to sit: For the best flavor, allow the dressing to sit for at least 10-15 minutes before using. This allows the flavors to meld together and intensify.

Conclusion

Mustard dressing is a quick, easy, and versatile addition to your culinary repertoire. Whether you’re using it to dress a fresh salad, spread it on a sandwich, or drizzle it over roasted vegetables, it adds a delightful tang that enhances any dish. With just a few ingredients, you can create a dressing that is both flavorful and light, making it the perfect addition to your meals.

Feel free to experiment with different variations to make this mustard dressing your own, and enjoy its bright, zesty flavors that bring your meals to life. Happy cooking!
