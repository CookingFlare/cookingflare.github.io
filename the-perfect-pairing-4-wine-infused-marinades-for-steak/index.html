The Perfect Pairing: 4 Wine-Infused Marinades for Steak


Steak and wine, a match made in heaven. The rich flavors of a perfectly cooked steak can be elevated to new heights when paired with a wine-infused marinade. In this post, we’ll explore four unique wine-infused marinades that will take your steak game to the next level.

Marinade 1: Cabernet Sauvignon and Garlic

This classic combination is a staple for a reason. The bold flavors of Cabernet Sauvignon pair perfectly with the pungency of garlic, creating a marinade that’s both savory and aromatic.

  • 1 cup Cabernet Sauvignon
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Combine all ingredients in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Place steak in a zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour marinade over it. Seal bag or cover dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 2: Merlot and Mushroom

For a more earthy and umami flavor profile, try pairing Merlot with sautéed mushrooms. The rich, fruity notes of the wine complement the earthy flavors of the mushrooms perfectly.

  • 1 cup Merlot
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 cup sautéed mushrooms (such as cremini or shiitake)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Combine all ingredients in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Place steak in a zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour marinade over it. Seal bag or cover dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 3: Pinot Noir and Lemon

For a lighter, brighter flavor profile, try pairing Pinot Noir with lemon and herbs. The acidity of the lemon cuts through the richness of the wine, creating a refreshing and citrusy marinade.

  • 1 cup Pinot Noir
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

Combine all ingredients in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Place steak in a zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour marinade over it. Seal bag or cover dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Marinade 4: Chardonnay and Butter

For a richer, more decadent flavor profile, try pairing Chardonnay with butter and herbs. The creamy butter and oaky notes of the Chardonnay create a luxurious and indulgent marinade.

  • 1 cup Chardonnay
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tablespoons freshly chopped parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Combine all ingredients in a blender or food processor and blend until smooth. Place steak in a zip-top plastic bag or a shallow dish, and pour marinade over it. Seal bag or cover dish with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

When cooking the steak, make sure to cook it to your desired level of doneness. For a perfectly cooked steak, aim for an internal temperature of 130°F to 135°F for medium-rare, 140°F to 145°F for medium, and 150°F to 155°F for medium-well.

With these four wine-infused marinades, you’ll be well on your way to creating the perfect pairing for your next steak dinner. Whether you’re in the mood for something classic and savory or light and citrusy, there’s a marinade on this list that’s sure to please. So go ahead, get creative, and elevate your steak game with these amazing wine-infused marinades!
