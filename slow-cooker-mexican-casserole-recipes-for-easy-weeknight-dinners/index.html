Slow Cooker Mexican Casserole Recipes for Easy Weeknight Dinners


Are you tired of spending hours in the kitchen after a long day at work? Do you crave the rich and vibrant flavors of Mexican cuisine, but don’t have the time to prepare a traditional meal? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious and easy slow cooker Mexican casserole recipes that will satisfy your taste buds and save you precious time in the kitchen.

Why Slow Cooker Mexican Casseroles?

Slow cookers, also known as crock-pots, are a game-changer when it comes to weeknight dinners. They allow you to prepare a flavorful and satisfying meal with minimal effort. By using a slow cooker, you can set it and forget it, allowing the flavors of the ingredients to meld together while you go about your day. Plus, slow cookers are incredibly versatile and can be used to make a variety of dishes, including these mouthwatering Mexican casseroles.

Recipe 1: Slow Cooker Chicken Enchilada Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 ounces) corn, drained
  • 1 can (10 ounces) enchilada sauce
  • 1 can (4 ounces) diced green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican cheese blend
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the chicken breasts in the slow cooker and season with salt, pepper, chili powder, cumin, and garlic powder.
  2. Add the black beans, corn, enchilada sauce, and diced green chilies to the slow cooker.
  3. Stir everything together to ensure the chicken is coated in the sauce and spices.
  4. Cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  5. Remove the chicken from the slow cooker and shred it using two forks.
  6. Return the shredded chicken to the slow cooker and stir to combine with the other ingredients.
  7. Sprinkle the shredded cheese on top of the mixture and cover the slow cooker with the lid.
  8. Cook on low for an additional 30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro, if desired, and serve hot.

Recipe 2: Slow Cooker Beef and Bean Burrito Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (10 ounces) diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1 can (8 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 6 large flour tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • Sour cream, for serving (optional)
  • Sliced green onions, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until the beef is browned and cooked through. Drain any excess grease.
  2. Transfer the cooked beef mixture to the slow cooker.
  3. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes with green chilies, tomato sauce, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  4. Cover the slow cooker with the lid and cook on low for 6-8 hours or on high for 3-4 hours, until the flavors have melded together.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  6. Spray a 9x13-inch baking dish with non-stick cooking spray.
  7. Place 3 large flour tortillas in the bottom of the baking dish, overlapping if necessary.
  8. Spoon half of the beef and bean mixture over the tortillas, spreading it evenly.
  9. Sprinkle half of the shredded cheddar cheese over the beef and bean mixture.
  10. Repeat the layers with the remaining tortillas, beef and bean mixture, and shredded cheese.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  12. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.
  13. Serve hot, with sour cream and sliced green onions as optional toppings.

Final Thoughts

These slow cooker Mexican casserole recipes are perfect for easy weeknight dinners. They are packed with bold flavors and can be customized to suit your preferences by adding or substituting ingredients. Whether you choose the chicken enchilada casserole or the beef and bean burrito casserole, you’ll be treated to a delicious and satisfying meal without the hassle of traditional cooking methods. So dust off your slow cooker and give these recipes a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
