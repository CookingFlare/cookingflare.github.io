Healthy and Flavorful Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Buddha Bowl


When it comes to nourishing and satisfying meals, the Buddha bowl has become a go-to option for many health-conscious individuals. Packed with nutrient-dense ingredients and bursting with flavor, the Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Buddha Bowl is a delightful combination of wholesome goodness on a single plate. This recipe is not only a feast for the taste buds but also a powerhouse of nutrition, making it an ideal choice for anyone looking to embrace a healthier lifestyle.

Ingredients

For the Buddha Bowl:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

For the Lemon Tahini Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating the oven to 425°F (220°C) and lining a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Cook the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, combine the quinoa and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid is absorbed.

  3. Roast the Vegetables: On the prepared baking sheet, arrange the sweet potato, red bell pepper, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, and chickpeas. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat evenly, then spread the vegetables in a single layer. Roast in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and lightly browned.

  4. Prepare the Lemon Tahini Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, water, minced garlic, and salt until smooth and creamy. Add more water if needed to achieve the desired consistency.

  5. Assemble the Buddha Bowl: Divide the cooked quinoa among serving bowls. Top with the roasted vegetables and chickpeas. Drizzle with the lemon tahini dressing and garnish with fresh parsley.

Health Benefits

Quinoa

Quinoa is a complete protein, meaning it contains all nine essential amino acids, making it an excellent source of plant-based protein. It is also high in fiber, iron, magnesium, and various beneficial antioxidants.

Roasted Vegetables

Roasting vegetables brings out their natural sweetness and enhances their flavors. They are rich in vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber, contributing to overall health and well-being.

Lemon Tahini Dressing

Tahini, made from ground sesame seeds, provides healthy fats and essential minerals such as phosphorus, lecithin, magnesium, potassium, and iron. Combined with the tangy freshness of lemon juice, it adds a delightful burst of flavor to the Buddha bowl.

Conclusion

The Healthy and Flavorful Quinoa and Roasted Vegetable Buddha Bowl is not only a treat for the senses but also a testament to the joys of nourishing the body with wholesome ingredients. This recipe offers a perfect balance of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, making it a delicious and fulfilling meal option for individuals seeking to embrace a healthier lifestyle. Whether enjoyed as a quick weeknight dinner or packed for a wholesome lunch, this Buddha bowl is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the art of mindful cooking, and savor every mouthful of this delightful and nutritious dish.
