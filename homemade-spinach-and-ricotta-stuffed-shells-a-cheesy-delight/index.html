Homemade Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells: A Cheesy Delight


There’s something inherently comforting about a dish that’s both indulgent and nutritious. Homemade Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells fit this bill perfectly. This Italian classic is a family favorite that brings together the creamy richness of ricotta cheese, the earthy goodness of spinach, and the satisfying bite of pasta shells. It’s a hearty meal that works just as well for a cozy family dinner as it does for entertaining guests.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the step-by-step instructions, let’s gather all the necessary ingredients:

For the Filling:

  • 1 box of jumbo pasta shells
  • 2 cups fresh spinach (or 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sauce:

  • 2 cups marinara sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish (optional)

Additional:

  • Olive oil for greasing the baking dish

Instructions

Preparing the Pasta Shells

  1. Cook the Pasta: Begin by cooking the jumbo pasta shells according to the package instructions. It’s essential to cook them al dente since they will continue to cook in the oven later. Once cooked, drain the shells and rinse them under cold water to prevent sticking.

Making the Filling

  1. Prepare the Spinach: If using fresh spinach, blanch it in boiling water for about 2 minutes until wilted. Drain and chop the spinach finely. If using frozen spinach, ensure it is thoroughly thawed and excess water is squeezed out.

  2. Combine the Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped spinach, ricotta cheese, shredded mozzarella, grated Parmesan, beaten egg, and minced garlic. Season with nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are fully incorporated.

Assembling the Dish

  1. Fill the Shells: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a baking dish with olive oil. Using a spoon, carefully fill each cooked pasta shell with the ricotta and spinach mixture. Place the stuffed shells in the baking dish in a single layer.

  2. Add the Sauce: Pour the marinara sauce evenly over the stuffed shells. Ensure that each shell is generously covered with sauce to prevent them from drying out during baking.

  3. Top with Cheese: Sprinkle the remaining grated Parmesan cheese over the top of the sauce.

Baking

  1. Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. After 25 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.

  2. Garnish and Serve: Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and let it sit for a few minutes to set. Garnish with fresh basil leaves if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

Tips for Perfect Stuffed Shells

  • Choosing the Right Cheese: Using high-quality ricotta and mozzarella cheese can make a significant difference in flavor and texture. Freshly grated Parmesan adds a depth of flavor that pre-grated versions often lack.
  • Homemade Marinara: If you have the time, making your own marinara sauce can elevate this dish. A simple sauce made with tomatoes, garlic, onions, and herbs can provide a fresh and vibrant flavor.
  • Freezing Option: These stuffed shells freeze exceptionally well. Prepare them up to the point of baking, then cover tightly and freeze. When ready to bake, thaw overnight in the refrigerator and bake as directed.

Nutritional Information

This dish offers a balanced combination of carbohydrates, protein, and fats, making it a well-rounded meal. The spinach provides a good dose of vitamins and minerals, while the cheeses contribute calcium and protein.

Variations to Try

  • Meat Lovers: For a heartier version, add cooked ground beef or Italian sausage to the filling.
  • Vegetable Medley: Incorporate other vegetables like sautéed mushrooms, bell peppers, or zucchini into the filling for added nutrition and flavor.
  • Herb Infusion: Experiment with different herbs such as oregano, thyme, or rosemary to infuse the dish with unique flavors.

Conclusion

Homemade Spinach and Ricotta Stuffed Shells are a delightful way to enjoy a hearty, cheesy meal that’s packed with flavor and nutrients. This dish is perfect for feeding a crowd or preparing ahead of time for busy weeknights. With its rich, creamy filling and savory marinara sauce, it’s sure to become a favorite at your dinner table. So, gather your ingredients, follow these simple steps, and treat yourself and your loved ones to a delicious homemade Italian meal. Buon appetito!
