Mindful Spice: A Holistic Approach to Healthy and Flavorful Cooking


In recent years, the mindful and conscious approach towards eating has garnered a considerable amount of attention from health enthusiasts, foodies, and busy professionals alike. With an emphasis on integrating nutrition, flavor, and wise consumption, the concept of mindful cooking has cultivated an incredibly strong following amongst people who wish to balance taste and wholesomeness in their diet. This is where the mindfully infused concept of spice steps in to simplify and enrich our culinary experiences.

Mindful Spicing: The Art of Harmonious Cooking

Mindful spicing is not merely a method of enhancing the flavor of one’s dishes but also promoting an environmentally conscious kitchen, reducing food waste, and ensuring the holistic wellbeing of the person consuming the food. This approach sheds light on the deep and natural connection between texture, flavor profiles, and the ingredients we choose to work with. The thought behind cooking becomes an act of reverence towards the food and the nature that provides it.

Ingredients hold their individual tales, and with the skillful combination of spices, we can honor and interlace these stories, creating a rich tapestry of flavors that not only tantalize our taste buds but addresses the body, mind, and soul in unison. Let us embark on a journey unraveling spices through a little-known culinary exploration: the art of mindful spice.

London Broil with Spiced Roasted Masala Vegetables

A splendid example of mindful spicing at its best is the “London Broil with Spiced Roasted Masala Vegetables.” This hearty savory preparation allows us to harness the power of spices in an exquisite and balanced manner. Begin by gathering your ingredients: London broil strip, salt, black pepper, olive oil, minced garlic cloves, lemon juice, lime juice, ground turmeric, ground cumin, ground coriander, smoked paprika, sustainably sourced fish sauce, coconut aminos or soy sauce, honey, fresh cilantro, a mix of seasonal vegetables, and a pinch of red pepper flakes for heat.

  1. Season the London broil with salt and black pepper on both sides, followed by rubbing in the minced garlic, lemon juice, lime juice, and olive oil. Allow the meat to marinate for a minimum of an hour, but ideally overnight.
  2. While your meat marinates, preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large bowl, combine your mixed vegetables, olive oil, turmeric, cumin, coriander, smoked paprika, salt, and red pepper flakes, and gently toss to evenly coat each piece.
  3. Spread the masala vegetables on a parchment-lined sheet tray, ensuring they are evenly spaced and blanch separately.
  4. Bake the vegetables in the oven for about 20 to 25 minutes or until tender and slightly caramelized, while giving them a gentle toss halfway through to ensure even cooking.
  5. Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over high heat and sear the marinated London broil on both sides until well browned and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  6. While the meat rests, pour the leftover marinade into the skillet and deglaze the pan, scraping the brown bits from the bottom. Add coconut aminos (or soy sauce), fish sauce, honey, and a pinch of salt to the skillet and stir to create a savory and slightly sweet sauce.
  7. Serve the sliced London broil with warm spiced roasted masala vegetables and garnish with fresh cilantro.

Mindful spicing allows us to cultivate an authentic connection with our food, the culture it represents, and the body and mind nourishment it provides. An ingredients’ origin and story as well as flavor pairing possibilities enhances our culinary experience, allowing progress towards a more mindful and delightful cooking experience. Let the harmony of spiced flavors transpire in all your dishes and enjoy the wholesome and flavorful cuisine your heart and body desires!
