Fall-Inspired Pasta Dishes: 7 Recipes to Try


As the crisp fall air settles in, there’s nothing better than a hearty bowl of pasta to warm you up. Fall is the perfect season to indulge in rich, comforting flavors, from roasted vegetables to creamy sauces and savory herbs. Whether you’re cooking for a cozy family dinner or hosting a gathering with friends, these seven fall-inspired pasta dishes will add seasonal flair to your table.

Get ready to explore the magic of autumn ingredients, including squash, pumpkin, mushrooms, and sage, combined with pasta in ways that celebrate the season’s best flavors. Let’s dive into these delicious recipes that are as satisfying as they are flavorful.

1. Butternut Squash and Sage Pasta

Butternut squash is the star of this fall-inspired dish, offering a natural sweetness that pairs perfectly with the earthy flavor of fresh sage. When combined with pasta, it creates a comforting and rich meal that embodies the essence of autumn.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup fresh sage leaves
  • 12 oz. pasta (such as fettuccine or pappardelle)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, toss the diced butternut squash with olive oil, salt, pepper, thyme, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender and lightly caramelized.
  2. In a large pot of salted boiling water, cook your pasta according to the package instructions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  3. While the pasta is cooking, melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add fresh sage leaves and sauté for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant and crisp.
  4. Once the squash is roasted, add it to the skillet with the butter and sage. Mash the squash lightly with a fork, leaving some chunks for texture. Add the reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Toss the cooked pasta in the sauce, making sure to coat it evenly. Add grated Parmesan cheese and mix until the cheese is melted.
  6. Serve the pasta with toasted pine nuts on top for added crunch and flavor.

This dish is a perfect representation of fall, combining the sweetness of roasted squash with the warmth of cinnamon, nutmeg, and sage. The creamy sauce brings everything together in a satisfying way.

2. Mushroom and Spinach Lasagna

Mushrooms are another key ingredient that shines in fall dishes. This mushroom and spinach lasagna is loaded with earthy, umami flavors and a creamy ricotta filling. It’s the perfect dish for a special dinner or even meal prep for the week ahead.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. mixed mushrooms (shiitake, cremini, or button), thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 10 oz. fresh spinach, wilted and chopped
  • 15 oz. ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 9 lasagna noodles, cooked
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and begin to brown, about 8 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Pour in the white wine and cook until the liquid reduces by half, about 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, combine ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, Parmesan, egg, and the sautéed spinach. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom. Lay three lasagna noodles on top, followed by a layer of the ricotta-spinach mixture, a layer of the sautéed mushrooms, and more marinara sauce.
  5. Repeat the layers until the baking dish is full, finishing with a layer of marinara sauce and mozzarella cheese on top.
  6. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  7. Let the lasagna rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Garnish with fresh parsley.

This hearty lasagna combines the rich flavors of mushrooms, spinach, and a cheesy filling, creating a comforting and indulgent fall meal.

3. Pumpkin Alfredo Pasta

Pumpkin and pasta are a match made in heaven, especially when paired with a creamy Alfredo sauce. This dish is the perfect way to celebrate fall’s pumpkin season while enjoying a rich and velvety pasta dish.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz. fettuccine or linguine
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the pumpkin puree and heavy cream. Season with cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is heated through and slightly thickened.
  4. Add the Parmesan cheese and stir until it melts into the sauce, creating a creamy texture.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the sauce, tossing to coat. If the sauce is too thick, add some reserved pasta water until you reach your desired consistency.
  6. Serve the pasta in bowls, garnished with fresh parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

This pumpkin Alfredo is the ultimate fall comfort food, combining the richness of cream with the seasonal flavor of pumpkin. The hint of cinnamon and nutmeg elevates this dish to a new level of autumn goodness.

4. Roasted Root Vegetable Pasta

Fall is the season for root vegetables like carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes. Roasting them brings out their natural sweetness, which pairs beautifully with a simple pasta dish. This roasted root vegetable pasta is easy to make and bursting with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 parsnip, peeled and chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 oz. pasta (such as rigatoni or penne)
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary, chopped
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the diced sweet potato, carrots, and parsnip with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread the vegetables evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  2. While the vegetables are roasting, cook the pasta according to package instructions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  3. Once the vegetables are roasted, toss them in a large bowl with the cooked pasta. Add Parmesan cheese and fresh herbs, mixing everything together. If the mixture is too dry, add some reserved pasta water to help create a sauce.
  4. Serve with an extra drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.

This simple yet flavorful dish highlights the sweetness of roasted root vegetables and the hearty texture of pasta. It’s a perfect way to enjoy fall produce in a satisfying meal.

5. Apple and Bacon Carbonara

For a fun twist on traditional carbonara, try adding fresh apples and crispy bacon. The sweetness of apples balances the savory saltiness of bacon, making this a unique and delicious fall pasta dish.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz. spaghetti or bucatini
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 large apple, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the diced apple and cook for 3-4 minutes, until the apples are softened and slightly caramelized.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan cheese, and heavy cream. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet with the apples and toss to combine. Remove from heat and quickly pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring constantly to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Add the crispy bacon and toss again. If the sauce is too thick, add some reserved pasta water to reach the desired consistency.
  7. Serve with fresh parsley on top for a burst of color and flavor.

The combination of apples and bacon in this carbonara adds a seasonal twist to a classic dish. The creamy sauce and crispy bacon are the perfect partners for the tender pasta.

6. Brussels Sprouts and Bacon Pasta

Brussels sprouts are a quintessential fall vegetable, and when paired with crispy bacon and pasta, they make for a savory and satisfying dish that is perfect for chilly evenings.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz. pasta (such as farfalle or fusilli)
  • 1 lb. Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the halved Brussels sprouts on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until crispy and browned on the edges.
  2. In a skillet, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and set aside.
  3. Add the minced garlic to the bacon drippings in the skillet and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet, along with the roasted Brussels sprouts, bacon, and chicken broth. Toss to combine and cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the pasta to soak up the flavors.
  5. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, then season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Garnish with fresh thyme and serve immediately.

This savory dish combines the rich flavor of Brussels sprouts with the crispness of bacon, creating a comforting fall pasta that will satisfy any craving.

7. Chestnut and Ricotta Stuffed Pasta

Chestnuts are a delightful fall ingredient that brings a subtle sweetness and earthy flavor to pasta. When combined with ricotta cheese, they create a deliciously creamy filling for stuffed pasta.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz. large pasta shells (such as manicotti or jumbo shells)
  • 1 cup roasted chestnuts, chopped
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup spinach, wilted and chopped
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cook the pasta shells according to the package instructions, then drain and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. In a bowl, combine the roasted chestnuts, ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, egg, and spinach. Mix until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Stuff each pasta shell with the chestnut-ricotta mixture and place them in a baking dish. Pour marinara sauce over the stuffed shells, ensuring they are fully covered.
  4. Cover with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the sauce is bubbling.
  5. Garnish with fresh basil and serve.

This rich and satisfying dish brings the flavor of fall directly to your plate, with the combination of sweet chestnuts and creamy ricotta filling in tender pasta shells.

Fall-inspired pasta dishes offer a delightful way to celebrate the season’s harvest. From creamy pumpkin sauces to roasted root vegetables and crispy bacon, these recipes showcase the warmth and richness that autumn ingredients bring to the table. Whether you’re looking for a hearty dinner or something to impress guests, these seven pasta dishes will make your fall meals unforgettable. Enjoy the flavors of the season, and don’t forget to pair your favorite dish with a warm, comforting drink!
