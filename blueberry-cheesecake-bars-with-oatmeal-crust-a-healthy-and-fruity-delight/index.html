Blueberry Cheesecake Bars with Oatmeal Crust: A Healthy and Fruity Delight


If you’re on the lookout for a dessert that balances indulgence with health, look no further than these Blueberry Cheesecake Bars with Oatmeal Crust. This recipe is not only easy to follow but also uses wholesome ingredients that make it a guilt-free treat. Packed with the goodness of fresh blueberries and a creamy cheesecake filling, these bars are perfect for any occasion—from an afternoon snack to a delightful dessert at your next gathering. Let’s dive into the details of how to make this deliciously healthy dessert!

Ingredients

For the Oatmeal Crust:

  • 1 and 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar (or coconut sugar for a healthier alternative)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted (or coconut oil for a dairy-free option)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup

For the Cheesecake Filling:

  • 16 ounces cream cheese, softened (or a dairy-free alternative)
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt (or a dairy-free yogurt)
  • 1/2 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 and 1/2 cups fresh blueberries (you can also use frozen, but thaw and drain them first)

For Garnish (optional):

  • Additional fresh blueberries
  • A sprinkle of powdered sugar
  • A dollop of whipped cream (or coconut whipped cream)

Equipment Needed

  • 9x9 inch baking pan
  • Mixing bowls
  • Electric mixer (or a whisk)
  • Rubber spatula
  • Parchment paper
  • Oven

Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This allows for an even baking temperature, ensuring your cheesecake bars come out perfectly set and delicious.

Step 2: Prepare the Oatmeal Crust

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, whole wheat flour, brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Stir until well mixed.
  2. Add Wet Ingredients: Pour in the melted butter and honey (or maple syrup). Mix until the dry ingredients are evenly coated and the mixture resembles a crumbly dough.
  3. Press into the Pan: Line your baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal. Firmly press the oatmeal mixture into the bottom of the pan to form an even crust. Use a flat-bottomed glass or measuring cup to help pack it tightly.
  4. Bake the Crust: Place the crust in your preheated oven and bake for about 10-12 minutes, or until it’s lightly golden. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly while you prepare the cheesecake filling.

Step 3: Make the Cheesecake Filling

  1. Beat the Cream Cheese: In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until it’s smooth and creamy. This step is crucial to avoid lumps in your filling.
  2. Add Yogurt and Sweetener: Mix in the Greek yogurt and honey (or maple syrup) until well combined.
  3. Incorporate Eggs and Flavorings: Add the eggs, vanilla extract, and lemon juice. Beat together until the mixture is smooth and creamy, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  4. Fold in Blueberries: Gently fold in the fresh blueberries, taking care not to crush them.

Step 4: Assemble and Bake

  1. Pour Filling over Crust: Pour the cheesecake filling over the pre-baked oatmeal crust, spreading it evenly with a spatula.
  2. Bake the Bars: Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes. The edges should be set while the center may still have a slight jiggle—this is normal and will firm up as the bars cool.
  3. Cool and Refrigerate: Once baked, remove the bars from the oven and let them cool at room temperature for about an hour. Then, refrigerate for at least 3-4 hours, or overnight for the best texture.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once the bars are chilled and set, lift them out of the pan using the parchment paper. Cut into squares and garnish with additional blueberries, a light dusting of powdered sugar, or a dollop of whipped cream if desired. These bars are perfect for sharing, but you might just want to keep them all to yourself!

Health Benefits

These Blueberry Cheesecake Bars are not just a delicious treat; they also boast several health benefits:

  • Blueberries: Rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals, blueberries contribute to overall health, supporting heart health and improving brain function.
  • Oats: The oatmeal crust provides a great source of dietary fiber, which aids digestion and helps you feel full longer.
  • Greek Yogurt: This ingredient adds protein and probiotics, promoting gut health and helping build muscle.

Storage Tips

These cheesecake bars can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. They also freeze well! Just wrap individual bars in plastic wrap and place them in a freezer-safe bag for easy grab-and-go snacks.

Final Thoughts

Indulging in a dessert doesn’t have to mean sacrificing your health goals. These Blueberry Cheesecake Bars with Oatmeal Crust are a delicious way to enjoy a sweet treat while still nourishing your body. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself, this recipe is sure to impress.

So, what are you waiting for? Grab your ingredients and start baking these delightful bars today. Your taste buds—and your body—will thank you!
