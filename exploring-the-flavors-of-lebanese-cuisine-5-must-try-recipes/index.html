Exploring the Flavors of Lebanese Cuisine: 5 Must-Try Recipes


Lebanese cuisine represents a harmonious blend of Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and ancient Levantine flavors. Rooted deeply in tradition, it offers an array of aromatic spices, fresh herbs, and wholesome ingredients, resulting in meals that are both delicious and nutritious. Below are five must-try Lebanese recipes that highlight the vibrant diversity of this culinary tradition. From light appetizers to heartier mains, this guide will immerse you in Lebanon’s rich gastronomic culture.

1. Tabbouleh (Traditional Lebanese Parsley Salad)

Tabbouleh is the epitome of freshness, showcasing a medley of finely chopped parsley, mint, tomatoes, and bulgur wheat, dressed simply with lemon juice and olive oil. This refreshing salad is a staple in Lebanese mezze, perfect as an appetizer or side dish. Here’s how to prepare it:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1/4 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 2 scallions, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the bulgur wheat in cold water and soak briefly until softened. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the parsley, mint, tomatoes, scallions, and hydrated bulgur wheat.
  3. Add the lemon juice, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Toss well to combine.
  4. Let the flavors meld for about 15 minutes before serving.

Pro Tip:

For a modern twist, substitute bulgur with quinoa for a gluten-free option. Serve with crisp romaine lettuce leaves for a great textural contrast.

2. Kibbeh (Lebanese Stuffed Meat Croquettes)

Kibbeh is often referred to as Lebanon’s national dish. These spiced meat croquettes are made from a combination of minced meat (traditionally lamb or beef), bulgur, and fragrant spices. Kibbeh can be served raw, baked, fried, or even simmered in a yogurt sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fine bulgur wheat, soaked and drained
  • 500g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely grated
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste

For the Stuffing:

  • 250g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:

  1. For the outer shell, combine the bulgur, meat, grated onion, spices, and salt in a bowl. Mix thoroughly until the mixture forms a smooth paste-like texture. Set aside.
  2. To make the stuffing, heat olive oil in a pan, sauté onions until translucent, then add the ground meat, spices, and pine nuts. Cook until browned and fragrant. Cool before use.
  3. Wet your hands with cold water and form the outer shell mixture into a ball. Hollow out the center and stuff it with the meat mixture. Close, shaping it into an oval.

Cooking Options:

  • Fry: Deep-fry the kibbeh until golden-brown.
  • Bake: Arrange on a greased baking dish, brush with olive oil, and bake at 375°F (190°C) until cooked through.

3. Fattoush (Crispy Bread Salad with Vegetables)

Fattoush is a bright, tangy salad that celebrates the use of colorful vegetables and crispy pita bread. The star of this dish is sumac, a tangy spice that adds depth to the zesty dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pita breads, cut into small pieces and toasted or fried
  • 2 cups romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 radishes, thinly sliced
  • 3 tablespoons fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh mint, finely chopped

For the Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon sumac
  • 1 tablespoon pomegranate molasses
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss the vegetables, parsley, and mint in a large salad bowl.
  2. Whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, sumac, pomegranate molasses, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Mix the toasted pita with the salad and drizzle with the dressing. Toss to combine and serve immediately.

4. Chicken Tawook (Lebanese Grilled Chicken Skewers)

Chicken Tawook, marinated in a spiced yogurt mixture, is a popular street food with incredible flavor. It’s perfect for grilling and pairs beautifully with garlic sauce and warm pita bread.

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and spices. Add chicken and coat evenly. Cover and let marinate for at least 2 hours (overnight is best).
  2. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers.
  3. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat and cook the skewers for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until cooked through and lightly charred.

Serving Suggestion:

Serve the skewers with garlic sauce (Toum), pickles, grilled vegetables, or wrapped in pita bread for a traditional experience.

5. Baklava (Lebanese Honey and Nut Pastries)

No exploration of Lebanese cuisine is complete without indulging in baklava, a rich, syrup-soaked dessert made with layers of phyllo pastry and chopped nuts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package phyllo dough, thawed
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (pistachios, almonds, walnuts), finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1 teaspoon rose water

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush a baking dish with melted butter.
  2. Layer half the phyllo sheets in the dish, brushing each sheet with butter as you go.
  3. Mix the nuts, sugar, and cinnamon, then spread evenly over the layered phyllo. Top with the remaining sheets, again brushing each with butter.
  4. Cut the baklava into small diamond or square shapes. Bake for 35-40 minutes, until golden-brown.
  5. Meanwhile, prepare the syrup by simmering sugar, water, honey, and rose water until slightly thickened. Pour over the hot baklava and let cool before serving.

Lebanese cuisine is characterized by its ability to balance hearty ingredients with bright, fresh flavors. Whether you’re savoring a tangy Fattoush, marveling at the intricacies of Kibbeh, or enjoying the sweetness of Baklava, each dish tells a story of Lebanon’s rich cultural heritage. Dive into these recipes to bring a taste of Lebanon into your own kitchen, and discover why this cuisine is beloved across the globe.
