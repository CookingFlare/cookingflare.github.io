How to Make Chocolate Hazelnut Ice Cream


If you adore the rich decadence of chocolate paired with the nutty warmth of hazelnuts, then making your own chocolate hazelnut ice cream at home is an absolute must. This luscious dessert combines creamy textures, deep cocoa flavors, and the subtle crunch and aroma of hazelnuts, bringing a gourmet experience right into your kitchen. Whether you’re looking to impress guests or treat yourself, this step-by-step guide will walk you through crafting an indulgent chocolate hazelnut ice cream that rivals any artisanal scoop.

Why Make Chocolate Hazelnut Ice Cream at Home?

Store-bought chocolate and hazelnut ice creams are tasty enough, but homemade versions offer so much more:

  • Freshness and quality control: You can choose premium chocolate and freshly roasted hazelnuts, avoiding artificial additives.
  • Adjust sweetness: Tailor the sweetness to your preference, whether you want it mild or decadently rich.
  • Customizable texture: From silky smooth to a little crunchy with hazelnut bits, you decide.
  • Fun cooking experience: There’s something wonderfully satisfying about making ice cream from scratch.

With that said, let’s dive into the magic of combining these flavors and turning them into a creamy frozen delight.

Ingredients for Chocolate Hazelnut Ice Cream

Here’s a detailed list of what you’ll need. Feel free to pick high-quality ingredients, as they make a real difference in the final taste.

  • 2 cups (480 ml) whole milk
  • 1 cup (240 ml) heavy cream
  • ¾ cup (150 g) granulated sugar
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup (60 g) unsweetened cocoa powder (preferably Dutch-processed for smoother flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • ½ cup chocolate hazelnut spread (such as Nutella) – this imparts that signature flavor with ease
  • 1 cup hazelnuts (toasted and chopped)
  • Pinch of salt

Essential Tools You’ll Need

Before starting, gather these kitchen tools for a smoother process:

  • Heavy-bottomed saucepan
  • Mixing bowls
  • Whisk
  • Fine mesh sieve
  • Ice cream maker (or alternative freezing techniques)
  • Spatula
  • Baking sheet and parchment paper for roasting hazelnuts

Step 1: Toast and Prepare the Hazelnuts

The first step is to release the deep, earthy aroma of hazelnuts by toasting them:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Spread hazelnuts on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  3. Roast for 10–12 minutes or until hazelnuts are fragrant and skins start to crack.
  4. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly.
  5. To remove the skins easily, place toasted nuts in a clean kitchen towel and rub vigorously. Removing the skins reduces bitterness.
  6. Roughly chop the hazelnuts and set aside.

Step 2: Prepare the Chocolate Hazelnut Custard Base

Ice cream richness comes from a smooth custard base. Here’s how to make a chocolate-infused custard:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the whole milk, heavy cream, and half the sugar. Stir over medium heat until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks with the remaining sugar until the mixture thickens and lightens slightly in color.
  3. Gradually pour about ½ cup of the hot milk mixture into the yolks while whisking continuously. This tempers the eggs and prevents scrambling.
  4. Slowly pour the warmed yolk mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining milk mixture.
  5. Cook over low to medium heat, stirring constantly with a spatula or wooden spoon, until the custard thickens enough to coat the back of the spoon (around 175°F or 80°C). Avoid boiling.
  6. Remove from heat and sift in the cocoa powder, whisking until smooth.
  7. Stir in the chocolate hazelnut spread and vanilla extract until fully melted and combined.
  8. Add a pinch of salt to enhance flavor depth.
  9. Pour the custard through a fine mesh sieve to catch any lumps or cooked egg bits.
  10. Let custard cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight. This chilling step improves texture and flavor melding.

Step 3: Churn the Ice Cream

The chilled custard is ready to transform into creamy ice cream:

  1. Pour the custard into your ice cream maker.
  2. Churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions, usually taking 20-30 minutes.
  3. When the ice cream reaches a soft serve consistency, add the toasted chopped hazelnuts and mix gently yet thoroughly.
  4. Transfer the ice cream to a freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 3 hours for firmer texture.

Step 4: Serving and Storing

Now the fun part — enjoying your creation!

  • Scoop the chocolate hazelnut ice cream into bowls or cones.
  • For extra flair: drizzle with melted chocolate, sprinkle crushed hazelnuts, or add a few fresh berries.
  • Store leftover ice cream tightly sealed in the freezer. It’s best consumed within 2 weeks for optimal flavor and texture.

Tips for Perfecting Your Chocolate Hazelnut Ice Cream

  • Use fresh eggs: Fresh egg yolks ensure a smooth, creamy texture.
  • Dutch-processed cocoa powder: This cocoa results in a more mellow and less acidic flavor, perfect for ice cream.
  • Balance the sweetness: Adjust sugar or hazelnut spread amounts based on preference. Sometimes Nutella can be quite sweet, so start with less and add more if desired.
  • Hazelnut texture: For chunkier bites, reserve some nuts to sprinkle on top at serving time.
  • No ice cream maker? Pour the custard into a shallow freezer-safe pan, freeze for 45 minutes, then whisk vigorously to break up ice crystals. Repeat every 30 minutes until smooth and frozen.

Variations to Consider

Once comfortable with the classic recipe, explore fun twists to surprise your palate:

  • Add espresso powder: A teaspoon boosts chocolate aroma and adds a coffee kick.
  • Use roasted white chocolate chips: For bursts of melty sweetness.
  • Swirl in hazelnut praline: Create ribbons of crunchiness swirled through the ice cream.
  • Make it vegan: Substitute coconut milk and use aquafaba or cornstarch to thicken.

Final Thoughts

Making your own chocolate hazelnut ice cream isn’t just about creating a dessert; it’s about the joy of combining luscious flavors and experimenting with texture and richness. The marriage of creamy chocolate custard with the aromatic crunch of hazelnuts is simply irresistible.

Homemade ice cream always tastes better because of the care and quality you bring into it. Whether you follow this recipe exactly or modify it to suit your taste, expect a delicious reward — velvety, nutty, and satisfyingly chocolaty.

Are you ready to scoop up some of this decadent delight? Grab your ingredients, heat up those hazelnuts, and start churning your way to chocolate hazelnut bliss today!
