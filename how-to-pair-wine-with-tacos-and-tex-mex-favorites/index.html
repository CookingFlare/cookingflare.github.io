How to Pair Wine with Tacos and Tex-Mex Favorites


Tacos and Tex-Mex cuisine are beloved for their vibrant flavors, bold spices, and comforting combinations. From smoky meats and zesty sauces to creamy guacamole and fresh pico de gallo, every bite delivers an explosion of taste. While beer or margaritas might be the go-to beverages for many, wine offers an equally satisfying way to highlight and complement the diverse flavors of Tex-Mex dishes. Whether you’re hosting taco night or savoring enchiladas at your favorite local spot, finding the perfect wine pairing can elevate your dining experience to new heights.

The Basics of Pairing Wine with Tex-Mex Dishes

To successfully pair wine with tacos and other Tex-Mex favorites, it’s essential to understand the key flavor profiles inherent in these dishes. Tex-Mex cuisine often features a mix of spicy, smoky, acidic, and rich flavors. Each wine acts as a complement or contrast, balancing out the dish or amplifying its defining qualities. Keep in mind the following guidelines:

  • Spicy Foods: Wines with lower alcohol content and a touch of sweetness tend to balance out spiciness, while overly acidic or tannic wines can intensify the heat.
  • Smoky Flavors: Smoky meats or grilled vegetables pair well with wines that exhibit a hint of oak or minerality.
  • Acidity: Citrus-based marinades, lime-drenched toppings, and tangy salsa generally work best with wines that have a bright acidity, enhancing these fresh flavors organically.
  • Richness: Creamy cheese, avocado-based sauces, and fatty meats need wines with enough structure to cut through their richness and create balance.

With these principles in mind, let’s dive into pairing specific types of wine with popular Tex-Mex dishes, starting with the taco.

Elevating Taco Night with the Right Wine Pairings

Tacos brimming with bold fillings and vibrant garnishes deserve a wine capable of holding its own. Depending on the protein and flavor profile of your tacos, a range of different wines can shine.

1. Beef Tacos

Hearty beef tacos often feature smoky, savory fillings like barbacoa, carne asada, or ground beef seasoned with traditional spices. To complement these flavors, opt for a red wine with moderate tannins and earthy undertones.

Wine Pairing:

  • Malbec: Smooth and structured, Malbec’s dark berry flavors and subtle smokiness pair well with spiced beef.
  • Tempranillo: This Spanish red wine boasts dried fruit, leather, and a touch of tobacco, perfectly matching the depth of flavors in beef tacos.
  • Zinfandel: With its jammy notes and a hint of pepper, Zinfandel can enhance the smoky spices in this taco.

2. Chicken Tacos

Chicken tacos, whether shredded and spiced, marinated in citrus, or grilled, tend to have a lighter profile compared to beef. For these tacos, wines with bright acidity and floral or fruity notes are ideal.

Wine Pairing:

  • Chardonnay: A lightly oaked Chardonnay adds a touch of creaminess to complement grilled chicken, without overwhelming its delicate flavors.
  • Sauvignon Blanc: Zesty and citrus-driven, Sauvignon Blanc harmonizes with lime-based sauces and salsas often found in chicken tacos.
  • Grenache Rosé: A refreshing rosé made from Grenache offers red berry flavors and crispness, great for chicken tacos topped with fresh, vibrant garnishes.

3. Carnitas Tacos

Carnitas, slow-cooked pork with a luscious mix of fattiness and crispy edges, is a taco staple. The richness of this dish pairs beautifully with wines that have bright acidity and moderate fruitiness to cut through the fat.

Wine Pairing:

  • Pinot Noir: The light body and red berry notes of Pinot Noir bring balance to carnitas, while its slight earthiness plays well with pork.
  • Riesling: A dry or off-dry Riesling with gentle sweetness and acidity works wonders to contrast the pork’s richness and spices.

4. Fish Tacos

Fish tacos, often featuring mild white fish, crispy batter, or blackened seasoning, require wines that don’t overpower their subtle textures and flavors. The right pairing will enhance the tacos’ freshness and any citrus or creamy elements.

Wine Pairing:

  • Albariño: With its maritime minerality and citrus notes, Albariño is a natural partner for fish tacos.
  • Verdejo: This Spanish white wine features vibrant fruit and herbaceous qualities, beautifully complementing fish dishes.
  • Sparkling Wine (Brut or Extra-Dry): The effervescence and crispness of sparkling wine can highlight the textures and flavors of battered or grilled fish tacos.

Wine Pairings for Tex-Mex Favorites Beyond Tacos

Tacos are just the beginning of Tex-Mex’s culinary wonders. From crowd-pleasing enchiladas to comforting bowls of chili, here are thoughtful wine pairings for other dishes you’re likely to encounter.

1. Enchiladas

Cheesy, saucy, and indulgent, enchiladas come with a variety of fillings and sauces like green tomatillo, creamy poblano, or bold red chili. The sauce plays a leading role in determining the best wine.

Wine Pairing:

  • Merlot: A soft Merlot complements spicy red chili sauce through its lush red berry flavors and velvety texture.
  • Rosé of Syrah: With light tannins and ripe fruit flavors, this rosé is versatile enough for cheesy green or red sauce-covered enchiladas.
  • Grüner Veltliner: For enchiladas with green or tangy sauces, this Austrian white wine delivers refreshing acidity and subtle spice.

2. Quesadillas

Cheese-stuffed quesadillas are comforting, rich, and versatile, often served with a side of guacamole or salsa. Wines with crisp acidity or fruit-forward notes are excellent companions for this dish.

Wine Pairing:

  • Prosecco: Bubbly and light, Prosecco’s effervescence cuts through melted cheese and enhances fresh condiments.
  • Chenin Blanc: A versatile wine, Chenin Blanc balances sweetness and acidity to work well with cheese and accompaniments.
  • Viognier: Lush and aromatic, Viognier pairs best with quesadillas featuring sweet or caramelized elements like sautéed onions or peppers.

3. Chili Con Carne

A warm and comforting bowl of chili, loaded with beans, meat, and spices, deserves a wine that packs a punch without clashing with the heat and richness.

Wine Pairing:

  • Syrah/Shiraz: This bold wine’s spicy, smoky flavors harmonize with chili and enhance its complex profile.
  • Cabernet Franc: A lighter-bodied red with herbaceous notes, Cabernet Franc complements the earthy beans and spices.

4. Tex-Mex Fajitas

With sizzling strips of meat or veggies served fresh off the skillet, fajitas invite aromatic wines that can hold their own against the dish’s bold seasonings.

Wine Pairing:

  • Carmenère: Ideal for smoky beef or lamb fajitas, this Chilean wine offers red fruit and bell pepper notes.
  • Chenin Blanc: For grilled chicken or veggie fajitas, Chenin Blanc’s crisp acidity makes for a vibrant pairing.

Tips for a Winning Tex-Mex and Wine Night

To make your Tex-Mex and wine experience unforgettable:

  • Adjust Serving Temperature: Serve red wines slightly chilled (around 55°F) to soften their tannins and avoid heightening spiciness. White wines and rosé should be served cold but not icy to preserve their nuanced flavors.
  • Consider Sweetness: An off-dry white or rosé can tame fiery spices while remaining food-friendly.
  • Experiment Boldly: Tex-Mex is all about creativity, so don’t hesitate to explore unique regional wines like Mexican Nebbiolo or Baja California whites.

Breaking away from traditional beverage choices and embracing wine pairings for Tex-Mex cuisine opens new dimensions of flavor and enjoyment. Whether you’re a wine enthusiast or a taco aficionado, the perfect pairing awaits to transform your meal into a memorable culinary treat.
