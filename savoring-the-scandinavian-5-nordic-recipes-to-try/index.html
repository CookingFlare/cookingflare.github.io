Savoring the Scandinavian: 5 Nordic Recipes to Try


The Scandinavian region, comprising of Denmark, Norway, and Sweden, is renowned for its bold flavors, rich traditions, and hearty cuisine. Home to some of the world's most celebrated chefs and food writers, the region's culinary scene is marked by simplicity, regionality, and abundance. As we delve into the world of Nordic food, we find ourselves indulging in the umami of smoked meat, the sweetness of foraged berries, and the warmth of dark, crusty bread. In this blog post, we'll explore five authentic Scandinavian recipes, each highlighting the unique flavors and techniques of this captivating culinary heritage.

  1. Pickled Herring with Fennel and Mustard Seeds (Nordic Pickles) Ingredients:

12 pickling herring fillets 1 large bulb of fennel, thinly sliced 1 cup of white vinegar 1/2 cup of granulated sugar 2 tbsp of mustard seeds 1 tsp of fennel seeds Salt and black pepper to taste Fresh dill, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

In a large saucepan, combine the vinegar, sugar, mustard seeds, and fennel seeds. Bring to a boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar and spices. Pack the herring fillets into a large glass jar, leaving space for the pickling liquid. Pour the pickling liquid over the herring, making sure they're completely submerged. Allow the herring to pickle for at least 24 hours, or up to a week in the refrigerator. Just before serving, slice the fennel into thin rings and serve alongside the pickled herring.

Pickled herring is a staple in Scandinavian cuisine, and when paired with the aniseed flavor of fennel, creates a classic combination. The fennel adds a welcome crunch and sweetness to the bold, fishy flavor of the herring. 2. Swedish Meatballs with Lingonberry Jam (köttbullar) Ingredients:

500g beef mince 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 tsp allspice 1 tsp salt 1/2 tsp black pepper 2 tbsp butter 1 cup lingonberry jam

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Mix the beef mince, onion, garlic, allspice, salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Roll the mixture into small balls, about 1 inch in diameter. Place the meatballs on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until cooked through. Serve the meatballs with lingonberry jam, which adds a sweet and tart flavor to the savory meat.

Swedish meatballs are a national treasure, and when paired with the vibrant, tart flavor of lingonberry jam, creates a match made in heaven. 3. Norwegian Poached Salmon with Aquavit and Fennel (Kvakkfisk) Ingredients:

4 salmon fillets 1 cup aquavit 1 large bulb of fennel, thinly sliced 2 tbsp unsalted butter Salt and black pepper to taste Fresh dill, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat a large saucepan with a tablespoon of butter over low heat. Add the aquavit and sliced fennel to the saucepan, stirring gently to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the fennel is tender but still crisp. Poach the salmon fillets in the fennel-infused aquavit for 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Serve the salmon with the poaching liquid spooned over the top.

Norwegian poached salmon is a dish steeped in tradition, and when paired with the aniseed flavor of aquavit and the sweetness of fennel, creates a dish fit for a king. 4. Danish Open Faced Sandwiches (smørrebrød) Ingredients:

4 slices of dense, dark rye bread 2 tbsp of pickled red onion 2 tbsp of mustard 2 tbsp of crème fraîche 4 oz smoked salmon, thinly sliced Fresh dill, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

Toast the rye bread slices until lightly browned. Spread a layer of pickled red onion, mustard, and crème fraîche over the bread. Top with a slice of smoked salmon and serve immediately.

Smørrebrød, the Danish open faced sandwich, is a staple in Scandinavian cuisine, and when paired with the bold flavors of pickled onion and smoked salmon, creates a dish that's sure to impress. 5. Swedish Pancakes with Cloudberry Jam and Whipped Cream (pannkakor) Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 2 tsp baking powder 1/4 tsp salt 1 cup milk 1 large egg 2 tbsp butter, melted 1 cup cloudberry jam Whipped cream (optional)

Instructions:

Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, and melted butter. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until combined. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and drop the batter by 1/4 cupfuls onto the pan. Cook the pancakes for 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown. Serve the pancakes with cloudberry jam and a dollop of whipped cream.

Swedish pancakes are a beloved breakfast staple, and when paired with the intense, tart flavor of cloudberry jam, creates a dish that's sure to satisfy. The Scandinavian cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors, traditions, and techniques waiting to be explored. With these five recipes, you'll be well on your way to savoring the bold flavors and rich traditions of this captivating culinary heritage.
