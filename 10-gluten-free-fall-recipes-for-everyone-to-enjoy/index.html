10 Gluten-Free Fall Recipes for Everyone to Enjoy


As the crisp autumn air settles in and the leaves transform into shades of amber and crimson, fall is a season that calls for comfort food. For many, this is prime time to indulge in warm, hearty dishes brimming with seasonal flavors like pumpkin, apples, squash, and cinnamon. However, if you or those you love follow a gluten-free lifestyle, creating delicious meals this time of year may take a little extra thought.

The good news is that gluten-free doesn’t mean compromising on flavor. In fact, fall is one of the easiest seasons to find naturally gluten-free ingredients that highlight the bounty of harvest time. From nourishing soups to decadent desserts, here are 10 gluten-free recipes perfect for fall that everyone can enjoy.

1. Creamy Pumpkin Soup with Coconut Milk

Nothing says fall quite like a steaming bowl of pumpkin soup. This recipe delivers all the comforting autumn flavors while remaining gluten-free, dairy-free, and easy to make.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium sugar pumpkin, roasted and pureed
  • 1 can full-fat coconut milk
  • 3 cups gluten-free vegetable broth
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat a drizzle of olive oil. Sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the pumpkin puree, coconut milk, and vegetable broth. Stir until smooth.
  3. Season with cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer on low heat for 20–25 minutes. Serve warm with a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds for extra texture.

2. Apple Cinnamon Baked Oatmeal

This gluten-free baked oatmeal dish tastes like fall in every bite. Perfect for busy mornings or as a comforting dessert, it’s a crowd-pleaser for all ages.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups certified gluten-free rolled oats
  • 2 large apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 cups almond milk
  • 1 egg (or a flaxseed egg substitute)
  • ½ cup maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with oil or butter.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the oats, baking powder, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Whisk together the almond milk, egg, and maple syrup. Pour over the oats.
  4. Stir in the diced apples, then transfer the mixture to the baking dish.
  5. Bake for 35–40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown. Serve warm.

3. Spaghetti Squash with Sage Brown Butter

For a simple yet flavorful gluten-free side dish, spaghetti squash is a fall favorite. The earthy sage butter adds a gourmet touch to this easy recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium spaghetti squash
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 6 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan (optional, for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Halve the spaghetti squash, scoop out the seeds, and roast cut-side down on a baking sheet for 40 minutes.
  2. While the squash roasts, melt the butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add the sage leaves and cook until crispy.
  3. Once the squash is done, scrape it with a fork to create “noodles.” Toss with the sage butter, season, and sprinkle with Parmesan if desired.

4. Butternut Squash Risotto

This creamy risotto is made entirely gluten-free and brings out the natural sweetness of butternut squash.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1½ cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups gluten-free chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Roast the squash until tender, then mash half of it and leave the other half in chunks.
  2. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil and sauté the onion. Add the rice and toast for 2 minutes.
  3. Stir in the wine and let it evaporate. Gradually add the broth, stirring until the rice is cooked.
  4. Fold in the mashed squash, butter, and Parmesan. Serve topped with squash chunks.

5. Gluten-Free Pumpkin Bread

This moist and fragrant bread is a must-have fall treat, ideal for everything from breakfast to an afternoon snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1¾ cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 2 large eggs
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a loaf pan.
  2. Whisk together the flour, sugar, baking soda, and spices.
  3. In another bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, eggs, and oil. Gradually mix the wet ingredients into the dry.
  4. Pour batter into the pan and bake for 50–60 minutes. Let it cool before slicing.

6. Crispy Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon

Bring on the crispy fall veggies! This gluten-free dish turns Brussels sprouts into an irresistible side with just a touch of salty bacon.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 4 slices of bacon, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet.
  2. Scatter the diced bacon over the sprouts, then roast for 25–30 minutes. Serve immediately.

7. Sweet Potato Shepherd’s Pie

Fall is synonymous with comfort food, and this gluten-free twist on shepherd’s pie swaps in sweet potatoes for an autumn-inspired flair.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey or beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cups frozen mixed vegetables
  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, brown the meat and onions. Add the vegetables and season.
  2. Spread the meat mixture in a baking dish, then top with mashed sweet potatoes mixed with milk and garlic powder.
  3. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25 minutes.

8. Gluten-Free Apple Crisp

This beloved dessert layers tender apples under a crisp, buttery topping, with no need for gluten to create the perfect crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large apples, peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup gluten-free oat flour
  • ½ cup gluten-free rolled oats
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Arrange the apples in a baking dish.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients with melted butter. Sprinkle over the apples.
  3. Bake for 30–35 minutes. Serve warm with ice cream.

9. Roasted Acorn Squash with Maple Glaze

This gluten-free recipe highlights the seasonal flavor of acorn squash with a sweet maple glaze.

Ingredients:

  • 1 acorn squash, sliced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp maple syrup
  • ½ tsp ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Toss the squash slices with oil, maple syrup, and cinnamon.
  2. Arrange on a baking sheet and roast at 400°F (200°C) for 25–30 minutes.

10. Cranberry Orange Chutney

This tangy and sweet chutney is a gluten-free condiment that pairs wonderfully with roasted poultry or as a topping for gluten-free crackers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh cranberries
  • ½ cup orange juice
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a saucepan and simmer over low heat until cranberries burst and the sauce thickens.
  2. Remove from heat and discard the cinnamon stick. Cool before serving.

These gluten-free fall recipes are as versatile as they are delicious. With the abundance of naturally gluten-free produce, you can celebrate autumn in all its glory without a single compromise on flavor. So grab your favorite ingredients, warm up the kitchen, and enjoy the season in every bite!
