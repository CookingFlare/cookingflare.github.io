Leftover Lovin': How to Turn Yesterday's Sausages into Today's Succulent Dishes


So, you had a BBQ or a big family dinner, and now you find yourself with leftover sausages. Don’t let them go to waste! Leftover sausages can be transformed into delicious, mouth-watering dishes that will have your family and friends coming back for more. In this post, we’ll explore some creative and tasty ways to repurpose those sausages into new and exciting meals. Let’s dive in!

Sausage and Pepper Pasta

Ingredients:

  • Leftover sausages, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 300g of pasta of your choice
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, sauté the onions, bell peppers, and garlic until they are softened.
  2. Add the sliced sausages and cook until they are heated through.
  3. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and sprinkle in the dried oregano. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. While the sauce is simmering, cook the pasta according to the package instructions.
  6. Toss the cooked pasta with the sausage and pepper sauce.
  7. Serve hot with a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese on top.

This hearty and flavorful pasta dish is a fantastic way to breathe new life into leftover sausages. The combination of savory sausages, sweet bell peppers, and tangy tomatoes makes for a truly satisfying meal.

Sausage and Potato Hash

Ingredients:

  • Leftover sausages, chopped
  • 4 potatoes, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • Fried or poached eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and cook until they are golden and crispy.
  3. Toss in the chopped onions and minced garlic, and continue to cook until they are softened.
  4. Add the chopped sausages to the skillet and cook until they are heated through.
  5. Sprinkle in the paprika, salt, and pepper, and give everything a good stir.
  6. Let the hash cook for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve the sausage and potato hash hot, garnished with fresh parsley. For an extra indulgence, top it with a fried or poached egg.

This comforting and rustic dish is perfect for a leisurely weekend brunch or a cozy weeknight dinner. The crispy potatoes, savory sausages, and aromatic spices create a delightful medley of flavors and textures.

Sausage Stuffed Peppers

Ingredients:

  • Leftover sausages, casings removed
  • 4 bell peppers, halved and deseeded
  • 1 cup of cooked rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon of Italian seasoning
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese for topping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the removed sausage meat, cooked rice, chopped onion, minced garlic, diced tomatoes, and Italian seasoning.
  3. Mix everything until well combined.
  4. Stuff the halved bell peppers with the sausage and rice mixture.
  5. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish and cover with foil.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender.
  7. Remove the foil, sprinkle the tops of the peppers with shredded mozzarella cheese, and return to the oven until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

These sausage stuffed peppers are a colorful and flavorful way to reinvent your leftover sausages. The hearty filling and gooey cheese topping make this dish an instant crowd-pleaser.

Conclusion

Leftover sausages are a versatile ingredient that can be transformed into a variety of delectable dishes. Whether you choose to incorporate them into pasta, hash, or stuffed peppers, there’s no doubt that yesterday’s sausages can become today’s succulent meals. So, the next time you find yourself with leftover sausages, unleash your culinary creativity and enjoy the process of turning them into something new and exciting!
