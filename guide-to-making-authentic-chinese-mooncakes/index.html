Guide to Making Authentic Chinese Mooncakes


Mooncakes are a symbol of celebration in Chinese culture, most commonly enjoyed during the Mid-Autumn Festival. This iconic pastry represents unity, prosperity, and family, as people gather to enjoy the full moon and share stories and memories. If you’re looking to try your hand at making traditional Chinese mooncakes at home, this guide will walk you through the steps in detail. By the end, you’ll have the knowledge and confidence to create these delicious, beautiful treats.

What Are Mooncakes?

Mooncakes are round or square pastries filled with various sweet or savory fillings. Their significance lies not only in their taste but also in their representation of family reunion and the harvest. The traditional mooncake is made with a soft, tender skin and features intricate patterns, often with Chinese characters or symbolic designs on the surface.

Mooncakes are most commonly filled with lotus seed paste or red bean paste, though other regional variations exist, such as salted egg yolks, nuts, and even savory fillings.

The Ingredients for Mooncakes

Making authentic mooncakes requires several essential ingredients. Let’s go over each one and their role in the recipe.

For the Mooncake Dough (Skin)

  1. Golden Syrup – Golden syrup is a thick, amber-colored sugar syrup that is a key ingredient in mooncake dough. It gives the dough its smooth texture and subtly sweet flavor.
  2. Lye Water – This is an alkaline solution used to help soften the dough and create the unique texture of mooncake skin.
  3. All-purpose Flour – This forms the base of the dough and provides structure.
  4. Vegetable Oil – Used to bring the dough together and keep it moist.

For the Fillings

  1. Lotus Seed Paste – This is the most traditional filling, made from lotus seeds that are ground into a smooth paste and sweetened with sugar.
  2. Red Bean Paste – Another common filling, made from cooked red beans and sugar.
  3. Salted Egg Yolks – These are often added to the fillings to provide a savory contrast to the sweetness of the paste. They symbolize the moon and are often placed in the center of the filling.
  4. Melon Seeds, Nuts, and Other Add-ins – These are often added to fillings for texture and additional flavor.

Step-by-Step Instructions for Making Authentic Chinese Mooncakes

1. Prepare the Fillings

The first step in making mooncakes is preparing your desired filling. Traditional mooncakes are often filled with lotus seed paste, but you can use red bean paste or even a combination of fillings.

Lotus Seed Paste Recipe

  • Ingredients:

    • 200g lotus seeds
    • 150g sugar
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt

  • Instructions:

    1. Soak the lotus seeds in water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
    2. Once soaked, remove the lotus seeds’ skins. This can be a bit time-consuming, but it’s necessary for a smooth paste.
    3. In a pot, cook the lotus seeds in water until tender, about 40 minutes.
    4. Drain the lotus seeds and blend them into a smooth paste using a food processor.
    5. In a wok or frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the lotus seed paste, sugar, and salt. Cook, stirring constantly, until the paste thickens and becomes less sticky. This should take about 15 minutes.
    6. Once the paste is cooked, allow it to cool. The filling is now ready to be used.

Salted Egg Yolks

Salted egg yolks are often used in traditional mooncakes, adding a savory flavor that contrasts beautifully with the sweetness of the filling. You can purchase salted eggs from an Asian grocery store or prepare them at home by soaking raw egg yolks in a salted brine for a few weeks. When they’re ready, simply separate the yolks from the whites.

2. Prepare the Dough

Now that the filling is ready, it’s time to make the dough. The dough for mooncakes is soft, elastic, and easy to work with.

Mooncake Dough Recipe

  • Ingredients:

    • 150g all-purpose flour
    • 100g golden syrup
    • 30g vegetable oil
    • 1/4 teaspoon lye water

  • Instructions:

    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the golden syrup, vegetable oil, and lye water. Stir until smooth.
    2. Slowly add the flour, mixing as you go. Use a spatula or your hands to knead the dough into a smooth ball. The dough should be soft and slightly tacky, but not sticky.
    3. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to an hour. This helps the dough relax and become easier to shape.

3. Assemble the Mooncakes

Once the dough and filling are ready, it’s time to assemble your mooncakes.

Step-by-Step Assembly:

  1. Divide the Dough: The dough should be divided into small portions. Typically, mooncakes weigh between 50g and 75g each. You can adjust the size based on your preference.

  2. Divide the Filling: Take your prepared filling and divide it into portions that are about 25g to 40g each, depending on the size of the mooncakes. If you’re using salted egg yolks, insert one yolk into the center of each portion of filling.

  3. Shape the Dough: Flatten each portion of dough into a small disc using your fingers or a rolling pin. Make sure the disc is large enough to wrap around the filling.

  4. Fill the Dough: Place the filling in the center of the dough disc. Gently fold the edges of the dough up and around the filling, pinching them together to seal the mooncake. Make sure the filling is completely enclosed in the dough.

  5. Mold the Mooncakes: If you have a mooncake mold, lightly dust it with flour to prevent sticking. Place the filled dough ball into the mold and press down firmly to create the desired shape and intricate design. If you don’t have a mold, you can simply use your hands to shape the mooncakes into round discs, though the traditional mooncake look may be missed.

  6. Chill: Once your mooncakes are shaped, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Refrigerate them for 30 minutes to help them hold their shape during baking.

4. Bake the Mooncakes

Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). The mooncakes need to be baked twice to get the perfect texture.

First Bake:

  1. Brush the mooncakes lightly with egg wash (a beaten egg mixed with a little water) to give them a golden color when baked.
  2. Bake the mooncakes for 10-15 minutes, or until the dough has set but is not fully browned.

Second Bake:

  1. After the first bake, remove the mooncakes from the oven and let them cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Brush the mooncakes with egg wash again, then return them to the oven for another 5-10 minutes or until the skin is golden brown.

5. Let the Mooncakes Rest

After baking, mooncakes need time to rest. Allow them to cool completely on a wire rack. The dough will become softer and more translucent as the mooncakes cool and sit overnight. This resting period is crucial as it allows the mooncakes to develop their signature texture and flavor.

Tips for Perfect Mooncakes

  • Moisture Control: If the mooncakes seem too dry after baking, you can cover them with a damp cloth or wrap them in plastic wrap to help them retain moisture. Letting them sit for at least 24 hours will ensure the perfect texture.

  • Adjust Fillings to Your Taste: While lotus seed paste and red bean paste are traditional, feel free to experiment with other fillings like black sesame paste or even sweet potato paste.

  • Make Your Own Fillings: If you can’t find pre-made fillings, you can easily make them at home. Sweet pastes are often smoother and tastier when prepared from scratch.

  • Use a Mold: If you don’t have a traditional mooncake mold, you can purchase one online or at an Asian market. A mold helps achieve the characteristic mooncake patterns and shapes.

Conclusion

Making authentic Chinese mooncakes at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. Whether you’re preparing them for the Mid-Autumn Festival or simply as a delicious treat for your family, this guide will help you create beautiful, traditional mooncakes with rich, flavorful fillings. By following these steps, you’ll master the art of mooncake making and enjoy these symbolic pastries at home.
