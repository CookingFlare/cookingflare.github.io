Taste of Vietnam: Wine Pairings for Fragrant Noodle Soups


When it comes to Vietnamese cuisine, fragrant noodle soups are a staple. These aromatic dishes are bursting with flavors and textures, making them a delight for the senses. But have you ever wondered what wine would pair perfectly with these delightful bowls of goodness? In this blog post, we will explore some wine pairings that will elevate your taste of Vietnam to new heights. So sit back, relax, and let’s dive into the world of wine and fragrant noodle soups!

Pho and Riesling

Let’s start with the iconic Vietnamese dish, pho. This aromatic soup is made with a flavorful broth, tender beef or chicken, and a variety of fresh herbs and spices. The complex flavors of pho call for a wine that can complement and balance them. Enter Riesling, a versatile white wine known for its acidity and ability to pair well with spicy and aromatic dishes.

The crisp and refreshing nature of Riesling cuts through the richness of the broth, while its subtle sweetness harmonizes with the fragrant spices. The wine’s acidity also helps cleanse the palate, preparing it for each delicious spoonful of pho.

Bun Bo Hue and Malbec

Next up is bun bo hue, a spicy noodle soup hailing from the city of Hue in central Vietnam. This hearty soup is known for its robust flavors, thanks to the combination of tender beef, pork, and aromatic lemongrass. To match the boldness of bun bo hue, we recommend a red wine with depth and intensity - Malbec.

The rich and velvety texture of Malbec complements the meaty flavors of the soup, while its dark fruit notes add a touch of sweetness. The wine’s tannins provide structure and balance to the spiciness of the dish, creating a harmonious pairing that will leave you craving for more.

Mi Quang and Sauvignon Blanc

Moving on to mi quang, a specialty from the Quang Nam province in central Vietnam. This vibrant dish features turmeric-infused noodles, tender pork or shrimp, fresh herbs, and a flavorful broth. To enhance the bright and fresh flavors of mi quang, we suggest reaching for a crisp and herbaceous white wine - Sauvignon Blanc.

The zesty acidity of Sauvignon Blanc cuts through the richness of the broth, while its herbaceous notes complement the fresh herbs in the dish. The wine’s citrus flavors also add a refreshing touch, making it a delightful pairing for a bowl of mi quang.

Banh Canh Cua and Chardonnay

Last but not least, banh canh cua, a thick and hearty crab noodle soup that will warm your soul. This indulgent dish is made with thick rice or tapioca noodles, succulent crab meat, and a flavorful broth. To complement the richness of banh canh cua, we recommend a full-bodied white wine with a creamy texture - Chardonnay.

The buttery and oaky flavors of Chardonnay enhance the decadence of the soup, while its acidity helps cut through the richness. The wine’s creamy texture also adds a luxurious mouthfeel, elevating the dining experience to a whole new level.

Conclusion

Vietnamese fragrant noodle soups are a culinary delight, and pairing them with the right wine can take your dining experience to new heights. Whether you’re enjoying a bowl of pho, bun bo hue, mi quang, or banh canh cua, there’s a wine out there that will perfectly complement the flavors and aromas. So next time you indulge in the taste of Vietnam, don’t forget to uncork a bottle of Riesling, Malbec, Sauvignon Blanc, or Chardonnay. Cheers to a delicious and unforgettable dining experience!
