How to Make Vegan Cheesecake Bars: Simple and Flavorful


Vegan desserts are more than just a trend; they’re a lifestyle choice for many looking to live a more plant-based, ethical, and health-conscious life. If you’re a fan of cheesecakes but prefer to avoid dairy, eggs, or any animal-derived ingredients, vegan cheesecake bars are the perfect solution. They’re rich, creamy, and packed with flavor—just like traditional cheesecake, but without any of the animal products. Whether you’re a long-time vegan or simply looking to try something new, this recipe will guide you through the steps to make the most indulgent, plant-based cheesecake bars.

Why Vegan Cheesecake Bars?

Vegan cheesecake bars are the perfect dessert for any occasion—whether it’s a family gathering, a dinner party, or just a treat to enjoy at home. These bars are not only cruelty-free but also free of refined sugars, gluten, and artificial ingredients. Using wholesome plant-based ingredients like cashews, coconut, and plant-based sweeteners, vegan cheesecakes are an easy, healthy alternative that doesn’t compromise on taste or texture.

They’re incredibly versatile as well, offering endless variations of flavors and toppings to suit any taste preference. From fruity to chocolatey, from classic vanilla to spiced cinnamon, the possibilities are endless. This recipe will walk you through making a basic vegan cheesecake bar, with the flexibility to customize it to your liking.

Ingredients for Vegan Cheesecake Bars

To make the most delicious and creamy vegan cheesecake bars, you’ll need a few essential ingredients. Some of these are pantry staples, while others may be new to you, but they work together to create the perfect texture and flavor. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the crust:

  • 1 ½ cups of crushed graham crackers (or use gluten-free crackers for a gluten-free option)
  • ¼ cup of melted coconut oil (or any plant-based butter or oil)
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup (or your preferred plant-based sweetener)

For the cheesecake filling:

  • 2 cups of raw cashews (soaked for at least 4 hours or overnight for the creamiest texture)
  • ¾ cup of coconut cream (for that rich, creamy texture)
  • ¼ cup of coconut oil (melted)
  • ⅓ cup of maple syrup (for sweetness, adjust to your taste)
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons of lemon juice (freshly squeezed)
  • Pinch of salt

Optional for topping:

  • Fresh fruit (berries, mango, or citrus)
  • Chocolate shavings or drizzle (for a decadent twist)
  • Shredded coconut (for texture)
  • Chopped nuts (such as almonds, pistachios, or walnuts)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Cheesecake Bars

Now that you have your ingredients ready, it’s time to get started on your vegan cheesecake bars! The process involves two main components: the crust and the filling. Both are simple to prepare and come together quickly.

Step 1: Prepare the Crust

  1. Crush the graham crackers: If you don’t have pre-crushed graham crackers, simply break the crackers into smaller pieces and pulse them in a food processor until finely ground. You can also crush them manually by placing the crackers in a sealed plastic bag and rolling a rolling pin over it.

  2. Mix the crust ingredients: In a medium bowl, combine the crushed graham crackers, melted coconut oil, and maple syrup. Stir everything together until the mixture is fully combined. The mixture should feel moist but not overly wet.

  3. Press into the pan: Line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper for easy removal. Pour the crust mixture into the pan and press it down evenly to form a solid base. You can use the back of a spoon or your fingers to do this. The crust should be firm and compact, as this will help hold the bars together once they set.

  4. Chill the crust: Place the pan with the crust in the fridge while you prepare the cheesecake filling. This allows the crust to firm up, ensuring it doesn’t crumble when you cut the bars later.

Step 2: Make the Cheesecake Filling

  1. Soak the cashews: To achieve a creamy texture, it’s essential to soak the cashews for at least 4 hours or overnight. If you’re short on time, you can speed up the process by soaking them in hot water for about 30 minutes. Drain and rinse the cashews before using them in the recipe.

  2. Blend the filling ingredients: In a high-speed blender or food processor, combine the soaked cashews, coconut cream, coconut oil, maple syrup, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and salt. Blend on high until smooth and creamy. Scrape down the sides of the blender or food processor as needed to ensure everything is fully blended.

  3. Taste and adjust: After blending, taste the filling. You can add more maple syrup if you like it sweeter, or more lemon juice for extra tartness. The texture should be thick and creamy, but if it’s too thick, you can add a little bit of water or more coconut cream to thin it out slightly.

Step 3: Assemble and Set the Bars

  1. Pour the filling over the crust: Once the cheesecake filling is smooth and ready, pour it over the chilled crust. Use a spatula to spread it evenly across the top.

  2. Smooth the top: After spreading the filling, smooth the top with the back of the spatula to make it even. This will help give your bars a polished look when they’re ready to cut.

  3. Chill and set: Place the cheesecake bars in the freezer to set for at least 4 hours, or overnight if you have the time. The bars will firm up as they chill, making them easier to slice.

Step 4: Slice and Serve

Once the bars have set, remove them from the freezer and allow them to thaw for about 10 minutes before cutting. This will make them easier to slice without cracking the filling. Using a sharp knife, cut the bars into squares or rectangles—whatever size you prefer.

If you’re feeling extra indulgent, you can top your cheesecake bars with fresh fruit, a drizzle of melted chocolate, or shredded coconut. Serve them chilled for the ultimate refreshing treat, or at room temperature for a softer, creamier texture.

Variations and Customizations

While this basic vegan cheesecake bar recipe is incredibly delicious on its own, there are many ways you can customize it to fit your preferences or seasonal ingredients. Here are some fun and easy variations to try:

1. Berry Bliss Vegan Cheesecake Bars

Top your bars with fresh berries like strawberries, raspberries, or blueberries for a fruity and tangy twist. You can also blend the berries directly into the filling for a burst of color and flavor throughout.

2. Chocolate-Lovers Vegan Cheesecake Bars

Add cocoa powder to the filling for a chocolate-flavored cheesecake base. Alternatively, swirl in some melted vegan chocolate into the filling before it sets for a marbled effect. You can also top the bars with chocolate ganache or chocolate chips for a decadent finish.

3. Citrus Zest Vegan Cheesecake Bars

For a refreshing, citrusy flavor, add lemon, lime, or orange zest to the filling. You can also mix a little citrus juice into the filling for added tang. A sprinkle of zest on top adds a vibrant finishing touch.

4. Coffee-Infused Vegan Cheesecake Bars

For a grown-up twist, add a shot of espresso or a few teaspoons of instant coffee granules to the filling for a coffee-flavored cheesecake. This pairs wonderfully with a dusting of cocoa powder or a few chocolate-covered espresso beans.

5. Nutty Vegan Cheesecake Bars

If you enjoy a little crunch in your desserts, top the cheesecake bars with a handful of chopped nuts, like almonds, walnuts, or pecans. You can also stir crushed nuts into the crust for added texture.

Tips for the Perfect Vegan Cheesecake Bars

  • Soak your cashews properly: The key to a smooth and creamy cheesecake filling is properly soaking the cashews. This step can’t be skipped, so make sure to give yourself enough time for the cashews to soften and blend well.

  • Don’t skip the chilling process: Chilling the crust and the bars is essential for achieving a firm and stable texture. Make sure the bars are fully set before cutting to avoid a messy, soft filling.

  • Store leftovers properly: Vegan cheesecake bars can be stored in the fridge for up to a week or in the freezer for longer storage. Just make sure to keep them in an airtight container to maintain freshness.

Conclusion

Making vegan cheesecake bars is surprisingly simple and yields delicious results. Whether you’re looking for a dairy-free dessert or trying to incorporate more plant-based options into your diet, these bars will satisfy your sweet tooth without any guilt. With a rich, creamy filling and a crunchy crust, they’re sure to be a hit at your next gathering—or whenever you need a little indulgence. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a plant-based dessert that will impress everyone!

Feel free to experiment with different flavors, toppings, and fillings to make this recipe your own. Once you taste how delicious these vegan cheesecake bars are, they’ll surely become your go-to dessert for any occasion. Happy baking (or rather, no-baking)!
