Satisfying Split Pea Soup Recipes for a Hearty Meal


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the warmth and satisfaction of a steaming bowl of split pea soup. This humble yet hearty meal, made from dried split peas, is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients, making it an ideal choice for a nourishing family dinner or a cozy night in. In this blog post, we will explore various satisfying split pea soup recipes that promise to fill your belly and warm your soul.

The Rich History of Split Pea Soup

Split pea soup has a long history, dating back to ancient civilizations. The earliest records of pea soup can be traced to the Greeks and Romans, who enjoyed it as a staple dish. Over time, this dish has evolved across cultures, with different regions putting their unique spin on it. Today, split pea soup is cherished worldwide for its simplicity, affordability, and incredible flavor.

The Nutritional Benefits of Split Peas

Before diving into our recipes, let’s take a moment to appreciate the nutritional benefits of split peas. They are a fantastic source of plant-based protein, making them an excellent choice for vegetarians and vegans. Additionally, they are high in dietary fiber, which supports digestive health and can help regulate blood sugar levels. Split peas are also rich in vitamins and minerals, including iron, magnesium, and folate.

Classic Split Pea Soup Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound dried green or yellow split peas, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 smoked ham hock (optional for flavor)
  • 6 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme (dried or fresh)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Rinse the split peas under cold water and set aside. Dice the onion, carrots, and celery, and mince the garlic.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery, sautéing for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the rinsed split peas, smoked ham hock (if using), vegetable broth, bay leaf, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  4. Simmer: Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the soup simmer for about 60-75 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the split peas are tender and the soup has thickened.

  5. Final Touches: Remove the ham hock (if used) and shred any meat from the bone back into the soup. Adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper to taste. If you prefer a creamier texture, use an immersion blender to puree part of the soup.

  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and enjoy with crusty bread or a fresh salad.

This classic recipe is a wonderful base that can be customized to suit your taste. Feel free to add other vegetables, such as potatoes or spinach, or experiment with different herbs and spices.

Curried Split Pea Soup

For a unique twist on the traditional split pea soup, try this curried version that brings a burst of flavor and warmth to the table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound dried split peas, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (fresh or ground)
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, cooking for about 5-7 minutes until softened. Stir in garlic, curry powder, cumin, and ginger, cooking for an additional minute.

  2. Add Split Peas and Broth: Stir in the rinsed split peas and vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat to low and let it simmer for 60-75 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Incorporate Coconut Milk: Once the peas are tender, stir in the coconut milk and let it simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Blend and Serve: For a creamier consistency, blend part of the soup using an immersion blender. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

This curried split pea soup is sure to impress with its rich, exotic flavors and creamy texture.

Smoky Bacon Split Pea Soup

For those who enjoy a smoky flavor, this bacon-infused split pea soup is a delightful option that adds depth to the dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound dried split peas, rinsed
  • 4 strips of bacon, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon thyme (dried or fresh)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Bacon: In a large pot, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove the bacon and let it drain on paper towels, leaving the drippings in the pot.

  2. Sauté the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion, carrots, and celery to the bacon drippings. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  3. Combine Ingredients: Add the rinsed split peas, chicken broth, bay leaf, thyme, and the cooked bacon back into the pot. Bring to a boil.

  4. Simmer: Reduce heat to low, cover, and let the soup simmer for 60-75 minutes until the split peas are tender.

  5. Final Adjustments: Remove the bay leaf and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. For a creamier texture, blend part of the soup.

  6. Serve: Ladle into bowls and top with reserved crispy bacon for added flavor and crunch.

This smoky bacon split pea soup is the perfect comfort food for chilly evenings, combining the hearty flavors of bacon with the nourishing qualities of split peas.

Vegetarian Split Pea Soup with Spinach

This vegetarian version is packed with nutrients and flavor, making it a wholesome choice for everyone.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound dried split peas, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 4 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon oregano (dried or fresh)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, sautéing for about 5-7 minutes until softened. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.

  2. Combine the Ingredients: Add the rinsed split peas, vegetable broth, bay leaf, oregano, salt, and pepper to the pot. Bring to a boil.

  3. Simmer: Reduce heat to low, cover, and let the soup simmer for 60-75 minutes until the split peas are tender.

  4. Incorporate Spinach: Stir in the fresh spinach and cook for an additional 5 minutes until wilted.

  5. Final Touches: Adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper to taste. For a creamier texture, blend part of the soup.

  6. Serve: Ladle into bowls and enjoy this nutritious and vibrant soup.

This vegetarian split pea soup with spinach is not only healthy but also bursting with color and flavor, making it an appealing dish for everyone at the table.

Tips for Perfect Split Pea Soup

  • Choose Quality Split Peas: Always start with fresh, high-quality split peas. Older peas may take longer to cook and can result in a less desirable texture.

  • Soaking: While soaking split peas isn’t necessary, doing so can reduce cooking time and improve digestibility.

  • Adjust Consistency: If you prefer a thicker soup, blend a portion of it. For a thinner consistency, simply add more broth or water.

  • Storage: Split pea soup can be refrigerated for up to a week or frozen for up to three months. Reheat gently on the stove, adding a splash of water or broth if needed.

Conclusion

Split pea soup is a timeless dish that offers comfort, nutrition, and versatility. From classic recipes to innovative twists, there’s a split pea soup for everyone. Whether you’re cooking for family or hosting friends, these recipes are sure to satisfy and warm the hearts of those who indulge. So grab your ingredients, gather around the table, and enjoy the delightful experience of homemade split pea soup!
