Authentic Southern Banana Pudding Recipe for Sweet Bliss


When it comes to classic Southern desserts, few can rival the irresistible charm of authentic Southern banana pudding. This timeless treat is a delightful combination of creamy custard, fresh bananas, and luscious whipped cream, all layered with tender vanilla wafers to create a heavenly dessert that never fails to impress. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, a family gathering, or simply craving a comforting sweet treat, this authentic Southern banana pudding recipe is sure to bring bliss to your table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 5 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 1 box (12 oz) vanilla wafers
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons confectioners’ sugar

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Custard

In a heavy saucepan, whisk together the granulated sugar, flour, and salt. In a separate bowl, lightly beat the egg yolks and stir them into the sugar mixture. Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly to ensure a smooth consistency. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook the mixture, stirring constantly, until it thickens to a custard-like consistency. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract. Allow the custard to cool to room temperature.

Step 2: Assemble the Layers

In a large serving dish or trifle bowl, create the first layer by arranging a single layer of vanilla wafers at the bottom. Top the wafers with a layer of sliced bananas, followed by a portion of the cooled custard. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, ending with a layer of custard on top.

Step 3: Whip the Cream

In a chilled mixing bowl, whisk the heavy cream and confectioners’ sugar until stiff peaks form. Spread the whipped cream over the top layer of custard, creating a luscious, cloud-like finish.

Step 4: Chill and Serve

Cover the assembled banana pudding with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, allowing the flavors to meld and the dessert to set. When ready to serve, garnish with additional banana slices and crumbled vanilla wafers for an extra touch of charm.
