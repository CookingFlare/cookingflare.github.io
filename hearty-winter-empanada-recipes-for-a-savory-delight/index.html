Hearty Winter Empanada Recipes for a Savory Delight


As the chilly winds and frosty mornings of winter set in, there’s nothing quite like the warmth and comfort of hearty, savory empanadas to satisfy your cravings. These delightful pastries, hailing from Latin America, are the perfect way to infuse your winter meals with a touch of coziness and flavor. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply seeking a comforting meal for a cozy night in, these hearty winter empanada recipes are sure to impress.

Classic Beef and Vegetable Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Empanada dough (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and bell pepper, and cook until the vegetables are tender.
  2. Stir in the frozen peas and carrots, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, then remove from heat and let the filling cool.
  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  4. Roll out the empanada dough and cut it into circles using a round cutter or a small plate.
  5. Spoon the beef and vegetable filling onto each dough circle, fold it over, and crimp the edges to seal.
  6. Place the empanadas on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

Serve these classic beef and vegetable empanadas with a side of chimichurri sauce for an authentic and flavorful experience.

Spicy Chicken and Chorizo Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup Spanish chorizo, diced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • Empanada dough (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, sauté the onion and garlic until translucent. Add the diced chorizo and cook until it releases its oils.
  2. Stir in the shredded chicken, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and tomato sauce. Cook for 5-7 minutes, then remove from heat and let the filling cool.
  3. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  4. Roll out the empanada dough and cut it into circles using a round cutter or a small plate.
  5. Spoon the chicken and chorizo filling onto each dough circle, fold it over, and crimp the edges to seal.
  6. Place the empanadas on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

These spicy chicken and chorizo empanadas are perfect for those who crave a bit of heat and bold flavors during the winter months.

Vegetarian Butternut Squash and Black Bean Empanadas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups butternut squash, diced and roasted
  • 1 cup black beans, cooked
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Empanada dough (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the roasted butternut squash, black beans, red onion, jalapeño, ground cumin, chili powder, and fresh cilantro.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Roll out the empanada dough and cut it into circles using a round cutter or a small plate.
  4. Spoon the butternut squash and black bean filling onto each dough circle, fold it over, and crimp the edges to seal.
  5. Place the empanadas on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.

These vegetarian butternut squash and black bean empanadas are a wholesome and satisfying option for those looking for a meatless delight during the winter season.

Whether you’re a fan of classic beef, spicy chicken, or vegetarian delights, these hearty winter empanada recipes are sure to warm your heart and delight your taste buds during the cold months. Embrace the warmth of the kitchen and the joy of sharing these savory delights with your loved ones. Happy cooking and bon appétit!
