Grilled Ratatouille Flatbread: A Mediterranean Delight


There’s nothing quite like charred crust and smoky flavors, achieved effortlessly in a grilled flatbread. As summer begins to fade, we can continue to enjoy the warmth of the grill and the distinct flavors of the Mediterranean with this Grilled Ratatouille Flatbread recipe.

The inspiration behind this recipe lies in the rich culinary traditions of the Mediterranean Basin, where street vendors serve families fiery, aromatic flatbreads laden with a colorful assortment of vegetables. This version takes a contemporary twist on the classic French ratatouille, substituting the traditional roasting method with a direct flame cook.

Our Grilled Ratatouille Flatbread incorporates an assortment of colorful vegetables, achieving a symphony of flavors. As you embrace this delectable creation, an effortless gust of Mediterranean breeze will invigorate your senses.

You’ll Need:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 yellow squash, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ cup canned diced tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp fresh basil, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • ¼ cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 flatbread, prepared
  • Drizzle of olive oil

Whisk the flour and salt together in a large bowl. Add warm water and olive oil, mixing until a soft dough forms. Knead lightly and let it rest for five minutes. Roll out the dough on a floured surface, creating an opening for air circulation.

Sauté zucchini, yellow squash, bell pepper, and onion with garlic on medium-high heat until vegetables are slightly caramelized. Incorporate diced tomatoes and cook for an additional five minutes. Take off heat and mix in basil and parsley.

Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Brush the flatbread with beaten egg and drizzle with olive oil. Place the flatbread on the grill, and once it begins to char, spread the ratatouille mixture over it.

Position your vegetables of choice on the flatbread in a colorful, appealing arrangement. Sprinkle crumbled feta cheese liberally over the ratatouille. Allow the flatbread to cook until the vegetables are soft and the cheese is melted, approximately ten minutes.

Remove your Grilled Ratatouille Flatbread from the grill, slice, and serve. Enjoy the medley of smoky, crispy, and aromatic flavors in every bite, embodying the essence of the Mediterranean Basin.

Lastly, pour a glass of your favorite Mediterranean wine and share the grilled ratatouille flatbread with family and friends as you embrace the warmth of the fading summer evenings. Ah, the Mediterranean delight!

This diverse, veggie-packed flatbread provides a perfect meal or appetizer. It’s a mouthwatering addition to year-round cookouts, impromptu gatherings, and backyard barbecue celebrations. Experiment with different vegetables or herbs, ensuring each dish is as unique as the cultures it celebrates. Welcome to the colorful world of Grilled Ratatouille Flatbread, a culinary adventure awaiting you on the open grill.
