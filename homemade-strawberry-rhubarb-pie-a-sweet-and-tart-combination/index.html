Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Pie: A Sweet and Tart Combination


There’s something undeniably nostalgic and comforting about a homemade pie. The sweet aroma of fresh fruit and buttery pastry wafting through the kitchen is enough to make anyone’s mouth water. If you’re looking to capture the essence of summertime in a dessert, look no further than a Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Pie. This delightful treat combines the sweetness of ripe strawberries with the tartness of rhubarb, resulting in a perfect balance of flavors that will have your taste buds singing.

The Perfect Pie Crust

Every great pie starts with a great crust, and this recipe is no exception. To create the perfect flaky, golden-brown crust, you’ll need:

  • 2 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 6-8 tablespoons of ice water

Start by combining the flour and salt in a large mixing bowl. Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least an hour.

The Filling

While the pie crust chills, it’s time to prepare the luscious filling. For the filling, you’ll need:

  • 3 cups of fresh rhubarb, chopped
  • 3 cups of fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon

In a large bowl, gently toss together the rhubarb, strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and cinnamon until the fruit is evenly coated. Set the filling aside while you roll out the pie crust.

Assembly and Baking

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out one disc of the chilled pie dough on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Gently place the dough into the pie dish, trimming any excess overhang. Pour the prepared filling into the crust, smoothing it into an even layer.

Roll out the second disc of pie dough and either place it on top of the filling as a whole crust or cut it into strips to create a lattice pattern. Seal the edges of the crust, and if using a whole crust on top, make a few slits to allow steam to escape during baking. If using a lattice pattern, weave the strips over the filling and press the edges to seal.

Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 20 minutes. Then, reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for an additional 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbly.

Enjoy the Fruits of Your Labor

Once the pie is golden and bubbling, remove it from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least an hour before slicing. This will allow the filling to set and make for easier serving. Serve the pie warm or at room temperature, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream if desired.

Final Thoughts

Homemade Strawberry Rhubarb Pie is a delightful celebration of summer’s bounty. The combination of sweet strawberries and tangy rhubarb nestled in a flaky, buttery crust is a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re serving it at a family gathering, a picnic, or simply indulging in a slice at home, this pie is sure to bring smiles and satisfy cravings. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create a dessert that will have everyone asking for seconds. Happy baking!
