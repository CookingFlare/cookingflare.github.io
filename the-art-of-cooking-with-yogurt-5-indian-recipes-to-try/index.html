The Art of Cooking with Yogurt: 5 Indian Recipes to Try


Yogurt has been a staple in Indian cuisine for centuries, and for good reason. Not only is it a versatile ingredient, but it also adds a tangy, creamy element to a wide range of dishes. From soups and salads to curries and desserts, yogurt is a common sight in traditional Indian kitchens. In this article, we'll explore the art of cooking with yogurt, and showcase five delicious Indian recipes that you can try at home. The Benefits of Cooking with Yogurt Before we dive into the recipes, let's talk about the benefits of cooking with yogurt. Yogurt is an excellent source of probiotics, which can aid in digestion and boost the immune system. It's also a good source of protein, calcium, and vitamins like B2 and B12. In terms of cooking, yogurt has a few advantages. Firstly, it acts as a binding agent, helping to hold ingredients together and create a smooth, consistent texture. Secondly, it adds a tangy flavor that balances out the richness of other ingredients. Finally, yogurt can be used as a marinade or a topping, adding moisture and flavor to dishes without overpowering them. Recipe 1: Yogurt Raita Raita is a popular Indian condiment made with yogurt, cucumber, cumin, and coriander. It's a simple yet refreshing side dish that pairs perfectly with spicy curries.

1 cup plain yogurt 1/2 cup grated cucumber 1/4 cup chopped cumin 1/4 cup chopped coriander 1 tablespoon lemon juice Salt to taste

Instructions:

In a bowl, whisk together the yogurt, cucumber, cumin, coriander, and lemon juice until smooth. Season with salt to taste. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Recipe 2: Chicken Tikka Masala with Yogurt Sauce Chicken Tikka Masala is a beloved Indian dish that originated in the UK. It's a creamy, flavorful curry that's perfect for special occasions.

1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts 1/2 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 2 teaspoons cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

Preheat the grill or grill pan to medium-high heat. In a bowl, whisk together the yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Add the chicken to the bowl and mix until coated. Grill the chicken for 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through. Meanwhile, heat the yogurt sauce over low heat. Simmer the chicken in the yogurt sauce for 5-7 minutes, or until heated through.

Recipe 3: Palak Paneer with Yogurt Palak Paneer is a creamy, spinach-based curry that's a staple in Indian cuisine. It's a great way to get your daily dose of greens!

1 cup paneer (Indian cheese) 2 cups fresh spinach leaves 1/2 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil 1 teaspoon garam masala 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder Salt to taste

Instructions:

In a blender, puree the spinach leaves with the yogurt until smooth. Heat the ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cumin powder and garam masala, and saut√© for 1 minute. Add the spinach-yogurt mixture and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until heated through. Stir in the paneer and season with salt to taste.

Recipe 4: Yogurt and Garlic Naan Bites Naan is a popular Indian flatbread that's perfect for sopping up sauces or serving as a snack. These Yogurt and Garlic Naan Bites are a delicious twist on traditional naan.

2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 cup plain yogurt 1/4 cup ghee or vegetable oil 1 garlic clove, minced 1 tablespoon ginger paste Salt to taste

Instructions:

In a bowl, whisk together the flour, yogurt, ghee or oil, garlic, ginger paste, and salt. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, or until smooth. Divide the dough into 8-10 portions and shape into balls. Roll out each ball into a thin circle. Brush with melted butter and sprinkle with chopped cilantro (optional).

Recipe 5: Ras Malai with Yogurt Foam Ras Malai is a classic Indian dessert that's a must-try. It's a creamy, sweet treat that's topped with a tangy yogurt foam.

1 cup plain yogurt 1 cup granulated sugar 1 cup milk 1 tablespoon cornstarch 1 teaspoon cardamom powder 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads 1 cup paneer (Indian cheese)

Instructions:

In a bowl, whisk together the yogurt, sugar, milk, cornstarch, cardamom powder, and saffron threads. Cook the mixture over low heat, stirring constantly, until the sugar dissolves. Meanwhile, boil the paneer in water for 5-7 minutes, or until cooked through. Drain and cut the paneer into small pieces. To make the yogurt foam, heat the yogurt in a saucepan over low heat until slightly thickened. Pour the yogurt foam over the paneer and sugar mixture, and serve chilled.

Conclusion As you can see, yogurt is an incredibly versatile ingredient that can be used in a wide range of Indian dishes. From soups and salads to curries and desserts, yogurt adds a tangy, creamy element that elevates the flavor and texture of any recipe. We hope you enjoyed these 5 Indian recipes that feature yogurt as the main ingredient. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, these recipes are sure to inspire you to experiment with yogurt in your cooking. Happy cooking!
