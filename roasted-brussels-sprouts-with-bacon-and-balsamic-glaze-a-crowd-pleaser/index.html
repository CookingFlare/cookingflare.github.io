Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze: A Crowd-Pleaser


Are you tired of the same old side dishes that seem to appear at every gathering? Look no further! I have a recipe that will not only impress your guests but also leave them begging for more. Introducing Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze – a crowd-pleaser that will elevate any meal to new heights.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound Brussels sprouts
  • 4 slices of bacon
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). While the oven is heating, prepare the Brussels sprouts by trimming off the ends and removing any loose outer leaves. Cut the larger sprouts in half, so they are all roughly the same size.

  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the bacon until crispy. Once cooked, remove the bacon from the skillet and set it aside on a paper towel-lined plate. Leave the bacon drippings in the skillet.

  3. Add the Brussels sprouts to the skillet with the bacon drippings, season with salt and pepper, and toss to coat. Cook the sprouts for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they are slightly browned.

  4. Transfer the Brussels sprouts to a baking sheet, arranging them in a single layer. Drizzle the olive oil over the sprouts, making sure each one is coated. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the sprouts are tender and golden brown.

  5. While the sprouts are roasting, crumble the cooked bacon into small pieces.

  6. In a small saucepan, heat the balsamic vinegar over medium-high heat until it comes to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 5 minutes, or until it has thickened slightly.

  7. Once the Brussels sprouts are done roasting, remove them from the oven and transfer them to a serving dish. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the sprouts, then sprinkle the crumbled bacon on top.

  8. Serve immediately and watch as your guests devour this flavorful and irresistible dish. The combination of the roasted Brussels sprouts, smoky bacon, and tangy balsamic glaze is a match made in culinary heaven.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, omit the bacon and use vegetable oil instead of bacon drippings.
  • If you prefer a sweeter glaze, you can add a teaspoon of honey or maple syrup to the balsamic vinegar while it simmers.
  • Feel free to experiment with different seasonings and spices to customize the flavor of the dish. Some popular options include garlic powder, paprika, or crushed red pepper flakes.

Conclusion

Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Bacon and Balsamic Glaze is a dish that will transform even the most skeptical Brussels sprouts haters into fans. The combination of crispy bacon, tender roasted sprouts, and tangy balsamic glaze creates a symphony of flavors that will leave your taste buds wanting more. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to impress your family at the next holiday gathering, this crowd-pleaser is guaranteed to be a hit. So, give it a try and prepare to be amazed by the magic that happens when these ingredients come together.
