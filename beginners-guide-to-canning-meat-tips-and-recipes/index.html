Beginner's Guide to Canning Meat: Tips and Recipes


Canning meat is an incredible way to preserve your protein for long-term storage while maintaining the flavor and nutrition of your dishes. If you’re new to canning, don’t worry! With the right tools and techniques, anyone can start canning meat at home. This guide will walk you through the essentials of canning meat, from understanding the process to offering tips and easy recipes that will give you a head start on your canning journey.

What is Canning Meat?

Canning is the process of preserving food by sealing it in jars and heating it to kill bacteria, yeast, and molds that can cause spoilage. The heat also creates a vacuum seal that helps keep the food fresh and safe for long periods. While fruits and vegetables are commonly canned, meat can also be preserved in a similar way. Canning meat allows you to store protein for up to a year (or even longer) without refrigeration, making it a perfect solution for long-term food storage, preparedness, and easy meals.

There are two main methods of canning meat: pressure canning and water bath canning. Since meat is a low-acid food, pressure canning is the only safe method for preserving it. A pressure canner uses high pressure to reach temperatures of 240°F (115°C), which is necessary to kill harmful bacteria like botulism that could survive in a water bath.

Benefits of Canning Meat

1. Convenience

Canned meat is a game-changer when it comes to convenience. Once you’ve canned your meat, it’s ready to use for a variety of dishes without requiring any additional cooking time. Whether you need beef, chicken, or pork, opening a jar of canned meat means you’ve got a quick and easy protein source for your meals.

2. Cost-Effective

Buying meat in bulk and canning it yourself is often cheaper than buying pre-canned meat. You can take advantage of sales, purchase cuts of meat that are on discount, and can your own meats at home. This not only saves you money but allows you to select the highest-quality meats for your jars.

3. Long-Term Storage

Canned meat can be stored for extended periods, making it perfect for prepping and emergency food storage. When stored in a cool, dark place, canned meat can last anywhere from 1-5 years. Having jars of canned meat on hand gives you peace of mind knowing that you have a ready supply of food in case of emergencies or unforeseen circumstances.

4. Great for Meal Prep

Canning meat is an excellent solution for meal prepping. If you often find yourself in a rush to get dinner on the table, canned meat can cut your cooking time significantly. Just open a jar, heat it, and incorporate it into soups, stews, casseroles, and salads.

Essential Equipment for Canning Meat

Before you begin, you’ll need the right equipment to ensure the canning process is done safely and effectively:

1. Pressure Canner

A pressure canner is a must for safely canning meat. It differs from a pressure cooker, as it is specifically designed for home canning and comes with safety features that allow you to maintain proper pressure. The pressure canner ensures that the jars reach the necessary temperature to kill harmful microorganisms.

2. Canning Jars and Lids

Choose high-quality canning jars that are specifically made for pressure canning. Wide-mouth jars are easier to fill, and they also offer more surface area for sealing. Use new lids each time you can, as lids that have already been used may not seal properly.

3. Canning Rack

A canning rack holds the jars off the bottom of the canner and prevents them from coming into direct contact with the heat source. This allows for even heat distribution during the process.

4. Jar Lifter

A jar lifter is an essential tool for safely removing hot jars from the pressure canner. It has a special grip that securely holds the jars, minimizing the risk of burns or breakage.

5. Canning Salt (Optional)

Canning salt can be used to enhance the flavor of your canned meat. It is free of iodine, which can cause discoloration in your canned food. While optional, a small amount of salt can improve the taste of the meat once it is canned.

6. Funnel

A canning funnel helps to fill the jars without making a mess. It’s especially helpful for meats, which can be quite greasy or fatty.

7. Cleaning Tools

Ensure you have a clean towel or paper towels on hand to wipe the rims of the jars before sealing them. Any food particles on the rim can prevent a proper seal, leading to spoilage.

Preparing Meat for Canning

Proper preparation is essential for ensuring the quality and safety of your canned meat. Here are some basic steps to follow:

1. Choosing the Meat

When selecting meat to can, aim for cuts that are tender and have a moderate amount of fat. Lean meats like chicken breasts or beef sirloin are excellent choices. Fatty meats like bacon or pork belly may need extra attention, as the fat can cause spoilage if not properly handled.

2. Trimming the Meat

Trim excess fat from the meat before canning. While some fat is necessary for flavor and texture, too much can cause the meat to spoil faster. Excess fat can also make it difficult for the jar to seal properly.

3. Cutting the Meat

Cut the meat into pieces that will easily fit into your canning jars. You can either can the meat in chunks or ground, depending on your preference. For example, you can can whole chicken breasts or chunk up beef stew meat. Ground meat can also be canned but needs to be browned beforehand to improve texture and flavor.

4. Packing the Jars

Pack the meat into jars as tightly as possible, but leave about 1-inch of headspace at the top of each jar. This allows for expansion during the canning process and ensures a proper seal. If you’re adding salt for flavor, include it now (typically 1 teaspoon of salt per pint jar or 2 teaspoons per quart jar).

5. Adding Liquids

While it is not necessary to add liquid when canning meat (as it will release its own juices), some people like to add broth, water, or even wine to enhance the flavor. If you choose to add liquid, leave at least 1-inch of headspace.

The Canning Process: Step-by-Step

Once your meat is prepared, it’s time to begin the canning process. Follow these steps for safe and successful canning:

1. Prepare the Pressure Canner

Before you begin, make sure your pressure canner is clean and in good working order. Fill the bottom of the canner with the appropriate amount of water, according to the manufacturer’s instructions. The amount of water will vary depending on the model of your canner.

2. Heat the Jars

While you are preparing the meat and the pressure canner, it’s important to keep the jars warm. You can do this by placing them in the oven on low heat or by filling them with hot water. Warm jars will prevent cracking when exposed to the high heat inside the canner.

3. Load the Jars into the Canner

Once your jars are filled and ready, place them into the pressure canner. Ensure they are spaced properly and not touching one another. Place the lid on the canner, making sure it’s tightly sealed.

4. Bring the Pressure Canner to Pressure

Turn on the heat and allow the pressure canner to come to the correct pressure. The amount of pressure needed will depend on your altitude, but generally, it’s around 10-15 pounds of pressure. Refer to your canner’s manual for specific instructions for your area.

5. Process the Meat

Once the canner has reached the proper pressure, start the timer. Processing times vary depending on the type of meat and the size of the jar. Generally, meat is processed for 75-90 minutes at 10-15 pounds of pressure. Be sure to adjust for altitude according to your pressure canner’s manual.

6. Cool Down

After the processing time is complete, turn off the heat and allow the pressure to naturally drop. This can take 10-20 minutes. Once the pressure has fully released, carefully remove the lid and use your jar lifter to take out the jars. Set the jars on a clean towel to cool.

7. Check the Seals

After the jars have cooled for 12-24 hours, check the seals. The lids should be concave, indicating a proper seal. If any jars haven’t sealed, refrigerate them and consume within a few days.

Tips for Canning Meat

  • Don’t Skip the Pressure Canner: For safety reasons, always use a pressure canner when canning meat. Water bath canning is not sufficient for preserving low-acid foods like meat.
  • Avoid Overfilling: Leave enough headspace in the jar to ensure proper sealing. Overfilling can cause the jar to fail to seal properly.
  • Don’t Can Bones: While it’s okay to can meat with bones (like chicken thighs), don’t can large bones like beef bones or pork bones. These can cause uneven heat distribution and improper sealing.
  • Use Fresh Meat: Always use fresh meat that has been properly refrigerated. Canning meat that is past its prime can lead to spoilage.

Easy Recipes for Canned Meat

Once your canned meat is ready, you can start incorporating it into meals. Here are some simple recipes that use canned meat:

1. Beef Stew

Use your canned beef to make a hearty stew. Just add your favorite vegetables (carrots, potatoes, onions, and celery) along with canned beef broth and seasoning. Simmer on the stove until the vegetables are tender, and you’ve got a delicious meal in no time.

2. Chicken Tacos

Canned chicken makes taco night a breeze! Simply open a jar of canned chicken, shred it, and heat it with taco seasoning. Serve with tortillas, salsa, sour cream, and all your favorite toppings.

3. Pork and Beans

Combine canned pork with baked beans for a comforting and filling dish. Add a bit of brown sugar, mustard, and ketchup for flavor, and simmer on the stove for an easy dinner option.

4. Canned Meat Chili

Turn your canned beef or pork into a delicious chili by adding beans, tomatoes, chili seasoning, and onions. Let it simmer for a while, and you’ll have a hearty and flavorful meal.

Conclusion

Canning meat is a rewarding and practical way to ensure you always have a protein source on hand. Whether you’re looking to save money, prepare for emergencies, or simply enjoy a more convenient way to prepare meals, canning meat offers numerous benefits. With the right tools, techniques, and a little practice, you can start canning meat at home with ease. So, grab your pressure canner, select your favorite cuts of meat, and start preserving today!
