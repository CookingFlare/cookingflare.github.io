Greek-style Roasted Red Pepper Dip: A Vibrant and Flavorful Appetizer


Looking for a vibrant and flavorful appetizer that will wow your guests? Look no further than this Greek-style Roasted Red Pepper Dip! Bursting with Mediterranean flavors, this dip is the perfect addition to any gathering or as a delicious snack for yourself. It’s easy to make, packed with healthy ingredients, and will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 4 large red bell peppers
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the red bell peppers on a baking sheet and roast them for 30-35 minutes, or until the skins are charred and blistered.

  2. Remove the roasted red peppers from the oven and immediately transfer them to a bowl. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let the peppers cool for about 15 minutes. This step will make it easier to remove the skins.

  3. Once the peppers have cooled, peel off the charred skins. Slice the peppers open and remove the seeds and stems. You should be left with soft and tender roasted red pepper flesh.

  4. In a food processor or blender, combine the roasted red peppers, minced garlic, extra-virgin olive oil, feta cheese, Greek yogurt, lemon juice, chopped fresh parsley, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.

  5. Taste the dip and adjust the seasonings to your liking. You can add more lemon juice for a tangier flavor or more feta cheese for a saltier taste.

  6. Transfer the dip to a serving bowl and garnish with a drizzle of olive oil, a sprinkle of chopped parsley, and a few crumbled feta cheese. Serve with pita bread, tortilla chips, or fresh vegetables for dipping.

Tips and Variations

  • For a smoky twist, you can roast the red peppers on a grill instead of in the oven. Simply char them over the open flame until the skins are blackened and blistered.

  • If you prefer a spicier dip, you can add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to the mixture.

  • This dip can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Just make sure to cover it tightly with plastic wrap or transfer it to an airtight container.

  • Feel free to get creative with your garnishes. You can sprinkle some crumbled feta cheese, chopped Kalamata olives, or a drizzle of balsamic glaze on top of the dip for added flavor and visual appeal.

  • This dip is not only perfect as an appetizer but also makes a great spread for sandwiches or wraps. Try spreading it on a toasted bagel or pita bread and top it with some fresh veggies and grilled chicken for a delicious lunch or snack.

So there you have it, a vibrant and flavorful Greek-style Roasted Red Pepper Dip that will surely impress your guests and satisfy your taste buds. With its rich Mediterranean flavors and creamy texture, it’s the perfect appetizer for any occasion. Give it a try and let the flavors transport you to the sunny shores of Greece. Enjoy!
