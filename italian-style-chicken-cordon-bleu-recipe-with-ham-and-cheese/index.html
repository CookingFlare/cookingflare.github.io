Italian-Style Chicken Cordon Bleu Recipe with Ham and Cheese


When it comes to Italian cooking, there’s one dish that stands out from the rest: Chicken Cordon Bleu. This classic recipe typically consists of chicken breast stuffed with ham and cheese, then breaded and fried. But why settle for the ordinary when you can give it an Italian twist?

In this recipe, we’ll be using prosciutto instead of traditional ham, and a blend of mozzarella and parmesan cheese for an extra burst of flavor. We’ll also be skipping the breading and frying, opting for a lighter and healthier approach. So, if you’re ready to give your taste buds a Mediterranean makeover, let’s get started!

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 slices of prosciutto
  • 1/2 cup of fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of grated parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). While that’s heating up, let’s start by preparing our chicken breasts. Season them with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Lay out your chicken breasts and place a slice of prosciutto on each one. You can trim the prosciutto to fit the chicken breast if needed.
  3. Add a slice of mozzarella cheese on top of the prosciutto. You can use as much or as little cheese as you like, but we recommend using a generous amount to get that gooey, melty goodness.
  4. Sprinkle some grated parmesan cheese over the mozzarella. This will add a nice salty flavor to balance out the sweetness of the cheese.
  5. Add a pinch of minced garlic to each chicken breast. You can never go wrong with a little garlic, amico!
  6. Drizzle a tablespoon of olive oil over the top of each chicken breast. This will help keep the chicken moist and add a richness to the dish.
  7. Roll up each chicken breast and secure with toothpicks. Make sure the prosciutto and cheese are tucked in nicely, and the chicken is rolled up tightly.
  8. Place the chicken breasts seam-side down in a baking dish. You can arrange them in a single layer or overlap them slightly, depending on your preference.
  9. Bake the chicken for 25-30 minutes, or until it reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C). You can check the temperature by inserting a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the chicken.
  10. Remove the chicken from the oven and let it rest for 5-10 minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute and the cheese to set.
  11. Serve the chicken hot, garnished with chopped parsley if desired. You can serve it with a side of pasta, roasted vegetables, or a simple green salad.

Tips and Variations:

  • To make this recipe more substantial, you can add some sautéed spinach or mushrooms to the chicken breast before rolling it up.
  • If you prefer a crisper exterior, you can brush the chicken with some olive oil and broil it for an additional 2-3 minutes after baking.
  • This recipe is perfect for a special occasion or a dinner party. You can also make it ahead of time and refrigerate or freeze it for later use.

Conclusion:

And there you have it, amici! A delicious Italian-Style Chicken Cordon Bleu recipe with ham and cheese that’s sure to impress your family and friends. With its creamy cheese, salty prosciutto, and tender chicken, this dish is a true taste of Italy. So go ahead, give it a try, and let us know what you think!
