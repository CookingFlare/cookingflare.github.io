Irresistible Caramel Apple Pie: A Fall Favorite


Fall is in the air, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a delicious homemade apple pie? But this isn’t just any apple pie – it’s an irresistible caramel apple pie that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. With its flaky crust, tender apples, and gooey caramel, this pie is a true showstopper. So, let’s get started and dive into the recipe that will make your fall dessert dreams come true.

Ingredients

  • 2 9-inch pie crusts (store-bought or homemade)
  • 6-7 medium-sized apples (Granny Smith or Honeycrisp work well)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup caramel sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Optional: whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place one of the pie crusts into a 9-inch pie dish and set aside.

  2. Peel, core, and thinly slice the apples. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and lemon juice. Toss everything together until the apples are evenly coated.

  3. Pour the apple mixture into the pie crust in the dish, making sure to evenly distribute the apples. Drizzle the caramel sauce over the top, making beautiful swirls of caramel throughout the pie. Dot the butter pieces on top of the apples.

  4. Cover the pie with the second pie crust. You can create a lattice pattern, cut out shapes, or simply cover the entire pie with the crust and make a few slits to allow steam to escape. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  5. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg wash. This will give your pie a beautiful golden color as it bakes.

  6. Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. If the edges of the crust start to brown too quickly, cover them with foil to prevent burning.

  7. Once the pie is done baking, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set and make it easier to slice.

  8. Serve your irresistible caramel apple pie warm or at room temperature, and if desired, top it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. The creamy sweetness of the caramel paired with the tartness of the apples will create a flavor explosion that will leave everyone wanting more!

There you have it – a recipe for an irresistible caramel apple pie that will make your taste buds sing. This fall favorite is the perfect dessert to enjoy on a cool autumn evening or to impress your guests at a holiday gathering. So, grab your apron, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create a mouthwatering masterpiece that will have everyone asking for seconds. Happy baking!
