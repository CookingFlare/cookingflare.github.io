Nut-Free Manuscripts: Uncover 45 Lost Nut-Free Recipes while Cooking


Are you tired of the same old nut-filled recipes that may allergic friends can’t partake in? Fear not because we have unearthed 45 hidden gems of nut-free recipes! Brace yourselves as you read through this treasure of culinary creations, designed to bring a smile to not just nut-allergic friends but every food lover.

1. Golden Beet Risotto

Smooth, creamy, and bursting with vibrant colors, the golden beet risotto is a recipe that transports you to the world of Italian nostalgia. The natural sweetness of the beets pairs brilliantly with a sharp Pecorino Romano cheese. Serve it with an extra drizzle of olive oil and some freshly cracked black pepper for that finishing touch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Golden beets, peeled and diced
  • 2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 2 cups vegetable broth, warm
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup Pecorino Romano cheese, grated

Procedure:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-based pot. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for another minute to release the aroma. You don’t want to burn it.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice to coat it in the mixture. Then add the white wine, letting it simmer until fully absorbed.
  4. Add the diced beets (and be sure to keep an eye on their texture). Continue to cook while stirring frequently.
  5. Gradually mix in the warm vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring between each addition until all liquid is absorbed. Wait until the rice reaches a creamy consistency.
  6. Stir in the grated Pecorino Romano cheese and season with salt, pepper, and an extra drizzle of olive oil.
  7. Cook for one more minute while making sure everything melds together beautifully.

2. Chilled Avocado Cucumber Soup

For a refreshing chilled starter, try the avocado-cucumber soup - perfect for those hot, lazy summer days. This creamy blend of fresh ingredients, pureed to perfection, takes just twenty minutes to make and delivers a satisfying, refreshing experience.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe Hass avocados, halved and pitted
  • 1 English cucumber
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 small red onion
  • 1 jalapeño pepper
  • 2 large tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Procedure:

  1. In a food processor, combine the halved avocados, cucumber, garlic, red onion, jalapeño pepper, tomatoes, lime juice, and salt. Blend until completely smooth.
  2. Check the flavor, adjust the seasoning if needed, and transfer the mixture to a large bowl.
  3. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, pressing the wrap directly onto the surface of the soup to prevent browning.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, up to 2 hours, to allow the flavors to marinate together.

3. Cumin-Spiced Sweet Potato Fries

A delicious twist to an all-time favorite, this sweet potato fries recipe combines heart-healthy potatoes, spices, and cumin, making them a must-try for any snack lover.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, washed and cut into 1/4-inch thick fries
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Procedure:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a large, rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, toss together the sweet potatoes, olive oil, cumin, paprika, and salt.
  3. Spread the fries out onto the prepared baking sheet in a single layer, making sure they’re not overcrowded.
  4. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until the fries are crisp on the outside and tender on the inside.

Enjoy cooking these nut-free recipes with your friends and family, ensuring everyone can partake in the endless offerings from a world of flavors!
