Savor the Spice with Dairy-Free Mexican Stuffed Peppers


If you’re a fan of Mexican cuisine and are looking for a flavorful and dairy-free dish, then you’re in for a treat! Our dairy-free Mexican stuffed peppers are not only a feast for the taste buds but also a healthy and vibrant addition to your dinner table. Packed with a colorful array of wholesome ingredients and a perfect balance of spices, these stuffed peppers are sure to become a family favorite. Let’s dive into the recipe and savor the spice together!

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers (assorted colors for visual appeal)
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 1 ¾ cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes, drained
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Avocado Crema:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • ½ cup coconut cream
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Peppers: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers, remove the seeds and membranes, and place them upright in a baking dish.

  2. Cook the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic, sautéing until softened. Stir in the quinoa and toast for a couple of minutes. Pour in the vegetable broth, bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 15 minutes or until the quinoa is cooked and the broth is absorbed.

  3. Prepare the Filling: In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Mix well to ensure the flavors are evenly distributed.

  4. Stuff the Peppers: Spoon the quinoa and vegetable mixture into the prepared bell peppers, packing them tightly. Place the pepper tops back on if desired.

  5. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender but still hold their shape.

  6. Make the Avocado Crema: While the peppers are baking, blend the avocados, coconut cream, lime juice, minced garlic, and salt in a food processor or blender until smooth and creamy.

  7. Serve: Once the stuffed peppers are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Drizzle the avocado crema over the peppers before serving.

Savor the Spice

These dairy-free Mexican stuffed peppers are a celebration of vibrant flavors, wholesome ingredients, and the rich tapestry of Mexican cuisine. The combination of quinoa, black beans, and a medley of spices creates a satisfying and nutrient-packed filling, while the creamy avocado crema adds a luxurious touch that perfectly complements the heat of the peppers.

Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a satisfying weeknight meal, these stuffed peppers are a fantastic way to savor the spice of life. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the aromas of Mexico, and indulge in a dish that’s as delightful to make as it is to eat. Enjoy every bite, and let the flavors transport you to the heart of Mexico!

We hope you enjoy preparing and savoring these dairy-free Mexican stuffed peppers as much as we do. The combination of wholesome ingredients, vibrant flavors, and a touch of spice is sure to make this dish a standout on your dinner table. Happy cooking and savoring the spice of life!
