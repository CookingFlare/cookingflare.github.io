Exploring Vegan Desserts from Around the World


Desserts are a universal language of sweetness that transcends borders, cultures, and culinary traditions. For those who follow a vegan lifestyle, the world of desserts may seem limited at times, but the truth is that vegan desserts can be just as diverse, delicious, and indulgent as their non-vegan counterparts. In this blog post, we will embark on a delightful journey, exploring vegan desserts from various corners of the globe. From rich chocolate treats to fruity delights, you’ll discover recipes that showcase the creativity and variety of vegan baking.

1. Coconut Pudding from the Philippines: Leche Flan

Traditionally made with eggs, the Filipino dessert leche flan is creamy and rich. Our vegan version uses coconut milk and agar-agar to achieve a similar luscious texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon agar-agar powder
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, coconut sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch and agar-agar with a few tablespoons of water to form a slurry.
  3. Slowly add the slurry to the coconut milk mixture, stirring continuously. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until thickened.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Pour the mixture into a mold and refrigerate for at least 4 hours until set. Serve chilled.

2. Japanese Mochi Ice Cream

Mochi is a delightful Japanese treat made from glutinous rice. This vegan version combines mochi with your favorite plant-based ice cream for an indulgent dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Cornstarch for dusting
  • 2 cups vegan ice cream (any flavor)

Instructions:

  1. In a microwave-safe bowl, mix the glutinous rice flour, sugar, and water until smooth.
  2. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 1-2 minutes, then stir. Repeat this process until the mixture becomes thick and sticky.
  3. Dust a clean surface with cornstarch and pour the mochi mixture onto it. Let it cool slightly.
  4. Divide the mochi into small portions and flatten each piece. Place a scoop of vegan ice cream in the center and wrap the mochi around it.
  5. Roll the mochi ball in cornstarch to prevent sticking. Freeze for at least 2 hours before serving.

3. Indian Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun, traditionally made with milk solids, can be easily adapted for a vegan diet using plant-based ingredients. These sweet, syrup-soaked balls are sure to impress!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy milk
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon rose water
  • Cardamom powder for flavor

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, almond flour, and baking powder. Gradually add the non-dairy milk to form a soft dough.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  3. Shape the dough into small balls and fry them in hot oil until golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In another saucepan, combine the sugar and water, bringing it to a boil. Add the rose water and cardamom powder.
  5. Soak the fried balls in the syrup for at least 30 minutes before serving.

4. French Chocolate Mousse

Rich, airy, and decadent, this vegan chocolate mousse uses silken tofu as a base, making it incredibly creamy without any dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dark vegan chocolate, melted
  • 1 cup silken tofu, drained
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Blend the silken tofu, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and salt in a food processor until smooth.
  2. Gradually add the melted chocolate and blend until fully combined.
  3. Spoon the mousse into serving glasses and refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

5. Middle Eastern Baklava

Baklava, a sweet pastry made of layers of filo dough, nuts, and syrup, can easily be made vegan by using plant-based butter or oil.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of filo dough (about 16 oz)
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (walnuts, almonds, pistachios)
  • 1 cup vegan butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Finely chop the mixed nuts and combine them with cinnamon and sugar in a bowl.
  3. Layer half of the filo dough in a greased baking dish, brushing each sheet with melted vegan butter.
  4. Spread the nut mixture evenly over the filo layers. Top with the remaining filo, again brushing each layer with melted butter.
  5. Cut into diamond shapes and bake for 30-35 minutes until golden.
  6. While baking, prepare the syrup by boiling the sugar, water, maple syrup, and vanilla extract together for about 10 minutes.
  7. Pour the syrup over the baklava once it has cooled slightly. Allow it to absorb for a few hours before serving.

6. Brazilian Brigadeiros

Brigadeiros are beloved Brazilian chocolate truffles traditionally made with condensed milk. Our vegan version uses coconut milk for a delicious twist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 1 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • Chocolate sprinkles for coating

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, cocoa powder, and coconut sugar. Cook over medium heat, stirring continuously until thickened (about 10-15 minutes).
  2. Once the mixture pulls away from the sides of the pan, remove it from heat and let it cool.
  3. Once cool, grease your hands with a little coconut oil and shape the mixture into small balls.
  4. Roll the balls in chocolate sprinkles and place them in mini cupcake liners. Enjoy your brigadeiros chilled or at room temperature.

7. Thai Mango Sticky Rice

This traditional Thai dessert is naturally vegan and celebrates the simplicity of sticky rice paired with ripe mango and a creamy coconut sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sticky rice
  • 1 1/2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the sticky rice and soak it in water for at least 4 hours.
  2. Steam the soaked rice for about 30 minutes until tender.
  3. In a saucepan, combine coconut milk, sugar, and salt, heating gently until dissolved.
  4. Mix half of the coconut sauce with the cooked sticky rice.
  5. To serve, place a portion of sticky rice on a plate, top with mango slices, and drizzle with the remaining coconut sauce.

8. Italian Panna Cotta

Traditionally made with cream, panna cotta can be made vegan using coconut cream and agar-agar for a delightful dessert that sets beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon agar-agar powder
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine coconut cream, sugar, and agar-agar. Heat gently until the agar-agar dissolves completely.
  2. Stir in the vanilla extract and pour the mixture into molds.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours until set. Serve with fresh berries on top.

Conclusion

Exploring vegan desserts from around the world reveals a treasure trove of flavors, textures, and culinary traditions. Whether you’re indulging in the creamy delight of coconut pudding from the Philippines, the rich, decadent chocolate mousse from France, or the sweet, syrupy goodness of Brazilian brigadeiros, there’s a world of vegan desserts waiting to be discovered. With creativity and a willingness to try new ingredients, anyone can enjoy these international treats while adhering to a vegan lifestyle. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the joy of cooking, and take your taste buds on a global adventure!
