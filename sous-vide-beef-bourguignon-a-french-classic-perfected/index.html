Sous Vide Beef Bourguignon: A French Classic Perfected


If you’re a fan of French cuisine, you’ve likely heard of Beef Bourguignon. This classic dish hailing from the Burgundy region of France is a hearty, flavorful stew that has become a beloved staple in kitchens around the world. Traditionally, Beef Bourguignon is prepared by braising beef in red wine, but today, we’re going to take a modern approach by using the sous vide method to achieve unparalleled tenderness and flavor infusion. Get ready to elevate your culinary skills and impress your taste buds with this Sous Vide Beef Bourguignon recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into cubes
  • 4 slices thick-cut bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Beef

  1. Season the beef cubes with salt and pepper, then place them in a sous vide bag.
  2. Seal the bag using the water displacement method to ensure the beef is fully submerged.

Step 2: Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 135°F (57°C).
  2. Gently lower the sealed bag of beef into the water bath and cook for 24 hours. This extended cooking time will allow the beef to become incredibly tender and absorb the flavors of the other ingredients.

Step 3: Sautéing the Aromatics

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced bacon and cook until crispy.
  3. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and garlic, sautéing until they begin to soften.

Step 4: Deglazing and Simmering

  1. Pour the red wine into the skillet, using a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom.
  2. Stir in the tomato paste and beef broth, then add the thyme and bay leaves.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the liquid to reduce slightly.

Step 5: Finishing the Dish

  1. Once the beef has finished cooking in the sous vide bath, carefully remove it from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels.
  2. Toss the beef in flour to lightly coat it, then sear it in a hot skillet until it develops a golden crust.
  3. Add the beef to the simmering sauce and let it cook for an additional 10 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 6: Serving

  1. Discard the thyme sprigs and bay leaves from the stew.
  2. Serve the Sous Vide Beef Bourguignon hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley.

Conclusion

Sous Vide Beef Bourguignon offers a unique twist on the traditional French dish, delivering melt-in-your-mouth tenderness and rich, complex flavors. By utilizing the precision of sous vide cooking, you can achieve a level of perfection that will elevate your culinary repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a comforting meal, this recipe is sure to impress. So, don your apron, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the essence of French cuisine in the comfort of your own kitchen. Bon appétit!
