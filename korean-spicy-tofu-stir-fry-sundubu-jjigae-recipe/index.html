Korean Spicy Tofu Stir-Fry (Sundubu Jjigae) Recipe


When it comes to Korean food, few dishes strike a balance between fiery intensity and creamy comfort as perfectly as sundubu jjigae — the iconic Korean spicy tofu stew. Packed with bold flavors, nourishing ingredients, and a satisfying heat that warms both your stomach and soul, this dish is loved not just in Korea but all around the world. While its traditional preparation leans toward soups and stews, today we’re putting a twist on the classic by adapting it into a flavorful spicy tofu stir-fry. This recipe retains the deep, delicious essence of sundubu jjigae while turning it into a versatile dish you can enjoy with rice, noodles, or even on its own.

What Makes Sundubu Jjigae Special?

Sundubu jjigae (순두부찌개) is traditionally a spicy Korean soft tofu stew made with fresh silken tofu, vegetables, fiery gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), and a rich, umami-packed broth. With its combination of spice, richness, and comforting textures, it has earned a special place at family dining tables. This dish highlights signature Korean ingredients like kimchi, soy sauce, sesame oil, and green onions, resulting in an explosion of harmonious flavors. By turning this dish into a stir-fry, we retain its comforting essence while creating something quick, versatile, and perfect for busy weeknights.

Why Opt for a Stir-Fry Version?

While sundubu jjigae is traditionally more soup-like, our stir-fry adaptation is equally satisfying and even quicker to make. Plus, it’s an excellent way to enjoy all the classic flavors without the need to simmer a rich broth for long hours. By pan-frying tofu and combining it with bold spices and fresh ingredients, the stir-fry delivers the irresistible textures and flavors you’re craving in less time. Whether you’re preparing a simple lunch or feeding a crowd, this dish is bound to impress.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To achieve the authentic flavors of Korean spicy tofu stir-fry, gather these essential ingredients:

For the Stir-Fry Base

  • Firm or Silken Tofu (400g): Choose silken tofu for a creamy texture or firm tofu for slightly more bite, depending on your preference.
  • Vegetable Oil (2 tbsp): For frying the tofu and sautéing the aromatics.

Essential Aromatics

  • Garlic (4 cloves, minced): Adds depth and a savory kick to the dish.
  • Ginger (1-inch piece, minced): Brings warmth and enhances the flavor profile.
  • Green Onions (2 stalks): Finely chopped for garnish and flavor.

Korean Spices and Flavorings

  • Gochugaru (Korean Red Chili Flakes) (1 tbsp): The star ingredient that delivers the spicy kick.
  • Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste) (2 tbsp): Adds richness and a smooth heat to the stir-fry.
  • Soy Sauce (2 tbsp): Balances the spice with savory umami.
  • Sesame Oil (1 tsp): Introduces a nutty aroma and rounds out the dish.
  • Rice Syrup or Sugar (1 tsp): A touch of sweetness to temper the heat.

Fresh Vegetables and Extras

  • Carrot (1, julienned): Adds crunch and sweetness.
  • Zucchini (1, sliced thinly): Brings a tender texture and mild flavor to balance the spice.
  • Kimchi (½ cup): Provides tanginess and depth essential to Korean cuisine.
  • Mushrooms (1 cup, sliced): Shiitake or button mushrooms work well here.

Step-by-Step Cooking Instructions

1. Prepare the Ingredients

Start by draining your tofu carefully and cutting it into bite-sized cubes or rectangles. For silken tofu, be gentle to preserve its delicate texture. Set the tofu aside and prepare the vegetables by slicing them finely for easy stir-frying.

2. Make the Sauce

In a bowl, combine gochugaru, gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and rice syrup (or sugar). Whisk everything together to create a bold, flavor-packed sauce. This will coat your tofu and vegetables perfectly later on.

3. Pan-Fry the Tofu

Heat a large skillet or wok over medium heat and add the vegetable oil. Once hot, carefully place the tofu pieces in the skillet. Fry them for about 2-3 minutes on each side until they’re lightly golden and crisp on the surface. Remove the tofu and set it aside — this will help prevent it from breaking while you add the other ingredients.

4. Sauté the Aromatics

Using the same skillet, add your minced garlic, ginger, and half of the green onions. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds, allowing the aromatics to release their fragrance.

5. Cook the Vegetables

Add the julienned carrots, sliced zucchini, mushrooms, and kimchi to the skillet. Stir frequently, coating the vegetables with the aromatic base. Cook for 4-5 minutes until they are tender yet still vibrant in color.

6. Add the Sauce and Tofu

Lower the heat slightly and pour the prepared sauce over the vegetables, stirring well to coat them evenly. Gently add the tofu back into the skillet and toss everything together carefully to avoid breaking the tofu. The tofu will soak up the sauce, amplifying its flavor. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to let the tofu slightly caramelize and the flavors meld together.

7. Garnish and Serve

Turn off the heat and transfer the spicy tofu stir-fry to a plate. Garnish with the remaining green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for the final touch. Serve immediately alongside steamed white rice, hot noodles, or simply enjoy it as is.

Tips for Perfect Korean Spicy Tofu Stir-Fry

  • Control the Spice Level: Adjust gochugaru and gochujang quantities to match your spice tolerance. For a milder version, reduce the chili paste and flakes slightly while adding extra vegetables for balance.
  • Optional Protein: Want more substance? Add thinly sliced beef, chicken, or seafood while cooking the vegetables for a heartier dish.
  • Double the Sauce: If you like your stir-fry saucier, increase the sauce quantities proportionately to enjoy that signature Korean flavor in every bite.

Sundubu Jjigae Stir-Fry as a Meal Idea

This Korean-style spicy tofu stir-fry is not just limited to being a standalone dish. Pair it with classic Korean side dishes like banchan, such as stir-fried spinach (sigeumchi namul), pickled radishes, or seasoned bean sprouts. Add a generous scoop of kimchi to your plate for a truly authentic dining experience.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

By combining silken tofu’s melt-in-your-mouth texture, vibrant vegetables, and bold flavors of Korean spices, this spicy tofu stir-fry is a feast for both your palate and your senses. It’s simple enough to make on a weeknight yet impressive enough to serve to guests. Whether you’re craving comfort food with a kick or looking to explore Korean cuisine without committing to a full stew, this recipe hits just the right mark.

Give this dish a try and let the harmonious flavors of sundubu jjigae inspire your cooking adventures! You’ll find yourself coming back to this vibrant recipe time and again.
