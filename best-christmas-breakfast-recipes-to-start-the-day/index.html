Best Christmas Breakfast Recipes to Start the Day


The magic of Christmas morning begins in the kitchen, where the aroma of freshly made breakfast fills the house and creates a warm, inviting atmosphere. Whether you’re waking up with the family or hosting friends, a festive and hearty Christmas breakfast sets the perfect tone for a joyful day. If you want to impress your loved ones with delicious, satisfying dishes that combine tradition with a creative twist, you’ve come to the right place. Below, discover a collection of carefully curated breakfast recipes that are perfect for Christmas morning, guaranteed to bring smiles, comfort, and a touch of holiday cheer to the table.

1. Cinnamon Swirl Dutch Baby with Berry Compote

Start Christmas morning with a showstopper: a beautifully puffed Dutch Baby pancake bursting with warming cinnamon and nutmeg, paired with a fresh berry compote that balances sweetness with a hint of tartness.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • For the berry compote:
    • 2 cups mixed berries (blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
    • 1/4 cup sugar
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Place a 10-inch cast iron skillet or oven-safe pan inside the oven to heat.
  2. In a blender or mixing bowl, combine eggs, milk, flour, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt until smooth.
  3. Remove the hot skillet from the oven and add the butter, swirling to coat the bottom as it melts.
  4. Pour the batter into the skillet and immediately bake for 20 minutes or until the Dutch Baby is puffed and golden brown around the edges.
  5. While it bakes, prepare the berry compote by combining berries, sugar, lemon juice, and vanilla in a saucepan over medium heat. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the mixture thickens slightly (about 10 minutes). Remove from heat and set aside.
  6. When the Dutch Baby finishes baking, serve immediately with a generous spoonful of berry compote on top and a dusting of powdered sugar if desired.

This dish not only tastes delicious but also brings an element of elegance and fun to your Christmas breakfast table. The bright berries and warm spices evoke the season perfectly.

2. Savory Maple and Sage Breakfast Sausage Casserole

For those who prefer a savory start, this maple- and sage-infused sausage casserole offers a satisfying blend of flavors with a cozy texture. It’s easy to assemble ahead of time, so you can spend less time morning prep and more time enjoying the festivities.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage (pork or turkey)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup pure maple syrup
  • 6 cups cubed day-old bread (such as sourdough or challah)
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet over medium heat, cook sausage, breaking it into small pieces, until browned and cooked through. Remove sausage and set aside. In the same skillet, sauté onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, maple syrup, sage, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add the cooked sausage, sautéed onion and garlic, cubed bread, and shredded cheddar cheese to the bowl. Stir gently until bread is soaked but still holds its shape.
  5. Transfer the mixture into a greased 9x13 inch baking dish and bake for 45-50 minutes until set and golden on top.
  6. Let cool for a few minutes before serving.

The combination of maple syrup and sage creates a harmony of sweetness and herbaceous warmth that complements the hearty sausage and bread. This casserole is a perfect Christmas morning comfort food.

3. Cranberry Orange Oatmeal Pancakes with Whipped Cream

Pancakes are a Christmas breakfast tradition in many households. This festive cranberry orange oatmeal pancake recipe brings out zesty citrus and bursts of tangy berries, elevated with a cloud of lightly sweetened whipped cream.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 1/2 cups milk (or plant-based alternative)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries (or frozen, thawed)
  • For whipped cream:
    • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
    • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, soak oats in milk for 15 minutes.
  2. In a separate large bowl, whisk flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Once oats are soaked, add eggs, melted butter, orange zest, and vanilla. Stir to combine.
  4. Mix wet ingredients with dry ingredients gently, then fold in cranberries.
  5. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease.
  6. Pour 1/4 cup batter for each pancake onto the skillet. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for another 2 minutes or until golden.
  7. For whipped cream, beat heavy cream, powdered sugar, and vanilla at medium-high speed until soft peaks form.
  8. Serve the warm pancakes stacked high, topped with whipped cream and additional fresh cranberries or a drizzle of maple syrup.

The nuttiness from the oatmeal and brightness from orange and cranberries make these pancakes unforgettable. Perfect for a joyful, energetic Christmas morning.

4. Classic Eggs Benedict with a Twist: Smoked Salmon and Hollandaise

Elevate the classic Christmas breakfast with a luxurious take on Eggs Benedict featuring silky smoked salmon, perfectly poached eggs, and rich, tangy hollandaise sauce infused with a hint of dill.

Ingredients:

  • 4 English muffins, split and toasted
  • 8 slices smoked salmon
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • For hollandaise sauce:
    • 3 large egg yolks
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and warm
    • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh dill sprigs, for garnish

Directions:

  1. Fill a wide skillet with about 3 inches of water and bring to a gentle simmer. Add vinegar.
  2. Crack eggs one at a time into a small bowl and gently slip them into the simmering water. Poach for about 3-4 minutes until whites are set but yolks remain runny. Use a slotted spoon to remove eggs and drain on paper towels.
  3. Prepare the hollandaise sauce by whisking egg yolks and lemon juice in a heatproof bowl placed over simmering water, ensuring the bowl doesn’t touch the water. Whisk continuously until beginning to thicken.
  4. Slowly drizzle in melted butter while whisking until sauce is thick and creamy. Remove from heat and stir in chopped dill, salt, and pepper.
  5. Assemble the Eggs Benedict by layering toasted English muffins, smoked salmon, and poached eggs. Spoon hollandaise over the top and garnish with dill sprigs.

This sophisticated dish is both visually stunning and delectably satisfying, perfect for a Christmas celebration that calls for indulgence.

5. Spiced Apple and Pecan Stuffed French Toast Bake

Stuffed French toast transforms into a holiday feast with apples, warming spices, and crunchy pecans baked to perfection. This dish is the perfect cozy centerpiece for Christmas morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf brioche or challah bread, sliced thickly (about 12 slices)
  • 3 large apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • 8 large eggs
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Butter for greasing baking dish
  • Powdered sugar, for garnish

Directions:

  1. In a skillet, sauté apples with cinnamon, nutmeg, brown sugar, and pecans until apples soften slightly and caramelize (about 8 minutes). Let cool.
  2. Butter a 9x13 inch baking dish. Lay half the bread slices in the dish.
  3. Spread the cooled apple and pecan mixture over the bread, then top with remaining bread slices.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk eggs, milk, maple syrup, and vanilla. Pour evenly over the bread, pressing gently to soak all slices.
  5. Cover and refrigerate overnight or at least 4 hours.
  6. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake uncovered for 45-50 minutes, until custard is set and the top is golden.
  7. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

This overnight bake is ideal for a leisurely Christmas morning where you want your oven to do the work while you relax and soak up seasonal moments.

6. Hot Chocolate and Gingerbread Muffins

No Christmas breakfast spread is complete without a sweet treat. These moist and flavorful hot chocolate muffins with a hint of gingerbread spice perfectly capture the essence of the holidays.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup mini chocolate chips
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, sift together flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, salt, and spices.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together brown sugar, buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla.
  4. Gradually add wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring just until combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Divide batter evenly into muffin cups and bake for 18-20 minutes or until toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool before serving.

Pair these muffins with a warm cup of spiced coffee or creamy hot chocolate for a festive treat that will have everyone reaching for seconds.

Wrapping Up Your Christmas Morning Feast

Choosing the right recipes to start your Christmas morning can turn an ordinary day into an extraordinary celebration. These recipes blend bold seasonal flavors, comforting textures, and a festive spirit to spark joy around your breakfast table. Whether you crave something sweet, savory, or nestled firmly in tradition with a modern flair, these dishes invite you to savor the anticipation and delight of the holiday season.

Preparing these recipes ahead where possible will set you free to relax and enjoy the day with your family and friends. Remember, Christmas breakfast is about warmth, togetherness, and the pleasure of good food shared. So, pick your favorites from this list, gather your ingredients, and let the holiday magic begin—one delicious bite at a time.

Happy cooking and Merry Christmas!
