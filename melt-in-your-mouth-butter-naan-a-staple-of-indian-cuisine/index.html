Melt-in-Your-Mouth Butter Naan: A Staple of Indian Cuisine


Naan, a traditional Indian bread, is an essential accompaniment to many Indian dishes. With its soft, pillowy texture and rich buttery flavor, naan has become a favorite not only in India but also around the world. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to make melt-in-your-mouth butter naan that will elevate your Indian culinary experience to new heights.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • Extra melted butter for brushing

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, baking powder, and baking soda. Mix well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the plain yogurt, milk, melted butter, and warm water. Slowly pour this mixture into the dry ingredients while stirring continuously. Keep stirring until a soft dough forms.

  3. Transfer the dough onto a clean, floured surface and knead it for about 5 minutes. The dough should be smooth, elastic, and slightly sticky. If the dough feels too dry, add a little more warm water, a tablespoon at a time.

  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover it with a damp cloth. Allow the dough to rest for at least 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.

  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball. Roll each ball into a circular shape, approximately 1/4 inch thick.

  6. Heat a skillet or a tawa over medium-high heat. Place one rolled naan on the skillet and cook for about 1 minute, or until bubbles start to form on the surface. Flip the naan and cook for another minute, or until it puffs up and develops light brown spots.

  7. Remove the naan from the skillet and brush it with melted butter. Place the cooked naan in a clean kitchen towel to keep it warm. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.

  8. Serve the melt-in-your-mouth butter naan hot with your favorite Indian curry or as a standalone snack. The naan pairs exceptionally well with creamy butter chicken, flavorful tikka masala, or even a simple dal.

Tips and Variations

  • For a garlic naan variation, finely mince 2-3 cloves of garlic and add them to the dough before kneading. This will give your naan a delightful garlic flavor.
  • If you prefer a softer texture, you can add a tablespoon of plain yogurt or milk to the dough while kneading.
  • Naan can be made in a traditional tandoor, but a skillet or tawa works just as well. Make sure the skillet is well-heated before cooking the naan to achieve the perfect texture.
  • To keep your naan warm and soft, stack them in a covered container or wrap them in a clean kitchen towel.

Now that you have the recipe for melt-in-your-mouth butter naan, you can recreate the authentic taste of Indian cuisine in your own kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a delicious bread to accompany your meal, this recipe will surely impress. Enjoy the softness, the buttery flavor, and the rich cultural heritage that naan brings to your dining experience.
