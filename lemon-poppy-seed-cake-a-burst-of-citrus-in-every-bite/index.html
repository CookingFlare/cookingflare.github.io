Lemon Poppy Seed Cake: A Burst of Citrus in Every Bite


If you’re a fan of citrus flavors, then this Lemon Poppy Seed Cake recipe is a must-try. Bursting with tangy lemon goodness and the delicate crunch of poppy seeds, this cake is a delightful treat for any occasion. Whether you’re hosting a brunch, celebrating a special event, or simply craving a sweet and zesty dessert, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch round cake pan or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and poppy seeds. Set aside.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This can be easily done using an electric mixer.

  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Mix until well combined.

  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the buttermilk. Begin and end with the dry ingredients, mixing just until incorporated. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense cake.

  6. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Tap the pan gently on the counter to remove any air bubbles.

  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The cake should be golden brown and spring back when lightly touched.

  8. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Then, transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.

  9. Once the cake has cooled, you can serve it as is or dust it with powdered sugar for an added touch of sweetness. For an extra lemony kick, you can also drizzle a simple lemon glaze over the top.

Serving Suggestions

This Lemon Poppy Seed Cake is perfect on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of your favorite ice cream. It pairs wonderfully with a hot cup of tea or coffee, making it an excellent choice for an afternoon treat.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra moist texture, you can substitute some of the butter with sour cream or Greek yogurt.
  • Feel free to add more lemon zest if you prefer a stronger lemon flavor.
  • If you don’t have buttermilk on hand, you can make a homemade substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to 1/2 cup of milk. Let it sit for a few minutes before using.
  • To make a lemon glaze, mix powdered sugar and lemon juice until you reach your desired consistency. Drizzle it over the cooled cake for a glossy finish.

Conclusion

Lemon Poppy Seed Cake is a classic dessert that never fails to impress. With its bright citrus flavor and delightful texture, it’s no wonder this cake has stood the test of time. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply indulging in a slice of heaven, this recipe is a surefire way to satisfy your cravings. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and let the aroma of lemon fill your kitchen as you create a cake that’s as beautiful as it is delicious. Enjoy!
