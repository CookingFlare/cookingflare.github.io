Classic Moroccan Couscous with Vegetables


Everyone loves a flavorful meal that is both filling and nutritious, and when it comes to Middle Eastern cuisine, Moroccan couscous is a standout dish that never fails to impress. A staple in Moroccan cuisine, couscous is a delicious and versatile North African staple that's a key part of any traditional meal. Couscous is essentially small, round particles made from crushed durum wheat semolina, giving it a delicate yet toothsome texture. To cook it is an art itself, carefully balancing the water temperature and steaming technique to ensure that the couscous comes out fluffy and light. In this recipe, we'll combine the fluffy couscous with a rich array of vegetables, salty spices, and tangy herbs to create a culinary experience that will transport you straight to the bustling streets of Marrakech. Servings: 4-6 people, Prep Time: 15 minutes, Cook Time: 40 minutes. Ingredients:

1 cup couscous 4 cups water 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 onion, finely chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 pound mixed vegetables (such as carrots, zucchini, bell peppers, and tomatoes) 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon paprika 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions Step 1: Preparation and Cooking In a large heat-resistant pot, warm 2 cups of water over a medium heat and bring it to a rolling boil. In another pot, carefully rinse the couscous and add the remaining 2 cups of water. Let it soak for a few minutes before steaming it. How to Steam the Couscous

Place a steamer basket over a saucepan filled with boiling water. Rinse the couscous carefully and drain off excess water. Relax and cover it with a clean dish towel. Steam it for 15-20 minutes, or until light and fluffy. Every few minutes, gently mix and mash the couscous gently to prevent it from lumps and sticking together.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables As the couscous is steaming, prepare the mixed vegetables by peeling, washing and chopping them according to desired size. In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the minced garlic and cook for 2 minutes or until softened. Next, add the mixed vegetables to the pan and cook until they tender, about 6-8 minutes. Adding Flavor to the Vegetables Add the cumin, paprika, salt, ground cinnamon, and black pepper to the pan and mix well. Be cautious not to burn the spices as you cook them. Step 3: Mixing the Dish To prepare the couscous, once it's cooked, carefully unmold the dish onto a large serving platter or plate. Mixing the Vegetables and Couscous Add the cooked vegetables to the harissa and artistically arrange the mixture on top of the couscous. Sprinkle the mixed greens over the top of the couscous mixture to add a pop of color and freshness to the dish. Lastly, decorate the dish with the chopped cilantro, for an added flair and authenticity. Serve and Enjoy Classic Moroccan Couscous with Vegetables is perfect for serving at family gatherings, dinner parties, or even your regular weeknights. Try a simple red wine pairing for an added touch of indulgence. Enjoy this simple yet mouthwatering dish that brings the taste and authenticity of Morocco right to your plate.

Serve the Challenge Try your hand at making the classic Moroccan couscous with vegetables at home. We promise you it's worth the effort and very healthy dish thrives on all occasions.
