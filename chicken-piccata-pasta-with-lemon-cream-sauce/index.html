Chicken Piccata Pasta with Lemon Cream Sauce


Chicken Piccata Pasta with Lemon Cream Sauce is a fusion dish that beautifully marries the bright, zesty essence of classic piccata with the creamy indulgence of pasta. Packed with flavor and easy enough to prepare for a weeknight dinner, this recipe brings fresh ingredients together to create a meal that is both comforting and sophisticated. Whether you’re hosting guests or treating yourself to something special on a quiet evening, this dish is bound to impress.

A Harmonious Blend of Flavors

The magic of Chicken Piccata Pasta lies in its balance of flavors. Tender, golden-brown chicken cutlets form the foundation of the dish, seasoned to perfection and lightly sautéed until juicy yet crispy at the edges. Pair this with al dente pasta and bathe it in a tangy lemon cream sauce that whispers notes of garlic and dry white wine.

The capers provide an unmistakable briny bite, while freshly squeezed lemon juice brightens the dish, complementing the creaminess of the sauce in a way that feels light and refreshing. Add fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese, and you’ve turned a simple supper into an elevated dining experience—without the hassle.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Chicken

  • 2 large chicken breasts, sliced into thin cutlets
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

For the Lemon Cream Sauce

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or chicken broth
  • Juice and zest of 2 lemons
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tablespoons capers, rinsed and drained
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Pasta

  • 12 oz spaghetti or linguine, cooked according to package instructions
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Additional Parmesan cheese for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Chicken

Begin by slicing the chicken breasts horizontally to create thin cutlets. This ensures the chicken cooks quickly and evenly. In a shallow dish, combine the flour, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Dredge each piece of chicken in the mixture, shaking off excess flour.

In a large skillet, heat olive oil and one tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Once the butter melts and the pan is hot, add the chicken fillets. Cook each side for 3–4 minutes or until golden brown and fully cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside, tenting loosely with foil to keep it warm.

2. Build the Lemon Cream Sauce

Using the same skillet, melt three tablespoons of butter over medium-low heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the dry white wine or chicken broth, scraping up the browned bits at the bottom of the skillet—this is where much of the flavor resides.

Lower the heat slightly and stir in the juice and zest of two lemons. Allow the mixture to simmer for 2–3 minutes. Next, slowly pour in the heavy cream, whisking continuously to create a smooth sauce. Add the grated Parmesan cheese and capers, stirring until the cheese melts and the sauce thickens slightly. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

3. Cook and Incorporate the Pasta

While the sauce simmers, boil the pasta in a large pot of salted water according to the package instructions. Aim for al dente—firm yet tender pasta yields the best texture when paired with creamy sauces.

Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and add it directly to the skillet with the lemon cream sauce. Toss well to coat the noodles evenly, allowing the pasta to soak up the flavors of the sauce.

4. Assemble the Dish

Slice the cooked chicken into thin strips and distribute it over the sauced pasta. Sprinkle fresh parsley and additional grated Parmesan cheese generously over the top. For added brightness, you can squeeze a touch more lemon juice over the dish or garnish with thin lemon slices.

Pro Tips for Perfect Chicken Piccata Pasta

  • Pasta Variety: While spaghetti or linguine work beautifully, you can experiment with fettuccine or tagliatelle for a fun variation.
  • Make It Lighter: Substitute half-and-half for heavy cream to reduce the richness of the sauce without compromising its silkiness.
  • Double the Citrus: If you’re a lemon lover, consider adding a third lemon for an extra burst of zing.

Pairing Suggestions

To complement the citrusy tang of Chicken Piccata Pasta, serve it alongside a crisp green salad dressed with olive oil and balsamic vinegar. A warm, crusty baguette is perfect for soaking up the extra sauce on your plate. As for beverages, a chilled glass of Pinot Grigio or Sauvignon Blanc balances the creamy texture and acidity of the dish with its refreshing notes.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, add a splash of chicken broth or heavy cream to the pasta as you warm it in a skillet over low heat. This prevents the sauce from drying out and helps bring back its silky consistency.

A Crowd-Pleasing Favorite

Chicken Piccata Pasta with Lemon Cream Sauce is the kind of recipe that transforms everyday ingredients into extraordinary meals. The combination of tender chicken, tangy lemon cream sauce, and perfectly cooked pasta is undeniably comforting and packed with flavor. Whether served as a family-style dish or plated for a formal gathering, this recipe is guaranteed to keep everyone reaching for a second helping.

From its delicate notes of garlic and wine to the bold twists of lemon and capers, this pasta embodies everything that’s delightful about cooking: fresh ingredients, thoughtful preparation, and a final product that feels both familiar and exciting. Prepare to make this dish a staple in your home kitchen. Satisfaction is always on the menu!
