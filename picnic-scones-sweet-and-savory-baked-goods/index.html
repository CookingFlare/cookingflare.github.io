Picnic Scones: Sweet and Savory Baked Goods


Picnics are the perfect opportunity to enjoy the great outdoors and indulge in delicious treats. And what better way to enhance your picnic experience than with a batch of freshly baked picnic scones? These versatile baked goods can be made in both sweet and savory variations, making them a crowd-pleaser for everyone. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering picnic scone recipes that are sure to elevate your next outdoor gathering.

Classic Plain Scones

Let’s start with the classic plain scone recipe. These scones are light, fluffy, and have a delicate buttery flavor. Perfect for pairing with clotted cream and jam, they are a quintessential addition to any picnic spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold butter to the dry ingredients and use your fingertips or a pastry cutter to cut it into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix together the milk and vanilla extract. Gradually add this mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick round.
  6. Using a round biscuit cutter, cut out scones from the dough and place them on the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each scone.
  7. Brush the tops of the scones with the beaten egg for a shiny golden finish.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the scones are lightly golden.
  9. Transfer the scones to a wire rack to cool before serving.

Savory Cheese and Herb Scones

For those who prefer a savory twist to their picnic treats, these cheese and herb scones are a must-try. Packed with flavors from cheddar cheese and aromatic herbs, they are perfect on their own or as a base for sandwiches.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as chives, parsley, or rosemary)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cold butter to the dry ingredients and use your fingertips or a pastry cutter to cut it into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and chopped fresh herbs.
  5. Gradually add the milk to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  6. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together. Pat the dough into a 1-inch thick round.
  7. Using a round biscuit cutter, cut out scones from the dough and place them on the prepared baking sheet, leaving some space between each scone.
  8. Brush the tops of the scones with the beaten egg for a shiny golden finish.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the scones are lightly golden.
  10. Allow the scones to cool on a wire rack before serving.

Conclusion

Picnic scones are the epitome of portable and delicious baked goods. Whether you prefer them sweet or savory, these scones are sure to be a hit at your next outdoor gathering. Experiment with various flavors and pairings to create your own unique combinations. So, pack your picnic basket, grab a blanket, and enjoy the delightful experience of indulging in freshly baked scones amidst nature’s beauty. Happy picnicking!
