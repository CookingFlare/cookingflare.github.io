Fermentation and Mindful Eating: Nourishing Body and Soul


Fermentation, a process as old as time itself, has gained increasing attention in recent years as a way to enhance the flavor, nutrition, and digestibility of foods. Mindful eating, the conscious practice of paying attention to the process of preparing, consuming, and enjoying food, promotes a deeper connection with our bodies and the environment. The union of fermentation and mindful eating delivers not just a meal, but an experience to nourish both body and soul.

Sourdough Bread: The Star of Fermentation

Sourdough bread, a staple in many households, showcases the magic of fermentation. The process begins by cultivating a starter culture consisting of yeast and lactic acid bacteria, which feed on sugars and produce carbon dioxide, alcohol, and lactic acid. This results in a tangy, slightly sweet bread with improved digestibility due to the presence of beneficial bacterias.

Ingredients:

  • 250g bread flour
  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 150g active sourdough starter
  • 350ml water
  • 10g salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine the active sourdough starter and water in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Gradually add the bread flour, all-purpose flour, and salt while mixing until a cohesive dough forms.
  3. Knead for 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and shape into a round loaf.
  5. Place the dough in a floured proofing basket and let it rest in a warm, draft-free area for 3-8 hours, allowing it to rise and ferment.
  6. Preheat the oven to 450°F (232°C) with a Dutch oven inside.
  7. Remove the proofing basket from the oven, invert it onto a floured surface, and gently shake the dough out onto a round shape.
  8. Score the top of the bread with a sharp blade and transfer it to the hot Dutch oven.
  9. Steam the bread for 30 minutes before removing the lid and continuing to bake it for an additional 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  10. Cool the bread on a wire rack before slicing.

Miso-Sweet Potato Soup: Immunity-Boosting Fusion

Miso-sweet potato soup combines the earthy flavors of miso paste with the comforting sweetness of potatoes. This soup serves as a tasty Reset Button for the body, replenishing lost nutrients and enhancing immunity.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small sweet potato (200g)
  • 1 sunchoke (150g)
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 500ml vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp doenjang (Korean fermented soybean paste) or 2 tbsp miso paste
  • 1/2 cup silicon-rich mineral water
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel and chop the sweet potato and sunchoke into small cubes.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat and add the chopped vegetables. Let them cook until soft and golden.
  3. Meanwhile, peel and crush a clove of garlic and add it to the pot.
  4. Pour in the vegetable broth, being mindful of preservation seaweed or dried mushrooms for added minerals.
  5. Allow the mix to simmer until everything blends perfectly.
  6. Add doenjang or miso paste to the pot and stir it thoroughly. It enhances the flavor and nutrients.
  7. Blend the contents of the pot and reheat. Add more vegetable broth if it’s too thick. Adjust salt and black pepper to perfection.
  8. Finish with a swirl of mineral water before serving.

The World of Fermented Foods

Fermented foods extend beyond bread and soup, offering a diverse range of options to incorporate into your daily diet.

  • KIMCHI (Korean pickled vegetables): A spicy and tangy mixture of napa cabbage and radish, often seasoned with Korean chili paste, garlic, and fish sauce.

  • SAUERKRAUT (German pickled cabbage): Characterized by a mild tang, sauerkraut is a fermented cabbage beloved for its versatility in dishes.

  • TEMPEH (Indonesian fermented soybean cake): Nutritious and protein-rich, tempeh pairs well with a variety of flavors.

  • KEFIR (Cultured milk): A yogurt-like drink, kefir contains live probiotics that help digestion and overall gut health.

Fermentation and mindful eating unite to deliver a gratifying culinary experience. By nurturing our inner microbiome and savoring each step in the process, we can enhance our relationship with food and the world around us. Embrace the art, science, and passion of fermentation, and delight in the journey from plate to palate.
