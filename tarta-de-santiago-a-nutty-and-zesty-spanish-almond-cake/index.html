Tarta de Santiago: A Nutty and Zesty Spanish Almond Cake


Welcome to our delightful culinary journey! Today, we’re exploring the flavors of Spain with a traditional and mouthwatering dessert: Tarta de Santiago. This nutty and zesty Spanish almond cake is a true delight for the senses, and its rich history adds an extra layer of charm to every bite.

The Origins of Tarta de Santiago

Tarta de Santiago originates from the beautiful region of Galicia in northern Spain. Legend has it that the recipe was created by Franciscan monks in the 16th century, making it a dessert with centuries of heritage. This cake holds a special place in the heart of the Galician people, as it is often enjoyed during festive occasions, such as the feast day of St. James.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To create your own Tarta de Santiago, gather these simple yet flavorful ingredients:

  • 250g ground almonds
  • 200g sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

The Method

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan and line the bottom with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground almonds, sugar, lemon zest, orange zest, and cinnamon. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs until they are well combined. Then, gradually add the beaten eggs to the almond mixture. Stir gently until a smooth batter forms.

  4. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan, ensuring it is spread evenly.

  5. Place the cake pan in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 40 minutes, or until the cake is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

  6. Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and allow it to cool completely in the pan.

  7. Once cooled, carefully transfer the cake to a serving plate. Dust the top of the cake generously with powdered sugar, creating a beautiful contrast of white against the golden brown cake.

  8. Your Tarta de Santiago is now ready to be enjoyed! Serve it as is or accompany it with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgence.

A Cake with Character

Tarta de Santiago not only delights the taste buds but also captivates with its unique appearance. Traditionally, the top of the cake is adorned with the cross of St. James, which is created by dusting powdered sugar over a stencil. This adds a touch of elegance to the cake and pays homage to its historical and cultural significance.

Embrace the Flavors of Spain

Tarta de Santiago is a true testament to the culinary heritage of Spain. With its nutty aroma, zesty citrus notes, and moist texture, this cake is a delightful representation of the country’s diverse flavors. Whether you’re hosting a Spanish-themed dinner party or simply want to indulge in a slice of culinary history, Tarta de Santiago is the perfect choice.

So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and embark on a journey to Spain with this exquisite Tarta de Santiago. The rich flavors and cultural significance of this almond cake are sure to leave a lasting impression on both your taste buds and your heart. ¡Buen provecho!
