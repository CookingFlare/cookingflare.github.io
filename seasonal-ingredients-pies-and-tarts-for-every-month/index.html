Seasonal Ingredients: Pies and Tarts for Every Month


Baking is an art that beautifully merges flavors, textures, and seasonal ingredients. Each month offers unique fruits, vegetables, and herbs that can elevate your pies and tarts to extraordinary heights. As the seasons change, so does the palette of ingredients available to us, allowing home bakers to explore a world of flavors and create delightful treats that reflect the time of year. In this guide, we will walk through delicious pie and tart recipes for each month, showcasing the best seasonal ingredients that can inspire your baking adventures.

January: Citrus Tart

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ cup powdered sugar
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 1 large egg yolk
    • 1-2 tablespoons cold water

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup fresh orange juice
    • Zest of 2 oranges
    • 2 large eggs
    • ½ cup granulated sugar
    • ¼ cup heavy cream

Instructions:

  1. Make the crust: In a mixing bowl, combine flour and powdered sugar. Add the chilled butter and mix until crumbly. Stir in the egg yolk and add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Press the dough into a tart pan and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the crust for 15 minutes or until lightly golden.
  3. Prepare the filling: Whisk together orange juice, zest, eggs, sugar, and cream in a bowl. Pour the mixture into the baked crust.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes until set. Let it cool before serving. Garnish with whipped cream if desired.

February: Chocolate Raspberry Tart

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ cup cocoa powder
    • ½ cup powdered sugar
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 1 large egg yolk
    • 2-3 tablespoons cold water

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup heavy cream
    • 8 ounces bittersweet chocolate, chopped
    • 1 cup fresh raspberries
    • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Combine flour, cocoa powder, and powdered sugar in a bowl. Add chilled butter and mix until crumbly. Stir in the egg yolk and add cold water as needed. Press into a tart pan and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the crust for 15 minutes, then let it cool.
  3. Make the filling: Heat the cream in a saucepan until it begins to simmer. Remove from heat and add chopped chocolate, stirring until smooth. Mix in vanilla extract.
  4. Assemble the tart: Pour the chocolate filling into the cooled crust, then arrange fresh raspberries on top. Chill for at least 2 hours before serving.

March: Apple and Elderflower Pie

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 6 cups thinly sliced apples (Granny Smith and Honeycrisp work well)
    • ½ cup sugar
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 2 tablespoons elderflower cordial
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Make the crust: In a food processor, pulse flour, salt, and sugar. Add butter and pulse until crumbly. Gradually add ice water until dough forms. Divide into two disks and chill for at least 1 hour.
  2. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disk for the bottom crust and place it in a pie dish.
  3. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, combine sliced apples, sugar, flour, elderflower cordial, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Fill the pie crust with the apple mixture.
  4. Top with the second crust, seal the edges, and cut slits for ventilation. Bake for 45-50 minutes until golden brown. Cool before serving.

April: Rhubarb Custard Pie

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 5-6 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 3 cups chopped rhubarb
    • 1 cup sugar
    • ½ cup heavy cream
    • 2 large eggs
    • ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 3 tablespoons cornstarch

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Cut in butter until crumbly, then add ice water until the dough holds together. Roll out and fit into a pie dish. Chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the crust for 15 minutes until lightly browned.
  3. Make the filling: In a bowl, mix rhubarb, sugar, heavy cream, eggs, vanilla, and cornstarch. Pour the filling into the pre-baked crust.
  4. Bake for 45-50 minutes until the filling is set and the rhubarb is tender. Allow to cool before slicing.

May: Strawberry Lemonade Tart

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
    • ¼ cup sugar

  • For the filling:

    • 1 cup fresh strawberries, puréed
    • ½ cup lemon juice
    • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk
    • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
    • 1 tablespoon gelatin dissolved in 2 tablespoons water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: Mix graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar in a bowl. Press into a tart pan and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the filling: In a mixing bowl, combine strawberry purée, lemon juice, sweetened condensed milk, and lemon zest. Add the dissolved gelatin and mix until smooth.
  3. Pour into the crust and chill for at least 4 hours until firm.
  4. Garnish with fresh strawberries before serving.

June: Blueberry Peach Galette

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 4-5 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups fresh blueberries
    • 2 cups sliced peaches
    • ½ cup sugar
    • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Make the crust: Combine flour and salt in a bowl. Cut in butter until crumbly, then add ice water until a dough forms. Chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Roll out the dough into a large circle on parchment paper.
  3. Prepare the filling: In a bowl, mix blueberries, peaches, sugar, cornstarch, vanilla, and lemon juice. Spoon the filling into the center of the dough, leaving a border.
  4. Fold the edges over the filling, brush with egg wash, and bake for 30-35 minutes until golden brown. Serve warm.

July: Summer Berry Pie

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon sugar
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups strawberries, hulled and sliced
    • 1 cup blueberries
    • 1 cup raspberries
    • ¾ cup sugar
    • ¼ cup cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the crust: In a food processor, combine flour, salt, and sugar. Add butter and pulse until crumbly. Gradually add ice water until the dough forms. Divide into two disks and chill.
  2. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disk for the bottom crust and place it in a pie dish.
  3. Make the filling: In a mixing bowl, combine all berries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Fill the pie crust with the berry mixture.
  4. Top with the second crust, seal the edges, and cut slits for ventilation. Bake for 45 minutes until the filling is bubbly. Cool before serving.
