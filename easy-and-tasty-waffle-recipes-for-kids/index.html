Easy and Tasty Waffle Recipes for Kids


Waffles are a beloved breakfast treat that both kids and adults enjoy. The crisp exterior and soft, fluffy interior make them a perfect canvas for various toppings and flavors. If you’re looking for easy and tasty waffle recipes that your kids will love, you’ve come to the right place. In this blog post, we’ll explore three delicious waffle recipes that are sure to delight your little ones.

1. Chocolate Chip Waffles

Who can resist the combination of chocolate and waffles? These chocolate chip waffles are a crowd-pleaser and are incredibly easy to make. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

To make the chocolate chip waffles, start by preheating your waffle iron. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In another bowl, beat the eggs and then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Gently fold in the chocolate chips.

Pour the batter onto the preheated waffle iron, spreading it out evenly. Cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions until the waffles are golden brown and crispy. Serve the chocolate chip waffles with a dusting of powdered sugar or a drizzle of maple syrup for an extra touch of sweetness.

2. Banana Nut Waffles

If your kids love bananas, they’ll adore these banana nut waffles. Packed with natural sweetness and a crunchy texture, these waffles are a healthy and delicious option. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups buttermilk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts

To make the banana nut waffles, start by preheating your waffle iron. In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, brown sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In another bowl, mix the mashed bananas, eggs, buttermilk, and melted butter. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Fold in the chopped walnuts.

Pour the batter onto the preheated waffle iron, spreading it out evenly. Cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions until the waffles are golden brown and crisp. Serve the banana nut waffles with a dollop of Greek yogurt and a sprinkle of cinnamon for a delightful breakfast treat.

3. Berry Blast Waffles

If you’re looking to add a burst of fruity flavor to your kids’ breakfast, these berry blast waffles are the answer. Loaded with fresh berries, these waffles are colorful, nutritious, and absolutely delicious. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup mixed berries (such as blueberries, raspberries, and strawberries)

To make the berry blast waffles, preheat your waffle iron. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. In another bowl, beat the eggs and then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Gently fold in the mixed berries.

Pour the batter onto the preheated waffle iron, spreading it out evenly. Cook according to the manufacturer’s instructions until the waffles are golden brown and crispy. Serve the berry blast waffles with a drizzle of honey or a dollop of whipped cream for a delightful burst of fruity goodness.

Conclusion

Waffles are a versatile and delicious breakfast option that kids of all ages can enjoy. Whether you prefer the classic chocolate chip, the fruity berry blast, or the nutritious banana nut waffles, these recipes are sure to be a hit at your breakfast table. Give them a try and watch your kids’ faces light up with delight. Happy cooking!
