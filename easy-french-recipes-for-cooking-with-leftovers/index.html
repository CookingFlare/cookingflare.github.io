Easy French Recipes for Cooking with Leftovers


Cooking with leftovers is an art form that not only reduces food waste but also saves you time and money. There's no better way to breathe new life into last night's roast chicken or the remainder of the steak from dinner than by introducing a dash of French chic to your kitchen. French cuisine, known for its simplicity, elegance, and rich flavors, is an ideal choice for repurposing leftover ingredients. With a few simple tricks and the right approach, you can transform leftover meat, vegetables, and cheese into exquisite dishes that wouldn't look out of place in a Parisian bistro. Easy Re-Envisioned Stews and Braises Stews and braises are quintessentially French, and they're perfect for using up leftover meat and vegetables. A lovely way to start is by making a reblochon and mushroom stew. If you have leftover roast vegetables, add them to the pot to enhance the flavor and texture. Similarly, a French oven-braised beef shin en croute can be made by using leftover beef neck or shin from a previous roast. Brown the leftover meat, then cover it with flour, melted butter, and leftover vegetables, topping it off with a crumbly croissant crust. Follow the French adherence to a slow cooking method, simmering the dish over low heat until the meat is tender and falls apart with the touch of a fork. To take this concept further, try whipping up a Paris-bonda-style stew. The Seines Saint Germain-inspired recipe originates from Paris, using leftover roasted meat, vegetables, and foraged wild argros (can actually be left over baby carrots and celery) RN bouquets tossed. You can easily prepare this for the family or a dinner party by combining sautéed onions, garlic, and mushrooms in stock or bouillon with whatever leftover meat or vegetables you have on hand.
