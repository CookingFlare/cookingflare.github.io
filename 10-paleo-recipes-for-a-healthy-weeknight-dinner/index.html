10 Paleo Recipes for a Healthy Weeknight Dinner


When life gets busy, dinner might be the last thing on your mind, but nourishing meals shouldn’t take a backseat. Whether you’re trying to stay on track with paleo living or looking for wholesome, grain-free options, these 10 Paleo recipes will keep your weeknight dinners fresh, nutritious, and satisfying. From hearty proteins to vibrant veggies, these dishes are simple yet packed with flavor, perfect for busy evenings.

1. Garlic Herb Lemon Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs are a staple ingredient in paleo cooking, offering plenty of flavor and versatility. For this recipe, season bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs with a zesty mix of minced garlic, fresh lemon juice, dried oregano, and thyme. Sear them in a cast-iron skillet until crispy and golden, then finish in the oven for a tender, juicy bite. Serve alongside roasted asparagus or a fresh spinach salad.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken thighs (4-6 pieces)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in a cast-iron skillet over medium heat and sear chicken thighs skin-side down until golden.
  3. Flip the thighs, add garlic, lemon juice, and herbs to the skillet, and transfer to the oven. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until internal temperature reaches 165°F.

2. Zucchini Noodle Bolognese

Swap out traditional pasta for zucchini noodles in this paleo twist on a classic Bolognese. This hearty, veggie-packed dinner combines ground beef, diced tomatoes, and a burst of Italian seasonings. Spiralized zucchini adds freshness and helps keep the meal light yet satisfying.

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat; brown ground beef and set aside.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic in the same skillet until translucent.
  3. Add diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and Italian seasoning, then return ground beef to the mix. Simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Toss zucchini noodles with the sauce just before serving.

3. Sheet Pan Salmon with Roasted Vegetables

This one-pan recipe makes cleanup a breeze and brings out the natural flavors of salmon, sweet potatoes, and green beans. A drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of spices elevates this well-balanced paleo dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, diced
  • 2 cups green beans, trimmed
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange sweet potatoes, green beans, and salmon fillets on a sheet pan.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and season with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Roast for 20-25 minutes, until the salmon is flaky and vegetables are tender.

4. Cauliflower Fried Rice

Satisfy your craving for takeout with paleo-friendly cauliflower fried rice. Packed with colorful veggies, eggs, and your favorite protein (chicken, shrimp, or tofu), this dish is quick and versatile.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium head of cauliflower, riced
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 cup peas
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 lb cooked shrimp or diced chicken
  • 2 tbsp coconut aminos
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet and scramble eggs; set aside.
  2. Add carrots, peas, and cauliflower rice to the skillet; cook for 5-7 minutes until tender.
  3. Stir in coconut aminos, scrambled eggs, and your chosen protein. Cook until heated through.

5. Spaghetti Squash Casserole

This comforting paleo dinner combines spaghetti squash with ground turkey, marinara sauce, and fresh herbs, all baked into a delicious casserole.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large spaghetti squash
  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1 cup marinara sauce (sugar-free)
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Roast spaghetti squash at 400°F (200°C) until tender; shred into strands.
  2. Brown ground turkey in a skillet, then mix with marinara sauce and Italian seasoning.
  3. Layer spaghetti squash, meat mixture, and basil in a baking dish. Sprinkle with nutritional yeast if using.
  4. Bake for 15-20 minutes until heated through.

6. Coconut Curry Shrimp

Creamy, mildly spiced, and brimming with shrimp and vegetables, this coconut curry is as nourishing as it is flavorful. Pair it with cauliflower rice for a complete paleo meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a skillet and sauté broccoli and bell peppers for 5 minutes.
  2. Add coconut milk, curry paste, and shrimp; simmer for 10 minutes until shrimp is cooked through.

7. Stuffed Bell Peppers

Bell peppers stuffed with ground beef, cauliflower rice, and savory spices make an easy weeknight dinner that’s full of nutrition and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers, tops removed and seeds cleared
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 cup cauliflower rice
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Brown ground beef with onion, cauliflower rice, and spices; stuff the mixture into bell peppers.
  3. Arrange in a baking dish, cover with foil, and bake for 30 minutes.

8. Lemon Garlic Shrimp Zoodles

For a lightning-fast meal, pair tender shrimp with zucchini noodles in a buttery lemon-garlic sauce that’s paleo-friendly and utterly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4 medium zucchinis, spiralized
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Melt ghee in a skillet and sauté garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add shrimp and cook until pink, then toss in zucchini noodles and lemon juice. Cook for 2-3 minutes.

9. Herb-Crusted Pork Chops

Pork chops coated with a flavorful blend of almond flour, parsley, and spices provide a crisp crust without the need for breadcrumbs.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bone-in pork chops
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix almond flour, parsley, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a shallow bowl.
  2. Coat pork chops in the mixture and pan-fry in avocado oil until golden and cooked through.

10. Roasted Chicken and Vegetable Medley

A simple yet impressive dish, this combination of roasted chicken and seasonal vegetables is perfect for any weeknight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 2 large carrots, sliced
  • 1 red onion, quartered
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss chicken and vegetables with olive oil and rosemary; arrange on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast for 35-40 minutes, turning halfway, until chicken is fully cooked.

These 10 paleo dinner recipes are perfect for crafting healthy, nourishing meals during the week. With a little planning and a handful of fresh, wholesome ingredients, you can enjoy flavorful dishes that align with your lifestyle while accommodating your busy schedule. Give them a try, and embrace the goodness of paleo living, one weeknight dinner at a time!
