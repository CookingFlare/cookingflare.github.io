Homemade Mixed Berry Jam: A Burst of Summer in a Jar


There’s nothing quite like the taste of summer captured in a jar of homemade mixed berry jam. The vibrant colors, the sweet and tangy flavors, and the delightful aroma all come together to create a burst of sunshine on your breakfast toast or dessert spread. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make your very own batch of homemade mixed berry jam that will bring a taste of summer to your table all year round.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

Instructions

  1. Start by washing and hulling the strawberries, if using. Then, prepare the rest of the berries by rinsing them under cold water and patting them dry gently with a paper towel.

  2. In a large, non-reactive saucepan, combine the mixed berries, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Stir gently to combine and let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to allow the berries to release their natural juices.

  3. Place the saucepan over medium-high heat and bring the mixture to a boil, stirring occasionally. Once it reaches a rolling boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the mixture has thickened and has a jam-like consistency.

  4. While the jam is simmering, prepare your jars by washing them in hot, soapy water and sterilizing them by placing them in a pot of boiling water for a few minutes. Make sure to also sterilize the lids separately.

  5. Once the jam has reached the desired consistency, remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly. Then, carefully ladle the hot jam into the sterilized jars, leaving about 1/4 inch of headspace at the top. Wipe the rims of the jars clean with a damp cloth to ensure a proper seal.

  6. Place the lids on the jars and tighten them securely. If you’re planning to store the jam for a longer period, you can also process the jars in a water bath canner for 10 minutes to ensure proper sealing.

  7. Allow the jars to cool completely at room temperature before storing them in a cool, dark place. The jam will continue to thicken as it cools.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different combinations of berries, depending on what’s in season or what you have on hand. You can also add a handful of fresh herbs like mint or basil for a unique twist.

  • If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can use an immersion blender or a food processor to puree the berries before cooking them.

  • This mixed berry jam is not only perfect on toast or scones but also makes a delicious topping for yogurt, ice cream, or even as a filling for cakes and pastries.

  • To give your jam a longer shelf life, make sure to use proper canning techniques and store the jars in a cool, dark place. Unopened jars can last up to one year, while opened jars should be consumed within a month and stored in the refrigerator.

So, why settle for store-bought jams when you can capture the essence of summer in your own homemade mixed berry jam? With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of time, you can enjoy the flavors of the season all year long. So go ahead, grab some fresh berries, and get ready to create a burst of summer in a jar!
