Baked Chicken Parmesan: A Healthier Twist on a Comfort Classic

There’s nothing quite like the satisfaction of indulging in a classic comfort food dish, but what if you could enjoy that same comforting flavor with a healthier twist? That’s where our Baked Chicken Parmesan recipe comes in. This delightful dish combines the familiar flavors of traditional Chicken Parmesan with a lighter, baked preparation that’s perfect for those looking to enjoy a healthier alternative without sacrificing taste. Ingredients 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts 1 cup breadcrumbs (you can use whole wheat for added fiber) 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1 teaspoon dried basil 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon onion powder 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 2 eggs 1 cup marinara sauce 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese Fresh basil leaves for garnish Instructions Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a shallow dish, combine the breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan cheese, dried oregano, dried basil, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and black pepper. In another dish, lightly beat the eggs. Dip each chicken breast into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off, then coat the chicken in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere. Place the coated chicken breasts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. Bake the chicken in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) and the coating is golden and crispy. Remove the chicken from the oven and spoon marinara sauce over each breast, then sprinkle with shredded mozzarella cheese. Return the chicken to the oven and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Once done, garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve hot. Why Baked Chicken Parmesan? This Baked Chicken Parmesan recipe offers a healthier alternative to the traditional fried version, making it a great choice for those who are conscious of their dietary intake. By baking the chicken instead of frying it, you can significantly reduce the amount of oil and fat used in the cooking process, without compromising on flavor or texture. ...
