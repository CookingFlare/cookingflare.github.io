Delicious and Creamy Shahi Paneer: Fit for Royalty


If you’re craving a truly luxurious Indian dish that’s fit for royalty, look no further than Shahi Paneer. This rich and creamy vegetarian curry is a staple in Mughlai cuisine and is sure to impress even the most discerning palates. The name “Shahi” itself means royal, and this dish lives up to its regal reputation with its velvety texture, aromatic spices, and indulgent flavor profile.

Ingredients

To create this decadent Shahi Paneer, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 500g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 4-5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Cashew Paste

  • Begin by making a smooth paste of the soaked cashew nuts. You can do this by blending the cashews with a little water until you achieve a creamy consistency. Set this paste aside for later use.

Step 2: Sauté the Onions

  • In a pan, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté them until they turn golden brown. This process is crucial as it imparts a rich color and sweetness to the curry.

Step 3: Add Aromatic Spices

  • Once the onions have caramelized, add the grated ginger and minced garlic to the pan. Sauté the mixture for a few minutes until the raw aroma of the garlic disappears. Then, add the cumin seeds and allow them to sizzle and release their earthy fragrance.

Step 4: Introduce the Tomato Puree

  • Pour in the pureed tomatoes and cook them until the oil begins to separate from the mixture. This indicates that the tomatoes have been cooked down and the rawness has dissipated, resulting in a harmonious base for the curry.

Step 5: Infuse with Cashew Paste and Yogurt

  • Now, it’s time to add the prepared cashew paste and yogurt to the pan. Stir the mixture gently to incorporate the ingredients, creating a luscious and creamy sauce. Allow the flavors to meld together, creating a velvety texture.

Step 6: Spice It Up

  • Once the base has thickened, it’s time to add the garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and cardamom powder. These spices impart warmth, depth, and a hint of heat to the curry, elevating the overall flavor profile.

Step 7: Introduce the Paneer

  • Gently add the cubed paneer to the simmering sauce, ensuring that each piece is coated in the creamy goodness. Be careful not to stir too vigorously, as paneer is delicate and can break apart easily.

Step 8: Finish with Fresh Cream

  • To achieve the signature richness of Shahi Paneer, drizzle in the fresh cream and gently stir it into the curry. The cream adds a luxurious touch and balances the spices, resulting in a truly decadent dish.

Step 9: Garnish and Serve

  • Once the paneer has absorbed the flavors of the sauce, garnish the Shahi Paneer with fresh coriander leaves for a burst of freshness and color. Serve this regal curry hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice for a truly indulgent experience.

Conclusion

Shahi Paneer is a dish that embodies the opulence and grandeur of Mughlai cuisine. Its creamy texture, rich flavors, and aromatic spices make it a standout dish that’s perfect for special occasions or when you simply want to treat yourself to something truly extraordinary. With this recipe, you can recreate the magic of Shahi Paneer in your own kitchen and savor every luxurious bite. Treat yourself and your loved ones to the royal experience of Shahi Paneer, and revel in the delight of indulging in a dish fit for royalty.
