Cooking with Gochujang: Spicy Korean Tofu Recipes


Gochujang, a spicy and flavorful Korean chili paste, has gained popularity in kitchens around the world. Its unique blend of heat and sweetness adds a delicious kick to any dish. If you’re a fan of tofu and crave bold flavors, you’re in for a treat. In this recipe blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering spicy Korean tofu recipes that showcase the versatility of gochujang. Get ready to tantalize your taste buds and discover the wonders of cooking with this fiery condiment.

1. Gochujang-Glazed Tofu Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, and minced garlic. Mix well to create a glaze.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add tofu cubes and cook until lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Remove tofu from the skillet and set aside. In the same skillet, add onion, bell pepper, and broccoli. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. Return the tofu to the skillet and pour the gochujang glaze over the tofu and vegetables. Stir well to coat everything evenly.
  5. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the tofu is heated through and the sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds. Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.

2. Spicy Korean Tofu Stew (Sundubu-jjigae)

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of soft tofu, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 4 shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pot over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sliced onion. Sauté until the onion is translucent.
  2. Add zucchini, carrot, and shiitake mushrooms to the pot. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, and vegetable or chicken broth. Mix well to create a spicy broth.
  4. Pour the spicy broth into the pot and bring to a simmer. Add the cubed tofu and gently stir to distribute the flavors.
  5. Cook for about 5 minutes, until the tofu is heated through and the flavors meld together.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions. Serve hot with a bowl of steamed rice.

3. Gochujang-Marinated Tofu Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of extra-firm tofu, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine gochujang, soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, and minced garlic. Mix well to create a marinade.
  2. Place tofu cubes in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Gently toss the tofu to ensure it is evenly coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight in the refrigerator for more intense flavors.
  3. Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Thread the marinated tofu cubes onto soaked bamboo skewers.
  4. Brush the grill or grill pan with vegetable oil to prevent sticking. Place the tofu skewers on the grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, until nicely charred.
  5. Remove from the heat and serve hot as an appetizer or main dish. Enjoy the spicy kick of gochujang-infused tofu!

These three recipes are just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to cooking with gochujang. Let your creativity soar and experiment with this flavorful Korean condiment in various dishes. From stir-fries to stews, gochujang adds a fiery punch that will leave you craving more. So, grab a jar of gochujang, gather your ingredients, and embark on a spicy culinary adventure with these Korean tofu recipes. Happy cooking!
