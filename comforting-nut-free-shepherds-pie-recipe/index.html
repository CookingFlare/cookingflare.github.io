Comforting Nut-Free Shepherd's Pie Recipe


There’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting shepherd’s pie to satisfy your cravings on a chilly evening. This classic dish is a crowd-pleaser, and with our nut-free twist, it’s perfect for those with allergies or dietary restrictions. So, let’s dive into this delectable Comforting Nut-Free Shepherd’s Pie Recipe!

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup beef or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup frozen corn

For the mashed potato topping:

  • 2 pounds potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and diced carrots, and sauté until they start to soften, about 5 minutes. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  3. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat if necessary.

  4. Stir in the dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and black pepper. Add the tomato paste and mix well to coat the meat and vegetables.

  5. Pour in the beef or vegetable broth, and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cook for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  6. Add the frozen peas and corn to the skillet, and cook for an additional 5 minutes. Remove the skillet from the heat and set aside.

  7. Meanwhile, prepare the mashed potato topping. Place the peeled and cubed potatoes in a large pot of salted water. Bring to a boil and cook until tender, about 15 minutes. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot.

  8. Add the butter and milk to the pot with the potatoes. Mash until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  9. Transfer the beef and vegetable mixture to a 9x13-inch baking dish. Spread the mashed potatoes evenly over the top.

  10. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  11. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to settle and makes it easier to serve.

  12. Serve this comforting nut-free shepherd’s pie hot, and enjoy the hearty flavors and creamy mashed potato topping.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to incorporate other vegetables into the filling, such as diced bell peppers or mushrooms, to add additional texture and flavor.

  • For a vegan version, substitute the ground beef with plant-based protein such as lentils or textured vegetable protein (TVP). Use vegetable broth and swap the butter and milk in the mashed potato topping for vegan alternatives.

  • If you’re a cheese lover, sprinkle some grated cheddar or Parmesan cheese over the mashed potato topping before baking for an extra layer of indulgence.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Reheat in the oven or microwave for a quick and delicious meal.

That concludes our Comforting Nut-Free Shepherd’s Pie Recipe! It’s a timeless dish that brings warmth and comfort to the table with every bite. Whether you’re catering to dietary restrictions or simply looking for a delicious meal, this shepherd’s pie is sure to hit the spot. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let’s get cooking!
