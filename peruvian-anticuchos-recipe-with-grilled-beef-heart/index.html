Peruvian Anticuchos Recipe with Grilled Beef Heart


If you’re ready to embark on a culinary adventure, then Peruvian Anticuchos with Grilled Beef Heart is a dish that will tantalize your taste buds and introduce you to the rich flavors of Peruvian cuisine. Anticuchos, a popular street food in Peru, are skewers of marinated and grilled meat, and the use of beef heart in this recipe adds a unique and delicious twist. Let’s dive into the vibrant world of Peruvian flavors and learn how to create this mouthwatering dish in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Anticuchos Marinade:

  • 1 lb beef heart, cleaned and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/2 cup aji panca paste (Peruvian red pepper paste)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Huacatay Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup fresh huacatay leaves (Peruvian black mint), finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup queso fresco (fresh white cheese), crumbled
  • 1/4 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For Serving:

  • 4 large potatoes, boiled and cut into chunks
  • 4 ears of corn, boiled and cut into rounds

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Beef Heart: In a bowl, combine the aji panca paste, minced garlic, ground cumin, dried oregano, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper to create the marinade. Add the beef heart cubes to the marinade, ensuring each piece is well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for the flavors to meld.

  2. Make the Huacatay Sauce: In a separate bowl, mix the finely chopped huacatay leaves, crumbled queso fresco, evaporated milk, lime juice, and salt. Stir until well combined, then cover and refrigerate until ready to serve.

  3. Skewer the Meat: Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Thread the marinated beef heart cubes onto skewers, then grill for about 5-7 minutes on each side or until the meat is cooked to your desired level of doneness.

  4. Serve and Enjoy: Arrange the grilled beef heart skewers on a platter, accompanied by the boiled potatoes and corn rounds. Drizzle the huacatay sauce over the anticuchos or serve it on the side for dipping.

The Flavors of Peru

The beauty of Peruvian cuisine lies in its diverse and vibrant flavors, influenced by indigenous traditions, Spanish colonial heritage, and immigrant contributions. The Anticuchos with Grilled Beef Heart encapsulates this rich tapestry of flavors, combining the smoky essence of grilled meat with the bold, earthy notes of aji panca and the fresh, herbaceous kick of huacatay.

The marinade infuses the beef heart with a depth of flavor, while the huacatay sauce adds a creamy, tangy element that perfectly complements the richness of the meat. The accompaniments of boiled potatoes and corn provide a satisfying balance to the dish, making it a complete and satisfying meal.
