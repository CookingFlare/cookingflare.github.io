Fourth of July Berry Galette: A Rustic and Sweet Dessert


Are you looking for a delicious and patriotic dessert to celebrate the Fourth of July? Look no further than this Fourth of July Berry Galette! With its rustic charm and sweet flavors, this dessert is sure to impress your family and friends at your Independence Day gathering.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tablespoon coarse sugar (for sprinkling)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt for the crust. Add the cold, cubed butter and using a pastry cutter or your fingers, cut the butter into the flour until you have a coarse crumb texture.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Form the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. While the dough is chilling, prepare the filling. In a separate bowl, combine the mixed berries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss until the berries are coated evenly. Set aside.

  5. Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into a circle, about 12 inches in diameter.

  6. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet. Spoon the berry filling onto the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border around the edges.

  7. Gently fold the edges of the dough over the filling, creating pleats as you go. This rustic look adds to the charm of the galette.

  8. Brush the edges of the dough with the beaten egg wash and sprinkle the coarse sugar over the top.

  9. Bake the galette in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  10. Remove the galette from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Serve warm or at room temperature. Top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • Feel free to experiment with different combinations of berries for the filling. Try adding blackberries or cherries for a unique twist.
  • If you prefer a sweeter galette, you can increase the amount of sugar in the filling to suit your taste.
  • For an extra flavor boost, sprinkle some cinnamon or nutmeg over the filling before folding the edges of the dough.
  • This galette is best enjoyed on the day it is made but can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days.

So there you have it – a delicious and patriotic Fourth of July Berry Galette that is sure to impress your guests. With its rustic appearance and sweet berry flavors, this dessert is the perfect way to celebrate Independence Day. Give it a try and let the flavors explode in your mouth, leaving you craving for more!
