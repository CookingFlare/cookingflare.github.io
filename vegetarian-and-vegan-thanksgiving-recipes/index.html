Vegetarian and Vegan Thanksgiving Recipes


Welcome to our ultimate guide for delicious and satisfying vegetarian and vegan Thanksgiving recipes! Whether you’re looking to celebrate the holiday with plant-based dishes or cater to guests with different dietary preferences, we’ve got you covered with a variety of mouthwatering recipes that will impress everyone at the table. From hearty mains to festive sides and decadent desserts, these recipes are perfect for a bountiful Thanksgiving feast without any meat or animal products.

Vegan Butternut Squash Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Coconut milk (optional for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, vegetable broth, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Simmer until the squash is tender.
  4. Blend the soup until smooth.
  5. Serve hot, drizzled with coconut milk if desired.

Vegetarian Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squashes, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet.
  3. In a bowl, mix quinoa, black beans, bell pepper, corn, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stuff the squash halves with the quinoa mixture.
  5. Bake for 30-40 minutes until the squash is tender.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Vegan Mushroom Wellington

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet vegan puff pastry
  • 2 cups mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegan butter for sautéing

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a pan, sauté the onion and garlic until translucent.
  3. Add the mushrooms, walnuts, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Cook until the mushrooms are tender and most of the moisture has evaporated.
  5. Roll out the puff pastry and spread the mushroom mixture on top.
  6. Roll the pastry into a log, sealing the edges.
  7. Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown.

Vegan Pumpkin Cheesecake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw cashews, soaked overnight
  • 1 can pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp pumpkin pie spice
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Graham cracker crust (vegan)

Instructions:

  1. Blend the soaked cashews, pumpkin puree, coconut oil, maple syrup, pumpkin pie spice, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into the prepared crust.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours until set.
  4. Serve chilled with a dollop of coconut whipped cream.

These vegetarian and vegan Thanksgiving recipes are sure to make your holiday feast a memorable and flavorful experience. Whether you follow a plant-based lifestyle or simply want to add more veggie-centric dishes to your table, these recipes showcase the diversity and deliciousness of meat-free cooking. Enjoy a Thanksgiving filled with gratitude, good food, and happy bellies!
