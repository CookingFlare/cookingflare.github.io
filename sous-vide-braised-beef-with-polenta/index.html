Sous Vide Braised Beef with Polenta


When it comes to hearty, comforting dinners, braised beef paired with creamy polenta always manages to hit the mark. This dish combines melt-in-your-mouth tenderness with rich, savory flavors that are guaranteed to impress your family, friends, or even your own discerning palate. Instead of relying on traditional methods, we’ll harness the precision of sous vide cooking to deliver consistent results that are nothing short of perfection. Pairing this with buttery polenta creates a dish that’s both rustic and refined—ideal for cozy nights or elegant gatherings.

The Magic of Sous Vide Braising

Sous vide is a method of cooking in which food is vacuum-sealed in a bag and submerged into a water bath held at a precisely controlled temperature. For braised beef, this technique allows the meat to retain its natural juices while achieving exceptional tenderness. Gone are the days of worrying about overcooked or underseasoned meat. By setting the right temperature and time for your sous vide, you’re guaranteed consistent results every time.

Moreover, sous vide cooking brings out the deep richness that’s expected of braised beef without sacrificing the essential balance of flavors. Combined with the creamy texture of polenta, this duo is destined to become the centerpiece of your dining experience.

Ingredients for Perfect Sous Vide Braised Beef

Here’s what you’ll need to craft this restaurant-worthy dish:

For the Braised Beef:

  • 2 pounds (about 1 kg) beef chuck roast or short ribs: These cuts deliver incredible flavor when braised.
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef stock or bone broth
  • ½ cup red wine: Choose a dry variety like cabernet sauvignon or pinot noir.
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf

For the Polenta:

  • 1 cup polenta (coarse cornmeal)
  • 3 cups water or chicken stock
  • ½ cup milk
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Before we dive into the step-by-step instructions, ensure you have a sous vide immersion circulator and large zip-top bags or vacuum-sealed bags on hand. These tools are essential for achieving the precision cooking needed for sous vide recipes.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Beef for Sous Vide

Season the beef generously with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper. Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed skillet over medium-high heat and sear the meat on all sides until caramelized. This step creates a golden crust and adds depth of flavor to the final dish.

Once seared, let the beef cool slightly. Transfer it to a sous vide bag along with the onion, garlic, beef stock, red wine, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water immersion method if using a zip-top bag.

Sous Vide Cooking

Set your sous vide immersion circulator to 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare or 145°F (63°C) for medium doneness. Lower the sealed bag into the water bath, ensuring it’s fully submerged, and cook for 24 hours. Yes, this long cook time is necessary to allow the beef’s connective tissues to break down, resulting in luxurious tenderness.

While your sous vide machine works its magic, you can prepare the polenta closer to mealtime.

Preparing Creamy Polenta

Polenta is a simple yet versatile side dish, and its creamy, comfort-food qualities balance the rich, savory nature of braised beef beautifully. Start by bringing the water or chicken stock to a gentle simmer in a medium-sized saucepan.

Gradually whisk in the polenta to prevent clumping. Reduce the heat to low and continue stirring for about 20 minutes, ensuring the mixture doesn’t stick to the pan. Add the milk and butter, stirring until fully incorporated. Finish with grated Parmesan cheese for a silky, decadent texture. Taste and season with salt and pepper as needed.

Cover the polenta to keep it warm until ready to serve.

Finishing the Beef

Once the sous vide cooking time is up, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Save the juices from the bag; you’ll want to reduce them into a flavorful sauce.

Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Remove the beef from the bag, pat it dry with a paper towel, and sear it quickly to refresh the crust. Meanwhile, strain the reserved juices into a saucepan and simmer over medium heat until thickened. You can add a small splash of red wine or a knob of butter for extra richness.

Slice the beef into portion-sized servings and spoon the sauce generously over the top.

Plating and Presentation

To assemble the dish, spoon the creamy polenta onto individual plates or shallow bowls. Top the polenta with the sous vide braised beef slices, ensuring they’re drenched in the luscious sauce. Garnish with a sprig of fresh thyme or rosemary for added elegance.

Pro Tips for Success

  1. Plan Ahead: Sous vide cooking requires time but minimal hands-on effort. Start the beef a day ahead to allow the 24-hour cooking period.
  2. Choose Quality Ingredients: Opt for high-quality beef and freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Your dish will only be as good as the ingredients you use.
  3. Polenta Variations: For added flavor, mix goat cheese, mascarpone, or smoked gouda into the polenta instead of Parmesan.
  4. Wine Pairing: Serve this dish with the same red wine used in the sauce for continuity in flavor.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This sous vide braised beef with polenta is not your average home-cooked meal—it’s a culinary experience. The tenderness achieved through sous vide cooking rivals what you’d find in upscale restaurants, while the creamy polenta adds balance and comfort to the overall dish. By combining precision techniques with timeless flavors, this recipe is a surefire way to elevate your dinner game.

Take the time to savor every bite. After all, food cooked with patience and care deserves to be enjoyed to the fullest.

Whether you’re celebrating the end of a busy week or simply indulging in the joy of cooking, this recipe will quickly become a favorite in your kitchen repertoire.
