Autumn Harvest Salad with Maple Vinaigrette Recipe


As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, there’s no better way to celebrate the arrival of autumn than with a vibrant Autumn Harvest Salad. This delightful dish combines the season’s freshest produce, earthy flavors, and a touch of sweetness from a homemade maple vinaigrette. This salad not only captures the essence of fall but also brings a burst of color and flavor to your table. Whether you’re looking for a side dish for your Thanksgiving feast or a light lunch that embodies the spirit of the season, this recipe is sure to impress.

Ingredients

For the Salad:

  • 4 cups mixed greens (such as arugula, spinach, and kale)
  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and diced
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, trimmed and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes, cubed
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup walnuts, toasted
  • 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1 apple or pear, thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for roasting

For the Maple Vinaigrette:

  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Vegetables

  1. Roast the Butternut Squash and Sweet Potatoes: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). On a baking sheet, spread the diced butternut squash and cubed sweet potatoes. Drizzle with olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Toss to coat evenly. Roast in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly caramelized. Make sure to stir halfway through for even cooking.

  2. Sauté the Brussels Sprouts: In a skillet over medium heat, add a drizzle of olive oil. Once heated, add the thinly sliced Brussels sprouts. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until they become tender and slightly browned. Season with salt and pepper, then remove from heat and let cool.

Step 2: Make the Maple Vinaigrette

In a small bowl or jar, combine the maple syrup, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper. Slowly whisk in the olive oil until the dressing is emulsified and well combined. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste. If you prefer a sweeter vinaigrette, add a little more maple syrup.

Step 3: Assemble the Salad

  1. In a large bowl, layer the mixed greens as the base of your salad.
  2. Add the roasted butternut squash and sweet potatoes, sautéed Brussels sprouts, apple or pear slices, dried cranberries, and toasted walnuts.
  3. Crumble the feta cheese on top for an added creamy texture.
  4. Drizzle the maple vinaigrette generously over the salad, and toss gently to combine. Ensure all the ingredients are coated with the dressing.

Step 4: Serve

This Autumn Harvest Salad can be served immediately or chilled in the refrigerator for up to an hour before serving. It pairs beautifully with grilled chicken or fish, making it a versatile option for your autumn meals. Enjoy this salad as a refreshing and nutritious way to celebrate the flavors of the season.

Nutritional Benefits

This Autumn Harvest Salad is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Let’s explore some of the health benefits of the key ingredients:

  • Mixed Greens: These leafy greens are low in calories and high in vitamins A, C, and K, along with important minerals like iron and calcium.

  • Butternut Squash: Rich in antioxidants and vitamins, butternut squash is a great source of beta-carotene, which is beneficial for eye health. It’s also high in fiber, aiding digestion.

  • Brussels Sprouts: These nutrient-dense vegetables are high in vitamins K and C, and they also contain cancer-fighting properties due to their high levels of glucosinolates.

  • Sweet Potatoes: Another source of beta-carotene, sweet potatoes are also rich in vitamins A and C, and they provide complex carbohydrates that give energy.

  • Walnuts: A powerhouse of omega-3 fatty acids, walnuts promote heart health and provide a good dose of protein, making them a great addition to any salad.

  • Cranberries: Known for their antioxidant properties, cranberries help boost immunity and promote urinary tract health.

  • Feta Cheese: Adding a creamy texture, feta cheese is a good source of calcium and protein, making it a nutritious topping.

Tips for Customization

While this Autumn Harvest Salad is perfect as is, feel free to customize it according to your preferences and seasonal availability. Here are some ideas:

  • Add Protein: Consider adding grilled chicken, turkey, or even chickpeas for a plant-based protein boost.

  • Experiment with Fruits: Try incorporating pomegranate seeds, persimmons, or sliced figs for an extra touch of seasonal sweetness.

  • Switch Up the Nuts: If you’re not a fan of walnuts, try pecans or almonds for a different flavor and texture.

  • Vegan Option: To make this salad vegan, simply omit the feta cheese or substitute it with a plant-based cheese alternative.

  • Herbs and Spices: Fresh herbs like parsley, thyme, or sage can elevate the flavors of the salad. A sprinkle of cinnamon or nutmeg in the vinaigrette can also enhance the autumnal vibe.

Conclusion

This Autumn Harvest Salad with Maple Vinaigrette is a celebration of flavors, colors, and textures that embody the beauty of the fall season. With its vibrant ingredients and healthy benefits, this salad not only nourishes the body but also delights the senses. Whether served at a festive gathering or enjoyed as a weekday lunch, it’s a dish that brings comfort and joy.

So, as you gather around the table this fall, let this Autumn Harvest Salad be a centerpiece of warmth, love, and gratitude. Embrace the season’s bounty and enjoy every delicious bite!
