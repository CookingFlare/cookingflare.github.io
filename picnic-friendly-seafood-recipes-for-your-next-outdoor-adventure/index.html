Picnic-Friendly Seafood Recipes for Your Next Outdoor Adventure


Picnics are a perfect way to enjoy the outdoors, and what better way to elevate your experience than with delicious seafood dishes? Whether you’re lounging on the beach, enjoying a sunny park day, or adventuring on a hiking trail, seafood can bring a refreshing and savory touch to your outdoor meal. The versatility of seafood makes it easy to prepare and pack for your picnic, from grilled shrimp to crab salads, making your outdoor feast both satisfying and healthy.

In this post, we’ll explore some picnic-friendly seafood recipes that will make your outdoor adventure even more delightful. We’ll cover a variety of options, from quick and easy-to-pack dishes to more elaborate preparations that will still be perfect for picnics. Let’s dive in!

1. Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 lemon (zested and juiced)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs like parsley or cilantro (for garnish)
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Shrimp: In a bowl, combine olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the shrimp to the mixture and toss to coat. Let the shrimp marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for a stronger flavor.

  2. Prepare the Skewers: Thread the shrimp onto the soaked wooden skewers. Aim for 4-5 shrimp per skewer, depending on the size of the shrimp.

  3. Grill the Shrimp: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the shrimp are pink and opaque. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley or cilantro before serving.

  4. Pack for Your Picnic: Allow the skewers to cool slightly before packing them for your picnic. Wrap them in foil or parchment paper for easy transportation. These grilled shrimp skewers are delicious on their own or paired with a dipping sauce like garlic aioli.

2. Tuna Salad Wraps

Tuna salad is a classic picnic dish that’s both easy to make and versatile. You can enjoy it in a wrap, sandwich, or even on crackers, but we recommend wrapping it in soft tortillas for a neat and portable option.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of tuna (drained)
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup diced celery
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped dill pickles or relish
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • Fresh lettuce or spinach leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Tuna Salad: In a bowl, combine the drained tuna, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, celery, red onion, and dill pickles. Mix everything together until well combined. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

  2. Assemble the Wraps: Lay out the flour tortillas on a clean surface. Place a few leaves of lettuce or spinach in the center of each tortilla. Spoon a generous amount of tuna salad on top of the greens.

  3. Roll and Wrap: Carefully fold in the sides of the tortilla and then roll it up tightly, ensuring the filling stays inside. Slice the wraps in half, and they’re ready to go.

  4. Pack for the Picnic: Wrap each tuna salad wrap in parchment paper or foil. These wraps hold up well during transportation, making them an ideal choice for a picnic.

3. Smoked Salmon and Cream Cheese Bagels

A smoked salmon bagel is a perfect seafood-based sandwich that’s quick to assemble and offers a great balance of flavors. The creamy cheese pairs wonderfully with the smoky richness of the salmon, creating a satisfying bite.

Ingredients:

  • 4 bagels (your choice of flavor)
  • 8 oz cream cheese (softened)
  • 4 oz smoked salmon (sliced)
  • 1/4 red onion (thinly sliced)
  • Fresh dill or chives (for garnish)
  • Capers (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Bagels: Slice the bagels in half and toast them lightly, if preferred. You can also leave them untoasted for a softer texture, depending on your preference.

  2. Spread the Cream Cheese: Generously spread cream cheese on both halves of the bagels. Be sure to coat each one evenly for the best taste.

  3. Assemble the Bagels: Layer slices of smoked salmon on top of the cream cheese. Add a few thin slices of red onion for a touch of sharpness, and garnish with fresh dill or chives. If you like, add a few capers on top for an extra burst of flavor.

  4. Pack for Your Picnic: To keep your smoked salmon bagels fresh and intact, wrap each one in parchment paper or plastic wrap. These sandwiches are perfect for a quick and delicious picnic lunch.

4. Shrimp and Avocado Salad

If you’re looking for a light and refreshing seafood dish, shrimp and avocado salad is an excellent choice. It’s packed with protein and healthy fats, making it a satisfying yet refreshing option for a warm day outdoors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb cooked shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 2 ripe avocados (diced)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • 1/4 red onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Salad: In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked shrimp, diced avocados, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, and red onion.

  2. Dress the Salad: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, cilantro, salt, and pepper. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.

  3. Pack for the Picnic: To prevent the avocados from browning, squeeze a little extra lemon juice over them before packing the salad. You can store the salad in an airtight container for easy transport.

5. Crab Cakes

Crab cakes are a classic seafood dish that can be made ahead of time and served cold, making them a great picnic option. Their crispy exterior and tender interior will delight your taste buds while keeping things simple and flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb lump crab meat
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 egg (beaten)
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp Old Bay seasoning (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Crab Cake Mixture: In a large bowl, combine the lump crab meat, breadcrumbs, mayonnaise, beaten egg, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, Old Bay seasoning, salt, and pepper. Stir gently to combine, being careful not to break up the crab meat too much.

  2. Form the Crab Cakes: Divide the mixture into 8-10 portions and shape them into patties, about 2-3 inches in diameter.

  3. Cook the Crab Cakes: Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the crab cakes and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, until golden brown and crispy.

  4. Cool and Pack for the Picnic: Allow the crab cakes to cool slightly before packing them for your picnic. Serve them with a tangy dipping sauce like tartar sauce or aioli for an extra burst of flavor.

6. Fish Tacos with Mango Salsa

For a tropical twist on your picnic menu, fish tacos are a great choice. The fresh fish, paired with a zesty mango salsa, brings a burst of flavor that will make your outdoor meal feel like a beachside getaway.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb white fish fillets (such as tilapia or cod)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • 1 cup diced mango
  • 1/2 red bell pepper (diced)
  • 1/4 cup red onion (diced)
  • 1 tbsp cilantro (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp lime juice

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Fish: Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Season the fish fillets with cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Cook the fillets for 3-4 minutes per side, or until fully cooked and easily flaked with a fork. Once cooked, break the fish into small pieces with a fork.

  2. Prepare the Mango Salsa: In a small bowl, combine the diced mango, red bell pepper, red onion, cilantro, and lime juice. Mix gently to combine.

  3. Assemble the Tacos: Warm the corn tortillas in a skillet or microwave. Place a few spoonfuls of the cooked fish on each tortilla, then top with a generous amount of mango salsa.

  4. Pack for the Picnic: Wrap each taco individually in foil or parchment paper for easy transport. These fish tacos are best enjoyed fresh, but they’ll still be delicious after a little time outdoors.

Conclusion

With these picnic-friendly seafood recipes, you can elevate your outdoor adventure with fresh, flavorful, and satisfying dishes. From grilled shrimp skewers to shrimp and avocado salads, these recipes are easy to prepare and perfect for outdoor meals. So, next time you’re planning a picnic, consider adding some seafood to your menu for a delicious and refreshing twist that will make your experience even more memorable. Happy picnicking!
