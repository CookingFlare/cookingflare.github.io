Cantonese-Style BBQ Pork: A Sweet and Savory Delight


If you’re a fan of Chinese cuisine, then you’ve probably come across Cantonese-style BBQ pork at some point. This mouthwatering dish is known for its sweet and savory flavors, tender meat, and crispy exterior. Whether you’re planning a weekend barbecue or simply want to try something new in the kitchen, Cantonese-style BBQ pork is a must-try recipe that will surely impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

To make this delicious dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds of pork shoulder or pork belly
  • 3 tablespoons of hoisin sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of honey
  • 2 tablespoons of Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of white pepper

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the marinade. In a bowl, combine the hoisin sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, honey, Shaoxing wine, minced garlic, Chinese five-spice powder, salt, and white pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  2. Next, prepare the pork shoulder or pork belly. If using pork shoulder, slice it into long strips, about 1-inch thick. If using pork belly, slice it into thin, rectangular pieces. Make sure to remove any excess fat.

  3. Place the pork in a large zip-top bag or a shallow dish, and pour the marinade over it. Ensure that all the pieces are evenly coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap, and let the pork marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight. This will allow the flavors to penetrate the meat and make it more tender.

  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you don’t have a grill, you can also use a grill pan or even bake the pork in the oven.

  5. Once the grill is hot, remove the pork from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off. Reserve the marinade for basting later.

  6. Grill the pork for about 5-7 minutes on each side, or until it is cooked through and has a nice char on the outside. Baste the pork with the reserved marinade occasionally to keep it moist and flavorful.

  7. Once the pork is cooked, remove it from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making it even more tender and succulent.

  8. Slice the Cantonese-style BBQ pork into thin strips and serve it with steamed rice or noodles. You can also garnish it with some chopped green onions or cilantro for added freshness.

Enjoy the Flavors of Cantonese Cuisine

Cantonese-style BBQ pork is a classic dish that showcases the rich flavors of Cantonese cuisine. The combination of sweet and savory, tender meat, and crispy exterior makes it a crowd favorite. Whether you’re hosting a barbecue party or simply want to indulge in some delicious Chinese food at home, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to experience the taste of authentic Cantonese-style BBQ pork.
