Honey Glazed Ham: A Sweet and Succulent Easter Main Course


Easter is a time for family gatherings, joyful celebrations, and, of course, indulgent feasts. One dish that has stood the test of time and remains a centerpiece at many Easter tables is the honey glazed ham. This delectable main course not only tantalizes taste buds with its sweet and savory flavors but also brings warmth and comfort to any holiday meal. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of preparing the perfect honey glazed ham, share some tips and tricks, and even suggest complementary side dishes to elevate your Easter feast.

The Allure of Honey Glazed Ham

Honey glazed ham is more than just a dish; it’s a tradition that many families cherish. The combination of tender, juicy ham with a sweet and sticky glaze creates a harmonious balance that appeals to both children and adults. The rich, smoky flavor of the ham pairs beautifully with the sweetness of honey, making each bite a delightful experience. Whether you’re hosting a large gathering or enjoying a quiet dinner with loved ones, a honey glazed ham is sure to impress.

Choosing the Right Ham

Before diving into the recipe, it’s essential to understand the types of ham available and how to select the best one for your Easter celebration. Here are some popular options:

1. Bone-In Ham vs. Boneless Ham

  • Bone-In Ham: Often considered more flavorful, bone-in ham retains moisture during cooking and offers a rich taste. The bone also adds a beautiful presentation.
  • Boneless Ham: Easier to slice and serve, boneless ham is a great option for those who prefer convenience. However, it may lack some of the depth of flavor found in bone-in varieties.

2. City Ham vs. Country Ham

  • City Ham: Typically wet-cured and smoked, city ham is sweet and tender, making it ideal for glazing.
  • Country Ham: Dry-cured and aged, country ham has a saltier, more intense flavor. While delicious, it may require additional soaking to reduce saltiness before glazing.

A Simple Yet Flavorful Honey Glaze Recipe

Now that you’ve chosen the perfect ham, let’s move on to the star of the show: the honey glaze. This simple recipe combines pantry staples to create a luscious coating that caramelizes beautifully during cooking.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup honey: The star of the glaze, providing sweetness and depth.
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar: Adds richness and enhances the caramelization process.
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard: For a tangy kick that complements the sweetness.
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar: Balances the sweetness and adds a hint of acidity.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon: Infuses warmth and a touch of spice.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves: Adds an aromatic flavor that works beautifully with ham.
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper: Enhances the overall flavor profile.

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Glaze: In a medium saucepan, combine the honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, cinnamon, cloves, and black pepper. Heat over medium heat, stirring until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is well combined. Allow it to simmer for 5-7 minutes until slightly thickened. Remove from heat and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Ham: Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Place the ham in a roasting pan, cut side down. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern, about 1/4 inch deep. This allows the glaze to penetrate the meat and enhances the presentation.

  3. Glaze the Ham: Brush a generous amount of the honey glaze over the surface of the ham. Reserve some glaze for basting later.

  4. Bake the Ham: Cover the ham loosely with aluminum foil and bake it in the preheated oven. The general rule of thumb is to bake for about 15-18 minutes per pound. For example, a 10-pound ham would take approximately 2.5 to 3 hours.

  5. Baste and Glaze: Every 30 minutes, remove the foil and baste the ham with the reserved glaze. This helps create a beautiful, caramelized crust.

  6. Finish Baking: About 30 minutes before the ham is done, remove the foil completely to allow the glaze to caramelize. Keep a close eye on it to prevent burning.

  7. Check for Doneness: The ham is ready when it reaches an internal temperature of 140°F (60°C). Use a meat thermometer for accuracy.

  8. Rest and Serve: Once the ham is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it rest for at least 15 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring each slice is moist and tender.

Serving Suggestions: Complementing Your Honey Glazed Ham

While honey glazed ham is undoubtedly the star of your Easter table, consider these delicious side dishes to create a truly memorable meal:

1. Scalloped Potatoes

Creamy and cheesy scalloped potatoes are the perfect side to balance the sweetness of the ham. Layer thinly sliced potatoes with cheese, cream, and seasonings for a rich and comforting dish.

2. Roasted Asparagus

Bright and vibrant, roasted asparagus drizzled with olive oil and a sprinkle of sea salt adds a lovely freshness to your meal. The slight crunch of the asparagus pairs wonderfully with the tender ham.

3. Honey Glazed Carrots

For a cohesive flavor profile, consider serving honey glazed carrots. Toss baby carrots in a mixture of honey, butter, and fresh herbs, then roast until tender and caramelized.

4. Classic Deviled Eggs

A staple at any holiday gathering, deviled eggs are a crowd-pleaser. The creamy filling complements the savory ham perfectly and adds a touch of nostalgia to your Easter feast.

5. Fresh Green Salad

A light and refreshing green salad with mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, and a tangy vinaigrette can balance the richness of the ham and hearty sides. Add nuts or cranberries for extra texture and flavor.

Storing Leftovers: Making the Most of Your Honey Glazed Ham

If you’re fortunate enough to have leftovers, honey glazed ham can be stored and enjoyed in a variety of ways. Allow the ham to cool completely before wrapping it tightly in plastic wrap or aluminum foil. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Alternatively, you can slice the ham and freeze it for later use. Simply wrap slices in freezer-safe bags, removing as much air as possible before freezing.

Creative Ways to Use Leftover Ham:

  1. Ham and Cheese Sandwiches: Layer slices of honey glazed ham with your favorite cheese and greens for a delicious sandwich.
  2. Omelets or Frittatas: Add diced ham to your breakfast eggs for a protein boost.
  3. Ham Salad: Chop leftover ham and mix it with mayo, mustard, and diced celery for a tasty ham salad.
  4. Soups and Stews: Use ham bones or diced ham in soups for added flavor and depth.

Conclusion

Honey glazed ham is a sweet and succulent main course that resonates with the spirit of Easter celebrations. Its tender, juicy meat enveloped in a luscious glaze creates a dish that is both comforting and indulgent. By following the recipe and tips outlined in this blog post, you can create a stunning centerpiece for your holiday table that will leave your guests raving.

As you gather with loved ones this Easter, remember that the magic of the holiday lies not just in the food but in the shared moments and memories created around the table. So, embrace the joy of cooking, savor each bite, and cherish the time spent with family and friends. Happy Easter!
