Succulent Grilled Mahi Mahi with Pineapple Salsa


Grilled fish is a delightful and healthy option for those looking to add variety to their dinner table. One such delicious dish is Succulent Grilled Mahi Mahi with Pineapple Salsa. This vibrant and flavorful recipe combines the natural sweetness of fresh pineapple with the delicate taste of Mahi Mahi, resulting in a mouthwatering dish that is sure to impress your guests. So, let’s jump right into it!

Ingredients

For the Grilled Mahi Mahi:

  • 4 Mahi Mahi fillets
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Pineapple Salsa:

  • 1 cup fresh pineapple, diced
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and minced
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  2. In a small bowl, combine the olive oil, minced garlic, paprika, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a flavorful marinade for the Mahi Mahi.

  3. Place the Mahi Mahi fillets in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Use your hands to coat the fillets evenly with the marinade. Let them marinate for at least 15 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.

  4. While the fish is marinating, prepare the pineapple salsa. In a mixing bowl, combine the diced pineapple, red bell pepper, red onion, minced jalapeno pepper, lime juice, chopped cilantro, and a pinch of salt. Mix well to ensure all the ingredients are evenly distributed. Set aside.

  5. Once the grill is hot, lightly oil the grates to prevent the fish from sticking. Place the marinated Mahi Mahi fillets on the grill and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Be careful not to overcook the fish, as it can become dry.

  6. Remove the grilled Mahi Mahi from the grill and transfer them to a serving platter. Allow them to rest for a couple of minutes before serving.

  7. To serve, spoon a generous amount of the pineapple salsa over each Mahi Mahi fillet. The combination of the juicy fish and the tangy, sweet salsa will create an explosion of flavors in your mouth.

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have access to a grill, you can also cook the Mahi Mahi fillets in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for about 10-12 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.

  • Feel free to customize the salsa according to your taste preferences. You can add diced avocado, mango, or even some finely chopped jalapeno for an extra kick.

  • Serve the Succulent Grilled Mahi Mahi with Pineapple Salsa alongside a bed of steamed rice or a fresh green salad for a complete and satisfying meal.

Conclusion

Succulent Grilled Mahi Mahi with Pineapple Salsa is a perfect choice for a light and flavorful dinner. The combination of the tender Mahi Mahi fillets and the refreshing pineapple salsa creates a harmonious balance of sweet and tangy flavors. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or simply looking to elevate your weeknight dinner, this recipe is sure to impress. So, fire up your grill and give this succulent dish a try. Your taste buds will thank you!
