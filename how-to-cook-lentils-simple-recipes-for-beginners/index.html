How to Cook Lentils: Simple Recipes for Beginners


Lentils are a wonderfully versatile ingredient that can transform any kitchen novice into a confident cook. Whether you’re looking to add more plant-based protein to your diet, experiment with new flavors, or simply enjoy some hearty dishes, lentils offer an excellent starting point. Packed with nutrients, cost-effective, and quick to prepare, lentils deserve a permanent spot in your cooking repertoire. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the basics of cooking lentils and share simple yet delicious recipes anyone can master.

Getting to Know Lentils

Before diving into recipes, it helps to understand the different types of lentils available:

  • Brown Lentils: The most common variety, brown lentils hold their shape fairly well when cooked and have a mild, earthy flavor.
  • Green Lentils: Slightly firmer than brown, these are ideal for salads or dishes where you want some texture.
  • Red Lentils: These cook very quickly and tend to break down easily, making them perfect for soups, stews, and curries.
  • Black Beluga Lentils: Tiny and shiny, these have a firm texture and are great for presenting in salads or side dishes.
  • Puy Lentils: Also known as French green lentils, they have a peppery flavor and maintain firmness after cooking.

For beginners, starting with brown or red lentils is often the easiest choice due to availability and straightforward cooking methods.

Basic Lentil Cooking Method

Here’s a simple, foolproof way to cook lentils that you can apply to most dishes:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lentils (brown or green recommended for beginners)
  • 3 cups water or broth (vegetable or chicken)
  • 1 bay leaf (optional)
  • Salt (to taste, added after cooking)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the Lentils: Place lentils in a fine mesh sieve and rinse under cold water to remove dust and debris.
  2. Simmer: Add lentils, water or broth, and bay leaf to a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer.
  3. Cook: Let simmer uncovered. Cooking time varies:
    • Brown/green lentils: 20-30 minutes
    • Red lentils: 10-15 minutes (they break down easily)
  4. Season: Add salt once the lentils are tender and cooked through. Adding salt too early can toughen the lentils.
  5. Drain Excess Water: If any water remains, drain off excess liquid or use it as a flavorful broth base.

Once you master this method, you can incorporate lentils into countless dishes.

Why Lentils Are a Fantastic Staple for Beginners

  • Quick Cook Time: Unlike many dried beans, lentils don’t require soaking and cook relatively fast.
  • Nutritious: High in protein, fiber, and essential minerals, lentils contribute to a balanced diet.
  • Affordable: Lentils are budget-friendly, ideal for those cooking at home frequently.
  • Versatile: Use them in soups, salads, stews, curries, or even lentil-based patties.

Simple Lentil Recipes to Get You Started

1. Basic Lentil Soup

A warm, comforting bowl that’s perfect for beginners and can be adjusted to your tastes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: a squeeze of lemon juice for brightness

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, carrot, and celery and sauté for 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in garlic, cumin, and thyme. Cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add rinsed lentils and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil.
  4. Lower heat and simmer uncovered for 25-30 minutes, or until lentils are tender.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and lemon juice before serving.

Tip: Serve with crusty bread for a filling meal.

2. Red Lentil Dahl

This Indian-inspired dish is flavorful and incredibly easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder or to taste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Juice of half a lime

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, heat oil. Sauté onion until soft.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, cooking for one more minute.
  3. Stir in spices (turmeric, coriander, cumin, chili powder).
  4. Add red lentils and water. Bring to a boil and then simmer uncovered for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are soft and have formed a thick stew.
  5. Add salt and lime juice. Garnish with fresh cilantro.

Note: Serve with basmati rice or naan bread.

3. Lentil Salad – Healthy and Refreshing

Great for lunches or as a side dish, this salad showcases the lovely texture of green or black lentils.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup green or black lentils, cooked and cooled
  • 1 small cucumber, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 red onion, finely chopped
  • Handful of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh parsley or mint, chopped
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: crumbled feta or goat cheese

Instructions:

  1. Combine all vegetables with the cooked lentils in a large bowl.
  2. Dress the salad with olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss to combine and sprinkle fresh herbs on top.
  4. Add cheese if desired for extra creaminess.

4. Lentil Patties

Transform cooked lentils into delicious patties that can be served as burgers or appetizers.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked brown lentils
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg (or flax egg for vegan option)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Olive oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Mash cooked lentils roughly in a bowl, leaving some texture.
  2. Add onion, garlic, spices, breadcrumbs, and egg. Mix well.
  3. Form mixture into small patties.
  4. Heat olive oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Cook patties for about 4 minutes each side until golden and crispy.
  5. Serve with salad, inside a bun, or with a dipping sauce.

5. Lentil and Vegetable Stir-Fry

For a quick weeknight dinner, combine lentils with colorful veggies and your favorite stir-fry sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked lentils (brown or green)
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas or green beans
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil or olive oil
  • Optional: chili flakes or sriracha for heat
  • Sesame seeds and green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  3. Add vegetables and stir-fry until tender-crisp, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Toss in cooked lentils and soy sauce, cook for another 2 minutes.
  5. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and green onions. Add chili flakes if desired.

Serve over steamed rice or noodles.

Tips for Perfect Lentils Every Time

  • Don’t Skip Rinsing: Always rinse lentils thoroughly to remove dust.
  • Avoid Overcrowding: Use enough water to allow lentils to expand and cook evenly.
  • Add Aromatics: Bay leaves, garlic, and herbs can elevate simple lentils.
  • Taste as You Go: Lentils can become mushy if overcooked, so keep an eye on texture.
  • Cook in Broth: For extra flavor, cook lentils in vegetable or chicken broth rather than water.

Storing and Using Leftover Lentils

Cooked lentils keep well in the fridge for up to 5 days. Cool completely before refrigerating in an airtight container. Use leftovers in salads, wraps, or mixed into grain bowls. They also freeze well—store in portion-sized freezer bags for future quick meals.

Final Thoughts

Lentils make an excellent first pulse ingredient for beginners due to their ease of cooking, nutritional value, and adaptability. With just a few staple spices and veggies, you can create a variety of delicious, wholesome meals that satisfy both taste buds and dietary needs. Start simple with a basic lentil soup or red lentil dahl, then branch out into salads, patties, and stir-fries as your confidence grows.

Once you get comfortable with cooking lentils, they’ll quickly become one of your go-to kitchen ingredients, providing endless inspiration for nutritious meals that don’t compromise on flavor.

Happy cooking! If you experiment with these recipes or develop your own, enjoy the journey with lentils—small legumes with big potential.
