Flavors of Lebanon: 8 Mezze Recipes Everyone Loves


The cuisine of Lebanon is a celebration of flavors, textures, and aromas that reflect the country’s vibrant culture and rich culinary heritage. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply looking to introduce more variety into your cooking repertoire, Lebanese mezze—a collection of small dishes perfect for sharing—is a delightful way to explore the cuisine’s distinctive tastes. From creamy dips to sizzling bites straight from the grill, these mezze recipes are sure to impress friends, family, and food enthusiasts alike.

In this blog post, we’ll uncover eight classic Lebanese mezze dishes that everyone loves, giving you step-by-step guidance on how to prepare them in your own kitchen. Each dish embodies the heart and soul of Lebanon, turning simple, wholesome ingredients into culinary masterpieces.

1. Hummus

A cornerstone of Lebanese cuisine, hummus is beloved worldwide for its creamy texture and nutty flavor. This ancient dish, made from blended chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, serves as both a standalone dip and a versatile accompaniment to other mezze.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 1/3 cup tahini (sesame paste)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: paprika or chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Place the cooked chickpeas in a food processor and pulse until smooth.
  2. Add the tahini, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and a pinch of salt. Blend until fully incorporated and creamy.
  3. Adjust the consistency with a few tablespoons of water, if necessary.
  4. Serve in a shallow bowl, drizzled with olive oil and garnished with paprika or parsley.

Hummus pairs perfectly with warm pita bread or a platter of fresh vegetables.

2. Baba Ghanoush

This smoky eggplant dip is another iconic Lebanese favorite. Baba ghanoush takes humble eggplants and turns them into a silky-smooth dish that’s as perfect for dipping as it is for spreading on bread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: pomegranate seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Roast the eggplants over an open flame or in the oven until the skins are charred and the flesh is soft. Let cool.
  2. Peel off the charred skin and discard. Mash the flesh in a bowl or blend for a smoother consistency.
  3. Mix the mashed eggplant with tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed, then transfer to a serving bowl.
  5. Garnish with a drizzle of olive oil and pomegranate seeds for extra flavor.

3. Tabbouleh

Tabbouleh is a refreshing salad bursting with fresh herbs, juicy tomatoes, and zesty lemon juice. It’s light yet flavorful, making it an essential part of a Lebanese mezze spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup finely chopped parsley
  • ½ cup finely chopped mint
  • 3 firm tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • ½ cup cooked bulgur wheat
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine the parsley, mint, tomatoes, onion, and bulgur wheat in a mixing bowl.
  2. Drizzle the lemon juice and olive oil over the mixture.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, then toss everything together until well-coated.
  4. Chill before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

Tabbouleh is vibrant and refreshing, making it a versatile addition to any table.

4. Falafel

Crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, falafel is a popular Lebanese street food that’s equally at home on the mezze table. These deep-fried chickpea balls are irresistibly savory.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • ½ cup chopped parsley
  • ½ cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Drain the soaked chickpeas and pulse them in a food processor along with onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, and the spices.
  2. Stir in baking powder and season with salt and pepper. Chill the mixture for 30 minutes.
  3. Form the mixture into small balls or patties.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan and fry the falafel until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot alongside tahini sauce or yogurt dip.

5. Kibbeh Nayeh

Kibbeh nayeh takes Lebanese cuisine to its roots with a dish made from raw meat. A delicacy reserved for special occasions, this dish is rich, flavorful, and absolutely unique.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound fine ground lamb or beef
  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat, soaked
  • ½ cup finely minced onion
  • ½ tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil to drizzle

Instructions:

  1. Thoroughly mix the raw meat with the soaked bulgur, onion, and spices.
  2. Knead until well combined and tender.
  3. Serve raw, spread on a plate with a drizzle of olive oil and optional garnishes of mint leaves, pine nuts, or onions.

6. Warak Enab (Stuffed Grape Leaves)

Tender grape leaves stuffed with a flavorful rice mixture make this dish a crowd-pleaser at any gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 jar grape leaves, rinsed
  • 1 cup rice
  • ½ cup finely chopped parsley
  • 2 tomatoes, diced
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix the rice, parsley, tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl to prepare the filling.
  2. Place a small amount of filling on each grape leaf, fold the sides, and roll tightly.
  3. Arrange the stuffed grape leaves in a saucepan. Cover with water and cook on low heat until the rice is tender.
  4. Serve warm or cold with a drizzle of olive oil.

7. Fattoush

This bright and tangy salad is a Lebanese staple, made with fresh vegetables and crispy pita chips.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head fresh romaine lettuce
  • 2 cucumbers, diced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 4 radishes, sliced
  • 3 scallions, chopped
  • 2 pieces of pita bread, toasted or fried
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sumac
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toss the lettuce, cucumbers, tomatoes, radishes, and scallions in a large bowl.
  2. Break the toasted pita bread into bite-sized pieces and add to the salad.
  3. Drizzle with lemon juice, olive oil, and sprinkle the sumac. Season with salt and pepper and toss until combined.

8. Shanklish

This unique aged cheese, often rolled in herbs and spices, is beloved for its bold and tangy flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ball of shanklish cheese
  • 1 cup finely chopped tomatoes
  • 1 cup finely chopped onions
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Optional: za’atar or chili flakes for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Crumble the shanklish cheese into a mixing bowl.
  2. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and onions.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle herbs as desired. Serve fresh with pita bread.

The beauty of Lebanese mezze lies in its adaptability: each dish can effortlessly take center stage while creating a harmonious balance of flavor when combined. These recipes will not only elevate your culinary game but also transport your taste buds straight to the heart of Lebanon’s bustling streets and sun-drenched villages.

Experiment with different combinations of mezze dishes to create your own Middle Eastern feast. With these recipes in hand, you’ll bring authentic Lebanese flavors to your kitchen and impress everyone at the table.
