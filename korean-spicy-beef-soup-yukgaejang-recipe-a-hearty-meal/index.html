Korean Spicy Beef Soup (Yukgaejang) Recipe: A Hearty Meal


Korean cuisine is known for its deep flavors, bold spices, and unique ingredients, making it a favorite for food lovers worldwide. Among the array of flavorful Korean dishes, Yukgaejang stands out as one of the most comforting and satisfying meals. This hearty soup, traditionally made with beef, vegetables, and spicy seasonings, offers the perfect combination of heat, savory flavors, and nourishing ingredients. Whether you’re seeking a meal to warm you up on a cold day or a flavorful dish to impress your friends and family, Yukgaejang is the ideal choice.

In this post, we’ll guide you through the process of making a traditional Korean Spicy Beef Soup (Yukgaejang), share tips on how to elevate the flavors, and provide variations to customize the dish to your liking. So, let’s dive into the world of Yukgaejang and explore how to create this authentic Korean delicacy in your own kitchen.

What is Yukgaejang?

Yukgaejang is a spicy beef soup that has been a staple in Korean cuisine for centuries. It is often enjoyed as a comforting meal during the colder months, offering warmth and satisfaction in every spoonful. The dish typically features thinly sliced beef, usually brisket or shank, which is simmered to perfection with a blend of aromatic vegetables, spicy seasonings, and a rich, flavorful broth. What sets Yukgaejang apart from other beef soups is its bold and fiery flavor, which is achieved through the generous use of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), gochujang (Korean chili paste), and garlic.

In addition to the spiciness, Yukgaejang has a variety of textures, with tender beef, crunchy vegetables, and chewy glass noodles, all coming together to create a well-rounded dish. It’s a great option for those who love spicy food and want to explore the unique flavors of Korean cuisine.

Key Ingredients for Yukgaejang

Before we get into the step-by-step recipe, let’s take a look at the essential ingredients you’ll need to make Yukgaejang. Many of these ingredients can be found in an Asian or Korean grocery store, and some might already be staples in your kitchen.

1. Beef (Brisket or Shank)

The star ingredient in Yukgaejang is the beef. Brisket or shank is often used because it becomes tender and flavorful after simmering for a long period. Thinly sliced beef is ideal for this dish because it absorbs the rich broth and spices, adding depth to the soup. If you’re looking for an alternative, you can use other cuts of beef, but brisket or shank is recommended for the best results.

2. Gochugaru (Korean Chili Flakes)

Gochugaru is a key ingredient that imparts the signature heat and red color to the soup. These chili flakes are mild to medium in heat, providing a perfect balance of spice without overwhelming the palate. The flakes also contribute to the depth of flavor in the soup. Make sure to use authentic gochugaru for the best results.

3. Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste)

Gochujang is a fermented chili paste that adds both heat and a subtle sweetness to the soup. Itâ€™s an essential ingredient for achieving the traditional flavor of Yukgaejang. This paste is made from red chili peppers, glutinous rice, fermented soybeans, and salt, and it adds a savory, umami-rich taste that enhances the overall profile of the dish.

4. Garlic

Garlic is one of the most important aromatics in Korean cuisine. It brings a fragrant, savory depth to the broth of Yukgaejang. Fresh garlic cloves are minced or finely chopped and added to the soup for maximum flavor.

5. Onions and Green Onions

Onions provide sweetness and depth to the broth, while green onions (scallions) add a mild, fresh flavor. Green onions are also used as a garnish at the end of the cooking process, giving the soup a fresh finish.

6. Shiitake Mushrooms

Shiitake mushrooms are commonly used in Korean soups due to their rich, umami flavor. They add both texture and flavor, complementing the beef and broth beautifully. You can use fresh or dried shiitake mushrooms for this recipe.

7. Korean Glass Noodles (Dangmyeon)

Korean glass noodles, or dangmyeon, are made from sweet potato starch and are commonly found in Korean soups and stir-fries. These chewy, translucent noodles absorb the broth and seasonings, making them a perfect addition to Yukgaejang.

8. Soy Sauce and Sesame Oil

Soy sauce and sesame oil are used to season the broth. Soy sauce provides a salty umami flavor, while sesame oil imparts a nutty aroma and richness to the soup. These ingredients are key to creating the balanced, savory flavor of Yukgaejang.

9. Sesame Seeds

Roasted sesame seeds are typically sprinkled over the soup before serving. They add a crunchy texture and a nutty flavor that enhances the overall taste experience.

10. Spinach or Watercress

Spinach or watercress is often added to the soup towards the end of cooking. The greens provide freshness, color, and nutrition to the dish, balancing out the spiciness of the broth.

Step-by-Step Recipe for Yukgaejang

Now that we know the key ingredients, let’s move on to the cooking process. This recipe serves approximately 4 to 6 people and takes around 1 to 1.5 hours to prepare and cook.

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef brisket or shank, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 6 cups beef broth (or water)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds (optional)
  • 1 cup shiitake mushrooms, sliced (fresh or rehydrated if dried)
  • 1/2 cup Korean glass noodles (dangmyeon), soaked in warm water for 30 minutes
  • 1 bunch spinach or watercress
  • 2-3 green onions, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Step 1: Prepare the Beef

Start by preparing the beef. If you are using a whole cut of beef, slice it thinly across the grain. This will ensure that the beef is tender and easy to chew once it’s cooked. If you’re using pre-sliced beef, you’re ready to go! Set the beef aside.

Step 2: SautÃ© the Aromatics

In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic. SautÃ© for 3-4 minutes, or until the onions become soft and translucent. This will create a flavorful base for the soup.

Step 3: Add the Spices and Gochujang

To the pot, add 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean chili paste) and 2 tablespoons of gochugaru (Korean chili flakes). Stir well to combine with the onions and garlic. Let the spices cook for about 1 minute, allowing them to release their flavors.

Step 4: Add the Beef

Add the thinly sliced beef to the pot and stir-fry for about 3-5 minutes, allowing the beef to brown slightly. The beef should absorb the flavors of the onions, garlic, and spices during this process.

Step 5: Add the Broth and Soy Sauce

Once the beef is lightly browned, pour in 6 cups of beef broth (or water) and 2 tablespoons of soy sauce. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes to allow the flavors to develop and the beef to become tender.

Step 6: Add the Mushrooms and Glass Noodles

After simmering the soup, add the shiitake mushrooms and soaked glass noodles (dangmyeon) to the pot. Continue simmering for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the noodles are cooked through.

Step 7: Add the Greens

Add the spinach or watercress to the soup, and cook for another 2-3 minutes, just until the greens are wilted.

Step 8: Season the Soup

Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning as necessary. Add salt and pepper to taste, and finish with a drizzle of sesame oil for added richness and flavor.

Step 9: Serve and Garnish

Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with chopped green onions and roasted sesame seeds. Serve the soup hot with steamed rice or as is for a complete meal.

Tips for Making the Perfect Yukgaejang

  • Beef Quality: The key to a flavorful Yukgaejang is tender beef. Opt for cuts like brisket or shank, which become tender after long simmering. Make sure to slice the beef thinly for better texture in the soup.
  • Adjust the Spice Level: If you’re not a fan of extremely spicy food, feel free to reduce the amount of gochugaru and gochujang. You can always add more later if you want to ramp up the heat.
  • Use Homemade Broth: For an even richer flavor, try making your own beef broth. This will add depth to the soup and enhance its umami profile.
  • Add Other Vegetables: While the traditional recipe calls for mushrooms and greens, feel free to add other vegetables like bean sprouts, zucchini, or carrots for additional texture and flavor.
  • Make It Ahead: Yukgaejang tastes even better the next day, as the flavors have more time to meld together. Prepare it in advance and store it in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

Conclusion

Korean Spicy Beef Soup, or Yukgaejang, is a hearty and flavorful dish that brings comfort and warmth to your table. With its tender beef, spicy broth, and a variety of vegetables and noodles, this dish is perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or serving a crowd, Yukgaejang is sure to impress with its rich flavors and satisfying textures. Try it today and experience the bold, spicy goodness of this beloved Korean dish!
