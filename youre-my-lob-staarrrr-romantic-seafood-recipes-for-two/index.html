You're my Lob-staarrrr: Romantic Seafood Recipes for Two


Fancy a night of romantic bliss over an intimate feast with your beloved? A special dinner for two is an excellent way to express love and appreciation, and what better way to do so than with the delectable flavors of seafood? This blog post brings you five tantalizing seafood recipes that’ll make your date night an unforgettable affair.

1. Citrus Lobster Bisque

Begin your romantic dinner with a creamy, tangy start.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound lobster tails (about 2 tails)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 ripe tomato, diced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 stalk celery, chopped
  • 1 carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 1 ripe red bell pepper, chopped
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Lemon and orange zest for garnish
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until translucent.
  2. Add garlic, tomato, celery, carrot, and red bell pepper. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour in white wine, bring to a simmer, and cook further for 3 minutes.
  4. Add lobster tails, smoked paprika, and tomato paste. Cook until lobster is cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  5. Remove lobster tails and set them aside to cool. Once cooled, cut meat from tails and set aside.
  6. Add seafood stock to the saucepan and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Using an immersion blender (or transferring portions to a blender and processing), purée the soup until smooth.
  8. Return the lobster meat to the pot. Stir in the heavy cream and cook until heated through.
  9. Season with salt and pepper, and serve hot, garnished with lemon and orange zest.

2. Scallop and Spinach Risotto

A creamy, elegant dish that’s sure to impress your date.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1 pound large scallops, patted dry
  • 4 cups baby spinach
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Lemon wedges and fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the seafood stock and keep it warm throughout the cooking process.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add shallot and cook until translucent.
  3. Add rice and toast for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Pour in white wine, stirring until the alcohol has evaporated.
  5. Begin adding the warm seafood stock, 1 ladleful at a time, and cook, stirring constantly, until each ladleful has been absorbed before adding the next.
  6. When rice is almost tender, add scallops and spinach to the pot. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, or until the scallops have seared and spinach has wilted.
  7. Stir in butter and Parmesan cheese, and cook for another minute.
  8. Season with salt and pepper, and serve garnished with lemon wedges and fresh chives.

3. Crispy Shrimp Lettuce Cups

A fresh, light dish to brighten up your date night.

Ingredients:

  • 2 heads butter lettuce
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thinly sliced green onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon Sriracha hot sauce
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk the ingredients for the sauce.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix shrimp with 2 tablespoons of the sauce.
  3. In a large skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add shrimp and cook until pink and cooked through, about 3-4 minutes. Set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, cook garlic until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the cooked shrimp and green onion, stirring to combine.
  5. Spoon the shrimp mixture into lettuce cups and garnish with cilantro. Serve with the remaining sauce on the side.

4. Baked Salmon with Dill and Garlic

The classic preparation of this flaky, delicate fish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 salmon fillets (about 6 ounces each)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 lemon, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Score the skin of the salmon fillets and pat dry with a paper towel.
  3. Season with salt and pepper on both sides.
  4. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, garlic, and dill.
  5. Coat the salmon fillets with the olive oil mixture and place on a lightly oiled baking sheet, skin-side down.
  6. Top each fillet with lemon slices.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, until salmon is cooked through and easily flakes with a fork.

5. Chocolate Raspberry Mousse

A decadent dessert to match the sophisticated seafood dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces dark chocolate
  • 1 cup heavy cream, divided
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup seedless raspberry jam
  • Fresh raspberries and chocolate shavings for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a metal bowl set over a pot of simmering water (making sure the bowl doesn’t touch the water), melt the chocolate until smooth. Set aside to cool slightly.
  2. In a medium saucepan, heat 1/2 cup of heavy cream over medium heat until it begins to simmer.
  3. Whisk egg yolks and sugar in a large mixing bowl. Gradually pour the hot cream over the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly.
  4. Once combined, add the melted chocolate and stir until smooth. Set aside to cool.
  5. In a separate bowl, whip the remaining heavy cream until soft peaks form.
  6. Gently fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture until incorporated.
  7. Spoon the mousse into individual dessert glasses and chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or until set.
  8. Serve with a dollop of raspberry jam, fresh raspberries, and chocolate shavings as garnish.

End your romantic dinner with a luscious dessert that complements the elegant seafood course. Celebrate love and great food while crafting unforgettable memories with your special someone, making every meal a beautiful story in the making.

Enjoy your romantic seafood dinner, my friends! Happy cooking!
