Step-by-Step Guide to Canning Fruit Pie Filling


Canning fruit pie filling is an excellent way to preserve seasonal fruits and enjoy delicious homemade pies all year round. Whether you are looking to extend the bounty of your summer harvest or want to prepare for the holidays, knowing how to can fruit pie filling gives you a convenient and tasty way to have a ready-made filling at your fingertips. This step-by-step guide will walk you through everything you need to know about canning fruit pie filling, from the best fruits to use, essential tools, and the canning process itself.

Why Canning Fruit Pie Filling Is a Great Idea

Canning fruit pie filling is a smart method to preserve fruit at its peak flavor. Instead of freezing fruit or leaving it to spoil, canning allows you to bottle the fruit and its syrup so that it’s available at a later time. When you need a quick dessert for a family gathering, holiday meal, or special occasion, you can simply open a jar, pour it into a pie crust, and bake it.

The process of canning fruit pie filling also maintains the integrity of the fruit, keeping it fresh, vibrant, and flavorful. It gives you the chance to enjoy homemade pies without needing to spend hours on preparation. Canned pie filling can last for a year or longer when stored properly, making it an excellent option for stocking up.

Essential Tools and Ingredients for Canning Fruit Pie Filling

Before diving into the canning process, it’s important to gather all the necessary tools and ingredients. This ensures that the canning process is safe, efficient, and successful. Here’s what you’ll need:

Tools:

  • Jars: For canning, you will need proper canning jars. Quart-sized jars are ideal for pie filling, but pint-sized jars work too, depending on your preference. Ensure they are sterilized before use.
  • Lids and Bands: Use canning lids and screw bands that are designed for canning purposes. These ensure that the jars are sealed properly.
  • Water Bath Canner: A large pot with a rack designed for submerging jars in boiling water. This is the most common method for canning fruit pie filling.
  • Jar Lifter: A tool that helps you safely remove hot jars from the canning pot.
  • Funnel: A wide-mouth funnel will help you fill your jars without making a mess.
  • Bubble Remover: This tool helps remove air bubbles from the jars to ensure proper sealing.
  • Ladles and Measuring Cups: For accurate measurement and transferring the filling into jars.

Ingredients:

  • Fruit: You can can a variety of fruits for pie filling, including apples, peaches, cherries, blueberries, and mixed berries. The fruit should be fresh, ripe, and free of blemishes.
  • Sugar: Sugar helps preserve the fruit and creates the syrup that coats the filling. Adjust the amount of sugar based on the sweetness of your fruit and your preference.
  • Thickening Agents: To achieve the right consistency for pie filling, you’ll need to use either cornstarch, clear gel, or tapioca. Cornstarch is the most common thickener.
  • Citrus Juice: Lemon juice is often used to prevent browning in fruits like apples and to balance out the sweetness of the filling.
  • Water or Fruit Juice: You’ll need water or fruit juice (such as apple juice for apple pie filling) to create the syrup base for the filling.
  • Spices: Spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice are optional, depending on the flavor profile you want for your pie filling.

Step-by-Step Guide to Canning Fruit Pie Filling

Canning fruit pie filling involves several steps, but with a bit of preparation and organization, the process becomes straightforward. Here’s a breakdown of the steps you’ll follow.

Step 1: Prepare the Fruit

The first step in canning fruit pie filling is preparing your fruit. This involves washing, peeling, and slicing it appropriately for your pie.

  • Peel and Slice: For apples, peaches, and pears, peel the fruit, core it, and slice it into thin, even pieces. For cherries or berries, remove any stems and pits as needed.
  • Prevent Browning: To prevent fruit like apples from browning, you can either soak the slices in a solution of water and ascorbic acid (vitamin C) or just lemon juice. This will help maintain the color of the fruit during canning.
  • Set the Fruit Aside: Once your fruit is prepared, set it aside in a bowl. If you’re canning a mixture of fruits, make sure to keep them separate so you can easily control the amounts of each in the filling.

Step 2: Prepare the Syrup

Next, you’ll prepare the syrup that will hold the fruit together in the jar. A basic syrup is made from water, sugar, and thickener.

  • Sugar Syrup: In a large saucepan, combine water (or fruit juice), sugar, and your thickening agent (cornstarch, clear gel, or tapioca). Bring the mixture to a boil, stirring constantly until the syrup thickens.
  • Spices: If you are making apple pie filling or a spiced fruit filling, this is the time to add cinnamon, nutmeg, and other spices to your syrup.
  • Boil: Once the syrup has thickened to the desired consistency, allow it to boil for an additional 1-2 minutes to ensure it’s properly cooked.

Step 3: Pack the Jars with Fruit

Now it’s time to pack the jars with fruit. Here’s how to do it properly:

  • Sterilize Jars: Before packing, ensure your canning jars are sterilized. You can do this by boiling them in a water bath for 10 minutes or running them through a dishwasher cycle.
  • Pack the Fruit: Fill each jar with the prepared fruit, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top of the jar. Be sure to pack the fruit tightly, but don’t crush it.
  • Add the Syrup: Pour the hot syrup over the fruit, filling each jar to within 1 inch of the top. Use a bubble remover to remove any air bubbles and ensure the fruit is evenly distributed.
  • Wipe the Rims: Before sealing the jars, wipe the rims of each jar with a clean, damp cloth to remove any residue or syrup. This will help ensure a good seal.

Step 4: Seal the Jars

Sealing your jars is crucial to prevent contamination and preserve the fruit pie filling for months.

  • Apply Lids and Bands: Place a canning lid on top of each jar and screw the metal band on firmly, but not too tight. The lid should be placed in the center of the jar, with the band tightened until it’s just secure.
  • Check for Tightness: Once the lids are on, check that they are tightly secured, but don’t over-tighten. The lid should be able to expand and contract during the canning process.

Step 5: Process the Jars

The next step is to process the jars in a water bath canner, which ensures that the jars are properly sealed and the pie filling is fully preserved.

  • Boil the Jars: Fill the water bath canner with enough water to cover the jars by at least 1 inch. Bring the water to a rolling boil. Once boiling, carefully lower the jars into the canner using a jar lifter.
  • Boil for the Right Time: Process the jars for the required time. For most fruit pie fillings, you should process the jars for about 25 minutes for pint jars and 30 minutes for quart jars. Adjust the time based on your altitude (higher altitudes require longer processing).
  • Cool the Jars: After processing, carefully remove the jars from the water and allow them to cool completely on a clean towel or wire rack. You should hear a “pop” sound as the jars seal.

Step 6: Check the Seals and Store the Jars

Once the jars have cooled, check the seals. The lids should be concave (curved inward), indicating a proper seal. If any jars haven’t sealed correctly, refrigerate them and use them within a few days.

  • Labeling: Label each jar with the type of pie filling and the date it was canned. This will help you keep track of when each batch was made.
  • Store: Store the sealed jars in a cool, dark place such as a pantry or cupboard. Properly canned pie filling will last for up to one year.

Tips for Perfect Canned Pie Filling

  • Choose Fresh Fruit: Always use fresh, ripe fruit for canning. Overripe or bruised fruit may not hold up well during the canning process.
  • Use the Right Thicker: Different thickeners (like cornstarch vs. clear gel) behave differently when canned. Research which is best for your desired pie filling consistency.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Don’t hesitate to get creative with your fruit pie fillings. Adding a dash of vanilla, almond extract, or ginger can enhance the flavors and make each pie filling unique.

Conclusion

Canning fruit pie filling is a rewarding process that allows you to preserve the flavors of summer and fall fruits for use year-round. With a little preparation and patience, you can enjoy delicious homemade pies whenever the craving strikes. Following these simple steps and using the right ingredients and tools ensures that your canned pie filling will turn out perfectly every time. Enjoy the convenience of having ready-made pie filling at your fingertips, and impress friends and family with your homemade pies!

Happy canning!
