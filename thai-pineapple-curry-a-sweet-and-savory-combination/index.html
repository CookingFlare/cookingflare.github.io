Thai Pineapple Curry: A Sweet and Savory Combination


When it comes to Thai cuisine, the perfect balance of sweet, spicy, and savory flavors is what makes it truly special. One dish that encapsulates this harmonious blend is the Thai Pineapple Curry. This delightful curry dish brings together the sweetness of pineapple, the creaminess of coconut milk, and the aromatic spices of Thai cuisine in a symphony of flavors that will tantalize your taste buds. In this blog post, I’ll guide you through the process of creating this mouthwatering Thai Pineapple Curry in the comfort of your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Curry Paste:

  • 2 shallots, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, sliced
  • 1-inch piece of galangal, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of ground turmeric
  • 4 dried red chilies, soaked in hot water
  • 1 tablespoon of shrimp paste (optional)

For the Curry:

  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of Thai red curry paste (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 ½ cups of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 2 cups of fresh pineapple chunks
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon of palm sugar or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • A handful of fresh Thai basil leaves

For Serving:

  • Cooked jasmine rice or rice noodles

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Curry Paste:

    • In a mortar and pestle or a food processor, pound or blend the shallots, garlic, lemongrass, galangal, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, turmeric, and soaked dried red chilies until a smooth paste forms. If desired, add the shrimp paste and continue blending until well combined.

  2. Cook the Curry:

    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the prepared curry paste and cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
    • Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, stirring to combine with the curry paste.
    • Add the pineapple chunks, red bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes to the pan, and let the curry simmer for about 15-20 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.

  3. Season the Curry:

    • Stir in the palm sugar, fish sauce, and soy sauce, adjusting the seasoning to your taste. Allow the curry to simmer for a few more minutes to let the flavors meld together.
    • Toss in the fresh Thai basil leaves and give the curry a final gentle stir.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Serve the Thai Pineapple Curry hot alongside jasmine rice or rice noodles, allowing the creamy and aromatic flavors to complement the sweetness of the pineapple.

Tips and Variations

  • For a vegetarian version, you can omit the shrimp paste and use soy sauce instead of fish sauce.
  • If you prefer a spicier curry, increase the amount of dried red chilies in the curry paste.
  • Feel free to add other vegetables such as snow peas, baby corn, or bamboo shoots to customize the curry to your liking.

Conclusion

The Thai Pineapple Curry is a wonderful representation of the diverse and vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine. Its delightful combination of sweet and savory elements, along with the richness of coconut milk, makes it a truly satisfying dish for any occasion. Whether you’re a fan of Thai food or looking to explore new culinary experiences, this curry is sure to impress and delight. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the aromatic spices, and embark on a culinary journey to savor the exquisite taste of Thai Pineapple Curry.
