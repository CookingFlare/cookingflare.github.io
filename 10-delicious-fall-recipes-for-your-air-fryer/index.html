10 Delicious Fall Recipes for Your Air Fryer


Autumn is the season of cozy sweaters, crisp air, and vibrant foliage. It’s also the perfect time to embrace comforting, hearty meals that celebrate the flavors of the season. If you’re looking to make the most out of your air fryer this fall, you’re in luck. From savory pumpkin dishes to warm spiced apples, these recipes will have your kitchen smelling like autumn in no time. Each recipe takes advantage of seasonal ingredients and the air fryer’s efficiency, so you can whip up delicious creations in no time.

1. Crispy Maple-Glazed Brussels Sprouts

Brussels sprouts become the ultimate fall side dish with this sweet and savory recipe. The air fryer crisps up the edges while giving the centers a tender bite.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp pure maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: A sprinkle of toasted pecans or bacon bits for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 375°F.
  2. In a bowl, toss Brussels sprouts with olive oil, maple syrup, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the sprouts evenly in the air fryer basket.
  4. Cook for 10-12 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through.
  5. Garnish with toasted pecans or crispy bacon if desired before serving.

2. Air-Fried Pumpkin Ravioli

Pumpkin ravioli is a fall favorite, and this air fryer twist turns it into a crispy delight. Serve as an appetizer or pair with a side salad for a satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 12 fresh store-bought pumpkin ravioli
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Marinara or browned butter sauce for dipping

Directions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 360°F.
  2. In a shallow bowl, mix together breadcrumbs, Parmesan, garlic powder, and Italian seasoning.
  3. Dip each ravioli in the beaten egg, then coat with the breadcrumb mixture.
  4. Lightly spray the air fryer basket with cooking spray and arrange ravioli in a single layer.
  5. Cook for 8-10 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve immediately with dipping sauce of your choice.

3. Butternut Squash Fries

Skip regular fries and go seasonal with butternut squash. These fries are naturally sweet, crunchy, and pair well with spicy aioli or a cool yogurt dip.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cut into fry-sized sticks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 400°F.
  2. Toss the butternut squash fries in olive oil and spices until evenly coated.
  3. Arrange in a single layer in the air fryer basket.
  4. Cook for 15-18 minutes, shaking the basket every 5 minutes to ensure even crisping.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

4. Spiced Air-Fried Apple Chips

When you’re craving a healthy snack, these apple chips hit the spot. A sprinkle of cinnamon perfectly complements the natural sweetness of the fruit.

Ingredients:

  • 2 apples, thinly sliced (use a mandoline if possible)
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Optional: A drizzle of honey

Directions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 300°F.
  2. Arrange apple slices in a single layer in the air fryer basket. Avoid overlapping as much as possible.
  3. Sprinkle with cinnamon.
  4. Cook for 15-20 minutes, flipping the slices halfway through.
  5. Let the chips cool completely; they will crisp up further as they cool.

5. Air Fryer Acorn Squash Slices

Acorn squash is as nutritious as it is delicious, and the air fryer makes it quick and easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 1 acorn squash, seeded and sliced into 1-inch rings
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg

Directions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 375°F.
  2. In a bowl, toss the squash rings with olive oil, brown sugar, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. Arrange the rings in the air fryer basket in a single layer.
  4. Cook for 12-15 minutes, flipping halfway through, until tender and caramelized.
  5. Serve as a side dish or snack.

6. Crispy Air-Fried Sage Leaves

These crispy sage leaves are the perfect garnish for soups, pastas, or roasts, and they only take minutes to make.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh sage leaves
  • Olive oil or cooking spray
  • Salt, to taste

Directions:

  1. Lightly coat sage leaves with olive oil or cooking spray.
  2. Arrange them in a single layer in the air fryer basket.
  3. Air fry at 350°F for 2-3 minutes. Monitor closely to avoid burning.
  4. Sprinkle with salt and serve as a crispy garnish.

7. Sweet Potato and Cranberry Hash

This sweet and tart hash feels like fall on a plate. It works as a hearty breakfast or a side dish for dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Toss the sweet potatoes and cranberries with olive oil, cinnamon, maple syrup, salt, and pepper.
  2. Air fry at 380°F for 12-15 minutes, shaking halfway through.
  3. Serve warm.

8. Air-Fried Chicken with Apple Cider Glaze

Chicken cooked in an air fryer is crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside. Add an apple cider glaze for a touch of fall flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup apple cider
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • ½ tsp thyme

Directions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 375°F.
  2. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. Cook in the air fryer for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  3. While the chicken cooks, simmer apple cider, Dijon mustard, honey, and thyme in a small saucepan until thickened.
  4. Brush the glaze over the chicken during the last few minutes of cooking.

9. Pumpkin Spice Donut Holes

These donut holes, seasoned with pumpkin spice and sugar, are a fall indulgence you can’t resist.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup pumpkin puree
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp pumpkin spice
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • Melted butter and cinnamon sugar for coating

Directions:

  1. Mix all the ingredients in a bowl until a sticky dough forms.
  2. Roll small balls of dough and place them on parchment paper.
  3. Preheat your air fryer to 350°F and cook the donut holes in batches for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Brush with melted butter and roll in cinnamon sugar.

10. Air-Fried Pear and Goat Cheese Tartlets

These elegant tartlets are perfect for a fall gathering or a comforting evening snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet of puff pastry, thawed
  • 2 pears, thinly sliced
  • 4 oz goat cheese
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme

Directions:

  1. Cut the puff pastry into small squares, approximately 3x3 inches.
  2. Spread goat cheese on each piece, leaving a small border.
  3. Top with pear slices, a drizzle of honey, and a sprinkle of thyme.
  4. Air fry at 370°F for 8-10 minutes until golden and puffy.

With these recipes, you’ll be ready to savor the best of fall flavors while keeping your kitchen time stress-free. The air fryer not only saves you time but also adds a crispy touch that takes autumn dishes to the next level. Happy cooking!
