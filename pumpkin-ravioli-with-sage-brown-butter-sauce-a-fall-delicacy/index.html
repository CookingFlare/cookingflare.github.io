Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Brown Butter Sauce: A Fall Delicacy


As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, it’s time to embrace the flavors of fall with a delightful dish that warms the soul: Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Brown Butter Sauce. This recipe not only celebrates the rich, earthy flavors of pumpkin but also showcases the aromatic touch of sage, creating a perfect harmony of taste that embodies the essence of autumn. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or enjoying a cozy night in, this dish will impress your guests and satisfy your cravings.

The Allure of Pumpkin Ravioli

Pumpkin ravioli is more than just a dish; it’s a celebration of the harvest season. The sweetness of pumpkin combined with the creamy filling creates a luxurious experience that is both comforting and sophisticated. Homemade ravioli can be a labor of love, but the end result is worth every minute spent in the kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Pasta Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • A splash of water (if needed)

For the Pumpkin Filling:

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (canned or homemade)
  • 1/2 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Sage Brown Butter Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 10-12 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving
  • Chopped walnuts or pecans (optional, for garnish)

Making the Pasta Dough

  1. Create the Dough: On a clean countertop, create a mound with the flour and form a well in the center. Add the eggs, salt, and olive oil into the well. Using a fork, gently whisk the eggs, gradually incorporating the flour from the edges of the well until a dough begins to form.

  2. Knead the Dough: Once the dough is formed, knead it by hand for about 8-10 minutes until it is smooth and elastic. If the dough is too dry, add a splash of water. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature.

Preparing the Pumpkin Filling

  1. Mix the Filling: In a mixing bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan, nutmeg, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Mix until the ingredients are well combined and creamy. Taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  2. Chill the Filling: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the filling for about 15-20 minutes. Chilling will make it easier to handle when filling the ravioli.

Rolling Out the Dough

  1. Divide the Dough: After resting, divide the dough into four equal pieces. Keep the pieces covered with a kitchen towel to prevent them from drying out.

  2. Roll the Dough: Using a pasta machine or a rolling pin, roll out one piece of dough to about 1/16 inch thick. Dust with flour as needed to prevent sticking. Repeat this process with the remaining dough pieces.

Assembling the Ravioli

  1. Cut the Sheets: Lay one sheet of rolled-out dough on a floured surface. Using a small spoon or a piping bag, place small mounds of filling (about 1 teaspoon) spaced evenly across the dough, leaving about 1 inch of space between each mound.

  2. Seal the Ravioli: Carefully lay another sheet of dough over the filled sheet. Press down around each mound of filling to seal, ensuring there are no air pockets. Use a ravioli cutter or a sharp knife to cut out the individual ravioli. Ensure the edges are well sealed by pressing them with a fork.

  3. Dust and Store: Dust the ravioli with flour to prevent sticking and place them on a floured baking sheet. Repeat the process until all the dough and filling are used.

Cooking the Ravioli

  1. Boil Water: In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Gently drop the ravioli into the boiling water, being careful not to overcrowd the pot.

  2. Cook the Ravioli: Cook the ravioli for about 3-4 minutes or until they float to the surface. Fresh pasta cooks quickly, so keep an eye on them to avoid overcooking.

  3. Drain and Set Aside: Using a slotted spoon, remove the ravioli and drain them gently. Place them on a plate and cover to keep warm.

Preparing the Sage Brown Butter Sauce

  1. Melt the Butter: In a large skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. Allow it to cook until it turns a light golden brown and begins to foam.

  2. Add Sage: Once the butter is browned, add the fresh sage leaves. Cook for about 1-2 minutes until the sage becomes crispy and aromatic. Be careful not to burn the butter; a nutty aroma indicates it’s ready.

  3. Season the Sauce: Season the brown butter sauce with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat.

Combining the Ravioli and Sauce

  1. Toss the Ravioli: Gently add the cooked ravioli to the skillet with the sage brown butter sauce. Carefully toss the ravioli to coat them evenly in the sauce.

  2. Serve: Plate the ravioli and sprinkle with additional grated Parmesan cheese. Optionally, garnish with chopped walnuts or pecans for added texture and flavor.

Final Touches: Pairing and Presentation

To elevate the dining experience, consider pairing your Pumpkin Ravioli with a crisp white wine, such as a Sauvignon Blanc or a light Pinot Grigio. These wines will complement the sweetness of the pumpkin and the richness of the brown butter sauce beautifully.

When it comes to presentation, serve the ravioli on warm plates, and add a drizzle of sage brown butter sauce over the top. A sprinkle of fresh herbs, such as parsley or chives, can add a pop of color and freshness.

Tips for Success

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the ravioli in advance and freeze them. Place the uncooked ravioli in a single layer on a baking sheet, freeze until firm, and then transfer them to a zip-top bag. Cook from frozen, adding a minute or two to the cooking time.

  • Use Fresh Pumpkin: If you want to make your dish even more special, consider using fresh pumpkin. Roast the pumpkin halves until tender, scoop out the flesh, and mash it before mixing it into your filling.

  • Experiment with Flavors: Feel free to experiment with the filling by adding ingredients like goat cheese, caramelized onions, or even a hint of maple syrup for a sweet twist.

Conclusion

Pumpkin Ravioli with Sage Brown Butter Sauce is not just a meal; it’s an experience that encapsulates the flavors of fall. The combination of sweet pumpkin and fragrant sage creates a comforting dish that’s perfect for any autumn occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a kitchen novice, this recipe invites you to embrace the beauty of homemade pasta.

So, roll up your sleeves and indulge in the enchanting process of making this delightful dish. With each bite, you’ll savor the flavors of the season and create memories that will last a lifetime. Enjoy the warmth, coziness, and deliciousness of fall with every forkful of this exquisite Pumpkin Ravioli!
