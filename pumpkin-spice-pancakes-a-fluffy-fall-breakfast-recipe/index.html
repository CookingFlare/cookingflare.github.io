Pumpkin Spice Pancakes: A Fluffy Fall Breakfast Recipe


Who doesn’t love waking up to the warm and comforting flavors of fall? And what better way to start your day than with a stack of fluffy pumpkin spice pancakes? These delightful pancakes are the perfect treat for crisp autumn mornings, and they’re incredibly easy to make. So grab your apron and let’s dive into this mouthwatering recipe!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, and salt. Set aside.

  2. In a separate bowl, combine the buttermilk, pumpkin puree, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined and smooth.

  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently fold them together until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense pancakes.

  4. Preheat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with cooking spray or a small amount of butter.

  5. Using a 1/4 cup measuring cup, pour the batter onto the skillet, spreading it slightly to form a round pancake. You can cook multiple pancakes at once, depending on the size of your skillet.

  6. Cook the pancakes for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Flip them carefully using a spatula.

  7. Once cooked, transfer the pancakes to a plate and cover them with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm while you cook the remaining batter.

  8. Serve the pumpkin spice pancakes with a drizzle of maple syrup, a dollop of whipped cream, and a sprinkle of cinnamon for an extra touch of fall flavor.

Tips and Variations

  • For a gluten-free version, you can substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.
  • Feel free to add some chopped pecans or walnuts to the batter for added texture and crunch.
  • To make the pancakes even more decadent, you can top them with a cream cheese glaze or a dusting of powdered sugar.
  • If you don’t have buttermilk on hand, you can make a buttermilk substitute by adding 1 tablespoon of lemon juice or vinegar to 3/4 cup of milk. Let it sit for 5 minutes before using.
  • These pancakes freeze well, so you can make a big batch and store them in an airtight container for future breakfasts. Simply reheat them in a toaster or microwave when you’re ready to enjoy.

There you have it – a delicious recipe for pumpkin spice pancakes that will make your fall mornings extra special. The combination of warm spices, fluffy texture, and the subtle sweetness of pumpkin will surely put a smile on your face. So grab your favorite mug of steaming coffee or hot apple cider, cozy up in your favorite sweater, and savor the flavors of the season with these delightful pancakes. Happy cooking!
