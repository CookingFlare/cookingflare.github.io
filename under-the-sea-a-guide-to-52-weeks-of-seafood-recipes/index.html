Under the Sea: A Guide to 52 Weeks of Seafood Recipes


Embark on a culinary journey and dive into the world of seafood with our guide to 52 Weeks of Seafood Recipes. Explore a variety of dishes from diverse coastal regions to satisfy your taste buds and tantalize your senses with the freshest flavors from the ocean. Each week, discover and recreate mouthwatering recipes featuring a diverse collection of ingredients, from crispy calamari to succulent lobster, and everything in between. Let’s set sail on this delicious escapade and gather inspiration from our favorite underwater creatures to transform you into a seafood master.

Week 01: Shrimp

Kick off the year right with the humble shrimp, a favorite among seafood lovers the world over. Versatile, scrumptious, and deceptively simple to cook, shrimp lends itself to an array of tasty dishes.

Shrimp Ceviche

A bold and refreshing dish that transports you straight to South America. The tangy, citrus marinated shrimp harmonizes beautifully with an array of veggies that explode with flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 cups freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 red onions, finely diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeno, finely diced
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine shrimp and lime juice, ensuring all shrimp are well-coated. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or up to 24 hours.
  2. Add onions, red bell pepper, jalapeno, and cilantro to the shrimp mixture. Stir to combine, seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Serve chilled with corn tortilla chips or fresh tostadas, and enjoy this zesty appetizer.

Week 02: Crab

Helpful, gentle, and oh-so-delicious, crab is your go-to ally in the realm of seafood. Its sweet, tender meat makes for truly irresistible meals.

Crab Cakes

Sink your teeth into these golden, crispy crab cakes that will leave you craving more. Paired with a zesty tartar sauce, this dish will transport you to an exquisite beachfront bistro.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. lump crabmeat
  • ½ cup mayo
  • 2 eggs
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • ¼ cup finely diced red bell pepper
  • ¼ cup finely diced red onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Old Bay seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, gently mix together crabmeat and mayo. Stir in eggs, breadcrumbs, red bell pepper, onion, garlic, Old Bay seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  2. Carefully form the mixture into patties, about 1-inch thick.
  3. Heat a large skillet over medium heat with 2 tbsp of oil.
  4. Cook crab cakes for 4-5 minutes on each side or until golden brown. Serve atop a simple salad or with lemon wedges for a burst of freshness.

Week 03: Fish

Nothing beats rich, flaky, perfectly cooked fish; it’s a symbol of the ocean’s bounty. Delve into the world of various fish species and bring their unique flavors into your kitchen.

Lemon Herb Baked Fish

Infused with fragrant herbs and the gentle zest of lemon, this tender baked fish dish is the epitome of elegant simplicity.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. fish fillets (such as cod, halibut, or salmon)
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh dill
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Parchment paper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Tear off a large sheet of parchment paper and place fish fillets in the center.
  3. In a small bowl, mix melted butter, garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, parsley, dill, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pour the butter mixture over fish fillets, ensuring that the flavors coat each piece evenly.
  5. Fold the parchment paper over the fillets, crimping the edges to seal the package.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until fish easily flakes with a fork. Serve with steamed vegetables or a side of your choice.

Let’s continue to explore the dense waters of seafood with new recipes every week. Stay tuned for more delicious adventures as we conquer the culinary seas, one bold dish at a time.
