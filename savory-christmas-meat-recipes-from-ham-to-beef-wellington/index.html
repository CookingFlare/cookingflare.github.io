Savory Christmas Meat Recipes: From Ham to Beef Wellington


The holiday season is a time for gathering with family and friends, sharing laughter, and, of course, indulging in delicious food. When it comes to Christmas dinners, the centerpiece of the table often involves savory meat dishes that are both hearty and delightful. In this post, we’ll explore a variety of savory Christmas meat recipes, from succulent ham to an elegant Beef Wellington. These recipes will not only tantalize your taste buds but also create memorable moments around your holiday table.

The Classic Christmas Ham

Ingredients

  • 1 fully cooked bone-in ham (about 10-12 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • Whole cloves, for studding the ham
  • Pineapple slices (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Begin by preheating your oven to 325°F (160°C).

  2. Prepare the Ham: Remove the ham from its packaging and pat it dry with paper towels. Score the surface of the ham in a diamond pattern, about 1/4 inch deep. This not only looks beautiful but allows the glaze to penetrate the meat.

  3. Stud the Ham: Insert whole cloves into the intersections of the diamond pattern for added flavor and aroma.

  4. Make the Glaze: In a medium saucepan, combine the brown sugar, honey, Dijon mustard, and apple cider vinegar. Cook over medium heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is smooth.

  5. Glaze the Ham: Place the ham in a roasting pan, and brush the glaze generously over the entire surface. Reserve some glaze for later basting.

  6. Bake the Ham: Cover loosely with aluminum foil and bake for about 1.5 to 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the reserved glaze. The ham is ready when it reaches an internal temperature of 140°F (60°C).

  7. Serve: Let the ham rest for about 20 minutes before slicing. Garnish with pineapple slices if desired, and serve warm.

Tips

  • For a smoky flavor, consider using a smoked ham.
  • Pair your ham with sides like mashed potatoes, green bean casserole, and cranberry sauce for a complete Christmas feast.

Herb-Crusted Roast Beef

Ingredients

  • 1 (5-7 pounds) prime rib roast
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Roast: Remove the prime rib from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about an hour before cooking. This helps it cook evenly.

  2. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 450°F (232°C).

  3. Make the Herb Mixture: In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, olive oil, salt, and pepper.

  4. Season the Roast: Rub the herb mixture all over the roast, ensuring it’s evenly coated.

  5. Roast the Beef: Place the roast on a rack in a roasting pan, bone side down. Roast for 30 minutes to develop a crust, then reduce the temperature to 325°F (160°C) and continue cooking until it reaches your desired doneness (about 1.5 to 2 hours for medium-rare).

  6. Rest and Serve: Let the roast rest for 20 minutes before slicing. Serve with au jus or horseradish sauce for a flavorful kick.

Tips

  • Use a meat thermometer to ensure perfect doneness.
  • Enhance your meal with sides like Yorkshire pudding, roasted vegetables, and a rich red wine sauce.

Glazed Turkey Breast

Ingredients

  • 1 bone-in turkey breast (about 5-7 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Prepare the Turkey: Pat the turkey breast dry and season with salt and pepper.

  3. Make the Glaze: In a bowl, whisk together the orange juice, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, and cinnamon.

  4. Glaze the Turkey: Place the turkey breast in a roasting pan and brush generously with the glaze.

  5. Roast the Turkey: Bake for about 1.5 to 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the glaze. The turkey is ready when it reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).

  6. Serve: Let the turkey rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. Serve with seasonal sides like stuffing and cranberry relish.

Tips

  • For added flavor, consider brining the turkey breast overnight.
  • Pair with a light salad for a refreshing contrast to the rich flavors.

Beef Wellington: A Showstopper

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef tenderloin, trimmed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 4 ounces pâté (optional)
  • 1 package puff pastry (thawed)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Sear the Beef: Season the beef tenderloin with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over high heat, then sear the beef on all sides until browned (about 2-3 minutes per side). Remove from heat and allow to cool.

  2. Prepare the Mushroom Duxelles: In the same skillet, add the chopped mushrooms, minced garlic, and thyme. Cook over medium heat until the mushrooms release their moisture and become dry, about 10 minutes. Let cool.

  3. Assemble the Wellington: Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface. If using pâté, spread it evenly over the pastry. Layer the mushroom mixture on top, then place the beef in the center. Wrap the pastry around the beef, sealing the edges with the beaten egg.

  4. Bake the Wellington: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the wrapped beef seam-side down on a baking sheet. Brush the top with the beaten egg. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the beef reaches an internal temperature of 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare.

  5. Serve: Let the Beef Wellington rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with a red wine reduction sauce and seasonal vegetables.

Tips

  • Use a meat thermometer for precise doneness.
  • Pair with creamy mashed potatoes and a rich gravy for an unforgettable meal.

Conclusion

This Christmas, elevate your holiday feast with savory meat recipes that will impress your guests and delight your taste buds. From the classic glazed ham to the decadent Beef Wellington, each dish offers a unique flavor profile that celebrates the spirit of the season. As you prepare these recipes, remember the joy of sharing good food with loved ones and the memories that are made around the dinner table. Happy cooking, and Merry Christmas!
