Loaded Baked Potato Soup: A Comforting Bowl of Goodness


There’s something undeniably comforting about a steaming bowl of loaded baked potato soup. This hearty, creamy dish is a classic favorite that warms the soul and satisfies the taste buds. Whether you’re looking for a comforting meal on a chilly day or a crowd-pleasing dish for a gathering, loaded baked potato soup is a go-to option. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the art of making this delicious soup from scratch, exploring the key ingredients, cooking techniques, and serving suggestions that will elevate your culinary experience.

Ingredients

For the Soup:

  • 6 large russet potatoes, scrubbed and dried
  • 6 slices of bacon, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • Additional shredded cheddar cheese
  • Chopped green onions
  • Crispy bacon bits

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Prick the potatoes with a fork and place them on a baking sheet. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until they are tender when pierced with a fork. Once done, allow them to cool.

  2. In a large pot, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until it turns crispy. Remove the bacon from the pot and set it aside, leaving the bacon drippings in the pot.

  3. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook it in the bacon drippings until it becomes translucent. Then, add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  4. Pour in the chicken broth and bring the mixture to a simmer. While the broth is simmering, peel and roughly chop the baked potatoes, then add them to the pot.

  5. Using a potato masher or a fork, mash the potatoes in the pot until the soup reaches your desired consistency.

  6. Stir in the whole milk and allow the soup to heat through. Then, add the sour cream and shredded cheddar cheese, stirring until the cheese is melted and the soup is creamy.

  7. Season the soup with salt and black pepper to taste. If the soup is too thick, you can add more milk or chicken broth to reach the desired consistency.

  8. Ladle the loaded baked potato soup into bowls and garnish with additional shredded cheddar cheese, chopped green onions, and crispy bacon bits.

Serving Suggestions

Loaded baked potato soup is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a variety of accompaniments. Consider serving it with a side of crusty bread, a fresh garden salad, or even a sprinkle of hot sauce for an extra kick. This soup also makes a delightful addition to any casual gathering or potluck, as it can be prepared in advance and kept warm in a slow cooker for guests to help themselves.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, loaded baked potato soup is a comforting and satisfying dish that never fails to impress. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe provides a wonderful opportunity to hone your culinary skills and treat yourself and your loved ones to a bowl of goodness. From its velvety texture to its rich, savory flavors, this soup is a true crowd-pleaser that captures the essence of comfort food. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that culminates in a steaming bowl of loaded baked potato soup.

I hope you find this recipe for loaded baked potato soup both informative and inspiring. Whether you’re an experienced home chef or just starting your culinary adventure, this dish is sure to delight your taste buds and warm your heart.
