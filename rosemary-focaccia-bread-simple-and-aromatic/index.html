Rosemary Focaccia Bread: Simple and Aromatic


If there’s one thing that can instantly elevate a meal, it’s the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through the air. And when that bread is infused with the earthy, piney fragrance of rosemary, the experience becomes truly magical. In this recipe, we’ll delve into the art of making rosemary focaccia bread, a simple yet incredibly satisfying bread that’s perfect for sharing with family and friends.

The Magic of Focaccia

Focaccia, an Italian flatbread, has been delighting taste buds for centuries. Its pillowy texture and versatile nature make it a beloved staple in many kitchens. What sets rosemary focaccia apart is the addition of fresh rosemary, which imparts a delightful flavor and aroma to the bread, transforming it into something truly special.

The Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups of warm water
  • 2 ¼ teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

For Topping:

  • Fresh rosemary leaves
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Additional olive oil for drizzling

The Method

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

In a small bowl, combine the warm water and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until the mixture becomes frothy, indicating that the yeast is active.

Step 2: Prepare the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the activated yeast mixture and olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix the ingredients until a shaggy dough forms.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. This process helps develop the gluten in the dough, giving the bread its characteristic texture.

Step 4: First Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 5: Shape the Focaccia

Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release the air. Transfer it to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and press it out to fit the size of the sheet. Use your fingertips to create dimples all over the surface of the dough.

Step 6: Second Rise

Cover the shaped dough with a kitchen towel and let it rise for an additional 30 minutes. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

Step 7: Add the Toppings

After the second rise, use your fingertips to create deeper dimples in the dough. Drizzle it generously with olive oil, ensuring that it seeps into the dimples. Sprinkle the top with fresh rosemary leaves and coarse sea salt.

Step 8: Bake to Perfection

Place the prepared focaccia in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until it turns golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

The Final Touch

Once out of the oven, let the focaccia cool slightly before slicing it into squares or wedges. Serve it warm, alongside a dish of good quality olive oil for dipping, or use it as a base for savory sandwiches and paninis.

A Bread to Remember

The beauty of rosemary focaccia lies in its simplicity and versatility. Whether enjoyed on its own, dipped in flavored oils, or paired with a hearty soup, this aromatic bread is sure to leave a lasting impression. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and embark on a journey to create a bread that’s as aromatic as it is delicious. Your home will soon be filled with the irresistible scent of rosemary, and your taste buds will thank you for it. Happy baking!
