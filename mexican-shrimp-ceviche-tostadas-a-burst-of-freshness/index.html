Mexican Shrimp Ceviche Tostadas: A Burst of Freshness

4 min · 820 words

Ceviche is a popular seafood dish that originated in Latin America and has made its way into the hearts and palates of food lovers all around the world. One of the most refreshing and vibrant variations of ceviche is the Mexican Shrimp Ceviche Tostadas. This dish combines the flavors of fresh shrimp, zesty lime juice, and a medley of colorful vegetables, all served on a crispy tostada shell. It’s a burst of freshness that will transport your taste buds to the sunny beaches of Mexico.

Ingredients

For the Shrimp Ceviche:

  • 1 pound of fresh shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 4-5 limes, juiced
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled, seeds removed, and finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, seeds removed and finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup of fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Tostadas:

  • 8 tostada shells
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of sour cream
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the peeled and deveined shrimp with the lime juice. Make sure the shrimp are fully coated in the lime juice, as it will help “cook” the shrimp. Let it marinate for about 10-15 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.

  2. Meanwhile, chop the red onion, jalapeno pepper, cucumber, tomatoes, and cilantro. Set them aside.

  3. After the shrimp has marinated, drain the excess lime juice. The shrimp should have turned opaque, indicating that they are cooked. Give them a quick rinse under cold water to remove any excess lime juice.

  4. Now it’s time to assemble the ceviche. Chop the shrimp into bite-sized pieces and transfer them to a clean bowl. Add the chopped red onion, jalapeno pepper, cucumber, tomatoes, and cilantro. Mix everything together gently.

  5. Season the ceviche with salt and pepper to taste. Give it a taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Remember, ceviche is all about balancing the flavors, so feel free to add more lime juice if desired.

  6. To serve, spread a spoonful of sour cream onto each tostada shell. Top it with a generous amount of the shrimp ceviche mixture. Garnish with sliced avocado and a squeeze of fresh lime juice.

  7. Mexican Shrimp Ceviche Tostadas are best enjoyed immediately after assembling, as the tostada shells can become soggy if left sitting for too long. Serve them as an appetizer at your next gathering or make it a main course by adding a side of rice and beans.

Conclusion

Mexican Shrimp Ceviche Tostadas are a delightful combination of flavors and textures. The tangy lime juice, the succulent shrimp, and the crunchy tostada shell create a delightful contrast that will leave you craving more. Whether you’re hosting a summer party or simply looking to add a burst of freshness to your everyday meals, this recipe is a winner. So gather your ingredients, put on some lively music, and embark on a culinary journey to Mexico with these mouthwatering Shrimp Ceviche Tostadas. ¡Buen provecho!
