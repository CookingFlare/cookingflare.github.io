The Art of Making Homemade Tonkotsu Ramen


Tonkotsu ramen is a beloved Japanese dish known for its rich, creamy broth and flavorful toppings. Making homemade tonkotsu ramen may seem like a daunting task, but with the right ingredients and techniques, you can create a delicious bowl of this iconic comfort food in your own kitchen. In this recipe, we’ll explore the art of making homemade tonkotsu ramen from scratch, including the broth, noodles, and toppings. Get ready to embark on a culinary adventure and treat yourself to a bowl of authentic tonkotsu ramen!

Ingredients

For the Broth:

  • 4-5 pounds pork bones
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 head of garlic, halved
  • 1 knob of ginger, sliced
  • 2-3 green onions, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup sake
  • 12 cups water

For the Tare (Seasoning):

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon chili oil

For the Noodles:

  • 3 cups bread flour
  • 1 cup cake flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon kansui (alkaline mineral water)
  • Cornstarch for dusting

For the Toppings:

  • Chashu pork (braised pork belly)
  • Soft-boiled eggs
  • Thinly sliced green onions
  • Nori (seaweed)
  • Menma (fermented bamboo shoots)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

  1. Blanch the Bones: Place the pork bones in a large pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil. Let the bones boil for 10-15 minutes, then drain and rinse them under cold water to remove any impurities.
  2. Simmer the Broth: In a clean pot, combine the blanched pork bones, onion, garlic, ginger, green onions, sake, and water. Bring the pot to a gentle simmer over low heat. Skim off any foam and impurities that rise to the surface. Let the broth simmer for 12-15 hours, adding water as needed to keep the bones submerged.

Step 2: Make the Tare

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a saucepan, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, brown sugar, sesame oil, and chili oil. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring to dissolve the sugar. Let the tare cool to room temperature before using.

Step 3: Prepare the Noodles

  1. Mix the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the bread flour, cake flour, water, and kansui. Knead the dough until smooth and elastic. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll and Cut: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to your desired thickness. Dust with cornstarch to prevent sticking, then cut the dough into thin noodles.

Step 4: Assemble the Ramen

  1. Cook the Noodles: Bring a pot of water to a boil and cook the fresh noodles for 1-2 minutes, or until al dente. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water.
  2. Build Your Bowl: In each serving bowl, add a portion of tare, then ladle in the hot tonkotsu broth. Add the cooked noodles and arrange the chashu pork, soft-boiled egg, green onions, nori, and menma on top.

Enjoy Your Homemade Tonkotsu Ramen!

Making homemade tonkotsu ramen is a labor of love, but the result is well worth the effort. The rich, creamy broth combined with the chewy noodles and flavorful toppings creates a truly satisfying dining experience. Whether you’re serving it to family and friends or enjoying a comforting bowl on your own, homemade tonkotsu ramen is sure to impress. Experiment with different toppings and flavor variations to make this recipe your own, and savor the art of creating authentic tonkotsu ramen in your home kitchen.
