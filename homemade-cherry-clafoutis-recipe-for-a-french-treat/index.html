Homemade Cherry Clafoutis Recipe for a French Treat


Clafoutis, a traditional French dessert, is a delightful treat that combines the richness of a custard with the sweetness of fresh fruit. This simple yet elegant dish is a perfect way to showcase the flavors of ripe cherries, making it a favorite during cherry season. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, enjoying a quiet evening at home, or just craving a delicious dessert, this homemade cherry clafoutis recipe is sure to impress your taste buds.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you step-by-step to create this decadent French dessert. You don’t need to be a professional baker to whip up a perfect clafoutis; all you need are a few basic ingredients and a little bit of patience. The result is a soft, velvety custard that surrounds juicy cherries, making every bite a moment of indulgence.

What is Clafoutis?

Clafoutis is a classic French dessert that originates from the Limousin region in central France. Traditionally, it is made with black cherries, which are placed at the bottom of a baking dish and then covered with a pancake-like batter made of eggs, milk, sugar, and flour. The dish is then baked until golden and slightly puffed. The cherries are not pitted, as the pits contribute to the rich flavor of the fruit during baking, but they can be removed if preferred.

Clafoutis is often compared to a flan or a souffle, as it has a similar custard texture with a light, airy quality. Despite its elegant appearance, it is a relatively simple dessert to prepare, making it a perfect choice for both beginner and experienced bakers alike.

Why You’ll Love This Homemade Cherry Clafoutis

  • Easy to Make: With just a handful of ingredients, you can create a delicious dessert in no time.
  • Perfect for Cherry Season: If you’re lucky enough to have fresh, in-season cherries, clafoutis is an excellent way to make the most of their sweet, juicy flavor.
  • Customizable: While this recipe uses cherries, you can easily substitute other fruits, such as berries, peaches, or even pears, for a personalized twist.
  • Decadent yet Light: The custard is rich but not too heavy, offering the perfect balance between indulgence and lightness.

Now, let’s dive into the details of creating your very own homemade cherry clafoutis.

Ingredients for Homemade Cherry Clafoutis

To make this cherry clafoutis, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of fresh cherries (about 300 grams), pitted or unpitted, depending on your preference
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup of sugar (you can use white or brown sugar for a slightly different flavor)
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of unsalted butter, melted, plus extra for greasing the baking dish
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting, optional)
  • A pinch of ground cinnamon (optional, but adds a warm, aromatic touch)

Equipment Needed

  • A 9-inch round or oval baking dish (you can also use a square dish)
  • A mixing bowl
  • A whisk or hand mixer
  • A spatula
  • A small sieve or sifter (for dusting powdered sugar)

Instructions for Making Homemade Cherry Clafoutis

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures the baking dish is ready to go once you’ve assembled the batter.

Step 2: Prepare the Baking Dish

Grease your chosen baking dish with butter, making sure to coat the entire surface well. This will help the clafoutis come out of the dish easily and give it a golden, crispy edge. You can also lightly dust the buttered dish with a bit of flour if you want to ensure it doesn’t stick.

Step 3: Prepare the Cherries

If you’re using fresh cherries, wash them thoroughly and remove the stems. You can leave the cherries whole and unpitted, or you can pit them if you prefer. Pitting the cherries will make the texture of the dessert a bit smoother, but some people prefer to leave the pits in, as they enhance the flavor while baking.

Once your cherries are prepared, scatter them evenly at the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Make sure to distribute them as evenly as possible for a consistent flavor in every bite.

Step 4: Make the Clafoutis Batter

In a mixing bowl, combine the eggs and sugar. Whisk them together until the mixture becomes light and slightly frothy. This should only take a minute or two.

Add the milk and vanilla extract to the egg and sugar mixture, and whisk until everything is well incorporated.

Sift the flour and salt directly into the mixture, then whisk until smooth. You should have a thin, pancake-like batter. The texture should be liquid enough to pour easily over the cherries but thick enough to coat them.

Next, add the melted butter to the batter and whisk to combine. The butter adds richness and helps create that lovely golden top as the clafoutis bakes.

Step 5: Pour the Batter Over the Cherries

Carefully pour the prepared batter over the cherries in the baking dish, ensuring the cherries are submerged in the custard mixture. If you see any cherries floating on top, gently nudge them under the batter with a spoon. This step is important as it ensures the batter cooks evenly around the fruit.

Step 6: Bake the Clafoutis

Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 35-45 minutes. The clafoutis is done when it is golden brown on top, slightly puffed, and a toothpick or knife inserted into the center comes out clean (or with just a few crumbs attached). The texture should be soft, with a custard-like consistency.

If the top of your clafoutis starts to brown too quickly, you can cover it with a piece of aluminum foil and continue baking until it is fully cooked through.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Once baked, remove the clafoutis from the oven and allow it to cool for about 10 minutes before serving. The dessert will settle slightly as it cools, and this is perfectly normal. It can be served warm, at room temperature, or even chilled, depending on your preference.

Before serving, you can dust the top with a little powdered sugar for a decorative touch. If you’d like, you can also sprinkle a pinch of ground cinnamon for added warmth and flavor.

Step 8: Enjoy!

Now that your homemade cherry clafoutis is ready, serve it with a dollop of whipped cream, a scoop of vanilla ice cream, or a drizzle of fresh cream for an extra indulgent treat. Enjoy the perfect balance of sweet, tart cherries and the delicate, creamy texture of the clafoutis. It’s a French dessert that will surely become a new favorite!

Variations and Tips for a Perfect Clafoutis

While this cherry clafoutis recipe is already delicious, there are a few ways you can mix things up and make it your own:

  • Use Other Fruits: While cherries are the traditional fruit used in clafoutis, feel free to experiment with other fruits like raspberries, blueberries, or even peaches. If you decide to use a fruit that releases a lot of juice, like berries, consider draining some of the juice before adding them to the dish.
  • Add Nuts: For added texture, sprinkle chopped nuts like almonds, hazelnuts, or pistachios over the top of the clafoutis before baking.
  • Use Different Spices: Experiment with adding a pinch of nutmeg or almond extract to the batter for a new twist on the classic recipe.
  • Make it Gluten-Free: If you’re looking for a gluten-free version, you can substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend or almond flour.
  • Serve with a Sauce: For extra richness, drizzle some chocolate sauce or a berry compote over the top before serving.

Conclusion

Making your own homemade cherry clafoutis is a delightful and rewarding experience. Whether you’re new to French desserts or a seasoned baker, this recipe is a wonderful way to explore the flavors of France and treat yourself to something truly special. By following the steps above, you’ll create a dessert that’s not only delicious but also a showstopper at any gathering.

So the next time you’re looking for a light yet indulgent dessert, give this cherry clafoutis recipe a try. With its delicate custard, sweet cherries, and simple preparation, it’s sure to become a beloved addition to your dessert repertoire. Bon appétit!
