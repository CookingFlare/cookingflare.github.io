The Best Sous Vide Recipe for Duck Breast


Sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we cook, and duck breast is one of the many ingredients that has benefited from this technique. The result is a perfectly cooked, tender, and juicy duck breast with a crispy skin. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to achieve this mouthwatering dish using your sous vide machine.

Ingredients:

  • 1 duck breast, skin on
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the duck breast: Rinse the duck breast under cold water, pat it dry with paper towels, and season it with salt and pepper.
  2. Create the marinade: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, garlic, honey, soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, and thyme.
  3. Marinate the duck breast: Place the duck breast in a large ziplock bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the duck breast, turning it to coat evenly. Seal the bag or cover the dish with plastic wrap. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Sous vide the duck breast: Remove the duck breast from the marinade, letting any excess liquid drip off. Place the duck breast in a sous vide bag or a heat-safe container that can fit in your sous vide machine. Seal the bag or cover the container with plastic wrap.
  5. Cook the duck breast: Set your sous vide machine to 130°F (54°C). Cook the duck breast for 1-2 hours, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. For medium-rare, cook for 1 hour and 15 minutes. For medium, cook for 1 hour and 30 minutes.
  6. Sear the duck breast: Remove the duck breast from the sous vide machine and pat it dry with paper towels. Heat a skillet over high heat and add a small amount of oil. Sear the duck breast for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until the skin is crispy and golden brown.
  7. Serve: Slice the duck breast against the grain and serve immediately. You can serve it with your favorite sides, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a salad.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a more intense flavor, you can add more aromatics to the marinade, such as onion, carrot, and celery.
  • If you prefer a crispy skin without searing, you can cook the duck breast in the oven after sous vide cooking. Simply preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and cook the duck breast for an additional 10-15 minutes.
  • To make the dish more substantial, you can add some sautéed mushrooms, spinach, or other vegetables to the pan with the duck breast.
  • For a different flavor profile, you can substitute the honey with maple syrup or the soy sauce with tamari.

Benefits of Sous Vide Cooking:

  • Consistent results: Sous vide cooking ensures that your duck breast is cooked to the same level of doneness every time.
  • Easy to cook: Simply place the duck breast in the sous vide machine and set the temperature. You can’t get any easier than that!
  • Tender and juicy: Sous vide cooking helps retain the natural moisture of the duck breast, resulting in a tender and juicy texture.
  • Crispy skin: The sous vide machine allows you to cook the duck breast to the perfect temperature for searing, resulting in a crispy and golden brown skin.

Conclusion:

Sous vide cooking has revolutionized the way we cook duck breast, and this recipe is a testament to its versatility and ease. With this recipe, you can achieve a perfectly cooked, tender, and juicy duck breast with a crispy skin. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress your family and friends. So, go ahead and give it a try!
