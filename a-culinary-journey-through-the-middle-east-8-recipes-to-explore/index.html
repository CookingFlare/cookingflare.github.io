A Culinary Journey Through the Middle East: 8 Recipes to Explore


The Middle East is a region rich in history, culture, and, most importantly, food. The culinary traditions of this area are as diverse as its landscapes, with each country offering unique flavors, ingredients, and cooking techniques. From the aromatic spices of Morocco to the fresh herbs of Lebanon, the Middle Eastern cuisine is a delightful tapestry of tastes and textures. In this blog post, we will embark on a culinary journey through the Middle East, exploring eight iconic recipes that will transport your taste buds to this vibrant region.

1. Hummus: The Creamy Chickpea Dip

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Paprika and parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, cumin, and salt.
  2. Blend until smooth, adding water a tablespoon at a time until you reach your desired consistency.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  4. Transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with paprika and chopped parsley.
  5. Serve with warm pita bread or fresh vegetables.

Hummus is a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine, often enjoyed as a dip or spread. Its creamy texture and nutty flavor make it a perfect appetizer or snack. This versatile dish can be customized with various toppings, such as roasted red peppers or olives, to suit your taste.

2. Falafel: Crispy Chickpea Fritters

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, baking powder, salt, and pepper. Pulse until a coarse mixture forms.
  2. Shape the mixture into small balls or patties.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  4. Drain on paper towels and serve with tahini sauce or in pita bread with fresh vegetables.

Falafel is a beloved street food throughout the Middle East. These crispy chickpea fritters are not only delicious but also packed with protein, making them a satisfying vegetarian option. Enjoy them in a wrap or as part of a mezze platter.

3. Shawarma: Flavorful Spiced Meat Wrap

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs or beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pita bread or flatbreads for serving
  • Toppings: lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, tahini sauce

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine olive oil, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, cinnamon, garlic, salt, and pepper. Add the sliced meat and marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the marinated meat until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Slice the cooked meat into thin strips and serve in pita bread with your choice of toppings and tahini sauce.

Shawarma is a popular street food that has gained international fame. The marinated meat is cooked to perfection, resulting in a flavorful and juicy filling for wraps. Customize your shawarma with your favorite vegetables and sauces for a delicious meal.

4. Tabouli: Fresh Parsley Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup bulgur wheat
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup tomatoes, diced
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place bulgur wheat in a bowl and pour boiling water over it. Cover and let it sit for about 30 minutes until the water is absorbed and the bulgur is tender.
