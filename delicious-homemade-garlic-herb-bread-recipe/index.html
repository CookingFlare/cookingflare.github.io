Delicious Homemade Garlic Herb Bread Recipe


If you’re looking for a way to elevate your meals with something comforting, flavorful, and homemade, look no further than this delicious garlic herb bread recipe. Perfectly golden and crispy on the outside with a soft and fluffy inside, this bread is loaded with aromatic garlic and fresh herbs that infuse every bite. Whether you’re serving it with a bowl of pasta, enjoying it with a hearty soup, or simply indulging in a slice with butter, this garlic herb bread is a delightful treat.

Why You’ll Love This Garlic Herb Bread

This garlic herb bread isn’t just any ordinary loaf of bread. It’s infused with the bold flavors of fresh garlic, parsley, and rosemary, making it the ideal accompaniment to a variety of dishes. The texture is perfectly balanced, offering a crusty exterior and a tender interior that will have you reaching for seconds.

The best part? It’s incredibly simple to make at home with a few basic ingredients. With minimal preparation, you’ll have freshly baked bread on your table that rivals anything you’d get from a bakery. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting, this recipe is perfect for anyone who loves the smell of homemade bread filling their kitchen.

Ingredients for Homemade Garlic Herb Bread

Before you get started, gather the following ingredients:

  • For the dough:

    • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
    • 1 ¼ cups warm water (about 110°F or 43°C)
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil

  • For the garlic herb butter:

    • 6 cloves garlic, minced
    • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
    • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or dried rosemary)
    • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme (optional)
    • ½ teaspoon salt
    • ¼ teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions for Preparing Garlic Herb Bread

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Begin by activating the yeast, which is a crucial first step in making sure your dough rises properly. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes, until the mixture becomes frothy. If it doesn’t bubble up, your yeast might be expired, and you’ll need to start over with a fresh batch of yeast.

Step 2: Prepare the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt. Create a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Using a wooden spoon or your hands, mix everything together until the dough begins to form. Once it does, turn the dough out onto a clean, floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes.

Kneading is essential because it helps to activate the gluten, giving the bread its structure. You’ll know the dough is ready when it’s smooth and elastic. If it’s too sticky, sprinkle in a bit more flour, but be cautious not to add too much, as this can make the bread dense.

Step 3: Let the Dough Rise

Place your dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free spot for about 1 to 1 ½ hours, or until it doubles in size. This rise is crucial for achieving a soft and airy interior, so be patient.

Step 4: Prepare the Garlic Herb Butter

While the dough is rising, prepare the garlic herb butter. In a small bowl, melt the butter and then stir in the minced garlic, chopped parsley, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. The garlic should infuse the butter as it melts, creating a flavorful base for the bread topping.

Step 5: Shape the Dough

Once your dough has risen, punch it down to release the air bubbles. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and shape it into a loaf or divide it into smaller rolls, depending on your preference. If you’re making a loaf, roll the dough into a log shape and tuck the ends underneath.

Step 6: Second Rise

Place the shaped dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet or into a greased loaf pan, depending on what shape you’re going for. Cover it with a towel again and let it rise for an additional 30 minutes. This second rise will allow the dough to become light and fluffy once baked.

Step 7: Bake the Bread

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) during the second rise. Once the dough has risen, it’s time to bake. Brush the top of the loaf generously with the prepared garlic herb butter mixture, ensuring the garlic and herbs cover the surface evenly. You can also reserve some of the garlic herb butter to brush on after baking for extra flavor.

Bake the bread in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. Keep an eye on the bread during the last few minutes of baking to prevent over-browning.

Step 8: Finishing Touches

Once the bread is done, remove it from the oven and allow it to cool for a few minutes. If you reserved any garlic herb butter, now’s the time to brush it over the top of the hot bread. This extra layer of garlic and herb goodness will add a burst of flavor that complements the crispy crust perfectly.

Let the bread cool slightly before slicing into it. The aroma will be irresistible, and the texture will be perfect—crusty on the outside and soft on the inside, just like a bakery-quality loaf.

Tips for Perfect Garlic Herb Bread

  • Let the dough rise properly: The key to a soft, fluffy loaf is allowing the dough to rise sufficiently. Don’t rush this process—let the dough double in size before baking.

  • Use fresh herbs: Fresh garlic and herbs provide the best flavor. If you don’t have fresh rosemary or thyme, you can use dried versions, but fresh will give you a more vibrant taste.

  • Adjust seasoning to taste: The beauty of this recipe is in the versatility. You can adjust the amount of garlic or herbs based on your personal preference. Add more garlic for a stronger flavor or reduce the amount of rosemary if you prefer a subtler taste.

  • Make ahead: This garlic herb bread is perfect for making ahead. After shaping the dough, you can refrigerate it for a slower rise overnight. Just take it out of the fridge about 30 minutes before baking to allow it to come to room temperature.

  • Storage: If you have any leftovers, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. You can also freeze slices of the bread to enjoy later—just toast them in the oven to bring back the crispy texture.

Serving Suggestions for Garlic Herb Bread

This homemade garlic herb bread pairs beautifully with a variety of meals. Here are a few ideas to serve it with:

  • Pasta: Serve with your favorite pasta dish like spaghetti marinara, Alfredo, or a rich lasagna.

  • Soup: A warm bowl of soup, such as tomato soup, chicken noodle, or creamy potato leek, is the perfect match for this bread. The garlic herb flavors complement the richness of the soup.

  • Salads: Garlic herb bread also pairs well with salads, especially heartier ones like a Caesar salad or a Mediterranean-inspired salad with olives, feta cheese, and cucumbers.

  • As an appetizer: Serve the bread as a starter with dips like hummus, olive tapenade, or a creamy spinach artichoke dip for a flavorful appetizer at your next dinner party.

Conclusion

Homemade garlic herb bread is a must-try recipe for anyone who loves the warm, comforting aroma of freshly baked bread. With its perfect balance of crispy crust, tender interior, and flavorful garlic herb butter, this bread elevates any meal and makes the experience that much more enjoyable.

Don’t be intimidated by the idea of making bread from scratch. With just a few simple ingredients and some patience, you can have a warm loaf of garlic herb bread ready to enjoy in just a couple of hours. Whether it’s served alongside pasta, soup, or enjoyed on its own, this recipe will quickly become a staple in your kitchen. So go ahead and give it a try—you’ll be glad you did!
