The Best Thanksgiving Appetizers for a Crowd


Thanksgiving is a time to gather with family and friends, share gratitude, and of course, enjoy some delicious food. While the turkey and stuffing are often the stars of the show, Thanksgiving appetizers set the tone for the feast. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a large crowd, having a variety of appetizers ensures your guests have plenty to snack on while they wait for the main meal. In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the best Thanksgiving appetizers that are sure to impress your guests and keep them satisfied until the main event.

1. Crispy Bacon-Wrapped Brussels Sprouts

Brussels sprouts often get a bad rap, but when they’re wrapped in crispy bacon and roasted to perfection, they become a savory treat that’s hard to resist. These bite-sized appetizers are perfect for passing around during cocktail hour, and the combination of smoky bacon and tender Brussels sprouts is always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 16 Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 8 slices of bacon, cut in half
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 teaspoons maple syrup (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the Brussels sprouts with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Wrap each Brussels sprout half with a slice of bacon and secure it with a toothpick.
  4. Place the bacon-wrapped Brussels sprouts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  5. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until the bacon is crispy and the Brussels sprouts are tender.
  6. Drizzle with a touch of maple syrup for an added sweet-savory flavor, if desired.
  7. Serve immediately, and watch them disappear!

2. Mini Spinach and Artichoke Dip Bites

Spinach and artichoke dip is a classic favorite, but these mini versions take the dish to a whole new level. Perfect for parties, these little bites are filled with creamy spinach and artichoke dip and baked in a flaky puff pastry. They’re easy to make and even easier to eat, making them a fantastic option for your Thanksgiving appetizer spread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 cup frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • 1 cup artichoke hearts, chopped
  • ½ cup cream cheese, softened
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ¼ cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the puff pastry on a lightly floured surface and cut it into small squares (about 2 inches each).
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the spinach, artichokes, cream cheese, sour cream, Parmesan, mozzarella, salt, and pepper.
  4. Place a small spoonful of the mixture in the center of each puff pastry square.
  5. Fold the corners of the puff pastry over the filling to form little parcels.
  6. Place the pastry bites on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until the puff pastry is golden brown.
  8. Serve warm and enjoy the creamy, cheesy goodness!

3. Sweet Potato Crostini with Cranberry Relish

Sweet potatoes and cranberries are two ingredients that scream Thanksgiving, and when combined, they create a stunning appetizer. This recipe takes a simple crostini and elevates it with a savory-sweet topping that pairs beautifully with the crispy baguette. It’s a fresh take on traditional Thanksgiving flavors that your guests will love.

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf French baguette, sliced
  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • ¼ cup orange juice
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Arrange the baguette slices on a baking sheet and brush both sides with olive oil. Toast them in the oven for 7-8 minutes or until crispy and golden.
  3. Meanwhile, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the sweet potato slices and cook for about 5-7 minutes, until tender. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. In a saucepan, combine the cranberries, orange juice, sugar, and balsamic vinegar. Simmer over medium heat for 8-10 minutes until the cranberries burst and the mixture thickens slightly.
  5. To assemble, place a slice of sweet potato on each toasted baguette slice. Top with a spoonful of cranberry relish.
  6. Garnish with fresh thyme and serve.

4. Stuffed Mushrooms with Sausage and Cream Cheese

Stuffed mushrooms are a holiday classic, and this version with sausage and cream cheese will have your guests coming back for more. The savory sausage combined with the creamy filling makes these mushrooms the perfect bite-sized appetizer for any Thanksgiving gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 24 large mushrooms, stems removed
  • 1/2 pound breakfast sausage, crumbled
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a skillet, cook the sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Add the garlic to the skillet and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked sausage, cream cheese, Parmesan cheese, breadcrumbs, salt, and pepper.
  5. Spoon the mixture into the hollowed-out mushrooms and place them on a baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden.
  7. Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

5. Roasted Butternut Squash and Goat Cheese Dip

This creamy, savory dip is a perfect combination of fall flavors. The sweetness of roasted butternut squash pairs beautifully with the tangy goat cheese, making it a unique and memorable appetizer. Serve it with crackers or vegetable sticks for a delicious dip that will stand out at your Thanksgiving celebration.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 4 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash with olive oil, cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Spread the squash out in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  3. Roast the squash for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  4. In a food processor, combine the roasted squash with goat cheese and sour cream. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  5. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh parsley.
  6. Serve with crackers, sliced baguette, or vegetable sticks for dipping.

6. Cheese and Charcuterie Board

Sometimes the simplest appetizers are the most elegant. A cheese and charcuterie board offers a variety of flavors and textures that appeal to a wide range of tastes. Featuring an assortment of cheeses, meats, nuts, fruits, and crackers, this appetizer allows guests to graze as they mingle and enjoy the festivities.

Ingredients:

  • A selection of cheeses (cheddar, brie, goat cheese, blue cheese)
  • Assorted cured meats (prosciutto, salami, chorizo)
  • Fresh and dried fruits (grapes, figs, apples, apricots)
  • Nuts (almonds, walnuts, pecans)
  • Crackers and bread (baguette slices, crackers, breadsticks)
  • Olives and pickles

Directions:

  1. On a large platter or wooden board, arrange the cheeses in different sections.
  2. Surround the cheeses with the cured meats, folding them into neat piles.
  3. Add a variety of fruits and nuts to fill in any gaps on the board.
  4. Place crackers and bread around the edges for easy access.
  5. Add small bowls of olives and pickles for extra flavor.
  6. Serve at room temperature and allow guests to enjoy the variety of flavors.

Conclusion

These Thanksgiving appetizers for a crowd are guaranteed to elevate your holiday gathering and satisfy your guests’ appetites before the main course. Whether you’re serving savory stuffed mushrooms, sweet potato crostini, or a classic cheese board, these appetizers incorporate the flavors of the season and are easy to prepare. By offering a mix of hot, cold, sweet, and savory options, you’ll create a spread that appeals to all tastes and ensures your Thanksgiving feast gets off to a delicious start.
