How to Make Spanish Seafood Salad: A Refreshing Recipe


Spanish seafood salad, known as “ensalada de mariscos,” is a vibrant and refreshing dish that beautifully captures the essence of coastal Spain. With its medley of fresh seafood, colorful vegetables, and zesty dressing, this salad is perfect for a light lunch, a starter for dinner, or even as a delightful side dish at your next gathering. In this post, we’ll take you through the steps to create an authentic Spanish seafood salad that is both delicious and visually appealing.

Ingredients

To prepare a traditional Spanish seafood salad, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Seafood:

  • 200 grams of cooked shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200 grams of cooked calamari, sliced into rings
  • 150 grams of mussels, cleaned and cooked (optional)
  • 200 grams of cooked octopus, sliced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Vegetables:

  • 1 cup of cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 medium cucumber, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bell pepper, diced
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup of sweet corn (fresh or canned)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

For the Dressing:

  • 1/4 cup of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Seafood

  1. Cook the Seafood: If you’re starting with raw seafood, ensure you cook it properly. For shrimp, boil them in salted water for about 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and opaque. For calamari, a quick boil of 1-2 minutes is sufficient. Mussels should be cooked until they open, and octopus can be boiled for about 30-40 minutes until tender. Drain and let cool.

  2. Chop the Seafood: Once the seafood has cooled, chop it into bite-sized pieces. This makes it easier to mix with the vegetables and ensures each bite is packed with flavor.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

  1. Chop and Dice: While your seafood is cooling, prepare the vegetables. Halve the cherry tomatoes, dice the cucumber and bell peppers, and finely chop the red onion. If using fresh corn, you can steam or boil it briefly for tenderness.

  2. Combine the Vegetables: In a large mixing bowl, combine all the chopped vegetables. The vibrant colors will make your salad visually appealing!

Step 3: Make the Dressing

  1. Whisk the Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, apple cider vinegar, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, and minced garlic. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the acidity by adding more vinegar or lemon juice according to your preference.

  2. Taste and Adjust: Give your dressing a taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. The dressing should be zesty and complement the seafood and vegetables perfectly.

Step 4: Combine Everything

  1. Mix the Seafood and Vegetables: Add the chopped seafood to the bowl of vegetables. Gently toss everything together with a spatula to ensure even distribution.

  2. Add the Dressing: Drizzle the dressing over the seafood and vegetable mixture. Carefully fold everything together until well combined. Be gentle to avoid breaking up the seafood too much.

  3. Season to Taste: Give the salad a final taste and add more salt, pepper, or dressing if needed.

Step 5: Chill and Serve

  1. Refrigerate: For the best flavor, let the salad chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.

  2. Garnish: Before serving, sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top for a touch of color and freshness.

  3. Serve: Serve the Spanish seafood salad in individual bowls or on a large platter. It’s perfect as a standalone dish or alongside crusty bread.

Tips for the Best Spanish Seafood Salad

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: The quality of seafood and vegetables directly impacts the flavor of your salad. Opt for the freshest ingredients available to elevate your dish.
  • Experiment with Seafood: Feel free to include other seafood options such as crab, lobster, or scallops. Each type of seafood brings its unique flavor and texture to the salad.
  • Add a Kick: If you enjoy some heat, consider adding sliced jalapeños or a dash of hot sauce to the dressing for a spicy twist.
  • Vegan Option: For a plant-based version of this salad, you can replace seafood with avocado, chickpeas, or marinated tofu, providing a protein-rich alternative.

Pairing Suggestions

Spanish seafood salad pairs beautifully with a variety of beverages. Here are a few suggestions:

  • White Wine: A crisp white wine like Albariño or Verdejo complements the seafood perfectly.
  • Sparkling Water: For a refreshing non-alcoholic option, serve with chilled sparkling water and a slice of lemon.
  • Light Beer: A light lager or pilsner can also accompany this dish well, balancing the flavors of the salad.

Conclusion

Making a Spanish seafood salad is not only easy but also a delightful way to celebrate the flavors of the Mediterranean. With its combination of fresh seafood, crunchy vegetables, and zesty dressing, this dish is sure to impress your guests or satisfy your cravings. Whether served at a summer barbecue or as a light lunch on a hot day, this refreshing salad is a versatile addition to any meal. Enjoy every bite and savor the taste of Spain right at your table!
