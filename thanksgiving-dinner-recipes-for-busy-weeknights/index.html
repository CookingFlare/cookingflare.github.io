Thanksgiving Dinner Recipes for Busy Weeknights


Thanksgiving is a cherished time of year, filled with love, gratitude, and delicious meals. But let’s be honest—many of us don’t have hours to dedicate to elaborate dishes during busy weeknights. Whether you’re juggling work, family, or seasonal planning, it’s possible to enjoy the flavors of Thanksgiving without spending an entire evening in the kitchen. With these simple yet savory recipes, you can bring the spirit of the holiday to your table and still have time to relax.

Sheet Pan Turkey Dinner

When time is tight, sheet pan meals can be a lifesaver. This recipe combines tender turkey, roasted vegetables, and a hint of cranberries for a meal that feels festive without overwhelming your schedule.

Ingredients

  • 1½ lbs turkey tenderloin or cutlets
  • 1 cup baby carrots
  • 1½ cups cubed sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¾ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ cup dried cranberries
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large bowl, toss the carrots, sweet potatoes, and Brussels sprouts with olive oil, garlic powder, thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Arrange the vegetables on one side of a large sheet pan. Place the turkey tenderloins on the other side, brushing them lightly with olive oil and seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, turning the vegetables halfway through. Ensure the turkey reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C).
  5. Sprinkle cranberries over the vegetables and turkey in the final five minutes of cooking.
  6. Serve warm for a quick, balanced Thanksgiving-inspired meal.

Quick Cranberry Sauce

No Thanksgiving dinner is complete without cranberry sauce, yet the thought of simmering fruit sauce from scratch can feel like a commitment. This 15-minute recipe offers all the flavor with minimal effort.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen cranberries
  • ½ cup orange juice
  • ½ cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine cranberries, orange juice, and maple syrup.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the cranberries begin to pop (about 10 minutes).
  3. Add the cinnamon stick and vanilla extract, simmering for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the cinnamon stick and let the sauce cool slightly.
  5. Serve alongside your meal or refrigerate for up to a week.

This recipe pairs perfectly with turkey or chicken and complements roasted vegetables beautifully.

Turkey and Stuffing Skillet

This one-pan wonder combines turkey, stuffing, and gravy for the ultimate weeknight comfort food. It’s quick to prepare, loaded with flavor, and requires minimal cleanup—what’s not to love?

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 1½ cups prepared stuffing (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup chicken or turkey gravy
  • ½ cup frozen peas and carrots
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the ground turkey until browned, breaking it apart as it cooks. Season with onion powder, sage, salt, and pepper.
  2. Stir in the frozen peas and carrots, cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add the stuffing to the skillet, mixing gently to combine.
  4. Pour in the gravy and simmer for 5 minutes until heated through.
  5. Serve the dish directly from the skillet and enjoy a hearty meal that delivers Thanksgiving vibes without requiring hours of prep.

Mashed Sweet Potato Bowls

Transform sweet potatoes into a quick, wholesome meal with this versatile recipe. Packed with flavor and color, these bowls are ideal for busy weeknights when you’re craving warmth and comfort.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • ¼ cup toasted pecans or walnuts (optional)
  • ¼ cup dried cranberries
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the sweet potato cubes until tender, about 10-12 minutes. Drain and transfer them to a mixing bowl.
  2. Add butter, maple syrup, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt to the bowl. Mash the sweet potatoes until smooth and creamy.
  3. Divide the mashed sweet potatoes into bowls. Top with toasted nuts and dried cranberries for texture and flair.
  4. Serve as a side dish or a light dinner option.

Turkey and Cranberry Wraps

When time is of the essence, these easy wraps are perfect for using leftover turkey while keeping your meal festive and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 4 large whole-grain tortillas
  • 1¼ cups cooked turkey, shredded or sliced
  • ½ cup cranberry sauce
  • 1 cup spinach or arugula
  • ½ cup cream cheese or whipped goat cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Spread a thin layer of cream cheese or goat cheese over each tortilla.
  2. Add a layer of spinach or arugula, followed by shredded turkey.
  3. Top generously with cranberry sauce and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Wrap tightly, slice in half, and serve.

These wraps make a fantastic grab-and-go option, perfect for busy evenings or packed lunches.

Shortcut Green Bean Casserole

A Thanksgiving classic reimagined for weeknight ease, this simplified green bean casserole relies on shortcuts without sacrificing the signature creamy-crunchy perfection.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb fresh or frozen green beans
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • ½ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 cup crispy fried onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. If using fresh green beans, blanch them in boiling water for two minutes and drain. Skip this step if using frozen green beans.
  3. Combine green beans, cream of mushroom soup, milk, garlic powder, salt, and pepper in a mixing bowl.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a small baking dish and top with fried onions.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until bubbling and golden on top.
  6. Serve alongside turkey, chicken, or your favorite main dish for a no-fuss Thanksgiving-inspired side.

Pumpkin Pie Mug Cake

End your meal with a personal-sized dessert that comes together in minutes. This pumpkin pie mug cake delivers the cozy essence of Thanksgiving without requiring an oven (or much effort).

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons canned pumpkin puree
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour or almond flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar or brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon milk
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of nutmeg and cloves
  • Whipped cream (optional, for topping)

Instructions

  1. In a microwave-safe mug, mix the pumpkin puree, flour, sugar, milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves until smooth.
  2. Microwave on high for 90 seconds, or until the cake is set.
  3. Let the mug cake cool for a minute before topping with whipped cream.
  4. Serve warm for an individual dessert that brings pumpkin perfection to your weeknight.

Balancing the spirit of Thanksgiving with the chaos of weekday schedules doesn’t have to be a challenge. From quick turkey dinners to cozy desserts, these recipes prove that you can savor the flavors of the season without compromising your time. Try one or mix and match several throughout the week—you’ll be amazed at how these dishes can transform busy nights into festive moments.
