Tasty Grilled Swordfish Steak with Citrus Marinade


Grilling is one of the most enjoyable ways to prepare fish, and swordfish is a standout choice for any grilling enthusiast. With its firm texture and mild flavor, swordfish steaks are perfect for absorbing marinades, making them a delicious option for a summer barbecue or a cozy dinner at home. In this post, we’ll explore how to create a tasty grilled swordfish steak with a vibrant citrus marinade that will elevate your dining experience.

Why Choose Swordfish?

Swordfish is a meaty fish that holds up well on the grill, making it an excellent choice for those who enjoy a hearty seafood dish. Unlike more delicate fish, swordfish can withstand high heat without falling apart, allowing for beautiful grill marks and a satisfying texture. Additionally, swordfish is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, making it a healthy option for your meals.

Ingredients for the Citrus Marinade

To create a flavorful citrus marinade, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 4 swordfish steaks (about 6 ounces each)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme or rosemary (optional)

These ingredients combine to create a marinade that not only enhances the flavor of the swordfish but also helps to keep it moist during grilling.

Preparing the Marinade

  1. Mix the Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, orange juice, lemon zest, orange zest, minced garlic, honey, salt, and black pepper. If you’re using fresh herbs, add them to the mixture as well.

  2. Marinate the Swordfish: Place the swordfish steaks in a shallow dish or a resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the fish, ensuring that each steak is well-coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, but no more than 2 hours. This allows the flavors to penetrate the fish without breaking it down too much.

Preparing the Grill

While the swordfish is marinating, it’s time to prepare your grill. Whether you’re using a gas grill or charcoal, follow these steps:

  1. Preheat the Grill: Heat your grill to medium-high heat (about 400°F to 450°F). This temperature is ideal for achieving a nice sear on the swordfish.

  2. Clean and Oil the Grates: Use a grill brush to clean the grates thoroughly. Once clean, oil the grates with a paper towel dipped in vegetable oil to prevent the fish from sticking.

Grilling the Swordfish

  1. Remove from Marinade: Take the swordfish steaks out of the marinade and let any excess drip off. Discard the leftover marinade to avoid contamination.

  2. Grill the Steaks: Place the swordfish steaks on the grill. Grill for about 4 to 6 minutes on one side, depending on the thickness of the steaks. You want to achieve a nice golden-brown color and grill marks.

  3. Flip and Finish Cooking: Carefully flip the steaks using a spatula and grill for an additional 4 to 6 minutes on the other side. The fish is done when it is opaque and flakes easily with a fork. Aim for an internal temperature of 145°F for perfectly cooked swordfish.

  4. Rest Before Serving: Once cooked, remove the swordfish from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute, ensuring a moist and flavorful steak.

Serving Suggestions

Grilled swordfish pairs beautifully with a variety of sides. Here are some ideas to complement your meal:

  • Grilled Vegetables: Bell peppers, zucchini, and asparagus make excellent accompaniments. Simply toss them in olive oil, salt, and pepper, and grill alongside the swordfish.

  • Citrus Salad: A fresh salad with mixed greens, avocado, and citrus segments (like oranges and grapefruits) can enhance the meal’s brightness.

  • Rice or Quinoa: Serve the swordfish over a bed of fluffy rice or quinoa for a hearty and satisfying dish.

  • Herb Sauce: Drizzle a simple herb sauce made from parsley, garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil over the grilled swordfish for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips for Perfect Grilled Swordfish

  • Don’t Overcook: Swordfish can become dry if overcooked. Keep an eye on the cooking time and use a meat thermometer to check for doneness.

  • Choose Quality Fish: Look for fresh, sustainably sourced swordfish. The quality of the fish will significantly impact the final dish.

  • Experiment with Marinades: While the citrus marinade is delicious, feel free to experiment with other flavors. Soy sauce, ginger, and herbs can create a different but equally tasty profile.

Conclusion

Grilling swordfish steaks with a citrus marinade is a delightful way to enjoy this versatile fish. The combination of zesty flavors and the smoky char from the grill creates a dish that is both satisfying and refreshing. Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue or enjoying a quiet dinner at home, this recipe is sure to impress. So fire up the grill, gather your ingredients, and savor the deliciousness of grilled swordfish steak with citrus marinade!
