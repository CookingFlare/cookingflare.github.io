Step-by-Step No-Churn Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream


If you’ve ever dreamed of creating a luscious, creamy ice cream that captures the essence of strawberry cheesecake without the hassle of an ice cream maker, this no-churn strawberry cheesecake ice cream recipe is your ticket to sweet bliss. Combining the rich tanginess of cream cheese with the natural sweetness of ripe strawberries, this recipe promises a velvety texture, bursting with flavor, that’s guaranteed to become a family favorite.

Why No-Churn Ice Cream?

Traditional ice cream recipes often require specialized equipment like ice cream makers that churn the mixture as it freezes to prevent ice crystals and maintain smoothness. However, the no-churn method cleverly uses whipped cream and sweetened condensed milk to replicate that creamy texture without any fancy gadgets. This recipe offers a convenient way to enjoy homemade ice cream, perfect for summer days, celebrations, or whenever you crave a delightful dessert.

Ingredients: What You’ll Need

Cream Base

  • 1 and 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream – Whipping cream gives the ice cream its creamy, rich texture.
  • 1 cup sweetened condensed milk – This ingredient sweetens and thickens the ice cream naturally.
  • 4 oz cream cheese, softened – The star of the cheesecake flavor; softening ensures smooth incorporation.

Strawberry Swirl

  • 1 and 1/2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced – For a fresh and vibrant strawberry taste.
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar – To macerate the strawberries, enhancing their natural juices.
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice – Adds brightness and balances sweetness.

Additional Flavor Enhancers

  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract – Deepens the overall flavor.
  • Pinch of salt – Balances the sweetness and elevates flavors.

Equipment Needed

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Electric mixer (handheld or stand mixer)
  • Blender or food processor
  • Spatula
  • Freezer-safe container with lid

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Strawberry Swirl

Start by prepping your strawberries, which will provide the fresh fruit element in this ice cream. Combine the sliced strawberries, granulated sugar, and lemon juice in a medium bowl. Stir well and let the mixture sit for at least 20 to 30 minutes at room temperature, or until the strawberries are juicy and slightly softened.

Once macerated, transfer the mixture to a blender or food processor. Pulse until you reach a chunky puree consistency—not a smooth sauce; retaining some texture gives the ice cream a delightful mouthfeel. Set aside.

Step 2: Whip the Cream

Pour the heavy whipping cream into a large mixing bowl. Using an electric mixer, whip on medium-high speed until stiff peaks form. This step is critical because the whipped cream is what gives the ice cream its fluffy, light texture without churning.

Step 3: Blend the Cream Cheese Mixture

In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth and creamy. Gradually add the sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt to the cream cheese, mixing well until the mixture is silky and uniform.

Step 4: Combine Cream Cheese Mixture with Whipped Cream

Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture in batches. Folding, rather than stirring vigorously, keeps the air in the whipped cream to maintain lightness. Use a spatula to carefully mix until all the white streaks disappear and the mixture looks smooth.

Step 5: Add Strawberry Swirl

Pour half of the ice cream base into your freezer-safe container. Spoon half of the strawberry puree on top. Using a knife or skewer, gently swirl the strawberry mixture into the ice cream base. Repeat layering with the remaining ice cream base and strawberry puree, finishing with a few additional swirls on the top for visual appeal.

Step 6: Freeze and Set

Cover your container tightly with a lid or plastic wrap to prevent ice crystals from forming. Place it in the freezer and let it freeze for at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. The ice cream will firm up as it chills.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

When ready to serve, remove the ice cream from the freezer about 5 to 10 minutes beforehand to soften slightly, making scooping easier. Use an ice cream scoop or sturdy spoon to dish out generous servings. You can garnish with fresh strawberries, crushed graham crackers, or a drizzle of strawberry syrup for extra flair.

Tips for Perfect No-Churn Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream

  • Use ripe, in-season strawberries: They provide the best flavor and natural sweetness.
  • Soften cream cheese thoroughly: Room temperature cream cheese blends more easily, preventing lumpy ice cream.
  • Don’t overmix whipped cream: Overbeaten cream can turn buttery; stop whipping when stiff peaks form.
  • Swirl carefully: For distinct strawberry ribbons, swirl gently but don’t mix completely.
  • Store properly: Keep ice cream sealed and refrigerated to maintain freshness and texture.
  • Experiment with mix-ins: Consider adding crushed graham crackers, chocolate chips, or toasted nuts for a crunchy surprise.

Variations and Serving Suggestions

Blueberry Cheesecake Ice Cream

Simply swap strawberries for fresh or frozen blueberries, adjusting sugar as needed for sweetness. Blueberry puree creates a beautiful purple swirl and a slightly tangy twist.

Vegan No-Churn Ice Cream

Use coconut cream in place of heavy cream and a dairy-free condensed milk alternative to create a luscious vegan version while keeping the cheesecake flavor with vegan cream cheese.

Mini Cheesecake Bits

To elevate this dessert, fold in small chunks of baked cheesecake before freezing. This adds an indulgent, creamy texture in every bite.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I make this ice cream dairy-free?

Yes! Substitute the heavy cream with coconut cream and use a plant-based cream cheese. Sweetened condensed coconut milk works as a dairy-free alternative as well.

How long can I store homemade ice cream?

When kept airtight in the freezer, the no-churn ice cream can last up to 2 weeks with minimal loss in texture and flavor.

Can I prepare this without fresh strawberries?

Frozen strawberries work well. Just thaw completely before macerating and pulsing. Frozen blackberries or raspberries can also create delicious variants.

Wrapping It Up

This no-churn strawberry cheesecake ice cream marries classic cheesecake richness with the fresh brightness of strawberries, all without needing a fancy machine. It’s an accessible recipe that delivers gourmet-level satisfaction with simple pantry staples and a little patience while it freezes. Perfect for summer parties, family dinners, or anytime you desire an extraordinary homemade dessert, this recipe invites you to indulge in creamy, dreamy sweetness with every spoonful.
