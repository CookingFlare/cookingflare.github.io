Hearty Minestrone Soup Recipe - Italian Vegetable Delight


There’s something truly comforting about a warm bowl of soup, especially when it’s packed with hearty vegetables and aromatic Italian flavors. Today, I’m excited to share with you my take on the classic Hearty Minestrone Soup. This recipe is a celebration of vibrant, seasonal vegetables, rich tomato broth, and the cozy warmth of Italian herbs. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, this soup is sure to become a favorite in your home.

Ingredients

To start our culinary journey, let’s gather the essential ingredients for this delightful Hearty Minestrone Soup:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 yellow squash, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and chopped
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup small pasta (such as ditalini or elbow macaroni)
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil, for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  2. Add the diced carrots, celery, zucchini, yellow squash, and green beans to the pot. Sauté the vegetables for 5-7 minutes, allowing them to soften slightly.

  3. Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth, stirring to combine. Bring the soup to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Once the vegetables are tender, add the drained kidney beans, cannellini beans, and pasta to the pot. Continue simmering the soup for 10-12 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente.

  5. Season the soup with dried oregano, thyme, rosemary, salt, and black pepper. Adjust the seasoning to your taste preferences, adding more herbs or salt as needed.

  6. Remove the pot from the heat and let the soup rest for a few minutes before serving. Ladle the Hearty Minestrone Soup into bowls, garnish with fresh basil, and offer grated Parmesan cheese on the side for those who desire an extra savory touch.

Serving Suggestions

This Hearty Minestrone Soup is a complete meal in itself, but it pairs beautifully with a slice of crusty Italian bread or a simple green salad. For a heartier meal, consider serving it alongside a platter of assorted Italian cheeses and cured meats.

Conclusion

The beauty of this Hearty Minestrone Soup lies in its versatility. Feel free to customize the recipe based on the vegetables you have on hand or your personal preferences. This soup is a fantastic way to enjoy the bountiful flavors of the season and nourish your body with wholesome, plant-based ingredients.

I hope this recipe brings a taste of Italy to your table and becomes a beloved addition to your culinary repertoire. Buon appetito!

This Hearty Minestrone Soup Recipe is a delightful celebration of Italian flavors and vibrant vegetables. Packed with aromatic herbs, tender beans, and al dente pasta, this soup is a comforting and satisfying meal. Whether enjoyed as a light lunch or a cozy dinner, this recipe is sure to warm your heart and nourish your soul.
