Korean Glass Noodle Stir-Fry: A Quick and Easy Dish for Busy Days


Are you looking for a delicious yet quick and easy meal to whip up on those busy weeknights? Look no further than this Korean Glass Noodle Stir-Fry recipe! Packed with flavor, colorful vegetables, and chewy glass noodles, this dish is a surefire way to satisfy your taste buds and have dinner on the table in no time.

Ingredients

  • 200g Korean sweet potato glass noodles
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups spinach
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: 200g thinly sliced beef or tofu for protein

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Glass Noodles: Start by soaking the Korean sweet potato glass noodles in hot water for about 10-15 minutes or until they are soft and pliable. Once softened, drain and set aside.

  2. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, brown sugar, rice vinegar, gochujang, and sesame seeds. Set the sauce aside.

  3. Stir-Fry: Heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant. If using beef or tofu, add it to the pan and cook until browned. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  4. Cook the Vegetables: In the same pan, heat the remaining sesame oil and add the sliced onion, julienned carrot, red bell pepper, and mushrooms. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.

  5. Combine Noodles and Vegetables: Add the drained glass noodles to the pan along with the prepared sauce. Toss everything together until the noodles are well coated with the sauce and the vegetables are evenly distributed.

  6. Add Spinach: Add the spinach to the pan and continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the spinach is wilted and the noodles are heated through.

  7. Season and Serve: Taste the stir-fry and season with salt and pepper if needed. If you set aside the cooked beef or tofu, add it back to the pan and toss to combine. Remove the pan from the heat.

  8. Serve: Transfer the Korean Glass Noodle Stir-Fry to a serving dish and sprinkle with additional sesame seeds for garnish.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicy kick, add more gochujang or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes.
  • Customize the vegetables to your liking by adding broccoli, snap peas, or zucchini.
  • If you prefer a vegetarian or vegan option, simply omit the meat and use tofu as a protein source.

Conclusion

In just a few simple steps, you’ve created a mouthwatering Korean Glass Noodle Stir-Fry that’s perfect for those hectic days when you need a flavorful meal without spending hours in the kitchen. This versatile dish allows for endless variations to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. Whether you’re a fan of Korean cuisine or simply love a good stir-fry, this recipe is sure to become a regular in your dinner rotation. Enjoy!
