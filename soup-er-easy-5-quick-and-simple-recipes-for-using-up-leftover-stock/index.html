Soup-er Easy: 5 Quick and Simple Recipes for Using Up Leftover Stock


Are you tired of letting that leftover stock go to waste? We’ve all been there - you make a big batch of soup or stew, and after enjoying a bowl or two, you’re left with a container of stock that just sits in the back of your fridge, forgotten and neglected. But fear not! In this post, we’re going to share five quick and simple recipes that will help you put that leftover stock to good use. Let’s get cooking!

1. Veggie Rice Pilaf

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup leftover stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, carrot, celery, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the cooked rice and leftover stock to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the stock is absorbed and the rice is heated through.
  6. Serve as a side dish or a light lunch.

2. Hearty Chicken Noodle Soup

Ingredients:

  • Leftover chicken, shredded
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups leftover stock
  • 2 cups egg noodles
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, sliced carrots, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the shredded chicken, leftover stock, and egg noodles to the pot. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the noodles are cooked through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Ladle into bowls and serve hot.

3. Creamy Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups leftover stock
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat some oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, and sliced mushrooms. Sauté until the mushrooms are golden brown.
  3. Add the Arborio rice to the skillet. Stir well to coat the rice with the oil.
  4. Slowly add the leftover stock, about 1/2 cup at a time, stirring constantly until the liquid is absorbed before adding more.
  5. Continue adding stock and stirring until the rice is cooked al dente.
  6. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve immediately, garnished with additional Parmesan cheese if desired.

4. Spicy Tomato and Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 cup red lentils
  • 4 cups leftover stock
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion, minced garlic, diced carrot, and celery. Sauté until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the red lentils, leftover stock, diced tomatoes, cumin, and paprika to the pot. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the lentils are cooked through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot with crusty bread.

5. Easy Pea and Ham Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups frozen peas
  • 4 cups leftover stock
  • Leftover ham, diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the frozen peas, leftover stock, and diced ham to the pot. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, or until the peas are cooked through.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, blend the soup until smooth.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with a sprinkle of diced ham.

There you have it - five quick and simple recipes to help you use up that leftover stock. Don’t let it go to waste! Get creative in the kitchen and enjoy these delicious and comforting dishes. Happy cooking!
