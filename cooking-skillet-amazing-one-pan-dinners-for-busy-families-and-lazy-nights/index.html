Cooking Skillet: Amazing One-Pan Dinners for Busy Families and Lazy Nights


For all of us who’ve ever stared blankly into the pantry or gazed wistfully at an overflowing dishwasher, there’s a savior in the form of a humble skillet. The skillet ships you directly to a galaxy of scrumptious, family-friendly, fun-loving meals. Ready for a #skilletmasterclass? Let’s dive into some warm, comforting, one-pan wondrous dishes for those unfailingly busy nights or lazier-than-usual evenings. After all, #nolifegenuinefood.

1. Skillet Pizza Pasta Chips

We blend two favorite foods, create an astonishing fusion, and this isn’t a dream. Promise.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pasta (shape of your choice - penne would be great)
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 cup shredded pizza cheeses (mix of mozzarella, cheddar, parmesan)
  • 1 cup chips (doritos or nacho chips)
  • 2 cups cooked, diced chicken
  • 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes
  • 1 cup halved olives
  • Salt and pepper, as per taste

Instructions:

  1. Mix the cooked pasta with marinara sauce in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Sprinkle in the chicken, tomatoes, and olives; gently mix to combine.
  3. Sprinkle chips over the mixture, let them get a little crispy, then cover the skillet.
  4. Sprinkle the shredded cheeses over the top. Once the cheese is melted and bubbly, it’s ready.

2. Ginger Soy Glazed Skillet Chicken & Veg

This is an aromatic, flavorsome meal ready to delight even the most discerning eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups of varied vegetables (broccoli, carrots, bell peppers)
  • 1/4 cup liquid honey
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger purée
  • 1 thinly sliced green onion

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, add to the skillet and cook until browned and cooked through.
  3. Remove the chicken, add the vegetables and stir-fry until tender.
  4. Mix honey, soy sauce, vinegar, ginger purée, and pour it over the veg.
  5. Toss in the chicken, cook until well coated and heated through.
  6. Garnish with sliced green onion.

3. Shrimp & Chorizo Skillet with Quinoa

A zesty, Spanish-inspired delight that is sure to steal the show.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 8 oz chorizo, sliced
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt, pepper, and paprika (as per taste)

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa in chicken broth as per package instructions; once done, set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook chorizo over medium heat. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add olive oil, sear the shrimp until pink, then return chorizo to the skillet.
  4. Add tomatoes and seasonings, cook until tomatoes soften.
  5. Mix in quinoa, cook until heated through. Toss in parsley right before serving.

4. Honey Sriracha Tofu Skillet

The wannabe vegetarian or the seasoned tofu-lover will swoon for this dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lbs tofu, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons Sriracha hot sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 bag frozen stir-fry vegetables
  • Salt, pepper, and garlic powder (as per taste)

Instructions:

  1. Tofu needs pressing, drain and pat dry. Slice into desired shape, fry in oil until golden. Set aside.
  2. Mix honey, soy sauce, Sriracha, and cornstarch until smooth.
  3. In skillet, cook vegetables until tender. Return tofu to skillet, pour sauce.
  4. Let simmer for a few minutes until the sauce thickens, add more heat with extra Sriracha if you wish.

Wrap up your busy nights or lazy Sundays with these skillet miracles - flavorful and fun without the overwhelming clean-up. Skillet, meet your new bestie - the family. Because, after all, the best recipes almost cook themselves. Happy cooking!
