Comforting One Pot Minestrone Soup


Minestrone soup is a classic Italian dish that warms the heart and nourishes the soul. This comforting one pot meal is packed with vegetables, beans, and pasta, making it a hearty and satisfying option for those chilly nights or when you simply crave a bowl of goodness. In this recipe, I will guide you through the steps to create a delicious minestrone soup that will leave you feeling warm and content.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup small pasta (such as macaroni or ditalini)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the diced carrots, celery, zucchini, and green beans to the pot. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables start to soften.

  3. Pour in the can of diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Stir in the dried basil, dried oregano, bay leaf, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Add the cannellini beans and pasta to the pot. Cook for an additional 10-12 minutes, or until the pasta is tender. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper, if needed.

  5. Remove the bay leaf from the soup. Ladle the minestrone into bowls and garnish with freshly chopped parsley.

  6. Serve the comforting one pot minestrone soup with some crusty bread on the side for a complete and satisfying meal.

Variations

  • Feel free to customize your minestrone soup by adding other vegetables such as diced bell peppers, spinach, or kale.
  • For a protein boost, you can add cooked chicken, ground beef, or even sausage to the soup.
  • If you prefer a creamier texture, stir in a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk towards the end of cooking.

Tips

  • To make this soup ahead of time, follow the recipe until step 4. Let the soup cool completely, then transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 3 days. When ready to serve, reheat the soup on the stovetop and continue with step 5.
  • Leftover minestrone can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days or frozen for up to 3 months. Thaw frozen soup in the refrigerator overnight and reheat on the stovetop before serving.

Conclusion

There’s something incredibly comforting about a bowl of homemade minestrone soup. The combination of vegetables, beans, and pasta creates a harmonious medley of flavors and textures. With this easy one pot recipe, you can enjoy a hearty and satisfying meal that will warm you up from the inside out. So grab a ladle and savor the goodness of this comforting one pot minestrone soup.
