Slow Cooker Beef Tips and Gravy: A Comforting Meal


There’s nothing quite like coming home to the aroma of a hearty, slow-cooked meal. One dish that never fails to deliver on comfort and flavor is slow cooker beef tips and gravy. This classic recipe is a crowd-pleaser, perfect for family dinners, potlucks, or just a cozy night in. The tender beef, savory gravy, and aromatic spices make it a timeless favorite. Plus, the convenience of using a slow cooker means you can set it and forget it, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully while you go about your day. So, let’s dive into the details of creating this soul-warming dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Add the beef tips to the bowl and toss to coat evenly.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef tips in batches and cook until browned on all sides. Transfer the browned beef to the slow cooker.

  3. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced mushrooms. Sauté until the vegetables are tender and aromatic, then transfer them to the slow cooker with the beef.

  4. Pour the beef broth, red wine (if using), and soy sauce into the slow cooker. Stir to combine all the ingredients.

  5. Cover the slow cooker and cook on low for 7-8 hours, or on high for 4-5 hours, until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  6. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch and water to create a slurry. Stir the slurry into the slow cooker and cook on high for an additional 30 minutes, or until the gravy has thickened.

  7. Once the gravy has reached the desired consistency, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Serve the beef tips and gravy over mashed potatoes, rice, or noodles, and garnish with fresh parsley.

Tips and Variations

  • For a richer flavor, you can add a tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce to the gravy.
  • If you prefer a thicker gravy, increase the amount of cornstarch in the slurry.
  • Feel free to customize the recipe by adding carrots, celery, or your favorite vegetables to the slow cooker.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or frozen for up to 3 months.

Final Thoughts

Slow cooker beef tips and gravy is a timeless, comforting meal that brings warmth and satisfaction to the table. Its simplicity, rich flavors, and tender beef make it a go-to choice for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. The convenience of the slow cooker and the minimal prep time make this recipe a winner in the kitchen. So, the next time you’re craving a meal that soothes the soul, give this recipe a try and savor the delightful flavors of slow-cooked goodness.
