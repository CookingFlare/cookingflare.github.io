Classic Cookies and Cream Ice Cream You Can Make Today


Few desserts rival the simple joy of classic cookies and cream ice cream—a luscious blend of creamy vanilla ice cream studded with chunks of chocolate cookies. It’s a timeless treat that delights both kids and adults alike, with its perfect balance of smooth, sweet, and crunchy textures. And the best part? You can whip up a batch right in your own kitchen today, no complicated machinery or fancy ingredients required.

Let’s dive deep into how to create this velvety, cookie-packed ice cream from scratch, plus some tips and variations that will elevate your homemade treat to the next level.

What Makes Cookies and Cream Ice Cream So Irresistible?

At its core, cookies and cream ice cream combines two main components:

  1. Rich, creamy base: Typically a vanilla-flavored custard or cream-based ice cream that’s smooth and slightly sweet.
  2. Chocolate cookie pieces: Usually crushed sandwich cookies like Oreos, which add crunch, cocoa flavor, and a visual contrast with their black-and-white motif.

The magic occurs when velvety ice cream melds with bittersweet cookie crumbs, creating a textural and flavor play that has fans around the world clamoring for more.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

For a no-fail homemade version, gather the following:

  • Whole milk: The foundation of your ice cream base; whole milk lends creaminess without heaviness.
  • Heavy cream: Adds richness and a silky texture.
  • Granulated sugar: For sweetness—it helps balance the flavors and improves texture.
  • Egg yolks: We’re making a custard-based ice cream, which uses egg yolks to thicken and enrich the base.
  • Vanilla extract or vanilla bean: The classic flavor backbone that complements the cookies perfectly.
  • Chocolate sandwich cookies: The star mix-in! Use a good quality brand; Oreos are classic, but any chocolate wafer cookie with a cream filling works.

Step-by-Step: Crafting Creamy Vanilla Ice Cream Base

Here’s the heart of the recipe: a smooth, creamy vanilla custard. The custard base not only adds depth and velvety richness but also gives the ice cream a luscious mouthfeel that makes every scoop a treat.

Step 1: Warm the Milk and Cream

  • In a medium saucepan, combine 1 cup whole milk and 2 cups heavy cream.
  • Warm over medium heat until small bubbles form around the edge; don’t let it boil.

This gentle warming is important to dissolve sugar easily and infuse flavor later.

Step 2: Whisk Egg Yolks and Sugar

  • In a separate bowl, whisk together 5 large egg yolks and ¾ cup granulated sugar.
  • Whisk until the mixture turns pale yellow and slightly thickened.

This is the base for your custard and is essential for a creamy smooth texture.

Step 3: Temper Egg Yolks With Warm Milk Mixture

  • Slowly pour about half of the warm milk-cream mixture into the yolks while whisking constantly.
  • This step gently raises the temperature of the yolks so they don’t scramble when you combine everything.

Step 4: Cook the Custard

  • Pour the tempered yolks back into the saucepan with the remaining milk and cream.
  • Cook over low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or heat-safe spatula, until the mixture thickens enough to coat the back of the spoon (around 170-175°F or 77-80°C). Do not boil.

Step 5: Add Vanilla

  • Remove the custard from heat and stir in 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract, or scrape the seeds from 1 vanilla bean pod.

Step 6: Chill the Custard

  • Pour the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any lumps or cooked egg bits.
  • Cover with plastic wrap, pressing it directly on the surface to prevent a skin from forming.
  • Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight, until thoroughly chilled.

Mixing In the Cookies

While your custard chills, gather your cookie mix-ins.

  • Take about 20-25 chocolate sandwich cookies.
  • Roughly crush them into large chunks using your hands or a rolling pin inside a plastic bag.
  • Avoid pulverizing them into powder, as you want that satisfying crunch and texture.

Churning Your Ice Cream

If you have an ice cream maker, great! Follow your machine’s instructions. Here’s a general approach:

  • Pour the chilled custard into your ice cream maker.
  • Churn for about 20-25 minutes until it reaches a soft-serve consistency.
  • In the last 5 minutes of churning, slowly add the cookie pieces so they distribute evenly without breaking apart too much.

No ice cream maker? No problem! Here’s a simple method to still get great results:

No-Churn Cookies and Cream Ice Cream Method

If you want to skip the ice cream maker:

  1. Whip 1 cup of heavy cream until stiff peaks form.
  2. Gently fold the chilled custard into the whipped cream, being careful not to deflate it.
  3. Fold in your cookie chunks carefully.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a freezer-safe container.
  5. Freeze for at least 6 hours until firm, stirring vigorously every hour during the first 3 hours to break up ice crystals.

Storage and Serving Tips

  • For the best texture and flavor, serve your ice cream within a week.
  • Store tightly covered in the freezer.
  • Before scooping, let the ice cream sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes to soften slightly.
  • Use a warm ice cream scoop dipped in hot water to create perfect scoops without cracking.

Creative Twists on Classic Cookies and Cream

Want to tweak this classic and experiment with flavors? Here are some ideas:

  • Mint Cookies and Cream: Add a few drops of peppermint extract to the custard before chilling, and mix in mint-flavored cookies.
  • Double Chocolate: Swirl in hot fudge or melted chocolate ribbons during the last stages of churning.
  • Salted Caramel Cookies: Add a salted caramel swirl and use cookies with caramel filling.
  • Coffee Kick: Infuse your custard with a shot of espresso or coffee powder for a delightful mocha flavor.
  • Vegan Variation: Blend coconut milk with cashew cream, swap sugar for maple syrup, and use dairy-free sandwich cookies.

FAQ: Answering Your Cookies and Cream Questions

Q: Can I use other types of cookies besides Oreos?
Absolutely! Chocolate wafer cookies are classic, but any chocolate cookies you enjoy will work. Just make sure they hold up well in cold temperatures.

Q: Can I make this recipe without eggs?
Yes, but the texture will be slightly different. Eggless versions usually rely on heavy cream and milk for richness, but the resulting ice cream may not be quite as creamy or dense.

Q: How long does homemade ice cream keep?
Best enjoyed within a week to maintain ideal texture and flavor. Over longer storage periods, ice crystals can form, which affects smoothness.

Final Thoughts

Making your own cookies and cream ice cream is an immensely satisfying endeavor. It’s simple enough for a weekend project yet offers satisfying results that rival store-bought favorites. Plus, you control the quality of your ingredients and can customize flavors to your heart’s content.

Craving that first creamy, crunchy scoop yet? Grab your ingredients and get started today—the delightful world of homemade cookies and cream ice cream awaits!

Enjoy every bite of homemade happiness!
