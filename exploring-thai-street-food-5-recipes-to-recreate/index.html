Exploring Thai Street Food: 5 Recipes to Recreate


Thai street food is a symphony of flavors, textures, and aromas—a true testament to the richness of Thailand’s culinary heritage. If you’ve ever wandered through the bustling streets of Bangkok or Chiang Mai, you’ll know that Thai street vendors are masters at turning humble ingredients into extraordinary dishes. The sizzling woks, vibrant arrays of fresh herbs, and authentic cooking techniques are enough to tempt anyone. Whether you’re craving the tanginess of lime, the heat of chili, or the subtle sweetness of palm sugar, Thai street food has something for everyone.

In this blog post, we’re diving into five iconic dishes from Thailand’s street food repertoire that you can recreate at home. From the iconic Pad Thai to lesser-known delights like Khanom Krok, these recipes will transport you straight to the heart of Thailand. Let’s get cooking and bring the streets of Bangkok right to your kitchen!

1. Pad Thai (ผัดไทย) – A Timeless Thai Classic

Pad Thai is arguably Thailand’s most famous dish, and it epitomizes the balance of flavors that Thai cuisine is celebrated for. This stir-fried noodle masterpiece is a staple at Thai street food stalls, loved by locals and tourists alike.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g shrimp or tofu (choose based on preference)
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts (crushed)
  • 2 spring onions (chopped)
  • Bean sprouts to garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving

Instructions:

  1. Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 30 minutes or until they’re soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar to create the Pad Thai sauce. Adjust the balance of flavors to suit your taste.
  3. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat. Crack the eggs and stir-fry until scrambled. Add shrimp or tofu and cook until done.
  4. Add the soaked rice noodles and pour in the Pad Thai sauce. Stir-fry everything until well combined.
  5. Toss in chopped spring onions and bean sprouts for a fresh crunch.
  6. Serve on a plate, garnish with crushed peanuts, and squeeze fresh lime juice over the dish.

Pad Thai is the perfect combination of sweet, tangy, salty, and nutty flavors. Its versatility makes it ideal for tweaking to your preferences, so don’t be afraid to experiment!

2. Som Tum (ส้มตำ) – The Spicy Papaya Salad

This fiery and refreshing dish is a quintessential Thai street food offering. Som Tum is made from shredded green papaya and features bold, zesty flavors that tantalize your taste buds. A staple of Northeastern Thai (Isaan) cuisine, it’s perfect as a side dish or light meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 green papaya (peeled and shredded)
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 bird’s eye chilies (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp palm sugar
  • 6 cherry tomatoes (halved)
  • 2 tbsp roasted peanuts

Instructions:

  1. In a mortar and pestle, mash garlic and bird’s eye chilies into a coarse paste.
  2. Add fish sauce, lime juice, and palm sugar to the paste, mixing until well combined.
  3. Toss shredded green papaya with halved cherry tomatoes in the sauce. Blend gently to coat the papaya evenly with the dressing.
  4. Garnish with roasted peanuts before serving.

Som Tum is a vivid explosion of spice and citrus, perfect for those who love bold flavors. Pair it with sticky rice or grilled chicken for an authentic street-style meal.

3. Moo Ping (หมูปิ้ง) – Grilled Pork Skewers

If you’ve wandered past a Thai street food stall, you’ve probably encountered the irresistible aroma of Moo Ping filling the air. These juicy, flavorful pork skewers are marinated to perfection and grilled over charcoal, making them a favorite grab-and-go meal in Thailand.

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork shoulder (thinly sliced)
  • 3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tbsp coriander root (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine minced garlic, coriander root, soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, and palm sugar. This marinade is key to Moo Ping’s authentic flavor.
  2. Add the pork slices to the marinade and refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or overnight for best results).
  3. Thread the marinated pork onto skewers, ensuring they are packed tightly.
  4. Grill the skewers over medium heat until cooked through, turning frequently to avoid charring.
  5. Serve hot with sticky rice and a side of spicy chili dipping sauce.

Hearty and rich, Moo Ping is ideal for barbecues or easy weeknight dinners. These skewers pair beautifully with almost any traditional Thai side dish.

4. Khanom Krok (ขนมครก) – Coconut Rice Pancakes

Khanom Krok is a beloved Thai dessert that’s sweet, creamy, and slightly crispy. These bite-sized coconut rice pancakes are a popular street food treat, delightful as a snack or dessert after a savory meal.

Ingredients:

For the Batter:

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Toppings: chopped green onions, sweet corn, or shredded coconut

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix rice flour, coconut milk, and water to form a smooth batter.
  2. Prepare the filling by combining coconut cream, sugar, and salt. Adjust sweetness to your liking.
  3. Heat a Khanom Krok pan or a takoyaki pan over medium heat, and grease lightly with oil.
  4. Fill each mold halfway with the batter, then add a spoonful of coconut cream filling on top. Sprinkle your chosen toppings while still cooking.
  5. Cook until the edges are crispy, and the center is creamy. Flip onto a plate and serve warm.

Khanom Krok’s delicate coconut flavors are irresistible, making them the perfect street-inspired dessert.

5. Tom Yum (ต้มยำ) – Hot and Sour Soup

The famous Tom Yum is an aromatic soup that embodies the vibrant flavors of Thailand. This dish is a staple of Thai street food cuisine, cherished for its invigorating mix of heat and sourness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or seafood stock
  • 2 lemongrass stalks (crushed)
  • 5 kaffir lime leaves (torn)
  • 2-inch piece of galangal (sliced)
  • 200g shrimp or chicken (thinly sliced)
  • 4 bird’s eye chilies (crushed)
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tsp Thai chili paste
  • Fresh mushrooms (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, heat the stock and add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal. Let it simmer for 5 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Add shrimp, chicken, or your chosen protein. Cook until done.
  3. Stir in crushed chilies, fish sauce, lime juice, and chili paste. Adjust the seasoning to balance the sour and spicy notes.
  4. Add mushrooms, if desired, and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. Serve Tom Yum hot with a garnish of fresh cilantro leaves.

This soup is refreshing yet fiery, a must-try dish whether you’re new to Thai cuisine or a seasoned fan.

Recreating Thai street food at home is an exciting culinary adventure that allows you to savor the vibrant flavors of Thailand without boarding a plane. By mastering these recipes, you’ll uncover the beauty of Thai cooking—its balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy—and bring a touch of Thailand’s bustling street food culture into your everyday meals. Try out these recipes and discover the magic of Thailand’s kitchen traditions in your own home!
