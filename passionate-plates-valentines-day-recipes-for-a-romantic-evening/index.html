Passionate Plates: Valentine's Day Recipes for a Romantic Evening


Valentine’s Day is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate this romantic occasion than with a delightful dinner for two? As the sun sets on this day of love, gather your sweetheart around the dinner table and indulge in a culinary journey that will leave you both feeling pampered and delighted. In this blog post, we’ll share a collection of Passionate Plates recipes that will make your Valentine’s Day dinner an unforgettable experience.

Start with an Exquisite Appetizer

Begin your romantic evening with a delectable appetizer that sets the tone for the rest of the meal. Our first recipe, Seared Scallops with Citrus-Ginger Emulsion, is a harmonious blend of flavors and textures that will tantalize your taste buds.

  • 12 large scallops
  • 1/4 cup citrus-ginger emulsion (see below)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Citrus-Ginger Emulsion:

  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed orange juice
  • 1/4 cup freshly squeezed grapefruit juice
  • 2 tablespoons grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon water

Combine all ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Main Course: A Symphony of Flavors

For the main course, we present to you Pan-Seared Filet Mignon with Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes and Broccolini. This indulgent dish is sure to impress your loved one with its rich flavors and tender textures.

  • 4 filet mignon steaks (6 oz each)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup roasted garlic mashed potatoes (see below)
  • 1 bunch broccolini, trimmed
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes:

  • 3-4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 heads garlic, separated into cloves
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss potatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender. Sauté garlic cloves in butter until golden brown. Mash potatoes with roasted garlic, heavy cream, and salt and pepper to taste.

Dessert: A Sweet and Decadent Treat

End your romantic dinner with a sweet and indulgent dessert that will leave your loved one feeling pampered and delighted. Our Chocolate Lava Cake with Salted Caramel Gelato is a rich and decadent treat that will satisfy any sweet tooth.

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 ounces best-quality dark chocolate, broken into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • Salted caramel gelato (store-bought or homemade)

Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). In a medium bowl, whisk together flour and sugar. In a separate bowl, melt chocolate in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval, until smooth. Whisk in eggs, one at a time, followed by flour mixture, butter, and vanilla extract. Pour batter into 4 (6-ounce) ramekins. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until edges are set and centers are still slightly jiggly. Serve with salted caramel gelato.

Beverage Pairing Suggestions

To elevate your Valentine’s Day dinner, we recommend pairing each course with a carefully selected beverage. Here are some suggestions:

  • Appetizer: Sauvignon Blanc or Sparkling Rosé
  • Main Course: Cabernet Sauvignon or Pinot Noir
  • Dessert: Tawny Port or Espresso

Conclusion

With these Passionate Plates recipes, you’ll be well on your way to creating a romantic dinner that your loved one will cherish for years to come. Remember to take your time, enjoy the process, and savor every moment with your sweetheart. Happy cooking, and Happy Valentine’s Day!
