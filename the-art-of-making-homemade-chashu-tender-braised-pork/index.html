The Art of Making Homemade Chashu: Tender Braised Pork


There’s something truly special about the tender, flavorful slices of chashu that crown a steaming bowl of ramen. The art of making homemade chashu is a labor of love that rewards the cook with melt-in-your-mouth pork that’s rich in umami flavor. In this recipe, I’ll guide you through the process of creating your own homemade chashu, from selecting the right cut of pork to achieving that perfect balance of savory and sweet flavors in the braising liquid.

Selecting the Right Cut

The key to achieving that sought-after tenderness in chashu lies in choosing the right cut of pork. For this recipe, I recommend using pork belly. The marbling of fat throughout the meat ensures a succulent and juicy result, while the layers of meat and fat allow for a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

Preparing the Marinade

The marinade is where the magic happens. It’s what infuses the pork with a symphony of flavors that will delight your taste buds with every bite. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, garlic, ginger, and a touch of sesame oil. The soy sauce provides that essential umami depth, while the mirin and sugar add a hint of sweetness that balances the savory notes. The garlic and ginger bring warmth and complexity to the marinade, while the sake rounds out the flavors with its subtle acidity.

Preparing the Pork

Once the marinade is prepared, it’s time to prepare the pork. Roll the pork belly into a tight cylinder and tie it securely with kitchen twine. This ensures that the pork retains its shape during the braising process, resulting in beautifully uniform slices of chashu.

Braising the Pork

In a pot or Dutch oven, place the rolled pork belly and cover it with the marinade. Add water to the pot until the pork is mostly submerged, then bring the liquid to a gentle simmer. Cover the pot and let the pork braise for several hours, allowing the flavors to meld and the meat to become incredibly tender.

Achieving the Perfect Texture

After the pork has braised to perfection, remove it from the pot and let it cool slightly. Then, carefully remove the twine and slice the chashu into thin, uniform slices. The pork should be so tender that it practically falls apart at the touch of a knife, showcasing its melt-in-your-mouth quality.

Serving Suggestions

Homemade chashu is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in a variety of ways. Of course, it’s a quintessential topping for a bowl of ramen, but it can also elevate a simple bowl of rice or noodle soup. The rich, savory flavor of the chashu pairs beautifully with the bright, fresh flavors of scallions and the creamy richness of a soft-boiled egg.

Conclusion

The art of making homemade chashu is a culinary journey that rewards patience and attention to detail. From selecting the perfect cut of pork to crafting a marinade that balances savory and sweet flavors, every step contributes to the creation of a truly exceptional dish. Whether you’re a ramen aficionado or simply a lover of good food, mastering the art of making homemade chashu is a skill that will undoubtedly impress and delight anyone who has the pleasure of savoring your creation.

I hope this blog post provides a comprehensive guide to creating your own homemade chashu, and that it inspires you to embark on your own culinary adventure. Enjoy the process and savor the results of your labor as you savor the tender, flavorful slices of homemade chashu.
