Korean Chicken and Potato Stew (Dakdori Jorim) Recipe


Korean cuisine is beloved worldwide for its bold flavors and heartwarming dishes. Among the many traditional recipes that have captured the hearts of food lovers, Dakdori Jorim stands out as a comforting and flavorful stew that combines tender chicken with hearty potatoes in a savory, slightly sweet, and spicy sauce. This dish, also known as Korean Braised Chicken with Potatoes, is often enjoyed as a family meal or served at gatherings, bringing people together with its rich taste and satisfying texture.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to prepare this delicious Korean stew step by step. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or new to Korean cooking, this recipe is approachable and sure to impress.

Ingredients for Dakdori Jorim

To prepare Dakdori Jorim, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Main Ingredients:

  • Chicken (Bone-in, Skin-on) – 4 pieces (drumsticks, thighs, or a whole chicken cut into pieces)
  • Potatoes – 2 medium-sized, peeled and cut into chunks
  • Carrot – 1 medium, sliced (optional for extra color and sweetness)
  • Onion – 1 medium, sliced
  • Garlic – 4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger – 1-inch piece, minced (optional for extra warmth)
  • Green Onions – 2, chopped (for garnish)

Seasoning & Sauce Ingredients:

  • Soy Sauce – 3 tablespoons
  • Sugar – 1 tablespoon (or brown sugar for a deeper sweetness)
  • Rice Wine (Mirin) – 2 tablespoons
  • Gochujang (Korean Chili Paste) – 1 tablespoon (adjust according to spice tolerance)
  • Gochugaru (Korean Chili Flakes) – 1 teaspoon (optional for added heat)
  • Sesame Oil – 1 teaspoon (for aroma and richness)
  • Black Pepper – 1/4 teaspoon
  • Water – 2 cups
  • Sesame Seeds – 1 tablespoon (for garnish)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

Start by preparing the chicken. You can use any part of the chicken that you prefer—thighs, drumsticks, or even a whole chicken cut into pieces. Bone-in chicken works best because the bones add richness to the broth, giving the stew its deep and savory flavor.

  1. Clean the chicken: If using whole pieces, rinse the chicken under cold water and pat it dry with paper towels.
  2. Season the chicken: In a bowl, season the chicken pieces with a pinch of salt and pepper. Set the chicken aside as you prepare the rest of the ingredients.

Step 2: Prepare the Vegetables

While the chicken is resting, peel and chop the vegetables.

  1. Potatoes: Peel the potatoes and cut them into medium chunks, ensuring they are roughly the same size for even cooking.
  2. Carrots: Peel the carrot and slice it into rounds or half-moons for a rustic look (optional).
  3. Onion: Slice the onion thinly to ensure it softens and caramelizes well during cooking.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

The sauce for Dakdori Jorim is what gives this stew its unique flavor profile. It’s a balance of salty, sweet, and spicy, with a deep umami undertone.

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, rice wine (mirin), gochujang (Korean chili paste), sugar, ginger, and garlic. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the sauce is well mixed. Add a pinch of black pepper and gochugaru if you like your stew spicier. The gochugaru will give the dish an extra layer of heat and a beautiful red color.

Step 4: Cooking the Stew

  1. Heat a pot or large pan over medium heat and add sesame oil. Once the oil is hot and shimmering, add the seasoned chicken pieces, skin side down. Sear the chicken for about 3–4 minutes on each side until golden brown. This step is crucial for building flavor and crispiness on the skin.

  2. Once the chicken is browned, remove it from the pan and set it aside. In the same pan, add the sliced onion, carrot, and a pinch of salt. Cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion softens.

  3. Return the chicken to the pan, skin side up, and add the potatoes. Pour the prepared sauce over the chicken and vegetables, ensuring everything is well-coated.

  4. Add water to the pan, ensuring the liquid covers at least half of the chicken. Bring the stew to a simmer over medium-high heat. Once it reaches a simmer, lower the heat to medium-low and cover the pot.

  5. Let the stew simmer for 30–40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the potatoes are tender. You can occasionally check and stir to make sure nothing is sticking to the bottom. The sauce should reduce and thicken, becoming more flavorful as it cooks.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the chicken is fully cooked and the sauce has thickened to a lovely glossy consistency, it’s time to garnish and serve!

  1. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions for a burst of color and added flavor.

  2. Serve the Dakdori Jorim hot with steamed white rice. The stew can be enjoyed as a comforting main dish with a side of kimchi or other banchan (side dishes) for a complete Korean meal.

Tips for the Perfect Dakdori Jorim

  • Use bone-in chicken: The bones provide additional flavor and richness to the stew. If you prefer boneless chicken, you can substitute, but the flavor profile may differ slightly.

  • Adjust the spice level: The gochujang and gochugaru can be adjusted to suit your spice tolerance. If you prefer a milder stew, use less chili paste and flakes, or omit them entirely.

  • Let it simmer: The longer you let the stew simmer, the more flavorful it becomes. If you have time, you can simmer it for up to an hour on low heat to allow the flavors to meld together.

  • Make ahead: Dakdori Jorim can be made ahead of time. The flavors will continue to develop as it sits, so it’s perfect for meal prep. Just store it in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days, and reheat when you’re ready to serve.

  • Substitute vegetables: If you don’t have potatoes or carrots on hand, you can use other root vegetables like sweet potatoes or turnips. These alternatives will add their unique flavors to the stew.

Nutritional Benefits

Dakdori Jorim is not only delicious but also nutritious. It’s a balanced meal with protein from the chicken, carbohydrates from the potatoes, and vitamins from the vegetables. The soy sauce, ginger, and garlic in the sauce also offer various health benefits, such as aiding digestion and providing antioxidants. As with many Korean dishes, it’s a great way to enjoy a satisfying meal while nourishing your body.

Variations of Dakdori Jorim

While the classic version of Dakdori Jorim is already a rich and flavorful dish, there are several variations and ways to make it your own:

  1. Spicy Dakdori Jorim: If you love extra heat, increase the amount of gochujang and gochugaru in the sauce. You can also add fresh chopped red chili peppers for an additional spicy kick.

  2. Sweet and Savory: Add a little more sugar or honey to balance out the spice and saltiness for a sweeter version. The sweetness will complement the richness of the chicken and potatoes.

  3. Vegetarian Version: For a vegetarian take, you can replace the chicken with tofu or mushrooms. These plant-based options will absorb the delicious sauce and offer a lighter, yet equally satisfying, meal.

  4. Add Kimchi: Serve your Dakdori Jorim with a side of kimchi for that quintessential Korean experience. The tangy and spicy flavors of kimchi will balance the richness of the stew perfectly.

Why You’ll Love Dakdori Jorim

Dakdori Jorim is a perfect example of the magic of Korean cooking. It’s a one-pot dish that’s easy to prepare and incredibly flavorful, making it an ideal weeknight dinner or weekend meal for family and friends. The combination of tender chicken, soft potatoes, and a savory-sweet-spicy sauce makes every bite an experience. Plus, the smell as it simmers on the stove will have your kitchen filled with delicious aromas, creating an irresistible atmosphere.

This dish reflects the essence of Korean comfort food—simple, heartwarming, and bursting with flavor. Whether you’re familiar with Korean cuisine or new to it, this recipe will quickly become a favorite in your culinary repertoire.

So, gather your ingredients and get ready to enjoy the warm, cozy goodness of Dakdori Jorim, a Korean chicken and potato stew that will leave everyone asking for seconds!
