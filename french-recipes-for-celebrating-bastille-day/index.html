French Recipes for Celebrating Bastille Day


Bonjour mes amis! Are you ready to celebrate Bastille Day, the French national holiday? What better way to honor this historic event than by indulging in some delicious French cuisine? In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering French recipes that will transport your taste buds straight to the streets of Paris. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Coq au Vin

First up on our culinary adventure is the classic French dish, Coq au Vin. This hearty and flavorful chicken stew is the epitome of French comfort food. To make this dish, you will need:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 200g of bacon, diced
  • 200g of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of flour
  • 750ml of red wine (preferably Burgundy)
  • 500ml of chicken broth
  • A bouquet garni (a bundle of herbs such as thyme, parsley, and bay leaves)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

To start, fry the bacon in a large pot until crispy. Remove the bacon and set it aside. In the same pot, brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Once browned, remove the chicken and set it aside as well.

Next, sauté the onions, garlic, and mushrooms until they are tender. Add the flour and stir well to coat the vegetables. Return the chicken and bacon to the pot, then pour in the red wine and chicken broth. Add the bouquet garni, carrots, salt, and pepper.

Cover the pot and let the Coq au Vin simmer on low heat for about 2 hours, or until the chicken is tender and the flavors have melded together. Serve this rich and flavorful stew with a side of buttery mashed potatoes or crusty French bread. Bon appétit!

2. Ratatouille

Our next dish is a colorful and vibrant vegetable medley known as Ratatouille. This vegetarian delight is not only a feast for the eyes but also a celebration of the flavors of summer. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 tomatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried herbs de Provence
  • Salt and pepper to taste

To start, heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté the onions and garlic until they are fragrant and translucent. Add the bell peppers and cook for a few minutes until they begin to soften.

Next, add the eggplant, zucchinis, and tomatoes to the skillet. Season with herbs de Provence, salt, and pepper. Cover the skillet and let the Ratatouille simmer on low heat for about 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender but still retain their shape.

This vibrant dish can be served as a main course with a side of crusty bread or as a side dish to complement grilled meats or fish. Enjoy the flavors of the Mediterranean with every bite!

3. Crème Brûlée

No French celebration would be complete without a decadent dessert, and what better way to satisfy your sweet tooth than with a classic Crème Brûlée? This rich and creamy custard topped with a caramelized sugar crust is sure to impress. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 6 egg yolks
  • 100g of granulated sugar
  • 500ml of heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Extra granulated sugar for caramelizing

Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C). In a bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sugar until they are pale and creamy. Add the heavy cream and vanilla extract, and whisk until well combined.

Divide the custard mixture among ramekins or oven-safe dishes. Place the ramekins in a baking dish and pour hot water into the dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins. This water bath will help ensure even cooking.

Carefully transfer the baking dish to the preheated oven and bake for about 45 minutes, or until the custard is set but still slightly jiggly in the center. Remove the ramekins from the water bath and let them cool to room temperature. Then, refrigerate the Crème Brûlées for at least 2 hours, or until they are chilled.

Just before serving, sprinkle a thin layer of granulated sugar over each custard. Use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until it forms a crispy and golden crust. Serve immediately and savor each spoonful of this indulgent French dessert.

Conclusion

Voilà! We have explored three delightful French recipes that are perfect for celebrating Bastille Day. From the rich and comforting Coq au Vin to the vibrant and colorful Ratatouille, and finally, the decadent and irresistible Crème Brûlée, these dishes will transport you to the heart of French cuisine.

So, gather your loved ones, raise a glass of French wine, and enjoy a culinary journey through the flavors of France. Happy cooking and Bonne Fête Nationale!
