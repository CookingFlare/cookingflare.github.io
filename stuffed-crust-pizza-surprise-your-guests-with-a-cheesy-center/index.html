Stuffed Crust Pizza: Surprise Your Guests with a Cheesy Center


When it comes to pizza, there’s something universally satisfying about biting into a slice and discovering a gooey, cheesy center hidden within the crust. Stuffed crust pizza brings that extra layer of delight to your dining experience, transforming a classic favorite into an extraordinary treat. Whether you’re hosting a casual get-together or planning a special meal, homemade stuffed crust pizza is sure to impress your guests.

Why Make Stuffed Crust Pizza at Home?

Control Over Ingredients

Making stuffed crust pizza at home allows you to choose high-quality ingredients and customize each component to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. From selecting organic vegetables to using gluten-free dough, you have complete control over what goes into your pizza.

Endless Customization

Homemade stuffed crust pizza offers endless possibilities for creativity. You can experiment with different cheese blends, include various toppings, and even try unique sauces. This flexibility ensures that every pizza you make is uniquely yours.

Family Fun Activity

Preparing pizza from scratch can be a fun and engaging activity for the whole family. Kids will love helping out with rolling the dough, stuffing the crust, and adding their favorite toppings. It’s a great way to spend quality time together while cooking up a delicious meal.

Ingredients for Stuffed Crust Pizza

Before diving into the step-by-step process, let’s gather all the necessary ingredients. You’ll need:

For the Dough:

  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water (110°F)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Stuffed Crust:

  • 10-12 string cheese sticks (mozzarella works best)
  • Olive oil (for brushing)

For the Sauce:

  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings:

  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Desired toppings (pepperoni, bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, olives, etc.)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  2. Mix the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir until a rough dough forms.

  3. Knead the Dough: Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour as needed.

  4. First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours or until it doubles in size.

Step 2: Prepare the Sauce

While the dough is rising, prepare the pizza sauce.

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the tomato sauce, tomato paste, oregano, basil, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.

  2. Simmer: Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Adjust the seasoning to taste.

  3. Cool: Once done, remove the sauce from the heat and let it cool slightly.

Step 3: Stuff the Crust

  1. Preheat Oven: Preheat your oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up as well.

  2. Roll Out the Dough: Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a floured surface. Roll it out into a large circle, about 14 inches in diameter.

  3. Arrange the Cheese Sticks: Place the string cheese sticks around the edge of the dough, leaving about 1 inch of space from the outer edge.

  4. Fold and Seal: Fold the edges of the dough over the cheese sticks and press down firmly to seal them inside the crust. Make sure the cheese is completely enclosed.

Step 4: Assemble the Pizza

  1. Transfer to Baking Surface: Carefully transfer the dough to a pizza peel or baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  2. Add Sauce: Spread an even layer of the prepared tomato sauce over the dough, leaving the stuffed crust exposed.

  3. Add Cheese and Toppings: Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce. Add your desired toppings, followed by a generous sprinkling of grated Parmesan cheese.

Step 5: Bake the Pizza

  1. Bake: Slide the assembled pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or place the baking sheet in the oven. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

  2. Brush with Olive Oil: For an extra touch, brush the exposed stuffed crust with a bit of olive oil about halfway through the baking time. This helps it turn a beautiful golden brown and adds a delightful flavor.

  3. Cool and Slice: Once baked, remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a couple of minutes. Use a pizza cutter to slice it into wedges, ensuring each slice includes a portion of the cheesy stuffed crust.

Tips for Perfect Stuffed Crust Pizza

  • Cheese Varieties: While mozzarella is the traditional choice for stuffing the crust, you can experiment with other cheeses like cheddar, Gouda, or a blend of different cheeses for a unique flavor profile.
  • Herb-Infused Crust: Add an extra layer of flavor by brushing the crust with garlic butter or herb-infused oil before baking.
  • Par-Baking the Crust: For a crispier crust, consider par-baking the dough for a few minutes before adding the sauce and toppings. This helps prevent a soggy bottom.
  • Use a Pizza Stone: If possible, use a preheated pizza stone to bake your pizza. It distributes heat evenly and helps achieve a crispy crust.

Conclusion

Stuffed crust pizza is more than just a meal; it’s an experience. The anticipation of biting into the cheesy center, the satisfaction of a perfectly baked crust, and the joy of sharing it with loved ones make it a memorable dish. By following these steps and tips, you can create a delicious stuffed crust pizza that’s sure to surprise and delight your guests.

Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or a beginner in the kitchen, making stuffed crust pizza is a rewarding and enjoyable endeavor. So roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to impress everyone with a homemade pizza that combines the best of comfort and creativity. Happy cooking!
