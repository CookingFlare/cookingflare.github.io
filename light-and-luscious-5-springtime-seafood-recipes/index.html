Light and Luscious: 5 Springtime Seafood Recipes


As the chill of winter melts away and the flowers begin to bloom, spring presents an exciting opportunity to refresh our culinary repertoire. With longer days and warmer weather, it’s the perfect time to enjoy light and luscious dishes that celebrate the bounty of the sea. Seafood is not only packed with flavor but also offers a nutritious option that pairs beautifully with the vibrant ingredients of the season. Here are five delightful seafood recipes that will elevate your spring dining experience.

1. Lemon Herb Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Ingredients

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Shrimp: In a bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, parsley, dill, salt, and pepper. Add the shrimp and toss until well coated. Allow the shrimp to marinate for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator.

  2. Prepare the Skewers: If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.

  3. Skewer the Shrimp: Thread the marinated shrimp onto the skewers, making sure to leave a little space between each shrimp.

  4. Grill: Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until the shrimp turn pink and opaque.

  5. Serve: Remove from the grill and serve immediately, garnished with extra herbs and lemon wedges. Pair with a fresh green salad for a light meal.

2. Zesty Citrus Ceviche

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh white fish (such as cod or snapper), diced
  • 1 cup fresh lime juice (about 8 limes)
  • 1 small red onion, finely diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Tortilla chips for serving

Instructions

  1. Cure the Fish: In a glass bowl, combine the diced fish and lime juice. Make sure the fish is fully submerged. Cover and refrigerate for 30-45 minutes until the fish is opaque and fully “cooked” by the citrus.

  2. Mix Ingredients: Once the fish is ready, drain a little of the lime juice. Add the red onion, jalapeño, cherry tomatoes, avocado, cilantro, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to combine.

  3. Serve: Spoon the ceviche into small bowls and serve with tortilla chips. This refreshing dish is perfect as an appetizer or a light lunch.

3. Spring Pea and Mint Risotto with Scallops

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup fresh peas (or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1 pound sea scallops
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a simmer. Keep it warm over low heat.

  2. Cook the Risotto: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for about 2 minutes, until lightly toasted.

  3. Add Wine: Pour in the white wine and stir until it’s mostly absorbed. Begin adding the warm broth one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until the liquid is absorbed before adding more. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is creamy and al dente.

  4. Incorporate Peas and Mint: In the last few minutes of cooking, stir in the fresh peas, Parmesan cheese, and chopped mint. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  5. Sear the Scallops: In a separate skillet, heat a little olive oil over high heat. Season the scallops with salt and pepper. Sear them for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown and cooked through.

  6. Plate and Serve: Spoon the risotto onto plates, top with seared scallops, and serve with lemon wedges. The combination of flavors and textures will delight your palate.

4. Asparagus and Crab Salad with Lemon Vinaigrette

Ingredients

  • 1 pound asparagus, trimmed
  • 1 cup lump crab meat, picked over for shells
  • 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh dill, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Mixed greens for serving

Instructions

  1. Blanch the Asparagus: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add the asparagus and blanch for about 2-3 minutes until bright green and tender-crisp. Drain and immediately transfer to an ice bath to stop the cooking process. Once cooled, cut the asparagus into 2-inch pieces.

  2. Make the Vinaigrette: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  3. Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, gently toss the blanched asparagus, lump crab meat, red onion, and dill. Drizzle with the lemon vinaigrette and toss to coat.

  4. Serve: Arrange mixed greens on a platter and top with the asparagus and crab mixture. This light and refreshing salad is ideal for a spring brunch or light dinner.

5. Baked Salmon with Honey Mustard Glaze

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Set your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Make the Glaze: In a small bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.

  3. Prepare the Salmon: Place the salmon fillets skin-side down on a lined baking sheet. Brush the glaze generously over each fillet.

  4. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.

  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh thyme and serve with steamed vegetables or a light grain salad. This dish is not only flavorful but also visually appealing.

Conclusion

Spring is the ideal time to embrace the light, fresh flavors of seafood. These five recipes celebrate the season’s bounty while providing a nutritious and satisfying dining experience. Whether you’re hosting a spring gathering or simply enjoying a quiet dinner at home, these dishes will surely impress. So roll up your sleeves, get cooking, and savor the delightful tastes of spring!
