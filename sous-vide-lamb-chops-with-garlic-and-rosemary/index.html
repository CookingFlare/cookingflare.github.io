Sous Vide Lamb Chops with Garlic and Rosemary


Lamb chops are a favorite among food enthusiasts for their tender, flavorful meat. Cooking them to perfection, however, can be tricky. The traditional methods of grilling or pan-searing can sometimes result in overcooked edges and underdone centers. If you want to elevate your lamb chops and ensure a perfectly tender, juicy result every time, then sous vide cooking is your secret weapon.

Sous vide, a French technique meaning “under vacuum,” involves cooking food in a temperature-controlled water bath, allowing it to cook evenly and retain all of its natural juices. This method is perfect for lamb chops because it allows the meat to cook at a precise temperature, resulting in a perfectly cooked chop every time. In this recipe, we’ll combine the sous vide technique with the bold flavors of garlic and rosemary to create a dish that is both luxurious and easy to prepare.

What You’ll Need

Before diving into the cooking process, let’s first gather the necessary ingredients. For the best results, opt for high-quality lamb chops, preferably from a trusted butcher. Here’s what you’ll need for your sous vide lamb chops with garlic and rosemary:

Ingredients:

  • 4 lamb chops, about 1 to 1.5 inches thick (rack of lamb or rib chops work great)
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary (or 2 tablespoons of dried rosemary)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of unsalted butter (for finishing)
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
  • Vacuum seal bags or resealable ziplock bags (if using the water displacement method)
  • Sous vide precision cooker
  • A large pot or container for the water bath

Optional for garnish:

  • Fresh rosemary sprigs
  • Lemon zest or a squeeze of fresh lemon juice
  • A pinch of sea salt or flaky salt

Why Sous Vide?

Sous vide has become a popular technique in both professional kitchens and home cooking due to its precision and ability to create consistently perfect results. This method allows the lamb chops to cook evenly from edge to edge, resulting in a tender, juicy bite that’s cooked to the exact doneness you desire. The ability to infuse flavors during the cooking process is another reason sous vide shines, especially with the aromatic combination of garlic and rosemary.

When cooking lamb chops sous vide, you can set your precision cooker to the exact temperature you prefer. This takes the guesswork out of cooking and ensures that your lamb chops will never be overcooked. Plus, the sous vide method helps retain all of the natural juices, resulting in a moist and tender texture that’s hard to replicate using traditional methods.

Preparing the Lamb Chops

To start, you’ll want to prepare your lamb chops. Start by seasoning them generously with salt and freshly cracked black pepper. These seasonings are important, as they’ll enhance the natural flavor of the meat and help form a nice crust when searing later.

Aromatic Infusion with Garlic and Rosemary

Once your lamb chops are seasoned, it’s time to introduce the garlic and rosemary. Smashing the garlic cloves with the side of a knife releases the oils and helps infuse the lamb with a robust garlic flavor during the sous vide process. Fresh rosemary sprigs are the perfect herb to complement lamb’s rich flavor, adding a woodsy and slightly piney note that pairs perfectly with the meat.

Place the lamb chops into a vacuum-seal bag or a resealable ziplock bag along with the smashed garlic cloves and rosemary. You’ll want to make sure the lamb is snug in the bag but not overcrowded. If using a vacuum seal bag, make sure to vacuum-seal the bag tightly, ensuring there’s no air left inside. If you’re using a ziplock bag, the water displacement method can be used to push out the air before sealing the bag.

Sous Vide Cooking

Now it’s time for the sous vide magic to happen! Set your sous vide precision cooker to your desired temperature. Here are a few recommended temperature ranges, depending on how you like your lamb:

  • Rare: 125°F (52°C) for 1.5 to 2 hours
  • Medium-rare: 130°F (54°C) for 1.5 to 2 hours
  • Medium: 135°F (57°C) for 1.5 to 2 hours
  • Medium-well: 145°F (63°C) for 1.5 to 2 hours

For the most tender and juicy results, I recommend cooking the lamb chops at 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare. This temperature ensures that the lamb will be tender, juicy, and pink in the center. However, you can adjust the temperature based on your personal preferences.

Fill a large pot or container with water and attach your sous vide precision cooker. Once the water reaches the desired temperature, carefully lower the bagged lamb chops into the water bath. Ensure the bags are fully submerged and that the chops are not overlapping. Seal the pot with plastic wrap or a lid to help maintain the temperature and prevent evaporation.

The beauty of sous vide cooking is that you can leave the lamb chops in the water bath for an extended period without overcooking them, thanks to the precise temperature control. You can let them cook for up to 2 hours, but you can also leave them for longer if needed, as the meat will not continue to cook beyond the set temperature.

Finishing the Lamb Chops

After the lamb chops have cooked to perfection, it’s time to finish them with a quick sear to develop a beautiful golden-brown crust. This step will give the lamb a crispy exterior while keeping the inside tender and juicy.

Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron works best) over medium-high heat. Add the olive oil and let it get hot. Once the oil is shimmering, remove the lamb chops from the sous vide bag, and carefully pat them dry with a paper towel. This step is crucial, as excess moisture can prevent the chops from searing properly.

Add the lamb chops to the hot skillet and sear for about 1-2 minutes per side, or until a deep golden-brown crust forms. During the last 30 seconds of searing, add a tablespoon of unsalted butter and baste the lamb chops with the melted butter using a spoon. This step infuses the meat with extra flavor and ensures a rich, savory finish.

Don’t forget to also add the smashed garlic cloves and rosemary sprigs to the pan while searing. These aromatics will further flavor the butter and infuse the lamb chops with their fragrant oils.

Serving Suggestions

Once your lamb chops have been seared to perfection, it’s time to serve them. Arrange the chops on a platter and garnish with fresh rosemary sprigs for a pop of color. A light drizzle of the butter from the pan will add even more richness and flavor. For a fresh, zesty contrast, you can also finish with a touch of lemon zest or a squeeze of lemon juice.

Side Dish Pairings

To complement the richness of the lamb chops, consider serving them with sides that balance the flavors. Here are a few excellent options:

  • Roasted Vegetables: Roasted carrots, parsnips, and potatoes, seasoned with olive oil and herbs, make a hearty, flavorful side.
  • Garlic Mashed Potatoes: Creamy mashed potatoes infused with garlic pair wonderfully with the lamb.
  • Green Beans Almondine: A light, nutty side dish featuring sautéed green beans and toasted almonds provides a fresh contrast to the lamb’s richness.
  • Couscous or Quinoa: A simple couscous or quinoa dish with fresh herbs and lemon zest offers a light yet flavorful accompaniment.

Tips for Success

  1. Resting the Lamb Chops: After searing, let the lamb chops rest for a few minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring a juicy and tender bite.
  2. Don’t Overcrowd the Bag: When vacuum-sealing or bagging your lamb chops, ensure that they are not packed too tightly. This ensures even cooking.
  3. Adjust the Time for Thickness: If your lamb chops are particularly thick, you may want to increase the cooking time by an additional 30 minutes to ensure they are fully cooked.

Conclusion

Sous vide lamb chops with garlic and rosemary are an incredible way to enjoy this flavorful cut of meat. The sous vide method ensures that your lamb chops are cooked perfectly every time, and the garlic and rosemary infuse the meat with their aromatic goodness. With just a few simple ingredients and the right technique, you can create a dish that’s fit for any special occasion or a simple weeknight dinner. Whether you’re a seasoned sous vide cook or a beginner, this recipe is sure to impress.

So, gather your ingredients, set your sous vide machine, and get ready to enjoy succulent, perfectly cooked lamb chops that will have everyone asking for seconds!
