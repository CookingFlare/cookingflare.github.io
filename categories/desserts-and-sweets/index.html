Decadent Salted Caramel Apple Pie: A Sweet and Salty Delight

If you’re a fan of classic apple pie but want to take it to the next level, then this Decadent Salted Caramel Apple Pie recipe is just what you need. The combination of sweet apples, rich caramel, and a hint of salt creates a truly indulgent dessert that will impress your family and friends. So, let’s dive into the delightful world of this sweet and salty treat. Ingredients For the Pie Crust: 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed 1 teaspoon salt 1 teaspoon granulated sugar 6-8 tablespoons ice water For the Filling: 6-7 cups thinly sliced peeled and cored apples (use a mix of sweet and tart varieties) 3/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon salt 1 tablespoon lemon juice For the Salted Caramel: 1 cup granulated sugar 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, room temperature 1/2 cup heavy cream 1 teaspoon sea salt Instructions Making the Pie Crust: In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your hands to combine until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, shape into two disks, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Preparing the Filling: In a large bowl, toss the sliced apples with lemon juice to prevent browning. In a separate bowl, mix the sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Add this mixture to the apples and toss until well coated. Making the Salted Caramel: In a heavy-bottomed saucepan, heat the granulated sugar over medium heat. Stir constantly with a high heat resistant rubber spatula or wooden spoon. As the sugar melts, it will clump, but keep stirring. Once all the sugar is melted, stop stirring and let it cook until it reaches a deep amber color. Watch it closely to avoid burning. Add the butter and stir until melted. Slowly drizzle in the heavy cream while stirring. Let the mixture boil for 1 minute. Remove from heat and stir in the sea salt. Assembling the Pie: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out one disk of the chilled dough on a floured surface and carefully transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish. Fill the pie crust with the apple filling and drizzle about 1/4 cup of the salted caramel over the apples. Roll out the second disk of dough and place it over the filling. Trim, seal, and flute the edges. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape. Brush the top crust with egg wash and sprinkle with a little sugar. Place the pie on a baking sheet and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Serving: Allow the pie to cool for at least 4 hours to allow the filling to set. Drizzle slices with more salted caramel before serving, if desired. ...
