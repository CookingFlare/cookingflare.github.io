Wholesome Dairy-Free Peanut Butter Granola for a Nutritious Snack


Granola is a popular and versatile snack that can be enjoyed at any time of the day. It’s delicious, nutritious, and easy to make at home. In this recipe, we’ll be making a wholesome dairy-free peanut butter granola that is not only packed with flavor but also provides a nutritious energy boost. So grab your apron and let’s get started!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup unsalted peanuts
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (162°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and set it aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats and unsalted peanuts. Stir well to evenly distribute the ingredients.

  3. In a separate microwave-safe bowl, heat the peanut butter and maple syrup together until they are smooth and well combined. This should take about 30 seconds to 1 minute in the microwave. Stir in the melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt.

  4. Pour the peanut butter mixture over the oats and peanuts. Use a spatula or your hands to thoroughly mix everything together until the oats and peanuts are coated evenly.

  5. Spread the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet in an even layer. Press it down slightly to encourage clumping.

  6. Bake the granola in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until it turns golden brown. Remember to stir the granola halfway through baking to ensure even browning.

  7. Once the granola is done baking, remove it from the oven and let it cool completely on the baking sheet. This will allow it to become crisp and crunchy.

  8. Once the granola has cooled, add in the dried cranberries and dark chocolate chips if desired. Give it a gentle toss to distribute the add-ins evenly.

  9. Transfer the granola to an airtight container or mason jar for storage. It can be stored at room temperature for up to two weeks.

Serving Suggestions

This wholesome dairy-free peanut butter granola can be enjoyed in various ways. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  • Serve it with your favorite plant-based milk or yogurt for a quick and nutritious breakfast.
  • Sprinkle it over smoothie bowls or ice cream for added crunch and flavor.
  • Pack it in a resealable bag for a convenient on-the-go snack during hikes or road trips.
  • Use it as a topping for salads or roasted vegetables to add a delightful crunch.

Final Thoughts

Making your own granola at home allows you to control the ingredients, ensuring a wholesome and nutritious snack. This dairy-free peanut butter granola is not only delicious but also packed with protein, healthy fats, and fiber. It’s a fantastic option for those with dietary restrictions or anyone looking for a tasty and satisfying snack. So why not give this recipe a try and enjoy the goodness of homemade granola?

Remember to experiment with different add-ins like dried fruits, nuts, or seeds to customize your granola to your liking. The possibilities are endless! Happy snacking!
