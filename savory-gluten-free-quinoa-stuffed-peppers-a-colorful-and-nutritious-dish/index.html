Savory Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Peppers: A Colorful and Nutritious Dish


If you’re looking for a vibrant and flavorful dish that’s both gluten-free and packed with nutrition, then you’re in for a treat with these savory quinoa stuffed peppers. This recipe is a celebration of color, taste, and health, making it a perfect addition to your regular meal rotation.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup shredded cheese (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Quinoa: In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the quinoa is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

  2. Prepare the Peppers: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Place the peppers in a baking dish.

  3. Prepare the Filling: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute. Then, add the black beans, diced tomatoes, corn, cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Stir in the cooked quinoa and chopped cilantro. Mix everything well.

  4. Stuff the Peppers: Spoon the quinoa mixture into the hollowed-out bell peppers, pressing down gently to fill them completely. If using cheese, sprinkle it over the tops of the stuffed peppers.

  5. Bake: Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.

  6. Serve: Once out of the oven, let the stuffed peppers cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot.

Nutritional Benefits

This dish is not only visually appealing but also incredibly nutritious. Bell peppers are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants, while quinoa is a complete protein and a great source of fiber. The black beans provide additional fiber and plant-based protein, making this dish a wholesome and balanced meal.

Customization

Feel free to customize this recipe to suit your taste preferences. You can add diced mushrooms, spinach, or even ground turkey to the filling for a different flavor profile. Additionally, if you prefer a spicier kick, you can incorporate diced jalapeños or a sprinkle of red pepper flakes.

Conclusion

Savory Gluten-Free Quinoa Stuffed Peppers are a delightful combination of textures and flavors, offering a satisfying meal that’s both gluten-free and packed with wholesome ingredients. Whether you’re catering to a gluten-free diet or simply looking to add more nutritious meals to your table, these stuffed peppers are sure to become a favorite in your household. Enjoy the burst of colors and flavors while indulging in a nourishing dining experience.
