Sweet and Tangy Pulled Pork Sandwiches


Pulled pork sandwiches are a beloved classic for a reason. With tender, flavorful meat and the perfect balance of sweetness and tang, this dish is a crowd-pleaser at any gathering, from family dinners to weekend barbecues. If you’re craving a mouthwatering meal that packs a punch of flavor, look no further than these sweet and tangy pulled pork sandwiches.

Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just looking to try something new in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress. The combination of slow-cooked pork, homemade barbecue sauce, and crispy, soft buns will have everyone asking for seconds. So let’s dive into this irresistible recipe, step by step.

Ingredients

Before we get started with the recipe itself, it’s important to gather all the ingredients to ensure your pulled pork sandwiches come out perfectly. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pulled Pork:

  • 4-5 lbs (1.8-2.3 kg) bone-in pork shoulder (also known as pork butt)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground mustard
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, smashed

For the Sweet and Tangy Barbecue Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon liquid smoke (optional for that smoky flavor)
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce (optional for a spicy kick)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

To Assemble the Sandwiches:

  • Soft sandwich buns or brioche buns
  • Coleslaw (optional but highly recommended for extra crunch)
  • Pickles (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Pulled Pork

The key to making the most delicious pulled pork is all about slow cooking. The long, low-temperature cooking process allows the pork to become incredibly tender and flavorful. Here’s how you do it:

  1. Season the Pork: Start by making the dry rub for the pork. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, ground cumin, mustard, chili powder, and cayenne pepper (if using). Mix it all together until well-combined. Rub this spice mixture generously over the pork shoulder. Be sure to coat every surface of the meat to infuse it with flavor.

  2. Sear the Pork: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, place the pork shoulder in the skillet and sear it on all sides for about 2-3 minutes per side until it develops a golden-brown crust. This step helps to lock in the flavors and juices.

  3. Slow Cooking: Transfer the seared pork shoulder to a slow cooker. Add the chopped onion, smashed garlic cloves, apple cider vinegar, chicken broth, and water to the slow cooker. These ingredients help to keep the pork moist and infuse it with rich flavor while cooking.

  4. Cook Low and Slow: Set your slow cooker to low and let the pork cook for 8 hours or until it becomes tender and easily shreds with a fork. If you’re short on time, you can cook it on high for 4-5 hours, but for the best results, slow cooking on low is recommended.

  5. Shred the Pork: Once the pork is done, carefully remove it from the slow cooker. Using two forks, shred the meat into bite-sized pieces, discarding any large chunks of fat. Return the shredded pork to the slow cooker to soak up the flavorful juices, keeping it warm until ready to serve.

Step 2: Making the Sweet and Tangy Barbecue Sauce

A great barbecue sauce can make or break a pulled pork sandwich, and this recipe is no exception. The homemade sauce is a perfect balance of sweet, tangy, and just a hint of spice. Here’s how you make it:

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a medium saucepan, combine the ketchup, apple cider vinegar, honey, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, and liquid smoke (if using). Stir everything together over medium heat.

  2. Simmer: Bring the mixture to a simmer, and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning, adding salt, pepper, or extra hot sauce if desired to suit your taste.

  3. Blend for Smoothness (Optional): If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can use an immersion blender to blend it to your desired consistency. However, leaving it a little chunky adds texture, which complements the pulled pork nicely.

  4. Finish the Sauce: Once your sauce has thickened and reached the perfect flavor balance, remove it from the heat and set it aside. You’ll drizzle this sweet and tangy goodness over the pulled pork when assembling your sandwiches.

Step 3: Assembling the Sandwiches

Now comes the fun part: putting everything together. The sweet and tangy pulled pork pairs beautifully with soft buns, coleslaw, and pickles, creating a mouthwatering sandwich that’s sure to impress your guests.

  1. Toast the Buns: Lightly toast the sandwich or brioche buns on a skillet or in the oven until they’re golden brown. This will help them hold up against the juicy pulled pork without getting soggy.

  2. Assemble the Sandwich: Start by placing a generous portion of the pulled pork onto the bottom half of each bun. Drizzle a good amount of the homemade sweet and tangy barbecue sauce over the top of the meat.

  3. Add Extras: If desired, top the pulled pork with a scoop of coleslaw for added crunch and creaminess. Pickles are also a great addition for an extra layer of tang. You can even add some fresh jalapeños for a bit of heat if you like it spicy.

  4. Finish the Sandwich: Place the top half of the bun on top and press down gently to hold everything together.

Step 4: Serving and Enjoying

Your sweet and tangy pulled pork sandwiches are now ready to serve! These sandwiches are perfect for any occasion, whether it’s a weeknight meal, a weekend barbecue, or a casual family gathering. Pair them with classic sides like French fries, potato salad, or corn on the cob for a complete meal.

If you’re making them for a party or event, you can also serve the pulled pork and barbecue sauce in a separate dish, allowing everyone to build their sandwiches just the way they like. This is especially convenient if you’re catering to different tastes and dietary preferences.

Tips for Perfect Pulled Pork Sandwiches

  1. Choose the Right Cut of Meat: While pork shoulder is the traditional cut for pulled pork, you can also use pork butt. Both cuts are marbled with fat, which helps keep the meat tender during the long cooking process.

  2. Don’t Rush the Cooking Process: Slow cooking is the key to tender pulled pork. Avoid rushing it by cooking on high heat. Low and slow is the way to go for the best results.

  3. Experiment with the Sauce: Feel free to adjust the balance of sweetness and tang in the barbecue sauce by adding more honey or vinegar. If you like a bit of heat, add a touch of cayenne or hot sauce.

  4. Make Ahead: Pulled pork can be made a day or two in advance and stored in the refrigerator. The flavors only get better as it sits, and you can reheat it gently in the slow cooker or on the stove.

  5. Leftovers: If you have leftovers (which is rare!), pulled pork can be used in a variety of dishes. Try it in tacos, on top of a pizza, or even in a casserole.

Conclusion

Sweet and tangy pulled pork sandwiches are a versatile and delicious dish that’s perfect for any occasion. With tender, flavorful pork, homemade barbecue sauce, and the perfect balance of sweetness and tang, these sandwiches will quickly become a favorite in your recipe rotation.

Whether you’re hosting a barbecue, enjoying a family meal, or just craving a hearty sandwich, this recipe is sure to satisfy. The combination of slow-cooked pork, crispy buns, and tangy sauce is irresistible. Try making these pulled pork sandwiches today, and watch as your friends and family rave about the incredible flavor.
