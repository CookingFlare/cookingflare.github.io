Savory and Satisfying: Meat Pie Recipes for Seniors


As we age, our taste buds may change, and we often crave comforting and familiar flavors. One dish that never fails to deliver on both taste and nostalgia is a delicious meat pie. These savory treats are not only satisfying but also easy to prepare, making them perfect for seniors who want to indulge in a hearty and flavorful meal. In this blog post, we will explore three mouthwatering meat pie recipes that are sure to delight your taste buds and bring back fond memories of home-cooked meals.

1. Classic Beef and Mushroom Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 pounds of lean ground beef
  • 1 cup of sliced mushrooms
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 sheets of store-bought puff pastry, thawed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the mushrooms, onion, and garlic until softened.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir well to combine.
  5. Slowly add the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir until the sauce thickens.
  6. Return the cooked ground beef to the skillet and mix well.
  7. Roll out one sheet of puff pastry and line a pie dish with it.
  8. Pour the beef filling into the pastry-lined dish.
  9. Cover the filling with the remaining sheet of puff pastry, sealing the edges.
  10. Cut a few slits on the top of the pastry to allow steam to escape.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown and cooked through.
  12. Allow the pie to cool for a few minutes before serving.

2. Chicken and Vegetable Pot Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup of frozen mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 sheets of store-bought pie crust, thawed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  3. Stir in the flour and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Slowly add the chicken broth and milk to the skillet, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens.
  5. Add the shredded chicken, frozen vegetables, thyme, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Mix well.
  6. Roll out one sheet of pie crust and line a pie dish with it.
  7. Pour the chicken and vegetable filling into the pastry-lined dish.
  8. Cover the filling with the remaining sheet of pie crust, sealing the edges.
  9. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
  10. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and cooked through.
  11. Allow the pie to cool for a few minutes before serving.

3. Hearty Lamb and Potato Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound of lamb shoulder, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 cups of beef broth
  • 1 cup of frozen peas
  • 2 sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 sheets of store-bought puff pastry, thawed

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the lamb cubes and brown them on all sides. Remove the lamb from the skillet and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the onion, carrots, and garlic until softened.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another minute.
  5. Slowly add the beef broth to the skillet, scraping the bottom to release any browned bits.
  6. Return the lamb to the skillet along with the frozen peas and rosemary. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Simmer the mixture for about 20 minutes or until the lamb is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  8. Roll out one sheet of puff pastry and line a pie dish with it.
  9. Pour the lamb and vegetable filling into the pastry-lined dish.
  10. Cover the filling with the remaining sheet of puff pastry, sealing the edges.
  11. Cut a few slits on the top of the pastry to allow steam to escape.
  12. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the pastry is golden brown and cooked through.
  13. Allow the pie to cool for a few minutes before serving.

Whether you’re looking to treat yourself or prepare a delicious meal for your loved ones, these savory meat pie recipes are sure to hit the spot. With their rich flavors and hearty fillings, they are a perfect choice for seniors who want a satisfying and comforting meal. So, put on your apron, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy the delightful experience of making and savoring these classic meat pies.
