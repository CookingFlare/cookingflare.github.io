Taco Time: Quick and Tasty Recipes for Kids


Tacos are a beloved dish that appeals to both kids and adults alike. With their delicious combination of flavors and customizable fillings, tacos are the perfect meal for any time of day. Whether you’re looking to introduce your little ones to new flavors or simply need a quick and easy meal option, these Taco Time recipes for kids are sure to be a hit!

1. Mini Taco Cups

These adorable Mini Taco Cups are a fun twist on traditional tacos. Instead of using tortillas, we’ll be using wonton wrappers to create bite-sized cups that are perfect for little hands. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Wonton wrappers
  • Ground beef or turkey
  • Taco seasoning
  • Shredded cheese
  • Lettuce, diced tomatoes, and other taco toppings of your choice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a muffin tin.
  2. Press a wonton wrapper into each muffin cup, forming a cup shape.
  3. In a skillet, cook the ground beef or turkey over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add taco seasoning and water according to the package instructions. Simmer for a few minutes until the mixture thickens.
  5. Spoon the meat mixture into each wonton cup, filling them about three-quarters full.
  6. Top each cup with shredded cheese.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the wonton wrappers are crispy and the cheese is melted.
  8. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before removing the taco cups from the muffin tin.
  9. Serve with your favorite taco toppings, such as lettuce, diced tomatoes, and sour cream.

2. Veggie Taco Wraps

If you’re looking for a vegetarian option, these Veggie Taco Wraps are a great choice. Packed with colorful vegetables and flavorful spices, these wraps are both nutritious and delicious. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Flour tortillas
  • Black beans, rinsed and drained
  • Corn kernels
  • Bell peppers, diced
  • Red onion, diced
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Avocado, sliced
  • Lime juice
  • Cilantro, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the black beans, corn kernels, diced bell peppers, red onion, cherry tomatoes, avocado slices, lime juice, and cilantro. Mix well.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Warm the flour tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for a few seconds on each side.
  4. Spoon the veggie mixture onto each tortilla, slightly off-center.
  5. Fold the sides of the tortilla over the filling, then roll it up tightly.
  6. Cut the wraps in half or into smaller portions for easier handling.
  7. Serve with a side of salsa or guacamole for dipping.

3. Taco Salad Skewers

For a fun and interactive meal, try these Taco Salad Skewers. They’re a great way to get your kids involved in the kitchen and encourage them to eat their vegetables. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Lettuce leaves, torn into bite-sized pieces
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber, sliced
  • Bell peppers, diced
  • Black olives
  • Shredded cheese
  • Cooked chicken or beef, diced (optional)
  • Tortilla chips, crushed
  • Ranch or salsa for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Thread the lettuce leaves, cherry tomatoes, cucumber slices, bell peppers, black olives, and diced chicken or beef onto skewers in any order you prefer.
  2. Sprinkle shredded cheese and crushed tortilla chips over the skewers.
  3. Serve with a side of ranch dressing or salsa for dipping.

These Taco Time recipes are quick, tasty, and perfect for kids of all ages. Whether you’re hosting a playdate or simply looking for a fun dinner option, these recipes are sure to be a hit. Get creative with the fillings, toppings, and presentation to make Taco Time a memorable and delicious experience for your little ones. Enjoy!
