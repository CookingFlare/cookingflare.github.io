Easy Chicken Stir-Fry with Vegetables Recipe


When it comes to quick, nutritious, and flavorful meals, a classic chicken stir-fry with vegetables stands out as a timeless winner. This simple dish not only satisfies your taste buds but also provides a balance of protein, fiber, and essential nutrients. Whether you’re a beginner in the kitchen or a seasoned cook seeking a wholesome weeknight dinner, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your culinary routine.

Why Make a Chicken Stir-Fry?

Stir-frying is one of the most efficient ways to prepare a meal. With its roots in Chinese cooking, this technique involves high-heat cooking in a wok or skillet, ensuring the ingredients retain their natural flavors, textures, and nutrients. Here’s why you’ll love preparing this dish:

  • Quick Cooking Time: A chicken stir-fry is ready in less than 30 minutes, making it perfect for busy schedules.
  • Customizable: You can switch up the vegetables, sauces, or protein to suit your preferences or accommodate dietary needs.
  • Minimal Cleanup: A one-pan solution means fewer dishes to wash after dinner.
  • Great for Meal Prep: Stir-fry reheats well, making it ideal for preparing in advance.

Now, let’s dive into the step-by-step process to create a delicious chicken stir-fry that’s bursting with bold flavors and crunchy textures.

Ingredients for Chicken Stir-Fry with Vegetables

Before you start cooking, gather the following fresh and flavorful ingredients. Using high-quality components ensures your stir-fry tastes its best.

For the Chicken:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs: Cut into bite-sized strips or cubes. Thighs are juicier, but breasts work equally well for a leaner option.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce: For marinating the chicken and infusing it with umami goodness.
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch: Helps tenderize the chicken and create a silky coating.

For the Vegetables:

Choose a mix of colorful, crisp vegetables for variety:

  • 2 cups broccoli florets: Adds a crunchy texture and a boost of antioxidants.
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced: Offers sweetness and vibrant color.
  • 1 medium carrot, thinly sliced: Delivers a subtly sweet crunch.
  • 1 zucchini, cut into half-moons: For a soft, earthy flavor.
  • 1 cup snap peas: Adds a fresh and slightly sweet taste.
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced: Enhances the dish with a savory bite.
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced: Adds a pungent, aromatic note.
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger: Brings warmth and a hint of spice.

For the Stir-Fry Sauce:

A well-balanced sauce is vital for tying together the flavors of the dish:

  • 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce: Base of the sauce with a salty, savory flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce: Enhances depth and adds a slight sweetness (optional).
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil: For a nutty aroma and rich taste.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar or lime juice: Adds a tangy, bright element.
  • 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar: Balances the saltiness with a touch of sweetness.
  • 1/3 cup chicken broth or water: To loosen the sauce and ensure it coats all ingredients.
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch: Helps the sauce thicken when heated.

Optional Garnishes:

  • Chopped green onions: For freshness and color.
  • Sesame seeds: Adds a toasty crunch.
  • A drizzle of sriracha or chili oil: For heat lovers.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prep the Ingredients

The key to a successful stir-fry is preparation. Since the cooking process is fast-paced, having everything ready ahead of time ensures smooth execution.

  1. Start by cutting the chicken into uniform pieces for even cooking. In a bowl, combine the chicken with soy sauce and cornstarch, and let it marinate for at least 10 minutes while you prep the vegetables.
  2. Wash and dry all the vegetables thoroughly. Slice and chop them into bite-sized pieces, keeping in mind that smaller, uniform pieces cook more quickly and evenly.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the sauce ingredients, ensuring the cornstarch dissolves completely.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

  1. Heat a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of oil (vegetable, canola, or avocado oil works well due to their high smoke points).
  2. Once the oil is shimmering, add the marinated chicken in a single layer. Avoid overcrowding the pan to ensure the chicken browns properly.
  3. Stir-fry the chicken for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Vegetables

  1. Add another tablespoon of oil to the same pan and heat it until shimmering. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, stirring quickly to prevent burning.
  2. Toss in the harder vegetables first—such as broccoli, carrots, and onions—and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Next, add the softer vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, and snap peas. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until they are tender-crisp. The vegetables should still have a slight crunch to preserve their freshness and texture.

Step 4: Combine Everything

  1. Return the cooked chicken to the pan with the vegetables.
  2. Stir the sauce one last time to ensure it’s well-combined and pour it over the chicken and veggie mixture.
  3. Toss everything together and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken and evenly coat all the ingredients.

Serving Suggestions

Your chicken stir-fry is ready to be served! To make it a complete meal, consider pairing it with one of the following:

  • Steamed Rice: Jasmine, basmati, or brown rice are classic accompaniments.
  • Noodles: Serve over cooked lo mein, rice noodles, or even spaghetti for a fun twist.
  • Cauliflower Rice or Zoodles: For a low-carb alternative.

Serve the stir-fry hot, garnished with green onions, sesame seeds, or a drizzle of chili oil if desired. It’s a meal rich in flavor, texture, and nutrition!

Tips and Tricks for Success

  • Use High Heat: Stir-frying works best at high temperatures. Preheat your pan to ensure the ingredients cook quickly and maintain their crunch.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Cook the chicken in batches if necessary to allow proper browning.
  • Experiment with Ingredients: Use whatever vegetables you have on hand—mushrooms, baby corn, or bok choy make great additions. You can also swap chicken for shrimp, beef, tofu, or tempeh.
  • Make It Spicy: For a kick of heat, add sliced chili peppers or chili paste to the sauce.

Conclusion

This easy chicken stir-fry with vegetables is a versatile and satisfying meal that brings together the best of bold flavors and wholesome ingredients. Perfect for busy weeknights or meal prep, it’s a dish that makes eating healthy exciting and enjoyable. With a few simple swaps and tweaks, you can tailor it to suit your exact tastes. So, grab your wok (or skillet) and get cooking—you’re just 30 minutes away from a deliciously nourishing homemade dinner that everyone at the table will adore!
