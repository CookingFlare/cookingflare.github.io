From Nonna's Kitchen: Traditional Italian Recipes Passed Down Through Generations


Nonna is the Italian word for grandmother, and when it comes to cooking, there’s no better source of warmth and comfort than the recipes passed down through generations. Drawing from Nonna’s kitchen allows us to experience the rich culinary heritage of Italy, offering an authentic taste of the traditions that make this cuisine so cherished.

As the sun sets over the rolling Tuscan hills, the memory of Nonna’s kitchen comes alive in each ingredient and every bite. The aroma of garlic, simmering tomatoes, and fresh basil transports us to cozy family dinners shared around a large wooden table.

Today, we’ll explore three mouthwatering traditional Italian dishes celebrating nonna’s timeless wisdom. These recipes are perfect for both seasoned home cooks and those new to the world of Italian cuisine.

  1. Nonna’s Baked Lasagna

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. fresh lasagna sheets
  • 3 cups prepared meat sauce
  • 3 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large pot, boil lasagna sheets until al dente, about 8 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  • In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, eggs, and 1 cup of mozzarella cheese, mixing well.
  • Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Top with 6 lasagna sheets, then a layer of meat sauce and half of the ricotta mixture. Sprinkle with 1 cup of mozzarella cheese.
  • Repeat the process, finishing with a layer of meat sauce and a generous amount of mozzarella cheese.
  • Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese and bake for 25-30 minutes or until bubbly. Allow to rest for 15 minutes before serving.
  1. Nonna’s Spaghetti Carbonara

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz. spaghetti
  • 5 eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon

Instructions:

  • Cook spaghetti according to package instructions, about 8-10 minutes, usually around 8 for al dente. Drain and set aside.
  • In a large skillet, cook bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate.
  • In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese.
  • In the same skillet, add spaghetti and cook for a minute or two, allowing it to absorb the bacon fat. Remove from heat and pour in the egg mixture, stirring rapidly to prevent scrambling.
  • Mix in crispy bacon and chili flakes, adjusting salt and pepper to taste. Serve immediately.
  1. Nonna’s Tiramisu

Ingredients:

  • 9 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup strong espresso or brewed coffee
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 2 tbsp. cocoa powder
  • Ladyfingers

Instructions:

  • In a heatproof bowl over simmering water, combine egg yolks and sugar, whisking continuously until the mixture thickens and reaches a ribbon-like consistency.
  • In another bowl, mix together mascarpone cheese and espresso.
  • Layer ladyfingers, creamy mixture, and espresso/mascarpone mixture in 4-6 glasses or a large serving dish, ensuring everything is evenly distributed.
  • Before the final layer, sprinkle cocoa powder over the top.
  • For a truly authentic Italian experience, serve with a glass of Prosecco or espresso.

Each of these recipes not only offers unique flavors but carries with it the warmth and comfort of a nonna’s kitchen, where love, tradition, and delicious food come together to create unforgettable memories. As we gather around the table, let us honor the bond between generations through the art of Italian cuisine.
