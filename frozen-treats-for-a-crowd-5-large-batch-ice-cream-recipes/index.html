Frozen Treats for a Crowd: 5 Large-Batch Ice Cream Recipes


When it comes to summer gatherings, celebrations, or any event with a crowd, few treats can compare to the refreshing and indulgent allure of homemade ice cream. Whether you’re hosting a birthday party, a family reunion, or just a casual get-together, large-batch ice cream recipes can be a lifesaver. Not only do they allow you to serve up a crowd-pleasing dessert, but they also give you the freedom to experiment with creative flavors and ingredients. Best of all, making your own ice cream means you can customize it to your liking and make it even more special for your guests.

In this blog post, we’ll share five delicious, easy-to-make, large-batch ice cream recipes that will keep everyone coming back for more. Whether you’re craving classic flavors, something a little more decadent, or a fruity twist, these ice cream recipes are perfect for satisfying large groups. Each recipe is designed to make enough to serve at least 10-12 people, so you’re guaranteed to have leftovers (if you’re lucky!).

1. Classic Vanilla Bean Ice Cream

There’s no better way to kick off a large-batch ice cream list than with a rich and creamy classic—vanilla bean ice cream. This beloved flavor is both simple and luxurious, making it a hit with all age groups. The subtle specks of vanilla beans not only give it a sophisticated touch but also elevate the natural flavor of the cream and milk. This recipe requires a bit of patience, but it’s well worth it for the ultra-creamy texture and natural sweetness.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract)
  • 8 large egg yolks
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the vanilla bean: Split the vanilla bean down the middle and scrape out the seeds. Set both the seeds and the pod aside.
  2. Heat the cream and milk: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, vanilla pod, and seeds. Heat over medium heat until the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  3. Whisk the eggs: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, sugar, and a pinch of salt until the mixture is light and fluffy.
  4. Temper the eggs: Gradually pour about a cup of the hot milk and cream mixture into the egg yolk mixture to temper the eggs, stirring constantly. Slowly pour the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining hot cream and milk.
  5. Cook the custard: Stir the mixture over low heat until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon (about 10-12 minutes). Do not let it boil.
  6. Chill the custard: Strain the custard through a fine mesh sieve into a bowl to remove the vanilla pod. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  7. Churn: Once chilled, churn the custard in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Transfer the finished ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

Serve this classic vanilla bean ice cream with fresh berries, caramel sauce, or a drizzle of chocolate syrup for extra indulgence.

2. Decadent Chocolate Fudge Swirl Ice Cream

For the chocolate lovers in the crowd, this decadent chocolate fudge swirl ice cream is a dream come true. Rich, velvety chocolate ice cream is swirled with homemade fudge sauce, creating an irresistible combination of textures and flavors. The fudge swirl adds a bit of extra drama, making this a showstopper dessert for your next event.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 6 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 8 large egg yolks
  • Pinch of salt

For the Fudge Swirl:

  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons light corn syrup

Instructions:

  1. Make the chocolate base: In a medium saucepan, whisk together the cocoa powder, sugar, and salt. Gradually whisk in the whole milk and heavy cream. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it is hot, but not boiling.
  2. Melt the chocolate: Add the chopped semi-sweet chocolate to the mixture, stirring until it’s fully melted and smooth. Remove from heat.
  3. Whisk the eggs: In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks. Slowly temper the eggs by adding a bit of the hot chocolate mixture to the egg yolks while whisking constantly. Once combined, return the egg mixture to the saucepan with the rest of the chocolate mixture and cook over low heat until it thickens.
  4. Cool the custard: Strain the custard through a fine mesh sieve into a clean bowl, stir in vanilla extract, and let it cool to room temperature. Once cool, refrigerate for 4 hours or overnight.
  5. Make the fudge swirl: While the custard is chilling, make the fudge swirl by combining chocolate chips, heavy cream, and corn syrup in a small saucepan. Heat over low, stirring constantly until smooth and glossy. Set aside to cool.
  6. Churn the ice cream: Once the custard is chilled, churn it in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  7. Swirl in the fudge: After churning, transfer the ice cream to a large container and drizzle the fudge sauce over the top. Use a spoon or spatula to swirl the fudge into the ice cream, creating ribbons of chocolate throughout.
  8. Freeze: Freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

Top with whipped cream and a sprinkle of chocolate shavings or crushed nuts for a truly decadent dessert.

3. Strawberry Cheesecake Ice Cream

For a fruity and creamy option, strawberry cheesecake ice cream is a winning flavor combination. The rich cream cheese base, combined with the sweetness of ripe strawberries, creates a unique and delightful dessert that will wow your guests. The addition of graham cracker crumbs gives it a cheesecake-like texture and crunch, making it an extra special treat.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups heavy cream
  • 1 ½ cups whole milk
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and chopped
  • 1 cup graham cracker crumbs

Instructions:

  1. Make the strawberry purée: In a blender or food processor, blend the fresh strawberries until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the cream cheese base: In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese until smooth. Add the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and vanilla extract, and mix until well combined.
  3. Combine the strawberry purée: Stir the strawberry purée into the cream cheese mixture until evenly distributed.
  4. Churn the ice cream: Pour the mixture into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  5. Add the graham cracker crumbs: In the last few minutes of churning, add the graham cracker crumbs to the ice cream maker, allowing them to mix into the ice cream.
  6. Freeze: Transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

Serve with additional graham cracker crumbs or fresh strawberries on top for a beautiful presentation.

4. Tropical Piña Colada Ice Cream

Transport your guests to a tropical paradise with this piña colada-inspired ice cream. With coconut milk, pineapple, and rum flavors, this creamy and refreshing ice cream is perfect for summer parties or poolside gatherings. It’s the ideal balance of sweet, creamy, and fruity, making it a unique and exciting addition to your dessert spread.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cups pineapple chunks (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons dark rum (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Make the pineapple purée: Blend the pineapple chunks in a blender until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the base: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium until it is hot, but not boiling.
  3. Mix the rum and vanilla: Stir in the dark rum (if using) and vanilla extract into the cream mixture.
  4. Combine the pineapple purée: Once the mixture is cool, stir in the pineapple purée.
  5. Churn the ice cream: Pour the mixture into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  6. Freeze: Transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

Garnish with toasted coconut flakes or a small wedge of fresh pineapple for a fun and tropical touch.

5. Mint Chocolate Chip Ice Cream

Mint chocolate chip is a refreshing and classic ice cream flavor, and this homemade version is both light and indulgent. With the perfect balance of cool mint and rich chocolate chips, this is a treat that always seems to disappear quickly at any gathering. It’s easy to make in a large batch and sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon peppermint extract
  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped into small chunks
  • Green food coloring (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the base: In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium until the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  2. Add the mint: Stir in the peppermint extract and a few drops of green food coloring if you want a vibrant minty color.
  3. Churn the ice cream: Once the mixture is cool, pour it into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  4. Add the chocolate chips: In the last few minutes of churning, add the chopped chocolate chips, letting them mix into the ice cream.
  5. Freeze: Transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze for at least 4 hours before serving.

Serving Suggestion:

Serve with extra chocolate chips sprinkled on top or with a drizzle of hot fudge sauce for extra indulgence.

Conclusion

Large-batch ice cream recipes are a fantastic way to treat a crowd to something special. With these five recipes—classic vanilla bean, decadent chocolate fudge swirl, strawberry cheesecake, tropical piña colada, and mint chocolate chip—you can offer a variety of flavors that everyone will love. The best part is that these recipes are customizable, so feel free to experiment with your own mix-ins and flavor twists.

Whether you’re hosting a summer barbecue, a birthday party, or just a casual hangout, homemade ice cream is sure to be a hit. Enjoy making and sharing these delicious frozen treats with family and friends!
