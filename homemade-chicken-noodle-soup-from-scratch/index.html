Homemade Chicken Noodle Soup from Scratch


There’s something timeless about homemade chicken noodle soup. It’s the kind of dish that feels like a warm hug on a chilly day, a comfort meal that soothes both body and soul. While canned versions have their place, making chicken noodle soup from scratch lets you control every ingredient, capturing flavors that are both rich and fresh. In this post, we’ll dive deep into how to create the perfect homemade chicken noodle soup, with tips on preparing the broth, cooking the chicken, making noodles from scratch, and assembling everything into a bowl of pure deliciousness.

Why Make Chicken Noodle Soup from Scratch?

Chicken noodle soup crafted from scratch isn’t just about taste — it’s a culinary ritual. Using whole ingredients allows flavors to meld naturally, resulting in a soup that is far superior to store-bought options. Plus, making your own broth extracts nutrients and gelatin that you won’t find in commercial soup cubes or broths, enhancing both taste and health benefits.

Ingredient Selection: The Heart of the Soup

Whole Chicken or Chicken Parts?

Using a whole chicken or bone-in pieces gives you a rich, full-bodied broth packed with collagen and flavor. The bones and skin lend body to the soup while the meat stays tender and juicy. If you’re short on time, boneless chicken breasts or thighs work decently but sacrifice some depth in flavor.

Fresh Vegetables and Aromatics

A classic combination includes onions, carrots, celery, and garlic. Fresh herbs like thyme, parsley, and bay leaves elevate the aroma and taste. Add a touch of black peppercorns to bring subtle heat. Picking quality produce here pays off immensely.

Homemade Noodles

Nothing beats homemade egg noodles in this soup. Their delicate texture soaks up the broth perfectly while offering a fresh bite. Flour, eggs, a pinch of salt, and a little water or milk are all you need. Rolling and cutting noodles by hand is easier than it sounds, and the taste difference is remarkable.

Step 1: Making the Broth

Ingredients for Broth:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds) or equivalent bone-in pieces
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 3 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 celery stalks, cut into chunks
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt, to taste
  • 12 cups cold water

Preparation:

  1. Prepare the chicken: Rinse the chicken under cold water, removing any excess fat. Place it in a large stockpot.
  2. Add vegetables and aromatics: Toss in the onion, carrots, celery, garlic, peppercorns, bay leaves, and thyme.
  3. Add water: Pour in cold water, ensuring the chicken is fully submerged.
  4. Bring to a simmer: Bring the pot to a gentle boil over medium heat, then lower to a simmer immediately to reduce scum formation.
  5. Simmer gently: Let simmer uncovered for 1.5 to 2 hours. Occasionally skim off any foam or fat that rises to the surface.
  6. Season lightly: Add salt towards the end to taste. Doing it early can concentrate saltiness as broth reduces.
  7. Strain broth: Once rich and golden, remove chicken and strain the broth through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container, discarding solids.

Pro tip: After removing the chicken, you can shred the meat for the soup. Discard skin and bones or save bones for a deeper stock later.

Step 2: Preparing the Noodles

Ingredients for Egg Noodles:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2-3 tablespoons water or milk (as needed)
  • Extra flour for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine flour and salt.
  2. Add eggs: Crack eggs into the bowl and stir with a fork, gradually incorporating flour.
  3. Form dough: Pour in water or milk a tablespoon at a time until dough comes together into a non-sticky ball.
  4. Knead: Turn dough onto a floured surface, knead for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Rest dough: Cover with towel or plastic wrap and let rest 20 minutes.
  6. Roll out: Divide dough into two portions. Roll each into a thin sheet (about 1/8 inch thickness) using a rolling pin.
  7. Cut noodles: Using a sharp knife or pizza cutter, slice dough into thin strips (approximately 1/4 inch wide).
  8. Dry or cook immediately: Lay noodles on a floured towel or tray. They can be cooked immediately or dried for later use.

Step 3: Assembling the Soup with Vegetables and Chicken

Additional Vegetables for Soup:

  • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced into coins
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Putting It All Together:

  1. Bring broth to boil: In a large pot, bring your homemade broth back up to a simmer.
  2. Cook vegetables: Add sliced carrots and celery, cooking for about 8-10 minutes until tender but still vibrant.
  3. Add noodles: Stir in fresh noodles, cooking gently for 3-5 minutes until al dente. Homemade noodles cook faster than dried.
  4. Add shredded chicken: Fold in the shredded meat, warming through.
  5. Taste and season: Adjust salt and pepper as needed.
  6. Garnish: Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley on top before serving.

Tips for Perfect Chicken Noodle Soup

  • Don’t rush the broth: Simmering slowly extracts maximum flavor and gelatin, giving the soup body.
  • Skim foam: Removing impurities early results in a clear and clean broth.
  • Noodle size varies: Adjust thickness and width depending on your preference; thicker noodles offer more chew.
  • Store broth and meat separately: Keeps noodles from becoming soggy if making soup ahead.
  • Freeze for future meals: Soup freezes beautifully when cooled completely. Leave noodles out if possible and add fresh when reheating.

Variations and Enhancements for Personal Touch

  • Add ginger or turmeric: For extra warmth and subtle spice.
  • Use fresh herbs: Dill or basil replace parsley for a fragrant twist.
  • Vegetarian option: Substitute chicken with tofu and mushroom broth for a meatless version.
  • Spice it: A pinch of cayenne or chili flakes livens up the flavor profile.
  • Lemon zest or juice: Brightens and balances richness for some recipes.

Nutritional Benefits

Homemade chicken noodle soup is not only comforting but also nourishing. The collagen and minerals from simmering chicken bones support joint and skin health. Fresh vegetables add fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants, making this soup a balanced and wholesome meal.

In Conclusion

Homemade chicken noodle soup from scratch is a satisfying project yielding comforting results. From simmering a rich broth to hand-cut noodles, every step contributes to a bowl full of warmth and flavor. With patience and fresh ingredients, you create not just a soup but a culinary experience — a taste that reminds us of home, care, and tradition.

Next time you crave something both nourishing and delicious, try this recipe. Embrace the process, savor the aroma, and enjoy the simple pleasure of homemade soup simmering on your stove.

Recipe Summary

  • Prepare homemade chicken broth with whole chicken and aromatics.
  • Make fresh egg noodles from scratch and cook them tender.
  • Simmer carrots and celery for texture and sweetness.
  • Combine shredded chicken, noodles, and broth.
  • Season and garnish with fresh herbs.
  • Serve hot and enjoy the ultimate comfort food.

Chicken noodle soup is the kind of classic recipe that transcends seasons and occasions — a culinary embrace in a bowl. With these techniques, you’ll be equipped to deliver that satisfying experience to your family and friends anytime.

Bon appétit!
