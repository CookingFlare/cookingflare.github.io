The Future of Fermentation: Emerging Trends for 2022 and Beyond


Fermentation has been part of our culinary repertoire for centuries. It’s a process that extends the shelf life of food and enhances flavors. With the rise of artisanal foods, modern technology, and scientific advancements, the fermentation landscape is evolving rapidly. Emerging trends in fermentation are pushing boundaries and opening up new perspectives for home cooks and chefs alike.

#1 – Microbial Matrimony: Blending Ferments

One striking trend is blending different types of ferments to create unique flavor profiles. “Ferment blends,” as they are commonly called, allow chefs to marry flavors that might not typically go together. Think kombu-tempeh broth or kimchi salsa. Get creative with your ferments – try blending sourdough starter with kefir grains, or miso with sauerkraut.

#2 – Fermentation for Health

As consumers become more aware of the gut-health benefits of fermentation, probiotics are taking center stage. Probiotic-rich ferments, like cultured veggies and yogurts, enhance the gut microbiome and improve digestive health. A growing number of companies are marketing functional foods containing probiotics, signaling a bright future for fermentation-based, health-centric products.

#3 – The Maker Revolution

Fermentation kits are growing in popularity as they allow home cooks to easily and safely make their own ferments. Starter cultures, like tempeh, kombucha, and sourdough, can now be conveniently ordered online and are perfect for DIY enthusiasts. With the rise of maker culture, we may soon see more shared fermentation spaces, akin to today’s co-working spaces, where individuals can collaborate on perfecting their perfect ferment recipe.

#4 – Fermented Beverages on the Rise

Fermented drinks are gaining popularity worldwide as consumers seek alternatives to traditional alcoholic drinks. Fermented beverages range from oat milk to seasonal fermented soda and hot sauces. They’re a delightful way to enjoy the tangy flavors of fermented foods and beverages without the buzz. Look for fermented beverages at local farmers markets and PL84 sources to create your home fermentation station.

#5 – Fermentation Meets Retail

Driven by consumer interest in at-home fermentation experiences, retailers are getting in on the fermentation action. Expect to see more artisanal ferments, fermentation kits, and books on fermentation techniques available in traditional and online retail outlets. Specialty stores will provide an even wider range of fermentation cultures and ingredients, including regional varieties.

As we look to the future, it’s clear that fermentation is not just a passing culinary trend. It’s a remarkably adaptable technique that can cater to the growing need for functional foods, health-conscious consumers, and those looking to satisfy their adventurous palettes. The future of fermentation is a vibrant one, full of new flavors, innovative techniques, and delightful experiments. So, dust off your fermentation crocks and get ready for a remarkable journey into the future of food.
