Sous Vide Brisket: Fall-Apart Tender and Full of Flavor


If you’re a fan of tender, flavorful brisket, then sous vide cooking is about to become your new best friend. This cooking method involves sealing the brisket in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a temperature-controlled water bath for an extended period, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture and a depth of flavor that’s hard to achieve with traditional cooking methods.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef brisket (5-7 pounds)
  • 2 tablespoons kosher salt
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh sage
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

Preparing the Brisket

  1. Season the Brisket: In a small bowl, mix together the kosher salt, black pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, brown sugar, cayenne pepper, and ground cumin. Rub the spice mixture all over the brisket, ensuring it’s evenly coated. Place the brisket in a large resealable bag.

  2. Adding Flavor: Add the fresh rosemary, thyme, sage, crushed garlic, and olive oil to the bag with the brisket. Massage the brisket gently to distribute the flavors. Seal the bag, removing as much air as possible.

Sous Vide Cooking

  1. Preheat the Water Bath: Fill a large container or a sous vide machine with water and set the temperature to 155°F (68°C).

  2. Cooking the Brisket: Submerge the sealed bag of brisket in the water bath, ensuring it’s fully submerged. Cook for 24-48 hours, depending on the thickness of the brisket.

  3. Chilling the Brisket: Once the brisket has finished cooking, remove it from the water bath and plunge the bag into an ice bath to cool it down quickly. This step is optional but helps in preserving the texture and flavor.

Finishing Touches

  1. Searing the Brisket: Remove the brisket from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. Preheat a skillet or grill to high heat. Sear the brisket for 1-2 minutes on each side until a golden-brown crust forms.

  2. Resting and Slicing: Let the brisket rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing it against the grain. The result should be a juicy, tender brisket with a rich, smoky flavor.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve the sous vide brisket with your favorite barbecue sauce, coleslaw, and pickles for a classic barbecue experience.
  • Alternatively, slice the brisket thinly and serve it on a platter with a selection of mustards, pickles, and freshly baked bread for a delightful charcuterie board.

Conclusion

Sous vide brisket is a game-changer when it comes to achieving that perfect, fall-apart tender texture and a depth of flavor that’s simply unmatched. By combining the precision of sous vide cooking with a carefully selected blend of spices and aromatics, you can create a show-stopping centerpiece for any gathering. Whether you’re a barbecue enthusiast or just someone who appreciates a good meal, sous vide brisket is sure to impress and satisfy. So, fire up your sous vide machine, and get ready to elevate your brisket game to a whole new level!
