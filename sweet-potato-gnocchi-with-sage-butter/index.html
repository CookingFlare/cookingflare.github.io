Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Sage Butter


Introducing a mouth-watering, elegant, and hearty dish that is perfect for chilly evenings: Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Sage Butter. This comforting and delectable recipe will leave your guests (or yourself) coming back for more. Its heavenly flavors and textures make it a wonderful option for any meal of the day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • Salt to taste
  • 20 fresh sage leaves
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

Step 1: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Scrub the sweet potatoes thoroughly and prick them all over with a fork. Roast the sweet potatoes in a baking dish lined with parchment paper for 45-50 minutes, or until tender.

Step 2: Once the sweet potatoes are cooked, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool down for about 10 minutes. Lower the oven temperature to 375°F (190°C).

Step 3: In a large mixing bowl, mash the cooled sweet potatoes with a fork or potato masher. Mix in the beaten egg, ¾ cup of the flour, and a pinch of salt until well combined. If the dough seems too sticky, gradually add more flour until it reaches a slightly tacky but manageable consistency.

Step 4: On a lightly floured surface, divide the dough into smaller portions. Roll each portion into a long rope-like shape and then cut it into 1-inch (2.5 cm) pieces. Use a knife or gnocchi cutter to create the signature grooves on the sides of the gnocchi.

Step 5: Place the gnocchi on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, making sure not to overcrowd them. Gently toss them with a bit of extra flour to prevent sticking. Working in batches, cook the gnocchi in boiling salted water for 2-4 minutes, until they rise to the surface. Then, using a slotted spoon, transfer them to an ice bath to halt the cooking process and maintain their shape. Gently pat them dry with a kitchen towel.

Step 6: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a small bowl, mix together melted butter and Parmesan cheese. Bake for 2-3 minutes, or until the butter is slightly browned and nutty-smelling, taking care not to burn it.

Step 7: In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the browned butter. Add the fresh sage leaves, cooking until they turn crispy and begin to pop, about 1-2 minutes. Be cautious not to overcook the sage, as it can quickly turn into burnt vegetation.

Step 8: Lastly, add the cooked gnocchi to the skillet with the sage butter. Gently toss the gnocchi in the buttery sauce until they are evenly coated. Serve immediately and garnish with additional crispy sage leaves and grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.

As you treat your palate to the exquisite flavors of this satisfying dish, you’ll understand its timeless charm. The natural sweetness of the sweet potatoes, the delightful, slightly bitter aroma of fresh sage, and the rich, comforting nature of the butter and Parmesan create an unbeatable combination. Moreover, this dish offers a versatile and scrumptious alternative to traditional pasta dishes, which could easily become part of your recurring meal plans.

So the next time you crave something warm, delicious, and unforgettable, try your hand atSweet Potato Gnocchi with Sage Butter. It’s sure to become a family favorite that warms both your heart and the souls of those sitting around the dinner table. Enjoy!
