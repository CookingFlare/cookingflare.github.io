How to Make Homemade Korean Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cakes)


When it comes to Korean cuisine, few dishes have reached the level of beloved street food status as Tteokbokki. These spicy, chewy rice cakes are a household favorite as well as a popular street snack across Korea, blending bold flavors with warm comfort. Whether you’re a fan of Korean food or a curious home cook looking to try something new, making Tteokbokki at home is surprisingly simple yet deeply rewarding.

Follow along as we dive into how to make this classic Korean dish that is as delicious as it is culturally iconic. Get ready for an explosion of flavor and texture with every bite as we recreate this spicy delight from scratch.

What Is Tteokbokki?

Tteokbokki, pronounced “teok-boh-kee,” is a dish featuring chewy Korean rice cakes (tteok) swimming in a vibrant, spicy-sweet sauce made with gochujang (Korean red chili paste) and other seasonings. Traditionally, Tteokbokki includes savory elements like fish cakes, boiled eggs, and green onions, creating a perfect balance of textures and flavors.

This dish has deep roots in Korean history, with a version of Tteokbokki—known as “royal court rice cakes” or Gungjung Tteokbokki—traced back to the Joseon Dynasty. While the royal court style is less spicy, modern Tteokbokki evolved into the beloved fiery dish we know today.

Essential Ingredients for Tteokbokki

Before diving into the recipe, let’s explore the key components that make Tteokbokki unforgettable. Each ingredient serves an important purpose in creating the dish’s signature taste and texture.

Rice Cakes (Tteok)

The star of Tteokbokki is the cylindrical, chewy Korean rice cakes. They can be made fresh or purchased in Korean grocery stores. Soaking store-bought rice cakes helps soften them before cooking.

Gochujang (Korean Red Chili Paste)

Gochujang is a thick, fermented paste with a deep, flavorful heat. It adds the bold spiciness needed to elevate the dish while imparting depth through its slightly sweet undertones.

Gochugaru (Korean Red Chili Flakes)

Korean chili flakes, or gochugaru, are used to enhance the spiciness of the sauce further. Choose mild or hot varieties depending on your heat preference.

Fish Cakes

Thin sheets of Korean fish cakes (Eomuk) bring salty, umami-rich notes that complement the rice cakes beautifully.

Soy Sauce

A touch of soy sauce helps add richness and savory depth to the sauce.

Sugar

Sugar balances the heat and adds a delightful sweetness to Tteokbokki’s spicy sauce.

Garlic

Minced garlic elevates the aroma and adds a punchy flavor to the dish.

Anchovy Broth

Traditional Tteokbokki often uses anchovy broth as the base. It adds an authentic depth of flavor, but water or chicken broth can be substituted for convenience.

Green Onions

Green onions lend freshness and a pop of color to the dish.

Optional Add-ons

Add variety by including hard-boiled eggs, cabbage, carrots, or mozzarella cheese for a fusion twist.

Homemade Korean Tteokbokki Recipe

Here’s a detailed recipe to achieve Tteokbokki perfection in the comfort of your own kitchen. This recipe serves 2-3 people and takes about 30 minutes to prepare and cook.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (450g) Korean rice cakes (soaked if store-bought)
  • 4 cups anchovy broth (or water/chicken broth for substitution)
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 cup Korean fish cakes, sliced into strips
  • 2 green onions, diagonally sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (optional for garnish)
  • Toasted sesame seeds (optional, for serving)
  • Optional: hard-boiled eggs, shredded mozzarella, or vegetables (cabbage, carrots)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Broth

If using anchovy broth, start by simmering dried anchovies and seaweed (kelp) in water for 15 minutes. Strain and set aside. Alternatively, heat up 4 cups of water or chicken broth for a quicker base.

Step 2: Soak the Rice Cakes

If using refrigerated rice cakes, soak them in warm water for 10-15 minutes to soften before cooking. Fresh rice cakes may not require soaking.

Step 3: Make the Sauce

In a medium-sized pot, combine the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, and minced garlic. Pour in the broth and stir until the sauce is smooth and evenly blended.

Step 4: Add and Cook the Rice Cakes

Bring the sauce to a gentle boil, then lower the heat to medium. Add the soaked rice cakes and cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring regularly to prevent them from sticking. The rice cakes will soften and absorb the spicy sauce as they cook.

Step 5: Add the Fish Cakes and Green Onions

Once the rice cakes become tender, add the sliced fish cakes and half of the green onions. Simmer for an additional 3-5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 6: Adjust Thickness (Optional)

If the sauce feels too thick, add a splash of water to reach your desired consistency. Conversely, simmer longer if it is too thin.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Transfer the Tteokbokki to a serving dish. Garnish with the remaining green onions, a drizzle of sesame oil, and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds. If desired, include hard-boiled eggs or top with melted mozzarella.

Tips for Perfect Tteokbokki

  • Adjust the Spice Level: For a milder version, reduce the amount of gochugaru and opt for mild gochujang. Alternatively, add a pinch of sugar or honey to balance the heat.

  • Experiment with Add-ons: Try adding vegetables like cabbage or carrots for extra texture and color. Cheese lovers can top Tteokbokki with shredded mozzarella and let it melt for a fusion-style twist.

  • Serve Hot: Always serve Tteokbokki warm, as the rice cakes can harden slightly when cooled.

  • Storage Tips: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container and refrigerate. Reheat on the stove with a splash of water to prevent the sauce from drying out.

Why Make Tteokbokki at Home?

Preparing Tteokbokki at home allows you to customize the spice level, portion size, and ingredients to suit your personal taste. Plus, you’ll gain a deeper appreciation for the flavors and techniques behind this iconic dish. With each step, you’ll uncover the magic of creating a crowd-pleasing dish that’s sure to delight your friends and family.

Homemade Tteokbokki also doubles as a great introduction to cooking Korean cuisine. The essential ingredients, like gochujang and rice cakes, can be used in other Korean dishes such as bibimbap, kimchi stew, or Budae Jjigae (Army Stew), making your kitchen adventure all the more versatile.

A Flavorful Conclusion

Making Korean Tteokbokki at home is more than just preparing a meal—it’s an adventure in flavors and cultural appreciation. The combination of spicy, sweet, and savory elements wrapped around the tender chewiness of rice cakes ensures that every bite delivers comfort and satisfaction.

So, whether you’re new to Korean food or a seasoned fan, put on your apron and give this Tteokbokki recipe a try. You’ll be surprised at how easy and enjoyable it is to recreate this beloved street food in your own kitchen. Be prepared to fall in love with the irresistible charm of Korea’s spicy rice cakes!
