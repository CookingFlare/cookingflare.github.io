Mouthwatering Blueberry Cheesecake: Perfect for Summer


Summer is here, and it’s the perfect time to indulge in sweet and refreshing treats. Among the many delightful desserts that come to mind, blueberry cheesecake stands out as a clear favorite. The combination of creamy cheesecake, juicy blueberries, and a crumbly graham cracker crust is a match made in heaven. In this recipe, we’ll take you on a journey to create a mouthwatering blueberry cheesecake that’s sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons (3/4 stick) unsalted butter, melted

For the cheesecake:

  • 2 pounds cream cheese, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries

For the blueberry topping:

  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Prepare a 9-inch springform pan by wrapping the outside with aluminum foil to prevent water from seeping in during baking.
  2. Prepare the crust: In a medium bowl, mix together the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter until well combined. Press the mixture into the prepared springform pan, making sure to evenly cover the bottom. Bake for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.
  3. Prepare the cheesecake: In a large mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Gradually add the granulated sugar, sour cream, and vanilla extract, beating until fully incorporated.
  4. Add the blueberries: Gently fold in the blueberries into the cheesecake batter.
  5. Pour the cheesecake batter: Pour the cheesecake batter into the prepared pan over the crust.
  6. Bake the cheesecake: Bake the cheesecake for 55-60 minutes or until the edges are set and the center is slightly jiggly. Avoid overbaking, as this can cause the cheesecake to dry out.
  7. Prepare the blueberry topping: In a small saucepan, combine the blueberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and the blueberries release their juice.
  8. Assemble the cheesecake: Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Run a knife around the edges to release the cheesecake, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  9. Top with blueberry topping: Once the cheesecake is completely cool, drizzle the blueberry topping over the top.

Tips and Variations

  • To ensure a smooth cheesecake, make sure to beat the cream cheese until it’s fully incorporated and smooth.
  • If using frozen blueberries, thaw them first and pat dry with a paper towel to remove excess moisture.
  • For a more intense blueberry flavor, use 1 1/2 cups of blueberries in the cheesecake batter.
  • Consider adding a layer of blueberry compote or jam to the cheesecake for an added burst of flavor.
  • This cheesecake is perfect for summer gatherings, potlucks, or even a special occasion like a birthday party.

Conclusion

With this mouthwatering blueberry cheesecake recipe, you’ll be the star of any summer gathering. The combination of creamy cheesecake, juicy blueberries, and a crumbly graham cracker crust is a match made in heaven. So go ahead, indulge in this sweet treat and make this summer one to remember.
