Baking with Coconut: Tropically-Inspired Desserts


If you’re a fan of the exotic and the tropical, then baking with coconut is an absolute must-try. Coconut adds a delightful nutty flavor and a hint of sweetness to any dessert, transporting your taste buds to a sun-kissed paradise. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delectable tropically-inspired dessert recipes that feature the versatile and delicious coconut.

Coconut Cream Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 9-inch pie crust, baked and cooled
  • 1 cup sweetened shredded coconut
  • 2 1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the milk, sugar, flour, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat the egg yolks. Gradually stir in about 1 cup of the hot milk mixture, then return the egg mixture to the saucepan.
  3. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Remove from the heat and stir in the butter, vanilla extract, and 3/4 cup of the shredded coconut.
  5. Pour the filling into the baked pie crust and chill for at least 4 hours, or until set.
  6. Before serving, top with whipped cream and sprinkle with the remaining shredded coconut.

Coconut Mango Cupcakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup shredded coconut
  • 1 ripe mango, peeled and diced

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with cupcake liners.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually mix in the flour mixture, alternating with the coconut milk.
  6. Fold in the shredded coconut and diced mango.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cupcakes to cool before frosting with your favorite coconut or mango-flavored frosting.

Coconut Pineapple Upside-Down Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 can (20 oz) pineapple slices, drained
  • Maraschino cherries
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x9-inch baking pan.
  2. In the prepared pan, melt the butter in the oven. Sprinkle the brown sugar evenly over the melted butter.
  3. Arrange the pineapple slices over the brown sugar and place a cherry in the center of each pineapple ring.
  4. In a bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. In another bowl, cream the sugar and eggs until light and fluffy.
  6. Stir in the coconut milk, shredded coconut, and vanilla extract.
  7. Gradually add the flour mixture and mix until just combined.
  8. Carefully spread the batter over the pineapple slices.
  9. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. Let the cake cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then invert it onto a serving platter.

These tropical-inspired desserts are sure to transport you to a sunny beach with every delicious bite. So, grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and let the magic of coconut take your taste buds on a vacation to the tropics!
