Unagi Don: Grilled Eel Over Rice


Unagi Don is a classic Japanese dish that combines the rich, smoky flavors of grilled eel with fluffy, steamed rice. This delectable meal is not only a feast for the taste buds but also a culinary experience that showcases the artistry and precision of Japanese cuisine. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of preparing Unagi Don from scratch, allowing you to savor the flavors of this traditional dish in the comfort of your own home.

Ingredients

To create the perfect Unagi Don, you will need the following ingredients:

  • 2 unagi fillets (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup of Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin (Japanese sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon of sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame seeds
  • 1 green onion, thinly sliced

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the rice. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Then, cook the rice according to the package instructions or using a rice cooker.

  2. While the rice is cooking, let’s prepare the eel. If you bought frozen unagi fillets, defrost them in the refrigerator overnight. Once defrosted, pat the eel dry with a paper towel.

  3. In a small saucepan, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat until the sugar has dissolved, stirring occasionally.

  4. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Brush the eel fillets with vegetable oil to prevent them from sticking to the grill. Place the fillets on the grill, skin side down, and cook for about 3-4 minutes until the skin becomes crispy. Flip the fillets and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the eel is heated through.

  5. Remove the eel from the grill and transfer it to a cutting board. Using a sharp knife, cut the eel into bite-sized pieces.

  6. Once the rice is cooked, divide it into bowls. Place the grilled eel on top of the rice, drizzle with the prepared sauce, and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Garnish with thinly sliced green onions.

  7. Serve the Unagi Don immediately and enjoy the tantalizing combination of flavors. The tender eel, perfectly grilled and glazed with the savory sauce, pairs harmoniously with the fluffy rice, creating a truly satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a more intense flavor, you can marinate the eel fillets in the sauce for 30 minutes before grilling them. This will infuse the eel with even more delectable flavors.

  • Unagi Don is often served with a side of pickled ginger, which adds a refreshing and tangy element to the dish. Feel free to include it as a garnish if desired.

  • For a healthier option, you can opt for steaming the eel instead of grilling it. Simply place the eel fillets on a steaming rack and steam for about 10 minutes until cooked through.

  • If you’re feeling adventurous, you can experiment with different toppings for your Unagi Don. Some popular additions include shredded nori (seaweed), grated daikon radish, or a drizzle of spicy mayo.

Unagi Don is a true culinary delight that showcases the elegance and simplicity of Japanese cuisine. With its delicate balance of flavors and textures, this dish is sure to impress your family and friends. So, roll up your sleeves, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey to discover the magic of Unagi Don!
