Cooking for Two: 5 Quick and Delicious Salad Recipes


Preparing meals for two has its perks—you get to experiment with portion sizes, customize flavors, and indulge in delightful meals without wasting time or ingredients. When it comes to quick and healthy options that don’t skimp on flavor, salads are an excellent choice. They’re vibrant, versatile, and ideal for lunch or dinner. Here are five fresh and exciting salad recipes perfectly portioned for two to add to your menu this week.

1. Honey-Mustard Chicken and Spinach Salad

This rich and satisfying salad combines tender grilled chicken and fresh spinach with a zesty homemade honey-mustard dressing. It’s packed with protein, greens, and a balance of sweet and savory flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for cooking the chicken)

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • A pinch of salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts lightly with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and cook the chicken, about 5–7 minutes per side, until fully cooked. Let it rest for 5 minutes before slicing into strips.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together Dijon mustard, honey, apple cider vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning if needed.
  3. Arrange the spinach on two plates. Top with cherry tomatoes, red onion, crumbled feta, and the sliced chicken.
  4. Drizzle the honey-mustard dressing generously over the salad, toss lightly, and enjoy.

2. Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

Bright, tangy, and oh-so satisfying, this Mediterranean-inspired salad features chickpeas as the star of the show. It offers a perfect balance of hearty textures and refreshing flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/3 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 small garlic clove, minced
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, onion, olives, parsley, and feta cheese.
  2. For the dressing, whisk together lemon juice, garlic, olive oil, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Let it sit for about 10 minutes to let the flavors meld together, then serve.

3. Strawberry and Goat Cheese Arugula Salad

Fruit and cheese are a match made in heaven, and this recipe perfectly showcases their delightful pairing. Sweet strawberries and creamy goat cheese are served atop peppery arugula for a simple yet elegant combination.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups fresh arugula
  • 1 cup fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons slivered almonds, toasted
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic glaze (optional)

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • A pinch of salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, toss the arugula with the sliced strawberries, goat cheese, and toasted almonds.
  2. Prepare the dressing by whisking olive oil, balsamic vinegar, honey, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and give it a light toss. For an extra burst of flavor, finish with a swirl of balsamic glaze.

4. Asian Sesame Beef Salad

For something bold and savory with a bit of crunch, this sesame beef salad is the perfect choice. Marinated beef strips paired with crisp veggies and a sesame dressing create a meal that’s both filling and fresh.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups mixed greens (e.g., romaine, spinach, or red leaf lettuce)
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup julienned red bell peppers
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Marinade:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • A pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine the marinade ingredients in a small bowl and add the sliced flank steak. Let it marinate for at least 20 minutes.
  2. Heat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat and cook the marinated beef strips for 2–3 minutes on each side until browned. Set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the dressing ingredients until blended.
  4. Assemble the greens, carrots, red bell peppers, and green onions on two plates. Top with the cooked beef and sesame seeds.
  5. Drizzle with the dressing and toss lightly before serving.

5. Quinoa and Avocado Power Salad

Quinoa makes an excellent base for a hearty and healthy salad. Pair it with creamy avocado, crunchy veggies, and a hint of lime for a dish that’s delicious and nutrient-packed.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup uncooked quinoa
  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 1/4 cup roasted pumpkin seeds (pepitas)

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the quinoa according to the package instructions. Allow it to cool completely before assembling the salad.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together lime juice, olive oil, honey, cumin, salt, and pepper for the dressing.
  3. Toss the cooked quinoa with the avocado, cherry tomatoes, purple cabbage, and cilantro in a large bowl.
  4. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and mix until everything is evenly coated. Sprinkle with roasted pumpkin seeds before serving.

Each of these salads highlights fresh ingredients, bold flavors, and quick prep time. Whether you’re planning a cozy weeknight dinner or a leisurely lunch, these recipes are proof that salads can be exciting, delicious, and anything but boring. Start experimenting with these crowd-pleasing combinations that celebrate health without sacrificing flavor—your taste buds will thank you!
