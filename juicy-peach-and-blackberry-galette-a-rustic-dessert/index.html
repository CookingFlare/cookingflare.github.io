Juicy Peach and Blackberry Galette: A Rustic Dessert


There’s something undeniably charming about a rustic dessert, and a galette perfectly captures that essence. With its free-form shape and rustic appeal, a galette is a delightful treat that showcases the natural flavors of seasonal fruits. And when it comes to summer fruits, juicy peaches and plump blackberries steal the show with their vibrant colors and sweet-tangy flavors.

Ingredients

For the crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the filling:

  • 4 ripe peaches, sliced
  • 1 cup blackberries
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon

For the egg wash:

  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tablespoon milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold butter cubes and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing the dough with a fork until it starts to come together. Gather the dough into a ball, flatten it into a disc, and wrap it in plastic wrap. Place it in the refrigerator to chill for at least 30 minutes.

  4. In the meantime, prepare the filling. In a separate bowl, combine the sliced peaches, blackberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and ground cinnamon. Gently toss the mixture until the fruits are evenly coated.

  5. Once the dough has chilled, remove it from the refrigerator and place it on a lightly floured surface. Roll it out into a rough circle, about 12 inches in diameter and 1/4-inch thick.

  6. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Spoon the fruit filling onto the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border around the edges.

  7. Fold the edges of the dough up and over the filling, pleating it as you go, to create a rustic crust. Press the pleats gently to secure them.

  8. In a small bowl, whisk together the beaten egg and milk to create the egg wash. Brush the edges of the galette crust with the egg wash to give it a golden-brown color when baked.

  9. Bake the galette in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  10. Once baked, remove the galette from the oven and let it cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature.

The Juicy Peach and Blackberry Galette is a versatile dessert that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The flaky crust, combined with the sweet peaches and tangy blackberries, creates a harmonious blend of flavors that will delight your taste buds.

So, the next time you find yourself with an abundance of ripe peaches and blackberries, give this rustic galette a try. It’s a simple yet impressive dessert that captures the essence of summer and is sure to be a hit at any gathering. Enjoy!
