Need a Thawing Idea? Ice Kachang: A Fusion Recipe of Chinese & Malaysian Roots


If you’re in need of a delightful dessert that’ll cool your taste buds and satisfy your cravings, then look no further than the tantalizing Ice Kachang! Originating from Malaysia and influenced by Chinese cuisine, this icy treat is a perfect combination of flavors, textures, and cultures. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through making this mouth-watering fusion dessert that’s bound to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

Before we get started on the preparation, let’s gather the essential ingredients:

  • Assorted Jellies: Aim for a variety of colors and flavors (e.g., lychee, strawberry, and blackcurrant).
  • Red Beans: Also known as azuki beans, you can find these in most Asian supermarkets.
  • Sweet Corn Cream: Available in certain supermarkets or online, but you can also make your own by blending sweet corn kernels with a touch of sugar and cream.
  • Condensed Milk: Adds sweetness and creaminess to the dessert.
  • Green Beans: Fresh green beans tossed in sugar and a touch of salt for a delightful crunch.
  • Sago Pearls: Small tapioca-like beads that soak up flavors and provide a unique texture.
  • Ice Shaved or Crushed Ice

Preparation

Soaking the Sago Pearls

Begin by rinsing the sago pearls under water, then place them in a pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer for about 10-15 minutes or until the pearls become translucent. Drain the water, then rinse the pearls under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set them aside in a bowl.

Cooking the Red Beans

If using dried red beans, soak them overnight in water, and then cook them until they reach a soft consistency. Drain and set them aside. If you have canned red beans, rinse them under water and drain well.

Preparing the Jellies

Follow the instructions on the jelly packaging, dissolving the jelly in boiling water, then pouring it into molds or small cups. Allow the jelly to set in the refrigerator for at least an hour, until firm. Once set, turn the jelly out onto a plate, then cut it into small cubes.

Assembling the Ice Kachang

In a large bowl, combine the sago pearls, red beans, jelly cubes, sweet corn cream, condensed milk, and green beans. Mix gently to ensure that everything is evenly distributed.

Next, fill a large container with the ice shaved or crushed ice. Pack the ice mixture on top of the ice, gently pressing down to create a layered effect. Finally, drizzle with more sweet corn cream or condensed milk for extra sweetness and aesthetics.

Serving

Serving the Ice Kachang is just as important as preparing it. Present the dessert in a tall glass, creating a pyramid-like effect with the ice mixture and toppings.

For a finishing touch, garnish your dessert with some mint leaves and perhaps a drizzle of dark syrup (such as palm sugar syrup) for an added layer of flavor. Now serve it up and indulge in this delightful fusion of Chinese and Malaysian flavors!

Tips & Variations

Having trouble finding sago pearls or desired flavors of jelly? Don’t fret! You can easily customize this recipe to suit your taste. Opt for other Asian-inspired toppings like coconut jelly, grass jelly, or even ice cream.

When it comes to sago pearls, don’t be discouraged if they become slightly sticky while soaking. This is normal and can be easily resolved by simply rinsing them under cold water to remove the stickiness.

Lastly, if you prefer a healthier alternative, you can replace some of the ice with frozen fruits for a nutrient-packed treat.

Conclusion

The Ice Kachang is a delightful fusion of Chinese and Malaysian flavors and brings a unique twist to traditional desserts. With its impressive combination of textures and flavors, it’s a surefire way to cool down and delight your taste buds. Do give it a try and let us know how it turns out! Happy cooking!
