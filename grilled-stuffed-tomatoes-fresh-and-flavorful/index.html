Grilled Stuffed Tomatoes: Fresh and Flavorful


If you’re in search of a refreshing and scrumptious summer dish, look no further than these grilled stuffed tomatoes. Bursting with flavors and packed with nutrition, these mouthwatering morsels will undoubtedly become a staple at your next barbecue or garden party. In this guide, we’ll walk you through everything you need to know to create these succulent stuffed tomatoes.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large ripe tomatoes
  • 1 cup seasoned breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh basil
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. This will ensure that the tomatoes cook evenly and develop those beautiful, slightly charred grill marks.
  2. Slice off the top of each tomato, just enough to create a flat surface for stuffing. Carefully remove the watery tomato center using a spoon or melon baller. Reserve the tomato centers for another use, such as in salads or soups.
  3. In a medium bowl, combine breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, basil, and garlic. Mix well to incorporate everything evenly.
  4. Lightly salt and pepper the interior of each tomato, then fill each with the breadcrumb mixture, packing it tightly to avoid any air gaps. The more stuffing you cram into those tomatoes, the more delicious they’ll be!
  5. Create a no-stick surface on the grill by rubbing a small amount of mayonnaise on the grate. Place the stuffed tomatoes on the prepared grill at a slightly skewed angle. This will help to create those beautiful, haphazard grill marks across the entire surface of the tomatoes rather than just the bottoms.
  6. Cook the stuffed tomatoes on the preheated grill for approximately 15 minutes, or until the breadcrumb mixture is golden and crispy, and the tomatoes are slightly tender. You’ll want to take care not to overcook the tomatoes, as they can quickly become mushy.
  7. Carefully transfer the grilled stuffed tomatoes to a serving platter using a spatula.
  8. Allow the tomatoes to cool slightly, then serve them as a delicious appetizer or side dish. They’re sure to win rave reviews from your guests!

Enjoy your grilled stuffed tomatoes while the weather is warm and the fruits are ripe. Transform an ordinary backyard barbecue into an extraordinary culinary experience with this delightful and fresh summer dish.

Tips for Success:

  • Choose tomatoes that are ripe but not overly soft, as they’ll hold up better on the grill.
  • Feel free to add various complementary flavors to the breadcrumb mixture, such as chopped herbs (e.g., parsley or oregano), grated cheese (e.g., provolone or mozzarella), or roasted red pepper.
  • For a little added flair, sprinkle a dusting of finely grated lemon zest over the finished stuffed tomatoes just before serving.
  • Don’t have a grill but craving these delectable stuffed tomatoes? No problem! You can also bake them in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until the breadcrumb mixture is golden and crispy.
