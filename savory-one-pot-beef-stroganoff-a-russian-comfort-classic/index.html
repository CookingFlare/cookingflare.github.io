Savory One-Pot Beef Stroganoff: A Russian Comfort Classic


There’s something truly special about a dish that has stood the test of time, transcending borders and cultures to become a beloved comfort food around the world. Beef stroganoff is one such dish, with its roots in 19th-century Russia and a flavor profile that continues to captivate the taste buds of food enthusiasts everywhere. In this recipe, we’ll explore a one-pot version of this classic dish, making it not only delicious but also convenient to prepare and clean up.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds of beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 cup of sour cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish
  • 12 ounces of egg noodles

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the sliced beef and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.

  2. In the same pot, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil and sauté the chopped onion until it becomes translucent. Add the minced garlic and continue to cook for another minute.

  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pot and cook until they are tender and have released their juices.

  4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables in the pot and stir to coat everything evenly. Cook for a couple of minutes to get rid of the raw flour taste.

  5. Gradually pour in the beef broth, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it thicken slightly.

  6. Stir in the Dijon mustard and Worcestershire sauce, then return the browned beef to the pot. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 10-15 minutes, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  7. While the stroganoff is simmering, cook the egg noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

  8. Once the stroganoff has reached the desired consistency, remove the pot from the heat and gently fold in the sour cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  9. To serve, spoon the stroganoff over a bed of the cooked egg noodles. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and freshness.

Tips and Tricks

  • For a richer flavor, you can use a combination of beef broth and beef stock in this recipe.
  • If you prefer a thicker sauce, you can increase the amount of flour used for the initial thickening step.
  • Leftovers can be refrigerated and reheated the next day, making this one-pot beef stroganoff a convenient option for meal prep.

Conclusion

Beef stroganoff is a dish that has the power to evoke feelings of warmth, nostalgia, and pure satisfaction. This one-pot version of the classic recipe not only preserves the essence of the original dish but also simplifies the cooking process, making it accessible to home cooks of all skill levels. The next time you’re in the mood for a comforting meal with a touch of elegance, consider preparing this savory beef stroganoff and savor the timeless flavors of Russia’s culinary heritage.
