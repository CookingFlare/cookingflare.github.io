Traditional Thanksgiving Potatoes Au Gratin Recipe


When Thanksgiving rolls around, nothing completes the feast quite like a creamy, cheesy, and golden-brown side dish. Potatoes au gratin are a classic choice that elevates the entire meal, and for good reason. This traditional recipe combines tender potatoes with rich cheese and a velvety sauce, creating a dish that’s irresistible and comforting. Whether you’re hosting a large family gathering or simply want to make your holiday dinner more special, this potatoes au gratin recipe will surely impress.

The Origins of Potatoes Au Gratin

Potatoes au gratin, also known as gratin dauphinois in France, has a rich culinary history. The word “gratin” refers to a dish that is topped with a golden, crispy crust formed by a combination of breadcrumbs or cheese. In the case of potatoes au gratin, thinly sliced potatoes are baked in a creamy sauce, often with cheese layered between them. The result is a rich, comforting dish that’s a perfect complement to roasted turkey, stuffing, and other Thanksgiving staples.

While variations of gratin potatoes exist around the world, the French version remains one of the most beloved. This dish’s creamy texture and crispy, cheesy top make it an elegant yet simple side dish. Let’s dive into the recipe that will bring this traditional favorite to your Thanksgiving table.

Ingredients You Will Need

For this potatoes au gratin recipe, we’ve used ingredients that are easy to find and will deliver the perfect balance of creaminess and flavor. You’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Potatoes:

  • 3 pounds of Russet potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced (about 1/8-inch thick)
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil (for greasing the baking dish)

For the Gravy Sauce:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves of garlic, finely minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper (freshly ground, to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional, but adds a lovely depth of flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (to thicken the sauce)

For the Cheese Layer:

  • 2 cups of shredded Gruyère cheese (you can also substitute with Swiss cheese or cheddar)
  • 1 cup of freshly grated Parmesan cheese (for extra flavor and that crispy top)

Optional Garnish:

  • Fresh herbs such as thyme or parsley for a pop of color and flavor

How to Prepare Traditional Potatoes Au Gratin

Preparing potatoes au gratin may seem like an intricate task, but the process is straightforward once you break it down into manageable steps. Follow these easy steps to create a mouthwatering dish your guests will rave about:

Step 1: Preheat the Oven and Prepare the Baking Dish

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish with olive oil to prevent the potatoes from sticking. A glass or ceramic baking dish works perfectly for this recipe because it helps the dish cook evenly, and the potatoes can brown beautifully.

Step 2: Slice the Potatoes

Next, peel the Russet potatoes and slice them thinly into rounds about 1/8-inch thick. It’s important to keep the slices as uniform as possible to ensure that the potatoes cook evenly. You can use a sharp knife or a mandoline slicer for precision.

Once sliced, arrange the potato rounds in a single layer at the bottom of the greased baking dish. Overlap the edges of the potatoes slightly, so they form a tight and compact layer. Repeat this process until you’ve used all of the potatoes.

Step 3: Prepare the Gravy Sauce

In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant but not browned.

Next, whisk in the flour and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, to form a roux. The flour will thicken the sauce and give it that rich, creamy texture. Be sure to cook the flour until it’s golden and no longer tastes raw.

Gradually add the heavy cream and whole milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Continue to cook the sauce over medium heat, whisking occasionally, until it thickens and coats the back of a spoon. This should take about 5-7 minutes. Add salt, black pepper, and nutmeg (if using), and stir until the seasoning is well incorporated.

Step 4: Layer the Cheese and Sauce

Once your sauce has thickened to the desired consistency, remove it from the heat and begin layering your potatoes. Pour about a third of the creamy sauce over the first layer of potatoes in your baking dish, ensuring it covers the potatoes evenly.

Sprinkle a generous amount of Gruyère cheese and Parmesan cheese over the sauce. Then, repeat the layering process with two more layers of potatoes, sauce, and cheese until all the ingredients are used. Finish with a final layer of cheese on top for that irresistible golden crust.

Step 5: Bake the Potatoes Au Gratin

Cover the baking dish loosely with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes. This will allow the potatoes to cook through and become tender. After 45 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbling. The cheese should be crispy and slightly browned, creating a perfect crust.

Step 6: Let the Dish Rest

Once the potatoes au gratin are out of the oven, let the dish rest for about 10 minutes before serving. This helps the sauce to set and makes it easier to serve. Garnish with fresh herbs such as parsley or thyme for an added touch of color and flavor.

Step 7: Serve and Enjoy

Serve the potatoes au gratin hot as the perfect side dish to your Thanksgiving turkey or any holiday meal. The combination of creamy, cheesy potatoes with a crispy, golden top is a crowd-pleaser that will be the star of your dinner table.

Tips for the Perfect Potatoes Au Gratin

While this traditional recipe is straightforward, there are a few tips that can help you make the best potatoes au gratin possible:

1. Use Starchy Potatoes

Russet potatoes are the best choice for this dish because they have a high starch content, which helps the sauce thicken and makes the potatoes tender. Avoid waxy potatoes, as they don’t break down as easily and can result in a less creamy texture.

2. Grate the Cheese Fresh

For the best flavor and texture, always grate your cheese fresh rather than using pre-shredded cheese. Pre-shredded cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can affect the texture of your sauce.

3. Thinly Slice the Potatoes

Slicing the potatoes evenly and thinly is crucial for ensuring they cook uniformly. If you have a mandoline slicer, use it to get even slices. The thinner the slices, the more evenly the potatoes will cook.

4. Don’t Skip the Resting Time

Allowing the dish to rest for 10 minutes after baking helps the sauce set, making it easier to serve and ensuring the dish holds together better.

Variations to Try

While the traditional version of potatoes au gratin is delicious on its own, you can also experiment with different ingredients to suit your taste or make the dish your own. Here are a few variations:

1. Add Bacon or Ham

For a savory twist, sprinkle crumbled bacon or diced ham between the layers of potatoes. The smoky, salty flavor will add depth and complexity to the dish.

2. Incorporate Herbs

Adding fresh herbs like rosemary, thyme, or chives to the layers will bring an aromatic freshness to the dish. You can also sprinkle some fresh herbs on top before serving for an added burst of color and flavor.

3. Use Different Cheeses

While Gruyère is the traditional cheese used in potatoes au gratin, feel free to experiment with other cheeses. Sharp cheddar, fontina, or even a blend of different cheeses can create unique flavors that complement the creamy potatoes.

4. Make it Lighter

If you prefer a lighter version, substitute the heavy cream with half-and-half or use a mixture of low-fat milk and butter. While it won’t have the same rich, decadent texture, it will still be a tasty side dish.

Conclusion

Potatoes au gratin are a time-honored classic that can’t be overlooked when planning your Thanksgiving meal. The creamy potatoes combined with rich, flavorful cheese create a perfect balance of textures and tastes that complement your holiday feast. Whether you stick to the traditional recipe or add your own twist, potatoes au gratin will undoubtedly be a hit with everyone at the table. So, gather your ingredients, follow these steps, and enjoy the delicious, comforting dish that is sure to make your Thanksgiving dinner unforgettable.
