Greek Galaktoboureko Recipe with Custard and Phyllo


If you’re a fan of Greek cuisine or simply looking to try something new, then you’re in for a treat with this delicious Galaktoboureko recipe! Galaktoboureko is a traditional Greek dessert that combines a creamy custard filling with layers of flaky phyllo pastry. It’s sweet, rich, and absolutely irresistible. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of phyllo pastry sheets
  • 1 liter of milk
  • 1 cup of fine semolina
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • Syrup:
    • 2 cups of water
    • 2 cups of sugar
    • 1 cinnamon stick
    • 1 lemon peel

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

  2. In a large saucepan, combine the milk, semolina, sugar, and vanilla extract. Place the saucepan over medium heat and stir continuously until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.

  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs. Gradually add the beaten eggs to the semolina mixture, whisking constantly to incorporate.

  4. Take your phyllo pastry sheets out of the package and cover them with a damp cloth to prevent them from drying out.

  5. Brush the bottom of a baking dish with melted butter. Layer half of the phyllo sheets on the bottom of the dish, brushing each sheet with melted butter before adding the next one.

  6. Pour the custard mixture over the phyllo sheets, spreading it evenly.

  7. Layer the remaining phyllo sheets on top of the custard, brushing each sheet with melted butter. Make sure to brush the top sheet generously with butter.

  8. Using a sharp knife, score the top layer of phyllo into diamond-shaped pieces. This will make it easier to cut later on.

  9. Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for about 45 minutes, or until the top layer of phyllo turns golden brown.

  10. While the Galaktoboureko is baking, prepare the syrup. In a saucepan, combine the water, sugar, cinnamon stick, and lemon peel. Bring the mixture to a boil and let it simmer for about 10 minutes, until the syrup thickens slightly.

  11. Once the Galaktoboureko is done baking, remove it from the oven and immediately pour the syrup over it. The hot pastry will absorb the syrup, giving it a sweet and moist texture.

  12. Allow the Galaktoboureko to cool completely before serving. This will allow the custard filling to set and the flavors to meld together.

  13. When ready to serve, cut the Galaktoboureko along the scored lines and garnish with a sprinkle of powdered sugar or ground cinnamon.

Enjoy this heavenly Greek dessert with a cup of coffee or tea, and savor every bite of its creamy custard and delicate phyllo layers. Galaktoboureko is perfect for special occasions or simply to satisfy your sweet tooth. Give this recipe a try and let the flavors transport you to the beautiful Greek islands!
