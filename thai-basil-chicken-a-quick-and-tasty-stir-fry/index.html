Thai Basil Chicken: A Quick and Tasty Stir-Fry


If you’re looking for a quick and tasty dish that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Thailand, then Thai Basil Chicken is the answer. This stir-fry recipe is bursting with flavors and can be whipped up in no time. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to impress. So, let’s dive right in!

Ingredients

  • 500 grams boneless chicken, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.

  2. Add the minced garlic and sliced red chili to the pan. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Add the thinly sliced chicken to the pan and cook until it turns white and is almost cooked through. This should take about 3-4 minutes.

  4. Now, add the sliced onion and bell pepper to the pan and continue stir-frying for another 2 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.

  5. In a small bowl, mix together the oyster sauce, soy sauce, fish sauce, and sugar. Pour this sauce mixture over the chicken and vegetables in the pan. Stir well to ensure everything is coated.

  6. Add the fresh basil leaves to the pan and toss everything together. The heat will wilt the basil leaves and release their aromatic flavors.

  7. Season with freshly ground black pepper to taste. Be careful with the saltiness of the sauces, so it’s best to taste before adding any additional salt.

  8. Continue stir-frying for another minute or so until the chicken is fully cooked and the flavors have melded together.

  9. Remove the pan from the heat and serve the Thai Basil Chicken over steamed jasmine rice or noodles. Garnish with some extra basil leaves for a pop of freshness.

Tips and Variations

  • For a spicier kick, you can increase the amount of red chili or add some Thai bird’s eye chili.

  • If you’re a vegetarian, you can substitute the chicken with tofu or your favorite veggies like mushrooms, broccoli, or snow peas.

  • Thai Basil Chicken is traditionally made with holy basil, but if you can’t find it, regular sweet basil works just fine.

  • Feel free to customize the sauce according to your taste preferences. Add a squeeze of lime juice for a tangy twist or a dash of sriracha for an extra kick.

  • To make it a complete meal, serve the Thai Basil Chicken with a side of steamed vegetables or a refreshing Thai cucumber salad.

Final Thoughts

Thai Basil Chicken is a classic Thai dish that showcases the perfect balance of sweet, salty, and spicy flavors. With its quick preparation and delightful taste, it’s no wonder it’s a favorite among both locals and visitors in Thailand. So, don your apron, grab your wok, and let the aroma of this tantalizing stir-fry fill your kitchen. Get ready to embark on a culinary journey that will leave you craving for more!
