Keto Holiday Baking: Traditional & Creative Low-Carb Recipes


The holiday season is a time for gatherings, laughter, and of course, indulging in mouthwatering treats. But if you’re on a keto diet, traditional holiday baked goods laden with sugar and flour can be a snowball of temptation you’d rather avoid. Fear not! This year, delight in guilt-free indulgences with our keto holiday baking guide - a delightful blend of classic and creative low-carb recipes.

Keto Gingerbread Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 2 tablespoons pumpkin pie spice
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup erythritol or monk fruit sweetener
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the almond flour, pumpkin pie spice, and baking soda. Mix well.
  3. Add softened butter and sweetener to the bowl, and cream them together using an electric mixer.
  4. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then add the vanilla extract, mixing until fully incorporated.
  5. Form the cookie dough into small, equal-sized balls and place them on the prepared baking sheet. Flatten the cookies slightly using a fork or a sugared spatula.
  6. Bake cookies for 10-12 minutes, or until lightly golden. Allow them to cool on a wire rack.
  7. Enjoy your gingerbread cookies with a cup of your favorite keto-friendly hot chocolate.

Sugar-Free Eggnog Cheesecake

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups almond flour
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 6 ounces cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup erythritol or monk fruit sweetener
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup eggnog

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch springform pan using butter.
  2. Combine almond flour and melted butter in a bowl and press the mixture into the bottom of the prepared pan to form the crust. Bake for 12 minutes, then remove from the oven and let it cool slightly.
  3. In a large bowl, beat together the softened cream cheese and sweetener until creamy and smooth. Add the vanilla extract and beat again.
  4. Add eggs one at a time, mixing briefly after each addition until just combined.
  5. Slowly blend in the eggnog, stirring until the mixture is smooth.
  6. Pour the batter over the crust, and return the pan to the oven.
  7. Bake for approximately 45-60 minutes, or until the center is nearly set. Let the cheesecake cool at room temperature for about an hour.
  8. Finally, refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, or overnight, before serving.

Almond Joy Fat Bombs

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 tbsp almond butter
  • 1 cup coconut cream, chilled
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  • 2 tbsp erythritol or monk fruit sweetener

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, gently combine almond butter and cocoa powder.
  2. Whip the chilled coconut cream in a separate bowl using an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.
  3. Add the whipped coconut cream, almond butter mixture, shredded coconut and sweetener to a mixing bowl, and mix until smooth.
  4. Scoop out 1-inch balls of the mixture using a spoon or cookie scoop, then roll them in the extra shredded coconut on a plate.
  5. Place the fat bombs on a lined plate and freeze them for 30 minutes before serving.
  6. Enjoy the delicious fusion of flavors inspired by the iconic Almond Joy candy bar, without the guilt!

Spiced Pecan Shortbread

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup erythritol or monk fruit sweetener
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 300°F (150°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium-sized bowl, mix the almond flour, sweetener, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. Stir in the melted butter until the mixture is crumbly.
  4. Spread half of the mixture into a thin layer at the bottom of the baking sheet, and sprinkle the pecan pieces over it.
  5. Crumble the remaining dough over the top of the pecans, and press down gently.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, until lightly golden. Allow the shortbread to cool on the baking sheet before breaking into pieces.
  7. Share the joy of this low-carb, baked goodness with your loved ones at your holiday gathering.

These recipes will allow you to enjoy the holiday season while staying true to the keto diet. Feel free to experiment with different flavor combinations and ingredients to elevate the festive spirit even more. The happiness shared in times of feasting and celebration lies not only in the taste but also in the memories created and the moments cherished with family and friends.
