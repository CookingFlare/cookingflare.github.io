Thanksgiving Breakfast Ideas to Start the Day Right


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. While the main event is usually the grand feast later in the day, why not kick off your Thanksgiving morning with a delightful breakfast spread? Starting the day right with a hearty and festive breakfast can set a positive tone for the rest of the holiday. Here are some Thanksgiving breakfast ideas that will leave your family and guests feeling satisfied and ready to embrace the day’s celebrations.

Pumpkin Spice Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • Maple syrup and whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and spices.
  2. In another bowl, mix the buttermilk, pumpkin puree, egg, and melted butter until well combined.
  3. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients until just combined. Do not overmix.
  4. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of batter for each pancake onto the skillet.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown.
  7. Serve the pancakes with maple syrup and a dollop of whipped cream.

Apple Cinnamon French Toast Bake

Ingredients:

  • 1 loaf of French bread, cut into cubes
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • Maple syrup for serving

Instructions:

  1. Grease a baking dish and place the bread cubes in it.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, cream, sugar, vanilla, and cinnamon.
  3. Pour the egg mixture over the bread cubes, making sure they are well coated.
  4. Gently fold in the diced apples and chopped pecans.
  5. Cover and refrigerate the mixture overnight.
  6. In the morning, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the French toast for about 45 minutes or until golden and set.
  7. Serve warm with maple syrup drizzled on top.

Cranberry Orange Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen cranberries
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • Turbinado sugar for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the melted butter, orange juice, eggs, and vanilla.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients until just incorporated.
  5. Gently fold in the cranberries and orange zest.
  6. Divide the batter among the muffin cups and sprinkle the tops with turbinado sugar.
  7. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  8. Let the muffins cool slightly before serving.

These Thanksgiving breakfast ideas are sure to delight your loved ones and kickstart your holiday on a delicious note. Whether you prefer sweet or savory dishes, there’s something here for everyone to enjoy. So, rise and shine on Thanksgiving morning with these flavorful breakfast treats that will make your day even more special. Happy Thanksgiving!

Remember, a good breakfast is the foundation for a great day ahead!
