Easy Greek Salad Recipe with Feta Cheese


Greek salad is the quintessential dish that brings together simplicity, vibrant flavors, and refreshing crunch. With roots deeply embedded in the Mediterranean, this salad is famous for its medley of fresh vegetables, tangy vinaigrette, and creamy feta cheese. Whether you’re hosting a dinner, meal prepping for the week, or whipping up a quick healthy side, this easy Greek salad recipe is your go-to option.

Ingredients You’ll Need

The beauty of a Greek salad lies in its simplicity. Here’s what you’ll need to create this flavorful dish:

For the Salad:

  • Cucumbers: 1 large cucumber, diced or sliced, preferably Persian or English cucumbers for extra crunch and minimal seeds.
  • Tomatoes: 3-4 medium ripe tomatoes, chopped into bite-sized pieces. Cherry tomatoes work excellently too.
  • Red Onion: ½ of a medium red onion, thinly sliced or chopped, depending on personal preference.
  • Bell Pepper: 1 medium green bell pepper, diced or julienned for a bit of color and crunch.
  • Kalamata Olives: ½ cup, pitted and halved, adding a tangy kick and authentic Greek flavor.
  • Feta Cheese: 8 ounces, crumbled or cubed for a creamy, salty burst. Authentic Greek feta is best.
  • Fresh Parsley: Optional, as a garnish for an herby twist.

For the Dressing:

  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil: ¼ cup, for a luscious, smooth base.
  • Red Wine Vinegar: 2 tablespoons, for acidity and brightness.
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice, enhancing the tanginess of the dressing.
  • Garlic: 1 small clove, minced for subtle aromatic depth.
  • Dried Oregano: 1 teaspoon, a staple herb in Greek cuisine.
  • Salt and Black Pepper: To taste, balancing the seasoning.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Vegetables

Start by prepping all the vegetables. Ensure the cucumbers and tomatoes are diced or sliced evenly for uniformity. The red onion should be thinly sliced to avoid a bite that’s too overpowering. If raw onions are too intense for your taste, soak them in cold water for about 10 minutes to mellow their sharpness. Bell peppers should be chopped beautifully to add pops of color and crunch to your salad presentation.

Assembling the Salad

In a large salad bowl, combine the cucumbers, tomatoes, red onions, bell peppers, and Kalamata olives. Toss them gently to mix the flavors while maintaining their structure. Add an even layer of feta cheese on top, either cubed or crumbled as per your preference. The creamy, salty nature of feta cheese ties the entire dish together beautifully.

Making the Dressing

The dressing is the star of this recipe and brings all the ingredients to life. In a small mixing bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, lemon juice, minced garlic, and dried oregano. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. The combination of olive oil, acidity, and oregano creates the perfect balance of flavors.

Tossing the Salad

Drizzle the dressing generously over the salad and gently toss using salad tongs. If you prefer, you can serve the dressing on the side to let individuals customize their servings. Garnish with a sprinkle of fresh parsley for an added layer of color and freshness.

Tips for the Perfect Greek Salad

  1. Use High-Quality Ingredients: The fewer the ingredients, the more important their quality. Choose fresh, ripe vegetables and authentic Greek feta cheese.
  2. Serve Fresh: Greek salad is best served immediately to maintain the crunchiness of the vegetables and the balance of vinaigrette.
  3. Customizations: Feel free to add extras like capers, artichoke hearts, or avocado for a twist on the classic recipe.
  4. Chill the Ingredients: Refrigerate the vegetables before making the salad to ensure it’s cold, crisp, and refreshing.

Why This Recipe is Perfect for Any Occasion

Greek salad works as a standalone meal or a versatile side dish. Pair it with grilled protein such as chicken or lamb for a satisfying Mediterranean-inspired dinner. You can also use it as a topping for pita wraps or enjoy it with crusty bread dipped in the leftover dressing. This salad is vibrant, nutritious, and packed with antioxidants from the vegetables and healthy fats from the feta cheese and olive oil.

The Nutritional Benefits of Greek Salad

This easy Greek salad is not only delicious but also highly nutritious. Cucumbers are hydrating and low in calories, while tomatoes are rich in lycopene, a powerful antioxidant. Olive oil provides healthy monounsaturated fats, promoting heart health. Feta cheese adds a dose of calcium and protein, while oregano brings anti-inflammatory properties. It’s a truly wholesome dish that you can enjoy without any guilt.

Serving Ideas and Storage Tips

How to Serve:

  • As a Main Course: Serve the salad alongside warm pita bread for a light but filling meal.
  • As a Side Dish: Greek salad complements grilled meats and seafood effortlessly, making it perfect for barbecues and gatherings.
  • In Wraps: Use the salad as stuffing for gyros or wraps to create a portable lunch.

Storage Tips:

If you have leftovers, store the salad in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two days. However, it’s best to store the dressing separately to prevent sogginess.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I prepare Greek salad in advance?
Yes, you can prep the vegetables ahead of time and make the dressing separately. Assemble the salad and toss with the dressing just before serving.

What can I substitute for feta cheese?
If you’re looking for a non-dairy option, try crumbled tofu with a sprinkle of nutritional yeast for a similar texture and flavor profile.

Are there variations of Greek salad?
Absolutely! While the classic version is most popular, some variations include adding protein like grilled chicken, shrimp, or chickpeas for a heartier meal.

Final Thoughts

This easy Greek salad recipe with feta cheese is a celebration of Mediterranean flavors in their simplest form. From its crisp vegetables to its zesty dressing, every bite bursts with freshness. Whether you enjoy it as a light lunch, hearty side dish, or healthy snack, this Greek salad is always a crowd-pleaser. Try it today and experience why it’s a beloved classic across the world!
