Soft and Chewy Bagels: A Classic Breakfast Staple


A warm, soft, and chewy bagel is the perfect way to start your day. The classic breakfast staple has been a favorite among breakfast enthusiasts for generations. With its rich history and versatility, it’s no wonder why bagels have become a beloved part of many cultures around the world. In this post, we’ll explore the art of making soft and chewy bagels from scratch, and share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect bagel.

The History of Bagels

The origins of the bagel date back to Eastern Europe in the 17th century. According to legend, bagels were created by a Polish baker named Shmuel, who was tasked with creating a bread that could be boiled and baked without falling apart. Shmuel’s solution was to shape the dough into a ring, which allowed the bagel to hold its shape during the boiling process. The result was a crispy, chewy, and delicious bread that quickly became a staple in many Eastern European households.

Ingredients and Equipment Needed

To make soft and chewy bagels, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 cups of warm water
  • 2 teaspoons of active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar
  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 1 egg, beaten (optional)
  • Sesame or poppy seeds for topping (optional)

You’ll also need the following equipment:

  • A stand mixer or hand mixer
  • A large mixing bowl
  • A measuring cup
  • A wooden spoon or dough whisk
  • A baking sheet lined with parchment paper
  • A large pot of boiling water

The Dough

To make the dough, combine the warm water, yeast, and sugar in a large mixing bowl. Stir gently to dissolve the yeast, and let the mixture sit for 5-10 minutes, or until it becomes frothy.

Next, add the flour, salt, and vegetable oil to the bowl. Mix the dough using a stand mixer or hand mixer until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can also mix the dough by hand using a wooden spoon or dough whisk.

Knead the dough on a floured surface for 10-15 minutes, or until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Shaping the Bagels

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 8-10 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and then use your thumbs to create a hole in the center of each ball. Stretch the dough out to form a bagel shape, making sure the hole is large enough to accommodate the yeast.

Place the shaped bagels onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch of space between each bagel. Cover the bagels with plastic wrap and let them rise for another 30-45 minutes, or until they have puffed up slightly.

Boiling the Bagels

Preheat a large pot of boiling water and add 1-2 tablespoons of sugar. Gently place the bagels into the boiling water, making sure they are fully submerged. Boil the bagels for 1-2 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown.

Baking the Bagels

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and place the boiled bagels onto it. Brush the bagels with egg wash (beaten egg mixed with a little water) and sprinkle with sesame or poppy seeds if desired.

Bake the bagels for 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown. Remove the bagels from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect soft and chewy bagel:

  • Use a high-quality all-purpose flour that contains a high protein content, as this will help the bagels to hold their shape and give them a chewy texture.
  • Make sure the yeast is fully activated before adding it to the dough, as this will help the bagels to rise properly.
  • Don’t overmix the dough, as this can lead to a dense and tough bagel.
  • Use a gentle touch when shaping the bagels, as this will help to prevent them from becoming dense and chewy.
  • Don’t overcrowd the pot when boiling the bagels, as this can cause them to stick together and become misshapen.
  • Keep an eye on the bagels while they’re baking, as they can go from perfectly golden to burnt quickly.

Conclusion

Making soft and chewy bagels from scratch is a simple and rewarding process that requires a little patience and practice. By following the steps outlined in this post and using the tips and tricks provided, you’ll be well on your way to creating delicious, chewy bagels that will become a staple in your household. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced baker, the art of making bagels is a skill that’s worth mastering. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the delicious results!
