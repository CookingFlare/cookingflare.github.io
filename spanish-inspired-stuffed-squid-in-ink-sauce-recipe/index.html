Spanish Inspired Stuffed Squid in Ink Sauce Recipe


If you’re looking to impress your guests with a unique and flavorful dish, look no further than the Spanish inspired stuffed squid in ink sauce. This dish is a true representation of the rich and diverse culinary heritage of Spain, with its bold flavors and elegant presentation. The combination of tender squid, savory stuffing, and a luscious ink sauce creates a dish that is as visually stunning as it is delicious. Let’s dive into the details of how to prepare this exquisite Spanish delicacy.

Ingredients

For the Stuffed Squid:

  • 6 medium-sized whole squid, cleaned
  • 1 cup cooked white rice
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped roasted almonds
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

For the Ink Sauce:

  • Ink sacs from the squid (if available)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 cup fish or vegetable stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Stuffed Squid:

    • In a skillet, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
    • Add the chopped tomato and cook until it breaks down and becomes saucy.
    • Stir in the cooked white rice, chopped parsley, roasted almonds, and raisins. Season with salt and pepper, and cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
    • Carefully stuff each squid with the rice mixture, leaving some room at the top to secure the opening with a toothpick.

  2. Make the Ink Sauce:

    • If the squid ink sacs are available, carefully remove them and set them aside.
    • In a separate saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
    • Add the smoked paprika and stir for a minute to release its flavors.
    • Pour in the white wine and let it simmer until slightly reduced.
    • Add the fish or vegetable stock and the reserved squid ink sacs (if available), then bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for about 15-20 minutes until the sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  3. Cook the Stuffed Squid:

    • In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the stuffed squid and sear them on all sides until golden brown.
    • Pour the prepared ink sauce over the squid, cover the skillet, and let the squid simmer in the sauce for 25-30 minutes, or until they are tender.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Once the squid is cooked through and tender, carefully remove the toothpicks.
    • Plate the stuffed squid and drizzle with the rich ink sauce. Garnish with a sprinkle of chopped parsley and serve hot.

Conclusion

The Spanish inspired stuffed squid in ink sauce is a show-stopping dish that will transport your taste buds to the coastal regions of Spain. The tender squid, flavorful stuffing, and luxurious ink sauce come together to create a dining experience that is truly unforgettable. Whether you’re hosting a special dinner party or simply looking to elevate your culinary skills, this recipe is sure to leave a lasting impression. Embrace the bold flavors of Spain and treat yourself and your loved ones to this exquisite seafood delight. Buena provecho!
