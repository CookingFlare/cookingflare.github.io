Pimientos de Padron: Small but Flavorful Spanish Peppers


If you’re a fan of Spanish cuisine, then you’ve probably come across Pimientos de Padrón. These small, green peppers are a staple in Spanish tapas and are loved for their mild yet delicious flavor. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or a novice in the kitchen, Pimientos de Padrón are a must-try ingredient that will add a burst of flavor to your dishes. In this blog post, we’ll explore the origins and characteristics of Pimientos de Padrón, as well as share a few mouthwatering recipes that showcase these delightful peppers.

Origins and Characteristics

Pimientos de Padrón, also known as Padrón peppers, are named after the town of Padrón in the province of A Coruña, Galicia, in northwestern Spain. These peppers are typically harvested when they are still green and small, measuring around 5 centimeters in length. While most Padrón peppers are mild, occasionally, you may come across a spicy one, which adds an element of surprise to any dish.

Choosing and Preparing Pimientos de Padrón

When selecting Pimientos de Padrón, look for firm peppers with smooth skin. Avoid any peppers that have soft spots or wrinkles, as this indicates they may be past their prime. To prepare the peppers, simply wash them thoroughly and pat them dry. Pimientos de Padrón are typically cooked whole, which helps to retain their unique flavor and texture.

Recipe 1: Pimientos de Padrón Tapas

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams Pimientos de Padrón
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Sea salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the Pimientos de Padrón to the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the peppers start to blister and char.
  3. Remove from heat and sprinkle with sea salt.
  4. Serve immediately as a tapa or appetizer.

Recipe 2: Pimientos de Padrón Stuffed with Cheese

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams Pimientos de Padrón
  • 100 grams goat cheese
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Sea salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. Slice a small slit lengthwise in each Pimiento de Padrón and remove the seeds.
  3. Stuff each pepper with a small amount of goat cheese.
  4. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Add the stuffed peppers to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side, until the cheese melts and the peppers are slightly charred.
  6. Transfer the peppers to a baking dish and bake for 10 minutes.
  7. Remove from the oven, sprinkle with sea salt, and serve as a delicious side dish or appetizer.

Recipe 3: Pimientos de Padrón Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 250 grams Pimientos de Padrón
  • 300 grams pasta of your choice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  4. Add the Pimientos de Padrón to the skillet and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the peppers start to blister and char.
  5. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss until well coated with the pepper and garlic mixture.
  6. Serve the pasta with a generous sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and garnish with fresh basil leaves.

In Conclusion

Pimientos de Padrón are a delightful addition to any dish, whether you’re looking to add a touch of Spanish flavor to your tapas, stuff them with cheese for a savory appetizer, or incorporate them into a pasta dish for a quick and tasty meal. Their mild yet flavorful taste makes them a versatile ingredient that can be enjoyed by everyone. So, the next time you’re craving something different, give Pimientos de Padrón a try and let their unique flavor transport you to the sunny streets of Spain. Buen provecho!
