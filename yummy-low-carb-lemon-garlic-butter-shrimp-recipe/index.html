Yummy Low Carb Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp Recipe


When it comes to quick, flavorful, and healthy meals, few dishes can compete with this Low Carb Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp recipe. Perfectly succulent shrimp are bathed in a rich and zesty lemon garlic butter sauce, making it an irresistible option for dinner. Whether you’re following a low-carb lifestyle or simply looking to enjoy a dish that’s bursting with flavor, this recipe checks all the boxes.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

1. Quick and Easy

This shrimp dish comes together in less than 30 minutes, making it an ideal choice for busy weeknights. With minimal prep and cooking time, you can enjoy a gourmet meal without the hassle.

2. Low Carb Delight

With its focus on fresh, low-carb ingredients, this recipe is perfect for those following ketogenic or low-carb diets. Shrimp is naturally low in carbohydrates and high in protein, making it a fantastic choice for any health-conscious meal.

3. Versatile and Delicious

This lemon garlic butter sauce is not only delicious but also versatile. You can pair it with a variety of sides, from zoodles (zucchini noodles) to cauliflower rice or simply enjoy it on its own.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this delectable dish, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2 lemons)
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for a bit of heat)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

How to Make Low Carb Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

Begin by rinsing the shrimp under cold water to remove any residue. Pat them dry with a paper towel. This step is crucial as it ensures that the shrimp sear nicely and do not steam in the pan.

Step 2: Sauté the Garlic

In a large skillet, melt the unsalted butter over medium heat. Once the butter is melted and bubbling, add the minced garlic. Sauté the garlic for about 1-2 minutes until it becomes fragrant, but be careful not to burn it. Burnt garlic can impart a bitter taste to your dish.

Step 3: Cook the Shrimp

Add the cleaned shrimp to the skillet, spreading them out in an even layer. Cook the shrimp for 2-3 minutes on one side without moving them. This allows for a nice sear. Once the underside is pink and opaque, flip the shrimp over to cook the other side.

Step 4: Add Lemon Juice and Seasoning

Once the shrimp are cooked through (about 3-4 minutes total), pour in the fresh lemon juice, lemon zest, salt, pepper, and optional red pepper flakes. Toss everything together to ensure the shrimp are well coated in the tangy sauce. Cook for another minute to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 5: Plate and Garnish

Remove the skillet from heat. Transfer the lemon garlic butter shrimp to a serving platter. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a pop of color and added freshness. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for an extra burst of citrus.

Serving Suggestions

This low carb lemon garlic butter shrimp is incredibly versatile and pairs beautifully with various sides. Here are a few suggestions to enhance your meal:

  • Zucchini Noodles: Spiralized zucchini is a fantastic low-carb alternative to pasta. Sauté the zoodles briefly in olive oil and serve under the shrimp for a complete dish.

  • Cauliflower Rice: Lightly sautéed or steamed cauliflower rice makes for a hearty base that absorbs the delicious garlic butter sauce.

  • Steamed Asparagus or Broccoli: These greens add a nutritious crunch and complement the shrimp perfectly. Simply steam them while you cook the shrimp for a balanced meal.

Nutritional Information

This Low Carb Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp recipe is not just delicious but also packed with nutrients. Here’s a rough breakdown of the nutritional profile per serving (based on 4 servings):

  • Calories: 250
  • Protein: 26g
  • Fat: 15g
  • Carbohydrates: 2g
  • Fiber: 0g

Keep in mind that nutritional values can vary based on the specific ingredients and portion sizes.

Tips for the Best Lemon Garlic Butter Shrimp

  • Fresh Ingredients: Using fresh garlic and lemon will elevate the flavor of this dish. If possible, avoid using bottled lemon juice for the best taste.

  • Don’t Overcook the Shrimp: Shrimp cooks quickly, and overcooking can lead to rubbery texture. Keep an eye on them and remove them from heat as soon as they turn pink.

  • Adjust the Heat: If you prefer a milder dish, you can omit the red pepper flakes. For more heat, feel free to add more according to your taste.

  • Save the Leftovers: If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat gently on the stove to avoid overcooking the shrimp further.
