Sous Vide Garlic Butter Scallops: A Gourmet Seafood Delight


Scallops are a delicacy that many seafood lovers adore. And when it comes to preparing them, there are numerous ways to bring out their natural flavors and textures. One technique that has gained popularity among chefs and home cooks alike is sous vide cooking. Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, involves cooking food in a precise temperature-controlled water bath. This method ensures that the scallops are cooked to perfection, tender and succulent, with a melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Ingredients

  • 8 large scallops
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 130°F (54°C). This temperature will give you perfectly cooked medium-rare scallops.

  2. While the water bath is heating up, pat dry the scallops with a paper towel to remove any excess moisture. Season them with salt and pepper on both sides.

  3. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the lemon juice. Set aside.

  4. Place the seasoned scallops in a single layer in a vacuum-sealable bag or a heavy-duty ziplock bag. Pour the garlic butter mixture over the scallops, making sure they are evenly coated. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer or the water displacement method.

  5. Gently lower the bag into the preheated water bath, making sure it is fully submerged. If necessary, use a clip or a heavy object to keep the bag from floating. Cook the scallops for 30 minutes.

  6. Once the cooking time is up, carefully remove the bag from the water bath. Open the bag and transfer the scallops to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess butter.

  7. Heat a skillet over high heat. Add a splash of oil and sear the scallops for 1-2 minutes on each side until they develop a golden crust. Be careful not to overcook them.

  8. Serve the sous vide garlic butter scallops immediately, garnished with fresh chopped parsley. They pair wonderfully with a crisp white wine or a light salad.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer your scallops cooked to a different doneness, you can adjust the cooking time accordingly. For rare scallops, cook them for 20 minutes, and for well-done scallops, cook them for 40 minutes.
  • Experiment with different herbs and spices to enhance the flavor of the garlic butter. Try adding a pinch of cayenne pepper or a sprinkle of fresh thyme.
  • Serve the scallops over a bed of creamy risotto or with a side of roasted vegetables for a complete gourmet meal.

Sous vide garlic butter scallops are a gourmet seafood delight that will impress your guests and satisfy your cravings. The precise temperature control of sous vide cooking ensures that the scallops are cooked to perfection every time, resulting in a luxurious and tender texture. The addition of garlic butter adds a rich and savory flavor that enhances the natural sweetness of the scallops. So why wait? Grab your sous vide machine and indulge in this exquisite dish today!
