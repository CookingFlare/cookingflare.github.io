Delicious Pumpernickel Bread: Rich and Dark


Pumpernickel bread is a classic German bread known for its rich and dark color, robust flavor, and dense texture. It is a delightful addition to any bread basket and pairs perfectly with a variety of toppings and spreads. In this recipe, we will take you through the steps to create your own delicious pumpernickel bread right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rye flour
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup bread flour
  • 1/2 cup cornmeal
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons caraway seeds
  • 2 teaspoons instant coffee granules
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 cups warm water
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rye flour, whole wheat flour, bread flour, cornmeal, cocoa powder, caraway seeds, instant coffee granules, yeast, and salt. Mix well to distribute the ingredients evenly.

  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm water, molasses, and vegetable oil until well combined. Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and stir until a shaggy dough forms.

  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, add a little more bread flour, but try not to add too much as it may make the bread too dense.

  4. Grease a large bowl with oil and place the dough in it, turning it to coat it with the oil. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  6. Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and shape it into a loaf. Place the loaf onto the prepared baking sheet.

  7. Cover the loaf with the kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let it rise for another 30 minutes.

  8. Using a sharp knife or razor blade, make a few diagonal slashes across the top of the loaf. This will help the bread expand during baking.

  9. Bake the pumpernickel bread in the preheated oven for about 40-45 minutes, or until the loaf is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

  10. Remove the bread from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack before slicing.

Serving Suggestions

Pumpernickel bread is versatile and can be enjoyed in many ways. Here are a few serving suggestions to get you started:

  • Spread a generous amount of cream cheese on a slice of pumpernickel bread and top it with smoked salmon, capers, and red onion for a delicious open-faced sandwich.
  • Toast slices of pumpernickel bread and serve them alongside a bowl of hot soup for a comforting meal.
  • Cut the bread into cubes, toss them with olive oil and seasonings, and bake until crispy for homemade croutons that can be added to salads or soups.
  • Use pumpernickel bread as a base for bruschetta by topping it with chopped tomatoes, garlic, basil, and a drizzle of balsamic vinegar.

Conclusion

Making your own pumpernickel bread at home allows you to enjoy the deep flavors and unique characteristics of this German specialty. With its rich and dark color, dense texture, and robust flavor, it is sure to impress your family and friends. Get creative with your toppings and serve it in various ways to explore the versatility of this delicious bread. So why not give it a try? Bake a loaf of homemade pumpernickel bread today and savor the goodness!
