Keto & Irish: Traditional & Modern Low-Carb St. Patrick's Day Recipes


St. Patrick’s Day is more than just green beer and corned beef and cabbage. It’s a time to celebrate and honor Irish cultural heritage, traditions, and delicious recipes. For those following a ketogenic lifestyle, it doesn’t mean you have to miss out on the festivities. We’ve compiled a list of traditional and modern low-carb St. Patrick’s Day recipes that are sure to satisfy your cravings without sacrificing the rich traditions and flavors.

1. Classic Colcannon

Colcannon is a classic Irish dish made with mashed potatoes and cabbage or kale. This low-carb version replaces the potatoes with cauliflower and ramps up the flavor with bacon and chives.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1/2 pound kale, chopped
  • 1/2 pound bacon, diced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup chives, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Boil the cauliflower florets in salted water until tender, about 10-15 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large frying pan, cook the bacon until crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon, leaving the grease in the pan.
  3. Add the kale to the pan and cook until wilted, about 5 minutes.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, mash the cooked cauliflower with a hand masher. Mix in the cooked bacon and kale, heavy cream, chives, garlic powder, salt, and pepper until well combined. Serve immediately.

2. Irish Lamb Stew with Celeriac

A hearty Irish dish, lamb stew pairs beautifully with a low-carb alternative to potatoes - celeriac. This recipe is sure to bring warmth to your St. Patrick’s Day table.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound lamb stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1 large celeriac (celery root), peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup Irish whiskey (optional)
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat, brown the lamb on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove lamb and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, saute the onion and garlic until soft and translucent, about 3-5 minutes. Add the whiskey and let it cook down.
  3. Add the browned lamb, beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. Bring to a simmer, then lower heat to low, cover, and cook for about 1 1/2 hours.
  4. Add the celeriac and carrots, and cook for another 20-30 minutes until the celeriac is tender. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  5. Once finished cooking, garnish with fresh parsley and serve with a side of keto-friendly vegetables or a salad.

3. Cabbage and Beef Soup

This creamy, low-carb cabbage and beef soup is a delicious and satisfying meal that pays homage to Ireland’s love of hearty soups and stews.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 large head of cabbage, cored and chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, brown the ground beef over medium heat, breaking it up as it cooks. Once cooked through, drain the excess fat and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, saute the onion and garlic until soft and fragrant, about 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped cabbage, carrots, and cooked ground beef to the pot. Cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste, beef broth, heavy cream, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then cover and lower the heat to low. Cook for another 30 minutes, until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed, and serve hot.

4. Irish Cheddar Soufflé with a Twist

A soufflé is a delightful and light way to end your St. Patrick’s Day feast. This rich, cheesy, low-carb cheddar soufflé baked with a sprinkle of nutmeg and topped with crisp, crunchy bacon bits.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs, separated
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup sharp Irish cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup chopped bacon, cooked and crumbled

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a soufflé dish with butter.
  2. In a heatproof bowl, whisk together the egg yolks, heavy cream, and 1/4 cup of the shredded cheddar cheese. Set aside.
  3. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk until smooth. Gradually add the cream and egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook until the mixture thickens, about 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the remaining cheddar cheese, salt, and nutmeg.
  4. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the beaten egg whites into the cheese mixture until just combined.
  5. Pour the soufflé batter into the prepared dish, ensuring not to tap the dish or let the batter Rest. Use a spatula to smooth the top. Sprinkle the cooked and crumbled bacon bits on top.
  6. Place the soufflé dish on a baking sheet for stability and bake for 30-35 minutes, until the top is golden brown and the soufflé has risen significantly. Serve immediately.

With these delicious, low-carb, keto-friendly recipes, you’ll be able to celebrate St. Patrick’s Day without feeling held back by dietary restrictions. These twists on traditional Irish recipes are perfect for sharing with friends and loved ones while still enjoying the rich flavors and cultural spirit of the holiday. May your plate be filled with good luck and fortune this St. Patrick’s Day!
