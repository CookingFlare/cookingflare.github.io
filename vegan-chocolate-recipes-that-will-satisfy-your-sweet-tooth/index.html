Vegan Chocolate Recipes That Will Satisfy Your Sweet Tooth


Craving something sweet, but trying to stick to a plant-based diet? Whether you’re vegan by choice or just want to explore healthier alternatives, the world of vegan chocolate offers delicious options that are just as satisfying, if not more so, than their dairy-laden counterparts. Chocolate lovers everywhere are discovering that vegan chocolate can be rich, creamy, and indulgent, without any animal products.

In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of vegan chocolate recipes that will keep you coming back for more. From gooey brownies to silky mousse, and even chocolate truffles, we’ve got you covered. These recipes are simple to follow and sure to impress anyone who tries them. Let’s dive right into these scrumptious vegan chocolate creations!

1. Vegan Chocolate Fudge Brownies

These fudgy, melt-in-your-mouth brownies are so rich and decadent, it’s hard to believe they’re vegan. The combination of dark chocolate and plant-based ingredients results in a dessert that’s both comforting and indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of almond flour
  • 1/2 cup of cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup of coconut sugar
  • 1/2 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/2 cup of melted coconut oil
  • 1/4 cup of unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 3/4 cup of dark chocolate chips (vegan)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the almond flour, cocoa powder, coconut sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the maple syrup, melted coconut oil, applesauce, and vanilla extract. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until fully combined.
  4. Stir in the dark chocolate chips, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and smooth out the top.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Let the brownies cool completely before cutting them into squares. Enjoy these rich, fudgy delights!

2. Vegan Chocolate Mousse

This vegan chocolate mousse is light, creamy, and packed with rich chocolate flavor. Made with coconut cream, this dessert is perfect for any occasion when you need a smooth and luxurious treat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of full-fat coconut milk (refrigerated overnight)
  • 1/2 cup of dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. Start by opening the can of refrigerated coconut milk and scoop out the thick coconut cream (leave the liquid behind).
  2. In a heatproof bowl, melt the dark chocolate chips using a double boiler method or by microwaving in 20-second intervals, stirring between each.
  3. Once the chocolate is melted, stir in the maple syrup, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt.
  4. Gently fold the melted chocolate into the coconut cream, stirring until fully incorporated.
  5. Spoon the mousse into individual serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow it to firm up.
  6. Top with a sprinkle of cocoa powder or fresh berries before serving.

This mousse is a must-try if you’re looking for a vegan chocolate recipe that’s both luxurious and easy to make. It’s perfect for impressing guests or indulging yourself!

3. Vegan Chocolate Truffles

Truffles are a classic chocolate treat, and with a few simple swaps, you can make them vegan! These vegan chocolate truffles are rich, smooth, and perfect for a bite-sized indulgence.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup of coconut milk (or other non-dairy milk)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup of cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Optional toppings: chopped nuts, shredded coconut, or sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the coconut milk over medium heat until it begins to simmer. Remove it from the heat.
  2. Add the dark chocolate chips to the coconut milk and let them sit for a minute. Stir until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth.
  3. Stir in the vanilla extract and let the chocolate mixture cool to room temperature. Once it has cooled, refrigerate it for about 30 minutes until it firms up slightly.
  4. Scoop out small portions of the chocolate mixture and roll them into balls using your hands.
  5. Roll each truffle in cocoa powder or any toppings of your choice, such as chopped nuts or shredded coconut.
  6. Place the truffles on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and refrigerate them for at least 1 hour until set.

These vegan chocolate truffles are a simple yet elegant treat. They’re perfect for special occasions, gifting, or simply satisfying your sweet tooth.

4. Vegan Chocolate Chip Cookies

Who doesn’t love a warm, gooey chocolate chip cookie? This vegan version uses a combination of plant-based butter and dairy-free chocolate chips to recreate a classic favorite that everyone can enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 cup of coconut oil, melted
  • 1/2 cup of coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup of maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 3/4 cup of dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Stir until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Fold in the dairy-free chocolate chips.
  6. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. Let the cookies cool on the sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These vegan chocolate chip cookies are soft, chewy, and satisfyingly sweet. You won’t miss the dairy at all!

5. Vegan Chocolate-Covered Strawberries

Chocolate-covered strawberries are a classic treat that’s perfect for a romantic evening or a sweet snack. This vegan version uses rich dark chocolate, making it a decadent yet healthier alternative.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pint of fresh strawberries
  • 1 cup of dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 1 tablespoon of coconut oil
  • Optional: chopped nuts or sea salt for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash the strawberries thoroughly and dry them completely with a paper towel.
  2. In a heatproof bowl, melt the dark chocolate chips and coconut oil together using a double boiler or in the microwave in 20-second intervals.
  3. Once the chocolate is smooth, dip each strawberry into the chocolate, holding the green top. Let any excess chocolate drip off.
  4. Place the dipped strawberries on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. If desired, sprinkle the strawberries with chopped nuts, shredded coconut, or a pinch of sea salt.
  6. Refrigerate the strawberries for at least 30 minutes to let the chocolate harden.

These chocolate-covered strawberries are an easy, elegant vegan dessert that’s sure to impress anyone!

6. Vegan Hot Chocolate

A warm, comforting mug of hot chocolate can be the perfect way to satisfy your sweet tooth. This vegan hot chocolate recipe is creamy, rich, and packed with chocolatey goodness, making it the perfect beverage for cold weather.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of unsweetened almond milk (or other non-dairy milk)
  • 1/4 cup of dairy-free dark chocolate chips
  • 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup (or to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the almond milk, cocoa powder, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the mixture is smooth and warm.
  2. Add the dark chocolate chips and continue stirring until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is creamy.
  3. Stir in the maple syrup and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the hot chocolate into a mug and enjoy it as is or with whipped coconut cream on top for extra indulgence.

This vegan hot chocolate is the ultimate comfort drink, perfect for cozy nights in. It’s sweet, creamy, and rich—everything you want in a chocolate beverage.

These vegan chocolate recipes are perfect for anyone who loves the taste of chocolate but wants to avoid animal products. Whether you’re baking for a special occasion or simply satisfying your sweet tooth, these indulgent treats will leave you feeling both satisfied and guilt-free. So go ahead, indulge in these creamy, rich, and decadent vegan chocolate delights, and treat yourself to the best of what plant-based desserts have to offer!
