10 Easy Fall Recipes for a Quick Weeknight Meal


As the crisp air of fall settles in, evenings call for comforting meals that are both hearty and hassle-free. Weeknight cooking doesn’t have to be stressful, especially when there are plenty of seasonal flavors to incorporate into simple dishes. These 10 easy fall recipes will help you create delicious, balanced meals without spending hours in the kitchen. Perfect for busy schedules, they highlight autumn’s best ingredients like squash, apples, root vegetables, and warming spices. Let’s dig into these fall-inspired dishes that are sure to satisfy your cravings and make weeknight dinners memorable.

1. Butternut Squash Stir-Fry with Garlic and Ginger

Embrace the sweet, nutty flavor of butternut squash with this quick stir-fry. Peel and dice the squash into bite-sized cubes. Heat oil in a skillet and sauté minced garlic and ginger until aromatic. Toss in the squash cubes and cook until they are tender but slightly caramelized. Add soy sauce or tamari for a touch of umami, and sprinkle sesame seeds as a garnish. Serve over rice or quinoa for a wholesome meal.

Time to Prepare: 25 minutes
Key Tip: To save time, use pre-cut butternut squash available at your local grocery store.

2. Apple and Cheddar Grilled Cheese

Upgrade your traditional grilled cheese with fall-inspired flavors. Layer thinly-sliced apples and sharp cheddar cheese between two slices of sourdough bread. Spread butter on the outside of the bread and cook in a skillet over medium heat until the bread is golden and the cheese has melted. The combination of sweet apples and savory cheddar creates a unique flavor that screams autumn. Pair with a bowl of tomato soup for the ultimate comfort food duo.

Time to Prepare: 15 minutes
Key Tip: For extra flavor, sprinkle a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg over the apples before assembling the sandwich.

3. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

These vegetarian tacos are quick, nutritious, and full of bold fall flavors. Roast diced sweet potatoes until golden and tender. Warm black beans in a skillet with cumin, smoked paprika, and a hint of chili powder. Fill soft tortillas with the roasted sweet potatoes, seasoned black beans, and top with avocado, pickled red onions, and a lime crema. These tacos are an easy way to celebrate the sweetness of fall.

Time to Prepare: 30 minutes
Key Tip: Roast the sweet potatoes ahead of time to save even more time on weeknights.

4. Pumpkin Sage Risotto

Risotto might sound fancy, but this pumpkin sage version is surprisingly simple to make. Heat your favorite vegetable or chicken stock, and sauté onion in a large pan with a drizzle of olive oil. Add Arborio rice and toast lightly before stirring in pureed pumpkin and ladles of warm stock one at a time. Stir regularly to create a creamy texture, then finish with fresh sage and grated Parmesan.

Time to Prepare: 40 minutes
Key Tip: Use canned pumpkin puree to streamline the cooking process while still enjoying rich seasonal flavor.

5. One-Pan Sausage and Brussels Sprouts

This dinner couldn’t be simpler to prepare, yet it packs a ton of flavor. Slice chicken sausage into rounds and arrange on a baking sheet with halved Brussels sprouts. Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of garlic powder, then roast until everything is browned and caramelized. Serve with a side of Dijon mustard for dipping.

Time to Prepare: 30 minutes
Key Tip: Opt for pre-shredded Brussels sprouts or trim them ahead of time for added convenience.

6. Chicken and Wild Rice Soup

A steaming pot of chicken and wild rice soup is perfect for chilly fall nights. Sauté onion, carrot, and celery in a large pot before adding diced chicken breast and chicken broth. Stir in cooked wild rice and let the flavors meld together. Finish with a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk for a creamy texture. This soup is both filling and comforting, ideal for busy weeknights.

Time to Prepare: 40 minutes
Key Tip: Use leftover rotisserie chicken to cut down on prep time.

7. Maple Glazed Salmon with Roasted Vegetables

Maple-glazed salmon is an elegant yet effortless weeknight meal. Whisk together maple syrup, Dijon mustard, and a splash of soy sauce, then brush over salmon fillets. Roast the salmon in the oven alongside your favorite fall vegetables—think sweet potatoes, carrots, and parsnips. Everything cooks together for a streamlined dinner bursting with natural sweetness and savory notes.

Time to Prepare: 30 minutes
Key Tip: To prevent overcooking, take the salmon out when it flakes easily with a fork.

8. Cabbage Stir-Fry with Ground Turkey

Cabbage is a cost-effective and versatile fall vegetable that shines in this quick stir-fry. Brown ground turkey in a skillet with minced garlic and onion, then add shredded cabbage, soy sauce, and a touch of rice vinegar. Cook until the cabbage softens but retains its crunch. Serve over brown rice or as a filling for tortillas.

Time to Prepare: 20 minutes
Key Tip: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes if you like a bit of heat.

9. Spaghetti Squash Alfredo

Spaghetti squash is a low-carb alternative to pasta and is naturally seasonal. Roast halved spaghetti squash until tender, then scrape out the strands with a fork. Toss the squash strands in a quick Alfredo sauce made by melting butter, garlic, cream, and Parmesan cheese in a saucepan. For added protein, stir in cooked chicken or chickpeas.

Time to Prepare: 45 minutes
Key Tip: Roast the spaghetti squash on the weekend and store the strands in the fridge for a ready-to-use base.

10. Quick Beef and Root Vegetable Stew

Fall calls for rich, hearty stews, but you don’t need hours to make one. Brown beef chunks in a large pot, then add diced carrots, parsnips, and potatoes along with beef broth and tomato paste. Simmer until the vegetables are fork-tender and the broth is slightly thickened. This cozy one-pot dinner is sure to become a fall favorite.

Time to Prepare: 45 minutes
Key Tip: Use a pressure cooker or Instant Pot to cut cooking time in half.

Creating quick and delicious weeknight meals during the fall season doesn’t have to be a challenge. These recipes are easy to prepare while elevating the hearty, seasonal flavors that autumn offers. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or a family, these dishes provide a variety of options to suit different tastes and dietary preferences. So, embrace the season, grab your favorite fall ingredients, and enjoy the joy of warm, wholesome cooking without the stress.
