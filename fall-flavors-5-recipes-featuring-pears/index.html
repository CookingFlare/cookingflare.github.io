Fall Flavors: 5 Recipes Featuring Pears


As the crisp autumn air sets in and the vibrant hues of fall leaves fill the landscape, seasonal flavors take center stage in the kitchen. Pears, with their delicate sweetness and luscious texture, are a quintessential fall fruit that pairs beautifully with both savory and sweet ingredients. Whether you prefer them poached, roasted, baked, or fresh, pears bring a touch of elegance to any dish. Here are five delightful recipes that celebrate the irresistible charm of pears and make the most of their versatility.

1. Pear and Butternut Squash Soup

When it comes to warming up during chilly fall evenings, few dishes are as comforting as a hearty bowl of soup. This pear and butternut squash soup is a perfect marriage of sweetness and earthiness, creating a creamy texture that’s both satisfying and refreshing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe pears, peeled, cored, and diced
  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh thyme for garnish

Directions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, then sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the diced butternut squash and pears, stirring to combine. Cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth, then add cinnamon, nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for about 25 minutes, or until the squash and pears are tender.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer small batches to a blender, blending with caution to avoid spills.
  5. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh thyme. Serve warm alongside crusty bread.

2. Pear and Walnut Salad With Maple Vinaigrette

A refreshing fall salad allows pears to shine in their natural form, bringing forth their crisp texture and balanced sweetness. Pairing them with crunchy walnuts and tangy maple vinaigrette creates a dish that’s colorful and palate-pleasing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large pears, thinly sliced
  • 5 oz mixed greens (such as arugula, spinach, and romaine)
  • ½ cup candied walnuts
  • ½ cup crumbled goat cheese
  • ¼ cup dried cranberries

For the Maple Vinaigrette:

  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions:

  1. Combine the mixed greens, sliced pears, candied walnuts, goat cheese, and dried cranberries in a large salad bowl.
  2. Prepare the maple vinaigrette by whisking together olive oil, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  3. Drizzle the vinaigrette over the salad and toss gently to coat. Serve immediately for a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

3. Savory Roasted Pears With Blue Cheese and Honey

This savory roasted pear recipe brings out the fruit’s juicy sweetness while complementing it with the tanginess of blue cheese and the subtle floral notes of honey. It’s an elegant appetizer or side dish that’s sure to impress family and guests alike.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pears, halved and cored
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • ½ cup crumbled blue cheese
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • Fresh thyme for garnish
  • Salt and freshly cracked black pepper

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place the halved pears on the baking sheet, cut side up, and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  3. Roast in the oven for 20–25 minutes, or until tender.
  4. Remove the pears from the oven and immediately add crumbled blue cheese to the center of each pear. Drizzle honey on top and garnish with fresh thyme. Serve warm or at room temperature.

4. Pear and Ginger Crisp

Nothing says fall quite like a bubbling fruit crisp fresh out of the oven. This pear and ginger crisp is a delightful dessert option with warm spices, a crunchy oat topping, and the unmistakable zing of fresh ginger.

Ingredients:

  • 6 pears, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ tsp nutmeg

For the Topping:

  • ¾ cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • Pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine sliced pears, grated ginger, sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, mix oats, flour, brown sugar, melted butter, and salt until crumbly. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the pear mixture.
  4. Bake for 40–45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

5. Pear-Stuffed Pork Tenderloin

For a main course that offers an extraordinary blend of flavors, this pear-stuffed pork tenderloin delivers. The sweet-savory filling highlights the pears while creating a succulent centerpiece for your fall gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pork tenderloin (about 1½ pounds)
  • 2 ripe pears, peeled and diced
  • ½ cup breadcrumbs
  • ¼ cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Butterfly the pork tenderloin by slicing it lengthwise down the middle without cutting all the way through, then flattening it. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Add diced pears, breadcrumbs, pecans, sage, salt, and pepper. Cook until pears soften and the mixture comes together, about 5 minutes.
  3. Spoon the filling onto the center of the tenderloin. Roll the pork tightly around the filling and secure with kitchen twine.
  4. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the tenderloin on all sides until golden brown. Transfer to the oven and roast for 20–25 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 145°F (63°C).
  5. Remove from oven and let rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with roasted vegetables for a stunning fall-inspired meal.

Pears, like the season itself, evoke warmth, sweetness, and coziness, making them an ideal ingredient for fall cooking. These recipes take full advantage of their versatility, offering you everything from light appetizers to memorable desserts and comforting entrees. Incorporate these dishes into your autumn menu, and savor the magic of fall flavors with every bite!
