Indulge in Middle Eastern Fatteh: A Layered Bread and Chickpea Dish


If you’re a fan of Middle Eastern cuisine, you’ve likely come across the delightful dish known as Fatteh. This iconic dish is a flavorful and comforting combination of layered bread, creamy yogurt, spiced chickpeas, and a tantalizing array of toppings. In this recipe, we’ll take a journey into the heart of Middle Eastern flavors and learn how to create a truly indulgent Fatteh dish that will transport your taste buds to another world.

Ingredients

For the Chickpeas:

  • 2 cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Yogurt Sauce:

  • 2 cups Greek yogurt
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Assembly:

  • 4 to 6 pita bread rounds, toasted and broken into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts, toasted
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • Sumac for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas:

    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    • Add the chickpeas, ground cumin, and coriander to the skillet. Season with salt and pepper, then stir to combine.
    • Cook the chickpeas for 5-7 minutes, allowing them to become slightly crispy and golden. Remove from heat and drizzle with lemon juice. Set aside.

  2. Make the Yogurt Sauce:

    • In a bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt, minced garlic, tahini, and lemon juice until smooth and creamy. Season with salt to taste. Set aside.

  3. Assemble the Fatteh:

    • Arrange the toasted pita bread pieces in a single layer on a serving platter.
    • Spoon the prepared chickpeas over the pita bread, creating an even layer.
    • Drizzle the yogurt sauce generously over the chickpeas.
    • Sprinkle the toasted pine nuts, chopped parsley, and mint over the yogurt sauce.
    • Finish with a generous sprinkling of sumac for a burst of tangy flavor.

  4. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Fatteh is best enjoyed immediately while the bread still retains its delightful crunch. Serve as a main dish or as part of a larger Middle Eastern feast.

The Flavors of Fatteh

Fatteh is a dish that celebrates the rich and vibrant flavors of the Middle East. The combination of creamy yogurt, zesty lemon, earthy chickpeas, and aromatic spices creates a symphony of tastes and textures that is truly irresistible. The toasted pita bread adds a delightful crunch, while the nutty pine nuts and fragrant herbs elevate the dish to a whole new level of indulgence.

Making it Your Own

One of the beautiful aspects of Fatteh is its versatility. While this recipe showcases the classic combination of chickpeas and yogurt, feel free to get creative with your toppings. Roasted vegetables, grilled meats, or a drizzle of pomegranate molasses can all add a unique twist to this beloved dish. Let your imagination run wild and tailor your Fatteh to suit your personal preferences.

A Taste of Tradition

Fatteh holds a special place in Middle Eastern cuisine, often gracing the tables during festive gatherings and family celebrations. Its comforting and satisfying nature makes it a beloved dish that brings people together to share in its exquisite flavors. By preparing Fatteh in your own kitchen, you’re not just indulging in a delicious meal – you’re partaking in a rich culinary tradition that spans generations.

In Conclusion

Indulging in the layers of flavor and texture that Fatteh offers is a culinary experience like no other. Whether you’re a seasoned fan of Middle Eastern cuisine or a newcomer eager to explore new flavors, this dish is sure to captivate your senses and leave you craving more. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the art of layering, and treat yourself to a taste of Middle Eastern indulgence with this delightful Fatteh recipe.
