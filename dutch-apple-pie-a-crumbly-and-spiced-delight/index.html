Dutch Apple Pie: A Crumbly and Spiced Delight


Dutch Apple Pie, a classic dessert that has been delighting taste buds for centuries. With its crumbly crust, tender apples, and warm spices, it’s no wonder why this pie has become a staple in many households. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making a traditional Dutch Apple Pie that’s sure to impress your family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cups peeled and sliced apples (Granny Smith or Golden Delicious work well)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Instructions

To begin, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

Next, add the granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract to the bowl. Mix until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

While the dough is chilling, prepare the apple filling. In a separate bowl, combine the sliced apples, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, ground cardamom, and salt. Mix until the apples are evenly coated with the spices.

Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch pie dish and trim the edges to fit.

Fill the pie crust with the apple mixture and dot the top with the heavy cream and cornstarch. Roll out the remaining dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch and use it to cover the pie. Crimp the edges to seal the pie and cut a few slits in the top to allow steam to escape.

Place the pie on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the apples are tender.

Tips and Variations

  • To make a more rustic crust, use a food processor to pulse the flour, butter, and sugar until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  • For a more caramelized crust, brush the top of the pie with a little bit of milk or cream before baking.
  • To make a gluten-free crust, substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free flour blend.
  • To make a vegan crust, substitute the butter with a vegan butter substitute and use a non-dairy milk.
  • To make a mini pie, use a 6-inch pie dish and adjust the baking time accordingly.

Conclusion

Dutch Apple Pie is a classic dessert that’s sure to impress your family and friends. With its crumbly crust, tender apples, and warm spices, it’s a perfect treat for any occasion. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, this recipe is sure to guide you through the process of making a traditional Dutch Apple Pie that’s sure to delight.
