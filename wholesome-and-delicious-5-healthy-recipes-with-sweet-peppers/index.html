Wholesome and Delicious: 5 Healthy Recipes with Sweet Peppers


Are you looking to add a burst of flavor and nutrition to your meals? Look no further than sweet peppers! These vibrant and versatile vegetables are not only delicious but also packed with essential vitamins and minerals. In this blog post, we’ll explore five wholesome and delicious recipes that showcase the incredible taste and health benefits of sweet peppers. So grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

1. Stuffed Sweet Peppers

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet peppers
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C).
  2. Cut the tops off the sweet peppers and remove the seeds.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, corn kernels, diced tomatoes, cilantro, shredded cheddar cheese, cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Stuff each sweet pepper with the quinoa mixture and place them in a baking dish.
  5. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the peppers are tender and the filling is heated through.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

2. Roasted Sweet Pepper Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 sweet peppers (mix of red, yellow, and orange)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400째F (200째C).
  2. Cut the sweet peppers in half and remove the seeds.
  3. Place the sweet peppers on a baking sheet, skin side up, and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until the skin is blistered and charred.
  4. Remove the peppers from the oven and let them cool. Once cooled, peel off the skin and chop the peppers.
  5. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  6. Add the chopped roasted sweet peppers to the pot and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  8. Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth.
  9. Stir in the coconut milk and season with salt and pepper.
  10. Heat the soup again until warmed through, and serve hot.

3. Sweet Pepper and Chickpea Salad

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup cooked chickpeas
  • 1 cup sliced sweet peppers (mix of red, yellow, and orange)
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup sliced red onion
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, cooked chickpeas, sliced sweet peppers, cherry tomatoes, sliced red onion, and crumbled feta cheese.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, extra virgin olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss to coat evenly.
  4. Serve as a refreshing and nutritious side dish or as a light main course.

4. Grilled Sweet Pepper Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 4 sweet peppers (mix of red, yellow, and orange), cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 8 cherry tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, balsamic vinegar, dried oregano, salt, and pepper.
  3. Add the sweet pepper chunks, red onion chunks, and cherry tomatoes to the bowl. Toss to coat the vegetables evenly with the marinade.
  4. Thread the marinated vegetables onto skewers, alternating between peppers, onions, and tomatoes.
  5. Place the skewers on the grill and cook for about 10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are charred and tender.
  6. Remove from the grill and serve as a colorful and flavorful side dish or as a vegetarian main course.

5. Sweet Pepper and Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 sweet peppers (mix of red, yellow, and orange), sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey, cornstarch, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add sliced chicken to the skillet and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  4. In the same skillet, add sesame oil, sliced sweet peppers, sliced onion, and minced garlic. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and pour the soy sauce mixture over the ingredients. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken and vegetables.
  6. Serve the stir-fry hot over steamed rice or noodles for a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Enjoy these five wholesome and delicious recipes that highlight the incredible taste and health benefits of sweet peppers. From stuffed peppers to grilled skewers, these dishes are sure to impress your taste buds and nourish your body. So next time you’re at the grocery store, don’t forget to grab some sweet peppers and get creative in the kitchen. Happy cooking!
