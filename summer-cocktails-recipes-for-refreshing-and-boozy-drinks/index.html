Summer Cocktails: Recipes for Refreshing and Boozy Drinks


Summer is here, and with it comes the perfect excuse to indulge in refreshing and boozy cocktails. As the sun beats down, there’s nothing quite like sipping on a cool and creamy drink to quench your thirst and lift your spirits. In this post, we’ll explore some of the most popular summer cocktails, each with its unique flavors and twists. From classic combinations to modern creations, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the perfect drink to complement your summer vibes.

1. Pimm’s Cup

One of the most iconic summer cocktails, the Pimm’s Cup is a British classic that’s been delighting palates for centuries. Made with Pimm’s No. 1, a gin-based liqueur, mixed with lemonade and garnished with cucumber, mint, and fruit, this refreshing drink is perfect for hot summer days.

To make a Pimm’s Cup, you’ll need:

  • 1 1/2 oz Pimm’s No. 1
  • 4 oz lemonade
  • Slice of cucumber
  • Sprig of mint
  • Fresh fruit (such as strawberries or orange slices)

Combine Pimm’s No. 1 and lemonade in a highball glass filled with ice. Stir gently and garnish with cucumber, mint, and fresh fruit. Serve immediately and enjoy!

2. Mojito Spritz

The mojito is a classic cocktail that’s perfect for summer. By adding a splash of Prosecco and some fresh lime juice, we can create a refreshing and bubbly twist on the original recipe.

To make a Mojito Spritz, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz white rum
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1 oz club soda
  • 1 oz Prosecco
  • Sprig of mint
  • Lime wheel

Muddle mint leaves and lime juice in a cocktail shaker. Add rum and fill with ice. Shake until chilled and strain into a wine glass filled with ice. Top with Prosecco and club soda. Garnish with a sprig of mint and a lime wheel.

3. Cucumber Gimlet

The gimlet is a classic cocktail that’s often overlooked, but it’s perfect for summer. By adding some fresh cucumber and a splash of lime juice, we can create a refreshing and light drink that’s perfect for hot days.

To make a Cucumber Gimlet, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz gin
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup
  • Slice of cucumber
  • Lime wheel

Muddle cucumber slices and simple syrup in a cocktail shaker. Add gin and lime juice. Fill with ice and shake until chilled. Strain into a chilled coupe glass and garnish with a lime wheel and a slice of cucumber.

4. Watermelon Basil Martini

The watermelon basil martini is a unique and refreshing twist on the classic martini. By combining watermelon puree, basil leaves, and vodka, we can create a drink that’s perfect for summer.

To make a Watermelon Basil Martini, you’ll need:

  • 2 oz vodka
  • 1 oz watermelon puree
  • 1/2 oz simple syrup
  • 1/4 oz fresh lime juice
  • Basil leaves
  • Watermelon slice

Combine vodka, watermelon puree, simple syrup, and lime juice in a cocktail shaker. Fill with ice and shake until chilled. Strain into a chilled martini glass and garnish with a basil leaf and a slice of watermelon.

5. Sangria Spritz

Sangria is a classic summer drink that’s perfect for warm weather. By adding some fresh fruit and a splash of soda water, we can create a refreshing and fruity drink that’s perfect for outdoor gatherings.

To make a Sangria Spritz, you’ll need:

  • 1 bottle red wine
  • 1 cup mixed fruit (such as strawberries, grapes, and orange slices)
  • 1/4 cup simple syrup
  • 1/4 cup soda water
  • Slice of orange

Combine red wine, mixed fruit, and simple syrup in a large pitcher. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours. Just before serving, stir in soda water. Serve in wine glasses over ice and garnish with a slice of orange.

These summer cocktails are sure to quench your thirst and satisfy your taste buds. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or just want to relax with a cool drink on a hot summer day, these recipes are sure to impress. So grab your cocktail shaker and get ready to refresh your senses with these boozy and delicious drinks!
