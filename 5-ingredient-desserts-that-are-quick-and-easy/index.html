5-Ingredient Desserts That Are Quick and Easy


There’s something utterly satisfying about whipping up a sweet treat that doesn’t require a dozen ingredients, dozens of steps, or a whole afternoon spent baking. Whether you’ve got an unexpected guest, a last-minute craving, or simply limited pantry options, simple desserts made with just five ingredients (or fewer!) can be your saving grace. They’re time-savvy, budget-friendly, and delicious enough to feel like an indulgence.

In this post, you’ll discover five standout recipes that prove minimal ingredients can yield maximum flavor. Each dessert is streamlined, straightforward, and designed to come together quickly—perfect for busy weeknights, beginner bakers, or anyone seeking effortless sweetness. Let’s dive into these easy, irresistible desserts!

1. Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Why You’ll Love It

This is a classic crowd-pleaser, and yes, it only takes five ingredients! The creamy richness of peanut butter pairs beautifully with sweet bursts of chocolate, creating chewy, flavorful cookies that come together in a flash.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup peanut butter (smooth or chunky)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup chocolate chips

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix peanut butter, sugar, egg, and vanilla extract in a bowl until smooth and consistent.
  3. Fold in chocolate chips gently.
  4. Spoon dough onto a lined baking sheet, spacing well apart.
  5. Bake for 8-10 minutes until edges are lightly golden.
  6. Cool on the pan for a few minutes, then transfer to a rack.

Pro tip: Press a few extra chocolate chips on top right after baking for that professional bakery look.

2. Lemon Posset

Why You’ll Love It

A traditional British dessert, lemon posset is incredibly silky, with bright citrus notes balanced by indulgent creaminess. It doesn’t require gelatin or eggs, just sugar, cream, and lemons.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • ⅔ cup granulated sugar
  • Juice of 2 large lemons
  • Zest of 1 lemon (optional, for extra zing)
  • Fresh berries or mint for garnish (optional)

Directions

  1. Heat cream and sugar in a saucepan over medium heat, stirring until sugar dissolves and mixture simmers gently.
  2. Simmer for 3 minutes without boiling.
  3. Remove from heat and gradually stir in lemon juice (and zest, if using).
  4. Pour mixture into small serving glasses or ramekins.
  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours until set.
  6. Serve with fresh berries or mint sprigs to brighten the presentation.

Why this works: The acidity of lemon thickens the cream naturally, giving you a luscious dessert with no fuss.

3. Banana Ice Cream (aka “Nice Cream”)

Why You’ll Love It

If you want a creamy, frozen treat that’s vegan, healthful, and requires no added sugar or fancy equipment, this is the recipe for you. It’s perfect for hot days or whenever the ice cream craving strikes.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas (frozen, peeled, sliced)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons almond milk (or any plant milk)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional add-ins: cocoa powder, peanut butter, or berries

Directions

  1. Place frozen banana slices in a food processor or high-powered blender.
  2. Add vanilla, almond milk, and salt.
  3. Blend until smooth and creamy, pausing to scrape down sides.
  4. If desired, fold in add-ins or swirl peanut butter before serving.
  5. Serve immediately as soft-serve, or freeze for an hour to firm up.

Bonus: This dessert is naturally sweetened by the bananas and contains no refined sugars.

4. Chocolate Mousse with 5 Ingredients

Why You’ll Love It

Rich, airy, and indulgent, chocolate mousse feels fancy but requires minimal effort or ingredients. This version uses cream, chocolate, and a splash of espresso to deepen the flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon instant espresso powder (optional but recommended)

Directions

  1. Melt chocolate gently over a double boiler or in short bursts in the microwave.
  2. Whip heavy cream with sugar, vanilla, and espresso powder until soft peaks form.
  3. Fold about a quarter of the whipped cream into the melted chocolate to lighten it.
  4. Carefully fold in the remaining whipped cream until no streaks remain.
  5. Spoon mousse into serving dishes and refrigerate for at least an hour.

Serving idea: Top with shaved chocolate or a sprinkle of sea salt for a flavor contrast.

5. Apple Cinnamon Crumble

Why You’ll Love It

This quick apple crumble is the best kind of comfort food—warm, fragrant, and slightly crunchy on top, with tender, spiced apples underneath. The crumble topping uses pantry staples and cooks swiftly.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium apples (peeled, cored, and sliced)
  • ½ cup brown sugar
  • ½ cup rolled oats
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter (cold, cubed)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon

(This is a 6-ingredient recipe, but you can omit the cinnamon if strictly needing 5. It’s highly recommended, though.)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss apple slices with half the brown sugar and cinnamon, then spread evenly in a baking dish.
  3. Combine oats, flour, remaining sugar, and cold butter in a bowl.
  4. Rub butter into the oat mixture with your fingers until crumbs form.
  5. Sprinkle crumble topping evenly over apples.
  6. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until topping is golden and apples are bubbly.
  7. Cool slightly before digging in, ideally with vanilla ice cream.

Tips for Fabulous 5-Ingredient Desserts

  • Choose quality ingredients: When you’re working with fewer flavors, each ingredient matters more. Opt for fresh eggs, real vanilla extract, and good chocolate.
  • Use pantry staples: Many simple desserts rely on basics like flour, sugar, butter, and eggs—things you likely already have.
  • Prep ahead: Many 5-ingredient dessert recipes can be made ahead and stored in the fridge or freezer.
  • Play with flavor twists: If you want, add spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, or a splash of liqueur to punch up the flavor.
  • Keep it simple: Don’t overcomplicate. Sometimes the beauty of these desserts is in their subtle, uncomplicated deliciousness.

Final Thoughts

You don’t need a fully stocked kitchen or a big chunk of free time to treat yourself to a homemade dessert. These 5-ingredient recipes prove that you can satisfy your sweet tooth quickly and easily—with minimal prep and cleanup.

Whether it’s chewy peanut butter chocolate chip cookies, an elegant lemon posset, or a wholesome banana ice cream, these recipes will become staples for you. Give them a try today and enjoy the natural magic of simplicity in baking.
