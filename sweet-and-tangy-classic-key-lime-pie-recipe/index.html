Sweet and Tangy: Classic Key Lime Pie Recipe


Are you looking for a dessert that combines the perfect balance of sweetness and tanginess? Look no further than this Classic Key Lime Pie recipe. With its smooth and creamy texture, zesty lime flavor, and crumbly graham cracker crust, this pie is a crowd-pleaser that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 can (14 ounces) sweetened condensed milk
  • ½ cup fresh key lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon key lime zest
  • Whipped cream, for garnish (optional)
  • Lime slices, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Stir until the mixture resembles wet sand.

  3. Press the crumb mixture into the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie dish, making sure to create an even layer. Use the back of a spoon or the bottom of a measuring cup to firmly press the crust.

  4. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 8-10 minutes, or until it turns golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool completely.

  5. In a large bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and sweetened condensed milk until well combined and smooth.

  6. Gradually add the key lime juice and zest to the egg mixture, stirring continuously. The mixture will thicken slightly as you incorporate the lime juice.

  7. Pour the filling into the cooled graham cracker crust, spreading it evenly.

  8. Bake the pie in the preheated oven for 15-17 minutes, or until the filling is set but still slightly jiggly in the center. Avoid overbaking as it may cause the pie to crack.

  9. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool to room temperature. Then, refrigerate it for at least 2 hours, or until it is chilled and firm.

  10. Once chilled, garnish the pie with whipped cream and lime slices, if desired. Slice and serve!

Tips and Variations

  • For the best results, use fresh key lime juice rather than bottled lime juice. The fresh juice provides a more vibrant and authentic flavor.

  • If you can’t find key limes, regular limes can be used as a substitute. The taste may be slightly different, but the pie will still be delicious.

  • To make a traditional graham cracker crust, you can substitute the graham cracker crumbs with finely crushed graham crackers mixed with melted butter and sugar.

  • For an extra tangy twist, add a teaspoon of lime zest to the whipped cream before garnishing the pie.

  • This pie is best enjoyed chilled, straight from the refrigerator. The cool and creamy texture is the perfect way to end any meal.

Conclusion

Indulge in the delightful combination of sweet and tangy flavors with this Classic Key Lime Pie recipe. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply craving a homemade dessert, this pie is sure to impress. With its creamy filling and crumbly crust, it’s a dessert that will transport you to the sunny shores of the Florida Keys. So, grab your apron and get ready to create a dessert that will leave everyone wanting more. Enjoy!
