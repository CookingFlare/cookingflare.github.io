5 Quick and Easy Dinner Ideas for Busy Beginners


As a busy beginner, cooking a delicious and satisfying dinner can be a daunting task. You’re probably thinking, “I don’t have time to spend hours in the kitchen, and I’m not exactly a culinary expert.” Fear not! With these 5 quick and easy dinner ideas, you’ll be able to whip up a tasty meal in no time, even on the most hectic of days.

1. One-Pot Pasta with Tomato Sauce

Pasta is a classic comfort food that’s easy to make and always a crowd-pleaser. This one-pot wonder is perfect for busy beginners, as it requires minimal prep work and can be cooked in under 30 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pasta of your choice
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package directions.
  2. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until softened.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the pot and toss to coat with the tomato sauce.
  5. Serve hot, topped with Parmesan cheese if desired.

2. Tacos with Pre-Cooked Ground Beef and Toppings

Tacos are a great option for busy beginners, as they can be customized to suit your tastes and dietary preferences. This recipe uses pre-cooked ground beef, which can be cooked ahead of time and reheated as needed.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pre-cooked ground beef
  • 8-10 taco shells
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar or Monterey Jack work well)
  • Canned black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Sliced avocado
  • Sour cream
  • Salsa
  • Cilantro, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Warm the taco shells according to package directions.
  2. Reheat the ground beef in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Assemble the tacos by spooning the ground beef into the shells, followed by your desired toppings.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy!

3. Baked Chicken and Sweet Potato Fries

Baked chicken and sweet potato fries are a match made in heaven. This recipe is easy to make and requires minimal cleanup, making it a great option for busy beginners.

Ingredients:

  • 2-3 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: your favorite seasonings (e.g. paprika, garlic powder, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  3. Cut the sweet potatoes into fry shapes and place on the baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the sweet potatoes are crispy.
  6. Meanwhile, bake the chicken breasts for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.
  7. Serve the chicken with the sweet potato fries and your favorite toppings.

4. Quesadillas with Shredded Chicken and Cheese

Quesadillas are another great option for busy beginners, as they can be filled with a variety of ingredients and cooked in a pan. This recipe uses shredded chicken and cheese for a creamy and satisfying filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (cheddar or Monterey Jack work well)
  • 4 large tortillas
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: your favorite fillings (e.g. diced onions, bell peppers, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Place a tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle with shredded cheese and chicken.
  3. Fold the tortilla in half and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the tortilla is crispy.
  4. Flip the quesadilla and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the other side is crispy.
  5. Repeat with the remaining tortillas and filling ingredients.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

5. Omelette with Vegetables and Cheese

Omelettes are a great option for busy beginners, as they can be filled with a variety of ingredients and cooked in a pan. This recipe uses a simple filling of vegetables and cheese, but feel free to get creative and add your favorite ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 cup diced vegetables (e.g. bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, etc.)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Swiss work well)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: your favorite fillings (e.g. diced ham, chopped herbs, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and whisk until smooth.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium heat and add the butter.
  3. Pour the eggs into the skillet and cook until the edges start to set.
  4. Add the diced vegetables and shredded cheese to one half of the omelette.
  5. Fold the other half of the omelette over the filling and cook until the cheese is melted and the eggs are cooked through.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

There you have it, 5 quick and easy dinner ideas that are perfect for busy beginners. These recipes are easy to make, delicious, and can be customized to suit your tastes and dietary preferences. So next time you’re short on time and energy, don’t worry – these recipes have got you covered!
