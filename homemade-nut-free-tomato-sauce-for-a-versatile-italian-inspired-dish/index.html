Homemade Nut-Free Tomato Sauce for a Versatile Italian-Inspired Dish


If you’re a fan of Italian cuisine, you’ll know that a good tomato sauce is the heart and soul of many classic dishes. From pasta to pizza, a rich, flavorful tomato sauce can elevate any dish to new heights. However, for those with nut allergies, store-bought tomato sauces can be a cause for concern due to the potential cross-contamination with nuts. That’s where homemade nut-free tomato sauce comes in - it’s a safe and delicious alternative that you can customize to your taste. In this article, we’ll guide you through a simple and versatile recipe for nut-free tomato sauce that you can use in a variety of Italian-inspired dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to avoid burning.

  2. Add the Tomatoes: Once the aromatics are soft and fragrant, pour in the crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Stir to combine, and then add the sugar, dried oregano, and dried basil. If you like a little heat, this is the time to add a pinch of red pepper flakes for a subtle kick.

  3. Simmer and Season: Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Let the sauce simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This allows the flavors to meld together and the sauce to thicken. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Blend or Keep Chunky: At this point, you can choose to blend the sauce using an immersion blender for a smooth consistency, or keep it chunky for a rustic texture. The choice is yours!

Versatility

This nut-free tomato sauce is incredibly versatile and can be used in various Italian-inspired dishes. Here are just a few ideas to get you started:

  • Classic Spaghetti: Toss the sauce with al dente spaghetti for a simple and satisfying meal.

  • Homemade Pizza: Spread the sauce over pizza dough and top with your favorite ingredients for a delicious homemade pizza.

  • Eggplant Parmesan: Layer the sauce with breaded and fried eggplant slices, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese for a comforting dish.

  • Stuffed Bell Peppers: Use the sauce as a base for a flavorful filling for bell peppers, topped with cheese and baked to perfection.

  • Chicken Parmesan: Smother breaded and baked chicken cutlets with the sauce and cheese for a classic Italian-American dish.

Final Thoughts

Homemade nut-free tomato sauce is a kitchen staple that opens up a world of culinary possibilities for those with nut allergies. By making your own sauce, you can ensure that it’s safe for you and your loved ones to enjoy, while also savoring the fresh, vibrant flavors of ripe tomatoes and fragrant herbs. Experiment with this recipe, adjust the seasoning to your liking, and explore the endless ways you can incorporate this versatile sauce into your cooking repertoire. Whether you’re a pasta aficionado, a pizza enthusiast, or simply someone who appreciates a good homemade sauce, this nut-free tomato sauce is sure to become a beloved go-to in your kitchen. Buon appetito!
