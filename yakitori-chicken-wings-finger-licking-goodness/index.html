Yakitori Chicken Wings: Finger-Licking Goodness


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, then you’ve probably heard of Yakitori - a popular dish consisting of skewered grilled chicken. Today, we’re taking that delicious concept and giving it a finger-licking twist with Yakitori Chicken Wings. These wings are bursting with flavor, juicy on the inside, and perfectly charred on the outside. Get ready to indulge your taste buds in this irresistible recipe!

Ingredients

To make these delectable Yakitori Chicken Wings, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds of chicken wings
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the marinade. In a bowl, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sake, honey, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Whisk the ingredients together until well combined.

  2. Place the chicken wings in a large ziplock bag or a shallow dish. Pour the marinade over the wings, ensuring they are completely coated. Seal the bag or cover the dish and let the wings marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, but overnight is even better for maximum flavor.

  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If you’re using a charcoal grill, make sure the coals are hot and ready.

  4. Remove the wings from the marinade and pat them dry with paper towels. This step is important to ensure the wings get a nice char on the grill.

  5. Brush the wings with vegetable oil to prevent sticking and place them on the grill. Cook the wings for about 10-12 minutes per side, or until they are cooked through and nicely charred. Keep an eye on them to prevent burning.

  6. Once the wings are cooked, remove them from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.

  7. Serve the Yakitori Chicken Wings hot, garnished with finely chopped green onions. These wings are perfect as an appetizer or as part of a main course. Enjoy their finger-licking goodness!

Tips and Variations

  • If you don’t have a grill, you can also cook the wings in the oven. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and place the wings on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for about 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are cooked through and crispy.
  • Feel free to adjust the sweetness of the marinade to your taste. You can add more honey if you prefer a sweeter flavor or reduce the amount for a more savory profile.
  • For an extra kick of flavor, you can sprinkle some toasted sesame seeds over the wings before serving.
  • If you like your wings spicy, try adding a teaspoon of Sriracha or chili paste to the marinade.

Final Thoughts

Yakitori Chicken Wings are a delightful twist on the traditional Yakitori skewers, bringing a new level of flavor and tenderness to this classic Japanese dish. Whether you’re grilling them up for a backyard barbecue or enjoying them as a weeknight treat, these wings are sure to impress. So fire up your grill and get ready to savor the finger-licking goodness of Yakitori Chicken Wings!
