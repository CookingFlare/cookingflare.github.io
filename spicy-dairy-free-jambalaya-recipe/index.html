Spicy Dairy-Free Jambalaya Recipe


Jambalaya is a classic Louisiana dish that combines bold flavors and a delightful mix of ingredients. Traditionally made with meat, seafood, and rice, this dairy-free version of Jambalaya is perfect for those who are lactose intolerant or follow a dairy-free diet. Packed with spice and bursting with flavor, this Spicy Dairy-Free Jambalaya will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 bell peppers, diced (any color will do)
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs, diced
  • 1 pound smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 1 pound shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 green onions, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and bell peppers, and sauté until they begin to soften, about 5 minutes.

  2. Stir in the minced garlic, paprika, cayenne pepper, dried thyme, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper. Cook for an additional minute, allowing the spices to release their flavors.

  3. Add the diced chicken thighs and sliced smoked sausage to the pot. Cook until the chicken is no longer pink and the sausage is lightly browned, about 8-10 minutes.

  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes (with their liquid) and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  5. Stir in the rice, making sure it is evenly distributed. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed most of the liquid.

  6. Add the shrimp to the pot, stirring gently to combine. Cover and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through.

  7. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  8. Serve the Spicy Dairy-Free Jambalaya hot, garnished with sliced green onions.

Enjoy the Flavors of Louisiana!

This Spicy Dairy-Free Jambalaya recipe is a crowd-pleaser that will transport you straight to the heart of Louisiana. The combination of spices, succulent chicken, smoky sausage, and tender shrimp creates a harmonious blend of flavors that will leave you craving more. Serve it as a main course for a hearty dinner or as a delicious option for your next gathering. Pair it with a side of cornbread or a fresh salad for a complete meal.

Remember, this recipe is dairy-free, making it suitable for individuals with lactose intolerance or those following a dairy-free lifestyle. The absence of dairy does not compromise the rich flavors and textures that Jambalaya is known for.

So, don’t wait any longer! Gather the ingredients, follow the simple steps, and savor the bold and spicy flavors of this Spicy Dairy-Free Jambalaya recipe. Get ready to impress your family and friends with a taste of Louisiana right in your own kitchen. Enjoy!
