Whip Up a Batch of Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies


If you’re looking for a delicious and wholesome treat that’s gluten-free, look no further than these mouthwatering oatmeal raisin cookies. Packed with fiber, flavor, and a hint of cinnamon, these cookies are sure to satisfy your sweet tooth while still being kind to your dietary needs. Whether you’re gluten-intolerant or simply looking for a healthier alternative, these cookies are a perfect choice. So let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups of gluten-free rolled oats
  • 1 cup of gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon of salt
  • ½ cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • ½ cup of brown sugar
  • ½ cup of granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of raisins

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper. This will prevent the cookies from sticking and make clean-up a breeze.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free rolled oats, gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking soda, ground cinnamon, and salt. Make sure all the dry ingredients are well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the softened unsalted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar. You can use an electric mixer or simply a wooden spoon. Mix until the ingredients are well incorporated and the mixture is smooth.

  4. Add the egg and vanilla extract to the butter and sugar mixture. Mix until everything is well combined and you have a creamy consistency.

  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring well after each addition. Make sure everything is evenly incorporated.

  6. Fold in the raisins. You can use your hands or a spatula to gently mix them into the cookie dough. The raisins will add a burst of sweetness and chewiness to every bite.

  7. Using a tablespoon or a cookie scoop, drop rounded portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Make sure to space them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading during baking.

  8. Bake the cookies in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. The centers may still be slightly soft, but they will firm up as they cool.

  9. Remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  10. Once the cookies have cooled, store them in an airtight container to maintain their freshness. They will stay delicious for up to a week, if they last that long!

Enjoy Your Gluten-Free Delight!

Now that you have a batch of freshly baked gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies, it’s time to indulge in their irresistible taste. Whether you enjoy them with a glass of cold milk or as an on-the-go snack, these cookies are sure to bring a smile to your face. Share them with your friends and family, and let them experience the joy of gluten-free baking. So go ahead, whip up a batch of these delightful treats and savor every bite. Happy baking!
