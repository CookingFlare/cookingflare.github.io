Haunted House Gingerbread Cookies: A Spooky Sweet Recipe


If you’re looking for a fun and spooky treat to make this Halloween season, look no further than these Haunted House Gingerbread Cookies! Not only are they deliciously sweet, but they also make for a great activity to do with friends or family. So grab your apron and get ready to create a haunted masterpiece!

Ingredients

For the gingerbread cookies:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 egg

For the royal icing:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons meringue powder
  • 3-4 tablespoons water

For decorating:

  • Assorted candies (gummy worms, candy eyes, licorice, etc.)
  • Food coloring (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, ginger, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add in the molasses and egg, and mix until well combined.

  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms. Divide the dough in half, wrap each half in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  4. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.

  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out one portion of the dough to about 1/4 inch thickness. Use cookie cutters to cut out haunted house shapes and place them onto the prepared baking sheets. Repeat with the remaining dough.

  6. Bake the cookies for 10-12 minutes, or until lightly golden brown around the edges. Allow them to cool completely on wire racks before decorating.

  7. While the cookies are cooling, prepare the royal icing. In a medium bowl, whisk together the powdered sugar and meringue powder. Gradually add in the water, one tablespoon at a time, until the icing reaches a thick, but spreadable consistency. You can add more water if needed.

  8. Divide the royal icing into separate bowls and add food coloring if desired. Transfer the icing to piping bags or ziplock bags with a small corner snipped off.

  9. Once the cookies are cooled, it’s time to get creative and decorate your haunted houses! Use the royal icing to pipe outlines, windows, doors, and spooky designs onto the cookies. Add candies and other decorations to bring your haunted houses to life.

  10. Allow the icing to dry completely before serving or storing the cookies in an airtight container.

Tips and Tricks

  • If you don’t have meringue powder for the royal icing, you can substitute it with pasteurized egg whites. Just make sure to use caution when consuming raw egg whites.

  • Get creative with your decorating! Use licorice to create fences, gummy worms for creepy crawlies, and candy eyes to give your haunted houses a spooky stare.

  • If you’re short on time, you can skip the royal icing and simply dust the cookies with powdered sugar for a more rustic look.

  • These cookies make great Halloween party favors or treats for trick-or-treaters. Package them in clear cellophane bags tied with ribbon for an extra festive touch.

So there you have it, a recipe for Haunted House Gingerbread Cookies that will satisfy your sweet tooth and unleash your creativity. Whether you’re hosting a Halloween party or just want to enjoy some festive baking, these spooky treats are sure to be a hit. So gather your supplies, put on some Halloween music, and have a blast creating your very own haunted houses! Happy baking!
