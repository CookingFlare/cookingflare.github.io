Savoring Spanish Stuffed Peppers: Recipe Inside


If you’re a fan of vibrant flavors and a touch of Mediterranean flair, then you’re in for a treat with this recipe for Spanish Stuffed Peppers. Bursting with a delightful combination of spices, fresh ingredients, and a hearty filling, these peppers are a true culinary delight. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup of rice (white or brown)
  • 1 pound ground beef (or a vegetarian substitute)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/2 cup of black olives, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of raisins
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for cooking

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking dish with parchment paper.

  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes. Set them aside.

  3. In a large pot, bring water to a boil and cook the rice according to the package instructions. Once cooked, set it aside.

  4. In a skillet over medium heat, add a drizzle of olive oil and sauté the onion until it becomes translucent. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  5. Push the onion and garlic to one side of the skillet and add the ground beef (or vegetarian substitute) to the other side. Cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.

  6. Add the diced tomato, black olives, raisins, cumin, smoked paprika, dried oregano, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir everything together and let it simmer for about 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  7. Remove the skillet from heat and fold in the cooked rice. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly combined.

  8. Stuff each bell pepper with the filling mixture, ensuring they are packed tightly. Place the stuffed peppers in the prepared baking dish.

  9. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes or until the peppers are tender and slightly charred on the edges.

  10. Once cooked, remove the stuffed peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes. Serve them warm and savor the delightful flavors of this Spanish-inspired dish.

Final Thoughts

Spanish Stuffed Peppers are a crowd-pleasing dish that combines the freshness of bell peppers with a flavorful filling. The combination of spices, ground beef (or vegetarian substitute), and other ingredients creates a harmonious blend that will transport your taste buds to the sunny shores of Spain.

This dish is not only delicious but also versatile. You can serve it as a main course alongside a salad or as a side dish to complement your favorite Spanish-inspired meal. The options are endless!

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a taste of Spain, try making these Spanish Stuffed Peppers. They are sure to impress your family and friends with their vibrant colors and irresistible flavors. ¡Buen provecho!
