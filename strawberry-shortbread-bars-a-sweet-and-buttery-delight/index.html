Strawberry Shortbread Bars: A Sweet and Buttery Delight


Strawberry shortbread bars are a delightful treat that perfectly blends the sweetness of fresh strawberries with the buttery richness of shortbread. These bars are a wonderful addition to any gathering, from picnics to afternoon teas. The combination of a crumbly shortbread crust, a layer of luscious strawberry filling, and a crumbly streusel topping creates a mouthwatering treat that will have everyone coming back for more.

Ingredients

For the Shortbread Crust:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Strawberry Filling:

  • 3 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Streusel Topping:

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into cubes

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.

  2. To make the shortbread crust, combine the flour, sugar, and salt in a large bowl. Add the cold butter cubes and vanilla extract. Using a pastry cutter or your hands, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Press the mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan.

  3. Bake the crust in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until lightly golden. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool slightly.

  4. While the crust is cooling, prepare the strawberry filling. In a medium saucepan, combine the sliced strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and the strawberries soften, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.

  5. Spread the strawberry filling evenly over the baked shortbread crust.

  6. To make the streusel topping, combine the flour and sugar in a bowl. Add the cold butter cubes and, using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture forms coarse crumbs. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the strawberry filling.

  7. Return the pan to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the streusel is golden brown.

  8. Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and allow the bars to cool completely before slicing into squares.

Serving Suggestions

These delectable strawberry shortbread bars can be served on their own as a delightful dessert or paired with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for an extra indulgent treat. They are perfect for summer gatherings, potlucks, or simply as a sweet treat to enjoy with a cup of tea or coffee.

Storage

Store any leftover bars in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. These bars can also be frozen for up to 2 months. Simply thaw at room temperature before serving.

Final Thoughts

Strawberry shortbread bars are a wonderful way to celebrate the sweetness of summer strawberries. The buttery shortbread crust provides the perfect base for the vibrant and fruity strawberry filling, while the crumbly streusel topping adds a delightful crunch. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, these bars are a delightful and easy-to-make treat that is sure to impress. So, go ahead, grab some fresh strawberries, and treat yourself to these delightful strawberry shortbread bars.
