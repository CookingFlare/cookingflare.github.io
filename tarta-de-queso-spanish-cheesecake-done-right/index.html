Tarta de Queso: Spanish Cheesecake Done Right


If there’s one thing that Spain is known for, it’s their love for delicious and indulgent desserts. And when it comes to cheesecake, the Spanish certainly know how to do it right. Tarta de Queso, or Spanish cheesecake, is a creamy and velvety dessert that will leave you craving for more. In this blog post, I’ll share with you a foolproof recipe to create the perfect Tarta de Queso in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 500g cream cheese
  • 200g sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 200ml heavy cream
  • 30g all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan and set it aside.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream cheese and sugar. Beat them together until smooth and creamy. This may take a few minutes, so be patient.

  3. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Make sure each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next one.

  4. Gradually pour in the heavy cream while continuing to mix. The mixture should become even creamier and smoother.

  5. Sift the all-purpose flour into the batter and gently fold it in. Be careful not to overmix at this stage, as it could result in a dense cheesecake.

  6. Add the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt to enhance the flavors. Mix everything together until well combined.

  7. Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan, smoothing out the top with a spatula.

  8. Place the cake pan in the preheated oven and bake for about 50-60 minutes, or until the edges are set and golden brown. The center may still be slightly jiggly, but it will firm up as the cheesecake cools.

  9. Once baked, remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool completely in the pan. This will help it to set properly.

  10. Once the cheesecake has cooled, refrigerate it for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. This will allow the flavors to meld together and the texture to become even creamier.

  11. When ready to serve, carefully remove the cheesecake from the pan and transfer it to a serving plate. Slice and enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For a twist on the classic Tarta de Queso, you can add some lemon zest or a splash of lemon juice to the batter. This will give the cheesecake a refreshing citrus flavor.
  • Serve the cheesecake with a dollop of whipped cream and fresh berries for an extra touch of indulgence.
  • If you prefer a crust for your cheesecake, you can make a simple graham cracker crust and press it into the bottom of the cake pan before adding the batter.

Conclusion

Tarta de Queso is a dessert that is loved by many for its creamy texture and rich flavors. With this recipe, you can easily recreate the magic of Spanish cheesecake in your own kitchen. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply indulging in a sweet treat, Tarta de Queso is sure to impress. Give it a try and let your taste buds embark on a delightful journey to Spain. Buen provecho!
