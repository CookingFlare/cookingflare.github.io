Elegant Beef Tenderloin Recipe for a Luxurious Christmas Feast


Are you looking to impress your guests this Christmas with a truly elegant and luxurious dish? Look no further than this exquisite beef tenderloin recipe! Tender, succulent, and bursting with flavor, this dish is sure to be the star of your holiday feast.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef tenderloin, approximately 5 pounds
  • 4 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).

  2. In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, chopped thyme, salt, and pepper. Mix well to create a flavorful herb rub.

  3. Place the beef tenderloin on a cutting board and pat it dry with paper towels. This will help the herb rub adhere to the meat.

  4. Drizzle the olive oil over the beef tenderloin and rub it all over the surface.

  5. Sprinkle the herb rub evenly over the entire tenderloin, pressing it gently to ensure it sticks.

  6. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, sear the beef tenderloin on all sides until nicely browned. This will help seal in the juices and create a delicious crust.

  7. Transfer the seared beef tenderloin to a baking dish or roasting pan and place it in the preheated oven.

  8. Roast the beef tenderloin for approximately 25-30 minutes, or until it reaches your desired level of doneness. For a medium-rare tenderloin, aim for an internal temperature of 135°F (57°C).

  9. Once cooked to perfection, remove the beef tenderloin from the oven and let it rest for at least 15 minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, resulting in a tender and juicy roast.

  10. Slice the beef tenderloin into thick, elegant portions and serve it alongside your favorite sides. Whether it’s creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a tangy horseradish sauce, the possibilities are endless.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of elegance, consider wrapping the beef tenderloin in bacon before roasting. This adds a smoky and savory flavor to the dish.

  • If you prefer a different herb blend, feel free to experiment with your favorite herbs and spices. Thyme, rosemary, and garlic are classic choices, but you can also try incorporating flavors like Dijon mustard, crushed peppercorns, or even a hint of truffle oil.

  • To ensure even cooking, it’s important to let the beef tenderloin come to room temperature before roasting. This allows for more even heat distribution and reduces the risk of overcooking the outer layers while the center remains undercooked.

  • Leftover beef tenderloin can be used to make delicious sandwiches or added to salads for a gourmet touch.

Now that you have this exquisite beef tenderloin recipe in your repertoire, you’re ready to create a truly luxurious Christmas feast. Prepare to dazzle your guests with this elegant and flavorful dish that will make this holiday season truly unforgettable. Enjoy!
