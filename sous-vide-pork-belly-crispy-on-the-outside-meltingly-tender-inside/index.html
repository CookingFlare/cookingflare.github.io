Sous Vide Pork Belly: Crispy on the Outside, Meltingly Tender Inside


If you’re a fan of pork belly, then you know that achieving the perfect balance of crispy skin and tender meat can be quite a challenge. But fear not, because I have a foolproof method that will give you the most mouthwatering sous vide pork belly you’ve ever tasted.

The Magic of Sous Vide

Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves sealing the food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method ensures that the pork belly cooks evenly and retains its natural juices, resulting in a melt-in-your-mouth texture that is impossible to achieve with other cooking methods.

Preparing the Pork Belly

To start, you’ll need a piece of pork belly with the skin on. Make sure to score the skin with a sharp knife, creating a crisscross pattern. This will help the skin get nice and crispy during the cooking process.

Next, season the pork belly with salt, pepper, and any other spices or herbs you prefer. This will help to enhance the flavors of the meat.

The Sous Vide Bath

Now it’s time to cook the pork belly sous vide style. Fill a large pot or sous vide container with water and set the temperature to 165°F (74°C).

Place the seasoned pork belly in a vacuum-sealed bag and remove as much air as possible. This can be done using a vacuum sealer or simply by using the water displacement method.

Once the water has reached the desired temperature, carefully lower the bag into the water bath, making sure the pork belly is fully submerged.

Patience is Key

Now comes the hard part - waiting. Let the pork belly cook in the water bath for 12 to 24 hours. Yes, it’s a long time, but trust me, it’s worth it. The low and slow cooking process will result in pork belly that is incredibly tender and juicy.

Crisping Up the Skin

After the long sous vide bath, the pork belly will be perfectly cooked, but the skin won’t be crispy just yet. To achieve that coveted crispy skin, you’ll need to give it a little extra love.

Remove the pork belly from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels. Place it skin side up on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

Preheat your broiler to high and place the pork belly under it for about 10 minutes, or until the skin is blistered and crispy. Keep a close eye on it to prevent it from burning.

Serving and Enjoying

Once the skin is crispy, remove the pork belly from the broiler and let it rest for a few minutes. This will allow the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, ensuring maximum flavor.

Now it’s time to slice and serve your sous vide pork belly. The meat should be tender and juicy, with a crispy and crackling skin that will make your taste buds sing.

You can enjoy the pork belly on its own, or serve it with a side of creamy mashed potatoes and sautéed greens. The choice is yours!

Conclusion

Sous vide pork belly is a true delight for any meat lover. The combination of crispy skin and tender meat is a match made in culinary heaven. By using the sous vide method, you can achieve restaurant-quality results in the comfort of your own kitchen. So give it a try, and prepare to be blown away by the incredible flavors and textures of this melt-in-your-mouth dish. Happy cooking!
