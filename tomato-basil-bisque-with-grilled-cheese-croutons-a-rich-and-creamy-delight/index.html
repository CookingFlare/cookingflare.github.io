Tomato Basil Bisque with Grilled Cheese Croutons: A Rich and Creamy Delight


As the summer sun sets, the aroma of fresh tomatoes and basil wafts through the air, transporting us to a world of warmth and comfort. In this season of abundance, we’re celebrating the simplicity and elegance of a classic tomato basil bisque, elevated by the addition of crispy grilled cheese croutons. This creamy, rich, and utterly delightful soup is the perfect accompaniment to a cozy evening with loved ones.

The Art of Bisque-Making

Bisques are a staple of French cuisine, and for good reason. This velvety-smooth soup is born from the careful blending of flavorful ingredients, expertly balancing acidity, sweetness, and savory notes. To create the perfect tomato basil bisque, we’ll start by roasting a medley of heirloom tomatoes, their natural sweetness caramelizing to bring out the deep, rich flavors.

Roasting the Tomatoes

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Cut 2 lbs (1 kg) of heirloom tomatoes in half, removing their seeds and excess moisture. Place the tomato halves on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, drizzle with 2 tbsp of olive oil, and season with salt and pepper. Roast the tomatoes in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they’ve released their juices and developed a deep, caramelized color.

Creating the Bisque

Remove the roasted tomatoes from the oven and let them cool slightly. In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted tomatoes, 1/4 cup of freshly chopped basil, 2 cloves of garlic, 1/2 cup of heavy cream, and 1/2 cup of chicken or vegetable broth. Blend the mixture until smooth, stopping to scrape down the sides of the blender as needed.

Adding the Aromatics

In a large saucepan, heat 2 tbsp of butter over medium heat. Add 1 small onion, finely chopped, and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the blended tomato mixture to the saucepan, stirring to combine with the onion and butter. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Finishing Touches

Stir in 1 tsp of dried thyme and 1/2 tsp of paprika. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. Serve the bisque hot, garnished with additional basil leaves and a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.

Grilled Cheese Croutons: The Perfect Companion

While the bisque is simmering, let’s prepare the grilled cheese croutons that will add a satisfying crunch to our soup. Butter two slices of your favorite bread, then place them in a skillet over medium heat. Top each slice with a slice of cheese – we recommend a rich, creamy brie or a sharp cheddar – and cook until the bread is golden brown and the cheese is melted.

Assembling the Croutons

Once the croutons are cooked, let them cool slightly before crumbling them into bite-sized pieces. Add the crumbled croutons to the bisque, stirring gently to combine. The crispy, cheesy texture will add a delightful contrast to the smooth, creamy soup.

A Delicious and Elegant Soup

Tomato basil bisque with grilled cheese croutons is a culinary masterpiece that’s sure to impress even the most discerning palates. The combination of rich, roasted tomatoes, fresh basil, and creamy cheese is a match made in heaven, elevated by the addition of crispy, crunchy croutons. This soup is perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in with loved ones. So go ahead, indulge in the comfort and sophistication of this delightful soup, and let the warmth of the season fill your heart and home.
