Vegetarian Stir-Fry Recipes for College Cooking


Are you a college student looking for quick, easy, and healthy vegetarian recipes to whip up in your dorm room or apartment? Stir-fry recipes are the perfect solution! Not only are they simple to make, but they also offer a great way to get your daily dose of veggies and protein without breaking the bank. In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious vegetarian stir-fry recipes that are perfect for college cooking. Let’s get started!

Teriyaki Tofu Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 block extra-firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup snap peas
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the cubed tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides. Remove the tofu from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  4. Add the broccoli, bell pepper, carrot, and snap peas to the skillet. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  5. Return the tofu to the skillet and pour in the teriyaki sauce and soy sauce. Stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  6. Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles.

This teriyaki tofu stir-fry is not only packed with flavor but also provides a healthy dose of protein and essential nutrients.

Spicy Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1/2 cup baby corn
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce
  • Cooked quinoa for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced onion and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  3. Toss in the bell peppers, mushrooms, snow peas, and baby corn. Stir-fry for 5-6 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce and sriracha sauce. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and stir to combine.
  5. Serve the spicy vegetable stir-fry over cooked quinoa for a satisfying and nutritious meal.

This spicy vegetable stir-fry is a perfect way to add some heat to your college cooking repertoire.

Garlic Ginger Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • Cooked brown rice for serving

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a wok or skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the broccoli and red onion to the skillet and stir-fry for 4-5 minutes or until the broccoli is tender-crisp.
  3. Stir in the garlic and ginger, and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the vegetable broth and hoisin sauce. Pour the mixture over the broccoli and stir to coat.
  5. Serve the garlic ginger broccoli stir-fry over cooked brown rice for a simple and satisfying meal.

This garlic ginger broccoli stir-fry is a flavorful and nutritious option for busy college students.

Conclusion

Stir-fry recipes are a fantastic option for college students looking to prepare quick, healthy, and budget-friendly meals. With these easy vegetarian stir-fry recipes, you can satisfy your hunger and impress your friends with your cooking skills. Experiment with different combinations of vegetables and sauces to create your own custom stir-fry masterpieces. Happy cooking!
