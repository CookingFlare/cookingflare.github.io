Pasta with Arugula Pesto: A Fresh and Peppery Pasta Dish


Are you ready to elevate your pasta game with a vibrant and delicious twist? This Pasta with Arugula Pesto is not just any ordinary pasta dish; it’s a celebration of fresh flavors, peppery notes, and a touch of culinary creativity. In this blog post, we’ll take you through the journey of making this delightful dish, share helpful tips, and explore the health benefits of arugula, ensuring your dinner is both nourishing and satisfying.

The Allure of Arugula

Arugula, often referred to as rocket or rucola, is a leafy green that packs a flavorful punch. Known for its peppery taste, arugula has become a favorite in salads and garnishes. However, its potential extends far beyond the salad bowl. When blended into a pesto, arugula transforms into a creamy, zesty sauce that pairs beautifully with pasta.

Arugula is not just delicious; it’s also a powerhouse of nutrition. Rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as essential minerals like calcium and potassium, this leafy green provides numerous health benefits. It’s low in calories and high in antioxidants, making it an excellent choice for a wholesome meal.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this fresh and peppery Pasta with Arugula Pesto, gather the following ingredients:

For the Arugula Pesto

  • 4 cups fresh arugula leaves: Washed and dried
  • 1/2 cup pine nuts: Toasted for enhanced flavor
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese: For that rich, savory taste
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil: To create a velvety texture
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice: For brightness

For the Pasta

  • 12 ounces pasta of your choice: Spaghetti, penne, or fusilli works great
  • Salt: For the pasta water
  • Fresh arugula: For garnish
  • Extra Parmesan cheese: For serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Arugula Pesto

Start by making your fresh arugula pesto. In a food processor, combine the arugula leaves, toasted pine nuts, minced garlic, and grated Parmesan cheese. Pulse the ingredients together until they form a coarse mixture.

As you process the ingredients, slowly drizzle in the extra virgin olive oil. This will help to emulsify the pesto, creating a smooth and creamy sauce. Depending on your desired consistency, you can adjust the amount of olive oil. Once blended, add salt, pepper, and lemon juice to taste. Give it one last pulse to mix everything well. Set the pesto aside while you cook the pasta.

2. Cook the Pasta

In a large pot, bring salted water to a boil. Add your chosen pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Once cooked, reserve about a cup of the pasta water and then drain the pasta.

3. Combine Pasta and Pesto

In a large mixing bowl or pot, combine the drained pasta with the arugula pesto. Toss the pasta gently to ensure it is evenly coated with the sauce. If the pesto is too thick, add a little of the reserved pasta water to achieve your desired consistency. This step is crucial as the starchy water helps the sauce cling to the pasta beautifully.

4. Serve and Garnish

To serve, divide the pasta among plates or bowls. Top with fresh arugula and a sprinkle of extra Parmesan cheese. For an added touch, you can drizzle a bit of olive oil over the top. Serve immediately and enjoy the fresh, peppery flavors of your pasta dish!

Tips for the Perfect Pasta with Arugula Pesto

  • Use fresh ingredients: The quality of your ingredients significantly impacts the flavor of your dish. Opt for fresh arugula, high-quality olive oil, and freshly grated Parmesan cheese whenever possible.

  • Toast the pine nuts: Toasting the pine nuts enhances their flavor and adds a delightful crunch to the pesto. Simply place them in a dry skillet over medium heat, stirring frequently until golden brown.

  • Adjust the flavors: Taste your pesto and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you prefer a stronger garlic flavor, add an extra clove. For a nuttier taste, increase the amount of pine nuts.

  • Choose the right pasta: While this recipe works well with any pasta, consider using whole wheat or gluten-free pasta for a healthier option.

  • Make it vegan: To create a vegan version of this dish, substitute the Parmesan cheese with nutritional yeast and use a plant-based alternative to olive oil.

Health Benefits of Arugula

Incorporating arugula into your diet provides numerous health benefits:

  • Rich in Nutrients: Arugula is a nutrient-dense leafy green, providing vitamins and minerals essential for overall health.

  • Low in Calories: Perfect for those looking to maintain or lose weight, arugula is low in calories yet high in flavor.

  • Antioxidant Properties: Arugula contains antioxidants that help combat oxidative stress in the body.

  • Supports Bone Health: The high vitamin K content in arugula is crucial for maintaining strong bones.

  • Aids Digestion: The fiber content in arugula promotes healthy digestion and can help prevent constipation.

Variations to Try

Once you’ve mastered the basic Pasta with Arugula Pesto recipe, feel free to experiment with variations. Here are a few ideas to inspire your culinary creativity:

  • Add Protein: Incorporate grilled chicken, shrimp, or chickpeas for added protein to make your meal more filling.

  • Mix in Vegetables: Roasted cherry tomatoes, sautéed zucchini, or spinach can add color and extra nutrients to your dish.

  • Spice it Up: For a kick of heat, add red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce to your pesto.

  • Cheese Alternatives: Try using feta or goat cheese instead of Parmesan for a different flavor profile.

  • Nuts and Seeds: Swap out pine nuts for walnuts, almonds, or pumpkin seeds for a unique twist on the traditional pesto.

Conclusion

Pasta with Arugula Pesto is a simple yet sophisticated dish that showcases the fresh and peppery flavors of arugula. Whether you’re preparing a quick weeknight dinner or impressing guests with a gourmet meal, this pasta dish is sure to delight. With its vibrant colors, rich flavors, and numerous health benefits, it’s a recipe you’ll want to make again and again.

So grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and dive into the world of arugula pesto pasta. Your taste buds will thank you!
