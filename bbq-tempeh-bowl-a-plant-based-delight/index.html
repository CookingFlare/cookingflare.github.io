BBQ Tempeh Bowl: A Plant-Based Delight


Tempeh is quickly becoming one of the most popular plant-based proteins for its nutty flavor, dense texture, and incredible versatility. Whether you’re vegan, vegetarian, or simply looking to add more plant-based meals to your weekly menu, tempeh is an excellent choice. Today, we’re diving into one of the most delicious ways to enjoy tempeh—BBQ Tempeh Bowl. This recipe is a perfect combination of smoky, savory tempeh, fresh vegetables, and a tangy BBQ sauce, all served in one hearty bowl. Whether you’re preparing this dish for lunch, dinner, or even meal prep for the week, it’s a nutritious, satisfying meal that the whole family will love.

What Is Tempeh?

Before we jump into the recipe, let’s take a moment to understand tempeh and why it’s such a great choice for a plant-based meal. Tempeh is a traditional Indonesian food made by fermenting cooked soybeans and then pressing them into a firm, dense cake. The fermentation process adds a unique, earthy flavor and helps break down the beans, making tempeh easier to digest than other soy-based products like tofu. It’s also a powerhouse of protein, fiber, and a wide array of essential vitamins and minerals. Unlike tofu, which has a softer, spongier texture, tempeh holds up well to grilling, baking, and stir-frying, making it perfect for dishes like BBQ Tempeh Bowl.

BBQ Tempeh Bowl Recipe

This BBQ tempeh bowl is everything you could want in a plant-based meal: savory, smoky, satisfying, and loaded with fresh vegetables and wholesome grains. It’s the kind of meal that will make you forget you’re eating plant-based. Perfect for those who are new to tempeh or seasoned plant-based eaters alike, this recipe is both easy to prepare and packed with flavor.

Ingredients for BBQ Tempeh:

  • 1 block (8 oz) tempeh, cut into ½-inch slices or cubes
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or other vegetable oil)
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or agave syrup)
  • 1 cup BBQ sauce (use your favorite kind, preferably one with a smoky flavor)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Ingredients for the Bowl:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa or brown rice (use your preferred whole grain)
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes (peeled and diced)
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli florets
  • ½ cup sliced avocado
  • ¼ cup pickled red onions (optional, for a tangy kick)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • A squeeze of lime (for added freshness)

Instructions for BBQ Tempeh:

  1. Prep the Tempeh: Start by cutting the tempeh into ½-inch slices or cubes. If you prefer a more crispy texture, you can steam the tempeh for 10 minutes before marinating it, which helps to remove any bitter taste. However, this step is optional.

  2. Marinate the Tempeh: In a bowl, combine the olive oil, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, soy sauce, and maple syrup. Whisk everything together to create a marinade. Add the tempeh pieces to the bowl, tossing them gently to coat each piece evenly. Cover the bowl with a lid or plastic wrap, and let the tempeh marinate for at least 30 minutes. If you have time, you can marinate it for up to 2 hours to intensify the flavor.

  3. Cook the Tempeh: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add the marinated tempeh and cook for 4–5 minutes on each side, or until the tempeh becomes golden brown and crispy. Be sure to avoid crowding the pan—cook in batches if needed. Once cooked, pour the BBQ sauce over the tempeh and toss gently to coat the pieces with the sauce. Let it simmer for another 2–3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Preparing the Bowl:

  1. Cook the Grains: While the tempeh is cooking, prepare your choice of whole grain. Quinoa or brown rice works wonderfully in this recipe. Cook the grain according to the package instructions, making sure it’s fluffy and fully cooked.

  2. Roast the Sweet Potatoes: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the diced sweet potatoes with a little olive oil, salt, and pepper, and spread them out in a single layer on a baking sheet. Roast the sweet potatoes for 25–30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are tender and slightly caramelized at the edges.

  3. Steam the Broccoli: While the sweet potatoes are roasting, steam the broccoli florets. You can do this easily by placing them in a steaming basket over boiling water or using a microwave-safe bowl with a lid. Steam the broccoli for about 5 minutes until it turns bright green and is tender but still crisp.

  4. Assemble the Bowl: To assemble your BBQ tempeh bowl, start by placing a scoop of cooked quinoa or brown rice as the base. Add the roasted sweet potatoes, steamed broccoli, and a few slices of avocado for creaminess. Then, generously top everything with the BBQ tempeh, ensuring each bite has plenty of the flavorful tempeh pieces. For an extra tangy touch, add some pickled red onions on top. Finish with a sprinkle of fresh cilantro and a squeeze of lime juice for added brightness.

Tips and Variations:

  • BBQ Sauce: The choice of BBQ sauce can make a big difference in flavor. Opt for a smoky, tangy sauce for the best balance with the tempeh. If you prefer a spicy kick, you can choose a spicy BBQ sauce or add chili flakes to the tempeh marinade.

  • Grilled Tempeh: If you prefer grilling your tempeh, you can preheat your grill to medium-high heat and cook the tempeh for 4–5 minutes on each side, brushing it with BBQ sauce during the grilling process.

  • Add More Vegetables: The beauty of this BBQ tempeh bowl is its versatility. Feel free to add other veggies like sautéed spinach, roasted Brussels sprouts, or shredded carrots. You can also incorporate a few greens like kale or arugula for added nutrition.

  • Nuts and Seeds: For some added crunch and texture, top your bowl with a sprinkle of toasted sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, or chopped almonds.

  • Make It Spicy: If you like your BBQ tempeh bowl with a bit of heat, drizzle some hot sauce or sprinkle red pepper flakes on top. You can also marinate the tempeh in a spicy BBQ sauce for a more intense flavor.

  • Meal Prep: This BBQ tempeh bowl is an excellent choice for meal prep. The tempeh, grains, and roasted veggies keep well in the fridge for up to 3–4 days. Simply reheat everything when you’re ready to eat, and enjoy a quick, satisfying meal.

Why You’ll Love This BBQ Tempeh Bowl:

1. Rich in Protein

Tempeh is an excellent source of plant-based protein. Just one 4-ounce serving contains around 21 grams of protein, making it an ideal protein source for anyone following a plant-based diet or looking to reduce their meat consumption.

2. Packed with Nutrients

This BBQ tempeh bowl is loaded with fiber, vitamins, and minerals. From the quinoa or brown rice to the roasted sweet potatoes and steamed broccoli, you’re getting a variety of nutrient-dense ingredients that support overall health. The avocado adds healthy fats, while the fresh cilantro provides antioxidants.

3. Customizable

One of the best things about this BBQ tempeh bowl is its flexibility. Whether you’re in the mood for different grains, additional veggies, or a spicy twist, you can easily adjust the recipe to suit your taste preferences or dietary needs.

4. Easy to Make

While this bowl looks impressive and tastes like it took hours to prepare, it’s actually quite simple to put together. The prep time is minimal, and the ingredients are easy to find at most grocery stores.

5. Great for Meal Prep

This dish stores well in the fridge and is perfect for meal prep. The components can be made in bulk, so you have a few days’ worth of satisfying lunches or dinners ready to go. Simply assemble the bowls when you’re ready to eat.

Conclusion

This BBQ Tempeh Bowl is a perfect example of how delicious, satisfying, and nutritious plant-based meals can be. The smoky, tangy BBQ tempeh pairs wonderfully with the sweetness of roasted sweet potatoes and the earthiness of quinoa or rice. Add in some fresh vegetables and a sprinkle of lime for brightness, and you’ve got yourself a meal that’s as good for your body as it is for your taste buds.

Whether you’re a lifelong vegetarian, a recent convert to plant-based eating, or just looking to try something new, this BBQ tempeh bowl will quickly become a staple in your meal rotation. It’s an easy, customizable dish that combines bold flavors, healthy ingredients, and plenty of protein in one vibrant bowl. So next time you’re craving a hearty, satisfying, and wholesome meal, give this BBQ Tempeh Bowl a try—you won’t be disappointed!
