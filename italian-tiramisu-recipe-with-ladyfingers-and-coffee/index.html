Italian Tiramisu Recipe with Ladyfingers and Coffee


Ah, the classic Italian dessert that has captivated taste buds around the world – Tiramisu. This decadent treat, with its layers of espresso-soaked ladyfingers and rich, velvety mascarpone cream, is a true culinary masterpiece. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, mastering the art of Tiramisu is a journey worth embarking on.

Originating in the northern Italian region of Veneto, Tiramisu has become a beloved staple in Italian cuisine. The name itself, “tiramisu,” translates to “pick me up” or “lift me up,” alluding to the energizing combination of coffee and cocoa that gives this dessert its signature flavor profile.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll delve into the intricacies of creating a truly authentic Italian Tiramisu, complete with the perfect balance of ladyfingers, coffee, and the creamy mascarpone filling. Get ready to indulge in a dessert that will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of Italy.

Gathering the Ingredients

To begin your Tiramisu adventure, you’ll need a few key ingredients:

  • Ladyfingers (also known as savoiardi biscuits)
  • Freshly brewed espresso or strong coffee, cooled
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Granulated sugar
  • Egg yolks
  • Heavy cream
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder

These simple yet essential components come together to create the layered masterpiece that is Tiramisu.

Preparing the Ladyfingers

The foundation of Tiramisu lies in the ladyfingers, also known as savoiardi biscuits. These delicate, spongy cookies are the perfect vehicle for soaking up the rich espresso or coffee that will infuse the dessert with its signature flavor.

Begin by quickly dipping the ladyfingers, one by one, into the cooled espresso or coffee. Be sure not to soak them for too long, as you want them to retain their structure and not become completely saturated. Arrange the soaked ladyfingers in a single layer at the bottom of a baking dish or serving container.

Creating the Creamy Mascarpone Filling

The heart of Tiramisu lies in the luxurious mascarpone cream. To prepare this decadent filling, start by separating the egg yolks from the whites. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks and granulated sugar together until the mixture becomes light and fluffy.

Next, fold in the mascarpone cheese, gently incorporating it until the mixture is smooth and creamy. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture, creating a light and airy texture.

Assembling the Tiramisu

Now that you have your soaked ladyfingers and the creamy mascarpone filling, it’s time to assemble the Tiramisu. Begin by creating a single layer of the coffee-soaked ladyfingers in the bottom of your baking dish or serving container.

Spoon a generous amount of the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers, smoothing it out evenly. Repeat this process, creating alternating layers of ladyfingers and mascarpone cream, until you reach the top of the dish.

For the final touch, dust the top layer of the Tiramisu with a generous sprinkle of unsweetened cocoa powder. This not only adds a beautiful visual contrast but also enhances the overall flavor profile of the dessert.

Chilling and Serving

Once your Tiramisu is assembled, cover the dish and refrigerate it for at least 6 hours, or ideally overnight. This allows the flavors to meld and the ladyfingers to fully absorb the coffee and cream.

When ready to serve, remove the Tiramisu from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for about 15-20 minutes. This will help the flavors and textures come alive. Using a sharp knife, cut the Tiramisu into individual portions and serve chilled.

Enjoy the rich, velvety texture and the perfect balance of coffee, cocoa, and creamy mascarpone in every bite. Buon appetito!
