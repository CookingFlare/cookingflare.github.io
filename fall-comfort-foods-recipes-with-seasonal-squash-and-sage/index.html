Fall Comfort Foods: Recipes with Seasonal Squash and Sage


Fall is a season that brings with it a sense of warmth and coziness. As the temperatures start to drop, our cravings for hearty and comforting dishes begin to increase. This is the perfect time to make use of the abundance of seasonal produce, particularly squash and sage. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious and soul-soothing recipes that incorporate these autumnal ingredients.

1. Butternut Squash Soup with Sage Croutons

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 45 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash, peeled, deseeded, and cubed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices of bread, cut into cubes
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Fresh sage leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, ground sage, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir well to coat the squash with the aromatic flavors.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, until the squash is soft and tender.
  4. While the soup is simmering, prepare the sage croutons. In a separate pan, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the bread cubes and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. Once the soup is done simmering, use an immersion blender or transfer the mixture to a blender to puree until smooth and velvety.
  6. Serve the butternut squash soup hot, garnished with a few sage leaves and a handful of sage croutons on top.

2. Roasted Acorn Squash with Sage Brown Butter

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 45 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 acorn squashes, halved and deseeded
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 8 fresh sage leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the acorn squash halves on a baking sheet, cut side up. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roast the squash in the preheated oven for about 30-40 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and easily pierced with a fork.
  4. While the squash is roasting, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the fresh sage leaves and cook until the butter turns golden brown and fragrant.
  5. Once the acorn squash is fully roasted, remove from the oven and drizzle the sage brown butter over each half.
  6. Serve the roasted acorn squash as a side dish or enjoy it as a light meal on its own.

3. Sage and Butternut Squash Risotto

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 40 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, peeled, deseeded, and cubed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 6 fresh sage leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become translucent.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash and chopped sage leaves to the pan. Cook for about 5 minutes, until the squash starts to soften.
  3. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for another 2 minutes, ensuring that each grain is coated with the fragrant mixture.
  4. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer until it has been absorbed by the rice.
  5. Gradually add the vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly until each ladleful has been absorbed. Continue this process until the rice is cooked al dente.
  6. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the sage and butternut squash risotto hot, garnished with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan and a few sage leaves on top.

Conclusion

Embrace the flavors of fall by indulging in these comforting recipes that incorporate seasonal squash and aromatic sage. Whether you are craving a soul-warming soup, a roasted side dish, or a creamy risotto, these dishes will surely satisfy your autumn food cravings. So gather your ingredients, get into the kitchen, and enjoy the cozy vibes of fall with these delicious fall comfort foods.
