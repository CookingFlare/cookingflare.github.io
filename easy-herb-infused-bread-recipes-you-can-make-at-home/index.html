Easy Herb-Infused Bread Recipes You Can Make at Home


Bread-making can seem like a daunting task, especially when you think about the time and effort involved. However, making bread at home doesn’t have to be complex or time-consuming. One of the best ways to elevate a simple loaf is by adding herbs, which not only add flavor but also bring a beautiful aroma to your kitchen. Herb-infused bread is perfect for any occasion, whether you’re looking for a special addition to a dinner party or just want to enjoy a homemade loaf with a touch of nature’s flavors.

In this post, we’ll explore some easy herb-infused bread recipes that anyone can make at home. These recipes are designed to bring you the flavors of fresh herbs, giving your bread a unique twist. From simple garlic herb bread to savory rosemary and thyme loaves, you’ll be able to experiment with different herbs to create a variety of delicious breads.

The Basics of Herb-Infused Bread

Before we dive into specific recipes, let’s talk about the basics of herb-infused bread. Infusing bread with herbs adds complexity and layers of flavor, turning a simple loaf into a gourmet treat. Fresh herbs, dried herbs, and even herb oils can be used to infuse bread dough, and each method offers a slightly different result.

Fresh vs. Dried Herbs

  • Fresh herbs bring a bright, vibrant flavor and can add a refreshing taste to your bread. However, fresh herbs can lose some of their potency when baked, so it’s important to use them in higher quantities than dried herbs.
  • Dried herbs are more concentrated in flavor and can provide a more subtle, background flavor. Dried herbs are often more intense, so a little goes a long way.
  • Herb oils are another fantastic option. Olive oil infused with garlic, rosemary, or basil can provide a strong herbal presence throughout the dough.

When incorporating herbs into bread, it’s crucial to ensure they are evenly distributed throughout the dough to guarantee a consistent flavor profile. Now, let’s explore some easy herb-infused bread recipes that will make your kitchen smell incredible and taste even better.

Garlic and Herb Bread

This garlic and herb bread is perfect for any garlic lover. Infused with fresh garlic, rosemary, and thyme, this loaf will become a staple in your bread-making repertoire. The garlic adds a savory depth of flavor, while the rosemary and thyme complement the earthiness of the garlic.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted (for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the garlic, rosemary, and thyme.
  3. Pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir to form a dough.
  4. Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes on a lightly floured surface, until it’s smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a kitchen towel, and let it rise for 1-1.5 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch the dough down and shape it into a loaf.
  7. Place the dough in a greased loaf pan and let it rise for an additional 30 minutes.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
  9. Brush the warm bread with melted butter and let it cool slightly before slicing.

This garlic and herb bread pairs wonderfully with pasta dishes, soups, or as a snack on its own with a little bit of olive oil for dipping.

Rosemary and Olive Oil Bread

Rosemary is one of the most popular herbs to use in bread making. It has a fragrant, pine-like flavor that works beautifully with the richness of olive oil. This rosemary-infused bread is easy to make and perfect for serving alongside a cheese platter, grilled meats, or a fresh salad.

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/4 cup olive oil, plus extra for brushing
  • 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes to activate the yeast.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt together. Add the rosemary and olive oil.
  3. Pour in the activated yeast mixture and stir until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 10 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for about an hour, or until doubled in size.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch down the dough and shape it into a round loaf or place it in a greased baking dish.
  7. Allow the dough to rise for another 20-30 minutes.
  8. Brush the top of the loaf with a little olive oil, then bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the bread sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.
  9. Let the bread cool before slicing and serving.

This rosemary and olive oil bread is ideal for serving as an appetizer or as a side to your main meal.

Sage and Cheddar Bread

If you enjoy the flavor combination of sage and cheese, this bread recipe is for you. The sage adds a savory, slightly peppery flavor, while the sharp cheddar cheese provides richness and a tangy bite. Together, they create a wonderfully indulgent loaf that is perfect for serving at dinner parties or enjoying with a hearty soup.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the warm milk, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the sage and mix well.
  3. Pour the yeast mixture and melted butter into the flour mixture. Stir to form a dough.
  4. Knead the dough for about 8 minutes until smooth. Add the shredded cheese during the last few minutes of kneading.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  6. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch the dough down and shape it into a loaf.
  7. Place the dough in a greased loaf pan and let it rise for an additional 20 minutes.
  8. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.
  9. Let it cool before slicing and serving.

This sage and cheddar bread is perfect for pairing with a rich soup or served with a slice of turkey or ham.

Thyme and Sea Salt Focaccia

Focaccia is an Italian flatbread that’s known for its light, airy texture and rich flavor. Infusing this classic bread with thyme and a sprinkle of sea salt elevates its taste, creating a savory and aromatic loaf. Focaccia is versatile and can be served as an appetizer, side dish, or even used as a sandwich base.

Ingredients:

  • 3 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
  • Coarse sea salt, for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in the warm water. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Stir until a dough forms, then knead for about 8-10 minutes on a lightly floured surface.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Punch down the dough and transfer it to a greased baking sheet.
  6. Press the dough out into a rectangular shape, then drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with thyme leaves.
  7. Use your fingers to create dimples all over the surface of the dough. Sprinkle with coarse sea salt.
  8. Let the dough rise for another 20 minutes before baking for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Let it cool slightly before slicing and serving.

This thyme and sea salt focaccia is excellent when served with a fresh salad or used as a base for a Mediterranean sandwich.

Conclusion

Herb-infused breads are an easy way to transform a simple loaf into something extraordinary. Whether you choose to add garlic, rosemary, thyme, or any other herb, the result is a delicious and fragrant bread that enhances any meal. These recipes are not only simple but also flexible—feel free to experiment with different herbs and flavors to create your perfect loaf.

Homemade herb-infused bread also makes for a thoughtful gift, a perfect accompaniment to meals, or an indulgent snack on its own. Next time you’re in the mood for baking, consider trying one of these easy herb-infused bread recipes to fill your kitchen with the enticing smells of freshly baked bread and aromatic herbs. Happy baking!
