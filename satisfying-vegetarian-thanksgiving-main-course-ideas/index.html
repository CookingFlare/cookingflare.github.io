Satisfying Vegetarian Thanksgiving Main Course Ideas


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and, of course, delicious food. For those who choose a vegetarian lifestyle, finding the perfect main course for this festive occasion can be a delightful challenge. Whether you’re a vegetarian yourself or hosting vegetarian guests, these satisfying and flavorful main course ideas are sure to impress everyone at the Thanksgiving table.

Stuffed Acorn Squash

Ingredients:

  • 4 acorn squash, halved and seeds removed
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1/2 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the squash halves cut-side down on a baking sheet and roast for 20 minutes.
  3. In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil and add the quinoa. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, until the quinoa is fluffy and the liquid is absorbed.
  4. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  5. Stir in the cooked quinoa, thyme, sage, cranberries, and pecans. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Flip the partially cooked squash halves over and stuff each with the quinoa mixture.
  7. Return the stuffed squash to the oven and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the squash is tender.

Lentil and Mushroom Loaf

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked lentils
  • 2 cups mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast for a vegan option)
  • 2 eggs (or flax eggs for a vegan option)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the mushrooms, onion, and garlic until the moisture has evaporated and the mixture is golden brown.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils, sautéed mushroom mixture, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, eggs, soy sauce, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Transfer the mixture to the prepared loaf pan and press it down evenly.
  5. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the loaf is firm and golden brown on the outside.

Butternut Squash Risotto

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast for a vegan option)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the vegetable broth to a simmer and keep it warm over low heat.
  2. In a separate large, deep skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  3. Add the diced butternut squash to the skillet and cook until slightly caramelized.
  4. Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing the rice to toast slightly.
  5. Pour in the white wine and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is absorbed.
  6. Begin adding the warm vegetable broth to the skillet, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly and allowing each addition to be absorbed before adding more. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and al dente.
  7. Stir in the Parmesan cheese (or nutritional yeast) and season with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve the butternut squash risotto garnished with fresh parsley.

Conclusion

These vegetarian Thanksgiving main course ideas are not only satisfying and flavorful but also showcase the variety and creativity that can be achieved with plant-based ingredients. Whether you’re hosting a vegetarian Thanksgiving or simply looking to incorporate more meatless options into your holiday feast, these recipes are sure to delight and impress. Embrace the spirit of Thanksgiving with these delicious vegetarian main courses that will have everyone coming back for seconds!
