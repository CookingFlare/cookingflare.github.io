How to Can Your Own Chili: A Hearty Recipe


Chili is one of those dishes that not only fills you up, but also brings warmth and comfort to the soul. Whether it’s a crisp fall evening or a hearty meal during winter, chili is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed by everyone. It’s also perfect for making ahead and storing for later. Canning your own chili ensures you have a hearty, homemade meal ready whenever you need it, without the preservatives or additives often found in store-bought versions.

Canning chili might seem like a daunting task, but with the right ingredients, equipment, and steps, you’ll be able to enjoy delicious homemade chili all year long. In this post, we will guide you through the process of canning your own chili, ensuring it stays fresh and flavorful for months to come.

Why Can Your Own Chili?

Canning your own chili offers several benefits:

  1. Control Over Ingredients: You can use fresh, high-quality ingredients and avoid unwanted preservatives, artificial flavorings, or excessive sodium.
  2. Long Shelf Life: Canned chili can last for up to a year or more when stored properly, providing a hearty meal at your fingertips for months to come.
  3. Cost-Effective: Making chili in bulk and canning it can save you money compared to buying canned chili from the store.
  4. Customization: You can adjust the flavors to your personal taste, whether you like it spicy, mild, or packed with different vegetables.

What You’ll Need to Get Started

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients and equipment.

Ingredients:

  • Ground beef (or ground turkey for a leaner version): 2 pounds
  • Beans (kidney beans, black beans, or pinto beans): 2 cans (15 oz each) or 2 cups dried beans (soaked and cooked)
  • Tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Diced tomatoes (canned or fresh): 4 cups
  • Chopped onion: 1 medium onion
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Green bell pepper: 1, chopped
  • Chili powder: 2 tablespoons
  • Cumin: 1 tablespoon
  • Oregano: 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt and pepper: to taste
  • Beef broth or water: 2 cups
  • Olive oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Vinegar (for canning): 1 tablespoon (optional, for acidifying the chili)

Equipment:

  • Canning jars (quart-sized or pint-sized)
  • Lids and bands for jars
  • Pressure canner: This is crucial, as chili is a low-acid food and needs to be processed at high temperatures.
  • Large pot or Dutch oven
  • Funnel (to avoid spills when filling the jars)
  • Ladles (for transferring chili into the jars)
  • Jar lifter (to handle hot jars)
  • Towels or oven mitts (for safety)

Step-by-Step Guide to Canning Chili

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Start by gathering all of your ingredients. If you’re using dried beans, be sure to soak them overnight and cook them before you begin the canning process. This step is essential because uncooked beans can make the chili difficult to preserve.

Step 2: Cook the Chili

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef (or ground turkey) and cook until browned. Stir occasionally to break up the meat and ensure it cooks evenly.

Once the meat is browned, add the chopped onions, garlic, and bell pepper. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender and fragrant.

Add the chili powder, cumin, oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir well to coat the meat and vegetables with the spices, allowing the flavors to meld together for another 2-3 minutes.

Next, add the tomato paste and diced tomatoes (including any juices) to the pot. If you like a thicker chili, you can add less liquid at this point. Pour in the beef broth (or water) to loosen the mixture. Add the cooked beans, stir everything together, and let the chili simmer on low heat for at least 45 minutes to allow the flavors to develop. Stir occasionally to prevent the chili from sticking to the bottom of the pot.

Taste the chili and adjust the seasoning as needed. If you like it spicier, add a dash of cayenne pepper or hot sauce. If you prefer a milder flavor, cut back on the chili powder or other spices.

Step 3: Sterilize Your Jars and Lids

While the chili is simmering, it’s time to sterilize your canning jars. Wash the jars, lids, and bands in hot, soapy water. Rinse them well and place them in a large pot of water. Bring the water to a boil and let the jars, lids, and bands sit in the boiling water for 10 minutes to sterilize them. Use tongs or jar lifters to remove them from the water and set them aside on a clean towel to dry.

Step 4: Fill the Jars

Once the chili has finished cooking, remove it from the heat. Using a ladle and funnel, carefully fill the sterilized jars with the chili, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top. It’s important to leave this space to allow for expansion during the canning process.

If you want to ensure your chili stays safe and properly preserved, it’s a good idea to add a tablespoon of vinegar to each jar. This adds a bit of acidity, which can help preserve the chili and prevent bacteria growth.

Wipe the rims of the jars with a clean towel to remove any food residue, which could prevent the lids from sealing properly.

Step 5: Seal the Jars

Place the sterilized lids on top of each jar and screw on the bands just until they are fingertip-tight. Avoid tightening the lids too much, as air needs to escape during the canning process.

Step 6: Process the Jars in a Pressure Canner

This is where the magic happens. To preserve the chili safely, you need to process the jars in a pressure canner. Chili is a low-acid food, so it must be processed at a higher temperature to kill harmful bacteria like botulism.

Add the recommended amount of water to the pressure canner (usually around 2-3 inches) and place the filled jars inside. Lock the lid of the canner, and bring it up to the recommended pressure according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

Process the jars for 75 minutes at 10 pounds of pressure (for quarts) or 65 minutes at 10 pounds of pressure (for pints). The pressure canner will maintain the necessary temperature to safely preserve your chili.

Step 7: Cool and Store

Once the processing time is complete, turn off the heat and let the canner cool naturally. Do not open the canner until it has fully depressurized, which can take up to 30 minutes.

Once the canner has cooled, carefully remove the jars with a jar lifter and set them on a towel or cooling rack to cool completely. As the jars cool, you should hear a popping sound, which indicates that the jars have sealed properly.

Check the lids after the jars have cooled. If the lid is concave and does not move when pressed in the center, the jar has sealed correctly. If any jars have not sealed, refrigerate them and use them within a few days.

Store your sealed jars in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. Properly sealed jars of chili can be stored for up to one year.

How to Serve Your Canned Chili

When you’re ready to enjoy your chili, simply open a jar, heat it up in a pot over low to medium heat, and serve. Chili is great on its own, or you can top it with your favorite additions such as shredded cheese, sour cream, chopped green onions, or even a dollop of hot sauce for extra heat.

You can also serve chili over rice, baked potatoes, or nachos for a hearty meal. Leftover chili can be used in casseroles, tacos, or even chili dogs.

Tips for Canning Chili

  • Use fresh ingredients: Fresh, high-quality ingredients will result in the best-tasting chili and ensure its long shelf life.
  • Adjust the spice level: You can tailor the spice levels to your taste preferences. Add more chili powder or hot peppers for extra heat, or keep it mild with just a little bit of chili powder.
  • Don’t overfill jars: Always leave about 1 inch of headspace in the jars to allow for expansion during the canning process.
  • Pressure canning is essential: Make sure to follow all safety guidelines when using a pressure canner, as improper processing can lead to spoilage or safety risks.
  • Proper storage: Store your jars in a cool, dark place and check the seals before use. If a lid is loose or popped, the contents may not be safe to eat.

Final Thoughts

Canning chili at home is a rewarding and practical way to enjoy your favorite hearty dish whenever you want. By following these steps, you can make chili in bulk, preserve it safely, and have a comforting meal ready at your fingertips. Whether you’re an experienced canner or new to the process, this recipe will help you make and preserve delicious chili that will last for months.

Now that you know how to can your own chili, you can experiment with different varieties, spices, and ingredients to make it your own. Happy canning, and enjoy your homemade chili all year round!
