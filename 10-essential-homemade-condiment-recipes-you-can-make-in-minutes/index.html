10 Essential Homemade Condiment Recipes You Can Make in Minutes


Condiments are the unsung heroes of the culinary world. They can elevate a dish from ordinary to extraordinary, adding depth, flavor, and a burst of freshness with just a spoonful. Whether you’re slathering them on burgers, drizzling them over salads, or using them as dips, homemade condiments bring something special to the table. The best part? You don’t have to spend hours in the kitchen to make them! In fact, many of these recipes can be whipped up in just a few minutes.

Here are 10 essential homemade condiment recipes that you can make in minutes and incorporate into any meal.

1. Classic Mayonnaise

Mayonnaise is a versatile and creamy condiment that works well in a variety of dishes, from sandwiches to dips. Making your own at home gives you full control over the ingredients, ensuring that your mayo is fresh and free of preservatives.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar or lemon juice
  • 1 cup vegetable oil or any neutral oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Pinch of white pepper (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place the egg, mustard, vinegar (or lemon juice), and a pinch of salt in a food processor or blender.
  2. Process or blend until everything is well combined.
  3. With the processor running, slowly drizzle in the oil in a very thin stream. The mixture will begin to thicken as you go.
  4. Once all the oil is incorporated and the mayo has thickened, taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  5. Transfer to a jar and refrigerate. The mayo will keep for about a week.

2. Quick Ketchup

Homemade ketchup offers a fresher taste compared to the store-bought version, and it’s easy to make in just a few minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Water, to thin (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine all the ingredients and whisk together.
  2. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, for 5-10 minutes until the ketchup thickens and the flavors meld.
  3. If the ketchup becomes too thick, add a little water to reach your desired consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, then let cool to room temperature.
  5. Store in a jar or bottle in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

3. Creamy Garlic Aioli

Aioli is a garlic-infused mayonnaise that’s incredibly creamy and flavorful. It’s a great addition to sandwiches, fries, or even as a dip for grilled veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt, to taste
  • Olive oil, for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice, and Dijon mustard.
  2. Stir everything together until smooth and well combined.
  3. Taste and add salt as needed.
  4. For an extra touch of flavor, drizzle in a little olive oil while stirring for a richer taste.
  5. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to use.

4. Spicy Sriracha Mayo

This spicy, creamy condiment combines the rich creaminess of mayo with the heat of sriracha. It’s perfect for burgers, sushi, or as a dip for fried foods.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, sriracha sauce, and lemon juice.
  2. Stir until smooth and well combined.
  3. Taste and add salt as necessary.
  4. Serve immediately or store in the refrigerator for up to a week.

5. Tangy Barbecue Sauce

Barbecue sauce is essential for grilling season, and making your own gives you the ability to control the flavors and sweetness. This tangy barbecue sauce is perfect for brushing on ribs, chicken, or veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons honey or brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine all ingredients and whisk together until smooth.
  2. Cook over medium heat for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more honey or vinegar if you prefer it sweeter or tangier.
  4. Once the sauce has thickened slightly, remove from heat.
  5. Allow it to cool before storing in a jar in the fridge for up to 2 weeks.

6. Zesty Lemon Herb Dressing

This fresh and zesty dressing is perfect for drizzling over salads, grilled chicken, or roasted vegetables. It’s simple to make and packs a punch of citrusy flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, basil, or thyme)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, Dijon mustard, and minced garlic.
  2. Add the fresh herbs, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  4. Pour over salads, grilled meats, or roasted veggies. It can also be used as a marinade.

7. Sweet Chili Sauce

Sweet chili sauce is a versatile condiment that’s great for dipping or drizzling over Asian-inspired dishes. This homemade version is perfectly balanced between sweet and spicy.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, honey, soy sauce, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally.
  3. If you prefer a thicker sauce, whisk the cornstarch with a tablespoon of water and add it to the sauce, stirring constantly until it thickens.
  4. Allow the sauce to cool before using as a dip or drizzle over your favorite dishes.

8. Homemade Mustard

Mustard adds a tangy kick to sandwiches, hot dogs, and pretzels. Making your own mustard at home is incredibly easy, and it lets you adjust the flavor to your preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup yellow mustard seeds
  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard seeds
  • 1/4 cup white wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the mustard seeds, vinegar, and water.
  2. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to soften the seeds.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a blender or food processor and blend until you reach your desired consistency. For a smoother mustard, blend for longer.
  4. Stir in the honey and salt, then taste and adjust the seasoning.
  5. Store in a jar in the refrigerator for up to a month.

9. Roasted Red Pepper Dip

This smoky and flavorful dip can double as a condiment, perfect for pairing with crackers, roasted vegetables, or grilled meats. It’s a wonderful addition to any spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
  • 1/4 cup Greek yogurt or sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Roast the bell peppers by placing them under a broiler or on an open flame until the skin is charred.
  2. Peel off the skin and remove the seeds.
  3. In a blender or food processor, combine the roasted peppers, Greek yogurt, olive oil, lemon juice, and garlic.
  4. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve immediately or refrigerate for later use.

10. Avocado Salsa Verde

For a fresh, creamy twist on salsa, try making avocado salsa verde. This sauce is ideal for tacos, grilled meats, or as a dip for tortilla chips.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, peeled and pitted
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup lime juice
  • 1/2 cup green salsa (or tomatillo salsa)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the avocados, cilantro, lime juice, and green salsa.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  4. Serve immediately or refrigerate for up to a day.

Final Thoughts

Homemade condiments are a fantastic way to personalize your meals and add extra flavor without relying on store-bought versions filled with preservatives and artificial ingredients. With these 10 simple recipes, you’ll be able to make a wide range of condiments in no time. Whether you’re looking to elevate a sandwich or add a flavorful punch to your next BBQ, these quick and easy condiments are sure to become staples in your kitchen. Enjoy the burst of fresh flavors they bring to every dish!
