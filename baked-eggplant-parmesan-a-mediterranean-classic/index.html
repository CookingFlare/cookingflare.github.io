Baked Eggplant Parmesan: A Mediterranean Classic


Eggplant Parmesan, a quintessential Italian-American dish, has long been a staple of Mediterranean cuisine. With its crispy exterior, tender interior, and rich flavors, it is a true delight for the senses. This beloved dish has its roots in the traditional Italian recipe "Parmigiana di Melanzane," where eggplant is breaded and fried, then layered with tomato sauce, mozzarella, and basil. In this recipe, we take inspiration from this Italian classic and elevate it to a new level by baking the eggplant instead of frying it, creating a healthier and just as delicious version of this Mediterranean masterpiece. Choosing the Perfect Eggplant When it comes to preparing Eggplant Parmesan, the star of the dish is, of course, the eggplant. Look for firm, smooth, Italian or Asian eggplant varieties, such as 'Black Beauty' or 'White Eggplant.' Their tender flesh and moderate size make them perfect for slicing and breading. Make sure to select eggplant with no signs of bruises or cracks, as these can cause it to absorb too much moisture while baking. Preparing the Eggplant Wash the eggplant thoroughly, then slice it into 1/2-inch thick rounds. Sprinkle both sides with salt to draw out excess moisture and help the breadcrumbs adhere evenly. Let the eggplant sit for 30 minutes to 1 hour. Rinse the eggplant slices with cold water to remove excess salt, then pat them dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture. The Breading Process In a shallow dish, mix together 1 cup breadcrumbs and 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese. In a separate dish, whisk together 1 cup milk and 1 egg. Dip each eggplant slice first into the milk mixture, coating both sides evenly, then coat in the breadcrumb mixture, shaking off excess. This two-step process helps the breadcrumbs adhere perfectly to the eggplant. Baking the Eggplant Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or aluminum foil. Place the breaded eggplant slices on the prepared baking sheet. Brush with olive oil and bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through. The eggplant is done when it's golden brown and crispy on both sides. Preparing the Tomato Sauce In a medium saucepan, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil over medium heat. Add 1 onion, finely chopped, and sauté until translucent. Add 2 minced garlic cloves and cook for 1 minute. Then, add 2 (28-ounce) cans of crushed tomatoes, 1 cup chopped fresh basil, and 1/4 cup red wine (optional). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing the flavors to meld together. Build the Baked Eggplant Parmesan In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of tomato sauce. Arrange a layer of baked eggplant slices, overlapping them slightly. Sprinkle with 1/4 cup shredded mozzarella cheese. Drizzle with 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese. Repeat the layers two more times, finishing with a layer of mozzarella cheese on top. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 20-25 minutes or until the cheese is golden brown and bubbly. Serving and Variations Remove from the oven and let the Eggplant Parmesan rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves. Consider garnishing with chopped parsley or oregano for a Mediterranean twist. For a vegetarian option, swap out the parmesan cheese with nutritional yeast for a cheesy, nutty flavor. Tips and Variations

For an extra crispy topping, try broiling the Eggplant Parmesan for 1-2 minutes after baking. For a meat-lovers version, add sliced pepperoni or Italian sausage to the layers. Experiment with different types of cheese, such as provolone or ricotta, for a unique flavor combination. Serve with a side of pasta or a simple green salad for a satisfying and well-rounded meal.

By incorporating these techniques and tips, you'll be creating an authentic, mouthwatering Eggplant Parmesan dish that is sure to impress family and friends.
