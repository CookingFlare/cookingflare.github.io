Comforting Nut-Free Beef Stew Recipe


There’s something undeniably comforting about a hearty beef stew. The aroma of simmering beef, savory vegetables, and flavorful herbs and spices can make any day feel warmer and brighter. This nut-free beef stew recipe is the perfect balance of wholesome ingredients and rich, satisfying flavors. Whether you’re cooking for a crowd or simply craving a cozy homemade meal, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 celery stalks, sliced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, toss the beef cubes with the flour until evenly coated.

  2. In a Dutch oven or large pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes in batches and cook until browned on all sides. Transfer the browned beef to a plate and set aside.

  3. In the same pot, add the diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté until the onion is translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  4. Add the carrots and celery to the pot, and cook for a few minutes until they begin to soften.

  5. Return the browned beef to the pot. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using), stirring to loosen any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

  6. Stir in the tomato paste, bay leaves, dried thyme, paprika, salt, and pepper.

  7. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  8. Remove the bay leaves and discard.

  9. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if necessary.

  10. Serve the beef stew hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Why This Recipe Works

This nut-free beef stew recipe is a classic for a reason. The combination of seared beef, aromatic vegetables, and a flavorful broth creates a deeply satisfying dish that’s perfect for chilly evenings or whenever you need a dose of comfort. The slow simmering process allows the beef to become tender and succulent, while the vegetables soak up the rich flavors of the broth.

The addition of red wine, though optional, adds a layer of complexity and depth to the stew, elevating it from simple to sublime. The savory notes from the tomato paste, thyme, and paprika complement the natural sweetness of the carrots and onions, resulting in a well-balanced and deeply satisfying flavor profile.

This recipe is also incredibly versatile. You can customize it by adding your favorite root vegetables, such as potatoes or parsnips, or incorporating herbs like rosemary or oregano for a unique twist. The leftovers are just as delicious, making it a practical and convenient meal option for busy weeknights.

In Conclusion

A nut-free beef stew is a timeless and versatile dish that’s perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as a comforting meal for one. With its rich flavors and tender beef, this recipe is a surefire way to warm both body and soul. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let the simmering aromas of this comforting beef stew fill your kitchen with warmth and joy.
