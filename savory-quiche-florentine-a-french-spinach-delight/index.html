Savory Quiche Florentine: A French Spinach Delight


If you’re a fan of French cuisine, you’ve likely come across the delectable dish known as Quiche Florentine. This savory tart, traditionally made with a buttery crust, creamy custard, and a filling of spinach and cheese, is a beloved classic that never fails to impress. Today, we’ll take a closer look at this timeless recipe and explore how you can recreate it in your own kitchen.

The History of Quiche Florentine

Quiche Florentine hails from France, where it is a staple of traditional French cuisine. The word “quiche” is derived from the German word “kuchen,” meaning cake, and it is believed that quiche originated in the medieval German kingdom of Lothringen, which the French later renamed Lorraine. The dish made its way into French cuisine and evolved into the quiche we know and love today.

The term “Florentine” in the context of cooking refers to a dish that is prepared in the style of Florence, Italy. In the case of Quiche Florentine, it typically denotes the incorporation of spinach, a nod to the Italian city’s culinary influence.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 1/4 cup ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups fresh spinach, coarsely chopped
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups grated Gruyère cheese

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crust: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, tossing with a fork until a dough forms. Shape into a disk, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  3. Roll Out the Crust: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to fit a 9-inch tart pan. Press the dough into the pan and trim any excess.

  4. Blind Bake the Crust: Line the crust with parchment paper and fill with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove the weights and parchment paper, then bake for an additional 10 minutes, or until the crust is golden.

  5. Prepare the Filling: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and let cool.

  6. Assemble the Quiche: In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Stir in the spinach mixture and Gruyère cheese. Pour the filling into the prebaked crust.

  7. Bake the Quiche: Place the quiche in the preheated oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and the top is golden brown.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

The Perfect Pairing

Quiche Florentine is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed at any time of day. For a delightful brunch, consider serving it alongside a crisp green salad dressed with a tangy vinaigrette. If you’re planning a light dinner, pair it with a chilled glass of Sauvignon Blanc to complement the creamy texture and earthy flavors of the quiche.

In Conclusion

Savory Quiche Florentine is a timeless French dish that embodies the marriage of delicate flavors and comforting textures. With its rich history and delightful taste, this spinach-filled delight is sure to become a favorite in your culinary repertoire. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself to a special meal, Quiche Florentine is a versatile and elegant choice that never fails to impress. Bon appétit!
