Tropical Twist: Try This Exotic Mango Coconut Panna Cotta


Indulge in a taste of the tropics with this delightful and refreshing Mango Coconut Panna Cotta. This exotic dessert combines the creamy richness of coconut milk with the sweet and tangy flavors of ripe mangoes, creating a tropical twist that will transport you to a sunny paradise. With its smooth texture and vibrant colors, this dessert is not only a treat for the taste buds but also a feast for the eyes.

Ingredients

For the Mango Puree:

  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice

For the Coconut Panna Cotta:

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) of full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • 1/3 cup of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons of powdered gelatin
  • 2 tablespoons of cold water

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the mango puree. In a blender or food processor, combine the ripe mangoes, sugar, and lemon juice. Blend until smooth and set aside.

  2. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over the cold water and let it sit for about 5 minutes to bloom.

  3. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, heavy cream, and sugar. Heat the mixture over medium heat until it starts to simmer, stirring occasionally to dissolve the sugar.

  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the bloomed gelatin. Stir until the gelatin is completely dissolved.

  5. Stir in the vanilla extract and let the mixture cool for about 10 minutes.

  6. Prepare your serving glasses or molds by lightly greasing them with a neutral oil or spraying them with cooking spray. This will make it easier to unmold the panna cotta later.

  7. Pour a small amount of the coconut mixture into each glass, filling them about halfway. Place the glasses in the refrigerator and let them chill for about 20 minutes or until the mixture is slightly set.

  8. Once the first layer is set, carefully spoon a layer of mango puree on top of each glass, filling them almost to the rim.

  9. Return the glasses to the refrigerator and let them chill for another 20 minutes or until the mango layer is slightly set.

  10. Finally, pour the remaining coconut mixture on top of the mango layer, filling the glasses to the rim.

  11. Place the glasses back in the refrigerator and let them chill for at least 4 hours or overnight to fully set.

  12. When ready to serve, you can garnish the panna cotta with additional mango slices or coconut flakes if desired.

Serving Suggestions

This Mango Coconut Panna Cotta is a wonderful dessert to serve at any tropical-themed party or gathering. Its bright colors and tropical flavors make it a visually stunning and delicious treat. You can also serve it as a refreshing dessert after a spicy meal to cool down the palate.

For an extra touch of indulgence, you can serve the panna cotta with a drizzle of caramel or a sprinkle of toasted coconut flakes. The possibilities are endless, so feel free to get creative with your garnishes.

Conclusion

With its exotic flavors and creamy texture, this Mango Coconut Panna Cotta is sure to impress your friends and family. The combination of sweet mangoes and rich coconut milk creates a dessert that is both refreshing and satisfying. So why not transport yourself to a tropical paradise with this delightful twist on a classic dessert? Give it a try and let your taste buds embark on a flavorful journey.
