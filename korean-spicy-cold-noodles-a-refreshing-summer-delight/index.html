Korean Spicy Cold Noodles: A Refreshing Summer Delight


As the temperature rises and the sun beats down, there’s nothing quite like a refreshing dish to cool you down. Korean Spicy Cold Noodles, also known as Bibim Guksu, are the perfect summer delight. This dish is a harmonious blend of chewy noodles, fresh vegetables, and a spicy, tangy sauce that will tantalize your taste buds and keep you feeling cool and satisfied. Let’s dive into the world of Korean cuisine and learn how to create this mouthwatering dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

For the Noodles:

  • 300g of somen noodles
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • Ice water for rinsing

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger, finely grated

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, sliced
  • 1/2 cup of kimchi
  • 2 tablespoons of roasted sesame seeds
  • 2 stalks of green onions, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. Cook the Noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the somen noodles and salt, and cook according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain the noodles and rinse them under cold running water. Transfer the noodles to a bowl of ice water to chill. This will help them maintain their chewy texture.

  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the gochujang, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, minced garlic, and grated ginger until well combined. Adjust the seasoning to your taste by adding more gochujang for extra heat or more sugar for sweetness.

  3. Assemble the Dish: Drain the chilled noodles well and place them in a serving bowl. Drizzle the spicy sauce over the noodles and toss until the noodles are evenly coated. Arrange the julienned cucumber, carrot, sliced hard-boiled eggs, and kimchi on top of the noodles.

  4. Garnish and Serve: Sprinkle the dish with roasted sesame seeds and chopped green onions for added flavor and texture. Serve the Korean Spicy Cold Noodles immediately and enjoy the refreshing burst of flavors with each bite.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra kick of heat, add a teaspoon of Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru) to the sauce.
  • Customize the toppings to your preference by adding thinly sliced beef, pickled radish, or even a sprinkle of crushed roasted seaweed.

Final Thoughts

Korean Spicy Cold Noodles are a fantastic choice for a light and satisfying summer meal. The combination of the cold, chewy noodles and the spicy, savory sauce is sure to keep you coming back for more. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering or simply craving a refreshing dish, these noodles are a must-try. Embrace the flavors of Korea and treat yourself to this delightful culinary experience. So, what are you waiting for? Get your apron on, gather your ingredients, and embark on a culinary adventure with this Korean Spicy Cold Noodles recipe. Cheers to a delicious and refreshing summer!
