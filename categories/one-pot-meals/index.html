Creamy One Pot Tuscan Chicken Pasta

Tuscan cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, bold ingredients, and hearty portions. Today, we’re going to combine the essence of Tuscany with the convenience of a one-pot dish to create a mouthwatering Creamy One Pot Tuscan Chicken Pasta. This recipe is perfect for a weeknight dinner or a special occasion, as it’s easy to prepare, packed with flavor, and sure to become a family favorite. Ingredients 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces 1 large onion, chopped 3 cloves garlic, minced 1 cup heavy cream 1 cup chicken broth 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 teaspoon dried basil 1 teaspoon dried oregano 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1 cup penne pasta 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese 1/4 cup chopped prosciutto or pancetta (optional) Instructions In a large, heavy pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside. Add the chopped onion to the pot and cook until it’s translucent and starting to caramelize, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning. Add the chicken broth, heavy cream, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir to combine, then bring the mixture to a simmer. Add the penne pasta to the pot, stirring to coat the pasta with the creamy sauce. Return the chicken to the pot and stir to combine. Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the dish simmer, covered, for 20-25 minutes, or until the pasta is al dente and the chicken is cooked through. Stir in the cherry tomatoes, chopped parsley, and grated Parmesan cheese. If using prosciutto or pancetta, add it to the pot and stir to combine. Serve the Creamy One Pot Tuscan Chicken Pasta hot, garnished with additional parsley and Parmesan cheese if desired. Tips and Variations To make this recipe more substantial, add some sautéed mushrooms, bell peppers, or zucchini to the pot along with the onion and garlic. For an extra burst of flavor, add a splash of red wine or lemon juice to the pot during the last 5 minutes of cooking. To make this recipe ahead of time, cook the pasta and chicken mixture, then refrigerate or freeze it until ready to serve. Simply reheat the dish in the pot over low heat, stirring occasionally, until warmed through. This recipe is easily scalable to feed a larger crowd. Simply multiply the ingredients and adjust the cooking time as needed. Conclusion Creamy One Pot Tuscan Chicken Pasta is a hearty, comforting dish that’s perfect for any occasion. With its rich flavors, creamy sauce, and tender pasta, it’s sure to become a family favorite. Whether you’re a busy parent looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a special occasion chef seeking a show-stopping main course, this recipe is sure to impress. So go ahead, gather your ingredients, and get cooking! ...

Healthy One Pot Quinoa and Vegetable Stir Fry

Are you looking for a delicious and nutritious meal that can be prepared quickly and with minimal cleanup? Look no further than this Healthy One Pot Quinoa and Vegetable Stir Fry recipe! Packed with vibrant colors, flavors, and nutrients, this dish is a perfect option for those looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into their diet. Ingredients 1 cup quinoa 2 cups water 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 onion, thinly sliced 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced 1 zucchini, thinly sliced 1 cup broccoli florets 1 cup snap peas 1 cup mushrooms, sliced 1 tablespoon soy sauce 1 tablespoon sesame oil 1 tablespoon rice vinegar Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Rinse the quinoa under cold water and drain well. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil. Add the quinoa and reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 15 minutes, or until the quinoa is tender and the water is absorbed. Remove from heat and set aside. In the same pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant. Add the bell peppers, zucchini, broccoli, snap peas, and mushrooms to the pot. Stir fry for about 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar. Pour the sauce over the vegetables in the pot and stir well to coat. Add the cooked quinoa to the pot and toss everything together until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Cover the pot and let the stir fry sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Serve hot and enjoy!
