Autumnal Baking: Sweet and Spicy Recipes for Fall


Autumn is a season that brings a multitude of flavors and aromas, making it the perfect time to indulge in sweet and spicy treats. From comforting spices like cinnamon and nutmeg to the vibrant sweetness of seasonal fruits, autumnal baking offers a delightful array of flavors that can warm both body and soul. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering recipes that capture the essence of fall. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and let’s get baking!

Apple Cinnamon Muffins

There’s nothing quite like the combination of fresh apples and warm cinnamon to evoke the spirit of fall. These apple cinnamon muffins are a perfect treat for chilly mornings or a cozy afternoon snack. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 1/2 cups diced apples
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)
  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture, alternating with the buttermilk. Mix until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the diced apples and chopped walnuts, if using.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Pumpkin Spice Latte Cupcakes

Pumpkin spice lattes have become synonymous with fall, and now you can enjoy the same delicious flavors in cupcake form! These pumpkin spice latte cupcakes are a delightful combination of moist pumpkin cake and a creamy coffee-infused frosting. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the cupcakes:

  • 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the frosting:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a cupcake tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  4. Stir in the pumpkin puree, milk, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the pumpkin mixture, mixing until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the cupcake liners, filling each about 2/3 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a cupcake comes out clean.
  8. Allow the cupcakes to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

For the frosting:

  1. In a large bowl, beat the softened butter until creamy.
  2. Gradually add the powdered sugar, mixing well after each addition.
  3. Add the cooled brewed coffee and vanilla extract, and beat until light and fluffy.
  4. Once the cupcakes have cooled completely, frost them with the coffee-infused frosting.

Spiced Pear Galette

Pears are a quintessential fall fruit, and this spiced pear galette is a rustic and elegant dessert that showcases their natural sweetness. The flaky pastry, spiced with cinnamon and ginger, perfectly complements the tender pears. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the pastry:

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water

For the filling:

  • 3-4 ripe pears, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, melted
  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a food processor, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Gradually add the ice water, one tablespoon at a time, pulsing until the dough comes together.
  4. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and shape it into a disk. Wrap it in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  5. On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a circle about 12 inches in diameter.
  6. Transfer the rolled-out dough onto the prepared baking sheet.
  7. In a small bowl, combine the sugar, cinnamon, ginger, and lemon juice. Toss the sliced pears in the mixture until they are evenly coated.
  8. Arrange the pear slices in the center of the dough, leaving a border of about 2 inches.
  9. Fold the edges of the dough over the pears, pleating as you go.
  10. Brush the melted butter over the exposed pears and sprinkle with a little extra sugar, if desired.
  11. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the pears are tender.
  12. Allow the galette to cool slightly before serving.

Conclusion

Autumnal baking offers a delightful opportunity to explore the warm and comforting flavors of fall. Whether you’re craving the familiar combination of apple and cinnamon, the indulgent taste of pumpkin spice, or the delicate sweetness of spiced pears, these recipes are sure to satisfy your autumn baking cravings. So, embrace the season, gather your ingredients, and let the enticing aromas of sweet and spicy treats fill your kitchen as you embark on a delicious baking adventure. Happy baking and happy fall!
