Savory and Creamy Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomato Risotto

3 min · 637 words

Risotto is one of those dishes that exudes comfort and elegance in every creamy, flavorful bite. Today, we’re diving into a delightful variation: Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomato Risotto. This dish combines the richness of Arborio rice with the earthy taste of spinach and the tangy sweetness of sun-dried tomatoes, creating a symphony of flavors that will tantalize your taste buds.

Ingredients

For the Risotto:

  • 1 1/2 cups Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable broth, kept warm
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Garnish (optional):

  • Additional grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly chopped parsley

Instructions

1. Prepare the Ingredients:

  • Vegetable Broth: Heat the vegetable broth in a saucepan over low heat. Keep it warm throughout the cooking process.
  • Sun-Dried Tomatoes: If your sun-dried tomatoes are not already chopped, do so now. Set aside.
  • Spinach: Rinse the spinach leaves thoroughly and chop them roughly. Set aside.
  • Onion and Garlic: Finely chop the onion and mince the garlic cloves.

2. Start Cooking:

  • Sauté Onion and Garlic: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil and 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 3-4 minutes until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant.

  • Toast the Rice: Add the Arborio rice to the pot. Stirring constantly, toast the rice for about 2 minutes until it becomes slightly translucent around the edges.

3. Add Wine and Begin Simmering:

  • Deglaze with Wine: Pour in the cup of dry white wine. Stir the rice mixture continuously until the wine is absorbed.

  • Start Adding Broth: Begin adding the warm vegetable broth to the rice mixture, one ladleful (about 1/2 cup) at a time. Allow each addition of broth to be absorbed before adding the next. Stir frequently to ensure the rice cooks evenly and creamy.

4. Incorporate Sun-Dried Tomatoes and Spinach:

  • Add Sun-Dried Tomatoes: After adding about half of the vegetable broth and the rice is nearly tender (about 15-18 minutes into cooking), add the chopped sun-dried tomatoes to the risotto. Continue adding the broth as before.

  • Add Spinach: About halfway through adding the remaining broth, add the chopped spinach to the risotto. Stir well to incorporate the spinach leaves into the creamy rice mixture.

5. Finish and Serve:

  • Final Seasoning: Taste the risotto and season with salt and pepper as needed. Remember that the Parmesan cheese will add some saltiness, so adjust accordingly.

  • Add Butter and Parmesan: Once the rice is creamy and cooked to al dente (it should be tender but still firm to the bite), remove the pot from the heat. Stir in the remaining tablespoon of butter and the grated Parmesan cheese until well combined and creamy.

  • Rest Before Serving: Cover the pot and let the risotto rest for about 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

6. Garnish and Enjoy:

  • Serve Warm: Spoon the Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomato Risotto into shallow bowls. Garnish each serving with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese and freshly chopped parsley if desired.

Tips for Perfect Risotto:

  • Consistency: Risotto should be creamy and slightly runny, with each grain of rice distinct but not mushy.
  • Stirring: Stirring frequently helps release the rice’s starches, creating that creamy texture.
  • Timing: Arborio rice typically takes about 18-20 minutes to cook after the initial toasting phase.

Final Thoughts

This Spinach and Sun-Dried Tomato Risotto is a celebration of simple ingredients that come together to create a dish that’s both hearty and sophisticated. Whether you’re cooking for a weeknight dinner or entertaining guests, this recipe is sure to impress with its creamy texture and vibrant flavors. Pair it with a crisp white wine or a side salad for a complete meal that satisfies.

Experiment with different variations by adding grilled chicken or mushrooms for extra protein, or adjust the seasoning to suit your palate. The versatility of risotto makes it a staple worth mastering in any kitchen. Enjoy the process of stirring, tasting, and savoring this comforting dish—it’s a labor of love that pays off deliciously. Cheers to good food and great company!
