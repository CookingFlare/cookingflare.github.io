Budget-Friendly Indian Chicken Recipes: Affordable and Spicy


Indian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and diverse regional specialties. When it comes to cooking Indian dishes, many people assume that they require exotic spices, specialized equipment, and a hefty budget. However, with a little creativity and resourcefulness, you can create authentic Indian chicken recipes that are both affordable and spicy. In this post, we’ll explore some budget-friendly Indian chicken recipes that will tantalize your taste buds without breaking the bank.

###Recipe 1: Chicken Tikka Masala

Chicken Tikka Masala is a popular Indian dish that has gained worldwide recognition. This creamy, spicy, and flavorful recipe is a staple in many Indian restaurants, but it’s surprisingly easy to make at home. To make Chicken Tikka Masala, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Marinate the chicken in yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper for at least 30 minutes. Then, grill or bake the chicken until it’s cooked through. Meanwhile, heat the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, and heavy cream in a saucepan. Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and simmer until the sauce thickens. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

###Recipe 2: Chicken Biryani

Chicken Biryani is a classic Indian rice dish that’s both flavorful and aromatic. This recipe uses a combination of spices, herbs, and basmati rice to create a dish that’s both affordable and impressive. To make Chicken Biryani, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Cook the basmati rice according to package instructions. Meanwhile, heat the ghee or oil in a saucepan and saut√© the chopped onion, minced garlic, cumin seeds, and coriander seeds until the onion is translucent. Add the chicken and cook until it’s browned. Add the turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Add the cooked rice to the saucepan and mix well. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

###Recipe 3: Chicken Tandoori

Chicken Tandoori is a popular Indian dish that’s both flavorful and visually appealing. This recipe uses a combination of yogurt, spices, and lemon juice to marinate the chicken, which is then cooked in a tandoor or oven. To make Chicken Tandoori, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves

Marinate the chicken in yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper for at least 30 minutes. Then, grill or bake the chicken until it’s cooked through. Meanwhile, heat the chopped cilantro and mint leaves in a saucepan until they’re fragrant. Serve the chicken hot, garnished with the cilantro and mint mixture.

###Recipe 4: Chicken Korma

Chicken Korma is a mild and creamy Indian dish that’s perfect for those who prefer a milder flavor. This recipe uses a combination of spices, herbs, and coconut milk to create a dish that’s both flavorful and comforting. To make Chicken Korma, you’ll need:

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro leaves
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Marinate the chicken in yogurt, lemon juice, ghee or oil, garam masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper for at least 30 minutes. Then, grill or bake the chicken until it’s cooked through. Meanwhile, heat the coconut milk in a saucepan until it’s warm. Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and simmer until the sauce thickens. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.

###Conclusion

Indian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, vibrant colors, and diverse regional specialties. With these budget-friendly Indian chicken recipes, you can create authentic and delicious dishes that will tantalize your taste buds without breaking the bank. From Chicken Tikka Masala to Chicken Korma, these recipes use a combination of spices, herbs, and affordable ingredients to create dishes that are both flavorful and impressive. So, gather your ingredients, fire up your stove, and get ready to cook up some delicious and affordable Indian chicken recipes!
