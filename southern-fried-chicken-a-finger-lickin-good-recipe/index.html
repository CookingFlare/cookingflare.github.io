Southern Fried Chicken: A Finger-Lickin' Good Recipe


If there’s one dish that embodies the soul of Southern cooking, it’s the classic Southern fried chicken. With its crispy, golden crust and juicy, tender meat, this iconic dish has been bringing people together around the dinner table for generations. Whether it’s a Sunday family gathering or a casual backyard cookout, Southern fried chicken never fails to impress. In this post, I’m going to share with you my tried and true recipe for making the most finger-lickin’ good Southern fried chicken right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Place the chicken pieces in a large bowl and pour the buttermilk over them. Make sure the chicken is fully submerged. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight. This step is crucial for ensuring tender, flavorful chicken.

  2. Prepare the Breading: In a shallow dish, combine the flour, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to ensure even distribution of the spices.

  3. Coat the Chicken: Remove the marinated chicken from the buttermilk, allowing any excess to drip off. Dredge each piece in the seasoned flour mixture, making sure to coat the chicken thoroughly. Place the coated chicken on a wire rack set over a baking sheet and let it rest for 20 minutes. This resting period helps the breading adhere to the chicken and creates a crispier crust.

  4. Heat the Oil: In a large, heavy-bottomed skillet, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C). Use a candy or frying thermometer to monitor the temperature for best results.

  5. Fry the Chicken: Carefully place the coated chicken pieces in the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the skillet. Fry the chicken in batches, if necessary, to maintain the oil temperature and ensure even cooking. Cook the chicken for about 15-20 minutes, turning once, until the crust is golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 165°F (75°C).

  6. Drain and Serve: Once the chicken is cooked through and beautifully golden, transfer it to a wire rack set over a paper towel-lined baking sheet to drain any excess oil. Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving to ensure the juices redistribute, resulting in a moist and succulent bite.

Tips for Success

  • Buttermilk Magic: The buttermilk marinade not only infuses the chicken with tangy flavor but also helps tenderize the meat, resulting in juicy and tender fried chicken.
  • Consistent Coating: Ensure the chicken is evenly and thoroughly coated with the seasoned flour mixture to achieve a crisp and flavorful crust.
  • Oil Temperature: Maintaining the right oil temperature is crucial for achieving a crispy crust without burning the breading. Use a thermometer and adjust the heat as needed.

Serving Suggestions

Southern fried chicken pairs perfectly with classic sides such as creamy mashed potatoes, buttery cornbread, and tangy coleslaw. For a refreshing contrast, serve it with a side of crisp dill pickles and a drizzle of honey for a touch of sweetness.

So there you have it – a foolproof recipe for creating the most delectable Southern fried chicken right in your own kitchen. This beloved dish is a true crowd-pleaser and is sure to become a staple in your recipe repertoire. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just starting your culinary journey, this Southern fried chicken recipe is bound to impress and satisfy. Gather your loved ones, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to savor the irresistible crispy, juicy goodness of homemade Southern fried chicken.
