Hearty One Pot Beef and Vegetable Stew


There’s nothing quite like a warm and comforting bowl of stew on a chilly day. And if you’re looking for a recipe that’s both hearty and delicious, then you’re in luck! This Hearty One Pot Beef and Vegetable Stew is the perfect dish to satisfy your cravings and warm your soul. Packed with tender beef, flavorful vegetables, and a rich broth, this stew is sure to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 potatoes, diced
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef stew meat and cook until browned on all sides. This will give the stew a rich flavor.

  2. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the pot and sauté until the onion is translucent and fragrant. This will add depth to the stew’s aroma.

  3. Next, add the sliced carrots, diced potatoes, and sliced celery to the pot. Stir everything together and let the vegetables cook for a few minutes, allowing their natural flavors to meld together.

  4. Pour in the beef broth and red wine (if using). The red wine will add a subtle richness to the stew, but feel free to omit it if you prefer.

  5. Add the bay leaves, dried thyme, salt, and pepper to the pot. These seasonings will enhance the overall taste of the stew, so be generous with them.

  6. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let the stew simmer for at least 1 hour, or until the beef is tender and the flavors have melded together.

  7. Just before serving, add the frozen peas to the pot and cook for an additional 5 minutes. This will ensure that the peas are cooked through but still retain their vibrant color.

  8. Remove the bay leaves from the pot and discard them. Serve the Hearty One Pot Beef and Vegetable Stew hot in bowls, and garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a thicker stew, you can mix 2 tablespoons of flour with a little water to make a slurry, then add it to the pot during step 4. This will help thicken the broth.

  • Feel free to customize this recipe by adding your favorite vegetables, such as mushrooms or green beans. The beauty of a one-pot stew is that you can make it your own!

  • If you’re short on time, you can use a slow cooker to make this stew. Simply follow steps 1 to 4, then transfer everything to a slow cooker and cook on low for 6 to 8 hours, or until the beef is tender.

  • Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for up to 3 months. Just make sure to cool the stew completely before storing it in an airtight container.

So there you have it – a delicious and comforting Hearty One Pot Beef and Vegetable Stew that’s perfect for those cold winter nights or anytime you’re in need of some comfort food. Give this recipe a try and enjoy the warm and satisfying flavors it brings to your table. Bon appétit!
