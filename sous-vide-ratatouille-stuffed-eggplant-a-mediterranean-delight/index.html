Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Eggplant: A Mediterranean Delight


Eggplants are a versatile vegetable that can be transformed into a variety of delicious dishes. One such dish is the Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Eggplant, which combines the flavors of the Mediterranean with the precision cooking technique of sous vide. This recipe is perfect for those who appreciate the rich flavors of the Mediterranean cuisine and are looking for a unique and delightful dish to serve to family and friends.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium-sized eggplants
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat your sous vide water bath to 185°F (85°C).

  2. Start by preparing the eggplants. Cut each eggplant in half lengthwise, and scoop out the flesh, leaving about a ½-inch thick shell. Set the flesh aside for later use.

  3. In a large skillet over medium heat, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant and translucent.

  4. Add the diced zucchini, yellow bell pepper, and red bell pepper to the skillet. Sauté until the vegetables are tender, but still have a slight crunch to them.

  5. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and black pepper to the skillet. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. While the ratatouille mixture is simmering, prepare the eggplant shells for cooking. Season the inside of each eggplant shell with salt and pepper, and place them in a large vacuum-sealable bag.

  7. Once the ratatouille mixture is ready, spoon it into the eggplant shells, filling them generously. Seal the bag using a vacuum sealer, ensuring that the eggplants are tightly sealed.

  8. Carefully place the sealed bag into the preheated sous vide water bath, making sure that the eggplants are fully submerged. Cook for 2 hours to allow the flavors to infuse into the eggplants.

  9. After 2 hours, remove the bag from the water bath and carefully take out the stuffed eggplants. Place them on a serving dish, garnish with fresh basil leaves, and serve hot.

Serving Suggestions

This Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Eggplant is a delightful dish on its own, but it can also be paired with a side of couscous or a fresh green salad for a complete Mediterranean-inspired meal. The flavors of the ratatouille meld perfectly with the tender eggplant, creating a harmonious dish that is both satisfying and full of flavor.

Conclusion

The Sous Vide Ratatouille Stuffed Eggplant is a unique and flavorful dish that brings together the best of Mediterranean cuisine and modern cooking techniques. By using the sous vide method, the eggplant becomes tender and perfectly infused with the flavors of the ratatouille. This dish is not only delicious but also visually appealing, making it a great option for dinner parties or special occasions. So, why not try this Mediterranean delight today and impress your guests with your culinary skills?
