A Dash of China: Vietnamese-Cantonese Fusion Recipes to Discover


Food has the power to unite people, creating connections that span borders and cultures. Today, we invite you on a culinary journey that fuses two rich and ancient cuisines – Vietnamese and Cantonese. By blending the signature flavors and cooking techniques of these two gastronomic traditions, we present to you some mouth-watering Vietnamese-Cantonese Fusion recipes. So, let’s dig in and ignite your senses with this delightful fusion of flavors!

1. Kung Pao Chicken and Lemongrass Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless and skinless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tbsp rice wine
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp chili paste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup roasted peanuts
  • 1 cup diced bell peppers
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For Lemongrass Marinade:

  • 3 stalks lemongrass, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper

For Lemongrass Skewers:

  • 500g boneless and skinless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 8 lemongrass stalks
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Begin with the Kung Pao Chicken. In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with rice wine and cornstarch. Let marinate for at least 15 minutes. In another bowl, combine the minced garlic, chili paste, salt, and pepper. Set aside.

  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or wok and cook the marinated chicken on medium heat until browned on the outside. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  3. In the same pan, add the minced garlic-chili paste mixture, bell peppers, and roasted peanuts. Sauté until the peppers are just tender.

  4. Return the chicken to the pan and mix well with the vegetables and peanuts. Season with more salt and pepper if desired. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, then serve hot.

  5. For the Lemongrass Skewers, begin by whisking together the lemongrass marinade ingredients.

  6. Thread the chicken pieces onto the soaked lemongrass stalks or bamboo skewers, then brush generously with the lemongrass marinade.

  7. Heat vegetable oil in a grill pan or on a barbecue grill over medium heat. Grill the skewers for 4-5 minutes on each side, basting occasionally with the remaining marinade until the chicken is fully cooked and lightly charred.

  8. Let the skewers cool down for a few minutes before serving them with a side of fresh herbs and a sprinkle of lime juice to enjoy the tangy Vietnamese notes in this unique fusion of Kung Pao Chicken and Lemongrass Skewers.

2. Vietnamese Spring Rolls with Cantonese Dipping Sauce

Ingredients for Spring Rolls:

  • 8 large rice paper sheets
  • 200g shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 200g sliced cooked chicken breast
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 8 mushroom slices
  • 8 fresh mint leaves
  • 8 fresh basil leaves

Ingredients for Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 red chile, finely chopped
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preparing the dipping sauce. Combine hoisin sauce, water, rice vinegar, minced garlic, red chile, and green onion in a small bowl. Stir well and set aside.
  2. Soften the rice paper sheets in warm water for about 1 minute, or until they become pliable.
  3. Assemble the spring rolls by placing a small amount of shrimp, shredded chicken, cabbage, carrots, mushrooms, mint leaves, and basil leaves onto the center of the softened rice paper sheet.
  4. Roll the spring rolls up tightly, folding in the edges as you go to seal the wrap.
  5. Cut the spring rolls in half on the diagonal and serve with the Cantonese dipping sauce for a delicious appetizer that combines traditional Vietnamese spring rolls with a touch of Cantonese flavor.

3. Stir-Fried Rice Noodles with Cantonese BBQ Pork (Char Siu)

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cooked char siu (Chinese BBQ pork)
  • 8 oz bean sprouts
  • 2 cups shredded green cabbage
  • 2Thai chili peppers, sliced (optional)

Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Begin by preparing the sauce by whisking together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, fish sauce, sugar, and rice vinegar in a bowl. Set aside for now.
  2. Parboil the rice noodles in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or large pan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and cook untilfragrant.
  4. Add the shredded char siu, bean sprouts, shredded cabbage, and Thai chili peppers (if desired). Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Toss in the rice noodles and pour the sauce over the noodles. Mix well until the noodles are evenly coated with the sauce. Cook for another minute or two, ensuring that the noodles are heated through.
  6. Serve the Stir-Fried Rice Noodles with Cantonese BBQ Pork hot and enjoy the seamless fusion of Vietnamese and Cantonese flavors in this delicious dish.

This Vietnamese-Cantonese fusion recipe collection will take you on a flavorful journey that will surprise and delight even the most discerning of palates. A true testament to the richness of Vietnamese and Cantonese cuisines, each dish offers an intriguing dance of traditional and unfamiliar elements that will leave you asking for just one more bite. So, treat yourself to an unforgettable food adventure by merging the best of these two unique culinary worlds. Happy cooking!
