Top 5 Bread Recipes for Holiday Baking


The holiday season is a time for warmth, family gatherings, and of course, delicious food. One of the most beloved foods that bring comfort and joy to the holiday table is fresh, homemade bread. The aroma of bread baking in the oven can transform any home into a haven of cozy smells, perfect for celebrating with loved ones. Whether you’re hosting a grand feast or simply enjoying a quiet evening with family, these top 5 bread recipes are sure to impress and satisfy.

In this post, we’ll walk you through five of the most delightful and unique bread recipes for your holiday baking. From classic favorites to new twists on traditional breads, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Let’s dive in and get ready to fill your home with the mouthwatering scent of freshly baked bread!

1. Classic Dinner Rolls

No holiday meal is complete without soft, pillowy dinner rolls. These rolls are a true crowd-pleaser, perfect for sopping up gravy or serving alongside your favorite main dishes. This recipe is simple, reliable, and produces rolls that are buttery, fluffy, and irresistibly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the warm milk, warm water, and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the liquid and let it sit for 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a separate large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Create a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, melted butter, and eggs.
  3. Mix the dough until it comes together, then knead it on a lightly floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal portions. Shape each portion into a ball and place them in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.
  6. Cover the rolls and let them rise for another 30 minutes. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown on top. Brush with melted butter before serving.

2. Cinnamon Swirl Bread

This sweet, cinnamon-filled loaf is perfect for breakfast or an afternoon snack during the holiday season. With its swirled cinnamon filling and soft, slightly sweet dough, cinnamon swirl bread adds a cozy and indulgent touch to any holiday gathering.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast (2 1/4 teaspoons)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 tablespoons ground cinnamon

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine 2 cups of flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. Stir in the warm milk, butter, and egg. Gradually add the remaining flour until a soft dough forms.
  2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it, and let it rise for 1 hour.
  3. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Punch down the dough and roll it out into a rectangle on a floured surface.
  4. Mix the brown sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl. Sprinkle this cinnamon mixture evenly over the dough. Carefully roll the dough into a log, starting from the long side.
  5. Place the dough seam-side down in a greased loaf pan. Let it rise for 30 minutes before baking for 30-35 minutes, or until golden brown. Let it cool before slicing.

3. Pumpkin Bread

Pumpkin bread is a fall and holiday favorite, and it’s perfect for sharing with friends and family. The warm spices, combined with the natural sweetness of pumpkin, create a soft, moist loaf that is both comforting and festive.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the eggs and sugar together until smooth. Add the pumpkin puree, oil, and vanilla, mixing until combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing just until combined. If desired, fold in the walnuts.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top. Bake for 60-70 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

4. Rosemary Focaccia Bread

Focaccia is a rustic Italian bread that pairs perfectly with savory holiday meals. Infused with the earthy aroma of rosemary and drizzled with olive oil, this bread is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making it a delightful addition to your holiday spread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • Coarse sea salt for topping

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let it sit for 5 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and 2 tablespoons of olive oil, stirring until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8 minutes until smooth. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  4. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Punch down the dough and transfer it to a greased baking sheet. Flatten it with your fingers to form a rectangle.
  5. Use your fingers to create dimples in the dough. Drizzle the remaining olive oil over the top, then sprinkle with rosemary and coarse sea salt.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown. Allow the bread to cool slightly before slicing and serving.

5. Cranberry Orange Bread

The combination of tart cranberries and sweet orange zest makes this bread a standout during the holidays. The citrusy flavor and festive color make it perfect for breakfast or as a holiday gift for friends and family.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • Zest of one orange
  • 1 1/2 cups fresh or frozen cranberries, chopped

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the orange juice, oil, egg, and orange zest.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Gently fold in the chopped cranberries.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the bread cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Conclusion

Baking bread during the holiday season is a cherished tradition that brings joy, warmth, and comfort to any home. These five bread recipes—classic dinner rolls, cinnamon swirl bread, pumpkin bread, rosemary focaccia, and cranberry orange bread—are sure to make your holiday gatherings even more special. Each recipe offers a unique twist on traditional breads, making them perfect for sharing with loved ones or gifting to friends.

So, roll up your sleeves, preheat your oven, and get ready to enjoy the delicious smells and satisfying textures of homemade bread this holiday season. Happy baking!
