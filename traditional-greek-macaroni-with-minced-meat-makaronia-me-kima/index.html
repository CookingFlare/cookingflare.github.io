Traditional Greek Macaroni with Minced Meat (Makaronia Me Kima)


If there’s one dish that seamlessly captures the essence of Greek comfort food, it has to be Makaronia Me Kima, or Traditional Greek Macaroni with Minced Meat. This hearty and flavorful pasta dish is a staple in Greek households, bringing together simple ingredients to create a culinary masterpiece that warms your soul with every bite. Packed with Mediterranean flavors and aromatic spices, it’s the perfect recipe to transport your taste buds to a bustling taverna in Greece.

Makaronia Me Kima is often considered a weeknight favorite due to its simplicity, but its depth of flavor makes it suitable for special occasions as well. Whether you’re cooking for your family, hosting friends, or indulging in a quiet evening of nostalgia, this dish never disappoints. Below, we’ll guide you through the steps to create an authentic version of this timeless classic that will soon become a staple in your own kitchen.

Ingredients for Makaronia Me Kima

The key to any good traditional dish lies in the quality of the ingredients. When preparing Makaronia Me Kima, aim for fresh and aromatic components to guarantee a rich and authentic flavor profile. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Meat Sauce (Kima):

  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef (or a mixture of beef and pork for extra flavor)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz/400g) diced tomatoes or tomato passata
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (to balance the acidity of the tomatoes)
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon (optional but highly recommended for that signature Greek flavor)
  • 2-3 whole cloves or 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves (optional)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/3 cup dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup water or beef broth

For the Pasta Base:

  • 1 pound (450g) spaghetti or tubular pasta (such as penne or ziti)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt for boiling water

For Serving (Optional):

  • Grated cheese such as Kefalotyri, Parmesan, or Pecorino Romano

Cooking Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Meat Sauce (Kima)

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan or pot over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes until softened and translucent. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute to release its aroma.
  2. Add the ground beef to the pan, breaking it apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Sauté until the meat is browned and fully cooked, ensuring no large clumps remain.
  3. Once the meat is browned, season it generously with salt, black pepper, and cinnamon. Sprinkle in the sugar to offset the tartness of the tomatoes.
  4. Pour in the white wine and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes, allowing the alcohol to evaporate while infusing the meat with added depth of flavor.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste, diced tomatoes (or tomato passata), bay leaf, oregano, and cloves. Mix well to combine. Add 1/2 cup of water or beef broth to loosen the sauce slightly.
  6. Lower the heat to medium-low and let the sauce simmer uncovered for 25-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The goal is to achieve a thick, aromatic meat sauce with a deep, rich flavor.

Step 2: Cook the Pasta

  1. While the meat sauce is simmering, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cooking your pasta in generously salted water is essential for enhancing its flavor and ensuring a better overall dish.
  2. Add the spaghetti or tubular pasta to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions. Make sure to stir intermittently to prevent the pasta from sticking together.
  3. Drain the cooked pasta thoroughly. Drizzle with a bit of olive oil and toss lightly to prevent the pasta from clumping.

Step 3: Assemble the Dish

  1. Remove the bay leaf from the meat sauce once it’s done simmering. Taste-test for seasoning and adjust if needed.
  2. For convenience, you can either mix the meat sauce directly into the pasta for an evenly coated dish or serve the sauce atop individual portions for a more traditional presentation.
  3. Sprinkle generously with grated Kefalotyri, Parmesan, or Pecorino Romano to finish the dish.

Tips for Perfecting Your Makaronia Me Kima

While the recipe itself is uncomplicated, a few tips can help elevate your dish to authentic Greek perfection:

  1. Balance the spices: The addition of cinnamon and cloves is optional, but these spices offer a unique warmth and unmistakable Mediterranean essence. Don’t go overboard, though—a small amount adds depth without overpowering.
  2. Let the sauce simmer: The meat sauce thrives on low and slow cooking. The more time you allow the ingredients to meld together, the richer and deeper the flavor becomes.
  3. Choose the right cheese: Traditional Greek versions of this dish often feature grated Kefalotyri, a hard, salty cheese made from sheep’s milk. If unavailable, Parmesan or Pecorino Romano make excellent substitutions without compromising authenticity.
  4. Pasta selection: While spaghetti may be the most commonly used pasta for this dish, other options like penne, rigatoni, or ziti are equally delightful and offer unique textures.

Serving and Pairing Suggestions

Makaronia Me Kima shines as a stand-alone dish, but it pairs beautifully with a simple green salad dressed with olive oil and lemon juice. For an authentic Greek dining experience, accompany the meal with freshly baked crusty bread and a glass of dry red wine.

Feeling indulgent? Complete the feast with a light dessert such as Greek yogurt topped with honey and walnuts or a slice of syrup-soaked baklava. These pairings will enhance the overall Mediterranean flavor journey and satisfy all your senses.

Why You’ll Love Makaronia Me Kima

Makaronia Me Kima is the ultimate intersection of simplicity and sophistication. This balanced dish draws on humble ingredients, transforming them into something extraordinary through time-honored Greek techniques and flavors. It’s family-friendly, budget-conscious, and adaptable to suit various palates. Whether it’s your first attempt at Greek cuisine or a nostalgic trip back to a much-loved classic, Makaronia Me Kima delivers bold flavors and timeless comfort in every warm, satisfying bite.

So, what are you waiting for? Gather your ingredients, invite a few friends, and let the magic of Makaronia Me Kima transport you straight to the Greek Isles. Dinner doesn’t need to be complicated, but with this dish, it will always feel special.
