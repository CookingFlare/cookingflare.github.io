How to Make Korean Spicy Hot Pot (Budae Jjigae)


Korean Spicy Hot Pot, or Budae Jjigae (부대찌개), is a beloved comfort food that perfectly blends bold flavors with the warmth of a hearty stew. Originating from the Korean War era, this dish was created using ingredients that were available during difficult times, such as canned meats, kimchi, and vegetables. Over time, it has evolved into a popular dish enjoyed in homes and restaurants all around Korea.

This dish is perfect for chilly evenings when you’re craving something comforting, spicy, and filling. With a delicious combination of spicy gochujang (Korean chili paste), savory broth, and various meats and vegetables, Budae Jjigae offers a rich depth of flavor that is irresistible. Whether you’re looking to recreate this dish at home or just curious about Korean cuisine, this guide will show you how to make a simple yet satisfying version of Budae Jjigae.

Key Ingredients for Budae Jjigae

The beauty of Budae Jjigae lies in its versatility. It can be made with a variety of ingredients, so you can easily customize it based on your preferences. The core ingredients are a spicy, flavorful broth and a mix of meat, vegetables, and seasonings. Let’s take a look at the essential ingredients that make this dish truly special.

Base Broth Ingredients

  • Kimchi: Fermented cabbage or radish kimchi provides both tang and spice to the dish. The more fermented the kimchi, the deeper the flavor it imparts to the stew.
  • Gochujang (Korean chili paste): This thick, spicy paste is a key flavoring in the stew, adding both heat and depth.
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes): To enhance the heat and add texture, Korean chili flakes are often added to the broth.
  • Garlic: Freshly minced garlic provides aromatic depth and a hint of sweetness to balance out the spice.
  • Soy Sauce: A splash of soy sauce adds an umami element to the stew, deepening the overall flavor profile.
  • Anchovy Stock (or Beef Broth): For a rich, savory base, anchovy stock is traditionally used, but beef broth can also be a good substitute.

Meat and Protein

  • Spam: A unique ingredient that’s often included in Budae Jjigae, Spam offers a salty and savory flavor that complements the broth.
  • Sausages: Hot dog-style sausages or Korean sausages add an extra layer of flavor and heartiness to the stew.
  • Ground Beef: Adding ground beef brings in a juicy and tender texture that balances well with the other meats.
  • Tofu: For a bit of texture and creaminess, soft tofu is included in the pot, soaking up the spicy broth.

Vegetables and Extras

  • Onions: Thinly sliced onions provide a mild sweetness that complements the other bold flavors.
  • Mushrooms: Shitake or oyster mushrooms add a delicious umami flavor and meaty texture.
  • Rice Cakes (Tteokbokki): Chewy rice cakes are a popular addition to Budae Jjigae, giving the stew a satisfying bite.
  • Noodles: Sometimes, noodles are added to the stew to make it even heartier and more filling.

Garnishes and Condiments

  • Chopped Scallions: Fresh green onions sprinkled on top add a burst of freshness.
  • Sesame Oil: A few drops of sesame oil at the end bring a nutty aroma and round out the flavors.
  • Sesame Seeds: Lightly toasted sesame seeds can be used as a garnish to add a bit of crunch and flavor.

Steps to Make Budae Jjigae

Now that you’re familiar with the ingredients, let’s dive into the steps for making your own delicious and comforting Budae Jjigae at home. This recipe will guide you through creating a flavorful, spicy hot pot that serves 4 to 6 people.

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you start cooking, it’s important to have all your ingredients ready to go. Here’s how you should prepare each component:

  • Kimchi: If you’re using store-bought kimchi, ensure it’s well-fermented for a more robust flavor. Cut it into bite-sized pieces.
  • Spam: Slice the Spam into thin strips, roughly 1/4 inch thick.
  • Sausages: Slice the sausages into bite-sized pieces, around 1/2 inch thick.
  • Ground Beef: If using ground beef, season it lightly with salt and pepper.
  • Tofu: Cut the tofu into cubes (about 1-inch in size).
  • Mushrooms: Slice the mushrooms into thin pieces.
  • Rice Cakes: If using fresh rice cakes, soak them in warm water for 10-15 minutes before using. If using pre-cooked rice cakes, they can go directly into the stew.

Step 2: Make the Broth

To start, you need to create a flavorful base for the stew. In a large pot, heat some vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté it until fragrant, which should take about 1 minute.

Next, add the kimchi to the pot and sauté it for 3-4 minutes until it softens and releases its juices. This step helps bring out the tangy and spicy flavors of the kimchi.

Now it’s time to build the broth. Add 4 cups of anchovy stock (or beef broth) to the pot. If you’re making the broth from scratch, you can simmer dried anchovies in water for 20 minutes to create a rich stock. Alternatively, beef broth can be used for a heartier flavor.

Stir in the gochujang (Korean chili paste) and gochugaru (Korean chili flakes). The paste should dissolve into the broth, creating a thick and spicy liquid. If you like more heat, you can adjust the amount of gochugaru to suit your taste.

Add 2 tablespoons of soy sauce to enhance the umami flavor. Bring the broth to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 3: Add the Meats and Vegetables

Once the broth is simmering and the flavors have developed, it’s time to add the meats and vegetables to the pot. Start with the Spam, sausages, and ground beef. Stir everything together, ensuring the meat is evenly distributed throughout the broth.

Add the tofu cubes and mushrooms, allowing them to soak in the delicious flavors of the stew. Toss in the rice cakes, ensuring they are submerged in the broth. The rice cakes will soak up the spicy, savory liquid, making them a delicious addition to the dish.

Let everything cook for an additional 10-15 minutes. The meats should be cooked through, and the rice cakes should become tender and chewy. The tofu should absorb the flavors of the broth without breaking apart.

Step 4: Final Touches

Once the stew is ready, taste the broth and adjust the seasoning if necessary. You can add more soy sauce for saltiness or a bit more gochujang if you prefer extra spice.

Before serving, drizzle a small amount of sesame oil over the top of the stew and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds. Garnish with freshly chopped scallions for a burst of freshness and color.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy!

Budae Jjigae is traditionally served hot and eaten family-style. Bring the pot directly to the table and let everyone serve themselves. It’s usually accompanied by steamed white rice, which helps balance the heat of the stew. You can also serve the dish with a few side dishes like kimchi or pickled vegetables for added flavor.

To enjoy, simply ladle the hot pot into bowls, making sure to include a mix of meats, vegetables, and rice cakes. The combination of tender meat, chewy rice cakes, and spicy broth is sure to satisfy your cravings for comfort food.

Tips for Perfecting Your Budae Jjigae

  • Adjust the spice level: Korean chili paste (gochujang) and chili flakes (gochugaru) can be adjusted to your preferred spice level. If you prefer a milder stew, reduce the amount of gochugaru or gochujang. For those who love heat, feel free to add more!
  • Broth consistency: If you like a thicker stew, you can add less broth or let it simmer for longer to reduce. If you prefer a more soupy consistency, add more broth or water.
  • Adding more vegetables: Feel free to add other vegetables like cabbage, spinach, or zucchini to suit your taste.
  • Leftovers: Budae Jjigae often tastes even better the next day as the flavors continue to develop. Store any leftovers in an airtight container and refrigerate for up to 2-3 days. Reheat before serving.

Why You Should Try Budae Jjigae

Budae Jjigae is more than just a meal—it’s an experience. The process of sharing this stew with family or friends is a perfect way to bring people together. Whether you’re a fan of Korean food or new to the cuisine, this spicy hot pot is bound to become a favorite.

With its comforting broth, variety of meats, and satisfying rice cakes, Budae Jjigae offers a one-pot meal that is both filling and flavorful. It’s a great way to introduce yourself to Korean flavors and experience a dish that has deep cultural significance.

So, grab your ingredients, heat up the pot, and get ready to enjoy a steaming bowl of Budae Jjigae. It’s a dish that’s sure to warm your heart and your stomach. Happy cooking!
