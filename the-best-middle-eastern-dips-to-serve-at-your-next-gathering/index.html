The Best Middle Eastern Dips to Serve at Your Next Gathering


Middle Eastern cuisine is known for its vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and unique combinations that create a symphony of tastes. One of the most beloved elements of this culinary tradition is the variety of dips, each offering its own distinctive flavor profile and texture. Whether you’re planning a casual get-together, a festive celebration, or just want to spice up your appetizer spread, Middle Eastern dips can be the perfect addition. From creamy hummus to rich baba ghanoush, these dips bring an irresistible burst of flavor that will leave your guests asking for more.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best Middle Eastern dips you can serve at your next gathering, along with tips for making them at home, pairing suggestions, and serving ideas. Let’s dive into the world of mezze!

1. Hummus: The Iconic Chickpea Dip

No list of Middle Eastern dips would be complete without mentioning hummus. This iconic dip has become a worldwide favorite, and for good reason. Made primarily from blended chickpeas, tahini (sesame paste), lemon juice, garlic, and olive oil, hummus is creamy, flavorful, and incredibly versatile. It’s perfect for dipping pita bread, vegetables, crackers, or even spreading on sandwiches.

How to Make Classic Hummus:

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 can (15 ounces) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
    • 1/4 cup tahini
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
    • Salt to taste
    • Water as needed for desired consistency

  • Instructions:

    1. Combine chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and cumin in a food processor.
    2. Process until smooth, scraping down the sides as needed.
    3. Add water, a tablespoon at a time, until the hummus reaches your desired consistency.
    4. Taste and adjust salt, lemon juice, or garlic to your preference.
    5. Garnish with olive oil, paprika, and chopped parsley for an extra touch of flavor.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Serve hummus with fresh pita bread, cucumber slices, or carrot sticks for a healthy, satisfying snack.
  • Hummus can also be served as a spread for sandwiches or wraps, paired with grilled vegetables or falafel.

2. Baba Ghanoush: The Smoky Eggplant Dip

Another standout in Middle Eastern cuisine is baba ghanoush. This smoky, creamy eggplant dip is a perfect contrast to the brightness of hummus. The key to baba ghanoush’s unique flavor is roasting the eggplant over an open flame or in the oven until the skin is charred and the flesh becomes soft and smoky.

How to Make Baba Ghanoush:

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 medium eggplants
    • 1/4 cup tahini
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Pomegranate seeds (optional, for garnish)
    • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) or prepare a grill if you prefer grilling the eggplant.
    2. Pierce the eggplants a few times with a fork and place them on a baking sheet.
    3. Roast the eggplants in the oven for about 40 minutes, or grill them over an open flame, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft.
    4. Let the eggplants cool slightly before peeling off the skin. Scoop out the flesh and transfer it to a bowl.
    5. Mash the eggplant with a fork or use a food processor to blend it until smooth.
    6. Add tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper to the eggplant and mix well.
    7. Garnish with pomegranate seeds and fresh parsley, if desired.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Serve baba ghanoush with warm pita, fresh vegetables, or crispy flatbreads.
  • It also pairs beautifully with grilled meats, such as kebabs or lamb chops.

3. Muhammara: A Fiery Red Pepper and Walnut Dip

For those who love a bit of heat and complexity in their dips, muhammara is the perfect choice. This bold and flavorful dip hails from Syria and combines roasted red peppers, walnuts, garlic, and olive oil, with a hint of pomegranate molasses for a touch of sweetness. It’s spicy, smoky, and creamy all at once, making it an excellent choice for any gathering.

How to Make Muhammara:

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 red bell peppers, roasted and peeled
    • 1 cup walnuts, toasted
    • 1/4 cup olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (or more for extra heat)
    • Salt to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. To roast the peppers, place them on a baking sheet and broil in the oven or char them over an open flame until the skin is blackened. Let them cool, then peel and discard the skin.
    2. In a food processor, combine the roasted peppers, toasted walnuts, olive oil, pomegranate molasses, garlic, cumin, red pepper flakes, and salt.
    3. Process until smooth, scraping down the sides as needed.
    4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more salt, cumin, or red pepper flakes if desired.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Serve muhammara with pita chips, toasted baguette slices, or fresh vegetables like bell peppers and cucumber.
  • It also pairs beautifully with grilled chicken or lamb, adding a fiery kick to your meal.

4. Labneh: Creamy Strained Yogurt Dip

Labneh is a creamy, tangy dip made from strained yogurt. It’s similar in texture to cream cheese but has a much fresher and lighter taste. Labneh is often served with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of herbs, making it an incredibly simple yet flavorful addition to any appetizer spread.

How to Make Labneh:

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 cups full-fat Greek yogurt (or regular yogurt, if unavailable)
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Olive oil for drizzling
    • Fresh herbs for garnish (mint, thyme, or oregano)

  • Instructions:

    1. Place the yogurt in a cheesecloth-lined sieve set over a bowl. Sprinkle with salt and stir to combine.
    2. Let the yogurt strain for 12 to 24 hours in the refrigerator, allowing excess liquid to drain off and leaving behind a thick, creamy consistency.
    3. Transfer the strained yogurt (labneh) to a serving dish, drizzle with olive oil, and garnish with fresh herbs.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Labneh can be served as a spread for pita bread or crackers.
  • It also pairs beautifully with grilled meats, fresh vegetables, and even drizzled over roasted vegetables for extra creaminess.

5. Tzatziki: A Refreshing Yogurt and Cucumber Dip

Originating from Greece, tzatziki is a cool and refreshing dip that combines yogurt, cucumber, garlic, and fresh herbs, typically dill or mint. Its light, tangy flavor makes it a perfect complement to grilled meats and vegetables, as well as a refreshing dip for pita or vegetables.

How to Make Tzatziki:

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 cup Greek yogurt
    • 1/2 cucumber, finely grated and excess water squeezed out
    • 1 garlic clove, minced
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
    • 1 tablespoon fresh dill or mint, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. Combine Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, garlic, olive oil, dill or mint, and lemon juice in a bowl.
    2. Stir until smooth and well-combined.
    3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
    4. Let the tzatziki chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Tzatziki is perfect as a dip for pita bread, grilled vegetables, or fresh vegetables like carrots and cucumber.
  • It also pairs well with gyros, grilled lamb, or roasted chicken, adding a cool and creamy element to the dish.

6. Fattoush: A Tangy Salad with a Twist

While not a dip in the traditional sense, fattoush is a vibrant Middle Eastern salad that can easily double as a dip. Made with fresh vegetables, herbs, and crunchy pieces of toasted pita bread, fattoush is bright and tangy, thanks to the addition of sumac and a zesty lemon dressing.

How to Make Fattoush:

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 tomatoes, diced
    • 1 cucumber, diced
    • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
    • 1 cup chopped fresh parsley
    • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
    • 2 pita breads, toasted and broken into pieces
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon ground sumac
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. In a large bowl, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, parsley, and mint.
    2. Add the toasted pita pieces and toss to combine.
    3. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, sumac, salt, and pepper.
    4. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat.
    5. Let the salad sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Serving Suggestions:

  • Serve fattoush as a side dish or as part of a larger mezze platter.
  • It’s also a great dip for pita bread, as the crunchy pieces of pita soak up the tangy dressing beautifully.

Conclusion

Middle Eastern dips are more than just appetizers—they’re an expression of the region’s rich culinary traditions, filled with vibrant flavors and fresh ingredients. Whether you’re serving classic hummus, smoky baba ghanoush, or fiery muhammara, these dips are sure to impress your guests and elevate any gathering.

By offering a mix of creamy, tangy, smoky, and spicy flavors, you’ll ensure that your spread caters to a variety of tastes and preferences. These dips also pair beautifully with a variety of accompaniments, from pita bread and fresh vegetables to grilled meats and flatbreads.

So, next time you’re planning a gathering, why not take a trip to the Middle East through your taste buds and serve up these delicious, crowd-pleasing dips? Your guests will be delighted, and you’ll be the star of the party!
