Seafood Quiche Recipe: A Perfect Brunch Option

When it comes to brunch, there’s nothing quite like a warm and savory seafood quiche to impress your guests. This delicious dish combines the richness of seafood with the comforting fluffiness of a classic quiche, making it a perfect option for a leisurely weekend brunch. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply craving a special homemade brunch, this seafood quiche recipe is sure to delight your taste buds. Ingredients For the Crust: 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced 1/4 cup ice water For the Filling: 1 cup cooked seafood (such as shrimp, crab, or a combination of your favorites) 1 cup shredded Gruyère cheese 1/2 cup chopped onion 4 large eggs 1 cup heavy cream 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon black pepper 1/4 teaspoon paprika 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme Instructions Making the Crust: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Add the diced butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, mixing until the dough comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour. Preparing the Filling: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a skillet, sauté the chopped onion until translucent. Add the cooked seafood and continue cooking for a few minutes until heated through. Set aside. Assembling the Quiche: Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges. Sprinkle the shredded Gruyère cheese over the bottom of the crust. Spread the seafood and onion mixture over the cheese. Making the Custard Filling: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, paprika, and dried thyme until well combined. Pour the custard filling over the seafood and cheese in the pie dish. Baking the Quiche: Place the quiche on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the center is set and the crust is golden brown. Allow the quiche to cool for 10 minutes before slicing and serving. Serving Suggestions This seafood quiche is best enjoyed warm, making it an ideal centerpiece for a brunch spread. Pair it with a simple green salad dressed with a tangy vinaigrette to balance the richness of the quiche. Additionally, a glass of crisp white wine or a refreshing mimosa would complement the flavors beautifully, adding a touch of sophistication to your brunch affair. ...
