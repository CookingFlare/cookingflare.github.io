Mummy Dogs: A Fun and Easy Halloween Snack Recipe


Halloween is the perfect time to unleash your creativity in the kitchen, and what better way to celebrate the spooky season than by making Mummy Dogs? These adorable snacks are not only easy to prepare but also a hit with both kids and adults alike. With their flaky, golden crust and savory sausage filling, Mummy Dogs are sure to steal the show at any Halloween gathering. In this post, we’ll guide you through the steps to create these delightful treats that are bound to make your Halloween festivities even more enjoyable.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the preparation, let’s gather all the ingredients required to whip up these Mummy Dogs. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Hot dogs: 8 regular-sized or mini hot dogs, depending on how many Mummy Dogs you wish to make.
  • Puff pastry: 1 sheet of thawed puff pastry, or you can use crescent roll dough if you prefer.
  • Egg wash: 1 egg, beaten, for that golden finish.
  • Mustard or ketchup: For eyes and dipping sauce, depending on your preference.
  • Flour: A little for dusting your work surface.

Preparing the Mummy Dogs

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). This will ensure that your Mummy Dogs bake evenly and achieve that perfect golden color.

Step 2: Roll Out the Dough

On a lightly floured surface, roll out your pastry dough. If you’re using puff pastry, aim for a thickness of about ¼ inch. If you’re using crescent roll dough, press the seams together to create a solid sheet.

Step 3: Cut the Dough

Using a sharp knife or pizza cutter, slice the rolled dough into thin strips. Aim for strips about ½ inch wide. These will serve as the “bandages” for your Mummy Dogs, so try to keep them uniform for an even look.

Step 4: Wrap the Hot Dogs

Take a hot dog and start wrapping it with the strips of dough, leaving a small gap at the top for the “face.” Be creative with the wrapping; you can overlap the strips to give it a more authentic mummy look. Repeat this step for all the hot dogs.

Step 5: Place on a Baking Sheet

Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat to prevent sticking. Carefully place your wrapped Mummy Dogs on the baking sheet, ensuring there is enough space between each one for even baking.

Step 6: Apply the Egg Wash

Using a pastry brush, gently apply the beaten egg over each wrapped hot dog. This will give your Mummy Dogs that beautiful, golden-brown finish once they’re baked.

Step 7: Bake to Perfection

Slide the baking sheet into the preheated oven and bake for about 15-20 minutes, or until the dough is puffed and golden brown. Keep an eye on them to prevent over-browning.

Creating the Spooky Face

While your Mummy Dogs are baking, it’s time to prepare the spooky faces. Once they’re out of the oven and cooled slightly, use mustard or ketchup to create little dots for eyes on each Mummy Dog. You can be as creative as you like with the expressions!

Serving Suggestions

Mummy Dogs make a fantastic Halloween snack, but they can also be served as a main dish at a Halloween party. Consider pairing them with some fun sides to elevate your spooky spread. Here are a few ideas:

  • Monster Mash Potatoes: Whip up some mashed potatoes, and add green food coloring for a ghoulish touch.
  • Pumpkin Veggie Platter: Arrange your favorite vegetables in the shape of a pumpkin, using a bell pepper for the top.
  • Witch’s Brew Punch: Serve a spooky punch made with green apple soda, lime sherbet, and floating eyeball candies for added flair.

Storage Tips

If you happen to have any leftover Mummy Dogs (though we doubt it!), you can store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, simply pop them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 10 minutes or until warmed through.

Why Mummy Dogs Are a Must-Try

Mummy Dogs are not only easy to make but also a versatile option for any Halloween celebration. They can be customized with different types of sausages, such as turkey or vegan alternatives, making them suitable for various dietary preferences. Plus, they’re a fun way to get kids involved in the kitchen, allowing them to help with the wrapping and decorating.

Healthier Alternatives

If you’re looking to make Mummy Dogs a little healthier, consider these substitutions:

  • Whole Wheat Dough: Use whole wheat pastry dough for a healthier crust.
  • Turkey or Chicken Hot Dogs: Swap regular hot dogs for turkey or chicken versions to reduce fat content.
  • Veggie Hot Dogs: For a vegetarian option, use plant-based hot dogs.

Fun Variations

While the classic Mummy Dogs are delightful on their own, you can also experiment with different flavors and fillings. Here are a few fun variations to consider:

  • Cheesy Mummy Dogs: Add a slice of cheese to the hot dog before wrapping it in dough for a gooey surprise.
  • Spicy Mummy Dogs: Use spicy sausages for a kick or add jalapeños to the hot dog for an extra heat level.
  • Sweet Mummy Dogs: For a dessert twist, use sweet dough (like crescent roll) wrapped around chocolate-filled sausages.

Conclusion

Mummy Dogs are the perfect blend of fun, flavor, and festivity, making them an ideal snack for Halloween. With simple ingredients and easy preparation, they can be a crowd-pleaser at your next gathering. Whether you’re hosting a spooky bash or just celebrating with family, these Mummy Dogs will add a fun touch to your Halloween festivities. So roll up your sleeves, gather the family, and create some deliciously spooky Mummy Dogs that will be the highlight of your Halloween spread!

This Halloween, don’t just settle for the ordinary. Get creative, have fun, and enjoy the delightful experience of making and sharing Mummy Dogs. Happy Halloween!
