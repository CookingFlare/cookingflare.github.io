Greek Olive Bread: A Savory and Flavorful Homemade Loaf


Greek cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, aromas, and textures, and its bread is no exception. Greek olive bread, in particular, is a staple in many Greek households, often served as a side dish or used as a base for various sandwiches. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the process of making a delicious and authentic Greek olive bread from scratch.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup chopped fresh Kalamata olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

To begin, combine the warm water and yeast in a large mixing bowl. Let the mixture sit for 5-7 minutes, or until the yeast is activated and frothy.

Next, add the olive oil, salt, and 2 cups of flour to the bowl. Mix the ingredients together until a shaggy dough forms.

Gradually add the remaining 2 cups of flour, one cup at a time, until the dough becomes smooth and elastic. You may not need all of the remaining flour, so add it gradually and be prepared to stop adding flour when the dough reaches the right consistency.

Once the dough is ready, knead it for 10-15 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. You can use a stand mixer with a dough hook attachment or knead the dough by hand.

Form the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl, turning the dough to coat it evenly with oil. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a damp towel and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Punch down the dough and shape it into a long, thin loaf. Place the loaf on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch of space between the loaf and the edge of the sheet.

Brush the top of the loaf with the beaten egg for a golden glaze. If using feta cheese, sprinkle it evenly over the top of the loaf.

Bake the loaf for 35-40 minutes, or until it’s golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. Remove the loaf from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing.

Tips and Variations

  • To enhance the flavor of the bread, use high-quality ingredients, such as freshly harvested olives and a good-quality feta cheese.
  • If you can’t find Kalamata olives, you can substitute them with other types of black olives.
  • To add an extra layer of flavor, sprinkle some chopped fresh herbs, such as oregano or thyme, over the top of the loaf before baking.
  • Greek olive bread is perfect for serving with soups, salads, or as a side dish for grilled meats or vegetables. You can also use it to make sandwiches or toast.

Conclusion

Greek olive bread is a delicious and flavorful homemade loaf that’s perfect for any occasion. With its rich flavors and textures, it’s sure to become a staple in your household. By following this recipe, you’ll be able to create a delicious and authentic Greek olive bread that’s sure to impress your family and friends. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the delicious flavors of Greece in the comfort of your own home.
