Classic Cocktails: How to Make Them Vegan-Friendly


Cocktails have been a staple of social gatherings, fine dining, and celebrations for centuries. Whether you’re hosting a cocktail party or simply enjoying a drink with friends, the variety of flavors and presentation that cocktails offer make them endlessly appealing. However, for those who follow a vegan lifestyle, traditional cocktails can sometimes be a challenge. Many classic drinks contain ingredients like egg whites, honey, and dairy products, making them unsuitable for a vegan diet.

But fear not, because there’s a way to enjoy these timeless cocktails without compromising your values. In this blog post, we’ll explore how to make classic cocktails vegan-friendly by replacing animal-derived ingredients with plant-based alternatives, ensuring everyone can enjoy a delicious drink while staying true to their ethical choices.

Understanding the Challenges of Traditional Cocktails

Before we dive into the process of making your favorite drinks vegan-friendly, it’s essential to understand the challenges posed by certain ingredients in traditional cocktails. Here are some common non-vegan elements you might encounter in classic cocktails:

  1. Egg Whites: Used in cocktails like the Whiskey Sour or Pisco Sour to add a frothy texture and a smooth mouthfeel.
  2. Honey: Often used as a sweetener in cocktails like the Bee’s Knees or as a garnish in others.
  3. Cream or Milk: Found in cocktails like the White Russian or Ramos Gin Fizz.
  4. Gelatin: Some cocktail garnishes, like those in jellied shots, may contain gelatin, which is derived from animal collagen.
  5. Wine Fining Agents: Some wines, used as bases in cocktails, may be processed with fining agents made from animal products.

Now, let’s go over how to swap these ingredients for vegan alternatives so that you can enjoy all the flavor without any ethical concerns.

Vegan-Friendly Alternatives for Classic Cocktail Ingredients

1. Replacing Egg Whites: Aquafaba and Other Alternatives

Egg whites are a common ingredient used in many cocktails to create foam and give the drink a smooth, velvety texture. If you’ve ever sipped on a classic Whiskey Sour, Pisco Sour, or Ramos Gin Fizz, you know that the foamy top is a signature feature. Fortunately, there’s a simple plant-based alternative: aquafaba.

Aquafaba is the viscous liquid found in canned chickpeas or the liquid left over from cooking beans. It has a similar texture and consistency to egg whites, making it an ideal substitute in cocktail recipes. To use aquafaba in place of egg whites, simply shake the liquid in the cocktail shaker for about 15 seconds to create a frothy texture.

Pro Tip: If you prefer a slightly firmer foam, you can also use the liquid from white beans like cannellini beans, as it has a lighter consistency than chickpea aquafaba.

Classic Cocktail Example: Vegan Whiskey Sour

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 oz Bourbon
    • 3/4 oz fresh lemon juice
    • 1/2 oz simple syrup
    • 1 oz aquafaba (chickpea brine)
    • Dash of Angostura bitters (optional)

  • Instructions:

    1. Add all the ingredients (including the aquafaba) to a shaker with ice.
    2. Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds to emulsify and create foam.
    3. Strain into a chilled glass and garnish with a cherry or lemon slice.

This vegan Whiskey Sour retains the iconic frothy texture and flavor of the original but without the egg whites.

2. Replacing Honey: Plant-Based Sweeteners

Honey is a common sweetener used in many classic cocktails, but as it’s produced by bees, it’s not considered vegan-friendly. Luckily, there are several plant-based sweeteners you can use as an alternative. Some great options include:

  • Maple Syrup: This is an excellent substitute for honey and adds a unique, slightly smoky sweetness to your drinks.
  • Agave Syrup: A vegan favorite, agave syrup is a neutral-sweet syrup extracted from the agave plant and is similar to honey in taste and consistency.
  • Date Syrup: If you prefer a more robust, caramel-like sweetness, date syrup is a great option, especially in darker cocktails.

Classic Cocktail Example: Vegan Bee’s Knees

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 oz Gin
    • 3/4 oz fresh lemon juice
    • 1/2 oz maple syrup or agave syrup

  • Instructions:

    1. Add all ingredients to a shaker with ice.
    2. Shake vigorously until chilled.
    3. Strain into a chilled coupe or martini glass and garnish with a lemon twist.

This Vegan Bee’s Knees maintains the floral and citrusy flavors of the original, but with a plant-based sweetener instead of honey.

3. Replacing Dairy: Plant-Based Milks and Creamers

For cocktails that require milk or cream, such as a White Russian or a Ramos Gin Fizz, you have plenty of options for vegan-friendly alternatives. Whether you prefer almond milk, oat milk, or coconut cream, there are many plant-based products available that can mimic the creamy texture and richness of dairy.

  • Almond Milk: A light, neutral-flavored option that works well in cocktails like the White Russian.
  • Oat Milk: A creamier alternative with a subtle sweetness that’s great for richer cocktails like the Ramos Gin Fizz.
  • Coconut Cream: For a more indulgent, tropical feel, coconut cream is perfect in drinks like the Piña Colada or coconut-flavored versions of the White Russian.

Classic Cocktail Example: Vegan White Russian

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 oz Vodka
    • 1 oz coffee liqueur (such as Kahlúa)
    • 1 oz oat milk or almond milk
    • 1 oz coconut cream (optional, for extra richness)

  • Instructions:

    1. Add vodka and coffee liqueur to a glass filled with ice.
    2. Stir to combine.
    3. Pour the plant-based milk and/or coconut cream over the back of a spoon to float it on top.

This Vegan White Russian is as creamy and comforting as the original, but with the benefit of plant-based milks and creams.

4. Finding Vegan Wines and Spirits

It’s important to note that while the majority of spirits (vodka, gin, rum, whiskey, etc.) are vegan-friendly, some wines and liqueurs may not be. Certain wines and spirits are clarified using fining agents that are derived from animals, such as isinglass (fish bladder), gelatin, and egg whites. However, there are plenty of vegan-certified wines and spirits available on the market.

To ensure your cocktail is fully vegan, check the labels for any mention of fining agents or look for wines and spirits that are labeled as “vegan-friendly.” There are also many online resources and apps that can help you identify vegan-friendly alcohol.

Popular Classic Cocktails Made Vegan

Now that you have a better understanding of how to swap out non-vegan ingredients, here are a few classic cocktails that can easily be made vegan-friendly with the right substitutions:

1. Vegan Margarita

The Margarita is a timeless cocktail made with tequila, lime juice, and orange liqueur. The traditional version doesn’t contain any animal products, so it’s naturally vegan. Just be sure to check if your orange liqueur is vegan-friendly, as some brands use animal-derived ingredients for flavoring.

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 oz Tequila
    • 1 oz fresh lime juice
    • 1/2 oz orange liqueur (ensure it’s vegan)
    • Salt for rimming (optional)

  • Instructions:

    1. Shake all ingredients with ice.
    2. Rim your glass with salt (optional).
    3. Strain into the glass and garnish with a lime wedge.

2. Vegan Mojito

The Mojito is a refreshing cocktail made with rum, mint, lime, and sugar. It’s already vegan-friendly, so all you need to do is use a vegan sugar and you’re good to go!

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 oz White rum
    • 1 oz fresh lime juice
    • 2 teaspoons sugar or simple syrup (use vegan sugar)
    • 6-8 fresh mint leaves
    • Soda water

  • Instructions:

    1. Muddle mint leaves and sugar in a glass.
    2. Add lime juice, rum, and ice.
    3. Top with soda water and garnish with a sprig of mint.

3. Vegan Daiquiri

The Daiquiri is a simple yet delicious cocktail made with rum, lime juice, and sugar. Just ensure you’re using a vegan-friendly sweetener, and this cocktail is naturally plant-based.

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 oz White rum
    • 1 oz fresh lime juice
    • 1/2 oz agave syrup or simple syrup

  • Instructions:

    1. Shake all ingredients with ice.
    2. Strain into a chilled coupe glass and garnish with a lime wheel.

Final Thoughts

Making classic cocktails vegan-friendly doesn’t have to be complicated. With a few simple substitutions, you can enjoy all your favorite drinks without compromising on taste or ethics. Whether you’re replacing egg whites with aquafaba, honey with maple syrup, or dairy with plant-based milks, there are plenty of delicious alternatives to ensure that your cocktail experience is inclusive for all.

The next time you’re mixing up a batch of drinks for friends or enjoying a solo night in, try these vegan-friendly versions of your favorite classic cocktails. Cheers to a more ethical, plant-based drinking experience!
