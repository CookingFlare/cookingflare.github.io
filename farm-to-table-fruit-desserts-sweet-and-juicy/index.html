Farm to Table Fruit Desserts: Sweet and Juicy


Are you looking for a delicious and healthy way to satisfy your sweet tooth? Look no further than farm-to-table fruit desserts! These delightful treats are not only bursting with natural sweetness, but they also celebrate the freshest seasonal fruits straight from the farm. In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering fruit dessert recipes that will leave you craving for more. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Strawberry Basil Shortcakes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream, plus extra for brushing
  • Whipped cream, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the sliced strawberries, basil, and honey. Toss gently to coat and set aside.
  2. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  3. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  4. Add the cold cubed butter to the dry ingredients and use a pastry cutter or your fingertips to incorporate the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  5. Gradually pour in the heavy cream, stirring gently until the dough starts to come together.
  6. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently for a minute or two.
  7. Roll out the dough to a thickness of about 1/2 inch and use a round biscuit cutter to cut out shortcakes.
  8. Place the shortcakes on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops with a little extra heavy cream.
  9. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. Allow the shortcakes to cool slightly before assembling.
  11. To serve, split the shortcakes in half and fill them with the strawberry basil mixture. Top with a dollop of whipped cream.

2. Grilled Peaches with Honey and Yogurt

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Greek yogurt, for serving
  • Fresh mint leaves, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Place the peach halves on the grill, cut side down, and cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until grill marks appear.
  3. Flip the peaches and drizzle the cut side with honey. Sprinkle with ground cinnamon.
  4. Grill for an additional 3-4 minutes, or until the peaches are tender.
  5. Remove the peaches from the grill and let them cool slightly.
  6. Serve the grilled peaches with a spoonful of Greek yogurt on top.
  7. Garnish with fresh mint leaves for a burst of freshness.

3. Blueberry Lemon Bars

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a baking dish.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, softened butter, and powdered sugar. Mix until crumbly.
  3. Press the mixture into the bottom of the greased baking dish to form the crust.
  4. Bake the crust for 15 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  5. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and lemon zest.
  6. Add the flour, baking powder, and salt to the egg mixture, and whisk until well combined.
  7. Gently fold in the fresh blueberries.
  8. Pour the blueberry mixture over the baked crust.
  9. Bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the center is set.
  10. Allow the bars to cool completely before cutting into squares.

Conclusion

Farm to table fruit desserts offer a delightful way to enjoy the natural sweetness of fresh fruits. From the succulent strawberries in the strawberry basil shortcakes to the grilled peaches with honey and yogurt, and the tangy blueberry lemon bars, these desserts are not only delicious but also packed with vitamins and nutrients. So, next time you’re in the mood for something sweet, head to your local farmer’s market and pick up some seasonal fruits to create these mouthwatering treats. Your taste buds will thank you!
