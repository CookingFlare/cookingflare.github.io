Baking with Kids: Fun and Easy Pie Recipes


Baking is a delightful way to spend quality time with your children, and what better treat to create together than a delicious pie? Whether it’s a classic apple pie, a creamy custard, or a berry-filled delight, baking pies offers a fantastic opportunity for kids to learn, taste, and unleash their creativity. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of fun and easy pie recipes that you can bake with your kids, ensuring a memorable experience in the kitchen.

Why Bake with Kids?

Baking with children is more than just creating tasty treats; it’s an educational experience that fosters a sense of accomplishment. Here are a few reasons why baking together is beneficial:

  • Develops Math Skills: Measuring ingredients helps kids practice their math skills in a practical setting.
  • Encourages Creativity: Decorating pies allows children to express themselves and experiment with flavors and designs.
  • Teaches Responsibility: Following a recipe teaches kids to follow instructions and be responsible for their tasks.
  • Creates Lasting Memories: The laughter and joy of baking together creates cherished family moments.

Essential Tools for Baking with Kids

Before we dive into the recipes, make sure you have the following baking tools ready:

  • Mixing bowls
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Rolling pin
  • Baking sheets or pie dishes
  • Whisk and spatula
  • Cookie cutter (optional for fun shapes)
  • Aprons to keep the mess at bay!

Fun and Easy Pie Recipes

1. Classic Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 6 to 8 tbsp ice water
  • 6 to 8 apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp butter, to dot

Instructions:

  1. Make the Pie Crust: In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar. Add the butter and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough forms a ball. Divide into two discs, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least one hour.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In another bowl, toss the sliced apples with sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice. Let them sit to release their juices while you prepare the crust.

  3. Roll Out the Dough: On a floured surface, roll out one disc of dough to fit your pie dish. Transfer it to the dish, pressing it into place.

  4. Fill the Pie: Pour the apple mixture into the crust, dotting with butter.

  5. Top it Off: Roll out the second disc and cover the pie. Cut slits in the top to allow steam to escape, and crimp the edges to seal.

  6. Bake: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and bake for 15 minutes. Reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and bake for an additional 35-45 minutes, or until the apples are soft and the crust is golden brown.

  7. Cool and Serve: Let the pie cool before serving. Enjoy a slice with a scoop of ice cream for a special treat!

2. Chocolate Cream Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/3 cup cornstarch
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 2 ¾ cups milk
  • 3 large egg yolks, beaten
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream, for topping

Instructions:

  1. Make the Filling: In a saucepan, mix sugar, cocoa, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually whisk in the milk until smooth. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the mixture thickens and begins to bubble.

  2. Add Egg Yolks: Temper the egg yolks by adding a small amount of the hot mixture to the yolks, whisking continuously. Then, gradually add the egg mixture back to the saucepan. Cook for another 2 minutes.

  3. Finish the Filling: Remove from heat and stir in butter and vanilla. Pour the filling into the pre-baked pie crust.

  4. Chill: Refrigerate for at least four hours until set.

  5. Serve: Top with whipped cream before serving. Enjoy the rich, creamy chocolate goodness!

3. Berry Galette

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
  • 6 to 8 tbsp ice water
  • 2 cups mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp sugar for sprinkling

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Form into a disc, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for an hour.

  2. Prepare the Filling: In a bowl, mix the berries with cornstarch, lemon juice, and sugar. Let it sit while you roll out the dough.

  3. Roll Out the Dough: On a floured surface, roll the dough into a large circle about ¼ inch thick. Transfer it to a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

  4. Assemble the Galette: Spoon the berry mixture into the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border. Fold the edges of the dough over the berries, pleating as you go.

  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake for 30-35 minutes or until the crust is golden brown.

  6. Cool and Serve: Let the galette cool slightly before serving. It’s delicious warm or at room temperature, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream on the side!

4. Mini Pumpkin Pies

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 can (15 oz) pumpkin puree
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • ½ tsp ground ginger
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ¾ cup evaporated milk
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Crust: Roll out the pie crust and cut out circles using a cookie cutter. Press the circles into a muffin tin to form mini pie shells.

  2. Make the Filling: In a bowl, mix pumpkin puree, sugar, salt, and spices. Gradually stir in evaporated milk and eggs until smooth.

  3. Fill the Shells: Pour the pumpkin filling into each mini crust, filling them about ¾ full.

  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C) and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the filling is set.

  5. Cool and Serve: Allow to cool slightly before serving. These mini pies are perfect for little hands to enjoy!

5. No-Bake Cheesecake Pie

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made graham cracker crust
  • 8 oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Fresh fruit or pie filling for topping

Instructions:

  1. Beat the Cream Cheese: In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla, mixing until well combined.

  2. Whip the Cream: In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture until fully incorporated.

  3. Fill the Crust: Spoon the cheesecake filling into the graham cracker crust and smooth the top.

  4. Chill: Refrigerate for at least four hours, or until set.

  5. Serve: Top with fresh fruit or pie filling before serving. This no-bake treat is light and refreshing!

Tips for a Successful Baking Experience

  • Prepare Ingredients Ahead: Measure and prepare all ingredients before starting to make the process smoother.
  • Encourage Participation: Let kids take part in every step, from mixing to decorating.
  • Don’t Rush: Allow ample time for baking and cooling. Enjoy the process without pressure.
  • Clean as You Go: Teach kids to clean up after themselves, making the experience more enjoyable for everyone.

Conclusion

Baking pies with your kids is an enriching experience that combines fun, education, and delicious results. With these easy pie recipes, you can create mouthwatering treats while nurturing a love for cooking in your children. Remember, the goal is not just to bake, but to bond, learn, and create happy memories together. So gather your ingredients, put on those aprons, and get ready for a delightful baking adventure that your family will cherish for years to come!
