Homemade Zaru Soba: Refreshing Buckwheat Noodles


If you’re a fan of Japanese cuisine, you probably know about the delightful and refreshing dish called Zaru Soba. This traditional dish consists of cold buckwheat noodles served with a dipping sauce and various toppings. Today, we’re going to show you how to make this delicious and healthy dish right in the comfort of your own home. So roll up your sleeves and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups buckwheat flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups cold water
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 green onion, finely chopped
  • Optional toppings: nori seaweed, grated daikon radish, wasabi, and thinly sliced green onions

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the buckwheat flour and all-purpose flour. Gradually add the cold water while stirring with chopsticks or a fork until the mixture forms a crumbly texture.

  2. Knead the dough with your hands until it comes together. If the dough feels too dry, add a tablespoon of water at a time until it reaches a smooth and elastic consistency.

  3. Divide the dough into two equal portions and shape each portion into a ball. Dust the dough balls with flour to prevent sticking.

  4. Roll out each dough ball into a thin sheet, about 1/16 inch thick. Dust the work surface and rolling pin with flour as needed to prevent sticking.

  5. Once the dough is rolled out, fold it into thirds like a letter, then cut the folded dough into thin strips, about 1/8 inch wide. Unfold the strips and dust them with flour to prevent sticking.

  6. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the soba noodles and cook them for about 5 minutes, or until they are al dente.

  7. While the noodles are cooking, prepare an ice bath by filling a large bowl with cold water and ice cubes.

  8. Once the noodles are cooked, drain them immediately and transfer them to the ice bath to stop the cooking process. Swirl the noodles around in the ice bath for a minute or two to cool them down completely.

  9. While the noodles are chilling, prepare the dipping sauce by combining the soy sauce, mirin, rice vinegar, sugar, and grated ginger in a small bowl. Stir well until the sugar dissolves.

  10. Once the noodles are cold, drain them from the ice bath and divide them into individual serving portions. Serve the noodles on a bamboo tray or a plate lined with a bamboo mat.

  11. Garnish the noodles with your desired toppings, such as nori seaweed, grated daikon radish, wasabi, and thinly sliced green onions.

  12. To enjoy your Zaru Soba, simply take a small portion of noodles with your chopsticks, dip them into the sauce, and savor the refreshing taste. Feel free to slurp the noodles, as it is considered good manners in Japanese culture.

Zaru Soba is not only a delicious and satisfying dish, but it is also a healthy option. Buckwheat noodles are gluten-free and rich in nutrients like fiber, protein, and minerals. This dish is perfect for hot summer days when you crave something light and refreshing.

So why not give this Homemade Zaru Soba recipe a try? It’s a simple yet flavorful dish that will transport your taste buds to Japan. Enjoy the process of making your own buckwheat noodles and savor the satisfaction of creating a restaurant-worthy meal in your own kitchen.
