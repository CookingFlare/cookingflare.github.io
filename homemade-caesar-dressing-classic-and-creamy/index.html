Homemade Caesar Dressing: Classic and Creamy


Are you tired of store-bought Caesar dressing that lacks flavor and freshness? Why not try making your own homemade Caesar dressing? It’s surprisingly easy, and the results are truly satisfying. In this recipe, we will show you how to make a classic and creamy Caesar dressing that will elevate your salads to a whole new level.

Ingredients

  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, minced
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. In a medium-sized bowl, combine the minced garlic cloves, minced anchovy fillets, Dijon mustard, and freshly squeezed lemon juice. Stir well to combine the ingredients.

  2. Add the mayonnaise and grated Parmesan cheese to the bowl. Mix everything together until well combined. The dressing should start to thicken at this point.

  3. Slowly drizzle in the extra virgin olive oil while continuously whisking the dressing. This will help emulsify the mixture and create a creamy consistency.

  4. Season the dressing with salt and black pepper to taste. Remember that the anchovies already have a salty taste, so go easy on the salt if you prefer a milder flavor.

  5. Transfer the dressing to an airtight container and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. This will also help the dressing thicken further.

Serving Suggestions

Now that you have your homemade Caesar dressing ready, let’s talk about how to use it. Of course, the most classic way to enjoy Caesar dressing is in a Caesar salad. Toss some crisp romaine lettuce leaves, croutons, and shaved Parmesan cheese with the dressing for a delicious and satisfying salad.

But why stop there? This versatile dressing can be used in a variety of ways. Here are a few suggestions to get you started:

  • Use it as a dip for fresh vegetables like carrots, celery, and bell peppers.
  • Drizzle it over grilled chicken or shrimp for an extra burst of flavor.
  • Spread it on sandwiches or wraps for a creamy and tangy kick.
  • Mix it into pasta salads for a unique twist on a classic dish.

Tips and Variations

  • If you’re not a fan of anchovies, you can omit them from the recipe. However, keep in mind that they add a distinct umami flavor that contributes to the authentic Caesar dressing taste.
  • For a lighter version of the dressing, you can substitute Greek yogurt for some or all of the mayonnaise. It will still be creamy and delicious with a fraction of the calories.
  • Feel free to adjust the amount of garlic and lemon juice according to your taste preferences. Some people prefer a stronger garlic flavor, while others enjoy a more citrusy dressing.

Conclusion

Making your own homemade Caesar dressing is a game-changer when it comes to elevating your salads and other dishes. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of effort, you can create a classic and creamy dressing that will impress your friends and family. Give it a try and taste the difference for yourself. Enjoy!
