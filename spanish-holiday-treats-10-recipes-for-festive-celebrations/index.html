Spanish Holiday Treats: 10 Recipes for Festive Celebrations


When the holiday season rolls around, Spanish cuisine offers a treasure trove of festive treats that beautifully blend tradition, flavor, and celebration. Whether you’re hosting a holiday gathering or simply eager to explore the rich culinary heritage of Spain during its most joyful time of year, these ten recipes capture the essence of Spanish festivities. From sweet delights to savory snacks, each recipe carries with it a story and a burst of authentic sabor (flavor) that will transport your taste buds to the heart of Spain’s holiday cheer.

1. Turrón de Jijona (Soft Almond Nougat)

Overview

Turrón de Jijona is a soft, chewy nougat made of finely ground almonds, honey, and sugar. It is a beloved staple during Christmas in Spain, especially popular in the Alicante region.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups raw almonds
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 egg whites
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Zest of one lemon

Instructions

  1. Toast the almonds gently in a pan until golden, then grind finely.
  2. In a saucepan, heat honey and sugar until syrupy, stirring constantly.
  3. Lightly beat egg whites until stiff peaks form.
  4. Gradually add the hot honey syrup to the egg whites, whisking continuously (beware, mixture will be hot).
  5. Add finely ground almonds, cinnamon, and lemon zest. Mix thoroughly.
  6. Pour into a lined mold, press down firmly, and allow to set for 24 hours before slicing.

Tips

Serve alongside coffee or traditional Spanish wine to enhance the flavors.

2. Polvorones (Spanish Almond Shortbread)

Overview

Polvorones, crumbly almond shortbreads dusted with powdered sugar, are an iconic Christmas cookie throughout Spain.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup lard or unsalted butter
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup ground almonds
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Extra powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Toast the flour in a pan over low heat until slightly golden. Allow to cool.
  2. Cream lard or butter with powdered sugar.
  3. Blend in the ground almonds and cinnamon.
  4. Slowly fold in the toasted flour until a dough forms.
  5. Shape into small rounds or ovals and bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Cool, then generously dust with powdered sugar.

Variations

Try adding a pinch of anise seed for a traditional twist.

3. Roscón de Reyes (King’s Cake)

Overview

Roscón de Reyes is a festive ring-shaped sweet bread traditionally enjoyed on January 6th, Three Kings’ Day. The cake is adorned with candied fruits and often hides a small figurine or bean inside for fun.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1/3 cup milk, warmed
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp dry yeast
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, softened
  • Zest of one orange and one lemon
  • 1 tbsp orange blossom water (optional)
  • Candied fruits for decoration
  • Powdered sugar for sprinkling

Instructions

  1. Dissolve yeast in warm milk with a teaspoon of sugar and let activate for 10 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, citrus zest, and eggs. Add activated yeast mixture and knead.
  3. Incorporate softened butter and orange blossom water; knead until a smooth dough forms.
  4. Let dough rise in a warm place for about 2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  5. Shape dough into a large ring and place on a baking tray. Let rise for another hour.
  6. Decorate the top with candied fruits and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 25-30 minutes.
  7. Once cooled, dust with powdered sugar and, if you like, slit horizontally and fill with whipped cream or pastry cream.

Serving Suggestion

Pair with a cup of hot chocolate for a traditional Spanish breakfast.

4. Marzipan Figures (Mazapán)

Overview

Marzipan is a sweet almond paste molded into charming shapes. In Spain, especially Toledo, creating festive marzipan figures is a beloved craft during Christmas.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground almonds
  • 1 ½ cups powdered sugar
  • 1 large egg white
  • Almond extract (optional)
  • Food coloring (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix ground almonds and powdered sugar in a bowl.
  2. Slowly add egg white and almond extract until a pliable dough forms.
  3. Roll out the dough and cut shapes using cookie cutters or mold by hand into fruits, animals, or holiday motifs.
  4. Allow to dry for a few hours or overnight.
  5. Optionally decorate with food coloring or edible paint.

Fun Fact

Marzipan was introduced to Spain by the Moors and has since become integral to Christmas festivities.

5. Magdalenas (Spanish Lemon Muffins)

Overview

Magdalenas are light, fluffy, lemon-scented muffins common in Spanish bakeries and holiday tables.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup milk
  • Zest of one lemon
  • 1 tbsp baking powder

Instructions

  1. Whisk eggs and sugar until creamy and light.
  2. Add lemon zest, milk, and oil, stirring to combine.
  3. Sift flour and baking powder; fold gently into wet ingredients.
  4. Pour batter into muffin molds and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Cool and dust with powdered sugar.

Tip

Magdalenas taste best fresh; enjoy them with café con leche during festive mornings.

6. Churros con Chocolate

Overview

No Spanish holiday celebration is complete without churros fallen into luscious, thick hot chocolate. This traditional treat is a symbol of winter warmth and joy.

Ingredients for churros

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Pinch salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Ingredients for chocolate sauce

  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 4 oz dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

Instructions

  1. Bring water, sugar, and salt to a boil. Add flour all at once and stir vigorously until a smooth dough forms.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan. Using a pastry bag with a star tip, pipe strips of dough into the oil. Fry until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  3. For the chocolate sauce, mix cornstarch with a little milk to make a slurry. Heat remaining milk, add chopped chocolate and sugar, then stir in slurry to thicken.
  4. Serve churros hot, dipped in thick, rich chocolate.

Serving Hint

Churros are perfect for sharing near the fire during the chilly holidays.

7. Crema Catalana (Catalan Cream)

Overview

Similar to crème brûlée, Crema Catalana is a creamy custard with a burnt sugar crust, traditionally flavored with citrus and cinnamon.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups milk
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Peel of one lemon
  • 4 egg yolks
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • Extra sugar for caramelizing

Instructions

  1. Heat milk with cinnamon stick and lemon peel until just boiling; remove from heat and let infuse for 15 minutes.
  2. Whisk egg yolks, sugar, and cornstarch until smooth.
  3. Remove cinnamon and lemon, warm milk again, and gradually pour into yolk mixture, whisking.
  4. Return mixture to the pot and cook on low heat, stirring until thickened.
  5. Pour into ramekins and chill for several hours.
  6. Before serving, sprinkle sugar and caramelize with a kitchen torch until golden and crisp.

Interesting Note

Crema Catalana is served on St. Joseph’s Day but fits perfectly into any holiday spread.

8. Empanadas de atún (Tuna Empanadas)

Overview

Savory turnovers like tuna empanadas offer a delicious contrast to the sweet dishes in Spanish Christmas celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ½ cup warm water
  • 1 can tuna in oil, drained
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 roasted red pepper, chopped
  • 1 hard-boiled egg, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, salt, olive oil, and water to form a dough. Rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Sauté onions until translucent. Mix in tuna, roasted pepper, and egg. Season to taste.
  3. Roll dough thin, cut into circles, place filling and fold over, sealing edges.
  4. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes until golden.

Options

Try substituting the tuna with chorizo or spinach for variety.

9. Mantecados (Traditional Spanish Christmas Cookies)

Overview

Mantecados are crumbly, buttery cookies often flavored with cinnamon or lemon, perfect for holiday gift-giving or afternoon tea.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup lard or butter
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • Zest of one lemon

Instructions

  1. Toast flour lightly and let cool.
  2. Cream lard or butter with sugar, cinnamon, and zest.
  3. Gradually add flour and mix until dough forms.
  4. Roll out, cut into rounds, place on baking tray, bake at 350°F (175°C) for 20 minutes.
  5. Cool and dust with powdered sugar.

Serving Suggestion

Pair with a glass of sweet moscatel wine for an indulgent treat.

10. Sobrasada and Honey Crostini

Overview

A simple yet irresistible appetizer, combining spicy-smooth sobrasada with the sweetness of honey on toasted bread, balancing flavors splendidly for holiday gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 1 baguette, sliced
  • ½ cup sobrasada (spreadable cured sausage)
  • Honey to drizzle
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Toast baguette slices until crisp.
  2. Spread a generous layer of sobrasada on each slice.
  3. Drizzle with honey and garnish with herbs if desired.
  4. Serve immediately.

Why It Works

The salty richness of sobrasada contrasts beautifully with the floral sweetness of honey, a duo beloved in Spanish cuisine.

Wrapping Up the Feast

Spanish holiday treats capture the warmth of tradition, the joy of festivity, and the richness of cultural flavors. From the nutty comfort of turrón to the crispy delight of churros con chocolate, each recipe invites you to create memorable moments around the table. Whether making these for friends, family, or indulging yourself, these Spanish classics offer a gateway to celebrating the holidays with passion and panache.

Enjoy the process, savor every bite, and let these flavors fill your home with Spanish holiday spirit. ¡Felices fiestas!
