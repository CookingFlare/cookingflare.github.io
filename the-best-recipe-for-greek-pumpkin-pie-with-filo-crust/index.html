The Best Recipe for Greek Pumpkin Pie with Filo Crust


Few cuisines evoke the sense of tradition, flavor, and comfort like Greek cooking. Central to many Greek desserts is the delicate balance of sweetness and spice, a combination that can turn simple ingredients into masterpieces. One dessert that perfectly embodies this magic is the Greek Pumpkin Pie with Filo Crust – a lesser-known gem that deserves a spotlight at your kitchen table. This dish is a delightful blend of earthy pumpkin, warm spices, crunchy filo pastry, and a subtle hint of sweetness, making it the ideal dessert for autumn gatherings, holiday festivities, or even a treat with your afternoon coffee.

In this blog post, you’ll learn how to make the best Greek-style pumpkin pie, complete with detailed instructions, insider tips, and all the details you need to perfect this dish. Whether you’re accustomed to working with filo pastry or trying it out for the first time, this recipe will guide you through every step to achieve flaky, golden layers and a rich, flavorful filling.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Filling:

  • 2 cups cooked pumpkin puree (fresh or canned)
  • ⅓ cup sugar (or adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons honey (preferably Greek honey for authenticity)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ⅓ cup fine semolina
  • ⅓ cup raisins (optional, for bursts of sweetness)
  • ⅓ cup chopped walnuts or almonds (optional, for added texture)
  • 4 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • Zest of 1 orange (essential for a citrusy undertone)

For the Filo Crust:

  • 8-10 sheets of filo pastry, thawed if frozen
  • ⅓ cup melted butter (for brushing filo sheets)
  • ⅓ cup olive oil (for extra flavor and layering)

To Finish:

  • Powdered sugar, for dusting
  • Ground cinnamon, for a final warm touch

Step-by-Step Instructions

Preparing the Pumpkin Filling

  1. Start with the Pumpkin: If you’re using fresh pumpkin, peel and cube it before boiling or steaming until tender. Then mash or blend it into a smooth puree. For convenience, canned pumpkin puree works perfectly fine, but make sure it’s unsweetened.

  2. Create the Flavor Base: In a medium saucepan, combine the pumpkin puree, sugar, and honey over medium heat. Stir until the sugar fully dissolves, creating a silky mixture.

  3. Add Warm Spices: Stir in the cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. These spices are quintessential in Greek desserts, adding depth and complexity to the filling.

  4. Thicken with Semolina: Gradually add the fine semolina, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Semolina not only thickens the filling but also gives it a slightly grainy texture that complements the smoothness of the pumpkin.

  5. Incorporate Texture: Fold in the raisins and chopped nuts, if you’re using them. These optional ingredients enhance the pie’s texture and balance the sweetness beautifully.

  6. Orange Zest and Eggs: Mix in the orange zest for brightness, then slowly whisk in the beaten eggs. The eggs will lend structure and richness to the filling.

  7. Cool Down: Remove the filling from the heat and let it cool completely before assembling the pie.

Working with Filo Pastry

  1. Prepare Your Workstation: Lay out your filo sheets flat and cover them with a damp kitchen towel to prevent them from drying out during assembly. Filo pastry, known for its paper-thin layers, becomes brittle quickly when exposed to air.

  2. Grease Your Pan: Choose a round or rectangular baking dish approximately 9 inches wide. Brush the bottom and sides with a mix of melted butter and olive oil to ensure the pastry doesn’t stick.

  3. Layer the Filo Sheets: Begin layering the filo sheets in the pan, brushing each sheet generously with the butter-olive oil mixture before adding the next. Aim for 4-6 sheets at the bottom to create a sturdy crust.

  4. Add the Filling: Spread the cooled pumpkin filling evenly over the filo base, smoothing it out to ensure a consistent thickness.

  5. Top with Filo Layers: Layer another 4-6 sheets of filo on top of the filling, brushing each sheet with butter and oil as you go.

  6. Seal the Edges: Tuck in the excess pastry edges to form a neat, enclosed pie. Brush the top layer generously with more butter to achieve that golden finish.

Baking the Pumpkin Pie

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Greek desserts often rely on a moderate baking temperature to achieve even baking without premature browning.

  2. Bake to Perfection: Place the pan in the oven and bake for 40-50 minutes or until the filo crust is crisp and golden brown. The filling should set, but it may jiggle slightly in the center – it will firm up as it cools.

  3. Cool and Serve: Allow the pie to cool in the pan for 30 minutes before slicing. Greek pumpkin pie is traditionally served warm or at room temperature, making it an irresistible treat during cooler months.

Presentation and Serving Tips

  1. Dust with Powdered Sugar: Before serving, lightly dust the pie with powdered sugar to add a touch of sweetness and elegance.

  2. Sprinkle Cinnamon: Garnish with a sprinkling of ground cinnamon for an extra layer of flavor that ties in with the warm spices of the filling.

  3. Accompaniments: Pair this pie with a dollop of Greek yogurt or a scoop of vanilla ice cream to balance the richness of the pumpkin filling. Alternatively, serve it alongside a strong brewed Greek coffee for an authentic Mediterranean experience.

Tips for Success

  1. Work Quickly with Filo: Filo pastry dries out rapidly, so assemble the pie efficiently. Keeping the sheets covered and buttered will ensure their pliability and prevent tearing.

  2. Customize the Sweetness: Taste the pumpkin filling before adding it to the pie, adjusting the sugar and honey levels according to your preference. Greek sweets are often lightly sweetened to allow the natural flavors to shine.

  3. Use Quality Ingredients: For maximum authenticity, opt for Greek honey, fresh spices, and high-quality olive oil. These staples of Greek cooking elevate the dish to another level.

  4. Experiment with Nuts and Raisins: If you’re serving a crowd, consider dividing the filling into halves – one with nuts and raisins, and one without – to suit varying tastes.

The Perfect Seasonal Indulgence

Greek Pumpkin Pie with Filo Crust is a dessert that beautifully bridges the gap between tradition and innovation. The flaky, buttery layers of filo pastry encasing the warmly spiced pumpkin filling offer a symphony of textures and flavors, making every bite a celebration of the season. Whether you’re exploring Greek cuisine for the first time or are a seasoned enthusiast, this recipe promises to become a favorite for years to come.

Gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and create a dessert that’s as delicious as it is memorable. This Greek pumpkin pie is more than a recipe; it’s a culinary experience steeped in tradition, perfect for sharing with loved ones. Happy baking!
