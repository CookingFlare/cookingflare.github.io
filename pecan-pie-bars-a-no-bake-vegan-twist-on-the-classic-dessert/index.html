Pecan Pie Bars: A No-Bake Vegan Twist on the Classic Dessert


If you’re a fan of pecan pie but looking for a healthier and easier alternative, then these Pecan Pie Bars are the perfect treat for you! This no-bake, vegan twist on the classic dessert is not only delicious but also incredibly simple to make. With just a few wholesome ingredients, you can indulge in the rich and nutty flavors of pecan pie in a convenient bar form. So, let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of dates, pitted
  • 1 cup of almonds
  • 1 cup of pecans, roughly chopped
  • 1/2 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup of coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sea salt

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, blend the dates and almonds until they form a sticky and crumbly mixture. This will serve as the base for our pecan pie bars.
  2. Line a square baking dish with parchment paper, ensuring that the paper extends over the edges for easy removal later.
  3. Press the date and almond mixture firmly into the bottom of the baking dish, creating an even layer. Use the back of a spoon or your hands to pack it down tightly.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine the chopped pecans, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, vanilla extract, and sea salt. Stir well until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  5. Pour the pecan mixture over the date and almond base, spreading it out evenly with a spatula.
  6. Place the baking dish in the refrigerator and allow the bars to set for at least 2 hours, or until firm.
  7. Once set, remove the bars from the baking dish by lifting the parchment paper. Place them on a cutting board and slice into squares or rectangles.
  8. Serve and enjoy immediately, or store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

Variations:

  • For an extra indulgent twist, drizzle some melted dark chocolate over the top of the pecan pie bars before refrigerating them. The chocolate will add a delightful richness to the already delicious bars.
  • If you prefer a sweeter flavor, you can add a tablespoon of pure maple syrup to the date and almond base mixture.

Health Benefits:

These Pecan Pie Bars not only satisfy your sweet tooth, but they also offer some health benefits. Pecans are packed with antioxidants and healthy fats that promote heart health and reduce inflammation. Dates provide natural sweetness and are a great source of fiber, potassium, and magnesium. Almonds offer a good dose of protein, vitamin E, and healthy fats. By choosing these wholesome ingredients, you can enjoy a guilt-free dessert that nourishes your body.

Final Thoughts:

Pecan Pie Bars are a delightful and healthier alternative to traditional pecan pie. This no-bake, vegan twist allows you to enjoy the flavors of the classic dessert without the hassle of baking. Whether you’re following a vegan lifestyle, looking for a quick and easy dessert, or simply want to try something different, these bars are sure to satisfy your cravings. Give this recipe a try and indulge in the rich, nutty, and sweet goodness of Pecan Pie Bars today!

Remember to share this recipe with your friends and family who appreciate delicious vegan desserts. They’ll thank you for introducing them to this delectable treat. Enjoy!
