Classic French Toast Recipes: Sweet and Savory Variations


French toast, often referred to as “eggy bread” in many parts of the world, is a beloved breakfast dish that has stood the test of time. It is a versatile recipe that can easily be adapted to suit any taste, whether you’re in the mood for something sweet, savory, or even a bit of both. With its crispy exterior and soft, custard-like interior, French toast can be enjoyed at any time of day, from breakfast to brunch, or even as a late-night snack. This guide will take you through a variety of sweet and savory variations of the classic French toast, along with helpful tips and tricks to perfect your dish every time.

What is French Toast?

At its core, French toast is made by soaking slices of bread in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and flavorings like vanilla or cinnamon, then frying it in butter or oil until golden brown. The result is a delicious, crispy texture on the outside and a tender, flavorful interior. The bread used for French toast is typically thicker and heartier than your average loaf, often brioche, challah, or a thick-cut sourdough, which allows it to absorb the egg mixture without falling apart.

While French toast may have originated in Europe centuries ago, it has become an international comfort food loved by people of all ages. Its simplicity makes it the perfect canvas for a wide range of toppings and fillings, allowing you to customize your French toast experience to your liking.

Classic French Toast Recipe

Before diving into all the delicious variations, let’s start with a classic French toast recipe that forms the foundation for many of these ideas.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk (or your preferred milk alternative)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 4 slices of thick bread (brioche, challah, or Texas toast work best)
  • 2 tablespoons butter, for frying
  • Maple syrup, powdered sugar, or your favorite toppings

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Egg Mixture: In a shallow bowl or a wide dish, whisk together the eggs, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt until the mixture is smooth and well-combined.
  2. Heat the Pan: Place a large skillet or griddle on medium heat and add 1 tablespoon of butter. Allow the butter to melt and start bubbling but not brown.
  3. Soak the Bread: Dip each slice of bread into the egg mixture, allowing it to soak for about 10-15 seconds on each side. Be sure the bread is well-coated but not soggy.
  4. Cook the French Toast: Place the soaked bread slices on the hot skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy. If necessary, add more butter to the pan for additional slices.
  5. Serve: Once cooked, remove the French toast from the pan and place it on a serving plate. Top with your favorite toppings like maple syrup, powdered sugar, fresh berries, whipped cream, or even a dusting of cinnamon.

Now that we’ve covered the basics, let’s explore some variations of French toast that will take your taste buds on a journey.

Sweet French Toast Variations

French toast is often associated with a sweet, indulgent breakfast, and there are countless ways to take the classic recipe to new heights. Whether you prefer fruits, syrups, or a more decadent touch, these sweet variations will add variety and excitement to your morning routine.

1. Berry-Infused French Toast

For a fruity twist on the traditional French toast, fresh berries like strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, or blackberries can be added to the batter, or simply used as a topping.

How to Make It:

  • Prepare the classic French toast base.
  • In a separate bowl, mash a handful of berries (such as strawberries or blueberries) and fold them into the egg mixture. Alternatively, you can cook the berries in a small pan with a bit of sugar until they form a syrup-like consistency.
  • Once the French toast is cooked, serve it topped with additional fresh berries, a drizzle of honey, and a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat.

2. Banana Bread French Toast

This variation uses thick slices of banana bread instead of traditional loaf bread for a warm, comforting twist. The banana flavor enhances the sweetness of the French toast, making it perfect for breakfast or brunch.

How to Make It:

  • Replace your standard bread with thick slices of homemade or store-bought banana bread.
  • Soak the banana bread in the classic egg mixture and cook as you would traditional French toast.
  • Serve with caramelized banana slices, a sprinkle of cinnamon, and a drizzle of maple syrup for an irresistible, sweet breakfast.

3. Nutella-Stuffed French Toast

Nutella lovers, this one’s for you! For a rich, gooey French toast experience, add a layer of Nutella between two slices of bread before cooking.

How to Make It:

  • Spread Nutella generously on one slice of bread and place another slice on top, creating a sandwich.
  • Dip the sandwich into the egg mixture and cook in butter, as you would for regular French toast.
  • Top with powdered sugar, sliced bananas, and a drizzle of Nutella for a decadent breakfast or dessert.

4. Cinnamon Roll French Toast

If you love cinnamon rolls, this French toast version is sure to be a hit. By using cinnamon rolls as the base, you get the same rich cinnamon flavor but with the added texture and crispiness that only French toast can offer.

How to Make It:

  • Cut cinnamon rolls into thick slices, about 1-inch thick.
  • Dip them into the classic egg mixture and cook as you would French toast.
  • Drizzle with icing, and garnish with a sprinkle of cinnamon and chopped pecans for a satisfying treat.

5. French Toast with Caramelized Apples

Caramelized apples bring a touch of elegance to your classic French toast. The sweet, buttery apples complement the custardy bread, creating a perfect balance of flavors.

How to Make It:

  • Peel and slice apples, then sauté them in a skillet with butter, brown sugar, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt until softened and caramelized.
  • Prepare the French toast as usual and top with the caramelized apples, a drizzle of maple syrup, and a sprinkle of chopped pecans for extra crunch.

Savory French Toast Variations

While French toast is often associated with sweetness, it can easily be adapted to savory flavors. Savory French toast offers a delightful contrast to its sweeter counterpart and is perfect for those who prefer a more filling, umami-packed meal.

1. Avocado and Poached Egg French Toast

For a modern twist on savory French toast, avocado and poached eggs make the perfect topping. The creamy avocado and rich egg yolk combine beautifully with the crisp French toast.

How to Make It:

  • Prepare the classic French toast and cook it until crispy.
  • Top each slice with mashed avocado, a poached egg, and a sprinkle of red pepper flakes and salt.
  • You can also add sautéed spinach or a drizzle of olive oil for extra flavor.

2. Bacon and Cheddar French Toast

This is a savory French toast that’s perfect for breakfast or brunch. Crispy bacon and melted cheddar cheese create an irresistible combination.

How to Make It:

  • Cook bacon until crispy, then chop into small pieces.
  • After soaking the bread in the egg mixture, place small pieces of bacon and shredded cheddar cheese between two slices of bread before cooking.
  • Serve with a side of fresh tomato slices or a simple salad for a balanced meal.

3. Mushroom and Gruyère French Toast

Mushrooms and Gruyère cheese are a classic pairing that brings earthy, savory flavors to your French toast. This variation is rich and satisfying, making it an excellent choice for a cozy brunch.

How to Make It:

  • Sauté sliced mushrooms in butter with garlic, thyme, and a pinch of salt until softened and browned.
  • Prepare the classic French toast base and layer the cooked mushrooms and shredded Gruyère cheese between two slices of bread.
  • Cook the sandwich and serve with a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

4. Tomato and Basil French Toast

For a lighter, Mediterranean-inspired take on French toast, try pairing fresh tomatoes and basil with a touch of mozzarella cheese.

How to Make It:

  • Spread a thin layer of ricotta cheese on one slice of bread before soaking it in the egg mixture.
  • Add slices of fresh tomato, mozzarella, and basil leaves.
  • Cook the French toast and serve with a drizzle of olive oil and balsamic vinegar for a delightful, savory meal.

5. Ham and Swiss French Toast

This version is similar to a Croque Monsieur but in the form of French toast. The combination of ham and Swiss cheese creates a savory, cheesy filling that’s perfect for brunch.

How to Make It:

  • Layer slices of ham and Swiss cheese between two slices of bread.
  • Dip in the classic egg mixture and cook in butter until golden brown.
  • Serve with a simple green salad or sautéed spinach for a delicious, savory dish.

Tips for Perfect French Toast Every Time

  • Choose the Right Bread: The bread is key to a perfect French toast. Go for thick, sturdy slices of brioche, challah, or sourdough. These types of bread soak up the egg mixture well without falling apart.
  • Let the Bread Soak: Don’t rush the soaking process. Let the bread soak in the egg mixture for 10-15 seconds on each side to ensure a custardy texture in the center.
  • Use a Nonstick Skillet: A nonstick skillet helps to ensure the French toast doesn’t stick and makes flipping easier. It also allows you to use less butter or oil if you prefer a lighter option.
  • Don’t Overcrowd the Pan: Make sure there is enough space between the slices of French toast in the pan. Overcrowding can cause them to steam rather than crisp up.

Conclusion

Whether you prefer your French toast sweet, savory, or somewhere in between, these variations allow you to explore endless possibilities. From indulgent Nutella-stuffed French toast to savory ham and Swiss creations, the options are as diverse as they are delicious. Try these recipes at home and experiment with your own toppings and fillings to create the perfect French toast dish that suits your taste. Happy cooking!
