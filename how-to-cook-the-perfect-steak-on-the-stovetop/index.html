How to Cook the Perfect Steak on the Stovetop


Cooking a perfect steak can seem like an intimidating task, but with the right approach, it’s easy to achieve restaurant-quality results right at home. The stovetop method is one of the simplest and most effective ways to cook a steak to perfection. Whether you prefer a juicy ribeye, a tender filet mignon, or a flavorful New York strip, the stovetop method offers a straightforward way to cook steak while allowing you to control the temperature and flavor.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the essential steps to cooking the perfect steak on the stovetop, from selecting the right cut to mastering cooking techniques. With a few tips and tricks, you’ll be able to impress your friends, family, or yourself with a steak that’s perfectly seared on the outside and tender on the inside.

Choosing the Right Steak

The first step to cooking the perfect steak is choosing the right cut. The type of steak you choose can have a big impact on the final result, so it’s important to select a steak that fits your preferences.

Popular Cuts for Stovetop Cooking:

  • Ribeye: Known for its rich marbling and tenderness, the ribeye is a favorite for stovetop cooking. Its fat content ensures a juicy and flavorful steak.
  • New York Strip: A slightly leaner option compared to the ribeye, the New York strip still delivers a delicious flavor and a tender texture. It’s a great choice if you’re looking for a steak with a bit of bite.
  • Filet Mignon: This cut is prized for its melt-in-your-mouth tenderness. While it doesn’t have as much marbling as a ribeye, it offers a mild, delicate flavor.
  • Sirloin: Leaner than the ribeye or strip, sirloin is a great budget-friendly option that can still deliver great flavor and texture with proper cooking techniques.

When selecting your steak, be sure to choose a cut that’s at least 1-inch thick for the best results. Thicker cuts allow for a better sear while also ensuring the steak cooks evenly.

Preparing the Steak

Before you begin cooking, it’s crucial to properly prepare the steak. Follow these steps to ensure the best possible outcome:

  1. Remove the Steak from the Fridge: Let your steak come to room temperature for about 30 minutes before cooking. This ensures even cooking, preventing the outside from overcooking while the inside remains underdone.

  2. Pat the Steak Dry: Using a paper towel, gently pat the surface of the steak to remove any excess moisture. This step is key to achieving a crispy, golden-brown crust when searing the steak.

  3. Season the Steak: Generously season both sides of the steak with salt and freshly ground black pepper. You can also experiment with other seasonings like garlic powder, onion powder, or fresh herbs such as thyme or rosemary. If you want a simple but effective flavor profile, salt and pepper are usually enough.

  4. Preheat Your Pan: Choose a heavy-bottomed skillet or cast iron pan, which will provide even heat distribution and help create a crisp sear. Preheat the pan on medium-high heat for about 5 minutes before cooking.

Cooking the Steak

Once your steak is properly seasoned and your pan is heated, it’s time to start cooking. Follow these steps to achieve a perfectly seared steak on the stovetop.

Step 1: Sear the Steak

Place the steak in the preheated pan. You should hear a satisfying sizzle as it hits the surface. Don’t move the steak around; let it sear undisturbed for the first few minutes. This ensures a flavorful crust forms.

  • For a 1-inch steak, sear the first side for about 3-4 minutes.
  • For a 1.5-inch steak, sear the first side for about 5 minutes.

During this time, resist the urge to flip or press down on the steak. Pressing on the steak releases juices, which can dry out the meat. Let the steak cook until a deep brown crust forms on the bottom.

Step 2: Flip and Sear the Other Side

Once the first side is perfectly seared, flip the steak over using tongs or a spatula. Again, let the steak sear without moving it for the same amount of time as the first side (about 3-5 minutes, depending on thickness).

Step 3: Add Butter and Aromatics (Optional)

For an extra layer of flavor, you can add butter, garlic, and fresh herbs to the pan after flipping the steak. Place 1-2 tablespoons of butter in the pan and allow it to melt. As the butter melts, use a spoon to baste the steak with the melted butter. This infuses the steak with rich, savory flavors.

Add a couple of garlic cloves (smashed with the side of a knife) and a few sprigs of fresh rosemary or thyme to the pan. These aromatics will enhance the flavor of the steak without overwhelming it.

Step 4: Check the Steak’s Internal Temperature

At this point, the steak should be well on its way to being perfectly cooked. To ensure it reaches your desired level of doneness, you’ll need to check the internal temperature using a meat thermometer.

  • Rare: 120-125°F (49-52°C)
  • Medium Rare: 130-135°F (54-57°C)
  • Medium: 140-145°F (60-63°C)
  • Medium Well: 150-155°F (66-68°C)
  • Well Done: 160°F (71°C) and above

Remember that the steak will continue to cook a little after you remove it from the pan due to residual heat, so it’s a good idea to remove it when it’s about 5°F below your desired temperature.

Step 5: Rest the Steak

Once the steak reaches the perfect temperature, remove it from the pan and place it on a cutting board or plate. Let the steak rest for about 5-10 minutes. Resting allows the juices to redistribute within the meat, making it more tender and flavorful when you cut into it.

Serving the Steak

After resting, your steak is ready to be sliced and served. If you like, you can drizzle the remaining butter and juices from the pan over the steak for added flavor. Depending on your preferences, you can serve your steak with a variety of side dishes such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a simple salad.

For an extra touch, you can also top your steak with a pat of compound butter (butter mixed with garlic, herbs, or spices) or a sauce like béarnaise or chimichurri.

Tips for Perfecting Your Steak

  1. Use the Right Heat: Cooking a steak on the stovetop requires a good balance of high heat to sear the meat and moderate heat to cook it through. If your pan is too hot, you risk burning the outside before the inside is cooked. If the heat is too low, the steak may not sear properly.

  2. Avoid Overcrowding the Pan: If you’re cooking multiple steaks, it’s important not to overcrowd the pan. This can lower the temperature of the pan, causing the steaks to cook unevenly and preventing them from getting a nice sear.

  3. Rest Your Steak: Don’t skip the resting step. Letting the steak rest allows the juices to redistribute throughout the meat, making it more flavorful and tender.

  4. Experiment with Flavors: While salt and pepper are classic, don’t be afraid to experiment with other seasonings. Try using a steak rub or adding a splash of wine or balsamic vinegar to the pan for an extra burst of flavor.

  5. Use a Meat Thermometer: The most reliable way to ensure your steak is cooked to your desired level of doneness is by using a meat thermometer. This will take the guesswork out of cooking and ensure a perfectly cooked steak every time.

Conclusion

Cooking the perfect steak on the stovetop is all about technique and practice. By selecting the right cut, properly seasoning the meat, and following the right cooking steps, you can enjoy a steak that’s beautifully seared on the outside and tender and juicy on the inside. With these simple tips, you’ll be able to cook steak like a pro in the comfort of your own kitchen. So, fire up your stovetop, grab your favorite cut of steak, and get ready to enjoy an unforgettable meal.
