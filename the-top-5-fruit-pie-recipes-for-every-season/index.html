The Top 5 Fruit Pie Recipes for Every Season


When it comes to desserts, few things can rival the comforting embrace of a homemade fruit pie. With their flaky crusts and juicy fillings, fruit pies are a delightful way to celebrate seasonal produce throughout the year. In this blog post, we’ll explore the top five fruit pie recipes that are perfect for every season. Whether you’re savoring the sweetness of summer berries or the warm spices of fall apples, these recipes will inspire you to bake and create delicious memories.

Spring: Strawberry Rhubarb Pie

As spring unfolds, the vibrant colors of strawberries and rhubarb emerge, making them a perfect pairing for a classic pie. The tartness of rhubarb complements the sweetness of strawberries, creating a harmonious balance that’s simply irresistible.

Ingredients

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
    • 2 cups rhubarb, chopped
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust: In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar. Cut in the chilled butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in ice water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough comes together. Divide the dough in half, form into discs, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least one hour.

  2. Make the filling: In a large bowl, combine strawberries, rhubarb, sugar, cornstarch, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and cinnamon. Toss until the fruit is evenly coated.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disc of dough to fit a 9-inch pie pan. Place the rolled dough into the pie pan and fill with the fruit mixture. Roll out the second disc and place it over the filling. Cut slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape, and crimp the edges to seal.

  4. Bake the pie: Bake in the preheated oven for 15 minutes. Reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for an additional 35 to 45 minutes, or until the filling is bubbly and the crust is golden brown. Allow to cool before serving.

Summer: Blueberry Peach Pie

Summer brings an abundance of juicy fruits, and the combination of blueberries and peaches creates a pie that’s bursting with flavor. This pie is not only visually stunning but also a refreshing dessert for warm days.

Ingredients

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups fresh blueberries
    • 2 cups fresh peaches, peeled and sliced
    • ¾ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • ½ teaspoon nutmeg (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust: Follow the same steps as the strawberry rhubarb pie to prepare the crust.

  2. Make the filling: In a large bowl, combine blueberries, peaches, sugar, cornstarch, vanilla extract, lemon juice, and nutmeg. Toss gently to combine.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disc of dough into a 9-inch pie pan. Fill with the blueberry-peach mixture. Roll out the second disc and place it over the filling. Cut slits in the top crust and crimp the edges.

  4. Bake the pie: Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F (220°C), then reduce to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 35 to 45 minutes until bubbly and golden. Let cool before serving.

Fall: Classic Apple Pie

No list of fruit pies would be complete without the quintessential apple pie. The warm spices and sweet-tart filling make it a beloved fall dessert that’s perfect for any gathering.

Ingredients

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 6 to 8 medium apples (Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or a mix), peeled and sliced
    • ¾ cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
    • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 tablespoon butter, diced

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust: As before, prepare the dough for the pie crust.

  2. Make the filling: In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are well coated.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disc of dough into a 9-inch pie pan. Fill with the apple mixture and dot with butter. Roll out the second disc, place over the filling, cut slits, and crimp the edges.

  4. Bake the pie: Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F (220°C), then reduce to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 45 to 55 minutes until the apples are tender and the crust is golden. Allow cooling before slicing.

Winter: Cranberry Orange Pie

As the holiday season approaches, cranberries become a staple ingredient in many kitchens. This cranberry orange pie is a festive treat that brightens up the winter months with its tart and citrusy flavors.

Ingredients

  • For the crust:

    • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
    • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and diced
    • 6 to 8 tablespoons ice water

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups fresh or frozen cranberries
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
    • 1 tablespoon orange zest
    • 1 tablespoon fresh orange juice
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • ½ teaspoon ground ginger (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust: Follow the same steps for preparing the crust.

  2. Make the filling: In a medium saucepan, combine cranberries, sugar, cornstarch, orange zest, orange juice, vanilla extract, and ginger. Cook over medium heat until the cranberries begin to soften and the mixture thickens, about 10 minutes.

  3. Assemble the pie: Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out one disc of dough into a 9-inch pie pan. Pour the cranberry mixture into the crust. Roll out the second disc, place it over the filling, cut slits, and crimp the edges.

  4. Bake the pie: Bake for 15 minutes at 425°F (220°C), then reduce to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 35 to 40 minutes until bubbly and golden. Let cool before serving.

Conclusion

No matter the season, fruit pies offer a delicious way to enjoy the bounty of nature. From the fresh flavors of spring strawberries and rhubarb to the warm spices of fall apple pie, each recipe tells a story and invites us to gather around the table with loved ones. So, roll up your sleeves, preheat that oven, and indulge in the joy of baking these top five fruit pie recipes throughout the year. Enjoy each slice, and share the warmth of homemade pies with family and friends. Happy baking!
