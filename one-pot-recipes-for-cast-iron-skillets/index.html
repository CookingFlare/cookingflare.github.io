One-Pot Recipes for Cast Iron Skillets


When it comes to versatility in the kitchen, few tools rival the cast iron skillet. Its seamless transition from stovetop to oven makes it a favorite among home cooks. The durability, heat retention, and naturally non-stick surface also make it a prime candidate for one-pot meals. These recipes minimize cleanup without sacrificing flavor, creativity, or nutritional value, making weeknight dinners a breeze. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or feeding a crowd, a single skillet is all you need to whip up satisfying dishes in no time.

Here are several recipes you’ll want to keep in rotation. Each one highlights the efficiency and adaptability of a cast iron skillet, proving that simplicity can lead to extraordinary flavors.

Skillet Chicken Pot Pie

Savory, comforting, and loaded with goodness, chicken pot pie gets even better when prepared in a cast iron skillet. This recipe offers all the creamy richness of the classic dish without the need for multiple pans.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 store-bought puff pastry, thawed or homemade pastry
  • 1 egg, beaten (for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Melt the butter in your cast iron skillet over medium heat. Add the diced chicken, cooking until no longer pink. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté onions and carrots until softened. Stir in the frozen peas and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables, stirring to combine. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, whisking continuously to prevent lumps.
  5. Add the cooked chicken back into the skillet. Stir in thyme, salt, and pepper, allowing the sauce to thicken for about 5–6 minutes.
  6. Lay the puff pastry over the skillet, tucking the edges in. Brush the top with beaten egg for a golden crust.
  7. Bake for 20–25 minutes, or until the pastry is flaky and golden. Serve warm, directly from the skillet.

Cast Iron Skillet Lasagna

Lasagna is a comfort food classic, but with a cast iron skillet, it becomes a streamlined dish that skips the need for multiple pots and pans.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pound ground beef or Italian sausage
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 6 no-boil lasagna noodles
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Heat olive oil in the cast iron skillet and cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Stir in marinara sauce, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Let simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Remove half of the meat sauce and set aside. Arrange a layer of lasagna noodles over the remaining sauce in the skillet.
  5. Spread a layer of ricotta cheese over the noodles, followed by a layer of meat sauce. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  6. Cover the skillet tightly with foil and bake for 20 minutes. Uncover and bake for another 10 minutes, allowing the cheese to melt and bubble.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil before serving directly from the skillet.

Skillet Shrimp and Grits

A Southern favorite, shrimp and grits come together effortlessly in a cast iron skillet. This recipe balances creamy, cheesy grits with flavorful shrimp, yielding a hearty and satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup stone-ground grits
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 cup sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 slices bacon, chopped
  • 1/2 cup diced green onions
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Bring water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Slowly whisk in grits, reducing heat to a simmer. Cook for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.
  2. Stir in cheddar cheese and butter. Keep warm while preparing the shrimp.
  3. In the cast iron skillet, cook the bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove bacon pieces and reserve for garnish, leaving the drippings in the skillet.
  4. Toss shrimp with paprika, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Sauté in the skillet for 2–3 minutes on each side until pink and cooked through.
  5. Add green onions and lemon juice to the skillet, stirring to combine.
  6. Plate the cheesy grits and top with shrimp mixture. Garnish with crispy bacon pieces.

Skillet Beef Stroganoff

Beef stroganoff transforms into a simple one-pot dinner using the reliable cast iron skillet. This hearty dish is perfect for busy nights when you crave something filling without the mess.

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 pound beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 8 ounces egg noodles
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in the cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. Sear beef sirloin slices on both sides until browned. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  2. Reduce heat to medium. Add mushrooms, onions, and garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Pour in beef broth and stir in Worcestershire sauce. Bring to a simmer and add the egg noodles, ensuring they’re submerged in the liquid.
  4. Cover the skillet and cook for 8–10 minutes until the noodles are tender and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Stir in sour cream and add the seared beef back into the skillet, heating through.
  6. Garnish with parsley before serving.

Why Cast Iron Skillets are Perfect for One-Pot Meals

One-pot recipes have become a hallmark of efficient cooking, and cast iron skillets are the ideal vessel for creating them. Their even heat distribution ensures that proteins, vegetables, and grains cook uniformly, while their ability to retain heat allows dishes to stay warm when served directly at the table. Moreover, the skillet’s durability means it can handle high temperatures, long simmer times, and all manner of ingredients without wear or damage.

Cleanup is a simple wipe-down or rinse, and with proper seasoning, your cast iron skillet will only improve over time. Its multifunctionality streamlines your cooking process, making it indispensable for any home kitchen.

By combining the practicality of one-pot meals with the strength of cast iron cookware, these recipes strike the perfect balance of convenience and flavor. Keep experimenting with your skillet, and you’ll find endless opportunities to simplify weeknight cooking while still wowing your taste buds.
