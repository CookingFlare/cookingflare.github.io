7 Delicious Paleo Recipes for the Grill


Grilling season is one of the most exciting culinary times of the year. The smoky char, sizzle of the flames, and fresh outdoor air set the perfect stage for irresistible meals. If you’re following a paleo lifestyle, grilling is a fantastic way to prepare meals that are nutrient-dense, flavorful, and align with your dietary preferences. Dive into these seven irresistible paleo grilling recipes to bring bold flavors and wholesome ingredients to your table.

1. Citrus Herb Grilled Chicken Thighs

Chicken thighs are a crowd-pleasing protein packed with flavor, and when paired with zesty citrus and fresh herbs, they make for a dish you’ll want on repeat. This recipe balances tangy and savory flavors, making it perfect for casual barbecues or weeknight dinners.

Ingredients:

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice and zest of 2 lemons
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tsp sea salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Combine olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, garlic, rosemary, thyme, sea salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Place the chicken thighs in a resealable bag or shallow dish, and pour the marinade over them. Marinate in the fridge for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Grill the chicken for 6-8 minutes per side, or until fully cooked with an internal temperature of 165°F.
  5. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving to allow the juices to redistribute. Pair with grilled veggies for a complete paleo-friendly meal.

2. Grilled Zucchini and Eggplant Stack with Basil Pesto

Give your vegetables the star treatment on the grill with this layered zucchini and eggplant dish topped with vibrant basil pesto. This is a perfect option for anyone craving a lighter, plant-forward meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 large eggplant, sliced
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • Sea salt and pepper, to taste

Basil Pesto:

  • 2 cups fresh basil leaves
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts or walnuts
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Brush zucchini and eggplant slices with avocado oil, and sprinkle with garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Grill the vegetables over medium heat for 3-4 minutes on each side, until tender and grill marks appear.
  3. To make the basil pesto, blend all pesto ingredients in a food processor until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  4. Layer zucchini and eggplant slices into stacks, drizzling pesto sauce over each layer.
  5. Serve as a side dish or vegetarian main course.

3. Sweet and Spicy Grilled Shrimp Skewers

Juicy shrimp marinated in a tangy, slightly spicy glaze will steal the show at any barbecue. These skewers are quick to make and full of flavor, making them ideal for both weeknights and weekend cookouts.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 tbsp honey (optional for paleo purists)
  • 1 tbsp hot sauce (like Tabasco or your favorite paleo-friendly brand)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a bowl, then add shrimp. Toss to coat and marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Thread the shrimp onto skewers (if using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes beforehand to prevent burning).
  3. Preheat the grill to medium heat. Grill shrimp for 2-3 minutes per side until opaque and slightly charred.
  4. Serve skewers with a squeeze of fresh lime juice and a side of paleo-friendly coleslaw.

4. Grilled Steak with Chimichurri Sauce

Few things are as enticing as the aroma of steak sizzling on the grill. Pairing it with a fresh chimichurri sauce elevates the dish with vibrant and aromatic flavors.

Ingredients:

  • 1.5-2 lbs grass-fed flank steak or ribeye
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Chimichurri Sauce:

  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Rub steak with avocado oil, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Let sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes.
  2. Grill steak over high heat for 4-5 minutes per side (for medium-rare, adjust cook time based on your preferred doneness).
  3. Remove the steak from the grill and let rest for 10 minutes.
  4. While the steak rests, make the chimichurri by combining all sauce ingredients in a bowl. Stir to mix.
  5. Slice steak against the grain, and serve topped with chimichurri sauce.

5. Grilled Pineapple with Cinnamon and Coconut

For a paleo-friendly dessert that feels indulgent, grilled pineapple checks all the boxes. With caramelized edges and a gentle kiss of cinnamon, this treat is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole pineapple, peeled and sliced into rings or spears
  • 2 tbsp melted coconut oil
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp unsweetened shredded coconut

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Brush pineapple slices with melted coconut oil, then sprinkle with cinnamon.
  3. Grill the pineapple for 2-3 minutes per side, or until grill marks form and the fruit is tender.
  4. Sprinkle with shredded coconut before serving.

6. Paleo Grilled Burger Bowls

Satisfy your burger cravings without the bun with these flavorful burger bowls. Packed with grilled veggies and homemade burger patties, this dish is fun to customize.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb grass-fed ground beef
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • Mixed greens or romaine lettuce
  • Favorite toppings: avocado slices, paleo ketchup, mustard, etc.

Instructions:

  1. Mix ground beef with onion powder, garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Form into burger patties.
  2. Toss sliced veggies with avocado oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Grill patties for 4-5 minutes per side. Grill veggies simultaneously until tender and charred.
  4. Assemble the bowls with mixed greens as a base, then layer on grilled patties, veggies, and toppings.

7. Lemon Garlic Grilled Salmon

Salmon is an excellent source of healthy fats and protein, and grilling brings out a rich, smoky flavor that’s hard to resist. This dish pairs perfectly with a side of grilled asparagus or sweet potato fries.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Juice of 2 lemons
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp dried dill
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, dill, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Pour over salmon fillets, and marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat your grill to medium heat and oil the grates to prevent sticking.
  3. Grill salmon skin-side down for 6-8 minutes, then flip and grill for 2-3 minutes, or until the fish flakes easily with a fork.
  4. Serve immediately with a wedge of lemon.

Grilling doesn’t have to feel constrained when you’re on a paleo diet. These seven paleo recipes combine fresh ingredients with bold flavors to create delicious meals you can enjoy all summer long. Fire up the grill, get creative, and savor every bite!
