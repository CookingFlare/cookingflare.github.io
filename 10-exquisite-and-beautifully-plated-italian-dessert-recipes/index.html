10 Exquisite and Beautifully Plated Italian Dessert Recipes


Italian desserts are renowned for their elegance, sophistication, and rich flavors. From creamy tiramisu to decadent cannoli, Italy has a long history of producing some of the world’s most beloved sweet treats. In this article, we’ll explore 10 exquisite and beautifully plated Italian dessert recipes that are sure to impress even the most discerning palates.

1. Tiramisù

Tiramisù is a classic Italian dessert that consists of ladyfingers soaked in espresso and liqueur, layered with a creamy mascarpone cheese mixture. To make it even more impressive, serve it in individual cups and garnish with cocoa powder and chocolate shavings.

Ingredients:

  • 12 ladyfingers
  • 1 cup strong brewed espresso
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped hazelnuts
  • 1/4 cup grated dark chocolate

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ladyfingers and espresso. Let it sit for 5 minutes, then stir in the cocoa powder and sugar.
  2. In a separate bowl, whip the mascarpone cheese until smooth. Add the heavy cream, vanilla extract, and salt. Whip until stiff peaks form.
  3. To assemble, place a layer of ladyfingers in the bottom of a serving cup. Top with a layer of mascarpone mixture, then sprinkle with hazelnuts and chocolate. Repeat until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of mascarpone on top.
  4. Dust with cocoa powder and serve chilled.

2. Panna Cotta with Fresh Berries

Panna cotta is an Italian cream dessert made with sweetened cream that’s set with gelatin. To give it a pop of color and flavor, serve it with a mix of fresh berries such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon unflavored gelatin
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, sprinkle the gelatin over 1/4 cup of cold water and let it sit for 5 minutes to soften.
  2. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is hot but not boiling.
  3. Remove the saucepan from the heat and add the softened gelatin, stirring until it’s fully dissolved.
  4. Strain the mixture into a clean bowl and stir in the vanilla extract. Pour into individual serving cups and refrigerate until set, at least 4 hours.
  5. Serve chilled, topped with fresh berries.

3. Cannoli with Chocolate Chips

Cannoli are Italian shell-shaped pastries that are typically filled with sweetened ricotta cheese. To give them a modern twist, fill them with chocolate chips and top with chopped pistachios.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package cannoli shells
  • 1 cup sweetened ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese and confectioners’ sugar. Mix until smooth.
  2. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. Fill the cannoli shells with the ricotta mixture, then drizzle with melted chocolate and sprinkle with pistachios. Serve chilled.

4. Zeppole with Powdered Sugar

Zeppole are Italian doughnuts that are typically filled with a sweet ricotta or cannoli cream filling. To give them a sweet and elegant touch, dust them with powdered sugar and serve with a side of espresso or coffee.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Espresso or coffee for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, milk, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until a smooth batter forms.
  4. Heat the oil in a deep frying pan to 375°F. Using a cookie scoop or a spoon, drop the batter into the oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  5. Fry for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and dust with powdered sugar. Serve warm with espresso or coffee.

5. Sfogliatelle with Candied Citron

Sfogliatelle are Italian shell-shaped pastries that are typically filled with sweetened ricotta cheese and candied citron. To give them a pop of color and flavor, use candied orange peel instead of citron.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package sfogliatelle shells
  • 1 cup sweetened ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/4 cup candied orange peel, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese and confectioners’ sugar. Mix until smooth.
  2. Fill the sfogliatelle shells with the ricotta mixture, then sprinkle with candied orange peel and pistachios. Serve chilled.

6. Babà with Rum Syrup

Babà are Italian small cakes that are typically soaked in rum syrup and filled with sweetened cream. To give them a modern twist, use Grand Marnier instead of rum and serve with a dollop of whipped cream.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package babà cakes
  • 1 cup Grand Marnier
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the Grand Marnier and sugar. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. Soak the babà cakes in the rum syrup for at least 30 minutes.
  4. In a medium bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Add the softened butter, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt. Whip until smooth.
  5. Fill the babà cakes with the whipped cream and serve chilled.

7. Torte di Ricotta with Fresh Fruit

Torte di ricotta is an Italian cheesecake that’s typically made with sweetened ricotta cheese and topped with a layer of chocolate. To give it a fresh and fruity twist, top it with a mix of fresh berries such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups sweetened ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fresh berries (such as strawberries, blueberries, and raspberries)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Grease a 9-inch springform pan and set aside.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, sugar, eggs, flour, and salt. Mix until smooth.
  3. Add the melted butter, heavy cream, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared pan and bake for 45-50 minutes, or until set.
  5. Let it cool completely, then top with fresh berries and serve chilled.

8. Pizzelle with Caramel Sauce

Pizzelle are Italian waffle cookies that are typically flavored with anise and topped with powdered sugar. To give them a sweet and indulgent twist, serve them with a side of caramel sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package pizzelle cookies
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup light corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Caramel sauce for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the sugar, corn syrup, and 1/4 cup of the heavy cream. Bring to a boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Remove the saucepan from the heat and let it cool slightly.
  3. Add the softened butter and vanilla extract. Whisk until smooth.
  4. Dust the pizzelle cookies with powdered sugar and serve with a side of caramel sauce.

9. Cream-Filled Cannoli with Chocolate Chips

Cream-filled cannoli are a classic Italian dessert that consists of crispy shells filled with sweetened ricotta cheese. To give them a modern twist, fill them with chocolate chips and top with chopped pistachios.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package cannoli shells
  • 1 cup sweetened ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese and confectioners’ sugar. Mix until smooth.
  2. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  3. Fill the cannoli shells with the ricotta mixture, then drizzle with melted chocolate and sprinkle with pistachios. Serve chilled.

10. Torta di Cioccolato with Hazelnuts

Torta di cioccolato is an Italian chocolate cake that’s typically made with dark chocolate and topped with a layer of chocolate ganache. To give it a nutty and indulgent twist, top it with chopped hazelnuts and serve with a side of whipped cream.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Chopped hazelnuts for topping
  • Whipped cream for serving

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Grease two 9-inch round cake pans and set aside.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients, beating until just combined.
  5. Melt the chocolate chips in a double boiler or in the microwave in 30-second increments, stirring between each interval until smooth.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until set.
  7. Let the cakes cool completely, then top with chocolate ganache and chopped hazelnuts. Serve with a side of whipped cream.
