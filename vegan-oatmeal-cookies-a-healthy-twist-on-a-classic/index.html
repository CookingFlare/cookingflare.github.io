Vegan Oatmeal Cookies: A Healthy Twist on a Classic


Who doesn’t love the comforting aroma of freshly baked oatmeal cookies? They are the epitome of sweet indulgence, but what if I told you that you could enjoy this classic treat in a healthier, vegan version? Yes, you heard it right. Vegan oatmeal cookies are not only delicious but also packed with wholesome ingredients that are good for you. So let’s dive into this recipe and discover how to make these delightful cookies.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • ½ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ½ cup maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup raisins (optional)
  • ½ cup chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the rolled oats, whole wheat flour, ground cinnamon, baking soda, and salt. Stir well to ensure all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until well combined. The coconut oil might solidify slightly when combined with the other ingredients, but that’s perfectly normal.

  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl of dry ingredients and mix until you have a thick, sticky dough. If desired, add in the raisins and chopped nuts for added texture and flavor.

  5. Using a tablespoon or a small ice cream scoop, portion out the dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Leave about 2 inches of space between each cookie to allow for spreading.

  6. Gently flatten each portion of dough with the back of a spoon or your palm. This will help the cookies bake evenly.

  7. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 12-15 minutes, or until the cookies turn golden brown around the edges.

  8. Once baked, remove the cookies from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes. This will allow them to firm up before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  9. Once cooled, these vegan oatmeal cookies are ready to be enjoyed. They are perfect for a midday snack or a sweet treat after dinner.

Why Choose Vegan Oatmeal Cookies?

Now that you have the recipe at your fingertips, let’s discuss why these vegan oatmeal cookies are a healthier alternative to the traditional ones.

  • No Dairy or Eggs: By eliminating the dairy and eggs commonly found in classic oatmeal cookies, you can enjoy these treats guilt-free. They are suitable for those following a vegan or plant-based lifestyle.

  • Wholesome Ingredients: The use of rolled oats, whole wheat flour, coconut oil, and maple syrup adds a nutritional boost to these cookies. Rolled oats are an excellent source of fiber, while whole wheat flour provides added vitamins and minerals. Coconut oil is a healthier fat alternative, and maple syrup adds natural sweetness.

  • Lower in Refined Sugar: Many traditional oatmeal cookies are loaded with refined sugar. However, this vegan version relies on the natural sweetness of maple syrup, reducing the overall sugar content.

  • Customizable: Feel free to experiment with different add-ins to make these cookies your own. Whether you prefer raisins, chopped nuts, or even vegan chocolate chips, the options are endless.

Conclusion

Vegan oatmeal cookies offer a healthier twist on a classic treat without compromising on taste. With their wholesome ingredients and easy preparation, they are a perfect choice for those seeking a guilt-free indulgence. So why not give this recipe a try and let the aroma of freshly baked cookies fill your home? Your taste buds will thank you, and your body will appreciate the nourishing ingredients. Enjoy!
