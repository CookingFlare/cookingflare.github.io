10 Quick Winter Soups for Busy Weeknights


As the temperature drops and the days grow shorter, nothing beats a warm bowl of soup to comfort the soul. Winter soups are not only hearty and nourishing but also incredibly versatile, allowing you to use whatever ingredients you have on hand. For busy weeknights, these quick soup recipes can be whipped up in no time, making them the perfect solution for your family’s dinner. Let’s dive into ten delicious winter soups that will keep you warm without consuming all your evening hours.

1. Creamy Tomato Basil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of diced tomatoes (14 oz each)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream (or coconut milk for a lighter version)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  2. Stir in the garlic and dried basil, cooking for an additional minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and vegetable broth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, you can blend in batches in a regular blender.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper. Heat through and serve with fresh basil on top.

2. Hearty Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion, carrots, and celery in a little olive oil until softened.
  2. Add the green beans, kidney beans, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil.
  3. Stir in thyme, salt, and pepper. Reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. Adjust seasoning as needed and serve hot.

3. Chicken Noodle Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 rotisserie chicken, shredded
  • 8 cups chicken broth
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cups egg noodles
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine chicken broth, carrots, celery, and onion. Bring to a boil.
  2. Add the egg noodles and cook according to package instructions.
  3. Stir in the shredded chicken and dried thyme, and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes, then serve garnished with fresh parsley.

4. Spicy Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup lentils (any variety)
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, sauté the onion, garlic, and carrots in olive oil until soft.
  2. Add the lentils, vegetable broth, cumin, paprika, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 25 minutes until lentils are tender.
  4. Blend if desired for a smoother texture, or serve as is.

5. Beef and Barley Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 cup barley
  • 8 cups beef broth
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add the onion, carrots, and celery; sauté until softened.
  3. Stir in the barley, beef broth, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes until barley is tender.

6. Minestrone Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (frozen or fresh)
  • 1 cup pasta (small shapes like ditalini)
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, combine vegetable broth, chickpeas, diced tomatoes, and mixed vegetables. Bring to a boil.
  2. Add pasta and Italian seasoning. Cook according to pasta package instructions until al dente.
  3. Season with salt and pepper, then serve warm.

7. Curried Butternut Squash Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic until soft.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash, vegetable broth, and curry powder. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer until squash is tender, about 20 minutes.
  4. Blend until smooth, then stir in coconut milk, and season with salt and pepper.

8. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add sweet potatoes, black beans, vegetable broth, and cumin. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 20-25 minutes until sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. Blend if desired for a creamy texture, season with salt and pepper, and serve.

9. Split Pea Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup split peas, rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the onion, carrots, and celery until soft.
  2. Add split peas, broth, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes until peas are tender.
  4. Remove bay leaf, season with salt and pepper, and serve hot.

10. Thai Coconut Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 tablespoon red curry paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pot, bring vegetable broth to a simmer. Add mushrooms and broccoli, cooking until tender.
  2. Stir in coconut milk, red curry paste, soy sauce, and lime juice. Heat through.
  3. Serve garnished with fresh cilantro.

Conclusion

These ten quick winter soups are not only easy to prepare but also offer a variety of flavors and nutrients to keep you warm and satisfied during the colder months. Whether you’re in the mood for a creamy tomato soup or a spicy lentil soup, there’s something in this collection for everyone. Perfect for busy weeknights, these recipes will ensure you and your family enjoy comforting meals without spending hours in the kitchen. So grab your pots and pans, and let the soup-making begin!
