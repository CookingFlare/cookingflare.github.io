Easy Baked Beans: A Comfort Food Side Dish


Baked beans are a classic comfort food that has graced tables for generations. Whether served at a summer barbecue, a cozy family dinner, or a festive holiday gathering, this dish is beloved for its rich flavor and hearty texture. In this post, we will explore the history of baked beans, the ingredients that make them special, and a simple recipe that will have you whipping up this delightful side dish in no time.

The History of Baked Beans

Baked beans have a long and storied history, tracing back to Native American cuisine. Indigenous peoples in North America would cook beans with maple syrup and bear fat, creating a dish that was both nutritious and delicious. When European settlers arrived, they adapted this recipe, incorporating their own ingredients and cooking methods. The dish evolved over the years, becoming a staple in American cuisine, particularly in New England.

Today, baked beans are often associated with barbecue and outdoor cooking, but they can be enjoyed year-round. Their versatility makes them an excellent side dish for a variety of meals, from grilled meats to vegetarian options.

Why Baked Beans Are a Comfort Food

Baked beans are more than just a side dish; they evoke feelings of nostalgia and warmth. The combination of sweet and savory flavors, along with the creamy texture of the beans, creates a comforting experience that many people cherish. Additionally, baked beans are packed with protein and fiber, making them a nutritious choice that can satisfy hunger and provide lasting energy.

Ingredients for Easy Baked Beans

To make easy baked beans, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Beans: You can use canned beans for convenience or dried beans if you prefer to cook them from scratch. Common choices include navy beans, pinto beans, or kidney beans.
  • Onion: A finely chopped onion adds depth and sweetness to the dish.
  • Bacon or Ham: For a smoky flavor, you can include diced bacon or ham. For a vegetarian option, you can skip this ingredient or use smoked paprika for a similar taste.
  • Brown Sugar: This ingredient provides sweetness and caramelization during baking.
  • Molasses: A key ingredient that adds richness and depth to the flavor profile.
  • Mustard: Yellow mustard or Dijon mustard adds a tangy kick that balances the sweetness.
  • Ketchup: A classic addition that enhances the overall flavor and adds moisture.
  • Apple Cider Vinegar: This ingredient adds acidity, brightening the dish and cutting through the richness.
  • Salt and Pepper: Essential for seasoning and enhancing the flavors of the other ingredients.

Easy Baked Beans Recipe

Now that we have covered the history and ingredients, let’s dive into the recipe for easy baked beans. This dish is simple to prepare and can be made in under an hour, making it perfect for busy weeknights or last-minute gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) of navy beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 4 slices of bacon, diced (or 1 cup of diced ham for a non-vegetarian option)
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup molasses
  • 2 tablespoons yellow mustard
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). This temperature is ideal for baking the beans and allowing the flavors to meld together.

  2. Cook the Bacon: In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the diced bacon until it is crispy. If you are using ham, you can skip this step and add it directly to the beans later. Once the bacon is cooked, remove it from the skillet and set it aside, leaving the drippings in the pan.

  3. Sauté the Onion: In the same skillet, add the chopped onion to the bacon drippings. Sauté the onion until it becomes translucent and fragrant, about 5 minutes. This step enhances the flavor of the dish.

  4. Combine Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained beans, sautéed onion, cooked bacon (or ham), brown sugar, molasses, mustard, ketchup, apple cider vinegar, salt, and pepper. Stir until all the ingredients are well combined.

  5. Transfer to Baking Dish: Pour the bean mixture into a greased 2-quart baking dish. Spread it out evenly to ensure even cooking.

  6. Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. After 30 minutes, remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes to allow the top to caramelize slightly.

  7. Serve: Once the baked beans are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.
