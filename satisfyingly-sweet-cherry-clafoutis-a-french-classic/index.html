Satisfyingly Sweet Cherry Clafoutis: A French Classic


Clafoutis, pronounced “klah-foo-tee,” is a traditional French dessert that perfectly embodies simplicity and elegance. This delectable treat is made with a delicate custard-like batter that cradles juicy, ripe cherries. The combination of the sweet fruit and the creamy texture creates a harmonious balance that is sure to satisfy your taste buds. Today, we will dive into the world of cherry clafoutis and explore how to create this timeless French classic in your very own kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups fresh cherries, pitted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch round baking dish with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, and salt until well combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs until lightly frothy. Add the milk, heavy cream, and vanilla extract to the beaten eggs, and whisk until smooth.

  4. Slowly pour the wet mixture into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Whisk together until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.

  5. Gently fold in the fresh cherries, being careful not to crush them. The cherries will release their natural juices during baking, adding a burst of flavor to the clafoutis.

  6. Pour the batter into the greased baking dish, spreading it evenly.

  7. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for approximately 45-50 minutes, or until the clafoutis is golden brown and set in the center.

  8. Once baked, remove the clafoutis from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. The clafoutis can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.

  9. To serve, dust the top of the clafoutis with powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness. You can also garnish with a few fresh cherries or a sprig of mint for an elegant presentation.

Tips and Variations:

  • Experiment with different fruits: While cherries are the traditional choice for clafoutis, you can also use other fruits such as raspberries, blackberries, or sliced peaches. Just remember to adjust the sugar quantity according to the sweetness of the fruit.

  • Add a hint of almond flavor: For a subtle twist, add a few drops of almond extract to the batter. The almond flavor pairs beautifully with cherries and adds a delightful aroma to the clafoutis.

  • Serve with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a decadent treat.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. Simply reheat in the oven or enjoy chilled.

Whether you are hosting a dinner party or simply craving a taste of France, Satisfyingly Sweet Cherry Clafoutis is a dessert that will impress your guests and leave them craving for more. The combination of the creamy custard and the burst of sweet cherries is a match made in dessert heaven. So, grab your apron, gather your ingredients, and transport yourself to the charming streets of Paris with this delightful French classic. Bon appétit!
