Quick and Easy Spanish Dinners: 7 Recipes for Busy Weeknights


Busy weeknights often leave little room for elaborate cooking, but that doesn’t mean you have to settle for takeout or uninspired meals. Spanish cuisine, known for its bold flavors, vibrant colors, and simple ingredients, offers plenty of dishes that can be prepared quickly without compromising on taste. From hearty stews to light tapas-inspired dishes, these recipes bring the spirit of Spain to your dinner table in no time. Let’s dive into seven practical, flavorful Spanish recipes perfect for busy evenings!

1. Gambas al Ajillo (Garlic Shrimp)

If you’re craving something quick and flavorful, Gambas al Ajillo is a go-to dish. This Spanish classic is not only packed with bold garlic flavors but also requires minimal prep and cooking time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 5 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 1 red chili or 1/2 tsp red chili flakes
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Fresh bread (optional, for serving)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the garlic slices and chili, cooking until fragrant and lightly browned.
  3. Toss in the shrimp, season with salt, and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring until the shrimp turn pink and are cooked through.
  4. Remove from heat, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and serve immediately with crusty bread to mop up the delicious garlic-infused oil.

2. Tortilla Española (Spanish Omelette)

Perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner, Tortilla Española is a wonderful one-pan dish that’s simple yet satisfying. It incorporates just a few pantry staples, making it an economical option for weeknights.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup olive oil (for frying)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Add potatoes and onions, cooking until tender (but not browned), about 10-12 minutes. Drain excess oil.
  2. In a bowl, whisk eggs with salt and pepper. Stir in the cooked potatoes and onions.
  3. Pour the mixture back into the skillet and cook over low heat until the bottom sets, about 5 minutes.
  4. Flip the tortilla carefully using a plate to cook the other side for an additional 3-4 minutes.
  5. Slice into wedges and serve warm or at room temperature.

3. Chorizo and Chickpea Stew

On chilly evenings, this hearty Spanish stew comes together with minimal effort. Packed with protein and spices, it’s a one-pot wonder that creates little cleanup.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 large chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the chorizo until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the smoked paprika, followed by the crushed tomatoes and broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
  4. Add the chickpeas and browned chorizo back to the pot. Simmer for 10 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

4. Pan con Tomate (Tomato Bread)

For nights when you barely have time to cook, Pan con Tomate is the ultimate Spanish fast food. This simple yet flavorful dish can also double as a starter or side.

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of crusty bread (baguette or sourdough works well)
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Toast the bread slices until golden and crisp.
  2. Cut the garlic clove in half and rub it onto the surface of the warm bread.
  3. Halve the tomatoes and grate them, discarding the skin. Spoon the grated tomato over the bread.
  4. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with salt. Serve immediately.

5. Pollo al Ajillo (Garlic Chicken)

This rustic garlic chicken dish combines tender protein with rich aromas, yet it’s surprisingly quick and easy to prepare for weeknight dinners.

Ingredients:

  • 4 chicken thighs or drumsticks
  • 6 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp chopped rosemary or thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet and brown the chicken on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, sauté the garlic until golden, then deglaze with white wine.
  4. Return the chicken to the skillet, sprinkle with rosemary or thyme, cover, and simmer for 15-18 minutes until fully cooked.
  5. Serve warm with roasted potatoes or a crusty baguette.

6. Patatas Bravas (Spicy Potatoes)

A favorite tapa across Spain, Patatas Bravas can also moonlight as a side dish or even a light dinner when you’re short on time.

Ingredients for Potatoes:

  • 3 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Ingredients for Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup canned tomato sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (optional for spice)
  • 2 tbsp mayonnaise (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Toss the potatoes with olive oil and salt. Roast in a preheated oven at 425°F for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway.
  2. While the potatoes cook, prepare the sauce. Simmer the tomato sauce, smoked paprika, and cayenne in a small pan until slightly thickened.
  3. Drizzle the sauce over the roasted potatoes and add a dollop of mayonnaise if desired.

7. Spanish-Style Pasta (Fideuà-Inspired)

This quick Spanish-inspired spaghetti or angel hair dish brings the flavors of the Mediterranean right to your kitchen.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 pound pasta (preferably thin spaghetti or angel hair)
  • 1/2 cup shrimp or seafood of your choice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 cup chicken or seafood broth
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet. Sauté garlic until fragrant, then add shrimp and cook until pink.
  3. Stir in smoked paprika and broth, simmering for 1-2 minutes to create a light sauce.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta in the skillet to coat, adding salt to taste.
  5. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and a parsley garnish.

Final Thoughts

Bringing Spanish flavors to your table doesn’t have to mean hours in the kitchen. These quick, easy dinners are perfect for those weeknights when time is tight but you still want something satisfying and delicious. Whether it’s the simplicity of Pan con Tomate or the comforting warmth of Chorizo and Chickpea Stew, these dishes will transport you straight to Spain with minimal effort.

Experiment with these recipes and make them your own by adding fresh vegetables, customizing spices, or pairing them with your favorite sides. Spanish cuisine is all about celebrating flavor and shared moments, so why not bring a bit of that ethos to your next weeknight meal? Enjoy!
