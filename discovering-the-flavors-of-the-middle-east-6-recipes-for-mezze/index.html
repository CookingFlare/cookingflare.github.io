Discovering the Flavors of the Middle East: 6 Recipes for Mezze


The vibrant and diverse cuisines of the Middle East are a treasure trove of flavors, aromas, and textures that will take your taste buds on a thrilling journey. One of the region's most beloved culinary traditions is Mezze, a delightful assortment of small plates and snacks that showcase the best of the region's fresh vegetables, herbs, meats, and dips. In this post, we'll delve into the world of Mezze and explore six mouthwatering recipes that will transport your senses to the bustling souks and cafes of the Middle East. What is Mezze? Mezze is a culinary staple in the Middle East, where social gatherings and celebrations are incomplete without an array of small, shareable dishes. The word "mezze" literally means "small eats" in Arabic, and that's exactly what these dishes are – bite-sized morsels of flavors and textures designed to be devoured with your hands, shared with friends and family, and enjoyed over lively conversation. 6 Recipes for Mezze

  1. Baked Fattoush Falafel Falafel is a Middle Eastern classic, and this baked version is a game-changer. By roasting the chickpea patties in the oven instead of frying them, you'll create a crispy exterior and a fluffy interior that's bursting with flavor. Serve with a creamy tahini sauce and a side of crumbled sumac for added depth. Mix together:

1 cup chickpeas 1/2 cup breadcrumbs 1/4 cup chopped parsley 1/4 cup chopped mint 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup lemon juice 1/4 cup olive oil Salt and pepper to taste Sumac for garnish

Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes or until crispy and golden brown. 2. Roasted Beet Hummus Hummus is a Mezze staple, but have you ever tried it with roasted beets? The sweetness of the beets adds a delightful twist to the classic recipe. This creamy dip is perfect for carrot sticks, pita chips, or even as a sauce for grilled meats. Combine:

1 cup cooked chickpeas 1/2 cup roasted beets, peeled and chopped 1/4 cup tahini 2 cloves garlic, minced 1/4 cup lemon juice 1/4 cup olive oil Salt and pepper to taste

Blend until smooth and creamy, garnish with parsley and paprika. 3. Grilled Halloumi with Honey and Thyme Halloumi is a Cypriot cheese that's perfect for grilling, and pairing it with honey and thyme creates a match made in heaven. This sweet and savory cheese plate is an excellent addition to any Mezze spread. Mix together:

1 block halloumi, sliced 2 tablespoons honey 2 sprigs fresh thyme 1 tablespoon olive oil

Grill until golden brown and caramelized, garnish with additional thyme and honey. 4. Stuffed Grape Leaves (Dolma) Stuffed grape leaves are a classic Mezze dish that's both healthy and flavorful. This recipe uses fresh grape leaves wrapped around a mix of rice, herbs, and spices for a delicious and visually stunning appetizer. Combine:

1 package grape leaves 1 cup cooked rice 1/4 cup chopped parsley 1/4 cup chopped dill 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon olive oil Salt and pepper to taste

Stuff the leaves with the rice mixture and steam or cook in a traditional dolma pot. 5. Spiced Lamb Koftas Koftas are another popular Mezze favorite, and these spiced lamb patties are infused with the warm, aromatic flavors of cumin and coriander. Serve with a side of yogurt sauce and pickled turnips for a refreshing contrast. Mix together:

1 pound ground lamb 1/4 cup breadcrumbs 1/4 cup chopped parsley 1/4 cup chopped mint 1 tablespoon olive oil 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander Salt and pepper to taste

Form into small patties and grill or bake until cooked through. 6. Lemon and Oregano Feta Tartlets These bite-sized tartlets are a brilliant way to showcase the brininess of feta cheese and the brightness of lemon. Perfect for a cocktail party or dinner gathering, these tangy treats are sure to impress. Combine:

1 package frozen puff pastry, thawed 1 cup crumbled feta 2 tablespoons lemon juice 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon olive oil 1 tablespoon chopped oregano Salt and pepper to taste

Roll out the pastry and use a cookie cutter to create small rectangles. Fill with feta mixture and fold in half to create a triangle. Tips and Variations

To add some heat to your Mezze, sprinkle some sumac or harissa on top of your dishes. For a crunchy texture, top your Mezze with chopped nuts or pita chips. Experiment with different types of cheese, such as goat cheese or feta, to create unique flavor combinations. Don't be afraid to get creative with your Mezze platter – feel free to add or subtract ingredients to suit your tastes and dietary restrictions.

Mezze is more than just a collection of small plates; it's an experience that brings people together. These recipes offer a glimpse into the vibrant flavors and traditions of the Middle East, and we hope they inspire you to create your own Mezze masterpieces. So gather your friends and family, grab some pita bread, and get ready to embark on a culinary adventure that will take you from the souks of Baghdad to the beaches of Marrakech.
