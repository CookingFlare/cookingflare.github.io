Simple Chinese Chicken with Oyster Sauce


Chinese cuisine is renowned for its well-balanced flavors, rich tradition, and simplicity in preparation. Among its repertoire, chicken dishes always take center stage for their versatility and universal appeal. One classic recipe that has delighted taste buds for centuries is Chinese chicken with oyster sauce. This dish is the epitome of simplicity combined with robust flavors, making it a perfect choice for both weeknight dinners and special gatherings.

With just a handful of ingredients and minimal cooking steps, this recipe delivers authentic Chinese flavors that will have everyone reaching for seconds. Whether you’re a seasoned home cook or just getting started in the kitchen, this recipe is sure to become your go-to for flavorful Chinese cuisine.

Why Oyster Sauce?

Oyster sauce is one of the foundational condiments in Chinese cooking. Made primarily from oysters, salt, and sugar, and often thickened with cornstarch, oyster sauce delivers a rich umami flavor that enhances the natural taste of ingredients without overwhelming them. When paired with chicken, oyster sauce creates a perfect harmony of savory, slightly sweet, and deeply satisfying flavors.

In this dish, oyster sauce plays the starring role, helping to tie together the freshness of the vegetables and the juiciness of the chicken into one cohesive and beautifully balanced dish.

Ingredients You’ll Need:

Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced chef, finding the ingredients for this recipe shouldn’t be a challenge. Many of these components are pantry staples in Asian kitchens, and you can easily source them in most grocery stores or Asian supermarkets.

Main Ingredients:

  • Chicken: 500g of chicken breast or thighs, sliced thinly
  • Vegetables: 1 medium-sized onion, thinly sliced; 1 bell pepper (any color), julienned
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon, grated or minced
  • Spring onions: 2 stalks, chopped (for garnish)

Sauce Ingredients:

  • Oyster Sauce: 3 tablespoons
  • Soy Sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Shaoxing Rice Wine (optional): 1 tablespoon, for added fragrance
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon
  • Cornstarch: 1 teaspoon, dissolved in 2 tablespoons of water (for thickening)

Cooking Oil:

  • 2 tablespoons of neutral oil, such as vegetable oil or canola oil

Optional Add-Ins:

  • Chili Paste or Fresh Chilies: For added heat
  • Sesame Oil: A few drops for a nutty aroma
  • Mushrooms: To add depth and texture

Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. Prepare the Chicken

Start by slicing the chicken thinly against the grain. This ensures that the chicken cooks quickly and remains tender. If you’re using chicken thighs, they are naturally juicier and perfect for stir-frying. Once sliced, season the chicken lightly with a pinch of salt and set it aside.

2. Mix the Sauce

In a small bowl, combine oyster sauce, soy sauce, sugar, Shaoxing rice wine, and the cornstarch slurry. Stir thoroughly until the sugar has dissolved, and the mixture becomes smooth. This sauce will be the heart of the dish, packing all the flavor you need.

3. Heat Up the Oil

In a large wok or skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. A wok is ideal for this dish because it distributes heat evenly, ensuring quick and efficient cooking.

4. Cook the Aromatics

Add the minced garlic and ginger to the hot oil. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant, making sure not to let them burn. This step creates a flavorful base for our dish.

5. Add the Chicken

Increase the heat to high and toss in the sliced chicken. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and begins to turn golden. Make sure to keep stirring so the chicken cooks evenly.

6. Incorporate the Vegetables

Add the sliced onion and bell pepper to the wok. Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes, allowing the vegetables to soften slightly while still retaining their crunch. If you’re adding mushrooms or any other optional ingredients, toss them in at this stage.

7. Pour in the Sauce

Reduce the heat to medium and pour the prepared sauce over the chicken and vegetables. Stir everything together, ensuring that each piece is coated with the flavorful oyster sauce mixture. Let the sauce simmer for 1–2 minutes until it thickens slightly and clings to the chicken and vegetables.

8. Finish with Garnish

Remove the wok from the heat and sprinkle chopped spring onions on top. If using sesame oil, drizzle a few drops for added aroma.

Serving Suggestions:

Chinese chicken with oyster sauce pairs beautifully with plain steamed rice, which acts as a neutral base to soak up the flavorful sauce. Alternatively, you can serve it with egg noodles or fried rice for a heartier meal. Adding a side of stir-fried bok choy or steamed broccoli can create a well-rounded dinner filled with freshness and nutrition.

To elevate this dish for a special occasion, consider serving it family-style, placing the chicken with oyster sauce in the center of the table along with other complementary Chinese dishes like sweet and sour pork, hot and sour soup, or dumplings.

Tips for Perfect Results:

  1. Choose Quality Oyster Sauce: Since it’s the star ingredient, opt for a high-quality brand like Lee Kum Kee or Kikkoman. Premium oyster sauces usually have a richer taste and thicker consistency.

  2. Prep Ingredients Before Cooking: Stir-frying is a fast cooking method, so having all your ingredients prepped beforehand ensures you won’t miss a beat.

  3. Use High Heat: High heat is essential for quick cooking and helps maintain the crunchiness of the vegetables while searing the chicken perfectly.

  4. Adjust Sweetness and Saltiness: Depending on your preference, feel free to tweak the amount of sugar and soy sauce for the perfect balance of flavors.

Why This Recipe is a Keeper:

What makes Chinese chicken with oyster sauce such a favorite is its simplicity. It doesn’t require complicated techniques, and its flavor is universally appealing. With a single wok or skillet and 20 minutes, you can create a dish that feels like a trip to your favorite Chinese restaurant.

Besides being a quick and easy weeknight dinner, this recipe is a great opportunity to experiment with other ingredients. Try swapping bell peppers for snow peas or baby corn, or add a sprinkle of crushed red pepper for spice lovers. The possibilities are endless yet always delicious.

So, whether you’re serving guests or enjoying a quiet dinner at home, this dish guarantees satisfaction with every bite. Dive into the flavors of China tonight with this simple yet incredibly tasty chicken recipe!
