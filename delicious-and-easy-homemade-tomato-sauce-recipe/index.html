Delicious and Easy Homemade Tomato Sauce Recipe


Tomato sauce is a staple in many cuisines around the world. It’s a versatile condiment that can be used as a base for pasta dishes, pizza, lasagna, and many other recipes. While store-bought tomato sauce can be convenient, making your own homemade tomato sauce from scratch can be a game-changer. Not only will you have control over the ingredients and flavor, but you’ll also save money and reduce waste. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to make a delicious and easy homemade tomato sauce that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh or canned tomatoes (preferably San Marzano or other sweet tomatoes)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup red wine (optional)

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the tomatoes: If using fresh tomatoes, wash and chop them into small pieces. If using canned tomatoes, drain the liquid and crush the tomatoes by hand or with a fork.
  2. Heat the oil and sauté the onion: In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it’s translucent and slightly caramelized, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for 1 minute: Add the minced garlic to the saucepan and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Add the tomatoes and spices: Add the prepared tomatoes, dried oregano, and dried basil to the saucepan. Stir well to combine.
  5. Season with salt and pepper: Add salt and pepper to taste, and stir well to combine.
  6. Add the red wine (if using): If using red wine, add it to the saucepan and stir well to combine.
  7. Simmer the sauce: Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and reduced slightly.
  8. Taste and adjust: Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning as needed. You can add more salt, pepper, or herbs to taste.
  9. Use or store the sauce: Your homemade tomato sauce is now ready to use. You can use it immediately, or store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze it for up to 6 months.

Tips and Variations

  • Use fresh tomatoes: If you have the opportunity, use fresh tomatoes instead of canned. Fresh tomatoes will give your sauce a brighter, more vibrant flavor.
  • Add a pinch of sugar: A small amount of sugar can help balance out the acidity of the tomatoes and create a richer flavor.
  • Experiment with spices: Add a pinch of cumin, paprika, or chili flakes to give your sauce a unique flavor.
  • Make it chunky: Leave some of the tomatoes chunky for a more rustic, textured sauce.
  • Make it smooth: Blend the sauce in a blender or with an immersion blender for a smoother, more refined sauce.

Conclusion

Making homemade tomato sauce from scratch is a simple and rewarding process that requires just a few ingredients and some patience. With this recipe, you can create a delicious and easy homemade tomato sauce that’s perfect for any occasion. Experiment with different spices, herbs, and ingredients to create your own unique flavor profile. Happy cooking!
