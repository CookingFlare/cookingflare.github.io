Irresistible Oreo Cheesecake: A Cookie Lover's Dream

If you’re a fan of both Oreos and cheesecake, then this recipe is about to become your new obsession. The combination of creamy, rich cheesecake and the irresistible crunch of Oreos creates a dessert that is truly a cookie lover’s dream. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party, celebrating a special occasion, or just craving a decadent treat, this Oreo cheesecake is sure to impress and satisfy. Ingredients For the Crust: 24 Oreo cookies 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted For the Cheesecake Filling: 32 oz cream cheese, at room temperature 1 cup granulated sugar 1 tsp vanilla extract 4 large eggs 1 cup sour cream 20 Oreo cookies, roughly chopped For the Topping: 1 cup heavy cream 2 tbsp powdered sugar 8 Oreo cookies, crushed for garnish Instructions Step 1: Preparing the Crust Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place 24 Oreo cookies in a food processor and pulse until they turn into fine crumbs. In a bowl, mix the Oreo crumbs with melted butter until well combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan, creating an even layer. Place the pan in the refrigerator to chill while you prepare the filling. Step 2: Making the Cheesecake Filling In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy. Add the vanilla extract and mix until incorporated. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed. Stir in the sour cream until the mixture is smooth. Gently fold in the chopped Oreo cookies, being careful not to overmix. Pour the filling over the chilled Oreo crust, spreading it into an even layer. Step 3: Baking the Cheesecake Place the springform pan on a baking sheet to catch any potential leaks. Bake the cheesecake in the preheated oven for 55-60 minutes, or until the edges are set, and the center is slightly jiggly. Turn off the oven and crack the door open, letting the cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour. Remove the cheesecake from the oven and run a knife around the edge of the pan to loosen the cake. Allow the cheesecake to cool completely, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight. Step 4: Adding the Topping In a mixing bowl, whip the heavy cream and powdered sugar until stiff peaks form. Spread the whipped cream over the chilled cheesecake. Sprinkle the crushed Oreo cookies on top for a beautiful garnish. Serving and Enjoying Once the cheesecake is fully set and chilled, carefully remove it from the springform pan. Slice it into generous portions and serve it to your eager guests. The luscious creaminess of the cheesecake, studded with Oreo pieces, and the delightful crunch of the Oreo crust will surely delight everyone’s taste buds. This Oreo cheesecake is an indulgent and satisfying dessert that will have your friends and family begging for the recipe. ...
