Hearty and Wholesome Casserole Recipes for Elderly Cooking


As we age, it becomes increasingly important to nourish our bodies with wholesome and nutritious meals. Casseroles are a fantastic option for elderly cooking, as they are not only delicious but also easy to prepare and packed with essential nutrients. In this blog post, we will explore a variety of hearty and wholesome casserole recipes that are perfect for elderly individuals. Let’s get cooking!

1. Classic Chicken and Vegetable Casserole

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Cooking time: 45 minutes

Servings: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, green beans)
  • 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup cooked white rice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a casserole dish with olive oil.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the mixed vegetables and cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, milk, flour, dried thyme, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  4. Pour the mixture into the saucepan with the vegetables. Stir in the shredded chicken and cooked rice.
  5. Transfer the mixture to the greased casserole dish and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the casserole is bubbling and golden on top.
  7. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

2. Beef and Sweet Potato Casserole

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour 15 minutes

Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 pounds lean ground beef
  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cup low-sodium beef broth
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced bell pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a casserole dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic to the skillet. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Stir in the beef broth, dried rosemary, dried thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Layer half of the sliced sweet potatoes in the greased casserole dish. Pour half of the beef mixture over the sweet potatoes.
  6. Repeat the layering process with the remaining sweet potatoes and beef mixture.
  7. Cover the casserole dish with aluminum foil and bake for 45 minutes.
  8. Remove the foil and sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese on top. Bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  9. Allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

3. Vegetable and Quinoa Casserole

Preparation time: 25 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour

Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed and drained
  • 2 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
  • 1 cup diced zucchini
  • 1 cup diced eggplant
  • 1 cup diced bell pepper
  • 1 cup diced tomato
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a casserole dish with cooking spray.
  2. In a saucepan, bring the vegetable broth to a boil. Add the quinoa, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let it sit for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork.
  3. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the zucchini, eggplant, bell pepper, tomato, onion, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  5. In the greased casserole dish, layer half of the cooked quinoa and then half of the vegetable mixture. Repeat the layering process with the remaining quinoa and vegetables.
  6. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
  7. Cover the casserole dish with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes.
  8. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
  9. Allow it to cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

Conclusion

Casseroles are a wonderful option for elderly individuals looking for hearty and wholesome meals. These recipes are not only delicious but also easy to prepare and packed with essential nutrients. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or a loved one, these casserole recipes are sure to satisfy your cravings while keeping you nourished. So, grab your apron and get cooking!
