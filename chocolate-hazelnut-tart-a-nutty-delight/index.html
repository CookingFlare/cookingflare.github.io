Chocolate Hazelnut Tart: A Nutty Delight


There’s something undeniably luxurious about the combination of chocolate and hazelnut. The rich, creamy texture of chocolate paired with the nutty, earthy flavor of hazelnuts creates a harmonious marriage of flavors that tantalizes the taste buds. If you’re a fan of this delightful duo, then you’re in for a treat with this Chocolate Hazelnut Tart recipe. This indulgent dessert is perfect for special occasions, dinner parties, or simply when you’re craving a decadent sweet treat.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 2 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup hazelnuts, toasted and finely ground
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 ounces bittersweet chocolate, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For the Topping (Optional):

  • 1/4 cup hazelnuts, chopped and toasted
  • Whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for serving

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Crust:

    • In a food processor, pulse the flour, sugar, and salt until combined. Add the cold butter and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    • In a small bowl, whisk together the egg yolk and ice water. Drizzle the egg mixture over the flour mixture and pulse until the dough comes together.
    • Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it gently until it forms a smooth ball. Flatten the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Preheat the Oven:

    • While the dough chills, preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

  3. Roll Out the Crust:

    • On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled dough into a 12-inch circle. Carefully transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Press the dough into the pan, trimming any excess from the edges.

  4. Blind Bake the Crust:

    • Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove the parchment paper and weights, then bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown. Let it cool while you prepare the filling.

  5. Make the Filling:

    • In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from the heat and add the chopped chocolate. Let it sit for a minute, then stir until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth.
    • In a separate bowl, whisk together the ground hazelnuts, sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, and salt. Gradually pour the chocolate mixture into the egg mixture, whisking constantly until well combined.

  6. Assemble and Bake:

    • Pour the filling into the pre-baked tart crust and spread it evenly. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set with a slight jiggle in the center. Let the tart cool completely on a wire rack.

  7. Optional Topping:

    • Sprinkle the chopped, toasted hazelnuts over the cooled tart. Serve slices with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream, if desired.

Tips and Tricks

  • To save time, you can use a store-bought pie crust instead of making the crust from scratch.
  • Be sure to toast the hazelnuts before grinding them for the filling. This enhances their flavor and adds a delightful crunch to the tart.
  • If you prefer a smoother filling, you can strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve before pouring it into the tart crust.

In Conclusion

This Chocolate Hazelnut Tart is a celebration of two classic flavors that never fail to impress. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or just starting to explore the world of homemade desserts, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. The buttery, flaky crust provides the perfect base for the luscious, chocolate-hazelnut filling, creating a dessert that’s both elegant and comforting. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and treat yourself to a slice of this nutty delight – you won’t be disappointed!
