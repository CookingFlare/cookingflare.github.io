Cooking with Korean Rice Cakes: Savory and Sweet Recipes


Korean cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique ingredients, and one such ingredient that stands out is the humble rice cake. Rice cakes, or tteok, are a staple in Korean cooking and are made from glutinous rice flour. These chewy and versatile cakes can be found in various shapes and sizes, making them perfect for both savory and sweet dishes. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious recipes that showcase the versatility of Korean rice cakes.

Savory Recipes

Tteokbokki (Spicy Rice Cake)

Tteokbokki is perhaps the most famous Korean rice cake dish. It features cylindrical rice cakes cooked in a spicy gochujang (Korean chili paste) sauce. To make this dish, start by sautéing garlic and onions in a pan. Then, add the rice cakes, gochujang, soy sauce, sugar, and water. Simmer until the sauce thickens and the rice cakes become tender. You can also add vegetables like cabbage, carrots, or fish cakes for extra flavor and texture. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions before serving.

Rabokki (Ramyeon and Tteokbokki Fusion)

Rabokki combines the popular Korean instant noodle dish, ramyeon, with tteokbokki. To make this fusion dish, cook instant ramyeon noodles according to the package instructions. In a separate pan, prepare the tteokbokki sauce by combining gochujang, soy sauce, sugar, and water. Add the rice cakes and cook until they soften. Then, add the cooked ramyeon noodles and stir until everything is well coated in the sauce. Top with a fried egg and sliced scallions for an extra touch of flavor.

Tteokguk (Rice Cake Soup)

Tteokguk is a traditional Korean soup often enjoyed during the Lunar New Year. It is made by simmering sliced rice cakes in a flavorful beef or vegetable broth. To make this comforting soup, start by boiling beef bones or anchovies with vegetables to create a rich broth. Once the broth is ready, add the rice cakes and cook until they become soft and chewy. Garnish with thinly sliced beef, fried eggs, seaweed, and green onions for a hearty and delicious meal.

Sweet Recipes

Gyeongdan (Sweet Rice Cake Balls)

Gyeongdan are small rice cake balls that are traditionally enjoyed as a dessert or snack. To make these sweet treats, mix glutinous rice flour with water to form a dough. Divide the dough into small portions and flatten them into disks. Place a small piece of sweet filling, such as sweetened sesame or red bean paste, in the center of each disk. Fold the edges of the dough over the filling and roll into a ball. Steam the rice cake balls until they become soft and slightly translucent. Coat them in various toppings like shredded coconut, roasted soybean powder, or sesame seeds for added flavor and texture.

Bingsu (Shaved Ice with Rice Cakes)

Bingsu is a popular Korean dessert that features shaved ice topped with various sweet toppings. To make a rice cake bingsu, start by shaving ice using a specialized machine or blender. Place the shaved ice in a bowl and top it with sweetened condensed milk, fruit, and rice cakes. The rice cakes add a chewy texture to the refreshing dessert. You can also add other toppings like sweetened red beans, nuts, or chocolate syrup for extra indulgence.

Conclusion

Korean rice cakes, or tteok, are a versatile ingredient that can be enjoyed in both savory and sweet dishes. Whether you’re craving a spicy and flavorful tteokbokki or a sweet and refreshing rice cake bingsu, there are endless possibilities when it comes to cooking with Korean rice cakes. So, gather your ingredients and embark on a culinary adventure to explore the delicious world of Korean cuisine.
