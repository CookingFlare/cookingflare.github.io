Classic Reuben Sandwich: A Deli Favorite


If there’s one sandwich that has stood the test of time and remained a beloved classic in delis across the country, it’s the Reuben. This iconic sandwich is a flavorful combination of corned beef, Swiss cheese, sauerkraut, and Russian dressing, all sandwiched between slices of rye bread. It’s a delightful medley of savory, tangy, and creamy flavors that never fails to satisfy.

The Origins of the Reuben

The exact origins of the Reuben sandwich are a subject of debate, with several stories claiming to be the true source of this delectable creation. One popular account traces its roots back to the early 20th century at Reuben’s Restaurant in New York City, where a group of hungry poker players requested a midnight snack, leading to the invention of the Reuben. Another tale attributes its creation to a chef at the Blackstone Hotel in Omaha, Nebraska, who whipped up the sandwich for a group of hungry poker players as well. Regardless of its precise origin, one thing is certain – the Reuben has become a staple in deli menus and home kitchens alike.

Crafting the Perfect Reuben

Making a classic Reuben sandwich at home is a rewarding endeavor that allows you to savor the timeless flavors of this deli favorite. Here’s a step-by-step guide to creating your own mouthwatering Reuben:

Ingredients:

  • 8 slices of rye bread
  • 1 pound of thinly sliced corned beef
  • 1 cup of sauerkraut, well-drained
  • 8 slices of Swiss cheese
  • 1/2 cup of Russian dressing
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, softened

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Russian dressing: In a small bowl, mix together the mayonnaise, ketchup, horseradish, Worcestershire sauce, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Refrigerate the dressing while you assemble the other ingredients.

  2. Build the sandwiches: Lay out the slices of rye bread and spread a generous amount of Russian dressing on each slice. Layer the corned beef, sauerkraut, and Swiss cheese on four of the slices. Top with the remaining bread slices to form four sandwiches.

  3. Grill the sandwiches: Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Spread a thin layer of butter on one side of each sandwich. Place the sandwiches, buttered side down, onto the skillet. Cook for 4-5 minutes, or until the bread is golden brown and the cheese has melted. Butter the top side of each sandwich before flipping them over. Continue cooking for an additional 4-5 minutes until the other side is golden brown and the cheese is gooey.

  4. Serve and enjoy: Once the sandwiches are golden and crisp, remove them from the skillet and let them cool for a minute or two. Slice them diagonally and serve hot.

Pairing Suggestions

The Reuben sandwich pairs wonderfully with a variety of side dishes and beverages. For a classic combination, serve it with a side of crispy potato chips or a dill pickle spear. If you’re feeling adventurous, consider pairing it with a refreshing coleslaw or a tangy potato salad. As for beverages, a cold glass of beer or a fizzy soda complements the bold flavors of the Reuben perfectly.

In Conclusion

The Reuben sandwich has earned its place as a timeless deli favorite, cherished for its rich history and unbeatable flavor profile. Whether you’re preparing it for a casual lunch or a cozy dinner, the Reuben never fails to impress. With its tantalizing blend of ingredients and simple preparation, it’s no wonder this sandwich has maintained its popularity for decades. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and indulge in the satisfying experience of crafting and savoring your very own Classic Reuben Sandwich. Cheers to good food and good company!
