Easy and Flavorful Thanksgiving Gravy Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. One essential component of any Thanksgiving meal is the gravy. Whether you prefer a rich, savory gravy or a lighter, herby version, there are countless ways to create a flavorful and easy gravy that will elevate your holiday feast. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering Thanksgiving gravy recipes that are sure to impress your guests and leave them craving more.

Classic Turkey Gravy

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup turkey drippings
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups chicken or turkey broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. After roasting your turkey, transfer it to a carving board to rest. Place the roasting pan with the drippings on the stovetop over medium heat.
  2. Sprinkle the flour into the drippings and whisk continuously until it forms a smooth paste. Cook for about 2 minutes to get rid of the raw flour taste.
  3. Gradually pour in the chicken or turkey broth, whisking constantly to prevent lumps from forming.
  4. Simmer the gravy for about 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches your desired consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This classic turkey gravy recipe is a foolproof way to create a rich and flavorful gravy that pairs perfectly with roasted turkey, mashed potatoes, and all the traditional Thanksgiving sides.

Vegetarian Mushroom Gravy

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional for a richer gravy)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the shallot and cook until softened, then add the mushrooms and garlic. Cook until the mushrooms are golden brown and most of the liquid has evaporated.
  2. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Cook for a minute to remove the raw flour taste.
  3. Gradually pour in the vegetable broth, stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
  4. Simmer the gravy for 10-15 minutes, allowing it to thicken. If using, stir in the heavy cream for a lusciously creamy texture. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This vegetarian mushroom gravy is a fantastic alternative for those who prefer a meat-free option. Its earthy, umami flavor makes it a delightful addition to any Thanksgiving table.

Herbed Pan Gravy

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup pan drippings
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups chicken or turkey broth
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs (such as thyme, sage, and rosemary)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. After removing the roasted meat from the pan, place the pan on the stovetop over medium heat. Add the flour to the drippings and whisk until smooth.
  2. Pour in the chicken or turkey broth, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Add the chopped herbs and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  3. Let the gravy cook for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the gravy to thicken. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

This herbed pan gravy is a delightful twist on the classic, offering a fragrant and herbaceous flavor that perfectly complements roasted meats and savory sides.

Whether you opt for a traditional turkey gravy, a vegetarian mushroom version, or an herbed pan gravy, these easy and flavorful recipes are guaranteed to take your Thanksgiving feast to the next level. With their simple ingredients and straightforward instructions, you can confidently whip up a delectable gravy that will have everyone reaching for seconds. So this Thanksgiving, treat your loved ones to the gift of mouthwatering gravy that will have them feeling thankful for every savory spoonful.
