Step-by-Step Recipe for Making Soft Sesame Seed Rolls


If you’ve ever walked into a bakery and been captivated by the warm, fragrant aroma of freshly baked bread, you know how comforting the smell of soft rolls can be. One variety that stands out for its texture and flavor is the sesame seed roll. These rolls have a soft, pillowy interior and a slightly crunchy sesame-topped exterior, making them the perfect accompaniment to any meal or a delicious snack on their own.

In this step-by-step guide, we will take you through the process of making soft sesame seed rolls from scratch. Whether you’re an experienced baker or just starting out, this recipe is simple, rewarding, and absolutely irresistible. Follow along, and soon you’ll have a batch of golden, fluffy sesame rolls fresh from your oven.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the steps, let’s gather the ingredients. The beauty of this sesame seed roll recipe is that it requires only a few simple ingredients that you likely already have in your kitchen. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 ½ cups all-purpose flour (plus more for dusting)
  • 1 tablespoon instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (or vegetable oil)
  • 1 ¼ cups warm water (about 110°F)
  • 1 large egg (for the dough)
  • 1 tablespoon milk (for brushing)
  • Sesame seeds (about ½ cup, for topping)

These ingredients are simple but come together to create a soft, fluffy, and flavorful roll with a beautifully golden, sesame-seed crust.

Preparing the Dough

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

To begin, you’ll need to activate your yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water with the sugar. Stir gently to dissolve the sugar, then sprinkle the instant yeast over the surface of the water. Stir the mixture and let it sit for about 5 minutes. You should notice small bubbles or foam starting to form on top, indicating that the yeast is active and ready to use. If you don’t see any bubbles or foam, it may mean your yeast is no longer good, and you should use fresh yeast.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour and salt. The flour provides the structure, while the salt helps to balance the flavor. Make a small well in the center of the dry ingredients to add the wet ingredients later.

Step 3: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Once the yeast is activated, pour it into the well of the dry ingredients. Add the olive oil and the egg to the mixture. Begin stirring everything together using a wooden spoon or spatula until the dough starts to come together.

Step 4: Knead the Dough

Turn the dough out onto a clean, lightly floured surface. Start kneading the dough with your hands for about 8-10 minutes. To knead, fold the dough in half, then press it down and away from you with the heels of your hands. Turn it a quarter turn and repeat the process. You should knead until the dough becomes smooth and elastic, no longer sticky to the touch. If it’s too sticky, sprinkle a little more flour, but be careful not to add too much, as this can make the rolls dense.

Step 5: First Rise

Once your dough is kneaded, form it into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean, damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Place the bowl in a warm area (like your oven with the light on) and let it rise for about 1-1.5 hours. The dough should double in size. This process is known as proofing, and it allows the yeast to ferment, creating the light, airy texture in the rolls.

Shaping the Rolls

Step 6: Punch Down the Dough

After the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. This step is crucial because it ensures the dough is properly aerated and helps to create a tender crumb in your rolls. Turn the dough out onto your lightly floured surface again.

Step 7: Portion and Shape the Rolls

Divide the dough into 12 equal portions. You can do this by rolling the dough into a log and cutting it into 12 even pieces, or you can eyeball it. Once you have your portions, shape each piece into a smooth ball by tucking the edges underneath and pinching them together at the bottom. Place the shaped rolls onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or lightly greased.

Step 8: Second Rise

Cover the baking sheet with a clean kitchen towel and let the rolls rise for another 30-45 minutes. The rolls should puff up and become soft and pillowy. This second rise gives the rolls their soft texture and light crumb. Make sure they are not too crowded on the sheet, as they will expand during baking.

Baking the Sesame Seed Rolls

Step 9: Preheat the Oven

While the rolls are rising, preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures that the rolls bake evenly and get a nice, golden color on the crust.

Step 10: Brush with Milk

Before baking, take your egg wash mixture (made by whisking the egg with a tablespoon of milk) and brush the tops of the rolls gently. This step not only gives the rolls a shiny, beautiful finish but also helps the sesame seeds stick. If you prefer a vegan option, you can substitute the egg wash with a simple milk brush.

Step 11: Add the Sesame Seeds

Once the rolls are brushed with the egg wash, sprinkle a generous amount of sesame seeds over the top. You can use either toasted or untoasted sesame seeds, depending on your preference. The seeds will toast slightly as the rolls bake, adding both texture and a nutty flavor.

Step 12: Bake the Rolls

Place the baking sheet with the rolls into the preheated oven and bake for about 18-22 minutes. Keep an eye on them as baking times can vary slightly depending on your oven. You want the rolls to be golden brown on top with a slight crispiness to the crust. They should feel light and hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Step 13: Cool and Serve

Once the rolls are baked, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool on a wire rack. Let them cool for at least 10 minutes before serving. This allows the steam to escape, making them easier to cut and giving you the perfect soft texture inside.

Tips for Perfect Sesame Seed Rolls

  • Water Temperature: Make sure the water for activating the yeast is warm but not too hot (about 110°F or 45°C). If it’s too hot, it could kill the yeast and prevent the dough from rising.
  • Resting Time: Don’t skip the rising times! Letting the dough rise properly ensures your rolls will be light and fluffy.
  • Flour Type: While all-purpose flour works perfectly, you can also experiment with a combination of whole wheat and all-purpose flour for a slightly denser, more nutty flavor.
  • Customization: Want to add extra flavor? Try mixing in herbs like rosemary or thyme, or even a bit of garlic powder into the dough for a savory twist.

Serving Suggestions

These soft sesame seed rolls are incredibly versatile. They can be served as a side dish to any meal, from soups and salads to roasted meats and sandwiches. They’re also perfect for making sliders or for pairing with a dollop of butter or jam. You can also serve them with a cheese platter or as a complement to any Mediterranean-inspired dish.

Conclusion

Making soft sesame seed rolls from scratch might seem daunting at first, but with this step-by-step guide, you’ll find that it’s a fun and rewarding process. From mixing the dough to baking the rolls to perfection, each step brings you closer to a batch of golden, pillowy rolls that are perfect for any occasion. With just a few simple ingredients and a bit of patience, you can create homemade bread that’s sure to impress your friends and family.

So, the next time you’re in the mood for a delicious homemade bread experience, skip the store-bought rolls and make these sesame seed rolls instead. The warm, comforting aroma of fresh bread baking will fill your home, and the soft, sesame-topped rolls will quickly become a new favorite in your baking repertoire. Happy baking!
