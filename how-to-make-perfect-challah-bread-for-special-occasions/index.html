How to Make Perfect Challah Bread for Special Occasions


Challah bread, with its soft, slightly sweet interior and eye-catching braided exterior, is a baking tradition that’s perfect for special occasions. Whether you’re celebrating a holiday or simply want to impress your friends and family with your baking skills, mastering challah bread is a delightful and rewarding pursuit. This recipe ensures the bread is tender on the inside, beautifully golden on the outside, and has the ideal balance of sweetness and richness.

Why Challah Bread is Special

Challah, a traditional Jewish bread, has a deep cultural and culinary significance. While it’s often associated with Shabbat and Jewish holidays, it has universal appeal whether enjoyed plain, slathered with butter, or repurposed for dishes like French toast or bread pudding. Its braided design reflects care, creativity, and craft, making it a wonderful centerpiece for gatherings of all kinds.

The secret to a great challah lies in its soft, fluffy crumb and lightly sweet flavor that pairs well with a variety of meals. But perhaps the most arresting characteristic of challah bread is its golden, glossy braid—an edible work of art that anyone can learn to create with the right technique.

Preparing the Ingredients

Before diving into the details of this recipe, let’s focus on the ingredients that set challah apart. It’s rich and indulgent thanks to the eggs and oil in the dough. To make this bread, you’ll need:

Dough Ingredients:

  • 4 ½ teaspoons (or 2 packets) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water (approximately 110°F)
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 3 large eggs (plus 1 more for egg wash)
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 4 ½ to 5 cups all-purpose flour

Optional Toppings:

  • Sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or coarse sea salt

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Begin by dissolving the active dry yeast in warm water. Sprinkle a pinch of sugar over the yeast and let it sit for about 5–10 minutes until it becomes foamy. This step is crucial—it ensures the yeast is alive and ready to help your dough rise.

Step 2: Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs together with the sugar, kosher salt, and vegetable oil. Once combined, add the activated yeast mixture and stir to incorporate. Gradually add the flour, one cup at a time, mixing after each addition.

As the dough starts to come together, switch to kneading by hand or using a stand mixer fitted with a dough hook. Continue adding flour until the dough is soft but not sticky, usually around 5 cups total. Knead the dough for about 8–10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

Step 3: First Rise

Once your dough is kneaded, transfer it to a lightly greased bowl, turning once so the top is also greased. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm spot for about 1–2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Step 4: Shape the Challah

After the dough has risen, punch it down gently to release excess air. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Divide it into equal portions depending on the type of braid you want: a three-strand braid, a four-strand braid, or something more elaborate like a round braid for Rosh Hashanah.

Basic Three-Strand Braid:

Roll three equal dough portions into long ropes, each about 16 inches long. Pinch them together at one end, then braid them as you would hair. Pinch the other end to seal the braid, and tuck both ends slightly underneath.

Round Braid:

If you prefer a round challah, divide the dough into four portions and braid it in a circular style, weaving the strands thoughtfully to create a symmetrical pattern.

Step 5: Second Rise

Place the braided dough on a parchment-lined baking sheet. Cover lightly with a clean kitchen towel and let it rise again for 30–45 minutes. This second rise is crucial, as it helps the bread become even airier and softer.

Step 6: Egg Wash and Optional Toppings

Preheat your oven to 350°F. Beat one egg in a small bowl and brush it generously over the entire surface of the challah. This egg wash gives the bread its signature golden color and glossy finish. If desired, sprinkle toppings such as sesame seeds, poppy seeds, or coarse sea salt over the egg wash for added texture and flavor.

Step 7: Bake

Bake the challah in a preheated oven at 350°F for 25–30 minutes, or until the loaf is beautifully golden and sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. If the top begins to brown too quickly, tent it with aluminum foil halfway through baking.

Step 8: Cool and Serve

Once baked, transfer the challah to a wire rack to cool completely. Though the temptation to slice into it immediately may be strong, allowing the bread to cool helps it retain its structure.

Pro Tips for Perfect Challah

  1. Mastering Braiding: Practice makes perfect when it comes to braiding. If it’s your first attempt, start simple with a three-strand braid before advancing to more intricate designs.
  2. Adjusting Flour: The amount of flour needed can vary based on humidity and other factors. Trust your instincts—use just enough to make a soft, workable dough.
  3. Timing the Rises: Don’t rush the rising process. Allowing the dough sufficient time for each rise is critical for achieving a light and airy loaf.
  4. Storage: Challah stays fresh for a day or two at room temperature, stored in an airtight container. For longer storage, freeze it after baking and cooling.

Ways to Enjoy Challah

Challah’s versatility makes it a beloved bread for many purposes. Serve it as-is with butter, jam, or honey for an indulgent treat. Transform it into decadent French toast by soaking slices in a mixture of eggs, milk, and vanilla before cooking. Leftovers also shine in bread pudding recipes or as a base for savory stuffing during holiday feasts.

Challah bread isn’t just food—it’s a labor of love, a tradition, and a connection to celebrations across generations. Taking the time to make challah from scratch allows you to share the joy and warmth of homemade bread with those you care about, creating memories as special as the occasions you’re baking for.

Start your challah-making journey today and discover the magic of turning simple ingredients into something extraordinary.
