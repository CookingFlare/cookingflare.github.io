Unforgettable Thanksgiving Soup Recipes to Warm Your Soul


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and indulging in a feast that warms both the body and the soul. While traditional Thanksgiving dishes like turkey, stuffing, and cranberry sauce often take center stage, why not start a new tradition this year by adding a comforting and flavorful soup to your holiday menu? In this blog post, we present to you three unforgettable Thanksgiving soup recipes that will not only tantalize your taste buds but also warm your soul.

1. Creamy Butternut Squash Soup

Preparation time: 10 minutes Cooking time: 40 minutes Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  2. Add the cubed butternut squash to the pot and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the butternut squash is tender.
  4. Using an immersion blender or a countertop blender, puree the soup until smooth and creamy.
  5. Return the soup to the pot and stir in the coconut milk, ground cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Heat the soup gently until warmed through.
  6. Ladle the creamy butternut squash soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot and enjoy the rich flavors of fall!

2. Hearty Turkey and Wild Rice Soup

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 1 hour Serves: 8

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of cooked turkey, shredded
  • 1 cup of wild rice, rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 6 cups of turkey or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, sauté the diced onion, carrots, celery, and minced garlic in a little bit of olive oil until the vegetables are tender.
  2. Add the cooked turkey, wild rice, turkey or chicken broth, dried thyme, bay leaf, salt, and pepper to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the rice is cooked through.
  3. Remove the bay leaf from the soup and discard.
  4. Ladle the hearty turkey and wild rice soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley. Serve hot and savor the comforting flavors of Thanksgiving.

3. Spiced Pumpkin Soup with Maple Glazed Pecans

Preparation time: 15 minutes Cooking time: 40 minutes Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of pumpkin puree
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup of pecans
  • 2 tablespoons of maple syrup

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  2. Add the pumpkin puree to the pot and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 25-30 minutes.
  4. While the soup is simmering, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). In a small bowl, mix the pecans and maple syrup until the pecans are well coated.
  5. Spread the maple-glazed pecans onto a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the pecans are golden brown and fragrant.
  6. Using an immersion blender or a countertop blender, puree the soup until smooth and creamy.
  7. Return the soup to the pot and stir in the coconut milk, ground cumin, ground nutmeg, salt, and pepper. Heat the soup gently until warmed through.
  8. Ladle the spiced pumpkin soup into bowls and top with the maple-glazed pecans. Serve hot and relish in the delightful flavors of autumn.

These unforgettable Thanksgiving soup recipes are sure to become a beloved addition to your holiday menu. They not only provide comfort and warmth but also showcase the flavors of the season. Whether you choose the creamy butternut squash soup, the hearty turkey and wild rice soup, or the spiced pumpkin soup with maple-glazed pecans, your guests will be treated to a memorable and soul-warming culinary experience. So, this Thanksgiving, gather around the table and savor the goodness of these delightful soups that celebrate the spirit of gratitude and togetherness.
