Grilled Fruit Desserts: Sweet and Refreshing Endings to Your BBQ


Summer is here, and what better way to celebrate the warm weather than with a delicious BBQ? While burgers and hot dogs are always a hit, why not add some sweet treats to your menu? Grilled fruit desserts are a refreshing and unique way to end your outdoor gathering. Not only are they easy to make, but they’re also perfect for hot summer days.

Grilled Pineapple with Brown Sugar and Cinnamon

This classic combination is a staple for a reason. The sweetness of the pineapple pairs perfectly with the warm spices, creating a dessert that’s both familiar and exciting.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe pineapple, cut into 1-inch slices
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Brush both sides of the pineapple slices with the sugar mixture.
  4. Grill the pineapple for 2-3 minutes per side, or until caramelized.
  5. Serve warm, with a dollop of heavy cream if desired.

Grilled Peach Cobbler

This twist on the classic cobbler is perfect for warm weather. The grilled peaches add a smoky flavor that pairs perfectly with the crispy, buttery crust.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe peaches, sliced
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the sliced peaches, granulated sugar, flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, heavy cream, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients over the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Pour the mixture into a 9x13 inch baking dish and grill for 20-25 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the peaches are tender.
  6. Serve warm, topped with vanilla ice cream if desired.

Grilled Banana Foster

This classic dessert gets a new twist when grilled. The caramelized bananas and brown sugar sauce are a match made in heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe bananas, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons rum (optional)
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the butter, brown sugar, and rum (if using).
  3. Brush both sides of the banana slices with the sugar mixture.
  4. Grill the bananas for 2-3 minutes per side, or until caramelized.
  5. Serve warm, topped with heavy cream, vanilla extract, and salt.
  6. Sprinkle with chopped walnuts if desired.

Grilled Fruit Skewers with Honey and Yogurt

This light and refreshing dessert is perfect for hot summer days. The combination of sweet and tangy flavors is a winner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mixed fruit (such as strawberries, grapes, and pineapple)
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 10 bamboo skewers

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.
  2. Thread the mixed fruit onto the bamboo skewers.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together the honey, yogurt, lemon juice, and salt.
  4. Brush the honey yogurt mixture onto the fruit.
  5. Grill the skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until the fruit is tender.
  6. Serve warm, garnished with additional honey and yogurt if desired.

These grilled fruit desserts are the perfect way to end your summer BBQ. They’re easy to make, delicious, and refreshing. Whether you’re looking for something classic and familiar or new and exciting, there’s a grilled fruit dessert on this list for you. So go ahead, fire up your grill, and get ready for a sweet and satisfying ending to your outdoor gathering.
