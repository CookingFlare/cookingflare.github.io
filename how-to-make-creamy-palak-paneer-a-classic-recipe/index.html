How to Make Creamy Palak Paneer: A Classic Recipe


Palak Paneer, a dish that needs no introduction, but requires a little effort to get it just right. This creamy and rich vegetarian delight is a staple of North Indian cuisine, and for good reason. The combination of tender paneer (Indian cheese), velvety spinach puree, and a subtle blend of spices is a match made in heaven. The Secret to a Silky Palak Paneer The key to a magnificent Palak Paneer lies in its sauce. A good Palak Paneer sauce should be smooth, rich, and have just the right amount of creaminess without being too heavy. To achieve this, we will use a combination of spinach puree, heavy cream, and a little bit of magic – tomato puree. Yes, you read that right, tomato puree! It may seem unusual, but trust us on this one. The acidity and sweetness of the tomatoes will help to balance out the richness of the cream and spinach, resulting in a sauce that's both creamy and light. Gather Your Ingredients Before we dive into the recipe, let's take a look at what you'll need:

1 1/2 cups fresh spinach leaves 1/2 cup paneer (Indian cheese), cut into small cubes 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or vegetable oil 1 onion, finely chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 teaspoon ground cumin 1 teaspoon ground coriander 1/2 teaspoon garam masala 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder (optional) 2 tablespoons tomato puree 1/4 cup heavy cream 2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro (for garnish)

The Recipe: Palak Paneer in 5 Simple Steps

Start by Blending the Spinach: Wash and dry the spinach leaves. In a blender or food processor, combine the spinach leaves, 1 tablespoon ghee (or oil), and a pinch of salt. Blend until you get a smooth puree. Set aside. Fry the Onions: Heat 1 tablespoon ghee (or oil) in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they're lightly browned and fragrant. This will take about 5 minutes. Add the Spice Blend: Add the cumin, coriander, garam masala, and chili powder (if using) to the pan. Sauté for another minute, stirring constantly, until the spices are fragrant. Combine the Spinach and Paneer: Add the spinach puree, paneer cubes, and salt to the saucepan. Stir well to combine. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 2-3 minutes. Finish with Cream and Tomatoes: Stir in the tomato puree and heavy cream. Simmer the sauce for another 2-3 minutes, or until it reaches your desired consistency. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.

Serve and Enjoy To serve, place the Palak Paneer on a bed of steaming basmati rice or with a side of naan bread. Garnish with chopped cilantro and a sprinkle of garam masala. The result is a dish that's both comforting and elegant – perfect for a special occasion or a cozy night in. Tips and Variations

For a creamier sauce, add more heavy cream or substitute it with coconut cream. Add some heat with a diced jalapeño or red chili pepper. Experiment with different spices, such as ground cinnamon or nutmeg, to create a unique flavor profile. To make Palak Paneer ahead of time, cook the sauce without the cream and paneer. Refrigerate or freeze until ready to serve. Simply reheat and add the cream and paneer before serving.

Conclusion Palak Paneer is a dish that's both humble and indulgent, comforting and elegant. With these simple steps and a few tricks up your sleeve, you'll be whipping up a batch of creamy Palak Paneer in no time. So go ahead, give it a try, and taste the magic for yourself!
