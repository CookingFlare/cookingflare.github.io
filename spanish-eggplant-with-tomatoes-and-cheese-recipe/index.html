Spanish Eggplant with Tomatoes and Cheese Recipe


If you’re looking for a delicious and flavorful vegetarian dish, look no further than this Spanish Eggplant with Tomatoes and Cheese recipe. Packed with the rich flavors of ripe tomatoes, creamy cheese, and tender eggplant, this dish is sure to impress even the most discerning palates.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 4 ripe tomatoes
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 200g of your favorite cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or manchego)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Start by preparing the eggplant. Cut off the ends and slice it into 1/2-inch thick rounds. Lay the slices on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and sprinkle them with salt. Let them sit for about 15 minutes to draw out any excess moisture. This will help prevent the eggplant from becoming mushy when cooked.

  3. While the eggplant is resting, prepare the tomato sauce. Bring a pot of water to a boil and add the tomatoes. Boil them for about 1 minute, then transfer them to a bowl of ice water to cool. Once cooled, remove the skin and chop the tomatoes into small pieces.

  4. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until they are soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.

  5. Add the chopped tomatoes to the skillet, along with the paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients and let the sauce simmer for about 10 minutes, until it thickens slightly.

  6. While the tomato sauce is simmering, rinse the salt off the eggplant slices and pat them dry with a paper towel. Brush each slice with a little olive oil and arrange them on a baking sheet.

  7. Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven and bake the eggplant slices for about 15 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly golden brown.

  8. Once the eggplant is cooked, remove it from the oven and reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (180°C).

  9. Now it’s time to assemble the dish. In a baking dish, arrange a layer of baked eggplant slices, followed by a layer of tomato sauce. Repeat this layering process until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of tomato sauce on top.

  10. Finally, grate your favorite cheese over the top of the dish. You can use cheddar, mozzarella, or manchego cheese for a rich and melty finish.

  11. Place the baking dish back in the oven and bake for an additional 20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  12. Once the dish is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs like basil or parsley for an extra burst of flavor.

Serving Suggestions

This Spanish Eggplant with Tomatoes and Cheese recipe makes a satisfying main course, but it can also be served as a flavorful side dish. Pair it with a fresh green salad and crusty bread for a complete and satisfying meal. It’s perfect for a cozy dinner at home or for entertaining guests.

Conclusion

Spanish Eggplant with Tomatoes and Cheese is a delightful dish that showcases the vibrant flavors of Mediterranean cuisine. With its combination of tender eggplant, tangy tomatoes, and creamy cheese, it’s a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to add more plant-based meals to your repertoire, this recipe is sure to become a family favorite. Enjoy the flavors of Spain right in your own kitchen!
