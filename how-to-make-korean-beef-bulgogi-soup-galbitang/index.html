How to Make Korean Beef Bulgogi Soup (Galbitang)


If you’ve ever craved a hearty, comforting, and flavorful dish, Korean Beef Bulgogi Soup—or Galbitang—is a must-try recipe. Known for its rich beef essence and nourishing ingredients, this traditional Korean soup is an excellent option for both special occasions and weeknight dinners. Its savory broth, tender beef short ribs, and distinct yet balanced seasonings make it a crowd-pleaser.

Galbitang is often associated with celebrations but is equally beloved as a home-cooked meal. With just a handful of steps and a few simple yet impactful ingredients, this soup will elevate your culinary repertoire while transporting you to the heart of Korea’s vibrant food culture. Let’s dive into this recipe and learn how to make an unforgettable bowl of Galbitang at home!

Ingredients for Authentic Korean Galbitang

Before starting, gather the necessary ingredients to ensure success in crafting this dish. Most components can be easily found at your local Korean or Asian grocery store. Here’s what you’ll need:

Main Ingredients:

  • Beef Short Ribs: 1.5 to 2 pounds, cut into smaller pieces for faster cooking (ask your butcher for “flanken-style” ribs if possible).
  • Water: 6 to 8 cups, depending on desired broth volume.
  • Radish (Korean Mu): 1 small piece, peeled and sliced into large cubes.
  • Garlic: 8–10 cloves, peeled and smashed.
  • Green Onions: 3–4 stalks, sliced into long sections for the broth, with extra chopped scallions for garnish.

Seasoning Ingredients:

  • Soy Sauce: 2 tablespoons.
  • Salt: Adjust to taste.
  • Ground Black Pepper: ½ teaspoon (freshly ground is best for extra aroma).
  • Sesame Oil: 1 teaspoon for added fragrance.
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon (optional, to balance flavors).
  • Optional Garnish: Thinly sliced egg garnish (known as “yellow egg strips”) or thin glass noodles for texture.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Galbitang

1. Preparing the Beef Short Ribs

Beef short ribs are the backbone of a flavorful Galbitang, so preparing them properly is essential. Begin by soaking the short ribs in cold water for at least 30 minutes to remove excess blood. This step is critical for achieving a clean broth that isn’t overly cloudy. If necessary, change the water halfway through soaking for better results.

Once soaked, drain the ribs and give them a light rinse under cold water. Blanch the short ribs: Add them to a large pot of boiling water and let them cook for 5 minutes. Drain and rinse again to remove impurities, ensuring you achieve the hallmark clarity of Galbitang.

2. Cooking the Broth

In a clean, large stockpot (preferably a heavy-bottom pot for even cooking), add the blanched ribs along with cold water. Heat until boiling, then reduce to a simmer. Add the garlic cloves and Korean radish cubes. Let the mixture simmer for 90 minutes to 2 hours so the ribs fully tenderize and the flavors of the beef permeate the broth.

Pay attention to skimming any foam or impurities that rise to the top during the cooking process. This step ensures a pristine, flavorful broth.

3. Seasoning the Soup

Once your broth has developed a rich and deep flavor from the ribs, radish, and garlic, it’s time to add your seasonings. Stir in soy sauce, sesame oil, and ground black pepper. Adjust the salt level to your preference; taste as you go to ensure the flavors complement the natural meatiness of the soup.

If you prefer a slightly sweeter undertone, you can add a small pinch of sugar, blending it thoroughly so it doesn’t overpower the dish.

4. Optional Add-Ons for Texture

Galbitang is delicious as it is, but you can elevate its texture with toppings and additional elements:

  • Glass Noodles: Soak thin mung bean or sweet potato glass noodles in cold water for 20 minutes, then cook them briefly and add them to the broth during the final stages of simmering.
  • Egg Garnish: Whisk a single egg yolk and fry it into a thin sheet. Slice the cooked egg into strips and layer it atop the soup before serving.

These extras enhance the dish visually while providing added layers of flavor and texture.

5. Serving the Galbitang

Once the ribs are tender, the broth flavorful, and the seasonings balanced, your Galbitang is ready to serve. Spoon the soup into individual bowls and garnish generously with chopped green onions. The vibrant green adds freshness to this ultra-satisfying dish.

Serve piping hot alongside a bowl of steamed rice. Korean tradition often includes kimchi and other banchan (side dishes) for a complete meal. A sprinkle of sesame seeds across your Galbitang can further complement the nutty flavors.

Tips for Perfecting Your Galbitang

Creating the perfect Galbitang is as much an art as it is a science. Here are some essential tips to refine your cooking process:

  1. Choose Quality Short Ribs: Seek out well-marbled and fresh beef ribs for a richer broth. Grass-fed beef adds depth to the broth that is both savory and nuanced.
  2. Don’t Rush: Simmering the soup for an extended period is key. Flavors meld together gradually, so patience is a virtue when making Galbitang.
  3. Customizable Flavors: Feel free to tweak seasonings based on personal preferences. Korean food often balances sweetness, saltiness, and savoriness—so experiment until you find what works best for your palate!
  4. Leftover Soup: If you have leftovers, store the broth and beef separately for reheating. This keeps the broth crisp and flavorful while avoiding unnecessary fat coagulation.

The History Behind Galbitang

Galbitang has been enjoyed for centuries in Korea as both a celebration dish and a comfort food. Its name stems from two Korean words: “galbi,” which refers to short ribs, and “tang,” meaning soup. Historically, Galbitang became popular during Korea’s royal court era due to its luxurious ingredients and painstaking preparation.

This dish eventually made its way into humble households, evolving into the hearty rendition we enjoy today. It carries a deep sense of tradition, representing homestyle meals shared with loved ones and showcasing the connection Koreans have with their cuisine.

Why You’ll Love Galbitang

Galbitang is a testament to how simple ingredients can transform into something extraordinary. The combination of tender beef, clear broth, and aromatic seasonings results in a dish that feels indulgent yet grounded. Whether you make it for yourself or share it with others, the warmth of this soup nourishes the body and soul.

From its historical significance to its incredible depth of flavor, Galbitang is the kind of dish that will keep your guests asking for seconds. Try making this rich and savory Korean soup to share in the joy of culinary exploration!
