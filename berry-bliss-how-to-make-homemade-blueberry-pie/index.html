Berry Bliss: How to Make Homemade Blueberry Pie


There’s nothing quite as comforting and delicious as a homemade blueberry pie. The combination of sweet, juicy blueberries with a flaky crust is a match made in heaven. If you’re looking to indulge in a dessert that will leave you craving for more, then this recipe is for you. Get ready to experience berry bliss with this step-by-step guide on how to make a mouth-watering homemade blueberry pie.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice water
  • 6 cups fresh blueberries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold, cubed butter and using a pastry cutter or your fingertips, cut the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  2. Gradually add the ice water, a tablespoon at a time, and mix until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix. Divide the dough in half and shape each half into a disk. Wrap them in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  3. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  4. In a separate bowl, combine the blueberries, sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, lemon zest, cinnamon, and salt. Gently toss the mixture until the blueberries are evenly coated.

  5. Roll out one of the dough disks on a lightly floured surface to fit a 9-inch pie dish. Carefully transfer the rolled dough to the pie dish, gently pressing it into the bottom and sides.

  6. Pour the blueberry mixture into the pie crust, ensuring it is evenly distributed. Dot the top with small pieces of butter.

  7. Roll out the second dough disk and place it over the blueberry filling. Trim any excess dough and crimp the edges to seal the pie.

  8. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking. You can also create decorative patterns if desired.

  9. Place the pie on a baking sheet to catch any juices that may bubble over. Bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.

  10. Remove the pie from the oven and allow it to cool for at least 2 hours before serving. This will give the filling time to set.

Serving Suggestions

Serve this delectable homemade blueberry pie warm or at room temperature. It pairs perfectly with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of freshly whipped cream. The combination of the sweet and tart blueberry filling with the buttery, flaky crust is simply divine.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, add a teaspoon of vanilla extract to the blueberry filling.
  • You can substitute fresh blueberries with frozen ones if they are not in season. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before using.
  • Experiment with different spices like nutmeg or cardamom to add a unique twist to your blueberry pie.
  • If you prefer a lattice crust, simply cut the rolled-out dough into strips and weave them over the filling.
  • This blueberry pie can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Just cover it tightly with plastic wrap or foil to keep it fresh.

Now that you have the recipe for a heavenly homemade blueberry pie, it’s time to gather your ingredients and get baking. Enjoy the process of creating this delightful dessert and savor every mouthful of the berry bliss it brings. Happy baking!
