How to Make Perfect Falafel from Scratch


Falafel is one of the most beloved dishes around the world, known for its crispy, golden exterior and soft, flavorful interior. This traditional Middle Eastern dish is made from ground chickpeas (or fava beans) mixed with fresh herbs and spices, then deep-fried to perfection. If you’ve ever had the pleasure of tasting falafel from a street vendor or restaurant, you know how irresistible it can be. But did you know that making falafel at home from scratch can be even more delicious? In this post, we’ll walk you through the process of making falafel from scratch, with tips and techniques to ensure that your falafel turns out perfect every time.

What is Falafel?

Falafel is a deep-fried ball or patty made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, fresh herbs, and spices. It is often served in pita bread or flatbread and topped with vegetables, sauces like tahini or yogurt, and sometimes pickles. Originating in the Middle East, falafel has become popular worldwide due to its delicious taste and vegetarian-friendly ingredients.

While falafel is traditionally made with chickpeas or fava beans, chickpeas (also known as garbanzo beans) are the more common choice. The recipe includes a variety of fresh herbs and spices, such as parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, and onion, which give it a rich flavor profile. The texture should be crispy on the outside while remaining soft and fluffy on the inside.

Why Make Falafel from Scratch?

There are plenty of pre-made falafel mixes available at the store, but making falafel from scratch allows you to control the quality of the ingredients and customize the seasoning to your taste. Homemade falafel also tends to have a fresher, more vibrant flavor and better texture than store-bought versions. Plus, it’s a fun and rewarding experience to make falafel from scratch, and it’s an opportunity to experiment with your own variations of the dish.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To make the perfect falafel, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Main Ingredients:

  • Dried chickpeas (garbanzo beans) – 1 cup
  • Fresh parsley – 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Fresh cilantro – 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Onion – 1 small, roughly chopped
  • Garlic cloves – 4 to 5
  • Ground cumin – 1 1/2 teaspoons
  • Ground coriander – 1 teaspoon
  • Salt – 1 teaspoon, or to taste
  • Black pepper – 1/2 teaspoon, or to taste
  • Baking soda – 1 teaspoon
  • Flour (all-purpose or chickpea flour) – 3 to 4 tablespoons, or as needed
  • Water – 1/4 cup (for soaking)
  • Lemon juice – 1 tablespoon (optional, but recommended for a zesty flavor)
  • Olive oil – for frying (or vegetable oil)

Optional Add-ins:

  • Chili flakes – for heat (adjust to your preference)
  • Cumin seeds – for a deeper cumin flavor
  • Sesame seeds – to coat the falafel before frying for extra crunch

The key to making great falafel is to use dried chickpeas and not canned chickpeas. Canned chickpeas are too soft and won’t hold up well when fried, while dried chickpeas provide the ideal texture when ground and mixed with spices.

How to Prepare the Ingredients

Soaking the Chickpeas

Before you begin making the falafel mixture, you need to soak the dried chickpeas. Soaking allows the beans to absorb water and become soft, making them easier to grind and ensuring they have the right texture when fried.

  1. Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover them with cold water. Make sure the water level is several inches above the chickpeas, as they will expand as they absorb the water.
  2. Let the chickpeas soak for 8 hours or overnight. If you’re short on time, you can soak them in hot water for about 2 hours, but overnight soaking is ideal for the best texture.
  3. After soaking, drain the chickpeas well and pat them dry with a towel to remove any excess moisture.

Preparing the Herbs and Spices

While the chickpeas are soaking, prepare the herbs and spices that will give your falafel its signature flavor:

  1. Chop the fresh parsley and cilantro finely, as this will help them blend evenly into the mixture.
  2. Peel and crush the garlic cloves. You can either use a garlic press or finely chop them by hand.
  3. Measure out the ground cumin, ground coriander, black pepper, and salt.

Once everything is prepped, you’re ready to make the falafel mixture.

How to Make the Falafel Mixture

  1. Grind the Chickpeas: After draining and drying the soaked chickpeas, transfer them to a food processor. Pulse the chickpeas until they are coarsely ground. The mixture should resemble a crumbly, slightly coarse texture, not a smooth paste. If it’s too smooth, the falafel will have a mushy texture once fried.

  2. Add the Herbs and Spices: Add the parsley, cilantro, garlic, onion, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper to the ground chickpeas. Pulse again until everything is well mixed, but be careful not to over-process it.

  3. Add Baking Soda and Flour: Once the herbs and spices are incorporated, add the baking soda and flour to the mixture. The baking soda helps the falafel puff up and become light and airy when fried, while the flour acts as a binder. If the mixture seems too wet, add a little more flour until it holds together when formed into a ball or patty.

  4. Test the Mixture: Before you shape all the falafel, test the mixture by taking a small amount and forming it into a ball. Heat some oil in a small pan and fry the test ball for 2-3 minutes. If it holds together and fries up crisp on the outside, the mixture is ready. If it falls apart, add a little more flour and refrigerate the mixture for 30 minutes before testing again.

Shaping the Falafel

Falafel can be shaped into either balls or patties, depending on your preference. The traditional shape is a ball, but patties are often used in falafel sandwiches for easier handling.

  1. Wet your hands slightly to prevent the mixture from sticking. Scoop out small amounts of the falafel mixture (about 1 1/2 tablespoons each) and roll them into balls. Alternatively, flatten them into small patties about 1/2-inch thick.

  2. Place the shaped falafel on a tray or plate while you prepare the oil for frying. Be sure to leave a little space between each one so they don’t stick together.

How to Fry the Falafel

The most authentic way to cook falafel is to deep-fry it in hot oil. Frying gives the falafel its signature crisp, golden exterior. Follow these steps for perfect falafel every time:

  1. Heat the Oil: Pour enough oil into a deep frying pan or pot to submerge the falafel. Heat the oil over medium-high heat to about 350°F (175°C). If you don’t have a thermometer, drop a small piece of the falafel mixture into the oil. If it sizzles immediately and rises to the top, the oil is ready.

  2. Fry the Falafel: Carefully drop the falafel balls or patties into the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Fry the falafel in batches if necessary. Cook each batch for 3-4 minutes, turning them occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy on all sides.

  3. Drain Excess Oil: Use a slotted spoon to remove the falafel from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to absorb any excess oil.

  4. Keep Warm: If you’re frying in batches, you can keep the cooked falafel warm in a 200°F (90°C) oven until all the falafel are fried.

Serving Your Homemade Falafel

Now that your falafel is golden and crispy, it’s time to serve it! Falafel can be enjoyed in a variety of ways, so feel free to get creative.

Falafel in Pita Bread:

  • Cut a pita bread in half and stuff it with falafel balls, along with fresh veggies like lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions.
  • Drizzle with tahini sauce, yogurt, or a spicy hot sauce for extra flavor.

Falafel Platter:

  • Serve falafel on a platter with side dishes like hummus, tabbouleh, baba ganoush, or a simple cucumber and tomato salad.
  • Include some warm pita or flatbread on the side for dipping.

As a Snack or Appetizer:

  • Serve the falafel as an appetizer with a dipping sauce like tahini, tzatziki, or harissa.

Tips for Perfect Falafel

  1. Don’t Overprocess the Mixture: The key to crispy falafel is a coarse texture. Over-processing the chickpeas can result in a mushy falafel.
  2. Soak, Don’t Cook, the Chickpeas: Do not use canned chickpeas in your falafel. Soaked, uncooked chickpeas give falafel the best texture.
  3. Fry in Batches: Overcrowding the pan will lower the oil temperature and result in greasy falafel. Fry in small batches to ensure they crisp up properly.
  4. Rest the Mixture: Letting the falafel mixture rest in the fridge for at least 30 minutes helps the flavors meld and the mixture firm up, making it easier to shape and fry.

Conclusion

Making falafel from scratch is a rewarding experience that results in the most flavorful, crispy, and satisfying falafel you’ve ever tasted. Whether you enjoy it in a pita sandwich, as a platter with sides, or as a snack with dipping sauces, homemade falafel is sure to impress. With the right ingredients, techniques, and a little patience, you can make falafel that rivals your favorite restaurant or street vendor. Enjoy experimenting with flavors and customize your falafel to suit your tastes—after all, falafel is a dish that’s as versatile as it is delicious.
