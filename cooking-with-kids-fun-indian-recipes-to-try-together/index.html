Cooking with Kids: Fun Indian Recipes to Try Together


As a parent, there's nothing more rewarding than sharing the joys of cooking with your little ones. Not only does it Bond you together, but it also helps teach essential life skills like measurement, following instructions, and kitchen safety. In this post, we'll explore some fun and easy-to-make Indian recipes that are perfect for kids to join in on the culinary fun.

  1. Vegetable Biryani Vegetable biryani is a classic Indian dish that's a staple in many households. It's an excellent way to introduce kids to the world of spice blending and cooking different textures. This recipe is a great place to start because it's relatively simple and requires minimal supervision. Ingredients:

1 cup basmati rice 2 cups Mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, cauliflower, and potatoes) 1 onion, chopped 2 cloves garlic, minced 1 tablespoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1 teaspoon garam masala powder Salt, to taste 2 tablespoons vegetable oil Chopped cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

Cook the rice according to package instructions. Set aside. Heat oil in a large pan and sauté the onion, garlic, and cumin seeds until fragrant. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté until they're tender. Add the coriander, garam masala, and salt, and mix well. Combine the cooked rice with the vegetable mixture and stir gently. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro.

Teaching moments: Encourage your child to help measure the ingredients, sauté the vegetables, and mix the rice and vegetable mixture. 2. Tandoori Chicken Tandoori chicken is a finger-licking favorite among kids. It's a great way to introduce them to the concept of marinating and grilling. With some adult supervision, kids can help prepare the marinade and even flip the chicken on the grill. Ingredients:

1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs 1/2 cup plain yogurt 2 tablespoons lemon juice 1 teaspoon garam masala powder 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt, to taste 1/4 cup chopped cilantro 1/4 cup butter or oil

Instructions:

In a blender or food processor, blend the yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and salt to form a smooth marinade. Add the marinade to the chicken and mix well. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Preheat the grill or grill pan to medium heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and cook until cooked through. Brush with butter or oil and garnish with chopped cilantro.

Teaching moments: Encourage your child to help measure and mix the marinade, and teach them how to handle raw chicken safely. 3. Chaat Masala Papadum Papadum is a popular Indian snack that's easy to make and fun to decorate. Kids can help crush the papadum, mix the chaat masala, and assemble the dish. Ingredients:

10-12 papadum sheets 2 tablespoons chaat masala powder 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/4 teaspoon coriander powder 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper Salt, to taste Chopped cilantro, for garnish Green chutney or yogurt, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

Crush the papadum sheets into small pieces and mix with the chaat masala, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, and salt. Add chopped cilantro and mix well. Serve with green chutney or yogurt, if desired.

Teaching moments: Encourage your child to help crush the papadum, mix the spice blend, and assemble the snack. 4. Gujiya Gujiya is a sweet Indian dessert that's perfect for kids to learn about sweet and spicy flavors. They can help mix the dough, fill the gujiyas, and decorate them. Ingredients:

2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup ghee or oil 1/4 cup sugar 1/2 teaspoon salt 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands Chopped nuts or dried fruit, for filling

Instructions:

Mix the flour, ghee or oil, sugar, salt, cardamom powder, and saffron strands to form a crumbly dough. Knead the dough for a few minutes until smooth. Divide the dough into small balls and flatten each into a disk shape. Place a tablespoon or two of chopped nuts or dried fruit in the center of each disk and fold the dough to form a triangle or square shape. Fry or bake the gujiyas until golden brown.

Teaching moments: Encourage your child to help knead the dough, fill the gujiyas, and decorate them with colorful sprinkles or powdered sugar. Tips and Tricks

Always supervise your child while cooking, especially when handling sharp objects or hot surfaces. Encourage your child to practice following recipes and measuring ingredients to help build their fine motor skills. Make cooking a fun and interactive experience by letting your child choose the recipe or contribute to the menu planning. Be patient and offer positive feedback to encourage your child to continue exploring their culinary creativity.

In conclusion, cooking with kids is a wonderful way to spend quality time together, learn new skills, and create lifelong memories. Whether you're making vegetable biryani, tandoori chicken, chaat masala papadum, or gujiya, the possibilities are endless. Don't be afraid to get messy and have fun – your child will thank you for it!
