Satisfying Sides: The Best Wines to Pair with Your Accompaniments


When it comes to cooking a delicious meal, we often focus on the main dish while neglecting the importance of sides. A well-chosen side dish can elevate a meal and take it from ordinary to extraordinary. But have you ever wondered which wines would pair perfectly with your sides? In this blog post, we will explore the best wines to complement your accompaniments and enhance your dining experience.

1. Creamy Mashed Potatoes

Creamy mashed potatoes are a classic side dish that pairs well with a variety of wines. To enhance the buttery richness of the potatoes, consider serving a Chardonnay. This white wine offers notes of oak and vanilla that beautifully complement the creamy texture of the potatoes. For a lighter option, a Sauvignon Blanc with its crisp acidity can also be a great choice.

2. Roasted Vegetables

Roasted vegetables are a versatile and flavorful side dish that can be enjoyed with a wide range of wines. For a medley of root vegetables, such as carrots, parsnips, and sweet potatoes, a Cabernet Sauvignon can be an excellent choice. The wine’s bold flavors and tannins complement the earthiness of the vegetables. If you prefer a lighter option, a Pinot Noir can provide a delicate balance of fruitiness and acidity.

3. Garlic Sauteed Spinach

Garlic sauteed spinach is a simple yet satisfying side dish that pairs well with a variety of wines. The herbaceous flavors of the spinach can be beautifully complemented by a crisp and refreshing white wine like a Sauvignon Blanc or a Pinot Grigio. These wines offer citrusy notes that cut through the richness of the garlic and enhance the overall flavor profile of the dish.

4. Grilled Asparagus

Grilled asparagus is a delightful side dish that lends itself well to a wide range of wines. The smoky charred flavors of the asparagus can be enhanced by a fruity and aromatic white wine such as a Riesling or a Gewürztraminer. These wines provide a balance of sweetness and acidity that complements the natural flavors of the asparagus.

5. Baked Mac and Cheese

Baked mac and cheese is a comforting and indulgent side dish that deserves a wine pairing that can match its richness. A full-bodied red wine like a Merlot or a Cabernet Franc can stand up to the creamy and cheesy flavors of the dish. The wine’s tannins help cleanse the palate and prevent the dish from becoming too heavy.

6. Quinoa Salad

Quinoa salad is a healthy and flavorful side dish that pairs well with a light and crisp white wine like a Sauvignon Blanc or a Chenin Blanc. These wines offer refreshing acidity that complements the nutty and earthy flavors of the quinoa. For a touch of sweetness, consider pairing the salad with a Riesling or a Gewürztraminer.

7. Sweet Potato Fries

Sweet potato fries are a delicious and popular side dish that can be enjoyed with a variety of wines. To enhance the natural sweetness of the fries, consider serving a sparkling wine like a Prosecco or a Champagne. The wine’s effervescence and fruity notes provide a delightful contrast to the crispy texture of the fries.

Conclusion

Pairing the right wine with your sides can greatly enhance your dining experience and take your meal to new heights. Whether you’re enjoying creamy mashed potatoes or crispy sweet potato fries, there is a wine out there that can perfectly complement your accompaniments. Experiment with different pairings and let your taste buds guide you to discover the perfect combination. Cheers to satisfying sides and unforgettable meals!
