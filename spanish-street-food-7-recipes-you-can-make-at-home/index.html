Spanish Street Food: 7 Recipes You Can Make at Home


Spanish cuisine is celebrated all over the world for its vibrant flavors, fresh ingredients, and rich heritage. While tapas and paella frequently steal the spotlight, the country’s street food culture is equally tantalizing, offering a unique opportunity to taste authentic Spanish flavors on the go. From bustling markets in Madrid to seaside stalls in Barcelona, Spain’s street food scene is a feast for the senses. The best part? You can recreate these dishes in your very own kitchen. Below are seven iconic Spanish street food recipes that are easy to prepare and sure to transport your taste buds straight to Spain.

1. Bocadillo de Calamares (Fried Squid Sandwich)

This Madrid classic is a simple yet satisfying sandwich that embodies the unpretentious charm of Spanish street food. Golden, crispy calamari is the star ingredient, nestled in a soft crusty baguette.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh squid rings (about 500g)
  • 1 baguette, sliced into sandwich-sized portions
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • Sunflower or olive oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Clean and pat dry the squid rings. Season them lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a deep fryer or a saucepan with oil to about 180°C (350°F).
  3. Coat the squid rings in flour, ensuring they are evenly coated.
  4. Fry the squid in batches until golden brown and crispy. This should take about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Drain the squid on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  6. Slice your baguette open and stuff it generously with the fried calamari. Squeeze some fresh lemon juice over the top if desired.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy the classic flavors of Madrid at home.

2. Patatas Bravas

Patatas Bravas is one of Spain’s quintessential comfort foods, often found at stalls, bars, and cafes. These crispy potatoes are served with a smoky, tangy bravas sauce and always deliver on flavor.

Ingredients for the Potatoes:

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes
  • Olive oil for frying
  • Salt

Ingredients for the Bravas Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt to taste
  • ¼ cup tomato puree

Instructions:

  1. Peel and cut the potatoes into bite-sized cubes. Par-boil them in salted water for 5-7 minutes, then let them cool completely.
  2. Heat olive oil in a deep pan and fry the potatoes until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
  3. In a separate pan, heat olive oil and whisk in flour over low heat to make a roux.
  4. Add smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, and tomato puree. Gradually stir in the broth to create a smooth sauce. Let it simmer for a few minutes until it thickens slightly.
  5. Drizzle the sauce over the potatoes or serve it on the side for dipping.

3. Churros con Chocolate

No roundup of Spanish street food would be complete without churros. Crunchy on the outside, soft on the inside, these fried dough pastries are perfect when dunked into thick, velvety hot chocolate.

Ingredients for Churros:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Sugar and cinnamon for coating

Ingredients for the Chocolate Sauce:

  • 200g dark chocolate, chopped
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring water, sugar, salt, and butter to a boil. Reduce heat and stir in the flour until the mixture forms a smooth dough. Add vanilla and let it cool slightly.
  2. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pan. Spoon the dough into a piping bag with a star-shaped nozzle.
  3. Pipe strips of dough into the hot oil and fry until golden. Drain on paper towels, then coat with sugar and cinnamon.
  4. For the chocolate sauce, heat milk in a saucepan. Mix sugar and cornstarch in a bowl, then whisk it into the heated milk.
  5. Add chopped chocolate and stir until melted and thickened. Serve immediately with the churros.

4. Tigres (Stuffed Mussels)

Commonly sold by street vendors along coastal towns, tigres (or mejillones rellenos) are mussels stuffed with a spicy béchamel mixture, breaded, and deep-fried.

Ingredients:

  • 1kg fresh mussels
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • ½ cup onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup tomato puree
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Breadcrumbs and eggs for coating
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Steam the mussels in a pot over medium heat until they open. Remove the meat and set aside, keeping the shells intact for stuffing.
  2. In a pan, sauté onions and garlic in olive oil until soft. Add the chopped mussel meat, tomato puree, and paprika. Set aside.
  3. In another pan, make a béchamel sauce by whisking flour into melted butter, gradually adding milk until smooth. Then, mix in the mussel filling.
  4. Stuff the mussel shells with this mixture, coat them in breadcrumbs and beaten eggs, and fry until golden brown.

5. Empanadas Gallegas

These savory hand pies are often filled with meat, seafood, or vegetables and are a popular snack across Spain, especially in Galicia.

Ingredients:

  • 2 sheets of puff pastry
  • 1 cup tuna (canned and drained)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • ½ cup tomato sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion and red pepper in olive oil until soft. Stir in the tuna, tomato sauce, and chopped eggs. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Roll out one sheet of puff pastry and spread the filling evenly across the surface, leaving a border around the edges.
  3. Top with the second sheet and crimp the edges. Brush with beaten egg.
  4. Bake at 200°C (400°F) until the pastry is golden brown, about 25-30 minutes.

6. Banderillas (Skewered Tapas)

Colorful and packed with flavor, banderillas are skewered tapas made with pickles, olives, anchovies, and peppers. These make for a quick, no-cook snack.

Ingredients:

  • Green olives
  • Pickled guindilla peppers
  • Pickled onions
  • Anchovy fillets
  • Small gherkins
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. Assemble the ingredients on skewers, alternating for a visually appealing presentation.
  2. Drizzle with olive oil and serve as a light snack.

7. Porras

Similar to churros but thicker and fluffier, porras are a staple in Spanish street breakfasts, often served alongside coffee or hot chocolate.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, water, and salt into a smooth batter.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan and pipe the batter into the hot oil in thick strips. Fry until golden brown.
  3. Dust with sugar and serve warm.

Experimenting with these recipes will not only delight your taste buds but also allow you to experience the rich culinary heritage of Spain from the comfort of your home. Whether you’re hosting a tapas night or simply craving authentic Spanish flavors, these street food dishes are guaranteed to impress.
