10 Thai Recipes for a Flavorful Vegan Feast


Thai cuisine is celebrated around the world for its vibrant flavors, aromatic herbs, and the perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy elements. Whether you are a seasoned vegan or just looking to explore plant-based dishes, Thai food offers an abundance of options that can excite your taste buds and nourish your body. In this post, we’ll explore ten delicious Thai vegan recipes that will bring the authentic taste of Thailand to your kitchen. From soups and curries to salads and snacks, these recipes are perfect for creating a flavorful vegan feast.

1. Thai Green Curry with Tofu and Vegetables

Green curry is a quintessential Thai dish, and when made vegan, it becomes a hearty and satisfying meal. The rich coconut milk base pairs beautifully with the spicy green curry paste, which is made from a blend of green chilies, lemongrass, garlic, and galangal. This vegan version features tofu and an assortment of vegetables like bell peppers, zucchini, and eggplant.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block of firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoons green curry paste
  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 small eggplant, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Cooked jasmine rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the onion and sauté until softened. Stir in the green curry paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the coconut milk and vegetable broth, bringing the mixture to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add the bell pepper, zucchini, and eggplant, cooking for 10-12 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Stir in the soy sauce and sugar, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  6. Return the cooked tofu to the pan and mix gently.
  7. Serve the curry over jasmine rice, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

2. Vegan Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a popular street food dish in Thailand, known for its combination of stir-fried rice noodles, vegetables, and a tangy, slightly sweet sauce. A vegan version of this classic dish replaces eggs with tofu and skips the fish sauce for a more plant-based approach. The result is a colorful and flavorful dish that will have you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 block firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden and crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add the carrots, green onions, and bean sprouts. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.
  4. Add the cooked noodles to the pan along with the tofu.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together the tamarind paste, soy sauce, sugar, and chili flakes to make the sauce. Pour it over the noodles and toss to coat everything evenly.
  6. Garnish with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and fresh cilantro.

3. Tom Yum Soup

This famous Thai soup is known for its bold and aromatic flavors. The combination of lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal creates a fragrant base, while the addition of chili peppers gives it a delightful spicy kick. For a vegan version, you can replace shrimp or chicken with mushrooms and tofu.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, cut into pieces and smashed
  • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves, torn into pieces
  • 2-3 slices galangal or ginger
  • 1-2 red chili peppers, sliced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 block firm tofu, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the vegetable broth, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal (or ginger), and chili peppers. Bring to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer for 10-15 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. Add the mushrooms and tofu to the pot, cooking for another 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms are tender.
  3. Stir in the soy sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Adjust the seasoning to taste.
  4. Serve the soup hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and lime wedges.

4. Vegan Thai Mango Sticky Rice

Mango sticky rice is a beloved Thai dessert made with coconut-infused sticky rice and fresh mango slices. It’s a sweet and satisfying treat that’s perfect for any occasion. This vegan version stays true to the traditional recipe while ensuring it’s entirely plant-based.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup glutinous (sticky) rice
  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
  • Sesame seeds or mung beans for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the sticky rice until the water runs clear, then soak it in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. After soaking, steam the rice in a bamboo or metal steamer for about 20-25 minutes until fully cooked.
  3. In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat over low heat, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves.
  4. Once the rice is done, transfer it to a large bowl and pour the coconut milk mixture over it. Stir to combine and let the rice absorb the coconut milk.
  5. Serve the sticky rice with slices of fresh mango, garnished with sesame seeds or mung beans if desired.

5. Thai Basil Tofu Stir-Fry

This fragrant and flavorful stir-fry features crispy tofu, fresh basil, and a savory sauce made with soy sauce, garlic, and chili. It’s quick and easy to prepare and can be served with steamed rice for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-2 red chili peppers, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (light)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Fresh basil leaves (preferably Thai basil)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add the onion, bell pepper, and zucchini. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until tender.
  3. Add the garlic and chili peppers and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the soy sauce, light soy sauce, and sugar. Add the cooked tofu back into the pan and toss to coat in the sauce.
  5. Finish by adding fresh basil leaves and tossing everything together until the basil wilts.
  6. Serve hot with steamed rice.

6. Thai Green Papaya Salad (Som Tum)

Som Tum is a refreshing and tangy Thai salad made with shredded green papaya, peanuts, tomatoes, and chilies. It’s often served with sticky rice, and in this vegan version, it’s as refreshing as it is flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 green papaya, peeled and shredded
  • 1 carrot, shredded
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts
  • 2-3 cloves garlic
  • 1-2 red chili peppers
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the shredded papaya, carrot, and tomatoes.
  2. In a mortar and pestle, crush the garlic, chili peppers, and roasted peanuts until coarsely ground.
  3. Add the soy sauce, lime juice, and sugar to the mortar and mix well.
  4. Pour the dressing over the shredded papaya mixture and toss to combine.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with additional peanuts and chili if desired.

7. Thai Peanut Sauce Noodles

This dish is a simple yet delicious combination of noodles, fresh vegetables, and a creamy peanut sauce. The peanut sauce has a rich, savory flavor, and when paired with rice noodles, it creates a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro

For the Peanut Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or sugar
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1 teaspoon chili paste (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, maple syrup, water, and chili paste (if using) to create the peanut sauce.
  3. In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the carrots, bell pepper, and cucumber, and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until tender but still crisp.
  4. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and pour over the peanut sauce. Toss to coat everything evenly.
  5. Serve garnished with fresh cilantro.

8. Vegan Thai Red Curry with Pumpkin

This vegan red curry is comforting and full of flavor, featuring pumpkin as the star ingredient. The richness of coconut milk pairs perfectly with the sweetness of pumpkin, while the red curry paste adds a satisfying heat.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small pumpkin, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 2 tablespoons red curry paste
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup vegetable broth
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add the red curry paste. Cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  2. Add the coconut milk, soy sauce, sugar, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir to combine.
  3. Add the pumpkin cubes to the pot and simmer for 20-25 minutes until the pumpkin is tender.
  4. Serve the curry hot, garnished with fresh cilantro.

9. Thai Stir-Fried Vegetables with Cashews

This quick and easy stir-fry is loaded with vegetables and topped with crunchy cashews for an added texture. The combination of soy sauce, sesame oil, and chili gives it a savory, slightly spicy flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 carrot, sliced
  • 1/2 cup cashews, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, broccoli, and carrot. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  2. Add the soy sauce, sesame oil, and chili flakes. Toss to coat the vegetables.
  3. Stir in the toasted cashews and cook for another minute.
  4. Serve the stir-fry hot with rice.

10. Thai Coconut Soup (Tom Kha)

This creamy coconut-based soup is tangy, slightly sweet, and full of aromatic flavors. With a blend of coconut milk, lime, and herbs, it makes for a perfect light appetizer or main dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can coconut milk (400 ml)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, smashed
  • 3-4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 1-2 red chili peppers, sliced
  • 1 block tofu, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine the coconut milk and vegetable broth. Add the lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and chili peppers. Bring to a simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the tofu cubes and soy sauce. Cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the lime juice, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  4. Serve the soup garnished with fresh cilantro.

These 10 Thai vegan recipes are just the beginning of what this vibrant cuisine has to offer. With a variety of flavors, textures, and ingredients, these dishes will provide you with a full and satisfying Thai-inspired vegan feast. Enjoy the process of cooking and the joy of sharing these flavorful meals with friends and family!
