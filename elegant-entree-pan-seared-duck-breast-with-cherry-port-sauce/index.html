Elegant Entree: Pan-Seared Duck Breast with Cherry Port Sauce


Duck breast is a delicacy that exudes elegance and sophistication. When pan-seared to perfection and paired with a luscious cherry port sauce, it becomes a show-stopping dish that is sure to impress your guests. In this recipe, we will guide you through the process of creating this exquisite entree.

Ingredients:

  • 2 duck breasts
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 cup port wine
  • 1 cup fresh cherries, pitted and halved
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Using a sharp knife, score the fat side of the duck breasts in a crisscross pattern. This will help render the fat and create a crispy skin.
  3. Season the duck breasts generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  4. Heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a large, oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat.
  5. Place the duck breasts in the skillet, skin side down, and cook for about 5 minutes or until the skin is golden brown and crispy.
  6. Flip the duck breasts and sear the other side for an additional 2 minutes.
  7. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and roast the duck breasts for 8-10 minutes for medium-rare or until desired doneness.
  8. Remove the skillet from the oven and transfer the duck breasts to a cutting board. Allow them to rest for a few minutes.
  9. While the duck breasts are resting, prepare the cherry port sauce.
  10. In the same skillet, add the finely chopped shallot and sauté for about 2 minutes until softened.
  11. Pour in the port wine and bring it to a simmer. Cook for about 5 minutes to allow the alcohol to evaporate.
  12. Add the fresh cherries, honey, and balsamic vinegar to the skillet. Stir well to combine.
  13. Continue simmering the sauce for another 5-7 minutes until it thickens slightly.
  14. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the unsalted butter until melted and incorporated.
  15. Slice the rested duck breasts diagonally into thin slices.
  16. To serve, arrange the slices of duck breast on a plate and drizzle the cherry port sauce over the top.
  17. Garnish with fresh cherries and a sprig of thyme if desired.

Tips and Tricks:

  • When scoring the fat on the duck breasts, be careful not to cut into the meat. The goal is to render the fat, not expose the flesh.
  • To achieve a crispy skin, make sure the skillet is hot before adding the duck breasts.
  • If you prefer your duck breasts cooked to medium or well-done, adjust the roasting time accordingly.
  • For a smoother sauce, you can strain it through a fine-mesh sieve before serving.
  • Serve this elegant entree with roasted vegetables or a side of creamy mashed potatoes for a complete meal.

Duck breast with cherry port sauce is a dish that combines richness, sweetness, and a touch of acidity in every bite. The tender and juicy duck meat, paired with the vibrant flavors of the cherry port sauce, creates a culinary experience that is both luxurious and unforgettable. Prepare this elegant entree for a special occasion or whenever you want to indulge in a gourmet delight. Your taste buds will thank you!
