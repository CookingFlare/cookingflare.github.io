Vegan Riced & Rejoiced: 20 Versatile, Zesty & Nutrient-Packed Recipes


Veganism has surged in popularity in recent years, transforming the way we think about food, nutrition and health. As more and more individuals adopt a plant-based lifestyle, the demand for satisfying and nutritious meals has never been higher. Enter the humble cauliflower – a vegetable so versatile it can bring any dish to life. Today, we’ll introduce 20 of the most exciting and innovative vegan recipes that center around this nutritious star ingredient.

1. Creamy Cauliflower Soup

A classic! This dish captures the essence of a soothing, nourishing soup with the added nutritional benefits of cauliflower. Serve with a sprinkle of chia seeds and fresh thyme, and enjoy this comforting vegan delight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme, for garnish
  • Chia seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the cauliflower florets and vegetable broth, and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer until the cauliflower is tender.
  5. Use an immersion blender or transfer to a blender to puree the soup until smooth.
  6. Stir in the nutritional yeast, salt, and pepper to taste.
  7. Reheat and serve with a sprinkle of fresh thyme and chia seeds.

2. Spicy Buffalo Cauliflower Bites

These utterly irresistible Buffalo Cauliflower Bites will blow your taste buds away. Crispy, spicy, and healthy? You bet!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1/4 cup hot sauce
  • 1/4 cup Frank’s RedHot
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup panko bread crumbs
  • 1 cup vegan ranch dressing, for dipping

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix the hot sauce, Frank’s RedHot, almond milk, flour, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss the cauliflower florets in the sauce mixture, making sure they’re evenly coated.
  4. In another bowl, mix the panko bread crumbs.
  5. Dip each floret into the panko crumbs, then place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the cauliflower is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Serve hot with vegan Buffalo ranch or your favorite dipping sauce.

3. Cauliflower Mac & Cheese

Who said vegan mac and cheese couldn’t be as delicious and creamy as the dairy version? This healthy twist on a classic recipe will make you never want to go back!

Ingredients:

  • 1 head of cauliflower, cored and cut into small florets
  • 4 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/2 cup whole wheat bread, torn into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup vegan cheese shreds (optional)
  • 4 cloves garlic, roasted
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 8 oz whole wheat macaroni pasta

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large pot, boil the cauliflower florets until tender. Drain, reserving 1 cup of the cooking liquid.
  3. In a blender or food processor, combine the almond milk, nutritional yeast, bread pieces, roasted garlic, vegan cheese shreds (if using), salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth, adjusting the consistency with the reserved cauliflower cooking liquid.
  4. Cook the macaroni pasta according to the package instructions, then drain.
  5. Toss the cooked pasta with the cauliflower mixture and transfer to a baking dish. Bake for 30 minutes, or until the topping is crispy and golden.

4. Harijan Cauliflower Curry

This aromatic recipe will tantalize your taste buds with warm, spicy flavors of a traditional Indian curry. Serve with rice, flatbread, or any other Indian-inspired dish for a memorable meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into bite-sized florets
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 1 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 can (14.5 oz, 411g) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat, and add the cumin seeds. Cook for 1 minute, or until they begin to sizzle and release their aroma.
  2. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and ginger, and cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Stir in the turmeric, coriander, cayenne pepper, and salt, then add the cauliflower florets, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth.
  4. Bring the curry to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer, partially covered, for 20-25 minutes, or until the cauliflower is tender and the sauce has thickened.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with rice or flatbread.

5. Grilled Cauliflower Steaks

These plant-based “steaks” are hearty, satisfying, and flavorful. They’ll make you forget you’re even eating cauliflower!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, trimmed to create a flat bottom
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Cut the cauliflower head in half, then cut two 1-inch thick “steaks” from each half.
  3. Brush the olive oil onto the cauliflower steaks and season with salt, pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and onion powder.
  4. Grill the cauliflower steaks for 5-6 minutes per side, or until they are tender and slightly charred.
  5. Let the steaks rest for a few minutes, then garnish with chopped parsley and serve.

6. Cauliflower Fried Rice

Skip the takeout and make your own delicious cauliflower fried rice at home. It’s the perfect substitute for the classic recipe, packed with vegetables, protein, and sustained by the versatile cauliflower.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups finely grated cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 2 large eggs, beaten (or 2 “flax eggs” for vegan option)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower and set aside.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the grated cauliflower and water. Microwave for 3-4 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender.
  3. In a large wok or frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the onion and garlic, and cook until fragrant.
  5. Add the frozen peas and cook for another 2 minutes.
  6. Stir in the cooked quinoa and cauliflower mixture, then push the ingredients to the sides of the pan, creating a well in the center.
  7. Pour the beaten eggs into the well and scramble until cooked. Mix the eggs with the quinoa and cauliflower mixture.
  8. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds before serving.

7. Cauliflower Tacos

Take your tacos to a whole new level with these crispy cauliflower tacos that are sure to impress your dinner guests and satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Salsa
  • Avocado slices
  • Chopped cilantro

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower and set aside.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the grated cauliflower and water. Microwave for 3-4 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender.
  3. In a large bowl, mix the flour, garlic powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add the cauliflower, stirring to coat.
  4. In another bowl, mix the cornmeal and almond milk.
  5. Heat the oil in a deep pot or fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Working in batches, scoop small portions of the coated cauliflower into the cornmeal mixture, then carefully drop into the hot oil.
  7. Fry until golden brown, about 2-3 minutes, then drain on paper towels.
  8. Warm the tortillas, then fill with shredded lettuce, fried cauliflower, salsa, avocado slices, and chopped cilantro.

8. Cauliflower Stir-Fry

A super speedy, flavorful, and packed with veggies stir-fry that’s perfect for a weeknight dinner. To kick things up a notch, mix in your favorite protein or tofu for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 cup frozen stir-fry veggies (like broccoli, snow peas, and water chestnuts)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce or tamari
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of the vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the onion and stir-fry for 2-3 minutes, until slightly softened.
  3. Add the bell pepper, zucchini, carrot, and frozen stir-fry veggies. Cook for 5 minutes, until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. Remove the vegetables from the pan and set aside.
  5. In a bowl, mix the soy sauce, garlic, and cornstarch. Set aside.
  6. Heat the remaining tablespoon of vegetable oil in the pan. Add the cauliflower florets and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, until lightly browned and tender.
  7. Return the vegetables to the pan and pour the soy sauce mixture over top. Stir-fry the vegetables for 1-2 minutes, until the sauce has thickened.
  8. Garnish the stir-fry with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds.

9. Cauliflower Shawarma Bowl

If you’re craving Mediterranean flavors, this nutritious take on a shawarma bowl is the perfect way to satisfy your taste buds without the guilt.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups finely grated cauliflower
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/2 cup hummus
  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. In a large bowl, toss the grated cauliflower with the olive oil mixture until evenly coated.
  3. On a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, spread the seasoned cauliflower in a single layer.
  4. Roast the cauliflower for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until tender and lightly browned.
  5. In separate small bowls, prepare the toppings: dice the tomato and cucumber, and mix the hummus, tahini, and lemon juice with water to create a smooth dressing. Stir in a tablespoon or two of water if the dressing needs to be thinned.
  6. To assemble the bowls, place a bed of cauliflower at the bottom, followed by a dollop of hummus, a drizzle of tahini dressing, and the diced vegetables.
  7. Top each bowl with a sprinkle of chopped parsley, and serve immediately.

10. Southwestern Cauliflower Rice

Ditch the usual Latino party standards in favor of this nutritious and hearty Southwestern recipe. It’ll be the talk of the town!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups grated cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 can (15 oz, 425g) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 avocado, diced
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower and set aside.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the grated cauliflower and water. Microwave for 3-4 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender.
  3. In a large frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  4. Add the onion, red bell pepper, and corn kernels, cooking until the vegetables are lightly softened.
  5. Stir in the cauliflower mixture, black beans, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the vegetables are heated through.
  6. Divide the cauliflower rice among serving bowls, then top with diced avocado and chopped cilantro.
  7. Serve with lime wedges for a burst of freshness and tanginess.

11. Cauliflower Green Chile Enchiladas

Make these enchiladas using store-bought or homemade green chile sauce for a mouthwatering meal that’s sure to be a hit with everyone at the table.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large cauliflower florets, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups shredded vegan cheese
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups green chile sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Sliced green onions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss the cauliflower pieces with a bit of olive oil, salt, and pepper, then spread them out on a baking sheet. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until they’re golden and tender.
  3. In a bowl, mix the baked cauliflower, shredded vegan cheese, and salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  5. Pour 1/2 cup of the green chile sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  6. Working one at a time, warm the corn tortillas in the microwave for 15-20 seconds (or over a gas stovetop flame), then fill with a heaping spoonful of the cauliflower and cheese mixture.
  7. Roll up the filled tortillas and place them seam-side down in the prepared baking dish. Continue filling and rolling the tortillas until the baking dish is full.
  8. Pour the remaining green chile sauce over the enchiladas, making sure they’re evenly coated. Sprinkle the remaining shredded vegan cheese on top.
  9. Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 20 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes, until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Garnish the enchiladas with chopped cilantro and sliced green onions before serving.

12. Cauliflower Risotto

Make a luxurious and creamy cauliflower risotto that’s completely free of dairy. You won’t believe how rich and satisfying this vegan version is!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into small florets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup short-grain rice (like Arborio or Carnaroli)
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Stir in the short-grain rice, and toast for 2-3 minutes, until the rice is lightly browned and fragrant.
  4. Pour in the white wine, stirring until the liquid has evaporated.
  5. Begin adding the vegetable broth, one ladle at a time, allowing the liquid to be absorbed before adding more. Stir frequently to prevent sticking and achieve a creamy consistency.
  6. When the rice is tender and the risotto is creamy, stir in the grated cauliflower and nutritional yeast. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the risotto immediately, garnished with fresh thyme.

13. Mashed Cauliflower

This mashed cauliflower recipe gives the traditional dish a delicious (and healthy) makeover. The cauliflower seems to melt in your mouth!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 3 cloves garlic, roasted
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Chopped chives, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, boil the cauliflower florets until tender.
  2. Drain the florets, then return them to the pot. Add the olive oil, almond milk, roasted garlic, and salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Use a potato masher or immersion blender to blend the ingredients together until smooth and creamy. Adjust seasoning if necessary.
  4. Serve the mashed cauliflower immediately, garnished with chopped chives.

14. Cauliflower Pizza Crust

Who needs traditional pizza crust when you’ve discovered this amazing alternative? It’s low-carb, gluten-free, and packed with veggies!

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups finely grated cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 egg (or flax egg for a vegan option)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce
  • 1 cup shredded vegan mozzarella
  • Toppings of your choice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower and set aside.
  3. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the grated cauliflower and water. Microwave for 3-4 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender.
  4. In a large bowl, mix the cooked cauliflower, shredded mozzarella, egg (or flax egg), garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and salt and pepper.
  5. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper, and spread the cauliflower mixture onto it. Press the mixture to form a 10- to 12-inch circle crust.
  6. Bake the pizza crust for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are lightly browned and crispy.
  7. Remove the crust from the oven and spread the pizza sauce evenly over the top. Add the shredded vegan mozzarella and any additional desired toppings.
  8. Return the pizza to the oven and bake for another 10-12 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly. Cool for a few minutes before slicing.

15. Cauliflower Gratin

Indulge in this creamy, cheesy cauliflower gratin that makes a satisfying side dish or main course. It’s sure to become a new family favorite!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 cups shredded vegan cheese
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. In a saucepan, combine the almond milk, nutritional yeast, shredded vegan cheese, olive oil, salt, pepper, and minced garlic. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the cheese has melted and the sauce is smooth.
  3. In a 9x13-inch baking dish, spread a thin layer of the cheese sauce. Arrange a layer of cauliflower florets on top, then pour another layer of cheese sauce. Repeat the layers until all the cauliflower is used, ending with the cheese sauce.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the breadcrumbs and chopped parsley. Sprinkle the mixture over the top of the gratin.
  5. Bake the cauliflower gratin for 30-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the florets are tender.

16. Cauliflower Steamed Dumplings

Transport your taste buds to Asia with these fabulous steamed dumplings that feature a scrumptious cauliflower filling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups finely grated cauliflower
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 green onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 package vegan gyoza or wonton wrappers
  • Dipping sauce, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower and set aside.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the grated cauliflower and water. Microwave for 3-4 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender.
  3. In a frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the green onions, minced garlic, and grated ginger, stirring until fragrant.
  4. Mix in the cooked cauliflower, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Lay out the wonton or gyoza wrappers on a clean work surface. Spoon a rounded teaspoon of the cauliflower mixture into the center of each wrapper.
  6. Wet the edges of the wrappers with water, then fold the dumplings in half, pressing the edges together to seal.
  7. Line a steamer basket with parchment paper, then place the dumplings in the steamer, leaving space between each one.
  8. Steam the dumplings over boiling water for 10-12 minutes, or until the filling is heated through and the wrappers are slightly crispy on the edges.
  9. Serve the dumplings hot with your favorite dipping sauce.

17. Cauliflower Bread

Forget your ordinary bread and try this exciting alternative, made from nutritious and delicious cauliflower. It makes for the perfect open-faced sandwich or toastie!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups finely grated cauliflower
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Toppings of your choice

Instructions:

  1. In a food processor, grate the cauliflower and set aside.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl, combine the grated cauliflower and water. Microwave for 3-4 minutes, stirring halfway through, until the cauliflower is tender.
  3. In a large bowl, mix the cooked cauliflower, all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Add the olive oil to the cauliflower mixture and mix well.
  5. Line a baking tray with parchment paper, then press the cauliflower mixture into a 1/2-inch thick round shape.
  6. Bake the cauliflower bread for 25-30 minutes, until lightly browned and crispy.
  7. Allow the bread to cool for a few minutes before slicing. Serve with your choice of toppings.

18. Cauliflower Falafel

Packed with cauliflower, chickpeas, and your favorite spices, these vegan falafel balls are sure to become a dinner staple in no time!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cored and cut into small florets
  • 1 can (15 oz, 425g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup flat-leaf parsley
  • 1/2 cup cilantro
  • 1 egg (or flax egg for vegan option)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Vegan yogurt or tzatziki
  • Toppings of your choice

Instructions:

  1. Pulse the cauliflower florets in a food processor until they resemble rice.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the processed cauliflower, chickpeas, chopped onion, minced garlic, flat-leaf parsley, cilantro, egg (or flax egg), all-purpose flour, tahini, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Form the mixture into small balls, then place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Bake the falafel balls for 20-25 minutes, until they are browned and crispy on the outside.
  5. Serve the falafel warm or at room temperature, with a dollop of vegan yogurt or tzatziki, and your choice of toppings.

19. Cauliflower Corn Chowder

Savor every spoonful of this satisfying, creamy chowder that’s packed full of potato, corn, and cauliflower. Perfect for cold nights!

Ingredients:

  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 ears of corn, kernels removed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh thyme, chopped
  • Cheddar cheese crumbles

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until softened.
  2. Add the cauliflower florets and cubed potatoes, then stir in the vegetable broth.
  3. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Blend the soup using an immersion blender or a regular blender, until smooth.
  5. Stir in the corn kernels and almond milk, then season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Simmer the chowder for an additional 10 minutes, then serve hot, garnished with fresh thyme and cheddar cheese crumbles.

20. Cauliflower Mac and Cheese

Indulge in a healthier version of your childhood favorite! Featuring whole-wheat macaroni, cashew cheese, and a crunchy breadcrumb topping, this vegan mac and cheese will satisfy and delight.

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz whole wheat macaroni
  • 1 large head of cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 cup raw cashews
  • 2 cups unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Chopped parsley, for garnish

Instructions:

Cashew Cheese

  1. Soak the raw cashews in water for at least 2 hours. Drain and rinse the cashews before using.
  2. In a food processor or high-speed blender, pulse the soaked cashews with the unsweetened almond milk until smooth and creamy. Set aside.

Cauliflower and Macaroni

  1. Cook the whole wheat macaroni according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, steam the cauliflower florets until tender, about 10 minutes. Set aside.

Cheese Sauce

  1. In a large frying pan, sauté the chopped onion and minced garlic until softened.
  2. Add the cooked cauliflower, cashew cheese, nutritional yeast, turmeric, and black pepper to the frying pan. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cheese sauce thickens.
  3. Combine the cooked macaroni with the cauliflower mixture, then transfer the mac and cheese to a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the breadcrumbs and olive oil. Sprinkle the breadcrumb mixture over the top of the mac and cheese.

Bake

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bake the macaroni and cheese for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and crispy.
  3. Allow the mac and cheese to cool for a few minutes before serving, garnished with chopped parsley.
