Leftover Fish Recipes That Will Surprise Your Taste Buds


When it comes to leftover fish, many people think the next meal options are limited to cold salads or reheated fillets—often disappointing and dry. But the beauty of leftover fish lies in its versatility. With a bit of creativity, you can transform those scraps into delicious dishes that breathe new life into your meals and keep your palate excited.

Whether you have grilled salmon, baked cod, pan-seared tilapia, or even canned tuna on hand, these recipes reinvent leftovers in unexpected and delightful ways. From robust curries to zesty tacos, flaky fish cakes, and comforting chowders, the following ideas make the most out of your fish remnants, turning yesterday’s dinner into today’s culinary adventure.

1. Mediterranean Fish Cakes with Lemon Aioli

Fish cakes are a classic use for leftover fish, but infusing Mediterranean flavors gives them a fresh twist. By incorporating sun-dried tomatoes, olives, capers, and herbs like parsley and dill, you create a complex and appetizing profile that pairs perfectly with a creamy, tangy lemon aioli.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked flaked leftover fish (any white fish or salmon)
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes or boiled potato chunks
  • 2 tbsp sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp pitted olives, chopped
  • 1 tbsp capers, rinsed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Lemon Aioli:

  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt to taste

Directions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flaked fish with mashed potatoes.
  2. Stir in sun-dried tomatoes, olives, capers, onion, garlic, parsley, and dill.
  3. Add the beaten egg, salt, and pepper; mix well to bind ingredients together.
  4. Shape the mixture into small patties.
  5. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat and fry the cakes for 3-4 minutes each side until golden brown.
  6. Meanwhile, whisk together the aioli ingredients until smooth.
  7. Serve fish cakes warm with a dollop of lemon aioli.

Why this works: The starchy potato base allows the cakes to hold together, while Mediterranean accoutrements create a flavor burst. The lemon aioli brightens the dish, making it a refreshing yet hearty meal.

2. Spicy Coconut Fish Curry

Forget bland leftovers and step into vibrant Indian-inspired territory. This spicy coconut fish curry is a great way to reintroduce cooked fish with a flavor-packed sauce that will have you craving more.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover fish chunks (flaked or diced)
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 tbsp curry powder (adjust spiciness to taste)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (fresh or canned)
  • 1 fresh chili, sliced (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Cooked rice to serve

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add onion, garlic, and ginger, cooking until softened and fragrant.
  3. Stir in curry powder, turmeric, and cumin; cook for 1-2 minutes to release the spices’ aroma.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and tomatoes; bring the mixture to a simmer.
  5. Add leftover fish chunks gently into the sauce.
  6. Cook for 5 minutes until heated through, being careful not to break the fish apart.
  7. Season with salt and add sliced chili if desired for extra heat.
  8. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve over rice.

Why this works: The creamy coconut softens and mellows the spices while complementing the delicate fish. This type of curry makes the perfect quick dinner, re-imagining leftovers into something richly layered and comforting.

3. Fish and Potato Tacos with Avocado Salsa

Tacos might seem unconventional for fish leftovers, but their inherent flexibility makes them an ideal option. Instead of frying fresh fish, leftover cooked fish paired with crispy roasted potatoes and a zesty avocado salsa provides crunch, creaminess, and a fresh kick.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover fish, flaked
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Salt and pepper
  • Olive oil for roasting

Avocado Salsa:

  • 1 ripe avocado, diced
  • 1 small tomato, chopped
  • ½ small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt to taste

Additional:

  • Small corn or flour tortillas
  • Lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Toss cubed potatoes with olive oil, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast potatoes for 25-30 minutes until golden and crispy, flipping halfway.
  4. While potatoes roast, combine all salsa ingredients in a bowl.
  5. Warm tortillas in a dry pan or oven.
  6. Assemble tacos by layering roasted potatoes, flaked fish, and avocado salsa.
  7. Squeeze fresh lime juice on top before serving.

Why this works: The smoky spices on the potatoes contrast with the creamy avocado salsa, while the tender fish adds protein without overpowering the dish. The textural mix satisfies the senses and turns leftovers into a fun, shareable meal.

4. Creamy Fish Chowder with Corn and Herbs

A hearty, warming chowder is a fantastic way to transform leftover fish into a satisfying soup perfect for chilly nights. The mixture of sweet corn, creamy broth, potatoes, and tender fish creates a balanced and nourishing one-pot meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups leftover fish, roughly chopped
  • 4 slices bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 cups potatoes, cubed
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • 1 cup frozen or fresh corn kernels
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large saucepan, cook bacon over medium heat until crisp, then remove with a slotted spoon.
  2. Add onion, celery, and garlic to the bacon fat and sauté until softened.
  3. Add potatoes and broth; bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until potatoes are tender (about 15 minutes).
  4. Stir in milk or cream, corn, thyme, and fish.
  5. Heat gently until the fish is warmed through and the chowder thickens slightly.
  6. Season with salt and pepper.
  7. Garnish with bacon and fresh parsley before serving.

Why this works: The smoky bacon aroma and gentle herbs build layers that complement the fish without masking it. The creamy base provides ample comfort, making this chowder an ideal way to enjoy the remnants of a fish dinner.

5. Asian-Inspired Fish Fried Rice

Take your leftover fish global by incorporating it into a quick fried rice recipe packed with umami-forward ingredients. This is a complete meal, perfect for using up not only fish but also stray veggies and rice in your fridge.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 1½ cups leftover fish, flaked
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 small carrot, diced
  • ½ cup peas
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Fresh cilantro or basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat some vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the beaten eggs and scramble until just set, then remove from the pan.
  3. Add a bit more oil, sauté garlic and carrots for 2 minutes, followed by peas.
  4. Add cooked rice, stirring to break up any clumps.
  5. Pour in soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil, stirring well to coat the rice evenly.
  6. Gently fold in flaked fish, scrambled eggs, and most of the green onions.
  7. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through.
  8. Sprinkle remaining green onions and fresh herbs on top before serving.

Why this works: The savory sauces and quick high-heat cooking meld the ingredients together beautifully, while the fish adds protein and delicate flavor. It’s a filling, delicious way to reinvent leftovers into a new, exciting dish.

Bonus Tips For Cooking with Leftover Fish

  • Flake fish gently: Over-handling can cause it to become mushy. Use a fork or your fingers carefully to preserve texture.
  • Balance moisture: Leftover fish is often already cooked, so avoid overcooking. Add it to dishes towards the end of cooking to keep it tender.
  • Add fresh herbs and acidity: Bright elements like lemon juice, fresh cilantro, or capers can elevate leftovers by adding freshness.
  • Include a starch: Whether potatoes, rice, or bread crumbs, a starch helps bind dishes and extends the meal.
  • Mind strong flavors: Leftover fish may have absorbed spices or sauces from its original preparation; use complementary seasonings to avoid clashing flavors.

Next time you have leftover fish lurking in the fridge, resist the temptation to reheat dull, dry fillets. Instead, use these recipes as inspiration to spice things up and surprise your taste buds with innovative dishes that make every bite memorable. Your second-day fish doesn’t have to be second best!
