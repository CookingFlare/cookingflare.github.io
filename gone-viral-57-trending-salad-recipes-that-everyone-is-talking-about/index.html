Gone Viral: 57 Trending Salad Recipes That Everyone is Talking About


The humble salad, once an afterthought in the world of cuisine, has transformed dramatically in recent years. With the rise of phytochemicals and other nutrients in diets, salads have taken on proportions and flavors previously unimaginable. Gastronomes and casual diners alike have succumbed to the allure of increasingly creative salads, bending culinary boundaries and capturing the hearts — and taste buds — of people around the globe. In this rollercoaster tour of salad varieties, we present to you 57 trending salad recipes on everyone’s radar.

1. Quinoa and Avocado Salad

  • Combine 1 cup cooked quinoa with 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, 1/2 avocado, and a handful of fresh cilantro.
  • Mix 2 tbsp of lime juice with 1 tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper, then pour over the salad.

2. Classic Caesar Salad

  • Toss together 4 cups romaine lettuce, 1/2 cup croutons, and 1/2 cup parmesan cheese.
  • Mix 1/2 cup of Caesar dressing, and serve immediately.

3. Turkish Kale Salad

  • Combine 2 cups chopped kale, 1/2 cup purple cabbage, and 1/2 cup diced red pepper.
  • Mix in 1/4 cup chopped walnuts and 2 tbsp lemon juice with olive oil.

4. Greek Salad

  • Toss together 4 cups mixed greens, 2 cups chopped cucumber, and 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes.
  • Add 2 tbsp of olive oil, 1 tbsp of red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  • Sprinkle 1/2 cup feta cheese and 1/4 cup Kalamata olives over the salad.

5. Superfood Broccoli Salad

  • Mix together 3 cups chopped broccoli, 1/2 cup sliced almonds, and 1/2 cup dried cranberries.
  • Mix in 1/4 cup honey, 1/4 cup olive oil, and 1 tbsp Dijon mustard.

6. Pear and Gorgonzola Salad

  • Combine 3 cups mixed greens, 1/2 cup sliced pears, and 1/2 cup crumbled Gorgonzola cheese.
  • Mix 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar with olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper.

7. Spinach and Strawberry Salad

  • Toss together 4 cups baby spinach, 1/2 cup sliced strawberries, and 1/2 cup sliced almonds.
  • Mix in 1/4 cup honey, 1/4 cup olive oil, and 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar.

8. Asian Sesame Slaw

  • Combine 4 cups shredded cabbage, 1/2 cup sliced green onions, and 1/2 cup chopped cilantro.
  • Mix 1/4 cup sesame oil, 2 tbsp soy sauce, and 1 tbsp rice vinegar.

9. Warm Bavarian Potato Salad

  • Boil 2 cups diced potatoes until soft, then mix in 2 cups chopped sauerkraut and 1/2 cup diced bacon.
  • Mix in 2 tbsp Dijon mustard and 2 tbsp olive oil, then sprinkle with salt and pepper.

10. Chopped Mediterranean Salad

  • Toss together 3 cups chopped cucumbers, 1 cup halved cherry tomatoes, and 1/2 cup chopped red onion.
  • Mix in 1/2 cup diced feta cheese, 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, and 1/4 cup chopped parsley.
  • Drizzle with 1/4 cup olive oil, and sprinkle with salt and pepper.

Habits and Tips:

Salad recipes trending nowadays reflect our desire for new experiences while still respecting the goodness of natural flavors. Building meals around salads can allow a creative and healthy spin to your daily diet. A good salad recipe keeps it simple and showcases the natural beauty of the flavors, enhancing the ingredients with a delicate touch of additional textures and taste.

Experiment with seasonal produce, and identify which ingredients don’t easily combine in a salad. Some ingredients may cause wilting or overpowers others. Additionally, dietary restrictions shouldn’t dictate your salad boundaries; check different resources for alternative ingredients to cater to any needs.

As you’ve seen from the selections above, there’s so much potential to create mouth-watering salad combinations. The old adage “safety first” still applies, but don’t be afraid to step out of the comfort zone. An extraordinary culinary adventure awaits you, and your taste buds won’t know what hit them.

Conclusion:

Whether it’s a classic Caesar or a modern fusion of flavors, everyone has something to look forward to when exploring the delightful world of salad recipes. Allow your palette to be whisked away on a culinary adventure, one crunch at a time. From the appetizing aroma to the lingering aftertaste, it’s no wonder salads have taken the world by storm!
