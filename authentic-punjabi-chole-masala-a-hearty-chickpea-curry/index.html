Authentic Punjabi Chole Masala: A Hearty Chickpea Curry


Chole Masala is a popular and flavorful dish from the vibrant region of Punjab in Northern India. This hearty chickpea curry, known for its rich and aromatic flavors, is a staple in Punjabi cuisine. Whether you’re a fan of Indian food or simply looking to explore new flavors, Authentic Punjabi Chole Masala is a must-try recipe that will surely satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight and boiled until tender)
  • 2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
  • 3 medium-sized tomatoes, pureed
  • 4-5 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 teaspoons cumin seeds
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pan or kadai over medium heat and add a tablespoon of oil. Once the oil is hot, add the cumin seeds and let them splutter.

  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. This will take around 8-10 minutes.

  3. Next, add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

  4. Add the tomato puree to the pan and cook until the oil separates from the masala. This will take approximately 10-12 minutes.

  5. Now, it’s time to add the dry spices. Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. Add the boiled chickpeas to the pan and mix them well with the masala. Cook for a few minutes, allowing the chickpeas to absorb the flavors.

  7. Pour in enough water to cover the chickpeas and bring the mixture to a boil. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes. This will allow the flavors to develop and the curry to thicken.

  8. After simmering, add garam masala and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Adjust the consistency of the curry by adding more water if needed.

  9. Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning according to your preference.

  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice, roti, or naan bread.

Tips and Variations

  • For a smoky flavor, you can add a small piece of charcoal to the curry. Heat the charcoal until red hot, place it in a small bowl, and put the bowl in the center of the curry. Drizzle a teaspoon of ghee or oil over the charcoal and quickly cover the pan. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes, and then remove the charcoal before serving.

  • To make the curry creamier, you can add a tablespoon of yogurt or cream towards the end of the cooking process.

  • For a spicier kick, increase the amount of red chili powder or add a teaspoon of Kashmiri red chili powder.

  • You can also add a teaspoon of dry mango powder (amchur) or a squeeze of lemon juice for a tangy flavor.

Enjoy the authentic flavors of Punjabi Chole Masala, a delightful and satisfying dish that will transport you to the heart of Punjab. This chickpea curry is perfect for a comforting meal with family and friends. So, gather your ingredients and get ready to indulge in the aromatic and mouthwatering world of Punjabi cuisine!

Note: Remember, cooking is an art, and you can always adjust the spices and flavors according to your taste preferences. So, feel free to experiment and make this recipe your own. Happy cooking!
