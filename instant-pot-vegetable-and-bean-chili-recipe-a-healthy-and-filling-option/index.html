Instant Pot Vegetable and Bean Chili Recipe: A Healthy and Filling Option


As the weather starts to cool down, there’s nothing like a warm and comforting bowl of chili to satisfy our cravings. But not all chilies are created equal. Traditional chili recipes can be high in sodium, saturated fat, and calories, making it difficult to enjoy this beloved dish without compromising our health goals. That’s where this Instant Pot Vegetable and Bean Chili recipe comes in – a healthy and filling option that’s perfect for a chilly evening.

The Benefits of Instant Pot Cooking

The Instant Pot has revolutionized the way we cook, allowing us to prepare meals quickly and efficiently while preserving nutrients and flavors. This electric pressure cooker is particularly well-suited for cooking chili, as it can tenderize tough beans and vegetables in a fraction of the time it takes with traditional cooking methods.

The Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups mixed bell peppers (any color), diced
  • 2 cups cooked kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Press the “Saute” button on the Instant Pot and heat the olive oil until shimmering. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute, until fragrant.
  3. Add the mixed bell peppers and cook until they start to soften, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the cooked kidney beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using). Stir to combine.
  5. Close the lid of the Instant Pot and set the valve to “Sealing”. Press the “Manual” or “Pressure Cook” button and set the cooking time to 20 minutes at high pressure.
  6. When the cooking time is up, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
  7. Open the lid and season the chili with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • To make this recipe more substantial, serve with a side of crusty bread, cornbread, or a green salad.
  • For an added boost of protein, add cooked ground turkey, chicken, or beans to the chili.
  • Experiment with different types of peppers, such as jalapeños or Anaheim peppers, for added heat and flavor.
  • Make this recipe in bulk and freeze for up to 3 months. Simply thaw and reheat when needed.

Conclusion

This Instant Pot Vegetable and Bean Chili recipe is a game-changer for anyone looking for a healthy and filling meal option. With its rich flavors and tender texture, it’s perfect for a chilly evening or a quick lunch. By using the Instant Pot, we can cook this recipe quickly and efficiently while preserving the nutrients and flavors of the ingredients. Whether you’re a busy professional or a busy parent, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your household. So go ahead, give it a try, and enjoy the warmth and comfort of a delicious bowl of chili!
