Korean Spicy Braised Tofu: A Fiery and Flavorful Vegan Treat


Are you ready to tantalize your taste buds with a dish that’s both fiery and flavorful? Look no further than Korean Spicy Braised Tofu! This vegan delight is a perfect combination of spicy, savory, and satisfying, making it a go-to dish for anyone craving a burst of Korean-inspired flavors. In this recipe, we’ll take you through the step-by-step process of creating this mouthwatering dish right in your own kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 block of firm tofu
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon of cooking oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Tofu: Start by draining the tofu and pressing it to remove excess water. Once drained, cut the tofu into bite-sized cubes and set aside.

  2. Create the Sauce: In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, gochujang, gochugaru, sesame oil, and sugar. Mix well until the sugar is fully dissolved and set the sauce aside.

  3. Sauté the Aromatics: Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic, sliced onion, and a pinch of salt. Sauté until the onions are translucent and the garlic is fragrant.

  4. Add the Tofu: Gently add the tofu cubes to the skillet, allowing them to brown slightly on each side.

  5. Incorporate the Sauce: Pour the prepared sauce over the tofu and gently toss to coat each piece evenly.

  6. Braise the Tofu: Pour the vegetable broth into the skillet and bring the mixture to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat to low and let the tofu simmer in the spicy sauce for about 15-20 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened and the tofu has absorbed the flavors.

  7. Finish and Garnish: Once the tofu is braised to perfection, sprinkle the chopped green onion and toasted sesame seeds over the top. Add a pinch of black pepper for an extra kick.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Korean Spicy Braised Tofu is best enjoyed hot, served over a bed of steamed rice. The combination of the fiery tofu and fluffy rice is sure to leave your taste buds singing with delight.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of freshness, consider adding thinly sliced carrots or bell peppers to the dish.
  • If you prefer a milder spice level, reduce the amount of gochugaru in the recipe.
  • Experiment with different types of tofu, such as silken or extra-firm, to find the texture that suits your preference.

The Fiery Flavors of Korea

Korean Spicy Braised Tofu is a dish that embodies the bold and vibrant flavors of Korean cuisine. The marriage of spicy chili paste, savory soy sauce, and nutty sesame oil creates a symphony of tastes that dance on your palate with each bite. Whether you’re a seasoned tofu enthusiast or someone looking to expand their vegan recipe repertoire, this dish is a must-try.
