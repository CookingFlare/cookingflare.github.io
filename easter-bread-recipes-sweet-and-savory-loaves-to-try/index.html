Easter Bread Recipes: Sweet and Savory Loaves to Try


Easter is a time of renewal, reflection, and celebration. It’s a holiday filled with family gatherings, festive meals, and vibrant traditions. One of the most cherished aspects of Easter is the food—rich, flavorful, and often symbolic. Among the many Easter foods, Easter bread holds a special place in many cultures, with its sweet and savory versions making an appearance on dinner tables all around the world. Whether you are preparing for a formal Easter dinner or simply want to bake something special, these Easter bread recipes will add a touch of warmth, flavor, and tradition to your holiday celebrations.

Sweet Easter Breads

Sweet Easter breads are often made with rich doughs, filled with fruit, nuts, and spices. These breads typically feature a soft, fluffy texture and are sometimes topped with colorful icing or sprinkles, making them as visually stunning as they are delicious. Here are some sweet Easter bread recipes that you should try this year.

1. Classic Italian Easter Bread

This traditional Italian Easter bread is a beautiful centerpiece for any Easter table. Known for its sweet, braided shape and vibrant eggs placed in the center, this bread symbolizes rebirth and new life. The dough is enriched with butter, eggs, and sugar, resulting in a soft, slightly sweet bread that is perfect for breakfast or dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 4 colored hard-boiled eggs

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm milk and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Add eggs, butter, vanilla extract, and the yeast mixture.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Cover the dough and let it rise in a warm place for 1 to 1.5 hours or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and divide it into three equal parts. Roll each piece into a long rope.
  6. Braid the ropes together, forming a circular shape. Place the colored hard-boiled eggs into the dough, securing them within the braid.
  7. Place the bread on a greased baking sheet and let it rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush the bread with beaten egg for a golden finish.
  9. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. Let cool before serving.

2. Greek Tsoureki

Tsoureki is a traditional Greek Easter bread, beloved for its soft, slightly sweet flavor, and its aromatic blend of spices like mahlab and orange zest. The bread is often braided and includes a red-dyed egg, representing Christ’s blood. This bread is a symbol of the Greek Orthodox faith and is enjoyed with family and friends.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon ground mahleb (or substitute with ground cardamom)
  • 1 teaspoon orange zest
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)
  • 1 red-dyed egg (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small saucepan, warm the milk and butter over low heat until the butter melts. Remove from heat and let cool until lukewarm.
  2. In a large bowl, dissolve yeast in the milk-butter mixture. Add sugar and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  3. Add eggs, mahleb, orange zest, salt, and flour to the yeast mixture. Stir until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise for 1.5 hours or until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down the dough and divide it into three equal pieces. Roll each piece into a long rope, then braid the ropes together.
  7. Form the braided dough into a circle and tuck a red-dyed egg into the braid if desired.
  8. Place the bread on a greased baking sheet and let it rise for 30 minutes.
  9. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Brush the dough with a beaten egg for a shiny finish.
  10. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.
  11. Let cool before slicing and serving.

3. Sweet Cinnamon Easter Bread

Cinnamon rolls are a favorite treat for many, and this sweet cinnamon Easter bread is a perfect twist on that classic comfort food. The dough is filled with cinnamon sugar, rolled up, and baked to perfection for a decadent and aromatic bread that will fill your home with a mouthwatering scent.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted (for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm milk with sugar. Let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add eggs, butter, and the yeast mixture. Mix until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 to 1.5 hours or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and roll it out into a rectangular shape on a floured surface.
  6. Spread melted butter over the dough and sprinkle cinnamon and brown sugar on top.
  7. Roll the dough up tightly and slice it into 8-10 pieces.
  8. Place the cinnamon rolls in a greased baking dish and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  9. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown.
  10. Brush with melted butter before serving.

Savory Easter Breads

While sweet Easter breads are a favorite, savory loaves also have a special place at the table. Savory Easter breads are often filled with herbs, cheeses, and meats, making them a perfect addition to a holiday brunch or dinner spread. If you’re looking for something with a little more bite, try these savory Easter bread recipes.

4. Savory Herb and Cheese Easter Bread

This savory Easter bread is a perfect balance of fresh herbs and gooey melted cheese. It makes an excellent accompaniment to Easter lamb, salads, or just served with a pat of butter. The dough is soft, yet sturdy enough to hold up to hearty fillings of herbs and cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh thyme
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add olive oil and the yeast mixture.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for about 10 minutes until smooth.
  4. Add rosemary, thyme, and mozzarella cheese to the dough and knead until evenly incorporated.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1 to 1.5 hours or until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down the dough and shape it into a loaf.
  7. Place the loaf on a greased baking sheet and let it rise for 30 minutes.
  8. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the dough with a beaten egg for a golden finish.
  9. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the bread is golden brown.
  10. Let cool slightly before slicing and serving.

5. Italian Easter Cheese Bread (Pane di Pasqua)

This savory Italian Easter bread is made with a blend of cheeses and is often served with cured meats and olives. It has a rich, hearty flavor and a dense texture that makes it a filling addition to any meal.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup shredded provolone cheese
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture, olive oil, eggs, and cheese.
  3. Mix until a dough forms, then knead for 10 minutes.
  4. Let the dough rise for 1 hour or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch down the dough and shape it into a round loaf.
  6. Place the loaf on a greased baking sheet and let it rise for 30 minutes.
  7. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the dough with beaten egg.
  8. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until golden brown.
  9. Let cool before serving.

Conclusion

Whether you’re making a sweet bread to share at brunch or a savory loaf to accompany your Easter dinner, these Easter bread recipes will bring joy and flavor to your table. Each loaf has its own distinct history and cultural significance, and they all share one thing in common: they bring people together. So, gather your ingredients, embrace the holiday spirit, and create some unforgettable Easter bread that will be enjoyed for years to come. Happy baking and happy Easter!
