How to Make Delicious and Crunchy Middle Eastern Falafel


Falafel is a beloved street food that has transcended its Middle Eastern roots to become a global sensation. Whether you’re enjoying them as a quick snack, a satisfying lunch, or as part of a larger meal, these crispy, golden-brown balls of flavor are hard to resist. But the key to truly irresistible falafel lies in getting the texture just right—crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, with a rich burst of flavor in every bite.

In this guide, we’ll take you through a step-by-step process on how to make perfect falafel, from selecting the right ingredients to frying the falafel to golden perfection.

What is Falafel?

Falafel is a deep-fried ball or patty made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with a variety of herbs, spices, and other seasonings. Originating from the Middle East, falafel has been enjoyed by people of different cultures for centuries. It’s usually served in pita bread or flatbread, often accompanied by fresh vegetables, tahini sauce, or yogurt.

Despite its rich history, falafel remains incredibly versatile and can be served in many ways, from wraps to salads, and even as a side dish. It’s a fantastic choice for both vegetarians and meat-lovers, as the deep-fried chickpeas provide a satisfying, protein-packed alternative to meat.

Key Ingredients for Making the Perfect Falafel

To make crispy and delicious falafel at home, you’ll need a few essential ingredients. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll need:

1. Chickpeas (Garbanzo Beans)

The foundation of falafel is chickpeas. You can use canned chickpeas in a pinch, but for the best texture and flavor, it’s recommended to use dried chickpeas. Soak them overnight in water. The chickpeas absorb the water, making them soft and easier to blend.

2. Fresh Parsley and Cilantro

The combination of fresh parsley and cilantro gives falafel its signature green color and fresh herbal flavor. Make sure to chop them finely for an even distribution of flavor in the falafel mixture.

3. Onion

Onions add sweetness and depth to the falafel. You can either use yellow onions or red onions depending on your preference. Make sure to chop the onion finely to avoid chunks in your falafel mixture.

4. Garlic

Garlic is essential in adding that deep, aromatic flavor to the falafel. Fresh garlic cloves should be finely minced or grated to ensure they blend well with the other ingredients.

5. Spices

The spices you choose will determine the final taste of your falafel. Common spices used include ground cumin, coriander, and a pinch of cayenne pepper for a slight kick. Some people also add a bit of ground turmeric for color and flavor.

6. Baking Powder

Baking powder is a key ingredient that helps create a light and airy interior for your falafel. This is what gives falafel its delicate fluffiness once fried.

7. Salt and Pepper

Seasoning your falafel with salt and pepper is crucial to enhancing the flavors of the herbs and spices. Be sure to taste the mixture before frying to ensure the seasoning is just right.

8. Flour or Chickpea Flour

Flour helps bind the falafel mixture together and prevents it from falling apart when frying. You can use regular flour or chickpea flour for a gluten-free option. The amount of flour you need will depend on the moisture content of your mixture.

9. Oil for Frying

Vegetable oil, canola oil, or sunflower oil are all good choices for frying. You’ll want to have enough oil in the pan so the falafel can float and fry evenly. Using oil at the right temperature is essential for a crisp exterior.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Falafel

Making falafel at home is a relatively simple process, but it does take some time. Here’s a detailed step-by-step guide to making falafel from scratch.

Step 1: Soak the Chickpeas

Start by soaking dried chickpeas in plenty of water for about 12 hours (overnight is best). This rehydrates the chickpeas and makes them soft enough to blend into a smooth mixture. Don’t skip this step—using canned chickpeas will give your falafel a soggy texture, which is hard to get crispy.

Step 2: Prepare the Ingredients

While your chickpeas are soaking, gather and prepare the other ingredients. Finely chop your parsley and cilantro, mince the garlic, and chop the onion into small pieces. The finer your chopping, the more evenly your falafel mixture will come together.

Step 3: Blend the Ingredients

Once the chickpeas have soaked, drain them thoroughly and place them in a food processor. Add the parsley, cilantro, garlic, onion, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Pulse the mixture until it forms a coarse, crumbly texture. You don’t want it to be too smooth—keeping some texture is key to the final result.

Step 4: Add Baking Powder and Flour

Once the mixture is blended, add the baking powder and a tablespoon of flour (or chickpea flour) to the mixture. Pulse again until everything is evenly combined. If the mixture seems too wet, add more flour a little at a time until it holds together well when pressed.

Step 5: Let the Mixture Rest

Once your falafel mixture is ready, transfer it to a bowl, cover it, and let it rest in the fridge for at least an hour. This step allows the flavors to meld and makes the mixture easier to form into balls or patties.

Step 6: Form the Falafel

After resting, it’s time to shape the falafel. Use your hands or a falafel scoop to form small balls about 1 ½ inches in diameter. You can also shape them into small patties if you prefer. It’s essential that the falafel holds together well. If the mixture is too loose, add a bit more flour. If it’s too dry, add a splash of water.

Step 7: Heat the Oil

In a deep pan or skillet, heat about 2 inches of oil over medium-high heat. You’ll want the oil to be around 350°F (175°C) for frying. To test if the oil is hot enough, drop in a small piece of the falafel mixture—if it sizzles and rises to the surface immediately, the oil is ready.

Step 8: Fry the Falafel

Carefully drop the falafel balls into the hot oil, a few at a time, being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to remove the falafel from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

Step 9: Serve and Enjoy

Falafel is best enjoyed hot and fresh. Serve your crispy falafel with pita bread, a drizzle of tahini sauce, a squeeze of lemon, and a side of fresh vegetables such as tomatoes, cucumber, and lettuce. You can also serve them with hummus, a yogurt-based dip, or any other sauce of your choice.

Tips for Perfect Falafel

1. Don’t Overwork the Mixture

While it’s important to blend the falafel ingredients well, overprocessing them will turn the mixture into a paste, which can affect the texture. A coarse, grainy texture is ideal for falafel.

2. Adjust the Seasoning

Taste the falafel mixture before frying. It’s essential to adjust the seasoning to your liking. If it needs more salt, pepper, or spices, don’t hesitate to add them. Remember, the flavors will intensify after frying.

3. Fry in Batches

Avoid overcrowding the pan when frying the falafel. Frying in batches ensures that the falafel have enough space to cook evenly and become crispy on all sides.

4. Experiment with Fillings and Toppings

While traditional falafel is typically served with tahini and vegetables, feel free to get creative with your toppings. You can add pickled vegetables, hot sauce, or even make a falafel bowl with quinoa and roasted vegetables.

5. Freeze for Later

If you have leftover falafel, you can freeze them for later. Allow them to cool completely, then store them in an airtight container in the freezer for up to three months. To reheat, bake them in the oven at 375°F (190°C) for about 10 minutes.

Variations of Falafel

1. Chickpea and Fava Bean Falafel

Some variations of falafel include fava beans instead of chickpeas. The fava beans add a slightly different texture and flavor, making the falafel even richer and more complex.

2. Baked Falafel

For a healthier version, you can bake your falafel instead of frying them. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and place the falafel on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.

3. Gluten-Free Falafel

If you need a gluten-free option, substitute the regular flour with chickpea flour or rice flour. This will ensure that the falafel holds together without any gluten-containing ingredients.

4. Spicy Falafel

For those who like a bit of heat, add more cayenne pepper or even finely chopped fresh chili peppers to the falafel mixture. This will give the falafel a spicy kick that pairs well with the creamy tahini sauce.

Conclusion

Making falafel at home can seem intimidating, but with the right ingredients and a little patience, you’ll be able to create a batch of perfectly crispy and delicious falafel. Whether you’re enjoying them as a snack, a meal, or sharing them with friends and family, these Middle Eastern delights are sure to impress. With the tips, variations, and step-by-step guide provided, you can now master the art of falafel-making and enjoy this classic dish whenever you want.
