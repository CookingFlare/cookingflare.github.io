Grilling Vegetarian: 5 Marinades for Flavorful Grilled Portobello


Are you tired of the same old veggie burgers and grilled vegetables? If you’re a vegetarian or simply looking to add more plant-based options to your grilling repertoire, then grilled portobello mushrooms are a must-try. These meaty mushrooms are perfect for grilling, and when marinated with flavorful seasonings, they become a delicious and satisfying main course. In this blog post, we’ll explore five mouthwatering marinades that will take your grilled portobello mushrooms to the next level.

1. Balsamic Glaze Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, minced garlic, honey, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them. Make sure the mushrooms are well-coated. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a more intense flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill them for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and lightly charred.
  4. Serve the grilled portobello mushrooms as a main course or slice them up and use them as a topping for burgers or salads.

2. Asian-inspired Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, minced garlic, grated ginger, and red pepper flakes (if using).
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in a ziplock bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and gently massage it to ensure the mushrooms are coated. Allow them to marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for a more robust flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill them for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they become tender and nicely charred.
  4. Serve the grilled portobello mushrooms on a bed of rice or alongside your favorite stir-fried vegetables for a complete Asian-inspired meal.

3. Mediterranean Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, ensuring they are well-coated. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a more pronounced flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill them for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and have nice grill marks.
  4. Serve the grilled portobello mushrooms with a side of quinoa salad or as a filling for a Mediterranean-inspired pita sandwich.

4. Smoky Chipotle Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 2 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, minced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the minced chipotle peppers, olive oil, lime juice, honey, minced garlic, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in a ziplock bag and pour the marinade over them. Seal the bag and gently massage it to ensure the mushrooms are coated. Allow them to marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for a more intense flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill them for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and have a smoky char.
  4. Serve the grilled portobello mushrooms on a toasted bun with avocado slices and a dollop of sour cream for a smoky and spicy treat.

5. Herb-infused Marinade

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped fresh rosemary, chopped fresh thyme, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place the portobello mushrooms in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, making sure they are well-coated. Let them marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a more pronounced herb flavor.
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Remove the mushrooms from the marinade and grill them for 4-5 minutes per side, or until they are tender and have a beautiful char.
  4. Serve the grilled portobello mushrooms alongside a fresh green salad or use them as a filling for a hearty sandwich.

Grilling vegetarian doesn’t have to be boring. With these five marinades, you can elevate the flavor of grilled portobello mushrooms and create a delightful and satisfying meal. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or looking for a delicious weeknight dinner, these marinades will surely impress your taste buds and leave you craving for more. So fire up the grill and let the deliciousness begin!
