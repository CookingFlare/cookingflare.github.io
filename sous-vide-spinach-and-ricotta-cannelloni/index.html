Sous Vide Spinach and Ricotta Cannelloni


Cannelloni, the quintessential Italian stuffed pasta dish, is a timeless crowd-pleaser. Its delicate tubes of pasta, brimming with lush, flavorful fillings, and smothered in robust tomato sauce then baked to golden perfection, make it a feast for the senses. If you’re looking for a way to elevate this classic dish into a gourmet masterpiece, sous vide cooking is a game-changer. Today, we’ll dive deep into scrumptious sous vide spinach and ricotta cannelloni that delivers unmatched texture, vibrant flavors, and professional-grade results in the comfort of your kitchen.

Why Use Sous Vide for Cannelloni?

Sous vide cooking has gained popularity for its precise temperature control, ensuring perfectly cooked ingredients every time. While it’s often associated with meat or delicate items like eggs, sous vide is equally transformative for vegetable-rich dishes like spinach and ricotta cannelloni. By using sous vide to prepare the spinach filling and even gently cook the finished cannelloni, you preserve the integrity of your ingredients and guarantee even cooking.

Unlike traditional baking alone, sous vide encourages moisture retention and melds flavors in a way that standard preparation methods simply can’t replicate. The result? Cannelloni with creamy, delicate filling that complements the silkiness of the pasta and the zing of your sauce, making every bite pure bliss.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Filling:

  • 250 grams fresh spinach, washed and trimmed
  • 250 grams ricotta cheese, strained for ideal texture
  • 100 grams grated Parmesan cheese, for nutty richness
  • 1 garlic clove, minced finely
  • A pinch of ground nutmeg, for warmth
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper, to taste

For the Cannelloni:

  • 12 cannelloni tubes, store-bought or homemade if you’re up for a challenge
  • 2 cups marinara or tomato basil sauce
  • 150 grams mozzarella cheese, shredded for melting glory
  • A few sprigs of fresh basil, for garnish

Optional Additions:

  • 1 egg yolk, for added creaminess in the filling
  • Zest of 1 lemon, for a touch of brightness

Equipment Required

  1. Sous vide immersion circulator
  2. Vacuum-sealer or resealable bags (compatible with water displacement for sous vide)
  3. Large pot or sous vide container
  4. Deep baking dish for the final phase
  5. Mixing bowls, spatula, and piping bag for assembling

Step-by-Step Recipe: Creating Cannelloni Perfection

Step 1: Prepare the Spinach

Begin by preheating your sous vide precision cooker to 82°C (180°F), the ideal temperature to retain spinach’s vibrant color while softening its texture. Place the fresh spinach in a vacuum-sealed bag or a resealable bag, using the water displacement method to remove excess air. Submerge the spinach into the water bath and cook it for 25 minutes.

Once ready, remove the spinach from the sous vide, drain any moisture, and finely chop. This process ensures the spinach retains its flavor without being watery—an essential step for the filling’s consistency.

Step 2: Mix the Ricotta Filling

In a mixing bowl, combine the chopped spinach, ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, garlic, nutmeg, and a touch of salt and pepper. If desired, fold in egg yolk and lemon zest for richer flavor and texture. Mix thoroughly until the ingredients meld into a creamy, green-speckled mixture.

Step 3: Prepare the Cannelloni Tubes

If using fresh pasta sheets, cut them into rectangles and roll them into tubes after filling. If store-bought tubes are your choice, ensure they’re uncooked; they will absorb the sauce’s moisture perfectly during sous vide baking. Load the spinach and ricotta filling into a piping bag to facilitate stuffing the cannelloni with ease.

Carefully fill each tube without overloading—aim for balance to ensure reheating doesn’t burst the delicate pasta shells.

Step 4: Assemble the Dish

Once filled, line a vacuum-sealable sous vide bag with marinara or tomato basil sauce to coat the base evenly. Arrange the stuffed cannelloni tubes inside the bag in a single layer. Pour additional marinara sauce over the tubes to coat and seal the bag, using the vacuum sealer or water displacement method.

Place the sealed cannelloni into the preheated water bath and cook at 85°C (185°F) for 45 minutes to 1 hour. During this time, the flavors integrate, and the pasta softens gently, absorbing the surrounding sauce.

Step 5: Finish and Serve

Preheat your oven to 230°C (450°F) or use the broil setting. Once the sous vide cannelloni is done, transfer them to a deep baking dish, along with the flavorful sauce in the bag. Sprinkle generously with shredded mozzarella cheese and bake for approximately 8-10 minutes, until the cheese is gooey and bubbly with golden brown spots.

For final touches, garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve piping hot. Pair the dish with crusty garlic bread or a crisp side salad for a complete meal.

Tips for Variations and Enhancements

  • Meaty Upgrade: Add ground beef, turkey, or Italian sausage to the filling if you’re craving a protein-packed indulgence.
  • Vegan Cannelloni: Swap ricotta cheese with tofu blended with nutritional yeast and opt for dairy-free mozzarella alternatives.
  • Alternative Sauces: Experiment with bechamel or a creamy Alfredo sauce instead of marinara for a decadent twist.
  • Herbs and Spices: Play with herbs like thyme, oregano, or parsley in the filling to enhance its aroma.

Storing and Reheating

If you have leftovers or want to make the dish ahead, store the cannelloni in an airtight container for up to three days in the refrigerator. To reheat, bake in an oven preheated to 180°C (350°F) for 10-15 minutes, adding a bit of extra sauce to maintain moisture.

Why This Recipe Stands Out

Cannelloni is a comfort food classic, but the sous vide technique transforms it into an extraordinary culinary experience. The gentle, precise cooking method ensures every element—from spinach’s vibrancy to pasta’s delicate softness—is perfect. Adding ricotta’s creaminess and tomato sauce’s depth guarantees a dish that’s equal parts indulgent and refined.

If you’re ready to tackle this sous vide spinach and ricotta cannelloni recipe, prepare to wow your dinner guests or simply treat yourself to a restaurant-worthy plate of Italian delight.
