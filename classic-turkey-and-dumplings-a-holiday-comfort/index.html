Classic Turkey and Dumplings: A Holiday Comfort


As the holiday season rolls around, kitchens across the nation fill with enticing aromas and the promise of cherished family traditions. One dish that evokes warmth and nostalgia is Classic Turkey and Dumplings. This comforting meal, often served as a centerpiece on holiday tables, showcases tender turkey simmered in a rich, savory broth, accompanied by fluffy dumplings that soak up the delicious flavors. In this blog post, we will dive deep into the history of this beloved dish, explore its ingredients, and provide you with a step-by-step guide to crafting the perfect Turkey and Dumplings at home.

The History of Turkey and Dumplings

Turkey and Dumplings has its roots in the heart of American comfort food. The dish is believed to have originated from the early settlers who sought to make the most of their resources. Turkey, as a staple protein, was readily available, especially during the autumn months when harvests were plentiful. Dumplings, made from simple ingredients such as flour, water, and fat, were an economical way to stretch meals further.

Over the years, this dish has evolved into a holiday classic. Many families have their own variations, often passed down through generations. The beauty of Turkey and Dumplings lies in its versatility. While some might prefer a creamy, thick broth, others may opt for a lighter, broth-based soup. Regardless of the variation, the essence of the dish remains the same: it embodies the warmth and togetherness of family gatherings.

Ingredients for Classic Turkey and Dumplings

To create a comforting pot of Turkey and Dumplings, you will need the following ingredients. The recipe is designed to serve approximately six to eight people, making it perfect for family gatherings or cozy dinners.

For the Turkey and Broth

  • 1 whole turkey (3-4 pounds) or 4-5 cups of cooked turkey meat, shredded
  • 8 cups of low-sodium chicken broth (or homemade turkey broth, if available)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2-3 carrots, sliced
  • 2-3 stalks of celery, diced
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil or butter
  • 1 cup of frozen peas (optional, for added color and sweetness)

For the Dumplings

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Classic Turkey and Dumplings

Step 1: Prepare the Turkey and Broth

  1. Cook the Turkey: If you have a whole turkey, roast it in the oven according to your preferred method. Alternatively, you can use leftover turkey from a previous meal. If using a whole turkey, be sure to keep the bones for the broth.

  2. Make the Broth: In a large pot, heat the olive oil or butter over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Saut√© for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are softened. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute until fragrant.

  3. Add the Broth: Pour in the chicken broth and bring it to a simmer. If using a whole turkey, add it to the pot, along with any drippings from the roasting pan. If using shredded turkey, set it aside for later.

  4. Season the Broth: Stir in the dried thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed. Let the broth simmer for about 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 2: Prepare the Dumplings

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.

  2. Mix Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, combine the milk and melted butter. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; the dough should be slightly lumpy.

Step 3: Assemble and Cook

  1. Add the Turkey: If you used a whole turkey, remove it from the pot and shred the meat, discarding the bones. Return the shredded turkey back to the pot.

  2. Form Dumplings: Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop spoonfuls of the dumpling batter directly onto the simmering broth. Make sure to leave some space between each dumpling, as they will expand while cooking.

  3. Simmer the Dumplings: Cover the pot with a lid and let the dumplings cook for about 15-20 minutes. Do not lift the lid during this time, as the steam is essential for cooking the dumplings properly. You will know they are done when they have puffed up and are no longer doughy in the center.

  4. Add Peas: For a pop of color and sweetness, add frozen peas during the last few minutes of cooking. Stir gently to combine.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Once the dumplings are cooked through, ladle the Turkey and Dumplings into bowls. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley for a touch of brightness. Serve hot and enjoy the comforting flavors that fill your home.

Tips for the Perfect Turkey and Dumplings

  • Use Homemade Broth: If you have the time, making homemade turkey broth is a game changer. It adds a depth of flavor that store-bought broth often lacks. Simply simmer turkey bones, vegetable scraps, and herbs in water for several hours to create a rich broth.

  • Customize Your Dumplings: Feel free to get creative with your dumplings! Add herbs like chives or dill for a flavor twist, or incorporate grated cheese for a cheesy version.

  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the turkey and broth a day in advance. Simply reheat it before adding the dumplings. This also allows the flavors to deepen overnight.

  • Storing Leftovers: Turkey and Dumplings can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stove, adding a splash of broth if needed to loosen the consistency.
