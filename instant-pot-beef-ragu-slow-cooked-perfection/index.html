Instant Pot Beef Ragu: Slow-Cooked Perfection


Ragu, a classic Italian meat sauce, is traditionally slow-cooked for hours to develop deep, rich flavors. With the help of the Instant Pot, you can achieve that same slow-cooked perfection in a fraction of the time. This beef ragu recipe is perfect for busy weeknights or when you want to impress guests with minimal effort. The combination of tender beef, aromatic vegetables, and a flavorful tomato-based sauce makes this dish a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

For the Beef Ragu:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck roast, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

For Serving:

  • Cooked pasta (such as pappardelle, fettuccine, or rigatoni)
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Begin by preparing all your ingredients. Cut the beef chuck roast into 2-inch pieces and season with salt and pepper. Finely chop the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Having everything prepped and ready will make the cooking process smoother.

Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

Set your Instant Pot to sauté mode. Add the olive oil and let it heat up. Once hot, add the finely chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are softened and translucent. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

Step 3: Brown the Beef

Push the sautéed vegetables to the side of the Instant Pot and add the seasoned beef pieces. Brown the beef on all sides, working in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding the pot. This browning step helps to develop a deeper flavor for the ragu.

Step 4: Add Tomato Paste and Wine

Once the beef is browned, add the tomato paste to the pot and stir well to coat the meat and vegetables. Cook for about 2-3 minutes to allow the tomato paste to caramelize slightly. Then, pour in the red wine, using a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let the wine simmer for a few minutes until it reduces by half.

Step 5: Add Tomatoes and Seasonings

Next, add the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, dried thyme, and bay leaf to the Instant Pot. Stir well to combine all the ingredients. Pour in the beef broth and give everything one last stir.

Step 6: Pressure Cook the Ragu

Secure the lid on the Instant Pot and set it to high pressure for 45 minutes. Once the cooking time is complete, allow the pressure to release naturally for 10 minutes, then perform a quick release to release any remaining pressure.

Step 7: Finish and Thicken the Sauce

Carefully remove the lid and discard the bay leaf. Using two forks, shred the beef chunks directly in the pot. The meat should be tender and fall apart easily. Stir in the balsamic vinegar, which adds a lovely depth of flavor to the sauce. If the sauce is too thin, you can set the Instant Pot to sauté mode and let it simmer for a few minutes to thicken.

Step 8: Serve the Beef Ragu

Serve the beef ragu over your favorite cooked pasta. Pappardelle, fettuccine, and rigatoni are excellent choices as they hold up well to the hearty sauce. Top with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a sprinkle of chopped fresh parsley for added freshness and color.

Tips for Perfect Beef Ragu

  • Choosing the Right Beef: For the best results, use a well-marbled cut of beef such as chuck roast. The marbling ensures that the meat becomes tender and flavorful during cooking.
  • Wine Selection: A good-quality red wine adds depth to the ragu. Opt for a dry red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot.
  • Vegetable Prep: Finely chopping the vegetables helps them break down and blend into the sauce, adding a natural sweetness and complexity.
  • Browning the Meat: Don’t skip the step of browning the beef. It creates a rich, caramelized layer that enhances the overall flavor of the ragu.
  • Natural Pressure Release: Allowing the pressure to release naturally for at least 10 minutes ensures that the meat remains tender and juicy.

Variations and Substitutions

  • Protein Options: While beef is traditional, you can also use other proteins like pork shoulder or lamb for a different flavor profile.
  • Vegetarian Ragu: Substitute beef with hearty vegetables like mushrooms and lentils for a vegetarian version of ragu.
  • Spicy Ragu: Add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a diced chili pepper for a spicy kick.
  • Herb Variations: Experiment with different herbs such as rosemary or sage to customize the flavor to your liking.
  • Gluten-Free Option: Serve the beef ragu over gluten-free pasta or vegetable noodles like zucchini or spaghetti squash.

Storing and Reheating

Storage:

  • Refrigerator: Store leftover beef ragu in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
  • Freezer: For longer storage, freeze the beef ragu in portions in freezer-safe containers or bags for up to 3 months.

Reheating:

  • Stovetop: Reheat the ragu on the stovetop over medium heat, stirring occasionally until heated through.
  • Microwave: Use a microwave-safe dish and reheat in the microwave in 1-minute intervals, stirring in between, until warm.

Serving Suggestions

Beef ragu is incredibly versatile and pairs well with various side dishes. Here are some serving suggestions to complete your meal:

  • Garlic Bread: Serve with warm garlic bread to soak up the delicious sauce.
  • Salad: A simple green salad with a tangy vinaigrette provides a refreshing contrast to the rich ragu.
  • Roasted Vegetables: Roasted Brussels sprouts, carrots, or asparagus make excellent side dishes.
  • Polenta: For a comforting twist, serve the beef ragu over creamy polenta instead of pasta.

Conclusion

Instant Pot Beef Ragu is a game-changer for anyone who loves rich, slow-cooked flavors but doesn’t have the time to spend hours in the kitchen. This recipe delivers tender beef and a deeply flavorful sauce in under an hour, making it perfect for both weeknight dinners and special occasions. By following these steps and tips, you can create a restaurant-quality beef ragu that will impress your family and friends. So, dust off your Instant Pot and get ready to enjoy a bowl of comforting, hearty beef ragu that tastes like it’s been simmering all day.
