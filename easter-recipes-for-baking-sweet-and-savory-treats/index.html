Easter Recipes for Baking: Sweet and Savory Treats


Easter is a special time when families and friends gather, share meals, and create lasting memories. It’s a season of renewal and celebration, making it the perfect occasion to bake delightful treats that everyone can enjoy. Whether you have a penchant for sweet delicacies or a craving for savory bites, this guide offers a collection of Easter recipes to brighten up your baking repertoire. From charming desserts to satisfying savory snacks, there’s something here for everyone.

Classic Hot Cross Buns

Hot cross buns are synonymous with Easter baking, offering a warm, spiced flavor perfect for breakfast or an afternoon snack. Here’s how to make your own batch of soft, pillowy buns.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk (about 110°F)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup dried currants or raisins
  • 1/4 cup chopped dried apricots (optional)
  • For the cross:
    • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
    • 6-8 tablespoons water
  • For the glaze:
    • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tablespoons water

Instructions

  1. Combine warm milk and yeast in a small bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until bubbly.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, salt, and spices.
  3. Add the melted butter, egg, and yeast mixture to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes or until smooth.
  5. Add dried currants and apricots, kneading them evenly into the dough.
  6. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.
  7. Divide the dough into 12 equal pieces and shape them into balls. Arrange on a baking sheet, leaving space for expansion. Let rise again for 30 minutes.
  8. Mix flour and water to make a thick paste. Pipe crosses onto each bun.
  9. Bake at 375°F for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. For the glaze, heat sugar and water until dissolved. Brush the warm buns for a glossy finish.

Easter Egg Sugar Cookies

Vibrant sugar cookies decorated like Easter eggs will add a festive touch to your celebration. Plus, they make a fantastic activity for kids to enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Food coloring
  • Assorted sprinkles (optional)
  • Royal icing (store-bought or homemade)

Instructions

  1. Whisk flour, baking powder, and salt in a medium bowl. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, cream butter and sugar until fluffy.
  3. Add the egg and vanilla, mixing until combined.
  4. Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients until a dough forms.
  5. Divide the dough into 2-3 portions and knead food coloring into each. Wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  6. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/4-inch thickness. Use an egg-shaped cookie cutter to cut out shapes.
  7. Bake at 350°F for 8-10 minutes, or until edges are lightly browned. Let cool completely.
  8. Decorate with royal icing and sprinkles. Get creative with patterns and colors for a personalized touch.

Cheesy Savory Scones

For those who prefer savory treats, these cheesy scones are a mouthwatering addition to an Easter brunch or tea spread.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped chives
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, salt, garlic powder, and smoked paprika.
  2. Cut in the butter with a pastry cutter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Fold in the cheese and chives.
  4. Gradually add buttermilk, mixing until the dough just comes together.
  5. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and gently pat into a 1-inch thick round. Slice into wedges.
  6. Arrange wedges on a baking sheet and brush with egg wash.
  7. Bake at 400°F for 18-22 minutes, or until golden brown.

Carrot Cake Muffins with Cream Cheese Frosting

These moist carrot cake muffins are a satisfying treat for Easter, topped with a decadent cream cheese frosting.

Ingredients

  • For the muffins:
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
    • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
    • 3/4 cup brown sugar
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 1/4 cups finely grated carrots
    • 1/3 cup crushed pineapple, well-drained
  • For the frosting:
    • 4 oz cream cheese, softened
    • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F and prepare a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat eggs, oil, applesauce, brown sugar, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Fold in carrots and pineapple.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups and bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean. Cool completely.
  6. For the frosting, beat cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla, mixing well.
  7. Pipe or spread the frosting onto the muffins.

Spinach and Feta Puff Pastry Tart

This savory pastry is a show-stopping centerpiece for Easter brunch. With its flaky crust and rich spinach-feta filling, it’s a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Roll out puff pastry and place it on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  2. Heat olive oil in a pan. Sauté onion and garlic until fragrant. Add spinach and cook until wilted. Season with oregano, salt, and pepper. Let cool slightly.
  3. Spread the spinach mixture evenly over the puff pastry, leaving a 1-inch border. Sprinkle feta cheese on top.
  4. Fold the edges of the pastry inward and brush with beaten egg.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown and puffed.

Final Thoughts

With these sweet and savory Easter baking recipes, you’re sure to impress your loved ones and create a meal that’s bursting with flavor. Whether you’re arranging a festive brunch or serving dessert after dinner, these treats are bound to be a highlight. Celebrate the season by experimenting with these flavorful recipes and creating a spread that will have everyone asking for seconds. Happy baking!
