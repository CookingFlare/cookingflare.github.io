5 Cheap and Easy Homemade Salad Dressing Recipes


Salad dressings are the unsung heroes of any salad. They have the power to elevate a simple bowl of greens into a flavorful and satisfying meal. While store-bought dressings are convenient, making your own at home is not only cost-effective but also allows you to customize the flavors to your liking. In this post, I’m sharing five delicious and easy homemade salad dressing recipes that are budget-friendly and full of flavor.

1. Classic Balsamic Vinaigrette

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, and minced garlic.
  2. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking continuously to emulsify the dressing.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Store any leftovers in a sealed container in the refrigerator for up to a week.

This classic balsamic vinaigrette is perfect for drizzling over mixed greens, tomatoes, and fresh mozzarella for a simple and elegant salad.

2. Creamy Lemon Tahini Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the tahini, water, lemon juice, honey, and minced garlic until smooth.
  2. Add more water if needed to reach the desired consistency.
  3. Season with salt to taste.
  4. This dressing can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.

The creamy texture and tangy flavor of this dressing make it a perfect companion for roasted vegetable salads or grain bowls.

3. Honey Mustard Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the Dijon mustard, honey, and apple cider vinegar.
  2. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking continuously until well combined.
  3. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. This dressing can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 10 days.

The sweet and tangy flavors of this honey mustard dressing pair perfectly with crispy chicken salads or as a dipping sauce for roasted vegetables.

4. Zesty Italian Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mason jar or bottle, combine the olive oil, red wine vinegar, dried herbs, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Close the lid tightly and shake vigorously until well combined.
  3. This dressing can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

The herbaceous and tangy notes of this zesty Italian dressing are perfect for marinating grilled vegetables or dressing a classic antipasto salad.

5. Spicy Peanut Dressing

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, rice vinegar, soy sauce, honey, grated ginger, minced garlic, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Add water gradually to reach the desired consistency.
  3. Season with salt to taste.
  4. This dressing can be refrigerated for up to 5 days.

The rich and spicy flavors of this peanut dressing are an excellent complement to Asian-inspired salads or cold noodle dishes.

These five homemade salad dressing recipes are not only easy to make but also budget-friendly, allowing you to enjoy delicious and healthy salads without breaking the bank. Experiment with these recipes, tweak the ingredients to suit your taste, and discover the joy of creating your own dressings at home. Happy salad-making!
