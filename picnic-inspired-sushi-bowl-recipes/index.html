Picnic-Inspired Sushi Bowl Recipes


When it comes to picnics, the first thing that comes to mind is a delightful array of finger foods, refreshing drinks, and of course, the perfect weather to enjoy it all. If you’re looking to bring a touch of creativity to your next outdoor gathering, why not consider whipping up some picnic-inspired sushi bowl recipes? Sushi bowls provide all the flavors and textures of sushi in a convenient and portable format, making them an ideal addition to any picnic spread.

Salmon and Avocado Sushi Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 pound fresh salmon, sliced
  • 2 ripe avocados, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/4 cup pickled ginger
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons sliced green onions

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice according to the package instructions. In a small bowl, mix the rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Once the rice is cooked, gently fold in the vinegar mixture and let it cool.
  2. Assemble the sushi bowls by dividing the seasoned rice among serving bowls. Top the rice with slices of fresh salmon, avocado, and julienned cucumber.
  3. Garnish the bowls with pickled ginger, a drizzle of soy sauce, sesame seeds, and sliced green onions.
  4. Serve the sushi bowls chilled or at room temperature for a refreshing picnic treat.

Vegan Sushi Bowl with Tofu

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup edamame, shelled and steamed
  • 1 small ripe mango, diced
  • 1/4 cup nori strips
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice and prepare the seasoned rice as mentioned in the previous recipe.
  2. In a skillet, lightly pan-fry the cubed tofu until golden brown. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, and maple syrup. Pour the mixture over the tofu and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  3. Assemble the sushi bowls by dividing the seasoned rice among serving bowls. Top the rice with the marinated tofu, julienned carrot, steamed edamame, diced mango, and nori strips.
  4. Sprinkle the sushi bowls with sesame seeds for added crunch and flavor.
  5. Enjoy this vibrant and refreshing vegan sushi bowl at your next picnic for a delightful plant-based option.

Spicy Tuna Sushi Bowl

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 pound sashimi-grade tuna, diced
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup masago (fish roe)
  • 1/4 cup sliced radishes
  • 1/4 cup sliced cucumbers
  • 1/4 cup spicy mayo (1/4 cup mayonnaise mixed with 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce)
  • 2 tablespoons sliced green onions
  • 1 tablespoon furikake seasoning

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the sushi rice and seasoned rice as per the previous recipes.
  2. In a bowl, mix the diced tuna with sriracha sauce and mayonnaise to create a spicy tuna mixture.
  3. Assemble the sushi bowls by dividing the seasoned rice among serving bowls. Top the rice with the spicy tuna mixture, masago, sliced radishes, cucumbers, and a drizzle of spicy mayo.
  4. Garnish the bowls with sliced green onions and a sprinkle of furikake seasoning for an extra burst of flavor and color.
  5. Spice up your picnic with these zesty and flavorful spicy tuna sushi bowls that are sure to be a hit with your fellow picnic-goers.

Whether you’re a seafood lover, a fan of plant-based cuisine, or someone who enjoys a bit of heat in their dishes, these picnic-inspired sushi bowl recipes offer a delightful twist on traditional sushi and are perfect for packing up and taking on a picnic adventure. So, the next time you’re planning an outdoor gathering, consider adding these sushi bowls to your menu for a truly memorable and delicious picnic experience.
