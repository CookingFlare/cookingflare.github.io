Spanish Gazpacho Recipe with Tomatoes and Bread


If you’re looking for a refreshing and flavorful dish to beat the summer heat, look no further than the classic Spanish Gazpacho. This cold soup is a beloved staple of Spanish cuisine, known for its vibrant color, tangy flavor, and simple preparation. In this recipe, we’ll guide you through the steps to create an authentic Spanish Gazpacho infused with the goodness of ripe tomatoes and the heartiness of bread.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, cored and roughly chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, cored and roughly chopped
  • 1 small red onion, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups day-old bread, crust removed and torn into pieces
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Ice cubes (optional, for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the chopped tomatoes, cucumber, green bell pepper, red onion, and minced garlic.

  2. Soak the Bread: Place the torn pieces of day-old bread in a separate bowl and drizzle with a bit of water. Allow the bread to soak for a few minutes until softened.

  3. Blend the Ingredients: In a blender or food processor, combine the soaked bread, olive oil, and red wine vinegar. Blend until the mixture forms a smooth paste.

  4. Combine the Ingredients: Add the blended bread mixture to the bowl of chopped vegetables. Stir to combine, ensuring that the ingredients are well mixed.

  5. Season the Gazpacho: Season the gazpacho with salt and freshly ground black pepper, adjusting to taste as needed.

  6. Chill the Gazpacho: Cover the bowl of gazpacho and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, allowing the flavors to meld and the soup to chill thoroughly.

  7. Serve the Gazpacho: When ready to serve, ladle the chilled gazpacho into bowls. If desired, add a few ice cubes to each bowl to keep the soup extra cold.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a smoother texture, strain the gazpacho through a fine-mesh sieve before chilling.
  • Garnish each serving with a drizzle of olive oil, a sprinkle of diced cucumber, or a few croutons for added crunch.
  • Experiment with different types of bread, such as sourdough or whole-grain, to add unique flavors to your gazpacho.

Why We Love Spanish Gazpacho:

Spanish Gazpacho is a celebration of fresh, seasonal ingredients and the vibrant flavors of the Mediterranean. This chilled soup is not only a delicious way to enjoy the bounty of summer produce but also a versatile dish that can be customized to suit your taste preferences. Whether served as a light appetizer, a refreshing midday snack, or a cooling dinner option, gazpacho is a true crowd-pleaser that embodies the essence of Spanish culinary tradition.

So, the next time you find yourself with an abundance of ripe tomatoes and a craving for something cool and invigorating, consider whipping up a batch of this delightful Spanish Gazpacho. With its balance of sweet, tangy, and savory notes, it’s sure to become a beloved addition to your summer recipe repertoire.

Now, go ahead and embrace the flavors of Spain with this simple, yet sensational, gazpacho recipe!
