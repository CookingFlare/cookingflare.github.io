Whip Up a Quick and Easy Paleo-Friendly Stir-Fry


As the days get shorter and the nights get cooler, our taste buds start to crave warm, comforting, and nourishing meals. One of the easiest and most satisfying ways to satisfy this craving is by whipping up a quick and easy paleo-friendly stir-fry. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a delicious and healthy stir-fry that’s perfect for any time of the year.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, carrots, broccoli, and mushrooms)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken breast, diced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (make sure it’s paleo-friendly)
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

Start by preparing your ingredients. Slice the onion and mince the garlic. Cut the mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces. Dice the cooked chicken breast into small cubes. Make sure all the ingredients are ready to go before you start cooking.

Step 2: Heat the Coconut Oil

Heat the coconut oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, add the sliced onion and cook for about 3-4 minutes, or until it starts to soften.

Step 3: Add the Garlic and Ginger

Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the skillet and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.

Step 4: Add the Vegetables and Chicken

Add the mixed vegetables and cooked chicken breast to the skillet. Stir-fry everything together for about 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp and the chicken is fully cooked.

Step 5: Add the Soy Sauce and Honey

In a small bowl, whisk together the paleo-friendly soy sauce and honey until well combined. Pour the sauce over the stir-fry and stir-fry everything together for another minute, or until the sauce is fully incorporated.

Step 6: Season and Serve

Season the stir-fry with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves, if desired. Serve the stir-fry hot over a bed of cauliflower rice or with a side of roasted vegetables.

Tips and Variations

  • Use any combination of your favorite vegetables and protein sources to make the stir-fry your own.
  • Add some heat to the stir-fry by incorporating red pepper flakes or sriracha sauce.
  • Experiment with different seasonings, such as curry powder or cumin, to add depth and complexity to the dish.
  • Make the stir-fry more substantial by serving it with a side of roasted sweet potatoes or brown rice.

Conclusion

In just a few simple steps, you can whip up a quick and easy paleo-friendly stir-fry that’s perfect for any time of the year. With its combination of protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates, this dish is sure to satisfy your cravings and nourish your body. So go ahead, get creative, and enjoy the delicious and healthy benefits of a paleo-friendly stir-fry!
