Comforting Slow Cooker Chicken and Mushroom Stroganoff Recipe


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can compete with a rich and creamy chicken stroganoff. This Slow Cooker Chicken and Mushroom Stroganoff is not only mouthwateringly delicious but also incredibly easy to prepare. Just toss the ingredients in your slow cooker, set it, and let it work its magic. By the time dinner rolls around, you’ll have a warm, hearty meal that’s perfect for any night of the week.

Why Slow Cooker Chicken Stroganoff?

The beauty of using a slow cooker lies in its ability to meld flavors beautifully over time. The chicken becomes tender, the mushrooms add an earthy depth, and the creamy sauce envelopes everything in a comforting embrace. This dish is perfect for busy families, meal prep enthusiasts, or anyone who simply wants to enjoy a hassle-free dinner.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this comforting dish, gather the following ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts: Opt for thighs if you prefer a richer flavor.
  • 8 ounces of mushrooms: Use button mushrooms, cremini, or a mix for added flavor.
  • 1 medium onion, diced: This adds a nice sweetness to the dish.
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced: Fresh garlic is a must for that aromatic touch.
  • 1 cup chicken broth: Use low-sodium broth to control the saltiness.
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce: This adds depth and umami flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard: A subtle tanginess that brightens the dish.
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme: Fresh thyme works too, if available.
  • Salt and pepper, to taste: Essential for enhancing the flavors.
  • 1 cup sour cream: This is the key to the creamy sauce.
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour: Helps thicken the sauce.
  • Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish: Adds a pop of color and freshness.
  • Egg noodles or rice, for serving: These will soak up the delicious sauce.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Chicken and Vegetables

Start by washing and trimming the chicken breasts. Cut them into bite-sized pieces to ensure even cooking. Next, slice your mushrooms and dice the onion. Mince the garlic, and set all the ingredients aside.

2. Layer the Ingredients in the Slow Cooker

In your slow cooker, layer the chicken pieces at the bottom. Follow with the diced onion, sliced mushrooms, and minced garlic. This layering helps the flavors to build as they cook. Pour in the chicken broth, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, and sprinkle the thyme on top. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

3. Set and Forget

Cover the slow cooker and set it to cook on low for 6 to 8 hours or on high for 3 to 4 hours. Cooking on low is recommended for the best flavor and texture, allowing the chicken to become tender and the mushrooms to release their juices.

4. Thicken the Sauce

About 30 minutes before serving, whisk the sour cream and flour together in a bowl until smooth. Remove some of the cooking liquid from the slow cooker and mix it into the sour cream mixture to temper it. This step helps to prevent curdling when you add it back into the slow cooker. Stir the tempered sour cream mixture back into the slow cooker, and cover again. Allow it to cook for an additional 30 minutes on low until the sauce is thickened and creamy.

5. Serve

Once the stroganoff is ready, taste and adjust the seasoning if necessary. Serve it over a bed of egg noodles or rice to soak up the luscious sauce. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of color and freshness.

Tips for the Perfect Chicken Stroganoff

  • Choose the Right Chicken: While chicken breasts are lean and cook quickly, chicken thighs provide more flavor and stay tender during slow cooking.
  • Don’t Skip the Searing: For added depth, consider searing the chicken and mushrooms in a skillet before adding them to the slow cooker. This step enhances flavor but is entirely optional.
  • Experiment with Mushrooms: Different types of mushrooms can change the flavor profile. Try shiitake, portobello, or oyster mushrooms for a unique twist.
  • Make it Gluten-Free: Substitute the all-purpose flour with cornstarch or a gluten-free flour blend to thicken the sauce.
  • Add Vegetables: To boost nutrition, consider adding carrots or peas for color and flavor in the last hour of cooking.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (which is unlikely given how delicious this dish is!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply warm it on the stove over low heat or in the microwave until heated through. The stroganoff may thicken as it cools; you can add a splash of chicken broth to loosen it back up.

Final Thoughts

Comforting and satisfying, this Slow Cooker Chicken and Mushroom Stroganoff is the perfect dish for busy weeknights or lazy weekends. The best part is that it requires minimal effort for maximum flavor, making it a staple in any home cook’s repertoire. So, pull out that slow cooker, gather your ingredients, and indulge in a bowl of creamy goodness that’s sure to warm your heart and fill your stomach. Enjoy!
