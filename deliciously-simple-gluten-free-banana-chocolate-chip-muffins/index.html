Deliciously Simple: Gluten-Free Banana Chocolate Chip Muffins


Are you a fan of baked goods but need to stick to a gluten-free diet? Look no further! These Deliciously Simple Gluten-Free Banana Chocolate Chip Muffins are the perfect treat for those with dietary restrictions or anyone looking to enjoy a tasty and healthy snack. Packed with the goodness of ripe bananas and rich chocolate chips, these muffins are sure to satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 3/4 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the mashed bananas, melted coconut oil, honey or maple syrup, eggs, milk, and vanilla extract. Mix well until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense muffins.

  4. Gently fold in the chocolate chips, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

  5. Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about 3/4 full. If desired, sprinkle a few additional chocolate chips on top of each muffin for an extra indulgence.

  6. Bake for 15-18 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean. Remember that baking times may vary depending on your oven, so keep a close eye on the muffins towards the end of the baking time.

  7. Once baked, remove the muffins from the oven and let them cool in the tin for a few minutes. Then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.

  8. Serve these Deliciously Simple Gluten-Free Banana Chocolate Chip Muffins warm or at room temperature. They are perfect for breakfast, a quick snack, or even as a dessert.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, add a teaspoon of cinnamon or nutmeg to the batter.
  • Feel free to substitute the chocolate chips with nuts, dried fruits, or a combination of both for added texture and taste.
  • If you don’t have gluten-free all-purpose flour, you can use a blend of gluten-free flours such as almond flour, rice flour, and tapioca flour in equal proportions.
  • These muffins can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. Alternatively, you can freeze them for later enjoyment.

Enjoy these Deliciously Simple Gluten-Free Banana Chocolate Chip Muffins as a guilt-free indulgence. They are not only gluten-free but also incredibly moist, flavorful, and easy to make. Whether you have dietary restrictions or simply love a good muffin, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, grab your apron and get baking!
