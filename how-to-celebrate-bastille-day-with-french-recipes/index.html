How to Celebrate Bastille Day with French Recipes


July 14th is a significant date in the French calendar, marking the anniversary of the Storming of the Bastille, a pivotal event that signified the beginning of the French Revolution. It was on this day in 1789 that French citizens stormed the infamous Bastille prison, symbolizing their fight for liberté, égalité, fraternité, and the end of absolute monarchy. As a tribute to this momentous occasion, we're going to dive into the rich culinary traditions of France and explore some delectable French recipes to help you celebrate Bastille Day with flair. Recipe 1: Coq au Vin One of the quintessential French dishes is Coq au Vin, a classic braised chicken in red wine that's sure to transport your taste buds to the rolling hills of Provence. This hearty dish is a staple of French cuisine and is often served with boiled potatoes, carrots, and onions. Ingredients:

1 chicken, cut into 8 pieces 2 tablespoons of butter 1 onion, diced 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 cup of red wine 1 cup of chicken broth 1 tablespoon of tomato paste 1 teaspoon of dried thyme Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Heat butter in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the chicken pieces until golden brown, then set aside. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, then add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the red wine, chicken broth, tomato paste, and thyme. Stir to combine, then return the chicken to the pot. Cover and simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.

Recipe 2: Bouillabaisse The iconic fish stew from Marseille, Bouillabaisse is a testament to the rich maritime heritage of southern France. This flavorful fish stew is typically served with croutons and a dollop of rouille, a zesty Provençal mayonnaise-like condiment. Ingredients:

2 tablespoons of olive oil 1 onion, diced 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 pound of fish of your choice (such as shrimp, mussels, and cod) 1 cup of fish broth 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh parsley 1 tablespoon of lemon juice 2 slices of bread, cubed 1/4 cup of rouille (see below for recipe)

Instructions:

Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened, then add the garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the fish, fish broth, parsley, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes or until the fish is cooked through. Serve the fish stew over croutons, topped with a dollop of rouille.

Recipe 3: Tarte Tatin A classic French dessert, Tarte Tatin is a caramelized apple tart that will leave you swooning. This iconic dessert is a staple of French patisseries and is sure to impress your loved ones. Ingredients:

3-4 apples, peeled and halved 2 tablespoons of butter 1 tablespoon of granulated sugar 1 sheet of puff pastry 1 egg, beaten 1 tablespoon of vanilla extract

Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface to a thickness of 1/8 inch. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sliced apples and cook until caramelized, stirring occasionally. Roll the puff pastry over a 9-inch tart pan and press into the bottom and sides. Fill the pastry shell with the caramelized apples, then brush the edges with the beaten egg. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown. Allow to cool before serving.

Rouille Recipe Ingredients:

1/4 cup of mayonnaise 1 tablespoon of lemon juice 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard 1/4 cup of chopped fresh tarragon Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

Mix all ingredients together in a small bowl until well combined. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.

As the French would say, "Bon appétit!" Savor the flavors of France with these delectable recipes that will transport you to the sun-kissed hills of Provence and the rich cultural heritage of France. Whether you're a foodie or a Francophile, these classic French dishes are sure to delight. Happy Bastille Day! ** Bonus Tip:** Pair these recipes with some delightful wine recommendations, such as a light and crisp white wine from the Loire Valley or a rich and fruity red wine from the Bordeaux region. Conclusion: Celebrating Bastille Day is a chance to indulge in the rich culinary traditions of France, a country renowned for its exquisite cuisine, elaborate pastries, and fine wines. These recipes showcase the diversity and elegance of French cuisine, with its emphasis on local ingredients, simplicity, and flavor. Whether you're a seasoned cook or a novice in the kitchen, these French recipes are sure to impress your loved ones and transport you to the picturesque streets of France. So, don your beret and apron, and get ready to cook up a storm in the kitchen, just like the French would!
