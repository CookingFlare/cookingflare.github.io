DIY Vegan Mango Ice Cream with Coconut Milk


There’s something about mango that immediately transports you to a tropical paradise—its bright, sunny flavor coupled with a luscious texture demands to be savored. Now, combine that with the rich creaminess of coconut milk, and you’ve got a dairy-free dessert that’s both indulgent and refreshing: vegan mango ice cream. This homemade delight is perfect for warm days when you crave something sweet without the heaviness of traditional ice cream.

In this post, you will discover how to create a smooth, creamy vegan mango ice cream using simple ingredients, with no ice cream maker required. We’ll dive into the step-by-step process, share tips for the best results, and explore subtle variations that let you customize your frozen treat.

Why Make Your Own Vegan Mango Ice Cream?

While store-bought vegan ice creams have made vast leaps in flavor and texture, making your own at home offers unique benefits:

  • Control over ingredients: No hidden stabilizers, emulsifiers, or artificial flavorings.
  • Freshness: You get to use fresh or frozen mangoes, coconut milk, and natural sweeteners.
  • Customization: Easily adjust sweetness, texture, and add-ins to suit your preferences.
  • Cost-effective: Premium vegan ice creams can be pricey; making your own is budget-friendly.
  • Fun and satisfying: Nothing beats crafting a luscious dessert from scratch.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the heart of this recipe, simplicity reigns. Here’s what you’ll need for about 4 generous servings:

  • 3 ripe mangoes (approximately 2 cups of flesh, peeled and diced)
    Ripe mangoes are essential for natural sweetness and vibrant color.

  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
    The fat content in coconut milk gives the ice cream that indulgent creaminess.

  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or agave nectar
    Adjust based on your mango’s sweetness and personal preference.

  • 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
    Brightens the flavor and balances the richness.

  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
    Adds a delicate aroma complementing the tropical flavors.

  • Pinch of salt
    Enhances flavor depth.

Optional add-ins for texture:

  • Toasted shredded coconut
  • Chopped pistachios
  • Tiny pieces of fresh mango or other tropical fruits

Step-by-Step Guide to Creamy Vegan Mango Ice Cream

1. Prepare Your Mango Puree

Begin by peeling and dicing the mango flesh. If you’re using frozen mango chunks, ensure they’re slightly thawed but still cold.

Place the mango in a high-speed blender or food processor and pulse until smooth and creamy—a vibrant orange-yellow puree with no large chunks.

2. Add Coconut Milk and Sweetener

Add the canned coconut milk to the mango puree. Make sure to use the full-fat version, and give the can a gentle shake before opening to mix the cream and water.

Add the maple syrup or agave nectar according to how sweet your mangoes are. Adjust sweetness after tasting.

3. Incorporate Flavor Enhancers

Add fresh lime juice, vanilla extract (if using), and a pinch of salt. The lime juice isn’t just for zest; it brightens the entire profile, balancing the sweetness and creaminess.

Blend again until all the ingredients meld into a uniform mixture that’s luscious and shiny.

4. Chill the Mixture

Pour the mixture into an airtight container and chill in the refrigerator for an hour or two. This step isn’t required but helps the ice cream firm up more evenly during freezing.

5. Freeze with or without an Ice Cream Maker

  • No ice cream maker method: Freeze the mixture in a container, stirring vigorously every 30 minutes for about 3 hours. This breaks the ice crystals and improves creaminess.
  • With an ice cream maker: Simply pour the chilled mixture into the ice cream maker and follow the manufacturer’s instructions. Typically, it takes about 20-30 minutes to reach a soft-serve consistency.

6. Final Freeze

If you prefer firmer ice cream, transfer to a freezer-safe container and freeze for an additional 2–3 hours to harden. Before serving, let it sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes for easy scooping.

Tips for the Best Vegan Mango Ice Cream

Pick Ripe Mangoes!

Sweet, fragrant, and juicy mangoes are the foundation of this recipe. Choose varieties like Ataulfo, Alphonso, or Kent for maximum flavor. When in season, fresh mangoes yield the best texture and taste, but quality frozen mango chunks work perfectly as well.

Use Full-Fat Coconut Milk

The fat content is crucial for a creamy texture. Avoid light or low-fat coconut milk, as this leads to icy textures and less richness.

Sweetness is Adjustable

Mango sweetness varies a lot—taste the mixture before freezing and add more maple syrup or agave if needed. You can even consider a couple of dates blended into the mixture for natural sweetness without refinement.

Experiment with Coconut Cream

If you want an extra indulgent touch, replace half the coconut milk with coconut cream. It thickens the ice cream and adds a velvety mouthfeel.

Mind the Salt

A little salt cuts through the sweetness and coco’s richness, making the flavors pop. A pinch is perfect—don’t skip!

Subtle Variations and Serving Suggestions

Tropical Twists

  • Mint and lime: Add crushed fresh mint leaves and a hint of lime zest to the mixture for a refreshing citrus-herb swirl.
  • Chili kick: Sprinkle a small amount of chili powder into the mixture for a subtle heat contrast to the sweet mango.
  • Ginger infusion: Blend in a teaspoon of freshly grated ginger for a warm, spicy undertone.

Nutty Crunch

Top your vegan mango ice cream with toasted coconut flakes, chopped macadamia nuts, or crushed pistachios to add texture and nutty flavors.

Coconut and Mango Sorbet Version

Freeze mango puree swirled with coconut water and a splash of lime juice for a lighter sorbet that’s also wonderfully refreshing.

Mango Coconut Ice Cream Parfait

Layer the homemade vegan mango ice cream with granola, fresh berries, and coconut yogurt for an instant tropical parfait dessert.

Storing and Shelf Life

Store your vegan mango ice cream in an airtight, freezer-safe container to prevent ice crystals and freezer burn. It’s best enjoyed within two weeks for optimal flavor and texture. Thaw for a few minutes at room temperature before scooping.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Ice Crystals

If you find your ice cream too icy, ensure you are:

  • Using full-fat coconut milk or cream for richness.
  • Stirring/stirring the mixture during freezing if not using an ice cream machine.
  • Chilling the mixture before freezing to reduce ice crystal formation.

Too Sweet or Not Sweet Enough

Mango sweetness and tartness vary by season. Always taste and adjust your sweetener before freezing. Remember that freezing dulls sweetness slightly.

Hard to Scoop

Let your ice cream thaw for 5 to 10 minutes at room temperature before scooping. Using a warm spoon or ice cream scoop aids in smooth serving.

Final Thoughts

This vegan mango ice cream with coconut milk recipe is your gateway to creating a luscious and natural tropical delight at home. Its creamy, dreamy texture and bright mango flavor make it a perfect dessert for summer parties, after-dinner treats, or anytime you crave a refreshing, guilt-free frozen indulgence.

Feel free to experiment with add-ins and toppings to make it uniquely yours. Once you try this recipe, you might find it your go-to that brings sunshine on even the cloudiest of days!

Ready to Dive In?

Gather your ripe mangoes, dust off that blender, and treat yourself to this expertly crafted vegan mango ice cream today. Your taste buds—and your body—will thank you.
