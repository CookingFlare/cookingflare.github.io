Tombstone Treats: Spoon Cookies For Halloween


🎃Spooky season is upon us once again, and there’s no better way to get into the spirit than by baking up a batch of delicious Tombstone Treats: Spoon Cookies for Halloween. 🧙‍♂️ Put your witch hat on and let’s dive into the magical world of these eerie yet delightful cookies that will make your night of ghouls and goblins unforgettable. 🍂

📃 Ingredients:

  1. 2 ¼ cups of all-purpose flour
  2. 1 teaspoon baking powder
  3. 1/4 teaspoon salt
  4. 1 cup of unsalted butter (room temperature)
  5. 1 cup of sugar
  6. 1 large egg
  7. 1 teaspoon of pure vanilla extract
  8. A generous handful of chocolate chips
  9. 2 tablespoons of coarse sugar for sprinkling

📝 Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Pan

Find your biggest non-stick skillet or an iron griddle and lightly grease it with a little butter. This will ensure your cookies have that perfect spoon-like shape. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C) while you’re at it!

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside for the moment.

Step 3: Cream the Butter

Take your butter and sugar and cream them together until they’re light and fluffy. You’ll know you’ve done it right when the mixture has more than doubled in size. This will make for extra soft and fluffy cookies!

Step 4: Add Egg and Vanilla

Now, add the large egg and vanilla extract into the mixture and stir in until just combined.

Step 5: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Slowly incorporate your dry mixture into the wet ingredients. Be gentle, and make sure you only mix until no dry ingredients remain. Overmixing can lead to tough cookies and we want these babies to be as soft as they are spooky!

Step 6: Stir In Chocolate Chips

Sprinkle in your delicious chocolate chips and fold them into the cookie dough. We recommend using a generous handful to make sure the chocolate chunks are evenly spread throughout your Tombstone Treats.

Step 7: Spoon onto the Pan

Using an ice cream scoop or two spoons, drop mounds of dough onto your preheated skillet or griddle (about 10-12 per pan). Then, sprinkle a little bit of coarse sugar on top of each cookie for that extra crunchy and sweet touch.

Step 8: Bake Those Cookies!

Bake the cookies in a preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes or until the bottoms are golden brown. This may seem counter-intuitive, but trust us–those golden bottoms give the cookies their desired texture and taste.

Step 9: Let Them Cool

Remove the cookies from the skillet or griddle while they’re still slightly warm and transfer them to a cooling rack. Let them cool down completely, as this will make them more delicious.

Step 10: Serve Up Your Tombstone Treats

Once the cookies have cooled down, it’s time to serve them up and indulge in your homemade Tombstone Treats! 🕯️ Serve your cookies to your guests on a spooky themed plate or arrange them around a skeleton figurine for a gory yet delightful presentation.

🍪 The Spooky Sewing: Decorating Tips

If you want to up the spook-factor, why not get creative with decorations using black frosting or make your own edible glitter using sanding sugar? Get the little ones involved, they’re sure to love decorating and contemplating on which face to adorn their tombstone cookies with. 🎨

🎃Happy Halloween: Tombstone Treats!
