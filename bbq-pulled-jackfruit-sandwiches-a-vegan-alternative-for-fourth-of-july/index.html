BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches: A Vegan Alternative for Fourth of July


As Fourth of July approaches, many people are excited about firing up their grills and enjoying some delicious barbecue. But what if you’re following a vegan diet or simply looking for a plant-based alternative? Look no further than BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches!

Introduction

Jackfruit, a tropical fruit native to Asia, has gained popularity in recent years as a meat substitute due to its unique texture and ability to absorb flavors. When cooked and shredded, jackfruit resembles pulled pork, making it a perfect choice for a vegan BBQ sandwich. In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a mouthwatering BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwich that will impress even the most die-hard meat lovers at your Fourth of July gathering.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cans of young green jackfruit in brine or water
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup of BBQ sauce (choose a vegan-friendly brand)
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon of maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 burger buns
  • Optional toppings: coleslaw, sliced pickles, and vegan cheese

Instructions

  1. Start by draining and rinsing the canned jackfruit. Use your hands or a fork to shred the jackfruit into smaller pieces, resembling pulled pork.

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, sautéing until translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the shredded jackfruit to the skillet and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing it to brown slightly.

  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the BBQ sauce, vegetable broth, maple syrup, apple cider vinegar, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and pepper.

  5. Pour the BBQ sauce mixture over the jackfruit in the skillet, stirring well to coat all the pieces. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the jackfruit to absorb the sauce.

  6. While the jackfruit is simmering, lightly toast the burger buns in a toaster or on a grill.

  7. Once the jackfruit is tender and fully infused with BBQ flavors, remove it from the heat.

  8. Assemble your BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches by placing a generous amount of the jackfruit mixture on the bottom half of each burger bun. Top with your favorite toppings such as coleslaw, sliced pickles, and vegan cheese.

  9. Serve the sandwiches alongside a side of vegan potato salad or corn on the cob for a complete Fourth of July meal.

Conclusion

With BBQ Pulled Jackfruit Sandwiches, you can enjoy a vegan alternative to traditional pulled pork this Fourth of July. The tender and flavorful jackfruit, combined with the smoky BBQ sauce, will satisfy your cravings and impress your friends and family. So fire up the grill, gather your loved ones, and celebrate Independence Day with this delicious and cruelty-free option. Happy Fourth of July!
