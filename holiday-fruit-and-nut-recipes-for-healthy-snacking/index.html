Holiday Fruit and Nut Recipes for Healthy Snacking


As the holiday season approaches, many of us are eager to indulge in sweet treats and festive flavors. However, traditional holiday foods can be high in sugar, calories, and unhealthy fats, which can be a concern for those looking to maintain a healthy diet. This season, why not try some delicious and nutritious holiday fruit and nut recipes that will satisfy your cravings while keeping your snacks on track? Dried Fruit and Nut Mixes One easy and convenient way to enjoy a healthy holiday snack is by making a dried fruit and nut mix. This can be customized to suit your taste preferences and dietary needs. Begin by combining a variety of dried fruits such as cranberries, apricots, and apples. Add some crunchy elements like almonds, cashews, and pistachios. Finally, add in some tastes of cinnamon and nutmeg to evoke the warmth of the holiday season. Spiced Pear and Apple Oat Crumble Who says crumbles are just for the summer months? This spiced pear and apple oat crumble is a perfect holiday treat that can be enjoyed warm or cold. Core several pears and apples, then sprinkle them with sugar and spices such as cinnamon, nutmeg, and allspice. Mix together some rolled oats, brown sugar, and chopped nuts, then sprinkle the mixture over the fruit. Bake until the topping is golden and the fruit is tender. Cranberry and Walnut Energy Balls Maintaining a healthy diet during the holiday season can be a challenge, especially with parties and gatherings aplenty. This simple recipe for cranberry and walnut energy balls saves the day. Combine rolled oats, chopped nuts, and dried cranberries in a bowl. Mix together some creamy peanut butter and honey until smooth, then stir it into the dry ingredients until everything is well combined. Roll the mixture into small balls and chill until firm. Chocolate-Dipped Orange and Almond Biscotti When it comes to holiday cookies, there are endless possibilities. These chocolate-dipped orange and almond biscotti take the classic Italian biscotti to new heights by incorporating the warm flavors of orange and the crunch of almonds. To make, combine flour, sugar, and chopped almonds in a bowl, then stir in some dried orange zest and an egg. Roll the mixture into a log, slice into individual biscotti, and bake until golden. Dip the biscotti in melted chocolate and enjoy as a sweet holiday treat. Pomegranate and Pistachio Granola Bites Starting your day with a nutritious and filling breakfast can set you up for a healthy day ahead. These pomegranate and pistachio granola bites are a new take on traditional oatmeal. Mix together some rolled oats, chopped nuts, and pomegranate seeds, then bind the mixture together with some honey and peanut butter. Form the mixture into bite-sized balls and refrigerate until firm. As a finishing touch, sprinkle some additional pomegranate seeds and chopped pistachios over the top. Tips for Storing and Preserving Holiday Fruits Some holiday fruits such as cranberries and persimmons can be paired with nuts to create a delicious snack. However, storing and preserving these fruits requires a bit of planning. An airtight container in the refrigerator helps to keep the fruits fresh for a longer period. When it comes to persimmons, a slow cooker can be a convenient way to ripen them at home. It is essential to wash and dry the nuts before using them to avoid contamination. Mom's Secret for Keeping Nuts Fresh A mom once shared her secret for keeping nuts fresh by storing them in an airtight container and using a silica gel pack. This simple trick is a lifesaver during the holiday season. The salt or cinnamon in the silicone pack absorbs moisture and does not allow the nuts to become stale or rancid, making it safe to snack or use in recipes during the long months. As the holiday season is fast approaching, try to indulge in these varied fruit and nuts recipes. Enjoy the sweet flavors, stirring in some brandy and cranberry juice to offer an extra depth to them. Invite friends, and break open some appetizers as they wait to enjoy homemade holiday treats. Begin your healthy holiday meal with colorful using vegetables and herbs grown in your own garden. Taking time to present every platter for your respected guests adds a wonderful sign of solidarity and joyfulness for each gathering.
