Grilled Stuffed Peppers: A Healthy BBQ Option


Welcome to another edition of our cooking blog series where we share delightful, unique, and healthy recipes for you to enjoy! Today, we are delving into a vibrant and tasty option that takes your delightful BBQ meals to another level - Grilled Stuffed Peppers.

Stuffed peppers are a popular and versatile dish enjoyed across the globe, and the idea is simple - peppers stuffed with savory goodness and cooked until perfectly tender. The concept of stuffed peppers isn’t new to the BBQ circuit, but with our special recipe, your grilling game is about to level up!

Let’s dive into a scrumptious and Healthy Grilled Stuffed Peppers Recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 6 large bell peppers
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt and black pepper
  • 1 pound ground turkey or lean beef
  • 1 cup uncooked quinoa
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup canned black beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup salsa

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your grill to medium heat.

  2. Carefully cut off the tops of the bell peppers, and remove the seeds and membrane.

  3. Drizzle the inside of each pepper with some olive oil and season with salt and pepper, ensuring every pepper is coated evenly.

  4. Place the peppers, cut-side down, onto the preheated grill. Allow them to cook for about 10 minutes or until the skins begin to char and they are soft but still firm enough to hold their shape.

  5. Remove the peppers from the grill and transfer them to a platter or cutting board. Cover the peppers loosely with a foil to continue steaming, which will help the filling adhere better. Allow them to cool for at least 10 minutes.

  6. While the peppers are resting, cook the filling. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook the ground turkey or beef in some olive oil until browned and cooked through. Add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened.

  7. Stir in the quinoa, broth, black beans, corn, cilantro, chili powder, and cumin. Allow the mixture to simmer for about 10 minutes or until the quinoa has absorbed the broth.

  8. Stuff each pepper with the filling. To prevent spillovers, you can line a baking sheet with foil and place the peppers on the sheet.

  9. Preheat your oven to broil. Place the stuffed peppers under the broiler for 4-5 minutes to brown the tops slightly.

  10. Remove from the oven, let cool for a few minutes, and serve immediately.

And that’s it! Enjoy your savory, scrumptious, protein-packed Grilled Stuffed Peppers that are perfect for your summer barbecues.
