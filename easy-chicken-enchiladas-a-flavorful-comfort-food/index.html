Easy Chicken Enchiladas: A Flavorful Comfort Food


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the warm, savory goodness of chicken enchiladas. This classic Mexican dish is not only delicious but also incredibly versatile, making it a favorite in households across the globe. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a dish to impress guests, easy chicken enchiladas are the perfect solution. In this blog post, we will explore the origins of enchiladas, the ingredients you’ll need, and a step-by-step guide to creating this mouthwatering meal.

The Origins of Enchiladas

Enchiladas have a rich history that dates back to ancient Mexico, where the Aztecs would eat rolled tortillas filled with various ingredients. The term “enchilada” comes from the Spanish word “enchilar,” which means to add chili. Traditionally, enchiladas are made with corn tortillas filled with meat, cheese, or beans, and then topped with a flavorful sauce. Over the years, this dish has evolved, and today, you can find countless variations, including vegetarian and gluten-free options.

Why Chicken Enchiladas?

Chicken enchiladas are a popular choice for many reasons. First and foremost, chicken is a lean protein that cooks quickly and pairs well with a variety of flavors. Additionally, enchiladas are a great way to use up leftover chicken, making them a practical option for busy weeknights. The combination of tender chicken, zesty sauce, and melted cheese creates a comforting dish that is sure to please even the pickiest eaters.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make easy chicken enchiladas, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Enchiladas:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken: Shredded or diced, you can use rotisserie chicken for convenience.
  • 8-10 corn tortillas: You can also use flour tortillas if you prefer.
  • 1 cup shredded cheese: Cheddar, Monterey Jack, or a blend works well.
  • 1 cup enchilada sauce: Store-bought or homemade.
  • 1/2 cup diced onion: Yellow or white onions add a nice flavor.
  • 1/2 cup black beans: Optional, for added texture and nutrition.
  • 1/2 cup corn: Optional, for sweetness and crunch.
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil: For sautéing the onions.

For the Toppings:

  • Sour cream: A dollop on top adds creaminess.
  • Chopped cilantro: For a fresh garnish.
  • Sliced jalapeños: For those who like a little heat.
  • Avocado: Sliced or diced, for a creamy texture.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Easy Chicken Enchiladas

Step 1: Prepare the Filling

  1. Sauté the Onions: In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onions and sauté until they are translucent, about 3-4 minutes.

  2. Mix the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine the cooked chicken, sautéed onions, black beans, corn, and half of the shredded cheese. Mix well to ensure all ingredients are evenly distributed.

Step 2: Assemble the Enchiladas

  1. Warm the Tortillas: To make them pliable, warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet for about 30 seconds on each side. Alternatively, you can wrap them in a damp paper towel and microwave them for about 30 seconds.

  2. Fill the Tortillas: Place a generous amount of the chicken mixture in the center of each tortilla. Roll them up tightly and place them seam-side down in a greased baking dish.

Step 3: Add the Sauce and Cheese

  1. Pour the Enchilada Sauce: Once all the enchiladas are in the baking dish, pour the enchilada sauce evenly over the top. Make sure to cover all the tortillas to prevent them from drying out during baking.

  2. Top with Cheese: Sprinkle the remaining shredded cheese over the sauce, ensuring an even layer for that gooey, melted goodness.

Step 4: Bake the Enchiladas

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 20 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

  1. Garnish: Once out of the oven, let the enchiladas cool for a few minutes. Serve them hot, garnished with sour cream, chopped cilantro, sliced jalapeños, and avocado.

  2. Pairing Suggestions: Chicken enchiladas pair wonderfully with a side of Mexican rice, refried beans, or a fresh garden salad.
