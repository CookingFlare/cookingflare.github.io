Flavorful Breakfast Sausage Gravy Recipes for a Southern Start


As the morning sun rises over the rolling hills of the South, the aroma of sizzling sausage and biscuits wafts through the air, signaling the start of a new day. There’s something special about a Southern breakfast, with its rich tradition of hearty, comforting dishes that warm the soul. And at the heart of it all is the humble sausage gravy. In this post, we’ll explore the art of making flavorful breakfast sausage gravy recipes that will set your taste buds singing.

Classic Sausage Gravy Recipe

For a true Southern experience, start with a classic sausage gravy recipe. This one’s a staple for a reason: it’s easy to make, packed with flavor, and pairs perfectly with flaky biscuits or crispy hash browns.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage (such as Jimmy Dean or homemade)
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter or oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sausage in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks, until browned and fully cooked.
  2. Remove the sausage from the skillet with a slotted spoon and set it aside on a plate. Leave the drippings in the skillet.
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add the flour to the skillet, stirring to combine with the drippings. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the mixture forms a smooth paste.
  4. Gradually add the chicken broth to the skillet, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the cooked sausage back into the gravy and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the sausage gravy over biscuits, hash browns, or eggs for a hearty breakfast that’ll keep you going all day.

Spicy Sausage Gravy Recipe

For a kick of heat in your morning routine, try this spicy sausage gravy recipe. It’s perfect for those who like a little excitement in their breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound spicy breakfast sausage (such as andouille or chorizo)
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter or oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1-2 teaspoons hot sauce (such as Tabasco or Frank’s RedHot)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sausage in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks, until browned and fully cooked.
  2. Remove the sausage from the skillet with a slotted spoon and set it aside on a plate. Leave the drippings in the skillet.
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add the flour to the skillet, stirring to combine with the drippings. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the mixture forms a smooth paste.
  4. Gradually add the chicken broth to the skillet, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the cooked sausage back into the gravy and stir to combine. Add the hot sauce and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the spicy sausage gravy over biscuits, hash browns, or eggs for a breakfast that’ll put some hair on your chest.

Mushroom and Onion Sausage Gravy Recipe

For a savory twist on the classic recipe, try adding some sautéed mushrooms and onions to the mix. This recipe is perfect for those who love a hearty, comforting breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound breakfast sausage (such as Jimmy Dean or homemade)
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons butter or oil
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (such as button or cremini)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sausage in a large skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks, until browned and fully cooked.
  2. Remove the sausage from the skillet with a slotted spoon and set it aside on a plate. Leave the drippings in the skillet.
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add the butter or oil to the skillet. Add the mushrooms and onion and cook until they’re softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the flour to the skillet and stir to combine with the mushroom mixture. Cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the mixture forms a smooth paste.
  5. Gradually add the chicken broth to the skillet, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add the cooked sausage back into the gravy and stir to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the mushroom and onion sausage gravy over biscuits, hash browns, or eggs for a breakfast that’s sure to please even the pickiest of eaters.

Whether you’re a Southerner at heart or just looking for a delicious breakfast recipe, these flavorful sausage gravy recipes are sure to become a staple in your kitchen. So go ahead, get creative, and start your day off right with a hearty, comforting bowl of sausage gravy and biscuits.
