Herbaceous Boulangère Potatoes: A French Side Dish


If you’re looking to add a touch of elegance to your dinner table, look no further than Herbaceous Boulangère Potatoes. This French side dish is a delightful combination of thinly sliced potatoes, aromatic herbs, and a rich, flavorful broth. It’s the perfect accompaniment to any main course, whether you’re serving roasted chicken, grilled steak, or even a vegetarian dish.

Ingredients

To make Herbaceous Boulangère Potatoes, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh rosemary leaves
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 cups of vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and minced garlic, and sauté until the onions are translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the thinly sliced potatoes to the skillet, along with the thyme, rosemary, parsley, salt, and pepper. Stir gently to combine all the ingredients.

  4. Transfer the potato mixture to an oven-safe baking dish, spreading it out evenly.

  5. Pour the vegetable or chicken broth over the potatoes, making sure to cover them completely. The broth will infuse the potatoes with incredible flavor as they bake.

  6. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for approximately 1 hour, or until the potatoes are tender and golden brown on top.

  7. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the top of the potatoes is crispy and golden.

  8. Once the Herbaceous Boulangère Potatoes are done, remove them from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

  9. Serve the potatoes as a side dish alongside your favorite main course. They pair exceptionally well with roasted chicken, grilled steak, or even a hearty vegetable stew.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra indulgent twist, sprinkle some grated cheese, such as Gruyère or Parmesan, over the potatoes before baking. The cheese will melt and create a deliciously gooey crust.

  • If you prefer a vegetarian version of this dish, simply substitute the chicken broth with vegetable broth.

  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste. Fresh thyme, rosemary, and parsley are classic choices, but you can also try adding a pinch of dried oregano or a sprinkle of paprika for a bolder flavor profile.

  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply place the potatoes in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 15-20 minutes, or until warmed through.

Conclusion

Herbaceous Boulangère Potatoes are a delightful and versatile French side dish that will elevate any meal. With their tender, thinly sliced potatoes, aromatic herbs, and rich broth, these potatoes are sure to impress your family and guests. Whether you’re hosting a fancy dinner party or simply want to add a touch of elegance to your weeknight meals, this recipe is a must-try. So go ahead, give it a whirl, and savor the flavors of France right in your own kitchen!
