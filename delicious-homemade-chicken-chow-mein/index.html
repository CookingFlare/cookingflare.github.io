Delicious Homemade Chicken Chow Mein


If you’re craving a flavorful and satisfying dish that combines tender chicken, fresh vegetables, and perfectly seasoned noodles, look no further than homemade chicken chow mein. This beloved Chinese stir-fry is packed with savory flavors and textures that will transport your taste buds to a bustling Chinese street food market. Not only is it incredibly delicious, but it’s also easy to make right in the comfort of your own kitchen.

In this post, I’ll walk you through how to make the perfect chicken chow mein from scratch. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner, meal prepping for the week, or simply enjoying a quick weeknight meal, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. With simple ingredients and straightforward steps, you’ll be able to whip up a restaurant-quality dish in no time.

Ingredients for Chicken Chow Mein

To make this delicious homemade chicken chow mein, you’ll need the following ingredients:

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

For the Chow Mein:

  • 4 oz (115g) chow mein noodles (or substitute with lo mein noodles)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup bell pepper, thinly sliced (use red, yellow, or green bell peppers)
  • 1/2 cup carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup mushrooms, sliced (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated (optional but adds great flavor)

For the Stir-Fry Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili paste (optional for a spicy kick)

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Homemade Chicken Chow Mein

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

Start by marinating the chicken to ensure it’s tender and flavorful. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, cornstarch, sesame oil, rice vinegar, salt, and black pepper. Whisk everything together to form a smooth marinade. Add the thinly sliced chicken to the marinade and toss it well to coat all the pieces evenly. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate for at least 15 minutes, or up to 30 minutes if you have more time. This step is crucial because the marinade helps tenderize the chicken and infuses it with delicious flavor.

Step 2: Prepare the Chow Mein Noodles

While the chicken is marinating, cook your chow mein noodles according to the package instructions. Usually, this involves boiling them for a few minutes in salted water until they’re just tender, then draining them well. Once drained, rinse the noodles with cold water to stop the cooking process and prevent them from becoming too sticky. Set the noodles aside while you prepare the stir-fry.

Step 3: Stir-Fry the Chicken

Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat and add the vegetable oil. Once the oil is hot, add the marinated chicken pieces. Stir-fry the chicken for about 4-5 minutes, or until it’s cooked through and lightly browned. Be sure to avoid overcrowding the pan—if necessary, cook the chicken in batches. Once cooked, remove the chicken from the skillet and set it aside.

Step 4: Cook the Vegetables

In the same skillet or wok, add a bit more oil if necessary, and toss in the onion, garlic, bell pepper, carrot, and cabbage. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 3-4 minutes, or until they begin to soften but still retain some crunch. If you’re using mushrooms, add them at this stage, and cook for an additional 2 minutes. If you enjoy a bit of heat, add the grated ginger during this step for an extra layer of flavor.

Step 5: Make the Stir-Fry Sauce

While the vegetables are cooking, it’s time to prepare the stir-fry sauce. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, water, and chili paste if you’re using it. This sauce will give the chow mein its signature umami flavor and a slight sweetness that pairs perfectly with the savory chicken and vegetables.

Step 6: Combine Everything

Once the vegetables are cooked to your liking, return the cooked chicken to the skillet or wok. Add the cooked chow mein noodles and pour the stir-fry sauce over the top. Use tongs or chopsticks to toss everything together, ensuring that the noodles, chicken, and vegetables are well-coated with the sauce. Continue to stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

Step 7: Garnish and Serve

Once everything is combined and heated through, remove the skillet from the heat. Garnish your homemade chicken chow mein with freshly chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds for extra flavor and crunch. Serve the dish hot, and enjoy the vibrant, savory goodness of this homemade chow mein!

Tips for the Best Chicken Chow Mein

  1. Use Fresh Noodles: If possible, opt for fresh chow mein noodles rather than dried noodles. Fresh noodles have a softer, more delicate texture and absorb the stir-fry sauce better. However, dried noodles are a perfectly fine substitute if fresh noodles are unavailable.

  2. Customize with Your Favorite Vegetables: Chow mein is incredibly versatile, so feel free to swap in other vegetables you enjoy. Try adding bean sprouts, snow peas, broccoli, or baby corn to make the dish even more colorful and nutritious.

  3. Adjust the Sauce to Your Taste: If you like a sweeter sauce, add more hoisin sauce or a bit of sugar. For a saltier taste, increase the soy sauce or add a splash of fish sauce for extra depth. You can also adjust the level of spice by adding more chili paste or omitting it entirely.

  4. Make It Spicy: If you enjoy spicy food, feel free to increase the amount of chili paste or add red pepper flakes to the stir-fry sauce. You can even garnish the chow mein with some sliced fresh chili peppers for an extra kick.

  5. Double the Recipe for Leftovers: This chicken chow mein recipe makes a great amount for dinner, but it also works well as leftovers. The flavors meld even more after sitting in the fridge overnight, so don’t hesitate to double the recipe and have lunch ready for the next day.

Healthier Chicken Chow Mein

If you’re looking to make your homemade chicken chow mein a bit lighter, consider these modifications:

  • Use Lean Chicken: Opt for skinless chicken breast to cut down on fat content. Chicken thighs are delicious, but they tend to have more fat.
  • Increase the Vegetables: Add extra vegetables to make the dish even more nutritious. The more colorful, the better—plus, it boosts the fiber and vitamin content of the meal.
  • Use Whole Wheat Noodles: For a healthier twist, try whole wheat chow mein noodles or other whole grain options. They provide more fiber and nutrients compared to regular white noodles.

How to Serve Chicken Chow Mein

Chicken chow mein is a complete meal on its own, packed with protein, vegetables, and carbohydrates. However, if you want to serve it alongside other dishes, here are some great options:

  • Appetizers: Serve with crispy spring rolls, egg rolls, or dumplings for an appetizer spread.
  • Soup: Pair with hot and sour soup or egg drop soup to start the meal.
  • Rice: If you’re craving extra carbs, serve the chow mein with steamed jasmine rice or fried rice.

Conclusion

Homemade chicken chow mein is an incredible dish that brings the flavors of Chinese takeout right into your kitchen. With tender chicken, fresh vegetables, and a savory stir-fry sauce, it’s a meal that the whole family will enjoy. Plus, it’s easy to customize with your favorite vegetables or spice levels to make it uniquely yours.

By following this recipe, you’ll be able to create a satisfying, flavorful, and healthy meal that rivals anything you’d order at a restaurant. So next time you’re craving Chinese food, skip the takeout and make your own delicious chicken chow mein at home—you won’t regret it!
