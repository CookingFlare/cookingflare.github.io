How to Make Delicious Baked Doughnuts: Tips and Recipes


Baked doughnuts are a delightful treat that can be enjoyed any time of day. Unlike their fried counterparts, baked doughnuts offer a lighter, fluffier texture while still delivering on flavor. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, mastering the art of baked doughnuts is easier than you might think. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the process of making delicious baked doughnuts, complete with tips, tricks, and mouthwatering recipes to try at home.

The Basics of Baked Doughnuts

Ingredients You Will Need

To create the perfect baked doughnut, you’ll need a few essential ingredients. Here’s a basic list to get you started:

  • All-purpose flour: The foundation of your doughnut batter. You can also experiment with whole wheat flour for a healthier option.
  • Sugar: Granulated sugar is typically used, but brown sugar can add a deeper flavor.
  • Baking powder: This is key for achieving that fluffy texture.
  • Salt: A pinch enhances the overall flavor.
  • Eggs: They provide structure and moisture to the doughnuts.
  • Milk: Use whole milk for richness, but you can substitute with almond or oat milk for a dairy-free option.
  • Butter: Melted butter adds a rich flavor; alternatives include coconut oil or vegetable oil.
  • Vanilla extract: A splash of pure vanilla elevates the taste.

Equipment You’ll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following equipment on hand:

  • Doughnut pan: A silicone or metal doughnut pan is essential for shaping your doughnuts.
  • Mixing bowls: Use both a large bowl for dry ingredients and a smaller bowl for wet ingredients.
  • Whisk: A whisk helps to combine your ingredients smoothly.
  • Spatula: For folding and mixing.
  • Cooling rack: Essential for letting your doughnuts cool after baking.

Tips for Perfectly Baked Doughnuts

1. Don’t Overmix the Batter

When combining your dry and wet ingredients, mix until just combined. Overmixing can lead to dense and tough doughnuts, so be gentle. A few lumps in the batter are perfectly fine!

2. Fill the Pan Correctly

When pouring the batter into your doughnut pan, fill each cavity about two-thirds full. This allows room for the doughnuts to rise without overflowing.

3. Preheat Your Oven

Always preheat your oven before baking. A properly heated oven ensures that your doughnuts will cook evenly and rise beautifully.

4. Experiment with Flavors

The beauty of baked doughnuts is their versatility. Don’t hesitate to experiment with flavors by adding spices like cinnamon or nutmeg, or incorporating ingredients like cocoa powder, citrus zest, or even pureed fruits.

5. Cool Before Glazing

After baking, allow your doughnuts to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a cooling rack. This helps them set and prevents the glaze from sliding off.

Delicious Baked Doughnut Recipes

Classic Vanilla Baked Doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease your doughnut pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  5. Spoon the batter into the prepared doughnut pan, filling each cavity about two-thirds full.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the doughnuts spring back when lightly pressed.
  7. Allow to cool before glazing or dusting with powdered sugar.

Chocolate Baked Doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease your doughnut pan.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  5. Fill the doughnut pan two-thirds full with the batter.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool before serving, and consider glazing with a chocolate ganache for an extra treat.

Lemon Glazed Baked Doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter

For the Lemon Glaze

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease your doughnut pan.
  2. In a bowl, mix together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and lemon zest.
  3. In another bowl, whisk the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Combine the wet ingredients with the dry ingredients and mix gently.
  5. Fill each cavity of the doughnut pan and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  6. For the glaze, whisk together the powdered sugar, lemon juice, and zest until smooth.
  7. Once the doughnuts have cooled, dip the tops into the glaze and allow to set.

Maple Bacon Baked Doughnuts

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1/2 cup cooked bacon, crumbled

For the Maple Glaze

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1-2 tablespoons milk (to desired consistency)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease your doughnut pan.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Mix the wet ingredients with the dry ingredients, then fold in the crumbled bacon.
  5. Fill the doughnut pan and bake for 10-12 minutes.
  6. For the glaze, whisk together the powdered sugar, maple syrup, and milk.
  7. Once cooled, dip the doughnuts in the glaze and sprinkle with additional bacon if desired.

Storing and Freezing Baked Doughnuts

Baked doughnuts are best enjoyed fresh but can be stored for a few days. Here are some tips for storage:

  • Room Temperature: Store glazed doughnuts in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days.
  • Refrigeration: If you want to keep them longer, store them in the refrigerator for up to a week. Just be aware that refrigeration may alter their texture.
  • Freezing: To freeze, place unglazed doughnuts in a single layer on a baking sheet until frozen solid, then transfer them to a freezer-safe bag. They can be frozen for up to three months. When ready to enjoy, thaw at room temperature and glaze as desired.

Conclusion

Baked doughnuts are a simple yet satisfying treat that can be customized to your taste. With a little creativity and the right techniques, you can impress friends and family with your homemade delights. Whether you opt for classic vanilla, rich chocolate, zesty lemon, or savory maple bacon, your baked doughnuts are sure to be a hit. Enjoy experimenting with flavors, and don’t forget to share your creations with loved ones. Happy baking!
