Fall Cooking for Kids: 7 Fun and Easy Recipes


As the cool autumn breeze rolls in and the leaves start to change color, it’s time to celebrate the flavors of fall. From sweet pumpkins to hearty squashes, apples, and cinnamon, the season offers a delightful array of ingredients perfect for cooking with kids. Fall cooking doesn’t have to be complicated—it’s about creating fun, easy, and tasty dishes that kids will love while embracing the flavors of the season.

Here are 7 fall-inspired recipes that are perfect for kids to help prepare, and even better to enjoy together as a family. These recipes are designed to be fun, simple, and full of autumn goodness, making them an ideal way to introduce children to the kitchen while teaching them about seasonal ingredients.

1. Pumpkin Pancakes

What better way to kick off fall than with a batch of fluffy, pumpkin-filled pancakes? These pumpkin pancakes are an excellent way to use the season’s favorite squash and get the kids excited about breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently until just combined. The batter should be thick but pourable.
  4. Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat. Lightly grease it with cooking spray or a little bit of butter.
  5. For each pancake, pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the griddle. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip the pancakes and cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  6. Serve with maple syrup, a dollop of whipped cream, or some chopped pecans for added texture.

Tip for kids: Let the little ones help with whisking the dry ingredients or pouring the pancake batter onto the griddle (with supervision, of course!).

2. Apple Cinnamon Muffins

Apples are synonymous with fall, and what better way to showcase them than in a warm, spiced muffin? Apple cinnamon muffins are an easy recipe that combines the natural sweetness of apples with the warmth of cinnamon—perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 large apple, peeled, cored, and chopped into small pieces
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease it with butter.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix together the milk, vegetable oil, egg, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Toss the chopped apples with lemon juice to prevent browning, then fold them into the batter.
  6. Spoon the batter into the muffin tin, filling each cup about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Let the muffins cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tip for kids: Kids can enjoy peeling and chopping apples (with a child-safe knife) and adding them to the batter. You can also let them sprinkle a bit of cinnamon sugar on top of the muffins before baking for an extra touch of sweetness.

3. Butternut Squash Soup

Butternut squash is a fall favorite, and making soup is a great way to enjoy its sweet and earthy flavor. This creamy butternut squash soup is mild, comforting, and kid-friendly, with a velvety texture that is sure to please even the pickiest eaters.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional for creaminess)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the cubed butternut squash on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender and lightly browned.
  2. In a large pot, sauté the onion and garlic over medium heat until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the roasted butternut squash, vegetable broth, and cinnamon to the pot. Bring to a simmer and cook for another 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth. Alternatively, you can blend the soup in batches in a regular blender.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream (if using) and season with salt and pepper to taste. Heat for a few more minutes if needed.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with a swirl of cream or some toasted pumpkin seeds for added texture.

Tip for kids: Kids can help scoop out the seeds from the squash before roasting and can also enjoy stirring the soup as it simmers on the stove.

4. Mini Pumpkin Pizzas

Who says pizza is only for summer? These mini pumpkin pizzas are a creative and fun way to incorporate pumpkin into a kid-friendly meal. The dough serves as a base for a variety of toppings, with the pumpkin adding a savory twist to the classic pizza.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package refrigerated pizza dough
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup cooked sausage or pepperoni (optional)
  • Fresh basil for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven according to the instructions on the pizza dough package.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface and cut it into small, personal-sized circles using a cookie cutter or a glass.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the pumpkin puree, cinnamon, and garlic powder to create a pumpkin sauce.
  4. Spread a thin layer of the pumpkin sauce over each mini pizza dough circle.
  5. Top with shredded mozzarella cheese and optional toppings like sausage or pepperoni.
  6. Bake according to the pizza dough package instructions or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil and serve.

Tip for kids: Let the kids choose their own toppings or help spread the pumpkin sauce on the dough. This recipe is a great way for children to get creative in the kitchen.

5. Roasted Cinnamon Apple Chips

Apple chips are a healthy, crunchy snack that kids will love to munch on. This easy recipe uses just a few ingredients and a little time in the oven to transform fresh apples into a crispy treat.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large apples (such as Granny Smith or Fuji)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°F (95°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Core and thinly slice the apples into rings (you can leave the skin on). A mandolin slicer works best for even slices.
  3. Lay the apple slices in a single layer on the baking sheet. Sprinkle with cinnamon and sugar, if using.
  4. Bake for 2-3 hours, flipping the slices halfway through, until the apple chips are crispy and golden brown.
  5. Let the apple chips cool completely before serving.

Tip for kids: Children can help sprinkle the cinnamon and sugar on the apple slices and arrange them on the baking sheet.

6. Sweet Potato Fries

Sweet potato fries are a nutritious and tasty alternative to regular fries. They’re simple to make and kids will love the slightly sweet flavor combined with the crispy texture.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Peel and cut the sweet potatoes into thin, fry-like strips.
  3. In a bowl, toss the sweet potato strips with olive oil, paprika, garlic powder, and salt until evenly coated.
  4. Spread the sweet potatoes in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the fries are crispy and golden brown.
  6. Serve with a side of ketchup or ranch dressing for dipping.

Tip for kids: Let the kids help cut the sweet potatoes into strips or toss them in the seasoning mixture. This is a great way to get them involved in the cooking process.

7. Pumpkin Spice Smoothie

For a quick, nutritious snack or breakfast, try this pumpkin spice smoothie. It’s packed with fall flavors, and the creamy texture will make it a hit with kids.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 banana
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the pumpkin puree, banana, Greek yogurt, milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and honey (if using).
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour into glasses and serve chilled.

Tip for kids: Let the little ones help add the ingredients to the blender and press the button to blend.

Conclusion

These 7 fall-inspired recipes are not only delicious but also fun to make with your kids. Whether you’re whipping up pumpkin pancakes in the morning or roasting sweet potato fries for an afternoon snack, these dishes are full of the flavors and warmth of autumn. Cooking together as a family is a great way to bond and teach kids about healthy eating while having fun in the kitchen. So, grab your apron and get ready to enjoy the flavors of fall!
