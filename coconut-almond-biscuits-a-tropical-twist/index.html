Coconut Almond Biscuits: A Tropical Twist


Welcome to our kitchen where we’re bringing you a delightful recipe that combines the tropical flavors of coconut and the nutty goodness of almonds in a perfect harmony - Coconut Almond Biscuits! These biscuits are not only a treat for your taste buds but also a lovely addition to your baking repertoire. Let’s dive into the recipe and discover how to create these mouthwatering treats.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup almond meal
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (180°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

  2. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, shredded coconut, almond meal, and a pinch of salt. Mix well to ensure even distribution of ingredients.

  3. Cream Butter and Sugar: In another bowl, cream together the softened unsalted butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy. This step is crucial for the texture of your biscuits.

  4. Incorporate Flavors: Add the vanilla extract to the butter-sugar mixture and mix until well combined. The vanilla adds a subtle depth of flavor to the biscuits.

  5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredient mixture into the wet ingredients. Mix until a dough forms. Be careful not to overmix; you want a cohesive dough, not a tough one.

  6. Shape the Biscuits: Take small portions of the dough and shape them into round biscuits. You can flatten them slightly with the back of a fork for a classic look.

  7. Bake to Perfection: Place the shaped biscuits on the prepared baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes or until the edges are lightly golden.

  8. Cool and Enjoy: Once baked, let the biscuits cool on a wire rack. These Coconut Almond Biscuits are best enjoyed with a cup of tea or coffee. Store them in an airtight container for freshness.

Tips and Variations:

  • For an extra crunch, you can sprinkle some sliced almonds on top of the biscuits before baking.
  • Experiment with different extracts like almond or coconut extract for a more intense flavor profile.
  • If you prefer a sweeter taste, you can dust the cooled biscuits with powdered sugar.

Final Thoughts:

Bringing a tropical twist to your baking endeavors with these Coconut Almond Biscuits is a delightful way to infuse some sunshine into your day. Whether you indulge in them as a snack or share them with loved ones, these biscuits are sure to impress with their unique flavor combination.
