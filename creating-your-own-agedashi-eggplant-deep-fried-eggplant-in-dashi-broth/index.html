Creating Your Own Agedashi Eggplant: Deep-Fried Eggplant in Dashi Broth


If you’re looking to add a touch of Japanese culinary flair to your cooking repertoire, Agedashi Eggplant is a dish that’s sure to impress. This traditional Japanese dish features deep-fried eggplant served in a flavorful dashi broth, making it a delightful appetizer or side dish for any occasion. The contrast between the crispy exterior of the eggplant and the umami-rich broth creates a harmonious balance of textures and flavors that will leave your taste buds wanting more. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of creating your own Agedashi Eggplant at home, so you can enjoy this delectable dish whenever the craving strikes.

Ingredients

For the Agedashi Eggplant:

  • 2 medium-sized Japanese eggplants
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Salt for seasoning

For the Dashi Broth:

  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Grated daikon radish (optional)
  • Shichimi togarashi (Japanese seven spice, optional)

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dashi Broth

In a saucepan, combine the dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring to dissolve the sugar. Once the broth is simmering, reduce the heat and keep it warm while you prepare the eggplant.

Step 2: Prepare the Eggplant

Wash the eggplants and cut off the stems. Slice the eggplants into 1-inch thick rounds, then make a few shallow cuts in a crisscross pattern on the surface of each round. This will help the eggplant cook evenly and absorb the flavors of the broth.

Step 3: Coat the Eggplant

Place the cornstarch in a shallow dish. Lightly coat each eggplant round in the cornstarch, shaking off any excess. The cornstarch will help create a crispy coating when the eggplant is fried.

Step 4: Fry the Eggplant

In a deep pan or pot, heat the vegetable oil to 350°F. Carefully add the coated eggplant rounds to the hot oil, working in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry the eggplant for 3-4 minutes, or until the exterior is golden brown and crispy. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the fried eggplant to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Step 5: Serve the Agedashi Eggplant

Place the fried eggplant in shallow serving bowls or plates. Ladle the warm dashi broth over the eggplant, allowing it to seep into the nooks and crannies of the crispy coating. Garnish with sliced green onions, a sprinkle of shichimi togarashi for a touch of heat, and grated daikon radish for a refreshing contrast.

Enjoy Your Culinary Creation

Your homemade Agedashi Eggplant is now ready to be enjoyed! The tender eggplant, encased in a crunchy coating, pairs perfectly with the savory, umami-rich dashi broth. Whether you’re serving it as an appetizer at a dinner party or as a comforting dish for a cozy night in, this Agedashi Eggplant is sure to be a hit. Experiment with the flavors and presentation to make it your own, and savor the satisfaction of mastering this classic Japanese dish in your own kitchen.
