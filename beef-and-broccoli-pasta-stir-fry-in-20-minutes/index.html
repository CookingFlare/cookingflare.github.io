Beef and Broccoli Pasta Stir-Fry in 20 Minutes


Looking for a quick, flavorful, and satisfying dinner that doesn’t require hours in the kitchen? This Beef and Broccoli Pasta Stir-Fry is exactly what you need. Packed with tender strips of beef, vibrant broccoli florets, and noodles tossed in a silky sauce, this recipe is perfect for busy weeknights. The best part? It’s ready in just 20 minutes, making it ideal for anyone craving homemade comfort without sacrificing time.

Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This stir-fry takes the classic combination of beef and broccoli and introduces a delightful twist with pasta. By incorporating noodles, this dish turns into a hearty meal that melds the bold flavors of stir-fry cooking with the satisfying texture of perfectly cooked pasta. With minimal prep and simple ingredients, you’ll be amazed at how easily this recipe comes together.

Not only is this dish quick and easy to make, but it also strikes the perfect balance between fresh vegetables, protein, and carbs. Whether you need to fuel up after a long day or serve something impressive on short notice, this is a recipe you’ll want to keep in your rotation.

Ingredients You’ll Need

This recipe uses pantry and fridge staples, ensuring you can whip it up anytime without multiple grocery store runs. Here’s what you’ll need:

For the Pasta:

  • 8 ounces of pasta (spaghetti, fettuccine, linguine, or even egg noodles work well)
  • Salt (for the pasta water)

For the Stir-Fry:

  • 1 pound of flank steak or sirloin steak, sliced thinly against the grain
  • 2 cups of fresh broccoli florets
  • 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil, divided
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of grated ginger
  • ½ cup of soy sauce (low-sodium is preferred)
  • ¼ cup of oyster sauce (optional for deeper flavor)
  • 2 tablespoons of honey or brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of sesame oil
  • ¼ teaspoon of red pepper flakes (optional, for a little heat)
  • 2 tablespoons of cornstarch
  • 3 tablespoons of water

Optional Garnishes:

  • Chopped green onions
  • Toasted sesame seeds

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Start by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to package directions until it’s al dente. Reserve about ½ cup of pasta water before draining. This starchy water will help bind the sauce later.

While the pasta is cooking, you can begin prepping the rest of your ingredients to save time.

Step 2: Prepare the Beef

Thinly slice the steak against the grain for maximum tenderness. Toss the slices with 1 tablespoon of cornstarch; this helps the beef sear beautifully while keeping it soft and juicy. Set the beef aside.

Step 3: Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, honey (or brown sugar), sesame oil, and red pepper flakes. Combine the remaining tablespoon of cornstarch with 3 tablespoons of water and stir until dissolved. Add this slurry to the sauce mixture and whisk until smooth. This will ensure the sauce thickens perfectly in the stir-fry.

Step 4: Cook the Broccoli

Heat 1½ tablespoons of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the broccoli florets and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes, until they’re bright green and slightly crisp. Remove from the skillet and set aside.

Step 5: Sear the Beef

Add the remaining 1½ tablespoons of vegetable oil to the skillet and increase the heat to high. Add the beef slices in a single layer and sear for 1–2 minutes per side. Work in batches if needed to avoid overcrowding the pan. Once the beef is browned, add the garlic and ginger, stirring for 30 seconds until fragrant.

Step 6: Combine Everything

Reduce the heat to medium and return the broccoli to the skillet. Pour the sauce over the beef and broccoli, stirring to coat evenly. Allow the sauce to simmer for 1-2 minutes until it thickens. If it becomes too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up.

Next, add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss everything together, ensuring the noodles are coated in the luscious sauce. If desired, add a bit more of the pasta water for extra sauciness.

Step 7: Serve and Garnish

Transfer the Beef and Broccoli Pasta Stir-Fry to serving plates or bowls. Garnish with chopped green onions and toasted sesame seeds for an added burst of flavor and visual appeal. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Expert Tips for Success

  1. Slice the Beef Thinly: Cutting the steak against the grain ensures tender bites. If your knife skills are still a work in progress, partially freeze the beef for 30 minutes before slicing—it makes the task much easier.

  2. Don’t Skip the Sauce Slurry: The combination of cornstarch and water is key to achieving a glossy, perfectly thickened sauce.

  3. Use High Heat for Stir-Frying: High heat lets you achieve that irresistible caramelization on the beef while keeping the broccoli crisp and vibrant.

  4. Customize to Taste: This recipe is super versatile. Add mushrooms, bell peppers, or snap peas for more veggie variety. You can also substitute tofu or chicken if you’d like a different protein.

  5. Make It Ahead: Prepare the sauce ahead of time and store it in the fridge for up to a week. When you’re ready to cook, simply toss it in with your ingredients for a lightning-fast meal.

Why This Recipe Works

The beauty of this Beef and Broccoli Pasta Stir-Fry is in its simplicity. By using ingredients that are easy to find and combining them in a thoughtful way, you end up with a dish that’s both elevated and approachable. The sauce is the star, delivering umami-packed flavors that tie the beef, broccoli, and pasta together seamlessly.

Additionally, this recipe hits a sweet spot between saving time and maximizing flavor. You get all the richness of a restaurant-quality meal in a fraction of the time. It’s a perfect strategy for those nights when you need dinner in a hurry but don’t want to sacrifice taste or nutrition.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (highly unlikely!), store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days. To reheat, simply warm the stir-fry in a skillet with a splash of water to loosen the sauce. You can also microwave portions on medium power for 2–3 minutes.
