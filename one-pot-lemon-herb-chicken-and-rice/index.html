One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice


One pot meals are a lifesaver when it comes to weeknight dinners. They are easy to make, require minimal cleanup, and are packed with flavor. This One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice recipe is no exception. With the bright and zesty flavors of lemon and the aromatic blend of herbs, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Let’s get cooking!

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 lemon, zest and juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large, deep skillet over medium-high heat. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper, then add them to the skillet, skin side down. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until the skin is golden brown and crispy. Flip the chicken thighs and cook for an additional 2 minutes. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  2. In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 2 minutes, or until the onion is translucent and fragrant.

  3. Add the rice to the skillet and stir to coat it in the oil and aromatics. Cook for 1 minute, then pour in the chicken broth, lemon juice, lemon zest, dried thyme, and dried rosemary. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Place the chicken thighs back into the skillet, skin side up, on top of the rice mixture. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.

  5. Once the rice is cooked, remove the skillet from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes. This will allow the flavors to meld together.

  6. Serve the One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice hot, garnished with fresh parsley for a pop of color. Enjoy!

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra burst of flavor, add a handful of frozen peas or diced bell peppers to the skillet along with the rice.
  • If you prefer boneless chicken, you can use boneless, skinless chicken thighs or chicken breasts. Adjust the cooking time accordingly, as boneless cuts cook faster.
  • Feel free to experiment with different herbs and spices to suit your taste. Fresh herbs like thyme and rosemary work wonderfully in this recipe, but you can also use dried herbs if that’s what you have on hand.
  • To make this dish even more vibrant, squeeze some fresh lemon juice over the cooked chicken and rice just before serving.

Conclusion

One Pot Lemon Herb Chicken and Rice is a delicious and satisfying meal that can be whipped up in no time. The combination of zesty lemon, aromatic herbs, and tender chicken with fluffy rice is simply irresistible. Plus, with only one pot to clean, you’ll have more time to relax and enjoy your meal. Give this recipe a try and let your taste buds thank you!
