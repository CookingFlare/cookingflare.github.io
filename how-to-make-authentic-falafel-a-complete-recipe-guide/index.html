How to Make Authentic Falafel: A Complete Recipe Guide


Falafel, the crispy, golden-brown delights made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, has become a beloved street food around the world. Originating in the Middle East, falafel has an irresistible combination of textures and flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner looking to explore new cuisines, this falafel recipe guide will provide everything you need to know to make authentic falafel from scratch.

The Origins of Falafel

Falafel has a rich history that dates back thousands of years. While its exact origins are debated, it is believed that falafel was first made in Egypt using fava beans. It eventually made its way across the Middle East, evolving into the chickpea-based falafel we know today. In many countries, falafel is enjoyed as a snack, a part of a mezze platter, or served in a pita or flatbread with a variety of accompaniments.

Why Make Homemade Falafel?

While store-bought falafel is convenient, homemade falafel allows you to control the quality of ingredients, ensuring they’re fresh and free of preservatives. Homemade falafel is also more customizable. You can adjust the seasoning and texture to suit your personal preferences.

Making falafel from scratch isn’t difficult; it just requires patience and the right technique. By following this step-by-step guide, you’ll be able to create falafel that’s crispy on the outside and tender on the inside—just like the best versions served in Middle Eastern restaurants.

Ingredients for Authentic Falafel

The key to authentic falafel lies in the ingredients. Fresh herbs and spices play an essential role in developing the distinct flavor profile. Below is a list of the ingredients you will need:

  • Chickpeas (garbanzo beans): Dried chickpeas are a must for authentic falafel. Using canned chickpeas won’t yield the same texture or flavor. Soak them overnight to soften.
  • Onion: A small onion adds sweetness and moisture to the mixture.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic gives the falafel its aromatic bite.
  • Parsley: Fresh parsley brings vibrancy and a herbaceous note.
  • Cilantro: Cilantro complements the parsley and adds freshness.
  • Cumin: This earthy spice is a key flavor in traditional falafel.
  • Coriander: Ground coriander adds a subtle citrusy flavor that pairs beautifully with cumin.
  • Baking soda: A little baking soda helps the falafel puff up and stay light and airy inside.
  • Flour (or breadcrumbs): Helps bind the ingredients together.
  • Salt and pepper: Season to taste.
  • Vegetable oil: For frying.

Equipment You’ll Need

To prepare falafel, you’ll need a few essential kitchen tools:

  • Food processor: This will be used to blend the ingredients into a coarse paste.
  • Large mixing bowl: For mixing and seasoning the falafel mixture.
  • Frying pan or deep fryer: A heavy-bottomed pan works well for frying, but a deep fryer gives a more even cook.
  • Slotted spoon or tongs: To remove the falafel from the oil.
  • Paper towels: For draining excess oil from the fried falafel.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Falafel

Now that you have your ingredients and equipment ready, it’s time to start preparing your falafel. Follow these steps for the perfect homemade falafel.

Step 1: Soak the Chickpeas

Begin by soaking your dried chickpeas in plenty of water. Make sure the water level is a few inches higher than the chickpeas as they will expand while soaking. Leave them to soak for at least 12 hours, or overnight, to ensure they soften completely.

Do not use canned chickpeas for this recipe, as they will yield a mushy mixture that won’t hold its shape during frying.

Step 2: Prepare the Ingredients

Once the chickpeas are ready, drain and rinse them thoroughly. You will then need to chop the onion, garlic, parsley, and cilantro. It’s best to chop the herbs finely, but avoid using a food processor for this step, as you want the herbs to remain somewhat intact for texture.

Step 3: Process the Chickpeas and Herbs

Place the drained chickpeas, chopped onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper into a food processor. Pulse everything together until the mixture forms a coarse paste. The texture should be slightly rough and grainy, not smooth like hummus.

Step 4: Add Baking Soda and Flour

Once the mixture is blended, transfer it to a large mixing bowl. Sprinkle in the baking soda and add enough flour (or breadcrumbs) to help bind the mixture together. Start with a few tablespoons of flour and add more if the mixture feels too wet or sticky. The goal is to have a dough-like consistency that holds its shape when formed into balls or patties.

Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate the mixture for at least 1 hour. This step helps the falafel hold its shape during frying.

Step 5: Shape the Falafel

Once the mixture has chilled, it’s time to shape the falafel. Use your hands or a spoon to form small balls or patties, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. You can lightly oil your hands to prevent the mixture from sticking.

If you want uniform-shaped falafel, you can use a falafel scoop or spoon. The balls or patties should be compact but not overly pressed, as they need to remain light and fluffy inside.

Step 6: Heat the Oil

Heat about 3 inches of vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large frying pan over medium-high heat. To test if the oil is hot enough, drop a small piece of the falafel mixture into the oil. If it sizzles and rises to the surface quickly, the oil is ready.

Step 7: Fry the Falafel

Carefully lower the falafel balls or patties into the hot oil, working in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry them for about 3-4 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy on all sides. Use a slotted spoon to remove the falafel and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.

Step 8: Serve the Falafel

Once your falafel is crispy and golden, it’s time to serve! Falafel is delicious on its own or with a variety of accompaniments. Traditionally, it’s served in pita bread or flatbread with tahini sauce, hummus, and a side of pickled vegetables. You can also serve it as part of a mezze platter with tabbouleh, baba ganoush, or a simple cucumber-tomato salad.

Tips for Perfect Falafel

While making falafel from scratch is straightforward, here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect batch every time:

  1. Use dried chickpeas: Soaking dried chickpeas is crucial for the right texture. Canned chickpeas can be too soft and may cause the falafel to fall apart.
  2. Don’t overprocess the mixture: You want the falafel mixture to remain coarse, so avoid blending it into a fine paste.
  3. Refrigerate the mixture: Chilling the falafel mixture before frying helps it hold its shape and results in a crispier texture.
  4. Fry at the right temperature: If the oil is too hot, the falafel will burn on the outside before cooking through. If the oil is too cool, they will absorb excess oil and become greasy. Maintaining a steady temperature of around 350°F (175°C) is ideal.
  5. Adjust the seasoning: Falafel is all about flavor. Taste the mixture before frying and adjust the salt, cumin, or coriander to your liking.

Variations on Classic Falafel

While the traditional falafel is made with chickpeas, there are a few variations you can try:

  • Fava Bean Falafel: Some recipes use fava beans instead of chickpeas for a smoother texture and a slightly different flavor.
  • Sweet Potato Falafel: Adding mashed sweet potatoes to the mixture can create a slightly sweeter falafel with a different color.
  • Spicy Falafel: For a kick of heat, add chili flakes or fresh chopped chili peppers to the mixture.

Storing and Reheating Falafel

If you have leftover falafel, you can store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, simply place the falafel in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes until they are heated through and crispy again.

Falafel can also be frozen. Place the shaped, uncooked falafel balls or patties on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze for 1-2 hours. Once frozen, transfer them to a freezer bag for up to 3 months. When you’re ready to fry, cook the frozen falafel straight from the freezer without thawing.

Conclusion

Making authentic falafel from scratch is a rewarding and delicious experience. With the right ingredients and a little patience, you can enjoy crispy, flavorful falafel at home that rivals your favorite Middle Eastern restaurants. Serve it in a pita with your favorite toppings, or enjoy it as part of a mezze platter for a satisfying meal.
