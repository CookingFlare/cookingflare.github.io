Traditional and Authentic Gluten-Free Italian Recipes


Italian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, aromas, and textures, and its emphasis on fresh ingredients, simplicity, and tradition. However, for those who suffer from gluten intolerance or sensitivity, traditional Italian dishes can be a challenge. Fear not, for this collection of recipes will guide you through the preparation of authentic, gluten-free Italian dishes that will transport your taste buds to the Tuscan countryside.

Pasta Fatta in Casa (Homemade Gluten-Free Pasta)

In Italy, pasta is a staple, and making it from scratch is a cherished tradition. This recipe for homemade gluten-free pasta, or “pasta fatta in casa,” is a game-changer for those who thought they had to give up pasta altogether.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gluten-free flour blend (containing rice flour, potato starch, and tapioca flour)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend and salt.
  2. Make a well in the center of the flour mixture and crack in the egg.
  3. Using a fork, gradually incorporate the flour into the egg, starting from the inner rim of the well.
  4. As the dough begins to form, switch to using your hands to knead it for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough into 4 equal pieces and roll out each piece into a thin sheet using a pasta machine or a rolling pin.
  7. Cut the pasta into your desired shape (e.g., spaghetti, fettuccine, or pappardelle).
  8. Cook the pasta in boiling, salted water for 2-3 minutes, or until al dente.
  9. Drain and serve with your favorite sauce.

Risotto alla Milanese (Gluten-Free Risotto with Saffron and Ossobuco)

Risotto is a classic Italian dish that is often associated with special occasions. This gluten-free version of Risotto alla Milanese, featuring saffron-infused risotto and slow-cooked ossobuco, is a show-stopper that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gluten-free Arborio rice
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, warmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup white wine (gluten-free)
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads, soaked in 2 tablespoons hot water
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (gluten-free)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup slow-cooked ossobuco (see below for recipe)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the chicken broth in a separate pot and keep warm.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Add the Arborio rice and cook, stirring constantly, for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the white wine and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is almost completely absorbed.
  6. Add 1/2 cup of the warmed broth to the rice and cook, stirring constantly, until the liquid is mostly absorbed. Repeat this process, adding the broth in 1/2-cup increments, until the rice is cooked and creamy, about 20-25 minutes.
  7. Stir in the saffron and its soaking liquid, and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  8. Stir in the Parmesan cheese and parsley.
  9. Serve the risotto with the slow-cooked ossobuco and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.

Ossobuco alla Milanese (Gluten-Free Braised Veal Shanks with White Wine and Vegetables)

Ossobuco is a classic Milanese dish that consists of braised veal shanks slow-cooked in white wine and aromatics. This gluten-free version is just as tender and flavorful as its traditional counterpart.

Ingredients:

  • 4 veal shanks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, chopped
  • 1 cup white wine (gluten-free)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 bay leaves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 300°F (150°C).
  2. Season the veal shanks with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the veal shanks until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per side. Remove the veal from the pot and set aside.
  4. Reduce the heat to medium and add the chopped onion, garlic, carrot, and celery to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender, about 10 minutes.
  5. Add the white wine, beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, and bay leaves to the pot. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
  6. Return the veal shanks to the pot and cover with a lid.
  7. Transfer the pot to the preheated oven and braise the ossobuco for 2 1/2 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls off the bone.
  8. Remove the pot from the oven and let the ossobuco rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Tiramisù (Gluten-Free Coffee-Soaked Ladyfingers with Mascarpone Cream)

Tiramisù is a beloved Italian dessert that consists of coffee-soaked ladyfingers layered with mascarpone cream. This gluten-free version uses gluten-free ladyfingers and is just as decadent as its traditional counterpart.

Ingredients:

  • 12-16 gluten-free ladyfingers
  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Cocoa powder, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the mascarpone cheese, granulated sugar, egg yolks, vanilla extract, and salt. Beat with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy.
  2. Dip each ladyfinger into the coffee for about 3-5 seconds on each side. They should be soft and pliable but not too wet.
  3. In a large serving dish, create a layer of ladyfingers. You may need to trim them to fit the dish.
  4. Spread half of the mascarpone cream over the ladyfingers.
  5. Repeat the layers, starting with the ladyfingers, then the mascarpone cream.
  6. Dust the top of the tiramisù with cocoa powder.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 3 hours or overnight before serving.

These traditional and authentic gluten-free Italian recipes are sure to transport your taste buds to the Tuscan countryside. With a little practice and patience, you’ll be creating delicious, gluten-free Italian dishes that will impress your family and friends. Buon appetito!
