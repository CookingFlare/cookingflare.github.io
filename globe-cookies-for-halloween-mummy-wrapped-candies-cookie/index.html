Globe Cookies for Halloween: Mummy Wrapped Candies Cookie


Fancy a devilishly devious yet delightful treat for your Halloween feast? Look no further! Our sought-after recipe of Mummy Wrapped Candies Cookies is guaranteed to entice your taste buds and delight your guests. With a touch of spooky charm, these mummy-wrapped candies are up a notch from your standard halloween cookie fare, and will surely have your guests asking for more.

Key Ingredients and Equipment:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, for rolling dough
  • 1/2 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup edible white decorating icing
  • 1 cup candy-coated chocolate candies
  • Rolling pin
  • Parchment paper
  • Wilton piping bag fitted with a small round tip (or a plastic bag with a small corner clipped)
  • Pastry brush
  • Baking sheet

Instructions:

  1. Whip the Whisk Magic: Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and line your baking sheets with parchment paper. In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened unsalted butter and sugars until creamy. This should take about two minutes using an electric mixer. It’s like magic, watching those two become one!

  2. Cracking the Egg-cellence: Mix in beaten eggs and the vanilla extract, one at a time, still using the electric mixer on medium speed. Your mixture should be smooth and palatable by now.

  3. A Flour-y Mix: In a separate bowl, whisk together 3 cups of all-purpose flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture into your wet mixture, blending until well incorporated. Don’t forget to mix in those semisweet chocolate chips too!

  4. Rollercoaster of Dough: Flour your surface before rolling out your dough to roughly 1/4-inch (0.6 cm) thickness. Using a cookie cutter of your desired shape, cut out cookie shapes and gently place them onto the prepared baking sheets. It’s fun to try different shapes for Halloween, from spooky pumpkins and dismal skulls to creepy bats and cats.

  5. Bake Time: Bake those beautiful designed cookies for 10-12 minutes or until they’re golden brown around the edges. Allow the cookies to cool entirely before proceeding to the next step.

  6. Royal Mummy Wrap: While the cookies are cooling, prepare your edible white decorating icing by kneading it according to the package’s instructions, then filling the piping bag fitted with a small round tip (or a plastic bag with a small corner clipped). Brush your cookies lightly with the confectioners’ sugar to ensure your mummy wrap sticks to the cookie.

  7. Wrapping the Mummy: Pipe criss-crossing lines of icing to create the appearance of a mummy’s bandages over your cookies. This step might take a bit of patience but the result will be stunning.

  8. The Finishing Touch: Once the icing has dried, attach candy-coated chocolate candies over the icing as the mummy’s eyes. Your delectably spooky Mummy Wrapped Candy Cookies are ready to be served and enjoyed.

Serve these dessert delights at your next Halloween gathering, workplace potluck, or spooky family dinner, and see these twitchy ghosts turn into your biggest fans! Don’t be surprised when these eerie cookies vanish without a trace!
