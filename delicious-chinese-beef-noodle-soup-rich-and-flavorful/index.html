Delicious Chinese Beef Noodle Soup: Rich and Flavorful


There’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of noodles to soothe the soul. In China, beef noodle soup is a beloved dish that has been passed down through generations, and for good reason. This hearty, rich, and flavorful soup is a staple of Chinese cuisine, and its popularity extends far beyond the country’s borders. In this recipe, we’ll explore the secrets to making a truly authentic and delicious Chinese beef noodle soup that’s sure to become a family favorite.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef brisket or beef shank, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons chopped scallions, for garnish
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro, for garnish
  • 1 package Chinese noodles (such as lo mein or thin rice noodles)

Instructions

Step 1: Marinate the Beef

In a large bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), sugar, and white pepper. Add the sliced beef and mix well to coat. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Step 2: Prepare the Aromatics

Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large pot or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced ginger and stir-fry until fragrant, about 1 minute.

Step 3: Cook the Beef

Add the marinated beef to the pot and stir-fry until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove the beef from the pot and set aside.

Step 4: Make the Broth

Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil to the pot. Pour in the beef broth, water, and browned beef. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 1 1/2 hours, or until the beef is tender.

Step 5: Thicken the Broth

In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and a splash of the hot broth until smooth. Stir the cornstarch mixture back into the pot and continue to simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the broth has thickened to your liking.

Step 6: Cook the Noodles

Cook the Chinese noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

Step 7: Assemble the Soup

Add the cooked noodles to the pot and stir to combine. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with chopped scallions and cilantro. Serve immediately.

Tips and Variations

  • For a richer broth, you can add 1-2 tablespoons of sesame oil or peanut oil to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  • To make the soup more substantial, you can add sliced mushrooms, bok choy, or other vegetables to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  • For a spicy kick, you can add a few dashes of hot sauce, such as sriracha or hot mustard, to the pot during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  • To make the soup ahead of time, you can cook the beef and broth up to a day in advance. Refrigerate or freeze until ready to reheat and serve.

Conclusion

Chinese beef noodle soup is a dish that’s sure to warm the hearts and bellies of anyone who tries it. With its rich, flavorful broth and tender, juicy beef, this soup is a true comfort food classic. Whether you’re looking for a quick and easy weeknight dinner or a special occasion meal, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your household. So go ahead, give it a try, and experience the delicious flavors of China for yourself!
