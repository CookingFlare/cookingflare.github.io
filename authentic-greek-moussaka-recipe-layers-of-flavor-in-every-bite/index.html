Authentic Greek Moussaka Recipe: Layers of Flavor in Every Bite


Moussaka is a classic dish from Greece that is known for its layers of flavor and hearty ingredients. This traditional recipe combines eggplant, ground lamb, and a rich tomato sauce to create a mouthwatering dish that will transport you to the Mediterranean with every bite. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out, this authentic Greek moussaka recipe is sure to impress your friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced lengthwise
  • 1 pound ground lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 cup red wine
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 3 cups bechamel sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the sliced eggplants on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, then roast in the oven for about 20 minutes, or until golden brown. Set aside.

  2. In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until softened and fragrant.

  3. Add the ground lamb to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat from the skillet.

  4. Stir in the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, dried thyme, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and red wine. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the mixture simmer for about 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. While the lamb mixture is simmering, prepare the bechamel sauce. In a separate saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Stir in flour until well combined, then gradually whisk in milk. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens, then remove from heat. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  6. To assemble the moussaka, start by greasing a large baking dish with olive oil. Place a layer of roasted eggplant slices on the bottom of the dish, followed by a layer of the lamb mixture. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of lamb on top.

  7. Pour the bechamel sauce over the top of the moussaka, ensuring it covers the entire dish. Sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese and breadcrumbs on top.

  8. Bake the moussaka in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes to an hour, or until the top is golden brown and bubbling.

  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This will allow the flavors to meld together and make it easier to slice.

Serving Suggestions

Moussaka is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a side salad or crusty bread. It’s also delicious when served cold the next day, making it a perfect make-ahead dish for gatherings and potlucks.

Conclusion

This authentic Greek moussaka recipe is a labor of love that will transport you to the sun-kissed shores of Greece with every bite. The layers of roasted eggplant, flavorful lamb, and creamy bechamel sauce come together to create a dish that is both comforting and satisfying. Whether you’re looking to impress your dinner guests or simply want to treat yourself to a taste of the Mediterranean, this moussaka recipe is sure to delight. Enjoy!
