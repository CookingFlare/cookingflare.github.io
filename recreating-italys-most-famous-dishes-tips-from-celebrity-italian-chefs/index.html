Recreating Italy's Most Famous Dishes: Tips from Celebrity Italian Chefs


As a culinary enthusiast, waking up to the scent of fresh garlic and home-cooked pasta never gets old. Italy’s rich culinary heritage is an endless source of inspiration and wonder, with its renowned chefs presiding over some of the world’s most renowned restaurants. Today, we dive into the heart of Italy through the eyes of renowned Italian chefs, as they share their tips on recreating some of Italy’s most-loved dishes.

  1. Chef Massimo Bottura’s Parmigiana di Melone

The three Michelin-starred chef, Massimo Bottura, has created a contemporary twist on the traditional Parmigiana di Melone. Instead of using tomatoes, he opts for a modern alternative - a synergy of milk and honey. The unique combination illuminates the subtle sweetness of melon and contrasts with Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, resulting in a symphony of flavors.

  • Sauté thinly sliced leeks in butter, followed by a splash of white wine until tender.
  • In a separate pan, melt a knob of butter with a drizzle of olive oil to deglaze with honey.
  • Add thinly sliced melon and cook until caramelized. Season with salt to bring forth its sweetness.
  • Combine leeks and melon, lining up a layer on a serving dish.
  • Pour over a layer of creamy milk infused with basil, thyme, and mint.
  • Sprinkle grated Parmigiano-Reggiano and breadcrumb mixture on top.
  • Repeat layers until you have a harmonious medley of ingredients; garnish with healthy arugula tuft and crispy Parmesan wafers.
  1. Chef Vincenzo Mandara’s Metamorphosis of Orecchiette

Chef Vincenzo Mandara’s playful take on the Sardinian classic, fregola, lifts this humble pasta dish to another dimension. Not just any common pasta will do; Mandara opts instead for a blend of semolina flour and whole wheat, resulting in a heartier, nuttier orecchiette.

  • Combine 400g semolina and whole wheat flour with a pinch of salt.
  • In three separate bowls, mix together 350ml warm water, an egg, and a small amount of olive oil.
  • Gradually incorporate the wet ingredients into the dry mixture, kneading a few times then resting for ten minutes.
  • Roll out the pasta dough and cut into orecchiette shapes,дале resulting in characteristic ’little ears.'
  • Cook the orecchiette in boiling salted water until al dente, typically around eight minutes.
  • Blend together crushed garlic, a cup of diced tomatoes, a teaspoon of finely chopped parsley, and fresh basil with olive oil for a luscious sauce.
  • Combine the pasta with your preferred protein medley – perhaps grilled squid, lobster or grilled asparagus – alongside vegetables of your choice, then pour over the sauce.
  • Mix everything well, and you’ll be dancing in delight, pensieve of Sardinian earth.
  1. Chef Massimiliano Corà’s Panna Cotta Evolution

For the epitome of Italian simplicity, we turn to dessert – the Panna Cotta. Chef Massimiliano Corà of I Tatti in Florence has mastered a sumptuous version of this beloved classic. By infusing milk with senses-evoking citrus, Corà achieves a gentle, remarkable contrast to the richness of the cream.

  • Pour one cup of fresh cream into a saucepan alongside one cup of filtered water and a splash of vanilla extract.
  • Heat the mixture until the edges start to simmer, but not to a boil; take it off from heat.
  • In a separate bowl, combine three tablespoons of gelatin with one cup of orange juice, giving it time to bloom.
  • Slowly whisk the bloomed gelatin into the warm cream mixture while gently heating on low flame.
  • Pour this mixture into ramekins or molds, then refrigerate until set.
  • To enhance your plate, prepare a pool of Passito utense – a magical concoction of reduction obtained from Protected Designation of Origin fruits.
  • Add a gustatory finale by serving your Panna Cotta with spiced apple jam or a dusting of cocoa power.

With the guidance of Italy’s prestigious chefs, let us enjoy the essence of Italy in our own kitchens. Dolce far niente, the sweet boredom of not doing anything, as you savor these culinary creations. Buon appetito!
