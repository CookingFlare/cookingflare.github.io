Cinnamon Rolls to Cupcakes: Bake Your Way to a Happy Childhood


Remember the warm, stickiness of globs of cinnamon and sugar that would stick to your fingers as you bit into a freshly baked cinnamon roll on a lazy Sunday morning? It’s impossible not to associate memories of childhood warmth and comfort with those delightful aromas of freshly baked treats. Well, I’ve got exciting news: you don’t have to just reminisce! You can relive that joy, with a little twist, by transforming cinnamon rolls into cupcakes. Today, let’s bake our way to a happy childhood, one sweet creation at a time.

First, we’ll start by preparing our cinnamon roll dough, which is the magical base for both our cinnamon rolls and cupcakes.

Cinnamon Roll Dough

Active Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 3 hours (including rise time)

  1. In the bowl of a stand mixer, whisk together 1 1/2 cups of warm milk, 2 tablespoons of sugar, and 2 1/4 teaspoons of instant yeast until the yeast granules have dissolved.

  2. Add 4 cups of all-purpose flour and 2 teaspoons of salt. Attach the dough hook attachment to your stand mixer, and mix on low speed until just combined.

  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together 6 tablespoons of softened unsalted butter, 1/2 cup of brown sugar, and 1 tablespoon of ground cinnamon. Set aside for later.

  4. Add 3 egg yolks to the dough, then slowly begin to mix. Pour in 1/4 cup of vegetable oil, mixing on medium speed until the dough has come together.

  5. Once fully combined, continue mixing on low speed while you slowly add in the butter-sugar-cinnamon mixture. Mix until the dough has pulled away from the sides of the bowl and is smooth and elastic.

  6. Cover the bowl with a damp cloth, and let the dough rise in a warm, draft-free space for about 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.

Transforming Cinnamon Roll Dough into Cupcakes

  1. Once the dough has risen, preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line cupcake pans with parchment liners.

  2. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface, and gently deflate. Roll out the dough to a 16x24-inch rectangle, about 1/4 inch thick.

  3. Spread the remaining 1/4 cup of softened butter over the surface of the dough, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.

  4. Sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of brown sugar and 1 tablespoon of cinnamon evenly over the butter.

  5. Starting at the long end of the dough, roll the dough tightly into a log. Cut the log into 12 even slices, about 2 inches wide each.

  6. Carefully place each roll into a prepared cupcake liner, curve-side up, about 2/3 of the way full. Be careful not to overfill, or your cupcakes will overflow during baking.

  7. Bake the cupcakes for about 20 minutes, or until they are just starting to show golden-brown color at the bottom.

  8. While the cupcakes are baking, whisk together 1 cup of powdered sugar, 2 tablespoons of softened butter, 1 tablespoon of milk, and 1/4 teaspoon of vanilla extract to create a smooth, creamy frosting.

  9. Once the cupcakes are out of the oven, allow them to cool in the pans for about 5 minutes, then transfer them to wire racks to cool completely.

  10. Once fully cooled, frost your cinnamon roll cupcakes with the prepared frosting. You can even sprinkle a little extra cinnamon on top, if you’d like, for an added touch of nostalgia.

  11. Serve your delicious creation to friends and family, or wrap them up and enjoy them as the perfect treat for a walk down memory lane.

Now, don’t you just feel like a kid in a candy store again? Rediscovering the joy of baking can rekindle long-forgotten memories but also create brand new ones. So, gather your ingredients, preheat your oven, and bake away to your heart’s content. Your taste buds will thank you!
