10 Flavorful Fall Recipes for Your Air Fryer


As the crisp autumn air sweeps in and the leaves begin to turn, it’s the perfect time to embrace the warmth and flavors of fall. The season brings with it an array of comforting ingredients like pumpkins, sweet potatoes, apples, and warming spices such as cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. The air fryer, a kitchen gadget that has gained popularity for its ability to create crispy, delicious dishes with less oil, is the ideal tool for making these seasonal ingredients shine. If you’re looking to elevate your fall cooking, here are 10 flavorful fall recipes for your air fryer that will bring the tastes of autumn right to your plate.

1. Air Fryer Pumpkin Fritters

There’s no better way to celebrate the arrival of fall than with pumpkin. These air fryer pumpkin fritters are golden, crispy on the outside, and tender on the inside. The sweet and savory combination of pumpkin, cinnamon, and a hint of nutmeg captures the essence of the season.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Cooking spray

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the pumpkin puree, flour, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, baking powder, salt, egg, and vanilla extract.
  2. Stir until the batter is smooth and well combined.
  3. Preheat the air fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  4. Scoop spoonfuls of the batter and shape them into small fritters.
  5. Spray the air fryer basket with cooking spray, then place the fritters in the basket, making sure they aren’t overcrowded.
  6. Air fry for 10–12 minutes, flipping halfway through until the fritters are golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Serve warm, dusted with powdered sugar, or drizzle with maple syrup.

2. Crispy Air Fryer Sweet Potato Fries

Sweet potatoes are a fall favorite, and making sweet potato fries in the air fryer gives you a crispy, crunchy texture without the need for excessive oil. The natural sweetness of the potatoes, combined with a sprinkle of cinnamon and a dash of paprika, creates the perfect balance of flavors for a side dish or snack.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into fries
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the sweet potato fries in olive oil, cinnamon, paprika, and salt until they are evenly coated.
  3. Place the fries in the air fryer basket in a single layer. You may need to do this in batches.
  4. Air fry for 15–18 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through to ensure even cooking.
  5. Serve immediately with a side of your favorite dipping sauce.

3. Air Fryer Apple Cinnamon Rolls

The smell of apples and cinnamon is the hallmark of fall, and these air fryer apple cinnamon rolls will fill your home with the most delicious fragrance. Flaky, gooey, and perfectly spiced, they make an ideal breakfast or dessert for any autumn morning.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can refrigerated cinnamon roll dough
  • 1 medium apple, peeled and diced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Roll out the cinnamon roll dough and separate it into individual rolls.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the diced apple with brown sugar and cinnamon.
  4. Place a spoonful of the apple mixture in the center of each cinnamon roll and then roll them back up.
  5. Brush the outside of each roll with melted butter.
  6. Place the rolls in the air fryer basket and cook for 8–10 minutes or until golden brown.
  7. Drizzle with the icing provided in the cinnamon roll package and serve warm.

4. Air Fryer Roasted Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Glaze

Brussels sprouts are a delicious fall vegetable that’s easy to cook in the air fryer. The crispness of the outer leaves combined with a tangy balsamic glaze creates a mouthwatering side dish that pairs perfectly with roasted meats or autumn salads.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp honey

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts in olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the Brussels sprouts in the air fryer basket in a single layer.
  4. Air fry for 15–18 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through.
  5. While the sprouts cook, combine the balsamic vinegar and honey in a small saucepan. Simmer over low heat until the glaze thickens (about 5–7 minutes).
  6. Drizzle the balsamic glaze over the roasted Brussels sprouts before serving.

5. Air Fryer Butternut Squash with Maple and Sage

Butternut squash is a fall staple, and the air fryer can cook it to perfection. The sweetness of the squash paired with the earthy flavors of sage and the richness of maple syrup makes for an irresistible side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp dried sage
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, maple syrup, sage, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the cubes in the air fryer basket in a single layer.
  4. Air fry for 18–20 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through.
  5. Serve warm with a drizzle of additional maple syrup if desired.

6. Air Fryer Stuffed Mushrooms with Cream Cheese and Herbs

Stuffed mushrooms are a fantastic appetizer for fall gatherings. These air fryer stuffed mushrooms, filled with a creamy mixture of cream cheese, garlic, and fresh herbs, are rich and savory and cook up in no time.

Ingredients:

  • 12 large mushroom caps, stems removed
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together the cream cheese, garlic, parsley, thyme, Parmesan, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  3. Spoon the cream cheese mixture into each mushroom cap.
  4. Place the stuffed mushrooms in the air fryer basket and cook for 7–10 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and the filling is golden.
  5. Serve warm as an appetizer or side dish.

7. Air Fryer Cinnamon Sugar Donuts

Fall wouldn’t be complete without a little indulgence, and these air fryer cinnamon sugar donuts are the perfect way to satisfy your sweet tooth. They are fluffy, soft, and coated in a delicious cinnamon-sugar mixture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup cinnamon sugar (for coating)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and mix until smooth.
  5. Spoon the batter into a donut pan that fits your air fryer.
  6. Air fry for 8–10 minutes until the donuts are golden and cooked through.
  7. Immediately toss the warm donuts in cinnamon sugar and serve.

8. Air Fryer Maple Bacon Brussels Sprouts

For a savory fall dish with a twist, these maple bacon Brussels sprouts are sure to impress. The sweet maple syrup and salty bacon complement the Brussels sprouts perfectly, making them a favorite side dish for any autumn meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 6 slices bacon, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the Brussels sprouts in maple syrup, salt, and pepper.
  3. Place the Brussels sprouts and chopped bacon in the air fryer basket.
  4. Air fry for 15–18 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through.
  5. Serve hot as a savory side dish.

9. Air Fryer Pumpkin Risotto Balls

These crispy risotto balls filled with pumpkin and cheese are an excellent appetizer or snack for fall gatherings. The pumpkin adds a touch of sweetness, while the creamy risotto and melty cheese create a rich and satisfying bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked risotto
  • 1/2 cup canned pumpkin puree
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil spray

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine the cooked risotto, pumpkin puree, Parmesan, mozzarella, breadcrumbs, egg, salt, and pepper.
  2. Form the mixture into small balls, about the size of a golf ball.
  3. Preheat the air fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  4. Spray the air fryer basket with olive oil and arrange the risotto balls in a single layer.
  5. Air fry for 8–10 minutes, or until golden and crispy on the outside.
  6. Serve warm with marinara sauce or a dip of your choice.

10. Air Fryer Pear and Gorgonzola Salad

The combination of sweet pears and tangy Gorgonzola is a match made in heaven for fall. This salad, with air-fried pears and a drizzle of balsamic glaze, is the perfect starter or light meal for a cozy fall evening.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pears, cored and sliced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups mixed greens
  • 1/4 cup crumbled Gorgonzola cheese
  • 1/4 cup candied pecans
  • Balsamic glaze for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the air fryer to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss the pear slices in olive oil and arrange them in the air fryer basket.
  3. Air fry for 8–10 minutes until the pears are tender and lightly caramelized.
  4. Assemble the salad by combining the mixed greens, crumbled Gorgonzola, candied pecans, and air-fried pears.
  5. Drizzle with balsamic glaze and serve.

These 10 fall-inspired air fryer recipes are the perfect way to bring the flavors of autumn into your kitchen. Whether you’re craving something sweet, savory, or a bit of both, the air fryer makes cooking fall ingredients a breeze. Try these recipes for yourself and enjoy the season’s best produce with ease and deliciousness!
