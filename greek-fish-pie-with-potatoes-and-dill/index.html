Greek Fish Pie with Potatoes and Dill


With its vibrant flavors and wholesome ingredients, Greek cuisine offers a delightful blend of simplicity and decadence. One dish that perfectly embodies this is the Greek Fish Pie with Potatoes and Dill—a flavorful and comforting recipe that brings the rustic charm of Mediterranean cooking straight to your kitchen. Infused with the herbal brightness of fresh dill, creamy potatoes, and tender fish, this unique pie is sure to be a showstopper at any meal.

In this post, we will guide you in crafting a Greek-inspired fish pie that’s both satisfying and nutritious, highlighting traditional techniques and modern tips to ensure the best results. Whether you’re entertaining guests or preparing a hearty dinner for your family, this dish promises to transport your taste buds to a cozy Greek taverna.

The Essence of Greek Fish Pie

Greek fish pie celebrates oceanic flavors combined with the simplicity of locally sourced produce. This dish is a testament to the way Greek cuisine nurtures harmony between land and sea. Dill plays a starring role here, imparting an aromatic freshness that complements the buttery richness of fresh fish and creamy mashed potatoes.

Unlike other fish pies, the Greek version heavily relies on fresh herbs, olive oil, and natural ingredients to create its distinct character. The balance of flavors is precisely what makes this dish so special. The focus is on enhancing the flavors of the fish without overwhelming it, making every forkful taste fresh and comforting.

Ingredients You’ll Need

This recipe calls for straightforward, high-quality ingredients that meld perfectly into the Mediterranean flavor profile. For optimal results, using fresh, natural produce and sustainably-sourced fish will elevate your cooking experience. Gather the following ingredients to make your Greek Fish Pie:

For the Fish Filling:

  • 1 pound (450g) of white fish fillets (such as cod, halibut, or haddock)
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard (optional)
  • 1 cup (240ml) of whole milk or fish stock
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh dill, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Potato Topping:

  • 1.5 pounds (680g) of potatoes (starchy varieties like Yukon Gold or Russet work best)
  • ½ cup (120ml) of heavy cream or milk
  • 3 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Additional dill for garnish

Optional Additions:

  • ½ cup of shredded Greek cheese, such as kefalotyri or feta
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano for an added Mediterranean touch

Preparing the Dish

Crafting this Greek Fish Pie is a straightforward process that includes preparing the filling, making the mashed potato topping, and assembling everything into a satisfying pie. Let’s break it down step by step.

Step 1: Prepare the Potatoes

Begin by peeling and chopping your potatoes into evenly-sized chunks. Place them in a large pot, cover with cold water, and add a generous pinch of salt. Bring to a boil and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender.

Once cooked, drain the water and return the potatoes to the pot. Mash them thoroughly using a potato masher or a fork until smooth. Add the butter, olive oil, and cream, stirring well to ensure the topping is creamy and well-blended. Season to taste with salt and black pepper, and set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Fish Filling

While the potatoes are boiling, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onion, cooking gently until they soften and release their aroma—this should take about 5 minutes.

Next, add your fish fillets to the skillet. Cook for 4-5 minutes on each side until the fish is firm, opaque, and slightly golden. Flake the fish into chunky pieces using a fork or spatula.

Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the milk or fish stock to the skillet, followed by lemon zest, lemon juice, mustard (if using), and freshly chopped dill. Stir everything together gently, allowing the flavors to mingle. Season the mixture with salt and pepper, tasting and adjusting as you go.

Let the filling simmer for another 3-5 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly and the fish absorbs the flavors. Remove from heat.

Step 3: Assemble the Pie

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a deep baking dish or pie dish, spread the fish mixture evenly across the bottom. If desired, sprinkle shredded Greek cheese or a small pinch of oregano on top for an extra layer of flavor.

Carefully spoon the mashed potatoes over the fish filling, spreading it with a spatula to create an even layer. Use a fork to make decorative ridges or swirls on the potato topping, which will crisp up nicely as it bakes.

Step 4: Bake and Serve

Place the assembled pie in the oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top turns lightly golden and the edges begin to bubble. Remove from the oven and allow the pie to cool slightly before serving.

Garnish with additional dill for a fresh touch and serve warm alongside a crisp green salad or roasted vegetables.

Bonus Serving Idea

For a rustic Greek experience, pair your fish pie with toasted pita bread and tzatziki sauce. These Mediterranean staples complement the dish beautifully, adding crunch and creaminess to every bite.

Tips for Success

  • Choosing the Right Fish: Opt for mild, flaky white fish varieties like cod, halibut, or haddock. Sustainable seafood options ensure the integrity of this dish’s flavor and its environmental impact.
  • Make Ahead: You can prepare the filling and topping in advance, assembling and baking the pie right before you’re ready to eat. This makes it a perfect dish for busy evenings or entertaining guests.
  • Season Generously: Taste as you go, keeping in mind that proper seasoning is key to highlighting the brightness of the herbs and the richness of the fish.
  • Boost Flavors: Don’t shy away from adding extras like feta cheese or pitted Kalamata olives to the recipe. These additions provide an even deeper nod to Greek traditions.

Why You’ll Love This Dish

Greek Fish Pie with Potatoes and Dill is more than just a recipe—it’s a celebration of Greek culinary heritage. The dish is hearty yet refreshing, rich yet balanced. Fresh dill and lemon give it a zingy vibrancy, while the creamy mashed potatoes and tender fish provide comfort with every bite.

Its versatility makes it ideal for any occasion, whether you’re planning a family dinner, meal prepping for the week, or hosting a Mediterranean-themed gathering. Packed with omega-3 fatty acids from the fish and nutrients from the potatoes, this pie is also as nourishing as it is delicious.

Conclusion

Transport yourself to the heart of the Mediterranean with this Greek Fish Pie recipe. It’s the ultimate marriage of fresh, wholesome ingredients and masterful flavors that will leave you craving another helping. With creamy potatoes, flaky fish, and the distinctive brightness of dill, this pie is bound to earn a permanent spot in your repertoire of favorite meals.

Take a culinary escape to Greece and savor the joy of crafting this classic dish in your own kitchen. Share it with friends, family, or simply savor it privately—you’ll find yourself captivated by every bite!
