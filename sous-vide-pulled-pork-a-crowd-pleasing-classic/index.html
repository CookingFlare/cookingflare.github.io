Sous Vide Pulled Pork: A Crowd-Pleasing Classic


If you’re a fan of tender, juicy, and flavorful pulled pork, then you’re in for a treat. Today, we’ll be diving into the world of sous vide cooking and exploring how it can take your pulled pork game to the next level. Sous vide, which means “under vacuum” in French, is a cooking technique that involves sealing food in a vacuum-sealed bag and cooking it in a water bath at a precise temperature. This method ensures even cooking and results in incredibly tender and flavorful meat.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds pork shoulder
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce

Instructions

  1. Preheat your water bath to 165°F (74°C).

  2. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, black pepper, dried thyme, and dried oregano. Mix well to create a flavorful dry rub.

  3. Rub the dry rub all over the pork shoulder, making sure to coat it evenly.

  4. Place the seasoned pork shoulder in a large vacuum-sealed bag, ensuring that it is completely sealed.

  5. Submerge the bag in the preheated water bath and cook for 24 hours. The long cooking time will result in incredibly tender meat that falls apart effortlessly.

  6. Once the pork shoulder has finished cooking, carefully remove it from the bag and transfer it to a cutting board.

  7. Using two forks, shred the pork shoulder into bite-sized pieces. The meat should be so tender that it practically falls apart with minimal effort.

  8. In a large skillet over medium heat, warm the barbecue sauce until heated through.

  9. Add the shredded pork to the skillet and toss until it is evenly coated with the barbecue sauce.

  10. Serve the sous vide pulled pork on your favorite buns or rolls, and garnish with coleslaw, pickles, or any other toppings of your choice.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra smoky flavor, you can add a teaspoon of liquid smoke to the dry rub before seasoning the pork shoulder.

  • If you prefer a spicier pulled pork, you can add a teaspoon of cayenne pepper to the dry rub.

  • Sous vide pulled pork can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it in a skillet or microwave before serving.

  • This recipe can easily be doubled or halved to accommodate different serving sizes.

  • If you don’t have a vacuum sealer, you can use a heavy-duty freezer bag and remove as much air as possible before sealing it tightly.

Now that you have the secret to perfectly tender and flavorful pulled pork, it’s time to gather your ingredients and give sous vide cooking a try. Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue or simply want to elevate your weeknight dinner, this crowd-pleasing classic is sure to impress. So fire up your water bath, seal that pork shoulder, and get ready for a melt-in-your-mouth experience like no other. Enjoy!
