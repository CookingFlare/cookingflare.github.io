Savory and Succulent Thanksgiving Ham Recipes


Thanksgiving is a time for gathering with loved ones, expressing gratitude, and indulging in delicious food. While turkey often takes center stage on this festive occasion, there’s no denying the allure of a perfectly cooked ham. With its savory flavors and succulent texture, ham has the power to elevate your Thanksgiving feast to new heights. In this blog post, we’ll explore three mouthwatering ham recipes that will leave your guests craving for more.

1. Honey Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in fully cooked ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1 cup of honey
  • 1/2 cup of Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of ground cloves

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
  2. Place the ham on a rack in a roasting pan, fat side up.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the honey, Dijon mustard, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, and ground cloves until well combined.
  4. Brush the glaze mixture generously over the entire surface of the ham.
  5. Tent the ham loosely with aluminum foil and bake for 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).
  6. Remove the foil during the last 30 minutes of baking to allow the glaze to caramelize.
  7. Let the ham rest for 10-15 minutes before carving. Serve with the remaining glaze on the side.

2. Pineapple-Glazed Ham

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in fully cooked ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1 can of pineapple rings in juice
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
  2. Place the ham on a rack in a roasting pan, fat side up.
  3. Drain the pineapple rings, reserving the juice for later use.
  4. In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and ground cinnamon.
  5. Brush the glaze mixture over the entire surface of the ham.
  6. Arrange the pineapple rings on top of the ham, securing them with toothpicks if necessary.
  7. Pour the reserved pineapple juice into the roasting pan.
  8. Tent the ham loosely with aluminum foil and bake for 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).
  9. Remove the foil during the last 30 minutes of baking to allow the glaze to caramelize.
  10. Let the ham rest for 10-15 minutes before carving. Serve with the pineapple rings and pan juices.

3. Maple-Glazed Ham with Mustard Crust

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in fully cooked ham (8-10 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup of maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup of grainy mustard
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
  2. Place the ham on a rack in a roasting pan, fat side up.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, grainy mustard, Dijon mustard, brown sugar, apple cider vinegar, and dried thyme until well combined.
  4. Brush the glaze mixture generously over the entire surface of the ham.
  5. Tent the ham loosely with aluminum foil and bake for 15 minutes per pound, or until the internal temperature reaches 140°F (60°C).
  6. Remove the foil during the last 30 minutes of baking to allow the glaze to caramelize and form a delicious mustard crust.
  7. Let the ham rest for 10-15 minutes before carving. Serve with the remaining glaze on the side.

These three savory and succulent ham recipes are sure to impress your family and friends this Thanksgiving. Whether you opt for the sweet and tangy Honey Glazed Ham, the tropical twist of Pineapple-Glazed Ham, or the rich flavors of Maple-Glazed Ham with Mustard Crust, your taste buds will thank you. So, go ahead and make your Thanksgiving feast truly unforgettable with a delicious ham as the centerpiece. Happy cooking and happy Thanksgiving!
