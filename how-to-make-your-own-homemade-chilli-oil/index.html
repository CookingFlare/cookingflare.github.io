How to Make Your Own Homemade Chilli Oil


There’s something special about a jar of vibrant, aromatic chili oil. Whether you’re drizzling it over soups, tossing it with noodles, or using it as the base for a marinade, homemade chili oil delivers a bold kick of flavor that elevates your dishes. Perfectly balanced between heat and richness, this condiment is not just a spice enhancer—it’s a game-changer for any home cook looking to experience authentic depth in their recipes.

Creating your own chili oil at home is easier than most people think. It requires basic ingredients, a few kitchen tools, and a touch of patience. Best of all, you’ll have the freedom to customize the flavor and spice level according to your preferences. Ready to fire up your cooking game? Let’s dive into the step-by-step process of crafting a perfect batch of homemade chili oil.

What Makes Chili Oil So Special?

Chili oil is a cornerstone of countless cuisines worldwide, known for adding dynamic complexity to meals. While it’s especially popular in Chinese, Thai, and Mexican dishes, this fiery oil has gained traction in kitchens everywhere.

The key to its magic lies in the infusion process. By heating a neutral cooking oil and pouring it over dried spices, garlic, and chilies, you allow their natural flavors to merge. The oil captures every nuance—the smoky heat, earthy complexity, and pungent warmth—creating an irresistible condiment that amplifies even the simplest recipes.

Step-by-Step Guide to Homemade Chili Oil

Before we jump into the recipe, let’s cover the basics. This chili oil recipe is incredibly versatile, and you can adjust it based on your flavor profile preferences. Want more heat? Choose spicier chili flakes. Craving a more aromatic version? Add additional spices like star anise or cinnamon. Now, let’s get started!

Ingredients You’ll Need:

Here’s what you’ll need for your homemade chili oil:

  • 2 cups neutral oil (such as vegetable, canola, or avocado oil)
  • 1/4 cup dried chili flakes (adjust to your preferred spice level)
  • 2 tablespoons Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1 piece star anise (optional, for extra aroma)
  • 3 garlic cloves, finely sliced
  • 1 small piece of ginger, sliced thinly

Each ingredient plays a unique role in the finished product. Neutral oil is the carrier, dried chili flakes provide heat, garlic and ginger deepen the complexity, and sesame seeds add texture and nuttiness. Feel free to experiment with additional aromatic spices if you want to make this chili oil your own.

Equipment You’ll Need:

  1. A small saucepan
  2. A heatproof bowl
  3. A fine mesh strainer (if desired)
  4. Jar or container with an airtight lid for storage

Making Homemade Chili Oil

Follow these steps carefully to create a batch of chili oil that will leave friends and family asking for your secret recipe.

Step 1: Prepare Your Dry Ingredients

Start by preparing your chili flakes, sesame seeds, salt, star anise, garlic slices, and ginger. Place them into your heatproof bowl. This is where the final infusion will take place, so ensure the bowl is large enough to handle a generous splash of hot oil.

For optimal flavor, toast your sesame seeds in a dry pan for a few minutes until they’re golden and aromatic. This adds an extra layer of nuttiness to your chili oil.

Step 2: Heat the Oil

Pour the neutral oil into a small saucepan and heat it over medium heat. You’re aiming for the oil to reach about 200°F (93°C)—hot enough to extract flavors but not so hot that it burns them.

While heating, keep an eye on the oil to ensure it doesn’t reach excessive temperatures. A thermometer can come in handy here, though you can also rely on visual cues. The oil is ready when you see slight shimmering but no smoking.

Step 3: Infuse Aromatics in the Oil

Once the oil begins to shimmer, lower the heat and carefully add your sliced garlic and ginger to the saucepan. Let them release their fragrance as the oil gently infuses their flavors. Keep the heat low to prevent burning; scorched garlic can ruin the taste of your oil.

Cook for about 5 minutes or until the garlic is lightly golden and the ginger has softened. At this point, remove the saucepan from the heat to ensure the aromatics don’t overcook.

Step 4: Combine Hot Oil with Spice Mixture

Carefully pour the hot oil into the heatproof bowl containing your dried chili flakes and other dry ingredients. As the oil cascades over them, you’ll witness an explosion of aroma—this is the magic moment where the flavors truly come together.

Stir the mixture gently to evenly coat everything in oil. You may hear a pleasant sizzling sound—that’s the heat activating the spices and releasing their essence into the oil.

Step 5: Allow the Oil to Cool

Let the chili oil sit undisturbed for about 30 minutes. This cooling period allows all the flavors to meld and deepen.

If you prefer a smooth chili oil, strain out the solids using a fine mesh strainer before transferring it to a jar. However, leaving the garlic and spices in the oil will give you a richer texture and added bursts of flavor each time you use it.

How to Store Your Chili Oil

Transfer your cooled chili oil into a clean, airtight jar or container. Store it in a dark, cool spot, such as your pantry or a cupboard. Homemade chili oil can last up to 2–3 months, but most home cooks find themselves needing a fresh batch within weeks because it’s so irresistible.

Tips for Customizing Your Chili Oil

One of the joys of making chili oil is experimenting until you create the perfect blend for your tastes. Here are some ideas for customization:

  • Choose your chili flakes: Use mild chili flakes for a subtle kick or fiery Sichuan pepper flakes for extra heat. Mix chili varieties to fine-tune the spice level.
  • Add whole spices: Star anise, cinnamon sticks, or bay leaves will introduce new layers of complexity.
  • Incorporate herbs: Toss in a sprig of fresh rosemary or thyme for herbal undertones.
  • Try different oils: While neutral oil is traditional, sesame oil can add a warm, nutty flavor to your chili oil.

Ways to Use Your Chili Oil

Once you’ve mastered this recipe, you’ll quickly realize how versatile chili oil truly is. Here are just a few ways to use it in your cooking:

  1. Drizzle over soups: Liven up a bowl of ramen, wonton soup, or pho with a spicy splash.
  2. Toss with noodles: Mix with soy sauce and vinegar for a quick, flavorful noodle dressing.
  3. Dip for dumplings: Pair chili oil with soy sauce for an unbeatable dipping sauce.
  4. Elevate marinades: Use it with other seasonings to marinate chicken, pork, or tofu.
  5. Stir into stir-fries: Boost the flavor of your favorite vegetable or protein stir-fry dishes.
  6. Sprinkle on pizza: It may sound unconventional, but chili oil adds magic to pizza toppings.

Conclusion

There’s no denying that homemade chili oil is a culinary treasure. With its bold flavor, captivating aroma, and endless versatility, it’s the perfect way to spice up meals and spark creativity in your cooking routine. Once you try making your own batch, store-bought versions simply won’t compare.

Start crafting chili oil that suits your personal taste—whether smoky, mild, or vibrantly hot—and enjoy the satisfaction of knowing you’ve created a condiment that transforms meals from ordinary to extraordinary. The best part? The process is satisfying, simple, and invites experimentation. Happy cooking!
