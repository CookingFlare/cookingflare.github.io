Light and Luscious: Springtime Spinach and Strawberry Salad


As the last wisps of winter’s chill dissipate, spring’s sweetness begins to unfold, bringing with it an explosion of fresh flavors and vibrant colors. One of the most iconic and refreshing salads of the season is the humble spinach and strawberry combination. When done right, this dish is a symphony of textures, temperatures, and tastes that will leave you craving more.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups fresh baby spinach leaves
  • 1 pint fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped pecans
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves (optional)

Instructions

Begin by washing and drying the spinach leaves. You want them to be crisp and free of excess moisture, as this will help them hold their texture throughout the salad. Next, slice the strawberries into thin wedges, taking care to remove any seeds or stems. You want the fruit to be the star of the show, so aim for uniform pieces that will distribute evenly throughout the salad.

In a large bowl, combine the spinach leaves, sliced strawberries, crumbled feta cheese, and chopped pecans. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, white wine vinegar, and Dijon mustard until smooth. Season the dressing with salt and pepper to taste, then pour it over the salad ingredients.

Toss the salad gently but thoroughly, taking care not to bruise the delicate spinach leaves. You want the flavors to meld together, but still retain their individual characters. If using fresh mint leaves, sprinkle them over the salad just before serving, allowing the bright green color to add a pop of contrast to the dish.

Tips and Variations

One of the most important aspects of this salad is the quality of the ingredients. Fresh, locally sourced spinach and strawberries will make all the difference in the world. If you’re having trouble finding baby spinach, you can substitute with regular spinach leaves, just be sure to chop them into smaller pieces to maintain the salad’s texture.

For an added crunch, try adding some toasted almonds or pistachios to the salad. You can also substitute the feta cheese with crumbled goat cheese or ricotta salata for a creamier flavor.

If you prefer a lighter dressing, you can reduce the amount of olive oil or substitute with a neutral-tasting oil like canola or grapeseed. Alternatively, you can add a splash of lemon juice or a pinch of red pepper flakes to give the dressing a bit more zing.

Conclusion

Springtime spinach and strawberry salad is a masterclass in simplicity and elegance. By combining the earthy sweetness of spinach with the tartness of strawberries, you create a dish that’s both refreshing and satisfying. With its perfect balance of textures and flavors, this salad is sure to become a staple in your repertoire, whether you’re entertaining guests or simply looking for a healthy and delicious meal. So go ahead, give it a try, and let the flavors of springtime transport you to a world of culinary bliss.
