Bring the Taste of France to Your Kitchen with These Classic Quiche Recipes


Are you craving a taste of France but can’t hop on a plane? Don’t worry! We’ve got you covered. Today, we’re bringing the flavors of France right into your kitchen with these classic quiche recipes. Quiche, a savory open-faced pie, is a staple in French cuisine and is loved for its rich and creamy filling encased in a buttery crust. So, grab your apron and let’s get cooking!

Classic Quiche Lorraine

We’ll start with the iconic Classic Quiche Lorraine. This recipe is simple yet bursting with flavor. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 6 slices of bacon, cooked and crumbled
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • A pinch of nutmeg

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the pre-made pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and crimp the edges.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, heavy cream, salt, pepper, and nutmeg until well combined.
  4. Sprinkle the bacon and shredded Gruyere cheese evenly over the pie crust.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the bacon and cheese, ensuring it’s evenly distributed.
  6. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until the quiche is set and golden brown on top.
  7. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Spinach and Mushroom Quiche

For a vegetarian twist on the classic, try this Spinach and Mushroom Quiche. It’s packed with earthy flavors and a healthy dose of greens. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Swiss cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sliced mushrooms, and sauté until they are tender and lightly browned.
  3. Add the chopped spinach to the skillet and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. Place the pre-made pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and crimp the edges.
  5. Spread the cooked mushroom, onion, and spinach mixture evenly over the pie crust. Sprinkle the shredded Swiss cheese on top.
  6. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and black pepper until well combined.
  7. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables and cheese in the pie crust.
  8. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and lightly browned.
  9. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Ham and Cheese Quiche

Last but not least, we have the classic Ham and Cheese Quiche. This recipe is a crowd-pleaser and perfect for any meal of the day. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 1 cup diced ham
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the pre-made pie crust in a 9-inch pie dish and crimp the edges.
  3. Sprinkle the diced ham and shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the pie crust.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and black pepper until well combined.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the ham and cheese in the pie crust, ensuring it’s evenly distributed.
  6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until the quiche is set and lightly browned.
  7. Allow the quiche to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Bon Appétit!

There you have it - three classic quiche recipes that will transport you to the beautiful cafes of France. Whether you choose the rich and smoky Quiche Lorraine, the healthy and vibrant Spinach and Mushroom Quiche, or the timeless Ham and Cheese Quiche, each bite will make you feel like you’re strolling along the streets of Paris. So, put on some French music, pour yourself a glass of wine, and enjoy these delicious quiches in the comfort of your own home. Bon appétit!
