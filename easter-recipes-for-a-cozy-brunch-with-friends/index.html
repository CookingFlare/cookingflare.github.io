Easter Recipes for a Cozy Brunch with Friends


Easter brings the perfect opportunity to gather loved ones and celebrate the joys of spring. A cozy brunch is the ideal way to achieve this, offering a delightful spread filled with a mix of sweet and savory dishes sure to please all palates. From fluffy baked goods to vibrant egg dishes and refreshing beverages, an Easter brunch spread is all about indulging in seasonal flavors that create warm and lasting memories. Let’s dive into a collection of recipes that will bring life and love to your Easter table.

1. Asparagus and Goat Cheese Frittata

This light and fluffy frittata is perfect for showcasing the fresh flavors of spring. Asparagus and creamy goat cheese come together to yield a crowd-pleasing dish.

Ingredients:

  • 8 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and chopped into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup crumbled goat cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh chives

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  3. Heat olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the asparagus and sauté for 3–4 minutes, until just tender.
  4. Pour the egg mixture over the asparagus, spreading it evenly in the skillet. Sprinkle goat cheese and chopped chives on top.
  5. Cook on the stovetop for 2–3 minutes until the edges begin to set, then transfer the skillet to the oven.
  6. Bake for 10–12 minutes until the frittata is puffed and the center is fully set.
  7. Let cool slightly before slicing into wedges and serving.

2. Glazed Honey Ham Sliders

Who can resist bite-sized sliders? These easy-to-make sandwiches bring sweet and smoky flavors to your Easter table.

Ingredients:

  • 12 slider rolls
  • 1 pound thinly sliced honey-glazed ham
  • 6 slices Swiss cheese
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Without separating the rolls, slice them horizontally. Place the bottom half of the rolls in the prepared dish.
  3. Layer the ham and Swiss cheese over the bottom section of the rolls. Top with the upper half of the rolls.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together melted butter, Dijon mustard, honey, and poppy seeds. Pour the mixture evenly over the sliders.
  5. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 15 minutes, then remove the foil and bake for an additional 5–7 minutes until the tops are golden brown.
  6. Serve warm, and watch them disappear!

3. Carrot Cake Pancakes with Cream Cheese Drizzle

Channel the flavors of an Easter classic—carrot cake—into this pancake recipe. With a touch of sweetness and warming spices, they’ll quickly become your brunch favorite.

Ingredients for Pancakes:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup grated carrots

Ingredients for Cream Cheese Drizzle:

  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 2–3 tablespoons milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, salt, and brown sugar.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, milk, applesauce, and vanilla extract.
  3. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Fold in the grated carrots.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease it. Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake onto the skillet. Cook until bubbles form and the edges look dry, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown.
  5. To make the drizzle, beat the cream cheese with powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla until smooth. Adjust the thickness with milk if necessary.
  6. Serve the pancakes with the cream cheese drizzle draped on top.

4. Spring Pea and Mint Salad

Balance your brunch spread with a refreshing salad. This vibrant and crunchy dish brings an earthy sweetness to your menu.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen peas (if frozen, cooked and cooled)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the peas, fresh mint, and feta cheese.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to coat.
  4. Serve as a light and refreshing side dish.

5. Lemon Poppy Seed Mini Donuts

No Easter brunch is complete without a touch of sweetness. These lemon poppy seed mini donuts are tangy, tender, and bite-sized.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/3 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a mini donut pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix buttermilk, egg, butter, and lemon zest.
  4. Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined. Fold in the poppy seeds.
  5. Spoon the batter into the donut wells, filling each about 2/3 of the way full.
  6. Bake for 8–10 minutes, or until the donuts spring back when lightly touched.
  7. Let cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack. Dust with powdered sugar before serving.

6. Sparkling Grapefruit Mocktail

Add a refreshing sparkle to your Easter brunch with this easy mocktail. It’s a bright, slightly tangy drink that everyone can enjoy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups fresh grapefruit juice
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1 tablespoon honey (optional, for sweetness)
  • 2 cups sparkling water
  • Ice
  • Grapefruit wedges and fresh mint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a pitcher, combine the grapefruit juice, orange juice, and honey if using. Stir well to blend the flavors.
  2. Add ice to glasses and pour the juice mixture about halfway into each glass.
  3. Top off with sparkling water and stir gently.
  4. Garnish with a wedge of grapefruit and a sprig of mint.

Easter brunch is all about creating a warm and cheerful ambiance with dishes that feel special yet attainable. These recipes bring together simplicity and elegance, perfect for sharing with friends and family. Whether it’s the creamy frittata, indulgent pancakes, or zingy mocktails, each dish serves as a symbol of spring’s renewal and joy. So, gather your loved ones, set the table with your favorite Easter décor, and delight in this comforting and celebratory feast.

Happy Easter! May your brunch be filled with laughter, love, and full plates!
