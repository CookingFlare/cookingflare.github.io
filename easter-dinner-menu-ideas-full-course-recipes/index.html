Easter Dinner Menu Ideas: Full Course Recipes


Easter is one of the most celebrated holidays in the world, and it brings together families and friends to share a special meal. Whether you are hosting a traditional Easter dinner or want to create a fresh, modern twist on classic recipes, a carefully curated menu can elevate the entire experience. In this blog post, we will guide you through a full-course Easter dinner, offering a variety of recipes for appetizers, main dishes, sides, and desserts. This menu is designed to satisfy diverse tastes while staying true to the seasonal flavors that define Easter.

1. Easter Dinner Appetizer Ideas

An Easter meal should begin with light and delicious appetizers that spark the appetite while setting the tone for the rest of the meal. These appetizers incorporate fresh ingredients that celebrate the season.

Deviled Eggs with a Twist

Deviled eggs are a classic appetizer, but adding a creative twist can make them more exciting. Here’s how you can take the traditional recipe to the next level:

Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh chives for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the eggs: Place eggs in a large saucepan, cover with cold water, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes. Once cooked, transfer to an ice bath to cool.
  2. Peel the eggs and slice them in half lengthwise. Remove the yolks and place them in a bowl.
  3. Mash the yolks with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, vinegar, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper until smooth.
  4. Spoon or pipe the mixture back into the egg whites and garnish with fresh chives and a sprinkle of smoked paprika.

These deviled eggs will provide a rich, creamy start to your meal and impress your guests with a smoky flavor.

Spring Vegetable Crudité Platter

Celebrate the bounty of spring with a fresh, vibrant vegetable platter. A variety of colorful vegetables paired with a tangy dipping sauce will provide a healthy and refreshing appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • Carrot sticks
  • Celery stalks
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Cucumber slices
  • Radishes
  • Bell pepper strips

Dipping Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Arrange the fresh vegetables on a large platter in a visually appealing way.
  2. For the dipping sauce, mix together sour cream, mayonnaise, lemon juice, fresh dill, salt, and pepper.
  3. Serve the vegetable platter alongside the dipping sauce for a light, seasonal appetizer that everyone will enjoy.

2. Easter Dinner Main Course Recipes

For the main course, Easter traditionally calls for lamb, ham, or a hearty roast. Here, we will share two main course recipes, one featuring lamb and another with ham, so you can choose the perfect option based on your guests’ preferences.

Herb-Crusted Rack of Lamb

A rack of lamb is a showstopper for any Easter dinner. This dish is simple to prepare yet incredibly flavorful, with a herb crust that enhances the natural tenderness of the lamb.

Ingredients:

  • 1 rack of lamb (8 ribs)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs (preferably panko)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Season the rack of lamb with salt and pepper. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat, and sear the lamb on all sides until golden brown (about 2-3 minutes per side).
  3. In a small bowl, mix together minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, Dijon mustard, and breadcrumbs.
  4. Brush the lamb with Dijon mustard and press the breadcrumb mixture onto the lamb to create a thick herb crust.
  5. Roast the lamb in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes for medium-rare (adjust cooking time for your preferred doneness).
  6. Let the lamb rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

The herb-crusted rack of lamb pairs beautifully with roasted vegetables and a light, tangy sauce.

Glazed Easter Ham

Ham is another popular choice for Easter dinner, and with the right glaze, it can be transformed into a sweet and savory masterpiece.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in ham (6-8 pounds)
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C).
  2. Score the ham in a diamond pattern, cutting about 1/4 inch deep into the surface.
  3. In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the brown sugar, Dijon mustard, apple cider vinegar, honey, cloves, and cinnamon. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the mixture is smooth. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Place the ham in a roasting pan and brush with the glaze. Roast the ham for about 2 hours, basting it every 30 minutes with the glaze.
  5. Once the ham is heated through and caramelized on the outside, remove from the oven and let it rest for 10 minutes before carving.

The sweet and tangy glaze on the ham provides a perfect contrast to the savory richness of the meat.

3. Side Dishes for Easter Dinner

Side dishes are an important part of any Easter meal, as they help balance out the rich flavors of the main course. Here are a couple of side dishes that will complement your Easter dinner perfectly.

Roasted Spring Vegetables

Spring vegetables like asparagus, carrots, and parsnips are at their peak during Easter and make for a delicious, seasonal side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 6 medium carrots, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 4 parsnips, peeled and cut into sticks
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Arrange the vegetables on a baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with thyme, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roast the vegetables for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized, tossing them halfway through cooking.
  4. Once roasted, sprinkle with lemon zest for a fresh, bright finish.

Creamy Garlic Mashed Potatoes

No Easter dinner is complete without mashed potatoes, and this creamy version infused with garlic will be a hit at your table.

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the potatoes and garlic in a large pot and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil and cook until the potatoes are tender (about 15 minutes).
  2. Drain the potatoes and garlic, then return them to the pot. Add butter and mash until smooth.
  3. Slowly add the heavy cream while continuing to mash the potatoes to your desired consistency.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and serve warm.

4. Easter Dinner Desserts

Easter desserts should reflect the freshness of spring, with light flavors and vibrant colors. These two desserts will end your Easter dinner on a sweet note.

Lemon Cream Pie

A tangy lemon cream pie is the perfect way to finish your Easter meal. The combination of buttery crust, creamy filling, and citrus zest is refreshing and delightful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-baked pie crust
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, sugar, lemon juice, lemon zest, vanilla extract, and salt until the mixture thickens.
  2. Pour the mixture into the pre-baked pie crust and smooth the top.
  3. Refrigerate the pie for at least 4 hours to allow it to set.
  4. Garnish with fresh whipped cream and extra lemon zest before serving.

Carrot Cake with Cream Cheese Frosting

Carrot cake is another Easter favorite, and this version is perfectly moist and spiced, with a creamy frosting that adds a rich contrast.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts

Cream Cheese Frosting:

  • 8 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 3-4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour a 9-inch cake pan.
  2. In a bowl, combine flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together oil, sugar, and eggs. Add the grated carrots and walnuts and mix until incorporated.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until smooth.
  5. Pour the batter into the cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Let the cake cool completely before frosting with the cream cheese frosting.
  7. For the frosting, beat together the cream cheese and butter until smooth. Gradually add powdered sugar and vanilla extract, beating until creamy.
  8. Frost the cooled cake and garnish with extra walnuts if desired.

Conclusion

Easter dinner is a time for families and friends to gather and enjoy delicious food together. With these full-course recipes, you can create a memorable meal that celebrates the flavors of the season. From appetizers like deviled eggs and vegetable platters to main courses featuring lamb or ham, and finishing off with sweet desserts like lemon cream pie and carrot cake, this Easter dinner menu will leave everyone satisfied and smiling. Enjoy the process of cooking and the joy of sharing these dishes with your loved ones!
