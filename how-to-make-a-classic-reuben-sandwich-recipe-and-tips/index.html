How to Make a Classic Reuben Sandwich: Recipe and Tips


The Reuben sandwich is an iconic deli classic beloved by many. It’s the perfect blend of salty, savory, tangy, and creamy flavors all wrapped up in a warm, crispy, toasted sandwich. Whether you’re a fan of hearty deli sandwiches or a newcomer to this American classic, learning how to make a Reuben sandwich at home is both rewarding and delicious. In this post, we will guide you through the entire process, from selecting the best ingredients to assembling your perfect Reuben. Plus, we’ll share tips on making it even better with a few twists.

What is a Reuben Sandwich?

A Reuben sandwich is a hot sandwich that typically features corned beef, Swiss cheese, sauerkraut, and Russian or Thousand Island dressing, all piled between two slices of rye bread. It’s a fusion of flavors that has won hearts across the United States, especially in delis. The bread is grilled or toasted to perfection, offering a crispy texture that contrasts nicely with the savory and creamy fillings.

The origins of the Reuben sandwich are debated. Some attribute it to Reuben Kulakofsky, a Jewish immigrant, while others argue it was created by a New York City deli owner. Regardless of its roots, the Reuben sandwich has become a staple in American cuisine.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make a classic Reuben sandwich, you’ll need to gather the following ingredients:

  • Corned beef: The star of the sandwich, corned beef is traditionally used. You can find pre-sliced corned beef at your local deli or prepare it from scratch.
  • Swiss cheese: Swiss cheese adds a smooth, mild flavor that balances the other ingredients perfectly.
  • Sauerkraut: This fermented cabbage gives the Reuben its signature tang. Be sure to drain it well to avoid a soggy sandwich.
  • Russian dressing or Thousand Island dressing: Both dressings are creamy and tangy, with Russian dressing usually being a little spicier. The dressing is a key component that ties all the flavors together.
  • Rye bread: A sturdy rye bread with a caraway seed flavor is the traditional choice for a Reuben sandwich. You can choose between light or dark rye depending on your preference.
  • Butter: For grilling the sandwich to perfection, butter is essential. It helps create that golden-brown, crispy crust.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making a Classic Reuben Sandwich

1. Prepare Your Ingredients

Before you begin assembling the sandwich, it’s important to prepare all of your ingredients. Start by slicing your rye bread if it’s not pre-sliced. You’ll need two slices per sandwich.

Next, gather your corned beef, Swiss cheese, and sauerkraut. If you bought whole corned beef, thinly slice it. Drain the sauerkraut in a colander to get rid of excess moisture, which could make the sandwich soggy.

If you’re using store-bought dressing, give it a quick stir to mix the ingredients. If you’re making your own dressing, combine mayonnaise, ketchup, horseradish, and a few other seasonings.

2. Toast the Bread

To achieve the perfect crispy texture, you’ll want to butter the outside of the bread slices generously. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Once hot, place the slices of rye bread on the pan, buttered side down. Toast each side until golden brown and crisp, which should take around 3-4 minutes per side.

Alternatively, you can toast the bread in a toaster if you’re short on time, but for the best results, using a skillet or griddle will give you that extra crispy texture.

3. Assemble the Sandwich

Once your bread is toasted, it’s time to assemble the sandwich. On the unbuttered side of one slice, spread a generous layer of your Russian or Thousand Island dressing. Be sure to cover it evenly, as this will add moisture and flavor to the sandwich.

Next, layer your Swiss cheese on top of the dressing, followed by a generous portion of corned beef. If you prefer a meatier sandwich, feel free to add extra slices of corned beef.

Add a layer of sauerkraut on top of the corned beef. Sauerkraut can be a bit tangy, so make sure to balance it well with the other ingredients.

Finally, top it with the second slice of rye bread, buttered side facing out. Now your sandwich is ready to be grilled!

4. Grill the Sandwich

With your sandwich assembled, return it to the skillet or griddle. Grill the sandwich on medium heat, pressing down lightly with a spatula to help the sandwich stick together and become crispy. Grill for 4-5 minutes on each side or until the bread is golden brown and the cheese has melted.

Be careful not to burn the bread. Adjust the heat if necessary to make sure the sandwich grills evenly without over-browning.

5. Slice and Serve

Once your Reuben sandwich is grilled to perfection, remove it from the skillet and let it rest for a minute. This will allow the cheese to set slightly, preventing it from spilling out when you slice the sandwich. Cut the sandwich diagonally or in half, depending on your preference.

Serve your Reuben with a side of pickles, chips, or coleslaw for a classic deli-style meal. The Reuben is best enjoyed hot and fresh, with all the melted cheese and tangy sauce combining in each bite.

Tips for Perfecting Your Reuben Sandwich

1. Choose High-Quality Ingredients

While the Reuben sandwich relies on a few simple ingredients, the quality of these ingredients is crucial. Opt for fresh, high-quality corned beef, especially if you’re not using pre-sliced deli meat. If possible, get your corned beef from a reputable butcher for the best flavor.

For the cheese, choose a good Swiss cheese that melts well. Aged Swiss will add a deeper flavor, but mild Swiss is a classic option.

When selecting sauerkraut, look for one that is naturally fermented, without added preservatives. Fresh sauerkraut will have a more authentic taste.

2. Don’t Overstuff the Sandwich

It can be tempting to pile on as much corned beef as possible, but this can lead to an overwhelming sandwich that’s difficult to eat. Aim for a balanced amount of each ingredient to maintain the harmony of flavors and textures.

3. Experiment with Different Sauces

While Russian dressing and Thousand Island dressing are the traditional choices, feel free to experiment with other sauces to give your Reuben a unique twist. Some people love to use a zesty mustard or even a creamy horseradish sauce to spice things up.

4. Add Extra Flavor with Pickles

Pickles are a great addition to a Reuben sandwich. You can serve a dill pickle on the side, or even add pickle slices directly inside the sandwich for an extra layer of flavor.

5. Don’t Rush the Grilling Process

To achieve the perfect golden-brown crust, be patient when grilling your sandwich. If you cook it too quickly on high heat, the bread may burn before the cheese melts, and the corned beef may not get heated through. Keep the heat at medium and allow the sandwich to cook slowly, ensuring that each bite is crispy and melty.

Variations and Twists on the Classic Reuben

While the classic Reuben sandwich is beloved by many, there are a few variations you might want to try if you’re looking for something a little different:

1. Turkey Reuben

For a lighter version of the Reuben, you can substitute turkey for the corned beef. This is often called a “Turkey Reuben” and provides a leaner alternative while still offering the same satisfying flavor combination.

2. Veggie Reuben

If you’re looking for a vegetarian option, you can replace the corned beef with grilled portobello mushrooms or even tempeh. You’ll still get the savory, tangy flavors of the sauerkraut and dressing, but with a plant-based twist.

3. Pastrami Reuben

Another variation on the classic Reuben is the pastrami Reuben. In place of corned beef, pastrami adds a smokier, spiced flavor to the sandwich. Pastrami is often used in deli sandwiches and works wonderfully in a Reuben.

4. Open-Faced Reuben

For those who prefer to skip the bread or want a more “gourmet” feel, you can make an open-faced Reuben. This is essentially a Reuben served on a single slice of toasted rye, with all the filling piled high. It’s a great way to showcase the delicious ingredients without the bread getting in the way.

Conclusion

Making a classic Reuben sandwich at home is a satisfying experience that allows you to enjoy this iconic deli favorite whenever you like. With the right ingredients, a few simple techniques, and a little patience, you can create a Reuben that rivals any deli’s offering. Whether you stick to the traditional recipe or experiment with some variations, the Reuben sandwich is sure to become a regular in your culinary rotation. So grab your ingredients, fire up the skillet, and get ready to indulge in this timeless sandwich delight!
