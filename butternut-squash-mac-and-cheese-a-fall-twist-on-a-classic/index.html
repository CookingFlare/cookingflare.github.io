Butternut Squash Mac and Cheese: A Fall Twist on a Classic


As the leaves turn and the air becomes crisp, there’s nothing quite like a warm, comforting bowl of mac and cheese to evoke the spirit of autumn. This Butternut Squash Mac and Cheese recipe takes the beloved classic and infuses it with seasonal flavors, creamy textures, and a delightful hint of sweetness. Packed with nutrients from the squash, this dish is not only delicious but also a great way to sneak in some veggies for the whole family. Whether you’re hosting a fall dinner party, enjoying a cozy night in, or simply craving a hearty meal, this Butternut Squash Mac and Cheese will surely satisfy.

The Allure of Butternut Squash

Butternut squash is a versatile ingredient that shines in the fall months. Its sweet, nutty flavor lends itself well to savory dishes, making it an excellent addition to mac and cheese. Not only does it contribute to a rich, creamy sauce, but it also packs a nutritional punch. Butternut squash is high in vitamins A and C, fiber, and antioxidants, which can support overall health and wellness.

Ingredients You’ll Need

For this indulgent yet wholesome dish, gather the following ingredients:

For the Squash Puree:

  • 1 medium-sized butternut squash (about 2 pounds)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Mac and Cheese:

  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni (or any pasta shape you prefer)
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole or 2%)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional for added flavor)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or chives, for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preparing the Butternut Squash

Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  1. Carefully slice the butternut squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds. Drizzle the cut sides with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Place the squash halves cut side down on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Roast in the oven for about 30-40 minutes, or until the flesh is tender and easily pierced with a fork.
  3. Once cooked, remove the squash from the oven and allow it to cool slightly. Scoop the flesh into a blender or food processor, and blend until smooth. Set aside.

2. Cooking the Pasta

While the squash is roasting, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the elbow macaroni and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain the pasta and set aside.

3. Making the Cheese Sauce

In a large saucepan over medium heat, melt the butter. Once melted, whisk in the flour and cook for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly to create a roux.

  1. Gradually add the milk to the roux, whisking continuously to avoid lumps.
  2. Continue to cook until the mixture thickens and begins to bubble, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the garlic powder, onion powder, mustard powder, smoked paprika, nutmeg, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and add the butternut squash puree to the sauce, stirring until fully incorporated.
  5. Gradually mix in the shredded cheddar and Parmesan cheese, allowing them to melt into the sauce. If you’re using feta, add it in at this stage for an extra layer of flavor.

4. Combining Pasta and Sauce

Once the cheese sauce is creamy and smooth, add the cooked pasta into the sauce. Gently stir until all the pasta is well-coated with the cheesy butternut squash goodness.

5. Baking (Optional)

For an added layer of texture, you can bake your mac and cheese. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Transfer the mac and cheese into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish.

  1. If you want a crispy topping, you can sprinkle some extra shredded cheese, breadcrumbs, or crushed crackers on top.
  2. Bake in the oven for about 20 minutes, or until the top is bubbly and golden brown.

6. Serving

Once baked, remove the dish from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley or chives for a pop of color and freshness. Serve warm and enjoy the comforting flavors of this fall-inspired dish.

Tips for Making the Perfect Butternut Squash Mac and Cheese

  • Choose the Right Squash: Look for squash that is firm and heavy for its size. Avoid any that have soft spots or blemishes.
  • Experiment with Cheeses: While sharp cheddar and Parmesan work wonderfully, feel free to mix in your favorite cheeses for different flavor profiles. Gruyère, Gouda, or even a hint of blue cheese can elevate this dish.
  • Add Protein: For those looking to add some protein, consider incorporating cooked chicken, bacon, or chickpeas into the mac and cheese before baking.
  • Herbs and Spices: Fresh herbs like thyme or sage can complement the flavors of butternut squash beautifully. Consider adding them into the cheese sauce for an aromatic twist.
  • Storage: Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave until warmed through.

Conclusion

This Butternut Squash Mac and Cheese is more than just a fall dish; it’s a celebration of comfort food reimagined with a nutritious twist. The creamy, cheesy sauce paired with the sweet earthiness of butternut squash creates a rich flavor that will leave you craving more. Whether you’re enjoying a cozy night in or serving it at your next gathering, this mac and cheese is sure to impress. So next time the leaves start to change, grab a butternut squash and get ready to enjoy a deliciously comforting meal that embodies the essence of fall.
