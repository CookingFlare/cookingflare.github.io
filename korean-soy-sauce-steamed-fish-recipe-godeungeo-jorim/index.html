Korean Soy Sauce Steamed Fish Recipe (Godeungeo Jorim)


Godeungeo Jorim, a classic Korean dish featuring mackerel braised in soy sauce, has long been a staple in Korean households and an undeniable favorite among food lovers seeking rich, umami-packed flavors. Its beauty lies in the perfect blend of soy sauce, spices, and fresh fish, creating a savory and slightly sweet profile that awakens the taste buds. The recipe is straightforward to follow, rewarding you with a deeply flavorful dish that embodies the essence of Korean home cooking.

If you’re looking to elevate your family dinners or explore the depth of Korean cuisine beyond bulgogi and kimchi, Godeungeo Jorim is the perfect dish to try. This guide will walk you through each step, ensuring you achieve a restaurant-quality result right in your kitchen.

Essential Ingredients for Godeungeo Jorim

Full flavors come from combining fresh ingredients with well-balanced seasoning. For this dish, you’ll need:

Fish:

  • Fresh Mackerel (Godeungeo): The star of this dish. Mackerel’s slightly fatty and firm texture pairs beautifully with the soy sauce-based broth. Opt for fresh mackerel for the best results, but frozen mackerel works in a pinch if properly defrosted.

Vegetables:

  • Potatoes: Russet or Yukon Gold potatoes add heartiness to the dish and absorb the delicious flavors of the sauce.
  • Carrots: Give the dish a pop of color and a hint of sweetness.
  • Onion: Adds depth to the flavor while providing natural sweetness to the dish.
  • Green Chili Peppers: For a touch of spice. Adjust the quantity to suit your heat preferences.
  • Scallions: Used as a garnish to enhance the visual appeal and add a subtle onion flavor.

Sauce:

  • Soy Sauce: The key ingredient that ties all the flavors together. Use a good-quality Korean soy sauce for authentic taste.
  • Gochugaru (Korean Chili Flakes): Provides a slight kick and traditional Korean smokiness.
  • Minced Garlic: For a punch of savory flavor.
  • Minced Ginger: Balances any fishy odors and adds a warm, aromatic note.
  • Sesame Oil: Enhances the rich, nutty undertones of the dish.
  • Rice Wine or Cooking Sake: To tenderize the fish and enrich its flavor.
  • Brown Sugar or Honey: Adds a balancing sweetness to the savory elements.
  • Korean Corn Syrup (Optional): Can be added for a deeper sweetness and glossy finish.

Preparation Tips

Selecting the Mackerel:

When preparing Godeungeo Jorim, the freshness of your fish is critical. Look for clean skin, firm texture, and a subtle ocean scent. Fresh fish will yield the best flavor and hold its shape during cooking. If scaling and cleaning the fish feels daunting, ask your fishmonger to prepare it for you.

Prepping Ingredients:

Chop your vegetables into evenly sized pieces to ensure even cooking. For potatoes, cut them slightly thicker to avoid them breaking down too much during the braising process. Prepare all your sauce ingredients beforehand to make cooking seamless.

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prep the Fish:

Begin by cleaning the mackerel. Rinse the fish under cold running water and pat it dry with paper towels. Cut the fish into manageable portions—typically three or four pieces per mackerel. Sprinkle a small amount of salt over the fish and let it rest for 15 minutes. This step helps enhance the flavor and reduces any lingering fishy odor.

2. Prepare the Vegetables:

Peel and slice the potatoes, carrots, and onions. For a visually appealing presentation, aim for thick slices. Chop green chili peppers into thin slices and set them aside. Thinly cut a few scallions for garnish.

3. Make the Sauce:

In a medium bowl, combine soy sauce, gochugaru, minced garlic, minced ginger, sesame oil, rice wine, brown sugar, and corn syrup (if using). Whisk the ingredients together until you achieve a smooth consistency and set aside. Adjust the sweetness or salt level depending on your preference.

4. Start Braising:

In a large skillet or shallow pot, layer the potato slices first to prevent the fish from sticking to the base. Then, place the onion and carrot slices on top. Lay the mackerel pieces over the vegetables. Pour the soy sauce mixture evenly over the fish and vegetables.

Add enough water to partially cover the ingredients. Avoid adding too much water; the sauce should concentrate as the dish cooks.

5. Simmer to Perfection:

Bring the pot to a gentle boil over medium heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Allow the dish to simmer for around 20-25 minutes. During this time, the vegetables should soften while the fish becomes tender and absorbs the flavors. Occasionally spoon the sauce over the fish to ensure even seasoning.

6. Final Touches:

After simmering, check the potatoes for doneness—they should be fork-tender. If the sauce appears too watery, uncover the pot and let it simmer for 5-10 more minutes to thicken. Toss in sliced green chili peppers and cook for an additional minute.

Remove the pot from heat and garnish the dish with fresh scallions. Serve immediately.

Serving Suggestions

Godeungeo Jorim pairs beautifully with steamed white rice, which helps balance the dish’s bold flavors. Prepare some Korean side dishes, like kimchi, pickled radish, or sesame spinach, to complement the meal and elevate its authenticity.

To complete the dining experience, serve with a warm bowl of soup such as Doenjang Jjigae (soybean paste stew) or Kongnamul Guk (bean sprout soup). Korean households often enjoy a variety of banchan (small side dishes), creating a satisfying, harmonious meal.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to two days. The flavors will continue to deepen over time, making it even more delicious. Reheat the dish gently over low heat, adding a splash of water to loosen the sauce if necessary.

The Heart of Korean Cuisine

Godeungeo Jorim is more than just a dish; it represents the heart and soul of Korean culinary traditions. Its rich flavors are rooted in the harmonious balance of savory, spicy, and sweet elements, all brought together by the versatility of soy sauce. Whether you’re new to Korean cooking or a seasoned enthusiast, this recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Give Korean Soy Sauce Steamed Fish a try in your kitchen and experience the comfort and warmth that this time-honored recipe offers. It’s a dish that brings people together, as every bite tells a story of rich tradition and extraordinary flavors. Don’t be surprised if this recipe earns a permanent place in your repertoire!
