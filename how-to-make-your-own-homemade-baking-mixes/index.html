How to Make Your Own Homemade Baking Mixes


Making your own homemade baking mixes is not only a fantastic way to save time in the kitchen, but it’s also a great way to take control of the quality of ingredients you use. Store-bought baking mixes often contain preservatives, artificial flavors, and additives that you may want to avoid. Plus, creating your own baking mixes allows you to customize recipes to suit your preferences, dietary needs, and personal tastes. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, crafting homemade mixes can simplify meal prep and elevate your baking game. This guide will walk you through the steps to create versatile, shelf-stable baking mixes for everything from pancakes to muffins.

Benefits of Making Your Own Baking Mix

When you craft homemade baking mixes, you empower yourself to make healthier choices. You control the ingredients, so you can avoid unhealthy fillers and create recipes using high-quality flour, natural sweeteners, and other wholesome ingredients. It’s also cost-effective, as buying ingredients in bulk is often cheaper than purchasing pre-made mixes.

Homemade mixes save time. Instead of gathering ingredients every time you bake, you simply measure out your mix, add the wet ingredients, and start cooking. They’re perfect for busy mornings, impromptu baking sessions, or planning ahead for the holidays.

Another great benefit is versatility. Want gluten-free pancakes? Prefer sugar-free muffins? Need to cater to vegan diets? You can customize your mixes as needed, tailoring them to fit a variety of dietary requirements without sacrificing flavor or texture.

Essential Ingredients for Baking Mixes

Before diving into recipes, it’s important to understand the core components that make up a reliable baking mix. These ingredients are the foundation for almost all baked goods:

  1. Flour
    All-purpose flour is the go-to option for most baking mixes, but you can also use whole wheat, almond, oat, or gluten-free flours depending on your dietary preferences.

  2. Leavening Agents
    Baking powder and baking soda are essential for helping baked goods rise. These ingredients provide the light, airy texture you expect from cakes, muffins, and pancakes.

  3. Sugar
    Granulated white sugar is the default option, but brown sugar, coconut sugar, or stevia are great alternatives for varying degrees of sweetness.

  4. Salt
    A small amount of salt enhances the flavor of baked goods, deepening their complexity and balancing out sweetness.

  5. Dry Milk Powder (Optional)
    Dry milk powder can add richness and moisture to your baked goods.

  6. Flavor Enhancements (Optional)
    Include spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, or vanilla powder if you want an extra layer of flavor in your mix.

Tips for Storing Homemade Baking Mixes

Proper storage is key to maintaining the shelf life of your mixes. Always store your mixes in airtight containers, such as glass jars with tight-fitting lids or resealable plastic bags. Keep them in a cool, dry place away from direct light to prevent moisture and spoilage.

To stay organized, label each container with the name of the mix, the date it was created, and any additional instructions for preparation. Homemade baking mixes typically last for about 3-6 months when stored properly, but always check the freshness of ingredients like flour and leavening agents.

Versatile Baking Mix Recipes

Basic All-Purpose Baking Mix

This is your multipurpose foundation for everything from biscuits to pancakes. It’s simple to put together and serves as a blank canvas for any recipe.

Ingredients:

  • 6 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup vegetable shortening or unsalted butter, finely chopped and chilled

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt until evenly combined.
  2. Add the shortening or butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to blend it into the dry ingredients. The mixture should resemble coarse crumbs.
  3. Store the mix in an airtight container in a cool place.

To use for biscuits, simply add milk or water to your desired consistency, roll out the dough, and bake.

Pancake and Waffle Mix

Perfect for quick breakfasts, this mix saves time while delivering fluffy, delicious pancakes and waffles.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 tablespoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Combine all the ingredients in a large bowl. Whisk thoroughly to ensure even distribution.
  2. Transfer the mix into an airtight jar or container.

To Prepare Pancakes:
1 cup of mix + ¾ cup of milk + 1 egg + 1 tablespoon of melted butter. Cook on a hot griddle until golden.

To Prepare Waffles:
Follow the same instructions as pancakes but cook in a waffle iron.

Muffin Mix

Customize this muffin mix to create sweet or savory muffins that take minimal effort.

Ingredients:

  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1½ cups granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. Store in a sealed container in a cool, dry location.

To Prepare Muffins:
2 cups of mix + 1 egg + ¾ cup of milk + ½ cup of oil or melted butter. Add any mix-ins like fruit, nuts, chocolate chips, or shredded cheese.

Brownie Mix

Rich, fudgy brownies are just a few steps away with this homemade mix.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1½ cups unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, sift together all the dry ingredients. Mix well.
  2. Transfer the mix to an airtight container.

To Prepare Brownies:
2½ cups of mix + ½ cup melted butter + 2 eggs + 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. Bake according to your preferred pan size and temperature.

Cornbread Mix

This versatile cornbread mix delivers a moist, crumbly texture and pairs wonderfully with soups, stews, or chili.

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cornmeal
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • ½ cup granulated sugar

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a large bowl and whisk until evenly mixed.
  2. Store in a sealed container in a cool, dry place.

To Prepare Cornbread:
2 cups of mix + 1 cup milk + 1 egg + ¼ cup melted oil or butter. Bake in a preheated oven until golden brown.

Customize Your Mixes

One of the best things about homemade baking mixes is how adaptable they are. Choose gluten-free flours for a gluten-free version. Experiment with natural sweeteners to reduce added sugar. Add spices like cinnamon or nutmeg for a hint of warmth. With homemade baking mixes, the possibilities are endless.

Make Ahead and Save Time

On busy days, homemade baking mixes are true lifesavers. Pre-mixing dry ingredients ensures you spend less time preparing recipes and more time enjoying the food. They’re ideal for meal planning and useful for gifting during the holidays.

Final Thoughts

Creating your own homemade baking mixes is a rewarding way to simplify your cooking routine while improving the quality of what you eat. It’s a small effort that offers big rewards in terms of time, money, and customization. Once you start experimenting with your own mixes, it’ll be hard to go back to store-bought options. So, grab your favorite mixing bowl and start crafting!
