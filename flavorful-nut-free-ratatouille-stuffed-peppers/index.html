Flavorful Nut-Free Ratatouille Stuffed Peppers

5 min · 886 words

If you’re looking for a delicious, healthy, and allergy-friendly dish, then you’re in for a treat! Our flavorful nut-free ratatouille stuffed peppers are a fantastic way to enjoy the goodness of fresh vegetables in a unique and satisfying way. This recipe is not only bursting with flavor, but it’s also a great option for anyone with nut allergies. So, let’s dive right into this delightful culinary adventure!

Ingredients

For the Ratatouille:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 2 zucchinis, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 3 tomatoes, diced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Stuffed Peppers:

  • 6 large bell peppers (any color of your choice)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

Making the Ratatouille:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until they turn translucent.

  2. Add the diced eggplant to the skillet and cook until it softens slightly, stirring occasionally.

  3. Next, add the diced zucchinis, red and yellow bell peppers, and tomatoes to the skillet. Stir the ingredients together and let them cook until the vegetables are tender but still retain their shape.

  4. Season the ratatouille with dried thyme, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir well to incorporate the flavors, then remove the skillet from the heat and set it aside.

Preparing the Stuffed Peppers:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes from the inside. Rinse the peppers thoroughly.

  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked quinoa and the ratatouille mixture. Stir until well combined.

  4. Carefully stuff each bell pepper with the ratatouille and quinoa mixture, packing it in tightly.

  5. Place the stuffed peppers in a baking dish. Sprinkle the grated mozzarella cheese over the tops of the peppers.

  6. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Once cooked, remove the stuffed peppers from the oven and let them cool for a few minutes before serving.

Serving and Garnishing:

  1. Arrange the nut-free ratatouille stuffed peppers on a serving platter.

  2. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a burst of color and added freshness.

  3. Serve the stuffed peppers hot and enjoy the delicious medley of flavors and textures.

Nutritional Information

  • Calories: 280 per serving
  • Total Fat: 12g
  • Saturated Fat: 4g
  • Cholesterol: 20mg
  • Sodium: 450mg
  • Total Carbohydrate: 32g
  • Dietary Fiber: 8g
  • Sugars: 12g
  • Protein: 14g

Final Thoughts

Our nut-free ratatouille stuffed peppers are not only a feast for the taste buds but also a wholesome and nutritious meal option for you and your loved ones. Whether you’re catering to nut allergies or simply seeking a delightful dish that celebrates the goodness of fresh produce, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your kitchen. So, go ahead, gather the ingredients, and embark on a culinary journey that promises to tantalize your senses with every delectable bite!
