Keto Aromatics: Infused with Flavors & Spices, 20 Signature Low-Carb Eats


Embark on a culinary journey as we explore a palette of flavors that is as curious as it is scrumptious. Indulge in the rich, aromatic world of ketogenic cuisine with our handpicked collection of 20 low-carb treats. Each dish weaves together an expert balance of captivating spices, aromatic herbs, and delectable ingredients to transform your keto cooking into a masterpiece.

1. Creamy Cauliflower Soup with Turmeric and Curry Leaves

This aromatic soup served with a dollop of homemade herb-infused heavy cream is a ketogenic twist you’d never imagine!

  • Ingredients:

    • 1 head of cauliflower
    • 2 cups of chicken broth
    • 1 tablespoon of curry leaves
    • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
    • 2 cloves of garlic
    • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. Chop the cauliflower into florets and steam until tender.
    2. Sauté garlic in a pot with olive oil until golden.
    3. Add turmeric, curry leaves and salt to taste, sauté for a minute until aromatic.
    4. Pour in the chicken broth followed by the cauliflower. Bring to a boil and then simmer until cauliflower is fully incorporated into the liquid.
    5. Blend the soup using an immersion blender or a standard blender. Stir in the heavy cream and season with salt and pepper.

2. Glazed Buttered Chicken with Cinnamon and Cardamom

Aromatic spices give a unique taste to this dish, turning plain buttered chicken into a delightful explosion of flavors.

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 lbs of boneless chicken
    • ½ cup of butter
    • 2 teaspoons of cinnamon
    • 1 teaspoon of cardamom
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces and season with salt and pepper.
    2. Melt the butter in a pan and sauté the chicken until browned on all sides.
    3. Add cinnamon and cardamom after removing the chicken from pan. Let it sizzle for a while to infuse the aroma.
    4. Return the chicken back to the pan, stirring well to make sure it’s all coated in the spiced butter.
    5. Lower the heat, cover the pan and let it simmer until the chicken is cooked through and glazed with buttery spices.

3. Mediterranean Zucchini Noodles (Zoodles) with Fennel and Oregano

A fresh, light dish that surprises the palate - perfect for those hot summer days.

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 large zucchinis
    • 1/2 bulb of fennel
    • A handful of fresh oregano
    • Lemon juice and olive oil
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. Use a spiralizer to make zucchini noodles (zoodles) and thinly slice the fennel.
    2. Mix zoodles and fennel in a large bowl. Toss with lemon juice, olive oil, chopped oregano, salt, and pepper to taste.

4. Spicy Keto Black Pepper chicken

For those who like a little heat, this aromatic pepper-blackened chicken will spice up your keto meals!

  • Ingredients:

    • 4 boneless chicken thighs
    • 1 tablespoon of finely crushed black pepper
    • Salt to taste

  • Instructions:

    1. Season the chicken with salt and crushed black pepper.
    2. Heat a pan with some olive oil and sear the chicken until fully cooked on both sides.
    3. Let it cook on low flames for a few minutes for the pepper to cook in and give a nice blackened effect.

5. Creamy Miso Soup with Sautéed Mushrooms

Miso and mushrooms make a magical combination in this savory soup, full of umami flavors without any of the carbs.

  • Ingredients:

    • 2 cups of chicken bone broth
    • 1/4 cup of white miso paste
    • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
    • 1 star anise
    • 1 1/2 cups of sliced mixed mushrooms
    • 1 tablespoon of mirin

  • Instructions:

    1. Rehydrate the star anise in the bone broth for about an hour.
    2. Strain the broth and discard the star anise.
    3. In the broth, dissolve the miso paste, ensuring it’s fully incorporated.
    4. In a frying pan, sauté the mushrooms with sesame oil until crispy. Add the mirin and cook for another minute.
    5. Add the sautéed mushrooms into the broth and serve.
