How to Make Dairy-Free Whipped Cream


Whipped cream is one of those timeless, versatile toppings that elevates everything from pies to hot drinks. But for those who follow a dairy-free diet or are lactose intolerant, traditional whipped cream is off-limits. The good news is that you can easily make a delicious, dairy-free alternative that’s just as fluffy, smooth, and indulgent. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to make dairy-free whipped cream that’s perfect for all your favorite desserts, drinks, and more.

Why Choose Dairy-Free Whipped Cream?

There are many reasons to opt for a dairy-free whipped cream, whether it’s due to lactose intolerance, vegan preferences, or simply a desire for a lighter alternative. Dairy-free whipped cream provides all the joy of the classic version but with added benefits:

  • Lactose-free: Perfect for those who experience discomfort with dairy products.
  • Vegan-friendly: Made without any animal products, making it suitable for those following plant-based diets.
  • Rich and creamy texture: You don’t have to sacrifice flavor and texture when going dairy-free. With the right ingredients, it’s just as satisfying as traditional whipped cream.
  • Versatile: Ideal for pies, cakes, coffee, hot cocoa, fruit bowls, and many other desserts or drinks.

Now, let’s explore the best methods and ingredients to create the perfect dairy-free whipped cream at home.

Key Ingredients for Dairy-Free Whipped Cream

Making dairy-free whipped cream is surprisingly simple, and the key ingredient that will give it that rich, creamy texture is coconut cream. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Coconut cream: The thick, rich part of coconut milk that rises to the top in a can. It’s naturally creamy and provides the base for your whipped cream.
  • Powdered sugar: This helps to sweeten the whipped cream without the grittiness that regular sugar might leave behind.
  • Vanilla extract: Adds depth and a lovely flavor. You can experiment with other extracts like almond or hazelnut for a unique twist.
  • Optional stabilizer (like agar-agar or cornstarch): If you want your whipped cream to hold its shape longer, consider adding a stabilizer. These are especially useful if you’re making whipped cream in advance.

Tools You’ll Need

To whip up this dairy-free whipped cream, you’ll need a few basic kitchen tools:

  • Electric mixer: A handheld or stand mixer will make the process quicker and more efficient. If you don’t have one, a whisk can also work, but it will require a bit more effort.
  • Mixing bowl: Choose a medium to large bowl, preferably one that’s stainless steel or glass, as they tend to keep the mixture cold and help the whipping process.
  • Spatula: For scraping down the sides of the bowl and ensuring everything is evenly mixed.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Dairy-Free Whipped Cream

Step 1: Chill the Coconut Cream

The key to achieving a fluffy, airy whipped cream is starting with chilled coconut cream. The colder the coconut cream, the more easily it will whip into a creamy texture. Here’s how to do it:

  • Place the can of coconut cream in the fridge for at least 4 hours or overnight. This allows the coconut cream to solidify and separate from the liquid.
  • After chilling, open the can carefully. You’ll see a thick, creamy layer at the top, and underneath, there will be some coconut water.
  • Scoop out the thick coconut cream into a mixing bowl, leaving the coconut water behind. You can use the coconut water in smoothies or other recipes, so don’t throw it away!

Step 2: Whip the Coconut Cream

Once you have your chilled coconut cream in the bowl, it’s time to whip it. Using an electric mixer, beat the cream on medium-high speed for about 3-4 minutes. Start slow to avoid splattering, and then gradually increase the speed.

  • After a few minutes of whipping, the coconut cream should begin to form soft peaks, which means it will hold its shape but will still be slightly soft when you lift the beaters out of the cream.
  • If you prefer a firmer texture, you can continue to whip it for a bit longer until stiff peaks form, meaning the whipped cream holds its shape firmly.

Step 3: Add Sweetener and Flavoring

Once your coconut cream is whipped, it’s time to sweeten it. Add powdered sugar, a tablespoon at a time, to achieve the desired sweetness. Powdered sugar is ideal because it dissolves easily and doesn’t leave any grittiness.

  • For a richer flavor, add about a teaspoon of vanilla extract. This will give your dairy-free whipped cream a lovely aroma and enhance its taste.
  • Mix the sweetener and vanilla extract in slowly, and continue whipping until the cream reaches your preferred consistency.

Step 4: Optional – Stabilize Your Whipped Cream

If you want your whipped cream to hold its shape for a longer period (such as for cakes or as a topping for fruit), you can add a stabilizer. Here are a couple of options:

  • Agar-Agar: This natural plant-based gelatin can help firm up the whipped cream. Dissolve a small amount (usually about 1/2 teaspoon) in warm water and then beat it into the cream at the end of the whipping process.
  • Cornstarch: A teaspoon of cornstarch can help the whipped cream hold its structure. If you go this route, you can add it while whipping the coconut cream or after adding the sugar.

Step 5: Chill and Serve

Once your whipped cream is perfectly whipped, you can serve it immediately or chill it for later. If you’re not using it right away, cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate it. The whipped cream will firm up in the fridge, but you can easily fluff it up again by giving it a quick whisk or stir.

Tips for the Perfect Dairy-Free Whipped Cream

  1. Use full-fat coconut cream: Low-fat coconut milk won’t give you the same rich, creamy texture. For the best results, always opt for full-fat coconut cream.
  2. Keep it cold: The colder your ingredients and equipment are, the better your whipped cream will turn out. You can even chill the mixing bowl and beaters for extra coldness.
  3. Don’t overwhip: If you whip the cream for too long, it can turn grainy and separate. Stop whipping when you see soft or stiff peaks, depending on your preference.
  4. Adjust the sweetness: Some people prefer their whipped cream less sweet, while others like it quite sugary. Taste as you go and adjust the amount of powdered sugar to suit your needs.
  5. Flavor variations: Don’t be afraid to experiment with different flavors. Add a few drops of almond, peppermint, or hazelnut extract for unique flavor profiles.

How to Use Dairy-Free Whipped Cream

Once you’ve mastered the art of making dairy-free whipped cream, you’ll want to use it in all sorts of delicious ways. Here are a few ideas:

  • On desserts: Top your favorite cakes, pies, and pastries with a generous dollop of whipped cream for added richness.
  • In drinks: Use it as a topping for coffee, hot chocolate, or even iced lattes.
  • With fruit: Serve it alongside fresh berries or a fruit salad for a light, refreshing treat.
  • In vegan pastries: Add it as a filling for vegan cream puffs or as a topping for vegan donuts.
  • As a dip: Pair it with cookies or even pretzels for a fun dip for a sweet-and-salty combination.

Conclusion

Making dairy-free whipped cream is an easy and rewarding process that anyone can master. With just a few simple ingredients and a little bit of patience, you can create a versatile topping that works for almost any dessert, drink, or snack. Whether you’re vegan, lactose intolerant, or just looking for a lighter alternative to traditional whipped cream, this dairy-free option is sure to satisfy your cravings. Enjoy the creamy goodness without any of the dairy!
