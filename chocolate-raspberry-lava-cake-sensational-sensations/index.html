Chocolate Raspberry Lava Cake: Sensational Sensations


Ah, the chocolate lava cake, a luscious dessert loved by many. It’s the perfect finale to a romantic dinner, an impressive addition to any dinner party, or even an indulgent treat for your solo movie night. The bonus? It only takes minutes to prepare and bake, and requires ingredients you most likely have in your kitchen right now.

Allow me to introduce a sensational twist on the classic recipe - the Chocolate Raspberry Lava Cake. A rich, fudgy cake filled with a burst of raspberry lava and crowned with a velvety frosting. As you take the first bite, the gooey center will make you question whether you are eating cake, pudding or directly savoring liquid chocolate-raspberry heaven.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 10 oz fresh raspberries
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for raspberry sauce)
  • 1/2 cup water (for raspberry sauce)
  • Confectioners’ sugar (for dusting)
  • Whipped cream (for serving, optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Grease four 6-ounce ramekins or oven-safe cups with butter or cooking spray.

  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and salt. Set aside.

  3. In another bowl, whisk the eggs and granulated sugar together. Mix in the milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract until fully combined.

  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix as this may result in a denser cake.

  5. Using a spoon, divide the batter among the 4 prepped ramekins. Next, add a few fresh raspberries (about 2-3 per ramekin) into the batter.

  6. To make the raspberry sauce, place 1/4 cup of granulated sugar and 1/2 cup of water in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the fresh raspberries and bring to a simmer. Cook until the berries have softened and release their juices, about 5 minutes. Strain the sauce into a bowl, pressing on the solids to extract as much juice as possible. Discard the solids and let the sauce cool.

  7. Divide the raspberry sauce evenly between the 4 ramekins, spooning it directly onto the layer of raspberries in the batter.

  8. Bake the ramekins directly on the oven rack for 12-14 minutes, or until the sides are firm but the center still looks slightly undercooked.

  9. Carefully remove the ramekins from the oven and let cool for 1-2 minutes. Place a small plate over each ramekin, then flip them over to release the cake. Gently pat the bottom of the ramekin to help release the cake. If desired, dust the cakes generously with confectioners’ sugar and serve with whipped cream on the side.

  10. Indulge in your warm Chocolate Raspberry Lava Cake sensation, savoring every bite of the gooey, raspberry-filled center. Just try not to let it get too hot before taking that first bite!

And thus concludes our exploration of the Chocolate Raspberry Lava Cake. This scrumptious dessert is sure to delight your taste buds and make any occasion special. You can thank me later.
