Classic American Chicken Noodle Soup: Comforting Recipe


Chicken noodle soup is more than just a dish; it’s a warm embrace on a cold day, a soothing remedy when you’re feeling under the weather, and a beloved classic that brings families together. This American staple is cherished for its simplicity, heartiness, and the nostalgic memories it evokes. In this blog post, we’ll explore the rich history of chicken noodle soup, the best ingredients to use, and how to make a pot of this comforting soup from scratch. Get ready to dive into a culinary adventure that will leave you with a delicious homemade soup perfect for any occasion.

A Brief History of Chicken Noodle Soup

The origins of chicken noodle soup can be traced back to ancient civilizations, where broth was made from boiling meat and bones. However, the version we love today has its roots firmly planted in American cuisine. The first recorded recipe for chicken soup appeared in the 17th century, but it wasn’t until the late 19th century that chicken noodle soup became widely popular in American households.

Immigrants from various cultures brought their own versions of chicken soup, contributing to the diverse flavors we find today. This soup became particularly popular during the Great Depression, as families sought affordable yet nourishing meals. Over the years, chicken noodle soup has earned a reputation as a healing remedy, often recommended by mothers and grandmothers alike for its comforting qualities.

Ingredients for Classic Chicken Noodle Soup

To make a classic American chicken noodle soup, you’ll want to gather the following ingredients:

For the Soup Base:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds) or 4-6 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 8 cups of chicken broth (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2-3 carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2-3 celery stalks, sliced
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried parsley
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Noodles:

  • 2 cups egg noodles (or any noodle of your choice)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Optional Add-ins:

  • Peas (for a pop of color and sweetness)
  • Corn (for a bit of crunch)
  • Lemon juice (for a zesty finish)

The Perfect Chicken Noodle Soup Recipe

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let’s get into the step-by-step process of creating a delicious pot of classic chicken noodle soup.

Step 1: Prepare the Chicken

  1. If using a whole chicken, rinse it under cold water and remove any giblets from the cavity. Place the chicken in a large pot and cover it with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 1 hour, skimming off any foam that rises to the surface.

  2. If using chicken thighs, season them with salt and pepper, and brown them in a bit of oil in a large pot over medium heat for about 5-7 minutes on each side. Remove from the pot and set aside.

Step 2: Create the Soup Base

  1. In the same pot used for browning the chicken, add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes until they begin to soften.

  2. Add the minced garlic, bay leaf, thyme, and parsley, stirring for about 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. If you used a whole chicken, add the chicken back into the pot along with the chicken broth. If you used chicken thighs, add them back in now. Bring the mixture to a boil.

Step 3: Simmer to Perfection

  1. Reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer for about 30-40 minutes. If you used a whole chicken, remove it after 30 minutes, let it cool slightly, then shred the meat, discarding the skin and bones.

  2. Return the shredded chicken to the pot, and taste the broth, adjusting the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

Step 4: Noodle Time

  1. Add the egg noodles to the pot and cook for about 8-10 minutes, or until they are tender. If you’re adding peas or corn, toss them in during the last few minutes of cooking.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

  1. Once the noodles are cooked, remove the pot from the heat. If desired, stir in a splash of lemon juice for a bright, zesty flavor.

  2. Garnish with fresh chopped parsley before serving.

Tips for the Best Chicken Noodle Soup

  1. Use Homemade Broth: If time allows, making your own chicken broth can elevate the flavor of your soup. The longer you simmer your broth, the richer the flavor will be.

  2. Vegetable Variations: Feel free to mix in other vegetables like green beans, mushrooms, or spinach for added nutrition and flavor.

  3. Storage: Chicken noodle soup can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. If you plan to freeze it, consider freezing the broth and noodles separately, as noodles can become mushy when thawed.

  4. Reheating: When reheating, add a splash of water or broth to loosen the soup, as it may thicken in the fridge.

Health Benefits of Chicken Noodle Soup

Chicken noodle soup isn’t just comforting; it also has several health benefits. The combination of chicken and vegetables provides essential nutrients, while the warm broth helps to keep you hydrated.

  1. Rich in Protein: Chicken is a great source of lean protein, which is essential for muscle repair and overall health.

  2. Nutrient-Dense: The vegetables in the soup contribute vitamins and minerals, boosting your immune system and promoting overall well-being.

  3. Hydrating: The broth helps to keep you hydrated, which is particularly important when you’re feeling unwell.

  4. Soothing Properties: The warm soup can help alleviate symptoms of colds and flu, making it a go-to remedy for many.

Conclusion: A Bowl of Comfort

Classic American chicken noodle soup is more than just a recipe; it’s a comforting tradition that has stood the test of time. Whether you’re feeling under the weather, looking for a quick weeknight meal, or simply seeking a dish that warms your soul, this chicken noodle soup is sure to satisfy.

As you savor each spoonful, you’re not only enjoying a delicious meal but also a piece of culinary history that connects generations. So gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and create a pot of this classic soup that will fill your kitchen with warmth and your heart with comfort. Enjoy!
