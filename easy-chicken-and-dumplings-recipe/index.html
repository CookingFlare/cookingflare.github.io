Easy Chicken and Dumplings Recipe


When chilly evenings sneak in, and comfort food cravings hit hard, nothing soothes quite like a hearty bowl of chicken and dumplings. This classic dish, baked into the tapestry of Southern cooking and cherished across countless kitchens, is both simple and profoundly satisfying. The creamy broth, tender chicken, and pillowy dumplings create a harmony of flavors and textures that’s nothing short of magic.

If you’re looking for a straightforward, foolproof version that doesn’t require hours of simmering or complicated ingredients, you’re in the right place. This easy chicken and dumplings recipe brings together minimal prep, basic pantry staples, and a little love to give you a bowl of pure comfort in under an hour.

Why Chicken and Dumplings?

Chicken and dumplings stand out for their ability to bridge generations and seasons. The dish balances protein and carbs in a way that feels indulgent but also nourishing. It’s adaptable: you can tailor the broth to your liking, experiment with herbs, or introduce vegetables for added depth. And best of all, it’s approachable enough for home cooks of any skill level.

Many recipes rely on making dumplings from scratch with flour and shortening, folding them delicately into simmering stew. That’s delicious, but can feel intimidating. Our method streamlines the process, using simple dough that cooks perfectly and absorbs the rich broth gracefully. Whether you serve it for a cozy family dinner or to guests craving comforting soul food, this recipe is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this easy chicken and dumplings recipe, gather the following:

  • Chicken: Bone-in, skinless chicken thighs or breasts work best, as they add flavor and moisture. Thighs are juicier and more forgiving, while breasts are leaner.
  • Broth: Chicken broth or stock forms the base of the stew. Opt for low sodium if you want to control salt levels.
  • Vegetables: Classic mirepoix vegetables—carrots, celery, and onions—add sweetness, texture, and aroma.
  • Flour: All-purpose flour thickens the broth and makes dumplings when combined with baking powder and milk.
  • Baking Powder: Gives dumplings their light, airy quality.
  • Milk or Buttermilk: Provides moisture and tenderness to the dumpling dough.
  • Butter: Adds richness to the dumplings and the stew.
  • Seasonings: Salt, black pepper, thyme, and bay leaves amplify flavors.
  • Parsley: For a fresh finishing touch.

Step-by-Step Cooking Process

Step 1: Prep the Chicken and Vegetables

Start by patting the chicken dry. This helps get a nice golden sear if you decide to brown it before simmering. Dice the onion, celery, and carrots into uniform pieces to ensure even cooking. This classic trio—the mirepoix—forms the aromatic foundation.

Step 2: Build the Broth

In a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven, sauté the onions, carrots, and celery in a couple of tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Stir often until they become fragrant and slightly softened, about 5 minutes.

Add your chicken pieces, seasoning generously with salt, pepper, and thyme. Pour in the chicken broth, enough to fully submerge the chicken, and toss in one or two bay leaves. Bring everything to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and cover the pot. Let the chicken simmer until tender—typically 30-40 minutes.

Step 3: Shred the Chicken

Once the chicken is cooked through and tender, remove it from the broth with tongs or a slotted spoon and set aside to cool enough to handle. Discard the bay leaves at this point.

Using two forks, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces. The shredded chicken will mingle beautifully with the dumplings and broth.

Step 4: Make the Dumplings Dough

For the dumplings, combine 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1 tablespoon of baking powder, and a pinch of salt in a mixing bowl. Incorporate about 1 cup of milk and 2 tablespoons of melted butter, stirring just until the dough forms. Be careful not to overmix—slightly sticky dough yields tender dumplings.

Step 5: Bring It All Together

Return the shredded chicken to the pot with the simmered vegetables and broth. If you want a thicker stew, whisk a slurry of 2 tablespoons flour and a bit of cold water into the broth and simmer until it thickens slightly.

Drop spoonfuls of the dumpling dough gently onto the simmering liquid—don’t overcrowd, as the dumplings will expand while cooking. Cover the pot tightly and let the dumplings steam for 15 minutes without lifting the lid, allowing them to puff up and cook through completely.

Step 6: Final Touches

After 15 minutes, remove the lid and check the dumplings; they should be fluffy and cooked inside. Add freshly chopped parsley and adjust salt and pepper to taste. A splash of cream or a knob of butter can be stirred in now for extra richness. Ladle generous servings into bowls and enjoy hot.

Tips for Perfect Chicken and Dumplings Every Time

  • Use Tasteful Broth: Homemade or high-quality store-bought chicken broth elevates the flavor dramatically.
  • Don’t Skip the Mirepoix: Onions, celery, and carrots add subtle sweetness and depth no shortcut can match.
  • Gentle Simmer: Keep the heat low during simmering and dumpling cooking. Vigorous boiling will break apart the dumplings.
  • Fresh Herbs: Thyme and bay leaf are traditional, but rosemary or sage make lovely variations.
  • Creaminess: Add a splash of cream, half-and-half, or even coconut milk just before serving for a richer mouthfeel.
  • Dumplings Variations: Try mixing in finely chopped chives or cheddar into the dumpling dough for a tasty twist.

Variations to Explore

  • Vegetable Boost: Add peas, corn, or chopped green beans in the last 5 minutes of cooking for extra color and nutrients.
  • Spicy Kick: Stir in a pinch of cayenne or smoked paprika to the broth for warmth and boldness.
  • Herb-Infused Dumplings: Substitute fresh dill or parsley into the dumpling dough for an herbaceous note.
  • Slow Cooker Version: Brown chicken and vegetables then transfer to a slow cooker with broth. Add the dumplings dough near the end, cooking on high for 30 minutes.

What to Serve with Chicken and Dumplings?

Chicken and dumplings are a meal in themselves, but pairing them with complementary sides can turn dinner into a feast:

  • Simple Green Salad: A crisp salad with vinaigrette cuts through the meal’s richness.
  • Steamed Greens: Wilted spinach, collard greens, or kale add fresh, earthy elements.
  • Buttery Cornbread: The sweet crumb of cornbread stands up perfectly to the creamy stew.
  • Roasted Root Vegetables: Adds sweet and savory contrast.

Storage and Reheating

This dish reheats beautifully and tends to taste even better the next day as the flavors meld. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. When reheating, warm gently on the stove or microwave with a splash of broth or water to prevent drying out.

Final Thoughts

Easy chicken and dumplings combine ease with comfort, creating a one-pot wonder that fills your kitchen with enticing aromas and your belly with warmth. It’s a versatile recipe that’s forgiving to work with and adaptable to your family’s preferences. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a beginner, this recipe will quickly become a beloved go-to for chilly evenings and family gatherings.

Pull out your biggest pot, gather your ingredients, and get ready to fill your home with the cozy, homey goodness of chicken and dumplings! Your taste buds will thank you.
