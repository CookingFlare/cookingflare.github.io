Zesty Lime Coconut Bars: A Tropical Twist on Classic Bars


If you’re looking for a delightful tropical treat that will transport your taste buds to a sunny beach, then look no further than these zesty lime coconut bars. This recipe is a unique and refreshing take on the classic dessert bars, infusing them with the tangy flavor of lime and the luscious sweetness of coconut. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering, craving a tropical escape, or simply want to add a burst of sunshine to your day, these zesty lime coconut bars are the perfect choice.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ⅓ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

For the Filling:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups granulated sugar
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup fresh lime juice
  • Zest of 2 limes
  • 1 cup shredded coconut

For the Glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2-3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • Zest of 1 lime

Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Crust

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 9x13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter. Mix until the crumbs are evenly coated.
  3. Press the mixture firmly and evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove it from the oven and set it aside to cool.

Step 2: Making the Filling

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until well combined.
  2. Add the flour, baking powder, lime juice, lime zest, and shredded coconut to the egg mixture. Stir until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
  3. Pour the filling over the cooled crust, spreading it out into an even layer.

Step 3: Baking and Glazing

  1. Bake the bars for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are golden and the center is set.
  2. While the bars are baking, prepare the glaze by whisking together the powdered sugar, lime juice, and lime zest in a small bowl.
  3. Once the bars are done baking, remove them from the oven and allow them to cool for 10-15 minutes. Drizzle the glaze over the bars, then let them cool completely in the pan.
  4. Once cooled, use the parchment paper overhang to lift the bars out of the pan. Cut them into squares or bars of your desired size.

Serving and Storing

These zesty lime coconut bars are best enjoyed chilled, making them the perfect cool and refreshing dessert for a hot summer day. The combination of tangy lime, sweet coconut, and buttery crust creates a symphony of flavors that will have your taste buds dancing with delight. They can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, but let’s be honest – they probably won’t last that long once you’ve had your first bite!

Whether you’re a fan of citrusy desserts, a lover of all things coconut, or simply in need of a taste of the tropics, these zesty lime coconut bars are sure to become a new favorite in your recipe collection. So, why not bring a slice of paradise into your kitchen and whip up a batch of these delightful bars today? Your taste buds will thank you for it!
