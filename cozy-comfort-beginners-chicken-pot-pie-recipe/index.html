Cozy Comfort: Beginner's Chicken Pot Pie Recipe


There’s nothing quite like a homemade chicken pot pie to warm the soul and satisfy the taste buds. This classic comfort food is a staple in many households, and for good reason. The combination of flaky pastry, savory chicken, and hearty vegetables makes for a truly comforting meal that’s perfect for any occasion. If you’re new to the world of cooking or looking for a simple yet delicious recipe to add to your repertoire, this beginner’s chicken pot pie recipe is just what you need.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and diced
  • 1 cup carrots, diced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup celery, diced
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon celery seed
  • 1 3/4 cups chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup milk

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pastry:

    • In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Cut in the butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice water, tossing with a fork until a ball forms. Wrap in plastic and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling:

    • Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
    • In a saucepan, combine the carrots, peas, and celery. Add water to cover and boil for 15 minutes. Drain and set aside.
    • In the same saucepan, cook the butter over medium heat until melted. Stir in the flour, salt, pepper, and celery seed. Gradually stir in the chicken broth and milk. Simmer over medium-low heat until thick. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Assemble the Pie:

    • Roll out the pastry to fit a 9-inch pie plate. Place bottom crust in the pie plate. Pour in the chicken mixture. Cover with top crust, seal the edges, and cut away any excess dough. Make several small slits in the top to allow steam to escape.

  4. Bake the Pie:

    • Bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until pastry is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Cool for 10 minutes before serving.

Tips for Success

  • To save time, you can use store-bought pie crust instead of making your own.
  • Feel free to customize the filling with your favorite vegetables or add herbs and spices for extra flavor.
  • Leftover chicken pot pie can be stored in the refrigerator for 3-4 days and reheated in the oven for a quick and delicious meal.

This beginner’s chicken pot pie recipe is a great introduction to the world of savory pies. Its simple preparation and delightful flavors make it a perfect dish for cozy family dinners or casual gatherings with friends. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or just starting out, this recipe is sure to become a beloved classic in your culinary repertoire. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create a warm and comforting chicken pot pie that will have everyone coming back for seconds. Enjoy!
