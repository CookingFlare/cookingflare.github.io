DIY Condiments: Healthier Versions of Store-Bought Favorites


Are you looking to jazz up your sandwiches, coz up your veggie trays, or cook up some healthier condiments? Well, you’ve come to the right place! Here’s a guide to making your favorite store-bought condiments at home without the high fructose corn syrup, artificial colors, or preservatives.

  1. Homemade Ketchup

Everyone loves ketchup, or as my four-year-old niece calls it, “itty-bitty red sauce.” And a version with fewer additives and preservatives awaits on this side! Whip up this healthier version of everyone’s favorite condiment in a few simple steps.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Combine all ingredients in a medium-sized stainless steel saucepan.

  2. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil over medium heat.

  3. Reduce heat and simmer mixture for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  4. Use an immersion blender (or regular blender in a drip/backflow-prevention container) to blend the mixture until smooth.

  5. Cool, bottle, and refrigerate.

  6. Spicy Mustard

I love adding a dollop of mustard on my burgers or hot dogs. Apart from the zesty taste, mustard is proven to be packed with nutrients. Bring some more goodness to your plate with this recipe!

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups brown mustard seeds
  • 1 cup yellow mustard seeds
  • 1 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup honey
  • 4 tablespoons horseradish
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon celery seed

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine brown and yellow mustard seeds.

  2. In a saucepan, heat apple cider vinegar, water, honey, and 1 tablespoon salt.

  3. Pour the hot mixture over the mustard seeds, stirring to combine.

  4. Add horseradish, garlic powder, onion powder, and celery seed.

  5. Cover the mixture and let it sit at room temperature overnight, then for a week or more in the refrigerator for the flavors to develop.

  6. Enjoy on sandwiches, hot dogs, or as a dip.

  7. Roasted Garlic Aioli

Roasted garlic aioli, the perfect upgrade from just plain mayonnaise! This creamy, flavorful sauce pairs excellently with grilled sandwiches or as a dip for fries.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mayonnaise
  • 3 large garlic cloves, roasted and minced
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, garlic, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.

  2. Taste and adjust seasonings as desired.

  3. Cool and refrigerate until ready to use.

  4. Sriracha Mayo

A supermarket version of Sriracha Mayo is bomb, but a homemade version, even better! You get to control the amount of spicy kick in this dip or spread depending on your preference.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • ¼ cup sriracha sauce
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together mayonnaise, sriracha sauce, lime juice, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Taste, and adjust seasonings if desired.

DIY condiments bring so much more than flavor to your meals - you get to control the ingredients, the sweetness, the spiciness, or creaminess. And the best part? Making condiments is a fun and creative activity you can do with your family or friends. Keep experimenting and creating your favorite condiments with whizzed-down versions, along with natural and healthier twists!
