Fall Cooking with Kids: 5 Fun Recipes to Make Together


As the crisp autumn air rolls in and the days get shorter, it’s the perfect time to get cozy in the kitchen with your little ones. Fall brings an abundance of delicious seasonal ingredients that make for tasty, comforting meals that both you and your kids will love. The cooler weather makes it an ideal season for baking, slow-cooking, and creating fun, hands-on dishes. This fall, why not take advantage of the seasonal bounty and create some memorable moments with your kids by cooking together?

Cooking with kids not only helps build essential skills, but it also creates lasting memories and teaches them about healthy eating habits. Whether you’re looking for fun breakfast options, savory dinners, or sweet treats, these five kid-friendly recipes will help you make the most of fall’s flavors while engaging your little chefs.

1. Apple Cinnamon Pancakes

Ingredients:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg
  • 1 ½ cups milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter, melted
  • 1 large apple, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Maple syrup, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prep the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt. This is a great step to let your kids help with. They can pour and mix, which will make them feel involved in the process.

  2. Prepare the wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat the egg and add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Stir well to combine.

  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently stir to combine. Be careful not to overmix—lumps are okay! Once combined, fold in the chopped apple pieces.

  4. Cook the pancakes: Heat a nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Grease with a little butter or cooking spray. Pour about ¼ cup of pancake batter onto the skillet for each pancake. Cook until bubbles start to form on the surface, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown.

  5. Serve and enjoy: Stack the pancakes on a plate and drizzle with maple syrup. Let your kids help drizzle the syrup, which will make them excited to eat the finished product.

These apple cinnamon pancakes are not only fluffy and delicious but are also an excellent way to incorporate fall flavors into breakfast. Plus, they’re easy to make and fun for kids to assist with!

2. Pumpkin Pasta

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound pasta (spaghetti, penne, or any type your kids prefer)
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree (canned or homemade)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions. Drain the pasta and set it aside.

  2. Prepare the sauce: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, or until fragrant. Stir in the pumpkin puree, sage, salt, and black pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Combine pasta and sauce: Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss it well with the pumpkin sauce until it’s evenly coated. If the sauce seems too thick, add a small splash of pasta water to thin it out.

  4. Top with cheese and garnish: Sprinkle the grated Parmesan cheese over the pasta and give it one final toss. Garnish with chopped parsley if desired.

  5. Serve: Serve the pumpkin pasta warm. Allow your kids to help sprinkle the cheese and stir the pasta, making them feel like little chefs.

This creamy pumpkin pasta is the perfect savory dish for fall. The mild sweetness of the pumpkin pairs wonderfully with the savory garlic and sage, making it a flavor-packed dish that will become a favorite for your whole family.

3. Sweet Potato Quesadillas

Ingredients:

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 flour tortillas
  • 1 ½ cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • Sour cream and salsa, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sweet potatoes: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the diced sweet potatoes on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and pepper. Toss to coat evenly. Roast the sweet potatoes for 20-25 minutes, or until they are soft and slightly crispy on the edges.

  2. Assemble the quesadillas: Once the sweet potatoes are done, remove them from the oven. In a large bowl, mash them with a fork until smooth, leaving a few chunks for texture. In the center of each tortilla, spread a spoonful of mashed sweet potatoes, top with black beans, and sprinkle with cheddar cheese.

  3. Cook the quesadillas: Heat a skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with cooking spray or a little olive oil. Place one tortilla in the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until the cheese begins to melt and the tortilla is golden brown. Flip the quesadilla and cook for another 1-2 minutes on the other side. Repeat with the remaining tortillas.

  4. Serve: Slice the quesadillas into wedges and serve with sour cream and salsa on the side.

These sweet potato quesadillas are a wonderful combination of savory and slightly sweet flavors. They’re easy for kids to help with, whether it’s mashing the sweet potatoes or layering the ingredients. Plus, they’re packed with nutrients!

4. Mini Apple Crisp Cups

Ingredients:

  • 4 apples, peeled and diced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 ½ cups rolled oats
  • ½ cup flour
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup butter, softened
  • Pinch of salt
  • Vanilla ice cream, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the apples: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large bowl, toss the diced apples with lemon juice, cinnamon, and maple syrup until well coated. Divide the apple mixture evenly among a muffin tin or cupcake tin, filling each cup about ¾ full.

  2. Make the crisp topping: In a separate bowl, combine the oats, flour, brown sugar, butter, and a pinch of salt. Use your hands or a fork to blend the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Top the apples: Sprinkle the oat topping evenly over the apples in each cup, pressing it down slightly to ensure it sticks.

  4. Bake: Bake the mini apple crisps in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the apples are soft and the topping is golden brown.

  5. Serve: Let the crisp cups cool slightly before serving. Top each with a scoop of vanilla ice cream if desired.

These mini apple crisps are the perfect fall dessert for kids to help with. They can assist in layering the apples, making the crisp topping, and even adding the ice cream on top! The individual serving size also makes them easy for kids to handle and enjoy.

5. Caramelized Onion and Butternut Squash Soup

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (optional)
  • Cream or yogurt, for serving (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Roast the squash: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Spread the butternut squash cubes on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Roast for 20-25 minutes or until tender and slightly caramelized.

  2. Caramelize the onions: While the squash roasts, heat a large pot over medium heat and add the remaining olive oil. Add the sliced onions and sauté for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onions are golden brown and caramelized.

  3. Combine and simmer: Once the squash is roasted, add it to the pot with the caramelized onions. Pour in the vegetable broth, thyme, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook for an additional 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  4. Blend the soup: Using an immersion blender or a regular blender, puree the soup until smooth and creamy. If using a regular blender, blend in batches and return to the pot once smooth.

  5. Finish and serve: Stir in maple syrup for added sweetness if desired. Serve the soup with a drizzle of cream or a dollop of yogurt for extra creaminess.

This butternut squash soup is a great way to introduce kids to the savory side of fall flavors. The caramelized onions bring out a rich sweetness that pairs wonderfully with the squash, making it a comforting and nutritious meal for the whole family.

Final Thoughts

Cooking with your kids during the fall season is a wonderful way to bond while creating delicious, seasonal dishes. From sweet breakfasts to savory dinners and comforting desserts, there are plenty of opportunities to make memories in the kitchen. Not only will your kids learn valuable cooking skills, but they’ll also get to experience the joy of creating something from scratch and enjoying the fruits of their labor.

So, gather your little chefs, roll up your sleeves, and start making these tasty fall recipes together!
