Tuscan Ribollita: A Hearty Italian Vegetable Soup


When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the heartiness and flavor of Tuscan Ribollita. This traditional Italian vegetable soup is not only delicious and satisfying, but it also has a rich history that dates back centuries. In this blog post, we’ll explore the origins of Ribollita and provide you with a step-by-step recipe to recreate this culinary masterpiece in your own kitchen.

Origins of Ribollita

Ribollita, which translates to “reboiled,” is a soup that originated in the Tuscany region of Italy. It is believed to have been created by peasants who would use leftover vegetables and stale bread to create a nourishing and economical meal. The soup was typically made in large quantities and reheated over the course of several days, with each reheating intensifying the flavors.

The Ingredients

To make a classic Ribollita, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch of kale, roughly chopped
  • 1 can of cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon of dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of dried rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 slices of stale bread, torn into small pieces

The Recipe

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5 minutes.

  2. Add the kale to the pot and cook until wilted, about 3 minutes.

  3. Stir in the cannellini beans, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, dried thyme, dried rosemary, salt, and pepper. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  4. Add the torn bread to the pot and stir well. The bread will break down and thicken the soup, creating a hearty and rustic texture.

  5. Continue to simmer the soup for another 10 minutes, or until the bread has completely dissolved and the soup has thickened.

  6. Serve the Ribollita hot, garnished with a drizzle of olive oil and a sprinkle of freshly grated Parmesan cheese, if desired.

Tips and Variations

  • For a more authentic flavor, use Tuscan kale, also known as Lacinato or dinosaur kale. Its dark, crinkly leaves add a unique texture to the soup.

  • Ribollita is traditionally made with stale bread, but you can also use fresh bread if that’s what you have on hand. Just be aware that fresh bread will dissolve more quickly, resulting in a slightly different texture.

  • Feel free to customize your Ribollita by adding other vegetables such as zucchini, potatoes, or cabbage. This soup is incredibly versatile, so don’t be afraid to get creative with the ingredients.

Final Thoughts

Tuscan Ribollita is a true testament to the ingenuity of Italian cuisine. With its humble origins and robust flavors, this vegetable soup has stood the test of time and continues to be a beloved dish around the world. Whether you’re looking for a comforting meal on a cold winter’s day or simply want to explore the flavors of Tuscany, Ribollita is sure to satisfy your cravings. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to savor a bowl of this hearty Italian soup. Buon appetito!
