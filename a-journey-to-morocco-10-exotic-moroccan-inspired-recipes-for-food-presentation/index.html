A Journey to Morocco: 10 Exotic Moroccan-inspired Recipes for Food Presentation


Morocco, a country where the scent of spices and the sound of the call to prayer from the mosque mingle in the air, is a culinary paradise. Moroccan cuisine is a unique blend of African, Arab, and Mediterranean flavors, with a focus on bold spices, vibrant colors, and intricate presentation. As we embark on this gastronomic journey, we’ll discover 10 exotic Moroccan-inspired recipes that will transport your taste buds to the bustling souks and tranquil riads of Morocco.

Recipe 1: Tagine of Chicken and Preserved Lemons

Tagine, a slow-cooked stew named after the earthenware pot in which it’s cooked, is a staple of Moroccan cuisine. This recipe combines chicken, preserved lemons, and aromatic spices to create a rich, tangy sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 preserved lemons, chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large tagine or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and turmeric. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  3. Add chicken, preserved lemons, and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer for 30 minutes or until chicken is cooked through.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with parsley and serve over couscous.

Recipe 2: B’stilla of Spinach and Feta

B’stilla, a Moroccan savory pastry, is typically filled with meat and onions. This vegetarian version substitutes spinach and feta for a lighter, yet still flavorful, filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package puff pastry, thawed
  • 1 package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Roll out puff pastry to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Cut into a large rectangle.
  3. In a bowl, combine spinach, feta, parsley, garlic, and cumin. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Spread spinach mixture evenly over the center of the pastry, leaving a 1-inch border around the edges.
  5. Brush edges with beaten egg for a golden glaze. Fold pastry over filling, pressing edges to seal.
  6. Brush top with egg wash and bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown.

Recipe 3: Chakchouka of Bell Peppers and Tomatoes

Chakchouka, a spicy vegetable stew, is a popular Moroccan dish. This recipe combines bell peppers, tomatoes, and onions for a colorful and flavorful meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large bell peppers, any color, sliced
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add bell peppers and cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Add tomatoes and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with parsley and serve over couscous.

Recipe 4: Msemen of Spinach and Feta

Msemen, a Moroccan savory pancake, is typically filled with meat and onions. This vegetarian version substitutes spinach and feta for a lighter, yet still flavorful, filling.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • 1 package frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar. Gradually add oil and water, mixing until a smooth dough forms.
  2. Knead dough for 5-7 minutes until elastic. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide dough into 4 equal pieces. Roll out each piece into a thin circle.
  4. In a bowl, combine spinach, feta, parsley, garlic, and cumin. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Place a spoonful of spinach mixture in the center of each dough circle. Fold dough over filling, forming a triangle, and press edges to seal.
  6. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. Cook msemen for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown.

Recipe 5: Couscous with Preserved Lemons and Herbs

Couscous, a traditional Moroccan dish, is typically served with meat and vegetables. This recipe elevates the dish with the addition of preserved lemons and fresh herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 preserved lemon, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Bring water to a boil in a large pot. Add couscous and stir to combine. Cover and remove from heat.
  2. Let couscous sit for 5-7 minutes or until water is absorbed and couscous is tender.
  3. Fluff couscous with a fork. Heat oil in a small skillet over medium heat. Add preserved lemon and cook until caramelized, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in parsley and cilantro. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve couscous with preserved lemon mixture spooned over the top.

Recipe 6: Zaalouk of Eggplant and Tomatoes

Zaalouk, a Moroccan stew, is typically made with meat and vegetables. This vegetarian version substitutes eggplant and tomatoes for a rich and flavorful dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants, sliced
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups water
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add eggplant and cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Add tomatoes and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with parsley and serve over couscous.

Recipe 7: Merguez of Lamb and Spices

Merguez, a Moroccan sausage, is typically made with lamb and spices. This recipe elevates the dish with the addition of fresh herbs and a tangy yogurt sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp honey

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine lamb, onion, garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper. Mix well with your hands until just combined.
  2. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  3. Preheat grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Form lamb mixture into patties. Grill for 4-5 minutes per side or until cooked through.
  4. In a bowl, combine parsley, cilantro, yogurt, lemon juice, and honey. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve merguez with yogurt sauce spooned over the top.

Recipe 8: Harira of Lentils and Spices

Harira, a Moroccan soup, is typically made with lentils and spices. This recipe elevates the dish with the addition of fresh herbs and a tangy lemon juice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green or brown lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh parsley
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Rinse lentils and pick out any debris. In a large pot, combine lentils and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until lentils are tender.
  2. Heat oil in a small skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  4. Stir in parsley, cilantro, and lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve harira hot, garnished with additional parsley and cilantro if desired.

Recipe 9: Bouchata of Carrots and Ginger

Bouchata, a Moroccan salad, is typically made with carrots and ginger. This recipe elevates the dish with the addition of fresh herbs and a tangy lemon juice.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large carrots, peeled and grated
  • 2 large pieces fresh ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine carrots and ginger. Stir to combine.
  2. Stir in parsley, cilantro, and lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Serve bouchata chilled or at room temperature, garnished with additional parsley and cilantro if desired.

Recipe 10: Makroud of Dates and Almonds

Makroud, a Moroccan cookie, is typically made with dates and almonds. This recipe elevates the dish with the addition of fresh herbs and a tangy orange juice.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pitted dates, chopped
  • 1 cup sliced almonds
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 2 tbsp orange juice
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, combine dates, almonds, parsley, and cilantro. Mix well with your hands until just combined.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together orange juice and cinnamon. Pour over date mixture and mix until a dough forms.
  4. Roll out dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Cut into desired shapes.
  5. Place on prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.

As we conclude our journey through Morocco, we’ve discovered 10 exotic recipes that showcase the country’s rich culinary heritage. From the bold flavors of tagine to the sweet treats of makroud, each recipe is a testament to the country’s unique blend of African, Arab, and Mediterranean flavors. Whether you’re a seasoned cook or a curious foodie, these recipes are sure to transport your taste buds to the vibrant souks and tranquil riads of Morocco. Bon appétit!
