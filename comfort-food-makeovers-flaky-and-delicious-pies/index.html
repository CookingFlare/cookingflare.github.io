Comfort Food Makeovers: Flaky and Delicious Pies


Who doesn’t love a good pie? The combination of a flaky crust and a delicious filling is a comfort food classic that never fails to satisfy. But what if we told you that you can still enjoy all the decadence of a pie while making it a bit healthier? In this post, we will explore some comforting pie recipes that have been given a makeover to be both nutritious and delicious. Get ready to indulge guilt-free!

Sweet Potato Pie with Oat Crust

Let’s start with a classic: sweet potato pie. This version swaps the traditional butter-laden crust for a lighter and more wholesome oat crust. The result? A pie that is just as tasty but with a little extra fiber and nutrients.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 ½ cups rolled oats
    • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
    • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
    • Pinch of salt

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups mashed sweet potatoes
    • 1/2 cup coconut milk
    • 1/4 cup maple syrup
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. In a food processor, pulse the rolled oats until they reach a flour-like consistency.

  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the oat flour, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and a pinch of salt. Mix until well combined and press the mixture into a pie dish to form the crust.

  4. In another mixing bowl, whisk together the mashed sweet potatoes, coconut milk, maple syrup, eggs, vanilla extract, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and a pinch of salt until smooth and well combined.

  5. Pour the sweet potato filling into the prepared oat crust and spread it evenly.

  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.

  7. Remove from the oven and let it cool before serving. Enjoy!

Chicken Pot Pie with Cauliflower Crust

Now let’s move on to a savory pie makeover: chicken pot pie. This version replaces the traditional pastry crust with a cauliflower crust, making it lower in carbs while still being incredibly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • For the crust:

    • 1 head of cauliflower, cut into florets
    • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
    • 1/4 cup almond flour
    • 1 egg
    • Salt and pepper to taste

  • For the filling:

    • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
    • 1 cup chicken broth
    • 1 cup chopped mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
    • 1/2 cup diced onion
    • 1/4 cup almond flour
    • 1/4 cup coconut milk
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  2. Place the cauliflower florets in a food processor and pulse until they resemble rice.

  3. Steam the cauliflower rice until tender, then transfer it to a clean kitchen towel and squeeze out any excess moisture.

  4. In a mixing bowl, combine the cauliflower rice, grated Parmesan cheese, almond flour, egg, salt, and pepper. Mix until well combined.

  5. Press the cauliflower mixture into a pie dish to form the crust. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown.

  6. In the meantime, heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until onions are translucent.

  7. Add the almond flour to the skillet and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.

  8. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and coconut milk, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens.

  9. Add the cooked chicken, mixed vegetables, dried thyme, salt, and pepper to the skillet and stir until well combined.

  10. Pour the chicken filling into the baked cauliflower crust and spread it evenly.

  11. Bake in the oven for an additional 20 minutes, or until the filling is bubbly and the crust is crispy.

  12. Allow the pie to cool slightly before serving. Dig in and enjoy the comforting flavors!

Conclusion

Comfort food doesn’t have to be unhealthy. With a few simple tweaks, you can transform your favorite pies into nutritious and delicious dishes that will satisfy your cravings without the guilt. Whether you’re in the mood for a sweet potato pie with an oat crust or a chicken pot pie with a cauliflower crust, these comfort food makeovers will leave you feeling satisfied and nourished. So go ahead, indulge in a slice of flaky goodness and enjoy the comfort of a pie that’s both wholesome and delicious.
