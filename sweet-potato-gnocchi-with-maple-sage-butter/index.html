Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Maple Sage Butter


There’s something about the crisp air and rich flavors of fall that make us crave comforting, hearty meals that also capture the essence of the season. One dish that perfectly embodies the spirit of autumn is Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Maple Sage Butter. This mouthwatering dish combines the soft, pillowy texture of homemade gnocchi with the earthy sweetness of roasted sweet potatoes, all enhanced by the deep flavors of maple syrup and aromatic sage. Whether you’re preparing a cozy meal for yourself or hosting a dinner party, this dish will quickly become a favorite.

In this recipe, we’ll guide you through every step of making tender, melt-in-your-mouth gnocchi, from roasting the sweet potatoes to crafting the perfect maple sage butter sauce. With a handful of ingredients and a bit of patience, you’ll be able to create an unforgettable dish that’s bursting with flavor. Let’s dive in!

Ingredients You’ll Need

For the Sweet Potato Gnocchi:

  • 2 medium-sized sweet potatoes
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • A pinch of nutmeg
  • A pinch of black pepper

For the Maple Sage Butter Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 8-10 fresh sage leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon (optional for added warmth)
  • A pinch of sea salt, to taste
  • Freshly cracked black pepper, to taste

Tools You’ll Need:

  • A baking sheet for roasting sweet potatoes
  • A large mixing bowl
  • A fork or potato masher
  • A pot of boiling water
  • A slotted spoon or spider
  • A sauté pan for the butter sauce
  • A gnocchi board or fork (optional, but helpful for shaping the gnocchi)

Step 1: Roasting the Sweet Potatoes

The first step in creating your gnocchi is to roast the sweet potatoes. Roasting brings out the natural sweetness and depth of flavor in the sweet potatoes, making them the perfect base for this dish.

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Pierce each sweet potato with a fork a few times to allow steam to escape while cooking.
  3. Place the sweet potatoes on a baking sheet and roast them for 45 to 60 minutes, or until they are tender when pierced with a fork.
  4. Once done, allow the sweet potatoes to cool for a few minutes before peeling off the skins. Mash the flesh with a fork or potato masher until smooth. You should end up with about 2 cups of mashed sweet potato.

This roasted sweet potato will serve as the base for your gnocchi, giving them a rich, silky texture and a slightly sweet flavor that perfectly balances the savory ingredients in the dish.

Step 2: Making the Gnocchi Dough

Once your sweet potatoes are roasted and mashed, it’s time to prepare the gnocchi dough. This is the fun part, where you’ll combine the mashed sweet potatoes with flour, egg, and seasoning to create a dough that can be shaped into little pillows of deliciousness.

  1. Place the mashed sweet potatoes into a large mixing bowl and make a well in the center.
  2. Add the egg, grated Parmesan cheese, salt, nutmeg, and black pepper into the well. Use a fork to gently beat the egg and mix it with the sweet potato.
  3. Gradually incorporate the flour into the sweet potato mixture, stirring with your hands until a dough begins to form. Be careful not to overwork the dough, as it can become tough. You want a dough that is soft and slightly sticky, but not too wet.
  4. Once the dough has come together, turn it out onto a floured surface. Lightly dust your hands and the surface with flour and gently knead the dough for 2 to 3 minutes until it’s smooth and cohesive.

Now, it’s time to shape your gnocchi!

Step 3: Shaping the Gnocchi

This step requires a bit of patience, but the result is totally worth it. Shaping the gnocchi is what makes them so iconic and recognizable.

  1. Divide the dough into 4 equal portions. Take one portion and roll it into a long log about 1/2 inch thick.
  2. Using a knife or bench scraper, cut the log into small pieces, about 1-inch long.
  3. To shape the gnocchi, you can either press each piece lightly with a fork to create ridges (which helps the sauce cling to the gnocchi) or use a gnocchi board to make the classic grooves. If you don’t have a gnocchi board, don’t worry—fork ridges will do just fine!

Repeat this process with the remaining dough portions. Once your gnocchi are shaped, it’s time to cook them.

Step 4: Cooking the Gnocchi

Now that your gnocchi are shaped and ready, it’s time to cook them. This step is quick and easy!

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a gentle boil. Be sure the water is simmering and not at a rolling boil—this will help the gnocchi cook evenly without falling apart.
  2. Drop the gnocchi into the water in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Once the gnocchi float to the surface, they are ready—this usually takes about 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Use a slotted spoon to carefully remove the gnocchi from the water and set them aside. Repeat with the remaining gnocchi.

At this point, your gnocchi are perfectly cooked and ready to be tossed in the most amazing maple sage butter sauce.

Step 5: Making the Maple Sage Butter Sauce

The star of this dish is the maple sage butter sauce. It’s a sweet and savory sauce that brings everything together in the most incredible way. The richness of the butter complements the earthiness of the sage, while the maple syrup adds a subtle sweetness that pairs perfectly with the roasted sweet potatoes in the gnocchi.

  1. In a large sauté pan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the fresh sage leaves and cook for about 2 to 3 minutes until they become crispy and fragrant. Be careful not to burn them.
  3. Add the maple syrup to the pan, followed by the cinnamon (if using), sea salt, and freshly cracked black pepper. Stir well to combine, and let the sauce simmer for 2 to 3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. If the sauce feels too thick, you can thin it out with a little bit of the water you used to cook the gnocchi. Just add a tablespoon at a time until you reach the desired consistency.

Once your sauce is ready, it’s time to toss the gnocchi!

Step 6: Combining the Gnocchi with the Maple Sage Butter Sauce

Now that your gnocchi are cooked and the maple sage butter sauce is bubbling away, it’s time to bring everything together.

  1. Add the cooked gnocchi to the pan with the maple sage butter sauce.
  2. Gently toss the gnocchi in the sauce, ensuring they’re all coated with the buttery, maple goodness. Cook for another 1 to 2 minutes so the gnocchi can soak up all the flavors.
  3. If desired, sprinkle a little more Parmesan cheese on top for an extra layer of flavor.

Your Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Maple Sage Butter is now ready to serve!

Step 7: Serving Suggestions

This dish is a comforting, hearty meal on its own, but it pairs beautifully with a variety of sides and accompaniments.

  • Salads: A light arugula or spinach salad with a simple vinaigrette can provide a refreshing contrast to the richness of the gnocchi.
  • Roasted Vegetables: Roasted Brussels sprouts, carrots, or butternut squash are perfect complements to the sweet and savory flavors of the dish.
  • Wine Pairing: For a beverage pairing, try a dry white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or a light red like Pinot Noir, which will complement the dish without overwhelming the flavors.

Final Thoughts

Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Maple Sage Butter is a dish that will make your taste buds dance with joy. With the perfect balance of sweetness, savory flavors, and melt-in-your-mouth gnocchi, it’s a dish that’s sure to impress any guest or satisfy your cravings for a cozy, comforting meal. The combination of roasted sweet potatoes, Parmesan, and fresh sage, topped off with the buttery sweetness of maple syrup, creates a symphony of flavors that’s simply irresistible.

Whether you’re celebrating fall, enjoying a weeknight dinner, or hosting a special occasion, this dish will steal the show. So, gather your ingredients, take your time, and enjoy the process of making this incredible Sweet Potato Gnocchi with Maple Sage Butter. Your taste buds will thank you!
