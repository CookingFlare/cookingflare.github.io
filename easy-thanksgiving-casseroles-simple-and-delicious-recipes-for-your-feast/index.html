Easy Thanksgiving Casseroles: Simple and Delicious Recipes for Your Feast


Thanksgiving is a time for family, gratitude, and of course, delicious food. While the turkey might take center stage on the table, let’s not forget about the mouthwatering side dishes that accompany it. This year, why not try something different and add some easy Thanksgiving casseroles to your feast? These simple and delicious recipes are sure to impress your guests and elevate your Thanksgiving meal to a whole new level.

1. Cheesy Green Bean Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans of green beans, drained
  • 1 can of cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup of crispy fried onions
  • 1/2 cup of milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained green beans, cream of mushroom soup, shredded cheddar cheese, milk, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and spread it out evenly.
  4. Sprinkle the crispy fried onions on top of the mixture, covering the entire surface.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the casserole is heated through and the onions on top are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

2. Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel

Ingredients:

  • 4 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of melted butter
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup of chopped pecans
  • 1/4 cup of melted butter

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Place the sweet potato cubes in a large pot and cover them with water. Bring to a boil and cook until the sweet potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain well.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the cooked sweet potatoes, granulated sugar, melted butter, beaten eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and nutmeg. Mix well until all the ingredients are combined.
  4. Transfer the sweet potato mixture into a greased casserole dish and spread it out evenly.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the all-purpose flour, brown sugar, chopped pecans, and melted butter to create the streusel topping. Sprinkle the streusel mixture over the sweet potato mixture, covering the entire surface.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the casserole is heated through and the streusel topping is golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

3. Cornbread Casserole

Ingredients:

  • 1 can of cream-style corn
  • 1 can of whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 box of cornbread mix
  • 1/2 cup of sour cream
  • 1/2 cup of melted butter
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup of sliced green onions (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cream-style corn, drained whole kernel corn, cornbread mix, sour cream, melted butter, shredded cheddar cheese, and sliced green onions (if desired). Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Transfer the mixture into a greased casserole dish and spread it out evenly.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes or until the casserole is golden brown on top and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Conclusion

These easy Thanksgiving casseroles are the perfect addition to your holiday feast. They are simple to make, packed with flavor, and guaranteed to impress your guests. Whether you choose the Cheesy Green Bean Casserole, the Sweet Potato Casserole with Pecan Streusel, or the Cornbread Casserole, you can’t go wrong. So this Thanksgiving, step outside the traditional side dish box and give your taste buds a treat with these delicious casseroles. Happy feasting!
