10 Spanish Dishes That Are Perfect for Summer


Summer is synonymous with vibrant colors, fresh ingredients, and light, flavorful dishes. Spain, with its rich culinary history and diverse regional influences, offers a variety of summer dishes that are both refreshing and satisfying. Whether you’re relaxing by the beach, hosting a summer picnic, or just enjoying a leisurely meal, Spanish cuisine has something to delight every palate.

In this blog post, we’ll explore ten of the best Spanish dishes that are perfect for the warm summer months. These dishes embrace the seasonal bounty of fresh vegetables, fruits, seafood, and herbs, offering a combination of flavors that are ideal for hot weather. From cold soups to grilled seafood, these dishes will take your summer dining experience to the next level.

1. Gazpacho – The Quintessential Spanish Summer Soup

Gazpacho is a cold, refreshing soup that is a hallmark of Spanish cuisine, especially in Andalusia. Made with a blend of ripe tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, garlic, and onions, gazpacho is a summer favorite that keeps you cool while bursting with flavor. The addition of extra virgin olive oil, vinegar, and a pinch of salt brings the whole dish together, creating a perfect balance of acidity and sweetness.

One of the beauties of gazpacho is its versatility. You can adjust the ingredients according to your preference, adding more vegetables or even incorporating fruits like watermelon for a sweet twist. Serve it chilled with a drizzle of olive oil and a few croutons for added texture.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes
  • 1 cucumber, peeled
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1/2 red onion
  • 1-2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Croutons (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. Blend until smooth.
  2. Add the olive oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper, and blend again.
  3. Chill the soup for at least two hours.
  4. Serve cold with croutons and a drizzle of olive oil.

2. Salmorejo – A Creamy Tomato Delight

Another classic from Andalusia, salmorejo is a thicker, creamier version of gazpacho. While both soups feature tomatoes as the base, salmorejo is made with fewer ingredients, allowing the flavor of the tomatoes to truly shine. This dish is made by blending ripe tomatoes with bread, olive oil, and garlic, resulting in a velvety smooth texture.

Salmorejo is traditionally topped with hard-boiled eggs and jamón (Spanish cured ham), making it a satisfying dish that works as both a starter or a light main course.

Ingredients:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes
  • 2 cups stale white bread (preferably baguette)
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic
  • Salt to taste
  • Hard-boiled eggs and jamón, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the bread in water for a few minutes, then squeeze out the excess moisture.
  2. Blend the tomatoes, soaked bread, garlic, and olive oil until smooth.
  3. Season with salt to taste and chill in the fridge for at least two hours.
  4. Garnish with chopped hard-boiled eggs and thin slices of jamón before serving.

3. Paella de Marisco – Seafood Paella

Paella is perhaps the most famous Spanish dish worldwide. Originating from Valencia, paella is a traditional rice dish that can be made with a variety of ingredients, but the seafood version (paella de marisco) is especially popular in the summer months when seafood is abundant.

This flavorful dish features a mix of fresh seafood, including shrimp, mussels, clams, and squid, cooked together with saffron-infused rice. The aroma and color of paella de marisco evoke the essence of summer, with every bite being a delightful explosion of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice (preferably bomba rice)
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup white wine
  • 1 tsp saffron threads
  • 4 cups seafood stock
  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb mussels and clams, cleaned
  • 1/2 lb squid, sliced
  • Lemon wedges, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the rice and stir to coat it with the oil.
  3. Pour in the white wine and let it reduce for a minute, then add the seafood stock and saffron.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes.
  5. Add the seafood and continue cooking for another 10-12 minutes, until the rice is tender and the seafood is cooked through.
  6. Let the paella rest for a few minutes before serving with lemon wedges.

4. Tortilla Española – Spanish Omelette

The tortilla española, also known as the Spanish omelette, is a simple yet incredibly flavorful dish that’s often enjoyed as a snack or light meal. Made with just potatoes, onions, eggs, and olive oil, this dish is a staple in Spanish homes and tapas bars.

The best part about tortilla española is its versatility. It can be served warm or at room temperature, making it an excellent choice for summer picnics or tapas gatherings. You can also add variations, such as chorizo, peppers, or even cheese, for an extra layer of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the potatoes and onions, seasoning with salt and pepper. Cook gently until the potatoes are soft, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Beat the eggs in a large bowl and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Once the potatoes and onions are cooked, drain them and add them to the beaten eggs. Mix gently.
  5. Heat a small amount of olive oil in the pan and pour the egg-potato mixture into it. Cook over low heat until the edges set, then flip the tortilla to cook the other side.
  6. Serve at room temperature or warm.

5. Pisto – Spanish Ratatouille

Pisto is a traditional Spanish vegetable stew that resembles the French ratatouille. Made with tomatoes, zucchini, peppers, onions, and eggplant, this dish is a great way to showcase the vibrant summer vegetables that are in season. Pisto is typically served with a fried egg on top, adding richness and protein to the dish.

This hearty yet light dish is perfect for those hot summer days when you want something flavorful and filling but not too heavy. It can be served as a side dish or as a main course, paired with crusty bread for a complete meal.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium zucchinis, diced
  • 1 eggplant, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 2 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 4 eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, bell pepper, zucchini, and eggplant. Cook until softened, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and tomatoes, and cook for another 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and form a sauce.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. If desired, fry eggs in a separate pan and serve them on top of the pisto.

6. Ensalada Rusa – Spanish Potato Salad

Ensalada rusa, or Spanish potato salad, is a popular side dish in Spain, especially during the summer months. It’s a creamy salad made with boiled potatoes, carrots, peas, and mayonnaise. The dish is simple, satisfying, and can be made ahead of time, making it perfect for gatherings or family meals.

While the classic ensalada rusa is made with mayonnaise, you can easily substitute it with Greek yogurt or a lighter dressing for a healthier version. You can also add ingredients like tuna, olives, or pickles for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise (or Greek yogurt)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the potatoes, carrots, and peas until tender, about 10 minutes.
  2. Drain the vegetables and let them cool.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooled vegetables with mayonnaise, salt, and pepper.
  4. Mix gently, then chill in the fridge for at least an hour before serving.

7. Churros – Spanish Pastry for Summer

While churros are often associated with breakfast or dessert, they are a great treat for summer evenings as well. These fried dough pastries are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, often served with a cup of hot chocolate for dipping. However, during summer, churros are just as enjoyable when paired with a refreshing cup of chilled chocolate or even a fruity dipping sauce.

Churros are a popular street food in Spain, particularly in cities like Madrid, where locals enjoy them as a late-night snack or breakfast.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Cinnamon and sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water, sugar, and salt to a boil.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in the flour until the dough forms.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Heat the oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  5. Pipe the dough into the oil and fry until golden and crispy.
  6. Dust with cinnamon and sugar before serving.

8. Tarta de Santiago – Almond Cake from Galicia

Tarta de Santiago is a traditional Spanish almond cake from the region of Galicia. It is made with ground almonds, eggs, sugar, and a touch of lemon zest. The cake is light, fragrant, and perfect for summer. It’s often topped with powdered sugar and a cross of Saint James, which is a symbol of the region.

This cake is gluten-free and incredibly easy to prepare. It’s a wonderful dessert to serve at a summer gathering or to enjoy with a cup of tea on a warm afternoon.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups ground almonds
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder (optional)
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Beat the eggs with the sugar until pale and fluffy.
  3. Fold in the ground almonds, lemon zest, and baking powder.
  4. Pour the batter into a greased cake pan and bake for 25-30 minutes.
  5. Let it cool before dusting with powdered sugar and serving.

9. Bocadillo de Calamares – Fried Squid Sandwich

Bocadillo de calamares is a popular sandwich in Madrid, typically enjoyed on a warm summer day. The sandwich consists of crispy fried squid rings served in a baguette, often accompanied by a squeeze of lemon or aioli. It’s a simple yet delicious street food that captures the essence of Spanish summer dining.

Fried squid is light, tender, and perfectly complements the crunchy bread. Whether enjoyed by the seaside or in the heart of the city, this sandwich is a must-try.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb fresh squid, cleaned and sliced into rings
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 baguettes
  • Lemon wedges

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, combine the flour, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Dredge the squid rings in the flour mixture and fry them until golden brown.
  4. Drain the squid on paper towels and season with a little extra salt.
  5. Assemble the bocadillos by placing the fried squid in the baguettes and serving with lemon wedges.

10. Piquillos Rellenos de Marisco – Piquillo Peppers Stuffed with Seafood

Piquillo peppers, a sweet and smoky variety of peppers from the Navarre region, are often stuffed with various fillings. During the summer months, seafood makes the perfect filling for these delicate peppers. Shrimp, crab, or a mix of both can be used to create a luxurious and satisfying dish that works as an appetizer or a light meal.

The rich, savory seafood filling contrasts wonderfully with the slight smokiness of the piquillo peppers, making this dish a standout on any summer table.

Ingredients:

  • 12 piquillo peppers (jarred or fresh)
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 lb crab meat (optional)
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the shrimp and cook until pink and tender.
  2. Stir in the crab meat and white wine, and cook for a few more minutes.
  3. Add the cream and season with salt and pepper.
  4. Stuff the piquillo peppers with the seafood mixture and place them on a serving platter.

These ten Spanish dishes are perfect for summer dining, offering refreshing, flavorful options that highlight the season’s best ingredients. Whether you’re in the mood for a cold soup, a hearty paella, or a sweet treat, Spanish cuisine provides a wealth of vibrant dishes that are sure to make your summer meals memorable. Enjoy these recipes, and let them bring the flavors of Spain right to your table!
