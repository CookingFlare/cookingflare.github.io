10 Sweet Treats You Can Make with Your Kids


Cooking with children is a wonderful opportunity to bond, teach kitchen skills, and create delicious memories. Making sweet treats together turns ordinary afternoons into celebrations of creativity and joy. From simple no-bake recipes to slightly more involved baking projects, these ten recipes are perfect for little hands eager to help and grown-ups looking to foster a love for the kitchen.

1. No-Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Bites

Why Kids Love It

These tiny, no-bake bites are sweet, rich, and satisfy chocolate cravings, plus kids enjoy shaping them into little balls.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 cups rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup mini chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix peanut butter and honey until smooth.
  2. Stir in the rolled oats until fully combined.
  3. Fold in mini chocolate chips gently.
  4. Roll the mixture into small bite-sized balls and place them on a parchment-lined tray.
  5. Chill in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving.

Tips

Use creamy peanut butter to keep the mixture smooth and easier for kids to handle. These treats store well in an airtight container for a week.

2. Rainbow Fruit Kabobs with Honey Yogurt Dip

Why Kids Love It

Slipping colorful fruit onto skewers feels almost like a game, and the creamy dip adds a sweet tangy touch.

Ingredients

  • Strawberries, sliced
  • Green grapes
  • Pineapple chunks
  • Blueberries
  • Kiwi slices
  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Thread the fruit pieces alternately on wooden skewers to create a rainbow effect.
  2. In a small bowl, combine Greek yogurt, honey, and vanilla extract to make the dip.
  3. Serve kabobs with a side of honey yogurt dip for dipping.

Tips

Use kid-safe plastic skewers for younger children. Encourage them to pick colors and piece the fruit in their own pattern to enhance engagement.

3. Funfetti Sugar Cookies

Why Kids Love It

Bright, colorful sprinkles on buttery cookies always bring smiles, and kids love mixing the dough and decorating.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup rainbow sprinkles

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, beat butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add egg and vanilla, mix until combined.
  5. Gradually add dry ingredients to wet, mixing just until incorporated.
  6. Fold in sprinkles gently.
  7. Roll dough into balls, place on baking sheet, and flatten slightly.
  8. Bake 8-10 minutes until edges are light golden. Cool on wire racks.

Tips

Add extra sprinkles on top before baking for visual fun. Use small cookie scoops to teach portion control.

4. Mini Banana Pancake Stackers with Maple Syrup

Why Kids Love It

Fluffy pancakes in bite-sized form are perfect for dipping and stacking, plus banana adds natural sweetness.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons melted butter
  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • Maple syrup for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk buttermilk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Stir wet ingredients into dry just until combined, then fold in mashed banana.
  4. Heat a lightly greased griddle or skillet over medium heat.
  5. Spoon small amounts of batter to form mini pancakes. Cook until bubbles appear, flip, and cook until golden.
  6. Stack pancakes and drizzle with warm maple syrup.

Tips

Let kids mash the bananas and pour batter onto the griddle with supervision. Add chocolate chips or blueberries for variation.

5. Colorful Jello Cups with Whipped Cream Topping

Why Kids Love It

Layering bright jello colors is visually exciting, and topping with whipped cream makes it feel like dessert at a party.

Ingredients

  • 3 different flavors/color jello (e.g., strawberry, lime, blueberry)
  • Whipped cream (store-bought or homemade)
  • Clear plastic cups

Instructions

  1. Prepare each jello flavor according to the package instructions.
  2. Pour the first flavor into cups about 1/3 full; chill until set.
  3. Carefully add the second and third flavors, chilling each layer until firm.
  4. Top with whipped cream and serve.

Tips

Use a small spoon to layer to prevent colors from mixing. Older kids can handle preparing the jello with supervision, while younger ones can dollop whipped cream.

6. Homemade Chocolate-Dipped Pretzels

Why Kids Love It

These salty-sweet snacks are fun to decorate with sprinkles and easy to make.

Ingredients

  • Pretzel rods or twists
  • 1 cup semi-sweet or white chocolate chips
  • Assorted sprinkles or crushed nuts

Instructions

  1. Melt chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth.
  2. Dip pretzels halfway into melted chocolate, letting excess drip off.
  3. Place on wax paper and quickly sprinkle with toppings.
  4. Chill in the fridge until set.

Tips

Provide a variety of toppings and let kids choose their own mix for creativity. Use parchment paper for easy clean-up.

7. Classic Rice Krispie Treats

Why Kids Love It

Sticky, sweet, and super simple—these treats are a hands-on project that delivers quick, tasty results.

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cups mini marshmallows
  • 6 cups Rice Krispies cereal

Instructions

  1. In a large saucepan, melt butter over low heat.
  2. Add marshmallows and stir until melted and smooth.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in cereal until well coated.
  4. Press mixture into a greased 9x13-inch pan.
  5. Cool slightly before cutting into squares.

Tips

Use cookie cutters to create fun shapes once slightly set but not fully cooled. Add chocolate chips or drizzle melted candy on top for flair.

8. Homemade Ice Cream Sandwiches

Why Kids Love It

Kids enjoy pressing cookies around ice cream and creating custom sandwiches with various fillings.

Ingredients

  • Your favorite soft cookies (chocolate chip works well)
  • Ice cream of choice
  • Sprinkles, mini chocolate chips, or shredded coconut for rolling

Instructions

  1. Lay out cookies on a tray.
  2. Place a scoop of ice cream on one cookie and top with another, gently pressing to form a sandwich.
  3. Roll edges in sprinkles or coatings if desired.
  4. Freeze sandwiches for at least one hour before eating.

Tips

Let kids pick their own cookie and ice cream combos for personalization. Soft cookies work best; if using store-bought hard cookies, warm slightly to soften.

9. Easy Cinnamon Sugar Churro Muffins

Why Kids Love It

These muffins capture churro flavor with minimal effort, and the sugary crust is a delightful surprise.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1/3 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup melted butter
  • 1/2 cup sugar mixed with 1 tablespoon cinnamon

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a muffin tin.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, mix buttermilk, oil, egg, and vanilla.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed.
  5. Spoon batter into muffin tin, filling 3/4 full.
  6. Bake for 15-18 minutes.
  7. Brush warm muffins with melted butter and roll in cinnamon sugar mixture.

Tips

Kids can shake the muffins in cinnamon sugar in a large plastic bag for less mess. Add chocolate chips to batter for a twist.

10. Mini Apple Pie Cups

Why Kids Love It

These cute, individual pies let kids help with filling and shaping dough while learning about creating pastries.

Ingredients

  • 1 package refrigerated pie crust dough
  • 2 apples, peeled, cored, and finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Combine apples, sugar, cinnamon, and lemon juice in a bowl.
  3. Cut pie crust into small circles to fit a muffin tin.
  4. Place dough circles in muffin cups, add a spoonful of apple filling.
  5. Top with another dough circle or lattice strips. Press edges to seal.
  6. Bake 18-20 minutes until golden and bubbly.

Tips

Make a small slit in the top to let steam escape. Let kids pinch edges and decorate tops with extra dough shapes.

Final Thoughts

Making sweet treats with your kids is more than just baking; it’s about creating experiences and sharing joy. These recipes are designed for simplicity and maximum fun, allowing children to immerse in every step, from mixing to decorating to tasting. Whether it’s simple no-bake bites or mini pies that teach pastry skills, these treats offer a delicious way to spend quality family time—and produce plenty of smiles along the way.
