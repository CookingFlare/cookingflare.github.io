Quick and Easy Nut-Free Pasta Salad Recipe

3 min · 583 words

Pasta salad is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed as a main course or a side. It’s perfect for picnics, potlucks, or a simple family dinner. Today, we’re diving into a quick and easy nut-free pasta salad recipe that is not only delicious but also allergy-friendly. This recipe combines vibrant vegetables, a zesty dressing, and tender pasta, creating a dish that’s as nutritious as it is tasty.

Ingredients

To get started, gather the following ingredients:

  • Pasta: 12 ounces of your favorite pasta (penne, fusilli, or farfalle work great)
  • Vegetables:
    • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 1 cucumber, diced
    • 1 bell pepper (any color), diced
    • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
    • 1/2 cup black olives, sliced
  • Cheese: 1 cup crumbled feta or mozzarella pearls
  • Herbs:
    • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
    • 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • Dressing:
    • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
    • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
    • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta: Start by cooking the pasta according to the package directions until al dente. Once cooked, drain and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process and cool the pasta quickly. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables: While the pasta is cooking, prepare your vegetables. Halve the cherry tomatoes, dice the cucumber and bell pepper, finely chop the red onion, and slice the black olives. Place all the vegetables in a large mixing bowl.

  3. Mix the Dressing: In a small bowl or jar, combine the extra virgin olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Whisk or shake well to emulsify the dressing.

  4. Combine Ingredients: Add the cooled pasta to the mixing bowl with the vegetables. Pour the dressing over the top and toss everything together until well coated.

  5. Add Cheese and Herbs: Gently fold in the crumbled feta or mozzarella pearls. Sprinkle the chopped parsley and basil over the salad and toss lightly to combine.

  6. Chill and Serve: For best results, cover the salad and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Before serving, give the salad a quick toss and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper if needed.

Tips for Perfect Pasta Salad

  1. Pasta Choice: Choose a pasta shape that holds onto the dressing well, such as penne, fusilli, or farfalle. These shapes have nooks and crannies that capture the flavors beautifully.

  2. Vegetable Variations: Feel free to swap out or add in other vegetables based on what you have available or what’s in season. Some great additions include artichoke hearts, roasted red peppers, or even blanched broccoli florets.

  3. Protein Boost: To make this pasta salad a more substantial meal, consider adding a protein source. Grilled chicken, shrimp, or chickpeas are excellent options that pair well with the other ingredients.

  4. Make Ahead: This pasta salad can be made a day in advance, making it a convenient option for busy schedules or when planning for an event. Just give it a good stir before serving to redistribute the dressing.

  5. Dressing Adjustments: If you prefer a creamier dressing, you can add a tablespoon of mayonnaise or Greek yogurt to the mix. This will alter the texture slightly but adds a delightful richness to the salad.

Nutritional Benefits

This nut-free pasta salad is not just convenient and delicious but also packs a nutritional punch. Here’s a breakdown of its health benefits:

  • Pasta provides energy through complex carbohydrates.
  • Vegetables contribute essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
  • Olive Oil offers healthy monounsaturated fats, which are good for heart health.
  • Feta/Mozzarella Cheese adds calcium and protein.
  • Herbs like parsley and basil are rich in antioxidants and have anti-inflammatory properties.

Why Nut-Free Matters

Creating a nut-free pasta salad ensures that those with nut allergies can enjoy this dish without worry. Nut allergies are common and can cause severe reactions, so it’s important to accommodate these dietary restrictions, especially in shared environments like schools, workplaces, or public gatherings.

Serving Suggestions

This pasta salad is incredibly versatile. Here are some ways to serve it:

  • As a Main Dish: Enjoy it as a light and refreshing main course, perfect for lunch or dinner on warm days.

  • As a Side Dish: Pair it with grilled meats like chicken, fish, or steak. It also complements vegetarian options like grilled tofu or portobello mushrooms.

  • At Picnics and Potlucks: This pasta salad is a crowd-pleaser at outdoor events. It’s easy to transport and stays fresh without needing to be reheated.

Storing Leftovers

If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator. The pasta salad will keep well for up to three days. Before eating, give it a stir and check the seasoning, as the flavors may mellow over time.

Final Thoughts

A quick and easy nut-free pasta salad recipe is a must-have in any home cook’s repertoire. It’s adaptable, nutritious, and caters to various dietary needs. Whether you’re preparing a meal for your family, bringing a dish to a gathering, or simply looking for a delicious and hassle-free option, this pasta salad delivers on all fronts.

Enjoy the vibrant flavors and simplicity of this dish, and feel free to make it your own with personal touches and favorite ingredients. Happy cooking!
