Easy and Delicious Stuffed Pork Chops Recipe


For those who adore savory flavors, a hearty dinner, and recipes that don’t require a culinary degree to master, stuffed pork chops are the ultimate go-to dish. These juicy, flavorful pork chops filled with a delectable stuffing make a perfect centerpiece for any dinner, whether you’re hosting guests or cooking a comforting family meal. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to prepare stuffed pork chops that are not only delicious but surprisingly simple to make.

Let’s dive straight into the recipe and tips for achieving perfectly cooked pork chops, all while enhancing flavor and texture with the right stuffing and cooking methods.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you start, it’s important to round up your ingredients ahead of time. Preparation is key to a successful cooking experience. Here’s what you’ll need for this recipe:

For the Pork Chops:

  • 4 thick-cut, bone-in pork chops (about 1½ inches thick)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For the Stuffing:

  • 1 cup seasoned breadcrumbs
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (or 1 tablespoon dried parsley)
  • ¼ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¼ teaspoon dried sage
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ½ cup chicken broth (or water)

Optional:

  • ¼ cup dried cranberries or chopped cooked bacon for added flavor in the stuffing

For Garnish (Optional):

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • A drizzle of balsamic glaze

Step-by-Step Instructions

Cooking stuffed pork chops may sound daunting, but with a few key steps, you’ll be serving up a restaurant-quality dish in no time.

1. Prepping the Pork Chops

Start by patting your pork chops dry with a paper towel. This step helps achieve a beautiful sear while cooking. Using a small sharp knife, create a pocket in each pork chop by slicing horizontally into the thickest part of the meat. Make sure not to cut all the way through to the other side—you just need enough space to hold the stuffing securely.

Once the pockets are ready, season the pork chops generously on both sides (and inside the pockets) with garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Set aside.

2. Preparing the Stuffing

In a medium skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced onion, garlic, and celery, sautéing until softened—about 3-4 minutes. Remove from heat and transfer the mixture to a bowl.

To the bowl, add breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, parsley, thyme, sage, and any optional ingredients like dried cranberries or bacon bits. Slowly pour in chicken broth, stirring as you go, until the mixture is moist but not overly wet. The stuffing should hold its shape when pressed together.

Let the stuffing cool slightly before handling.

3. Stuffing the Pork Chops

Carefully spoon the stuffing mixture into the prepared pockets of the pork chops. Use your fingers or the back of a spoon to press the stuffing firmly so it doesn’t spill out during cooking. If necessary, use toothpicks to secure the opening of each chop.

Cooking Methods (Choose Your Favorite!)

Now that the pork chops are stuffed and seasoned, it’s time to decide how you want to cook them. Each method brings its own unique flavor and texture to the finished dish.

A. Pan-Seared and Baked Pork Chops (Recommended)

This method ensures a golden-brown crust and moist, tender meat.

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, sear the stuffed pork chops for 2-3 minutes per side, until golden brown.
  3. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the internal temperature of the pork reaches 145°F (63°C). Use a meat thermometer for accuracy.
  4. Remove from the oven and allow the pork chops to rest for 5 minutes before serving.

B. Grilled Stuffed Pork Chops

If you love smoky, grilled flavors, this method is for you.

  1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 375-400°F).
  2. Lightly oil the grill grates to prevent sticking.
  3. Sear the stuffed pork chops for 3 minutes on each side, then move them to indirect heat. Close the grill lid and cook for an additional 15-20 minutes, checking for doneness.

C. Slow-Cooked Stuffed Pork Chops

For a hands-off approach, the slow cooker ensures maximum tenderness.

  1. Sear the pork chops on the stovetop for 2 minutes per side to lock in the juices.
  2. Place the chops in your slow cooker and cook on low heat for 4-5 hours or high heat for 2-3 hours. Adding a splash of chicken broth or white wine to the slow cooker will keep the meat moist.

Tips for Success

  • Choose the Right Chops: Bone-in, thick-cut pork chops hold up better to stuffing and stay juicier than thinner, boneless cuts.
  • Don’t Overstuff: Avoid packing the stuffing too tightly. Leave a little room for it to expand as it cooks.
  • Resting Time is Essential: Always let the pork chops rest for 5 minutes after cooking. This allows the juices to redistribute and ensures tender meat.
  • Pairing Ideas: Serve these pork chops with roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a fresh green salad for a complete meal.

Variations to Try

If you’re feeling adventurous, try these variations to customize the recipe to your taste:

  • Cheesy Spinach Stuffing: Replace the breadcrumbs with sautéed spinach and mix in shredded mozzarella or feta cheese.
  • Apple and Sausage Stuffing: Add diced, cooked sausage and finely chopped apples for a sweet and savory twist.
  • Herbed Rice Stuffing: Use cooked wild rice or brown rice in place of breadcrumbs for a hearty, gluten-free option.

Storage and Reheating

If you have leftovers (and that’s a big “if” because they’re so tasty), here’s how to store and reheat them:

  1. Storage: Refrigerate any leftover pork chops in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
  2. Reheating: To keep the meat moist, reheat them in a 350°F oven, covered with aluminum foil, for 10-15 minutes. Alternatively, you can microwave them on a lower power setting for a minute or two.

Final Thoughts

Stuffed pork chops are one of those dishes that feel indulgent yet surprisingly accessible to make at home. With a crisp exterior, juicy interior, and flavorful stuffing, this meal is sure to impress at dinnertime. Whether you’re an experienced chef or someone who prefers quick and easy recipes, stuffed pork chops cater to every skill level and palate.

Ready to elevate your cooking repertoire? Head to the kitchen, whip up this recipe, and treat yourself to a satisfying and memorable dining experience. Bon appétit!
