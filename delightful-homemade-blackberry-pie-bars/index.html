Delightful Homemade Blackberry Pie Bars


If you’re a fan of blackberries and pie, then you’re in for a treat with these delightful homemade blackberry pie bars. This recipe combines the sweetness of ripe blackberries with a buttery crust and crumbly streusel topping, creating a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Whether you’re looking for a dessert to impress your guests or simply craving a sweet treat, these blackberry pie bars are sure to satisfy your cravings.

Ingredients

For the Crust:

  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 large egg

For the Blackberry Filling:

  • 2 cups fresh blackberries
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice

For the Streusel Topping:

  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a 9x9-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving an overhang on the sides for easy removal later.

  2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt for the crust. Add in the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  3. Add the egg to the mixture and mix until a dough forms. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan.

  4. In another bowl, combine the blackberries, sugar, cornstarch, and lemon juice for the filling. Gently toss until the blackberries are coated. Spread the blackberry filling evenly over the crust.

  5. In a small bowl, mix together the flour and sugar for the streusel topping. Add in the cold cubed butter and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the mixture until crumbly. Sprinkle the streusel topping over the blackberry filling.

  6. Bake the blackberry pie bars in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.

  7. Remove from the oven and let the bars cool completely in the pan before cutting into squares. This will allow the filling to set.

  8. Once cooled, lift the bars out of the pan using the parchment paper overhang and transfer them to a cutting board. Cut into squares and serve.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra touch of sweetness, you can dust the blackberry pie bars with powdered sugar before serving.

  • Feel free to replace the blackberries with any other berries of your choice, such as raspberries or blueberries. The recipe works well with various fruits.

  • Serve the blackberry pie bars with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for a delicious dessert.

  • These bars can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. They can also be frozen for longer storage.

Conclusion

These delightful homemade blackberry pie bars are a wonderful way to enjoy the flavors of blackberries in a portable and easy-to-make dessert. The buttery crust, sweet blackberry filling, and crumbly streusel topping come together to create a truly irresistible treat. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or simply treating yourself, these blackberry pie bars are sure to be a hit. Give this recipe a try and indulge in the sweet and tangy goodness of homemade blackberry pie bars.
