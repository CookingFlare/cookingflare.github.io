Delight Your Senses with Tarte au Citron: A French Lemon Tart


Tarte au citron, a classic French dessert that has been delighting palates for centuries. This tangy and sweet tart is a masterclass in balance and harmony, with its velvety lemon filling, buttery pastry crust, and subtle notes of citrus. In this recipe, we’ll take you on a journey to create this iconic treat, perfect for warm weather gatherings, special occasions, or simply a delightful treat for yourself.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup confectioners’ sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 1/4 cup ice-cold water
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves (optional)

Step 1: Make the Pastry Crust

In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and confectioners’ sugar. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work it into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the ice-cold water, stirring with a fork until the dough comes together in a ball. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

Step 2: Roll Out the Pastry Crust

On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled pastry dough to a thickness of about 1/8 inch. Transfer the dough to a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom. Trim the edges and press the dough into the corners of the pan. Prick the bottom of the dough with a fork to prevent it from bubbling up during baking.

Step 3: Prepare the Lemon Filling

In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, lemon juice, granulated sugar, and melted butter until well combined. Stir in the heavy cream, kosher salt, and grated lemon zest.

Step 4: Assemble and Bake the Tarte au Citron

Pour the lemon filling into the pastry-lined tart pan and smooth the top. If using, sprinkle the chopped fresh mint leaves over the filling. Bake the tart in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 40-45 minutes, or until the filling is set and the crust is golden brown.

Step 5: Allow the Tarte au Citron to Cool

Remove the tart from the oven and let it cool to room temperature on a wire rack. Once cooled, refrigerate the tart for at least 2 hours or overnight to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

To serve, remove the tart from the refrigerator 30 minutes before serving. Release the tart from the pan and transfer it to a serving plate. Slice the tart into thin wedges and serve chilled, garnished with additional fresh mint leaves if desired.

Tips and Variations:

  • For a more intense lemon flavor, use 3-4 tablespoons of freshly squeezed lemon juice.
  • Substitute the heavy cream with whole milk or half-and-half for a lighter filling.
  • Add a sprinkle of powdered sugar on top of the tart for a decorative touch.
  • Use a combination of lemon and orange zest for a unique flavor profile.
  • Experiment with different types of citrus fruits, such as blood oranges or Meyer lemons, for a unique twist.

With these simple steps and a few basic ingredients, you’ll be well on your way to creating a Tarte au Citron that will delight your senses and impress your friends and family. Bon appétit!
