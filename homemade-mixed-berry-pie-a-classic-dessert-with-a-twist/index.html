Homemade Mixed Berry Pie: A Classic Dessert with a Twist


There’s something undeniably nostalgic about a homemade pie, and when it comes to summer desserts, nothing beats a classic mixed berry pie. Bursting with the vibrant flavors of fresh berries, this timeless dessert is a crowd-pleaser that never goes out of style. But why settle for the ordinary when you can take your berry pie to the next level with a tantalizing twist? In this recipe, we’ll show you how to create a homemade mixed berry pie that will leave your taste buds begging for more.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ cups of mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, blackberries)
  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • ¼ cup of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 2 prepared pie crusts (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1 tablespoon of butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • Optional: powdered sugar for dusting

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the mixed berries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss the ingredients together until the berries are well coated. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes to allow the berries to release their natural juices.

  3. Roll out one of the pie crusts and carefully transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish. Press the crust into the dish, making sure it covers the bottom and sides evenly.

  4. Pour the berry mixture into the prepared crust, spreading it out evenly.

  5. Dot the top of the berry mixture with small pieces of butter. This will add richness and help create a luscious filling.

  6. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the berry filling. Trim any excess crust, leaving a 1-inch overhang. Fold the overhang under the bottom crust, then pinch the edges together to seal the pie.

  7. Using a sharp knife, make a few decorative slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.

  8. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg wash. This will give the pie a beautiful golden-brown color when baked.

  9. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any potential drips) and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling.

  10. Once baked, remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours. This will allow the filling to set.

  11. Optional: Dust the top of the pie with powdered sugar for an extra touch of sweetness and visual appeal.

  12. Slice and serve your homemade mixed berry pie on its own or with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Enjoy!

The Perfect Summer Dessert

Homemade mixed berry pie is the epitome of summer indulgence. The combination of juicy strawberries, tart blueberries, sweet raspberries, and rich blackberries creates a symphony of flavors that is hard to resist. With the addition of the buttery crust and the hint of lemon, every bite is a delightful explosion of taste.

Whether you’re hosting a backyard barbecue, attending a potluck, or simply craving a slice of heaven on a lazy summer afternoon, this classic dessert with a twist is sure to impress. So, grab your mixing bowl, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to create a homemade mixed berry pie that will have everyone coming back for seconds.
