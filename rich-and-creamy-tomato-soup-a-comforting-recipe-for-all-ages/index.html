Rich and Creamy Tomato Soup: A Comforting Recipe for All Ages


When it comes to comfort food, few things can beat a bowl of rich and creamy tomato soup. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight meal or a cozy weekend treat, this timeless classic is sure to hit the spot. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create a lusciously smooth and flavorful tomato soup that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cans (28 ounces each) of whole peeled tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Saute the Aromatics: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes, or until fragrant.

  2. Simmer the Tomatoes: Add the cans of whole peeled tomatoes, including the juices, to the pot. Break up the tomatoes with a spoon and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer. Allow it to cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  3. Blend the Mixture: Once the tomatoes have softened, use an immersion blender to puree the mixture until smooth. If you don’t have an immersion blender, carefully transfer the mixture to a regular blender in batches and blend until smooth. Be cautious when blending hot liquids to avoid splattering.

  4. Add the Broth and Seasonings: Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, and then sprinkle in the sugar, salt, and pepper. Stir the soup well to combine the flavors, and let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.

  5. Finish with Cream: Reduce the heat to low and pour in the heavy cream, stirring gently to incorporate it into the soup. Let the soup warm through for a few more minutes, being careful not to let it come to a boil.

  6. Serve and Enjoy: Ladle the rich and creamy tomato soup into bowls, garnish with fresh basil leaves, and serve hot. Pair it with a slice of crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich for a truly satisfying meal.

Tips and Variations

  • For an extra depth of flavor, consider adding a splash of balsamic vinegar or a sprinkle of dried oregano to the soup.
  • If you prefer a dairy-free version, you can substitute the heavy cream with coconut cream for a velvety finish.
  • To make the soup more substantial, toss in a handful of cooked pasta or rice before serving.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for longer-term enjoyment.

There you have it – a soul-warming bowl of rich and creamy tomato soup that is both simple to prepare and undeniably satisfying. Whether you’re cooking for yourself, your family, or a gathering of friends, this recipe is bound to become a beloved go-to. So, gather your ingredients, get simmering, and prepare to savor every spoonful of this timeless comfort food.
