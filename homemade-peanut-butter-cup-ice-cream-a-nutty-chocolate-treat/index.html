Homemade Peanut Butter Cup Ice Cream: A Nutty Chocolate Treat


If you’re a fan of the classic combination of peanut butter and chocolate, then you’re in for a treat with this homemade peanut butter cup ice cream recipe. This rich and creamy dessert is the perfect indulgence for those warm summer days when you’re craving something cool and decadent. Made with real peanut butter, luscious chocolate, and a hint of vanilla, this homemade ice cream will have your taste buds dancing with delight. So, roll up your sleeves, grab your ice cream maker, and let’s dive into the delightful world of homemade peanut butter cup ice cream!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped peanut butter cups
  • 1/2 cup chopped dark chocolate

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Ice Cream Base

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, and granulated sugar over medium heat. Stir the mixture occasionally until it starts to steam, but not boil.
  2. Once the mixture is heated, remove it from the heat and let it cool for about 5 minutes.

Step 2: Incorporate the Flavor

  1. In a separate bowl, whisk the creamy peanut butter until it becomes smooth and creamy.
  2. Gradually add the warm cream mixture to the peanut butter, whisking continuously until well combined.
  3. Stir in the pure vanilla extract, ensuring that it is evenly distributed throughout the mixture.

Step 3: Chill the Mixture

  1. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, ensuring that it touches the surface of the mixture to prevent a skin from forming.
  2. Refrigerate the mixture for at least 4 hours or, preferably, overnight. This chilling process will allow the flavors to meld and the mixture to reach the ideal temperature for churning.

Step 4: Churn the Ice Cream

  1. Once the mixture is thoroughly chilled, pour it into your ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This usually takes about 20-25 minutes, or until the ice cream reaches a soft-serve consistency.

Step 5: Add the Mix-Ins

  1. During the last few minutes of churning, add the chopped peanut butter cups and dark chocolate to the ice cream.
  2. Continue churning until the mix-ins are evenly distributed throughout the ice cream.

Step 6: Freeze and Enjoy

  1. Transfer the churned ice cream to a freezer-safe container, ensuring that it is tightly sealed.
  2. Freeze the ice cream for at least 4 hours, or until it reaches your desired firmness.

Serving Suggestions

This homemade peanut butter cup ice cream is delightful on its own, but you can take it to the next level with a variety of toppings and serving ideas. Drizzle it with warm fudge sauce, sprinkle it with extra chopped peanut butter cups, or sandwich it between two chocolate cookies for a decadent ice cream sandwich.

Conclusion

With its creamy texture, nutty flavor, and decadent chocolate swirls, this homemade peanut butter cup ice cream is a true delight for the senses. Whether you’re hosting a summer gathering, treating yourself after a long day, or simply craving a sweet indulgence, this homemade ice cream is sure to satisfy your cravings and leave you coming back for more. So, why not whip up a batch of this delicious treat and savor the delightful combination of peanut butter and chocolate in every luscious bite?
