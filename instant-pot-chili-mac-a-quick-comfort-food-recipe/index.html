Instant Pot Chili Mac: A Quick Comfort Food Recipe


Few meals check all the boxes like chili mac. It’s hearty, comforting, packed with bold flavors, and simple to whip up. And when you combine this satisfying dish with the ease of the Instant Pot, it becomes even easier to get dinner on the table in no time. Whether you’re serving a hungry crowd, feeding your family, or meal prepping for the week, Instant Pot Chili Mac is a go-to recipe that delivers every time.

Why You’ll Love Instant Pot Chili Mac

Imagine a blend of rich chili spices, filling ground meat, tender pasta, and gooey cheese coming together in one pot for the ultimate comfort food experience. What makes this recipe even better is how it simplifies your cooking process. No need to stand over a stove, juggling multiple pans or worrying about overcooked pasta. Once everything is loaded into the Instant Pot, it works its magic while you focus on other things. The result? A flavorful dish with minimal cleanup and maximum comfort.

This recipe also has room for customization, allowing you to adjust the ingredients to suit your personal preferences—whether you want to make it spicier, sneak in extra vegetables, or swap out the protein.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, ensure you have the following ingredients on hand:

For the Chili Base:

  • Ground beef (or ground turkey for a lighter option): 1 lb
  • Onion, finely chopped: 1 medium
  • Garlic cloves, minced: 3 cloves
  • Bell pepper, diced: 1 (any color)
  • Canned diced tomatoes (with their juices): 2 cans (14.5 oz each)
  • Tomato sauce: 1 cup
  • Kidney beans, drained and rinsed: 1 can (15 oz)
  • Black beans, drained and rinsed: 1 can (15 oz)
  • Chili powder: 3 tbsp
  • Ground cumin: 1 tbsp
  • Smoked paprika: 1 tsp
  • Salt: 1 tsp or to taste
  • Black pepper: ½ tsp
  • Cayenne pepper (optional): ¼ tsp, for an extra kick

For the Macaroni:

  • Elbow macaroni (uncooked): 2 cups
  • Chicken broth (or vegetable broth): 3 cups
  • Cheddar cheese, shredded: 2 cups (or more if you love extra cheesiness)
  • Olive oil: 2 tbsp

Optional Toppings:

  • Sour cream
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Sliced green onions
  • Additional shredded cheese

How to Make Instant Pot Chili Mac

Let’s break the process down into manageable steps that ensure perfectly cooked chili mac every time.

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics and Meat

Set your Instant Pot to the sauté setting and heat the olive oil. Once hot, add the chopped onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Stir and cook for about 2-3 minutes until softened and fragrant. Next, add the ground beef (or turkey) to the pot. Break it up with a spoon and cook until browned, which typically takes an additional 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.

Drain any excess fat if necessary and proceed with the recipe.

Step 2: Add the Chili Ingredients

After sautéing, it’s time to layer in the chili components. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, kidney beans, black beans, and your seasonings: chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using). Stir well, ensuring the spices are evenly distributed among the ingredients.

Step 3: Add the Pasta and Broth

Next, pour the dry elbow macaroni directly into the pot, followed by the chicken broth. Gently stir to ensure the pasta settles evenly within the chili mixture. The liquid should just cover all the ingredients, helping everything cook evenly. Avoid over-stirring at this stage to prevent sticking.

Step 4: Pressure Cook

Secure the Instant Pot lid, ensuring the valve is set to “Sealing.” Select the “Pressure Cook” or “Manual” setting and set the timer for 4 minutes on high pressure. While this might sound like a short time, remember that the Instant Pot takes some additional time to build up pressure before the countdown begins.

Step 5: Release Pressure

Once the timer finishes, perform a quick pressure release by carefully switching the valve to “Venting.” Be mindful of the hot steam escaping from the pot and ensure your hands and face are kept at a safe distance.

Step 6: Add Cheese and Serve

Open the lid and give everything a quick stir. You’ll notice the chili has thickened, and the macaroni is beautifully cooked. It’s time for the finishing touch. Gradually add the shredded cheddar cheese to the pot, stirring it in until melted and fully incorporated. The cheese creates a creamy texture that ties everything together.

Taste and adjust seasonings if needed, and your Instant Pot Chili Mac is ready to serve!

Tips for Success

  • Don’t Over-Stir the Pasta: When adding the uncooked macaroni, avoid over-stirring to prevent it from clumping together or sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  • Customize the Spice Level: Love a fiery chili mac? Increase the amount of cayenne pepper or incorporate diced jalapeños. Prefer a milder dish? Use less chili powder and skip the cayenne altogether.
  • Try Different Cheeses: While cheddar is a classic choice, feel free to experiment with mozzarella, Monterey Jack, or even a spicy pepper jack for added flavor.
  • Add Veggies: If you’re looking to boost nutrition, chop up zucchini or carrots and sauté them with the onion and garlic. Finely diced vegetables can blend seamlessly into the dish.
  • Keep the Pasta Al Dente: Elbow macaroni is ideal for chili mac because it holds its shape well. Avoid overcooking the pasta by performing the quick pressure release as soon as the timer is done.

A Meal for All Occasions

This Instant Pot Chili Mac recipe is a great choice for weeknight dinners, game day gatherings, or even potluck parties. Its crowd-pleasing combination of chili and macaroni appeals to kids and adults alike. Plus, the leftovers—if there are any—reheat beautifully, making it a fantastic option for next-day lunches.

Storage and Freezing Tips

Got leftovers? No problem! Chili mac can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. To reheat, simply microwave individual portions or heat them in a saucepan on the stove, adding a splash of broth or water to refresh the dish.

Looking to meal prep for future cravings? Chili mac freezes well! Divide into freezer-safe containers and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight and reheat as instructed above. The pasta will soften slightly, but the flavors remain delicious.

Final Thoughts

Instant Pot Chili Mac combines the best of two comfort food classics into one irresistible dish. Its rich flavors, cheesy textures, and ease of preparation make it a staple recipe you’ll want to keep in rotation. With no-fuss cooking and quick cleanup, you can satisfy your chili mac cravings any day of the week. Plus, its versatility lets you make it as basic or bold as you like!

So grab your Instant Pot and ingredients, and prepare to indulge in this savory, cheesy classic that’s ready in no time. Trust us—your taste buds will thank you!
