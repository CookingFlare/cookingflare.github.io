Spring Brunch: Recipes with Seasonal Herbs


Spring is a time of renewal and fresh beginnings, and what better way to celebrate the season than with a delightful spring brunch? With the abundance of seasonal herbs available, you can infuse your dishes with vibrant flavors and aromatic scents. In this blog post, we will explore some delicious recipes that showcase the beauty of spring herbs. So let’s dive right in and discover the wonders of a herb-infused spring brunch!

Herb-Crusted Quiche

Preparation Time: 20 minutes
Cooking Time: 40 minutes
Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 pre-made pie crust
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Gruyere)
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh herbs (such as parsley, chives, and dill)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the pre-made pie crust and press it into a 9-inch pie dish.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and pepper until well combined.
  4. Stir in the shredded cheese and chopped herbs, making sure they are evenly distributed.
  5. Pour the egg mixture into the pie crust.
  6. Bake the quiche in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the center is set and the top is golden brown.
  7. Remove from the oven and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

Serve this herb-crusted quiche warm or at room temperature, alongside a fresh green salad for a complete and satisfying spring brunch.

Lemon-Herb Pancakes

Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Cooking Time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 large egg
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh herbs (such as basil or mint)
  • Butter or oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, egg, lemon zest, and chopped herbs.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a small amount of butter or oil.
  5. Pour 1/4 cup of the pancake batter onto the skillet for each pancake.
  6. Cook until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Repeat with the remaining batter.

Serve these lemon-herb pancakes with a dollop of Greek yogurt, a drizzle of honey, and some fresh berries for a refreshing and indulgent spring brunch treat.

Herbed Spring Salad

Preparation Time: 15 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups mixed salad greens
  • 1 cup fresh herbs (such as basil, cilantro, and mint)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup sliced almonds, toasted
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, fresh herbs, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, and toasted almonds.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  3. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately as a refreshing and vibrant side dish.

This herbed spring salad is the perfect accompaniment to any spring brunch. The combination of fresh greens, aromatic herbs, and tangy feta cheese will surely impress your guests.

Herb-Infused Lemonade

Preparation Time: 10 minutes
Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups cold water
  • 1 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh herbs (such as rosemary or lavender)

Instructions:

  1. In a pitcher, combine the cold water, lemon juice, and sugar. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. Add the chopped herbs and stir to infuse the lemonade with their flavors.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Serve over ice and garnish with a sprig of fresh herbs.

This herb-infused lemonade is a refreshing and invigorating beverage that perfectly complements a spring brunch. The combination of citrusy lemon and fragrant herbs will quench your thirst and leave you feeling rejuvenated.

Conclusion

With the arrival of spring, it’s time to embrace the abundance of seasonal herbs and create a memorable brunch experience. From the herb-crusted quiche to the lemon-herb pancakes, herbed spring salad, and herb-infused lemonade, these recipes showcase the versatility and freshness of seasonal herbs. So gather your loved ones, set a beautiful table, and savor the flavors of spring with these delicious herb-infused dishes. Happy brunching!
