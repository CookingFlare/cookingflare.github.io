Cooking with Leftovers: 10 Tasty Recipes for Leftover Makeovers


We’ve all been there - standing in front of the fridge, staring at a container full of leftovers, wondering what to do with them. Instead of letting them go to waste, why not get creative and turn those leftovers into delicious meals? In this blog post, we’ll share 10 tasty recipes that will transform your leftovers into mouthwatering makeovers. Let’s get started!

1. Leftover Roast Chicken Pot Pie

Got leftover roast chicken? Turn it into a comforting pot pie! Start by sautéing onions and garlic in a pan. Add diced carrots, peas, and any other veggies you have on hand. Stir in the leftover roast chicken, some chicken broth, and a bit of cream. Season with salt, pepper, and herbs. Top with a store-bought or homemade pie crust and bake until golden and bubbling.

2. Turkey Cranberry Quesadillas

If you have leftover turkey and cranberry sauce from Thanksgiving, this recipe is for you. Spread cranberry sauce on a tortilla, top with shredded turkey, grated cheese, and some chopped green onions. Fold the tortilla in half and cook in a skillet until the cheese is melted and the quesadilla is crispy. Serve with a side of sour cream or guacamole.

3. Vegetable Fried Rice

Leftover rice and veggies make a perfect combination for a quick and flavorful fried rice. Heat some oil in a wok or large skillet. Add minced garlic and ginger, followed by chopped vegetables like carrots, peas, and bell peppers. Stir in the leftover rice and season with soy sauce, sesame oil, and a pinch of sugar. For an extra protein boost, toss in some cooked shrimp or diced chicken.

4. Ham and Cheese Breakfast Casserole

Turn leftover ham into a hearty breakfast casserole. In a baking dish, layer slices of bread, diced ham, and shredded cheese. Whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and pepper, then pour the mixture over the layers. Let it sit in the refrigerator overnight, then bake in the morning until the eggs are set and the top is golden brown. Serve with a side of fresh fruit for a complete meal.

5. Mashed Potato Pancakes

Leftover mashed potatoes are ideal for making crispy and savory pancakes. Mix the mashed potatoes with some flour, an egg, and chopped green onions. Shape the mixture into small patties and pan-fry until golden brown on both sides. Serve as a side dish with sour cream or applesauce.

6. Spaghetti Frittata

Got leftover spaghetti? Turn it into a delicious frittata! Whisk together eggs, grated cheese, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Stir in the leftover spaghetti and any additional ingredients you like, such as cooked bacon, sautéed mushrooms, or diced tomatoes. Pour the mixture into a greased baking dish and bake until the frittata is set and golden. Slice and serve with a side salad.

7. Leftover Steak Tacos

Transform leftover steak into mouthwatering tacos. Slice the steak into thin strips and warm them in a skillet with some sliced onions and peppers. Heat tortillas on a griddle until soft and pliable, then fill them with the steak mixture. Top with your favorite taco toppings like salsa, guacamole, and cilantro. Enjoy a quick and satisfying meal!

8. Pulled Pork Sliders

Leftover pulled pork can be easily repurposed into delicious sliders. Toast mini slider buns, then pile on the pulled pork. Top with coleslaw, pickles, and a drizzle of barbecue sauce. Serve these bite-sized sandwiches at your next gathering or enjoy them as a quick and flavorful weeknight dinner.

9. Leftover Vegetable Soup

When you have a variety of leftover vegetables, why not throw them all into a pot and make a hearty vegetable soup? Sautee onions, celery, and carrots in a large pot. Add the leftover vegetables, some vegetable broth, and a can of diced tomatoes. Season with your favorite herbs and spices, then let the soup simmer until all the flavors meld together. Serve with crusty bread for a satisfying meal.

10. Bread Pudding

Leftover bread is perfect for making a rich and indulgent bread pudding. Tear the bread into chunks and place them in a greased baking dish. In a separate bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of cinnamon. Pour the mixture over the bread, making sure it is evenly coated. Let it sit for a while to allow the bread to soak up the liquid, then bake until golden and set. Serve warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a drizzle of caramel sauce.

These 10 recipes prove that leftovers can be transformed into delicious and satisfying meals. Don’t let your leftovers go to waste - get creative and give them a tasty makeover! Try these recipes and let your taste buds rejoice. Happy cooking!

Note: Remember to always practice proper food safety guidelines when handling leftovers, including proper storage and reheating temperatures.
