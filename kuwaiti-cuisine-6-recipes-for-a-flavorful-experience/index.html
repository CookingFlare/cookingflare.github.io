Kuwaiti Cuisine: 6 Recipes for a Flavorful Experience


Kuwaiti cuisine is a treasure trove of flavors, colors, and aromas that reflect the rich cultural heritage of Kuwait. With influences from Persian, Indian, and Mediterranean cuisines, Kuwaiti dishes are a celebration of spices and fresh ingredients. Here, we will explore six traditional recipes that showcase the essence of Kuwaiti cooking, offering you a flavorful culinary experience right in your kitchen.

1. Machboos (Kuwaiti Spiced Rice with Meat)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or lamb, cut into pieces
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh coriander or parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Meat: In a bowl, mix the meat with salt, pepper, turmeric, cumin, coriander, and cinnamon. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Sauté the Onions and Garlic: Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Stir in the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Cook the Meat: Add the marinated meat to the pot, browning it on all sides. Once browned, add the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste. Cook for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  4. Add Water/Broth: Pour in the water or chicken broth and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes until the meat is tender.
  5. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Add the rinsed rice to the pot, ensuring it is submerged in the liquid. Add more water if necessary. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Cook the Rice: Cover the pot and simmer on low heat for 20 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and has absorbed the liquid. Fluff the rice with a fork.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander or parsley. Serve hot with a side of salad or yogurt.

2. Jireesh (Cracked Wheat with Chicken)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cracked wheat (jireesh)
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or butter
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon cardamom
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh lemon slices for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cracked Wheat: Rinse the cracked wheat in cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté Onions: In a large pot, melt the ghee over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Brown the Chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides. Season with cumin, cardamom, salt, and pepper.
  4. Add Broth and Wheat: Pour in the chicken broth and bring it to a boil. Stir in the cracked wheat, reduce the heat, and cover. Simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Check Consistency: If it’s too thick, add more broth or water. Adjust seasoning as necessary.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh lemon slices. Enjoy this comforting dish warm, perhaps with a side of pickles.

3. Harees (Wheat Porridge)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups wheat grains (or 1 cup fine wheat flour)
  • 1 pound chicken or lamb
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or butter
  • Sliced almonds and pine nuts for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak the Wheat: If using whole wheat grains, soak them in water for several hours or overnight.
  2. Cook the Meat: In a large pot, combine the meat, chopped onion, and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the meat is tender, about 1 hour.
  3. Add Wheat: Add the soaked wheat (or fine wheat flour) to the pot. Stir in the cinnamon and salt. Cook on low heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens to a porridge-like consistency (about 1-2 hours).
  4. Blend (if desired): For a smoother texture, you can blend the mixture using an immersion blender.
  5. Serve: Transfer to a serving dish, drizzle with melted ghee, and sprinkle with sliced almonds and pine nuts.

4. Qouzi (Kuwaiti Roasted Lamb)

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole lamb (about 2 kg), cleaned and butterflied
  • 2 cups rice
  • 4 cups water or lamb stock
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground spices (cumin, coriander, cardamom, cinnamon)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 4 tablespoons ghee
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Mix of dried fruits (raisins, apricots) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: Mix the ground spices with salt, pepper, and ghee. Rub this mixture all over the lamb, ensuring it is well coated. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
  2. Sauté Onions and Garlic: In a large pot, heat some ghee and sauté the onions until golden brown. Add the garlic and sauté for another minute.
  3. Prepare the Rice: Add the rice to the pot and stir for a few minutes. Then add the water or lamb stock, and bring to a boil.
  4. Roast the Lamb: In a separate oven-proof dish, place the marinated lamb and roast it in a preheated oven at 180°C for about 2-3 hours, or until tender.
  5. Combine and Serve: Once the lamb is cooked, serve it over a bed of rice garnished with dried fruits. Drizzle some roasted lamb juices over the top for added flavor.

5. Falafil (Kuwaiti Chickpea Fritters)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Mixture: Drain the soaked chickpeas and place them in a food processor with onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Blend until a coarse mixture forms.
  2. Form Patties: Shape the mixture into small balls or patties. If it’s too wet, add a bit of flour to help bind it.
  3. Heat Oil: In a deep skillet, heat oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Fry the Falafel: Fry the patties in batches until golden brown on all sides. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. Serve: Enjoy warm in pita bread with tahini sauce, fresh vegetables, and pickles.

6. Ghubba (Stuffed Eggplant)

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium eggplants
  • 250g ground lamb or beef
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon allspice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups tomato sauce
  • Olive oil for drizzling

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Eggplants: Cut the tops off the eggplants and scoop out the insides, leaving a shell about 1 cm thick. Reserve the flesh.
  2. Cook the Filling: In a skillet, heat olive oil and sauté the onions until soft. Add ground meat, cooking until browned. Stir in the reserved eggplant flesh, tomatoes, cinnamon, allspice, salt, and pepper. Cook for about 5-7 minutes until well combined.
  3. Stuff the Eggplants: Fill each eggplant shell with the meat mixture, pressing down to pack it tightly.
  4. Bake: Preheat the oven to 180°C. Arrange the stuffed eggplants in a baking dish, pour tomato sauce over the top, and drizzle with olive oil. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes.
  5. Serve: Allow to cool slightly before serving. Drizzle with extra olive oil and enjoy as a hearty meal.

Kuwaiti cuisine is not just about food; it’s about tradition, family, and the joy of sharing meals with loved ones. Each recipe tells a story, inviting you to explore the diverse flavors and aromas that define this enchanting culinary landscape. Whether you’re cooking for a special occasion or just wanting to indulge in something new, these recipes offer a delicious glimpse into the heart of Kuwait. Enjoy your flavorful journey through Kuwaiti cuisine!
