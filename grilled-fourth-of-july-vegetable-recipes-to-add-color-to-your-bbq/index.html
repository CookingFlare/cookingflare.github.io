Grilled Fourth of July Vegetable Recipes to Add Color to Your BBQ


The Fourth of July is a time for celebration, and what better way to add color and flavor to your BBQ than with some delicious grilled vegetable recipes? Whether you’re hosting a backyard cookout or attending a potluck, these vibrant, flavorful dishes are sure to be a hit with your guests. From smoky grilled corn to zesty vegetable skewers, these recipes will elevate your Independence Day feast to a whole new level. So fire up the grill, gather your freshest produce, and get ready to impress your family and friends with these mouthwatering grilled Fourth of July vegetable recipes.

Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili-Lime Butter

Ingredients:

  • 6 ears of corn, husks removed
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Zest and juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter, chili powder, lime zest, and lime juice until well combined.
  3. Season the corn with salt and pepper, then place on the grill.
  4. Grill the corn, turning occasionally, until it’s tender and slightly charred, about 10-12 minutes.
  5. Remove the corn from the grill and generously brush with the chili-lime butter.
  6. Sprinkle with chopped cilantro and serve immediately.

This grilled corn on the cob is a showstopper at any BBQ, with its smoky flavor and zesty, tangy butter. It’s the perfect way to add a pop of color to your Fourth of July spread.

Rainbow Vegetable Skewers

Ingredients:

  • 1 red bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 orange bell pepper, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced into rounds
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • Cherry tomatoes
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Thread the vegetables onto the skewers in a rainbow pattern, alternating colors.
  3. Place the skewers on the grill and cook, turning occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and lightly charred, about 10-12 minutes.
  4. Remove the skewers from the grill and arrange on a platter.

These vibrant rainbow vegetable skewers are not only visually stunning, but they’re also packed with flavor. The natural sweetness of the grilled vegetables shines through, making them a colorful and healthy addition to your Fourth of July BBQ.

Grilled Veggie Platter with Balsamic Glaze

Ingredients:

  • 1 eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 2-3 zucchinis, sliced lengthwise
  • 2-3 bell peppers, halved and seeded
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil, chopped, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Brush the eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and cherry tomatoes with the balsamic mixture.
  4. Grill the vegetables until tender and lightly charred, about 8-10 minutes for the eggplant and zucchini, and 10-12 minutes for the bell peppers and cherry tomatoes.
  5. Arrange the grilled vegetables on a platter and drizzle with any remaining balsamic glaze.
  6. Sprinkle with chopped fresh basil and serve.

This grilled veggie platter is a feast for both the eyes and the taste buds. The balsamic glaze adds a touch of sweetness to the smoky grilled vegetables, making it a delightful addition to your Fourth of July celebration.

Conclusion

These grilled Fourth of July vegetable recipes are guaranteed to add color, flavor, and vibrancy to your BBQ spread. Whether you’re a vegetarian, a veggie enthusiast, or just looking to add some healthy options to your Independence Day feast, these recipes are sure to impress. So fire up the grill, embrace the summer flavors, and enjoy a festive and flavorful Fourth of July with these delicious grilled vegetable dishes. Happy Independence Day!
