Heavenly Homemade Salsa: A Recipe for Canning


There’s nothing quite like the taste of homemade salsa. Whether you’re enjoying it with tortilla chips, using it as a topping for tacos, or incorporating it into your favorite recipes, a good salsa can elevate any dish. And when you make it yourself, the flavors are unparalleled. In this blog post, I’m going to share with you my recipe for Heavenly Homemade Salsa that’s perfect for canning.

The Ingredients You’ll Need

To create this delicious salsa, you’ll need the following fresh and flavorful ingredients:

  • 10 pounds of tomatoes
  • 5 onions
  • 5 jalapeño peppers
  • 3 bell peppers (any color will do)
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 cup of fresh cilantro
  • 1 ½ cups of apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of cumin
  • ½ teaspoon of black pepper

The Process

Step 1: Prepare the Tomatoes

Start by blanching the tomatoes in boiling water for about a minute. Then, transfer them to an ice water bath to stop the cooking process. This will make the skins easy to peel. Once the tomatoes are cool, remove the skins and chop them into small pieces, removing the cores as you go.

Step 2: Chop the Vegetables

Next, it’s time to dice the onions, jalapeño peppers, bell peppers, and garlic. For a milder salsa, you can remove the seeds and membranes from the jalapeños. If you prefer a spicier salsa, leave them in.

Step 3: Cook the Salsa

In a large pot, combine all the chopped vegetables, along with the cilantro, apple cider vinegar, salt, sugar, cumin, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Step 4: Prepare for Canning

While the salsa is simmering, prepare your canning jars and lids according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Make sure to sterilize the jars and have them ready for the hot salsa.

Step 5: Can the Salsa

Once the salsa is done simmering, it’s time to ladle it into the prepared jars, leaving about ½ inch of headspace. Wipe the jar rims clean, place the lids on top, and screw on the bands until they’re fingertip tight.

Step 6: Process the Jars

Place the filled and sealed jars into a boiling water canner and ensure they are covered by at least one inch of water. Process the jars for 15 minutes, then carefully remove them from the canner and let them cool at room temperature.

Enjoy Your Homemade Salsa

Once the jars have cooled, you’ll hear the satisfying “pop” of the lids sealing, indicating that your salsa is ready for long-term storage. Store your canned salsa in a cool, dark place, and it will be good for up to a year.

Conclusion

Making your own salsa at home is a rewarding experience, and when you can it, you’re able to enjoy the flavors of summer all year long. With this recipe, you’ll have a delicious and versatile salsa that’s perfect for sharing with friends and family or keeping all to yourself. So, gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to savor the taste of your very own Heavenly Homemade Salsa.
