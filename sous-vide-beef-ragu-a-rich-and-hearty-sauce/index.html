Sous Vide Beef Ragu: A Rich and Hearty Sauce


When it comes to creating a dish that is rich, flavorful, and comforting, few things can compare to a well-made beef ragu. And if you’re looking to take your ragu to the next level, using the sous vide method to cook the beef is a game-changer. The sous vide technique ensures that the beef is tender, succulent, and bursting with flavor, making it the perfect base for a luscious ragu sauce. In this recipe, we’ll explore how to create a mouthwatering sous vide beef ragu that will have your taste buds singing.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck roast, trimmed of excess fat
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups crushed tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

Instructions

Sous Vide Beef

  1. Preheat the water bath of the sous vide machine to 135°F (57°C).
  2. Season the beef chuck roast generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Place the seasoned beef in a vacuum-sealed bag, drizzle with 1 tbsp of olive oil, and vacuum-seal the bag.
  4. Submerge the bag in the water bath and cook for 24 hours.

Ragu Sauce

  1. In a large skillet, heat the remaining 1 tbsp of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened, about 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer until it has reduced by half.
  5. Stir in the beef broth, crushed tomatoes, bay leaves, dried thyme, and dried oregano.
  6. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and the flavors meld together.
  7. Once the beef has finished cooking in the sous vide, remove it from the bag and pat it dry with paper towels.
  8. Heat a skillet over high heat and sear the beef on all sides until it forms a golden-brown crust.
  9. Let the beef rest for a few minutes, then shred it using two forks.
  10. Add the shredded beef to the ragu sauce, stirring to combine.
  11. Simmer the ragu for an additional 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  12. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Serving

  1. Remove the bay leaves from the ragu sauce.
  2. Serve the sous vide beef ragu over your favorite pasta, such as pappardelle or rigatoni.
  3. Garnish with freshly chopped parsley and a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese.

Conclusion

The beauty of sous vide cooking lies in its ability to transform tough cuts of meat into tender, melt-in-your-mouth goodness. By infusing the beef with flavor through the sous vide method and then simmering it in a rich, hearty ragu sauce, you’ll create a dish that is nothing short of extraordinary. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to savor every delicious bite of this sous vide beef ragu. Your taste buds will thank you!
