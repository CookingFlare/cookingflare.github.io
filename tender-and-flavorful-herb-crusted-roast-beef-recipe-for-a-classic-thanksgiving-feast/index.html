Tender and Flavorful Herb-Crusted Roast Beef Recipe for a Classic Thanksgiving Feast


As the holiday season approaches, many of us are starting to plan our Thanksgiving menu, and what better way to celebrate this special occasion than with a mouthwatering roast beef at the center of the table? This Herb-Crusted Roast Beef recipe is guaranteed to be a showstopper, delivering tender, juicy, and flavorful meat that will have your guests coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 1 (5-6 pound) boneless ribeye roast
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh thyme, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Herb Rub

Start by preheating your oven to 450°F (230°C). In a small bowl, combine the minced garlic, chopped rosemary, thyme, parsley, salt, pepper, and olive oil to create a fragrant herb rub.

Step 2: Prepare the Roast

Place the ribeye roast on a cutting board and pat it dry with paper towels. This step is crucial for achieving a beautifully seared crust on the roast. Once the roast is dry, generously season it with salt and pepper.

Step 3: Apply the Herb Rub

Next, evenly spread the herb rub all over the surface of the roast, ensuring that the entire roast is coated with the aromatic herb mixture. The combination of garlic, rosemary, thyme, and parsley will infuse the meat with incredible flavor as it cooks.

Step 4: Roast the Beef

Place the herb-crusted roast beef on a rack in a roasting pan, and transfer it to the preheated oven. Allow the roast to sear for 15 minutes, then reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (165°C). Continue roasting for about 2 hours, or until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the roast registers 135°F (57°C) for medium-rare or 145°F (63°C) for medium doneness.

Step 5: Rest and Carve

Once the roast reaches the desired doneness, remove it from the oven and transfer it to a cutting board. Loosely tent the roast with foil and allow it to rest for 20-30 minutes. This resting period allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more tender and juicy roast. After resting, carve the herb-crusted roast beef into thin slices and serve it alongside your favorite Thanksgiving sides.

Serving Suggestions

This herb-crusted roast beef pairs beautifully with classic Thanksgiving sides such as creamy mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, and a rich gravy made from the pan drippings. The aromatic herb crust complements the savory flavors of the beef, creating a harmonious and indulgent dining experience for your Thanksgiving feast.

Conclusion

This Herb-Crusted Roast Beef recipe is a timeless centerpiece for a classic Thanksgiving celebration. The combination of fragrant herbs and perfectly cooked beef is sure to impress your guests and create lasting memories around the holiday table. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a larger feast, this roast beef recipe is a delicious and elegant addition to your Thanksgiving menu. Cheers to a bountiful and flavorful holiday season!
