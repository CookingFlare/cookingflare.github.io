How to Make a Delicious Shrimp Po' Boy Sandwich


The Shrimp Po’ Boy sandwich is a classic dish that hails from the vibrant culinary scene of New Orleans. This sandwich is not just a meal; it’s a celebration of flavors, textures, and the rich history of Louisiana cuisine. In this post, we will guide you through the process of making a mouthwatering Shrimp Po’ Boy that will impress your family and friends. From selecting the freshest shrimp to crafting the perfect remoulade sauce, we’ll cover everything you need to know to create this iconic sandwich at home.

What is a Shrimp Po’ Boy?

A Shrimp Po’ Boy is a traditional Louisiana sandwich that typically consists of fried shrimp served on a crusty French bread roll, often dressed with lettuce, tomatoes, pickles, and a zesty remoulade sauce. The origins of the Po’ Boy date back to the 1920s, when it was created by a couple who wanted to feed striking streetcar workers. The name “Po’ Boy” is believed to be a colloquial term for “poor boy,” reflecting the sandwich’s humble beginnings.

Ingredients for the Perfect Shrimp Po’ Boy

To make a delicious Shrimp Po’ Boy, you will need the following ingredients:

For the Shrimp

  • 1 pound of large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 cup of buttermilk
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup of cornmeal
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Remoulade Sauce

  • 1/2 cup of mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons of Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of hot sauce (like Tabasco)
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembling the Sandwich

  • 4 to 6 pieces of French bread (preferably po’ boy rolls)
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced pickles

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Shrimp

  1. Marinate the Shrimp: In a bowl, combine the peeled and deveined shrimp with buttermilk. Allow the shrimp to marinate for at least 30 minutes. This step not only adds flavor but also helps to tenderize the shrimp.

  2. Prepare the Breading: In a separate bowl, mix together the flour, cornmeal, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. This seasoned mixture will give the shrimp a delicious crunch.

Step 2: Fry the Shrimp

  1. Heat the Oil: In a deep skillet or frying pan, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. To test if the oil is ready, drop a small amount of the breading mixture into the oil; if it sizzles, the oil is hot enough.

  2. Coat the Shrimp: Remove the shrimp from the buttermilk, allowing any excess to drip off. Dredge each shrimp in the seasoned flour and cornmeal mixture, ensuring they are well-coated.

  3. Fry the Shrimp: Carefully place the breaded shrimp into the hot oil, working in batches to avoid overcrowding. Fry the shrimp for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they are golden brown and cooked through. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the shrimp to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Step 3: Make the Remoulade Sauce

  1. Combine Ingredients: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, hot sauce, chopped parsley, garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.

  2. Chill the Sauce: For the best flavor, cover the remoulade sauce and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes before serving. This allows the flavors to meld beautifully.

Step 4: Assemble the Po’ Boy Sandwich

  1. Prepare the Bread: Slice the French bread rolls in half lengthwise. If desired, you can lightly toast the insides of the bread for added texture.

  2. Layer the Ingredients: Start by spreading a generous amount of remoulade sauce on the bottom half of the bread. Next, add a layer of shredded lettuce, followed by sliced tomatoes and pickles.

  3. Add the Fried Shrimp: Pile the crispy fried shrimp on top of the vegetables.
