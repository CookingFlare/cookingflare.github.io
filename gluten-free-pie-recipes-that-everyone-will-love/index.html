Gluten-Free Pie Recipes That Everyone Will Love


Pies are one of the ultimate comfort foods. Whether you crave something sweet or savory, a perfectly baked crust paired with delicious fillings can warm your heart instantly. For those following a gluten-free diet, pies might feel like a tricky indulgence due to the traditional reliance on wheat-based flours for the crust. However, there’s good news: crafting a gluten-free pie that’s just as delicious as its wheat-based counterpart is entirely possible! In this guide, you’ll discover gluten-free pie recipes that everyone in your household will love — regardless of dietary restrictions.

By combining innovative ingredients and tested techniques, these recipes deliver on both flavor and texture, making them perfect for any occasion. From decadent desserts to satisfying savory options, here are some unforgettable gluten-free pie recipes you need to try.

1. The Classic Gluten-Free Apple Pie

Apple pie is a timeless favorite. With its flaky crust and sweet, cinnamon-spiced apple filling, this gluten-free version will delight pie enthusiasts while being completely safe for gluten-sensitive eaters.

Ingredients for the Crust:

  • 2 ½ cups gluten-free all-purpose baking flour
  • 1 teaspoon xanthan gum (if not included in the flour blend)
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter or dairy-free alternative, chilled and cubed
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice-cold water

Ingredients for the Apple Filling:

  • 6 medium-sized apples, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup light brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch or tapioca flour
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Instructions:

  1. Make the Crust: In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour, xanthan gum, sugar, and salt. Add the chilled butter and use a pastry cutter or fork to mix until you get a crumbly texture. Slowly add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until a dough forms. Divide the dough in half, flatten into disks, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

  2. Prepare the Filling: Toss the sliced apples with sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, cornstarch, and lemon juice. Ensure the apples are evenly coated.

  3. Assemble the Pie: Roll out one of the dough discs on a lightly floured surface and place it into your pie dish. Fill it with the apple mixture. Roll out the second dough disc to cover the pie, crimping the edges to seal. Cut small slits in the top crust to vent.

  4. Bake: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the pie for 50-60 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling. Allow to cool slightly before serving.

2. Flourless Chocolate Silk Pie

For chocolate lovers, this flourless pie is a dream come true. It’s rich, velvety, and incredibly indulgent, with no compromise on texture or flavor.

Ingredients for the Crust:

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • ¼ cup cocoa powder
  • ¼ cup melted coconut oil
  • 3 tablespoons maple syrup

Ingredients for the Filling:

  • 12 ounces dark chocolate, finely chopped
  • 1 cup heavy cream or dairy-free alternative
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Crust: Combine almond flour, cocoa powder, coconut oil, and maple syrup in a bowl. Mix until you get a moist, crumbly texture. Press it into a pie pan to form a crust. Chill in the fridge for 30 minutes.

  2. Make the Filling: Melt the chopped chocolate in a heatproof bowl over a saucepan of simmering water. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs and sugar until thick and pale. Gently fold the melted chocolate into the egg mixture, then stir in the vanilla extract.

  3. Bake: Pour the mixture into the prepared crust. Preheat oven to 325°F (165°C) and bake for 25 minutes until the center is set but slightly jiggly. Let cool completely before refrigerating to firm up.

  4. Serve: Top with whipped cream and chocolate shavings for a stunning presentation.

3. Savory Gluten-Free Chicken Pot Pie

Switch gears into the savory world with this comforting chicken pot pie. This hearty dish is packed with tender chicken, fresh vegetables, and a creamy sauce encased in a flaky gluten-free crust.

Ingredients for the Filling:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • 1 cup peas
  • ½ cup diced celery
  • ¼ cup diced onion
  • 3 tablespoons gluten-free flour
  • 1 ½ cups chicken broth
  • ½ cup milk or dairy-free alternative
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Ingredients for the Crust:

Use the same crust recipe as the Gluten-Free Apple Pie above.

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onions, carrots, and celery until softened. Sprinkle the gluten-free flour over the vegetables and stir until combined. Slowly add the chicken broth and milk, whisking constantly to prevent lumps. Cook until thickened, then stir in the chicken and peas. Season with salt and pepper.

  2. Assemble: Roll out the prepared gluten-free crust and press it into your pie dish. Pour the chicken mixture into the crust. Roll out a second crust to cover the filling, sealing the edges and cutting vents on top.

  3. Bake: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until crust is golden brown. Let cool slightly and enjoy!

4. Gluten-Free Lemon Meringue Pie

If you’re in the mood for something bright and citrusy, this gluten-free lemon meringue pie delivers the perfect balance of tangy and sweet.

Ingredients for the Crust:

Use the almond flour crust recipe from the Flourless Chocolate Silk Pie.

Ingredients for the Lemon Filling:

  • 1 cup lemon juice
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 4 egg yolks
  • ¼ cup cornstarch
  • ½ cup water
  • 3 tablespoons butter or dairy-free alternative

Ingredients for the Meringue:

  • 4 egg whites
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon cream of tartar

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Filling: Whisk together sugar, cornstarch, water, and lemon juice in a saucepan. Cook over medium heat until thickened. Remove from heat and stir in egg yolks and butter.

  2. Prepare the Meringue: Beat egg whites and cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar, one tablespoon at a time, until stiff peaks form.

  3. Bake: Pour the lemon filling into the prepared crust. Spread the meringue over the filling, ensuring it touches the crust edges to seal. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 12-15 minutes, or until lightly browned. Cool before serving.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Pie:

  • Chill Your Dough: Gluten-free pie dough can be fragile and sticky to work with. Keeping it chilled makes it easier to handle.
  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Avoid kneading or rolling the dough too much, as it can lead to a dense crust.
  • Use Pie Shields: Prevent the crust edges from browning too quickly by covering them with foil or a pie shield during baking.
  • Experiment with Flour Blends: Different brands of gluten-free flours have varying compositions. You might need to trial a few to find your favorite.

These gluten-free pie recipes prove that dietary restrictions don’t have to stand in the way of enjoying a good pie. From sweet classics to savory masterpieces, the possibilities are endless. So grab your rolling pin, gather your ingredients, and let’s bake a pie that everyone around your table will love — gluten-free or not!
