Fall-off-the-Bone Tender: Braised Short Ribs Recipe


There’s something undeniably comforting about tender, succulent short ribs that have been slow-cooked to perfection. The rich, deep flavors and melt-in-your-mouth texture make braised short ribs a favorite for cozy dinners and special occasions. If you’re looking for a show-stopping dish that will impress your guests or simply want to treat yourself to a luxurious meal, this braised short ribs recipe is the perfect choice.

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds beef short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

  2. Prepare the Short Ribs: Pat the short ribs dry with paper towels and season them generously with salt and pepper.

  3. Sear the Short Ribs: In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the short ribs and sear them on all sides until deeply browned, about 8 minutes. Transfer the ribs to a plate and set aside.

  4. Sauté the Aromatics: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

  5. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the red wine and scrape the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Let the wine simmer for a few minutes to cook off the alcohol.

  6. Braise the Short Ribs: Return the short ribs to the pot. Add the beef broth, tomato paste, thyme, and rosemary. The liquid should come about halfway up the sides of the ribs. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  7. Cover and Cook: Cover the pot with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Let the short ribs braise for 2 ½ to 3 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender and falling off the bones.

  8. Serve and Enjoy: Carefully remove the short ribs from the pot and place them on a serving platter. Skim off any excess fat from the cooking liquid. Serve the short ribs with the rich, flavorful sauce, and enjoy with your favorite side dishes.

Tips for Success

  • Use a good-quality red wine for the best flavor. A dry red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot works well in this recipe.
  • Don’t rush the searing process. Allowing the short ribs to develop a deep, caramelized crust is essential for building flavor.

Why Braised Short Ribs?

Braising is a cooking method that involves browning meat in fat, then slow-cooking it in a flavorful liquid until it becomes tender. This technique is perfect for tougher cuts of meat like short ribs, as it breaks down the connective tissues and results in incredibly tender, juicy meat.

The combination of beef broth, red wine, and aromatic vegetables creates a luxurious braising liquid that infuses the short ribs with layers of complex flavor. The slow, gentle cooking in the oven allows the meat to become incredibly tender, practically falling off the bone with each forkful.

Pairing Suggestions

Braised short ribs are a versatile dish that pairs beautifully with a variety of sides. For a classic presentation, serve the short ribs over creamy mashed potatoes or silky polenta. The rich, savory sauce also complements buttery noodles or fluffy rice. If you’re feeling adventurous, try pairing the short ribs with roasted root vegetables or a crisp, tangy salad to balance the richness of the dish.

Final Thoughts

This braised short ribs recipe is a true celebration of comfort food at its finest. The slow, gentle cooking process yields meat that is so tender, it quite literally falls off the bone. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply want to savor a special meal at home, these braised short ribs are sure to impress. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy the luxurious, soul-warming experience of cooking and savoring these fall-off-the-bone tender short ribs.
