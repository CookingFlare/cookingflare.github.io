Quick and Easy Veggie Stir-Fry: Healthy Dinner Recipe

Are you looking for a quick and easy dinner recipe that is not only delicious but also healthy? Look no further! This veggie stir-fry recipe is packed with nutritious vegetables and bursting with flavor. It’s the perfect dish to whip up on those busy weeknights when you want something tasty and satisfying without spending hours in the kitchen. Ingredients 1 tablespoon olive oil 2 cloves of garlic, minced 1 onion, sliced 1 red bell pepper, sliced 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced 1 zucchini, sliced 1 cup broccoli florets 1 cup snap peas 1 cup mushrooms, sliced 2 tablespoons soy sauce 1 tablespoon sesame oil Salt and pepper to taste Instructions Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion to the skillet and sauté until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant. Add the sliced bell peppers, zucchini, broccoli florets, snap peas, and mushrooms to the skillet. Stir-fry the vegetables for 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender-crisp. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce and sesame oil. Pour the mixture over the vegetables in the skillet and toss to coat evenly. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Continue to stir-fry for an additional 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Remove the skillet from the heat and serve the veggie stir-fry hot. For added protein, you can toss in some tofu, tempeh, or cooked chicken breast. To make this dish gluten-free, use tamari instead of soy sauce. Serve the stir-fry over brown rice or quinoa for a complete and filling meal. Garnish with fresh cilantro or sesame seeds for an extra burst of flavor. Why Choose Veggie Stir-Fry? Simple and Tasty Eggplant Parmesan: Family Dinner Recipe

Are you looking for a delicious and hearty meal that the whole family will love? Look no further than this simple and tasty Eggplant Parmesan recipe! It’s a classic Italian dish that’s perfect for a cozy family dinner or for entertaining guests. The crispy breaded eggplant slices, layered with rich tomato sauce and gooey melted cheese, create a flavor explosion that will leave everyone asking for seconds. Ingredients: 2 large eggplants, sliced into 1/2-inch rounds 2 cups all-purpose flour 4 eggs, beaten 2 cups Italian seasoned breadcrumbs 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese 4 cups marinara sauce 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese Fresh basil leaves for garnish Salt and pepper to taste Olive oil for frying Instructions: Step 1: Preparing the Eggplant Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Place the eggplant slices in a colander and sprinkle them with salt. Let them sit for about 30 minutes to draw out any bitterness. Then, rinse the slices and pat them dry with paper towels. Set up three shallow dishes for breading the eggplant: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with a mixture of breadcrumbs and grated Parmesan cheese. Dredge each eggplant slice in the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it in the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off before coating it in the breadcrumb mixture. Make sure to press the breadcrumbs onto the eggplant to help them adhere. Step 2: Frying the Eggplant In a large skillet, heat about 1/4 inch of olive oil over medium-high heat. Working in batches, fry the breaded eggplant slices for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden brown and crispy. Transfer the fried slices to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil. Step 3: Assembling the Eggplant Parmesan Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices over the sauce, slightly overlapping them. Spoon more marinara sauce over the eggplant, then sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese on top. Repeat the layers until all the eggplant slices are used, finishing with a final layer of marinara sauce and mozzarella cheese. Step 4: Baking the Eggplant Parmesan Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly. Let the Eggplant Parmesan rest for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and added flavor. Serving Suggestions: This Eggplant Parmesan pairs beautifully with a simple green salad dressed with balsamic vinaigrette and a crusty loaf of garlic bread. It’s also a great dish to serve alongside pasta for a more substantial meal. For a complete Italian dinner experience, consider adding a bottle of your favorite red wine to the table. ...
