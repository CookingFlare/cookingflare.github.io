Peach Raspberry Crisp: A Warm and Comforting Dessert


Are you looking for a delicious dessert that combines the sweet juiciness of peaches with the tartness of raspberries? Look no further! Peach Raspberry Crisp is the perfect treat to satisfy your cravings for something warm and comforting. This easy-to-make dessert is bursting with fruity flavors and topped with a crispy, buttery oatmeal topping. Let’s dive right into the recipe!

Ingredients

For the Filling:

  • 4 cups sliced peaches
  • 2 cups fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9x9-inch baking dish and set it aside.

  2. In a large bowl, combine the sliced peaches, raspberries, granulated sugar, cornstarch, lemon juice, and vanilla extract. Gently toss everything together until the fruit is evenly coated. Transfer the mixture into the prepared baking dish and set aside.

  3. In another bowl, combine the flour, oats, brown sugar, granulated sugar, cinnamon, and salt for the topping. Mix well. Add the cold butter pieces and use your fingers or a pastry cutter to cut the butter into the dry mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the fruit filling, covering it completely. Press down lightly to ensure the topping adheres to the fruit.

  5. Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the fruit filling is bubbly.

  6. Remove the Peach Raspberry Crisp from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dessert is best enjoyed warm, on its own or with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.

Tips and Variations

  • If you prefer a sweeter filling, you can increase the amount of granulated sugar used. Adjust it according to your taste.
  • Feel free to add a pinch of nutmeg or ginger to the topping for a hint of warm spice.
  • You can substitute frozen peaches and raspberries if fresh ones are not available. Just make sure to thaw and drain them before using.
  • Experiment with different fruit combinations! Try using blueberries, blackberries, or even a mix of stone fruits like plums and nectarines.
  • Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

So there you have it, a delightful Peach Raspberry Crisp recipe that will surely become a favorite in your household. It’s the perfect dessert for any occasion, whether you’re hosting a dinner party or simply treating yourself to a cozy evening at home. The combination of juicy peaches, tangy raspberries, and crunchy oatmeal topping is a match made in dessert heaven. Give it a try and let your taste buds dance with joy!
