Vegetarian Stir-Fry Noodle Recipes for College Cooking


Are you a vegetarian college student looking for quick and easy meals that are both delicious and nutritious? Look no further! In this blog post, we will explore some mouthwatering vegetarian stir-fry noodle recipes that are perfect for college cooking. These recipes are not only budget-friendly but also packed with flavor and nutrients. So, grab your wok and let’s get cooking!

1. Spicy Garlic Noodles

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz of your favorite noodles (rice noodles or udon noodles work well)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red chili pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, broccoli, etc.)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sliced chili pepper. Stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the cooked noodles to the wok and toss to combine with the garlic and chili.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and honey. Pour the sauce over the noodles and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables to the wok and stir-fry until they are cooked but still crisp.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with chopped green onions.

2. Peanut Sesame Noodles

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz of your favorite noodles (soba noodles or whole wheat spaghetti work well)
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup hot water
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup edamame
  • Chopped peanuts for garnish
  • Chopped cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together peanut butter, soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, honey, minced garlic, and hot water until smooth.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked noodles, shredded cabbage, shredded carrots, and edamame.
  4. Pour the peanut sauce over the noodle mixture and toss to coat everything evenly.
  5. Garnish with chopped peanuts and cilantro.
  6. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

3. Teriyaki Tofu Noodle Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz of your favorite noodles (ramen noodles or lo mein noodles work well)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 8 oz firm tofu, drained and cubed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (snow peas, mushrooms, bell peppers, etc.)
  • 3 tablespoons teriyaki sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Chopped green onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes and stir-fry until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the mixed vegetables, minced garlic, and grated ginger. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together teriyaki sauce, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce into the skillet and stir to coat the vegetables.
  5. Add the cooked noodles and tofu back into the skillet. Toss everything together until well combined and heated through.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions.
  7. Serve hot.

These vegetarian stir-fry noodle recipes are perfect for college students who want to enjoy a delicious and satisfying meal without spending hours in the kitchen. They are versatile, allowing you to swap in your favorite vegetables and customize the flavors to suit your taste. So, grab your ingredients, follow these simple steps, and enjoy a tasty and wholesome meal in no time. Happy cooking!
