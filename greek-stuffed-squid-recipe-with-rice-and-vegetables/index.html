Greek Stuffed Squid Recipe with Rice and Vegetables


Greek cuisine is renowned for its harmonious blend of fresh ingredients, vibrant flavors, and time-honored cooking techniques. One dish that beautifully represents this Mediterranean balance is stuffed squid with rice and vegetables. Perfect as a main course or centerpiece for a special dinner, this recipe marries tender squid with a flavorful filling of herbed rice, fresh vegetables, and hints of citrus.

This dish not only pays homage to traditional Greek culinary principles but also appeals to adventurous eaters who love trying unique seafood recipes. Whether you’re hosting a Mediterranean-inspired dinner party or exploring new ways to feature squid in your meals, this stuffed squid recipe is sure to delight.

Ingredients

To make this dish shine, focus on sourcing fresh ingredients whenever possible. The flavor comes alive when the squid, vegetables, and herbs are at their peak freshness.

For the Squid and Filling

  • 8 medium-sized fresh squid, cleaned (reserve the tentacles for the filling)
  • 3/4 cup uncooked Arborio rice or medium-grain rice
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 tomato, peeled, seeded, and chopped (or a 1/4 cup of canned chopped tomatoes if fresh isn’t available)
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon (optional, for a warm, earthy dimension)
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For the Sauce

  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Preparation

Step 1: Preparing the Squid

Begin by cleaning the squid thoroughly under cold, running water. Remove the heads and tentacles, which will be used later for the stuffing. Peel away the outer membrane if desired, though this step is optional. Once cleaned, set the squid tubes aside on a plate lined with a paper towel.

Finely chop the tentacles, as they’ll be folded into the filling. Their mild, slightly sweet flavor adds subtle umami to the dish while making the most of every part of this seafood treasure.

Step 2: Cooking the Filling

In a skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, sautéing until fragrant and lightly golden. Next, stir in the diced carrot, bell pepper, and tomato. Allow the vegetables to soften for 5–7 minutes to ensure they’re tender and evenly cooked.

Add the chopped squid tentacles to the skillet and cook for an additional 3–4 minutes. Stir often so they don’t stick to the pan.

Lower the heat and introduce the rice to the mixture, gently folding it in along with the parsley, oregano, cinnamon (if using), salt, and pepper. Add one cup of water to the skillet, allowing the rice to partially cook and absorb the flavors. This should take about 10 minutes. Stir in the juice of one lemon and remove the pan from heat. Let the filling cool slightly before proceeding to the next step.

Step 3: Stuffing the Squid

Take the prepared squid tubes and carefully spoon the filling into each tube, leaving a small bit of space at the top to ensure the rice has room to expand while cooking. Use a toothpick to seal the open end of the tube. Repeat this process for all the squid tubes.

Step 4: Cooking the Stuffed Squid

In a large skillet or shallow saucepan, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. Sear the stuffed squid for 2–3 minutes on each side or until lightly golden.

Pour the white wine and lemon juice into the pan, followed by the dill, olive oil, and about 1/4 cup of water. Season the liquid with salt and pepper, then lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Cover the pan and allow the squid to cook for 25–30 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking and flavor distribution.

The stuffed squid should become tender as it absorbs the rich, tangy flavors of the sauce. If the liquid reduces too quickly, you can add small amounts of water, wine, or broth to maintain a moist cooking environment.

Step 5: Serving the Dish

Once the squid is fully cooked, remove the toothpicks from the ends of the tubes—this step is optional but makes for a more elegant presentation. Serve the squid warm, garnished with freshly chopped dill and an extra drizzle of olive oil if desired.

Accompany this dish with a crisp Greek salad or roasted potatoes seasoned with lemon and oregano for a complete Mediterranean feast. Warm, crusty bread can also be served alongside to soak up the fragrant sauce.

Tips for Success

  • Squid Selection: Opt for squid of medium size—too small, and they’ll be tricky to fill; too large, and they may overpower the delicate flavors of the filling.
  • Season Generously: Mediterranean cooking thrives on bold flavors, so don’t shy away from seasoning with herbs, spices, and citrus.
  • Cook the Rice Partially: To prevent the rice from becoming mushy, partially pre-cooking it before stuffing ensures that it finishes cooking inside the squid tubes evenly.
  • Don’t Rush: Slow simmering allows the squid to tenderize and absorb the flavors of the sauce.

A Taste of Greece in Every Bite

Stuffed squid with rice and vegetables is more than just a meal—it’s an exploration of classic Greek flavors that highlight both simplicity and elegance. With every bite, you’ll experience the interplay of tender seafood, tangy citrus, and the earthy depth of herbs and spices.

Whether you’re experimenting with Mediterranean recipes or elevating your seafood repertoire, this dish promises an unforgettable dining experience that captures the very essence of Greek cooking. It’s perfect for adventurous home cooks who want to bring a taste of the Aegean Sea to their table.

So, gather your fresh ingredients, embrace the slow rhythms of traditional cooking, and enjoy the rewards of this satisfying recipe that echoes the heart and soul of Greece.
