Baking with Kids: Simple and Delicious Oatmeal Raisin Cookies


Baking with kids can be a fun and rewarding experience for both the children and the adults involved. Not only does it create lasting memories, but it also teaches valuable skills and encourages creativity. One of the best recipes to start with when baking with kids is oatmeal raisin cookies. These cookies are simple to make, delicious, and perfect for little hands to help with. In this blog post, we’ll explore a step-by-step guide to making oatmeal raisin cookies with kids, along with tips and tricks to make the experience enjoyable for everyone.

Ingredients

Before diving into the baking process, let’s gather all the ingredients needed for our oatmeal raisin cookies:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup raisins

Step 1: Preparing the Ingredients

The first step in baking with kids is to gather all the necessary ingredients and tools. Encourage the children to help measure the ingredients, pour them into bowls, and prepare the baking sheets with parchment paper. This step not only involves them in the process but also teaches them about organization and preparation.

Step 2: Mixing the Dough

Once the ingredients are prepared, it’s time to start mixing the dough. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, brown sugar, and white sugar until smooth. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture into the wet mixture, and then stir in the oats and raisins. This is the perfect step for kids to get their hands dirty and feel the texture of the dough.

Step 3: Shaping the Cookies

After the dough is mixed, it’s time to shape the cookies. Show the kids how to take spoonfuls of the dough and roll them into balls, then place them onto the prepared baking sheets. This is a great opportunity for kids to get creative with different shapes and sizes, and it encourages them to take pride in their contribution to the baking process.

Step 4: Baking the Cookies

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and bake the cookies for 8 to 10 minutes. While the cookies are baking, take this time to clean up the kitchen and involve the kids in the tidying process. This reinforces the idea that cooking is not just about creating delicious treats but also about responsibility and teamwork.

Step 5: Enjoying the Results

Once the cookies are golden brown, remove them from the oven and let them cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to wire racks to cool completely. Finally, it’s time to enjoy the fruits of your labor! Sit down with the kids, pour a glass of milk, and savor the warm, soft oatmeal raisin cookies together.

Tips for Baking with Kids

  • Safety First: Always supervise the children around hot ovens and stoves, and teach them about the potential dangers in the kitchen.
  • Embrace the Mess: Baking with kids can get messy, and that’s perfectly okay! Let them enjoy the process, and worry about the cleanup later.
  • Teach and Learn: Use this opportunity to teach the kids about different ingredients, measurements, and the science behind baking. You might just learn something new yourself!

Baking with kids is not just about creating delicious treats; it’s about fostering a love for cooking, creating memories, and spending quality time together. So, the next time you’re looking for a fun activity to do with the little ones, gather the ingredients for oatmeal raisin cookies and let the baking adventure begin!
